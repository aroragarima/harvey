Ram Karan Gupta v J. S. Exim Ltd. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 494; (2012) 13 SCC 568; AIR 2013 SC 24; 2013 (1) AWC 929; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 577; 2012 (6) CTC 710; JT 2012 (12) SC 8; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 837; 2013 (1) RLW 855; 2012(11) SCALE 599; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 89 - Auction sale - Belated amount deposited - Challenged - Decree holders/respondents and judgment debtors/appellants were co-sharers of suit property which was sold in public auction and sale proceeds were directed to be distributed among shareholders - Aggrieved, appellant filed an application before Executing Court - Executing Court confirmed auction and dismissed application - On appeal, HC upheld order of Trial Court confirming sale and directed parties to execute documents of title in favour of auction purchaser - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a mode of application had been prescribed for making an application for setting aside sale - Held, there was no reference at all to provisions of O. 21 r. 89 CPC in the application filed by appellant, even then appellant had not complied with mandatory requirements of depositing amount. Cl. (a) of r. 89(1) O. 21 CPC requires applicant to deposit in Court 5 per cent of purchase money for payment to auction purchaser - However, deposit made under r. 89 of O. 21 CPC should be unconditional and unqualified and decree holder or auction purchaser should be able to get amount at once - Further, it was already indicated that rule was in nature of a concession shown to judgment debtor, so he had to strictly comply with requirements thereof and a sale would not be set aside unless entire amount specified in rub-rule (1) was deposited within 60 days from date of sale and, if it was beyond 60 days, Court could not allow application - Therefore, appellant-judgment debtor did not pay amount within stipulated time and he only made an application on 1-12-2010 without depositing amount and hence Court could not entertain such an application and bound to confirm sale which, in instant case, the Court did on 23-10-2010 - Thus, no error in judgment and orders of Executing Court as well as HC and belated offer made by appellant for depositing amount could not be entertained - Appeal dimsised.
