Kamal Pramanik v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 306; (1975) SCC (Cr) 107; (1975) 3 SCC 543; AIR 1975 SC 730; 1975 CRLJ 609; 1975 CRLR 131B; 1975 UJ 127

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Maintenance Of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3 - Detention - Validity - Order passed after one year of incident - Order backed by sufficient reason for delay - Whether order of detention is rendered invalid by delay in order of detention? - Reason for passing detention order about a year after happening of alleged incidents is sufficiently explained - Detention order was necessitated because criminal cases could not proceed and detenu was discharged - There was no unreasonable delay between passing of detention order and his release in criminal cases - Held, detention order valid - Petition dismissed.
