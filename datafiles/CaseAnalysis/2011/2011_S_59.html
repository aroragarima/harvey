Sham @ Kishor Bhaskarrao Matkari v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 608; (2011) 10 SCC 389; AIR 2012 SC 301; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 136; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 750; 2011(11) SCALE 206; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 201 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly murdered three persons in a family - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder and sentenced him to life imprisonment - However, HC, in the appeal of the State, enhanced the sentence of life imprisonment to death - Whether the confirmation of conviction and enhancement of sentence recorded by the HC was justifiable in the instant case - Held, prosecution established its case beyond reasonable doubt for an offence u/s. 302 IPC - Trial Court considering the fact that the murders were neither pre- meditated nor pre-planned on the part of the appellant, and a simple case of land dispute which led to altercation and murdering of three persons, imposed life imprisonment u/s. 302 IPC - Such conclusion of the Trial Court was acceptable - Though the appellant caused death of three persons, he had no pre-plan to done away with the family of his brother and the quarrel started due to the land dispute and, in fact, on the fateful night, he was sleeping with the other victims in the same house - In those circumstances and other materials placed clearly showed that he had no pre-plan or pre-determination to eliminate the family of his brother - At the time of the incident, the appellant was 28 years old and was jobless - Appellant remained in jail for more than 10 years and more than five years in death cell - Materials placed on record showed that the antecedents of the accused-appellant were unblemished as nothing was shown by the prosecution that prior to that incident, he was indulged in criminal activities - No weapon much less dangerous was used in commission of offence - Only on account of property dispute, the appellant went to the extent of committing murders - No reason was there to disbelieve that the appellant should not be reformed or rehabilitated and that he was likely to continue criminal acts of violence as would constitute a continued threat to the society - Considering the facts and circumstances, it cannot be said that the appellant-accused would be a menace to the society - Reasoning assigned by the HC for awarding extreme penalty of death sentence was not acceptable - It was relevant to point out that the Trial Court which had the opportunity of noting demeanour of all the witnesses and the accused thought it fit that life sentence would be appropriate - However, the HC while enhancing the sentence from life to death, had not assigned adequate and acceptable reasons - Further, it was not a rarest of rare case where extreme penalty of death was called for instead sentence of imprisonment for life as ordered by the Trial Court would be appropriate - Therefore, conviction of the appellant-accused for the offence u/s. 302 IPC was maintained and death penalty awarded by the HC was set aside and sentence of life imprisonment awarded by the Trial Court was restored - Appeal partly allowed.
