State Of U.P. v Anil Singh

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 28; (1988) Supp SCC 686; (1989) SCC (Cr) 48; AIR 1988 SC 1998; 1988 AllCriR 621; 1988 (2) AWC 1264; 1988 (3) Crimes 367; 1989 CRLJ 88; 1989 CrLR(SC) 49; JT 1988 (3) SC 491; 1988(2) SCALE 436; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 611

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (1) Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Scope - Appeal against acquittal - Held, Supreme Court has not overriding powers to reweigh evidence - (2) Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 386 and 378 - Appreciation of evidence - Duty of Court - Rejection of prosecution version - Merely on ground that all witnesses were not examined - Or, for want of corroboration by independent witnesses - Further held, is not proper (3) Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Chance witness - Witness figured as eye-witness in First Information Report - Corroborated by another eye-witness - Further held, witness cannot be categorized as chance witness.
