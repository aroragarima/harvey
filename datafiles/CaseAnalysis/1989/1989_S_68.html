Commissioner Of Wealth Tax v Smt. Hashmatunnisa Begum

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 723; (1989) Supp2 SCC 43; AIR 1989 SC 1024; 1989 (75) CTR 194; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 239; [1989] 176 ITR 98; JT 1989 (1) SC 92; 1989(1) SCALE 85; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 155; 1989 (1) UJ 383

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth-tax Act, 1957, s. 29 (1) - Finance Act, 1971 - A/Y 1967-68 - Gift tax - Claim of benefit of exemption under wealth tax - Entitlement - Assessee was legal representative of late Nawab claimed in respect of AY 1967-68, that value of immovable properties gifted by late Nawab to his wives before 1-4-1964 should not be included in the net wealth of Nawab as on valuation dt.31-9-1966 - Nawab under 3 deeds of gift one dt.25-5-1962, in favour of assesse/his first wife, and 2 other deeds dt.17-8-1962 and 26-4-1962, in favour of 'F'/his second wife, gifted in their favour certain lands and buildings of a total value of Rs. 1,96,950 - Gifts were chargeable to gift-tax and were accordingly assessed to gift-tax in A/Y 1963-64 - On behalf of estate of Nawab, it was claimed in proceedings for assessment to wealth for A/Y 1967-68 - Though gifts were otherwise includible as belonging to Nawab u/s. 4(I) (a) (i), as transfers made to spouses otherwise than for adequate consideration, however, as gifts were chargeable to gift tax, proviso to s. 4(I) (a) was attracted and that assets so transferred were not includible in net wealth of Nawab for any assessment year commencing after 31-3-1964 - Wealth-tax Officer rejected this claim -On appeal, Asst. Commissioner confirmed assessment - On appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal and held that assets transferred, which had attracted gift-tax, were not includible in net wealth of Nawab for A/Y 1964-65 onwards - On reference, HC answered reference in affirmative and against revenue - Hence instant petition - Whether gifts made after 31-3-1964 or earlier would attract benefit of the exemption as claimed by assesses - Held, words 'for any assessment year commencing after 31-3-1964' were referrable to assessment to be made under Act1957 - They render the provisions of s. 4(1)(a) inoperative irrespective of fact whether transferred asset was chargeable to gift tax or not chargeable to gift-tax - Proviso specified period of exemption up to 31-3-1964 - Irrespective of the year of gifts when assets were gifted, they would not be included in computation of net wealth of individual till A/Y 1964-65 - Therefore, intention of Parliament was to exempt transfers made s. 4(1)(a) (i) to (iv) from being computed in net wealth of individual upto wealth-tax assessment year commencing after 31-3-1964 - Order accordingly.
