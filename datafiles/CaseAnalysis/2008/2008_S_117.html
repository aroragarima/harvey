Sannaia Subba Rao and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1143; (2008) 17 SCC 225; JT 2008 (8) SC 136; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 973; 2008 (3) RLW 2689; 2008(10) SCALE 401; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366A, 372 r/w 511 - Trial acquitted the appellants but High Court reversed acquittal - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, in 366A what is required to be proved by the prosecution is that there is cogent and reliable evidence to prove and establish that a minor girl under the age of 18 years was induced to come from one place to the other with the intention that such girl may be, forced to have illicit intercourse with another person - There is no reliable and cogent evidence to prove and establish that prosecutrix was kidnapped by the accused persons with the intention of selling her for prostitution, therefore, charge under s. 372 r/w s. 511 IPC is also not proved - Appellant No. 3 is guilty of the offence under s. 363 - Order accordingly.
