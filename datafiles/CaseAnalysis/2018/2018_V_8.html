Pappu and others v Vinod Kumar Lamba and another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2018

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra, Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2018 Indlaw SC 25

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: accepted, age, arrears of land revenue, authorised person, burden of proof, circumstance, Claim Petition, Claims Tribunal, compensation amount, compensation awarded, compliance, compulsory insurance, contract of insurance, disqualification, finding of fact, inquiry, Insurance Company, Insurance Policy, jurisdiction, Motor Accident, Motor Vehicle, opportunity, period, permission, policy conditions, presumption, rash and negligent driving, scrutiny, third party risks

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance â Liability of insurance company â Deceased succumbed to injuries caused when he was knocked down by offending truck and later, claimants filed claim petition before Tribunal â Upon analyzing evidence, Tribunal awarded compensation to claimants but allowed claim petition only against owner of offending vehicle and absolved Insurance Company from any sought of liability â Claimants filed appeal before HC but HC affirmed view taken by Tribunal in absolving Insurance Company and dismissed appeal â Hence, instant appeal â Whether the fact that the offending vehicle was duly insured by Insurance Company would, per se make Insurance Company liable.

Held, owner of the offending vehicle merely raised a vague plea in the Written Statement that the offending vehicle was being driven by a person having valid driving license but had not disclosed the name of the driver and his other details and had also not entered the witness box or examined any witness in support of his plea. Further, owner of the offending vehicle had not produced any evidence except a driving license of one Joginder Singh, without any specific stand taken in the pleadings or in the evidence that the same Joginder Singh was in fact, authorized to drive the offending vehicle at the relevant time. However, owner of the vehicle had produced the insurance certificate indicating that offending vehicle was comprehensively insured by Insurance Company for unlimited liability. Hence, as per dictum of SC in the case of National Insurance Company and also to sub-serve the ends of justice, insurance company is to pay the claim amount awarded by Tribunal to appellants in the first instance, with liberty to recover the same from the owner of the offending vehicle. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio â Even if the insurer succeeds in establishing its defence, the Tribunal or the Court could direct the insurance company to pay the award amount to the claimants and in turn, recover the same from the owner of the offending vehicle.
