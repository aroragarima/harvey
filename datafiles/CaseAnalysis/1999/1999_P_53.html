Pradeep Kumar Jain v Citi Bank and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1334; (1999) 6 SCC 361; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1124; AIR 1999 SC 3119; [1999] 98 Comp Cas 785; 1999 (2) CPJ 7A; JT 1999 (5) SC 639

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 21 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 146 and 196 - Deficiency in service - Vehicle purchased on hire purchase - Bank promising to renew insurance policy of vehicle - Vehicle owner giving cheques for premium to its banker - Insurance not renewed - Vehicle met with an accident - Award for damages passed - Claim filed before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission - Failed - Held, for deficiency in service, banker cannot be liable to pay entire damages - Obligation to obtain policy was on owner of vehicle - Merely by giving cheques for premium owner could not be said to have discharged all his duties in matter of obtaining policy.
