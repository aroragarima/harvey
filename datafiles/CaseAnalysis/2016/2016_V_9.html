VLS Finance Limited and another v Commissioner of Income Tax and another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 322; AIR 2016 SC 2073; [2016] 384 ITR 1; JT 2016 (4) SC 448; 2016(4) SCALE 403; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 390; [2016] 239 TAXMAN 404

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Assessing Officer, Assessment Proceedings, Limitation, Exclusion, Audit Report, Special Audit, Block Assessment, Income-tax Act, 1961, Seizure, Finance Act, 2013

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 142(2A), 158BE(2) - Order for special audit - Interim stay - Commencement of limitation period - Search and seizure took place in business premises of appellant companies on 22-6-1998 which went up 23-6-1998 which followed till 5-8-1998 - Further, direction was issued u/s. 142(2A) of Act on 29-6-2000 for conducting special audit for block period from 1-4-1988 to 22-April 1, 16-198 - Appellant contended that time limit for completion of Block Assessment expired on 30-6-2000 in terms of s. 158BE of Act, since 2 years period expired on that date - Interim stay of orders dt. 29-6- 2000 was passed - HC vide impugned order held that period between 24-8-2000, i.e., date on which interim order was passed staying special audit direction u/s. 142(2A) dt. 29-6-2000 and 15-12-2016, i.e., when HC has passed order setting aside direction for special audit, be excluded in counting limitation for concluding block assessment - Whether impugned order passed by HC in favor of Revenue is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Explanation 1 to s. 158BE(2) of Act states that period during which assessment proceedings are stayed by order of court is to be excluded while computing period of limitation for completion of Block Assessments. Further, with respect to commencement of period of limitation period, SC held that revenue authorities visited and searched premises of appellants for first time on 22-6-1998 and panchnama drawn on that date, it was remarked 'temporarily concluded' and it was only on 5-8-1998 when premises were searched last, panchnama drawn on that date recorded remarks that search was 'finally concluded'. Further, appellant had not challenged validity of searches on subsequent dates on ground that same were illegal in absence of any fresh and valid authorisation. Hence, limitation period is not to be counted from last date of search when search operation completed, i.e. 5-8-1998. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - In view of specified provision in Act, period during which assessment proceedings are stayed by order of court is to be excluded while computing period of limitation for completion of Block Assessments.
