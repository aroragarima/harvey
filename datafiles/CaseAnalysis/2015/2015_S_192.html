Bennet Coleman and Company Limited v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 133; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1620; 2015 (145) FLR 217; JT 2015 (2) SC 479; 2015 (2) LLJ 590; 2015(2) SCALE 571; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 372; 2015 (3) SLR 641

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Working Journalists And Other Newspaper Employees (Conditions Of Service) And Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, Journalists

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Working Journalists and Other Newspaper Employees (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, s. 3 - Recommendations of Wage Board - Award - Deputy Labour Commissioner filed complaint against appellant alleging that recommendations of Manisana Wage Board were not properly implemented and section of journalists were discriminated in hostile manner by appellant and sought prosecution u/s. 25U read with s. 29 of 1947 Act - Appellant filed petition before HC u/s. 482 of CrPC but the same was dismissed holding that complaint was maintainable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant is liable for prosecution u/s. 25U read with s. 29 and sl.no.13 of Fifth Schedule of 1947 Act.

Held, recommendations of Wage Board is neither an award nor settlement in terms of provisions under 1947 Act. It is not passed by Labour Court or Industrial Tribunal or National Industrial Tribunal and it is not an Arbitration Award in terms of s. 10A of 1947 Act. It is not a settlement in terms of s. 2(b) of 1947 Act. It is not an agreement between the parties. Its enforceability, being recommendation, depends on the order passed by Central Govt. The Central Govt. has passed order by issuing notification and if the same is not complied with, then the remedy lies u/s. 17 of 1955 Act for recovery or u/s. 18 of 1955 Act for penalty but not under the provisions of 1947 Act. Further, employees' union had already taken recourse to remedy u/s. 17(2) of 1955 Act before Labour Court with regard to amount due in terms of notification issued by Central Govt. u/s. 12 and if Labour Court passes appropriate award and the same is not implemented then alone question of prosecution u/s. 25U read with sl.no.13 of Fifth Schedule of 1947 Act would lie. Hence, appeal is allowed and impugned order is set aside and complaint and order of Magistrate taking cognizance are quashed. Further, in view of the same, other appeals are also allowed and orders impugned therein are set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s. 25U read with Sl.No.13 of Fifth schedule of 1947 Act, prosecution lies in case of failure to implement award, settlement or agreement. Award means determination of industrial dispute or any question relating thereto by any Labour Court, Industrial Tribunal or National Industrial Tribunal or arbitration award u/s. 10A and Settlement as per s. 2(p) of 1947 Act, is settlement arrived at in the course of conciliation proceedings and includes written agreement between employer and workmen otherwise than in the course of conciliation proceedings.
