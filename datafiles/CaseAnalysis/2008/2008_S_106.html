Sanghi Brothers (Indore) Pvt. Ltd. v Sanjay Choudhary And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2667; (2008) 10 SCC 681; AIR 2009 SC 9; 2009 CRLJ 338; 2008 (4) JCC 2819; JT 2008 (11) SC 460; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 640; 2008(13) SCALE 133; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 206

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 406 r/w. s. 34 - Framing of charges - Agreements were executed between the complainant and accused for delivery of dumpers and light commercial vehicles - Accused persons took the delivery of the aforesaid vehicles from the complainant and agreed for payment of the monthly lease rent for 36 months - After some time, Complainant was unable to get any rent in time from the accused persons and also found that accused in violation of the condition of the agreement had illegally sold eight vehicles to other parties with ulterior motive, thus committing criminal breach of trust also and had cheated the complainant - Complainant filed a complainant u/ss. 420 and 406 of IPC against the respondents - Trial Court ordered for framing of the charges - Accused filed petition against said order - Whether trial court rightly ordered for framing of charges against accused persons? - Held, even if there is a strong suspicion about the commission of offence and the involvement of the accused, it is sufficient for the court to frame a charge - At present stage, there is no necessity of formulating the opinion about the prospect of conviction - Hence, impugned order of the HC cannot be sustained and set aside - Appeal allowed.
