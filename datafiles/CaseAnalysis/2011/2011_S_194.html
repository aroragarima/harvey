Om Prakash v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 424; (2011) 14 SCC 309; AIR 2011 SC 2682; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2707; 2011 CRLJ 4225; JT 2011 (8) SC 69; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2563; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 372; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 616; 2011(7) SCALE 396; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 1080

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 363, 366, 368 and 376(2)(g) - Kidnapping - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - 'J' was committed to the Court of Additional Sessions Judge u/ss. 363, 366 and 376(2)(g) of the IPC and appellant u/ss. 368 and 376(2)(g) of IPC - Appellant denied the incident and stated that he had never known either 'J' or the prosecutrix - Trial Court held that all the essential ingredients constituting offence for which the accused were charged were fully proved and convicted both the accused of the said offences - Conviction and sentence was upheld by HC - Hence, instant appeal filed by appellant challenging his conviction - Appellant contended that he had been falsely implicated in the case and he had no role to play either in the alleged kidnapping of the prosecutrix or in raping her - Whether appellant was entitled to the benefit of doubt? - Held, as per the facts of the instant case, it was proved that the intention to kidnap and commit rape or subject her to sexual assault was the intention of 'J' alone - There was no prior plan or meeting of minds between the appellant and the 'J' to either kidnap or to rape the prosecutrix - As per the statement of the prosecutrix, the appellant had provided a room to both 'J' and the prosecutrix - Hence, as per the evidence on record the role of the appellant was limited to wrongfully confining the prosecutrix and not rendering help when asked for - Further, there was no evidence that there was a common concert or common intention or meeting of minds prior to commission of the offence between the two accused - Judgment of the Trial Court convicting the accused u/s. 376(2)(g) of the IPC was set aside and he was acquitted of the said charge - However, his conviction u/s. 368 of the IPC was maintained - Appeal dismissed.
