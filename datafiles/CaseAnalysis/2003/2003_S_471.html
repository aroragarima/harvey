M. Narayandas v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 772; (2003) 11 SCC 251; (2004) SCC (Cr) 118; AIR 2004 SC 555; 2003 (4) CCR 351; 2003 (4) Crimes 159; 2004 (1) CRJ 386; 2004 CRLJ 822; 2003 (3) JCC 1618; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 412; 2003(8) SCALE 1; 2003 (1) SCW 6030; 2003 (7) SLT 51; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 973; 2003 (6) Supreme 863

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 468, 470, s. 471 - CrPC, 1973, ss.154(1), 156, 157, 195, 341, 482 - Quashing if FIR - Held, it is settled law that power to quash must be exercised very sparingly and with circumspection - It must be exercised in rarest of rare cases - It is also settled law that Court would not be justified in embarking upon an inquiry as to reliability or genuineness or otherwise of allegations made in FIR Court also cannot inquire whether allegations in complaint are likely to be established or not - Further held, ss 195 and 340 do not control or circumscribe power of police to investigate under CrPC, 1973 - Once investigation is completed, then embargo in s 195 would come into play and Court would not be competent to take cognizance - However that Court could then file a complaint of offence on basis of FIR and material collected during investigation provided procedure laid down in s 340, CrPC is followed - Thus, no right of respondents, much less right to file an appeal u/s. 341, is affected.
