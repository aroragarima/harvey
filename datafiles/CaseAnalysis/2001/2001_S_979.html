Punjabrao v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21027; (2002) 10 SCC 371; AIR 2002 SC 486; 2001 (2) CCR 121; 2001 (3) Crimes 309; JT 2001 (5) SC 532; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 231; 2001 (3) SLT 353; 2001 (5) Supreme 231

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Socio-Economic - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(2) and (2)(d) - Whether High Court in exercise of its power against an order of acquittal was duty-bound to examine reasons, which persuaded Trial Judge to come to a conclusion that explanation offered by accused was probable and reasonable, and having not done so, order of acquittal could not have been interfered? - Held, where accused offers an explanation for receipt of alleged amount, question that arises for consideration is whether that explanation can be said to have been established - Accused is not required to establish his defence by proving beyond reasonable doubt - HC erred in interfering with well reasoned judgment - Appeal allowed.
