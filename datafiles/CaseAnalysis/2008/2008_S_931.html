Maharaj Krishan Bhatt and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Othrs

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1206; (2008) 9 SCC 24; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 615; 2008 (118) FLR 883; JT 2008 (13) SC 593; 2008(11) SCALE 29; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 670; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 586

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Jammu & Kashmir Police Manual, regn.174 of chap. VII - Appointment to the post of of Sub-Inspector of Police - By granting necessary relaxation in Rules against 50% direct recruitment quota as envisaged by Regn. 174 - DGP recommended the name of one of the five only - Non consideration of candidate despite directions from the Court - Government filing intra-Court and the Division Bench interfered Appeal against judgment passed by the Division Bench in Letters Patent Appeal as well as rejected Review Petition in the light of judgement in similar case of Abdul Rashid's case - Submitted that the impugned action was clearly violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Division Bench held, had committed an error of law as well as of jurisdiction - Govt. ought to have accepted and respected the decision without filing intra-Court appeal - Moreover, once a similar case of Abdul Rashid Rather came up for consideration before a Single Judge and his writ petition was allowed, a direction was issued to the authorities to appoint him as PSI by granting consequential benefits, the learned Single Judge could not be said to have committed any error of law in following the said decision, in allowing the writ petition filed by the present appellants-writ petitioners and in issuing similar directions to the State Authorities - Division Bench ought not to have interfered with the said decision - Appellants are held entitled to all the reliefs which had been granted to Abdul Rashid Rather in pursuance of the judgment - Appeal Allowed.
