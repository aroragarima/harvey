Commissioner Of Income Tax, Bhopal v Ralson Industries Ltd

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1; (2007) 2 SCC 326; AIR 2007 SC 668; 2007 (214) E.L.T. 166; [2007] 288 ITR 322; JT 2007 (1) SC 356; 2007(1) SCALE 44; 2007 (4) SCJ 344; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 53; 2007 (7) S.T.R. 497; [2007] 158 TAXMAN 160

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fresh Assessment

Summary: Income Tax Act - Interpretation of provisions of ss. 154, 263 - Held, initiation of a proceeding under s. 263 of the Act cannot be held to have become bad in law only because an order of rectification was passed - Only because an order of assessment has undergone rectification at the hands of the Assessing Officer, the same would not mean that revisional authority shall be denuded of exercising its revisional jurisdiction - It would not be correct to contend that only because a proceeding for rectification was initiated subsequently, the revisional jurisdiction could not have been invoked under any circumstances whatsoever - Commissioner of Income Tax is directed to have a fresh look at the matter in the light of the order of rectification passed by the assessing authority - Appeal allowed.
