Tika Ram & Sons Ltd. Etc. v The Commissioner Of Sales Tax U.P., Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1968 SC 1286; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 512; 1968 (22) STC 308

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2(h), expln. II, cl. (ii) and 11 - Constitution, art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.115 - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Sales Tax liability - Theory of nexus - Appellants voluntarily submitting to jurisdiction of Revisional Authority under 1948 Act and HC - (A) Manufacture or production after agreement for sale is required u/ss. 2(h), expln. II, cl. (ii) - (B) Validity of expln. II to s. 2 (h) of 1948 Act - (C) Whether, now he can take exception to jurisdiction of HC - (D) Ammendment in s.11 of 1948 Act - But only existence of order u/s.10 is required for accrual of right - Right exists regardless of when application for revision was made - Whether right to apply for reference to HC is altered by ammendment - Held, not a requirement - Not ultra vires - Held, having taken a chance of judgment in their favour could not take exception - Amendment does not alter any vested or substantive right and presumption against retrospective construction has no application to enactments affecting only procedure and practice of Courts - Appeal Dismissed.
