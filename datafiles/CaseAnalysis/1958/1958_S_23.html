Gummalapura Taggina Matadakotturuswami v Setra Veeravva and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1959 SC 577; 1959 (1) MLJ 158; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 158; 1959 MPLJ 662; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 968; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Women Children Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r.7, O. 41 r.27 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Specific Relief Act 1877, s. 42, illustration (f) - Adoption validity - Meaning of word 'possessed' in s. 14 of Hindu Succession Act - Possession of property by female - Word 'possessed' in s. 14 is used in a broad sense and in context means state of owning or having in one's hand or power - Property must be in possession of female concerned at date of commencement of Act - That possession might have been either actual or constructive or in any form recognized by law, but unless the, female Hindu, whose limited estate in disputed property is claimed to have been transformed into absolute estate under this particular section, was at least in such possession - Section would not apply - (B) Whether Appellate Court can take into account any later change in law - Held, yes it can - Whether s. 42 of Specific Relief Act, 1871, illustration (F) refers to full owner ? Held, it is applicable only to Hindu widow to whom reference is made - No reference to full owner - Appeal dismissed.
