Banwari Lal v Chando Devi Smt. (Through LRs.) and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 723; (1993) 1 SCC 581; AIR 1993 SC 1139; AIR 1993 SCW 354; 1994 (23) ALR 22; 1993 (1) AnWR 64; 1993 (1) BLJR 163; 1992 (3) CCC 780; 1993 (49) DLT 276; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 420; 1993 MahLJ 773; 1993 MPLJ 469; 1995 RD 133; 1992(3) SCALE 448; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 524

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Non Appealable Order

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 96, 151, O. 23 rr.1, 3, O. 43 rr. 1-A, 1(m) - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Suit had been dismissed as withdrawn - Appellant got from records, Advocate of appellant, in collusion with defendant 2 filed a fabricated petition of compromise although no compromise had been effected - It was stated that as alleged compromise itself was void, illegal and against requirement of O. 23, r. 3 of CPC, 1908 - Order recording such compromise recalled and suit restored - Against it revision application filed before High Court, which set aside aforesaid order passed - Whether Subordinate Judge was justified in considering question as to whether there had been a lawful agreement or compromise on basis of which court could have recorded such agreement or compromise? - Held, yes O. 23 r.3 of CPC, 1908 does not require just a seal of approval from Court to an alleged agreement or compromise said to have been entered into between parties - Statute requires Court to be first satisfied that agreement or compromise which has been entered into between parties is lawful - Court is expected to apply its judicial mind while examining terms of settlement before suit is disposed of in terms of agreement arrived at between parties - Appeal allowed.
