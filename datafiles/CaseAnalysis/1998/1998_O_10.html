Chetak Construction Limited v Om Prakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1659; (1998) 4 SCC 577; AIR 1998 SC 1855; AIR 1998 SCW 1653; JT 1998 (3) SC 269; 1998(3) SCALE 153; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1016; 1998 (4) SLT 533; 1998 (4) Supreme 191

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Comment, Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Suit filed by appellant for declaration and permanent injunction - Trial court granted an ex parte temporary injunction to the appellant but the same came to be vacated after hearing both sides - Appeal filed by appellant which was dismissed by learned single judge - Flat in which respondent no. 1 was residing was purchased by Learned Judge - Appellant contended that Learned Judge cannot hear the appeal after the purchase of the flat - Whether appellant can be held for contempt of court? - Fair comments, even if, outspoken, but made without any malice or attempting to impair the administration of justice and made in good faith, in proper language, do not attract any punishment for contempt of court - But when deliberate attempt is discernible to bring down the image of judiciary in the estimation of the public or to impair the administration of justice - It is improper for a Judge of the High Court to "direct" that an appeal pending before him be decided by the Supreme Court itself "finally" and to further suggest that this Court should not "remand the appeal" to the learned Single Judge "or to any other Judge of any HC - Held, no cause to have been made out to institute contempt proceedings - Appeal disposed off.
