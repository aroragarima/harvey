Smt. Savitri v Govind Singh Rawat

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 62; (1985) 4 SCC 337; (1985) SCC (Cr) 556; AIR 1986 SC 984; 1985 BBCJ 160; 1986 (88) BomLR 223; 1985 (2) Crimes 872; 1986 CRLJ 41; 1986 CRLR 1; 1985 (28) DLT 437; 1985(2) SCALE 697; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 615; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure Criminal Procedure Code,1973, s. 125 - Jurisdiction of Magistrate u/s 125, CrPC, 1973 - Whether a Magistrate can make an interim order regarding disposal of a pending application? -Held, s. 125, CrPC, 1973 should be interpreted as conferring power by necessary implication on Magistrate to pass an order directing a person against whom an application is made under it to pay a reasonable sum by way of interim maintenance subject to other conditions referred to pending final disposal of application - If allegations in application or affidavit are not true, it is always open to person against whom such an order is made to show that order is unsustainable -Petition disposed.
