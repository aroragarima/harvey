Kasturi Bai and others v Anguri Chaudhary

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 167; (2003) 3 SCC 225; AIR 2003 SC 1773; 2003 (50) ALR 680; 2003 (1) ARC 415; 2003 (2) AWC 1143; 2003 (1) CGLJ 332; 2003 (2) CHN 176; 2003 (1) CLR 507; JT 2003 (2) SC 159; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 54; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 765; 2003 (1) RD 493; 2003(1) SCALE 735; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 892; 2003 (1) SLT 806; 2003 (1) Supreme 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: C.P.C. - Order 32 Rule 15 - suit for partition of certain immovable properties - appellant, aged 87, alleged that she had lost her ability to understand and further was not capable to give instructions to her lawyer or anybody else relating to the said suit - prayer to enable the Court to inquire about her state of mind and upon medical examination, if necessary a guardian be appointed for defending her in the suit - held, the High Court could only issue a direction directing the learned trial Judge to hold an inquiry so as to enable it to arrive at a finding as to whether the respondent herein was incapable of protecting her interest by reason of any mental infirmity or not. As no such inquiry was held, there cannot be any doubt whatsoever that the learned Single Judge committed a jurisdiction error in passing the impugned judgment.
