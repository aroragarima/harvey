Â Kamal Narain Sharma v Shri Pandit Dwarka Prasad Mishra And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 172; AIR 1966 SC 436; 1966 JabLJ 293; 1966 MahLJ 493; 1966 MPLJ 765; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 478

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dismissed, Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Election - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 94-A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 139, 139(c) and O.XIX - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83(1) -Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, s.539 - Indian Oaths Act, s. 4 - Election petition filed after last General Election to Madhya Pradesh legislative assembly - Affidavit sworn before Commr. of Oaths - Affidavit sworn before district clerk of court - (A)Whether clerk of court is a persons having by law authority to receive evidence? - S. 4 of Oaths Act which is a general provision mentions generally persons having by law authority to receive evidence- Held, clerk of court is not persons having by law authority to receive evidence - (B)Whether original affidavit did not satisfy Conduct of Election Rules,1961 and requirements of r.94-A of Representation of People Act, 1951? - R. 94-A of Conduct of Election Rules,1961 makes receivable an affidavit sworn before a Commr. of Oaths without specifying of what kind - Further held, affidavit sworn before district clerk of court, who undoubtedly was a Commr. of Oaths could only be excluded by taking an extreme and technical view which was not justified - Appeal allowed.
