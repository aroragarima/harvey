Harish Kumar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 856; (2015) 2 SCC 601; JT 2014 (13) SC 584; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 669; 2015 (1) RLW 139; 2014(14) SCALE 66

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry Death - Dying declaration - Conviction - Challenged - Prosecution alleged that appellant put deceased on fire - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC, and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for 7 years - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, dying declaration made by the deceased, before Naib Tehsildar in the presence of Medical Officer, is voluntary and truthful. Defence has discharged its onus to rebut the presumption that could have been gathered u/s. 113B of the Act, in respect of offence punishable u/s. 304-B of IPC. That being so, following the principle of law laid down in Nallam Veera Stayanandam and others v. Public Prosecutor, High Court of A.P. 2004 Indlaw SC 170, SC uphold the conviction of the appellant u/s. 498A of IPC, and sentence him to rigorous imprisonment for a period of 3years, which he has already undergone, and set aside the conviction and sentence recorded against the appellant, in respect of the offence punishable u/s. 304B of IPC. Appeal partly allowed
