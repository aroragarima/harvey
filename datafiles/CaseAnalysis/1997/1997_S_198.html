Sirpur Paper Mills Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 74; (1998) 1 SCC 400; AIR 1998 SC 1489; AIR 1998 SCW 366; 1998 (74) ECR 1; 1998 (97) E.L.T. 3; JT 1997 (10) SC 82; 1997 (23) RLT 886; 1997(7) SCALE 594; 1998 (1) SLT 336; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 431; 1997 (10) Supreme 402

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act 1944, s. 3 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(26) - Excise duty - Claim of exemption on ground machinery installed is immovable property attached to earth - Tribunal finding that machine was attached to earth for operational efficiency and could be sold by dismantling it and selling its parts - Plea was that whatever is embedded in earth must be treated as immovable property - Held, Plea not sound - Machinery installed for better functioning not immovable property- Property is liable to excise duty.
