Board of Wakf, West Bengal v Anis Fatma Begum and another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 990; 2011 (1) ALD(SC) 61; 2011 (84) ALR 238; 2011 (1) CTC 636; 2010 (4) KLT 765; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 219; 2011 (1) OLR 463; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 303; 2011 (112) RD 280; 2010(12) SCALE 323; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Wakf Act, 1995, ss. 83 and 84 - Jurisdiction - 'X' by Wakf deed created the Wakf with the object of dedicating the impugned property partly for the benefit of the Wakifa, her family and descendants and partly for pious and religious purposes - In order to give effect to the object of the Wakf, the impugned property had been demarcated and/or divided into two portions having 75% share for Wakf-al-al-aulad and 25% share for pious and religious purposes - Suit was filed challenging said division of impugned property - Single Judge held that the said partition is not correct and has not been made in consonance with the provisions of Wakf Deed - Single Judge also held that the Wakf Act, 1995 is applicable for the portion of the impugned property divided and earmarked for Wakf-al-al-aulad - On appeal, DB held that the said partition is not correct and Wakf Act, 1995 is not applicable for the portion of the impugned property - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that only Wakf Tribunal has jurisdiction in the matter under the Wakf Act, 1995 and hence the suit filed in the HC was without jurisdiction - Whether impugned suit filed in HC was without jurisdiction? - Held, all matters pertaining to Wakfs should be filed in the first instance before the Wakf Tribunal constituted u/s. 83 of the 1995 Act and should not be entertained by the Civil Court or by the HC straightaway u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India - A party aggrieved by the decision of the Tribunal can approach the HC which can call for the records for satisfying itself as to the correctness, legality or propriety of the decision of the Tribunal - Hence, impugned judgment of HC cannot be sustained and it is set aside - Appeal allowed.
