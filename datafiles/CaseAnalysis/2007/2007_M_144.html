Mahalaxmi Motors Limited v Mandal Revenue Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1639; (2007) 11 SCC 714; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1397; JT 2007 (12) SC 200; 2007(12) SCALE 40; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 964

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Courts Act, 1972, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition of Inams Act, 1955, Andhra Pradesh Land Encroachment Act, 1905

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition of Inams Act, 1955 - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982, s. 8 - Land in question was vested in the State under the provisions of 1955 Act, however, the land was sold by the predecessor-in-title to appellant company - Predecessor-in-title, in the meantime, filed a writ petition seeking occupancy certificate from the State, which was dismissed by the HC - Appellant made huge investment on the land, however, respondent Revenue send a notice to appellant for eviction - Appellant challenged the legality of notice before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Further, appellant's application for regularization of occupation was also rejected by the State Government - Respondent revenue initiated eviction proceedings u/s. 8 of the Act against the appellant before the Special Court - Special Court ordered eviction - Special Court order was challenged before the HC, however, HC dismissed the same - Hence the instant appeal - (A) Whether the Special Court had the jurisdiction for ordering eviction - Held, Special Court had the jurisdiction with regard to the case of land grabbing, determination of question of title and ownership involved on it and possession thereof - Condition precedent for assuming jurisdiction by the Special Court was that the case should have arisen out of any alleged act of land grabbing and a District Judge while acting as a Special Court merely acts as a Presiding Officer of the Court - Further, Civil Court was barred from trying the matters which fall within the exclusive jurisdiction of the Special Court constituted under the Act - Lawful entitlement on the part of a party to possess the land being the determinative factor, it was axiomatic that so long as the land grabber would not be able to show his legal entitlement to hold the land, the jurisdiction of the Special Court could not be held to be ousted - (B) Whether the appellant company entitled to obtain the order of regulation with respect to the land in question - Held, predecessor-in-title had no title over the land and it was obligatory on the part of the appellant keeping in view the doctrine of caveat emptor to examine the title of the vendor over the land it purchased - Within a period of two years from the date of purchase, the appellant became aware that it had acquired no title thereto and it had illegally been occupying the land, as the same belonged to the Government and, thus, the latter was entitled to possess the same - Further, appellant had made constructions illegally on the disputed land and the same was specifically pleaded in its written statement before the Special Court - Appellant filed the application for regularization knowing fully well that they had no title and ownership over the land - Once appellant had taken a specified stand knowing fully well that it had no right, title and interest in or over the land in question, it could not in law turn around and contend that the same was not binding on it - Doctrine of estoppel would squarely be applicable in the instant case - An abstract belief on the part of the appellant that its vendor had a marketable title and it was getting a good title to the land was not decisive - Whether any action was taken by the authorities of the State in regard to the possession of predecessor-in-title or the appellant was wholly irrelevant inasmuch predecessor-in-title and consequently the appellant had no title over the property nor acquired any title by prescription - No case had been made out by the appellant for calling interference on impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
