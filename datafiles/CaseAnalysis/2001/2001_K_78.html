State of Madhya Pradesh v Kajad

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 58; (2001) 7 SCC 673; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1520; AIR 2001 SC 3317; 2001 (4) Crimes 285; 2002 (1) CRJ 91; 2001 CRLJ 4240; 2001 (2) JCC 173; JT 2001 (7) SC 560; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 219; 2001(6) SCALE 150; 2001 (3) SCJ 360; 2001 (6) SLT 437; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 617; 2001 (6) Supreme 844; 2001 (2) UC 654

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 37 and 85 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Grant of bail - Justification - Opium weighing 7 kgs. was seized from the accused which he had kept in his bag - Charge-sheet was submitted against the accused for offence punishable u/s. 18 of the Act - Application filed for bail by the accused was rejected by the Trial Court - Accused filed application for bail before HC, which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, for granting the bail the court must, on the basis of the record produced before it, be satisfied that there are reasonable grounds for believing that the accused is not guilty of the offences with which he is charged and further that he is not likely to commit any offence while on bail. It has further to be noticed that the conditions for granting the bail, specified in s. 37(1)(b) of the Act are in addition to the limitations provided under the CrPC or any other law for the time being in force regulating the grant of bail. In the instant case, impugned order having been passed in violation of the provisions of the Act by ignoring the mandatory requirements of s. 37 of the Act and the conditions governing the grant of bail under the CrPC and is thus not sustainable. Appeal allowed.
