Mahesh Kumar (Dead) By L.Rs. v Vinod Kumar And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 97; (2012) 4 SCC 387; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 71; 2012(3) ALL MR 470; 2012 (92) ALR 480; 2012 (3) ALT 36; 2012 (3) AWC 2203; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 144; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 433; 2012 (2) JabLJ 214; JT 2012 (3) SC 338; 2012 (4) MahLJ 482; 2012 (3) MPLJ 367; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 493; 2012 (116) RD 401; 2012 (2) RLW 1786; 2012(3) SCALE 388

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 59, 63(c) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 67, 68 - Execution of Will - Joint family property - Partition - Validity - Father of appellants and respondents and their father were members of joint family - Respondent No. 2 took his share and separated from joint family - Another partition took place among remaining members of joint family wherein appellant, respondent No. 1 and their father shared properties - Appellant filed suit for declaration, possession, permanent injunction and recovery of rent in respect of the share of his father contending that father died executing Will dt. 10-02-1992, bequeathing his share in his favor - Trial Court decreed suit of appellant - On appeal, HC reversed decree of trial court declared that respondents shall be entitled to possession of his share in suit property in terms of Will dt. 09-06-1989 - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in concluding that execution of Will dated 10.2.1992 was shrouded with suspicion and appellant failed to dispel suspicion - Held, fact that appellant was present at time of execution of Will dated 10.2.1992 and that testator did not give anything to respondents from his share in joint family property are not decisive of issue relating to genuineness or validity of Will - Evidence produced by parties unmistakably show that respondent No. 2 had separated from family in 1965 after taking his share and respondent No. 1 also got his share in 2nd partition which took place in 1985, neither of them bothered to look after parents in their old age - Attitude of respondents left father and his wife with no choice but to live with appellant, who took care of old parents and looked after them during their illness - Therefore, there was nothing unnatural or unusual in decision of father to give his share in joint family property to appellant - Any person of ordinary prudence would have adopted same course and would not have given anything to ungrateful children from his / her share in property - HC, was clearly in error in reversing the well-reasoned finding recorded by trial Court - Appeals allowed.
