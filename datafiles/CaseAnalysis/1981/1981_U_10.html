K. Janardhan Pillai & Anr. Etc. Etc. v Union Of India & Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 201; (1981) 2 SCC 45; AIR 1981 SC 1485; 1981 KLT 251; 1981(1) SCALE 193; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 676; 1981 UJ 612

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Trade

Keywords: Cashewnut, Declaration, Turmeric, Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Madras Essential Articles Control And Requisitioning (Temporary Powers) Act, Kerala Raw Cashewnuts (Procurement and Distribution) Order 1977, Pulses, Edible Oilseeds And Edible Oils (Storage Control) Order, 1977, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Regulation Of Supply And Purchase Order, 1954

Summary: Trade - Administrative - Kerala Essential Articles Control (Temporary Powers) Act, 1961, ss. 2(a), 2(a)(v), 3 - Kerala Raw Cashewnuts (Procurement and Distribution) Order, 1977 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Cashewnut - Essential commodity - Declaration - Validity - State of Kerala in exercise of power u/s. 2(a) of 1961 Act and the Order, which was made u/s. 3 of 1961 Act, regulating the procurement and distribution of raw cashewnuts grown in the State - Petitioners were engaged in cashewnut processing industry in the State of Kerala - Since impugned declaration and the Order seriously interfered with the right of petitioners to purchase sufficient quantities of raw cashewnuts for processing in their factories and imposed several other restrictions on them, they have filed instant petitions - Petitioner contended that raw cashewnut was a foodstuff, which was an essential commodity under 1955 Act, State Govt. could not make a declaration to the effect that it was an essential article u/s. 2(a) of 1961 Act and consequently impugned Order was outside the scope of 1961 Act - Whether declaration made by Sate Govt., declaring that raw cashewnut was an essential article under 1961 Act and the Order were valid - Held, it was no doubt true that cashewnut having become expensive, it was now more of a luxury - Due to export of cashewnut on a large scale, it was a commodity which was in short supply in country and thus the price at which it sells was beyond the reach of common man - But nevertheless it was an article of food - It was eaten in raw form and after it was fried - It was also commonly used in various preparations of food - When cashewnut was exported, it was exported as a foodstuff - It could not be that cashewnut eaten abroad was a foodstuff, and whatever was consumed within the country was not a foodstuff - It was thus, a foodstuff and should be classified as an essential commodity - Central Government could make an Order under the 1955 Act even when an essential commodity was used for industrial purpose or for purposes of export - Essential commodities did not cease to be essential commodities under 1955 Act merely because they were exported after they were processed in India - Raw cashewnut was a foodstuff falling u/s. 2(a)(v) of 1955 Act and could not be declared as an essential article u/s. 2(a) of 1961 Act - It follows that no Order could be made by Govt. of Kerala u/s. 3 of 1961 Act in respect of raw cashewnut - Action of Govt. of Kerala was beyond the power conferred on it by Kerala Legislature - Declaration made by Govt. of Kerala to the effect that raw cashewnut was an essential article under 1961 Act and the impugned Order made thereunder were liable to be quashed - If striking down of declaration and the Order adversely affect number of workmen, it could always be set right by competent Legislature or appropriate Govt. taking needful remedial action in light of Entry 33 of List III of the Seventh Schedule to Constitution - Petitions allowed.
