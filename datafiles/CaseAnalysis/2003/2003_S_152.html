Union of India and others v Shivendra Bikaram Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 386; (2003) 6 SCC 359; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1343; AIR 2003 SC 2481; 2003 (3) AllCJ 2101; 2003 (2) CCR 101; 2003 (3) Crimes 17; 2003 (5) CRJ 381; 2003 CRLJ 3028; 2003 (4) ESC 463; 2003 (2) JCC 912; JT 2003 (4) SC 235; 2003 (3) LLN 5; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 713; 2003(4) SCALE 284; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 881; 2003 (3) SLT 300; 2003 (3) Supreme 537

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Navy Act, 1957

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.325, 355, 376, 452, 497, 506 - Navy Act, 1957, ss.102, 103, 105 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226, art. 227 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963 - Army Act, 1950, s.130 - Controversy regarding nature of authority exercised by trial Judge Advocate to reject summarily, without reference to members of Court Martial, any objection not made on a ground, which affects competency of a member to act as an impartial judge - Held, when an objection to any member is raised on a ground other than ground, which affects his competency to act as an impartial judge, trial Judge Advocate is authorized to reject same summarily without reference to members of court martial - But if any member is objected to on ground, which affects his competency to act as an impartial judge, trial Judge Advocate has no discretion in matter and he must place matter before court, which must consider objection in accordance with procedure laid down in cls. (b) to (e) of s. 102 - In instant case, it cannot be said that ground on which objection was taken was not one, which affected concerned member to act as an impartial judge - Question whether objection was sustainable or not, was a question which had to be decided by members of court martial in accordance with provisions of cls (b) to (e) of s. 102 - Trial Judge Advocate clearly over stepped limitations of his jurisdiction and decided a matter, which court alone, and not he, was empowered to decide - Appeal dismissed.
