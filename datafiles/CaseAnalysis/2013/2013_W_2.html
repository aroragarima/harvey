Woman's Education Trust and another v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 747; (2013) 8 SCC 99; 2013 (4) AWC 4126; JT 2013 (7) SC 50; 2014 (123) RD 215; 2013(6) SCALE 684

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Women

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Sections 48, 6 and 5A-Release from acquisition - Discriminatory treatment-Appellants raised plea of discrimination by asserting that State Government released other parcels of land but their land not released despite fact that they had already constructed 'A' class building and were running school-Admittedly, 95 persons whose land released from acquisition raised construction in area earmarked for green belt-Appellants also raised similar construction-Only difference between constructions made by appellants and others was that of size-Difference in size of plot did not constitute valid ground for discrimination between those who raised construction on land which fell within green belt-High Court not justified in approving decision of competent authority not to release appellants land ignoring that other similar parcels of land on which constructions had been made had not been acquired-Appellants subjects to discriminatory treatment-High Court committed serious error by negating appellants' plea of discrimination-No compliance with mandate of Section 5A-Impugned acquisition ultra vires provisions contained in Section 6-Impugned order of High Court set aside-Matters remitted back to High Court for fresh disposals of writ petitions filed by appellants-Parties free to urge all legally permissible contentions in support of their respective cause.
