Dalbir Kaur and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 172; (1976) 4 SCC 158; (1976) SCC (Cr) 527; AIR 1977 SC 472; 1977 CRLJ 273; 1976 CrLR(SC) 417; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Evidence - Constitution of India, 1950 - Art. 136 - Indian Penal Code - ss. 34 and 302 - Whether the Supreme Court can in a criminal appeal by special leave reappraise the evidence and examine the question of credibility of witnesses where the subordinate Courts have held that the prosecution case against the appellants has been proved - Held, Both Subordinate Courts have held that prosecution case has been proved beyond reasonable doubt - Apex Court does not exercise its special jurisdiction as the accused had opportunity to make similar application before Subordinate Courts and they failed to avail the same - Conclusions of fact dependent on the credibility of witnesses and have been believed by the trial Court which had the advantage of seeing them and hearing their evidence cannot be allowed to be challenged except in exceptional circumstances - Appeal disposed accordingly.
