Laxman Naskar v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 137; (2000) SCC (Cr) 509; (2000) 2 SCC 595; AIR 2000 SC 986; 2000 (1) CCR 207; 2000 (1) Crimes 215; 2000 CRLJ 1471; 2000 (1) JCC 118; JT 2000 (2) SC 48; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2000(1) SCALE 530; 2000 (2) SCJ 158; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 796; 2000 (2) SLT 148; 2000 (1) Supreme 451

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Life Convict, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006), West Bengal Rules

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 433 A - West Bengal Rules (Relating to Premature Release of Life Convicts), r. 591(4)(29) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 161 - Premature release - Life convicts completing their 20 years of detention - Rejection of prayer for premature release by Govt. - On basis of objections by police - Consideration of - Held, their loosing potentiality in committing crime - Socio economic conditions of convict's family - Must be taken into account - There is no fruitful purpose in confining them any more.
