Vijay Cotton and Oil Mills Limited v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 337; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 386; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 60

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5 A, 6 and 18 - Whether Govt. can take inconsistent positions at successive stages of same litigation to detriment of its opponent? - Procedure of s. 5 A had been waived by appellant and that notification D/- 01-02-1955 could be treated as a composite one u/ss 4(1) and 6(1) - District Judge could therefore lawfully award market value of land that day - Relying on concession made by Govt., appellant acted to its detriment - It did not challenge acquisition and took no steps to recover land - Govt. has been in adverse possession of land for more than 12 years and gained an advantage which it could not otherwise obtain - A party litigant cannot be permitted to take up inconsistent positions in court to detriment of his opponents - Held, in these circumstances Govt. cannot be permitted to resile from election which it deliberately made and to say that appellant is not entitled to market value of land on 01-02-1955 - Matter is remanded to High Court for disposal on merits - Appeal allowed.
