State of Madhya Pradesh v Messrs Saith and Skelton Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 313; (1972) 1 SCC 702; AIR 1972 SC 1507; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 2(c), 14(2), 17 and 21 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 61(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 178 - (A) Applicability of Limitation Act - Whether plea of limitation can be raised with respect to the suo motu filing of award by arbitrator? - Held, as arbitrator in this case made his award on August 24, 1971, and filed same next day, question of limitation, if any, does not at all arise - Further held, that arbitrator can suo moto file an award - Question whether period of limitation will apply when arbitrator files his award suo motu left open - (B) Definition of word court in s. 2(c) of Arbitration Act - Held, in absence of any other court having been invested with such jurisdiction by order, only conclusion that is possible is that such a request must be made only to court which passed orders concerning arbitration, namely, Supreme Court - Expression court occurring in s. 14(2) of Act will have to be understood in context in which it occurs - So understood, it follows that Supreme Court is court u/s. 14(2) where arbitrator award could be validly filed - Held, Supreme Court retain complete control over arbitral proceedings - (C) Competency of Trial Court to make order of reference - Held, it is competent to do so - Expression 'Court' occurring in s. 21 includes also Appellate Court, proceedings before which are a continuance of suit - Further held that word 'suit' in s. 21 includes also appellant proceedings - Expression Court occurring in s. 21 includes also Appellate Court, proceedings before which are a continuance of suit - (D) Whether interest prior to institution of suit can be awarded? - Held, if there is any provision of law to justify so, award of such interest is valid - Arbitrator has made interest payable from 7-06-1958, on ground that it was date on which final inspection took place and when amount become payable to respondent - U/s. 61(2), in absence of a contract to contrary, seller is eligible to be awarded interest on amount of price for goods sold - Held, it is valid - (E) Exorbitant rate of interest - Held, u/s. 61 of sales of goods act interest calculated is at 12% - Arbitrator awarding interest at 9% is not exhorbitant - Appeal dismissed.
