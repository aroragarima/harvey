Govind Singh v Harchand Kaur

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 986; (2011) 2 SCC 621; JT 2010 (12) SC 613; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 179; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 262; 2010(12) SCALE 287

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Registration of Electors Rules, 1960

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83, 116A and 123(1)(A) - Corrupt practices of bribery - Election petition - Punjab legislative assembly election - Election of appellant (returned candidate) was challenged by the respondent (defeated candidate) and although she had secured third position in the polling, she challenged the election of the appellant alleging corrupt practice against him within the meaning of s. 123(1)(A) of the Act - It was alleged that the appellant with the active help of his supporters indulged in corrupt practice and offered bribery in the form of gift and promise to those who voted in his favour - HC held that respondent established that the appellant had committed corrupt practices and set aside the election of appellant - Hence, present appeal - (A) Maintainability of Election petition - Non-disclosure of material facts and material particulars - Whether election petition was fit to be rejected for want of material facts and material particulars? - Held, if the election petition fails to disclose any cause of action and there is non-compliance of the mandatory requirements of s. 83 of the Act - In the present case, respondent categorically stated the date, time and place of occurrence of the alleged corrupt practice at the instance of the appellant and also gave out the names of the witnesses in support of the election petition filed by respondent - Hence, contention that the Election petition was fit to be rejected for lack of material facts and material particulars not accepted - (B) Whether disbursement of money for old age pension etc. between the day of nomination and polling day by the returned candidate or by his consent by other persons through department of Social Security Women and Child Development, of which he was a Minister amounts to a corrupt practice u/s. 100(1)(b) of the Act? - Held, where the appellant in discharge of his official duty distributed, sanctioned, approved or permitted the grant of old age/widow/handicapped pension prior to the filing of his nomination, it cannot be construed as indulgence in corrupt practice - Hence, alleged case that this was done even after he ceased to be a Minister and after he had filed his nomination for contesting election, could not be proved with unimpeachable evidence since there was no reason for the appellant to ensure compliance of formality of filling pension forms for distribution of cash as he had ceased to be a Minister on those dates - Judgment and order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
