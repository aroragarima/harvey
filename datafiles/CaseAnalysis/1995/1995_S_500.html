Swamy Prahaladdas v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1020; (1995) Supp3 SCC 438; (1995) SCC (Cr) 943

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, s. 306 - Instigation of deceased to commit suicide - Whether Session Court was in error in summoning appellant to face trial ? - Held, words are casual in nature which are often employed in heat of moment between quarrelling people - Nothing serious is expected to follow thereafter - Said act does not reflect requisite mens rea on assumption that these words would be carried out in all events - Besides deceased had plenty of time to weigh pros and cons of act by which he ultimately ended his life - It cannot be said that suicide by deceased was direct result of words uttered by appellant - Appeal allowed.
