Pakalapati Narayana Gajapathi Raju and Others v Bonapalli Peda Appadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 541; (1975) 4 SCC 477; (1975) SCC (Cr) 543; AIR 1975 SC 1854; 1975 CRLJ 1646; 1975 UJ 580

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 439(1) and 439(4) - Whether, on revision application filed by a private party, High Court was justified in ordering a re-trial of appellants in exercise of its revisional powers u/s. 439, CrPC, 1973? - Provisions of s. 439(1), CrPC, 1973 are made expressly subject to sub-s. (4) of s. 439 under which nothing contained in section shall be deemed to authorise a HC to convert a finding of acquittal into one of conviction; revisional jurisdiction cannot be invoked merely because lower court has not appreciated evidence properly - Held, in present case, Sessions Court held that PWs 5, 7, 8 and 12, on their own showing, had not seen occurrence and therefore their evidence could not assist prosecution in proving charges against accused and though it has referred to contents of FIR at more than one place in judgment, references are directed mainly towards pointing out inconsistencies in prosecution case, therefore, interference with order of acquittal passed by Trial Court was not called for in exercise of revisional jurisdiction of HC - Appeal allowed.
