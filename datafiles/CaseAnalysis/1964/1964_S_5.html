S. S. Gadgil v Lal and Company

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 248; AIR 1965 SC 171; [1964] 53 ITR 231; 1964 (2) SCJ 499; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 72

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Finance Act, 1956, s. 34(1) (b) (iii) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Ss. 34, 43, 34(1) Cl.(Iii)Prov. 3, 5 - Right to Assess - Time lapsed - Depreciation Allowance - Agent of Non-resident Parties - On examination of respondent's/assessor's books of account, appellant/Income Tax Officer issued a notice as to why respondent/assesses should not be treated u/s. 43 of 1922, Act as an agent in respect of non-resident parties - Appellant/Officer hold that transactions disclosed from books of account of respondent clearly showed that he had regular business connection with non-resident parties and s. 43 of 1922 Act was clearly applicable to him and treated him as an agent of non-resident parties and accordingly assessed income, estimating income of parties residing outside taxable territories - Respondent filed a petition u/art. 226 of Constitution before HC praying for mandamus or prohibition, restraining and prohibiting appellant from taking any steps or proceedings by way of recovery, pursuant to orders of assessment - HC found said notice as invalid, and exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 34 of 1922 Act was not maintainable - Whether appellant/Income Tax Officer could issue a notice of assessment to an agent of a non-resident party under amended provision, before amended Act came into force expired - Held, Court viewed that, even when right to assess or re-assess had lapsed on account of expiry of ion of limitation prescribed under earlier statute, appellant/Income Tax Officer could exercise his powers to assess or re-assess under amending statute which gives an extended ion of limitation, could not be accepted - U/s. 18 of 1956 Act authority was conferred upon Income Tax Officer to assess a son as an agent of a foreign party u/s. 43 of 1922 Act, within two years from end of year of assessment - But authority of Income Tax Officer under said Act before it was amended by Act of 1956 had already come to an end, amending provision would not assist him to commence a proceeding - Legislature had given to s. 18 of 1956 Act, 1956 only a limited retrospective effect - Appeal dismissed.
