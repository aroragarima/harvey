Municipal Council, Sujanpur v Surinder Kumar

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 202; (2006) 5 SCC 173; 2006 (7) AWC 6777; 2006 (3) JhrCR (SC)106; JT 2006 (11) SC 226; 2006(5) SCALE 505; 2006 (5) SLR 39; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 914

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Punjab Municipal Act

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(c), 2(S), 25F - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 106, 11A, 41 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16 - Punjab Municipal Act - Labour Court held that respondent completed 240 days of work within a period of twelve months preceding his termination - Respondent is directed to be reinstated in service with full back wages and allied benefits from date of termination - Writ dismissed - Appeal against - Held, it is a trite law that only because some documents have not been produced by management, an adverse inference would be drawn against management - Labour Court and HC proceeded wrongly on premise that burden of proof to establish non-completion of 240 days of work within a period of twelve months preceding termination, was on management - Burden was on workman - Grant of monetary compensation would sub-serve interests of justice - Appeal allowed.
