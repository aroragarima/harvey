Assistant Executive Engineer, Karnataka v Shivalinga

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20557; (2002) 10 SCC 167; (2003) SCC (L&S) 87; 2002 (1) AWC 394; 2002 (92) FLR 601; JT 2001 (10) SC 428; 2002 (1) LLJ 457; 2002 (2) LLN 19; 2002 (1) SLT 18

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether decision of labour court to reject case due to a delay of more than years is correct? - In cases where there is a serious dispute or doubt in such relationship and records of employer become relevant, long delay would come in way of maintenance of same - In such circumstances, to make them available to a labour court or industrial tribunal to adjudicate dispute appropriately will be impossible - A situation of that nature would render claim to have become stale - Held, High Court is wrong in having interfered with award made by tribunal - Order made by HC in writ proceedings shall stand set aside and award made by labour court shall stand restored - Appeal allowed.
