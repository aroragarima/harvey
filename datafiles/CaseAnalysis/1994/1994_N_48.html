Nedurimillij. Reddy v Progressive Democratic Students’ Union

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1057; (1994) 6 SCC 506; JT 1994 (6) SC 170; 1994(4) SCALE 240; 1994 (5) SLR 550; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Unaided Private Medical And Dental Colleges

Summary: Education - Andhra Pradesh Education Act, 1982, ss. 2(12), 20, 21, 24, 26 to 29, 31, 45, 49, 50, 51, 60, 64, 69, 72, 79, 80, 83- Andhra Pradesh Unaided Private Medical And Dental Colleges - (A)Whether reliance placed by the State Government on the rules in question to justify the action of inviting applications for granting permission to establish educational institutions is valid? - Held, in the absence of the competent authority which is to be constituted under the Act and not under the Rules, no private educational institution could be established after the commencement of the Act. Hence the reliance placed by the State Government on the rules in question to justify the action of inviting applications for granting permission to establish educational institutions, is not well-merited - (B)Whether the absence of competent authority permission could have been given under the Act to establish any educational institution? - Held, in the absence of the constitution of the competent authority as defined under Section 2(12) of the Act no permission could have been given under the Act to establish any educational institution - Appeal dismissed
