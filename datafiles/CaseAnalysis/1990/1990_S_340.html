Dabur India Limited and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Saikia, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 602; (1990) 4 SCC 113; AIR 1990 SC 1814; 1990 (29) ECR 417; 1990 (49) E.L.T. 3; JT 1990 (3) SC 109; 1990(2) SCALE 29; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Keywords: Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Constitution - Excise - Health & Drug - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, ss. 2(K), 3 and 4, rr. 11 and 12 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Excise duty - Alcohol in medicinal product - Whether in the process any ingredient was used containing alcohol in respect of the product which is medicinal in nature and as such would be dutiable under the 1955 Act? - Held, in view of the facts alleged and found by the District Excise Officer, Ghaziabad, it appears that the Homeodent was a medicinal and toilet preparation and liable to excise duty under the 1955 Act, relied on ,( Baidyanath Ayurved Bhawan (Pvt.) Ltd., Jhansi v. Excise Commr., U. P. ((1971) 1 SCC 41971 (1) SCC 41970 Indlaw SC 14 : (1971) 2 SCR 5901971 (2) SCR 5901970 Indlaw SC 14), held that in order to attract duty under the 1955 Act, all that is required is that required is that a medicinal preparation should contain alcohol - Alcohol may be part of the preparation either because it is directly added to the solution or it came to be included in it because one of the components of that preparation contained alcohol - Petitions and Appeals disposed of.
