Subhash Krishnan v State Of Goa

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 448; (2012) 8 SCC 365; AIR 2012 SC 3003; 2012 CRLJ 4369; 2012 (2) OLR 977; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 119; 2012 (4) RLW 2953; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 342, 504, 362 and 364 r/w. s. 34 - Kidnapping - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of wrongful confinement, intent to provoke breach of the peace and kidnapping - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court u/ss. 342, 504 and 364 r/w. s. 34 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellant was sustainable - Held, HC u/s. 362, IPC held that when by force or deceit if any person was compelled or induced to go from any place and such abduction took place in order to ultimately eliminate him, offence would be made out u/s. 364, IPC - Examining the conduct of the appellant along with the other accused in wrongfully restraining the deceased by inflicting severe injuries on the body of the deceased i.e. by causing as many as 36 injuries in which process the person lost his conscious where after he was shifted to different place, where it ultimately came to light that the person was killed by hanging, every description of the offence u/ss. 342 and 364 of IPC r/w. s. 34 of IPC was clearly made out - Therefore, SC did not find any fault in the said conclusion of the HC in having reversed the judgment of Trial Court for convicting the appellant for the offence under the said Sections - Appellant submitted about the impossibility of the eyewitnesses in having noted the participation of the appellant and the other accused in the crime was on the basis that according to PW-35 the distance between the place of occurrence and the point from which the eye witnesses stated to have seen the occurrence was more than 70 metres - In the first place, when the occurrence had taken place at 4.30 in the evening there would be no difficulty for anyone to have clear view of what was happening before them - Even in the vicinity of 70 metres when about 8 persons were assaulting the deceased with sword, knife and danda on the road, in full public gaze, it would have definitely caught the eye of everyone standing there at - Presence of the eye witnesses at the place of occurrence was not in dispute - Witnesses made it clear that they were seeing the occurrence from the shop called Marina stores - It was not as if they were not looking at the occurrence - According to the witnesses, as well as, the prosecution, the eye witnesses were viewing the occurrence from the entrance of Marina stores - Therefore, the version of the eye witnesses that they were able to see the specific part played by different accused and, in particular, the appellant who was using talwar in the absence of any malafide attributed to the witnesses, their version could not be rejected - SC therefore did not find any substance in the said submission of appellant - As regards the time factor, it could not be held that since the incident happened within 4-5 minutes, it was not possible for the witnesses to have noted the participation of the accused in the crime - It was relevant to note that according to PW 9, as many as 36 injuries were found in the body of the deceased which were caused by the blunt side of the talwar, knife as well as danda - In inflicting so many injuries, the time taken would have been sufficient enough for the witnesses to have made observation as to the role played by the accused in the crime - Therefore, on that score as well there was no scope for doubting the version of witnesses as regards the participation of the appellant in the crime - It was true that PW 23 in his evidence stated that he saw the appellant having suffered bleeding injury which was not proved - It was also true that it was A 4 who suffered the bleeding injuries on his forehead which was caused with the aid of knife - SC had concurred with the conclusion of the Courts below about the possibility of A 4 having suffered the injury with the aid of talwar which was widely used by the appellant and inasmuch as A 4 was also actively involved in the crime - Since the appellant used talwar extensively, there was every possibility of A 4 having suffered the injury - In the light of the overwhelming evidence of the other eye witnesses, the medical evidence and the forensic reports, the wrong statement of PW 23 could not be said to have caused any serious dent in the case of the prosecution - Therefore, on that score, SC did not find any scope to interfere with the judgment impugned - Appeal dismissed.
