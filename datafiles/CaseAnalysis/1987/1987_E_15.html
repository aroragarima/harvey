Supreme Court of India

22 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, K. Jagannatha Shetty, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28416; (1988) 1 SCC 418; AIR 1988 SC 1520; 1988 (2) ARBLR 356; JT 1987 (4) SC 8; 1987(2) SCALE 675; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - CPC, 1908 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Interest Act, 1839 - Interest Act, 1978 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 29 and 30 - Arbitration - (A) Proceedings - Interest Act, 1978 applicable to concerned cases - Award of interest prior to proceedings - Pendente lite interest, if can be claimed - (B) Determination of jurisdiction of court to interfere with interest awarded by the arbitrator - Held, award not questionable, pendente lite interest could not be claimed by claimants, as arbitrator was not Court within meaning of s. 34 of CPC, 1908 and reference to arbitration was not made in course of suits - Court can interfere on ground of an error apparent on the record but not if there is slightest possibility of the entitlement of the claimant to interest - Appeal dismissed.
