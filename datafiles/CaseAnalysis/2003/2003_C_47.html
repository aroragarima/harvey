Vimal Suresh Kamble v Chaluverapinake Apal S.P. And Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 6; (2003) 3 SCC 175; (2003) SCC (Cr) 596; AIR 2003 SC 818; 2003 (1) CCR 140; 2003 (1) Crimes 378; 2003 CRLJ 910; 2003 (1) JCC 164; JT 2003 (1) SC 122; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 390; 2003(1) SCALE 69; 2003 (1) SLT 356; 2003 (1) Supreme 256

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: IPC - Ss. 342 and 376 - offences under - Held, in law the conviction of an accused on the basis of the testimony of the prosecutrix alone is permissible, but that is in a case where the evidence of the prosecutrix inspires confidence and appears to be natural and truthful. The evidence of the prosecutrix in this case is not of such quality, and there is no other evidence on record which may even lend some assurance, short of corroboration that she is making a truthful statement. - No reason to disagree with the finding of the High Court in appeal against acquittal.
