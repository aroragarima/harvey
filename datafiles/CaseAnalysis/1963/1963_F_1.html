Â Jayantilal Amrit Lal Shodhan v F.N. Rana and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 309; AIR 1964 SC 648; 1964 (5) G.L.R. 481; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5(A), 3(e), 7, 9 and 6(1) - Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, s. 87 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 256, 257, 258(1), 132(1), 133(1), 226 and 227 - Bombay Reorganisation Act, s. 87 - (A)Whether publishing, printing and selling of text books for use of students may be regarded as an executive function of State Govt? - Held, it cannot however be assumed that legislative functions are exclusively performed by Legislature, executive functions by executive and judicial functions by judiciary alone - By notification in question only 'the functions of Central Govt. under Land Acquisition Act 1 of 1894, in relation to acquisition of land for purpose of Union' have been entrusted to Commr. of Divisions - (B) Whether notification issued by President is law within meaning of s. 87 r/w s. 2(d) of Bombay Reorganisation Act? - Held, there is no doubt that investment of power or authority upon President is part of Constitution and has necessarily force of law - President is authorised by art. 258(1) to entrust functions with which Union Govt. is invested, provided functions are in relation to any matter to which executive power of Union extends - President is indisputably executive bead of Union, but it cannot be assumed on that account that exercise of power by him u/art. 258(1) cannot have effect of law within meaning of s. 87 of Bombay Reorganisation Act - (C) Whether notification was law or not? - Held, s. 139 of Bombay Prohibition Act, No. 25 of 1949, which gave power to Govt. by general or special order to exempt any intoxicant or class of intoxicants from operation of any of' provisions of that Act - Such an order would clearly have force of law being subordinate legislation and that was what was held in that case - Appeal dismissed.
