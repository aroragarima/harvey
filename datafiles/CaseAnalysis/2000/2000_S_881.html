Pradyut Bordoloi v Swapan Roy

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2724; (2001) 2 SCC 19; AIR 2001 SC 296; 2001 (44) ALR 215; JT 2001 (1) SC 136; 2000(8) SCALE 297; 2001 (1) SCJ 414; 2000 (8) SLR 9; 2001 (1) SLT 30; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 525; 2000 (8) Supreme 506; 2001 (1) UC 228

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Assam Legislative Members (Removal and Disqualifications) Act, 1950.

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 191 - Assam Legislative Members (Removal and Disqualifications) Act, 1950 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 10, 116A, 98(b), 80 and 81 - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 617 - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973, s. 3 - Complaint against candidature of respondent filed submitting that respondent was holding an office of profit under Govt. of India within meaning of art. 191(1)(a) of Constitution and was a managing agent, manager or Secy. of any company or corporation in capital of which Govt. of India has not less than 25% shares - (A) Whether respondent was holding an office of profit under Govt. of India on date of his nomination? - Govt. of India does not exercise any control on appointment, removal, service conditions and functioning of respondent - Respondent does hold an 'office' and there is 'profit' attached with office but such office of profit is not 'under Govt. of India', being a clerk in Coal India Ltd. does not and cannot bring any influence or pressure in his independent functioning as a member of Legislative Assembly - (B) Whether respondent was disqualified being a manager of any company in capital of which Govt. of India has not less than 25% shares? - Respondent was merely a Clerk Grade I - In absence of his senior officer on account of leave or absence, he exercised some supervisory function over his subordinates but this would not make him a manager of Company - Held, respondent's nomination was improperly rejected, appellant's election was liable to be declared void without proof of result of election, insofar as it concerns returned candidate, having been

materially affected - Order of High Court is affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
