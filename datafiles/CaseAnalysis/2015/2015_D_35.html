Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Nazul Rules, 1981

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 72, 73, and 74 - Compensation for breach of contract u/s. 74 of the Act - Principles to be followed.

Held, where a sum is named in a contract as a liquidated amount payable by way of damages, the party complaining of a breach can receive as reasonable compensation such liquidated amount only if it is a genuine pre-estimate of damages fixed by both parties and found to be such by the Court. In other cases, where a sum is named in a contract as a liquidated amount payable by way of damages, only reasonable compensation can be awarded not exceeding the amount so stated. Similarly, in cases where the amount fixed is in the nature of penalty, only reasonable compensation can be awarded not exceeding the penalty so stated. In both cases, the liquidated amount or penalty is the upper limit beyond which the Court cannot grant reasonable compensation - Appeal allowed.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 72, 73, and 74 - Breach of contract - Fixation of reasonable compensation.

Held, reasonable compensation will be fixed on well known principles that are applicable to the law of contract, which are to be found inter alia in s. 73 of the Act. Since s. 74 of the Act awards reasonable compensation for damage or loss caused by a breach of contract, damage or loss caused is a sine qua non for the applicability of the Section. The Section applies whether a person is a plaintiff or a defendant in a suit. The sum spoken of may already be paid or be payable in future. Further, the expression 'whether or not actual damage or loss is proved to have been caused thereby' in s. 74 of the Act means that where it is possible to prove actual damage or loss, such proof is not dispensed with. It is only in cases where damage or loss is difficult or impossible to prove that the liquidated amount named in the contract, if a genuine pre-estimate of damage or loss, can be awarded - Appeal allowed.

(C) Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 72, 73, and 74 - Breach of contract - Applicability of s. 74 of the Act.

Held, s. 74 of the Act will apply to cases of forfeiture of earnest money under a contract. Where, however, forfeiture takes place under the terms and conditions of a public auction before agreement is reached, s. 74 of the Act would have no application - Appeal allowed.
