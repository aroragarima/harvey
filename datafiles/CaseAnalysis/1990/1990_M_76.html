Elizabeth Antony v Michel Charles John Chown Lengera

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 882; (1990) 3 SCC 333; AIR 1990 SC 1576; 1990 (2) CCC 133; JT 1990 (2) SC 183; 1990(1) SCALE 713; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 486; 1990 (1) UJ 686

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Succession Act, 1925, ss. 227, 263, 284 and 285 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Will - Letters of administration - Caveat against - Based on Will and sale deed executed by daughter of testatrix and on caveator's appointment as trustees - Trust found to be not in existence - Neither Will nor sale deed produced before Court - Caveatable interest, not established - Rejection of caveat - (A) Validity - (B) Subsequent events - Consideration - Succession proceedings - Caveat - Rejected, as will not prduced - Appeal against - Will in favour of caveator probated subsequently - (C) Probate and letters of administration - Revocation - Whether party against whom finding has been given that he has no caveatable interest can apply - Held, (A) Proper - (B)Subsequent grant of probate not considered by Court as it was not clear whether citations were issued to all interested persons and probate was granted without notice to party claiming grant of letters of administration - (C) Can also invoke s. 263 and findings regarding caveatable interest have a limited effect - Petition dismissed.
