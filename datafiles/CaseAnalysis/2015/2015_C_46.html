State of Uttar Pradesh v Charan Singh

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 235; (2015) 8 SCC 150; 2015 (5) AWC 5349; 2015 (145) FLR 679; JT 2015 (3) SC 344; 2015 (2) LLJ 367; 2015(4) SCALE 294; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 806; 2015 (3) SLR 694

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 33 - Back wages - Entitlement - Respondent worker who was appointed as a temporary Tube-well Operator by appellant company was terminated stating thereby that he was a temporary employee - Tribunal held that termination was illegal and directed that respondent be reinstated on any post equivalent to the post of Tube-well Operator - In pursuance of the Award, appellant offered a letter of appointment to the respondent - Appellant contended that as respondent did not respond to several letters of the appellant which was calling him back for work, he is not entitled to any wages on the principle 'no work no pay' - HC, however, rejected the contention of the appellant and held that State was liable to pay the entire amount due to the workman - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, State is liable to pay the entire amount due to the workman for the period 24-2-1997 to 31-1-2005, as the State has kept the workman out of job for many years arbitrarily and unreasonably despite the Award of reinstatement of the respondent on an equivalent post which was passed by the Industrial Tribunal. Thus, not reporting for the duty of fisherman offered to him by the appellant cannot be said to be unjustified on the part of the respondent. Denial of back wages to the respondent in the absence of evidence of gainful employment of the respondent during the relevant period, amounts to arbitrary exercise of power by the Tribunal for no fault of the respondent and the same is contrary to law as laid down by this Court in a catena of cases. Hence, it is a fit case for this Court to exercise its power under O. 41 r.33 of the CPC, to award back wages to the respondent. The State is liable to pay 50% of the back wages to the respondent from the date of his termination order dt. 22-8-1975 till the date of the Award passed by the Industrial Tribunal, i.e. 24-2-1997. Appeal dismissed.
