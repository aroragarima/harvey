Khambalia Municilpality & Anr. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 467; AIR 1967 SC 1048; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Nagar Panchayats, Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1963, Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, s. 179 - Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, ss. 9(1), (2), 321, 298, 299, 300, 301 and 3 - Nagar Panchayats Act, 1961, s. 9(1) - (A) Whether revenue villages or hamlets or other administrative units or parts thereof can be conveniently grouped so as to form a gram or nagar? - Held, local area of a municipal borough is not' any local area, comprising a revenue village, or a group of revenue villages or hamlets forming part of a revenue village, or such other administrative unit or part thereof within meaning of s. 9 of Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961 - Consequently local area of municipal borough for which Khambalia municipality was constituted could not be declared to be a nagar - (B) Whether notification u/s. 9(1) D/- 14-06-1965, was made malafide? - Held, it was after municipality indicated its unwillingness to include vadi areas within its limits that Development Commr. issued a notification u/s. 9(1) - State Govt. having failed to impose its opinion regarding inclusion of vadi areas upon municipality, adopted device of declaration u/s. 9(1) - (C) Whether Govt. is empowered to declare a local area to be a gram or nagar? - Held, yes - A declaration u/s. 9(1) that a local area shall be a nagar or a gram is a legislative function - As stated on several occasions by Supreme Court an essential legislative function consists in determination of legislative policy and its formulation as a binding rule of conduct - Act provides a three-tier Organisation consisting of a Gram or Nagar Panchayat, a Taluka Panchayat and a District Panchayat in each district - Govt. thus is empowered (1) to declare a local area to be a nagar or a gram depending upon its population - Appeal allowed.
