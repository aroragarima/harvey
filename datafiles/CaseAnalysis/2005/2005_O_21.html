M/s. Oswal Agro Furane Limited and another v Oswal Agro Furane Workers Union and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 94; (2005) 3 SCC 224; (2005) SCC (L&S) 381; AIR 2005 SC 1555; 2005 (5) AWC 4077; 2005 (1) CLR 816; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 10; JT 2005 (2) SC 260; 2005 (1) KLT 936; 2005 (1) LLJ 1117; 2005 (2) LLN 21; 2005(2) SCALE 134; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 94; 2005 (2) SLR 362; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 793

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 12(3), 18, 25-C, 25-N, 25-O - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Whether in a case of closure of an industrial undertaking, prior permission of appropriate Govt. is imperative or if a settlement arrived at by and between employer and workmen would prevail over statutory requirements as contained in ss. 25-N, 25-O? - Held, obtaining a prior permission from appropriate Govt. must be held to be imperative in character - Provisions being imperative in character would prevail over right of parties to arrive at a settlement - Appeal dismissed.
