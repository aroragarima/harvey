Peerappa Hanmantha Harijan (Dead) by Lrs. and others v State of Karnataka and another

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 543; (2015) 10 SCC 469; AIR 2015 SC 2908; JT 2015 (7) SC 535; 2015 (6) MLJ 216; 2015(8) SCALE 569; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Acquiring Authority, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board Regulations, 1969

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 32 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board Regulations, 1969 regn. 4,7,10(b), 10(c), 10(d) - Claim of beneficiary ogf land - Determination - Land in question belonging to appellant was acquired by State Govt. for establishment of industries - Further, compensation was awarded to appellant, however, same was enhanced after re-determination of market value of land - Further, appeal filed for enhancement of compensation was rejected by HC - Further, allottee on ground of lease agreement between State and itself contended that it is beneficiary or interested person entitled for hearing before determination of market value to award just and reasonable compensation in respect of acquired land of appellants before Court - Whether allottee Company is beneficiary in respect to land acquired in question.

Held, lease agreement executed between parties in respect of land of appellants, states that said agreement is executed by Development Board in favour of Company after allotment of land was made in favour of Company. SC held that from reading lease agreement along with provision s. 32(2) of Act and regn. 4, 7, 10 (b), (c) and (d) of Regulations, it is clear that Company is only lessee by way of allotment of land as same has been allotted by Development Board in its favour and has executed lease deed in its favour in respect of allotted land. Further, from notification issued for acquisition of land it can be said that land is acquired for industrial purpose by Development Board. Hence, beneficiary of acquired land is only Development Board but not Company. Hence, claim made by Company that it has got every right to participate in proceedings for determination and re-determination of market value of acquired land and award of compensation passed by Land Acquisition Officer or Deputy Commissioner or before Reference Court or Appellate Court is wholly untenable in law and is rejected. Further, impugned order passed by HC remanding matter to lower Court anallowing Company to participate in proceedings for re-determination of compensation for acquired land is wholly impermissible in law. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition of land - Enhancement of compensation - Justification - Whether appellant is entitled for enhancement of compensation.

Held, land of appellants along with other lands that was acquired vide notification in 1981 have been allotted in favour of Company for purpose of extracting mineral of limestone which is raw material used for purpose of manufacturing cement used for commercial purpose. Hence, land of appellants is acquired for non-agricultural potentiality and same is used for commercial purpose. Hence, determining deductions towards de-escalation at 5% per year for 7 years and 10% towards waiting and other incidental charges would justify re-determination of market value of land of appellants. There is no need to deduct developmental charges as has been done by Reference Court and Appellate Court as there is no development activities involved in respect of land involved in these appeals, as same is being used by Company for extraction of minerals from land, which are used as raw material for purpose of manufacturing cement and also for development of infrastructure of its factory. Hence, enhancement of compensation at Rs.1,92,000/- per acre as per calculation below would be just and reasonable. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Compensation paid to land owners should be reasonable and according to market prices for land acquired by authority.
