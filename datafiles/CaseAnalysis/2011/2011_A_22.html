Central Board of Secondary Education and anothe v Aditya Bandopadhyay and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 590; (2011) 8 SCC 497; 2011 (9) ADJ 7; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 38; 2011 (88) ALR 701; 2011 (6) AWC 5567; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 71; 2011 (4) ESC 600; JT 2011 (9) SC 212; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 1237; 2011 (2) OLR 746; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 914; 2011(8) SCALE 645; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 1028; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 967

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Revaluation, Freedom to Information Act, 2002, Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Board Rules, 1977

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 19(1)(a) - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 2(f), 2(h), 2(i), 2(j), 2(e), 3, 4(1)(b), 4(1)(c), 4(1), 4(2), 6, 8, 8(1)(e), 8(1), 8(1)(g), 8(3), 9, 10, 19(8), 22 - Revaluation - Fiduciary relationship - Sustainability -Respondent no.1 appeared for Secondary School Examination conducted by CBSE/appellant - When respondent no.1 got mark sheet he was disappointed with his marks - Respondent no.1 made an application for inspection and re-evaluation of his answer-books - Appellant rejected said request by letter - Aggrieved by same respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether examinee's right to information under the 2005 Act includes a right to inspect his evaluated answer books in a public examination or taking certified copies and whether such right was subject to any limitations or conditions. - Held, right of information was a facet of freedom of 'speech and expression' as contained in art. 19(1) (a) of Constitution and such a right was subject to any reasonable restriction in interest of security of State and subject to exemptions and exceptions - Further, examining bodies contended that evaluated answer-books were exempted from disclosure u/s. 8(1)(e) of the 2005 Act, as they were 'information' held in its fiduciary relationship - They fairly conceded that evaluated answer-books would not fall under any other exemptions in s. 8(1) - Every examinee would have right to access his evaluated answer-books, by either inspecting them or take certified copies unless evaluated answer-books are found to be exempted u/s. 8(1)(e) of the 2005 Act - Therefore, it could not be said that examining body was in a fiduciary relationship either with reference to examinee who participates in examination and whose answer-books were evaluated by examining body - Thus, that an examining body did not hold evaluated answer-books in fiduciary relationship not being information available to an examining body in its fiduciary relationship, exemption u/s. 8(1)(e) was not available to examining bodies with reference to evaluated answer-books - As no other exemption u/s. 8 was available in respect of evaluated answer books, examining bodies would have to permit inspection sought by examinees - Appeal disposed of.
