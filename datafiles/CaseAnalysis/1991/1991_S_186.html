State of Bihar Etc v P.P. Sharma, Ias and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1053; (1992) SCC (Cr) 192; (1992) Supp1 SCC 222; AIR 1991 SC 1260; 1991 (2) Crimes 113; 1991 CRLJ 1438; 1991 CRLR 334; JT 1991 (2) SC 147; 1991(1) SCALE 539; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 156, 173, 197 and 482 - Powers of High Court to quash criminal proceedings - HC could not have assumed jurisdiction and put an end to process of investigation and trial - Entertaining writ petitions against chargesheet amounts to pre-trial of criminal trial u/arts. 226 or 227 even before competent Magistrate takes cognizance of offence - Mere allegations cannot be basis for quashing proceedings - Appeals allowed.
