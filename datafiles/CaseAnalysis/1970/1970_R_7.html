Magraj Patodia v R. K. Birla and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 453; (1970) 2 SCC 888; AIR 1971 SC 1295; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 118; 1970 UJ 905

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 116A, 123 and 123(6) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Election - (1) Corrupt practice - Finding of fact - Supreme Court, interference sought from - (2) Corrupt practice - Proof - (3) Incurring of expenditure beyond prescribed limit- Proof - (4) Applicability of s.77 - Expenses incurred by political party - (5) Expenditure suppressed - (6) No provision in law compelling appellate Court to award costs to successful party election appeal - No proof but merely preponderance of probability of corrupt practice having been committed by respondent - (B) Evidence - Document - Admissibility - Document procured by illegal means - (A) Held, SC will not interfere - Evidence must be cogent and conclusive - Authority or consent of candidate or his election agent to be proved - Held, s. 77 not applicable - Court should try to estimate expenditure - Held case fit for not awarding costs to respondent returned candidate - Appeal dismissed but parties directed to bear their own costs in the appeal - (B) No bar to its admissibility - Appeal Dismissed.
