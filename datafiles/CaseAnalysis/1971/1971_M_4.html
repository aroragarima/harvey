M. N. Sankaranarayanan Nair v P. V. Balakrishnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 786; (1972) SCC (Cr) 55; (1972) 1 SCC 318; AIR 1972 SC 496; 1972 CRLJ 301; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 599; 1972 UJ 284

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Withdrawal of Prosecution - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 494 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 467 and 478 and 420 r/w 109 - Respondent 1 and respondent 2 were committed to stand trial before Asst. Sessions Judge - Public Prosecutor withdraw prosecution by filing a Memo u/s. 494 of CrPC - Permission was accorded by Asst. Sessions Judge - Challenged - Held, wide and general powers which are conferred u/s. 494 on Public Prosecutor to withdraw from prosecution though they are subject to permission of court have to be exercised by him in relation to facts and circumstances of that case in furtherance of, rather than as a hindrance to object of law and justified on material in case which substantiate grounds alleged, not necessarily from those gathered by judicial method but on other materials which may not be strictly on legal or admissible evidence - Court also while considering request to grant permission under said section should not do so as a necessary formality - Further held, none of grounds given for withdrawing prosecution would justify withdrawal from prosecution - There was a prima facie case against accused and accordingly framed charges - Appeal allowed.
