Shri Ramnik Vallabhdas Madhvani and Others v Taraben Pravinlal Madhvani

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 972; (2004) 1 SCC 497; AIR 2004 SC 1084; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 217; JT 2004 (6) SC 144; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 194; 2003(9) SCALE 412; 2003 (1) SCW 6839; 2003 (7) SLT 639; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 230; 2003 (8) Supreme 208

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh High Court (Original Side) Rule

Summary: Partnership firm - Partnership did not have any proprietory interest in the estate - Determination of quantum of mesne profits payable to the respondent-plaintiff by the appellants; (2) rate of interest to be awarded on the decretal amount; (3) adjustment of admitted liability of a partner towards the partnership firm - Held, the total amount of mesne profits found due by the High Court on the basis of Commissioner's report comes to Rs. 38,42,920/-. This amount is the total of mesne profits calculated on yearly basis. Interest cannot be allowed on the whole amount from the beginning. Interest had to be worked out on amounts falling due towards mesne profits on yearly basis i.e. on the amount of mesne profits which could be taken to be due to the plaintiff at the end of each successive year; (2) the appellant will be liable to pay interest under both the heads at the rate of 6% per annum; (3) there is no need to interfere with the finding arrived at by the High Court on these matters.
