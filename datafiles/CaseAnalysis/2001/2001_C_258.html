Bharti Telecom Limited v Commissioner of Customs

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 416; (2001) 10 SCC 18; AIR 2002 SC 74; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 327; JT 2001 (9) SC 501; 2001 (47) RLT 257; 2001(8) SCALE 134; 2001 (7) SLT 732; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 205; 2001 (8) Supreme 240

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 56A and 57A - Notification No. 203/92, D/-19-05-1992 - Whether appellant entitled to benefit of duty free import under notification No. 203/92, D/-19-05-1992? - Appellant did not fulfill conditions of Amnesty Scheme - Interest for period between date of export, and date of reversal was deposited after date prescribed date - Held, there is no provision for relaxation or extension of time to deposit amount of interest - Such schemes or exemption notifications have to be strictly construed - In a taxing statute, there is no room for any intendant and regard must be had to clear meaning of words and that matter should be governed only by language of notification - Provision in notification D/-10-01-1997 for deposit of interest by a specified date has to be interpreted strictly in manner stated in notification and on no other basis - Further held, appellant not entitled to benefit of said notification - Appeal dismissed.
