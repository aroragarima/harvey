Gottumukkala Appala Narasimha Raju and Others v National Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 260; (2007) 13 SCC 446; 2007 (2) ACC 880; AIR 2007 SC 2907; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 36; 2007 (4) G.L.R. 3380; 2007 (2) JCC 1064; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 112; JT 2007 (3) SC 556; 2007 (2) LLJ 531; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1001; 2007 (146) PLR 490; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 294; 2007(3) SCALE 364; 2007 (5) SCJ 950; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 939

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Liability Of The Insured, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Person In Charge, Non Obstante Clause, Additional Amount

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Interpretation of s. 167 - Order that no Award could be passed against the insurer in the proceeding under the provisions of 1923 Act - Held, if the deceased, with all intent and purport, was the owner of the tractor, the claim petition under the 1988 Act might not have been maintainable - A petition under 1923 Act certainly would not lie - S. 19(1) of the 1923 Act specifically provides that any question in regard to the liability of a person who is required to indemnify the employer must be determined in the proceeding under the said Act and not by way of a separate suit - Appeal dismissed.
