Firm Ashok Traders and Another v Gurumukh Das Saluja and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 13; (2004) 3 SCC 155; AIR 2004 SC 1433; 2004 (55) ALR 5; 2004 (1) ARBLR 141; 2004 (2) AWC 949; 2004 (2) CompLJ 419; JT 2004 (2) SC 352; 2004 (3) MahLJ 592; 2004 (3) MPLJ 266; 2004 (137) PLR 526; 2004 (1) RAJ 270; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 725; 2004(1) SCALE 297; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 404; 2004 (1) Supreme 754

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Corporate - Arbitration & ADR - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 40 rr. 1, 2, 3, 4 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, ss. 36, 69, 69(3) - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 17, 20 - Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2(1)(h), 37(1)(a), 9, 9(ii)(d) - Partnership firm - Dispute - Violation of rights by other partners - Appointment of receiver - Effect of bar created by s 69(3), Partnership Act on maintainability of an application u/s. 9 of 1996 Act - Held, bar enacted by s 69, Partnership Act does not affect maintainability of an application u/s. 9 of A and C Act - Further held, applicant directed u/s. 9, to take steps for appointment of arbitrator/s, without any further loss of time - Directions issued.
