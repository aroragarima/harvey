P. M. Abubakar v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 819; (2017) 1 SCC 302; AIR 2016 SC 5602; 2017 (120) ALR 281; 2017 (1) BC 3; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 123; JT 2016 (11) SC 151; 2016 (8) MLJ 674; 2017 (134) RD 614; 2016(12) SCALE 85

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Auction, CONTRACT & COMMERCIAL, Limitation Act, 1963, Communication, Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act, 1959, Karnataka Co-operative Societies Rules, 1960, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Banking & Finance - Karnataka Co-operative Societies Act, 1959, ss. 89A, 106 - Karnataka Co-operative Societies Rules, 1960, r. 38 - Under valuation of property - Auction sale - As debtor committed default in repayment of loan to Bank, recovery proceedings were initiated by bank and later, Joint Registrar passed award against debtor for recovery of Rs.13,65,899 but as debtor failed to repay, execution petition was filed - Later, notice of auction was issued for auction sale - While so, after prolonged litigation, finally, auction sale was held and appellant-auction purchaser was highest bidder for Rs.51,50,000 - However, debtor filed objections for setting aside sale but same was rejected by ARCS - Along with objections before ARCS, debtor also filed writ petition before HC challenging sale in favour of appellant-auction purchaser and the same was dismissed by HC - While so, Sales Officer, recommended confirmation of sale in favour of appellant-auction purchaser and basing on same, ARCS passed order confirming sale in favour of appellant-auction purchaser - Aggrieved, debtor filed appeal before Deputy Registrar wherein it was held that it was case of under valuation of property and as such, confirmation of sale was set aside - Aggrieved, both appellant-auction purchaser and bank filed writ petitions before HC and Single Judge of HC opined that reason recorded by Deputy Registrar was just and proper and accordingly, dismissed petitions by common judgment - On appeal, DB of HC vide common judgment passed in both appeals, opined that as property was under valued at time of auction sale, no fault is found with discretion exercised to set aside sale under proviso to r.38(6)(a) and disposed of appeals - Hence, instant cross appeals by appellant-auction purchaser assailing judgment dt.24-8-2011 passed by DB of HC and also orders passed on clarification applications and debtor filed appeal challenging judgment of DB dt.24-8-2011 - Whether impugned judgment of DB of HC is sustainable.

Held, remedy of appeal before Deputy Registrar could be availed only in terms of s.106 of the Act, against an order passed by Authority (Registrar) in exercise of powers ascribable to the provisions referred to therein. The order of confirmation of sale is ascribable to s.89A of the Act read with r.38 of the Rules. No remedy of appeal against that decision is provided. S.106 of the Act does not provide for an appeal against the order confirming an auction sale, passed u/s.89A read with r.38. S.89A of the Act read with r.38 of the Rules provides for a special dispensation. Hence, order passed by Deputy Registrar on the appeal preferred by debtor in appeal no.7/2008-2009 is without jurisdiction. However, Single judge and DB of HC have completely glossed over such crucial aspect. Hence, HC committed manifest error in dismissing the writ petitions filed by appellant-auction purchaser challenging the decision of Deputy Registrar dt.18-7-2009. The HC ought to have allowed the writ petition as the Deputy Registrar had no jurisdiction to entertain appeal against the order of confirmation of sale issued under s.89A read with r.38 of the Rules; and also because, debtor failed to pay the awarded amount in spite of repeated opportunities given to him from time to time. Further, the debtor could not succeed in Writ Petition filed by auction purchaser and Bank against decision of Deputy Registrar and get higher or further relief in such proceedings. Hence, DB having finally disposed of writ appeal ought not to have entertained the application preferred by debtor in the guise of clarification and pass any order thereon which would enure to the benefit of debtor who is in default, having become functus officio. Hence, appeals preferred by auction purchaser are allowed. Further orders of Deputy Registrar and HC confirming order of Deputy Registrar of setting aside the sale of the subject mortgage property in favour of auction purchaser, are set aside. Furthermore, appeals of debtor are dismissed and Appropriate Authority is to proceed to disburse the amount already deposited by debtor and including amount of sale proceeds, in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.
