Bhopal Gas Peedith Mahila Udyog Sangathan and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 227; (2012) 8 SCC 326; AIR 2012 SC 3081; 2012 (6) AWC 6177; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 201; 2012(7) SCALE 293; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 947

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Constitution - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950, art.21 - Medical assistance - Relief and Rehabilitation - Petitioner filed PIL for free and proper medical assistance from respondents for victims of Bhopal Gas Disaster - Petitioner prayed that Indian Council of Medical Research (ICMR) be directed to resume and conduct research studies to make public reports published to provide basic ground for issuance of appropriate directions by SC - SC passed various directions from filing of instant PIL and directed respondents to provide appropriate medical treatment to victims - Whether instant PIL should be kept pending before SC or should be transferred to appropriate forum, including HC, for more effective and purposeful management of institutions and to ensure to serve satisfactorily the purpose of public service and benefit for which they were constituted - Held, ICMR undertook certain research works after Bhopal Disaster and appropriate steps were taken, as claimed by State and Central Govt., to deal with medical problems of victims - However, it appeared and averred before SC that after 1994, ICMR allegedly took irrational decision to disband all Bhopal Gas Disaster related medical research - Abandoning of research work was seriously criticised in instant petition - Further, SC directed creation of Bhopal Memorial Hospital and Research Centre (BMHRC) and Bhopal Memorial Hospital Trust (BMHT) which was constituted for purposes of healthcare of affected victims - SC ordered the constitution of 2 expert committees being Monitoring Committee and Advisory Committee' - Advisory Committee was submitting its reports from time to time and it was assured by State Govt. that Committee would be provided with all facilities and technical inputs - Paramount functions of Monitoring Committee were to monitor suitability, availability and maintenance of medical equipment's, deployment of adequate and competent medical personnel, more specifically treatment offered at hospitals and functioning of these hospitals run by e Govt. for victims, purchase and availability of medicines to affected persons - Advisory Committee, determined its own rules of procedure, to examine treatment practices currently followed by medical personnel in hospitals run by Govt. for victims in relation to various ailments suffered - Further, Advisory Committee was to recommend and advice on appropriate line of treatment to be offered to victims - there was no co-ordination between various functionaries dealt with tragedy and, views of Advisory Committee were not given due weightage by implementing agencies, thereby added suffering and agony of affected parties - Further, it would be appropriate that day-to-day directions were passed by a jurisdictional HC - Therefore, it not only desirable but also in the interest of all concerned that this matter should henceforth be dealt with by HC of Madhya Pradesh - It was also necessary for SC to pass further directions to provide clarity and precision and to ensure effective implementation of various orders which should remain an integral part of wide scheme sought to be enforced for betterment of victims - UOI had passed resolution directing ICMR to establish permanent research centre at Bhopal which, already established in name of NIREH - It indicated intent of Govt. of India to provide and procure necessary machinery for research related works to further process to get much needed scientific manpower and research, which could contribute in research activities related to victims - suggestions made by various applicants, recommendations of expert bodies, issuance of certain specific directions were inevitably called for - These orders would ensure proper progress and implementation of e Relief and Rehabilitation programme for penurious gas victims as well and to ensure that research work was result-oriented and continued with exactitude - Directions should be in aid of various orders passed by SC from time to time in instant petition and not in derogation thereto - Further, it was directed complete computerization of medical information in Govt. as well as non-government hospital/clinics - Instant petition was transferred to HC of Madhya Pradesh - Order accordingly.
