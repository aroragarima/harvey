Unit Trust of India v Ravinder Kumar Shukla Etc

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 567; (2005) 7 SCC 428; AIR 2005 SC 3528; 2005 (61) ALR 803; 2005 (4) AWC 3451; 2005 (6) Bom.C.R. 805; 2006 (1) CCC 17; [2005] 127 Comp Cas 559; 2005 (4) CPJ(Sc) 10; 2005 (3) G.L.R. 2757; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 214; JT 2005 (12) SC 345; 2005 (4) KLT 303; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 171; 2005 (4) RLW 2319; 2005(7) SCALE 364; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 281

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: [A.] Consumer Protection - Unit Trust of India Act, 1963, s. 3 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Cheques issued towards maturity amount of units purchased and/or towards repurchase value and sent them to payee by registered post - Non-receipt of cheques - Deficiency in service - Consumer Forums held that post offices were agents of Appellant and loss has to be borne by Appellant - Whether loss is to be borne by unit holder payee and/or by Appellant? - Held, where there is no contract or request, either express or implied, post office would continue to act as agent of drawer - In that case loss is of drawer - No proof in any of these matters - All Forums have on facts held that there was an obligation to send amounts and that there was negligence - No reason to interfere on questions of facts - Appeal dismissed.
