K.M. Chinnappa Applicant In T.N. Godavarman v Union of Indian and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1386; (2002) 10 SCC 606; AIR 2003 SC 724; 2003 (2) CHN 55; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 105; JT 2002 (9) SC 200; 2002(8) SCALE 204; 2003 (1) SCW 23; 2002 (6) SLT 564; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 541; 2002 (7) Supreme 620

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Water Pollution, Environmental Damage, Environmental Legislation

Summary: Environment, Wildlife and Animal - Wildlife (Protection) Act, 1972 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Forest Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2(ii) - Forest Conservation Rules, 1981, r. 4 - Forest Conservation Rules, 1981 - rr.4, 5 and 6 - Mining activity - De-reservation of forests, sanctuaries and national parks was prohibited under the order - Therefore, exclusion of company's land in terms of the Notification u/s. 35(4) of the Act though same was being used for mining by the company was not in order, to that extent - It is proper to accept the time period fixed by the Forest Advisory Committee constituted u/s. 3 of the Conservation Act - Held, mining should be allowed till the end of 2005 by which time the weathered secondary ore available in the already broken area should be exhausted subject to fulfillment of the recommendations made by the Committee on ecological and others aspects - Application disposed of accordingly.
