Brathi Alias Sukhdev Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 88; (1991) SCC (Cr) 203; (1991) 1 SCC 519; AIR 1991 SC 318; 1991 CRLJ 402; JT 1991 (5) SC 217; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 503

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 386 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149 and 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Criminal appeal - Powers of appellate Court - More than one accused - Co-accused acquitted - No appeal against acquittal - Whether Appellate Court can still find acquittal to be unwarranted by reappraising evidence to determine constructive liability of accused - Interested witness - Murder case - Mechanical rejection of evidence on ground of it being interested - Whether warranted - Held, appellate Court can reappraise evidence to determine constructive liability of accused - Not warranted - Appeal dismissed.
