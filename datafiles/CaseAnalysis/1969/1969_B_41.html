Ram Dayal v Brijraj Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 136; (1969) 2 SCC 218; AIR 1970 SC 110; 1970 ALJ 92; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 2(1), 33(1), 36, 77(1), 90, 116A and 123(6) -

Representation of the People (Conduct of Elections) Rules, 1961, r. 2(2) - CPC , 1908, O.6, r. 17 - (A) Amendment of election petition - Application for, introduce new ground for setting aside election filed after bar of limitation prescribed u/s. 81(1) - (B) Meaning of 'Sign' u/s. 2(1) of 1951 Act - Person unable to write his name - (C) Requirement of signature of candidate and proposer on nomination paper in mandatory - Nomination paper bearing thumb impression of proposer but not attested as required by law filed on last date for receiving nomination paper - Defect is substantial within s. 36(4) and cannot be remedied at the time of scrutiny - Nomination has to be rejected - (D) Election petition by voter - High Court refusing to set aside election of successful candidate but recording an order of disqualification on account of his corrupt practice - Appeal by voter against order of High Court refusing to set aside election impleading him as respondent - (E) Corrupt practice - Return of expenses - If include only the expenditure - Held, amendment cannot be allowed - Should put his mark or thumb impression as prescribed by r. 2(2) of 1961 Rules - Defect is substantial within s. 36(4) and cannot be remedied at the time of scrutiny, nomination has to be rejected - Held that in absence of appeal by him against order passed against him, scope of appeal could not be expanded by permitting him to plead that he had not committed any act amounting to corrupt practice - Return of expenses must include only the expenditure incurred or authorised by candidate himself or his election agent - Express incurred by any other agent without anything more need not be included - Appeal Dismissed
