Wasim Khan v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1956 SC 400; 1956 ALJ 457; 1956 CRLJ 1790; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; 1956 SCJ 437; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 342 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - Death sentence confirmed by High Court - Circumstantial evidence - Last seen with - Appellant unable to explain circumstances and further found in unexplained possession of property of deceased - Charge framed against appellant for murder and robbery - Whether conviction of appellant for murder and robbery was justified ? - Held, appellant has been rightly convicted of offences of murder and robbery - Appeal dismissed.
