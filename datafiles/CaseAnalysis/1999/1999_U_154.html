Zunjarrao Bhikaji Nagarkar v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 97; (1999) 7 SCC 409; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1299; AIR 1999 SC 2881; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 843; 1999 (2) CLR 642; 1999 (84) ECR 29; 1999 (112) E.L.T. 772; 1999 (3) ESC 2169; JT 1999 (5) SC 366; 1999 (32) RLT 125; 2000 (1) SLJ 291; 2000 (6) SLR 276; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 71; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 87; 1999 (6) Supreme 523

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Administrative - Service - Practice & Procedure - Central Excise Rules, 1944 r. 173Q - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965 r. 14 - Favoured order - Disciplinary proceedings - Maintainability - - Appellant was posted as Collector of Central Excise, Nagpur in year 1995 - Appellant was served with memorandum dt. 2-9-1997 u/r. 14 of the 1965 Rules, informing about proposed inquiry against him on allegation that he favoured assessee by not imposing penalty on it u/r. 173Q of the 1944 Rules - Appellant approached Central Administrative Tribunal, Mumbai (CAT) challenging proposed inquiry by filing original application on 18-3-1998, this application was dismissed by CAT by order dt. 12-8-1998 - Appellant filed writ petition in Bombay HC, it was dismissed in limine by order dt. 7-9-1998 - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment dt. 7-9-1998 of HC was justified - Held, when penalty was not levied, assessee certainly benefits, but it could not be said that by not levying penalty officer had favoured assessee or shown undue favour to him, there had to be some basis for disciplinary authority to reach such conclusion even prima facie - Record in instant case did not show if disciplinary authority had any information within its possession from where it could form opinion that appellant showed 'favour' to assessee by not imposing penalty, he might have wrongly exercised his jurisdiction, but that wrong could be corrected in appeal - That could not always form basis for initiating disciplinary proceedings for officer while he was acting as quasi judicial authority, it should be kept in mind that being quasi judicial authority, he was always subject to judicial supervision in appeal - Thus, initiation of disciplinary proceedings against officer could not take place on information which was vague or indefinite, suspicion had no role to play in such matter - There should exist reasonable basis for disciplinary authority to proceed against delinquent officer, merely because penalty was not imposed and Board in exercise of its power directed filing of appeal against that order in Appellate Tribunal could not be enough to proceed against appellant - Further, there was no other instance to show that in similar case appellant invariably imposed penalty - If, every error of law were to constitute charge of misconduct, it would impinge upon independent functioning of quasi judicial officers like appellant, since in sum and substance misconduct was sought to be inferred by appellant having committed error of law, charge-sheet on face of it did not proceed on any legal premise rendering it liable to be quashed - In other words, to maintain any charge-sheet against quasi judicial authority something more had to be alleged than mere mistake of law, eg, in nature of some extraneous consideration influencing quasi judicial order - Since nothing of sort was alleged herein impugned charge-sheet was rendered illegal, charge-sheet, if sustained, would thus impinge upon confidence and independent functioning of quasi judicial authority - Entire system of administrative adjudication whereunder quasi judicial powers were conferred on administrative authorities, would fall into disrepute if officers performing such functions were inhibited in performing their functions without fear or favour because of constant threat of disciplinary proceedings - Thus, it was not case for initiation of any disciplinary proceedings against appellant, charge of misconduct against him was not proper, it had to be quashed - Therefore, order of CAT dt. 12-8-1998 and impugned judgment dt. 7-9-1998 of HC were set aside and article of charge issued against appellant was quashed - Appeal allowed.
