Subramaniam Shanmugham v M.L. Rajendran And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28390; (1987) 4 SCC 215; AIR 1987 SC 2166; JT 1987 (3) SC 515; 1987 (2) RCJ 420; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 363; 1987 (2) RentLR 724; 1987(2) SCALE 423; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 1146

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Rent Act

Summary: Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease & Rent Control) Act, 1960 - Tenancy - (A) Let out portion not forming separate and distinct unit of a building - Whether landlord is entitled to eviction of tenant u/s. 10(3)(c) of Act - (B) Connotation of expression 'as case may be' - (C) Determination of comparative hardship - Front room of residential building let out for non-residential purpose - Tenant an affluent businessman - Landlord having no other residential house - Held, entitled - Concept of need of landlord, applicable only when separate and distinct unit of a building is let out and not when small portion of residential building is let out - Further held, he will be put to immense hardship if not allowed to occupy rented portion - Appeal dismissed.
