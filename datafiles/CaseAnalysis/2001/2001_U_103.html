Central Mine Planning and Design Institute Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20326; (2001) SCC (L&S) 471; (2001) 2 SCC 588; AIR 2001 SC 883; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 62; 2001 (1) CLR 704; 2001 (90) FLR 120; JT 2001 (2) SC 87; 2001 (34) LabIC 736; 2001 (1) LLJ 1069; 2001 (2) LLN 122; 2001(1) SCALE 374; 2001 (1) SCJ 521; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 651; 2001 (1) SLT 804; 2001 (1) Supreme 344; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 825

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Government of India Act, 1915, ss. 107 and 108 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 2(9) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(d) and 17-B - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 225 and 227 - Whether against order of Single Judge passed on an application u/s. 17-B of ID Act, an appeal u/cl. 10 of Letters Patent of Patna before DB of HC is maintainable? - S. 17-B of the ID Act confers valuable rights on the workmen and correspondingly imposes onerous obligations on the employer - The order in question passed by the learned Single Judge determines the entitlement of the workmen to receive benefits and imposes an obligation on the appellant to pay such benefits provided in the said section - That order cannot but be 'judgment' within the meaning of cl. 10 of Letters Patent, Patna - The HC is obviously in error in holding that the said order is not judgment within the meaning of cl. 10 of the Letters Patent of Patna -Held, appeal shall lie to a larger Bench of HC of Judicature at Patna from a judgment of one Judge of said HC or one Judge of any Division Court pursuant to art. 225 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
