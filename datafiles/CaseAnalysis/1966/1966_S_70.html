Girdharilal Amratlal Shodan and Others v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 378; AIR 1966 SC 1408; 1966 (2) SCJ 528; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition- Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 48 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Whether Govt. has to withdraw from acquisition as provided u/s. 48 when a s. 6 notification is invalid? - Having regard to the proviso of s. 6 of the Act, a declaration for acquisition of the land for a public purpose could only be made if the compensation to be awarded for it was to be paid wholly or partly out of public revenues or some fund controlled or managed by a local authority - The Govt. had no power to issue a notification for acquisition for a public purpose where the compensation was to paid entirely by a company - Held, where notification u/s. 6 is lawful and valid, a question may well arise whether Govt. can cancel it without withdrawing from acquisition, as provided for under s. 48 - No such question arises in instant case since notification u/s. 6 is invalid - Appeal dismissed.
