Rayala Corporation Private Limited v Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 580; AIR 2016 SC 3796; [2016] 386 ITR 500; JT 2016 (8) SC 70; 2016(7) SCALE 697; [2016] 243 TAXMAN 360

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Leasing

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Assessment - Tax imposition - Sustainability - Appellant-assessee, a private limited company, was having house property, which has been rented and the assessee was receiving income from the said property by way of rent - HC directed that income earned by appellant should be treated as "Income from House Property" - Whether the income so received should be taxed under the head 'Income from House Property' or 'Profit and gains of business or profession'.

Held, SC followed judgment in case of Chennai Properties & Investments Limited, Chennai v. Commissioner of Income Tax Central III, Tamil Nadu, 2015 Indlaw SC 313. In instant case assessee company has only one business and that is of leasing its property and earning rent therefrom. HC was not correct while deciding that income of assessee should be treated as Income from House Property. SC directs that income of assessee shall be subject to tax under the head "Profits and gains of business or profession. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.
