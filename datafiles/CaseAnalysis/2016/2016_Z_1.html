State of U. P. and others v Z. U. Ansari

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 707; JT 2016 (9) SC 608; 2016(9) SCALE 520; 2016(9) SCALE 537; 2017 (1) UPLBEC 373

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Conditions Of Service, Governor, Charge-Sheet, Pension, Rule, Posts, Recruitment, Validity Of, Appeal, Scheme, Recovery, Defence of India Rules, 1962, Misconduct, U.P. (Allocation) Rules, 1975, Writ Petition, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Civil Services Regulations, 1975, Business of Uttar Pradesh (Allocation) Rules, 1975, Uttar Pradesh Business Transaction Rules, 1975, Business of Uttar Pradesh (Allocation) Rules, M.P. Civil Service Pensions Rules, 1976

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 166(3), 309 - Civil Services Regulations, 1975, regn. 351A - Uttar Pradesh Rules of Business, 1975 - Initiation of disciplinary proceedings against retired Govt. employee - Sanction of Governor - In view of financial irregularities allegedly committed by respondent being noticed, proposal for initiating disciplinary proceedings was mooted by Department and sanctioned by State Govt. on 7-1-2011 - Later, charge sheet dt.27-6-2011 was issued and Chief Engineer was nominated as Enquiry Officer to conduct enquiry into charges levelled against respondent - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC contending that in absence of valid sanction from Governor u/regn.351-A of Civil Services Regulations, 1975 framed u/art. 309 of Constitution, disciplinary action proposed against respondent was legally impermissible - DB of HC held inter alia, that sanction granted by Minister for initiating departmental proceedings against respondent were insufficient to maintain such proceedings and allowed writ petition and quashed disciplinary proceedings including charge-sheet served on respondent - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether sanction accorded by Minister is valid sanction for purpose of regn.351-A of 1975 Regulations.

Held, there was nothing in Schedule Nos.1 and 2 of Uttar Pradesh Rules of Business, 1975 that would require the grant of sanction to initiate a disciplinary enquiry against a serving or retired Govt. servant to be brought up either before the Cabinet, the Chief Minister or the Governor. As such, sanction for initiation of disciplinary proceedings against retired Govt. servant is a matter left to be dealt with by Minister in charge of the Department concerned and neither the Constitution nor the rules framed by Governor u/art. 166(3) of Constitution require matters touching grant of sanction in cases like the present to be dealt with by Governor and the Governor alone. Further, the power to sanction stands validly conferred on Minister concerned and once he takes a decision on the subject, it is in law and in the constitutional scheme deemed to be a decision or action taken by Governor for all intended purposes including for the purpose of regn. 351(A) of Civil Service Regulations, 1975. Further, in instant case, sanction order was issued under the signature of Under Secretary and does not purport to be in the name of the Governor but that does not make any material difference as the immunity in art. 166(2) of Constitution will be available even to such an order, no sooner it is found on the basis of the material on record that an order had indeed been made by competent authority under the Business Rules. Hence, the order was in law and in constitutional scheme an order passed by Governor of the State within the meaning of regn. 351-A of the Regulations and was therefore, valid in law. However, another Judge of the Bench hearing appeal dissented with views of CJI and was of opinion that in absence of any evidence on record to show that Governor had delegated his power to concerned Minister u/regn. 351A of Civil Services Regulations, 1975 to accord sanction, the sanction granted by Minister in charge could not be said to be valid sanction and sustainable in law. Hence, in view of difference of opinion between Judges of the Bench, Registry is directed to place matter before CJI for constitution of appropriate Bench for hearing matter. Reference to larger Bench - Order accordingly.
