Calcutta Port Sharmik Union v Calcutta River Transport Association And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 618; (1988) Supp SCC 768; (1989) SCC (L&S) 106; AIR 1988 SC 2168; 1989 (1) CalLT(SC) 39; 1988 (57) FLR 689; JT 1988 (3) SC 70; 1989 LabIC 1215; 1989 (1) LLJ 223; 1989 (1) LLN 1; 1988(2) SCALE 955; 1989 (1) SLJ 77; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 1034

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Unloading, National Tribunal, Shipping

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 - Dock Workers (Regulation Of Employment) Act, 1948 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.10, 15, sch.3, item 1 - Reference - (A) Validity of interference by higher courts with award passed by Industrial Tribunal - (B) Determination of jurisdiction of Board to decide whether bargemen are dock workers in a reference - Considering applicability of recommendations of Wage Board for Port and Dock workers to bargemen - Held, should not be on hyper-technical grounds, wages and allowances fixed by the National Tribunal were just and not at all excessive - Question being incidental tribunal had jurisdiction to decide it - Appeal allowed.
