Uttar Pradesh State Sugar Corporation Limited and Another v Sant Raj Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 807; (2006) 9 SCC 82; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1610; AIR 2006 SC 2296; 2006 (7) AWC 7028; JT 2006 (11) SC 545; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 618; 2006(6) SCALE 205; 2006 (7) SCJ 30; 2006 (4) SLR 788; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 636; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2933

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Women & Children

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 39(d) - Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, s. 2(h) - Whether educational qualification can be considered to be a relevant criteria for purpose of payment of wages? - Doctrine of equal pay for equal work, as adumbrated u/art. 39(d) of Constitution of India r/w art. 14 thereof, cannot be applied in a vacuum - Constitutional scheme postulates equal pay for equal work for those who are equally placed in all respects - Court held possession of a higher qualification has all along been treated by Supreme Court to be a valid basis for classification of two categories of employees - Matter relating to grant of scale of pay may be based upon a policy decision of State - Appeal allowed.
