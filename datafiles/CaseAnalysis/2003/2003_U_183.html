Federation of Railway Officers Association and others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 281; (2003) 4 SCC 289; AIR 2003 SC 1344; 2003 (4) AWC 2738; 2003 (104) DLT 79; JT 2003 (5) SC 123; 2003(3) SCALE 251; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1085; 2003 (2) SLT 813; 2003 (3) Supreme 265

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Writ Proceeding

Summary: Railways Act, 1989 - S. 3 - contention that the notification issued for formation of new zones is violative of Section 3 of the Railways Act as the same is not formed for the purpose of efficient administration of the railways - Held, when technical questions arise and experts in the field have expressed various views and all those aspects have been taken into consideration by the Government in deciding the matter, it cannot still be said that the Supreme Court should reexamine to interfere with the same. The wholesome rule in regard to judicial interference in administrative decisions is that if the Government takes into consideration all relevant factors, eschew from considering irrelevant factors and act reasonably within the parameters of the law, courts would keep off the same.
