State of Uttar Pradesh v Hari Ram

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 139; (2013) 4 SCC 280; 2013 (4) ADJ 249; AIR 2013 SC 1793; 2013 (98) ALR 686; 2013 (3) AWC 2595; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 539; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 10; JT 2013 (4) SC 275; 2013 (3) MLJ 408; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 499; 2013 (120) RD 241; 2013(3) SCALE 348; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 6, 10, 10(3), 10(5), 10(6), - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999, ss. 10(3), 10(5), 10(6), 3, - Vacant land - Voluntary surrender - Entitlement of - Respondent filed a statement giving details of vacant land he was holding in excess of ceiling limit prescribed under the Act, as provided u/s. 6 of the 1976 Act - Competent authority issued a notification stating land should be deemed to have been vested with Govt., free from all encumbrances - Prescribed authority issued a notice u/s. 10(5) of the 1976 Act directing respondent to hand over possession of land declared as surplus to a duly authorized person - On appeal, District Court, allowed appeal and quashed order passed by Competent authority - Aggrieved, Competent authority filed petition before HC - DB dismissed appeal holding that s. 10(5) of the 1976 Act did not envisage, taking physical and de facto possession of surplus land, for which proceedings u/s. 10(5) of the 1999 had to be followed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether deemed vesting of surplus land u/s. 10(3) of the 1976 Act would amount to taking de facto possession depriving land holders of benefit of saving Clause u/s. 3 of the 1999 Act - Held, mere vesting of land u/s. 10(3) of the 1976 Act would not confer any right on State Govt. to have de facto possession of vacant land unless there was a voluntary surrender of vacant land before 18-3-1999 - State had to establish that there was voluntary surrender of vacant land or surrender and delivery of peaceful possession u/s. 10(5) or forceful dispossession u/s. 10(6) of the 1976 Act - On failure to establish any of those situations, land owner or holder could claim benefit of s. 3 of the 1999 Act. - Thus, State Govt. could not establish any of those situations - Hence, HC was right in holding that respondent was entitled to get the benefit of s. 3 of the 1999 Act - Appeal dismissed.
