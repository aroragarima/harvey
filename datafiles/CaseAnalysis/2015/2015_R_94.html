Ranjeet Kumar Ram @ Ranjeet Kumar Das and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 369; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2455; 2015 CRLJ 2944; 2015 (3) JCC 2065; JT 2015 (5) SC 165; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 42; 2015(6) SCALE 529; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kidnapping, Ransom

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 302 - Kidnapping & Murder - Conviction - Justification - Accused kidnapped child of complainant PW18 and accused demanded ransom from PW18 - Further, PW 18 arranged for ransom amount and same was given to accused, however, PW18's child was not returned - Further, complainant informed about accused person to IO and raid was conducted and accused person were arrested - After recovery of death body of victim, FIR was lodged u/ss. 302, 201 and 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted accused and death sentence was awarded to accused no.5 - HC confirmed conviction to accused nos. 1,3, 4 and 5, however, HC converted death sentence awarded to A 5 as life imprisonment - Whether conviction of accused by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to testimony of PW2 minor, SC held that when Trial Court has ascertained discernment of PW2 and has formed opinion that PW2 is competent to testify and then recorded her evidence, there is no reason to discredit PW2's testimony. Further, SC held that evidence of PW 2 coupled with evidence of PW 8 clearly establishes that accused nos. 5 and 3 and kidnapped PW8's son PW8 informant paid ransom amount. SC held that on evidence of PW2, courts below rightly recorded concurrent findings that prosecution has established that deceased boy was last seen alive in company of accused nos. 5 and 3. Further, in furtherance of common intention, A 1 had been persuading PW8 to pay ransom amount even before there was no such demand from kidnappers, hence, common intention is proved and A 1 is rightly convicted by lower Court. With respect to A 4, SC held that neither any recovery was made from him nor any incriminating evidence is available against him, hence, case against A 4 is not proved beyond reasonable doubt and his conviction is set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Guilt of accused in criminal cases is to be proved by prosecution beyond reasonable doubt.
