Kaliya v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 475; (2013) 10 SCC 758; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 1780; 2014 (1) JabLJ 86; JT 2013 (10) SC 414; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 958; 2013(9) SCALE 661; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 498-A - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was convicted u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced to life imprisonment and a fine of Rs.500/- had also been imposed, though, other co-accused (son of appellant) and X (husband of the appellant) stood convicted u/s. 498-A IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 3 years - HC

dismissed the appeal of the appellant filed against said order but allowed the appeal of the other co-accused acquitting them of the said charges - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, Trial Court as well as HC relied mainly upon the dying declaration made by deceased wherein she had stated that she was subjected to harassment by appellant (mother-in-law) and co-accused ( father-in-law and her husband) - Moreover, PW18 (Doctor) deposed that 100% burnt patient can also be in a fit mental and physical condition to give statement - PW14, who performed the post-mortem of deceased, deposed that she was completely burnt and the burn injuries were anti-mortem - Both the courts below were of the considered opinion that the appellant was responsible for causing the death of deceased - Defence taken by the appellant that she had gone out of her house to provide water to the buffalo had been disbelieved by the Court - As the incident occurred in the house of the appellant, and she was present therein at the relevant time, she could have furnished the explanation as to how and under what circumstances deceased died - Matter was within her special knowledge - Appeal dismissed.
