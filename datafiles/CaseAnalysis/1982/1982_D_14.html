State of Madhya Pradesh v Dewadas and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 8; (1982) SCC (Cr) 275; (1982) 1 SCC 552; AIR 1982 SC 800; 1982 CRLJ 614; 1982 MLJ 341; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 378 - Application for leave to appeal - Maintainability of - Whether an application for grant of leave u/s. 378(3) of Code be treated as a part of appeal preferred by State Govt. u/s. 378(1) and should have been placed before a Bench of two Judges of High Court? - Held, the legislative object in re-enacting provisions of s. 417 of old Code with addition of new provision contained in sub-s. (3) of s. 378 of Code, was that there had to be a further scrutiny of a State appeal by court even prior to stage of admission - Requiring court to consider at very outset whether appeal should be entertained or not - It was only after appeal was entertained with 'leave' of court that it had to be heard for admission and it may be dismissed summarily without notice to other side - Under law it will be perfectly in order if a composite application is made giving necessary facts and circumstances of case along with grounds which may be urged in appeal with a prayer for leave to entertain appeal - It is not necessary that an application for leave to entertain appeal should be lodged first and only after grant of leave by HC an appeal may be preferred against order of acquittal - If such a procedure is adopted it is likely, as it has happened in the present case, appeal may be time-barred if HC takes more than ninety days for disposal of application for leave - Appeal allowed.
