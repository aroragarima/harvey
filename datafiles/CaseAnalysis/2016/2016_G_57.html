Haryana State and another v Gram Panchayat Village Kalehri

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 454; 2016 (5) ALT(SC) 1A; 2016 (5) AWC 5279; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 69; 2016 (4) CTC 526; JT 2016 (6) SC 302; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 436; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 159; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 173

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Second Appeal, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100, O. 27 r. 5B - Suit for declaration - Dismissal - Validity - Respondent/panchayat filed a suit for declaration that they were owner of suit land against appellant/State - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal - Aggrieved, appellants filed second appeal before HC - Single Judge dismissed appeal holding that appeal does not involve any substantial question of law as required u/s.100 CPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appeal does involve substantial questions of law. It is obligatory on HC to decide legality and correctness of such findings as to which party's documents are to be preferred for conferring title over suit land. Therefore, HC should have admitted appeal by framing substantial questions of law and giving notice to respondent for its final hearing as provided u/s. 100 CPC. No such endeavour was made by HC or by parties to arrive at a settlement in view of O. 27 r. 5B CPC. Thus, HC committed jurisdictional error in dismissing appeal holding that it only involves question of fact. Impugned order of HC was set aside. HC was directed to decide the second appeal in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Court is required to properly interpret nature of documents, it does not involve any issue of fact as such but it only involves legal issue based on admitted documents.

Legislation Cited

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 100
