Food Corporation Of India v Provident Fund Commissioner And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 289; (1990) SCC (L&S) 1; (1990) 1 SCC 68; 1989 (3) CCC 473; 1990 (1) CLR 20; 1990 (60) FLR 15; JT 1989 (4) SC 380; 1989 (2) LLN 987; 1989(2) SCALE 1029; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 755; 1990 (1) SLJ 140; 1990 (1) SLR 147; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 755; 1990 (1) UJ 257

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Discovery and production of documents

Summary: Civil Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Employees Provident Fund Act, s. 7-A - Whether Commr. who is statutory authority has exercised powers vested in him to collect relevant evidence before determining amount payable under said Act? - Commr. u/s. 7-A has same powers as are vested in a court under CPC for trying a suit and is authorised to enforce attendance in person and also to examine any person on oath - Power was given to Commr. to decide not abstract questions of law, but only to determine actual concrete differences in payment of contribution and other dues by identifying workmen - Commr. should exercise all his powers to collect all evidence and collage all material before coming to proper conclusion - That is legal duty of Commr. - It would be failure to exercise jurisdiction particularly when a party to proceedings requests for summoning evidence for a particular person - Appeal allowed.
