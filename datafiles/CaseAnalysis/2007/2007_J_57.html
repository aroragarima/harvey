Delhi Development Authority, N.D. and Another v Joint Action Committee, Allottee of Sfs Flats and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1464; (2008) 2 SCC 672; AIR 2008 SC 1343; 2008 (146) DLT 609; JT 2007 (13) SC 548; 2007(14) SCALE 507; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Agreements Void For Uncertainty, Disposal Of Property, Actual Possession, Novation Of Contract, Subject-Matter Of Contract, Delhi Development Authority (Management and Disposal of Housing Estates) Regulations, 1968

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Delhi Development Act, 1957, ss. 2, 5, 6, 41, 52, 57 and 5 A - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 29 and 60 - (A) Whether action of Development Authority in levying 20% surcharge from registrants of South Delhi is justified? - (B) Whether demand for payment of current cost as calculated by Delhi Development Authority from defaulter registrants could be said to be justified? - Held, Full Bench of High Court wrongly relied on the decisions of P.N. Verma v. UOI, (1984 Indlaw DEL 251) and Premji Bhai Parmar and Ors. v. Delhi Development Authority, (1979 Indlaw SC 109) which were decided on wholly different set of facts as scheme(s) involved therein was for low income group of people in terms whereof payments were to be made on completion of construction - Whereas in case of SFS, installments of payment is sought for immediately after allocation - Levy of surcharge for subsidized schemes for weaker sections of society having, inter alia, been upheld in Premji Bhai Parmar's case, impugned policy decision must be held to be reasonable so as to satisfy tests of art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950 - Cost of land even in South Delhi having been worked out on valuation of land situated in Dwarka, classification made in respect of allocation at South Delhi where market rates were much higher from actual cost, scheme for restoration of flats in that area cannot be held to be arbitrary or discriminatory - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeals filed by DDA are dismissed - Appeals allowed.
