D. M. Manasvi v Commissioner of Income Tax Gujarat Ii, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 199; (1973) SCC (Tax) 155; (1973) 3 SCC 207; AIR 1973 SC 22; 1972 (1) CTR 437; [1972] 86 ITR 557; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 389; 1973 TAXLR 8

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 271 and 274(2) - (A) Satisfaction of ITO - (1) Need for notice to assessee - (2) Need of preliminary inquiry regarding necessity of initiating proceedings - (B) Matter referred to Inspecting Asst. Commr. - Whether proceedings u/s. 271 can be initiated - Held, subsequent to making of assessment orders would not show that there was no satisfaction of ITO during assessment proceedings - Preliminary inquiry regarding necessity of initiating proceedings not necessary - Proceedings u/s. 271 can still be initiated by ITO - Appeal dismissed.
