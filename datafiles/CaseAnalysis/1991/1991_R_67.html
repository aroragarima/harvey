Rabindra Chamria And Ors., Etc., Etc. v Registrar Of Companieswest Bengal And Ors., Etc., Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, Kuldip Singh, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 370; (1992) Supp2 SCC 10; AIR 1992 SC 398; [1992] 73 Comp Cas 257; 1992 (1) CompLJ 1; 1992 (64) FLR 939; JT 1991 (4) SC 487; 1992 (1) LLJ 313; 1992 (1) LLN 921; 1991(2) SCALE 1021; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Lock Out

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Companies Act, 1956, s. 633(2) - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act,1952, s. 14A(1) - Non-payment of employees Provident Fund dues by officers of Company - Liability governed only by s. 14A (1) of 1952 Act - A beneficial legislation for employees - Provisions of s. 633 can not be interpreted as overriding any other penal provisions - (A) Whether the releif u/s. 633 of 1956 Act can be extended in respect of liability under any other Act - (B) Whether s. 633 can be invoked for offence u/s. 32 of 1947 Act - Held, the officers of the company are not entitled to relief u/s. 633 of Companies by defeating the provisions of a social welfare legislation namely the Employees' Provident Fund Act - And s. 633 of the Companies Act could not be invoked for offences u/s. 32 of 1947 Act - Appeal dismissed.
