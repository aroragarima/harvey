Income Tax Officer, I Ward, Dist, Vi, Calcutta & Ors. v Lakhmani Mewal Das

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 135; (1976) SCC (Tax) 402; (1976) 3 SCC 757; AIR 1976 SC 1753; 1976 (5) CTR 220; [1976] 103 ITR 437; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 956; 1976 TAXLR 726

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 148 - Escaped assessment - Issue of Notice - Held, two conditions required to be satisfied before ITO issued a notice u/s. 148 of Income Tax Act are that he must have reason to believe (i) that income chargeable to tax had escaped assessment and (ii) that such income had escaped assessment by reason of omission or failure on part of assessee, to disclose fully and truly material facts necessary for assessment for that year - Both these conditions must co-exist in order to confer jurisdiction on ITO - Further ITO should record his reasons before initiating proceedings u/s. 148(2); before issuing notice after expiry of four years from end of relevant assessment year, Commr. should be satisfied on reasons recorded by ITO that it was a fit case for issue of such notice - Duty cast upon assessee does not extend beyond making a true and full disclosure of primary facts It is then for ITO to draw correct inference from primary facts - High Court were not in error in holding that said material could not have led to formation of belief that income of assessee respondent had escaped assessment because of his failure or omission to disclose fully and truly all material facts - Appeal dismissed.
