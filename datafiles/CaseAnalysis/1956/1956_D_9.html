Dwarka Dass Bhatia v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 63; AIR 1957 SC 164; 1957 CRLJ 316; 1957 SCJ 133; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 948

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Ordinance, 1946, Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act 1964

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, s. 3(1) - Detention - Petitioner was charged of smuggling essential goods to Pakistan through Ferozpur and Amritsar border - Since tightening of said borders he shifted smuggling activities to Ranbirsinghpura Pakistan borders in State of Jammu and Kashmir and carried on illicit smuggling of essential goods such as cloth, zari and mercury to Pakistan through this border - (A) Whether decision of subjective satisfaction of an authority is bad where satisfaction is based on a variety of reason and some of them are found to be irrelevant? - Matter being one for subjective satisfaction, it must be properly based on all reasons on which it purports to be based and if some out of them are found to be non-existent or irrelevant, Court cannot predicate what subjective satisfaction of said authority would have been on exclusion of those grounds or reasons - Held, where power is vested in a statutory authority to deprive liberty of a subject on its subjective satisfaction with reference to specified matters, if that satisfaction is stated to be based on a number of grounds or for a variety of reasons, all taken together, and if some out of them, are found to be non-existent or irrelevant, very exercise of that power is bad - (B) Whether order of detention in instant case is valid? - No material is placed before us enabling us to say that smuggling attributed to petitioner was substantially only of mercury and that smuggling as regards other two commodities was of an inconsequential nature - Held, order of detention in this case is bad and must be quashed - Appeal allowed.
