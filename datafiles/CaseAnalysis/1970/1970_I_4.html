Hirday Narain v Income-Tax Officer, Bareilly

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 142; (1970) 2 SCC 355; AIR 1971 SC 33; [1970] 78 ITR 26; 1971 (1) SCJ 706; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(3)(a)(ii) - A/y 1950-51 and 1951-52 - 'X' and his five sons were members of a Hindu Undivided Family (HUF) - Till the a/y 1950-51 the income received by 'X' was assessed to tax as the income of a HUF - Subsequently, property of the joint family was partitioned between 'X' and his sons on 19.11.1949 - In assessing the income for the a/y 1951-52 the Income-Tax Officer recorded an order that the property was partitioned, but he still assessed the income received by 'X' as income of a HUF - On appeal, the Appellate Asst. Commissioner treated Rs. 18,520 earned between 1.10.1949 and 18.11.1949 as income of the former HUF and directed that it be 'excluded from the assessment' - Pursuant to that order, the ITO made two orders of assessment, first assessing Rs. 18,520 earned upto 18.11.1949 as income of the HUF of 'X' and his five sons and secondly assessing the balance also as income of a HUF but liable to tax in the hands of 'X' - 'X' applied for rectification of a mistake in the order of assessment which he claimed was apparent from the record - ITO declined to give said relief by holding that for the period between 19.11.1949 and 30.09.1950. 'X' should have been assessed as an individual - Single Judge dismissed petition filed against said order holding that the 'X' had not applied in revision to the Commissioner u/s. 33A of 1922 Act - DB also dismissed appeal filed against said order holding that the rectification u/s. 35 of 1922 Act was 'discretionary', and if the ITO thought that the proceedings were 'substantially fair' he was not bound to rectify the assessment on technical grounds - Hence, present appeal - Whether ITO rightly dismissed 'X' rectification application u/s. 35 of 1922 Act? - Held, HC was in error in assuming that exercise of the power u/s. 35 of 1922 Act was discretionary and the Income-tax Officer could, even if the conditions for its exercise were shown to exist, decline to exercise the power - For the a/y 1952-53 the assessee is also entitled to relief claimed by him - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
