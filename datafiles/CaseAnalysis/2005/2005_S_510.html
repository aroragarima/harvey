M.P. Gopalakrishnan Nair and another v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 338; (2005) 11 SCC 45; AIR 2005 SC 3053; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 76; JT 2005 (4) SC 436; 2005 (2) KLT 779; 2005(4) SCALE 229; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Trusts & Associations - Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1926 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 164, 19, 21, 25, 26, 26(d) - Guruvayoor Devaswom Act, 1978, ss. 3, 4, 4(1), 4(1)(d) - Hindu Religious Institutions Act, 1950, ss. 4, 6 - Management of temple - Whether vesting of power in 'Hindus' in Council of Ministers to nominate members of Managing Committee could be held to violate art.25 and 26?; what is right that is sought to be protected?; whether by reason of impugned Act administration of institution had been taken from hands of religious denomination and vested in another body? - Held, HC in its impugned judgment arrived at a finding as regard categorical existence of a subsisting religious practice that as on date of coming into force of Constitution of India it has not been established that denomination of temple worshippers had any right to be on management committee or members of such a committee were being elected / nominated by an electoral college consisting exclusively of members of such denomination - Nothing has been pointed out to show that such a finding is contrary to materials on records - Appeals dismissed.
