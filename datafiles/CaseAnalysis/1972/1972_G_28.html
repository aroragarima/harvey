Gouri Shankar Jha v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 396; (1972) SCC (Cr) 328; (1972) 1 SCC 564; AIR 1972 SC 711; 1972 CRLJ 505; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 129; 1972 UJ 604

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.561A - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss.344, 395 - Arrest and detention - Dacoity - Confession - Appellant arrested and detained in custody without being informed of grounds for arrest and detention - Appellant not produced before Magistrate within 24 hours after his detention as required under Code - Appellant was removed to Darbhanga jail and threatened that he would be falsely involved in several cases of dacoity unless he made certain incriminating statements which police wanted him to make - On these allegations, appellant claimed release forthwith from detention and quashing of criminal proceedings against him - HC rightly observed it was not correct as jail Superintendent's report clearly showed that appellant had refused to go from the jail for fear that he would be seen or be shown to probable witnesses - Appeal was against dismissal by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s.344 would apply at stage of investigation - Held, that s. 344 did not apply at stage of investigation and could apply only after Magistrate had taken cognizance of and issued processes or warrant for production of accused - Magistrate competent to pass remand orders from time to time subject to each order being not for period exceeding 15 days - No doubt that Magistrate had satisfied that condition - Appellant had made confession before police - Confession by two others implicated appellant in commission of offences u/s. 395 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.The Judgment was delivered by Shelat, J.Shelat, J. This appeal, by special leave, is. against the dismissal by the High Court of Patna of the Writ Petition and an application under S. 561A of the Code of Criminal
