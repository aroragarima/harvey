Mohan And Another v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1585; (2007) 9 SCC 431; AIR 2007 SC 2625; 2007 (67) ALR 736; 2007 (3) AWC 3021; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 879; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 383; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 454; 2007(4) SCALE 335; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 11A - Award - Legality - Writ petition filed for quashing the award dated 4.2.2003 published by the Special Land Acquisition Officer in land acquisition proceedings was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether award was illegal in view of s. 11A? - Respondent submitted that after the declaration u/s. 6 dated 28.2.2000 the acquiring body had intimated to the Land Acquisition Officer proposing deletion of some of the area which was proposed to be acquired and accordingly, a corrigendum to that effect was issued on 25.1.2003, and hence, it was submitted that the award dated 4.1.2003 was well within time - Held, in view of s. 11A of the Act an award has to be made within two years from the date of publication of the declaration u/s. 6 - Failure to adhere to this time frame is fatal to the award, as the provision is mandatory - Further, u/s. 11A what has to be seen is the date of last publication of the declaration u/s. 6, and not any subsequent corrigendum to the said declaration - Only circumstance under which the period between the declaration u/s. 6 and the award can be extended is mentioned in the explanation to s. 11A - There is no mention in s. 11A that the period after the publication of the declaration u/s. 6 of the Act and the publication of any corrigendum to the said declaration has also to be excluded - Impugned award quashed - Appeal allowed.
