Bharat Heavy Electrical Limited Etc. v Union Of India And Others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 736; (1996) 4 SCC 230; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 481; AIR 1996 SC 1854; JT 1996 (4) SC 427; 1996(3) SCALE 746; 1996 (102) STC 373; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Subsequent Sale

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3(a) and 9 - Sale in course of inter-State Trade and exigibility to tax - BHEL, entering into contract for setting up of power plants for a State undertaking in Orissa - Unit of BHEL in Andhra Pradesh manufacturing parts and components required for boiler systems and sending to executing unit of BHEL for contract in question in Tamil Nadu and some parts to work site in Orissa, for incorporation in boiler system - (A) Whether it is a case of sale of parts, in pursuance of supply contract in question - (B) Is it inter-State sale - (C) In which State is Central Sales Tax leviable - Held, (A) It is a sale of parts - (B) The sale is inter-state sale - (C) As movement of goods commenced in Andhra Pradesh, it is in that State that Central Sales Tax is leviable - Appeal dismissed.
