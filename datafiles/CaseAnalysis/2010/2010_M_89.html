Commissioner, Central Excise, Meerut v M/S. Monsanto Manufacture Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1097; (2011) 2 SCC 754; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 335; 2010(12) SCALE 493

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Ice Cream

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules 1944, r. 9A - Demand - Penalty - Assessee along with other companies entered into an agreement with 'X' for manufacturing Ice Cream - On the basis of the said agreement, assessee filed price list in respect of the manufactured product with the Department and paid duty on the basis of price so declared - Subsequently, department issued show cause notice to the assessee as to why differential duty should not be demanded u/r. 9(A) of 1944 Rules r/w. s. 11A of 1944 Act together with penalty and interest, alleging that assessee received additional consideration from 'X' over and above the assessable value declared by it in several forms like non-competition reserve, interest free deposit, consideration for sale of marketing undertaking, interest on deposits as security advances received by it, value of the brand name etc. - Assessee contended that the transaction between assessee and 'X' was on a principal to principal basis with price as a sole consideration for the sale of the manufactured products - Assessee also raised objections to the allegations on the ground of limitation - Commissioner rejected all the contentions raised by assessee on the issue of limitation and held that full details of settlement, between the parties to the source agreement was not made available to the department and merely furnishing a copy of the agreement was not enough - On appeal, Tribunal held that there was no direct and indirect considerations received by assessee - Tribunal also held that show cause notice was hopelessly barred by limitation as agreement entered into by and between the parties was made available to the Department and all transactions between the parties thereto were on the basis of the agreement which were within the knowledge of the Department - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the tribunal could be upheld? - Held, the entire transaction between the parties was on the basis of the agreement which was within the knowledge of the Department - Moreover, it is not the allegation in the show cause notice that the assessee has received any direct and indirect consideration over and above as to what has been agreed under the agreement - The price fixation was in accordance with the formula agreed to between the parties which has been specifically incorporated in the source agreement - Hence, show cause notice was barred by limitation - Impugned order of the tribunal upheld - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
