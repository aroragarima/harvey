O.M. Baby (Dead) By Lrs. v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 187; (2012) 11 SCC 362; 2012 CRLJ 3794; 2012 (3) JCC 1949; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 825; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 663; 2012(6) SCALE 159

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 342, 376, and 506 (ii) - Rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant allegedly committed rape on prosecutrix (PW2) after putting her into fear of death - Trial court convicted appellant u/ss. 376, 506 (ii) and 342 of IPC - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellant contended that in view of the 2 contradictory reports of chemical analysis of the sample of vaginal swab and smear, the benefit of the first report which did not indicate presence of spermatozoa should go in favour of the appellant - Appellant further contended that the sample in respect of which first report was submitted was taken on the day of the incident itself, whereas the sample, which was the subject matter of the second report was taken 10 days later - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, while it was correct that the 2 reports of the analysis of vaginal swab and smear were contradictory, however, the prosecution has clearly proved and established the circumstances which necessitated the second medical examination of the victim and a second set of sample of vaginal swab and smear to be taken and sent for chemical analysis - The aforesaid exercise was carried out on the basis of the application filed by the mother of the victim (PW 4) before the court of Judicial Magistrate as she had serious doubts with regard to the fairness of the doctors in the District hospital who had carried out the first medical examination and had taken the samples of vaginal swab and smear on the date of the occurrence - The application by PW 4 before the court of Judicial Magistrate was filed without delay - No motive and interest can be attributed to PW 8 who had conducted the second round of medical examination and had taken the second set of sample of vaginal swab and smear for chemical analysis - That apart, it was clear from the evidence of PW 8 that after sexual intercourse, though active spermatozoa would be present for 36 hours, the same would remain in the vaginal canal for as long as 17 days - There was no evidence to the contrary - Impugned judgment of trial court as affirmed by the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
