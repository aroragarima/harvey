Parvinderjit Singh And Anr v State (U.T. Chandigarh) And Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2088; (2008) 13 SCC 431; AIR 2009 SC 502; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 274; 2008 (4) JCC 2873; JT 2008 (11) SC 560; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 210; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 471; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 897; 2008(13) SCALE 711; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Banking Regulations Act, 1956

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code,1973, ss.438, 439 - Companies Act, 1956 - Banking Regulations Act, 1956 - Anticipatory bail - Entitlement - Citibank and Citigroup Wealth Advisors ('CWA') were two separate legal entities - Citibank carried on banking activities and was incorporated under 1956 Act, and was guided by directions and guidelines of RBI - Whereas CWA was wealth advisory body incorporated under 1956 Act and was regulated by directions and guidelines as set out by SEBI and Stock Exchanges - Appellants (who are employees of Citi Bank) prayed for anticipatory bail that allegations in complaint was of civil nature since arbitration proceedings had been initiated at behest of both complainant and CWA much prior to institution of criminal case - Prayer was opposed by State and complainant - HC held that it was not case where any protection in terms of s.438 of CrPC was to be extended - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether case had been made out of for granting relief sought - Held, applicant availed remedies upto higher Courts and requirements of s.439 of CrPC become dead letter - Grounds on which belief applicant was based that he might be arrested in non-bailable offence might be capable of being examined - However, application was made to HC or Court of Session, to decide case had been made out of for granting relief sought - Provisions could not be invoked after arrest of accused - Blanket order should not be passed - Thus, order u/s. 438 of CrPC was device to secure individual's liberty' it was neither passport to commission of crimes nor shield against any and all kinds of accusations - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 438 - Arrest - Passing of interim order - Maintenance of law and order - Whether Court could pass interim order to arrest applicant, where application u/s.438 of the CrPC was pending - Held, arrest was part of process of investigation intended to secure several purposes - Accused had to be questioned in detail regarding various facets of motive, preparation, commission and aftermath of crime and connection of other persons - It should be necessary to curtail his freedom in order to enable investigation to proceed without hindrance and to protect witnesses and persons connected with victim of crime, to prevent his dis-appearance to maintain law and order in locality - Therefore, arrest became inevitable part of process of investigation - Legality of proposed arrest could not be gone into application u/s.438 of CrPC - Thus, Court would not interfere with investigation of crime or with arrest of accused in cognizable offence - Interim order restraining arrest, if passed while dealing with application u/s.438 of CrPC would amount to interference in investigation, which could not be done u/s.438 of CrPC - Appeal disposed of.
