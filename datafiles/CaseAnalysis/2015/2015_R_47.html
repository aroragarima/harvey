Rajiv Singh v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 898; 2016 (2) JCC 1270; JT 2015 (12) SC 305; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 432; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 235; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 640; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Photographs, Criminal Justice, Oath, Hair, Clothes, Distance, Joint Account, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Facilitate, Conjectures

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 304, 304B, 398A, 498A - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Appellant husband and his wife was travelling by train for honeymoon - However, during journey, it was found that appellant's wife was missing - Missing Report was lodged by appellant husband - Further, death body of female was found on railway tracks, however, the same was unidentified - Respondent i.e. parents of missing daughter filed FIR against appellant husband alleging that their daughter was tortured by appellant and his family for dowry demand which had caused death of their daughter - Trial court convicted appellant husband u/ss. 304B, 201, 498A of IPC - HC vide impugned order affirmed conviction of appellant - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, from consistent evidence of these witnesses PWs. 5,6,7 who have not been declared hostile by prosecution, it is very unlikely that dead body recovered near station could have been of appellant's wife. Further, neither respondents nor any of her family members had claimed that dead body is of their daughter. Further, it is mentioned in post-mortem report that time lag between death and post-mortem examination was more than six days. Hence, death had occurred to female concerned prior to 13-8-2007 which argumentatively as well suggests to rule out possibility that it was that of appellant's wife as she was alive and travelling in Train in intervening night of 14-8-2007 and 15-8-2007. PW 8 doctor who had conducted post-mortem, has also affirmed in his testimony, margin of time between death and post-mortem examination of dead body. Hence, SC held that prosecution has failed to prove beyond reasonable doubt that dead body retrieved from bush near railway tracks in vicinity of Police Station was that of appellant's wife. Further, with respect to alleged cruelty, SC held that testimony of IO involved, indicates that prior to complaint dt. 5-9-2007, no allegation of cruelty or harassment for or in connection with dowry demand had been made against appellant or his family members. Further, even contents of complaint dt. 5-9-2007 do not unassailably establish cruelty or harassment for or in connection with dowry demand as contemplated by ss. 398A and 304B of IPC when compared with testimony of PW11 and DW4. Further, both father and brother of victim in their depositions have admitted that money invested in mutual funds had neither been withdrawn by appellant nor had been diverted for his use or appropriation. Further, alleged diary written by victim has not been produced at trial and as complained by PW 11 IO that it had not been handed over to her as well even when sought for. Hence, improvement in versions of PWs 1,2 and 3 with regard to demand for dowry and ill-treatment do not commend for acceptance. Hence, SC held that prosecution has failed to prove charge u/ss. 304B/498A/201 of IPC against appellant. Further, SC held that investigation carried out by IO was not in accordance with law. Impugned order passed by lower Court against appellant is not justified and is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused is not justified when prosecution has failed to prove guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
