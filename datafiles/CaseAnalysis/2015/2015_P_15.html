Securities and Exchange Board of India v Pan Asia Advisors Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 484; AIR 2015 SC 2782; 2015 (3) BC 513; [2015] 191 Comp Cas 410; 2015 (3) CompLJ 241; 2015(7) SCALE 694; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Misfeasance, Depository, Bonds, Investments, Pledge, Institutional, Securities Appellate Tribunal, Issuer, Lead Managers, Custodian, Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of Security By a Person Resident Outside India) Regulations, 2000, Foreign Currency Convertible Bonds and Ordinary Shares (Through Depository Receipt Mechanism) Scheme, 1993

Summary: Securities and Exchange Board of India - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 11, 11(2), 11B, 12A - Foreign Exchange Management (Transfer or Issue of Security by a Person Resident Outside India) Regulations, 2000 - Alleged Fraudulent Activity - Debar from security market - Jurisdiction of SEBI - Respondents as Lead Managers dealt with to Global Depository Receipts (GDRs) issued by six entities - SEBI initiated proceedings against respondents as Lead Managers to Global Depository Receipts (GDRs) issued outside India based on investigations held by it and on its conclusion that in relation to transaction of sale/purchase of underlying shares released on redemption of GDRs in securities market in India, Lead Managers had committed fraud on investors in India and that such fraudulent intention existed at every stage of GDR process till sale/purchase of underlying shares in securities market in India - Further, SEBI passed impugned order debarring respondents from rendering services in connection with instruments and such debarment for period of 10 years prohibiting respondents from accessing capital market directly or indirectly under Act and regulations - Members of Tribunal by way of their majority view set aside order of SEBI debarring respondents - Whether SEBI had jurisdiction in passing impugned order debarring respondents for period of ten years in dealing with securities while considering role played by respondents as Lead Managers relating to GDRs.

Held, if there is going to be false pretext or misleading information circulated with view to lure both foreign investors and Indian investors and in that process very purpose of creation and trading in GDRs are found to be not true or bona fide, it cannot be said that simply because creation of such GDRs and its trading is in global market, SEBI should not take action on ground that it cannot extend its statutory arm beyond Indian territory to control any such misdeeds deliberately committed with view to defraud Indian investors and thereby their interest in investment of securities and its protection is at great stake. SC held that having regard to nature of allegations in interests of investors in securities and statutory obligation/duty cast upon SEBI to protect their interests, SEBI has got every jurisdiction to proceed against respondents and issuing company. Further, there is no statutory prohibition either under 1999 or 1934 Act preventing SEBI from taking action in exercise of its powers u/ss. 11, 11B and 12A of 1992 Act. Further, under 1993 Scheme and 2000 Regulations, there are provisions which make specific reference to role of SEBI in dealing with securities. Hence, SC held that SEBI had jurisdiction in passing order debarring respondents for period of 10 years in dealing with securities while considering role played by respondents as Lead Managers relating to GDRs issued by six companies which issued such GDRs. Further, Tribunal is bound to examine correctness of impugned order of SEBI in appeal preferred by respondents. Impugned order passed by majority members of Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Order passed by SEBI to protect interest of investor cannot be challenged on grounds of jurisdiction when power to take action is provided by legislature.
