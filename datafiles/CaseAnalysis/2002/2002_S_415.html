Kanthi Enterprises and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1694; (2002) 7 SCC 283; AIR 2002 SC 3227; JT 2002 (7) SC 19; 2002(6) SCALE 321; 2002 (5) SLT 205; 2002 (128) STC 182; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 209; 2002 (6) Supreme 275

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales tax Act, 1957, s. 5(1A) - Challenged validity of retrospective operation of explanation to first proviso to s. 5(1A) - Legislature can impose tax retrospectively though it cannot be arbitrary and unreasonable - Held, effect of retrospective effect is only for six months and even if they have not passed on burden of tax to customers during that period, effect cannot be said to be so unreasonable, arbitrary and harsh as to invalidate explanation - Appeals dismissed.
