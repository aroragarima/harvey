Government of Andhra Pradesh and others v Mohd. Ghouse Mohinuddin and others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 40; (2001) 8 SCC 416; (2002) SCC (L&S) 30; AIR 2001 SC 3424; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 114; 2001 (91) FLR 434; JT 2001 (7) SC 146; 2001(5) SCALE 491; 2001 (4) SLR 126; 2001 (6) SLT 156; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 180; 2001 (6) Supreme 464

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Constitution (Thirty-Second Amendment) Act, 1973

Summary: Service - Constitution of India,1950, art. 371 - Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Organisation of Local Cadres and Regulation of Direct Recruitment) Order, 1975 - Re-determination - Inter se seniority zone-wise basis - Instant appeal filed against the order of the Tribunal - By the impugned order, the Tribunal directs the re-determination of inter se seniority zone-wise basis in different cadres for promotion to the higher posts - Tribunal dealt with three different depts. of the Govt. of Andhra Pradesh, Commercial Tax Dept., Revenue Dept. and Police Dept. - Tribunal in the impugned order came to the conclusion that Division was a unit for the purpose of administration and zone was a unit for the purpose of organisation of cadres for direct recruitment, appointment, seniority, promotion and transfer under the Presidential Order - It also held that organisation of various local cadres under the notification issued by the State Govt., did not satisfy the requirement of the Presidential Order as provided and such separate cadres in smaller units affect the conditions of service mentioned - Tribunal found that the Sales Tax Dept., the Revenue Dept. and the Police Dept. have acted without reference to the Presidential Order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Presidential Order, that has been issued in exercise of powers u/art. 371-D(1) and (2) of the Constitution, unequivocally authorises the State Govt. in para 3 of the Order for organisation of local cadres for different parts of the State. Sub-para (7) of Para 3, itself stipulates that in the matter of organising a separate cadre in respect of any category of posts, in any dept. for any part of the State, nothing stated in the Presidential Order can be deemed to prevent the State Govt. from such act. Such power has been designedly conferred upon the State Govt. to achieve the main objective for which art. 371-D of the Constitution was engrafted, viz. to provide equitable opportunities to different areas of the State, in the matter of employment and career prospects in public services, and for achieving the aforesaid objective, the State Govt. would be in possession of all dates and materials, enabling it to organise different local cadres. Tribunal committed serious error in interpreting different provisions of the Presidential Order and the notifications of the State Govt., issued in exercise of powers conferred upon it under paragraph 3(1) of the Presidential Order, and accordingly, the said order of the Tribunal is set aside. The conclusions of the Tribunal, on interpreting the provisions of the Presidential Order, on the face of it, are erroneous and cannot be sustained. Appeals allowed.
