India Power Engineer Federation and others v Sasan Power Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 919; (2017) 1 SCC 487; 2017 (3) MahLJ 57; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 352A; 2016(12) SCALE 553; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Waiver, Consumer, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Rates, Tariff, Testing, Licensee, Main, Energy, Engineer, Electricity Act, 2003, Voltage, Generating Company, Station, System, Power System

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 1, 62, 63 - Electricity Act, 2002, ss. 125, 28, 29, 61, 62, 63 - Award of Contract - Waiver of Right - Central govt. in exercise of powers u/s. 63 of the Act issued guidelines for tariff based competitive bid process initiated by distribution licensees and 'X' was elected as nodal agency and respondent was incorporated to implement purpose - Respondent awarded contract to 'Y' and PPA was executed between the parties - Commission decided matter with regard to commercial operation date (COD) in favour of appellant - Appellate Tribunal in appeal by respondent determined date of COD - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, u/s. 63of 1872 Act, it is open to a promisee to dispense with or remit, wholly or in part, the performance of the promise made to him or he can accept instead of it any satisfaction which he thinks fit. Under the Indian law neither consideration nor an agreement would be necessary to constitute waiver. 'Waiver' is an intentional relinquishment of a known right or advantage, benefit, claim or privilege which except for such waiver the party would have enjoyed. Further, if any element of public interest is involved and a waiver takes place by one of the parties to an agreement, such waiver will not be given effect to if it is contrary to such public interest. Further, whenever waiver is pleaded it is for the party pleading the same to show that an agreement waiving the right in consideration of some compromise came into being. Statutory right, however, may also be waived by his conduct. In instant case, it is clear that the moment electricity tariff gets affected, the consumer interest comes in and public interest gets affected. Even if a waiver is claimed of some of the provisions of the PPA, such waiver, if it affects tariffs that are ultimately payable by the consumer, would necessarily affect public interest and would have to pass muster of the Commission u/ss. 61 to 63 of 2002 Act. This is for the reason that what is adopted by the Commission under Section 63 is only a tariff obtained by competitive bidding in conformity with guidelines issued. Hence, impugned order passed by Appellate Tribunal is set aside and the order that of Commission is reinstated. Appeals allowed.
