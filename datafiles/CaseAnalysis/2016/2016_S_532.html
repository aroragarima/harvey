Axis Bank v Sus Sbs Organics Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 304; AIR 2016 SC 2024; 2016(3) ALL MR 906; 2016 (116) ALR 865; 2016 (4) ALT 6; 2016 (4) AWC 3470; [2016] 196 Comp Cas 236; 2016 (3) CompLJ 449; 2016 (4) CTC 92; 2016 (1) DRTC 761; JT 2016 (4) SC 293; 2016 (3) MLJ 854; 2016 (132) RD 507; 2016(4) SCALE 296; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 920

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Central Excise Act, 1944, Contract, Memorandum Of Appeal, Consumer Protection Act, 1986, Borrower, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Aggrieved, Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002, Secured Debt, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Attachment, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Banking & Finance - Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 17, 18 - Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002, r. 11 - Withdrawal of deposit amount - In compliance of proviso u/s.18 of the Act, respondent no.1 made deposit of Rs.50 Lakh before Appellate Tribunal - However, pending appeal, Securitization Application itself was disposed of by Tribunal setting aside sale - Hence, as appeal did not survive, respondent no.1 sought permission to withdraw appeal and for refund of deposit of Rs.50 Lakh - Permission was granted subject to disposal of appeal and hence, respondent no.1 moved HC and the same was disposed of by Single Judge setting aside condition and permitted respondent no.1 to withdraw amount unconditionally - On appeal, DB upheld order of Single Judge and hence, instant appeal by bank - Whether deposit amount could be returned to respondent no.1.

Held, appeal u/s. 18 of the Act is permissible only against the order passed by Tribunal u/s.17 of the Act. The partial deposit before Appellate Tribunal as a pre-condition for considering appeal on merits in terms of s.18 of the Act, is not a secured asset and it is not a secured debt either, since the borrower or the aggrieved person has not created any security interest on such pre-deposit in favour of secured creditor. As such, on disposal of the appeal, either on merits or on withdrawal, or on being rendered infructuous, in case, the appellant makes a prayer for refund of the pre-deposit, the same has to be allowed and the pre-deposit has to be returned unless Appellate Tribunal had already appropriated pre deposit. Further, respondent no.1 had in fact sought withdrawal of the appeal, since the appellant had already proceeded against the secured assets by the time the appeal came up for consideration on merits. There is neither any order of appropriation during the pendency of the appeal nor any attachment on the pre-deposit. Hence, the deposit made by respondent no.1 is to be returned. Appeal dismissed.
