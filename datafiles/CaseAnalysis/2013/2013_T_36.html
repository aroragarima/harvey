Yakub Abdul Razak Memon v The State Of Maharashtra, Through Cbi , Bombay

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 171; (2013) 13 SCC 1; JT 2013 (5) SC 142; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 129; 2013(3) SCALE 565

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Curable, Joint Trial, Deem, Mitigating Circumstance, Approver, Respectable, Coast Guard Act, 1978, Quran, Job, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (P) Rules, 1987, Special Court (Trial of offences relating to prosecutions in Securities), Act, 1992, Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Ordinance, 1987

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, 3(3), 5 and 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 120B - Arms Act, 1959 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 353, 354, 362 and 363 - Bomb blast - Illegal arms - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (A1) committed offences u/ss. 3 and 4 r/w. s. 6 of 1884 Act by providing premises, having procured, concealed, aided and abetted Tiger Memon and his associates for smuggling arms, ammunitions and explosives into the country for commission of terrorist act and also by having in his possession and control explosive substances like handgrenades and detonators with intent, and by means thereof, to endanger the lives and for causing serious damage to property in India and to enable his co- conspirators to do such acts - A1 was convicted and sentenced to death u/ss. 3(3), 5 and 6 of 1987 Act, s. 120-B IPC and ss. 3, 4 and 6 of 1884 Act - Hence instant appeal - A1, contended that in the absence of whole judgment for perusal of the accused, the sentence imposed could not be sustained - In support of the claim, A1 relied on ss. 353, 354, 362 and 363 of CrPC - A1 further pointed out that only 'operative portion' was read out and after hearing the accused, conviction and sentence was imposed - As per the operative portion, A1 was convicted u/ss. 3(3), 5 and 6 of 1987 Act r/w. s. 120-B IPC and ss. 3, 4 and 6 of 1884 Act - A1 further pointed out that after convicting and sentencing A1, Presiding Officer stated that the reasons would be given within two months which showed that, admittedly, the judgment was not ready on the date of the pronouncement - A-1 was awarded death sentence, but no special reasons were assigned by the Designated Court and he was not even furnished the whole judgment - By highlighting various aspects on the issue, in view of the fact that the judgment pronounced was not full judgment A1 prayed for remand to the Special Court to go through all the reasoning and hear afresh on the question of sentence - Held, s. 354(1)(c) of CrPC stated that every judgment referred to in s. 353 of CrPC would specify the offence of which, and the section of IPC or other law under which, the accused was convicted and the punishment to which he was sentenced - In view of the same, the judgment u/s. 353(1)(c) of CrPC was to be pronounced only after the sentence in case where conviction was determined - Process of delivery of judgment included the determination of guilt, or otherwise, of accused and in the event of such guilt being established, also included the process of sentencing the accused - It was pointed out that the judgment was reserved on 23-11-2003 - Till 2006, Court proceeded to formulate its reasons and make judicial determination of guilt or otherwise in respect of each accused - Process of delivery of judgment commenced on 12-9-2006 when the Court pronounced its verdict on the guilt or otherwise of specific accused - Whilst doing so, Designated Judge explained the offences for which the accused were being convicted and invited the accused persons to make their statements with reference to the quantum of sentence - It was evident that at this stage, the detailed reasoning might not have been finally communicated to the accused, but the determination of the Court as well as the broad understanding of the operative part of the judgment was communicated - In case there was objection on the part of the accused regarding not knowing the reasons for his conviction, it contextually meant that he had not been made aware as to the specific pieces of evidence or marshalling of facts which led to his conviction - In view of the same, there was no illegality or irregularity in the process followed and specifically u/s. 353, 354 and 235 of CrPC keeping in mind the magnitude of the task before the Designated Judge inasmuch as he was trying 123 accused persons and had to deliver judgment which ran in about 4,300 pages - SC held that the pronouncement of the judgment was in compliance with the above said provisions of the Code and did not violate any of the provisions of CrPC as contended by the appellant - It was also clear from the reasoning of the Designated Court that by adopting the same procedure, Designated Judge conveyed the conclusion with regard to various charges leveled against other accused (convicted total accused 100) and also apprised each one of them including A1 the reasoning and other materials for arriving at such conclusion as well as their pleaders - Designated Judge also apprised that because the convicted accused were 100 in number and the common judgment was running into thousands of pages, it might require some time and as soon as the full judgment would be made ready, same would be supplied to them free of cost - It did not mean that on the date of pronouncing the decision (decision was pronounced on various dates), the whole judgment was not ready or incomplete - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, 3(2)(i)(ii) and 3(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 149 - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 3, 4 and 6 - Bomb blast - Illegal arms - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant A32 along with other co-accused went to Mahim Slope on 12-3-1993 and threw hand grenades on the hutments, which exploded causing death of three persons and injuries to six persons and damage to property worth Rs.50,000/- and thereby committed offence punishable u/s. 3(2)(i)(ii) of 1987 Act r/w. s. 149 IPC - A32 was convicted and sentenced to death by the Designated Court u/s. 3(3) of 1987 Act - Hence instant appeal - A32 contended that the appellants joined the company of Tiger Memon and had gone to Dubai and Pakistan due to circumstance and by force - A32 also contended that they had no intention to go to Dubai and to attend weapons training at Islamabad - Their main aim was to secure some job at Dubai - He also pointed out that though they participated in the smuggling activities of the Tiger Memon group, they had no intention to involve in the terrorist act as claimed by the prosecution - According to A32 though the prosecution had relied on their own confessional statements inasmuch as all of them have retracted from their statements, there was no need to give importance to those confessions - A32 also prayed that considering their poverty and also that at the relevant time they were in search of some job for their livelihood, death sentence imposed by the Special Court was not warranted - Held, perusal of the confessional statements of A23, A29, A49, A52, A57, A64, A94, A98 and A100 established the fact that A32 was present at Al- Hussaini Building in the night intervening 11-3-1993 and 12-3-1993 and actively participated in filling of RDX in the cavities of vehicles which were later planted at various targets in Bombay causing irreparable damage to life and property - It was also sufficiently established that they went to Pakistan via Dubai and received training in handling of arms and ammunitions at the hands of Tiger Memon and all of them also took oath on holy Quran to take revenge for the riots and to keep the training in Pakistan secret - It was also sufficiently proved that in pursuance of the said conspiracy, A32 threw hand grenades in Fishermen's Colony causing death of 3 persons and injuring 6 others - Perusal of the deposition of PW2, PW5, PW6 and PW13 established and fully corroborated the confessions of A32 in all material particulars that A32 played active and crucial role in achieving the object of the conspiracy - It further corroborated the fact that they travelled to Pakistan and received training in handling of arms and ammunitions, explosive substances and throwing of hand grenades - They attended meetings at Dubai and in India and also took oath on holy Quran to take revenge - They participated in filling RDX in vehicles which were used to cause explosions at targets and hurled hand grenades at innocent people in Fishermen's colony - PW2, PW5, PW6 and PW13 had identified A32 in Test Identification Parade - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, 3(2)(ii) and 3(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 120B, 324, 436 and 307, 435, 436 and 511 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3 and 4(a)(b) - Bomb blast - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant A44 committed offence punishable u/s. 3(3) of 1987 Act by committing following overt acts - A44 attended meeting with co-conspirators at hotel and surveyed Stock Exchange Building and Municipal Corporation Building, along with co-accused for the purpose of committing terrorist acts by planting bombs -

On 12-3-1993, A44 planted suitcase filled with RDX in hotel which exploded, causing injuries to three persons and loss of property to the tune of Rs.2.1 crore, thereby committing offence punishable u/s. 3(2)(ii) of 1987 Act and ss. 324, 436 and 307 of IPC and s. 4(a)(b) of 1908 Act - On 12-3-1993, A44 planted explosive laden scooter with intent to cause death and destruction of properties by explosion and thereby committed offence punishable u/s. 3(3) of 1987 Act and ss. 435, 436 r/w. s. 511 of IPC - A44 also committed offence punishable u/s. 3 of 1908 Act - A44 was sentenced to death u/s. 3(3) of 1987 Act and s. 120-B of IPC for the offences - Hence instant appeal - A44 contended that confession of the appellant dt. 26-5-1993 and 28-5-1993 should not be relied upon since it was sought to be retracted by the appellant on 7-6-1994 - Held, confession of A44 established the charges framed against him in the trial - Fact that A44 knowingly committed the overt act of planting the bomb at hotel was evident from his own confession - A44 himself informed that he had planted the bag containing bomb at hotel - A44 was fully conscious of the gravity and diabolic nature of his act which was apparent from his confession wherein he stated that after committing the overt acts he himself informed everything to A16 who consoled him by saying that God would help him as he has done all this for his community - It was also relevant to point out that the retraction allegedly made by the appellant also failed to pin-point the reason behind failure to make complaint to the authorities or police officers or any other authority including the court regarding his signatures being obtained on blank papers and/or the papers containing some typed material and the reason to effect the said signatures - Designated Court rightly relied upon the original confession and discarded the subsequent retraction - Since SC had elaborately discussed the admissibility or otherwise of the retraction statements there was no need to refer the same once again - Appeals disposed of.
