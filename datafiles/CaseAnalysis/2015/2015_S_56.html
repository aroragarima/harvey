Sant Ram v Dhan Kaur and others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 736; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 130; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 282; JT 2015 (9) SC 382; 2015(11) SCALE 629; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 1029

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Mesne Profits, Supreme Court Rules, 1966

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Mense Profit -Alleged giving up of right - Determination - Predecessor-in-interest and wife of applicant purchased plot in question vide registered sale deed - Further, right to recover damages was also granted as it was noticed that predecessor-in-interest of respondents had encroached portion of said land and had also illegally constructed room thereon - Further, suit was passed in favor of applicant for possession of plot in question along with interest - Further, with respect to mense profit, Lower Court fixed mense profit as Rs. 200/-p.m. - However, appellate Court disposed of appeal and directed respondents to pay mesne profits to applicant @ Rs.30/- p.m. with 10% increase every three years from date of filing of suit till 21-8-1990 together with interest @ 12% - Further, it is stated that SC vide order dt. 21-4-2009 recorded statement of applicant with respect to giving up claim of mense profit and closed matter - Further, applicant filed application u/s. 151 of CPC for complying with its order earlier order and to execute said order of mesne profits as confirmed by HC - Further, objections were raised by respondent against demand of mense profit - Applicant filed application before SC for clarification of Sc's order dt. 21-4-2009 - Whether applicant is entitled to mense profit.

Held, question before SC was whether applicant was entitled to claim mesne profits at rate of Rs.200/- p.m. Since applicant was already awarded mesne profits at rate of Rs.30/- p.m. with 10% increase every three years from date of filing of suit till 21-8-1990 together with interest @ 12% at amount accruing due month after month, which was not under challenge at instance of respondents, hence, question was whether applicant was entitled to claim mesne profits for balance amount, i.e. Rs.170/- p.m. from respondents. SC held that it is this claim, i.e., Rs. 170/- p.m., which was given up by applicant that being subject matter of petition which SC recorded and accordingly disposed of applicant's petition by order dt. 21-4-2009. SC held that its order dt. 21-4-2004 could not have been construed so as to deprive applicant to claim mesne profits at rate of Rs.30/- p.m. with 10% increase as sthis claim which was already adjudicated in applicant's favour by Courts below and which was neither challenged by respondents and nor was it subject matter of dispute in applicant's petition, same could not be held to have been given up by applicant by order dt. 21-4-2009. SC held that since in petition before SC, issue with regard to award of mesne profits at rate of 30/- p.m. was not subject matter at instance of any party to lis, question of its giving up at instance of applicant did not arise. Hence, applicant's right to lciam mense profit at Rs. 30 p.m. with increase remains intact for recovery from respondents and is not affected in any manner by order dt. 21-4-2009. Executing Court is directed to take up applicant's execution application and decide same in accordance with law in light of clarification made hereinabove of order dt. 21-4-2009. Application allowed.
