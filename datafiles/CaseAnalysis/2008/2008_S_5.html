(1) Gobarbhai Naranbhai Singala; (2) Jayeshbhai @ v (1) State of Gujarat and Others; (2) Jayrajsinh

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 118; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 743; (2008) 3 SCC 775; AIR 2008 SC 1134; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1354; 2008 CRLJ 1618; 2008 (3) G.L.R. 2192; JT 2008 (2) SC 96; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 903; 2008 (3) RLW 2067; 2008(2) SCALE 82; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 302, 307, 143, 147, 148, 149, 341, 120B, 201; Arms Act, ss. 25(1) A, (1-a), 27; Bombay Police Act, s. 135 - Appeals seeking cancellation of the bail granted to the respondents - Held, learned Judge, who incidentally happens to be the same Judge who had declined to release the respondent on bail earlier, did not advert to any of the reasons given by him declining to release the respondent on bail - There was no change of circumstances - Reasons given by the learned Judge in the impugned order for grant of bail are untenable - When the basic requirements necessary for grant of bail are completely ignored by the High Court, this Court would be justified in canceling the bail - High Court has completely ignored the general principles, for grant of bail in a heinous crime of commission of murder in which the sentence, if convicted, is death or life imprisonment - Appeals allowed.
