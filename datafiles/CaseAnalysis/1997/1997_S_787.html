Surendra Kumar Jain v Royce Pereira

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 702; (1997) 8 SCC 759; AIR 1998 SC 394; 1998 (2) Bom.C.R. 300; JT 1997 (9) SC 185; 1997(7) SCALE 88; 1998 (1) SLT 31; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 221; 1997 (9) Supreme 438

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1947, ss. 5(4A), 5(6A) - Accommodation - Paying guest - Suit for possession - Grant of decree - Sustainability - Respondent filed suit, in 1979, for possession against appellant of one room adjoining kitchen on ground floor of bungalow and for arrears of paying guest charges till 31-12-1978 and for mesne profits from 1-1-1979 till vacant possession was granted - Appellant too filed suit for declaration that he was tenant in respect of bathroom - Trial Court disposed of both suits by common judgment holding that appellant was not paying guest but tenant from beginning and respondent's suit was dismissed - On appeal, appellate court allowed appeals preferred by respondent - Appellant filed two petitions before HC, against said judgment, which dismissed the same - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that he was in exclusive possession of bed room no. 3, dining hall, bathroom and pantry as tenant - Whether owner was required to reside physically in remaining accommodation on ground floor - Held, Trial Court, in instant case, came to conclusion that in view of language in s. 5(6A) of the 1947 Act defining 'paying guest' and requirement of licensor must 'reside', the owner must establish that he was also residing in very room in which the paying guest was staying - This view was not warranted by words in s. 5(6A) of the Act defining 'paying guest' - It would not be necessary that owner should physically reside in remaining accommodation on ground floor - Appellant was using part of the premises on licence and respondent was residing in remaining part of the same premises de jure in ground floor and de facto in first floor and whether ground floor was taken as 'premises' or both floors were taken as 'premises', s. 5(6A) of the Act was satisfied - Appellant was only paying guest - If so, he was not licensee and as he was not 'licensee' as on 1-2-1979, he should not claim to be deemed tenant - Appeals dismissed.
