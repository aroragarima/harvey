Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore v Gurunath V. Dhakappa

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 278; [1969] 72 ITR 192

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(4), 16(4) - Hindu Undivided Family Business - Partnership Business - Income from Family Assets - Respondent belonging to Hindu undivided family becomes a partner in a firm and was doing business - Appellant proceeded against respondent that everything received must be treated as income of respondent - HC found against revenue that Income did not belong to respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether partnership income be treated as income of Hindu undivided family - Held, no - Income was not received from assets of family utilized in partnership - Income cannot be treated as the income of the Hindu undivided family - Appeal dismissed.
