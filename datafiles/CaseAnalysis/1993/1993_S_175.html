Maharashtra Tubes Limited v State Industrial and Investment Corporation of Maharashtra Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 870; (1993) 2 SCC 144; AIR 1993 SCW 991; [1993] 78 Comp Cas 803; 1994 (2) CompLJ 346; JT 1993 (1) SC 320; 1993(1) SCALE 223; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 340; 1993 (1) UJ 447

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Exchange Control & FDI

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986

Summary: Corporate - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 - Companies Act, 1956 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Whether in a case where an industrial concern makes any default in repayment of any loan or advance or any installment thereof or otherwise fails to meet its obligations under terms of any agreement with Financial Corporation, such as respondent herein, can latter take recourse of ss. 29 and/or 31 of State Financial Corporations Act, 1951 notwithstanding bar of s. 22 of SICA, 1985? - Held, where an inquiry is pending u/s. 16/17 or an appeal is pending u/s. 25 of 1985 Act there should be cessation of coercive activities of type mentioned in s. 22(1) to permit BIFR to consider what remedial measures it should take with respect to sick industrial company - Expression 'proceedings' in s. 22(1), therefore, cannot be confined to legal proceedings understood in narrow sense of proceedings in a court of law or a legal tribunal for attachment and sale of debtor's property - Appeal allowed.
