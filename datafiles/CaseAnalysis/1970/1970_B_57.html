Burmah Shell Oil Storage & Distribution Coof India v Burmah Shell Management Staff Association& Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 301; (1970) 3 SCC 378; AIR 1971 SC 922; 1971 (22) FLR 11; 1971 LabIC 699; 1970 (2) LLJ 590; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Demonstrations, Manual Labour, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Air Force Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Members of the respondent trade union were classified into various categories, out of which ten categories were disputed in the offence - The six categories namely, Transport Engineer, District Engineers, Foreman (Chemicals), Fuelling Superintendents, Chemists, Sales Engineering Representatives were held to be workmen by the decision of the Tribunal whereas four categories namely, Blending Supervisors, Foremen, Depot Superintendents, District Sales Representatives were held not to be workmen by the decision of the Tribunal - Appellant challenged the decision of the Tribunal in respect of the six categories held to be workmen and respondent challenged the correctness of the decision of the Tribunal in respect of the four categories held not to be workmen - Whether, the Tribunal was right while classifying the members of the respondent trade union - Held, instant court reconsidered the classification of the disputed ten categories in the light of work condition and nature of the work of each of the disputed category and gave its decision accordingly for each category - Tribunal was not in whole error while classifying the members of the respondent trade union, hence, appeal of the each party was allowed in part - Appeals disposed of.
