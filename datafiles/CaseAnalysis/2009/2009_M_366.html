Musauddin Ahmed v The State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 907; (2009) 14 SCC 541; AIR 2010 SC 3813; 2009 (3) JCC 2002; JT 2009 (9) SC 65; 2009(9) SCALE 155; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1135; 2009 (3) UC 1361

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Charge- Sheet

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction by Trial Court - Conviction upheld by High Court, but sentence reduced - Convictions whether proper? - Held, No - Prosecution version contradictory - Altogether different case put up before HC - For age, parents not examined but only employers of prosecutrix - Character of prosecutrix doubted; found to be habituated of sex - No independent witness - Torn clothes not recovered; no effort to take the semen, blood samples etc. from the appellant - Impugned judgments set aside - Appellant acquitted from the charge u/s. 376 - Appeal allowed.
