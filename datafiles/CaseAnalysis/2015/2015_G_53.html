City Municipal Council Bhalki, by its Chief Officer v Gurappa (D) by Lrs and another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 665; (2016) 2 SCC 200; AIR 2015 SC 3826; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 146; 2015(6) ALL MR 476; 2015 (113) ALR 720; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 22; 2016 (4) CTC 208; JT 2015 (9) SC 1; 2016 (1) KarLJ 466; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3753; 2016 (5) MahLJ 1; 2016 (3) MPLJ 533; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 637; 2016 (130) RD 447; 2015(10) SCALE 297

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Res Judicata, Municipality

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Title of Property - Suit Res Judicata - Determination - Land in question belonged to ancestors of respondent no.1 - Further, part of land came to be held by State Govt. - However, respondent no.1 retained ownership of land, however, during revision of survey and resettlement of lands in village, discrepancies had crept in while preparing new revenue records and that due to wrong entries, names of owners and their actual possession did not tally with survey numbers - Further, lower Court passed order in favor of respondent no.1 - Further, Trial court reversed order passed in favor of respondent no.1 on grounds that suit is also not maintainable as it is barred by res judicata - Further, HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and passed order in favor of respondent no.1 - Appellant contended that suit is barred by res judicata - Whether suit filed before lower court is barred by res judicata.

Held, deceased respondent no.1 had filed suit in which was dismissed. Thereafter, he filed suit before lower Court, after impleading certain other defendants therein and changing description of suit property. SC held that neither parties, nor subject matter was same in earlier suit. Further, SC held that since neither reliefs claimed in two suits were identical, nor parties are same and nor could decision in first suit said to have been on merits, it cannot be held in singular facts and circumstances that suit in question was barred by res judicata. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Title of property - Determination - Whether deceased respondent no.1 has succeeded in proving his title over ownership of suit property.

Held, on perusal of material evidence on record, legal heirs of deceased respondent no.1 herein have succeeded in establishing their title to suit property. Further, respondent no.1 has established before lower Court and HC that he was owner of property in question. Further, he produced before lower court khasra patrak for village for year 1954-55 and on basis of same, lower Court held that land in question was ancestral property of deceased respondent no.1. Further, he produced extract of revision survey register and copy of village map. Further, complainant had admitted in plaint in that suit that there was interchange and that he was wrongly shown as owner of land when infact he was owner of land of other land. Hence, deceased respondent no.1 has established his title to suit property. Further, appellant Municipality has not produced any document to prove their title to suit property, hence, it did not have any right to confer better title upon suit property upon other defendants in suit. Hence, SC held that impugned order passed by HC is confirmed by SC. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Title of property cannot be denied when owner has produced evidence on record and is based on testimony of witness.
