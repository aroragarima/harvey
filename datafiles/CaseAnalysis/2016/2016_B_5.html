B. H. Khawas v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 583; (2016) 8 SCC 715; AIR 2016 SC 4027; 2016(6) ALL MR 437; 2016 (5) AWC 4818; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 14; JT 2016 (8) SC 118; 2016(7) SCALE 700; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Writ Petition, Post, Appointment

Summary: Service - Termination - Challenged - Appellant was appointed as "Chemical Examiner Grade-I" in Customs and Central Excise Dept. - Dept. terminated appellant, consequent to decision of Scrutiny Committee that appellant belonged to caste which is not a ST - Aggrieved, appellant filed an application before Tribunal challenging impugned order - Tribunal allowed application and directed re-instatement of appellant in service by setting aside order of termination - Dept. filed writ petition before HC - DB allowed petition relying on the case of Union of India v. Dattatray s/o Namdeo Mendhekar and Ors, 2008 Indlaw SC 628, and restored termination order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appointment of appellant to the post of "Chemical Examiner" in Customs and Central Excise Dept. has become final.

Held, appellant is not entitled for any relief on finding that his appointment as Chemical Examiner in Dept. vide appointment had not attained finality. Scrutiny Committee has finally answered the factum of caste claim of appellant on the basis of relevant material, which is indicative of fact that in the relevant official record pertaining to even the close relatives of appellant (grandfather and uncle), caste recorded is "Koshti" and occupation shown as weaving separately. For, caste "Koshti" is neither a synonym nor part of a notified ST "Halba" in State. Appellant has allowed that decision of Scrutiny Committee dt. 10-2-2003/22-4-2004 to attain finality. Scrutiny Committee has unambiguously held that appellant does not belong to "Halba" Community, a notified ST in State. Therefore, HC was right in allowing petition filed by dept. and to restore the termination order. Appeal dismissed.
