Â Hindustan Forest Company v Lal Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1959 SC 1349; 1960 SCJ 41; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Limitation Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Jammu and Kashmir Limitation Act, art. 115 - Whether suit was governed by art. 115 of Act? - Transactions on each side creating independent obligations on other and not merely transactions which create obligations on one side, those on other being merely complete or partial discharges of such obligations - Held, there was no reciprocity of dealings; there were no independent obligations - Sellers were under an obligation to deliver goods but that obligation arose from contract and not from payment of advance alone - When contract provided that advance was towards price to become due, it followed by necessary implication that advance had to be adjusted against price when it became due - There was a provision in contract for adjusting advance - There is no application of art. 115 - Appeal allowed.
