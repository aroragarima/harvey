Calcutta Jute Manufacturing Company and Another v Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2174; (1997) 6 SCC 262; AIR 1997 SC 2920; 1997 (93) E.L.T. 657; JT 1997 (5) SC 690; 1997(4) SCALE 411; 1997 (106) STC 433; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 474; 1997 (6) Supreme 256

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act 1941, s.10A - Interest on turnover tax - (A) Failure to furnish return as envisaged in s. 10A(2) - Liability to pay interest - (B) Liability for period recovery of tax is stayed by order of interim injunction passed by High Court - In writ challenging s. 6B imposing tax - Held, dealer is liable to pay interest - Appellant is liable to pay interest u/s. 10A for period during which recovery of amount was stayed by orders of HC, moreso, when HC finally upheld validity of s. 6B - Appeal dismissed.
