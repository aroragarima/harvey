State of Madhya Pradesh and others v Mala Banerjee

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 196; (2015) 7 SCC 698; 2015 (6) AWC 6129; 2015 (146) FLR 72; 2015 (2) JabLJ 44; 2015(3) M.P.H.T. 8; 2016 (1) MPLJ 246; 2015(3) SCALE 721; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Revision Of Pay, Madhya Pradesh Revision of Pay Rules, 1990, Madhya Pradesh Revision of Pay Rules, Benefit Scheme

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Applicability of Scheme - Discrimination - Respondents claimed benefits of Kramonnati Scheme with effect from 19-4-1999 but appellants assert that said scheme would be made applicable with effect from 1-8-2003 only and so, respondents approached HC wherein impugned judgment in favour of respondents was passed - Hence, instant appeals by appellants - Whether impugned judgment is sustainable.

Held, appellants failed to explain why teachers were denied benefit of second Kramonnati Scheme while all other employees were granted said benefit and thus discriminating against teachers and violating their fundamental rights enshrined u/arts.14 and 16 of the Constitution. Further, no explanation was given for granting second Kramonnati benefit from 1-8-2003 which date is neither in the original scheme nor justifiable on basis of any other material available on record. Further, many employees had completed twenty four years of service by 1999 and hence, in postponing their second Kramonnati by four years, appellants departed from basic object of Scheme. The order dt.3-9-2005 of State Govt. to extend benefit of Scheme from 1-8-2003 failed to explain basis of decision and hence arbitrary in nature and discriminatory towards respondents and others in their position. Hence, there is no illegality in impugned judgment and so, appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per decision of SC, where a policy is contrary to law or is in violation of the provisions of the Constitution or is arbitrary or irrational, Courts should, in pursuance of Constitutional duties, strike down the same.
