State of Madhya Pradesh v Badri Yadav and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 106; (2006) 9 SCC 549; AIR 2006 SC 1769; 2006 CRLJ 2128; 2006 (2) CTC 538; 2006 (1) JCC 593; JT 2006 (4) SC 154; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 468; 2006(3) SCALE 669; 2006 (4) SCJ 756; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 193, 302, 34 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 164, 233, 311 - Perjury - Trial Court convicted respondents-accused and sentenced RI for life and a fine of Rs. 200/- - But HC acquitted respondents - Appeal against acquittal - Held, there is no provision in CrPC that by filing affidavit witnesses examined as PWs (PW-8 and PW-9 in this case) could be juxtaposed as DW-1 and DW2- and be examined as defence witnesses on behalf of accused - Subsequent statements as defence witnesses were concocted well an after thought - HC was not justified in reversing conviction recorded by Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
