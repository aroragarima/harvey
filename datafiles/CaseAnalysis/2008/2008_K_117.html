Kanta Devi and Others v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2040; (2008) 15 SCC 201; AIR 2008 SC 3107; 2008(5) ALL MR 969; 2008 (4) AWC 3752; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 609; 2008(10) SCALE 242; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 367; 2008 (3) UC 1417

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 279, 304-A - Murder - Conviction - Maintainability - Appellant was convicted for offences punishable u/ss. 279, 304-A IPC by Trial Court - Appeal was preferred before Appellant Court which was dismissed - Aggrieved by same revision petition was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether revision petition filed by appellant was maintainable - Held, HC by a cryptic order dismissed revision petition - Further, it had not noticed various submissions made by appellant - Thus, matter to be remitted back to HC for fresh consideration and order of HC to be set aside - Appeal disposed of.
