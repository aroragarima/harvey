K. M. Shanmugam v S. R. V. S. Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 303; AIR 1963 SC 1626; 1964 (2) AnWR 13; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 13; 1964 (2) SCJ 120; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles (Madras Amendment) Act, 1948 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Whether State Transport Appellate Tribunal committed an error of law apparent on face of record? - Held, disobedience of instructions which are administrative in nature may not afford a cause of action to an aggrieved party, but transgression of statutory law certainly does - Whatever conflict there may be, on which we do not express any opinion, in a tax law or company law, in context of marking system and evaluation of an amenity in interest of public, it is obviously an untenable proposition to hold that even if a company has a well equipped office on a route in respect of which a permit is applied for, it shall be ignored if company has some other branch somewhere unconnected with that route - That was what Appellate Tribunal held and in our view it is an error apparent on face of record - Appeal dismissed.
