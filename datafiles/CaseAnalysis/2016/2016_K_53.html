Yatin Narendra Oza v Khemchand Rajaram Koshti and others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1000; (2016) 9 SCC 343; AIR 2016 SC 4099; JT 2016 (9) SC 289; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 990; 2016(8) SCALE 531; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 756

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, Apology, Association

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Unwarranted speeches - Contempt - Apology - Appellant filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC whereby HC held issued notice under the Act to appellant/contemnor directing him not to indulge in any kind of scandalous activity or holding official meeting and passing resolution on the subject matter as could be said to be contemptuous as per the verdict of SC in C. Ravichandran Iyer v. Justice A.M. Bhattacharjee & Ors., 1995 Indlaw SC 711.

Held, SC emphasise the necessity of dignified behavior, obedience to the norms of professional ethics and sustenance of decorum of institution, for all combined stabilize the nobility of profession and ensure the faith in the justice delivery system which was extremely dear to a civilized society. However, apart from being critical of irresponsible proclivity of appellant, has also expressed his anguish that he was been indulging in similar activities and giving interviews to the electronic media. Appellant was clarified that he should neither speak nor give any kind of interview to either electronic or print media on the subject in question. Appellant was repentant and repentance was sincere and regret, honest. The tenor of affidavits was unmistakably relatable to expression of regret and unconditional apology. SC treat the apology offered by appellant to be sincere and accordingly exonerate him. Needless to say, if appellant would speak in the tenor he has spoken, that might tantamount to ex facie contempt of Court. SC expect appellant to constantly remind himself that the standing and dignity of Court matter to the nation and also to the collective. Appeal disposed of.
