State Of Tamil Nadu v Thirukkural Perumal

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 577; (1995) SCC (Cr) 387; (1995) 2 SCC 449; 1995 (2) CCR 8; 1995 (1) Crimes 566; JT 1995 (3) SC 166; 1995(1) SCALE 423; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 712; 1995 (1) UJ 456

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, s. 482 - Quashing of FIR - First information report lodged against against respondents for offences u/ss. 109, 147, 148, 342, 323, 395 and 500 - Investigation was started and some evidence was collected by police - Respondent filed a petition u/s. 482 for quashing FIR which was allowed - Whether order of quashing FIR by High Court was justified - Held, no - While quashing proceedings, HC has placed reliance upon evidence collected by police during investigation - HC erred in evaluating genuineness and reliability of allegations made in FIR on basis of evidence collected - Reliance upon evidence which is yet to be produced before Trial Court, is not proper - Power of quashing a FIR and criminal proceeding should be exercised sparingly by courts - Normal process of criminal trial cannot be cut short in a casual manner - Order of HC not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
