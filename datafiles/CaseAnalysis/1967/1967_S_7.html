Â State of Mysore v S. R. Jayaram

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1968 SC 346; 1968 LabIC 357; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 349; 1968 SLR 92

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Meritorious, Mysore Recruitment of Gazetted Probationers Rules 1959

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1) and 309 - Mysore Recruitment Gazetted Probationers Rules, 1959, rr. 3, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9(2) - Admission to competetive exams - Validity of latter part of r. 9(2) of Rules 1959 framed by Governor in exercise of his powers under proviso to art. 309 - Held, latter part of r. 9(2) provides arbitrary power to Govt. in case of recruitment to offices and violative of art. 14 and 16(1) and it must be struck down - Order passed by High Court directing Govt. to decide to which post or cadre respondent should be appointed u/r. 9(2) is set aside - Direction issued to State of Mysore to appoint respondent to post of Assistant Commr. in Mysore Administrative Service - For purpose of seniority, respondent will be treated as appointed according to his rank in order of merit - Appeal dismissed.
