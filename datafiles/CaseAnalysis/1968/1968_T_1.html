Â Rattan Lal and Company and Another v Assessing Authority and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1970 SC 1742; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 544; 1970 (25) STC 136

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Collected, Central Sales Tax Act 1955

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act 1955, ss. 2 and 15 - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14 and 19 - Punjab General Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967 - Punjab Sales Tax (Haryana Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967, ss. 5 and 11 AA - Whether Punjab General Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act and Punjab Sales Tax (Haryana Amendment and Validation) Act are valid in law? - Acts by specifying stage as last purchase or sale by a dealer liable to pay tax makes stage quite clear and by giving an option to him not to include such transactions in his return saves him from liability to pay tax till he is dealer liable to pay tax - Present provisions of Acts are quite clear and are quite sufficient to make amended Act accord with Central Act - Acts have been amended in new States in relation to area of that State and it is inconceivable that this could not be within competence - It may be that when rate is applied resulting tax is somewhat higher but that does not offend against equality contemplated by art. 304 - That is consequence of ad valorem tax being levied at a particular rate - So long as rate is same, art. 304 is satisfied - Held, both Acts are valid - Petitions dismissed.
