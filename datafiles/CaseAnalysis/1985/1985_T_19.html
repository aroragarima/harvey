Jaswant Singh And Anr. v The Custodian Of Evacuee Property, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 221; (1985) 3 SCC 648; AIR 1985 SC 1096; 1985 (2) CCC 537; 1985 (28) DLT 333; 1985(1) SCALE 1173; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 331; 1986 (1) UJ 11

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land and Property - Practice and Procedure - CPC, 1908, ss. 11, 47, O. 21 r. 90 - The Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - East Punjab Evacuees' (Administration of Property) Act, 1947 - Execution proceedings - Applications for setting aside sale of evacuee property - Earlier application u/s. 8 of 1947 Act was dismissed - Applicability of rule of res judicata to subsequent application u/s. 17 of 1949 Ordinance - Appeal on the ground that order of lower Court having become final, it would operate as a bar to any enquiry into the application by the Custodian after the promulgation of Ordinance No. XII of 1949 and Ordinance No. XXVII of 1949 - Held, not barred by the rule - However, setting aside of the court sale does not come in the way of any other remedy open to the decree-holders to recover the amount which may be due under the decree from the judgment debtor or the Custodian or any other authority - Appeal dismissed.
