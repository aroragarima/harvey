Â Indian Aluminium Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 248; (1972) 2 SCC 150; AIR 1972 SC 1880; 1972 (1) CTR 51; [1972] 84 ITR 735; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 15; 1972 TAXLR 873

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Constitution, art. 141 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - (A) Review by Supreme Court of its earlier decision - When open - (B) Test for permissible deduction - Payment of wealth tax, if covered - Held, when certain aspects of the question involved in earlier case were not brought to the attention of the Supreme Court and remained unnoticed, and the decision in later case was not likely to cause any public inconvenience hardship or mischief, SC in later case would review its earlier decision if erroneous and affecting numerous persons - such payment is an permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Appeal allowed.
