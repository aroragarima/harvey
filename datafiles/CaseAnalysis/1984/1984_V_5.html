State of Andhra Pradesh v Vallabhapuram Ravi

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 349; (1984) 4 SCC 410; (1984) SCC (Cr) 635; AIR 1985 SC 870; 1984 (2) Crimes 552; 1984 CRLJ 1511; 1984 CrLR(SC) 397; 1984(2) SCALE 386; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Education; Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Andhra Borstal Schools Act, 1925, s. 10-A - Prisoners Act, 1900 - Prison Act, 1894 - Bombay Borstal Schools Act, 1929 - Whether on coming into force of s. 433 A, CrPC, an adolescent offender who is sentenced to imprisonment for life on being convicted of an offence for which death is also one of punishments prescribed by law and who later on by an order made by State Govt. is directed to be sent to a Borstal School under s.10-A of Andhra Borstal Schools Act, 1925 is liable to be kept in a Borstal School or in a prison at least for a period of fourteen years - Held, if a person detained in a Borstal School under s. 10-A of Act is to be retransferred to prison after he serves out full term of detention in School, it will defeat very object and purpose of Act - Applicability s. 433 A, CrPC, 1973 does not apply to person sent to Borstal School under s. 10 A of Act.
