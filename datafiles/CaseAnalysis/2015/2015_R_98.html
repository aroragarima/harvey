Central Bureau of Investigation v Rathin Dandapat and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 639; (2016) 1 SCC 507; AIR 2015 SC 3285; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3678; 2015 CRLJ 4488; 2015 (1) JCC 2631; 2015(9) SCALE 120

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Further Investigation, Police Remand

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167(2), 309(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 307, 326 - Absconding Accused - Remand in police custody by Investigating Officer - Justification - FIR in respect of offences punishable u/ss. 148, 149, 326, 307, 302 of IPC and also in respect of offences punishable u/s. 25, 27 of Act was lodged against respondent alleging that after forming unlawful assembly, respondent killed nine persons and injured large number of villagers - Investigation of case was initially done by regular police, but later transferred to Criminal Investigation Department (CID) of State - Further, vide order of HC, case was transferred to appellant i.e. CBI - On completion of investigation, CBI submitted charge sheet against 21 accused, including arrested ones and absconders - Trial Court framed charges against accused and out of eight proclaimed offenders, five, namely, were arrested on 29 -4-2014, whereafter on 30-4-2014 CBI sought their remand in police custody - However, Lower Court rejected prayer of CBI - Further, three other application was filed by CBI for remand in police custody of three other absconder accused which were also rejected - Whether remand in police custody can be given to investigating agency in respect of absconding accused who is arrested only after filing of charge sheet.

Held, Dinesh Dalmia v C.B.I., 2007 Indlaw SC 935, relied on SC held that HC is not justified in upholding refusal of remand in police custody by Lower Court, on ground that accused stood in custody after his arrest u/s. 309 of CrPC. Further, State Through Cbi v Dawood Ibrahim Kaskar & Ors., 1997 Indlaw SC 3282, relied on SC held that police remand can be sought u/s. 167(2) of CrPC in respect of accused arrested at stage of further investigation, if interrogation is needed by investigating agency. Further, expression 'accused if in custody' in s. 309(2) of CrPC does not include accused who is arrested on further investigation before supplementary charge sheet is filed. Hence, SC held that refusal of police remand in present case is against settled principle of law laid down by SC. Hence, impugned order passed by HC affirming order passed by Lower Court is set aside. Lower Court is directed to pass fresh orders on applications made by appellant before it relating to granting of police remand of respondents in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Remand in police custody can be given to investigating agency in respect of absconding accused who is arrested only after filing of charge sheet.
