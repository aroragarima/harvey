State of Uttar Pradesh v M. K. Anthony

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 19; (1985) SCC (Cr) 105; (1985) 1 SCC 505; AIR 1985 SC 48; 1985 CRLJ 493; 1985 CRLR 41; 1984(2) SCALE 728

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 162, 313 and 366 - Respondent committed murder of his wife and children as he was facing financial constraints - After murder, made extra judicial confession to witness - Respondent was convicted by Trail Court but conviction was set aside by HC rejecting evidence of prosecution witness - Whether HC justified in rejecting evidence of prosecution witness? - Held, no - Families as well respondent and prosecution witnesses were on very good terms - Testimony of witness is reliable, convincing and trustworthy - A hyper-technical approach should not be adopted by courts - Testimony of witness is further corroborated by neighbour of family to whom prosecution witness told about confession made by respondent - Confession of murders was illness of wife of deceased - Conviction upheld - Appeal allowed.
