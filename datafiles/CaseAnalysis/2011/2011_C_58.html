Colvin School Society and another v Anil Kumar Sharma and another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 726; (2012) 1 SCC 200; 2012 (3) AWC 2755

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Education

Keywords: U.P. Intermediate Education Act, Delhi Education Act, 1973

Summary: Education - Administrative - Societies Registration Act,1860 - Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act,1921 - Delhi Education Act,1973 - Constitution of India,1950,art.19 - Educational institution - Sought change of affiliation - Challenged - Validity - Appellant/educational society wanted to shift its affiliation from State Board to ICSE for some courses and sought No objection and was granted by State Govt. - ICSE granted affiliation - Appellant/college imparted education under both ICSE Board and State Board - As there were less students taking State Board and more opting to ICSE syllabus management decided to close down State Board Section and wanted to continue with ICSE syllabus - Further, informed same to State Govt. and published in local news paper - Challenged by teachers and parents by separate writs and PIL and were disposed of by common judgment by DB with some directions to appellant - Aggrieved, appellant filed instant appeals - Whether appellant was justified in closing down State Board section in accordance with law - Held, as appellant consented to start classes under State Board and assured to retain teachers who were earlier teaching in State Board Syllabus all cases were disposed of by amending judgment of HC dt.17-11-2004 and issued necessary directions to State Board and appellant - Order accordingly.
