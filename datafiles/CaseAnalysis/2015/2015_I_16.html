Rahul Yadav and another v Indian Oil Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 460; (2015) 9 SCC 447; AIR 2015 SC 2742; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 54; 2015 (112) ALR 510; 2015 (111) ALR 761; 2015 (4) AWC 4184; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 149; JT 2015 (6) SC 238; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 818; 2015 (2) RCR(Rent) 1; 2015 (129) RD 94; 2015(7) SCALE 251; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Contract & Commercial - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Execution of dealership and Lease agreement - Cancellation of dealership agreement - Hand over of possession - Respondent no.1 Corporation issued advertisement in newspaper for retail outlet dealership for which appellant applied and was selected and letter of intent was issued in his favour - Further, as required by Corporation, appellant bought land in question for purpose of getting dealership agreement and executed long-term lease of 30 years in accordance with terms of advertisement and letter of intent in favour of Corporation at monthly rent and dealership agreement was entered - However, it was alleged that allotment of petrol pumps by competent authorities were done to near and dear ones of political functionaries on account of political consideration - Hence, grant of dealership in favour of appellant was cancelled by Committee and was approved by SC - Corporation intended to take back possession from appellant with view to appoint another dealer - Appellant refused to hand over possession which constrained Corporation to initiate proceeding for eviction under Act - Competent authority passed order of eviction - Appellant Court passed order against appellant which was confirmed by DB on grounds that once dealership stood terminated pursuant to order passed by SC, lease agreement also stood terminated - Whether appellant is liable to handover possession of land in question to respondent Corporation.

Held, dealer ship agreement and lease agreement are independent of each other. Appellant was dealer under lessee, that is, Corporation. Dealership is liable to be cancelled on many ground. In case there is termination, dealership is bound to be cancelled and at that juncture, if lease deed is treated to have been terminated along with dealership, it will lead to situation which does not flow from interpretation of instruments. Mukund Swarup Mishra v Union Of India & Ors, 2007 Indlaw SC 13, relied on dealership agreement has been terminated. Hence, appellant has to vacate premises and Corporation has liberty to operate either independently or through another dealer. Appellant cannot be allowed to cause obstruction or create impediment. Further, with respect to application of Act, SC held that respondent is public sector undertaking. Appellant whose dealership has been cancelled, cannot claim possession to retain possession on basis of ownership of land as lease is in continuance, hence, he is trespasser. Hence, appellant is directed to hand over peaceful possession of land and structure and other fixtures standing thereon to Corporation after demolishing wall. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Lessor is not entitled to retain property once lease agreement is terminated between parties.
