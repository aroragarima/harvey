Y.S. Jagan Mohan Reddy v Central Bureau Of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 301; (2013) 7 SCC 439; AIR 2013 SC 1933; 2013 CRLJ 2734; JT 2013 (8) SC 449; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 108; 2013(7) SCALE 7; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant Of Bail, Denied, Propriety, Continuation of investigation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 437 - Economic offences - Continuation of investigation - Grant of bail - Denied - Propriety - Respondent Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) alleged that the then Chief Minister (CM) of Andhra Pradesh State had adopted several ingenious ways to amass illegal wealth which resulted in great public injury and abused public office to the benefit of his son, appellant (accused no. 1) - Appellant and 73 others were charge sheeted for alleged offences u/s. 120B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A of IPC and u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) of the Act - Appellant was arrested for his involvement and complicity in case and was in judicial custody - Appellant filed a petition for statutory bail before Trial Court, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that even according to CBI, several Ministers and IAS officers were involved, but no one was arrested so far and that in view of non-compliance of s. 167 of CrPC appellant was entitled to statutory bail - Whether appellant made out a case for bail - Held, considering all details furnished by CBI in the form of Status Report and counter affidavit sworn by Deputy Inspector General of Police and Chief Investigating Officer, at instant stage, release of appellant would hamper the investigation as it might influence the witnesses and tamper with material evidence - Though it was pointed out by appellant that he was in no way connected with persons in power, apprehension raised by CBI could not be lightly ignored considering the claim that appellant was the ultimate beneficiary and prime conspirator in huge monetary transactions - In view of facts and huge magnitude of case and request of CBI asking for further time for completion of investigation in filing the charge sheets, without expressing any opinion on merits, release of appellant at instant stage might hamper the investigation - However, CBI was directed to complete investigation and file charge sheets within 4 months - As held in an earlier order, appellant was free to renew his prayer for bail before Trial court and Trial Court was free to consider the prayer for bail independently on its own merits without being influenced by dismissal of instant appeal - Appeal dismissed.
