Abdul Gafur & Anr. v State Of Uttarakhand & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1304; (2008) 10 SCC 97; AIR 2009 SC 413; 2009(3) ALL MR 915; 2008 (4) AWC 3699; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 117; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 483; 2009 (153) PLR 490; 2009 (106) RD 681; 2008(11) SCALE 263; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 1142

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Ex-Parte Order

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Suits for perpetual injunction dismissed in limine - Appeal against - whether the High Court was justified in dismissing the two suits on the sole ground that it was proposing to examine a similar issue in the Writ Petition preferred by the original owner of the land - Held, when the plaint read as a whole does not disclose material facts giving rise to a cause of action which can be entertained by a civil court, it may be rejected in terms of O. 7, r. 11 of the Code - A plea of bar to jurisdiction of a civil court has to be considered having regard to the contentions raised in the plaint - Court would not be justified in determining the question, one way or the other, only having regard to the reliefs claimed de'hors the factual averments made in the plaint - Order accordingly.
