D.C.M. Limited and Anr v Union of India and Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 191; (1996) 5 SCC 468; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 494; AIR 1996 SCW 3672; 1996 (3) AWC 1886; 1996 (3) CCC 293; 1996 (3) CLT 315; 1996 (66) ECR 425; JT 1996 (7) SC 623; 1996(5) SCALE 826; 1997 (104) STC 590; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 589; 1996 (6) Supreme 255

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Sugar factories - Expansion - Incentives - Decontrol of sugar - Claim for incentives - Eligibility - Appellants owned two sugar factories and was carrying on business of manufacturing and selling of sugar - Govt sanctioned scheme in 1975 providing incentives to new sugar factories during 1-11-75 to 20-10-80 for effecting expansion in sugar factories and incentives consisted partly of higher percentage of levy-free sugar quota -Later, Govt announced revised scheme, after decontrol of sugar for brief period, to provide incentives to new sugar factories and scheme came into effect from sugar year 1980-81 - Appellants after completing expansion projects applied for necessary eligibility certificate for additional free-sale sugar entitlements as per incentives announced - Respondents allowed incentives as per 1980 scheme but appellant asserted that they should be given incentives as per 1975 scheme which allowed higher percentage of incentives - Appellants filed petition before HC with prayer to issue writ of mandamus directing respondents to issue supplementary eligibility certificate - HC dismissed petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that Principle of Promissory Estoppel would squarely apply to facts of instant case - Held, Doctrine of promissory estoppel would be displaced in such case because on facts, equity would not require that Govt or public authority should be held bound by promise or representation made by it -

In instant case it was found that Govt before refusing 1975 incentive scheme had taken into account various factors including decontrol of sale of sugar for period from 16-8-78 to 17-12-79- HC was right in its observation that 'petitioners who availed of resulting benefit due to decontrol should not in all fairness lay claim to be restored benefit of incentives in full now over again though basic premise became non-existent - Benefit under subsequent scheme in force from 1980 had already been accorded to them in full - On facts, Principle of Promissory Estoppel would have no application at all in instant case - Union of India & Ors. v. Godfrey Philips India Ltd, 1985 Indlaw SC 275, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
