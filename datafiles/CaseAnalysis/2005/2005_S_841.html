Meera Kanwaria v Sunita and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1014; (2006) 1 SCC 344; AIR 2006 SC 597; 2006 (1) AWC 678; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 566; 2005 (125) DLT 776; JT 2005 (10) SC 429; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 182; 2005(10) SCALE 39; 2006 (1) SCJ 242; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross-Examination, Examination Of Witness

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 330 and 332 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - First respondent married to member of Scheduled Caste - Granted a certificate of being belonging to Scheduled Caste by birth - Inquiry - Certificate granted in her favour was cancelled - First respondent contested an election for Municipal Councilor, a seat reserved for a Scheduled Caste woman - Declared elected - Election petition - Election of First respondent was held to be void and of no effect and was consequently set aside - High Court opined that as first respondent was accepted by her husband's family and biradari, judgment of Learned District Judge was unsustainable - Present appeal - A person who is a high caste Hindu and not subjected to any social or educational or backwardness in his life, by reason of marriage alone cannot ipso facto become a member of Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe - In absence of any strict proof he cannot be allowed to defeat very provisions made by State for reserving certain seats for disadvantaged people - Held, HC may or may not be right in holding that no special ceremony was required for conversion from upper caste to Jatav, but finding of fact arrived at by Learned District Judge that her marriage had taken place as per Vedic Hindu Rites and her marriage has been accepted by her Biradari meaning thereby elders of her husband's family only cannot be held to be same as that she had been accepted by community of her husband - HC committed a manifest error in coming to conclusion that purposes of reservation u/arts. 15(4) and 16(4) of Constitution, on one hand, and arts. 330 and 332, on other, are different - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained which is set aside accordingly - Appeal allowed.
