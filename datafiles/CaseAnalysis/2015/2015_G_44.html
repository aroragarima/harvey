Vipulbhai M. Chaudhary v Gujarat Cooperative Milk Marketing Federation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 397; (2015) 8 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 1960; 2015(5) ALL MR 441; 2015 (6) CTC 770; JT 2015 (3) SC 501; 2015 (2) KLT 397; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 553; 2015(3) SCALE 841; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 997; 2015 (4) SLR 31

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Co-operative Societies Act, 1912, Orissa Panchayat Samiti Act, 1959, Government of India Act, 1919, Punjab Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, Madhya Pradesh Panchayat Raj Avam Gram Swaraj Adhiniyam, 1993, Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, Rajasthan Municipalities Act, 2009, Manipur Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, Orissa Grama Panchayats Act, 1964, Cooperative Credit Societies Act, 1904, Uttar Pradesh Panchayati Raj Act, 1947

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 243ZH(b) - Removal of Chairman - Constitutional mandate provided for procedure - Justification - National Policy on Cooperatives announced in March 2002 has recognized democracy, equality, equity and solidarity as values of cooperatives - Cooperative society has been declared as democratic institution - Democratic principles have all through been recognized as one of cooperative principles though Constitutional affirmation of those principles came only in 2012 - Further, by 12-1-2013, all laws on cooperative societies were bound to be restructured in consonance with Ninety Seventh Amendment of Constitution and any provision in Act or Rules or Bye-laws otherwise inconsistent with Constitution will be inoperative thereafter - Appellant was removed from office of Chairperson of respondent no.1 cooperative society through no confidence motion - Appellant stated that in absence of specific provision on removal by no confidence in Act, Rules or even Bye-laws of Cooperative Society, Chairperson/elected office bearer cannot be removed by motion of no confidence - Whether removal of appellant through no confidence motion is in accordance with law.

Held, once co-operative society is conferred constitutional status, it should rise to Constitutional aspirations as democratic institution. So, it is for respective legislative bodies to ensure that there is democratic functioning. Further, despite Constitutional mandate, if legislative body concerned does not carry out required structural changes in statutes, then, it is duty of court to provide statute with meaning as per Constitution. SC held that if procedure is prescribed in any Act or Rule or Bye-law regarding election of office bearer by Board, as defined u/art. 243ZH(b) of Constitution, and for removal thereof, by way of motion of no confidence, same procedure has to be followed. In case there is no express provision under Act or Rules or Bye-laws for removal of office bearer, such office bearer is liable to be removed in event of loss of confidence by following same procedure by which he was elected to office. SC held that with respect to provisions regarding motion of no confidence in local self-governments, there is no uniformity with regard to procedure and process regarding motion of no confidence. Hence, SC directed that in case of cooperative societies registered under any Central or State law, motion of no confidence against office bearer shall be moved only after two years of his assumption of office. In case motion of no confidence is once defeated, fresh motion shall not be introduced within another one year. Further, motion of no confidence shall be moved only in case there is request from one-third of elected members of Board of Governors/Managing Committee of cooperative society concerned. Motion of no confidence shall be carried in case motion is supported by more than fifty per cent of elected members present in meeting. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Any rule, law or statute passed by local self governmants should abide and be in accordance with Constitutional mandate.
