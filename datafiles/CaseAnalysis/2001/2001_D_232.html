Syed Saleemuddin v Dr. Rukhsana and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19868; (2001) 5 SCC 247; (2001) SCC (Cr) 841; AIR 2001 SC 2172; 2002 (4) AWC 2936; 2001 (4) Bom.C.R. 600; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 1; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 613; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 591; 2001(3) SCALE 448; 2001 (3) SLT 527; 2001 (3) Supreme 497

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Rules of Practice

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Appellant is husband of respondent 1 - Writ Petition filed seeking writ of habeas corpus and for transfer of custody of two children from their father to mother - Respondent 1 sustained burn injuries when she was in her house - High Court deciding case embark on an inquiry about recording of statement of injured - Whether HC has erred in traveling beyond scope of inquiry in writ petition? - In an application seeking a writ of habeas corpus for custody of minor children principal consideration for court is to ascertain whether the custody of the children can be said to be unlawful or illegal and whether welfare of children requires that present custody should be changed and children should be left in the care and custody of somebody else - Judgment of the HC does not show that the Court has paid any attention to these important and relevant questions - Held, case was one relating to custody of children for disposal of which neither was it necessary for HC to take such an unusual step nor was matter relating to investigation into criminal case relevant for deciding case therefore order of HC is unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
