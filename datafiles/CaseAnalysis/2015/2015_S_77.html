State of Punjab v Saurabh Bakshi

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 223; (2015) 5 SCC 182; 2015 (2) ACC 761; 2015 ACJ 1297; 2015 CRLJ 2459; 2015 (2) JCC 1313; JT 2015 (3) SC 372; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1606; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 248; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 495; 2015(4) SCALE 340

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 279, 304A - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Rash and Negligent driving - Trial Court convicted respondent-driver of offending vehicle u/s. 279/304A of the IPC for causing death of victims due to rash and negligent driving - HC upheld the conviction and reduced the sentence by 24 days to the period already undergone - Whether order of HC reducing sentence of respondent was sustainable.

Held, HC has been swayed away by the passion of mercy in applying the principle that payment of compensation is a factor for reduction of sentence to 24 days. It is absolutely in the realm of misplaced sympathy. It is, in a way mockery of justice. It ultimately shatters the faith of the public in judicial system. The sentence of one year as imposed by the trial Magistrate which has been affirmed by the appellate court should be reduced to six months. Respondent be taken into custody forthwith to suffer the remaining period of sentence. Appeal partly allowed.
