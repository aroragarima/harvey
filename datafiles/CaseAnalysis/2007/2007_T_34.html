Rosali V. v Taico Bank And Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 48; (2009) 17 SCC 690; AIR 2007 SC 998; 2007 (66) ALR 798; 2007 (2) AWC 1965; 2007 (2) DRTC 554; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 92; JT 2007 (2) SC 611; 2007 (2) KarLJ 321; 2007 (146) PLR 375; 2007(2) SCALE 301; 2007 (4) SCJ 641; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 1169; 2007 (2) UC 704

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Purchase-Money

Summary: Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 84 - Suit for realization of some amount against respondent - Auction sale - High Court opined that in view of the fact that the 25% of the bid amount was not deposited on 26.10.1988 and was deposited on 27.10.1988, the provision of O. 21 r. 84 was not complied with and in that view of the matter the auction sale was bad in law - What would be meaning of the term "immediately" occurring in O. 21 r. 84, in view of the peculiar facts and circumstances of this case?; Whether the sale was void only because it was confirmed before expiry of the period of 30 days? - Held, term "immediately" may mean "within reasonable time - Having regard to the order of the court and the other circumstances stated by the appellant, his predecessor-in- interest not being able to deposit the 25% of the bid amount upon acceptance of bid did not render the auction sale void - Only because the sale was confirmed within a period of 30 days from the date of acceptance of the bid, the same by itself, was not decisive to set aside the sale after 8 years - Appeal allowed.
