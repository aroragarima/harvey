Seshasayee Paper and Boards Limited v Deputy Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Rohinton Fali Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 368; [2015] 374 ITR 619; JT 2015 (7) SC 330; 2015(6) SCALE 505

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1976, Unabsorbed Investment Allowance, Claim of set off

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act,1961, ss.32,32(2) - Unabsorbed depreciation - Claim of set off - Assessee filed its income-tax return wherein it chose to carry forward investment allowance and claimed set off of unabsorbed investment allowance - However, AO adjusted unabsorbed depreciation of earlier years and accepted 'Nil' income return as filed by assessee - Appeal was filed by assessee before Commissioner(Appeals) who dismissed same - Assessee approached Tribunal as well as HC whereby order passed by authorities below was upheld - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether Tribunal is right in holding that unabsorbed depreciation should be allowed before allowance of unabsorbed investment allowance in computing income.

Held, it is clear from provision of s.32 of the Act that, depreciation allowance or part thereof to which effect has not been given in particular assessment year owing to there being no profits for that previous years or owing to profits and gains chargeable being less than the allowance, such unabsorbed depreciation allowance is to be added to amount of the allowance for depreciation for the following previous year. Unabsorbed depreciation of the past years, by legal fiction, becomes depreciation of year in question and can be set off against income chargeable under any head. Assessee claimed depreciation allowance insofar as it pertained to current year. At same time, it did not want to claim set off of the unabsorbed depreciation allowance of previous years. Once entire depreciation, namely, unabsorbed depreciation allowance of previous year gets merged into depreciation of current year, it would become integral part thereof. Legal fiction makes it one whole thereby making it possible to assessee to claim set off of unabsorbed carried forward depreciation as well. Court clarified that avowed purpose of the legal fiction created by deeming provision contained in s.32(2) of the Act is to make unabsorbed carried forward depreciation partake same character as current depreciation in the following year, so that it is available, unlike unabsorbed carried forward business loss for being set off against other heads of income of that year. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Provision in respect of depreciation is for benefit of assessee and if assessee does not wish to avail said benefit for some reason, then it could not be forced upon him.
