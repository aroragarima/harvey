Â State of Uttar Pradesh v M. P. Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1960 SC 569; 1960 CRLJ 750; 1960 (1) FLR 520; 1960 (1) LLJ 270; 1960 SCJ 499; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 605

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Factories Act, 1948, ss. 12,13, 26 - United Provinces Shop and Commercial Establishment Act 1947, ss. 2, 27, cl. 3 - General Clauses Act,1897, s. 8 - Field workers - Employees - Commercial Establishment - Appellants owns factory for manufacturing sugar - Respondents were respectively General Manager, Assistant Manager and Secretary of company - Company employs certain classes of field workers to guide, supervise and control growth and supply of sugarcane for use in Factory - Deputy Chief inspector of Shops and Commercial Establishment, filed 3 complaints against respondents before Trial Court charging them with contravention of provisions of ss. 12, 13 and 26 of the 1948 Act - Respondents contended that Act did not apply to those employees as they were workers within the meaning of the Act and accordingly exempt from operation of the Act which was rejected by Trial Court and convicted respondents - Aggrieved respondents filed revision applications before Session Court which referred cases to HC which accepted references and ordered that respondents be acquitted - Hence, instant appeal - whether field workers, i.e., Supervisors and Kamdars employed by sugar factory to guide, supervise and control growth and supply of sugarcane for use in sugar factory were employees of ' Commercial Establishment' within meaning of United Provinces Shop and Commercial Establishment Act, XXII of 1947 - Held, HC, viewed that Supervisors and Kamdars connected with subject of manufacturing process, namely sugarcane, were workers within meaning of the Act and accordingly they were excluded from the definition of ' Commercial Establishment' under the 1947 Act - However, even if Supervisors and Kamdars were employed in any other kind of work connected with subject of manufacturing process' unless they were employed in factory, provisions of the Act did not apply to them, there was no dispute that they were employees of 'Commercial Establishment' within meaning of the 1947 Act - Hence, set aside order of HC and restored order of Conviction of Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
