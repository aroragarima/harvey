Kamarunnissa and Another v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 85; (1991) SCC (Cr) 88; (1991) 1 SCC 128; AIR 1991 SC 1640; AIR 1991 SCW 1630; 1991 (1) Bom.C.R. 410; 1991 (1) Crimes 131; 1991 CRLJ 2058; 1990 CRLR 645; JT 1990 (4) SC 7; 1990(2) SCALE 485; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 457; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 -Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Customs Act, 1962 - Customs officer - Criminal prosecution - Habeas Corpus, Currency - Three persons were intercepted by the officers of the Dept. of Revenue Intelligence at the Sahar International Airport, Bombay, as they were suspected to be involved in smuggling activities - They were later interrogated - It was found on interrogation that one of the accused boarded a flight to Hong Kong while other two were to proceed to Dubai - They were searched - Two balloon covered rolls secreted in the rectum of one of the accused were removed and were found to contain diamonds and precious stones - The said diamonds and precious stones valued at about Rs.70 lacs were attached under a Panchnama - In addition to the same foreign currency of the value of Rs. 10,706 was also recovered and attached - Passport was also seized - Other two persons were found to have swallowed 100 capsules each containing foreign currency of the total value of Rs. 6,99,930 - Capsules were extracted from their persons and the currency was recovered and attached under the Panchnama - Foreign currency of the value of Rs. 1,466.50 was also found on their person during their search and the same too was attached and seized - All the persons belonged to the same village - Statements were recorded on the same day - Whether the Court was justified in quashing the declaration made u/s. 9(1) of the Act - Held, declaration u/s. 9 (1) was challenged on the ground that the second respondent failed to forward the copies of the document on which he placed reliance for arriving at the subject to satisfaction that the detenu were likely to smuggle goods out of and through Bombay Airport, an area highly vulnerable to smuggling as defined in Explanation 1 to s. 9 (1) of the Act - Second respondent merely relied on the grounds of detention and the material in support thereto which had already been served on the detenu and nothing more - If the documents relied on for the purpose of framing a declaration u/s. 9 (2) were the very same which were earlier supplied to the detenu along with the grounds of detention u/s. 3 (1), the Court failed to see what purpose would be served by insisting that those very documents should be supplied afresh - Such a view would result in wasteful expenditure and avoidable duplication - Therefore the Court would not be justified in quashing the declaration made u/s. 9 (1) of the Act on such a hyper-technical ground - Petitions dismissed.
