Tmt. Kasthuri Radhakrishnan and others v M. Chinniyan and another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 66; (2016) 3 SCC 296; AIR 2016 SC 609; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 69; 2016 (117) ALR 874; 2016 (2) AWC 1943; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 86; 2016 (2) CTC 292; JT 2016 (1) SC 487; 2016 (1) MLJ 800; 2016 (1) RCR(Rent) 197; 2016(1) SCALE 569; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 18; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 116

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Lease Deed, Powers-Of-Attorney Act, 1882, Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960

Summary: Rent Control - Tenancy - Eviction petition - Impleament of parties - Appellants filed eviction petition against respondent no.1 seeking eviction from suit premises on ground of willful default in paying monthly rents and bona fide need for use - Rent Controller allowed petition and directed eviction of respondent no.1 - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed appeal but the same was dismissed confirming judgment of Rent Controller and aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed revision petition before HC assailing said judgment - HC dismissed revision petition but review petition was allowed and so, matter was again heard on merits and vide impugned judgment, HC allowed revision holding that eviction petition is not maintainable as daughter of deceased was not made party to eviction petition and also that appellants failed to establish relationship of landlord and tenant with respondent no.1 and set aside order of First Appellate Court and Rent Controller - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether impugned judgment of HC is sustainable.

Held, in view of decision of SC in Dhannalal v. Kalawatibai and Others, 2002 INDLAW SC 1309, it was not necessary for appellants to implead daughter of deceased in eviction petition and even otherwise, HC should not have allowed respondent no.1 to raise such objection for first time in revision because it was not raised in lower courts and eviction petition could not be dismissed on ground of non-joinder of daughter of deceased. Further, HC was not right in holding that tenancy in relation to suit premises was with POA holder as appellants proved that tenancy in relation to suit premises was between deceased and respondent no.1 and on death of deceased, it was created between appellants and respondent no.1 by operation of law which entitled appellants to maintain eviction petition against respondent no.1 seeking his eviction. Further, as concurrent findings of facts recorded by Rent Controller and affirmed by First Appellate Court in appellants favour on issue of appellants bona fide need for their personal residence and default committed by respondent no.1 in paying rent to appellants were binding on HC, revision petition filed by respondent no.1 is liable to be dismissed. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside and that of First Appellate Court is restored and consequently, eviction petition filed by appellants against respondent no. 1 is allowed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not necessary to implead all the co-owners in the eviction petition.
