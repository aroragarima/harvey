Ch. Ramalinga Reddy v Superintending Engineer and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1; (1999) 9 SCC 610; 1999 ARBLR 440; 1995 (1) CCC 211; 1997 (68) ECR 610; 1994(5) SCALE 67; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Art. 119(b), Limitation Act, 1963 - Whether, the petition filed by the respondents to set aside the award under ss. 30 and 33 of the Arbitration Act, 1940 was barred by time? - Art. 119(b), Limitation Act, 1963 - It did not lie in the mouth of the party who had filed the award in court through his advocate to contend that he did not have knowledge of the filing of the award and he could not contend that it was only the subsequent date upon which the court issued notice that was the starting point for limitation - When the award had factually been placed before the court and the court had accepted its placement into it on 25-10-1988 itself, the factual filing of the award had been made and subsequently notice to the respondent through his counsel.
