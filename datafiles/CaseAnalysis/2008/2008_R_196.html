General Manager, Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited v Rameshbhai Jivanbhai Patel and another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2625; (2008) 14 SCC 745; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 465; 2008 (4) AWC 3945; 2008 (4) G.L.R. 2833; JT 2008 (9) SC 480; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 78; 2009 (153) PLR 494; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 487; 2008(11) SCALE 637; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 927

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 23(1A) and 23(2) - Land acquisition - Compensation - Present appeals by special leave are by the beneficiary of acquisition (ONGC), aggrieved by the quantum of compensation awarded to the respondents - Before the Reference Court, the respondents did not place any evidence by way of contemporaneous sale transactions in the neighbourhood - But they placed reliance on some awards passed by the said Court in other acquisition cases - Reference Court allowed the claim in part and determined the market value of the acquired lands at Rs.17.10 per sq.m - Appellant challenged the said award of the Reference Court before the HC who dismissed the appeals holding that the determination of the market value by the Reference Court did not call for interference - Hence, present appeal - Whether compensation awarded by the Reference court could be upheld? - Held, claimants/respondents will be entitled to compensation at the rate of Rs.13/- per sq. m. with additional amount u/s. 23(1A) and solatium u/s. 23(2) as awarded - Respondents-claimants will be entitled to interest at the rates awarded by the reference court (9% per annum for one year and 15 per cent per annum thereafter) on the total compensation amount including additional amount u/s. 23 (1A) and solatium u/s. 23(2) of Act - Appeals partly allowed.
