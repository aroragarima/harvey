Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab Jammu and Kashmir v Messrs Alps Theatre, Patiala

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1967 SC 1437; [1967] 65 ITR 377; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10, 10(2), 10(2)(vi), 10(2)(vii) proviso, sub-clss.(iv), (v), (vi-a), (vii) of ss. 10(2),and 34(1)(b) - Respondent carries on business as exhibitor of films - ITO initiated proceedings u/s. 34(1)(b) of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, on ground that in original assessment depreciation was allowed on entire cost inclusive of cost of land - ITO recomputed depreciation, excluding cost of land - Whether cost of land is entitled to depreciation under schedule to Income Tax Act alongwith cost of building standing thereon? - Definition of 'Building' as per Act of 1922 does not include land on which it stands - Depreciation means decrease in value of property through wear, deterioration or obsolescence - Held, in this sense land cannot depreciate - Cost of land is therefore, not entitled to depreciation under schedule to Act along with cost of building standing thereon - Appeal allowed.
