Sunita Devi v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1553; (2005) SCC (Cr) 435; (2005) 1 SCC 608; AIR 2005 SC 498; 2005 (1) CGLJ 257; 2004 (115) DLT 517; 2005 (2) G.L.R. 1096; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 7; JT 2004 (10) SC 491; 2005 (1) KLT 748; 2005 (2) MahLJ 534; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 410; 2004(10) SCALE 227; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 438, 439 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141- Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 323, 341, 376, 384, 511 - Assault - FIR - Anticipatory Bail - Grant - Sustainability - FIR was lodged on 15-2-2002 against respondent no. 2 alleging that respondent no. 2 and others armed with revolver and lathi assaulted appellant and also attempted to commit rape on her - On same day, FIR was lodged by respondent no. 2, alleging commission of offences punishable u/ss. 341, 323 r/w s. 34 IPC against brother of appellant - Respondent no. 2 was granted with anticipatory bail u/s. 438 CrPC by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether blanket protection given to respondent no. 2 against arrest by HC was sustainable - Held, s. 438 CrPC contemplated was not anticipatory bail, however, merely order directing release of accused on bail on event of his arrest - There was no question of bail unless person was arrested in connection with non-bailable offence by police - Term 'custody' used in s. 439 CrPC, should be taken to be compendious expression referring to events on happening of which Magistrate could entertain bail petition of accused - In Salauddin Abdulsamad Shaikh v. State of Maharashtra, 1995 Indlaw SC 1866, it was held that protection in terms of s. 438 CrPC was for limited period during which regular Court had to be moved for bail - Hence, respondent no. 2 would surrender to custody as required in law so that his application u/s. 439 CrPC could be taken for disposal - Order accordingly.
