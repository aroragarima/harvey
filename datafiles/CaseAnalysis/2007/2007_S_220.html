Niyamat Ali Molla v Sonargon Housing Co-Operative Society Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1474; (2007) 13 SCC 421; AIR 2008 SC 225; 2008 (1) AWC 1069; 2008 (1) CalLT(SC) 10; 2008 (1) CTC 161; JT 2007 (12) SC 367; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1268; 2007(12) SCALE 374; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 346

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Ministerial Act, Review Application, Application For Impleadment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151 and 152, O. 21, rr. 97 and 99 - (A) Application for amending plaint and decree - On ground of inadvertence in description of suit property - Objection to Application rejected by executing court and affirmed by High Court - Whether the Order of HC is valid? - Held, statements contained in body of plaint have sufficiently described suit lands - Only because some blanks in schedule of property have been left, may not be a ground to deprive respondents from fruit of decree - It is also not a case where by reason of an amendment, one property is being substituted by other - If Court has requisite power to make an amendment of decree, it would not mean that it had gone beyond decree - If appellant herein did not file any written statement, he did so at its own peril - Application for impleadment - Dismissal - Remedy of - Held, one who is not a party to suit is not bound by decree and is entitled to take recourse to other remedies in law - Appeal and Application for impleadment dismissed.
