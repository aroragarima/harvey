Bai Chanchal and Others v Syed Jalaluddin and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 185; (1970) 3 SCC 124; AIR 1971 SC 1081; 1970 RCJ 984; 1970 RCR(Rent) 915; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 171

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1939, ss. 4(2)(b) and 11 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 116 and 105 - CPC, 1908, O. 23, r. 3 and O. 12, r. 6 - (A) Provisions of ss. 4(2)(b) and 11 of 1939 Act - (1) Applicability of provisons - Lease of lands to construct houses and to let out or to use it in any manner - Lease is not for purpose of business or trade - (2) Words 'business' or 'trade' - Interpretation - (B) Land and Property - Decree for eviction of lessees from leased lands on basis of consent - Judgment-debtors though liable to immediate eviction allowed to continue in possession for period of five years from date of decree in view of granting consent - Mesne profits at higher rate required to be paid by judgment-debtors - Clause in decree that in case of default of mesne profits they could be immediately called upon to deliver possession - (C) Practice and Procedure - CPC, 1908 - There can be more than one decree passed at different stages in same suit - Suit by lessor for eviction of number of lessees - Decree passed on compromise with some of defendants at one stage - Decree at later stage determining rights of remaining defendants - (A) Held, provisions of Act do not apply - Word 'business' as interpreted in income-tax law, or in terms of a covenant or the Companies Act cannot be used in interpreting the words in s. 4(2)(b) - (B) Held, that the terms of the consent decree neither constituted a new tenancy nor a licence - (C) Held, that the two decrees were separate and independent and neither was a nullity - Appeal Dismissed.
