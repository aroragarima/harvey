C. Sampath Kumar v Enforcement Officer, Enforcement Directorate

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 613; (1997) 8 SCC 358; (1998) SCC (Cr) 74; AIR 1998 SC 16; 1997 (35) AllCriC 598; 1997 (3) CCR 177; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 313; 1997 (72) ECR 799; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 511; JT 1997 (8) SC 135; 1997(6) SCALE 140; 1997 (Supp) AWC 711; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 142; 1997 (8) Supreme 268

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI

Summary: Exchange Control and FDI - Constitution - Practice and Procedure - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, s. 40 - Constitution, art. 20 -Whether the statement made by person summoned under s. 40 was extracted under compulsion ? - Held, no - There can be no presumption that the statement under s. 40 was involuntary - Only a bare assertion and no material produced by petitioner to show that it was extracted nor statements recorded by authorities produced - Extraction of statement under compulsion cannot be assumed - Administration of caution to person summoned that not making truthful statement is an offence - It cannot be said to be use of pressure when the person summoned is cautioned that not making truthful statement is an offence - Appeal dismissed.
