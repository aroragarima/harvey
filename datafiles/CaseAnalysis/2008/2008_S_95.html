Sami Ullaha v Superintendent, Narcotic Central Bureau

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2091; (2008) 16 SCC 471; AIR 2009 SC 1357; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 930; 2009 CRLJ 1306; 2009 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 5; JT 2008 (12) SC 297; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 464; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 40; 2008(14) SCALE 355; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 626

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 37 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Rules, 1985 - Contraband Luggage - Release on bail - Cancellation of bail - Sustainability - Luggage of two persons who were traveling in bus were searched and allegedly contraband weighing 2 kgs was recovered - Statement was made by accused persons that said contraband (heroin) was meant to be delivered to appellant and on basis of statement appellant was arrested - Report was sent to investigating officer stating that sample did not contain any contraband substance and appellant filed application for discharge but Court did not pass order of discharge in favour of appellant but released him on bail - Application for cancellation of bail was filed and by order bail granted to appellant was cancelled - Revision application filed by appellant before HC was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of bail granted in favour of appellant was directed to be cancelled on basis of report of analysis of articles recovered from him containing 'heroin' - Held, it was found that as Central Revenue Control Laboratory was not designated chemical examiner as defined in the Rules, reliance thereupon could not be placed particularly when laboratory which comes within definition of term 'Chemical Examiner' had opined otherwise - While entertaining application for cancellation of bail, it should be found that accused had misused liberty granted to him - Quantity alleged to be recovered from co- accused persons could be said to be intermediate quantity and rig ours of provisions of s. 37 of the Act relating to grant of bail could not be justified - Authorized laboratory categorically found that seized substance did not contain any contraband - Therefore, for purpose of grant of bail, Court could not be said to have committed any illegality in relying thereupon - There existed difference of opinion insofar as Central Revenue Control Laboratory had since opined that sample contained 2.6% heroin and effect of said contradictory report should be gone into only at trial - Therefore, impugned judgment could not be sustained and was set aside - - Appeal allowed.
