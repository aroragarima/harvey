Dhanvantrai Balwantrai Desai v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 82; AIR 1964 SC 575; 1963 (65) BomLR 332; 1964 CRLJ 437; 1963 (2) LLJ 415; 1964 (1) SCJ 133; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 485

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 ,ss. 5(1)(d) r/w s.5(2),4 - Evidence Act, 1872 , s.114 - Presumption - Rebutted - Evidence - Reasonable - Appellant was resident Engineer for Light Houses and complainant had a contract for reconstructing one of light houses - For construction contractor used water from a temple well and used a temple room for storing cement - On completion of work appellant asked contractor to carry out certain repairs to temple but he declined - At time of payment of final bill contractor paid a sum amount to appellant and amount was recovered from him upon a search - Explanation offered by appellant was that contractor had given sum of money for payment to temple authorities for repairs to temple - Appellant convicted u/s.161 IPC raised presumption u/s. 4 of 1947 Act - Rebutted by explanation was both reasonable and probable - HC affirming conviction and sentences passed on appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether presumption of law rebutted by evidence to interfere with HC - Held, nature of presumption where assessment of facts made by HC was manifestly erroneous - Appellant had failed to rebut presumption and was rightly convicted - Burden was not discharged by merely giving a reasonable and probable explanation - Explanation was supported by proof, presumption could not be said to be rebutted - Evidence was not sufficient to show that explanation offered could not reasonably be rejected - Burden of rebutting such a presumption resting upon accused raised u/s.114 Act - Presumption of law could not be successfully rebutted by merely raising a probability, that actual fact was reverse of fact which was presumed - Appeal dismissed.
