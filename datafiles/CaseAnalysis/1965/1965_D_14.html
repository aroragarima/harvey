Â Mafatlal Narandas Barot v Divisional Controller, State Transport, Mahasana (By J. D. Rathod), and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1966 SC 1364; 1976 (2) ALT(SC) 219; 1966 (12) FLR 191; 1966 (1) LLJ 437; 1967 (2) SCJ 64; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Long absence - No opportunity to defend - Dismissal from service - Legality - Appellant was a permanent employee of the State Transport Corporation - Services of appellant were terminated on the ground of long absence - Appellant made representation to Divisional Controller and thereafter preferred an appeal to the General Manager of the Corporation were rejected - On further appeal before Appellate Committee was also unsuccessful - Appellant challenged the order before HC which dismissed the petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to an opportunity to show cause against the proposed punishment as required by regulation No. 61 of the Regulations which govern the service conditions of the employees of the Corporation - Held, it was true that respondent may visit the punishment of discharge or removal from service on a person who has absented himself without leave and without reasonable cause, but that could not entail automatic removal from service without giving such person reasonable opportunity to show cause why he be not removed - Appellant was entitled to a reasonable opportunity to show cause which included an opportunity to deny his guilt and establish his innocence which he could do, only when he knows what the charges levelled against him were and the allegations on which such charges were based - Order of termination passed against appellant was bad in law since it contravenes the provisions of cl. 4(b) of the Regulation and also the principles of natural justice - Impugned order was quashed - Appeal allowed.
