Sampat Prakash v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 361; (1969) 1 SCC 562; AIR 1969 SC 1153; 1969 CRLJ 1555; 1969 MLJ 717; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22 - Jammu and Kashmir Preventive Detention Act, 1964, ss. 3, 8, 9, 10, 12 and 13 A - Whether case of petitioner was not referred to Advisory board and on that account his detention was invalid? - Held, no - Govt. may decide not to refer case of detenu to Advisory Board, because period for which he is to be detained is not to exceed six months and s. 13 A is an exception to s. 10 as well as to all other relevant provisions of Act, and in case of conflict s. 13 A prevails - (B) Whether grounds in support of order were vague and indefinite? - Held, no - Facts which in view of Govt., were against public interest to disclose, were withheld from petitioner and Govt. have power to withhold information about those facts - Petition dismissed.
