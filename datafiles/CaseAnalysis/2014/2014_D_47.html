Union of India and others v Dinshaw Shapoorji Anklesari and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 480; (2014) 14 SCC 204; 2014 (105) ALR 749; 2014 (4) AWC 3649; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 65; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 80; JT 2014 (7) SC 351; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 114; 2014 (125) RD 230; 2014(6) SCALE 662; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 1073

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Suit For Possession

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Civil Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Government Grants Act, 1895, s. 3 - Suit for possession - Sustainability - plaintiffs-respondents thereafter filed suit for possession, arrears of rent and damages against the defendants-appellants - Appellants stated that defendants were trying to set up adverse title - Appellants contested the suit by filing detailed written statement - Trial Court partially decreed the suit on ground of breach of terms and conditions of tenancy that's non-repair of suit premises - Appellants filed appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court dismissed appellants appeal and held that respondents were entitled to recover amount of Rs.20,972/- along with cantonment taxes at 6 per cent per annum - Aggrieved filed Revision Application before HC - Application was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, it was clear that land belonged to appellant - Said land was leased by way of grant originally to X under conditions of GGO 14 dtd. 6-1-1827 - Super structure including bungalow, garage and servant quarter on plot was constructed by him - In year 1891, super structure bequeathed to his X's son - X's son and two others sold their right, title and interest over super structure thats bungalow no.1A in favour of respondent and two others - Thereby file of super structure was transferred in favour of respondent and two others but title of the land remained with appellant - GOI of Defence by notice dtd. 11-6-1971 intimated respondents and two others that land belongs to President of India that's the Govt. and was held on Old Grant terms under which Government was entitled to resume the same - It was informed that Government resumed said property under terms of Old Grant for its use and therefore, agreed to offer sum of Rs.31,537/- as value of the authorized erection standing on said land - It was further intimated that in case if amount of compensation offered was not acceptable to respondent, committee of arbitration would be convened to assess the value of erection on the land - Cheque for amount was also attached along with said notice - Not only Transfer of Property Act was made inapplicable to Government grants but s. 3 of the Government Grants Act, 1895 further made it clear that Govt. grants was to take effect according to their tenor, notwithstanding any rule of law, statute or enactment of the Legislature to contrary - Therefore, it was clear that Govt. unfettered discretion and u/s. 3 of the 1895 Act imposed any condition, limitation or restriction in its grants and rights, privileges and obligations of grantee would be regulated only according to terms of grant itself though they might be inconsistent with provisions of any Statute or Common Law - In suit of respondents falsely claimed that suit premises described in the plaint was owned by plaintiffs as freehold property, this would be evident from pleadings made by respondents - Respondents also misled Court by stating that resumption notice dtd. 11-6-1971 was set aside by HC and said order had attained finality - Thus, liberty was given to UOI to dispossess respondents from suit property clearly indicated that decision of HC, that suit premises did not belong to UOI was not acceptable to SC - Foresaid misleading pleading made by respondents was without any evidence and same influenced the Court in wrong conclusion that respondents were landlords and appellants were tenants of suit premises - Appellate Court also failed to appreciate evidence and erred in affirming Trial Court's view that respondents were landlords and appellants were tenants - Land of suit premises belonged to UOI - Therefore, they should not be held to be tenants of suit premises comprising of area of 0.90 acres together with structure consisting of main Bungalow, Servant Quarter and Garage - Respondents had only right with regard to structure built on suit premises - Appellants have right for resumption of suit premises, as evident from evidence on record and issue was not properly appreciated by Trial Court, Appellate Court and HC which failed to notice appellants' right - Hence, impugned judgment passed by HC, Appellate Court and Trial Court was set aside - Appeal allowed.
