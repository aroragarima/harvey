State of Kerala v N. Sami Iyer

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 1966 SC 1415; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 361; 1966 (17) STC 338

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - States Reorganisation Act, 1956 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939,s. 3 (5) - Travancore-Cochin General Clauses Act, 1125, s. 4(c) - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Tax liability - Assessee objected to assessment of turnover of Rs. 7,757,54 for assessment year 1957-58 on ground that goods were subject-matter of purchases which had already been assessed at point of purchase - Whether this turnover was not liable to tax? - By virtue of s. 4(c) of Travancore-Cochin General Clauses Act, 1125 dealer continued to be liable to taxation under Madras General Sales Tax Act in respect of disputed turnover at purchase point - If Legislature had intention to override right attached to liability u/s. 3(5) of Madras General Sales Tax Act, it would have used more clear and precise words - Held, agree with High Court that turnover of Rs. 7, 757 54 is not liable to taxation - Appeal dismissed.
