State of Himachal Pradesh and Others v Gujarat Ambuja Cement Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1244; (2005) 6 SCC 499; JT 2005 (6) SC 298; 2005(5) SCALE 548; 2005 (142) STC 1; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 684

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals; Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Sales Tax (Registration And Turnover) Rules, 1957, New Industrial Unit, State Sales Tax Act, Rajasthan Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1977, Law of property Act, 1925, Coal Act, 1938, Landlord and Tenant Act, 1927, Opencast Coal Act, 1958, Settled Land Act, 1925, Revised (Amendment-III) Rules regarding grant of Incentives to Industrial Units in Himachal Pradesh, 1995, Revised (Amendment II) Rules regarding grant of Incentives to Industrial Units in Himachal Pradesh, 1994, Revised (Amendment) Rules regarding grant of Incentives to Industrial Units in Himachal Pradesh, 1992, Revised Rules regarding grant of Incentives to Industrial Units in Himachal Pradesh, 1991, Himachal Pradesh General Sales Tax (Deferred Payment of Tax) Scheme, 1992, Bombay Mineral Extraction Rules, 1955

Summary: (A) Sales Tax - Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Himachal Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1968 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 9, 9(3), 9A(1) and 9(A)(2) - A/ys 1995-96 and 1996-97 - Liability to pay purchase tax on royalty - Respondent a public limited company, carried on the business of manufacture and sale of cement - Director of Industries issued a registration certificate registering the respondent-company as a 'prestigious unit' whereby certain incentives were granted to the respondent subject to the condition that the production should commence by January, 1995 - However, further extension was granted to the respondent-company till 30-9-1995 - Subsequently, notifications dt 27-3-1991 and 31-7-1992 was brought into existence according to which an industrial unit must go into commercial production after 1-5-1992 and be registered with the Empowered Committee of the Department of Industries - Respondent was declared as a 'prestigious cement industrial unit' in accordance with the said notifications - In light of all these, Excise and Taxation Department issued a notification dt. 31-12-1994 granting exemption from payment of sales tax to the respondent-company under CST Act, 1956 and Himachal Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1968 - Assessing Authority granted exemption from payment of sales tax w.e.f. 30-1-1996 - Respondent-company filed an appeal contending that exemption should have been granted from the date of commencement of the commercial production, i.e. from 26-9-1995 and not from 30-1-1996, the date of issuance of the exemption notification - While the appeal was pending, show cause notice issued to the respondent as to why the exemption granted could not be revoked on the ground that the declaration of the respondent as a 'prestigious cement unit' was not correct and, therefore, the respondent was not entitled to any exemption from sales tax either under the Central Act or the State Act - Appeals filed by respondent against said order was dismissed - On further appeal, Revisional Authority cancelled and annulled the sales tax exemption certificate with retrospective effect - HC allowed petition filed against said order and quashed the orders of sales tax authorities and also held that the levy of purchase tax on royalty paid was not legally sustainable - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be allowed? - Held, cement manufacturing units registered as 'prestigious unit' between 1-5-1992 and 1-12-1994 were entitled to exemption - Such units not required to seek re-registration as a 'prestigious cement unit'- Once extension of time to commence commercial production was granted, such units entitled to the benefit of the Notification - No interference in HC's order - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Sales Tax - Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Himachal Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1968 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 9, 9(3), 9A(1) and 9(A)(2) - A/ys 1995-96 and 1996-97 - Liability to pay purchase tax on the royalty - Whether purchase tax can be imposed on royalty? - Held, 'royalty' is not a tax - Simply because royalty is levied by reference to the quantity of the minerals produced and the impugned cess too is quantified by taking into consideration the same quantity of the mineral produced, the latter does not become royalty - Royalty is the rent of the land on which the mine is situated or the price of the privilege of winning the minerals from the land parted by the government in favour of the mining lessee - Cess is a levy on mineral rights with impact on the land and quantified by reference to the quantum of mineral produced - Hence, royalty being part of rent of land cannot be treated as part of sales price - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Sales Tax - Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Himachal Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1968 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 9, 9(3), 9A(1) and 9(A)(2) - A/ys 1995-96 and 1996-97 - Alternative remedy - Held, HC empowered to entertain writ petition if alternative remedy itself is abuse of process of law - No interference in impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
