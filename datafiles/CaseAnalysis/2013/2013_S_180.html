Modinsab Kasimsab Kanchagar v State Of Karnataka & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 143; (2013) 4 SCC 551; AIR 2013 SC 1504; 2013 CRLJ 2056; 2013 (2) JCC 992; JT 2013 (4) SC 41; 2013 (4) KarLJ 564; 2013(4) M.P.H.T. 416; 2013 (170) PLR 665; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 446; 2013(3) SCALE 443; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 304B r/w s. 34 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4, 6 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) and his mother (co-accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 498A, 304B r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/ss. 3, 4, 6 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted appellant and his mother - Aggrieved respondent (State) filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment reversed Trial Court judgment by convicting appellant u/ss. 304B, 498A of IPC and u/ss. 3, 4, 6 of the Act and sentenced appellant to undergo imprisonment for 7 years - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was no demand for dowry and that demand of Rs.10,000/- was not towards dowry but was for repayment of a society loan - Whether impugned conviction judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, evidence of PW2 (mother of deceased) on which HC heavily relied upon in impugned judgment for convicting appellant was clear that when deceased came to her house on occasion of Holi festival and she demanded money, she told her to ask from her uncle (PW3) - Thus, PW3 was the person who knew exactly what were the demands upon deceased in connection with her marriage - From evidence of PW3 it was clear that at the time of marriage there was no demand of Rs.10,000/- towards society loan, and only Rs.1,000/- in cash, one tola of gold and other articles were demanded and were agreed and given to appellant - Further appeared that after marriage, appellant and deceased were happy for 6 months and thereafter deceased demanded Rs.10,000/- stating that there was a society loan of appellant and family expressed their inability and consoled the deceased that availability of amount would be seen later and again when deceased came to her house, Rs.2000/- was paid to her but the balance was not paid and she committed suicide due to harassment by appellant - Thus, demand of Rs.10,000/- was not a dowry demand but was in connection with a society loan of Rs.10,000/- of appellant - Even if, there was demand by appellant, it was not a demand in connection with dowry and offence u/s. 304B of IPC was not attracted - But was certainly an unlawful demand for a property or valuable security and there was clear evidence of prosecution to show that deceased was subjected to harassment by appellant on account of her failure to meet said demand - Appellant was liable for offence u/s. 498A of IPC - Conviction u/s. 304B of IPC was set aside and conviction of appellant u/s. 498A of IPC and under the Act was maintained - Appeal disposed of.
