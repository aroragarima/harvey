Sreenivasa General Traders and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 266; (1983) 4 SCC 353; AIR 1983 SC 1246; 1983 ALJ 519; 1983(2) SCALE 422; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 843

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, Commercial Crops, Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Rules, 1969, Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Andhra Pradesh Foodgrains Dealers Licensing order, 1964

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Constitution of India, 1950, Art.226, 136 - Andhra Pradesh Markets Act, 1966 - Enhancement - Exemption - Auction - Requirement - State provided for levy of market fee for every hundred rupees of aggregate amount for agricultural produce livestock purchased or sold - Director of Marketing through Market committees enhanced market fee from 50 paisa to one rupee with approval of State - Petitioners contended enhancement of market fee and claimed exemption - Petitioners filed petitions u/art.226 of Constitution was dismissed by HC - Petitioners applied for grant of special leave u/art. 136 of Constitution was dismissed - Hence instant petitions - Whether petitioners entitled to exemption u/r.74(1) of 1969 Rules - Held, levy of market fee u/s. 12(1) of Act was correlated to development purposes u/s. 15 of Act and it was clear that purposes were extremely beneficial to growers and traders - There was no material on record by petitioners to show that no service was rendered by market committees - It would appear that every purchase or sale of agricultural produce, livestock attracts levy of market fee - According to s.12(1) of Act, market fee levied on both purchase paddy and sale of rice by miller to trader as service was rendered by market committee at each stage - No exemption u/r.74(1) of 1969 Rules could claimed on purchase or sale of products of livestock takes place by auction or in other manner prescribed in bye-laws in market and it was liable to pay market fee on each sale - Unless requirements of r. 74 (1) 1969 satisfied, petitioners not entitled to relief - Petitions disposed of.
