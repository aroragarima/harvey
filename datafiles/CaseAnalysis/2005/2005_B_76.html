Joseph Severance and Others v Benny Mathew and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 577; (2005) 7 SCC 667; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 17; 2005 (61) ALR 191; 2005 (4) AWC 3781; JT 2005 (8) SC 509; 2005 (4) KLT 290; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 559; 2005 (99) RD 661; 2005 (4) RLW 2377; 2005(7) SCALE 479; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100(4) - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 60(b) - Whether suit was filed within a 'reasonable time'? - Held, no specific plea was raised before trial Court and first appellate Court that time taken was unreasonable - Question of reasonable time was to be factually adjudicated - A dispute essentially founded on factual foundation could not have been raised for first time in Second Appeal - Appeal allowed.
