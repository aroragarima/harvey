Ekta Shakti Foundation v Govt. Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1431; (2006) 10 SCC 337; AIR 2006 SC 2609; 2006 (64) ALR 907; 2007 (1) AWC 91; 2006 (131) DLT 287; JT 2006 (6) SC 500; 2006(7) SCALE 179; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Writ petitions filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution questioning legality of certain terms in inviting offers for implementation of the scheme called the "Detailed Scheme for Capacity Building of Self Help Groups to Prepare and Supply Supplementary Nutrition under the Integrated Child Development Service (ICDS) Programme" - In accordance with SC's order the Delhi Government framed a detailed scheme - Writ petitioners question the rational of the stipulation regarding three years experience of working as a non-profit organization or public trust registered under the Indian Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Held, correctness of the reasons which prompted the Government in decision making, taking one course of action instead of another is not a matter of concern in judicial review and the Court is not the appropriate forum for such investigation - In assessing the propriety of a decision of the Government the Court cannot interfere even if a second view is possible from that of the Government - In the present case, Scheme indicates that in order to implement SC's order there was desirability to discourage contractors and involve SSG through non-profit organizations - Nothing illicit in the impugned criteria to warrant interference - Writ petitions dismissed.
