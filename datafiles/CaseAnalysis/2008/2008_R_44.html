Secretary, Department of Horticulture, Chandigarh and Another v Raghu Raj

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1663; (2008) 13 SCC 395; AIR 2009 SC 514; 2008 (73) ALR 892; 2009 (1) AWC 953; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 65; JT 2008 (11) SC 397; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 184; 2008(13) SCALE 612; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Back-Wages, Industrial Disputes, Stay Of Proceedings, Appearance In Court, Procedure For Hearing, Personal Appearance

Summary: Service - Termination - Civil Court confirmed the termination - Sub judge set aside termination being violative of principles of natural justice - High Court dismissed the appeal on merits without hearing the appellant - Appeal against - Held, when a party engages an advocate who is expected to appear at the time of hearing but fails to so appear, normally, a party should not suffer on account of default or non-appearance of the advocate - High Court ought not to have decided the appeal in absence of the appellants' counsel - Once an appeal is admitted and is placed for hearing i.e. hearing on merits, it can be dismissed for default but cannot be decided on merits in absence of appellant - Matter is remitted to the High Court.
