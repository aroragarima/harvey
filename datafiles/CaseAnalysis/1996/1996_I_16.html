Kuldeep Industrial Corporation v Income Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1755; (1997) 3 SCC 377; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 237; AIR 1997 SC 3631; 1997 (138) CTR 193; [1997] 223 ITR 840; JT 1996 (11) SC 207; 1996(9) SCALE 343; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 782; [1997] 90 TAXMAN 132; 1997 (2) TLR 413

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 144-B and 245 C - Finance Act, 1979 - Admission of case of assessee for settlement for three assessment years, 1977-78, 1978-79 and 1979-80 - Draft assessment order of assessment years passed few days after assessee's application - Whether order of commission rejecting case of assessee for first year but admitting his case for later two years is illegal? - Held, ITO had discovered that there was no manufacturing activity of assessee - Assessee has admitted irregularities in his application that he had sold quota of stainless steel sheets and had maintained false accounts for all three assessment years without making distinction between first and later two assessment years - Thus, order of commission rejecting case of assessee for first year but admitting his case for later two years is illegal - Appeal dismissed.
