Maharshi Dayanand University and Another v Anand Co-Operative Society Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 412; (2007) 5 SCC 295; AIR 2007 SC 2441; 2007 (3) AWC 2363; 2007 (3) ESC 412; JT 2007 (6) SC 175; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 128; 2007(6) SCALE 194; 2007 (8) SCJ 26; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 596; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2112

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Claim And Counter Claim

Summary: Tender invited for construction of sheds near swimming pool - Tender accepted by respondents and earnest money paid - Appellant later informed respondent that work would not be executed on the site - Respondent issued a notice to the appellant invoking clause 25A of the tender conditions, calling upon them to appoint an arbitrator - District Judge appointed Superintending Engineer as arbitrator leaving it to the parties to raise all objections, including the objection to his jurisdiction, before the arbitrator - Aggrieved by the order, writ petition in High Court filed wherein the High Court held that the objections sought to be raised could be raised by the appellant before the arbitrator and there was no reason for the High Court to interfere with the order appointing an arbitrator - Appeal against the order of High Court - Held, the arbitrator, in the first instance, has to decide whether the existence of an arbitration agreement in terms of Section 7 of the Act is established and also to decide whether the claim now made is a claim that comes within the purview of clause 25A of the tender conditions in case it is found to be an agreement within the meaning of Section 7 of the Act - No interference in the appointment of an arbitrator - Appeal dismissed.
