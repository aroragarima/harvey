Rajendran Chingaravelu v Mr. R.K. Mishra, Addl. Commissioner Of I T & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2065; (2010) 1 SCC 457; 2010 (3) CTC 773; [2010] 320 ITR 1; JT 2009 (15) SC 251; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 858; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 999; 2009(14) SCALE 211; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1113; [2010] 186 TAXMAN 305

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Administrative - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Detention at airport on carrying huge sum of money - Appellant travelled by air from Hyderabad to Chennai carrying Rs. 65 lakhs with a bank certificate certifying the source and withdrawals for finalising one property deal - Security officers at Chennai seized entire amount under a mahazar, gave him a receipt and permitted him to leave - In this process, he was detained for about 15 hours without any justifiable reason - Tax Intelligence Officers prematurely and hurriedly informed the newspapers - Later after completing the investigation, seized money was returned to the appellant without any interest - Appellant filed writ petition submitting that following acts on part of income-tax officials are objectionable and violative of his fundamental rights - Said writ petition was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether compensation could be given to appellant for the illegal acts and actions of income-tax officials? - Held, no - Actions of the officers of the investigation wing in detaining the appellant for questioning and verification, and seizing the cash carried by him, were bona fide and in the course of discharge of their official duties and did not furnish a cause of action for claiming any compensation - (B) Whether seizure made by income-tax officials was illegal as appellant was carrying bank certificate certifying the source and withdrawals of said money? - Held, no - Money which is drawn from a bank and legitimately belonging to the carrier, may still be used for an illegal purpose - Intelligence officers are, therefore, entitled to satisfy themselves, not only that the money is from a legitimate source, but also satisfy themselves that such a large amount is being carried for a legitimate purpose - That is necessary in the interest of preventing crimes and offences - Therefore, even if the carrier is not guilty of any offence in carrying the money, the verification or seizure may be warranted to ensure that the money is not intended for commission of a crime or offence - (C) Whether Intelligence officers has prematurely and maliciously disclosing to the media a completely false picture of the incident? - Held, yes - There is a growing tendency among investigating officers to inform the media, even before the completion of investigation, that they have caught a criminal or an offender - Such crude attempts to claim credit for imaginary investigational breakthroughs should be curbed - Premature disclosures or 'leakage' to the media in a pending investigation will not only jeopardize and impede further investigation, but many a time, allow the real culprit to escape from law - Remedial action by the Department and the expression of regret serves and achieves the purpose of the appellant filing the writ petition - Writ petition disposed of.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Administrative - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Jurisdiction - Whether the Andhra Pradesh High Court was justified in holding that as the seizure took place at Chennai, the appellant could not maintain the writ petition before it? - Held, genesis for the entire episode of search, seizure and detention was the action of the security/intelligence officials at Hyderabad airport (in Andhra Pradesh) who having inspected the cash carried by him, alerted their counterparts at the Chennai airport that the appellant was carrying a huge sum of money, and required to be intercepted and questioned - A part of the cause of action, therefore, clearly arose in Hyderabad - It is also to be noticed that the consequential income- tax proceedings against him, which he challenged in the writ petition, were also initiated at Hyderabad - Writ petition ought not to have been rejected on the ground of want of jurisdiction - Writ petition disposed of.
