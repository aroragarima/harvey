Industrial Credit and Investment Corporation of India Limited v Grapco Industries Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1401; (1999) 4 SCC 710; AIR 1999 SC 1975; 1999 (36) ALR 592; 1999 (3) AWC 2299; [1999] 97 Comp Cas 117; 1999 (2) CTC 460; 1999 (80) DLT 418; JT 1999 (3) SC 619; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 98; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Shall Not Be Bound

Summary: Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993-Sections 19 (6) and 22-Ex parte order of stay or injunction- Power of Tribunal to pass order-Section 19 (6) empowers Tribunal to pass interim order by way of injunction or stay-Power to grant that order even ex parte inheres in Tribunal-But it must record reasons while passing ex parte order of injunction or stay-High Court wrong in denying such power to Tribunal.
