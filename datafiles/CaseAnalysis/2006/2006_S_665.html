Nagarathinam and Others v State

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 117; (2006) 9 SCC 57; AIR 2006 SC 1736; 2006 (2) CGLJ 413; 2006 CRLJ 2120; 2006 (2) JCC 651; JT 2006 (4) SC 288; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 505; 2006(4) SCALE 92; 2006 (5) SCJ 709; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 833; 2006 (2) UC 1222

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 34 - CpPC,1973, s. 161 - Appeal against conviction - Held, normally it is not for prosecution to prove injuries on person of accused, in each and every case irrespective of nature thereof, but in a case of this nature same would require serious consideration as a plea of right of exercise of self- defence was raised - Any motive on part of Appellant and other accused persons for hiding themselves near place of occurrence and committing offence has not been established - Totality of circumstances brought on record do not thus point out to guilt of appellants - Appeal allowed.
