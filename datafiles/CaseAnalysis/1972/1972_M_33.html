Malankara Rubber and Produce Company, and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Hans Raj Khanna, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 373; (1972) 2 SCC 492; AIR 1972 SC 2027; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Cardamom Act, 1965 - Coffee Act, 1942 - Coir Industry Act, 1953 -Constitution of India, 1950,arts.14,31,31-A(1) - Kerala Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1969 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963 (Act 1 of 1964) - Rubber Act, 1947 - States Reorganization Act, 1956 - Tea Act, 1953 - Possession - Acquisition - Lands detained - Withdrawal of exemption - Petitions challenging vires of Act 1 of 1964 with object of preventing State from acquiring lands in possession of petitioners in excess of ceilings imposed - Act in so far as it makes provision for compulsory transfer of lands under petitioner's personal cultivation to 'k' a law of acquisition within meaning of art. 31 A and was not entitled to protection - Hence, instant petition - Whether provisions of Act withdrawing protection to pepper and areca plantations could not be challenged u/art.14 of constitution - Held, provisions relating to plantations were violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Petitioners establish that lands detained by them and mentioned in petitions were not 'estates' so that they could be out of purview of Act - In absence of material in petitions to show prima facie that lands of petitions were not estates - Petitioners were not affected by 1963 Act as amended in 1969 Act - Provisions of 1964 Act could not be challenged u/art.31 by reason of its inclusion in ninth Schedule to Constitution - Reduction of ceiling limit by amending Act of 1969 did not attract operation of second proviso to art.31-A(1) - Provisions of Act withdrawing protection to pepper and areca plantations could not be challenged u/art.14 of constitution - Withdrawal of exemption from lands contiguous to rubber plantations by amending Act of 1969 could not be challenged - Petitions disposed of.
