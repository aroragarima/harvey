Universal Radiators, Coimbatore v Commissioner of Income Tax, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 425; (1993) 2 SCC 629; AIR 1993 SC 2254; 1993 (112) CTR 61; [1993] 201 ITR 800; JT 1993 (3) SC 150; 1993(2) SCALE 393; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 775; [1993] 68 TAXMAN 45; 1993 (2) TLR 831

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.10, 28, 10(3), Proviso - Income Tax - Manufacturing of radiators - Copper ingots imported for being converted into strips and sheets - Vessel carrying it seized by Pakistan - Claim settled by insurance company in America - Rupee devalued - (A) Income - Exclusion of receipts from - Should not be result of business activity - Assessee receiving something in excess of its claim in terms of rupee - If arising from business - (B) Revenue receipt - Devaluation surplus, if amounts to - Excess in order to be considered as income, goods lost have to be stock in trade - Held, : (A) No - (B) Not revenue receipt - Business activity not being buying ingots, goods lost are not stock in trade - Further, income from goods purchased for business is not income from business - Moreover compensation paid was not for any trading or business activity - Appeal allowed.
