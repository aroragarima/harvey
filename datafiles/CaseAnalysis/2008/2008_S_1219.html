Mahadev Prasad Kaushik v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1653; (2008) 14 SCC 479; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 834; AIR 2009 SC 125; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1864; 2008 (4) JCC 2722; JT 2008 (11) SC 218; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 801; 2008(13) SCALE 356; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: New Offence, Nature Of Proceedings, Process Fee, Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Act, 1870

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 304, 504, 506 - Appeal against order issuing summons - Held, there is no whisper about the threat alleged to have been given by the appellant to the complainant nor the Magistrate recorded even a prima facie finding as to such threat - No summons could have been issued to the appellant-accused for commission of offences punishable under ss. 504 and 506 - In every mishap or death during medical treatment, a medical man cannot be proceeded against in a criminal Court - Criminal prosecutions of doctors without adequate medical opinion pointing to their guilt would be doing disservice to the community at large - Process could only be issued by the learned Magistrate to the appellant-accused for an offence punishable under s. 304A - Appeal partly allowed.
