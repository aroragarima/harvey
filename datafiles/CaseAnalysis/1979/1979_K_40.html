State Of Uttar Pradesh v Klshori Lal Minocha

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, V.D. Tulzapurkar, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 107; (1980) 3 SCC 8; AIR 1980 SC 680; 1980 ALJ 278; 1980 (6) ALR 133; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 724; 1980 UJ 115

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Tort

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Administrative - Excise - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 299(1) - Highest bids - Failed to deposit amount - Resale - Loss - Annual excise auctions were held by appellant/ State under 'excise rules' and respondent offered highest bid for two groups of country liquor shops - Resale was made as respondent had failed to deposit 1/6th on conclusion of sales and appellant suffered loss of Rs.20,100 which was difference between respondent had offered and amount for which resale was made - Appellant filed suit in Trial court for recovery of said loss, which was decreed - On appeal HC dismissed suit of appellant stated that there was no valid contract enforced by plaintiff as required u/art. 299(1) of Constitution - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent is not liable to pay the damage even though no contract in writing had been executed in accordance with art. 299(1) of Constitution.

Held, that suit is dismissed for a slightly different reason, there was no concluded contract between the parties, nor there was any statutory rule permitting recovery of the deficiency on resale from, the respondent. Further, sale proclamation which is said to have contained the conditions of sale was not produced. Hence, in view of the majority judgment, appeal is dismissed. Appeal dismissed.
