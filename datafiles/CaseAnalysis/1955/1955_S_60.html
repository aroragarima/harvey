Sunder Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1956 SC 411; 1956 CRLJ 801

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 103 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Appellant accused of murder - At trial prosecution adduced no direct evidence implicating appellant - Depended entirely on circumstantial evidence - Convicted -(A) Whether certificate to appeal should be granted by High Court in this case? - Appeal comes before this Court on leave granted by HC - Does not fulfil requirements of art. 134(1)(c) - Observed by this Court that grant of a certificate u/art. 134(1)(c) is not a matter of course but that of power and has to be exercised after considering what difficult questions of law or principles were involved in case which should require further consideration of this Court - If a case does not involve any question of law, then however difficult question of fact may be, it would not justify grant of a certificate u/art. 134(1)(c), because if HC has any doubt about facts of a criminal case, benefit of that doubt must go to the accused - Held, present case does not at all involve any question of law - (B) Whether the testimony of witnesses could be upheld in light of s. 103, CrPC? - Section applies where a search is to be made of a place and does not apply to search of a person - Held, it was not necessary that there should have been two independent search witnesses - Assuming that witnesses were not respectable inhabitants of locality, that circumstance would not invalidate search - It would only affect weight of evidence in support of search and recovery - It only affected weight of evidence which is a matter for courts of fact - Appeal dismissed.
