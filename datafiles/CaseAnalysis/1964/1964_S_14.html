Â Ben Gorm Nilgiri Plantations Company, Coonoor and Others v Sales Tax Officer, Special Circle, Ernakulam and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 197; AIR 1964 SC 1752; [1964] 51 ITR 345; 1964 (2) SCJ 693; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 706; 1964 (15) STC 753

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale In The Course Of Export, Tea Act, 1953

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.286 (1) (b) - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956, art. 286 (2) - Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125 M.E - Sale by auction to agent of foreign buyer - Exemption from tax - Rejection - Sustainability - It was case of appellants that Sales-tax officer assessed appellants under 1125M.E Act to pay sales tax on transactions of sale of tea chests at auction held at 'C' in years 1956-57 to 1958-59 and rejected contention of appellants that sales were exempted from tax by virtue of art.286 (1) (b) of Constitution - Aggrieved by said order, appellants filed writ petitions before HC - HC dismissed petitions - On appeal, DB confirmed order of HC - Hence instant appeal - Was sale by auction to the agent of intermediary of foreign buyer, in course of export within the meaning of art.286 (1) of the Constitution - Held, Tea could not be exported otherwise than under a licence such a licence might be issued to a manufacturer or to purchaser of the quota granted by Central Govt. to manufacturer when tea was sold with export rights - When auctions of tea with export rights were held at 'C', it was in common ground that, sellers on whose behalf auctioneer acted as agent knew that bids were offered by buyers of tea for the purpose of export - It was also known that bidder was an agent or an intermediary of a foreign buyer - Further, if sale was in course of export out of territory of India - Any State law which imposed or authorised imposition of a tax on such sale was, because of art. 286(1) (b), invalid - Before Constitution was amended by 1956 Act, there was no legislative guidance as to what were transactions of sale in course of export out of territory of India - But by 1956 Act, art. 286 (2) was substituted for original clauses, and thereby Parliament was authorised to formulate principles for determining when a sale or purchase of goods takes place in any of the ways mentioned in cl (1) - Appellants set out in their respective petitions the manner in which sales tax of tea chests were conducted at 'C' and in certain petitions affidavits in reply even were not filed by State - It was true that there was no finding by Sales-tax authorities that respective purchasers at auction were agents of foreign buyers, but State argued case before HC on the footing that bids were offered at auctions by the agents or intermediaries or foreign buyers, and the Court proceeded to dispose of the case before it on that footing -Transactions of sale in instant case did not occasion export of goods, even though appellants knew that buyers in offering bids for chests of tea and export quotas were acting on behalf of foreign principals, and that buyers intended to export goods - There was between sale and export no such bond as would justify inference that sale and export formed parts of a single transaction or that sale and export were integrally connected - Appellants were not concerned with actual exportation of goods, and sales were intended to be complete without export, and as such it could not be said that said sales occasioned export - Therefore, sales were for export, and not in course of export - Appeals dismissed.
