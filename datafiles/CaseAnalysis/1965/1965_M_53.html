Â Chhitarmal v Messrs Shah Pannalal Chandulal

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1965 SC 1440; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court was right in refusing to grant a certificate of appeal u/art. 133(1) (b) of Constitution in respect of property of value of not less than Rs. 20000/-? - Held, claim in court of first instance did not reach Rs. 20,000 and one of conditions for a certificate under that clause being absent, claim could not be maintained - To attract application of art. 133(1) (b) it is essential that there must be omitting from consideration other conditions not material - A judgment involving directly or indirectly some claim or question respecting property of an amount or value not less that Rs. 20, 000 - Variation in language used in cls. (a) and (b) of art. 133 pointedly highlights conditions which attract application of two clauses - U/cl. (a) what is decisive is amount or value of subject-matter in court of first instance and 'still in dispute' in appeal to Supreme Court u/cl. (b) it is amount or value of property respecting which a claim or question is involved in judgment sought to be appealed from - Expression 'property' would apparently include money - But property respecting which claim or question arises must be property in addition to or other than subject-matter of dispute - If in a proposed appeal there is no claim or question raised respecting property other than subject-matter, cl. (a) will apply - If there is involved in appeal a claim or question respecting property of an amount or value not less than Rs. 20, 000 in addition to or other than subject-matter of dispute cl. (b) will apply - Subject-matter in dispute was a claim for money - Judgment did not involve any claim or question relating to property in addition to or other than subject-matter in dispute of value of Rs. 20,000 - Petition dismissed.
