Madan Lal and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2120; (1995) 3 SCC 486; (1995) SCC (L&S) 712; AIR 1995 SC 1088; AIR 1995 SCW 1109; 1997 (35) AllCriC 546; 1995 (29) ATC 603; 1995 (1) CLR 633; JT 1995 (2) SC 291; 1995 LabIC 1575; 1995 (2) LLN 22; 1995(1) SCALE 494; 1995 (3) SCJ 218; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 908; 1995 (2) SLJ 161; 1995 (2) SLR 209; 1995 (2) UJ 817

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service - Jammu and Kashmir Civil Service (Judicial) Recruitment Rules of 1967, rr. 9, 10 and 11 - Advertisement notice issued by PSC in closing months of 1992, invited applications from eligible candidates for filling up post of Munsif in State of J and K - Petitioners being eligible for competing for said advertised posts submitted their applications to Secy. of Commission - Respondents, who are selected for said posts also submitted their application - Petitioners and contesting respondents cleared written examination and eligible for viva voce - Viva voce test conducted u/r. 10 by four Members of Commission and an expert (sitting Judge of High Court) - Chairman of Commission and one Member, did not participate in viva voce test on ground that one of candidates selected as per result of written test, is related to them - According to petitioners they fared well in viva voce test but they were kept guessing as to result of this test - Came to know that respondents were given confidential information to appeal before Medical Board for medical test while no such intimation was sent to petitioners - Cause for apprehension that they may have been illegally left out of selection for said posts - Filed Writ Petition - (A) Whether viva voce test conducted is patently illegal as there is nothing to show that Members who conducted test had assigned separate marks faculty wise for assessing performance of candidates concerned as per r. 10 of Rules? - Held, petitioners took a chance to get themselves selected at said oral interview - Only because they did not find themselves to have emerged successful as a result of their combined performance both at written test and oral interview they have field this petition - If a candidate takes a calculated chance and appears at interview, then, only because result of interview is not palatal to him, he cannot turn round and subsequently contend that process of interview was unfair or Selection Committee was not properly constituted - Therefore, assessment on merits as made by such an expert committee cannot be brought in challenge only on ground that assessment was not proper or justified as that would be function of an appellate body and not acing as a court of appeal over assessment made by such an expert committee - So far as r. 10(1) (b) is concerned it does not provide for any separate assessment of marks for candidates at viva voce examination faculty wise - Interviewing Committee has to keep in view overall performance of candidates at oral interview and while doing so their intelligence, general knowledge, personality, aptitude and suitability have to be kept in centre - Rule merely lays down object of assessing such candidates in viva voce examination - It is a general guidance given to Interviewing Committee members - Therefore, not necessary for members of Interview Committee must separately assess and give marks on different listed topics faculty wise as per said rule - (B) Whether sitting Judge of HC was entitled to award only 60 marks for viva voce test while remaining 80 marks were permitted to be given by other Members of Commission and that affected overall assessment of candidates in viva voce test? - Held, all Members of Interview Committee participated in process of selection of candidates at oral interview keeping in view requirement of r. 10(1) (b) - There is no factual basis on record of this case for supporting this contention - (C) Whether absence of tape-recording at viva voce test has vitiated said test? - Held, r. 10(1) (b) nowhere provides that a tape-recording should be kept of questions put by members of committee and answers given by candidates concerned at oral interview and that in absence of such tape-recording interview process would fail - It cannot be said that merely because there is nothing on record to show that any tape-recording of questions and answers at interview was done, viva voce test should on that score fail - (D) Whether they were treated unfairly at said viva voce test? - Held, petitioners subjectively feel that as they had fared better in written test and had got more marks therein as compared to selected respondents concerned, they should have been given more marks also at oral interview - Merely on basis of petitioners apprehension or suspicion that they were deliberately given less marks at oral interview as compared to rival candidates, it cannot be said that process of assessment was vitiated - Not alleged any mala fides on part of Interview Committee in this connection - Remains in exclusive domain of expert committee to decide whether more marks should be assigned to petitioner or to respondents concerned - Held that it cannot be subject-matter of an attack before us as we are not sitting as a court of appeal over assessment made by committee so far as candidates interviewed by them are concerned - (E) Whether viva voce test was conducted in an unfair manner only with a view to select candidates belonging to a particular community? - It is a mere conjecture on part of petitioners - Validity to viva voce test cannot be judged simply on basis of result thereof unless there is anything to show that entire selection process was vitiated on account of mala fides or bias or that Interview Committee Members had acted with an ulterior motive from very beginning - Held this contention is found to be devoid of any factual basis and stands rejected - (F) Whether respondent 10 and respondent 13 whose names were included in impugned list of recommended candidates were not eligible to be appointed as Munsifs as they failed to satisfy eligibility requirement of r. 9 of Rules? - Held, Commission would be justified in not going behind certificate issued by District Judge concerned in not holding any further enquiry into extent of actual practice put in by such candidate at bar for being permitted to appear at written and viva voce test - As both these candidates are armed with certificates which clearly indicate that before 28-12-1992, that being last date for submitted applications by candidates concerned for such recruitment, these candidates had completed al least 2 years of actual practice at bar as certified by their District Judge - Further held, that respondents 10 and 13 were eligible for competing for said posts of Munsif - (G) Whether respondent 13 was not eligible as she was related to Committee members? - Held, only because respondent 13 was daughter of Chairman and daughter-in-law of another Member of Commission, both whom disassociated themselves from selection process as she was competing, she cannot be said to be disqualified from being considered for selection only on ground of here relationship with Members concerned of Commission - (H) Whether preparation of merit list of 20 candidates is violative of r. 41 of Rules as vacancies for which advertisement was issued by Commission were only 11? - Held, Govt. requested Commission to hold selection for filling up 11 clear vacancies only - Mentioned that a select list of 20 candidates may be prepared and furnished to Govt. but these 9 additional candidates would serve as waiting list candidates from which eligible candidates can be drawn in order of merit if any of first 11 candidates selected did not join or for any reason could not join - That is precise reason why no separate list of waiting list candidates was directed to be prepared - Appeal disposed of.
