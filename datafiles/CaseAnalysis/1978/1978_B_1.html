Narendra Purshotam Umrao and another v B. B. Gujral and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, R.S. Sarkaria, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 272; (1979) SCC (Cr) 557; (1979) 2 SCC 637; AIR 1979 SC 420; 1979 CRLJ 469; 1979 CrLR(SC) 50; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 315

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962 - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Scope and object of ss. 3(1)(i), 3(1)(ii) of COFEPOSA, 1974 - A vessel known as 'Jamnaprasad' BLS-61, found grounded in a creek off coast near village KimKhadi - laden with rolls of stainless steel sheets - Vessel and contraband goods found aboard it were seized by Customs officers - Intelligence gathered by Customs officers clearly indicate that appellant was main person connected with smuggling of aforesaid cargo of contraband goods - (A) Whether detention of individuals without trial for is in conformity with basic ideas of Govt? - Courts have always viewed with disfavour detention without trial whatever be nature of offence - Held, detention of individuals without trial for any length of time, howsoever short, is wholly inconsistent with basic ideas of our Govt. - (B) Whether s. 8(b) of COFEPOSA, 1974 is devoid of any obligation cast on Govt. to consider representation made by detenu? - Held, merely because there is no express provision in s. 8(b) of Act of 1974 placing an obligation to forward representation made by detenu along with reference to Advisory Board, unlike those contained in s. 9 of Preventive Detention Act, 1950 and s. 10 of Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971 it cannot be said that there is no obligation cast on Govt. to consider representation made by detenu before forwarding it to Advisory Board - (C) Whether appellant can be treated as a smuggler u/s. 39(i) of Act of 1974? - It is manifest that appellant could in instant case be detained u/s. 3(1) of Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 both under cls. (i) and (ii) thereof - Even assuming that appellant was merely an abettor of smuggling of 12 rolls of stainless steel sheets on this occasion, still his activities in this transaction afforded sufficient grounds for prognosis that he would have himself indulged in actual smuggling - Further held, clear that for all intents and purposes appellant was actual smuggler and not a mere abettor - Appeal and Petition dismissed.
