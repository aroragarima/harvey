Commissioner of Income Tax, Allahabad and Others v Ms. Vindhya Metal Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1227; (1997) 5 SCC 321; 1997 (139) CTR 436; 1997 (139) CTR 495; [1997] 224 ITR 614; JT 1997 (3) SC 522; 1997(2) SCALE 620; 1997 (3) Supreme 212; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 192

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 132A - Warrant u/s. 132 A - Validity - Whether, authorisation issued by the Commr. u/s. 132-A(1) of the Act on the ground that condition precedent for the exercise of the powers under the said provision was not satisfied? - Mere unexplained possession of the amount, without anything more, could hardly be said to constitute information which could be treated as sufficient by the reasonable person, leading to an inference that it was income which would not have been disclosed by the person in possession for purpose of the Acts - Held, warrant was issued without reasonable grounds - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
