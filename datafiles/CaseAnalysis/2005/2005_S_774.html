Gurmeet Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1238; (2005) 12 SCC 107; 2005 CRLJ 4384; JT 2005 (8) SC 628; 2005(7) SCALE 610; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Third Judge

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant along with other brutally killed his family members - Trial Court awarded death sentence - Reference was made to HC for confirmation which was allowed - Aggrieved by said order appellant preferred appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of Court could be challenged u/arts. 14 or 21 of the Constitution maintainable - Held, a judgment of Court could never be challenged u/arts. 14 or 21 of Constitution and therefore judgment of Court awarding sentence of death was not open to challenge as violating arts. 14 or 21 of Constitution - Further, only jurisdiction which could be sought to be exercised by a prisoner for infringement of his rights could be to challenge subsequent events after final verdict was pronounced and it was because of that on ground of long or inordinate delay a condemned prisoner could approach SC - Furthermore, it would not be open to SC in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 32 of Constitution to go behind or to examine final verdict reached by a competent Court convicting and sentencing condemned prisoner and even while considering circumstances in order to reach a conclusion as to whether inordinate delay coupled with subsequent circumstances could be held to be sufficient for coming to a conclusion that execution of death sentence would not be just and proper - Therefore, nature of offence, circumstances in which offence was committed would have to be taken as found by competent Court when finally passing verdict - It might also be open to Court to examine or consider any circumstances after final verdict was pronounced if it was considered relevant - Thus, time for consideration of delay in execution of death sentence could not be said to be a case of delay in execution of death sentence requiring death sentence being substituted by sentence of life imprisonment for reason of delay in execution of death sentence - Order accordingly.
