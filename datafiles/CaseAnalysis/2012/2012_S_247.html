Amit v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 58; AIR 2012 SC 1432; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1353; 2012 (4) CalLT 13; 2012 (2) CGLJ 505; 2012 CRLJ 1791; JT 2012 (2) SC 553; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 11; 2012(2) SCALE 675; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1978

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 376, 377, 302 and 201 - Murder - Kidnap - Beyond reasonable doubt - Death Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court for offence u/ss. 364, 376, 377, 302 and 201 IPC and HC confirmed Sentence of imprisonment and death awarded by Trial Court - Prosecution case was that appellant under pretext that he would buy biscuits took away 3 year old daughter of his neighbour and did not return till evening - When he returned and neighbour inquired about whereabouts, appellant ran away - Police arrested appellant and during course of investigation recovered dead body and other material evidences which were sent to Forensic laboratory which confirmed presence of human blood and human sperms on materials - Trail Court convinced by evidence and witnesses produced convicted appellant - HC confirmed death sentence awarded by Trail Court - Aggrieved instant appeal - Respondent contended that prosecution had proved appellant guilty without any reasonable doubt and that sentence of death awarded by Trial Court and confirmed by HC was justified - Whether conviction and sentence of death awarded by Trial Court is sustainable - Held, when appellant committed offence he was a young person aged about 28 years only and there was no evidence to show that he had committed offences of kidnapping, rape or murder on any earlier occasion - There is nothing on evidence to suggest that he is likely to repeat similar crimes in future, on other hand, given a chance he may reform over a period of years - Hence, death sentence awarded to appellant to imprisonment for life and direct that life sentence of appellant will extend to his full life subject to any remission or commutation at instance of Government for good and sufficient reasons - Appeal disposed of.
