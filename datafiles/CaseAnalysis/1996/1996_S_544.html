Surjit Singh v State Of Punjab And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3445; (1996) 2 SCC 336; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 1003; AIR 1996 SC 1388; 1996 (1) CLT 487; JT 1996 (2) SC 28; 1996(1) SCALE 648; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1095; 1996 (1) SLR 786; 1996 (2) Supreme 11; 1996 (1) UJ 523; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2949

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, art.311 - Reimbursement - Medical expenses - Medical institutions in India including private and also Govt. institutions, duly recognised by State for particular ailments as per policy - Claim for Reimbursement at rate of private institution - Held, entitled to be allowed - Govt. cannot insist on employee to get himself treated at recognised Govt. institution and claim rates prevailing there.
