Tata Engineering and Locomotive Company Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Pune

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Tarun Chatterjee, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 945; 2006 (203) E.L.T. 360

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Whether Appellants entitled to benefit of Notification No. 281/86 which provided for goods manufactured in workshop within factory? - Tribunal held that Appellant not entitled to benefit of aforesaid notification as goods were not so used - Tribunal did not record a clear finding that production was not being carried out by Appellant in workshop situated within factory or that goods were being used for repair or maintenance of machinery installed therein - It simply upheld order passed by Commissioner by applying ratio of previous judgments - It is not sufficient in a judgment to give conclusions alone but it is necessary to give reasons in support of conclusions arrived at - Held, finding recorded by Tribunal, being cryptic and non-speaking, is set aside - Case remitted back to Tribunal for a fresh decision by a speaking order in accordance with law after affording due opportunity to both parties - Appeals allowed.
