Nagar Panchayat, Kurwai and another v Mahesh Kumar Singhal and others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 566; (2013) 12 SCC 342; 2013 (3) JabLJ 274; JT 2013 (12) SC 233; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 317; 2013(11) SCALE 51; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 290

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: U.P. Municipalities Act, 1916, Madhya Pradesh Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1947, Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Taxation Laws (Extension) Act, 1957, M.P. Municipal Council Act

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Municipality Act, 1961, s. 357(3) r/w ss. 349(2), 357 (5), 358(4) (b) and (d) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 243Q, 243W(a)(i) and (ii) r/w Entry 17, Schedule 12 - Parking fee by local govt. - Legality - Appellant Nagar Panchayat, in exercise of powers conferred u/s. 357(3) r/w ss. 349(2), 357 (5), 358(4) (b) and (d) of the Act, imposed parking fee on motor vehicles - Respondent vehicle owners challenged imposition of fee on them before HC - Single Judge dismissed writ petition - However, DB allowed respondents' appeal holding that the Act did not confer any power to impose license fee on appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Held, Nagar Panchayat was a unit of self-government, which was a sovereign body having both Constitutional and statutory status - Arts. 243Q and 243W(a)(i) and (ii) r/w Entry 17 of Constitution, conferred considerable powers on Nagar Panchayat to carry out various schemes for economic development and social justice - Municipalities needed funds for carrying out various welfare activities and for the said purpose, it could always utilize its assets in a profitable manner to its advantage so that various welfare activities entrusted to it under law could be properly addressed and implemented - Bus stand was provided by Nagar Panchayat for benefit of all vehicle owners and passengers, spending public money - Nagar Panchayat should get a reasonable return for its upkeep and maintenance - Art. 243W(a)(i) and (ii) r/w Entry 17 of Twelfth Schedule of Constitution and s. 358(7)(m) of the Act and general principle that nobody had a fundamental right to use land belonging to another without latter's permission or paying for it, if demanded, gave ample powers to Nagar Panchayat to impose parking fee for parking vehicles in Bus stand owned and maintained by it - However, if Nagar Panchayat was demanding exorbitant or unreasonable parking fee without any quid pro quo, the same could always be challenged in accordance with law - Impugned HC judgments were set aside and Resolution passed by appellants imposing bus stand fee was upheld - Appeals allowed.
