B. Arvind Kumar v Government of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 624; (2007) 5 SCC 745; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1306; 2007 (4) ALD(SC) 77; 2007 (7) AWC 6805; 2012 (5) CompLJ 172; 2007 (4) G.L.R. 3450; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2007 (8) SC 602; 2007 (5) KarLJ 243; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 329; 2008 (104) RD 213; 2007(8) SCALE 520

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Forcible Dispossession, Permanent Lease, Non- Joinder, Tenancy At Will

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, s. 80 - Suit claiming possession of the suit property and for a declaration that he was the absolute owner of the suit property - Whether the lease under deed - Ex.P1 dated 30.9.1921, is a perpetual lease?; whether the plaintiff's father did not secure any manner of right, title or interest in the suit property, as the sale certificate in his favour was not followed by a registered deed of transfer?; whether the transfer of leasehold interest in favour of plaintiff's father was void, for want of notice to lessor and consent of the lessor?; whether the plaintiff was forcibly dispossessed in September 1975 and entitled to a decree for possession?; whether the suit was barred by limitation? - Held, absolute discretion to resume the land at any time without assigning any reason, and absence of any express grant in perpetuity and absence of any consideration, militates against the instrument being construed as a lease in perpetuity - Though the judgment of the High Court may be erroneous in regard to certain issues of fact, final decision of the High Court to dismiss the suit was correct and just and does not call for interference - Appeal dismissed.
