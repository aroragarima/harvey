Chikkam Koreswara Rao v Chikkam Subba Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 564; (1970) 1 SCC 558; AIR 1971 SC 1542; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 127

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Suit for partition - Self acquired property - Legality - Respondent (plaintiff) filed a suit for partition against appellant and others - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal, HC affirmed the decree of Trial Court in respect of properties covered by Exhs.B2 to 5 and 7 but as regards the properties covered by Exh. B6 (sale deed) relying on certain alleged admission by appellant, it held that they were joint family properties - Whether properties covered by Exh. B6 were the properties of joint Hindu family of parties to suit and consequently available for partition.

Held, statement made by appellant, in his chief-examination he along with his other evidence, it is clear that what the appellant admitted was that the acquisition in question was made by his father on his behalf and the consideration for the same was paid by his father from out of the appellant's private funds that were in the hands of his father. Thus Court is unable to agree with HC that the appellant had admitted that the properties covered by Exh.B6 were the acquisitions of his father. There is no basis whatsoever for distinguishing the acquisitions under Ex.B2 to B5 and B7 and B6. They all stand on the same footing. The respondents have not challenged the finding of Trial Court and HC as regards the acquisitions under Ex.B2 to B5 and B7. Judgment of HC to the extent appealed against is set aside. Appeal allowed.
