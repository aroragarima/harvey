J. Ramesh Kamath and others v Mohana Kurup and others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 370; AIR 2016 SC 2452; 2016 CRLJ 2870; 2016 (2) JCC 1242; 2016 (3) JCC 1935; JT 2016 (5) SC 254; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 104; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 380; 2016(5) SCALE 716; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 103

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Case, FIR, Jurisdiction, Misappropriation, Interest Of Justice, Final Report, Credit, Inherent Power, Criminal Proceedings, Settlement, Secretary, Reliance, Quash, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Association

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 320 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 408, 409, 477A, 120B - Quashing of proceedings - Nature of disputes - Respondent nos. 4 to 7 (Association) filed written complaint against respondent nos. 1 to 3 (accused-respondents), alleging that in furtherance of criminal conspiracy and with common intention, had misappropriated huge amounts of funds of Association by misusing their position as office bearers of Association and so, FIR was registered accordingly - Appellant no.2 also filed similar complaint making similar allegations against accused-respondents - While so, police filed final report before Court for offences u/ss. 406, 408, 409, 477A and 120B of IPC based on complaint made by Association against accused-respondents - However, aggrieved by initiation of proceedings against them, accused-respondents filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of final report contending that matter was settled between parties through amicable settlement - Further, respondent nos. 1 to 9 also filed joint petition seeking compounding u/s. 320 of CrPC - HC by impugned order, quashed proceedings pending before CJM, Ernakulam - Hence, instant appeal by appellants contending that most of provisions under which accused-respondents were charged, were non-compoundable u/s.320 of CrPC - Whether allegations levelled in complaint against accused-respondents, would fall within the purview of HC, so as to enable it to quash the same, in exercise of its jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC.

Held, allegations levelled against accused-respondents are of a nature, which could not be treated as purely of a personal nature and the basis on which the impugned order was passed, was incorrectly determined as of a personal nature. Further, accusations were not of a nature which could be classified by SC, as were amenable to be quashed, u/s.482 of CrPC. Further, it was not the case of accused-respondents that final report filed before Court had not contained adequate material to substantiate the charges levelled against them. Hence, quashing of proceedings is not sustainable and so, appeal is allowed and impugned order is set aside and, case is restored on file of CJM, Ernakulam. Further, directions are passed to investigate role of respondent nos.4 to 9 and initiate proceedings against them, if made out, in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per rulings of SC, quashing of criminal proceedings u/s. 482 of CrPC is not limited to quashing proceedings within the ambit and scope of s.320 of CrPC but it could also be based on settlements between private parties, and could also on a compromise between the offender and the victim and the exception is that the said power is not extended to crimes against the society.
