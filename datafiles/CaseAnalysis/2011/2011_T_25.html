The Commissioner, Corporation Of Chennai v R. Sivasankara Mehta Andanother

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 281; (2011) 13 SCC 285; 2011 (4) AWC 3276; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 41; JT 2011 (5) SC 453; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 896; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 692; 2011(5) SCALE 114; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Tamil Nadu Amendment) Act, 1996

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 48-B - Re-conveyance of acquired land - Challenged - Special Secretary for Land Acquisition passed an award vide notification dt. 03.01.1949 in respect of the land which was acquired under the provisions of the 1894, Act - Upon getting the enhanced compensation, the respondents (land owners) did not take the challenge any further - Long thereafter, in 1995 representation was made by the respondents for release/re-conveyance of a portion of the land which was acquired in 1949 on the ground that the appellant (Corporation) was not utilising the same - Government by an order dt. 10.03.1995 directed the appellant to re-convey a portion of the impugned lands to the respondents and to their legal heirs on collection of the compensation amount paid by the government for the acquisition of impugned lands with interest - Subsequently, appellant made a representation to the Secretary for Land Acquisition submitting that impugned land can be better utilised for the purpose of parking of vehicles in view of manifold increase in traffic in that part of the city - Government by order dt. 25.07.1995 cancelled the order of re-conveyance dt. 10.03.1995 - Respondents filed writ petitions against said order - Single Judge allowed said writ petitions on the ground that the Government was bound by provisions of promissory estoppel and also by reason of the fact that the order of cancellation of re-conveyance was passed without affording any opportunity of hearing to the land owners - On appeal, DB affirmed the order of the Single Judge - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that at the time when the exercise was made by the Government for re-conveyance, s. 48-B of the Act was not in existence - Whether order of the Single Judge as affirmed by the DB could be upheld? - Held, as per s. 48-B of the Act, when re-conveyance was purportedly ordered, equity has no application - Nor was there any scope for principle of natural justice to operate when the person complaining of its infraction cannot show any right of his which has been violated - Hence, respondents have no right to apply for re-conveyance in respect of a land which had vested in the Government long ago - Impugned order of the Single Judge as affirmed by the HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
