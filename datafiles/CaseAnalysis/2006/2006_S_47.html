Saheb Khan v Mohd. Yousufuddin and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 221; (2006) 4 SCC 476; AIR 2006 SC 1871; 2006 (3) AWC 2269; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 14; JT 2006 (5) SC 1; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 1; 2006(4) SCALE 352

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - CPC, 1908, o. 21, rr. 67(1), 90, s. 151 - Partition Act, 1893, ss. 2, 3 - Appellant purchased property in a Court sale - HC set aside sale holding that no notice was given to respondent No 1 to purchase property in terms of ss.2, 3 of the Act before selling property by public auction and also not given adequate publicity to sale - Appeal against - Held, there is also no material irregularity in publishing or conducting sale - There was sufficient compliance with o. 21, r 67(1) r/w o. 21, r.54(2) - HC erred in setting aside sale in favour of appellant - Appeal allowed.
