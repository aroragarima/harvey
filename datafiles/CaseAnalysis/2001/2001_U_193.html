Ex-Subedar Joginder Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Kirpal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20554; (2001) 9 SCC 602; AIR 2001 SC 3430; 2001 (3) CLR 996; 2002 (92) FLR 273; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 81; JT 2001 (9) SC 23; 2001 LabIC 4012; 2001(7) SCALE 263; 2001 (5) SLR 712; 2001 (7) SLT 348; 2001 (7) Supreme 619; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 150

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Pension - Army Act, 1950, s. 52(a) - Pension Regulations for Army, 1961, regn. 113(a) - Appellant joined Indian Army - Promoted to rank subedar - Charge-sheeted for offence u/s. 52(a) of Army Act, 1950 - Dismissed from service - Appeal filed by him u/s. 164(2) of Army Act, 1950 was dismissed - Appellant made representation to President of India u/regn. 113(a) of for grant of service pension under exceptional circumstances - Said representation was rejected - Whether appellant entitled to grant of pension? - Supreme Court in UOI v. R. K. L. D. Azad 1994 Indlaw SC 909 observed that grant of pension and gratuity to junior commissioned officers, other ranks and non-combatants (enrolled) is regulated by Chap. III of Pension Regulations - U/regn. 113(a), an individual who is dismissed under provisions of Army Act, 1950 is ineligible for pension or gratuity in respect of previous service, however, as an exception, President may in his discretion grant service pension or gratuity partially and fully - Held, having regard to fact that appellant was dismissed from service as a sequel to general court martial for an offence u/s. 52(a) of Army Act, 1950 for committing theft of property belonging to Govt. and that his representation u/regn. 113(a) pleading exceptional circumstances for grant of pension was rejected, of merit in this appeal - Appeal dismissed.
