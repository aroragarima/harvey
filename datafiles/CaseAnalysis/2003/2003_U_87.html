Â Saurabh Chaudri and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 959; (2003) 11 SCC 146; AIR 2004 SC 361; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 140; JT 2003 (8) SC 296; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 113; 2003(9) SCALE 272; 2003 (1) SCW 6392; 2004 (1) SLR 287; 2003 (6) SLT 724; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 152; 2004 (1) Supreme 26; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 279

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Domiciled In India, Equal Justice, Equal protection of law, Equality before law

Summary: Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 141, 142, 143, 144, 15, 16, 19(1), 246, 29(2), 41, 47 - Prevention of the Capitation Fee Act - Admission - Post Graduate Courses in Govt. run medical colleges - Constitutional validity of reservation - Whether based on domicile or institution - Whether reservation on basis of domicile is impermissible in terms of art 15(1) of Constitution - whether reservation by way of institutional preference comes within suspected classification warranting strict scrutiny test - whether reservation by institutional preference is valid - Held, A distinction, therefore, has been made by makers of Constitution themselves to effect that expression 'place of birth' is not synonymous to expression 'domicile' and they reflect two different concepts - applying test of presumption of constitutionality, no case has been made out for invoking doctrine of strict construction or intermediate construction - whether institutional reservation fulfils aforementioned criteria or not must be judged on following: (a) there is a presumption of constitutionality; (b) burden of proof is upon WPers as they have questioned constitutionality of provisions; (c) there is a presumption as regard State's power on extent of its legislative competence; (d) hardship of few cannot be basis for determining validity of any statute - Further held, for purpose of selecting candidates, it is necessary to hold an All India Entrance Examination by an impartial and reputed body AIIMS in terms of an order passed by this Court has been conducting said examination - It may continue to do so unless a competent body is created by Central Govt. in terms of a Parliamentary Act or otherwise All expenses for conducting such examination shall be borne by Central Govt. which would also provide requisite infrastructure therefor - One test shall be held for all students taking admission throughout country - This order is passed keeping in view fact that now one common entrance test is held for admission against 25% of all India quota and other tests are being held by respective universities - Disparities in such tests should be done away with and merit of students should be judged on basis of one test held therefor - Directions issued.
