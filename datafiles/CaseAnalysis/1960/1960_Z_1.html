Kundan Sugar Mills v Ziya Uddin and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 86; AIR 1960 SC 650; 1960 (1) LLJ 266; 1961 (1) SCJ 176; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 7(i) - (A) Whether right to transfer an employee by an employer from one of his concerns to another is implicit in every contract of service? - Held, two factories are distinct entities, situated at different places and, to import a term conferring a right on employer to transfer respondents to a different concern is really to make a new contract between them - That it was not a condition of service of employment of respondents, either express or implied, that employer has right to transfer them to a new concern started by him subsequent to date of their employment - (B) Whether Appellate Tribunal had jurisdiction u/s. 7(i) of Act to set aside finding of State Industrial Tribunal, that both concerns formed one unit? - Further held, substantial question of law was involved, since important principle governing right of an employer to transfer his employees from one concern to another of his in circumstances of the present case was answered - Appeal dismissed.
