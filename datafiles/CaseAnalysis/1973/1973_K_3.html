Â Raval & Co. v K. C. Ramachandran & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 283; (1974) 1 SCC 424; AIR 1974 SC 818; 1975 RCJ 180; 1974 RCR(Rent) 170; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 629

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: T.N. Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 2(6), 2(8) and 4 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949 - Constitution, art.141- Evidence Act, 1872, s. 92 - (A) Tenancy - Fixation of fair rent - Landlord whether entitled to apply for fixation of fair rent during the subsistence of contractual tenancy - (B) Precedents - Binding nature of general observations in Supreme Court decisions - (C) Evidence - Variation in terms of registered lease deed - Oral evidence of it is barred - Held, landlord is not given the right to apply for fixation of fair rent during the subsistence of the contractual tenancy - The general observations in earlier cases should be confined to the facts of those cases - It was barred u/s. 92 as any variation of rent reserved by a registered lease deed must be made by another registered instrument - Appeal Dismissed.
