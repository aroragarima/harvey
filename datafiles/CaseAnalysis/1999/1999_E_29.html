S. I. L. Import v Exim Aides Silk Exporters

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1405; (1999) 4 SCC 567; (1999) SCC (Cr) 600; AIR 1999 SC 1609; AIR 1999 SCW 1218; 1999 (36) ALR 300; 2000 (1) BC 668; [1999] 97 Comp Cas 575; 1999 CRLJ 2276; 1999 (79) DLT 414; JT 1999 (3) SC 325; 2000(1) M.P.H.T. 576; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 142 - Scope and applicability of - Held, date when notice sent by fax reached drawer of cheque, period of 15 days (within which he has to make payment) has started running and on expiry of that period, offence is completed unless amount has been paid in meanwhile - If no complaint was filed within one month therefrom, payee would stand forbidden from launching a prosecution thereafter, due to clear interdict contained in s. 142 of Act - Appeal allowed.
