State Of Haryana v Dalmia Dadri Cement Ltd

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, S. Mukharji, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28798; (1987) Supp SCC 679; AIR 1988 SC 342; 1988 (14) ECR 292; 2004 (178) E.L.T. 13; JT 1988 (3) SC 1; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1988 (68) STC 173; 1988 (1) UJ 287

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 5(2)(a)(iv) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - (A) Sale of cement to State Electricity Board on basis of certificate - That it is required for use in generation or distribution of electrical energy - Whether actual use is to be proved by assessee for exemption? - (B) Certificate u/s. 5(2)(a)(iv) of Act - Whether conclusive as in Form C? - Held, it is not necessary - Further held, it cannot be compared with certificate in Form C which is statutory certificate and neither it can be regarded as completely conclusive - Appeal dismissed.
