India Marine Service Private Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1963 SC 528; 1962 (5) FLR 223; 1963 (1) LLJ 122; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 575

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Unjustified

Summary: Labour & Industrial - (A) Whether payment should be given to labourers for period of illegal strike? - Held, in case where strike is unjustified that is followed by a lock out which has, because of its long duration, become unjustified, it would not be a proper course for an industrial tribunal to direct payment of whole of wages for period of lock-out - In a case where strike is unjustified and lock-out is justified, workmen would not be entitled to any wages at all - Where strike is justified and lock-out is unjustified workmen would be entitled to entire wages for period of strike and lock out - A strike is unjustified and is followed by a lock-out which becomes unjustified, a case for apportionment of blame arises - Where blame for situation which resulted after strike and lock-out could be apportioned, roughly half and half between company and its workers, workmen should get half of their wages for lock-out period - Appeal partly allowed.
