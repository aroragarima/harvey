Chief of Marketing (Marketing Division), Coal India Limited and Another v Mewat Chemicals and Tiny Ssi Coal Pulverising Unit and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1150; (2004) 4 SCC 146; AIR 2004 SC 2640; JT 2004 (5) SC 522; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 26; 2004(4) SCALE 71B

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Allotment of coal - Grant for linkages - Issuance of Circular - Respondents made applications for allotment of coal and had also applied for linkages - As their applications were not decided, respondents filed petitions which were disposed of - Joint Secretary (Coal) sent fax message to Coal Collector setting out that order of HC had not been complied with - Joint Secretary, Ministry of Coal issued order that Coal Controller was not authorized to grant any long term linkages and that allotment of coal by Coal Controller had to be subject to such instructions that Central Govt. should issue from time to time - Respondents filed writ Petition challenging validity of order Coal Controller which was came to be allowed - Appeal of Appellants was dismissed holding that Coal Controller was competent to grant linkages and that in any case he being competent authority, Central Govt. could not sit in review over order passed by Coal Controller - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Coal controller could be competent to grant linkages and allotment of coal - Held, it was found that HC was in error in concluding that position prevailing on date of application must apply - It was settled law that there was no vested right however person made application - Position prevailed at time allotment was to apply - Before allotment was made circular had already been issued - Coal Controller whilst allotting was bound to take note of that circular - Joint Secretary by his fax had brought it to notice of Coal Controller - Thereafter, guidelines had also been issued - Coal Controller was bound to take note of those guidelines also - It was unable to understand reasoning given by HC that those guidelines had been issued by Director and thus could not be said to be guidelines issued by Central Govt. - Those guidelines had been issued by Ministry of Coal - Merely, since they were forwarded not by Joint Secretary but by Director would not mean that they were not binding on Coal Controller - However, there was any doubt as to they had been issued by Central Govt., Coal Controller should have asked for clarification from Central Govt. - Thus, order of Single Judge or DB were not sustained and they were set aside - Appeal disposed of.
