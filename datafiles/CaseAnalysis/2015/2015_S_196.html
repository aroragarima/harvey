Soorajmull Nagarmull v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 562; (2015) 10 SCC 270; AIR 2015 SC 3400; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 54; 2015(6) ALL MR 936; 2015 (112) ALR 897; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 381; JT 2015 (7) SC 279; 2016 (3) MahLJ 129; 2015 (6) MLJ 714; 2016 (2) MPLJ 350; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 129; 2016 (130) RD 74; 2015(9) SCALE 1; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Forest Act, 1927

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 17 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, s. 29 - Acquisition of land - State initiated acquisition proceedings by issuing notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 of 1894 Act, both dt.25-3-1981, invoking urgency provisions u/s. 17 - Later, said land was declared to be 'protected forest' as envisaged in s. 29 of 1927 Act - Later, proceedings were once again initiated by State and urgency provisions were invoked and s. 5A was yet again dispensed with - Aggrieved, appellant-landowner challenged proceedings before HC and HC allowed petition - Later, State took steps to annul second proceedings by attempting to rely on s. 48 with objective to withdraw from acquisition - Hence, appellant filed another writ petition and sought issuance of direction commanding State to release land in question and hand over its possession to appellant - Further, forest officer also filed writ petition challenging action State to withdraw from acquisition proceedings - While so, PIL was also filed and pending said petitions, award was published on 27-9-2006 in continuity of 1981 acquisition proceedings - HC heard all three writ petitions together and held that possession of land as well as title thereof vested in State - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appeals could be allowed.

Held, actions of State denied appellant just and fair compensation as envisaged and postulated in 1894 Act, for its land from which it was dispossessed well over three decades ago. Hence, 1981 acquisition is set aside for non-compliance with provisions of s. 11A of 1894 Act. Further, acquisition proceedings with regard to subject lands have lapsed. Hence, State is directed to initiate fresh acquisition proceedings or take any other action available to it in accordance with law. Appeals are allowed to such extent. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Old notifications are superseded and obliterated by subsequent ones, as the subsequent acquisition proceeding manifest and indicate the intention of the State to abandon the preceding one.
