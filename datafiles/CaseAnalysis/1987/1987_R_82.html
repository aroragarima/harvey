Hindu Jea Band, Jaipur v Regional Director, Employees' State Insurance

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28258; (1987) SCC (L&S) 88; (1987) 2 SCC 101; AIR 1987 SC 1166; 1987 (1) CCC 816; 1987 (54) FLR 443; JT 1987 (1) SC 518; 1987 LabIC 894; 1987 (1) LLJ 502; 1987 (1) LLN 778; 1987(1) SCALE 393; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 377; 1987 (2) UJ 27

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21 and 32 - Employees State Insurance Act,1948, ss. 1(5), 2(9)(i)l, 2(12) and 75 - Shop - Notification - Retail basis - Petitioner firm a partnership concern carrying on business of playing music on occasions and other social functions - Liability to pay contribution under provisions of Act - Petition filed u/s.75 of Act before Employees' State Insurance Court - Said petition was rejected and directed to pay amount as arrears by Regional Director of Corporation - Appeal filed by petitioner was dismissed - Petitioner filed a writ questioning validity of s.1 (5) of 1948 Act and notification issued by State Govt. - Whether place of carrying business was shop or not and intermittent or seasonal character act could be extended to its business or not - Held, place of carrying business was a shop and act applicable by virtue of notification - Not selling any goods in place of its business but was only engaged in arranging for musical performances on occasions - Petitioner was not entitled to claim any exemption - Services of employees were not confined only to marriages - Provides music at several other social functions also which might take place during all seasons - Term 'employees' defined u/s. 2(9)(i) of 1948 Act - Paid daily wages or part-time employees were employees for purposes of the act - Writ Petition questioning validity of notification issued by State Govt. has no merit - Petition dismissed.
