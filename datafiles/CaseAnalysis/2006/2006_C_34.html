Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut v Century Laminating Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 664; 2006 (198) E.L.T. 321

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Excisability of goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4(l)(a) and 35 L(b) - Companies Act, 1955 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9(2) - Whether tribunal erred in holding that duty had to be levied on basis of wholesale price ascertainable at factory gate? - S. 4(1) provides that where duty of excise is chargeable on any excisable goods with reference to value, such value shall, subject to other provisions of this section be deemed to be normal price thereof, that is to say, price at which such goods are ordinarily sold by assessee to a buyer in course of wholesale trade for delivery at time and place of removal, where buyer is not a related person and price is sole consideration for sale - Held, no infirmity in decision of tribunal - Appeals dismissed.
