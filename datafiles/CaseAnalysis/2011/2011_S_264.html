Jagpal Singh & Ors. v State Of Punjab & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 70; (2011) 11 SCC 396; AIR 2011 SC 1123; 2011 (85) ALR 500; 2011 (3) AWC 2379; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1304; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 711; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 760; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 912; 2011 (113) RD 329; 2011(2) SCALE 42; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1954

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1961 - Unauthorized occupants - Gram Panchayat filed an application u/s. 7 of the Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1961 to evict the appellants who had unauthorizedly occupied the said land - It was alleged that the appellants have illegally raised constructions on the said land, and the lower officials of the department and even the Gram Panchayat colluded with them - Collector held that it would not be in the public interest to dispossess them, and directed the Gram Panchayat to recover the cost of the land from the appellants - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed - HC affirmed said order - Hence, present appeal - Held, appellants were trespassers who illegally encroached on to the Gram Panchayat land by using muscle power/money power and in collusion with the officials and even with the Gram Panchayat - Such illegalities cannot be regularized - Even if the appellants have built houses on the land in question they must be ordered to remove their constructions, and possession of the land in question must be handed back to the Gram Panchayat - Appeal dismissed.
