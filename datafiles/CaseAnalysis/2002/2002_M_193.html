Special Land Acquisition Officer, BTDA, Bagalkot v Mohd. Hanif Sahib Bawa Sahib

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 184; (2002) 3 SCC 688; AIR 2002 SC 1558; 2002 (47) ALR 320; 2002 (2) AWC 1236; JT 2002 (3) SC 176; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 164; 2002(3) SCALE 22; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 550; 2002 (2) SLT 598; 2002 (2) Supreme 473; 2002 (1) UC 673; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1547

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894-Sections 18 and 23-Acquisition of land-Compensation-Market value of land - Determination - Land acquired for construction of new township-Land not agricultural but situated within municipal limits having building potential-Market value of similar land acquired in 1979 fixed at Rs. 3 per sq. ft.-10% appreciation in market value per year reasonably admissible - Hence, market value in 1985 to be fixed at Rs. 5.10 and not at Rs. 6.85 per sq. ft. as wrongly calculated by reference court-Since base market value of Rs. 3 fixed in 1979 after deducting development charges-Development charges not deductible-And market value for acquisition in 1985 fixed at Rs. 5 per sq. ft.-Similarly, market value for acquisition in 1991 comes to be Rs. 8 per sq. ft. on basis of 10% appreciation every year-But market value fixed at Rs. 7 per sq. ft. by reference court and affirmed by High Court - No cross-appeal - Hence, market value of Rs. 7 per sq. ft. does not call for any interference.
