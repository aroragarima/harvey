M.P. Housing Board v Progressive Writers & Publishers

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 342; (2009) 5 SCC 678; AIR 2009 SC 1585; 2009 (3) AWC 2618; JT 2009 (4) SC 219; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 145; 2009(4) SCALE 119

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Whether the third building agreement dated May 31, 1980 stood automatically cancelled on account of non- compliance of the terms thereunder by the depositor and whether the second agreement dated May 4, 1977 stood automatically revived? - Held, time is not normally an essence of any agreement qua immovable properties and even there was an express covenant of time being an essence, the overall agreement have to be looked at to determine whether the time was the essence - No restatement that the award of the arbitrator is ordinarily final and the courts hearing applications under s. 30 the Act do not exercise any appellate jurisdiction - On the material available and upon appreciating the same the arbitrator arrived at the finding that the time was not of the essence and the agreement subsisted even after 31st October, 1980 - Finding cannot be said to be perverse to give rise to legal misconduct deserving intervention - Appeal dismissed.
