Kishore Bhadke v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Jagdish Singh Khehar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 3; (2017) 3 SCC 760; AIR 2017 SC 279; 2017 ALL MR (Cri) 1316; 2017 CRLJ 988; JT 2017 (1) SC 24; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 606; 2017(1) SCALE 270

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, Wrist, Vehicle, Gold, Legal Evidence, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Opportunity, Signature, Proof, Spot, Prosecution Evidence, Version, Reliance, Blood, Chain, Doctor, Dead Body, Last Seen, Disclosure Statement, Imprisonment For Life, Inference, Dealing, Two Courts, Watch, Discovery, Discrepancies, Clothes, Human Blood, Strangulation, Concurrent Finding, Panchnama, Statement Of Accused, Concurrent, Inadmissible, Rigorous Imprisonment, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Husband, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Investigating Officer, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 201, 302, 3, 364, 411 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5, 26, 27 - Murder - Conviction Challenged - Appellants nos. 1, 3, 6 along with four other accuse were charged for murder of deceased 'X' - Trial Court convicted accused nos. 1-4 and 6 u/ss. 302 r/w 120B of IPC and sentenced to suffer imprisonment for life with fine of Rs. 2000 each i/d to suffer RI for four months and u/ss. 364 r/w 120B of IPC to suffer with RI for 3 years with fine of Rs. 2000 each i/d to suffer RI for two months - A7 was convicted u/s. 201 of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for 2 years with fine of Rs 1000 i/d to suffer SI for 2 months and A5 and A7 were acquitted of offences u/s. 302, 120B, 364 of IPC - HC dismissed appeals of A1-3, 6,and 7 and granted benefit of doubt to A4, thereby acquitted of offences charged with - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, mere absence of proof of motive for commission of a crime cannot be a ground to presume the innocence of an accused if the involvement of the accused is otherwise established. But in the case of circumstantial evidence motive, does assume some relevance. If it is evident from the evidence on record that the accused had an opportunity to commit the crime and the established circumstances along with explanation of the accused, if any, exclude the reasonable possibility of anyone else being the perpetrator of the crime then the chain of evidence may be considered to show that within all human probability the crime must have been committed by the accused. Further, s. 25 of the Act mandates that no confession to a Police Officer while in police custody shall be proved as against a person accused of any offence. S. 27 of the Act however, provides that any fact deposed to and discovered in consequence of information received from a person accused of any offence, in the custody of a Police Officer, so much of such information, whether it amounts to a confession or not, as relates distinctly to the fact thereby discovered, may be proved. In instant case, the fact disclosed by A2-3 and the discovery made at their instance, was admissible against both the accused in terms of s. 27 of the Act. The interference by the Apex Court with concurrent finding of fact is justified only when it is possible to take the view that the findings are manifestly erroneous, unreasonable, unjustified or illegal or violative of some fundamental rules of procedure or natural Justice. On the facts of the present case, do not fine any tangible reason to disturb the concurrent findings recorded by the two Courts below. Hence, appeals filed by appellants are devoid of any merit and the same are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
