Sahara India (Firm), Lucknow v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central-I and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, B.N. Agrawal, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 734; (2008) 14 SCC 151; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 308; 2008 (226) E.L.T. 22; [2008] 300 ITR 403; JT 2008 (6) SC 83; 2008(6) SCALE 733; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 427; [2008] 169 TAXMAN 328

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 142(2A) - Proposition of law laid down in 'Rajesh Kumar vs Deputy CIT', as to that in every case where Assessing Officer(AO) issues a direction u/s. 142(2A) of IT Act assessee has to be heard before such an order is passed - Whether correct? - Principles of natural justice discussed, and precedents quoted and referred - Held, proposition of law laid down in Rajesh Kumar's case is correct - Exercise of power u/s. 142(2A) of Act leads to serious civil consequences and, therefore, even in absence of express provision for affording an opportunity of pre-decisional hearing to an assessee and in absence of any express provision therein barring giving of reasonable opportunity to an assessee, requirement of observance of principles of natural justice is to be read into said provision - Appeals allowed.
