Thimmappa Rai v Ramanna Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1217; (2007) 14 SCC 63; JT 2008 (8) SC 99; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 1245; 2007(7) SCALE 119; 2007 (5) SCJ 347; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Court's Jurisdiction, Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, Madras Cultivating Tenants Protection Act, 1955, Karnataka Land Reform Rules, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 4, 48-A, 55(1) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 58 - Code of Civil Procedure,1908, s. 99 - Joint Property - Occupancy Right- Partition -Justifiability - Respondent filed suit for partition in respect of certain properties - Trial Court held settlement made in favour of appellant by grant of occupancy right ensured to benefit of all heirs and legal representatives of 'X' - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC upheld order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Trial Court and HC had committed a serious error in passing decree for partition in respect of schedule 'B' property - Whether HC was justified in confirming order of Trial Court - Held, s. 4 of the 1961, Act provides that person lawfully cultivating any land belonging to another person would be deemed to be tenant, if such land was cultivated personally by landlord/owner - However, Joint family, might consist a group of persons, who need not be joint tenants - They might be tenants in common but still then if they were in joint possession of a property, same would vest in all of them, although certificate might be granted in favour of only one - Once party to suit made admission, the same could be taken in aid for determination of issue having regard to provision u/s. 58 of 1872, Act - Thus, judgment of Trial Court and HC in respect of schedule (B) and (C) of respondent were affirmed - With regard to schedule 'D' property, order was set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
