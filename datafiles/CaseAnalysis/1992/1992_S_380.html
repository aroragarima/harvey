Bhagwan Singh and Another Etc v State of Punjab Etc

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, R.C. Patnaik

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1113; (1992) 3 SCC 249; (1992) SCC (Cr) 629; AIR 1992 SC 1689; 1992 (2) CCR 100; 1992 (2) Crimes 329; 1992 CRLJ 3144; 1992 CrLR(SC) 454; JT 1992 (3) SC 216; 1992(1) SCALE 1274; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 180; 1992 (2) UJ 41

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 379 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364, 201, 34, 365, 325 and 323 - Punjab Police Rules - Kidnapping in order to murder - Trial Court acquitted appellants from charge of murder but High Court convicted them for murder on appeal by State Govt. - Whether HC correct in reversing acquittal order? - Injured witness have categorically stated that deceased became unconscious on receipt of injuries - If a person is in police custody then what has happened to him is peculiarly within knowledge of police officials who have taken him into custody - When other evidence is convincing enough to establish that deceased died because of injuries inflicted by accused circumstances would only lead to an irresistible inference that police personnel who cause his death must also have caused disappearance of body - Held, one and only inference that can be drawn is that deceased expired because of injuries caused by accused and accused persons must have caused disappearance of dead body - Appeals dismissed.
