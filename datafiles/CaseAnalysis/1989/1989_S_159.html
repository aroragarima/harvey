Lalji And Ors. v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 398; (1989) SCC (Cr) 211; (1989) 1 SCC 437; AIR 1989 SC 754; 1989 CRLJ 850; 1989 CrLR(SC) 230; JT 1989 (1) SC 109; 1989(1) SCALE 77; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 130

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1 860, ss. 302, 149, 148, 147 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants accused allegedly committed murder of deceased - Accused were convicted and sentenced u/s. 302/149, 147, 148 IPC - Appeal before HC - HC held the conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, on basis of the evidence on record both accused membership of the unlawful assembly at the relevant time has been satisfactorily established. Both the courts below having held them to have been members of the unlawful assembly, the mere fact that they were not active participants, would be of no avail. It is not open to the court to scrutinise as to whether any member of the unlawful assembly actively participated. From evidence on record it could not be held that both the accused were not members of the unlawful assembly at the the relevant time. Whether any specific injury could individually be attributed to them or not could not at all be material. The submission that the two be acquitted on ground of lack of corroboration has, therefore, to be rejected. It is not open to SC to apply the reasoning of HC to acquit members of the unlawful assembly for lack of corroboration as to their participation. No other submission was made for the other appellants. Hence, there is no merit in instant appeal. Appeal dismissed.
