Â Atam Prakash v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, G.L. Oza, P.N. Bhagwati, R.B. Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 276; (1986) 2 SCC 249; AIR 1986 SC 859; 1986 (1) CCC 641; 1986 (1) PLR 329; 1986(1) SCALE 260; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 399; 1986 (1) UJ 642

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 and 246 - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, ss. 15(1), 15(2) and 15 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 57 - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Determination of constitutional validity of statute - Guides for - (B) Pre-emption right - Validity of - (1) Right granted to co-sharers and tenants - (2) Right granted to kinsfolk on basis of consanguinity - Held, preamble to Constitution and Directive principles of State Policy are guides - Further held, ss. 15(1)(a)(iv), 15(1)(b)(iv) and (v) and 15(1)(c)(iv)granting the right of pre-emption in favour of tenants valid - Further held, pre-emption based on consanguinity is illegal and relevant clauses of s. 15(1)(c) and s. 15(2)granting such right are ultra vires - Order accordingly.
