Commissioner of Income Tax Kerala Andcoimbatore v Krishna Warriar

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1965 SC 59; [1964] 53 ITR 176; 1964 (2) SCJ 366; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Religious & Charitable purposes - Ayurvedic drugs - Claim of exemption - Notices - W who was physician and carried on business in Ayurvedic drugs died after executing a will wherein he created a trust in respect of his properties, including the Arya Vaidya Sala - As per will, 60 % of income directed to be spent on his three institutions and % to be given to his descendants - Till the Amending Act ,Income tax Department gave exemption 60 % u/s.4(3) (i) after amending exemption refused - Officer issued notices - Appeals filed against the said orders, dismissed - Further appeal to Tribunal was allowed exempting 60 % of the said income - References made to HC dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether Cl.(b) of proviso to s. 4(3) (i) applies only to a business, which is carried out in trust - Held, no - Cl.(b) of the proviso to s.4(3) (i) applies only to a business not held in trust but carried on behalf of religious or charitable institutions - For the foregoing reasons HC correctly answered the question referred to it - Business referred to in cl. (b) of the proviso need not be business which is held for religious or charitable purposes, provided it is business carried on behalf of a religious or charitable institution - Cl.(i) of s. 4(3) of Act takes in every property or a fractional part of it held in trust wholly for religious or charitable purposes - It also takes in such property held only in part for such purposes - Appeal dismissed.
