Pitta Naveen Kumar and Others v Raja Narasaiah Zangiti and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 567; (2006) 10 SCC 261; 2007 (1) ESC 1; 2006(9) SCALE 298; 2007 (1) SLR 341; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Competition; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 309 - Andhra Pradesh State and Subordinate Service Rules, rr. 16, 22 - Selection process - Notification issued - Held, relaxation can be given only if there exists any provision therefor in Rules - GOMs No 200 dated 30 th April, 2005, must fall having regard to vacation of interim order by Tribunal and consequent dismissal of original application - Govt. did not apply its own mind - Only those candidates who came within purview of rule existing thereto before could have been subjected to further selection process - Those who have not passed written examination would not be entitled to be considered in terms of directions - Appeals partly allowed.
