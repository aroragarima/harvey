Carew and Company Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 394; (1975) 2 SCC 791; AIR 1975 SC 2260; [1976] 46 Comp Cas 121; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Monopolies & Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 2 and 23(4) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 13 - Interpretation of statutes - Scope of definition u/s. 2 and applicability of s. 23(4) of 1969 Act - Which of the two feasible interpretations of a remedial legislation should be construed? - Permissibility of grammatical construction of words and phrases - Definition attracted if context so requires 'Undertaking' - Held, that s. 23 (4) did not apply to facts - Interpretation advancing remedy should be preferred - Grammar observed to be a good guide to meaning but bad master to dictate - Appeal allowed.
