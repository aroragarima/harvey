Suraz India Trust v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 463; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 721; 2017(7) SCALE 101

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Oath, Right To Information Act, 2005, Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968, Supreme Court Rules, 1966, Constitution of India, 1950, Supreme Court Rules, 2013, National Judicial Appointments Commission Act, 2014, Constitution (Ninety-ninth Amendment) Act, 2014, Criminal Procedure Amendment Act, 1923, Rajasthan Right to Information (High Court & Subordinate Court) Rules, 2006

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 , art.124(4) - Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968, s.3 - Exemplary costs for wasting time of Judiciary - Allegations on Judges - Instant petition by petitioner-Trust to declare the provisions of s.3 of the Act as unconstitutional and void, the same being inconsistent and in contravention of the provisions of art.124(4) of the Constitution.

Held, posting of matter filed by petitioner, by the then CJ, before three-judge bench was also a matter which was unnecessarily agitated repeatedly and more so, by filing contempt petitions against then CJ himself. Filing contempt petitions, on issues which lacked justification, also highlighted the petitioner-Trust's illegitimate misadventures. Further, Rajiv Daiya appearing for petitioner-Trust expressed his ire even against six judges of Rajasthan HC including its CJ and against three Judges of SC besides CJI. However, all such actions of petitioner-Trust are wholly unjustified and Rajiv Daiya had not attempted to even make slightest effort to reason out the same or to demonstrate the veracity of his actions. For such actions of Rajiv Daiya, though sterner action could be taken, yet, just for once same is not done and in future, such leniency could not be shown and instant order is to be considered as a warning for future. However, petitioner-Trust is directed henceforth to refrain itself absolutely, from filing any cause in public interest, before any Court in the country. Further, Rajiv Daiya also absolutely refrain himself from filing any cause in public interest, either directly or through any other individual, in any Court. Further, in all pending matters, before SC or before any HC, initiated by petitioner-Trust or by Rajiv Daiya, as a cause of pubic interest, it is imperative for them, to place instant order on record of the case, in case the petitioner decides not to withdraw the same unilaterally. Further, in view of judicial time wasted by petitioner-Trust, exemplary costs of Rs. 25,000 are imposed on petitioner-Trust and in case said costs are not deposited, then Registry is to list the matter for directions for recovery of costs. Petition disposed of.
