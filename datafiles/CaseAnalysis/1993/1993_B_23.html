Gadakhyashwantrao Kankarrao v Balasaheb Vikhe Patil

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 779; (1994) 1 SCC 682; AIR 1994 SC 678; 1994 (1) CCR 102; JT 1993 (6) SC 345; 1993(4) SCALE 424

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 123, 123(4), 101(b) - Post Office Rules, 1933, r. 195 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Corrupt Practices - (A) Malpractices - Prescription of ceiling on expenditure by candidate - (B) Ambit of 'Statement of fact' used in s. 123(4) - (C) Whether following statements amounts to 'Corrupt practice' - Statements - (1) Candidate contributed Rs. 50 lakhs to election fund of political party for getting its support - (2) By returned candidate about payment of Rs. 20 lakhs by election petitioner to one of candidates for withdrawing his nomination and shifting to another constituency - Reasonably calculated to prejudice prospects - Statement proved beyond any doubt - (3) By returned candidate as to proposal of election petitioner to take out a rally of 5,000 bicycles and then distribute bicycle to participants - Merely an expression, could not affect mind of voters - Others requirements of s.123(4) not satisfied - (4) Mere statement as to hiring of workers for election campaign - (D) Statement made by Chief Minister of party at public meeting - If amount to corrupt practice in light of s. 123(4) - Statement (1) Expression of his opinion based on apprehensions about likely future conduct of said candidate or relating only to his political character - (2) That opposite candidate had filed his nomination from constituency instead of another one which he represented five times because it was a poor region and a scarcity area thinking that it was a good circumstance to win easily - If relates to personal character or conduct of opposite candidate envisaged by s. 123(4) - (3) That candidate in opposition suffered from illusion that poor people of constituency could be purchased with his resources but those people were men possessed of self respect who could not be purchased with anybody's money - (4) Referring to likelihood in future of distribution of articles and wealth by opposite candidate - Emphasis was on fact that voters cannot be bought even if such a distribution was made by any candidate and not that any such distribution was being made by opposite candidate - (5) Campaign by Senior leaders of political parties - It is their duty to ensure that campaign of vilification ( E) Post Office Rules - Certificate of posting - Creditability as an evidence - (F) Validity of Election, in light of mere fact that election petitioner secured next highest number of votes after returned candidate -

(A) Is mere eye-wash in absence of ceiling on expenditure incurred by party or anyone else in his election campaign - (B) Any statement made which is a conjecture of livelihood in future, not covered - (C) In circumstances it is doubtful if such payment can amount to corrupt practice - Said false statement of fact in respect of personal character and conduct of election petitioner amounts to corrupt practice - Not a corrupt practice - (D) Is not a disparaging act relating to personal character or conduct of anyone unless further requirements of s. 123(4) are proved - Such statement even if made by returned candidate does not constitute corrupt practice u/s. 123(4) - Statement not covered by s. 123(4) - Statement does not fall within s. 123(4) - Though not amounting corrupt practice, held were not up to desired level of electioneering (E) Is easy to procure, does not inspire confidence - (F) Void for commission of corrupt practices by returned candidate and fact not sufficient to declare him elected - Appeal allowed.
