Â B. V. Patankar and Others v C. G. Sastry

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 460; AIR 1961 SC 272; 1961 (1) SCJ 221; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Prohibition Act - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 254 and 372 - Appellants are decree-holders and respondent is judgment debtor - Father of appellants leased to respondent house in dispute for a period of 10 years with an option of renewal for further periods for as long as respondent wanted - HC held that Executing Court had no jurisdiction to order eviction of respondent because of provisions of Mysore House Rent and Accommodation Control Order, 1948, which was in operation on date of eviction - (A) Whether provisions of two house Rent Control Orders of 1945 and 1948 and how far they were applicable to proceedings in suit and execution? - Held, laws of State applying to leases which would include Mysore House Rent Control Order of 1948 continued to be in force and applicable to cases that were pending till it was repealed by Mysore Rent Control Act of 1951 which received President's assent on 16-08-1951 - Argument, therefore, that as from 01-04-1951, as a result of repugnancy House Rent Control order of 1948 stood repealed must be repelled as unsound and cannot be sustained, because it was an existing law which was saved by art. 372 of Constitution and remained unaffected by art. 254 - (B) Whether plea of res judicata is available to appellants? - Further held, plea of res judicata is not available as prohibition on account of House Rent Control Order was not against passing of decree but against its execution and therefore objection to executability could only be taken at time of execution of decree which in instant case could not be done because order for delivery by Executing Court was passed without notice to respondent - Appeal dismissed.
