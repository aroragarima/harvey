State of Punjab v Hardyal

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 364; (1985) 2 SCC 629; AIR 1985 SC 920; 1985 ARBLR 297; 1985 (2) CCC 182; 1985(1) SCALE 675; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 649; 1985 UJ 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 28, 30, 28(1) and 28(2) - (A) Fixing of time limit within which award will be given by arbitrator - Whether permissible? - It is open to parties to fix time within which arbitrator must give award, but it has to be so stated in agreement itself - If perchance no time has been specified then award must be given within four months of arbitrator entering on reference or after having been called upon to act by notice in writing from any party to arbitration agreement or within such extended time as court may allow - Further held, s. 28(2) makes it evident that court alone has power to extend time - It further provides that a clause in arbitration agreement giving arbitrator power to enlarge time shall be void and of no effect except when all parties consent to such enlargement - It is not open to arbitrators at their own pleasure without consent of parties to agreement to enlarge time for making award - (B) What will be effect if a party to arbitration took part in proceeding before arbitrator, even after expiry of four months? - If award made beyond time is invalid parties are not estopped by then conduct from challenging award on ground that it was made that it was made beyond time merely because of their having participated in proceedings before arbitrator after expiry of prescribed period - Parties have been taking willing part in proceedings before arbitrator without a will be a fit case, for extension of time - Accordingly extend time for giving award and award was be deemed to have been given in time - Appeal partly allowed.
