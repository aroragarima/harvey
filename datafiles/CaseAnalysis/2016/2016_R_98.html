Ratti Ram v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 2016 SC 1120; 2016(5) ALL MR 471; 2016 (116) ALR 540; 2016 (2) AWC 2111; JT 2016 (3) SC 50; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2539; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 463; 2016 (132) RD 184; 2016(9) SCALE 78; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 989; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 188

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 28 - Land acquisition - Denial of statutory benefits - Appellant's land was acquired - Land value was fixed by SC - Appellant denied statutory benefits for a short period on the ground that proceedings initiated at the instance of appellants remained stayed before Reference Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, once the land value is fixed by SC, it refers to the value of the land as per the Award passed by the Collector. That should carry all eligible statutory benefits. It is failed to understand how the appellants could be denied the statutory benefits available under the Act in respect of the value of this land fixed, merely because there was a period of stay operating, may be in a proceeding at the instance of the appellants. Those are not relevant considerations or factors at all for the purpose of grant of statutory benefits available to a person, whose land has been acquired in terms of s. 28 of the Act. There is no exclusion of any period contemplated on whatever account u/s. 28 of the Act. The only reference is to the date of dispossession. Liability to pay interest starts to run from that date. It is directed that the appellants shall be entitled to interest for the compensation, as per s. 28 r/w s. 23(1A), in respect of the land acquired from the appellants, on value for the period of stay also. Order accordingly.
