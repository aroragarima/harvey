Fazal Ghafoor v Principal, Osmania Medical College, Hyderabad and others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 951; (1988) 4 SCC 532; AIR 1988 SC 2288; JT 1988 (3) SC 699

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admissions) Order, 1974, Andhra Pradesh University of Health Sciences Act, 1986

Summary: Education - Andhra Pradesh University of Health Sciences Act, 1986 - Andhra Pradesh Educational Institutions (Regulation of Admissions) Order, 1974, r. 2(f) - Petitioner ranked as number one in medical entrance examination and opted to undertake DM (Neurology) course where it had only one seat - Petitioner's claim rejected on the basis that he was not a local candidate within the meaning of Presidential Order - Petitioner filed writ petition contending that he was a local candidate and there was no such reservation for local candidates for super-speciality course - Single Judge rejected petitioner's contention holding that he could not be considered as a local candidate as he studied for the MD course in Telangana region only for three years while the requirement was that the candidate should have studied in an educational institution in the local area for a period of not less than four consecutive years ending with the academic year in which he first appeared in the relevant qualifying examination - Petitioner filed writ appeal - DB held that State-wide university was defined in r. 2(f) and the expression would not take within its fold either the University of Health Sciences or the concerned Medical College - Held, no merit in the petition - Petition dismissed.
