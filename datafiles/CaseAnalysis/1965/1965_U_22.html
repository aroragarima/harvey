State of Orissa v Utkal Distributors Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 1966 SC 1170; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 55; 1966 (17) STC 320

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax; Socio-Economic

Summary: Sales Tax - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1957- Issue arise - Whether in facts and circumstances of case, Tribunal is right in holding that Central sales tax paid by opposite party at its purchase point and charged on to its customers does not form a part of sale price of commodity sold so as to be taxable under Orissa Act? - Held, following principles laid down in Madras decision, Orissa High Court held that Central sales tax could never form part of 'sale price' as defined in Orissa Act, and was rightly deducted while estimating taxable turnover - HC is right - Expression 'valuable consideration' and word 'turnover' do not include central sales tax paid by assessee - Further held, fact that price which stock-holder was entitled to charge was statutorily fixed and stock-holder was not entitled to and did not charge more are sufficient to enable to come to conclusion that central sales tax paid under provisions of Iron and Steel (Control) Notification did not form part of price paid by customer to assessee - Assessee by paying central sales tax when he bought goods did not do anything to goods, and tax was paid in respect of transaction of purchase and not in respect of goods - Appeals dismissed.
