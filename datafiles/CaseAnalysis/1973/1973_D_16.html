B. G. Goswami v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 158; (1973) SCC (Cr) 796; (1974) 3 SCC 85; AIR 1973 SC 1457; 1974 CRLJ 243; 1973 CRLR 541; 1973 CrLR(SC) 541; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal -Constitution - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Illegal gratification - Appellant convicted by trial court but sentence reduced by High Court to one year - Present appeal - Held, in the present case it is the same facts which constitute an offence u/s. 161, IPC and u/s. 5(1)(d) r/w s. 5(2 ) - Case u/s. 161 IPC, 1860 fully proved by prosecution - Further held, sentence of imprisonment can be for a lesser period but in that event Court has to assign special reasons which must be recorded in writing - Punishment is designed to protect society by deterring potential offenders as also by preventing guilty party from repeating offence, it is also designed to reform offender and reclaim him as a law abiding citizen for good of society as a whole - Too lenient as well as too harsh sentences both lose their efficacy - It would meet ends of justice if sentence is reduced to that already undergone but increase sentence of fine - Appeal partly allowed.
