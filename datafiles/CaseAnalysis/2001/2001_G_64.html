Mohandas and Others v Ghisia Bai and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21043; AIR 2002 SC 2436; 2001 (44) ALR 385; JT 2001 (5) SC 263; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 266A; 2001 (92) RD 732; 2001 (3) SLT 185

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 1, O. 17, rr. 2, 3 and 16 - Suit for declaration and permanent injunction - Application moved u/O. 17, r. 16 rejected - Plaintiff-appellants sought adjournment u/O. 7, r. 1 rejected and finally Trial Court dismissed suit u/O. 17, r. 3 of CPC, 1908 - First appeal dismissed and second appeal High Court was of view that suit was dismissed not u/O. 17, r. 2, but was dismissed u/O. 17, r.3 - Validity of High Court order challenged - Held, neither plaintiff-appellant nor his witnesses were present on date of case, therefore, case has to be dismissed u/O. 17, r. 2 - Even r. 3 itself provides that if parties or any of them absent, court shall proceed to decide suit u/O. 17, r. 2 - Appeal allowed.
