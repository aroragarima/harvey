Kanta Prashad v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 58; AIR 1958 SC 350; 1958 CRLJ 698; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 113; 1958 SCJ 668; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1218

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Non Cognizable

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 367, 233, 537, 196 A, 337 and 338 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 (2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 211, 222, 224 and 109 - (A) Whether failure to hold a test identification parade would make inadmissible evidence of identification in court? - Held, such evidence is generally of a weak nature - Courts of fact has to determine what weight has to be attached to such identification - (B) Whether Supreme Court can enquire into question that a sanction has been granted u/s. 196 A? - Further held, such question is a question of fact to be raised at trial stage or before High Court - It is impossible at appellate stage in Supreme Court to look into this question of fact - (C) Whether District Magistrate has power to tender pardon u/s. 337 of CrPC, 1898? - Further held, yes - District Magistrate has authority to tender a pardon u/s 337 as court of special judge is in law, a Court of Session - Appeal dismissed.
