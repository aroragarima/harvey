Bhagwan Sahai v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 269; AIR 1960 SC 487; 1960 CRLJ 676

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(1)(a) - Conviction of appellant for abusing official position u/s. 5(1)(a) of Corruption Act - Appellant abuses official position for pressing others to buy shares of company of his son so that to get mutations attested by appellant - Whether sanction for prosecution purported to be u/s. 5(1)(d) and therefore appellant could not be convicted u/s. 5(1)(a) and (2) that High Court had no power to convert conviction from one u/s. 5(1)(a) to one u/s. 5(1)(d) because charge also was u/s. 5(1)(a)? - Held, in the present case what the appellant was accused of doing and is proved to have done was attesting mutations and that was the doing of an official act - Therefore, the sanction, falls both within cl. (a) of s. 5 (1) and cl. (d) of s. 5(1) - Since the sanction is construed as relating to both offences u/s. 5(1)(a) and s. 5(1)(d) of the Act the charge u/s. 5(1)(a) could have been validly framed - HC has not in terms altered the conviction from 5(1)(a) to 5(1)(d) although some of its observations have given room for such an argument - What the HC meant was that the appellant had not taken a bribe in the sense that word in usually understood - The appellant had abused his position and helped another person to derive a pecuniary advantage thereby - The facts, however, show that he had done this consistently for over several months - This would amount to his habitually obtaining in the discharge of his duty gratification for another person - The HC's judgment is therefore consistent with the charge framed u/s. 5(1)(a) - Accordingly, Supreme Court construes the HC judgment as upholding the conviction u/s. 5(1)(a) - Appeal dismissed.
