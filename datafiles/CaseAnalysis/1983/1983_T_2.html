A. A. Calton v Director of Education and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.P. Thakkar, Amarendra Nath Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 20; (1983) 3 SCC 33; (1983) SCC (L&S) 356; AIR 1983 SC 1143; 1983 ALJ 516; 1983 (9) ALR 304; 1983 (47) FLR 34; 1983 (1) LLJ 502; 1983 (1) LLN 819B; 1983(1) SCALE 316; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 598; 1983 (1) SLJ 454; 1983 (1) SLR 785; 1983 UJ 398

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - U.P. Intermediate Education Act, 1921, s. 16-E - Appointment - Validity - Selection Committee recommended three persons along with appellant for the post of Principal of Ranikhet Intermediate College - Appellant was given third rank in that recommendation - Regional Deputy Director of Education did not approve of said selection - Matter was remitted to Selection Committee - Selection Committee recommended names of appellant and respondent No. 2 assigning respondent No. 2 higher rank and same was disapproved by Deputy Director - Selection Committee made third recommendation where in appellant preferred petition before HC questioning validity of third selection made by Selection Committee - HC allowed the same and quashed the selection made by Selection Committee - Director appointed respondent No. 2 to post by his order - Appellant filed petition before HC and questioned validity of appointment of respondent No. 2 as Principal and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified or does it call for interference - Held, proceedings for selection had commenced in year 1973 and after Deputy Director had disapproved recommendations made by Selection Committee twice Director acquired jurisdiction to make an appointment from amongst qualified candidates who had applied for vacancy in question - At instance of appellant himself in earlier petition filed by him HC had directed Director to exercise that power - Such proceedings had to be continued in accordance with law as it stood at commencement of said proceedings - SC did not find any substance in contention of appellant that law as amended by the Act should have been followed in present case - It was to be stated that Act does not state that person who had been recommended by Selection Committee once and whose selection had been disapproved by Deputy Director should not be considered for post in question by Director when he exercises his power u/s. 16-F (4) of the Act - Respondent No. 2 satisfied requirement of u/s. 16-F (4) of the Act - Fact that Deputy Director had disapproved recommendation of Selection Committee recommending him for post in question once before could not be construed as amounting to disqualification - It was also to be noticed that u/s. 16-F(4) of the Act it was Director who was authorized to make appointment of Principal and not Deputy Director - There was therefore, no ground to interfere with judgment of HC - Appeal dismissed.
