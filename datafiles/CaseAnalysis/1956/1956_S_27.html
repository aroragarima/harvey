Kuldip Singh v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 110; AIR 1956 SC 391; 1956 CRLJ 781; 1956 (58) PLR 595; 1956 SCJ 387; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prohibition Act - Respondent no. 2 filed civil suit against appellant for recovery of money on basis of a mortgage - Appellant filed receipt which purported to show that he had paid, Trial court and all appellate courts held that the receipt filed by appellant was not satisfactory - Respondent then made application in Court asking that complaint be filed against, appellant u/ss. 193, 471 of IPC - But before it could be heard first class judge was transferred, Subordinate Judge of fourth class, was sent who had reported to District Judge that he had no jurisdiction because offences had been committed in Court of Subordinate Judge of first class - DJ there upon transferred matter to Senior Subordinate Judge, Appellant filed appeal against order to Additional District Judge to adjudicate matter - HC, held that Senior Subordinate Judge had jurisdiction and that material disclosed prima facie case - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in coming to conclusion that Senior subordinate judge had no jurisdiction - Held, that HC had no jurisdiction to act u/s. 476 in this case, s. 195 contains express prohibition against taking cognizance of kind of complaint unless bar was lifted either by original Court or Court to which it was subordinate within meaning of s. 195(3) - Those were only Courts invested with jurisdiction to lift ban and make complaint - According to special definition in s. 195(3), it had no jurisdiction to make complaint of its own authority - Therefore, all that HC could, and should, had done was to send case to District Judge for disposal according to law - Appeal disposed of.
