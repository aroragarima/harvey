United Bank of India v Pijush Kanti Nandy and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1088; (2009) 8 SCC 605; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 185; 2009 (122) FLR 988; 2009 (4) LLJ 13; 2009(11) SCALE 318; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension, Pending Enquiry, United Bank of India (Officers) Service Regulations, 1979, United Bank of India (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1993, United Bank of India (Employees) Pension Regulation, 1995, Central Reserve Police Force, the Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972

Summary: Service - United Bank of India (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1995, regn.29(5) - Pension - Exercise of option - Denial - Respondent voluntarily retired from appellant bank service - Pension introduced in banking service - Appellant has given option for pension to employees retired since 1986 - Respondent applied for exercising the option - Application of respondent was dismissed on ground that he failed to complete qualifying service - Respondent sought writ - Single Judge allowed respondent's writ petition - However, DB dismissed appellant's intra court appeal - Legality - Respondent contended that he entitled to pension as per regn.29(5) - Held, service 'otherwise' rendered by an employee u/regn.29 means relationship of employer and employee must continue at all relevant times - Respondent was not in service during the relevant time - Regn.29(5) would be attracted only in a case where concerned employee has completed 20 years of qualifying service - Respondent has completed only 17 years of service - Therefore, respondent not entitled to pension, hence impugned judgments set aside - Appeal allowed.
