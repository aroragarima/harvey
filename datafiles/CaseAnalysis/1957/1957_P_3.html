Kanai Lal Sur v Paramnidhi Sadhukhan

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 96; AIR 1957 SC 907; 1958 SCJ 99; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 360

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1953 - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, ss. 2(5), 3 and 5(1) - Whether u/s. 5 (1) of Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949 as amended by Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1953, execution proceedings taken out by decree holder against him could be entertained only by controller and not by Civil Courts? - Held, s. 3 lays down six grounds on which a thika tenant may be ejected - Grounds other than those mentioned in s. 3 of Act of 1949 on which a landlord would have been entitled to eject his thika tenant under provisions of Transfer of Property Act became inapplicable to case of thika tenants by virtue of s. 3 of Act of 1949 - If words used are capable of one construction only then it would not be open to courts to adopt any other hypothetical construction on ground that such hypothetical construction is more consistent with alleged object and policy of Act - When material words are capable of two constructions, one of which is likely to defeat or impair policy of Act whilst other construction is likely to assist achievement of said policy, then courts would prefer to adopt latter construction sub-s.(1) of s. 3 deals with cases where landlord initiates original proceedings for ejecting his thika tenant (2) refers to a landlord wishing to eject a thika tenant on one or more of grounds specified in s. 3 - Landlords who have obtained decrees of ejectment against their thika tenants cannot be required to apply under provisions of s. 5(1) of Act - After a decree has been passed in favour of landlord, a claim for execution of decree would have to be entertained and considered by Controller u/s 5(1) - Civil Courts had no jurisdiction to entertain execution petition filed by respondent against appellant - Appeal dismissed.
