Naseem Bano Smt v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 553; (1993) Supp4 SCC 46; (1994) SCC (L&S) 31; AIR 1993 SC 2592; 1993 (22) ALR 307; 1993 (2) CLR 750; JT 1993 (4) SC 552; 1993 (4) SLR 803; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1172

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Education - Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921, s. 16 G - (A) Regulations under, regns. 5(2)(a) and 6(1) Appendix A - Promotion to L.T. grade - Teacher in C.T. grade fulfilling conditions contemplated by CL 1 of regn. 6, i.e., having five years' teaching experience and minimum qualifications for teaching subject as prescribed in Appendix A to Regulations - 40% of posts in L. T . grade not filled by promotion - Whether teacher is entitled to promotion? - (B) S. 16 G of Act - Regulations under, regns. 5(2)(a) and 6(1) - Alleged that 40% of L. T. grade posts not filled in by promotion as contemplated by regn. 5(2)(a) - Proof - Out of ten teachers working in L .T. grade only three teachers having requisite experience - Appointment of remaining, if could be presumed to be by way of promotion - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114, illustration (e) - Held, (A) Is entitled to promotion - Could not be so presumed, in disregard of prescribed qualifications - (B) Appointment being governed by statutory regulation is official act and presumption of regularity attaches to it - Appeal allowed.
