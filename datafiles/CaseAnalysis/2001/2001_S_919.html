Siyaram Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21503; 2001 (4) MPLJ 205

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Discretion of authority - Interference by HC - Permissibility - In N. D. Singhal vs. State of MP, 1998 Indlaw MPLJ 19, HC issued certain directions that in motor vehicles accidents claims, claimants are facing a great deal of inconvenience in getting compensation, and as such the direction purports to redress the grievance of the claimants generally - In view of said directions, HC by impugned order refused to exercise its power u/s. 482 CrPC - Hence, instant petition - Petitioner contended that said direction takes away discretion of appropriate authority under CrPC and tantamounts to a legislation by Court, which Court does not possess - Held, direction in question takes away discretion of appropriate Court under different provisions of CrPC, which cannot be taken away by observation of HC howsoever beneficiary directions may be - Hence, discretion of the appropriate Court under any provision of law is not fettered with the said directions - Petition disposed of.
