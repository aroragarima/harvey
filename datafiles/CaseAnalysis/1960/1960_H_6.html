Â Hingir-Rampur Coal Company, Limited and Others v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 452; AIR 1961 SC 459; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 537

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals

Summary: P. B. Gajendragadkar, J. (Majority)

Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952 - Constitutionality of Act - Instant petition filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution in which the validity of the Act was challenged - Petitioners contended that the impugned Act and' the rules made thereunder were ultra vires the powers of the Legislature of the State of Orissa, or in any event they were repugnant to the provisions of an existing law - It was stated that the cess levied under the impugned Act was not a fee but was a levy in the nature of a duty of excise on the coal produced at the petitioner's Rampur colliery, and as such was beyond the legislative competence of the Orissa Legislature - Whether the levy imposed by the impugned Act amounts to a fee relatable to Entry 23 read with Entry 66 in List II.

Held, scheme of the Act thus showed that it has been passed for the purpose of the development of mining areas in the State. The object of the Act is to regulate the scheduled industries with a view to improvement and development of the service that they may render to the society, and thus assist the solution of the larger problem of national economy. Further, where an impugned statute passed by a State Legislature is relatable to an Entry in List II it is not permissible to challenge its vires only on the ground that the method adopted by it for the recovery of the impost can be and is generally adopted in levying a duty of excise. Thus considered the conclusion is inevitable that the cess levied by the impugned Act is neither a tax nor a duty of excise but is a fee. The impugned Act is relatable to Entries 23 and 66 in List II of the Seventh Schedule, and its validity is not impaired or affected by Entries 52 and 54 in List I. Petition dismissed.

WANCHOO, J.- (Dissenting)

Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Orissa Mining Areas Development Fund Act, 1952, s.4 - Constitutionality of Act - Instant petition filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution in which the validity of the Act was challenged - Whether the cess in the case is a tax or a fee.

Held, the instant case is not a tax on mineral rights; it is a tax on the minerals produced and can be no different in pith and substance from a tax on goods produced which comes under Item 84 of List I, as duty of excise. The present levy therefore u/s. 4 of the Act cannot be justified as a tax on mineral rights. The Act is beyond the Constitutional competence of the Orissa Legislature to pass it. The whole Act must be struck down because there will be very little left in the Act if s. 4 falls as it must. The legislature would never have passed the Act without s. 4 of the Act. Petition allowed.
