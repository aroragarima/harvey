J. B. Boda and Company Private Limited v Central Board of Direct Taxes

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3546; (1997) 1 SCC 719; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 247; AIR 1997 SC 1543; 1997 (137) CTR 287; [1997] 223 ITR 271; JT 1996 (10) SC 47; 1996(7) SCALE 870; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 145; 1996 (7) Supreme 760; [1996] 89 TAXMAN 311

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80O - Interpretation of s. 80O - Appellant a private company and brokerage by reinsurance - Assessee Co. remitted reinsurance premia to foreign companies after deduction of amount of technical assistance - Held, income is received in India in convertible foreign exchange, in a lawful and permissible manner through Reserve Bank of India - Proceedings of Central Board of Direct Taxes declining to approve agreements is proper and illegal - Answered in favour of assessee - Appeal allowed.
