A. N. D’Silva v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 323; AIR 1962 SC 1130; 1962 (2) SCJ 126; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950 arts. 311(2) and 320(3) - Civil Services Rules -(A) Whether art. 311(2) of Constitution is violated if punishment imposed is stricter than punishment prescribed under Civil Service Rules? - Held, Civil Services Rules merely prescribe diverse punishments which may be imposed upon delinquent public servants - Power of President to impose any punishment is unrestricted - Constitution guarantees protection of a reasonable opportunity of showing cause against action proposed -(B)Whether UPSC acts as an appellate authority over disciplinary matters affecting Govt. employees? - Further held, u/art. 320(3) of Constitution, Union Public Service Commission shall be consulted in all disciplinary matters affecting Govt. employees - But Union Public Service Commission is not an appellate authority over enquiry officer - Appeal dismissed.
