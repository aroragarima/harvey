Ravi Khullar and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 287; (2007) 5 SCC 231; AIR 2007 SC 2334; 2007 (3) CTC 574; 2007 (139) DLT 506; JT 2007 (6) SC 25; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 660; 2007 (4) MPLJ 205; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 757; 2007(5) SCALE 236; 2007 (6) SCJ 12; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 598

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals disposed of., Displaced Person, Non Compliance

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Acquisition of land in Village Nangal Dewat for a public purpose, namely - Development of Palam Airport - Whether in view of the public purpose declared in the Notification under s. 4 of the Act, the lands can be utilized for any other public purpose? - Held, acquisition cannot be invalidated only on the ground that the public purpose is sought to be achieved through another agency - Since there is no change of public purpose for which the acquired land is being utilized, the acquisition cannot be invalidated on that ground - Acquisition cannot be challenged on the ground that the acquired lands are not being utilized for the declared public purpose - So far as the acquisition of the lands belonging to Palam Potteries is concerned, the proceedings lapsed for failure of the Collector to make an award within the prescribed period of limitation - Appeals disposed of.
