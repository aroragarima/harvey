Basudeo Yadav v Surendra Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1356; (2008) 15 SCC 124; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 744; AIR 2009 SC 546; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 569; 2008 (4) JCC 2254; JT 2008 (9) SC 429; 2008(11) SCALE 700; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 593

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conjectures

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 149, 201, 148 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, High Court has no where stated that the analysis of evidence and the conclusions arrived at by the Trial Court were erroneous - Without recording such a finding, the High Court was not justified in drawing different conclusions without indicating any reason to justify the same - It would not be possible for the High Court to act on surmises and conjectures and disturb the findings recorded by the Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
