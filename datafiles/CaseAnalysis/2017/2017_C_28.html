Central Bureau of Investigation v M. Sivamani

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 541; JT 2017 (7) SC 378; 2017 (3) KLT 701; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 611

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conspiracy, CBI, Interest Of Justice, Cognizance, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 195(1), 195(1)(a)(i) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 182, 420, 468, 471, 511 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 13(i)(d) - Cognizance of offence - Legality - Appellants/CBI filed charge sheet u/ss. 120B r/w ss.182, 420, 468, 468 r/w s. 471 IPC and ss. 13(2) r/w 13(i)(d) of the Act r/w s. 511 IPC against A1 to A9/respondents - Respondent/A5 filed a petition before Trial Court contending that cognizance of offence u/s. 182 IPC could not be taken except on complaint in writing of public servant concerned or of some other public servant to whom he was administratively subordinate - Trial Court dismissed petition - On a revision before HC, HC reversed the order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, bar against cognizance of a specified offence was mandatory, the same has to be understood in the context of the purpose for which such a bar is created. The bar is not intended to take away remedy against a crime but only to protect an innocent person against false or frivolous proceedings by a private person. It was implicit in the direction of HC that it was necessary in the interest of justice to take cognizance of offence in question. Direction of HC was at par with the direction of an administrative superior public servant to file a complaint in writing in terms of the statutory requirement. The protection intended by the Section against a private person filing a frivolous complaint is taken care of when HC finds that the matter was required to be gone into in public interest. Such direction cannot be rendered futile by invoking s. 195 CrPC to such a situation. Once HC directs investigation into a specified offence mentioned in s. 195 CrPC, bar u/s. 195(1)(a) CrPC cannot be pressed into service. The view taken by HC would frustrate the object of law and cannot be sustained. Appeals allowed.
