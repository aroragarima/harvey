Vice Chancellor, Guru Ghasidas University v Craig Mcleod

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 265; (2012) 11 SCC 275; AIR 2012 SC 3356; 2012 (6) AWC 6191; 2012(7) SCALE 330; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 270; 2012 (5) SLR 603; 2012 (3) UPLBEC 2430

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Rustication - Challenged - Respondent grossly misbehaved with 2 Professors of the University - As a result of the incident, respondent was suspended from attending his classes, stopped from availing facilities of the University and restrained from entering the University premises - Respondent challenged said order before HC who stayed an order of rustication - Subsequently HC granted liberty to the University to take a final decision in the matter of the alleged gross misbehaviour of respondent and not extended the interim order - Respondent filed an appeal against said order - An office order was passed by the University rusticating respondent from the University for a period of 5 years - It was also ordered that respondent was not entitled to get admission in any course in the University or any affiliated college of University during this period of 5 years - Respondent challenged said order before HC - Later, respondent withdraw it with liberty to move an appropriate application in SC since this appeal was still pending - Held, liberty was granted to the respondent to revive earlier writ petition filed by him in the HC challenging the office order passed by the University - Impugned order was set aside - Appeal disposed of.
