Veer Prakash Sharma v Anil Kumar Agarwal and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 795; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 370; (2007) 7 SCC 373; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2618; 2008 (1) BC 253; 2007 CRLJ 3735; 2007 (3) JCC 2192; JT 2007 (10) SC 57; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 960; 2007(9) SCALE 502; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Indian Penal Code, s. 406, 409, 402, 417 - Application for quashing of criminal proceeding dismissed - Appeal against - Held, only because appellant had issued cheques which were dishonoured, the same by itself would not mean that he had cheated the complainant - There is nothing on record to show that any part of the cause of action arose within the jurisdiction of the court concerned - Non-payment or under-payment of the price of the goods by itself does not amount to commission of an offence of cheating or criminal breach of trust - Appeal allowed.
