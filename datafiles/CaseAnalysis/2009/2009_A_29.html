V. Laxminarasamma v A. Yadaiah (Dead) & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 277; (2009) 5 SCC 478; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 711; JT 2009 (4) SC 287; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1327; 2009(3) SCALE 685; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Questions Of Title, Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Rules, 1988, Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) (Amendment) Act, 1987, Andhra Pradesh Land Encroachment Act, 1905

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Determination of a question of adverse possession whether would come within the purview of the jurisdiction of Special Tribunal and/or Special Court constituted? - Held, Special Courts and Tribunals, indisputably are entitled to determine any question or issue including the question of title or possession in the proceedings initiated before it - Special Courts and the Tribunal not only have trappings of a court but also of a civil court and, thus, are entitled to determine complicated questions of title - Tribunal/Special Court constituted under the Act has the requisite jurisdiction to go into the question of adverse possession - Order accordingly.
