Surendra Nath Pandey and Others v UP Cooperative Bank Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 183; (2010) 12 SCC 400; 2010 (125) FLR 1045; 2010 (2) LLJ 297; 2010(3) SCALE 106; 2010 (5) SLR 329

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Daily wage - Increments - Benefit of pay scales - Entitlement - Appellants were appointed on daily wage basis by respondent no.1-Bank, by way of stop gap arrangement - From 1-7-1981, appellants were paid consolidated salary of certain amount per month which was increased certain amount from dt.1-4-1982 - From dt.1-7-1983, they were extended benefit of minimum in pay scale applicable to regular employees, i.e. Rs.325/- per month, with allowances, but without yearly increments - Appellants were regularized on different dates and they were extended benefit of regular pay scales with all allowances - Appellants approached HC by filing writ petition sought benefit of regular pay scale, allowances and other benefits which were extended to regular employees, with effect from date of their stop gap or ad-hoc appointment - Single Judge of HC allowed petitions extending benefit of regular pay scale with annual increments with effect from dt.1-7-1981 - Bank appealed and DB of HC allowed special appeals of Bank, set aside judgment of Single Judge and dismissed petitions - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellants were entitled to such relief on ground that such relief had been extended to some similarly placed employees in pursuance of decision in Jai Kishan - Held, particular judgment of HC should not be challenged by State where financial repercussions were negligible or appeal was barred by limitation - It should not be challenged due to negligence or oversight of dealing officers or on account of wrong legal advice, or on account of non-comprehension of seriousness or magnitude of issue involved - However, similar matters subsequently crop up and magnitude of financial implications was realized, State was not prevented or barred from challenging subsequent decisions or resisting subsequent writ petitions, though judgment in case involved similar issue was allowed to reach finality in case of others - Thus, appellants were not entitled to any relief based on decision in 'Jai Kishan' - Division Bench of HC was justified in reversing decision of Single Judge which extended benefit of 'Jai Kishan' to appellants - Appeal dismissed.
