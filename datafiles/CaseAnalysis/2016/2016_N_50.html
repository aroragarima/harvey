Sandhya Rani Debbarma and others v National Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 657; 2017 (2) ACC 665; 2016 ACJ 2717; 2016 (118) ALR 854; 2016 (6) AWC 5517; JT 2016 (9) SC 212; 2016 (4) KLT 951; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 465; 2016(8) SCALE 820

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Insurance Company, National Insurance, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Enhancement - Deceased met with an accident while travelling in a vehicle due to collision with a bus coming from opposite direction - Appellants (legal heirs of deceased) filed suit before Tribunal claiming compensation amounting to Rs.33,45,000/- - Tribunal held that age of deceased being 31 years at the time of accident, a multiplier of 17 would be applicable and ordered that insurer of both the vehicles were equally liable to pay compensation of Rs.16,26,350/- each with 6% interest p.a. - Aggrieved respondent/insurance filed writ petition before HC - Single Judge allowed petition and modified award passed by Tribunal by reducing amount from Rs.32,52,700/- to Rs.20,40,000/- - On appeal, DB dismissed appeal holding that order passed by Single Judge was u/art. 227 and not u/art. 226 of Constitution and the same was not amenable to being challenged by way of appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC arrived at the amount of Rs.20,40,000/- in complete and utter forgetfulness of the principles of computing compensation laid down by SC. The logic applied by HC in modifying the award to Rs.20,40,000/-, which is only the annual loss of dependency, thereby, completely missing the next crucial step in determining the award of compensation due to dependents of deceased under other different heads such as loss of estate, funeral expenses, loss of consortium etc It is difficult for SC to understand what is the legal principle on which Single Judge has relied on to arrive at the conclusion that no further award under any other head is called for, when the same has been the well settled position of law by SC. Thus, decision of SC in case of Rajesh & Ors. v. Rajbir & Ors., 2013 Indlaw SCO 1392, and in case of Kalpanaraj & Ors. v. Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation, 2014 Indlaw SC 282, appellants were entitled to a total compensation of Rs.27,51,120/-. This amount is payable by respondents to appellants at the rate of 9% p.a. to the principle laid down SC in case of Municipal Corporation of Delhi, Delhi v. Uphaar Tragedy Victims Association & Ors, 2011 Indlaw SC 746. Impugned judgments of HC were set aside. Both the insurance companies shall be liable to pay half a share each of compensation amount of Rs.27,51,120/-, at an interest rate of 9% p.a. Appeal allowed.
