Basant Singh and another v Roman Catholic Mission

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1137; (2002) 7 SCC 531; AIR 2002 SC 3557; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 51; 2003 (1) AllCJ 3; 2003 (1) AWC 132; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 216; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 186; JT 2002 (7) SC 563; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 44; 2002 (132) PLR 677; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 572; 2002(7) SCALE 266; 2002 (3) SCJ 580; 2002 (5) SLT 545; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 70; 2002 (7) Supreme 62

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 5 r. 19A - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Service of notice - Registered post - Whether the service of notice sent by registered post with acknowledgement card in terms of O. 5 second proviso to r. 19A of the CPC r/w s. 27 of Act can be accepted as a sufficient notice.

Held, O. 5 proviso to r. 19A(2) of CPC provides that where the summons are properly addressed, prepaid and duly sent by registered post with acknowledgement due, notwithstanding the fact that the acknowledgement having been lost or mislaid, or for any other reason, has not been received by the Court within thirty days from the date of the issue of the summons, the Court shall presume that notice is duly served. Further, s. of the Act provides similar provision. The presumptions are rebuttable. It is always open to the defendants to rebut the presumption by leading convincing and cogent evidence. Appeal dismissed.
