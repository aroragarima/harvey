Ugar Sugar Works Limited v Delhi Administration and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19890; (2001) 3 SCC 635; AIR 2001 SC 1447; JT 2001 (4) SC 31; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 219; 2001(2) SCALE 534; 2001 (2) SCJ 579; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 630; 2001 (3) SLT 63; 2001 (2) Supreme 459

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 19(1)(g) and 32 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, ss. 5, 34 and 35 - Minimum sales figures - Liquor - Whether notification laying down MSF cirteria for year 2000-01 is arbitrary and discriminatory as against small-scale manufacturers and violative of arts. 14, 16 and 19(1)(g) and unconstitutional? - Relied on State of A.P. v. McDowell 1996 Indlaw SC 660 , State of Bombay v. F. N. Balsara, 1951 Indlaw SC 63, Cooverjee B. Bharucha v. Excise Commr. 1954 Indlaw SC 159 and T.N. Education Deptt. Ministerial and General Subordinate Services Assn. v. State of T.N. 1979 Indlaw SC 81 - Held, policy in impugned notification made in public interest and state has every right to regulate the supply of liquor within its territorial jurisdiction to ensure good quality liquor in public interest of health, morals and welfare - Writ petition dismissed without any costs.
