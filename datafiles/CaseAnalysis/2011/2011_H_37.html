Cauvery Coffee Traders, Mangalore v Hornor Resources (Intern.) Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 653; (2011) 10 SCC 420; 2012 (1) AWC 269; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 435; 2011 (4) CompLJ 241; 2012 (1) CTC 193; JT 2011 (11) SC 575; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 1183; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2011(10) SCALE 419

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - International - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(9), 11(5) - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Contract was entered into between parties wherein applicants agreed to sell and respondents agreed to purchase Calibrated Lumpy Ore Fines with chemical specification of more than 63% and could reject if found less - Applicants had shipped consignment from India to China - Respondents received consignment and informed applicants that Fe contents were at 62.74% and hence revised rates would be paid - Respondents offered US$ 1.5 million towards full and final settlement and applicants had accepted and on instructions of applicants banker received US$ 1.5 million as full and final settlement - Applicants issued legal notice demanding balance payment and settlement by arbitration in Singapore or in Australia as per agreement - Respondent neither made payment nor came forward for arbitration proceedings - Hence, instant appeal for appointment of Arbitrator - Whether applications to refer matter to arbitration outside India was acceptable u/s. 11 of the 1996 Act - Held, provisions of part I of 1996 Act would equally apply to international commercial arbitrations held outside India - In Bhatia International v. Bulk Trading S.A, Indlaw SC 265 relied on - Respondents could reject whole consignment as iron contents were less than 63% but accepted as goods had already reached China and made proposal for adjustment of price - In pursuance of negotiations applicants had instructed their banker to accept and banker by email dt.8-10-2009 accepted proposal and agreed to receive US$500,000.00 as full and final settlement for consignment in issue - After three months of receiving payment applicants served legal notice for making reference to Arbitrator - Since applicants had accepted full and final settlement money could not invoke Arbitration proceedings as laid down by SC in Nathani Steels Ltd. v. Associated Constructions, 1995 Indlaw SC 2118 - Once full and final settlement of claim was received there would be no arbitral dispute for reference to arbitration as laid down by SC in M/s. P.K. Ramaiah & Company v. Chairman & Managing Director, NTPC, 1993 Indlaw SC 1710 - Hence, applicants could not seek for arbitration even though there was arbitration clause in agreement having received amount towards full and final satisfaction - No one could be permitted to 'blow hot and cold', 'fast and loose' - Dispute came to end when offer of respondents was accepted by applicants and received amount towards full and final settlement - Applicants could not take complete somersault and agitate that offer of respondents was erroneously accepted - No dispute survived to refer to arbitration as full and final settlement recorded - Applications dismissed.
