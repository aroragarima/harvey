Dattonpant Gopalvarao Devakate v Vithalrao Maruthirao Janagaval

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 570; (1975) 2 SCC 246; AIR 1975 SC 1111; 1975 (1) ALR 386; 1975 RCJ 344; 1976 RCR(Rent) 128; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 67; 1975 UJ 381

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, s. 50 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 106, 111and 116 - CPC, 1908 - Constitution, art.136 - (A) Tenancy - Powers of revision of High Court - (B) Land and Property - (1) Lease not for manufacturing purpose - Lessee holding over - (2) Notice - Necessity of - (3) Tenancy determined by efflux of time Notice to quit - Held, interference by Supreme Court - Tenancy commencing on 10th day of a month but notice requiring tenant to vacate on 8th day - The appellant was a contractual tenant who would have become a statutory tenant u/s. 2(r) of 1961 Act if he would have continued in possession after the termination of the tenancy in his favour and otherwise not - No notice is necessary if a lease of immovable property determines u/cl.(a) of s. 111 by efflux of the time limited thereby - Appeal Allowed.
