(1) Raju Premji; (2) Arun Kanungo v (1) Customs Ner Shillong Unit; (2) D. Pakyntein

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 700; (2009) 16 SCC 496; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1046; 2009 CRLJ 3972; 2013 (296) E.L.T. 435; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 153; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 129; 2009(7) SCALE 568; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 839

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure,1898

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 25, 28, 29, 50 and 67 - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmed by High Court - Whether proper? - No - Confession - Information as to commission of offence received by the police authorities - Information was required to be put down in writing for apprising higher officers thereabout; but in instant case no search warrant or authorization was obtained; custody of accused taken but no statement sought, they were not even summoned; persons of accused were searched without complying with the provisions of s. 50 of Act; conduct of authorities found to be doubtful; transferring of case to the customs authorities by the police found to be illogical - Alleged confessions by accused - Admittedly from the very beginning appellants/accused were in the police custody; they were put to interrogation by police; so they were not free person; confession thus vitiated u/s. 26 of Evidence Act - Fact of retraction from confession further noted - Held, conviction not proper - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeals allowed.
