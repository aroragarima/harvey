Harendra Jha v Ministry of Law through its Secretary to Government of India

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2509; (2008) 9 SCC 465; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 819; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 28; 2008(11) SCALE 623

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 33 - Judicial review - Sustainable - Petitioner filed a curative petition challenging a decision passed by SC - Decision passed by SC was dismissed and it was said that decision could not be subjected to judicial review u/art. 32 of Constitution - Hence, instant petition - Whether order passed by Court was sustainable - Held, neither in petition nor in his oral submissions petitioner could articulate what directions he wants from Court - Therefore, petitioner had not made out any reasonable cause justifying registration of instant petition - Thus, there was no quarrel with this legal preposition but we fail to comprehend how this case could in any help petitioner - Petition dismissed.
