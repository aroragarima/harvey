Harijana Thirupala and Others v Public Prosecutor, High Court of A. P., Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1635; (2002) 6 SCC 470; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1370; AIR 2002 SC 2821; 2002 (3) CCR 104; 2002 (3) Crimes 87; 2002 (6) CRJ 91; 2002 CRLJ 3751; 2002 (3) JCC 1497; JT 2002 (5) SC 551; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 861; 2002(5) SCALE 434; 2002 (4) SLT 602; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 379; 2002 (5) Supreme 229

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused persons allegedly murdered deceased - Respondent/State approached Trial Court which acquitted appellants/accused persons - Aggrieved respondent/State filed appeal before HC which reversed order of Trial Court and convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Held, Court opined that HC committed serious error in distributing order of acquittal recorded by Trial Court that too without dislodging reasons given by Trial Court - Assuming one other view was possible, that itself was no ground to interfere with order of acquittal unless it was shown that appreciation of evidence by Trial Court was either perverse or untenable and that in ordering acquittal, Trial Court either ignored material evidence or view taken by it was patently untenable - Further, HC strangely convicts appellants/accused persons by taking aid of s. 34 IPC and case was registered in police station for offence u/s. 302 IPC - Appellants/accused persons were tried for charge u/s. 302 IPC only - Evidence of PW- 6, doctor, clearly shows that no injuries were found on legs of deceased attributable to appellants 1 and 2 and cause of death given by him was because of injuries attributed to appellant No. 3 - As per prosecution case itself appellants 1 and 2 had gone first to scene of occurrence and after heated exchange, they picked up sticks from fence on spot and assaulted deceased - Appellant no. 3 came later and assaulted deceased with crow-bar - There was absolutely nothing on record to show that appellants 1 to 3 had any pre-meditation or any intention to cause death of deceased - It was also not shown that how appellant no. 3 was concerned with appellants 1 and 2 - Thus, finding of HC was patently unsustainable - Hence, set aside HC 's order and restored Trial Court's order and appellants were set free at liberty forthwith if they were not required in any other case - Appeal allowed.
