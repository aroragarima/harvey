Union of India and others v P. Gunasekaran

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 783; (2015) 2 SCC 610; AIR 2015 SC 545; 2014 (310) E.L.T. 417; 2015 (144) FLR 219; JT 2014 (13) SC 499; 2014(13) SCALE 24; 2015 (4) SLR 244

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Substitution of punishment - Legality - Respondent delinquent was involved in a criminal case - Departmental proceedings initiated against respondent with three charges - On enquiry, charges against respondent were proved - Respondent was dismissed from service - However, criminal court acquitted respondent from criminal charges - Respondent filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal set aside 2 charges but upheld first charge and substituted termination to compulsory retirement - Respondent challenged Tribunal order before HC - HC set aside Tribunal order and directed appellant department to reinstate respondent - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, impugned conduct of the respondent working as Deputy Office Superintendent in a sensitive department of Central Excise, according to the disciplinary authority, reflected lack of integrity warranting discontinuance in service. That view has been endorsed by the Tribunal also. Thereafter, it is not open to the HC to go into the proportionality of punishment or substitute the same with a lesser or different punishment. The finding on first charge has attained finality. Tribunal, after elaborately discussing the factual as well as the legal position, has come to the conclusion that the punishment of compulsory retirement is not outrageous or shocking to its conscience, it was not open to the HC to interfere with the disciplinary proceedings from stage one and direct reinstatement of the respondent with back-wages. Hence, HC order was set aside and Tribunal order was restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It was not open to the HC, in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226/227 of the Constitution of India, to go into the proportionality of punishment so long as the punishment does not shock the conscience of the court.
