Dy. Inspector General of Police v K. S. Swaminathan

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2131; (1996) 11 SCC 498; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 728; 1997 (1) ESC 212; 1997 (75) FLR 2; JT 1996 (10) SC 40; 1997 (2) LLJ 101; 1997 (1) SLR 176; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 197; 1996 (8) Supreme 73

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Misconduct stated in Charge Memo - Quashing of charge memo - Whether Tribunal was justified in setting aside charge memo on ground that charges were vague? - Held, if charge memo is totally vague and does not disclose any misconduct for which charges have been framed, tribunal or court would not be justified at that stage to go into whether charges are true and could be gone into, for it would be a matter on production of evidence for consideration at enquiry by enquiry officer - Charge memo and statement of facts disclose misconduct alleged against delinquent officer - Tribunal was totally wrong in quashing charge memo - Appeal allowed.
