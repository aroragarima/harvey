State of Gujarat v Reliance Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 1057; 2017 (12) SCALE 75

Case Digest

Subject: VAT

Keywords: cash memorandum, Commercial Tax, dealer, registered dealer, registration, revenue, Sales Tax, tax invoice

Summary: VAT - Indirect Tax - Gujarat Value Added Tax Act, 2003, s. 11(3) - Assessment - Tax credit - Permissibility - Respondent/assessee was manufacturing polymers and chemicals and transfers those goods outside State - AO held that in respect of such goods tax credit was required to be reduced at the rate of 4% which order was upheld by Commissioner - On further appeal, Tribunal held that deduction could be at 4% only and there cannot be double reduction in tax credit admissible to assesse - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition challenging the same - HC dismissed writ petition and upheld order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether tax credit was to be reduced at the rate of 4% under sub-clause (ii) and again at the same rate under sub-clause (iii) as well or deduction permissible was only once.

Held, material in question, namely, furnace oil, natural gas and light diesel oil were admittedly subject to VAT under the Act. When the finished product after manufacture was sold, VAT would be again payable thereon. Such VAT is payable on the price at which such goods were sold costing whereof was done keeping in view the expenses involved in the manufacture of such goods plus the profits which manufacturer intends to earn. In that manner, when the final product is sold and VAT paid, component of raw material would be included again. However, reduction of 4% would be applied whenever a case gets covered by sub-cl. (ii) and again when sub-cl. (iii) was attracted. However, it would be subject to one limitation. In those cases where VAT paid on such raw material was 4%, as in the case of furnace oil, reduction cannot be more than that as s.11 of the Act deals with giving credit in respect of tax that was paid. Therefore, if some reduction is to be made from the said credit, it cannot be more than the credit given. Thus, so far as furnace oil was concerned, tax credit should be reduced by 4%. On the other hand, tax credit given in case of natural gas and light diesel oil (other fuels), it should be reduced by 4% under sub-cl. (ii) and 4% under sub-cl. (iii) of cl. (b) of s. 11(3) of the Act. Appeals allowed.
