Thaman Kumar v State of Union Territory of Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 470; (2003) 6 SCC 380; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1362; AIR 2003 SC 3975; 2003 (2) CCR 132; 2003 (3) Crimes 86; 2003 (5) CRJ 247; 2003 CRLJ 3070; 2003 (2) JCC 965; JT 2003 (4) SC 478; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 416; 2003(4) SCALE 531; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1190; 2003 (3) SLT 501; 2003 (4) Supreme 206

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Supreme Court (Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, (the Act), s. 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 34 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss.161, 313 - Conviction - Appeal u/s. 2 of the Act - (A) conflict between oral testimony and medical evidence - Held, conflict between oral testimony and medical evidence can be of varied dimensions and shapes - Manner and method of assault, position of victim, resistance offered by him, opportunity available to witnesses to see occurrence like their distance, presence of light and many other similar factors will have to be taken into consideration in judging reliability of ocular testimony - In case at hand there is no inconsistency between testimony of eye-witnesses and medical evidence and case of prosecution does not suffer from any infirmity on that account - (II) Further held, absence of any evidence on point of motive cannot have any such impact so as to discard other reliable evidence available on record, which unerringly establishes guilt of accused - Prosecution has succeeded in establishing its case against accused -appellants beyond any shadow of doubt and view taken by learned Sessions Judge was wholly perverse which was rightly set aside by HC - Appeal dismissed.
