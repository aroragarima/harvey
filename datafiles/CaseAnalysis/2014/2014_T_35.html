Life Insurance Corporation of India and others v Triveni Sharan Mishra

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 574; (2014) 10 SCC 346; 2014 (6) AWC 5636; 2014 (4) CGLJ 44; 2014 (143) FLR 464; 2014 (3) JabLJ 336; JT 2014 (10) SC 141; 2014 (4) LLJ 257; 2014(10) SCALE 57; 2014 (5) SLR 761

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reinstatement, Dismissal from services

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 23, 341, 294, 506B r/w s. 34 - Dismissal from services - Reinstatement - Challenged - Respondent was removed from services of petitioner - Appeal before Appellate authority by respondent against imposition of penalty of removal - Appeal was dismissed - Respondent filed petition before HC - Petition was allowed by HC - Whether order of HC was sustainable -

Held, on behalf of appellants it is contended that suppression of material information and making false statement to secure the employment, is a serious offence to attract the dismissal of service. On behalf of the appellants cannot be applied to instant case for the reason that in the said case the employee had concealed the facts relating to his character and antecedents. In said case, the employee who was selected for the post of a Teacher suppressed the information that criminal case relating to offences punishable u/ss. 323, 341, 294, 506B r/w s. 34 IPC was registered against him. It appears that for mentioning less qualification to secure the job, similarly situated another employee was let off by the Life Insurance Corporation of India by awarding punishment of stoppage of increments for two years with cumulative effect. SC opined that HC rightly taken note of said fact while allowing petition and directing the employer to consider the imposition of similar penalty after reinstatement of petitioner. Thus, there was no sufficient reason to interfere with impugned order passed by HC. Appeal dismissed.
