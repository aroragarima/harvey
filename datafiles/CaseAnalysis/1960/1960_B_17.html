Â State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Basti Sugar Mills Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1961 SC 420; 1961 (2) FLR 101; 1961 (1) LLJ 220; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 330

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Conciliator, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG), Trade Union Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 19(1)(g), 19(6), 226 - United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG), ss. 3(b), 3(d) - State Govt order - Constitutionality - Respondents/companies filed Writ Petition before HC challenging orders made by appellant/Govt. u/s. 3(b) of the Act requiring respondents to pay bonus at certain rates for years 1947-48 and 1948-49 to their workers and also payment of retaining allowances to skilled seasonal workmen and clerical staff - HC concluded that s. 3(b) of the Act was prospective in operation and it was not competent there under to require employer to pay bonus to workmen in respect of period of employment which was already past - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order of the HC was valid - Held, it was clear that s. 3 empowers State Govt to require employers and workmen or both to observe any term or condition of employment for specified period - Since law enables State Govt to impose term it was apparent that legislature which enacted that law did not import into that word consensual sense - Therefore, there was nothing in s. 3(b) of the Act which prohibited State Govt. from making direction to sugar factories with regard to payment of bonus for years 1947-48 and 1948-49 in their order of 7-7-1950 and that by making such direction State Govt. was not giving retrospective effect to provisions of that clause - Further, any direction as to payment of bonus should inevitably be based on available surplus, and such surplus could be determined only at end of given year - Therefore, what impugned order purported to do was to require employers to pay specified amounts in future, though said amounts were fixed by reference to profits made in 2 preceding years - Thus, if direction as to payment of bonus could be issued u/s. 3(b) of the Act it could not, therefore, be said to be retrospective - Further, provisions of 3(b) of the Act were not in any sense alternative to those of s. 3(d) of the Act and that former could be availed of by State Govt only in emergency and as temporary measure - Similarly fact that action was taken by Govt in emergency in public interest would be complete answer to argument that that action was violative of provisions of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Restriction placed upon employer by such order was only temporary one and having been placed in public interest would fall u/art. 19(6) of Constitution - Therefore, HC was in error in issuing writ against State Govt quashing their order in so far as it related to payment of bonus and order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
