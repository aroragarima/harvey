Bhadresh Bipinbhai Sheth v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 591; (2016) 1 SCC 152; AIR 2015 SC 3090; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4116; 2016 (1) G.L.R. 798; 2015 (1) JCC 2603; JT 2015 (8) SC 125; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3254; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 67; 2015 (4) RLW 3551; 2015(9) SCALE 403; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Anticipatory Bail, Blackmail

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Cancellation of anticipatory bail - Respondent no. 2 leveled allegations against appellant of maltreatment, blackmail etc. before Police Officer on 31-5-2001 and basing on the same, FIR was lodged and charge sheet was filed against appellant - However, after 10 years, respondent no. 2 made application for addition of charge u/s. 376 of IPC and matter was carried upto SC and later, police filed revised charge sheet adding charge u/s. 376 of IPC - While so, appellant filed application for grant of anticipatory bail and the same was granted by Trial Court - Assailing said order of granting anticipatory bail to appellant, respondent no. 2 filed criminal revision petition before HC and vide impugned order, HC cancelled anticipatory bail granted to appellant - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant is entitled for anticipatory bail.

Held, no purpose would be served in compelling the appellant to go behind bars, as an under trial, by refusing the anticipatory bail in respect of alleged incident which was 17 years old and for which the charge is framed only in the year 2014. The investigation is complete and there is no allegation that appellant would flee the course of justice. Further, appellant participated in the proceedings which were started in 2001 and there was no allegation during said period that he had tried to influence the witnesses. Further, even when there is a serious charge leveled against appellant, that by itself could not be the reason to deny anticipatory bail when the matter is examined keeping in view other factors. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside and order of Trial Court granting anticipatory bail to appellant is restored. Appeals partly allowed.
