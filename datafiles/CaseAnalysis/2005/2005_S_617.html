State of Orissa and another v Saroj Kumar Sahoo

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 938; (2005) 13 SCC 540; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 272; 2006 (1) JCC 67; JT 2005 (10) SC 390; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 324; 2005(10) SCALE 24; 2006 (2) SCJ 804; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 482 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(a), 13(2) - CrPC,1898, ss. 482, 561A - IPC, 1872, ss. 120(B), 420, 468, 471 - Challenge is to legality of orders passed by Single Judge of HC quashing proceedings initiated against respondents on basis of FIRs lodged by functionaries of State of Orissa - Held, investigation was not complete and at that stage it was impermissible for HC to look into materials, acceptability of which is essentially a matter for trial - Order of quashing of investigation and proceedings set aside - Appeals allowed.
