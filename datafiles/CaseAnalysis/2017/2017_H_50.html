Dr. K. S. Palanisami (Dead) and others v Hindu Community In General and Citizens of Gobichettipalayam and others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 157; AIR 2017 SC 1473; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 128; 2017 (122) ALR 470; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 108; 2017 (4) CTC 532; JT 2017 (3) SC 151; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 601; 2017(3) SCALE 479

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Arrangement, Receiver, Judicial Officer, Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, Alienate, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land & Property

Summary: (A) Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8, ss. 5, 92 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, s. 108 - Suit for declaration - Barred - Validity - Plaintiff filed suit seeking that Will dt. 27-11-1980 was not executed by testator in a sound state of mind and that trust properties covered under the Will dt. 27-9-1968 to be administered by defendant nos. 1 to 3 or other new Trustees to be appointed by Court - Trial Court dismissed suit holding Trust was not formed under the Will dt. 27-9-1968, plaintiffs were not entitled for framing a scheme u/s. 92 CPC - On appeals, HC dismissed appeals holding that Will dt. 27-9-1968 as mutual and joint Will and after the death of testator his wife had no right to alienate any property and all alienations of the properties made by her after the death were null and void - Hence, instant appeals - Whether suit filed by plaintiff was barred u/s. 108 of the Act.

Held plain reading of s. 108 of the Act indicates bar with regard to suit or other legal proceeding was in respect of the administration or management of a religious institution. Thus, unless the provisions of the Act were extended to charitable endowments the bar u/s. 108 of the act should not be attracted. There was no case set up by appellant that suit filed by plaintiff relates to a religious institution, as contemplated by the Act. Therefore, SC was of view that suit filed by plaintiff was not barred as u/s. 108 of the Act. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8, ss. 5, 92 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, s. 108 - Will executed - Joint and mutual - Irrevocable - Whether Will dt. 27-9-1968 was a joint and mutual Will irrevocable after death of one of the testators.

Held, HC came to the conclusion that it was a Joint and Mutual Will, since both testator and testatrix agreed to devote their properties for carrying out charities, HC concluded that intention of both testator and testatrix to give property to charities was manifest from the reading of Will in its entirety.However, intention of testator/testatrix in testamentary disposition has to be gathered from Will itself and the words used therein. HC after opined that expression 'absolutely' should be read to mean that the surviving testator would have only the life interest. Thus, giving absolute right to the survivor during his lifetime to deal with the properties in no manner cannot be said to be right given in disregard of object of trust. The charitable purpose of the Will was not lost even if survivor was given absolute right. The obligation of survivor to act in furtherance of object as agreed by both testators survives and binds survivor. Though, Will was irrevocable after the death of survivor but Will expressly granted absolute right to survivor. SC endorse the view of HC that Will dt. 27-9-1968 was a joint and mutual Will, but with a rider that said joint and mutual Will was with an express condition that survivor should have absolute right to deal with the property keeping the object of trust alive. Giving of right of disposition to survivor was also one of the joint decision and agreement between testator and testatrix which does not diminish the nature and character of Will as joint and mutual Will. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8, ss. 5, 92 - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, s. 108 - Will deed - Proof of - Whether Will set up by defendant nos. 4 and 5 i.e. 27-11-1980 has rightly been held to be not proved by Trial Court as well as by HC.

Held, in instant case unless the alienation by survivor was held to be completely in breach of object of trust and fraud on trust, Court was to be slow in disregarding such alienations. The alienation made by survivor in favour of appellants could not have been declared null and void as was done by HC. Decision of HC in so far as in declaring the alienation made by survivor after the death of testator during her lifetime was null and void deserves to be set aside. Thus, alienation made by survivor by registered sale deeds as noticed by HC in favour of appellants needs to be deleted from the list of the properties as described in plaint and they should not be included in trust property by virtue of Will deed dt. 27-09-1968. However, said deletion was only with regard to alienations made by survivor and not to the alienations made by defendant nos. 4 & 5. Therefore, above eventuality as contemplated by Will never came into existence during the life time of testator and his wife but as per provisions of Will dt. 27-9-1968 on the death of survivor on 27-12-1980, the three members committee was obliged to carry out charities and Trust came into operation. Declaration made by HC in so far as alienations made by defendant nos. 4 & 5 as null and void was maintained. Appeals disposed of.
