Anil Ratan Sarkar and others v Hirak Ghosh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 157; (2002) 4 SCC 21; AIR 2002 SC 1405; 2002 (1) AllCriR 868; 2002 (48) ALR 121; 2002 (2) AWC 1123; 2002 CRLJ 1814; JT 2002 (2) SC 602; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 157; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 517; 2002(2) SCALE 525; 2002 (2) SCJ 372; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 346; 2002 (2) SLT 441; 2002 (2) Supreme 383

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The respondents have willfully and deliberately violated the orders of the Supreme Court in the guise of a totally non-acceptable and sham defence of understanding and thus rendered themselves punishable under the provisions of Article 142 of the Constitution and also under the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971.
