Satpal & Co. Etc. v Lt. Governor Of Delhi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 245; (1979) 4 SCC 232; AIR 1979 SC 1550; 1980 CRLJ 831; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 651; 1979 TAXLR 2486; 1979 UJ 631

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Wealth Tax Act - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 246(1), (2), (3), (4), 248, 301, 19(1) and 47 - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914 - Delhi Fiscal Duty Order, 1979 - Punjab Excise (Delhi Amendment) Ordinance, 1979, ss. 3(5 A) and 31 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, shall extended to Delhi - On 20-01-1979, President of India, promulgated Ordinance purporting to amend Punjab Excise Act with retrospective effect by Punjab Excise (Delhi Amendment) Ordinance, 1979 - Pursuant to Ordinance amending principal Act, Delhi Fiscal Duty Order, 1979, was issued levying special duty on import of country liquor into Delhi - (A) Whether Parliament is competent to enact legislation which would directly impinge upon competence of President to issue impugned Ordinance? - Held, scheme underlying distribution of legislative powers is that except matters specifically enumerated in List II Parliament's plenary power to legislate extends to all conceivable matters which can be topic of legislation, and even this limitation on its power vanishes when Parliament legislates for part of territory of India not included in a State - As country liquor is not manufactured in Delhi, Parliament could not under Entry 51 of State List levy either excise or countervailing duty on it - Where there is a specific entry legislation must squarely fall within entry and correspondingly is must be assumed that it would not be covered by residuary entry - If by a legislation tax is sought to be imposed in exercise of certain legislative power which under judicial review is found to be wanting, it does not prohibit legislature from exercising same power if it can be traced to provisions of Constitution - It would be too trite a saying that if countervailing duty cannot be imposed there would be no power to impose duty in any form on import of country liquor into a territory where that country liquor is not manufactured - (B) Whether imposed unreasonable restrictions on freedom of inter-State trade, commerce and intercourse guaranteed by art. 301 or that freedom to carry on trade or business in liquor is a fundamental right under art. 19(1) being not in public interest, enactment is constitutionally invalid? - Held, there is no fundamental right to do trade or business in intoxicants - A regulatory measure imposing a tax cannot be questioned on ground of unreasonableness or want to public interest because one must not lose sight of well accepted legal proposition that all taxes are imposed in public interest - Even apart from this reasonable restrictions can be imposed on freedom of trade - Therefore, one can look upon this measure both as a fiscal measure and one safeguarding public health and even public morals because it is well recognised that liquor trade is instinct with injury to individual and community and has serious side-effects recognised everywhere in every age - Loophole sought to be plugged by a valid legislation - Petitions dismissed.
