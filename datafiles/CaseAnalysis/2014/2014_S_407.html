Baljinder Kaur v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 776; (2015) 2 SCC 629; 2015 CRLJ 758; JT 2014 (13) SC 1; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4610; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 78; 2014(13) SCALE 96

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Challenged - Instant appeal filed against judgment passed by HC, in and by which, the HC confirmed the conviction of the appellants u/s. 304B of IPC and sentence imposed on the appellant (sister-in-law) and second accused (husband) while acquitting father-in-law and mother-in-law - Whether the courts below were right in convicting the appellant u/s. 304B of IPC.

Held, in the instant case, appellant demanded gold karra two months after the solemnization of marriage of the deceased and the demand was not 'soon before her death'. There is no evidence showing any persistent dowry demand or the conduct of the appellant subjecting deceased to cruelty or harassment for or in connection with dowry. The alleged demand of gold karra about two months after the marriage cannot be said to constitute a proximate live link with the death of deceased and the conviction of the appellant u/s. 304B of IPC cannot be sustained. Evidence on record makes out an offence u/s. 498A of IPC. Conviction of the appellant u/s. 304B of IPC is set aside. The appellant is convicted u/s. 498A of IPC and sentenced to undergo the period already undergone by her. Appeal partly allowed.
