Municipal Corporation For Greater Bombay and v Advance Builders (India) Private Limited and

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 508; (1971) 3 SCC 381; AIR 1972 SC 793; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 408; 1971 UJ 838

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1955, s. 55 - Constitution, art. 226 - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966 - Municipalities - (A) Duty to frame and implement scheme - Removing the huts, sheds, stables and other temporary structures which contravene the scheme - (B) Constitution - Mandamus - Writ is discrtionary - Directing lawful act - Interfernce, if - Held, duty to frame and implement scheme is of the Corporation and the responsibility for removing is that of the Corporation and not of the owners of plots - Discretion directing that which is lawful cannot be interfered with - Appeal dismissed.
