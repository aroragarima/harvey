State Of Haryana v Hasmat

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 499; (2004) 6 SCC 175; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1757; AIR 2004 SC 3936; 2004 CRLJ 3840; JT 2004 (6) SC 6; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 829; 2004(6) SCALE 274; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 132

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 389 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Questions grant of bail to accused and suspension of execution of sentence pending appeal - HC granted bail on ground that there was no allegation of any misuse of liberty during period of parole - Held, Appellate Court is duty bound to objectively assess matter and to record reasons for conclusion that case warrants suspension of execution of sentence and grant of bail - Mere fact that during period of parole accused not misused liberties does not per se warrant suspension of execution of sentence and grant of bail - Suspension of sentence and grant of bail unsustainable and set aside.
