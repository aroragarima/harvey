Ram Pal v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 587; (2003) 7 SCC 141; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1595; AIR 2003 SC 4168; 2003 (3) CCR 138; 2003 (3) Crimes 234; 2003 (6) CRJ 606; 2003 CRLJ 3760; 2003 (2) JCC 1280; JT 2003 (6) SC 502; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 685; 2003(6) SCALE 109; 2003 (4) SLT 882; 2003 (5) Supreme 297

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 149, 302, 307, 436, 440 - Death sentence - Rarest of rare cases - Death of 21 people - Held, mitigating circumstances like (i) murder of two close relative of the appellant by the victims family; (ii) role played by the accused similar to those who have been awarded lesser sentences; (iii) specific overt act attributed to the accused being an after thought, and (iv) accused having already spent 17 years in custody, outweigh the aggravating circumstances - Further held, death sentence substituted by imprisonment for life.
