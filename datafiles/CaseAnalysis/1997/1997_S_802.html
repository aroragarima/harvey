Union of India and Others v Sushil Kumar Modi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1640; (1997) 4 SCC 770; AIR 1997 SC 1672; AIR 1997 SCW 725; 1997 (1) CLT 392; 1997 CRLJ 1168; JT 1997 (1) SC 679; 1997(1) SCALE 432; 1997 (2) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Portions of orders - Unwarranted - Mandamus - Performance of duty -Special leave against some portions of orders dt. 13-11-1996 and 19-12-1996 passed by HC - Attorney General, on behalf of appellants had indicated certain portions of these two orders and contended that they do not match with earlier order dt. 5-12-1996 - Attorney General submitted that tracts of two orders made by HC in particular were unwarranted apart from certain other observations which could have been avoided - CBI does not appear to be appropriate - Nature of proceedings described as that of continuing mandamus to require performance of its duty by CBI and other concerned Govt. agencies - Whether nature of proceeding and manner in which exercise was to be performed to ensure performance of its duty by CBI - Held, purpose of proceedings pending had no bearing on merits of accusation, and was not to influence trial in any manner - HC was only required to ensure that Director, CBI does not close any investigation based upon his individual - Nature of proceeding and manner in which exercise was to be performed to ensure performance of its duty by CBI, guidance for CBI could have been given by HC without entering into merits of any of accusations - Cases pending in different HC, CBI personally may not be left with sufficient time from his official duties to appear personally at hearings of these matters in HC - It was duty of Director, CBI to ensure proper representation on his behalf in HC - Delicate task of ensuring implementation of rule of law by requiring proper performance of its duty by CBI and other Govt. agencies, while taking care to avoid the likelihood of any prejudice to accused at ensuing trial because of any observation made on merits of accusation in the proceeding - No grievance to CBI or any other authority HC order and proceedings before it were understood and continued - Orders made by HC were to be construed and understood in manner - Appeal disposed of.
