Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v S. Nelliappan (Deceased By His Legal Representatives)

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 326; [1967] 66 ITR 722

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,ss. 33(4),66 - Assessment - Tax liability - Cash credit - Excessive quantum - Respondents/Assessees were carrying business of plying motor buses and lorries - Assessment to income-tax for the years 1946-47 and 1947-48 and for excess profits tax for the accounting periods 17-8-1944 to 16-8-1945 and 17-8-1945 to 31-3-1946 - Assessing officer rejected books of account maintained by assessees - Orders of assessing officer were confirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal dismissed the appeals against those orders observing that final computation of profits made by Income Tax Officer gave an average rate very much less and final assessments at rates less than the rates - Whether Tribunal was barred from adjusting the tax liability of the assessee - Held, Tribunal was competent to allow the assessees to raise the contention relating to the cash credits which was not made the subject-matter of a ground in the memorandum of appeal - Tribunal was not precluded from adjusting the tax liability of the assessee in the light of its findings merely because the findings were inconsistent with the case pleaded by the assessees - There was no direct evidence of any connection between the cash credit entries and income withheld from the books of account by the assessees - But if Tribunal inferred that there was a connection between the profits withheld from the books and cash credit entries, it cannot be said that conclusion was based upon speculation - Question sought to be raised was purely one of fact and could not be referred u/s.66of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
