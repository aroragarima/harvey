N. E. Horo v Jahan Ara Jaipal Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 99; (1972) 1 SCC 771; AIR 1972 SC 1840; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Divorce Act, 1869 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 82 and 123 - Scheduled Tribes Order, 1952 - Acquiring the status of tribe - Whether petitioner could legally acquire status of a Munda by virtue of her marriage in Tribe and accepted as a member of Munda Tribe by said Tribe? - Held, without invoking doctrine of domicile respondent's marriage with late Shri Jaipal Singh who was a Munda having been approved and sanctioned by Parha Panchayat of Munda tribe it can be said that she became a member of Munda tribal community - Appeal dismissed.
