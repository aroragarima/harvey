Union of India and Another v V.N. Bhat

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 970; (2003) 8 SCC 714; AIR 2004 SC 3200; 2003 (5) ESC 1; 2003 (99) FLR 1053; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 175; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 610; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 79; 2003(9) SCALE 517; 2004 (1) SLR 21; 2003 (7) SLT 42; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 857; 2003 (8) Supreme 48

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority

Summary: [A.] Service - Transfer on request - Took seniority at bottom of gradation list as per departmental rules - 'One Time Bound Promotion Scheme' and 'B C R Scheme' - Respondent given benefit of Scheme, which was latter withdrawn - Whether period of service rendered by respondent in Ministry of Defence should be wiped off for all purposes - Held, well settled principle of law that even in case where transfer has been allowed on request, concerned employee merely loses his seniority, but same by itself would not lead to a conclusion that he should be deprived of other benefits including his experience and eligibility for promotion - Further held, even in a case where promotion is to be made on selection basis, concerned employee, even if he be placed at bottom of seniority list in terms of order of transfer based in his favour, he cannot be deprived of being considered for promotion to next higher post if he is eligible therefor.
