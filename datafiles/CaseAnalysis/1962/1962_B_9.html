Board of High School and Inter-Mediate Education U. P v Bagleshwar Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 257; AIR 1966 SC 875; 1963 ALJ 676; 1963 (2) ALT(SC) 119; 1963 (7) FLR 415; 1963 (2) ILR(All) 499; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 767

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Unfair practice - High School Examination - Whether order passed by tribunal liable to be quashed by High Court in a writ petition u/art. 226? - Enquiry Committee did not write an elaborate report, does not mean that it did not consider all relevant facts before it came to conclusion that respondent had used unfair means - In dealing with validity of impugned orders passed by Universities u/art. 226, HC is not sitting in appeal over decision in question - Held, enquiry has been fair and respondent had an opportunity of making his defence - Appeal disposed of.
