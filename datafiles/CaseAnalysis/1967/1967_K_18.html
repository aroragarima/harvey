Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City Ii v Keshavji Morarji and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 289; [1967] 66 ITR 142

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Assets - Settlement - Consideration - Respondent had son J, and three daughters I, M and D - Respondent transferred sum of Rs. 5 lakhs to his son, J - J by deed, settled sum of Rs. 1,54,000 upon his three sisters - Income-tax Officer held that respondent had indirectly transferred assets - Appellate Assistant Commissioner upheld view of Income-tax Officer - Income-tax Appellate Tribunal confirmed order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - HC held that two deeds of settlement were executed on same day did not justify that K and J made indirect transfers of assets - Hence instant appeal - Whether there was indirect transfer of assets - Held, it cannot be denied that minor children of each of two assessees had received benefit - It clearly constitutes indirect transfer by each of two assessees to his respective minor child - Hence, department can lift veil and declare true nature of transaction as being one within section - Appeal allowed.
