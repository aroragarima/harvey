Â Gannon Dunkerley and Company and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.H. Kania, Yogeshwar Dayal, A.S. Anand, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 743; (1993) 1 SCC 364; AIR 1993 SCW 2621; 1993 (49) DLT 205; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 324; 1992(3) SCALE 173; 1993 (88) STC 204; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 103; 1993 (1) UJ 270

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax Constitution of India - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act 1954 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1955 - Immunity - Works contract - Validity of State legislation - Constitutional validity of Fourty-Sixth Amendment and Amendment made in State legislations on that basis were challenged - W.P. filed u/art. 32 of the Constitution by appellant no. 2, a company incorporated under the 1956 Act - Validity of imposition of tax on transfer of property in goods involved in execution of works contract contained in the Sales Tax Laws of State of Tamil Nadu, Andhra Pradesh and Rajasthan were challenged - In spite of the SC decision Builders' Association of India and Others v Union of India and Others, 1989 Indlaw SC 557, petitioner was being subjected to demands for tax in relation to works contracts in the State of Rajasthan - Whether r. 29(2)(i) of the 1955 Rules was unconstitutional and void - Held, in exercise of its legislative power to impose tax on sale or purchase of goods under Entry 54 of the State List r/w art. 366(29-A)(b) of the Constitution, the State Legislature, while imposing a tax on the transfer of property in goods (whether as goods or in some other form) involved in the execution of a works contract was not competent to impose a tax on such a transfer which constituted a sale in the course of inter-State trade or commerce or a sale outside the State or a sale in the course of import or export - R. 29(2)(i) of the 1955 Rules suffered from infirmity - S. 5(3) of the 1954 Act and r. 29(2)(i) of the 1955 Rules was unconstitutional and void - Appeals disposed of.
