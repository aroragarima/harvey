Allahabad Bank and Another v Deepak Kumar Bhola

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1067; (1997) 4 SCC 1; (1997) SCC (L&S) 897; 1997 (3) AWC 1429; 1997 (1) CCR 268; 1997 (1) CLR 834; 1997 (76) FLR 98; JT 1997 (3) SC 539; 1997 (1) LLJ 854; 1997 (2) LLN 644; 1997(2) SCALE 623; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1055; 1997 (2) SLR 236; 1997 (3) Supreme 112; 1997 (1) UJ 761

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 429, 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Respondent suspended for cheating and criminal misconduct - Writ petition filed by respondent for quashing of proceedings allowed by High Court - Held, the respondent was dealing with money of general public and any misconduct of his part is not liable to be condoned - Appeal allowed.
