General Manager, Northern Railway and Another v Sarvesh Chopra

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1716; (2002) 4 SCC 45; AIR 2002 SC 1272; 2002 (3) ALD(SC) 31; 2002 (46) ARBLR 506; 2002 (96) DLT 447; JT 2002 (2) SC 445; 2002 (1) RAJ 521; 2002(2) SCALE 394; 2002 (2) SCJ 241; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 156; 2002 (2) SLT 306; 2002 (2) Supreme 170; 2002 (1) UC 682

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Whether 'excepted matters' within meaning of cl. 63 of general conditions of contract were not liable to be referred to arbitration? - Held, it is incorrect to say that interpretation of arbitration clause itself can be or should be left to be determined by arbitrator and such determination cannot be done by court at any stage - 'Excepted matters' taken by division bench of High Court cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
