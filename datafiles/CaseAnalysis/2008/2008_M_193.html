Mahalakshmi Sugar Mills Company Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2595; (2009) 16 SCC 569; AIR 2009 SC 792; JT 2008 (6) SC 177; 2008(6) SCALE 275; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature; Socio-Economic; Trade; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Producer Of Sugar, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Sugarcane Control Order, 1966, Uttar Pradesh enacted Sugar Control Act, 1938

Summary: (A) Agriculture & Agri. Products - Trade - Socio-Economic - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3(3C) - Sugarcane Control Order, 1966, cl. 5A - Price of levy sugar - Determination of - Whether additional price paid under cl. 5A of the Order and State Advisory Price (SAP) would be the relevant factors for determining the price of levy sugar - Held, SAP and additional price paid under cl. 5A are the factors cannot be kept out of consideration for the purpose of determination of the price of levy sugar - - Central Government cannot ignore any of the factors specified in s. 3(3C) of the Act for the purpose of fixation of price for levy sugar - Therefore, direction issued to the Central Government to re-fix the price of levy sugar - Referred the decision of SC in Shri Malaprabha Cooperative Sugar Factor v. Union of India, 1993 INDLAW SC 651, Appeals disposed of.

(B) Parliament & Legislature - Trade - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3(2)(f) - Fixation of quota - Power of Central Government - Held, direction by the Central Government to the owners of the sugar mills that a part of their products would be sold at a price determined by it was within its realm of power - Such a quota could be fixed by the Central Government in exercise of its power u/s. 3(2)(f) of the Act - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Practice & Procedure - Trade - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Price determination - Interference by SC - Scope of - Held, determination of price in terms of the provisions of the Act is a legislative function and SC ordinarily would not interfere therewith - However, when such a function is carried out in contravention of the statutory requirements, the courts are not powerless and hence it becomes the duty of the Court to interpret its earlier judgments relating to that matter and refer the one which is applicable - Appeals disposed of.
