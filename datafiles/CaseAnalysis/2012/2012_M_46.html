M/S Virgo Industries (Eng.) P.Ltd v M/S.Venturetech Solutions P.Ltd

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 286; (2013) 1 SCC 625; 2013 (96) ALR 248; 2012 (5) ALT 57; 2012 (6) AWC 5479; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 143; 2012 (5) CTC 359; JT 2012 (8) SC 600; 2013 (2) MahLJ 535; 2013 (2) MPLJ 22; 2013 (1) OLR 290; 2013 (169) PLR 163; 2013 (118) RD 662; 2012(8) SCALE 565; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 933

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2(3) - Two suits for same issue - Plaintiff filed suit seeking a decree of permanent injunction restraining the defendant from alienating, encumbering or dealing with the plaint schedule properties to any other third party other than the plaintiff - Another suit was filed by the plaintiff seeking a decree against the defendant for execution and registration of the sale deeds in respect of the same property and for delivery of possession thereof to the plaintiff - Defendant contended that the cause of action for both sets of suits were the same, namely, the refusal or reluctance of the defendant to execute the sale deeds - Therefore, suits filed by the plaintiff for specific performance were barred under the provisions of O. 2 r. 2(3) of the CPC - HC held that the provisions of O. 2 r. 2(3) of CPC would render a subsequent suit not maintainable, only, if the earlier suit had been decreed and the said provisions of the CPC will not apply if the first suit remains pending - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC rightly held that the provisions of O. 2 r. 2(3) of CPC would render a subsequent suit not maintainable, only, if the earlier suit had been decreed and the said provisions of the CPC will not apply if the first suit remains pending -

Held, O. 2 r. 2 of CPC contemplate 2 different situations, namely, where a plaintiff omits or relinquishes a part of a claim which he was entitled to make and, secondly, where the plaintiff omits or relinquishes one out of the several reliefs that he could have claimed in the suit - It was only in the latter situations where the plaintiff can file a subsequent suit seeking the relief omitted in the earlier suit proved that at the time of omission to claim the particular relief he had obtained leave of the Court in the first suit - In the instant case the second set of suits were filed during the pendency of the earlier suits, the provisions of O. 2, r. 2(3) of CPC will not be attracted - Appeals allowed.
