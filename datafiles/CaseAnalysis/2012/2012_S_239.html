Ramesh Harijan v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 139; (2012) 5 SCC 777; AIR 2012 SC 1979; 2012 CRLJ 2914; JT 2012 (5) SC 240; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 534; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 998; 2012(5) SCALE 561; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Reversal of acquittal order - Sustainability - Appellant was chargesheeted u/ss. 302 and 376 of IPC - Considering the evidence on record, the Trial Court acquitted the appellant of both the charges - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of Trial Court and convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 376 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC was justified in reversing the judgment and order of acquittal recorded by the Trial Court - Held, acquittal in the instant case by the Trial court was totally illegal, unwarranted and based on mis-appreciation of evidence for the reason that the court had given undue weightage to unimportant discrepancies and inconsistencies which resulted in miscarriage of justice - Thus, the HC was justified in reversing the order of acquittal - Appeal dismissed.
