Hiralal Patni v Loonkaram Sethiya and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 213; AIR 1962 SC 21; 1961 (2) ILR(All) 389; 1962 (2) SCJ 418; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Receivers

Summary: Land & Property - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - At Agra, there were three spinning mills and one flour mill - Following persons were joint owners of mills, (1) Hiralal Patni, appellant, and Munni Lal Mehta - 19/40 th share, (2) Gambhirmal Pandiya Private Ltd. - 8/40 th share (3) Messrs John and Co-11/40 th share and (4) I. E. John - 2/40 th share - Sethiya, respondent No. 1, advanced large amounts to Messrs John and Co. on security of its business assets and stocks - On 18-04-1949, said Sethiya filed a suit for recovery of amount due to him by sale of assets of said company - Pending suit, said Sethiya filed an application for appointment of a Receiver - Sethiya made another application for appointment of a Receiver for property of appellant and learned Civil Judge directed Receivers to take possession of appellant's share in mills also - On 05-04-1954, Civil Judge passed a preliminary decree against defendants - Preliminary decree directed that Receivers should continue on property until discharged - Hiralal Patni preferred a appeal to High Court against said preliminary decree - Said appeals were compromised and under terms of compromise parties agreed to take different mills on lease for a period of three years from Receiver On 14-01-1956, Receiver executed a lease in respect of flour mill in favour of appellant for a period of three years, a final decree was made in suit for sale of properties, but final decree was silent in regard to Receiver appointed earlier - On 29-09-1958, Hiralal applied to HC for extension of lease by three years - HC rejected application on ground that lease was only a stopgap arrangement and that it was for Receiver to make a fresh arrangement for future under supervision and directions of Civil Judge - On 17-01-1959, Receiver applied to Civil Judge for instructions whether he should proceed at once to dispossess appellant - Learned Civil Judge held that Receiver derived his authority from preliminary decree, and directed Receiver to lease out said flour mill by auction for a period of two years - Pursuant to that order, an auction was held, and appellant was highest bidder, and he paid lease amount and executed a formal lease deed - Not satisfied with order of Civil Judge, Hiralal Patni preferred an appeal - (A) Whether Receiver has no power to dispossess appellant in such a way as to prevent him from working his flour mill? - Held, under earlier orders, all properties of defendants were put in possession of Receivers, and under compromise order, Receiver was directed to run mills under agreed scheme - Compromise order in appeals covered also flour mill - Construction of relevant orders that flour mill of appellant was also put in possession of Receiver and that appellant was running said mill under compromise formula - (B) Whether after final decree, Receiver cannot exercise any powers in respect of rights of parties? - Further held, when a receiver has been appointed on an interlocutory application without any limit of time, it is not necessary to provide for continuance of his appointment in final judgment - Silence of judgment does not operate as a discharge of receiver or determination of his powers - Preliminary decree in express terms directed Receivers to continue till they were discharged - In circumstances, Receivers continued by preliminary decree are entitled to function in that capacity till they are discharged - (C) Whether, as appellant acquired a right under a lease deed and continued in possession after its expiry, he could be dispossessed only by a suit and not by a summary procedure? - Further held, Receiver, during course of administration of property, under a compromise arrangement for running mills, leased out flour mill to appellant - Term of lease had expired, and court directed Receiver to take possession of mill - Court was legally competent to confer a power on Receiver u/O. XL, r. 1(1)(d), of CPC to recover property from appellant - Appeal dismissed.
