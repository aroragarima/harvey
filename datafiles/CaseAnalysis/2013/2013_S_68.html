N. Sengodan v Secretary To Government, Home (Prohibition & Excise) Department, Chennai And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 391; (2013) 8 SCC 664; JT 2013 (9) SC 516; 2013 (2) OLR 1056; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 988; 2013(8) SCALE 213

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Police (Incitement To Disaffection) Act, 1922

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug Offenders, Forest Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slumgrabbers Act, 1982, ss. 3 and 10 - Police (Incitement to Disaffection) Act, 1922, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 505 - Illegal detention - Appellant was an Ex-service man who served in the Indian Army for a period of seven years; later he joined in the Tamil Nadu Subordinate Police Services and retired from the service on 21-10-1997 - respondent no. 2 was a former Inspector General and Commissioner of Police and the respondent no. 3 was former Inspector of Police - Respondent no. 4, was former Inspector of Police - It was alleged that Tamil Daily Malai Murasu published a news item allegedly authored by the appellant; based on the said news item, respondent no. 3, had registered a case for offence u/s. 3 of the Act, 1922 and s. 505(1)(b) of the IPC - Appellant was arrested by the respondent no. 3 and remanded to judicial custody - Later, appellant was detained under - Detention order was placed before the Advisory Board u/s. 10 of the Act, 1982 - After taking into consideration the representation and the connected records the Advisory Board expressed its unanimous opinion that there was no sufficient cause for detention of the appellant - In view of the non-approval of the detention order by the Advisory Board and its finding, the State Govt. revoked the detention order - Writ petition was filed by appellant alleging that the appellant was very much affected both in body and mind - Appellant was also subjected to mental cruelty and was also physically affected as a result of the confinement in Central Prison - Single Judge dismissed the writ petition on the ground that the appellant failed to establish malafide intention on the part of the respondents in registering a criminal case and detaining him under Act, 1982 - Said judgment was upheld by the DB by the impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled for any damage for having detained for around two months u/s. 3(2) of the Act, 1982 - Held, in the instant case nothing had been brought to the notice of Court to prove that the appellant with intent to cause, fear or alarm to the public, or to any section of the public or to induce to commit an offence against the State Govt. or against the public tranquility, issued the said press statement - Respondents failed to bring on record the evidence to show that the appellant was engaged, or was making preparations for engaging, in any of his activities as a 'Goonda' which may affect or are likely to affect adversely the maintenance of public order - In absence of any such ingredients, the Advisory Board constituted u/s. 10 of the Act, 1982 rightly held that there was no sufficient cause for detention of the appellant - Respondents failed to bring on record any evidence to suggest that the appellant incited the police personnel of Tamil Nadu to form an association to fight their rights against the Govt. - Further, press statement did not make out a case either u/s. 3 of the Act, 1992 or u/s. 505(1)(b) of the IPC - Hence, action on the part of the respondent nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4 in support of their act of detaining the appellant illegally by placing some material which beyond the record justified the appellant's allegation that the respondents abused their power and position to support their unfair order - Respondent-State and its officers grossly abused legal power to punish the appellant to destroy his reputation in a manner non-oriented by law by detaining him based on the wrong statements which were fully unwarranted - Cost of Rs.2 lacs imposed on the State of Tamil Nadu for payment in favour of the appellant - Appeal allowed.
