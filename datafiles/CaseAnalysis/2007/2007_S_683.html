M.P.Ram Mohan Raja v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 416; (2007) 9 SCC 78; AIR 2007 SC 1742; 2007 (3) CTC 506; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 123; JT 2007 (6) SC 195; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 961; 2007(6) SCALE 186; 2007 (7) SCJ 952; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1959 - r.39 - Appeals against the order of Division Bench of High Court wherein the High Court dismissed the petition on the ground of delay and laches - On 8.10.1996 an order was passed by the Collector in pursuance to the order passed by the High Court, rejecting the application of the writ petitioner for consideration of the grant of mining lease - Petitioner challenged the order of Collector passed on 27.4.2003 which was dismissed by High Court- Hence appeal - Held, a person who can sit tight for such a long time for no justifiable reason, cannot be given any benefit, the rule 39 under which the writ petitioner sought direction for consideration of his application has already been repealed within the time frame directed by the High Court, therefore the basis on which the order was passed has been totally knocked out, therefore, it could not have been possible for the authorities to have acceded to the request of the writ petitioner - Appeal dismissed.
