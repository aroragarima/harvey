Madhya Pradesh Housing Board and Another v Manoj Shrivastava

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 259; (2006) SCC (L&S) 422; (2006) 2 SCC 702; AIR 2006 SC 3499; JT 2006 (3) SC 73; 2006(2) SCALE 572; 2006 (2) SCJ 577; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 537; 2006 (7) SLR 316

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960, ss. 31(3), 64-A - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1961 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 162, 226, 309 - Respondent appointed on daily wage, as a Sub-Engineer - Labour Court held that applicant is entitled to claim pay scale of permanent category from 10/5/2000 date of submitting application - WP dismissed - Appeal against - Held, a daily wager does not hold a post unless he is appointed in terms of Act and rules - He does not derive any legal right in relation thereto - An employee would not come within purview of definition of 'permanent employee' only because he has completed six months' satisfactory service - Service must be rendered in a clear vacancy in one or more posts which was established - Appeal allowed.
