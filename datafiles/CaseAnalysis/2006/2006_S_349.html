Ramjee Rai and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 459; (2006) 13 SCC 229; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 626; 2007 (2) ALT 76; 2006 CRLJ 4630; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 356; JT 2006 (7) SC 610; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 289; 2006(8) SCALE 440; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 240

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 149, 201, 302, 34 - Conviction - Sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life under ss 302/34 and five years rigorous imprisonment u/s. 201 - Appeal against - Held, some of witnesses examined by prosecution are independent - Evidence of all witnesses are more or less consistent - Nothing has been pointed out to discredit their testimonies - Sessions Judge as also HC, therefore, cannot be said to have committed any mistake in relying upon testimonies of said witnesses - Appeal dismissed.
