Rudra Kumar Sain and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, D.P. Mohapatra, Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2837; (2000) 8 SCC 25; (2000) SCC (L&S) 1055; AIR 2000 SC 2808; 2000 (3) CLR 294; JT 2000 (9) SC 299; 2000 (4) LLN 29; 2000(6) SCALE 54; 2000 (4) SLR 787; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Service - Miscellaneous - Delhi Higher Judicial Service - Inter se seniority between direct recruits and promotees - In service jurisprudence, a person who possesses requisite qualification for being appointed to a particular post and then he is appointed with approval and consultation of appropriate authority and continues in post for a fairly long period, then such an appointment cannot be held to be 'stopgap or fortuitous or purely ad hoc' - Held, it is to be quashed seniority list, both provisional and final, so far as, it relates to appointees either by direct recruitment or by promotion in Delhi Higher Judicial Service, prior to amendment of Recruitment Rules in year 1987, and their inter se seniority must be redetermined on basis of continuous length of service in cadre - Petition allowed.
