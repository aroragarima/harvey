Mustakmiya Jabbarmiya Shaikh v M. M. Mehta, Commissioner of Police and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1622; (1995) 3 SCC 237; (1995) SCC (Cr) 454; 1995 (4) CCR 89; 1995 (3) Crimes 18; JT 1995 (4) SC 215; 1995(2) SCALE 376; 1995 (2) SCJ 127; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 960; 1995 (1) UJ 792

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Betting

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Validity of - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 32 - Arms Act, 1959, - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 212 and 214 - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-Social Activities Act, 1985, ss. 2, 3 and 9 - Habeas Corpus - Petitioner detained by order of commissioner of police for preventing him from acting prejudicial to public order - Found in possession of firearm which was rusty and barrel broken - Held, mere possession of firearm does not bring case of petitioner within ambit of Act - Individual acts of offence needs to be distinguished from one that effect public order - There was no material which may lead to a reasonable and definite conclusion that detenu-petitioner was habitually engaged in criminal activities and, therefore, is a dangerous person - Detention order set-aside - Petition allowed.
