Satya Narain Singh Etc. Etc. v The High Court Of Judicatureat Allahabad And Ors., Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 242; (1985) SCC (L&S) 196; (1985) 1 SCC 225; AIR 1985 SC 308; 1985 (50) FLR 102; 1985 LabIC 1230; 1985 (2) LLJ 1; 1985 (2) LLN 72; 1984(2) SCALE 790; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 112; 1985 (2) SLR 819; 1985 UJ 29

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - service - Direct recruitment - Petitioners, members of judicial service, in response to advertisement, applied to be appointed by direct recruitment - As questions arise regarding eligibility of members, some petitioners filed writ before HC which were dismissed - Appeals were filed before SC which were also dismissed - Remaining petitioners filed instant petitions.

Held, that service of the Union or the State' has been interpreted by court to mean judicial service and while the first clause makes consultation by the Governor of the State with the HC and, the second clause requires that the HC must recommend a person for appointment as a District Judge. It is only in respect of the persons covered by the second clause that there is a requirement that a person shall be eligible for appointment as District Judge if he has been an advocate or a pleader for not less than 7 years. Therefore, in the case of candidates who are not members of a Judicial Service they must have been advocates or pleaders for not less than 7 years and they have to be recommended by the HC before they may be appointed as District Judges, while in the case of candidates who are members of a Judicial Service the 7 years rule has no application but there has to be consultation with HC. Thus, a clear distinction is made between the two sources of recruitment and the dichotomy is maintained and two streams are separate until they come together by appointment. Petitions dismissed.
