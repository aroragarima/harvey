Assistant Controller of Estate-Duty v V. Devaki Ammal

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1980; (1995) Supp2 SCC 39; AIR 1995 SCW 30; 1995 (125) CTR 134; [1995] 212 ITR 395; JT 1994 (7) SC 543; 1994(4) SCALE 995; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Estate Duty Act, 1953, s. 34(1)(c) - Whether s. 34(1)(c) of Estate Duty Act is discriminatory and violative of art. 14 of Constitution as discrimination is brought about between members of a Mitakshara joint Hindu family and members of a Dayabhaga family? - Held, s. 34 sets out at one place in statute cases in which aggregation is to be made - That aggregation is to be made in respect of properties which fall u/cls. (a) and (b), which pass on death, and also in respect of property u/cl. (c), which is deemed to pass on death, does not lead to conclusion that unequals are treated equally - In case of a member of a Dayabhaga family dying, no question of aggregation can arise at all, for, member of such a family dying possessed by reason of his personal law a defined share in assets of family, unlike a deceased member belonging to a joint Hindu family governed by Mitakshara law - It is precisely for that reason that in case of a member belonging to a joint Hindu family governed by Mitakshara law dying, principle of aggregation has been embodied in s. 34(1)(c) - But for principle of aggregation, rate applicable to such a case will be rate corresponding to value of benefit that can be regarded as having accrued to each of lineal descendants of deceased - Whereas, in case of a Dayabhaga family, in view of fact that share of deceased member is a crystallised one, rate applicable in that case would be a rate corresponding to value of share of deceased member - It may be seen, therefore, that, but for principle of aggregation envisaged by s. 34(1)(c), there would be discrimination - S. 34(1)(c) avoids such a discrimination - Civil Appeal No. 71 of 1975 allowed.
