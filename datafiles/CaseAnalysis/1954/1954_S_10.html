Â M. P. Sharma and Others v Satish Chandra, District Magistrate, Delhi, and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 154; AIR 1954 SC 300; 1954 CRLJ 865; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 287; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 680; 1954 (56) PLR 366; 1954 SCJ 428; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1077

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code 1898, Indian Companies Act, Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 20(3), 19(1)(f) and 32 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 94, 96 and 342 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 119 and 132 - Indian Companies Act, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 408, 409, 418, 420, 465, 467, 468, 471 and 477(a) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 7 - Investigation into affairs of company - Inception of company - Various dishonest and fraudulent transactions and false records - Dishonest transfers of money made - Offenses u/ss. 406, 408, 409, 418, 420, 465, 467, 468, 471 and 477(a) had been committed - Issue of warrants for search of documents - Searches were made - Petitioners pray that search warrants may be quashed as being absolutely illegal, and ask for return of documents seized - Whether search warrants for seizure of documents are unconstitutional and illegal as it tantamount to compelled production of evidence? - Issue of a search warrant is normally judicial function of Magistrate - When such judicial function is interposed between individual and officer's authority for search, no circumvention thereby of fundamental right is to be assumed - Held, searches in present cases cannot be challenged as illegal on ground of violation of any fundamental rights - Applications dismissed.
