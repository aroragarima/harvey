Noida Entrepreneurs Assn v Noida & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 27; (2007) 10 SCC 385; 2007 (2) ADJ 86; AIR 2007 SC 1161; 2007 (6) AWC 6541; 2007 (3) CTC 211; JT 2007 (2) SC 620; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 884; 2007(2) SCALE 131; 2007 (4) SCJ 839; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 892; 2007 (2) SLR 164; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 962; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1431

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Conducting The Enquiry, Vigilance Manual

Summary: Irregularities in allotments and conversions of land in New Okhla Industrial Development Authority - Decision of Uttar Pradesh Government not to take disciplinary action respondent - Writ petition against - Held, standard of proof required in departmental proceedings is not the same as required to prove a criminal charge and even if there is an acquittal in the criminal proceedings the same does not bar departmental proceedings - That being so, the order of the State Government deciding not to continue the departmental proceedings is clearly untenable and is quashed - Order accordingly.
