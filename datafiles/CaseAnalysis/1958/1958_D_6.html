Deity Pattabhiramaswamy v S. Hanymayya and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1959 SC 57; 1958 ALT(SC) 834

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Considering oral, documentary and circumstancial evidence, dstrict judge held that appellant had title to suit property - High Court set aside fingings of district judge - Whether HC had jurisdiction to interfere with findings of learned district judge in exercise of its power u/s. 100, CPC? - Held, no direct evidence to prove title of appellant - District judge was in error in not considering any document of title but only on consideration of relevant documentary and oral evidence - HC clearly exceeded its jurisdiction in setting aside findings of district judge - Provisions of s. 100 are clear and unambiguous - HC had no jurisdiction to interfere in second appeal with findings of fact given by first Appellate Court based on evidence - Appeal allowed.
