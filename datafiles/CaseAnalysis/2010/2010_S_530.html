Kunga Nima Lepcha and Others v State of Sikkim and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 207; (2010) 4 SCC 513; AIR 2010 SC 1671; 2010(3) ALL MR 439; 2010 (80) ALR 741; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 357; 2010 CRLJ 2411; JT 2010 (3) SC 222; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 531; 2010(3) SCALE 309

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 32 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Misused of public office - Petitioners filed writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution alleging that Chief Minister of State of Sikkim (respondent no. 2) has misused his public office to amass assets disproportionate to his known sources of income - Petitioners also alleged that he has misappropriated a large volume of public money at the cost of the Government of India and the Government of Sikkim - Relief sought by the petitioners is the issuance of a writ of mandamus directing the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) to investigate the allegations that have been levelled against respondent no. 2 - Whether writ of mandamus could be issued and directions could be given to CBI to investigate the allegations that have been levelled against respondent no. 2? - Held, no - Remedies evolved by way of writ jurisdiction are of an extraordinary nature - They cannot be granted as a matter of due course to provide redressal in situations where statutory remedies are available - It is quite evident that the onus is on the petitioners to demonstrate a specific violation of any of the fundamental rights in order to seek relief under writ jurisdiction - In the present petition, petitioners have made a rather vague argument that the alleged acts of corruption on part of respondent no. 2 amount to an infringement of art. 14 of the Constitution of India - Alleged acts can easily come within the ambit of statutory offences such as those of 'possession of assets disproportionate to known sources of income' as well as 'criminal misconduct' under the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Onus of launching an investigation into such matters is clearly on the investigating agencies such as the State Police, Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) or the Central Vigilance Commission (CVC) among others - It is not proper for SC to give directions for initiating such an investigation under its writ jurisdiction - Writ petition dismissed.
