Secy. And Commissioner v R. Kirubakaran

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1482; (1994) Supp1 SCC 155; (1994) SCC (L&S) 449; AIR 1993 SC 2647; 1994 (26) ATC 828; 1993 (2) CLR 860; 1993 (67) FLR 978; JT 1993 (5) SC 404; 1994 (1) LLJ 673; 1994 (2) LLN 1007; 1993(3) SCALE 829; 1994 (1) SLJ 141; 1993 (5) SLR 585; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Administrative - Constitution, art. 226 and 311 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 14 - T.N. Service Manual, rr. 49 and 49A - Correction of date of birth - Petition for interim relief for continuation in service - (A) Whether permissible only on basis of report submitted by Revenue Officer after holding enquiry - Alteration of date of birth - Sought on eve of superannuation or near about that time - Held, (A) Court or Tribunal should be slow in granting it, unless prima facie evidence of unimpeachable character is produced - Not permissible - (B) Court or Tribunal should be more cautious because of growing tendency amongst section of Public Servants to raise such dispute without explaining as to why it was not raised earlier - Petition allowed.
