Sanobanu Nazirbhai Mirza and others v Ahmedabad Municipal Transport Service

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 653; (2013) 16 SCC 719; 2013 (4) ACC 825; 2013 ACJ 2733; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 116; 2013(6) ALL MR 981; 2013 (101) ALR 721; 2014 (1) AWC 315; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 446; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 177; 2013 (7) MLJ 530; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 732; 2013(12) SCALE 329; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Deduction in compensation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Sections 166 and 168-Compensation-Death case-Enhancement-Deceased was performing as polisher, which is skilled job-Compensation awarded in favour of claimants/appellants by Tribunal reduced from R 3,51,300 to R 2,51,800 by High Court-Whether interference of High Court with quantum of compensation awarded by Tribunal, legal, valid and justified?-What amount claimants/appellants entitled to?-State Government in exercise of its statutory power under Section 3 of Minimum Wages Act, 1948 must issue notification for fixing wages of polisher-Even in absence of such notification annual income of deceased would came to R 60,000-No specific mention regarding enhancement of compensation in appeal-Appellants basically requested to set aside judgment and order of High Court-Compensation awarded by Apex Court, more than what was claimed by appellants-It is statutory duty of Tribunal and appellate court to award just and reasonable compensation to legal representations of deceased to mitigate their hardship and agony-Even though certain claims not preferred by appellants, they are still legally and legitimately entitled for-Appellants entitled for amount of R 16,96,000 by way of compensation under various heads-Compensation amount of R 16,96,000 to carry interest @ 7.5% p.a.
