Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan And Ors v Shri Dharmendra Sharma

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 915; (2007) 8 SCC 148; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 804; AIR 2007 SC 3210; 2007 (4) AWC 4049; 2007 (4) ESC 599; 2007 (115) FLR 351; JT 2007 (11) SC 533; 2007(11) SCALE 157; 2007 (7) SCJ 558; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1007; 2007 (6) SLR 463; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 464

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Union of India and others were directed to consider the request of the respondent for appointment on compassionate ground against 5% of the 53 Group-D vacancies available - Appeal against - Held, a policy decision had been taken not to make appointment of Group 'D' post - Since the policy decision was not challenged, it was incumbent upon the Tribunal and the High Court to examine the applicability of the policy decision - No direction could have been given to KVS to act contrary to its policy decision - If at any point of time KVS wants to adopt any compassionate appointments scheme and intends to make appointments in Group D posts, the case of the respondent shall be duly considered - Appeal partly allowed.
