United India Insurance Company Limited and another v Orient Treasures Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 30; (2016) 3 SCC 49; 2016 ACJ 2161; AIR 2016 SC 363; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 157; 2016(3) ALL MR 441; 2016 (117) ALR 10; 2016 (2) AWC 1091; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 106; 2016 (1) CPJ(Sc) 6; JT 2016 (1) SC 147; 2016 (1) MLJ 559; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 878; 2016(1) SCALE 308; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 106

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Contract Of Insurance, Proposal Form, Assured, Ambiguity

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 23 - Repudiation of claim - Policy conditions - Respondent insured their jewellery kept in their shop with appellant-insurance company under successive 'Jewellers Block Policies' w.e.f.2-7-1993 onwards and later, policy was renewed for further one year, from 2-7-1994 to 1-7-1995 - While so, on 2-6-1995, there was burglary in shop and respondent after completion of formalities, submitted claim with appellant-insurance company on basis of insurance policy towards loss of jewellery - However, said claim of respondent was repudiated inter alia on the ground that the stolen jewellery was kept in contrary to terms of policy - Aggrieved, respondent filed complaint before Commission claiming sum of Rs.1,32,06,786 but Commission partly allowed said claim and directed appellant-insurance company to pay Rs.36,10,211 and also costs of Rs.50,000 - Aggrieved, both appellant-Insurance Company and respondent filed instant appeals - Whether Commission was justified in allowing complaint in part and awarding sum of Rs.36,10,211 to respondent.

Held, a contract of insurance is one of the species of commercial transaction between the insurer and insured. It is for the parties (insurer/insured) to decide as to what type of insurance they intend to do to secure safety of the goods and how much premium the insured wish to pay to secure insurance of their goods as provided in the tariff. If the insured pays additional premium to the insurer to secure more safety and coverage of their insured goods, it is permissible for them to do so. In instant case, it is the case of the respondent that the burglary took place at night and the insured items kept in display window and some lying out of safe were stolen. Due to these facts, clauses 4, 5 and 12 were attracted against the respondent. Further, clause 12 of the policy provides in clear terms, that the appellant would not be liable to indemnify any loss under the policy if such loss or damage to the insured property occurs while the insured property was kept in window display at night or while it was kept out of safe after business hours. Further, in order to claim benefit of the policy, it was obligatory upon the respondent to have removed the insured items from display window every day after business hours and keep them in safe during night hours till opening of the shop next day. However, the same was not done by respondent. Appeal filed by the insurance company allowed. Appeals disposed of.
