ANZ Grindlays Bank Limited @ Standard Chartered v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 928; (2005) 12 SCC 738; AIR 2006 SC 296; 2006 (1) CLR 6; 2006 (2) CTC 589; JT 2005 (9) SC 413; 2006 (1) LLJ 271; 2005(9) SCALE 174; 2006 (1) SLR 246; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 18(1) - Settlement arrived at between appellant-Bank and All India Grindlays Bank Employees Association (3rd respondent) - Whether Central Govt. was justified in making a reference to Industrial Tribunal at the instance of All India Grindlays Bank Employees Federation (2nd respondent) for adjudication by the Industrial Tribunal? - Held, settlement arrived at between Bank and Association was u/s. 18(1) and consequently it did not bind those who are not parties to settlement like Federation and thus rights, if any, of Federation were not affected in any manner by settlement - To protect its interest Bank was perfectly justified in asking for a receipt from those employees, who were not members of Association but wanted to avail of benefit of settlement - Reference made by Central Govt. is wholly uncalled for and deserves to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
