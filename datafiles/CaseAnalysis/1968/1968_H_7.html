Supreme Court of India

27 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 468; AIR 1970 SC 755; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 350; 1970 (1) SCJ 554; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Intention And Attempt

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(d), First sch., item 19 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Exemption - Notification dt. 31.07.1959 as amended by notification dt. 30.04.1960 - Cotton fabrics produced on power looms - Appellant-dealer of textiles, entered into an agreement with a co-operative Society for weaving yarn supplied by him into cotton fabrics on power looms owned by its member - Appellant claimed exemption under notification dt. 31.07.1959 from excise duty in respect of' the cotton fabrics which were manufactured for it on power looms by the Co-operative Society - Said claim was rejected by the department - Appeal filed against said order was dismissed by appellate authority - Thereafter the appellant moved the HC for grant of a writ u/art. 226 of the Constitution - HC partially allowed said writ petition and held that exemption to be granted only when cotton fabrics were manufactured by a Co-operative Society for itself - Hence, present appeal - Whether exemption under notification dt. 31.07.1959 could be granted only when the cotton fabrics were manufactured by a co-operative society for itself? - Held, for claiming exemption under notification dt. 31.07.1959 as amended by notification dt. 30.04.1960, cotton fabrics must be produced on power looms owned by the co-operative society - There is no further requirement under the said two notifications that the cotton fabrics must be produced by the co-operative society on power looms for itself - Therefore, appellant was entitled to the said claim - Appeal allowed.
