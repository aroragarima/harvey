Jyotendrasinhji v S. I. Tripathi and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 880; (1993) Supp3 SCC 389; AIR 1993 SC 1991; 1993 (111) CTR 370; [1993] 201 ITR 611; JT 1993 (2) SC 664; 1993(2) SCALE 408; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 938; [1993] 68 TAXMAN 59; 1993 (2) TLR 854

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 245D(4) and 245D - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - (A) Settlement commission - Appeal against its order to High Court or Supreme Court - Scope of enquiry in appeal - (B) Special leave petition - Party directly approaching Supreme Court u/art. 136, instead of approaching High Court u/art. 226 - Whether gains any advantage - (C) Settlement Commission - Appeal against its order to Supreme Court - Interference - Plea that Commission has wrongly interpreted trust deeds - Can it be ground for interference - Held, (A) Limited to question whether its order is contrary to Act and whether such contravention has prejudiced appellant - (B) A party does not and cannot gain any advantage by approaching Supreme Court directly - (C) Cannot be ground for interference, because it cannot be said to be violative of provisions of Act - Appeal dismissed.
