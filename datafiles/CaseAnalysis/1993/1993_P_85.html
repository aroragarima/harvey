Punjab and Haryana High Court Bar Association v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 177; (1994) SCC (Cr) 455; (1994) 1 SCC 616; AIR 1994 SC 1023; 1994 (1) CCR 7; 1994 CRLJ 1368; JT 1993 (6) SC 650; 1993(4) SCALE 636; 1994 (1) UJ 199

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution, 1950, art. 136 - Criminal Procedure Code ,1973, s. 156 - In instant case it was alleged that a practicing lawyer, his wife and child were abducted and murdered and lawyers fraternity were not satisfied with police investigation and demanded judicial enquiry - Whether fresh investigation id to directed in gruesome crime committed in instant case? - Held, Supreme Court ordinarily do not re-open investigation but in facts and circumstances of instant case, to do complete justice in matter and to instill confidence in public mind it is necessary to have fresh investigation in this case through a specialised agency like Central Bureau of Investigation and CBI was directed to take up investigation of case - Appeal disposed of.
