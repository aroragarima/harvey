Â Filterco and Another v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra, V. Khalid, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 660; (1986) 2 SCC 103; AIR 1986 SC 626; 1986 (8) ECC 150; 1986 (6) ECR 558; 1986 (24) E.L.T. 180; 1986(1) SCALE 171; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 239; 1986 (61) STC 318; 1986 TAXLR 2275; 1986 (2) UJ 203

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Sales Tax - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, ss.10, 42B , sch.1 item 6 and sch.2 Part 6, item 1 - Constitution, art.226 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.115 - Sales Tax - Exemption - Compressed woolen felts - (A) Compressed woolen felts, if come under expression 'cloth' - (B) Assessment - Exemption claimed u/ item 6 of sch.1 - Taking different view from earlier one , commissioner refused exemption - Application u/s. 42.B ( for taxability) - Ordered that for claiming exemption pliability is essential - High Court summarily dismissed the writ petition filed - (C) Order exempting material as cloth on basis of single sample - 26 samples sent by assesse for determininig taxability - Now Commissioner holding that pliability is essential - Applicability of principle of estoppel - Held, compressed woolen felts must possess basic feature of pliability to constitute cloth - Held, not justified, HC should have disposed of the petition on merits because remedies under the Act were not efficacious - Held, that principle of issue estoppel was not attracted, however, it was a fit case for Government to consider waiver of tax during that period to save industry from financial ruination - Appeal dismissed.
