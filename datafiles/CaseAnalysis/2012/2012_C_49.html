Catholic Syrian Bank Ltd. v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Thrissur

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 56; (2012) 3 SCC 784; AIR 2012 SC 1538; [2012] 343 ITR 270; JT 2012 (2) SC 493; 2012(2) SCALE 573; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 739; [2012] 206 TAXMAN 182

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1979, Finance Act, 1985, Finance Act, 1986, Income Tax (Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - the Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 143(1), 143(2), 36(1) 36(1)(viia) - A/y 2002-2003 - Double taxation - Written off - Assessee, Scheduled Bank, filed its return of income for A/y 2002- 2003, declaring total income of Rs. 61,15,610/- , return was processed u/s. 143(1) of Act, and eligible refund was issued in favour of assessee - Assessing officer issued notice u/s. 143(2) of Act to assessee, after which assessment was completed - AO noticed that observed that assessee having a provision of Rs. 15,01,29,990/- for bad and doubtful debts u/s. 36(1)(viia) of Act could not claim amount of Rs. 12,65,95,770/- as deduction on account of bad debts because bad debts did not exceed credit balance in provision for bad and doubtful debts account and also, requirements of clause (v) of Sub-section (2) of s. 36 of Act were not satisfied - Assesse challenged before CIT(A), which was partly allowed holding that claim of the appellant was fully supported by decision and since entire bad debts written off by bank u/s. 36(1)(vii) were pertaining to urban branches only and not to provision made for rural branches u/s. 36(1)(viia), it was entitled to deduction of full claimed amount of Rs. 12,65,95,770/-, directed deletion of said amount - Appeal before ITAT by revenue was dismissed and also certain other benefits were granted to assessee - Revenue dissatisfied with order of ITAT in A/y 2002-2003, filed an appeal before HC u/s. 260A of Act - DB hearing bunch of appeals against order of ITAT, expressed view that judgment was not a correct exposition of law, directed matter to be placed before a Full Bench of HC - DB, allowed appeals by setting aside orders of Tribunal and restoring assessments confirmed in first Appeals - Aggrieved, instant appeal - Whether on facts and circumstances of case, assessee is eligible for deduction of bad and doubtful debts actually written off in view of s. 36(1)(vii) which limits deduction allowable under proviso to excess over credit balance made under clause (viia) of Section 36(1) of ITA - Held, s. 36(1)(vii) and 36(1)(viia) of Act are distinct and independent items of deduction and operate in their respective fields, bad debts written off in debts, other than those for which provision is made u/cl. (viia), will be covered under main part of s. 36(1)(vii), while proviso will operate in cases u/cl. (viia) to limit deduction to extent of difference between debt or part thereof written off in previous year and credit balance in provision for bad and doubtful debts account made u/cl. (viia) - Proviso to s. 36(1)(vii) will relate to cases covered u/s. 36(1)(viia) and has to be read with s. 36(2)(v) of Act - Appeal allowed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - the Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 143(1), 143(2), 36(1) 36(1)(viia) - A/y 2002-2003 - Double taxation - Written off - CIT(A), which was partly allowed holding that claim of the appellant was fully supported by decision and since entire bad debts written off by bank u/s. 36(1)(vii) were pertaining to urban branches only and not to provision made for rural branches u/s. 36(1)(viia), it was entitled to deduction of full claimed amount of Rs. 12,65,95,770/-, directed deletion of said amount - DB, allowed appeals by setting aside orders of Tribunal and restoring assessments confirmed in first Appeals - Aggrieved, instant appeal - Whether on facts and circumstances of case, assessee is eligible for deduction of bad and doubtful debts actually written off in view of s. 36(1)(vii) which limits deduction allowable under proviso to excess over credit balance made under clause (viia) of Section 36(1) of ITA - Held, cls. (viia) of s. 36(1) relating to deduction on account of provision for bad and doubtful debt(s) is distinct and independent of provisions of s. 36(1)(vii) relating to allowance of bad debt(s) - Scheduled commercial banks would continue to get full benefit of write off of irrecoverable debt(s) u/s. 36(1)(vii) in addition to benefit of deduction for provision made for bad and doubtful debt(s) u/s. 36(1)(viia) - Circulars issued by CBDT indicates that normally a deduction for bad debt(s) can be allowed only if debt is written off in books as bad debt(s), no deduction is allowable in respect of a mere provision for bad and doubtful debt(s), but in case of rural advances, a deduction would be allowed even in respect of a mere provision without insisting on an actual write off, above question is answered in affirmative, in favour of assessee - Appeal allowed.
