M/S. Xerox India Ltd. v Commissioner Of Customs, Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 985; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 161; JT 2010 (13) SC 1; 2010(12) SCALE 281

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: CEGAT

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, headings 8479.89 and 8471.60 - Classification - Multi-Functional Machines - Whether the Multi-Functional Machines imported by the appellants fall under Chapter Heading 8479.89 (Residual Heading) as claimed by the Revenue or under Chapter Heading 8471.60, as claimed by the appellants, under Customs Tariff Act, 1975? - Held, based on the nature of the functions Multi- Functional Machines perform, the Multi- Functional Machines would serve as input and output devices of an ADPM (computer) and thus serve as unit of an ADPM, which on a reading of chapter note 5(C)of Tariff Act, 1975, clearly classifies them as falling under heading 84.71.60 of the Tariff Act, 1975 - Hence, correct classification for the imported Multi-Functional Machines should be under Customs Tariff Chapter heading 84.71.60 of Tariff Act, 1975 - Appeal allowed.
