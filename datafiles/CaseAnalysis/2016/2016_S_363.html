Surinderjit Singh Mand and another v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 484; (2016) 8 SCC 722; AIR 2016 SC 3251; 2016 CRLJ 3937; 2016 (4) JCC 2220; JT 2016 (6) SC 270; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 267; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 654; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, Habeas Corpus, FIR, Jurisdiction, Opportunity, Electricity, Official Duty, Reliance, Rank, Cognizance, Torture, Employed, Registered, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Corruption

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 193, 197, 319, 36, 49, 50, 50A - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 19 - Sanction for prosecution - Accused was arrested for theft of motorcycles and other vehicles - Accused remained in jail from 28-06-1999 to 30-06-1999 - Mother of accused filed a complaint stating that her son was apprehended illegally and unauthorisedly for the period from 24-06-1999 to 28-06-1999 - Investigation was conducted and it was concluded that there was no material to establish that accused was in police detention from 24-06-1999 onwards, till his arrest on 28-06-1999 - Mother of accused filed an application u/s. 319 CrPC before Trial Court - Trial Court framed charges against appellants/police officials - Aggrieved appellants filed revision before HC alleging that no sanction was obtained by prosecution u/s.197 CrPC before taking cognizance against them - HC dismissed revision petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether arrest of accused from 28-06-1999 to 30-06-1999 would fall within the purview of acting or purporting to act in discharge of official duties.

Held, based on legal position declared by SC in Pp Unnikrishnan And Another v. Puttiyottil Alikutty Anr Another, 2000 Indlaw SC 265, sanction for prosecution of appellants in relation to detention of accused for period from 24-06-1999 to 28-06-1999, would not be required before a Court of competent jurisdiction, takes cognizance with reference to alleged arrest of accused. If appellants had not detained accused during the above period, it is not open to anyone to assume the position that detention of accused during the above period was while acting or purporting to act in discharge of their official duties. Therefore, HC was correct in the conclusions drawn to the above effect. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 193, 197, 319, 36, 49, 50, 50A - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 19 - Cognizance of offence - Sanction - Whether protection afforded to public servants u/s. 197 CrPC while discharging their official duties is to shield public servants from frivolous harassment of prosecution at the hands of private individuals.

Held, u/s. 197 CrPC sanction mandated under a special statute (as postulated u/s.19 of the Act) would be a necessary pre-requisite, before a Court of competent jurisdiction, takes cognizance of an offence (whether under IPC or under concerned special statutory enactment). The procedure for obtaining sanction would be governed by provisions of CrPC and mandated under special enactment. Therefore, a Court just cannot take cognizance without sanction by appropriate authority. Thus, no merit that where cognizance is taken u/s. 319 CrPC sanction either u/s. 197 CrPC (or under the concerned special enactment) is not a mandatory pre-requisite. The grant of sanction u/s.197 CrPC can be assailed by accused by taking recourse to judicial review. Similarly, order declining sanction can be assailed by complainant or prosecution. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Court cannot take cognizance of offences without sanction of appropriate authority.
