United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v M/S. Pushpalaya Printers

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 163; (2004) 3 SCC 694; AIR 2004 SC 1700; 2004 (2) AWC 1876; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 920; 2004 CCC 13; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 132; 2004 (2) CompLJ 23; 2004 (1) CPJ 22; JT 2004 (4) SC 352; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 182; 2004 (137) PLR 296; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 253; 2004(2) SCALE 684B; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 631; 2004 (2) SLT 263; 2004 (2) Supreme 225

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; Insurance

Keywords: Uberrima Fides, Payment Of Premium

Summary: Insurance - meaning of the word impact - in the absence of specific exclusion and the word "impact" having more meanings in the context, it cannot be confined to forcible contact alone when it includes the meanings "to drive close", "effective action of one thing upon another" and "the effect of such action" - reasonable and fair to hold in the context that the word "impact" contained in clause 5 of the insurance policy covers the case of the respondent - damage caused to the building and machinery on account of the bulldozer moving closely on the road was on account of its "impact" - also settled position in law that if there is any ambiguity or a term is capable of two possible interpretations one beneficial to the insured should be accepted consistent with the purpose for which the policy is taken, namely, to cover the risk on the happening of certain event - rule of contra proferentem applied - appeal dismissed.
