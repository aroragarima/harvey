State Of Andhra Pradesh And Ors. Etc. Etc. v G. Sreenivasa Rao And Ors. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 511; (1989) 2 SCC 290; (1989) SCC (L&S) 339; 1989 (10) ATC 61; 1989 (2) CompLJ 178; 1989 (58) FLR 771; JT 1989 (1) SC 615; 1989 (2) LLJ 149; 1989 (2) LLN 1; 1989(1) SCALE 627; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 1000; 1989 (2) UJ 107

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: AP Fundamental Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 39(d), 14 and 16 - Andhra Pradesh Fundamental Rules, rr. 22(a)(i) and 27 - Whether payment of less salary to a senior than his junior in same cadre having same pay scale is violative of principle of 'equal pay for equal work' enshrined in art. 39(d) r/w arts. 14 and 16? - Held, educational qualifications and various other incidents of service - When a single running pay scale is provided in a cadre constitutional mandate of equal pay for equal work is satisfied - Ordinarily grant of higher pay to a junior would ex facie be arbitrary but if there are justifiable grounds in doing so seniors cannot invoke equality doctrine - Equal pay for equal work is a fundamental right - But equal pay must depend upon nature of work done, it cannot be judged by mere volume of work - So long as value judgment is made bona fide, reasonably on an intelligible criteria which has a rational nexus with object of differentiation, such differentiation will not amount to discrimination - Appeal allowed.
