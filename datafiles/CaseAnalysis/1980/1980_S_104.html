Hari Datt Kainthla & Anr. v State Of Himachal Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 183; (1980) 3 SCC 189; (1980) SCC (L&S) 335; AIR 1980 SC 1426; 1980 LabIC 825; 1980 (2) LLJ 128; 1980 (2) LLN 430; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 364; 1980 SLJ 646; 1980 (2) SLR 154

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Extra Provincial Jurisdiction Act, 1947, Himachal Pradesh (Court) Order 1948

Summary: Constitution, arts. 16, 233 and 235 - Judial Services - (A) Appointment of District Judges either by promotion or by direct recruitment - Criterion - Validity of promotion to cadre of District/Additional District & Sessions Judges on criterion of seniority-cum-merit - (B) Promotion - To to higher post in the same service - Who is competent to decide upon - (C) Power of confirmation in subordinate judicial service - Vest in - (D) Appointment of District Judges - Challenged by way of writ petition - Duty of administrative side of HC - Held that the criterion adopted by the HC was a valid criterion u/art. 16 and not violative of art. 233 - HC alone is competent to decide question of promotion - Power vests in HC - Administrative side of the HC, if joined as a party, must appear and place before the Court the entire record for a fair and judicial adjudication of the issues on the judicial side of the HC - Appeal dismissed.

.
