Dipak Banerjee v Lilabati Chakraborty

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28342; (1987) 4 SCC 161; AIR 1987 SC 2055; 1987 (1) CalLT(SC) 50; JT 1987 (3) SC 454; 1987 (2) RCJ 644; 1987 (2) RCR(Rent) 311; 1987 (3) RentLR 208; 1987(2) SCALE 239; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 680; 1987 (2) UJ 564

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 107 - Eviction of tenant on ground of sub-letting without permission - Issues arise : (A) Whether sub-tenancy exists when alleged sub-tenant was not in exclusive possession and there was no evidence of payment of rent - Further acceptance of service in lieu of right to occupy does not amount to acceptance of rent under Rent Act - (B) Change of user of premises - Allegation that sub-tenant was doing tailoring work in portion of premises - (A) Held, there was no sub-tenancy created - (B) Sub-tenancy not proved - Sewing machine was used as part of apparatus and not been used separately or independently and cannot constitute change of user - Appeal allowed.
