Food Corporation Of India Workers’ Union v Food Corporation Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 165; (1985) 2 SCC 294; (1985) SCC (L&S) 456; AIR 1985 SC 488; 1985 (50) FLR 50; 1985 LabIC 732; 1985 (2) LLN 30; 1985(1) SCALE 383; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 150; 1985 (1) SLJ 658; 1985 (1) SLR 615; 1985 UJ 849

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Controlled, Food Corporations Act, 1964, Contract Labour Act 1970

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, ss. 2(1)(a) and 2(1)(e) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(a)(i) - Representative action by contract labourers working with the FCI, distressed by the unhelpful attitude of both the Central and the State Govt. in not redressing their grievances for either departmentalising them or in the alternative extending to them the benefit of the Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act - Which is the appropriate Govt. for the implementation of the provisions of the Act - State Govt. held to be appropriate Govt.- Writ of mandamus issued - Petition allowed.
