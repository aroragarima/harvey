Neptune Assurance Company Limited v Life Insurance Corporation of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 251; AIR 1963 SC 900; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 289; [1963] 48 ITR 144; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 7 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(2), 18(3) and 48 - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 10 - Income Tax - Composite insurer - Income from shares and securities appertaining to life insurance business - Tax deduction at source - Later found to be in excess of tax due - Orders of assessment passed after 01-09-1956 - Refund u/s. 48 Income Tax Act - Maintainability of claim by Life Insurance Corporation to part of refund - Whether right to refund exist on 01-09-1956 - Whether it appertained to life insurance business of insurer within s. 7 of 1956 Act - Held, right to refund existed on 01-09-1956 as an asset - It appertained to life insurance business - Appeal dismissed.
