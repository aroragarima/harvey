TCI Finance Ltd. v Calcutta Medical Centre Ltd. Andanr.

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1721; (2005) 8 SCC 41; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 23; 2005 (61) ALR 197; 2005 (4) AWC 3803; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 230; JT 2005 (8) SC 537; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 347; 2005(7) SCALE 503; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47, O. 47 O. 21 - Money suit - Attachment of property - Rights of tenant - Determination - Appellant filed suit for recovery of money against 1st respondent private company - Since 1st respondent was absent in suit proceedings, Trial Court passed ex-parte decree in favour of appellant - Appellant filed execution petition and application for attaching 1st respondent's property - 1st respondent filed objection on the ground that it had been taken over by a public limited company and new public limited company was a tenant in that attached property - Respondent further contended that since receiver was appointed without notice to new company, its substantial rights and interest over properties as tenants were being affected - Executing Court dismissed appellant's objection and ordered for attachment - HC, in 1st respondent's appeal, set aside Executing Court's order and directed to decide question of tenancy of new company - Whether new company had any tenancy right over attached property - Held, 1st respondent did not claim any right of ownership over attached properties - No claims of nature set forth by 1st respondent could be examined in terms of s. 47 or O. 21 r. 58 CPC - By impugned judgment, HC had enlarged the scope of execution proceedings and had treated it as a full-blown suit without even recording any reason as to how 1st respondent had any adjudication interest in proceedings - Question of tenancy could not be decided by executing court - HC misconceived nature of claim set up by 1st respondent - Executing Court rightly noted that 1st respondent was not having independent right to properties - Right claimed by new company was as assignee under judgment-debtor and agreement, if any, in that regard was not produced before Court and, therefore, Executing Court drew adverse inference - Without indicating any reason as to how reasoning of Executing Court was wrong HC enlarged scope of controversy and directed Executing Court to decide question of tenancy, which was legally impermissible - HC permitted Executing Court to deal with matters which were clearly beyond scope of its adjudication - Impugned HC order was set aside and Executing Court order was affirmed - Appeals allowed.
