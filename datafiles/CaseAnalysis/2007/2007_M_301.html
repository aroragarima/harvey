Municipal Corporation, Jabalpur and Others v Messrs Rajesh Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 373; (2007) 5 SCC 344; AIR 2007 SC 2069; 2007 (3) AWC 2736; JT 2007 (5) SC 450; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 878; 2007 (4) MPLJ 26; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 821; 2007(5) SCALE 669; 2007 (7) SCJ 541; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 137

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration And Conciliation Act 1996

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act 1996 Act, 1940 - Notice was issued on the application for condonation of delay and also on the special leave petitions - Municipal Corporation directed to constitute a Board of Arbitrators under Clause 29 of the Agreement without any preconditions - High Court allowed the application to appoint an arbitrator - whether the High Court was justified in appointing a retired Chief Justice of a High Court as the sole arbitrator to resolve the disputes raised by the parties - Held, no reference for arbitration shall be maintainable unless the contractor furnishes the security deposit of a sum determined as per contract by the Corporation - The High Court was not justified in appointing a retired Chief Justice of a High Court to act as sole arbitrator - Contrary to Clause 29 of the contract -High Court, by its earlier order had directed to appoint an arbitrator in compliance with Clause 29 of the contract entered into between the parties - The order dated 29th July 2004 has been set aside - Appeal arising out of Special Leave petition has become infructuous.
