Kedar Mishra v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 293; (2016) 7 SCC 478; AIR 2016 SC 2110; 2016(4) ALL MR 963; 2016 (117) ALR 263; JT 2016 (4) SC 403; 2016 (132) RD 543; 2016(4) SCALE 318; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Applicant, Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961, Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Rules 1963

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961, s. 16(3) - Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Rules, 1963, r. 19 - Pre-emption - Depositing of amount under wrong head - In pursuance of orders of HC to determine whether appellant deposited consideration money along with ten percent amount in favour of Collector in accordance with law or not, Board of Revenue heard parties afresh and dismissed petitions filed by appellant holding that requisite money was not deposited in favour of Collector in compliance with r.19 and Form L.C.13 of the Rules and consequently, pre-emption applications of appellant stood dismissed - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petitions before HC but the same were dismissed on ground that there was no sufficient compliance of r.19 and Form L.C.13 of the Rules - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant complied with r.19 of the Rules.

Held, in compliance of s. 16(3)(i) and r. 19, appellant deposited 10% of purchase money for all three pre-emption cases but as against the column 'Account Officer who would collect the amount', because appellant mentioned a wrong head could not be a ground to dismiss the pre-emption applications of the appellant at the threshold. The appellant being a raiyat could not be expected to know the correct head under which the amount is to be deposited and so, a pedantic approach could not be adopted. Further, to non-suit the appellant on the ground of a technical objection that the amount has not been deposited under the head '2029 Land Revenue' but deposited under the head '0029 L.R.' would defeat the benevolent object of s. 16(3) of the Act. A party could not be denied right of adjudication of the matter on merits merely because of some inadvertent mistake. Hence, HC was not justified in viewing the treasury challan with a pedantic approach and was also not right in affirming the order passed by Board of Revenue. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside and matter is remitted back to Board of Revenue, to consider cases afresh on merits. Order accordingly.

Ratio - In terms of s.16(3)(i), no pre-emption application is to be entertained by the Collector unless the purchase money together with a sum equal to 10% thereof is deposited by the person claiming right of pre-emption in the prescribed manner within the specified period.
