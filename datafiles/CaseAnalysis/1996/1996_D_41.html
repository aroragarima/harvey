Sh. Satish Mehra v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2249; (1996) 9 SCC 766; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1104; 1996 (4) CCR 4; 1996 (3) Crimes 85; 1996 JCC 507; JT 1996 (7) SC 6; 1996(5) SCALE 523; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 197; 1996 (5) Supreme 742

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Sexual abuse of minor daughter - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354 and 376 - Respondent no. 2, who is wife of appellant alleged that appellant had molested and committed rape on their minor 3 year old daughter while they were living in USA and another time when they had come to India - Relationship between appellant and respondent were strained - Respondent on her return to India filed complaint against her husband alleging sexual abuse of their minor daughter - Charges were framed against appellant u/s. 354 and 376 - Whether sessions court was justified in framing charges against appellant? - Held, no - Reliance cannot be placed upon testimony of three year old child to prove guilt of appellant - Medical examination of child conducted once in USA as well as in India rule out any possibility of any rape committed with child - Charges framed against petitioner quashed - Appeal allowed.
