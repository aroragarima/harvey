Pothula Rama Rao v Pendyala Venakata Krishna Rao And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 820; (2007) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC 2924; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 30; 2007 (5) ALT 52; 2007 (4) AWC 3715; JT 2007 (10) SC 147; 2007 (4) KLT 307; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 1403; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 5; 2007(10) SCALE 33; 2007 (6) SCJ 147; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Statement Of Fact, Ballot Paper, Contesting Candidate, Representation of People Act, 1950, Decision Of The High Court, Publication Of List Of Contesting Candidates, Stating Material Facts

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(c), 100(1)(d)(i), 100(1)(d)(iv) - Election petition for declaring the election of the returned candidate to be void dismissed as not disclosing any cause of action - Appeal against - Held, nomination of an official candidate set up by a recognized political party (by securing B-Form) is required to be subscribed by one elector as a proposer - But a candidate who is not set up by a recognized political party, requires ten proposers for making his nomination to be valid - There is no irregularity or error in the preparation of list of candidates - Election petition did not disclose any cause of action - Appeals dismissed.
