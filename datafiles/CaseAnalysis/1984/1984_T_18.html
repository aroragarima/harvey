Inder Pal Gupta v The Managing Committee, Model Inter College Thora

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 124; (1984) 3 SCC 384; (1984) SCC (L&S) 555; AIR 1984 SC 1110; 1984 ALJ 543; 1984 (49) FLR 150; 1984 LabIC 900; 1984 (2) LLN 49; 1984(1) SCALE 714; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 752; 1984 (2) SLJ 47; 1984 (2) SLR 422; 1984 UJ 654

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Intermediate Education Act, 1921

Summary: Service - U.P. Intermediate Education Act, 1921, s. 16G - U.P. Intermediate Education Regulations, reigns. 35, 36, 37 and 38 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Termination - during the extended period of his probation by a letter from the Manager of the Managing Committee - Without holding an enquiry under Regns. 35 and 36 - Division Bench of High Court holding that order of termination of probationary Principal was an innocuous one and did not carry any stigma - Held, termination bearing stigma - Termination illegal - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Provisions of s.16-G of 1921 Act and regns. 35 to 38 of the Regulations relating to procedure for termination is same as provided by art. 311 (2) of Constitution - Held, principles which govern such cases of termination should be the same as those underlying art. 311 (2) - Appeal allowed.
