A. P. State Financial Corporation v Official Liquidator

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 456; (2000) 7 SCC 291; AIR 2000 SC 2642; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 75; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 1; JT 2000 (8) SC 587; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 288; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2229

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 529(1), proviso, s. 529 A (Inserted by amending Act of 1985) - Right of corporation to sell property of defaulting industrial concern - Exercise of - Non obstante clause - Interpretation of - Held, non obstante clause would prevail later in point of time - New s. 529 - A confers upon a Company Court a duty - To ensure that workmen's dues are paid in priority to all other debts in accordance with provisions of above section - Right of corporation to sell property cannot be exercised in ignorance of pari passu charge in favour of workmen.
