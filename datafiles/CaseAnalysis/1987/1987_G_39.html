Smt. Aruna Kumari v Government Of Andhra Pradesh And Others.

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28061; (1988) SCC (Cr) 116; (1988) 1 SCC 296; 1988 (25) ACC 15; AIR 1988 SC 227; 1987 (3) Crimes 741; 1988 CRLJ 411; 1988 CrLR(SC) 7; 1988 (33) E.L.T. 4; JT 1987 (4) SC 378; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 973

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Black marketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980, ss. 3 and 14 - Constitution, art. 32 - Detention order - (A) Effect of delay in passing order - (B) Supreme Court cannot go into probative value of evidence before detaining authorityrit petition against detention order - Detenu's statement u/s. 161 CrPC, 1908 - (C) When exercise of powers of revocation of order is open - Need for formal disposal of successive representations based on same grounds rejected earlier - (D) Subjective satisfaction of detaining authority - Whether Court can substitute its own opinion for that of the authority - Held, delay cannot by itself vitiate the decision to detain a person - It cannot be brushed aside for purposes of preventive detention - Not necessary - Held, cannot substitute - Application dismissed.
