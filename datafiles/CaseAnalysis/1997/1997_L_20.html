Chandigarh Administration v Laxman Roller Flour Mills Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1732; (1998) 8 SCC 326; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 4; JT 1998 (9) SC 167

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - Writ jurisdiction of High Court - Writ petition - Allotment of plot to allottee - Allottee transferring plot to respondent company - Construction of building on plot not as per original plan submitted with appellant/administration - Failure on part of respondent to construct building within extended time - Lease granted was ordered by appellant to be cancelled - Writ petition filed before HC against order of cancellation - Prayer was inter alia to quash such order of cancellation - HC granted relief by issuing direction for appellant to issue completion certificate, whether proper? - Held, no - Law is settled that unless allegations are made in writ petition and a relief to that effect is also prayed for in writ petition, HC is not justified in issuing any order in excess of relief prayed for in writ petition - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
