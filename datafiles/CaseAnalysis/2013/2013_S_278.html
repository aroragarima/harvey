Parbin Ali and another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 10; (2013) 2 SCC 81; AIR 2013 SC 542; 2013 (1) JCC 560; JT 2013 (1) SC 383; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 689; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 333; 2013(1) SCALE 113; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Oral dying declaration - Reliability - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302 r/w s. 344 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC - Single Judge dismissed appeal, holding that oral dying declaration was credible - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended oral dying declaration did not inspire confidence - Whether impugned conviction order passed by Trial Court and upheld by HC was justified - Held, PW4 (Doctor) in its report stated that death was caused due to shock and haemorrhage as a result of ante mortem injuries in abdomen caused by sharp weapon and homicidal in nature, opinion was not challenged either before Trial Court or HC - PW1, 2, 3, 5 and 6 (who testified about oral dying declaration) were not at all cross-examined - Whether such a person receiving certain injuries would be in a position to speak or not was not brought out anywhere in evidence - PW1, relative, PW2, wife of deceased, PW3, father and PW5, father-in-law of deceased, deposed that deceased had named 3 accused persons as assailants -Thus, real witnesses to oral dying declaration were PW1, 2, 3 and 5 - They deposed in a categorical manner that by the time they arrived at place of occurrence, deceased was in a fit state of health to speak and make a statement and did made a statement as to who assaulted him - Nothing was suggested to those witnesses about condition of deceased - PW4 was not cross-examined - It could be concluded that deceased was in a conscious state and in a position to speak - In absence of any real discrepancy or material contradiction or omission and additionally non cross-examination of PW4 in that regard made the dying declaration absolutely credible and conviction based on same really could not be faulted - Impugned conviction judgment was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
