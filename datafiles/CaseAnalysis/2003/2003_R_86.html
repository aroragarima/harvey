Ram Ashrey Singh and another v Ram Bux Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 126; (2003) 9 SCC 154; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1036; AIR 2003 SC 1579; 2003 (2) ESC 183; 2003 (96) FLR 995; 2003 LabIC 1210; 2003 (2) LLJ 176; 2003 (3) LLN 853; 2003(2) SCALE 51; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1029; 2003 (2) SLJ 199; 2003 (3) SLR 128; 2003 (2) SLT 78; 2003 (2) Supreme 215; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3359

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - U.P. Intermediate Education Act, 1921 - Dismissal - After preferred before authority under Act after lapse of six years - Dispute relates to entitlement of service/reinstatement and retiral benefits of respondent No 1 - Claim of back wages in view of the limited notice issued - It is a well-settled position in law that on reinstatement there is no automatic entitlement to full back wages - Held, in the case at hand, there was no challenge to the order of termination for six years - Some time was lost because the State and its functionaries on the one hand and the appellant on the other differed on the issue as to who was liable to make the payment in terms of the first direction of the HC - It cannot also be lost sight of that the HC while fixing a sum of Rs 30,000/- to be paid in terms of its order recorded a finding about lack of aptitude of the employee and the likelihood of absence in sincerity of work - Taking all these aspects into consideration, ends of justice would be best served if the appellants are directed to pay a sum of Rs 35,000/- in addition to what has already paid, within a period of four weeks from today - Appeal allowed.
