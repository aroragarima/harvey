Sushila Devi v Ramanandan Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 288; (1976) 1 SCC 361; AIR 1976 SC 177; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 845

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Ex-Parte Order

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure 1905 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Tenancy and Land Laws Koshi Area Act 1951 - Execution - Arrears of rent - Restoration - Installment - Respondent land was sold to A in execution of decree for arrears of rent - A obtained delivery of possession and sold land to appellant - Respondent applied for restoration of land u/s.3 of 1951 Act - Appellant alleged that she spent large sum of money on reclamation - Circle Officer made order for restoration that respondent was to pay compensation to appellant in three annual installments and on default ,applicant would lose benefit of order of restoration - Respondent did not paid installment within allowed time - Respondent appealed to Collector against order of Circle Officer was dismissed for default - Respondent filed revision before Commissioner was allowed and remanded for rehearing - Petition filed by appellant was allowed by HC and order of Commissioner was quashed - Hence instant appeal - Whether Additional Collector was right in dismissing application filed - Held, order granted three annual installments stated clear that on failure of first installment within period mentioned respondent would lose benefit of order of restoration and it was conditional order - Respondent could not ask as right to exclude time spent on proceedings - HC was misdirected in s.5 of 1963 Act as application did not appear to had made for retention of time to pay installments - Hence, Additional Collector was right in dismissing application - Appeal allowed.
