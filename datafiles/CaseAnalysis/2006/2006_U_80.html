S.L. Srinivasa Jute Twine Mills Private Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 48; (2006) 2 SCC 740; 2006 (2) AWC 1150; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 235; JT 2006 (2) SC 397; 2006(2) SCALE 345; 2006 (3) SCJ 316; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 235; 2007 (2) SLR 139

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Employees Provident Funds And Miscellaneous Provisions (Amendment) Act, 1988, Employees Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1960, Employees Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1958

Summary: [A.] Service - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952 , ss. 16, 16(1)(d), 16(d) - Employees' Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1958 - Employees' Provident Funds (Amendment) Act, 1960 - Employees Provident Funds And Miscellaneous Provisions (Amendment) Act, 1988 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Co-operative Societies Act, 1912 - Infancy protection - Effect of amendment on existing rights - Writ of mandamus to declare that Act 10 of 1998 seeking to amend provisions of s.16 shall not apply to petitioners and they would continue to have 'infancy protection' for period of 3 years starting from date of establishment of industry dismissed - Appeal against - Held, unless a different intention appears repeal shall not affect any right, privilege or liability acquired, accrued or incurred under enactment repealed - Appellants shall be entitled to protection as had accrued to them prior to amendment in 1997 for period of 3 years starting from date establishment was set up irrespective of repeal of provision for such infancy protection - Appeals allowed.
