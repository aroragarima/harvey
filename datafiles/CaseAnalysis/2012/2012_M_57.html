Hardeep Kaur v Malkiat Kaur

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 92; (2012) 4 SCC 344; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 80; 2012(3) ALL MR 407; 2012 (91) ALR 863; 2012 (3) ALT 29; 2012 (3) AWC 2220; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 185; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 48; JT 2012 (3) SC 326; 2012 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2322; 2012 (4) MahLJ 498; 2012 (3) MPLJ 339; 2012 (1) OLR 911; 2012 (167) PLR 785; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 412; 2012 (116) RD 154; 2012(3) SCALE 501; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 478

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100, 101 and 103 - Specific Performance - Substantial question of Law - Sustainability - Suit for specific performance of contract was filed by respondent-plaintiff - Appellant-defendant agreed to sell his portion of land and executed agreement of sale, but she did not execute sale deed - Since defendant failed to perform her contract, suit was filed - Trial court decreed suit in favor of plaintiff - First Appellate Court upheld decree of trail court - Second appeal before HC was allowed and decree and judgment of Trial Court was set aside - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reversing decree and judgment of Trial Court without formulating substantial question of law - Held, HC had ignored and overlooked mandatory requirement of second appellate jurisdiction as provided in s. 100 CPC and that vitiated its decision as no substantial question of law had been framed and yet judgment and decree of First Appellate Court had been reversed - Law consistently stated by SC was that formulation of substantial question of law was a sine qua non for exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 100 CPC admitted of no ambiguity and permitted no departure - HC had allowed second appeal and set aside judgment and decree of First Appellate Court without formulating any substantial question of law, which was impermissible and that rendered judgment of HC unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
