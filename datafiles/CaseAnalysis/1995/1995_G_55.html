Laxmi Chand And Ors. v Gram Panchayat, Kararia And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 767; (1996) 7 SCC 218; AIR 1996 SC 523; 1995 (4) CCC 378; 1996 (1) CLT 208; JT 1995 (8) SC 195; 1996 (1) RentLR 133; 1995(6) SCALE 351; 1996 (1) SCJ 506; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Acquisition - Challenged - Notification for acquiring land for construction of the school for public purpose was published - Validity of said notification was challenged - HC dismissed said writ petition - Validity of the acquisition and of the award was challenged by filing a civil suit for a declaration that the land could not be acquired - The Civil Court on a preliminary issue held that the suit was not maintainable - Single Judge upheld the decision of the Civil Court - DB dismissed appeal filed against said order - Thus this special leave petition.

Held, Gram Panchayat had initially expressed about its lack of funds but soon thereafter it came forward to proceed with the acquisition and thus lack of funds with the Gram Panchayat does not divest the power and jurisdiction of the Land Acquisition Officer to proceed with the enquiry u/s. 11 of Act and to make the award there under. The Land Acquisition Officer does not lack jurisdiction or power to make the award. The Civil Court as well as the HC thereby committed no error of law warranting our interference. Petition dismissed.
