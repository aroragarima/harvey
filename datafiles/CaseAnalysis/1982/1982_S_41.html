M.M. Gupta And Ors. Etc. Etc, v State Of Jammu & Kashmir & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 136; (1982) 3 SCC 412; (1983) SCC (L&S) 32; AIR 1982 SC 1579; 1982 (45) FLR 436; 1982 LabIC 1970; 1983 (1) LLN 271; 1982(2) SCALE 913; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 593; 1983 (1) SLJ 82; 1983 (1) SLR 160

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir

Summary: Constitution -Constitution,arts. 50,136,Art.233, 235 and Art.311, - Constitution of J. and K.,arts.109 and 111 - Power of High Court -Appointment of District Judges from cadre of subordinate Judges - Recommendations made by High Court - Not considered by Governor -High Court decline to hear matter -(A) Whether the High Court is the competent authority to appoint District Judges? -Held, the right authority to appoint is Governor and not the HC but at the same time the recommendations made by the HC for the appointment of a District Judge should be accepted by the State Government and the Governor should act on the same - If in any particular case, the State Government for good and weighty reasons find it difficult to accept the recommendations of the HC, the State Government should communicate its views to the HC and the State Government must have complete and effective consultation with the High Court in the matter -(B)Whether the appointments were in conformity with constitutional mandates? -It is well settled that consultation or deliberation is not complete or effective before the parties thereto make their respective points of view known to the other or others and discuss and examine the relative merits of their views. If one party makes a proposal to the other who has a counter proposal in his mind which is not communicated to the proposer, the direction to give effect to the counter proposal without anything more, cannot be said to have been done after consultation. -Held,in the circumstances the appointments were violative of constitutional requirement and invalid -(C) Whether the HC can be compelled to recommend an offiver on the ground of seniority for promotion? - In considering the suitability, no doubt, the seniority plays a very important role. If, on a proper consideration of the performance and merit of an officer for a longer period, the High Court comes to the conclusion that the performance of the officer concerned though for a period longer than any officer junior to him is not satisfactory and meritorious enough, to entitle him to be promoted - Held, the HC cannot be compelled to recommend such an officer only on the ground of the seniority for promotion - Appeal Allowed.
