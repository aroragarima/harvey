Commissioner of Income Tax, Nagpur v Rai Bahadur Jairam Valji and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 114; AIR 1959 SC 291; [1959] 35 ITR 148; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 116; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 110; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 4, 66(1), 66a(2) - Execution of Contract - Capital Expenditure - Capital Receipt - Capital Asset - Respondent entered into an agreement to carry on business and Rs. 2,50,000 was received - Appellant charged Income Tax treating amount received as revenue and respondent contended it was capital expenditure before Tribunal but it ordered in favor of appellant and on Appeal to HC order of Tribunal reversed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs. 2,50,000 received by respondent was revenue and subjected to Tax under Law - Held, yes - Contract dt. 9-5-1940 was simply an agreement to carry on business and in settlement of that contract Rs. 2,50,000 was paid to respondent - Payment was not on account of any capital expenditure incurred by him in execution of contract - Hence, receipt of Rs. 2,50,000 by respondent was revenue receipt and chargeable to tax - Appeal allowed.
