Chandra Prakash and others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 350; (2014) 8 SCC 340; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2248; 2014 CRLJ 2884; JT 2014 (8) SC 97; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2851; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 30; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 274; 2014(6) SCALE 705; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 819

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Approver, Prevention of Damage To Public Property Act, 1984

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 307, 427 - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984, s. 3 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, s. 3 - Explosive Act, 1884, s. 9 - Foreigners Act, 1946, s. 14 - Bomb explosions - Criminal conspiracy - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Sustainability - On 26-1-1996 there was a blast of explosive substances - FIR was registered u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 307 and 427 IPC and u/s. 3 of the 1984 Act and u/s. 3 of the 1908 Act and also u/s. 9B of the 1884 Act were registered and the investigation commenced - On information appellants and others were arrested - Trial Court framed different charges against the four accused persons - Trial Court convicted appellant No. 3 was convicted and sentenced u/s. 14 of the 1946 Act and u/s. 4 of the 1984 Act, u/s. 456 IPC, u/s. 307 r/w s. 120B IPC, u/s. 435 r/w s. 120B IPC, u/s. 9B of the 1884 Act, u/s. 3, 4, 5 of the 1908 Act - Appellant No. 1 was convicted u/s. 9B of the 1884 Act, u/s. 3 r/w s. 6 of the 1908 Act, u/ss. 4 and 5 1908 Act r/w s. 6 of the 1908 Act - Appellant No. 2 and another were convicted u/s. 307 r/w s. 120B IPC, u/s. 435 r/w s. 120B IPC, u/s. 118 r/w s. 120B IPC, u/s. 3 r/w s. 6 of the1908 Act, u/s. 4 r/w s. 6 of the 1908 Act, u/s. 5 r/w s. 6 of the 1908 Act - On appeals filed by appellants, HC dismissed the appeals - Hence instant appeal - Whether prosecution was able to prove beyond reasonable doubt and whether there was criminal conspiracy between the convicts in execution of crime -

Held, s. 2 of the 1908 Act had a deeming provision which stated that explosive substance would include any materials for making any explosive substance - Similarly, s. 4(d) of the 1884 Act has a broader spectrum which include coloured fires or any other substances, whether single chemical compound or a mixture of substances - Apart from ammonium nitrate other articles had been seized - Combination of the same, as per the evidence of the expert witness, was sufficient to prepare a bomb for the purpose of explosion - In addition to the same, huge quantity of ammonium nitrate was seized and it was seized along with other items - Cumulative effect was that the possession of these articles in such a large quantity by accused gives credence to the prosecution version that the possession was conscious and it was intended to be used for the purpose of the blast - Chain of events and the participation of accused persons which had the genesis in the discussion and the meetings, the purchase of ammonium nitrate and other items, carrying of the boxes and all other factors cumulatively show that there was conspiracy - In case of a conspiracy, there could not be any direct evidence - Express agreement between the parties could not be proved - Circumstances proved before, during and after the occurrence have to be considered to decide about the complicity of accused - Such a conspiracy was never hatched in open and, therefore, evaluation of proved circumstances play a vital role in establishing the criminal conspiracy - Opinion expressed by Trial Court as well as by HC that there was conspiracy between the parties to commit the blast on a particular day could not be found fault with - Trial Court, has come to hold that the appellant No. 3 was a resident of Pakistan and he had no valid document to be in India - In his statement u/s. 313 of CrPC, he had not disputed that he was not having passport or visa and he was of Pakistan nationality - Thus, the offence under the said Act was held to be proved - HC concurred with the said view - In HC's considered opinion, the offence under the said Act was proved beyond reasonable doubt - Grounds assailing the judgment of conviction and the order of sentence have no legal substantiality and, accordingly, they were rejected - Charges were proved beyond reasonable doubt, the law rightly visited appellants and, accordingly, we concur with the same - Appeals dismissed.
