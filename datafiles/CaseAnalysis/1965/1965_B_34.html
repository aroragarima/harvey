Binayak Swain v Ramesh Chandra Panigrahi and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 429; AIR 1966 SC 948; 1966 (32) CutLT 609; 1967 (1) LLJ 114; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 24

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 144 - Restitution of properties - Plaintiff suit for claiming suit money on basis of promissory note - Suit decreed exparte by District Judge - High Court reversed decree - Suit remended for rehearing and fresh disposal u/s. 144 - Trial Court dismissed suit - District Court decided in favour of respondent and HC upheld order - Appellant entitled to restitution of properties as decree under which respondent obtained properties set aside by HC in previous proceeding - Held, possession to be restored to appellant and sale of execution case set aside - Appellant will not be entitled to any past mense profits but if possession not given then appellant will be entitled to future mense profits - Appeal partly allowed.
