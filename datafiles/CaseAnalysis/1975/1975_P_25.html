Prabhu Narayan v A. K. Srivastava

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 100; (1975) 3 SCC 788; AIR 1975 SC 968; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Source Of Information

Summary: M.P. High Court Rules (in respect of election petitions), r. 9 - Constitution, art. 225 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83(1), Proviso, 86 and 123(4) - Election - (A) Petition u/s.123 (4) - Charge of publication of pamphlets u/s. 123 (4) - Particulars of printing not given in affidavit - Only evidence regarding printing was relied upon to corroborate publication - Whether petition could be dismissed - And applicability of r. 9 - (B) Proof of circulation of pamphlets - Value of oral evidence in support to printing - Held, r.9 framed by reference to r. 7 found in chap. III regarding affidavits cannot be made use of for this purpose - Where the question of publication and distribution is a matter to be decided on the basis of oral evidence, it is easy to dispose of them by saying that the evidence is of persons interested in the election petitioner, the unsuccessful candidate - Appeal Allowed.
