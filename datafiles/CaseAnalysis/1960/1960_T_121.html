Padam Sen and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 244; AIR 1961 SC 218; 1961 BLJR 103; 1961 CRLJ 322; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 22; 1961 (2) SCJ 79; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 884

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 75 and 151 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 21 and 165A - Appellants offered bribe to public servant - Conviction u/s. 165 A IPC - Appellants went to Commissioner's Office and offered him bribe - Conviction challnged on ground that Commissioner was not a public servant, and therefore they did not commit any offence u/s. 165 - Whether commissioner is a public servant and whether appointment of commissioner u/s. 151 CrPC, 1898 is proper exercise of powers? - Held, appointment of commissioner was not proper in view of fact that account books were not property but merely peice of evidence - Office of commissioner was not pre-existing and hence commissioner appointed by Munsif u/s. 151 CrPC, 1898 not a public servant and hence bribe offered to him does not attract s. 165 A - Appeal allowed.
