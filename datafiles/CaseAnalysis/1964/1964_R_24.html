Ramchandra Rambux v Champabai and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 420; AIR 1965 SC 354; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 814

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Land & Property - Succession Act, 1925, ss. 61 and 63- Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5 and 118 - Execution of will - Testing genuineness of will - Onus on propounder to be proved - (A)Whether court has jurisdiction to look into surrounding circumstances as well as probabilities to asses credibility of a will and a witness to it ? - Held, in order to judge credibility of witnesses, court is not confined only to way in which witnesses have deposed or to demeanour of witnesses, but it is open to it to look into surrounding circumstances as well as probabilities, so that it may be able to form a correct idea of trustworthiness of witnesses - Whether testator did execute a will and if he did, then whether it was duly attested by witnesses, cannot be determined by considering evidence adduced in Court separately from surrounding circumstances which have also been brought out in evidence, or which appear from nature and contents of document itself - (B) Whether mode of proving a will is different from proving other documents? -Further held, mode of proving a will does not ordinarily differ from that of proving any other document except as to special requirement of attestation prescribed in case of a will by s. 63 of Succession Act, 1925 - Where there are suspicious circumstances, onus is on propounder to explain them to satisfaction of court before court accepts will as genuine - In such a case court would naturally expect that all legitimate suspicion should be completely removed before document is accepted as last will of testator - Appeal dismissed.
