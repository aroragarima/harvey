Adi Saiva Sivachariyargal Nala Sangam and others v Government of Tamil Nadu and another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 899; (2016) 2 SCC 725; AIR 2016 SC 209; 2016 (117) ALR 242; 2016 (1) AWC 1018; 2016 (2) CTC 217; JT 2015 (12) SC 332; 2016 (1) MLJ 110; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 591

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Hindu Succession Act, 1956, Operation, Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, Bombay Hindu Places of Public Worship (Entry Authorization) Act, 1956, Madras Temple Entry Authorization Act, Madras Hindu Religious Institutions (Officers and Servants) Service Rules, 1964

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appointment of authority temples - Legality of Govt. order - Justification - Tamil Nadu Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1959, ss. 55, 55(2) - In order to amend and consolidate law relating to administration and governance of Hindu religious and charitable institutions in State, State Legislature has enacted 1959 Act - Further, s. 55 of 1959 Act which provided that in case where office holders or servants of religious institution are required to be filled up on principle of hereditary succession person next in line of succession is entitled to succeed was amended - Hence, by amendment, principle of next in line of succession was abolished - Further, in earlier case, validity of amendment was upheld - Further, Govt. order was issued to effect that, any person who is Hindu and possessing requisite qualification and training can be appointed as Archaka in Hindu temples - Further, ordinance followed Govt. order seeking to further amend s. 55(2) of 1959 Act which stated that no person shall be entitled to appointment to any vacancy referred merely on ground that he is next in line of succession to last holder of office or on ground of any custom or usage - Further, amendment brought by Ordinance was dropped from Amending Act 15 of 2006 - Whether impugned Govt. order passed is legal and valid.

Held, validity of impugned Govt. order would depend on facts of each case of appointment. Further, exclusion of some and inclusion of particular segment or denomination for appointment as Archakas would not violate art. 14 of Constitution so long such inclusion/exclusion is not based on criteria of caste, birth or any other constitutionally unacceptable parameter. So long as prescriptions under particular Agama or Agamas is not contrary to any constitutional mandate, impugned Govt. order dt. 23-5-2006 by its blanket fiat to effect that any person who is Hindu and possessing requisite qualification and training can be appointed as Archaka in Hindu temples is not justified. SC held that determination of claimed custom or usage would be imperative and it is in that light that validity of impugned Govt. order will have to be decided in each case of appointment of Archakas whenever and wherever issue is raised. Seshammal and Others v State of Tamil Nadu, 1972 Indlaw SC 607, followed, SC held that appointments of Archakas will have to be made in accordance with Agamas, subject to their due identification as well as their conformity with Constitutional mandates and principles. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - If appointment of authority is in accordance with Constitution, same cannot be challenged and quashed by Govt. order.
