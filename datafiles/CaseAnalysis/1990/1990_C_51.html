Laminated Packings (P) Ltd v Collector Of Central Excise, Guntur

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 261; (1990) 4 SCC 51; 1990 (30) ECC 36; 1990 (30) ECR 166; 1990 (49) E.L.T. 326; JT 1990 (3) SC 493; 1990(2) SCALE 272; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 630; 1990 (2) UJ 498

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, ss. 35-L(b) and 35 - Whether lamination of duty paid kraft paper with polyethylene resulting in 'polyethylene laminated kraft paper' would amount to 'manufacture' and excisable under law or not? - Lamination, indisputably by well settled principles of excise law, amounts to 'manufacture' - Process of lamination of kraft paper with polyethylene different goods come into being - Laminated kraft paper is distinct, separate and different goods known in market as such from kraft paper - Held, order of CEGAT impugned in this appeal does not contain any error - Appeal dismissed.
