Sunil Rai @ Paua & Ors. v Union Territory, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 390; (2011) 12 SCC 258; AIR 2011 SC 2545; JT 2011 (7) SC 58; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 144; 2011 (2) OLR 259; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 636; 2011(6) SCALE 612; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Challenged - Three appellants were convicted for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC for committing murder and were awarded life sentences - There was no ocular evidence of the commission of the offence and the prosecution case was based entirely on circumstantial evidence - Circumstantial evidence was relied upon by the prosecution and accepted by the Trial Court and the HC to hold the appellants guilty of the offence - (A) Last seen evidence - It was alleged that deceased was last seen being chased by the appellants yelling at him and shouting that they would not spare him - Held, PW-15 did not support the prosecution case on the point of last seen and he did not identify the appellants present in court - He was declared hostile by the prosecution - Also, there was lot of inconsistency in the statement of PW 9 regarding the last seen evidence - Vacillations in the deposition of PW-9 could not be brushed aside as 'minor discrepancy' especially when it was to form the basis for life sentences to three persons - Hence, evidence of last seen could not be relied on - (B) Extra judicial confession - Appellant No. 1 made an extra judicial confession before PW-10 - Held, an extra judicial confessional statement made orally before a person with whom the maker of the confession had no intimate relationship was not a strong piece of evidence and in any event it could only be used for corroboration - In the instant case, alleged confessional statement was oral and it was not recorded in writing - Appellant No. 1 had no personal acquaintance, much less any intimacy with PW-10 - Further, in the confessional statement allegedly made before PW-10 there was an inherent improbability - Hence, it was not possible to rely upon the evidence of PW-10 and extra judicial oral confession by appellant No. 1 - Further, extra judicial confession of appellant No. 1 could not be fastened upon the other two appellants for holding them guilty of murder and the HC was wrong in using the confessional statement of appellant No. 1 as a circumstance against the other two appellants - Prosecution evidence could not satisfactorily establish any of the circumstances for holding the appellants guilty of the offence of murder - Conviction of the appellants was based on completely insufficient evidence and was unsustainable - Judgments and orders of the HC and the Trial Court set aside - Appeals allowed.
