Ashok Kumar Srivastav v National Insurance Company, Limited & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 703; (1998) 4 SCC 361; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1137; AIR 1998 SC 2046; 1998 ALJ 1363; 1998 (33) ALR 386; 1998 (4) AWC 886; 1998 (2) CLT 157; JT 1998 (3) SC 519; 1998 (2) LLJ 699; 1998 (2) LLN 987; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 95; 1998(3) SCALE 265; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1199; 1998 (2) SLR 742; 1998 (4) SLT 772; 1998 (4) Supreme 292

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Specific Relief Act 1963, ss.14(1)(a) and 34 ; Constitution of India, art.226 ; Civil Procedure Code 1908, s.11 - Contractual appointment - Suit for declaration that termination is invalid - Res judicata - Operates as Res judicata in subsequent judicial proceedings - Declaratory relief - For continuation in service - Appellant initially put on probation for period of 12 months but no appointment letter issued to the appellant before expiry of probation period - Held, suit not maintainable under s.14 but can be admitted by Civil Court for declaratory relief under s.34 - Appellant not entitled to declaration that he continues to be in service.
