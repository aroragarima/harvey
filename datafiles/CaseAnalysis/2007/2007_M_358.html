HMT Limited and Another v Mudappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 773; (2007) 9 SCC 768; AIR 2007 SC 1106; JT 2007 (3) SC 112; 2007 (2) KarLJ 145; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 617; 2007(2) SCALE 573; 2007 (4) SCJ 384; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 363

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 28 (1) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Official Secrets Act, 1923 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - In year 1978, Industrial Area Development Board, Karnataka ('Board' for short) acquired land for purpose of establishing a Watch Factory, namely, H.M.T Limited (appellant) herein) - By a communication 20-07-1984, Board called upon owners of land to show cause as to why actual extent of acquired land should not be continued to be occupied by H.M.T - Owners filed a suit - Order passed by Executing Court was challenged by H.M.T by filing a revision which came to be allowed and matter was remanded to Executing Court - Meanwhile, however, H.M.T appears to have requested State Govt. to acquire land and a notification u/s. 28(1) of Act for proposed acquisition of land for public purpose, viz,for developing industry came to be issued on 13-11-1997 which was published in Official Gazette on 11-12-1997 - A writ petition was filed by owners of land - In spite of decree in favour of owners, possession was never returned to successful plaintiffs and they were constrained to take out execution proceedings - Whether High Court has committed an error of law in allowing petition filed by owners and in setting aside a statutory notification issued by State of Karnataka in exercise of power u/s. 28 (1) of Act? - Held, HC was not right in quashing notification issued under Act, particularly, when it was a preliminary notification reflecting intention of State to acquire land for public purpose - When request to return possession of excess land was ignored by appellants, owners naturally approached a Court of law and obtained a decree - It is not in dispute that decree was confirmed in appeal and had become final - Passing of a decree by a competent court is one thing and exercise of statutory power by authority is altogether a different thing - Issuance of preliminary notification after a decree by a court of law would not ipso facto make it vulnerable and exercise of power mala fide - Authorities were right in raising a preliminary objection that petition was premature as by issuance of notification u/s. 28(1) of Act, an intention was declared by State to acquire land for public purpose i.e.,for developing industry - Appeals disposed of.
