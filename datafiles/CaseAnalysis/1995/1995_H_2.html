Kaivelikkalambunhi (Dead) By Lrs. And Others v H. Ganesh Bhandary

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1172; (1995) 5 SCC 444; AIR 1995 SC 2491; 1995 (3) CCC 362; 1996 (1) CLT 383; 1995 (60) DLT 832; 1995 (2) HLR 513; JT 1995 (7) SC 164; 1995(5) SCALE 23; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 35

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Will - Challenged - Order of the HC was challenged on the ground that the will in question was not properly interpreted and that the testator having created an absolute estate in favour of X, son of his direct sister, Y, it was not open to the GC to rely upon the subsequent recital that Schedule 'A' properties which as per the earlier part of the Will had already been bequcathed in favour of X, shall be possessed and enjoyed as 'Tavazhi'.

Held, the rules of interpretation of the 'Will' are different from the rules which govern the interpretation of other documents say. A Will may contain several clauses and the latter clause may be inconsistent with the earlier clause. In such a situation, the last intention of the testator is given effect to and it is on this basis that the latter clause is held to prevail over the earlier clause. No infirmity in the judgment passed by the HC and the special leave petition which has been heard after notice -to the respondent is hereby dismissed. Petition dismissed.
