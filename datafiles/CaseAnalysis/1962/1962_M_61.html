Â Municipal Board, Pushkar v State Transport Authority, Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 461; AIR 1965 SC 458; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 373; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 64 A, 68 and 76 - Prohibition Act - (A) Whether order of Regional Transport Authority had been made u/ss 76 or 68 of Motor Vehicles Act? - Held, in view of this Court's decisions in Ibrahim's Case [ [1953] S C R 2901953 SCR 290], it will be proper to hold that r. 134 was made in exercise of powers u/s. 68 - R 134 in so far as it empowers Regional Transport Authority to fix or alter bus stands is thus a rule made under rule-making authority u/s. 68 - Accordingly, order of Regional Transport Authority fixing new bus stand and discontinuing old should be held to have been made under a rule made u/s 68 and thus liable to revision u/s. 64 A - (B) Whether application was barred by imitation? - Further held, words date of order should not be read as from date of knowledge of order in absence of clear indication to that effect - So long as notification is not made there is in law no effective fixation of a new bus stand or discontinuance of old bus stand - Order fixing a new bus stand at Pushkar and discontinuing old bus stand was in effect made not on 04-12-1959, but on 28-06-1960, when notification about fixation of a new bus stand was published - It is this order, made on 28-06-1960, that was liable to revision and as application for revision was made before that date, in anticipation of notification, plea of limitation raised on behalf of appellant was rightly rejected by Regional Transport Authority - (C) Whether State Transport Authority having already rejected one application in revision against Regional Transport Authority's order changing bus stand could not entertain another application on absolutely same grounds? - Further held, question of merger could only arise if revision was of an order that had come into existence - If even though an application for revision was made before notification but State Transport Authority had considered and disposed of matter after notification was made it would be possible and indeed reasonable to say that application for revision should be deemed, at time when State Transport Authority, dealt with matter, to be one for this completed order and order of Regional Transport Authority merged in revising authority's order - As, however, revising authority's order was also made before notification had been published there was no operative order even by State Transport Authority's order made on 18-02-1960 - The contention that second revision was incompetent, must therefore be rejected - Appeal allowed.
