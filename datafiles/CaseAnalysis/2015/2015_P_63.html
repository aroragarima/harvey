Securities and Exchange Board of India v Prebon Yamane (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 880; 2015 (4) BC 541; 2016 (2) CompLJ 9; JT 2015 (11) SC 372; 2015 (8) MLJ 107; 2015(12) SCALE 280; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Partnership, Stock, Trading, Conversion, Stock Exchange, Membership, Brokers, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stock-Brokers and Sub-Brokers) Regulations, 1992

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, s. 15T - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stock Brokers and Sub-Brokers) Regulations, 1992, regns. 10, 10(1) - Fee Continuity Benefit - Refund of Fee Challenged - 'X' entered into venture with respondent no.1, however, NSE demanded fee for approval of segregation - Further, respondent application for membership in derivatives segment was returned by appellant/SEBI and respondent paid dues under protest with interest accrued - Tribunal held that at time of grating fee continuity to respondent no.1 by appellant, there was no provision for segmental surrender - Further, directed appellant to refund both principal amount as well as interest to respondent no.1 - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, a noting by an officer is an expression of his viewpoint on the subject and do not have the sanction of law to be an effective order. Notings in the file culminate into an executable order, affecting the rights of the parties, only when it reaches the final decision-making authority in the department; gets his approval and the final order is communicated to the person concerned. Hence, a right created under an order of a statutory authority must be communicated to the person concerned so as to confer an enforceable right. Thus, file notings cannot be relied upon with the intent of binding the concerned Authority or Department. Further, cl.4 of sch. III of the Regulations clearly refers to a newly formed entity through conversion from either a sole proprietorship or a partnership to a limited Company, which alone has been bestowed the benefit of continuity. In instant case, the respondent was an entity quite distinct from 'X', with the consequence that it would be bound to pay the fee in accordance with Sch. III, Clause (a) or (b) as the case may be. Hence, respondent is not entitled to any refund there from. Appeal allowed.
