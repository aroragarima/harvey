Bhim Singh and Another v Kan Singh(And Vice Versa)

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 108; (1980) 3 SCC 72; AIR 1980 SC 727; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, s. 82 - Benami transaction - Determination of - Whether transaction in question was of benami nature? - Held, principle governing determination of question whether a transfer is a benami transaction or not may be summed up thus: (1) burden of showing that a transfer is a benami transaction lies on person who asserts that it is such a transaction; (2) if it is proved that purchase money came from a person other than person in whose favour property is transferred, purchase is prima facie assumed to be for benefit of person who supplied purchase money, unless there is evidence to contrary; (3) true character of transaction is governed by intention of person who has contributed purchase money and (4) question as to what his intention was has to be decided on basis of surrounding circumstances, relationship of parties, motives governing their action in bringing about transaction and their subsequent conduct etc. - Transaction under which patta was obtained was not a benami transaction - Appeal No. 626 of 1971 allowed and appeal No. 629 of 1971 dismissed.
