New India Assurance Company Limited v Shri G.N. Sainani

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2399; (1997) 6 SCC 383; AIR 1997 SC 2938; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 426; 1997 (3) CompLJ 380; 1997 (3) CPJ 1; 1997 (5) CTJ 569; 1997(4) SCALE 531; 1997 (2) SCJ 427; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 750; 1997 (6) Supreme 338

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Marine Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 24-A - Claim on insurance policy denied - Determination of period of limitation - Held, time would begin to run from date of denial/repudiation of policy by insurer - Complaint by assignee under Act for deficiency in service, is not maintainable - He cannot be said to be beneficiary of any service and is not a 'consumer' - Complaint is not maintainable - He only has right to recover loss on account of short landing of goods from insurer by filing civil suit - Appeal allowed.
