Karanpura Development Company, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 440; AIR 1962 SC 429; [1962] 44 ITR 362; 1962 (2) SCJ 460; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Business profits tax - Court of Wards representing the proprietor of the Ramgarh Estate granted a prospecting licence to the assessing company of an area of coal-bearing lands described as the Karanpura Coal Fields - The licence was for 12 years but was renewable for another term of 12 years - Licence reserved to the licensee the right to take coal mining leases of the Karanpura Coal Fields or any part thereof - Licence was transferable - Appellant assigned rights under the prospecting licence to the assessee Company - Assessee Company then acquired from time to time diverse coal mining leases over areas aggregating 20,000 standard bighas - Assessee Company developed these coal fields by providing means of communication, and then subleased them to collieries and other companies - In the head leases which the assessee Company had obtained, the term was 999 years - In the sub-leases the term was the balance of the period minus 2 days - Apart from obtaining head leases, developing the coal fields and subleasing its rights, the assessee Company admittedly did not do any business - As a condition of the acquisition of the head leases, the assessee Company had paid salami at the rate of Rs. 40 per standard bigha, and had agreed to pay royalty at certain rates - From the sub-lessees, the assessee Company charged salami at the rate of Rs. 400 per standard bigha and royalties at higher rates - For the A/Y, 1949-50, the assessee Company realised Rs. 19,14,035 as salami for the mining subleases granted in the relevant account year, and in the A/Y, 1950-51, it realised Rs. 3,96,000 on the same account - Income of assessee Company arising from the enhanced royalties would be taxable - Assessee Company's contention that the excess amount realised by way of increased salami was on capital account and could neither be included in the assessable income for purposes of income-tax nor in the profits for purposes of business profits tax - Whether the excess amount realised by way of increased salami was liable to be included in the assessable income for purpose of income tax or in the profits for purpose of business profits tax - Held, in acquiring the head leases and in granting the sub-leases, the assessee Company was carrying on a business within its Memorandum of Association and the increased salami received from the subleases represented profits of that business liable to be included in the assessable income for income-tax purposes and in the profits, for purposes of the business profits tax - Appeal dismissed.
