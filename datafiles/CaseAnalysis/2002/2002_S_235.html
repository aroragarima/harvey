Sekar Alias Raja Sekharan v State Represented By Inspector of Police, T. N

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1836; (2002) 8 SCC 354; (2003) SCC (Cr) 16; AIR 2002 SC 3667; AIR 2002 SCW 4315; 2002 (4) CCR 169; 2003 (1) CGLJ 58; 2002 (4) Crimes 443; 2003 (1) CRJ 706; 2003 CRLJ 53; 2002 (3) JCC 2064; JT 2002 (8) SC 21; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 477; 2002(7) SCALE 342; 2002 (3) SCJ 633; 2002 (5) SLT 621; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 113; 2002 (7) Supreme 124; 2003 (1) UC 474

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 96 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Trial Court placing reliance on evidence of PWs1, 2 and 3 held that accused was responsible for death of deceased - Plea of accused that assaults were made in exercise of right of private defence was not accepted in view of fact that even after deceased had fallen down accused inflicted further blow on neck - In appeal HC did not find any merit in submissions made to effect that this was case which was clearly covered by accused's exercise of right of private defence - Having rejected this stand of accused, HC analysed evidence and concluded that conviction was justified - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, altercation between accused and deceased there was no pre-meditation and assaults were given by accused during course of sudden quarrel - This brought case within ambit of Exception IV to s. 300 IPC - Even otherwise, it appeared to be case of accused exceeding right of private defence - Appropriate conviction would, be u/s. 304 Part I, IPC and custodial sentence of 10 years - Court further held that a spur of moment loss of faculties led to unfortunate occurrence therefore sentence was reduced to 10 years keeping in view the economic condition of the accused - Appeal disposed of.
