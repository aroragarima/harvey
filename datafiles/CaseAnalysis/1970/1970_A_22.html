Lakshmiratan Cotton Mills Co. Ltd v Aluminium Corporation Of India Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 608; (1971) 1 SCC 67; AIR 1971 SC 1482; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 19 - Whether letter (Ex 1) amounted to an acknowledgement extending period of limitation? - Held, s. 19(1) of Limitation Act, 1908 provides that where, before expiration of period prescribed for a suit in respect of any property or right, an acknowledgement of liability in respect of such property or right has been made in writing signed by party against whom such property or right is claimed, a fresh period of limitation shall be computed from time when acknowledgement was so signed - Statement on which plea of acknowledgement is founded must relate to a subsisting liability as section requires that it must be made before expiration of period prescribed under Act - An acknowledgment is an admission by writer that there is a debt owing by him, either to receiver of letter or to some other person on whose behalf letter is received but it is not enough that he refers to a debt as being due from somebody - An agreement of reference to arbitration containing an unqualified admission that whoever on account should be proved to be debtor would pay to other has been held to amount to an acknowledgement - Since letter (Ex 1) called for confirmation of amount of Rs. 1, 07, 447 as being balance due to appellant company and as appellant company failed to confirm it admission of liability was conditional, and therefore cannot operate as an acknowledgment - Appeal allowed.
