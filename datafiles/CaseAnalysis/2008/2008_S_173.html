Jaladi Suguna (Deceased) Through Lrs. v Satya Sai Central Trust And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2621; (2008) 8 SCC 521; AIR 2008 SC 2866; 2008 (6) ALD(SC) 8; 2008 (4) AWC 3361; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 68; 2008(8) SCALE 709; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 734; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 492

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: (A) Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, - 'Legal representative' - Procedure - Determination of - Held, filing an application to bring the LRs on record, does not amount to bringing the LRs on record - When an LR application is filed, the court should consider it and decide whether the persons named therein as the LRs, should be brought on record to represent the estate of the deceased - Until such decision by the court, the persons claiming to be the LRs have no right to represent the estate of the deceased, nor prosecute or defend the case - If there is a dispute as to who is the legal representative, a decision should be rendered on such dispute - Only when the question of legal representative is determined by the court and such legal representative is brought on record, it can be said that the estate of the deceased is represented - Appeal allowed

(B) Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 r. 4 and 5 - 'Legal representative' - Determination of - 'S' (deceased) filed a suit seeking a declaration that the registered gift deed executed by her in favour of the Trust in respect of the suit property was null and void and for a consequential injunction restraining the said Trust from interfering with her rights - Suit was decreed - Trust filed appeal - 'S' who was the first respondent in the said appeal, died during the pendency of the appeal - Trust filed an application to bring her husband on record, as her LR - Appellants, who were the nieces and nephews of deceased also filed an application seeking leave to come on record as her LRs - Thus, there was a dispute as to who were the LRs of the deceased - Therefore, the HC directed the Trial Court, under the proviso to r. 5 of O. 22 of CPC to try the said question and submit its finding - Trial Court submitted a report recording a finding that the deceased had executed two wills in favour of the appellants under which the suit property was bequeathed to them - However, HC without deciding the question as to who are the LRs of the deceased, as required by O. 22 r. 5 of CPC proceeded to hear the main appeal itself as also the said two LR applications and rendered its Judgment - HC upheld the validity of the gift deed, allowed the appeal of the Trust, and set aside the decree of the Trial Court - Present appeal - Whether procedure adopted by the HC in hearing the appeal without bringing the LRs on record and deciding the appeal on merits first and thereafter deciding the issue relating to legal representative justified? - Held, when a respondent in an appeal dies, and the right to sue survives, the LRs of the deceased respondent have to be brought on record before the court can proceed further in the appeal - Where the respondent-plaintiff who has succeeded in a suit, dies during the pendency of the appeal, any judgment rendered on hearing the appeal filed by the defendant, without bringing the LRs of the deceased respondent-plaintiff on record, will be a nullity - In the appeal before the HC, the first respondent was the contesting respondent - When first respondent in the appeal died, the right to prosecute the appeal survived against her estate - Therefore it was necessary to bring the legal representative/s of the deceased respondent on record to proceed with the appeal - Entire judgment of HC is a nullity and inoperative - HC directed to first decide the dispute between the husband of the deceased on the one hand, and her nieces and nephews on the other and after such determination, the person/s determined to be the person/s entitled to represent the estate of the deceased shall be brought on record as the LRs of the deceased - Thereafter, the appeal shall be heard on merits and disposed of in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
