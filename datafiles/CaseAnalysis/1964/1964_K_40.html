Bombay Steel Rolling Mills, Limited, and Others v Khemchand Rajkumar Steel Mills and Pahorpur Yards Labour Union, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 26; 1964 (9) FLR 1; 1964 (2) LLJ 120

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 19(2) - Payment of subsistence allowance - Whether workmen entitled to subsistence allowance and/or reinstatement in their services in view of agreements D/- 4-05-1957? - Held, settlement of 04-05-1957 entitled workmen to reinstatement on their final acquittal in criminal cases - If after such acquittal workmen preferred to remain away and did not ask for work, it is neither fair nor reasonable to ask management to comply with demand for reinstatement made months and months after such acquittal - During this long period, employer employed new workmen - Therefore, reinstatement order by tribunal cannot be sustained - Order for payment to these workmen of suspension allowance till date of their reinstatement must be set aside - Workmen entitled to allowance at 50 per cent of their basic wage from 04-05-1957 till date of their acquittal - Appeal partly allowed.
