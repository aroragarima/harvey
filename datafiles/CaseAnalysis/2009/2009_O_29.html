S. Suresh v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2083; (2010) 13 SCC 777; 2010 ACJ 487; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 368; 2010(2) ALL MR 467; 2010 (1) AWC 65; 2010 (124) FLR 1; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 214; JT 2009 (14) SC 359; 2010 (2) KarLJ 650; 2010 (1) LLJ 839; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 418; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 666; 2009(14) SCALE 48; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 990

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 2(1)(l) - Total disablement - Appellant-lorry driver met with an accident while driving suffered serious injuries, although he survived but ultimately his right leg had to be completely amputated just below the knee - Appellant filed petition for adequate compensation - Doctor certified that claimant suffered 93% permanent disability in his right leg - Accepting the evidence of the doctor, the Commissioner came to the conclusion that the claimant's right leg up to the knee having been amputated, he has suffered a loss of 100% of his earning capacity as a driver and awarded compensation of Rs. 5,20,584/- with interest @ 12% per annum - On appeal, HC reduced the compensation by 50% - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, on account of amputation of right leg below knee, appellant rendered unfit for work of a driver and lost 100% of his earning capacity - Order of HC set aside - Commissioner's order restored - Appeal allowed.
