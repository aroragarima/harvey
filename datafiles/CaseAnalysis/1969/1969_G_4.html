Kedar Nath Lal and Another v Ganesh Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 100; (1969) 2 SCC 787; AIR 1970 SC 1717; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Lis Pendens

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 52 and 60 - Doctrine of lis pendens - Applicability of - Whether in a proceeding in a money suit doctrine of lis pendens could be applicable? - Held, proceeding was to get a mortgage award, equivalent of a mortgage decree - Court made a mistake and treated it as a proceeding for a money decree - When court corrected its order, mortgage award related back to petition as made and whole of proceeding must be treated as covered by doctrine - Hence, doctrine is applicable at any rate on this suggested ground - Appeal allowed.
