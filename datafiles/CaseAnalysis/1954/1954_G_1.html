Â State of Madhya Pradesh v G. C. Mandawar

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1954 SC 493; 1954 (2) LLJ 673; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 51; 1954 SCJ 503; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether a resolution of Govt. of Central Provinces and Berar, now Madhya Pradesh, dated 16-09-1948, fixing a scale of dearness allowance to be paid to its servants is repugnant to art. 14 of Constitution? - Art. 14 does not authorise striking down of a law of one State on ground that in contrast with a law of another State on same subject its provisions are discriminatory - Nor does it contemplate a law of Center or of state dealing with similar subjects being held to be unconstitutional by a process of comparative study of provisions of two enactments - Held, sources of authority for two statutes being different, art. 14 can have no application - Scale of dearness allowance recommended by Commission and sanctioned by Central Govt. can furnish no ground for holding that scale of dearness allowance recommended by Committee and adopted by appellant is repugnant to art. 14 - Appeal allowed.
