Harihar Prasad Etc v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Subimal Chandra Roy, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 847; (1972) 3 SCC 89; (1972) SCC (Cr) 409; 1972 CRLJ 707

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 5(2) - Criminal conspiracy - Cheating involved Govt. funds - Official duties - Convicted and sentenced - Three appellants were officers of Govt. of State of Bihar and other three appellants were contractors - It was alleged that they criminally conspired, committed criminal breach of trust and had cheated in respect of large amounts of Govt. money earmarked for Project known as 'Mahuadar Development Block Pilot Project' - Offences occurred between February 1957 and March 1958 - Govt. had sponsored scheme of intensive multi-purpose development for welfare of scheduled tribes - Outline of scheme was communicated - For execution of programme allotments of funds were made by State Govt. from time to time - Block Advisory Committee meeting was held but no decision was taken - Respondents stated that schemes were illegally and dishonestly made by appellants at cost of Govt. - Assistant Development Commissioner noted certain irregularities, books were audited - Auditors' report revealed series of serious irregularities in connection with implementation made and ultimately first information report was filed - Investigation was conducted - Trial was concluded - It was found that transactions were entered into behind back of Deputy Commissioner, thus he was kept in dark - Appellants strongly indicated that all expenditure incurred had been brought to knowledge of the Deputy Commissioner - However HC held contrary view - HC noted that amended schematic programme was prepared in absence of Deputy Commissioner from his headquarters - Appellants were convicted and sentenced by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether acts complained of were directly concerned with official duties of three appellants who were public servants - Held, appellants acts were deliberate and reflected mind of conspirators - Revised schematic programme was not sent to Deputy Commissioner - Monthly reports failed to give higher authorities true picture of manner in which expenditures had been incurred at block level - On question of urgency it was pointed out that although Govt. wanted expeditious implementation of programme, it had never suggested that Rules should be abandoned - Appellant had no powers either to call for tenders or accept them - Considerable part of expenditure was totally unauthorised because no decision was actually taken at Block Advisory Committee meeting - HC made correct observations while summing up position of misappropriation - Offences of criminal conspiracy punishable u/s. 120B read with s. 409 of IPC and also s. 5(2) of the Act were not of nature mentioned in s. 197 of CrPC - Public servant, while discharging his official duties, were not permitted to enter into criminal conspiracy or to indulge in criminal misconduct - Want of sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC was therefore no bar - Court rejected appellants submissions - Sentences for each appellant along with fines was stated - Appeals dismissed.
