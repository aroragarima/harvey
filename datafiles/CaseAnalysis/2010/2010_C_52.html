Ittianam and others v Cherichi alias Padmini

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 606; (2010) 8 SCC 612; AIR 2010 SC 2994; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 51; 2010 (82) ALR 524; 2011 (1) AWC 187; 2010 (5) CTC 494; JT 2010 (7) SC 590; 2010 (3) KLT 447; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 1098; 2010 (4) MPLJ 647; 2011 (1) OLR 75; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 158; 2010 (111) RD 505; 2010(7) SCALE 495; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1135

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Contrary Intention, English Wills Act, 1837

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 90 and 278 - Dispute over properties bequeathed by will - In the will of 'X', seven items of properties were bequeathed out of which items 1 to 3 were given to 'Y', daughter of the testator by his first wife - Items 4 to 7 of the properties were previously owned in equal moieties by 'A' and 'B', the second wife of 'X' - 'B' sold her share to 'X' on 2.5.67 but the sale deed was registered on 8.5.67, the same day when the Will was registered - After the death of 'A', his wife and children (appellant nos. 1 to 5) jointly applied u/s. 278 of Act for grant of Letters of Administration of the Will of the testator - District Judge granted the letters of administration to the appellant's in respect of all the items of property in the Will - On appeal, HC upheld the genuineness of the Will but modified the grant of letters of administration only to items 1 to 3 - HC declined to grant the letters of administration in respect of items 4 to 7 on the ground that on the date of the Will the testator's title to half of the property, namely over item Nos. 4 to 7 was not perfected - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, as per s. 90 of the Act, in the absence of a contrary intention in the Will, the description of the properties in the Will shall be deemed to refer to and include the property answering that description at the death of the testator - In present case, Will does not contain any contrary intention in respect of the bequest of items 4 to 7 of the properties - Much before his death, testator acquired full title over items 4 to 7 of the property - Impugned order of HC set aside and order of the District Court restored - Appeal allowed.
