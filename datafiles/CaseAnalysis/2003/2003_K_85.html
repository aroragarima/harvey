State of Punjab v Karnail Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 617; (2003) 11 SCC 271; (2004) SCC (Cr) 135; AIR 2003 SC 3609; 2003 (3) CCR 261; 2003 (3) Crimes 292; 2004 (1) CRJ 585; 2003 CRLJ 3892; 2003 (3) JCC 1313; JT 2003 (7) SC 543; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 156; 2003(6) SCALE 434; 2003 (1) SCW 4065; 2003 (5) SLT 221; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 593; 2003 (5) Supreme 508

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 313, 394 - Accused acquitted by HC on ground of unexplained delay in lodging FIR and dispatch of same to concerned Magistrate and unusual conduct of witnesses who were brothers of deceased - Held, considering distance between place of occurrence, police chowk, police station and court of Magistrate, it cannot be said that there was any unexplained delay so far as registration of FIR and dispatch to Magistrate are concerned - Merely because information was not lodged at police chowk or police station in night, that cannot be a suspicious circumstance in view of factual position noted by Trial Court - Further held, ground that witnesses being close relatives and consequently being partisan witnesses, should not be relied upon - Keeping in view legal principles and factual scenario, inevitable conclusion is that HC was not justified in directing acquittal of accused persons.
