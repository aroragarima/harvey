Â State of Kerala and others v Maharashtra Distilleries Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 408; (2005) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2005 SC 2594; 122 ECR 289; JT 2005 (5) SC 427; 2005(5) SCALE 2; 2005 (141) STC 358; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Sale Price, Levy Of Tax, Foreign Liquor Rules

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - Excise - Kerala Abkari Act, ss. 12, 14, 17, 18 - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, ss. 2(xxvii), 5(1), 5(2C) - Finance Act, 2001 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311 - Abkari Act, ss. 17, 24, 6, 7 - Foreign Liquor Rules - Whether incidence of excise duty, having regard to provision of Kerala Abkari Act on Indian made Foreign Liquor and relevant Rules, falls upon manufacturer/distiller such as respondents herein and therefore includable in their turnover for purpose of levy of turnover tax, or whether incidence of excise duty falls on Kerala State Beverages (Manufacturing and Marketing) Corporation (KSBC) Limited, a Govt. company which alone is liable to pay excise duty on Indian Made Foreign Liquor? - Held, manufacturers are liable to include in their turnover amount of duty paid to them by KSBC and included in consideration for sale of IMFL to Corporation with effect from January 5, 1999 and pay turnover tax accordingly - Appeals disposed of.
