Gaddam Ramakrishnareddy and others v Gaddam Rami Reddy and another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 754; (2010) 9 SCC 602; AIR 2011 SC 179; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 33; 2011(1) ALL MR 448; 2011 (84) ALR 272; 2011 (2) AWC 1620; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 348; JT 2010 (10) SC 125; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 564; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 568; 2011 (112) RD 264; 2010(9) SCALE 402; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 656

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1) and 14(2) - 'A' executed and registered a Deed of Gift giving a limited right in his share of the properties to his wife, 'B' that she has a right of enjoyment of the properties during her lifetime, without right of alienation, and the remainder was vested in 'C' - It was stipulated that after 'B' death, the properties would devolve on 'C' - 'B' died in or about 1957 - Before her death 'B' executed a Deed of Relinquishment and also executed two sale deeds in favour of defendants - 'C' filed petition for permanent injunction against said defendants from interfering with his possession in the said properties which was decreed in favour of 'C', holding that the sale deeds executed by 'B' in favour of the defendants were invalid, inasmuch as, 'B' had no right of alienation as she did not get an absolute right in the properties - Defendants filed appeal against said order submitting that impugned properties had been given to 'B' for life in lieu of her maintenance and that the same developed into an absolute estate u/s. 14(1) of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - HC dismissed said appeals on the ground of res judicata in view of the judgment passed earlier in O.S.No.17 of 1975 - Hence, present petitions - Whether the life-estate created by 'A' in favour of his wife, 'B', by the Deed of Gift could be said to be an interest in lieu of maintenance which subsequently became enlarged into a full-fledged right of ownership u/s. 14(1) of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956? - Held, life-estate created by 'A' in favour of 'B' was not in lieu of her maintenance as she was already managing the impugned properties and in no uncertain terms it was the 'A's desire that the said properties should ultimately go to his son the 'C' - Once that is established, the immediate fallout is that 'B's rights in the properties came to be governed by s. 14(2) of the Hindu Succession Act, 1956, and her right does not blossom into an absolute estate as contemplated u/s. 14(1) of Act - Hence, both the Courts below have correctly decided that 'B' did not acquire any right beyond a life-estate in the suit properties and on her death, the said properties devolved on the 'C' in terms of the Deed of gift executed by 'A' - Petitions dismissed.
