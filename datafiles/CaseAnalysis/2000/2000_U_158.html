S. Ramanathan v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2735; (2001) 2 SCC 118; (2001) SCC (L&S) 340; AIR 2000 SCW 4549; 2001 (88) FLR 734; JT 2001 (1) SC 268; 2000(8) SCALE 102; 2001 (2) SLJ 293; 2000 (7) SLR 585; 2001 (1) SLR 616; 2001 (1) SLT 331; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 450; 2000 (8) Supreme 608; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 613

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Indian Police Service cadre Rules, 1954, r. 9 (1) - Indian Police Service Promotion Regulations, 1955 - All India Service Act, 1951, s. 3(1) - Competent authorities - Promotion - Triennial Review - Entitlement - Appellants were State Police Service Officers, who had been promoted to Indian Police Service - Appellants had sole grievance that inaction on part of Competent Authority to had triennial review, in accordance with law and consequential directions for reconsideration of appellants for promotion to post of Indian Police Service from anterior date - Aggrieved, appellants filed petition before Tribunal which came to conclusion that there had not been triennial review for re-determination of cadre strength, in accordance with statutory provisions, but refused to issue mandamus - Hence, instant appeal - Whether infraction on part of appropriate authority, in matter of discharge of its obligation in relation to determination of cadre, entitles employee to obtain mandamus from Court was challenged - Held, Devendra Narayan Singh and Ors. Vs. State of Bihar and Ors., 1996 Indlaw SC 1780, relied on - Court had been insisting performance of duty upon authority under statute quite meticulously and on admitted position that process of determining cadre strength was initiated in year 1989 and it was finalised in year 1991 - There was no rhyme and reason why respondents would not be directed to reconsider question on basis of altered strength of cadre, as if it was so altered in year 1989 when process of determination of cadre strength was initiated - Therefore, directed Union Govt. and State Govt. to reconsider question of promotion of State Cadre Officers to Indian Police Service on basis of re-determined strength of cadre, treating same to be in year 1989 and re-consideration relief would be available to any of appellants for promotion to IPS on basis of quota available to them in cadre, same should be given to them - Hence, set aside impugned orders of Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
