Binod Singh v District Magistrate Dhanbad Bihar And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 784; (1986) 4 SCC 416; (1986) SCC (Cr) 490; AIR 1986 SC 2090; 1986 (3) Crimes 284; 1986 CRLJ 1959; 1986 CrLR(SC) 451; JT 1986 SC 561; 1986(2) SCALE 531; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 905

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3(2) -Detention order challenged - Order of detention served on detenu when he was in jail in respect of a murder case - No indication of his imminent release and necessity to put him back in jail - Whether continued detention of detenu under Act of 1980 was justified ? - In this case there were grounds for passing of detention order but after that detenu has surrendered for whatever reasons, therefore order of detention though justified when it was passed but, at time of service of order there was not proper consideration of fact that detenu was in custody or that there was any real danger of his release - Held, in facts and circumstances of this case continued detention of detenu under Act was not justified - Order accordingly.
