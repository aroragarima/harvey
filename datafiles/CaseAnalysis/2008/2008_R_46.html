Raghunath Anant Govilkar v State Of Maharashtra And Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 158; (2008) 11 SCC 289; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1486; 2008 CRLJ 2054; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1042; 2008(2) SCALE 303; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Discharge Of Duty, Armed Forces, Old Act, Indian Penal Code, 1986

Summary: Maharashtra Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1982, s. 27; Indian Penal Code, 1986 - Application for discharge in terms of s. 228 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 on the ground that sanction was necessary and proceedings could not have been initiated after his retirement in view of r. 27, dismissed - Held, an accused facing prosecution for offences under the old Act or new Act cannot claim any immunity on the ground of want of sanction, if he ceased to be a public servant on the date when the court took cognizance of the said offences - But the position is different in cases where Section 197 of the Code has application - Though the view of High Court about the need for sanction in the case of retired Government servant was erroneous, in view of the finding that the charged offences are not relatable to any official duty, the appeal fails - Appeal dismissed.
