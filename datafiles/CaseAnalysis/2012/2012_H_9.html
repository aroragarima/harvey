R.C. Chandel v High Court Of M.P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 445; (2012) 8 SCC 58; AIR 2012 SC 2962; 2012 (5) AWC 4367; 2012 (3) JabLJ 245; 2013 (1) MahLJ 503; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 78; 2012(5) M.P.H.T. 1; 2013 (1) MPLJ 261; 2013 (1) RLW 346; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 205; 2012 (5) SLR 578; 2012 (4) WLN(SC) 145

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1976, Madhya Pradesh District and Sessions Judges (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1964, Madhya Pradesh Higher Judicial Service (Recruitment and Service Conditions) Rules, 1994

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Compulsory retirement - Justifiability - Appellant, who was working on the post of District and Sessions Judge was compulsorily retired from the service in the public interest by the Govt. of Madhya Pradesh - On appeal DB concurred with view of Govt. - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of compulsory retirement was sustainable -

Held, conduct of the appellant in involving an M.P. and the Ministry of Law, Justice and Company Affairs, in a matter of HC concerning an administrative review petition filed by him for expunging adverse remarks in ACRs of 1993 and 1994 was most reprehensible and highly unbecoming of a judicial officer - Appellant's conduct had tarnished the image of the judiciary and he disentitled himself from continuation in judicial service on that count alone - A Judge was expected not to be influenced by any external pressure and appellant was also supposed not to exert any influence on others in any administrative or judicial matter - Secondly and still worst, the appellant had an audacity to set up a plea in the rejoinder that he never made any representation to M.P. for any purpose whatsoever - But for the appellant's approaching M.P and his request for help, M.P would have never written the letter quoted above to the then Minister of State for Law, Justice and Company Affairs - On instant ground also appellant's writ petition was liable to be dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
