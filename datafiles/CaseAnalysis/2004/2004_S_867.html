Sardar Harcharan Singh Brar v Sukh Darshan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 907; (2004) 11 SCC 196; 2005 (58) ALR 492; 2005 (5) AWC 4160; JT 2004 (9) SC 269; 2005 (139) PLR 394; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 749; 2004(9) SCALE 105; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 682

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 80A, 81 and 123(7) - Election petition - Challenging election of respondent - Ground, corrupt practice - Election of respondent challenged on ground that nomination of respondent was improperly accepted - Whether election petition could have been held liable to be dismissed at threshold and without being tried and heard on merits? - Held, deficiency in particulars could not have been a ground for dismissing petition at threshold - It is only non-supply of particulars though ordered by Court which could have led to either striking off of pleadings or refusal to try related instances of alleged corrupt practice - Appeal allowed.
