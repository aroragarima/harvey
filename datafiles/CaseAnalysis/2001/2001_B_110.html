Naresh Chandra Agarwal v Bank of Baroda and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20294; (2001) 3 SCC 163; AIR 2001 SC 1253; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 102; 2001 ALJ 581; 2001 (1) AllCJ 547; 2001 (42) ALR 789; 2001 (1) AWC 821; JT 2001 (2) SC 392; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 76; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 80; 2001 (92) RD 333; 2001(1) SCALE 708; 2001 (2) SLT 1; 2001 (1) Supreme 629

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 151, O. 9, r. 13 - Application u/O. 9, r. 13, r/w s. 151 CPC before ADJ (SC and ST Act), for setting aside ex parte judgment and decree - Dismissed - Appeal also dismissed by High Court - Whether decree passed without notice to impleaded as a party to suit will be binding? - Held, no - Appellant's share in estate of his father is bound to be affected by execution of decree, appellant ought to have been served with notice of, application for substitution, therefore application to set aside ex parte to be granted - Appeal allowed.
