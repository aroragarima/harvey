National Insurance Company v Prembai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 281; (2005) 6 SCC 172; (2005) SCC (L&S) 819; AIR 2005 SC 2337; 2005 (60) ALR 159; 2005 (3) AWC 2126; 2006 (1) CalLT(SC) 31; [2005] 125 Comp Cas 86; JT 2005 (4) SC 399; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 794; 2005 (2) LLJ 1109; 2005 (3) LLN 83; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 497; 2005(4) SCALE 185; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 3 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 146(1), 147, 147(1), 147(1)(b), 149, 166, 167, 173 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 95(2) - Claim petition - Whether insurance company is liable to satisfy entire award made in favour of claimants or its liability is restricted to that which is prescribed under Workmen's Act? - Held, insurance policy taken by owner contained a cl that it was a policy for 'Act Liability' only - This being nature of policy liability of appellant would be restricted to that arising under Workmen's Act - Owners of vehicle would be liable to satisfy remaining portion of award -Appeal disposed of.
