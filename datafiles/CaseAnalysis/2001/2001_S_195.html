Supreme Court of India

19 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 132; (2002) 1 SCC 134; AIR 2001 SC 2920; AIR 2001 SCW 4377; 2001 (45) ALR 691; 2002 (1) AWC 97; JT 2001 (9) SC 145; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 134; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 625; 2001(7) SCALE 403; 2001 (7) SLT 604; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 467; 2001 (7) Supreme 812; 2002 (1) UC 100

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Under Section 100 of the Code of Civil Procedure the High Court assumes jurisdiction to entertain the second appeal only if the case involves a substantial question of law and not merely a question of fact. Further, even if time is not the essence of the contract, the court may infer that the plaintiffs must perform his part of the contract within a reasonable time.
