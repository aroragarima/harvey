Bindumati Bai v Narbada Prasad

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 355; (1976) 4 SCC 626; AIR 1977 SC 394; 1977 HLR 611; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 988; 1976 UJ 923

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - (A) Whether appellant relinquished her right of survivorship in property which fell to share of Shantibai as a result of deed of partition dated January 13, 1955? - Held, every shareholder may get property fallen to her share, mutated and may take possession thereof and thus may become absolute owner thereof - Every shareholder may get her name separately mutated in Patwari's papers - Shareholder may sell it - If other shareholder claim it, it will be contrary to law By taking our respective share from entire property in partition we become separate from entire property - In face of recitals in deed of partition and admissions made by appellant in witness box, there is no reason whatsoever to disturb finding of courts below that appellant had relinquished her right of survivorship in property which fell to share of Shantibai - (B) Whether even though Shantibai became entitled to dispose of during her life time property which fell to her share as a result of deed of partition, she could not bequeath same by means of a will? - Held, power of Shantibai to make a will in respect of property in dispute was co-extensive with her power to transfer it inter vivos - Question as to what effect will would have on right of male reversioner, if any, of Laxmi Dayal need not be gone into in this case - So far as Bindumati appellant is concerned, there is no doubt that in light of arrangement contained in deed of partition dated January 13, 1955, she cannot resist claim of plaintiff-respondent who is a legatee under will of Shantibai - To hold otherwise would be tantamount to permitting appellant to assert her right of survivorship in property which fell as a result of partition to share of Shantibai even though appellant has relinquished such right of survivorship - Appeal dismissed.
