Food Corporation of India and others v Bhanu Lodh and others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 165; (2005) SCC (L&S) 433; (2005) 3 SCC 618; AIR 2005 SC 2775; 2005 (2) AWC 1296; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 96; 2005 (2) CLR 51; 2005 (3) ESC 297; 2005 (105) FLR 1058; JT 2005 (2) SC 562; 2005 (2) LLN 643; 2005(2) SCALE 347; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 350; 2005 (2) SLR 747; 2005 (3) Supreme 310; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1842

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Food Corporations Act, 1964, Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971

Summary: Service - Food Corporations Act, 1964 - Direct Recruitment - Posts of Joint Managers / Deputy Managers in Corporation - Govt of India, Ministry of Food, issued a directive, purported in exercise of its power under s.6(2) which have effect of putting an embargo on direct recruitment of employees - Whether these directives were within power of Central Govt? - Held, s.6(2) is a provision of Act itself which empowers Central Govt to issue directives and bind Board of Directors of Food Corporation to comply with such directives - Not possible to read any regulation framed u/s.45 as inconsistent with or overriding a directives or instruction validly given by the Central Government to FCI u/s. 6(2) - HC judgment set aside - Appeals allowed.
