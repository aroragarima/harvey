Bhakra Beas Management Board v Krishan Kumar Vij & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 643; (2010) 8 SCC 701; AIR 2010 SC 3342; 2010 (127) FLR 948; JT 2010 (8) SC 497; 2010(8) SCALE 363; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 462; 2011 (5) SLR 176

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab State Electricity Board, Service of Engineers (Civil) Regulations, 1965, Punjab State Electricity Board, Service of Engineers (Electrical) Regulation, 1965

Summary: Service - Punjab State Electricity Board, Service of Engineers (Civil) Regulations, 1965, regns. 7(a)(ii), 10(4) - Stagnation of posts - Time bound higher pay scale - Entitlement - State Government introduced time-bound higher scales to various cadres of stagnated posts of regular employees who had completed 9/16 years of service - State Electricity Board issued Office Order dated 23-4-1990 implementing the said G.O and subsequently, issued another Order dated 24-5-1990 (1990 Order) specifying the promotional scales and eligibility conditions for Assistant Engineers - Appellant/Board adopted the said Orders of State Electricity Board - 1st respondent/Assistant Design Engineer sought time bound scale - Appellant rejected 1st respondent's request on the ground that he was a promotee and not a direct recruit - 1st respondent challenged appellant's order before the HC - HC allowed 1st respondent's writ petition and directed the appellant to grant the next higher pay scale to 1st respondent after he completing 16 years of service - Whether 1st respondent entitled to higher pay scale in terms of 1990 Order since he had completed requisite length of service of 16 years on the post of Assistant Design Engineer - Held, 1990 Order contemplates that it is to be followed as per 1965 Regulation which provides that only such persons who have been promoted u/regn. 7(a)(ii) r/w regn. 10(4) shall be treated as direct recruits and 1990 Order does not apply to the promotees irrespective of their academic qualifications nor they can be treated at par with the direct recruits - Although 1990 Order was issued only with an intention to remove the stagnation but that would not give an absolute right to any employee to be entitled to higher pay scale even if he does not fulfil pre-requisite qualifications for holding the higher post - If an employee possesses the required qualifications but is unable to get the higher post on account of non-availability of such post, only then can he be categorised as suffering from stagnation as per 1990 Order - Obviously, an employee who does not fulfil the qualification as per regn. 10(4) for the higher post would be ineligible for promotion and/or higher pay scale and in that eventuality, such an employee cannot complain of stagnation - Since the 1st respondent did not fall into the category of regn. 10(4), he was not entitled to the higher scale - Further, appellant had categorically raised the ground of delay of over eight years in approaching the HC for grant of relief by 1st respondent, however, condoned such an inordinate in a casual manner - Therefore, HC clearly erred in setting aside the appellant's order and hence, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
