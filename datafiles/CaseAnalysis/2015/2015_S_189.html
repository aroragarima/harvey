Jogendra Yadav and others v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 533; (2015) 9 SCC 244; AIR 2015 SC 2951; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 3707; 2015 CRLJ 4186; 2015 (4) JCC 2663; JT 2015 (8) SC 191; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2514; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 448; 2016 (1) RLW 570; 2015(8) SCALE 442; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Standard Of Proof

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 323 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227, 319 - Murder - Discharge application - FIR was lodged u/ss. 149, 302, 323 of IPC for murder of victim against 8 accused, however, charge sheet was submitted only against four accused and final form was submitted excluding four accused - Trial Court after giving opportunity to accused held added for accused to proceedings - Further, appellant got relief by way of discharge u/s. 227 of CrPC - HC set aside order of lower Court with respect to discharge of appellants - Appellant contended that s. 227 of CrPC can be availed of by them, even if he is added as accused u/s. 319 of CrPC since effect of adding such person is that he becomes newly added accused who was entitled to avail of all remedies available to him under CrPC i.e. remedy of discharge - Whether appellant is entitled to avail remedy of discharge provided under CrPC.

Held, extraordinary power u/s. 319 of CrPC, can be exercised only if very strong and cogent evidence occurs against person from evidence led before Court. Hardeep Singh v. State of Punjab and Others, 2014 Indlaw SC 17, relied on SC held that standard of proof employed for summoning person as accused u/s. 319 of CrPC, is higher than standard of proof employed for framing charge against accused. SC held that it does not stand to reason that person who is summoned as accused to stand trial and added as such to proceedings on basis of stricter standard of proof can be allowed to be discharged from proceedings on basis of lesser standard of proof such as prima facie connection with offence necessary for charging accused. SC held that person is added as accused u/s. 319 of CrPC, on basis of evidence; whereas accused is discharged u/s. 227 of CrPC on sifting of material collected in order to find out whether or not there is sufficient ground for proceeding against accused. Further, accused summoned u/s. 319 of CrPC are entitled to invoke remedy under law against illegal or improper exercise of power u/s. 319 of CrPC, but cannot have effect of order undone by seeking discharge u/s. 227 of CrPC. If allowed to, such action of discharge would not be in accordance with purpose of CrPC in enacting s. 319 of CrPC which empowers Court to summon person for being tried along with other accused where it appears from evidence that he has committed offence. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Person summoned u/s. 319 of CrPC cannot avail remedy of discharge u/s. 227 of CrPC as s. 319 of CrPC stands on different footing.
