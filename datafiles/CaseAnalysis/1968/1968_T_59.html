Â Tukaram G.Gaokar v R. N. Shukla and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 239; AIR 1968 SC 1050; 1968 CRLJ 1234; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(3)- Sea Customs Act 1962, s. 112 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, s. 8 - Action of the customs officers - Imposing penalty - Whether any proceeding amount to contempt of court of magistrate before whom trial for offences in connection with smuggling of gold by appellant is imminent and in violation of art. 20(3) of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, initiation and continuance proceedings in good faith cannot amount to contempt of criminal court - To constitute contempt of court, there must be involved some 'act done or writing published calculated to bring a court or a judge of court into contempt or to lower his authority or something calculated to obstruct or interfere with due course of justice or lawful process of courts' - One cannot be punished for contempt of court in absence of willful error proceeding from improper or corrupt motives - Customs officers are not actuated by any oblique motive, their action is authorized not in excess of their jurisdiction - They have a discretion to stay proceedings during pendency of trial in criminal court - Action is not mala fide or arbitrary - Appeal dismissed.
