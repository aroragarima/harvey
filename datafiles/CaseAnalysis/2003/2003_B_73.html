Bantialias Guddu v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1485; 2004 CRLJ 372; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 911

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 231 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellants assaulted deceased with knives on his chest , stomach & thigh - PW-1 lodged complaint - Accused were arrested and presented before Trial Court which had passed conviction u/s. 302 IPC - Aggrieved by same appeal was preferred which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of lower Court was sustainable - Held, if lack of motive as pleaded by accused was a factor, at same time it could not be lost sight of that there was no reason as to why PW-1 would falsely implicate accused persons - Further, there was no suggestion of any motive for such alleged false implication and because PW-1 was a relation of deceased, and PW-2 was known to him, that per se could not be a ground to discard their evidence - Furthermore, if there were too many witnesses on same point Public Prosecutor was at liberty to choose two or some among them alone so that time of Court could be saved from repetitious depositions on same factual aspects - Therefore, if explanation offered for delayed examination was plausible and acceptable and Court accepts same as plausible, there was no reason to interfere with conclusion - Thus, there were no justifying reason or ground substantiated to interfere with findings recorded by lower Courts - Appeals dismissed.
