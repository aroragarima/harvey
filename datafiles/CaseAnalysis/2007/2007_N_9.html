Special Deputy Collector (L.A.) v N. Vasudeva Rao & Ors

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1188; (2007) 14 SCC 165; AIR 2008 SC 944; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 38; 2008 (70) ALR 488; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 601; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 459; 2007(13) SCALE 508; 2007 (8) SCJ 843; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 625

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Contumacious Conduct

Summary: Lands have been assigned to the respondents on the basis of D Form pattas - As per the terms and conditions of the pattas, whenever the land is required for any public purpose, the same can be resumed by the Government on payment of certain ex-gratia amount - Alleged non-payment of ex-gratia amount - Held, actually there is no definite material as to whether the land was resumed or it was an excavated land - One of the basic issues was whether the land was resumed or excavated land - There is no definite material in this regard brought by the respondents on record - Neither Single Judge nor Division Bench addressed the basic issues and on the other hand came to abrupt conclusions - Authorities shall however consider the matter in detail and record findings keeping in view the GO, the factual position and evidence led before it - Appeals disposed of.
