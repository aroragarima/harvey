Â Kamla Prasad Khetan v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 13; AIR 1957 SC 676; 1958 (2) LLJ 461; 1957 SCJ 811; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Industries (Development And Regulation) Amendment Act, 1953, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Socio-Economic - Trade - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, ss. 15 and 18 A - Essential Supplies (Temporary powers) Act, 1946, s. 3(4) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - (A) Whether power to issue an order under any Central Act includes power of revocation? - Held, power to issue an order also includes power to amend or rescind order - (B) Whether an order altering or rescinding a previous order under a Central Act is open to judicial review? - Held, only question to be decided by Court is whether amending order complied with like conditions under which original order was made -(C) Whether appointment of authorised Controller by Central Govt. can be challenged before a court of law? - Held, appointment can be challenged on ground of mala fide - Test to be applied is whether appointment was made for some ulterior purpose, some purpose other than object for which law was enacted - Petition dismissed.
