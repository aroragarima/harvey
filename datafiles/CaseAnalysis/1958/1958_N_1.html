N. T. Veluswami Thevar v G. Raja Nainar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1959 SC 422; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 48; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 623; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 84, 102, 136, 173, 191 and 226 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(c) and 36(2) - (A) Whether the Tribunal can entertain a petition on new grounds which were not placed before the Returning Officer? - Held, if something has not been brought to the notice of the Returning Officer, that cannot debar that party to urge something before the authority trying the election petition - (B) What is the nature and extent of the writ jurisdiction of the High Court, in view of s. 116A of Representation of the People Act, 1951? - Held, proceedings before the Tribunal should go on with expedition and without interruption - It would be proper for HC to decline the exercise of discretion u/art. 226 to interfere with interlocutory orders - Appeal allowed.
