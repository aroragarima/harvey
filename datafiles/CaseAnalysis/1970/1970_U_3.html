Parbhat General Agencies Etc v Union Of India & Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 287; (1971) 1 SCC 79; AIR 1971 SC 2298; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 564; 1971 UJ 20

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1940, ss. 8(1)(b), 20(4) - Appointment of Arbitrator - Judicial Commissioner - Appellants and respondents entered into agreement - Dispute arose between the appellants and respondents - Appellant filed application u/s. 20 of the Act for ordering respondents to filed agreements and referring the matter to the judicial Commissioner - Said application was allowed - Judicial Commissioner declined to act as an arbitrator - Subordinate Judge dismissed the application for appointment of arbitrator - On revision, Judicial Commissioner dismissed the revision - Hence instant appeal - Whether the interpretation placed by the courts below on the relevant provision in the arbitration agreements is correct.

Held, Judicial Commissioner as well as the subordinate judge erred in thinking that merely because the arbitrator was designated with reference to the office held by him, it should be inferred that the parties intended not to supply the vacancy. Evidently the parties did not mention the name of any particular Judicial Commissioner as arbitrator because there may be a change in the personnel. Appointment of Judicial Commissioner as arbitrator by itself does not afford any indication that the parties to be agreement intended not to supply the vacancy if the Judicial Commissioner refused to act or is incapable of acting. After the arbitration agreement has been ordered to be filed the court shall proceed to make a reference firstly to the arbitrator appointed by the parties in the agreement; secondly to the arbitrator not named in the agreement, but with regard to whom the parties agree otherwise; and thirdly when, the parties cannot agree upon an arbitrator, to an arbitrator appointed by itself. After referring to the various decisions rendered u/s. 8(1)(b) and s. 20(4) of the Act, and noticing the conflict of the judicial opinion rejected the revision petition solely on the ground that he would not be justified in the exercise of his revisional powers in setting aside the view taken by the lower court. Orders passed by the subordinate judge as well as by the Judicial Commissioner set aside and remit the cases to the trial Judge for appointing a new arbitrator in place of the Judicial Commissioner. Appeals allowed.
