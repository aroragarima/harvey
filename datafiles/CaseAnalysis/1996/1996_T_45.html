Bhim Singh, Presidentjammu and Kashmir Pantheres Party v Election Commissioner of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 703; (1996) 4 SCC 188; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 54; 1996 (2) CLT 166; JT 1996 (5) SC 569; 1996(3) SCALE 481; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 15

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 30 (d) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 324 - Constitution - This is a writ petition for a declaration that s. 30 (d) of the Representation of the People Act, 1951, is unconstitutional and ultra vires art. 14 of the Constitution of India "as it does not specify the maximum period for holding the election after the withdrawal of nomination - For a direction to the Election Commission of India to issue a separate notification for holding elections in the State of J and K within 20 days from the date for withdrawal of nominations - The UOI filed a counter affidavit - Stated that "owing to the prevailing peculiar situation in the State of J and K, it is no desirable that the time for canvassing in the State should be more than the necessary minimum statutory period" - Held, there can be little doubt that the aforesaid provisions read together require close consultation between the UOI and the Election Commission in the matter of fixing the election programme - It is clearly both impossible and undesirable that any outer limit should be placed in s. 30(d) for the date of the poll - The fixation of the date of the poll would depend upon a variety of circumstances, all of which have to be taken into account by the Election Commission acting in consultation with the Govt. of India, which would have the necessary material in this behalf - We do not, therefore, find s. 30(d) arbitrary or unconstitutional - Writ petition disposed of.
