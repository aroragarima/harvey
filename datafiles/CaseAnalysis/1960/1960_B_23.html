Bhagwantrao v Vishwasrao and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 480; AIR 1960 SC 642; 1960 SCJ 557; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Inam Rules, 1859, rr. I, II, V, VI and XV - Berar Patels and Patwaris Law, 1900, ss. 3, 9, 10, 11 and 20 - Berar Land Revenue Code, 1928, ss. 190 and 192 - (A) What is true character of 'patelki inam' lands of this case? - In revenue case of 1908 Amrit Patel had himself stated that land was given to his ancestors in lieu of patelki huq and it should not be assessed to land revenue; in second resumption proceedings of 1917 Govt. had first decided to resume jagir and regrant it to then Jagirdar Amerulla Khan and in orders passed, it was stated that Jagirdars would be at liberty to allow working patels to hold such lands as were considered reasonable by Deputy Commr free of revenue and in lieu of mushahara (emoluments or wages); these statements show clearly enough what appellant and his brother Amrit, understood to be character of 'patelki inam' lands, they clearly said that lands were given in lieu of emoluments or remuneration which view ultimately prevailed and earlier orders were modified on Commr. Standan's recommendation who said that lands held by patel as 'patelki inam' should be excluded from resumption and patel should be allowed to hold lands free of any payment in lieu of cash remuneration for his office; further, u/s. 3 of Patels and Patwaries Law, office was rotatory; but each patel retained his inam land even when it was not his turn to work as patel, however this circumstance does not establish that grant was other than what High Court held it to be - Held, there is clear and unimpeachable evidence on record to support HC's finding that they constitute a grant by way of remuneration or emoluments of patel's office by use of lands - (B) Whether u/.r VI(2) r/w r. V(2) of Inam Rules, appellant was entitled to his share in 'inam' as an undivided brother of Amritrao Patel ? - Firstly, r. VI(2) applies to inams granted in lieu of lands or money stipends, service of which has either been dispensed with or otherwise discontinued which is not case here; secondly, cl. (8) of r. VI makes it clear that when inam attached to office is wholly or partially in enjoyment of members of family who do not perform service, such portion of alienated inam as may be considered necessary for efficient performance of duties will be attached to office-holders, and r. XIV(2) says that service grants are not liable to be alienated by purchase or otherwise - Further held, there is nothing in rules on basis of which appellant can claim as of right that he has a share in 'patelki inam' lands as a member of patel's family, irrespective of whether he performs service or not; further, provisions of Patels and Patwaris Law clearly indicate that 'patelki inam' lands are subject to orders passed by revenue authorities in respect of matters mentioned therein, and no right is given to a member of family of patel to claim a share in emoluments, power to resume lands granted on condition that holder shall render certain services is specifically referred to in s. 190 of Berar Code - (C) Whether two plots with survey no 2/1 A and 9/1 A ceased to be inam lands when they fell in resumed portion of Jagir in view of decrees obtained by appellant and his brother Amrit and having obtained possession in execution ? - Further held, trial Judge rightly pointed out that said decrees related to property other than plots 2/1 A and 9/1 A; moreover, it is not disputed that entire 'patelki inam' lands in possession of Amritrao patel, including plots which were made khalsa in 1904-05, were excluded from resumption and Amrit's 'patelki inam' lands were treated as a homogenous unit by orders passed on 28-08-1922, therefore two plots, 2/1 A and 9/1 A, stand on same footing as other 'patelki inam' lands of Amritrao - Appeal dismissed.
