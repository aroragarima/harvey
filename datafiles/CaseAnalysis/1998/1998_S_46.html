Sunil Enterprises and another v SBI Commercial & International Bank Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 722; (1998) 5 SCC 354; AIR 1998 SC 2317; 1998 (33) ALR 483; 1998 (4) Bom.C.R. 634; 1998 (2) CLT 154; [1998] 92 Comp Cas 772; JT 1998 (3) SC 641; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 98; 1998(3) SCALE 377; 1998 (4) SLT 793; 1998 (4) Supreme 324

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Civil Procedure Code, 1908, o.37, r.3 - Leave to defend - Grant of - Suit by bank - Bill of exchange - Acceptors sought leave to defend themselves - Contention, same were executed without consideration as neither goods were sold nor supplied in transaction in question - Alleging entire transaction was fraud - Held, in circumstances, defence is not baseless - Leave to defend granted.
