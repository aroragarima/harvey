Dashrath @ Champa and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1060; (2007) 12 SCC 487; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 254; AIR 2008 SC 316; 2007 (4) JCC 3085; JT 2007 (12) SC 400; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 903; 2007(12) SCALE 424; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 642

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: X- Ray

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 part I r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, dying declaration is only a piece of untested evidence and must like any other evidence, satisfy the Court that what is stated therein is the unalloyed truth and that it is absolutely safe to act upon it - If after careful scrutiny the Court is satisfied that it is true and free from any effort to induce the deceased to make a false statement and if it is coherent and consistent, there shall be no legal impediment to make it basis of conviction, even if there is no corroboration - There is no material to show that dying declarations were result of product of imagination, tutoring or prompting - Appeal dismissed.
