Vesa Holdings Private Limited v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 343; (2015) 8 SCC 293; 2015 (2) BC 320; [2015] 189 Comp Cas 580; 2015 CRLJ 2455; JT 2015 (3) SC 541; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 108; 2015 (1) OLR 1031; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 442; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 417, 418, 420 - Cheating - Filing of FIR - Justification - Appellant company availed loan from bank and respondent no.3 complainant as AGM of said bank at relevant time, dealt with their loan application and had sanctioned same - Appellant company defaulted in repayment and wanted to settle loan amount and respondent no.3 on retirement from bank agreed to act as Consultant of company in settling loan and company issued letter dt. 6-8-2008 stating that settlement of Company dues should be at Rs.8.25 crores and acceptance letter from bank should be obtained on or before 30-10-2008 and it was also agreed that amount would be given towards consultancy fees for settlement, out of which part payment to be given in advance to respondent no.3 and balance amount to be paid on completion of assignment - However, respondent noo.3 alleged that appellant company and its directors failed after settlement failed to pay him consultancy fees - Complaint was lodged against appellant company and directors u/ss. 417, 418, 420, 120B and 34 of IPC - HC refused to quash FIR filled against appellants - Hence, present appeal.

Held, bank did not issue any acceptance letter on or before 30-10-2008 with regard to settlement of disputes of appellant company. Further, respondent no.3 also did not present cheque dt. 6-8-2008 issued by appellant company for encashing amount. Further, due to efforts of appellant company, bank finally agreed and issued letter of acceptance dt. 5-1-2009. One year later, respondent no.3 sent letter dt. 6-3-2010 to appellant company demanding balance amount towards consultancy fee. No allegation whatsoever was made against appellants herein in said letter. It was only mentioned in it that consultation fee remains unpaid and company is delaying payment on one pretext or other. Further, SC held that after expiry of validity period of cheque dt. 6-8-2008, respondent no.3 did not ask for re-issue of same. SC held that in present case there is nothing to show that at very inception there was any intention on behalf of accused persons to cheat which is condition precedent for offence u/s. 420 IPC and complaint does not disclose any criminal offence at all. Hence, SC held that allowing police investigation to continue would amount to abuse of process of court and HC committed error in refusing to exercise power u/s. 482 of CrPC to quash proceedings. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Case u/s. 420 of IPC cannot be filed against parties when it cannot be shown that there was any intention on behalf of accused persons to cheat which is a condition precedent.
