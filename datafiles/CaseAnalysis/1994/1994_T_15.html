T.R. Kothandaraman v T.N. Water Supply And Drainage Bd.

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1022; (1994) 6 SCC 282; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1366; AIR 1994 SCW 4367; 1994 (28) ATC 276; 1995 (70) FLR 138; JT 1994 (6) SC 157; 1994 (2) LLN 1066; 1994(4) SCALE 115; 1994 (3) SLJ 109; 1994 (7) SLR 1; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 513; 1995 (1) UJ 47

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: (A) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 19 - Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage Board Service Regulations, 1972, regn. 19(2)(b) - Appellants of the service had the grievances that the restrictions on the promotions were violating the mandate of art. 16, of the Constitution - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the proviso to regn. 19(2)(b), of the 1972 Regulations, was violative of art. 16 , of the Constitution - Held, that the challenge had to fail because the diploma-holders being less educationally qualified than degree-holders could be made non-eligible for promotion to higher post, but the proviso really took out the rigour by permitting the diploma-holders to be considered for promotion in case they were to show exceptional merit in their work - Proviso being thus favourable to the diploma-holders had really to be welcomed by them, instead of inviting their wrath - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 19 - Tamil Nadu Water Supply and Drainage Board Service Regulations, 1972, regn. 19(2)(b) - Special Rules for the Tamil Nadu Agriculture Engineering Service, r. 2(b) - Whether the r. 2(b), of the Special Rules, was violative of guarantee of quality, in art. 16, of the Constitution - Held, that the post of Executive Engineer (Agricultural Engineering) called for higher skill, administration, planning and evolving of proposals and drafting, in which aspects most of the diploma-holders were found lacking, therefore it was not violative and was based on reasonable grounds - It had been mentioned in this affidavit that the degree-holders had studied for six years at college level after leaving school stage, whereas diploma-holders had only three years' study at the level of college - Higher technical calibre in degree-holders was presumed - Higher educational qualification had relevance insofar as the holding of higher promotional post was concerned, in view of the nature of the functions and duties attached to the post - Classification had nexus with the object to be achieved - Appeals dismissed.
