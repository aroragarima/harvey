Â Ahmedabad Manufacturing and Calicoprinting Company, Limited v S. C. Mehta, Income Tax Officerand Another

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 293; AIR 1963 SC 1436; [1963] 48 ITR 154; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 92; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 35(10) and 154 - (A) Whether s. 35(10) of Act would be applied on an assessment which had become final before 01-04-1956 i.e, applied retrospectively? - Held, language of sub-s. 35(10) required it to have a retrospective operation - It mentions years individually - It mentions an event which is subsequent, namely, declaration of dividend from an amount on which rebate was allowed - Declaration of dividends must be after grant of rebate - Is only condition - If in earlier assessment in any of years mentioned a rebate was allowed and subsequently in any year there was a declaration of dividend utilising amount on which rebate was given amount so utilised - Incorrect relief - This fiction comes into force from 01-04-1956 but it is not stated that circumstances in which it comes into being should also be after 01-04-1956 - Recalling of rebate is after enactment of sub-s. (10) but conditions for exercise of power may be before or after commencement of subsection - A statute which is not declaratory of a pre-existing law nor a matter relating to procedure but affects vested rights, cannot be given a greater retrospective effect than its language renders necessary - Appeal dismissed.
