State of Bihar v D. N. Ganguly and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 68; AIR 1958 SC 1018; 1958 (2) LLJ 634; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 178; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1191

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: General Clauses Act, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Industrial Disputes Act 1947, ss. 7 and 10(1) - General Clauses Act,1897, s. 21 - Dismissal of workmen - Entitlement to reinstatement - The Govt. of Bihar referred an industrial dispute between management of Bata Shoe Co. Ltd., Digbaghat (Patna), and their 31 workmen, u/s. 7 r/w s. 10(1) of Act of 1947 - Dispute on dismissal of workmen-entitled to reinstatement or any other relief For adjudication of this dispute- Later a similar industrial dispute between same Bata Company and its 29 other workmen was referred by Govt. of Bihar to same tribunal - (A) Whether Govt. has power to cancel or supersede a reference made u/s.10(1) of Industrial Disputes Act,1947? - Held, Act does not expressly confer any power on appropriate Govt. to cancel or supersede a reference made u/s. 10(1) of Act -such power cannot be claimed u/s. 21 of General Clauses Act,1897 - (A1) Whether a mandamus or certiorari would be a proper writ against a notification u/s. 10(1) of Industrial Disputes Act 1947 found invalid? - Further held, it would be more appropriate to issue a writ of mandamus against such notification found invalid and ultra vires then issuing a writ of certiorari - Writ of certiorari would quash impugned notification - Appeal dismissed.
