Lala Ram and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 843; (1990) 2 SCC 113; (1990) SCC (Cr) 276; AIR 1990 SC 1185; 1990 ALJ 269; 1990 (1) Crimes 647; 1990 CRLJ 1205; 1990 CRLR 190; JT 1990 (1) SC 169; 1990(1) SCALE 141; 1990 (1) UJ 614

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Concurrent judgment of Sessions Court and High Court convicting accused although Supreme Court interfered in appeal by special leave - Argument of omnibus allegation of eye-witness that as many as 10 accused fired 15 fire shots at victims at a time when victims and other persons including witness were sitting in close proximity although no other person got injuries - Held, conviction could not be based on such omnibus allegation and accused was acquitted.
