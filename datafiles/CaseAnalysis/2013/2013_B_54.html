Laxmibai (Dead) Thr. Lrs. & Anr. v Bhagwantbuva (Dead) Thr. Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 48; (2013) 4 SCC 97; AIR 2013 SC 1204; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 145; 2013(2) ALL MR 420; 2013 (97) ALR 506; 2013 (3) AWC 2544; 2013 (2) Bom.C.R. 30; JT 2013 (2) SC 362; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 895; 2013(2) SCALE 106; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 632

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 10,3,16 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 146,134,138,57(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 18 r. 16 - Adoption - Customs - Registered deed - Presumption - Interested Witnesses - Sustainability - Appellant-wife was decedent of Sant who was vested with exclusive right to carry Palki and Padukas of Sri Sant Eknath Maharaj from Paithan to Pandharpur at time of Ashadi Ekadashi - After death of Sant respondents issued notice to BP calling him not to give his son in adoption to appellant - BP's son was adopted by appellant to perform all requisite ceremonies and adoption deed was registered - Appellant field suit for injunction restraining respondents from interfering with ceremonies performed by her son - Suit was decreed by Trial Court holding that adoption of son of BP was valid - Respondents filed appeal before appellate court which reversed orders of Civil Court and held that respondents had proved, that there did in fact exist a custom which prohibited taking of a male child in adoption from outside, adoption itself was suspicious as independent witnesses were not examined and witnesses who proved validity of the adoption were interested witnesses, and adoption deed was also suspicious - Appellant before appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was a presumption of validity with respect to registered adoption deed u/s.16 of 1956, Act - Respondents, opposed contending that first appellate court had right to re-appreciate all material on record, after which it had rightly reached a conclusion as regards suspicious nature of adoption deed and adoption ceremonies, and had also rightly concluded, that since over a period of 375 years only four adoptions had taken place, and as in each case, a male child was adopted only from within family, there certainly existed a custom which did not permit adoption of a male child from outside family, such findings did not warrant any interference by this court - Whether First appellate Court was justified in reversing judgment of Trial Court and whether there was custom of adopting child from within family - Held, appellate court had erred by considering irrelevant material, while most relevant evidence, i.e., adoption ceremony and adoption deed, had been disregarded on basis of mere surmises and conjectures, correctness or authenticity of adoption deed was not disputed but natural parents of adoptive child who were definitely executing parties of deed had signed as witnesses along with 7 other witnesses in such a fact-situation, by gathering intention of parties and by reading document as a whole and considering its purport, it could be concluded that adoption stood test of law - SC, viewed that cause of justice would be served, instead of being thwarted, where there had been substantial compliance of legal requirements, specified in s. 16 of 1956, Act - When substantial justice and technical considerations were pitted against each other, cause of substantial justice deserved to be preferred and courts may in larger interests of administration of justice may excuse or overlook a mere irregularity or a trivial breach of law for doing real and substantial justice to parties and pass orders which will serve interest of justice best - Appeal allowed.
