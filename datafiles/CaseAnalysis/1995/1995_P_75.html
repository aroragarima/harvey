State Of U.P. And Ors.Etc. v Pradhan Sangh Kshettra Samiti And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 396; (1995) Supp2 SCC 305; AIR 1995 SC 1512; AIR 1995 SCW 2303; 1995 ALJ 1688; JT 1995 (3) SC 252; 1995(2) SCALE 453; 1995 (2) SCJ 395; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 1015; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 874

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Gram Sabha, Finance Commission, Uttar Pradesh District Board Act, 1922, Uttar Pradesh Panchayat Raj. Act, 1947

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Constitution - U.P. Panchayat Raj Act, 1947, Pre., ss. 2(t), 2(g), 2(11), 3, 11, 96A - Constitution, arts. 14, 39A., 40, 154, 163, 243(g), 243(e), 243D, 243C, 243K - Constitutionality of 1947 Act - Considering - (A) Organisation of village panchayat - (B) Whether definition of village - (1) u/art. 243(g) is without sticking to any particular concept of village - (2) Mean revenue village u/s. 2t , fall foul of art. 243(g) or foreclosed authority of Governor to specify village by notification -(C) Panchayat area - (1) Declaration of - (2) Whether s. 2(11) carving out panchayat area on basis of population is ultra vires art. 243(e) - (3) Finalisation - (D) Gram Sabha - (1) Vires of definition in s. 2(g) - (2) Whether by establishment of one Gram Sabha for group of villages, the villages lose their name and identity as separate villages - (E) Electoral matters - Bar on Courts to interfere - Challenge to delimitation of panchayat area or constituencies in said area or to allotment of seats to constituencies - Raised after election notification was issued - (F) Delegation of powers under Act by State Government to authority subordinate to it , s. 96-A, providing for such delegation, If ultra vires art. 243(g) - (G) Interpretation of Statutes - Legal documents - Construction

(H) Natural justice - Post-decisional hearing - (I) Nyaya panchayat - Act providing for establishment of - Validity - Held, (A) Factors that are to be kept in mind - (B) Going through any pre-conceived notion of village in the Article is therefore not warranted - Not inconsistent with art. 243(g) as Constitution itself equates Governor with State Govt. - Notification by Governor is in fact notification by State government and enactment of State legislature is a higher form of legal instrument than notification - (C) Section does not militate against concept of democracy and of self governance - Not ultra vires -Reasonable opportunity for raising objections and hearing them ought to be given before finalisation of area as change in area of local bodies does involve civil consequences - (D) Not ultra vires - Villages do not lose it - (E) Cannot and should not be entertained - (F) Neither art. 243 (g) nor any other provision in Part IX of the Constitution prevents the Governor and, therefore, the State Government from delegating its power mentioned in the said Part to any subordinate officer - (G) Substance and not form of document should be considered - (H) Sufficient in case of urgent matters - (I) Not ultra vires Constitution of that ground, constitution does not prohibit the establishment of nyaya panchayat and organisation of the nyaya panchayats will be in promotion of the directive principles contained in art. 39A - Appeal allowed.
