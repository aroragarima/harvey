Â Daruka and Company v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 4; (1973) 2 SCC 617; AIR 1973 SC 2711; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 419; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 570; 1973 UJ 821

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Trade - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14, 19(1)(g) and 265 - Export Control Order, 1968, cl 4 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s.4(a) - Export Control Order, 1968, cl 6(1) - Scheme of canalisation of export through Trading Corporation contained in Trade Notice D/- 29-01-1972 issued u/cl 6(1) - (A) Constitutionality - Grounds, violates art.19(1)(g) and 14 - (B) Press Note prescribing procedure to be adopted - Whether a device to transfer business and goodwill of traders in favour of Corporation? - (C) Service charge collected by Corporation whether a tax - (D) Scheme of canalisation of export of all grades and varieties of mica - Exclusion of mica powder from scheme - Whether violative of art.14? - Held, restrictions on traders is reasonable, therefore there is no violation of art.19(1)(g) and it cannot be said that notice shows preference for Corporation in infringement of art.14 - It cannot be said that procedure for export through Corporation where no irrevocable letters of credit were opened before 24-1-1972 is in reality not a canalisation scheme but is a device to transfer business and goodwill of traders in favour of Corporation - Service charges are nothing but quid pro quo for services rendered by Corporation - Notice cannot be challenged as violative of art.14 on ground that there was discrimination between exporters of mica powder on one hand and exporters of mica scrap on other - Petition dismissed.
