Punjab State Industrial Development Corporation Limited, Chandigarh v Commissioner of Income Tax, Patiala

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1622; (1997) 10 SCC 184; 1997 (140) CTR 594; [1997] 225 ITR 792; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 5

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Whether in facts and circumstances of case, Tribunal was right in law in holding that amount of Rs. 1, 50, 000 paid to Registrar of Companies, as filing fee for enhancement of capital was not revenue expenditure? - When an expenditure is made, not only once and for all, but with a view to bringing into existence an asset or an advantage for enduring benefit of a trade, there is very good reason,(in absence of special circumstances leading to an opposite conclusion), for treating such an expenditure as properly attributable not to revenue but to capital - Held, fee paid to Registrar for expansion of capital base of company was directly related to capital expenditure incurred by company and although incidentally that would certainly help in business of company and may also help in profit-making, it still retains character of a capital expenditure since expenditure was directly related to expansion of capital base of company - View taken by different High Courts in favour of Revenue is preferable - Reference answered accordingly - Order accordingly.
