Debabrata Bandopadhyay v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, V. Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 92; AIR 1969 SC 189; 1969 CRLJ 401; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 304

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Apology

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Court - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Appellants, were a District Magistrate and his four assistants - Appellants were charged for accepting a bond which was not in a proper manner - Order granting stay passed by Session Court took some time to reach its destination - Whether delay amounted to Contempt of Court? - It is only when a clear case of contumacious conduct not explainable otherwise, arises that the condemner must be punished - Held, no - Delay caused was not deliberate - Contempt is actionable for intentional and deliberate defiance of authority of a superior forum - Delay was due to result of system of transmission of orders of superior courts which had to pass through several hands - No case of contempt made out - Appeal allowed.
