State of Rajasthan v Parmanand and another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 135; (2014) 5 SCC 345; AIR 2014 SC 1384; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1475; 2014 CRLJ 1756; 2014 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 66; JT 2014 (5) SC 271; 2014 (1) KLT 1030; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1449; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 245; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 40; 2014 (3) RLW 2063; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 522; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Acquittal, Possession of opium

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18, 28, 29, 42, 50, 50(1), 8 - Possession of opium - Acquittal - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused persons/respondents u/ss. 8, 18, 28, 29, 42, 50, 50(1) of the Act - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal before HC - HC by impugned order acquitted respondents - Hence, instant appeal by State - Whether s. 50 of the Act was complied with or not -

Held, a joint communication of right available u/s. 50(1) of the Act to respondent would frustrate very purport of s. 50 of the Act - Communication of said right to person who was about to be searched was not an empty formality - Respondents must be individually informed that u/s. 50(1) of the Act, he has right to be searched before a nearest gazetted officer or before a nearest Magistrate - Repetition to state that on written communication of right available u/s. 50(1) of the Act, respondent no. 2 has signed for himself and for respondent no.1 - Respondent no. 1 has not signed on it at all - It was only to be presumed that he had authorized respondent no. 2 to sign on his behalf and convey his consent - Therefore, right has not been properly communicated to respondents - Search of bag of respondent no.1 and search of person of respondents was vitiated and resultantly their conviction was also vitiated - It was prosecution case that respondents informed officers that they would like to be searched before PW5 by PW10/S.I - This was again breach of s. 50(1) of the Act - It was improper for PW10 to tell respondents that third alternative was available and that they could be searched before PW5, Superintendent, who was part of raiding party could not be called an independent officer - But PW10 could not have given third option to respondents when s. 50(1) of the Act does not provide for it and when such option would frustrate provisions of s. 50(1) of the Act - Search conducted by PW10 was vitiated - Conviction of respondents was illegal - Respondents have rightly been acquitted by HC - Appeal dismissed.
