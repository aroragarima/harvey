Inderjeet Kaur v Nirpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2619; (2001) 1 SCC 706; AIR 2000 SCW 4539; 2001 (2) ALT(SC) 1; 2001 (89) DLT 27; JT 2001 (1) SC 308; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 33; 2000(8) SCALE 424; 2001 (1) SCJ 386; 2001 (1) SLT 1; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 707; 2000 (8) Supreme 350; 2001 (1) UC 291

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1) and 25-B - Leave to defend - Whether Additional Rent Controller (ARC) was justified in deciding disputed questions of facts at stage of leave to defend? - Held, Chapter III - A deals with summary trial of certain applications expressly stating that every application by a landlord for recovery of possession on ground specified in cl. (e) of proviso to sub-s. (1) of s. 14 of Act, or u/s. 14-A or 14-B or 14-C or 14-D shall be dealt with in accordance with special provisions prescribed in s. 25-B of Act - As per broad scheme of this Chapter a tenant is precluded from contesting an application filed for eviction on grounds mentioned in aforementioned provisions unless he obtains leave from Controller to contest eviction petition - In default of obtaining leave to defend or leave is refused to him an order of eviction follows - As is evident from ss. 25-B(4) and (5) of Act, burden placed on a tenant is light and limited in that if affidavit filed by him discloses such facts as would disentitle landlord from obtaining an order for recovery of possession of premises on ground specified in cl. (e) of proviso to s. 14(1) of Act, are good enough to grant leave to defend - Unless tenant at that stage itself establishes a strong case as would non-suit landlord, leave to defend should not be granted when it is not requirement of s. 25-B(5) - A leave to defend sought for cannot also be granted for mere asking or in a routine manner which will defeat very object of special provisions contained in Chap. III-A of Act - At stage of granting leave real test should be whether facts disclosed in affidavit filed seeking leave to defend prima facie show that landlord would be disentitled from obtaining an order of eviction and not whether at end defence may fail - S. 25-B(6) states that where leave is granted to a tenant to contest eviction application, Controller shall commence hearing of application as early as practicable - S. 25-B(7) speaks of procedure to be followed in such cases - S. 25-B(8) bars appeals against an order of recovery of possession except a provision of revision to High Court - Thus a combined effect of ss. 25-B(6), (7) and (8) would lead to expeditious disposal of eviction petitions so that a landlord need not wait and suffer for a long time - Additional Rent Controller exceeded jurisdiction vested in him in refusing leave to defend to appellant - Additional Rent Controller has acted with material irregularity and committed a manifest error in accepting case of respondent landlord when facts were seriously disputed and correctness or otherwise of documents required to be examined - Order set aside - Appeal allowed.
