Member For The Board of Agricultural Income Tax, Assam v Sindhurani Chaudhurani and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1957 SC 729; 1957 CRLJ 1086; [1957] 32 ITR 169; 1957 SCJ 646; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 1019

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Assam Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1939, s. 27 - Board Of Revenue Act, 1913 - Salami - Agricultural income - Transfer of a right - Zamindari land - Assessed/'J' died and on his death his widow, 'S' and others substituted - Agricultural income of assessee and income from salami received from settlement - Out of gross income from salami 15% was allowed as collection charges - Amount in dispute is Rs. 7,934 - Agricultural Income Tax Officer held this sum to be 'agricultural income' by his order - On appeal, it was affirmed - Revision u/s. 27 was dismissed - HC found these receipts to be agricultural income - Hence instant appeal - Whether Salami was a rent and could be called as revenue - Held, no - Salami was not rent and both parties proceeded on that basis and it could not be called revenue within definition of agricultural income u/s.2(1)(a) of the Act because it was a payment to landlord by tenant as a consideration for the transfer of a right in zamindari lands owned by landlord - It had therefore all the characteristics of a capital payment and is not revenue - Therefore, when a tenant paid salami he did so in order to get in return an estate in land owned by zamindar - Payments by way of salami made by lessees anterior to relationship of landlord and tenant as price for lessor agreeing to parting of his rights in an agricultural holding in favour of proposed lessee - Appeals allowed.
