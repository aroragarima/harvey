Deepak Gulati v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 334; (2013) 7 SCC 675; AIR 2013 SC 2071; 2013 CRLJ 2990; 2013 (3) CTC 567; 2013 (4) JCC 2680; JT 2013 (9) SC 105; 2013 (2) KLT 762; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1881; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 82; 2013 (2) OLR 684; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 96; 2013(7) SCALE 383; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 544

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consensual sex

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure -Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 417, 365, 375, 376 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 90, 114-A - Rape - Consensual sex - Conviction - Benefit of doubt - Legality - Appellant (accused) allegedly induced prosecutrix (PW7) to get her married and took her to a place and committed rape upon her, and committed u/ss. 365, 375, 376, 417 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 365, 376 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo RI for 7 years - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecutrix's consent was obtained on false promise of marriage by appellant and impugned conviction was justified - Held, admittedly, prosecutrix never raised any grievance before any person at any stage - In fact, she seemed to have submitted to the will of appellant, possibly in lieu of his promise to marry her - Thus, a question aroused with respect to whether, in light of facts and circumstances of instant case, appellant had an intention to deceive her from the very beginning when he had asked prosecutrix to leave for 'Ku' with him from 'K' - Undisputed facts were that prosecutrix was 19 years of age at the time of incident and she had inclination towards appellant, and had willingly gone with him to 'Ku' to get married -Appellant had been giving her assurance of the fact that he would get married to her - Physical relationship between parties had clearly developed with consent of prosecutrix, as there was neither a case of any resistance, nor had she raised any complaint anywhere at any time despite the fact that she had been living with appellant for several days, and had travelled with him from one place to another - Even after leaving hostel of 'Ku', prosecutrix agreed and proceeded to go with appellant to 'A', to get married to him there - Facts in instant case did not warrant that provisions of s. 114-A of the Act be pressed into service - There was a clear distinction between rape and consensual sex and in a case like instant, Court should very carefully examine whether accused had actually wanted to marry victim, or had mala fide motives, and had made a false promise to that effect only to satisfy his lust, as the latter fall within ambit of cheating or deception - There was a distinction between mere breach of a promise, and not fulfilling a false promise - Thus, Court should examine whether there was made, at an early stage a false promise of marriage by accused; and whether consent involved was given after wholly, understanding the nature and consequences of sexual indulgence - There might be a case where prosecutrix agree to have sexual intercourse on account of her love and passion for accused, and not solely on account of misrepresentation made to her by accused, or where an accused on account of circumstances which he could not have foreseen, or which were beyond his control, was unable to marry her, despite having every intention to do so - Such cases should be treated differently - An accused could be convicted for rape only if Court reach to a conclusion that intention of accused was mala fide, and that he had clandestine motives - In instant case, prosecutrix had left her home voluntarily, of her own free will to get married to appellant - According to version of events provided by prosecutrix, she had called appellant went with him to 'K' lake where they indulged in sexual intercourse - Prosecutrix did not raise any objection at that stage and made no complaints to anyone - Prosecutrix voluntarily became intimate with appellant - In facts and circumstances, charge of deceit/rape could not be leveled against appellant - Appellant was entitled to benefit of doubt - Impugned conviction and sentences awarded by Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
