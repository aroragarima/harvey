Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation and v Zakir Hussain

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 472; (2005) 7 SCC 447; (2005) SCC (L&S) 945; 2005 (4) AWC 3467; 2005 (107) FLR 105; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 63; JT 2005 (7) SC 512; 2005 (4) KLT 91; 2005 (3) LLN 1048; 2005 (4) RLW 2512; 2005(6) SCALE 641; 2005 (5) SLR 602; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 311(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 12, 17, 17(1), 18, 19, 2(k), 2-A, 31(2), 33(1), 33C(1), 33C(2) - Termination during period of probation - Civil suit whether maintainable? - Held, respondent was appointed as a conductor purely on ad hoc basis - Only remedy available to respondent was by way of reference under provisions of Industrial Disputes Act - Services were terminated by an order of termination simpliciter - Order innocuous without any stigma nor evil consequences - Order not open to challenge - Order of reinstatement not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
