Bank of India and Others v T. Jogram

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 786; (2007) 7 SCC 236; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 615; AIR 2007 SC 2793; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 109; 2007 (3) CLR 700; 2007 (115) FLR 15; JT 2007 (9) SC 584; 2007 (3) LLJ 363; 2007 (4) LLN 1; 2007(9) SCALE 549; 2007 (6) SCJ 66; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 763; 2007 (5) SLR 516

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Bank of India Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976

Summary: Bank of India Officer Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1976, reg. 3(1) - Alleged violation - Service - Punishment of compulsory retirement from service - Single Judge confirmed the punishment - Division Bench upset reasoning recorded by Single Judge by re-appreciating the evidence - Appeal against - Held, there are no allegations of procedural irregularities/illegality and also there is no allegation of violation of principles of natural justice - Allegation of malafide was not substantiated - It is well settled law that the allegation of malafide cannot be based on surmises and conjectures - It should be based on factual matrix - Order of Single Judge is restored - Appeals disposed of.
