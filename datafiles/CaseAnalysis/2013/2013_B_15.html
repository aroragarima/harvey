Babu @ Balasubramaniam and another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 387; (2013) 8 SCC 60; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 109; 2013 CRLJ 3649; JT 2013 (9) SC 148; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 736; 2013(8) SCALE 48

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/A1 and A2 were tried by Trial Court for offences punishable of murder and dowry harassment punishable u/ss. 498A and 302 r/w. s.34 of the IPC - A1 was convicted u/s. 498A of IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for three years and to pay a fine of Rs.500/-, in default, to undergo further rigorous imprisonment for two months - A1 was also convicted for offence punishable u/s.304 Part I of the IPC and sentenced to suffer rigorous imprisonment for seven years for the same - A2 was convicted for offence punishable u/s. 498A of the IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for three years and to pay a fine of Rs.500/-, in default, to undergo further rigorous imprisonment for two months - A2 was also convicted for offence punishable u/s.304 Part I r/w. s.109 of the IPC and sentenced to suffer rigorous imprisonment for seven years for the same - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction and sentence - Held, perusal of Forensic laboratory report disclosed that the stomach and intestine of the deceased were found to contain the poisonous substance but the liver and kidney did not contain the said substance - PW5 had stated that if poison had been consumed prior to the head injury, it would have reached the liver and kidney - PW5 had added that if poison was administered to a person when he was in an unconscious state there was a possibility that it would reach the stomach and intestine - Considering the medical evidence, particularly the evidence of PW5 that the head injury was anti-mortem and should have been inflicted prior to the consumption of poison and considering the other circumstances of the case, SC concurred with HC that A1 first caused the head injury to the deceased and when she became unconscious in order to create evidence to suggest that the deceased committed suicide, he administered poison to her - It reached her stomach and intestine but before it could reach the kidney and liver she died - When she succumbed to the head injury, the poison did not pass on to the liver and kidney - HC had rightly observed that it was the reason why there was no evidence of any resistance being offered by the deceased and no bruises were found on her lips - Trial court has convicted A1 for offence punishable u/s. 304 Part I of the IPC and not for offence punishable u/s.302 of the IPC on the ground that the deceased had suffered only one head injury - HC had concurred with Trial Court - SC also did not interfere with the impugned order - Appeal disposed of.
