Kum. Michael v Regional Manager, Oriental Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 719; (2013) 14 SCC 774; 2013 (97) ALR 538; 2013 (2) AWC 1268; JT 2013 (3) SC 311; 2013 (5) KarLJ 117; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 139; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 966

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Entitlement of

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement of - Appellant/claimant was eight years old, met with an accident and was admitted in hospital - Appellant filed claim petition before Tribunal - Tribunal accepted evidence on record and awarded compensation - Dissatisfied with order passed by Tribunal, appellant filed appeal before DB - DB confirmed judgment of Tribunal and partly allowed the appeal - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal seeking enhancement of compensation - Whether compensation awarded by Tribunal and DB was just and reasonable - Held, having noted various disadvantages suffered by appellant by virtue of accident, SC was convinced that appellant was entitled for still higher amount than what was granted by Tribunal and DB on account of pain and sufferings as well as loss of amenities - Though it would be impossible to make precise assessment of pain and suffering of appellant considering age at which appellant met with accident and consequent disability and also taking note of debridement of better prospects in life of appellant due to physical disability suffered, SC determined compensation in sum of Rs. 4,00,000/- as claimed by appellant Rs. 2,80,000/- towards pain and sufferings and permanent disability, Rs. 20,000/- towards medical expenses, conveyance, nourishing food and attendant charges, Rs. 1,00,000/- towards loss of amenities - Impugned judgment of DB and award of Tribunal was modified, granting compensation of Rs. 4,00,000/-, enhanced compensation comes to Rs. 3,00,000/- with interest - Appeal allowed.
