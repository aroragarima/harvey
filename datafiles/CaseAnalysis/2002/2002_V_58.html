Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai v Virgo Steels

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1054; (2002) 4 SCC 316; AIR 2002 SC 1745; 2002 (81) ECC 13; 2002 (141) E.L.T. 598; JT 2002 (3) SC 558; 2002 (49) RLT 634; 2002(3) SCALE 341; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 934; 2002 (3) SLT 42; 2002 (3) Supreme 142

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Person Concerned, Importation, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Jammu and Kashmir (Government Servants) Prevention of Corruption Act, 1962

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 28 - Notification No. 210/82 - Whether Tribunal had erred in holding that non-issuance of show cause notice as required u/s. 28 of Customs Act had vitiated proceeding initiated by Collector of Customs? - S. 28 only provides for procedural aspect for recovery of duty, hence, any irregularity committed by a proper Officer in following procedure laid down in s. 28 would not denude that Officer of his jurisdiction to initiate action for recovery of escaped duty but it may make such proceedings initiated by that Officer voidable - Held, issuance of a notice u/s. 28 is a mandatory requirement of that Section - (B) Can a mandatory requirement of a statute be waived by party concerned? - Even though a provision of law is mandatory in its operation if such provision is one which deals with individual rights of person concerned and is for his benefit, said person can always waive such a right - Further held, right of notice u/s. 28 being personal to person concerned, same can be waived by that person - Appellant shall be entitled to costs payable by M/s. Virgo Steels, Mumbai - C.A. No. 3711 of 2000 is allowed.
