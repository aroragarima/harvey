Dharappa v Bijapur Co-Operative Milk Producers Societies

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1013; (2007) 9 SCC 109; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 703; AIR 2007 SC 1848; 2007 (6) AWC 6262; 2007 (2) CLR 942; 2007 (113) FLR 1159; 2007 (4) KarLJ 161; 2007 (2) LLJ 844; 2007 (3) LLN 139; 2007(6) SCALE 307; 2007 (6) SCJ 741; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Industrial Disputes, Andhra Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1964, Industrial Disputes (Karnataka Amendment) Act, 1987, Karnataka Co-operative Societies (Amendment) Act, 1976, Karnataka Co-operative Societies (Second Amendment) Act, 1997, Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 25-F - Industrial Disputes (Karnataka Amendment) Act, 1987, s. 10(4-A) Karnataka Co-operative Societies (Amendment) Act, 1976, s. 70 -operative Societies (Second Amendment) Act, 1997, s. 70 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - (A) Whether jurisdiction of Labour Court under ID Act, was barred by s. 70 of KCS Act with reference to co-operative societies and if so, from when? - Held, no - Even though cl. (d) was added in s. 70(2) with effect from 20-1-1976, s. 70(1) did not exclude or take away jurisdiction of Labour Courts, Industrial Tribunals.under ID Act to decide an industrial dispute between a society, its employees - Consequently, even after insertion of cl. (d) in s. 70(2) with effect from 20-1-1976, Labour Courts and Industrial Tribunals, under ID Act, continued to have jurisdiction to decide disputes between societies and their employees - Jurisdiction of Labour Courts and Industrial Tribunals to decide disputes between co-operative societies and their employees was taken away only when sub-s. (1) and sub-section (2)(d) of s. 70 were amended by Act 2 of 2000 and amendment received assent of President on 18-3-2000 and was brought into effect on 20-6-2000 - (B) Even if Labour Court had jurisdiction, whether appellant was entitled to file an application u/s. 10(4-A) of ID Act in respect of a cause of action which occurred in 1978? - Intent of s. 10(4-A) is to give a right to aggrieved workman to challenge termination order within six months from date of accrual of cause of action and not to furnish an one time revival in regard to stale and non-existing claims - Therefore, a claim application of petitioner filed on 4-10-1988 in regard to alleged termination on 1-3-1980 for 19-2-1978 as fund by Labour Courts was not maintainable u/s. 10(4-A) of ID Act and could not have been entertained by Labour Court - Appeal dismissed.
