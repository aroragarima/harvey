Shakti Travel and Tours v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2576; (2002) 9 SCC 415; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1217; AIR 2001 SCW 2307; 2000 (4) CCR 121B; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 409; 2000 (4) Crimes 150; JT 2000 (7) SC 563; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 465A; 2000 (7) SLT 319; 2000 (7) Supreme 90

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Miscellaneous

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s.138 - Dishonour of Cheque - Quashing of complaint - Appellant/accused was alleged to issued cheque to complainant which was rejected while presented for encashment - Complaint u/s. 138 of Act was filed against appellant - Appellant approached HC for quashing complaint u/s. 138 of Act which was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant contended that on assertions made in paragraph 8 of complaint, it must be held that notice has not been served and, therefore, an application u/s.138 of Act could not have been maintained. Accused has a right to pay money within 15 days from date of service of notice. Only when it fails to pay, it is open for complainant to file a case u/s. 138 of Act. In complaint itself having not been mentioned that notice has been served, on assertions made in paragraph 8, complaint itself is not maintainable. Court accordingly quashes complaint. Appeal allowed.
