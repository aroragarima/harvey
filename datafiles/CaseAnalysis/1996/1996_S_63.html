Rama Murthy v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

23 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1570; (1997) SCC (Cr) 386; (1997) 2 SCC 642; AIR 1997 SC 1739; 1997 (3) Bom.C.R. 634; 1997 CRLJ 1508; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 106; 1997(1) SCALE 95; 1997 (1) Supreme 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Prisons Act, 1894, ss. 4, 37 and 59 - Jail reforms - Letter was ordered to be treated as a writ petition - Points under consideration of Supreme Court - (A) Over crowding - (B) Delay in trial - (C) Entrusting of duty of production of under trial prisoners before Court on remand dates, to Prison staff - (D) Torture and ill treatment of Prisoners - (E) Giving proper medical facilities and maintaining appropriate hygienic conditions in jails - (F) Need of complaint box in all jails, introduction of liberalisation of communication facilities, streamlining of jail visits - (G) Prison vices jail manual - (H) Introduction of more open air prisons - Held, Supreme Court directed authorities to take appropriate decision within six months - Supreme Court desired full implementation of its decision in previously decided case regarding release of prisoner in case of protracted trials - Directed to be considered within six months - Need to replace century old Prisons Act stated and question of framing of model new All India Jail Manual also directed to be examined - Need to take steps in that regard stated directed to be considered - A sympathetic approach is required rather than awarding hard punishment to them - Order accordingly.
