Dilawar Singh v Parvinder Singh @ Iqbal Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 715; (2005) 12 SCC 709; AIR 2006 SC 389; 2006 CRLJ 136; 2006 CRLJ 145; JT 2005 (9) SC 476; 2005 (4) KLT 1029; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 735; 2005(9) SCALE 200; 2006 (2) SCJ 24; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 19 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 190, 319, 482 - IPC, 1860, ss. 406, 498A - Appellant a public servant summoned for facing prosecution u/s. 13(2) - Review application dismissed with observation that there was no provision for review under CrPC - Appealed herein - Held, sanction for prosecution has to be granted with respect to a specific accused and only after sanction has been granted that Court gets competence to take cognizance of an offence punishable u/ss.7, 10, 11, 13, 15 alleged to have been committed by such public servant - Impugned order of HC directing summoning of appellant is wholly illegal and cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
