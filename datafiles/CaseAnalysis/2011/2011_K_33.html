Kerala State Housing Board and another v Kerala State Housing Board, Nellikode Housing Colony Allottees Assn. and others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 575; (2011) 9 SCC 653; 2011(6) ALL MR 474; 2011 (89) ALR 264; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 23; JT 2011 (11) SC 425; 2011 (4) KLT 84; 2011(10) SCALE 437; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 390

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 34 - Allotment of plots - Interest on payment of final price - Appellant Housing Board acquired land under a Housing Scheme and allotted acquired plots to various respondent allotees wherein appellant reserved its right to fix the final price with 15% interest thereto pending disposal of appeals by the landowners for enhanced compensation - Reference Court finalized the enhanced compensation to landowners in the year 1997 and appellant deposited the enhanced compensation in the court - However, appellant served the demand notices on the allottees for payment of the difference between the tentative price and the final price with interest only in the year 1999 - Respondent allottees filed writ petitions challenging the demand of interest on differential amount - Single Judge refused to interfere with the claim of interest on the differential amount till 1997, however, held that appellant Board was not entitled to any interest on the differential amount from the allottees for the period from 1997 till the date of service of individual account statements on allottees - Appellant Board filed appeal before the DB, that was dismissed subsequently by impugned order - Whether the appellant Board was entitled to charge 15% interest on differential amount - Held, reason why a clause in the agreements of sale executed by the Board and the allottees for payment of interest at the rate of 15% per annum on the differential amount between the tentative price and the final price of the land allotted to the allottees was inserted was that in the proviso to s. 34 of the Act it was provided that if the compensation for the acquired land or any part thereof was not paid or deposited within a period of one year from the date on which possession of the acquired land was taken, interest at the rate of fifteen per centum per annum should be payable from the date of expiry of the said period of one year on the amount of compensation or part thereof which had not been paid or deposited before the date of such expiry - Once the compensation was finalized by the Court and the enhanced compensation was paid or deposited in the year 1997, the Board was not liable for any interest under the proviso to s. 34 of the Act from the date of such payment or deposit - Since the purpose of stipulating the rate of interest of 15% per annum was to take care of the liability on the enhanced compensation provided in the Act and not to enrich the Board by recovery of high rate of interest from the allottees - However, HC was not right in taking a view that the appellant Board was not entitled to any interest for the period from the date of payment or deposit of the enhanced compensation in 1997 till the date of service of individual account statements on the allottees - Relevant clause in the agreements of sale required the Board to serve only a notice of demand on the allottee and such notice of demand should obviously indicate the tentative price and the final price as determined by the Board and the differential amount between the tentative price and the final price, which the allottee was required to pay along with interest - Clause did not stipulate that the individual account statements giving the details and calculations were also required to be served on the allottees by the Board - It was not disputed that notices of demand were served on the allottees not immediately after finalization of the compensation by the Court and payment or deposit of the enhanced amount by the Board in the year 1997, but after a period of more than an year some time in 1999 - During the period the allottees did not make payment of the differential amount between the tentative price and the final price, they retained the differential amount in their hands and used the same and the Board lost the opportunity to utilize that for its activities, the Board would be entitled to interest on the differential amount at a reasonable rate as had been held by SC in Chandigarh Housing Board, Chandigarh v. K.K. Kalsi & Ors, 2003 INDLAW SC 1782 - Hence, interest at the rate of 8% per annum on such differential amount between the tentative price and the final price would be reasonable, which the allottees should pay to the Board - Impugned HC orders were set aside and direction issued that the respondent allottees would be liable to pay interest to appellant Board at the rate of 8% on differential amount from the date of enhance compensation by the Board in 1997 till actual payment of the differential amounts by the allottees - Appeals partly allowed.
