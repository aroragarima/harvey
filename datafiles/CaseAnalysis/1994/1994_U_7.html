State Of Uttar Pradesh v U. P. University Colleges Pensioners' Association

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2077; (1994) 2 SCC 729; (1994) SCC (L&S) 747; AIR 1994 SC 2311; 1994 (27) ATC 179; 1994 (1) AWC 625; 1994 (1) CLR 982; JT 1994 (2) SC 569; 1995 (2) LLJ 72; 1994 (2) LLN 362; 1994(1) SCALE 744; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 171; 1994 (2) SLJ 52; 1994 (2) SLR 4; 1994 (1) UJ 596; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1067

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Commutation Of Pension, Computation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311, 14 and 53 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 1 Retirement benefits - Pension and gratuity - Liberalisation - Justiciable - Employees of aided colleges - Demanding retirement benefits similar to Govt. colleges teachers - Scheme for aided colleges pegging computation of pension - To pay at age of 58, retirement age of Govt. teacher, against 60 years retirement age of aided Colleges - Held, scheme not unreasonable or against public interest - Matter of policy - Cannot be objected - Pension is different from gratuity - Order accordingly.
