Commissioner of Income Tax, Hyderabad v Sri Rajareddy Mallaram

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1964 SC 825; [1964] 51 ITR 285; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 75; 1964 (1) SCJ 256; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1958 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s 23(4) - Notice - Income - Recovery of tax - Cancellation of assessment - B, P and R formed an association of persons called for sake of brevity - For Fasli year 1358 i.e. October 1, 1948 to September 30, 1949 Group carried on business - With end of Fasli year 1358 contracts came to an end - Business was discontinued and Group was dissolved - Group did not make a return of its income pursuant to general notice u/s. 22(1) of 1922 Act - Income-tax Officer issued a notice u/s. 34 of 1922 Act calling upon B of members of Group-to file a return of income of Group, but B failed to file return on due date - Income-tax Officer assessed taxable income of Group u/s. 23(4) at Rs. 51,000 and determined Rs. 8,826-14-0 as tax payable - Attempts made by Income- tax Department to recover tax from B having proved unsuccessful, Income-tax Officer issued a notice of demand addressed to R another member of Group - Latter applied u/s. 27 of 1922 Act for cancellation of assessment - Application was rejected by Income-tax Officer - Hence instant appeal -, Whether applicant was liable to pay tax under order - Held, By virtue of s. 44 personality of association was continued for purpose of assessment and Ch. IV applied thereto - For purpose of such assessment, procedure was applicable for assessment of income of association as it continued - A notice to appropriate person u/s. 63(2) would be sufficient to enable authority to assess to tax association - When association is existing and after it is dissolved there can be no partial assessment of income of an association, limited to share of member who is served with notice of assessment - Applicant was liable for amount of tax payable under order of assessment - Appeal allowed.
