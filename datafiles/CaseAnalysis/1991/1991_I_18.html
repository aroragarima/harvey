Messrs Barnagore Jute Factory Company and Others v Inspector of Central Excise and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 279; (1992) 1 SCC 401; 1992 (38) ECC 57; 1992 (38) ECR 1; 1992 (57) E.L.T. 3; JT 1991 (4) SC 475; 1991(2) SCALE 1163; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 95; 1992 (1) UJ 298

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982

Summary: Excise - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - Constitution of India, 1950, sch. 1 and 7 - Finance Act, 1982 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, ss. 2, 3, 6, 9 and 30 - Jute Manufactures Cess Rules, 1976, rr. 2 and 3 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Monopolies And Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 13, 55 and 37 - Notices were issued to jute manufacturers to pay thecess at specified rate on their products including jute twine and jute yarn, produced in their units - Cess was held payable even in respect of jute yarn and jute twine consumed within same factory/unit in manufacturing jute textiles - Jute manufacturers, challenging levy of cess filed Writ Petitions in High Court - Writ Petition dismissed by Single Judge - Division Bench also dismissed appeal - Whether jute twine and yarn captively consumed within same factory, though exempt from payment of Central Excise Duty, is yet liable to pay cess u/s.9 of Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951? - By making declaration contemplated by Entry 52 of List I in Seventh Schedule to Constitution, Parliament has placed control of industries specified in First Schedule in Union Govt. - Sub-heading 2, Textiles, made wholly or in part of Jute including jute twine and rope must be read along with s.9 of Act which empowers Central Govt. to levy cess on all goods manufactured or produced in any such scheduled industry - Manufacturers' factories, jute yarn is first produced and captively consumed in manufacture of jute textiles is of no relevance - Jute yarn is a product known to market - Held, s. 9(1) contemplates levy of cess only ad valorem - No warrant for doing so or for restricting the amplitude of the words used in the main limb of s. 9(1) - It is not necessary to do so for giving effect to the proviso - The proviso can be respected and given effect to even without reading such restriction into the main limb of s. 9(1) - It is open to the Central Govt. to adopt such basis as they may think appropriate for levy cess, so long as such levy does not violate the ceiling prescribed by the proviso - In this connection, it is well to remember that the Central Excise Act levies duties not only ad valorem but in several other ways - So far as the infringement of the ceiling prescribed by the proviso is concerned, no material has been placed in support thereof before the Court - Appeal dismissed.
