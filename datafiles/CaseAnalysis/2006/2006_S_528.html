Gajanand Agarwal v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 506; (2006) 12 SCC 131; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 568; AIR 2006 SC 3248; 2006 CRLJ 4618; 2006 (3) JCC 1823; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 313; 2006 (4) KLT 143; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 311; 2006(9) SCALE 378; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 304B, 34, 406, 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 173(8), 438, 82, 83 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142(1) - Bail granted to accused - Appeals against - Held, there is a need to indicate in order, reasons for prima facie concluding why bail was being granted particularly where an accused was charged of having committed a serious offence - Any order dehors of such reasons suffers from non- Application of mind - Appeals allowed.
