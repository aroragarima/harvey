Nishan Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2701; AIR 2008 SC 1661; JT 2008 (13) SC 648; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1766; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2147; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 302; 2008(3) SCALE 416; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 500

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 and 307 r/w. s. 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 319(4), 375 and 465 - Compliance of - 'X' was charged for commission of an offence u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC whereas 'Y' was charged u/ss. 307 and 302/34 of IPC - 'Z' was charged for commission of offence u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted 'X' of the charges u/s. 307 IPC, however, convicted him for commission of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court also convicted 'Y' for commission of the offence u/s. 302/34, however, acquitted him for commission of offence u/s. 307 IPC - Trial Court acquitted 'Z' for the charges framed against him - Appeal filed against orders of the Trial Court submitting that prosecution has not made compliance of s. 319(4) of CrPC. - Whether order of the Trial Court could be set aside on the ground that the prosecution has not made compliance of s. 319(4) of CrPC.? - Held, sub-section 4 of s. 319 CrPC. must be read with the residuary provision contained in of s. 375 of CrPC. - Complaint in regard to non compliance of statutory provisions must be made at the earliest opportunity - When a judgment is pronounced, a case must be made out that by reason of a procedural irregularity, failure of justice had occurred, which is not done in the instant case - Moreover, the first information report was lodged by 'A' - Keeping in the view the fact that each had a link with the other, it is difficult to hold that the appellants would be falsely implicated by 'A' whose son was one of the deceaseds - One of the accused may be acquitted as the prosecution witness might not have attributed any overt act to him - But it is well settled that acquittal of one accused itself would not lead to the conclusion that the entire prosecution case was false - Order of the trial court upheld - Appeal dismissed.
