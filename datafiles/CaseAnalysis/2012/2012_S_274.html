Arup Das and others v State of Assam and others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 436; (2012) 5 SCC 559; 2012 (2) AWC 1940; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 529; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 445; 2012 (3) SLR 489

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Selection Board - Vacant posts - Invalidating excess appointments - Respondent published selected list of candidates and sent candidates for training - Further, director sent three more lists, but same were not approved by Govt. - Petitioners challenged list in writ petition on ground that when vacancies were available, there was no bar in same being filled up from select list of candidates by petitioner - Single Judge dismissed said writ petitions - Order passed by Single Judge was challenged by petitioners in writ appeal before Division Bench of HC - Writ appeal were dismissed by Division Bench of HC - Hence, instant Special Leave Petition - Whether appointments could be made in Govt. service beyond number of vacancies advertised - Held, it was well-established that authority could not make any selection/appointment beyond number of posts advertised, even if there were larger number of posts available than those advertised - However, principle behind said decision was that if that was allowed to be done, such action would be entirely arbitrary and violative of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution, since other candidates who had chosen not to apply for vacant posts which were being sought to be filled, could have also applied if they had known that other vacancies would also be under consideration for being filled up - Therefore, state could deviate from advertisement and make appointments in posts falling vacant thereafter in exceptional cases or in an emergent situation, and that too, by taking policy decision in that behalf - It was in such situation, that fresh advertisement was required to be published for filling up remaining number of vacancies after vacancies advertised were filled up - Thus, instead of invalidating excess appointments, relief could be molded in such manner so as to strike just balance, if it was in interest of State and in interest of person seeking public employment - Hence, no extra-ordinary and/or exceptional circumstances exist in present case requiring filling up of vacant seats available after filling up seats advertised which deal with exception and not rule - Order accordingly.
