Income Tax Officer v Ch. Atchaiah

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1861; (1996) 1 SCC 417; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 302; AIR 1996 SC 883; 1996 (130) CTR 404; [1996] 218 ITR 239; JT 1995 (9) SC 441; 1995(7) SCALE 186; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 543; [1996] 84 TAXMAN 630

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Land Acquisition

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 4 and 148 - AY 1964-65, 1965-66 and 1968-69 - In the assessment proceedings relating to AY 1965-66, ITO included a certain sum , treating it as the capital gain, in the income of the respondent - Again, in the assessment relating to AY 1968-69, the enhanced compensation falling to the share of respondent was brought to tax as capital gain - Whether Association of persons charge of income tax has to be brought to tax as income of association of persons ? - Held, ITO has no option to tax either association of persons or its members individually or for that matter tax firm or its partners individually - Consideration of interest of revenue has no place in the scheme of new Act - Appeal allowed.
