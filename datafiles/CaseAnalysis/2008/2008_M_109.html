M/S Synco Industries Ltd v Assessing Officer, Income Tax,Mumbai & Anr

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 444; (2008) 4 SCC 22; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1738; [2008] 299 ITR 444; JT 2008 (4) SC 1; 2008(4) SCALE 263; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 919; [2008] 168 TAXMAN 224

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Co-Operative Society, Set Off, Export House, Manufacture And Sale, Business Loss, Such Profits, Carry Forward, Carry Forward And Set Off, Unabsorbed Depreciation, Body Of Individuals, Insurance Premia, Set Off Of Loss, Finance Act, 1965, Finance (No.2) Act, 1967, Income Tax Act, 1948

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1948 - Order that that gross total income must be determined by setting off against the income, the business losses of the earlier years, before allowing deduction under Chapter VI-A and if the resultant income is ''Nil'', then the assessee cannot claim deduction under Chapter VI-A - Appeals against - Held, gross total income of the assessee has got to be computed in accordance with the Act after adjusting losses etc. and if the gross total income so determined is positive then the question of allowing deductions under Chapter VI-A arises, but not otherwise - Appeals dismissed.
