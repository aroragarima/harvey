Messers Om Prakash Bhatia v Commissioner of Customs, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 493; (2003) 6 SCC 161; AIR 2003 SC 3581; 2003 (88) ECC 457; 2003 (109) ECR 336; 2003 (155) E.L.T. 423; JT 2003 (5) SC 279; 2003 (57) RLT 371; 2003(5) SCALE 197; 2003 (4) SLT 389; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 412; 2003 (4) Supreme 553

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Exchange Control & FDI

Keywords: Importation, Material Particulars, Any Prohibition, Exportation, Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992, Foreign Trade (Regulation) Rules, 1993, Ascertainable, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, Import Control Order, 1955, Shipping Bill & Bill of Export (Form) Regulations, 1991

Summary: [A.] Trade - Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss.11, 111(d), 113(d), 14, 14(1), 2(33), 2(41), 50, 76 - Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947, s. 3 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss.14, 18, 18(1), 2(41) - Foreign Trade (Development And Regulation) Act, 1992, s.11(1) - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, s.12(1) - Foreign Trade (Regulation) Rules, 1993, r. 11 - Shipping Bill & Bill of Export (Form) Regulations, 1991 - (1) Whether over-invoicing of goods for export would mean attempt to export 'prohibited goods - (2) whether, while exporting goods, exporter has to give value of goods as provided u/s. 14, Customs Act, 1962 or value of goods which he exports to receive on sale of goods in overseas market - Held, (1) in cases where export value is not correctly stated, but there is intentional over-invoicing for some other purposes, not mentioning true sale consideration of goods, then it would amount to violation of conditions for import/export of goods purpose may be money laundering or some other purpose, but it would certainly amount to illegal/ unauthorised money transaction - In any case, over-invoicing of export goods would result in illegal/irregular transactions in foreign currency - (2) Section 14 would be applicable for determining value of goods for purpose of tariff or duty of customs chargeable on goods In addition, by reference, it is to be resorted to and applied for determining export value of goods as provided u/s. 2(41) - This is independent of any question of assessability of goods sought to be exported to duty - Hence, for finding out whether export value is truly stated in shipping bill, even if no duty is leviable, it can be referred to for determining true export value of goods sought to be exported.
