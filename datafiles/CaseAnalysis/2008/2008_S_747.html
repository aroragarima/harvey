Novartis India Limited v State Of West Bengal And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2620; (2009) 3 SCC 124; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 595; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 836; 2009 (1) AWC 533; 2009 (1) ESC 28; JT 2008 (13) SC 12; JT 2008 (13) SC 12B; 2009 (2) LLJ 9; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 289; 2008(15) SCALE 470; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 918; 2009 (1) SLR 675

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Back wages - Entitlement - Appellant/Company issued transfer orders to respondents/Sales Representatives to another station - Respondents failed to report to the transferred station - Appellant terminated the services of respondents, however, respondents raised an industrial dispute in the meantime - Government referred the dispute to Industrial Tribunal - Industrial Tribunal passed an award of reinstatement since appellant failed to conduct domestic enquiry before the termination - Appellant challenged the award before HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition, however, DB held that the Government which referred the dispute to Industrial Tribunal was not the appropriate government - Respondents challenged the matter before SC - SC remitted the matter back to DB for fresh disposal on merits - DB dismissed the appellant's appeal - Whether respondents are entitled to the back wages - Held, order of the Industrial Tribunal attained finality - Respondents were unemployed during the interregnum period - Fact that respondents survived and did not die of starvation itself could not be a ground for denying back wages to them - Respondents were in private employment and not in public employment and their services were permanent in nature - Termination of respondents' services was held to be illegal since it was done without conducting enquiry prior to the issuance of the termination order - Order of discharge was, thus, void ab initio and therefore, respondents are entitled to the back wages - No interference on impugned order of DB - Appeal dismissed.
