Abdul Sayeed and others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 743; (2010) 10 SCC 259; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3691; JT 2010 (10) SC 434; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 699; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 550; 2010(9) SCALE 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 147, 148, 149 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - FIR u/ss. 302, 147, 148, 149 and 307 of IPC was lodged against 17 accused including 5 appellants - It was alleged that accused persons assaulted deceased - Trial Court convicted appellants - On appeal, HC convicted appellants u/ss. 302/34 of the IPC while conviction u/ss. 147 and 148 of IPC awarded by the Trial Court were set aside - Present appeals - (A) Evidence of injured Witness - Held, testimony of the injured witness accorded a special status in law and hence deposition of the injured witness should be relied upon unless there are strong grounds for rejection of his evidence - In the present case, PW.2 had given clear description of the entire incident and also stood fully corroborated by the evidence of PW.1 and PW.4 - Hence, depositions so made cannot be brushed aside merely because there have been some trivial contradictions or omissions - (B) Contradiction between medical evidence and ocular evidence - Effect thereof - Held, though the ocular testimony of a witness has greater evidentiary value vis-a-vis medical evidence, when medical evidence makes the ocular testimony improbable, that becomes a relevant factor in the process of the evaluation of evidence - However, where the medical evidence goes so far that it completely rules out all possibility of the ocular evidence being true, the ocular evidence may be disbelieved - In the instant case, a very large number of assailants attacked one person, thus the witnesses cannot be able to state as how many injuries and in what manner the same had been caused by the accused - In such a fact-situation, discrepancy in medical evidence and ocular evidence is bound to occur - (C) Effect of failure to frame proper charges - It was submitted that there was no charge framed u/s. 34 IPC by the Trial Court and appellants and other co-accused have been charged u/s. 147/148 IPC and all of them have been acquitted for the said charges - Whether it was permissible for the HC to convict the appellants with the aid of s. 34 of IPC - Held, unless the accused is able to establish that the defect(s) in framing the charge(s) has caused real prejudice to him; that he was not informed as to what was the real case against him; or that he could not defend himself properly, no interference is required on mere technicalities - Further, there is no bar in law on conviction of the accused with the aid of s. 34 of IPC in place of s. 149 of IPC if there is evidence on record to show that such accused shared a common intention to commit the crime and no apparent injustice or prejudice is shown to have been caused by application of s. 34 of IPC in place of s. 149 of IPC - Absence of a charge under one or the other or the various heads of criminal liability for the offence cannot be said to be by itself prejudicial to the accused, and before a conviction for the substantive offence without a charge can be set aside, prejudice will have to be made out - (D) Whether appellants could be convicted with the aid of s. 34 of IPC? - Held, ingredients of s. 34, i.e., that the accused had acted in furtherance of their common intention is required to be proved specifically or by inference, in the facts and circumstances of the case - S. 34 intends to meet a case in which it is not possible to distinguish between the criminal acts of the individual members of a party, who act in furtherance of the common intention of all the members of the party or it is not possible to prove exactly what part was played by each of them - In the present case, HC rightly proceeded in the matter while setting aside the conviction of the appellants u/s. 147/148 IPC and convicting them with the aid of s. 34 of IPC - No reason to interfere with the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
