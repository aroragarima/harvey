C.W.T. v Sharvan Kumar Swarup And Sons

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1065; (1994) 6 SCC 623; AIR 1994 SCW 5145; 1994 (122) CTR 380; [1994] 210 ITR 886; JT 1994 (6) SC 446; 1994(4) SCALE 413; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 750; [1994] 76 TAXMAN 620

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Direct Tax Laws (Amendment) Act, 1989 - Wealth-tax Act, 1957 - Wealth Tax Rules, 1957 - Assessee, an HUF, contended that its immovable property be valued, applying the r. 1-BB, of the 1957 Rules - Wealth Tax Officer rejected the claim and proceeded to value the immovable properties - Appeals were allowed, and the Appellate authority held in favor of the applicability of r. 1-BB, of the 1957 Rules - Tribunal upheld the same - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the r. 1-BB, of the 1957 Rules, was a provision of substantive law, or merely procedural - Held, that the r. 1-BB, of the 1957 Rules, was essentially a rule of evidence as to the choice of one of the well accepted methods of valuation in respect of certain kinds of properties with a view to achieving uniformity in valuation and avoiding disparate valuations resulting from application of different methods of valuation respecting properties of a similar nature and character - R. 1-BB, of the 1957 Rules, laid down the formula for determining the market value of a house used wholly or mainly for residence - Since the rule provided a formula or mechanical method of valuation, it was difficult to agree with the Revenue that it was substantive in character - Appeals dismissed.
