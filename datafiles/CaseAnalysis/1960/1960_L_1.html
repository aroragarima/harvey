Â L. Hazari Mal Kuthiala v Income Tax Officer, Special Circle, Ambala Cantt. and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 311; AIR 1961 SC 200; [1961] 41 ITR 12; 1961 (1) SCJ 617; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 892

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 5(5), 5(7A) - Patiala Income Tax Act, 34, 5(5), 5(7A) - Reassessment - Forest lessees - Timber merchants - Firm carried business as forest lessees and timber merchants - Prior to the integration of the State, income of firm duly assessed, and tax also paid - 2 Ordinances were issued - S. 13 of 1950 Act, repealed the Income tax laws obtaining in area of Part B States except for purposes of levy, assessment and collection of income tax and super tax - Income tax Office issued notice purporting to be u/s. 34 of 2001 Act to the appellant firm calling upon it to file a return of its income and total world income, as income was under assessed - Commissioner of Income tax ordered that assessment of the appellant firm would be done by Income tax Officer, Ambala and not by the Income tax Officer, Patiala, who ordinarily would be the competent authority u/s. 64 - Appellant filed writ seeking prohibition, certiorari, quo warranto in respect of reassessment of the income of the firm for the account year - HC dismissed the petitions - Hence instant appeal - Whether transfer of case was required as stated by Commissioner - Held, no doubt that s.5(7A) of Act authorizes, Commissioner to transfer individual cases - Any case from one Income tax Officer subordinate to him to another, such transfer may be made at any stage of the proceedings clearly indicate this. Sub-section (7A) of Act not applicable here, because in respect of the cognate subsection of 1922 Act it was ruled by this Court that it could apply to pending case only - Appeal dismissed.
