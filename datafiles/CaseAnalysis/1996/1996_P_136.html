Union of India and Others v Praveen Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2135; (1997) 9 SCC 78; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 875; AIR 1997 SC 170; 1996 (4) CCC 127; JT 1996 (9) SC 624; 1996(7) SCALE 819; 1996 (2) SCJ 388; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 201; 1996 (7) Supreme 775; 1997 (1) UJ 78

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 10, 5-A, 11 and 17(4) - Notification u/s. 4(1) of Act - Enquiry u/s. 5-A and declaration u/s. 6 - Single Judge held that there was no justification for invoking urgency cl. in s. 17(4) of Act and quashed the declaration u/s. 6 - Whether invoking urgency cl. u/s. 17(4) of Act is justified? - It is now settled legal position that decision on urgency is an administrative decision and is a matter of subjective satisfaction of the appropriate Govt. on basis of material available on record - Therefore, there was no need to pass any reasoned order to reach conclusion that there is urgency so as to dispense with the enquiry u/s. 5-A in exercise of power u/s. 17(4) - Since acquisition is for shifting of timber business from walled city to outskirts of city, shifting itself is for urgent purpose, viz., to relieve the traffic congestion in the walled city - Under those circumstances, exercise of power u/s. 17(4) cannot be said to be unwarranted in this case - Held, that mere mention in notification that land is likely to be needed for public purpose does not take away power of appropriate Govt. to exercise power of urgency cl. u/s. 17(4) of Act - Appeal allowed.
