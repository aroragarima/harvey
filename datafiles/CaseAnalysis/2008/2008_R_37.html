Rajasthan Lalit Kala Academy v Radhey Shyam

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1054; (2008) 13 SCC 248; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 919; 2008 (6) AWC 6246; 2008 (2) CLR 1000; 2008 (118) FLR 950; JT 2008 (8) SC 311; 2008 (3) LLJ 562; 2008 (3) LLN 654; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 94; 2008(10) SCALE 561; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 820; 2008 (5) SLR 566

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Back-Wages

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Held, in the event of retrenchment of a workman, employed in any industry, continuously for not less than one year under an employer, compliance with the provisions of s. 25-F - Finding of the Labour Court to the effect that the appellant has failed to adduce any evidence in support of their plea that an amount of Rs.1800/- had been paid to the respondent, does not suffer from any perversity - Though, illegality of the order of termination is one of the prime considerations for determining the question and quantum of back-wages, but it cannot be the sole criterion - instead and in place of direction for reinstatement and back-wages a sum Rs.3 lakhs is directed to be paid to the respondent by way of compensation - Appeal partly allowed.
