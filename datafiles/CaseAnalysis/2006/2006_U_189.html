Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Cessation Of Interest

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11, 12, 15, 16, 18, 23(1), 23(1)(A), 28, 34, 4, 54, 6 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 141, 142 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 21, rr. 1, 2, ss. 2(2), 2(9), 23(1) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 59, 60, 61 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 57 - What is rule of appropriation in execution of money decrees? - Is rule same in case of an award decree under Land Acquisition Act or, is there anything in Land Acquisition Act, 1894 as amended by Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, Act 68 of 1984 making that rule inapplicable or not wholly applicable? - Held, essential ratio in Prem Nath Kapur on appropriation being at different stages is justified though if at a particular stage there is a shortfall, awardee decree holder would be entitled to appropriate same on general principle of appropriation, first towards interest, then towards costs and then towards principal, unless, of course, deposit is indicated to be towards specified heads by judgment debtor while making deposit intimating decree-holder of his intention - Order accordingly.
