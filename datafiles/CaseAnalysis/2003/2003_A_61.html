Harish Verma and Others v Ajay Srivastava and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 761; (2003) 8 SCC 69; AIR 2003 SC 3371; 2003 (53) ALR 317; 2003 (6) AWC 4733; 2003 (4) ESC 604; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 79; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 322; 2003(7) SCALE 598; 2003 (6) SLR 32; 2003 (5) SLT 610; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 833; 2003 (6) Supreme 706

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug

Summary: [A.] Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14, 15(4) - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 33, 20 - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, reg. 9 - Qualifying marks - Inservice candidates - Writ challenging constitutional validity of first proviso to regn. 9 and seeking its being declared ultra vires in its applicability to in- Whether any reserved quota could not be separately specified for in-service candidates merely because such in-service category in not mentioned in art.15(4)? - Whether prescribing of a separate quota for in-service candidates withstands test of reasonable classification under art.14? - Held, admissions given to such of in-service candidates who have secured marks less than minimum prescribed by reg 9 framed by Medical Council of India are struck down and set aside counseling shall have to be done afresh to extent necessary - There would be some delay in commencement of post-graduation studies and to some extent 2002 and 2003 batches would overlap - However, it is a situation which cannot be avoided It is an inevitable consequence for which successful candidates for year 2002 and 2003, i e those who will be held entitled for admission in post-graduation course of studies consequent upon this judgment, cannot be made to suffer for no fault of theirs - It will be for State of Rajasthan, if necessary then in consultation with Medical Council of India, to sort out difficulties and to run regular courses of studies.
