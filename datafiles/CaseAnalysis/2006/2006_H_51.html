Centrotrade Minerals and Metal. Inc v Hindustan Copper Limited Per S.B. Sinha

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 608; (2006) 1 SCC 245; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1770; JT 2006 (5) SC 507; 2006(5) SCALE 535; 2006 (4) SCJ 217; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1992, Doctrine Of Public Policy, Arbitration Act, 1991, Indian Arbitration Act, 1890

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Limitation Act, 1963 - Carriage of Goods by Sea Act, 1992 - Arbitration Act, 1991 - Indian Arbitration Act, 1890 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Constitution of India, 1950 - (A) Whether validity of a two tier arbitration agreement can be upheld having regard to provisions of 1996 Act? - Held, a two tier arbitration entered into before or after coming into force of Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Act invalid, permissible in India - (B) Whether it is valid, on interpretation of cl. 14 of agreement, can it be said that ICC Arbitrator sat in appeal against award of Indian Arbitrator? - Further held, ICC arbitrator dealt with correctness of first award and was not acting as a mere second arbitrator but rather as an appellate forum - (C) Whether ICC award is a Foreign Award or not? - Further held, award of ICC arbitrator was not a domestic award but a foreign award as rightly held by learned Singled Judge of HC - (D) Whether HCL was given proper opportunity to present its case before ICC arbitrator? - U/s. 48(1) (b) enforcement of a foreign award can be refused - Further held, HCL could not effectively present its case before ICC arbitrator and therefore enforcement of ICC award should be refused in view of s. 48 (1) (b) of Act - Directed ICC arbitrator to pass a fresh award within three months from date of commencement of fresh arbitral proceedings - Appeals disposed of.
