State Of Rajasthan Th. Secy. Home Dept v Abdul Mannan and another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 516; (2011) 8 SCC 65; 2011 CRLJ 4351; 2011 (3) JCC 1899; JT 2011 (9) SC 48; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 254; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 663; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 1099

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 313, 379 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134(1)(a), 134(1)(b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.34, 148, 149, 302, 304, 324, 449 - Communal violence - Common Intention - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused were convicted by Trial Court for communal violence u/ss. 302, 148, 324, 149 and 449 IPC - Aggrieved by judgment of Trial Court, all three accused preferred an appeal before HC prayed for setting aside of judgment of Trial Court and claimed acquittal - HC acquitted all accused - Hence, instant appeals - Whether judgment of acquittal by HC was sustainable - Held, common object to commit murder had been fully proved - Out of mob of 50-60 persons only 7 to 10 persons had broken door of house and some of them had climbed wall to enter house of deceased - These persons had raised slogan 'maro! maro!' and thereafter had inflicted injuries upon body of deceased - Common intention could even develop at spur of moment when three accused, as duly identified, were actively inflicting injuries on body of deceased - Therefore, they not only caused injuries to vital body parts of deceased, including head injury, but kept on inflicting injuries even after deceased had fallen to ground - Unlawful assembly and in furtherance to their common object and intent, assaulted and caused injuries to vital parts of bodies of deceased, ultimately resulting in their death - Every member of that assembly was guilty of same offence, which other members had committed in prosecution of common object - Accused had inflicted injuries after raising slogan and had commonly participated in committing offence which resulted in death of deceased - Appeals allowed.
