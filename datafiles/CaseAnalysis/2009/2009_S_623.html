Rathinam @ Rathinan v State Of Tamil Nadu And Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2143; (2011) 11 SCC 140; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 289; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 3; 2010(11) SCALE 6; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Rape and Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant, along with other accused, allegedly raped and murdered the deceased - Trial Court acquitted the appellant - Respondent/State filed appeal before the HC - HC reversed the Trial Court order and convicted the appellant for rape and murder - Hence the present appeal by the appellant/accused - Whether the conviction is sustainable - Held, statement of eye-witness was comprehensively different vis-a-vis the statement he had given in the sessions trial of other accused persons - Eye-witness did not give any information about the appellant to Investigating Officer during the investigation of case against other accused persons - Eye-witness referred the name of appellant in the first time to the second Investigating Officer about three and half years after the incident - Trial Court, therefore, weighed several reasons with the eye-witness while discarding his evidence - However, HC projected the evidence of eye-witness and believed his evidence although he was declared as hostile in the trial of other accused persons - HC was wrong in relying upon the uncertain evidence of the eye-witness - Further, HC wrongly chose to draw an inference without material that eye-witness had kept away as eye-witness felt that he may be implicated in the murder - Therefore, impugned DB order is set aside and appellant is acquitted - Appeal allowed.
