Bobbili Ramakrishna Raju Yadav and others v State of Andhra Pradesh, Represented by its Public Prosecutor High Court of Hyderabad, Andhra Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 37; (2016) 3 SCC 309; AIR 2016 SC 442; 2016 CRLJ 1141; 2016 (2) JCC 826; JT 2016 (1) SC 298; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1365; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 519; 2016 (1) OLR 594; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 853; 2016 (2) RLW 1005; 2016(1) SCALE 350; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 103; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 225

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Prosecution, Dowry, Matrimonial, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4, 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Quashing of proceedings - Allegations in complaint - Victim died under suspicious circumstances and so, case was registered against appellants u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC read with ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - Later, on private complaint filed by respondent no. 2, father of victim, against appellants u/s. 6 of the Act, Magistrate took cognizance of offence - Aggrieved, appellants filed petition before HC to quash complaint contending that said complaint did not disclose offence and that FIR was already registered against them at Bangalore city - However, vide impugned order, HC dismissed said petition holding that previous case was registered u/ss.304B and 498A of IPC read with ss.4 and 4 of the Act, whereas subsequent case is registered u/s. 6 of the Act, which is independent of previous case - Hence, instant appeal - Whether allegations in complaint and other materials accompanying complaint disclose offence punishable u/s. 6 of the Act.

Held, there are no specific allegations against appellant nos. 2 to 6 that dowry articles were entrusted to them and that they have not returned dowry amount and articles to victim. Further, there are no allegations that those dowry articles were kept in Vizianagaram and used by appellant nos. 2 to 6 who were separately living away from couple in Bangalore. Even though complainant alleged that dowry amount was paid at the house of accused, there are no specific allegations of entrustment of dowry amount and articles to appellant nos. 2 to 6. Hence, facts and circumstances of the case and also uncontroverted allegations made in complaint had not constituted offence u/s. 6 of the Act against appellants 2 to 6 and there is no sufficient ground for proceeding against appellant nos. 2 to 6. Further, even though criminal proceeding u/s. 6 of the Act is independent of criminal prosecution u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act, in the absence of specific allegations of entrustment of the dowry amount and articles to appellant nos. 2 to 6, continuation of criminal proceeding against appellant nos. 2 to 6 is not just and proper and so, impugned order is quashed qua appellant nos. 2 to 6. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - As per decision of SC, when a prosecution at the initial stage was asked to be quashed, the test to be applied by the Court was as to whether the uncontroverted allegations as made in the complaint prima facie establish the offence.
