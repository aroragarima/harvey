Secretary, Indian Tea Association v Ajit Kumar Barat and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 136; (2000) 3 SCC 93; (2000) SCC (L&S) 321; AIR 2000 SC 915; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 58; 2000 (2) AWC 1139; 2000 (1) CLR 625; JT 2000 (2) SC 70; 2000 LabIC 706; 2000 (1) LLJ 809; 2000 (2) LLN 25; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 79; 2000(1) SCALE 515; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 787; 2000 (3) SLJ 31; 2000 (1) SLR 683; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 857

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Reimbursement

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Reference - Question whether employee concerned was workman? - Govt. considering salary, allowances drawn by employee and his nature of work - Power to sanction expenses incurred by his office were included - Reference refused on conclusion that employee was not workman - Held, Mandamus to Govt. to make reference could not be issued u/art. 226 of Constitution of India.
