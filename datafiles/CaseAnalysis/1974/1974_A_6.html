Â Aflatoon and Others v Lt. Governor of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 256; (1975) 4 SCC 285; AIR 1974 SC 2077; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 802; 1974 UJ 765

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Society

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi (Control of Building Operations) Act, 1955 - Delhi Development Act, 1957, ss. 7, 12(1), 12(3) and 15 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5A and 23 - Constitution of India, arts. 19(1)(f) and 31(5) - Whether acquisition of property for planned development of Delhi was illegal? - Acquisition was challenged on ground that public purpose specified in notification issued u/s. 4, namely, planned development of Delhi was vague as neither a Master Plan nor a Zonal Plan was in existence on date of notification and as purpose specified in die, notification was vague - Held, fact that actual development is permissible in an area other than a development area with approval or sanction of local authority did not preclude Central Govt. from acquiring land for planned development under Act - S. 12 is concerned only with planned development - It has nothing to do with acquisition of property; acquisition generally precedes development - Appellants and writ petitioners cannot be allowed to challenge validity of notification u/s. 4 on ground of laches and acquiescence - Mere fact that after acquisition Govt. proposed to hand over, or, in fact, handed over, a portion of property acquired for development to cooperative housing societies would not make acquisition one for company - In light of averments in counter affidavit filed in writ petitions here, it is difficult to hold that it was cooperatives which provided fund for acquisition - Merely because Govt. allotted a part of property to cooperative societies for development, it would not follow that acquisition was for cooperative societies, and therefore, Part VII of Act was attracted - Appeals dismissed.
