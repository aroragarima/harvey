State of Andhra Pradesh v Kandimalla Subbaiah and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 493; AIR 1961 SC 1241; 1961 CRLJ 302; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 198; 1961 (2) SCJ 686; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Trial - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 234(1), 235(1) and 239 - Indian Penal Code, ss. 109 and 120 B - Whether as per s. 234(1) of CrPC, not more than three offences of same kind committed within space of 12 months can be tried at same trial? - S. 235(1) provides that if in any one series of acts so connected together as to form same transaction more offences than one are committed by same person, he may be charged with and tried at one trial for every such offence - Where a number of offences are committed by several persons in pursuance of conspiracy it is usual to charge them with those offences as well as with offence of conspiracy to commit those offences - Held, no - Where offences were committed in course of same transaction, limitation prescribed by s. 234(1) cannot operate - A number of offences were committed by several person in pursuance of conspiracy - All person can be tried together in view of s. 239 - Matter remitted back for framing fresh charges - Appeal allowed.
