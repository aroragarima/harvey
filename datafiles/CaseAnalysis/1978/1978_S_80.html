Sarwan Simgh & Ors Etc v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 74; (1978) 4 SCC 111; (1978) SCC (Cr) 549; AIR 1978 SC 1525; 1978 CRLJ 1598; 1978 CrLR(SC) 491; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 383

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal- Fatal Accidents Act, 1855 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 304 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 357 - Five accused armed with various weapons - Caused injuries to deceased resulting into his death - Common object - Whether accused were liable of offence u/s. 302, IPC, 1860? - On a consideration of injuries in instant case it could not be held that common object of unlawful assembly was to cause death - Unexpected quarrel was between members of same family over a dispute as to water rights, it was not possible to hold that offence u/s 302 r/w s. 149 was made out - Held, On a consideration of circumstances and nature of injuries it was not possible to hold that common object of assembly was to cause bodily injury which is sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death -It can be said that common object of assembly was to cause bodily injury as is likely to cause death -The accused therefore, held guilty u/s. 304 (1) r/w s. 149 - Order Accordingly.
