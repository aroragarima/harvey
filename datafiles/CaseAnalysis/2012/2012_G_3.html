Sinnamani & Anr. v G. Vettivel & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 154; (2012) 5 SCC 759; AIR 2012 SC 2372; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 128; 2012(3) ALL MR 981; 2012 (93) ALR 254; 2012 (4) AWC 3415; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 156; 2012 (3) CTC 571; JT 2012 (5) SC 42; 2012 (6) MLJ(SC) 117; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 235; 2012 (116) RD 829; 2012 (2) RLW 1908; 2012(5) SCALE 235; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 rr. 1 to 3, 5 to 7 and 26, O. 7 r. 11 - Trust Act, 1882, ss. 61, 62, 65, 66 and 92 - Conversion of original petition into suit - Trust original petition was filed before the Principal District by the appellants who were beneficiaries of six trusts Judge u/ss. 61, 62, 65, 66 and 92 of the Act r/w O. 6 rr. 1 to 3, 5 to 7 and 26 of CPC - During the pendency of the original petition, respondent Nos. 1 to 14 and 16 filed interlocutory applications under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC requesting the court to reject the said Trust original petition - Applications were allowed - Appeals were filed thereagainst - HC held that the District Court was justified in allowing the applications filed under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC rejecting Trust original petition and it was also ordered that the Trust original petition could not be converted as a civil suit - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Trust original petition could be converted as a civil suit - Held, when statutory provision clearly stated as to how the suit had to be instituted, it could be instituted only in that manner alone, and no other manner - Act provides for filing of a suit then suit alone could be filed and when it provides for original petition then original petition alone could be filed and there was no question of conversion of original petition to that of a civil suit or vice-versa, especially in the absence of a statutory provision under the Act - In the instant case, in order to execute the trust, the right was only to file a suit and not any original petition - Further, the Act contained no such enabling provision to convert the original petition into a suit - Hence, Trust original petition could not be allowed to be converted into a suit - Appeals disposed of.
