South Central Railway Employees Co-Operative Credit Society Employees Union v B. Yashodabai and others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Kurian Joseph, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 832; (2015) 2 SCC 727; 2015(1) ALL MR 931; JT 2014 (13) SC 529; 2015 (4) MahLJ 505; 2015 (3) MPLJ 329; 2014(13) SCALE 632; 2015 (2) SLR 573

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Reversion by virtue of SC order - Interference by HC - Permissibility - SC, in C.A. No.4343 of 1988, held that, in the matter of promotion, policy of reservation was not to be followed and the persons who had been wrongly promoted on the basis of reservation policy had to be reverted - Appellant Society reverted respondent employees - Respondents filed writ petition challenging reversion - Single Judge set aside reversion order - DB upheld Single Judge order on appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC has committed a grave error by taking a different view than the one which had been taken by SC in C.A. No.4343 of 1988, especially when the rules governing the promotion policy had not been amended after the SC judgment. A review application had been filed in C.A. No.4343 of 1988 and the same had been rejected and therefore, the judgment delivered by SC in C.A. No.4343 of 1988 had become final. Once in pursuance of a judgment delivered by SC, orders had been issued by appellant to its employees who had been wrongly promoted, the HC could not have held that the orders were not valid because there were certain other factors which had made the promotions given to the concerned employees valid. The HC should not have considered any other factor especially when SC had come to a final conclusion that the policy with regard to reservation in the matter of promotion to the employees was not legal and proper. Hence, Single Judge as well as the DB committed a serious error in law by not following the judgment delivered by SC and by quashing and setting aside the impugned orders. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When a higher court has rendered a particular decision, the said decision must be followed by a subordinate or lower court unless it is distinguished or overruled or set aside. By not following the law laid down by SC, the HC or the Subordinate Courts would also be violating the provisions of art. 141 of the Constitution of India, 1950.
