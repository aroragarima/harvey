High Court of Madhya Pradesh Thru. Registrar and Others v Satya Narayan Jhavar

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20612; (2001) 7 SCC 161; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1087; AIR 2001 SC 3234; AIR 2001 SCW 3112; 2001 (3) CLR 540; 2001 (91) FLR 626; JT 2001 (6) SC 368; 2001 (34) LabIC 3281; 2001(5) SCALE 233B; 2001 (3) SCJ 135; 2001 (3) SLR 645; 2001 (5) SLT 789; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 532; 2001 (6) Supreme 106

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madhya Pradesh Government Servants' General Conditions of Service Rules, 1961 - Madhya Pradesh Judicial Service (Classification, Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1955, r. 24 - Whether respondent was entitled to be deemed confirmation as a civil judge? - Held, r. 24 does not provide for a deemed confirmation on expiry of maximum period of probation, on other hand it contemplates an order of confirmation to be passed by Appropriate Authority, if it is satisfied about fitness of probationer, if probationer has passed departmental examination - Mere continuance of probationer after considering his case for confirmation during period of probation and finding him unsuitable for confirmation by decision of Full Court, by no stretch of imagination can be construed to be a confirmation by implication - Appeal allowed.
