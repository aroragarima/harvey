Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation v Cork Manufacturing Co

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1572; (2007) 8 SCC 120; AIR 2008 SC 56; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 85; 2007 (4) AWC 3774; JT 2007 (10) SC 473; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2700; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 78; 2007(10) SCALE 515; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Resumption - Maintainability - Appellant/defendant allotted plot to respondent by a letter dated 24-09-1987 - Respondent/plaintiff entered into an agreement with appellant on 12-02-1988 for starting construction within three months and complete construction within one and half year from date of issuance of allotment letter - Appellant reserves right of resumption of plot in case of breach of contract - Appellant issued notice for non compliance of terms of agreement - Respondent filed suit before Trial Court which was allowed - Aggrieved by said order appeal was made before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether Resumption of plot allotted to respondent was illegal - Held, SC relied upon Sunder Lal & Son Vs. Bharat Handicrafts Pr. Ltd. 1967 Indlaw SC 395 it was stated that non realization of the importance of the documents due to inadvertence or lack of proper legal advice as noted hereinabove also would bring the case within the expression "other substantial cause" in o. 41 r. 27 of the 1908 Act - Appellant had failed to satisfy HC as to why legal notice which was admittedly lying with them could not be produced during all these years i.e. from 5-10-1995 till 31-1-2005 - Thus HC was not justified in rejecting application for acceptance of additional evidence at second appellate stage - Appeal dismissed.
