Swamy Shraddananda Alias Murali Manohar Mishra v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1403; (2007) 12 SCC 288; AIR 2007 SC 2531; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2622; JT 2007 (7) SC 403; 2007 (6) KarLJ 1; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 302; 2007(8) SCALE 1; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 616

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Doctrine Of Proportionality, Cross Case, Joint Account, Criminal Justice Act, 1991, Criminal Justice Act, 2003, Super Imposition, Character Of Accused

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Rarest of rare case - Deceased was a lady of great fortune - She separated from her husband and married appellant - From May 1991, deceased disappeared - Deceased daughter from her husband inquired about deceased from appellant but he kept on giving excuses about deceased not being present - A missing person complaint was lodged by daughter - Upon Investigation, appellant stated in detail as to manner in which he had killed his wife and disposed of her dead body - Her body was exhumed and articles and clothing recovered were identified to be of deceased - Whether appellant is liable to be convicted for murder of deceased? - Held, yes, deceased was murdered in cold blood and buried in backyard of her house - This is case of a cold-blooded, calculated, diabolical murder by appellant of an innocent lady who came from a good family, but unfortunately fell into his clutches of appellant - Motive behind murder was to grab property of deceased - Even though exact method of murder has not been established by prosecution, no doubt that it was appellant who murdered deceased - Murder fell into category of rarest of rare cases - Death sentence awarded - Appeal dismissed.
