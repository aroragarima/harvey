Karam Singh Sobti and Another v Shri Pratap Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, K.C. Das Gupta, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 369; AIR 1964 SC 1305; 1963 CurLJ 174; 1964 (66) PLR 210; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 57 - Ajmer Rent Control Act 1952, s. 13(1)(c)(i) - Eviction - Legality - Whether respondent-landlord is entitled to rely on exception provided by cl. (c), sub-cl. (i), of proviso to sub-s. (1) of s. 13 of Control Act of 1952? - Learned Subordinate Judge found (i) that sub-letting had commenced not later than November, 1950, and (ii) that thereafter respondent-landlord continued to receive rent with full knowledge of sub-letting - On these findings he held that respondent-landlord was not entitled to avail himself of exception stated in cl. (c), sub-s. (1) of s. 13 of Control Act of 1952 - New Act came into force on February 9, 1959 - High Court held that there was no evidence to justify finding of learned Subordinate Judge that respondent-landlord had acquiesced in sub-letting of room in favour of appellant, and case being one where there is no evidence to justify a finding, it was open to HC in interfere in revision - Revision before it had to be disposed of in accordance with provisions of Control Act of 1952 - Held, respondent-landlord is entitled to benefit of cl. (c), sub-cl. (i), of proviso to s. 13(1) of Control Act of 1952 and first proviso to sub-s. (2) of s. 57 of Control Act of 1958 does not stand in his way - He is, therefore, entitled to succeed, as appellant has failed to make out any acquiescence by landlord to sub-letting in question - Therefore, HC rightly allowed petition in revision and restored decree for possession made by Trial Court - Appeal dismissed.
