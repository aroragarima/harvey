Shri Ishar Alloy Steels Limited v Jayaswals Neco Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 3, 72 and 138 - Meaning of 'the bank' u/s. 138(a) - Which bank cheque is to be presented for purposes of attracting penal provisions of s. 138 of Act? - Use of words 'a bank' and 'the bank' in s. 138(a) indicator of legislature's intention - 'The' refers to a specified bank, not any bank - Thus, 'the bank' u/s. 138(a) means drawee bank and not bank where cheque is presented for collection - Also, as per ss. 3, 72 and 138 cheque be presented at drawee bank to held drawer criminally liable u/s. 138 - Held, cheque not presented before drawee bank within statutory period, therefore, court had no jurisdiction to proceed - Appeal allowed.
