Gurudwara Sahib v Gram Panchayat Village Sirthala and another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 603; (2014) 1 SCC 669; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 3; 2014(7) ALL MR 813; 2013 (5) AWC 4939; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 322; JT 2013 (12) SC 580; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 193; 2014 (4) MahLJ 74; 2014(4) M.P.H.T. 389; 2014 (3) MPLJ 36; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 703; 2014 (122) RD 193; 2013(11) SCALE 564; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 46

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Suit-For declaration-Appellant claims ownership by adverse possession on ground that it is in possession of land in dispute for sufficiently long period-Relief of ownership by adverse possession denied holding that such suit not maintainable-Appellant is in possession of suit property since 13.4.1952 and has granted decree of injunction-It obviously means that possession of appellant cannot be disturbed except by due process of law-Even if plaintiff is found to be in adverse possession, it cannot seek declaration to the effect that such adverse possession has matured into ownership-Only if proceedings filed against appellant and appellant is arrayed as defendant then it can use plea of adverse possession as shield/defence-Though suit of appellant seeking relief of declaration dismissed, in case respondents file suit for possession and/or ejectment of appellant, it would be open to appellant to plead in defence that appellant had become owner of property by adverse possession-Appellant at liberty to plead findings to the effect that appellant is in possession of adverse possession since 13.4.1952 to operate as res judicata.
