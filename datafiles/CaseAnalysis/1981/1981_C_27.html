Associated Cement Company Limited v Commercial Tax Officer, Kota and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 395; (1981) 4 SCC 578; (1982) SCC (Tax) 3; AIR 1981 SC 1887; 1981(3) SCALE 1338; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 563; 1981 (48) STC 466; 1981 TAXLR 3057

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1979 - Liability - Interest - Penalty - Delay - Assessee/public limited company carrying on business of manufacture and sale of cement filed returns of sales - Assessee did not include amount of freight in taxable turn-over in respect of goods sold - Assessee prepared revised returns with amount of freight as part of taxable turn-over to Commercial Tax officer - Assessing Authority levied penalty u/s.7-AA of 1979 Act and interest u/s.11-B of 1979 Act for delay in payment of tax - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee liable to pay interest u/s.11-B of 1979 Act - Held, assessee could not escape liability to pay interest u/s. 11-B(a) of 1979 Act on amount of tax either by delay filing of return or not filing it by filing return wrongly claiming that certain part of turnover was not taxable or by not disclosing part of taxable turnover - Statutory liability u/s.11-B(a) of 1979 Act arises in default in payment of tax within period allowed by law irrespective of doubt which assessee may be entertaining about liability to pay tax - It was clear that amount of tax due of freight charges was payable u/s. 7(2) of 1979 Act was not paid within period allowed, and s. 11-B of 1979 Act was clearly attracted and liability to pay interest as required by it arises - Assessee was liable to pay interest on tax u/s. 11-B of 1979 Act as it stood prior to tile amendment - Hence, assessee was directed to pay penalties and levy of interest under orders was dismissed - Appeal disposed of.
