Nirmal Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 236; (2000) 4 SCC 41; (2000) SCC (Cr) 740; AIR 2000 SC 1416; 2000 (2) CCR 7; 2000 (2) Crimes 101; 2000 CRLJ 1803; 2000 (1) JCC 379; JT 2000 (4) SC 85; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 465; 2000(2) SCALE 645; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 807; 2000 (3) SLT 373; 2000 (3) Supreme 164

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 299 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 33 - Evidence - Recording of statement in absence of accused - Admissibility of - Person died before service of summons to appear as witness - Statement recorded under s. 299 considered as evidence - Compliance of pre - requisite conditions in question - Legality - Held, facts suggest arrest of accused could not be procured - May be treated as admissible evidence.
