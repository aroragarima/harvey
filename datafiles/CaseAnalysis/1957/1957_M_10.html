Â Maulana Abdul Shakur v Rikhab Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1958 SC 52; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 88; 1958 SCJ 329; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 387

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Durgah Khwaja Saheb (Emeregency Provisions) Act, 1950

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 81 - Durgah Khwaja Saheb (Emeregency Provisions) Act, 1950, ss. 11(f), 20, (2), 6(2), 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 102(1)(a) - Religious Endowment Act, 1867, s. 7 - Whether successful candidate, appellant, held an 'office of profit' under Govt. ? - Held, no - Art. 102(1) states that a person shall be disqualified for being chosen as, and for being, a member of either House of Parliament- (a) if he holds any office of profit under Govt. of India or Govt. of any State, other than an office declared by Parliament by law not to disqualify its holder - Appellant was holding his appointment under a Committee which is a statutory body and such appointment cannot be called an appointment by or under control of Govt. of India nor is his salary paid out of revenues of Govt. but out of funds of Durgah Endowment - Appellant is neither appointed by Govt. of India nor is removeable by Govt. of India - Merely because Committee or members of Committee are removeable by Govt. of India or Committee can make bye-laws prescribing duties and powers of its employees cannot convert servants of Committee into holders of office of profit under Govt. of India - Appeal allowed.
