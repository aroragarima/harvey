Workmen of Firestone Tyre and Rubber Company Of India Private Limited v Firestone Tyre and Rubber Company

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 406; (1976) SCC (L&S) 504; (1976) 3 SCC 819; AIR 1976 SC 1775; 1976 (32) FLR 170; 1976 LabIC 1154; 1976 (1) LLJ 493; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(kkk), 10(1), 25C, 25E, 33C(2) - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 - Insustrial Dispute - Case not governed 1947 Act - Power of management to lay-off workmen - Powers of industrial tribunal and labour Court to award compensation - Held, laid-off without any power under contract of service - Power to award some compensation exist u/s. 10 but not u/s. 33C(2) of 1947 Act - Appeals allowed.
