Jatinder Singh and Others v Ranjit Kaur

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20322; (2001) SCC (Cr) 354; (2001) 2 SCC 570; AIR 2001 SC 784; 2001 (1) CCR 181; 2001 (1) CGLJ 333; 2001 (1) Crimes 218; 2001 CRLJ 1015; 2001 (1) JCC 133; JT 2001 (2) SC 198; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 692; 2001(1) SCALE 431; 2001 (1) SCJ 514; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 707; 2001 (1) SLT 752; 2001 (1) Supreme 417; 2001 (1) UC 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Bigamy - Second Complaint - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 494 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 300 - Whether a complaint, once dismissed by a Magistrate for default, can be refiled? - Held, yes - No provision which debars complainant from preferring a second complaint on same allegations if first complaint was not disposed on merits - S. 300 of CrPC, 1973, provides that dismissal of a complaint or discharge of an accused is not an acquittal for this purpose - However, when a Magistrate dismisses complaint on merits, a second complaint on same facts is not maintainable unless there are exceptional circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
