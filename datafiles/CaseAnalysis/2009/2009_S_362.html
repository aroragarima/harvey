Dhanapal v State By Public Prosecutor, Madras

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1083; (2009) 10 SCC 401; 2009 CRLJ 4647; 2009 (4) JCC 2914; JT 2009 (12) SC 45; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1350; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 353; 2009(12) SCALE 127; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.34, 302 and 307 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s.2(a) - Appellant along with other accused were acquitted by Trial Court of offences punishable u/ss.307 and 302 r/w s.34 of IPC - On appeal, HC set aside acquittal recorded by Trial Court - Whether HC was justified in setting aside order of acquittal passed by Trial Court? - It is a well settled legal position when view which has been taken by Trial Court is a possible view, then acquittal cannot be set aside by merely substituting its reasons by HC - Court is under bounden duty and obligation to deal with evidence as it is - No improvement or rewriting of evidence is permissible - Held, view taken by Trial Court was certainly a possible or a plausible view - HC was not justified in weaving out a different and new prosecution version - HC in impugned judgment was not justified in interfering with well reasoned judgment and order of Trial Court - Impugned judgment of HC contrary to settled legal position and deserves to be set aside - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeal disposed of.
