Jayawant Dattatray Suryarao and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20884; (2001) 10 SCC 109; (2002) SCC (Cr) 897; AIR 2002 SC 143; AIR 2001 SCW 4717; 2001 (4) CCR 277; 2002 (1) Crimes 31; 2002 (2) CRJ 628; 2002 CRLJ 226; JT 2001 (9) SC 605; 2001(8) SCALE 36; 2001 (5) SCJ 180; 2001 (7) SLT 664; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 54; 2001 (8) Supreme 362

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 27, 5 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 401, 433(b), 433-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 30 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 120-B, 149, 212, 302, 307, 34 - Prisons Act, 1894, s. 59 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 15, 3(2), 3(2)(i), s. 5, s. 6 - Act of Terrorism - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused was alleged to have made preparation, such as procuring sophisticated weapons and had committed murder of Prisoner who was undergoing treatment in hospital and two police constables on duty - It was also alleged that they attempted to commit murder of six other persons who was on duty - All of them were charged for offence punishable u/s. 120-B IPC r/w ss. 3(2)(i), 3(2)(ii), 3(3), 3(4), 5 and 6 of the 1987 Act and s. 302 r/w s. 34, ss. 149, 307 r/w s. 34, and s. 27 r/w s. 5 of the 1959 Act - Designated Court convicted accused noS. 2, 6 & 7 and acquitted of accused no. 1,3, 4, 8,9 and 10 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Designated Judge was justified in convicting accused under the 1987 Act - Held, Designated Judge had rightly tried and convicted accused for offences punishable under the 1987 Act - Shoot out at Govt. Hospital of 1500 beds at midnight causing death of three persons and injuries to six others was, nothing but an act of terrorism which could not be termed as simple act of gang rivalry - Whether criminal violent act was committed with intent to strike terror in people or section or people would always depend upon facts of each case - There would hardly be any case where there could be direct evidence to find out intention of accused - It was to be inferred from manner and mode adopted while committing act and its after effect including fear psychosis - Hence, irresistible inference should be drawn that crime was committed to create terror and also to take revenge - Such act creates terror in minds of people or section of the people so that the targeted persons would succumb to the dictates or extortion because of fear for survival - Effect of fear psychosis also can be seen from statement of President of Municipal Corporation (accused) that he was compelled to use his official vehicle along with police constable for the movement of the accused. In such cases, we should accept the ground reality that it would hardly be possible to get evidence of eye-witnesses - Further held, that evidence brought on record, designated Court rightly acquitted accused no.1, 3, 4, 8,9 10 - Court has rightly held that confessional statements without there being sufficient corroborative evidence would not be sufficient for convicting accused for offences for which they were charged - Appeals disposed of.
