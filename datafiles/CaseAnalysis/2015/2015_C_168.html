Commissioner, Customs and Central Excise, Aurangabad v Roofit Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 289; (2015) 8 SCC 229; 2015 (319) E.L.T. 221; JT 2015 (5) SC 589; 2015 (5) MLJ 91; 2015(5) SCALE 470; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 716

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Excisable Goods, Normal Price, Place Of Removal

Summary: Excise - Alleged under valuation of goods - Demand of Duty - Justification - Respondent is holder of Central Excise Registration for manufacture of pipes under Act - Further, respondent entered into agreements for manufacture of goods, however, appellant authority alleged that on basis of general intelligence collected, assessee was indulging in evasion of central excise duty by not computing assessable value of finished goods properly to extent that it was deducting amount of freight, insurance and unloading charges from price excisable goods though place of removal of finished goods was different from factory gate - Adjudicating authority vide its order in original confirmed demand of amount on account of under valuation and on ground that place of removal finished goods was buyer's premises and not at factory gate - Tribunal vide impugned order allowed appeal of appellant - Hence, present appeal of appellant.

Held, terms and conditions of agreement state that goods were to be delivered at place of buyer and it is only at that place where acceptance of supplies was to be effected. Price of goods was inclusive of cost of material, central excise duty, loading, transportation, transit risk and unloading charges etc. Even transit damage/breakage on assessee account which would clearly imply that till goods reach destination, ownership in goods remain with supplier namely assessee. As per 'terms of payment' clause contained in procurement order, 100% payment for supplies was to be made by purchaser after receipt and verification of material. Further, there was no money given earlier by buyer to assessee and consideration was to pass on only after receipt of goods which was at premises of buyer. Hence, SC held that sale of goods did not take place at factory gate of assessee but at place of buyer on delivery of goods in question. Hence, order passed by appellant authority is set aside and order passed by Adjudicating authority is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When sale of goods did not take place at factory gate of assessee but at place of buyer on delivery of goods in question, assessee is not liable to pay excise duty under Act.
