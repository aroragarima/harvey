Excise Superintendent, Malkapatnam v K.B.N. Visweshwara Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2321; (1996) 6 SCC 216; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1420; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 174; AIR 1996 SCW 3979; 1997 (75) FLR 353; JT 1996 (9) SC 638; 1997 (1) LLJ 567; 1997 (1) LLN 8; 1996(6) SCALE 676; 1996 (3) SLR 649; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 73; 1996 (7) Supreme 201

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Employment Exchange Act 1959

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employment Exchange Act, 1959 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Selection - Through medium of employment exchange - Constitutionality - Tribunal held that sponsorship of candidates through medium of employment exchange was valid and not violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Contention of respondents consistent with principles of fair play, justice and equal opportunity - Many candidates unable to have names sponsored, though their names are either registered or are waiting to be registered in employment exchange, with result that choice of selection is restricted to only such of candidates whose names come to be sponsored by employment exchange - Many a deserving candidate is deprived of right to be considered for appointment to a post under State - Held, employment exchange should sponsor names of candidates to requisitioning departments for selection strictly according to seniority and reservation - Equality of opportunity in matter of employment would be available to all eligible candidates - Appeals disposed of.
