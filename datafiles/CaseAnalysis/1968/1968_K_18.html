Karnesh Kumar Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1968 SC 1402; 1968 CRLJ 1655; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 201 and 302 - Dispute over irrigation issues - Appellants killed two deceased with spears and fire-weapons and burned their bodies - Eye-witness to incident were family members of deceased - Whether appellants can be convicted based upon testimony of family members? - Held, Yes - Testimony of family members is reliable has to be construed with caution as they are interested witnesses - In present case, their testimony is supported by circumstantial evidence also - Conviction justified - Death sentence awarded to appellants commuted to life imprisonment - Appeal dismissed.
