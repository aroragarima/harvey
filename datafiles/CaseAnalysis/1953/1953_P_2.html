State of Bombay v Pandurang Vinayak Chaphalkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1953 SC 244; 1953 (55) BomLR 526; 1953 CRLJ 1094; 1953 SCJ 330; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 773; 1976 TAXLR 1275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cotton Cloth and Yarn (Control) Order, 1945, Bombay General Act, 1904, Bombay Building (Control on Erection) Act, 1948, Bombay Building (Control on Erection) Ordinance, 1948, Cotton Cloth and Yarn (Control) Order, 1943

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Bombay Building (Control on Erection) Act, 1948, ss.9(2),4 - Bombay Building (Control on Erection) Ordinance, 1948 - Bombay General Act, 1904, ss.25,7 - Erecting Cinema Hall - Necessary Permission - Punishable Offence - Acquittal - On 15-8-1948 Respondents erected a cinema hall in Ratnagiri without obtaining permission from Controller of Buildings - Respondents were charged u/s. 9(2) r/w s.4 of 1948 Act - Trial Court held that Act did not extend till Ratnagiri and no permission was needed and acquitted Respondents - Appellant State filed appeal before HC and order of acquittal was upheld - Appellant State aggrieved by order of HC filed Appeal for question of law to be decided as a Test Case as decision of HC would have far reaching effects if left unchallenged - Whether Appellant State was entitled to relief against order passed by HC in acquitting Respondents - Held, Respondent started construction after commencement of Act XXXI of 1948 - Construction placed by HC on language of ss.15 of 1948 and 25 of 1904 Act was erroneous and full effect was not given - Notification issued on 15-1-1948 under ordinance had continued in force under act XXXI of 1948 and it stood extended to other areas in State to extent indicated in notification - On undertaking given by Attorney-General not to proceed further order of acquittal was not set aside - Appeal allowed.
