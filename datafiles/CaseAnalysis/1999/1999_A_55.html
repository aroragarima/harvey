Ajay Jadhav v Government of Goa

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 698; (1999) 9 SCC 4; AIR 2000 SC 451; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 108; 1999 (2) CLR 865; 1999 (83) FLR 393; JT 1999 (6) SC 596; 1999 (4) LLN 73; 2000 (1) SLJ 223; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 371; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 169

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether appellant was entitled to higher salary in consonance with post held by him? - It is not that appellant was having less qualification than prescribed in circular or that his appointment was for a fixed tenure of 3 or 6 months as a stop gap arrangement - Appellant has also pointed out with reference to a comparative chart filed in proceedings that teachers similarly situated and having same qualifications are getting higher pay-scale - Held, appellant has wrongly denied pay scale respondent was entitled to other teachers with same qualification are getting higher pay-scale of Grade-I Teacher - It appears that it is a clear case of discrimination - Appeal allowed.
