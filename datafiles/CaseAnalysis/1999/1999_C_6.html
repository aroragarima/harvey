Rajathi v C. Ganesan

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 593; (1999) 6 SCC 326; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1118; AIR 1999 SC 2374; 1999 (2) AllCriR 1663; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 227; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 776; 1999 CRLJ 3668; JT 1999 (5) SC 29; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1047; 1999 (6) Supreme 201

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Minority, Adoption & Guardianship

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 18 (A) - Maintenance to wife - Whether fact that husband is living with another woman justifies refusal of wife to live with husband? - Whether wife entitled to maintenance as it amounts to 'neglect or refusal by husband to maintain her'? - (B) Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 125 and 482 - Inherent powers - Petition against grant of maintenance to wife High Court concluded that wife was living separately without reasonable cause and interfered with paltry amount of Rs. 200/- - Whether proper held? - (A) proviso to sub-s. (3) would squarely apply and justify refusal of wife to live with her husband Statement of wife that she is unable to maintain herself would be enough and it would be for husband to prove otherwise - (B) HC minutely examined evidence and interfered with paltry amount granted by Trial Court as maintenance and confirmed in revision not proper more so, when only prima facie view of matter is to be taken by Trial Court while granting maintenance - Appeal allowed.
