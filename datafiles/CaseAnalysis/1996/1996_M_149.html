Vazir Glass Works Limited v Maharashtra General Kamgar Union and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1150; (1996) 2 SCC 118; (1996) SCC (L&S) 421; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 553; AIR 1996 SC 1282; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 553; 1996 (72) FLR 335; JT 1996 (1) SC 129; 1996 LabIC 1061; 1996 (1) LLJ 962; 1996 (1) LLN 430; 1996(1) SCALE 181; 1996 (2) SCJ 239; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 110; 1996 (1) SLR 749; 1996 (1) Supreme 305; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 435

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. XVII, r. 1 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938, s. 25(o) - Whether or not on the date when reference to Industrial Tribunal was made, the State Govt. had jurisdiction to entertain the Review Application and pass order on the same? - Held, the impugned decision to the effect that the State Govt. would cease to have jurisdiction to review its order on the application for closure of an industrial unit after expiry of one year from the date of rejection of the application for permission to close, is correct - Further, after expiry of one year from the date of rejection of the application for permission for closure, the appellant-company was entitled to make a fresh application - Directed to publish the award within one month from the date of receipt of the award on being transmitted to the concerned Industrial Tribunal - Further directed that date of receipt of the award by the Industrial Tribunal on transmission from this Court will be deemed to be the date of the award - Appeal disposed of.
