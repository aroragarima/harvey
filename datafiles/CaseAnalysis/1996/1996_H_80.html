Hindustan Aeronautics Limited v Smt A. Radhika Thirumalai

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1714; (1996) 6 SCC 394; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1427; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 809; AIR 1997 SC 123; JT 1996 (9) SC 197; 1997 (1) LLJ 492; 1997 (1) LLN 132; 1996(7) SCALE 566; 1997 (1) SLJ 105; 1996 (6) SLR 21; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 357; 1996 (7) Supreme 527

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Denied - Challenged - Respondent filed a writ petition praying for a writ of mandamus directing the appellant to provide suitable permanent employment to the respondent by creating a supernumerary post - Appellant contended that no vacancy was available since there was a ban on fresh recruitment and, therefore, appointment could not be given to the respondent - Single Judge directed the appellant to consider the candidature of the respondent on compassionate grounds to any suitable post in Class III or Class IV only and, if found suitable and eligible to appoint her to such post within a period of 2 months - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, no vacancy is available for making appointment in medical department. All that can be said is that in the event of the appellant making fresh appointment on a Class III or Class IV post the application of the respondent for appointment on such post shall be given due consideration in accordance with her ranking in the waiting list. Appeal allowed.
