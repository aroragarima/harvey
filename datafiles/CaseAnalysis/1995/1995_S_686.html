Butu Prasad Khumbhar And Ors. v Steel Authority Of India Ltd. Andors.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 405; (1995) Supp2 SCC 225; 1995 (3) CCC 257; JT 1995 (3) SC 428; 1995(2) SCALE 539; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 76; 1995 (2) SLR 451; 1995 (2) UJ 129

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Socio-Economic - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Petitioners were residents of villages which formed part of Rourkela - Whether respondents were bound to give employment to all the erstwhile residents and even their descendants and in any case to treat them preferentially for employment as they or their members of families were, displaced due to setting up of Rourkela Steel Plant even though they were given market value for their land acquired? - Held, giving employment to 20% out of 247 persons may take longer time and since the age bar has been put at 35 it would be appropriate if the SAIL expedited the absorption of these persons by increasing their number from 20% to 40% each year - Thus, subject to the observations made in respect of 247 persons identified by the respondents - Petition dismissed.
