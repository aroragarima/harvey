Â Fertilizer Corporation Kamgar Union (Regd.), Sindri and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.D. Koshal, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 243; (1981) 1 SCC 568; AIR 1981 SC 344; 1981 (42) FLR 192; 1980 LabIC 1367; 1981 (1) LLJ 193; 1981 (1) LLN 288; 1981(3) SCALE 1137; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 43A - Sale of company - Hampering interest of workers - Justifiability - By the petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution, the petitioners challenged the legality of the sale of certain plants and equipment of the Fertilizer Factory, whereby the highest tender submitted by respondent No.4 in the sum of Rs. 4.25 crores was accepted - Relief sought by the petitioners was that the respondents should be directed not to sell away the plant and equipment, that they should be asked to withdraw their decision to sell the same and that the said decision should be quashed as being illegal and unconstitutional - Whether petition for disallowing respondent to sell company was sustainable - Held, in the case a worker, who, clearly, had an interest in the industry, brought this action regarding an alleged wrong-doing by the Board of Management - Art. 43A of the Constitution conferred, in principle, partnership status to workers in industry and SC could not, therefore, be deterred by technical considerations of corporate personality to keep out those who sought to remedy wrongs committed in the management of public sector - It took SC to the question of justiciability of questions like sale of public property by public bodies - Certainly, it was not part of the judicial process to examine entrepreneurial activities to ferret out flaws - SC was least equipped for such oversights - Nor, indeed, was it a function of the judges in SC's constitutional scheme - SC did not think that the internal management, business activity or institutional operation of public bodies could be subjected to inspection by the Court - To do so, was incompetent and improper and, therefore, out of bounds - Nevertheless, the broad parameters of fairness in administration, bona fides in action, and the fundamental rules of reasonable management of public business, if breached, would become justiciable - If a citizen was no more than a wayfarer or officious intervener without any interest or concern beyond what belonged to any one of the 660 million people of this country, the door of the court would not be ajar for him - But citizen belonged to an organisation which had special interest in the subject matter, if he had some concern deeper than that of a busybody, he could not be told off at the gates, although whether the issue raised by him was justiciable might still remained to be considered - SC, therefore, took the view that instant petition would clearly have been permissible u/art. 226 of Constitution - Petition allowed.
