Bhavesh Jayanti Lakhani v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1176; (2009) 9 SCC 551; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3417; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 920; 2009(11) SCALE 467; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Human Right, Extradition, Appeal allowed, Interpretation Of Statute, Criminal Matter, Counter Affidavit, Notification, Legislative Power, Reasonable Restriction, Individual Case, Air Force, Foreign State, Surveillance Register, Citizens Of India, International Law, Municipal Law, Treaty, Civil Liberty, Trivial Nature, Police Regulation, Rearrest, Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: (A) Criminal - Family & Personal - Extradition Act, 1962 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 13 and 44A - Matrimonial dispute - Custody of child -Appellant and respondent No. 6 Indian citizens - Appellant after marrying respondent No. 6 moved to California and a daughter was born - Application before Probate and Family Court of Massachusetts for grant of divorce filed by respondent No. 6 and for custody of daughter - Order of temporary custody of child passed - Appellant came to India with child in violation of court custody orders - Decree for divorce as also custody of child was passed by Court of Massachusetts - Respondent No. 6 filed application for custody of child before Family Court at Mumbai - Family Court granted custody of child to respondent No. 6 - On appeal, HC passed order of stay - Atlanta City Police and American Court in meanwhile issued a warrant of arrest (red corner notices) against appellant which was transmitted through INTERPOL to Govt. of India - Appellant filed a writ petition questioning legality of said warrant - (A) Whether having regard to concept of sovereignty Executive Govt. of India can enforce a warrant passed by Probate and Family Court, Massachusetts? - Held, Act as also treaties entered into by and between India and foreign countries are admittedly subject to municipal law - Municipal Laws of a country reign supreme in matters of Extradition - It is thus for State concerned to take a decision in regard to such Notices, keeping in view Municipal Laws of country - HC wrong in holding that a Red Corner Notice should not be interfered with - (B) Custody of Child - Held, although Family Court at Bombay for all intent and purposes relying on or on basis of order passed by Massachusetts Court directed custody of girl in favour of her mother, Bombay HC stayed operation thereof - Appellant therefore, must be held to be in lawful custody of his daughter unless any other or further order is passed by a court of competent jurisdiction - (C) Whether having regard to provisions contained in ss. 44A and 13 of CPC, is foreign judgment enforceable in India? - Held, no - State does not seek for enforcement of custody and/ or restrain order passed by Probate and Family Court, Massachusetts in view of rigours contained in ss. 13 and 44A of CPC - Appeal allowed.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 362 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 - Whether C.B.I established under DPSE Act has authority to deal with INTERPOL notices? - Held, C.B.I. for intent and purport exercises power of NCB in terms of Treaty and thus derive its authority from INTERPOL itself - C.B.I. is designate of INTERPOL in India and endorsing warrants is an essential attribute of procedure for implementation of Treaty - Power of C.B.I. and its delegated authority namely, State police to keep a person under surveillance; arrest him in terms of warrant of arrest issued by a foreign country and Red Corner Notice is an absolute one - Appeal allowed.P /&gt;(C) Criminal - Enforcement of Red Corner notice - For purpose of applying provisions of Act, existence of a treaty between requesting State and requested State plays an important role - Requirements are specific in nature and are required to be accompanied by a large number of documents - Held, no request has yet been made to Executive Govt. of India for extradition of appellant upon compliance of provisions of ss. 2-18 or otherwise - Govt. of India could act only when a request is received - Act cannot be bypassed in red corner cases concerning Indian citizens - In absence of any request having been made by Government of United States of America to Executive Government of Union of India or any authorization made by latter in this behalf - Appeal allowed.P /&gt;(D) Criminal - Arrest of person - Requirement - Held, arrest of a person must be effected in terms of provisions of Act - Person wanted for an offence in a foreign jurisdiction may be arrested on fulfillment of following conditions: (i) That offence should be counted as one by Indian Law as well, and (ii) person must be liable to be arrested in India either under any law relating to extradition, or otherwise - Such an arrest can be effected only pursuant to a warrant issued by Magistrate in view of Sections 6, 16 and 34B of Act or an arrest warrant issued by a foreign country and endorsed by Central Government under Section 15 of Act - Appeal allowed.P /&gt;(E) Constitution -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19, 21 and 226 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 13 and 44A - Issuance of Red Corner notice - Infringement of Fundamental rights - Whether consequences of Red Corner Notice contravene civil liberty of a citizen in terms of Articles 14, 19 and 21 of Constitution? - When a person complains of a violation of his Fundamental Right he is entitled to right of judicial review - HC committed a serious error insofar as it failed to take into consideration provisions of Act, in absence of any request having being made by Govt. of USA to Executive Government of Union of India or any authorisation made by latter on its behalf - Fundamental Right of a citizen whenever infringed, HC having regard to their extraordinary power u/art. 226 of Constitution as also keeping in view that access to justice is a human right would not turn them away only because a Red Corner Notice was issued - If a violation of any order passed by a civil court is made ground for issuance of a Red Corner Notice, indisputably, court will enquire as to whether same has undergone tests laid down u/ss. 13 and 44A of CPC - Appeal allowed.
