Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P., Nagpur and Bhandara v Nandlal Bhandari Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 316; AIR 1966 SC 1026; [1966] 60 ITR 173; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 925

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1950, s. 12 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(5)(b),42 - Indore Companies Act, 1914 - Income-tax Rules, 1962 - Indian Income-tax Rules, 1922 , r.33 - Income - Assessment - Accounting year - Written down value - Depreciation - N was a public company incorporated under 1914Act - Income-tax Officer assessed Company for assessment years 1950-51, 1951-52, 1952-53 and 1953-54 on its income of corresponding accounting years, being calendar years 1949, 1950, 1951 and 1952 - Assessee was a non-resident and for some years as a resident under1922 Act - Company was also assessed to Industrial Tax under 1927Rules - Assessed as a non-resident under 1922Act - During assessment years, pursuant to terms of Order, Income-tax Officer took into account depreciation allowances for years including 1944 as computed and for subsequent years 1945 to 1948, depreciation allowance was computed under 1927 rules - On appeals, Tribunal confirmed orders of Income-tax Officer - Hence instant appeal - whether Company was assessed as resident or non-resident - Held, computation of income of a non-resident may be necessary to determine and allow depreciation. ascertainment of total profits, either for purposes of assessment in ordinary manner when income of assessee was determinable, or where a fraction was to be adopted for purpose of second method contemplated by r. 33 and there was no scope for assuming that only a fraction of depreciation was actually allowed and depreciation was deducted once and for all and deprecation was deducted in determining total profits of business - Amount which bears same proportion to total profits of business of such person as receipts so accruing or arising bear to total receipts of business as such profit being computed in accordance with provisions of Indian Income-tax Act - Appeal dismissed.
