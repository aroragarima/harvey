Â Ram Nath and Another v Messrs Ram Nath Chhittar Mal and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 213; AIR 1961 SC 104; 1961 (1) SCJ 197; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 600

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Delhi & Ajmer Rent Control Act

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1952, ss. 13(1)(g), 15(3) and 35 - Eviction on ground that premises were bona fidely required for purposes of rebuilding - Whether High Court was in error in ordering possession to be, delivered to respondents? - Suits for eviction were brought within framework of Act and were based on provisions of s. 13 (g) - No eviction would have been possible excepting when conditions laid down in s. 13 were satisfied - Applications for being put into possession were really u/s. 15 (3) of Act - As respondents did not deliver possession to appellants on or before dates specified in decree provisions of s. 15 contained in sub-section (3) of that Act were not available to them and they were, not entitled to be put into possession as prayed by them - Appeals allowed.
