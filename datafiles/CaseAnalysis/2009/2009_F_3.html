Faridabad Gas Power Project, Ntpc Ltd., Etc. v Om Prakash & Ors., Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2171; (2009) 4 SCC 719; 2009 (2) AWC 1936; JT 2009 (4) SC 461; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 703; 2009 (107) RD 695B; 2009(2) SCALE 219; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 912; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 125

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Town Improvement Trust Act, 1960

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Acquisition proceedings - Enhancement of compensation - Determination of market value - State of Haryana issued Notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act for acquisition of land at villages 'J' and 'P' for public purpose - Land Acquisition Collector awarded compensation - Being aggrieved against and dissatisfied with the award, the claimants filed reference applications u/s. 18 of the Act - Reference Court allowed the reference applications made by the claimants and accordingly, enhanced the amounts of compensation - On appeal, HC confirmed the award made by the Reference Court - Hence, present appeals - Whether the market value determined by the Reference Court and confirmed by the HC was correct? - It was submitted that yearly increase should have been calculated at the rate of 10% per annum for 7 years over the market value of Rs. 155/- per square yard and increase awarded in these cases at the rate of Rs. 5/- per year is at a lower rate and wholly unjustified - Held, in the facts and circumstances, Reference Court should have worked out the market value of the acquired land by calculating an increase at least at the cumulative rate of 7.5% per annum for 7 years to arrive at the market value as determined in the year 1995 and then it ought to have deducted 20% in that value - Thus, market value for the 'J' lands determined accordingly at Rs. 205/- per square yard - Further, Reference Court and HC both have not found any tangible evidence led by NTPC to rebut the claim of the claimants regarding land acquired at village 'P' - Hence, no perversity in the judgment of the reference court fixing the market value of the acquired land at village 'P' confirmed by HC - Appeals disposed of.
