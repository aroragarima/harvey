Mohinder Pal Jolly v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 207; (1979) 3 SCC 30; (1979) SCC (Cr) 635; AIR 1979 SC 577; 1979 CRLJ 584; 1979 CrLR(SC) 105; 1979 MLJ 413; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 805

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code ,1860, ss. 97, 99, 101,300 and 304 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Onus of establishing right of private defence of property or person - Dispute between management and workmen in regard to payment of wages - Settlement - Demand made by workers but refused - Slogans demanding wages - Firing towards workers - Shot hit one worker on his forehead resulting in death on spot - Availability of private defence against mischief -(A) Whether accused exceeded to power granted to him under right of private defence? - Right of private defence of property in appellant's case extended to causing of any harm other than death - Appellant did exceed this right of private defence and apparently murder which he committed within meaning of cl. 4 of s. 300 of IPC, 1860 squarely fell within Exception 2 thereof - Held, appellant exceeded power given to him by law and caused death of Sant Ram against whom he was exercising such right of defence - (B) Whether appellant was guilty under Part-I or II of s. 304 of IPC, 1860? - If accused commits an act while exceeding right of private defence by which death is caused either with intention of causing death or with intention of causing such bodily injury as was likely to cause death then he would be guilty under Part-I of s. 304 of IPC, 1860 - Further held, appellant could be convicted only under Part-II of section 304 and not Part-I of IPC, 1860 - Even so on facts and in circumstances of this case court not persuaded to let off appellant with an imposition of fine only - Appeal dismissed.
