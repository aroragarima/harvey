Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City v Bipinchandra Maganlal and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 318; AIR 1961 SC 1040; [1961] 41 ITR 290; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 8; 1962 (2) SCJ 649; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Indirect Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.10(2)(Vi), 2(6C), 23A - Dividend - Profits and Gains - Taxable income - Assessment - Written Down Value - Company purchased certain machinery and sold it later for the price for which it was originally purchased - Trading profits of Company disclosed and Company declared a dividend - Income Tax Officer computed the assessable income of Company after adding back to the profit returned by the Company, realised in excess of written down value of machinery sold - Income Tax Officer passed an order u/s. 23A of the Act that Rs. 15,429 deemed to be distributed as dividend amongst the shareholders - Appeals preferred proved unsuccessful - But Appellate Tribunal at the instance of the Company referred the question set out herein before to the HC - HC found Rs. 15,608 not liable to be taken into account - Whether written down value of machinery to be included in taxable income - Held, computing profits and gains of Company u/s. 10 of 1922 Act, for purpose of assessing the taxable income, difference between written down value of machinery in the year of account and sold to be deemed to be profit in year of account, and being such profit, it was liable to be included in assessable income in year of assessment - HC was right in holding that the amount of Rs. 15,608 was not liable to be taken into account in considering whether having regard to the smallness of the profit made by the Company, it would be unreasonable to declare a larger dividend - Appeal dismissed.
