Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I v Tata Locomotive and Engineering Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 232; AIR 1966 SC 1506; [1966] 60 ITR 405; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 235

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 s.66A(2) - Surplus - Devaluation - Sustainability - Respondent-Firm-assessee was manufacturing locomotive boilers and locomotives and for purpose of manufacturing activity assessee had to make purchases of plant and machinery, etc., in various countries and had appointed a company in USA as its purchasing agent with sanction of Exchange Control Authorities and remittance of $33,830 was made in 1949 to for purpose of purchasing capital goods from USA and meeting other expenses connected therewith - Income-tax Officer assessed amount of Rs. 70,147 on ground that it represented profits that arose to assessee 'incidentally to its carrying on business' and whether funds were sent to USA with object of purchasing of capital equipment or for purchase of stores, or for reimbursement of revenue expenditure there need not be distinction that only such portion of profits arising on funds remitted for revenue expenditure only had to be treated as revenue and balance should be treated as capital - Appellate Assistant Commissioner substantially affirmed the order of the Income-tax Officer - Appeal before Tribunal held that sums of $3,567/04 and $8,882/24 included in sum of $48,572/30 were held by assessee for capital purposes and hence any profit that arose to it as a result of its conversion into rupee currency on account of appreciation of dollar, in relation to rupee, must be held on capital account and,' accordingly, Tribunal excluded' profits attributable to these amounts and with regard to $36,123/02, held that it would not be justified in coming to conclusion that there was any constructive remittance, first in direction USA to India and then of an equivalent sum from India to USA further held that 'amount was earned as commission' - HC, negated order of Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether act of keeping money, i.e. $36,123-02 for capital purposes after obtaining sanction of Reserve Bank was part of or a trading transaction - Held, there was no doubt that amount of $36,123.02 was a revenue receipt in assessee's business of commission agency, instead of repatriating it immediately assessee obtained sanction of Reserve Bank to utilise commission in its business of manufacture of locomotive boilers and locomotives for buying capital goods - That was quite an independent transaction and it was nature of this transaction which had to be determined and it was not a trading transaction in business of manufacture of locomotive boilers and locomotives, it was clearly a transaction of accumulating dollars to pay for capital goods, first step to acquisition of capital goods - If assessee had repatriated $36,123.02 and then after obtaining sanction of Reserve Bank remitted $36,123.02 to USA, fact that assessee kept money there does not make any difference especially, that it was a new transaction which assessee entered into, transaction being first step to acquisition of capital goods - Appeal dismissed.
