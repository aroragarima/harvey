Dolai Molliko and Others v Krushna Chandra Patnaik and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1967 SC 49; 1967 CRLJ 154; 1967 (33) CutLT 1; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit filed by appellants for declaration that they were tenants with occupancy rights in lands in dispute - Munsif dismissed suit - Subordinate Judge allowed appeal - One of the appellants died - Application moved for impleding legal heirs of deceased appellant as party - Application not opposed by respondents - Respondents preferred appeal before High Court - HC allowed and held that three legal heirs left out of deceased appeal so appeal abated in toto - Whether left out heirs of deceased are bound by order of Subordinate Court? - No question of any diligent or bona fide enquiry for deceased appellant's heirs must be known to heirs who applied for being brought on record - Held, estate of deceased appellant was fully represented by heirs who had been brought on record in Subordinate Judge's Court and that absent heirs also who would be equally bound by result - Appeal allowed.
