Ashwinkumar K. Patel v Upendra J. Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 430; (1999) 3 SCC 161; AIR 1999 SC 1125; 1999 (2) AWC 1481; 1999 (2) CLT 99; JT 1999 (2) SC 136; 1999(1) SCALE 689; 1999 (2) SLT 470; 1999 (2) Supreme 436

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Order of remand by High Court to Lower Court - Appeal - (A) Powers of HC to remand, when to be exercised - (B) Order of temporary injunction passed by Trial Court - HC remanded it back - Order of remand found to be not proper - Held, should not ordinarily be exercised merely because in its view reasoning of Lower Court in some aspects was wrong - Supreme Court however instead of remitting matter to HC confirmed order of temporary injunction passed by Trial Court on ground of existence of prima facie case - Appeal allowed.
