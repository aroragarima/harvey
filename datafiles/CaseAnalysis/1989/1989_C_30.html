Government Of India v Citedal Fine Pharmaceuticals Madras Andors.Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 656; (1989) 3 SCC 483; AIR 1989 SC 1771; 1989 (2) CCC 642; 1989 CRLR 588; 1989 CrLR(SC) 588; 1989 (23) ECC 307; 1989 (24) ECR 440; 1989 (42) E.L.T. 515; [1990] 184 ITR 467; JT 1989 (3) SC 118; 1989(2) SCALE 44; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 465; 1989 (2) UJ 368

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Health & Drug - Constitution - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, ss. 3(3) and 19 - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties), Rules, 1956, r. 12 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 14 - Recovery of excise Duty - Rule 12 provides for recovery as contemplated by s. 3(3) and not a recovery of escaped duty - Constitutionality - Held, it carries out purposes of Act and is not ultra vires Act - It is also not violative of art. 14 of Constitution on ground that it does not provide limitation for recovery of duty - Appeal allowed.
