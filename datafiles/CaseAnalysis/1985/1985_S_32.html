Â Mohd. Ahmed Khan v Shah Bano Begum and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, E.S. Venkataramiah, Ranganath Misra, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 47; (1985) SCC (Cr) 245; (1985) 2 SCC 556; AIR 1985 SC 945; 1985 (87) BomLR 435; 1985 (1) Crimes 975; 1985 CRLJ 875; 1985 (1) DMC 474; 1985 (2) HLR 77; 1985(1) SCALE 767; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 844

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Law

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 125(1)(a), (3),127(3)(b) and 488 - Whether there is any conflict between provisions of s. 125 and those of Muslim Personal Law on liability of Muslim husband to provide for maintenance of his divorced wife? - Held, yes - Under Muslim Personal Law, liability of husband to maintain a divorced wife is limited to period of iddat - Muslim law does not recognise any obligation on part of a man to maintain a wife whom he had divorced - In Jagir Kaur v. Jaswant Singh (1963 Indlaw SC 387) it was held that under Code of 1898 that provisions regarding maintenance were independent of personal law governing parties - Induction of definition of 'wife' so as to include a divorced woman lends even greater weight to that conclusion - 'Wife' means a wife as defined, irrespective of religion professed by her or by her husband - Therefore, a divorced Muslim women 'wife' for purpose of s. 125 - Statutory right available to her under that section is unaffected by provisions of personal law applicable to her - Appeal dismissed.
