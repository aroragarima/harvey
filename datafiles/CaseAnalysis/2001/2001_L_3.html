Bharat Coking Coal Limited v L. K. Ahuja and Company

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19761; (2001) 4 SCC 86; AIR 2001 SC 1179; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 73; 2001 (1) ARBLR 656; JT 2001 (3) SC 293; 2001 (1) RAJ 259; 2001(2) SCALE 159; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1152; 2001 (2) SLT 289; 2001 (2) Supreme 147

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Limitation Act, 1963 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Neither speaking award nor non-speaking - Mental process of Arbitrator - (A) Whether Arbitrator made an error by way that he has gone into in fixing lump award that is not discernible? - Held, in what manner this aspect has gone into in fixing lump-sum by Arbitrator is not discernible - Therefore, setting aside entire award in respect of both agreements made by Arbitrator and remit matter - (B) Whether objections filed on are in nature of an application u/s. 30 of Arbitration Act to set aside award and is an application u/art. 119 of Schedule to Limitation Act, 1963 and would situation attract s. 5 Limitation Act 1963? - Yes - Situation will attract s. 5 - Further held, on totality of circumstances, there was a sufficient cause for delay in making application and time should be extended till when application was made - Delay condoned in filing - Appeals disposed of.
