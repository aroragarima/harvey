National Insurance Company Limited v Abhaysing Pratapsing Waghela and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2073; (2008) 9 SCC 133; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 703; 2008 (4) ACC 41; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 961; 2008 (4) AWC 3826; 2008 (1) CCC 354; 2009 (1) G.L.R. 306; JT 2008 (9) SC 493; 2008 (4) KLT 657; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 796; 2009 (1) MPLJ 31; 2008 (152) PLR 729; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 173; 2008(12) SCALE 5; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 1049

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Cover Note, Amount Of Premium, Public Service Vehicle, Third Party Risks, Material Particular, Use Of The Vehicle In a Public Place, Branch Manager

Summary: Banking & Finance - Carriers & Transportation - Insurance Act, 1938,ss. 54(v), 64VB - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 145, 146, 147, 149(2) - Dishonour of cheque - Claim - Maintainability - Cheque was tendered to appellant/company to insure truck on 23-1-1995 - Cheque was presented for clearance which was dishonoured on 30-1-1995 - Respondent was injured while driving moped which met accident by truck on 27-1-1995 - Respondent filed compliant before Tribunal which was allowed - Aggrieved by same appeal was made before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was legally obligated to reimburse claim of third party was maintainable - Held, SC relied on National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Laxmi Narain Dhut 2007 Indlaw SC 179 stated that statute was beneficial one qua third party but that benefit could not be extended to the owner of the offending vehicle - Further, HC could not be faulted no doubt, a contract of insurance was to be governed by terms thereof, but a distinction must be borne in mind between a contract of insurance which has been entered into for purpose of giving effect to object and purport of statute and one which provides for reimbursement of liability of owner of vehicle strictly in terms - Furthermore, a contract of insurance entered into for purpose of covering a third party risk would not be purely contractual and that an ordinary contract of insurance does not have a statutory favor, the 1988 Act merely imposes an obligation on part of insurance company to reimburse the claimant both in terms of the 1988 Act as also the Contract - Therefore, so far as liability of insurance company which comes within purview of ss. 146 and 147 of the 1988 Act were concerned, same subserves a constitutional goal, namely, social justice - Thus, contract of insurance covering third party risk should, therefore, be viewed differently a contract of insurance qua contract - Appeal dismissed.
