Sharad Kumar v Government of NCT of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1211; (2002) 4 SCC 490; AIR 2002 SC 1724; 2002 (2) CLR 235; 2002 (97) DLT 326; 2002 (5) ESC 204; 2002 (93) FLR 826; JT 2002 (4) SC 49; 2002 (35) LabIC 1464; 2002 (2) LLJ 275; 2002(3) SCALE 442; 2002 (3) SCJ 83; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 1057; 2003 (1) SLJ 185; 2002 (4) SLR 673; 2002 (3) SLT 129; 2002 (3) Supreme 283; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1709

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957

Summary: Constitution - - Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Water - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 12(5), 2(k) and 2(s)- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether the High Court rightly declined to refer the dispute relating to discharge/termination of the appellant's service for adjudication to the industrial tribunal? - Determination of this question depends on the types of duties assigned to or discharged by the employee and not merely on the designation of the post held by him - The state Govt., merely considered the designation of the post held,which is extraneous to the matters relevant for the purpose - From the appointment, it cannot be held that the appellant did not come within the first portion of s. 2(s) of the Act - Determination of the question requires examination of factual matters for which materials including oral evidence will have to be considered - State government could not arrogate the power to adjudicate and hold that the respondent was not a workman within the meaning of s. 2(s) of the Act, there by terminating the proceedings prematurely - Matter should be decided by the industrial tribunal or Labour Court on the basis of the materials to be placed before it by the parties - The rejection order passed by the state Govt. is clearly erroneous and the order passed by the HC maintaining the same is unsustainable - Appeal is allowed.
