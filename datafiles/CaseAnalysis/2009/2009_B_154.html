Bhupendra Singh & Ors v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 460; (2009) 12 SCC 447; AIR 2009 SC 3265; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 365; 2009(5) SCALE 653; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147 and 302 r/w 149, 307 r/w 149 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Common object - Rioting - Accused 17 in numbers reached field wherein complainant alongwith his two brothers, one of whom was deceased, and nephew were working - Accused persons held grudge against complainant in view of cases registered against YS(one of the accused) and others for offence of abduction and rape - On reaching the spot main accused NS exhorted 'Jugendra Pal Singh Ko Pakad lo tatha jan se mar do, Kyunki hamare khilaf jhutha mukadam darj karaya hai' - On such exhortation accused persons attacked deceased while other victims escaped - Conviction for all awarded by Trial Court - Upheld by High Court - Pending appeal eight of the accused were dead thus appeal for them abated - Conviction as awarded and upheld, whether proper? - (A) Testimonies of eye witnesses who were relatives of deceased, challenged on ground of interestedness - Such evidence cannot be discarded on mere ground of witnesses being relatives or family members, if evidence is otherwise cogent and reliable - It is more often than not that a relation would not conceal actual culprit and make allegations against an innocent person - Foundation has to be laid if plea of false implication is made - 'Dalip Singh vs. State of Punjab' - (B) Applicability of s. 149 questioned by defence - Court deliberated upon, common object vis-a-vis common intention; formation of common object and its timing, effect of timing of such formation; two limbs of s. 149 - For making member of unlawful assembly liable u/s 149, only thing required is that he should have understood that the assembly was unlawful and was likely to commit any of the acts which fall within the purview of s. 141 - Common object can developed during the course of incident at the spot eo instanti - Timing of formation of unlawful intent not relevant - No hard-and-fast rule can be laid down under the circumstances from which the common object can be culled out, it may reasonably be collected from the nature of the assembly, arms it carries and behaviour at or before or after the scene of incident - Word 'knew' in second limb of s. 149 - Meaning and essence; something more than possibility; positive knowledge is necessary - Held, finding of guilt by Trial Court and HC to be proper - Appeal dismissed.
