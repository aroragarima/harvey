Lakhan Lal, Pappu Lal @ Manoj Kumar Srivastava v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudarshan Reddy, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 72; (2011) 2 SCC 251; AIR 2011 SC 842; 2011 CRLJ 1116; 2011 (1) JCC 287; JT 2011 (6) SC 315; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 939; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 494; 2011(1) SCALE 504; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Children Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 2(k) and 15 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Determination of Juvenility - Present appeals filed against the order of HC whereby the HC dismissed the appeals filed by the appellants, confirmed their conviction for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - It was submitted that since at the time of commission of the said offence, the appellant had not completed 18 years of age, he was a 'juvenile' within the meaning of s. 2(k) of Act, 2000 and therefore, the order of sentence passed against the appellant to be set aside - Whether appellants who were admittedly not 'juvenile' within the meaning of the Act, 1986 when the offences were committed but had not completed 18 years of age on that date were entitled for the benefit and protection under the provisions of the Act, 2000? - Held, relied on SC judgment in Pratap Singh v. State of Jharkhand & Anr., 2005 Indlaw SC 81 wherein it was held that the relevant date for determining the age of a person who claims to be a juvenile/child would be the date on which the offence was committed and not the date when he was produced before the authority or in the Court - In the present case, when the inquiry was initiated against the appellants, they were admittedly 'juvenile' even under the provisions of 1986 Act but this issue was ignored by the Courts below - However, appellants have crossed the age of 40 years as at present and also they have undergone an actual period of sentence of more than three years the maximum period provided u/s. 15 of the 2000 Act - In the circumstances, while sustaining the conviction of the appellants for the offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC, the sentences awarded to them set aside - Appeals partly allowed.
