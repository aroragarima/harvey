Â Sheo Nandan Paswan v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza, S. Natarajan, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 39; (1987) SCC (Cr) 82; (1987) 1 SCC 288; AIR 1987 SC 877; 1987 CRLJ 793; JT 1986 SC 1132; JT 1986 SC 1188A; 1986(2) SCALE 1099; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Withdrawal of prosecution - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - S. 321 Large amounts of money embezzled - whether the order withdrawal of prosecution against the Chief Minister was valid - Held, Yes - No unfettered or unrestricted power is conferred on the Public Prosecutor to apply for withdrawal from the prosecution and must be guided by Art. 14 of the Constitution - No malafides on part of Public Prosecutor to evoke remedy of withdrawal from prosecution - No evidence found against the Chief Minister - Appeal dismissed.
