P. Vijayan v State Of Kerala And Another.

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 58; (2010) 2 SCC 398; AIR 2010 SC 663; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 588; 2010 CRLJ 1427; JT 2010 (1) SC 459; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 220; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 826; 2010(1) SCALE 604; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 78; 2010 (1) UC 334

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 207 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Discharge - Appellant, a retired IPS officer aged about 85 years, and other accused were charge-sheeted for offence punishable u/ss. 302/34 for a fake encounter case - Appellant filed a petition u/s. 227, CrPC for discharge, which was dismissed - Trial Judge ordered framing of charge for offence u/ss. 302/34, IPC - Criminal revision petition filed thereagainst also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether Courts below were justified in dismissing the discharge petition filed u/s. 227, CrPC? - Held, s. 227, CrPC confers special power on the Judge to discharge an accused if upon consideration of the records and documents 'there is not sufficient ground' for proceeding against the accused - Consideration of the record and document at that stage is for the limited purpose of ascertaining whether or not there is sufficient ground for proceeding against the accused and if the Judge comes to a conclusion that there is sufficient ground to proceed, he will frame a charge under Section 228, if not, he will discharge the accused - In the present case, after evaluating the materials produced by the prosecution and after considering the probability of the case, the Trial Judge dismissed the discharge petition - HC rightly affirmed the order passed by the Trial Judge dismissing the discharge petition - Appeal dismissed.
