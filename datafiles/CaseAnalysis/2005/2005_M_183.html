Manish Ujwal and others v Maharishi Dayanand Saraswati University and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1958; (2005) 13 SCC 744; JT 2005 (8) SC 382; 2005 (6) SLR 451

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Key answers, Erroneous, Impact of evaluating answers, Ranking

Summary: Education - Entrance tests - Key answers - Erroneous - Impact of evaluating answers - Ranking - Legality - Student community filed a writ petition before HC challenging their ranking in the entrance tests, which were conducted by respondent no. 1 (University) on 9-5-2005, 10-5-2005 and 11-5-2005, for admission to medical and dental courses in various colleges in the State - Grievance of the students was that various key answers on the basis whereof the answer-sheets were evaluated were wrong and, consequently, wrong and erroneous ranking was prepared - Single Judge held that it could not be said with certainty that the answers to the 6 questions given in the answer keys were erroneous and incorrect, which conclusion was affirmed by DB by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by Single Judge and affirmed by DB were sustainable - Held, it was cleared that fresh evaluation of papers by feeding correct key answers would not affect the students who have secured admissions as a result of the first counseling on the basis of ranking given with reference to the results already declared - HC committed a serious illegality in coming to the conclusion that 'it could not be said with certainty that answers to 6 questions given in the key answers were erroneous and incorrect' - Key answers were palpably and demonstrably erroneous - In that view of matter, student community, whether appellants or intervenors or even those who did not approach the HC or SC, could not be made to suffer on account of errors committed by the University - There was nothing on record as to how that error crept up in giving the erroneous key answers and who was negligent - However, University and those who prepare the key answers have to be very careful and abundant caution was necessary in those matters for more than one reasons - It was directed for re-valuation of all the questions by feeding correct answers and on that basis correct number of marks obtained by all the students should be assigned and their ranking prepared - Second counseling and admissions thereafter should be made on the basis or ranking as per the list which would prepared by University pursuant to instant directions - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeals allowed.
