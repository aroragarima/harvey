Chief Commissioner of Income Tax(Administration), Bangal v V K Gururaj

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1122; (1996) 7 SCC 275; (1996) SCC (L&S) 579; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 980; 1996 (33) ATC 269; 1996 (1) CLR 433; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 485; JT 1996 (1) SC 709; 1996(1) SCALE 696; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 841; 1996 (1) SLR 773; 1996 (2) Supreme 17

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Special Pay - Failure to file appeal - Held, it being a question of law and the matter is of perennial problem applicable to several places - Failure to file an appeal in one case does not have the effect of following in all other cases - Further held, Tribunal was wholly incorrect in directing payment to all the persons who did not discharge such duties - Appeal allowed.
