Â State Of Bihar and Others v Bhabapritananda Ojha

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 68; AIR 1959 SC 1073; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 624

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Bihar Hindu Religious Trust, 1950

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Family & Personal - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f), 25, 26 and 27 - Code of Civil Procedure 1908, s. 92 - Bihar Hindu Religious Trusts Act, 1951, ss. 2(b), 3, 4(5), 28 and 29 - (A) Whether Bihar Hindu Religious Trusts Act infringes arts. 14, 19, 25, 26 and 27 of Constitution? - Held, provisions of Bihar Act do not infringe fundamental rights of trusts in Bihar guaranteed u/arts. 14, 19(1)(f),25, 26 and 27 of Constitution - Nor is Act bad on ground that it imposes an unauthorised tax - (B) Whether Baidyanath temple in Bihar is public trust u/s. 3 of Bihar Hindu Religious Trusts Act? - Further held, Bihar Act does not apply to private trusts, Baidyanath Temple situated in town of Deoghar within limits of Santal Parganas in State of Bihar is a public trust - Two conditions must be fulfilled for application of Act - (a) religious trust or institution itself must be in Bihar and (b) part of its properly must be situated in State of Bihar - Two conditions are fulfilled in case of Baidyanath temple, temple is in Bihar and properties belonging to temple lie mainly in Bihar though there are some properties in present State of West Bengal - Act is not bad on ground of extra-territorial operation because it takes trusts in Bihar out of purview of s. 92, CPC, 1908 as State of Bihar has legislative power over it and over its trustees and their servants or agents in Bihar who administer trust - There is really no question of Act having extra-territorial operation - Appeal allowed.
