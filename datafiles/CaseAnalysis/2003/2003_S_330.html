Golakonda Venkateswara Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 562; (2003) 9 SCC 277; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1904; AIR 2003 SC 2846; 2003 (2) AllCriC 686; 2003 (3) CCR 81; 2003 (3) Crimes 210; 2003 (6) CRJ 581; 2003 CRLJ 3731; 2003 (2) JCC 1221; 2003 (2) JIC 782; JT 2003 (6) SC 438; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 465; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 581; 2003(5) SCALE 646; 2003 (1) SCW 3612; 2003 (4) SLT 742; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 96; 2003 (5) Supreme 202

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 201, 302, 376 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - s. 27 - Extra judicial confession - No eyewitness to the occurrence and conviction of the appellant solely based on circumstantial evidence - Held, in cases where the evidence is purely circumstantial in nature, the facts and circumstances from which the conclusion of guilt is sought to be drawn must be fully established beyond any reasonable doubt and such circumstances must be consistent and unerringly point to the guilt of the accused and the chain of circumstances must be established by the prosecution - Further held, there is no reasonable apprehension with the material exhibits being planted to rope in the appellant with the crime.
