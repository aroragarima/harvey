Gurpreet Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1925; (2002) 8 SCC 18; (2003) SCC (Cr) 186; AIR 2002 SC 3217; 2002 (2) AllCriC 934; 2002 (2) CGLJ 397; 2002 (4) Crimes 254; 2003 (1) CRJ 148; 2002 CRLJ 4688; JT 2002 (7) SC 58; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 566; 2002(6) SCALE 363; 2002 (5) SLT 231; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 337; 2002 (6) Supreme 304

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Indian Penal Code - S. 302 - Husband and wife, living together but having strained relationship and divorce proceedings for mutual consent were pending - Divorce proceedings entitled the wife to a sum of Rs. 3,00,000 - Dead body of wife found burnt in one of the rooms of house - Husband present in another room - Plea of alibi raised - Whether the appellant can be held liable for murder of his deceased wife - Held, yes - Deceased was assaulted by the appellant few days before the incident - Evidence and chain of events conclusively prove guilt of the appellant - Appeal dismissed
