State of Kerala and another v P.V. Neelakandan Nair and others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 415; (2005) 5 SCC 561; (2005) SCC (L&S) 698; AIR 2005 SC 3066; 2005 (4) ESC 537; 2005 (106) FLR 622; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 214; JT 2005 (6) SC 160; 2005 (3) KLT 717; 2005 (3) LLN 738; 2005(5) SCALE 424; 2005 (8) SLR 234; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 426; 2005 (4) Supreme 719

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Education - Kerala Education Rules, 1959, r. 62 - Kerala Service Rules, 1959, r. 60(c) - Benefit of increment or promotion - Whether teachers superannuating during a particular academic year but continuing in service by virtue of Chap. 14(A), r. 62 are entitled to benefit of pay revision coming into effect during such extended period? - Held, r. 60(c) does not refer to pay revisions which is conceptually different from annual increments within prescribed pay scale - Entitlement of concerned teachers for benefits of pay revision cannot be doubted - Appeals dismissed.
