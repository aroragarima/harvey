Haryana Urban Development Authority v B.K. Sood

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 925; (2006) 1 SCC 164; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 106; 2005 (61) ALR 898; 2006 (1) AWC 147; 2006 (1) CCC 24; 2006 (2) CompLJ 1; 2005 (4) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2005 (9) SC 503; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 92; 2005(9) SCALE 124

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - 24-A - Delay in filing complaint - Whether condonable? - Auction of booth sites plots/building at Panchkula by appellant - Allotment of booth site to respondent by appellant - Complaint filed by respondent alleging deficiencies in booth and for compensation for appellant's alleged gross failure in discharging their legal duties - Claim of respondent accepted by State Commission - Award of Rs. two lacs compensation by National Commission which was to be paid by appellant to respondent - Appeal - Non consideration of preliminary objections raised by appellant that claim of respondent was barred by time by State Commission or National Commission - Complaint before State Commission was filed by respondent in 1997, ten years after taking of possession, eight years after cause of alleged damage commenced and three years after that cause ceased - There was not even any prayer by respondent in his complaint for condoning delay - Hence claim of respondent on basis of allegations contained in complaint, was clearly barred by limitation as two year period prescribed by s.24 A of Act had expired much before complaint was admitted by State Commission - Appeal allowed and decision of National Commission in so far as it directed appellant to pay compensation to respondent set aside.
