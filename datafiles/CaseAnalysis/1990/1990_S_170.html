G.J. Fernandez v State Of Karnataka And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 643; (1990) 2 SCC 488; AIR 1990 SC 958; JT 1990 (1) SC 134; 1990(1) SCALE 117; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 229; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 2082

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Contract Act, 1872, s. 2(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 136 - Tender - Notice - Pre qualifications of tenderer, supply of certain documents - Failure - Invalidation of contract - Appeal - Interference sought - Considering following issues (1) Maintainability - (2) Certificates supplied by tenderer - Sufficiency - Justifiability - (3) Relaxation of pre-qualification - Permissibility - Considering rule of invalidation of grant of contract on account of relaxation - (4) Interpretation bona fide placed by authority inviting tenders - Interference, if needed - Held, Tenderer can be excluded from consideration - Sufficiency to be decided by authority calling tenders and not by Court - Authority can relax qualification provided not arbitrary and rule does not readily apply where attempt of applicant is to gain immunity from competition - No interference by Court - Appeal dismissed.
