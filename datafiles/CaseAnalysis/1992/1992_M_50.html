Food Corporation Of India Through Its Districtmanager, Farid v Makhan Singh And Anr

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 972; (1992) 3 SCC 67; AIR 1992 SC 1406; AIR 1992 SCW 1466; JT 1992 (4) SC 1; 1992(1) SCALE 928; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23 and 54 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Appeal - Locus standi - Acquisition proceedings - Compensation amount awarded by Court over and above amount awarded by Collector in reference - Corporation for whose purpose land was acquired, was also party to reference - Maintainablity of appeal against award - (B) New plea - Land acquisition - Appeal against award - Can objection as to filing of appeal by corporation for whose purpose land was acquired be raised for first time in appeal before Supreme Court - (C) Compensation for land - Poor locale, disadvantageous position and lack of contiguity to expansion of town due to obstructing railway line - Whether fixation of rate of Rs. 1,20,000 per acre on basis of sale instance of very small areas proper - Held, (A) Maintainable - (B) Cannot be allowed to be raised - (C) Not proper - SC reduced compensation to Rs. 80,000 per acre - Appeal allowed.
