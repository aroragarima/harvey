P.L. Lakhanpal v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 145; AIR 1967 SC 1507; 1967 CRLJ 1390; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 114

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security forces - Practice & Procedure - Defence of India Rules (1962), rr. 23, 30 A(9) and 30(1)(b), ss. 3 (2)(15)(i) and 44 - (A) petitioner was arrested by an order u/r. 30(1) (b) of Defence of India Rules, 1962 was detained in Central Jail, Tihar, New Delhi - Whether function entrusted by r. 30 A(9) to Govt. and its decision is judicial or quasi-judicial? - Held, function entrusted to authority under r. 30-A (9) as distinguished from power under r. 30 (l)(b) is quasi-judicial and decision which it has to arrive at cannot be any thing other than quasi judicial - Rules of natural justice to be complied with - If disclosure of materials in possession of Govt. may be embarrassing and detrimental to larger interests of country - Proper remedy against such consequence is not to deny elemental right of representing his case to a person detained but by providing a rule to claim privilege against such disclosure - Petition allowed.
