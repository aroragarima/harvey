State Of Andhra Pradesh & Others v B.Noorulla Khan & Another Etc.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 438; (2004) 6 SCC 194; 2004 (2) ACC 569; AIR 2004 SC 2413; 2004 (5) ALD(SC) 23; 2004 (56) ALR 758; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 24; 2004 (137) PLR 808; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 652; 2004(5) SCALE 618; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 432

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989 - r. 297-A(6)(f) Andhra Pradesh Motor Vehicles Rules whether ultra vires provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, and art.19(1)(g) of Constitution of India? - r 297-A (1)(c)by State Govt. does not run counter to provisions of Section 2(7) of Act either in its intent or in its expression and has been framed to fulfill object with which Section 2(7) has been enacted - r.297-A(6)(f)it provides for settling names of passengers undertaking journey under an agreement for hiring a public service vehicle - Both r. 297-A(1)(c ) and 297-A (6)(f) held to be intra vires of Act - Granting of all-India tourist permit - Such permits would be exclusively governed by Rules framed by Central Govt. or conditions prescribed by Central Govt. and not Rules framed by State Govt.
