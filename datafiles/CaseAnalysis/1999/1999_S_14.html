Rajat Baran Roy and Others v State of W.B. and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1306; (1999) 4 SCC 235; (1999) SCC (L&S) 852; AIR 1999 SC 1661; 1999 (1) CLR 1084; 1999 (3) ESC 2202; JT 1999 (3) SC 89; 1999 (3) LLN 1; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 618

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - West Bengal Service Rules, Part 1, r. 75 - Compulsory retirement - Validity - (A) Whether High Court's recommendation for compulsory retirement on basis of pre - retirement assessment of members of West Bengal judicial service following directions of Supreme Court in All India Judges' Association case in 1993, is valid? - No - Direction issued in 1993 case would cease to exist when appropriate rule enhancing retirement age of judicial officers to 60 years is made-Pre-retirement assessment will not be applicable unless same is specifically provided under rules - By virtue of Govt. order D/-20-6-1992, members of West Bengal Higher Judicial Service were treated on a par with members of Indian Administrative Service in all matters - Retirement age of officers of Indian Administrative Service stood extended from 58 years to 60 years, retirement age of members of West Bengal Judicial Service also automatically got extended from 58 years to 60 years - Held, it was not open to High Court to have recommended compulsory retirement of petitioners, following directions of this court which had ceased to exist - (B) Whether orders of compulsory retirement have been done in 'public interest'? - Further held, a careful perusal of impugned orders nowhere shows that said orders are being issued in 'public interest' which is a condition precedent for invoking rule - Nor does it advert anywhere in impugned orders in regard to conditions specified in sub - paras (1) and (2) of rule - Petitions allowed.
