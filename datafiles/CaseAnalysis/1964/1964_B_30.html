Â Board of Revenue, Uttar Pradesh v Rai Saheb Sidhnath Mehrotra

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1965 SC 1092; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 24, 57 - Executed deed - Mortgaged properties - Amount due - Stamp duty - Respondent/vendors was one of executants of deed dt. 15-12-1952, were lessees of two plots of land on which they had constructed oil mill, Factory, and buildings in which factories stood - Factory was being run by vendors in partnership with X and Y and vendors had equitably mortgaged those properties with Bank, and Rs. 10,00,000 was due to Bank - For repayment off debt, vendors entered into contract with Messrs Oil Corporation of India Ltd/vendees, for sale of properties for Rs. 5,55,000, and out of that Rs. 66,000 was payable to X and Y in respect of their share in Factory, and remainder to vendors - Bank agreed to release from its charge properties to be conveyed to vendees provided of Rs. 5,00,000 was paid to it and vendees agreed to pay Bank sum of Rs. 4,89,000, while vendors agreed to pay Rs. 11,000 to Bank to make up balance - In pursuance of said agreement, vendors handed over possession of factories to vendees, who paid before 15-12-1952, Rs. 3,89,000 to Bank - On 15-12-1952, sale-deed in respect of buildings and lessee rights was executed - Cl. 2 of deed provided that 'vendees declared that properties conveyed were free from all encumbrances except charge in favour of Bank, which would be paid off so far as properties conveyed were concerned in manner set forth above - Appellant/Board of Revenue referred questions to HC, which was answered in favour of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, On 15-12-1952, when deed was executed, for Rs. 3,89,000 had already been paid by vendees to Bank - Appellant's counsel contends that amount should be included because it was consideration moving from vendees, and says that stamp duty could not be avoided by simple device of paying money before conveyance was executed - He was right in that but he might show that Rs. 3,89,000 was an advance payment for immovable property conveyed by deed, dt. 15-12-1952 - It was quite clear from terms of deed that Rs. 4,55,000, was to be paid for items other than immovable property conveyed by said deed, and sum of Rs. 3,89,000, had nothing to do with immovable property and payment of Rs. 3,89,000, to Bank left outstanding Rs. 1,11,000, as mortgage money and Rs. 1,00,000, was expressed to be consideration for conveyance of immovable property, and, therefore, falls within art. 23 - That leaves Rs. 11,000, and question arises whether that sum should be taken into consideration for purpose of levying stamp duty - It had already been noticed that sum of Rs. 11,000, forms part of price for items other than immoveable property and appellant's counsel had not seriously controverted finding of HC on that point - SC, hold that sum of Rs. 11,000, could not be included for purpose of levying stamp duty - In result, SC, agreed with HC that stamp duty was to be calculated only on sum of Rs. 1,00,000 - Appeal dismissed.
