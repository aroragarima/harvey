Ludina Pavanakumari v Thankamma John(Dead) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1505; (2010) 15 SCC 342; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 87; 2011 (2) ALT 4; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 158; 2011 (112) RD 443; 2010(12) SCALE 316

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951, Travancore Christian Succession Act, 1092, Kerala Government Land Assignment Rules, 1964

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Government Land Assignment Rules, 1964, r. 9(2) - Travancore Christian Succession Act, 1092 - Land - Claiming partition - Succeed - After death of deceased, land remained in possession of son of deceased/father of appellant, and after 14years land was assigned to father of appellant issued by Stat Govt. on dt.2.6.1977 u/r. 9(2) of 1964 Rules - Respondent/daughters of deceased filed suit in Trial Court for partition of land by asserting that plaint schedule property belong to their father/deceased and after his death, they were entitled to get 1/3rd share under the 1092 Act, - Trial Court its judgment declared that respondents were entitled to 2/3rd share in plaint schedule property and also restrained defendant from committing any waste or obstructing respondents from enjoying properties till partition was effected - On appeal, Appellate Court, questioning legality and correctness of judgment and decree of Trial Court - Appellate Court allowed appeal, set aside judgment and decree of Trial Court and remanded case for fresh disposal of suit filed by respondents - In furtherance of direction given by Lower Appellate Court, Trial Court re-evaluated evidence and held that respondents had no right to seek partition of plaint schedule property - Aggrieved, respondents filed appeal before HC - HC allowed appeal and held respondents had right to seek partition of property which was granted to their father/deceased - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents claim for partition was succeed - Held, HC did not pay due attention to the facts that after 1961 land remained in possession of father of appellant, who paid tax and constructed building after obtaining permission from municipality and in 1977 same was assigned to him u/r. 9(2) of 1964 Rules and thereby he had become absolute owner of entire property, which was transferred to appellant in 1983 - Hence, respondents had not challenged partition and therefore they had no right to claim partition of plaint schedule property and Trial Court and HC gravely erred in passing decree in their favour - Appeal allowed.
