Arvind Kumar v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 610; (2016) 9 SCC 221; AIR 2016 SC 3651; 2016 (118) ALR 280; 2016 (5) AWC 4552; JT 2016 (12) SC 453; 2016 (133) RD 359; 2016(7) SCALE 621; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1972, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1974, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1972, s. 9(2) - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1974, ss. 1(1), 1(2) - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amendment) Act, 1976, s. 31(3) - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, ss. 10(2),13, 19, 19(2), 31(2), 31(3) - Surplus land - Ceiling proceedings lapsed - Validity - Notice u/s. 10(2) of the 1960 Act was served on late tenure-holder to file objections against a proposal to declare 51.29 acres as surplus land - Appellants legal heirs of tenure holder filed objections alleging that under the 1960 Act there was no surplus land - Prescribed Authority passed an order dt. 13-1-1975 by which the entire land that was subject matter of notice was declared surplus - On appeal, Appellate Authority dismissed appeal holding that no fresh notice had been issued u/s. 9(2) of the 1972 Act and as this was so, the proceedings had not abated - Appellants filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether ceiling proceedings in respect of land in question have lapsed owing to s. 31 of the 1976 Act.

Held, s. 19 of the 1972 Act, which is a transitory provision, provides for abatement of proceedings that are pending on the commencement of the 1972 Act. Thus, order dt. 13-1-1975 is a consequence of s. 19(1) of the 1972 Act. S. 19(2) of the 1960 Act has no application reason that surplus land had not in instant case been determined finally before commencement of the 1972 Act i.e. an appeal had not been decided u/s. 13 of the 1960 Act prior to such date. Further, u/s. 31(2) of the 1976 Act, order determining surplus land in instant case had been made four days before 17-1-1975 and thus the first condition or pre-requisite for application of the Section is met. The second pre-requisite is also met for the simple reason that s. 9 of the 1974 Act, which forms part of the same legislative scheme as the 1972 Act, would apply for the reason that an order determining surplus land had been made prior to commencement of the 1972 Act. That being the case, language of s. 31(2) of the 1976 Act makes it clear that every appeal preferred against such orders and pending immediately before 10-10-1975, shall be deemed to have abated on the said date. It was necessary for prescribed authority to re-determine surplus land u/s. 31(3) of the 1976 Act in accordance with the 1960 Act as amended by the 1976 Act, for which purpose, the provisions of s. 13 of the 1960 Act shall apply mutatis mutandis to every order re-determining surplus land u/s. 9 of the 1974 Act (vide s. 31(4) of the 1976 Act). However, that never done on facts in instant case, it is clear that appeal filed in 1975 has abated and could not therefore have been heard by Addl. Commissioner on merits. Impugned judgment and order passed by Commissioner dt. 13-12-1975 is without jurisdiction. Both Appellate Authority and HC were of the view that no fresh notice had been issued u/s. 9(2) of the 1972 Act, in fact such a notice had been issued on 24-11-1975. Appellate Authority and HC, in their anxiety to decide against abatement, have wrongly held no such notice was proved to have been issued. Therefore, abatement u/s. 31 of the 1976 Act does not depend upon the issuance or non-issuance of any notice u/s. 9(2) of the 1972 Act. Such being the case, finding of fact of non-issuance of notice itself being a non-issue, which was unnecessary for SC to pursue. It is only necessary to reiterate that no fresh exercise under the 1976 Act was undertaken by Prescribed Authority as is required by s. 31(3) of the 1976 Act. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
