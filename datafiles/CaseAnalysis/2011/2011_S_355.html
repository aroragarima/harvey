R. L. Kalathia and Company v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 44; (2011) 2 SCC 400; AIR 2011 SC 754; 2011 (3) AWC 2634; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 32; 2011 (2) CTC 117; 2011 (1) G.L.R. 819; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 172; JT 2011 (2) SC 69; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 213; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 832; 2011(1) SCALE 441; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Agreement for construction of a Dam was executed between appellant-firm and State Government - During execution of the said work, the Executive Engineer, directed the appellant to carry out additional and alteration work - Appellant-firm was required to attend the larger quantity of work and thus entitled for extra payment for the additional work - However, as per the works contract, the appellant-firm was not paid the running bill within the specified time - Appellant-firm lodged a consolidated statement of their claims for the additional or altered works etc. to the Executive Engineer - As there was no response, the appellant-firm served a statutory notice - Thereafter, appellant-firm filed suit praying for a decree of the aggregate amount of Rs.3,66,538.05 with running interest at the rate of 9% p.a. - It was also the specific claim of the appellant-firm that on the direction of the Department, it had performed additional work and hence entitled for additional amount/damages as per the terms of agreement - Civil Judge allowed the suit and passed a decree in favour of appellant-firm - On appeal, HC accepted the claim of the State that by the conduct of the appellant-firm in accepting the final bill and the Department made full payment to the appellant-firm, sending statutory notice and filing suit for recovery of the differential amount was barred by the principle of estoppel - HC dismissed the suit of the appellant-firm - Hence, present petition - Held, after execution of full and final discharge receipt by one of the parties, if the said party is able to establish that he is entitled to further amount, then said party is not barred from claiming such amount merely because it accepted the final bill 'without prejudice' or by issuing 'No Due Certificate' - Hence, in the present case, merely because the appellant-firm accepted the final bill, it cannot be deprived of its right to claim damages if it had incurred additional amount and able to prove the same by acceptable materials - Further, appellant-firm had a genuine claim which was supported by oral and documentary evidence - Conclusion arrived at by the Trial Court was correct and appellant-firm was entitled to the said amount as granted by the Trial Court - Impugned judgment of the HC set aside and the judgment and decree of the Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
