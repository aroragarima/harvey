Subhashis Bakshi & Ors. v West Bengal Medical Council & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 121; (2003) 9 SCC 269; AIR 2003 SC 1563; 2003 (2) CHN 138; 2003(2) SCALE 110; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1150; 2003 (3) SLR 138; 2003 (2) SLT 27; 2003 (2) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Prescription, Bengal Medical Act, 1914

Summary: Health and Drug - Bengal Medical Act, 1914 - Medical practitioners - Appellants completed the diploma course of Community Medical Service in duly recognized institutions in the State of West Bengal and posted in different parts of the State by theGovt.of West Bengal - Amendment by notification - Single judge of HC held Diploma Holders will not have the right to private practice and entry in the register is only for the right to prescribe medicines and issue certificates - Division Bench of HC relying on A K Sabhapathy vs State of Kerala found that 'a person can practice in allopathic system of medicine in a state or in the country only if he possesses a recognized medical qualification' and since the appellants don't possess the required qualification, it was held that their names could not be included in the Medical Register - Whether the Appellants, who are having the right to treat could issue prescription or sickness or death certificates? - Held, the appellants are validly holding the right to treat certain diseases So their right to issue prescriptions or certificate cannot be detached from their right to treat Once the right to treat is recognized, then the right to prescribe medicine or issue necessary certificate flows from it Or else the right to treat cannot be completely protected - Order of the Division Bench set aside and that of the learned Single Judge restored - Appeal allowed.
