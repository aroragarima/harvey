Sorath Builders v Shreejikrupa Buildcon Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2115; (2009) 11 SCC 9; 2009 (3) AWC 2298; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 1976; JT 2009 (2) SC 673; 2009(4) SCALE 1; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 893

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tender - Construction of College building - University-Respondent no. 2 issued a tender notice dt. 21.11.2008 inviting tenders for construction of college building which was widely advertised through publication in local newspaper and also through internet - Pursuant to the aforesaid advertisement 3 bids were received by respondent no. 2 out of which one was disqualified at the threshold for not having the requisite experience - As bid of respondent no. 1 was sent three days after the last date of receiving documents his bid could not have been opened - Respondent no. 1 filed an appeal before HC praying for quashing the decision of respondent no. 2 disqualifying respondent no. 1 from participating in the tender selection process for the construction of College building - HC allowed said writ petition and set aside the order of the respondent no. 2 - HC also directed respondent no. 2 to issue a fresh tender notice with the same terms and conditions - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC has rightly set aside the order of the respondent no. 2? - Held, respondent no. 1 never specifically raised the issue regarding paucity and shortage of time as one of the grounds for challenging the decision of the respondent no. 2 - The only stand that was taken by respondent no. 1 for late submission of his pre qualification documents is that he came to know about the tenders only on 27.11.2008 as he was undertaking various construction works, and therefore, could not submit all the required pre qualification documents in time stipulated in the notice inviting tenders - Hence, respondent no. 1 was negligent and was not sincere in submitting his pre qualification documents within the time schedule laid down despite the fact that he had information that there is a time schedule attached to the notice inviting tenders - Therefore, respondent no. 2 was justified in not opening the tender submitted by respondent no. 1 on 01.12.2008, which was late by three days - HC proceeded to interfere with the entire process as if acting as an appellate authority over the decision of the respondent no. 2 which was beyond the jurisdiction of the Court - Impugned order of the HC set aside and decision of the respondent no. 2 upheld - Appeal allowed.
