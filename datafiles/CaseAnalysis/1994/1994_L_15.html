Deepchand v Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1312; (1994) 4 SCC 99; AIR 1994 SC 1901; 1994 (23) ALR 625; 1994 (1) CCC 829; 1994 (54) DLT 481; JT 1994 (3) SC 319; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 19; 1994 (2) PLR 197; 1994 (1) RentLR 380; 1994(2) SCALE 325; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 530; 1994 (Supp) RD 167

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 2(2) and 96 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 49(1) - Whether decision of Civil Court is a decree within meaning of s. 2(2) of CPC, 1908 so as to make it appealable u/s. 96 of CPC, 1908? - Held, title to receive compensation would be in issue u/s. 30 and such a decision attains finality and binds parties - Decision therein is conclusive between parties unless it is varied or versed by any competent court on appeal - Under those circumstances, Courts have held that it is a decree u/s. 2(2) of CPC, 1908 - So, analogy is inapplicable to determination u/s. 49(1) of Act in which no rights of parties to adjudication are involved - Determination u/s. 49(1) of Act is not a decree within meaning of s. 2(2) CPC, 1908 and that therefore, an appeal u/s. 96 CPC, 1908 does not lie - Appeal dismissed.
