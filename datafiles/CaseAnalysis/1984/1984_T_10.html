Sudhir Chandra Sarkar v Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 66; (1984) 3 SCC 369; (1984) SCC (L&S) 540; AIR 1984 SC 1064; 1984 (49) FLR 1; 1984 LabIC 790; 1984 (2) LLJ 223; 1984 (2) LLN 229; 1984(1) SCALE 586; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 325; 1984 (1) SLJ 575; 1984 UJ 986

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Payment Of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether the appellant is entitled for gratuity? - Constitution of India, 1950 envisages a society governed by rule of law - Absolute discretion uncontrolled by guidelines which may permit denial of equality before law is the antithesis of the rule of law - Absolute discretion not judicially reviewable inheres the pernicious tendency to be arbitrary and is, therefore, violative of art. 14 - Equality before law and absolute discretion to grant or deny benefit of the law are diametrically opposed to each other and cannot co-exist - Therefore, also the conterment of absolute discretion by r. 10 of the Gratuity Rules to give or deny the be rejected as unenforceable - Held, the company declined to meet its obligation on an utterly unreasonable stand and denied to the plaintiff for a period of a quarter of a century what the plaintiff was legitimately entitled without the slightest shadow of doubt - Therefore, while allowing the appeal in order to compensate the loss suffered by the plaintiff who died before enjoying the fruits of his decree, court direct that the interest shall be paid at 15% per annum - Appeal allowed.
