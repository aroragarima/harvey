Shrikant Anandrao Bhosale v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1928; (2002) 7 SCC 748; (2003) SCC (Cr) 144; AIR 2002 SC 3399; 2002 (4) CCR 94; 2002 (4) Crimes 365; 2003 (1) CRJ 421; 2002 CRLJ 4356; JT 2002 (7) SC 386; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 612; 2002(7) SCALE 37; 2002 (4) SCJ 459; 2002 (5) SLT 470; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 612; 2002 (6) Supreme 582

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 84 - Indian Evidence Act, 1972, s. 105 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability -- Appellant was found guilty by Trial Court for offence u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced to undergo RI for life - Appeal against conviction and sentence having been dismissed by HC appeal had been filed on grant of leave - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, appellant was suffering from paranoid schizophrenia unsoundness of mind before and after incident was relevant fact - From circumstances of case clearly inference could be reasonably drawn that appellant was under delusion at relevant time - He was under an attack of ailment - Anger theory on which reliance has been placed was not ruled out under schizophrenia attack - Having regard to nature of burden on appellant Court held that appellant had proved existence of circumstances as required by s. 105 of the Act so as to get benefit of s. 84 IPC - Court further held that crime was committed not as result of extreme fit of anger there was reasonable doubt that at time of commission of crime appellant was incapable of knowing nature of act by reason of unsoundness of mind and he was entitled to benefit of s. 84 IPC - Hence, conviction and sentence of appellant was not sustained - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
