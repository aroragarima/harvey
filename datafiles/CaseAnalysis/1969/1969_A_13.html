D. R. Gurushantappa v Abdul Khuddus Anwar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 258; (1969) 1 SCC 466; AIR 1969 SC 744; 1969 LabIC 1139; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 425

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Election - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25FF - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 58(2), 66(4), 102(1), 148 and 191(1)(a) - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Representation of the Peoples Act, 1951, ss. 6A and 10 - Disqualification - On the ground of holding of office of profit - Held, s. 10 limits scope of disqualification to holders of three particular offices only and in companies in which share holding of Govt. is not less than 25 per cent - This provision clearly indicates that, for purposes of determining disqualification for candidature to a Legislature, it would not be appropriate to attempt to lift veil and equate a company with Govt. merely because share-capital of company is contributed by Govt. - Appeal dismissed.
