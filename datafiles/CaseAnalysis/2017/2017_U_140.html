Re: Exploitation of Children in Orphanages, Tamil Nadu v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 2017 SC 2546; 2017 CRLJ 3217; JT 2017 (6) SC 393; 2017 (4) MLJ 358; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 887; 2017(5) SCALE 787

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Women & Children

Keywords: Voluntary, Welfare, Juvenile, Harassment, Jurisdiction, Abuse, Dealing, Adoption, Children, Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, Juvenile Justice (Care And Protection Of Children) Act, 2000, Sexual Harassment, Sexual Abuse, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, Commissions for Protection of Child Rights Act, 2005, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009, Mahatma Gandhi National Rural Employment Guarantee Act, 2005, Protection of Children from Sexual Offences Act, 2012, Registered, Constitution of India, 1950, Criminal, National Food Security Act, 2013, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, Adoption Regulations, 2017, Legal Services Authorities Act, 1986, Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Model Rules, 2016

Summary: Women & Children - Constitution - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2015, ss. 2(14), 41, 54 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Model Rules, 2016, rr. 19, 21, 89 - Child Abuse - Counselling sought - Validity - Petitioner filed instant PIL seeking that problem of sexual abuse of children especially in govt. institutions has become a serious problem and requires immediate redressal and recommended a Counseling Cell in each school where children were taught how to recognize abuse and providing a complaint mechanism accessible to children in case of any such incident.

Held, definition of the expression "child in need of care and protection" u/s. 2(14) of the Act should not be interpreted as an exhaustive definition. The definition was illustrative and benefits envisaged for children in need of care and protection should be extended to all such children in fact requiring State care and protection. Union Govts. and govts. of States and Union Territories were directed to enforce minimum standards of care as required by and in terms of the Act and the Rules positively on or before 31-12-2017. The govts. of States and Union Territories should draw up plans for full and proper utilization of grants (along with expenditure statements) given by Union Govt. under the Integrated Child Protection Scheme. It was imperative that Union Govt. and govts. of States and Union Territories should concentrate on rehabilitation and social re-integration of children in need of care and protection. The preparation of individual child care plans was extremely important and all govts. of States and Union Territories should ensure that there was a child care plan in place for every child in each child care institution. It was imperative that process of conducting a social audit should be taken up in right earnestness by National Commission for Protection of Child Rights as well as by each State Commission for Protection of Child Rights. SC request each Juvenile Justice Committee to seriously consider establishing a Secretariat for its assistance and direct each State Govt. and Union Territory to render assistance to Juvenile Justice Committee of each HC and to cooperate and collaborate with Juvenile Justice Committee on that regard. Order accordingly.
