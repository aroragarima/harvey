Hirabhai Jhaverbhai v State Of Gujarat And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 279; (2010) 6 SCC 688; AIR 2010 SC 2321; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1592; 2010 (3) G.L.R. 1941; 2010 (3) JCC 2087; JT 2010 (5) SC 404; 2010 (2) OLR 471; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 824; 2010(3) SCALE 679; 2010 (1) UC 679

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 324 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2005 - Compoundable offence - Appellant was convicted u/s. 324 of the IPC and the application seeking permission of the Court to compound offence punishable u/s. 324 IPC was rejected - Hence, present appeal - Held, after coming into force of the Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2005 from 23.06.2006 the offence u/s. 324 of IPC is made non-compoundable - However, in present case offence u/s. 324 of IPC was committed on 23.07.1986 on which date it was compoundable with the permission of the Court - Therefore, as the Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2005 is not applicable to the facts of the case, offence u/s 324 IPC would be compoundable with the permission of the Court - Injured complainant and two other injured permitted to compound the offence punishable u/s. 324 of IPC - Appeal allowed.
