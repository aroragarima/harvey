Â Mohd. Ayub Khan v Commissioner of Police, Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1965 SC 1623; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 162; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 884

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners; International

Keywords: Acquisition Of Citizenship, Deprivation Of Citizenship, Rights Of Citizenship

Summary: Foreigners - Practice & Procedure - Foreigners Act, 1946 - Foreigners Law (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss. 9(1) and (2) - Citizenship Rules,1956, r. 30(2), Sch. III, cl. 3 - (A) Whether an Indian citizen loses his citizenship on acquiring passport of another country u/s 9 of Citizenship Act, 1955? - Held, s. 9 lays down an objective test, once it is found that passport is obtained in fact by an Indian citizen from another country, law determines legal consequences of that conduct, no question of his intent or understanding arises - If voluntary acquisition of citizenship of another country determines Indian citizenship within meaning of s. 9(1), and by virtue of para 3 of Sch. III of Citizenship Rules, a conclusive presumption of voluntary acquisition of citizenship is to be raised from obtaining of a foreign passport, it would be implicit that passport was obtained voluntarily and citizen has to prove contrary - This view is strengthened by scheme of s. 9(2) r/w r. 30 which contemplates an enquiry by an authority prescribed under sub-section (2) for determination of question whether citizenship of another country has been acquired by an Indian citizen -(B) Whether termination of Indian citizenship and acquisition of foreign passport is voluntary or not, can be determined by foreign country?- Held, foreign country cannot similarly question, that acquisition of foreign passport is voluntary or not , it is as decided by India - Appeal allowed.
