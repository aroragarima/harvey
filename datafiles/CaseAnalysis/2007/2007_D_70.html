All Cargo Movers (I) Private Limited and Others v Dhanesh Badarmal Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1477; (2007) 14 SCC 776; AIR 2008 SC 247; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 306; JT 2007 (12) SC 345; 2007(12) SCALE 391; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Misappropriation

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 114, 407 and 420 - Companies Act, 1956 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Parties entered into a contract of carriage - Respondent approached appellants which are companies registered and incorporated under Indian Companies Act, 1956 - For delivery of six consignment to Kenya - Rejected on ground of being inferior in quality - Negligence attributed to agencies in delivering cargo without original bill of lading - Suit filed by respondent - Suit is still pending - More than one year after filing of suit a complaint petition was filed - Perusal of complaint petition shows that it did not contain any averment in regard to ingredients of offence u/ss. 406 and 420 IPC - Application for quashing of order issuing summons to appellant by Metropolitan Magistrate dismissed by High Court - Whether guilty of cheating? - Held, criminal proceedings should not be encouraged when it is found to be mala fide or otherwise an abuse of process of Court - Jurisdiction u/s. 482 of Code has to be exercised with great care - It is to be seen if a matter, which is essentially of a civil nature, has been given a cloak of criminal offence - Before issuing process, a criminal court has to exercise a great deal of caution - Substance of complaint is to be seen - Mere use of expression 'cheating' in complaint is of no consequence - For establishing offence of cheating, complainant is required to show that accused had fraudulent or dishonest intention at time of making promise or representation - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
