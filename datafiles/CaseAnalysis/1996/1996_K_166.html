Krishnan and Another v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2101; (1996) 10 SCC 508; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1375; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 521; AIR 1997 SC 383; AIR 1996 SCW 3754; 1996 (4) CCR 58; 1996 CRLJ 4444; JT 1996 (7) SC 613; 1996(6) SCALE 362; 1996 (3) SCJ 173; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 300 - Murder trial - Evidence - Incident occurring in open field on cloudless starry night - Plea of insufficiency of light for doubting presence of eye-witness at place of occurrence - (A) Whether material presence of undigested rice particles in deceased's stomach raises doubts about time of occurrence - Accused causing superficial injury to victim otherwise complicity of accused established - (B) Common intention - Murder - Whether proof of commission of overt act obligatory for conviction of accused u/s. 34 - Held, (A) Immaterial as eye-witnesses were close relatives of accused and had also attempted to prevent accused from assaulting victim - Need not raise doubts as digestion of rice can take place long after, three to four hours - Conviction of accused confirmed - (B) Not obligatory - All concerned persons had shared common intention is to be established by prosecution - Appeal dismissed.
