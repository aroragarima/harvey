Suryalakshmi Cotton Mills Ltd. v Rajvir Industries Ltd. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 16; (2008) 13 SCC 678; AIR 2008 SC 1683; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1422; 2008 (1) JCC (NI) 42; JT 2008 (1) SC 340; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 652; 2008(1) SCALE 331; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 432; 2008 (1) UC 453; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 5

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Legal Notice, Cheques, Subsequent Conduct, Non- Cognizable

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, ss. 138, 141 - First Information Report quashed - Appeal against - Held, maintainability of a criminal should not be determined only upon raising a presumption in terms of s. 139 of the Negotiable Instruments Act, it being a rebuttable one - High Court, ordinarily, shall not have interfered with investigation thereof by the statutory authority - Appeal partly allowed.
