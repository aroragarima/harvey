Case Analysis

Bench

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005

Summary: Hindu Succession Act, 1956-Sections 8 and 6-Ancestral Property of Hindu coparcenary-Succession-Male Hindu dying intestate-Plaintiff's (appellant) father got property on partition of ancestral property-Alienation of property-Case of plaintiff (appellant) that property received by his father in partition is ancestral property and, therefore, alienation of same by him-Null and Void-Plaintiff (appellant) prayed for declaration that release deed, sale-deeds and mutation entries in favour of respondent (defendant No. 1)-Illegal, null and void and not binding-When plaintiff's father got property in partition, it was his separate property vis-a-vis his relations-But after birth of plaintiff, plaintiff acquired interest in property as coparcener-Suit land ancestral property of Hindu coparcenary-Hence, sale deed and release deed executed by plaintiff's father to extent of entire coparcenary property-Illegal, null and void-View taken by lower appellate court as affirmed by High Court in second appeal-Erroneous in law-Impugned judgment and decree of lower appellate court as affirmed by High Court-Set aside-Judgment and decree of trial court-Restored.
