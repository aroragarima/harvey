Kulja Industries Limited v Chief Gen. Manager W.T. Proj. BSNL and others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 658; (2014) 14 SCC 731; AIR 2014 SC 9; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 142; 2014 (2) AWC 1776; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 178; 2013(12) SCALE 423

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Fraudulent

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 299-Government contracts-Blacklisting for allotment of future contracts-Respondent-B.S.N.L. blacklisted appellant-Company (contractor) for allotment of future contracts for all times to come-According to respondent-B.S.N.L. appellant-company had fraudulently withdrawn huge amount of money which was not due to it in collusion and conspiracy with officials of respondent-Corporation-Even so permanent debarment from future contracts for all times to come may sound too harsh and heavy punishment to be considered reasonable especially when (a) appellant-company supplying bulk of its manufactured products to respondent-B.S.N.L. and (b) excess amount received by it already been paid back-While blacklisting order of appellant-company affirmed, period for which such order remains operative to be determined afresh by competent authority-Matter remanded back to competent authority to determine period for which appellant-company would remain blacklisted.(2) Constitution of India-Article 299-Government contracts-Judicial review-Freedom to contract or not to contract is unqualified in case of private parties-But any such decision is subject to judicial review when same is taken by State or any of the instrumentalities-This implies that any such decision will be open to scrutiny not only on touchstone of principles of natural justice but also on doctrine of proportionality. .(3) Constitution of India-Article 299-Government contracts-Blacklisting-Fair hearing to party being blacklisted is essential pre-condition for proper exercise of power and for valid order of blacklisting made pursuant thereto-Order of blacklisting itself being reasonable, fair and proportionate to gravity of offence is similarly examinable by writ court.
