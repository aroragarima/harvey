Jagannath Choudhary and others v Ramayan Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 320; (2002) 5 SCC 659; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1181; AIR 2002 SC 2229; 2002 (2) CCR 222; 2002 (2) Crimes 409; 2002 (5) CRJ 719; 2002 CRLJ 2945; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2743; 2002 (2) JCC 1077; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 139; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 813B; 2002(4) SCALE 492; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 936; 2002 (3) SLT 616; 2002 (4) Supreme 21; 2002 (2) UC 404

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: The main question which the High Court must consider in an application in revision under Section 401 of the Code of Criminal Procedure is whether substantial justice has been done, and if, the order passed by the trial court is presentable, without any such infirmity which may render it completely perverse or unacceptable and when there is no failure of justice, the High Court cannot interfere in exercise of its revisional jurisdiction.
