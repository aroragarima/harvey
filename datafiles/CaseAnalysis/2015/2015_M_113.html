Shailesh Dhairyawan v Mohan Balkrishna Lulla

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 737; (2016) 3 SCC 619; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 1; 2016 (1) AWC 8; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 734; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 196; 2015 (4) CompLJ 434; JT 2015 (9) SC 461; 2015 (7) MLJ 876; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 928; 2015(11) SCALE 684

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitration Clause, Foreign Awards (Recognition And Enforcement) Act, 1961, Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Uncitral

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 15(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 89 -Appointment of Substitute Arbitrator - Authority of HC - Justification - Parties to suit entered into consent terms settling disputes between them - However, with regard to two specific differences, respondent and appellant agreed to refer said differences to arbitration of retired SC Judge - However, nothing materialized and arbitrator resigned as arbitrator - Further, respondent vide notice applied for appointment of substitute arbitrator, however, notice was dismissed - Further, HC appointed retired Judge of HC as substitute arbitrator - Appellant contended that u/s. 15(2) of Act, where mandate of named arbitrator terminates, there being no rules that would apply to appointment of arbitrator being replaced, s. 15(2) of Act would not apply - Hence, HC is wrong in appointing substitute arbitrator - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, there is nothing in clause of consent terms to show that resignation of arbitrator would lead to her vacancy not being supplied. All that parties have done by clause is to agree to refer their disputes to arbitration of independent retired Judge belonging to higher Judiciary. There is no personal qualification of resigned arbitrator that is required to decide dispute between parties. Further, SC held that resigned judge belongs to pool of independent retired HC and SC Judges, from which it is always open to appointing authority to choose substitute arbitrator. Further, following mandate of s. 89 of CPC, HC disposed of suit between parties by recording settlement between parties a by referring remaining disputes to arbitration. Further, SC held that HC applied its mind to consent terms as whole and appointed arbitrator for disputes that were left to be resolved by parties. Further, said appointing authority has been approached by respondent for appointment of substitute arbitrator, which was then done by impugned judgment. Hence, HC correctly appointed another independent retired Judge as substitute arbitrator in terms of s. 15(2) of Act.

Further, whenever parties agree for mediation, and even name specific arbitrator with no specific provision for appointment of another arbitrator on recusal/withdrawal of said arbitrator, said omission is made up by s. 15(2) of Act and unless arbitration agreement between parties provides categorical prohibition or debarment in resolving question or dispute or difference between parties by substitute arbitrator in case of death or named arbitrator or non-availability of said arbitrator, Courts have power to appoint substitute arbitrator, which power is given by s. 15(2) of Act as provision is to be given liberal interpretation so as to apply to all possible circumstances under which mandate of earlier arbitrator may be terminated. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - It is justified to appoint substitute arbitrator when agreement entered between parties does not specifically bar appointment of such arbitrator.
