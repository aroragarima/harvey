Jagir Kaur and Another v Jaswant Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 387; AIR 1963 SC 1521; 1963 CRLJ 413; 1964 (1) SCJ 386; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 73

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Last Resided

Summary: Criminal - Jurisdiction - Criminal Procedure Code, s. 488(8) - Maintenance proceedings - Whether Magistrate of Ludhiana had jurisdiction to entertain petition filed u/s. 488(8) of CrPC, which demarcates jurisdiction limits of a Court to entertain a petition? - Temporary residence with animus manendi will amount to residence within meaning of provisions of sub-section - Held, yes - Appellant last resided within jurisdiction of First Class Magistrate - S. 488 prescribes alternative forums to deserted wife or helpless child, to get urgent relief - Proceedings against husband or father, can be initiated in a place where he resides, permanently or temporarily, or where he last resided in any district in India or where he happens to be at time proceedings are initiated - First Class Magistrate, Ludhiana, had jurisdiction to entertain petition u/s. 488(8) - Appeal allowed.
