Laxmi Smt v Om Prakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20085; (2001) 6 SCC 118; (2001) SCC (Cr) 993; AIR 2001 SC 2383; AIR 2001 SCW 2481; 2001 (3) CCR 24; 2001 (3) Crimes 140; 2001 CRLJ 3302; 2001 (2) JCC 71; JT 2001 (5) SC 280; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 358; 2001(4) SCALE 241; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 777; 2001 (5) SLT 136; 2001 (5) Supreme 26

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India,1950, art. 136 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 34, 302, 385 - Suicide/murder - Dying declarations - Circumstantial evidence - Validity - Accused informed police over phone that deceased committed suicide burnt herself by pouring kerosene on her - Police came and shifter her to hospital in PCR van - On enquiry from neighbours police came to know that it was attempt to suicide - Deceased gave 5 dying declarations(DD) that accused/ her husband, mother in law and sister in law had set her ablaze and died on next day - Accused were arrested - Trial Court did not rely on DD's based on witnesses evidence and acquitted accused - Hence instant appeal by mother of deceased - Whether Trial Court was justified in acquitting accused persons - Held, DD had utmost value but it should be proved that person making it was of sound and fit mental condition - Doctors held that deceased suffered 85% deep burns and hence gave heavy sedation, antibiotics and fluids and gasping for breath and both her lips were burnt and her condition was deteriorating - In that circumstances alleged DD's were doubted as her condition was not good and as not proved by circumstantial evidence - Roznamcha register was not produced by prosecution even after ample time was given -DD should always be recorded by Magistrate and could be recorded by police in exceptional cases only - No statement or signature of doctor in all DD's with regard to fit mental condition of deceased - DD recorded by Magistrate had grave doubts - SC in Kake Singh @ Surendra Singh Vs State of M.P,1981 Indlaw SC 16 refused DD as there was no specific statement by doctor that deceased was in fit mental condition to make statement - SC in Mohar Singh and Ors. etc. Vs. State of Punjab , 1981 Indlaw SC 49 did not consider DD recorded by IO as it was not attested by doctor who was present in the hospital or by anyone else present - In Munnu Raja and Anr. Vs. The State of Madhya Pradesh, 1975 Indlaw SC 280 did not consider DD recorded by IO as failed to explain why services of Magistrate were not obtained - PW1 evidence clearly proved intention of deceased that she would implicate accused even at cost of her death based on facts and circumstances of case - Making five DD's in such state of deteriorating condition was doubted and could not be believed - Trial Court was justified in acquitting accused - Appeal dismissed.
