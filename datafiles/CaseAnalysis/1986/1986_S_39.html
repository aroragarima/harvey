Sawai Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 269; (1986) 3 SCC 454; (1986) SCC (L&S) 662; AIR 1986 SC 995; 1986 (53) FLR 172; 1986 LabIC 855; 1986 (2) LLJ 390; 1986 (2) LLN 91; 1986(1) SCALE 1282; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 957; 1986 (2) SLJ 265; 1986 (2) SLR 47; 1986 (2) UJ 605

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appellant was an employee of Rajasthan Govt. and was appointed as returning officer to conduct Panchayat elections - Inquiry was proposed to be held against appellant on charge that he showed undue favour to one of contesting candidates and manipulated withdrawal of contesting candidate and malafidely and improperly rejected his nomination form - Departmental inquiry - Removal from service - Challenged - Charges involving consequences of termination of service must be specific, though a departmental inquiry is not like a criminal trial - There is no such rule that an offence is not established unless it is proved beyond doubt - But a departmental inquiry entailing consequences like loss of job which now-a-days means loss of livelihood, there must be fair play in action - In respect of an order involving adverse or penal consequences against an employee, there must be investigations to charges consistent with requirement of situation in accordance with principles of natural justice in so far as these are applicable in a particular situation - Application of those principles of natural justice must always be in conformity with scheme of Act and subject matter of case - Held, having regard to consequences of offences with which delinquent officer was charged and having regard to nature of charge and evidence of hand-writing expert and absence of opportunity for cross-examination and conflicting nature of evidence, report of inquiry officer finding appellant guilty should not have been sustained and Govt. should not have acted upon it - High Court was in error in not bearing in mind these aspects which have been indicated - Appeal allowed.
