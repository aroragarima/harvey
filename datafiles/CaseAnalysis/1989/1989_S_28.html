Gurbachan Singh v Satpal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 44; (1990) SCC (Cr) 151; (1990) 1 SCC 445; AIR 1990 SC 209; 1989 (3) Crimes 526; 1990 CRLJ 562; 1990 CrLR(SC) 100; 1990 (3) DMC 1; 1990 (1) HLR 353; JT 1989 (4) SC 38; 1989(2) SCALE 677; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 292; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 292

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Dowry death - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 and 498-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-A - Deceased a newly married girl - She was constantly harassed for bringing less dowry - Allegations were also made against her for carrying an illegitimate child - Abetment caused by husband of deceased and his parents - Whether order of acquittal of appellants was justified? - Held, no - Adequate evidence to effect that deceased was constantly harassed for bringing less dowry and carrying an illegitimate child - Testimony of family members of deceased inspire confidence - Acquittal set aside - Appellants convicted u/s. 306 of IPC, 1860 - Appeal allowed.
