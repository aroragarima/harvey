Pandurang Chandrakant Mhatre & Ors. v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1456; (2009) 10 SCC 773; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 204; JT 2009 (13) SC 242; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 431; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 612; 2009(13) SCALE 177; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Distinct Offence, Partisan Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 307 - Murder - Unlawful assembly - Conviction - Challenged - Alleged, accused persons (nineteen in number) caused grievous hurt to victims including death of deceased - Trial Court acquitted all the accused persons - On appeal, HC reversed conclusion of Trial Court in respect of eight accused persons and convicted them for offences punishable u/ss. 302/149, 326/194 and s. 148, IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC justified in interfering with judgment of acquittal passed by Trial Court? - Held, evidence of eye-witnesses corroborated by medical evidence - Prosecution established that assailants comprised of more than five persons and they formed unlawful assembly and at least five persons chased deceased and then attacked him - Conviction of A-2, A-3, and A-12 u/s. 302/149 upheld as their overt acts in chasing and assaulting deceased proved - A-4, A-5, A-6, A-10 and A-11 given benefit of doubt with regard to offence u/s. 302/149, IPC since evidence against them not consistent - However, all eight appellants guilty of offences punishable u/s. 148 and s. 326/149 IPC - Judgment of Trial Court acquitting all accused persons suffered from factual and legal errors justifying interference by HC in appeal within permissible limits - Appeal partly allowed.
