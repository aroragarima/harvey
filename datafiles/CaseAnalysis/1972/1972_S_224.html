Gunwantlal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 558; (1972) 2 SCC 194; (1972) SCC (Cr) 678; AIR 1972 SC 1756; 1972 CRLJ 1187; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 508; 1973 UJ 72

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 25(1)(a) and 39 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 189 - Arms Act, 1878, ss. 14, 15 and 19 - Whether the offence u/s. 25(1)(a) of the Arms Act, 1959 is confined only to the case of a person who has physical possession of the firearm or does it include a person having constructive possession of the firearm, or over which he exercises the power to obtain possession thereof when he so intends? - The first precondition for an offence u/s. 25(1)(a) is the element of intention, consciousness or knowledge with which a person possessed the firearm before it can be said to constitute an offence and secondly that possession need not be physical possession but can be constructive, having power and control over the gun, while the person to whom physical possession is given holds it subject to that power and control -Under the Arms Act all that is required for sanction u/s. 39 is, that the person to be prosecuted was found to be in possession of the firearm, the date or dates on which he was so found in possession and the possession of the firearm was without a valid licence - Held, if the possession of the appellant includes the constructive possession of the firearm in question then even though he had parted with physical possession on the date when it was recovered, he will nonetheless be deemed to be in possession of that firearm, therefore, the charge that he was in possession of the revolver on 17-09-1966, does not suffer from any defect particularly when he is definitely informed in that charge that he had control over that revolver - Since all the elements are contained in the sanction in this case, it is not an illegal sanction nor can it be said that the charge travels beyond that sanction - Appeal dismissed.
