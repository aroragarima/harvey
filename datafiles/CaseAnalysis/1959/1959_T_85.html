Â Union of India and Others v Messrs. Bhana Mal Gulzari Maland Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 127; AIR 1960 SC 475; 1960 CRLJ 664; 1960 SCJ 584; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Iron and Steel (Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, Iron and Steel [Control of Production and Distribution] Order 1941

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1) (f) (g) - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, ss. 2,3,6,7 - Iron & Steel Control of Production and Distribution Order,1941, cls. 2(d), 11B - Sale of Iron and Steel - Price - Challenged - Respondent registered as stockholder by Iron and Steel Controller u/cl. 2(d) of Order - Respondents sold their old stock of steel for higher prices than those prescribed by appellant/Controller u/cl.11B of the Order - Appellant filed several criminal proceedings against respondents - Aggrieved respondents filed writ petitions before HC which quashed criminal proceedings against respondents and declared cl.11B of the Order was unconstitutional, inoperative and infringes fundamental rights guaranteed/art.19 (1)(f) and (g) of the Constitution - Hence, instant appeal - Whether cl. 11B of the Order was violates provisions of art.19(1) (f) (g) - Held, that by prescribing maximum prices for different categories of iron and steel cl. 11B directly carries out legislative object prescribed in s. 3 of the Act - Court opined that, if cl. 11B was considered as part of composite scheme evidenced by whole of the Order and its validity was examined in the light of provisions of ss. 3 and 4 of the Act, it would be difficult to sustain plea that it confers on the delegate uncanalised or unbridled power - Court was inclined to hold that power conferred on Central Govt. by s. 3 and on authority specified by s. 4 was canalised by clear enunciation of legislative policy in s. 3 and that cl. 11B of the Order seeks further to canalize exercise of said power -Therefore cl. 11B of the Order was not unconstitutional on ground of excessive delegation - Powers exercisable by Controller u/cl. 11B of the Order were in terms made subject to general power of Central Govt. to give directions prescribed by cl. 12 of the Order - In Harishankar Bagla v. The State of Madhya Pradesh 1954 Indlaw SC 135, relied on - Hence, hold that neither cl. 11B of the Order nor impugned notification issued by Controller on 10-12- 1949, violate respondents' fundamental rights u/arts. 19(1)(f) and (g), and so their validity could not be successfully challenged - Appeals allowed.
