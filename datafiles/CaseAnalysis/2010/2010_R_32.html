Commissioner of Central Excise, Jaipur v Rajasthan Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 485; (2010) 12 SCC 186; 2010 (255) E.L.T. 481; JT 2010 (7) SC 64; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 717; 2010(6) SCALE 662; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57Q - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, chapter 85 - MODVAT Credit on capital goods - Assessee availed MODVAT credit on 'capital goods' in respect of steel plates and M.S. channels used by them for erection of chimney for the diesel generating set, falling under Chapter 85 of the Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Show cause notice issued to the assessee alleging that MODVAT credit availed of on steel plates and M.S. channels used in the fabrication of chimney, was inadmissible as the subject items were not 'capital goods' - Assessee submitted that the items in question being components of chimney which in turn was an accessory of the diesel generating set, falling under heading 85.02, they also qualify the test of 'capital goods' and therefore, MODVAT credit in respect of the said items was clearly admissible - Assistant Commissioner disallowed the MODVAT credit availed of by the assessee and imposed a penalty of Rs.2000/- - Commissioner (Appeal) dismissed appeal filed against said order, however, deleted the penalty levied on the assessee - Tribunal allowed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether the assessee was right in availing MODVAT credit in respect of the impugned items by treating them as 'capital goods' in terms of r. 57Q? - Held, yes - Applying the 'user test', the steel plates and M.S. Channels, used in the fabrication of chimney would fall within the ambit of 'capital goods' as contemplated in r. 57Q of Rules - Moreover, it is not the case of the Revenue that both these items are not required to be used in the fabrication of chimney, which is an integral part of the diesel generating set, particularly when the Pollution Control laws make it mandatory that all plants which emit effluents should be so equipped with apparatus which can reduce or get rid of the effluent gases - Therefore, any equipment used for the said purpose has to be treated as an accessory - Order of tribunal was correct - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
