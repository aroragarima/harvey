D. N. Chanchala and Others v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 641; (1971) 2 SCC 293; AIR 1971 SC 1762; [1971] S.C.R. 608; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Mysore Medical Colleges (Selection for Admission) Rules, 1970, rr. 4, 9(1), 5, 3, 4(h) - Constitution, arts. 14 and 15(4) - Education - Validity - Challenged on grounds - (A) Rules providing for requirements over and above the minimum qualifications laid down by the Universities in the State for eligibility for admission - (B) Constitutionality - (1) Of r. 9(1) - University-wise distribution of seats in the Govt. Colleges - (2) Of r. 4 - Setting apart 60 seats at the disposal of Government - (3) Of r. 5 - Reservation of 48% of seats for SC, STs and for socially and educationally backward classes, though apparently appearing on high side - (4) Of r. 4(h) - Provision making reservation for children of political sufferers - (C) Resident of the State - If mean an intermittent stay -- Held, (A) Rules cannot be challenged for providing - (B) University wise distribution of seats in the Government Colleges is not violative of art.14 - Is not a 'reservation' within art.15 of Constitution but laying down sources for selection necessitated by overriding considerations such as obligations towards those who serve the interest of country's security certain reciprocal obligations and the like and no objection, therefore can be taken on the ground that it is excessive - Held not violative of art.15 (4) as it had not been shown as unreasonably excessive - Held, not unconstitutional as the definition for children of political sufferer not only makes the children of such sufferers distinguishable from the rest but such classification has reasonable nexus with the object of the rules to make just and fair distribution of seats - Residence as contemplated by the provision must prima facie have an element of continuity or regularity in residence and would not mean an intermittent stay such as during the vacations Petition Dismissed.
