Traders and Agencies and Another v Meenal Eknath Kshirsagar (Mrs)

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3033; (1996) 5 SCC 344; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 606; AIR 1997 SC 59; 1996 (3) CCC 154; 1996 (3) CLT 275; JT 1996 (6) SC 468; 1997 (1) RCJ 395; 1996 (2) RCR(Rent) 233; 1996 (2) RentLR 399; 1996(5) SCALE 302; 1996 (5) Supreme 635; 1996 (2) UJ 703

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Suit for eviction on ground that requires suit premises reasonably and bona fide for personal occupation in Court of Small Cause - Trial court passed decree for eviction - On appeal Appellate Bench reversed order of eviction, suit dismissed - Appellant, filed writ petition in High Court which dismissed - Whether Appellate Bench and HC applied correct test while determining question of bonafide need of suit premises for occupation? - Held, the fact that the appellant is the owner of the suit premises and that she does not own any other premises in the city of Bombay is not in dispute - She does not possess, even as a tenant, any premises in Bombay - No doubt, she would be entitled to stay in the premises of which her husband is a tenant but if for any reason her husband had parted with possession of such premises and the same were occupied by her husband's brother, it cannot be said that the said premises were available to her and by not referring to those facts she had come to the court with unclean hands and that by itself was sufficient to disentitle her from getting a decree of eviction - If appellant believed that 'Olympus' flat was not available for occupation as same was vacated by her husband many years back and was occupied by Sridhar and his family and not possible or convenient for her and her family to go and stay there, it was not absolutely necessary for her to refer to those facts in her plaint - Therefore Appellate Bench and HC clearly went wrong in holding that said omission was sufficient to disentitle her from getting a decree of eviction and her claim was mala fide and not bona fide as required by law - Appeal allowed.
