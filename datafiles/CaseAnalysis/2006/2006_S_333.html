State of Bihar and Others v Amrendra Kumar Mishra

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 524; (2006) 12 SCC 561; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 132; 2006 (6) AWC 6037; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 33; 2006(9) SCALE 549; 2006 (6) SLR 1; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Respondent could not join service - Representation for allowing him to join so late - HC allowed him to join - Appeal against - Held, Life of a panel, it is well known, remains valid for a year - Once it lapses, unless an appropriate order is issued by State, no appointment can be made out of said panel - Respondent did not have any legal right to be appointed - In absence of any legal right, Court should not issue a writ of or in nature of mandamus on basis of sympathy - Appeal allowed.
