Champalal Binani v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 330; (1971) 3 SCC 20; AIR 1970 SC 645; [1970] 76 ITR 692; 1970 UJ 274

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 33 B and 63(1) - Show cause notice u/s. 33B - Revision of assessment - Three notices sent to assessee - Assessee was not present on date of hearing - Assessee did not prefer an appeal, instead moved a petition in High Court for a writ quashing order of Commr. of Income Tax - HC dismissed the same - (A) Whether in passing order u/s. 33B of Income Tax Act, 1922, Commr. had 'violated principles of natural justice' and 'express provisions of law'? - Held, the assessee had an adequate remedy under Income Tax Act which he could have availed of - Commr. had taken early steps to serve notice upon assessee - The conduct of assessee leaves little room for doubt that he was anxious to ensure that period of two years within which Commr. was competent to make in order u/s. 33 B should expire - HC was right in holding that Order u/s. 33 B of Income Tax Act was properly passed - (B) Whether HC was competent to try present case? - Held, the assessee had an adequate remedy under Income Tax Act which he could have availed of - Where party feeling aggrieved by an order of an authority under Income Tax Act has an adequate alternative remedy which he may resort to against improper action of authority and he does not avail himself of that remedy HC will require a strong case to be made out for entertaining a petition for a writ - Further held, present case was one in which jurisdiction of HC could not be invoked - Appeal dismissed.
