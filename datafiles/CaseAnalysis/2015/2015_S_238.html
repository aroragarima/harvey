Baldev Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 784; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4967; 2016 (1) CGLJ 163; 2016 CRLJ 154; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3773; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 486; 2015(12) SCALE 308; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 969

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Credibility, Distance

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 16, 17, 18, 35, 61, 85 - Possession of Narcotic drug - Conviction - Justification - On day of incident, appellant was searched by police personnel and bags of narcotic drugs were found by them - FIR was registered against appellant u/ss. 15,16,61 and 85 of Act - Further, Trial Court acquitted appellant observing that no other witness except PW 1 police inspector was examined and that PW 1' s evidence was not trustworthy to base conviction - However, HC vide impugned order convicted appellant u/s. 15 of Act on account of having been found in possession of narcotic drug - Whether impugned order passed by HC convicting appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, there is no legal proposition that evidence of police officials unless supported by independent evidence is unworthy of acceptance. SC held that evidence of police witnesses cannot be discarded merely on ground that they belong to police force and interested in investigation and their desire to see success of case. Further, mere fact that they are police officials does not by itself give rise to any doubt about their creditworthiness. SC held that testimony of PW1 and evidence on record amply establishes physical possession of contraband by appellant. Further, SC held that appellant being driver of vehicle by all probabilities must have been aware of contents of bags transported in trolley attached to tractor. Once physical possession of contraband by accused has been proved, s. 35 of Act comes into play and burden shifts on appellant accused to prove that he was not in conscious possession of contraband. Further, mere non-examination of IO does not in every case cause prejudice to accused or affects credibility of prosecution case. Further, SC held that police officials had no previous enmity with appellant. Hence, SC held that prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt. Impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. However, SC held that occurrence was in year 1990 and appellant has suffered protracted proceeding of about twenty five years. Hence, sentence of imprisonment imposed on appellant is reduced from twelve years to ten years. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - No infirmity is attached to testimony of police officials merely because they belong to police force, hence, conviction can be based on testimony of police officials.
