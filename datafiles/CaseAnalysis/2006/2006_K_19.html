K.N. Sathyapalan (Dead) By Lrs v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 867; (2007) 13 SCC 43; 2007 (5) AWC 5233; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 9; JT 2006 (10) SC 615; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 270; 2006(12) SCALE 654; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration Act - Whether in the absence of any price escalation clause in Original Agreement and a specific prohibition to the contrary in the Supplemental Agreement, the appellant could have made any claim on account of escalation of costs?; whether Arbitrator exceeded his jurisdiction in allowing such claims as had been found by the High Court? - Held, appellant was prevented by unforeseen circumstances from completing the work within the stipulated period of eleven months and that such delay could have been prevented had the State Government stepped in to maintain the law and order problem which had been created at the work site - Even space for dumping of excess earth was not provided by the respondents which compelled the appellant to dump the excess earth at a place which was far away from the work site entailing extra costs for the same - Arbitrator appears to have acted within his jurisdiction in allowing some of the claims on account of escalation of costs which was referable to the execution of the work during the extended period - Appeal allowed.
