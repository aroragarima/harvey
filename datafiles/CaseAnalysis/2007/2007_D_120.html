D. Gopinathan Pillai v Dr.Ar.Lakshmanan & Altamas Kabir

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 43; (2007) 2 SCC 322; AIR 2007 SC 2624; 2007(4) ALL MR 313; 2007 (68) ALR 330; 2007 (2) AWC 1935; 2007 (3) MahLJ 54; 2007 (2) MPLJ 293; 2007(2) SCALE 222; 2007 (4) SCJ 847; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Petition to set aside the award happened to be decided against the appellant - Held, when a mandatory provision is not complied with and when the delay is not properly, satisfactorily and convincingly explained, the court cannot condone the delay, only on the sympathetic ground - Orders passed by the learned Sub Judge and also by the High Court are far from satisfactory - Appeal allowed.
