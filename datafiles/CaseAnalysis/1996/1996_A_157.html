State of Orissa and Others v Arnab Kumar Dutta

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3797; (1996) 7 SCC 203; (1996) SCC (L&S) 489; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 829; 1996 (32) ATC 10; 1996 (32) ATC 810; 1996 (721) FLR 833; JT 1996 (2) SC 516; 1996 (2) LLJ 87; 1996(1) SCALE 539; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 892; 1996 (2) SLR 474; 1996 (1) Supreme 634

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Orissa Service Code, r. 71 (a) - Retention of service till age of 60 years - Whether a 'workman' as per second proviso to rule entitled to retain in service up to age of 60 years? - Object of rule appears to bring 'artisan-workman' on a par with Class IV employees and he alone is required to retire on completion of 60 years of age - Not gazetted or non - gazetted Class III Govt. servants or even Class II or I which would be result if all artisans were given benefit of retention of service up to 60 years inasmuch as even a Director of Town Planning or Chief Architect could be considered to be an artisan - Held, a draftsman would not be a workman to get benefit of retention of service up to age of 60 years - Appeal allowed.
