Earabhadrappa Alias Krishnappa v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 161; (1983) 2 SCC 330; (1983) SCC (Cr) 447; AIR 1983 SC 446; 1983 (1) Crimes 784; 1983 CRLJ 846; 1983 CrLR(SC) 268; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Murder and robbery - Conviction on the basis of circumstantial evidence - Legality of - Held, in cases in which evidence is purely of a circumstantial nature, facts and circumstances from which conclusion of guilt is sought to be drawn must be fully established beyond any reasonable doubt - Fact and circumstances should not only be consistent with guilt of accused but they must be in their effect as to be entirely incompatible with innocence of accused and must exclude every reasonable hypothesis consistent with his innocence - For applicability of s.27 therefore two conditions are prerequisite, namely (1) information must be such as has caused discovery of fact; and (2) information must 'relate distinctly' to fact discovered - Circumstances alleged have been fully proved and they are consistent only with hypothesis of guilt of accused - Appeal dismissed.
