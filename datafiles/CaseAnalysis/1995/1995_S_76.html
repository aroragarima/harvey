Sate Of Punjab And Ors. v Chaman Lal Goyal

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 162; (1995) SCC (L&S) 541; (1995) 2 SCC 570; 1995 (29) ATC 546; 1995 (1) CLR 429; 1995 (70) FLR 834; JT 1995 (2) SC 18; 1995 (2) LLJ 679; 1995(1) SCALE 390; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 695; 1995 (2) SLJ 126; 1995 (1) SLR 700; 1995 (1) UJ 552

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether delay in serving charges be a ground for quashing charges? - Disciplinary proceeding must be conducted soon after irregularities are committed - They cannot be initiated after lapse of considerable time - Such delay also makes task of proving charges difficult and is thus not also in interest of administration - Delayed initiation of proceedings is bound to give room for allegations of bias, malafides and misuse of power - Held, If delay is too long and is unexplained, Court may well interfere and quash charges - But how long a delay is too long always depends upon facts of given case - Moreover, if such delay is likely to cause prejudice to delinquent officer in defending himself, enquiry has to be interdicted - Wherever such a plea is raised, Court has to weigh factors appearing for and against said plea and take a decision on totality of circumstances - In other words, Court has to indulge in a process of balancing - Appeal allowed.
