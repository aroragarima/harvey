Bobby Art International v Om Pal Singh Hoon and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2587; (1996) 4 SCC 1; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 281; AIR 1996 SC 1846; 1996 (2) CCC 193; 1996 (2) CLT 333; JT 1996 (4) SC 533; 1996(4) SCALE 75; 1996 (3) SCJ 70; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 136; 1996 (3) Supreme 772

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Media & Entertainment; Tort

Keywords: Examining Committee, Board Of Film Censors, Film Censor

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Socio-Economic - Tort - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(2) and 226 - Cinematograph Act, 1952, ss. 5-A, 5-B, 5-C and 5-D - Cinematograph (Certification) Rules, 1983, r. 24(1) - Tribunal granted an 'A' certificate to film - Respondent filed writ petition before High Court seeking to quash the certificate and restrain its exhibition - Single Judge quashed the certificate - Division Bench upheld Single Judge order - Order of the HC challenged - Court held, that scenes of nudity and rape and the use of expletives, so far as the Tribunal had permitted them, were in aid of the theme and intended not to arouse prurient or lascivious thoughts but revulsion against the perpetrators and pity for the victim, therefore 'A' certificate issued to the film by the Appellate Tribunal is restored - Appeal allowed.
