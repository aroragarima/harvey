Raju Ramsing Vasave v Mahesh Deorao Bhivapurkar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2626; (2008) 9 SCC 54; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 514; 2008 (4) AWC 3951; JT 2008 (9) SC 445; 2008(12) SCALE 252; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 992; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976, Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, De-notified Tribes, (Vimukta Jatis), Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Category Regulation of Issuance and Verification of Caste Certificate Act, 2000

Summary: (A) Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226, 141, 142 - Special Leave Petition (SLP) with public interest - Maintainability - 1st respondent filed a writ petition before the HC claiming that he belonged to the Scheduled Tribe of Halba Khoshtis - HC allowed 1st respondent's writ petition on the basis of DB decision in Milind Sharad Katware and others v. State of Maharashtra and others, 1987 Mh. L.J. 572 - Subsequently, SC, in Milind case, 2000 INDLAW SC 2635, held that only the Parliament had the authority to include or exclude a tribe into the list of Scheduled Tribe and not any other authority - 1st respondent sought Scheduled Tribe Caste Certificate - Authorities referred 1st respondent's case to Schedule Tribe Certificate Scrutiny Committee (STCSC) - STCSC rejected 1st respondent's claim - Decision of STCSC was also upheld by Caste Scrutiny Committee (CSC) - In the meantime, appellant and 1st respondent together with two others were appointed as Field Officers in State Pollution Control Board (SPCB) wherein 1st respondent and appellant were placed at Sl. No. 69 and 73 of the Select List respectively against the names of Scheduled Tribe candidates - 1st respondent, subsequently, challenged the CSC order before the HC - HC, by impugned order, held that CSC wrongly interpreted the earlier HC order and committed an error in refusing to grant caste certificate to 1st respondent - Further, HC directed the CSC to grant Scheduled Tribe certificate to 1st respondent - Whether a co-employee of the 1st respondent who was working as a Field Officer with the SPCB can maintain an independent special leave questioning the judgment of a HC setting aside an order of the CSC - Held, a special leave petition ordinarily would not have been entertained at the instance of the appellant - Validity of appointment or otherwise on the basis of a caste certificate granted by a committee is ordinarily a matter between the employer and the employee - SC, however, when a question is raised, can take cognizance of a matter of such a grave importance suo motu and such SLP may not be treated as a Public Interest Litigation, but as a Public Law Litigation (PLL) - PLL is, in a proceeding of that nature, permissible for the court to make a detailed enquiry with regard to the broader aspects of the matter although it was initiated at the instance of a person having a private interest - SLP of the appellant cannot be dismissed because as a constitutional court, it is the duty of the SC to lay down the law correctly so that same mistakes are not committed in future -Therefore, present private case is a fit case for adjudication in the interest of public - Appeal allowed.

(B) Administrative - Socio-Economic - Claim of Scheduled Tribe - Entitlement - Whether 1st respondent is entitled to the benefit of Scheduled Tribe 'Halba Khoshtis' in terms of impugned HC judgment - Held, factual foundation arrived at by CSC that a person was not a member of the Scheduled Tribe would remain operative unless set aside by a superior court - Judgment of the HC in favour of the 1st respondent was rendered on a wrong premise - Claim of the respondent may be that he belonged to the Halba tribe but, no factual foundation was placed before the HC - HC relied solely on its earlier decision to hold that Koshti would come within the purview of the Scheduled Tribe of Halba or Halbi - Records maintained by the school where the respondent studied were not placed before the HC - If the vital evidences were ignored while granting a caste certificate, it would be open to the CSC to arrive at a different conclusion - HC proceeded on the premise that once the surname of the respondent tallied with the name of the tribe, which finds mention in one or the other entries of the Schedule appended to the Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes Order (Amendment) Act, 1976, the same must be treated to be sacrosanct and no enquiry in relation to the correctness of the said certificate can be gone into by any committee - Therefore, observations and directions of the HC were not only contrary to the judgments of the SC but also fall short of the ground realities - Since appellant and 1st respondent were in service for long time, their appointments would not be disturbed, however, they would be treated as general candidates in the matter of promotion and other wise - Appeal allowed.
