Sohan Lal and Others v Amin Chand and Sons and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 273; (1973) 2 SCC 608; AIR 1973 SC 2572; 1982 (2) PTC 390; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 453; 1973 UJ 872

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Arbitration Act, English Arbitration Act (ENG)

Summary: Intellectual Property - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 13(b) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 107, O. 47 r. 7 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 43 - (A) Whether there was nay justification for passing order of injunction and appellate court was right in confirming it? - Held, order of the Trial Court was a combined order granting the review and disposing of the application for injunction on merits - Therefore, the appellants were not only entitled to challenge the order on the grounds mentioned in O. 47 r. 7, but also on merits - (B) Whether the respondents have the exclusive right of using the Trade Mark of the firm? - Held, respondents not entitled to exclusive use of two trade marks which formed part of assets of partnership firm in which three brothers were partners - Appeal being LRs of deceased partner also entitled to shares of assets of that partnership firm - No injunction can be granted restraining appellants from using trade marks - Appeal allowed.
