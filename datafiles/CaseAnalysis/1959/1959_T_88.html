INDIAN HUME PIPE Company Limited v WORKMEN and ANOTHER

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1960 SC 251; 1959 (2) LLJ 830; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, Provident Funds Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947,s. 25F - Retrenchment - Claim of gratuity along retrenchment compensation - Challenged - Disputes arose between appellant/company and respondents/workers and on reference Tribunal had passed award dt.13-8-1951 wherein scheme of gratuity was provided - Award became final as appeals of both parties were dismissed by appellate Tribunal - Respondents issued notice terminating award demanding gratuity at higher rate along with retrenchment compensation - Reference was made to Tribunal as conciliation proceedings failed - Tribunal held that respondents/workers were entitled to claim both gratuity and retrenchment compensation - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal and was dismissed by Appellate Tribunal but made some changes in Award - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of appellate Tribunal was justified - Held, gratuity was retirement benefit intended to help them after retirement whereas retrenchment compensation was not retirement benefit - Retrenched employee could claim gratuity in addition to retrenchment compensation - Legislature had incorporated s. 25 F in the 1947 Act only to have uniform procedure and to regulate irregular practices by industries - s. 25 F of the 1947 did not bar right to claim gratuity - If legislature intended to take away gratuity it would have specifically mentioned in s. 25 F of the 1947 Act - Respondents/workmen were entitled to claim gratuity along with retrenchment compensation - Order of appellate Tribunal was justified - Appeal dismissed.
