Union of India v Madras Telephone S. C. and S. T. Social Welfare Association, Etc

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2861; (2000) 9 SCC 71; (2000) SCC (L&S) 835; AIR 2000 SC 1717; JT 2000 (6) SC 471; 2000 (33) LabIC 1834; 2000 (3) LLN 33; 2000(4) SCALE 241; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 618; 2000 (3) SLR 120

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service; Telecom

Summary: Telecom - Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Administrative Tribunal Act, 1985, s. 29 - Telegraph Engineering Service Class II Recruitment Rules, 1966 - Seniority - Promotion - Clarification - Entitlement - Respondent/Madras Telephone SC/ST Social Welfare Association had filed writ petition before HC with prayer that eligibility list be prepared by determining seniority on basis of confirmation as Junior Engineer and that list should form basis for promotion to Class II service - Therefore, said writ petition stood transferred to Tribunal u/s. 29 of the Act which came to conclusion that directions given by Tribunal really amounts to re-writing rules, and eligibility list had to be prepared according to year of recruitment - Therefore SLP aroused in said case there, appellants sought clarification with regard to contrary judgment runs in case of Union of India vs. P.N.Lal and Ors., in S.L.P. Nos. 3384-86/86 dt. 13-2-97 in case of Union of India vs. Madras Telephone SC/ST Social Welfare Association in C.A. No. 4339 of 1995 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether selection list drawn up for promotion should be done as per 1966 Rules or Post and Telegraph Manual Volume IV was challenged - Held, the 1966 Rules, framed in exercise of powers conferred by provision to art. 309 of the Constitution of India, came into force promotion from post of erstwhile Engineering Supervisor Telecom to post of Assistant Engineer was being made in accordance with instructions contained in Post and Telegraph Manual Volume IV (P & T Manual ) - Therefore, said instructions were obviously executive instructions which were governed in absence of statutory rules - Further, Court had noticed arguments advanced by placing reliance on provisions of recruitment rules of 1966 but it ultimately came to conclusion that views of Allahabad High Court had reached finality because of dismissal of P.N.Lal case S.L.P. Nos. 3384-86/86 and as such eligibility list was required to be prepared in accordance with P & T Manual - Aforesaid conclusion was undoubtedly incorrect, as judgment of Allahabad HC proceeded by interpreting paragraph 206 of P & T Manual, which was administrative instruction which governed field until promulgation of recruitment rules framed under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution - Once statutory recruitment rules had come into force, procedure had also been prescribed under said rules for preparation of eligibility list of officers for promotion to Engineering service Class II by notification dt. 28-6-1966, it was that procedure which had to be adopted and earlier administrative instruction contained in paragraph 206 of P & T Manual could not be adhered to - Further, recruitment rules read with Schedule appended thereto and Appendix I to rules, recruitment to service in Class II had to be made entirely by promotion on basis of selection through qualifying departmental examination - Hence, Court opinioned that Judgment of Court in Civil Appeal No. 4339 of 1995 had rightly been decided in interpreting relevant provisions of recruitment rules read with procedure prescribed under memorandum dt. 28-6-1966, however, made it clear that persons who had already got benefit by virtue of judgments in their favour, they would not suffer and their promotion already made would not be affected by said judgment - Appeal disposed of.
