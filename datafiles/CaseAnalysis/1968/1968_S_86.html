State of Gujarat v Dr. R.B. Chandrachud

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 355; (1969) 1 SCC 300; AIR 1971 SC 846; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Pensions Act, 1871, Baroda Land Revenue Code, Baroda Merger Agreement

Summary: Constitution, art. 363 - Baroda Land Revenue Code, s. 148 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 6(g) - CPC, 1908, s. 60(1)(g) - Pensions Act, 1871, s. 1 - Service matter - (A) Constitution - Huzur order of Maharaja of Baroda - Granting pension and premature retirement compensation to civil servant of State after accession but before merger - By virtue of cl. 2 of art. VIII of Merger Agreement, Successor Govt. is bound by the order - Forfeiture of compensation paid - Consideration of word 'pension' in cl. (a) of art. VIII - (B) Baroda Land Revenue Code - Recovery of monies paid under orders of Executive Council of State not allowed by s.148 - Order passed by Maharaja in exercise of his prerogative and inherent powers - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - Word 'pension' u/s. 1 of the 1871 Act - implies periodical payments to pensioner - Wider sense - Compensation cannot be recovered u/s. 143, Baroda Code and words 'pension' in include lump sum payable as compensation on retirement - Monies paid under the order cannot be recovered u/s. 148 - In wider sense, in which it is used in cl. 2 of art. VIII of the Baroda State Merger Agreement D/- 21-03-1949, it means lump sum paid as retirement benefit to civil servant upon his retirement - Appeal dismissed.
