State Of Andhra Pradesh v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, T.S. Thakur, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 116; (2013) 5 SCC 68; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 246; JT 2013 (3) SC 482; 2013(3) SCALE 102; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 153

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Water

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Orissa Irrigation Act, 1959, Inter State River Water Disputes Act, 1956

Summary: Water - Constitution - Administrative - Irrigation project - Unauthorized construction of barrage - Legality - Construction of irrigation project at Pochampad, which was located close to inter-state border of petitioner/State and defendant no. 1/State involved submergence of area within border of defendant no. 1 - Agreements were entered into between petitioner and defendant no.1 on 6-10-1975 and 19-12-1975 whereby defendant no. 1 agreed that petitioner could go ahead with Pochampad dam project - Acting on agreement, petitioner constructed Pochampad dam on Godavari river at a distance of 764 km - Meanwhile, defendant no. 1 illegally and unauthorisedly constructed Babhali barrage within reservoir bridge of Pochampad dam without any right and entitlement - Matter was referred to Tribunal which passed an award dt. 7-7-1980 - Petitioner filed instant suit complaining violations of agreements dt. 6-10-1975 and 19-12-1975 which were endorsed by award of Tribunal - Whether action of defendant no. 1 in undertaking and proceeding with construction of Babhali barrage on River Godavari within water spread area of Pochampad reservoir and to utilize water from said reservoir was contrary to Tribunal's award - Held, under agreement dt. 6-10-1975 and award of Tribunal utilization of 60 TMC water by Maharashtra for new projects below Paithan dam site on Godavari and below Siddheswar dam site on Purna and below Nizamsagar dam site on Manjra and up to Pochampad dam site on Godavari was not confined to flowing waters alone in territory of defendant no. 1 - Further, thrust of parties in Cl. II(i) of agreement dt. 6-10-1975 and essence of that Clause was to put a cap on right of defendant no. 1 to utilize water of Godavari river below 3 dams mentioned therein up to Pochampad dam site to extent of 60 TMC for new projects and in no case exceeding that limit - There was no demarcation made in agreement that utilization of waters not exceeding 60 TMC for new projects by defendant no. 1 should be from flowing water through river from catchment area only - However, commitment of defendant no. 1 that Babhali barrage project requires 2.74 TMC of water out of allocation of 60 TMC for new projects under agreement of which only 0.6 TMC is from common submergence of Pochampad reservoir and Babhali barrage if accepted and its compliance was ensured, it might be conveniently held that Babhali barrage would not enable defendant no. 1 to draw and utilize 65 TMC of water from storage of Pochampad project as alleged by petitioner - Thus, even if interpretation placed on agreement dt. 6-10-1975 by petitioner was accepted that utilization of waters to extent of 60 TMC for new projects by defendant no. 1 from below 3 dam sites mentioned in Cl. II(i) up to Pochampad dam site could be only from water flowing through river from catchment area and not from pondage/water spread area of Pochampad dam, on basis of facts which have come on record, a case of substantial injury of a serious magnitude and high equity that moves conscience of Court had not been made out by petitioner justifying grant of injunction - Hence, petitioner was not entitled to reliefs, as prayed for in instant suit - However, a three member supervisory committee was constituted which would supervise operation of Babhali barrage - Petition disposed of.
