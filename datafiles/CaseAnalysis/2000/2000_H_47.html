Mammu v Hari Mohan and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 554; (2000) 2 SCC 32; AIR 2000 SC 650; JT 2000 (1) SC 65; 2000 (1) KarLJ 489; 2000 (1) KLT 835; 2000(1) SCALE 52; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 84; 2000 (1) SLT 227; 2000 (1) Supreme 86

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963 , ss. 103 , 80-B - Kudikidappu right - Purchase property - Permitted - Respondent owns an extent of 28.5 cents of property - Four tenants filed original applications before Land Tribunal for purchase of Kudikidappu right u/s.80-B of Act - Appellate Authority holded that appellant was a Kudikidappukaran entitled to purchase Kudkidappu - Order was challenged by respondent - Revision filed by respondent u/s.103 of 1963 Act challenging Judgment of Land Reforms Appellate Authority - HC allowed revision petition that respondent was not a Kudikidappukaran with respect to structure - Order of Appellate Authority under challenge on ground that authority did not pay attention to different relevant factors noted in previous remand order of HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants was Kudikidappukaran with respect to structure and entitled to purchase property - Held, that HC could not be faulted either in fact or in law for having detained that appellant was not a Kudikidappukaran with respect to structure - HC option that structure with respect to which Kudikidappu claimed was not an independent structure it is only an adjunct or appurtenant to shop room previously let out to appellant - No revision would lie against order of Land Tribunal remanding matter to Land Board for fresh disposal - Appeal dismissed.
