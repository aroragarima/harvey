Mahesh v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 675; (2008) 13 SCC 271; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 428B; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1949; 2008 CRLJ 3602; JT 2008 (6) SC 1; 2008(7) SCALE 10; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Eye-Witness, Gunshot injury, Post Mortem Report, Medical Certificate, Earlier Decision, Power Of The Court, Mother-In-Law, Ballistic Expert, Non-Examination, Natural Witness, Blood-Stains, Fire Arm Injuries, Sufficient In Ordinary Course Of Nature, Inimical Witness, Non-Explanation, Simple Injury, Gunshot, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Relative Witness, Sharp- Edged

Summary: Appeal filed u/s. 379 Cr.P.C. r/w s. 2(A) Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 r/w O. 21 rr. 12 to 19 of the Supreme Court Rules, 1966 partly setting aside judgment of trial court convicting appellant u/s. 302 of IPC but acquitting appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 307 and 324 of the IPC and ss. 25 and 27 of the Arms Act - Appellant was proved to be responsible for causing the injury to deceased, which later on proved fatal to her - Held, principle to be followed by the Appellate Court considering the appeal against the judgment of acquittal is to interfere only where there are compelling and substantial reasons for doing so - Merely because the witnesses examined by the prosecution are relatives of the victim, that fact by itself will not be sufficient to discard and discredit the evidence of the relative witnesses, if otherwise they are found to be truthful witnesses - Testimony of eye-witnesses including injured eye-witness PW-Sanjay finds complete corroboration from the medical evidence in regard to the severe injuries sustained by deceased Sunita at hands of the appellant with knife (art. 20), weapon of offence used in the commission of the crime - No ground is made out to disbelieve and discard the evidence of PWs-4, 8 and 16, who are injured and non-injured eye- witnesses and whose evidence is corroborated by other oral and documentary evidence including the medical evidence - Interference made in the present case by the High Court with the order of acquittal passed by the learned Additional District Judge, was wholly justified and warranted - Appeal dismissed.
