Union of India and Another v Sher Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 921; (1997) 3 SCC 555; AIR 1997 SC 1796; 1997 (2) CLT 58; JT 1997 (2) SC 659; 1997(2) SCALE 165; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1048; 1997 (2) Supreme 550; 1997 (1) UJ 473

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887

Summary: Land and Property - Civil Procedure - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887 s.11 - CPC, 1908 - Constitution, art.136 - Special leave petition dismissed without speaking order - (A) Whether amounts to res judicata - Held, it does not constitute res judicata - (B) Land Acquisition Act, s.23 - Compensation - Entitlement of - Land described as 'gair mumkin pahar' in revenue records and it vests in Gaon Sabha - Whether Gaon Sabha is entitled to compensation relating thereto - Further Held, land vests in Gaon Sabha - Gaon Sabha entitled to compensation - Appeal allowed.
