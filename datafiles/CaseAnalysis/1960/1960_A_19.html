Â Atiabari Tea Company Limited v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 410; AIR 1961 SC 232; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Sales Tax

Keywords: Pith And Substance, Finance Commission, Competence Of The Legislature, Freedom Of Movement, Attorney-General, Extent Of Laws Made By Parliament

Summary: Indirect Tax - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Assam Taxation (on Goods carried by Roads or Inland Waterways) Act, 1954 - Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956 - Tea Act - Constitution (Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Whether the Assam Taxation (on Goods carried by Roads or Inland Waterways) Act, 1954 is ultra vires the Constitution being repugnant to the provisions of art. 301 of the Constitution, 1950 as the tax on carriage of tea through the State of Assam had the effect of interfering with the freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse? - The freedom of trade guaranteed by art. 301 is freedom from all restrictions except those which are provided by the other articles in Part XIII - In dealing with constitutional questions courts should be slow to embark upon an unnecessarily wide or general enquiry and should confine their decision as far as may be reasonably practicable within the narrow limits of the controversy arising between the parties in the particular case - Held, the content of freedom provided for by art. 301 was larger than the freedom contemplated by s. 297 of the Constitution Act of 1935 - Includes movement of trade which is of the very essence of all trade and is its integral part - If the transport or the movement of goods is taxed solely on the basis that the goods are thus carried or transported it directly affects the freedom of trade as contemplated by art. 301 - If the movement, transport or the carrying of goods is allowed to be impeded, obstructed or hampered by taxation without satisfying the requirements of Part XIII the freedom of trade u/art. 301 would turn to be illusory - Held, art. 301 provides that trade shall be free throughout the territory of India unhampered by any restriction either at the boundaries of the States or at any other points inside the States themselves - Free movement or the transport of goods from one part of the country to the other is intended to be saved - Acts imposing any direct restrictions on the very movement of such goods it attracts the provisions of art. 301 - Further held, previous sanction of the President has not been obtained - Validity of the tax cannot be sustained - Held, unnecessary to consider the other points urged - Appeals and Petitions allowed.
