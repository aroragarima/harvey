Sham Lal v State of Haryana etc.

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2412; (1997) 9 SCC 759; AIR 1997 SC 1873; AIR 1997 SCW 1614; 1997 (1) CCR 231; 1997 (1) Crimes 245; 1997 CRLJ 1927; JT 1997 (3) SC 91; 1997(2) SCALE 328; 1997 (3) SCJ 4; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 309; 1997 (2) Supreme 614

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 B and 498 A - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113 B - Dowry death - Presumption - Evidence of 'panchayat' which was held to resolve dispute between parties - No evidence, however, of cruelty or harassment with demand for dowry during period between her taking back to nuptial home and her tragic end - Held, no presumption for dowry death - Accused cannot be convicted u/s. 304-B but 498 A.
