Lakshmiratan Cotton Mills Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 1969 SC 917; 1969 (2) CompLJ 195; 1969 (2) ITJ 353; [1969] 73 ITR 634; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 951

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Whether Income-Tax Appellate Tribunal was justified in holding that the involved sum as expended by the assessee company was not wholly and exclusively for the purpose of its business for allowance u/ s. 10(2)(xv)? - Remuneration payable under the managing agency agreement was for a two-fold consideration (1) the services rendered by the managing agents in the promotion of the company, and (2) for rendering services to the company - Expenditure incurred for remunerating the persons who had promoted the company was not in law a revenue expenditure admissible u/ s. 10(2)(xv) and if no services were rendered by the managing agents, the remuneration must be regarded as exclusively payable for the services rendered by the managing agents in the promotion of the company - Held, expenditure could not, in the circumstances, be said to be made wholly and exclusively in the interest of the business of the company - Further held, there was ample material on which the conclusion of the Tribunal could be founded - Appeal dismissed.
