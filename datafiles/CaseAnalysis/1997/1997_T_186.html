Tuticorin Alkali Chemicals And Fertilizers Ltd. Madras v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S.P. Bharucha, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3320; (1997) 6 SCC 117; AIR 1998 SC 315; [1997] 227 ITR 172; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 4 and 14 - Whether interest on short term deposits of funds borrowed from financial institutions be chargeable to tax under head 'Income from other sources' or would go to reduce interest payable on borrowing, which would be capitalised after commencement of commercial production? - Amendment in 1988 has no relevance - Income generated by utilisation of capital will be of revenue nature and not accretion of capital - Assessee cannot claim set-off of loss from one head against income from another since its business had not started - Accountancy practice is not necessarily good law - Tax is attracted at point when income is earned - Held, Tribunal had come to correct decision, interest earned as above would be revenue in nature and chargeable to tax - Appeal disposed of.
