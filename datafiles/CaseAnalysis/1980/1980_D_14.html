Prem Shankar Shukla v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 335; (1980) 3 SCC 526; (1980) SCC (Cr) 815; AIR 1980 SC 1535; 1980 CRLJ 930; 1980 CrLR(SC) 740; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Universal Declaration of Human Rights, High Court Rules, Prisoners (Attendance in Courts) Act, 1955, International Covenant on Civil and Political Rights

Summary: Practice and Procedure -Prisoners (Attendance In Courts) Act, 1955- Punjab Police Rules,1934- Delhi High Court Rules -Punjab Police Rules,1934, rr. 26.21 A, 26.22 -A prisoner sent a telegram to a judge of the SC complaining of forced handcuffs on him and other prisoners, implicitly protesting against the humiliation and torture of being held in irons in public, back and forth, when, as under-trials kept in custody in the Tihar Jail, they were being taken to Delhi courts for trial of their cases-the practice persisted, bewails the petitioner, despite the court' s direction not to use irons on him and this led to the telegraphic 'litany' to the Supreme Court which is the functional sentinel on the qui-vive where 'habeas' justice is in jeopardy - (A)Whether the provisions of the Punjab Police Manual is art.14, 19 and 21 of the constitution? - The Punjab Police Manual, in so far as it puts the ordinary Indian beneath the better class breed (para 26.21A and 26.22 of Chapter XXVI) is untenable and arbitrary - Indian humans shall not be dichotomised and the common run discriminated against regarding handcuffs - Held, The provisions in para 26.22 that every under-trial who is accused of a non-bailable offence punishable with more than 3 years prison term shall be routinely handcuffed is violative of art.14, 19 and 21 of the Constitution - (B) Whether handcuffing a person by the escorting is violation of art.21 of the Constitution ? - Even in cases where, in extreme circumstances, handcuffs have to be put on the prisoner, the escorting authority must record contemporaneously the reason for doing so - Held, u/art. 21 the procedure will be unfair and bad in law - The minions of the police establishment must make good their security recipes by getting judicial approval- And once the court directs that handcuffs shall be off, no escorting authority can overrule judicial direction - Petition disposed of.
