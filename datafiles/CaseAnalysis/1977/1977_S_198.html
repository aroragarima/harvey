State of Uttar Pradesh v Sughar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 291; (1978) SCC (Cr) 83; (1978) 1 SCC 178; AIR 1978 SC 191; 1978 ALJ 466; 1978 CRLJ 141; 1978 CRLR 11B; 1978 (1) KarLJ 432

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 141, 147, 148, 149, 202, 302 and 379 - Whether the High Court erred in interfering with appreciation of prosecution evidence by holding that witnesses are partisan and that the prosecution version does not fit in with medical evidence, and directing acquittal of the accused? - (i) It was a practice of witness Mannu Lal, the father of the deceased, to accompany his son whenever the latter went out for recitation of katha and as such Mannu Lal would be the most natural witness to the occurrence, as to the delay in lodging the FIR, he stated that it was rainy season, at several places water had collected on the road, the major part of the road was kachha road and because of that he reached Orai late in the evening, further, it is not expected that all the details would be mentioned in the FIR, the aspects brought out in his cross-examination are not such as would throw any doubt on the veracity of his version given in examination-in-chief, (ii) Dhani Ram has stated that he did not know whether any complaint had been lodged by Ram Gopal against him, thus it would be difficult to conclude that Dhani Ram belonged to the opposite party and had come to give false evidence against the accused, he has deposed about the occurrence in almost similar terms as Mannu Lal, also from the fact that he had purchased a house at Karmer from one Nathu Sonar some 4 or 5 years prior to his giving evidence, that he was actually staying in that house, that his name had been included as a voter in the Panchayat of that village, it does appear that he has been a resident of Karmer which explain his presence at Karmer at the house of Achhey Lal on the day of the occurrence, (iii) the prosecution version that Accused 1 emerged from eastern side and fired a shot in deceased's stomach and immediately thereafter Accused 2 and 3 who had emerged from the western side fired a shot each, which shots must have hit the right arm of the deceased would be clearly consistent with the injuries noted in medical evidence and substantially tallies with the medical evidence available on the record - Held, the evidence of eye witnesses cannot be rejected only on the ground that they are partisan witnesses, the so-called improvements made by them in their evidence with regard to the manner of assault are nothing but matters of details furnished during their cross-examination, further the learned Sessions Judge was right in rejecting the defence case and in coming to the conclusion that the plea of alibi put forward by Accused 1 had not been established him, the HC was clearly in error in interfering with the convictions - Appeal allowed.
