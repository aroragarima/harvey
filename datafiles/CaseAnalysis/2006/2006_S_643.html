Hari Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 June 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 820; (2006) 5 SCC 733; AIR 2006 SC 2464; 2006 CRLJ 3283; 2006 (130) DLT 591; 2006 (2) JCC 992; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 265; 2006(6) SCALE 489; 2006 (7) SCJ 419; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 59

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Investigation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 190, 200 and 203 - Petition filed u/art. 32 for direction to conduct enquiry by Central Bureau of Investigation into murder of son of petitioner - Petitioner alleged that his son had been murdered and did not commit suicide and despite lodging of FIR, no action had been taken by police - Whether writ petition is entitled to be entertained? - Held, no - When no action is taken by police with regard to FIR/complaint, complainant can seek remedy u/ss. 190 and 200 before Magistrate having jurisdiction - If prima facie case made out, Magistrate is empowered to direct police concerned to investigate into offence and submit report - If no prima facie case made out, Magistrate can dismiss complaint u/s. 203 - Petition dismissed.
