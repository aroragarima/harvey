Srilekha Ghosh (Roy) and Another v Partha Sarathi Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1728; (2002) 6 SCC 359; AIR 2002 SC 2500; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 136; 2002 (48) ALR 507; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 61; JT 2002 (5) SC 169; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 90; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 548; 2002(5) SCALE 104; 2002 (4) SLT 372; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 45; 2002 (4) Supreme 611

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Female Heirs, Succession & Inheritance, Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Hindu Women's Right to Property Act, 1937, s. 3(3) - Partition Act, 1893, s. 4 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 23 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 44 - Owner suit property died - Leaving son/ respondent and two daughters/appellants herein - Respondent and mother joint owners - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 came into force - Widow's share absolute - Respondent and mother became co-sharers - Widow by deed of gift gifted her share to appellants - Decree of partition - Preemption - (A) Whether defendant is entitled to purchase share of his sister, u/s. 4 of Act? - (B) Whether for purpose of application of s. 4 of Act a married daughter can be said to be "a person who is not a member of such family"? - Held, last owner of suit property left one male heir (son) and three female heirs (widow and two daughters) who succeeded to suit property - Widow transferred her interest in suit property by gift in favor of her two daughters, who in course of time got married - Two daughters filed suit for partition of suit property which was a family dwelling house - Partition suit was decreed preliminary - At stage of execution proceedings petition has been filed by male heir, that is,the brother of plaintiffs claiming right of pre-emption to purchase share of one of sisters (plaintiff 2) - In stricto sensu provision of s. 4 of Partition Act has no application in case - Neither can plaintiffs who are daughters be said to be strangers to family nor is there any material to show that they have expressed their intention not to reside in suit property or to transfer their interest in same to a person who is a stranger to family - It is also to be kept in mind that plaintiffs have acquired interest in property by gift from their mother - Therefore they have stepped into shoes of their mother - Under circumstances petition filed by defendant u/s. 4 of Partition Act was not maintainable and was liable to be dismissed as premature - At same time, keeping in view object and purpose of preserving unity of family dwelling house for occupation of members of family plaintiffs cannot be given a right to transfer their interest in family dwelling house in favor of a stranger - If they decide not to reside in suit dwelling house and desire to transfer their interest then they must make an offer to defendant and if he is willing to purchase interest of sisters then he will be entitled to do so on payment of consideration mutually agreed or fixed by court - High Court order modified - Appeal disposed of.
