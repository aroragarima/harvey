Saeed Zakir Hussain Malik v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 225; (2012) 8 SCC 233; AIR 2012 SC 3235; 2012 CRLJ 4297; 2012 (5) CTC 793; 2012 (4) JCC 2541; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 154; 2012(7) SCALE 285; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 235; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 17

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs

Keywords: Import Export Code

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Customs Act, 1962 - Delay of preventive detention - Challenged - The Directorate of Revenue Intelligence (DRI) on the basis of information, initiated investigation into the claim of fraudulent exports allegedly made by a syndicate of persons in the name of fictitious firms - During the course of investigation, several fictitious firms were identified which had availed the drawback scheme of the Act allegedly running into several crores - The role of the appellant's brother (detenu) also came to light as one of the racketeers who was involved in using fictitious IECs and forged documents for fraudulent exports under the drawback scheme and he was arrested on 21-10-2005 - Detenu was released on bail on 11-11-2005 - While the detenu was on bail, a Detention Order was issued against him by the Principal Secretary (Appeals and Security) to the Govt. of Maharashtra and on the same day, the detention order was received by the executing authority - However, after a delay of 14Â½ months, the said order was served upon the detenu - Appellant filed petition challenging said detention order - HC dismissed said petition - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, the delay in passing the detention order, namely, after 15 months vitiates the detention itself - The question whether the prejudicial activities of a person necessitating to pass an order of detention was proximate to the time when the order was made or the live-link between the prejudicial activities and the purpose of detention was snapped depends on the facts and circumstances of each case - Though there was no hard and fast rule and no exhaustive guidelines can be laid down in that behalf, however, when there was undue and long delay between the prejudicial activities and the passing of detention order, it was incumbent on the part of the court to scrutinize whether the Detaining Authority has satisfactorily examined such a delay and afforded a reasonable and acceptable explanation as to why such a delay has occasioned - In the instant case, in the absence of proper explanation for a period of 15 months in issuing the order of detention, the same was set aside - Appeal allowed.
