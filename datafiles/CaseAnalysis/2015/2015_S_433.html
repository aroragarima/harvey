State of Rajasthan v Sri Chand

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 352; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2437; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 244; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 1011; 2015(6) SCALE 224; 2016(6) SCALE 224; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 321

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 354, 376, 511 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Attempt to commit Rape - Grant of probation - Justification - It was alleged that on date of incident, respondent accused attempted to commit rape on prosecutrix who was a minor girl, however, on being seen by other persons who were passing by, accused flew from place of incident - FIR was registered u/ss. 376/511 of IPC - However, Trial Court acquitted accused respondent by granting him probation - Further, appeal of before HC for grant of leave to appeal against order of acquittal, which was rejected. Hence, present appeal.

Held, FIR was recorded u/s. 376 read with s. 511 of IPC i.e. attempt to rape and not rape per se. There is no eye witness on record apart from prosecutrix herself as PW3 only saw accused fleeing away and alleged eye witness, was never produced before Court nor her statement was recorded u/s. 161 of CrPc. Further no medical examination of prosecutrix has been conducted. Further, prosecutrix in clear terms did not state that accused attempted to commit rape on her and statement of her father cannot be taken into consideration as he was not eye witness and was not even told about incident by prosecutrix. He was told details of incident by Biharilal (PW3) who is not eye witness to incident. Hence, SC held that question of attempt to rape is not proved beyond reasonable doubt. Further, SC held that accused fled away on when PW3 came to place of incident due to shouting of prosecutrix and it showed that he wasn't determined to have sexual connection with prosecutrix despite all resistance and odds. However, with respect to release of accused on grant of probation, SC held that accused is not minor, rather he has committed offence against minor girl who is helpless. Further, it is clear from evidence on record that he ran away only when prosecutrix screamed and PW3 came to place of incident, which goes on to show that accused could have had worse intentions. Offence is heinous in nature and there is no reason for granting benefit of probation in this case. Trial Court has not given any special consideration to character of accused apart from fact that this was first conviction of accused. SC held that is far from sufficient to grant probation in offence like outraging modesty of woman. Hence, SC allowed appeal to limited extent that accused respondent is not granted benefit of probation but his conviction is maintained u/s. 354 of IPC only and accused respondent is sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for two years. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - It is not justified to release offender on probation when offence committed by him is heinous and affects society at large.
