Messrs B. R. Enterprises and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1635; (1999) 9 SCC 700; AIR 1999 SC 1867; 1999 ALJ 1215; 1999 (3) AWC 2236; JT 1999 (3) SC 431; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 1111; 2000 (120) STC 302

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Morality, Constitutionality, English Lotteries Act, 1823, Betting, Gambling And Lotteries Act, 1963, Uttar Pradesh unauthorized Lottery (Prevention) Act, 1995, Common Wealth of Australian Constitution Act

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 254, 301, 303 and 298, sch. 7, list 1, entry 40, sch. 7, list 2, entry 34 - Lotteries (Regulation) Act, 1998, ss. 4(a)(g)(h) and 5 - Free trade and commerce - State lotteries: (A) If trade and commerce as contemplated by art. 301 of Constitution - (B) Authorisation to run, flows from art. 298 - Does it make state lotteries, to constitute trade? - (C) Restrictions imposed by s. 5 on State lotteries - But not on Bhutan lotteries - Plea of discrimination - (D) Validity of s. 5 of Act - (E) Power of State Govt. to prohibit sale of lotteries of every other State - Conferment - If delegation of essential legislative power - (F) Conditions imposed by cls. (a)(g)(h) of s. 4, if ultravires Constitution - Held, lottery which is res extra commercium does not become commercium merely by putting on cloak of State authority and becoming a State lottery - Field of art. 298 is wider than art. 301, it is not limited to trade - Plea held not tenable since in absence of Law by Parliament and in view of specific stipulation in treaty with Bhutan Govt. that sale of Bhutan lottery tickets would be subject to relevant laws in India - Section not discriminatory or violative of art. 303 - No, however section must be read down as limiting exercise of such power only by a State which decides not to have any lottery within its territory including its own lottery - No, provision is valid - Order accordingly.
