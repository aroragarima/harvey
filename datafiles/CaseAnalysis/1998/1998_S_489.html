Rajammal v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Wadhwa, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1964; (1999) SCC (Cr) 93; (1999) 1 SCC 417; AIR 1999 SC 684; AIR 1999 SCW 139; 1999 (1) AllCriC 933; 1998 (4) CCR 132; 1999 CRLJ 826; 1999 (1) JCC 64; JT 1998 (8) SC 598; 1999 (1) KLT 37; 1999 (3) RCR(Criminal) 9; 1998(6) SCALE 574; 1999 (1) SCJ 208; 1998 (10) SLT 313; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 551; 1998 (9) Supreme 398; 1999 (5) Supreme 9

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot-leggers, Drug-Offenders, Forest-Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1982, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Detention - Unexplained delay of five days in considering and disposing detenu's representation - Argument given was that Minister was absent at Headquarters - Held, mere absence of Minister at headquarters not sufficient to justify delay - Detention vitiated.
