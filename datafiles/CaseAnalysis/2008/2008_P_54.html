Pankaj Kumar v State Of Maharashtra & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1058; (2008) 16 SCC 117; AIR 2008 SC 3077; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2921; 2008 CRLJ 3944; 2009 (122) FLR 790; 2008 (3) JCC 1932; JT 2008 (8) SC 109; 2008 (2) LLN 606; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 890; 2008 (3) RLW 2368; 2008(9) SCALE 760; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1110

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Milk, Quashing Of Proceeding, Public Trial

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Petition to quash the charge sheet for offences punishable under ss. 120B, 409, 420, 465, 468, 471, 477 (A) 101 and 34 of IPC and ss. 5(1)(c)(d) along with s. 5(c) of the Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 dismissed - Appeal against - Held, right to speedy trial in all criminal persecutions is an inalienable right under art. 21 of the Constitution - Where the court comes to the conclusion that the right to speedy trial of an accused has been infringed, the charges or the conviction, as the case may be, may be quashed unless the court feels that having regard to the nature of offence and other relevant circumstances, quashing of proceedings may not be in the interest of justice - Appeal allowed.
