Navneet Kaur v State of (NCT of Delhi) and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Dattu, R. M. Lodha, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 197; (2014) 7 SCC 264; AIR 2014 SC 1935; 2014 CRLJ 2474; JT 2014 (4) SC 280; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1611; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 397; 2014(4) SCALE 459; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 1138

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mercy Petition, Mercy, Commutation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 72 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Death Sentence - Converted to Life imprisonment - X, wife of accused filed the present Curative petition against the dismissal of review petition, wherein she prayed for setting aside the death sentence imposed upon accused by commuting the same to imprisonment for life on the ground of supervening circumstance of delay of 8 years in disposal of mercy petition - Whether Curative Petition filed by the X could be allowed -

Held, the report of Director & Chairman as well as 4 members of the Medical Board clearly shows that accused was suffering from acute mental illness - SC in Shatrughan Chauhan and another v. Union of India and others 2014 Indlaw SC 34 held that insanity/mental illness/schizophrenia was also one of the supervening circumstances for commutation of death sentence to life imprisonment - By applying the principle enunciated in Shatrughan Chauhan case, the accused could not be executed with the said health condition - Court deem it fit to commute the death sentence imposed on accused into life imprisonment both on the ground of unexplained/inordinate delay of 8 years in disposal of mercy petition and on the ground of insanity - Petition allowed.
