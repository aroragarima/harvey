Savitri Devi v District Judge, Gorakhpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, A.S. Anand, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 963; (1999) 2 SCC 577; AIR 1999 SC 976; 1999 ALJ 684; 1999 (36) ALR 93; 1999 (2) AWC 1249; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 38; 1999 (2) CLT 33; JT 1999 (1) SC 643; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 110; 1999(1) SCALE 516; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 725; 1999 (2) SLT 167; 1999 (2) Supreme 172

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - A civil suit has been filed by appellant against her four sons for a decree for maintenance and for creation of a charge over ancestral property of family - Court passed an order and directed parties not to transfer disputed property described in plaint in favour of any other person till final disposal of suit - First defendant sold his share in lands to respondents 3 to 5 by registered sale deeds - Whether a person who purchases property during pendency of suit is allowed to get impleaded in suit? - Plea raised by respondents 3 to 5 that they were bona fide transferees for value in good faith may have to be decided before it can be held that sales in their favour created no interest in property - If application for impleadment is thrown out without a decision on aforesaid questions, respondents 3 to 5 will certainly come up with a separate suit to enforce their alleged rights which means a multiplicity of proceedings - Held, thus in such circumstances, it cannot be said that respondents 3 to 5 are neither necessary nor proper parties to suit - Appeal dismissed.
