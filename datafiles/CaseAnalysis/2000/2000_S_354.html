Gauri Shankar Prasad v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 691; (2000) 5 SCC 15; (2000) SCC (Cr) 872; AIR 2000 SC 3517; 2000 (2) CCR 102; 2000 (2) Crimes 176; 2000 CRLJ 4031; 2000 (2) JCC 690; JT 2000 (4) SC 613; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 708; 2000(3) SCALE 386; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 159; 2000 (4) SLT 507; 2000 (3) Supreme 358

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prosecution of judges - Whether on basis of facts and allegations made in complaint, s. 197 CrPC, 1973 made out? - Appellant given work by High Court for demolition of illegal encroachments - Appellant illegally demolished some part of complainant's property - S. 197 CrPC, 1973 affords protection to a Judge or a magistrate or a public servant not removable from his office save by or with sanction of Govt. against any offence which is alleged to have been committed by him while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duty - Held, appellant was present at site in capacity of SDM so benefit of s. 197, CrPC, 1973 needs to be extended to appellant - Appeal allowed.
