Pankaj Sharma and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 525; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1722; 2008 (3) ESC 370; JT 2008 (4) SC 127; 2008(4) SCALE 322; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1864

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Public Service Commission (Business and Procedure) Rules, 1980, Jammu and Kashmir Combined Competitive Examination Direct Recruitment Rules, 1995

Summary: Service - Challenge to the selection process - High Court allowed writ petitions and issued some directions - Appeal against - Held, Single Judge was right that the assumption on which the writ petitioners based their claim and proceeded to convince the Court that there were only three members over and above the Chairman and the decisions were taken by one and the same person, i.e. Chairman of the Commission was totally ill-founded and without basis whatsoever - Approach adopted by the Commission cannot be said to be unreasonable or irrational - Single Judge considered the controversy in its proper perspective, and in the light of mistakes/ errors/ inaccuracies, issued certain directions which benefited the student-community - Appeals dismissed.
