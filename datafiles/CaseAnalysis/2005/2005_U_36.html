Common Cause ’A Registered Society, Etc. v Union Of India & Ors., Etc

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1983; (2006) 9 SCC 295; AIR 2005 SC 4442; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 101; 2005 (61) ALR 904; 2005 (4) AWC 3898; 2005 (4) CPJ(Sc) 4; 2005 (123) DLT 664; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 65; JT 2005 (12) SC 208; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 350; 2005(7) SCALE 648

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Bar Council of India (Conduct and Disciplinary) Rules

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Bar Council of India (Conduct and Disciplinary) Rules - Strike of lawyers - Issuance of direction - Procedural irregularity - Contempt - Question of lawyer's going on strike has been subject matter in number of decisions of SC - However, it was hoped that Bar Council of India would incorporate direction issued by SC in the Rules - Unfortunately, Bar Council of India had not deemed it fit to incorporate those directions into its Rules - Hence, instant Contempt Petition - Whether action of Bar Associations, i.e., Delhi HC Bar Association and SC Bar Association, in visiting Advocates, who refused to participate in strike call, with punitive action of suspension and action of Bar Council of Delhi passing resolution which inter alia proposes to take against lawyers who did not participate in strike call, amounts to contempt of Judgment of SC - Held, Bar Councils were expected to rise to occasion as they were responsible to uphold dignity of Courts and majesty of law and to prevent interference in administration of justice - Therefore, it was duty of Bar Councils to ensure that there was no unprofessional and/or unbecoming conduct - This being their duty no Bar Council could even consider giving call for strike or call for boycott - It follows that Bar Councils and even Bar Associations could never consider or take seriously any requisition calling for meeting to consider call for strike or call for boycott - Further, it was the duty of every Advocate to bodily ignore call for strike or boycott - Thus, even provided that, Bar Councils did not rise to occasion and perform their duties by taking disciplinary action on complaint from client against advocate for non-appearance by reason of call for strike or boycott - Petition disposed of.
