Guru Jambheshwar University Through Registrar v Dharam Pal

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 56; (2007) 2 SCC 265; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 792; 2007 (2) ADJ 84; AIR 2007 SC 1040; 2007 (6) AWC 5578; 2007 (1) CLR 931; 2007 (112) FLR 880; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 126; JT 2007 (2) SC 418; 2007 (1) LLJ 1006; 2007 (3) MahLJ 63; 2007(2) SCALE 340; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 993; 2007 (2) SLR 475; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2264

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Treating

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Allegation of illegal termination - Plea that University was adopting unfair labour practice and that retrenchment was illegal as neither any notice was given nor any compensation was paid at the time of retrenchment - Wether the Labour Court was correct in holding that one day's average pay of the respondent should be calculated by dividing his monthly salary of Rs.1642/- by 26 and the quotient so arrived at should be multiplied by 30 (15 x 2) as he had worked for two years and one month? - Held, concept of 26 working days was evolved having regard to the definition of the word "wages" as given in s. 2(s) of Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 which uses the expression "all emoluments which are earned by an employee while on duty or on leave - There is no warrant or justification for importing the principle of 26 working days for determining the compensation which is payable in terms of s. 25F(b) - University had paid the retrenchment compensation to the respondent in accordance with law and there is no infirmity in the order passed whereby his services were terminated - Appeal allowed.
