State of Andhra Pradesh v P. Venkateshwarlu

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 330; (2015) 7 SCC 283; 2015 CRLJ 2902; JT 2015 (4) SC 495; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 225; 2015(6) SCALE 117; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 11, 13(1)(d), 13(2), 20, 7 - Illegal gratification - Acquittal - Complainant, approached accused/respondent to get Will deed registered in name of his wife for transfer of certain extent of land - Respondent demanded money for registration work - Case was registered against respondent u/ss. 7 and 11 of Act - Trial Court convicted Respondent u/ss. 7 and 13 (1)(d) r/w s. 13 (2) of the Act. Appeal filed by respondent was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC is justifiable.

Held, evidence of P.W.1 showed that after the Will was presented and registration fee paid, the A.O. demanded from the de facto complainant to pay the bribe amount. Evidence of P.W.2 further goes to show that he accompanied P.W.1 and de facto complainant to the office of the A.O. where he witnessed that the A.O. firstly collected the registration fee and later demanded and accepted the tainted amount. Coming to the testimonies of P.W.7 and P.W.8, their testimonies fully corroborate the testimony of P.W.1. The testimonies of the material witnesses have been fully corroborated and are trustworthy. The Phenolphthalein test goes further to prove that there was demand and acceptance of the tainted money. The recovery of the tainted money has gone unchallenged by the respondent. The prosecution is duty bound to establish that there was illegal offer of bribe and acceptance thereof and it has to be founded on facts. In the present case the factum of demand and acceptance has been proved by the recovery of the tainted amount and the factum of there being a demand has also been stated. The respondent has neither taken the plea of alibi for the visit and nor has proved the factum of not being present on the first date when the alleged demand was made, beyond all doubt. The defense also suggests that the complainant was a petty vendor who has no children and the second wife alone is in existence, and therefore, execution of the Will Deed was not required. Order of acquittal passed by the High Court struck down. Order of Trial Court restored. Appeal allowed.
