Gujarat Electricity Board v Shantilal R. Desai

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1969 SC 239; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 349; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 580

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Compulsory Acquisition of Land and Property

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 7 - Undertaking - Maintainability - Main question that called for decision was whether on basis of notice issued by State Electricity Board on 8-1-1959 u/s. 7 of the Act prior to its amendment in 1959, appellant could compulsorily purchase from respondent his concern - In his application before HC u/art. 226 of Constitution respondent challenged vires of s. 7 of the Act - But that contention remains to be examined - HC had chosen to allow petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, it was not case of respondent that either expressly or by necessary implication new law had taken away right acquired earlier - That being so s. 6 of the Act comes to aid of appellant - Section provided that where that Act or any Central Act or Regulation made after commencement of that Act repeals enactment hitherto made or hereafter to be made then unless different intention appeared, repeal shall not affect any right, privilege, obligation or liability acquired, 'accrued or incurred under any enactment so repealed - It also saved previous operation of any enactment so repealed or anything duly done or suffered there under - Right to purchase respondent's undertaking came to vest firstly in Bombay State Electricity Board subsequently in appellant in view of various notifications referred to earlier - That right had to be worked on basis of law as it stood on date notice u/s. 7(4) of the Act was given - Appellant could not demand possession of undertaking without paying price after same was determined according to law - Appeal allowed.
