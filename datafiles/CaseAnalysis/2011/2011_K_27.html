Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board & Anr v M/S Prakash Dal Mill & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 234; (2011) 6 SCC 714; AIR 2011 SC 1570; 2011(3) ALL MR 1018; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 451; JT 2011 (4) SC 215; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 724; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 577; 2011(4) SCALE 306; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Karnataka Industrial Area Development Act, 1966, Karnataka Industrial Area Development Board Regulations, 1969

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Enhancement of allotment price - Challenged - Petitioner formed an industrial layout and invited interested purchasers to make applications for allotment of industrial sites - Respondents applied for the same - Lease-cum-sale agreements entered into between the Board constituted by the petitioner and the respondents - Subsequently, after 11 years, appellants issued letters to the respondents, raising the demands with regard to the final allotment price - Respondents declined to pay the increased amount and contended that the final allotment price was unreasonable, arbitrary, unjust and contrary to what was legitimately expected and assured by the appellant - Thereafter, the appellant reduced the final allotment price marginally and issued demand notices to the respondents - Respondents filed writ petition for quashing the letters enhancing the price and for a direction to the appellant to execute the sale deeds on the basis of the price indicated in the lease deed - HC dismissed said writ petition - DB allowed appeal filed against said order and quashed the enhanced demands - Hence the instant appeals - Whether DB rightly quashed the enhanced demands? - Held, under Clause 7(b) of the lease deed, the Board reserved to itself the right to fix the final price of the demised premises and communicate the same to the concerned lessee - Determination of the price by the Board was binding on the lessee - However, the said clause would not permit the Board to arbitrarily or irrationally fix the final price of the site without any rational basis - The power of price fixation under Clause 7 of the lease deed being statutory in nature would have to be exercised, in accordance with statutory provisions, it cannot be permitted to be exercised arbitrarily - Hence, DB rightly concluded that the fixation of final price by the Board was without authority of law and violates art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
