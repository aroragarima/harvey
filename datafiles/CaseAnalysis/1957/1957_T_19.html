Sree Meenakshi Mills, Limited v Their Workmen. (And Connected Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1958 SC 153; 1958 (1) LLJ 239; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 24; 1958 SCJ 557; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 878

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Full Bench Formula, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss.10 and 11 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.47 r.1 - (A) Whether workmen's fulfilled condition as they claim for bonus?- Held, true nature and character of workmen's claim for bonus against their employers is well settled - Term 'bonus' is applied to a cash payment made in addition to wages, it generally represents cash incentive given conditionally on certain standards of attendance and efficiency being attained - Claim for bonus cannot be effectively made unless two conditions are satisfied - (i) wages paid to workmen fall short of what can be properly described as living wages (ii) industry must be shown to have made profits which are partly result of contribution made by workmen in increasing production - (B) Whether Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to review its own order under provisions of O.47 of Code? - Held, as CPC applies to proceedings before Labour Appellate Tribunal, provisions of O.47 of that Code apply to these proceedings - Hence, Appellate Tribunal has jurisdiction to review its own order under provisions of O.47 of Code - Appeal dismissed.
