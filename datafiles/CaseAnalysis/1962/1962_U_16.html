 Adhyaksha Mathur Babu’s Sakti Oushadhalaya Dacca (P) Ltd. v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1963 SC 622; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 210; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 118; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 957

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Ayurvedic Medicines

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act - s. 2(g) - Petitioners manufactured three medicinal preparations according to the Ayurvedic system of medicines by the process of fermentation and distillation - Whether the three preparations are "medicinal preparations" as defined in s. 2(g) of the Act and would they be liable to be taxed in accordance with the provisions of the Act? - Held, it would be impossible to say that these preparations are not remedies prepared for internal use of human beings and are not intended to be used for or in the treatment, mitigation or prevention of disease in human beings - Therefore, clearly medicinal preparations within the meaning of s. 2(g) of the Act - Would be liable to be taxed under the Schedule to the Act and not under the various Excise Acts of the different States concerned - Only necessary to add that the definition of "medicinal preparation" contained in s. 2(g) of the Act, does not depart from the meaning of that expression when it occurs in Item 84 of List I.
