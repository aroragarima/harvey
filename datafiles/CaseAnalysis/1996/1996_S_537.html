Thapar Institute Of Engineering And Technology Patiala Techn v State Of Punjab & Anr. Maharshi Dayanand University

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1864; (1997) 2 SCC 65; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 613; AIR 1997 SC 793; AIR 1997 SCW 615; 1997 (2) CalLT(SC) 40; 1997 (1) ESC 600; JT 1996 (11) SC 708; 1996(9) SCALE 217; 1997 (1) SLR 1; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 831; 1997 (1) Supreme 247

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Education Norms, Eligibility - Merit

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956 - Admito technical courses - Reservation for wards of employees of institute - Not permissible - Direction not to indicate in Brochure-cum- admission form any proposed reservation for wards of employees - (A) Admission as per merit in entrance exam of university - But by drawing separate merit list for reserved seats - If permissible? - (B) Permissibility in case when institute concerned is 'deemed University' - Held, not permissible - Further held, were impermissible - Direction is proper and valid - Appeal dismissed.
