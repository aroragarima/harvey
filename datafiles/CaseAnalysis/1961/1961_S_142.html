Smt. Sitabati Debi and Another v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 91; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 949

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, artS. 19(1)(f) and 31 - West Bengal Land (Requisition and Acquisition) Act, 1948 - (A) Whether 1948 Act is ultra vires Constitution? - Held, Babu Barkya Thakur's case about a law relating to acquisition of property by state would apply to a law relating to requisition - It would follow that validity of Act cannot be questioned, on ground that it offends art. 19(1)(f) - Bhanji Munji's case and Lilavati Bai's dealt with a statute giving power to the state to requisition land and held, that such a law if valid u/art. 31 as it stood before amendment, would not be void on ground, that it infringed art. 19(1)(f) - Reasoning in some passages of judgment in Kavalappara (2) decision would appear to suggest that a law providing for 'acquisition' and 'requisition' by state as understood in sense indicated by art. 31(2)(a), does not fall within art. 19 (1)(f) and that validity of such a law is not to be tested by criterion in art. 19(5) - Appeal dismissed.
