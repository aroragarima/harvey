Hatti v Sunder Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 184; (1970) 2 SCC 841; AIR 1971 SC 2320; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Land Reforms Act, 1954 ss. 11, 13, 84 - Property - Declaration - Possession - Challenged - Appellant was declared Bhumidar of some land belonging to respondent u/s.13 of the Act - Respondent filed suit in Trial court that declaration as Bhumidar was illegal, void and he was entitled to Bhumidar u/s.11 of the Act and possession of land - Trial court decreed said suit in favour of respondent and on appeal same was upheld - Appellant filed appeal against said order before High court which upheld order of Trial court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, jurisdiction of the Civil Court is limited to deciding the issue of title referred to it by Revenue Court and this implies that, if a question of title is raised in an application for declaration of Bhumidari rights under item 4 of Schedule I of the Act, that question will then be referred by the Revenue Assistant to the Civil Court; but a party wanting to raise such a question of title in order to claim Bhumidari right cannot directly approach the Civil Court. Further, declaration by a Gaon Sabha of the right of any person can also be sought without any period of limitation. If there is dispute as to possession of agricultural land, the remedy has to be sought u/s. 84 r/w item 19 of the First Schedule. Hence, reliefs claimed by the respondent in instant suit were, within the competent jurisdiction of the Revenue Assistant, and the Civil Court had no jurisdiction to entertain the suit and decree passed by High court is set aside and suit of respondent was dismissed. Appeal allowed.
