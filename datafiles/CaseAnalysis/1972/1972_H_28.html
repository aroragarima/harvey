Hukam Chand Etc v Union Of India & Others(With Connected Appeal)

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 174; (1972) 2 SCC 601; AIR 1972 SC 2427; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 896

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 309 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, s. 40 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955, r. 49 - Whether in exercise of powers conferred by s. 40 of Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 Central Govt. could amend r. 49 of Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955 with retrospective effect? - Held, act of Central Govt. in laying rules u/s. 40 of Act before each House of Parliament would not prevent courts from scrutinising validity of rules and holding them to be ultra vires if on such scrutiny rules are found to be beyond rule making power of Central Govt. - Therefore, Explanation to r. 49 could not be given retrospective effect - Appeal allowed.
