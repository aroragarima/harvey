Federation Of All India Customs And Centralexcisestenographers v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 472; (1988) 3 SCC 91; (1988) SCC (L&S) 673; AIR 1988 SC 1291; 1988 (7) ATC 591; 1988 (57) FLR 258; JT 1988 (2) SC 519; 1989 LabIC 1157; 1994 (3) LLJ 979; 1988 (2) LLN 145; 1988(1) SCALE 1088; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 998; 1988 (2) SLR 721; 1988 (2) UJ 177

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1) and 39(d) - Equal pay for equal work - Differentiation in pay scales of stenographers in Customs and Excise Departments and their counterparts in Central Secretariat before report of Fourth Pay Commission - (A) Whether Court can interfere with value judgment of administrative authorities with regard to pay for work? - Held, if it has a rational nexus with object to be sought for, a certain amount of value judgment of administrative authorities who are charged with fixing pay scale has to be left with them and it cannot be interfered with by court unless it is demonstrated that either it is irrational or based on no basis or arrived at mala fide either in law or in fact - (B) Whether differentiation in pay scales of stenographers in Customs and Excise Departments and their counterparts in Central Secretariat has a rational nexus with object sought to be avhieved? - Differentiation in pay scales of stenographers in Customs and Excise Departments and their counterparts in Central Secretariat before report of Fourth Pay Commission was justified on similarity of functional work but on dissimilarity of responsibility, confidentiality and relationship with public etc. - Held, it could not be said that differentiation was based on no rational nexus with object sought for to be achieved - Differentiation sought to be justified on ground of dissimilarity of responsibility, confidentiality and relationship with public etc. - Petition dismissed.
