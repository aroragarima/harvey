Sardar Trilok Singh and Others v Satya Deo Tripathi

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 532; (1979) 4 SCC 396; (1979) SCC (Cr) 987; AIR 1979 SC 850; 1979 ALJ 634; 1980 CRLJ 822; 1979 CRLR 86; 1979 UJ 306

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395,468, 465, 471, 412, 120-B/34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 202 - Hire-purchase agreement - Dacoity - Punishment - Sustainability - According to complainant's case amount advanced by said firm was by way of loan while according to case of appellants it was on basis of hire-purchase agreement entered into between parties in support of which formal agreement in writing was also executed - Complainant's case further was that he had paid back two monthly installments total of which was Rs. 3566 and third installment was payable on 7- 31-1973, but before that all accused in high-handed manner during his absence came to his house and in spite of protest by his wife forcibly under threat of arms removed truck and thus they were said to have committed various offences including offence of dacoity - Respondent filed complaint against 10 persons, including three appellants u/ss. 395, 468, 465, 471, 412, 120-B/34 of IPC in Trial Court - Trial Court passed order directing issue of summons against nine accused only u/s. 395 of IPC - Appellants filed appeal before HC to quash criminal proceeding in question in exercise of its inherent power u/s. 482 of the Code - HC by its order dt. 2- 21-1977 has refused to quash proceeding and dismissed appellant's application - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, facts and in the circumstances of this case criminal prosecution deserves to be quashed - Hence, SC opinion point was so wholly without substance that it has been stated merely to be rejected, since parties during course of hearing in this appeal showed their inclination to settle up and end all their disputes and quarrels in relation to matter in question after SC indicated quash proceedings - Therefore, set aside order of HC as also of Trial court and quash criminal proceeding in question initiated by respondent against appellants and others - Appeal allowed.
