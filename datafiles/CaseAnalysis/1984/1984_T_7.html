Associated Cement Companies Limited and another v T.C.Shrivastava & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 133; (1984) Supp SCC 87; (1984) SCC (L&S) 488; AIR 1984 SC 1227; 1984 (49) FLR 102; 1984 LabIC 864; 1984 (2) LLJ 105; 1984 (2) LLN 36; 1984(1) SCALE 596; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 361; 1984 (1) SLJ 657; 1984 (2) SLR 156; 1984 UJ 627

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 5 - Interpretation of para 3 of Standing Order No.17 of Certified Standing Orders of the Company - Whether on its proper construction the certified Standing Order 17 provides for second opportunity being given to a workman after conclusion of the inquiry into his misconduct and before inflicting on him the punishment of dismissal and if so whether the enquiry gets vitiated by not affording him such opportunity? Second opportunity before inflicting punishment of dismissal, not contemplated - The solitary ground on which the inquiry was held to be invalid having disappeared it must follow that the Arbitrator had no Jurisdiction to enter into the merits of the case or interfere with the punishment of dismissal - Appeal No. CA. 1140/74 dismissed; Appeal No.C.A. 209/72 allowed.
