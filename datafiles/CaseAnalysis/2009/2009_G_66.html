Ghulam Rasool Lone v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 883; (2009) 15 SCC 321; 2009 (122) FLR 983; JT 2009 (13) SC 422; 2009(9) SCALE 644; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 591; 2009 (5) SLR 449

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Jammu and Kashmir Police - Recruitment Rules - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - One HD was promoted to the post of Assistant SI of Police illegally upon relaxing the prescribed Rules in 1987 - In the same year one constable filed petition claiming promotion which was allowed and sustained till Supreme Court - Another writ petition by four more persons in 1997, which was allowed by the HC in its single Judge and SLP before the Supreme Court was allowed - In instant case petitioner filed petition in 2000, which was allowed by single Judge, and in LPA filed by State, order of single Judge was set aside - Hence present appeal - Was the DB wrong in its approach so as to warrant interference therewith u/art. 136 of Constitution? - Held, No - Court proceeded on the basis that promotion of HD was illegal, as no particulars had been placed before HC in any of the cases, with regard to the power of the State and/or competent authority to relax the Rules - All the employees should be treated equally, art. 14 and 16 - But equality clause must be enforced in legality and not illegality - Petitioner filed writ petition after more than a decade, on this, Court pointed out settled position of law that who claims equity must enforce his claim within a reasonable time - Art. 226 jurisdiction is a discretionary jurisdiction - While considering the question of delay and latches on the part of petitioner, Court must also consider the effect thereof - Effect in instant case looked into; promotion of HD was effected in 1987; one ARR filed his writ petition immediately thereafter; petitioner waited till ARR was in fact promoted - If petitioner is directed to be promoted, seniority of those who had been promoted in the meantime or have been directly recruited would be affected, Court noted; State would also have to pay the back wages to him which would be a drainage of public funds - Court observed that, whereas employee cannot be denied his promotion in terms of the Rules, the same cannot be granted out of the way as a result whereof the rights of third parties are affected - Aspect of public interest as also the general administration considered by the Court - Impugned judgement, upheld - Petition dismissed.
