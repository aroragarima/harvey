Â Straw Products Limited v Income Tax Officer, Bhopal and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 417; AIR 1968 SC 579; [1968] 68 ITR 227; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2 (2), 10 (1), 10 (2), 10 (5), 19 (2) and 60A - Taxation Laws (Extension to Merged States and Amendment) Act, 1949, ss. 3, 6, 6 (1) and 7 - Assessee commenced business as a manufacture of wrapping paper in 1939 and entered into an agreement with the Ruler of Bhopal under which the assessee was exempted from payment of all taxes to the State for a period of ten years expiring on 31-10-1948 - State of Bhopal merged with India on 1-08-1949 - Ordinance 21 of 1949 and the Taxation Laws (Extension to Merged States and Amendment) Act 67 of 1949 which replaced it had the effect of extending the Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 to the merged States - Under the Ordinance and the Act the Central Government was given power to pass appropriate orders to remove difficulties in the application of the Indian Act to the merged States - Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1949 provided that in making any assessment under the Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 all depreciation actually allowed under any laws or rules of a merged State relating to income-tax and super-tax shall be taken into account in computing the depreciation allowance u/s. 10(2) (vi) (c) of IT Act - Another such order namely Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Amendment Order, 1962 was passed in 1962 which added an explanation to the Removal of Difficulties order, 1949 - In 1962 Act it was provided that where income had been exempted from tax under any laws or rules will force in a merged State or under any agreement with a Ruler, the depreciation that would have been allowed had the income not been so exempted shall be deemed to be the depreciation "actually allowed" for the purpose of computing depreciation allowance and written down value - For a/y 1949-50 depreciation allowed to the appellant company was taken as a percentage of the original cost of its assets and in the 4 subsequent years written down value of the assets was determined on that footing - However, Income Tax Officer commenced proceedings for re-assessment u/s. 34(1)(b) of the Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, against the assessee in respect of the a/ys 1952-53 and 1953-54 and recomputed the taxable income on the footing that since the commencement of the business the assessee must be deemed notionally to have been allowed depreciation under the Bhopal Income-tax Act - Appellate Assistant Commissioner and the Income-tax Appellate Tribunal disagreed with the Income-tax Officer and restored the original assessment - On a reference made by the Appellate Tribunal, the HC held in favour of the assessee - In the meantime, Central Government passed Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1949 and added where income had been exempted from tax under any laws or rules in force in a merged State or under any agreement with a Ruler, the depreciation that would have been allowed had the income not been so exempted - Assessee filed writ petition before HC to declare order passed by Central Government as void - HC rejected said writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of Central Government could be upheld? - Held, no return was filed by assessee, no proceedings for assessment were taken and no depreciation was allowed for purpose of Bhopal Income-tax Act - Exercise of the power to make provisions or to issue directions as may appear necessary to the Central Government is conditioned by the existence of a difficulty arising in giving effect to the provisions of any, rule or order - Power conferred by s. 6 of 1949 Act is power to remove difficulty arising i assessment of income in merged States - Impugned order sought, in purported exercise of the power u/s. 6, to remove a difficulty which had not arisen - Fact that Courts had not accepted the contention of the department that notional Computation of depreciation should be allowed in cases where the assessee had been exempt from tax in a merged State, was not the kind of difficulty for the removal of which the power u/s. 6 could be used - Hence, clause (b) of the Explanation in the Taxation Laws (Merged States) (Removal of Difficulties) Order, 1962, is ultra vires the Central Government when exercising the power u/s. 6 of Act 67 of 1949 and the Revenue authorities are not entitled to levy tax on the basis of depreciation allowance computed in accordance with that clause in the Order - Order of Central Government set aside - Appeal allowed.
