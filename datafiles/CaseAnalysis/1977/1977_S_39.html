Â State of Karnataka and Another v Shri Ranganatha Reddy and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Bhagwati, P.S. Kailasam, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 247; (1977) 4 SCC 471; AIR 1978 SC 215; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Insurance; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - Karnataka Contract Carriages (Acquisition) Act, 1976 - Whether this law of acquisition is for public purpose or not? - Acquisition of vehicles, namely contract carriages, their permits and other assets for running them for purposes of State Road Transport Corporation could not be challenged as being not for a public purpose merely because it was for purposes of transferring them to State Road Transport Corporation - Operators were misusing their permits granted to them as contract carriages permits - In many cases vehicles were used as stage carriages picking up and dropping passengers in way -Held, to prevent such misuse and to provide for better facilities to transport passengers and to general public it was necessary to acquire vehicles, permits and all rights, title and interest of contract carriage operators in or over lands, buildings, workshops and other places - Acquisition was in public interest.
