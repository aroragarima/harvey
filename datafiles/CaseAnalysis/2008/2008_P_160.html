Ashutosh Chaturvedi v Prano Devi @ Parani Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 709; (2008) 15 SCC 610; AIR 2008 SC 2171; 2008(4) ALL MR 927; 2008 (3) AWC 2384; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 162; JT 2008 (6) SC 354; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 840; 2008(7) SCALE 279; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 801

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Survivorship, Co-Heirs

Summary: Appellant filed a suit for declaration of his title and confirmation of possession as also for setting aside a deed of sale executed by the respondent - Application for amendment of plaint dismissed - Appeal against - Held, application for amendment was filed 13 years after the filing of the suit - A suit claiming preferential right was required to be filed ordinarily within the prescribed period of limitation - Plea that two deeds of sale were executed in violation of the order of injunction and in that view of the matter, the deeds of sale must be held to be invalid in law, cannot be a ground for allowing the amendment of the plaint - Appeal dismissed.
