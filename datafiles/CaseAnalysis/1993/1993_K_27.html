D.Devaji v K.Sudarashana Rao

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1440; (1994) Supp1 SCC 729; JT 1993 (6) SC 421; 1993 (2) RCJ 421; 1994 (1) RCR(Rent) 185; 1993 (2) RentLR 606; 1993(4) SCALE 41; 1994 (1) UJ 49

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction petition - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act 1960,s.10(3)(a)(iii) - Whether landlord who was occupying a non-residential building of his own was not entitled to evict a tenant in occupation of another non-residential building? - Landlord must be in possession of a non-residential building in the city, town or village and if he requires another non-residential building for expansion of his business or to establish another business or needs additional accommodation of a non-residential building in the same city, town or village,s.10(3)(a)(iii) creates an embargo - Held, when respondent already has an independent office f or carrying on his business.the requirement does not appear to be bona fide as found by the appellate court - Apart from the fact that he had already 8 shops and other houses to carry on his business.the bona fide claim is belied from the above evidence and his conduct - Finding of the appellate court that the respondent does not bona fide require the demised building for business is well founded - Respondent's application for eviction stands rejected - Appeal allowed.
