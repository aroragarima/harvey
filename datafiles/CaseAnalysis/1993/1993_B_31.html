Chaya and Others v Bapusaheb and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 867; (1994) 2 SCC 41; 1993 (3) AWC 1375; 1993 (1) CCC 301; 1993 (1) HLR 317; JT 1993 (1) SC 267; 1993(1) SCALE 195; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Death Of Defendant

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 33 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Suit filed by members of public claiming declaration of customary right to bury dead in land - Both owners and vendees were joined as defendants - Trial Court decreed suit against all defendants - Defendant 1 alone filed Regular Appeal to District Court and joined defendants 2 and 3 as respondents 55 and 56 - District Court dismissed appeal - Defendant 1 alone filed a second appeal in High Court challenging decree - During pendency of appeal defendant 2 (respondent 55) died leaving behind his widow and minor children and they were not brought on record in appeal - HC held customary right was not established and set aside decree of Trial Court and restricted decree to defendant 1 only - Widow and children of defendant 2 moved HC by a review petition, to modify decree and to extend relief to their land - HC held review petition is barred by limitation - (A) Whether appeal before District Court had abated in view of non-impleadment of appellants? - Held, whatever relationship of defendants 1 and 2 inter-se between themselves on one hand and between defendants 1, 2, 3 and vendee-defendants on other, plaintiffs proceeded on presumption that they were concerned with entire suit property and customary right was to be asserted against whole of suit property as such, which was sufficiently represented in law by surviving defendants - Since right to sue survived against whole of property and against surviving defendants, notwithstanding death of defendant 2, appeal had not abated - (B) Whether HC could have passed decree embracing entire suit property? - Held, HC ought to have exercised its power under O. 41, r. 33 of CPC, 1908 - Non-exercise of power has resulted not only in miscarriage of justice but in contradictory results in respect of same subject-matter and based on same alleged right - Appeal allowed.
