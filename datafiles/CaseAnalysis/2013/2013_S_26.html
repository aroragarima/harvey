Samrendra Beura v U.O.I. & Others

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 335; (2013) 14 SCC 672; AIR 2013 SC 2414; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4492; 2013 CRLJ 3040; JT 2013 (7) SC 446; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 282; 2013(7) SCALE 378; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Absence Without Leave, Pre-trial detention

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Air Force Act, 1950, ss. 39(a), 161(1) - Absence without leave - Conviction - Pre-trial detention - Set off - Legality - Petitioner (employee) was found guilty of offence u/s. 39(a) of the Act, and was awarded sentence to suffer RI for 3 months along with other punishments, which was affirmed by Competent Authority (Air Officer Commanding-in-Chief) u/s. 161(1) of the Act - Petitioner filed instant petition u/art. 32 of Constitution praying prayed for issue of a writ of habeas corpus directing the respondents to release him as he was in illegal detention because he had already spent 1 and half months in custody before the conviction was recorded by Court-martial - Petitioner contended that sentence should commence from 1-2-2013, the date on which he surrendered and was taken into custody - Whether the period of custody prior to date of passing and signing of order by District Court-martial was to be set off in respect of sentence imposed - Held, pre-trial detention could not be set off against the sentence of imprisonment passed by Court-martial for the offence u/s. 39(a) of the Act which was affirmed u/s. 161(1) of the Act and the period of sentence should commence from the date when the original proceeding was signed by Presiding Officer - Thus, there was no illegal detention warranting issue of writ of habeas corpus - It was it apt to recommend the UOI to seriously consider to bring an amendment in the Act so that hardships faced by persons convicted by Court-martial were avoided - Petition disposed of.
