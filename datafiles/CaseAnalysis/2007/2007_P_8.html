K. Prakashan v P. K. Surenderan

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1381; (2008) 1 SCC 258; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 314; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 480; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 372; 2007 (4) JCC (NI) 357; JT 2007 (11) SC 573; 2007 (4) KLT 502; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 543; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 588; 2007(12) SCALE 96; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 1010

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Post-Dated Cheque, Court's Jurisdiction, Stop Payment, Banking Operation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 &139 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Dishonor of Cheque - Acquittal - Sustainable - Respondent presented cheque for encashment, issued by appellant in respect of money borrowed from respondent - Cheque was dishonoured due to insufficient funds - Respondent filed complaint and Trial Court acquitted appellant - Respondent filed appeal before HC, which reversed judgment of Trial Court and convicted appellant/accused - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that having been found to be sufficient for shifting burden of proof upon respondent/complainant , as appellant/accused had discharged his primary onus, HC committed a serious error in reversing judgment of Trial Court - Whether impugned judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, standard of proof in discharge of burden in terms of s. 139 of the 1881 Act, being of preponderance of a probability, inference therefore could be drawn not only from materials brought on record but also from reference to the circumstances upon which accused relied upon - Thus, if two views were possible, Appellant Court shall not reverse judgment of acquittal only because another view was possible to be taken - In instant case, HC proceeded on premise that appellant had not been able to discharge his burden of proof in terms of s. 139 of the 1881 Act, without posing unto itself further question as to whether said burden of proof could be discharges - Further it did not take into consideration legal principle that standard of proof upon prosecution and upon accused was different - Hence, judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
