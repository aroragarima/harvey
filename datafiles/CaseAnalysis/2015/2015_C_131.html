Fortis Hospital Limited v Commissioner of Customs Import

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 416; 2015 (318) E.L.T. 551; JT 2015 (5) SC 527; 2015(5) SCALE 68; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Adjudicating Authority, Confiscation, Such Goods

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, s. 124, 125 (2) - Confiscation of equipment - Payment of fine - Appellant was issued with show cause notice u/s. 124 of the Act alleging breach of conditions stipulated for grant of exemption from payment of import duty and after hearing appellants, orders were passed by adjudicating authority holding that appellant committed breach of notification dt.1-3-1988 and confiscated medical equipment apart from demand of duty and imposition of penalty - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal before Appellate Tribunal which allowed the same holding that demand of duty was not legally sustainable and set aside that part of order of adjudicating authority - Aggrieved, respondent- revenue challenged order before HC and by impugned judgment, HC allowed appeal and set aside order of Appellate Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether revenue is justified in demanding payment of duty.

Held, show cause notice issued u/s. 124 of the Act was confined to confiscation of imported machinery and imposition of penalty and nothing was stated about the payment of duty. Hence, there could not have been direction to that effect in the final order. Further, s. 125(2) of the Act is not applicable to cases where option to pay fine in lieu of confiscation is not exercised by importer. Only when said contingency is met, duty becomes payable. However, contingency contained in s. 125(2) of the Act did not occur in the present procedure for want of exercise of option to pay fine by appellants. Hence, view taken by Appellate Tribunal is correct and contrary view taken by HC in impugned judgment is not warranted on the interpretation of s. 125(2) of the Act. Hence, order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - U/s. 125(1) of the Act, option is given to the importer whose goods are confiscated, to pay the fine in lieu of confiscation and redeem the confiscated goods and before said action is taken, show cause notice is to be issued u/s. 124 of the Act.
