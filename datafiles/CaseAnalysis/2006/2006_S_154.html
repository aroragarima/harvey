Baldev Singh v Shinder Pal Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 717; (2007) 1 SCC 341; JT 2006 (9) SC 442; 2007 (145) PLR 252; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 891; 2006(11) SCALE 54; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Equality Of Votes, District Election Officer

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Punjab State Election Commission Act, 1994 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 15 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Punjab Panchayati Election Rules, 1994, rr. 33, 33(2)(e), 35 - Whether Tribunal was correct in directing recounting? - Held, returning Officer was categorical in his statement that both candidates have received equal number of votes and thus, result of election to post of Sarpanch was declared by draw of lots - Official act should be presumed to have been done in ordinary course of business - A recounting, as is well known, should not ordinarily be directed to be made - There exists certain limitation in this behalf - Appeal allowed.
