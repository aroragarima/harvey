All Party Hill Leaders’ Conference, Shillong v Captain M.A. Sangma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 129; (1977) 4 SCC 161; AIR 1977 SC 2155; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 393; 1977 UJ 606

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Reserved Symbol, A0031n, Election Symbols Order 1968, Code of Ejection Rules 1961, Punjab Welfare Officers Recruitment and Conditions of Service Rules 1952, Assam Reorganisation (Meghalaya) Act, 1969

Summary: Constitution - Elections - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136(1) and 324 - Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968, Paras 6 and 7- Meaning of word Tribunal - It should have been constituted by State and invested with some judicial power - Election Commission is a creation of Constitution - Election Commission resolves disputes between rival parties with regard to claims for being a recognised political party for purpose of electoral symbol - Held, Election Commission is a tribunal within ambit of art. 136 (1) - It is however, absolutely necessary that authority in order to come within ambit of art. 136 (1) as Tribunal must be constituted by State and invested with some function of judicial power of State - Appeal allowed.
