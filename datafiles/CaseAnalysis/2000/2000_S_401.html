Rajender Singh and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 354; (2000) 4 SCC 298; (2000) SCC (Cr) 796; AIR 2000 SC 1779; 2000 (41) AllCriC 696; 2000 (2) CCR 67; 2000 (3) Crimes 21; 2000 CRLJ 2199; 2000 (2) JCC 460; JT 2000 (4) SC 293; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 537; 2000(3) SCALE 137; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1073; 2000 (3) SLT 569; 2000 (4) Supreme 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Former Statement

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 34 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - Murder - Prosecution witnesses was found to be trustworthy and non - explanation of injuries on accused cannot be held to be fatal - Further, injury caused by co - accused on body of deceased found sufficient in ordinary course to cause death, there was contradiction among prosecution witnesses regarding assault by accused - Neither unlawful assembly was formed nor was there any common object to murder of deceased and even accused party were not armed initially - Held, provisions of s. 145 were not complied with and accused could not have shared common intention which developed at spur of moment with co - accused to murder deceased and therefore conviction of accused was altered from s. 302/34 to s. 324.
