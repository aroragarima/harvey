Brace Transport Corporation of Monrovia, Bermuda v Orient Middle East Lines Limited, Saudi Arabia and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 822; (1995) Supp2 SCC 280; AIR 1994 SC 1715; 1994 (1) ARBLR 123; 1993 (3) CCC 687; 1994 (4) CompLJ 214; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 461; 1993(4) SCALE 207; 1994 (1) UJ 190

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award, Domestic Award, Enforcement Of Foreign Judgments/ Decrees

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 5 - Foreign award - (A) Effect of fact that mere recognition of foreign award can be sought - (B) Jurisdiction of court to take award on file arising out of sale of ship by one foreign company to another and landing in hands of firm in India - Maintainablity of application - Held, it acts as a shield against re-agitation of issues settled by award - Application held, maintainable not on ground that ship was in India but because money payable under award was held in India - Appeal allowed.
