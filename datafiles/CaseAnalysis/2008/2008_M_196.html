Surjit Singh v Mahanagar Telephone Nigam Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 677; (2009) 16 SCC 722; AIR 2008 SC 2226; 2008 (71) ALR 803; 2008 (3) AWC 2442; 2008 (1) CCC 137; 2008 (149) DLT 515; JT 2008 (5) SC 325; 2008 (2) KLT 473; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 106; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 229; 2008(6) SCALE 207; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Telecom

Keywords: Indian Telegraph Rules

Summary: Indian Telegraph Rules - Held, telephone line in the name of the person who is really paying the bills in connection with the telephone line in the name of another person who is economically dependent on the former can be disconnected for non payment of bills in connection with the telephone line in the name of the latter - Both the telephone lines in the name of the appellant, one at his residence and the other at his business premises, can be disconnected for non-payment of the dues in connection with the line in the name of his dependent wife - Appeal dismissed.
