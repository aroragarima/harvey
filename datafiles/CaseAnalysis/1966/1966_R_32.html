Â State of Nagaland v Ratan Singh

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1967 SC 212; 1967 CRLJ 265; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Naga Hills-Tuensang Area Act, 1957, State of Nagaland Act, 1962

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 109, 201, 436, 34 - Murder - Trial before authority - Maintainability - Respondents were members of Central Reserve Police (CRP), allegedly committed murder of hostile Nagas for which charges were framed against them u/ss. 302, 109, 201 r/w s. 34 of IPC by State/appellant before Additional Deputy Commissioner - Aggrieved respondents filed writ before HC challenging that concerned authority did not had power to conduct trial u/rr. of 1937, which was affirmed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, that rr. of 1937 continue to be in force and govern trial of those respondents - CrPC admittedly does not apply there and concerned authority was therefore right in holding trial u/rr. of 1937 - Therefore, it was obvious that in following spirit of the Code and in applying warrant procedure concerned authority had followed right procedure and HC was in error in thinking that neither rr. of 1937, nor any rules applied to this area - Hence appeals were allowed and order of HC was set aside and directed that trial of respondents should proceed u/rr. of 1937 - Appeals allowed.
