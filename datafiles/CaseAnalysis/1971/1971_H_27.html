Hasib v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Subimal Chandra Roy, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 794; (1972) 4 SCC 773; AIR 1972 SC 283; 1972 CRLJ 233; 1971 UJ 830

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 395 and 398 - Dacoity - Convicted u/s. 395 - Only evidence against appellant that appellant identified by one prosecution witness - Held, FIR cannot be used to corraborate or contradict other witnesses - Test indentification parade not held in satisfactory manner - No legal evidence connecting appellant with offence - Appellant acquited - Appeal allowed.
