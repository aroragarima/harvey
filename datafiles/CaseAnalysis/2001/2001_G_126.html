Land T. MC. Neil Limited etc. v Government of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20319; (2001) 3 SCC 170; (2001) SCC (L&S) 550; AIR 2001 SC 844; 2001 (1) CLR 804; 2001 (88) FLR 1030; JT 2001 (2) SC 484; 2001 LabIC 734; 2001 (1) LLJ 735; 2001(1) SCALE 425; 2001 (1) SCJ 671; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 701; 2001 (1) SLR 767; 2001 (1) SLT 749; 2001 (1) Supreme 414; 2001 (1) UC 451; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1519

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, s. 10(1) - Whether notification issued by Govt. of Tamil Nadu prohibiting employment of contract labour in process of sweeping and scavenging in establishment/factories which are employing 50 or more workmen without consultation with State Contract Labour Advisory Board is justified u/s. 10(1) of Act? - Held, it is not very clear as to how the Govt. could have reached the conclusion one way or the other in the absence of any advice by the Board and in the absence of any other material - The decision of the Govt. in issuing the notification u/s. 10(1) of the Act is thus vitiated because of non-consideration of relevant materials - The mere fact that several notifications have been issued in relation to contract labour or that system in sweeping and scavenging has been abolished in some other industries may not, by itself, be sufficient to hold that a common notification applicable to all industries and establishments abolishing contract labour in sweeping and scavenging could have been issued by the Govt. without necessary material - The Govt. ought to take into consideration the relevant factors contained in s. 10(2)(a) to (d) of the Act and thereafter decide the matter - These aspects were, however, lost sight of by the High Court in the decisions rendered by it earlier - Quash the notification issued by the Govt. of Tamil Nadu - However, it is open to the Govt to issue a fresh notification after due consideration of the matter in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
