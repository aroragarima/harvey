Â A. B. Abdulkadir and Others v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 561; AIR 1962 SC 922; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 741

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Board Of Revenue Act, 1913

Summary: Trade - Cochin Tobacco Act - Travancore Tobacco Act - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 13 (2) - Finance Act, 1950 - Whether Rules framed under Acts repealed by a Central Act is void ab initio? - Held, where Act repealed provides substantially for all matters contained in Act effecting repeal there is correspondence between two Acts and earlier Act would thus stand repealed - It is not necessary that there should be complete identity between repealing Act and Act repealed in every respect - Thus there is no law to support Rules framed by State Govt. in January 1951 and hence same were invalid ab initio and have no force and effect whether Rules could be declared invalid even after their abrogation - Since Rules were in force when petitions were filed, petitioners are entitled to a declaration that Rules were invalid because at any rate that would give them relief so far as period after their petitions is concerned while Rules remained in force - Petitions allowed.
