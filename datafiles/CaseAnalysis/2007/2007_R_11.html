S. Sethuraman v R. Venkataraman and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 556; (2007) 6 SCC 382; (2007) 2 SCC 471; AIR 2007 SC 2499; 2007 (4) ESC 539; JT 2007 (7) SC 82; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 449; 2007(7) SCALE 301; 2007 (7) SCJ 636; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 922; 2007 (4) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Corporate Body, Tamil Nadu Recognised Private Schools (Regulation) Act, 1973, Tamil Nadu Private Schools (Regulation) Rules, 1974

Summary: Tamil Nadu Recognised Private Schools (Regulation) Act, 1973; Tamil Nadu Private Schools (Regulation) Rules, 1974 (Rules) - Recruitment/Promotion to the post of Headmaster in an aided or unaided school in the State of Tamil Nadu - Second respondent opined that the merit and ability of both the appellant and the first respondent were equal and, therefore, since the first respondent was senior, he should be selected for the post of Headmaster - Held, Appellate Authority failed to take into consideration the fact that the appellant had passed the Accounts test - Why acquisition of a higher qualification for the purpose of holding the post of Headmaster which would be helpful to him in his functioning as a head of an educational institute would not be relevant - Matter is remitted to the Joint Director of School Education for consideration of the matter afresh strictly in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
