Associated Cement Co. Ltd. v State Of M.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 395; (2004) 9 SCC 727; AIR 2004 SC 4516; 2004 (168) E.L.T. 151; 2004(5) SCALE 718; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - By a resolution, Municipal Corporation, Katni levied tax on export of goods from within area of Municipal Corporation - Whether refractory cement is cement so as to attract liability of export tax - Cement is exclusively used as a building material and is a commodity of everyday use - Main property of refractory is that it can withstand very high temperature, corrosion and abrasion - Refractory is used for entirely different purpose namely for furnaces, linings and for insulation - Refractory material produced not fall within Entry 'all types of cement' - Set aside HC judgement.
