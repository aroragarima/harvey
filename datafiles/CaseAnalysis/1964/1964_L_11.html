State of Rajasthan v Leela Jain

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1965 SC 1296; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan City Municipal Appeals (Regulation) Act, 1950, s. 4(1) - (A)Whether words of an enactment can be rejected for being in contravention with context in which they occur or purpose with which they were intended? - Held, words of an enactment cannot be rejected if they are in contravention with context in which they occur or purpose with which they were intended - (B)Whether Rajasthan City Municipal Appeals (Regulation) Act, 1950, s. 4(1) proviso, by its characteristics fulfills purpose of a proviso or not? - Further held, operative words in main part of s.4(1) prohibit all appeals from appellate orders of Commr. - Primary purpose of proviso is to provide a substitute or an alternative remedy to that which is prohibited by main part of s. 4(1) - There is no question as to proviso carving out any portion out of area covered by main part and leaving other part unaffected - Proviso is really not a proviso in accepted sense but an independent legislative provision by which to a remedy which is prohibited by main part of section, an alternative is provided - Proviso is not co-extensive with but covers a field wider than main part of s. 4(1) - Words in s. 4(1) proviso - Orders of a Municipal authority include final orders not subject to a municipal appeal which would fall into same category as appellate orders of a Commr. - State Govt. has power to entertain revision against order of President of Municipal Council - Appeal allowed and case remanded.
