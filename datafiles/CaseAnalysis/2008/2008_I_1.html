(1) India Cine Agencies; (2) Computer Graphics Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2175; (2008) 17 SCC 385; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 8; [2009] 308 ITR 98; 2008(15) SCALE 73; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 1122; [2008] 175 TAXMAN 361

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Finance (No.2) Act, 1977, Cine-Workers And Cinema Theatre Workers (Regulation Of Employment) Act, 1981

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Finance (No.2) Act, 1977 - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 32A, 32AB, 80-I, 80-I(2), 80CC(3)(a)(i), 80HH, 80J(4) - Manufacture - Entitlement to benefit - Assessment - Maintainability - Assessee filed appeal in HC relating to entitlement of benefit u/ss. 32AB, 80HH, 80I of the 1961 Act which were decided in favour of revenue - Hence, instant appeals - Whether activity undertaken was manufacture or production u/s. 2(f) of the 1944 - Held, SC relied on CIT v. N.C. Budharaja and Co. 1993 Indlaw SC 1439 said that production was much wider than word manufacture - Further, if there was no manufacturing activity question of referring to Item 10 of Eleventh Schedule for purpose of exclusion did not arise - Thus, Eleventh Schedule, which was inserted by the 1977 Act had reference to ss. 32A, 32AB, 80CC (3)(a)(i), 80-I(2), 80J(4) and 88A (3)(a)(i) of the 1961 Act - Appeals allowed.
