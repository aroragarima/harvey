Bhagwati Prasad and another v Delhi State Mineral Development Corporation

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 347; (1990) SCC (L&S) 174; (1990) 1 SCC 361; AIR 1990 SC 371; 1990 (1) CLR 163; 1990 (40) DLT 457; 1990 (26) ECR 480; 1990 (60) FLR 157; JT 1989 (4) SC 541; 1990 LabIC 126; 1990 (1) LLJ 320; 1989(2) SCALE 1337; 1992 (8) SLR 784; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 513; 1990 (1) UJ 254

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.32, 39(d), 309 and 311 - Industrial dispute - Daily rated workers appointed did not possess minimum prescribed educational qualification at the time of appointment - Gained sufficient experience after many years of service - Whether confirmation can be refused on ground that they did not possess requisite qualification? - Held, confirmation cannot be refused on that ground and they would be entitled to pay equal to persons appointed in regular basis - Appeal allowed.
