Â Parbhani Transport Co-Operative Society Limited v Regional Transport Authority, Aurangabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 1960 SC 801; 1960 SCJ 1250; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939,s. 42(3) - Prohibition Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether State of Bombay was entitled to apply for permits under ch. IV? - Held, Govt. has of course power to do any business it likes and therefore business of running stage carriages - There was a change made in cl. (a) of s. 42(3) by amendment of 1956 - Previously, it was not necessary for Govt. to obtain permits u/s 42 (1) for buses that it intended to run as stage carriages - Since amendment, Govt. can no longer run transport vehicles for commercial purposes without obtaining permits u/s 42 (1) - Now plying of buses as stage carriages is a commercial enterprise and for such buses, therefore under sections as they stand, Govt. would require permits as any one else - That being so, sections clearly contemplate that Govt. may apply for and obtain permits for its buses run as stage carriages - (B) Whether provisions of art. 14 had been infringed by decision of respondent? - Further held, respondent No 1 was acting as a quasi judicial body and if it has made any mistake in its decision, there are appropriate remedies available to petitioner for obtaining relief - It cannot complain of a breach of art. 14 - Petition dismissed.
