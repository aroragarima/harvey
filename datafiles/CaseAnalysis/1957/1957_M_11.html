Commissioner of Income Tax v Mcmillan and Company

Supreme Court of India

16 October 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 75; AIR 1958 SC 207; [1958] 33 ITR 182; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1958 SCJ 530; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.13,31,31(3),31B - Non Resident company - Return of income - Bad debt - Assessment - Assessee/respondent was a non resident company which has its head office in London and branches in India - It sells and publishes books and magazines in various parts of world - It submitted a return of income in which with regard to all publications sold in India, whether printed in India or elsewhere, a fixed percentage of marked price was adopted as cost of production - Income tax Officer accepted it and subject to certain minor modifications as respects some items of expenditure and an alleged bad debt and assessed Rs. 82,623 - Assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Latter issued a notice u/ s.31(3) of the Act against assessee, and enhanced assessment - Assessee/company appealed to Appellate Tribunal, who remanded the case to Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal then held that Appellate Assistant Commissioner had no jurisdiction - Appealed to HC - HC held, appellate Commissioner on appeal cannot reject assessee's books of account, which was accepted by Income tax Officer - Whether conclusion drawn by HC stands justified - Hence instant appeal by Commissioner - Held, yes - PC was of opinion that conclusion by HC was correct - If Commissioner considers that any order passed in any proceedings by Income tax Officer is erroneous in so far as it was prejudicial to interests of Revenue, it was open to him after giving assessee an opportunity of being heard as he deems necessary to pass such orders thereon as circumstances of the case justify - Ends of justice would be equally served if Commissioner in such a case makes an order cancelling assessment and directing a fresh assessment and remands case back to Income tax Officer with a direction to apply his mind properly to facts of case and determine question which is within his exclusive province under terms of proviso to s. 13 of income tax act - Appeal allowed.
