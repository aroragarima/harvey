C. Mackertich v Steuart and Company, Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 587; (1971) 3 SCC 39; AIR 1970 SC 839

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - By a registered lease, M and F let out subject matter to four persons - Term of lease was for 50 years and by another registered lease between same parties premises were leased out for a period of 46 years and three months - A company limited by shares namely Steuart and Co. Ltd., was incorporated - An agreement was made and signed between partners of partnership firm and Steuart and Co. Ltd., wherein it was stated that partners of partnership firm would sell business and transfer all assets of firm to incorporated company - No separate deed of transfer in respect of leasehold properties was executed by partnership firm in favour of new incorporated company - But lessors treated incorporated company as monthly tenants and accepted rents from them - F ultimately sold his interest as owner of property to M - Appellant C is son of M and has inherited all his interests and is, therefore, sole landlord of two premises in suit - C claimed that defendant had forfeited protection against eviction - Notice determining tenancy was issued and calling upon defendant to vacate premises - As defendant did not give up possession of premises, plaintiff instituted two suits decree for ejectment - Respondent contested suits on ground that tenancies were governed by terms of lease deed antidates and that in circumstances tenancies were not governed by West Bengal Premises Rent Control Act, 1950 and could not be determined by service of 15 days' notice - Subordinate Judge found that defendant being a separate legal entity from partnership firm members of which had been granted two leases leasehold could not be transferred to defendant company without registered documents and in absence of such registered documents defendant company could not claim to be lessees governed by registered lease deeds, further held that plaintiff accepted rent for many years from defendant as a tenant - In circumstances defendant must be deemed to be tenant from month to month terminable by 15 days notice u/s. 106 of Act and rejected claim of defendant that purpose of tenancy was manufacturing and therefore 6 months notice ending with year of tenancy was necessary therefor granted plaintiff decree for ejectment as well as arrears of rent and mesne profits in both suits - Against this decision defendant company preferred appeals - By its judgment HC held that plaintiff was not entitled to a decree for eviction as purpose of lease was manufacturing and, therefore, notice was bad - HC, however, upheld findings of Subordinate Judge and held that suit should be decreed in part for arrears of rent claimed but dismissed so far as claim for ejectment and damages and mesne profits is concerned - Whether such order is valid in law? - Held, appellant contended that HC was in error in holding that tenancy was for manufacturing purpose and that six months notice terminable with a year of tenancy was required - It was said that onus of proving that tenancy was for manufacturing purpose was upon defendant and as that point was not raised in written statement HC should not have allowed respondent to raise question - Even upon that assumption, there is no evidence in case to support finding of HC that purpose of lease was dominantly for manufacturing purpose - There is no proper material on record of case from which it can be inferred that dominant purpose of lease was manufacture or that respondent substantially used premises for manufacturing purposes - It follows, therefore that Trial Court was right in holding that tenancy was monthly and notices were legally valid for termination of tenancy - Appeal allowed.
