Bharat Bhushan Bansal v U.P. Small Industries Corporation Limited Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 4; (1999) 2 SCC 166; AIR 1999 SC 899; 1999 ALJ 679; 1999 (1) ARBLR 326; 1999 (1) CLT 110; JT 1999 (1) SC 97; 1999 (1) RAJ 378; 1999(1) SCALE 109; 1999 (2) SCJ 118; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 181; 1999 (1) SLT 307; 1999 (1) Supreme 166

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Discovery and production of documents

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8 - Arbitration clause - 'Decision of M.D. of U.P.S.I.C. on all other matters shall be final' - Not mentioning that any dispute could be referred to arbitration of managing director nor spelling any duty on him to record evidence or hear both parties before deciding questions before him - Held, intention appears to avoid disputes rather to decide in quasi judicial manner - Clause does not contemplate any arbitration.
