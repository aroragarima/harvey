Committee - GFIL v Libra Buildtech Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 780; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 106; 2016(6) ALL MR 486; 2015 (113) ALR 739; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 424; 2016 (130) RD 465; 2015(10) SCALE 316; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Corporate - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 65 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 49(d)(2), 50 - Cancellation of sale agreement - Refund of Stamp duty - Justification - Respondent Company went into liquidation, hence, claims were filed by creditors against respondent company - Further, property of company was sold in auction which was acquired by appellant being highest bidder - Further, full payment was made by appellant and stamp papers was purchased for execution of sale deed - Further, sale deed was executed in favor of appellant, however respondent company failed to handover possession of properties to appellant - Further, vide HC directed respondent Company to refund amount deposited by bidders -Further, respondent company refunded entire sale consideration with interest to appellant, however, while refunding it, respondent company deducted TDS on interest accrued on amount deposited by appellant despite fact that bank had already deducted same - Further, SC vide order cancelled transaction in question and directed respondent company to refund the entire sale consideration with interest to applicant - Further, with respect to refund of stamp duty, SC permitted applicants to approach State Govt. to claim refund - Whether appellant is entitled to refund of stamp duty from respondent company.

Held, despite applicants depositing entire sale consideration and payment of for stamp duty to purchase properties in question, and having performed their part of contract, in letter and spirit, respondent company Committee i.e. seller failed to place applicants in possession of properties. Hence, it resulted in frustrating purpose as was originally intended between parties. Further, SC passed order and cancelled transaction in question. Further, with respect to refund of stamp duty amount, SC held that applicants are also entitled to claim refund of entire stamp duty amount from State Exchequer, which they spent for execution of sale deeds in their favour in relation to properties in question because appellants throughout performed their part of contract and ensured that transaction in question is accomplished as was originally intended but for reasons to which they were not responsible, transaction could not be accomplished. Further, applicants in law were entitled to claim restoration of all such benefits/advantages from State once the transaction was cancelled by SC in light of principle contained in s. 65 of 1872 Act which enable party to contract to seek restoration of all such advantage from other party which they took from such contract when contract is discovered to be void or becomes void. SC held that applicants' bona fide genuine claim of refund cannot be denied on technical grounds. Further, SC held that case of applicants can be brought u/s. 49 (d)(2) of 1899 Act read with s. 50(3) of 1899 Act to enable State to entertain application made by applicants seeking refund of stamp duty amount. Hence, SC directed State entire stamp duty spent by applicants for purchasing of stamps papers for execution of sale deeds in relation to purchase of properties in question. Application allowed.

Ratio- It is settled principle of law based on principle of equity that person cannot be penalized for no fault of his and Act of court would cause no prejudice to any of his right.
