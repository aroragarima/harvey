G.L. Didwania and Another v Income Tax Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 789; (1995) Supp2 SCC 724; 1997 (140) CTR 273; 1999 (108) E.L.T. 16; [1997] 224 ITR 687

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Authorized authority - Genuine firm - False statement - Appellant was an assessee filed his return of income showing his income as Rs.26, 224 in prescribed form and verification was signed by him and return was filed - Appellant showed his business income from firms in Delhi and Bombay - Assessment was made by officer concerned taking income to be of Rs.35, 699 - Another firm Young India and Transport Company in which minor children of appellant and his two employees were partners - During course of assessment proceeding assessing authority reached conclusion that it was not a genuine firm and instrument of partnership was invalid and inoperative - Proceedings u/s.147 and 148 of Act were initiated against appellant and assessment was reopened - Appellant filed his return showing his income as Rs.29,500 in prescribed form and verification was signed by him - By order Income-tax Officer assessed income of appellant at Rs.52, 634 by adding income of Young India and Transport Company and for same assessment year as though it was income of appellant - Appellant made a statement in verification to return filed and delivered an account/statement which according to assessing authority was false or which assessee knew or believed to be false - Appellant/assessee filed an appeal before Income-tax Appellate Tribunal and Tribunal by its order allowed appeal and held there was no substantial material to hold appellant was owner of the entire business - Appellate Tribunal allowed appeal in favour of appellant and filed a petition before magistrate to drop criminal proceedings - Appellant / assessee filed an application u/s482, CPC, before HC and HC dismissed - Hence instant petition filed - Whether appellant/assessee made a false statement regarding income - Held, appellant wrongly and falsely declared income of Young India and Transport Company did not belong to him and made a false verification and income of Young India and Transport Company did belong to him and failed to include in income of Young India and Transport Company and appellants income amounted to suppression - Appeal allowed.
