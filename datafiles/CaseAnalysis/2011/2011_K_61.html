J & K Housing Board & Anr. v Kunwar Sanjay Krishan Kaul & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 770; (2011) 10 SCC 714; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 600; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 976

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition Rules, Jammu & Kashmir Land Acquisition Act, 1990

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Jammu & Kashmir Land Acquisition Act, 1990, s. 4 (1) (a), (b) and (c) - Housing Board Act, 1976 - Land acquisition -Notification - Acquisition proceedings - Quash - Appellant issued notification u/s. 4 (1), Act 1990 for development of housing colony - Notification was published in two newspapers - Appellant issued notice u/ss. 5 and 5-A, Act 1990, to respondents for hearing of objections - None of respondent was present - Declaration u/s. 6, 1990 Act, was made - Further, appellant was directed to take possession of afore said land subject to Land Acquisition Rules - Appellant informed respondents about acquisition of land u/ss. 9 and 9-A, 1990 Act and requesting them to present - Notification was issued u/s. 17-A, the same was published in newspapers - Appellant passed draft award and possession of land was taken over by appellant/Housing Board - Respondent filed Writ Petition before HC challenging award - HC directed respondents to file their objections afresh - On appeal, DB affirmed the order of Single Judge - Hence, Instant appeal - Whether appellant fully complied with the mandates and procedures provided in s. 4 (1) (a), (b) and (c), Act 1990 - Held, Appellant was failed to comply with procedures u/s. 4 (1) (c) of 1990 Act i.e. notification to be published in two daily newspapers, one should be in regional language - In instant case, notification was published in Himalayan Mail and in Greater Kashmir but none of them was a newspaper of regional language - Further, appellant failed to publish corrigendum in any newspaper - There was no option left with anyone to give up or waive any of mode and all such modes to be strictly resorted to - Hence, order of Single Judge and DB upheld - Appeal dismissed.
