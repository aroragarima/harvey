Pt. Rishikesh and Another v Salma Begum Smt

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K. Venkataswami, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 920; (1995) 4 SCC 718; AIR 1995 SCW 2476; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 6; 1995 ALJ 1336; 1995 (26) ALR 78; JT 1995 (4) SC 401; 1996 (2) RCJ 272; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 505; 1995 (2) RentLR 19; 1995(3) SCALE 354; 1995 (2) SCJ 496; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 1062

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Amending Act, 1903

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 150, r. 1(b) and O. 15, rr. 597(1) and (3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 133 and 254(1) - U. P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Whether r. 5 of O. 15 is inconsistent with Central Act and thereby became void under art. 254(1) of Constitution, 1950? - Held, repugnancy arises not with making of law as envisaged in cl. (1) of art. 254 but when Act was brought into operation since State Act came into force with effect from 1-2-1977 while Central Act became operative from 1-2-1977, U. P. Act is an earlier enactment to Central Act - Parliament evinced intention to bring about an exhaustive amendment to CPC, 1908, and is applicable to all States State amendments, being inconsistent with Central Act, became void - There is no inconsistency in operation of r. 5 of O. 15 since same is not occupied by Central Act or Act 5 of 1908 and that, therefore, it did not become void - Appeal dismissed.
