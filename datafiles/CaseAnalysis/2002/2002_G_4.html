G. K. Bhatnagar (Dead) By Lrs v Abdul Alim

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2039; (2002) 9 SCC 516; 2002 (6) ALD(SC) 60; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 155

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e) and 39(2) - Sub-letting - Partnership - Whether tenant liable to be evicted on grounds of subletting premises or entering into partnership? - A conjoint reading of provisions shows that on and after 9-6-1952, sub-letting, assigning or otherwise parting with possession of whole or any part of tenancy premises, without obtaining consent in writing of landlord, is not permitted and if done, same provides a ground for eviction of tenant by landlord - However, inducting a partner in his business or profession by tenant is permitted so long as such partnership is genuine - If purpose of such partnership may ostensibly be to carry on business or profession in partnership, but real purpose be sub-letting of premises to such other person who is inducted ostensibly as a partner, then same shall be deemed to be an act of sub-letting attracting applicability of s. 14(1)(b) of Act - In present case, partnership is evidenced by a written deed - Whether High Court justified in interfering order of Courts below? - Held, it is true that an appeal u/s. 39(2) before HC lies only on substantial question of law - However, Appellate Authority in this case reversed well-considered and well-reasoned judgment of Rent Controller by resorting to conjectures and surmises - A substantial question of law, therefore, arose before HC justifying interference in second appeal with judgment of reversal recorded by Appellate Authority - In addition, case involved interpretation of partnership deed and general power of attorney so as to see whether on a totality of interpretation of recitals contained therein, read in light of other facts and circumstances, a case of sub-letting disguised as partnership was made out and needless to say, such interpretation of deeds is a question of law - Appeal dismissed.
