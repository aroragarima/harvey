State of Uttar Pradesh v Iftikhar Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A. Alagiriswami, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 506; (1973) SCC (Cr) 384; (1973) 1 SCC 512; 1973 ACC 366; AIR 1973 SC 863; 1973 CRLJ 636; 1973 CrLR(SC) 196; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure 1898, ss. 410, 374 and 286 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art.136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Appeal against acquittal - Reference is made by Sessions Judge for confirmation of death sentence - High Court is duty bound to independently consider matter carefully and examine all relevant and material circumstances - Held, merely because witnesses are partisan, their evidence cannot be rejected - In order to invoke s. 34 against accused, prior concert or a pre-arranged plan has to be established - Not necessary for accused to have committed any overt act - It is enough if criminal act has been done by one accused in furtherance of common intention.
