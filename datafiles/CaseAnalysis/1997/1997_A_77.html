Punjab National Bank and Others v All India New Bank of India Employees' Federation and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2672; (1997) 10 SCC 627; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1729; AIR 1997 SC 1086; 1997 ALJ 668; 1997 (2) CLT 508; JT 1997 (2) SC 432; 1997 (1) LLJ 863; 1998 (4) LLN 70; 1997(2) SCALE 89; 1997 (1) SCJ 437; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1126; 1997 (1) SLR 806; 1997 (2) Supreme 134

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Amalgamation of companies

Summary: Banking & Finance - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1980, s. 9 - New Bank of India (Amalgamation and Transfer of Undertakings) Scheme, 1993 - New Bank of India Scheme - Officers and other employees of New Bank of India become part and parcel of Punjab National Bank right from date of commencement of scheme - (A) Whether framing of placement scheme condition precedent for deploying or transferring any employee of NBI? - (B) Whether framing guidelines and then effecting transfers in accordance therewith inconsistent with or contrary to statutory amalgamation and placement schemes? - (C) Whether transfers contrary to any award/Settlement/agreement in force? - Held, (A) framing of placement scheme not a condition precedent for deploying or transferring any employee of NBI - (B) cannot be said to be inconsistent with or contrary to statutory amalgamation and placement schemes - (C) object of transfers were to avoid any retrenchment or adverse effect on their terms and conditions of service and not contrary to any award/Settlement/agreement in force - Appeal allowed - Appeal allowed.
