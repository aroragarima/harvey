L. V. Jadhav v Shankarrao Abasaheb Pawar and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 133; (1983) 4 SCC 231; (1983) SCC (Cr) 813; AIR 1983 SC 1219; 1983 (2) Crimes 470; 1983 CRLJ 1501; 1983 CrLR(SC) 463; 1983 (2) DMC 242; 1983(2) SCALE 175; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Respectable

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Complaint filed before Judicial Magistrate, First Class alleging commission of offence u/s. 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act, on obtaining necessary sanction as required - On verification of complaint, processes issued and summons sent to United States - Magistrate's order issuing summons challenged before High Court - HC quashed proceedings - Held, offence under s. 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act, is complete when demand for dowry is made - Consent for meeting demand is not necessary - Inherent powers of court under s. 482, CrPC should be used sparingly and with circumspection when there is reason to believe that process of law is being misused to harass a citizen - HC should have refrained from exercising nherent powers to quash proceedings when complaint was filed after obtaining previous sanction of State Govt. as required under s. 4 of Dowry Prohibition Act.
