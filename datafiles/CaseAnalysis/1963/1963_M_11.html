Bank of Bihar Limited v Mahabir Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 393; AIR 1964 SC 377; 1964 BLJR 1; [1963] 33 Comp Cas 783; 1964 (43) ILR(Pat) 356; 1964 (2) SCJ 611; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Documents Of Title

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 85 - Money not having passed into actual custody of drawer or that of custody of a person who was a servant or agent of drawer, cannot be held liable - For, vicarious liability may, in appropriate cases, rest on master with respect to his servant's acts, but it cannot possibly rest on a stranger with respect to criminal acts of a servant of another - If servant was not acting within scope of his authority, master would not be liable and it is person who did particular act, that is servant, would alone be liable - To take aid of s. 85, Bank required to establish that payment had in fact been made to drawer or to a person on behalf of drawer - Payment to a stranger to drawer or agent of Bank, not payment to drawer - Held, drawer of cheque to whom no part of money was paid by Bank cannot be held liable to make it good to Bank - Further held, statement recorded in judgment of a court, ordinarily not open to challenge unless both parties agree to it or court itself admits that statement is erroneous - Appeal dismissed.
