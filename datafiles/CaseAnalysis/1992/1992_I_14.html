A. A. Haja Muniuddin v Indian Railways

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 745; (1992) 4 SCC 736; 1993 (1) ACJ 235; AIR 1993 SC 361; 1992 (3) CCC 730; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 642; 1993 (1) MLJ(SC) 38; 1992(3) SCALE 214; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 72; 1993 (1) UJ 196

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Railway Claims Tribunal (Procedure) rules 1989

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 33 - Railway Claims Tribunal Act, 1987, ss. 16(2), 13, 15, 16, 18 and 24 - Railway Claims Tribunal Rules, 1989, rr. 6 and 44 - Whether an indigent person who is not possessed of sufficient means to enable him to pay fee prescribed by s. 16(2) of Railway Claims Tribunal Act, 1987 can be permitted to prefer his claim as an indigent person on principle embodied in O. 33 of CPC, 1908 ? - Held, yes - Access to justice cannot be denied to an individual merely because he does not have means to pay prescribed fee - Such a view would leave indigent persons without a remedy - It is, therefore, essential that provisions of Act and Rules must be broadly interpreted to ensure access to justice - If a claimant is left without redress even if he has a valid claim against railway administration merely because he is an indigent person, it would be a sad day and poor will lose confidence in system - No one can be heard to say, much less railway administration, that even though claimant has a genuine claim for compensation against railway administration, he must forgo same if he does not have means to pay requisite fee - Such an interpretation which denies justice must be avoided - Appeal allowed.
