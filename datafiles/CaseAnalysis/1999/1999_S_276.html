Supreme Court of India

10 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.S. Anand, Sujata V. Manohar, K. Venkataswami, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1424; (1999) 9 SCC 620; AIR 1999 SC 3125; AIR 1999 SCW 3074; JT 1999 (5) SC 422; 1999(4) SCALE 516; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Edible Oil, Sugarcane Control Order, Rice-Milling Industry (Regulation) Act, 1958, Commercial Crops, Tea Act, 1953, Prohibition Act, Vegetable Oil

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Prohibition Act - Sugarcane Control Order - Bihar Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1960 - (A) Whether imposition of market fee under the Market Act, 1960 by the respective market committees is justified in the absence of any service rendered to the appellant sugar mills under the provisions of the Market Act, 1960? - The entire machinery of the Market Act, 1960 cannot apply to the transactions of purchase of sugarcane by the appellant sugar factories as they are fully covered by the special provisions of the Sugarcane Act - Held, all future transactions of purchase of sugarcane by the sugar factories concerned in the market areas as well as the sale of manufactured sugar and molasses produced therefrom by utilising this purchased sugarcane by these factories will not be subjected to the levy of market fee u/s. 27 of the Market Act, 1960 by the Market Committees concerned - (B) Whether even though wheat is an agricultural produce, atta, maida, suji can be treated as agricultural produce and brought under the general sweep of the Market Act? -
