Lal Chand v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1168; (2009) 15 SCC 769; AIR 2010 SC 170; 2009 (6) ALD(SC) 66; 2009 (163) DLT 299; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 172; 2009(11) SCALE 627; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Value Of Agricultural Land

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land acquisition - Determination of market value - Proof of - Sale transactions or awards relating to neighboring village would not be relied on when acceptable evidence by way of contemporaneous sale transactions or awards available.P /&gt;(B) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Determination of - Compensation awarded - Award of Reference Court challenged - HC relied upon allotment rate of Delhi Development Authority (DDA) shown in its Brochure - 60% deducted from freehold market value towards development cost - Appeal filed before SC - Matter remanded back to HC for fresh determination - Determination of value by relying upon 2 sale deeds by HC - Legality - Held, market value of adjoining undeveloped freehold lands cannot be determined by relying upon allotment rates/auction rates in regard to plots formed by DDA in developed layout, hence DDA Brochure price being irrelevant - No record showing that circle rates having been determined by any statutorily appointed committee by adopting scientific basis with regard to land in question - Award in land acquisition effected in 1961 would not be of any use in determining market value for present acquisition - HC cannot reject the sale deeds adduced by appellants as not bona fide Therefore, compensation increased on the basis of sale deeds produced by appellants - Appeals partly allowed.
