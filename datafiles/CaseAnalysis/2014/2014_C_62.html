P. Krishna Murthy v Commissioner of Sericulture Andhra Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 545; (2014) 12 SCC 549; AIR 2014 SC 3774; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 37; 2015(2) ALL MR 484; 2014 (142) FLR 813; JT 2014 (8) SC 579; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 907; 2015 (1) SLR 510

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Denied, Withdrawal notice of voluntary retirement

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Revised Pension Rules, 1980, r. 43(1) - Voluntary retirement - Withdrawal notice of voluntary retirement - Denied - Justifiability - Appellant (employee) was working as Superintendent in respondent (State Dept.) - On the allegation against appellant that he neglected in discharging his duties, a disciplinary proceeding was initiated and a charge memo was issued - Appellant requested to permit him to retire from service and accordingly appellant was permitted to retire and an order to that effect was issued by Commissioner, dt.4-3-2004 - Thereafter, respondent requested to revoked the order dt.4-3-2004, which was rejected and Commissioner imposed punishment of 25% cut in pension - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before State Govt., which was allowed and thereafter, appellant filed another application seeking issuance of appropriate order for his reinstatement, which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed an applications before Tribunal - Tribunal and passed a common order setting aside order dt.4-3-2004 issued by Commissioner, permitting appellant to retire from service and directed respondent to allow appellant to continue in service till he attains the age of superannuation - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petitions before HC - DB by impugned order allowed petitions and set aside order passed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that there could not be unilateral termination of service and employee was at liberty to withdraw his notice of voluntary retirement any time before 1-5-2004, and order of rejection passed by Commissioner, was clearly contrary to the principle laid down in Balram Gupta v. Union of India and another, 1987 Indalw SC 28862 and that merely because of having accepted the retirement benefit, appellant was not stopped from challenging the non-acceptance of his withdrawal notice of voluntary retirement -

Held, appellant's application requesting the authorities to permit him to retire from service with effect from 1-5-2004 was allowed by order dt.4-3-2004 and he was permitted to retire from service with effect from 1-5-2004 - Before the expiry of the said date, appellant submitted another application dt.15-4-2004 requesting the authorities to permit him to continue in service and to revoke the order issued on 4-3-2004 - Said application was considered by the authority concerned and finally rejected on 28-4-2004 - Curiously enough the said order dt.28-4-2004 was not challenged by the appellant before any authority or any forum - After about 3 months, disciplinary proceedings was concluded and on 29-7-2004, authority passed an order imposing punishment of 25% cut in pension amount of appellant on the ground of gross negligence in discharging duties - Said order was, however, challenged by filing an appeal before State Govt. - In the said memo of appeal, appellant had not whispered or challenged the legality and validity of the order dt.28-4-2004 rejecting the request of appellant for withdrawal of order of voluntary retirement - In said memo of appeal, appellant only assailed the order of penalty of 25% cut in pension on various reasons including his wife's ailment and his performance in service - Request for revocation of withdrawal of voluntary retirement was finally rejected, appellant instead of challenging the said order filed several representations for release of the pensionary benefits - Said representations were considered and the eligible pensionary benefits were drawn and paid to the appellant in 2004 and 2005 - Taking advantage of leniency shown by Govt. in the order passed in appeal on 3-4-2004, appellant took a chance to move an application after two 2 i.e., on 27-4-2006 requesting the authorities for reinstatement in service - HC in impugned order rightly came to the conclusion that appellant preferred appeal before State Govt. against impugned order of cut in pension as a retired employee and he himself stated that he has submitted the pension proposals for fixation of pension - Having regard to fact that appellant did not assail the order rejecting his application for revocation of pension at any time rather he proceeded and assailed only the order of 25% cut in pension -Appellant could not be allowed to proceed further, that too after expiry of 2 years seeking reinstatement in service taking the benefit of the order passed by State Govt. - Appeals dismissed.
