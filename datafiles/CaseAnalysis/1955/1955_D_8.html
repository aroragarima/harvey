Â Smt. Vidya Verma and Another v Dr. Shiv Narain Verma

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 38; AIR 1956 SC 108; 1956 ALJ 156; 1956 CRLJ 283; 1956 SCJ 121; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 983

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, o.35, rr.5 and 10 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 491 - An advocate presented a petition under art. 32 for a writ of habeas corpus on behalf of a lady and appeared as her next friend - Petition did not disclose that he made any attempt to consult the person who he said was the husband of lady nor did it show that he made any attempt to contact either lady or even her uncle - He had three hearings in Court despite the warning he was given about costs and Attorney - General was also asked to appear - When arguments were fully concluded and he found that Court was against him and he asked for permission to withdraw petition - (A) Whether violation of right to personal liberty by private individual attracts art. 32 of Constitution to seek remedy? - Violation of right to personal liberty by a private individual is not within purview of art. 21 of Constitution - Held, a person whose right to personal liberty is infringed by a private individual must seek his remedy under ordinary law and not u/art. 32 of the Constitution - (B) Whether petitioner is allowed to withdraw petition after being subjected to three hearings in court and announcement of cost supposed to be paid by him? - Held, petition for withdrawal dismissed and advocate directed to pay costs personally - Petition dismissed.
