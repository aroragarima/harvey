S. A. L. Narayana Row, Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City, and Another v Model Mills Nagpur Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 249; [1967] 64 ITR 67

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.35 - Excess Dividend - Additional Tax Levied - Refund Amount of Tax - Challenged - Respondent Company had dividended of Rs.2,66,788 in previous year - Appellant/ Income Tax Officer levied additional tax and passed order on excess dividend declared by respondent-company - Respondent- company approached Income- Tax Officer (ITO) for refund of tax paid which was rejected - Respondent-company filed application before Commissioner of Income Tax to revise order passed by ITO, rejected application - Aggrieved respondent-company filed writ petition before HC which allowed application - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was justifiable - Held, HC rejected contention principally on ground that income-tax authorities had themselves treated application as one u/s. 35 of the Act and had rejected same on merits - As already pointed out, application to Income-tax Officer was one in which request for rectification of order was implicit and Commissioner in dealing with application for refund treated that application in that light - Hence, directed appellant/Commissioner to refund amount of tax which was illegally collected - Appeal dismissed.
