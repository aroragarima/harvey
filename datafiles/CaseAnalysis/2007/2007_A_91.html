Amrit Agro Industries Limited And Another v Commissioner Of Central Excise, Ghaziabad

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 486; (2007) 15 SCC 148; AIR 2007 SC 1523; 2007 (210) E.L.T. 183; JT 2007 (4) SC 584; 2007(5) SCALE 86; 2007 (5) SCJ 736; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Appeals allowed., Sale Price, Normal Price, Edible Preparation, Central Excises and Salt Act, 1994

Summary: Excise - CH.s 21.08, 20.01, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - 'Roasted peanuts' and 'moongfali masala mazedar' - Appellant claimed that the items fell under c.H. 21.08 as namkeen, so were exempted vide notification No. 4/97-C.E. - Department held that the impugned goods fell under Heading 20.01 as the essential character of moongfali is not lost even when it is salted and fried - Held, roasted peanut falls under Chapter 20 and not under Chapter 21 as roasting is not one of the enumerated processes in Chapter Note No. 1 to Chapter 20 - During preparation of moongfali masala mazedar, the capacity to germinate is obliterated - Falls under C.H. 21.01, and therefore, the assessee is entitled to exemption; (2) Limitation - Show cause notice issued on 5.5.1998 - Revised declaration filed by the assessee on 19.11.1997 - Held, show cause notice within six months - Question of extended period does not arise; (3) Duty - Section 4(4)(d)(ii), Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944 - Recomputation - Cum-duty price - Assessee had cleared impugned goods without payment of duty on the footing that roasted peanuts were exempted - Held, one cannot go by general implication that the wholesale price would always mean cum-duty price, particularly when the assessee had cleared the goods during the relevant years on the basis of the exemption notification - Matter remitted to the adjudicating authority for recomputation of duty.
