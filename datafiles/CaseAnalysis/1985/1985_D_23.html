Mohd. Azeem v District Judge, and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 422; (1985) 2 SCC 550; AIR 1985 SC 1118; 1985 (11) ALR 358; 1985(1) SCALE 1097; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Deemed Vacancy

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 12 - One Manzoor Hussain was admittedly the tenant of a premises located at Aligarh - On his death, the appellant that being the eldest member of the family, was paying that rent - Rent Control Inspector submitted a report that Naim, appellant's brother, had built a house four or five years ago, therefore, the tenancy must be deemed to have terminated in view of the provisions contained in s. 12 of the Act and a notice was issued accordingly - Revisional authority placed reliance on a Full Bench decision of the Allahabad HC in Rama Devi's case - It was held, where one of the co-tenants builds or otherwise acquires another residential building within the meaning of s. 12 (3), the tenant, namely, the entire set of co-tenants shall be deemed to have ceased to occupy the building under his sub-tenant - It cannot be that the share belonging to the co-tenant in default alone shall fall vacant - Whether the interpretation put on s. 12(3) was erroneous? - Held, as the definition of 'tenant' indicates, on a tenant's death his heirs as normally resided with him would also be tenant qua residential buildings - Therefore, the widow, all the three sons and unmarried daughter became tenants when the Act came into force - Naim's building a house and moving into it cannot wipe out the interest of the widow, other two sons, including the appellant and the daughter, if in their own right they were tenants by satisfying the requirements of the definition - If everyone's interest was to be wiped out,s. 3 had to provide differently and instead of 'he shall be deemed to have ceased to occupy the building under his tenancy' as occurring in sub-s. (3) reference would have been made to all the tenants - Decision in Rama Devi's case is set aside - Appeal allowed.
