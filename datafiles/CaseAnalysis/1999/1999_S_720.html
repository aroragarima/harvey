R. Rathinavel Chettiar and Another v V. Sivaraman and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1049; (1999) 4 SCC 89; 1999 (36) ALR 9; 1999 (2) AWC 1531; JT 1999 (2) SC 468; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 47; 1999 (1) SCJ 646; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r. 10, O. 23, rr. 1 and 1-A - Appeal against High Court judgment allowing application of respondent No. 1 for dismissing suit as not pressed and recording compromise arrived at between appellant and defendant - The suit has already been decreed or dismissed and a decree has been passed determining rights of parties to suit - Can such decree be destroyed by making an application for dismissing suit as not pressed or unconditionally withdrawing suit at appellate stage? - Held, since withdrawal of suit at appellate stage, if allowed, would have effect of destroying or nullifying decree affecting thereby rights of parties which came to be vested under decree, it cannot be allowed as a matter of course but has to be allowed rarely only when a strong case is made out - Proceedings either in appeal or in revision have to be allowed to have a full trial on merits - Where a decree passed by trial court is challenged in appeal, it would not be open to plaintiff, at that stage, to withdraw suit so as to destroy that decree - Rights which have come to be vested in parties to suit under decree cannot be taken away by withdrawal of suit at that stage unless very strong reasons are shown that withdrawal would not affect or prejudice anybody's vested rights - HC also committed an error in not considering impact of r. 1-A which was inserted in O. 23 by CPC (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Impugned judgment passed by HC, is set aside - Application for withdrawal of suit rejected - Matter remitted back to HC.
