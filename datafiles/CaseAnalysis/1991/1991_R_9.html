R.S. Raghunath v State Of Karnataka And Anr

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Jayachandra Reddy, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 604; (1992) SCC (L&S) 286; (1992) 1 SCC 335; AIR 1992 SC 81; 1992 (19) ATC 507; JT 1991 (4) SC 82; 1991 (2) LLN 837; 1991(2) SCALE 808; 1991 (5) SLR 630; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 387; 1992 (1) UJ 140

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Karnataka General Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1976, r. 1 - Karnataka Civil Services (General Recruitment) Rules, 1977, rr. 1(3)(a), 3(2) and 4 - Constitution of India, 1953 art. 309 - Repeal - Promotion by selection - Provision as to in 1976 - Special Rules - Not affected by later General Rules 1977 - Special Rules not abrogated by non obstante cl in r. 3(2) - No patent inconsistency between General and Special Rules - No express repeal of Special Rules - Whether repeal can be inferred - Held, repeal by implication cannot be inferred - Appeal dismissed.
