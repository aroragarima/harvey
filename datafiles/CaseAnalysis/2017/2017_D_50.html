Voestalpine Schienen GmbH v Delhi Metro Rail Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 385; (2017) 4 SCC 665; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 394; 2017 (3) MLJ 153; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 815; 2017(2) SCALE 335

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitral Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Arbitration Clause, Conciliation, Uncitral, English Arbitration Act, 1996, Arbitration and Conciliation (Amendment) Act, 2015

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 11(6), 11(8), 12, 12(5) - Disputes arose - Appointment of arbitrator - Applicability - Petitioner/Company claims to be a European market leader was engaged in the business of steel production - Respondent awarded contract to petitioner for supply of rails - However, certain disputes arose between parties with regard to contract - Respondent suspended business dealings with petitioner for a period of 6 months - Petitioner filed instant petition u/s. 11(6) r/w s. 11(8) of the Act for appointment of sole arbitrator/arbitral Tribunal under cl. 9.2 of General Conditions of Contract (GCC) r/w cl. 9.2 of Special Conditions of Contract (SCC).

Held, it cannot be said that simply because the person was retired officer who retired from govt. or other statutory corporation or public sector undertaking and had no connection with respondent, he would be treated as ineligible to act as an arbitrator. Bias or even real likelihood of bias cannot be attributed to such highly qualified and experienced persons, simply on ground that they served Central Govt. or PSUs, even when they had no connection with respondent. The very reason for empaneling those persons was to ensure that technical aspects of dispute were suitably resolved by utilising their expertise when they act as arbitrators. Respondent forwarded the list of all 31 persons on its panel thereby giving a very wide choice to petitioner to nominate its arbitrator. They were not the employees or ex-employees or in any way related to respondent. However, persons who were picked up as arbitrators would have to disclose their interest in terms of amended provisions of s. 12 of the Act. Therefore, SC do not find it to be a fit case for exercising jurisdiction to appoint and constitute arbitral tribunal. Further, sub-clauses (b) & (c) of cl. 9.2 of SCC need to be deleted and instead choice should be given to the parties to nominate any person from the entire panel of arbitrators. Some comments are also needed on the cl. 9.2(a) of GCC/SCC, as per which respondent prepares the panel of 'serving or retired engineers of govt. depts. or public sector undertakings'. In instant case, though choice was given by respondent to opposite party but it was limited to choose an arbitrator from the panel prepared by respondent. It becomes imperative to have a much broad based panel, so that there was no misapprehension that principle of impartiality and independence would be discarded at any stage of the proceedings, specially at the stage of constitution of arbitral tribunal. SC direct that respondent should prepare a broad based panel on aforesaid lines within 2 months. Petition dismissed.
