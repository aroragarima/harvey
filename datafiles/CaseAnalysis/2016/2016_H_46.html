Hemant Madhusudan Nerurkar v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 324; (2016) 6 SCC 504; AIR 2016 SC 2219; 2016 CRLJ 2679; 2016 (149) FLR 930; JT 2016 (5) SC 31; 2016 (3) LLJ 309; 2016(4) SCALE 643; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Welfare, Contract Labour, Contract, Factories Act, 1948, Breach, Labour, Occupier, Bihar Factories Rules, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Factories Act, 1948, s. 92 - Bihar Factories Rules, 1950 - Non-compliance of provision under Act - Liability of occupier - Determination - It is stated that inspection was conducted in factory of appellant where it was found that there were certain deficiency in respect to compliance of provisions under Rules - Hence, cognizance was taken against Occupier and Manager of factory - Further, appellants vide affidavit stated that defects with respect to contract labour are rectified - HC vide impugned order set aside petition of appellant for quashing criminal proceeding against him - Whether in view of rectification of defects, appellants could be punished u/s. 92 of Act.

Held, J.K. Industries Limited and Others v Chief Inspector of Factories and Boilers and Others 1996 Indlaw SC 2480, relied on, where is was held that s. 92 of Act contemplates joint liability of occupier and manager for any offence committed irrespective, of fact as to who is directly responsible for offence. Further, offences under Act are not a part of general penal law but arise from breach of duty provided in a special beneficial social defence legislation, which creates absolute or strict liability without proof of any mens rea. Hence, SC held that taking into consideration allegations made against appellants and in terms of mandate of s. 92 of Act, ends of justice would be met, if penalty of Rs.50,000/- each is imposed on appellants. Impugned order passed by HC is confirmed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - It is not justified to quash criminal proceeding against occupier of factory when allegation against non-compliance of Rules is proved.
