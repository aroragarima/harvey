Vishesh Kumar v Shanti Prasad

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 322; (1980) 2 SCC 378; AIR 1980 SC 892; 1980 ALJ 411; 1980 (6) ALR 233; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC,1908 s. 115 - Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887, s. 25 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Constitution, art. 227 - Maintainability of revision in High Court, against revisional Order of District Court u/s. 115 of the Code - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Proviso - Construction to defeat the basic intent - Permissibility - (C) Revisional order of District Court u/s. 25 of 1887 Act - Whether can be revised u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 -

(D) Whether petition u/s.115 can be converted into one u/art. 227 of the Constitution -

Held, revision against order u/s.115 in High Court, not maintainable - A proviso cannot be premitted by construction to defeat the basic intent expressed in the substantive provision - Order not open to revision u/s.115 - Cannot be converted - Appeal Dismissed.
