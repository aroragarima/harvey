Â Girdharilal Bansidhar v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 415; AIR 1964 SC 1519; 1964 CRLJ 461; 1984 ECR 491; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167(8) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) Whether a component part which had no use other than as a component of an article whose importation was prohibited is included in a ban or restriction as regards importation of that article ? - Held, it was not intention of rule that importers were permitted to do indirectly what they were forbidden to do directly, and that it permitted importation separately of components which had no use other than as components of an article whose importation was prohibited, and that an importer was thereby enable to assemble them here as a complete article though if they were assembled beyond Customs -(B) Whether High Court can look into correctness of decision of Customs Authorities under writ jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226?- Further held, court dealing with a petition u/art. 226 is not sitting in appeal over decision of Customs Authorities and therefore correctness of conclusion reached by those authorities on appreciation of several items in Hand book or in Indian Tariff Act which is referred to in these items, is not a matter which falls within writ jurisdiction of HC - Particularly where there is no complaint of any procedural irregularity of kind which would invalidate order - Appeal dismissed.
