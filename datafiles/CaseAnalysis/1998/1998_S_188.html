Sri Tarsem Singh v Sri Sukhminder Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 465; (1998) 3 SCC 471; AIR 1998 SC 1400; 1998 (2) AWC 1225; 1998 (1) CLT 165; JT 1998 (2) SC 149; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 54; 1998 (3) RAJ 172; 1998(2) SCALE 58; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 456; 1998 (3) SLT 383; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 110; 1998 (2) Supreme 275

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act , 1872, ss. 2(h), 20, 65, 73 and 74 - (A) Whether a contract is a bilateral transaction between parties, either written or unwritten? - Whether the remedies provided u/ss. 73 and 74, for the breach of contract is available in all contracts? - (B) Whether the restitution of advantage obtained under void agreement , applies even to cases where agreement is void from its inception? - Held, contract is a bilateral transaction - Available only when agreement breached is valid and binding and forfeiture clause in a void agreement cannot be enforced - Petition dismissed.
