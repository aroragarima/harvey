Union of India v Messrs V. Pundarikakshudu & Sons and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 732; (2003) 8 SCC 168; AIR 2003 SC 3209; 2003 (53) ALR 445; 2003 (52) ARBLR 168; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 175; 2003 (3) RAJ 271; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 356B; 2003(7) SCALE 323; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 506; 2003 (6) Supreme 898

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - ss.15, 16, 30, 32 - Award - Contract for construction of an auditorium complex - Time for completion of contract extended owing to increase in scope of work - Contract terminated after due date - Whether District Judge and HC exceeded their jurisdiction in passing impugned judgments - Held, in this case District Judge as also HC of Madras clearly held that award cannot be sustained having regard to inherent inconsistency contained therein arbitrator committed a legal misconduct in arriving at an inconsistent finding as regard breach of contract on part of one party or other - Once arbitrator had granted damages to first respondent which could be granted only on a finding that appellant had committed breach of terms of contract and, thus, was responsible therefor, any finding contrary thereto and inconsistent therewith while awarding any sum in favour of appellant would be wholly unsustainable being self contradictory - Further held, Court upon setting aside whole award could have remitted back matter to arbitrator in terms of s 16 of Act or could have appointed another arbitrator, but at this juncture, no such order can be passed as award in part has become final.
