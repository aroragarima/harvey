Andhra Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation and another v Pampa Hotels Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 443; (2010) 5 SCC 425; AIR 2010 SC 1806; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 102; 2010(4) ALL MR 416; 2010 (81) ALR 266; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 178; [2010] 160 Comp Cas 1; 2010 (3) CompLJ 21; 2010 (5) CTC 706; JT 2010 (4) SC 343; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 740; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 296; 2010(4) SCALE 195; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 942

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Corporate - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Arbitration clause - Existence of - Respondent/Company entered into a lease agreement and a management agreement with the appellant/Corporation - Appellant terminated the agreement - Respondent filed a petition u/s. 11 of the Act before the Chief Justice of HC for appointment of an arbitrator - Appellant contended that the respondent was not incorporated as a company at the time of entering into the agreements and which was incorporated an year thereafter, therefore, arbitration clause in the agreement cannot be invoked - Chief Justice appointed an arbitrator referring all the disputes between the parties including the existence of the agreement - Where the party seeking arbitration is a company which was not in existence on the date of the signing of the contract containing the arbitration agreement, whether it can be said that there is an arbitration agreement between the parties - Held, if one of the two parties to the arbitration agreement was not in existence when the contract was made, then there was no contract and if there was no contract, there is no question of a clause in such contract being an arbitration agreement between the parties - Further, if the lease agreement and the management agreement were entered into by the promoters of the company stating that they are entering into the contract for the purpose of the company to be incorporated, in their capacity as promoters and that such contract is warranted by the terms of the incorporation of the company, the agreement would have been valid; and the term regarding arbitration therein could have been enforced - However, the impugned agreements entered into not by the promoters of respondent but the Managing Director on behalf of a company proposed to be incorporated by them which was not existed at the relevant time - Therefore, there is no arbitration agreement between the respondent and appellant against whom such agreement is sought to be enforced - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Existence of the arbitration agreement - Adjudication - Jurisdiction of the Chief Justice - Scope - Whether the question as to the existence or validity of the arbitration agreement, has to be decided by the Chief Justice/Designate when considering the petition u/s. 11 of the Act or by the Arbitrator - Held, issue of the existence of an agreement is ought to be decided by the Designate/Chief Justice and it cannot be left to the arbitrator - Chief Justice, by relying on the decisions of SC in Konkan Rly. Corpn. Ltd, v. Rani Construction (P) Ltd, 2002 INDLAW SC 60 and Sarwan Kumar v. Madan Lal Aggarwal, 2003 INDLAW SC 171, proceeded in the present case on the basis that while acting u/s. 11 of the Act, he was not acting under a judicial capacity but only under an administrative capacity - However, the said decisions were overruled by Seven Judge Bench of SC in SBP & Co. v. Patel Engineering Ltd., 2005 INDLAW SC 696, wherein it was held that the question whether there is an arbitration agreement and whether the party who has applied u/s. 11 of the Act, is a party to such an agreement, is an issue which is to be decided by the Chief Justice or his Designate under section 11 of the Act before appointing an arbitrator in his judicial capacity - Therefore, direction issued to the appointed arbitrator, without interfering his appointment, to decide the issue in regard to the existence/validity of the arbitration agreement as a preliminary issue relating to jurisdiction - Appeal disposed of .

(C) Practice & Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Judicial process - Seven Bench decision of SC in SBP & Co. v. Patel Engineering Ltd., 2005 INDLAW SC 696 - Prospective overruling - Effect - Held, no right of appeal subsist against the decision of Chief Justice u/s. 11 of the Act - Further, the seven Judge Bench in SBP's case issued the categorical direction that appointment of Arbitrators made till the date of the said judgment are to be treated as valid and all objections are to be left to be decided u/s. 16 of the Act - On account of the prospective overruling direction in SBP, any appointment of an arbitrator u/s. 11 of the Act made prior to the date of judgment (26-10-2005) has to be treated as valid and all objections including the existence or validity of the arbitration agreement, have to be decided by the arbitrator u/s. 16 of the Act - Legal position enunciated in the judgment in SBP would govern only the applications to be filed u/s. 11 and pending applications u/s. 11(6) of the Act on the date of the said judgment - Appeals disposed of.
