United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v Shila Datta & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 743; (2011) 10 SCC 509; 2011 (4) ACC 287; 2012 (2) ACC 303; 2011 ACJ 2729; AIR 2012 SC 86; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 129; 2012(1) ALL MR 411; 2011 (89) ALR 709; 2011 (6) AWC 6313; 2012 (2) JCC 849; 2011 (4) KLT 378; 2012 (2) MahLJ 444; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 743; 2012(1) M.P.H.T. 355; 2012 (1) MPLJ 526; 2011 (164) PLR 806; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 582; 2012 (2) RLW 1167; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 763; 2012 (1) WLN(SC) 51

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Joint Appeal

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 2, 149, 173 - Motor accident claim- Insurance policy - Compensation - Validity - A Two Judge Bench of Court made order of reference from which following issues were raised - (A) Whether insurer can contest a motor accident claim on merits, in particular, in regard to quantum, in addition to the grounds mentioned in section 149(2) of 1988 Act for avoiding liability under policy of insurance - Held, u/s. 149 (2) of Act, where an insurer was imp- leaded by claimants as a party, it could contest claim on all grounds, without any restrictions or limitations - But where an insurer was not imp leaded by claimant as a party, but was only issued a statutory noticee by Tribunal requiring it to meet liability, he was made a party to deny liability on grounds mentioned - (B) Whether an insurer can prefer an appeal under section 173 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, against an award of the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal, questioning the quantum of compensation awarded - Held, insurer could continue to context claim by filing an appeal as provided u/s. 173 of the Act - (C) Considerations raised were claimants implead insurer as a respondent in claim petition,

maintainability of joint appeal, extent of liability by insurers, validity of sec 170 of 1988 Act with respect to filing of appeal and contesting award on merits and defending of cliam by insurer -- Held, u/s 149(2) of 1988 Act, an insurer need not be impleaded as a party to claim proceedings and it should only be issued a statutory notice so that it can be made liable to pay compensation awarded by tribunal - Further, an appeal can be maintainable when both owner of vehicle and insurer files it, owner of vehicle does not cease to be a person aggrieved when insurer is co-appellant - Where a notice is issued under section 149(2) of Act, insurer as 'noticee' can not 'deny' its liability as an insurer on grounds other than those mentioned in section 149(2)(a) and (b) of 1988 Act, nothing prevents it as a person liable to pay the compensation - U/s 149(2), insurer cannot raise any kinds of contentions based on terms of policy to avoid contract of indemnity - But it does not require insurer to concede wrong claims or false claims or not challenge erroneous determination of compensation

- Matters were directed to be placed before Hon'ble Chief Justice for constituting a larger bench to consider points - Order accordingly.
