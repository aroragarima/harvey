Registrar General, Patna High Court v Pandey Gajendra Prasad & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 157; (2012) 6 SCC 357; AIR 2012 SC 2319; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 119; 2012(3) ALL MR 984; 2012 (5) ALT 27; 2012 (5) AWC 4492; 2012 (134) FLR 548; JT 2012 (5) SC 457; 2013 (5) KarLJ 223; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 142; 2012(5) SCALE 404; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 994; 2012 (4) SLR 743; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 20

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Scope of judicial review - Decision of Full Court - Assessment of conduct of judicial officer - Respondent No. 1 was appointed in Bihar Judicial Service and was functioning as a Railway Judicial Magistrate - On receipt of some reports, alleging misconduct on the part of the respondent No. 1, the District and Sessions Judge conducted a preliminary inquiry - Upon consideration of his report, the Standing Committee, consisting of five Judges of the HC, issued a show cause notice to respondent No. 1 - Dissatisfied with his reply, the Standing Committee recommended initiation of departmental proceedings against him and to place him under suspension - Standing Committee recommended to dismiss the respondent No. 1 from service - Recommendation of the Standing Committee was approved and ratified by the Full Court - Writ petition was filed by respondent No. 1 challenging the decision of the Full Court recommending his removal from service as a Railway Judicial Magistrate - DB set aside the notification/communication dismissing him from service - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB ought to have refrained from interfering with the order of punishment - Held, recommendation of the Standing Committee to dismiss the respondent No. 1 from service was based on the findings in the enquiry report submitted by the enquiry officer pursuant to the departmental enquiry; his reply to the show cause notice; his ACR and other materials placed before it - Recommendation of the Standing Committee was approved and ratified by the Full Court - There was nothing on record to suggest that the evaluation made, firstly by the Standing Committee and then by the Full Court, was so arbitrary, capricious or so irrational so as to shock the conscience of the DB to justify its interference with the unanimous opinion of the Full Court - DB exceeded its jurisdiction by interfering with the decision of the Full Court - Impugned judgment of the DB set aside - Appeal allowed.
