Savitri Devi v State of Uttar Pradesh and ohters

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, H.L. Dattu, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 520; (2015) 7 SCC 21; 2015 (111) ALR 736; 2015 (4) AWC 4207; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 179; JT 2015 (5) SC 102; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 424; 2015 (129) RD 71; 2015(6) SCALE 667; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquired, Urgency, Restoration, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4,6,5A,17(1), 17(4),17A, 18 - Acquisition of land - Invocation of urgency provisions - Justification - Notifications dt. 12-3-2008 was issued by respondent State u/s. 4 of and declaration was issued u/s. 6 of Act vide notification dt. 30-6-2008 for acquisition of land in question for Industrial Development - Further, urgency provisions u/s. 17(1) and 17(4) of Act were invoked thereby dispensing with right of objection otherwise given to land holders u/s. 5A of Act - HC held that invocation of s. 17 of Act was justified and dismissed petition - However, DB reversed order passed by lower Court and quashed notification - Further, matter was placed before large bench and it was accepted that invocation of emergency clause contained in s. 17 of Act was impermissible and unwarranted, however, petitions were dismissed by impugned judgment on ground that they are filed with inordinate delay and laches - Further, notification was quashed in respect of three villages where no development work had taken place and in respect of other villages, instead of quashing action of acquisition of land in spite of accepting plea that s. 17 was wrongly invoked, it has enhanced compensation as well as extent of entitlement for allotment of developed Abadi plot - Whether impugned order passed by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, appellants did not challenge acquisition per se inasmuch as when land was acquired even after invoking urgency provisions contained in s. 17 of Act and dispensing with requirement of s. 5A of Act, this position was accepted by land owners. They even allowed authorities to proceed further in passing award and taking possession from many of these land owners and even paying compensation to them. Further, before coming SC and filing writ petitions, most of these appellants had received compensation. They also sought reference u/s. 18 of Act for higher compensation. Physical possession of land of many of these appellants have also been taken. Hence, SC held that HC considered ground realities of matter and arrived at more practical and workable solution by adequately compensating land owners in form of compensation as well as allotment of developed Abadi land at higher rate i.e. 10% of land acquired of each of land owners against eligibility and to policy to extent of 5% and 6% of Noida and Greater Noida land respectively. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is affirmed. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - Acquisition proceedings cannot be challenged after inordinate delay after compensation is accepted by landowners and physical possession is taken.
