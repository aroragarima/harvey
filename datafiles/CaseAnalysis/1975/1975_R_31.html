Rikhi Ram and Another v Ram Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 548; (1975) 2 SCC 318; AIR 1975 SC 1869; 1975 UJ 546

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913 - Pre-emption - Tenancy - Respondent no. 3-land owner sold land to respondent nos. 1 and 2, vendees, and appellants who were tenants of disputed land, filed pre-emption suit against respondents in respect of suit land - Trial Court decreed suit in respect of portion of land and aggrieved, vendees, approached first appellate court and later filed second appeal before HC which allowed the same and dismissed appellants suit - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether appellants could claim pre-emption right over suit land.

Held, appellants did not obtain an order of injunction in their suit to restrain the respondents from proceeding with their application for eviction. The order of injunction was confined to a restrain on dispossession. Appellants did not continue to be tenants of any portion of the land sold to respondent nos. 1 and 2 on the date the decree for pre-emption was passed in their favour. Hence, decree was not sustainable in respect of any portion of the land. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - The relationship of the landlord and the tenant ceases to exist between the parties after the passing of an order of ejectment against the tenant. Further, dispossession in execution of the order is not necessary for determination of the tenancy.
