Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carrier & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Rash driving - Accident - Compensation - Enhancement of - Appellant was hit by bus belonging to respondent no. 2 and sustained grievous injuries - In petition filed by appellant u/s 166 of the Act, he claimed compensation of Rs.5,00,000/- with interest - Tribunal held that accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving of bus owned by respondent no. 2, hence, appellant was entitled to compensation of Rs.45,000/- with interest at rate of 8% - On appeal, DB held that appellant had suffered 26% disability in right lower limb, 25% urethral injury and 38% disability to whole body but granted meager enhancement of Rs.31,000/- and declared that appellant was entitled to total compensation of Rs.76,000/- with interest at rate of 6% - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that he was working as Carpenter and after the accident he was not in position to work as carpenter - Whether compensation awarded to appellant was just or he was entitled to enhanced compensation - Held, under first head i.e, loss of earning and other gains during the period of hospitalisation (one month), appellant was entitled to compensation of Rs.5,000/- - It was also not in dispute that appellant suffered 26% disability of right lower limb, 25% disability due to urethral injury and 38% disability to whole body - Although, percentage of disability of whole body was 38%, evidence produced by appellant in form of own affidavit and affidavit of PW-2 showed that he would not be able to work as carpenter or do any manual work throughout his life - Hence, appellant was entitled to compensation of Rs.3,32,640/- for loss of future earning - Issue regarding expenses for future treatment was decided by awarding Rs.1,50,000/- under that head - For pain, suffering and trauma caused due to accident, sum of Rs.1,50,000/- was awarded to appellant - Likewise, for loss of amenities including loss of prospects of marriage Rs.2 lacs was awarded - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
