M. Sudakar v V. Manoharan And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1093; (2011) 1 SCC 484; JT 2011 (1) SC 441; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 630; 2010(13) SCALE 195

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: (A) Trusts & Associations - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Petitioner, president of the Trust, was debarred from holding any office of the Trust for a period of 10 years by a resolution of the General Body of Trust because of the charges of financial impropriety - Writ petition was filed thereagainst inter alia, praying to restrain the respondent from registering the same - Meanwhile, the resolution was registered - Accordingly, Single Judge was of the view that the said prayer has become infructuous but it proceeded to mould the relief sought for by the petitioner and held that the resolution was illegal and that there was no provision in the bye-laws of the Trust to debar any person from holding any post - On appeal, DB of HC set aside the order of the Single Judge on its finding that the petitioner had approached the HC belatedly - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Single Judge was correct in granting relief to the petitioner when he already observed that the writ petition had become infructuous? - Held, a writ petition broadly speaking is held infructuous when the relief sought for by the petitioner is already granted or because of certain events, there may not be necessity to decide the issue involved in the writ petition - In the present case, the resolution of the Governing Body was still in force when the writ petition was heard and in fact was to operate for a further period, hence it cannot be said that the relief claimed by the petitioner had become infructuous - Hence, as the effect of the order continued, the learned Single Judge was right in moulding the relief - Action being patently illegal, the Single Judge could not have declined the relief taking into account the alleged action - (B) Whether DB of the HC erred in setting aside the order of the Single Judge? - Held, bye-laws which governs the Trust do not provide for debarring any member of the Trust from holding a post for specified period - Bye-laws provide for removal of the membership in accordance with the procedure prescribed therein in case a member is found to be indulging in activities prejudicial to the Trust - Power to remove the member shall not include power to debar the member from holding an office of the Trust - In the present case, as the resolution of the Governing Body debarring the petitioner from holding the office of the Trust was valid and operative when the matter was pending before the Single Judge, he did not err in quashing the resolution - Impugned order of the DB set aside and that of the Single Judge restored - Appeal allowed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Power of Court to mould relief - Held, power to mould relief is always available to the Court possessed with the power to issue high prerogative writs - In order to do complete justice it can mould the relief, depending upon the facts and circumstances of the case - In the facts of a given case a writ petitioner may not be entitled to the specific relief claimed by him but this itself will not preclude the Writ Court to grant such other relief which he is otherwise entitled - Further delay and latches does not bar the jurisdiction of the Court - It is a matter of discretion and not of jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
