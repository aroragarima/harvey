Hero Cycles Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Ludhiana

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 799; [2015] 379 ITR 347; JT 2015 (12) SC 213; 2015(12) SCALE 627; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 55; [2016] 236 TAXMAN 447

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Interest on borrowings from Bank - Exemption - Assessee claimed deduction of interest paid on borrowed sums from Bank u/s. 36(1)(iii) of the Act but AO re-calculated figures and disallowed claim to extent of Rs. 16,39,010 against claim of Rs. 20,53,120 on ground that firstly, deduction was claimed as business expenditure but substantial money out of the loans taken from the Bank was diverted by giving advance to Hero Fibres Limited on which no interest was charged by assessee and so, money borrowed on which interest was paid was not for business purposes and no deduction could be allowed - Secondly, charging of interest at rate of 10% on advances given to Directors and paying interest at much more rate on money borrowed from bank by assessee could not be treated for purpose of business of assessee - On appeal, appellate authority set aside order of AO holding that interest paid by assessee was for business purposes and hence, entire interest paid by assessee should have been allowed as business expenditure - Aggrieved, revenue filed appeal before Tribunal but the same was dismissed by Tribunal upholding view of appellate authority - Revenue carried matter to HC vide appeal wherein impugned judgment was passed allowing appeal - Hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether claim of assessee could be allowed.

Held, advance given to Hero Fibres Limited became imperative as business expediency in view of undertaking given to financial institutions by assessee to the effect that it would provide additional margin to Hero Fibres Limited to meet the working capital for meeting any cash loses. Further, with regard to loans to Directors, assessee had a credit balance in the bank account when the said advance of Rs.34 lakh was given and so, assessee could in any case, utilize those funds for giving advance to its Directors. Hence, order of HC is set aside and that of Tribunal is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Commercial expediency is an expression of wide import and includes such expenditure as a prudent business man incurs for the purpose of business. The expenditure could not have been incurred under any legal obligation, but yet it is allowable as business expenditure if it was incurred on grounds of commercial expediency.
