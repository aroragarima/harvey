State Of Tamil Nadu & Ors. v K. Shyam Sunder & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 493; (2011) 8 SCC 737; 2011 (4) CTC 874; JT 2011 (9) SC 166; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 43; 2011(8) SCALE 474; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 1094

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Uniform System of School Education Act, 2010, Tamil Nadu Uniform System of School Education (Amendment) Act, 2011

Summary: Constitution - Education - Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education Act, 2009 - Tamil Nadu Uniform System of School Education (Amendment) Act, 2011 - Tamil Nadu Uniform System of School Education Act, 2010 - Uniform System of School Education - Act 2010 was enacted to enforce the Uniform Education System in the State of Tamil Nadu in order to impart quality education to all children, without any discrimination on the ground of their economic, social or cultural background - Act 2010 provided for the State Common Board of School Education (Board); imposition of penalties for wilful contravention of the provisions of the Act or the Rules made thereunder (s. 11); offences by companies in the same regard (s. 12); and it also enabled the State Government to issue directions on policy matters to the Board from time to time which would be binding on the Board (s. 14) - Writ petitions were filed in the HC challenging the validity of various provisions of the Act 2010 - DB of the HC held that the provisions of ss. 11, 12 and 14 were unconstitutional and struck down the same while the Court issued elaborate directions for implementation of the common syllabus and the textbooks for Standards I and VI by the academic year 2010-11; and for all other Standards by the academic year 2011-12 or until the State makes the norms and the syllabus and prepares the textbooks in advance for the same - Further directions were issued by the Court to the State Government to bring the provisions of the Act 2010 in consonance with the Act 2009 and notify the Academic Authority and the State Advisory Council under the Act 2009 - Judgment of the HC was duly approved by SC, the State Authorities referred the enumerated components of the curriculum in respect of Classes II to V and VII to X to an Expert Committee for its opinion - Curriculum and syllabus prepared for uniform system of school education as well as the textbooks for Classes II to V and VII to X for uniform system of school education in Government schools and Government aided schools were approved by the Board - However, there was a change of State Government following the general elections of the State Assembly - After completing the formalities, the Government amended the Act 2010 by the Amendment Act 2011, by which it substituted s. 3 by a new Section providing that the schools would follow the common syllabus as may be specified by the Board for each subject in Standards I to X from such academic year as may be notified by the Government in the official Gazette - Government may specify different academic years for different Standards - Amendment also omitted ss. 11, 12 and 14 from the Act 2010 since those Sections had been struck down by the HC as unconstitutional - New academic session was to commence on 1.6.2011 and the Amendment Act 2011 came into force on 7.6.2011 - Writ petitions were filed challenging the said amendment - HC stayed the operation of the Amendment Act 2011 - Interim order passed by the HC was challenged before SC - SC modified the said interim order inter-alia, directing constitution of a committee of experts - In pursuance of the said order, an Expert Committee was constituted and a joint report was submitted to the HC - HC after considering the said report, struck down s. 3 of Amendment Act 2011 with a direction that the State shall distribute the textbooks printed under the uniform system of education to enable the teachers to commence classes, and complete distribution of textbooks on or before 22.7.2011 - Hence, instant appeals - Held, HC as well as SC upheld the validity of the Act 2010 - Thus, it was not permissible for the legislature to annul the effect of the said judgments by the Amendment Act 2011, particularly so far as the Ist and VIth Standards were concerned - List of approved textbooks had been published and made known to all concerned - Thus, the Act 2010 stood completely implemented so far these Standards were concerned - Further, legislature was competent to enact the revalidation Act under certain circumstances, where the statutory provisions were struck down by the court, fundamentally altering the conditions on which such a decision was based, but the legislature could not enact, as had been enacted in instant case, an invalidation Act, rendering a statute nugatory - Also, statement of Objects and Reasons of the Act 2011 clearly stipulated that legislature intended to find out a better system of school education - Thus, the object had been to repeal the Act 2010 - Also, rolling back the Act 2010 at this belated stage and withdrawal thereof even for Standard I and VI would be unjust, iniquitous and unfair to all concerned - Appellants directed to enforce the HC judgment impugned herein - Appeals dismissed.
