I.C.D.S. Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore and another

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 23; (2013) 3 SCC 541; AIR 2013 SC 3037; [2013] 350 ITR 527; JT 2013 (1) SC 396; 2013(1) SCALE 342; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 1082; [2013] 212 TAXMAN 550

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 260 - Non-Banking Finance - Hire Purchase - Registration - Depreciation - Exemption - Eligibility - Appellant-Assessee-Public limited company was non-banking finance company and was engaged in business of hire purchase, leasing and real estate etc. - Assessee, as a part of its business, leased out vehicles to its customers and thereafter, had no physical affiliation with vehicles, lessees were registered as owners of vehicles, in certificate of registration issued under Act - Return of income for relevant a/y, Assessee in his returns claimed, among other heads, depreciation in relation to certain assets, which, had been financed by assessee but registered in name of third parties and claimed depreciation at higher rate on ground that vehicles were used in business of running on hire - AO, disallowed claims both of depreciation and higher rate, on ground that assessee's use of these vehicles was only by way of leasing out to others and not as actual user of vehicles in business of running them on hire and that it merely financed purchased of these assets and was neither owner nor user - Assessee preferred appeals before Commissioner of Income Tax, who dismissed claim of in so far as question of depreciation at normal rate was concerned, but agreed with assessee's claim for depreciation at higher rate did not find favour with Commissioner - Dissatisfied, both assessee and Revenue carried matter in appeal before Income-tax Appellate Tribunal who agreed with assessee on both counts - Aggrieved revenue filed appeal before HC, who framed substantial questions of law Whether Appellant (assessee) was owner of vehicles which were leased out by it to its customers and Whether Appellant (assessee) was entitled to higher rate of depreciation on vehicles, on ground that they were hired out to Appellant's customers - HC, answered both in favor of revenue holding that vehicles were not registered in name of assessee, and that assessee had only financed transaction, it could not be held to be owner of vehicles, and thus, was not entitled to claim depreciation in respect of these vehicles - Hence instant appeals - Whether appellant was eligible for depreciation u/s. 32 of Act and whether HC, was justified in holding that appellant was not owner as such was not entitled - Held, Act, mandates that during period of lease, vehicle be registered, in certificate of registration, in name of lessee and, on conclusion of lease period, vehicle be registered in name of lessor as owner - Section leaves no choice to lessor but to allow vehicle to be registered in name of lessee, thus, no inference could be drawn from registration certificate as to ownership of legal title of vehicle and if lessee was in fact owner, he would have claimed depreciation on vehicles, which, as specifically recorded in order of Tribunal, was not done - It would be a strange situation to have no claim of depreciation in case of a particular depreciable asset due to a vacuum of ownership, entire lease rent received by assessee was assessed as business income in its hands and entire lease rent paid by lessee had been treated as deductible revenue expenditure in hands of lessee, which reaffirms position that assessee was in fact owner of vehicle, in so far as s. 32 of Act was concerned - Assessee was owner of vehicles, as owner, it used assets in course of its business, satisfying both requirements of s. 32 of Act and hence, was entitled to claim depreciation in respect of additions made to trucks, which were leased out - Appeals allowed.
