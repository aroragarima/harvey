Harbans Lal v M. L. Wadhawan and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 29; (1987) SCC (Cr) 47; (1987) 1 SCC 151; AIR 1987 SC 217; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 747; 1987 (2) Crimes 89; 1987 CRLJ 324; 1987 CrLR(SC) 23; 1987 (12) ECC 128; 1987 (10) ECR 437; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 584; JT 1986 SC 960; 1986(2) SCALE 925; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 425; 1987 (11) ShimLC 185; 1987 (1) UJ 510

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Denied, Right lead evidence in rebuttal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Right lead evidence in rebuttal - Denied - Justifiability - Petitioner (father of detenue) filed a petition before HC against the detention order passed by respondent no. 1 u/s. 3(1) of the Act against the detenue - Detenue was allegedly in possession of a large quantity of contraband goods hidden in his premises, which were brought during the various trips that he made to Hong Kong - High Court dismissed the petition by impugned order - Hence, instant SLP - Whether Advisory Board acted in violation of law in denying to the detenu his right to examine witnesses and whether Advisory Board was bound under law to send the entire records of the proceedings before it to Central Govt. and Central Govt. in turn bound to apply its mind to the entire materials before proceeding to make the order of confirmation.

Held, SC in A.K. Roy, Etc. v. Union Of India And Anr, 1981 Indlaw SC 381, laid down that the witnesses on behalf of the detaining authority cannot be cross examined by the detenu. It is not stated in that Judgment nor in any other Judgment of SC that the witnesses on behalf of the detenu produced in rebuttal of the allegation against him cannot be cross-examined. Cross examination of such witnesses has to be by the detaining authority and that right cannot be denied to them. The mistake committed by HC is in its assessment of the worth of the affidavit evidence and the oral evidence. This is a matter to be decided by the detenu. Unless there is any legal bar for oral evidence of the detenu being adduced before the Advisory Board it should be left to the detenu to choose between affidavit evidence and' oral evidence subject of course to the rigorous limitation placed upon this right by SC in A.K. Roy's case relating to constraints of time. The HC disbelieved the case of the detenu that the witnesses declined to file their affidavits and has characterised it as 'just flimsy and without any valid reason.' Court is bound by the law laid down by the Constitution Bench of SC in A.K. Roy's case, the Advisory Board committed an error in law in denying to the detenu the right to examine the witnesses, rendering his continued detention bad. Order of detention is quashed and impugned judgment is set aside and respondent is direct that petitioner's son be released forthwith. Petition allowed.
