Hindustan Ferodo Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 62; (1997) 2 SCC 677; 1997 (68) ECR 2; 1997 (89) E.L.T. 16; JT 1996 (11) SC 40; 1996 (17) RLT 807; 1996(9) SCALE 24; 1997 (106) STC 214; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff - Item 22 F - Appellant contended that rings in question were intermediate products and did not fall u/item 22 F of Central Excise Tariff - Onus of establishing that said rings fell within item 22 F laid upon Revenue - However, Revenue led no evidence - Tribunal held that said rings fell within item 22 F - Whether Tribunal justified in holding that said rings fell within item 22 F? - Held, it is not disputed that onus of establishing that said rings fell within item 22 F lay upon Revenue - Revenue led no evidence - Onus was not discharged - Tribunal being not fully conscious of dispassionate judicial function it was expected to perform - Order of Tribunal quashed - Appeal allowed.
