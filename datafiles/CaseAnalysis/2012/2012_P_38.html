Priya Gupta v State Of Chhatishgarh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 244; (2012) 7 SCC 433; AIR 2012 SC 2413; 2012 (6) AWC 6299; JT 2012 (5) SC 102; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 817; 2012(5) SCALE 328; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 768; 2012 (5) SLR 124

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical Council of India regulations, Graduate Medical Education (Amendment) Regulations, 2004, Chhatisgarh Chikitsha Tatha Dant Chikitsha Snatak Pravesh Pariksha Niyam, 2006, Medical Council of India Eligibility Certificate Regulations, 2002, Chhattishgarh Medical and Dental Graduate Examination Rules, 2006'

Summary: Education - Administration - Constitution - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129,141 and 142 - Chhattishgarh Medical and Dental Graduate Examination Rules, 2006, r. 4(1) (2) - Admission through influence - Cancellation - Justifiability - Appellants got admission in MBBS course for academic year 2006-07 in Govt . Medical College by influencing authorities -Later respondent department cancelled admission granted to appellants - Aggrieved by said order appellants filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether cancellation of admission to appellants was in violation of principles of natural justice - Held, fault was attributed to all stake holders involved in process of admission, i.e., concerned Ministry of Union of India, Directorate of Medical Education, Dean of the concerned College and all three members of Committee which granted admission to both appellants but students were also not innocent - Appellants had certainly taken advantage of being persons of influence - There was violation of basic principles of equality of opportunity and of equal consideration for allotment of seats and candidates of higher merit was excluded - On peculiar facts and circumstances to make complete justice between parties within ambit of art.142 of Constitution and permit appellants to complete their professional courses subject to condition that each one of them should pay sum of RS. 5 lakhs to concerned college which would be used for developing infrastructure of the college - Thus directions and order issued to initiate contempt proceedings against all stake holders under the provisions of Act r/w art.129 of Constitution and to show cause why they were not be punished in accordance with law - All concern authorities were directed to carry directions and orders forthwith, for strict compliance and adherence, without demur and default - Appeals disposed of.
