State Of Bihar & Anr. v Sunny Prakash & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 30; (2013) 3 SCC 559; 2013 (98) ALR 225; 2013 (2) AWC 1526; JT 2013 (1) SC 608; 2013 (3) MLJ 110; 2013(1) SCALE 451; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 362; 2013 (3) SLR 261

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 166, 166(3) - Commitment agreements - Non-Compliance - Strike - Challenged - Appellant (State Govt.) declared the non-teaching staff of Universities and Constituent Colleges equivalent to Govt. staff through an Order dt.25.2.1987 - An agreement was arrived at between appellant and respondent (College Employees Federation) (Federation) for implementation of GO dt.25.2.1987 - However, said GO was not implemented due to which Federation called indefinite strike in Universities - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 (student) addressed to HC requesting to end the strike, which was treated as PIL - DB by impugned order directed appellant to implement commitment under agreement and dismissed application for modification of impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that said agreement was not in accordance with Rules of Executive Business, framed u/art. 166(3) of Constitution - Whether commitment given by Govt. was binding upon it and impugned direction given by HC was justified - Held, in instant case, commitment made by State Govt. as early as in 1987, subsequent demands made by Federation on various occasions and final decision by Minister concerned, various officers including HRD and Finance Depts., representatives of Federation and all other persons connected with issue in question - Directions were issued to Vice Chancellors and Registrars of all Universities for implementing said Govt.'s decision - It could not be open to State to contend that it was not a Govt.'s decision in terms of art. 162 r/w art. 166 of Constitution - It was clear that merely because of change of elected Govt. and decision of previous Govt. not expressed in the name of Governor in terms of art. 166 of Constitution, valid decision could not be ignored and it was not open to State to contend that those decisions did not bind them - Provisions of art. 166 of Constitution were only directory and not mandatory in character and if they were not complied with, it could be established as a question of fact that impugned GO was issued in fact by State Govt. - Various communications issued by Govt. for implementation of earlier decision - All persons who were competent to represent were parties to said agreement and after making such commitment by State Govt., same has to be honored without any exception - Impugned order of HC not only directed State Govt. to implement commitment given by it having reduced into writing on 18.7.2007, honoured by State Govt. itself in subsequent letters/correspondences but also directed Federation to call off strike immediately in the interest of student community - Govt. was directed to implement impugned order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
