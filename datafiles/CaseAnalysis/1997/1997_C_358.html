Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1760; AIR 1999 SC 1579; 1998 (150) CTR 616; [1998] 233 ITR 199; 1999 (2) TLR 163

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952 - Deduction - Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was justified in allowing deduction of the liability of Rs. 34, 131 incurred by the assessee for the payment of damages u/s. 14B of the Act.

Held, in view of the decision in Prakash Cotton Mills P. Ltd.'s case 1993 Indlaw SC 1338, question requires to be examined in accordance with the principles laid down by the Andhra Pradesh HC in CIT v. Hyderabad Allwyn Metal Works Ltd. 1987 Indlaw AP 199 which have been approved by SC. Matter remitted to the HC for consideration. Appeal disposed of.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Penalty - Deduction - Allowability - Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding that the penalty of Rs. 7, 667 levied on the assessee under the Central Sales Tax Act could not be allowed as a deduction while computing the income of the assessee.

Held, the judgment of the Tribunal that the penalty that was imposed was not on account of any delayed payment of Central Sales Tax but was for contravention of the provisions of the Central Sales Tax Act. There is nothing on the record to show that the amount of penalty had a compensatory element in it. Appeal disposed of.
