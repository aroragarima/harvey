Nalinikant Ambalal Mody v S. A. L. Narayan Row, Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 241; AIR 1967 SC 193; 1967 CRLJ 843; [1966] 61 ITR 428; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(1), 12 - A/ys 1959-60 and 1960-61 Receipts - Outstanding Dues - Professional Tax - Challenged - Appellant/assessee was practicing advocate elevated to Bench ceased his practice, received sum of Rs. 30,570 and Rs. 15,240 respectively on account of professional fees for work done by him before 1-3-1957 and filed returns for A/ys, 1959-60 and 1960-61 - Respondent/Income-tax Officer subjected aforesaid two sums to tax treating them as receipts of fees for professional services rendered in earlier years and as part of total income of appellant - Appellant filed 2 revision petitions before Commissioner of Income-tax, u/s. 33-A of the Act - Commissioner of Income-tax held that two sums were assessable on general principles and also on the authority of the decision in Re. B. M. Kamdar 1945 Indlaw MUM 3and rejected revision petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether professional income of appellant/assessee whose accounts were kept on cash basis, received by him during his life-time after discontinuance of profession and after close of accounting year in which profession was discontinued, was assessable to income-tax either u/ss. 10 or 12 of the Act was challenged - Held, referred Commissioner of Income-tax v. Cocanada Radhaswami Bank Ltd. 619 1965 Indlaw SC 100, said income should be chargeable to tax under head 'other sources' if it did not come under any other head of income mentioned in the s. 12 of the Act therefore did not assist contention of Revenue that professional income which could not be brought to tax u/s. 10 might be so brought u/s. 12 of the Act - Therefore, Court came to conclusion that receipts were not chargeable to tax either under head of professional income or under residuary head and were not chargeable to tax at all - In instant case, appellant/assessee discontinued his profession as soon as he became Judge and could not carry on profession - Appellant/assessee did not carry on his profession as advocate at any time during Calendar years, 1958 and 1959 - Thus, receipts of outstanding professional fees during 1958 and 1959 were not profits and gains of profession carried on by appellant during those years, and were not assessable to tax u/s. 10(1) of the Act - Appeals disposed of.
