Saraswathi Ammal and Another v Rajagopal Ammal

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 88; AIR 1953 SC 491; 1953 (2) MLJ(SC) 803; 1953 SCJ 714; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 277

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Plaintiff and 1st defendant are daughters of one Kanakasabapathi Pillai - 2nd defendant is husband of 1st defendant - Pillai's widow continued motor service of her husband and managed other properties with help of 2nd defendant as her manager - On mother's death defendants' 1 and 2 both got into possession of all properties including motor service - Shortly before her death, widow, Gomathi Ammal, executed two documents (1) a sale deed by which she conveyed entire bus service as a going concern to 2nd defendant for consideration of Rs. 80, 000/- (2) a settlement deed by which she dedicated some immovable properties worth about Rs. 27, 000/- For performance of certain services purporting to be of a religious and charitable character - Sale deed was set aside by courts below as it was executed for grossly inadequate consideration and brought about by undue influence and fraud of 2nd defendant - Kanakasabhapati was entombed after this death - Whether dedication made therefore is valid? - Held, there is no evidence in case as to what 'Gurupooja' contemplated in deed consists of any whether it is not merely worship of deceased entombed in samadhi - It is clear, therefore, that all these various items of expenses are contemplated as expenses for samadhi kainkariyam and not for any other kind of religious or charitable purpose as such - Indeed it may be assumed that such a practice is not likely to grow up amongst Hindus where cremation and not burial of dead is normal practice, except probably as regards sannyasis and in certain dissident communities - No reason to think that Madras decisions are erroneous in holding that perpetual dedication of property for worship at a tomb is not valid amongst Hindus - Appeal dismissed.
