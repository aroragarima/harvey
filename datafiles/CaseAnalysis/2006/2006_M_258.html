State Bank Of India And Another v Mula Sahakari Sakhar Karkhana Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1463; (2006) 6 SCC 293; AIR 2007 SC 2361; 2006 (5) ALD(SC) 35; 2006 (3) AWC 3124; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 143; JT 2006 (6) SC 152; 2006(6) SCALE 600; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Of Guarantee

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 92 - Document indemnifying loss - Nature of - Determination - Respondent/Society entered into a purchase contract with another Company (Supplier) for installation and commissioning of a machinery in its plant wherein supplier agreed that respondent was entitled to retain 10% of purchase price till satisfactory commissioning of the plant - Parties, however, modified the said clause in the agreement to the effect that respondent had waived its right to retain 10% purchase price whereas respondent would furnish a Bank Guarantee equivalent to 10% of purchase price - Disputes and differences arose by and between the parties - Respondent invoked Bank Guarantee, however, appellant/Bank resisted respondent's invocation on the ground that respondent could not invoke the Bank Guarantee unless it had proved any loss or damage for design and performance of machinery through a competent court - Respondent filed a suit against appellant/Bank - Trial Court dismissed the suit, however, HC, in appeal, decreed the suit in favour of respondent holding that document in question was a Bank Guarantee and not a contract of indemnity for loss - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Bank - Whether the document in question was a Bank Guarantee or contract of indemnity - Held, document in question constitutes a document of indemnity and not a document of guarantee as is clear from the fact that by reason thereof the appellant was to indemnify the respondent against all losses, claims, damages, actions and costs which may be suffered by it - Document does not contain the usual words found in a bank guarantee furnished by a Bank as, for example, 'unequivocal condition', 'the purchaser would be entitled to claim the damages without any delay or demur' or the guarantee was 'unconditional and absolute' - HC, thus, misread and misinterpreted the document as a contract of guarantee and not a contract of indemnity - Document was executed by the appellant/Bank in favour of the respondent at the instance of supplier/Company - HC proceeded on the basis that s. 92 of the Act would be attracted in the instant case, which was incorrect - Document in question clearly stated therein that the bank guarantee constitutes a separate, distinct and independent contract between the appellant/Bank and the supplier - Further, document in question does not specifically refer to any particular clause of the contract of purchase and in fact the contract does not contain any clause requiring supplier to furnish any Bank Guarantee thereof - Therefore, document in question constitutes a contract of indemnity and not an absolute or unconditional bank guarantee and the HC erred in construing the same to be an unconditional and absolute bank guarantee - Impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
