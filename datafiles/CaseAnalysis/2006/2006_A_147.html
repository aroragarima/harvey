Avas Vikas Sansthan and another v Avas Vikas Sansthan Engineers Association and

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 146; (2006) 4 SCC 132; AIR 2006 SC 3413; 2007 (5) AWC 5049; JT 2006 (4) SC 118; 2006 (2) RLW 1606; 2006(3) SCALE 583; 2006 (3) SCJ 247; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 516; 2007 (1) SLR 312; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1609

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: AVS Employees Service Regulation, 1993, Rajasthan Civil Services Rules, 1969, Rajasthan Civil Services (Absorption of Surplus Personnel) Rules, 1969

Summary: [A.] Service - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 311 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-FF - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - AVS Employees Service Regulation, 1993 - Rajasthan Civil Services Rules, 1969 - Rajasthan Civil Services (Absorption of Surplus Personnel) Rules, 1969 - Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951 - Employees of Avas Vikas Sansthan - Appellant company dissolved - Right to seek reemployment or absorption in other departments - Held, after liquidation of AVS due to any reason unless such liquidation was malafide, there exists no right on employees of such liquidated society for re-employment - Rajasthan Govt. did formulate a scheme to absorb employees of society into various other organizations with various terms and conditions to which respondent employees agreed - It is unfair to take claims of service conditions other than ones that are stipulated and accepted earlier - Employees of Avas Vikas Sansthan are not Govt. servants nor Rajasthan Service Rules, 1951 were applicable to them and as such provisions of Rajasthan Civil Services (Absorption of Surplus Personnel) Rules are not applicable - Appeals disposed of.
