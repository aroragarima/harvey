Estralla Rubber v Dass Estate (Private) Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21392; (2001) 8 SCC 97; AIR 2001 SC 3295; 2002 AllCJ 168; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 181; JT 2001 (7) SC 657; 2001(6) SCALE 275; 2001 (3) SCJ 488; 2001 (6) SLT 577; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 68; 2001 (7) Supreme 53

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 6 r. 17 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 17(2) and 17(2A) - Amendment of application - Plaintiff filed suit against defendant in respect of suit property for eviction on the ground of reasonable requirement for building or rebuilding and on the ground of default in payment of rent - Defendant filed an application u/ss. 17(2) and 17(2A) of 1956 Act raising certain contentions - Thereafter, defendant filed an application for amendment u/o. 6 r. 17 of CPC which was contested by the plaintiff - Trial Court rejected said application, taking a view that the proposed amendment would be inconsistent and it will have the effect of displacing the plaintiff from admission made by the defendant - Defendant filed a revision petition against the said order before the District Judge who allowed the revision petition, reversed the order of the trial court and allowed the amendment application filed by the defendant - On appeal, HC set aside the order of District Judge - Hence, present appeal - Held, HC set aside the District Judge's order on ground that proposed amendment will have the effect of displacing the plaintiff from admission made by the defendant in the petition and that such admission could not be permitted to be withdrawn - Merely because there was delay in making the amendment application, when no serious prejudice is shown to have been caused to the plaintiff so as to take away any accrued right, the application could not be rejected - Hence, it cannot be said that allowing amendment caused irretrievable prejudice to the plaintiff - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
