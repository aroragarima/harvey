Central Bank of India v Shri Gokal Chand

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 58; AIR 1967 SC 799; 1967 (2) SCJ 828; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 38(1) - Appeal from order rejecting application for issue of a commission to prepare a plan of premises lay to Rent Control Tribunal u/s. 38(1) - Object of s. 38(1) is to give a right of appeal to a party aggrieved by some order which affects his right or liability - Words 'every order of Controller made under this Act', though very wide, do not include interlocutory orders, which are merely procedural and do not affect rights or liabilities of parties - Parties are open to set forth error, defect or irregularity, if any, as grounds of objection in appeal from final order in main proceeding - Subject to this limitation, an appeal may lie to Rent Control Tribunal from every order passed by Controller - Held, order of Controller refusing to issue a commission being mere procedural order not affecting any right or liability of appellant hence no appeal lay to Rent Control Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
