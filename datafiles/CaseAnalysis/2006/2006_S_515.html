Pandit D. Aher v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 880; (2007) 1 SCC 445; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 89; 2006(11) SCALE 195; 2007 (1) SLR 753; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1979, r. 3 - Appellant working as Block Development Officer - Departmental proceeding initiated against him on purported charge that he had committed serious misconduct, causing loss to Govt. - Departmental inquiry - Show cause notices were issued to him - Disciplinary Authority imposed a punishment of forfeiture of entire pension and gratuity permanently - Challenged - Question as to whether proceedee has committed grave misconduct or negligence during his tenure of service is essentially a question of fact - (A) Supply of document - Held, copy of inquiry report was supplied to him - Copy of document which has not been relied upon, is not required to be supplied to a delinquent officer - Documents which are required to be supplied are only those whereupon reliance has been placed by Department - (B) Whether charges against appellant were grave in nature? - Held, charges are of grave nature - Appellant has not only been charged with negligence in his duty, State is also said to have suffered losses on account of his action and/or inaction in implementing Jeevandhara Well Scheme - (C) Whether charges against appellant were proved? - Imposition of punishment - Held, it is now well-settled that what was necessary for imposition of punishment was to arrive at a finding of misconduct which is of grave nature or misconduct involving negligence on part of delinquent officer - Charge-sheet issued against appellant fulfills aforementioned conditions - He was found guilty of commission of alleged acts of misconduct - Appellant was incharge of Scheme which was to be implemented in his Block and therefore he, being a Supervisory Head, had a duty to see that Scheme is implemented in its letter and spirit - Two of charges framed against him clearly relate to administrative lapses on his part - Imposition of punishment justified - Appeal dismissed.
