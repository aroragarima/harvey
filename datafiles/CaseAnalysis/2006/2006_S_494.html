State of Maharashtra and Others v Ravi Prakash Babulalsing Parmar and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1337; (2007) 1 SCC 80; AIR 2007 SC 295; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 102; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 20; JT 2006 (10) SC 110; 2006(10) SCALE 575; 2007 (8) SCJ 90; 2006 (6) SLR 631; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Whether Caste Scrutiny Committee could go into validity or otherwise of certificate granted by authorities? - Held, it was allowed - A certificate is granted to a person who is not otherwise entitled thereto, it is entirely incorrect to contend that State shall be helpless spectator in matter - Committee can go into question as to whether a caste certificate has rightly been issued or not - Appeal allowed.
