Chindha Fakira Patil (D) Through L.Rs. v Special Land Acquisition Officer, Jalgaon

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 702; (2011) 10 SCC 787; AIR 2012 SC 481; 2012 (1) AWC 1; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 735; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 251; JT 2011 (12) SC 527; 2012 (2) MahLJ 530; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 802; 2012 (2) MPLJ 179; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 10; 2011(12) SCALE 321

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Enhanced compensation - Entitlement - Appellant's land was acquired by the respondent authority - Respondent passed award and appellant received compensation under protest - Appellant sought reference of the matter to Reference Court and accordingly, matter was referred to Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation - Respondent challenged the Reference Court order before the HC - HC interfered with the Reference Court order and reduced compensation on the ground that the sales instances produced by the appellant was a special case where the purchaser had paid the higher amount for some special reasons - Whether the HC was right in interfering with the fixation of market value by Reference Court in the instant case - Held, approach adopted by the HC was clearly erroneous and there was no basis for the assumption that the purchaser of the land would have offered higher price for special reasons - Further, it was neither the pleaded case of the respondent nor it had been argued that the sale deed in question had not been proved or that the price mentioned therein was not the highest price paid for the adjacent land in the area - Hence, HC was not right in interfering with the fixation of market value by the Reference Court for the acquired land - Mere fact that average sale price of the transactions relied upon by the respondent was substantially less could not be made a ground for discarding sale instance in question - Further, there was no valid reason for the HC to interfere with the finding recorded by the Reference Court that parts of the lands were agricultural land and for such land appellants were entitled to a reduced compensation - HC also committed error by rejecting the reports submitted by consultant in Agriculture and Horticulture who had made valuation of the standing tress in the acquired land, that was amply supported by the market rates of fruits fixed by Agriculture and Horticulture Department of the State - Thus, HC had no reason to overturn the findings recorded by the Reference Court on the issue of existence of trees on the acquired land and their valuation - Hence, impugned HC judgment was set aside and award passed by the Reference Court was restored - Appeals allowed.
