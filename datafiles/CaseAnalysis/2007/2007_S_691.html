Iqbal Bano v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 618; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 258; (2007) 6 SCC 785; AIR 2007 SC 2215; 2007 (4) ALJ 587; 2007 (4) CTC 90; 2007 (3) JCC 1711; JT 2007 (8) SC 648; 2007 (3) KLT 63; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 297; 2007(8) SCALE 426; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 949; 2007 (4) UC 418

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Utterance

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, S. 125 - Judicial Magistrate directed respondent no.2 to pay a monthly maintenance of Rs.450/- to the appellant - Additional Sessions Judge set aside the order - Appeal against - Held, view expressed by the First Revisional Court that no Muslim woman can maintain petition under s. 125 Cr.P.C. is clearly unsustainable - The Act only applies to divorced women and not to a woman who is not divorced - Proceedings under s. 125 Cr.P.C. are civil in nature - Set aside the order impugned of the High Court and remit the matter for fresh consideration - Appeal disposed of.
