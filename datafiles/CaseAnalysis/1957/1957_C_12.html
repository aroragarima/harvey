United Commercial Bank Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1957 SC 918; [1957] 32 ITR 688; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 26; 1958 SCJ 46; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-tax Act, ss. 10, 24(2) - Set Off - Taxable Income - Interest on Securities - Aggregate Income - Appellant/ assessee claimed computation of profits for assessment year under review - Assessee is a Bank carrying on banking business - Income Tax Officer divided income into 2 heads interest on securities and business income and Interest on securities in year of assessment was under head business income there was a loss - Necessary adjustments and deducting business loss from Interest on securities net income was determined - Previous year's loss was computed by setting off business loss against interest on securities - Assessee made claim on basis that it was a part of business of Bank to deal in securities and no distinction should be made between income from securities and income from business for purpose of set-off - Contention was repelled and matter was taken to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal stated Income-tax Officer was right in splitting income of appellant into two heads and refusing set-off of business loss brought forward from last year against income from Govt. securities earned in present year - HC rejected contention of assessee - Whether assessee was entitled to set off business loss - Held, true nature and character of securities in hands of an assessee was one of trading assets would be excluded - interest on securities and profits and gains of business are dealt income tax , ss. 8 ,10 and s. 8 provides that tax shall be payable by an assessee under head interest on securities in respect of interest receivable by him on any security of Central Govt. and in provisos to section are given allowable deductions - Income of assessee from its banking business which includes dealing insecurities was really income from same source and whatever accrues in form of interest - All interest accrues from business carried on by assessee and banking was only business - Income derived from property must be regarded as income derived from business and purchase and sale of securities was assessee's business as receiving deposits from clients and withdrawals by them - Appeal allowed.
