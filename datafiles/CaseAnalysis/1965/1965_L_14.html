Â Lord Krishna Sugar Mills v Municipal Committee, Saharanpur

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 436; AIR 1966 SC 1519; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 959

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: K. N. Wanchoo, J

(A) Indirect Tax - Municipalities & Local Governments - Payment of toll Tax - Municipal limits - Appellant (Mills) was situated outside limits of municipality and exported goods by railway which was situated within municipal limits and therefore lorries have to enter municipal limits - Dispute arose between appellant and municipality that appellant was entitled to benefit of r. 8 (a) of Tolls which would exempt it from payment of toll tax - Municipal Board did not accept interpretation of r. 8 (a) of Tolls, therefore appellant had to pay toll tax - Petition filed before HC in which Single Judge rejected interpretation placed on r. 8 (a) of Tolls holding that what r. 8 (a) of Tolls contemplated was that goods should leave municipality as appellant's lorries did not leave municipal limits but were unloaded at railway station which was admittedly within municipal Limits therefore appellant was not entitled to transit passes for its lorries - Appellant filed appeal before Division Bench which was dismissed upholding interpretation on r. 8 (a) of Tolls by Single Judge - Thereupon appellant applied for certificate which was granted by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Appellant was liable to pay toll tax to municipality - Held, lorries were never meant to proceed beyond railway station and railway station was within municipal area so there could be no question of grant of transit passes to such lorries - Crucial words in r. 8 (a) of Tolls was 'for immediate export from such limits without sorting and change of bulk' and those mean that goods should go out of municipal limits as soon as possible on lorry on which they had entered and unless that was done lorry would not be entitled to transit pass - Appeal dismissed.

Hidayatullah, J.

(B) Indirect Tax - Municipalities & Local Governments - Payment of Toll Tax - Municipality limits - Whether Appellant was liable to pay toll tax to municipality - Held, Municipality had established its barrier convenient to itself so as to segregate railway yard from town proper - Municipality imposed taxes only when there was entry into town from railway yard and same thing obtained when goods were exported through export barrier and enter railway yard - Municipality satisfied itself that goods passed out of municipal area and was not likely to re enter without paying toll - Rule should be applied in fair and equitable manner and one of cardinal principles of law was that law did not expect, nor compel man to do that which he could not possibly perform - Municipality by its own arrangement regards station yard as being outside its export barrier - If same goods were brought in again next day or day after, they would bear tax at import barrier - Said goods had paid toll at other end of Municipality and were within municipal limits all time - Import barrier would be treated toll barrier even for said goods - Appeal dismissed.
