Suraj Lamp and Industries Private Limited v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 647; (2012) 1 SCC 656; AIR 2012 SC 206; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 92; 2012(1) ALL MR 464; 2011 (89) ALR 445; 2011 (6) AWC 6296; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 293; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 8; [2012] 169 Comp Cas 133; 2011 (6) CTC 90; 2011 (183) DLT 1; [2012] 340 ITR 1; JT 2011 (12) SC 564; 2011 (6) KarLJ 69; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2881; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 43; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 669; 2012 (115) RD 1; 2011(11) SCALE 438; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 848; [2011] 202 TAXMAN 607; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 28

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 53A - Agreements entered into prior to alienation - Scope and ill-effects - Validity - Whether the General Power of Attorney Sales (GPA Sales) or Sale Agreement/General Power of Attorney/Will transfers (SA/GPA/WILL) should be considered as a valid transfer of immovable property - Held, a SA/GPA/WILL transaction did not convey any title nor created any interest in an immovable property - Immovable property should be legally and lawfully transferred/conveyed only by a registered deed of conveyance - Transactions of the nature of 'GPA sales' or 'SA/GPA/WILL transfers' did not convey title and did not amount to transfer, nor could they be recognized or valid mode of transfer of immoveable property - Courts would not treat such transactions as completed or concluded transfers or as conveyances as they neither conveyed title nor created any interest in an immovable property - Such agreements should not be recognized as deeds of title, except to the limited extent of s. 53A of the Act - Such transactions should not be relied upon or made the basis for mutations in Municipal or Revenue Records - However, it would not apply not only to deeds of conveyance in regard to freehold property but also to transfer of leasehold property - A lease would be validly transferred only under a registered Assignment of Lease - It was required to put an end to the pernicious practice of SA/GPA/WILL transactions known as GPA sales - SA/GPA/WILL transactions were not 'transfers' or 'sales' and that such transactions could not be treated as completed transfers or conveyances - Such transactions could continue to be treated as existing agreement of sale - Nothing prevented the affected parties from getting registered Deeds of Conveyance to complete their title - SA/GPA/WILL transactions should also be used to obtain specific performance or to defend possession u/s. 53A of the Act - If such transactions were entered before the date of the instant order, they would be relied upon to apply for regularization of allotments/leases by Development Authorities - If the documents relating to 'SA/GPA/WILL transactions' had been accepted acted upon by Delhi Development Authority or other developmental authorities or by the Municipal or revenue authorities to effect mutation, they should not be disturbed, merely on account of instant decision - Order accordingly.
