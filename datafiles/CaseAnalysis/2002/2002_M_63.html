Madho Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2250; (2010) 15 SCC 588; JT 2002 (8) SC 305

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 302, 341 and 34 - Primary basis for conviction was that deceased was last seen with accused - Whether sustainable in view of absence of any other substantial evidence? - Held, in absence of proof of homicidal death appellants cannot be convicted merely on theory of last seen - Appellants' conviction cannot be maintained merely on suspicion, however strong it may be, or on their conduct - These facts assume further importance on account of absence of proof of motive particularly when it is proved that all three were good friends for over a decade - Further held, in order to convict an accused for an offence u/s. 302, first and foremost aspect to be proved by prosecution is homicidal death - Evidence on record produced by prosecution falls short of proof of homicidal death not possible to sustain sentence - Appeal allowed.
