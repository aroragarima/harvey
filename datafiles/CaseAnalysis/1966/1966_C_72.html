Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Madras v Ramaraju Surgical Cotton Mills, Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 1967 SC 509; [1967] 63 ITR 478; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 25; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 761

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Industries (Development & Regulation) Act, 1951 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 u/s. 5(1)(xxi) - Wealth tax - Deduction - Entitlement - Respondent company had resolved to establish new unit - Said unit was completed in the year 1957 - Respondent was assessed to wealth tax for A/Y 1957-1958, and claimed deduction of amount spent in setting up said unit - Wealth tax officer disallowed said claim on ground that said unit was set up prior to the date on which the Act of 1957, came into force - Aggrieved respondent appealed before Assistant commissioner and Tribunal which upheld order of Wealth tax officer - Said order was referred before HC which was held in favour of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, that HC had rightly, proceeded on basis that unit was completed and became ready to go into business only after 1st April, 1957, when the Act had already come into force and consequently, condition laid down in principal clause of s. 5(1)(xxi) of the Act was satisfied, and company became entitled to exemption in respect of value of assets used up in setting up said unit - Therefore, first A/Y after commencement of operations for establishment of said unit was A/Y 1957-58 - Under the 1957, Act, A/Y had been defined to mean the year for which tax was chargeable u/s. 3 of the Act - Since the Act came into force on 1st April, 1957, F/Y 1957-58 was first A/Y for which tax became chargeable, and for purposes of second proviso to s. 5(1)(xxi) of the Act - A/Y following commencement of operations for establishment of said unit in case of any company which commenced operations any time before 1st April, 1957, would be A/Y 1957-58 - Therefore, prior to year 1957-58, there was no A/Y as defined under the Act, and consequently, first A/Y for which exemption could be claimed was A/Y 1957-58 - Hence, respondent which had commenced operations for establishment of its new unit prior to 1st April, 1957, was rightly allowed exemption in respect of amount that had been invested by it upto relevant valuation date and order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
