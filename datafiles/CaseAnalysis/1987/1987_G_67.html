M. K. Agarwal v Gurgaon Gramin Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28485; (1987) Supp SCC 643; (1988) SCC (L&S) 347; AIR 1988 SC 286; JT 1987 (4) SC 511; 1988 LabIC 380; 1988 (1) LLN 298; 1987(2) SCALE 1073; 1988 (1) SLJ 75; 1988 (1) SLR 790; 1988 (1) UJ 40

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Gurgaon Gramin Bank (Staff) Services Regulations, 1980, regns. 10(2) and 10(2)(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14 and 32 - Petitioner, a Branch Manager was terminated u/regn. 10(2) which enabled a termination-simpliciter by way of an arbitrary and unregulated power of dismissal - Petitioners appeal to HC against his dismissal was dismissed - Whether regn. 10(2) is violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, yes - Bank is State within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution - Regn. 10(2)(a) confers on Bank an arbitrary and unguided power and is unconstitutional - Order of termination set aside - Order of reinstatement and 50% back salary in favour of petitioner - Appeal disposed of.
