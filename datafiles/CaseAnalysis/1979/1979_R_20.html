Rajinder Nath v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 136; (1979) 4 SCC 282; AIR 1979 SC 1933; 1979 (12) CTR 201; [1979] 120 ITR 14; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 272; [1979] 2 TAXMAN 204; 1979 UJ 762

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - (A) Meaning of expressions 'Finding' and 'Direction' u/s. 153(3)(ii) and explanation 3 - Direction by statutory authority - Nature of - (B) Whether an observation that ITO is free to take action is a direction? - Held, finding must be one necessary for disposal of case and contemplated by explanation of another person - While direction means express direction necessary for disposal of case given by competent court or authority - Direction by statutory authority is in nature of order requiring positive compliance - Further held, not a direction - Appeals allowed.
