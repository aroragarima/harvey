State of Assam and others v Bhaskar Jyoti Sarma and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 804; (2015) 5 SCC 321; 2015 (2) AWC 2118; JT 2014 (13) SC 301; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 578

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999, ss. 2, 3 - Issuance of notice - Possession by Govt. - Urban land ceiling proceedings were initiated against father of respondents (Owner) and later notification dt.16-5-1984 was issued declaring 7981sq.mts. vacant land to be in excess of ceiling limit - While so, in November, 1984, major portion of land in question was sold to others by Owner through various sale deeds - On 1-1-1987 notification u/s. 10(3) of the Act was issued declaring land in question as Ceiling Surplus Govt. land and part of said land was allotted in favour of 8 families in terms of land policy of Govt. and remaining land was retained by Govt. - After prolonged legal proceedings under the Act initiated by purchasers of land from Owner, land allotted to said 8 families by Govt. was upheld - While so, Govt. by order dt.27-11-2003 allotted remaining part of land to Guwahati Metropolitan Development Authority (GMDA) and the same was challenged by respondents in HC which was dismissed upholding allotment of land to GMDA - However, on appeal, DB allowed the same and directed restoration of possession of disputed part of land to respondents - Hence, instant appeals by State and GMDA on ground that actual physical possession was taken over from Owner in December 1991, itself, even though there were no records of any notice issued u/s. 10(5) of 1976 Act to land owners - Whether failure of Govt. to issue notice to land owners in terms of s. 10(5) of 1976 Act would mean that such dispossession is no dispossession in law and so insufficient to attract s. 3 of 1999 Act.

Held, dispossession without notice u/s. 10(5) of 1976 Act will be of no consequence and would not vitiate or obliterate the act of taking possession for the purposes of s. 3 of 1999 Act because father of respondents had not made any grievance based on breach of s. 10(5) at any stage during his lifetime implying thereby that he had waived his right to do so. Further, DB while reversing view taken by Single Judge not recorded any specific finding to effect that possession had actually continued with the erstwhile owner even after the vesting of the land u/s. 10(3) and the proceedings dt.7-12-1991. Hence, order of DB is set aside and appeals are allowed. Further, order passed by Single Judge is affirmed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s. 3 of 1999 Act, repeal of 1976 Act does not affect the vesting of any vacant land u/s. 10(3), where possession has been taken over by the State Govt. or any person duly authorized by the State Govt. in that behalf or by the competent authority.
