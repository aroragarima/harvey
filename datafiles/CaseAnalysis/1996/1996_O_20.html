B. V. Nagaraju v Oriental Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3887; (1996) 4 SCC 647; 1997 ACC 123; AIR 1996 SC 2054; AIR 1996 SCW 2466; 1996 (3) AWC 1313; 1996 (2) CCC 373; [1998] 94 Comp Cas 278; 1996 CPJ 18; 1996 (4) CTJ 373; JT 1996 (6) SC 32; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; 1996(4) SCALE 640; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Insurance Policy - Liability of insurance Co. for damage caused to vehicle in head on collision as claimed by truckowner - Alleged breach of terms of insurance policy by carrring more than peermissible persons - Effect of liablity - Held, it is not so fundamental a breach so as to afford to insurer to eschew liability altogether - Appeal allowed.
