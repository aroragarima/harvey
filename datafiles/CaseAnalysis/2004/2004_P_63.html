Bala Seetharamaiah v Perike S. Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1419; (2004) 4 SCC 557; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1332; AIR 2004 SC 2172; 2004 CRLJ 2034; JT 2004 (4) SC 549; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 318; 2004(3) SCALE 571; 2004 (1) UC 681

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 324, 326 - Charges not framed - Error by Sessions court - Sentences undergone - Accused were charged u/s. 148 of IPC, it was alleged that they formed themselves into unlawful assembly with common object, and caused death deceased and thereby committed offence punishable u/s. 148 of IPC - Second charge was u/s. 302 of IPC simpliciter against each of accused - Third charge was framed for offence punishable u/s. 324 of IPC for having caused injury appellant - Accused were found guilty by Sessions Court and were sentenced - Aggrieved accused appealed - HC altered sentences - Aggrieved by judgment of HC State filed appeals and de facto appellant appealed - Hence, instant criminal appeal - Whether though specific charge was not framed against accused u/ss. 302, 149 of IPC, was it only an irregularity which had not prejudiced accused and whether HC seriously erred in not convicting them for the offence of murder - Held, Sessions Judge had not framed charge against accused persons for offence punishable u/s. 302, 149 of IPC and relevant prosecution allegations to establish ingredients of offence were also not incorporated in charge framed by Sessions Judge - Mere omission to mention s. 149 may be considered as irregularity, but failure to mention nature of offence committed cannot be said to be mere irregularity - Accused were told to face charge punishable u/s. 302 of IPC simplicitor, but there was no charge u/s. 302 of IPC read with s. 149 of IPC, therefore, it was not possible to reverse conviction of accused u/s. 326 IPC and substitute the conviction for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 149 of IPC as there was no charge framed against them for such offence - Since incident happened in 1990 and appeal was disposed of in 1996 by HC, sentences were undergone - Appeal disposed of.
