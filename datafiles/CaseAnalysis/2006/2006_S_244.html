Raj Pal And Others v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1444; (2006) 9 SCC 678; 2006 (2) JCC 775; JT 2006 (11) SC 124; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 457; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 38; 2006(4) SCALE 456; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 74

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 300 and 304 - Murder or culpable homicide - Distinction - Held, difference between clause (b) of s. 299 and clause (3) of s. 300 is one of degree of probability of death resulting from the intended bodily injury - It is the degree of probability of death which determines whether a culpable homicide is of the gravest, medium or the lowest degree - Word "likely" in clause (b) of s. 299 conveys the sense of probability as distinguished from a mere possibility - Words "bodily injury ... sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death" mean that death will be the "most probable" result of the injury, having regard to the ordinary course of nature - Under clause (3) of s. 300 IPC, culpable homicide is murder, if both the following conditions are satisfied i.e. (a) that the act which causes death is done with the intention of causing death or is done with the intention of causing a bodily injury; and (b) that the injury intended to be inflicted is sufficient in the ordinary course of nature to cause death - It must be proved that there was an intention to inflict that particular bodily injury which, in the ordinary course of nature, was sufficient to cause death viz. that the injury found to be present was the injury that was intended to be inflicted - Clause (4) of s. 300 would be applicable where the knowledge of the offender as to the probability of death of a person or persons in general as distinguished from a particular person or persons, being caused from his imminently dangerous act, approximates to a practical certainty - Such knowledge on the part of the offender must be of the highest degree of probability, the act having been committed by the offender without any excuse for incurring the risk of causing death or such injury as aforesaid - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and 304 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Murder or culpable homicide - Plea of right of private defence - Dispute over property - Accused attacked deceased and others - Appellants raised plea of right of private defence on basis of injuries sustained by them - Pleas rejected by Trial Court - Trial Court noted that there was no intention to commit murder and the appellants did not repeat the blows on the head of the deceased; but knowledge can be clearly attributed to them that by giving blow on the head of the deceased death was only consequence - Appellants convicted for offence punishable u/s. 304 Part I read with s. 34 of the IPC and u/s. 325 r/w s. 34 of IPC and s. 323 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Conviction confirmed on appeal by HC - HC did not accept the plea of exercise of right of private defence and also did not accept the plea that death was not intended - Hence, present appeal - Appellants submitted that the Trial Court and the HC did not take note of the fact that the accused persons had suffered serious injuries and Trial Court having noted that there was no intention to cause homicidal death should not have convicted the appellants in terms of s. 304 Part I of IPC - Held, merely because there was a quarrel and some of the accused persons sustained injuries, that does not confer a right of private defence extending to the extent of causing death as in present case - Though such right cannot be weighed in golden scales, it has to be established that the accused persons were under such grave apprehension about the safety of their life and property that retaliation to the extent done was absolutely necessary - In the present case, no evidence much less cogent and credible was adduced in this regard - Right of private defence as claimed by the accused persons rightly discarded - However, taking the totality of the evidence into consideration and the special features noticed, it would be appropriate to convict the accused persons in terms of s. 304 Part II r/w s. 34 of IPC instead of s. 304 Part I r/w s. 34 of IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
