Karya Palak Engineer, Cpwd, Bikaner v Rajasthan Taxation Board, Ajmer and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 612; (2004) 7 SCC 195; AIR 2004 SC 4499; 2004 (177) E.L.T. 3; JT 2004 (6) SC 384; 2004(6) SCALE 647; 2004 (136) STC 641

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 - s. 2(14) - Demand of Sales Tax - Whether a supply of materials by Union of India to its contractors under agreements of works contract would amount to a sale so as to attract provision of Sales Tax Act and whether state has authority to levy Tax on property of Union - Held, by use or consumption of material supplied in work of construction, there was passing of property and by virtue of receipt of value of such transferred property by way of adjustment in bills consideration has also passed which satisfies definition of 'sale' in local Sales Tax Act - Held further Union is not exempted from the levy of indirect tax under Article 285 of the Constitution - Appeals Dismissed.
