Gajanan L. Pernekar v State of Goa and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M. Jagannadha Rao, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1610; (1999) 8 SCC 378; AIR 1999 SC 3262; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 46; 1999 (2) CLR 831; 1999 (83) FLR 503; JT 1999 (6) SC 261; 1999 LabIC 3241; 1999 (4) LLN 19; 1999(5) SCALE 71; 1999 (4) SLR 742; 1999 (7) SLT 33; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 488; 1999 (7) Supreme 192

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Consequential benefits - Entitlement of - Appellant was absorbed from services but no consequential benefits were granted to him - Writ petition filed by appellant was disposed of but no relief was granted as regard to consequential benefits - During the pendency of the proceedings in the Govt., influenced by the observations made in order of the HC, made an order on 21st/22nd January, 1999. The Govt. not only rejected the request of the appellant for grant of consequential benefits flowing form the earlier order but even rescinded the order of absorption of the appellant as Headmaster of the Government High School without putting the appellant to any notice and without hearing him in that behalf - Hence instant appeal -

Held, it is not disputed that the issue before the HC in Writ Petition No. 261/96 was a limited one, confined to the grant of consequential benefits. The manner in which the order dt. 21st/22nd January, 1999 came to be made was, to say the least, not proper. The appellant was denuded of the benefits of the order dt. 16-2-1994 unheard. There has been a breach of the principle of natural justice and a violation of fair play in action. The earlier order made in favour of the appellant as early as on 16-2-1994 was rescinded without giving any opportunity to the appellant to show cause against it. Absorption of the appellant as Headmaster of Government High School by the order dt. 16-2-1994 had not been put in issue through any proceedings by any party at any point of time. That benefit could not have been taken away from the appellant without affording him any opportunity of hearing, even where the absorption as Head Master of High School had been put in issue. The principles of natural justice have been respected in their breach. Order dt. 21st/ 22nd January, 1999 cannot be sustained and we accordingly set it aside. Appeal allowed.
