Messrs D.L.F. Universal Limited v Prof.A.Lakshmi Sagar and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 626; (1998) 7 SCC 1; AIR 1998 SC 3369; 1998 (4) CLT 101; JT 1998 (6) SC 145; 1998(5) SCALE 55; 1998 (7) SLT 393; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 335; 1998 (7) Supreme 117

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, 1961 - Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964, ss. 95, 49, 148, 4, 5, 6 and 2(38) - Land Reforms Act, ss. 79-A and 79-B - Scheme for developing garden colony of country type plots having villa - Land situated near river and water reservoir that is main source of supply of water to city - By order D/- 29-06-1991 proposal of DLF approved - (A) Whether in giving its approval to proposed scheme has State Govt. kept in view interest of public in matter of pollution of waters of river and water reservoir and availability of supply of water to city of Bangalore? - Order of State Govt. takes note that revised scheme submitted by DLF involves construction of individual septic tanks coupled with soil absorption system with dispersion trenches - Order shows that reference had also been made to Karnataka State Pollution Control Board and said Board, in it reply had stated that proposal of DLF may be approved subject to conditions set out in said letter - While granting permission State Govt. has kept in view danger of pollution and has taken adequate precautions against possibility of such pollution by imposing strict conditions - Held, that order D/- 29-06-1991 granting approval to proposed scheme submitted by DLF State Govt. has taken into consideration matters of public interest - (B) Whether proposed scheme is for establishment of a township and that mandatory procedure for declaring new village as laid down in s. 6 of Land Revenue Act which postulates publication of a notice of proposal inviting objections and consideration of any objections to such proposal? - Word 'township' is not found in any provision of Land Revenue Act - Village is smallest unit for purpose of Land Revenue Act - Question whether colony which comes into existence under scheme as proposed is to be declared to be a new village or is to be included in an existing village will have to be considered after development takes place - Held, thus State Govt. was not required to follow procedure laid down in s. 6 of Land Revenue Act before passing order - (C) Whether there is diversion of land from agricultural use to non-agricultural use for construction of villas? - U/s. 95 of Land Revenue Act power to grant necessary permission is conferred on Deputy Commissioner - Matter required examination from various aspects and a composite view had to be taken after ascertaining views of concerned departments - Before taking a decision on matter State Govt. had obtained views of Special Deputy Commissioner - After taking into consideration views of these departments order D/- 29-06-1991 was passed - Held, that since Special Deputy commissioner had expressed opinion that conversion could be allowed it cannot be said that he has not exercised power conferred on him u/s. 95 of Act and said power has been exercised by State Govt. - Order accordingly.
