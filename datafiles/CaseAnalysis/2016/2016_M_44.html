Syeda Rahimunnisa v Malan Bi (Dead) by L. Rs. and another

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 703; (2016) 10 SCC 315; AIR 2016 SC 4653; 2017 (1) ALD(SC) 8; 2016 (119) ALR 485; 2016 (6) AWC 5454; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 76; 2016 (5) CTC 862; JT 2016 (9) SC 534; 2017 (1) MahLJ 934; 2016 (7) MLJ 374; 2017 (2) MPLJ 245; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 774; 2017 (134) RD 135; 2016(9) SCALE 473; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 149

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Rent Control, Relationship Of Landlord And Tenant, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Substantial question of law - Adverse possession - Parties filed in all five civil suits against one another and since all five suits were in relation to one suit-land and between same parties pending in different courts, all five civil suits were clubbed together for disposal in accordance with law - Upon perusal of evidence adduced, Trial Court passed common judgment and decree dt.22-4-1997, dismissing three civil suits filed by respondents and decreed two civil suits filed by appellants - Further, Trial Court held that respondents failed to establish their title over suit land and so not entitled to claim permanent injunction against appellants over suit land but on contrary, appellant were able to establish the relationship of landlord and tenant between appellant no.1 and respondent and so, entitled to claim the eviction of respondents from suit land and that appellants are entitled to claim money by way of damages from respondents - Aggrieved, respondents filed five appeals and First Appellate Court dismissed all five appeals in five separate judgments and affirmed judgment and decree of Trial Court - Aggrieved, respondents filed five Second Appeals before HC and by impugned order HC allowed appeals and set aside two courts judgment/decree remanded cases to Trial Court for fresh trial on merits and permitted parties to amend pleadings and to frame additional issues and to adduce evidence - Aggrieved, appellants filed instant appeals - Whether impugned order of HC remanding cases to Trial Court is sustainable.

Held, HC while admitting second appeal had formulated questions but said questions could not be regarded as satisfying test of being a 'substantial questions of law' within the meaning of s.100 of CPC and said questions were essentially questions of fact. Further, no case was made out by respondents before HC for remanding cases to Trial Court for de novo trial in all civil suits. Further, no party to appeals complained at any stage of proceedings that trial in the suits was unsatisfactory which caused prejudice to them requiring remand of the cases to Trial Court to enable them to lead additional evidence. Further, HC had not framed any substantial question of law on question as to whether any case for remand of case to Trial Court has been made out and grounds thereof. Hence, when HC had not framed any question on question of remand, to the Trial Court a fortiori it had no jurisdiction to deal with such question much less to answer in respondent's favor. Further, the main case set up by respondents for claiming title over the suit land was founded only on plea of adverse possession against State but had not claimed title on the strength of any grant or Lease deed etc. issued by State in their favour. The respondents having set up said plea were required to prove it with satisfactory evidence as the burden of proof lay on them being plaintiffs. However, both Trial Court and First Appellate Court held on appreciation of evidence that respondents failed to establish their adverse possession over suit land qua State for want of adequate evidence. It being question of fact, a finding on said question was binding on HC unless any error of law in such finding had been pointed out but it was not so pointed out. Under such circumstances, reasoning and conclusion arrived at by HC is not legally sustainable and so, impugned judgment is set aside and judgment and decree of First Appellate Court and that of Trial Court are restored and appeals dismissed with costs. Appeals disposed of.
