M/s Bhagwati Prasad Pawan Kumar v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 255; (2006) 5 SCC 311; 2006 (3) ACC 1; AIR 2006 SC 2331; 2006 (64) ALR 475; 2006 (3) AWC 3070; JT 2006 (11) SC 178; 2006 (144) PLR 76; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 255; 2006(6) SCALE 328; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 63, 8 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Acceptance under protest - Non delivery of consignments - Offer given by Railway and gave two cheques on condition that if offer is not acceptable Cheque should be returned failing which it will be deemed that have been accepted offer in full and final satisfaction - Cheques accepted under protest - Held, if appellant accepted cheques and encashed them without anything more, it would amount to an acceptance of offer - Protest and non acceptance must be conveyed before cheques are encashed - Encashing of cheques amounted to acceptance - Appeals dismissed.
