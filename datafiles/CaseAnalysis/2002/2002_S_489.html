Sanju @ Sanjay Singh Sengar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 277; (2002) 5 SCC 371; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1141; AIR 2002 SC 1998; AIR 2002 SCW 2035; 2002 (2) CCR 189; 2002 (2) CGLJ 128; 2002 (5) CRJ 625; 2002 CRLJ 2796; 2002 (2) JCC 847; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 248; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 687; 2002 (2) RCR(Rent) 687; 2002(4) SCALE 270; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 668; 2002 (3) SLT 513; 2002 (3) Supreme 650; 2002 (2) UC 334

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, ss. 107 and 306 - Conviction thereunder - Whether justified? - Considering the definition of 'abetment' u/s.107 I P C , the charge and conviction of the appellant for an offence u/s.306 is not sustainable merely on the allegation of harassment to the deceased and when, the ingredients of 'abetment' are totally absent - Appeal allowed.
