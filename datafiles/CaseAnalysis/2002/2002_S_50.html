Ram Khilona and Others v Sardar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1976; (2002) 6 SCC 375; AIR 2002 SC 2548; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 17; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1541; 2002 (49) ALR 47; 2003 (50) ARBLR 1; 2002 (5) AWC 4387; 2002 (2) CGLJ 273; 2002 (2) CRC 339; JT 2002 (5) SC 503; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 114; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 524; 2002 RD 862; 2002(5) SCALE 210; 2002 (4) SLT 417; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 187; 2002 (5) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, s. 41 - Specific Relief Act, s. 19 - Whether the High Court was right in setting aside the concurrent decision of the courts below on recording a finding that the alterations found to have been made in the agreement of sale by introducing two more marginal witnesses was a material alteration of the document? - Held, this was considered by the lower courts and was not believed by the HC - The observations of the first appellate Court quoted by HC in the impugned judgment were mere observations - HC had not discussed any legality by the courts below in taking the decision - Finding recorded by the HC based on surmise - Judgment clearly unsustainable - Common judgment and decree passed by HC on 24-09-1996 set aside - Appeal allowed.
