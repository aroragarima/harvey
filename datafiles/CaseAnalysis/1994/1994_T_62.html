Union of India Others v Tushar Ranjan Mohanty and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 844; (1994) 5 SCC 450; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1118; 1994 (27) ATC 892; 1994 (69) FLR 395; JT 1994 (4) SC 397; 1995 LabIC 1768; 1994(3) SCALE 273; 1995 (1) SCJ 1; 1995 (1) SLJ 111; 1994 (4) SLR 475; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 651; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1622

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Gujarat Panchayats Act, 1961, Gujarat Panchayats (Third Amendment) Act, 1978, Punjab Service Of Engineers, Class I, Public Works Department (Irrigation Branch)

Summary: Constitution - Service - Indian Statistical Service Rules, 1961 - Quota - Reservation - Indian Statistical Service was governed by the 1961 Rules - Respondents were members of the Service - Respondent no.1 belonged to the general category whereas Respondents nos.2 to 9 were members of Scheduled Castes - Respondents nos.2 to 9 were promoted from Grade-IV to Grade-III in the Service against the vacancies reserved for the Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes under the instructions issued by the Govt. from time to time - Respondent no.1, being senior to respondents nos.2 to 9 in Grade-IV, challenged their promotions before the Central Administrative Tribunal on the ground that under the reservation, appointments to the Service by promotion was not permitted under the 1961 Rules - Tribunal accepted the contention of respondent no.1 and concluded that promotion of respondents nos.2 to 9 in supersession of the claim of respondent no.1 was against the 1961 Rules - Tribunal, however, did not disturb the promotions already given to the respondents and directed that respondent no.1 be deemed to have been promoted to Grade-III from the same date when respondents were promoted and he be placed above the respondents in the seniority list of Grade-III-A - Request was made by respondent no.1 to the Cadre Controlling Authority to consider his case for promotion under the proviso to r. 8(1)(b)(i) of the 1961 Rules as his juniors had been promoted without declaring him unfit - It was alleged by respondent no.1 that retrospective operation of amended r. 13 of the 1961 Rules took away vested rights of the general category candidates senior to respondents nos. 2 to 9 - Whether Respondent no.1 was liable to be considered for promotion as against the promotion of persons junior to him - Held, that it was settled proposition of law that the vested rights could not be abrogated by retrospective legislation - Retrospective operation of amended r. 13 of the Rules was arbitrary and, as such, violative of arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Proviso to r. 8(1)(b)(i) of the 1961 Rules made it obligatory that when a junior officer in Grade-IV was eligible and was considered for promotion, all officers senior to him in that grade shall also be considered for promotion - It was, therefore, clear that respondent no.1 and other senior general category Grade-IV officers had a vested right under the 1961 Rules as also u/art. 16(1) of the Constitution to be considered for promotion when persons junior to them were being considered and in fact promoted - Appeal dismissed.
