Â Malik Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 309; AIR 1961 SC 1575; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rajasthan State Road Transport Services (Development) Rules, 1960, Rajasthan State Road Transport Services rules 1960

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Rajasthan State Road Transport Services Rules, 1960 - Rajasthan State Road Transport Services (Development) Rules, 1960 - (A) Whether officer was wrong in view he took that it was not open to him to reject draft scheme in its entirety? - Held, u/s. 68-D(2) it is open to State Govt. to say after hearing objections that it does not approve of draft scheme at all, in which case draft scheme will stand rejected and State Transport Undertaking may have to frame a fresh scheme in accordance with procedure provided in Chap. IV-A - Officer therefore was wrong in holding that he had no power to reject scheme in sense that he could withhold approval of it altogether - (B) Whether officer was wrong in holding that he could not take evidence, whether oral or documentary, and all that he had to do u/s. 68-D of Act was to hear arguments on either side? - Further held, in circumstances of provision contained in s. 68-D(2) and purpose of hearing thereunder, taking of evidence, whether oral or documentary, that maybe desired to be produced by either party, may be necessary before State Govt. can arrive at a just conclusion with respect to objections to draft scheme - Court cannot therefore agree with officer that there is no warrant for taking any evidence at all at a hearing u/s. 68- D(2) - Appeal allowed.
