Commissioner of Wealth Tax, West Bengal v Aluminium Corporation of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 411; (1973) 3 SCC 643; [1972] 85 ITR 167; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 484; 1972 TAXLR 1192; 1972 UJ 429

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Summary: Indirect Tax - Land & Property - Wealth-tax Act, 1957, s. 7(2) - A/ys 1957-1958, 1958-1959 and 1959-1960 - Fixed assets -Keywords??? - Written down value - Assessee (Aluminum Corporation of India Limited) whose value of assets were increased effected before 31-3-1956 was carried over to 31-3-1958 and 31-3-59 and assessee in submitting its return of wealth-tax at relevant valuation dates claimed before Wealth-tax Officer that its fixed assets should be valued according to written down value as per income-tax records after allowing depreciation according to the Income-tax Act, which was rejected - Aggrieved assessee appealed before Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Wealth-tax which was allowed - Aggrieved Department appealed to Tribunal who allowed appeal partially - Thereafter at request of assessee as well as Commissioner, Tribunal referred two questions seeking opinion of HC who answered question no. 1, in favour of assessee and did not answered question no. 2 - Thereafter, for A/y 1957-1958 was concerned matter had come up to this Court and this Court remanded case to HC to decide case afresh after remanded of case HC had answered question no. 1, in favour of Revenue and question no. 2, was answered, in favour of assessee - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, turning to facts of assessee's case, revaluation of assets was made in 1956, and that revaluation in absence of any evidence to show that it was incorrect, undoubtedly afforded sound basis for valuing assessee's assets - But then, when value of those assets had to be determined on valuation dates concerned in those cases, Wealth-tax Officer should had deducted from 1956, valuation value of depreciation of those assets after date they were revalued - Undoubtedly those assets were subject to wear and tear and there was no evidence to show that market value of those assets had gone up after they were revalued in 1956 - SC conclusion regarding valuation for year 1957-1958 applies with equal force as regards valuation for 1958-19 59 and 1959-1960 - Following decision of SC in Aluminum Corporation of India Ltd.'s case, 78 I.T.R. 483 1969 Indlaw SC 302 SC answered question no. 1, which was referred to HC in all those appeals in favour of Department and on that question this Court see no justification for reservations made by HC in judgment under appeal in Civil Appeal No. 1075 of 1971 - However, now turning to question no. 2, this Court were of opinion that finding of Tribunal on that question was essentially finding of fact - That finding was based on relevant evidence - It was not vitiated in any manner - In this Court opinion, Tribunal took correct view of scope of s. 7 (2) of the Act and its approach to question was in accordance with law - Thus, in result, those appeals were allowed to extent mentioned above - Appeals partly allowed.
