Major R. S. Budhwar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4091; (1996) 9 SCC 502; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1072; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 213; AIR 1996 SC 2000; JT 1996 (5) SC 39; 1996(4) SCALE 269; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 528; 1996 (4) Supreme 100

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Court Martial, Finding

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 s. - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Army Act, 1950, ss. 69, 164(1)and 165 - Appellants court - martialled and sentenced to death for murder - Whether death sentence just and proper in facts of case? - Held, appellants did not murder deceased for their own benefit but on instigation of their senior, who in turn was sentenced to life imprisonment - High Court did not consider mitigating factors in case - Death sentence commutted to life imprisonment - Appeal allowed.
