Bombay Ammonia Private Limited v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 417; (1976) 3 SCC 6; AIR 1976 SC 2136; 1976 CTR 271; 1976 (5) CTR 294; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 856; 1976 (37) STC 517; 1976 TAXLR 1716

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 12(3), 32 - A/y 1964-1965 - Claim for exemption - Challenged - Assessee's/appellants (Dealers in refrigerators, etc) filed return for A/y 1964-1965, to Assessing Authority under the Act, showing total taxable turnover of Rs. 6,41,031.77, but on checking of accounts Assessing Authority made an addition to turnover and assessed sales tax on turnover of Rs. 6,74,742.65, but appellants did not appealed against that assessment - Further, in year 1967, Deputy Commissioner issued notice to appellants to show cause why penalty of Rs. 5,056, on undisclosed turnover of Rs. 33,710.88, not levied against them and appellants submitted their objections and in alternative, prayed to be granted exemption in respect of Rs. 6,32,142, which represented works contracts which did not amount to sale of goods - Deputy Commissioner revising the assessment levied penalty of Rs. 3,371, u/s. 12(3) of the Act, and also declined to allow exemption claimed by appellants - Aggrieved appellants appealed from said order to Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal who allowed appeal, and cancelled penalty - Aggrieved respondent filed petition before HC who allowed revision petition in part and set aside order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, appellants not had raised question by claiming exemption, Assessing Authority had no occasion to consider it and it could not, therefore, be said that order of assessment suffered from any illegality - It was also significant that appellants acquiesced in order of assessment passed by assessing authority and did not preferred any appeal against it nor did they took any other step to have it modified - Even in objections filed by them to show cause notice issued by Deputy Commissioner in regard to levy of penalty, they made half-hearted attempt to claim exemption - It would be relevant in that connection to advert to prayer made by them which was couched in following terms, 'In circumstances appellants request was that Deputy Commissioner may either totally drop proposal to levy penalty or in alternative totally cure illegality of assessment and render justice' - Plea put forth by appellants that they did not claim exemption under mistaken impression that transactions amounted to sale of goods could not also be countenanced in view of fact that as far back as in 1954, it was held by HC in that works contracts did not involve any element of sale of materials and levy of sales tax thereon was unlawful and that ruling was affirmed by this Court in State of - SC was of view that Deputy Commissioner rightly refused to exercised his revisional jurisdiction in favour of appellants and HC was right in reversing order of Appellate Tribunal in so far as it related to appellants' claim to aforesaid exemption - Thus, for foregoing reasons, this Court did not find any merit in instant appeal which was dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
