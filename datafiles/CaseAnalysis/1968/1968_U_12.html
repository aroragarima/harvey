Â B. S. Vadera v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 379; AIR 1969 SC 118; 1968 (17) FLR 411; 1969 LabIC 100; 1970 (1) LLJ 499; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 575; 1969 SLR 6

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Indian Railway Board Act, 1905 - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Indian Railway Establishment Manual - Railway Act, 1890 - Whether the petitioners were illegally reverted to lower posts? - Held, u/ r. 157, the President has directed the Railway Board, to make rules, of general application to non-gazetted railway servants, under their control - Rules, which are embodied in the Schemes, framed by the Board, under Annexures 4 and 7, are within the powers, conferred u/r. 157, and, in the absence of any Act, having been passed by the 'appropriate' Legislature, on the said matter, the rules, framed by the Railway Board, will have full effect and, if so indicated, retrospectively also - This decision can be distinguished, on two grounds: (i) that the rule, in question, construed by the court, was held to be one, not coming within the purview of the proviso to art. 309, and (ii) the question, as to whether a rule, under the proviso to art. 309, can be framed, to have retrospective effect, has been left open - Petition dismissed.
