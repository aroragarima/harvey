Montford Brothers of St. Gabriel and another v United India Insurance and another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 52; (2014) 3 SCC 394; 2014 (1) ACC 461; 2014 ACJ 667; 2014 (2) ADJ 413; AIR 2014 SC 1550; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 141; 2014 (104) ALR 221; 2014 (2) AWC 1914; 2014 (5) Bom.C.R. 405; 2014 (1) CTC 886; JT 2014 (2) SC 488; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1221; 2014 (4) MahLJ 15; 2014 (2) MPLJ 715; 2014 (174) PLR 636; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 884; 2014 (3) RLW 2079; 2014(1) SCALE 645; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 835; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 34

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Claim, Entitlement of, Mizoram Motor Accident Claims Tribunal Rules, 1988

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988-Section 166-Claim petition under Section 166-Maintainability-Locus standi-Accidental death of 'Brother' belonging to appellant-society-Deceased, Director-cum-Head Master of school of appellant society-Deceased was Roman Catholic and joined Church services after denouncing his family-Respondent-insurance company never raised issue that deceased was 'Brother' and therefore without any family or heir, appellants could not file claim petition for want of locus standi-Tribunal decided issue regarding maintainability of claim petition in favour of claimants/appellants and held claimants/appellants legal representative of deceased-Issue as to who is legal representative is basically issue of fact-Such issue of facts could not be decided by High Court for first time in writ petition-Such issue could only be entertained under Article 227 of the Constitution for limited purpose-High Court not correct in law in holding that appellants not competent to claim compensation under Motor Vehicles Act-High Court erred in law in setting aside judgment of Tribunal by ignoring fact that respondent-insurance company not pressed issue regarding maintainability of claim petition nor it had pleaded and led evidence in respect to said issue-Impugned orders of High Court-Set aside-Judgment and order of Tribunal-Restored.
