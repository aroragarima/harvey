State Of T.N. v T.Thulasingam

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1536; (1994) Supp2 SCC 405; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1504; AIR 1995 SC 1314; 1994 (3) CCR 566; 1995 CRLJ 2080; JT 1994 (4) SC 73; 1994(2) SCALE 1065

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Identification of Prisoners Act, 1920, s. 2(b) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 2(o) and 197 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 21 - (A) Prisoners Act - Accused persons admitted their fingerprints - Head Constables taking finger prints - Not cross-examined - Taking of finger prints by them, never questioned before Trial Court - Whether case can be rejected by High Court? - Ground, Head Constables were not competent officers u/s. 2(b) to take finger prints - (B) Corruption Act - If councillors of Municipal Corporation are public servants - (C) CrPC - Sanction for prosecution - Sanction granted by authority superior to competent authority - Validity - Held, (A) Could not be rejected (B) Are not public servants - (C) Valid - Appeal allowed.
