Assam Bengal Cement Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1955 SC 89; [1955] 27 ITR 34; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 118; 1955 SCJ 205; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 972

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Indian Income-tax Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(2)(xv) - A/ys 1945-46 and 1946-47 - Capital expenditure - Appellant company acquired lease of certain limestone quarries for the purpose of carrying on the manufacture of cement - Said lease was for 20 years commencing from 1.11.1938 with a clause for renewal for a further term of 20 years - The rent reserved was a half-yearly rent certain of Rs. 3,000 for the first 2 years and thereafter a half-yearly rent certain of Rs. 6,000 with the provision for payment of further royalties in certain events - In addition to these rents and royalties 2 further sums were payable under the special covenants contained in clauses 4 and 5 of the lease as 'protection fees' - Appellant paid its lessor sums of Rs. 40,000/- as 'protection fee' in accordance with these two covenants and claimed to deduct the sums in the computation of its business profits u/s. 10(2)(xv) of Act - Said contention of the appellant was rejected by the Income Tax Officer (ITO) on the ground that the said sum was come under capital expenditure - Said view of the ITO was affirmed by the Appellate Asst. Commissioner, Tribunal and the HC - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the sum of Rs. 40,000/- paid under clauses 4 and 5 of the deed were rightly disallowed as being expenditure of a capital nature and so not allowable u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act? - Held, under clause 4 and 5 of the deed the lessors undertook not to grant any lease, permit or prospecting licence regarding limestone to any other party in respect of the group of quarries - The consideration of Rs. 40,000 per annum was to be paid by the appellant to the lessor during the whole period of the lease and this advantage or benefit was to endure for the whole period of the lease - It was not a lump sum payment but was spread over the whole period of the lease and it could be urged that it was recurring payment - The asset which the company had acquired in consideration of this recurring payment was in the nature of a capital asset, the right to carry on its business unfettered by any competition from outsiders within the area - Hence, the expenditure made by the appellant in acquiring this advantage was thus of the nature of capital expenditure and was not an allowable deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
