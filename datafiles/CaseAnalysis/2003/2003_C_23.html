Reserve Bank of India and Another v Cecil Dennis Solomon and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1074; (2004) 9 SCC 461; (2004) SCC (L&S) 737; 2004 (1) CLR 312; 2004 (100) FLR 441; JT 2003 (10) SC 156; 2004 (1) LLJ 782; 2004 (1) LLN 503; 2003(10) SCALE 449; 2004 (1) SLR 431; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement

Summary: Reserve Bank of India Pension Regulations, 1990 - Pension - Entitlement - High Court held that the petitioners were entitled to pension - Appellants resigned from service - Held, the respondents who claim that they were not claiming the benefit under the Pension Regulations could not point out any other source to which their claims could be linked. The respondents-employees were getting superannuation benefits accruing to them under the contributory provisions and gratuity schemes. The High Court was in error in equating the case of resignation to voluntary retirement - Further held, the High Court judgment is indefensible and is set aside.
