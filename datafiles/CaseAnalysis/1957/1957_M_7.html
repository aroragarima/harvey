Â State of Uttar Pradesh v Manbodhan Lal Srivastava

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, A.K. Sarkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 51; AIR 1957 SC 912; 1957 ALJ 921; 1958 (2) LLJ 273; 1958 SCJ 150; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132(1), 226, 261, 31, 311, 311(2), 32, 320, 320(3), 320(3)(c), 321 - Unsatisfactory conduct - Demotion - Maintainability - Respondent was employed in education department of State and was promoted to United Provinces Education Service (Junior Scale) - Respondent was appointed officer on special duty and managing editor of quarterly journal - Respondent's conduct was not found to be satisfactory and respondent was transferred as Headmaster but he did not join his post and went on leave on medical grounds - While on leave respondent was suspended from service - Director of Education issued orders, framing charges against respondent and after thorough inquiry submitted report that charges framed had been proved - Respondent filed first petition challenging validity of order of Govt. suspending him - After considering opinion of Commission inquiry report and explanations sub mitted by respondent, State Govt. passed final order reducing respondent in rank - During pendency of first writ petition, and after it had been heard by HC respondent filed second writ application covering same grounds and praying for same reliefs - DB of HC disposed of both writ petitions holding that orders impugned were invalid for reason that provisions of art. 320(3) (c) of Constitution had not been fully complied - HC quashed orders of Govt. - Appellant had filed appeal from this part of judgment and order of HC - HC refused respondent's prayer in respect of full salary during time which he had been deprived of it by orders of Govt.- From this part of judgment respondent had preferred appeals - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in taking view that art. 311 of Constitution was subject to provisions of art. 320(3)(c) of Constitution, which were mandatory, and, as such, non compliance with those provisions in instant case was fatal to proceedings ending with order passed by Govt. - Held, art. 320(3)(c) of Constitution did not confer any rights on public servant so that absence of consultation or any irregularity in consultation, should not afford him cause of action in court of law, or entitle him to relief under special powers of a HC u/art. 226 of Constitution or of this Court u/art. 32 - It was not right which could be recognized and enforced by writ - Art. 311 of Constitution had been construed as conferring right on civil servant of Union or State, which he can enforce in court of law - Therefore, if provisions of art. 311, had been complied with in instant case and it has not been contended at any stage that they had not been complied with he has no remedy against any irregularity that State Govt may have committed - Hence, provisions of art. 320(3)(c) of Constitution were not mandatory and that non compliance with those provisions does not afford cause of action to respondent in court of law - Appeals disposed of.
