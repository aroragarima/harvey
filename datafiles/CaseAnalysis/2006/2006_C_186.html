Messrs Alembic Glass Industries Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 420; (2006) 7 SCC 395; 2006 (201) E.L.T. 161; 2006(8) SCALE 62; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 550

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Excise - Companies Act, 1956 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, ss. 11A(1), 4(a) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173Q(1) - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, r. 5 - Expenditure on publicity and sales promotion - Show-cause notice proposing to recover duty amounting to Rs. 18, 79, 775 31 stating that appellant had gradually transferred expenditure on sales promotion and/or publicity of its product to M/s Darshak Ltd and amount spent by M/s Darshak Ltd was to be included in assessable value declared by appellant - Held, there was no finding recorded by CEGAT that assessee had any enforceable legal right - Circumstances under which M/s Darshak Ltd gave an assurance of bulk purchase and commenced progressively increasing outlay on advertising appellant's product indicated that it was package outlay or revival of appellant's factory arrived at between them as a part of cost reduction exercise of assessee - No material was placed by Revenue to justify this inferential presumption, which was also not spelt out in show cause notice - Appeals allowed.
