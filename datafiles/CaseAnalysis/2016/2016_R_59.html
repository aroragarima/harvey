Ram Kumar Gijroya v Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board and another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 178; (2016) 4 SCC 754; AIR 2016 SC 1098; JT 2016 (2) SC 414; 2016(2) SCALE 547; 2016 (3) SLR 202

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Appeal, Reservation, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14, 15, 16, 39A - Recruitment - Denial of Appointment Challenged - Appellant/candidate subscribed to advertisement of respondent/service board, in respect of selection to post of staff nurse - Further, appellant appeared, shortlisted for selection, however could not make it to final list of selected candidates - HC disposed of writ petition of appellants and directed respondent to consider application of appellant as well as other aggrieved candidates against OBC - DB of HC in LPA by board, set aside order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, the object of providing reservation to the SC/ST and educationally and socially backward classes of the society is to remove inequality in public employment, as candidates belonging to these categories are unable to compete with the candidates belonging to the general category as a result of facing centuries of oppression and deprivation of opportunity. The Constitutional concept of reservation envisaged in the Preamble of the Constitution as well as arts. 14, 15, 16 of Constitution, and 39A of the Directive Principles of State Policy is to achieve the concept of giving equal opportunity to all sections of the society. The DB thus, erred in reversing the judgment and order passed by the Single Judge. Further, order passed by DB is not only erroneous but also suffers from error in law as it has failed to follow the binding precedent of the judgments of this Court in the cases of Indra Sawhney and Valsamma Paul. Hence, impugned order passed by DB of HC is set aside and order of Single Judge is restored. Appeals allowed.
