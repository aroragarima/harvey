Mam Chand Pal v Smt. Shanti Agarwal

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 98; (2002) 3 SCC 49; AIR 2002 SC 955; 2002 ALJ 590; 2002 (1) AllCJ 761; 2002 (47) ALR 1; 2002 (1) ARC 370; 2002 (2) AWC 1257; 2002 (1) CRC 535; JT 2002 (2) SC 141; 2002 (1) LCD 331; 2002 (130) PLR 784; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 326; 2002(2) SCALE 151; 2002 (2) SCJ 53; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1032; 2002 (2) SLT 51; 2002 (1) Supreme 667; 2002 (1) UC 540B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: If, all the dues of arrears of rent as well as other amounts liable to be deposited under Section 20(4) of the UP Urban Building (Regulation, Letting and Eviction) Act 1972, had been duly deposited by the tenant by the first date of hearing, then, there was sufficient compliance of the said section. Further, the first date of hearing would be the date on which the Court applies its mind to the facts and controversy involved in the case and any date prior to such a date would not be date of first hearing.
