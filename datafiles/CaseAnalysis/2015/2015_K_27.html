Kali Aerated Water Works, Salem v Commissioner of Central Excise, Madurai

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 516; 2015 (320) E.L.T. 692; JT 2015 (7) SC 472; 2015(7) SCALE 44; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Brand Names, Aerated Water

Summary: Excise - Claim of Exemption - Ownership of trademark in third party - Determination - Appellant was Small Scale Industrial Unit and was manufacturing Aerated Water under various brand names using trade mark with 'Kalimark / M/s.Kali Aerated Water Works' - Appellant sought exemption from payment of excise duty in terms of notification dt. 28-2-1993 as amended vide notification dt. 1-3-1994 for goods manufactured in its factory - However, exemption is denied to assessee on ground that brand name 'Kalimark' has been used on goods which belong to other part and since assessee is using aforesaid brand name of third party, by virtue notification , exemption would not be allowed to respondent - Order passed by department is confirmed by Tribunal on ground that in terms of Mutual Agreement signed between parties, ownership of trademark in question no longer remained with appellant assessee and it belongs to other party - Whether appellant is entitled to claim exemption from excise duty.

Held, impugned order passed by Tribunal is against record and contrary to Deed of Mutual Agreement which has been entered into between erstwhile partners. Taking into consideration Deed of Mutual Agreement, SC held that appellant is legal owner of trade Marks used in his product in his own marketing area, Trade Mark certificates produced clearly establish that appellant had been having right of ownership over Brand names in year 1962 itself when he became coparcener in HUF firm. Further, appellant has had his exclusive ownership rights even prior to said impugned notification. Hence subsequent notification cannot take away ownership right of appellant over brand names and applying same to specified goods manufactured by appellant and marketing same within his own marketing area in exclusion of others. Hence, it is manifest that appellant has been using its own brand name and it is entitled to claim exemption. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Rights of party cannot be questioned when same is established through mutual agreements between parties.
