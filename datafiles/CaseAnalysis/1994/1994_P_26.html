S.Chandra v Pallvan Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1241; (1994) 2 SCC 189; (1994) SCC (Cr) 347

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - On 01-11-1979, appellant's husband fell out of bus and died as a consequence of injuries sustained by him - Tribunal allowed application and awarded Rs. 75,000 - High Court upheld finding of Tribunal, but on basis of perverse reasoning reduced compensation to Rs. 48,680 - Whether HC was justified in reducing compensation? - Held, HC fell into patent error in reaching finding that average expectancy of life in India was 55 years - It cannot be disputed that life expectancy in India even in year 1979 was not less than 65 years - Appellants were entitled to a multiplier of 20 - Accepting HC finding that annual expenditure by deceased on his family was Rs. 5040, Court give a multiplier of 20 to reach compensation - Appeal allowed.
