Prafulla Kumar Swain Etc v Prakash Chandra Misra and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, L.M. Sharma, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 336; (1993) Supp3 SCC 181; (1993) SCC (L&S) 960; 1993 (25) ATC 242; 1993 (1) CLR 436; JT 1993 (1) SC 360; 1993 LabIC 853; 1993 (1) LLJ 749; 1993(1) SCALE 162; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 241; 1993 (1) SLR 565

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Orissa Forest Service Class II Recruitment Rules, 1959, rr. 3(e), 5(3), 6, 9(a), 12(c) and 13 - Orissa Forest Service Class II Recruitment Rules, 1984, rr. 16 and 24 - (A) Whether direct recruits are to be considered as recruited in year in which they were selected by Service Commission and sent for training into Forest College or in year in which they were actually appointed to a working post on completion of training and whether 1959 Rules will govern or 1984 Rules? - 1959 Rules were framed under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution and they are statutory in character - U/r. 12 finally selected candidates are required to undergo 2 years training and r. 12(c) says period of training will not count as service under Govt., such service will count only from date of appointment to service after successful completion of course of training - Further, term 'recruitment' connotes enlistment, acceptance, selection or approval for appointment but, certainly, this is not actual appointment or posting in service - In contradistinction word 'appointment' means an actual act of posting a person to a particular office, thus recruitment is just an initial process and cannot tantamount to an appointment - After successful completion of training when appointment order is issued direct recruits are put on probation, similar is in case of promotees, both of them undergo probation, hence their seniority cannot be reckoned from date of their recruitment - Further since appointments in question have been made under 1959 Rules, 1984 Rules will be inapplicable - 1984 Rules came into force only on 21-12-1984 - Explanation u/r. 16 is a substantive provision, therefore, it cannot be retrospective - As regards r. 24, proviso clearly states that 1984 Rules cannot be construed as affecting or invalidating appointments already made, therefore, these appointments which are governed by 1959 Rules will continue notwithstanding repeal - Held, seniority of direct recruits will have to be reckoned only from date of appointment and not from date of recruitment - (B) Whether there was a quota fixed for promotees in Orissa Forest Service during relevant years? - Held, promotions in excess of prescribed quota had to be made since no more directly recruited candidates were available during that year - Govt. did not want its work to be hampered by allowing posts to remain vacant, therefore promotions given in excess of 1/3 rd quota are valid and there is no justification to push down promotees in seniority - Further, there have been laches on part of direct recruits in seeking remedy - List was published in 1985, nothing prevented them to approach earlier which is point to be put against them - Appeals allowed.
