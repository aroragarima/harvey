Sunil Dutt Sharma v State Government of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 678; (2014) 4 SCC 375; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4068; 2013 (204) DLT 221; 2013 (4) JCC 2718; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 478; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 399; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 694; 2013(12) SCALE 473; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304B - Dowry death - Sentence reduced - Appellant was tried for offences u/ss. 302 and 304-B of IPC for causing the death of his wife - Appellant had been acquitted of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC on the benefit of doubt though found guilty for the offence u/s. 304-B of IPC following which the sentence of life imprisonment had been imposed - Conviction and sentence had been affirmed by the HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sentence of life imprisonment imposed on the appellant for commission of the offence u/s. 304-B of IPC was in any way excessive or disproportionate so as to require interference by SC - Held, death of the wife of the appellant occurred within 2 years of marriage - There was, a demand for dowry and there was evidence of cruelty or harassment - Autopsy report of the deceased showed external marks of injuries but the cause of death of deceased was stated to be due to asphyxia resulting from strangulation - In view of the finding of Doctor who had conducted the post mortem, the Trial Judge thought it proper to acquit the appellant of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC on the benefit of doubt as there was no evidence that the appellant was, in any way, involved with the strangulation of the deceased - Proved facts on the basis of which offence u/s. 304-B of IPC was held to be established, while acquitting the appellant of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC, does not disclose any extraordinary, perverse or diabolic act on the part of the appellant to take an extreme view of the matter - Moreover, at the time of commission of the offence, the appellant was about 21 years old and as on date he was about 42 years - Appellant also had a son who was an infant at the time of the occurrence - Appellant had no previous record of crime - Hence, present was not a case where the maximum punishment of life imprisonment ought to have been awarded to the appellant - Sentence of 10 years rigorous imprisonment would be appropriate - Impugned order was modified - Appeal partly allowed.
