Bhagwan Singh & Ors. v State of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 36; (2003) 3 SCC 21; (2003) SCC (Cr) 712; AIR 2003 SC 1088; 2003 (1) CCR 173; 2003 (2) CGLJ 9; 2003 (2) Crimes 82; 2003 (3) CRJ 376; 2003 CRLJ 1262; 2003 (1) JCC 454; JT 2003 (3) SC 599; 2003 (3) MPLJ 297; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 660; 2003(1) SCALE 407; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 506; 2003 (1) SLT 461; 2003 (1) Supreme 698; 2003 (1) UC 536

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC - ss. 302/34, 396, 460 and 404 - M P [Dacoity Vihavaran Kshetra] Adhiniyam 1981 - s. 11/13 - Offences under - Prosecution case based on the solitary testimony of child eye-witness and the judicial confession of acquitted co-accused - Held, the law recognises the child as a competent witness but a child particularly at such a tender age of six years, who is unable to form a proper opinion about the nature of the incident because of immaturity of understanding, is not considered by the court to be a witness whose sole testimony can be relied without other corroborative evidence - Evidence of the child witness suffers from serious infirmity due to omission of the prosecution in not holding test identification parade and since it was not available immediately after the occurrence of the incident and before there were any possibility of coaching and tutoring him - Further held, before proceeding to record the confessional statement, a searching enquiry must be made from the accused as to the custody from which he was produced and the treatment he had been receiving in such custody in order to ensure that there is no scope for doubt of any sort of extraneous influence proceeding from a source interested in the prosecution - When a judicial confession is found to be not voluntary and more so when it is retracted, in the absence of other reliable evidence, the conviction cannot be based on such retracted judicial confession - HC, therefore, erred in reappreciating the evidence to substitute its own view for that of the trial Judge - Appeal allowed.
