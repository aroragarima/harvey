Delhi Stationers and Printers v Rajendra Kumar

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 726; (1990) 2 SCC 331; AIR 1990 SC 1208; 1990 (40) DLT 446; JT 1990 (1) SC 372; 1990 (1) RCJ 494; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 491; 1990 (1) RentLR 667; 1990(1) SCALE 319; 1990 (1) UJ 367

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 13(1)(e) - Eviction - On the ground of subletting without permission - Whether appellant had sub-let leased premises without permission of respondent, owner of premises? - Held, appellant has either sub-let or parted with possession of a part of premises in favour of Mahendra Singh who is brother-in-law of appellant and is also employed with appellant - Mahendra Singh is a tenant under respondent in respect of room marked 'J' in site plan mere usage of kitchen and latrine by Mahendra Singh while residing in portion let out to him by respondent cannot mean that appellant has transferred exclusive right to enjoy kitchen and latrine and has parted with legal possession of said part of premises in favour of Mahendra Singh - It was also found that exclusive possession was not proved by evidence of respondent and much less any proof of rent paid to appellant - Appeal allowed.
