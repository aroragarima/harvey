Union of India and Others v Dulal Dutt

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, L.M. Sharma, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1680; (1993) 2 SCC 179; (1993) SCC (L&S) 406; AIR 1993 SCW 1008; 1993 (2) ALT 4; 1993 (24) ATC 106; 1993 (2) AWC 1153; 1993 (1) CCC 503; 1993 (67) FLR 271; JT 1993 (3) SC 706; 1993 (2) LLJ 360; 1993(1) SCALE 578; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 853; 1993 (1) UJ 776

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Railway establishment Code, Vol II 1987, r. 1802 (a) - Fundamental Rules, Fr 56(j) - Compulsory retirement - Validity of - Order of compulsory retirement is not a punishment - It is a prerogative of Government but it should be based on material and has to be passed on subjective satisfaction of Govt. - No order of compulsory retirement is required to be a speaking order - Held, from very order of Tribunal it is clear that Govt. had, before it, report of Review Committee yet it thought it fit of compulsory retiring respondent - Order cannot be called either mala fide or arbitrary in law - Application against impugned order of compulsory retirement of respondent dismissed - Appeal allowed.
