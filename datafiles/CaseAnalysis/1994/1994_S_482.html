Shaktikumar M. Sancheti v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1814; (1995) 1 SCC 351; 1995 (1) ACC 657; AIR 1994 SCW 5007; JT 1994 (7) SC 718; 1995 (96) STC 659; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 98

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Cess Act, 1956

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indirect Tax - Maharashtra Tax on Entry of Motor Vehicles into Local Areas Act, 1987, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 286 and 301 - Whether levy of entry tax under Act on vehicles purchased from outside State and brought them into State of Maharashtra, valid? - The levy was challenged basically for two reasons-one, that the incidence of tax being on the purchase value of the motor vehicle it was in the nature of a purchase tax - Further local area having a connotation of its own and being understood as an area which was administered by a local authority, the tax on entry of the vehicle in the State as such was bad for being vague and contrary to the concept of the local area as understood - It was also urged that it being in addition to the tax levied and collected as octroi by a Municipal Corporation or other local authorities was violative of art. 286 of the Constitution - Levy cannot be held to be bad because legislature intended to avoid any loss of sales tax in State so long it is not found to be invalid either because of any constitutional or statutory violation - It is not intention or propriety of a legislation but it is legality or illegality which renders it valid or invalid - Appeals and Writs dismissed.
