Commodore Commanding, Southern Naval Area, Cochin v V.K. Rajan

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 272; (1981) SCC (L&S) 428; (1981) 2 SCC 636; AIR 1981 SC 965; 1981 (42) FLR 323; 1981 LabIC 605; 1981 (2) LLJ 1; 1981 (1) LLN 593; 1981(1) SCALE 510; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 165; 1981 (2) SLJ 48; 1981 (1) SLR 656; 1981 UJ 250

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Legality of - Whether termination of service of respondent by appellant was legally justified? - Held, Yes - Decision to terminate services of respondent had been taken at highest level on ground of unsuitability of respondent in relation to post held by him and it is not by way of any punishment and no stigma is attached to respondent by reason of termination of his service - Appeal allowed.
