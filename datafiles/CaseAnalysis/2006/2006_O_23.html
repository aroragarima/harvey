Lal Chand v Oriential Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1318; (2006) 7 SCC 318; 2006 (3) ACC 731; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 16; 2006 (65) ALR 187; 2006 (4) AWC 3578; 2007 (1) CCC 266; JT 2007 (10) SC 122; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 952; 2006 (144) PLR 741; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 204; 2006(8) SCALE 531; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 210; 2006 (3) UC 1794

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Recovery Of Compensation, Use Of Vehicle, Licensed Driver

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 174 and 149(2)(a)(ii) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether High Court erred in upholding tribunal decision that insurance company is liable to recover insured amount from insured on ground that insured has not taken adequate step in checking licence of driver and therefore liability of vehicle owner established is justified? - Relied on Karala's case 2001 Indlaw SC 19818, United India Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Lehru and Ors 2003 Indlaw SC 243 and National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Swaran Singh and Ors 2004 Indlaw SC 14 - Held, vehicle owner not only examined licence of driver but also took test drive with driver and thereafter appointed as driver of vehicle and therefore no breach of s. 149(2)(a)(ii) of Act established - Further held, insurer company failed to prove guilty mind of owner of insure vehicle and compensation awarded by HC rightly done - Appeal allowed.
