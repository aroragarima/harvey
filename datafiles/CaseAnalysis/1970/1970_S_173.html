Â Shri Ramtanu Co-Operative Housing Society and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 607; (1970) 3 SCC 323; AIR 1970 SC 1771; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Maharashtra Industrial Development Act, 1961 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Whether State of Maharashtra is competent to enact Maharashtra Industrial Development Act, 1961? - True character, scope and intent of act is to be ascertained with reference to purposes and provisions of Act - In deciding pith and substance of legislation, true test is not to find out whether Act has encroached upon or invaded any forbidden field but what pith and substance of Act is - Act is designed to have a planned industrial city as opposed to haphazard growth of industrial areas in all parts of State - Act is intended to prevent growth of industries in developed parts of State - Object of Act is to carve planned areas for industries - Held, acquisition or requisition of land falls under Entry 42 of Concurrent List - In order to achieve growth of industries it is necessary not only to acquire land but also to implement purposes of Act - Corporation is therefore established for carrying out purposes of Act - Pith and substance of Act is establishment, growth and organisation of industries, acquisition of land in that behalf and carrying out purposes of Act by setting up Corporation as one of limbs or agencies of Govt. - Powers and functions of Corporation show in no uncertain terms that these are all in aid of principal and predominant purpose of establishment, growth and establishment of industries - Corporation is established for that purpose - Act is a valid piece of legislation - (B) Whether there is procedural discrimination between Maharashtra Industrial Development Act, 1961 and Land Acquisition Act, 1894? - Held, policy underlying Act is not acquisition of land for any company but for one and only purpose of development, organisation and growth of industrial estates and industrial areas - Land Acquisition Act is a general Act and that is why there is specific provision for acquisition of land by State for public purpose and acquisition of land by State for companies - Under Land Acquisition Act acquisition is at instance of and for benefit of a company whereas under present Act acquisition is solely by State for public purposes - Two Acts are dissimilar in situations and circumstances - (C) Whether Corporation is not a trading one? - Held, underlying concept of a trading corporation is buying and selling - There is no aspect of buying or selling by corporation in present case - Corporation carries out purposes of Act, namely, development of industries in State - Hard core of a trading Corporation is its commercial character - True character of corporation in present case is to act as an architectural agent of development and growth of industrial towns by establishing and developing industrial estates and industrial areas - Corporation is not a trading one - (D) Restriction on compensation - Held, there is no restriction on powers of Collector in matter of determination of compensation, although approval of Govt. may be necessary in Govt. interest - Petitions dismissed.
