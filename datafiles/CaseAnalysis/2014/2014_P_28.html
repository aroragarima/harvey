Patel Maheshbhai Ranchodbhai and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 666; (2014) 14 SCC 657; JT 2014 (10) SC 569; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2014(11) SCALE 526; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 201, 302 and 498A - Cruelty - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court framed charges against all the accused for the offences punishable u/ss. 498A, 306, 201 and 114 of the IPC - Trial Court acquitted all the accused except accused no.1 (husband), who was convicted for offence u/s. 498A of IPC - On appeal, HC convicted the appellants-accused of the charges u/s. 306 r/w s. 114 of IPC and also convicted accused no.2 and 3 for the offence punishable u/s. 498A of the IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, courts are expected to perform its duties and functions effectively. In the instant case, Trial Court has failed to perform its duties to reach to the real truth and to convict the accused. Besides dying declaration, there was available evidence on record to prove the factum of cruelty and death of deceased, but it was not brought on record by the prosecuting agency. Instead, all concerned were in hurry to finish the case in a day. Prosecution submitted a list of 17 documents to be produced and exhibited, but the trial Judge exhibited only four documents and prosecution also did not raise any objection. The role of prosecuting agency during the trial along with the trial judge appears to be dubious. The HC has correctly re-appreciated the evidence on record and reversed the acquittal decision of the Trial Court. Appeals dismissed.
