Mohan Singh and Others v International Airport Authority of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1682; (1997) 9 SCC 132; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 617; JT 1996 (10) SC 311; 1996(8) SCALE 251; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 569; 1997 (6) Supreme 169

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, Mysore Town Municipalities Act, 1951, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 6(1), 9(1), 17(1), 17(2) and 17(4) - International Airport Authority of India (IAAI) requisitioned Govt. of India to acquire land for rehabilitation of 1000 families displaced by acquisition of land for Indira Gandhi International Airport - (A) Meaning of phrase 'a declaration may be made u/s. 6 in respect of land at any time after date of publication of notification u/s. 4 (1)' used in s. 17(4) of Act? - Object of s. 17 appears to be that when Govt. exercises urgency power u/s. 17(1) or emergency power u/s. 17(2), they form opinion that land is needed for public purpose - If possession of land is needed urgently or immediately they are required to have notification u/s. 4(1) published in Official Gazette and within a gap of one day to make declaration u/s. 6 and have same published u/s. 6(1) - Thus, what is mandatory is publication of notification u/s. 4(1) in Gazette - Thereafter within a gap of a day publication of declaration u/s. 6(1) is mandatory - Thereby, public purpose becomes conclusive, as envisaged under sub-s. (3) of s. 6 and Collector is empowered to take immediate possession of land for said public purpose - (B) Whether it is mandatory in such a situation, i.e., after publication of notification in Gazette publication in two local newspapers and giving of notice of substance of notification at convenient places in locality, to await exercise of power u/s. 17(4)? - Held, though compliance of these three steps required u/s. 4(1) is mandatory, for exercise of power u/s. 17(4) it is not necessary that all three steps should be completed before making declaration u/s. 6(1) and have it published for directing Collector to take possession u/ss. 17(1) or 17(2) - What is needed is that there should be a gap of time of at least a day between publication of notification u/s. 4(1) and of declaration u/s. 6(1) - Date of notification and declaration published as mentioned in Gazette is conclusive but not actual date of printing in Gazette - If it is needed urgently u/s. 17(2), even without waiting for 15 days on issue of notice u/s. 9(1) to owner, appropriate Govt. would direct Collector to take possession of land immediately - Further held, lands stood vested in State on 29-01-1987 and after lands including land belonging to appellants to an extent of 81.9 bighas out of total extent of 713.2 bighas, were taken into possession, they stood vested in State free from all encumbrances - Award also became final - Appeals dismissed.
