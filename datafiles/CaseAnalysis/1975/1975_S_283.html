Sadhu Roy v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 600; (1975) SCC (Cr) 296; (1975) 1 SCC 660; AIR 1975 SC 919; 1975 CRLJ 784; 1975 CRLR 234; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Habeas Corpus - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 3 - Preventive detention - Criminal court discharged the detenu on the basis of Police Report - Detention again on the ground that non-application of mind by concerned authority - Validity of - Held, two jurisdictions are different, two jurisprudential principles diverge, objects of enquiry and nature of mental search and satisfaction in two processes vary argument that detention without trial, for long spells as in this instance, is undemocratic has its limitations in modern times when criminal individuals hold community to ransom, although vigilant check of executive abuse becomes a paramount judicial necessity - It cannot be said, that solely or mainly because petitioner has been discharged in two criminal cases, he is entitled to be enlarged from preventive captivity - This casualness also shows that mind of authority concerned was really not applied to question of detention of petitioner in present case - In this view of matter, the petitioner is entitled to release, as order by which he was detained, is no order under Rules, for it was passed without application of mind of authority concerned - Court is not satisfied that detention order is not a cloak to avoid irksome procedure of a trial in court - Petition allowed.
