Krishan Kumar v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, H.L. Dattu, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 935; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2139; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 597; 2015(3) SCALE 263

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquired

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition of land for public purpose - Claim for enhancement of Compensation - Justification - Land in question was acquired for public purpose after issuing notification and in accordance with law - Compensation was granted to land owners for which Collector classified land in question into three categories and taking into consideration previous sale deeds - Further, Reference Court enhanced quantum of compensation - Order passed by lower Court is confirmed by HC - Appellant contended that for ascertaining market value of acquired land, sale deed pertaining to land in that very area was best exemplar and there was no reason to discard it which was produced to reflect market value of land - Further, Govt. orders relied for fixation of compensation is not appropriate basis - Whether appellant are granted adequate compensation for acquisition of land.

Held, Reference Court in discarding earlier sale deed in question does not appear to be correct. Reference Court has noted that since sale deed is of year 1993 and acquisitions took place 5 and 10 years thereafter, respectively, sale deed of 1993 would not reflect market price that was prevailing in years of acquisition. If sale price stated in said sale deed is of year 1993, value of land would even be higher in year 1998 and 2003. Hence, SC held that reason is not convincing at all. However, HC while considering sale deed in question on proper appreciation of evidence on record rejected credit worthiness of sale deed for purposes of ascertaining market value of land. Further, SC held that apart from sale deed, parties had not produced any other sale deeds either of year 1998 or of year 2003. Some sale deeds post 2003 were produced in evidence but parties rightly chose not to refer to them, fact specifically noted by High Court. Hence, there is no sale deed of area in question which could provide exemplar for fixing market value of land. Further, with respect to Govt. notification, SC held that where there is total absence of exemplars, some increase, but only marginal one, can be ordered over and above circular rates fixed by Govt. Orders. Hence, increase of Rs. 1,00,000/- per acre is granted, in peculiar facts and circumstances of this case. Hence, market value of land acquired in question is increased. Further, with respect to categorization of land, SC held that there is no need to categorize land as all land in question is used for same purpose and potentiality of land would be same for purpose and it did not matter as to whether particular parcel of land was different from other , hence, similar treatment is to be given to all lands in question. Amount of compensation of acquired land is enhanced and interest @ 9% p.a. on enhanced compensation shall be paid from date of HC judgment, in addition to all other statutory benefits available to these appellants along with proportionate costs. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Land owners should be granted adequate and reasonable compensation for acquisition of land based on earlier sale deed and other relevant factors.
