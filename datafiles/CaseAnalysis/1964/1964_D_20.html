Â Murarilal v Dev Karan

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 391; AIR 1965 SC 225; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Land & Property - Equity of Redemption - Mortgage property - Mortgaged property consisted of shop which was delivered over in possession of mortgagee after execution of the mortgage deed - Mortgage deed provided that amount due under mortgage should be repaid to mortgagee within 15 years whereupon property would be redeemed and if payment was not made mortgagee would become owner of property - Mortgagor died and respondent claimed to be heir and legal representative filed suit for redemption before trial judge - Trial Judge dismissed suit of respondent - Respondent filed appeal before HC in which HC upheld contention that relevant provision as to period within which mortgage amount was repaid amounted to clog on equity of redemption and could not be pleaded as bar to suit - Further, held that relevant clause on which plea about bar was raised did not support plea, because it was not clear that even after expiration of 15 years mortgagee was intended to be absolute owner of property - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether mortgagee was liable to be owner of property after clause end of stipulated period of 15 years - Held, amount due under mortgage deed was not paid by mortgagor or his heir within stipulated period and that would extinguish title of mortgagor and make mortgagee to be owner of property - It was duty of HC to enforce that principle where they was dealing with stipulations introduced in mortgage transactions which' appear to them to be unreasonable, oppressive or unjust - Equitable principle of justice, equity and good conscience was consistently applied by Civil Courts in dealing with mortgages and that lends support to contention of respondent that if mortgage deed contained stipulation which unreasonably restrains or restricts mortgagor's equity of redemption, Courts were empowered to ignore that stipulation and enforce mortgagor's right to redeem - Case was not made out by appellant to justify interference with conclusion of HC - Appeal dismissed.
