Anil Sharma & Ors. v State Of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 348; (2004) 5 SCC 679; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1706; AIR 2004 SC 2294; 2004 (2) Crimes 373; 2004 CRLJ 2527; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 559; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 774; 2004(5) SCALE 289; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 907; 2004 (2) UC 845

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cross-Examination, Eye-Witness

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 326, 34, 452 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 164, 366 - Applicability of s.34 - HC in appeal made detailed analysis of evidence and came to hold that there was no infirmity in conclusions of trial Court - Charge of common intention, prosecution has to establish by evidence, whether direct or circumstantial, that there was plan or meeting of mind of all accused persons to commit offence for which they are charged with aid of Section 34, be it pre-arranged or on spur of moment; but it must necessarily be before commission of crime - Confirmed HC Judgment.
