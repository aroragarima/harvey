Standard Triumph Motor Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 373; (1993) 3 SCC 315; 1993 (110) CTR 270; [1993] 201 ITR 391; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 46; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 96; [1993] 67 TAXMAN 160

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Assessee firm was the non- resident company who entered into the into a collaboration agreement with one Indian company, according to the agreement assessee-firm was entitled to royalty on all sales effected by the Indian company - Assessee-firm had not disputed its liability to assessment but while filing returns, it had filed a nil return saving that it was maintaining its accounts on cash basis and not on mercantile basis, that no part of the royalty amount had been received by it from Indian company and, therefore, nothing was taxable - Entry for the same was made in the Books of Indian company - The view of the assessee-firm was not accepted by the Income Tax Authorities and Tribunal - Hence, assessee firm filed instant appeal - Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, the Tribunal was right in holding the decision against the appellant - Held, that the credit entry to the account of the assessee in the Books of the Indian Company did amount to its receipt by assessee and was accordingly taxable and that it was immaterial when did it actually received by assessee in its country - And that in the circumstances of the case, the method of accounting adopted by the assessee firm for the relevant accounting years was really irrelevant, the very concept of 'receipt' as espoused by the assessee was untenable and unacceptable - Appeals dismissed.
