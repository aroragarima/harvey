Â Ujagar Prints Etc. Etc. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji, S. Natarajan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 909; (1989) 3 SCC 531; AIR 1989 SC 972; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 493; JT 1989 (1) SC 157; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 344

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Manufacturing profit - Assessable value - Excise duty - In respect of civil petition for clarification of SC judgment, it was made clear that assessable value of processed fabric would be value of grey-cloth in hands of processor plus value of job-work done plus manufacturing profit - Manufacturing expenses whatever those could be, which would either be included in price at factory gate or deemed to be price at factory gate for processed fabric - Hence instant petition.

Held, if trader, who entrusts cotton or man-made fabric to processor for processing on job-work basis, would give declaration to processor as to what would be price at which he would be selling processed goods in market, that would be taken by Excise authorities as assessable-value of processed fabric and excise duty would be charged to processor on that basis provided that declaration as to price at which he would be selling processed goods in market, would include only price or deemed price at which processed fabric would leave processor's factory plus his profit. Price at which petitioner was selling goods must be value of grey cloth or fabric plus value of job work done plus manufacturing profit and manufacturing expenses but not any other subsequent profit or expenses. It was necessary to include processor's expenses, costs and charges plus profit, but it was not necessary to include trader's profits who gets fabrics processed, as those would be post manufacturing profits. Order accordingly.
