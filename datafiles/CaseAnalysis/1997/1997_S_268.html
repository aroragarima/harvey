Associate Banks Officers' Association v State Bank of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1587; (1998) SCC (L&S) 293; (1998) 1 SCC 428; AIR 1998 SC 32; JT 1997 (8) SC 422; 1998 LabIC 152; 1998 (1) LLJ 1137; 1998 (1) LLN 49; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 475; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 2145

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Equal Remuneration Act, 1976, Medical Benefits, Entitlement of, Claim of parity, Equal Benefits

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - State Bank of India Act, 1955 - State Bank of India (Subsidiary Banks) Act, 1959 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Claim of parity - Equal Benefits - Entitlement of - Respondent (Bank) was constituted under 1955 Act and business of subsidiary banks, which was constituted under 1959 Act, were under the control and management of respondent and employees of subsidiary banks should be considered as employees of respondent as provided u/s. 50(2) of 1959 Act - Instant petitions filed by petitioner unions of employees of various banks which were subsidiaries of respondent bank have claimed higher terminal benefits, better medical benefits and extra increments in their pay-scale on the ground that such benefits were available to employees holding equivalent or similar ranks in respondent bank - Whether looking to nature of relationship between respondent bank and each of subsidiary banks, could the employees of subsidiary banks be considered as employees of respondent bank - Held, doctrine of equal pay for equal work was inapplicable when alleged discrimination was between employees of 2 different authorities functioning as a State u/art. 12 of Constitution - In instant case petitioners were faced with a further difficulty - They claimed parity regarding other benefits - There was no disparity in pay-scales -terminal or retiral benefits, employees of subsidiary banks were entitled to provident fund or pension, and were also entitled to service gratuity - Employees of respondent bank were entitled to provident fund and pension - They were also entitled to gratuity under 1972 Act - It was pointed out by respondent that terminal benefits in a subsidiary bank were comparable to terminal benefits in nationalised banks, where also there was an option between pension or contributory provident fund - Employees of a nationalised bank were entitled to service gratuity or gratuity as per 1972 Act, whichever was higher, which was position in subsidiary banks also - Thus, no reason to infer discrimination - It was not as if no medical benefits were provided to subsidiary banks' employees - Employees were provided substantial medical benefits, though they were not identical with medical benefits given by respondent - Responsibilities of respondent' bank officers were not comparable in view of the extent of operations of subsidiary banks - In those circumstances, if respondent has offered increments to persons joining the respondent bank, same could not be given to officers joining subsidiary banks - Respondent submitted that benefits which were extended to employees of subsidiary banks were negotiated settlements with unions of their employees - Benefits which were conferred were in accordance with agreements which were reached between unions of employees and management of each bank - In those circumstances, principle of 'equal pay for equal work' could not be applied in instant case - Petitions dismissed.
