New India Assurance Company Limited v Roshanben Rahemansha Fakir and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 813; (2008) 8 SCC 253; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 476; 2008 (4) ACC 759; AIR 2008 SC 2266; 2008 (3) AWC 2477; 2008 (1) CCC 379; 2008 (4) G.L.R. 3064; 2009 (1) MPLJ 270; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 267; 2008(8) SCALE 572; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 328; 2008 (3) ShimLC 249

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Condition, Definition Clause, Public Service Vehicle, Passengers, Cause Of Accident, Intervening Cause

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Accident Claims - Held, it is evident that the driver of the vehicle was not holding an effective licence - Possession of an effective licence is necessary in terms of s. 10 of the Motor Vehicles Act - Insurance Company was not liable to reimburse the claim of the claimants - Appeal allowed.
