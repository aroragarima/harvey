Commissioner of Income Tax, Central II v Suresh N. Gupta

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 7; (2008) 4 SCC 362; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1423; [2008] 297 ITR 322; JT 2008 (1) SC 208; 2008(1) SCALE 99; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 157; [2008] 166 TAXMAN 313

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Separate Assessment, Interest And Penalty

Summary: Finance Act, 2001 - Whether on the facts and circumstances of this case, the Finance Act, 2001 was applicable to 'block assessment' under Chapter 14-B in respect of the search carried out on 17.1.2001; secondly, whether the proviso inserted in s. 113 by the Finance Act, 2002 is clarificatory?; whether Finance Act, 2001 was applicable to block assessment under Chapter XIV-B up to 1.06.2002? - Held, undisclosed incomes have to be related in different years in which income was earned under block assessment - In such cases, the 'block period' is for previous years relevant to 10/6 assessment years and also the period of the current previous year up to the date of the search, i.e., from 1.4.2000 to 17.01.2001 - Assessee is entitled to claim that income-tax on his undisclosed income to be calculated by applying the rate or rates as prescribed in the Finance Act, 2001, but he cannot claim that the amount of income-tax so determined should not be increased by addition of the surcharge - AO has rightly imposed surcharge at 17% on the undisclosed income of the assessee - Appeal allowed.
