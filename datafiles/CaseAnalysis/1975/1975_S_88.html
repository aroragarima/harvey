Pratap Narain Singh Deo v Srinivas Sabata and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 166; (1976) SCC (L&S) 52; (1976) 1 SCC 289; 1976 ACJ 141; AIR 1976 SC 222; 1976 (32) FLR 92; 1976 LabIC 222; 1976 (1) LLJ 235; 1976 (1) LLN 16; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 872; 1976 UJ 139

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, ss. 2(1)(1) and 2(g), 3, 4, 4A and 19 - Constitution, art.136 (A) Accident - (1) Resulting in personal injury to workman - Liability of employer arises as soon as personal injury is caused - Provision of s.19 does not suspend such liability - Failure to pay compensation - (B) Total disablement - Personal injury to carpenter in the course of employment - Amputation of left hand above elbow - (C) - Appeal by special leave - New case on facts - Permisssibiliy - Employer liable to pay interest and penalty - Since carpenter cannot work with one hand disablement is total and not partial - New case on facts neither admitted nor established, cannot be allowed to be set up - Appeal Dismissed.
