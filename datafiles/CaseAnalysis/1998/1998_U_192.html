Ram Ujarey v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1298; (1999) SCC (L&S) 374; (1999) 1 SCC 685; AIR 1999 SC 309; 1999 (81) FLR 10; JT 1998 (7) SC 603; 1999 LabIC 172; 1999 (1) LLJ 804; 1999 (1) LLN 1052; 1999 (1) LLN 1053; 1998(6) SCALE 133; 1999 (2) SLJ 43; 1999 (1) SLR 76; 1998 (9) SLT 259; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 684; 1998 (8) Supreme 405

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fitter

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Board - Appellant appointed as Khalasi under Loco Foreman - Promoted after passing requisite trade test as Skilled Fitter - Appellant was reverted to post of Khalasi in Carriage and Wagon Department - Appellant filed claim petition u/s. 19 - Dismissed - Present appeal - (A) Reversion - Validity of - Held, promotion of appellant to post of Skilled Fitter is valid - It is based on trade test which appellant has successfully passed - Reversion order passed by respondents was wholly illegal and cannot be sustained - (B) Practice and Procedure - Commencement of limitation period - Held, limitation would not run from date of order, but would run from date on which plaint was returned and made available to appellant, if appellant was not at fault - Delay of two and half month is condoned - Appeal allowed.
