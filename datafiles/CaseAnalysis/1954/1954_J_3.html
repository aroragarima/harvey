Â Jai Ram v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1954 SC 584

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(3) - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(b)(i) of Chap. IX - Suit filed for a declaration that order passed by Govt. of India, retiring plaintiff from his service was wrongful, void and inoperative and that plaintiff should be deemed to continue still in service of defendant - Plaintiff joined service on 07-05-1912 - Plaintiff filed application and obtained leave preparatory to retirement - Change his mind subsequently and expressing a desire to continue in service - Order passed retiring him from service - Challenged - It is open to a servant who has expressed a desire to retire from service and applied to his superior officer to give him requisite permission, to change his mind subsequently and ask for cancellation of permission thus obtained; but he can be allowed to do so long as he continues in service and not after it has terminated - Held, r. 56(b)(i), which speaks of a ministerial servant being 'ordinarily' retained in service till 60, does not, contemplate a case of this description and does not preclude a ministerial servant from waiving, by express agreement a right to which he might otherwise have been entitled under this rule - Plaintiff's service ceased on 27-11-1946, leave, which was allowed to him subsequent to that date, was post-retirement leave which was granted under special circumstances mentioned in F. R. 86 - He could not be held to continue in service after 26-11-1946, and consequently it was no longer competent to him to apply for joining his duties on 16-05-1947, even though post-retirement leave had not yet run out - Appeal dismissed.
