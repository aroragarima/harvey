Commissioner of Income Tax v Patel Brothers and Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, N.P. Singh, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1208; (1995) 4 SCC 485; AIR 1995 SC 1829; 1995 (126) CTR 132; [1995] 215 ITR 165; JT 1995 (5) SC 364; 1995(3) SCALE 650; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 235; [1995] 81 TAXMAN 156

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 37(2A) - Finance Act, 1970 - Expression 'Entertainment expenditure' - Whether widened meaning can be extended to past period? - Held, declaration and clarification involved in explanation 2 are only for purposes of assessments - This provision widens concept of 'entertainment expenditure' by including in its scope such of expenditures which are otherwise traditionally understood as routine business expenditures incurred in connection with 'business-hospitality' - Therefore, widened meaning cannot be extended to past period when amended explanation 2 was not in operation - Appeal dismissed.
