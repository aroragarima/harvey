Shri Krishan v The Kurukshetra University, Kurukshetra

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Hans Raj Khanna, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 275; (1976) 1 SCC 311; AIR 1976 SC 376; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 722; 1976 UJ 15

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Kurukshetra University Calendar, Vol.I, Ordinance X, cl.2(b) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 18 and 115 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 17 - Constitution, arts. 136 and 226 - Education - (A) Kurukshetra University Calendar - Candidate allowed to appear in examination - His candidature, if can be withdrawn for any reason - (B) Proof of fraud - When - (C) Admission made in ignorance of legal rights or under duress - Binding nature of - Undertaking given by candidate for University examination, if keeps him out of court - (D) Writ Petition challenging order of University cancelling candidate's examination - Order suffering from errors of law patent on face of record - High Court dismissed the WP in limine - Held, University had no power to withdraw the candidature of the candidate - Where a person on whom fraud is committed is in a position to discover the truth by due diligence, fraud is not proved and it is neither a case of suggestio falsi, or suppressio veri - Held that the undertaking given by him did not put him out of courts if the university cancels his candidature - Held, is not justified thereby Order set aside in appeal by special leave - Appeal Allowed.
