Capt Dushyant Somal v Smt. Sushma Somal and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 22; (1981) 2 SCC 277; (1981) SCC (Cr) 413; AIR 1981 SC 1026; 1981 CRLJ 719; 1981 CRLR 576; 1981(1) SCALE 615

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Court Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 363 - Kidnapping - Habeas Corpus - Appellant prosecuted for kidnapping and a writ of habeas corpus filed by respondent - High Court issued writ of habeas corpus against appellant and ordered to produce son of respondent but as appellant failed he was committed to civil prison for contempt - Submission of petitioner that a petition for issue of writ was not appropriate in cases where he was also charged with a criminal offence - Held, protection against testimonial compulsion did not convert position of person accused of offence into a position of privilege, with immunity from any other action contemplated by law - Another criminal proceeding pending against appellant cannot preclude HC from sentencing appellant for contempt - (B) Whether when alternate remedies under Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, and CrPC, 1973, available and writ should not have been issued? - Held, alternate remedy ordinarily inhibits a prerogative writ, but it is not an impassable hurdle - Where what is complained of is an impudent disregard of an order of a Court, the fact certainly cries out that a prerogative writ shall issue - Order accordingly.
