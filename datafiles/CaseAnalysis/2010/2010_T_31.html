Thanu Ram v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 826; (2010) 10 SCC 353; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3647; JT 2010 (11) SC 32; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 280; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 735; 2010(10) SCALE 557; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 710; 2010 (3) UC 1889

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 306 and 498-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-A - Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Challenged - Deceased (Wife) committed suicide in the 4th year of her marriage when she was 6 months' pregnant - Petitioner (Husband) was tried and convicted for the offence punishable u/ss. 498-A and 306 of IPC by the Trial Court on the basis of dying declaration of the deceased - On appeal, HC upheld the conviction and sentence of the petitioner - Petitioner filed appeal against said order submitting that there is no evidence on record to indicate that the Petitioner had, in any way, instigated deceased with the intention of making her commit suicide - Accordingly, the charge u/s. 306 IPC cannot be sustained - Whether there is sufficient evidence on record to indicate that by any of the acts of cruelty attributed to the petitioner, there was an intention to instigate deceased into committing suicide? - Held, deceased stated in her dying declaration that she had been treated with both mental and physical cruelty and the she has also stated that how she poured kerosene on her body and set herself on fire - Moreover, evidence of P.W.13, the younger brother of the deceased, corroborates the story of the prosecution as to the manner in which deceased was treated by the petitioner, which triggered her immediate intention to commit suicide which was the culminating point of ill-treatment meted out to her by the petitioner - Moreover, the evidence of P Ws.2, 3, 7, 9, 11 and 13 is sufficient to establish the prosecution case against the petitioner u/ss. 498-A and 306 IPC - Hence, conviction of the appellant u/s. 498-A and 306 of IPC upheld - Petitions dismissed.
