C. M. Arumugam v S. Rajgopal and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 434; (1976) 1 SCC 863; AIR 1976 SC 939; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Scheduled Caste

Summary: Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order 1950, Paras 2 and 3, sch., Part VIII, cl.(1) - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 116A - (A) Election petition - (1) High Court acted on respondent in conceding that he embraced Christianity in 1949 - Question if can be reagitated before Supreme Court - (2) Mixed question of law and fact - Question whether on conversion to Christianity First respondent ceased to be member of Adi Dravida caste - Decision given in case relating to 1967 General Election - Binding nature in case relating to 1972 General Election - (C) Convert to Christianity again professing Hindu religion and accepted by members - Effect in the light of 1950 Order -Question cannot be reagitated before SC - Held, cannot be res judicata in the case in question which relates to 1972 General Election - He becomes member of his original caste - Appeal Dismissed.
