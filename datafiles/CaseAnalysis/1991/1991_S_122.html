Dr. Arun Kumar Agrawal And Ors. Etc v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 269; (1991) Supp1 SCC 287; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1060; AIR 1991 SC 1514; 1991 (16) ATC 931; 1991 (62) FLR 821; JT 1991 (2) SC 352; 1992 (2) LLJ 321; 1991 (2) LLN 59; 1991(1) SCALE 812; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 491; 1991 (2) SLR 772; 1991 (2) UJ 224

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Medical Council

Summary: Service - Constitution - Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 11- Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - (A) Appointment - Post of Asst. Professor in Super speciality, Neurosurgery, in Medical College - Preference to degreeholders in super speciality with research work or working experience over candidate having degree in M S (General Surgery) only - Constitutionality whether proper? - (B) PG Medical Course - Recognition - Course started by University with consent of Medical Council of India - State Govt. recognised it - Plea that degree obtained by candidate in such course was of no value as same has not been recognised so far by Medical Council of India - Plea whether maintainable ? - Held, (A) Was proper - (B) Plea not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
