Kishansing Tomar v Municipal Corporation Of The City Of Amedabad & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S.H. Kapadia, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1413; (2006) 8 SCC 352; AIR 2007 SC 269; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 332; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 66; JT 2006 (9) SC 320; 2006(10) SCALE 438; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 454; 2007 (1) UC 278

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation (Delimitation of Wards in the City & Allocation of Reserved Seats) Rules, 1994, Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation (Registration of Voters) Rules, 1994

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 243-U - Bombay Provisional Municipal Corporations Act, 1949 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation (Delimitation of Wards in the City & Allocation of Reserved Seats) Rules, 1994 - Delimitation of wards - Delay in process of election - Justified - Appellant Chairman of Standing Committee of Municipal Corporation filed petition before Single Judge apprehending that authorities might delay process of election to constitute new Municipal body which was dismissed - Respondent contended that it would take two months' time to complete process of delimitation as preparation of voters' list in each ward had to be revised in accordance with the Rules, 1994 - Aggrieved by same appeal was preferred before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of DB was succeed - Held, entire provision in Constitution was inserted to see that there should not be any delay in Constitution of new Municipality every five years and in order to avoid mischief of delaying process of election and allowing nominated bodies to continue, provisions have been suitably added to Constitution - Hence, in this direction, it was necessary for all State governments to recognize significance of State Election Commission, which was constitutional body and it should abide by directions of Commission in same manner in which it follows directions of Election Commission of India during elections for Parliament and State Legislatures - Therefore, matter of conduct of elections, concerned Govt. should have to render full assistance and co-operation to State Election Commission and respect latter's assessment of needs in order to ensure that free and fair elections were conducted - Appeal disposed of.
