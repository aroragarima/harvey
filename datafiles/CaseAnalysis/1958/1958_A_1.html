A. Govindarajulu Mudaliar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1959 SC 248; [1958] 34 ITR 807

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6 c) and 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether cash receipts are to be treated as taxable income under Act or not? - Held, taxability of cash receipts depends very largely on facts and circumstances of each case - Failure of assessee to prove satisfactorily source and nature of certain amount of cash received during accounting year entitles ITO to presume that such receipts are assessable for tax - (B) Whether decision of High Court in an appeal from a decision of Appellate Tribunal can be further brought before Supreme Court under art. 136? - Further held, an appeal lies under art. 136 of Constitution to Supreme Court against a decision of Appellate Tribunal under Income Tax Act - If appellant approaches HC and a decision adverse to his interest is passed - Correctness of decision of tribunal on grounds which might have been taken in an appeal against judgment of HC cannot be questioned - Appeal dismissed.
