Joginder Tuli v S. L. Bhatia and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2188; (1997) 1 SCC 502; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 181; 1998 (33) ALR 37B; 1996 (4) CCC 155; JT 1996 (11) SC 19; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 230; 1996(7) SCALE 942; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 221

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) Whether matter can be taken up by District Court and proceeded from stage at which it was returned from Trial Court? - (B) Whether appellant can be given an opportunity to adduce evidence in respect of counter claim before further proceedings? - Appellant did not aid court fees within time and thus admission of counter claim is time barred - Held, original order passed by HC directing District Judge to proceed from stage at which suit stood transferred to District Court appears to be correct since evidence was already adduced by parties - Appeal dismissed.
