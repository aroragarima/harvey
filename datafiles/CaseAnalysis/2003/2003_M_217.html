Ved Prakash and others v Ministry of Industry, Lucknow and another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 282; (2003) 9 SCC 542; AIR 2003 SC 3479; 2003 (2) AWC 1462; 2003 (1) CLR 848; JT 2003 (3) SC 284; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 294; 2003(3) SCALE 342; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1000; 2003 (2) SLT 793; 2003 (Supp) RD 532; 2003 (2) Supreme 939

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.17(4), 5-Order of acquisition by the authority, whether proper as land acquired under the Act was in the nature of 'abadi' which was exempt under relevant policy of State Govt.? - Held, the Competent Authority in compliance with the directions given by the SC in Om Prakash case, in the light of observations made therein having considered the evidence placed on record and after hearing the parties, recorded findings and held that it was not feasible to release the lands of the appellants from acquisition - In examining the validity of an order in such matters the test is to see whether there is any infirmity in the decision making process and not the decision itself When choices are open to the authority it is for that authority to decide upon the choice and not for the court to substitute its view - No good or valid reason so as to interfere with the impugned judgment of the HC affirming the order passed by the Authority - Appeals Dismissed.
