Ganesh Prasad Sah Kesari and Another v Lakshmi Narayan Gupta

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 425; (1985) 3 SCC 53; AIR 1985 SC 964; 1986 (1) RCJ 407; 1985 (2) RCR(Rent) 51; 1985(1) SCALE 806; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Rent Control - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, s.11A - CPC,1908,O.9 r. 13 - Suit for eviction of tenant on ground of default in payment of rent - (A) High Court directing that the defence be deemed to be struck off - View is founded on the use of the expression 'shall' in that part of section by which power in conferred on the court to strike off the defence - Construction of expression 'Court shall order the defence....... to be struck off' u/s. 11A of the Act - What is the nature of word 'shall therein - (B) Direction to deposit rent u/s.11A of Act - What is the effect of setting aside ex-parte decree under O. 9 r. 13 - Held, word 'shall' is directory - Earlier direction for making deposit is revived - Appeal allowed.
