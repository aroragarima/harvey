Priya Bala Ghosh v Suresh Chandra Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 160; (1971) SCC (Cr) 362; (1971) 1 SCC 864; AIR 1971 SC 1153; 1971 CRLJ 939; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 961

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Bigamy

Summary: Criminal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 5, 7 & 17 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 494 - Criminal Procedure Code,1973, s. 488 - Bigamy - Appellant-wife alleged that respondent-husband married again during her lifetime - High Court held second marriage of respondent not proved - Whether second marriage of respondent validly performed and whether thereof respondent can be convicted u/s. 494 IPC? - It must be proved by prosecution that second marriage as a fact has taken place after performance of essential ceremonies - Held, Homo and Saptapadi are essential rites-for a marriage according to law governing parties and that there is no evidence that these two essential ceremonies have been performed when respondent is stated to have married - Appeal dismissed.
