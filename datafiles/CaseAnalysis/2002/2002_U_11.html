M/S. Unique Butyle Tube Industries Pvt. Ltd v U.P. Financial Corporation & Ors

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1568; (2003) 2 SCC 455; AIR 2003 SC 2103; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 52; 2003 ALJ 427; [2003] 113 Comp Cas 374; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 156; 2003 (3) RCR(Civil) 378; 2002(9) SCALE 778; 2003 (1) SLT 235; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 666; 2003 (1) Supreme 333; 2003 (1) UC 522; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 901

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Small Industries Development Bank Of India Act, 1989, Uttar Pradesh Public Monies (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972, Haryana Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1979, Sick Industrial Company (Special Provisions) Act, 1985

Summary: Banking and Finance - Public Monies (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972 - Recovery of Debts Due to Bank a Financial Institutions Act, 1993, s.34(2) - UP Public Monies (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1972 - Recovery of Debts Due to Bank and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - s. 34(2) - Whether the proceedings for recovery initiated by U P Financial Corporation under the UP Act are maintainable in view of s. 34(2) of the Recovery of Debts Act? - Held, U P Act deals with separate modes of recovery and such proceedings are not relatable to proceedings under the Financial Act - Plea of casus omissus - Court cannot read anything into a statutory provision which is plain and unambiguous - A casus omissus cannot be supplied by the court except in the case of clear necessity and when reason for it is found in the four corners of the statute itself but at the same time a casus omissus should not be readily inferred and for that purpose all the parts of a statute or section must be construed together and every clause or section should be construed with reference to the context and other clauses, thereof so that the construction to be put on a particular provision makes a consistent enactment of the whole statute - Appeal allowed.
