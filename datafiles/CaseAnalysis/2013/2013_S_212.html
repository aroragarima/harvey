Atmaram S/O Raysingh Rathod v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 77; (2013) 12 SCC 286; 2013 (1) JCC 700; JT 2013 (2) SC 505; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 514; 2013(2) SCALE 322; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 r/w s. 34, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113A - Abatement of suicide - Cruelty - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused), his father, mother and his first wife, allegedly ill-treaded and harassed the deceased, who was second wife of appellant - Appellant and others committed offences u/ss. 306 and 498A of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant and others u/ss. 306 r/w s. 34 and 498A of IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 3 years - Aggrieved appellant and other filed an appeal before HC, which was partly allowed acquitting others and upheld conviction of appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was no injury on body of deceased and that presumption u/s. 113A of 1872 Act did not attract - Whether impugned conviction order passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC were justified - Held, there was no allegation in instant case that appellant had harassed deceased with a view to coerce her or any person related to her to meet any unlawful demand for any property or valuable security or that appellant subjected deceased to harassment on account of failure by her or any person related to her to meet such demand - Exts. 47 and 48 were evidence of some misbehaviour of appellant towards deceased but the nature of misbehaviour of the appellant towards deceased was not stated in those two Exhibits - For holding appellant guilty of offences u/ss. 306 and 498A of IPC, there should be evidence of willful conduct of appellant towards deceased soon before her drowning which could have driven her to commit suicide and that was what PW1 said in examination-in-chief on what happened before drowning of deceased - It was thus clear from evidence of PW1 and FIR lodged by him that he had no personal knowledge about cause of death of deceased but on enquiry he come to learn that there was lot of beating of deceased and no food was given to her and for such ill-treatment she had jumped into well with her daughter - Prosecution was not able to prove beyond reasonable doubt that appellant was guilty of any willful conduct which was of such a nature as was likely to drive deceased to commit suicide - It appeared from evidence of PW1 and PW4 (father and sister of deceased) that deceased was sad due to a daughter was born to her and a son was born to first wife of appellant - Those circumstances might have driven Purnabai to commit suicide by jumping into well along with her daughter - Such a consequence from mental state of deceased could not be a ground for holding that appellant was guilty of cruelty u/cl. (a) of s. 498A of IPC - Thus, presumption u/s. 113A was not attracted and appellant could not be held guilty of abetting suicide of deceased - Impugned judgment of HC and order of Trial Court were set aside - Appeal allowed.
