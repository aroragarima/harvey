Vikas and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 59; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 486; (2008) 2 SCC 516; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1356; JT 2008 (1) SC 541; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 805; 2008(1) SCALE 545; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 933

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 302, 342 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Evidentiary value of dying declarations - Held, High Court has considered the evidence of PW1 in detail and has come to the conclusion that for some unknown reasons, he wanted to oblige the accused - Both the courts were wholly right and fully justified in relying upon two dying declarations - See no ground to interfere with the order of conviction and sentence recorded by the trial Court and confirmed by the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
