Chandigarh Administration and Others v Rajni Vali (Mrs) and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 563; (2000) 2 SCC 42; (2000) SCC (L&S) 247; AIR 2000 SC 634; 2000 (2) ALD(SC) 33; 2000 (84) FLR 637; JT 2000 (1) SC 159; 2000 LabIC 694; 2000 (2) LLN 30; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 59; 2000(1) SCALE 101; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 158; 2000 (1) SLR 486; 2000 (1) SLT 268; 2000 (1) Supreme 135; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 870

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Parity

Summary: Service - Parity in pay scale - Equal pay for equal work - Respondents 1 to 12 are Lecturers teaching different subjects in 11th and 12th classes of School - Claimed grant of salary on a par with their counterparts working in privately managed recognised aided schools in Chandigarh - Claim of respondents was refuted by appellants mainly on ground that permission to open 11th and 12th classes in School was subject to condition that no grant-in-aid will be provided for additional staff and therefore claim of respondents for parity of salary with their counterparts in other aided institutions cannot be accepted - High Court directed appellants to pay same salary to Respondents 1 to 13 - Present appeal - Imparting primary and secondary education to students is bounden duty of State Administration - State Govt. provides grant-in-aid to private schools with a view to ensure smooth running of institution and to ensure that standard of teaching does not suffer on account of paucity of funds - It needs no emphasis that appointment of qualified and efficient teachers is a sine qua non for maintaining high standards of teaching in any educational institution - Held, there is no justification for denying claim of respondents for parity of pay scale and to accept contention of appellants will amount to confirming discriminatory treatment against respondents - HC rightly rejected case of appellants - Appeal dismissed.
