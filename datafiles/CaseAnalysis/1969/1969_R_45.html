Â Indu Bhushan Bose v Rama Sundari Debi and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 522; (1969) 2 SCC 289; AIR 1970 SC 228; 1969 RCR(Rent) 889; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 443

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, sch.7, list 1, entry 3 and list 2, entry 18, art. 246 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, sch.VII, List II, entry 21 - CPC, 1908 - (A) Provision of sch. 7, List 1, entry 3, art. 246 - Scope and effect - (B) Expession 'land tenures' provision of sch.7, List 2, entry 18 of Constitution - If covers tenancy of buildings or of house accommodation - (A) Held, entry 3 is not restricted to houses acquired, requisitioned or allotted for military purposes and includes even private letting out of houses in cantonment areas - Expression includes regulation in all its aspects and is not confined to allotment only - Effect of entry 3 is that Parliament alone can legislate and not State Legislatures notwithstanding fact that similar power may be found in any entry in List II or List III - It would not appropriately cover tenancy of buildings or of house accommodation - Appeal Dismissed.
