Md. Monir Alam v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2082; (2010) 12 SCC 26; AIR 2010 SC 698; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 652; 2010 CRLJ 1418; 2010 (1) JCC 534; JT 2009 (15) SC 456; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 737; 2010(1) SCALE 239; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 522; 2010 (1) UC 342

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304(11) and 323 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, s. 4 - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court, acquitted all the accused on the charge of murder but convicted them u/ss. 304(11) and 323 of the IPC and sentenced them to various terms of imprisonment with the appellant being awarded a sentence of three years on the premise that the incident was in a free fight between the two parties - On appeal, HC held that the acquittal of some of these accused was not justified but refused to interfere in revision on the ground that the proceedings had gone on for several years - HC therefore, in effect, confirmed the judgment of the trial court - Present appeal has been filed only by appellant who has been awarded a sentence of 3 years rigorous imprisonment - Held, appellant has secured a doctorate and is presently employed as a Senior Assistant Professor in the Department of Strategic and Regional Studies, University of Jammu and that he had secured this appointment in the year 1997 - His professional qualifications have also been provided to us which show his expertise in his specialty and also portrays his association with prestigious organizations worldwide in the field of strategic studies - Therefore, his conduct and attainment after his involvement in this matter justifies his release on probation - Direction given to release the appellant on probation u/s. 4 of the Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 on terms to be settled by the trial court - Appeal dismissed.
