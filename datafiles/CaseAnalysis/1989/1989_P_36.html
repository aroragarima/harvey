State of Uttar Pradesh v Pheru Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 519; (1989) Supp1 SCC 288; (1989) SCC (Cr) 420; AIR 1989 SC 1205; 1989 (2) Crimes 124; 1989 CRLJ 1135; 1989 CRLR 405; 1990 (26) ECR 448; 1989(1) SCALE 689

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 64 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 193, 201, 218, 341, 346, 384, 388, 420 and 466 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 378 and 386 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution - (A) Jurisdiction of Court - (B) Document - Admissibility - Employee involved in certain case gave a petition to his superiors narrating entire facts - (C) Appeal - Powers of Court - Charge of conspiracy to extort money under fear of injury - Absence of approver - Death of material witness, before investigation - Failure to conduct identification parade - No evidence as to accused's entering into Criminal conspiracy - Acquittal by High Court - Interference sought - Held, (A) Supreme Court cannot take a contrary view to that of HC on mere presumption - (B) Not admissible in evidence - (C) Cannot be interfered with u/art. 136 - Appeal dismissed.
