Government of Andhra Pradesh, through Principal Secretary and another v Pratap Karan and others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 708; (2016) 2 SCC 82; AIR 2016 SC 1717; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 142; 2015(10) SCALE 584; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Right To Sue, Letters Patent, Defence of India Act, 1939, Jagirdar, High Court Rules, Hyderabad (Abolition of Jagirs) Regulation, 1358, Patna High Court Rules, Letters Patent Act, Andhra Pradesh (Rights in Land) and Pattadar Pass Books Act, 1971

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 98 - Letters Patent Act - Suit for title of property - Appeal abated - Determination - Plaintiff stated that their predecessor in title was pattadar and absolute owner of suit schedule property - Further, as per certified copies of pahanies for years 1949-50 and 2000-2001 land in question stood in name of their predecessor, however, it alleged that in revenue records there was duplication of survey number in respect of same land and same land was granted in favor of State - Further, revenue authority failed to correct entries in revenue records on ground that unless plaintiffs get their title declared in court of law, mutation in name of plaintiffs cannot be effected - Appeal was filed before DB where conflicting judgments were delivered by two judges - Further, third judge allowed appeal and passed order in favor of plaintiff - Defendant contended failure to substitute legal representative on death of respondents, appeal stood abated by operation of law - Further, appellant contended that procedure adopted by HC in disposal of appeal is not in consonance with provisions contained in s. 98 of CPC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, plaintiffs joined together and filed suit for rectification of revenue record by incorporating their names as owners and possessors in respect of suit land on ground inter alia that after death of their predecessor-in-title, plaintiffs succeeded estate as sharers being sons of Khatadar. Hence, all plaintiffs had equal shares in suit property left by their predecessors. Hence, in event of death of any of plaintiffs, estate is fully and substantially represented by other sharers as owners of suit property. Hence, SC held that by reason of non-substitution of legal representatives of deceased plaintiffs, who died during pendency of appeal in HC, entire appeal shall not stand abated. Remaining sharers, having definite shares in estate of deceased, shall be entitled to proceed with appeal without appeal having been abated. Further, with respect to compliance of s. 98 of CPC, SC held that in present case, both judges differed not only on point of facts but also on point of law. Hence, Chief Justice, referred matter to third Judge for deciding appeal. Further, third Judge, after going through judgments of learned differing Judges, formulated various issues and recorded its finding on all points and finally upheld finding recorded by one of differing Judges and allowed appeal. Hence, SC held that there has been complete compliance of Clause 36 of Letters Patent of Andhra Pradesh HC and impugned judgment cannot be vitiated on that account. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Title of Property - Correction in revenue records - Justification - Whether impugned order passed by lower Court in favor of plaintiff is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, it is not disputed by defendant State that land in question was held and possessed by plaintiff's predecessor. Further, succession of Estate of predecessor was declared by authority in his favor. Further, it is admitted in written statement that in records which is prepared by Board of Revenue, name of plaintiff's predecessor is recorded as owner. Hence, title of owner supported by various documents including Khasra Pahani, which is document of title has been proved beyond doubt. Hence, SC failed to interfere with impugned order passed by third judge. Further, appellant-State have totally failed to prove as to under which proceeding and under what circumstances, suit land was suddenly shown as Govt. land. No proceeding was initiated before alleged confiscation of suit land. SC held that Trial Court recorded incorrect finding that subsequent Revenue entries do not contain name of plaintiff's predecessor either pattadar/khatadar and in all records instead of his name land was either shown as Kancha-Sarkari or land confiscated by Govt. Further, Trial Court erroneously held that even in khasra- pahani of year 1954-1955 which is important Revenue Record, name of predecessor was not shown as khatadar/ patadar. Further, SC held that certified copies of Pahani for year 1949-1958 and 2000-2001 of Survey no. make it clear that there is duplication of survey numbers. Hence, impugned order passed by lower Court is confirmed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Title of property is to be granted in favor of person taking into consideration possession of property, revenue records and succession granted in favor of person.
