Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation and another v Gopal Shukla and another

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 607; 2016(1) ALL MR 449; 2015 (6) AWC 5631; JT 2015 (8) SC 527; 2015 (4) LLJ 1; 2015(9) SCALE 567; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 423; 2015 (6) SLR 341B

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Bus, Conductor, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Alleged Corruption - Reduction in punishment awarded - Justification - Respondent no.1 was serving as a Conductor under appellant, Corporation - Respondent no.1 , while he was the conductor of bus allowed 25 passengers to travel in bus without ticket - Report being received from Assistant Traffic Inspector of area, a disciplinary proceeding was initiated against him and in said proceeding, he was found guilty and accordingly was visited with punishment of dismissal - However, Labour Court held that allegation of personal gain and corruption had really not been established - Further, it was held that it would be appropriate to give chance to workman for improvement in future and punishment of dismissal was substituted with stoppage of two annual increments - Order passed by lower Court is affirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, lower courts have been guided by perception that there was no recovery of money and, therefore, there was no corruption or embezzlement. But it fails to notice nature of duties and obligation of conductor. Even finding on no corruption or embezzlement is ambiguous and contradictory. Further, loss caused to Corporation cannot be marginalized. SC held that conductor holds post of trust under Corporation and it is expected to behave with discipline, loyalty and also maintain fiscal sanctity. Further, when first three charges were treated to have been established by adducing cogent evidence, neither Labour Court nor HC should have been guided by sense of mercy and direct reinstatement. SC held that when such kind of indiscipline causes financial loss to Corporation, adequate punishment has to be imposed and such misconduct does not stand on lesser footing than embezzlement or corruption and more importantly results in loss of faith and breaches trust. SC held that conduct of conductor would clearly show that factum of personal gain was established and eventual act was to conceal corruption which was rooted in his personal gain. Hence, impugned order passed by lower Courts reducing sentence of respondent is unjustified and is set aside and order of dismissal imposed by Corporation is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to reduce quantum of punishment when it is proved that offence involved corruption, personal gain and loss to Govt. authority.
