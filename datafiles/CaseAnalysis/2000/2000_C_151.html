Jaya Gokul Educational Trust and another v Commissioner and Secretary To Government Higher Education Department, Thiruvanathapuram, Kerala State and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 513; (2000) 5 SCC 231; AIR 2000 SC 1614; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 7; JT 2000 (5) SC 118; 2000 (2) KLT 267; 2000(3) SCALE 226; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1234; 2000 (2) SLR 670; 2000 (5) SLT 440; 2000 (3) Supreme 467; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2010

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Government Policy

Summary: Madras University Act, 1923 - All India Council for Technical Education Act 1987, s. 10(k) - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 254 - Kerala University First Statute, Statute 9(7) - Whether for establishment of new technical institutions, approval of State Govt. is necessary or not? - Provisions of AICTE Act, 1987 occupied the field - University statute merely requires 'views' of State Govt. to be obtained - Kerala University First Statute, Statute 9(7) reads there was no statutory requirement for obtaining approval of State Govt. and even if there was one, it would have been repugnant to AICTE Act - Held, obtainment of views does not amount to obtaining approval - State Govt's letter refusing to grant approval was set aside.
