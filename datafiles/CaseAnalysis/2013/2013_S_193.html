Rajendra Yadav v State Of M.P. & Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 80; (2013) 3 SCC 73; 2013 (2) AWC 1104; 2013 (137) FLR 239; 2013 (1) JabLJ 429; JT 2013 (2) SC 627; 2013 (3) MLJ 101; 2013 (2) OLR 48; 2013(2) SCALE 416; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 1029; 2013 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Termination - Challenged - Appellant and 2 others alleged to have received an amount of Rs. 3,000/- for not implicating certain persons involved in a crime - Detailed inquiry was conducted against appellant and other two persons - Appellant and another were dismissed from service - Aggrieved on appeal Appellate authority, dismissed the appeal - Appellant then preferred writ petition, which was dismissed by the Single Judge against which an appeal was also preferred, which was also dismissed by the DB - Hence instant appeal - Whether doctrine of equality applies to all who were equally placed - Held, doctrine of equality applies to all who were equally placed; even among persons who were found guilty - Persons who have been found guilty could also claim equality of treatment, if they could establish discrimination while imposing punishment when all of them were involved in the same incident - Parity among co-delinquents had also to be maintained when punishment was being imposed - Punishment should not be disproportionate while comparing the involvement of co-delinquents who were parties to the same transaction or incident - Disciplinary Authority could not impose punishment which was disproportionate, i.e., lesser punishment for serious offences and stringent punishment for lesser offences - SC in Director General of Police and Others v. G. Dasayan, 1998 Indlaw SC 285, relied on - Action of the Disciplinary Authority imposing a comparatively lighter punishment to the co-delinquent and at the same time, harsher punishment to appellant could not be permitted in law, since they were all involved in the same incident - Consequently, SC inclined to allow the appeal by setting aside the punishment of dismissal from service imposed on appellant and order that he be reinstated in service forthwith - Appellant was, therefore, to be re-instated from the date on which co-delinquent was re-instated and be given all consequent benefits as was given to co-delinquent - Appeal allowed.
