Delhi Judicial Services Association and Others v Delhi High Court and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19861; (2001) 5 SCC 145; (2001) SCC (L&S) 776; AIR 2001 SC 2102; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 202; 2001 (34) LabIC 1743; 2001 (2) LLN 824; 2001(3) SCALE 737; 2001 (5) SCJ 1; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 314; 2001 (3) SLT 755; 2001 (3) Supreme 610

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority, Un-amended provisions

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(1), 233, 309, 32 - Delhi Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1970, rr. 16, 16(2), 17, 2(e), 2(g), 2(i), 5, 6, 7, 7(b) - Determination of seniority - Un-amended provisions - Dispute was manner in which inter se seniority had to be computed between direct recruits and promotees in Judicial Service - Association/petitioners claimed relief that vacancies available prior to 1987, when the Rules stood amended, will have to be filled up under pre-amended rules - Whether temporary posts having been created prior to amendment of the Rules, is it law that those posts could be filled up only in accordance with un-amended rules.

Held, the earlier order of this Court unequivocally stipulates that those posts should be filled up in accordance with the Rules to be amended, which at that stage was only in a draft form. That apart, the process of selection not having been started and even the advertisement itself not having been issued and such advertisement having been issued only subsequent to the amendment of the Rules, it is futile to contend that the posts could be filled up under the pre-amended rules, merely because the posts had been created while the amended rules have not come into force. Art. 233 of the Constitution itself provides for appointment of District Judges and while cl. (1) lays down that the appointment could be made by the Governor in consultation with the HC, exercising jurisdiction in relation to the State by promotion from the Subordinate Judicial Service, cl. (2) provides for appointment by a person not already in service of the Union or the State as District Judge, provided he has been an Advocate for not less than seven years. The Judicial Service having been framed in consultation with and on recommendations of the HC and the Rules having provided for filling up the posts in Judicial Service by promotion as well as by direct recruitment with the rider that the direct recruits cannot be more than 1/3rd of the total number of posts, the contention that such rule violates arts. 14, 16 and 233 of the Constitution is wholly misconceived. Petitions dismissed.

Ratio - Vacancies which occur prior to amended rules are required to be filled up under old rules and not by amended rules.
