State of Uttar Pradesh and another v Sangam Nath Pandey and another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1095; (2011) 2 SCC 105; JT 2010 (13) SC 658; 2010(13) SCALE 440; 2011 (1) SLR 263; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 91

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Roster Points, Uttar Pradesh Public Service (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backwards Classes) (Amendment) Act, 2001, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Regulation for Backward Classes) Act, 1989, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1993, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backwards Classes) (Amendment) Act, 2002, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backwards Classes) (Amendment) Ordinance, 2002, Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and the Other Backward Classes) Ordinance, 1994

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Act, 1994, s. 3 - Reserved category - Unfilled vacancies - Treatment as backlog vacancies - Legality - State Irrigation Department made a requisition to State Public Service Commission (SPSC) for initiating recruitment for the posts of Junior Engineer (Civil) - SPSC, consequently, notified 945 vacancies and conducted written examination thereof - However, due to reorganization of the State, number of notified vacancies was reduced to 887 - SPSC, thereafter, gave an option to the reserved category candidates as to their choice for being considered against the 520 posts of general recruitment or against 367 posts of special recruitment meant exclusively for the reserved category - Reserved category candidates gave their option for the special recruitment category of 367 posts - SPSC, subsequently, conducted interview and declared final results - Respondent/Candidates belonging to the general category filed a writ petition before the HC - Respondents contended that the exclusion of 367 vacancies from the general recruitment was without any legal sanction and a vacancy can only be declared as a backlog vacancy of reserved category provided there was a complete selection procedure in any recruitment year and the vacancy remained unfilled - Respondents further contended that since there had been no efforts earlier to fill in all the 367 posts and declared as backlog, the exclusion of the same from the general recruitment was illegal - Single Judge held that in view of the number of posts occupied by the general category in the department, it cannot be said that there had been any choking of the general category - Single Judge concluded that 50 per cent limit as provided in the second proviso to s. 3 of the Act had not been transgressed by the State in offering the 367 vacancies for special recruitment as backlog vacancies - Respondents filed appeal before the DB - DB held that the total number of vacancies which were advertised earlier i.e. 954 but subsequently reduced to 887 were not covered by any earlier advertisement nor were part of any process of selection and, therefore, none of the vacancies which were subject matter of the advertisement in question (belonging to the reserved category) can be termed to be backlog vacancy, therefore, the action of the appellant/State treating 367 vacancies belonging to the reserved category as backlog vacancies was legally not justified - Hence the present appeal - Whether the appellant/State has unfairly treated the unfilled vacancies of reserved category as backlog vacancies - Held, proviso to s. 3 clearly postulates a two-fold restriction on the extent to which vacancies can be reserved in a year of recruitment as also the cadre strength of the service - S. 3 is clearly provided that in any year of recruitment reservations of vacancies for all categories of persons shall not exceed 50 per cent of the total vacancies of the year of recruitment in which such recruitment takes place - Under the second part of the proviso to s. 3, reservation can also not exceed 50 per cent of the cadre strength of the service to which recruitment is to be made - In any recruitment year, if the candidates belonging to the reserved category may not be available to fill the vacancies falling to the share of the particular reserved category, s. 3(2) enables the State to carry forward the unfilled vacancy/vacancies to be filled through special recruitment as a separate class of vacancy - Such class of vacancy cannot be intermingled with the vacancies of the year of recruitment in which it is filled and such class cannot be counted for the purpose of determining of ceiling of 50 per cent reservation of the total vacancies of that year - State Government, in the present case, ought to have initiated the necessary selection procedure upon due verification of the posts available for the reserved categories - State Government was not sufficient to merely send the requisition to the SPSC but to pursue the matter with the SPSC for completion of the selection process otherwise, the very purpose of introducing the roster system and a running account would be totally defeated - However, 367 posts lying vacant for a number of years were meant only for the reserved categories and they were calculated on the basis of the percentages reserved for various categories - In segregation of the said 367 posts, none of the unreserved categories would be deprived of any posts which ought legitimately to have fallen to their share - Therefore, course adopted by the State Government in segregating 367 posts for special recruitment ought not to be disturbed - Impugned judgment of DB is set aside - Appeal allowed.
