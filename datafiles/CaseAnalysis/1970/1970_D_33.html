Â Dr. M. C. Sulkunte v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 405; (1970) 3 SCC 513; (1971) SCC (Cr) 119; AIR 1971 SC 508; 1971 CRLJ 519; 1971 UJ 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5A - Permission to investigate - Magistrate granted it to Inspector of Police Anti-corruption - Since case required immediate investigation - Trap laid down by Inspector and after further investigation accused charge-sheeted - Special Judge directing reinvestigation by DSP Anti-corruption after the stage of carrying out of the trap - Effect - Sanction held was not invalid or conviction maintainable on ground that trap had been carried out by a police officer below rank of DSP - To set aside conviction it must be shown that there has been miscarriage of justice as a result of irregular investigation - Appeal Dismissed.
