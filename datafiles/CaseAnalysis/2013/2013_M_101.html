State of Madhya Pradesh v Mohan and others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 476; (2013) 14 SCC 116; AIR 2013 SC 3521; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3333; 2013 CRLJ 4007; 2014 (1) JabLJ 146; 2013 (4) JCC 2665; JT 2013 (10) SC 402; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 453; 2014 (1) OLR 123; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 949; 2013(9) SCALE 679; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 294 and 307 - Reduction of sentence - Legality - Respondents were charge-sheeted for the offences punishable u/ss. 294, 307 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court, after holding the respondents guilty of the charges levelled against them, took a lenient view, and sentenced respondents to suffer three years' rigorous imprisonment and to pay a fine of Rs.1,000/ each - On appeal, HC reduced the sentence awarded to the respondents to the period already undergone by them - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in reducing the sentence of respondents - Held, in order to attract s. 307 of IPC, the injury need not be on the vital part of the body - Gun shot, as in the instant case, may miss the vital part of the body, may result in a lacerated wound, that itself was sufficient to attract s. 307 of IPC - HC while reducing the sentence, had not properly appreciated the scope of s. 307 of IPC under which the respondents were found guilty - HC was, therefore, in error in reducing the sentence, holding that the injury was not on the vital part of the body - Period undergone by way of sentence also was not commensurate with the guilt established - Judgment of the HC reducing the sentence was set aside and the judgment and order of the Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
