Binod Kumar v State Of Jharkhand & Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 228; (2011) 11 SCC 463; 2011 (86) ALR 466; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 28; 2011 (3) JCC 1636; JT 2011 (4) SC 486; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 702; 2011(4) SCALE 109; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 13(1)(d) - Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, s. 43(2) - Offences relating to money laundering - Jurisdiction of Investigating Agency - Determination of - Appellant allegedly amassed wealth disproportionate to his known source of income and invested the same in other countries - HC, in writ petition, referred the matter to Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) and advised transfer of investigation from Enforcement Directorate (ED) to CBI - HC further held that if investigation was not transferred by Central Government, any material found by CBI during investigation, which led to an inference of money laundering within 2002 Act would be shared by CBI with ED from time to time, to enable ED to take such action, as may be necessary - Being aggrieved by HC order, appellant filed instant appeal - Appellant contended that as allegations in complaint against appellant related to national and trans-national offences, only authority which was legally and factually equipped to investigate offences was ED and not that of CBI - Whether ED had any exclusive jurisdiction for investigating instant case - Held, s. 43(2) which provided that while trying an offence under 2002 Act (which means offence of Money Laundering alone) Special Court should also try an offence other than referred to s. 43(1) with which accused under CrPC be charged at same trial - However, in written submission, CBI categorically stated that it was investigating into commission of offences u/ss. 120-B IPC and s. 13 (2) r/w s. 13(1) (d) of 1988 Act alone and presently was not investigating any offence under 2002 Act as investigation under 2002 Act was solely and exclusively within the jurisdiction and domain of ED, which was subject to exercise of powers by Central Government u/s. 45 (1-A) of said Act - No interference called for in impugned HC order - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
