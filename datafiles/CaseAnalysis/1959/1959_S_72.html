Messrs Raman and Raman Limited v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 250; AIR 1959 SC 694; 1959 AllCriR 844; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 236; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1948, ss. 43 A, 47, 57(2), 67 and 68 - Whether granting of permit to respondent no. 4 is valid and justified? - Held, Yes - The orders were made under a statutory provision - That itself would make them binding - Further, the statute expressly says that the 'Transport Authority shall give effect to all such orders and directions' - The statute applies to all; every one is entitled to the benefit of it - Any person interested has therefore a right to claim that an order passed under the section shall be observed by the Transport Authority - The respondent himself made such a claim and has got the benefits of one of these orders - It is true that the Transport Authorities owed a duty to observe the orders but that was a duty they owed to the Govt alone and that a breach of this duty only exposed them to disciplinary action by the Govt but did not vitiate their decisions - S. 43A does not limit the scope of the duty imposed by it on the Transport Authorities - The nature of the orders not intended to visit a breach by disciplinary action - These orders lay down principles to be applied in deciding whether a person should or should not be given a permit - They affect persons materially; they affect persons' living - It cannot be said that sanction for such rules can be disciplinary action - It seems to be abhorrent that judicial bodies should in the discharge of their functions be subjected to disciplinary action - It is very unusual statute which sets up quasi-judicial tribunals with power to affect people materially and binds the tribunals on pain of disciplinary action only to proceed according to rules made under its authority but gives the persons deeply affected by the tribunal's decision no right to claim that the rules should be observed - The Transport Authorities in the present case were certainly meant to administer the orders issued u/s. 43-A - Appeal dismissed.
