Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Jamshedpur

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1170; (2002) 8 SCC 338; AIR 2003 SC 144; 2002 (84) ECC 231; 2003 (106) ECR 620; 2002 (146) E.L.T. 3; [2003] 263 ITR 466; JT 2002 (8) SC 319; 2002 (53) RLT 131; 2002(7) SCALE 450; 2002 (3) SCJ 692; 2002 (6) SLT 158; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 244; 2002 (7) Supreme 357

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Normal Price, Iron and Steel (Control) Order, 1956

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s.4(4)(d)(ii) - (A) Whether the elements required to be added by the members steel plants, as per the decision of the JPC, are admissible deductions u/s.4(4)(d)(ii) of the Act i e whether they fall within the definition of the term 'other taxes' and (B) Whether such addition, which is a compulsory impost, can be considered and price on which excise duty is payable by the parties? - Held, Principles on which 'income' is to be determined under the Income Tax Act cannot apply when determining 'value' for purpose of Excise Duty Under the Income Tax Act, Tax is payable on income which reaches the assessee On the other hand, s.4 of the said Act shows that excise is payable on the price at which goods are ordinarily sold to the buyer - Principles for computing value for purposes of Sales Tax are similar to those of computing value for purposes of Excise Duty It is these principles which would apply - Further, neither JPC nor the SPC could have made any compulsory exaction from the purchaser They could only regulate prices as the powers which they derived are only those which are conferred on them by the Notification which established them - Appeals Disposed Of.
