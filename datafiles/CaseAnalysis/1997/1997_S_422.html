Vijender v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2684; (1997) 6 SCC 171; (1997) SCC (Cr) 857; 1997 (1) CCR 212; 1997 (1) Crimes 158; 1997 (66) DLT 347; JT 1997 (3) SC 131; 1997(2) SCALE 127; 1997 (1) SCJ 322; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 1181; 1997 (2) Supreme 329

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Fingerprints, Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 5 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Evidence Act, ss. 60, 145, 32 and 27 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 161 and 162 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 302, 34 and 201 - Murder - Appellants allegedly kidnapped one boy and murdered him - Pistols and cartridges also found in possession of appellants - Held, trial Judge's reliance upon statement made by PW 2 before police unsustainable in view of express bar of s. 162, CrPC - No charge was framed against him u/s. 27 of Arms Act on an allegation that he used it for offences - In absence of such an accusation, he could not have been jointly tried for illegal possession of a firearm and ammunition with offences of kidnapping and murder in view of sub-s. (1) of s. 218, CrPC and non-applicability of sub-s. (2) thereof - Such procedural irregularity caused failure of justice - Evidence led by prosecution relating to kidnapping and murder has been utilised for convicting appellant for unauthorised possession of firearm - Conviction u/s. 25 of Arms Act must also fail for simple reason that no previous sanction for such prosecution as required u/s. 39 of Arms Act was produced during trial - Trial Court based its judgement on totally inadmissible evidence - In view of injuries accidental death can not be ruled out - Convictions set-aside - Appeals allowed.
