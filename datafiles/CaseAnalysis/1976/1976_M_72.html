Union of India v Moksh Builders and Financiers Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 356; (1977) 1 SCC 60; AIR 1977 SC 409; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 967

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Tax Demand, Attachment And Sale

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property- Liability - Assessment - Recovery - Sham transaction - Defendant No. 3, was father of defendant No. 2 - Liability of defendant No. 3 to income-tax and super tax for assessment was fixed and demand was made - Defendant No.3 neglected to payment and certificate was issued to Collector for recovery of land revenue - Defendant No. 3 appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner which was allowed and fresh assessment was directed - Assessments were completed raising tax demand and recovery certificate was issued to Collector - Collector attached two houses of defendant No. 3 for recovery - Defendant No.2 filed objection that house was purchased in his own money was dismissed - Defendant No.2 alleged that they purported to form limited company/Defendant No.1 and house was sold to it with execution of sale deed - Defendant No. 1 filed objection to attachment of house for arrears of income-tax of defendant No. 3 was allowed - Plaintiff/Revenue contended that sale deed was void and house was continued to be owned by defendant No. 3 - Trial Court allowed suit of plaintiff - Aggrieved defendants appealed to HC was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order declared by Trial Court was justified - Held, it was sufficient to show that finding of trial court was justified and did not call for interference - Defendant No. 3 had failed to pay amount on demand and recovery certificate was issued - It was sufficient to justify that sale of house to Company was sham transaction to save house from sale for realization of income-tax - Defendant No.1 was dominated by defendant No. 2 and his close relations not even paid sale price in cash - Hence, no reason to disagree with Trial Court that Company was formed to transfer house with effort to save it from attachment and sale for realization of income tax arrears of defendant No. 3 - HC committed serious error of law in not examining aspect of matter - Appeals disposed of.
