Â R. S. Nayak v A. R. Antulay

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 338; (1984) SCC (Cr) 172; (1984) 2 SCC 183; AIR 1984 SC 684; 1984 (86) BomLR 365; 1984 (3) Crimes 568; 1984 CRLJ 613; 1984 CrLR(SC) 163; 1984(3) SCALE 198; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 495; 1984 (1) SLJ 347; 1984 (1) SLR 619

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Air Force, Constitution (Twenty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1971, Obedience, Speculative, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958

Summary: Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 6 - In the instant case respondent was the Chief Minister of the State of Maharashtra - In the complaint filed by the appellant it was broadly alleged that the respondent during the period he was holding the office of Chief Minister of the State of Maharashtra conceived scheme of aggrandizement involving obtaining of funds from the members of the public and putting them substantially under his own control for the disbursal of the funds so obtained - It was held that as on the date of taking cognizance of the offence the respondent was a public servant as in capacity of member of legislative assembly (M.L.A.), hence, he could not be prosecuted without a valid sanction as contemplated by s. 6 of the 1947 Act - Whether, the lower court was right in holding its decision - Held, lower court was totally erred while holding that respondent in capacity of M.L.A. was a public servant within the meaning of the expression in s. 12(a) of the 1947 Act and further erred in holding that a sanction of the Legislative Assembly of Maharashtra or majority of the members was a condition precedent to taking cognizance of offences committed by the respondent, and hence, both the conclusions were wholly unsustainable - Order of the lower court was set aside and directions were given to proceed the trial from the stage from where the respondent was discharged by the lower court - Appeal allowed.
