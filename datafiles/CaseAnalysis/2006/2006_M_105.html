Manager (Now Regional Director) R.B.I v Gopinath Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 327; (2006) 6 SCC 221; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1298; 2006 (5) ADJ 603; AIR 2006 SC 2614; 2006 (6) AWC 6220; 2006 (3) ESC 263; JT 2006 (6) SC 394; 2006(7) SCALE 151; 2006 (6) SCJ 160; 2006 (5) SLR 674; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 597; 2006 (5) Supreme 613

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-B, 25F, 25G - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 142, 16, 226 - Whether HC in a petition u/art.226, assailing correctness of judgment of Labour Tribunal on a dispute arising under Industrial Disputes Act, was justified in examining policy of RBI on a touchstone of art 14 and whether a person can be ordered reinstatement even when he was engaged on day-to-day basis and it is not established that he was working on regular post and without establishing any right to hold any post particularly when respondent No 1 had worked only for 58 days? - Held, HC, on erroneous view of facts allowed Writ filed by respondent herein without taking into account categoric finding of fact that respondent No 1 had not completed 240 days of service in Bank and held that this will not make much difference - Appeal allowed.
