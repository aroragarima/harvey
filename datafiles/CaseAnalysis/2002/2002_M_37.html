State Bank of India and another v M.R. Ganesh Babu and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S. Rajendra Babu, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 244; (2002) 4 SCC 556; (2002) SCC (L&S) 568; AIR 2002 SC 1955; 2002 (2) CLR 309; 2002 (93) FLR 853; JT 2002 (4) SC 129; 2002 LabIC 1682; 2002 (2) LLJ 829; 2002(3) SCALE 682; 2002 (3) SCJ 416; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 149; 2002 (3) SLR 427; 2002 (3) SLT 289; 2002 (3) Supreme 471; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1680

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: State Bank of India Officers (Determination of Terms and conditions of Service) Order, 1979

Summary: The decision taken by the State Bank of India cannot be faulted on the ground of its being either unreasonable, arbitrary or discriminatory as the Specialist Officers cannot claim to fall in the same class as Generalist Officers, i.e. the Probationary Officers and Trainee Officers, considering the different nature of duties and responsibilities of the various categories of officers.
