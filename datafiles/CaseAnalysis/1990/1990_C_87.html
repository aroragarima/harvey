Jute Corporation of India Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, Kuldip Singh, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 887; (1991) Supp2 SCC 744; AIR 1991 SC 241; 1990 (88) CTR 66; 1991 (51) E.L.T. 176; [1991] 187 ITR 688; JT 1990 (4) SC 346; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 340; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 340; [1990] 53 TAXMAN 85

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 251(1)(a) - Is tribunal justified in holding that Appellate Asst. Commr. had exceeded his powers in entertaining additional ground of appeal taken before him in respect of claim for deduction? - Held that order of Appellate Income Tax Tribunal was liable to be set aside and matter should be remitted to Appellate Income Tax Tribunal to consider merit of deduction permitted by Appellate Asst. Commr. - Appeal disposed of.
