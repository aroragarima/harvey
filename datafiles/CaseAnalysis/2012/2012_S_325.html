Kavita Solunke v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 229; (2012) 8 SCC 430; AIR 2012 SC 3016; 2012 (5) AWC 4390; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 234; 2012 (5) MahLJ 921; 2012(7) SCALE 316; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 251; 2012 (5) SLR 564

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950

Summary: Service - Termination - Cancellation of caste certificate - Challenged - 'X' (School), invited applications for 3 vacant posts of teachers - 1 each of said 2 posts was reserved for Scheduled Caste and Scheduled Tribe candidates - The 3rd post was ostensibly in open category and required a minimum qualification of B.P.Ed., which the appellant did not possess - Appellant claiming to be a 'Halba' applied for the solitary post reserved for the Scheduled Tribe candidates and was appointed as a low grade co-teacher - A decade after initial appointment of appointment, respondent No.5 asked the appellant to get her caste credentials verified from the Scheduled Tribe Certificate Scrutiny Committee - Appellant complied with the said direction and submitted her certificate to the Scrutiny Committee, who concluded that the Caste Certificate of the appellant was invalid as appellant's father was a 'Koshti' by caste which caste was not a Scheduled Tribe in Maharashtra - Scrutiny Committee accordingly cancelled the caste certificate of appellant - This led to the 'X' passing an order whereby the services of the appellant were terminated with immediate effect - Appellant filed an appeal which was dismissed by the Tribunal - On appeal, HC saw no reason to interfere and dismissed the said appeal - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether services of the appellant was rightly terminated - Held, SC noticed the prevailing confusion arising out of different circulars and instructions on the question of 'Halba-Koshti' being Scheduled Tribes - The position emerging from the said circulars, resolutions and orders issued by the competent authority from time to time notwithstanding, SC on an abstract principle of law held that an inquiry into the question whether 'Halba-Koshti' were 'Halbas' within the meaning of the Presidential order was not legally permissible - SC found merit in the contention of the appellant that if 'Halba-Koshti' has been treated as 'Halba' even before the appellant joined service as a Teacher and if the only reason for her ouster was the law declared by SC in State of Maharashtra v. Milind 2000 INDLAW SC 2635, there was no reason why the protection against ouster given by SC to appointees whose applications had become final should not be extended to the appellant also - Moreover, there was no reason to hold that the appellant had fabricated or falsified the particulars of being a Scheduled Tribe only with a view to obtain an undeserved benefit in the matter of appointment as a Teacher - There was, therefore, no reason why the benefit of protection against ouster should not be extended to appellant - Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
