Amba Bai and Others v Gopal and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20130; (2001) 5 SCC 570; AIR 2001 SC 2003; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 130; 2002 (48) ALR 351; 2001 (2) CGLJ 183; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 223; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 169; 2001(4) SCALE 41; 2001 (3) SCJ 271; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 551; 2001 (4) SLT 213; 2001 (4) Supreme 165; 2001 (2) UC 104

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Abatement, Effect Of

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, rr. 3, 9, 11 and 22 - Defendant died during pendency of second appeal and no LRs brought on record - High Court held that decree passed in second appellate court was a nullity as it had been passed against a dead person and this decree had merged with decree passed in the first appellate court - When there was abatement of second appeal, could there be a merger of same with decree passed by First Appellate Court? - Held, no - There is no question of application of doctrine of merger - Defendant died during pendency of appeal, and in absence of his LRs having taken any steps to prosecute second appeal, decree passed by First Appellate Court must be deemed to have become final - Further held, failure on the part of LRs of defendant to get themselves impleaded in the second appeal and pursue the matter further shall not adversely affect plaintiff decree-holder as it would be against the mandate of O. 22, r. 9, CPC, 1908 - Appeal allowed.
