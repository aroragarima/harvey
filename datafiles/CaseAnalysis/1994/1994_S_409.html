Suraj Pal v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 549; (1995) 2 SCC 64; (1995) SCC (Cr) 313; 1995 (1) CCR 14; 1994 (3) Crimes 862; JT 1994 (7) SC 352; 1994(4) SCALE 897; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 373

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Risk

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 396, 397 and 412 - Dacoity - Test identification parade - Acceptability - Holding of identification parades to determine whether an unknown person, accused of an offence, is really the culprit or not, to be identified as such by those who claimed to be the eye witnesses of the occurrence so that they would be able to identify the culprit if produced before them by recalling the impressions of his features left on their mind - Held, appellants declined to submit themselves for test identification parade - Dock identification was not proper and should not be accepted - Contention of appellants shown by the police to the witnesses, earlier, is rejected - Appeals dismissed.
