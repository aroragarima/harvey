Kukapalli Mohan Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 529; (2013) 11 SCC 713; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1460; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 923; 2012(12) SCALE 71; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 1086

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Illicit intimacy - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly committed murder of deceased on suspicion that deceased was having illicit intimacy with his wife - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC dismissed said appeal and confirmed conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that evidence of PW nos. 2 and 3 could not be believed since they were interested witnesses as such, their testimony could be considered only with suspicion - Further contended that omission to mention names of eye-witnesses in FIR and unexplained delay in despatch of FIR would throw serious doubt on prosecution case - Appellant also contended that prosecution miserably failed to prove alleged motive for commission of offence and, as such, appellant be given benefit of doubt - Whether prosecution proved guilty of appellant beyond all reasonable doubt - Held, pw nos. 2 and 3 were crucial witnesses to establish that it was the accused who had committed the crime, evidence of PW 2 was trustworthy and there was no reason to disbelieve that she was implicating accused and she had no motive to do so as well - There was no necessity of the detailed narration of the incident, as to how PW nos. 2 and 3 saw it, in the FIR - PW nos. 2 and 3 after all were not the authors of the complaint and their statements could not be disbelieved on the ground that their finding accused on the scene of occurrence with an axe, had not found any place in the FIR -Further, direct evidence of illicit intimacy could not always be expected, but, taken into consideration of the evidence of PW no. 5 and PW nos. 8 and 9, the prosecution could establish that accused had a grudge or ill-feeling towards deceased that led him to commit the murder - Prosecution proved that axe was seized from the scene of occurrence by PW no. 15, in the presence of PW nos. 1 and 11 - Further, there was no inordinate delay in informing the incident to the police, there was sufficient explanation for the delay of 10 hours in intimating the offence to the police and prosecution had succeeded in establishing the guilt of the accused beyond all reasonable doubt - Hence, appeal dismissed.
