Modern School, Mahavir Senior Model School, Mater v Union of India and Others, Government of Nct of

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 325; (2004) 5 SCC 583; AIR 2004 SC 2236; 2004 (4) ALD(SC) 50; 2004 (111) DLT 317; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 113; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 362; 2004(5) SCALE 170; 2004 (3) SLR 693; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 668; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 2012

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Education - Delhi School Education Act, 1973, ss. 10, 15, 17(3), 18(4), 18(4)(a), 18(4)(b), 18(5), 2(v), 24(3), 28, 4, 5, 6 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 142, 19(1)(g), 19(6), 21, 26, 30(1) - Delhi School Education Rules, 1973, rr. 151, 157, 172, 172(1), 172(2), 173, 174, 175, 176, 177, 177(1), 177(1)(b), 50, 59 - Whether Director of Education has authority to regulate quantum of fees charged by un-aided schools u/s. 17(3) of Delhi School Education Act - Whether order passed by Director u/s. 24(3) of Act is contrary to rule 177 - Whether managements of recognised unaided schools entitled to set-up a Development Fund Account under provisions of Delhi School Education Act, 1973 - Held, Director has authority to regulate fees u/s. 17(3) of Act - Fees shall be collected from student only for school and not for trust or society - Noconflict between rule 177 and cl 8 - On account of increased cost due to inflation, management is entitled to create Development Fund Account - Appeal disposed of.
