Gursharan Singh and Others Etc v New Delhi Municipal Committee and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3453; (1996) 2 SCC 459; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 48; AIR 1996 SC 1175; AIR 1996 SCW 749; 1996 (1) CLT 307; JT 1996 (1) SC 647; 1996(1) SCALE 615; 1996 (2) SCJ 153; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1154; 1996 (1) Supreme 683; 1996 (1) UJ 628

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities and Local Government - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Reasonable classification - Shopping complex of Municipality - Allotment of shop - Stall holders running shops on certain land for more than 3 decades - Land underneath shops required for road widening - (A) Whether reasonable to allot to such stall holders of shops in shopping complex at concessional rate and without trade zone restriction ? - No - (B) Whether equality clause can be invoked to perpetuate an illegal or irregular order? - Held, it is a positive concept - Cannot be enforced in negative manner - Cannot be invoked to perpetuate an illegal order - Appeal dismissed.
