Kripal and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1954 SC 706; 1954 CRLJ 1757

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302 and 323 r/w 149 - Sessions Judge acquitted ten persons of all charges, but three appellants though acquitted in respect of charge u/s. 302 were convicted u/s. 304(1) and 323 r/w s. 34 - Appeal in respect of their convictions and sentences was dismissed - Held, no common intention to kill deceased can be attributed to three appellants, only common intention that can be attributed to all three appellants in so far as assault is concerned is the common intention to beat the deceased which were likely to produce grievous injuries - All three would be guilty in respect of their assault for an offence u/s. 326 while only one accused alone would be guilty in respect of offence u/s. 302 - Conviction u/s. 302 set aside and that of only one accused maintained - Order accordingly.
