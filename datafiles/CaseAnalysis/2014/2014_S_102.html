Shlok Bhardwaj v Runika Bhardwaj and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, S.J. Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 847; (2015) 2 SCC 721; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 791; JT 2014 (13) SC 514; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 475; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 252; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 249; 2014(13) SCALE 729

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3 and 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 498A and 506 - Matrimonial dispute - Divorce by mutual consent - Appellant (Husband) filed a divorce petition - Respondent No. 1 (Wife) lodged FIR making allegations of cruelty against the appellant - Husband and four of his family members were tried u/ss. 498-A, 406 and 506 of IPC and ss. 3 /4 of Act before the Judicial Magistrate - Trial Court acquitted all the appellant - Family Court passed the decree of divorce - Against the Order of the Magistrate acquitting the appellant and his family members, the Respondent had preferred Criminal Revision petition - HC had allowed said revision petition filed by the respondent and set aside the order passed by Magistrate - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it was clear from perusal of the impugned order of the HC that the development of settlement between the parties during pendency of the revision petition has not even been adverted to the HC. Wife after settling the matter, estopped from continuing the proceedings. HC, in exercise of revisional jurisdiction was not justified in interfering with the order of acquittal particularly when the parties had reached the settlement before this Court on the basis of which divorce by mutual consent was granted by the Family Court which fact was placed on record of the HC. Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside and order of the Magistrate was restored. Appeal allowed.

.
