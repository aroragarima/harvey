Nabam Rebia and Bamang Felix v Deputy Speaker and others

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Pinaki Chandra Ghose, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 965; 2016(6) SCALE 506

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Arbitrariness, Election Commission, Jurisdiction, Speaker, Special Provision, State Legislature, Bills, Government of India Act, 1935, Elections, Conduct Of Business, Rules Of Procedure, Constitutionality, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Legislative Assembly, Constitution (Fifty-Second Amendment) Act, 1985, Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, 1992, India (Central Government and Legislature) Act, 1946, India (Provisional Constitution) Order, 1947, Punjab Regional Committees Order, 1957, Conduct of Business Rules, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Constitution (Thirteenth Amendment) Act, 1962, State of Maharashtra (Special Responsibility of Governor for Vidarbha, Marathwada and the rest of Maharashtra) Order, 1994, State of Gujarat (Special Responsibility of Governor for Kutch) Order, 1977, Manipur Legislative Assembly Hill (Areas Committee) Order, 1972, Andhra Pradesh Regional Committee Order, 1958, Punjab Legislature (Regulation of Procedure in relation to Financial Business) Ordinance, 1968, Constitution (Amendment) Act, 1988, Arunachal Pradesh Legislative Assembly (Disqualification on Ground of Defection) Rules, 1987

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 163, 174, 175, 179, 356 - Resolution for Removal of Speaker - Exercise of Power Challenged - Governor passed order and message thereby proceedings of house for 6th session were prepond in view of representation of 13 MLA' of opposite party for removal of appellant/speaker of assembly -Governor authrorized deputy speaker to conduct proceedings of house - Speaker passed disqualification order of 14 MLA's which includes deputy speaker in representation by president of ruling party against breach of sincerity and commitment towards party - Deputy speaker held disqualification order of MLA's as void - HC passed interim order in writ petition by speaker against actions of Governor and matter was listed before another single bench of HC by order of acting Chief justice of HC - HC dismissed writ petitions of speaker as well two MLAs - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, art. 163 of Constitution, carves out the way in which the power of the Governor must be exercised. Governor is mandated to discharge his functions in consonance with the aid and advice of the 'Council of Ministers with the Chief Minister at the head'. The only exception to the above position was in situations, where an express provision of the Constitution, required the Governor to exercise his functions in his own discretion/judgment such as to give assent or withhold or refer a Bill for Presidential assent, the appointment of the Chief Minister, dismissal of a govt. which has lost confidence but refuses to quit, since the Chief Minister holds office during the pleasure of the Governor; dissolution of the House u/art. 174; Governor's report u/art. 356 of Constitution. However the area for the exercise of discretion is limited and even in this limited area, his choice of action dictated by reason, activated by good faith and tempered by caution. In ordinary circumstances during the period when the Chief Minister and his Council of Ministers enjoy the confidence of the majority of the House, the power vested with the Governor u/art. 174, to summon, prorogue and dissolve the House(s) must be exercised in consonance with the aid and advice of the Chief Minister and his Council of Ministers. However, only in a situation, where the govt. in power on the holding of such floor test is seen to have lost the confidence of the majority, it would be open to the Governor to exercise the powers vested with him u/art. 174 at his own, and without any aid and advice. The art. 175 of Constitution deals with right of Governor to address and send messages to the House or Houses. However, the messages addressed by the Governor to the Assembly, must abide by the mandate contained in art. 163(1), namely, that the same can only be addressed to the State Legislature, on the aid and advice of the Council of Ministers with the Chief Minister as the head. Further, art. 179 of Constitution provides for with vacation and resignation of, and removal from, the offices of Speaker and Deputy Speaker and u/art. 179 (c) may be removed from his office by a resolution of the Assembly passed by a majority of all the then members of the Assembly. The same would depend on the result of the vote cast by CM and Council of Minister on the notice of resolution for removal of speaker. The Governor has no role whatsoever in the removal of the Speaker (or the Deputy Speaker). Thus, no role direct or indirect can be assumed by the Governor, u/art. 179(c) of Constitution. The difficulty arises only, if the disqualification petition is taken up first, and the motion for the removal of the Speaker is taken up thereafter. Constitutional purpose and Constitutional harmony would be maintained and preserved, if a Speaker refrains from adjudication of a petition for disqualification under the Tenth Schedule, whilst his own position, as Speaker, is under challenge. This would also, allow the two provisions (art. 179(c), and the Tenth Schedule) to operate in their individual Constitutional space, without encroaching on the other. Hence, the Governor must remain aloof from any disagreement, discord, disharmony, discontent or dissension, within individual political parties. The provisions of the Constitution do not enjoin upon the Governor, the authority to resolve disputes within a political party, or between rival political parties. The action of the Governor, in bringing the aforesaid factual position to the notice of the President, in his monthly communications, may well have been justified for drawing the President's attention to the political scenario of the State. Hence, impugned order and message passed by Governor are violative of art. 163 r/w art. 174 of Constitution and the same are set aside. Further, all steps taken by assembly pursuant to Governor's order and message are also set aside. The status quo ante as it prevailed on dt. 15-12-2015 is restored. Appeals allowed.
