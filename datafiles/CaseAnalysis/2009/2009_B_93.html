State of Madhya Pradesh v Basodi

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 575; (2009) 12 SCC 318; AIR 2009 SC 3081; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3470; 2009 CRLJ 4284; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 842; 2010 (1) RLW 259; 2009(6) SCALE 353; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 1166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Mitigating Factor

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, chap. XVI, s. 376 - Punishment for rape - Conviction and sentence by Trial Court - In appeal High Court reduced the sentence of imprisonment to the period already undergone - Judgement of HC whether proper and reasoned? - Gravity of offence of 'rape' and its consequences on victim deliberated upon - Provisions and its genesis further deliberated upon - Reduction of sentence by High Court and requirements to be complied with - Such discretion has to be exercised after recording 'adequate and special reasons' - What is applicable to trial Courts regarding recording reasons for a departure from minimum sentence is equally applicable to HC - In instant case only reason indicated was that accused belonged to rural areas, was an illiterate labourer and belonged to Schedule Tribe - Reason neither adequate nor special - Trial Court's order restored - HC's judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
