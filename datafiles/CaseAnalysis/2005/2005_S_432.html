Sudesh Kumar v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1403; (2005) 11 SCC 525

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 (2) - Dismissed from service - Dispensing of inquiry - Appellant, Head Constable was dismissed from service by resorting to the provisions of art. 311(2)(b) of the Constitution, dispensing with the inquiry - Appellant challenged said order before the disciplinary authority which was dismissed - Appellate Authority also dismissed appeal filed against said order - HC upheld the order of the Appellate Authority - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether appellant was rightly dismissed from service? - Held, an inquiry u/art. 311(2) of Constitution was a rule and dispensing with the inquiry was an exception - The authority dispensing with the inquiry u/art. 311(2) (b) of the Constitution must satisfy for reasons to be recorded that it was not reasonably practicable to hold an inquiry - A reading of the termination order would clearly shows that no reasons whatsoever have been assigned as to why it was not reasonably practicable to hold an inquiry - Hence, appellant was not rightly dismissed from service - Appeal allowed.
