Delhi Administration v Gurdip Singh Uban and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 416; (2000) 7 SCC 296; AIR 2000 SC 3737; JT 2000 (9) SC 245; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 45; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 368; 2000(5) SCALE 651; 2000 (6) SLT 477; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 496; 2000 (6) Supreme 58

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Soldiers (Litigation) Act, 1925

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5-A, 6 and 48 - Applicant is respondent in main appeals, who questioned acquisition of his land - (A) Whether a party who had lost his case in civil appeal could be permitted to bypass procedure of circulation in review matters and adopt method of filing applications 'clarification', 'modification' or 'recall' of said order in civil appeals? - Whether such applications could be filed even after dismissal of review applications? - If parties file review petitions indiscriminately, time of Court is unnecessarily wasted - Unless otherwise ordered by Court, an application for review shall be disposed of by circulation without any oral arguments, but petitioner may supplement his petition by additional written arguments - Held, by describing an application as one for 'clarification' or 'modification', though it is really one of review, a party cannot be permitted to circumvent or bypass circulation procedure and indirectly obtain a hearing in open court - (B) Whether u/s. 6 of 1894 Act, while dealing with an inquiry report u/s. 5-A, Govt. is required to give elaborate reasons? - To what extent could a person who had not filed objection in s. 5-A inquiry challenge s. 6 declaration? - Further held, s. 6 specifies manner in which declaration should be made, if it was so made, it was conclusive - If satisfaction was challenged, it would be sufficient if such satisfaction is proved by producing record on basis of which s. 6 declaration was issued - S. 5-A provides for 3 objections - So far as objection (i) is concerned, even in case objections are not filed, affected party can challenge in Court that purpose was not a public purpose - But in (ii) and (iii) types of objections, there is a personal element which has to be pleaded in s. 5-A inquiry and if objections have not been filed, notification must be conclusive proof that said person had 'waived' all objections which were personal and which he could have raised - (C) Whether IA filed by X for 'recall' of order in civil appeal is to be considered in open Court even though her review petition is pending before this Court and is yet to be circulated? - Further held, by describing this application as one for 'recall' of order in civil appeals, applicant cannot be allowed to circumvent O. XL, r. 3, obtain personal hearing in open court of issues which Court has to consider in chambers where no oral hearing is permitted - Application dismissed.
