State of Haryana and Others v Champa Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2141; (2002) 10 SCC 78; (2003) SCC (L&S) 74; 2002 (2) JhrCR(SC) 47; JT 2002 (1) SC 400; 2002 (2) LLN 399; 2002 (2) SLR 1; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 655

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Four circulars i.e., circulars D/- 14-05-1991, 07-08-1992, 07-01-1994 and 07-01-1994 - First two circulars have been issued as an incentive to employees of Govt. of Haryana state and third circular is an interim relief granted to them while matter of revision of pay was under consideration before Pay Commission - Fourth circular D/- 08-02-1994 is a higher scale of pay to teachers of Govt. schools in Haryana on certain contingencies - High Court came to conclusion that teachers of privately managed schools would be entitled to all benefits emanating from four circulars which were undoubtedly meant only for Govt. servants in State of Haryana Meaning of expression 'parity in employment' - Whether teachers of privately managed recognized aided schools would be entitled to benefits which Govt. had conferred by issuing circulars at different point of time? - Held, following Haryana Adhyapak sangh Case HC rightly came to conclusion that fourth circular D/- 08-02-1994 is unassailable - Therefore, that part of conclusion of impugned judgment is sustained - So far as other three circulars are concerned court is of considered opinion that HC committed error in granting benefits of those circulars to employees of private schools - State of Punjab v Om Parkash Kaushal ((1996) 5 SCC 3251996 (5) SCC 3251996 Indlaw SC 2947 : 1996 SCC (L and S) 1166) relied upon and ultimately came to conclusion that all incentives granted to employees of Govt. cannot be claimed as a matter of right by employee under private management, as that would not be within expression 'parity in employment' - Court unequivocally said that scale of pay and dearness allowance to a Govt. servant or teacher of a Govt. school can be claimed as a matter of right by teachers of a private school and not other incentives which Govt. might be intending to confer on its own employees - This being position and on examining aforesaid three circulars which were annexed as annexures P-3, P-4 and P-6 in writ petition filed before HC, court is of considered opinion that HC committed error in granting benefit of those circulars also to teachers of privately managed schools - Therefore, that part of conclusion in impugned judgment is set aside - Appeal allowed.
