Benga Behera and Another v Braja Kishore Nanda and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 559; (2007) 9 SCC 728; AIR 2007 SC 1975; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 97; 2007 (3) AWC 3011; 2007 (104) CutLT 552; JT 2008 (8) SC 86; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 159; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 240; 2008 (104) RD 61; 2007(7) SCALE 228; 2007 (6) SCJ 478; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Math

Summary: Indian Succession Act, 1925-Section 63-Evidence Act, 1872-Sections 68 and 71-Execution of Will-Proof-Will allegedly executed by testatrix aged about 70 years in favour of stranger on 15.1.1982-She expired on 5.6.1983-Original Will not produced-At least one witness to be examined to prove execution and attestation of Will-Why both Will and sale deed should have been executed on same day-Not explained - Variance, inconsistencies and contradictions brought on record, particularly in statements of P.Ws. 4 and 9 and other witnesses vis-a-vis contents of document-Trial court and High Court did not take into consideration effect of such contradictions and inconsistencies - Particularly interpolation/variance in Xerox copy of Will vis-a-vis certified copy thereof-Non-production of original Will stating that Will got lost-Giving rise to inference that original Will might not have contained thumb impression of testatrix-Existence of suspicious circumstances-Itself may be held sufficient to arrive at conclusion that execution of Will not duly proved-Impugned judgment unsustainable-And set aside.
