Shyamalika Das v General Manager, Gridco & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 804; (2010) 15 SCC 268; 2010(10) SCALE 470

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: LSA Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss, 89, 100 and 151 - Jurisdiction of HC - Appellant filed a suit for a permanent injunction to restrain the respondents from interfering with her possession claiming to be the owner of the impugned plot - Appellant submitted that the respondents has erected tower for drawing high tension transmission lines in the appellant's property - Said suit was decreed in favour of the appellant - Appeal filed by the respondents against said order was dismissed - HC allowed second appeal and permitted the respondents to draw the electricity transmission line over the appellant's land as per its project scheme and declared that the appellant will be entitled to Rs.1,75,000/- as compensation, for raising the high tension tower over the appellant's land - Appellant filed appeal against said order submitting that HC has acted beyond its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC by erroneously purporting to exercise power u/ss. 89 and 151 of CPC - Whether HC has acted beyond its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC? - Held, decision of the HC is neither in the exercise of its judicial power to decide the lis before it, nor as a consequence of the Single Judge acting as a facilitator enabling the parties to arrive at a settlement - What the court has assumed is a jurisdiction, which is an extraordinary amalgamation of imagined judicial and conciliatory power, unknown to the system of law which is followed in Indian Courts - HC has heard the parties and rendered a judgment where the decision is not based either on any judicial reasoning or any settlement between the parties but based on what the Judge considered as a just and expeditious solution to the dispute u/s. 89 r/w. s. 151 of CPC - Moreover, impugned suit was for a permanent injunction - In such a suit, the court cannot issue a direction permitting the respondents to erect the tower and direct respondents to pay compensation of Rs.1,75,000/- - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Matter remanded back to the HC for disposal of the Second Appeal in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
