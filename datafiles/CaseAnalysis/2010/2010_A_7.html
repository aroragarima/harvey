M/S J.P. Builders & Anr v A. Ramadas Rao & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1001; (2011) 1 SCC 429; 2011(5) ALL MR 899; 2011 (3) AWC 2698; 2010 (6) CTC 901; JT 2010 (12) SC 588; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 222; 2012 (115) RD 509; 2010(12) SCALE 400; 2010 (3) UC 2122

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Reliefs Act, 1963, s. 16 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 56 - Decree for specific performance - Right of marshalling - Appellants availed of certain financial assistance from the Bank and for the said assistance appellants offered various properties including the suit property as security for the principal as well as interest amount - To discharge the bank loan, appellants decided to sell the properties and offered the same for sale - Respondent no. 1 has agreed to purchase the said property and paid the sale price - Subsequently, respondent No. 1 issued a legal notice to the appellants calling upon them to liquidate the loans out of the amounts received from him and retrieve the original documents from the Bank in order to execute the sale deed - Appellants replied to the notice stating that the respondent no. 1 had not paid the balance sale consideration in spite of repeated requests and raised doubt that the respondent no. 1 was no longer interested to buy the suit property, therefore, a legal notice was sent calling upon respondent no. 1 to pay a sum of Rs. 1 crore as liquidated damages - Respondent no. 1 filed suit for specific performance against the appellants and the bank - Trial Court decreed the said suit partly, granting the relief of specific performance directing appellants to specifically perform their part of the obligations arising out of the agreement for sale by executing the sale deed in favour of respondent no. 1 on receipt of the balance sale consideration of Rs. 4,80,48,000/- subject to the mortgage of the Bank - DB confirmed the order of the trial court and also directed respondent no. 1 to deposit the balance sale consideration of Rs.4,80,48,000/- with 18% interest from the date of filing of the suit and also directed the appellants to execute the sale deed conveying the subject property to respondent no. 1 and the Bank was directed to proceed against the various other properties of the appellants for recovering the balance amount - Review petition filed against said order was dismissed by DB - Respondent no. 1 filed appeal against said order submitting that having secured a decree for specific performance as per s. 56 of 1882 Act by applying the principles of Marshalling, directions may be issued to the Bank to exhaust its remedy from other items of property before bringing the properties covered in the agreement of sale - Whether order of the DB could be upheld? - Held, in view of the sale agreement which results into decree for specific performance, the respondent no. 1 is entitled to insist upon appellants to have the mortgage debt satisfied out of the properties not sold to the respondent no. 1 and in any case if the sale proceeds are not sufficient then to proceed against the said suit properties - Therefore, HC is right in applying the principle of marshalling in favour of the respondent no. 1 that too by safeguarding the interest of the Bank - Impugned judgment of HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
