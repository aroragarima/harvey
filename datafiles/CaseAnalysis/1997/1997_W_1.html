Martin and Harris Limited v Vith Additional Distt. Judge and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1204; (1998) 1 SCC 732; AIR 1998 SC 492; 1998 ALJ 200; 1998 (32) ALR 106; 1998 (1) AWC 580; JT 1997 (10) SC 34; 1998 (1) RCJ 273; 1998 (1) RCR(Rent) 1; 1997(7) SCALE 597; 1998 (1) SLT 411; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Soldiers (Litigation) Act, 1925

Summary: Land & Property - Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Soldiers (Litigation) Act, 1925 - Soldiers (Litigation) Act, 1925 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21(1)(a) - Respondent 3 purchased suit property wherein appellant-Co. is occupying as a tenant - Respondent gave a notice D/- 20-09-1985 to appellant seeking possession on ground that he had purchased property for his residential purpose and he bona fide required same - Appellant replied to said notice and refuted claim of respondent-landlord - Respondent 3 thereafter filed an application u/s. 21(1)(a) rw s. 21(1-A) of Act in court - (A) Whether, respondent's application u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act was not maintainable in view of proviso to said section as it was filed before expiry of 3 years from date of purchase of suit premises by respondent? - Held, it must be held that when legislature has provided that no application u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act shall be entertained by prescribed authority on grounds mentioned in cl. (a) of s. 21(1) of Act before expiry of 3 years from date of purchase of property by landlord it must necessarily mean consideration by prescribed authority of grounds mentioned in cl. (a) of s. 21(1) of Act on merits - On facts of present case, that stage was reached after 1988 when prescribed authority on basis of affidavit evidence led before it took up plaintiff's case for consideration on merits of grounds u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act and at that stage more than three years had expired from date on which respondent had purchased property - Consequently no fault can be found with decision of High Court to effect that prescribed authority was justified in entertaining consideration of grounds u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act at that stage and decree passed on said ground, therefore, cannot be said to be a nullity, nor can entertaining of such application on ground u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act be said to be illegal - First point for consideration is, therefore, answered in negative, in favour of respondent - So far as this point is concerned it must be held on clear language of first proviso to s. 21(1) of Act that application for possession u/s. 21(1)(a) had to be filed by landlord concerned not earlier than expiry of six months from date of issuance of notice by landlord - (B) Whether, said application was not maintainable on additional ground that it was filed prior to expiry of 6 months from date on which notice was given by respondent to appellant as required by very same proviso? - Held, on facts of present case it cannot be disputed that when notice was issued on 20-09-1985 application for possession could not have been filed by respondent invoking grounds mentioned in cl. (a) of s. 21(1) of Act, at least till 20-03-1986, while application was filed in January 1986 - To that extent it can be said that application was premature - Provision in this connection has to be treated to be mandatory - That opportunity was lost to respondent as appellant did not pursue this contention any further - On contrary appellant joined issues on merits by seeking permission to cross-examine plaintiff on merits of case on grounds as pleaded u/s. 21(1)(a) of Act - When decree was passed against appellant, even while challenging said decree in appeal no such ground was taken in memo of appeal, nor was it argued before first Appellate Court - Under these circumstances, HC rightly held that contention, regarding suit being premature as filed before expiry of six months from date of notice, must be treated to have been waived by appellant - (C) Whether, bona fide requirement of respondent-landlord did not survive in view of subsequent event, namely, that respondent's wife had acquired an undivided interest in adjoining part of building in which suit premises were situated and wherein respondent-landlord was staying with his wife? - Held, the said subsequent event was rightly held to have no effect on merits of respondent's claim as respondent was a retired Army Major General who had no property of his own in Lucknow town and who could not be compelled to stay as a licensee of his wife in a property which did not even exclusively belong to her but was jointly owned by her brother - Third point for consideration is also answered in negative, in favour of respondent - Appeal dismissed.
