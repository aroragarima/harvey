K.A. Nagamani v Indian Airlines & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 385; (2009) 5 SCC 515; AIR 2009 SC 3240; 2009 (2) AWC 1763; 2009 (2) CLR 83; 2009 (122) FLR 198; JT 2009 (4) SC 674; 2009 (2) LLN 497; 2009(4) SCALE 670; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 89; 2009 (4) SLR 648

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Criteria For Promotion, Abolition Of Post, Indian Airlines Corporation Employees Service Regulations, 1955

Summary: Service - (A) Whether the Recruitment & Promotion Rules are statutory in nature or mere administrative instructions? - Held, Recruitment and Promotions Rules do not draw any statutory flavour from the service Regulations - (B) Whether the merger of the two cadres is valid? - Held, once it is to be held that the Recruitment and Promotions Rules are not statutory in nature but are in the nature of guidelines, there are no impediments to uphold the merger of software and hardware cadres into one cadre - (C) Whether the promotion of the respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 is not in accordance with law? - Held, respondent nos. 3 and 4 had outstanding ratings in their annual performance appraisals and were found suitable by the Selection Board - Decision to select and appoint respondent nos. 2, 3 and 4 is not vitiated for any reason whatsoever - Appeal dismissed.
