Commissioner Of Income Tax v P. Mohanakala

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 892; (2007) 6 SCC 21; AIR 2007 SC 2116; [2007] 291 ITR 278; JT 2007 (7) SC 533; 2007(7) SCALE 599; 2007 (6) SCJ 701; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 680

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals allowed.

Summary: Income Tax - Section 68, Income Tax Act, 1961 - Cash credits - Addition - Foreign gifts - Dispute relates to the addition made by the Assessing Officer in respect of several foreign gifts stated to have been received by the assessees from one common donor - Assessing officer added it as income of the assessees from undisclosed sources - Tribunal took note of the various other attending circumstances and found it difficult to accept the explanation offered by the assessees - High Court reversed the finding of fact and allowed the appeals - Held, authorities concurrently found the explanation offered by the assessees unacceptable - Where the explanation offered by the assessee about the nature and source of sums found credited in the books is not satisfactory, the burden is on the assessee to rebut the same, and if he fails to rebut it, it can be held against the assessee that it was a receipt of an income nature - May be the money came by way of bank cheques and was paid through the process of banking transaction but that itself is of no consequence - Impugned order set aside.
