Vinod Kumar v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 125; (2015) 3 SCC 220; AIR 2015 SC 1206; 2015 CRLJ 1442; JT 2015 (1) SC 493; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 288; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 647; 2015(1) SCALE 542; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Interested Witness, Hostile Witness, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1985, Octroi

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2) - Hostile witness - Conviction - On knowing about corrupt practices of accused of demanding money for permitting entry of tractor trolley inside municipal area, trap was laid by DSP Vigilance and accused was caught and put on trial - Trial Court held that though PW5, complainant had not supported case of prosecution yet prosecution had been able to prove the demand and acceptance of bribe and recovery of tainted money from accused and hence, presumption as envisaged u/s. 20 of the Act would get attracted and accordingly convicted accused u/ss. 7 and 13(2) of the Act and awarded sentence - On appeal, HC affirmed the same and hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecution proved charges levelled against accused.

Held, PW8, who was a member of raiding party had sent the report to the police station and thereafter carried the formal investigation. However, nothing was put to him to elicit that he was anyway personally interested to get the accused convicted. Hence, investigation by PW8 is not vitiated. S.Jeevanatham v. State (through Inspector of Police, T.N.), 2004 INDLAW SC 1115, followed. Further, it had not been laid down as a proposition of law that when complainant turns hostile and does not support the case of the prosecution, and the prosecution could not prove its case otherwise and then Court could not legitimately draw the presumption u/s. 20 of the Act. Hence, proposition of accused that when PW5, complainant, had turned hostile the whole case of prosecution would collapse is not acceptable and accordingly rejected. Further, accused pocket contained phenolphthalein smeared currency notes when he was searched and currency notes that have been recovered from the right side of the pant pocket were actually prepared by PW8 by smearing them with phenolphthalein powder. The accused was caught red-handed with those currency notes and even though pleaded innocence, yet no explanation was given as regards the recovery. Hence, presumption could be drawn that the accused had received or accepted said currency notes on his own volition. The factum of presumption and testimony of PWs 6 and 7 go a long way to show that the prosecution has been able to prove demand, acceptance and recovery of the amount. Hence, charges leveled against accused were duly proven by prosecution and is not a case that here is no other evidence barring evidence of complainant and on contrary there are adequate circumstances which establish ingredient of offences he was charged with. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Trap witness is an interested witness and his testimony, to be accepted and relied upon requires corroboration and the corroboration would depend upon facts and circumstances, nature of crime and the character of the trap witness.
