State Of Uttar Pradesh And Anr. v Kaushal Kishore Shukla

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.D. Ojha, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1013; (1991) SCC (L&S) 587; (1991) 1 SCC 691; AIR 1991 SCW 793; 1991 (16) ATC 498; 1991 (1) AWC 651; 1991 (1) CLR 732; 1991 (62) FLR 350; JT 1991 (1) SC 108; 1991 (1) LLN 343; 1991(1) SCALE 15; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 29; 1991 (2) SLJ 96; 1991 (1) SLR 606; 1991 (1) UJ 731; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 152

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Character Roll, Uttar Pradesh Temporary Government Servants (Termination of Service) Rules 1975

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Temporary Govt. Servants (Termination of Service) Rules, 1975 - Whether order terminating respondent's services, who was admittedly an ad-hoc and temporary employee, is vitiated in law? - Held, respondent was a temporary Govt. servant and there was adverse report regarding his work which was reflected in adverse remarks made for year 1977-78 - Competent authority held a preliminary inquiry in allegations of improper conduct in carrying out unauthorised audit of boys fund of an educational institution, on result of preliminary enquiry no charges were framed against respondent, no officer was appointed for holding departmental inquiry instead competent authority to terminate respondent's service in exercise of its power under terms of contract as well as under relevant rules applicable to a temporary Govt. servant - It never intended to dismiss respondent from service - Holding of preliminary inquiry does not affect nature of termination order - Allegations made against respondent contained in counter-affidavit by way of defence filed on behalf of appellants also do not change nature and character of order of termination - High Court failed to consider question in proper perspective and it interfered with order of termination in a casual manner - Appeal allowed.
