Anuj Kumar Gupta @ Sethi Gupta v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 463; (2013) 12 SCC 383; AIR 2013 SC 3013; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3314; 2013 CRLJ 3895; JT 2013 (10) SC 424; 2013(9) SCALE 535; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kidnap for ransom

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364(A), 302, 201, 120B - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Kidnap for ransom - Death sentence - Conviction - Justifiability - Appellant-accused no.1 to accused no. 3 allegedly kidnapped and murdered deceased - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 364(A), 302, 201 and 120-B IPC - Trial Court imposed death penalty against accused no.1 and A2 , A3 and A4 were acquitted - Appeal and death reference before HC - HC upheld conviction of A1 and A2 was acquitted of all the charged - HC commuted death sentence to life imprisonment u/ss. 302, 201 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether circumstances considered by Trial Court and HC were sufficient to confirm ultimate conviction of accused and sentence imposed on him - Held, conclusion of Trial Court and HC in holding that chain of circumstances was complete in order to lead to conclusion that accused was squarely responsible for killing of deceased was well justified - Though accused attempted to point out some discrepancy in matter of arrest of A2 and recording of alleged confessional statement of accused pursuant to which body was traced out, said discrepancy was very trivial and on that score there was no scope to dislodge findings of Courts below - Therefore, ultimate conviction of accused u/s. 302 of IPC and sentence of life imprisonment imposed on accused by commuting death penalty imposed by Trial Court, was perfectly justified - There was no good grounds to interfere with same - Appeal dismissed.
