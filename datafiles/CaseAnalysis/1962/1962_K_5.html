K. Simrathmull v S. Nanjalingiah Gowder

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 545; AIR 1963 SC 1182; 1962 (2) KerLR 72; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 476; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 58, 12 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 74 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 35 - (A)Whether transaction which took place between plaintiff and defendant is a mortgage by condition sale? - Held, sale deed, deed of reconveyance and rent note were parts of same transaction and that transaction was not one of mortgage by conditional sale - (2) court could not relieve plaintiff against forfeiture clause - If plaintiff failed to act punctually according to terms of contract right to repurchase would be lost and could not be specifically enforced - Refusal to enforce terms specifically for failure to abide by conditions did not amount to enforcement of a penalty and court had no power to afford relief against forfeiture arising as a result of breach of such a condition -(B) Whether principle of equity applies in absence of fraud, accident or surprise?- Further held, it does not - Where under a contract, conveyance, or will a beneficial right is to arise upon performance by beneficiary of some act in a stated manner, or at a stated time, act must be performed accordingly in order to obtain enjoyment of right, and in absence of fraud, accident or surprise, equity will not relieve against a breach of terms - Appeal allowed.
