Aurangabad Electricals (P) Ltd. v The Commissioner Of Central Excise And Customs, Aurangabad

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 958; (2011) 1 SCC 121; 2010 (6) Bom.C.R. 794; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 24; JT 2011 (1) SC 453; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 106; 2010(12) SCALE 1

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4(1)(a), 11A, 11AB and 11AC - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 209A - Demand - Interest - Penalty - Undervaluation - Appellants were the manufacturers of Magneto Assemblies and were receiving inputs, namely 'Pick-up Coil', com bush, charging coil etc. from 'X' on which appropriate duty was paid by 'X' - Commissioner issued show cause notice to appellants alleging that the appellants have undervalued the Magneto Assemblies by not including the element of landed cost of inputs incurred on account of Sales Tax, Octroi, Freight, Insurance, loading, unloading and handling charges - Appellants contended that they have cleared the Magneto Assemblies in accordance with approved price declarations and finalization of RT 12 return assessment - Adjudicating Commissioner confirmed the differential duty demand of Rs. 84,27,889/- u/s. 11A(2) of 1944 Act r/w. r. 9(2) of the Rules and penalty of Rs. 69,72,104/- u/s. 11AC of 1944 Act - On appeal, Tribunal remanded the matter to the Adjudicating Commissioner for re-computation of excise duty to be levied and also reduced the penalty - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the tribunal could be upheld? - Held, adjudicating commissioner and the Tribunal has non-suited the appellants mainly on the ground that the appellants did not produce any material/data as to actual expenses incurred on account of freight, loading, unloading charges, profit margin etc. - Appellants submitted that the they could have supported their defence pleaded in their objections filed to the show cause notice by producing relevant documents including the certificate issued by its chartered accountant but due to unavoidable and unforeseen circumstances, they could not produce the same - Appellant produced the Certificate along with the other papers in support of their submission - Matter remanded to the tribunal to verify and consider whether the Certificate which is placed on record by the appellant, would assist them in support of their defence - Impugned order of the tribunal set aside - Appeals allowed.
