Ms. Neelu Arora and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 31; (2003) 3 SCC 366; AIR 2003 SC 1082; JT 2003 (1) SC 447; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 451; 2003(1) SCALE 424; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 562; 2003 (2) SLR 33; 2003 (1) SLT 802; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 859

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Admission to MBBS/BDS courses - Scheme framed by the SC prescribing the procedure to complete the process of allotment of 15 per cent of All-India quota for admission to MBBS/BDS courses - Contention that the IInd round of counselling for the All-India quota seats which was scheduled to be held have neither been held as the Pre-Medical Test is not conducted nor counselling for the seats under the State quota is completed - Held, when a detailed scheme has been framed through orders of this Court and the manner in which it has to be worked out is also indicated therein, then, if in a particular year there is any short fall or certain number of seats are not filled up, the same should not be done by adopting one more round of counselling because there is no scope for the third round of counselling under the Scheme It would not be advisable to go on altering the scheme as and when seats are found vacant - Even if seats are unfilled that cannot be a ground for making mid-session admissions and there cannot be telescoping of unfilled seats of one year with permitted seats of the subsequent year - Petitions Dismissed.
