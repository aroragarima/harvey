University Grants Commission and another v Neha Anil Bobde (Gadekar)

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 623; (2013) 10 SCC 519; 2014 (2) AWC 1786; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 35; 2013 (4) KLT 302; 2013 (5) MahLJ 934; 2013 (7) MLJ 24; 2013 (4) MPLJ 1; 2013(11) SCALE 593; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 521; 2014 (2) SLR 698

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Declaration Of Results, University Grants Commission (Minimum Qualifications required for the Appointment and Career Advancement of Teachers in Universities and Institutions affiliated to it) Regulations, 2000, Powers, University Grants Commission Regulations, 2010, Eligibility for lectureship, Fixation of qualifying criteria

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, ss. 26(1), 26()(e), 26(1)(g) - University Grants Commission (Minimum Qualifications for Appointment of Teachers and other Academic Staff in Universities and Colleges and other Measures for the Maintenance of Standards in Higher Education) Regulations, 2010 - University Grants Commission Regulations, 2010 - National Eligibility Test (NET) - Eligibility for lectureship - Declaration of results - Fixation of qualifying criteria - Powers - Legality - Appellant (University Grants Commission, UGC) prepared supplementary result qualifying 15,178 additional candidates which was declared on 12-11-2012 - That was in addition to candidates declared as qualified in original result of June 2012 UGC-NET declared on 18-9-2012 - Altogether 5,71,630 candidates appeared in UGC-NET Examination, 2012, out of which 2,04,150 candidates got the minimum marks prescribed separately in Paper I, Paper II and Paper III and fell in the consideration zone - From that, 57,550 candidates were declared passed in the NET Examination for year 2012, applying the qualifying criteria laid down by Expert Committee of UGC - Candidates who have obtained the minimum marks in Paper I, Paper II and Paper III approached the HC seeking a declaration that the change of qualifying criteria reflected in final declaration of results was arbitrary, illegal and without authority of law and was violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Further, declaration of NET alone being the minimum eligibility standard, UGC had attempted to fix the Aggregate Criteria as an additional qualifying criteria, which action of UGC goes beyond the scope of the notification and that if at all the UGC got the power to fix any additional qualifying criteria prior to the declaration of results, the same should have been notified at the time of taking the NET examination - However, HC ruled that the UGC lacked the competence to fix the aggregate marks as the final qualifying criteria, after the candidates obtained the minimum marks, prescribed in the notification before the declaration of results of NET Examination, agreeing with a similar view expressed by a Single Judge of Kerala HC - Whether UGC had got the power to fix the final qualifying criteria, for those who have obtained the minimum marks for all the papers, before the final declaration of results of the NET for the year 2012 -

Held, UGC as an expert body was entrusted by the Act the general duty to take such steps as it might think fit for the determination and maintenance of standards of teaching, examination and research in Universities - It was also duty bound to perform such functions as may be prescribed or as may be deemed necessary by the Commission for advancing the cause of higher education in India - UGC had also got the power to define the qualification that should ordinarily be required for any person to be appointed to the teaching staff of the University and to regulate the maintenance of standards and coordination of work and faculties in the Universities - UGC, in exercise of its powers conferred u/cls. (e) and (g) of s. 26(1) of the Act, issued the 2010 Regulations- Cl. 3.3.1 of 2010 Regulations specifically stated the NET should remain the minimum eligibility condition for recruitment and for appointment of Asst. Professors in Universities/Colleges/Institutions - Cl. 4.4.1 of 2010 Regulations stipulated that before fulfilling the other prescribed qualifications, the candidates should have cleared the National Eligibility Test conducted by UGC - Notification dealt with the Scheme of the Act which clearly indicated that the candidates were required to obtain minimum marks separately in Paper I, Paper II and Paper III - It clearly indicated that only such candidates who obtain minimum required marks in each paper would be considered for final preparation of results - final qualifying criteria for JRF and eligibility for lectureship shall be decided by UGC before declaration of result - All the steps taken by UGC were strictly in accordance with cl. 7 of the Notification for the NET Examination, 2012 - Prescribing the qualifying criteria as per cl. 7, did not amount to a change in the rule of the game as it was already pre-meditated in the notification - Court was not inclined to say that the UGC had acted arbitrarily or whimsically against the candidates - UGC in exercise of its statutory powers and the laid down criteria in the notification for NET June, 2012, had constituted a Moderation Committee consisting of experts for finalising the qualifying criteria for lectureship eligibility and JRF - UGC acted on the basis of the recommendations made by the Expert Committee - Candidates were not misled in any manner - Much emphasis was made on the words 'clearing the National Eligibility Test' - 'Clearing' means clearing the final results, not merely passing in Paper I, Paper II and Paper III, which was only the initial step, not final - To clear the NET Examination, candidate should satisfy the final qualifying criteria laid down by UGC before declaration of the results - It was open to UGC to lay down any 'qualifying criteria', which had a rational nexus to the object to be achieved, that was for maintenance of standards of teaching, examination and research - Candidates declared eligible for lectureship might be considered for appointment as Asst. Professors in Universities and colleges and the standard of such a teaching faculty had a direct nexus with the maintenance of standards of education to be imparted to the students of the universities and colleges - UGC had only implemented the opinion of Experts by laying down the qualifying criteria, which could not be considered as arbitrary, illegal or discriminatory or violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
