Dr. Suresh Chandra Verma And Ors. v Chancellor, Nagpur University And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 386; (1990) 4 SCC 55; (1991) SCC (L&S) 194; AIR 1990 SC 2023; 1991 (16) ATC 439; JT 1990 (3) SC 552; 1990 LabIC 1628; 1991 (1) LLJ 574; 1990 (2) LLN 639; 1990(2) SCALE 338; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 883; 1990 (5) SLR 53; 1990 (2) UJ 594

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: University, Selection Committees, Nagpur University Act, 1974

Summary: Education - Nagpur University Act, 1974, ss. 57(4)(a), 57(4)(d) and 57(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appointment - Teaching staff - Advertisement - (A) Subject wise reserved seats not notified in advertisement - Contents - (B) Termination of service by Chancellor without personal hearing on ground of 'change in law' by Supreme Court's declaration - Held, mere mention of total number of reserved posts is not sufficient but subject wise details necessary - Personal hearing to each appointee not necessary as termination was due to change of law and not because of individual misdemeanor - Appeal dismissed.
