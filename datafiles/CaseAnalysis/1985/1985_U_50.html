K.C. Joshi v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 423; (1985) 3 SCC 153; (1985) SCC (L&S) 656; AIR 1985 SC 1046; 1987 (61) CTR 10; 1985 (51) FLR 145; [1987] 163 ITR 597; 1985 LabIC 1032; 1985 (2) LLJ 416; 1985 (2) LLN 247; 1985(1) SCALE 707; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 869; 1985 (2) SLJ 68; 1985 (2) SLR 204; 1985 UJ 950

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Constitution, arts. 12, 14, 16, 226 and 311 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch. 2, items 3 and 6 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Oil and Natural Gas Commission (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1976, Regns. 32 and 36 - ONGC, an instrumentality of State - (A) Still its employees, not entitled to protection u/art. 311 of Constitution - (B) Administrative Law - Natural justice - Termination of service without enquiry in accordance with natural justice - Ground, unsuitability and unsatisfactory work - Unsustainable - Held, they are entitled to protection of fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 16 - Termination of service, not valid or justified - Appeal allowed.
