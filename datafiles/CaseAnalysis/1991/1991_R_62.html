State of Maharashtra and Others v Ravikant S. Patil

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, Mr.Justice S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 578; (1991) 2 SCC 373; (1991) SCC (Cr) 656; 1991 (2) ACJ 888; AIR 1991 SCW 871; 1991 (1) Crimes 810; 1991 CRLR 261; JT 1991 (5) SC 442; 1991 (1) PLR 690; 1991(1) SCALE 486; 1991 (2) UJ 188

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Entitlement of, Censure of police officer on under trial prisoner

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Censure of police officer on under trial prisoner - Compensation - Entitlement of - One 'G' was murdered, during investigation, police suspected that respondent was party to said murder - He was arrested in Karnataka State and was brought to Sholapur - A local paper in its news items stated that respondent, an under trial prisoner, would be taken in procession or a parade from Police Station through main squares of city for purpose of investigation - Accordingly respondent was handcuffed and both his arms were tied by rope and he was taken through streets - Respondent filed writ petition before DB seeking censure of police officer and to award damages - DB having exonerated Superintendent of Police and other respondents, held that appellant no. 2/Inspector of Police had subjected under trial prisoner to an unwarranted humiliation and indignity which could not be done to any citizen of Indian and accordingly directed him to pay compensation and he was also censured - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by DB was justified - Held, after going through entire record SC was unable to disagree with some of findings of HC regarding handcuffing and SC did not propose to interfere with order directing payment of compensation - But SC thought appellant no. 2 could not be made personally liable - Appellant no. 2 had acted only as an official and even assuming that he had exceeded his limits and thus erred in taking under trial prisoner handcuffed, still SC did not think that he could be made personally liable - In Rudul Sah v. State of Bihar 1983 Indlaw SC 231, 1983 Indlaw SC 231 SC directed State to pay compensation to person illegally detained - HC also noted this decision observed that court could order payment of compensation either by State or persons acting on behalf of State - SC modified order of HC - Compensation of Rs. 10,000/- as awarded by HC, should be paid by State - Concerned authorities might, if they think it necessary, hold an enquiry and then decide whether any further action had to be taken against Inspector - Appeal disposed of.
