Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan and Another v S.C. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 41; (2005) SCC (L&S) 270; (2005) 2 SCC 363; AIR 2005 SC 768; 2005 (5) AWC 4182; 2005 (1) ESC 125; 2005 (104) FLR 863; JT 2005 (1) SC 336; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 375; 2005 (1) LLN 1047; 2005(1) SCALE 317; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 374; 2005 (2) SLR 1; 2005 (1) Supreme 311; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1289

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 19(ii) - Dismissal from service - Whether authorities were justified in invoking r.19(ii)? - Held, for application of r.19(ii) in background of r. 14 basic requirement is that a conclusion has to be recorded that it is not reasonably practicable to hold inquiry proceedings - Such a finding does not appear to have been recorded - Views expressed by CAT as affirmed by HC do not suffer from any infirmity - Respondent had neither pleaded nor placed any material to show that he was not gainfully employed - Not entitled to full back wages - Appeal disposed of.
