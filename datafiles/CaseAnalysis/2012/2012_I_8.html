Kumari Shaima Jafari v Irphan @ Gulfam and others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 527; 2013 CRLJ 1829; 2013 (170) PLR 383; 2012(12) SCALE 60

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 328, 323, 506, 368 and 376(2)(g) - Kidnap - Order to face trial - Acquittal - Challenged - Accused persons faced trail for offences punishable u/ss. 363, 366, 328, 323, 506, 368 and 376(2)(g) IPC - Aggrieved accused persons filed appeal before Appellate Court, which was acquitted the accused persons - Govt. filed appeal before HC - HC declined to entertain the appeal directed against the judgment of acquittal rendered by Appellate Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether appeal preferred by Govt. questioning the legal substantiality of judgment of acquittal was dismissed by HC in such manner as it was done - Held, it was graphically clear that deliberation by HC while exercising criminal appellate jurisdiction had to be reflective of due cogitation and requisite rumination - It should reflect application of mind, consideration of facts in proper perspective and appropriate ratiocination either for affirmation or reversal of judgment - However, reasons ascribed might not be lengthy but it should be cogent, germane and reflective - It was clear that as cloudless sky that it did not show any contemplation or independent application of mind as required of Appellate Court - Judgment passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
