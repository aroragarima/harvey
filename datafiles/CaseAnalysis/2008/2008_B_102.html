Gullipilli Sowria Raj v Bandaru Pavani @ Gullipili Pavani

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1918; (2009) 1 SCC 714; AIR 2009 SC 1085; 2009(1) ALL MR 488; 2009 (74) ALR 638; 2009 (1) AWC 427; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 906; 2008 (6) CTC 37; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 24; JT 2008 (13) SC 450; 2009 (1) KLT 1; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 750; 2009 (2) MPLJ 48; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 442; 2008(16) SCALE 109; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 35; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 78

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Whether a marriage entered into by a Hindu with a Christian is valid under the provisions of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955? - Held, in the event the conditions remain unfulfilled, a marriage between two Hindus could not be solemnized - Expression 'may' used in the opening words of Section 5 is not directory, as has been sought to be argued, but mandatory and non-fulfilment thereof would not permit a marriage under the Act between two Hindus - Preamble itself indicates that the Act was enacted to codify the law relating to marriage amongst Hindus - Application under s. 12(1)(c) of the 1955 Act and the admission of the appellant that he was and still is a Christian belonging to the Roman Catholic denomination, the marriage solemnized in accordance with Hindu customs was a nullity - Appeal dismissed.
