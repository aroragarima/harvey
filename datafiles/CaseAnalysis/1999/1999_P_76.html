A.P. Pollution Control Board v Prof. M. V. Nayudu (Retd.) and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 939; (1999) 2 SCC 718; AIR 1999 SC 812; AIR 1999 SCW 434; JT 1999 (1) SC 162; 1999(1) SCALE 140; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 235; 1999 (1) SLT 349; 1999 (10) Supreme 195

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, National Environment Tribunal Act, 1995, Stockholm Declaration Of The U.N. Conference On Human Environment, 1972

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 136, 226 and 32 - Environmental matters - Involving complex issues relating to environment, pollution, science and technology - Held, equal importance as Human Rights concerns as involving question of life under art. 21 - Court can refer scientific and technical aspects for investigation and opinion to expert bodies - Precautionary principle explained.
