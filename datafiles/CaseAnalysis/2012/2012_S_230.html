Amar Pal Singh v State Of U. P. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 137; (2012) 6 SCC 491; AIR 2012 SC 1995; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 2413; 2012 CRLJ 2921; 2012 (2) JCC 1474; JT 2012 (5) SC 530; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 853; 2012 (2) OLR 291; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 963; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 943; 2012 (4) RLW 2972; 2012(5) SCALE 642; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 1154

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156 - Expunging of adverse remarks - Application filed u/s. 156(3) of CrPC before the Chief Judicial Magistrate (appellant) for issue of direction to the police to register an FIR and investigate the matter was dismissed - Revision was filed against said order before the HC - Single Judge found that it was a fit case where direction should have been issued for registration of FIR and investigation into the alleged offences - Single Judge also made observations against appellant, stating that conduct of appellant was deplorable and wholly malafide and illegal - Appellant being aggrieved by the comments and observations passed by the Single Judge filed instant appeal - It was submitted that the observations and the consequential direction were totally unwarranted and indubitably affect the self-esteem and career of a member of the subordinate judiciary and therefore deserve to be expunged - Whether remarks and the directions were made in consonance with the principles that have been laid down by the various pronouncements of SC and was in accord with judicial decorum and propriety - Held, a Judge was required to maintain decorum and sanctity which were inherent in judicial discipline and restraint - A judge functioning at any level had dignity in the eyes of public and credibility of the entire system was dependent on use of dignified language and sustained restraint, moderation and sobriety - Unwarranted comments on the judicial officer create a dent in the said credibility and consequently lead to some kind of erosion and affect the conception of rule of law - A judge of a superior Court however strongly he may feel about the unmerited and fallacious order passed by an officer, but was required to maintain sobriety, calmness, dispassionate reasoning and poised restraint - In the instant case, the observations, the comment and the eventual direction were wholly unwarranted and uncalled for - Remarks and directions expunged - Appeal allowed.
