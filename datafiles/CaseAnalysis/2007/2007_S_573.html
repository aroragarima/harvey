Kishori Lal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 671; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 701; (2007) 10 SCC 797; AIR 2007 SC 2457; 2007 (4) CTC 158; 2007 (3) JCC 1829; 2007 (3) KLT 259; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2007(8) SCALE 591; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Distinct Offence, Specific Offence

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, offence of abetment is a separate and distinct offence provided in the Act as an offence - In cases of alleged abetment of suicide there must be proof of direct or indirect acts of incitement to the commission of suicide - Mere fact that the husband treated the deceased-wife with cruelty is not enough - Merely on the allegation of harassment conviction in terms of s. 306 IPC is not sustainable - Prosecution failed to establish its case - Appeal allowed.
