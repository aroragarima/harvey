Paresh Kalyandas Bhavsar and another v Sadiq Yakubbhai Jamadar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 998; (1993) 3 SCC 95; (1993) SCC (Cr) 612; AIR 1993 SC 1544; 1993 CRLJ 1857; JT 1993 (2) SC 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154, 161 and 162 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Communal riots - Murder - (A) Evidence - Credibility of eyewitness - (1) Interestedness - (2) Partisan Injured - Presence at scene undoubted - Fact that his brother figured as panch witness and some of his relations were accused in earlier cases - (B) (1) First Information Report - Delay in sending it to Magistrate - Murder inside house - Informant stated in First Information Report only that part of occurrence which he had seen from a nearby house and not inside it - Names of only 9 accused - Indicated that there was no attempt of exaggeration or wholesale roping of persons of any community after deliberations - (2) Appreciation of evidence of seriously injured eye - Did not give out names of accused in his dying declaration - And recorded after operation and under effect of general anesthesia - Nothing to indicate that he did not mention assailants in spite of being asked specifically - (3) Information about riots already with police - First Information Report about murder lodged by informant afterwards - First Information Report held hit by s. 162 - If statement made therein can be used to contradict informant - (4) Examination of witnesses - Delay - Witnesses, victims who have suffered serious injuries - Their evidence found to be clear, cogent, convincing and truthful - (C) Murder - Statement of eye-witnesses that kerosene was poured over deceased and they were burnt alive - Panch witnesses or medical evidence not saying that dead bodies smelt of kerosene - Medical evidence however stating that death of deceased was because of burns - Complaint lodged of occurrence also speaking of burning by kerosene - Held, (A) Not a ground to reject evidence, more so when witness is injured Cannot be a ground to doubt his veracity - (B) First Information Report - Report was true, delay if any in sending it to Magistrate would not be a ground to doubt its genuineness - Omission to name assailants in dying declaration cannot be a ground to discard evidence of eyewitness - Statement can be used for it and also to corroborate evidence of other eyewitnesses - Delay in their examination by police would not affect their evidence - Held, it could not be said that there is any infirmity in evidence of eyewitness - Appeal dismissed.
