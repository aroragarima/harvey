Smt. Angoori Devi v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 783; (1997) 2 SCC 434; AIR 1997 SC 875; 1997 ALJ 418; 1997 (1) CLT 401; JT 1997 (2) SC 88; 1997(1) SCALE 447; 1997 (1) Supreme 647

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 2, 4 and 6 - Surplus land - Determination of - X filed a statement u/s. 6(1) of the Act in the office of the Competent Authority - Competent Authority, after examination of the facts, held that an area measuring 910.50 square meters was vacant land of X - Objection u/s. 8(3) of the Act was filed by the legal heirs of X - District Judge held that the Competent Authority had wrongly construed s. 4(9) of the Act - It was held that there was no dwelling unit but a factory and therefore, the covered area on which factory stood could not be taken into account in computation of vacant land - On appeal, HC held that interpretation of s. 2(g)(i) of the Act made by the District Judge was not correct - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Gopi Chand at the material time held vacant land in excess of ceiling limit fixed by the Act.

Held, the contention raised by the respondents should be considered by a larger Bench. Instant case may be placed before the Chief Justice of India for appropriate direction. Order accordingly.
