Krishnakant Raghunath Bibhavnekar v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3059; (1997) 3 SCC 636; (1997) SCC (L&S) 847; AIR 1997 SC 1434; 1997 (1) CLR 840; JT 1997 (3) SC 726; 1997 LabIC 1538; 1997 (1) LLJ 119; 1997 (1) LLJ 1190; 1997 (2) LLN 602; 1997(3) SCALE 180; 1997 (1) SCJ 597; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 591; 1997 (2) SLJ 166; 1997 (2) SLR 396; 1997 (3) Supreme 276

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Maharashtra Civil Services (Joining Time, Foreign Services and Payment during Suspension, Dismissal and Removal) Rules, rr. 72(5) and 72(7) - Public servant suspending during pendency of criminal trial - Subsequent acquittal - Consequential relief - (A) Whether reinstatement with all consequential benefits and back wages etc. should be granted as matter of course if misconduct alleged is foundation of prosecution - (B) Whether Disciplinary Authority may enquire into misconduct alleged - Held, (A) when suspension period of employee, charged for committing criminal breach of trust was treated to be suspension pending trial and even after acquittal, he was reinstated into service, he would not be entitled to consequential benefits - (B) Disciplinary Authority may enquire and take appropriate action thereon - Appeal dismissed.
