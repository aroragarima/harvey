Patna Electric Supply Company, Limited v Patna Electric Supply Workers' Union

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1959 SC 1035; 1959 (2) LLJ 366; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 761

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, ss. 7 and 10 - Factories Act, 1948 - Whether the direction, calling upon the appellant to construct quarters for its employees is justified? - Financial ability of the appellants have not been considered - In the present economic condition it would be inexpedient to impose such demands - A scheme of wages necessarily takes into account house-rent, dearness allowance etc., therefore, adjustments can be made accordingly - Such demands may retard the progress of industrial development and production thereby prejudicially affecting the national economy result in an increase in price of goods, which is not desirable from a national point of view - Held, appeal allowed and the impugned award set aside.
