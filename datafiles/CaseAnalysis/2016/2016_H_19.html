Amarsang Nathaji as Himself and As Karta and Manager v Hardik Harshadbhai Patel and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 844; (2017) 1 SCC 113; AIR 2016 SC 5384; 2017 (1) Bom.C.R. 265; 2017 CRLJ 758; JT 2016 (11) SC 188; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 481; 2017 (1) OLR 306; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 92; 2016(12) SCALE 269

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interest Of Justice, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Inquiry, Criminal

Summary: Practice & procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 340, 340(1), 343, 195(1)(b)(i) - False Statements - Initiation of Criminal Proceedings Challenged - Trial Court refused to grant interim injunction order to plaintiff/ respondent in suit and HC in appeal confirmed order of Trial Court - SC dismissed SLP in view of amicable settlement between the parties - Further, HC in exercise of powers u/s. 340 of CrPC directed Registry to initiated proceedings against appellant/defendant no.1 for offence u/ss.199 and 200 IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, u/s. 343 of CrPC the Magistrate has to deal with the complaint referred to in s. 340 of CrPC as if it was instituted on a police report. Therefore, on the offences referred to u/s. 195(1)(b)(i) of CrPC all falling within the purview of warrant case, the Magistrate has to follow the procedure for trial of warrant cases under Chapter XIX Part A comprising of ss. 238 to 243 of CrPC. It is only in view of such seriousness of the matter, s. 340 of CrPC has provided for a meticulous procedure regarding initiation of the inquiry. In instant case, Court has not followed the procedure in making opinion that it was expedient in interest of justice to file complaint against appellant in exercise of his power conferred u/s. 340 of CrPC. Hence, impugned order to extent of initiating proceedings u/s. 34 of CrPC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
