Assam Urban Water Supply and Sewerage Board v Subash Projects and Marketing Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 594; (2012) 2 SCC 624; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 105; 2012 (2) AWC 1843; 2012 (2) CTC 364; JT 2012 (1) SC 362; 2012 (4) MahLJ 14; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 837; 2012 (3) MPLJ 31; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 490

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 16, 34 - Limitation Act, 1963, s.4 - Extension of time - Entitlement - Appellants and respondents entered into 2 contracts for construction of Town Water Supply Scheme - Disputes arose between parties concerning these contracts and to resolve such disputes, Sole Arbitrator was appointed by HC - Appellants filed application u/s. 16 of 1996 Act questioning the jurisdiction of Sole Arbitrator as according to appellants there was no arbitration clause in the agreement - Sole Arbitrator rejected said application and passed 2 awards - Appellants made applications for setting a side of awards u/s. 34 of 1996 Act along with application for extension of time u/s. 34 (3) of 1996 Act - Trial Court dismissed said applications on the ground of limitation - On appeal, DB upheld the view of Trial Court and dismissed appeals - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants were entitled to extension of time u/s. 4 of 1963 Act - Held, in instant case, arbitral awards were received by appellants on 26-8-2003 and no application for setting aside arbitral awards was made by appellants before elapse of 3months from receipt thereof - As a matter of fact, 3months from date of receipt of arbitral award by appellants expired on 26-11-2003 - There was Trial Court vacation for the period from 25-12-2003 to 1-1-2004 - On 2-1-2004, admittedly, appellants made applications for setting aside those awards u/s. 34 of 1996 Act - If the period during which Trial Court remained closed during vacation, 2003 was extended and appellants get benefit of that period over and above the cap of 30 days as provided in s. 34(3) of 1996 Act, then the view of HC and Trial Court could not be sustained, but this would depend on the applicability of s. 4 of 1963 Act - Context of S. 34(3) of 1996 Act, it amply clear that prescribed period for making an application for setting aside arbitral award was 3 months - Period of 30 days mentioned in said proviso was not the 'period of limitation' and, therefore, not 'prescribed period' for the purposes of making application for setting aside arbitral award - Period of 30 days beyond 3months which court might extend on sufficient cause being shown under the proviso appended to s. 34 (3) of 1996 Act being not the 'period of limitation' or, in other words, 'prescribed period', Hence, s. 4 of the 1963 Act was not, at all, attracted to the facts of instant case - Appeal dismissed.
