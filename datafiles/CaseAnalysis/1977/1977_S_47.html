Sita Ram Bhau Patil v Ramchandra Nago Patil (Dead) By Lrs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 419; (1977) 2 SCC 49; AIR 1977 SC 1712; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 671

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, s. 135J - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 76 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 145, 21 - Burden Of Proof - Presumption - Depository - Sustainability - Appellant was owner of land his wife sold land to respondent No. 1 - Appellant made application u/ s. 70(b) of the 1879 Act for declaration that he was tenant of two of four plots of land , Survey Nos. .194/15 and 200/29 - Dispute between appellant and respondent with regard to tenancy claim went to Tribunal - Tribunal rejected claim of appellant to tenancy - Respondent filed application u/s. 70(b) of the 1879 Act for declaration that appellant was not tenant of remaining two Survey Nos. 201/2 and 194/13 - Matter was heard by Mamlatdarand rejected claim of appellant to be tenant - Thereafter matter was taken up to District Deputy Collector who upheld Mamlatdar's order - Before Mamlatdar and Deputy Collector respondent examined himself - Certified copy of deposition in earlier proceedings was placed on record and appellant examined himself, saying that he was tenant of land and he had no other evidence to show in support of his case except certified copy of statement which was produced on that day - Appellant also relied on extracts of record of rights showing that respondent was shown as 'Kabzedar' of Survey Nos. 201/2 and appellant was shown as tenant of same - In regard to Survey No. 194/13 it also appeared from record of rights that respondent was shown as 'kabzedar' and appellant as tenant - On this evidence Mamlatdar held that appellant was not Cultivating lands as tenant of respondent and he declared that appellant was not tenant - Deputy Collector affirmed order of Mamlatdar - Tribunal held that appellant was proved to be tenant of land - Respondent took matter to HC u/art. 227 of Constitution - HC set aside order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Tribunal seemed to consider approach of Mamlatdar and Deputy Collector to be erroneous because according to Tribunal burden was shifted to respondent to rebut entry in record of rights and that respondent failed to discharge that burden - When entire evidence was before Court, it was well settled that burden of proof became immaterial - Further Tribunal fell into error of entertaining Revision when there was no error of law on face of record - Presumption which arose in record of rights was before Deputy Collector as well as Mamlatdar - If authority entrusted with adjudication goes into question and assesses same, decision may be right or wrong but that would not go to show that there was any error of law on fact of record - Appeal disposed of.
