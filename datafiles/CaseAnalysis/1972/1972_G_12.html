Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh and another v Gangadhar Baijnath, Generalganj, Kanpur and another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 257; (1972) 4 SCC 28; AIR 1973 SC 1011; 1972 (1) CTR 310; [1972] 86 ITR 19; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 928

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12-B, 66(1) - Capital receipt - Revenue receipt - Taxability - Appellant (Revenue) filed instant appeals against the judgment of HC in a Reference u/s. 66(1) of the Act holding that receipt in question was not taxable u/s. 12-B of the Act - Whether the receipt was a capital receipt or a revenue receipt.

Held, receipt of Rs.35,01,000/- is an income from business and as such was liable to be brought to tax u/s. 10 of the Act . The partners representing the assessee firm in 'B' company had surrendered their rights in the partnership to the remaining partners and obtained certain payments for surrendering their rights. This is not a case of parting with any agency rights. This is really a case of cancellation of a contract which had been entered into in the ordinary course of business. Such contracts are liable, in the ordinary course of business, to be altered or terminated on terms and any payment received in settlement of the rights as a result of the termination of the contract really represents the profits which the assessee would have made had the contract been performed. The entire sum of Rs.35,01,000/- received by the assessee was a revenue receipt assessable u/s. 10 of the Act. Appeals dismissed.
