Parhlad and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 523; (2015) 8 SCC 688; 2015 (1) JCC 2681; JT 2015 (7) SC 192; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 455; 2015(8) SCALE 436; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 363, 366A, 376, s. 376(2)(g) - Offence of Rape - Reduction in Sentence - Justification - It is alleged that prosecutrix was taken by appellant no.1 being cousin of her father - Further, appellant no.1 and 2 committed rape on prosecutrix who is a minor - Trial Court convicted appellant nos. 1 and 2 u/ss. 363, 366A, 376, 34 of IPC - Conviction order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether prosecutrix was minor on day of incident and conviction order passed by lower Court against appellants is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Trial court has relied upon testimony of prosecutrix, her father, and school leaving certificate, which has been brought on record and tendered in evidence; and HC , on re-appreciation of testimony of prosecutrix and her father coupled with testimony of PW 1, Head Master of concerned school has found that version of prosecution is truthful. Further, prosecutrix has deposed that she was about 14 years of age at time she went with her uncle and made prey of uncontrolled debased conduct of appellants. Further, father of prosecutrix has testified in categorical manner about factum of age of prosecutrix. Principal, PW 1, who has proved school leaving certificate has stood embedded in his testimony. Hence, conclusion has been arrived at that prosecutrix was below 16 years of age. Further, radiologist who had conducted test had opined that age of prosecutrix might be 16-17 years. Further, material brought on record clearly reveal that accused in absence of her parents at home had asked victim to go with him for harvesting wheat crop to village and accordingly she had accompanied him to residence of appellant No. 2, who is maternal uncle of appellant no.1. Further, prosecutrix has deposed that she was in totally helpless situation and despite her resistance she was sexually abused. SC held that with regard to gravity of offence, sentence awarded by lower court cannot be reduced. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Sentence of convict in rape cases cannot be reduced taking into consideration heinous nature of crime
