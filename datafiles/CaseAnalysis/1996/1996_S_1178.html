State of Madhya Pradesh v Swaropchandra

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2473; (1996) 11 SCC 175; (1997) SCC (Cr) 155; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 306; AIR 1997 SC 301; 1996 (4) CLT 263; 1996(7) SCALE 616; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 684; 1997 (5) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Van Upaj (Vyapar Viniyaman) Adhiniyam, 1969, s. 19(1)(b) - Forest Act, 1927, ss. 22, 52 and 52 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Carrying timber without permit - Confiscation of vehicle used for carriage - Permissible - Provisions under Central Forest Act having occupied field in respect of confiscation of vehicles etc. - Providing of confiscation procedure again under 1969 Act - Whether necessary? - Whether by operation of s. 22 of 1969 Act, vehicle used for transportation of specified forest produce in contravention of Act has excluded applicability of provisions of Forest Act? - Held, not necessary - It could not be said that by operation of s. 22 of 1969 Act, applicability of provisions of Forest Act excluded - Appeal allowed.
