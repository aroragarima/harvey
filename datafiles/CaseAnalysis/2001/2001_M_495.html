Lekh Raj v Muni Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20305; (2001) 2 SCC 762; AIR 2001 SC 996; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 97; 2001 (43) ALR 268; JT 2001 (2) SC 317; 2001 (128) PLR 426; 2001 (92) RD 465; 2001(1) SCALE 650; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 864; 2001 (2) SLT 100B; 2001 (1) Supreme 607

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, ss. 13 and 15(5) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - (A) Whether High Court under revisional jurisdiction, which limits to examine 'legality and propriety' of Appellate Court order was justified in reversing its findings based on evidence on record? - Held, where fresh evidence was permitted to be brought on record, reversing of finding of fact by HC, exercising revisional jurisdiction, cannot be said to be such that it acted beyond its jurisdiction vested to it under law - (B) Whether HC could have appointed a Local Commr. while exercising its revisional jurisdiction and reversed finding? - Held, once Court could bring on record subsequent fact, event or happening, which has direct bearing on issues or relief claimed, on facts and circumstances of this case, then HC committed no error of jurisdiction to permit Commr.'s report to be placed on record and on which to rely while exercising its revisional power - Appeal dismissed.
