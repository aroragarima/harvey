Chief Forest Conservator (Wild Life) and others v Nisar Khan

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 244; (2003) 4 SCC 595; AIR 2003 SC 1867; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 161; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 33; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 205; 2003(2) SCALE 458; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 196; 2003 (2) SLT 512; 2003 (2) Supreme 271; 2003 (1) UC 668

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Wild Life (Transactions and Texidermy) Rules, 1973, Wild Life (Protection) Licensing (Additional Matters for Consideration) Rules, 1983

Summary: Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - issuance of a writ in the nature of mandamus directing the appellants to grant a licence for carrying on business as dealer in birds which are bred in captivity - appeal - Held, when the licensing authority arrives at a finding of fact having regard to the past transactions of a licensee that it cannot carry on any business by reason of breeding of captive birds but necessarily therefore he is to hunt, he would be justified in refusing to grant a licence in terms of the provisions of the Act. Unless the provisions of the Act and the Rules are construed strictly and in the manner as observed hereinbefore, the very purpose for which the Act has been enacted would be lost. - The High Court has proceeded on a wrong premise by issuing the impugned directions.
