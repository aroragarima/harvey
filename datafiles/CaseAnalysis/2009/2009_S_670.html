Raj Narain Singh v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1157; (2009) 10 SCC 362; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 432; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2317; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 46; JT 2009 (12) SC 342; 2009(12) SCALE 627; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Self-Defence, Self-Inflicted, Finding Of Facts, Gunshot, Non- Explanation, Delay In Medical Examination

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art.136 - Appeal by Special Leave - Scope - Scope of art.136 is limited - SC does not exercise its overriding powers u/art.136 to reweigh evidence - SC does not disturb concurrent findings of facts reached upon by Trial Court after proper appreciation of evidence - If two views are possible, one indicating conviction and other acquittal, SC would not interfere with order of acquittal - However, if acquittal is perverse or imprudent or manifestly illegal or grossly unjust, SC would interfere with.P /&gt;(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Conviction - Reversal of - Appellants alleged to have shot the deceased persons to death - Appellants convicted by Trial Court for murder - However, appellants' appeal allowed and acquitted by HC - Legality - Held, incident fully proved and statements in FIR corroborated by PWs 1, 2, and 3 eye witnesses - No inconsistency in the statements of all eye witnesses - Although PW3 working under PW1, former was an independent witness, not influenced by latter - Post mortem report and testimony of PW7 doctor indicate that bullet injuries on vital part of deceased persons caused to their instant death - Contention by appellant that they exercised private defence when deceased made an attempt to rob, however, no proof supported such contention, hence the same failed - No reason to doubt prompt registration of FIR - PW3 eye witness not in any way interested with PW1 or PW2 nor had enmity with appellants - All those present in spot need not be examined to prove guilt of accused, hence non-examination of any other witness not fatal to prosecution - Prosecution witnesses cannot be disbelieved or discarded merely on reason that non-examination of other witness - Fact of PW2 being held captive by appellant proved by PW11 Investigating Officer and eye witnesses - Therefore, HC clearly erred in reversing order of conviction by Trial Court, hence HC order set aside - Appeals allowed.
