R.S.R.T.C. & Ors. v Ramdhara Indoliya

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1483; (2006) 6 SCC 287; 2006 (5) ADJ 256; 2006 (2) AllCJ 1424; 2006 (2) ARC 681; 2006 (4) AWC 3355; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 45; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1249; 2006(7) SCALE 121; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 448

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Validity - Respondent was appointed as Conductor on daily wages by appellant Corporation - Respondent's service was terminated and his termination was challenged before Trial Court - Trial Court ordered reinstatement, which was upheld by First Appellate Court and HC thereafter - Whether order of reinstatement was legal - Held, HC had committed a grave error in not considering fact that respondent being workman and a dispute being an industrial dispute, Civil Court had no jurisdiction and try suit for reinstatement - Trial Court which passed decree had got no pecuniary jurisdiction and, therefore, decree passed by Trial Court was without jurisdiction - In instant case, respondent who was also similarly placed as in other case covered by Industrial Disputes Act, had failed to approach Industrial Court, but approached Civil Court, which on facts and circumstances of case had no jurisdiction to entertain and try suit - Respondent, therefore, was not entitled for any payment by way of salary or other wages and was not entitled for any reinstatement or back wages - Appeal allowed.
