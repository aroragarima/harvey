Committee of Management, Mumtaz Post Graduate Degree College and Another v Vice-Chancellor, University of Lucknow and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2129; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 424; (2009) 2 SCC 630; AIR 2009 SC 1159; 2009(3) ALL MR 907; 2009 (1) AWC 437; JT 2009 (2) SC 305; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 323; 2008(16) SCALE 327; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 774; 2009 (1) SLR 698; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1345

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: U.P. Universities Act, 1973

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 226-U. P. State Universities Act, 1973-Section 35 (2) proviso-Employment-Minority P.G. College-Removal of Principal - Vice-Chancellor refused to grant approval to removal proposed by C.O.M.-Writ petition thereagainst dismissed on ground of availability of alternative remedy-Whether justified?-Held, "no"-In view of legal questions arising in matter, High Court should not have refused to entertain writ petition-High Court to consider matter on merits.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 226-Writ petition-Maintainability-Alternative remedy - Question whether power exercised properly or not-Is intricate question-Should ordinarily fall for determination by High Court.

(3) Constitution of India-Article 226 - Writ petition-Alternative remedy-When impugned order passed without jurisdiction or in violation of principles of natural justice-Superior Courts shall not refuse to exercise their jurisdiction-Although there is alternative remedy.
