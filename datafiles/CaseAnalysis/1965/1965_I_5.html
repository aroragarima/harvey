India Cements Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 318; AIR 1966 SC 1053; [1966] 60 ITR 52; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 31; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Appellant obtained a loan of Rs. 40 lakhs from 'X' - Said loan was secured by a charge on the fixed assets of the company - In the accounts said sum was written off by appropriation against profits of that year - Income Tax Officer (ITO) disallowed said deduction and held that the expenditure was incurred in obtaining capital and should be distinguished from interest on borrowed capital which alone was admissible as a deduction u/s. 10(2)(iii) of IT Act - Appellate Authority agreed with the ITO - On appeal, Tribunal and HC also upheld the order of the ITO - Hence, present appeal - Whether the sum of Rs. 84,633 expended by the assessee in obtaining the loan or any part thereof is an allowable expenditure? - Held, where there is no express prohibition, an outgoing, by means of which an assessee procures the use of a thing by which it makes a profit, is deductible from the receipts of the business to ascertain taxable income - In the present case, money secured by the loan was the thing for use of which this expenditure was made - There is no distinction, as drawn by the ITO, between interest in respect of a loan and an expenditure incurred for obtaining the loan - Hence, expenditure of Rs. 84,633 was not in the nature of capital expenditure and was laid out or expended wholly and exclusively for the purpose of the assessee's business - Deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of IT Act in respect of expenditure incurred in obtaining capital allowed - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
