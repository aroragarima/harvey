Rani Laxmibai Kshetriya Gramin Bank (now Sarva U.P. Gramin Bank and others) and another v Manoj Kumar Chak and others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 227; (2013) 6 SCC 287; AIR 2013 SC 2473; 2013(4) ALL MR 415; 2013 (3) AWC 2760; 2013 (137) FLR 984; JT 2013 (4) SC 549; 2013(5) SCALE 486; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 562; 2013 (3) SLR 427; 2013 (2) UPLBEC 897

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1988, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and Other Employees) Rules, 1998, Entitlement of, Denied, Regional Rural Banks (Amendment Act), 1988, Claim of promotions

Summary: Service - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1998, r. 2(e) - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, ss. 17, 29 - Misconduct - Claim of promotions - Denied - Entitlement of - Appellants (Regional Rural Banks) in pursuant to circular dt.30-11-2009, which were issued by two nationalised banks, which entitled 'appointment and promotion of officers and other employees of Regional Rural Banks, issued circular dt.12-7-2010 - Aggrieved respondent (employees) filed petitions for promotion, which were allowed by common judgment and final order passed by HC in batch of petitions, whereby HC allowed all petitions and set aside Circulars dt.30-11-2009 and 12-7-2010 in so far as they make a provision to exclude the employees from consideration for promotion, who were otherwise eligible to be considered for promotion and were within the zone of consideration, on the basis that they have either obtained the 'D' rating in annual performance report or were penalized for any misconduct in preceding 5 years - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that circulars were not ultra vires of the Rules and have only supplemented the Rules where the rules were silent - Further contended that circulars were issued in order to bring about uniformity as different Regional Rural Banks (RRBs) were following different procedures for making promotions on similar posts - Whether impugned judgment quashing circulars issued by appellants was justified - Held, it was settled beyond cavil that statutory rules could be supplemented but could not be supplanted - Rules provided that officers holding post in 8 years as an officer on regular basis in RRB should be considered for promotion to next higher post - Rules did not provide that any employee/officer, who has suffered a punishment or received an adverse appraisal/Grade 'D' in performance appraisal, should not be eligible - However, said 2 circulars enabled appellants to eliminate such employees, which was clearly contrary to provisions contained in statutory service rules - Procedure prescribed under 2 circulars clearly had the effect of supplanting provision of eligibility, which was not permissible - Such an additional provision could not be justified on the basis that it would form part of minimum merit required to be considered for promotion - Determination of bare minimum criteria was function of DPC and could not be taken-over by management at the time of determining the eligibility of a candidate u/r. 2(e) of the Rules - It was fallacious to presume that under the Rules, once an officer get minimum marks in written examination, he would be entitled to be promoted on the basis of seniority alone - There was no warrant for such a presumption - Misconduct committed by eligible employee/officer would be a matter for DPC to take into consideration at the time of performance appraisal - Past conduct of an employee could always be taken into consideration in adjudging the suitability of officer for performing the duties of higher post -Different rules/regulations of banks provide specific punishments such as 'withholding of promotion, reduction in rank, lowering in ranks/pay scales' - However, there was another range of penalty such as censure, reprimand, withholding of increments etc. which were prescribed under various staff regulations - To debar such an employee from being considered for promotion would tantamount to also inflicting on such employee, the punishment of withholding of promotion - In those circumstances, a punishment of censure/ reprimand would, in fact, read as censure/reprimand + 5 years debarment from promotion - Thus, circulars issued by appellants debarring such employees from being considered would be clearly contrary to statutory rules - Circulars clearly did not fall within ratio of Sant Ram Sharma v. State of Rajasthan and Another, 1967 Indlaw SC 234 - HC rightly quashed said two circulars and directed that respondent be considered for promotion - Appeals dismissed.
