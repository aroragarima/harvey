Sunil Kumar v Vipin Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 506; (2014) 8 SCC 868; AIR 2014 SC 3400; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3367; 2014 CRLJ 4171; JT 2014 (9) SC 1; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3303; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 126; 2014(9) SCALE 234

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.376 - grant of bail - Legality - Convicted respondent nos. 2 and 3 filed criminal appeal against their conviction by the Additional District and Sessions Judge - Criminal Revision No was also filed against the acquittal of the appellant from charges u/s. 376 IPC - Criminal Appeal and the Criminal Revision were still pending for disposal before the HC - In the meanwhile, the first application for bail moved by the convicted respondent nos. 2 and 3 in the Criminal Appeal was rejected by the DB of HC - However, the subsequent application of the convicted respondent nos. 2 and 3 in the same Criminal Appeal was allowed by HC vide Order requiring them to furnish individually, a personal bond of Rs.1 lakh with two sureties each, to the satisfaction of the Trial Court - It was against the enlargement of the respondent nos. 2 and 3 on bail by the HC, that the appellant had appealed before SC - It was contended by State that the HC erred in granting bail to the respondents in exercise of power u/s. 389 of CrPC without assigning any legal and acceptable reason being oblivious to the nature and gravity of the offence, the evidence being led thereof and the punishment awarded by the Trial Court - It was further contended that the deceased and the father of the appellant were assaulted with repeated blows on chest, head and shoulder - That was to say that the deceased was assaulted mercilessly by the respondents - Therefore, respondent nos. 2 and 3 did not deserve to be enlarged on bail by the HC -

Held, SC was of the opinion that the HC had rightly applied its discretionary power u/s. 389 of CrPC to enlarge the respondents on bail - Firstly, both the Criminal Appeal and Criminal Revision filed by both the parties were pending before the HC which means that the convictions of the respondents are not confirmed by the appellate court - Secondly, it was an admitted fact that the respondents had been granted bail earlier and they did not misuse the liberty - Also, the respondents had conceded to the occurrence of the incident though with a different version - SC was of the opinion that the HC had taken into consideration all the relevant facts including the fact that the chance of the appeal being heard in the near future is extremely remote, hence, the HC had released the respondents on bail on the basis of sound legal reasoning - Appeal dismissed.
