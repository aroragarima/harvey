Rajinder Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, T.S. Thakur, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 155; (2015) 6 SCC 477; AIR 2015 SC 1359; 2015 CRLJ 1934; 2015 (2) JCC 1293; JT 2015 (2) SC 607; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1928; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 737; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 129; 2015 (3) RLW 2359; 2015(3) SCALE 174; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, dowry death, Woman, Sarpanch, Commission Of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Deceased died due to consumption of pesticide - Appellant accused and his older brother and his wife were charged for offence of dowry death - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 304B IPC and acquitted other accused - Appeal before HC - HC confirmed the conviction and sentence of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, demand for dowry should not be stale but should be the continuing cause for death of married woman u/s. 304B IPC. In instant case, demands for money were made shortly after one year of the marriage. A she-buffalo was given by father to deceased as a peace offering. The peace offering had no effect. Deceased was ill-treated. Deceased went back to her father and demanded money again. The father, then, went along with his brother and Sarpanch of the village to the matrimonial home with a request that deceased be not ill-treated on account of the demand for money. The father also assured the said persons that their money demand would be fulfilled and that they would have to wait till the crops of his field are harvested. Fifteen days before her death, deceased again visited her parents' house on being maltreated by her new family. Then came death by poisoning. The cross-examination of the father of deceased has, in no manner, shaken his evidence. On the facts, therefore, the concurrent findings recorded by both the courts below are upheld. Appeal dismissed.
