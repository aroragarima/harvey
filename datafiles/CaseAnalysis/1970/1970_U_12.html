Becker Gray and Company (1930) Limited and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, S.M. Sikri, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 261; (1970) 1 SCC 352; AIR 1971 SC 116; [1971] 41 Comp Cas 887; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 445

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Exchange Control & FDI; Fema

Keywords: Under Invoicing

Summary: Exchange Control & FDI - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 12(1), 27, 23 and 23A - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 167(8) - Payment for exported goods - Filing of declaration as to valuation - Incorrect declaration by party in contravention of rules framed u/s. 27 or forms - Does undervaluation in declaration amount to contravention of restrictions u/s. 12(1) - Whether incorrect declaration may be penalized u/ss. 23 or 23A of FERA, 1947 - Held, since s. 23A cannot be invoked for levying penalty u/s. 167(8) of Sea Customs Act, 1878, such declaration may only be penalised u/s. 23 of FERA, 1947 - Appeals allowed.
