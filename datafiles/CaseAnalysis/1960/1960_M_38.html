Messrs North Brook Jute Company Limited And Another v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1960 SC 879; 1960 (1) FLR 183; 1960 (1) LLJ 580; 1961 (1) SCJ 90; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Closure, Rationalisation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 3(2), 9 A, 33 and 33 A - Conditions of service - Works committee - (A) Whether refusal to do additional work under rationalisation scheme, which employer has no right in law to order amount to strike?- Held, refusal of work by workers under rationalisation scheme, which employer has no right in law to order does not amount to a 'strike' by workmen; but even if it could be said to be a 'strike' such strike is certainly not illegal or unjustified - (B) At what time conditions of service of workers can be said to be altered as per s. 33 of 1947 Act ? - (C) Validity of order of tribunal holding that closure amounted to lock out - Held, conditions of service of workers can be said to be altered as per Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33 when change of notice u/s 9 A given is actually effected - Further held that, Works Committee was not intended to supplant or supersede unions for purpose of collective bargaining and are not authorised to consider real or substantial changes in conditions of service - Further held that, closure of mills by employer amounted to an illegal lock-out, and workmen, unable to work in consequence of lockout, are entitled to wages for period of absence, used by such lock out - Appeal dismissed.
