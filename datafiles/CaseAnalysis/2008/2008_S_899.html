Dalbir Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 856; (2008) 11 SCC 425; AIR 2008 SC 2389; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 3278; 2008 CRLJ 3061; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1365; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1964; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 353; 2008(8) SCALE 504; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1026

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Co-Accused, Blood Stained Earth, Duty Of Court, Lalkara

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148 - Appeal against conviction and conviction - Held, even if major portion of evidence is found to be deficient, residue is sufficient to prove guilt of an accused, notwithstanding acquittal of large number of other co-accused persons, his conviction can be maintained - Where chaff can be separated from grain, it would be open to the Court to convict an accused notwithstanding the fact that evidence has been found to be deficient to prove guilt of other accused persons - Appeal dismissed.
