R.L.Gupta & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 406; (1988) 2 SCC 250; (1988) SCC (L&S) 492; AIR 1988 SC 968; 1988 (7) ATC 80; 1988 (34) DLT 343; 1988 (57) FLR 242; JT 1988 (1) SC 556; 1989 LabIC 1169; 1988 (2) LLJ 113; 1988 (1) LLN 915; 1988(1) SCALE 517; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 255; 1988 (2) SLJ 164; 1988 (2) SLR 133B; 1988 (1) UJ 633

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Civil Services Rules

Summary: Judges & Advocates - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Delhi Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1970, rr. 12 and 16 - Seniority - Member of judicial service going on deputation - Held, that petitioner shall be deemed to have been on probation and his services shall be regulated accordingly, i.e., his seniority won't be affected - (A) Whether justified by Delhi Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1970, to place a judicial officer, promoted to higher judicial service, on probation for nearly 9 years after his promotion ? - Further held, that by appointing judicial officers of higher judicial service to temporary posts instead of appointing them to permanent posts Delhi Administration has virtually made a mockery of rules of recruitment - To place a judicial officer, promoted to higher judicial service, on probation for nearly 9 years after his promotion as in this case is a mere farce - (B) Whether judicial officer should not retain his seniority if goes on a deputation having been informed by High Court and at his own risk? - Merely because they have given their consent to go on deputation they should not be allowed to suffer unless there is a specific rule to contrary or other good reason for it - Further held, it could not be contended that petitioner judicial officer having been informed by HC that he was going on deputations at his own risk he could not retain his seniority during period of deputation - Petition allowed.
