Union of India v Shri Ram Mehar and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, A.K. Mukherjea, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 458; (1973) 1 SCC 109; AIR 1973 SC 305; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 28 - Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967, ss. 4(3) and 23(2) - Whether market value on which interest has to be awarded in circumstances mentioned in s. 4(3) of Amending Act would include statutory charge of 15% on that market value as provided for by s. 23(2) of Principal Act? - Held, term 'market value' has acquired a definite connotation by judicial decisions - Any addition to value of land to owner whose land is compulsorily acquired which addition is result of such factors as are unrelated to open market cannot be regarded as a part of market value - It is significant and has been noticed at an earlier stage also that according to other sections which appear in Principal Act interest is payable on such amount which is either a part of compensation or is total compensation which is payable itself - If market value and compensation were intended by legislature to have same meaning it is difficult to comprehend why word 'compensation' in ss. 28 and 34 and not 'market value' was used - The key to meaning of word 'compensation' is to be found in s. 23(1) and that consists (a) of market value of land and (b) sum of 15% on such market value which is stated to be consideration for compulsory nature of acquisition - Market value is therefore only one of components in determination of amount of compensation - If Legislature has used word 'market value' in s. 4(3) of Amending Act of 1967 it must be held that it was done deliberately and what was intended was that interest should be payable on market value of land and not on amount of compensation otherwise there was no reason why Parliament should not have employed word 'compensation' in aforesaid provision of Amending Act - Court is unable to accept view of High Court that market value in s. 4(3) of Amending Act means same thing as compensation and includes amount of 15% payable u/s. 23(2) on market value of land - Appeal succeeds to extent that amount awarded to claimants shall be computed in accordance with court's decision - Appeal dismissed.
