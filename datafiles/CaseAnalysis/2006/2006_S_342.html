Ram Pravesh Singh and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 515; (2006) 8 SCC 381; 2006 (6) AWC 5562; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 62; 2006(9) SCALE 468; 2006 (6) SLR 33; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar Co-operative Societies Act

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 141, 142, 21 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 78A - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25-FF, 25F(c) - Appellants were employees of a co-operative society under liquidation - Merger of Society with Bihar State Electricity Board - Whether there is any obligation on part of Board - Either contractual or statutory, or on equitable considerations-to absorb services of appellants? - Held, Board had never agreed nor decided to take services of any of employees of Society - Employees had no right to claim any retrenchment compensation from Board, nor did they have any right to claim to be in continuous employment on same terms and conditions, after purchase of undertaking by Board - Board has no obligation towards employees of previous owner of undertaking - Appeal dismissed.
