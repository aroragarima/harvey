Sahdeo @ Sahdeo Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 393; (2010) 3 SCC 705; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 790; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1299; 2010 CRLJ 2034; JT 2010 (2) SC 423; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 1011; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2010(2) SCALE 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 364 - Contempt petition - 'X' filed writ petition alleging that the respondents had detained 'Y' illegally since 29-5-1997 and his whereabouts were not known - HC disposed of said writ petition by transferring the investigation to the CBI - HC also held that taking the 'Y' into custody was in violation of the directions issued by this Court in D. K. Basu vs. State of West Bengal 1996 INDLAW SC 1546, and held all the three alleged contemnors guilty, thus, awarded sentence to them, and direction was issued to the State to terminate their services after holding disciplinary proceedings - Appellant contended that contempt proceedings are quasi-criminal in nature and Court while deciding the criminal case does not have competence to issue any direction affecting the civil rights of the parties - Hence, present appeal - Whether contempt proceedings had been concluded in conformity with the Allahabad High Court Rules, 1952? - Held, no - Contempt proceedings had been concluded without ensuring the compliance of the mandatory provisions of the Rules 1952 as appellants had never been informed as to what were the charges against them - The relevant documents on the basis of which the HC had taken a prima facie view while initiating the contempt proceedings suo motu, had not been made available to them - Notice itself was not only defective, but inaccurate and totally misleading - Facts and circumstances of the case warrant reversal of the aforesaid judgment and order - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
