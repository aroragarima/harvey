Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal II v Birla Gwalior Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 257; (1973) SCC (Tax) 519; (1974) 3 SCC 196; AIR 1973 SC 2486; 1973 (2) CTR 349; [1973] 89 ITR 266; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 902; 1973 TAXLR 1317

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Agent - Revenue expenditure - Office allowance - Deductions - Real Income - Assesses/respondent is the managing agent of the National Bearing Co. Ltd. and Gwalior Rayon and Silk Manufacturing Co - As managing agent of the former company it was entitled to receive a commission of 12 1/2 per cent on the net profits of the managed company together with a sum of Rs. 18,000 as office allowance - In the agreement entered into between the assesses company and managed companies no date for payment of the managing agency commission appears to have been stipulated - Commission was given up by assesses company after the end of the financial year but before the accounts of the managed Co. were made up -Accounts of the managed companies appear to have been made up somewhere during the end of September of the year following the respective accounting years - But in the case of office allowance the same was given up even before the end of the financial year - On the basis of these facts the Income tax Officer as well as Appellate Assistant Commissioner came to the conclusion that the deductions claimed were not allowable - Income-tax Appellate Tribunal differed from the view taken by the Income-tax Officer and the Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal held that the commission as well as the office allowance were given up by the assesses on the ground of commercial expediency - HC agreed with the view taken by the Tribunal - Hence this appeal - Whether assesses as Managing Agency commission was allowable as a revenue expenditure under 1922 Act and whether sum of Rs. 30,000 said to have been foregone by assessee as office allowance receivable from Gwalior Rayon and Silk Manufacturing Co. Ltd. was allowable - Held, assessee company gave up the office allowance on the ground of commercial expediency - It opined that managed company's financial position was not sound during the relevant accounting years and it was necessary for the assessee company to give up the office allowance in order to stabilize the finances of the managed company because of the sacrifices made by the assessee company the finances of the managed company improved subsequently as a result of which the assessee company was able to earn more profits in the later years - Expenditure incurred came within the scope of S. 10(2) (xv) - Assessee was maintaining its accounts on the basis of mercantile system - Its accounting year was the financial year - It gave up the commission after the end of the financial year - No due date was fixed for the payment of the commission under the managing agency agreements - Commission receivable could have been ascertained only after the managed company made up its accounts - Assessee had given up the commission even before the managed company made up its accounts - Commission given up by assessee cannot be considered as it real income - Appeals dismissed.
