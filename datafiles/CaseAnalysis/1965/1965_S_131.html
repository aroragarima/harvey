State of Maharashtra v Mohanlal Devichand Shah

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 121; AIR 1966 SC 189; 1966 CRLJ 176; 1965 (11) FLR 108; 1965 (2) LLJ 157; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Mines Act, 1923 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 2(b), 18, 22A and 22(B) - Minimum Wages (Central) Rules, 1950 - Whether quarry in which employees are alleged to be carrying on operation of stone breaking or stone crushing is a mine, within s.2(b) of 1948 Act ? - There is no indication whatsoever in Act that word 'mine' has narrower meaning - Held, examining definition of 'appropriate Govt.' in s. 2(b) it seems that Central Legislature must have intended to include quarries in word 'mine', otherwise it would be rather incongruous that some matters such as health and safety, hours and employment in quarries should be regulated by Central Govt. and minimum wages by state Govts. - Appeals allowed.
