H. H. Sethu Parvathi Bai v Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 276; [1968] 69 ITR 864

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(m), 27(1) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3 - Deduction of Wealth Tax - Challenged - Respondent/Income Tax Officer made assessment of appellant/assessee's to wealth tax for years 1958-59, 1959-60, 1960-61 - Appellant took matter before respondent/appellant Assistant Commissioner of Wealth Tax and contended that liability to income-tax and wealth-tax should be deducted in computing net wealth of appellant - Respondent/Appellant Assistant Commissioner rejected claim - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before Tribunal which allowed appeal and directed that liability to wealth-tax should be deducted in computing net wealth of appellant for respective years - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before HC u/s. 27 of the 1957 Act which answered two issues in favour of appellant and 1 issue related liability of wealth tax payable to respective years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, on facts and circumstances of case, liability towards wealth-tax calculated on basis of wealth-tax returns filed by assessee was admissible deduction u/s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act for purpose of computation of net wealth of assessee for a/ ys 1958-59, 1959-60 and 1960-61 - Held, so far as a/y 1958-59 was concerned, liability, imposed on appellant to pay wealth-tax on 31-3-1958, which was valuation date, and so amount of wealth-tax should be deducted from estimated value of assets as on that date in determining assets taxable for a/y 1958-59 - Hence, Court hold, that in facts and circumstances of case, liability towards wealth-tax calculated on basis of wealth-tax return filed by appellant was admissible deduction u/s. 2(m) of the 1957 Act for purpose of computation of net wealth of appellant for a/ys 1958-59, 1959-60 and 1960-61 - Appeal allowed.
