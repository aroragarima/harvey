Hussainara Khatoon and Others (Iv) v Home Secretary, State of Bihar, Patna

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 112; (1980) SCC (Cr) 40; (1980) 1 SCC 98; AIR 1979 SC 1369; 1979 CRLJ 1045; 1980 MLJ 86; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 532

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Equal Justice, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Protective custody - Speedy trial - Legality of - Petitioners were previously confined in Central Jail prior to their release were regularly produced before Court as and when required by Courts - Remand orders were made from time to time by Magistrates in regard to under-trial prisoners - These under-trial prisoners was in jail for period of over six to seven years - SC in order to know whether under-trial prisoners who were directed to be released on their personal bond were periodically produced before Magistrates directed to State to filed revised chart in regard to under-trial prisoners in all 65 jails in manner which would show year-wise as to what was date from which each of them was in jail after making broad division into two categories of minor offences and major offences - Hence, instant Petition.

Held, State cannot avoid its constitutional obligation to provide speedy trial to accused by pleading financial or administrative inability. It is also Constitutional obligation of SC as guardian of the fundamental rights of people, as sentinel on qui vive, to enforce fundamental right of accused to speedy trial by issuing necessary directions to State which may include taking of positive action, such as augmenting and strengthening the investigative machinery, setting up new courts, building new Court houses, providing more staff and equipment to Courts, appointment of additional judges and other measures calculated to ensure speedy trial. Powers of SC in protection of Constitutional rights are of widest amplitude and it do not see why SC should not adopt similar activist approach and issue to State directions which may involve taking of positive action with view to securing enforcement of fundamental right to speedy trial. But in order to enable Court to discharge this constitutional obligation, it is necessary that Court should have requisite information bearing on problem. Thus, State is directed to furnish particulars as to number of cases where FIR have been lodged and cases are pending investigation by police in each sub-division of State and where such cases have been pending investigation for more than six months, State of Bihar will furnish broadly reasons why there has been such delay in investigative process. Petition disposed of.
