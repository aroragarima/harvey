Union of India and others v Ramesh Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 811; (2012) 1 SCC 476; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 417; 2012 CRLJ 3500; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 664; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Private Party

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Orders by fraud - Misrepresentation - Criminal Conspiracy - Validity - Private company committed breach of contractual obligations - Respondent who were obliged in law to take penal action against private company for breach, failed to take penal action - Due to breach committed, supply was stopped to private company - Private company approached court seeking enforcement of directions of Coal Controller - Accused deliberately suppressed fact that private company had committed a breach of its contractual obligations and enabled private company to obtain favourable order - Accused committed fraud by suppression of crucial fact of breach of contractual obligations of private company - Suppression of fact, amounts to criminal conspiracy enable private company to secure an illegal monetary gain by manipulating judicial process - Respondent appeal in HC who quashed said FIR which was approved by SC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether orders of HC and SC which were obtained by respondent by means of fraudulent and misrepresentation was sustainable - Held, power of quashing criminal proceeding should be exercised very sparingly and with circumspection and that too in rarest of rare cases, court will not be justified in embarking upon an enquiry as to reliability or genuineness or otherwise of allegations made in FIR or complaint and that extraordinary or inherent powers do not confer an arbitrary jurisdiction on Court to act according to its whim or caprice - In view of law laid down impugned FIR does not merit interference, as it was not a case of even respondent (writ petitioners) that FIR was required to be quashed on any one of the grounds legally recognised by SC to be sufficient ground for quashing an FIR - Judgment cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
