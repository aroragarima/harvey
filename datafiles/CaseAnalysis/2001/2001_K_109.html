Chetan Dass v Kamla Devi

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20206; (2001) 4 SCC 250; AIR 2001 SC 1709; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 34; 2001 (43) ALR 789; 2001 (3) AWC 1965; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 327; 2001 (2) CGLJ 169; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 162; JT 2001 (5) SC 21; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 26; 2001(3) SCALE 399; 2001 (4) SCJ 552; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 20; 2001 (3) SLT 420; 2001 (3) Supreme 403; 2001 (1) UC 706

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13, 23 and 9 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Dissolution of marriage - Application for divorce by husband - 2 years of marriage - Wife had deserted him and had not been living with him - Consequent to improvement in relationship petition for divorce dismissed by court - Wife filing criminal complaint u/ss. 494 and 120 B of IPC - Husband filed for restitution of conjugal rights - Subsequently petition converted to dissolution of marriage - Wife contesting petition and alleging adulterous conduct of husband - Wife still willing to live with husband provided husband disassociates himself from lady with whom he was having illicit relationship - Trial court refusing to grant divorce - High Court upholding findings of trial court - Whether decision of HC in upholding findings of Trial Court and refusing decree for divorce is valid and whether decree of divorce can be granted on ground of marriage having broken irretrievably? - Held, averments regarding desertion by wife having been made without any reasonable cause, petition liable to be dismissed on that ground alone - Behavior of appellant certainly falls in category of misconduct on his part - In such circumstances, it is too much on his part to claim that he be given advantage of his own wrong and be granted a decree of divorce on ground of desertion on part of his wife who is still prepared to live with him provided he snaps his relationship with other woman - Similar offer had also been made on behalf of appellant - A decree of divorce on ground of marriage having been irretrievably broken cannot be granted in facts and circumstances of case as indicated above - Appeal dismissed.
