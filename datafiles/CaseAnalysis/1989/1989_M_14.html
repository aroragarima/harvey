Pujari Bai v Madan Gopal

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 833; (1989) 3 SCC 433; AIR 1989 SC 1764; JT 1989 (3) SC 43; 1989(2) SCALE 1; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 383; 1989 (2) UJ 454

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948, ss. 14 and 30 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, s. 10-A - Consolidation proceedings - Transfer of property referred in s. 30 of 1948 Act - Without reference to transfer of Sanad u/s. 10 - In execution of High Court's directions - By depriving other allotees - But appellant had got sanad before it - Claim of absolute ownership - Plea that it was not open for consolidation authority to cancel sanad - What is impact of grant of sanad u/s. 10 of 1954, Act and its effect on jurisdiction of authorities under Consolidation Act? - (B) Res judicata - Writ petition filed by party - Dismissal in limine or dismissal on ground of laches or availability of alternative - Held, (A) Authorities under Consolidation Act have no jurisdiction or power to modify or cancel grant of proprietary rights granted in Sanad u/s. 10 of 1954 Act - (B) Would not operate as res-judicata - Appeal allowed.
