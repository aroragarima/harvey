Mukhtiar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 434; AIR 2016 SC 3100; 2016 CRLJ 4191; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 558; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Currency Notes, Signature, Bribe, Trap, Raiding Party, Discrepancy, Phenolphthalein, Valuable Thing, Reward, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Complainant, Investigating Officer, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 20(1), 7 - Acceptance of Bribe - Conviction Challenged - Appellant/accused rendered service as revenue patwari and complainant 'X' approached and requested accused for copy of jamabandi of land - Trial Court convicted accused for commission of offence u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 7 of Act and sentenced to suffer RI for two years each under both the section - HC in appeal confirmed order of conviction passed by Trial Court, however, reduced sentence of imprisonment from two years to one year for each of two offences - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, demand of illegal gratification is a sine qua non to constitute the offence under the Act. Further mere recovery of currency notes itself does not constitute the offence under the Act, unless it is proved beyond all reasonable doubt that the accused voluntarily accepted the money knowing it to be bribe. In other words, once the demand and voluntary acceptance of illegal gratification knowing it to be the bribe are proved by evidence then conviction must follow u/s. 7 of the Act, against the accused. Indeed, these twin requirements are sine qua non for proving the offence u/s. of the Act. In instant case, twin requirements of demand and acceptance of illegal gratification were proved in the case on hand on the basis of evidence adduced by the prosecution against the appellant. Hence, appellant was rightly convicted and sentenced for the offence punishable u/s. 7 of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
