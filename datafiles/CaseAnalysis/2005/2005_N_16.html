Mahindra and Mahindra Limited v N.B. Narawade

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Tarun Chatterjee, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 142; (2005) 3 SCC 134; (2005) SCC (L&S) 361; AIR 2005 SC 1993; 2005 (5) AWC 3905; 2005 (2) CalLT(SC) 121; 2005 (1) CLR 803; 2005 (117) DLT 697; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 4; JT 2005 (2) SC 583; 2005 (2) KLT 32; 2005 (1) LLJ 1129; 2005 (1) LLN 1074; 2005(2) SCALE 302; 2005 (2) SLR 765; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1641

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Supervisor, Miscarriage

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Misconduct - Dismissal altered to one of reinstatement with continuity of service and 2/3 rd back wages by Division Bench - Whether Labour Court right in reducing punishment? - Held, there must be existence of any mitigating circumstances which requires reduction of sentence, or past conduct of workman which may persuade Labour Court to reduce punishment - In absence of any such factor existing, Labour Court can not by way of sympathy alone exercise power under s.11-A and reduce punishment - Upheld order of disciplinary authority dismissing respondent- Workman from service - Appeal allowed.
