Shanti Sharma And Others v Ved Prabha And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28909; (1987) 4 SCC 193; AIR 1987 SC 2028; JT 1987 (3) SC 409; 1987(2) SCALE 393; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(e) - Decree for eviction - Bona fide requirement - Challenged - Respondent-landlord filed an application for eviction before the Rent Controller - Rent Controller granted order for eviction u/s. 14(1)(e) i.e. the premises were bona fide required by the respondent-landlord for their own residence - Trial Court and the appellate court both came to the conclusion that the respondent-landlord has established his bona fide requirement and order of eviction was maintained - Hence, present appeal - Appellant contended that as there was a notice from the DDA cancelling the lease of the respondent-landlord and as the lease was cancelled of the plot of land on which part of the premises in dispute stands the respondent-landlord is not entitled to decree for eviction - Held, phrase 'owner' has to be understood, where the person builds up his property and lets out to the tenant and subsequently needs it for his own use, he should be entitled to an order or decree for eviction, the only thing necessary for him to prove is bona fide requirement and that he is the owner thereof - In this context, meaning of the term 'owner' is vis-a-vis the tenant i.e. the owner should be something more than the tenant - In the present case, where the plot of land is taken on lease the structure is built by the respondent-landlord and admittedly he is the owner of the structure - He holds a long lease and in view of the matter as against the tenant it could not be doubted that he will fall within the ambit of the meaning of the term 'owner' as is contemplated u/s. 14(1)(e) - It could not be said that the respondent-landlord has ceased to be the owner thereof, in view of broad meaning of term 'owner' - Appeal dismissed.
