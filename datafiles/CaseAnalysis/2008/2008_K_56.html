Ranganayakamma And Another v K.S. Prakash (D) By L.Rs. And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2081; (2008) 15 SCC 673; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1218; 2008(6) ALL MR 904; JT 2008 (8) SC 510; 2009 (2) KarLJ 1; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 550; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 601; 2008(9) SCALE 144; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 297

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Charges Act, 1925

Summary: Land & Property - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 59, 65, 110, 3 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Instant appeal was filed against the judgment and order of the HC, dismissing an appeal preferred from the judgment and decree of lower court partly decreeing the suit for partition and separate possession - Appellants alleged on the various points such as, court below had committed an error while deciding case, that, averments made in the suit being fraught with the elements of fraud and misrepresentation, that, the deed of partition and the deed of relinquishment were void ab initio - Whether, appellant should be granted with relief - Held, after consideration of the facts and the circumstances of the case, and considering and discussing the contentions so raised by the appellant and the respondent, instant court reached to the conclusion that, the suit initially filed was barred by the limitation - Instant court decided all the contentions of appellant against him - Deed of partition and effect thereof were set aside - Appeal dismissed.
