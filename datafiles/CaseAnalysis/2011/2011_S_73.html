Sherimon v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 785; AIR 2012 SC 493; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 3895; 2012 CRLJ 988; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 946

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120 (B), 302, 324 and 392 - Conspiracy - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/A4 and A1 to A3 allegedly murdered deceased - Trial Court convicted A1 u/s. 302, IPC and convicted appellant A2 and A3 u/s. 324 r/w s. 120-B, IPC - Appeal against conviction filed before HC, which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal against conviction of appellant u/s. 324 r/w s. 120B IPC - Appellant contended that he was not present when offence was committed and there was no cogent evidence on basis of which conspiracy could be inferred - Whether HC and Trial Court was justified in convicting appellant u/s. 324 r/w. 120-B, IPC - Held, gist of offence of conspiracy was agreement between two or more persons to do or cause to be done an illegal act or a legal act by illegal means and there must be meeting of minds resulting in an ultimate decision taken by conspirators regarding commission of crime - In instant case, there was no evidence to establish meeting of minds between appellant and other accused persons - PW-5 only witness examined by prosecution to prove alleged meeting between appellant and other accused turned hostile - Hence, evidence on record were in adequate to establish that appellant was a party to alleged criminal conspiracy - Order of HC and Trial Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
