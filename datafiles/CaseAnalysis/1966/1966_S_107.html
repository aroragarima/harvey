State of Mysore and Another v S. V. Narayanappa

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1967 SC 1071; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 128; 1967 SLR 117

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Mysore Civil Service Rules 1958

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311 and 311(2) - Mysore Civil Service Rules 1958, r. 8(27 A) - Mysore Govt. Servants (Seniority) Rules, 1957, r. I - State Govt, by an order terminated service of respondent as a second division clerk - High Court of Mysore quashed memorandum - Whether continuity of service is indispensable for regularisation and whether HC had correctly interpreted impugned Government Order terminating service of respondent? - Held, since break in service of respondent during material time was only of one day - Govt. assured that it would not do anything to adversely affect respondents service - On point of correctness of HC's interpretation of sub-cl. (ii) which provides that services of such candidates shall be regularized with effect from date of their appointment from which their services are continuous provided they were in service on 1-1-1960 and continue to be in service at time their services are regularized - It is clear from express words that continuity of service from 1-1-1960 until date of order is a condition prescribed for regularization - A candidate claiming benefit of this order has to satisfy that he was initially appointed prior to 31-12-1959, that he was in service on 1-1-1960 and continued in that service, till date of order, i.e, 22-09-1961 - Construction finds support from sub-cl. (iii) which provides that local service prior to regularization would be counted for purpose of leave, pension and increments though not for seniority as seniority was to be fixed from length of service calculated from date of regularization - Unless local service was continuous such service could not be taken into account for purposes, in particular of pension and increments - It is impossible to consider increments being granted unless service prior to such increments was continuous - Sub-cl. (iv) provides that a break in service would not be condoned for a period howsoever short - Continuity of service is thus a condition for both sub-cls. 2 and 3 - HC was in error - Proper interpretation would be that in order that regularization order may apply to a particular case ,local candidate must be initially appointed prior to 31-12-1959, he must be in service on 1-1-1960 and continue to be in service without any break till date of said order - Appeal dismissed.
