Â M/S. Ramchand Jagadish Chand v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 174; AIR 1963 SC 563; 1962 (2) SCJ 189; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 72

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Constitution - Customs - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 19 - Petitioners firm was engaged in business of exports and imports - Petitioner applied licenses for Artsilk yarn for Rs. 4,04,218.62 np. and Rs. 3,03,490.93 np. respectively for months of February and March 1959 and claimed that they had, pursuant to Export Promotion Scheme, exported artsilk goods to abroad and had earned net foreign exchange of value of Rs. 7,07,709.55 np. and that they were entitled to import licences for Artsilk yarn of that amount - In September 1959, petitioners were informed by Controller of Imports and Exports that a consolidated licence for months of February and March, 1959 was granted to them for import of artsilk goods of value of Rs. 3,19,354/- It appears that Govt. had came to learn of certain malpractice by importers of artsilk yarn, while suspending Export Promotion Scheme as from March 9, 1959, announced that applications which were pending with port licensing authorities will be scrutinized by a Committee and accordingly appointed a Committee for verification of value of goods exported - Petitioners appeared before Committee and furnished documentary evidence in support of their claim for 100% of rupee equivalent of cloth exported but committee accepted few export rates as reasonable and few as unreasonable and Controller of licences accepted recommendation of Committee and issued to petitioners an import licence for Rs. 3,19,354/- only - Hence instant Petition seeking direction for directing Controller of Imports and Exports to grant licence for months of February and March 1959 equivalent to 100% of goods exported by them and to quash impugned order as said order was against art. 19 Constitution - Whether licence granted by controller by reducing license amount was justified - Held, controller of Imports and Exports under his affidavit stated that Export Promotion Scheme was taken for wrongful advantage by some exporters of artsilk fabrics it was found by Govt. that index number of wholesale price in respect of "silk and rayon" fabrics during month of June 1957 was 85 and during the month of March 1959 it rose to 95.7 only thus showing a rise of about 11% against this rise price invoiced by exporters showed a rise of over 125% during span of same period this would clearly show that aforesaid rise was shown by merchants merely with a view to get licences for higher value for import of speculative item like "Art Silk Yarn" - Relied upon this evidence respondent-Union contended that this perversion of Export Promotion Scheme had serious repercussions on foreign exchange position and scheme was suspended by notification dt. 6-3-1995 and Govt. directed that pending applications for import licences for artsilk yarn be scrutinised by a Committee appointed in that behalf and in instant case Committee had scrutinised cases of 1106 parties including petitioners and petitioners were given a, licence for Rs. 3,19,354/-, and by reducing value of' import licence - Hence no fundamental right of petitioners u/art. 19 of Constitution was infringed and committee also observed that petitioners were "not able to produce adequate justification of prices of Art Silk Bush Shirting Cloth samples cannot be linked with relative purchase vouchers or export invoices - Thus it was case of respondents that many exporters were guilty of malpractices and with a view either to speculate in artsilk goods or to repatriate unlawfully foreign assets value of goods exported was unduly inflated hence controller was fully justified in reducing license amount - Petition dismissed.
