Mohammad Hasnuddin v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Jaswant Singh, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 515; (1979) 2 SCC 572; AIR 1979 SC 404; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 265

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Government of India Act, 1919, Hyderabad Land Acquisition Act, 1309

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Hyderabad Land Acquisition Act, 1309, s. 14(1) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Land acquisition - Reference to court - Limitation - Appellant's land was acquired and an award was made by Assistant Collector - Appellant filed a revision petition before Assistant Collector - Assistant Collector referred matter to District Collector, however, District Collector rejected revision - Appellant, thereafter, sought reference to Reference Court - Assistant Collector referred matter to Reference Court - Reference Court dismissed reference on ground that reference application was time barred - HC, in appellant's appeal, held that time taken for revision proceedings before Assistant Collector could not be excluded while computing period of limitation prescribed u/s. 14 (1) of 1309 Act (corresponding to s. 18 (1) of 1894 Act) - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that that court while dealing with a reference u/s. 14 (1) of 1309 Act, should not go behind reference and decline to answer it - Whether a court in dealing with a reference u/s.14 (1) of 1309 Act, corresponding to s. 18 (1) of 1894 Act should go behind reference made by Collector if application on which reference had been made was beyond period of limitation prescribed therein - Held, if an application was made which was not within time, Collector did not have power to make a reference - Even if a reference was wrongly made by Collector court would still have to determine validity of reference because very jurisdiction of court to hear a reference depends on a proper reference being made u/s. 18 of 1894 Act, and if reference was not proper, there was no jurisdiction in court to hear reference - Court had duty to see that statutory conditions laid down in s. 18 had been complied with, and it was not debarred from satisfying itself that reference which it was called upon to hear was a valid reference - It was only a valid reference which gave jurisdiction to court, and, therefore, court had to ask itself question whether it had jurisdiction to entertain reference - In deciding question of jurisdiction in a case of reference u/s. 18 by Collector to court, court was certainly not acting as a court of appeal; it was only discharging elementary duty of satisfying itself that a reference which it was called upon to decide was a valid and proper reference according to provisions of Act under which it was made - Court had, therefore, jurisdiction to decide whether reference was made beyond period prescribed by the, proviso to s. 18(2) of Act, and if it found that it was so made, decline to answer reference - Appeal dismissed.
