Improvement Trust, Ludhiana v Ujagar Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 448; (2010) 6 SCC 786; 2010 (81) ALR 767; 2010 (4) AWC 3890; JT 2010 (6) SC 205; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2241; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 141; 2010 (159) PLR 777; 2010 (111) RD 89; 2010(6) SCALE 173; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 90 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Delay condonation - Scope - Appellant/Trust acquired the lands of respondent Nos. 1 to 4, however, appellant did not deposit the compensation - Respondent/land owners approached the Court to attach the acquired land for realisation of compensation - Trial Court attached the property and Executing Court, subsequently, put the same for auction sale - Appellant filed objections to the sale u/O. 21 r. 90 CPC - Since appellant did not appear for the case, the Executing Court dismissed the objection in default due to non-appearance - Executing Court confirmed the sale in favour of 5th respondent - Appellant challenged the Trial Court order before the First Appellate Court with a delay condonation petition - First Appellate Court dismissed the appeal on the ground that appellant had failed to show good and sufficient cause for condoning the delay - Appellant filed second appeal before the HC - HC dismissed the second appeal - Held, while considering the application for condonation of delay no straight jacket formula is prescribed to come to the conclusion if sufficient and good grounds have been made out or not - Each case has to be weighed from its facts and the circumstances in which the party acts and behaves - From the conduct behaviour and attitude of the appellant it cannot be said that it had been absolutely callous and negligent in prosecuting the matter - No sooner the appellant came to know about the dismissal of its objection filed before the Executing Court, u/O. 21 r. 90 of the CPC it made enquiries and filed the appeal - Delay in filing the first appeal before the First Appellate Court for setting aside the sale has not been so huge warranting its dismissal on such hyper-technical ground - Appellant had taken all possible steps to prosecute the matter within time - Therefore, impugned orders are set aside and matter is remitted back to Executing Court to consider the appellant's objection u/O. 21, r. 90 - Appeal partly allowed.
