Isha Marbles and Others v Bihar State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, P.B. Sawant, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 383; (1995) 2 SCC 648; 1995 (1) BC 529; JT 1995 (2) SC 626; 1995(1) SCALE 721; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Bihar State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 - Liability of old consumer - In the instant case, the appellant had purchased the industry in an open auction sale held by the corporation under the Act - The appellant had paid a substantial sum towards the said transaction and thereafter got the possession of the industry - However, the electrical connection of the premises was disconnected when the appellant got possession of the said unit and he was called upon to the discharge all the liabilities of the previous consumer - Calling upon to pay the liabilities of the previous consumer was challenged in by the appellant - Whether, appellant in capacity of the auction purchaser was liable to meet the liability of old consumer or electricity to the premises which was purchased by him in the auction sale from the corporation - Held, that it was inadequate to enforce the liability of the old consumer against the auction purchaser who was a third party and was in no way connected with the previous owner/occupier, if it would have done, it would became a permit to the dishonest consumers and they would keep transferring their units from one hand to another, from time to time, without the payment of the dues - Instant court, after considering the facts and provisions of the law, reached to the conclusion that, it was impossible to impose on the appellant-purchasers a liability which was not incurred by him, and accordingly he was granted with relief so demanded by him - Appeals allowed.
