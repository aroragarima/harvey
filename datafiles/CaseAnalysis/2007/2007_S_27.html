Union of India and Another v S.K. Goel and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 98; (2007) 14 SCC 641; AIR 2007 SC 1199; 2007 (6) AWC 6513; 2007 (138) DLT 237; 2007 (209) E.L.T. 334; JT 2007 (3) SC 361; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 107; 2007(3) SCALE 5; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 432; 2007 (3) SLR 642

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Character Roll, Government Instructions

Summary: Service - Seniority - Held, observations of the High Court are wholly unjustified inasmuch as the post of Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise is a post required to be filled up on selection made strictly on the basis of merit - No judicial review of DPC proceedings, which are ordinarily conducted in accordance with the standing government instructions and Rules is warranted - DPC enjoyed full discretion to devise its method and procedure for objective assessment of suitability and merit of the candidate being considered by it - Interference by the High Court is not called for - Appeal allowed.
