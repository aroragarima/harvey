Ram Rattan Gupta v Director Of Enforcement, Foreign Exchangeregulation, And Ano

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1966 SC 495; [1966] 36 Comp Cas 49; 1966 CRLJ 457; 1966 MWN 21; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss.4(1), 4(3), 19(2) and 23(1)(a) - Whether appellant had lent money to bank and whether by said process he contravened provisions of Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947? - Held, when a person deposits free currency in current account of a bank in order to draw it whenever necessary for purpose for which it was given, it is not possible to hold that he enter% into a contract of loan with bank within meaning of s. 4(1) of Act - The person only deposits money for said purpose - Admittedly foreign exchange was acquired by appellant for purpose of meeting his expenditure during his tour of Far East countries; but he had not used entire foreign exchange - If so, the person should have without delay sold same to an authorised dealer - Instead he kept said amount in current account of various branches of Bank for a number of years - The order of the Foreign Exchange Regulation Appellate Board is modified - Order accordingly.
