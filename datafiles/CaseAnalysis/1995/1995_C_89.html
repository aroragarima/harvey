Pushpam Pharmaceuticals Company v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 954; (1995) Supp3 SCC 462; 1997 (71) ECR 329; 1995 (78) E.L.T. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11A - A/y 1978-79, 1979-80, 1980-81 and 1981-82 - Limitation - Demand - Appellant manufactured an item falling under Tariff Entry 14-E as well as another item under Item 68 of Tariff 1985 - The Item under Item 68 was fully exempt from payment of duty - Notification No. 111 of 1978 was issued on 9-5-1978 exempting turnover of goods manufactured under Item 14-E if it was below Rs. 5 lakhs - Department issued show cause notice alleging that if the turnover of the two items, i.e. exempted under Item 68 was clubbed together with turnover of Item 14-E, then it exceeded Rs 5 lakhs and the goods became liable to duty - Department invoked extended period of limitation of 5 years as according to it the duty was short-levied due to suppression of the fact that if the turnover was clubbed then it exceeded Rs. 5 lakhs - Whether the Department was justified in initiating proceedings for short levy by invoking proviso to s. 11A of 1944 Act for the years 1978-79, 1979-80, 1980-81 and 1981-82? - Held, section 11A of 1944 Act empowers the Department to reopen proceedings if the levy has been short-levied or not levied within six months from the relevant date - But the proviso carves out an exception and permits the authority to exercise this power within 5 years from the relevant date in the circumstances mentioned in the proviso, one of it being suppression of facts - A perusal of the proviso indicates that it has been used in company of such strong words as fraud, collusion or wilful default - It does not mean any omission - The act must be deliberate - In taxation, it can have only one meaning that the correct information was not disclosed deliberately to escape from payment of duty - Where facts are known to both the parties the omission by one to do what he might have done and not that he must have done, does not render it suppression - Extended period of limitation not be invoked - Matter remitted back to the authority for determining the turnover of the assessee in respect of only that period which is within six months from the date of issue of show-cause notice - Appeal allowed.
