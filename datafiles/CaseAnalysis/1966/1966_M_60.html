Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay South Poona v Murlidhar Jhawar and Purna Ginning and Pressing Factory, Dharam

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1966 SC 1536; [1966] 60 ITR 95; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.3, 23 B, 23(3) - Whether, assessment of unregistered firm was proper and legal, two partners of this partnership having been assessed in respect of their shares of income from this partnership business - Partners may be assessed individually or they may be assessed collectively in status of an unregistered firm - ITO cannot however seek to assess one income twice - Contention of appellant that ITO making first assessment of three parties to joint venture was not informed that three parties constituted an unregistered firm was not accepted - Order of ITO clearly indicates that he was cognizant of fact that income of joint venture was taxable collectively, but he thought that he could in law in first instance make an 'assessment provisionally' of three parties separately and then rectify assessments later - Held, assessement was not proper - Appeal dismissed.
