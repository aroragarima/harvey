State of Haryana and Others v Ram Kumar Mann

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1017; (1997) 3 SCC 321; (1997) SCC (L&S) 801; AIR 1997 SCW 1574; 1997 (76) FLR 746; JT 1997 (3) SC 450; 1997 LabIC 1541; 1997 (2) LLJ 1039; 1997 (2) LLN 616; 1997(2) SCALE 541; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 329; 1997 (2) SLR 391; 1997 (3) Supreme 110; 1997 (1) UJ 776; 1997 (2) UPLBEC 963

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Tendering of resignation by the respondent from service to enable him to contest election - Withdrawal of resignation pursuant to electoral loss - High Court allowed his writ noting that since three similarly situated persons had been given the same relief, he should be reinstated in service - Whether, finding of HC is justified? - Held, HC wrong in directing reinstatement of the respondent by a mandamus with all consequential benefits - Further held, wrong order cannot be the foundation for claiming equality for enforcement of the same order - Right must be founded upon enforceable right to entitle lien to the equality treatment for enforcement thereof - Appeal allowed.
