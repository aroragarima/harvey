Ram Kishan Fauji v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 208; (2017) 5 SCC 533; AIR 2017 SC 1535; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 1; 2017 (3) CTC 839; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 356; 2017(3) SCALE 561

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: MUNICIPALITIES & LOCAL GOVERNMENTS, Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, Government of India Act, 1935, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Karnataka Lokayukta Act, 1984, Maharashtra High Court (Hearing of Writ Petitions by the Division Bench and Abolition of Letters Patent Appeals) Act, 1986, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Haryana Lokayukta Act, 2002, Bombay City Civil Court and Bombay Court of Small Causes (Enhancement of Pecuniary Jurisdiction and Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 8 - Harayana Lokayukta Act, 2002, ss. 14, 8(1) - Registration of Complaint - Exercise of Jurisdiction Challenged - Respondent no.1/state govt. in exercise of power u/s. 8(1) of the Act made reference to Lokayukta to enquire into allegation against appellant/public servant - Reference was registered as complaint and lokayukta issued notice u/s. 14 f the Act - HC in writ petition by public servant directed state to inquire into authentication of item in question and to file status report based on judgment of Lalitha Kumari case, which dealt with enquiry to be conducted in respect of corruption cases - Further single judge of HC quashed FIR and DB of HC allowed LPA of state govt.- DB of HC declined to vacate interim order, thereby made it absolute after admit of LPA - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, (a) an appeal shall lie from the judgment of a Singleto a Division Bench of the High Court if it is so permitted within the ambit and sweep of the Letters Patent. (b) the power conferred on the HC by the Letters Patent can be abolished or curtailed by the competent legislature by bringing appropriate legislation. (c) a writ petition which assails the order of a civil court in the HC has to be understood, in all circumstances, to be a challenge u/art. 227 of Constitution and determination by the HC under the said Article and, hence, no intra-court appeal is entertainable. (d) the tenability of intra-court appeal will depend upon the Bench adjudicating the lis as to how it understands and appreciates the order passed by the Single Judge. There cannot be a straitjacket formula for the same. However, if an appeal is barred u/cl.10 or cl 15 of the Letters Patent, as the case may be, no appeal will lie. Further, the conception of 'criminal jurisdiction' as used in cl. 10 of the Letters Patent is not to be construed in the narrow sense. It encompasses in its gamut the inception and the consequence. It is the field in respect of which the jurisdiction is exercised, is relevant. The nomenclature of a writ petition is not the governing factor. What is relevant is what is eventually being sought to be enforced. In instant case, neither the Lokayukta nor Upa-Lokayukta has any jurisdiction or authority to direct implementation of his report by the constitutional functionary but when after investigation, it is found that the public servant has committed any criminal offence, prosecution can be initiated for which prior sanction of any authority is required under any law for such prosecution and the same shall be deemed to have been granted. Writ petition was filed u/art. 226 of Constitution for quashing recommendation of lokayukta, which led to launching of criminal investigation. The Single Judge of HC quashed registration of FIR and subsequent investigation. In such a situation to hold that the Single Judge, in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution, whether Judge has passed order in civil proceedings as the order of Lokayukta was that of quasijudicial authority, would be conceptually fallacious. It is because what matters is the nature of the proceeding, and that is the litmus test. Hence, the LPA was not maintainable before DB and order passed there in is wholly unsustainable. Hence, impugned order is set aside, however, liberty is granted to the State to challenge the order of the Single Judge. Appeal allowed.
