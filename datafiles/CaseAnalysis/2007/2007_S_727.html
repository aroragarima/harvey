Vishwanath Gupta v State of Uttaranchal

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1565; (2007) 11 SCC 633; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 33; 2007 CRLJ 2296; 2007 (2) JCC 1274; JT 2007 (5) SC 48; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 511; 2007(5) SCALE 114; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.181 - Commission of offence - Jurisdiction - There was news item published in newspaper to effect that one Advocate was pleading matters of members of one gang and was in touch with them on telephone and cell phone - Advocate had gone from Dehradoon to Lucknow by train along with one contractor and thereafter family members of Advocate were receiving calls for ransom amount and it was stated that advocate was abducted at Lucknow for ransom money - Thereafter, two bodies one that of contractor and other that of advocate were recovered from factory of accused - Application was filed that Nainital Court had no jurisdiction to frame charges as offence was committed in State and, therefore, State of Uttaranchal had no territorial jurisdiction and Additional Sessions Judge held that in view of provisions of s.181 (2) of CrPC, case should be tried in Court located either in District Unnao or District Lucknow in State of Uttar Pradesh - HC set aside order of Additional Sessions Judge and dismissed application - Hence, instant petition - Whether Additional Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to try suit - Held, in case offence was committed in two local areas then it could be tried by any of them - Any offence of kidnapping or abduction could be inquired or tried by Court within whose local jurisdiction person was kidnapped or abducted or was conveyed or concealed or detained - In case of kidnapping and abduction local jurisdiction could be where person concerned was abducted or kidnapped or was conveyed or concealed or detained - Therefore, it was case wherein offence was taken place at three places i.e. at Haldwani, Nainital, where threat to life of victim was given and demand of money was raised, victim was abducted from Lucknow and he was ultimately put to death - Thus, trial could be conducted in any of local jurisdiction within State - However, case was registered at police Station, Haldwani, Nainital and investigation started at Haldwani, therefore, local jurisdiction to try offence could be at Haldwani/Nainital - Hence, it was wrong to say that Additional Sessions Judge had no jurisdiction - Petition dismissed.
