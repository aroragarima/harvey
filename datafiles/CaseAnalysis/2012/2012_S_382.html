Sathya Narayanan v State represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 391; (2012) 12 SCC 627; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 674; JT 2012 (11) SC 57; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 694; 2012(10) SCALE 641; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 147, 302,149, 201 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Complete chain of link - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 147,302,149,201 of IPC, prosecution had filed chargesheet against 11 accused but Trial Court acquitted accused No. 6 to No. 11 and convicted accused No. 1 to 5 - Prosecution story was that accused No. 1 and No. 2 were having illicit relationship and deceased threatened to disclose if they did not share with her property - Trial Court recorded evidence and convicted appellants and acquitted other accused - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC, but same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that conviction was solely on basis of circumstantial evidence without there being any continuity of incriminating circumstances and presumption of certain facts which was not substantiated - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting appellants on basis of circumstantial evidence - Held, in case of circumstantial evidence, motive also assumes significance for the reason that the absence of motive would put the court on its guard and cause it to scrutinize each piece of evidence closely in order to ensure that suspicion, omission or conjecture do not take the place of proof - There was no direct evidence about cause of death, various circumstances projected by prosecution complete chain of link and established that, in all probability, act must have been done by appellants, all circumstances had been clearly discussed by trial Court and it rightly convicted and awarded appropriate sentence - Appeals dismissed.
