Lic Of India v R. Suresh

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 453; (2008) 11 SCC 319; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1887; 2008 (2) AWC 1806; 2008 (2) ESC 278; 2008 (118) FLR 1189; JT 2008 (5) SC 342; 2008 (2) KLT 95; 2008 (2) LLJ 708; 2008 (3) LLN 483; 2008(4) SCALE 512; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 208

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Terms And Conditions Of Service

Summary: Insurance Corporation Act, 1956 - Whether jurisdiction of Industrial Courts are ousted in regard to an order of dismissal passed by the Life Insurance Corporation of India, a Corporation constituted and incorporated under the Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956? - Held, LIC is a "State" within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution of India - Its duties and functions are provided for under the 1956 Act. The same by itself, however, having regard to the definition of "Industry" as contained in s. 2(j) of the Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 cannot take within its umbrage the functions of the Life Insurance Corporation outside its purview - Jurisdiction of the Industrial Court must be held to be ousted only when the remedy sought for by the workman is premised on a right under the industrial laws which is in conflict with the right granted to an employee, an agent or LIC - Appeal dismissed.
