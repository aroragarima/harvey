Deepak Sibal and Another v Punjab University and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 457; (1989) 2 SCC 145; (1989) SCC (L&S) 284; AIR 1989 SC 903; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 2; 1989(1) SCALE 409; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 689; 1989 (3) SLR 717; 1989 (1) UJ 512

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(4) and 136 - Admission to evening classes in LL B course - (A) Classes started with objective of accommodating employees - Changed rule restricting admission to employees of Govt. / semi-govt and similar institutions - Constitutionality and sustainability - (B) Reservation - Cent per cent - For certain class of persons to exclusion of meritorious candidates - Validity - (C) Refusal of admission under invalid rule - University directed to admit students if otherwise eligible - All seats are filled up - Held, (A) Rule is discriminatory and liable to be struck down as a whole since valid and invalid portions are inextricably mixed up - (B) Impermissible - (C) No answer - Appeal allowed.
