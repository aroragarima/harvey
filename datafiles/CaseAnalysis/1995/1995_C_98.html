Shree Baidyanath Ayurved Bhavan Limited and Others v Collector of Central Excise, Nagpur and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 982; (1996) 9 SCC 402; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 492; 1995 (10) RLT 327

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 (1) - Dant Manjan Lal is manufactured by appellant company - Appellant contends its a ayurvedic preparation - Notification issued by in exercise of power conferred by r. 8(1) of Central Excise Rules, 1944, for exemption from excise duty - Whether tribunal was justified in concluding that Dant Manjan Lal manufactured by appellant-Company does not fall within meaning of an Ayurvedic Medicine to qualify for exemption from payment of excise duty under Notification No. 62/78-CE D/- 1-3-1978? - In interpreting statutes like Excise Act, primary object of which is to raise revenue and for which purpose various products are differently classified, resort should not be had to scientific and technical meaning of terms and expressions used but to their popular meaning, that is to say meaning attached to them by those using product - Held, tribunal applied correct principles in concluding that product in question was not a medicinal preparation ('Ayurvedic') and, therefore, appellant was not entitled to benefit of exemption notification - Appeal dismissed.
