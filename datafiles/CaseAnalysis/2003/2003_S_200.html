Jineshwardas (D) through L.Rs. and Others v Smt. Jagrani and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 816; (2003) 11 SCC 372; AIR 2003 SC 4596; 2003 (53) ALR 599; 2003 (4) AWC 3388; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 233; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 158; 2004 (136) PLR 555; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 606; 2004 (1) RD 548; 2003(8) SCALE 253; 2003 (1) SCW 5385; 2003 (6) SLT 91; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 179; 2003 (7) Supreme 93; 2004 (1) UC 334

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 23, r. 1, 3, o. 3, r. 1, s. 100 - Code Of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Decree for specific performance and recovery of possession of suit land or in alternative, damages at market value as may be proved for non-performance of contract - Whether courts below were in error in holding that agreement was not a genuine agreement to sell property in suit and same is not enforceable - Whether Court below was right in non-suiting plaintiff also on ground of limitation - Held, a judgment or decree passed as result of consensus arrived at before court, cannot always be said to be one passed on compromise or settlement and adjustment - Further held, so-called questions formulated cannot be considered to be even questions of law and, at any rate, not substantial questions of law, as required u/s. 100, CPC, 1908 courts below have concurrently rejected to claim of plaintiff/appellants on pure findings of fact based upon relevant evidence and nothing survived for consideration at all in such an appeal.
