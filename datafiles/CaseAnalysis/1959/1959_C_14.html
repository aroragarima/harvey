Chhadami Lal Jain and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 223; AIR 1960 SC 41; 1960 CRLJ 145; 1960 SCJ 901; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Curable, Warrant Case

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1955, ss. 208, 209, 347(1) - Whether Magistrate has failed to comply with provisions of s. 208 during committal proceedings and same has prejudiced accused? - Held, yes - Accused has a right u/s. 208 to produce evidence in defence, before Magistrate proceeds to decide upon charges - Magistrate's decision whether charges should be framed or not is bound to be affected if evidence is produced by accused - Accused was denied opportunity of leading evidence which was sufficient to prejudice accused, denying right to produce defence u/s. 208 - When proceedings were initiated, Magistrate proceeded in manner prescribed for trial of warrant cases and did not indicate to accused from beginning that proceedings were in nature of an inquiry - Further held, proceedings before Magistrate began as trial of a warrant case and if Magistrate at a subsequent stage was of view that case should be committed to Sessions Court, he would have to act u/s. 347(1) - Committal bad in law and quashed - Matter remitted back to Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
