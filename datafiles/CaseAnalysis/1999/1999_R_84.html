Mahendra Pal v Ram Dass Malanger and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 791; (2000) 1 SCC 261; AIR 2000 SC 16; JT 1999 (8) SC 468; 1999(6) SCALE 655; 1999 (3) SCJ 593; 1999 (9) SLT 209; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 170; 1999 (9) Supreme 265

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 16, O. 7, r.11 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 83(1)(a), 97 - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, r. 56-B(7) - Election petition on improper reception of invalid votes - Dismissed without trial, that election petition lacks in material facts and particulars and not furnish cause of action - Whether in an election petition, a particular fact is material or not and as such required to be pleaded? - Held, pleadings have to be read as a whole to ascertain their true import, it is substance and not merely form, which is required to be looked into for construing pleadings - Further held, non-mention of serial numbers of improperly counted ballot papers, keeping in view averments made in para 16 of petition, could not be a ground to non-suit election petitioner at threshold, without trial - Appeal allowed.
