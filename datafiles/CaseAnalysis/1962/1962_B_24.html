Bhiva Doulu Patil v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 497; AIR 1963 SC 599; 1963 CRLJ 489; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal- Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 -Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114 and 133 - Testimony of approver - Whether conviction of appellant is sustainable on statement of approver and has been corroborated qua appellant? - Held, no - There should be corroboration of approver in material particulars and with respect to each accused - Without corroboration of approver qua appellant conviction is unsustainable - Conviction of an accused on testimony of an accomplice cannot be said to be illegal but courts will not accept evidence of such a witness without corroboration in material particulars - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
