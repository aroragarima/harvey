Pritish v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, Y.K. Sabharwal, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 206; (2002) SCC (Cr) 140; (2002) 1 SCC 253; AIR 2002 SC 236; 2001 (4) CCR 324; 2002 (1) Crimes 180; 2002 (2) CRJ 750; 2002 CRLJ 548; JT 2001 (9) SC 574; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 92; 2001(8) SCALE 235; 2002 (1) SCJ 88; 2001 (8) SLT 144; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 302; 2001 (8) Supreme 336; 2002 (1) UC 48

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Under Section 340 (1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, the purpose of preliminary inquiry, if the court opts to conduct it, is not to decide the guilt or innocence of the party, but only to decide whether it is expedient in the interest of justice to inquire into the offence, which appears to have been committed.
