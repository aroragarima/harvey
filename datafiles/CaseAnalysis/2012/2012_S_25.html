Samaj Parivartana Samudaya & Ors v State Of Karnataka & Ors

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 267; (2013) 8 SCC 219; JT 2012 (8) SC 215; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 494; 2012(8) SCALE 433

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Lifting of ban - The Central Empowered Committee (CEC) submitted a report regarding implementation of the R and R Plans, resumption of mining operations in 'Category A' mining leases and issues incidental thereto - The recommendations made in the report of the CEC were accepted subject to any modification that the Court may feel necessary to make later on and the ban imposed on mining operation all the mining leases in the district of Bellary, Tumkur and Chitradurga was lifted in respect of the 18 'Category A' mines - Applications were filed relating to the opening up of 'Category A' mines - Held, directions were issued that no mining operation under any of the mining leases shall take place unless all the statutory sanctions, permissions and approvals were subsisting - In case, after the resumption of mining operations in terms of this order any statutory sanction, permission or approval was lapsed or was not renewed in time, the mining operations would remain stopped until the required statutory sanction, permission or approval was duly granted - However, if the Monitoring Committee found any slackness in the implementation of the R and R Plan in leasehold area under a mining lease, the Monitoring Committee shall apprise the CEC in that regard and it would be open to the CEC to direct suspension of the mining operations under the concerned mining lease and to report the matter to SC - Permission for resumption of mining operations shall not come in the way, in any manner whatsoever, in any investigation, inquiry or proceedings that may be pending against any of the 18 mining leases covered by this order or that may be instituted against any one of them in future - The report of investigation, inquiry or proceeding in respect of any of the 18 leases should also be submitted before SC - Order accordingly.
