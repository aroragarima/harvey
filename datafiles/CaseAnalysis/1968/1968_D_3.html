Prem Raj v D.L.F. Housing and Construction Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1968 SC 1355; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 648

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115(c), O. 7 r. 7 - Specific performance - Claim of alternative reliefs - Propriety - There was fresh agreement by appellant/plaintiff to sell lands to respondent no. 1/defendant - Appellant gave notice to respondent no. 1 for repudiating arrangement dt. 11-6-1958 as void, in alternative appellant prayed for decree for specific performance of agreement dt. 11-6-1958 - Preliminary objection was raised by respondent no. 1 to effect that appellant having claimed that agreement dt. 11-6-1958 was void and inoperative, could not in same suit pray for specific performance of same agreement - Trial Court rejected preliminary objection - Respondent no. 1 filed Revision in HC, which was allowed and concluded that suit should fail so far as relief for specific performance was concerned - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, it was true that under O. 7 r. 7, CPC it was open to plaintiff to pray for inconsistent reliefs, but it should be shown by plaintiff that each of such pleas was maintainable - Thus, plaintiff suing for specific performance of contract could alternatively sue for rescission of contract but converse was not provided, it was therefore not open to plaintiff to sue for rescission of agreement and in alternative sue for specific performance - Further, in suit for specific performance plaintiff should allege that he was ready and willing to perform his part of contract, in instant case, no such averment was made in plaint - On other hand, plaintiff had alleged that agreement was result of fraud and undue influence and was not binding upon him - Therefore, it should be held that so far as relief of specific performance was concerned, plaintiff had no cause of action and hence appellant was not entitled to be put to election with regard to 2 alternative reliefs - In holding that appellant was entitled in alternative to ask for relief of specific performance, Trial Court had committed an error of law and so had acted with material irregularity or illegality in exercise of its jurisdiction within meaning of s. 115(c) CPC - There was no merit in instant appeal - Appeal dismissed.
