Krishna S/O Bulaji Borate v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20332; (2001) 2 SCC 441; AIR 2001 SC 695; 2001 (2) Bom.C.R. 293; JT 2001 (1) SC 631; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 483; 2001(1) SCALE 339; 2001 (2) SCJ 197; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 504; 2001 (1) SLT 669; 2001 (1) Supreme 323; 2001 (1) UC 339

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15 and 136 - Nagpur Improvement Trust Act, 1936, ss. 4(1)(e), 6 and 10 - Removal of trustee - Whether s. 6 confers power on state Govt. independent of power of State Govt. u/s. 10, to remove trustees appointed u/s. 4(1)(e) at pleasure of Govt. even before trustee concerned completes a period of five years? - Held, field of s. 6 and s. 10 are separate - Removal spoken u/s. 6 is removal without any stigma while removal u/s. 10 is removal with penal consequences attaching stigma - It is open for courts, while interpreting any provision to spell or r/w other provisions of statute if so intended to read implicitly, in absence of any explicit words that subserve intent of legislature - Removal of trustee u/s. 6 is based on principle of doctrine of pleasure - Once doctrine of pleasure is applicable neither principle of natural justice would step in nor any question of giving an opportunity before removal would arise - Removal of appellant as trustee of second respondent was valid - Appeal dismissed.
