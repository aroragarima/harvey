Collector of Central Excise, Pune v Tata Engineering and Locomotives Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 909; (2003) 11 SCC 193; 2003 (90) ECC 521; 2003 (111) ECR 257; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 130; JT 2003 (8) SC 557; 2003 (59) RLT 237; 2003(9) SCALE 231; 2003 (7) SLT 400; 2003 (7) Supreme 745

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - Notification No. 217/86-C.E. - Exemption - Captive use - Respondent manufacturing fork lift trucks, lifting tackles, trolleys, conveyors and measuring instruments such as gauges/templates for being utilised captively in the manufacture of their final products - Scope of the exclusion under the Explanation - Held, all machinery etc. which are inputs, are entitled to exemption from excise duty excepting those which actually produce or process or bring about a change. It is not the appellant's case that the items in question have any of the last mentioned characteristics. They are, therefore, exempted from payment of excise duty under the 1986 Notification.
