Megha Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1730; (1996) 11 SCC 709; (1997) SCC (Cr) 267; AIR 1995 SC 2339; AIR 1995 SCW 3477; 1995 CRLJ 3988

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 156 and 157 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Arms Act, 1959 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985, s. 6(1) - (A) Appreciation of evidence of two police officers as to recovery of pistol and cartridges from accused - Discrepancy as to no of cartridges and non examination of independent witnesses - (B) Legality of investigation by Head Constable who arresting accused, complained and lodged first information report - Held, such discrepancy would not inspire confidence about reliability of prosecution case - Further held, being a complainant, he should not have proceeded with investigation - Appeal allowed.
