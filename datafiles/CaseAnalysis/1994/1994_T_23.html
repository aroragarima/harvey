Tarseem Kumar v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 85; (1994) Supp3 SCC 367; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1735; AIR 1994 SC 2585; 1994 (3) Crimes 181; 1995 CRLJ 470; 1994 JCC 614; JT 1994 (5) SC 264; 1994(3) SCALE 806; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 740; 1994 (2) UJ 527

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, 8, 24 and 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Murder case - Case based on circumstantial evidence - Dead body found in accused's room - Testimony of witness unworthy as he is close relative and extra judicial confession not acceptable - Absence of motive and vital links in forming chain of circumstantial evidence were doubtful - Validity of conviction - Held, not proper - Appeal allowed.
