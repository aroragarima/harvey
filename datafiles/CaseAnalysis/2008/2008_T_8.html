Khoday Distilleries Limited v Scotch Whisky Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 908; (2008) 10 SCC 723; AIR 2008 SC 2737; 2008 (4) AWC 3258; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 942; 2008 (37) PTC 413; 2008(9) SCALE 40; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Unfair Competition, Discretionary Relief, Absence Of Evidence, Registrar Of Trade Mark, Class Of Goods, Rendition Of Account, Concurrent Use, Concurrent User, Prohibition Of Registration, Similar Mark, Confusion And Deception, Rectification Proceedings, Continued Use, Goods Act, 1999, Trade Marks Act, 1995

Summary: Whether an application for rectification can be dismissed on the ground of principle of delay, acquiescence, waiver or abandonment? - Whether the term 'Scot' would itself be a sufficient ground to opine that the mark 'Peter Scot' is deceptive or confusing? - Held, what would be the reasonable period in a given case would not depend upon the nature of action initiated before a statutory authority but also upon the purport and object of the statute - Conduct of the person aggrieved in filing the application for rectification would be relevant - Doctrine of continuing wrong has nothing to do with the refusal on the part of a statutory authority or a court of law to exercise its discretionary jurisdiction on the ground of latches, acquiescence or waiver; action of the respondents is barred under the principles of acquiescence and/ or waiver - Class of buyer is supposed to know the value of money, the quality and content of Scotch Whisky, they are supposed to be aware of the difference of the process of manufacture, the place of manufacture and their origin - So far as the applicability of the 1999 Act is concerned, having regard to the provisions of ss. 20(2) and 26(2), Geographical Indication of the Goods Act, 1999 will have no application - Appeal allowed.
