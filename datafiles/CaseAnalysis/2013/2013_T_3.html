T. C. Gupta v Bimal Kumar Dutta and others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 702; (2014) 14 SCC 446; 2014(1) ALL MR 433; 2014 (1) AWC 687; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 97; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 1043; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1028; 2013(13) SCALE 265; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 170

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Contempt of Courts Act, 1971-Section 12-Civil contempt-Wilful disobedience of Court's order-Order of court in respect of which violation is alleged must be clear, unambiguous and unequivocal and defiance thereof must be apparent on very face of action with which contemnor is charged-Interpretation of terms of Court's order in respect of which disobedience is alleged would not be appropriate while dealing with charge of contempt-Such charge cannot be brought home by unravelling true meaning of Court's order by subsequent order when there is apparent ambiguity, lack of clarity or dichotomy in initial order-Impugned order of High Court-Not sustainable and set aside. (2) Contempt of Courts Act, 1971-Section 12, Explanation -Contempt-Apology-Apology tendered by contemnor-Ought not to be rejected merely on ground that it is qualified or conditional so long as it is made bona fide.
