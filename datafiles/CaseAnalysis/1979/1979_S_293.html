Rajendra Prasad and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 277; (1979) 3 SCC 646; (1979) SCC (Cr) 749; AIR 1979 SC 916; 1979 CRLJ 792; 1979 CrLR(SC) 259; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 78

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: White Collar Crime, Separation Of Powers

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Ss. 354, 354(3) - Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution of India, 1950, Arts. 14, 19 and 21 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 84, 302 - Murder - Death Sentence -Fundamental Right - Social Justice - Appellant No. 1 was convicted of murder of three relatives and sentenced to death - Appellant No. 2 and 3 were found guilty of murder, by Sessions Judge of an offence of u/s. 302 r/w. 34 of the Code and awarded life imprisonment - As murder of appellants was a pre-planned, cold-blooded murder, HC allowed appeal of Appellant No. 2 and 3 and Appellant No. 1's appeal was dismissed, both on crime and punishment and confirmed death sentence - Hence instant appeal - Whether imposition of sentence of death for crime under laws were violated and Whether death penalty was necessary in order to prevent an outraged community - Held, Court's award of death sentence to appellants was 'erroneous in principle' and was arbitrary or excessive or indicative of an improper exercise of discretion - To allow appellants to escape with lesser punishment, after they had committed such intentional, cold-blooded, deliberate and brutal murders, would deprive law of effectiveness and result in travesty of justice - Court had to take into consideration all circumstances which did not merit extreme penalty - Court was sure that commutation of death sentence, would commit a few more murders and they would again become a menace to community - Court considered that there were "special reasons" u/s. 354(3) of the Code, 1973 for passing of death sentence for each of them - Therefore, HC's were justified in confirming death sentences passed, u/s. 368(a) of the Code - Court viewed that any interference with the sentence of death, would be wholly unwarranted - If there was a law reform, some regard should be had to plight of victim their family by making provision for payment of compensation - In confinement of death sentence u/art. 136 of Constitution, this Court had not only power, but also duty to interfere to set aside sentence of death and substitute it for sentence of imprisonment for life, for appellants - Court hold that imposition of death penalty, except in classes of cases, would be violation of Arts. 14, 19 and 21 of Constitution - Appeals disposed of.
