K. Raj and Another v Muthamma

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20166; (2001) 6 SCC 279; AIR 2001 SC 1720; 2002 (46) ALR 612; 2001 (2) CGLJ 413; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 651; 2001(3) SCALE 386; 2001 (3) SLT 441; 2001 (3) Supreme 391

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Whether High Court was justified in interfering with findings of fact recorded by first appellate court, by reappraising evidence in violation of provisions contained in s. 100, CPC, 1908? - No substantial question of law while entertaining and deciding second appeal formulated by High Court - Held, HC has not made any effort to find out whether any substantial question of law is involved or not, it has also totally failed to address itself to that aspect of matter in utter disregard of provisions contained u/s. 100(4), CPC, 1908 which has been held to be mandatory in nature - Matter remanded back to HC - Appeal allowed.
