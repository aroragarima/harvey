Prem Kumar Gulati and another v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 646; (2014) 14 SCC 646; 2015 CRLJ 159; 2014 (4) JCC 2818; JT 2014 (11) SC 385; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 469; 2014(11) SCALE 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161 and 319 - Death - Dying declaration - Prosecution alleged that accused persons being deceased's husband, Jeth and Jethani poured kerosene oil on deceased and set ablaze - Prosecution also alleged that deceased husband gave her beatings under the influence of intoxication - Trial Court convicted all the 3 accused persons u/ss. 302/498-A r/w s. 34 of IPC - HC dismissed appeal against said order - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, deceased declared that her husband along with the accused and X (deceased) have poured kerosene oil upon her. Except that, nothing has been said in the dying declaration as against the accused or X as to which accused poured kerosene oil upon her and the accused lighted the fire. In the later part of dying declaration, the deceased stated that her husband used to reside with his Bhabhi. After drinking liquor, she was beaten up by her husband with lathi and shoes. Benefit of doubt should be given to accused and his conviction cannot be sustained. Sufficient evidence has come on record and the prosecution has established the case that it was deceased husband at whose instance and instigation she was subjected to death by pouring kerosene oil and lit on fire. Finding recorded by the trial court as also by the Appellate Court as against main accused (husband of the deceased) cannot be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.
