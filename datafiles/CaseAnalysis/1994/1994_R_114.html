Jaw Ajee Nagnatham v Revenue Divisional Officer

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1292; (1994) 4 SCC 595; AIR 1994 SCW 2852; 1994 (2) CCC 41; JT 1994 (2) SC 604; 1994 (1) RentLR 592; 1994(2) SCALE 298; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 368; 1994 (2) UJ 17

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Vendor

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Stamp Act, 1899,s. 47-A - Land Acquisition Act, ss. 4(1), 18 and 23(1) - Appellant is owner of land which was acquired by and compensation awarded, appellant filed appeal in High Court for increasing compensation - Whether Basic Valuation Register is evidence to determine market value? - Methods of valuation may be (1) opinion of experts, (2) price paid within a reasonable time in bona fide transactions of purchase of lands acquired or lands adjacent to lands acquired an possessing similar advantages and (3) a number of years purchase of actual or immediately prospective profit of lands acquired - It is settled law that in determining market value, court has to take into account either one or other three methods to determine market value of lands appropriated on facts of a given case to determine market value - Generally second method of valuation is accepted as best - Held, basic of registration has no statutory base, and it cannot form any basis to determine market value of acquired lands u/s. 23 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
