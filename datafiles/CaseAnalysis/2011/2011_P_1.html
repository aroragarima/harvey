P. C. Paulose, Sparkway Enterprises v Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 36; (2011) 2 SCC 230; JT 2011 (1) SC 465; 2011 (1) KLT 446; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 841; 2011(1) SCALE 420; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 872; 2011 (21) S.T.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service Tax

Summary: Service Tax - Indirect Tax - Finance Act, 1994, ss. 65, 75 and 76 - Airports Authority of India Act, 1994, s. 3 - Collecting entry fee at airport on behalf of Airport Authority of India (AAI) - AAI entered into a licence agreement with the appellant by which the appellant was entrusted with the responsibility and the activity of collecting airport admission ticket charges on behalf of AAI - As per the said agreement the appellant was permitted to collect Rs. 50/- per visitor as airport admission ticket charges for which the appellant was required to pay an amount of Rs. 2,66,797/- per month as licence fee - A show cause notice was issued to the appellant demanding service tax amounting to Rs. 1,80,845/- and education cess amounting to Rs. 3,617/- - There was also a proposal to demand interest u/s. 75 of the Act on the above service tax and education cess as well as penalty u/s. 76 of the Act - The adjudicating authority confirmed the demand of service tax of Rs. 1,64,106/- and education cess of Rs. 3,282/- with interest u/s. 75 of the Act - Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - Tribunal, allowed the appeal filed by the appellant by holding that the appellant is only a collecting agent and therefore the liability to pay the service tax rest on AAI which is the actual service provider - HC allowed the appeal filed against said order - Hence, the present appeal - Whether appellant is a service provider and therefore, liable to pay the service tax? - Held, yes - The licence agreement clearly stipulates that AAI is entitled in law to grant licence at its Calicut Airport for the purpose of airport admission so as to provide amenities and facilities to passengers and visitors at the Airport and that the licensee, i.e., appellant, has agreed under the licence agreement to render such services to AAI on the terms and conditions mentioned in the said licence agreement - One of such stipulations was that the licensee would pay all rates, assessment, out goings and other taxes as leviable on the licensee - Hence, appellant being a person authorized by AAI to provide service in express terms and conditions of the agreement, it becomes liable to pay such tax as it was an authorized person to provide taxable service and collect the admission ticket charges on a contract basis - Appeal dismissed.
