State of Bihar and Another v J. A. C. Saldanha and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 344; (1980) SCC (Cr) 272; (1980) 1 SCC 554; 1980 (17) ACC 279; AIR 1980 SC 326; 1980 (28) BLJR 18; 1980 CRLJ 98; 1980 CRLR 17; 1980 MLJ 382; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 16

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Police Act, 1861 - In the instant case, though investigation was incomplete, on his transfer, respondent no. 6, submitted a report - Trial court ordered awaiting the report on further investigation - HC quashed the order of the Trial court and hence, the instant two appeals - Whether, on the facts and the circumstances of the case, the HC erred while exercising its power while deciding the case - Held, on consideration of the facts and the circumstances of the case, instant court was of the view that, the HC had erred while giving the direction virtually amounting to a mandamus to close the case before the investigation was complete - And instant court concluded that, the aspersions cast on the character of respondent no. 6 and the allegations of mala fides made against him were not sustainable - And hence, instant court allowed the appeal of the respondent no. 6 - Subsequently, since, the appeal of the respondent was allowed no question of considering the appeal by the appellant arisen - Order accordingly.
