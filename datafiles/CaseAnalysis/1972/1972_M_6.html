Â State Of Jammu And Kas v M. S. Farooqi And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Grover, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 129; (1972) 1 SCC 872; AIR 1972 SC 1738; 1972 LabIC 900; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 881; 1972 SLR 361

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Madras Prohibition Act, 1937, Punjab Restitution of Mortgaged Lands Act, 1938, Jammu and Kashmir Government Servants Prevention of Corruption (Commission) Act, 1962, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, All India Service (Discipline & Appeals) rules 1955

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - J and K Govt. Servants' Prevention of Corruption (Commission) Act, 1962 - Whether J and K Govt. Servants' Prevention of Corruption (Commission) Act, 1962 applies to members of all India Services?- Held, J and K Govt. Servants' Prevention of Corruption (Commission) Act, 1962 sections, does not apply to members of all India Services - Act is repugnant to All India Services Act, 1951 and All India Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules,1955 - Has to give way to these statutory provisions by virtue of art. 254 of Constitution - Act dealing with extent of infliction of disciplinary punishments is repugnant to (Discipline and Appeal) Rules - Whole Act must be read so as to leave members of All India Services outside its purview - Appeal dismissed.
