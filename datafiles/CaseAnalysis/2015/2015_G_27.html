Golbar Hussain and other v State of Assam and another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 304; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2477; 2015 (3) JCC 1785; JT 2015 (4) SC 507; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 236; 2015(5) SCALE 630; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants were charge sheeted for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - On analysis of the evidence the Trial Court held that the evidence of PWs4 and 5 was full of contradictions on material particulars and as such the testimony of these witnesses did not inspire any confidence - Uncorroborated testimony of PWs4 and 5 by some independent eye witness could not be accepted to warrant the conviction of the accused persons - On appeal, HC overruled the decision of the Trial Court and convicted all the five accused u/s. 302 r/w s.. 149 of IPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the Trial Court has given a reasoned decision after careful and thorough analysis of the evidence produced by the parties. The Trial Court also had the advantage of looking at the demeanor of the witnesses, and was correct in granting the benefit of doubt to the accused and acquitting them. The HC erred in presuming a version against the accused as the view which is favourable to the accused should be taken in cases where two views are probable. Further, as the testimonies of PWs4 and 5 are not completely reliable, this is a fit case where corroboration by an independent witness was required. The case of the prosecution also weakens on the ground that the only independent witness PW8 turned hostile. The view taken by the Trial Court was reasonable and probable on the facts of the instant case. Appeal allowed.
