Ranjan Sinha and another v Ajay Kumar Vishwakarma and others

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Jagdish Singh Khehar, Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 978; 2017 (7) SCALE 234

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: operation, Register, Registration

Summary: (A) Parliament & Legislature â Bihar Reorganization Act, 2000, ss.84,85 â Pharmacy Act, 1948, ss.30,31,32 â Registration of Pharmacist â Eligibility - Petitioners filed writ petition before HC of Jharkhand with a prayer to quash press notification dt.14-1-2002 wherein all persons who have qualification u/s.31 of 1948 Act were qualified to apply for registration as pharmacist and also prayed for instructions to Registration Tribunal to register only such persons, as pharmacists, who have requisite qualifications in terms of the Regulations, having qualification of diploma or degree recognized by Pharmacy Council of India (PCI) or State Councils â However, DB of HC held that Regulations, applicable to erstwhile Bihar, are law for the new State of Jharkhand in terms of ss.84, 85 of 2000 Act and so, unless a person is qualified as per the Regulations, could not get himself registered â Hence, instant appeals â Whether First Register prepared by State of Bihar are deemed to be the First Register of the State of Jharkhand in view of ss. 84 and 85 of 2000 Act.

Held, when the First Register of Pharmacists prepared by the Registration Tribunal was published by Govt. of Bihar u/s. 30(4) of 1948 Act, the same is conclusive and any amendments by way of inclusions could be carried out till the framing of the Education Regulations by Pharmacy Council of India. For doing so, the competent authority could take into consideration the qualifications as prescribed by s.31 of 1948 Act. However, after the coming into force of the Education Regulations as well as at the time of subsequent Registration, Govt. has to necessarily adhere to the Education Regulations. Any person who does not satisfy the qualifications as per the Education Regulations is not entitled to seek entry in the pharmacy register. Hence, when the State of Bihar is precluded from preparing the First Register again, then the State of Jharkhand is equally not entitled in law to prepare the First Register again. Further, all pharmacists in the First Register of pharmacist for the former State of Bihar, whose residential address, as shown therein, fall in the territory of State of Jharkhand, shall be construed to be part of First Register of Jharkhand. Future inclusion of additional names in the Register is to be made strictly in terms of s.32(2) of 1948 Act. Hence, Order of HC to the extent of quashing the notification of State Govt. of Jharkhand, dt.12-11-2001, constituting the Registration Tribunal in terms of s.30 of 1948 Act and advertisement calling for applications in terms of s.31 of 1948 Act is upheld. Further, orders are issued to the effect inter alia that â First Register prepared by erstwhile State of Bihar is to be treated as the First Register for newly formed State of Jharkhand and State of Bihar; First Register as prepared by erstwhile State of Bihar is to be bifurcated based on the territorial nexus with the residential address as provided by pharmacists at the time of registration; State of Jharkhand is at liberty to take all necessary steps to constitute a] State Council. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Parliament & Legislature â Pharmacy Act, 1948, ss.30,31,32 â State of Chhattisgarh issued notification on 1-3-2001 purporting to constitute Registration Tribunal and said Tribunal sought to undertake registration as per qualifications laid down u/s.31 of the Act to prepare the First Register of pharmacists â However, PCI requested Secretary to Govt. and Tribunal to withdraw the notification dt.1-3-2001 and also sought to cancel registrations if done other than u/s.32(2) of the Act and make the register of pharmacists for the State of Chhattisgarh by bifurcating from the register of pharmacists of MP â Writ petition was filed against Chhattisgarh Pharmacy Council with prayer for directions to Council not to usurp the function of Pharmacy Council of MP in renewing the registration of pharmacists who were not First Registered with Chhattisgarh Pharmacy Council â HC allowed writ petition â Hence, instant appeal by PCI contending inter alia that observations and directions of HC are capable of being misused and amount to validating the preparation of First Register u/s.30 of the Act yet again for the State of Chhattisgarh â Whether the Registration Tribunal was competent to undertake the exercise of renewal of the registration of pharmacists who are already included in the First Register of pharmacists prepared by erstwhile State of MP. Held, once the First Register prepared by erstwhile State of Madhya Pradesh is deemed to be also the First Register of State of Chhattisgarh, there could not be any prohibition for the Pharmacy Council of Chhattisgarh to undertake renewal u/s.32(2) of the Act or as per law. Hence, directions are issued to the effect that First Register prepared by erstwhile State of Madhya Pradesh is to be treated as the First Register for newly formed State of Chhattisgarh and State of Madhya Pradesh; the First Register as prepared by the erstwhile State of MP is to be bifurcated based on the territorial nexus with the residential address as provided by pharmacists at the time of registration; further, constitution of Registration Tribunal by State of Chhattisgarh and consequent registrations carried out by Registration Tribunal are invalid and illegal. Appeals disposed of.
