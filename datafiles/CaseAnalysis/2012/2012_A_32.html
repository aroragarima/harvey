Ishwardas Rohani v Alok Mishra and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 167; (2012) 7 SCC 309; JT 2012 (4) SC 585; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 952B; 2012(5) SCALE 90; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 285

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Amendment Of The Election Petition, Purity

Summary: Altamas Kabir J.Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election petition - Petitioner (returned candidate) and the respondent No. 1 (election petitioner') contested the General Election to the Legislative Assembly of the State of Madhya Pradesh - Election petitioner lost the election with a margin of 24731 votes to the returned candidate - Election petitioner questioned the validity of the election of the returned candidate before HC - Election petitioner complained that various corrupt practices, i.e., bribery (B); unduly influencing the voters; corrupt practice and procuring the assistance from the employees of the State, were committed - In said petition, application was filed by the returned candidate under O. 7 r. 11 of the CPC, seeking the dismissal of the election petition on the grounds that the allegations of corrupt practice lacks material facts and particulars, inasmuch as it was not disclosed on what date and time the alleged corrupt practice had been committed; mandatory affidavit in Form 25 of the Conduct of Election Rules did not fulfil the mandatory contents as required in law; election petitioner had not filed affidavit as required under the provisions of CPC; averments were frivolous in nature and did not disclose any cause of action - Application was partly allowed by the impugned Judgment - Hence, instant petition - Whether averments were frivolous in nature and did not disclose any cause of action - Held, charge of corrupt practice had a two dimensional effect, namely, its impact on the returned candidate had to be viewed from the point of view of the candidate's future political and public life and from the point of view of the electorate to ensure the purity of the election process - In the instant case, while accepting the case made out regarding the deficiencies in the election petition, the DB did not commit any error in directing the election petitioner to cure the defects in the election petition, which had been brought out during the hearing of the election petition - No reason to interfere with the order of the HC appealed against - Appeal dismissed.

Chelameswar, J.

Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Election petition - Whether averments were frivolous in nature and did not disclose any cause of action - Held, election petition lacks in stated the material facts constituting the various corrupt practices mentioned in the election petition to enable the declarations sought by the election petitioner - Hence, conclusion recorded by the HC that allegations suffer from lack of certain material particulars particularly as to the consent of the returned candidate or his election agent was wholly erroneous in law as consent by the candidate or his election agent was an essential material fact, which was required to be pleaded and proved when the allegation was that somebody other than the candidate or his election agent committed a corrupt practice - Further, election petition, as originally presented, did not contain the necessary material facts to constitute the cause of action to challenge the election of the returned candidate, the application filed by the election petitioner, even if it contain the necessary material facts, could not be allowed as it would amount to permitting the amendment of the election petition beyond the period of limitation - Appeal allowed.
