Sree Bank Limited v Sarkar Dutt Roy and Company

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 1403; AIR 1966 SC 1953; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 881; 1965 (2) CompLJ 244; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: K. Sarkar, J.

(A) Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, ss. 45-O, 45-F - Banking Companies (Amendment) Act, 1953 - Winding up - Execution - Applicability of provisions - Retrospective effect - Decree was passed in favour of appellant bank against respondents provided that if respondents failed to pay any of installments mentioned in it within four months of date of its becoming due, appellant bank 'shall deem all installments in default and should be entitled to realise all said amounts by execution' - None of those installments was paid and application for realizing them by execution was made on 26-8-1957 - Petition for winding up appellant bank had been presented and order for winding up had been made on 3-8-1948 - Application for execution was made by liquidator in course of winding up - On 30-12-1953, s. 45-O of the Act was introduced in 1949 Act, by 1953 Act - Appellant presented application in Tabular form for execution of decree, on ordinary original civil side of HC - Interim order for appointment of receiver was made which was confirmed - HC dismissed application and set aside order for appointment of receiver - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether s. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act had retrospective operation - Held, ss. 45-O, 45-F of 1949 Act had been extended existing period of limitation in regard to claims by bank against its debtors - That being so, it would be natural to think that largest extension which language used was capable of giving was intended - There was no reason of distinction between debtors claims against whom have become barred before section was enacted and those claims against whom became barred - It was expressly made in s. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act applicable to banking company being wound up on petition presented before amending Act - That would indicate that s. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act was intended to apply to suits and applications by banking company in liquidation even where winding up petition had been filed before amending Act - It should apply to all such suits or applications even when they had become barred before amending Act - S. 45-0 (3 ) of 1949 Act gave no hint that such exclusion was to be confined to cases where right had not become barred before that Act - It gave s. 45-0(1) of 1949 Act retrospective operation by permitting exclusion of antecedent period - Hence, there was strong indication that sub- s. (1) was to have retrospective operation - Appeal allowed.

(B) Corporate - Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, ss. 45-O - Execution of winding up- Computation of limitation - Whether s. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act allowed period commencing from date of presentation of petition for winding up to be excluded in computation of period of limitation - Held, bar of limitation was completely lifted in case of debt accruing due before presentation of petition for winding up which had not become time barred then, and it was natural to think that intention must be to lift bar completely in case of debts accruing due - There was no reason to make distinction between two classes of debts - Removal of bar of limitation should not keep debtor in suspense for inordinately long time - S. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act had that effect in case of debts which accrued due prior to presentation of winding up petition and had not become barred on that date, even though sub-section did not expressly say so - Absence of certain words was not reason leading to view that debts which became due after presentation of petition for winding up were not intended to be protected - S. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act should be read as permitting exclusion of entire period commencing from date of presentation of petition for winding up where debts became due before that date and in cases where debt became due such part of that period as commenced from date of accrual of debt -- However, s. 45-0 (1) of 1949 Act permitted whole of period commencing from presentation of petition for winding up to be excluded where it could in fact be so done and part of that period only where whole of it could not be excluded - Any other reading would defeat object of the Act - Appellant could be entitled to take all steps for such execution as were permitted to it in law - Appeal allowed.

Wanchoo, J.

(C) Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, s. 45-O - Limitation - Interpretation of provisions - Whether language of s. 45-O (1) r/w 45-O (3) of the Act was retrospective in operation and revived claims that might became barred by Limitation on date when said section came into force - Held, if limitation had begun to run before 11-5-1948 period from 11-5-1948 upto date of application for execution on 26-8-1957, would have to be excluded - It was evident that there was default in payment of instalment due on 30-12-1947 - Period of limitation for that instalment certainly began to run from that date - If payment was not made of any instalment within after four months of due date, entire remaining decretal amount would also become due - Those four months expired on 30-4-1948 and from 1-5-1948 appellant bank was entitled to execute entire decretal amount that remained due - Right to execute all remaining instalments arose on 1-5-1948 - Limitation for all instalments from second to seventh began on 1-5-1948 while application for winding up was made on 11-5-1948 - In view of interpretation of s. 45-0 (1) of the Act, appellant would be entitled to exclusion of entire period from 11-5-1948 upto date of execution application, and would be entitled to execute decree which was total of instalments 2 to 7 with interest - Appeal allowed.

Raghubar Dayal, J.

(D) Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949, s. 45-O - Retrospective effect - Limitation - Whether s. 45-0 (1) of the Act had retrospective operation - Held, object and intention of Legislature in enacting s. 45-O(1) of the Act was that period subsequent to presentation of petition for winding-up be not taken into consideration in computing period of limitation - Entire period could be excluded from consideration if limitation had begun to run prior to presentation of petition for winding-up and period commencing from accrual of cause of action subsequent to date of presentation of petition for winding-up of company would be excluded from period of limitation which also commenced from accrual of cause of action - Entire period of limitation was abrogated with respect to causes of action arising subsequent to date of presentation of petition for winding-up - It did not meant construing provisions of s. 45-O(1) of the Act in context of circumstances and reasons for enactment of those provisions - It would be anomalous to hold that action could be taken with help of provisions of s. 45-O(1) of the Act with respect to causes of action which had arisen much earlier than date of presentation of petition for winding-up but action could not be taken with respect to causes of action arising subsequent to such date if it had not been taken within prescribed period of limitation - There was nothing in language of s. 45-O(1) of the Act to accept contention for respondent whose acceptance would lead to results which would not be contemplated by Legislature - Appellant's application for execution presented in August 1957 was presented within limitation - Appeal allowed.
