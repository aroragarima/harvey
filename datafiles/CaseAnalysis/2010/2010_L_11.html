Land Acqn. Officer and Assistant Commissioner and another v Shivappa Mallappa Jigalur and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 491; (2010) 12 SCC 387; 2010 (5) AWC 4581; JT 2010 (7) SC 475; 2010 (3) KLT 264; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 302; 2010(6) SCALE 623; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 833

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Liability to pay interest on solatium - Land Acquisition proceedings - Award made by Collector - References filed u/s. 18 of Act by aggrieved landowners/claimants against award - Civil court enhanced market value of subject lands - Appeals filed against order of civil court - During pendency of appeal, judgment in Sunder vs. Union of India, 2001 INDLAW SC 67 was pronounced which settled issue regarding liability of payment of interest on amount of solatium - Later on, appeals filed by landowners were allowed - HC enhanced rate of compensation - State of Karnataka was directed to pay interest on amounts of solatium - Relying upon judgment of SC in Gurpreet Singh vs. Union of India, 2006 INDLAW SC 721, it was submitted that in cases where full payments were made in terms of decree and execution proceedings were consequently closed, proceedings could not be re-opened; further, any direction for payment of interest on solatium could only be for period subsequent to date of decision in Sunder (September 19, 2001) - Therefore, it was argued that in present cases amount of solatium as determined by civil court was paid long before September 19, 2001, following which execution proceeding was closed and hence, no liability of any interest on amount of solatium could be fastened upon State in light of decision in Gurpreet Singh - Held, in judgment of Gurpreet Singh, SC clearly said that execution court could not go beyond decree - In event, claim of interest was rejected expressly or by necessary implication in decree, it would not be permissible for execution court to grant interest relying upon decision in Sunder - SC clarified that if award of reference court or appellate court was silent on issue of solatium and interest then it would be open to execution court to apply ratio of Sunder and say that compensation awarded would include solatium and in such an event interest on amount could be directed to be deposited in execution - Further, reference to "closed executions" in decision of Gurpreet Singh does not mean cases in which main proceeding arising from landowner's claim for enhanced compensation remains pending before civil court or at appellate stage - In appeal, superior court may enhance compensation which would lead to enhancement of solatium and consequently interest on additional amounts of compensation and solatium - In such cases, execution proceedings cannot be deemed to be closed and neither was it intent of observations in decision in Gurpreet Singh - Appeal being continuation of original proceeding, there can be no question of accrual of interest only after date of decision in Sunder - Appeals disposed of.

CIVIL APPEAL NOS. 4988-5047, 5048-5051, 5052 & 5053 OF 2010

(B) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Liability to pay interest on solatium - Land Acquisition proceedings - Award made by Collector - References filed u/s. 18 of Act by aggrieved landowners/claimants challenging the award - Civil court enhanced market value of subject lands - It also held claimants entitled to solatium - However, Civil court expressly rejected landowners' claim for interest on solatium - No appeal filed - After decision in Sunder, an appeal was filed against judgment and award given by reference court, which was dismissed - Review petitions filed thereagainst were also dismissed - Revision filed before HC were allowed - Present appeal - Held, on September 19, 2001 when decision in Sunder was rendered, land acquisition proceedings (including execution proceedings) were over and closed - It is, thus, patent that a concluded and closed proceeding was sought to be revived by filing a review petition and then filing a revision against order dismissing review petition - This was plainly impermissible in view of decision of SC in Gurpreet Singh - HC decision untenable and set aside - Appeals disposed of.
