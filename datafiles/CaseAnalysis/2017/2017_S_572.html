MGR Industries Association and another v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 76; (2017) 3 SCC 494; AIR 2017 SC 709; 2017 (121) ALR 458; 2017 (1) AWC 1034; JT 2017 (2) SC 93; 2017(2) SCALE 230; 2017 (2) UPLBEC 1211

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Labour & Industrial Law, Industrial, CONSTITUTION, Societies Registration Act, 1860, MUNICIPALITIES & LOCAL GOVERNMENTS, Municipality, Municipal, Panchayats, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, Constitution (Seventy-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1992, Gujarat Industrial Development Act, 1962, Uttar Pradesh Kshettra Panchayats and Zila Panchayats Adhiniyam, 1961, Constitution of India, 1950, Gujarat Municipalities Act, 1962

Summary: Indirect Tax - Administrative - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, ss. 11, 12(A), 2(d) - Collection of Tax from Members - Denial of Exemption Challenged - Appellant/association of members, who runs small industries and respondent initiated proceedings for realization of tax from members of association - By virtue of direction from HC in writ petition, appellant application for exemption from tax was once again rejected by respondent - HC dismissed writ petition of association and DB of HC upheld dismissal order for want of failure to declaration of area as industrial township - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976 has been enacted to provide for the constitution of an authority for the development of certain area in the State into industrial township and for matters connected therewith. S. 12-A of the Act specifically provides that 'where an industrial development area or any part thereof is specified to be an industrial township under the proviso to clause (1) of art. 243-Q of Constitution, such industrial development area or part thereof, if included in a Panchayat area, shall, with effect from the date of notification made under the said proviso, stand excluded from such Panchayat area and no Panchayat shall be constituted for such industrial development area or part thereof. In other words, s. 12A of the Act, specifically contemplates issuance of notification under proviso to clause (1) of art. 243-Q of Constitution and exclusion from Panchayat area is consequent and dependent upon such notification. Hence, treating industrial area as industrial township notification under proviso to art. 243-Q(1) was contemplated which is also the statutory scheme under the Act. HC rightly held that exemption u/art. 12A of the Act was not available in view of facts of the case. Hence, appellant are not entitled to relief claimed. Appeal dismissed.
