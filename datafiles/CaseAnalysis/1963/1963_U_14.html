Champaklal Chimanlal Shah v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1964 SC 1854; 1964 (66) BomLR 319; 1964 (8) FLR 421; 1964 (1) LLJ 752; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 190

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art.16 - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949, rr. 2, 3 and 5 - (A) Whether rr. 3 and r. 2(b) be read conjunctively?- Held, it must be r/w r. 2(b) which defines 'Quasi-permanent service'- Context of r. 3 itself requires that that rule must be read in harmony with definition of 'Quasi-permanent service' in r. 2(b), for it could not be possible intention of rule making authority to create disharmony between definition in r. 2(b) and provision in r. 3 - Both conditions must be fulfilled before a Govt. servant can be deemed to be in quasi-permanent service, namely, (i) that he has been in continuous Govt. service for more than three years, and (ii) that appointing authority after satisfying itself as to suitability in various respects for employment in quasi-permanent capacity has issued a declaration to that effect - (B) Whether r. 5 of Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949 is hit by art. 16 of Constitution? -Further held, it is not - R. 5 gives power to Govt. to terminate services of temporary Govt. servant by giving him one month's notice or on payment of one month's pay in lieu of notice or such shorter or longer notice or payment in lieu thereof as may be agreed to between Govt. and employee concerned - Rule is not hit by art. 16 - Govt. may have to employ temporary servants to satisfy needs of a particular contingency and such employment would be perfectly legitimate - There is no denial of equal opportunity if out of class of temporary employees some are made quasi-permanent depending on length of service and their suitability in all other respects for permanent employment eventually and thus assimilated to permanent employees - Rule which applies to one class of Govt. servants in matter of termination but does not apply to other two classes cannot be said to violate equality of opportunity provided in art. 16 - (C) Whether temporary employees are entitled to protection u/art. 311(2) of constitution ?- Further held, yes they are - Temporary servants are also entitled to protection of art. 311(2) in same manner as permanent Govt. servants, if Govt. takes action against them by meting out one of three punishments i.e. dismissal, removal or reduction in rank - But this protection is only available where discharge, removal or reduction in rank is sought to be inflicted by way of punishment and not otherwise - (D) Whether preliminary enquiry to punish a temporary employee amounts to departmental enquiry? Further held, it does not - A preliminary enquiry is held to determine whether a prima facie case for a formal departmental enquiry is made out - Two should not be confused - Even where Govt. does not intend to take action by way of punishment against a temporary servant on a report of bad work or misconduct a preliminary enquiry is usually held to satisfy Govt. - There is reason to dispense with service of a temporary employee or to revert him to his substantive post, for Govt. does not usually take action of this kind without any reason - (E) Whether preliminary enquiry amounts to any punishment u/art. 311? - Further held, mere fact that some kind of preliminary enquiry is held against a temporary servant and following that enquiry services are dispensed with in accordance with contract or specific service rule would not mean that termination of service amounted to infliction of punishment of dismissal or removal within meaning of art. 311 (2) - Whether such termination would amount to dismissal or removal within meaning of art. 311(2) would depend upon facts of each case and action taken by Govt. which finally leads to termination of service - So far as preliminary enquiry is concerned there is no question of its being governed by art. 311(2) - (F) Whether memorandum stating enquiry to be a disciplinary proceeding becomes a disciplinary proceeding? - Further held, where no formal departmental enquiry as contemplated under art. 311(2) r/w relevant Central Services Rules was ever held after memorandum but his services were terminated under rules, termination cannot be challenged because provisions of art. 311(2) were not followed - It cannot be said that once Govt. issues a memorandum, but later decides not to hold a departmental enquiry for taking punitive action, it can never thereafter proceed to take action against a temporary Govt. servant in terms of r. 5, even though it is satisfied otherwise that his conduct and work are unsatisfactory - Appeal dismissed.
