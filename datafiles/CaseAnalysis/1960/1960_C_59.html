Â Vitthalbhai Naranbhai Patel v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh, Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 104; AIR 1967 SC 344; 1961 (12) STC 219

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, s. 2(g) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132 and 286 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Whether High Court had erred in dismissing petition of appellant? - When a lis commences, all rights get crystallised and no clog upon a likely appeal can be put, unless law was made retrospective, expressly or by clear implication - Held, from record of this case, it cannot be said when lis commenced, and unless it can be proved conclusively that it was before amendment of law - There is no averment that a right of appeal had vested, and has been wrongly taken away - HC had not erred - Appeal dismissed.
