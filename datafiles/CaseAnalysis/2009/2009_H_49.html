Harmanpreet Singh Ahluwalia and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 604; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 620; (2009) 7 SCC 712; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2774; 2009 CRLJ 3462; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1643; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 956; 2009(7) SCALE 85; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Joint Account, Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 405, 406, 415, 417 and 420 - Quashing of FIR - Inherent power of High Court - HC declined to exercise jurisdiction u/s. 482, in an application moved for quashing an FIR - Whether declining to exercise jurisdiction u/s. 482 by HC, justified? - Held, No - Respondent 3(R3) wife of appellant 1(A1) - R3 was a citizen of Canada and on her sponsorship A1 was living in Canada and employed there - Relationship turned strained, divorce petition filed in Court of Canada by R3 - Father of R3, a retired police inspector and a permanent resident of Canada, during his trip to India, lodged a complaint at Jallandhar, against the appellants u/ss. 406/420 with allegations that appellants demanded dowry and misappropriated the dowry articles - Pursuant to FIR enquiry conducted by Superintendent of Police(SP); submission of report; recommendation made to cancel the suit/case - Facts, parties were living at Canada, and offence, if any, had been committed in Canada; lodging of FIR at Jalandhar, only after divorce application was filed; no allegation in FIR made as to that appellants at the time of marriage or thereafter demanded any dowry; alleged demand of dowry was made only in Canada and that too after a period of five years from the date of marriage; opinion of SP as to cancellation of FIR - On perusal of facts and looking into allegations made, no offence found to be made out u/ss. 406/420 - Court took surprise from the fact of father of R3 coming all over from Canada to Jalandhar to lodge an FIR, and found it incomprehensible - Held further, allegations in FIR made with an ulterior motive to harass the appellants; continuance of criminal proceeding against them would amount to abuse of process of the Court - Invocation of jurisdiction u/s. 482, made out - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
