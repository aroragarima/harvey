Basant Roadways v State Transport Appellate Tribunal 7 Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 205; (1986) 4 SCC 504; AIR 1987 SC 116; JT 1986 SC 605; 1986(2) SCALE 587; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 1002; 1987 (1) UJ 10

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Indian Tourism Development Corporation (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules 1978, r. 31(v) - Termination of service - (A) Constitutionality of the r. 31(v) - (B) Order of termination passed under regulation - Which was found to be unconstitutional - Relations between employee and employer, strained - Trade union threatened strike - Determination of proper relief? - (C) Determination of compensation in lieu of reinstatement - When full salary and allowances till date of superannuation (around 8 years) claimed - Held, is violative of arts. 14 and 16 - Held, in circumstances that compensation in lieu of reinstatement, and not reinstatement, was warranted - Claim, held, not reasonable, award of compensation to equivalent to 3.33 years salary with allowances found to be reasonable, as it would yield 50% of annual salary and allowances to employee if it is invested at prevailing rate of interest - Appeal allowed.
