Collector of Customs, Bombay v Messrs Business Forms Limited Thr. O.L

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2155; (2005) 7 SCC 143; 2002 (142) E.L.T. 18; JT 2002 (1) SC 424; 2002 (50) RLT 375

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Collector (Appeals)

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Import of photographic apparatus - Explanatory notes - Validity - Tribunal declined to place reliance upon explanatory notes in H.S.N. stating that, at best, these have only persuasive value and passed order against Revenue - Whether Tribunal is justified in holding explanatory notes in HSN is only persuasive value.

Held, HSN explanatory notes are entitled to far greater consideration than Tribunal has given there. Tribunal has also said that Collector (A) had not relied upon HSN explanatory notes. That was clearly an oversight of Tribunal because its order says, earlier, therefore 'Collector (A) held that photographic apparatus, as has been imported, for making printing blocks were excluded from chapter heading 84.38 vide explanatory notes. Court therefore allows appeals and set aside orders under appeal. Appeals before Tribunal are restored to it for being heard and disposed of afresh. Appeals allowed.
