Arikala Narasa Reddy v Venkata Ram Reddy Reddygari and another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. Chelameswar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 72; (2014) 5 SCC 312; AIR 2014 SC 1290; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2014 (2) AWC 1881; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 158; JT 2014 (2) SC 549; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2301; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 935; 2014(2) SCALE 26; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Votes in question

Summary: (1) Election-Recounting of votes-Counting of ballots is not to be interfered with frequently-Secrecy of ballot which is sacrosanct gets exposed if recounting of votes is made easy-Court has to be more careful when margin between contesting candidates is very narrow-Court cannot exercise discretion of ordering recounting of ballots just to enable election petitioner to indulge in roving inquiry with view to fish material for dealing election to be void-Order of recounting can be passed only if petitioner sets out his case with precision supported by averments of material facts-Conditions relevant for recounting of votes-Enumerated.

(2) Election-Election dispute-Is statutory proceeding unknown to common law and thus, doctrine of equity, etc. does not apply in such dispute-Statutory requirements relating to election law have to be strictly adhered to in respect to election dispute-Answer to every question raised in election dispute is to be solved within four corners of statute-All technicalities prescribed/ mandated in election law provided to safeguard purity of election process-Courts have duty to enforce same with all rigours and not to minimise their operation.

(3) Election-Of member of legislative council-Recounting of Votes-Contesting candidates got equal number of votes-No occasion for High Court to direct recounting of all votes-In case certain discrepancies in recounting of all votes by Registrar of High Court as per direction of High Court-Not permissible for High Court to take into consideration all such discrepancies and decide election petition or recrimination petition on basis thereof-Course adopted by High Court cannot be taken note of being in contravention with statutory requirements-In such fact-situation decision as to who will be returned candidate is to be decided by draw of lots by virtue of provisions of Section 102 of the Representation of People Act, 1951-In presence of counsel for parties Supreme Court drawn lots in open Court and by draw of lots, appellants succeeds.

(4) Election-Rights to be elected-Neither fundamental right nor common law right-Though it may be very fundamental to democratic set-up of governance.

(5) Pleadings-Court cannot go beyond pleadings of parties-It is neither desirable nor permissible for Court to frame issue not arising on pleadings.
