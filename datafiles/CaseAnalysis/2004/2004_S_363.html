Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil, K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1067; (2005) SCC (L&S) 246; (2005) SCC (Cr) 546; (2005) 2 SCC 673; AIR 2005 SC 752; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 204; 2005 (116) DLT 81; 2010 (254) E.L.T. 196; JT 2005 (1) SC 97; 2005 (1) KLT 486; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 429; 2004(10) SCALE 501; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1054; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 539

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 25(2), 26(b) - Bombay Prevention of Ex-communication Act, 1949 - Seeking re-consideration of Sardar Syedna Taher Saifuddin Saheb Vs State of Bombay (1962 Indlaw SC 423) - A five-Judge Bench held, Act ultra vires Constitution - Whether it is necessary to place before a seven Judge Bench? - Held, there is no reference made by any Bench of any strength at any time for hearing by a larger Bench and doubting correctness of Constitution Bench decision - Matter should be placed for hearing before a Constitution Bench (of five Judges) and not before a larger Bench of seven Judges - It is only if Constitution Bench doubts correctness of law laid down in Sardar Syedna Taher Saifuddin Saheb's case (supra) that it may opine in favour of hearing by a larger Bench consisting of seven Judges.
