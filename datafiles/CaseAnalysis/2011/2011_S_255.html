Himanshu @ Chintu v State Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 4; (2011) 2 SCC 36; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 281; JT 2011 (1) SC 123; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 405; 2011(1) SCALE 30; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 48

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Additional Sessions Judge convicted the appellants for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, DB of HC affirmed the judgment of the Additional Sessions Judge - Hence, present appeal - (A) Evidence of hostile witness - It was contended that all the three eye-witnesses PW-7, PW-8 and PW-11 were declared hostile and, therefore, their evidence could not have formed the basis for the conviction - Held, evidence of a hostile witness remains admissible evidence and it is open to the court to rely upon the dependable part of that evidence which is found to be acceptable and duly corroborated by some other reliable evidence available on record - In the present case, HC and the Trial Court, thus, cannot be said to have erred in acting on the evidence of PW-11 which was duly corroborated by the other reliable evidence on record - (B) Delay in registering FIR - It was contended that FIR was registered belatedly and this time was used by the prosecution to falsely implicate the accused because of their previous enmity - Held, sequence of facts do not lead to an inference that there was delay in the registration of FIR or it lacked spontaneity - It cannot, therefore, be said that the time of two hours was used to falsely implicate the accused due to their previous enmity - (C) Evidence of eye witness - It was also submitted that PW-7, PW-8 and PW-11 were interested witnesses inasmuch as PW-7 and PW-8 were friends of the deceased and PW-11 was his younger brother and it was not safe to rely on their testimony - Held, DB took into consideration the discrepancies, omissions and contradictions and on careful consideration of their evidence held that the presence of these three witnesses at the time and place of occurrence was not doubtful and the evidence of PW-11 was corroborated by PW-7 and PW-8 with regard to the manner in which the crime was committed - Conclusions recorded by the Trial Court and confirmed by HC concerning A-2 and A-3 cannot be said to suffer from any factual or legal error or that such conclusions could not reasonably be arrived at by those courts - Complicity of A-3 also established by the evidence of PW-11 which is duly corroborated by medical and other evidence although PW-7 and PW-8 have not specifically named him - Concurrent finding of the HC and the Trial Court that the prosecution evidence was sufficient to bring home the guilt of appellants as well beyond any reasonable doubt correct - Appeals dismissed.
