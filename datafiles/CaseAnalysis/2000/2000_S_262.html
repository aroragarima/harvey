Ambika Prasad And Another v State (Delhi Administration, Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 595; (2000) SCC (Cr) 522; (2000) 2 SCC 646; AIR 2000 SC 718; 2000 (1) CCR 130; 2000 (2) Crimes 63; 2000 CRLJ 810; 2000 (1) JCC 197; JT 2000 (1) SC 273; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 643; 2000(1) SCALE 219; 2000 (2) SCJ 472; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 342; 2000 (1) SLT 442; 2000 (2) Supreme 633

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154, 309 and 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 341, 148, 149 and 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Murder - Appellants convicted by Trial Court and conviction upheld by High Court - Hence, present appeal - Held, evidence of injured eye-witnesses fully corroborates prosecution case - Fact that no independent witness was examined does not effect prosecution case - No reason to interfere with orders of Courts below - No ground to show that complainants were aggressors - Appeal dismissed.
