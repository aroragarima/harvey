G. Mohanasundaram v R. Nanthagopal and another

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 461; (2014) 13 SCC 172; AIR 2015 SC 141; 2014 (5) AWC 4920; JT 2014 (8) SC 544; 2014 (7) MLJ 761; 2014(8) SCALE 626; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 920; 2015 (1) SLR 497; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 69

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, regn. 5(4) r/w. regn. 6 - Tamil Nadu Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1955, r. 17(b) - Promotion - Justifiability - Appellant filed application before Tribunal and challenged the Government notifications 10-2-2012 and 13-April-2012 whereby respondent no.1 was promoted and appointed to Indian Administrative Service - Application was allowed by Tribunal Respondent no.1 filed petition before HC - HC set aside order of Tribunal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded y HC was justifiable -

Held, terms of regn. 5(4) of the Regulations it was incumbent upon State Government to forward complete service records of all eligible candidates including respondent no.1 to UPSC for considering them for promotion to IAS cadre - Withholding of ACRs of the year 2003-2009 of respondent no.1 on wrong presumption that they were invalid, was illegal and fatal in case of respondent no.1 towards his appointment to post of Indian Administrative Service - Fact though came to notice of UPSC which sought clarification from Government of Tamil Nadu, State Government misled UPSC which resulted in wrong assessment of service records of respondent no.1 in violation of regn. 5(4) r/w regn. 6 of the Regulations - Tribunal rightly held that Selection Committee had not taken into account all relevant facts and records and concluded that respondent no.1 was superior to appellant - Tribunal considered issue of departmental proceedings pending against respondent no.1 u/r. 17(b) of the Rules, was noticed by Selection Committee as apparent from recommendation of name of respondent no.1 with star mark shown against same with note that in view of pendency of departmental proceedings inclusion of name of respondent no.1 was provisional - In said departmental proceedings Enquiry Officer after going through evidence and reply submitted by respondent no.1 held that charge No.2 was proved against respondent no.1 - In spite of same, State Government dropped charges - Tribunal noticed that State Government dropped charges against respondent no.1 without giving detailed reasons for such action - Considering the same Tribunal held that State Government failed to furnish valid reasons for dropping charges and for subsequent issuance of integrity certificate to respondent no.1 - For said reason Tribunal held that action on part of State was case of hasty decision - HC failed to appreciate guidelines 4-4-2007 issued by State Government with regard to ACR and wrongly accepted stand of respondents that invalid ACRs were not to be considered - HC exceeded its jurisdiction in discussing charges framed against respondent no.1 and in justifying grounds for dropping charges, though it was not disclosed by State Government - Thus, impugned judgment and order passed by HC was set aside and order passed by Tribunal was upheld - Appeal allowed.
