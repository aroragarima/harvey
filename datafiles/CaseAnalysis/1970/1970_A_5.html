A. Peeriakaruppan, Etc. v State Of Tamil Nadu & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 164; (1971) 1 SCC 38; AIR 1971 SC 2303; 1971 (2) MLJ(SC) 65; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 430

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 15, 15(4) and 226 - Education - Admission to Medical Colleges - (A) Unitwise distribution of seats - Constitutionality of method - (B) Executive action - Plea of mala fide - Based on probabilities - (C) Interview - Objective tests - (D) Rules requiring Selection Committee to assess candidates on basis of certain specified objective tests - Inabsence of specific provision in rules committee must distribute total marks equally on various items - Giving marks in lumpsum and not on itemised basis - Legality - (E) Classification of backward classes on basis of caste - Held, method violates arts. 14 and 15 - Mere probabilities cannot form its basis - Assessment of marks on basis of (i) NCC or sports activities, (ii) Extra curricular special service, (iii) General physical condition and endurance (iv) General ability (v) Aptitude - Tests are objective in character - Held, is illegal and selection Committee cannot also consider matters falling outside scope of specified items - It is within purview of art. 15(4) - Petition Allowed.
