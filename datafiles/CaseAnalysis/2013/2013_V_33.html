Vinodkumar M. Malavia v Maganlal Mangaldas Gameti and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 647; (2013) 15 SCC 394; 2014(1) ALL MR 959; 2013(12) SCALE 259

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Dissolution, Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Practice & Procedure - Societies Registration Act, 1860, s. 13 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, s. 50A - Unification of churches - Validity - Change Reports were filed by First District Church of the Brethren (FDCB) a registered religious society property of which was vested with its 'Property Committee' and the Church of North India (CNI) - Change Reports were filed to give effect to the unification of six churches which included the FDCB - It was alleged that Resolution No. 70/08 was passed pursuant to which the CNI was formed by merging the six churches - FDCB being one of the six churches, discussed the unification internally within its 21 Societies and put the same to vote at different junctions and in the final decision, the resolution was approved by 3/5th majority of the representatives of the Governing Body - Allegedly, on 20-11-1970, the FDCB merged with the other six churches to form CNI and accepted the same as its legal continuation and successor and vested with the CNI its rights, titles, claimed and FDCB's interests together with its privileges and obligations - In 1976, the Church of North India Trust Association (CNITA') was formed and appointed as the trustee of CNI - It had been alleged that the annual meetings of the FDCB were discontinued post 1971 - Certain members which had earlier given consent to Resolution 70/08 raised objections that FDCB continued to exist - Subsequently, the plaintiff filed Civil Suit for a declaration that FDCB had come to an end and that CNI was the legal successor and continuation of the same - During the pendency of the suit, CNI got itself registered retrospectively and Change Report Nos. 44/81 and 665/81 were filed before the Charity Commissioner to give effect to the changes resulting the unification - Suit was dismissed - Later, Charity Commissioner adjudicated on the disputes arising out of the Change Report Nos. 44/81 and 665/81 - Charity Commissioner dismissed the objections raised against the Change Reports and allowed the properties vested in FDCB to be vested in CNI - Applications were filed thereagainst before Civil Court - Civil Court Judge, held that the FDCB had not been dissolved as there was no proper proof of the same - Furthermore, as a trust and society were creations of statutes, they should be dissolved accordingly and the question of merger was a factual one, wherein the merging trust continued to exist unless specifically dissolved under the statute - Furthermore, without following s. 50A of the 1950, Act which dealt with the dissolution of trust, the FDCB property could not be vested with CNI - Thus, the Civil Court Judge quashed and set aside the order of the Charity Commissioner - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the alleged unification of the FDCB with the CNI was correct - Held, society and the Trust being creatures of statute, had to resort to the modes provided by the statute for its amalgamation and the so-called merger could not be treated or could give effect to the dissolution of the Trust - Hence, without taking any steps in accordance with the provisions of law, the effect of the resolutions or deliberations was not acceptable in the domain of law - Further, it was the duty of the society to take steps in accordance with s. 13 of the 1860, Act for its dissolution - Unless the properties vested in the Trust were divested in accordance with the provisions of the 1860, Act and in accordance with the 1950, Act, merely by filing the Change Report/s, CNI could not claim a merger of churches and thereby claim that the properties vested in the Trust would vest in them - City Civil Court correctly held, which had been affirmed by the HC, that there was no dissolution of the society and further merger was not carried out in accordance with the provisions of law - Further, question of estoppel also could not stand in the way as the HC correctly pointed out that the freedom guaranteed under the Constitution with regard to the faith and religion, could not take away the right in changing the faith and religion after giving a fresh look and thinking at any time and thereby could not be bound by any rules of estoppel - Therefore, the resolution only resolved to accept the recommendation of joint unification but did not refer to dissolution - HC and the City Civil Court rightly adjudicated on the matter in question and correctly set aside the order passed by the Charity Commissioner - Appeals dismissed.
