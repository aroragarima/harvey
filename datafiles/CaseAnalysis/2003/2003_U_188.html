People's Union of Civil Liberties and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1399; (2004) 9 SCC 580; AIR 2004 SC 456; JT 2003 (10) SC 70; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 379; 2003(10) SCALE 967; 2003 (1) SCW 7233; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 860

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Fingerprints

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Prevention of Terrorism Act, 2002, ss. 21(2), 29(6) and 49(7) - Whether POTA, 2002 lacks legislative competence? - Parliament possesses power under art. 248 and entry 97 of list I of Seventh Schedule of Constitution of India, 1950 to legislate Act - Need for Act is matter of policy and court cannot go into same - Once legislation is passed, Govt. has obligation to exercise all available options to prevent terrorism within bounds of Constitution - Mere possibility of abuse cannot be ground for denying vesting of powers or for declaring statute unconstitutionally - Definition of abets is same as in Indian Penal Code, 1860 and mens rea is essential to prove that such person has been connected with those steps of transaction - Possession of weapon under POTA Act, 2002 presupposes conscious possession - Power is given to investigating officer under Act to ask for furnishing information that will be useful or relevant to purpose of Act - Such information could be asked only after obtaining written approval from officer not below rank of Superintendent of Police and such power to investigating officers is quite necessary in detection of terrorist activities - Court have issued clarification on interpretation of various provisions while dealing with constitutionality - Held, court upheld constitutional validity of various provisions of POTA - Appeal dismissed.
