Chandra Mohini Srivastava v Avinash Prasad Srivastava and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 158; AIR 1967 SC 581; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 864

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Civil Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(1), 10(1)(b) and 15 - Suit was brought for dissolution of marriage and grant of a decree of divorce on allegation of adultery - Respondent married after divorce being granted by High Court - Whether, decree of divorce be made without a decree of judicial separation having been first made? - Held, before a decree for divorce can be granted thereunder, there must first be a decree for judicial separation and thereafter under amendment a decree for divorce, will follow if one of two conditions is satisfied, namely that either, a period of two years has elapsed, or the case is one of exceptional hardship to petitioner or of exceptional depravity on part of other party - Appeal allowed.
