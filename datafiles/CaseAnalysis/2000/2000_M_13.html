New Delhi Municipal Council v Hirinder Sachdev, W/o Late Sohan Lal Sachdev

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 437; (2000) 2 SCC 494; AIR 2000 SC 1859; 2000 (1) CLT 326; JT 2000 (1) SC 646; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 219; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 313; 2000(1) SCALE 492; 2000 (2) SCJ 290; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 731; 2000 (2) SLT 13; 2000 (2) Supreme 141

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Electricity charges - Demand of electricity - Non-domestic rates - Commercial use - Injunction - Corporation Authorities by landlord demands of electricity and water charges were made according to non-domestic rates - Landlord protested against demand stating that user of premises for running a guest house could not be said to be a commercial use of premises - Demand was unsustainable - Landlord sought a decree of injunction against New Delhi Municipal Council/NDMC restraining it from raising demand on basis of commercial user of premises - Trial Court detained that NDMC was competent to convert charges of electricity supply from domestic category to commercial category - On appeal that NDMC was competent to vary electricity charges of suit premises - HC took view that NDMC was not entitled to charge electricity and water charges on basis of commercial use - Hence, instant appeal - Whether use of premises for purpose of a guest house could be termed as domestic use for purpose of electricity charges by NDMC - Held, that HC was not right in setting aside order that use of electricity for running guest house does not come under category of commercial use - Guest houses were maintained by company or commercial undertakings as a part of their commercial venture - HC was unsustainable - New Delhi Municipal Council was entitled to charge for use of electricity in guest house at rate applicable to commercial use - Appeal allowed.
