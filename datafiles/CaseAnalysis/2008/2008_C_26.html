Three Cheers Entertaintment Private Limited and v C.E.S.C. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2083; (2008) 16 SCC 592; AIR 2009 SC 735; 2008(6) ALL MR 970; 2008 (4) AWC 4035; 2009 (1) CalLT(SC) 69; JT 2008 (11) SC 584; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 936; 2008(13) SCALE 493; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contempt of Courts Act, 1970, Contempt of Courts (Calcutta High Court) Rules, 1975

Summary: Civil Procedure - Media & Entertainment - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 40 r. 1, - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136, 142 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 19(1), 2(b) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - Contempt of Courts (Calcutta High Court) Rules, 1975 - Defamation - Injunction - Challenged - Appellant- Company was alleged to have telecasted programs by ETV (Bangla) alleging some illegal acts and malpractices on part of CESC in undertaking engaged in generation and supply of Electricity - CESC filed defamation suit claiming not only damages but also decree for permanent injunction - Indisputably, Single Judge passed an ex parte ad interim order of injunction and receiver was appointed to hand over all documents and CDs - No Notice to such effect was intimated to appellants - Even Joint Receivers did not intimate appellant about their appointment - Joint Receivers however, visited office of appellant and asked appellants to hand over all documents and CDs - Appellants expressed their inability to hand over said documents as such documents had already been handed over to representatives of CESC - Appellant No. 3 was asked to affirm affidavit as regards events which purported to have taken place - In view of affidavit and stand taken by respondent company, trial was ordered on following issues by Single Judge in terms of an order - Contempt matter, however, keeping in view provisions contained in Rule 5 and 6 of Rules of 1975 was placed before Single Judge - Single Judge proceeded to hear contempt matter on affidavits filed by parties although trial on aforementioned issues was incomplete - Each one of alleged contemnors was held guilty of contempt of court on premise that they had violated directions issued to them to hand over documents and DVD Cassettes and other materials to Joint Receiver - Appeal in terms of s. 19(1) of the Act was preferred by appellants before DB - DB affirmed judgment and order of Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of HC was justified in passing impugned judgment - Held, HC failed to notice that Single Judge passed interim order only in terms of prayers 'a' and 'b' - Further, on bare perusal of said order would clearly indicate that even prima facie finding had not been arrived at warranting appointment of receiver - No reason as to why contempt proceeding was heard on affidavits when trial was pending - Witnesses affirming affidavits before Single Judge were not being cross-examined so as to enable counsel for parties to draw their attention to earlier statement made by them in terms of S. 145 of Evidence Act - Contempt of court was matter which deserves to be dealt with all seriousness - Hence, impugned judgment could not be sustained and exercising jurisdiction u/art. 142 of Constitution contempt proceedings be dropped - Appeal allowed.
