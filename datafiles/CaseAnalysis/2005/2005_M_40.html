M.M. Malhotra v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1048; (2005) 8 SCC 351; (2005) SCC (L&S) 1139; 2005 (6) AWC 6046; JT 2005 (9) SC 506

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Defence & Security Forces; Family & Personal; Service

Keywords: Court Martial, Superior Officer, Severe Reprimand, Air Force Rules, 1969, Court-Martial, Warrant Officer, Air Force Act, 1950, a Warrant Officer, Unbecoming Conduct, Regulations For The Air Force

Summary: Service - Air Force Act, 1950, ss. 19, 45 and 46 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 11 - Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Air Force Rules, 1969, r. 16 - Army Rules, 1954 - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964 - Whether there is need for remand to authorities to reconsider question of punishment once it is held that plural marriage was not established? - Held, if Court on considering material before it concludes that punishment awarded is not shockingly disproportionate it can maintain order - Disciplinary authority show as to how acts of appellant were clearly unbecoming of a member of disciplined force and his continuance would be prejudicial to good order and discipline - Charge of plural marriage has not been established, yet taking into account other allegations, no interference called for - Appeal dismissed.
