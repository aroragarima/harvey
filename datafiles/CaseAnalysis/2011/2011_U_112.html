Uttar Pradesh Financial Corporation and others v Sri Bharat Paper Udyog Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 626; (2011) 10 SCC 385; 2011 (8) ADJ 871; 2011 (89) ALR 484; 2012 (3) AWC 2293; 2011 (4) BC 486; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 41; 2012 (2) DRTC 609

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Default, Loan, One time settlement, No due certificate

Summary: Banking & Finance - Loan - Default - One time settlement - No-due certificate - Release order - Sustainability - Respondent- Writ petitioner -company took a loan from the appellant Corporation and defaulted repayment of loan with accumulation of interests which grew to very large sum - Respondent made application and was allowed facility of one time settlement -OTS with terms and conditions - Respondent paid first installment and demanded for release of no-dues certificate and title deeds of disputed plot and hand over possession of property, but appellant refused contending that it will be released after full amount is paid - Respondent filed writ petition contending that it was condition of OTS that appellant would release document on payment of first installment, hence appellant ought to release documents - HC, held that it was a term of onetime settlement between two sides that on payment of first instalment under settlement by respondent, Corporation would release in its favour a piece of land, , which respondent might sell or give on lease in order to facilitate payment of remaining instalments under OTS and held Corporation's insistence on realizing from respondent amount of interest on delayed payments of instalments even after it had paid full amount under OTS was unjust, unreasonable, bad and illegal - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing orders directing appellants to release No-dues certificate and title deeds - Held, respondent was bound by terms of OTS, further, terms of OTS were independent of any condition that might had come from respondent in course of negotiations preceding OTS or any condition that respondent might have put in its application for grant of OTS - Terms of OTS would also not be controlled or altered by any decision taken in any regional industrial meeting chaired by the State's Industrial Commissioner, terms of OTS would continue to bind respondent until those are duly changed and amended by Corporation - SC, was satisfied that respondent was not entitled to relief claimed on its behalf and writ petition filed in HC was liable to be dismissed, order of HC was, accordingly, set aside and writ petition filed on behalf of respondent was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
