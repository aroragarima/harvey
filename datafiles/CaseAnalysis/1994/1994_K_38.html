Tilak Chand Magatram Obhan v Kamala Prasad Shukla

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1769; (1995) Supp1 SCC 21; (1995) SCC (L&S) 251; 1994 (28) ATC 750; 1995 (5) SLR 809

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Charge sheet - Violation - Natural justice - Handicapped - Respondent/delinquent who was appointed as teacher was suspended from service and was charge-sheeted - Enquiry Committee was appointed found respondent guilty and removed him from service - Respondent appealed to Deputy Director of Education was allowed and declared rules of natural justice had been violated - Management filed appeal was allowed by Joint Director of Education - Respondent filed W.P was dismissed by HC - In appeal Division Bench of HC reversed decision - Hence instant appeal - Whether enquiry proceedings was in violation of principle of natural justice - Held, principal of school was deeply biased against delinquent - Charge sheet should be made as clean as possible - Distinction between defect in enquiry and lapse destroys enquiry - Lapse was of enquiry being conducted by officer deeply biased against delinquent that entire enquiry proceedings were rendered void - Appellate Authority could not repair damage done to enquiry - Delinquent was asked to appear before committee of one member was deeply hostile towards him, that would made delinquent handicapped in conducting his defense - Consequences flowing from one of members of Enquiry Committee being biased - Appeal dismissed.
