Md. Raisul Islam & Ors v Gokul Mohan Hazarika & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 537; (2010) 7 SCC 560; AIR 2010 SC 3064; JT 2010 (6) SC 632; 2010(6) SCALE 643; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 732; 2011 (1) SLR 69

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Assam Civil Service (Class-I) (Amendment) Rules, 1986, Assam Civil Services (Class-I) Rules, 1960, A.P. Subordinate Service Rules, ACS (Class-II) Rules, 1962

Summary: Service - Assam Civil Services (Class-I) Rules, 1960, rr. 19, 4(1) - Inter se seniority - Determination - Assam Public Service Commission (APSC) invited application for the post of ACS Class-I post by way of direct recruitment u/r. 4 of the Rules - APSC conducted written examination and viva voce and send its list of recommended candidates to Government - R. 4(1) of the Rules was amended and amendment rules replaced the 50 % quota reserved for promotees with discretion of the Governor - APSC appointed 45 ACS Class-I officers including petitioners by way of direct recruitment and 129 officer including respondents by way of promotion - State published final gradation list wherein respondent/officers were placed senior to petitioner/officers - Petitioner challenged the final gradation list before the HC - Single Judge dismissed petitioners' writ petition - Petitioners challenged Single Judge order before the DB - DB allowed petitioners' appeal holding that the seniority of direct recruits and promotees would be governed by the un-amended Rules as the selection process was initiated prior to the amendments, therefore, directed the Government to place the respondents above the petitioners in the seniority list - Whether the 1960 Rules as amended would govern the seniority of the persons recruited in the process of selection commenced earlier to the amendment - Held, process of selection commenced on the basis of the Rules then in existence would continue under the said Rules, even though the Rules may have been amended in the meantime - Recommendation list of direct recruits made by the APSC was submitted to the Government before the amended Rules came into operation whereby the quota system was discarded - Seniority of members of the service would be governed u/r. 19, but the selection process has to be completed u/r. 4 in order to attract the provisions of r. 19 - Vacancies for which the advertisement had been published before the amendment of the Rules were directed to be filled up by the HC on the basis of the un-amended r. 4 which provided for quota between promotees and direct recruits and, accordingly, placed 45 of the direct recruits immediately below the first 45 promotees - HC findings is based on well-established principle that once a process of selection is started on the basis of the existing Rules of recruitment, the said Rules will continue to govern the selection process, notwithstanding any amendment which may have been effected to the said Rules in the meantime - Therefore, impugned HC order does not warrant any interference - Appeal dismissed.
