Cambay Electric Supply Industrial Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat II

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 241; (1978) SCC (Tax) 119; (1978) 2 SCC 644; AIR 1978 SC 1099; 1978 CTR 50; [1988] 113 ITR 84; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 660; 1978 TAXLR 753; 1978 UJ 412

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 32(2), 33(2), 41(2), 72 and 80E(1) - Deduction - Assessee engaged in the business of generation and distribution of electricity and, as such, is covered by the provisions of s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act and is entitled to claim the deduction contemplated by the said provision - During a/y 1967-68 assessee sold some of its old machinery and building resulting in balancing charges contemplated by s. 41(2) of 1961 Act which the Income-tax Officer worked out at Rs. 7,55,807/- - It further appears that there was unabsorbed depreciation of Rs. 1,42,955/- and unabsorbed development rebate of Rs. 1,11,658/- aggregating to Rs. 2,54,613/- of the earlier years which were required to be set-off against the profits of that period - Income Tax Officer treated the item of Rs. 7,55,807/- as profits attributable to the business of generation and distribution of electricity and allowed deduction at 8% thereon u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act - Additional Commissioner took the view that the manner of computing the deduction admissible to the assessee u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act was erroneous and prejudicial to the interests of the revenue, in that the deduction of 8% on the item of profit of Rs. 7,55,807 had been wrongly allowed and that for the purpose of calculating the deduction of 8% the items in respect of the unabsorbed depreciation and development rebate should not have been excluded, and the assessee was not entitled to any deduction - On appeal, Tribunal set aside the order of the Additional Commissioner and restored the order of the Income Tax Officer - HC upheld the view of the Tribunal regarding the item of Rs.7,55,807/- and as to the items of unabsorbed depreciation and development rebate, HC held that they were deductible before arriving at the figure that would be exigible to the deduction of 8% u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether profits u/s. 41(2) of the 1961 Act arising from the sale of machinery and building, amounting to Rs. 7,55,807/- should be taken into account while computing the deduction of 8% u/s. 80E(1) of the 1961 Act? - Held, s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act has been enacted for the purpose of providing for certain special deduction to be made in computing the total income in the case of specified industries, over and above the other general deductions contemplated by the Act - Three important steps are required to be taken before the special deduction permissible u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act is allowed i.e. first compute the total income of the concerned assessee in accordance with the other provisions of the Act, secondly, ascertain what part of the total income so computed represents the profits and gains attributable to the business of the specified industry and thirdly, if there be profits and gains so attributable, deduct 8% thereof from such profits and gains and then arrive at the net total income exigible to tax - Hence, the item of Rs. 7,55,807/- will have to be taken into account before computing the 8% deduction contemplated by the said provision - (B) Whether unabsorbed depreciation and development rebate amounting to Rs. 2,54,613 is not deductible in computing profits u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act? - Held, on proper construction of the provisions contained in sub-s. (1) of s. 80E of 1961 Act item like unabsorbed depreciation and absorbed development rebate will have to be deducted in arriving at the figure which would be exigible to deduction of 8% u/s. 80E(1) of 1961 Act - Appeals dismissed.
