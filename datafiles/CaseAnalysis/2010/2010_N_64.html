Niranjan Panja v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 397; (2010) 6 SCC 525; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2283; 2010 (3) JCC 2024; JT 2010 (5) SC 204; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 709; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 750; 2010(5) SCALE 651; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant for murder - Whether conviction by the Trial Court is sustainable - Held, appellant did not have any strong motive to commit the murder - Evidences of the prosecution witnesses were extremely suspicious in the absence of corroborated evidence - Where the prosecution depends upon the theory of 'last seen together', it is always necessary that the prosecution should establish the time of death, which the prosecution failed to do in the present case - Weapon allegedly used by the appellant had not been produced in the court nor had the witness identified the same as well as any explanation provided about the disappearance of the said weapon - Doctor who conducted post mortem, did not fix the time of death in the post mortem report - Investigating Officer did not execute the spot Panchanama from where the weapon used for the murder was allegedly procured by the appellant - Hardly any clinching evidence to believe the theory that the appellant had committed the murder - Prosecution utterly failed to prove that the appellant had committed the murder of the deceased - Judgments of the Trial Court as well as the Appellate Court are incorrect judgments and hence set aside - Appellant is acquitted - Appeal allowed.
