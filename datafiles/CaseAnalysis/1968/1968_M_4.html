Special Land Acquisition and Rehabilitation Officer, Sagar v M. S. Seshagiri Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 342; AIR 1968 SC 1045; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 892

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Whether the High Court rightly observed that since the Govt. had failed to exercise the right which it had under the terms of the grant and had adopted the procedure prescribed by the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 compensation for acquisition under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 and the process by which the grantees were to be deprived of the land must be followed? - The State in a proceeding for acquisition does not acquire its own interest in the land, and the Collector offers and the Civil Court assesses compensation for acquisition of the interest of the private persons which gets extinguished by compulsory acquisition and pays compensation equivalent to the market value of that interest - There is nothing in the Act which prevents the State from claiming in the proceeding for acquisition of land notified for acquisition that the interest proposed to be acquired is a restrictive interest - The contention observing that the Govt. availing itself of the machinery under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 for compulsory acquisition and treating the subject-matter of the acquisition as not belonging to itself but to others, is under an obligation to pay compensation as provided in the Act, and that the Govt. was incompetent in the proceeding under the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 to put forward its own title to the property sought to be acquired are in our judgment, not accurate - Held, the order passed by the HC is maintained subject to the modification that the market value of the interest of the grantees in the land at the date of the notification under s. 4 of the Land Acquisition Act, 1894 shall be determined and paid to the grantees in addition to the compensation paid for the improvement in the land.
