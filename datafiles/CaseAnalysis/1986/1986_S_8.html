Maj. Genl., A. S. Gauraya and Another v S. N. Thakur and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 53; (1986) SCC (Cr) 249; (1986) 2 SCC 709; AIR 1986 SC 1440; 1986 AllCriC 346; 1986 (12) ALR 606; 1986 (12) AWC 995; 1986 BBCJ 104; 1986 (3) Crimes 1; 1986 CRLJ 1074; 1986 CrLR(SC) 225; 1986 (30) DLT 211; 1986 MPLJ 438; 1986(1) SCALE 1128; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 771; 1986 (1) UJ 654

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 249 and 256 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - (A) Whether Magistrate has inherent power to review order of dismissal of complaint case for default - (B) Precedent - Scope - Question as to binding nature of Supreme Court's decision - Held, no inherent power to review his order of dismissal and restore case - Law laid down by SC applies to all pending proceedings even with retrospective effect - Appeal allowed.
