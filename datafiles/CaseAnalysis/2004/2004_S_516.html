Palitana Sugar Mills Private Limited and Another v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1418; (2004) 12 SCC 645; JT 2004 (9) SC 526; 2004(8) SCALE 871; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 552

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act, 1976 - Gujarat Agricultural Land Ceiling Act, 1960 - On 09-09-2003, appellant submitted its revised plans of survey no. 469/1 and submitted to Bhavnagar Municipal Corporation for sanctioning lay out and building plans in view of Development Control Rules - An interim order was passed by this court on 09-09-2003 directing authorities to sanction plan of survey no. 469/1 and make entries in revenue records - According to appellant, this order was not complied with officers of State Govt. - Whether authorities have erred in not permitting appellant to carry out construction? - Held, not only that Revenue Entry was confirmed but State Govt. had sanctioned construction scheme prepared by petitioner company for construction of dwelling units for weaker sections of society - It is too late now for Collector, Bhavnagar to raise an issue that part of land was bid land and erstwhile ruler of Bhavnagar could not have sold it to petitioner company - In respect of land bearing Revenue survey no. 469/1, all dues have been paid for sanctioning plan and further appellant-company has always been ready to pay non-agricultural tax for revenue survey no. 469/1 but Collector and Commr. have refused to grant any permission even after various reminders sent to them by appellant-company - Through this letter, appellant no. 1 once again called upon Collector, Commr., Deputy Secy., Revenue Department and other officers of state Govt. to forthwith comply with orders passed by this court on 05-05-2004 - In our opinion, in respect of specific direction issued by this court in 2004, authorities are deliberately not complying with orders passed by this court and prevented appellants from carrying out construction on lands in question for last four years even though appellants have succeeded in all petitions before this court - Directions issued.
