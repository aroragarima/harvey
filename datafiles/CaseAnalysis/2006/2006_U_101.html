Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 74; (2006) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2006 SC 1383; 2006 (201) CTR 346; [2006] 282 ITR 273; JT 2006 (3) SC 114; 2006(2) SCALE 752; 2006 (2) SCJ 522; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 823; 2006 (145) STC 91; 2006 (2) S.T.R. 161; [2006] 152 TAXMAN 135

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Telephone, Sales Tax Authorities, Statement Of Objects And Reasons, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, Transfer Of Right To Use Goods, Situs Of Sale, Telephone Service, Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Service Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 131, 136, 226, 248(1), 286, 32, 366, 366 (29A)(d), 366(12), 366(29-A) - Finance Act, 1994, s. 65(72)(b) - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2(aa), 2(c), 2(d), 2(h) -Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, ss. 3(1), 3(6), s. 4 - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, s. 2(k) - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, s. 2(n) - Finance Act, 2002, s. 149(90)(b) - Finance Act, 2003, s. 159(105)(110)(b) - Nature of transaction by which mobile phone connections are enjoyed, Is it a sale or is it a service or is it both? - What are 'goods' in telecommunication for purposes of art.366 (29 A)(d)? - Is there any transfer of any right to use any goods by providing access or telephone connection by telephone service provider to a subscriber? - Is nature of transaction involved in providing telephone connection a composite contract of service and sale? - If so, is it possible for States to tax sale element? - If providing of a telephone connection involves sale is such sale an inter state one? - Would 'aspect theory' be applicable to transaction enabling States to levy sales tax on same transaction in respect of which Union Govt. levies service tax? - Held, Goods do not include electromagnetic waves or radio frequencies for purpose of art.366(29 A)(d) - Goods in telecommunication are limited to handsets supplied by service provider - Nature of transaction involved in providing telephone connection may be a composite contract of service and sale - It is possible for State to tax sale element provided there is a discernible sale and only to extent relatable to such sale - Aspect theory would not apply to enable value of services to be included in sale of goods or price of goods in value of service - Remanded matter to Sales Tax Authorities concerned for determination of issue relating to SIM Cards - Appeals disposed of.
