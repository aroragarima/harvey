Om Prabha Jain v Abnesh Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 231; AIR 1968 SC 1083; 1968 CurLJ 711; 1968 (2) SCJ 807; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 111

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Discretionary grant - Bribe - Disbursement - Anxiety - Election petitioner filed petition contended that appellant made discretionary grants to bribe voters of her constituency and by paying money - HC on appraisal of circumstances, declared election of appellant was void - Hence instant appeal - Whether source of information and witnesses against appellant was justified - Held, election petitioner was fumbling with facts and not able to state categorically - Oral evidence was practically non-existing - Source of information was not attempted to be proved and witnesses were found to be unreliable - Money was to be disbursed through Municipal Committee - Money disbursed in hurry did not emanate from appellant that it was result of anxiety on part of recipients that money should be made available soon - Order accordingly.
