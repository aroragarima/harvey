Â Commissioner of Sales Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Modi Sugar Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 256; AIR 1961 SC 1047; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 189; 1961 (12) STC 182

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - United Provinces Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 3, 3-A, 7, 11 and 24 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Amendment Act - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Rules, rr. 39, 40 and 41 - Whether High Court had erred in holding respondent company liable to pay tax for assessment year 1948-49 on turnover of previous year in respect of sales of non-edible oils at flat rate of 3 pies per rupee? - A legal fiction must be limited to purposes for which it has been created and cannot be extended beyond its legitimate field - Held, conclusion of HC is correct - Assessment to sales-tax of respondent company by applying to its turnover of year 1947-48, rate of tax specified in notification of 08-06-1948, as determined by Sales-tax Officer was in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
