Pratibha Nema and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 569; (2003) 10 SCC 626; AIR 2003 SC 3140; 2003 (4) AWC 2902B; JT 2003 (6) SC 256; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 28; 2003(5) SCALE 622; 2003 (4) SLT 724; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 890; 2003 (5) Supreme 557

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 3(e), 3(f), 38A - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226 - Land Acquisition Act - ss.4(1), 5 A, 5 A(2), 6, 9(2) - Establishment of Diamond Park for setting up diamond cutting and polishing units with modern technology - Whether acquisition is for a public purpose - Whether public purpose stated in Notifications under ss 4 and 6 is vague - Whether area of land proposed to be acquired is far in excess of reasonable requirements - Whether environmental considerations were not kept in view while taking a decision to acquire land for industrial purpose - Held, what is considered to be an acquisition for facilitating setting up of an industry in private sector could get imbued with character of public purpose acquisition, if only Govt. comes forward to sanction payment of a nominal sum towards compensation - Public purpose does not cease to be vague merely because acquisition facilitates setting up of industry by a private enterprise and benefits it to that extent - Nor existence or otherwise of public purpose be judged by lead and initiative taken by entrepreneurs desirous of setting up industry and measure of coordination between them and various state agencies fact that despite unwillingness expressed by AKI Ltd, to go ahead with project, Govt. is still interested in acquisition is yet another pointer that acquisition was motivated by public purpose - It is primarily within domain of State Govt. to decide how much extent of land has to be acquired keeping in view present and future needs - topography of area and development around area are some of factors that could be legitimately taken into account - It is not possible to hold that proposed diamond park project will be detrimental to public health, safety or security so as to override public interest that is served by setting up export-oriented industries.
