Ranvir Singh v State Of Haryana & Anr

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1518; (2009) 9 SCC 642; 2009 (4) JCC 2805; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2876; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1009; 2009(12) SCALE 89; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1063; 2010 (2) UC 907

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Process Fee

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 202, 203, 190, 319, 362, 369 and 424 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 7, 8, 9, 11, 13 and 19 - Second complaint - Maintainability of - Petitioner filed applications seeking direction for quashing complaint filed by respondent No.2 u/ss. 420/34 IPC and another complaint u/ss. 7, 8, 9, 11 and 13 of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - HC dismissed both criminal revision petitions - Hence, present special leave petitions - Petitioner submitted that both complaints filed without sanction u/s. 19 of Prevention of Corruption Act were not maintainable, particularly when earlier complaint on same cause of action was dismissed - Whether second complaint would be maintainable when earlier one not dismissed on merits, but for failure of complainant to put in process fees for effecting service? - Held, relied on SC in Pramatha Nath Talukdar's case 1961 INDLAW SC 411 wherein it was held that even if a complaint was dismissed u/s. 203 CrPC a second complaint would still lie under exceptional circumstances - Also in Jatinder Singh's case 2001 INDLAW SC 20322 it was held no provision which debars complainant from preferring a second complaint on same allegations if first complaint was not disposed on merits - Second complaint maintainable in different circumstances as enumerated in Pramatha Nath Talukdar's case and Jatinder Singh's case - Special Leave Petitions dismissed.
