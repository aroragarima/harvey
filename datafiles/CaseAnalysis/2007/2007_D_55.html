Gedela Satchidananda Murthy (D) By Legal v Deputy Commissioner, Endowments Department

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 673; (2007) 5 SCC 677; AIR 2007 SC 1917; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 44; 2007 (3) CHN 152; JT 2007 (8) SC 10; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 177; 2007(7) SCALE 614; 2007 (4) SCJ 667; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Residential House, A.P. Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1966

Summary: A.P. Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1966, s. 78 - Held, only because members of the public are freely admitted to the temple, that by itself would not be sufficient to come to the conclusion that the temple was a public institution - Merely because the appellant has a residential house in the portion of the property which is the subject matter of the trust, the same is not outside the purview of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
