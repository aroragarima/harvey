Alapati Venkataramiah v Commissioner of Income Tax Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1966 SC 115; [1965] 57 ITR 185; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 9; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 12 B - Transfer of Property Act, s. 53 A - Whether capital gains arise in relevant assessment year? - Agreement was entered into by assessee for sale of business which was ratified by Board of Directors (of Purchaser Co.) in next financial year - Sale deed was executed and registered in next financial year and therefore title to assets did not pass to Company and no sale took place in relevant financial year - Date of sale or transfer according to s. 12B is date when sale or transfer takes place - Held, no capital gains in respect of immovable assets arose in relevant previous year but as regards movable assets namely furniture, title passed on delivery and capital gains to that extent was assessable - Appeal partly allowed.
