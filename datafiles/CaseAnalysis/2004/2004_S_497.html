Deelip Singh @ Dilip Kumar v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 999; (2005) 1 SCC 88; (2005) SCC (Cr) 253; AIR 2005 SC 203; JT 2004 (9) SC 469; 2005 (2) MahLJ 147; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 972; 2004(9) SCALE 278; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 909

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, ss. 375, 376 and 90 - Convicted and sentenced - Appeal against conviction - Whether appellant had sexual intercourse with victim girl against her will?; whether consent obtained on basis of promise to marry which was not acted upon, could be regarded as consent for purpose of s. 375? - Held, awareness of prosecutrix that marriage may not take place at all in view of caste barrier was an important factor - Her participation in sexual act was voluntary and deliberate - No evidence to an inference beyond reasonable doubt that accused had no intention to marry her at all from inception and that promise he made was false to his knowledge - No circumstances emerging from prosecution evidence establish this fact - Conviction and sentence set aside - Appeal allowed.
