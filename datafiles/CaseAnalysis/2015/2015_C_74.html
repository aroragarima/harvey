Tata Chemicals Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 539; (2016) 1 SCC 263; JT 2015 (7) SC 198; 2015(8) SCALE 531; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Assessable Value, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Excisable Goods, Sale Price, Customs, Excise And Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1982

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Claim of arrangement - Return of packaging material - Determination - Appellant stated that there is arrangement between manufacture and their customer for return of durable packing, namely, gunny bags, for reuse as packing material for selling soda ash in bulk for which appellant produced letters - However, Tribunal failed to accept letters in question and held that assessee's effort to establish that there was arrangement between manufacturer and their customers to return durable packing, namely, gunny bags, and accordingly claim put forth by them that value of gunny bags used for packing soda ash manufactured by them should be excluded in finding out assessable value was unsustainable - Tribunal remanded matter to adjudicating authority for computation of actual amount of duty - Whether impugned order passed by Tribunal against appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, by first judge of bench that once it is accepted that it has nature and character of arrangement, then authority is required to ascertain from record whether buyers continued to have choice to return packing material for reuse. Further, there is no need to indicate method of verification of existence of arrangement for period in question. Triveni Glass Limited, 2005 Indlaw SC 115, relied on it is held that once existence arrangement and choice to return packing material for reuse are established for period in question, packing cost would not be included. If assessee succeeds in establishing choice mentioned in documents which and is prevalent during relevant period i.e. 1981 to 1985, appellant shall be given benefit. If he fails to establish same, adjudicating authority shall look into consideration actual return. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside and matter is remanded to adjudicating authority for adjudication. However, second judge of bench dissented with order passed by first judge on grounds that with reference to letter in question, it is apparent that no such express arrangement has been made between parties because value of gunny bags have been included in final sale price of soda ash and no express arrangement has been made by appellant with buyers for return of gunny bags for reason that there would be deduction in sale price, only when gunny bags were returned to appellants. Further, it is held that exclusion of cost of packing material from value of goods, irrespective of customers returning same to appellants is illegal and invalid and same cannot be justified by appellant by taking plea that letters in question indicate that there is arrangement between parties to return used gunny bags to appellant. Further, credit notes dt. 12-3-1988 and 31-3-1988 cannot be relied, since credit notes relate to year 1988, whereas present case is concerned with period 1981 to 1985. There is no independent evidence which can help establish case of appellant during that relevant period of time. Further, in most of letters sent by buyers to appellant, buyers send their own packing material and in case they cannot provide appellant with packing material, appellant was required to send soda ash in its own packing material on which packing charges have been incurred by buyers. Hence, order passed by Tribunal rejecting claim of appellant so far as exclusion of cost of packing material with value of soda ash is concerned is confirmed and hence, it is liable to pay tax liability. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - In absence of any arrangement between parties, assessee cannot claim exemption from duty.
