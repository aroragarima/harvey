Riju Prasad Sarma etc. etc. v State of Assam and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 475; (2015) 9 SCC 461; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 286; JT 2015 (6) SC 555; 2015 (5) MLJ 727; 2015(7) SCALE 602; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Arrangement, State Action, Morality, Women, Religion, Constitutionality, Punjab Pre-Emption Act, 1913, Charitable Institutions, Shebaits, Assam State Acquisition of Lands Belonging to Religious or Charitable Institutions of Public Nature Act, 1959, Assam State Acquisition of Lands Belonging to Religious or Charitable Institution of Public Nature (Election of managing committee of Sri Sri Maa Kamakhya Temple) Rules, 2012, Kamakhya Debutter Regulation, 1998

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Assam State Acquisition of Lands belonging to Religious or Charitable Institutions of Public Nature (Amendment) Act, 1987, s. 25A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Management of affairs of temple - Locus Standi of Board - Determination - Suit is filed by appellant u/s. 92 of CPC against head priest of temple seeking fresh scheme for management of endowment which was however, declared in favor of priest i.e. Dolois - Further, Dolois as agents of Bordeoris are recognized as Head of public religious endowment of temple - Amendment act introduced s. 25A for constitution of Managing Committee which was challenged by appellant - Deputy Commissioner issued order for immediate dissolution of then Managing Committee of temple headed by then two Dolois and directed to hand over charge of office to Deputy Commissioner - Additional Deputy Commissioner was appointed to discharge functions of Managing Committee till Committee u/s. 25A of Act could be constituted - Lower court upheld validity of s. 25 of Act and had upheld locus standi of appellants on ground that it does not lie in State respondents to challenge authority of Board to manage affairs of temple as they have not made any attempt to de-recognize or question its authority in any court of law - Further, DB upheld validity of s. 25 of Act however, reversed order passed by lower Court with respect to locus standi of appellant - Further, DB held that administration of temple to be carried on by appellant Board and two Dolois has been given exclusive monopoly in religious affairs excluding female members - Whether order passed by DB against appellant with respect to locus standi is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, writ petitions filed before lower court and DB claimed rights in petitioners as administrator or as lawful representative of religious endowment or deity and were not in nature of PIL. Further, in view of strong and categorical denial made by respondents to right of Board to represent deity in petition, DB could not have ignored issue of rights and status. Hence, it was necessary for DB on being called upon through pleadings, to decide locus or status of appellants representing Board. SC held that DB could not have shut its eyes and ears to such serious dispute arising in context of public religious endowment relating to temple. Hence, contention of appellants that DB erred in deciding locus or status of Board represented by appellants cannot be accepted. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Assam State Acquisition of Lands belonging to Religious or Charitable Institutions of Public Nature (Amendment) Act, 1987, s. 25A - Management of affairs of temple - Order passed in favor of Board - Justification - Whether findings of DB upholding control of appellant over religious and secular affairs of temple and endowment as per customs is correct.

Held earlier DB judgment had merely affirmed Constitutionality of s. 25 A of Act at instance of another religious institution but had no occasion to weigh powers of statutory Committee vis-Ã -vis customary rights of Bordeori Samaj and its elected representatives, Dolois. SC held that DB therefore rightly examined width and scope of powers of customary trustees - Bordeories and their elected agent, Dolois considering all relevant materials and custom, it committed no error in upholding their right to take care of management of secular as well as religious affairs of Temple. Further powers of Bordeories and Dolois has not been taken away or adversely affected by Act as it stood earlier or even after s. 25A of Act was inserted. Further, statutory Committee u/s. 25A of Act is concerned only with annuity payable or paid under Act to Head of Institution and not with its ownership or management. Power of Committee is limited to verification of proper maintenance of accounts of Institution concerned and that too relating only to utilization of annuity and other government grants under Act, if any. SC held that favouring statutory Committee with powers to manage or oversee even only secular aspect of management of Institution will not only run counter to objects and reasons for Amending Act. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Administrative - Assam State Acquisition of Lands belonging to Religious or Charitable Institutions of Public Nature (Amendment) Act, 1987, s. 25A - Election of ex-officio secretary - Validity of Rules - Determination - Assam State Acquisition of Lands Belonging to Religious or Charitable Institution of Public Nature (Election of managing committee of Sri Sri Maa Kamakhya Temple) Rules, 2012 - Petitioner contended that impugned provisions of Rules are contrary to mandate of s. 25A of Act which u/cl. (b) requires that ex-officio secretary be elected by Deories/Bordeories - Whether Rules framed u/s. 25 of Act is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, s. 25A (b) of Act gives choice or option for electing ex-officio secretary either by Deories or Bordeories depending upon facts of particular religious or charitable institution. Further, it is not case of petitioners that all institutions in State have both Deories and Bordeories. In that view of matter it would be inevitable to get ex-officio secretary elected either by Deories or Bordeories, whosoever may be managing concerned institution. Under statutory powers, State Govt. in context of this particular institution has recognized only Bordeories by referring to traditional Bordeori Samaj of temple and other Devotees, Shebaits and Deories, if any, have been included in category of 'Devotee' with right to participate in election of other five members of Managing Committee. Rules thus supplement provisions of Act and do not run counter to intention of legislature which has accepted Rules without exercising its power to make modifications. SC held that Rules must be treated as part of Act and in absence of any conflict it has to be held that Rules of 2012 only explain real intention of legislature. Appeals dismissed.

(D) Land & Property - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Alleged in-equality in election - Determination - Petitioner contended that custom relating to election of Dolois to extent it violates art. 14 of Constitution, must be treated as void and relief should be granted to women members of Bordeories families and also to Deories by ordering for inclusion of their names in electoral college.

Held, dispute of facts may be resolved only on basis of detailed proper study of customs and practices in temple but there is no authoritative textual commentary or report which may help SC in coming to definite finding that women belonging to Bordeori families are equally adapt in religious or secular matters relating to that temple. Further, relevant scriptures have also not been disclosed to SC which could have helped in ascertaining whether basic religious tenets governing in Temple would not stand violated by permitting female Bordeories to elect or to get elected as Dolois. Hence, SC held that on facts it is not in position to come to definite finding on issue of equality for purpose at hand as claimed by petitioners. Further, SC directed District administration to ensure that premises are vacated by members or representatives of Board at earliest and premised and other properties of Temple shall be placed back in possession of Bordeories Samaj through last elected Dolois. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Granting authority in favor of a religious group to manage affairs of temple would depend upon prevailing customs and legislature intention provided in statute.
