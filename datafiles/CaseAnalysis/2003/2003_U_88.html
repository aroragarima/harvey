V.K. Majotra with Shambhu Dayal with Gopal Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 707; (2003) 8 SCC 40; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1202; AIR 2003 SC 3909; 2003 (4) AWC 3370; 2003 (99) FLR 527; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 137; 2003 LabIC 3176; 2003 (2) LCD 1205; 2003 (4) LLN 86; 2003(7) SCALE 297; 2003 (6) SLR 471; 2003 (5) SLT 388; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 483; 2003 (6) Supreme 943; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2760

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - ss. 6, 6(2)(a) - Empanelling challenged on the ground of non-qualification to be chosen as Vice-Chairman of Central Administrative Tribunal - High Court dismissed the petition on the point which was not raised by either parties - Whether the appointment of Vice-Chairman should be made from amongst the persons mentioned in of s. 6(2)(a) of the Act alone - Held, it is not desirable to stop the appointment of members of administrative services as administrative members to the Tribunal. Rather the judicious mix of Judicial Members and those with grassroot experience would serve purpose better for which the Tribunals were created. Contention that Tribunal should consist only of Judicial members would attack the primary basis of the theory pursuant to which the Tribunals were constituted.
