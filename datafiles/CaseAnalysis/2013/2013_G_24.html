Gian Chand and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 467; (2013) 14 SCC 420; AIR 2013 SC 3395; 2013 CRLJ 4058; 2013 (4) JCC (Narcotics) 137; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2749; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 916; 2013 (3) RLW 2663; 2013(9) SCALE 544; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 728

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 15 of 1985 Act and sentenced them to undergo rigorous imprisonment for a period of 10 years each and to pay a fine of Rs.1 lakh each - Judgement of Trial Court was affirmed by the HC - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that no independent witness was examined by the prosecution in the case, though a large number of people have gathered at the place of the alleged incident which led to the appellants being apprehended - Whether order of the Trial Court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, mere non-joining of an independent witness where the evidence of the prosecution witnesses may be found to be cogent, convincing, creditworthy and reliable, could not cast doubt on the version forwarded by the prosecution if there seems to be no reason on record to falsely implicate the appellants - In the instant case at the time of incident some villagers had gathered there - Investigating Officer in his cross-examination had made it clear that in spite of his best persuasion, none of them were willing to become a witness - Therefore, he could not examine any independent witness - Moreover, s. 114 of 1872 Act gives rise to the presumption that every official act done by the police was regularly performed and such presumption requires rebuttal - When acts were of official nature and went through the process of scrutiny by official persons, a presumption arises that the said acts have regularly been performed - Hence, submissions of the appellants in this regard, were held to be without any substance - Appeal dismissed.
