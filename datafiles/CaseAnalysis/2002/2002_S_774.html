Baldev Singh v Surinder Mohan Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1376; (2003) 1 SCC 34; AIR 2003 SC 225; 2003 (1) AllCJ 564; 2002 (49) ALR 650; 2002 (5) AWC 4239; 2002 (2) CRC 637; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 58; JT 2002 (9) SC 235; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 781; 2003 (1) RD 195; 2002(8) SCALE 296; 2002 (6) SLT 287; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 43; 2003 (1) UC 170

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Section 96-First appeal-Locus standi-Decree passed for dissolution of marriage between husband and wife-Husband having filed suit for possession against a third party-Third party filing appeal against order in matrimonial suit-No cause of action for filing appeal or revision shown-No prejudice likely to be caused if order allowed to stand-Third party having no right to intrude into relationship between husband and wife-No locus standi is available to third party to file the appeal.
