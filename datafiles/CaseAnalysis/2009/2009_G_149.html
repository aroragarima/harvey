Union Of India & Ors. v Gyan Chand Chattar

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1015; (2009) 12 SCC 78; 2009 (2) CLR 715; 2009 (3) ESC 506; 2009 (122) FLR 684; 2009 (3) LLN 70; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 44; 2009(8) SCALE 575; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 124; 2009 (5) SLR 214

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Railway Service Conduct Rules, 1966

Summary: Service - Railway Service Conduct Rules, 1966 - Western Railways - Misconduct - Removal - Six charges found to be proved by enquiry officer - Finding of EO accepted by disciplinary authority and order of removal passed - In appeal, statutory authority upheld the order - Special Civil Application filed before High Court - Application was allowed wherein order of removal was quashed and direction was issued as to imposition of minor penalty - In Letters Patent Appeal Division Bench of HC upheld the judgment of single Judge - Judgement of HC whether proper and justified? - Held, Yes - Judgement of HC by its single Judge which was of 140 pages, noted, and findings given and grounds of such findings on each of the six charges examined - Court noted the attending fact, of agitation by Railway staff demanding the payment of pay and allowances, and their act of detaining the train, at the time when enquiry was initiated and observed that enquiry was the result of anguish of superior officers due to such agitation - Out of all the charges, charge no. 6 was related to payments to be made to the concerned staff with respect to some arrears, wherein it was the allegation that commission of 1% was demanded by the respondent to which employees had refused; and Court observed on it that this was the only charge which could be made ground for imposing major penalty of removal, as it is settled proposition of law that if a charge of corruption is proved, no punishment other than dismissal can be awarded; but charge no. 6 was found to be totally vague, for which there were only hearsay evidences available - Impugned judgements upheld - Lapse of three decades from the date of respondent's suspension, noted by Court and further it observed that respondent had reached the age of superannuation long back - Direction issued to appellant to pay to respondent 50% pay and allowances without interest till respondent reached the age of superannuation and arrears of retiral benefits with 9% interest within a period of three months from the instant day of the judgment - Appeal disposed of.
