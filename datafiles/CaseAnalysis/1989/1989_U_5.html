U.S. Sasidharan v K. Karunakaran And Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 204; (1989) 4 SCC 482; AIR 1990 SC 924; JT 1989 (3) SC 611; 1989(2) SCALE 401; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 958

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81(3), 83(a), 83(b), 86(1) and 123(7) - (A) Election petition - Service of document on respondent - Allegation of corrupt practice - Petition must set forth material facts constituting corrupt practice - Material facts contained in document and document referred to in petition without pleading material facts - (B) Procurement of assistance of Govt. servant - Allegation of use at instance of elected candidate, of cassette containing speeches of Govt. officers relating to progress of certain community - Held, (A) Copy thereof must be supplied - (B) Speeches simpliciter do not constitute corrupt practice, they must be with a view to obtaining or procuring assistance for furtherance of prospects of election of candidate - Appeal dismissed.
