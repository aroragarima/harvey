Tata Iron and Steel Co. Ltd. And Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2229; (1996) 9 SCC 709; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 678; AIR 1996 SC 2462; AIR 1996 SCW 3031; 1996 (3) CCC 177; JT 1996 (6) SC 685; 1996(5) SCALE 414; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 808; 1996 (5) Supreme 699

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 39(b) and 226 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 3, 6, 8(1), 8(2) and 8(3) - Mining lease of high commercial value and long duration granted to a company - Proceedings for its renewal - (A) More than a year prior to date of expiry of lease, Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited(TISCO) applied for a second renewal u/s. 8(3) of the Act for a further period of 20 years - State Govt., recommended to Central Govt. that the entire lease of TISCO be renewed for a period of 10 years u/s. 8(3) of the Act - On 3-6-1999, Central Govt. authorised renewal of lease over entire area of 1261.476 hectares - Whether High Court was justified in striking down decisions of Central Govt. D/- 3-6-1993 and 5-10-1993 on ground that requirement of s. 8(3) of Act had not been met? - Language of s. 8(3) is quite clear in its import - Ordinarily, a lease is not to be granted beyond time and number of periods mentioned in cls. (1) and (2) - If however, Central Govt. is of the view that to allow a lessee's lease to be renewed further would be in the interest of mineral development, then it is empowered to do so, provided there exists on record sound reasons for such an action and those reasons are recorded - Held, order D/- 3-6-1993 is a statement which declares grant of a second renewal to TISCO but it does not profess to give any reasons for such a decision and, for that reason, falls foul of requirement of s. 8(3) - (B) Whether report of Rao Committee and decision of this Court in Indian Metals case' 1990 Indlaw SC 998 are relevant for consideration of renewal of leases u/s. 8(3) of Act? - Further held, Central Govt, though not bound to follow recommendations of Rao Report, was at very least under an obligation to record reasons why it sought to depart from recommendations of Report; especially in matters such as these where leases of considerable commercial value are granted for long periods of time - (C) Whether High Court and Committee were justified in hearing prospective applicants while considering issue of renewal of TISCO's lease? - Held, issues involve considerably high stakes, both in terms of commercial value and effect that such a decision will have on concept of mineral development and consequent national interest - To that extent, those likely to be affected and indeed, those who can legitimately have a stake in proper formulation of such a vital policy, can be heard - (D) Whether Committee was justified in interpreting concept of 'mineral development' u/s. 8(3) of Act as requiring assessment of captive mining requirement of different industries and application of principle of equitable distribution of mining leases? - Held, Committee undertook a comprehensive analysis of submissions made by counsel appearing for various parties before it and also studied relevant material in order to gain an understanding of concept of mineral development u/s. 8(3) of Act - Captive requirement of an industry is a factor that has to be kept in mind while granting leases but it is to be done on a comparative scale - This aspect has been specifically examined by Committee - Committee had correctly interpreted relevant material available for appreciating concept of 'mineral development' and adopting stance that it encompassed concept of captive mining as well as principle of equitable distribution - Appeals dismissed.
