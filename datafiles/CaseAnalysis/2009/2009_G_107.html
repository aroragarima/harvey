State of Orissa v Government of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1256; (2009) 5 SCC 492; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 261; JT 2009 (2) SC 233; 2009(2) SCALE 271; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 992

Case Digest

Subject: Water

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Inter- State Water Dispute (Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Constitution - Water - Inter State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 2(c) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Constitution of Water Disputes Tribunal - Inter-state water dispute - State of Orissa filed present writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution of India, 1950 seeking direction to Government of India to constitute a Water Disputes Tribunal and thereafter, refer to it the dispute between State of Orissa and State of Andhra Pradesh relating to construction of Side Channel Weir and Flood Flow Canal Project at Katragada on river Vansadhara by State of Andhra Pradesh - (A) Whether dispute between State of Orissa and State of Andhra Pradesh constitutes a water dispute within meaning of s. 2(c) of the Act? - Held, dispute between two States does not confine itself to construction of Side Channel Weir and Flood Flow Canal, but primarily it involves unilateral decision taken by State of Andhra Pradesh to divert river waters to State of Andhra Pradesh, which could possibly disturb agreement to share waters of river equally - Such a dispute must be held to be a water dispute within meaning of s. 2(c) (i) of the Act - (B) Grant of interim order during interregnum - Held, unless some interim protection is given till constitution of Water Disputes Tribunal by Central Government, objection raised by State of Orissa will be rendered in fructuous - Notwithstanding powers vested by s. 9 of the Act in Water Disputes Tribunal to be constituted by Central Government u/s. 4 of the Act, which includes power to grant interim order, SC u/art. 32 of Constitution has ample jurisdiction to pass interim orders preserving status quo till a Tribunal is constituted which can then exercise its powers u/s. 9 of the Act - Central Government directed to constitute a Water Disputes Tribunal and to refer to it dispute between State of Orissa and State of Andhra Pradesh - Writ petition allowed.
