Pawan Kumar v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 351; (2017) 7 SCC 780; AIR 2017 SC 2459; JT 2017 (4) SC 555; 2017 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1303; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 777; 2017(5) SCALE 443

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Dying Declaration, Conspiracy, Suicide, Harassment, Omission, Review, Abetment, Treatment, Kerosene, Version, Head Constable, Reliance, Doctor, Girl, Innocence, Innocent, Informant, Dealing, Interfere, Panchayat, Advocate General, Appeal Against Acquittal, Instigation, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 107, 306, 307, 363, 364, 366, 376 - Abetment to suicide - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant/accused allegedly poured kerosene on prosecutrix/deceased who succumbed to injuries - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 306, 363, 366 and 376 IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused of charges - On appeal, DB reversed the judgment of acquittal rendered by Trial Court and convicted accused u/s. 306 IPC and sentenced him to suffer RI for 7 years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether accused could be convicted u/s. 306 IPC.

Held, mere allegation of harassment without any positive action in proximity to the time of occurrence on part of accused that led a person to commit suicide, a conviction in terms of s. 307 IPC was not sustainable. In instant case, accused by his acts and his continuous course of conduct created such a situation as a consequence of which deceased was left with no other option except to commit suicide. The active acts of accused have led deceased to put an end to her life. SC do not find any material on record which compels Court to conclude that victim committing suicide was hypersensitive to ordinary petulance, discord and difference in domestic life quite common to the society to which victim belonged. On the other hand, accused has played active role in tarnishing the self-esteem and self-respect of victim which drove victim to commit suicide. The cruelty meted out to her has, in fact, induced her to extinguish her life-spark. HC has not reversed the judgment of acquittal solely on the basis of dying declaration. It has placed reliance on the evidence of parents and other witnesses. It has also treated the version of Pradhan of Gram Panchayat as credible. All those witnesses have deposed that accused after his acquittal engaged himself in threatening and teasing victim and did not allow her to live in peace.The harassment caused to her had become intolerable and unbearable. As the materials on record would reflect, father could not afford deceased treatment when case of his daughter was referred to hospital. The deplorable depravity of appellant has led to a heart breaking situation for a young girl who was compelled to put an end to her life. Therefore, HC had correctly reversed the judgment of acquittal and imposed the sentence. Appeal dismissed.
