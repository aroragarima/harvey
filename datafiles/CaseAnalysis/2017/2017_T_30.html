Transparency International India and another v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, D.Y. Chandrachud, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 217; (2017) 4 SCC 748; AIR 2017 SC 1333; 2017 CRLJ 2242; JT 2017 (6) SC 203; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 250; 2017(3) SCALE 342

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisdiction, SERVICE, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, Contempt of Courts Act, 1997, Judiciary, Writ Petition, Notice, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1997, s. 15(2) - Ranbir Penal Code, ss. 2, 6, 499, 500, 501 - Authority to initiate contempt proceedings and procure attendance through warrant - Petitioner no.2 conducted study on governance with reference to bureaucracy and functioning of administrative machinery and judiciary and said study report was published by petitioner no.1 - While so, in view of findings recorded in their report with regard to lower judiciary in the State, Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Kangan initiated action against five parties including petitioners and issued notices - Petitioners filed replies to said notices but as petitioners failed to appear before court, impugned order dt.24-8-2006 for procuring their attendance through bailable warrants was passed - Hence, instant writ petition contending that Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Kangan had no authority to direct attendance of petitioners under contempt proceedings - Whether impugned order is beyond the jurisdiction of Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class.

Held, the term 'of a subordinate court', used in s.15(2) of 1997 Act, could contemplate a situation, where the alleged contemptuous action is aimed at more than one court, or a large number of courts, all at once. Under such circumstances, any one of such courts could make a reference to HC, under the provisions of 1997 Act. However, Judicial Magistrate, Kangan had absolutely no jurisdiction or authority to pass the impugned order dt.24-8-2006, whereby, it contemplated to enforce the attendance of the petitioners by way of arrest. Having received the response from the petitioners and petitioners having not entered appearance before Judicial Magistrate, then said Judicial Magistrate ought to have proceeded further with the matter, in consonance with law. And if it was the Court's understanding, that the matter needed to be taken further, either under 1997 Act and/or u/s.2/6 read with ss.499, 500/501 of Ranbir Penal Code, the Court ought to have done so. Hence, Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Kangan is directed to proceed with the matter in furtherance of original show cause notice dt.4-5-2006 and it is open to petitioners to enter their appearance before Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class either in person or through their counsel. The order dt.24-8-2006 is quashed and set aside. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - Where the contemptuous action is of a general nature, and is not aimed at specific judges or courts, any one of such judges or courts, which perceives that the same is aimed at him (or it), would be well within its right, to make a reference of the same to the jurisdictional HC and thereupon, whether cognizance and initiation of contempt proceedings need to be taken, would fall within the realm of HC itself.
