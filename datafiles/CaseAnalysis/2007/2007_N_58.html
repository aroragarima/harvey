National Council For Civil Liberties v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 718; (2007) 6 SCC 506; AIR 2007 SC 2631; JT 2007 (9) SC 201; 2007(9) SCALE 80; 2007 (5) SCJ 507; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 198

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Foreign Contribution (Regulation) Act, 1976, Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888

Summary: Petition for enforcement of the petitioner's fundamental rights under Articles 14 and 21 of the Constitution perpetually on account of arbitrary inaction of the respondents and for protection of a better right to live of the inhabitants of Gujarat, Madhya Pradesh and Rajasthan - Prayer to issue appropriate writ order or direction directing respondent No. 7 to undertake detailed investigation into the affairs of the respondent Nos. 4, 5, 6 and their support groups - Held, there is evidence to show that certain monetary awards had been received jointly by the respondent No.5 and Baba Amte which had been vested in a trust which had no connection with the activities of the respondent No.5 - Materials in the writ petition consist only of vague allegations without any proper foundation - No case has been made for a direction to the CBI to investigate into the allegations - Petition dismissed.
