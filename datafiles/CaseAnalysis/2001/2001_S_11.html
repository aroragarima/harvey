M. N. Damani v S. K. Sinha and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20144; (2001) 5 SCC 156; (2001) SCC (Cr) 823; AIR 2001 SC 2037; AIR 2001 SCW 1941; 2001 (2) CCR 181; 2002 (1) CRJ 746; 2001 CRLJ 2571; 2001 (2) JCC 12; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 375; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 653; 2001(3) SCALE 654; 2001 (4) SCJ 135; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 415; 2001 (3) SLT 765; 2001 (3) Supreme 647; 2001 (2) UC 120

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Corporate - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 406, 407 and 500 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 251, 436, 468, 482 and 499 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Whether the proceedings u/s. 500 IPC, 1860, against the respondent for knowingly having made imputations against the appellant rightly quashed by the High Court at the stage of issuance of summons by the learned magistrate? - Two aspects to be satisfied: (1) whether the uncontroverted allegations, as made in the complaint, prima facie establish the offence and (2) whether it is expedient and in the interest of justice to permit a prosecution to continue? - Assuming that the imputations made could be covered by exception 9 of s. 499 IPC, 1860 - Several questions remain to be examined - Whether such imputations were made in good faith, in what circumstances, with what intention, etc. - These can be examined on the basis of evidence in the trial - Questions may arise for consideration depending on the stand taken by the accused at the trial and how the complainant proposes to demolish the defence and that stage for deciding these questions had not arrived at the stage of issuing process - Held, on a plain reading of the order of the Magistrate issuing summons to the respondents, keeping in view the allegations made in the complaint and sworn statement of the appellant, a prima facie case is made out at that stage - No special features in the case to say that it is not in the interest of justice to permit the prosecution to continue - The High Court committed a manifest error in quashing the criminal proceedings exercising jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 - Appeal allowed.
