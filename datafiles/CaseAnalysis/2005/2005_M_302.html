Punjab and Sind Bank and Others v Mohinder Pal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, S.B. Sinha, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 709; (2005) 12 SCC 747; JT 2005 (10) SC 222; 2005(9) SCALE 97; 2005 (8) SLR 647; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 989

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Leave Encashment

Summary: [A.] Service - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 12 - Punjab and Sind Bank Employee's Voluntary Retirement Scheme, 2000 - Applicant withdrew his offer/application to avail the scheme before same was accepted - Whether applicant herein waived his right? - Held, a person cannot be said to have waived his right unless it is established that his conduct was such so as to enable Court to arrive at a conclusion that he did so with knowledge that he had a right but despite same acted in such a manner which would imply that he has waived same - Conduct of Applicant does not indicate any knowledge about payment in his account or his willful appropriation thereof as to constitute a waiver - Bank is directed to permit Applicant to join his duties - He is entitled to be reinstated in service with all consequential benefits and continuity in service - Application allowed.
