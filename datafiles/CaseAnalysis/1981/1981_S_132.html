New Bihar Biri Leaves Company and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 430; (1981) 1 SCC 537; AIR 1981 SC 679; 1981 (29) BLJR 393; 1981 CRLR 360; 1981 CrLR(SC) 360; 1981(1) SCALE 1; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Bihar Kendu Leaves Act, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Bihar Kendu Leaves Rules, 1972 - Regulation - Violation - Monopoly - Statutory agreement - Petitioners/firms or individuals carrying on trade in Kendu leaves in State of B - Right to pluck and extract Kendu leaves was auctioned by State - State issued 1972 Ordinance to regulate trade and collection and sale through agency of State to registered manufacturers - Petitioners contended that provisions of cls. 4(bb) and 13 amount to unreasonable restriction on fundamental freedom to carry trade u/art. 19(1) (g) of Constitution - Petitioners contended that provisions were in operation of monopoly and violate fundamental rights u/art. 14 of Constitution - Whether provisions of scheme violate fundamental rights u/art. 14 of Constitution and in operation of monopoly - Held, it could not be accepted that provisions of were harsh and unreasonable as they obligate purchasers to pay for undelivered shortfall of Kendu leaves - Scheme could not be supported on ground that it imposed reasonable restrictions, u/art. 19 (6) of fundamental rights of traders to carry on business in Kendu leaves - Action of Govt. was bona fide and could not be effective to challenge - Scheme did not operate to create monopoly in favour of particular class of purchasers nor enable purchasers to make large profits at cost of public revenue or others - Rules framed under Act envisage strict and exclusive control of Govt. over Agents and their activities, and provide for their liability to compensate Govt. for loss occasioned by their misconduct or neglect - Condition 4(bb) inconsistent with and repugnant to S. 9(1), proviso of Act was invalid - Condition 13 of statutory Agreement to pay for undelivered quantity of leaves was valid - Order accordingly.
