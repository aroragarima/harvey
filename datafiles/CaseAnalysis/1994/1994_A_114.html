Ram Autar Shukla v Arvind Shukla and another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1210; (1995) Supp2 SCC 130; 1994(4) SCALE 1088; 1995 (1) SCJ 310; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c) and 13 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 129 - Recourse to fraud deflects course of judicial proceedings - Fabricating order of Court - (A)Whether apology of appellant should be acceptable or is liable to be punished in case where he has forged document? - Unconditional apology should be contrite for acts committed by him - Conditional apology would not be a premium to avoid conviction - Held, thus conditional apology of respondent is rejected - He should be convicted and accordingly convicted u/art. 129 and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for a period of three months - Appeal dismissed.
