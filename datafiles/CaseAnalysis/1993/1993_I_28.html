Jajodia (Overseas) Private Limited v Industrial Development Corporation of Orissa Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 334; (1993) 2 SCC 106; 1993 (1) ARBLR 334; 1993 (1) CCC 110; JT 1993 (2) SC 334; 1993(1) SCALE 135; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 229

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 , ss. 14, 30 and 20 - Setting aside of Award - Grounds upon which an award can be set aside are limited - Held, there are no inconsistencies upon the face of the award as can be characterised as errors that vitiate the award - An award has to be read as a whole and harmoniously - Court should be very circumspect about setting aside an award reached by an arbitrator - Parties have agreed that the disputes that may arise or have arisen between them should be resolved not by a court of law but by arbitration - Appeals disposed of.
