Bank of Baroda v Ghemarbhai Harjibhai Rabari

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 198; (2005) 10 SCC 792; (2005) SCC (L&S) 963; AIR 2005 SC 2799; 2005 (5) AWC 4270; 2005 (105) FLR 383; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 62; JT 2005 (3) SC 312; 2005 (2) LLJ 475; 2005 (2) LLN 671; 2005(3) SCALE 253; 2005 (3) SLR 566; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1671

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Service - Termination of services of respondent - Tribunal directed to reinstate respondent in service at his original post with continuity of service and full back wages - Appeal against this order - Held, workman has established fact that he did work as a driver of car belonging to bank during relevant period which come to more than 240 days of work - No evidence led to establish that vouchers produced by workman were either not genuine or did not pertain to wages paid to workman - Respondent-workman established his claim as held by tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
