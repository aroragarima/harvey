Mrs. Hem Nolini Judah (Since Deceased) and After Her Legal v Mrs. Isolyne Sarojbashini Boseand Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 225; AIR 1962 SC 1471; 1962 ALJ 695; 1962 (2) ILR(All) 683; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 294; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Indian Succession, Act, 1925, s. 213 - Plaintiff-respondent, in appeal to High Court, contended that in view of s. 213 of Indian Succession Act, appellant could not claim any right to house in dispute as will of Dr. Miss Mitter, in favour of her mother was neither probated nor letters of administration were obtained with respect thereto - HC accepted this contention of plaintiff-respondent - (A) Whether HC was not right in accepting said contention of respondent? - Held, as soon as appellant wanted to establish that Mrs. Mitter was legatee of Dr. Miss Mitter and was therefore entitled to whole house she could only do so if Will of Dr. Miss Mitter in favour of Mrs. Mitter had resulted in grant of probate or letters of administration - Admittedly that did not happen and therefore s. 213(1) would be a bar to appellant showing that her mother was full owner of property by virtue of Will made in her favour by Dr. Miss Mitter - (B) Whether suit was barred by res judicata? - Further held, questions of title are not decided in proceedings for grant of probate or letters of administration - Whatever therefore might have happened in those proceedings would not establish title to house either of appellant or of Mrs. Mitter - In these circumstances there can be no question of res judicata as to title to property in dispute - (C) Whether plaintiff-respondent was estopped from contesting title of Mrs. Mitter to property in dispute? - Further held, before Mrs. Bose can be estopped from pleading that Mrs. Mitter was not owner of entire property it must be shown that by her showing house as property of Mrs. Mitter in her application for letters of administration, she intentionally caused or permitted appellant to believe that thing to be true and to act on that belief - Appellant cannot be said to have acted in her turn with respect to this house simply because Mrs. Bose said in her application for letters of administration that house belonged to Mrs. Mitter - After death of Mrs. Mitter three sisters put forward three separate Wills each in her favour and there was no question of one sister acting on any representation made by another - No question of estoppel arises in this case - Appeal dismissed.
