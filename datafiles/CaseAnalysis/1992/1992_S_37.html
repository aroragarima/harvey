Mulakh Raj Etc v Satish Kumar And Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1059; (1992) 3 SCC 43; (1992) SCC (Cr) 482; AIR 1992 SC 1175; 1992 (2) CCR 41; 1992 CRLJ 1529; 1992 CrLR(SC) 423; 1992 (1) HLR 587; JT 1992 (2) SC 554; 1992(1) SCALE 804; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 484

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 300 and 302 - Offence of murder - Death of young lady by strangulation and postmortem burn injuries - (A) Evidence of Doctor conducting autopsy - Reliable and consistent with medical jurisprudence - Inference - (B) Motive - Proof - Absence of - Effect - (C) Evidence - Fact that investigating officer was helpless in collecting evidence from neighbours - If prove innocence of accused - (D) Suspicion that her in-laws might have facilitated accused to screen evidence - Not a substitute for proof - Acquittal of in-laws - Held, (A) Death was homicide and not suicide, husband convicted u/ss. 300 and 201 in view of his unnatural conduct after incident and fact that death took place in his bed room - (B) Does not break link in chain in circumstances connecting accused with crime nor militates against prosecution case - (C) Is not an insurance that accused was innocent - (D) was proper - Appeal allowed.
