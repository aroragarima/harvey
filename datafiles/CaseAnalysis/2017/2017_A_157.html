Assam State Electricity Board and others v Buildworth Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dr. D.Y. Chandrachud, Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 857; AIR 2017 SC 3336; 2017(7) SCALE 297

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Extension Of Time, Contract, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Arbitration Act, 1940, Payment Of Interest, Arbitration, Award Interest, Absence, Interest Act, 1978

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 29 - Arbitral proceedings - Interest awarded - Prohibition - Appellant/Board entered into an agreement with respondent/claimant for installation of circulating Water Piping System - Disputes arose between parties and matter was referred to Arbitration - Arbitrator awarded a sum of Rs 10,73,969/- on account of idling charges of labour and machinery and towards price escalation with interest at 18 per cent p.a. - On appeal, DB upheld the award holding that s. 29 of the Act did not confer jurisdiction on arbitrator to award interest prior to the date of reference - Hence, instant cross appeals - Whether interest could be awarded for pre-reference period under the Act.

Held, arbitrator has duly borne in mind the circumstance that "the claimant also contributed to a certain extent and should share responsibility for causing delay in completion of project". Once the aspect of contributory delay was present to the mind of arbitrator, as is reflected in the reasons in the award, and that was taken into consideration in assessment of damages, award does not fall for interference. While noticing that, HC rejected the contention that claimant had failed to produce evidence that its men and machinery remained idle at the work site. Contract in instant case contains no bar or prohibition against award of interest. However, such provision in contract was correctly held by arbitrator to apply only during scheduled term of contract and not in respect of extended period. Respondent in its initial demands made claims on account of price escalation and submitted a consolidated bill. Claimant was entitled to claim interest. HC was in error in setting aside the award of interest. Therefore, claimant was entitled to interest on the sum awarded from the date of the decree of Trial Court until payment or realisation. The rate of interest was modified to 12 per cent p.a. in respect of both the periods. Appeals disposed of.
