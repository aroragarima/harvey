K. G. Premshankar v Inspector of Police and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1616; (2002) 8 SCC 87; (2003) SCC (Cr) 223; AIR 2002 SC 3372; 2002 (2) AllCriC 920; 2002 (4) CCR 29; 2002 (2) CGLJ 290; 2002 (4) Crimes 261; 2003 (1) CRJ 239; 2002 CRLJ 4343; 2002 (3) JCC 1975; 2003 (1) JIC 206; JT 2002 (7) SC 30; 2002 (3) KLT 389; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 330; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 596; 2002(6) SCALE 371; 2002 (4) SCJ 286; 2002 (5) SLT 318; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 350; 2002 (6) Supreme 313; 2003 (1) UC 211

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure ,1973, ss. 473 and 482 - Indian Penal Code , 1860, ss. 143, 166, 219, 228A, 323, 324, 344, 357 and 420 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 40, 41, 42 and 43 - Protection of Civil Rights Act, ss. 7(1)(d) and 11 - (A) Whether finding recorded by Criminal Court stands superseded by finding recorded by Civil Court? - Held, observation made by Supreme Court in V M Shah's case, 1995 Indlaw SC 321 that finding recorded by Criminal Court stands superseded by finding recorded by Civil Court is not correct enunciation of law - Judgment, order or decree passed in a previous civil proceeding, if relevant, as provided u/ss. 40 and 42 or other provisions of Evidence Act then in each case, court has to decide to what extent it is binding or conclusive with regard to matters decided therein - (B) Whether finding on certain facts by a Civil Court is relevant before criminal court when it is called upon to give a finding on same facts or vice versa ? - Further held, when a party has been able to satisfy a Civil Court as to justice of his claim and has in result succeeded in obtaining a decree which is final and binding upon parties, it would not be open to Criminal Courts to go behind findings of Civil Court is to place latter without any valid reason in a much higher position than what it actually occupies in system of administration in this country and to make it master not only of cases which it is called upon to adjudicate but also of cases which it is not called upon to determine and over which it has really no control - Appeal dismissed.
