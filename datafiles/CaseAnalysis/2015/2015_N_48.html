Nandkishore Lalbhai Mehta v New Era Fabrics Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 480; (2015) 9 SCC 755; AIR 2015 SC 3796; 2015 (113) ALR 243; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 296; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 182; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 257; 2015 (5) CTC 577; JT 2015 (6) SC 267; 2015 (6) MLJ 102; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 98; 2016 (130) RD 217; 2015(7) SCALE 665; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Refusal, Not Found, Development Control Regulations, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 22, 27 - Agreement to sale - Failure to fulfill stipulation - Cancellation of agreement - Respondent Nos. 1 and 2 agreed to sell their respective right, title and interest in property in question in favor of appellant for which agreement was executed - Earnest money was paid by appellant - Further, agreement of sale was subject to permission being obtained u/ss. 22 and 27 of Act - However, Labour Union informed respondents that they were not agreeable to sale of said property which was informed to appellant stating that agreement stood cancelled - Further, appellant waived stipulation of obtaining consent of labour, however, agreement did not materialize - HC passed suit of specific performance in favor of appellant - DB reversed order passed by lower Court and passed order in favor of respondent - Appellant pleaded that at instigation of respondents and in collusion with them, Labour Union has refused to give its permission to sale of mill premises to appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, it was not case set up by appellant that Labour Union had agreed to proposed sale on certain conditions offered by respondents. SC held that in view of settled position of law, fresh pleadings and evidence which is in variation to original pleadings cannot be taken unless pleadings are incorporated by way of amendment of pleadings. SC held that DB was perfectly justified in holding that unless plaint is amended and specific plea is taken that Labour Union had agreed for proposed sale on certain terms and conditions offered by respondents herein, two letters claimed to be written by Labour Union could not have been taken into consideration at all. SC held that two letters in question are contradictory to each other and it is clear that Labour Union categorically refused to give their consent to sale of mill premises. Further, submission that appellant has waived condition regarding taking of consent from labour for proposed sale and, therefore, this could not be ground for cancelling contract is misconceived. SC held that in view of agreement moment labour do not agree to sale contemplated, under terms of contract, respondents were not bound to complete sale. Hence, SC held that agreement for sale could not have been performed and had ceased and appellant is only entitled to refund of amount along with interest @ 18% p.a. stipulated therein. Hence, order passed by DB setting aside order passed by lower Court is justified. Appeals dismissed.
