Ponds India Limited (Merged With H.L. Limited) v Commissioner of Trade Tax, Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1399; (2008) 8 SCC 369; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 664; 2008 DC 610; 2008 (227) E.L.T. 497; JT 2008 (9) SC 94; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 1262; 2008(9) SCALE 277; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 496; [2008] 15 VST 256

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Sales Tax

Keywords: Excise Commissioner, Definition Clause, Government Analyst, Different Rates Of Tax, U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, local Sales Tax Act, Purchase Tax Act

Summary: Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Whether petroleum jelly is a 'drug' or a 'cosmetic' within the meaning of the provisions of U.P. Trade Tax Act, 1948? - Held, Court may not only be required to consider the contents thereof, but also the history of the entry, the purpose for which the product is used, the manner in which it has been dealt with under the relevant statute as also the interpretation thereof by the implementing authorities - Any event having regard to the definition of drugs, any product which prevents a disorder of human function would also come within the purview of drug - Appeals allowed.
