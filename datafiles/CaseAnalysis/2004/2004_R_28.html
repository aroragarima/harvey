Radha Kumar v State of Bihar (Now Jharkhand)

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1780; (2005) 10 SCC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.304-II - First information report - Non-disclosure of name - Acquittal - Appellant fired two shots which hit âXâ/deceased who succumbed to injuries - First information report was lodged by PW 7 against appellant - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court u/s.304 Part II of IPC and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for period of eight years - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction and sentence - Hence instant appeal

Held, occurrence had taken place on 19-3-1993 and witnesses PWs 2,4, 6 were examined in Trial Court after several months of date of occurrence. Witnesses PWs 2,4, 6 have not disclosed complicity of appellant in crime in their statement made before police in as much as they have not even disclosed name of appellant as accused in their statement made before police and for first time in Trial Court after several months they have disclosed complicity of appellant in crime. No reason has assigned by prosecution for non-disclosure of name of appellant before police by these witnesses. Hence it would not be safe to place reliance upon statement of witnesses PWs 2,4,6 made for first time in Trial Court after several months of occurrence without there being any reasonable excuse for not naming accused before police especially when prosecution case has not supported by informant who also claimed to be eyewitness. Prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt and HC was not justified in uphold conviction of appellant. Conviction and sentence of appellant was set aside and he was acquitted of charge. Appeal allowed.

Ratio â Burden of proof is on prosecution to establish guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt and if prosecution fails to discharge the burden then accused would be entitled to benefit of doubt.
