State Of Punjab v Salil Sabhlok & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 102; (2013) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 526; JT 2013 (3) SC 214; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 553; 2013(2) SCALE 573; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 18; 2013 (2) SLR 659

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Quo Warranto, Punjab High Court Rules, Punjab State Public Service Commission (Conditions of Service) Regulations, 1958

Summary: (A) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 316 and 318 - Appointment - Quash of appointment - Maintainability - State Govt. appointed H as Chairman of Public Service Commission (Commission) - Respondent No. 1 filed PIL prayed directing State Govt. to frame regulations governing conditions of service and appointment of chairman and members of Commission as envisaged in art. 318 of Constitution - DB held that even though art. 316 of Constitution did not prescribed any particular procedure for appointment of Chairman of Commission, having regard to purpose and nature of appointment, it could not be assumed that power of appointment need not be regulated by any procedure - DB held that question arises as to how such persons were to be identified and selected for appointment as Chairman of Commission and in instant case, procedure adopted was valid - DB referred matter to Full Bench, which was quashed appointment of H as Chairman of Commission by - Aggrieved, State and H filed instant appeals - Whether HC was right in entertaining the writ petition as a PIL at instance of respondent No. 1 - Held, respondent No.1 filed instant writ petition for espousing cause of general public of State with view to ensured that a person appointed as Chairman of the Commission was a man of ability and integrity so that recruitment to public services in State were from best available talents and were fair and was not influenced by politics and extraneous considerations - However, writ petition was just a service matter in which only aggrieved party had locus to initiate a legal action in Court of law - Writ petition was a matter affecting interest of general public in State and any member of public should espoused cause of general public so long as his bonafides were not in doubt - It was cleared that the order of DB of HC found that art. 316 of Constitution, which provided for appointment of Chairman and other Members of Commission by the Governor, did not prescribed any particular procedure and took view that, having regard to purpose and nature of appointment, it could not be assumed that power of appointment need not be regulated by any procedure - DB of HC was of view that persons to be appointed should have competence and integrity, but how such persons were to be identified and selected should be considered by Full Bench of HC - Division Bench also referred question to larger Bench as to whether procedure adopted in instant case for appointing H as Chairman Commission was valid and if not, what was effect of not following procedure - It was not found that DB of HC having found in its order that irregularities and illegalities pointed out in writ petition against H were unsubstantiated, should not made an academic reference to Full Bench of HC - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226, 316, 318 - Appointment - Quash - Constitutional power - Challenged - Whether selection and appointment of Chairman and Members of Commission by State Govt., HC could not direct the Govt. to exercise its discretion by following a procedure prescribed by HC - Held, under art. 316 of Constitution, Governor of a State had not only express power of appointing Chairman and Members of Commission but also implied powers to lay down procedure for appointment of Chairman and Members of Commission - HC could not u/art. 226 of Constitution usurp this constitutional power of Govt. and laid down procedure for appointment of Chairman and other Members of Commission - Full Bench of HC, could not have laid down procedure for appointment of Chairman and Members of Commission by impugned judgment - Full Bench of HC, in its judgment acted beyond its jurisdiction and had usurped Constitutional power of Governor in laying down procedure for appointment of Chairman and Members of Commission - Full Bench of HC judgment was set aside and remanded matter to HC for disposal of writ petition - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 316, 318 and 320 - Appointment - Quash of appointment - Extra-ordinary powers by HC - Challenged - Whether Full Bench of HC could hold the selection and appointment of H as Chairman of commission was not in accordance with the Constitution - Held, art. 316 did not specify the qualities of Chairman of Commission and qualities were amongst implied relevant factors which had taken into consideration by Govt. while determining competency of person to be selected and appointed as Chairman of Commission under art. 316 of Constitution - However, if these relevant factors were not taken into consideration by State Govt. while selecting and appointing Chairman of Commission, Court could hold selection and appointment as not in accordance with Constitution - HC should not normally, in exercise of its power u/art. 226 of Constitution, interfere with discretion of State Govt. in selecting and appointing Chairman of Commission, but in an exceptional case if it was shown that relevant factors implied from very nature of duties entrusted to Commission's u/art. 320 of Constitution had not considered by State Govt. in selecting and appointing the Chairman of Commission - HC could invoke its wide and extra-ordinary powers u/art. 226 of Constitution and quash selection and appointment to ensure that discretion of State Govt. was exercised within bounds of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.

(D) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 320 - Appointment - Quash - Non-consideration of factors - Challenged - Whether Full Bench of HC was justified in quashing the appointment of H as Chairman of Commission - Held, DB of HC held that irregularities and illegalities alleged against H had not substantiated - Decision of State Govt. to appoint H as Chairman of Commission was thus invalid for non-consideration of relevant factors implied from very nature of duties entrusted to Commissions u/art. 320 of Constitution - It was not inclined to interfere with impugned order of DB of HC quashing the selection and appointment of H as Chairman of Commission - But set aside the judgment of Full Bench of HC, Considering, fact that State Govt. had already selected and appointed S as Chairman of Commission - Appeal dismissed.
