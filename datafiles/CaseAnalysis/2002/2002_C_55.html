Alembic Glass Industries Ltd v Collector Of Central Excise & Cus

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1357; (2002) 9 SCC 463; [2002] 112 Comp Cas 379; 2002 (83) ECC 288; 2002 (104) ECR 308; 2002 (143) E.L.T. 244; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 459; 2002 (51) RLT 384; 2002 (7) Supreme 353

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4 - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether assessee company and purchaser company could be considered 'related persons' because they held shares in each other and Chairman and three Directors were common? - Shareholders of a Public Ltd. Co. do not, by reason only of their shareholding, have an interest in business of Company - Fact that two Public Ltd. Companies have common Directors does not mean that one company has an interest in business of other - Held, it is not possible to uphold conclusion of Tribunal that assessee and Chemical Co. were related persons - Appeal allowed.
