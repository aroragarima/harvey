Shriomani Gurudwara Prabandhak Committee, Amritsar v Shri Som Nath Dass & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 617; (2000) 4 SCC 146; AIR 2000 SC 1421; 2000 (2) CLT 174; 2000 (160) CTR 61; JT 2000 (4) SC 30; 2000 (125) PLR 826; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 609B; 2000(2) SCALE 663; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 705; 2000 (3) SLT 476; 2000 (3) Supreme 302

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Land & Property -- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - The Sikh Gurdwaras and Shrines Act ,1922 (VI of 1922), ss. 18(1)(a), 18(1)(d) - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925 (Punjab Act No. 8 of 1925) ss. 7(1), 8, 10 - Jurisdic person - Challenged - Validity - About 56 persons moved petition u/s. 7(1) for declartion that disputed property was Sikh Gurdwara - State Govt. by Notification no. 1702 G.P. dt.14-9-1962 published aforesaid petition in Gazette including boundaries of said gurdwaras which were to be declared as Sikh Gurdwaras - Challenged by respondents by filing composite petition u/ss. 8 and 10 and claimed it as dharamshala and Dera of Udasian and received said Dera in succession in accordance with their ancestral share - Petition forwarded by State Govt. to Tribunal - Appellant claimed it to be Sikh Gurdwara having been established by Sikhs for their worship wherein Guru Granth Sahib was only object of worship and sole owner of gurdwara property and denied respondents plea and further challenged locus standi of respondent to file this objection to notification - Tribunal rejected respondents petition holding that respondent had no locus standi u/s. 8 of the 1925 Act - Aggrieved respondents filed first appeal and was dismissed by HC and became final - Tribunal took second petition of respondent u/s. 10 of the 1925 Act - Notification u/s. 9 of the 1925 Act was published declaring disputed gurudwara to be sikh gurudwara - Appellants submitted that disputed land and buildings were properties of Gurdwara Sahib Dharamshala Guru Granth Sahib and respondents and their predecessors along with their family members had all along been its managers without any personal rights in it - Tribunal rejected respondents petition holding infavour of appellant - Aggrieved respondents filed first appeal that Guru Granth Sahib was not jurisdic person - Appellants also filed suit against respondents and was decreed by Trial Court - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal in HC - HC tried both appeals together and DB by majority view held that Guru Granth Sahib was not jurisdic person and allowed appeals filed by respondents - Hence, instant appeals - Whether finding of HC that Guru Granth Sahib was not jurisdic person was justified - Held, juristic person was conferred with rights and obligations in law just like natural person - Guardian would be appointed for minor accordingly jurisdic person for charitable institutions like church and hospital and gurudwara etc. - When donor endows for idol or for mosque or for any institution it necessitates creation of juristic person - SC had delivered direct ruling on this issue in Pritam Dass Mahant Vs. Shiromani Gurdwara Prabandhak Committee,1983 Indlaw SC 285 with reference to case under the 1925 Actheld that central body of worship in Gurdwara was its holy book Guru Granth Sahib and it was Juristic entity - SC in Sarangadeva Periya Matam & Anr. Vs. Ramaswami Goundar (dead) by LR's, 1965 Indlaw SC 419 held that Mutt was juristic person and could own acquire or possess any property -- SC in Ram Jankijee Deities and Ors. Vs. State of Bihar and Ors., 1999 Indlaw SC 813 held that existence of two separate juristic persons was valid - Based on above decisions SC held that Guru Granth Sahib was jurisdic person and found error with HC finding that in absence of manager could not be treated as jurisdic person - Mutation in revenue records in name of Guru Granth Sahib was done in 1928 and was not objected or disputed by respondents ancestors and now after 40 years respondents filed objections u/ss. 8, 10 of the 1925 Act was not acceptable in law - Disputed property was given in trust to respondents ancestors for specified purpose which was not being performed by respondents - Settled law was that once property given to endowment at later stage could not be reverted even to donor and so claim of respondents over suit property fails and was rejected - HC had committed serious mistake of law in holding that Guru Granth Sahib was not juristic person and hence Orders of HC were set aside - Appeals allowed.
