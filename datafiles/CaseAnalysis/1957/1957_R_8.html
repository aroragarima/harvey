Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, Calcutta v Raja Benoy Kumar Sahas Roy

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 165; AIR 1957 SC 768; [1957] 32 ITR 466; 1957 SCJ 740; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 101

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(viii)and 2(1) - Constitution Of India,1950, Entries 14, 19 and 46 of List II of sch. 7 and art. 366(1) - Devolution Rules made under Govt. of India Act, 1919 - Whether income from sale of jungle produce of spontaneous growth is agriculture income? - Statement of case as submitted by ITAT established that forestry operations were carried on by assessee which involved expenditure of human skill and labour - ITAT also found that portions of forest has from time to time been denuded and assess had planted fresh trees in those areas - Whole of income derived from such forest cannot be treated as non-agricultural income - Appellant had not attempted to establish which portion of income is attributable to forest of spontaneous growth - Held, substantial portion of income must had been derived from trees planted by assessee - Appeal dismissed.
