Â Ghulam Sarwar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 141; AIR 1967 SC 1335; 1967 CRLJ 1204; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 271

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners

Keywords: National, Supreme Court Rules 1950

Summary: Foreigners - Constitution - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Customs Act, 1962 - Foreigners Act, 1946, s. 3 - Constitution, 1950, art. 32, 14 and 359 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950 - (A) Whether order of High Court in dismissing Writ of Habeas Corpus operated as Res Judicata before Supreme Court? - Before HC petitioner did not question constitutional validity of President's order made under art. 359 of Constitution - If doctrine of constructive res judicata be applied Supreme Court, though it is enjoined by Constitution to protect right of a person illegally detained, will become powerless to do so - That would be whittling down wide sweep of constitutional protection - Held, that HC made in petition for habeas corpus filed by petitioner does not operate as res judicata and Supreme Court will have to decide petition on merits - (B) Whether s. 3 (2) of Foreigner's Act is ultra vires? - Further held, there is nothing in art. 359 which prevents President from restricting scope of order to a class of persons, provided operation of order is confined to an area and to a period - Impugned orders apply to entire country and fact that only persons who are affected by that order could not move Court for enforcement of their right, cannot make them any less valid orders - Further, compared to foreigners, nationals, with some unfortunate exceptions, can be relied upon to support country's integrity and security - Therefore, a clear nexus between classification of foreigners and citizens and object sought to be achieved thereby - S. 3 does not violate art 14 and is not in excess of power granted under art. 359 - Petition dismissed.
