Anil Joshi and others v State of Himachal Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 168; 2015 (146) FLR 741; JT 2015 (3) SC 134; 2015(3) SCALE 256; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 351; 2015 (3) SLR 678

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Himachal Pradesh Forest Service (Class-II) Rules

Summary: Service - Himachal Pradesh Forest Service (Class-II) Rules - Entitlement to Post - Plea of Promissory Estoppel - Respondents are State employees working in Forest Department and were appointed during 1989-1990 and accordingly posted as 'Range Officers' in Forest Department and their services are governed by Rules - Respondents, however, claimed that they having qualified State Forest Service Course (Diploma Course) from different colleges were eligible to be posted as ACF (Assistant Conservator of Forest) and accordingly were eligible for being treated as direct recruits - Further, they claimed that letter dt. 28-7-1983 was sent by Director of Forest Education promising same by State and since State declined to grant reliefs, they filed application by Tribunal - Tribunal vide judgment held that letter in question does not give any right to respondents to claim reliefs. Further, no case of promissory estoppel was made out in their favour on strength of letter in question - However, HC vide impugned order, claim of respondent was allowed and order passed by Tribunal was quashed - Further, it was held that case of promissory estoppel is made out against State - Whether HC was justified in allowing petitions by granting declaration in favour of respondent employees.

Held, no case of promissory estoppel was made out against State. Further, service conditions of State employee are governed by Statutory Rules framed by State from time to time, hence, employee is entitled to enforce his statutory right recognized in Rules in relation to his service condition if it is breached due to any action on part of State. SC held that plea of promissory estoppel can be set up by person against State only when he is able to prove with adequate evidence that State has promised him in writing in express terms to grant specific benefit and acting upon such promise he has altered his position. In such situation, State cannot be allowed to go back to promise made to such person and he can enforce promise made to him. SC held that respondents were not able to show any Rule, which enabled them to claim relief of nature for which application was filed. Further, perusal of letter in question showed that it only provided that Forest Rangers, who passed Ranger Course with Honours, were considered eligible to secure admission to 2nd year of State Forest Services Course (Diploma Course) in Forestry being conducted at State Forest Service Colleges and such deserving Forest Rangers if found suitable, could be considered for admission in State Forest Services Course, hence, letter in question could not be construed as being in nature of promise made by State to respondents, so as to enable them to seek its enforcement on plea of promissory estoppel. Further, letters were exchanged between one State Authority to other and not addressed to respondents and no enforceable right of nature in question was created in respondents' favour on strength of these letters. Hence, impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Plea of promissory estoppel can be entertained only when same is proved with adequate evidence in writing in express terms to grant specific benefit and same has not been granted to claimants.
