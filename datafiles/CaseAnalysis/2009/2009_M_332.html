Inderchand Jain (D) Through L.Rs. v Motilal (D) Through L.Rs

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 914; (2009) 14 SCC 663; 2009 (76) ALR 782; 2010 (2) AWC 1651; 2009 (5) CTC 365; JT 2009 (9) SC 552; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 743; 2009 (108) RD 299; 2009(9) SCALE 777; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 252

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Review Application, Limitations

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Review of Order - Scope - Review court does not sit in appeal over its own order - Re-hearing is impermissible in review - Once a judgment signed or pronounced, it should not be altered by way of review - Inherent jurisdiction cannot be invoked in reviewing the order.P /&gt;(B) Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16 (c) - Specific performance of contract - Discretionary reliefs - Relief of specific performance can be granted only in the event of plaintiff not only makes in necessary pleadings but also established that he had all along been ready and willing to perform his contract - Such readiness and willingness should be continued at all the stages of suit.P /&gt;(C) Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 114, O. 47 r. 1 - Suit for specific performance - Appellant-defendant entered into agreement for sale with respondent-plaintiff and received advance - Failure to perform on the part of defendant - Trail Court decreed in favor of plaintiff - In the intra court appeal, HC passed order in favor of plaintiff - Review petitions filed by both the parties and HC listed the matter for re-hearing - Legality - Held, readiness and willingness on the part of plaintiff was conditional, however, he deposited the entire balance amount - Defendant's 3rd stay application rejected by the HC with direction to execute the sale deed in favor of plaintiff - Plaintiff raised the plea of novation of contract - Plaintiff's readiness and willingness to perform conditioned by the consideration of his plea of novation by court - However, no conditional offer permissible in specific performance of contract - Moreover, plea of novation of contract had never raised before appellate court - Re-appreciation of evidence not permissible in review - Review filed by the plaintiff to adjust the amount he spend for renovation with the original price, not sustainable - However, plaintiff could recover such amount by filing appropriate application - Impugned order of review, set aside - Appeal allowed.
