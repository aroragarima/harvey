P. V. Anvar v P. K. Basheer and others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 630; (2014) 10 SCC 473; AIR 2015 SC 180; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 203; 2014(6) ALL MR 951; 2015 (111) ALR 811; 2015 (1) AWC 156; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 1; 2017 (352) E.L.T. 416; JT 2014 (10) SC 459; 2015 (1) KarLJ 547; 2014 (4) KLT 104; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4558; 2015 (2) MahLJ 135; 2015 (1) MPLJ 507; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 504; 2015 (129) RD 112; 2014(10) SCALE 660; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 399

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Youth Justice and Criminal Evidence Act, 1999

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(b), 100(1)(d)(ii), 123(2), 123(2)(ii), 123(4) - Corrupt practices - Election - Legality - Appellant and respondent no. 1 contested election and respondent no. 1 was elected - Appellant filed petition before HC and sought to set aside election - HC dismissed election petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC id justified -

Held, there is no evidence at all to prove that Exb.P1 leaflet was printed at instance of respondent no. 1. One 'H', who placed order for printing of Exb.P1 is not examined. 'Z', who is said to be Manager of Press at relevant time, was not examined. 'M', who is said to have told appellant that expenses for printing of Exb.P1 were borne by respondent no.1 and same have been shown in electoral return of respondent no. 1 is also not examined. No evidence of electoral returns pertaining to expenditure on printing of Exb.P1 by respondent no. 1 is available. It is unsafe if not difficult to connect respondent no. 1 with distribution of Exb.P1, even assuming that allegation on distribution of Exb.P1 at various places is true. It was not necessary to deal with aspect of oral evidence since main allegation of corrupt practice is of publication of Exb.P1 leaflet apart from other evidence based on CDs. Since there is no reliable evidence to reach the irresistible inference that Exb.P1 leaflet was published with consent of respondent no. 1 or his election agent, election cannot be set aside on ground of corrupt practice u/s. 123(4) of the Act - The ground of undue influence u/s. 123(2) of the Act has been given up, so also ground on publication of flex boards. Regard to admissible evidence available on record, though for different reasons, SC find it extremely difficult to hold that appellant has founded and proved corrupt practice u/s. 100(1)(b) r/w. s. 123(4) of the Act against respondent no. 1. Appeal dismissed.
