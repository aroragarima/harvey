Vijay Thakur v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 634; (2014) 14 SCC 609; (2014) 14 SCC 619; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4159; 2014 CRLJ 4906; JT 2014 (11) SC 349; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4052; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 389; 2014(11) SCALE 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 392, 34 - Robbery - Murder - Life imprisonment - Sustainability - Appellants were charged and convicted u/ss. 302, 392 r/w s. 34 IPC - On appeal filed by appellants, HC affirmed the decision of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal -

Held, except the so-called recoveries, there is no other circumstances worth the name which has been proved against these two appellants. It is a case of blind murder. There are no eyewitnesses. Conviction is based on the circumstantial evidence. In such a case, complete chain of events has to be established pointing out the culpability of the accused person. The chain should be such that no other conclusion, except the guilt of the accused person, is discernible without any doubt. Insofar as these two appellants are concerned, there is no circumstance attributed except that they were with RT till S and the alleged disclosure leading to recoveries, which appears to be doubtful. When we look into all these facts in entirety in the aforesaid context, we find that not only the chain of events is incomplete, it becomes somewhat difficult to convict the appellant only on the basis of the aforesaid recoveries. Therefore, SC have no hesitation in allowing these appeals and setting aside the conviction and sentence of the two appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC. Appeals allowed.
