K. N. Beena v Muniyappan and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19809; (2001) 8 SCC 458; (2002) SCC (Cr) 14; AIR 2001 SC 2895; 2002 (1) AllCJ 635; 2002 (1) AllCriR 247; 2002 (1) AWC 110; 2006 (4) BC 287; 2002 (1) Bom.C.R. 342; 2002 (1) CGLJ 62; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 459; 2001 (4) Crimes 376; 2002 (2) CRJ 76; 2001 CRLJ 4745; 2001 (2) JCC 305; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 83; JT 2001 (9) SC 228; 2001 (3) KLT 950; 2002 (130) PLR 221; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 545; 2001(7) SCALE 331; 2001 (7) SLT 387; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 374; 2001 (7) Supreme 810; 2002 (1) UC 351

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 118, 138 and 139 - Whether conviction can be set aside on ground that complainant failed to prove if cheque been issued for any debt or liability? - U/s. 118, unless proved contrary, it is to be presumed that negotiable instrument been drawn for consideration - Also, u/s. 139 it is to be presumed, unless proved contrary, that holder of cheque received cheque for discharge, in whole or in part, of a debt or liability - Thus, in complaint u/s. 138, court to presume that cheque been issued for a debt or liability - This presumption is rebuttable - Burden to prove contrary is on accused by leading cogent evidence during trial - Held, in this case no such evidence let by accused - Accused, thus, did not discharged his burden - High Court therefore, erred in setting aside conviction - Sentence modified - Order accordingly.
