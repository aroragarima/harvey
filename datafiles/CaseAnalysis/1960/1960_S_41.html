Â Radhakisan Laxminarayan Toshniwal v Shridhar Ramchandra Alshi and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 1960 SC 1368; 2001 (1) KLT 415; 1960 MPLJ 1124; 1961 (1) SCJ 215; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 248

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Registration Act, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882,s. 54 - Right of preemption - (A) Whether a right of pre-emption had accrued to respondent Sridhar under provisions of Code? - Held, u/s. 54 of Transfer of Property Act, a contract for sale does not of itself create any interest in or charge on immoveable property and consequently contract in instant case created no interest in favour of vendee and proprietary title did not validly pass from vendors to vendee and until that was completed no right to enforce pre-emption arose - Wherever Transfer of Property Act is in force Mohammedan Law or any other personal law is inapplicable to transfers and no title passes except in accordance with that Act - Therefore when suit was brought there was no transfer by way of sale which could be subject to preemption - (B) Whether appellant was guilty of fraud in that in order to defeat right of preemptors a deed of sale was not executed and for all intents and purposes appellant had become owner of property and that conduct such as this would defeat very law of preemption? - Held, right to pre-empt sale is not exercisable till a pre-emptible transfer has been effected and right of pre-emption is not one which is looked upon with great favour by courts presumably for reason that it is in derogation of right of owner to alienate his property - It is neither illegal nor fraudulent for parties to a transfer to avoid and defeat a claim for pre-emption by all legitimate means - Appeal allowed.
