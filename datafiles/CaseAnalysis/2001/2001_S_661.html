Bipin Shantilal Panchal v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 412; (2001) 3 SCC 1; (2001) SCC (Cr) 417; AIR 2001 SC 1158; 2001 (1) CCR 278; 2001 (1) CGLJ 366; 2001 (1) Crimes 288; 2001 CRLJ 1254; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 611; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2024; 2001 (1) JCC 269; JT 2001 (3) SC 120; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 859; 2001(2) SCALE 167; 2001 (1) SCJ 460; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 29; 2001 (2) SLT 311; 2001 (2) Supreme 65; 2001 (1) UC 471

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Admissibility, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 268 and 309 - Bail procedure for an under-trial prisoner - (A) Accused in jail as under-trial for a record period of more than seven years - Insensitivity of Trial Court towards trial procedure - Application for extension of time for further period - Not allowed - Held, Trial Judge should have shown more sensitivity - Adopt all measures to accelerate trial procedure in order to finish it within time frame - (B) Procedure to be followed by Trial Courts whenever an objection is raised regarding admissibility of any material or any item of oral evidence - Further held, an archaic practice - Can be substituted - Whenever an objection is raised during evidence taking stage regarding admissibility of any material or item of oral evidence Court can make a note of such objection and mark objected document tentatively as an exhibit in case subject to such objections to be decided at last stage in final judgment - If Court finds at final stage that objection so raised is sustainable Judge or Magistrate can keep such evidence excluded from consideration - This practice will have benefits - First, time in Trial Court, during evidence taking stage, would not be wasted on account of raising such objections and court can continue to examine witnesses - Second, Superior Court, when same objection is re-canvassed and reconsidered in appeal or revision against final judgment of Trial Court, can determine correctness of view taken by Trial Court regarding that objection, without bothering to remit case to Trial Court again for fresh disposal - This measure would not cause any prejudice to parties to litigation and would not add to their misery or expenses - Made as procedure to be followed by Courts - Application disposed of.
