Tamil Nadu Housing Board v A. Viswam (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3359; (1996) 8 SCC 259; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 360; AIR 1996 SC 3377; 1996 (1) CCC 272; 1996 (2) CLT 138; JT 1996 (2) SC 549; 1996 (4) KarLJ 700; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 24; 1996 (1) RentLR 305; 1996(2) SCALE 418; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 402; 1996 (2) Supreme 385

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, r. 2 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 16 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114(e) - (A) Injunction against Housing Board acquiring land for public purpose - Injunction suit filed by landowner of small part of acquired land - Land Acquisition Officer took possession of whole land and delivered it to Housing Board - Non impleadment of Land Acquisition Officer as party defendant to suit - Whether presumption u/s. 114(e) of Evidence Act is attracted? - Whether land-owner is in possession of land? - Can injunction be issued against Board in whom land stood vested? - (B) Acquisition of land - Mode of taking possession - Recording of a memorandum or panchnama by Land Acquisition Officer in presence of witnesses signed by them - Whether would constitute taking possession? - Held, (A) Presumption attracted - Cannot be said to be in possession - Cannot be issued - (B) Would constitute taking possession of Land - Appeal allowed.
