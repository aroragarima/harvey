Nagappa v Gurudayal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1470; (2003) 2 SCC 274; (2003) SCC (Cr) 523; 2002 (3) ACC 766; AIR 2003 SC 674; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2003 (50) ALR 176; 2003 (1) AWC 694; 2003 (1) CGLJ 273; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 897; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 120; JT 2002 (10) SC 144; 2003 (1) KLT 115; 2003 (1) LCD 46; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 258; 2002(9) SCALE 37; 2002 (7) SLT 101; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 499; 2002 (8) Supreme 497

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Quantum Of Damages, Employer's Liability

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether one time payment of compensation to a poor agriculturist would be sufficient to meet the future medical expenses? - It is true that lump-sum compensation contemplating future eventualities can be granted but at the same time, is it permissible under the Act to grant recurring medical expenses to such a victim? - Secondly, whether amendment to the claim petition could be granted at the appellate stage? - Held, under the provisions of the Act, there is no restriction that Tribunal/Court cannot award compensation amount exceeding the claimed amount - Function of the Tribunal/Court is to award 'just' compensation, which is reasonable on the basis of evidence produced on record - Further, in such cases there is no question of claim becoming time barred or it cannot be contended that by enhancing the claim there would be change of cause of action - However, the Act does not provide for passing of further award after final award is passed Hence, only alternative is that at the time of passing of final award, Tribunal/Court should consider future medical expenses and fix compensation accordingly - Appeal allowed accordingly.
