S. H. Rangappa v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19943; (2002) 1 SCC 538; AIR 2001 SC 3863; 2001 (45) ALR 628; 2001 (4) AWC 3176; JT 2001 (8) SC 545; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 105; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 629B; 2001(7) SCALE 219; 2001 (7) SLT 286; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 545; 2001 (7) Supreme 687; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2449

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Prohibition Act - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 (1)(2) - Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967 - Whether notification u/s. 6(2) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 should be published within period prescribed by proviso to s. 6(1) of said Act? - Held, u/s. 6(1), it is only a declaration which is required to be made, time-limit being within one year of publication of s. 4 notification - S. 6 (2) does not prescribe any time-limit within which declaration made u/s. 6(1) is to be published - Whereas time-limit for making of an order is provided u/s. 6(1), legislature advisedly did not provide for any time-limit in respect of steps required to be taken u/s. 6 (2) - Observations of this Court in Khadim Hussain case ((1976) 1 SCC 8431976 (1) SCC 8431975 Indlaw SC 308) state that s. 4(2) of Amendment Act of 1967, itself makes a distinction between a 'declaration' u/s. 6 and its 'notification' u/s. 4 of principal Act - It does not say that no notification u/s. 6 of principal Act can take place beyond time fixed - Prohibition is confined to declarations made beyond specified period - Appeals dismissed.
