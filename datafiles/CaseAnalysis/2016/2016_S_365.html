State of Punjab and others v Jagjit Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 738; (2017) 1 SCC 148; AIR 2016 SC 5176; JT 2016 (10) SC 434; 2016 (4) LLJ 513; 2016(10) SCALE 447; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Labour & Industrial Law, Tribunal, Welfare, Dearness Allowance, University, Equal pay for equal work, Jurisdiction, Health, Review, Agriculture, Minimum Wages, Age, Regularisation, Research, Municipal, Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, Junior, Grievance, Labour, Parity, Equality, Carpenter, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Service - Temporary employees - Equal Pay for Equal Work - In appeal no.337/2003, DB of HC held that temporary employees were not entitled to minimum of pay-scale, as was being paid to similarly placed regular employees - However, another DB of HC in appeal no.1024/2009, held that temporary employees were entitled to minimum of pay-scale along with permissible allowances which were being given to similarly placed regular employees - Hence, as there was conflict of views expressed in judgments of two division benches of HC, matter was referred to full bench of HC and full bench rendered judgment vide its orders dt.11-11-2011 - Hence, instant appeals challenging judgments rendered by both DBs of HC and judgment of Full Bench of HC - Whether temporarily engaged employees (daily-wage employees, ad-hoc appointees, employees appointed on casual basis, contractual employees etc.,) are entitled to minimum of the regular pay scale, along with DA.

Held, it was acknowledged by State that all temporary employees in instant bunch of appeals, were appointed against posts which were also available in regular cadre/establishment. It was also accepted, that during the course of their employment, the concerned temporary employees were being randomly deputed to discharge duties and responsibilities, which were assigned to regular employees. Further, regular employees holding substantive posts, were also posted to discharge the same work, which was assigned to temporary employees, from time to time. Further, duties and responsibilities discharged by temporary employees in instant appeals, were the same as were being discharged by regular employees. Further, it was not submitted by appellants, that respondent-employees were not possessing qualifications prescribed for appointment on regular basis or that any of temporary employees would not be entitled to pay parity. Hence, principle of 'equal pay for equal work' would be applicable to all concerned temporary employees, so as to vest in them the right to claim wages, at par with the minimum of pay-scale of regularly engaged Government employees holding the same post. Hence, all concerned temporary employees are entitled to draw wages at minimum of the pay-scale, extended to regular employees, holding the same post. Further, decision of Full Bench and judgment of DB in appeal no.337/2003 are set aside and decision of DB in appeal no.1024 is affirmed with modification that concerned employees were entitled to minimum of pay-scale of the category to which they belong, but are not entitled to allowances attached to the posts held by them. Appeals disposed of.
