Inder Singh v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1031; (1994) 6 SCC 275; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1653; AIR 1995 SC 312; AIR 1995 SCW 1545; 1995 (1) CCR 59; 1994 (3) Crimes 495; JT 1994 (6) SC 146; 1994(4) SCALE 129; 1995 (1) SCJ 343; 1995 (4) SLR 627; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Habeas Corpus petition - Police officers abducting seven persons by using official machinery - Complaint regarding above found true by Director General of Police (Crimes) upon enquiry - No disciplinary action taken yet against delinquent police officers - Not brought into notice of Director General of Police till filing of writ petition - Held, direction to Central Bureau of Investigation to conduct enquiry and submit report - Order accordingly
