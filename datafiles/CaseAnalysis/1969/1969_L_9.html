Lala Ram v Hari Ram

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 214; (1969) 3 SCC 173; (1970) SCC (Cr) 25; AIR 1970 SC 1093; 1970 CRLJ 1014; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963, s. 12(2) - CrPC, 1898, s. 417(4) - Constitution, art. 136 - IPC, 1860, s. 99 - (A) Limitation - Application for special leave u/s. 417(3) - Provision of s. 417(4), CrPC, 1898 itself prescribes a period of 60 days limitation - Period u/s. 12(2) of 1963 Act - Legislature could provide for such period in Code itself - Insertion of separate article in Limitation Act - (B) Criminal Procedure - 'Shall be entertained' u/s. 417(4) of the Act - Word 'entertain' means 'file or received by the Court' - It has no reference to actual hearing of application for special leave to appeal - (C) Constitution, art. 136 - High Court holding that Magistrate was not entitled to reject evidence of independent eye-witnesses - Interference, if - (D) When right of private defence u/s. 99 of IPC, 1860 not available - Insertion held, was not necessary - It has no reference to actual hearing of application for special leave - No interference with finding of fact by SC in absence of any reason being shown - No right of private defence when there is ample opportunity of having recourse to authorities - Appeal Dismissed.
