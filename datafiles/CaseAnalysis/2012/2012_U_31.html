Dimpey Gujral W/O.Vivek Gujral & Ors v Union Territory Through Administratator, U.T. Chandigarh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 518; (2013) 11 SCC 497; AIR 2013 SC 518; 2013 CRLJ 520; 2012 (6) CTC 829; JT 2012 (12) SC 80; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 745; 2012(11) SCALE 589

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 406, 173 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 323, 307, 452 and 506 - Quashing of proceedings - FIR - Maintainability - Petitioners and complainant are educated and respectable citizens, who enjoy high social status - Certain unfortunate incidents relating to pet dogs of petitioners have dragged them to instant Court - Those incidents took ugly turn which resulted in the lodging of FIR u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 323, 307, 452 and 506 of IPC - Since the offences involved in instant case were of a personal nature and were not offences against the society, Court enquired with counsel appearing for the parties whether there was any possibility of a settlement - Due to efforts made by counsel, parties have seen reason and have entered into a compromise - Petitioners filed instant application for quashing of the said FIR and all consequential proceedings arising there from including the final report presented u/s. 173 of the Code and charges framed by the Trial Court - Whether since one of the offences alleged in the FIR was non-compoundable, the FIR could be quashed - Held, in certain decisions of instant Court in view of the settlement arrived at by the parties, SC quashed the FIRs though some of the offences were non-compoundable - Instant case where the continuation of criminal proceedings would tantamount to abuse of process of law because the alleged offences were not heinous offences showing extreme depravity nor were they against the society - They were offences of a personal nature and burying them would bring about peace and amity between the two sides - FIR and all consequential proceedings arose there from including the final report presented u/s. 173 of the Code and charges framed by the Trial Court were quashed - Petition disposed of.
