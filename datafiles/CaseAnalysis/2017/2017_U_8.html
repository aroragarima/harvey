U. Manjunath Rao v U. Chandrashekar and another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 589; JT 2017 (7) SC 498; 2017 (6) MLJ 489

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Joint Family, Letters Patent, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 31, s. 96 - Suit for possession - Decreed - Challenged - Plaintiff filed suit for injunction restraining defendants from interfering with the possession of property in question - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeal, Single Judge dismissed appeal holding that no infirmities in the findings recorded by Trial Court and certain documents brought on record showed that plaintiff was in possession of property in question - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, Appellate Court has a defined role and its judgment should show application of mind and reflect the reasons on the basis of which it agrees with Trial Court. There has to be an "expression of opinion" in the proper sense of the said phrase. It cannot be said that mere concurrence meets the requirement of law. It was one thing to state that the appeal is without any substance and it is another thing to elucidate, analyse and arrive at the conclusion that appeal was devoid of merit. In instant case, Single Judge has not ascribed any reason. There has been no analysis of facts or law. There was no discussion with regard to the points urged. While agreeing with the general approval of reasons to support the conclusions of the judgment in appeal, HC has to keep in view the language employed in O. 41 r. 31 CPC and the view expressed in Santosh Hazari v. Purushottam Tiwari. (Deceased By Legal Representatives), 2001 Indlaw SC 266. Analysis and reason are to be manifest. When that was not done, judgment of HC becomes indefensible. Impugned judgment and decree passed by HC was set aside and remit the matter for fresh disposal in accordance with law within 6 months. Appeal allowed.
