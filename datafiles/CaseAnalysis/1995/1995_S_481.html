R.Balakrishna Pillai v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 714; (1996) SCC (Cr) 128; (1996) 1 SCC 478; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 769; AIR 1996 SC 901; AIR 1996 SCW 293; 1996 (1) CCR 77; 1995 (4) Crimes 704; JT 1995 (9) SC 580; 1996 (1) KarLJ 112; 1996 (1) KLT 250; 1996 (1) LLN 289; 1995(7) SCALE 255; 1996 (1) SCJ 40; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.197(1) - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) r/w. s. 5(1) - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Criminal Conspiracy - Public Servant - Sanction for Prosecution - Discharge of Official Duties - Appellants/Accused/Public Servants entered into criminal conspiracy and illegally sold electricity units to Respondent/State without consent of Govt., under Act, 1948 and against State Electricity Board Rules - Appellants caused resultant profit to private party and committed an offence punishable u/s. 120-B, of Code, 1860 - Appellants were charged for commission of an offence punishable u/s. 5(2) r/w. s. 5(1) (d) of Act, 1947 - Hence instant appeal - Whether sanction u/s. 197(1) of Code, 1973 was required for prosecution under Act, 1947 and whether criminal conspiracy of appellants/public servants had relation with their official duties - Held, act constituting an offence directly or reasonably connected with his official duty, would require sanction for prosecution u/s. 197 of Act to concerned public servant only - Appellant had committed illegality and hence liable to be punished for criminal conspiracy u/s. 120-B, of Code, 1860 - Alleged act directly and reasonably connected with appellant's official duty as Minister, would not be offence u/s. 197(1) of Act - decision of HC relating to charge of criminal conspiracy set aside - Appeal allowed.
