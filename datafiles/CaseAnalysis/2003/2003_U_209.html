Savitri Cairae and another v Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad and another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 508; (2003) 6 SCC 255; AIR 2003 SC 2725; 2003 ALJ 1805; 2003 (52) ALR 432; 2003(5) SCALE 93; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1081; 2003 (4) SLT 320; 2003 (4) Supreme 489; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 1970

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, ss.29, 32, 55 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.4, 6, 23, 23(1-A) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Land Acquisition Officer granted different rates of compensation in respect of lands acquired for purpose of housing schemes under Adhiniyam - Claimants dissatisfied with compensation offered - (1) Whether equality cl enshrined in art 14, Constitution of India, 1950 can be invoked in matter of enforcement of a state legislation vis-Ã -vis a parliamentary legislation and/or legislation of another State - (2) Whether provisions of Land Acquisition Act is to be read into provisions of Adhiniyam - Held, (1) art 14 does not authorise striking down of a law of one state on ground that in contrast with a law of another state on same subject, its provisions are discriminatory, nor does it contemplate a law enacted by Parliament or by state legislature dealing with similar subjects being held to be unconstitutional by a process of comparative study of provisions of two enactments - as sources of authority for two statutes being different, art 14 can have no application - (2) Adhiniyam provides for acquisition of land in terms of Land Acquisition Act - By reason of legal fiction created under Adhiniyam, Parishad is deemed to be a local authority Parishad invokes provisions of Land Acquisition Act in its capacity as a local authority under Land Acquisition Act state issues a notification in terms of s 29 of Adhiniyam, and make declaration in terms of s 32 thereof which are on similar terms as contained in ss 4 and 6, Land Acquisition Act respectively.
