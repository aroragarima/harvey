A.C. Razia v Government of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 15; (2004) SCC (Cr) 618; (2004) 2 SCC 621; AIR 2004 SC 2504; 2004 (1) CCR 126; 2004 (1) Crimes 296; 2004 CRLJ 2498; 2004 (91) ECC 377; 2004 (113) ECR 486; 2004 (1) JCC 179; JT 2004 (1) SC 264; 2004 (1) KLT 836; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 236; 2004 (60) RLT 359; 2004(1) SCALE 378; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 437; 2004 (1) SLT 497; 2004 (1) Supreme 489

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments, Specially Empowered

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22, 22(4), 22(5), 22(7), 226 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 11, 11(1)(b), 3(1)(i), 3(1)(ii), 3(1)(iii), 3(1)(v), 3(2), 3(3), 8(c), 8(f), 9 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, s. 14 - Writ of habeas corpus - Detention under COFEPOSA - Challenged - Foreign currency seized from relative of detenue who named detenue - Documents furnished to detenu were not translated into Malayalam on account of which he was unable to make proper representation against his detention - Representation disposed of by Central Govt. - Whether improper disposal of representation violative of art 21 and 22 - Held, Central Govt. should not necessarily have translated copies of documents referred to and relied upon in detention order, nor can it be said that there was no application of mind on part of Central Govt. for simple reason that translated copies of documents were not available before it physical availability of such documents or translated version thereof, would have made no difference as regards disposal of representation or consideration of question whether detention was in conformity with law - Further held, validity of detention order has to be negatived.
