Â K. S. Rashid & Sons And Another v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, U.P. Andothers

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 1964 SC 1190; [1964] 52 ITR 355; 1964 (2) SCJ 209; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 826

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Assessment - Notices - Excess profit tax - Ultra vires - Writ filed under Art. 32 of Constitution in respect of the notices served on them and order of excess profit tax levied on them - Parties contended that notices are invalid, because s.34(1A) was itself ultra vires - Respondents to the appeals are Commissioner of Income-tax and Income-tax Officer - Appeals arise out of the four writ petitions filed by firm and its partner in HC challenging the validity of notices served u/s.34(1A) in respect of their income - Writ petitions dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant entitled to receive a copy of reasons recorded by Income-tax Officer - Held, yes - What was urged before the HC by appellant was not that no reasons had been recorded by the Income-tax Officer as required by the proviso; the argument was that the appellant had not been given a copy of the said reasons and it appears to have been urged that the appellant was entitled to have such a copy - Powers conferred on the Income-tax Officer u/s.23(2) to take evidence would then not be available to him, and, indeed, all the powers prescribed and the procedure laid down by s. 23 would become irrelevant - Provisions in regard to appeals and revisions contained in ss.30, 31, 33, 33A and 33B would also be inapplicable - Appeals dismissed.
