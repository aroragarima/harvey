Manjunath Anandappa urf. Shivappa Hanasi v Tammanasa and others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 280; (2003) 10 SCC 390; AIR 2003 SC 1391; 2003 (51) ALR 357; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 50; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 713; 2003 (94) RD 742; 2003(3) SCALE 331; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 1068; 2003 (2) SLT 843; 2003 (3) Supreme 248; 2003 (2) UC 835

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963 - S. 16(c) - readiness and willingness on the part of the plaintiff to perform his part of contract as is mandatorily required in terms of Section 16(c) of the Act - Held, a Plaintiff in a suit for specific performance of contract not only must raise a plea that he had all along been and even on the date of filing of suit was ready and willing to perform his part of contract, but also prove the same - having regard to the facts and circumstances of the case the plaintiff cannot be said to have even substantially complied with the requirements of law - Further, the High Court should not have interfered therewith without arriving at a finding that the discretion has been exercised by the Courts below on wrong legal principle.
