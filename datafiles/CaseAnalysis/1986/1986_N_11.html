Supreme Court of India

24 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 256; (1986) 4 SCC 566; AIR 1987 SC 251; 1987 CRLR 128; 1987 CrLR(SC) 128; JT 1986 SC 701B; 1986(2) SCALE 638; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 1; 1987 TAXLR 1830

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Distillery and Warehouse Rules

Summary: Excise - M.P. Excise Act - Rules framed under, r. 22 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Distillery and Warehouse Rules - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Grant of licence for manufacture and sale of country liquor - (A) M.P. Excise Act - Rules framed under, r. XXII - Policy decision of Govt. D/- 30-12-1984 -(1) Modes u/r.XXII and manner of their applicability - (2) If grant of licenceto respondents result in monopoly - (3) Decision was single intergrated one - High Court divide it and sustain one and reject other in writ jurisdiction on ground of delay in filing petition - (4) Prior publication of rules u/s.62(2)(h) for giving effect to policy decision - Need of - (B) Constitution - Construction of new distilleries - (I) Delay and laches in filing writ petition - Meanwhile Licensee acquiring land, constructing buildings etc. - Maintainability - (2) Whether advertising and tenders for it, is a must - (3) Scrutiny of Govt. decision , by the Court - Decision of Cabinet produced by petitioners without objection - Jurisdiction - (4) Manufacture and sale of country liquor is not fundamental right - Ignorance of - (C) Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - New distilleries - Nothing to show that 50 or more workers were employed - Need for licence from Central Govt. - Held: (A) Modes are alternative, any one of them can be resorted to and it is not necessary to resort to modes in serial order - Grant on facts held was for 5 years and it did not create monopoly in their favour - Held, HC could not do so - Held, is not necessary - (B) Held, petitions cannot be allowed - Govt. can negotiate with persons who come with offer and it is not necessary to advertise and invite tenders - Court can look into it - However State Govt. cannot ignore requirement of art.14 - (C) Held, is not necessary - Appeal allowed.
