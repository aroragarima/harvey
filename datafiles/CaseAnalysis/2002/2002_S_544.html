Ocl India Limited v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2010; (2003) 2 SCC 101; AIR 2003 SC 2148; 2002(9) SCALE 729; 2003 (130) STC 35; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Oriss Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 23(4)(a) - Companies Act, 1956 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Oriss Sale Tax Rules, 1947 - Whether after Asst. Commr., as a delegatee of power of Commr., Sales Tax, u/s. 23(4)(a) of Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 r/w r. 80 of Orissa Sales Tax Rules, 1947, has exercised revisional jurisdiction in respect of an order of Sales Tax Officer, Commr. can exercise power under said provision to revise same order over again? - Held, in regard to orders passed by Sales Tax Officer, after delegation, Asst. Commr. was competent to revise and, in fact, he did exercise power to revise order of Sales Tax Officer, after issuing a show cause notice - If that be so, power of Commr.(the delegator) under afore-quoted provisions has been exhausted by Asst. Commr. and Commr. cannot, in law, exercise delegated power over again - Appeal allowed.
