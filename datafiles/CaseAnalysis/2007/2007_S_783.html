Ravindran and another v Superintendent of Customs

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1558; (2007) 6 SCC 410; AIR 2007 SC 2040; 2007 CRLJ 3414; 2007 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 89; JT 2007 (7) SC 47; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 80; 2007(7) SCALE 153; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 22, 22(c), 29, 42, 42(2), 43, 50, 57, 8(c) - Conspiracy - Confessional Statement - Validity - Appellants were tried by Trial Court and were charged of offences u/s. 8 (c) r/w ss. 22 and 29 of Act - Trial Court by judgment and order found them guilty of offences charged and sentenced them to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years - Aggrieved by judgment of Trial Court accused preferred appeal before HC - HC by its impugned common Judgment and Order upheld conviction of appellants but acquitted H against whom it found no satisfactory evidence to prove charges - Hence, instant appeals - Whether confessional statement of appellant was sufficient to uphold conviction particularly when same had been retracted and there was no other reliable evidence to convict him - Held, benefit extended to H ought to be extended to appellant as well - HC did not find confession of co-accused reliable enough to be used either against him, or his co-accused - Appellant and H were arrested and brought and their confessions were recorded - HC had not considered it safe to act on confession of H and acquitted him of charge leveled against him - If confession of H could not be used against him, certainly it could not be used against appellant - Both confessions were recorded one after other - Evidence that remains against appellant was rather slender and it was not safe to uphold his conviction particularly when person who was supposed to have given him contraband had been given benefit of doubt by HC - Charge against appellant was one of conspiracy to commit offences under the Act - HC had not accepted involvement in conspiracy of H - If confessional statement of H was discarded, there remained no evidence except his own confession to implicate appellant - HC had not found it safe to act on retracted confession of H- Confession of appellant was also recorded by same team of officers at about same time - Hence, appellant was also entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeals allowed.
