State of Haryana v Ram Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20100; (2001) 6 SCC 254; AIR 2001 SC 2532; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 36; 2001 (44) ALR 719; 2001 (3) AWC 2391; 2001 (2) CGLJ 401; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2001 (6) SC 8; 2001 (3) KLT 130; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 95; 2001 (129) PLR 299; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 832; 2001(4) SCALE 527; 2001 (4) SCJ 325; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1178; 2001 (5) SLT 427; 2001 (5) Supreme 321

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 18 and 23(1) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, 77, 74(2) and 76 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 51 - A and 57 - Market value - Whether High Court erred in rejecting exhibits R2 and R - 3 produced by appellant which showed rate of land as similar to acquired land, as inadmissible only on ground that parties to documents had not been examined by state? - Held, there was no allegation by claimants that exhibits R - 3 did not represent genuine transactions and HC was in error in refusing to consider transactions evidenced therein merely because parties to documents were not examined - Issue remanded to HC - Appeals disposed of.
