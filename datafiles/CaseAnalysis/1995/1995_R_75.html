State Of Rajasthan & Ors. v Rameshwar Lal Gahlot

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1914; (1996) SCC (L&S) 348; (1996) 1 SCC 595; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 428; AIR 1996 SC 1001; 1996 (2) CalLT(SC) 22; JT 1995 (9) SC 621; 1996 LabIC 914; 1996 (1) LLJ 888; 1996 (1) LLN 296; 1996 (1) SLR 595; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 673; 1996 (1) Supreme 228; 1996 (1) UJ 229

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25F - Reinstatement - Challenged - Respondent was appointed on 8-1-1988 and was terminated on 19-11-1988 - Respondent challenged said order - Single Judge held that since respondent had completed more than 240 days, the termination was in violative of s. 25F of Act and directed to make fresh appointment of the respondent - On appeal, DB set aside the latter part of the order and directed reinstatement with back wages - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, where the termination is in terms of letter of appointment saved by clause (bb) of s. 25 of Act, neither reinstatement or fresh appointment could be made. Since the appellant has not filed any appeal against the order of the Single Judge and respondent came to be appointed afresh on 27-6-1992, he would continue in service, till the regular incumbent assumes office as originally ordered. Since the order is found to be in terms of letter of appointment, respondent is not entitled to back wages. The DB was incorrect in directing payment of back wages. Appeal allowed.
