Koli Chunilal Savji and Another v tate of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 753; (1999) 9 SCC 562; (2000) SCC (Cr) 432; AIR 1999 SC 3695; AIR 1999 SCW 3727; 1999 (4) CCR 74; 1999 CRLJ 4582; 2000 (4) G.L.R. 3298; 1999 (2) JCC 523; JT 1999 (7) SC 568; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 594; 1999(6) SCALE 267; 1999 (3) SCJ 437; 1999 (8) SLT 253; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 284; 1999 (8) Supreme 717

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 34 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 386 - Dying declaration - Credibility - Declaration recorded by Magistrate - No endorsement by doctor indicating mental state of deceased - Doctor not examined - Held, declaration cannot be said untruthful merely on that ground - Appellate Court must look at reasoning of Trial Judge and be satisfied whether those are just and proper while setting aside acquittal be rejected.
