Bandlamuddi Atchuta Ramaiah and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2507; (1996) 11 SCC 133; (1997) SCC (Cr) 128; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 367; AIR 1997 SC 496; 1996 (4) CCR 130; 1996 CRLJ 4463; JT 1996 (8) SC 502; 1996(7) SCALE 230; 1996 (3) SCJ 351; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304 Part-II, 326, 324 - Murder - Right of private defence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly involved in an altercation with PW1 (brother-in-law of deceased) and appellants stabbed the deceased on his chest to which deceased succumbed to his injuries - Trial Court acquitted appellant no. 1 but convicted accused no. 2 u/ss. 304 Part-II, 326 and 324 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo RI for life - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment set aside the Trial Court judgment and convicted accused no. 2 u/s. 302 of IPC and accused no. 1 was convicted u/s. 326 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo RI for 3 years - Aggrieved accused filed instant appeal - However, pending instant appeal, accused no. 1 was died - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, Ex.P-24 (first information statement recorded from appellant no. 1) cannot, undoubtedly, be used against the second accused or the third accused - None of prosecution witnesses had a case that any cash or even any property of accused was taken away by PW1 or his party on the previous night - HC, thus, went wrong in relying on Ex.P-24 to reach the finding that accused had a strong motive to launch an attack on PW1 and his men on the night of occurrence - There were certain other broad features in evidence to assume with some degree of certainty that PW1 and his people would have been the aggrieved party at the close of first day's skirmishes and consequently they would have had the animus to retaliate - From the evidence the view took Trial Court that PW1 and deceased would have been waiting to retaliate for previous night's occurrence seems to be reasonable - Aggressor, in all probabilities, would have been PW1 and his party - No doubt that accused no. 2, by inflicting the fatal injury on deceased had exceeded the limit of right of private defence - Accused no. 2 was liable to be convicted u/s. 304 Part-I of IPC and was sentenced to RI for 5 years - Appeal allowed.
