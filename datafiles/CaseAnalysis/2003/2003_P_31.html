State of Andhra Pradesh v P.V. Hanumantha Rao (D) through LRs. and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 867; (2003) 10 SCC 121; AIR 2004 SC 627; JT 2003 (9) SC 438; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 49; 2003(8) SCALE 688; 2003 (1) SCW 6203; 2003 (7) SLT 22; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 736; 2003 (7) Supreme 456

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Courts Act, 1972, Inams Abolition Act, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition Inams Act, Andhra Pradesh Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - s. 2(d) - Land grabbers - High Court reversed the judgment of the Special Court holding that the respondents were not 'land grabbers' - Scope of jurisdiction of High Court under art. 226, Constitution of India, 1950 - Held, according to the High Court, the Special Court was clearly wrong in holding the occupants of the land as 'land grabbers' when they had produced documents of title and orders of Revenue Board and the High Court. The Special Court on unsubstantial grounds had rejected their claims to title and possession of the land. Therefore, the High Court in its writ jurisdiction was fully justified in examining the documents of title and upsetting the judgment of the Special Court on the ground that material evidence and circumstances proved by the occupants were overlooked in holding the occupants as land grabbers - Further held, the High Court did not exceed its jurisdiction in upsetting the judgment of the Special Court.
