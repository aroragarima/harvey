Bishna @ Bhiswadeb Mahato and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1047; (2005) 12 SCC 657; AIR 2006 SC 302; 2006 (3) CalLT(SC) 9; JT 2005 (9) SC 290; 2005(9) SCALE 204; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 892

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence, Old Age, Blood-Stained Earth, Fire-Arms, Non-Explanation, Mutual Fight, Right Of Self Defence, Injuries On Accused, Graphic Description, Arrows, Criminal Law Act, 1967

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148, 149, 34, 324, 109, 326, 324, 325, 96 and 97 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Arms Act, 1959 - Criminal Law Act, 1967 - Accused persons were variously armed - Altercations started between appellants and deceased - Claimed they exercised right of private defence - Trial judge acquitted appellants for commission of all offences except one u/s. 148 - High Court on appeal sentenced of all accused u/s. 148, IPC and variously u/s. 302 r/w ss. 109 and 302 r/w ss. 34 and 326 r/w ss. 34 and 324 r/w ss. 34 and 325 and s. 324 of IPC - Appeal - Enmity between two groups about possession of said land - (A) Whether testimony of prosecution witness should be disbelieved? - Held, testimony of an injured witness vis-a-vis improvement and inconsistencies in their evidence as regard part played by each of accused may not itself be a ground to disbelieve witnesses - (B) Whether they are entitled to claim private defence? - To claim a right of private defence extending to voluntary causing of death, accused must show that there were circumstances giving rise to reasonable grounds for apprehending that either death or grievous hurt would be caused to him - Court laid down law that a person who is apprehending death or bodily injury cannot weigh in golden scales on spur of moment and in heat of circumstances, number of injuries required to disarm assailants who were armed with weapons - In moments of excitement to use exactly only so much force in retaliation commensurate with danger apprehended to him where assault is imminent by use of force - As appellants were aggressors, no right of private defence could be claimed by them particularly when victims were first chased and then assaulted - (C) Whether s. 149 is attracted? - For purpose of attracting s. 149 of IPC, it is not necessary that there should be a pre-concert by way of a meeting of persons of unlawful assembly as to common object - If a common object is adopted by all persons and shared by them, it would serve purpose - Further held, if common object or common intention of an offence u/s. 149 or 34, IPC was to be invoked, same should have been invoked against those who shared common object or intention - HC as to why appellants have been found guilty u/s. 302/34, IPC and not u/s. 302/149, IPC - Further held, although in this case right of private defence was not exercisable, having regard to peculiar facts and circumstances of case, of opinion that possibility of appellants committing crime without any intention to cause death cannot be ruled out - Appeal partly allowed.
