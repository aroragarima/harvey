Â Asgarali Nazarali Singaporawalla v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 155; AIR 1957 SC 503; 1957 CRLJ 605; 1957 SCJ 421; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 114, 116, 161 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Illegal gratification - Acquittal - Re-trial - Sustainability - Appellant and accused Nos. 4 and 5, receivers of firm, were charged with offences u/s. 161 r/w ss. 116, 109 and 114 IPC for offering sum of Rs. 1,25,000 as illegal gratification to one Sub-Inspector of police as a reward showing favour to accused and to firm in exercise of his official functions - Trial Court acquitted appellant and accused Nos. 4 and 5 - On appeal by State, HC set aside order of Trial Court and ordered a re-trial of appellant and co-accused - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that provisions of the impugned Act were violative of fundamental right enshrined in art. 14 of Constitution and were therefore ultra vires - Held, provisions contained in s. 10 of the impugned Act would work to prejudice of appellant in that he would be subjected to a re-trial before Trial Court having jurisdiction over case involving a re-hearing of whole case - Further, words of s. 10 of the impugned Act were clear and categorical and were not capable of being construed in any other manner except that all cases triable by Trial Court which were pending immediately before commencement of the impugned Act before any magistrate should be forwarded for trial to Trial Court having jurisdiction over such cases, magistrates having cognizance of same and trying them being divested of jurisdiction to proceed further with trial thereof immediately after commencement of the impugned Act - Thus, case of appellant was unfortunate - Special Judge acquitted him of offence with which he was charged in same manner as Magistrate himself did, but there was no escape from fact that he would have to face a re-trial and undergo expenses and anxiety in defending himself over again - Therefore, order for re-trial of appellant made by HC was correct - Appeal dismissed.
