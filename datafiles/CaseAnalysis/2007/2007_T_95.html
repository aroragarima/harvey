State Of Maharashtra v Tulshiram Bhanudas Kamble And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 832; (2007) 14 SCC 627; AIR 2007 SC 3042; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2586; 2007 CRLJ 4319; JT 2007 (10) SC 597; 2007(10) SCALE 166; 2007 (8) SCJ 875; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Charge- Sheet, Inimical Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 324, 188 and 452 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, once the Court has accepted that the appellants have committed the offence under s. 452 it follows that High Court has accepted the prosecution version that the accused along with others are the aggressors and the incident has happened in the courtyard of Laxman as alleged by the prosecution - Only because witnesses were inimical to the respondents, the same by itself cannot be a ground to discard their evidences - It is not correct to say that merely because some part of the evidence is found to be false the entire evidence has to be rejected - High Court has not assigned cogent or sufficient reasons for disagreeing with the findings of the trial court - Case of great injustice which has been caused by the judgment of the High Court which has acquitted the accused of the offence under s. 302 on flimsy grounds - Appeals allowed.
