Om Prakash Etc. Etc. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 756; (1992) 4 SCC 212; (1992) SCC (Cr) 848; AIR 1993 SC 138; 1992 (3) CCR 253; 1992 (3) Crimes 581; 1992 CRLJ 3935; 1992 CrLR(SC) 639; 1992 (2) DMC 353; JT 1992 (4) SC 524; 1992(2) SCALE 196; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 921; 1992 (2) UJ 710

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32 and 3 -Indian Penal Code,1860, s. 300 - Accused, appellants in this case were found guilty of murder and questioned evidentiary value of dying declaration and other witnesses -The appellant, husband of deceased killed her by sprinkling kerosene oil on her body and then set on fire - Deceased was rescued by her sister who lived opposite door - Death was due to burn injuries and two doctors examining deceased found her to be in position of making statement - (A)Whether dying declaration of deceased can be rejected on grounds that she had suffered burn injuries at that time? - Held, unless there are materials on record to show that deceased was not in a position to make a statement it is not possible to reject her statement which has been treated as dying declaration - (B) Whether High Court was justified in validating statement of witnesses as evidence ?- Held, PW6 and PW7 lived just opposite to house of accused persons - As such they are most natural witnesses and there is no reason to take a contrary view than view taken by High Court, so far as veracity of aforesaid witnesses are concerned - (C) Whether accused are guilty of common intention to murder deceased ? - Finding of guilt on charge of murder has to be recorded on basis of circumstances of each case and evidence adduced before Court - Further held, burning of deceased, took place in open courtyard during day-time which is not consistent with theory of suicide and dying declaration of victim along with evidence of natural witnesses fully established charges of murder with common intention leveled against accused and in-laws of deceased, it was a case of murder and not suicide - Appeal Dismissed.
