S.G. Chemical and Dyes Trading Employees' Union v S.G. Chemicals and Dyes Trading Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 648; (1986) SCC (L&S) 303; (1986) 2 SCC 624; 1986 (1) CLR 360; 1986 (52) FLR 661; 1986 LabIC 863; 1986 (1) LLJ 490; 1986 (1) LLN 986; 1986(1) SCALE 1048; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 126; 1986 (3) SLJ 29

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Maharashtra Recognition Of Trade Unions And Prevention Of Unfair Labour Practices Act, Trade Union Act, 1926, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1984, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1982, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, ss. 26, 27, 8 and 28, item. 9, schs. II, III and IV - Industrial Dispute Rules, 1957, r. 82(A) - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, ss. 25(FFA), (FFF), (O), (K)(1) and (L) - Constitution of India, arts. 136, 226 and 227 - Industrial Dispute (Amendment) Act, 1982, ss. 1(2), 12, 2(m), (b), (cc) and 7 - Bombay Shops and Establishment Act, s. 4 - Appeal against the order of dismissing a complaint filed under s. 28 of the Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - (A) Whether the Churchgate office was in legal parlance a part of the Trombay factory and the Company was not bound to follow the procedure prescribed by s. 25(O)? - The work of purchasing statutory items, printing forms, etc., for the Trombay factory and the Worli Division was also done by the Churchgate Division and the maintenance of the Express Building at Churchgate and of the factory at Trombay was done by personnel in the Churchgate Division - It is thus clear from the evidence on the record that the Trombay factory could never have functioned independently without the Churchgate Division being there - (B) Whether the act of closing down the Churchgate Division was an unfair labour practice under s. 28 of the Maharashtra Act read? - Held, yes - The period which has elapsed is, however, too short for the moneys received by such workmen from the alternative employment taken by them to aggregate to any sizeable amount, and it would be fair to let the workmen retain such amount by way of solatium for the shock of having their services terminated - It is declared that the closure of the Churchgate Division of the Company was illegal and the workmen whose services were terminated on account of such illegal closure continued and are entitled to receive from the Company their full salary and all other benefits - Appeal allowed
