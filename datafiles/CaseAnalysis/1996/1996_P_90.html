Reserve Bank Of India And Ors. v Peerless General Finance and Investment Company

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1148; (1996) 1 SCC 642; AIR 1996 SC 646; 1996 (1) CCC 45; [1996] 85 Comp Cas 920; 1996 (1) CompLJ 385; JT 1996 (1) SC 8; 1996(1) SCALE 13; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, s.45K; Residuary Non-banking companies (Reserve Bank) Directions, 1987, para 4A (inserted on 19-4-1993) - Scope - Deposits in non-banking companies - Fixation of uniform maximum ceiling of Rs.10/- under Para 4 towards cost or expenses for issuing brochure / application form, servicing the depositors' account, etc. - held, is not violative of art.14 - Close monitoring and supervision and regulation of working of such companies - Necessary - Direction to Union Govt. to consider creation of separate instrumentality for entrusting said task.
