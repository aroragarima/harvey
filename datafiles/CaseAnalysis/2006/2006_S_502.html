Vijay v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 387; (2006) 6 SCC 289; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 541; JT 2006 (7) SC 112; 2006(7) SCALE 370; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Village Panchayats Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(J-2) - Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, 1992 - Appellant was elected as a member of Grampanchayat also elected as Sarpanch of village thereafter Councilor of Zilla Parishad - In view of amendment of s.14(1)(J-2), he was held to have disqualified himself to hold said post by Additional Collector - Held, appellant was elected in terms of provisions of a statute - Right to be elected was created by a statute and, thus, can be taken away by a statute only - When a literal reading of the provision giving retrospective effect does not produce absurdity or anomaly, the same would not be construed to be only prospective - When a law is enacted for benefit of community as a whole, even in absence of a provision, statute may be held to be retrospective in nature - Appeal dismissed.
