V. P. Shrivastava and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3802; (1996) SCC (L&S) 670; (1996) 7 SCC 759; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 1026; 1996 (33) ATC 363; JT 1996 (2) SC 374; 1996(1) SCALE 805; 1996 (2) SCJ 392; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 59; 1996 (1) SLR 819; 1996 (2) Supreme 116

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh State Industries (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1965 - Madhya Pradesh State Industries (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1985 - Inteseniority - Between direct recuitees and promotees - (A) Whether promotees are entitiled seniority over direct recruitees? - No - Seniority had to be determined in accordance with general principle laid down in Direct Recruit case - Held, initial appointment of respondents on promotion not having been made following procedure laid down by Recruitment Rules of 1965 and even though they are continuing in post uninterruptedly but Public Service Commission having not approved their appointments as yet - Appellants/direct recruits must be held senior to respondents/private respondents/ad hoc promotees - (B) Whether non - inclusion of affected parties is fatal to appellants' case? - Further held, no - Appellants do not challenge so-called ad hoc appointments of promotee respondents but they do challenge position of said ad hoc promotee respondents over appellants in seniority list - Very principle of 'determination of seniority' made by State Govt. is under challenge and for such a case State is necessary party who has been impleaded - (C) Whether there is any delay and laches in challenging promotion in favour of respondents by appellants? - Earlier final gradation list was prepared only on 23-12-1988 and appellants had approached tribunal in 1989 - Question of delay does not arise - Appeal allowed.
