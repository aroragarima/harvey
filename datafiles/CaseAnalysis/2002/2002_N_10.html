State of Karnataka and others v N. Parameshwarappa and others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2412; (2003) 12 SCC 192; 2002 (5) AWC 3588; JT 2002 (9) SC 393; 2002 (4) SLR 251; 2003 (1) SLR 251; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 454

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Circular, Implementation Of Order, Quashing of

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Circular - Implementation of order - Quashing of - Instant appeals were filed against order passed by HC - Held, a faint challenge has also been made, but not of any substance or merit, of acceptance. Relief in this regard has been so confined for more than one relevant and justifying reason and one such was absence of Union of India and University Grants Commission before Court as party to proceedings. Even that apart, some of the facts of the submission made counsel for respondent teachers, which found favor of SC acceptance and dissuaded us from interfering with judgment under challenge in these appeals, if persuaded to their logical extent only fortifies said conclusion arrived at by HC. One such contention was that it was permissible for Govt., independently of Central Govt. assistance also to extend benefits to teachers beyond that which has been provided for by Govt. of India. SC is in agreement with judgment of HC relief accorded should be confined to period with effect from 1-4-1990 only. At same time SC do not find any reasonable justification to confine relief to only such of teachers who approached Court and having regard to fact that relief related to revision of scales of pay, everyone of that class of teachers who approached would be entitled to benefit, notwithstanding that they have not approached Court. SC are in equal agreement with HC in denying payment of interest at compounded rates which, SC viewed that cannot be justified at all on facts and circumstances of case wherein a serious and genuine doubt existed about applicability of Govt. order Appeals dismissed.
