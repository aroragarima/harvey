Grih Kalyan Kendra Workers’ Union v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 900; (1991) 1 SCC 619; (1991) SCC (L&S) 621; AIR 1991 SC 1173; 1991 (16) ATC 507; 1991 (1) CLR 844; 1991 (62) FLR 337; JT 1991 (1) SC 60; 1991 (1) LLJ 349; 1991 (1) LLN 319; 1991(1) SCALE 1; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 15; 1991 (1) SLR 618; 1991 (1) UJ 468

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Parity, Honorarium

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16 and 39(b) - Grih Kalyan Kendra - (A) Whether comes under definition of State? - (B) Applicability of principle of 'Equal pay for equal work', which has assumed status of fundamental right - Held, question not considered in details, assumed that it is instrumentality of state - Is applicable with full vigour to establishment which is instrumentality of state, but similarity between work need not be found by mathematical formula - Appeal dismissed.
