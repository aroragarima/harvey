Union Public Service Commission and another v Hiranyalal Development and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 412; (1988) 2 SCC 242; (1988) SCC (L&S) 484; AIR 1988 SC 1069; 1988 (7) ATC 72; JT 1988 (1) SC 609; 1989 LabIC 1210; 1988 (1) LLN 926; 1988(1) SCALE 573; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 302; 1988 (3) SLJ 60; 1988 (2) SLR 148; 1988 (1) UJ 608

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 29 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Powers of Administrative Tribunal as to promotion - (A) Non inclusion of name of respondent-employee in select list - And uncommunicated adverse remarks to him considered by Selection Committee - Whether tribunal can direct to include his name - (B) Supersession within meaning of art. 311 of Constitution - (C) IPS (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, regn. 1 - Selection - Reasons for not selecting a person - Not recorded - Held, it cannot direct that respondent's name be included in select list - Possible only in case of promotion and not of selection - Committee need not record reasons - Appeal allowed.
