Gajanan Kamlya Patil v Addl. Collector and Comp. Authority and others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 87; (2014) 12 SCC 523; AIR 2014 SC 1843; 2014 (103) ALR 717; 2014 (2) AWC 1692; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 292; 2014 (2) CTC 665; JT 2014 (3) SC 211; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 477; 2014 (123) RD 495; 2014(2) SCALE 286; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 1032; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 170

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Possession, Justifiability, Land in question

Summary: Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 (U.L.C. Act)-Section 10 (5)-Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999 (1999 Act)-Notice under Section 10 (5) of U.L.C. Act issued to appellant for taking possession of his land-Appellant aggrieved by notice, filed writ petition before High Court to quash notice and sought declaration that in view of 1999 Act proposed action of respondents or State or its authorities for taking possession of land be declared null and void-High Court granted liberty to appellant to move to civil court for establishing his claim over property in question and to get benefit of 1999 Act-Respondents have no case that forcible possession of land in question was taken-Nothing to show that respondents had taken peaceful possession, nor there is anything to show that appellant had given voluntary possession-Facts clearly indicate that only de jure possession taken by respondents and not de facto possession-Since there is nothing to show that de facto possession taken from appellant, respondents cannot hold on to land in question, which are legally owned and possessed by appellant-Consequently, notice and subsequent action taken therein in view of repeal of U.L.C. Act-Quashed-High Court committed error in holding that Mumbai Metropolitan Region Development Authority is in possession of land in question and hence question as to whether possession had been legally taken or not has to be decided by civil court-Proceedings initiated against appellants-Quashed.
