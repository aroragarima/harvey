Â Ronald Wood Mathams v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 1954 SC 455; 1954 CRLJ 1161; 1954 SCJ 649; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Government of India Act, Indian Independence Act

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 197 and 257 - Conspiracy to cheat Govt. and bribery - Appellants charged with conspiracy to cheat Govt. and bribery - Special Tribunal acquitted appellants under charge of conspiracy but convicted them for offence of bribery - High Court in turn convicted appellants under both charges - Appellants preferred appeal to Federal Court - Contention raised before Federal Court as to that Special Tribunal(ST) had contravened s. 257 of CrPC, 1898 which amounted to serious irregularity - HC after giving opportunity, confirmed conviction under both charges - HC's conviction, whether proper? - Held, no - Objection as to contravention of s. 257 upheld - Situation at time of independence of India, and crisis period, some of witnesses were not traceable, noted by Court - Court gave finding that three of witnesses as coming from facts of case, were real, and it would be unsafe to act on view that others must be fictitious, and if they are real persons who could not be examined for not fault of appellants, grave injustice would result in accused being condemned without evidence of these witnesses having being taken - Further held, conviction by HC not justified - Appellants acquitted - Appeals allowed.
