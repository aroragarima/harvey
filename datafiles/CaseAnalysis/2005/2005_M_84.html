State of Andhra Pradesh v M/s. Kone Elevators (India) Limited.

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 113; (2005) 3 SCC 389; AIR 2005 SC 1581; 123 ECR 189; 2005 (181) E.L.T. 156; JT 2005 (2) SC 314; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 40; 2005(2) SCALE 178; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 152; 2005 (140) STC 22

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 5G r/w 5F, Sch. I, entry 82 - Manufacture, supply, installation and commissioning of elevators and lifts - Whether contracts entered into and executed by assessee were contracts for sale or works-contract?; whether s. 5G of Act applicable? - Held, major component of end-product is the material consumed in producing lift to be delivered and skill and labour employed for converting main components into end-product was only incidentally used - Delivery of end-product by assessee to customer constituted a 'sale' and not a 'works-contract' - Transactions in question constitute 'sale' in terms of entry 82 of first schedule to the Act and s. 5G of Act was not applicable - Appeal allowed.
