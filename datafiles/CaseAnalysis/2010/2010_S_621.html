Jagtu v Suraj Mal and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 966; (2010) 13 SCC 769; AIR 2010 SC 3490; 2011 (84) ALR 433; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 858; 2010 (2) RCR(Rent) 498; 2011 (112) RD 252

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 79 r/w. O. 27, r. 1 and O. 1, r. 9 - Plaintiff filed the suit seeking declaration of certain rights over the suit land admitting the factual position that the land belonged to the State of Haryana - Defendants raised the preliminary objection that the suit for declaration of certain rights in the land belonging to the State of Haryana was not maintainable without impleading the State of Haryana - Trial court dismissed the suit for want of necessary parties - The First Appellate Court reversed the finding on the said issue merely observing that the plaintiffs had not claimed any relief against the State, therefore, State was not a necessary party and decreed the suit - HC affirmed the judgment and findings of the First Appellate Court and dismissed the Second Appeal preferred by the defendants - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the First Appellate as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, the Appellate Court failed to appreciate that declaration in respect of certain rights over the land belonging to the State was the relief sought in the suit - Thus, in absence of the owner of the land, no such declaration could be granted - Therefore, State of Haryana was a necessary party - The impugned suit, therefore, could not proceed for want of necessary parties - Appeal allowed.
