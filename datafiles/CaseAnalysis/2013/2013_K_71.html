Kumar v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 310; (2013) 12 SCC 699; AIR 2013 SC 2802; 2013 CRLJ 3443; JT 2013 (7) SC 175; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 187; 2013(7) SCALE 32; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 434

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.376, 302, 201, 506 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - It was case of prosecution that appellant-accused allegedly given blow with 'poorikatai' on deceased/victim head due to which she fell unconscious - Taking undue advantage of her condition, appellant-accused had sexual intercourse with her and attacked her 13 months' old baby by giving a forcible punch on her face on account of which she also became unconscious - Further, appellant with intention of causing disappearance of evidence and in order to show it a suicidal case, caused death of deceased and her daughter by pouring kerosene and set them on fire - Trial Court convicted appellant for offences punishable u/ss. 376, 302 r/w s. 201 and 506 of IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction of appellant and dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that conviction solely based on extra-judicial confession made to PW2, could not be sustained since she had not disclosed the same at the earliest point of time - Further, offence u/ss. 376, 302 r/w s. 201 of IPC was not proved with aid of medical evidence, beyond reasonable doubt, therefore, conviction and sentence under these sections had to be set aside - Held, SC was satisfied that Trial Court, after exhaustive consideration of oral and documentary evidence adduced by both sides, rightly found appellant-accused guilty of all the charges and passed order of conviction and imposed appropriate sentence - Reports submitted by Scientific Officers, viz., PWs 11 and 16, coupled with post mortem certificate and evidence of Medical Officer, established beyond doubt that this was a clear case of murder - Further, extra-judicial confession made to PW2 was rightly accepted by Trial Court as the same was within parameters of law and withstood test of reasonableness and credibility - Overall assessment of evidence of prosecution witnesses clearly established circumstances against accused in a cogent manner - It was seen from evidence of PWs 2 and 3 that appellant had motive, namely, he had a lustful eye towards his sister-in-law, which was proved beyond doubt - Appeal dismissed.
