M. Krishna Reddy v State Deputy Superintendent of Police, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 511; (1992) 4 SCC 45; (1992) SCC (Cr) 801; AIR 1993 SC 313; 1992 (2) CCR 238; 1992 (2) Crimes 1197; 1993 CRLJ 308; 1992 CRLR 613; JT 1992 (4) SC 436; 1992(2) SCALE 120; 1992 (2) UJ 613

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(e) and 5(2) - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 5(1)(e) r/w s. 5(2) of the Act - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order but reduced the sentence of 2 years' rigorous imprisonment to six months and enhancing the fine of Rs. 30, 000 imposed by the trial court to Rs. 50, 000 in default to suffer simple imprisonment for three months - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, on the face of the totality of the circumstances and the cumulative effect , the prosecution cannot be said to have successfully fixed the criminality u/s. 5(1)(e) of the Act on the appellant who had completed an unblemished service for well over a period of 25 years. Hence, conviction recorded by the HC affirming the judgment of the HC sentencing the appellant. Under s. 5(1)(e) r/w. s. 5(2) of the Act and the sentence imposed therefore cannot be sustained and are liable to be set aside. Appeal allowed.
