Sri Ramachandrappa v The Manager, Royal Sundaram Alliance Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 495; (2011) 13 SCC 236; 2011 (3) ACC 698; 2011 ACJ 2436; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 75; 2011 (88) ALR 695; 2011 (5) AWC 5130; 2011 (4) CGLJ 560; JT 2011 (8) SC 628; 2011 (164) PLR 652; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 107; 2012 (1) RLW 453; 2011(8) SCALE 399; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 922

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Loss Of Future Earning

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Appellant met with an accident and sustained grievous injurious while he was riding as pillion on a motorcycle - Appellant filed claim petition before the Tribunal - Tribunal awarded compensation - Appellant filed appeal for enhancement of compensation - HC marginally increased the compensation - Being dissatisfied with the quantum of compensation, appellant filed the instant appeal - (A) Whether the Tribunal was justified in reducing the income of appellant from rupees 4500/- per month as claimed by him to rupees 3000/- - Held, appellant was aged about 35 years and was working as a Coolie and was earning 4500/- per month at the time of accident - Claim was reduced by the Tribunal to a sum of '3000/- only on the assumption that wages of the labourer during the relevant period was 100/- per day, however, that assumption had no basis - Before the Tribunal, though Insurance Company was served, it did not choose to appear before the Court nor did it repudiate the claim of the claimant - Therefore, there was no reason for the Tribunal to have reduced the claim of the claimant and determined the monthly earning a sum of 3000/- per month - Further, appellant was working as a Coolie and therefore, it could not expect him to produce any documentary evidence to substantiate his claim - In the absence of any other evidence contrary to the claim made by the claimant, in the facts of the instant case, the Tribunal should have accepted the claim of the claimant - Appellant's claim was honest and bona fide and, therefore, there was no reason for the Tribunal to have reduced the monthly earning of the appellant from 4500/- to 3000/- per month - (B) Whether the quantum of compensation determined by the Tribunal was justifiable - Held, assessment of compensation under the head of loss of earning capacity was calculated abysmally on the lower side - On the question of disability caused due to the accident, the Doctor, who had been examined as claimant's witness, stated that because of the injury sustained by the claimant, he was not able to work as a Coolie and was not able to do any other manual work - Doctor's evidence was not controverted by the insurance company by subjecting the claimant to cross-examination - Hence, it could safely conclude that claimant had become permanently disabled and, therefore, had lost the future earning capacity permanently - Claimant had also suffered prolonged medical treatment and hospitalization - Amount awarded by the Tribunal was too less and, therefore, compensation awarded by the Tribunal was enhanced - Appeal partly allowed.
