Honda Siel Power Products Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1274; (2007) 12 SCC 596; 2008 (221) E.L.T. 11; [2007] 295 ITR 466; JT 2007 (13) SC 481; 2007(13) SCALE 472; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 552; 2008 (9) S.T.R. 117; [2007] 165 TAXMAN 307; [2008] 12 VST 500

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeal allowed, Income Tax Act, 1961, Actual Cost, Original Order, Apparent From Record, Mistake Apparent From Record, Rectification Of Mistakes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Appeal concerns application of s. 154 which provides for rectification of any mistake apparent from the record by any income tax authority - High Court came to the conclusion that vide order dated 10.9.2003, in the guise of rectification, the Tribunal had, in fact, reviewed its earlier order which fell outside the scope of s. 254(2) - Appeal against - Held, Tribunal was justified in exercising its powers under s. 254(2) when it was pointed out to the Tribunal that the judgment of the coordinate bench was placed before the Tribunal when the original order came to be passed but it had committed a mistake in not considering the material which was already on record - High Court was not justified in interfering with the order - Appeal allowed.
