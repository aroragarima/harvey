Union of India v Prakash P. Hinduja and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 490; (2003) 6 SCC 195; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1314; AIR 2003 SC 2612; AIR 2003 SCW 3258; 2003 (3) CCR 7; 2003 (3) Crimes 142; 2003 CRLJ 3117; 2003 (105) DLT 510; 2003 (2) JCC 1164; JT 2003 (5) SC 300; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 556; 2003(5) SCALE 103; 2003 (4) SLT 335; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 307; 2003 (4) Supreme 466

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Developments, Central Vigilance Commission (Amendment) Ordinance, 1998

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.120-B, 161, 165, 165A, 420 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, ss.3, 4(1), 5(1)(d), 5(2), 5(2)(i), 5(4), 6 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss.19, 22, 7A - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, s.4 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss.173, 173(2), 173(8), 190, 197, 2(h), 36, 482, 561A - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971, s.19(2)(c) - Central Vigilance Commission Ordinance, 1998 - Central Vigilance Commission (Amendment) Ordinance, 1998 - Whether Court can go into validity or otherwise of investigation done by authorities charged with duty of investigation under relevant statutes and whether any error or illegality committed during course of investigation would so vitiate charge-sheet so as to render cognizance taken thereon bad and invalid - Held, legal position is absolutely clear and also settled by judicial authorities that Court would not interfere with investigation or during course of investigation which would mean from time of lodging of First Information Report till submission of report by officer in charge of police station in court u/s. 173(2), CrPC, this field being exclusively reserved for investigating agency - Further held, HC having arrived at aforesaid findings, only result which could logically follow was to dismiss petition - There was absolutely no occasion for allowing same and quashing cognizance and further proceedings in case - Appeals allowed.
