Om Kr. Dhankar v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 598; (2012) 11 SCC 252; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 58; 2012 (2) CTC 871; JT 2012 (3) SC 485; 2012 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1216; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 563; 2012 (167) PLR 120; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 209; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420,409,427 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(1)(d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Illegal Gratification - Misuse of Official Powers - Sanction from Competent Authority - Summons - Dismissed - Sustainability - Complainant-appellant filed complainant u/ss. 420,409,427 of IPC and s. 13(1)(d) of Act, against respondent No. 2 on ground that he had cheated him and public money had been embezzled and accused received illegal gratification - Complainant case was that he had 3 transport buses and due to non-payment of taxes, his vehicle was impounded and respondent No. 2 asked complainant to deposit amount at his residence and get his vehicle released - Complainant had paid amount due at residence of respondent No. 2 and got his vehicle released - Magistrate found sufficient grounds against respondent No.2 to be summoned to stand trial - Respondent No. 2, challenged by filing revision before Sessions Judge, which was disposed holding that in absence of sanction by competent authority summoning order could not have been issued - Complainant's challenge before HC, was not successful - Hence instant appeal - (A) Whether Criminal Revision Petition against order of summoning was maintainable - Held, SC, in Rajendra Kumar Sitaram Pande and Others Vs. Uttam and Another (1999) 3 SCC 134 1999 Indlaw SC 427,had held that 'it would not be appropriate to hold that order directing issuance of process was purely interlocutory and, therefore bar u/s. 397(2) would apply, therefore revisional jurisdiction u/s. 397 could be exercised - (B) whether in facts and circumstances sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC was required - Held, SC, in Prakash Singh Badal case 2006 Indlaw SC 941 held that offence of cheating u/s. 420 or for that matter offences relate able to ss. 467, 468, 471, 120-B could by no stretch of imagination by their very nature be regarded as having been committed by any public servant while acting or purporting to act in discharge of official duty in such cases, official status only provides an opportunity for commission of offence - Sessions Judge and HC, were not right in holding that for prosecuting respondent No. 2 for offences for which summoning order had been issued, sanction of competent authority u/s. 197 CrPC was required - Appeal allowed.
