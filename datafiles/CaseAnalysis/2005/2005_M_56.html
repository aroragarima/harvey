Smt. Claude-Lila Parulekar v M/S. Sakal Papers Pvt. Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 196; (2005) 11 SCC 73; 2005 (2) BC 542; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 429; [2005] 124 Comp Cas 685; JT 2005 (3) SC 523; 2005(3) SCALE 365; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 1063

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Instrument Of Transfer, Issued Capital, Sales of Goods Act, 1930

Summary: Corporate - Practice and Procedure - Companies Act, 1956 s.108 - Dispute, relate, to transfer of share, in company - Rights of pre-emption - Prayer for cancellation of impugned transfers and rectification of Register of Members of Company by removal of names of Respondent 5 and his group - Whether there is violation of provisions of s.108 of Act in transferring shares to Pawar group? - Held, it would not be appropriate at this stage to exercise discretion to grant relief of rectification - Fact remains that appellant has been wronged and is entitled to be compensated - Ends of justice would be met by directing that appellant should be compensated with an amount of Rs. 3 crores to be paid by company to appellant - Appeals disposed of.
