Pantha Chatterjee and others v Amit Khan Deb and others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1755; (2005) 10 SCC 355

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Monetary benefits - Non-compliance of order - Contempt - Petitioners stated that SC made specific directions to State Govt. to clear all consequential monetary benefits to petitioners with statement of account to be forwarded to Central Govt. for reimbursement - SC further had directed that there ought to be no delay in payment to be made by State Govt. to petitioners nor by Central Govt. to State Govt. on account of reimbursement - Case of petitioners was that directions of SC had not been complied by State Govt. - Hence instant petitions.

Held, three months time fixed was extended from time to time and more than one year has passed and going by time fixed in original judgment, directions had not complied by State Govt. Clearly directions in judgment dt.7-7-2003 have not been fully complied with. Instead, letter has circulated seeking adjournment and application has also filed for issue of directions to Central Govt. to release Rs 38 crores so as to enable State to pay arrears of Border Wing Home Guards. Application of State was misguided and misplaced. SC not concerned with matters between State Govt. and Central Govt. as also inter-department problem, if any, of State Govt. No justification for non-compliance with order. State Govt. to fully comply with directions of SC as contained in judgment and report compliance or else Finance Secretary, Secretary, Civil Defence of State and Chief Secretary should remain present in Court on next date of hearing to receive orders on contempt petitions. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Contempt proceedings could be initiated against any party when the alleged contemnor has without any bonafide reasons violated any order of Court or has not complied with order of Court
