State of Rajasthan and Others v Basant Nahata

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 552; (2005) 12 SCC 77; AIR 2005 SC 3401; 2005 (4) AWC 3085; JT 2005 (8) SC 171; 2005(7) SCALE 164; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Powers-Of-Attorney Act, 1882, Doctrine Of Public Policy, Rajasthan Amendment Act, 1976, Uttar Pradesh (Temporary Control of Rent and Evictions) Act, 1947

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Registration Act, 1908, s. 22-A - Rajasthan Amendment Act, 1976, s. 22-A - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 245, 246, 31-A, 31-B - Powers Of Attorney Act, 1882, s. 1A - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Registration Act as amended by State of Rajasthan - Grant of power of attorney - Constitutionality of s 22A challenged - S 22A confers arbitrary powers on State Govt. to determine as regard declaring a particular document being opposed to public policy - Held invalid by HC - Appealed herein - Held, a subordinate legislation which is not backed up by any statutory guideline under substantive law and opposed to enforcement of a legal right would not be valid - Transaction between two persons capable of entering into a contract which does not contravene any statute would be valid in law - In absence of any substantive provisions contained in a parliamentary or legislative act, a person cannot be refrained from dealing with his property in any manner he likes - Such statutory interdict would be opposed to one's right of property as envisaged u/art. 300A of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
