Pratap Chandra Mehta and another v State Bar Council Of M.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 481; (2011) 9 SCC 573; 2012 (3) AWC 2296; 2012 (2) JabLJ 44; JT 2011 (9) SC 135; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 1079; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 183; 2011(8) SCALE 506; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 965; 2011 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 882

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Right To Practise, State Bar Councils, U.P. Municipalities Act, Bar Council of India Rules, 1975, Advocates (Amendment) Act, 1977, Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (Procedure, Terms and Conditions for Grant of Trading Licence and other Related Matters) Regulations, 2004, State Bar Council of Madhya Pradesh Rules

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 15(1) or 15(2)(c) - Power of State Bar Council to frame Rules - State Bar Council of Madhya Pradesh Rules, rr. 15, 121 and 122-A - (A) Whether the provisions of rr. 121 and 122-A of the M.P. Rules were ultra vires s. 15 of the Advocates Act, inter alia for the reason that there was no nexus between the rule making power of the State Bar Councils and the powers provided u/ss. 15(1) or 15(2)(c) of the Advocates Act? - R. 122-A of the M.P. Rules contemplated moving of a 'no confidence motion' and upon such motion being passed by majority of the members, present and voting, the office bearer against whom such a motion was moved would be liable to be removed from such office - Challenge to the vires of r. 122-A was that s. 15 of the Advocates Act did not contemplate the framing of such a Rule by the State Bar Councils - It was stated that the introduction of such provision suffered from the vice of excessive delegation - Held, power of the State Bar Council to frame rules u/s. 15 of the Advocates Act as a delegate of the Bar Council of India had to be construed along with the other provisions of the Advocates Act, keeping in mind the object sought to be achieved by the Advocates Act - Further, provisions of the Advocates Act were a source of power for the State Bar Council to frame rules and it would not be in consonance with the principles of law to give that power a strict interpretation, unless restricted in scope by specific language - Also, Bar Council of India had also framed rules and permitted moving of 'no confidence motion' against its Chairman/Vice-Chairman subject to compliance of the conditions stated therein - Similarly, r. 122-A of the M.P. Rules contemplated the removal of a Chairman/Vice-Chairman by a motion of no confidence, passed by a specific majority of the members and subject to satisfaction of the conditions stated therein - This provision, thus, could neither be termed as vesting arbitrary powers in the elected body, nor could it be said to be suffering from the vice of excessive delegation - Power delegated to the elected body was within the framework of the principal Act, i.e., s. 15, r/w other provisions, of the Advocates Act - Hence, provisions of rr. 121 and 122-A of the M.P. Rules were not ultra vires of the provisions, including the provisions of s. 15 of the Advocates Act and also did not suffer from the vice of excessive delegation - (B) Whether rr. 121 and 122-A of the M.P. Rules were invalid for want of prior approval from the Bar Council of India? - Held, amended rules of the M.P. Rules had received the approval of the Bar Council of India, particularly r. 122-A - Rules would not be invalidated for want of issuance of any notification, as it was not the requirement in terms of s. 15(3) of the Advocates Act and in any case would be a curable irregularity - Appeals dismissed.
