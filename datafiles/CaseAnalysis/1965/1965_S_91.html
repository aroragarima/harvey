Dr. G. H. Grant v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 466; AIR 1966 SC 237; 1965 (2) MLJ 29; 1965 (2) SCJ 404; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Apportionment Of Compensation

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 12, 16 and 30 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - High Court held that title of owner to land acquired under Land Acquisition Act could not be extinguished under that Act till possession was taken u/s. 16, and that since title of Dr. Grant in land acquired stood statutorily vested in State by virtue of notification issued under Bihar Land Reforms Act, he was not entitled to receive compensation money - Whether Land Acquisition Officer can, after making award u/s 12 of Act fixing compensation for land acquired and apportioning same among persons interested in land, refer question of apportionment u/s. 30 of Act to decision of court? - Held, under Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 title of Dr. Grant to land notified for acquisition became vested in state, and therefore right to compensation for land acquired devolved upon state - A dispute between Dr. Grant and state as to their conflicting claims to compensation money was clearly a dispute which could be referred u/s. 30 of Land Acquisition Act to court - Right of State of Bihar has undoubtedly arisen after award was made, but once title which was originally vested in Dr. Grant stood statutorily transferred to state, it was open to state to claim a reference, not because state was a person interested in compensation money before date of award, but because of right which has arisen since award was made - Appeals dismissed.
