Essar Steel Limited v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 306; AIR 2016 SC 1980; JT 2016 (4) SC 242; 2016 (3) MLJ 864; 2016(4) SCALE 267; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 326

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Petroleum & Gas

Keywords: Essential Commodities Act, 1955, Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 73 - Policy decision by Central Govt. - Jurisdiction - Determination - Parties entered into contract for supply of petroleum product at a specified price - Further, it is stated that respondent issued policy directive whereby prices were changed and it was held that uniform pooled prices should be charged for all existing and new customers - Aggrieved by increase in price due to policy decision, appellant filed petition before HC challenging validity of policy decision - HC vide impugned order held that respondent is competent to take policy decision and it is not arbitrary, unjust or violative of fundamental rights of appellants - Whether impugned policy decision of respondent is bad in law.

Held, price revision on account of change in Govt. policy is situation which had been envisaged by parties themselves at time of entering into Supply Agreement. Further, by virtue of art. 73 of Constitution read with Entry 53 of List I, respondent has power to legislate and take policy decisions in relation to matters pertaining to mineral oil resources and inflammable substances, which includes petroleum product in question. SC held that respondent passed impugned policy decision in larger public interest, keeping in view need to provide petroleum product at viable prices to existing and new customers alike and due deliberation and exploring all other possible alternatives. Further, nothing has been brought on record to show that said decision is arbitrary, mala fide, and unreasonable or taken after non-application of mind. Hence, impugned policy decision does not suffer from any infirmity in law and is hereby upheld. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Court is not justified to quash policy decisions taken by Govt. unless it is proved that same is malafide and arbitrary.
