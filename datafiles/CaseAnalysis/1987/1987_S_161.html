G. V. Nanjundiah v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28099; (1987) Supp SCC 266; (1988) SCC (Cr) 77; AIR 1987 SC 2402; 1987 (3) Crimes 217; 1988 CRLJ 152; 1987 CRLR 580; JT 1987 (3) SC 313; 1987(2) SCALE 636; 1988 (1) ShimLC 153; 1987 (3) SLJ 244

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(i)(d) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Appellant convicted u/s. 161, IPC, 1860 and s. 5(2) r/w s. 5(1)(d), Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Whether charged of corruption have been proved against appellant ? - Appellant was found by learned Special judge to be honest and his service record shows that he was an officer of integrity throughout his career - Held, prosecution has not been able to prove guilt of appellant beyond all doubts - Appeal allowed.
