State of Haryana v Mahender Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1087; (2007) 13 SCC 606; 2008 CRLJ 444; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3156; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 909; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 932

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Prison Act, 1894, Untouchability (Offences) Act, 1955, West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992

Summary: A circular letter issued by the State of Haryana laying down criteria for pre-mature release of the prisoners has been declared to be unconstitutional by a Division Bench of the Punjab and Haryana High Court - Appeal against - Whether new policy decision adopted by the State of Haryana will have a prospective operation? - Held, High Court might not be correct in holding that the State has no power to make any classification at all - A classification validly made would not offend art. 14 of the Constitution of India - Although do not agree with all the reasonings of the High Court, sustain the judgment - Appeals dismissed.
