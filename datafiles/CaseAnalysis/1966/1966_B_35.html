Â Shankaranarayana B. R. and Others v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1966 SC 1571; 1967 (2) LLJ 751

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: (A) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 309, - Mysore Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, s. 1(3) - States Re-Organization Act, 1956 -Mysore General Service Rules, 1961 - Madras Hereditary Village Offices Act, 1895 - Abolition of Village Offices - Constitutionality - Maintainability - By virtue of States Reorganization Act, 1956, new State known as State of Mysore was formed comprising territories of the then existing State - State legislature of new State of Mysore enacted Mysore Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961 and received assent of President on 8-7-1961and came into force on 1-2-1961- Governor of State in exercise of powers vested in him under proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution and other powers enabling him in that behalf, framed rules called Mysore General Services Rules, 1961 in order to make recruitment to posts of village accountants - State Govt. directed Deputy Commissioners to issue notification and appoint persons recruited under rules and relieve the then holders of their offices and if number of candidates fell short, continue existing holders in their posts - There was further direction that other village officers, viz., patels, thoties and talaries, whose posts were also abolished under the Act, should be continued in their present posts pending consideration by Govt. - Appellants filed writ petition before HC challenged impugned Act which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Act attracts Colourable Legislation of the Constitution was challenged - Held, Gajapati Narayan Deo 1953 Indlaw SC 20, provisions of the Act and rules made there under plainly provide for abolition of hereditary village offices and make those offices stipendiary posts - The Act makes no secret of its intention to abolish hereditary posts but argued that even after abolition, same posts were sought to be continued - There was no doubt that names of offices - It is no doubt true that the names of the offices had not been changed but there was basic structural difference between posts that had been abolished and posts that had been created - Therefore, posts created by new Act were stipendiary posts and carry salaries according to grades created by rules - Further, incumbents were transferable and their service was pensionable Different qualifications were prescribed for new posts for consideration of incidents attaching to new posts it was clear that old posts had been abolished and new posts had been created and that whole complexion of posts has been changed -Hence, Court opined impugned Act could not be held to be piece of colourable legislation and as such invalid - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 309, - Mysore Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, s. 1(3) - States Re-Organization Act, 1956 -Mysore General Service Rules, 1961 - Madras Hereditary Village Offices Act, 1895 s.6 - Abolition of Village Offices - Constitutionality - Maintainability Whether 1895 Act did not in terms apply to village offices of district of South Kanara was challenged - Held, relied on Musti Venkata Jagannadha v. Musti Veerabhadrayya 96 1921 Indlaw PC 10, Board's Standing Orders had not been repealed in their application to district of South Kanara, which was now part of State of Mysore, would not make any difference - Hence, Board's Standing Orders recognize principle of heredity underlying 1895 Act and repealed said Act and Karnams Regulation - Appeals dismissed.
