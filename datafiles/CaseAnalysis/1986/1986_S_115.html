Neelima Shangla Ph.D. Candidate v State Of Haryana And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 194; (1986) 4 SCC 268; (1986) SCC (L&S) 759; AIR 1987 SC 169; 1986 (2) CCC 922; 1987 (54) FLR 652; JT 1986 SC 445; 1987 LabIC 34; 1986 (2) LLN 829; 1986(2) SCALE 435; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 785; 1987 (1) SLJ 195; 1986 (3) SLR 389; 1986 (2) UJ 739

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 315 - Selection for post of Subordinate Judges - Examination of rules - Appellant, though qualified, her name was not sent to Govt. - Withheld full list of successful candidates on ground that only a limited number of vacancies were available - Held, petitioner was one of candidates who was entitled to be selected but her name was not communicated and her name be included in selection list - Appeal allowed.
