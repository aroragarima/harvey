State of Kerala v K. M. Charia Abdullah and Company

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 1965 SC 1585; 1965 (2) ITJ 351; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 601; 1965 (16) STC 875

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Revisional Jurisdiction, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Madras General Sales Tax Rules 1939

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, ss. 8, 12(2)(i), 12(2) - Madras General Sales Tax Rules 1939, r. 14-A - A/y 1950-1951 - Claimed exemption - Challenged - Assessee's/respondents submitted their return under the 1939, Act, for A/y 1950-1951, and claimed exemption on ground that they were commission sales exempted u/s. 8 of the 1939, Act, to AO, who accepted exemption claimed - Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, in exercise of powers u/s. 12(2)(i) of the 1939, Act directed fresh enquiry in respect of said exemption - On basis of fresh evidence, Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes revised order of AO and assessed assessee's on larger turnover - Aggrieved assessee's appealed before Tribunal, who set aside order passed by AO - Aggrieved appellant/State moved to HC, who declared r. 14-A of the Rules to be ultra vires - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC, was justified - Held, SC, was not called upon to express any opinion about correctness of order passed by Deputy Commissioner on merits - HC had not investigated that question, and SC had no materials before which would justify SC, in launching upon enquiry into that unexplored field - SC, had however, thought it necessary to explain restrictions inherent in exercise of power u/s. 12(2), r/w r. 14-A of the Rules, because respondent's counsel urged before SC, that enquiry made by Deputy Commissioner was inconsistent with scheme of the Act, in violation of rules of natural justice, and in circumvention of restrictions on power to re-assess - That was matter which would demand investigation before HC - SC, desired only to impress view taken by SC, that r. 14-A of the Rules, was not ultra vires was not sufficient to dispose of revision application filed before HC - HC, would have to make enquiry whether in circumstances of case Deputy Commissioner, was competent to proceed in manner he has done and to pass order which was impugned before Tribunal - Order passed by HC, declaring r. 14-A of the Rules to be ultra-vires was set aside, and proceedings were remanded to HC, to be dealt with according to law - Appeal allowed.
