Jayrajsinh Digvijaysinh Rana v State of Gujarat and another

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 207; (2012) 12 SCC 401; 2012 CRLJ 3900; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 65; 2012 (3) JCC 1943; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 307; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2012(6) SCALE 525; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 467, 468, 471, 420 and 120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 320 and 482 - Respondent No. 2 registered FIR for offences punishable u/ss. 467, 468, 471, 420 and 120-B of IPC against three accused persons including appellant - Being aggrieved and dissatisfied with the same, the appellant (Accused No.3) filed an application u/s. 482 of the CrPC to quash and set aside the said FIR - Application was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it would be possible to quash the FIR by the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC or by SC exercising jurisdiction u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Held, exercise of power must be for securing the ends of justice and only in cases where refusal to exercise that power may result in the abuse of the process of law - In the instant case, appellant had sworn an affidavit with bona fide intention securing the right, title and interest in favour of respondent No.2 - In such bona fide circumstances, the power u/s. 482 may be exercised - Further, in view of the settlement arrived at between respondent No. 2 and the appellant, there was no chance of recording a conviction insofar as appellant was concerned - Furthermore, inasmuch as the matter had not reached the stage of trial, HC, by exercising the inherent power u/s. 482 of the CrPC even in offences which were not compoundable u/s. 320, may quash the prosecution - Terms of settlement accepted insofar as the appellant was concerned - Impugned FIR quashed insofar as the appellant was concerned - Appeal allowed.
