Kartarey and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 499; (1975) SCC (Cr) 803; (1976) 1 SCC 172; AIR 1976 SC 76; 1976 CRLJ 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1898, ss. 509, 374 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 8 - IPC , 1896, ss. 302, 34 - (A) Concurrent findings of fact by lower courts - Interference by SC - Held, evidence against appellant has been concurrently believed by the courts below - Rule of practice - SC do not disturb it unless the finding is manifestly erroneous, clearly unreasonable, unjust or illegal or violative of some fundamental rule of procedure or natural justice -(B) Plea of right of private defence - Contention of deceased attempted to ravish the accused - Sustainability of - Held, no foundation on evidence on record - They are speculative, unnatural and improbable - Fantastic story cannot be swallowed even by the most credulous man - Rejecting the contention - (C) Reliability of eyewitness - Successful escape of the accused person through a narrow route - Non-arrest by the witness - Improbability of participation of crime - Sustainability of - Held, crime was committed after proper planning - Route and the manner of escape were thought out in advance - Escape route lay in the direction in which they could not have been intercepted by the witnesses - Site-plan show that the entrance door of the house of appellant faces the north - The witnesses are supposed to have come from the north to this entrance door - Iit was comparatively easy for the two appellants to elude arrest by the witnesses - (D) Medical evidence - Importance of medical opinion with regard to injury and the weapon used to cause it - It is the duty of the Prosecution, not the Court to show the weapon to medical witness and invited his opinion - Failure to do so may cause aberration in the course of justice - Held, the number, nature and dimensions of the injuries of the deceased, as deposed to by Medical witness, afford a sure indication that they were caused with three different weapons - There were sixteen stab wounds which fall into three groups - The wounds in each group being of almost identical length and breadth - (E) Absence of dragging mark on the body - Veracity of ocular evidence of eyewitness - Held, manner in which deceased carried from the courtyard into the kotha, is unknown - It is not known whether the ground was soft or hard - Presence of blood and marks of dragging at kotha noticed by the IO - Ocular evidence is credible - (F) Appreciation of defence evidence by HC - Necessity - Held, proceedings u/s. 374 is the continuation of the trial on same evidence - It is the duty of the HC to reappraise the entire evidence and conclude on merits - HC approach of non-consideration of defence evidence is not in conformity with settled rule of practice - Remitted the matter to HC for redecision entail hardship to appellants - Examining evidence by SC - (G) Plea of alibi - Failure to establish - Intentionally remained absent from the village for four days after the occurrence - Maintain the conviction - Appeal dismissed.
