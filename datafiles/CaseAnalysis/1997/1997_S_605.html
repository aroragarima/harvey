State of Punjab and Others v Boota Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1395; (2000) SCC (L&S) 435; (2000) 3 SCC 733; 2000 (2) LLN 920

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether respondents are entitled to benefit conferred by notification D/- 09-07-1985 and circular letter D/- 24-11-1988, though they had retired long before coming into force of these changes? - Retirement benefits which are claimed by respondent are benefits which are conferred by subsequent orders/notifications - Persons who retired after coming into force of notifications and order are governed by different rules of retirement than those who retired under old rules and were governed by old rules - Two categories of persons, who retired were governed by two different sets of rules which cannot be equated - Granting of additional benefits has financial implications also therefore specifying date for conferment of such additional benefits cannot be considered as arbitrary - Held, respondents are not entitled to claim benefits which became available at a much later date to retiring employees by reason of changes in rules relating to pensionary benefits - Appeal allowed.
