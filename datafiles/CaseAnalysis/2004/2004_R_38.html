The State Of Andhra Pradesh v Raj Gopal Asawa And Anr

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 205; (2004) 4 SCC 470; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1306; AIR 2004 SC 1933; 2004 (1) CGLJ 426; 2004 (2) Crimes 9; 2004 CRLJ 1791; 2004 (2) JCC 852; JT 2004 (3) SC 560; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 330; 2004(3) SCALE 339; 2004 (2) SLT 793; 2004 (2) Supreme 478

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 2, 3, 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 30, 304B, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113-B, 114 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Conjoint reading shows that there must be material to show that soon before her death victim was subjected to cruelty or harassment - Prosecution has to rule out possibility of a natural or accidental death so as to bring it within purview of 'death occurring otherwise than in normal circumstances' - Expression 'soon before' is very relevant - Prosecution is obliged to show that soon before occurrence there was cruelty or harassment and only in that case presumption operates -'Soon before' is a relative term and it would depend upon circumstances of each case and no strait-jacket formula can be laid down as to what would constitute a period of soon before occurrence - Hazardous to indicate any fixed period, and that brings in importance of a proximity test both for proof of an offence of dowry death as well as for raising a presumption u/s. 113-B of Evidence Act - Held on facts that in view of death occurring within very few months of marriage, and evidence of PWs 2, 3, 4 and 6 that shortly before deceased committed suicide, demand of dowry was made, plea in untenable accusations clearly stand established so far as A-1, respondent no 1 is concerned - So far as accused A-3 is concerned, there is no evidence that he ever made any demand of dowry - Custodial sentence of 7 years would meet end of justice for respondent no 1 - Appeal partly allowed.
