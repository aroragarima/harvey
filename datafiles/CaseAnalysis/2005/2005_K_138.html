R & M Trust v Koramangala Residents Vigilance Group and others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 77; (2005) 3 SCC 91; AIR 2005 SC 894; JT 2005 (1) SC 507; 2005(1) SCALE 425; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 582; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1264

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - City of Bangalore Improvement Act, 1945 - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Karnataka Municipal Corporation Act, 1973, ss. 321, 505 - Public interest petition - Building licence issued for construction of multi-storeyed / multi-apartments - Maintainability of public interest litigation - Whether there is delay in filing petition? - Whether permission granted by Bangalore Municipal Corporation to appellant for raising construction up to third floor is in violation of any of provisions of Act and Rules? - Held, if lessee or his successor wants to raise a construction, then provisions of Karnataka Municipal Corporation Act will come into play and he has to obtain prior sanction for construction of building - As soon as permission is granted by Corporation, then he is to abide by those conditions along with condition laid down in absolute sale deed - Corporation at time of granting permission has to keep in mind provisions of Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act - No illegality in granting permission - There is no doubt that delay is a very important factor while exercising extraordinary jurisdiction u/art.226 - Appeals disposed of.
