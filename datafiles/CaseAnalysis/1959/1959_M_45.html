Â Western India Theatres Limited v Municipal Corporation of The City of Poona

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 253; AIR 1959 SC 586; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, ss. (1) (xi) and 60 - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, ss. 60 and 76 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245 - (A) Whether s. 59(1)(xi) of Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901, is unconstitutional on ground of excessive delegation of legislative function? - Held, power of Municipality cannot exceed power of Provincial Legislature itself and Municipality cannot impose any tax - Taxes can be levied by municipality only for implementing purposes mentioned in Chap. VII of Act - Provincial legislature was not abdicated in favour of municipality, for taxing power of municipality was made subject to approval of Governor-in-Council - Ultimate control was left with Governor's legislative council - S. 59(1)(xi), Bombay District Municipalities Act cannot be challenged as unconstitutional on ground of excessive delegation of legislative functions - (B) Whether dropping of word 'Reduce' and introduction of word 'modify' in body of s. 60 of Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901 legalizes enhancements of rates of tax ordinally imposed by Poona Corporation u/s. 59(1)(xi) of Act? - Further held, dropping of word 'Reduce' and introduction of word 'modify' in body of s. 60 of Act indicates an intention on part of legislature to widen scope of this section - Word 'modify' has been used in this Act in the sense that there may be modification involving an increase - Enhancement of tax u/s. 59(1)(xi) is valid - (C) Whether interpretation of a statute can be affected by a marginal note? - Further held, marginal note cannot affect construction of language used in body of section if it is otherwise clear and unambiguous - Appeal dismissed.
