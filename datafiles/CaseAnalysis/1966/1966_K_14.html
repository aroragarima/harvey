Kantamani Venkata Narayana and Sons v First Additional Income Tax Officer, Rajahmundry

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 274; AIR 1967 SC 587; [1967] 63 ITR 638; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 984

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.34 - Assessee is Hindu Undivided Family involved in money-lending - ITO issued notices u/s.34 for re-assessment of income of assessee, who prayed for a writ of prohibition directing ITO not to proceed under notices - Sufficiency of grounds which induced ITO to act is not a justiciable issue, but existence of belief can be challenged by assessee - Belief must be held in good faith - Held, averments made by ITO in his affidavit which have been accepted by Court of First Instance, prima facie, establish that ITO had reason to believe that by reason of omission or failure on part of assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment, income chargeable to Income Tax has escaped assessment - No attempt was made by assessee to furnish some reasonable proof of source of additional wealth - Increase in wealth discovered was wholly disproportionate to known sources of income of assessee - Appelas dismissed.
