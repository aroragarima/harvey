State Bank of India v Rajendra Kumar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1969 SC 401; 1969 CRLJ 659; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 517 and 520 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 406 and 420 - (A) Whether High Court was justified in reversing order of Sessions Judge directing the return of the currency notes without giving a notice to the appellant and without giving an opportunity to it for being heard? - Held, statute does not expressly require a notice to be issued, or a hearing to be given to the parties adversely affected - But though statute is silent and does not expressly require issue of any notice there is in the eye of law a necessary implication that the party adversely affected should be heard before the court makes an order for return of the seized property - (B) Whether HC was justified on merits in ordering currency notes to be returned to respondents 1 and 2? - Held, ss. 517 and 520 of CrPC confer a discretion on HC as regards disposal of property seized or produced before it or regarding which any offence was said to have been committed - But HC has not exercised its discretion according to proper legal principle and its order is hence liable to be set aside - (C) Whether appellant had 'right to possess' currency notes within the language of s. 517 of CrPC? - Held, court opined that in the circumstances of this case HC should have directed the return of said currency notes to appellant which had the 'right to possess' the currency notes within the language of s. 517, CrPC - Appellant asserted that it had obtained' currency notes in normal course of its business and without any knowledge or suspicion of their having been involved in the commission of any offence - Respondents have not alleged fraud or lack of good faith on the part of appellant - Therefore, appellant are entitled right to possess - Appeal allowed.
