G. L. Hotels Limited and Others v T. C. Sarin and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 587; (1993) 4 SCC 363; (1994) SCC (L&S) 3; 1993 (25) ATC 848; JT 1993 (5) SC 119; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, s. 2(k) - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 1(4) and (5), 2(12), 2(14-AA) - Whether hotels are factories within meaning of provisions of s. 2(12) of Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 because process of cooking food is carried on in its kitchen? - Held, it is enough that manufacturing activity has a broad connection with activities carried on in rest of premises - In present case, it cannot be denied that kitchen is an integral part of hotel-business - Those who occupy a hotel do depend upon food and beverages which are prepared in its kitchen, it is not possible to conceive of a hotel without a kitchen, hence, it cannot be denied that activity in kitchen has a connection with activities carried on in rest of hotel premises - Order accordingly.
