Rajendra Datta Zarekar v State Of Goa

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1260; (2007) 14 SCC 560; AIR 2008 SC 572; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 569; 2008 (3) CGLJ 135; 2008 CRLJ 710; 2008 (1) JCC 119; JT 2007 (13) SC 387; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 229; 2007(13) SCALE 664; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 840

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Charge- Sheet, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 376(2)(f), 342 - Trial Court acquitted the accused but High Court reversed the acquittal - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, there was not even an iota of evidence to show that PW-1 and her husband had any reason whatsoever to falsely implicate the accused - Evidence lead by the prosecution fully establishes the case against the accused beyond any shadow of doubt - Substantive sentence of ten years R.I. awarded under s. 376(2)(f) and one month R.I. under s. 342 are maintained - Modified the fine imposed - Appeal dismissed.
