Shantabai and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 309; (2008) 16 SCC 354; AIR 2008 SC 1571; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1967; 2008 CRLJ 2048; 2008 (2) JCC 1080; JT 2008 (3) SC 130; 2008(3) SCALE 325; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Police Record, Finger Prints, Stock Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, prosecution rests its case solely on circumstantial evidence - Prosecution failed to complete the chain of circumstances holding A-1, A-2 and A-3 guilty of the crime beyond reasonable doubt and the High Court was not justified in upholding the conviction of A-1, A-2 and A-3 on surmises and hypothesis - Appeal allowed.
