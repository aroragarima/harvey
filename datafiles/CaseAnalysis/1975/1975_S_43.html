Mahabir Jute Mills Limited, Gorakhpur v Shibban Lal Saxena and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 36; (1975) SCC (L&S) 460; (1975) 2 SCC 818; AIR 1975 SC 2057; 1975 (31) FLR 135; 1975 LabIC 1497; 1975 (2) LLJ 326; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 168

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 133 and 226 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2K and 3 - Industrial Dispute - (A) Whether administrative order should be speaking order - (B) Govt. Order u/s.4-K - Purely administrative - If required to be a speaking order - (C) Discretion of Govt. to make a reference - Interference with, by High Court under writ jurisdiction - (D) Govt. notification - Limited nature of functions of Board of Conciliation indicated - Chairman not showing his report to other members - (E) HC in its writ jurisdiction setting aside order of Govt. refusing to make reference of industrial dispute and directing it to reconsider matter - (A) Held, not necessary, unless the statute specifically enjoins it , but it is desirable - (B) Order need not be speaking order and it is not vitiated for absence of reasons - (C) HC had no jurisdiction to direct the Government how to act and how to exercise its statutory discretion - (D) Principles of natural justice not violated - (E) HC's order quashed in appeal u/art. 133 - Order of reference made by Government as a result of HC's order also falls to the ground even in the absence of any prayer by the appellant - Appeal Allowed.
