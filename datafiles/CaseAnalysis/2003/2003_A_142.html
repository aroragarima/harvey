Rekha Mukherjee v Ashish Kumar Das and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1059; (2004) 1 SCC 483; AIR 2004 SC 443; 2004 (54) ALR 744; 2004 (1) CHN 191; JT 2003 (10) SC 63; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 567; 2003(9) SCALE 698; 2003 (1) SCW 6493; 2003 (7) SLT 315; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 646; 2003 (8) Supreme 280

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - s. 53 - Consent order - Interpretation - Undertaking given by the appellant - Dismissal of suit - Held, application of doctrine of merger or for that matter the principle that appeal is a continuation of the suit will have no application - Further held, an undertaking of this nature must be construed in favour of the person giving such undertaking. It should not be stretched too far. A party giving an undertaking is bound thereby but by reason thereof, the same cannot be given a meaning whereby the scope and extent thereof is enlarged.
