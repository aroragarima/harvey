State of Uttar Pradesh v Sahai and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 52; (1982) SCC (Cr) 223; (1982) 1 SCC 352; AIR 1981 SC 1442; 1981 CRLJ 1034; 1981 CRLR 416; 1981(1) SCALE 939

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1973, s. 386 - Constitution, art. 136 - Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Appreciation of evidence in an appeal against conviction - High Court passed Order of acquittal - Validity of acquittal - (B) Interference by Supreme Court sought - (C) Sentence - Occurrence took place in 1972 and more than 8 years have elapsed since - Accused convicted by trial Court but acquitted by HC - Appeal against acquittal remained pending for five years - (A) Order held was not justified - (B) Interference by SC in appeal by special leave - (C) Held that although the murders committed were extremely gruesome, brutal and dastardly, extreme penalty was not called for in circumstances of the case - Hence accused were sentenced to imprisonment for life - Appeal Allowed.
