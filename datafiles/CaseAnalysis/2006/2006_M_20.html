State of Haryana and Others v M. P. Mohla

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1434; (2007) 1 SCC 457; 2007 (1) AWC 872; 2006(11) SCALE 658; 2007 (1) SLR 864; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 926

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Service

Keywords: Haryana Civil Services (Assured Career Progression) Rules, 1998, Haryana Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1998, H.C.S (A.C.P.) Rules, 1968

Summary: Service - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Haryana Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1998 - Haryana Civil Services (Assured Career Progression) Rules, 1998 - Promotion - Respondent was promoted to the post of Deputy Director - Respondent claimed fixation of his pay in the higher promotion revised pay scale of Rs. 143000-18300 as per Haryana Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1998 and Haryana Civil Services (Assured Career Progression) Rules, 1998- Said claim of respondent was rejected - Respondent filed writ petition against said order submitting that consequent to his promotion he was entitled to next higher revised pay scale of Rs. 14300-18300 - Said writ petition was allowed by HC - State filed special leave petition raising question of applicability of ACP Rules and non-applicability of Revised Pay Rules - Said SLP was dismissed by SC - State filed review petition against said order seeking clarification regarding applicability of Rules - Said review petition was also dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether review petition filed by state was maintainable? - Held, review petition filed by state was not maintainable as there was no error apparent on the face of the record - The effect of a judgment may have to be considered afresh in a separate proceeding having regard to the subsequent cause of action which might have arisen but the same by itself may not be a ground for filing an application for review - It was not open to the State to ask for a clarification contrary to or inconsistent with its stand taken in the Writ Petition - Question as regards applicability of one or the other Rules could not have been decided in the Review Petition - Appeal dismissed.
