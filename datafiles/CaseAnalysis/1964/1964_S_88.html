Indore Malwa United Mills Limited, Indore v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 293; AIR 1965 SC 1272; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 274; 1965 (1) ITJ 614; [1965] 55 ITR 736; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 559

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Indore Industrial Tax Rules, 1927, r. 3 - Income tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xi)(xv) - Company - Money lending business of cotton mill - Bad debt - Excess borrowings - Investment of surplus in managing agents who went into liquidation - Debt becoming irrecoverable - Mixed question of law and fact - Plea raise for first time before Supreme Court - Applicability of Rules to money-lending activity of cotton mill company - Whether assessee can claim deduction in respect of such debt u/r. 3 of Rules - Held, plea not allowed - Rules apply - Assessee can claim deduction - Civil appeals nos. 183 to 187 allowed, Civil appeals Nos. 188 to 192 dismissed.
