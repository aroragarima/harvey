Commissioner, Central Excise and Customs, Kerala and others v Larsen and Toubro Limited another and others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 575; (2016) 1 SCC 170; AIR 2015 SC 3600; JT 2015 (9) SC 245; 2015(9) SCALE 91; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 1046; 2015 (39) S.T.R. 913; [2015] 84 VST 403

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, Computation Of, Taxable Service, Government of India Act, 1935, Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, Bihar Sales Tax Rules, Finance Act, 2007, Works Contract (Composition Scheme For Payment of Service Tax) Rules, 2007, Service Tax (Determination of Value) Rules, 2006, U.P. Large Land Holdings Tax Act, 1957, Bihar Taxation on Passengers and Goods (Carried by Public Service Motor Vehicles) Act, 1961, Andhra Pradesh Land Revenue (Additional Assessment) and Cess Revision Act, 1962, Service Tax (Determination of Value) Rules, 2012

Summary: Service Tax - Indirect Tax - Finance Act, 1994 - Finance Act, 2007 - Indivisible works contract - Instant appeals by both assessee and revenue on whether service tax could be levied on indivisible works contracts prior to introduction of 2007 Act which expressly makes such works contracts liable to service tax.

Held, as per SC in Gannan Daunkerley and Co. and Others, Larsen and Toubro Ltd. v. State of Rajasthan and Others, Union of India and others, 1992 INDLAW SC 743, unless the splitting of an indivisible works contract is done taking into account the eight heads of deduction, the charge to tax that would be made would otherwise contain, apart from other things, the entire cost of establishment, other expenses, and profit earned by the contractor and would transgress into forbidden territory namely into such portion of such cost, expenses and profit as would be attributable in the works contract to the transfer of property in goods in such contract. Hence, service tax charging section itself should lay down with specificity that the levy of service tax could only be on works contracts, and the measure of tax could only be on that portion of works contracts which contain a service element which is to be derived from the gross amount charged for the works contract less the value of property in goods transferred in the execution of the works contract. However, 1994 Act has not done the same and so, any charge to tax under the five heads in s. 65(105) would only be of service contracts simpliciter and not composite indivisible works contracts. Hence, there is no charge to tax works contracts in 1994 Act and so, appeals of assessees are allowed and that of revenue are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Composite indivisible work contracts could be taxed by Parliament as well as State Legislatures. However, Parliament could only tax service element contained in said contracts and State could only tax the transfer of property in goods element contained in said contracts. Hence, it is important to segregate the two elements completely for if some element of transfer of property in goods remains when a service tax is levied, the said levy would be found to be constitutionally infirm.
