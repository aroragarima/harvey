Commissioner of Income Tax and Others v Oriental Rubber Works

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.P. Madon, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 447; (1984) 1 SCC 700; AIR 1984 SC 230; 1983 (3) CompLJ 381; 1984 (38) CTR 154; 1984 ECR 1233; [1984] 145 ITR 477; 1983(2) SCALE 682; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 817; 1984 TAXLR 145; [1983] 15 TAXMAN 51; 1984 (2) TLR 145; 1984 UJ 214

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: It Act, 1961, Income Tax Act, 1922

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss.(10), (12), 132(8) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Search - Books and documents - Inspect seized books - Illegal - Search was conducted by Income-tax Department in factory premises as well as the offices and godown belonging to respondent/assessee and various books of account and documents were seized from premises - Respondent/assessee was given opportunity to inspect seized books and documents as also to make copies of the entries - Respondent/assessee was that retention of seized books of account and documents beyond period of 180 days from date of the seizure was illegal and invalid - Whether revenue was under a statutory obligation to communicate to person from whose custody books of account and documents had been seized u/s.132(1) of 1961 Act - Held, enforcement of his right to have such books and documents returned to him by being kept ignorant about factum of fulfillment of conditions it was obligatory upon revenue to communicate - There was a statutory obligation on revenue to communicate to person concerned not merely Commissioner's approval but recorded reasons on which same had been obtained - Default of such expeditious communicating any further retention of seized books or documents would become invalid and unlawful - Approval will not become effective person legally entitled to return of seized books and documents a right to object to approval - Appeal disposed of.
