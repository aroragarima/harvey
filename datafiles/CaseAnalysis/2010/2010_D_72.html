B. T. Krishnappa v Divisional Manager, United Insurance Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 586; (2010) 12 SCC 246; 2011 (1) ACC 650; 2010 ACJ 1971; AIR 2010 SC 2630; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 85; 2010 (81) ALR 780; 2010 (4) AWC 3271; JT 2010 (5) SC 400; 2010 (5) KarLJ 390; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 511; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 223; 2010(6) SCALE 7; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicle Accident Claim - Assessment of compensation by HC - Failure to provide reasons - Appellant met with an accident due to rash and negligent driving of the 2nd respondent - Appellant preferred claim against 2nd respondent and appellant, owner and insurer of the offending vehicle - Tribunal granted compensation - Appellant filed appeal before the HC for enhancement of compensation - HC enhanced the compensation, however, being dissatisfied with the enhancement, appellant filed the present appeal - Whether appellant's case was properly considered by the HC - Held, HC accepted the Tribunal's assessment of the appellant's physical disability and payment of compensation under the heads 'loss of amenities and enjoyment of life and loss of earnings during laid up period' on the lower side - However, HC awarded an additional compensation only for future medical expenditures and did not deal with the aspect of future loss of earnings at all - HC should have given reasons and made its own assessment under the head of loss of future earnings, since HC, as the first appellate authority, is an authority both on facts and law - HC order starkly lack in any details on assessment of compensation under the head loss of future earnings - Therefore, matter remanded back to HC for consider the case of enhancement of compensation to the appellant in its proper perspective and keeping in mind the factual aspects of the case and in the light of the views expressed by SC in several judgments - Appeal allowed.
