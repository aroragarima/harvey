Morvi Industries Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 355; (1972) 4 SCC 451; (1974) SCC (Tax) 140; AIR 1971 SC 2396; 1974 (3) CTR 149; [1971] 82 ITR 835; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 970

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(1)(b)(i) and 10(2)(xv)- Accrual of income - Managing agent - Mercantile system of accounting - Amounts of income relinquished after they had become 'due' but before they had become 'payable' - Amounts, held, includible in total income - Relinquishment could not be construed as permissible expenditure u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act in the absence of proof to show that it was for business purposes - Appeals Dismissed.
