Mathai Samuel & Ors. v Eapen Eapen (Dead) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 471; (2012) 13 SCC 80; AIR 2013 SC 532; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 116; 2013 (96) ALR 216; 2013 (1) AWC 740; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 469; JT 2012 (11) SC 364; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 829; 2013 (118) RD 606; 2012(11) SCALE 167; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1098

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Registration Act - 1908 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Partition Suit - Testamentary disposition - Title - Sustainability - Suit for partition and separate possession was filed by original plaintiff-respondents - Trial Court passed preliminary decree, holding that A1 document did not preclude executants rights for disposing as same had characteristics of testamentary disposition, therefore not available for partition since sale deed executed by plaintiff were valid - Plaintiffs appealed against order of trial court and appellate court allowed and modified and preliminary decree was passed allowing partition and possession of 3/6th share of properties - Aggrieved appeal was filed before HC, which affirmed judgment of trial court and held that disposition of property was a settlement though possession and enjoyment were deferred - Hence instant appeal - Whether Trial Court, were justified in holding that property No. 1 was not available for partition - Held, right, title, interest, possession and ownership of property No.1 were with executants and they had full control and freedom to deal with that property as they liked unlike Schedule Nos. 1 to 6, therefore, SC, had no hesitation in holding that so far as that item was concerned, document in question could not be construed as a settlement or a gift because there was no provision in document transferring any interest in immovable property in praesenti in favour of settlees i.e. their sons - Judgment and decree of appellate court and HC, was set aside and judgment and decree of trial court was restored - Appeal allowed.
