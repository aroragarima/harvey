K. C. Sareen v C. B. I., Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20745; (2001) 6 SCC 584; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1186; AIR 2001 SC 3320; 2001 (3) CCR 141; 2001 (3) Crimes 399; 2002 (1) CRJ 705; 2001 CRLJ 4234; JT 2001 (6) SC 59; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 718; 2001(4) SCALE 644; 2001 (3) SCJ 374; 2001 (5) SLT 553; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 224; 2001 (5) Supreme 437; 2001 (2) UC 299

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 389(1) and 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) - Scope and ambit of powers of Appellate Court envisaged in s. 389 of CrPC, 1973 - Public servant convicted of an offence under Prevention of Corruption Act - Sentence suspended by High Court during pendency of appeal - Petition to suspend conviction was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - S. 389(1) of CrPC, 1973 deals with powers of Appellate Court regarding suspension of execution of 'sentence or order appealed against' during pendency of appeal - Though power to suspend an order of conviction, apart from order of sentence, is not alien to s. 389(1) of CrPC, its exercise should be limited to very exceptional cases - Merely because convicted person files an appeal in challenge of conviction Court should not suspend operation of order of conviction - Court has a duty to look at all aspects including ramifications of keeping such conviction in abeyance - Held, when a public servant was found guilty of corruption after a judicial adjudicatory process conducted by a Court of Law, judiciousness demands that he should be treated as corrupt until he is exonerated by a Superior Court - When a public servant who is convicted of corruption is allowed to continue to hold public office it would impair morale of other persons manning such office, and consequently that would erode already shrunk confidence of people in such public institutions besides demoralising other honest public servants who would either be colleagues or subordinates of convicted person - Hence, it is necessary that court should not aid public servant who stands convicted for corruption charges to hold any public office until he is exonerated after conducting a judicial adjudication at appellate or revisional level - When conviction is on corruption charge against a public servant Appellate Court or Revisional Court should not suspend order of conviction during pendency of appeal even if sentence of imprisonment is suspended - Appeal dismissed.
