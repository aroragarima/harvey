Union Of India And Ors. Etc. Etc. v Bombay Tyre International Ltd. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 1; (1984) SCC (Tax) 17; (1984) 1 SCC 467; AIR 1984 SC 1429; [1986] 59 Comp Cas 460; 1983 (3) CompLJ 270; 1983 ECR 1627; 1983 (14) E.L.T. 1896; 1983(2) SCALE 449; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 347

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4 - Whether wholesale price of an article can validly be basis for determining excise duty or should excise duty be limited to manufacturing costs and manufacturing profits? - Held, incidence of tax is different from mode of its collection or basis of its determination - Legislature can mandate a broader base for determining excise duty than manufacturing cost and profits - Packaging costs would be included in manufacturing costs except for secondary packaging done at special request - Individual appeals set for hearing based on proposition of law pronounced - Order accordingly.
