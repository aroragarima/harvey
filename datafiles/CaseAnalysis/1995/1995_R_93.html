Ravji @ Ram Chandra v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 761; (1996) 2 SCC 175; (1996) SCC (Cr) 225; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 165; AIR 1996 SC 787; AIR 1996 SCW 132; 1996 (1) CCR 68; 1995 (4) Crimes 695; JT 1995 (8) SC 520; 1995(6) SCALE 745; 1996 (1) SCJ 144; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 195

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder Reference, Mitigating Factor

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 368 - Should death penalty be awarded or not was question - Murder of five persons by accused including his wife and three minor children - Attempt to commit murder of two others - Brutal and heinous murders committed in cool and calculated manner while victims were asleep - Argued there was no provocation - Held, High Court was wrong in commuting imprisonment for life instead of death penalty as case of temporary psychic disorder was not acceptable - Appeal dismissed.
