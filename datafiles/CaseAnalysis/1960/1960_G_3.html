Â Gazula Dasaratha Rama Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1961 SC 564; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 63; 1961 (1) SCJ 310; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Descent, Madras Hereditary Village-Offices Act, 1895

Summary: Administrative - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Madras Hereditary Village Offices Act, 1895, s. 6(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Division of Village - Appointment as Village Munsif - Legality - Due to bifurcation of villages, RDO invited applications for post of village Munsif - Petitioner and respondent no.4 along with others had applied-Petitioner was appointed for said post - Aggrieved respondent no.4 and other unsuccessful applicants appealed before respondent no. 3, which was allowed and appointed respondent no.4 to said post - Petitioner appealed before respondent no.2 which was dismissed - Petitioner also appealed before respondent no.1 which was of no avail - Hence instant writ petition - Whether appointment of respondent no.4 u/s.6 of the Act was void and infringed the right of petitioner u/arts.14, 16 of the Constitution - Held, that if office in question was an office under the State, then art. 16 of Constitution in terms applies - Therefore, question was whether the office of Village Munsif was an office under the State was held in affirmative - Hence, orders of respondent nos. 1 to 3 in respect of appointment to post of Village Munsif in favour of respondent no. 4 was set aside and directed to concerned authorities to consider application of petitioner for said office on merits by on the footing that s. 6(1) of the Act infringes the right of citizens of India and was void u/art. 16 of Constitution - Petition allowed.
