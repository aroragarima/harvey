Travavancore Tea Company Limited Etc v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 June 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 128; (1980) 3 SCC 619; AIR 1980 SC 1547; 1980 KLT 568; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1388; 1980 UJ 774

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 -

Kerala Motor Vehicles (Taxation of Passengers and Goods) Act, 1963, ss. 3, 3(2), 5, 6 and 13 - Levy of tax - Exemption - Entitled to - Appellant company owned 17 motor vehicles, tractors, trailers and lorries all of which were registered in company's name under the 1939 Act - Lorry owned by appellant company was seized by police and taken into custody u/s. 13 of the 1963 Act - Appellant wrote to respondent Dept. stating that vehicle was used for agricultural purpose on private roads in Estates and appellant was not liable to pay tax and asked for release of vehicle - Respondent proceeded to prosecute appellant before Trial Court - Appellant filed petition before HC - HC dismissed the appeal and held that language in s. 3 of the 1963 Act showed that there was a departure from legislative policy of restricting tax liability only to vehicles using public roads - However, tax was imposed by s. 3 of the 1963 Act on all motor vehicles used or kept for use in State irrespective of any question as to whether they were used or kept for use on public roads or not - Hence, instant appeal - Whether on assumption that motor vehicles were used or kept for use within estate, and not intended to be used on public roads of State, the tax was leviable - Held, tax was only eligible on vehicles used or kept for use on public roads - In order to claim exemption from payment of tax requirements of s. 3(2) of the 1963 Act or ss. 5 and 6 of the 1963 Act should be satisfied - Surrender of registration certificate contemplated u/s. 5 of the Act was for making sure that motor vehicle was not being put to any use and did not had effect of annulling certificate of registration - If requirement contemplated under the 1963 Act was not satisfied registered owner or person m possession or control of vehicle should not be entitled to claim any exemption from payment of tax - However, the 1963 Act was enacted, the 1939 Act was amended by Act 10 of 1956 and amended definition on date when 1963 Act came into force exempted only motor vehicles which were of a special type adopted for use only in a factory or in any other enclosed premises - Appeals allowed.
