Priya Darshni Dental College & Hospital v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 217; (2011) 4 SCC 623; JT 2011 (3) SC 553; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 868; 2011(3) SCALE 543; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 945; 2011 (5) SLR 280

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Dental Council of India (Establishment of New Dental Colleges, Opening of New or Higher Course of Study or Training and Increase of Admission Capacity in Dental Colleges) Regulations, 2006

Summary: Education - Dentists Act, 1948, s. 10A(4) - Central Government granted permission to the petitioner college, u/s. 10A(4) of the Dentists Act for establishing a new Dental College from the academic year 2007-08 - Central Government renewed permission for the academic years 2008-09 and 2009-10 - Application for fourth year renewal permission for the academic year 2010-2011 was submitted before Dental Council of India (DCI) - Three-member Committee was constituted to give a personal hearing to them, as required under the first proviso to s. 10A(4) of the Act to consider the proposal for renewal of permission for the BDS Course for the academic year 2010-2011 - However, hearing was not granted and Central Government communicated its decision not to grant renewal permission to the Dental College for the academic year 2010-11 - Petition was filed thereagainst - HC remitted the petitioner's application for renewal of permission for 2010-2011, for re-consideration by the Central Government, by giving a due hearing to the petitioner - In pursuance of the said order, the three-member Committee gave a hearing to the petitioner college and thereafter, the Committee recommended the renewal of permission for the fourth year of BDS Course for the academic year 2010-11 - Accepting the recommendation, the Central Government sent a communication to the petitioner college granting renewal of permission subject to a condition that the institute obtained the permission of SC - In compliance with the direction of the Central Government, the petitioner college filed instant petition - Whether it was proper for the Ministry to issue an order granting renewal of permission with a condition that petitioner should seek approval of its order from SC, so as to 'regularize' its order accordingly? - Held, Central Government was bound to consider and pass orders granting or refusing permission in terms of s. 10A of the Act, taking note of the recommendations of DCI, by following the procedure prescribed by the Act and DCI regulations - Executive power of the Central Government to grant permission or renewal of permission u/s. 10A of the Act, was not subject to the control or supervision of SC, nor subject to confirmation or approval by SC - Neither SC, nor any other court, had any role to play in the decision making process relating to grant or refusal of permission under the Act, by the Central Government - Hence, a stipulation by an authority entrusted with the power to consider and grant permissions/recognitions, while granting such permission/recognition, that the petitioner college should seek and obtain order from a court, approving the grant of such permission/recognition, as a condition precedent to give effect to such grant, would be improper and irregular - Neither Central Government, nor the DCI, could shift the onus of decision making to the courts, blurring and obliterating the line of separation between the executive and the judiciary - Condition requiring approval of SC quashed - Writ petitions allowed.
