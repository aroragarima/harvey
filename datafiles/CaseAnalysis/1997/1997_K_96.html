Dena Bank v Kiritikumar T. Patel

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1677; (1999) 2 SCC 106; (1999) SCC (L&S) 466; AIR 1998 SC 511; 1998 (1) AWC 768; 1998 (78) FLR 45; JT 1997 (9) SC 167; 1998 LabIC 578; 1998 (1) LLJ 1; 1998 (1) LLN 375; 1998 LLR 1; 1997(7) SCALE 56; 1998 (1) SLR 69; 1998 (1) SLT 61; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 263; 1997 (9) Supreme 428

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17-B - Whether expression 'full wages last drawn' in s. 17-B of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 means wages drawn by a workman at time of termination of his employment or wages which he would have drawn on date of award? - S. 17-B, Parliament has also used words 'inclusive of any maintenance allowance admissible to him under any rule' - These words indicate that maintenance allowance that is admissible under any rule is required to be paid irrespective of amount which was actually being paid as maintenance allowance to workman - But with regard to wages, Parliament has used words 'full wages last drawn' indicating that wages that were actually paid and not amount that would be payable are required to be paid - Since payment is of such a character, Parliament thought it proper to limit it to extent of wages which were drawn by workman when he was in service and when his services were terminated and therefore used words 'full wages last drawn' - To read these words to mean wages which would have been drawn by workman if he had continued in service if order terminating his services had not passed since it has been set aside by award of Labour Court or Industrial Tribunal, would result in so enlarging benefit as to comprehend relief that has been granted under award that is under challenge - Since amount is not refundable or recoverable in event of award being set aside, it would result in employer being required to give effect to award during pendency of proceedings challenging award before High Court or Supreme Court without his being able to recover said amount in event of award being set aside - Held, words 'full wages last drawn' must be given their plain and material meaning and they cannot be given extended meaning - Court is unable to construe provisions contained in s. 17-B to cast such a burden on employer - Appeal allowed.
