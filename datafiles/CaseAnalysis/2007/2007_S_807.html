Â Sri Rajendra Singh Rana & Ors v Swami Prasad Maurya & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, K.G. Balakrishnan, H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 132; (2007) 4 SCC 270; AIR 2007 SC 1305; 2007 (3) ALJ 169; 2007 (6) AWC 6418; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 315; JT 2007 (3) SC 435; 2007(3) SCALE 64; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Constitution (Fifty-Second Amendment) Act, 1985, Suo Motu Cognizance

Summary: Constitution of India, art. 191 - Disqualification for membership of he Legislative Assembly - Whether on 27.3.2003 the 13 members who met the Governor with the request to invite the leader of the Samajwadi Party to form the Government had defected, on 27.8.2003 and whether they have established their claim that on 26.8.2003 there had been a split in the Bahujan Samaj Party and one third of the members of the Legislature of that party had come out of that party? - Held, it is necessary not only to show that 37 MLAs had separated but it is also necessary to show that there was a split in the original political party, the above finding necessarily leads to the conclusion that the 13 MLAs sought to be disqualified had not established a defence or answer to the charge of defection under paragraph 2 on the basis of paragraph 3 of the Tenth Schedule - Nothing is produced to show that there was a split in the original political party on 26.8.2003 as belatedly put forward or put forward at a later point of time - Very giving of a letter to the Governor requesting him to call the leader of the opposition party to form a Government by them itself would amount to their voluntarily giving up the membership of their original political party within the meaning of paragraph 2 of the Tenth Schedule - 13 members of BSP who met the Governor on 27.8.2003 stand disqualified in terms of Article 191(2) of the Constitution read with paragraph 2 of the Tenth Schedule thereof, with effect from 27.8.2003 - Appeals disposed of.
