Southern Petrochemicals Industries Corporation v Administrator of Specified Undertaking of Unit

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 971; (2007) 2 SCC 282; AIR 2007 SC 533; 2007 (7) AWC 6691; 2007 (2) BC 83; [2007] 135 Comp Cas 474; 2007 (1) DRTC 149; JT 2007 (1) SC 372; 2007 (145) PLR 656; 2006(14) SCALE 252; 2006 (7) SCJ 560; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 932

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Appointed Day, Unit Trust Of India Act, 1963, Industrial Reconstruction Bank Of India Act, 1984, Small Industries Development Bank Of India Act, 1989, Unit Trust of India (Transfer of Undertaking and Repeal) Act, 2002

Summary: Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Action against the appellant company by respondents 1 and 2 for recovery of debts due to them - Whether respondents 1 and 2, namely, Administrator of Specified Undertaking of Unit Trust of India and UTI Trustee Company Private Limited are "financial institutions"? - Held, vesting in the Administrator or the Specified Company is complete - Concept of mere vesting of management cannot be imported into the scheme of the Act - Administrator and Specified Company were therefore, fully authorized in law to recover the dues from the appellants as "financial institutions" - High Court was right in dismissing the writ petition preferred by the appellants challenging the jurisdiction of the Debts Recovery Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
