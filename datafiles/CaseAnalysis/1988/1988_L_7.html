Collector of 24 Parganas and Others v Lalith Mohan Mullick and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 653; (1988) Supp SCC 578; AIR 1988 SC 2121; JT 1988 (1) SC 598

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O 22 r. 4 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O.40, r.1 - Review - (A) Appeal decided on merits by SC - Review petition instituted on the plea that original respondents dying during pendency of appeal - Estate of the deceased was sufficiently represented before this Court - Plea that the appeal had abated and the judgment on merits requires to be set aside on this ground - Held, plea untenable Under the circumstances it is not possible to uphold the plea that the appeal had abated and the judgment on merits rendered by this Court on February 13, 1986 requires to be set aside on this ground - Petition dismissed.
