Des Raj v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1428; (2007) 12 SCC 494; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 262; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 810; 2007 (4) JCC 2974; JT 2007 (11) SC 516; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2972; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 133; 2007(10) SCALE 642; 2007 (8) SCJ 237; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning, Murder Reference, Mitigating Circumstance, Mitigating Factor, Bystanders

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 149, 307 and 114 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 354(3) and 34(2) - Appellant accused of murder - Convicted by Trial Court for murder and attempt to murder and sentenced to death - Appeal rejected by High Court and sentence of death upheld - Imposition of death sentence challenged - Whether courts were justified in imposing sentence of death? - Court has recognised that special reasons attracting death penalty may relate to manner of commission of murder, or motive for murder, abhorrent nature of crime or magnitude of crime or even personality of victim - Various guidelines have been outlined - Number of persons killed coupled with shocking nature of other features of crime, can furnish grounds for choice or punishment - Even if only one person is killed, gruesome or shocking nature of crime and/or motive for murder may make it rarest among rare cases deserving death penalty - Held, this case does not call for imposition of death sentence - This is not a murder to satisfy any greed or just - Action was impulsive and without premeditation arising out of a sudden quarrel - Repeated loading and firing in utter disregard for life is not an indication of extreme depravity or brutality, but of a drunken rage - Further held, this is not a rarest of rare case, which invites death penalty - Appeal partly allowed.
