Bank of Sharjah v Joplin Overseas Investment Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 140; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 18; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 836; 2015(2) SCALE 672

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Transfer petitions - Sale of vessel - 3 Admiralty suits were filed before HC of Bombay and 1 admiralty suit before HC of Gujarat by different plaintiffs with regard to M.V.Meem vessel (Vessel) plying flag of Panama and orders of arrest of vessel were passed in said suits - While so, respondent no. 1 filed applications before HC of Bombay in said admiralty suit seeking intervention and also vacation of order of arrest granted and later filed admiralty suit before HC of Gujarat - Further, Bank of Sharjah also filed admiralty suit before HC of Bombay claiming to be mortgagee of vessel and filed application for sale of vessel and the same was allowed - However, sale of vessel was deferred in view of application filed by plaintiff in admiralty suit filed before HC of Gujarat - Later, instant transfer petitions were filed, one by Bank of Sharjah for transfer of petition from HC of Gujarat to HC of Bombay and another by Joplin for transfer of petition from HC of Bombay to HC of Gujarat - Further, instant special leave petitions were by owner of vessel aggrieved by common order HC of Gujarat passed in appeals arising out of admiralty suits, wherein prayer of defendants for disposal of suits in terms of settlement arrived at between parties was rejected in view of petitions pending which are inter-linked - Whether directions could be passed for transfer of petitions from HC of Bombay to HC of Gujarat and for sale of vessel.

Held, though HC of Gujarat should have been the appropriate HC to hear all suits since the vessel has always been positioned in the territorial waters of India abutting the State of Gujarat and within the area of a Port over which the HC of Gujarat has territorial jurisdiction, yet, as there were various orders of arrest passed by HC of Bombay prior to the filing of suit by Joplin in HC of Gujarat and in view of the fact that Joplin filed caveat before HC of Bombay and took advantage of orders of arrest passed by HC of Bombay without raising any objection to the jurisdiction of HC of Bombay, and in view of the fact that the conduct of Joplin before HC of Bombay is found to be less than wholesome, it is appropriate to transfer all Admiralty suits pending in HC of Gujarat with regard to vessel in question to HC of Bombay. Hence, Registry of HC of Gujarat is directed to transfer records of said suits. Further, in view of precarious condition of vessel, directions were passed to HC of Bombay to sell vessel. Petitions disposed of.
