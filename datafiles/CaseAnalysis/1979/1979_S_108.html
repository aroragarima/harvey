Bachan Singh Etc. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 63; (1979) 4 SCC 754; (1980) SCC (Cr) 174; AIR 1980 SC 267; 1980 CRLJ 211; 1979 CrLR(SC) 651; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 645; 1979 UJ 811

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - IPC,1860,ss.148,149,302 and 304 - CrPC, 1973, ss.377,386,397 and 401 -Accused filed appeal against their conviction and sentence u/s.304 Part I r/w s.149, IPC,1860 before High Court - State filed appeal against acquittal u/s. 302, IPC,1860 and also a revision petition u/s. 401 CrPC for enhancement of sentence - Heard together by express order of High Court dismissing the appeal of the accused and accepting the State appeal for enhancement in part, enhanced the sentence of the accused under s.304 Part I r/w s.149, IPC,1860 - Whether High Court committed error of law in enhancing the sentence of the accused without giving reasonable opportunity of showing cause against such enhancement and without allowing them to plead for their acquittal or for reduction of the sentence as contemplated by s.377(3) of CrPC,1973 ? - Held, Although the State appeal was directed against the acquittal of the accused and not against the sentence u/s.377 (1) CrPC,1973 the High Court committed no illegality in not complying with the requirement of s.377(3) of CrPC,1973 for giving the opportunity to the accused of showing cause against the enhancement of the sentence or of pleading for their acquittal or for reduction of the sentence - Petition dismissed.
