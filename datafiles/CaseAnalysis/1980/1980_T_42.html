Â Tumati Venkaish Etc. Etc. v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Y.V. Chandrachud, V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 304; (1980) 4 SCC 295; AIR 1980 SC 1568; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 1143

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Urban Land Act, 1976 - Andhra Pradesh Land Reforms Act, 1973 - Andhra Pradesh Land Reforms Amendment Act 1977 - Amendment - Accommodation - Ultra vires - Legislative competence - A Legislature enacted 1973 Act - Petitioners contended that 1973 Act was constitutionally void and filed W.P. - Full Bench of HC declared that 1973 Act was constitutionally valid - A Legislature enacted 1977 Act with certain amendments and introduction of s. 41-A of 1977 Act - Petitioners contended that 1973 Act had become void and inoperative by enactment of 1976 Act - Petitioners contended Constitutional validity of Act and filed W.P. was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether Act enacted by A Legislature was constitutionally valid - Held, s.23 provides State for disposal of vacant land acquired under Act and it empowers State to allot that land to any person for purpose relating to or connection with industry or for providing residential accommodation approved by State to employees of any industry - It would be seen that 1976 Act imposes ceiling on holding of land in urban agglomeration other than land mainly used for purpose of agriculture - Urban agglomerations was not within legislative competence of A Legislature to provide for imposition of ceiling on land - It was evident that 1977 Act was with in legislative competence of A Legislature - Hence, it could not be accepted that 1977 Act was ultra vires and void as being outside area of legislative competence of A Legislature - 1977 Act was agrarian reform legislation and protected against challenge on ground of infraction of arts. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution by protective umbrella of art.31-A of Constitution - Appeals disposed of.
