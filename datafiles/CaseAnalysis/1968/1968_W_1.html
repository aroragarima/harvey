Â Sudhir Chandra Nawn v Wealth Tax Officer, Calicut, and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 271; AIR 1969 SC 59; [1968] 69 ITR 897; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246, Sch. VII, List I, Entry 86, Sch. VII List II Entry 49 - Wealth-tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(m), (e), 3 and 7 - Notice of demand - Proceedings for recovery of tax - Penalty - Writ petition against the Order of Assessment - Wealth tax - Legislative competency - (A) Whether Parliament had no power to legislate in respect of levy of wealth-tax in respect of lands and buildings? - Held, merely because in determining taxable quantum under taxing statutes made in exercise of power under entries 86, List I, and 49, List II, basis of valuation of assets is adopted, trespass on field of one legislative power over another may not be assumed - Parliament is competent to legislate in respect of levy of wealth-tax in respect of lands and buildings which may form part of assets of assessee - (B) Whether enactment of Wealth-tax Act by Parliament is ultra vires? - Held, power of Parliament to legislate in respect of a matter which is exclusively entrusted to it must supersede pro tanto exercise of power of State legislature - Further held, power to levy tax on lands and buildings under entry 49, List II, does not trench upon power conferred upon Parliament by entry 86, List I, therefore, enactment of Wealth-tax Act by Parliament is not ultra vires - (C) Whether s. 7 of Wealth-tax Act, 1957 is valid as there are no rules framed for valuation of a certain type of asset? - Held, s. 7 of Act of 1957 directs that valuation of any asset other than cash has to be made subject to rules - It does not contemplate that there shall be rules before an asset can be valued - Further held, failure to make rules for valuation of a type of asset cannot therefore affect vires of s. 7 of Act of 1957 - Petition dismissed.
