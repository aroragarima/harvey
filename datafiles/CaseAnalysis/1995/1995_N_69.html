Manohar Joshi v Nitin Bhaurao Patil And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1862; (1996) 1 SCC 169; AIR 1996 SC 796; AIR 1996 SCW 145; JT 1995 (8) SC 646; 1995(7) SCALE 30; 1996 (1) SCJ 359; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 421

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Decision Of The High Court

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 81(1), 83,86, 100 and 123 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 10 - CPC, 1908, O.6, r. 6 - (A) Election petition - Presentation - Limitation - Computation - Applicability of s.10 of 1897 Act - (B) Scope of s. 86 of 1951 Act - Presentation of election petition - Non compliance of s. 83 of 1951 Act - Whether a ground for dismissal of election petition u/s. 86 - (C) ss. 100,123, 83(1) of 1951 Act - Election - Corrupt practice - Consent of candidate - Any act done by leader of party of candidate - Whether 'Implied consent' of candidate can be read into ground in s. 100(1)(b) or definition of corrupt practice to dispense with clear pleading and proof of candidates or his election agent's consent - (D) Election - Corrupt practice - Use of word 'Hindutva' - Whether by itself invariably means Hindu religion - (E) Corrupt practice - General averments deficient in requisite pleadings of all constituent parts of corrupt practice - Whether constitutes a pleading of full cause of action - Whether to be ignored and struck out in accordance with O. 6, r. 16, CPC - (F) ss. 99, 98, 100 of 1951 Act - Election - Corrupt practice - Allegation of commission, by leaders of candidates - Whether absence of notices to them u/s. 99 vitiates final order made u/s. 98 by HC declaring election to be void - Held, (A) Is applicable - (B) To be construed strictly being a penal provision - Is no ground for dismissal of petition - (C) Cannot be read into ground in s. 100(1)(b) or definition of corrupt practice - (D) Does not mean Hindu religion - It is the context and the manner of its use which is material for deciding the meaning of words 'Hindutva' - (E) Does not constitute a pleading of full cause of action and, therefore, had to be ignored and struck out - (F) Vitiates final order - Appeal allowed.
