K. Kandaswamy v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 150; (1995) 6 SCC 162; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1361; AIR 1996 SC 277; 1995 (31) ATC 479; 1995 (2) CLR 996; JT 1995 (7) SC 80; 1995 LabIC 2709; 1996 (1) LLJ 979; 1996 (1) LLN 170; 1995(5) SCALE 439; 1995 (6) SLR 47; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - (A) Whether compulsory retirement amounts to dismissal from service - (B) Fundamental rules, r. 56(j) - Determination of arbitrariness of order of employee's compulsory retirement - When special report on integrity of employee stating that he sought permission to purchase Coffee Estate worth Rs. 25 lakhs and also information of unauthorised collections through his subordinate - Held, does not amount to dismissal - Not arbitrary on ground of being based on no evidence - Appeal dismissed.
