State of Tripura and Others v Sudhir Ranjan Nath

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 826; (1997) 3 SCC 665; AIR 1997 SC 1168; AIR 1997 SCW 1178; 1997 (3) CLT 416; JT 1997 (2) SC 530; 1997(2) SCALE 154; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 29; 1997 (2) Supreme 444; 1997 (1) UJ 563

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 301, 302, 304 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 41, 42, 76 - Union Territories (Laws) Act, 1950 (Act 30 of 1950) - Tripura Forest Transit Rules, r. 3 - Ultravires - Sustainability - HC declared r. 3 of Tripura Forest Transit Rules framed by State Govt. u/ss. 41 and 42 of the 1927 Act as illegal and ultravires Constitution - Correctness of said decision was challenged by State govt in instant appeal - Whether order of HC could be sustained - Held, Indian Forest Act was post-constitutional Parliamentary enactment insofar as Tripura was concerned for the reason that it had been extended to Tripura (which was then Union Territory) by the 1950 Act - SS. 41 and 76 of the 1927 Act were, therefore, laws within meaning of art. 302 of Constitution which empowered Parliament to impose such restrictions on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse between one State and another or within any part of the territory of India, as might be required in public interest - If ss. 41 and 76 of the 1927 Act were saved by art. 302, any rule made to carry out purposes of the said provisions should equally be protected by art. 302 of Constitution - Under cl. (2) to (6) of art. 19 of Constitution, it was held by SC that power to impose reasonable restrictions would include power to prohibit also in appropriate situations - Hence, r. 3 of Tripura Forest Transit Rules should not be said to be violative of art. 301 of Constitution nor was it required to comply with requirement of proviso to cl. (b) of art. 304 of Constitution - R. 3 of Tripura Forest Transit Rules, except sub-rule (5) thereof, was declared to be perfectly valid and effective - Judgment of HC was set aside, M/s. Krishan Lal Praveen Kumar v. State of Rajasthan, 1981 Indlaw SC 476, relied on - Appeal partly allowed.
