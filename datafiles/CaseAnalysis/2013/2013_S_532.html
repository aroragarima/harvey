The Rajasthan State Industrial Development And Investment Corporation And Investment Corporation v Subhash Sindhi Cooperative Housing Society Jaipur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 85; AIR 2013 SC 1226; 2013 (4) AWC 4110B; JT 2013 (3) SC 1; 2013 (5) MahLJ 61; 2013 (3) MPLJ 591; 2013(2) SCALE 434; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Lands (Restrictions on Transfer) Act, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 4, 4(1), 48 and 6 - Release of land - Challenged - Respondent filed appeals against order of the HC whereby HC had issued directions to the Rajasthan State Industrial Development and Investment Corporation (RIICO), the appellant herein, to release the land in dispute from land acquisition in favour of respondent No.1 - housing society - Whether appeals filed by the respondent against order of the HC could be allowed -

Held, society members had entered into an agreement to sell even though, a Notification u/s. 4 of Act to carry out acquisition had been issued by the Govt., fully knowing the legal consequences that may arise - Agreement to sell, made by the society, did not create any title in favour of the society - Acquisition proceedings were challenged after a decade of the issuance of Notification u/ss. 4, and 5 of Act years after the date of award, by the society alongwith original khatedars - Petitions in which the aforesaid acquisition proceedings were challenged were dismissed by the HC on the ground of delay and latches - Respondent society never made any application for release of the land on any ground whatsoever, before the Competent Authority i.e. Secretary to the Department of Industries, instead, it applied for regularization before the JDA and before the revenue authorities for conversion of user of the land - HC entertained the writ petition, without comparing the Actual facts of the respondent society qua other societies - There was no denial in specific terms as to whether the tenure holders had received compensation for the land in dispute, even though in the earlier proceedings, some khatedars were parties - Schemes floated by the State Govt. (knowing well that acquiring land after the issuance of s. 4 of Act Notification would be void), indicates a sorry state of affairs - Such orders have been passed without realizing that administration does not include mal-administration - Land had been acquired for industrial development and thus, could not be permitted to be used for residential purposes - Therefore, the demand of the respondent-society cannot be held to be justified - Appeals allowed.
