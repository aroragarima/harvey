Kollam Chandra Sekhar v Kollam Padma Latha

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 614; (2014) 1 SCC 225; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 131; 2013(6) ALL MR 428; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 51; 2013 (6) AWC 6389; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 1; 2013 (5) CTC 665; JT 2013 (12) SC 554; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 499; 2014 (3) MahLJ 547; 2014 (2) MPLJ 547; 2013 (2) OLR 967; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 655; 2013(11) SCALE 617; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: (1) Hindu Marriage Act, 1955-Sections 13 (1) (iii) and 9-Divorce-Appellant-husband made out ground for dissolution of marriage under Section 13 (1) (iii)-Question involved-Whether marriage between parties can be dissolved by granting decree of divorce on basis of one spouse's mental illness which included schizophrenia under Section 13 (1) (iii)-Contents of medical report do not support case of appellant-husband that respondent-wife suffering from serious case of schizophrenia, in order to grant decree of divorce under Section 13 (1) (iii)-Appellant-husband not proved fact of mental disorder of respondent-wife with reference to allegation against her that she has been suffering from schizophrenia by producing positive and substantive evidence on record-On the other hand, it has been proved by medical report that respondent-wife is in much better health condition and does not show signs of schizophrenia-Trial court erroneously came to conclusion that respondent-wife was suffering from schizophrenia by relying on evidence of appellant-husband-High Court rightly came to different conclusion that respondent-wife not suffering from ailment of schizophrenia or incurable unsoundness of mind-Appellant-husband cannot simply abandon his wife because she is suffering from sickness-Court cannot grant dissolution of marriage on basis of one spouse's illness-High Court rightly not granted decree of divorce-Decree for restitution of conjugal rights under Section 9 granted in favour of respondent-wife-Impugned judgment of High Court-Upheld.(2) Hindu Marriage Act, 1955-Section 13 (1) (iii)-Divorce-"Mental disorder" or "unsound mind" as ground available to party to get dissolution of marriage-Inability to manage his or her affairs is essential attribute of "incurably unsound mind"-Section 13 (1) (iii) does not make mere existence of mental disorder of any degree sufficient in law to justify dissolution of marriage.
