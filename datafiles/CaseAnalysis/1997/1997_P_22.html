Union of India and Another v P.V.Hariharan and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3082; (1997) 3 SCC 568; (1997) SCC (L&S) 838; JT 1997 (3) SC 569; 1997(2) SCALE 618; 1997 (1) SCJ 627; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1050; 1997 (2) SLJ 127; 1997 (2) SLR 232; 1997 (3) Supreme 121

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - CCS [CC and A] Rules, r. 6 - Classification of posts - Validity - Whether action of the Govt. to classify the posts without changing the pay scales are valid? - Yes - Pay scales are what are prescribed for each post by the Govt. which is very often done on the basis of recommendations of a Pay Commission or a similar expert body - Classification of posts has nothing to do with fixation of pay scales - It only classifies posts into several grounds based upon the pay scales already fixed - Classification and prescribing pay scales for several posts are two different and distinct functions - Held, any interference on the pay scale is unsustainable in law - Further held, change of pay scale of a category has cascading effect on several other categories similarly situated, as well as those situated above and below, put forward their claims on the basis of such change - Pay Commission is the proper authority to decide upon this issue - Interference of tribunal in pay matter is unwarranted - It would be in the fitness of the things if all matters relating to pay Scales, I.E. matters asking for a higher pay scale or an enhanced pay scale, as the case may be in one or the other ground, are heard by a Bench comprising at least one judicial member - Appeal allowed.
