Shri Baradakanta Mishra and another v Registrar of Orissa High Court and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 484; (1974) SCC (Cr) 128; (1974) 1 SCC 374; AIR 1974 SC 710; 1974 CRLJ 631; 1974 CrLR(SC) 124; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Advisor, Civil Liberties, Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Appellant started his career as a Munsif in 1947, and got promoted to the post of Subordinate Judge, and later to being an Additional District Magistrate - But due to his unsatisfactory work, he was reverted back to the post of Subordinate Judge, on 4-1-1963 - Order of reversion was challenged in a W.P., which was dismissed and later an appeal, which also got dismissed - Appellant was suspended from service on 9-4-1967, during the pendency of a disciplinary proceedings against him, which resulted in a light punishment, and appellant filed an appeal to the State Govt, which allowed the same - Appellant was promoted again to the post of Additional District Magistrate, on trial basis and due to vacancies further promoted - Appellant committed a grave misdemeanor and showed gross indiscretion, so he was reverted back to the post of Additional District Magistrate - Appellant was suspended pending disciplinary proceedings against him - Appellant addressed a letter to the Registrar intimating him that he would not submit any explanation to the charges framed against him until his representation to the Governor was disposed of, against which a show cause notice was issued for contempt - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the contempt of court was committed by the appellant - Held, that the appellant was an incorrigible contemnor, who had made it almost his latter, day professional occupation to cross the HC's path, had come in appeal - He had been reckless, persistent and guilty of undermining the HC's authority in his intemperate averments in both petitions - However, having regard to the fact that he was a senior judicial officer who had at some stage in his career displayed zeal and industry and was now in the sombre evening of an official career, a punishment short of imprisonment would had met the ends of justice and inspired in the public mind confidence in the justice administration by showing that even delinquent judges would be punished if they play with or pervert the due course of justice, as the contemnor had done - Fine of Rs. 1000/ with three months' imprisonment in default of payment would meet the ends of justice - Appeal dismissed.
