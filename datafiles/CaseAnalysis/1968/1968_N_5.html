V. Sanjeevaraya Mudaliar v N.A. Raghavachary

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1969 SC 435; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 90; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Madras City Tenants Protection Act 1921, s. 9 - (A) Whether tenant of a vacant site in backward of a residential house, a tenant of land within purview of Madras City Tenants' Protection Act, 1921? - Held, Act was passed with a view to give protection to tenants who in certain areas had constructed buildings on others' lands in hope that they would not be evicted so long as they paid fair rent for land - S. 9 provides that a tenant who is entitled to compensation u/s. 3 and against whom a suit in ejectment has been instituted may within prescribed time apply to Court for an order that landlord should be directed to sell whole or part of land for a price - (B) Whether having regard to proviso to s. 12 is such a tenant entitled to protection u/ss. 3 and 9 of Act in a case where he has erected buildings on land in contravention of an express stipulated in a registered lease? - Held, u/s. 3, a tenant on ejectment is entitled to be paid as compensation value of any building erected by him - A tenant entitled to compensation under s. 3, against whom a suit for ejectment has been instituted is entitled to purchase whole or part of land by invoking procedure under s. 9 - Registered lease deed also contained an express stipulation that appellant would not erect permanent structures of any kind on land so as to entitle him to claim in future value thereof - Effect of proviso to s. 12 is that nothing in Act affects stipulation u/ss. 3 and 9 are subject to and controlled by proviso to s. 12 - S. 3 provides that a tenant shall on ejectment be entitled to be paid as compensation value of any building erected by him - If tenant erects a permanent structure in contravention of stipulation he is not entitled to any compensation u/s. 3, as he is not entitled to any compensation u/s. 3 he cannot claim benefit of s. 9 - Appeal dismissed.
