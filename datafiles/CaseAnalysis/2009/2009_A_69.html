Chandrashekhar and Others v Additional Special Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 840; (2009) 14 SCC 441; AIR 2009 SC 3012; 2009 (76) ALR 489; 2009 (4) AWC 3242; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 817; JT 2009 (9) SC 1; 2009 (6) KarLJ 1; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 99; 2009 (108) RD 216; 2009(9) SCALE 434; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 505; 2009 (3) UPLBEC 2319

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - Compensation - Enhancement thereof - Acquisition of land for establishment of rehabilitation centre for the ousters of two villages submerged due to construction of Upper Krishna Project - Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) determining compensation @ Rs. 54, 500/- per acre - Civil Court in reference valued the lands @ Rs. 23/- per sq. ft. on basis of comparable lands acquired by LAO at such rate, and after further deductions held market value to be Rs. 17/- per sq. ft; final amount worked out, thus was Rs. 7, 40, 500/- per acre - In appeal High Court held market value of the lands to be more than Rs. 32.20/per sq. ft., but it restricted the amount to Rs. 23/- only, for the reason of deficit in the Court Fee paid by the appellants - (A) Submission was made that such restriction by the HC on the technical ground of deficit Court fees is fallacious - Support for submission was taken from 'Bhag Singh v. Union Territory of Chandigarh, (1985) 3 SCC 737' - Submission whether acceptable? - Held, Yes - Impugned judgment of HC perused - Once the Court has taken the view that the claimants were entitled to enhanced compensation they should not be denied the same on the mere technical ground of non payment of the Court fees and an opportunity must be given for payment of the same - (A) (i) Contention was raised by the respondent that decision in Bhag Singh's case has been overruled by the decision of the Court in 'Scheduled Caste Coop. Land Owning Society Ltd. v. Union of India [(1991) 1 SCC 174' - Contention whether acceptable? - No - Decisions perused - In Buta Singh v. Union of India [(1995) 5 SCC 283], Constitution Bench of the Court had affirmed Scheduled Caste case and made an opinion that decision in 'Chand Kaur v. Union of India [(1994) 4 SCC 663' was per incuriam the decision in Scheduled Caste case wherein Bhag Singh case was relied upon - Court concluded that decision of the Constitution Bench in Buta Singh's case has not reversed the decision in Bhag Singh's case and the law laid down in 'Scheduled Caste' case is materially different from the law established by the Court in Bhag Singh's case since both the decisions dealt with different matters and moreover the Scheduled Caste decision has in fact recognized the validity of the law laid down in Bhag Singh's case - (B) Further submission was made showing in evidence the earlier judgement of HC wherein it had awarded a higher rate of compensation for the lands in the vicinity - Compensation was awarded @ Rs. 100.50/- per sq. ft. - Decision in 'Pal Singh v. UT of Chandigarh, AIR 1993 SC 225' was relied upon for the submission - Submission whether acceptable? - Held, No - Position of law as per Pal Singh's case is that, 'a judgement of a Court in a land acquisition case determining the market value of a land in the vicinity of the acquired lands, even though not inter partes, could be admitted in evidence either as an instance or one from which the market value of the acquired land could be deduced or inferred..' - Court noted further requirement that it must have been proved by the person relying upon such judgment by adducing evidence aliunde - Materials submitted perused - Appellants had failed to satisfactorily furnish the basis for determining the market value of the acquired land according to the decision of the same HC - Impugned decisions of HC on the determination of the market value of the lands of the appellants, set aside - Market value fixed at Rs. 32.10/-per sq. ft. - Rest of the decision of HC affirmed - Appropriate directions given - Appeal partly allowed.
