Amteshwar Anand v Virender Mohan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1256; (2006) 1 SCC 148; AIR 2006 SC 151; 2006 (1) ALD(SC) 84; 2005 (124) DLT 588; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 147; JT 2005 (9) SC 559; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 485; 2005(8) SCALE 359

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appurtenant Land, Ex-Parte, Ground For Setting Aside

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23, r. 3, O. 22, r. 10 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17(1) - Whether disputes between parties were set at rest by a valid consent decree D/- 25-08-1993 disposing of Suit No. 63 of 1975 and Suit No. 1495 of 1989? - Findings of fact arrived at concurrently by courts below do not require interference by this Court - Both courts below had concurrently found that parties had enjoyed material benefits under agreements - Held, final decree D/ -25-08-1993 was on basis of all three agreements each of which had been signed by concerned parties and their statements in support thereof were on record in compliance with O. 23, r. 3 CPC - Appeals dismissed.
