Â Municipal Council, Khurai and Another v Kamal Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 230; AIR 1965 SC 1321; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 653

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, ss. 71(v), 78, 92, 134, 134(1), 135 136, 137, 138, 138(2), 140, 323, 323(2), 327(1), 140(1),141 and 146 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 265 - Whether assessment list of house tax and conservancy tax confirmed by Municipal Council, Khurai, at a special meeting on 24-02-1964 is effective or is liable to be quashed on ground that it was not made in accordance with provisions of Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961? - Assessment list which has to be published u/s. 136 of Act must contain full and accurate particulars specified in s. 134(1) of Act - Amongst those particulars are following: (1) Valuation of property based on capital or annual letting value, as case may be, on which property is assessed; (2) Rate, of tax applicable; (3) Amount of tax assessed thereon - U/art. 265 of Constitution no tax shall be levied or collected except by authority of law - Procedure for imposing liability to pay a tax has to be strictly complied with - Where it is not so complied with liability to pay tax cannot be said to be according to law - Objections which assessees had filed in pursuance of notification actually published by Chief Municipal Officer were based upon list published u/s. 136 and not in pursuance of what liability would be under Resolution of Municipal Council - Therefore, it cannot he said that opportunity as contemplated by Act was at all given to assessees for lodging their objections as required by s. 137 of Act - Held, assessment list authenticated by Chief Municipal Officer was not prepared according to law and, therefore, provisions of s. 141 were not available to Council - Appeal dismissed.
