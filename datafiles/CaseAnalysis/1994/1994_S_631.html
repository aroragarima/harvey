Manakshi Bala v Sudhir Kumar

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1640; (1994) 4 SCC 142; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1181; 1994 (3) CCR 561; 1994 (2) Crimes 970; JT 1994 (4) SC 158; 1994(2) SCALE 973; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 1008; 1994 (2) UJ 97

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Prize Chit and Money Circulation Scheme (Banning) Act, 1978, s. 3 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 239, 240 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 498A - Criminal breach of trust and cruelty by husband or relatives - High Court quashed entire proceedings against accused persons - Whether HC correct in quashing proceedings? - If charges are framed in accordance with s. 240 CrPC, 1973 on a finding that a prima case has been made out as has been done in the instant case - Persons arraigned may, if he feels aggrieved, invoke revisional jurisdiction of HC or Sessions Judge - Once charges are framed u/s. 240 CrPC, 1973 HC in its revisional jurisdiction would not be justified in relying upon documents other than those referred to in ss. 239 and 240 CrPC, 1973; nor would it be justified in invoking its inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 to quash the same except in those rare cases where forensic exigencies and formidable compulsions justify such a course - In such exceptional cases HC can look into only those documents which are unimpeachable and can be legally translated into relevant evidence - Held, HC wrongly quashed the proceedings because chargesheet was already submitted and even charges were framed against accused persons - If accused persons had any grievance regarding framing of charges proper way was to move revision - Matter remanded back to HC for disposal as per law - Appeal allowed.
