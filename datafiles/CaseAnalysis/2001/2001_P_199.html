Patangrao Kadam v Prithviraj Sayajirao Yadav Deshmukh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20259; (2001) 3 SCC 594; AIR 2001 SC 1121; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 99; JT 2001 (3) SC 167; 2001(2) SCALE 227; 2001 (1) SCJ 654; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 118; 2001 (2) SLT 521; 2001 (2) Supreme 492

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contesting Candidate, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Any Other Candidate, Purity

Summary: Election - Non-compliance of Mandatory provisions - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 116-A, 79(b), 82, 82(a), 82(b), 100(1)(b), 100(1)(d)(ii), 100(1)(d)(iv), 123(4) and 99(1)(a)(ii) - Appellant filed Election Petition impugning election of respondent 1 - In election petition, allegations were made against respondent 1, his agents, supporters and his election agent - Preliminary objection that election petition was not maintainable for non-compliance with mandatory provisions of ss. 81 and 83 - A validly nominated candidate, had not been made a party to election petition - Election petition was dismissed - (A) Whether High Court was correct in dismissing petition by upholding preliminary objection raised by respondent No. 1? - From plain and unambiguous language and terms of ss. 79(b) and 82(b) without anything more, had to be necessarily joined in as a respondent - S. 82 speaks of all three categories, contesting candidates, returned candidates and any other candidate - Merely because he became an election agent of respondent 1 subsequently after withdrawal of his candidature, he did not cease to be a candidate within meaning of s. 79(b) - S. 82 is mandatory in relation to joining of respondents - S. 86(1) does not leave any option to HC but to dismiss an election petition for non-compliance with ss. 81, 82 and 117 - Since serious consequences follow against a candidate within meaning of s.79(b) indulging in corrupt practices in an election, principles of natural justice do demand of providing an opportunity to such a candidate - (B) CPC, 1908, O. 1, r. 10, O. 6, r. 17 - Amendment to petition - Whether can be allowed? - When Act makes a person a necessary party and provides that petition shall be dismissed if such a party is not joined, power of amendment or to strike out parties cannot be used at all - CPC, 1908, applies subject to provisions of Representation of People Act and any rules made thereunder - Held, when Act enjoins penalty of dismissal of petition for non-joinder of a party provisions of CPC, 1908, cannot be used as curative means to save petition - Appeal dismissed.
