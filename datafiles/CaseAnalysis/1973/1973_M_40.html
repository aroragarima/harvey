Messrs Kanpur Vanaspati Stores, Kanpur v Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 530; (1973) 4 SCC 110; AIR 1973 SC 2373; 1973 (2) CTR 289; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 424; 1973 (32) STC 655; 1973 TAXLR 2427

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 3A and 9, Proviso - Levy of tax - (A) Vanaspati selected for single point levy - Levy imposed on successive dealers - Importer, whether a successive dealer - (B) meaning of expression 'tax admitted' in s. 9, Proviso - Admission to be considered is one made before assessing authority and not which is made before appellate authority - Appeal filed without paying tax admitted - Maintainability - Held, importer is a successive dealer - Appeal is not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
