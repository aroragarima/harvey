Bapubhai Mohanbhai and another v Mahila Sahakari Udyog Mandir

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 360; (1975) 2 SCC 492; AIR 1975 SC 2128; 1975 RCJ 616; 1975 RCR(Rent) 733; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 411; 1975 UJ 741

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12(1), 13(1)(g), 15 and 25 - Whether High Court has rightly taken view that s. 13(1)(g) is not subject to s. 25 and therefore question whether requirement of landlord is reasonable and bona fide has to be decided apart from provisions of s. 25? - Courts ought not to construe a statute in a manner which will encourage breach of any of its provisions and, most certainly, a decree ought not to be passed which, if honored, will attract penal consequences - To pass a decree in favour of respondent on grounds accepted by HC is to invite respondent to commit a breach of statutory injunction contained in s. 25(1) - Though evidence led by respondent is sufficient to prove that it requires suit premises for purpose of its business, no decree for possession can be passed in its favour as its requirement cannot be said to be reasonable - Requirement runs across a statutory prohibition and is therefore not reasonable - Held, order of HC set aside - Suits filed by respondent against appellants stand dismissed - Appeal allowed.
