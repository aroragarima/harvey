Delhi Development Authority v Anant Raj Agencies Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 267; AIR 2016 SC 1806; 2016 (4) AWC 3688; 2016 (4) CalLT 75; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 141; 2016 (229) DLT 197; JT 2016 (4) SC 1; 2016 (1) RCR(Rent) 464; 2016(4) SCALE 80; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 787; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Second Appeal, Lease Deed, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, Public Premises (Eviction Of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971, Delhi Development Act, 1957, Unauthorised Occupant, Delhi Development Authority (Disposal of Developed Nazul Land) Rules, 1981, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Delhi Development Act, 1957, ss. 21, 22 - Delhi Development Authority (Disposal of Developed Nazul Land) Rules, 198, r. 43 - Termination of lease - Grant of third party interest after termination - Right of third party - It is stated that appellant authority had granted property in question on lease to original lessee for specified period, however, it is stated that after expiry of lease period, appellant terminated lease agreement on ground of alleged breach of conditions - Further, it is stated that original lessee after termination of lease agreement, created third party right in favor of respondent by way of sale of property in question - However, lower Court passed order in favor of original lessee which was confirmed by appellant Court - Further, HC vide impugned order held that appellant had accepted rent after expiry of lease period which amount to renewal of lease - Whether impugned order passed by lower Court against appellant authority is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, original lessee was given time to remedy breaches, however, original lessee failed to rectify breaches notified to him. Hence, appellant authority terminated lease of property in question. Further, Shanti Prasad Devi and Another v Shankar Mahto and Others 2005 Indlaw SC 401 relied on, where it was held that it is necessary to obtain assent of landlord for continuation of lease after expiry of lease period and mere acceptance of rent by lessor, in absence of agreement to contrary, for subsequent months where lessee continues to occupy lease premises cannot be said to be conduct signifying assent on its part. Hence, SC held that as per cl. of lease deed and ss. 21, 22 of Ac and r. 43 of Rules, there cannot be automatic renewal of lease in favour of original lessee once it stands terminated by efflux of time and also by issuing notice terminating lease. Further, with respect to right of respondent in property in question, SC held that sale of property in question to give effect to compromise decree in suit is void-ab-initio in law as original lessee, in absence of renewal of lease in his favour himself had no right, title or interest, at time of execution of sale deed, in respect of property in question. Hence, sale deed is not binding on appellant authority. Further, in view that original lessee and respondent were in unauthorized possession of property, SC held that they are liable to pay damages to appellant authority and appellant authority is directed to take possession of property. Impugned order passed by lower Courts against appellant authority is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In case of breach of conditions of agreement, aggrieved party is justified to terminate agreement and occupation of property after said termination is unauthorized.
