Heinz India Pvt. Ltd. & Anr. v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 102; (2012) 5 SCC 443; 2012 (92) ALR 685; 2012 (4) AWC 3662; JT 2012 (3) SC 534; 2012 (116) RD 543; 2012(3) SCALE 607; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 898; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 888; [2012] 50 VST 13

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, Citizenship Rules, 1956, Rebuttable Presumption, U.P. Large Land Holdings Tax Act, 1957, Bihar Taxation on Passengers and Goods (Carried by Public Service Motor Vehicles) Act, 1961, Travancore-Cochin Land Tax Act, 1955, Andhra Pradesh Land Revenue (Additional Assessment) and Cess Revision Act, 1962

Summary: Trade - Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, s. 17 (iii), 32 - Assessment of Mandi Fee - Presumption - Exemption - Sustainability - Appellant was manufacturer of milk products and energy beverages and it also produced ghee as by-product - Specified agricultural produce within Mandi limits attracted levy of Mandi Fee as per s. 17(iii) of Act - Appellant resisted on ground that bulk of ghee produced in unit was sent out of Mandi limits on stock transfer basis and there was no sale involved so as to attract levy of Mandi Fee - SC, had observed in earlier cases that as per s. 17(iii) of Act there was a presumption open to Mandi Samiti to raise demands against dealers before issue of passes, If there is a valid rebuttal to the presumption and it is shown that no sale took place within the notified market area the dealers will be entitled to the passes, otherwise not - Appellant claimed refund of amount paid towards market fee and furnished documents - Mandi Samiti rejected claim concluding that document produced were not sufficient to rebut statutory presumption - Aggrieved appellant filed revision petitions before Director, Mandi Parishad u/s. 32/33 of Act, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellants filed Writ Petition, HC allowed and remitted matter back to Director for fresh consideration and disposal accordance with law - Directed held that claim of the companies for refund remained unsubstantiated and presumption un-rebutted - Appellant filed Writ Petitions, HC concurred decision of director and dismissed writ petitions - Aggrieved instant appeal - (A) Whether Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam did not contain necessary machinery provisions for assessment of fees and for adjudication of disputes in relation thereto - Held, - power to decide revisions vested with Board in terms of s. 32 who also enjoyed power to delegate that function to Director - So long as there was statutory sanction for Director to exercise revisional power vested in Board, any argument that such a delegation was either impermissible or did not serve purpose of providing a suitable machinery for adjudication of disputes should have to be rejected - R. 133-A of the Rules framed under the Act regulated filing and disposal of revision petitions u/s. 32 thereof - R. 133-A empowered Board either to decide revision petition itself or to nominate an officer for doing so - R. 133-A also provided for grant of an opportunity of being heard to person concerned and a time bound disposal of revision - Hence, r. 133-A was a step in direction of providing a machinery under the Act for adjudication of disputes that would arise between dealers on one hand and market committee on the other - Thus, the Act was not completely bereft of a machinery - In order to make Board's revisional power more effective and its exercise more transparent and credible, Board would do well to delegate power of hearing and disposal of revision petitions to a senior and experienced officer who was well-versed in dealing with legal issues concerning assessment and/or determination of liability under Act - (B) What precisely was correct legal standard/test for determining whether or not presumption arising under Explanation to s. 17(iii) of Act had been rebutted - Held, while amount payable was not a tax it was nevertheless a statutory levy which was attracted moment transaction of sale took place within market area - Circumstances in which transactions, which statute presumed to be sales, but which appellants claim were simple transfer of stocks were within exclusive knowledge of appellants - Entire evidence relevant to transactions, being available only with appellants and true nature of transactions being within their special knowledge, there was no reason why rebuttal evidence should not satisfy higher standard of proof and clearly and convincingly established that fact presumed was not actual fact - Evidence intended to rebut statutory presumption u/s. 17 of Act ought to be clear and convincing evidence showing that what was presumed under provision was not real fact - (C) Whether orders passed by Mandi Utpadan Samiti and that passed by Director, as delegate of Mandi Parishad, suffer from any legal infirmity to call for interference - Held, Mandi Samiti appreciated each piece of evidence and found same to be insufficient to hold that sale transactions had, in fact, taken place outside mandi area so that presumption arising u/s. 17(iii) of Act stood rebutted - Director exercising powers of Mandi Parishad had evaluated evidence and concurred with view taken by Mandi Samiti - A remand was neither mechanical nor a routine affair, if there was nothing wrong in orders under challenge, there was no question of interference - Appeal dismissed.
