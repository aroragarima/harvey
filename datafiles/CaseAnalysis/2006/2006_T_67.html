United India Insurance Company Limited, Shimla v Tilak Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 233; (2006) 4 SCC 404; 2006 (2) ACC 1; AIR 2006 SC 1576; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 75; 2006 (63) ALR 462; 2006 (2) AWC 2015; 2006 (2) CTC 661; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2379; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 9; JT 2006 (4) SC 280; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 422; 2006 (143) PLR 297; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 168; 2006 (3) RLW 1781; 2006(4) SCALE 67; 2006 (4) SCJ 596; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988,s. 157 - Motor Vehicle Act, 1939,s. 103-A - General clauses Act, 1897 - English Road Traffic Act, 1960 - Whether a statutory insurance policy under Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, intended to cover risk to life or damage to properties of third parties, would cover risk of death or injury to a gratuitous passenger carried in a private vehicle? - Held, situation in law which arises from failure of transferor to notify insurer of fact of transfer of ownership of insured vehicle is no different, whether u/s. 103-A of 1939 Act or u/s. 157 of 1988 Act in so far as liability towards a third party is concerned - It is not transfer of vehicle but accident which furnishes cause of action for application before Tribunal - Undoubtedly, accident took place after 1988 Act had come into force - Hence it is 1988 Act which would govern situation - Contention of appellant-Insurance Company that it owed no liability towards injuries suffered by deceased Rajinder Singh who was a pillion rider, as insurance policy was a statutory policy, and hence it did not cover risk of death of or bodily injury to gratuitous passengers - Appeal allowed.
