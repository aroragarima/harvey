Chatti Konati Rao & Ors. v Palle Venkata Subba Rao

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1056; AIR 2011 SC 1480; 2011 (4) AWC 4091; JT 2010 (13) SC 578; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 824; 2010(13) SCALE 173

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Adverse possession - Proof of - Plaintiffs (legal heirs of predecessor-in-title) handed over suit property to defendants for management - Plaintiffs, subsequently, sought to redeem the possession, however, defendants denied plaintiffs' title and possession over the suit property - Plaintiffs filed suit for recovery of possession and for mense profit against defendants - Trial Court dismissed the suit holding that defendants had perfected title over the suit property by adverse possession as well as a sale allegedly executed by the predecessor-in-title in favour of defendants - HC, in appeal, reversed the findings of the Trial Court and allowed the suit - Hence the present appeal by defendants - Whether defendants had perfected their title by adverse possession - Held, defendants failed to prove that they had perfected their title by adverse possession - Initial plea of the defendants was that they had purchased the property from the predecessor-in-title, alternatively by virtue of agreement to sale they came in possession of the property, however, such plea was not substantiated - Neither the purported sale deed nor agreement to sale had been placed on record of the Court - Defendants are required to prove that their possession was adverse to the true owner, however, defendants have not proved the necessary ingredients to establish their title by adverse possession - Therefore, HC is absolutely right in rejecting defendants' plea of adverse possession and decreeing plaintiff's suit, after setting aside the judgment and decree of the Trial Court - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
