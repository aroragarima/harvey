Kuria and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 400; (2012) 10 SCC 433; AIR 2013 SC 1085; 2012 CRLJ 4707; 2012 (4) JCC 2644; JT 2012 (9) SC 296; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 108; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 364 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Common intention - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) along with other accused persons allegedly murder deceased and committed offences u/ss. 302, 364 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ ss. 302, 364 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that there was contradiction between ocular and medical evidence and that evidence of PW1 and 5 was doubtful - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified - Held, it was difficult to state any hard and fast rule which could be applied universally to all cases - It would always depend on facts and circumstances of given case whether person involved in the commission of crime with a common intention could be held guilty of the main offence committed by them together - Provisions of s. 34 of IPC come to the aid of law while dealing with cases of criminal act and common intention - In instant case, large number of persons had attacked one person - Witnesses could not be expected to explain the role in the inflicting of injuries by each one of them individually and weapons used - Such conduct would be opposed to normal conduct of a human being - Fear for his own life and anxiety to save the victim would be so high and bothersome to the witness that it would not only be unfair but also unfortunate to expect such a witness to speak with precision with regard to injuries inflicted on the body of deceased and the role attributable to each of the accused individually - In instant case, result of blunt injuries was evident from the report of post mortem (Exhibit P/11), ribs of deceased were broken and they had punctured the lungs - Pleural cavities were full of blood and his body was dragged causing injuries on his back - In those circumstances, some blood would but naturally ooze out of the body of deceased and his clothes would be blood stained - Post mortem report, inquest report, statements of PW2, PW3, PW4, PW7 and PW15 were in line with each other and there was no noticeable conflict between them - Injuries on the body of deceased were so severe that they alone could be cause of death and statement of PW6 in relation to cause of death was definite and certain - No doubt, the eye witnesses stated that neck of deceased was broken, while according to other witnesses, it was lying in a twisted condition - According to post mortem report and statement of PW6, there were bruises on the entire back including shoulders - However, no apparent external injury was noticed on the neck of deceased - But after dissecting the neck, doctor (PW6) came to know that there was swelling in the neck muscles and hard bone edges had fractures which were prior to the death of deceased - In inquest report (Exhibit P/2), when IO examined the neck of deceased, he noticed that neck was not stable and was loosely turning both sides with external aid - That clearly shows that neck of deceased was badly injured and even had a fracture - It was obvious that there was no contradiction between statement of witnesses and medical evidence even in that regard - According to doctor cause of death, was as a result of injuries to both lungs and pleural cavity being full of blood which caused pressure on neck, causing the deceased to suffocate - Doctor was subjected to cross-examination but nothing material was found - Eye account given by witnesses fully finds support from statement of IO, inquest report, post-mortem report and recoveries effected from the place of occurrence including the blood stained earth and wood from the door of house of accused - All witnesses were present at the place of occurrence and their statements were reliable - Ingredients of s. 34 of IPC were fully satisfied in instant case - Undoubtedly, all the accused had committed criminal acts punishable under the provisions of IPC - They had done so with common intention, as was evident from statement of witnesses and documents on record - Impugned order of conviction was justified - Appeal dismissed.
