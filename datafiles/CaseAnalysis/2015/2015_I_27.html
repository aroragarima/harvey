Bhandari Udyog Limited v Industrial Facilitation Council and another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 2015 SC 1320; 2015 (2) AWC 1961; JT 2015 (2) SC 439; 2015 (2) MLJ 621; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 918; 2015(2) SCALE 608; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 498; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 183

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 34, 42 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 20 - Arbitration Award - Jurisdiction - Appellant filed an application u/s. 11 of the Act before Karnataka HC for appointment of Arbitrator, which was allowed - Thereafter, Arbitration Award was challenged by respondent no. 2 (company) before District Court, at 'L', which decided the jurisdiction to entertain the application u/s. 34 of the Act - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before Bombay HC, which was dismissed holding that Chief Justice of HC dealing with an application u/s. 11 of the Act was not a Court, and that no application was filed in any Court prior to the filing of application u/s. 34 of the Act and that goods were supplied at 'L', it is the District Court at 'L' which had jurisdiction to entertain application u/s. 34 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Bombay HC correctly decided the jurisdiction of a Court to entertain application u/s. 34 of the Act.

Held, arbitration proceeding has been conducted within the jurisdiction of 'R' District Court, which has jurisdiction as per s. 20 of CPC and is subordinate to Karnataka HC, which entertained application u/s. 11 of the Act. Hence, the Award cannot be challenged before a Court subordinate to Bombay HC. Exercise of jurisdiction by such Court shall be against the provision of s. 42 of the Act. The District Court at 'L' and Bombay HC committed error of law in entertaining the application u/s. 34 of the Act and dismissing the revision petition. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
