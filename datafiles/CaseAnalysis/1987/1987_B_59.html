Bhairab Chandra Nandan v Ranadhir Chandra Dutta

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28134; (1988) 1 SCC 383; AIR 1988 SC 396; 1988 (1) RCJ 143; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 48; 1987(2) SCALE 1391

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 13, 14 and 13(1)(ff) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 14, r. 1, O. 41, r. 24 and s. 100 - Appeal by special leave u/art. 136 - Eviction proceedings - (A) Grounds, subletting and bona fide need of landlord - Findings in favour of landlord by Lower Courts on basis of evidence - Validity of interference by High Court - (B) Partly self occupied and partly let house - Additional accommodation - Applicability of condition imposed in s. 13 of Act - (C) Remand of appeal - Availability of alternative suitable accommodation when Trial Court not framing any issue and still parties went to trial and adduced evidence with this issue in mind - Landlord occupied another house also - Held, was unjustified - Condition may not be strictly applied - Further held, there was no need for appeal being remanded for a finding on that question - Appeal allowed.
