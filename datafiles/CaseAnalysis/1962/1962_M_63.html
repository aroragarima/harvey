Â Ram Sarup v Munshi and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 496; AIR 1963 SC 553; 1963 (65) PLR 531; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 858

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, ss. 14, 15 - Adaptation of Laws (Third Amendment) Order, 1951 - Punjab Alienation of Land Act, 1900, s. 2 - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f) - Punjab Act, s. 10 of 1960 - (A) Whether appeal No. 214 ought to be dismissed as having been abated on death of first appellant without his LRs. being brought on record? - Held, there can be no partial pre-emption because pre-emption is substitution of pre-emptor in place of vendee and if decree in favour of pre-emptors in respect of share of deceased has become final it is manifest that there would be two conflicting decrees if appeal should be allowed and a decree for pre-emption insofar as appellant s. 2 - 5 are concerned is interfered with; where a decree is a joint one and a part of decree has become final by reason of abatement, entire appeal must be held to be abated - (B) Whether rights conferred upon pre-emptor is an unreasonable restriction on right of vendors 'to hold and dispose of property' and of prospective vendees 'to acquire property' guaranteed by art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution and whether restriction imposed is reasonable and in interest of general public within art. 19(5)? - Further held, general question about impact of right conferred by art. 19(1)(f) on right of pre-emption has been dealt with exhaustively in judgment of this court in Bhau Ram v Baij Nath [1962 Indlaw SC 540]; grounds for upholding s. 15(a) as reasonable and in interest of general public resolve into two grounds : (i) to preserve integrity of village and village community; and (ii) to implement agnatic rule of succession; that next in succession should have chance of retaining property in family, would suffice to render restriction reasonable and in interest of general public within art. 19(5); u/s. 15, particularly after 1960 amendment, right of pre-emption is confined to members of family of vendor, i.e.,those who would have succeeded to property in absence of any alienation - (C) Whether claim of pre-emption of certain agricultural land in appeal based on s. 15(c) 'thirdly' valid? - Further held, case is clearly covered by judgment of this Court in Bhau Ram v Baij Nath [1962 Indlaw SC 540] where Court upheld validity of right of pre-emption granted u/Chap. XIV of Berar Land Revenue Code (Appeal 430 of 1958); an 'estate' is an unit of assessment and there is a joint and several liability on persons owning land within 'estate' to pay entire assessment due on estate, thus though it is not really case of a co-sharer, it is somewhat akin to that of a co-sharer because of joint liability for payment of land revenue, therefore, restriction on right of a vendor in such a case is a reasonable one and not repugnant to art. 19 - (D) Whether by reason of amendment in law, respondent is entitled to benefit of decree which he obtained under previously existing enactment? - That s. 31 is plainly retrospective and that it affects rights to pre-emption which had accrued before coming into force of Amending Act is not in controversy for s. 31, in plain terms, makes substantive provisions of enactment applicable to suits whether instituted 'before or after' commencement of Amending Act - Further held, in present case, language used in s. 31 is plain and comprehensive so as to require an appellate court to give effect to substantive provisions of Amending Act whether appeal before it is one against a decree granting pre-emption or one refusing that relief - Appeals No. 214 of 1961 dismissed.
