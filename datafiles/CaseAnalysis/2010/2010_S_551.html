Leela Gupta and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110-A - Compensation - Deceased-Officiating Executive Engineer, aged 39 years, killed in a motor accident - Claimants (Wife and children of deceased) filed a claim petition u/s. 168 of Act, 1988 claiming compensation in the sum of Rs. 7,00,000/- Tribunal held that deceased would have contributed Rs. 2,200/- per month (Rs. 26,400/- per year) to the family and by applying a multiplier of 18, reached the finding that the pecuniary loss to widow and children would be Rs. 4,75,200/- up to the age of his retirement - Tribunal then deducted 1/3rd of the above considering the amount being paid in lump sum and uncertainty in life and by further deducting a sum of Rs. 40,000/- towards group insurance scheme, assessed compensation to the extent of Rs. 2,76,800/- - As amount of Rs. 15,000/- had already been paid to the claimants, tribunal held that claimants were entitled to a sum of Rs. 2,61,800/- and directed the respondents to pay the said amount with pendente lite and future interest thereon @ 9% per annum - HC reduced 1/3rd compensation assessed after ascertainment of multiplicand capitalized with the multiplier of 16 and held that the claimants were entitled to Rs. 4,70,000/- as compensation along with 9% simple interest per annum - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was in error in reducing by 1/3 rd the compensation assessed after ascertainment of multiplicand capitalized with the multiplier of 16? - Held, HC was in error in reducing by 1/3rd the compensation assessed after ascertainment of multiplicand capitalized on a particular multiplier since the very method of ascertainment of multiplicand takes into consideration many factors of imponderables and the contingencies of the future - Once the multiplicand and multiplier are ascertained, the assessment of damages to compensate the dependants is arrived at by multiplying the two and no further deduction needs to be made towards uncertainties and other contingencies - Capitalization of multiplicand on a multiplier of 16 is on the higher side and multiplier of 14 in the facts of the case one would meet the ends of justice - Hence, appellants become entitled to Rs. 6,04,800/- as compensation with 9% simple interest per annum - Order accordingly.
