Sakshi v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 466; (2004) 5 SCC 518; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1645; AIR 2004 SC 3566; 2004 (98) CutLT 491; 2004 (112) DLT 457; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 405; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 702; 2004(6) SCALE 15; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 723

Case Digest

Subject: Women & Children

Keywords: Child Abuse, Woman, Sexual Abuse, Offences against the Person Act, 1861, Aliens Control Act, 1991, United Nations Convention On the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women, 1979 (ENG), Sexual Offences (Amendment) Act, 1976

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 20, 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 377, 354, 376 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 167, 273, 327 - United Nations Convention On the Elimination of All Forms of Discrimination Against Women, 1979 - Public interest litigation - Definition of rape, cannot be enlarged to include all forms of 'penetration' - But is restricted only to cases of penile-vaginal penetration - Meaning of words 'sexual intercourse' occurring in definition of rape - Words and phrases in penal statutes are to be construed strictly - Child sexual abuse case - Inquiry into trial of - Evidence to be recorded - Provisions of S. 327(2) which contemplate trial in camera shall apply - Video conferencing is permissible - Screen or some such arrangements may be made where victim or witnesses do not see body or face of accused - Questions put in cross-examination on behalf of accused which relate directly to incident, should be given in writing to Presiding Officer of Court who may put them to victim or witnesses in a language which is clear and is not embarassing - Victim of child abuse or rape, while giving testimony in Court, should be allowed sufficient breaks as and when required - Cases of child abuse and rape are increasing at alarming speed and appropriate legislation in this regard is, therefore, urgently required and hope and trust that Parliament will give serious attention and make appropriate legislation with all promptness which it deserves.
