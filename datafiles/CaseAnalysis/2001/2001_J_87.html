Commissioner of Central Excise, Coimbatore v Jawahar Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 435; (2001) 6 SCC 274; AIR 2001 SC 2500; 2001 (97) ECR 541; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 3; JT 2001 (6) SC 65; 2001 (45) RLT 739; 2001(4) SCALE 596; 2001 (5) SLT 483; 2002 (125) STC 264; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 58; 2001 (5) Supreme 415

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57Q - MODVAT Credit - Capital goods - Power cables, capacitors, control panels, cables distribution boards, switches, starters, air compressors, electric wires and cables - Whether impugned goods manufactured by the assessee can be treated as 'capital goods' in terms of r. 57Q of 1944 Rules for availing MODVAT Credit? - Held, definition of 'Capital goods' is very wide - Capital goods can be machines, machinery, plant, equipment, apparatus, tools or appliances - Any of these goods, if used for producing or processing of any goods or for bringing about any change in any substance for manufacture of final product would be 'Capital goods', and, therefore, qualify for availing MODVAT Credit - In the present case, it was not the case of the revenue that an item does not satisfy the requirement of 'Capital goods' within the meaning of the Rule on the ground of its user - The case of the revenue has all through been that the items in question per se are not 'Capital goods' within the meaning of the expression as defined in Explanation 1(a) of r. 57Q of 1944 Rules - Language used in the explanation 1(a) of r. 57Q is very liberal - Hence, impugned goods manufactured by the assessee are capital goods and are also entitled to MODVAT Credit - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
