Dr. Karan Singh v State Of Jammu & Kashmir & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 261; (2004) 5 SCC 698; AIR 2004 SC 2480; 2004 (1) ARBLR 599; JT 2004 (5) SC 142; 2004(4) SCALE 418; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 43

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Right To Property

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 143, 291, 363, 366 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 5(1)(ivx) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 115, 17, 21, 31 - Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971 - Wealth Tax (Exemption of Heirloom Jewellery of Rules) Rules, 1958, r. 3 - Whether 563 articles lying in 'Toshakhana' (Treasury of State of Jammu and Kashmir) can be declared as private property - Bar of art.363 of Constitution of India to maintainability of WP - Whether appellant is disentitled to relief on applicability of doctrine of estoppel, abandonment and waiver - Whether decision of Govt. of India rejecting representation deserves to be quashed and declaration granted that articles are private property of appellant or issue either deserves to be remitted to Govt. of India for reconsideration or referred for adjudication to an arbitrator to be appointed by this Court - Any right arising out of or relating to a treaty covenant, agreement etc as mentioned in art.363, is barred to be determined by any court - Appellant failed to assert his right at proper opportunity - Having regard to these facts, conclusion of DB that appellant has waived and/or abandoned his right in respect of articles in question cannot be faulted - Prima facie opinion expressed in report is not a ground to refer issue to arbitration for adjudication in absence of any agreement requiring reference to arbitration - Appeal dismissed.
