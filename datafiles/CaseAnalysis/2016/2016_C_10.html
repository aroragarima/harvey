State of West Bengal and others v Calcutta Club Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 398; (2017) 5 SCC 356; JT 2016 (6) SC 533; 2016(5) SCALE 742; [2016] 96 VST 20

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Tribunal, Deemed Sale, Levy, Companies Act, 1956, Consumption, Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, Deferred Payment, Supply Of Goods, Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, Constitution (Forty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1982, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 2(30) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 366(29A), 366(29A)(e), 366(29A)(f), s. 30 - Assessment of sales tax - Declaration - Validity - Appellant/Assistant Commissioner issued a notice to respondent/Club assessee apprising that it had failed to make payment of sales tax on sale of food and drinks to permanent members during the quarter - Respondent submitted before Tribunal for a declaration that it is not a dealer within the meaning of the Act as there is no sale of any goods in the form of food, refreshments, drinks, etc. to its permanent members - Tribunal opined that it is not exigible to tax under the Act - Appellant filed writ petition before HC - HC upheld the order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, principle of mutuality was regarded as the base of imposition or non-imposition of sales tax. Authorities below had not recorded any finding with regard to exact relationship or mutuality facet. The contention before SC is that even if principle of mutuality or agency is in existence or established, still it would be a sale on basis of cls.29A(e) or 29A(f) of Constitution. Thus, controversy in instant case has to be authoritatively decided by a larger Bench in view of the law laid down in Cosmopolitan Club v State of Tamil Nadu and Others 2008 Indlaw SC 1634 and Fateh Maidan Club v Commercial Tax Officer, Hyderabad and Another 1998 Indlaw SC 2267. SC held that none of the judgments really lay down that doctrine of mutuality would apply or not but proceed on the said principle relying on earlier judgments. Thus, matter was referred to a larger Bench. Order accordingly.
