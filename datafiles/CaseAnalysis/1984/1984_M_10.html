Shiromani Gurudwara Prabhandhak Committee v Mahant Kirpa Ram & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 134; (1984) 2 SCC 614; AIR 1984 SC 1059; 1984(1) SCALE 572; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 372; 1984 UJ 613

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925

Summary: Family and Personal - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, ss. 2(9) , 14, 16(2)(iii) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.3 - Gurudwara - Whether impuned religious and/or charitable institution is a Sikh Gurdwara within the meaning of the expression in the Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925 - At the time of presentation of the petition it was, along with the follower of Udasi sect used for worship of Granth Sahib by the Sikhs - Held on facts that there was no evidence to show that the institution was established for use by Sikhs for the purpose of public worship and therefore one of the material conditions for attracting s.16(2)(iii) of the Act is not established - It must also be used for such purpose before and at time of presentation of petition - Appeal dismissed.
