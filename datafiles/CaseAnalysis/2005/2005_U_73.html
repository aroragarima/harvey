Supreme Court of India

02 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 80; (2005) 4 SCC 649; AIR 2005 SC 2677; 2005 (4) CompLJ 283; JT 2005 (2) SC 8; 2005(1) SCALE 666B; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 913

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Right To Work

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 141, 145(3), 19(1)(g), 226, 32 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - United Nations Covenant on Civil and Political Rights, 1966, art. 18 - Writ petiton - Maintainability - Whether Writ Petition against Board of Control for Cricket in India is maintainable? - Whether Board is a state within meaning of art.12? - Majority view - Held, respondent-Board cannot be held to be a State for purpose of art. 12 - Writ filed u/art.32 of Constitution is not maintainable - Petition dismissed.
