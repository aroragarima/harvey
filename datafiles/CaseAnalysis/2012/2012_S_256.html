Bishnupada Sarkar & Anr. v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 158; (2012) 11 SCC 597; AIR 2012 SC 2248; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3790; 2012 (4) CGLJ 149; 2012 (3) JCC 1947; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 860; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 661; 2012 (3) RLW 2705; 2012(6) SCALE 7; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 230; 2012 (3) WLN(SC) 24

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 Part I r/w. s. 34 - Culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Conviction - Quantum of sentence - Appellants allegedly assault the deceased that resulted to his death - Charge sheet was filed against the appellants for commission of offence punishable u/s. 304 r/w. s. 34 of IPC - Trial court held that the deceased had died a homicidal death because of the injuries inflicted by appellant no.2 at the exhortation of appellant no.1 - Both of them were accordingly convicted u/s. 304 part I r/w. s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed the conviction awarded by the trial court - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellant no. 1 submitted that he had not inflicted any injury on the deceased and that all that was alleged against him was that he exhorted appellant no.2 to assault the deceased - Appellant no. 1 also submitted that he was more than 65 years of age and had already undergone 1Â½ years sentence in jail - Appellant no. 2 submitted being the only earning member of the family he deserves a reduction in the sentence especially when there was no intention to kill the deceased - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, there was no evidence to suggest any pre-meditation on the part of the appellants to assault the deceased - There was no previous enmity between the parties who were residents of the same locality - Even on the following day the incident involved the appellant no. 1 and the complainant (son of the deceased) - It was only when the deceased noticed the incident and intervened to save the complainant, that appellant no. 2 started assaulting the deceased and inflicted injuries on his body that resulted in his death - Both the Courts below have believed the prosecution case that appellant no. 1 was exhorting appellant no. 2 to assault the deceased and, therefore, convicted him u/s. 304 Part I r/w. s. 34 of IPC - In the totality of the circumstances, SC were of the view that a rigorous sentence of 3 years to appellant no.1 and 7 years to appellant no.2 would meet the ends of justice - Sentence awarded by the Trial court as affirmed by the HC to be modified - Appeal partly allowed.
