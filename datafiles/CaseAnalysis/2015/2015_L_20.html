Tamilnadu Terminated Full Time Temporary LIC Employees Association v Life Insurance Corporation of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 346; (2015) 9 SCC 62; 2015 (145) FLR 761; JT 2015 (3) SC 520; 2015 (2) LLJ 335; 2016 (2) MahLJ 490; 2016 (2) MPLJ 64; 2015 (1) OLR 1072; 2015(3) SCALE 861; 2015 (4) SLR 93

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: LIC of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960

Summary: Service - Temporary Employees - Absorption of employees - Determination - Workmen are members of appellant Associations have been working as temporary, badli and part-time workmen claiming that they have been appointed by management of Corporation on daily wage basis against leave vacancies and other vacancies of its employees - Workmen have raised industrial dispute between concerned workmen and management of Corporation regarding their absorption as regular and permanent service employees in their respective posts of Corporation - Workman claimed for regularisation based on two Awards passed of Tribunal with regard to absorption of similarly placed workmen by Corporation who had been working on temporary/badli/part-time basis - Tribunal passed award in favor of workmenRespondent Corporation stated that during course of SLP, compromise was entered into between Corporation and 8 out of 9 Unions of above SLP - Further, Corporation framed Scheme for regularization of employees in their service who were granted ad-hoc appointments for 85 days at intervals from time to time and placed - Award passed by Tribunal in relation to concerned workmen of appeals was set aside by HC - Whether workmen in question are eligible for absorption as regular and permanent service.

Held, in order passed by SC, nowhere it has been stated in terms of compromise between parties that earlier award passed by judges are set aside by SC or substituted compromise terms in place of Award. Hence, effect of award of judge with regard to direction given to Corporation regarding absorption of badli, temporary employees as permanent employees has not been substituted by terms and conditions of compromise. Further, Tribunal has rightly adjudicated industrial dispute referred to it by Central Government at instance of concerned workmen on points of dispute, on basis of pleadings and evidence on record and legal principles laid down in awards passed by lower Tribunal .SC held that award passed by Tribunal after adjudicating points of dispute in industrial dispute raised by similarly placed workmen is not disturbed by substituting terms and conditions of compromise between parties therein, hence, awards in relation to absorption of workmen as permanent workmen in Corporation have got statutory force. Further, compromise between parties in SLP and Scheme formed in do not amount to substitution of awards passed by Tribunal, hence, HC was not justified in interfering with said Award passed by Tribunal. SC held that awards passed by National Tribunal is binding upon Corporation till it is substituted by another award or replaced by another settlement in relation to service conditions of workmen of Corporation. Hence, award passed by Tribunal restored and implemented by Corporation by absorbing concerned workmen in permanent posts and if they have attained age of superannuation, Corporation will be liable to pay all consequential benefits including monetary benefits taking into consideration pay scale and revised pay scale from time to time by Corporation. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Corporation is bound to absorb temporary employees if order is passed by Tribunal and no compromise between parties would effect order that is passed by Tribunal which is binding.
