State of Chhattisgarh v Lekhram

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 115; (2006) 5 SCC 736; AIR 2006 SC 1746; 2006 CRLJ 2139; 2006 (2) JCC 686; JT 2006 (4) SC 477; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 475; 2006 (3) RLW 1938; 2006(4) SCALE 118; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 826; 2006 (2) UC 1280

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 366, 376 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 35 - Whether State has brought enough materials on record to prove that PW-1 was a minor as on date of occurrence? - Held, an entry in school register is not conclusive but it has evidentiary value - Such evidentiary value of a school register is corroborated by oral evidence as same was recorded on basis of statement of mother of prosecutrix - Respondent is directed to be sentenced to period already undergone - Appeal allowed.
