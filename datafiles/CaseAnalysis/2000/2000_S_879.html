Swadeshi Polytex Ltd v Collector Of Central Excise, Meerut

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3514; 2000 (122) E.L.T. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Duty Levied, Facts And Circumstance, Appeal is disposed of, Levy Of Excise Duty

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A and 33 - Remand by tribunal - Classification - Denial of the principles of natural justice - Cross-examination of witnesses on whose statements the Collector had relied upon - Whether a commodity which is categorised as waste by the appellant can be regarded as good quality fibre by the department and excise duty levied thereon? - Held, if the Collector intends to rely upon the statement of any such person, he shall give an opportunity of cross-examination to the appellant - Appeal disposed of.
