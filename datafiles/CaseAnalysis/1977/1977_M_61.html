Munshi Dass v Mal Singh and Mal Dass and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 311; (1977) 4 SCC 65; AIR 1977 SC 2002; 1977 UJ 555

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Customs - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 145 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 50 - Whether the appellant was Chela of Sobha Ram and was duly installed as Mahant? - Held, relying upon the decision of Dolgobinda Paricha v. Nimai Charan Misra [1959 Indlaw SC 237], the High Court has rightly held that the evidence of PWs 1, 2, 3, 4, 5, 6 and 8 does not fullfil the test of s. 50 of the Evidence Act, evidence of solitary witness PW 7 was neither trustworthy nor sufficient to prove that Munshi Dass had been taken as Chela of Sobha Ram, one of the factors of substratum of the custom to claim Mahantship thus became absent, unless he was proved to be the Chela, he could not claim that according to the custom governing succession to the office of management of the Dharamshala, he could be installed as a Mahant, also, no reliance could be placed upon the entry in the Bahi as the recitals of two succession contained in Mutation Order of the year 1908, are not identical and one instance given in it and the other, an earlier instance given therein not falling in line with it, could not prove the custom, also, shortly after the death of Mahant Sobha Ram, a dispute cropped up between Pritam Dass and Mal Dass leading to a proceeding u/s. 145, Cr.P.C., 1898 leading to passing of Mutation Order in his favour in February, 1962, on the basis of a compromise entered into in the criminal proceeding, it does not stand to reason that Munshi Dass would not be aware of the said dispute and could smoothly get himself installed as Mahant in November, 1961, therefore, the appellant did not succeed in getting possession of either the office of Mahant or institution's properties even for a day and the High Court's judgment is correct - Appeal dismissed.
