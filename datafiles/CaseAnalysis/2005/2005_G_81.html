K. C. P. Sugar and Industries Corporation Limited v Government of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1414; (2005) 11 SCC 544

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Administrative - Certificate of registration - Inspection - Additional payment - Appellant was distillery and manufactures arrack and arrack produced by appellant was purchased by State Govt. at prices fixed by latter - Appellant applied for certificate of effluent treatment plant from pollution Board - Distillers Association inspected premises of appellant and submitted report against appellant and Assistant Commissioner passed order as per report submitted by association - Appellant then approached Excise Authorities for grant of enhanced price for arrack and Commissioner said that additional payment would be allowed only if required parameters for purpose of pollution were certified each month for payment - Appellant filed Writ petition against said order passed by Commissioner was dismissed and on further appeal Division Bench partly allowed appeal by providing grant of enhanced price but subject to production of a certificate from Pollution Control Board - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant approached Excise Authorities for grant of enhanced price for arrack. The Commissioner of Excise said that additional payment would be allowed only if required parameters for purpose of pollution were certified each month for payment. Appellant challenged this condition by filing a writ petition which was dismissed. On appeal Division Bench partly allowed appeal by providing grant of enhanced price but subject to production of a certificate from Pollution Control Board on a month-to-month basis certifying that functioning of appellant's effluent treatment plant was achieving standard fixed by Pollution Control Board. This requirement has been challenged by appellant. According to appellant, original order of Govt. did not prescribe any such condition and that both Commissioner of Excise as well as Divisional Bench was wrong in making grant of enhanced price dependent upon monthly production of certificate. It is true that order issued by Govt. does not speak of production of a certificate from Pollution Control Board on a monthly basis. However, tenor of order appears to be that effluent treatment plant to be set up must be a functional one. In that view of matter appeal is disposed of by modifying order under appeal to extent that appellant will be entitled to enhance price subject to production of an annual certificate from Pollution Control Board that effluent treatment plant set up by appellant is functional and achieving required parameters of purity. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - When company introduced any new effluent treatment plant it is mandatory to obtain valid certification from pollution control Board.
