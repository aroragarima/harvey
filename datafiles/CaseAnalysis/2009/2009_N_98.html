Sonic Surgical v National Insurance Company Ltd

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 17 - Jurisdiction - Appellant's godown caught fire in Ambala - Claim filed before the Consumer Commission of the Union Territory, Chandigarh, u/s 17 which was allowed - Respondent filed appeal before National Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission (NCDRC) - NCDRC allowed said appeal on the ground that the Consumer Commission at Chandigarh had no jurisdiction to entertain and adjudicate the complaint - Whether Consumer Commission at Chandigarh has the territorial jurisdiction to hear the appeal or not? - 'Cause of action' means that bundle of facts which gives rise to a right or liability - Held, insurance policy was taken at Ambala and the claim for compensation was also made at Ambala - Thus, no part of the cause of action arose in Chandigarh - HC does not have territorial jurisdiction - Refer Union of India vs Adani Exports Limited, 2001 INDLAW SC 413 - Hence, appeal dismissed.
