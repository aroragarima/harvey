Guljag Industries v Commercial Taxes Officer

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 888; (2007) 7 SCC 269; [2007] 293 ITR 584; JT 2007 (10) SC 1; 2007(9) SCALE 564; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 793; [2007] 9 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, Transporter, Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, Processing, Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1955, Sale Outside The State, Securities And Exchange Board Of India (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996, RST Rules, 1994, RST Rules, 1995

Summary: Sales Tax - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, ss. 78(2) and 78(5) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1955 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1994 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1995 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996 - Whether the presumption that mens rea is an essential ingredient in every offence is displaced by the words of s. 78(5) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994? - Held, in the present case, the goods in movement were not supported by duly filled in form No. 18 A/18 C - Therefore, there was contravention of s. 78(2) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 - There is no question of proving of intention or of mens rea as the same is excluded from the category of essential element for imposing penalty - Penalty u/s. 78(5) is attracted as soon as there is contravention of statutory obligations - Intention of parties committing such violation is wholly irrelevant - Assessees were fully aware that the goods in movement had to be supported by form ST 18 A/18C - However, the said forms are left blank in all material respects - Therefore, A O was right in drawing inference of mens rea against the assessees - S. 78(5) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 [s. 22 A(7) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954] is the section enacted to provide remedy for loss of revenue and it is not enacted to punish the offender for committing economic offence and, therefore, mens rea is not an essential ingredient for contravention of s. 78(2) of the Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1994 - Appeals disposed of.
