Gaiv Dinshaw Irani and others v Tehmtan Irani and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 324; (2014) 8 SCC 294; AIR 2014 SC 2326; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 191; 2015(2) ALL MR 456; JT 2014 (6) SC 169; 2015 (1) MahLJ 228; 2014 (4) RLW 3537; 2014(6) SCALE 99; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Bomaby Rent Control Act, 1974

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotel And Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 4(1) - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 - Tenancy rights - Surrender of tenancy - Legality - Trial Court dismissed both the suits filed by plaintiffs by two separate judgments - Findings of the Trial Court was that the plaintiffs failed to prove joint tenancy and therefore the transfer of rent bills in the name of defendant No. 2 was not illegal - In the other case Trial Court held that as plaintiffs failed to prove their case of joint tenancy, the surrender of tenancy in favour of BMC was not hit by an illegality and the lease granted to him is legal and valid - Respondents preferred two separate appeals HC by a common judgment and order allowed both the appeals and held that the original plaintiffs/respondents were joint tenants with original defendant No. 2/appellant consequently, the surrender of tenancy by defendant No. 2 in favour of BMC was illegal and the transfer of tenancy by BMC in the name of defendant No. 2 was incorrect, void and not binding upon the plaintiffs - Judgments and orders of the Trial Court were set aside and the reliefs prayed for in the suits filed by plaintiffs were allowed by HC - However, HC directed appellants to handover possession of the five flats to respondent Nos. 1 to 5 - Hence instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned orders -

Held, appellants claimed that leasehold tenancy rights could be bequeathed as against the holding of the Trial Court which held that 'it was well established principle that tenancy rights could not be bequeathed' - Divesting of tenancy rights by means of a Will was a highly debated topic and was subject to the tenancy laws of the concerned State - In instant matter, the tenancies being the suit premises were owned by the local authority of Mumbai and were subject to the State Act being the 1947 Act - Said Act, since repealed, exempts the present tenancy from its purview as per s. 4 (1) of the 1947 Act - HC correctly opined that the conduct of BMC lacked bona fide and same was not challenged by the BMC being respondent No. 6 before SC - Appellants during the pendency of the Civil Suits sought interim orders from the HC and on the basis of order constructed the structure on the condition that rights of five flats were to be retained and they were subject to the outcome of the suit - In another order appellants were once again restrained from the creation of third party rights with respect to the five demarcated flats - Appellants being well aware of the risks and consequences, carried on with the construction - During the pendency of the First Appeal, it was pointed out that appellants had given two of the five flats on leave and licence and continued to enjoy benefits from the same since 1997 - Appellants were occupying two of the other nine flats and benefits from the remainder were being enjoyed by them - SC was of the opinion that HC taking note of the subsequent events has correctly moulded the relief and allotted five flats to the respondent Nos. 1 to 5 as per their share - Considering the said submissions of respondent No. 6 SC find that appellants could not shift the onus on the BMC and the HC has correctly held - Share of respondent Nos.1 to 5 was claimed to be 6/15th and the same was challenged - However, there were no specific submissions to the contrary in that regard and as it was a question of fact - HC correctly determined the same as appellants were getting more than their share being heirs of only one brother juxtaposed to respondent Nos. 1 to 5, who represent branches of two brothers - Appeals dismissed.
