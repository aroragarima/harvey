Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North v Chamanlal Mangaldas and Company

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 339; AIR 1960 SC 1336; [1960] 30 Comp Cas 293; [1960] 39 ITR 8

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Accounts & Audits - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4 - (A) Whether amount of commission given up by managing agents as a voluntary surrender is to be included while calculating taxability of commission for a particular accounting year - Held, a mere voluntary surrender would not affect taxability of whole amount of commission accruing to managing agents - Thus, entire sum accruing as commission in previous year is taxable - (B) Whether amount of commission given up by assessee as voluntary surrender is income in hands of assessee? - Held, as per terms of contract, right of managing agents (i.e., assessee) to receive commission or its accrual was at end of accounting year, when all sales were and could be added up and accounts were made up - Amount which actually accrued or which assessee was entitled to receive was alone taxable and not amount which would have been payable had there been no modification of agreement - (1) Whether fact that amounts of commission were credited in books of managed company every six months will affect terms of payment of commission? - Held, it was merely an interim arrangement - Amount which arose or accrued and which assessees had right to receive was not affected by manner in which entry was made - Appeals dismissed.
