M/S. Basant Industries, Agra v Collector Of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1616; (1995) 1 SCC 534; 1995 (49) ECC 42; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 21; JT 1995 (1) SC 152; 1995 (6) RLT 1; 1994(5) SCALE 181; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Notification No. 85/72 dated 17- 3-1972 - Whether oil-driven pumps sold by the appellant were exempt under Notification No. 85/72 or they were assessable to duty under Item 30-A of the Central Excise Tariff? - The test to determine leviability under the Act is whether a new commercial commodity has emerged - No such finding has been recorded by the Tribunal nor any material could be pointed out which could establish that it was the appellant who manufactured the pumps or the independent units from whom it got the pumps manufactured were doing so on behalf of the appellant - Held, the oil-driven pumps sold by the appellant having not been manufactured by it was entitled to claim exemption under Notification No. 85/72 - Appeal allowed.
