Ashok Sadarangani & Anr v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 82; (2012) 11 SCC 321; AIR 2012 SC 1563; 2012 CRLJ 1850; 2012 (2) JCC 1140; JT 2012 (3) SC 469; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 513; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 280; 2012 (2) RLW 1357; 2012(3) SCALE 404; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 826

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cash Credit, Letters Of Credit

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Letters of Credit - Default - Quashing - Sustainability - Petitioners-directors of company had availed credit facility from respondent-bank by way of Letters of Credit, including Bills of Lading, Invoice and Bills of Exchange - Petitioners defaulted payment and CBI registered case alleging that they had secured credit facilities by submitting forged property documents as collaterals and utilized such facilities in a dishonest and fraudulent manner - Charge-sheet was filed u/ss. 120-B, r/w ss. 465, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC - Respondent also filed civil suit for recovery - Meanwhile respondent offered one-time settlement of disputes, which was accepted and compromise was entered - Instant Writ petition was filed for quashing of cases against petitioners - Whether an offence which was not compoundable under provisions of CrPC, could be quashed - Held, continuance of a criminal proceeding after a compromise was arrived at between complainant and accused, would amount to abuse of process of court and an exercise in futility, since trial could be prolonged and ultimately, would conclude in a decision which would be of any consequence to any of other parties - It was not that there was any restriction on power or authority vested in SC in exercising powers u/art. 142 of Constitution, but that in exercising such powers Court had to be circumspect, and had to exercise such power sparingly in facts of each case - In instant case, emphasis is more on criminal intent of Petitioners than on civil aspect involving dues of Bank in respect of which a compromise was worked out - Petition dismissed.
