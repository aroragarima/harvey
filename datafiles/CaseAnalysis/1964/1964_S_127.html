Â Epari Chinna Krishna Moorthy, Proprietor and others v State of Orissa(With Connected Petition)

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 228; AIR 1964 SC 1581; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 185; 1964 (15) STC 461

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Orissa Sales Tax Act 1947, Sales Tax Validation Act 1961

Summary: Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Municipalities & Local Govts - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, s. 6 - Orissa Sales Tax Validation Act, 1961, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 245 and 246 - (A) Does s. 2 of Act contravene art. 14 of Constitution?- Held, it does not - Persons who get benefit of exemption notification as a result of provisions of s. 2 of Act cannot be said to belong to same class as that of petitioners - Object of Act is to benefit goldsmiths who actually make gold ornaments and that object can be carried out only if exemption is granted to persons who keep in their continuous employment artisans who produce gold ornaments - If a person produces gold ornaments himself and if a person employs artisans to produce ornaments for him, they fall within protection of exemption - Petitioners do not keep any artisans in their continuous employment, and so, if legislature thought it was not necessary to give them benefit of exemption, it cannot be said that classification made by legislature has no rational connection with object intended to be achieved by it - (B) Orissa Sales Tax Validation Act - Whether s. 2 of Act is violative of art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution?- Further held, it is not - Retrospective operation may operate harshly in some cases, therefore, legislation itself is invalid - Besides, in present case, retrospective operation does not spread over a very long period either - Restriction imposed is reasonable and in interest of general public - (C) Orissa Sales Tax Validation Act, 1961 - Whether exemption validly granted can be withdrawn retrospectively u/s. 2 of Act? - Further held, s. 2 declares that intention of delegate in issuing notification granting exemption was to confine benefit of said exemption only to persons who actually produce gold ornaments or employ artisans for that purpose - So, if State Govt. was given power either to grant or withdraw exemption, that cannot possibly affect legislature's competence to make any provision in that behalf either prospectively or retrospectively - (D) Orissa Sales Tax Validation Act, 1961 - What do you mean by word 'manufacturer' in notification? - Further held, s.2 of Act provide that notwithstanding anything contained in any judgment, decree or order of any court, it shall mean, shall always be deemed to have meant a person who by his own labour works up materials into suitable forms, a person who owns or runs a manufactory for purpose of busines with respect to articles manufactured therein - Petitions dismissed.
