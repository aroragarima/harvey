Â Meghraj Kothari v Delimitation Commission and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 173; AIR 1967 SC 669; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Cotton Textiles (Control of Movement) Order, 1948, Allocation of seats in the house of the people, Delimitation Commission Act 1962

Summary: Election - Constitution - Govternment of India Act, 1935 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Delimitation Commission Act, 1962, ss. 4, 8, 9, 10(1) and 10(4) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 82 and 327 - Cotton Textiles (Control of Movement) Order, 1948 - Whether Notification of Delimitation reserving a Constituency for Scheduled Castes is not made validly u/art. 327? - Delimitation Commission Act shows that it is an Act to provide for readjustment of allocation of seats in House of People to states, total number of seats in legislative assembly of each state, division of each State into territorial constituencies for elections to House of People and legislative assemblies of states - Objection to delimitation of constituencies could only be entertained by Commission before date specified - If orders made u/s 8 and 9 were not to be treated as final, effect would be that any voter could hold up an election indefinitely by questioning delimitation of constituencies from court to court - Held, provisions of Act are in the same street as a law made by Parliament itself which could only be done u/art. 327, and consequently objection that notification was not to be treated as law cannot be given effect to - Appeal dismissed.
