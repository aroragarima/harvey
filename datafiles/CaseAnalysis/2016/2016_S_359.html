Harsh Kumar Sharma v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 988; (2017) 4 SCC 366; AIR 2017 SC 124; JT 2017 (2) SC 137; 2016(12) SCALE 870; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Vacancy, Review, Promotion, Procedure, Date, Grade, Dpc, Interest, Government Servants, Charges, Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, Indian Forest Act, 1927, Sealed Cover Procedure, Punjab Land Preservation Act, 1900, Employee, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 9(6)(b)(i) - Irregularities in Service - Order Challenged - Appellant/employee rendered service as Conservator of Forests and committed irregularities in service thereby caused loss to state - Criminal proceedings were initiated and Departmental promotional committee submitted result of appellant in sealed cover - Tribunal allowed application filed u/s. 19 of the Act by appellant and granted relief - HC reversed order of Tribunal in appeal - Hence, instant appeals and contempt petitions.

Held, when a govt. servant comes under investigation, he may pass through three stages, namely, investigation for a criminal charge in departmental Proceedings and or prosecution of criminal charges followed by either penal/conviction or exoneration/acquittal. During the stage of investigation prior to issue of charge-sheet in disciplinary proceedings or prosecution, if the govt. is of the view that the charges are serious and the officer should not be promoted, it is open to the govt. to suspend the officer which will lead to the DPC recommendation to be kept in sealed cover. The sealed cover procedure is to be resorted to only after the charge memo/charge-sheet is issued or the officer is placed under suspension. The pendency of preliminary investigation prior to that stage is not adopt the sealed cover procedure. Further, r. 9(6)(b)(i) of the Rules, provides that criminal charge would be treated as pending in the case of criminal proceedings, on the date on which the complaint or a report of police officer, on which Magistrate takes cognizance, is made. It, thus, makes it clear that the date on which report of police is made would be the relevant date. In instant case, charge-sheet was filed by the CBI, after completion of the investigation, on 9-12-2008 and the meeting of the DPC was held thereafter i.e. on 29-3-2011. Therefore, move on the part of the DPC to resort to the sealed cover procedure is justified. However, the persons involved in the said main case stand discharged. Even the public servants involved therein stand promoted. In view thereof, the govt. should have considered the desirability of giving the appellant ad-hoc promotion when prima facie none of the circumstances mentioned in para 5 of O.M. dt. 14-9-1992 appear to exist. Hence, direct the respondents to consider the case of the appellant for ad-hoc promotion keeping in view the circumstances of the case. Appeals disposed of.
