Hansraj H. Jain v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1223; (1993) 3 SCC 634; AIR 1993 SCW 2923; 1994 (2) Bom.C.R. 223; 1993 (2) CCC 593; JT 1993 (4) SC 360; 1993 (2) RentLR 196; 1993(3) SCALE 153; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 11-A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 113 and 125 - Notices issued between 1968 and 1970, u/s. 4 of Land Acquisition Act acquire huge tracts of lands for purpose of planned development and utilisation of said lands for industrial, commercial and residential purposes - Declarations u/s. 6 of Land Acquisition Act were also issued in 1971 and 1972 - Acquisition proceedings were, however, concluded after a long lapse of time by giving awards in 1985 and 1986 - WPs challenging validity and inordinate delay in acquisition before High Court and appeal before DB dismissed - Order of DB challenged - Held, issue notification u/s. 4 of Land Acquisition Act was correct so as to discourage lands speculator in area and to make acquisition proceedings and scheme of new township economically viable - Project of this magnitude where area proposed to be developed into New Bombay admittedly is very large area and lands appertained to about 86 villages and about 25, 000 objections petitions had to be disposed of - It reasonably expected that substantial time would be required for detailed planning for development of area and complete acquisition proceedings - Appeals disposed of.
