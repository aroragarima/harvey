Vijaybhai Bhanabhai Patel v Navnitbhai Nathubhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1468; (2004) 10 SCC 583; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2032; AIR 2004 SC 4607; 2004 CRLJ 3832; JT 2004 (4) SC 80; 2004(3) SCALE 704; 2004 (2) UC 805

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Service

Keywords: Acquittal, Justifiability

Summary: Criminal - Service - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 302, 149 - Acquittal - Justifiability - Respondents were found guilty by Trial Court for causing death of deceased u/s. 302 read with s. 149 of the 1951 Act and were sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life - HC held that there were certain infirmities in the prosecution case, and hence the accused persons were not guilty of the offences charged against them - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, as it is an appeal against acquittal, this Court would be slow in reversing the finding entered by the HC unless there is perverse and erroneous appreciation of evidence. If the HC, for acquitting the respondents has given certain tenable reasons, this Court would not be justified in interfering with such acquittal. Though, the prosecution could establish a serious and strong suspicion against the respondents, it is not opined that this case calls for reversal of the Judgment of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
