Narinder Singh Arora v State (Govt. Of Nct Of Delhi) And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 771; (2012) 1 SCC 561; AIR 2012 SC 1642; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 305; 2012 CRLJ 2167; 2012 (283) E.L.T. 481; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 211; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 243; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Advocates & Judges - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498-A, 304-B r/w s. 34, 302 - Recusal - Revision - Principles of natural justice - Appellant lodged a complaint - FIR was registered against accused - Charges were framed against accused - Case was listed before Additional Sessions Judge - Judge had recused from hearing trial for personal reasons and matter was made over to another Sessions Judge - Trial was concluded and accused were acquitted by Trial Court - Aggrieved appellant filed revision petition before HC - HC dismissed revision - Hence instant appeal - Whether recusal at trial stage for personal reasons and dismissal of revision thereafter was against principles of natural justice and fair trial - Held, recusal of case at trial by first Jude himself was not brought to notice in the revision petition before HC either of parties to case - Owing to inadvertence regarding his earlier recusal, HC had dismissed the revision petition by impugned judgment - Judgment passed by first judge subsequent to his recusal at trial stage for personal reasons, was against the principles of natural justice and fair trial - A person who tried a cause should be able to deal with the matter placed before him objectively, fairly and impartially - No one should act in a judicial capacity if his previous conduct gave ground for believing that he could not act with an open mind or impartially - A person, trying a cause, should not only act fairly but should be able to act above suspicion of unfairness and bias - Impugned judgement of HC was set aside and revision petition filed by appellant was remanded to HC for fresh disposal in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
