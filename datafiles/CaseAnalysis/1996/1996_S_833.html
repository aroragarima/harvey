Ramniklal N. Bhutta and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1894; (1997) 1 SCC 134; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 68; AIR 1997 SC 1236; AIR 1997 SCW 1281; 1997 (1) CLT 302; JT 1996 (10) SC 452; 1996(8) SCALE 417; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Notification for acquisition of two lands - By agreement in good faith, with owner of one of lands, beneficiary got portion of land free of cost - Legality of award passed by Land Acquisition officer in regard to other piece of land - (B) Powers of High Court in land acquisition proceedings regarding granting stay or injunction - Held on facts, award not incompetent in law in absence of mala fides and challenge for seven years from award - Supreme Court observed that High Court should keep larger public interest in mind while exercising such powers - Appeal dismissed.
