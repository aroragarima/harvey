Ruma Chakraborty v Sudha Rani Banerjee and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 595; (2005) 8 SCC 140; AIR 2005 SC 3557; 2005 (6) ALD(SC) 116; 2006 (62) ALR 144; 2005 (4) AWC 3791; JT 2005 (12) SC 134; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 394; 2005 (4) RLW 2882; 2005(8) SCALE 124; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Married Women's Property Act, 1882

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Rent Control - Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 1, r. 10 (2) - Hindu Adoptions And Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 18, 23, 3, 3(b) - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 13(1)(a), 13(2), 2(d), 2(h) - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13-B, 25 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882 - Application for being added as a party defendant - Plea of appellant that she is directly interested in tenanted suit premises by way of her entitlement towards maintenance which includes residence as per Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act - Whether appellant has a right to be impleaded as party defendant? - Held, Court has no jurisdictional power to add a person as a party who is neither a necessary party nor a proper party - Appellant in status of divorcee cannot claim interest in suit premises - Appellant is also not a person whose presence is necessary to enable Court effectually and completely to adjudicate all questions involved with suit - No error in dismissing application - Appeal dismissed.
