Union of India and Others v Neelam Engineering and Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 160; (2010) 3 SCC 642; AIR 2010 SC 1374; 2010(4) ALL MR 445; 2010 (79) ALR 941; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 348; JT 2010 (3) SC 69; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 501; 2010(2) SCALE 815; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Service Of Notice

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14(2), 17 and 29 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 119 - Filing of objection - Agreement was entered into between the appellant and respondent - Disputes arose between the appellants and the respondent which were referred to arbitration - Arbitrator made his award in favour of the Respondent and on the same day Respondent filed a petition in the Civil Court for making the Award a Rule of Court - After two years, on 3.01.1998, the Appellants filed an objection petition u/s. 30 and 33 of the Act, for setting aside the Award - Civil Court rejected the said objection by holding that since the objection had been filed prior to filing of the Award, the same was premature and could not be taken note of - Civil Court ordered that the Award be made a Rule of Court - On appeal, Appeal Court held objections having been filed even before notice of filing of the award was issued were pre-mature and could not, therefore, be taken note of - Civil Revision filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether Trial Court and HC erred in holding that the objection filed on behalf of the appellants could not be taken note of? - Held, in view of art. 119 of the Limitation Act, 1963, the period of limitation for filing an application commences only after the date of service of the notice of the making of the Award - Filing an objection against something which did not exist on the date when the objection was filed is unacceptable and must be rejected - In the present case, appellants filed their objection, the same was done prematurely even prior to the filing of the award and such objection could not be treated as a valid objection u/ss. 30 and 33 of the Act in view of the provisions of art. 119 of the Limitation Act, 1963 - Objections filed by the appellants therefore, have been rightly held to be pre-mature and could not be treated to be an objection filed after the filing of the Award - Appeal dismissed.
