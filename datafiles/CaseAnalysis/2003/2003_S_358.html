State of Bihar v Kalika Kuer @ Kalika Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 395; (2003) 5 SCC 448; AIR 2003 SC 2443; JT 2003 (4) SC 489; 2003(4) SCALE 302; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 919; 2003 (4) SLT 252; 2003 (3) Supreme 505

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Bihar Consolidation of Holdings and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1956, (the Act), ss.15, 15(1), 15(2), 3, 3(1), 37, 4(b), 4(c) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 141 - Rule of per incuriam - ss.15(1), 15(2) of the Act - Whether ultra vires art.13 and 14 of Constitution - Full Bench of three judges of HC held Section 15 of Act ultra vires - Contention that Full Bench decision is in direct conflict with decision in case of Ramkrit Singh, in which case also same or similar arguments and grounds were raised - Whether decision in case of Ramkrit Singh is not binding, having been rendered per incuriam - Held, an earlier decision may seem to be incorrect to a Bench of a coordinate jurisdiction considering question later, on ground that a possible aspect of matter was not considered or not raised before Court or more aspects should have been gone into by Court deciding matter earlier but it would not be a reason to say that decision was rendered per incuriam and liable to be ignored - Doctrine of per incuriam has been misapplied by HC to earlier decision in case of Ramkrit Singh - Case remanded to HC to consider it in light of this judgment.
