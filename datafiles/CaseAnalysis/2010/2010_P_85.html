Guru Gobind Singh Refineries Limited v Punjab State and others etc.

Supreme Court of India

20 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 874; (2010) 10 SCC 401; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 71; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 28; 2011(1) ALL MR 443; 2011 (85) ALR 239; 2011 (2) AWC 1490; JT 2010 (11) SC 210; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3457; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 573; 2010(11) SCALE 105

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Belting

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Respondent/State issued notification u/s. 4 r/w s. 17 of the Act for acquisition of lands - Respondent, subsequently, issued declaration u/s. 6 of the Act - Land Acquisition Collector divided the land into three categories and passed award on the basis of such classification - Appellant/landowner raised objections against award - Matter referred to the Reference Court - Reference Court clubbed the classification of land, however, maintained the market valued fixed by the Land Acquisition Collector - Appellants challenged the award of Reference Court before the HC - HC remanded the matter to Reference Court for reconsideration of issue with regard to the clubbing of classification of lands, however, declined to interfere with the fixation of market value - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether direction of HC to the Reference Court with regard to the clubbing of different categories of land was proper - Held, reasons recorded by the HC for remanding the case to the Reference Court for deciding the issue of clubbing the land categorized were not flawed - Reference Court was not justified in deciding the issue relating to fixation of market value of the acquired land by presuming that irrigation facilities were available in all the pieces of lands in question - Reference Court should have referred to the substantive evidence produced by the parties and then decided whether it was just and proper to club the land categorization - Therefore, there is no error in the direction given by the HC to the Reference Court to decide issue relating to clubbing of different categories of land - However, HC should have ordered an open remand so as to enable the parties to lead additional evidence on all the issues including the clubbing of lands and fixation of market value at a flat rate keeping in view of the rate fixed by the State Government for different types of land - Therefore, impugned HC order is set aside and matter remanded to the Reference Court - Hence, direction issued to the Reference Court to decide all the issues afresh after giving opportunity to the parties to adduce additional evidence, in the interest of justice - Appeal disposed of.
