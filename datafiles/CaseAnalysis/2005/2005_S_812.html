Gaya Prasad Kar v Subrata Kumar Banerjee

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 650; (2005) 8 SCC 14C; AIR 2005 SC 4348; JT 2005 (12) SC 127; 2005(8) SCALE 40; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 891

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956 - Ss. 17(2) and 2A - Eviction suit on ground of default in payment of rent - Order Passed by Civil Judge( Junior Division) Howrah holding that appellant tenant was not a defaulter in payment of rents - On Revision application, High Court held that appellant tenant was defaulter in payment of rent from month of march 1994 - On a suit for eviction being filed tenant was required to deposit rent either in court or with Rent Controller or pay to landlord an amount equivalent to rate of rent at which it was last paid, for period for which tenant may have made default, including period subsequent thereto up to end of month previous to that in which deposit of rent was made together with interest at rate indicated therein - No willful default in tendering of rents by tenant to landlord - It was only on account of initial refusal of landlord that tenant was compelled to tender rents for month of March 1994 for second time which was according to time prescribed under S. 4 of Act - Hence finding of Civil Judge (Junior Division) that appellant-tenant was not a defaulter was justified - Since S. 17 (2A) confers power upon court to extend time for making deposits of all arrears for period in default, application made by appellant-tenant under S. 5 of Limitation Act for condonation of delay in initial tendering of rent was justified - Impugned order of High Court being unsustainable set aside and that of Civil Judge (Junior Division) restored
