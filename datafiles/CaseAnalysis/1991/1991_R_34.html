Munindra Kumar and Others v Rajiv Govil and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 245; (1991) 3 SCC 368; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1052; AIR 1991 SC 1607; 1991 (16) ATC 928; 1991 (2) CLR 356; 1991 (63) FLR 128; JT 1991 (2) SC 537; 1991 LabIC 1468; 1991 (2) LLJ 103; 1991 (2) LLN 415; 1991(1) SCALE 635; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 812; 1991 (3) SLR 712; 1991 (2) UJ 199; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 821

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Personality

Summary: Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch 2, item 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 136 and 311 - Appointment - Mode of selection - Interview and Group discussion - Allocation of marks - (A) Marks alloted should not exceed 15% of total marks - Appointment of Assistant Engineer in Electricity Board - Rule prescribing 40% marks to interview and group discussion - Whether arbitrary? - (B) Equal marks cannot be allotted to interview and group discussion, if so then what should be criterion - (C) Equitable relief - Petition challenging recruitment Rule with prayer to quash selection - Held, (A) Rule quashed, being arbitrary - (B) 5% of total marks should be allotted to group discussion and 10% to interview - (C) Relief of quashing selection not granted though rule prescribing 40% marks was arbitrary - Appeal partly allowed.
