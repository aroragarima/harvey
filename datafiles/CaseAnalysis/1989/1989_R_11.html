Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Interpretation of s. 30(2) of the Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Before the enactment of the Amendment Act, solatium was provided u/s. 23(2) of the Land Acquisition Act (Parent Act) at 15% on the market value of the land computed in accordance with s. 23(1) of the Act, the solatium being provided in consideration of the compulsory nature of the acquisition - S. 15 of the Amendment Act amended s. 23(2) of the Parent Act and substituted the words '30 per centum' in place of the words '15 per centum' - (A) Whether under the Parent Act as amended by the Amendment Act the claimants were entitled to solatium at 30 per cent of the market value irrespective of the dates on which the acquisition proceedings were initiated or the dates on which the award had been passed? - Held, s. 30(2) of the Amendment Act extended the benefits of the enhanced solatium to cases where the award by the Collector or by the Court was made between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984 or to appeals against such awards decided by the HC and the SC whether the decisions of the HC or the SC were rendered before 24 September, 1984 or after that date - All that was relevant was that the award by the Collector or by the Court should have been made between 30 April, 1982 arid 24 September, 1984 - Hence, benefit of the enhanced solatium was intended by s. 30(2) in respect of an award made by the Collector between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984 - Likewise the benefit of the enhanced solatium was extended by s. 30(2) to the case of an award made by the Court between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984, even though it be upon reference from an award made before 30 April 1982 - (B) Meaning of the words 'or to any order passed by the HC or SC on appeal against any such award' - Whether it was limited to appeals against an award of the Collector or the Court made between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984, or do they include also appeals disposed of between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984 even though arising out of awards of the Collector or the Court made before 30 April, 1982? - Held, Parliament identified the appeal before the HC and the appeal before the SC by describing it as an appeal against 'any such award' - Having regard to the existing hierarchical structure of for a contemplated in the parent Act these appellate orders could only be orders arising in appeal against the award of the Collector or of the Court - The words 'any such award' were intended to have deeper significance, and in the context in which those words appear in s. 30(2) it was clear that they were intended to refer to awards made by the Collector or Court between 30 April, 1982 and 24 September, 1984 - Order accordingly.
