Narcotics Control Bureau v Murlidhar Soni and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1496; (2004) 5 SCC 151; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1561; AIR 2004 SC 4524; 2004 CRLJ 3850; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 358; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 900; 2004 (62) RLT 366; 2004(5) SCALE 322; 2004 (2) UC 934

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 42, 43, 50, 57 - Search - Recovery of heroin - Arrest - Acquittal - Justifiability - Raiding party went to accused persons and expressed their desire to search bag carried by accused - Two accused persons expressed their desire to be searched by Gazetted Officer and Assistant Director, Narcotics Control Bureau being Gazetted Officer conducted search of cloth bag from which 2.41 kg. of heroin was recovered and arrested them - Trial Court convicted them accordingly, as against convicted accused persons preferred appeal before HC which came to be decided by impugned judgment, allowing said appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether finding of Courts below that there had been violation of ss. 42, 43, 50 and 57 of the Act was wholly erroneous - Held, it was not necessary to go into legal arguments as to applicability of various provisions of the Act since appeal could be disposed of on factual finding arrived at by HC - Therefore, Court had not express any opinion on findings of HC on applicability of ss. 42, 43, 50 and 57 of the Act and proceed to examine facts which justify dismissal of appeal - It was to be noted that even though accused submitted before Court that they had suffered injuries at hands of investigating agency - Defence of prosecution that these injuries might have been suffered by accidental fall of accused, could not be accepted, thus, Court was in agreement with finding of HC that statements of accused persons had been obtained by prosecuting agency under duress - Hence, since prosecution had not placed any material to show conscious possession of contraband by respondent, it was held that contentions advanced on behalf of respondent as to possession of contraband by respondent had to be accepted - Appeal dismissed.
