Government of Andhra Pradesh and Another v N. Ramanaiah

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 805; (2009) 7 SCC 165; 2009 (5) ALD(SC) 7; 2010 (124) FLR 425; 2009(8) SCALE 408; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 754; 2009 (4) SLR 575

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, A.P.Civil Services (CC & A) Rules, 1991, Kerala Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1957

Summary: Service - A.P. Civil Services (CC & A) Rules, 1991, r.20(3) - A.P. Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309, 310 and 311 - Dismissal - Appointing authority and the authority ordering dismissal - Order of dismissal passed by Govt. of AP - Order of dismissal upheld by A.P. Administrative Tribunal - Reversed by High Court, on ground that the impugned order of dismissal was passed by the State Govt. imposing the major punishment of dismissal from service depriving the employee of his right of appeal provided under respective rules framed u/art. 309 of Constitution which regulates the service conditions of the Govt. employees; HC concluded that a valuable right of appeal has been denied to the respondent delinquent since the appellate authority itself has passed the impugned order of dismissal instead of appointing authority prescribed under the Rules - Whether the impugned order of dismissal passed by the Government of Andhra Pradesh suffers from any illegality on the ground that it had deprived the respondent of his valuable right to prefer an appeal had it been passed by the disciplinary authority which is subordinate to the Government or whether the impugned order of the Government dismissing the respondent from service suffers from any jurisdictional error? - Held, No - Constitutional importance of civil services noted - Trinity provisions of arts. 309, 310 and 311 deliberated upon, Rules drafted thereunder analysed, contentions heard - Art. 311(1) does not command that the dismissal must be by the very same authority who made the appointment or by its direct superior; dismissal can be either by the appointing authority or by any other authority to which the appointing authority is subordinate - In instant case Engineer-in-chief was the appointing authority for the post that was held by the respondent/delinquent at the time of initiation of disciplinary enquiry, who was undoubtedly subordinate to the Govt.; thus it cannot be said that the Govt. had no jurisdiction or the authority under Rules to impose a major penalty on a member of subordinate service - There is nothing in the Constitution which debars the Govt. from exercising the powers of appointing authority to dismiss a Govt. servant from service - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
