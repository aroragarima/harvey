C. R. Seshan and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 61; (1989) SCC (L&S) 530; (1989) Supp1 SCC 610; AIR 1989 SC 681; 1989 (11) ATC 376; 1989 (58) FLR 760; JT 1989 (1) SC 542; 1989 LabIC 1332; 1989 (2) LLJ 177; 1989(1) SCALE 647; 1989 (2) SLJ 175; 1989 (2) SLR 181; 1989 (1) UJ 582

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Parity in pay and service conditions - Entitled to - Whether basis of classification of Private Secretaries from Personal Assistants is arbitrary? - Relied on State of U.P. v. J. P. Chaurasia (1989-I-LLJ-309) - Held, there is a difference in quality of work performed by persons of longer experience and a higher pay scale to avoid stagnation or resultant frustration for lack of promotional avenues is very common for career service entitlement to higher pay scales depending upon seniority cum-merit or merit-seniority and distinction so made in same cadre will not amount to discrimination - It was also stated that classification based on experience is a reasonable classification having a rational nexus - Petition dismissed.
