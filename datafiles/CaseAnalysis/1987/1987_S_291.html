Gonal Bihimappa v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28791; (1987) Supp SCC 207; (1988) SCC (L&S) 105; AIR 1987 SC 2359; 1987 (5) ATC 205; JT 1987 (3) SC 321; 1987 (2) LLJ 442; 1987(2) SCALE 323; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 885; 1988 (1) SLJ 140; 1987 (4) SLR 526; 1987 (2) UJ 610

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Karnataka Administrative Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1957 - Karnataka Civil Services (General Recruitment) Rules, 1977 - Promotion - obstinate problem of inter se seniority - Between direct recruits and promotes - Sustainability - Obstinate problem of inter se seniority, amongst officers in State Administrative Service had fallen for determination in this group of appeals by special leave and writ petitions u/art. 32 of the Constitution at the instance of both direct recruits and promotes - It was not disputed that under the relevant Rules in regard to Class I Junior Scale posts there was a quota system- 889 two-thirds of the vacancies had to be filled up by promotion by selection from Class II Officers and the remaining one- third by direct recruitment by competitive examination to be held by the Public Service Commission - When direct recruitment had not been made timely as envisaged by the scheme in the Rules officiating promotions were given in respect of posts covered by the direct recruit quota - Such temporary promotions remained effective for a number of years some- times varying between 5 and 8 and later when the vacancies within the direct recruitment quota were filled up, the appointments made in latter years were deemed to carry weight age for seniority on the footing of deemed filling up when vacancies had arisen - Thus the dispute as to seniority inter se between those who had manned the promotional posts beyond the 2 3rds limit and the direct recruits subsequently appointed had come for judicial determination - Held, SC took the view that temporary posts which were created due to exigencies of service, were posts which were outside the cadre - In other words, the SC seemed to have thought that temporary posts added to the cadre were ex-cadre posts - Attention of the SC did not appear to have been drawn to Note 2 to r. 49 of 1977 Rules which classified temporary posts into two categories, namely, posts created to perform the ordinary work for which permanent posts already exist in the cadre, and isolated posts created for the performance of special task unconnected with the ordinary work which a service was called upon to perform - HC in the judgment in one case case relied upon Note 2 of r. 49 of 1977 Rules and thought that SC was not properly informed of the factual situation when in another case it said that temporary posts were not to be taken into consideration for the purpose of working out the quota - Note to r. 49 of 1977 rules had indeed no bearing on the point and SC was of the view that there was really no justification for the doubt indicated by the HC - Apart from the fact that the conclusion of SC in one case on that score was a binding authority on it, from an examination of the matter SC also reiterated that conclusion to be correct - V.P. Badami etc. v. State of Mysore & Ors, 1975 Indlaw SC 51, relied on - Appeals disposed of.
