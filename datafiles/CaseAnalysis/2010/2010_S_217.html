Sanjay Kumar Jain v State Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1091; (2011) 11 SCC 733; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 649; 2010 (175) DLT 614; JT 2010 (13) SC 377; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 309; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 270; 2010(13) SCALE 544; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 16

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304B - Murder - Dowry - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was charged u/ss. 302 and 304B of the IPC - Trial Court held that the charge u/s. 302 of IPC was established against the accused, therefore, there was no necessity to discuss the next alternative charge u/s. 304B of IPC - Conviction was upheld by the HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Conviction u/s. 302 of IPC - Whether the appellant can be convicted u/s. 302 of IPC? - Held, in a case of circumstantial evidence all circumstances must lead to the conclusion that the appellant was the only one who had committed the crime and none else - However, in the present case, in view of infirmities in the prosecution's version the conviction u/s. 302 of the IPC cannot be sustained - Appellant acquitted as far as his conviction u/s. 302 of the IPC concerned - (B) Conviction u/s. 304B - Whether appellant can be convicted u/s. 304B of the IPC? - Held, in order to bring home the guilty u/s. 304B of IPC the following ingredients are necessary: (i) The victim was subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or his relatives; (ii) Such cruelty or harassment was for, or in connection with any demand for dowry; (iii) Such cruelty or harassment was done within seven years of the marriage - In the present case, all these three ingredients were available - Deceased was subjected to cruelty and harassment by her husband, the appellant and the harassment was in connection with the demand of dowry and deceased died within one year and two months of the marriage - Hence, it was proved that the appellant committed an offence u/s. 304B of the IPC - Appellant convicted u/s. 304B of the IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
