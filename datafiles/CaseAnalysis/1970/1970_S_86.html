Munney Khan v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 155; (1970) 2 SCC 480; (1970) SCC (Cr) 491; AIR 1971 SC 1491; 1971 ALJ 1299; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 943

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 96, 97, 98, 99, 101, 100 , Exception 2 , 300, 302 and 304 - Right of private defence - (A) Exceeding right - Deceased picked up quarrel with accused's brother -Sitting on his chest and giving him fist blows - Accused on failing to stop him giving knife blow at the back, cause his death - (B) Right of private defence Scope and limitation of - Duty of Court - Held, culpable homicide not amounting to murder committed as death was caused in exercise of right of private defence, but by exceeding that right , so conviction must be under first part of s.304 and not u/s. 302 - Courts have to be careful in seeing that no one on the mere pretext of the exercise of the right of private defence takes sides in a quarrel between two or more persons and inflicts injuries on the one or the other , in a free fight without disclosing as to who is the initial aggressor it may be dangerous as a general rule to clothe either of them or his sympathiser with a right of private defence - Appeal Partly Allowed.
