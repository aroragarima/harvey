Amarendu Jyoti and others v State of Chhatisgarh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 550; (2014) 12 SCC 362; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4502; 2014 (4) JCC 2365; JT 2014 (9) SC 353; 2014(9) SCALE 162

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 178, 179, 482 - FIR - Jurisdiction - Continuing offence - Sustainability - Appellant No. 1/husband of respondent No. 2 challenged the order passed by HC in dismissing appellants' application u/s. 482 CrPC and holding that the FIR lodged by respondent No. 3 was liable to be tried by the Court at Ambikapur, which had jurisdiction to try the offence - Main contention of appellants was that the incident of cruelty alleged by respondent No. 2 took place only at Delhi, where the couple resided after which the respondent No. 2 went to stay with her parents at Ambikapur in the State of Chhattisgarh, therefore, the Court at Ambikapur had no jurisdiction to try the alleged offence against the appellants in the FIR u/s. 498-A, IPC lodged by respondent No. 3 - Hence instant appeal - Whether the allegations made in the FIR constitute a continuing offence -

Held, from the FIR that all the incidents alleged by complainant in respect of the alleged cruelty were said to have occurred at Delhi - Cruel and humiliating words spoken to respondent No. 2/wife by her husband, elder brother-in-law and elder sister-in-law for bringing less dowry were said to have been uttered at Delhi - Allegedly, arbitrary demands of lakhs of rupees in dowry have been made in Delhi - Incident of beating and dragging respondent No. 2 and abusing her in filthy language also was said to have taken place at Delhi - Suffice it to say that all overt acts, which were said to have constituted cruelty have allegedly taken place at Delhi - Offence of cruelty could not be said to be a continuing one as contemplated by ss. 178 and 179 of Code - SC did not agree with the HC that in instant case the mental cruelty inflicted upon the respondent No. 2 'continued unabated' on account of no effort having been made by appellants to take her back to her matrimonial home, and the threats given by appellants over the telephone - It might be noted incidentally that the HC does not make reference to any particular piece of evidence regarding the threats said to have been given by appellants over the telephone - Thus, going by complaint, SC was of the view that it could not be held that the Court at Ambikapur has jurisdiction to try the offence since the appropriate Court at Delhi would have jurisdiction to try the said offence - Appeal allowed.
