Dr. Smt. Shipra v Shri Shanti Lal Khoiwal

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 630; (1996) 5 SCC 181; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 429; AIR 1996 SC 1691; AIR 1996 SCW 1772; 1996 (2) CCC 147; 1996 (2) CLT 110; JT 1996 (4) SC 67; 1996(3) SCALE 369; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1175; 1996 (3) Supreme 694; 1996 (2) UJ 228

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 83(1)(c) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 81 - Whether copy of affidavit supplied to respondent without attestation portion contained in it (though contained in original affidavit) can be considered to be a 'true copy'? - When a petitioner is enjoined to file an election petition accompanied by an affidavit duly sworn by applicant duly verifying diverse allegations of corrupt practices imputed to returned candidate and attested by prescribed authority it would be obvious that statute intended that it shall be performed in same manner as prescribed in Form 25 r/w r. 94-A of Rules - Attestation of affidavit by prescribed authority, therefore, is an integral part of election petition - Verification by a Notary or any other prescribed authority is a vital act which assures that election petitioner had affirmed before Notary etc. that statement containing imputation of corrupt practices was duly and solemnly verified to be correct statement to best of his knowledge or information as specified in election petition and affidavit filed in support thereof, that reinforces assertions - Thus affirmation before prescribed authority in affidavit and supply of its true copy should also contain such affirmation so that returned candidate would not be misled in his understanding that imputation of corrupt practices was solemnly affirmed or duly verified before prescribed authority - For that purpose, Form 25 mandates verification before prescribed authority - Therefore, compliance of statutory requirement is an integral part of election petition and true copy supplied to returned candidate should as a sine qua non contain due verification and attestation by prescribed authority and certified to be true copy by election petitioner in his/her own signature - Held, election petition not maintainable due to material defect in true copy as it was not duly signed before notary etc. - Appeal dismissed.
