Harbhajan Singh v State Of Himachal Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 991; (2010) 13 SCC 555; JT 2010 (13) SC 189; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 934; 2011 (113) RD 101; 2010(12) SCALE 372

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Himachal Pradesh Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1971, Himachal Pradesh Land Revenue Act, 1954, Himachal Pradesh Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Rules, 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Himachal Pradesh Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1971 - Consolidation proceedings - Cancellation of notification - Legality - Director of Land Consolidation Department, Government of Himachal Pradesh, issued a notice u/s. 14 of the Act declaring that Government has made a plan for land consolidation of few villages including Revenue Estate Damtal Khas land owned by appellant and the respondent No.2 - During the consolidation operation, State Government of Himachal Pradesh issued a notification dated 27.09.1999 under sub-section (1) of s. 16 of the Act cancelling the declaration of the Director to the extent the declaration related to appellant's land - As a consequence, Revenue Estate Damtal Khas ceased to be under consolidation operation with effect from 27.09.1999 - Writ petition was filed before HC - HC held that the notification u/s. 16(1) of the Act cancelling the declaration u/s. 14 of the Act was issued by the State Government in larger public interest and was also issued before the closure of the consolidation operation u/s. 15(1) of the Act and was not arbitrary and unreasonable - HC held that the notification dated 27.09.1999 issued u/s. 16(1) of the Act was legal, valid and in accordance with law - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether for any area where the consolidation operation has not been closed by publication of a notification u/s. 15 of the Act, the State Government has the power u/s. 16(1) of the Act to cancel the declaration made u/s. 14 in respect of that area? - Held, as per s. 16(1) of the Act, State Government has the power to cancel the declaration made u/s. 14 in respect of any area "at any time" - Expression "at any time" in Section 16(1) of the Act though wide is controlled by other provisions of the Act - Hence, State Government can issue a notification u/s. 16(1) of the Act cancelling the declaration u/s. 14(1) of the Act in respect of any area at any time before the persons entitled to possession of holdings under the Act have entered into possession of the holdings allotted to them - In the present case, since the consolidation scheme had not come into force in the Revenue Estate Damtal, the State Government had the power u/s. 16(1) of the Act to cancel the declaration u/s. 14(1) of the Act - (B) Principles of natural justice - Whether State Government was bound to follow the principles of natural justice before issuing the notification dated 27.09.1999 u/s. 16(1) of the Act cancelling the declaration u/s. 14(1) of the Act in respect of the Revenue Estate Damtal? - Held, s. 16(1) of the Act does not state that a show cause notice is to be issued to any party or that any party has to be heard before a notification is issued thereunder - Further, a notification u/s. 16(1) of the Act issued by the State Government before delivery of possession of the allotted holdings to persons has no civil consequences and therefore the State Government not required to follow the principles of natural justice before issuing such a notification - (C) Whether State Government acted arbitrarily, unreasonably and in a malafide manner in cancelling the declaration u/s. 14 of the Act in respect of Revenue Estate Damtal by the notification dated 27.09.1999? - Held, where after a declaration is made u/s. 14(1) of the Act, the State Government finds that interest of general public and purposes of better cultivation do not warrant the consolidation operation to continue in a particular area, it can cancel the declaration made u/s. 14(1) of the Act by issuing a notification u/s. 16(1) of the Act and such cancellation of the declaration will not be extraneous to the object of the Act - In the present case, Deputy Commissioner reported that interest of general public and better cultivation of land would not be achieved by continuing the consolidation operation - Dominant purpose for issuing a notification u/s. 16(1) of the Act was, therefore, not extraneous but in accord with the objects of the Act - HC was, thus, right in coming to the conclusion that the notification issued by the State Government u/s. 16(1) of the Act in relation to Revenue Estate Damtal was legal and valid - Appeal dismissed.
