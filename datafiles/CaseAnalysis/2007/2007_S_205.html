The New India Assurance Company Limited v Smt. Kalpana & Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 26; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 94; (2007) 3 SCC 538; 2007 (1) ACC 356; AIR 2007 SC 1243; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 122; 2007 (2) ALJ 766; 2007 (67) ALR 488; 2007 (1) CCC 258; 2007 (1) CTC 523; 2007 (1) JCC 532; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 41; JT 2007 (2) SC 353; 2007 (4) MahLJ 411; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1173; 2007 (145) PLR 652; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 772; 2007(2) SCALE 227; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 985

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Pecuniary Benefit, Measure Of Damages, Subject Matter Of Insurance, Assessment Of Damages, Award Of Damages, Fatal Accident Act, 1976

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Matter of fixing the appropriate multiplier and computation of compensation - Deceased was about 33 years of age at the time of accident - High Court fixed compensation by applying multiplier of 17 and monthly income was taken at Rs.4, 000/-- Appeal against - Held, highest multiplier of 18 has to be for the age group of 21 years to 25 years when an ordinary Indian citizen starts independently earning - Considering the age of the deceased it would be appropriate to fix the multiplier at 13 - Absence of definite material about the income - Contribution to the family is fixed at Rs.3, 000/- per month i.e. annually Rs.36, 000/- - Applying the multiplier of 13, the compensation works out to Rs.4, 68, 000/ - Appeal partly allowed.
