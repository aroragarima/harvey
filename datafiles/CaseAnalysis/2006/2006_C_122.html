Anjaleem Enterprises Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 162; (2006) 2 SCC 336; 2006 (194) E.L.T. 129; JT 2006 (1) SC 353; 2006 (72) RLT 631; 2006(1) SCALE 314; 2006 (2) SCJ 225; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 L - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - (A) whether a programmed or designed EPROM is an integral part of STD-PCO Unit? - Held, in present case, levy is on a computer based embedded system - Software embedded in programmed EPROM, which is an IC chip, constitutes 'brain' of system - Programmed EPROM is an integral part of system - Levy is on unit - Levy is not on programmed EPROM - Programme embedded is not an easily removable - Hence, it will not fall in category of recorded media under tariff item 85 24 and remains an IC under tariff item 85.42 - (B) Whether essential character of programmed EPROM, in present case, as an IC changed to become a 'recorded media' or a software under CH 85.24 - Further held, EPROM cannot be compared to a floppy - Floppy is a dumb box - That is not case with EPROM - EPROM is basically an integrated circuit or a chip - EPROM is, therefore, classifiable as an integrated circuit under tariff item 85.42 - Appeal dismissed.
