R.C. Patuck v Fatima A. Kindasa & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2022; (1997) 5 SCC 334; (1997) SCC (Cr) 679; AIR 1997 SC 2320; 1997 (3) AWC 1658; 1997 (4) Bom.C.R. 306; 1997 (3) CCR 1; 1997 (2) Crimes 102; 1997 CRLJ 2756; JT 1997 (5) SC 468; 1997(4) SCALE 58; 1997 (2) SCJ 85; 1997 (4) Supreme 503

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 145(4), Proviso - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Restoration of possession - Landlady permitting certain party to occupy portion in premises - Occupants constructing wall and dividing said portion from portion in occupation of landlady - Allowing some other to occupy property claiming him to be her husband - Held, not different from ordinary cases where a person permits licencee or tenant to occupy premises and on termination of licence or tenancy, licensee or tenant does not vacate or makes some construction - No relief - Landlady was 75 years old and occupant was engaged in similar litigations at other place, is not ground for relief.
