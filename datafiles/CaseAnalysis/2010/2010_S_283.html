R.D. Gardi Medical College & Anr. Etc. v State Of M.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 811; (2010) 10 SCC 225; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 35; 2011 (1) JabLJ 346; JT 2010 (11) SC 157; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 483; 2010(10) SCALE 371; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Admission Rules, 2008, M.P. Niji Vyavsayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyaman Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007

Summary: Education - Admission Rules, 2008, r. 8 - M.P. Niji Vyavsayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyaman Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007 - Distribution and allocation of seats - Writ petition was filed challenging the legality of the admissions of 19 students who were not eligible in terms of the relevant rules - HC declared the same to be illegal - On appeal, SC held that the college was not justified in giving admission to students who were not eligible in terms of the relevant rules - SC, however, permitted the students to continue their studies but directed that an equal number of seats shall be reduced from the management quota of the college for the academic session 2009-2010 - Pursuant thereto, entrance test was held for the academic session 2009-2010 - Meanwhile, writ petition was filed challenging the constitutional validity of M.P. Niji Vyavsayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyaman Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007 which was disposed of by the HC - On appeal, SC made an interim arrangement under which 15% seats were to be first excluded towards NRI quota to be filled up by the private institutions and out of remaining 85% seats available for admission to the under-graduate and post-graduate courses 50% were to be given to the State Government while the remaining 50% were to be filled up on the basis of a selection process to be conducted by the Association of Private Medical and Dental Colleges - Thereafter, Government of Madhya Pradesh constituted a Counseling Committee, who undertook an exercise for distribution and allocation of seats in the appellant-college for the academic session 2010-11 - Meanwhile, writ petition was filed inter alia, praying for a writ of certiorari quashing the allocation and distribution of seats made by the appellant-college - It was alleged that allocation of seats between the management and the State was not proper nor was the reduction of 21 seats from the management quota given effect to as directed by this Court - HC allowed the writ petitions and directed 10 seats to be reduced from out of the management quota for the academic session 2010-11 - HC held that 21 seats permitted to be reduced from the management quota was erroneously reduced from the total of 100 seats available in the college which was not correct understanding of the order passed by SC - HC directed 5 seats which were wrongly filled up by the management of the college for the session 2009-10 to be reduced from out of the management quota taking the total number of seats to be reduced for the session 2010-2011 to 15 - Hence, present appeals - (A) Whether HC rightly directed that 5 seats which were wrongly filled up by the management of the college for the session 2009-10 to be reduced from out of the management quota? - Held, yes - Reduction of the seats had to be from out of the management quota alone - Counseling Committee and the government officials adopted a wrong method of calculating the seats by reducing 21 seats from the total number of 100 seats - Above method of calculation was not the correct method to be adopted in the matter - Direction of SC that 15% seats towards NRI will be first reduced from the total was ignored by the authorities - By doing so the reduction of 21 seats has taken place vis-'-vis not only the management quota but even the State quota - (B) Whether direction regarding surrender of 5 unfilled NRI seats for the session 2009-2010 to the State was justified? - Held, unfilled NRI seats had to be transferred to the general pool to be filled up on the basis of the merit of the candidates in the State level common entrance test - Unfilled seats in the NRI quota were, therefore, to be treated as a part of the general pool and once that was done the share of the college in terms of the order passed by SC would be 50% out of the said seats - However, as the appellant-college utilized the unfilled NRI seats all by itself it had committed clear irregularity justifying reduction of the excess seats during the session 2010-2011 - HC rightly held that while the management was justified in filling up 5 unfilled seats in NRI quota, the remaining 5 could not have been filled up otherwise than on the basis of the entrance test referred to in r. 8 - Appeals dismissed.
