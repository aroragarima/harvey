M/s. Mangalore Ganesh Beedi Works v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 63; (2005) 2 SCC 329; AIR 2005 SC 1308; 2005 (4) CompLJ 29; [2005] 273 ITR 56; JT 2005 (2) SC 442; 2005(1) SCALE 642; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 882; [2005] 142 TAXMAN 720

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-Tax Act, 1961, s. 260A - Appeals were filed by revenue questioning correctness of certain conclusions arrived at by Income-Tax Appellate Tribunal in appeals filed by assessee as well as revenue - Held, it is true in an order of affirmation, repetition of reasons elaborately may not be necessary - But arguments advanced, points urged have to be dealt with - Reasons for affirmation have to be indicated, though in appropriate cases they may be briefly stated - Remitted matter to HC - Appeals disposed of.
