Sanjay Bahal v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1994

Case Analysis

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 104; 1995 CRLJ 1650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 201 - Evidence Act, s. 27 - Bail application - Name of applicant not mentioned in First Information Report but his name surfaced during investigation - Confessional statements of applicant and co-accused - Recoveries effected from applicant - Right of liberty is not an absolute one - If a person is accused of an offence and there is sufficient material available to incriminate him and it has come on record he is likely to tamper with evidence or he is likely to create terror or disturbance in society, his right of liberty can be curtailed by keeping him in jail for such a period as may be found necessary - Held, prima facie case against applicant and co-accused - Applicant and co-accused also tried to tamper with dead body - Not safe to release applicant on bail - Bail application rejected.
