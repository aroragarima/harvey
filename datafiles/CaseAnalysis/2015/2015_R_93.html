Laxmidevamma and others v Ranganath and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 38; (2015) 4 SCC 264; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 122; 2015 (109) ALR 638; 2015 (2) AWC 1804; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 313; JT 2015 (2) SC 228; 2015 (6) MahLJ 11; 2015 (4) MLJ 124; 2015 (4) MPLJ 270; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 591; 2015 (127) RD 529; 2015(1) SCALE 489; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 921; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 58

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - concurrent findings of fact - Interference by HC - Appellants-plaintiffs were the owners of the revenue land which was converted for non-agricultural purpose - Layout was formed from the said land and the site Nos.12 and 13 and portions of site Nos.11 and 14 were sold to the defendant No. 1 by the appellants-plaintiffs by executing two sale deeds - 'A' schedule property as shown in the suit was earmarked for the purpose of road - However, the City Development Authority did not approve the same and hence no road was formed - Subsequently, defendant No.1 sold the property defendants No. 2 and 3 - Appellants-plaintiffs filed a suit for declaration that they were the absolute owners of 'A' schedule property and for possession of the 'B' schedule property - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal, First Appellate Court confirmed the judgment and decree of the Trial Court and dismissed the appeal - On second appeal, HC modified the judgment and decree passed by the courts below and held that the appellants-plaintiffs were not entitled for a declaration that they are the absolute owners of the suit 'A' schedule property and consequently their claim for injunction was also not granted - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, based on oral and documentary evidence, both the courts below have recorded concurrent findings of fact that appellants-plaintiffs have established their right in 'A' schedule property. In the light of concurrent findings of fact, no substantial questions of law arose in the HC and there was no substantial ground for reappreciation of evidence. The HC did not keep in view that the concurrent findings recorded by the courts below, are based on oral and documentary evidence and the judgment of the HC cannot be sustained. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC, concurrent findings of fact cannot be upset by the HC unless the findings so recorded are shown to be perverse.
