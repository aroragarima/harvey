Union of India v Indalco Industries

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 330; (2003) 5 SCC 194; AIR 2003 SC 4086; AIR 2003 SCW 2062; 2003 (87) ECC 1; 2003 (108) ECR 3; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 481; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 567; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 808; 2003 (56) RLT 744; 2003(3) SCALE 670; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 377; 2003 (4) SLT 206; 2004 (135) STC 281; 2003 (3) Supreme 170

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Officer, Excisability of goods

Summary: Central Excise Act, 1944 - S. 4(1)(a) and 4(1)(b) - whether the High Court is justified in quashing paragraphs (14) to (18) of the impugned show cause notice dated June 30, 1995 - Held, allegations contained in paragraphs (14) to (18), proceed on the basis as if the valuation of the goods ought to be under Section 4(1)(b) ignoring the provision of Section 4(1)(a). There being no valid foundation for ignoring the price under Section 4(1)(a), the authority lacks jurisdiction to issue notice calling upon the assessee to show cause in the matter. Further held, if an authority which has jurisdiction in regard to one aspect takes upon itself to make enquiry into a matter in respect of which it had no jurisdiction then merely because in regard to one aspect it has jurisdiction, the court cannot ignore the fact of lack of jurisdiction and allow the Tribunal to proceed with the matter in respect of which it has no jurisdiction to make inquiry.
