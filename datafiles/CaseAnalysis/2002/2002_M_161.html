Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi v Messrs Maruti Udyog Limited and Hilton Rubbers

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Shivaraj V. Patil, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 133; (2002) 3 SCC 547; AIR 2002 SC 1245; AIR 2002 SCW 1039; 2002 (1) CRC 655; 2002 (80) ECC 249; 2002 (141) E.L.T. 3; JT 2002 (2) SC 469; 2002 (49) RLT 1; 2002(2) SCALE 405; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 99; 2002 (2) SLT 337; 2002 (2) Supreme 260

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: When cum-duty price is charged, then in arriving at the excisable value of the goods the element of duty which is payable has to be excluded, and therefore, the Tribunal has rightly proceeded on the basis that the amount realised by the respondent from the sale of scrap has to be regarded as a normal wholesale price and in determining the value on which excise duty is payable the element of excise duty which must be regarded as having been incorporated in the sale price, must be excluded.
