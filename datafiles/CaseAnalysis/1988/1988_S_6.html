Smt. Rajbir Kaur And Anr. v S. Chokesiri And Co.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 615; (1989) 1 SCC 19; AIR 1988 SC 1845; JT 1988 (3) SC 593; 1988 (2) RCJ 316; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 328; 1988 (2) RentLR 553; 1988(2) SCALE 461; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 310; 1988 (2) UJ 554

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Form Of Pleadings

Summary: Rent Control - CPC, 1908 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949 - Indian Easements Act, 1882 - Evidence Act, 1872 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.105 - (A) Whether lease or licence - (B) Revision - Powers of High Court - Concurrent findings of fact - Interference by HC - Permissibility - (C) Eviction sought on ground of subletting - Tenants plea that there was not even a pleading by landlord on the point of money consideration for parting of possession - Landlord pleaded 'subletting' - (D) Whether an inference that the parting of the exclusive possession was prompted by monetary consideration could be drawn or not - Held, tuns on operative intention of parties as exclusive possession itself is not decisive test - Not permissible - Held, in the circumstances the pleadings could be construed liberally - Permissible - Appeal allowed.
