Saurabh Prakash v Dlf Universal Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1271; (2007) 1 SCC 228; 2007 (1) CCC 269; 2007 (1) CompLJ 215; 2007 (1) CPJ(Sc) 4; 2006(12) SCALE 531; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 625

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Compensation Application

Summary: Contract & Commercial - MRTP - Practice & Procedure - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 70 & 74 - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969, ss. 2(o),2(u), 12-B & 36-A - Breach of Contract - Compensation - Justifiability - Appellant approached respondent for sale of properties and paid earnest money - Appellant agreed terms of respondent - Respondent unilaterally altered time of period of handing over possession, which burdened appellant with interest for such periods - Appellant filed application before Commission ,for breach of contract, which held that appellant was not seeking possession of apartment but was seeking refund of money deposited by him along with interest - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that s. 36-A of 1969, Act was clearly attracted and there had been no cancellation, offer remained valid - Further insistence on part of developer not to deposit further amount by way of additional charges - Whether act of respondents amounts to breach of contract or deficiency in service - Held, Act was enacted to provide operation of economic system does not result in concentration of economic power to common detriment for control of monopolies, for prohibition of monopolistic and restrictive trade practices, as well as matters connected or incidental there to - In instant case, power of commission to award compensation was restricted to case where loss or damage had been caused as result of restrictive or unfair trade practice - It had no jurisdiction where damage was claimed for mere breach of contract - Thus, Commission had no jurisdiction in entertaining such case - Appeal disposed of.
