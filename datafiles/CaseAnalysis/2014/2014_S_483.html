Lalit Kumar Yadav @ Kurhi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 299; (2014) 11 SCC 129; 2014 CRLJ 2712; JT 2014 (5) SC 536; 2014 (3) MLJ(Crl) 53; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 905; 2014(5) SCALE 616; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 r/w s. 511 - Murder - Rape - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly attempted to rape prosecutrix and later murdered her - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 376 r/w s. 511 IPC - Trial Court convicted and imposed death sentence - Appeal before HC - HC affirmed the order recorded by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, question of quantum of sentence was raised that accused was young at time of commission of offence, that's 21 years of age, that he had no intention to kill deceased and there was no past criminal antecedent - Trial Court and HC wrongly held that case of accused was similar to that of Dhananjoy Chatterjee Alias Dhana v State Of West Bengal & Ors. 2004 Indlaw SC 235 - In instant case, circumstantial evidence concluded that accused attempted to commit rape and because of resistance he committed murder of deceased - Accused was aged about 21 years at the time of offence, initially when matter for confirmation of death sentence was heard by two Judges of HC there was divided opinion, one Judge confirmed death sentence while other acquitted accused - It was other Bench which affirmed death sentence - It was not case of prosecution that accused should not be reformed - In fact possibility of his reformation should not be ruled out - There was no criminal antecedent of accused - Court had to consider different parameters as laid down in Machhi Singh And Others v State Of Punjab 1983 Indlaw SC 116; Bachan Singh Etc. Etc. v State Of Punjab Etc. Etc. 1980 Indlaw SC 624 and balance mitigating circumstances against need for imposition of capital punishment - Court opined that considering age of accused, possibility of reforming him should not be ruled out and he should not be termed as social menace - Further, case did not fall under 'rarest of rare' category - Hence, Court was unable to uphold death sentence and death sentence of accused was modified to life imprisonment but affirmed rest part of conviction and sentence - Appeal partly allowed.
