State of Goa and Others v Leukoplast (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1042; (1997) 4 SCC 82; AIR 1997 SC 1875; 1997 (92) E.L.T. 19; JT 1997 (3) SC 322; 1997(2) SCALE 372; 1997 (3) SCJ 68; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 516; 1997 (105) STC 318; 1997 (2) Supreme 625

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Health & Drug - Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, ss. 23(1), 23(3) and 24 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Sales Act, s. 8(2 A) - Whether products manufactured by petitioners and listed in paras. 2 and 4 of petition are 'drugs and medicines'? - Assessee's contention that it has got a licence to manufacture these products under Drugs and Cosmetics Act and its production is controlled at every stage by Drug Control authorities does not conclude matter - It has to be found out how these products are understood and treated in market - Medicinal content and curative function of products is to be ascertained - Held, question is basically of fact and it should be agitated before statutory appellate authority - Assessee should not have been allowed to bypass statutory remedies where questions of fact could have been properly agitated and ascertained - Appeal is allowed and order of High Court is set aside - Assessee-Co. is at liberty to prefer appeal against assessment order in accordance with law within a period of six weeks from date of order.
