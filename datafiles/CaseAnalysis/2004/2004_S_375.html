State Of Mizoram & Another v Mizoram Engineering Service Association and

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 399; (2004) 6 SCC 218; (2004) SCC (L&S) 857; AIR 2004 SC 3644; 2004 (4) AWC 2920; JT 2004 (5) SC 516; 2004 (3) LLN 845; 2004(5) SCALE 629; 2004 (5) SLR 1; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 496; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2230

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Parliament & Legislature; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - North-Eastern Areas (Reorganisation) Act, 1971 - Central Civil Services (Revised Pay) Amendment Rules, 1987 - Notification No G 12011/3/87 F Est 1989 whereby certain categories of engineers in Mizoram State Engineering Service had been excluded for purposes of revision of pay scales - State Govt. had duly accepted recommendations of Fourth Central Pay Commission Having done so, it cannot be permitted to discriminate between individuals and not allow same to rest - Claim of respondents is fully justified by facts on record - Central Govt. as well as State Govt. accepted recommendations of Fourth Central Pay Commission and scales being allowed to members of respondent Association are based on those recommendations - Appeal dismissed.
