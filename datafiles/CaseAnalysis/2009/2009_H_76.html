Bonder & Anr. v Hem Singh (Dead) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 653; (2009) 12 SCC 310; 2009(5) ALL MR 433; 2009 (75) ALR 796; 2009 (4) AWC 3773; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 11; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 775; 2009 (107) RD 354; 2009(8) SCALE 463; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Adverse possession by one co-heir against another - Suit for possession of agricultural land and for future and past mesne profit - Of two brothers one leaving the house to look after maternal uncle's property; on his return he demanded his share of property and mesne profit, which was denied; thus the suit - Defendant pleaded adverse possession - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of plaintiff and in turn 1st appellate Court upheld finding of facts by TC - High Court in second appeal reversed findings of Courts below - Whether such reversal justified or whether plea of adverse possession has any force? - Held, No - 'P.Lakshmi Reddy v. L. Lakshmi Reddy AIR 1957 SC 314'; in order to establish adverse possession of one co-heir as against another it is not enough to show that one of them is in sole possession or enjoyment of the profits of the properties, but ouster of the non-possessing co-heir by the co-heir in possession who claims his possession to be adverse, should be made out; as between co-heirs there must be evidence of open assertion of hostile title, coupled with exclusive possession and enjoyment by one of them to the knowledge of the other so as to constitute ouster - Per revenue records name of plaintiff continues to show that defendant never considered the plaintiff as ousted and not continuing as a co-heir - HC's findings and order found to be perverse, illegal and unsustainable, and deprecated; Court inter alia observed that impugned judgement is against the norms of any civilized society and has demolished the entire fiber of joint family system of our country - Appeal allowed.
