Ramkanali Colliery of Bccl v Workmen By Secretary, Rashtriya Colliery Mazdoor Sangh and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20215; (2001) 4 SCC 236; (2001) SCC (L&S) 689; AIR 2001 SC 1945; 2001 (90) FLR 182; 2001 (34) LabIC 1350; 2001 (1) LLJ 1407; 2001(3) SCALE 14; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 847; 2001 (2) SLR 11; 2001 (3) SLT 161; 2001 (4) Supreme 569

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Colliery, Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Mines & Minerals - Practice & Procedure - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973, ss. 7, 14 - Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972, ss. 9 and 17 - Coal Mines Nationalisation Laws (Amendment) Ordinance and Act, 1986, s. 19 - Whether u/s. 14 of the Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973, a workman who was in the employment on the appointed date i.e. 01-05-1973 alone is entitled to be protected in employment and the tribunal could not have passed the award in the year 1987 that the workmen concerned were workmen of the Ramkanali Colliery at the time of takeover? - If there is a vested right and that right is to be taken away, necessarily the law will have to be retrospective in effect and if such a law retrospectively takes away such a right, it can no longer be contended that the right should be enforced - Held, the right enforced by the employees will not attract the amended provision of the Act which came into force on 15-12-1986; the award made by the tribunal is not wrong in any ways particularly when the decision has been given on facts that as on the date of the takeover the workmen concerned were employees of the appellant management - Workmen never ceased to be employees and all that happened was they were prevented from working in the colliery, which was set right by the award - Appeals dismissed.
