Intelligence Bureau Employees Association and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2853; (1997) 11 SCC 348

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Intelligence Organisations (Restriction Of Rights) Act, 1985, Constitution (Fiftieth Amendment) Act,1984

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 14, 19 and 33 as amended - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Constitution - Whether a result of the impugned amendment the members of the petitioner Association can be deprived of their rights under Part III of the Constitution? - Held - It does not mean that the amendment is destructive of the basic structure of the Constitution - After the amendment of art. 33 by the fiftieth amendment and enactment of Act No. 58 of 1985 the circular memorandum has lost its significance and no purpose would be served by a going into the validity of the same - The writ petitions have, therefore, become infructuous - Dismissed accordingly.
