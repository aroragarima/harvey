Ningappa Yallappa Hosamani and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 721; (2009) 14 SCC 582; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 872; 2010 (4) KarLJ 337; 2009(8) SCALE 59; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 421

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Statement u/s. 27 of Evidence Act, leading to recovery of dead body and incriminating material - Circumstantial evidence - Accused seven in numbers - Conviction by Trial Court - Conviction by HC for the appellants which was converted to u/s. 302 r/w 34 - Conviction whether proper? - Held, Yes - Statement u/s. 27 leading to recovery of dead body buried under ground packed in a gunny bag, further recovery of motor cycle of deceased with the help of a swimmer PW15 - On 30-01-2005 deceased left at about 4.00 pm to a place Mudhol on his motorcycle and met one K at about 8.00pm and their he told K that he was returning to Bommanabudni via Halki; later on at 9.00 pm he was seen at Halki by PW11 going on the motorcycle towards Bommanbudni; on that day after that, deceased had not returned and on next day as well - A1, 2 and 4 to 7 were seen at Bommanabudni bus stand at about 8.00 pm of 30-01-2005 by PW 10; PW 16 saw accused persons in two motorcycles, one of which was being driven by A6 on which A1 was sitting with a gunny bag, and on enquiry about such gunny bag, reply came as to that it contained jaggery block; timing of all this was stated to have happened at about 11 pm on 30-01-2005 and later the same persons were seen near the canal with both the motorcycles, from where dead body was recovered near the canal and motorcycle was recovered from a river; Courts below gave finding as to that dead body was their in the gunny bag in all its possibility; finding given upheld - Held further, prosecution conclusively proved that the A 1, 2, 6 and 7 had disposed of the dead body of deceased by putting it in a gunny bag and burying it at a place near the canal which was detected in furtherance of the voluntary information furnished by A 1 and 2, further recovery of motorcycle was also proved in the same way; natural presumption, in the absence of explanation by them is that it was those two persons, who had murdered deceased and buried the dead body - 'State of Maharashtra vs. Suresh (2000 (1) SCC 471' - Being without merit - Appeals dismissed.
