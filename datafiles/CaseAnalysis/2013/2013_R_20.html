Rajaram Prasad Yadav v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 404; (2013) 14 SCC 461; AIR 2013 SC 3081; 2013 CRLJ 3777; 2013 (3) JCC 2179; JT 2013 (11) SC 118; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 726; 2013(8) SCALE 316; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Re-examination complainant - allowed - Sustainability - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 324, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Respondent no. 2/complainant filed complaint against his brother/accused and appellant/accused (son of respondent no. 2 brother) alleging that at instance of his brother, his brother son fired with a country made pistol at him and caused injury - Respondent no. 2 filed petition dt.24-8-2007 u/s. 311 of CrPC and sought for permission for his re- examination - Additional Public Prosecutor also filed petition on 5-12-2007, for the sam e purpose - Trial Court passed common order on 18-11-2009, dismissing both applications and posted the case for evidence of investigation officers and doctors on 18-12-2009 - On appeal, HC directed Trial Court to allow respondent no. 2 to examine himself as a witness on a specified date by its order dt.9-12-2010 - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that while Trial Court passed reasoned order after hearing both parties extensively, HC passed impugned order in absence of appellant - Further, respondent even without impleading appellant, persuaded HC to pass impugned order, which was not sustainable u/s. 311 of CrPC - Held, respondent no. 2 filed application u/s. 311 of CrPC on 24-8-2007, i.e., nearly after 5 months after his examination by Trial Court - While filing said application, respondent no. 2 claimed that his evidence tendered on 16-3-2007, was not out of his own free will and volition, but due to threat and coercion at instance of accused persons, including appellant - Trial Court examined all factors in its order dt.18-11-2009 and conclusion arrived at by Trial Court were all appropriate and just, while deciding application filed u/s. 311 of CrPC - Applying various principles, SC was convinced that order of Trial Court impugned before HC did not call for any interference in any event behind back of appellant - Impugned order passed HC was set aside, Order of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
