Godhra Electricity Company Limited v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 253; (1975) 1 SCC 199; AIR 1975 SC 32; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 42; 1974 UJ 630

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Constitution - Electricity & Energy - Constitution of India, 1950 -

Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss.6,7,7A - Commencement - License - Expiry - Violation - Electricity License of 'G'/Company was transferred to Appellants/company for period of 50 years - Respondents, stated that initial period of 50 years was to expire - Electricity Board/Respondent No.2 exercised option to purchase undertaking of appellant by notice u/s. 6(1) of 1910 Act to appellant - Govt. handed over management to respondent No.2 - Appellants contended that purchase of electrical undertaking u/ss. 6, 7 and 7-A of 1910 Act were ultra vires to arts. 14, 19(1) (f), 19(1) (g) and 31 of Constitution - Appellants filed W.P in HC was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether purchase of electrical undertaking u/ss. 6, 7 and 7-A of 1910 Act was ultra vires to constitution - Held, undertaking belonged to licensee and purchase price must be paid before delivery otherwise license would not cease to had operation and licensee would be entitled to carry on business - Deprive licensee of his undertaking without payment of purchase price and direction issued to go to Court to enforce liability for interest for purchase price was unreasonable - Hence, appellant was entitled to challenge validity of ss. 6, 7 and 7-A of 1910 Act as it abridged his fundamental right u/arts. 19(1) (g) and 19(1) (f) of Constitution - No valid purchase of undertaking and taking delivery of undertaking was unlawful - Respondent No.2 was directed to redeliver undertaking to licensee - Appeal disposed of.
