B. S. Yadiyurappa v Mahalingappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Brijesh Kumar, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19941; (2002) 1 SCC 301; AIR 2001 SC 4041; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 84; JT 2001 (8) SC 515; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 103; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 633; 2001(7) SCALE 225; 2001 (7) SLT 269; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 12; 2001 (7) Supreme 648

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 82 and 86(1) - Whether impleading persons as respondents in excess of requirements as provided under respective section amounts to contravention of s. 82? - Held, those who are mentioned in s. 82 of said Act must be made parties to an election petition and, if they are not, election petition is one which does not comply with provisions of s. 82 and must, therefore, be dismissed by reason of terms of s. 86(1) - It does not, however, follow that if to an election petition parties other than those who are necessary parties u/s. 82 have been impleaded, election petition is one that does not comply with provisions of s. 82 and must be dismissed - Such a petition can be amended by striking out from array of parties those additionally impleaded - Appeal allowed.
