Airfreight Limited v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1529; (1999) 6 SCC 567; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1185; AIR 1999 SC 2459; JT 1999 (5) SC 320; 1999 (2) LLJ 70; 1999 (4) LLN 1; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Karnataka shops and commercial establishments Act, 1962, ss. 2(4), 2(e) and 2(u) - 'Shops' and 'commercial establishments' - Appellant company engaged in courier, cargo, travel and related services - Minimum rates of wages applicable to all 'shops' and 'commercial establishments' - Variable dearness allowance (VDA) - Separate payment of - Held, not required to pay separately when employer is paying total sum which is higher than minimum rates of wages fixed under Act - Minimum wages fixed includes basic wage plus Dearness Allowance as one pay package - Company also sells services on retail basis - Appellant company is a 'shop' and also a 'commercial establishment'.
