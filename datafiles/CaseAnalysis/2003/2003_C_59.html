Chandigarh Housing Board, Chandigarh v K. K. Kalsi and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1782; (2003) 12 SCC 734

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Interest up to the actual date of payment - On the revised offer of flat - Maintainability - Respondents/allotees of flats were under a Self-financing Scheme floated by the appellant/Board - There was some dispute between the parties and it led the respondents into filing a writ petition in the HC for vindicating their grievances against the appellant Board - HC found that the writ petitions were not maintainable - In spite of forming that opinion, the HC, instead of driving back the respondents to the circuitous route of filing civil suits and inviting adjudication of questions on facts, showed indulgence by persuading the parties to arrive at a settlement so as to give a quietus to the litigation - HC found the proposal to be very reasonable and accepted the same and allowed the respondents an opportunity of accepting the revised offer of the appellant Board which offer, the HC directed the appellant to issue to each of the allottees, consistently with the offer made before the Court and incorporated in the judgment - In spite of the dispute having been so settled, the controversy did not terminate - It appeared that the hearing before the HC had taken place sometime in the beginning of the month of January 1996 when the appellant Board submitted the proposal for settlement and made calculations by taking into account the interest which would become due and payable by 31-1-1996 - Judgment appeared to have been reserved and came to be delivered on 15-3-1996, that was, much after 31-1-1996, the date up to which the calculations were made - Other controversy which arose was that the respondents had approached the appellant for issuance of revised offers which according to the respondents were not issued by the appellant Board - Reason for not issuing revised letters of allotment, was, according to the appellant that it had moved an application before the HC sought clarification/direction for payment of further interest up to the date of payment in view of the judgment were delayed - Fact remained that sometime in the month of April or somewhere around it the respondents had made the payments in terms of the proposal of appellant recorded by the HC in its judgment - Possession over the flats was delivered to the allottees and they were in occupation thereof - Only controversy which survived for adjudication was the appellant's claim for interest up to the actual date of payment - Whether appellant's claim for interest up to the actual date of payment was maintainable - Held, having heard the learned counsel for the parties, SC was satisfied that fault could not be found with the appellant for delay in issuance of allotment letters - Appellants were seeking a clarification from the HC which, in the peculiar facts and circumstances of the case, especially the factum of delay in delivery of judgment, they were justified in seeking from the Court - If appellants would have issued revised letters of allotment in the terms offered by them in the Court, then they would have been held bound by the terms of the revised offer - Appellants were rightly advised in seeking clarification from the Court before issuing the revised letters of allotment - Fact remained that for the period between 1-2-1996 and the actual dates of payment the respondent allottees had retained the money with them and made use thereof while the appellant was deprived of the use of the money - It would be equitable for the respondent allottees to pay a reasonable interest to the appellant Board, the calculation whereof should remain confined to the amount of original price in excess of the amount in deposit by the allottees with appellant - Appeal disposed of.
