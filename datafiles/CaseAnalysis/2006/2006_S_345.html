Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 431; (2006) 10 SCC 115; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 166; AIR 2006 SC 2855; 2006 CRLJ 4070; 2006 (3) JCC 1611; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 215; JT 2006 (7) SC 490; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 104; 2006(8) SCALE 210; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 795

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 319 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - IPC, 1860, ss. 201, 304B - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Accused were convicted under ss 304-B, 201 - Whether prosecution was able to prove demands of dowry?; whether deceased had died because of harassment and cruelty meted out at hands of accused persons in connection with demands of dowry?; whether death had occurred within seven years of marriage? - Held, trial court has properly analyzed evidence and justly convicted appellants u/s. 304 - HC also examined entire evidence on record and came to same conclusion - No infirmity can be found with impugned judgment of HC - Prosecution has successfully proved its case against appellants - Appeals dismissed.
