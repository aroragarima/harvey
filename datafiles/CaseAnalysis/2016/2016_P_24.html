Velugubanti Hari Babu v Parvathini Narasimha Rao and another

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 513; AIR 2016 SC 3285; 2016 (6) ALT(SC) 7; 2016 (5) AWC 4621; 2016 (3) CLT(SC) 204; 2017 (1) CTC 105; JT 2016 (6) SC 498; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 134; 2016(6) SCALE 797; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 228

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitral Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 11(5), 11(6), 9 - Dispute - Adjudication - Appointment of arbitrator - Appellant and respondents entered into Memorandum of Understanding (MoU) provided that respondents would resolve certain disputes that are pending between appellant and other persons and in return appellant will sell 50% of land to respondents at the rate of Rs.1 crore per acre - Disputes arose between parties - Respondents filed an application before HC u/ss. 11(5) and 11(6) of the Act for appointment of an arbitrator - HC allowed application and appointed a retired HC Judge as sole arbitrator to adjudicate disputes raised by parties including to decide question regarding legality and genuineness of MoU - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in not deciding the question as to MoU is valid and genuine document and directing arbitrator to decide the said question.

Held, such directions issued by HC is against the law laid down by SC in S.B.P. and Company v. Patel Engineering Limited and Anr, 2005 Indlaw SC 696, National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. M/S. Boghara Polyfab Pvt. Ltd., 2008 Indlaw SC 1506 and in Bharat Rasiklal Ashra v. Gautam Rasiklal Ashra and another, 2011 Indlaw SC 564. HC while deciding the application did not take note of any of those decisions and passed impugned order which is against the law laid down in those decisions. HC ought to have decided the questions itself and recoded a finding as to whether MoU is a valid and genuine document or it is a forged and fabricated document and then depending upon the findings, appropriate directions, if necessary, should have been passed for disposal of application finally, which was not done. Appellant denied having signed or/and executed MoU and its existence of. Therefore, that was sufficient for joining issue on validity and genuineness of MoU which was raised timely in appropriate proceedings by appellant. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Where allegations of forgery or fabrication are made in regard to documents, it would be appropriate for Chief Justice or his designate to decide the issue and if finding was affirmative, he could proceed to appoint Arbitrator.
