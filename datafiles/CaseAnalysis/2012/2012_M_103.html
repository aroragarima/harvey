Mohd. Ayub & Anr. v Mukesh Chand

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 3; (2012) 2 SCC 155; AIR 2012 SC 881; 2012(2) ALL MR 940; 2012 (90) ALR 908; 2012 (2) AWC 1450; JT 2012 (1) SC 4; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 565; 2012 (2) RLW 1565; 2012(1) SCALE 47; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21 - Bona-fide requirement - Comparative hardship - Eviction - Sustainability - Appellant purchased property occupied by respondent as tenant - Appellant filed eviction suit on ground of bona-fide requirement - Prescribed Authority dismissed case - District Court also dismissed - HC partly allowed directing respondent to hand over 1 room out of four rooms - Aggrieved, instant appeal - HC had rightly concluded genuine requirement of appellant but erred in granting partial relief without going into aspect of comparative hardship - Respondent contended that HC had balanced interest of both sides, hence no interference is necessary - Whether HC was justified in passing partial relief without dealing with issue of comparative hardship - Held, that HC had not given any reasons why only partial relief was being granted to appellants and that it had not discussed issue of comparative hardship - HC has rightly found need of appellants to be bona fide, but erred in directing respondent to handover only one room - Hardship appellants would suffer by not occupying their own premises would be far greater than hardship respondent would suffer by having to move out to another place - Appeal allowed
