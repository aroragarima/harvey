Shashikant v Central Bureau of Investigation and others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1447; (2007) 1 SCC 630; AIR 2007 SC 351; 2007 CRLJ 995; JT 2006 (9) SC 603; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 961; 2006(11) SCALE 272; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Service - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154 and 157 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act. 1946, s. 2 - Appellant made an anonymous complaint to the CBI alleging corrupt practices and financial irregularities on the part of some officers of his department - First respondent No.1 on the basis of a source information, conducted a preliminary inquiry as he was of the opinion that it was not necessary to register a FIR - First respondent recommended for holding of departmental proceedings against the concerned officers - Pursuant to said recommendation, the Railway Administration initiated departmental proceedings against the concerned officers and imposed different penalties on them - Appellant filed writ petition submitting that even in a case where the Investigating Officer may exercise his option of closing a case, it would be obligatory on his part to comply with the provisions of s. 157(1)(b) of the Cr.PC and register a FIR - Said writ petition was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether it was obligatory on the part of the first respondent to lodge a FIR and carry out a full- fledged investigation about the truthfulness or otherwise of the allegations made in the said anonymous complaint? - Held, an anonymous complaint was made in June 2004 - Evidently it was within the province of the first respondent to commence a preliminary inquiry - Procedure laid down in the CBI Manual and in particular when it was required to inquire into the allegation of the corruption on the part of some public servants, recourse to the provisions of the Manual cannot be said to be unfair - It did not find any reason to convert the preliminary inquiry into a regular case - Pursuant to or in furtherance of the recommendation made by the first respondent, which had received the imprimatur by the Central Vigilance Commission, departmental proceedings were initiated - Moreover, First Respondent is a statutory authority - It has a statutory duty to carry out investigation in accordance with law - Ordinarily, it is not within the province of the court to direct the investigative agency to carry out investigation in a particular manner - A writ court ordinarily again would not interfere with the functioning of an investigative agency - Only in exceptional cases, it may do so and no such case has been made out by the appellant - Hence, appeal dismissed.
