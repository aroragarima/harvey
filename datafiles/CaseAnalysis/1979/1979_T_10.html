Tarkeshwar Sio Thakur Jiu v Bar Dass Dey & Co. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 279; (1979) 3 SCC 106; AIR 1979 SC 1669; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 18

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.105 - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953 - Sand mines - Lease - Land Acquisition - Possession - Appellant/Plaintiff granted lease of land to Respondent/Defendant for mining of sand for 9 years with an option of renewal - After expiry of 9 years another grant of 9 years was given - On non payment of license fee Appellant/Plaintiff terminated license and filed suit for ejectment of Respondent/Defendant - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that Respondent/Defendant were not licensees but tenant under State - First Appellate Court reversed decision of Trial Court holding that grant being a license Plaintiff had right to retain land - On further appeal by Respondent/Defendant HC allowed held that grant was not a license and that Plaintiff was not directly working u/s.28 of 1953 Act - Aggrieved by order Appellant/Plaintiff filed appeal - Whether Appellant/Plaintiff was entitled retain possession of land as against order of HC - Held, annual fixed payment had no relation to amount of sand extracted and Respondent/Defendant had right to enter into and remain in Khas possession for a period of 9 years, as per referred case, relied on - Ex.1 was held to have all essentials of lease u/s.105 of 1882 Act - Appellant/Plaintiff's claim of right to retain land in dispute dismissed - Respondent/Defendant was held tenant - Appeal dismissed.
