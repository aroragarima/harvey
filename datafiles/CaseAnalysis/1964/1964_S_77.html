Â Gulabchand Chhotalal Parikh v State of Bombay (Now Gujarat)

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 229; AIR 1965 SC 1153; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Matter Directly And Substantially In Issue, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Can subsequent suits involving same questions and for same relief be barred under principles of res judicata? - Held, s. 11 of CPC not exhaustive - It is not necessary that Court deciding matter formerly be competent to decide subsequent suit or that former proceeding and subsequent suit have same subject matter - Nature of former proceeding is immaterial - There is no good reason to preclude such decisions on matters in controversy in writ proceedings under art. 226 or 32 of Constitution from operating as res judicata in subsequent regular suits on same matters in controversy between same parties and thus to give limited effect to principle of finality of decisions after full contest - Appeal dismissed.
