Padma Ben Banushali and Another v Yogendra Rathore and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 300; (2006) 12 SCC 138; AIR 2006 SC 2167; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 121; 2006 (3) AWC 2406; 2006 (3) CTC 67; JT 2006 (5) SC 122; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 57; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 375; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 90; 2006 (3) RLW 2474; 2006(4) SCALE 546; 2006 (Supp) RD 603B; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 36, 37, 38, 39, 40, 42, 47 and 115, O. 21, r. 2, O. 23, r. 1 - Suit for eviction - Decree to landlords - Tenant appeal dismissed in terms of application u/O. 23, r. 1, CPC, 1908 - Later appellants tried to execute decree that adjustments in terms of O. 21, r. 2, CPC, 1908 was not recorded - High Court held decree became in-executable as there was a conscious waiver - Validity of HC judgment challenged - Held, there was no certificate as needed u/O. 21, r. 2, CPC, 1908, therefore question of conscious waiver does not arise, rights had not been surrendered, decree remained preserved, executable - Appeal allowed.
