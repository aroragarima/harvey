M/S. Julien Educational Trust v Sourendra Kumar Roy And Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2072; (2010) 1 SCC 379; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 55; 2010(2) ALL MR 413; 2010 (78) ALR 898; 2010 (2) AWC 1250; 2010 (1) CLT 110; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 191; JT 2009 (15) SC 335; 2010 (111) RD 540; 2009(14) SCALE 393; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1260; 2010 (1) UC 625

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 - Injunction - Balance of convenience and inconvenience - Appellant-trust interested in expansion of the school premises and for that purpose entered into negotiations with the respondents for purchase of the property, which was orally accepted by respondents - However, on account of an interim order of status-quo passed in an appeal filed by one of the co-sharers, no written agreement for sale was executed - Draft Deeds of Conveyance were sent by the appellant-trust to the respondents in respect of their undivided shares in the suit property for their approval - Meanwhile, appellant-trust came to know that respondents were planning to sell the suit property to a third party - Appellant-trust filed a suit for specific performance of the contract - In the said suit, the appellant-trust filed an application under O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 r/w s. 151, CPC for an order of injunction to restrain the respondent from alienating and/or changing the nature and character of the suit schedule property during the pendency of the suit - Application was allowed - On appeal, HC set aside the order of injunction - Hence, present appeal - Whether the balance of convenience and inconvenience lay in favour of the grant of an interim order of injunction in favour of the appellant-trust? - Held, from the materials on record a prima facie case has been made out in favour of the appellant-trust - Moreover, since the principal object of the appellant-trust in wanting to acquire the suit property was to extend its school unit, if the suit property is allowed to be commercially exploited by raising multi-storyed structures thereupon, the entire object of the suit filed by the appellant-trust will be rendered meaningless and the purpose for which the suit been filed would be completely defeated - Therefore, in view of principles of balance of convenience and inconvenience, interim relief should be granted to the appellant-trust - Appeals allowed.
