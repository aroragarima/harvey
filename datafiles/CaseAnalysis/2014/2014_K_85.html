Rajinder Kumar and others v Kuldeep Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 75; AIR 2014 SC 1155; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 100; 2015(1) ALL MR 435; 2014 (104) ALR 741; 2014 (2) AWC 1976; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 298; 2014 (2) CTC 93; JT 2014 (2) SC 530; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1234; 2014 (2) MLJ 496; 2014 (1) OLR 902; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 980; 2014 (124) RD 240; 2015 (1) RLW 374; 2014(2) SCALE 135; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Executing Court

Summary: (1) Specific Relief Act, 1963-Section 28-Rescission of contract-Discretionary power vested in court by Section 28-Application under Section 28 for rescission of agreement on ground that purchaser failed to deposit balance purchase money-Under Section 28, vendor is free to apply to court which made decree to have contract rescinded in case purchaser has not paid purchase money or other sum which court has ordered him to pay within period allowed by decree or such other period as court may allow-On such application court may, by order, rescind contract "as justice of case may require"-In peculiar facts and circumstances of case, while considering application for rescission-Equitable order should have been passed-High Court not made attempt to balance equity and dismissed application for rescission-Unjust and unfair to relegate parties to trial court at distance of time-For doing complete justice to parties, Supreme Court itself passed appropriate orders in instant case.(2) Decree-Ex parte decree-Merely because decree is ex parte decree-Same does not cease to have force of decree-It is valid decree for all purposes.(3) Suit-For specific performance-Does not come to an end on passing of decree-Court which passed decree retains control over decree even after decree has been passed.(4) Justice-Is not abstract proposition-It is concrete reality-Parties on approaching court must get feeling that justice has been done in facts and circumstances of case, particularly in specific performance related cases, in terms of equity, equality and fairness.
