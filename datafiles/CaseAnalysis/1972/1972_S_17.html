R. P. Khanna & Ors. v S.A.F. Abbas & Ors. Etc

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, S.M. Sikri, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 301; (1972) 1 SCC 784; AIR 1972 SC 2350; 1972 LabIC 1031; 1972 (1) LLJ 490; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 548; 1972 SLR 373

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Indian Administrative Service (Regulations of Seniority) Rules, 1954, rr. 3(3)(b), 3(3)(b), Proviso 2 and r. 2(g) - (A) Members of State Civil Service - Promotion to IAS after commencement of 1954 Rules - Assigning year of allotment to promotees vis-a-vis direct recruits IAS - Determination of - (B) Officiation in a senior post - Officiation prior to inclusion of name in select list - Approval of Central Govt. - Necessity - Power of State Govt. to make a retrospective declaration about a post being equivalent to a senior post - (C) Central Govt. memorandum D/- 20-09-1967 revising seniority of promotees to IAS Cadre - Validity - Held, assigning year of allotment to promotees vis-a-vis direct recruits I.A.S. has to be determined in accordance with r. 3(3)(b) - Retrospective declaration will be under the check of approval by the Central Govt. such approval will always act as a safety valve against any abuse or mischief of retrospective declaration - Memorandum was bad and direction therein could not be sustained - Appeal Dismissed.
