G. L. Bhatia v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1112; (1999) 5 SCC 237; (1999) SCC (L&S) 996; 1999 (3) LLN 835; 1999 (9) SLT 366; 1999 Supreme 308

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, r. 54 - Whether appellant, who happens to be the husband of the deceased government servant, is entitled to family pension? - Held, yes, in view of the provisions contained in r. 54 of the rules though deceased wife in her nomination did not include the husband - Where rights of the parties are governed by statutory provisions, the individual nomination contrary to the statute will not operate - There being no divorce between the husband and wife even though they might be staying separately, the appellant husband would be entitled to the family pension in terms of the rules - Appeal allowed.
