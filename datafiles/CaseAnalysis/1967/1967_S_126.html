Mandanlal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 503; AIR 1967 SC 1590; 1967 CRLJ 1401; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 196 A(2)and 537 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 409 and 477 A - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103 and 104 - Ravi Datt Joshi was Asst. District Inspector of Schools - Appellant was working as a clerk - Authorised to draw certain amounts from bank - Certain sum was to be paid to Indian Red Cross Society and few other persons - He made an entry in cash book showing as if that amount was paid to said Society and got that entry initiated by Joshi - Amounts was not paid to any of respective persons for payment - (A) Whether trial under s. 409, of CrPC is vitiated on ground of non obtaining of sanction in respect of complaint under S. 120-B of IPC? - If that offence does not require sanction though offence of conspiracy does and sanction is not obtained, court can proceed with a trial as to substantive offence as if there was no charge of conspiracy - Held, where accused was charged not only under S. 120-B but also u/ss. 409 and 477-A, IPC, fact that sanction was not obtained in respect of complaint u/s. 120-B did not vitiate trial on substantive charge u/s. 409 - (B) Whether there is any bar to convict accused under second charge even if prosecution fails to establish conspiracy? - There was no prejudice caused to him as he was aware that there was a substantive charge u/s. 409 against him - Further held, If charge of conspiracy to commit criminal breach, of trust is followed by a substantive charge of criminal breach of trust in pursuance of such conspiracy there is nothing to prevent court convicting an accused under second charge even if prosecution fails to establish conspiracy - (C) Whether in SLP under art. 136 scope is similar to that of pleas before a High Court? - Held, conviction under s. 409 -Plea that moneys to accused were not entrusted in his capacity as clerk or as part of his duties not being raised in HC and being dependent on evidence cannot be raised before Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
