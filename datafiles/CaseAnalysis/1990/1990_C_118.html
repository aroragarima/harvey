Gwalior Rayons Silk Manufacturing (Wvg) Company Limited v Custodian of Vested Forests, Palghat and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 836; (1990) Supp SCC 785; AIR 1990 SC 1747; JT 1990 (2) SC 130; 1990 (1) KLT 819; 1990(1) SCALE 689; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 401; 1990 (1) UJ 735

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971

Summary: Land & Property - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Kerala Land Reforms Act, 1963, s. 2(47)(iv) - Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971, ss. 2(f)(1)(i)(c) - Madras Preservation of Private Forests Act, 1949 - Meaning of term 'private forest' under Act, 1971 - Whether, term pvt. forest excluded eucalyptus plantation? - Object of Act, 1971 was to provide for vesting in Govt. of private forest in State of Kerala for assignment thereof to agriculturists and agricultural laborers for cultivation - Preamble of Act provides that such agricultural lands should be so utilised as to increase agricultural production in State and to promote welfare of agricultural population in State - Two separate definitions have been provided in vesting Act; first is applicable to Malabar district where Madras Act applied immediately before appointed day; second concerned is in relation to remaining areas in State of Kerala - Thus, if land is shown to be private forest on date on which Madras Act came into force, it would continue to be a forest, even if there was subsequent replantation - Lands involved were all forests under Madras Act and continued to be so when Act, 1971 came into force - No indication that words in sub-cl. (C) 'any other agricultural crop' are quite wide enough to comprehend all species of trees including eucalyptus plantations - Held, eucalyptus plantation is a pvt. forest under Act, 1961 and not excluded therefrom is vested in State with no right, title and interest subsists with company - Appeal dismissed.
