Punjab National Bank By Chairman and Another v Astamija Dash

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2070; (2008) 14 SCC 370; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 673; AIR 2008 SC 3182; 2008 (5) CTC 554; 2008 (3) ESC 469; 2008 (118) FLR 438; 2008 (3) LLJ 584; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 490; 2008(7) SCALE 726; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 365; 2008 (5) SLR 232

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Woman, Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) Class III Rules, 1961, Punjab National Bank (Officers) Service Regulations, 1979, Madhya Pradesh Government Servants' General Conditions of Service Rules, 1961, Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Selection) Second Amendment Regulations, 1989

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16, 21 - Appointment - Termination - Maintainability - Appellant was appointed as Management Trainee in Bank and offer of appointment was made to her on certain terms and conditions - Appellant service's was terminated by authorities - Appellant preferred appeal before Appellate authority which was dismissed - Aggrieved by same petition was filed before HC which was disposed of - Hence, instant appeal - Whether termination of service by authority was maintainable - Held, Appellate authority had fixed number of chances to be given to an employee in confirmation test - Further, if it was enforced against writ petitioner having regard to her physical position, to appear in second examination, the provisions thereof, keeping in mind principle underlying the statutory provisions of the 1961 Act may not be held to be applicable - Furthermore, appellant was entitled to another opportunity to appear at examination - Thus, Appellate authority could not exercise power of relaxation in a discriminatory manner and it was expected to act judiciously, assuming that employer had a discretion in this behalf and discretion could not be equated with whims and caprices - Appeal dismissed.
