New India Assurance Company Limited v (1) Nusli Neville Wadia and Another; (2) Klm

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1303; (2008) 3 SCC 279; AIR 2008 SC 876; 2008 (71) ALR 184; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 550; JT 2008 (1) SC 31; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 875; 2008 (1) RCR(Rent) 208; 2007(14) SCALE 556; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 598

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1958, Companies Act, 1950, Government Public Premises Eviction Act, 1950, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Rules, 1971

Summary: Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971 - Who should begin to lead evidence in a proceeding under 1971 Act? - Held, when an application for eviction filed, which require production of positive evidence on part of the landlord, it would be for it to adduce evidence first; more so in a composite application where the evidence is also required to be led on the quantum of damages to be determined by the Estate Officer - When a notice is issued, the occupant of the public premises would not only be entitled to show cause but would also be entitled to produce evidence in support of the cause shown - Even if assume that in terms of the statutory provisions the respondents must lead evidence first the same can be waived, Appellant not only had filed affidavits in one of the cases but time and again sought adjournments when the deponent of the affidavit was to be cross-examined - Appeals dismissed.
