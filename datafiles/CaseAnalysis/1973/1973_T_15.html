Datar Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 217; (1975) 4 SCC 272; (1975) SCC (Cr) 530; 1974 (11) ACC 262; AIR 1974 SC 1193; 1974 CRLJ 908; 1974 CrLR(SC) 104; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal- Constitution of India,1950, art.136 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s 367 - Indian Penal Code 1860, ss 299 and 300 - Evidence Act 1872, s.8 - Supreme Court had to interfere with findings as there was miscarriage of justice - Held, that two eye-witnesses were unreliable and their very presence at scene of murder by shooting was unreliable, their statements about time of incident were contradictory and irreconcilable - Appeal set aside
