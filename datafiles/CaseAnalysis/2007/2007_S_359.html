Dayal Singh v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 452; (2007) 12 SCC 452; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 236; 2007 (3) ALT 285; 2007 CRLJ 3265; JT 2007 (6) SC 424; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 909; 2007(6) SCALE 464; 2007 (7) SCJ 845; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 1173; 2007 (4) UC 318

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal code, 1860 - s. 498-A and 302 - Appeal against the judgement of High Court affirming the conviction and sentence passed by II Additional Session Judge - Dying declaration challenged on the ground that it had not been recorded by a Magistrate but by a Head Constable - Held, the date of incident was "Holi" and being an occasion of festivity it is possible that the Magistrate may not have been present at his residence or the information may not have been conveyed to him personally, the charge against the appellant is fully established from the evidence on record and there is absolutely no ground to take a different view from what has been taken by the learned Sessions Judge and also by the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
