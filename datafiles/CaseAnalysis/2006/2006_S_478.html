Pappu v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 317; (2006) 7 SCC 391; AIR 2006 SC 2659; 2006 CRLJ 3640; 2006 (2) JCC 1095; JT 2006 (6) SC 308; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 737; 2006(7) SCALE 24; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal -IPC, 1860, ss. 294, 300, 302, 307, 34, 506(2) - Conviction of appellant - Sentence of RI for life and fine of Rs. 500/- Imposed by trial Court were maintained by HC - Appeal against - Plea relates to applicability of Exception 4 of s.300 - Held, for application of Exception 4, it is not sufficient to show that there was a sudden quarrel and there was no premeditation - It must further be shown that offender has not taken undue advantage or acted in cruel or unusual manner - Convicted appellant u/s. 304 instead of s.302 - Appeal partly allowed.
