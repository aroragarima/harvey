Hussan Mithu Mhasvadkar v Bombay Iron and Steel Labour Board and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20587; (2001) 7 SCC 394; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1190; AIR 2001 SC 3290; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 173; 2001 (3) CLR 560; 2001 (91) FLR 232; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 19; JT 2001 (7) SC 466; 2001 (34) LabIC 3330; 2001 (2) LLJ 1520; 2001(6) SCALE 156; 2001 (3) SCJ 518; 2001 (4) SLR 301; 2001 (6) SLT 477; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 626; 2001 (6) Supreme 779; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2392

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Women & Children

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1) and 12(5) - (A) Whether Bombay Iron and Steel Labour Board constituted under provisions of Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and other Manual Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969 falls within definition of 'Industry' as defined in Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Operations of Govt. which are pure and simpliciter administrative and of Govt. character or incidental thereto cannot be characterized to be 'industrial' in nature, be it performed by a department of Govt. or by a specially constituted statutory body - (B) Whether appellant, appointed and working at relevant point of time as an Inspector, discharging duties, powers and obligations envisaged u/s. 15 answers description of 'workman' as defined in Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - In deciding about status of an employee, his designation alone cannot be said to be decisive nature of his duties and powers conferred upon as well as functions assigned to him should also be considered - Even if whole undertaking be an industry, those who are not workmen by definition may not be benefited by said status - Held, appellant cannot be considered to be engaged in any manual, unskilled, skilled, technical, operational, clerical or supervisory work and mere fact that in course of performing his duties he had to also maintain records to evidence duties performed by him, everyday, cannot result in conversion of post of 'Inspector' - Appeal dismissed.
