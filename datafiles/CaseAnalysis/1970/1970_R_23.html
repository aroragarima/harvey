Rajanikant v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 169; (1970) SCC (Cr) 575; (1970) 2 SCC 866; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.326, 324 - Grievous hurt - Conviction - Validity - Appellant was convicted by Trail court for offence under ss. 326, 324 of IPC for having voluntarily caused hurt with a dangerous weapon to one person and causing grievous hurt to 3 other persons - Trial court convicted appellant u/s. 326 of IPC instead of s.307 of IPC for stabbing and sentenced him to rigorous imprisonment for 4 years and also convicted him u/s. 324 of IPC for causing hurt to other 3 persons and sentenced him to rigorous imprisonment for 1 Â½ for each of the 3 offences - All sentences were directed to be run concurrently - On Appeal to HC it dismissed his appeal with one word 'dismissed - Appellant contended that statements of the 3 witnesses made in committing court from which they had resiled at trial should not have been acted upon by trial court in support of prosecution version and witness who did not resile from statement in committing court was a highly interested witness and therefore his evidence should not be implicitly accepted - Another witness who was produced at trial without having been examined in committing court was also declared hostile and was permitted to be cross-examined by prosecutor and his evidence was no better and therefore did not add strength to prosecution case - Hence instant appeal - Held, that evidence on record was not trustworthy and it did not establish appellant's guilt beyond reasonable doubt - Statements of 3 witnesses in committing court from which they resiled at trial and which were duly brought on record of trial court u/ s. 288 of CrPC constituted substantive evidence and if court was satisfied that those statements were true whereas those made in trial court were untrue then earlier statements could have been safely be relied upon to sustain conviction - In instant case a mere reading of statements at trial demonstrated their unconvincing nature and it seemed clear that there was some ulterior motive for witnesses to resile from earlier statements which appear to have a ring of truth about them - SC was therefore satisfied that trial court was right in convicting appellant for offences under ss. 326, 324 of IPC - SC was in view that sentence was of somewhat dubious nature of transaction which led to occurrence and fact that appellant had felt somewhat annoyed at repeated visits of witnesses to his house where unpleasant scenes were created in presence of his mother sentence imposed was somewhat severe - In SC's opinion a sentence of 2 years rigorous imprisonment would meet ends of justice - SC normally did not interfere with quantum of sentence on appeal u/art.136 of constitution but in instant case as HC Court was bad in erroneously dismissing appeal summarily without giving reasons - SC had chosen to interfere with decision of HC - Appellant was ordered to surrender to his bail bond to serve out remaining sentence - Appeal dismissed.
