Lakha Ram Sharma v Balar Marketing Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1241; (2008) 17 SCC 671; 2003 (27) PTC 175; 2003(2) SCALE 363

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Practice & Procedure - Trademark infringement - Suit by proprietor of trademark 'KUNDAN' and 'KUNDAN CAB' in respect of PVC Wires and Cables against respondent - High Court disallowed one portion of amendment, where appellant sought to raise valuation of suit on basis that claim is arbitrary and not based on any cogent material - Held, it is settled law that while considering whether amendment is to be granted or not, court does not go into merits of matter and decide whether or not claim made therein is bona fide or not - That is a question which can only be decided at trial of suit - It is also settled law that merely because an amendment may take suit out of jurisdiction of that Court is no ground for refusing that amendment - No justifiable reason on which HC has refused this amendment - Appeal disposed of.
