Purushottam Kumar Jha v State of Jharkhand and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 438; (2006) 9 SCC 458; AIR 2006 SC 3655; 2006 (7) AWC 6621; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 263; JT 2006 (7) SC 46; 2006(4) SCALE 561; 2006 (5) SLR 271; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 215

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Appellant appointed as Field Clerk on compassionate ground - Refused to discharge his duties as Clerk - Plea of appellant that, from first day, he had worked as Law Officer/Legal Assistant and it was, therefore, not open to respondents to treat him as Clerk - Order of compulsory retirement from service - Held, since appellant was a Clerk, he was required to do clerical work - HC was right in holding that when appointment of appellant was as Clerk, he could be asked to discharge duties in clerical capacity - Order compulsorily retiring appellant cannot be held illegal - Appeal dismissed.
