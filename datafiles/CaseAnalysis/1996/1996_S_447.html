R.M Tewari, Advocate v State (Nct of Delhi) and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 580; (1996) SCC (Cr) 361; (1996) 2 SCC 610; 1996 (33) ACC 377; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 166; AIR 1996 SC 2047; 1996 (1) CCR 212; 1996 (1) Crimes 149; 1996 CRLJ 2872; 1996 JCC 279; JT 1996 (2) SC 657; 1996 (4) KarLJ 453; 1996(2) SCALE 389; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 898; 1996 (2) Supreme 366

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 321 - TADA Act, 1987, ss. 3, 19 and 20 - Whether withdrawal of prosecution under TADA is allowed on the recommendation of review committee - Withdrawal is not to be permitted mechanically by the Court on an application for that purpose made by the Public Prosecutor - Held, public prosecutor must satisfy himself that it is fit case for withdrawal - Petition and appeal disposed of.
