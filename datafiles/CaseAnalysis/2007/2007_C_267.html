Commissioner, Income Tax,Thiruvananthapuram v K. Ravindranathan Nair

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1139; (2007) 15 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1193; [2007] 295 ITR 228; JT 2007 (12) SC 504; 2007(13) SCALE 134; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 1097; [2007] 165 TAXMAN 282

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1985

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Computation of Export Incentive under s. 80HHC(3) - Whether the Department was right in including processing charges, amounting to Rs.1, 54, 68, 811, in the total turnover while arriving at export profits under Section 80HHC(3) of the Act, as it stood at the material time?; Whether in the matter of computation of deduction, under s. 80HHC(3)(c) of the I.T. Act, losses suffered by the taxpayer in the export of trading goods can be set off/adjusted against profits from export of manufactured goods and vice versa and whether the assessee would be entitled to deduction if after such adjustments/set off the net figure is a loss? - Held, since, processing charges constituted independent income similar to rent, commission, etc., which formed part of the gross total income, the same had to be reduced by 90% to arrive at Business Profits - Said processing charges were includible in the total turnover in the formula under s. 80HHC(3) - If there is a positive profit, the assessee would be entitled to deduction - If there is a loss he will not be entitled to any deduction - Order accordingly.
