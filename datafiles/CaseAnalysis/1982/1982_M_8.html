Chandrapal Singh and Others v Maharaj Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 3; (1982) SCC (Cr) 249; (1982) 1 SCC 466; AIR 1982 SC 1238; 1982 ALJ 507; 1982 CRLJ 1731; 1982 CRLR 126; 1982 (2) RCJ 476; 1982(1) SCALE 73; 1982 UJ 517

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1973, ss. 195(1)(b)(i) and 195(3) - Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 , 199 and 201 - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 34(2) - (A) Offence u/s.193, IPC, 1860 of filing a false affidavit - Before Rent Control Officer - Whether RCO is a 'Court' - Complaint by Court whether a pre-condition for taking cognizance of such offence by criminal Court - (B) Complaint under Penal Code - (1) Not even the slightest allegation to constitute an offence except mentioning number of the section - (2) Gound, certain averments filed in affidavits before rent Control Officer are false - No specific averment singled out in the complaint - Held, Rent Control Officer shall be deemed to be a civil court - For the purpose of s. 195 (1) (b) (i) a complaint by the Court is a pre-condition for taking cognizance of such offence by any criminal court- Complaint liable to be quashed - Complaint, held, was not entertainable - Appeal allowed.
