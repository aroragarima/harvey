Himachal Pradesh Housing And Urban Development Authority v Universal Estate And Another

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1009; AIR 2011 SC 887; JT 2010 (13) SC 57; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1303; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 950; 2010(12) SCALE 389

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Himachal Pradesh Housing and Urban Development Authority Act, 2004

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Himachal Pradesh Housing and Urban Development Authority (Allotment, Sale of Houses, Flats and Plots) Regulations, 2004 - Rejection of bid after deposition of 10% of the bid money - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition submitting that even though his bid was highest and it had deposited 10% of the bid amount, the Chief Executive Officer arbitrarily cancelled the bid - Petitioner pleaded that the highest bid of respondent No.1 was rejected because the rate offered by it were on lower side as compared to other commercial site - DB allowed said writ petition and quashed the order of Chief Executive Officer by observing that the lease will be deemed to have taken effect from the date of acceptance of bid and as soon as 10% amount approved by the Presiding Officer was deposited, the bid of respondent No.1 stood accepted - DB also held that after acceptance of 25% or 10% of the bid money, the Chief Executive Officer had no right to reject the bid - Petitioner filed appeal against said order contending that the deposit of 10% of the bid money did not create any tangible right in favour of respondent no.1 and the reasons assigned by the HC for nullifying rejection of the bid of respondent No.1 are legally unsustainable - Whether Chief Executive Officer could reject the highest bid given by respondent No.1 in respect of commercial site after it had deposited 10% of the bid money? - Held, when any land or building is sold or leased out by open auction, the premium to be charged is the reserve price/premium or any higher amount offered by the highest bidder - In terms of clause 5(2) of the Regulations, the highest bidder is required to deposit 10% of the bid on the spot - Thereafter, the Chief Executive Officer has to take a decision on the issue of acceptance of bid - The Chief Executive Officer has to ensure that the land or building belonging to the Authority is sold or leased out or otherwise transferred at the maximum price - If the Chief Executive Officer finds that the bidders have formed a cartel or joined hands to manipulate the price or have done anything which is detrimental to the interest of the Authority or public interest, he is not only entitled but is duty bound to reject the highest bid - Moreover, there is nothing in the plain language of clause 5(2) from which it can be inferred that the highest bid is deemed to have been accepted on receipt of 10% of the bid money - The Regulations do not contain any time schedule within which the Chief Executive Officer is required to take a decision on the issue of acceptance or rejection of the bid - Therefore, his inaction in the matter or failure to pass a positive order within a specified time cannot lead to an inference that the highest bid has been accepted and the lease has become effective - Hence, DB was clearly wrong in declaring that the bid of respondent No.1 stood accepted as soon as 10% amount approved by the Presiding Officer of the Auction Committee was deposited and that the Chief Executive Officer had no right to reject the bid after acceptance of 10% of the bid money - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
