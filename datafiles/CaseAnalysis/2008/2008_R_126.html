United India Insurance Company Limited v Rakesh Kumar Arora and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1591; (2008) 13 SCC 298; 2008 (4) ACC 709; AIR 2009 SC 24; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 136; 2008 (73) ALR 650; 2008 (4) AWC 3823; 2008 (1) CCC 359; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 105; JT 2008 (11) SC 23; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3199; 2008 (152) PLR 758; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 684; 2008(13) SCALE 35; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Compensation For Death, Breach Of Condition, Use Of Vehicle, Prescribed Form, Liability Of Insurer

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act - Held, s. 4 prohibits driving of a vehicle by any person under the age of eighteen years in any public place - S. 5 imposes a statutory responsibility upon the owners of the motor vehicles not to cause or permit any person who does not satisfy the provisions of Sec.3 or 4 to drive the vehicle - Tribunal rightly held that deceased did not hold any valid licence on the date of accident - High Court proceeded on a wrong premise that it was for the Insurance Company to prove breach of conditions of the contract of insurance - Appeal by the Insurance Company allowed.
