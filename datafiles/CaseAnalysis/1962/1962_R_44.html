Â Khardah Company Limited v Raymon and Company (India) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 575; AIR 1962 SC 1810; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952 - (A) Whether contract dated is illegal is one for arbitrator to decide and that it was not open to respondents to raise same in present proceedings u/s. 33 of Arbitration Act? - Held, if one party to alleged contract is contending that it is void ab initio (because, for example, making of such a contract is illegal), arbitration clause cannot operate, for on this view clause itself also is void - (B) Whether respondents are estopped from questioning validity of award by reason of their having submitted to jurisdiction of arbitrators? - Further held, it is unable to accept contention that respondents are estopped by their conduct from questioning validity of award - (C) Whether it is illegal as falling within prohibition enacted in notification of Central Govt. d/- 29-10-1953? - Further held, contract was entered into on 7-09-1955, when notification aforesaid was in force, and so it would be hit by it, unless it is a non-transferable specific delivery contract and point for decision is whether it is that - Appeals allowed.
