State of Gujarat and others v Nirmalaben S. Mehta and another

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 515; (2016) 9 SCC 240; AIR 2016 SC 3324; 2016 (5) AWC 4406; 2017 (2) G.L.R. 1451; JT 2016 (6) SC 508; 2017 (2) MahLJ 18; 2017 (1) MPLJ 526; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 1042; 2016(7) SCALE 409; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, Renewal Of Lease, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Mines & Minerals - Practice & Procedure - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss.13, 30, 9, 9(3) - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 27(3), 54, 55, 64D - Mining lease - Renewal - Validity - Respondents entered into lease agreement with appellant/State for mining lease of bauxite in respect of lands under the Act - On 16-01-1980, Central Govt. granted permission to appellant for de-reservation of areas for exploitation of bauxite mineral, which were earlier reserved and for imposition of conditions on mining lease - Respondent no.1 applied to appellant by letter dt. 18-02-2008 for sale of NPG bauxite which was rejected by appellant holding that respondent no.1 failed to establish captive plant within a period of 2 years in accordance with prevailing policy - Respondent no.1 approached Central Govt. by filing revision against order passed by appellant which was allowed - Aggrieved, appellant filed application before HC - Single Judge dismissed application holding that appellant ought not to have suppressed material fact of writ petition filed before HC with regard to same matter - On appeal, DB partly allowed appeals to the extent of setting aside order of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant has power at the time of renewal of lease of mining area in question to impose condition of setting up of a captive plant by respondents.

Held, both Revisional Authority as well as HC have erred in coming to conclusion that a renewal of lease of mining area in question is not a fresh grant and have wrongly concluded that at the time of grant of renewal of lease of the area, appellant is not empowered to impose or enforce condition inter-alia, of setting up of a captive plant by respondents. In the instant case appellant after 16-01-1980 had power to impose condition for setting up of a captive plant for bauxite by respondents at the time of renewal of their lease. Therefore, impugned order passed by both Revisional Authority and HC vitiated in law and was set aside. Appeals allowed.

(B) Mines & Minerals - Practice & Procedure - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, ss.13, 30, 9, 9(3) - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960, rr. 27(3), 54, 55, 64D - De-reserved area - Discrimination - Challenged - Whether permission granted by Central Govt. u/r. 27(3) of the Rules vide letter dt. 16-01-1980 would be applicable to respondents mine.

Held, permission for inclusion of certain conditions including condition for setting up of a captive plant by leaseholders u/r. 27(3) of the Rules for de-reserved area granted by Central Govt. would not be applicable to respondents lease which was granted prior to 16-01-1980 merely because their mines were not affected by notification of reservation dt. 26-02-1964 issued by appellant. Any other interpretation of above order of Central Govt. in this regard would lead to discrimination between fresh leases granted post 16-01-1980 on the one hand and renewals of existing leases granted after 16-01-1980 on the other. Such a distinction sought to be made by respondents is impermissible in law as after 16-01-1980, a fresh grant and a renewal of existing lease of mining area stands on same footing. The aforesaid important legal aspect of matter was not taken note of by HC as well as Revisional Authority. Therefore, impugned order passed by HC confirming passed by Revisional Authority being contrary to the approval given by Central Govt. vide letter dt. 16-01-1980 suffers from error in law. Therefore, impugned order passed by both Revisional Authority and HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Right to renewal of a permit is not a vested or accrued right but a privilege to get renewal according to law in operation and after compliance with preconditions abiding law.
