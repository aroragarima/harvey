Munna Roy v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3038; (2000) 9 SCC 283; (2000) SCC (L&S) 1098; 2000 (4) AWC 3010; 2000 (3) CLR 356; JT 2000 (9) SC 168; 2001 (34) LabIC 2121; 2000 (5) SLR 114

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appellant, pursuant to an advertisement issued by Railway Recruitment Board for post of Craft Teacher (Bengali Medium), applied for same - Appellant had requisite qualification and she became successful in written test as well as in viva-voce - List of successful candidates included her name - Ground of cancellation of entire list without even informing appellant was that though minimum qualification required was a matriculate she was a graduate and thus dubious method has been adopted - Whether non selection of appellant was on arbitrary and irrational grounds? - If administrative authority takes a decision and reasons for such decision are erroneous then such a decision can be interfered with by Court of Law - Held, if a candidate possesses a qualification higher than required qualification and advertisement itself had prescribed same then how can authority come to a conclusion that selection has been made by adopting a dubious method - Reasons which weighed with authorities to quash selection are not germane and must be held to be arbitrary and irrational - Appeal disposed of.
