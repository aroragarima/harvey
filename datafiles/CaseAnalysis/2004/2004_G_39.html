State of Orissa Through Kumar Raghvendra Singh v Genesh Chandra Jew

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 218; (2004) 8 SCC 40; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2104; AIR 2004 SC 2179; 2004 CRLJ 2011; 2004 (2) JCC 735; JT 2004 (4) SC 52; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 663; 2004(3) SCALE 608; 2004 (2) Supreme 757; 2004 (2) UC 972

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s.197 - Orissa Forest Act, 1972 - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 325, 34, 341, 386, 506 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Mandatory character of protection afforded to a public servant is brought out by expression, 'no court shall take cognizance of such offence except with previous sanction' - Use of words, 'no' and 'shall' make it abundantly clear that bar on exercise of power by court to take cognizance of any offence is absolute and complete - Very cognizance is barred - Held on facts, complaint's very credibility shaken by fact scenario - Continuance of proceedings would be an abuse of process of law - Appeal allowed.
