State, Nct of Delhi v Malvinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

21 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 694; (2007) 11 SCC 314; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 237; 2007 CRLJ 3512; 2007 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 114; JT 2007 (9) SC 283; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 602; 2007(8) SCALE 557; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 1109; 2007 (4) UC 127

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeal is allowed, Non Compliance, Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1995

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1995. s. 17 - Sessions Judge sentenced accused to undergo rigorous imprisonment for ten years with a fine of Rs.1, 00, 000/- with default stipulation - High Court acquitted - Appeal against acquittal - Held, no effort was made by the accused to call for the records of information, if any, sent - Further question is whether in a case of this nature while the police officer on patrol duty stops the vehicle in transit in a public place and conducts search and seizure, s. 42 has no application - Order of the High Court is clearly unsustainable - S. 42 has no application to the facts of the case - Appeal allowed.
