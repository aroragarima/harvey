Yogesh Yadav v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 512; (2013) 14 SCC 623; AIR 2013 SC 3372; 2013 (6) AWC 5676; 2013 (4) CGLJ 180; 2013 (139) FLR 481; JT 2013 (11) SC 178; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3567; 2013(10) SCALE 333; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 194; 2013 (5) SLR 429; 2013 (4) UPLBEC 2733

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Mode Of Selection, Punjab Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Service Rules

Summary: (1) Service -Appointment to post of Deputy Director (Law) in O.B.C. category-Selection Procedure-Change in criteria of selection in midstream-Appellants challenged their non-selection primarily on ground that selection criteria was changed arbitrarily that too after advertisement-Fixation of benchmark agitated by appellants according to them it amounts to changing selection procedure mid-way, i.e., after initiation of recruitment process which is impermissible-Issue involved-Whether there was any change in mode of selection after process of selection had started?-Held, "no"-Fixation of benchmark not amount to change in criteria of selection in midstream when there was no such stipulation in that regard in advertisement-Fixation of benchmark was legal and justified-Entire selection undertaken in accordance with criterion laid down at time of recruitment process-Not case of changing rules of game-Fixation of bench-mark is permissible in law-Short listing of candidates for purpose of selection/appointment-Always permissible-No change of norm or procedure-No mandate was fixed that candidate should secure minimum marks in interview-To have meritorious persons for posts, fixation of minimum 65% marks for selecting person from O.B.C. category and minimum 70% marks for general category-Was legitimate for giving demarcating choice to employer-No change in criteria of selection, which remained of 80 marks for written test and 20 marks for interview without any subsequent introduction of minimum cut-off marks in interview-Short listing done by fixing benchmark, to recruit best candidate on rational and reasonable basis-Clearly permissible under law-Non-selection of appellants not result of altering prescribed mode of selection.(2) Employment-Selection/ appointment-Short listing of candidates for purposes of selection/appointment-Always permissible.
