Hungerford Investment Trust Limited (In Voluntary v Haridas Mundhra and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 612; (1972) 3 SCC 684; AIR 1972 SC 1826; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 690

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 35, 28, 20, Pre. - Contract Act, 1872, s. 46 - CPC, 1908, O. 21, rr. 30 and 32 - Rescission of contracts - Decree for specific performance of agreement for sale - (A) Repeal of Act about a week thereafter - Maintainability of application u/s. 35 , after repeal of 1963 Act - (B) Application to rescind a decree for specific performance of an agreement to sell movables - Maintainability - (C) Control of court over decree, once t has passed it - (D) Provision of s. 46 of 1872 Act - Time for performance of promise - When no time is fixed - (E) CPC,1908, O. 21, r. 32 - Execution of decree for specific performance - Manner - Held, not be maintainable , as the applicant had no accrued right on the date of repeal to apply for rescission u/s.35 which was saved u/s.6 of the General Clauses Act - Held, not maintainable u/s.28 since it provides only for rescission of decree for specific performance for the sale or lease of immovable property - Court retains control over the decree even after the decree has been passed, it can entertain the application for rescission of the decree and the Act is not an exhaustive enactment - it must be implied that it is to be performed within a reasonable time - Cannot be executed as a money decree and the execution can only be in the manner prescribed by r. 32 - Appeal Allowed.
