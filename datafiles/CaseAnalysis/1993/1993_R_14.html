Govind Prasad v R.G. Prasad

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 356; (1994) 1 SCC 437; (1994) SCC (L&S) 579; 1994 (26) ATC 612; JT 1993 (6) SC 233; 1994 (1) LLJ 943; 1994 (1) SLR 30

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Whether the memorandum specified the service rules or not? - Held, the scheme of the memorandum clearly shows that it did not lay down conditions of service for any of the branches of the Public Works Department - Contents of the said memorandum were the various policy decisions taken by the Govt. from time to time, which were to be implemented in future by making necessary provisions in the relevant rules, or by amending the existing executive instructions - Memorandum does not talk of electrical or mechanical branches in any manner - High Court fell into patent error in treating the memorandum to be laying down the conditions of service of the Electrical and Mechanical branches of the Public Works Department - Appeal disposed of.
