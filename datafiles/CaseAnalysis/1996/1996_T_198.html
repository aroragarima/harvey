Ansal Engineering Projects Limited v Tehri Hydro Development Corporation Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 12; (1996) 5 SCC 450; 1996 ARBLR 344; 1996 (3) CCC 252; [1997] 88 Comp Cas 149; JT 1996 (7) SC 336; 1996(5) SCALE 769; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 226; 1996 (2) UJ 581

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Corporate

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41, sch. II - Serious dispute as to who has committed breach of contract - Whether amount in bank guarantee is only due and payable after arbitrator declares award? - Held, liability of bank is absolute and unequivocal, it would thereby be clear that bank is not concerned with ultimate decision of a court and a tribunal in its finding after adjudication as to amount due and payable by petitioner to first respondent - Bank should verify whether amount claimed is within terms of bank guarantee or letter of credit - Petition dismissed.
