(1) Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur; (2) v (1) Baidyanath Ayurved Bhawan Limited; (2)

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 443; (2009) 12 SCC 419; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1090; 2009 (237) E.L.T. 225; 2009(5) SCALE 396; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 879

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Trade

Keywords: Tariff Item, Adjudicating Authorities, Pharmaceutical Products, Medicament, Ayurvedic Medicines, Revenue's Appeal Allowed

Summary: Excise - Trade - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, headings 3003.31 and 33.06 - Drugs and Cosmetic Act, 1940, s. 3(a) - General Rules of interpretation, r. 3(a) - Exemption - Notification No. 62/78-CE. - Classification - Dant Manjan Lal (DML) - Impugned product is a powder compounded with Geru, Peepall, Sonth, Kali Mirch, Tambakuh, Clove Oil, Camphor, Pepperment, Babul Chhal, Tumber Beej - (A) Whether impugned product is classifiable under heading 3003.31 as medicament as contended by assessee or under heading 33.06 of Tariff as cosmetic/toiletry preparation/tooth powder as contended by Dept.? - Held, common parlance test is well recognized as one of twin tests which should be applied to classify a product as medicament or cosmetic - Moreover, without a change in nature or a change in use of product and in absence of a statutory definition, product will not change its character - Impugned product used routinely for dental hygiene, hence classifiable under heading 33.06 of Tariff and as per Chapter Note 1(d) of Chapter 30 even if product DML has some therapeutic or medicinal properties, product stands excluded from Chapter 30. - (B) Whether assessee is entitled to benefits of notification no. 62/78 - CE. in respect of impugned product? - held, impugned product is not a medicinal preparation and, therefore, not entitled to benefit of exemption notification - (C) Does Chapter Sub- heading 3003.31 contain a definition of Ayurvedic Medicine and, if so, common parlance test for classifiability of product, whether medicament or cosmetic, is inapplicable? - Held, what is provided in column 3 of Schedule is description of goods against sub-heading 3003.31, it cannot be treated as definition - (D) Whether definition of one statute should be applied mechanically to another statute? - Held, definition of one statute having different object, purpose and scheme cannot be applied mechanically to another statute - Object of Excise Act is to raise revenue for which various products are differently classified in New Tariff Act - (E) Whether maxim Nemo debet bis vexari pro una et eadem causa, Interest republicae ut sit finis litium and Res judicata pro veritate occipitur applied to present case? - held, maxims cannot be applied as a rule of thumb in taxation matters - Revenue's Appeal Allowed.
