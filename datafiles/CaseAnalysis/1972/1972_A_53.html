Y. A. Mamarde And Ors v Authority Under The Minimum Wages Act(Small Causes Court) Na

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, C.A. Vaidyialingam, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 228; (1972) 2 SCC 108; AIR 1972 SC 1721; 1972 (25) FLR 186; 1972 LabIC 894; 1972 (2) LLJ 136; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 161

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 3, 4, 5 and 14 - Madhya Pradesh Minimum Wages Rules, 1951, r. 25 (1) (b), explanation - (A) Interpretation of Statutes - Recourse to preamble, when can be taken? - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of words 'double ordinary rate of wages' in r. 25 (1) (b) of 1951 Act - (C) Provisions of s. 5 of 1948 Act - Fixation of minimum wages by notification D/-21-02-1951 in respect of unskilled labour only - Subsequent notification D/-23-02-1956 fixing revised rates of minimum wages - Determination of scope when words 'unskilled labour' not mentioned in it - (A) Held, a preamble though a key to open mind of Legislature cannot be used to control or qualify precise and unambiguous language of enactment - It is only in case of doubts or ambiguity that recourse may be had to preamble to ascertain reason for enactment in order to discover true legislative intendment - (B) Do not mean double rate of minimum wages fixed under Act - (C) Held second notification did not apply to all categories of labour - It had to be read in background of earlier notification - Hence, it must also be confined to 'uskilled labour' - Appeal allowed.
