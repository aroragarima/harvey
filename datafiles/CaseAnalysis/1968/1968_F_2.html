Additional Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax, Indore Region, Indore v Firm Jagmohandas Vijay Kumar

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 59; 1970 MPLJ 573; 1970 (25) STC 74

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 22 - Interpretation Act, 1889 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954,s. 16 - Constitution of India, 1953 art. 20(1) - Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules, 1955 - Interpretation of General clauses Act - Penalty - Respondent did not file any quarterly return of its sales for assessment years 1955-56 and 1956-57 as required by Rajasthan Sales Tax Act - Appellant after giving notice to respondent imposed penalty u/s. 16 of Act - On appeal, High Court dismissed order of imposition of penalty on ground that Rules framed initially were not valid for purpose of imposing penalties and Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules Validating Ordinance No. 5 of 1959 and Rajasthan Sales Tax Rules Validating Act No. 43 of 1959 could not be effective for purpose of imposing penalty u/s. 16 of Act since Rules could not be given retrospective effect - Whether HC decision could be upheld on above mentioned grounds? - Rules were published by Rajasthan Govt. in Rajasthan Gazette on 28-03-1955, before Act came into force - U/s. 22 of General clauses Act, power is expressly conferred on rule-making authority to make rules even before date of commencement of Act but rules so made shall not take effect till actual enforcement of Act - Held, by reason of s. 22 of General clauses Act rules published by Rajasthan Govt. on 28-1955, must be held to be validly made in exercise of rule-making power of State Govt. but they would be deemed to be not in force for period between 28-03-1955 to 31-03-1955 - Rules should be deemed to have validly come into effect from 01-04-1955- HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
