Â H.R. Adyanthaya v Sandoz (India) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, G.N. Ray, Kuldip Singh, N.P. Singh, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1159; (1994) 5 SCC 737; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1283; AIR 1994 SC 2608; AIR 1994 SCW 3678; 1994 (2) CLR 552; 1994 (69) FLR 593; JT 1994 (5) SC 176; 1995 (1) LLJ 303; 1994 (2) LLN 1017; 1994(3) SCALE 816; 1994 (3) SLJ 145; 1994 (5) SLR 255; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955, Sales Promotion Employees (Conditions of Service) Act, 1976

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2s - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions & Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Workman - Medical representatives - Whether the 'medical representatives' were workmen according to the definition of 'workman' u/s. 2(s) of the 1947 Act.

Held, since the medical representatives are not workmen within the meaning, of the 1971 Act the complaint made to the Industrial Court under that 1947 Act was not maintainable. Hence the complaint filed by the appellant- workmen under the 1971 Act in the present case was not maintainable and hence it was rightly dismissed by the Industrial Court. Appeal disposed of.
