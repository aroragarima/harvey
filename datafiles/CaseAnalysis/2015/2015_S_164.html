Vijay Pal v State Govt. (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 160; (2015) 4 SCC 749; AIR 2015 SC 1495; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1611; 2015 CRLJ 2041; JT 2015 (3) SC 15; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 722; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 457; 2015(3) SCALE 283; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Deceased wife had entered into wedlock with appellant husband prior to almost eleven years of date of occurrence i.e. 2-11-1997 - Further, on day od incident i.e. on 2-11-1997, PW 3, minor daughter of deceased informed father and brother of deceased that, appellant was threatening to burn her mother - However, on appearing on the spot it was alleged that deceased was burnt by appellant by putting kerosene on her - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 of IPC and order was confirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, from oral evidence and seized items from place of occurrence, it is quite vivid that deceased had suffered burn injuries which lead to her death. Further, PW5 doctor has categorically denied suggestion that injuries received by deceased could have been sustained because of kerosene oil from stove fell on her body due to pinning of stove and also by fall of tin of kerosene oil on floor. He has deposed without any equivocation that burn injuries sustained by deceased were not possible due to accidental burns. Further, presence of kerosene on scalp hair of deceased and presence of dust particles in larynx of deceased clearly evince that kerosene oil was poured on skull of deceased which could not have happened by accident. Hence, testimony of PW3 minor daughter that kerosene oil accidentally spilled on body of her mother is thus absolutely unbelievable. Further, as per evidence of PW 1 brother, he after reaching place of occurrence found his sister ablaze and she had stated that her husband has poured kerosene on her and put her ablaze. There is material to show that PW8 father arrived after his son and prosecution has explained about delayed arrival of father. SC held that when Trial Court and HC have disbelieved plea of alibi which is concurrent finding of fact, there is no warrant to dislodge same. SC held that nothing has been brought on record that it was physical impossibility of presence of accused to be at scene of offence by reason of his presence at another place. Plea can succeed only if it is shown that accused was so far away at relevant time that he could not be present at place where crime was committed. Hence, conviction order passed by Trial Court and HC are confirmed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Conviction of accused cannot be questioned when same is proved by prosecution beyond reasonable doubt.
