Delhi Cloth and General Mills Limited v Kushal Bhan

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1960 SC 806; 1960 (1) LLJ 520; 1960 SCJ 711; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act 1947, s. 33(2) - Misconduct - Dismissal - Legality - Respondent was employee of appellant company - Appellant passed dismissal order on ground of misconduct - Appellant filed application before Tribunal for approval of action and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, case is of a very simple nature and so employer cannot be blamed for course adopted by him. In the circumstances, there was in our opinion no failure of natural justice in this case and if respondent did not choose to take part in the enquiry, no fault can be found with that enquiry. SC opined that this was a case in which tribunal patently erred in not granting approval u/s. 33(2) of the Act. Besides it is apparent that in making the order under appeal, tribunal has completely lost sight of the limits of its jurisdiction u/s. 33(2) of the Act. Order passed by Tribunal was set aside. Appeal allowed.
