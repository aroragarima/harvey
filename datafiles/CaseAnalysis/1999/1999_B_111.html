Bihar State Electricity Board and another v Bijay Bhadur and another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2229; (2000) 10 SCC 99

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bihar Government Service (Hindi Examination) Regulation, 1968

Summary: Service - Bihar Government Service (Hindi Examination) Regulation, 1968, regn.8 - Increment - Recovery of diverse amount - Basis of memorandum - Implementation of requirement - Deduction of increments - Petitioners/respondent asserted that they had been given yearly increments based on service records without any misrepresentation on their part and that they have passed Hindi Noting and Drafting Examination - Board, however, on basis of two memoranda had ordered for recovery of diverse sums of money paid to them as and by way of increments - It was contended before HC that Board itself was not very serious as regards implementation of such requirement as otherwise resolution of 1979 could not have been dealt with in manner as it had been by resolution of 1996 Subsequent different view was expressed by some of Single Judges of HC by reason matter was placed before DB and latter by its judgment quashed two recovery orders - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether judgment of DB of HC wherein two memoranda issued by Board was justifiable - Held, since payments was made without any representation or misrepresentation, appellant-Board could not possibly be granted any liberty to deduct or recover excess amount paid by way of increments at earlier point of time - Further, act or acts on part of appellant Board could not under any circumstances be said to be in consonance with equity, good conscience and justice - As such actions initiated for recovery could not be sustained under any circumstances - Those order be restricted to facts of petitioners - It was clarified that regn. 8 of the Regulation operated on its own and Board would be at liberty to take appropriate steps in accordance with law except however in case or cases which has/have attained finality - However, in regard to decision of matter in issue and in particular reference to factual aspect did not feel inclined to accept observations of HC pertained to regn. 8 of Regulation - HC in judgment observed that Board should not be allowed to pass order for recovery of said amount as said amount had already become due to them - Those observation sounds contrary to regn. 8 of the Regulations which recorded that no arrears of stopped increments should be payable though person would pass examination later on - Appeal disposed of.
