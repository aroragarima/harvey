Jayaswals Neco Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 160; (2007) 13 SCC 807; 2006 (195) E.L.T. 142; 2007 (8) S.T.R. 305; [2009] 20 VST 353

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 11 A and 35 L - Central Excises Act, 1944 - Whether Tribunal erred in holding that assessee is not entitled to exemption under Notification No. 223/88-C.E.? - If a Bench of co-ordinate jurisdiction does not agree with a Bench of same strength, then it should refer matter to a larger Bench and refrain from taking upon itself not to follow such decision and take a contra view - Classification of particular goods adopted in earlier decisions must not be lightly disregarded in subsequent decisions, lest such judicial inconsistency shake public confidence in administration of justice - Held, assessees would be entitled to benefit under exemption Notification No. 223/1988-C.E. - Impugned orders are set aside - Appeal allowed.
