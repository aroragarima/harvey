Bank of India v Lekhimoni Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 489; (2000) 3 SCC 640; AIR 2000 SC 1172; AIR 2000 SCW 844; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 64; 2000 (39) ALR 215; 2000 (1) CLT 386; [2000] 102 Comp Cas 350; JT 2000 (3) SC 73; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 185; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 455; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 344; 2000(2) SCALE 312; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 216; 2000 (2) SLT 700; 2000 (Supp) RD 105; 2000 (2) Supreme 299

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Attachment Of Property, Bailiff, Wrongful, Malice, Wrongful Attachment

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 95, O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 172 - Tort - Malicious legal process - Liability of pledge when injunction obtained wrongfully in suit for compensation/damages - Not barred u/s. 95 Civil Code and plaintiff obtained possession on order of ejectment of tenant - Injunction restraining plaintiff from removing subtenant's goods - Maintainability of claim for damages by plaintiff when grounds are insufficient and improbable - Held, maintainable - Application disposed of.
