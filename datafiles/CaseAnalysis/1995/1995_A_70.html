Karnail Singh v Anil Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 630; (1995) 2 SCC 9; JT 1995 (2) SC 516; 1995 (2) RentLR 176; 1995(1) SCALE 141; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, s. 15(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 15 - Neeru sold the property in dispute to the appellant by a registered sale deed - Her brother, the respondent laid the suit for preemption u/s. 15(1)(b) - Second appeal was dismissed in limine - This court in Atam Prakash v. State of Haryana1 declared s. 15(1)(b)(i) to (iii) of the Act as amended in 1960 as ultra vires arts. 14 and 15 of the Constitution - Claim of the respondent on the basis of s. 15(1)(a)(ii) having been declared to be ultra vires, this court granted leave - Whether the respondent is a co-sharer entitled to pre-emption? - Held, it is now concluded that even relations who would be otherwise not entitled u/cls. (i) to (iii) of s. 15(1)(b) of the Act would also become 'co-sharers' under cl. (iv) - Being not a party to the sale transaction of joint property, they are entitled to claim pre-emption - Respondent was not a party to the sale transaction executed by his sister Neeru - He would be other co-sharer, entitled to pre-emption u/s. 15(1)(b)(iv) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
