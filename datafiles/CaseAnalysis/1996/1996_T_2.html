Sant Singh v Additional District Judge

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 552; (1996) 3 SCC 400; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 126; 1996 (2) AWC 902; JT 1996 (3) SC 74; 1996 (87) RD 233; 1996(2) SCALE 583; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 653; 1996 (2) Supreme 629; 1996 (2) UJ 79

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, ss. 4A and 5 - (A) Whether the sale deed D/- 26-10-1970 executed by appellant transferring land to his minor daughter, is in good faith and for adequate consideration and is not a 'Benami' transaction for immediate or deferred benefit of tenure-holder or other family members? - Sale deed was executed by the father making the wife as the guardian of the minor daughter; under personal law, father during his life-time remains to be the legal guardian of the minor and mother cannot be a legal guardian and he cannot sell the land to himself - Held, she being a minor and the appellant being a tenure holder in respect of land under question, even though sale was made, the land would be includable in his holding - (B) Whether or not, the 12 plots of land transferred by appellant to his private temple can be included in his holding? - Further held, though the transfer was made in favour of the temple which is found to be a private temple, it being after the appointed day (it was done on 26-10-1970), is invalid in law and the land is required to be included in his holding - (C) Whether the plots Nos. 5 and 13 are part of unirrigated land and therefore, have to be treated as an unirrigable land? - U/s. 4 A, cl. (a)(iii) states that in determining any land as irrigated land, land cultivated by any State tube-well or a private irrigation work would be considered as irrigable land - Further held, in view of the fact that the lands are being irrigated by tube-well, treating this land as irrigated land cannot be said to be unwarranted - Appeal dismissed.
