Trai Foods Limited v National Insurance Company and others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1708; (2004) 13 SCC 656; 2012 (3) CPJ(Sc) 17

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Insured against Earthquake - Damages - Entitlement - Appellant made claim against damages caused by earthquake with respondents/corporation, which was partly allowed - Appellant filed claim before National Commission for damages - National Commission refused to entertain application and directed to refer matter to Civil Court - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that commission was fully empowered to decide matter and case in fact did not require elaborate as claim could be decided on documents on record - Whether National Commission was justified in not entertaining application of appellant - Held, Commission should address itself to quantity and nature of claim as well as nature of evidence which could be required to be submitted both in respect of claim and damages suffered and nature of legal issues before deciding that matter ought to be decided by Civil Court in regular course - Consumer Forum had been set up to grant speedy remedy and had been given responsibility of achieving this object - They were not meant to duplicate Civil Courts and subject litigants to delay - However, in instant case, reasons given in impugned order for referring matter to Civil Court was correct - There was no doubt with regard to nature of claim, large amount of damages claimed and extensive enquiry into evidence which would be necessary to resolve disputes between parties that this was not a matter to be decided summarily at all - Hence, directed that in case appellant approach Civil Court, period spent between filling and disposal of matter by SC, would be excluded u/s. 14 of the 1963 Act - Appeal dismissed.
