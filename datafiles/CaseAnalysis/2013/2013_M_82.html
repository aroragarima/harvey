Mary Pappa Jebamani v Ganesan and others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 808; (2014) 14 SCC 477; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 364; 2014 CRLJ 1012; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 367; 2013(15) SCALE 154; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 1042

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 294(b) and 323 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Voluntarily causing hurt - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents (accused) allegedly abused and beaten appellant (complainant) and his father and thereby committed offence u/ss. 294(b) and 323 of IPC - Trial Court gave benefit of doubt to accused and acquitted them - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before Lower Appellate Court, which was allowed convicting accused - Aggrieved accused filed a revision before HC, which was allowed setting aside order of Lower Appellate Court - Hence, instant appeals - Appellant contended that investigation conducted in the matter was full of legal and procedural infirmities and required retrial - Whether impugned judgment passed by Single Judge setting aside Lower Appellate Court was justified - Held, appellant herself had failed to disclose as to what exactly was the genesis of occurrence as also the contents of abuse which could persuade SC that a de novo trial of the accused was essential - Instant case was not fit to be entertained as it was not possible to take a view that investigation was shoddy or suffered from grave lacunae which would justify the parameters for retrial at the instance of appellant for the mere asking as it did not meet the legal requirements justifying a retrial - HC certainly had the legal obligation to assign reasons while allowing the revision of accused stating why it had set aside the judgment and order of First Appellate Court while exercising revisional jurisdiction specially when Lower Appellate Court found sufficient evidence on record to set aside the acquittal of accused and upheld their conviction u/s. 294(b) and 323 of IPC - HC was directed to reconsider and assign reasons for setting aside the order of conviction and for fresh consideration - Appeals disposed of.
