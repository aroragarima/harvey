Power Machines India Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, S. Abdul Nazeer

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 315; (2017) 7 SCC 323; AIR 2017 SC 2567; JT 2017 (6) SC 37; 2017 (4) MLJ 82; 2017(4) SCALE 690B

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Delegated Legislation, BANKING & FINANCE, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Aggrieved, Subordinate Legislation, CONSTITUTION, Arbitration, Legislative Policy, Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, Plural Remedies, Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, Cantonments Act, 1924, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Repugnancy, Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) Act, 1955, Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Bihar and Orissa CoOperative Societies Act, 1935, Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Constitution of India, 1950, Income-tax Act, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Council Rules, 2006

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21, O. 21 r. 22, O. 21 r. 26, O. 21 r. 58, O. 21 r. 58(1), O. 21 r. 66, O. 21 r. 69, O. 21 r. 89, O. 21 r. 92 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 18(2), 34, 36, 36(1), 65, 66, 67, 68, 69, 70, 71, 72, 73, 74, 75, 76, 77, 78, 79, 80, 81, 82, 83, 84, 85 - Micro, Small and Medium Enterprises Development Act, 2006, ss. 18, 18(1), 18(3), 21, 21(3), 30 - Madhya Pradesh Micro and Small Enterprises Facilitation Council Rules, 2006, r. 5 - Arbitral award - Ultra vires - Challenged - An award was passed under the 2006 Act by which appellant was directed to pay a sum of Rs.1,15,77,630/- with an amount of Rs.1,04,96,746/- towards interest to respondent no. 3 - Collector initiated recovery of the amount under the Rules - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC for declaring r. 5 of the Rules as ultra vires - HC dismissed writ petition holding that r. 5 of the Rules was not ultra vires and was in strict conformity with the 2006 Act - Whether it was permissible to State Govt. to enact r. 5 of the Rules for recovery of the amount as arrears of land revenue and speedy remedy could have been provided under the Rules framed under the 2006 Act, while providing remedy State has exceeded its ken of powers.

Held, recovery of arrears of land revenue was resorted to after adjudication process when arbitral award was passed and was not objected to within the time prescribed u/s. 34 of the 1996 Act. CPC cannot be the only remedy it was open to legislate recovery mechanism without interference of Civil Court. In instant case, by exercising the rule making power conferred u/s. 30 of the 2006 Act, the purpose of the 2006 Act was being protected. The rule intends to implement the object. It cannot be said that authority had exceeded nor it could be said that the scope of the 2006 Act was widened or constricted under the garb of rulemaking power. Object of both provisions was to ensure recovery. In SC opinion r. 5 of the Rules being a remedial provision was ancillary. It was open to provide for an additional speedier remedy so as to carry out the objective of the 2006 Act. Procedural provision of recovery of arrears of land revenue cannot be said to be prejudicial to appellants. Once adjudication of dues was made it was expected of appellant to honour it after lapse of time u/s. 34 of the 1996 Act. It was only procedural provision and intends to simplify the procedure of execution, once arbitral award was passed. The procedure for recovery of land revenue envisaged u/r. 5 of the Rules could not be said to be discriminatory, it being quite reasonable procedure. It cannot be said to be harsh or drastic however quite a reasonable procedure was and it furthers the mandate of the 2006 Act. The difference between the procedure of execution of r. 5 of the Rules and that of CPC cannot be said to be unconscionable so as to attract the vice of discrimination. Appeal dismissed.
