Buddhu Singh and another v State Of Bihar (Now Jharkhand)

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 313; (2011) 14 SCC 471; JT 2011 (6) SC 617; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 895; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 Part II - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court found accused persons guilty u/s. 302 of IPC - On appeal, HC also affirmed the conviction and sentenced awarded by the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Accused persons contended that this was a case of single blow and the blow could not have been intended to be given on the head though it did land on the head - It was further contended that once the injury was unintended, the offence could be converted into s. 304 Part II of IPC from s. 302 IPC because the accused ought to have the knowledge that a single assault by an axe could result into the death of the deceased - Held, common intention on part of accused persons could be attributed since they had done the over act of grappling with and pinning down the deceased - Accused 'B' dealt an axe blow which could not be said to be intended towards the head and could have landed anywhere - However, it landed on the head of the deceased - Therefore, the element of intention was ruled out - Further, defence that there could not have been the intention to commit the murder of the deceased was justified by the fact that the accused 'B' did not repeat the assault - Prosecution was able to establish the guilt of the accused persons u/s. 304 Part II of IPC - Conviction of the accused converted to s. 304 Part II of IPC from s. 302 IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
