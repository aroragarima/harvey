Kishinchand Chellaram v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 176; AIR 1963 SC 390; [1962] 32 Comp Cas 1046; [1962] 46 ITR 640; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 268

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(2) and 66 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Companies Act, 1913 - Income Tax - Dividend declared and credited by company - Subsequent resolution passed to treat payments made as loans - (A) Effect on liability of shareholder to pay tax - (B) Whether a question which is not arising out of order of Income Tax Tribunal can be raised before Supreme Court in appeal - Tax liability on payment received as dividend not affected - Question cannot be raised in Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
