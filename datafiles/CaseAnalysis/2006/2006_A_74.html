Anand Hanumathsa Katare v Additional District Magistrate and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 703; (2006) 10 SCC 725; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 102; 2007 CRLJ 30; 2006 (3) JCC 2007; 2006(10) SCALE 385; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Karnataka Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot-Leggers, Drug Offenders, Gamblers, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1985

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Karnataka Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot-Leggers, Drug Offenders, Gamblers, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1985, ss. 11(1), 13, 2(b), 3(2), 3(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 11, 3, 8 - Habeas corpus petition filed by appellant against detention order dismissed - Appeal against - Plea that Detaining Authority became functus officio moment State Govt. accords approval and Detaining Authority should not have dealt with representation and should have referred matter to State Govt. - Held, in grounds of detention it was specifically indicated to appellant that if he wanted to represent to Govt. of Karnataka he was to submit same directly to Govt. through Superintendent of Central Jail in which he is detained - Appeal dismissed.
