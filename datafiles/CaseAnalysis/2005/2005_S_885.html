Syed Khadim Hussain v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1762; (2006) 9 SCC 195

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Dependent of Deceased Employee - Compassionate Ground - Appointment - Entitlement - Appellant, deceased father was employed with respondents and application filed by appellant mother for appointment on compassionate grounds was rejected - Appellant filed application to respondent and was rejected after 5 years of its submission - Appellant filed petition before DB - DB rejected petition on ground of delay in filing - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that in matters of compassionate appointments authorities should extend service in effective manner so that eligible candidate might avail opportunity - Whether DB was justified in rejecting petition of appellant - Held, rules framed by State there was no specific provision as to what should be done in case dependents were minors and there would be any relaxation of age in case they did not attain majority within prescribed period for submitting application - In instant case, appellant had submitted application , when he was 13 years' old and application was rejected after a period of 6 years and that too without giving any reason and reason given by authorities was incorrect as at time of rejection of application he should had crossed 18 years and he could had been very well considered for appointment - Application submitted by widow in time should had been considered by authorities - Hence, directed respondent to consider application of appellant for appointment - Appeal disposed of.
