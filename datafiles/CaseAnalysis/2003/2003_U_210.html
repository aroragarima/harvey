Ram Preeti Yadav v Uttar Pradesh Board of High School and

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 713; (2003) 8 SCC 311; AIR 2003 SC 4268; 2004 (55) ALR 102; 2003 (5) AWC 4449; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 50; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 25; 2003(7) SCALE 316; 2003 (6) SLR 71; 2003 (5) SLT 394; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 352; 2003 (7) Supreme 74

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 226 - Result of intermediate examination of the respondent cancelled after more than 10 years - Marks-sheet were issued - Fraud - Held, a presumption against him must be raised particularly having regard to the fact that he had not been able to produce any material to show as to why no attempt was made by him to obtain a final marks-sheet and/or certificate for passing the examination - Further held, there is no equity in favour of the respondent, inasmuch as he knew that his result has been withheld because of the allegation of having used unfair means in the examination. Suppressing this fact, he took admission in B.A. and studied further.
