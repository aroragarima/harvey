Haresh Dayaram Thakur v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2603; (2000) 6 SCC 179; AIR 2000 SC 2281; 2000 (2) ARBLR 401; JT 2000 (6) SC 349; 2000(4) SCALE 451; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1140

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: India Evidence Act, 1872, Maharashtra Housing and Development Act, 1966

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, Part I and Part III - Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1966, s. 66 (1) (MHADA) - Conciliation Proceedings - Conciliator's Report - Challenged - MHADA had granted lease of said flat to one of original occupier/allotee who transferred his right title and interest to other party under agreement of transfer dt. 7-4-1986 same was purchased by appellant and respondent No.3 for consideration of Rs. 3,45,000 under agreement of transferred dt. 21-11-1989 right, title and interest of flat and also became member of society of flat owners of building called Melody Cooperative Housing Society - Appellant had applied to MHADA for regularization of allotment of flat in his name - In December 1992 on routine inspection of premises Estate Manager of MHADA reported that property was in occupation of appellant and his family members including respondent No. 3, though it stood in name of original occupier and therefore, they were unauthorized occupants of flat - On receipt of report proceeding was initiated u/s. 66(1) of the 1966 Act - In pursuance of order dt. 23- 4-1997 MHADA evicted all unauthorized occupants from flat and sealed same - In said order leave was given to present appellant to establish his claim in respect of property in light of deed of transfer dt. 21-11-1989 and other documents executed by original occupier/allottee in his favour - Subsequently, after examining relevant documents MHADA regularized allotment of flat in favour of appellant by order under the 1966 Act - Aggrieved respondent No.3 filed writ petition before HC which disposed of writ petition and directed inter alia that competent authority of MHADA would re-examine claims of respondent No. 3 as well as appellant herein and pass speaking order in accordance with law - Therefore, in compliance with directions of HC competent authority of MHADA passed order dt. 18-12-1998 rejecting claim of respondent no. 3 and confirming allotment/regularization of flat in name of appellant - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition u/art. 226 and 227 before HC which appointed conciliator with regard to dispute between parties with assent of both parties - Conciliator sent report without signatures of both parties through registered post to HC - Appellant filed objections before HC which was rejected and confirmed conciliator's report - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Conciliators report confirming by HC was justifiable - Held, passing impugned order failed to notice apparent illegalities committed by conciliator in drawing up so called settlement agreement, keeping it secret from parties and sending it to Court without obtaining their signature on same - It was well settled that if statute prescribes procedure for doing thing, it had to be done according to that procedure - Therefore, order passed by HC confirming settlement agreement received from conciliator was wholly unsupportable - Thus, set aside HC's order and conciliator's report and directed HC to dispose of writ petition afresh on merits in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
