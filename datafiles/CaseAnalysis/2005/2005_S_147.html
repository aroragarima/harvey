Atma Ram v Shakuntala Rani

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 521; (2005) 7 SCC 211; 2005 (61) ALR 450; 2005 (123) DLT 127; JT 2005 (8) SC 104; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 12; 2005 (4) RLW 2469; 2005(7) SCALE 35; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1071

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1934, T.N. Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1951, Punjab Urban Rent Act

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1951, (the Act) ss. 11, 14(1), 14(1)(a), 17(1), 17(4), 27, 8 - Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1934, (the Punjab Act) s. 31 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 26 - Eviction - Default in payment of rent - Second default - Trial Court and Tribunal rejected eviction - HC allowed petition - Whether appellant defaulted in payment of rent inasmuch as he had not paid or tendered or deposited rent in manner required by law? - Whether deposit of rent under Punjab Act can be construed to be a valid deposit under Act? - Held, there is a specific provision which provides procedure to be followed in a contingency - If rent is not deposited in Court of Rent Controller as required by s. 27 and is deposited somewhere else, it shall not be treated as a valid payment/tender of arrears of rent within meaning of Act - HC interfered because there was a serious error of law committed by courts below - Appeal dismissed.
