Municipal Committee, Patiala v Model Town Residents Assn. and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1223; (2007) 8 SCC 669; AIR 2007 SC 2844; JT 2007 (9) SC 590; 2007 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2368; 2007 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2429; 2007 (148) PLR 333; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 754; 2007(9) SCALE 507; 2007 (6) SCJ 103; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Hypothetical

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss. 3(1)(b), 71(1) and 3(8aa) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Registration Act, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 265 - Whether High Court was right in holding that ss. 3(1)(b) and 3(8 aa) in Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 as substituted by Punjab Amending Act 11 of 1994 suffers from vice of discrimination and, therefore is unconstitutional? - Held, under amended s. 3(1)(b), a formula has been evolved by which in case of self occupied premises tax has to be imposed on annual value calculated on basis of present market value of land plus cost of construction minus 10% deduction on account of depreciation - S. 3(8aa) states that while estimating present market value of land Assessing Officer will keep in mind principles mentioned in Land Acquisition Act, 1894 whereas under above formula, Assessing Officer will keep registered sale instances of buildings before him in order to compare cost of construction of houses in same locality, area etc. - In present case, Legislature seems to have taken cognizance of fact that land prices have been increasing which remains excluded from composite valuation of an asset - Appeal allowed.
