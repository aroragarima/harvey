Haji C. H. Mohammad Koya v T. K. S. M. A. Muthukoya

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 497; (1979) 2 SCC 8; AIR 1979 SC 154; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 664

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Press and Registration of Books Act, 1867, s. 7 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 123(3), 3(A) and 4 - (A) Whether petitioner can avail of presumption to be drawn u/s. 7 of Press Act? - Held, yes - In order to avoid multiplicity of suits and uncertainties of liabilities, it was necessary to choose one of persons from staff and make him liable for all articles or matters published in paper - In case of Chandrika it is unmistakably mentioned name of Aboobaker as printer, publisher and editor of Chandrika - Appellant is merely shown as Chief Editor - Presumption u/s. 7 of Press Act, 1867, is a rebuttable presumption and editor can rebut presumption by showing that he had nothing to do with publication of editorial or news report - (B) Whether appellant is liable for corrupt practices alleged by petitioner u/s. 123(3A)? - Held, no - Speeches and articles published are of various persons - However para 5 may amount to an allegation as contemplated by s. 123(3A) of Act - Extract of speech quoted above also shows that there does not appear to be any intention on part of speaker to preach hatred or enmity between two classes of citizens - Appellant had merely condemned dissident leaders of community who had stabbed organization - Actual cartoon seems to depict Janasangh as a pig and Shri E M S Namboodiripad, Marxist Leader, cutting flesh of pig and serving it to Muslims - There is no reliable evidence to show that appellant had any knowledge or had given prior consent to publication of cartoon - Appellant will get benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
