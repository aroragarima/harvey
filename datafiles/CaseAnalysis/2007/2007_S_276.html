Dadan Ram & Ors v State Of Bihar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1171; (2007) 13 SCC 583; AIR 2008 SC 588; JT 2007 (13) SC 293; 2007(13) SCALE 461; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961

Summary: Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961 - Whether the 'parcha holders', who are in possession of the land in question, have any right to be heard in a proceeding arising out of s. 45-B? - Held, prior to re-opening, notice to all the parties including person(s) in possession was mandatory - After re-opening the case, the State Government is to dispose of the matter afresh in accordance with law - Before passing any order in a concluded issue, the authority is expected to satisfy the minimum requirement of principles of natural justice by issuance of notice and hearing - Orders impugned suffer from the infirmity of not giving reasonable opportunity to the appellants before reopening the proceedings - Appeal partly allowed.
