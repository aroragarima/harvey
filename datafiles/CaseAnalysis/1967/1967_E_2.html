D.Sanjeevayya v Election Tribunal Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 133; AIR 1967 SC 1211; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 112; 1967 (69) PLR 70; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 489

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81,and 150 - Writ of Mandamus - For filing vacant seat - Whether High Court was right in holding that no case was made out for issue of a Writ of Mandamus to Election Commission on application of appellant to fill vacant seat irrespective of fact that an election petition in that regard was pending? - If candidate who filed election petition eventually gets a declaration that election of member is void and that he himself had been duly elected there will be two candidates representing same constituency at same time, one of them declared to be duly elected at General Election and other declared to have been elected at bye election and an impossible situation would arise - It cannot be supposed that Parliament contemplated such a situation while enacting s. 150 of Act - Held, HC was right in holding that no case was made out for issue of a Writ of Mandamus to Election Commission - Appeal dismissed.
