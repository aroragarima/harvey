H.M.T. House Building Co-Operative Society v M. Venkataswamappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2349; (1995) 3 SCC 128; AIR 1995 SC 2253; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 96

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.4(1), 6(1) - Land acquisition - Maintainability - Lands on basis of notifications issued u/ss. 4(1), 6(1) of the Act was acquired for petitioner society, treating acquisition to be for public purpose - No order of State Govt. as required granting prior approval for acquisition of the lands in question for housing scheme of e petitioner society had been produced - Petitioner had entered into agreement with contractor more or less on the same terms and conditions as was in the case of Society and assured that lands in question should be acquired on basis of notification issued by State Govt. u/ss. 4(1), 6(1) of the Act - Petitioners allegations was regarding collection of huge amounts from different applicants for site who were not even members of the society and how the society had entered into an agreement with agents, who with their influence have got the lands acquired - HC quashed the notification u/ss. 4(1), 6(1) of the Act - Whether the order of HC was maintainable - Held, HC concluded that Society itself was not bona fide House Building Society - HC issued at instance of agents appointed by petitioner society, to whom huge amounts had been paid for influencing Govt. to issue notification - Hence, there was no scope to interfere with order of HC quashing notifications u/ss. 4(1) and 6(1) of the Act - Petitions dismissed.
