Allahabad Development Authority v Nasiruzzaman And Ors,

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2099; (1996) 6 SCC 424; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 257; 1997 (1) AWC 153; 1996 (4) CCC 31; 1996 (4) CLT 137; JT 1996 (8) SC 429; 1996 (2) RentLR 710; 1996(7) SCALE 28; 1998 (Supp) RD 363; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 435; 1996 (7) Supreme 305

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Land Acquisition Act, 1894,ss. 6, 11 A and 17(4) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Whether High Court was right in passing order declaring that acquisition proceedings by operation of s. 11 - A stood lapsed and direction given for delivery of possession to respondents would be in accordance with law? - Once lands stood vested in State, question of divesting and revesting acquired land in erstwhile owner did not arise - Amendment Act has to be relied upon only in pending proceedings - But once possession was taken pursuant to exercise of power u/s. 17(4) of Act, lands stood vested in State u/s. 16 free from all encumbrances - Thereby, question of lapse of proceedings of notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 does not arise - A statutory direction or prohibition cannot be overridden or defeated by a previous judgment between parties - In view of fact that land had already stood vested in State free from all encumbrances, question of divesting does not arise - After vesting has taken place, question of lapse of notification u/s. 4(1) and declaration u/s. 6 would not arise - Held, original direction was erroneous and later direction with regard to delivery of possession of land, in consequence, was not valid in law - Further, respondents are entitled to interest at 9% for one year from date of taking possession and thereafter at 15% per annum till date of deposit into court - Respondents are not entitled to market value as on date of award - Appeal allowed.
