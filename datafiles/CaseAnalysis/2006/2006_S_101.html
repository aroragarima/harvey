Satya Narain Shukla v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 646; (2006) 9 SCC 69; AIR 2006 SC 2511; 2006 (7) AWC 6790; 2006(5) SCALE 627; 2006 (6) SCJ 272; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 312 - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3 - Indian Administrative Service (Probation) Rules, 1954 - Indian Administrative Service (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1987 - Indian Administrative Service (Fixation of Cadre Strength) Regulations, 1955 - Indian Administrative Service (Cadre) Rules, 1951 - Whether appellant's non-empanelment to Addl. Secy. post is arbitrary and vitiated on account of mala fides, arbitrariness or violative of applicable rules? - Held, no strong motive for any of officers to bear animus against appellant to ensure that he was not empanelled - Dissent is essence of democracy and merely because one disagrees with another, one cannot jump to conclusion that other harbors a grudge against former - Para 14 of Central Staffing Scheme, read in light of judgment of this Court in Union of India v. Samar Singh's case, appointment of State cadre IAS officers for post of Additional Secretary/Secretary to Govt. of India does not amount to promotion - Non-empanelment of appellant for post of Addl. Secy to Govt. of India was neither arbitrary nor contrary to Rules nor vitiated by mala fides as alleged - Civil Appeal No. 2081 of 2003 allowed and Civil Appeal No. 2082 of 2003 dismissed.
