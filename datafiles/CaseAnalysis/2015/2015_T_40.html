Edara Haribabu and another v Tulluri Venkata Narasimham and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 827; (2016) 2 SCC 640; AIR 2016 SC 597; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 120; 2016 (1) ALT(SC) 24; 2015 (6) AWC 6512; JT 2015 (8) SC 388; 2015 (7) MLJ 120; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Dismissed, Disqualified, Candidate, Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, Andhra Pradesh Conduct of Election of Member (Co-opted), President and Vice-President of Mandal Parishad and Member (Co-opted), Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson of Zilla Parishad Rules, 2006

Summary: Election - Andhra Pradesh Conduct of Election of Member (Co-opted), President and Vice-president of Mandal Parishad and Member (Co-opted), Chairperson and Vice-Chairperson of Zillaparishad Rules, 2006, r. 22(1) - Andhra Pradesh Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, ss. 181A, 22(5), 22A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Disqualification - Mandatory Directions Challenged - Appellant, elected member of zilla parishad constituency contested and elected to post of Chairperson of zillaparishad - However, respondent no.2/ presiding officer disqualified appellant and 'X' was appointed temporarily - Single Judge allowed writ petition of appellant against denial of interim grant by Lower Court and suspended disqualification proceedings of respondent no.2 - Further, respondent no.1/ one of contestant in election filed writ appeals and DB of HC directed Vice-Chairperson to discharge functions of Chairpersons for office of parishad till further orders - Hence, instant appeals - Whether DB was justified in allowing applications filed in pending writ appeals and was, therefore, justified in issuing mandatory directions.

Held, the Courts are always vested with inherent and statutory power under O. 39 rr. 1, 2 and O.41 r. 5 of CPC, to stay/restrain the execution of the Action impugned in the lis during pendency of the lis. Further, s. 22A of the Act, states bar of jurisdiction as 'No order passed or proceedings taken under the provisions of this Act, shall be called in question in any Court, in any suit, or application, and no injunction shall be granted by any Court except District Court in respect of any action taken or about to be taken in pursuance of any power conferred by or under this Act'. In instant case, the effect of the suspension order dated 07.11.2014 of the Single Judge was that the appellant's disqualification from the post of member of ZPTC and the Chairperson of ZPP was kept in abeyance till the disposal of the election petitions. Hence, once writ petitions filed by the appellant were allowed by suspending the proceedings, the respondents had no option but to allow the appellant to function as the Chairman of ZPP. Further, if the reasoning given by the High Court while passing the interim order is perverse and legally unsustainable being against the settled principle of law laid down by this Court, then interference of this Court by virtue of art.136 of Constitution, in such order is called for regardless of the nature of the order impugned in appeal. Hence, impugned direction/order passed by DB of HC is set aside and as a consequences all the pending appeals/petitions before the High Court also stand finally disposed. Appeals allowed.
