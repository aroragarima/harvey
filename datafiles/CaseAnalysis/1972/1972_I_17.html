Jayaram Mudaliar v Iyyaswaar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 379; (1972) 2 SCC 200; AIR 1973 SC 569; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 139

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu High Court (Appellate Side) Rules, 1965,r. 28 of O.4- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 -Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Land Improvement Loans Act, 1883, ss. 7(1)(a)and 7(1)(c) - Tamil Nadu Revenue Recovery Act, 1864, s. 42 - Letters Patent (Mad ), cls. 15 and 37 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - (A) Rule prescribing oral application for certificate immediately after delivery of judgment - Vires as to cls. 15 and 37 of Letters Patent (Mad ) - (B) Special leave granted after hearing respondent - Object as to competency of appeal - First time at hearing - (C) Land and property - Doctrine of 'lis pendens' - Object and scope of s. 52 - Applicability to voluntary sale and revenue sale for recovery of loans due under Land Improvement Loans Act - Held, is not ultra vires of cl. 37 of Letters Patent and is not inconsistent with its cl. 15 - Further held, not allowed for first time at hearing of appeal - Could not be removed from ambit of lis pendens so long as sale was not in execution of mortgage-decree - Appeal dismissed.
