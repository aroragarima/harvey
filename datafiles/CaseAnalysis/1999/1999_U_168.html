Haridas Parsedia v Urmila Shakya and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 843; (2000) 1 SCC 81; (2000) SCC (L&S) 99; AIR 2000 SC 278; 2000 (84) FLR 283; JT 1999 (9) SC 152; 2000 LabIC 222; 2000 (1) LLN 14; 2000 (1) SLJ 212; 2000 (1) SLR 310; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16(4) - Three appellants took exams and they scored 40% in all papers - All three appellants were appointed by state - Respondent 1 herein, who was also a departmental candidate belonging to SC category, challenged appointments of appellants on ground that she deserved to be appointed in reserved vacancy meant for Scheduled Castes (viz three in number) as she had got more than 50% marks in each of written test papers while appellants had not got those marks, they were ineligible to be considered for appointment - She, accordingly, filed Original Application No. 1147 of 1993 before State Administrative Tribunal, Madhya Pradesh - All three appellants were joined as party respondents along with State of Madhya Pradesh - In meantime, appellants were put on probation for two years - Tribunal took view that partly allowed petition and quashed appointment of three appellants - Writ petition was filed in High Court - HC dismissed writ petition stating that there was no substance - Review petition filed against judgment of HC was also dismissed - Whether general passing marks provided by rules at 50% in each paper could be brought down to 40% by giving 10% relaxation for these SC/ST candidates? - Held, it is patently erroneous to hold that the decision of the Govt. for relaxation of passing marks for SC/ST departmental candidates at the departmental examination can be applicable only when in such examination, the departmental candidates belonging to general category also compete with the SC/ST candidates and not otherwise - Holding general examinations for all types of candidates at a time or separate examinations for only SC/ST candidates for being appointed to the reserved posts on the one hand and for general category candidates on the other wherein SC/ST candidates can also compete for unreserved posts, is totally irrelevant for judging the efficacy of the relaxation policy - The general sweep of the policy decision already taken by the State of Madhya Pradesh u/art. 16(4) cannot get diluted on the basis of such an irrelevant and fortuitous circumstance - Further held, appointment orders issued by State of Madhya Pradesh to all appellants are held to be perfectly legal and valid and fully operative - State of Madhya Pradesh is directed to give effect to these appointment orders and fix seniority of appellants in higher cadre of Transport Sub-Inspectors, pursuant to their aforesaid appointment orders - State of Madhya Pradesh is also directed to make available to appellants all consequential benefits, including monetary benefits flowing from said orders of appointment as Transport Sub-Inspectors - In case any of appellants are reverted in meantime pursuant to impugned order of tribunal, they will be entitled to be restored to posts of Transport Sub-Inspector in light of initial appointment orders and also be entitled to all other reliefs, including permissible monetary benefits and difference of salary and other emoluments legally permissible to appellants - All these benefits will have to be worked out by respondent State accordingly and made available to them as expeditiously as possible - Appeals dismissed.
