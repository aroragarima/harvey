State Of Punjab v Balwinder Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 5; (2012) 2 SCC 182; 2012 ACJ 563; AIR 2012 SC 861; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 717; 2012 (4) CGLJ 291; 2012 CRLJ 1066; 2012 (1) JCC 606; JT 2012 (1) SC 43; 2012 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 473; 2012 (167) PLR 125; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 424; 2012 (3) RLW 2606; 2012(1) SCALE 62; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 45; 2012 (1) WLN(SC) 72

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Penal Code (Amendment) Act 27

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304A, 337 and 279 - Negligence - Quantum of Sentence - Reduction - Validity - Respondents were convicted u/s. 304A IPC to undergo 2 years rigorous imprisonment and for offence u/ss. 337 and 279 to undergo 6 months - Respondents appeal in Session court, was upheld - aggrieved filed revision in HC, HC reduced quantum of sentence and enhanced fine amount - Hence instant appeal - Respondents preferring revision did not dispute finding relating to negligence rendered by courts below and confined their submissions to quantum of sentence and contended that they had suffered protracted trial for 17 years and had already undergone custody for 15 days - Appellant contended that negligence was proved beyond reasonable doubt, therefore no leniency should be shown to respondents - Whether HC was justified in reducing the quantum of sentence - While considering quantum of sentence for offence causing death or injury by rash and negligent driving, prime considerations should be deterrence - Persons driving motor vehicles should not take a chance thinking that even if he is convicted he would be dealt with leniently by Court - For lessening high rate of accidents, courts are expected to consider all relevant facts and circumstances bearing question of sentence and proceed to impose a sentence commensurate with gravity of offence if prosecution is able to establish guilt beyond reasonable doubt - Reasoning of HC in reducing sentence and enhancing fine amount is not sufficient ground to drastically reduce sentence - Appeal allowed
