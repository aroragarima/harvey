Â Kavalappara Kottarathil Kochuni and Others v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 463; AIR 1960 SC 1080; 1960 KLT 31; 1961 (2) SCJ 443; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 887

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Constitutional Safeguards

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 245, 21, 13(2), 31(1), 19, 31, 19(1)(F), 19(5), 19(1) and 31A - Madras Marumakkathayam (Removal of Doubts) Act, 1955 - (A) Whether sthanam is an estate within meaning of art. 31 A of Constitution and therefore Madras Act extinguishing or modifying rights pertaining to said sthanam cannot be questioned on ground that it infringes arts. 14, 19 and 31 of Constitution? - Held, in this case Court would not be justified in not allowing respondents to raise contention, as validity of impugned Act depends upon application of art. 31A of Constitution Court, would, therefore, for purpose of this petition, assume against petitioner that he is in possession of properties in janmam right and proceed to consider on that basis contention raised - Impugned Act does not purport to modify or extinguish any right in an estate - Avowed object of it is only to declare particular sthanams to be Marumakkathayam tarwads and property pertaining to such sthanams as property of said tarwads - It declares particular sthanams to have always been tarwads and their property to have always been tarwad property - Result is that sole title of sthanee is not recognised and members of tarwad are given rights therein - Impugned Act does not effectuate any agrarian reform and regulate rights inter se between landlords and tenants - Respondents cannot rely upon art. 31A to deprive petitioner of his fundamental rights - (B) Whether Madras Marumakkathayam (Removal of Doubts) Act, 1955, violates arts. 14 and 19(1)(f) of Constitution? - Further held, under cl. (5), State can make a law imposing reasonable restrictions on fundamental rights embodied in art. 19(1)(f) in interests of general public but restrictions sought to be imposed shall not be arbitrary but must have reasonable relation to object sought to be achieved and shall be in interests of general public - Impugned Act is only a legislative device to take property of one and vest it in another without compensation, and, therefore, on its face stamped with unreasonableness - In short, impugned Act is expropriatory in character and is directly hit by art. 19(1)(f) and is not saved by cl. (5) of art. 19 - Petition dismissed.
