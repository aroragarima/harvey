State of Gujarat v Kothari and Associates

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 718; 2015 (113) ALR 690; 2016 (1) AWC 344; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 223; 2016 (1) CTC 109; JT 2015 (9) SC 421; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4596; 2015 (7) MLJ 729; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 893; 2015(11) SCALE 621; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 133; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 30

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Limitation Act, 1908

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Claim of damages - Limitation period - Determination - Appellant State invited tenders for providing lining to main canal line - Respondent was awarded work, however, there was delay on part of appellant State in handing site to respondent on three occasions - Respondent claimed damages from appellant State which was rejected by appellant State - Trial Court passed order in favor of respondent and awarded compensation in favor of respondent along with interest - Further, HC confirmed order passed by Trial Court and held that suit was found to be within prescribed period of limitation - Whether suit filed by respondent is within prescribed period of limitation and respondent is entitled to claim damages.

Held, contract was not broken as respondents chose to keep it alive despite its repeated breaches by appellant State. Further, there is successive or multiple breaches, rather than of continuous breach, as each delay in handing over site by appellant State constituted to breach that was distinct and complete in itself and gave rise to separate cause of action for which respondent could have rescinded contract or claimed compensation due to prolongation of time and resultant escalation of costs. Further, SC held that in present case cause of action had arisen on each occasion when appellant State failed to hand over site at contractually stipulated time. Specifically, limitation periods arose on 15-11-1976, 15-11-1977, 15-11-1978 and 15-11-1979, i.e. on first day of each season, when appellant State committed breach by failing to hand over site. Hence, period of limitation did not commence at termination of contract period or date of final payment. Suit, however, was filed on 25-1-1985, after limitation period of three years for even final breach, as various causes of action became time barred on 15-11-1979, 15-11-1980, 15-11-1981 and 15-11-1982 respectively. SC held that since no legal action was taken it is presumed that claim for damages had been abandoned and given go by Respondent. Hence, SC held that respondent failed to file suit within limitation period. Impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In case of multiple breaches, limitation period commences from each breach and suit for damages should be filed within limitation period.
