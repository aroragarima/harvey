Mohd. Swaleh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3049; (1997) 6 SCC 200; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1439; JT 1997 (10) SC 749; 1997(4) SCALE 244; 1998 (1) SLJ 1; 1997 (4) SLR 608; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 298; 1997 (4) Supreme 654

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Delegation of Financial Power Rules, 1978, r. 13 - Central Administrative Tribunal (Procedure) Rules, 1987, r. 28 (3) - Fundamental Rules, r. 49 - Addl. remuneration - Entitlement - (A) Whether appellant's claim for Addl. remuneration for holding current/additional of a higher post is valid? - If the Central govt. appointing the appellant in an officiating capacity in the higher post, only he shall be allowed the pay admissible to him, as if he was appointed to officiate in the higher post u/r. 49 (1) - For the purposes of discharging the power and functions of the Registrar, an order of the Chairman or Vice-Chairman, would no doubt be sufficient - But the right to claim the pay is squarely governed by r. 49 of the Fundamental Rules - (B) Whether the Central Govt. has delegated the power u/r. 49 to the Chairman/Vice- Chairman of the Central Administrative Tribunal for appointment? - Power of the President to appoint persons to Group B, C, D posts has been delegated but not the power to appoint to Group A posts - The post of Registrar, Joint Registrar and Deputy Registrar are group A posts - Though under the Procedure Rules, 1987 the Chairman or the Vice-chairman are authorised to delegate the powers and functions of the Registrar to the Deputy Registrar still in the absence of the power of delegation by the Central Govt. u/r. 49 of the F.R., the appellant who as Deputy Registrar exercised the powers and functions of the Registrar, could not be granted the per attributable to the post of the Registrar - Held, the condition mentioned in Appendix 3 to the F.R. wherein the Central Govt. has delegated powers u/r. 49 to the Head of the Department must have been authorised to make appointment to the higher post, is not satisfied in the facts of the case inasmuch as the power to appoint a Registrar has not separately been delegated to the Chairman or Vice-chairman of the Central Administrative Tribunal - Hence, the Addl. remuneration cannot be granted as claimed - Further held, principle of quantum u/s. 70 of the Contract Act is not applicable in respect of claim of additional remuneration where the field is governed by specific statutory rules - Appeal dismissed.
