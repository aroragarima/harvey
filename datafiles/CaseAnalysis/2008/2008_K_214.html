Shail Kumari Devi and Another v Krishan Bhagwan Pathak @ Kishun B. Pathak

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1201; (2008) 9 SCC 632; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 839; AIR 2008 SC 3006; 2008 CRLJ 3881; 2008 (5) G.L.R. 4331; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 125; JT 2008 (8) SC 227; 2008 (3) KLT 576; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 899; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 868; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 842; 2008(10) SCALE 602; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 386; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Majority Act, Indian Majority Act, 1875

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Whether interim maintenance could be awarded in absence of specific and express provision; whether the applicant-wife and her daughter are entitled to maintenance from the date of the order passed by the Family Court or from the date of application made by them under s. 125; what could be the amount of maintenance which could be awarded by the Court - Held, interim maintenance could have been granted by the learned Magistrate even before the amendment of Section 125 in 2001 - High Court is not right in holding that as a normal rule, the Magistrate should grant maintenance only from the date of the order and not from the date of the application for maintenance - It is open to the Magistrate to award maintenance from the date of application and there is nothing which requires recording of 'special reasons' though he must record reasons as envisaged by s. 354(6) of the Code in support of the order passed by him - Family Court could not have granted maintenance exceeding Rs.500/- p.m either to appellant No.1 or appellant No.2 from the date of application - Such an order could have been made effective from the date the Amendment Act, 2001 came into force - Appeal partly allowed.
