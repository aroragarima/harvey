Ashok Kumar Uppal and Others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 199; (1998) 4 SCC 179; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1055; AIR 1998 SC 2812; AIR 1998 SCW 1417; JT 1998 (2) SC 309; 1998 LabIC 1394; 1998 (2) LLJ 1108; 1998 (2) LLN 71; 1998(2) SCALE 232; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 164; 1998 (2) SLR 167; 1998 (3) SLT 626; 1998 (3) Supreme 103

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Constitution of Jammu and Kashmir, s. 127 - Promotion to posts of Senior Scale Stenographers - By relaxing prescribed standard by Govt. - Power of exercise - Held, power of Govt. to relax standard prescribed for promotion - Post of Senior Scale Stenographers - First five candidates, who were topping in merit list of 26 candidates were left out and candidates who were in lower merit and even failed in test were picked up and promoted as Senior Scale Stenographers - This course of action was taken by amending retrospectively existing recruitment rules - Recruitment Rules provide for appointment on basis of merit and suitability to be determined by written test - Relaxation of Rules made to obviate genuine hardships caused to class of employees - Act of Govt. neither arbitrary nor capricious - Therefore when test for promotion to post of Senior Scale Stenographers was held and candidates who were far below in merit list and even failed in test were promoted on basis of their seniority - Subsequent action of Govt. on promoting candidates who were nearest to prescribed standard and topping merit list by relaxing standard prescribed for promotion would be proper when there were vacancies available - Recruitment Rules provide for appointments to cadre of Senior Scale Stenographers on basis of merit and suitability to be determined by written test - This rule was changed retrospectively to provide for appointment to Senior Scale Stenographers on basis of seniority alone - This course of action was taken when panel of candidates approved by Board was available - Power to relax conditions of rules to avoid undue hardship in any case or class - Power to relax Recruitment Rules or any other Rule made by State Govt., u/art. 309 of Constitution of which corresponding provisions is contained in s. 124 of Constitution of J and K, is conferred upon Govt. to meet any emergent situation where injustice might have been caused or is likely to be caused to any individual employee or class of employees or where working of Rule might have become impossible - Under service jurisprudence as also Administrative Law, such a power has necessarily to be conceded to employer particularly State Govt. or Central Govt. who have to deal with hundreds of employees working under them in different departments including Central or State Secretariat - Appeal allowed.
