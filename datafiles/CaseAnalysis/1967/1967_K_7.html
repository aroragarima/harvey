Sawan Ram and Others v Kala Wanti and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 259; AIR 1967 SC 1761; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 687

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss. 5, 8, 9(2), 12, 13 and 14 - Suit for possession of property - Deed of Adoption - Point raised on behalf of appellant is negatived by evidence on record - (A) Whether adoption of a child by a widow would invalidate adoption on grounds that there was no consent of father? - Held, deed clearly mentions that parents gave their child to a widow as her adopted son - Recitation that mother put her thumb mark in token of consent - Mention of consent, held did not show that it was mother who gave child in adoption and not father - (B) Whether on adoption of a son by a widow he becomes adopted son of widow only and not son of her deceased husband? - Further held, adoption will be not only to herself but also to her husband - Adopted son is to be deemed to be member of family of deceased husband of widow - He loses all his rights in family of his birth - Restriction on his rights u/s 12, Proviso (c) and s. 13 - Restrictions on rights of adopted child would not mean that child will not be deemed to be adopted son of deceased husband - Above presumption is not affected - Appeal dismissed.
