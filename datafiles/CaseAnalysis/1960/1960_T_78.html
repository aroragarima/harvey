Standard-Vacuum Refining Company Of India Limited v Its Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 190; AIR 1960 SC 948; 1960 (1) FLR 98; 1960 (2) LLJ 233; 1961 (1) SCJ 84; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 466

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Contract employment - Regularisation - Propriety - Appellant company had system of contract workmen - Respondents demanded for abolition of contract system and absorbing them into regular service with retrospective effect with all benefits and amenities - Dispute was referred for conciliation which was failed - State made reference u/s. 10 of the Act before tribunal which directed appellant to discontinue practice of getting work done through contractors from 1-11-1958 and other demands were rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether tribunal was justified in giving direction for abolition of contract system in the manner in which it had done so - Held, tribunal's decision was right and no interference was called for, except that date; should be changed, for such a direction cannot be put into force with retrospective effect from 1-11-1958 - Hence, order of Tribunal would be carried into effect from such date on which present contract in force in company comes to an end - Appeal dismissed.
