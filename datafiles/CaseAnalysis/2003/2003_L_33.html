Laxminarayan R. Bhattad and others v State of Maharashtra and Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 329; (2003) 5 SCC 413; AIR 2003 SC 3502; 2003 (5) Bom.C.R. 549; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 819; 2003(3) SCALE 711; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 409; 2003 (4) SLT 80; 2003 (3) Supreme 199

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, Development Control Rules, 1967, Bombay Municipal Act, 1888, Bombay Town Planning Act, 1955, Development Control Regulations for Greater Bombay, 1991, Bombay Town Planning Act, 1915

Summary: Miscellaneous - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966 - Development Control Regulations for Greater Bombay, 1991 - Development Control Rules, 1967 - Arbitrator appointed to give effect to the Town Planning Scheme of acquisition of land and determining the rights and interests of the persons holding plots - Award made - On appeal being dismissed by the tribunal the award became final and binding on the parties - Appeal - Whether the appellant can claim the benefit of land potential in lieu of compensation awarded in his favour by the Arbitrator? - Held, once it is held that the 1991 Regulations would not be applicable in the case of the appellants having regard to the sanction of the Scheme prepared by the Arbitrator the question of grant of any benefit thereunder in favour of the appellants herein would not arise - Once a Scheme is approved, no deviation therefrom can be made for purposes other than referred to in the Scheme itself unless the same is amended or modified by the State Govt.in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
