All Saints High School, Hyderabad Etc v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 328; (1980) 2 SCC 478; AIR 1980 SC 1042; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 924; 1980 (1) SLR 716

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Constitutionality, Gujarat University Act, 1949, Kerala University Act, 1957, Kerala University Act, 1969, Kerala Education Bill, 1957

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950 - Gujarat University Act, 1949 - Kerala Education Bill, 1957 - Kerala University Act, 1957 - Kerala University Act, 1969 - Andhra Pradesh Recognized Private Educational Institutions Control Act, 1975 - Minority institution - Violation - Suspension - Regulation - Appellants/minority educational institutions, established by members of Christian community - Appellants contended that several provisions of 1975 Act violate guarantee contained u/art. 30(1) of Constitution by compelling interference with internal administration of private educational institutions established by minorities - Appellants aggrieved by provisions of ss. 3 to 7 of 1975 Act filed W.P. was rejected by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether provisions of 1975 Act violate guarantee contained u/art. 30(1) of Constitution - Held, ss. 3(3)(a) and 3(3)(b) of 1975 Act relate to suspension of teacher, that teacher employed in private institution should not be placed under suspension - Authority extend period of suspension for two months as enquiry could not be completed hence, provisions relate to suspension were regulatory in nature and are intended to safeguard teachers from being suspended for long periods without enquiry into gross misconduct - According to s.3(4) of 1975 Act, teacher placed under suspension should be paid subsistence allowance at rates prescribed during period of his suspension, and it was regulatory in nature and unobjectionable - Hence, ss. 3(3) (a), 3(3) (b), 6 and 7 of 1975 Act were valid while ss. 3(1), 3(2), 4 and 5 of Act were invalid in their application to minority educational institutions - Matters remanded to HC - Order accordingly.
