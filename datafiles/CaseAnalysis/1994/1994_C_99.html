Indian Cable Company Limited, Calcutta v Collector of Central Excise, Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1053; (1994) 6 SCC 610; AIR 1995 SC 64; AIR 1994 SCW 4071; 1994 (55) ECR 20; 1994 (74) E.L.T. 22; JT 1994 (6) SC 243; 1994(4) SCALE 260; 1995 (1) SCJ 241; 1995 (97) STC 307; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 2(d) and 3 - (A) Interpretation of Fiscal statute of item of entry of every day use - (B) Whether marketability a pre-requisite while levying of duty on finding that goods are marketable? - Words and phrases - Whether means 'Marketable' or 'saleable'? - Held, (A) To be construed normally, as understood in common parlance - (B) Is a pre-requisite - Means saleable or suitable for sale - Matter remitted to High Court.
