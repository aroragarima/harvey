Milind Moreshwar Kowley v Manohar Bhaskar Kowley (Dead) by Lrs and others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1407; (2006) 5 SCC 691; AIR 2006 SC 2454; 2006 (64) ALR 484; 2007 (5) AWC 5031; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 203; 2006(6) SCALE 111

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 3B - Partition - Consent decree - Delaying signing of agreement - Clarence certificate - Succeed - In present suit preliminary decree of partition dt. 17-9-1975 was passed by consent to effect and commissioner was appointed to ascertain and determine shares of parties and to partition same by metes and bonds - Ultimately final decree was passed on 28-2-1980 by consent of parties and consent terms were signed by all defendants of suit - plaintiff/appellant instituted suit no.2493 of 1988 on dt. 16-8-1988, claimed that he was minor when consent decree was passed on dt. 28-2-1980, which was not binding on him - Trial Court held, decree, passed on dt. 28-2-1980 was, in fact, confirmed by subsequent decree and only mode of sale was varied by consent order of dt.13-8-1985 - In any event, O. 23 r. 3-B was not applicable to execution proceedings and variation of Order dt. 13-8-1985 was merely, in execution of consent decree dt.28-2-1980 and this aspect of matter, according to trial court, stood concluded by order in review petition filed by plaintiff which was not appealed against and Trial Judge therefore, found no merit in Notice of Motion taken out by plaintiff - Aggrieved, appeal filed before HC, which was also considered material on recorded and came to same conclusion of Trial Judge and rejected submission that consent decree was void as the leave of the Court was not expressly recorded in the proceedings - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of HC and Trial Judge was succeed - Held, held it was not correct to say that leave of Court was not expressly recorded in proceedings and fact that consent terms were tendered in Court and Court proceeded to pass decree in accordance with consent terms was sufficient to hold that leave of Court was expressly recorded - Hence, It was not necessary to use particular words to record leave of Court, and fact that the Court proceeded to pass consent decree was sufficient indication that Court had granted leave - Therefore, Trial court as well as HC have exhaustively dealt with submissions urged before them and they have concurrently found that there was no justification for issuance of an interim order as prayed for in Notice of Motion and they have not taken into account any extraneous matter - Hence, no justification for interfering with orders passed by Lower Courts - Appeals dismissed.
