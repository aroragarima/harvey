Commissioner Of Income-Tax,Bombay City-I, Bombay v Associated Cement Companies Ltd., Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 435; (1988) Supp SCC 378; 1988 (2) CompLJ 157; 1988 (70) CTR 28; [1988] 172 ITR 257; JT 1988 (2) SC 287; 1988(1) SCALE 829; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 917; [1988] 38 TAXMAN 110

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment year - Capital expenditure - Deduction - Liability - Assessee, sum of Rs.2,09,459 was spent during year of account under agreement and amount pertained to laying of pipelines, installations and accessories of Municipality became owner under agreement and amount was claimed as deduction - Income Tax officer disallowed amount and ordered that it was capital expenditure on basis company derived advantage of enduring nature and not to pay municipal taxes for period of fifteen years - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner allowed deduction alleged that amount was payment of composite sum of revenue outgoings for following 15 years - Revenue filed appeal to Income Tax Appellate Tribunal - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal passed order directing Income Tax officer to scrutinize expenditure and allowed deduction of expenditure to extent that it did not result in company becoming owner of any asset - HC concluded that expenditure was revenue expenditure and liable to be allowed as deduction and decided in favour of assessee - Whether expenditure incurred by company in accounting period relevant to assessment period was allowable as deduction in determining profits of company for assessment year - Held, advantage secured by assessee by making expenditure was securing of absolution or immunity from liability to pay municipal rates and taxes under normal conditions for period of fifteen years - If these liabilities had to be paid, on revenue account and advantage secured was in field of revenue and not capital - Expenditure incurred there was no addition to capital assets of assessee company and no change in its capital structure - Pipelines, etc.regarded as capital assets and came into existence as result of expenditure incurred did not belong to assessee company but to municipality - Expenditure clearly liable to be allowed as deductible from profits u/s.10(2) (xv) of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
