Commissioner of Income Tax v Swaran Singh Kanwar

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2453; (1997) 11 SCC 69; AIR 1999 SC 1593; 1998 (150) CTR 156; [1998] 232 ITR 350

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 57(ii), 80 B, 80 K and 256(2) - Assessee made investments in shares of both industrial and non-industrial companies, for which he borrowed funds - Claimed deduction of amount of dividend on shares - But ITO disallowed deduction to extent of Rs. 51,612 out of interest amount of Rs. 55,197 paid by assessee, but he permitted deduction of Rs. 18,516 u/s. 80 K - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, tribunal was right in allowing claim of interest amounting to Rs. 51, 612 paid on borrowed capital by assessee for purpose of shares when dividend income therefrom was exempted u/s. 80 K of Act? - High Court held that in view of s. 57(iii) amount of interest paid on borrowed capital used for earning income had to be allowed as deduction and question sought to be referred was only academic in nature - Held, agree with HC in rejecting application u/s. 256(2), although HC was influenced by Supreme Court's decision which has subsequently been overruled - Before applying s. 80 K it is necessary to determine income in accordance with other provisions of Act, including s. 56 r/w s. 57(iii) of Act, and only thereafter deduction u/s. 80 K has to be made - Further held, interest amount of Rs. 55,197 paid on borrowings for investment in shares had first to be deducted out of dividend income of Rs. 62,160 and balance amount would have been income of dividend - Order accordingly.
