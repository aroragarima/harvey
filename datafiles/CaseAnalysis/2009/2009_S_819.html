Sunil v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1788; (2010) 1 SCC 742; 2009 (10) ADJ 457; AIR 2010 SC 392; 2010 CRLJ 839; 2010 (1) JCC 140; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 65; JT 2009 (14) SC 644; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1027; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 167; 2009(14) SCALE 370; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 203; 2009 (3) UC 1851

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366A and 376 - Kidnapping and raping minor - Conviction - Trial court convicted appellant u/s. 363, 366A and 376 of IPC - HC upheld order of Trial Court - Appellant contended that prosecution has failed to prove that prosecutrix was a minor at the time of the incident as School Leaving Certificate was obtained from school after incident - Hence, present appeal - Whether prosecutrix was a minor? - Held, in absence of primary evidence, reports of Dental Surgeon and Radiologist would have helped in arriving at conclusion regarding age of prosecutrix - Prosecution also failed to produce any Admission Form of school which would have been primary evidence regarding age of prosecutrix - School Leaving Certificate produced by prosecution was also procured six days after incident and three days after arrest of appellant - As per that certificate also, prosecutrix joined school in middle of session and left school in middle of session - Attendance in school of 100 days is also not reliable - In a criminal case, conviction of appellant cannot be based on an approximate date which is not supported by any record - Appellant entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
