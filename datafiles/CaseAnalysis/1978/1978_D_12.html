Nandini Satpathy v Dani (P.L.) and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 318; (1978) SCC (Cr) 236; (1978) 2 SCC 424; AIR 1978 SC 1025; 1978 CRLJ 968; 1978 CrLR(SC) 195; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 160, 161, 160(1), 161(2), 401, art. 266 r/w. art. 136 and s. 401 of Cr PC - Constitution of India, 1950- art. 20(3), 22(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 179, 161/165, 120-B and 109 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5 (2) - Suspect - Criminal Charge - Fundamental Right - self-incrimination - Appellant/accused/former Chief Minister was directed by DSP, Vigilance to appear for examination, in connection with case registered against her u/s. 5(2) r/w. 5 (d)(e) of Act, 1988 and u/s. 161/165 and 120-B and 109 of IPC, - Judicial Magistrate, issued summons for appearance against appellant/accused - Aggrieved by this, appellant/accused moved before HC u/art. 226 of Constitution and u/s. 401 of Cr PC, challenging validity of magisterial proceeding - Appellant unsuccessful before HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether interrogations of appellant by police for charge were sustainable u/art. 20(3) of Constitution - Held, Court regretted, that higher level police officer, ignorantly insisted appellant/woman appearing at police station in flagrant contravention of s. 160(1) of Code, 1973 - SC held further that appellant could not be forced to answer questions, merely because answers were not implicative - When there was reasonable doubt indicated by appellant's explanation, she was entitled to benefit and could not be forced to substantiate ground lest - Court was satisfied that many of questions put by police before appellant were not self- incriminatory, being wholly irrelevant - Appeals allowed.
