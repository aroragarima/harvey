Commnr. Of Central Excise, Meerut-Ii v M/S. Sundstrand Forms P. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 827; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 185

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Sheets, Intermediary Product

Summary: (A) Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A, s. 11A(1) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r.9(2) - Charge duty - Intermediary product - Final or end product - Assessee/respondent firm engaged in manufacture of computer stationery, business forms, etc., [carbonless or with carbon] - Assessee claimed that goods produced by them, namely, computer stationery, business forms and other allied products fall under Schedule to 1985 Act, and, therefore, said articles were chargeable to NIL rate of duty - Team of Central Excise Officers visited factory premises of assessee and examined manufacturing process of carbonless stationery - It was found that assessee-company was purchased carbonless paper in roll form, coated with chemical on backside or front side or on both sides, from market and such carbonless paper was subjected to process of only printing and perforation - Commissioner issued show cause notice - Proceedings were initiated against MD and Deputy MD of assessee/respondent - Respondent/assessee filed appeal - Appellate Tribunal held that impugned product was not classifiable under heading 48.16 as carbonless paper and allowed appeal of assessee - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether intermediary product could be treated as final or end product, under heading 4820.00 of schedule to 1985, Act - Held, respondent /assessee company might be registered for newspapers, etc., but it could not be said that either end product or intermediary product would fall under Chapter 49, heading 49.01- Hence, end product was computer stationery which would specifically fall under Chapter 48, heading 48.20, sub heading 4820.00 - Appeal allowed.

(B) Excise - Marketability prospect - Whether intermediary products had marketability prospect and capability - Held, there was enough evidence available on record to show that not only intermediary products were capable of being bought and sold in market but they were in fact sold and purchased in open market - Thus, findings arrived at by Tribunal by upsetting findings of Commissioner were unjustified and judgment and Order passed by Tribunal was set aside and restore order passed by Commissioner Central Excise - Appeal allowed
