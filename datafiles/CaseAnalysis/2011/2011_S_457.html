Dayaram v Sudhir Batham & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 725; (2012) 1 SCC 333; 2011 (6) AWC 6161; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 684; 2011 (6) CTC 192; 2011 (5) ESC 761; 2012 (1) JabLJ 43; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 930; 2011 (4) MPLJ 374; 2012 (5) RCR(Civil) 820; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1092

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Uchcha Nyayalaya (Khandpeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005

Summary: Service - Socio-Economic - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 136, 142 and 226 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Uchcha Nyayalaya (Khandpeeth Ko Appeal) Adhiniyam, 2005 - Termination - Reinstatement - Jurisdiction - Sustainability - Respondents no. 1 to 3, were terminated from service, by order of committee, cancelling their caste certificates - Respondents no. 1 to 3 filed writ petition before HC - HC quashed order of committee - Aggrieved by HC order, appellant filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed as not maintainable - Hence instant appeals - (A) Whether directions 1 to 15 of Madhuri Patil case, 1994 Indlaw SC 760 (supra), referred were impermissible, being legislative in nature - Held whenever Court found that socio-economic rights of citizens were required to be enforced, but there was a vacuum on account of absence of any law to protect and enforce such rights, Court had invariably stepped in and evolved new mechanisms to protect and enforce such rights, to do complete justice - This had been done by re-fashioning remedies beyond those traditionally available under writ jurisdiction by issuing appropriate directions or guidelines to protect fundamental rights and make them meaningful - (B) Whether directions 11 and 12 of Madhuri Patil case (supra) violates s. 9 of CPC - Held , having regard to scheme for verification formulated in Madhuri Patil,(supra) scrutiny committees carry out verification of caste certificates issued without prior enquiry, as for example caste certificates issued by Tehsildars or other officers of departments of Revenue/Social Welfare/Tribal Welfare, without any enquiry or on basis of self- affidavits about caste - If there was to be a legislation governing or regulating grant of caste certificates and if certificates were issued after due and proper inquiry, such certificates would not call for verification by scrutiny committees - Madhuri patil case provides for verification only to avoid false and bogus claims which was functioning from last one and half decades - In case of any short comings, Govt. could always come up with appropriate legislation to substitute scheme - In instant case we find no reason as to why procedure laid down in above mentioned case was not applicable in absence of any legislation - (C) Whether direction 13 of Madhuri Patil,(supra) barring appeal, when such appeals was specifically provided for in State enactments/Letters Patents, was valid and proper - Held, right to file writ appeal under the Act, was a vested right which could be taken away only by express amendment to the Act or by repeal of that Act, or by necessary intendment, where clear inference could be drawn from some legislation that legislature intended to take away said right - Right to appeal to DB was made available to party to a writ petition - Power u/art. 142 was not intended to be exercised, when such exercise would directly conflict with express provisions of statute - Therefore, a remedy by way of appeal provided expressly by statute could not be taken away by executive fiat of a judicial order - Hence second sentence of direction No.(13)was not legally proper and stands overruled - In view of above, appeal no.3467/2005 was allowed and judgment of DB was set aside - Appeal no. 3467/2005 was dismissed as infructuous - Appeals disposed of.
