Sudama Devi v Commissioner, Gorakhpur, and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 294; (1983) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1983 SC 653; 1983 (1) ALJ 731; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 273; [1983] 142 ITR 824; 1983(1) SCALE 100A; 1983 (1) SLR 777; 1983 UJ 364

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Period of limitation for filing writ petition - Held, no period of limitation prescribed by any law for filing a writ petition u/art. 226 - No such period of limitation can be laid down either under rules made by High Court or by practice - In every case it would have to be decided on facts and circumstances of whether petitioner is guily of laches and that would be done without taking into account any specific period as a period of limitation - Appeal allowed.
