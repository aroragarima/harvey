United Riceland Limited v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 17; (2011) 2 SCC 423; JT 2011 (1) SC 332; 2011(1) SCALE 180; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 186; [2011] 37 VST 599

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Levy Of Purchase Tax, Haryana General Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Ordinance, 1990, Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, ss. 6 and 15-A - Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act 9 of 1993 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5 - A/y 1990-91 - Constitutional validity of Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act 9 of 1993 substituting s. 15-A of 1973 Act retrospectively - Appellant was engaged in the business of purchase and dehusking of paddy to produce rice, in the State of Haryana - Rice so produced was exported outside the country within the meaning of s. 5 of the 1956 Act - The turnover of the paddy purchased by the dealer during the relevant year was subjected to purchase tax u/s. 6 and 15-A of the 1973 Act vide assessment order - Aggrieved by the said levy, the dealer preferred a writ challenging the substitution of s. 15-A in the 1973 Act vide Act 9 of 1993, with retrospective effect - HC dismissed said writ petition relying on the Full Bench judgment in United Riceland Limited & Anr. vs. State of Haryana & Ors. 1995 INDLAW PNH 198, wherein it held that the s. 15-A of 1973 Act is constitutional valid - Hence, the present appeal - Whether Haryana General Sales Tax (Amendment) Act 9 of 1993 substituting s. 15-A of 1973 Act retrospectively is constitutional valid? - Held, SC in Satnam Overseas (Export) & Ors. vs. State of Haryana & Anr. 2002 INDLAW SC 1361, held that in the specified circumstances in which charge of purchase tax on the raw material is imposed, clause (b) of sub-section (1) of s. 9 of the 1973 Act and the exemptions provided therein would apply - SC further held that while s. 9 of 1973 Act remained on the statute-book till 1-4-1991, retrospective amendments of s. 2(p), 6, 15 and 15- A of the 1973 Act would make no difference in regard to levy of purchase tax on paddy and adjustment of purchase tax paid on paddy (raw material) is permissible u/s. 15-A of the 1973 Act during the relevant period - SC further held that by virtue of s. 15-A of the 1973 Act, denial of refund of purchase tax, if any, paid by a dealer is not illegal much less unconstitutional - Section 9 of 1973 Act ceased to exist in the statute book from the date of promulgation of the ordinance i.e. 15.10.1990 - Therefore, the benefit of the exemption contained in s. 9(1)(b) of the 1973 Act was available to the dealer only upto 15.10.1990; and not till 1.04.1991 as elucidated in Satnam Overseas (Exports) (supra) - Hence, dealer will not be liable to pay purchase tax on the purchase of paddy made by them upto 15.10.1990 - Appeal partly allowed.
