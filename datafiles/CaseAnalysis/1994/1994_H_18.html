State Of W.B. v Hari Narayan Bhowal

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1401; (1994) 4 SCC 78; (1994) SCC (L&S) 869; AIR 1994 SCW 2578; 1994 (27) ATC 524; 1994 (2) BLJR 1069; 1994 (1) CLR 985; JT 1994 (2) SC 610; 1995 (2) LLJ 328; 1994 (2) LLN 366; 1994(2) SCALE 263; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 24; 1995 (1) SLJ 19; 1994 (2) SLR 54; 1994 (1) UJ 750; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1060

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - West Bengal National Volunteer Force Act, 1949 - Whether a volunteer force is entitled to same pay scale as that of police? - It need not be impressed that principle of 'equal pay for equal work' can be enforced only after persons claiming satisfy court that not only nature of work is identical but in all other respects they belong to same class and there is no apparent reason to treat equals as unequals - Held, merely because academic qualifications and physical requirements of both are similar or that Agragamies are also given certain fire-fighting training along with other training, it cannot be said that they perform similar duties, functions and responsibilities as Firemen - Appeal allowed.
