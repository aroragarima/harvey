Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, J. Chelameswar, Madan B. Lokur

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Legality, Multiplier method, Future prospects

Summary: (A)Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 110B, 163A, 163B, 165, 166, 168 - Application For Compensation - Multiplier method - Future prospects - Legality - Hence instant reference - Whether multiplier specified in Second Schedule appended to the Act should be scrupulously applied in all cases -

Held, standard method for selection of multiplier was surely better than a criss-cross of varying methods - It was high time that we move to a standard method of selection of multiplier, income for future prospects and deduction for personal and living expenses - Courts in some of overseas jurisdictions have made that advance - In Sarla Verma v Delhi Transport Corporation and Another, 2009 Indlaw SC 488, it was held for selection of multiplier in claim applications made u/s. 166 of the Act in cases of death -As regards cases where age of victim happens to be up to 15 years, in such cases irrespective of ss. 163A or 166 of the Act under which claim for compensation was made, multiplier of 15 and assessment as indicated in Second Schedule subject to correction as pointed out in Column (6) of table in Sarla Verma v Delhi Transport Corporation and Another, 2009 Indlaw SC 488, should be followed on - Reference answered.

(B) Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 110B, 163A, 163B, 165, 166, 168 - Application For Compensation - Multiplier method - Future prospects - Legality - Whether for determination of multiplicand, the Act provides for any criterion, particularly as regards determination of future prospect -

Held, SC approved method that an addition of 50% of actual salary be made to actual salary income of deceased towards future prospects where deceased had a permanent job and was below 40 years and addition should be only 30% if age of deceased was 40 to 50 years and no addition should be made where age of deceased was more than 50 years - Where annual income was in taxable range, actual salary should mean actual salary less tax - In cases where deceased was self-employed or was on a fixed salary without provision for annual increments, actual income at the time of death without any addition to income for future prospects would be appropriate - Hence, departure from above principle should only be justified in extraordinary circumstances and very exceptional cases - Reference answered.
