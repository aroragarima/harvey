Sharad Subramanyan v Soumi Mazumdar and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 568; (2006) 8 SCC 91; AIR 2006 SC 1993; 2006 (64) ALR 169; 2007 (1) AWC 360; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 342; JT 2006 (11) SC 535; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 47; 2007 (1) MPLJ 223; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 447; 2006 (101) RD 252; 2006(5) SCALE 197

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14(2) - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Succession - Female Hindu - Whether under will of late husband suit property was bequeathed to R for her life? - Held, s. 14 of Act was enacted by Parliament in order to ensure that limited estate devolving upon a female Hindu be abolished, female Hindu who possessed property, acquired before or after coming into force of Act, should hold it as full owner thereof, not as a limited owner but in view of fact that there were no indications, either in will or externally, to indicate that property had been given to female Hindu in recognition of or in lieu of her right to maintenance, it was held that situation fell within ambit of sub-s. (2) of s. 4 of Act, that restricted life estate granted to female Hindu could not be enlarged into an absolute estate - Not only was there no material to indicate to High Court that property was given to R in lieu of her right of maintenance, but such an argument was not even advanced before court - R had only a limited right, namely, life interest in suit property and could not have created a long-term lease as she has purportedly done - Appeal dismissed.
