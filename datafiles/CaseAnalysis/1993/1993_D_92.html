J and K Public Service Commission v Narinder Mohan Dr. and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1798; (1994) 2 SCC 630; (1994) SCC (L&S) 723; AIR 1994 SC 1808; AIR 1994 SCW 1701; 1994 (27) ATC 56; 1994 (1) CLR 1; 1994 (68) FLR 363; JT 1993 (6) SC 593; 1994 (1) LLJ 780; 1993 (2) LLN 938; 1993(4) SCALE 597; 1994 (1) SLJ 218; 1994 (1) SLR 246

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 16, 30, 141, 162, 142 and 309 Proviso - Jammu and Kashmir Medical Education (Gazetted) Services Recruitment Rules, 1979, rr. 9, 5, sch III - Ad hoc appointment - (A) Existence of Statutory rules for - (B) Regularisation of services of lecturers by placing their records before PSC for recommendation - If proper mode contemplated by Rules - (C) Yearly need for recruitment by notification of vacancies and inviting applications - Held, same should be consistent with arts. 14 and 16 and existence of statutory rules is not necessary which, if made, can be supplemented and not supplanted in exercise of executive power - Open advertisement inviting applications from eligible candidates necessary - Need not be done every year by adopting chain system - Appeal allowed.
