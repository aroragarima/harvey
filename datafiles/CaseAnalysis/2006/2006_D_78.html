Standard Chartered Bank v Directorate of Enforcement

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 252; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 221; (2006) 4 SCC 278; AIR 2006 SC 1301; 2006 (2) BC 391; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 621; 2006 (127) DLT 747; 2006 (197) E.L.T. 18; 2006 (1) JCC 488; JT 2006 (3) SC 421; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 85; 2006 (4) SCJ 160; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 709

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Fema

Summary: FEMA - Constitution - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 50, 51, 61 and 68 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 31 B - Companies Act, 1956 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 267-C - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Constitutional validity of ss. 50, 51, 56 and 68 of FERA - Whether FERA is violative of arts. 14 and 21? - Admitted that FERA has been included in Ninth Schedule to constitution, as Item No. 100 - Therefore, in terms of art. 31 B none of provisions of FERA can be deemed to be void on ground that FERA or any of provisions thereof, are inconsistent with or take away or abridge any of rights conferred by Part III of Constitution - Rights conferred by arts. 14 and 21 are rights flowing from Part III of Constitution and, therefore, it is clear that no challenge based on violation of arts. 14 or 21, even if it has substance, can enable appellants to get relevant provisions of Act struck down - No merit can also be found in argument that s. 68 of FERA confers an unfettered power on investigating officer to pick and choose at his will, those whom he desires to prosecute and omit those whom he does not want to prosecute - There is no occasion to whittel down ambit of provisions to save them from charge of arbitrariness hit by art. 14 - Proviso to s. 61(2) of Act provides that no complaint regarding offences referred to in that Section shall be made unless an opportunity is given to concerned person to show that he had requisite permission where offence charged is an act which requires permission under Act - S. 68(1) says that if commission of an offence by company is proved, person who was in charge and was responsible to company for conduct of business of company at time contravention was committed, was to be deemed to be guilty of contravention - A person who is instrumental in commission of an act by company that is in contravention of FERA or rules or directions issued thereunder, also lays himself open to prosecution - No reason to restrict scope of any of provisions of Act, especially in context of presence of 'without prejudice' clause in s. 56 dealing with offences and prosecutions - FERA subserves a twin purpose - One, to ensure that no economic loss is caused by alleged contravention by imposition of an appropriate penalty after an adjudication u/s. 51 - Two, to ensure that tendency to violate is curbed by imposing an appropriate punishment after a due prosecution in terms of s. 56 - Cannot be accepted that an adjudication has to precede a prosecution - Two proceedings are seen to be independent - Nothing in FERA to indicate that a finding in adjudication, is binding on court in a prosecution u/s. 56 - S. 50 providing for imposition of penalty and s. 56 providing for prosecution, speak of contravention of provisions of Act - Contravention makes a person liable both for penalty and for prosecution - Even though heading to s. 56 refers to offences and prosecutions, what is made punishable by Section is contravention of provisions of Act and prosecution is without prejudice to any award of penalty - S. 50 does not refer to every person as envisaged by s. 68 - S. 68 cannot be availed of to indict officials of company for purposes of penalty - S. 50 also lays down an outer limit of penalty - Nothing in FERA which, confines expression 'punished' only to a punishment for a criminal prosecution - An imposition of a penalty can also be a punishment - Held, no reason to interfere with interim order passed by High Court - All appeals except Civil Appeal Nos. 1751 and 1944 of 1999 dismissed - Civil Appeal Nos. 1751 and 1944 of 1999 are allowed.
