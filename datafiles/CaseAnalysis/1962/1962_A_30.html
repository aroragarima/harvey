Â J.Pandurangarao v Andhra Pradesh Public Servicecommission

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1963 SC 268; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 234 and 309 - Rules framed by Governor of Andhra Pradesh r. 12(h) - (A) Whether r. 12(h) framed by Governor of Andhra Pradesh u/art. 234 and Proviso to art. 309 of Constitution for appointment of District Munsiff is constitutional or not? - Held, unconstitutional - Expression 'the HighCourt' in context meant Andhra High Court and not any HC in India - Introduced a irrational classification between one class of Advocates and rest - No nexus between basis of said classification and object intended to be achieved - Based on geographical considerations or it may have reference to objects or occupations or like - Presence of one invalid rule cannot invalidate other rules which may be valid, so presence of number of valid rules will not help to validate an impugned rule if it is otherwise invalid - Petition allowed.
