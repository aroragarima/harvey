Haji Mohammed Ishaq Md. Sk. Mohammed & 3 Others v Mohamed Iqbal & Mohamed Ali & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 168; (1978) 2 SCC 493; AIR 1978 SC 798; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 571; 1978 UJ 474

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Banking & Finance - Dishonor of cheque - Recovery - Legality - Plaintiff/respondent was a registered partnership firm carrying on business - Defendants also carried on partnership business manufactured Bidi in their factory - Plaintiff dispatched by railway 630 bags of tobacco - Defendants accepted goods and gave cheques out of which, few were honored but rest of cheques were dishonored - Balance of price which remained due from defendants came to Rs. 90,000/- - Plaintiff filed a suit before Trial Court for recovery of Rs. 90,000/- - Trial Court decreed suit on its finding that goods were sold to defendants by plaintiff through 'R' who acted as defendants' agent - Aggrieved by said order defendants filed appeal before HC - HC disposed of appeal by setting aside judgment of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal by plaintiff - Whether orders placed with 'R' were executed by plaintiff or defendants - Held, it was found that the cheques drawn by defendants in name of 'R' were all endorsed by him in favor of plaintiff and ultimately to knowledge of defendants payment of part of price was by defendants to plaintiff - No goods were supplied on account of 'R' - No part payment was really and actually made to him and defendants were liable to pay balance of price to plaintiff - There was no difficulty in assuming or even inferring that initially express contract for supply of goods was between defendants and 'R' - Fact whether 'R' acted as plaintiff's agent or defendants' was immaterial - It was clear that orders placed with 'R' were in fact executed by plaintiff by supply of goods to defendants - It was so done on account of plaintiff from its own warehouse as well as from 'M' warehouse - Defendants by their clear conduct of accepting goods and never repudiating any of numerous letters and telegrams of plaintiff demanding money from them on assertion that goods were dispatched by plaintiff and defendants should pay money, clearly showed that direct contract which in law was called an implied contract by conduct was brought about between them - 'R' and defendants and in whatever manner be acted as a go-between-man, between plaintiff and defendants, it was clear that eventually and finally supply of goods by plaintiff was to defendants on its own account and not on account of 'R' - Defendants clearly and unerringly accepted goods as such and became liable to pay whole of price directly to plaintiff - A part was paid and liability to pay balance was definitely incurred by them - Appeal dismissed.
