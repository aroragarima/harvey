Â Madras Bar Association v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, H.L. Dattu, Arun Mishra, R.F. Nariman, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 358; (2015) 8 SCC 583; [2015] 190 Comp Cas 484; 2015 (3) CompLJ 1; JT 2015 (5) SC 33; 2015 (4) MLJ 184; 2015(6) SCALE 331; 2016(6) SCALE 331; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 638

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Keywords: Cost And Works Accountants Act, 1959, Chartered Accountants Act, 1949, Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002, NCLAT (Salaries, Allowances and other terms and conditions of service of the Chairperson and other Members) Rules, 2014, NCLT (Salary, Allowances and other Terms and Conditions of Service of President and other Members) Rules, 2013

Summary: (A) Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 10FD(3)(f)(g)(h), 10FE, 10FF, 10FL(2), 10FR(3), 10FT - Companies Act, 2013, ss. 408, 410 - Constitution of Tribunal - Constitutional validity - Determination - Proceedings took place before HC challenging constitution of creation of National Company Law Tribunal (NCLT) and National Company Law Appellate Tribunal (NCLAT) and certain other provisions pertaining thereto which were incorporated by Legislature in Parts 1B and 1C of 1956 Act - Constitutional Bench vide judgment held that creation of NCLT and vesting powers hitherto exercised by HC and Company Law Board (CLB) in said Tribunal was not unconstitutional - However, HC pointed out certain defects in various provisions of Part 1B and Part 1C of 196 Act and, in particular, in ss. 10FD(3)(f)(g)(h), 10FE, 10FF, 10FL(2), 10FR(3), 10FT of 1956 Act and held that provision sin questions offended basic Constitutional scheme of separation of powers, it was held that unless these provisions are appropriately amended by removing defects which were also specifically spelled out, it would be unconstitutional to constitute NCLT and NCLAT to exercise jurisdiction which is being exercised by HC or CLB - Further, Companies Act, 2013 had similar provisions incorporated it which is challenged in present petition - Whether creation of NCT and NCLAT is constitutional valid.

Held, in prayer clause, though challenge is laid to vires of s. 408 of 2013 Act, it conspicuously omits s. 410 of Act, hence, in essence, there is no challenge to Constitution of NCLAT insofar as relief claimed is concerned. Further, entire writ petition takes umbrage under constitution Bench judgment in 2010 judgment. SC held that Court specifically went into gamut of all those arguments raised and emphatically repelled same. Court specifically rejected contention that transferring judicial function, traditionally performed by Courts, to Tribunals offended basic structure of constitution. Further, question of validity of NCLAT was directly and squarely in issue. Further, Court specifically affirmed decision of HC which held that creation of NCLT and NCLAT was not unconstitutional. Hence, it is not open to petitioner even to argue this issue as it clearly operate as res judicata. SC held that judgment in case of 2010 judgment is of Constitution Bench and that judgment of co-ordinate Bench binds this Bench as well. Further, NTT was matter where power of judicial review hitherto exercised by HC in deciding pure substantial question of law was sought to be taken away to be vested in NTT which was held to be impermissible. In instant case, there is no such situation. On contrary, NCLT is first forum in hierarchy of quasi-judicial forum set up in Act, 2013. NCLT, thus, would not only deal with question of law in given case coming before it but would be called upon to thrash out factual disputes/aspects as well. Hence, NCLAT which is first appellate forum provided under Act, 2013 to examine validity of orders passed by NCLT, will have to revisit factual as well as legal issues. Hence, situation is not akin to NTT. Further, scope of appeal to SC is restricted only 'to question of law arising out of such order'. Further, it is common feature to provide one appellate forum wherever enactment is complete Code for providing judicial remedies. Providing one right to appeal before appellate forum is well accepted norm which is perceived as healthy tradition. Hence, there is no merit in present issue. Petitions disposed of.

(B) Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, 2013, ss. 409, 411(3) - Qualification of members - Legality of section - Determination - Petitioner challenged to prescription of qualifications including term of their office and salary allowances etc. of President and Members of NCLT and Chairman and Members of NCLAT.

Held, in 2010 judgment constitution Bench took view that since NCLT would now be undertaking work which is being performed, inter alia, by High Court, technical Members of NCLT/NCLAT should be selected from amongst only those officers who hold rank of Secretaries or Additional Secretaries and have technical expertise. Further, taking into consideration clear and categorical dicta in 2010 judgment, tinkering therewith would evidently have potential of compromising with standards which 2010 judgment sought to achieve. Hence, SC held that s. 409(3)(a) and (c) are invalid as these provisions suffer from same vice. Likewise, s. 411(3) of Act as worded, providing for qualifications of technical Members, is also held to be invalid. For appointment of technical Members to NCLT, directions contained in judgment of HC in 2010 will have to be scrupulously followed and these corrections are required to be made in s. 409(3) to set right defects contained therein. Petitions disposed of.

(C) Corporate - Constitution - Companies Act, 2013, ss. 412 - Selection committee - Validity - Determination - Whether constitution of Selection Committee for selecting Members of NCLT and NCLAT is in accordance with law.

Held, structure of Selection Committee was found fault with by constitution Bench in 2010 judgment. Court specifically remarked that instead of 5 members Selection Committee, it should be 4 members Selection Committee and even composition of such Selection Committee was mandated in direction of judgment. However, SC held that there is deviation in composition of Selection Committee that is prescribed u/s. 412 (2) of Act. SC held that prime consideration in mind of Bench was that it is Chairperson, viz. Chief Justice of India, or his nominee who is to be given final say in matter of selection with right to have casting vote. That is ratio of judgment and reasons for providing such composition are not far to seek. In face of all pervading prescript available on this very issue in form of binding precedent, there is no scope for any relaxation as sought to be achieved through impugned provision and SC found it to be incompatible with mandatory dicta of 2010 judgment. Hence, SC held that provisions of s. 412(2) of Act, are not valid and direction is issued to remove defect by bringing this provision in accord with 2010 judgment. Petitions disposed of.
