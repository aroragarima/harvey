Â Patnaik and Company v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 480; AIR 1965 SC 1655; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 14; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 782; 1965 (16) STC 364

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Written Contracts, Breach Of Warranty

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract for Sale of Goods - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Indian Contract Act - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, the Tribunal is right in holding that the amounts received by the opposite party on the construction of bodies on chassis supplied by its customers under written contracts are not chargeable to the Orissa Sales Tax? - If, on a review of all the terms of the contract, it appears that the intention of the parties was that the appellants were to sell "bus bodies" to the State of Orissa the contract would clearly be one for sale and consideration paid would be taxable under the Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - If the contract is one for securing a certain result namely the building of a body on the chassis supplied by the State with the materials belonging to the appellants, the contract would be one for work done and not liable to sales tax - The present contract is one for work and not a contract for sale, because the contract is not that the parties agreed that the "bus body" constructed by the appellants shall be sold to the State of Orissa - The contract is one in which the appellants agreed to contract "bus bodies" on the chassis supplied to them as bailees and such a contract being one for work, the consideration paid is not taxable under the Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Held, the appeal should be allowed.
