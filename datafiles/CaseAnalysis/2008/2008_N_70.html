Kamla Chaturvedi v National Insurance Co. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1774; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 198; (2009) 1 SCC 487; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 550; 2009 (1) ACC 60; 2009 (1) AWC 509; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 106; JT 2008 (13) SC 40; 2008 (4) KLT 862; 2009 (1) LLJ 542; 2009 (2) MPLJ 542; 2009 (153) PLR 354; 2008(15) SCALE 51; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Liability Of The Insured, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Amending Act, Additional Amount

Summary: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Held, a contract of Insurance is governed by the provisions of the Insurance Act, 1938, unless the said contract is governed by the provisions of a statute - Accident in question arose on account of vehicular accident and provisions of MV Act are clearly applicable - No such exception as was the case in New India Assurance Co.'s case was stipulated in the policy of insurance - Insurance Company is liable to pay the interest - Appeal partly allowed.
