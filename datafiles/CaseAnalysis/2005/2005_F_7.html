Amit Kumar Shaw & Anr v Farida Khatoon & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 279; (2005) 11 SCC 403; AIR 2005 SC 2209; 2005 (4) ALD(SC) 98; 2005 (59) ALR 584; 2005 (2) AWC 1348; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 690; JT 2005 (5) SC 20; 2005 (2) KLT 806; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 786; 2005 (3) MahLJ 330; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 36; 2005 (141) PLR 201; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 651; 2005 (99) RD 193; 2005(4) SCALE 108; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrong Plaintiff

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10, O. 22 r. 10 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Indian Limitation Act, 1877 - Suit for declaration of title - Application for substitution of their names as respondents filed by appellants - Whether on a combined reading of O 1 r.10, O 22 r.10 of CPC and s.52 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882, an application for substitution by a subsequent transferee can be rejected and he be non-suited altogether? - Held, presence of appellants are absolutely necessary in order to decide appeals on merits since appellants are only persons who has got subsisting right, title and interest in suit - Appeals allowed.
