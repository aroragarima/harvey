Subal Ghorai & Ors. v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 196; (2013) 4 SCC 607; 2013 CRLJ 3626; JT 2013 (4) SC 527; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 898; 2013(5) SCALE 275

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 141, 142, 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 324, 325, 436 - Unlawful Assembly - Discrepancies - Attack - FIR - Sustainability - Appellants/accused were armed with weapons allegedly attacked deceased - Trial Court convicted appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 148, 324, 302, 436 r/w s. 149 IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - Appellants filed appeal before HC - During pendency of appeal, A3 and A24 died - Hence, HC recorded that appeal related to them as abated but confirmed conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court on accused and acquitted A25, A27, A30 to A34 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was sustainable - Held, witnesses have stated that all accused were carrying weapons - Further, evidence discloses that several persons pounced on deceased and attacked them mercilessly with weapons - Thus, in instant case those to whom, overt act was not attributed or those who might not have used weapons would also be liable to be convicted for murder and other offences with aid of s. 149 IPC because they were members of unlawful assembly at all crucial stages and shared common object of assembly at all stages - Prosecution had successfully proved its case against all accused except A26, A28, A29, A35 and A36 - However, absence of names of those accused in FIR, a doubt was created as to whether they could be really involved in offence but that did not however, make evidence of PW-1 and other witnesses unreliable - Hence, conviction with aid of s. 149 of IPC was perfectly justified, but so far as A26, A28, A29, A35 and A36 were concerned, evidence on record gives rise to suspicion about their involvement - Absence of their names in FIR, lead SC to give them benefit of doubt - Appeal disposed of.
