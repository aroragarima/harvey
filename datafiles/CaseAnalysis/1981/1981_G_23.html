Dr. C. Girijambal v Govt. Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 274; (1981) 2 SCC 155; (1981) SCC (L&S) 293; AIR 1981 SC 1537; 1981 (43) FLR 185; 1981 LabIC 979; 1981 (1) LLJ 314; 1981 (2) LLN 13; 1981(1) SCALE 281; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 782; 1981 (2) SLJ 206; 1981 (1) SLR 364; 1981 UJ 224

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 - Revision - Denied higher pay scale - Entitled to - Appellant got selected to post of Medical Officer and after several representation appellant was denied scale of pay which was mentioned in advertisement and based on judgement of HC in writ filed by appellant was given higher pay scale - Later on there was revision and appellant made representation to Govt. but not received any reply - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before Tribunal which was rejected by holding that appellant was not entitled to higher scale as she did not possess requisite qualification - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, rendering professional services at any rate the principle of equal pay for equal work would be inapplicable and in present case Medical Officers holding the qualification of G.C.I.M., or the qualification of L.I.M. or the qualification of D.A.M., though in charge of dispensaries run by Zilla Parishads, cannot, therefore, be treated on par with each other and if the State Govt. or the Zilla Parishads prescribe different scales of pay for each category of Medical Officers no fault could be found with such prescription. Thus, when the revision was undertaken the appellant was put in the pay scale of Rs. 200-400 which was also the pay scale prescribed for Medical Officers holding L.I.M. but that was because under the earlier order of the HC the appellant had been fixed initially in the pay scale of Rs. 180-320-the pay scale also meant for Medical Officers holding L.I.M. and when that pay scale of Rs. 180-320 was revised to Rs. 200-400 she was required to be given that revised scale. Therefore, when the second revision was undertaken it was perfectly open to the State Govt. to split the revision into two categories, one meant for Medical Officers holding L.I.M. and the other for Medical Officers other than L.I.M. as has been done in present case and since the appellant was not a holder of L.I.M. but fell in the other category she was, in our view, properly fixed in the lower revised pay scale of Rs. 430-800. Appeal dismissed.
