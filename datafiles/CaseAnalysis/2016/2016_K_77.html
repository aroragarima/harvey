Kunapareddy @ Nookala Shanka Balaji v Kunapareddy Swarna Kumari and another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 503; AIR 2016 SC 2519; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3143; 2016 (117) ALR 268; 2016 CRLJ 2921; 2016 (4) CTC 314; 2016 (3) JCC 1649; JT 2016 (5) SC 365; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2497; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 315; 2016 (3) RLW 2138; 2016(5) SCALE 703; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maintenance, Curable, Jurisdiction, Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, Aggrieved Person, Prayer, Infirmity, Dealing, Violence, Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 12, 125, 19, 20, 21, 22, 23, 28(1), 31, 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 23(2), 24 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 12, 18, 19, 20, 21, 23(2), 26, 28, 28(2), 37(2)(C), 9B - Domestic violence petition - Amendment - Legality - Respondent no. 1/wife of appellant filed a complaint against appellant and his family members alleging appellant inflicted mental and physical cruelty and harassed respondent no. 1 with beatings for dowry - Respondent no.1 has also filed a divorce petition before Trial Court - Respondent no. 1 filed another application seeking amendment of petition before Trial Court which was allowed - On appeal, Appellate Court allowed appeal holding that there was no specific provision for amendment of complaint - Aggrieved, respondent no. 1 filed a revision petition before HC - HC allowed revision and restored order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a Court dealing with petition/complaint filed under the 2005 Act has power to allow amendment to petition/complaint originally filed.

Held, Magistrate allowed amendment application mainly on ground that no cognizance was taken of complaint before disposal of amendment application. Therefore to avoid multiplicity of proceedings Trial Court allowed the amendment application. Considering these factors which weighed in the mind of the Courts below, HC rightly declined to interfere with the order passed by Trial Court allowing the amendment application and impugned order does not suffer from any serious infirmity warranting interference in exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 136 of Constitution. It is not that there is a complete ban/bar of amendment in complaints in criminal Courts which are governed by CrPC, though such power to allow amendment has to be exercised sparingly and with caution under limited circumstances. One of the circumstances where such an amendment is to be allowed is to avoid multiplicity of the proceedings. The contention of appellant that there is no power of amendment has to be negated. Thus, amendment was rightly allowed by Trial Court and there is no blemish in impugned judgment of HC affirming order of Trial Court. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - There is no complete ban/bar of amendment in complaints in criminal Courts though such power to allow the amendment has to be exercised sparingly and with caution under limited circumstances.
