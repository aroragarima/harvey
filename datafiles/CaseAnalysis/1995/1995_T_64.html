Tata Press Limited v Mahanagar Telephone-Nigam Limited & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.B. Majmudar, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 124; (1995) 5 SCC 139; AIR 1995 SC 2438; 1995 (3) CTJ 677; JT 1995 (5) SC 647; 1995(4) SCALE 595; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 467; 1996 (1) UJ 125

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Drugs, Subscriber, Telephone

Summary: Telecom - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(a) - Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 7 - Telegraph Rules, 1951, rr. 457 and 458 - (A) Whether freedom of speech and expression 'commercial speech' - (B) Telegraph Rules - Legality of restraining private company from publishing 'Tata Press Yellow paper' comprising paid advertisements from businessman, traders, and professionals - Held, it is part of freedom of speech and expression - Not permissible, but permission for publication of list of telephone subscribers r. 458 is mandatory - Appeal allowed.
