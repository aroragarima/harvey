Â Sardar Kapur Singh v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1960 SC 493; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 116; 1960 SCJ 487; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Whether a sufficient opportunity of defence was given to appellant? - By Constitution, an opportunity of showing cause against action proposed to be taken against a public servant is guaranteed and that opportunity must be a reasonable opportunity - Whether opportunity afforded to a public servant in a particular case is reasonable must depend upon circumstances of that case - Held, enquiry in this case was held by Enquiry Commissioner who occupied high office of Chief Justice of East Punjab High Court - Appellant himself examined 82 witnesses and produced a large body of documentary evidence and submitted an argumentative defence - An opportunity of making an oral representation not being a necessary postulate of an opportunity of showing cause within meaning of art. 311 of Constitution - Plea that appellant was deprived of constitutional protection of that article because he was not given an oral hearing by President cannot be sustained - Appeal dismissed.
