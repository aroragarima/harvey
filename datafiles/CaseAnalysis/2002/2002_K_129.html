Haryana State Electricity Board v Krishna Devi

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1209; (2002) 10 SCC 246; (2003) SCC (L&S) 248; 2002 (2) AWC 1411; 2002 (2) CLR 14; 2002 (3) ESC 4; 2002 (93) FLR 606; JT 2002 (3) SC 485; 2002 (2) LLJ 773; 2002 (2) LLN 888; 2002 (3) SLR 323; 2002 (4) SLT 500; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1283

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Dying in harness - Application before appellant to give employment to her son - Application of respondent was rejected by board - Appellant was directed to give employment to son - Held, it is well-settled that employment on compassionate ground is given only on pure humanitarian consideration and no appointment can be claimed as a matter of right - Employment under compassionate ground cannot be made in absence of rules or instructions issued by Govt. or any public authority - No rule for such an employment - Application for employment was not to meet immediate financial need of family - High Court order set aside - Appeal allowed.
