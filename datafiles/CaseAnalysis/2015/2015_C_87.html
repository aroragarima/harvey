Oswal Chemicals & Fertilizers Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Bolpur

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 427; 2015(5) SCALE 193; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 487

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Claim of refund

Summary: Excise - Central Excise, Act, 1944, s. 11B - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 192, 233B - Claim of refund - Locus standi - Legality - Appellant (assessee/company) purchased Naptha from Corporation (manufacturer) and paid excise duty thereon for a specific period, during that time appellant did not have the CT-2 certificate and L6 licence issued by Dept. u/r. 192 of the Rules - However, appellant after obtaining the said certificate and licence and filed application u/s. 11B of the Act, claiming refund of said paid duty, which was rejected by Asst. Commissioner and appeal against which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before Appellate Tribunal, which was dismissed - However, appellant filed instant appeal.

Held, the appellant who had paid the excise duty to the manufacturer (Corporation) (in the instant case, had the necessary locus standi to file the application claiming the refund of the duty. Appellant had filed the refund claim before the Central Excise Authorities at 'D'. The appellant had purchased the material from Corporation which is having its refinery at 'D'. The show cause notice was also issued by the Superintendent of Central Excise at 'D'. It appears that the Appellate Tribunal is influenced by the reason that the depot is located at 'H' and on that ground, it has come to the conclusion that the authorities at 'D' had no jurisdiction. Said reason given by the Appellate Tribunal is factually incorrect. The purchases were from depot at 'R' under the Corporation refinery at 'D' and therefore, the Central Excise authorities at 'D' had the requisite jurisdiction over Corporation Depot located at 'R', as it comes under 'D' Commissionerate. Reasons given by the Appellate Tribunal in dismissing the appeal of the appellant are not correct. The appellant is relying upon the second proviso to s. 11B of the Act which stipulates that the limitation of 6 months would not apply where any duty has been paid under the protest. Even if instant appeal is treated as a form of protest that was much beyond 6 months period from the date of purchase that is 25-9-1996 to 16-10-1996. Therefore, the so-called protest would not come to the aid of the appellant. Application for refund was time barred and on this ground alone, the appellant will not be entitled to refund of the amount. Appeal dismissed.
