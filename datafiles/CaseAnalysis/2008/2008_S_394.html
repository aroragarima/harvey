Commissioner of Customs, New Delhi v Sony India Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1717; (2008) 13 SCC 145; 2008 (231) E.L.T. 385; JT 2008 (10) SC 666; 2008(12) SCALE 706; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Hsn Explanatory Notes, Synthetic, Interpretative Rules

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 28(1), 111(d), 111(m), 112(a) and 114(a) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - General Rules for Interpretation, rr. 1 and 2(a) - Whether Tribunal had erred in holding that components imported by appellant could not be treated as complete CTV Sets and duty demand, direction for confiscation and imposition of penalty were unsustainable in law? - A mere PCB or a CRT, under any circumstances, cannot be held to have essential character of CTV - There is no finding recorded anywhere on facts that all these goods could make 1500 CTVs - R. 2(a) would not be applicable to present case since there is no question of goods having essential character of CTVs - Held, Tribunal's judgment needs no interference - Appeal dismissed.
