N. Adithayan v Travancore Devaswom Board and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1150; (2002) 8 SCC 106; AIR 2002 SC 3538; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 28; JT 2002 (8) SC 51; 2002 (3) KLT 615; 2002(7) SCALE 280; 2002 (3) SCJ 606; 2002 (6) SLT 5; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 76; 2002 (7) Supreme 242

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Keywords: Social Reform

Summary: Constitution of India -- Articles 25 and 26 -Held the appointment of a person, who is not a Malaya Brahmin, as "Santhikaran" or Poojari (Priest) of a Hindu Temple is not violative of the constitutional and statutory rights of the appellant. Further held, there is no justification to insist that a Brahman or Malayala Brahman in this case, alone can perform the rites and rituals in the Temple, as part of the rights and freedom guaranteed under Article 25 of the Constitution and further claim that any deviation would tantamount to violation of any such guarantee under the Constitution.
