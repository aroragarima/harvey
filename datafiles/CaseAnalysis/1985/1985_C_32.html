Shree Sajjan Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, M. P. and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 325; (1985) 4 SCC 590; (1986) SCC (Tax) 82; AIR 1986 SC 484; 1985 (49) CTR 193; 1986 (9) ECR 276; [1985] 156 ITR 585; 1985(2) SCALE 737; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 593; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 593; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 37; 1986 (2) TLR 48

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Provision For Gratuity, Approved Gratuity Fund

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 37(1) and. 40A(7) - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - A/y 1973-74 - Deduction - The assessee was a Public Limited Company - With the coming into force of the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 a statutory liability was created on the assessee to pay gratuity to its employees and the assessee arranged for actuarial determination of its liability - The assessee company sought to deduct a sum of Rs. 20 lakhs towards the amount of gratuity payable to its employees and worked out actuarially on the ground that the provisions of s. 40A (7) of the Act was not applicable - Said claim for deduction was set up by the Income Tax Officer (ITO) on the ground that there was non-compliance with the requirements of s. 40A(7) of the Act and allowed deduction only to the extent of actual payment which came to Rs. 24,366 towards payment of gratuity to the employees during the relevant a/y - On appeal, Appellate Asst. Commissioner held that the provisions of s. 40A(7) of the Act did not constitute any bar to the assessee claim for deduction u/s. 37 of the Act as the assessee had not made any provision in its books in respect of the amount of gratuity determined actuarially and the provision of Rs. 20 lakhs had also been added back in the statement of income - Tribunal held that the sum of Rs. 20 Lakhs could not be allowed as deduction but the balance of Rs, 28,59,431/- for which no provision was made in the books was allowable u/s. 37(1) of the Act - HC held that the tribunal was not justified in allowing the deduction of Rs. 28,59,431/- u/s. 37 of the Act on the ground that in view of the non-obstante clause in s. 40A of the Act, no deduction was permissible u/s. 37 of the Act - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether provision made towards liability for gratuity can be allowed as deduction u/s. 40A(7) of the Act? - Held, on a plain construction of clause (a) of sub-section (7) of s. 40A of the Act, what it means was that whatever was provided for future use by the assessee out of the gross profits of the year of account for payment of gratuity to employees on their retirement or on the termination of their services would not be allowed as deduction in the computation of profits and gains of the year of account - The embargo was on deductions of amounts provided for future use in the year of account for meeting the ultimate liability to payment of gratuity - Hence, order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
