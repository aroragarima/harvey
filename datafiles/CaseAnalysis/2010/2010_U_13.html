Coal India Limited and another v Ujjal Transport Agency and others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 879; (2011) 1 SCC 117; 2011 (84) ALR 766; 2011 (2) AWC 1488; JT 2010 (11) SC 321; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 851; 2011 (2) MPLJ 474; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 80; 2010(11) SCALE 162; 2010 (4) WLN(SC) 32

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 14 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11 and 34 - Condonation of delay - Dispute arose between respondent and appellants - Respondent filed an application u/s. 11 of 1996 Act and an arbitrator was appointed - Arbitrator made an Award dt. 6.2.2009 - Appellant filed appeal against said order u/s. 34 of 1996 Act before HC - On receiving notice in the execution, appellants realized that the proceedings u/s. 34 of 1996 Act was not by way of an appeal before the HC but by way of an original application before District Court - Appellants filed an application u/s. 34 of 1996 Act before District Court for setting aside the said award dt. 6.2.2009 alongwith an application u/s. 5 of 1963 Act for condonation of the delay in filing the application u/s. 34 of 1996 Act - Subsequently, appellants took back the application for condonation filed under a wrong provision of law i.e. s. 5 of 1963 Act and filed an application u/s. 34(3) of the 1996 Act on 3.11.2009 - At the hearing, Court was of the view that application should have been filed u/s. 34(3) of 1996 Act r/w. s. 14 of 1963 Act - Thereafter, appellant filed yet another application u/s. 34(3) of 1996 Act r/w. s. 14 of 1963 Act for condonation of delay - District Court dismissed said application for condonation of delay - HC also dismissed appeal filed against said order - Hence present appeal - Whether the appellants were bona fide and diligently pursuing the remedy before a wrong forum and their application for condonation of delay could be allowed? - Held, filing of an application for condonation under a wrong provision of law will not vitiate the application - In fact though the application for condonation of delay was initially filed u/s. 5 of 1963 Act, that was subsequently replaced by an application u/s. 34(3) of the 1996 Act, and again by an application u/s. 34(3) of the 1996 Act r/w. s.14 of the 1963 Act - Moreover, appellants demonstrated their diligence and bona fides by filing the application u/s. 34 of the 1996 Act without waiting for a formal order of withdrawal of the 'appeal' u/s. 34 before the wrong forum - Therefore, it cannot be said that filing of the application u/s. 34 of the 1996 Act on 19.10.2009 was belated - Further if the period spent before wrong forum is excluded, the application is filed within three months and there is no question of explaining any delay - Hence, application u/s. 34 of the Act was filed in time, by excluding the time spent before the wrong forum - Impugned judgment of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
