Sujir Keshav Nayak v Sujir Ganesh Nayak

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 419; (1992) 1 SCC 731; AIR 1992 SC 1526; 1992 (1) HLR 114; JT 1992 (1) SC 134; 1992 (1) KLT 283; 1991(2) SCALE 1359; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 409; 1992 (1) UJ 240

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Court Fee Act, 1870, s. 7(iv)(c) - Kerala Court Fees and Suit Valuation Act, 1959, s. 36 - Whether if court fee liable to be paid u/s. 36 of Kerala Court Fees and Suits valuation Act, 1959 in a suit for dissolution of partnership and accounting is to be computed on value of plaintiff's share in a partnership as estimated by him or as found by court? - Held, in a suit for declaration with consequential relief falling u/s. 7(iv)(c) of Court Fees Act, 1870 plaintiff is free to make his own estimation of reliefs sought in plaint and such valuation both for purposes of court fee and jurisdiction has to be ordinarily accepted - It is only in cases where it appears to court on a consideration of facts and circumstances of case that valuation is arbitrary, unreasonable and plaint has been demonstratively undervalued, court can examine valuation and can revise same - Suit was filed in 1983 but defendant by raising objection and court entertaining it has succeeded in delaying suit for nine years only on preliminary issue - That is why court have construed provision in s. 36 of Kerala Court Fees and Suit Valuation Act, 1959 in a manner so as to avoid such recurrence - Appeal allowed.
