Divisional Personnel Officer, Southern Railway and Another v T.R. Chellappan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 386; (1976) 3 SCC 190; (1976) SCC (L&S) 398; AIR 1975 SC 2216; 1976 (32) FLR 96; 1975 LabIC 1598; 1976 (1) LLJ 68; 1976 (1) LLN 269; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 783; 1976 SLJ 8; 1975 (2) SLR 587

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968

Summary: Service - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968, r. 14(1) - Probation Of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 3, 4, 9 and 12 - Service Rules - (1) Interpretation of r. 14(1) of 1968 Rules - (2) Scope and ambit of concluding part of r. 14(1) of 1968 Rules - Employee convicted of criminal charge released on probation but penalty of removal from service imposed by Disciplinary authority without applying mind to facts and circumstances - Combined effect of ss. 3, 4 and 9 if 1958 Act - Order of Magistrate releasing convicted accused on probation - Stigma - Automatic disqualification - Held, words 'where any penalty is imposed' should be interpreted as penalty impossable by Disciplinary authority under Rules and not one imposed by Criminal Court on conviction - Penalty cannot be upheld - Further held, does not obliterate stigma of conviction and does not contemplate automatic disqualification of a person released on probation - Appeals dismissed.
