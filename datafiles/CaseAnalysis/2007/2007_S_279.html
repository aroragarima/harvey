Pradeep Kumar @ Pradeep Kumar Verma v State Of Bihar And Another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 817; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 407; (2007) 7 SCC 413; AIR 2007 SC 3059; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2602; 2007 CRLJ 4333; 2007 (4) JCC 2633; JT 2007 (10) SC 246; 2007 (4) KLT 41; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 51; 2007(10) SCALE 97; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 376, 406 - Application for discharge dismissed - Appeal against - Held, a promise to marry without anything more will not give rise to misconception of fact within the meaning of s. 90, it needs to be clarified that a representation deliberately made by the accused with a view to elicit the assent of the victim without having the intention or inclination to marry her, will vitiate the consent - There was a specific finding in that case that initially the accused's intention to marry cannot be ruled out - On a bare reading of the FIR, it shows that no offence had been made out for proceeding situation would be different - It would have been proper for the High Court to deal with the matter elaborately - Remitted the matter to High Court for fresh consideration - Appeal disposed of.
