Municipality of Taloda v Charity Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1968 SC 418; 1968 (70) BomLR 332; 1968 MahLJ 435; 1968 MPLJ 318; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 652

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay District Municipal Act, 1901

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Public Trusts Act, ss. 1, 2(13), 9, 19, 29 and 72(4) - Trust - Determination - Whether there was a public trust and City Survey Nos. 371 to 379 of Taloda Municipality were property of Trust and Municipality was entitled as trustee of that trust? - Held, it was open to Asst. Charity Commr. to determine whether a public trust existed, and if Asst. Charity Commr. was satisfied that there existed a public trust, whatever may be claim made by applicants, Asst. Charity Commr. was bound to declare existence of public trust and register it - A person interested, as Charity Commr. is in due administration of property, cannot be denied a right to appeal against an adverse decision in a proceeding to which he is a party - Appeal dismissed.
