State Of Uttaranchal v Alok Sharma & Ors

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 484; (2009) 7 SCC 647; 2009 (2) CompLJ 353; 2009 (2) ESC 348; 2009 (121) FLR 816; 2009(6) SCALE 277; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1329

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Irregular Appointment, Age-Limit, Uttar Pradesh Absorption of Retrenched Employees of Government of Public Corporations in Government Services Rules, 1991

Summary: Service - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Absorption of Retrenched Employees of Government of Public Corporations in Government Services Rules, 1991 - Retrenched employees - Interpretation and/ or application of various circular letters issued by the State of Uttar Pradesh which have been adopted by the State of Uttarakhand - Held, High Court failed to take into consideration that the circular letters dated 30.12.1995 and 26.02.1996 being not notified orders as envisaged in the Rules would not be law within the meaning of art. 13 of the Constitution of India - High Court did not find that the cut-off date to be arbitrary or discriminatory and was, thus, liable to be struck down being ultra vires art. 14 - Appeals allowed.
