Tulsi Ram v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 228; (1984) 4 SCC 487; (1985) SCC (Cr) 4; AIR 1985 SC 299; 1985 (22) AllCriC 28; 1985 (1) Crimes 313; 1984 CRLJ 1731; 1984 CrLR(SC) 454; 1984(2) SCALE 578; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 949

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Central Food Laboratory, Article Of Food, Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules 1955

Summary: Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955 - Food Inspector, Sorar, purchased 375 grams of Soyabean oil - Purchased Soyabean oil was divided into three parts one of which sent to Public Analyst for analysis - Found that sample was adulterated, containing traces of cotton seed oil - Complaint filed on basis of report of Public Analyst - Convicted under s. 16(1)(a)(i) of Prevention Food Adulteration Act - Urged Public Analyst's report not sent 'immediately' to vendor as required under r. 9-A of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955 - Held, expression 'immediately' in r. 9-A conveys sense of continuity rather than urgency - Non-compliance with r. 9-A not fatal and is only a question of prejudice - Vendor is liable to conviction irrespective of whether mixture has injurious effect or not - Sale of an article of food, which is prohibited by any rule made under Act also renders person selling article of food liable to punishment u/ s. 16(1)(a)(i) of Prevention of Food Adulteration Act.
