Ramkubai Smt. Deceased By Lrs. and Others v Hajarimal Dhokalchand Chandak and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 628; (1999) 6 SCC 540; AIR 1999 SC 3089; 1999 (37) ALR 837; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 628; JT 1999 (5) SC 630; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - The Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947,ss.12 & 13(1)(e)&(g) - Tenancy - Bonafide requirement - Challenged - Validity - Appellants/land lords filed eviction suit on respondents/tenants on grounds of sub tenancy and default in rent payment and for bonafide requirement and was opposed by tenants/respondents - Trial Court decreed suit and passed eviction decree holding that all grounds were established by land lord - Aggrieved tenants/respondents filed appeal and was allowed by appellate Court and set aside decree of Trial Court - Aggrieved landlords filed appeal in HC and was dismissed by HC confirming Appellate Courts order - Hence, instant appeal by landlords - Whether HC was justified in dismissing appeal - Held, as per s.13(1)(g) of the 1947 Act landlord was entitled to recover possession of any premises if he could satisfy Court inter alia that premises were bonafidely required by him for occupation by himself or by any person for whose benefit premises were held - Trial Court accepted bonafide requirement whereas negatived by appellate court and HC - Reasoning given by appellate court and confirmed by HC were not justified and appellate court erred in arriving at those reasoning - Appellant may be partner with her husband's brother but that would not debar landlord from setting up his own business - Appellate Courts another reasoning that there was no bonafide requirement as landlord's son joined under contractor was also found fault as he could not wait idle till disposal of litigation - Appellate Courts reasoning that landlord did not enter into witness box and hence bonafide requirement could not be considered was also found fault as her son who was GPA holder gave entered witness box and gave evidence - None of reasons raised by appellate court leads to inference that landlord had no intention to start family Kirana business and so relief could not be denied to landlady to recover suit premises for personal requirement to establish Kirana business independently - Appellants had established bona fide requirement to recover possession of suit premises from respondents - Judgments of appellate Court and HC were set aside - Restores Trial Courts judgment- Appeal allowed.
