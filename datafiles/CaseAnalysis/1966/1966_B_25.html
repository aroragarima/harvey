Biswabham Das v Gopen Chandra Hazarika & Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 177; AIR 1967 SC 895; 1967 CRLJ 828; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Assam Forest Regulation, 1891 - Appellant was lessee of a shop from 1956 to 1962 - Shop was settled with him again for term 1962-64 by Deputy Commissioner - On appeal to Board of Revenue, this was set aside, on basis of a report submitted by Inspector of Excise and shop was settled with present respondent - Appellant filed a WP and succeeded on ground that evidence was taken behind his back and as such should not be taken into consideration - The Board went into question as to whether Hazarika was a suitable person because as holder of a firewood mahal licence he had compounded an offence of illegally felling green trees by paying Rs. 50 - Board was not satisfied with this explanation and took view that fact of Hazarika having compounded offence did not clear his conduct although he had succeeded in getting a sub-sequent settlement of a forest mahal - Whether HC was right in quashing order of Board of Revenue on ground that very basis on which appellate order of Board was founded did not exist and that Board had gone wrong in taking into consideration compounding of an offence under Forest Regulation by petitioner before HC as affecting his suitability in getting settlement of an excise shop in Dibrugarh area? - Held, in this case Board of Revenue had not gone wrong in law in taking into consideration Hazarika's conduct in rendering compensation for a forest offence - Board was quite competent to take view that Hazarika was not vigilant in observing law even if it had found - When it did not that, Hazarika's explanation was not unconvincing - Board cannot be said to have exceeded its jurisdiction under law or committed an error apparent on face of record - It follows that HC was not justified in quashing appellate order of Board under art. 226 of Constitution - Order of HC is set aside and that of Board of Revenue is restored respondent will pay - Appeal allowed.
