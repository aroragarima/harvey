Gurbinder Kaur Brar and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 459; AIR 2013 SC 3028; 2013 (5) AWC 4807; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2743; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 963; 2013(9) SCALE 529

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Chandigarh (Delegation of Powers) Act, 1987

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5A, 6(1) - Notification - Land acquisition - Declaration - Legality - Respondents/UOI issued notification issued u/s. 4(1) of the Act for acquisition of land for various purposes - Appellants/landowners filed objections u/s. 5A(1) of the Act - Land Acquisition officer (LAO) submitted a report that land notified could be acquired - Declaration was issued u/s. 6(1) of the Act - Appellants filed writ petitions challenging acquisition proceedings - DB dismissed writ petitions - Hence, instant appeals - Whether reports submitted by LAO u/s. 5A(2) were vitiated due to non- consideration of objections filed by landowners - Held, report of LAO was vitiated due to total non-application of mind by concerned officer to large number of substantive objections raised by appellants u/s. 5A(1) of the Act - However, LAO mechanically rejected objections and senior officers of Administration accepted report despite fact that same was prepared in violation of s. 5A(2) of the Act - Hence, respondents could not issue a declaration u/s. 6(1) of the Act after rectifying illegalities committed in preparation of report u/s. 5A(2) of the Act and issue of earlier declaration - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
