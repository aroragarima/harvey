R. Balakrishna Pillai v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 201; (2003) 9 SCC 700; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1876; AIR 2004 SC 1012; 2003 (1) CCR 428; 2003 (2) Crimes 214; JT 2003 (3) SC 120; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 198; 2003 (2) KLT 135; 2003(2) SCALE 560; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 436; 2003 (2) SLT 554; 2003 (2) Supreme 720

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Electricity & Energy; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - S. 5(1)(d) r/w 5(2) - charge and conviction under - appeal - whether the appellants A1 and A2 have illegally sold electricity to M/s. GIL by official position which amounted to 'causing to obtain' valuable thing to M/s. GIL resulting in pecuniary advantage as well to M/s. GIL - Held, facts leading to charges are not proved and the element of mens rea and intention is totally lacking. The electrical energy was exported to Karnataka/KEB at the request of State of Karnataka during the period of crisis of shortage of energy which is not objected to, so as to be illegal but for a part of it which is allocated by the State of Karnataka/KEB to M/s. GIL which constitutes no offence. The prosecution failed to prove the case of sale of electricity by KSEB to M/s. GIL or the KSEB or A1 and A2 having caused profit to M/s. GIL. Admittedly, appellants did not stand to gain in any manner. The prosecution case thus fails.
