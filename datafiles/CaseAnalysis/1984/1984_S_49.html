Smt. Kaushalya Devi Bogra And Others Etc. v The Land Acquisition Officer, Aurangabad And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 280; (1984) 2 SCC 324; AIR 1984 SC 892; 1984(1) SCALE 304; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 900; 1984 UJ 429

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23, 24 and 54 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133, 136 - Land acquisition - Acquisition for Medical College and Hospital in Aurangabad, a tourist place and also exposed to Industrial development - (A) Question regarding fixation of compensation considering the potential value for land - (B) Practice and Procedure - Supreme Court remanding appeal against decision in land acquisition appeals - Earlier High Court decision becoming non-existent - Effect - Held, compensation to be fixed at the rate of Rs. 1.50 per square yard or Rs. 7,260 per acre - Held, HC should dispose it of by independent judgment - Appeal allowed.
