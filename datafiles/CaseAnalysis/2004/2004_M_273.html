Mir Mohammad Khasim v Union Of India And Ors

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1882; (2004) 10 SCC 721; AIR 2004 SC 3256; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 20; JT 2004 (5) SC 38; 2004(4) SCALE 91; 2004 (3) SLR 259

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: A.P. State and Subordinate Services Rules, Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966, rr. 6(a), 7(e) - Deemed confirmation - Entitlement - Appellant Deputy Superintendent of Police (DSP) Category 3 was promoted to DSP Category 2 and was placed him on probation - Appellant's probation was terminated subsequently and reverted to DSP Category 3 - Appellant challenged the reversion before HC - HC declared that appellant had satisfactorily completed the probation in view of the order passed by respondent authority on 6-10-1989 wherein respondent had relaxed the requirement of three years of service in Category 2 for further promotion - Respondent, however, excluded appellant's name while preparing seniority list for the selection to Indian Police Service (IPS) - Appellant filed application before the Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed application - Appellant contended that he had successfully completed the confirmation and he had been granted relaxation for the requirement of three years service, hence his confirmation should be considered as deemed confirmation - Held, appellant was appointed as DSP Category 2 on 1-3-1982 and the declaration of satisfactory completion of period of his probation was with effect from 27-1- 1987 i.e., for a period of about 5 years - Declaration of satisfactory completion of period of probation u/r. 7(e) was without any strings and noticeably the said declaration of satisfactory completion of period of probation was in the cadre of DSP Category 2 - In absence of the order dated 6-10-1989 granting relaxation to the appellant in respect of rr. 6(a) and 7(e), the appellant would not have been in a position to claim the benefit of deemed confirmation - However, once that relaxation had been granted and appellant was taken to have cleared the eligibility tests in time and it was declared that he would have successfully completed the period of probation no other formality had to be undergone, thus he would inevitably be deemed to be confirmed - In such circumstances, appellant was unreasonably put out of consideration for selection to the cadre of the IPS - Order passed by the Tribunal therefore was set aside and the respondents were directed to consider the case of the appellant for selection to the IPS and in case he was selected he should be entitled to notional promotions and financial benefits - Appeal allowed.
