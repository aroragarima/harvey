Manubhai, Nandlal Amersey v Popatial Manilal Joshi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 267; (1969) 1 SCC 372; AIR 1969 SC 734; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 217

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Bombay Animal Preservation Act, 1954

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation Of People Act, 1951, ss. 83(1), 86(5), 116 A, 116 C and 123(2) provision (a)(ii) - (A) Whether appellant was guilty of corrupt practice? - (1) Whether Trial Judge should have allowed Respondent's amendment? - Normally an application for amendment u/s. 86(5) should be made within a reasonable time before trial commences - Court has power to allow an amendment even after commencement of trial, but leave to amend at a later stage should be given as an exception where petitioner could not with reasonable diligence have discovered new facts earlier - Leave to be denied, if applicant is not acting in good faith or has kept back facts known to him before trial started - Held, respondent no. 1 had sufficient opportunity to bring forth charges - Order allowing amendment has resulted in manifest injustice to appellant - Respondent No. 1 moved application for amendment in bad faith at a very late stage of trial - Order of trial judge allowing amendment was erroneous - Considering speeches - Corrupt practice is committed, if speech is calculated to interfere with free exercise of electoral right and to leave no choice to electors - Having regard to character of audience, speech was calculated to interfere with free exercise of electoral right - Offending passages fell within s. 123(2) proviso (a)(ii) - Appeal dismissed.
