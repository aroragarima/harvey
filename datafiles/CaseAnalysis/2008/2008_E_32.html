Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited v Esi Corporation

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2587; (2008) 3 SCC 247; AIR 2008 SC 1449; 2008 (2) CLR 309; JT 2008 (2) SC 407; 2008 (2) LLJ 302; 2008 (2) LLN 58; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 795; 2008(2) SCALE 454; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 900; 2008 (2) SLR 377; 2008 (1) UC 469

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Service

Keywords: Employeesâ States Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950

Summary: (A) Insurance - Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 45A - Employees Provident Fund Act, 1952 - Quantum of amount - Proceedings under both the Act - Held, purpose of the proceedings, both under the Act as also the Employees Provident Fund Act, is to determine the amount due from any employer in respect of the employees under the statutory schemes - Quantum of amount due has to be determined in respect of all contract workers engaged by the contractors - Principal employer would be entitled to recover the contributions from the contractor; they being the immediate employers - Whereas under the Provident Fund Act, the principal employer is statutorily liable in terms of the provisions of the Act to comply with the provisions therein; in terms of the Act, the principal employer is entitled to recover the amount of contribution payable by the immediate employer for them - Appeal allowed.

(B) Insurance - Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 45A - Employees' State Insurance contribution - Appellant a Public Sector Undertaking used to engage contractors for various purposes - Appellant received notice u/s. 45A of the Act on the ground that they had not deposited the Employees' State Insurance contribution - Appellant request for impleading contractors who were involved during the period in question as parties in said proceedings was rejected - Writ Petition was filed thereagainst wherein a decision in Madras Gymkhana, Madras v. Employees' State Insurance Corporation, Madras 1990 INDLAW MAD 105 was relied upon - DB of HC overruled the said decision - Hence, present appeal - Held, s. 45A of the Act enables the appropriate authority to recover such dues both from the principal as also the immediate employer. It provides for an opportunity of hearing to both of them - determination of the exact liability on the part of the contractors is necessary keeping in view the fact that they or some of them may not be under the control of the principal employer having regard to the fact that the contract has come to an end - It will bear repetition to state that the principal employers have a statutory right to recover the dues from the contractors/immediate employers - In the present case, determining authority did not give an opportunity of hearing to the petitioner in regard to the names and other particulars of the contractors - Matter remitted to the ESI Corporation/determination authority for considering the matter afresh - Authority shall either implead the contractors as parties and/or summon them for producing necessary records for the said purpose - Appeal allowed.
