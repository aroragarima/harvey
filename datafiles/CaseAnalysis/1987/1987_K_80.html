Jagannathan Pillai v Kunjithapadam Pillai And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28246; (1987) 2 SCC 572; AIR 1987 SC 1493; 1987 (2) HLR 246; JT 1987 (2) SC 235; 1987 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1987(1) SCALE 861; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1070; 1987 (2) UJ 690

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land and Property - Family and Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s.14(1) - Scope of s.14(1) - Transfer of the property by Hindu widow having limited estate before commencement of the Act - And retransfer of the very same property to her after commencement of the Act - What is the nature of right vested in her by the retransfer - Supreme Court held that she becomes absolute owner of that property - Appeal dismissed.

(Note: A controversial issue with different High Court having different opinions on it - As per the Judge made law, a Hindu widow who has inherited property in Orissa or Andhra Pradesh would be a 'limited owner' and whereas if she has inherited property in Madras, Punjab, Bombay or Gujarat she would become an 'absolute owner' )
