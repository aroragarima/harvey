Ambika Sharan Singh v Mahant Mahadeva and Giri and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 564; (1969) 3 SCC 492; 1969 UJ 215

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Corrupt Practices - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 97 and 100(1) - General election in February, 1967, appellant was Minister of State in Finance Department of State of Bihar - Appellant was declared elected - Election petition filed by Respondent 1, he made a number of allegations of diverse corrupt practices giving particulars thereof in more than 10 schedule - High Court after examining voluminous evidence led by parties held that appellant was guilty of three corrupt practices, namely, of distributing money, of having canvassed on basis of his caste, and of having obtained or procured assistance of four gazetted officers, being in Department of Commercial Taxes which was under charge of appellant - Whether HC conclusion justified? - Evidence shows that campaign on basis of caste was carried on at numerous places, at some places by appellant himself and at some places by others in his presence and at others by several workers of appellant including his election agent - It is impossible to think that appellant was not aware of it and yet there was not even a whisper in his evidence or in that of any of his witnesses that he either disowned it or endeavoured to prevent it- Held, HC was right in its conclusion that appellant was proved to have committed corrupt practice - Trial Judge was right in declaring election void - Appeal dismissed.
