Union of India and another v Venkateshan S. and another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 257; (2002) 5 SCC 285; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1121; AIR 2002 SC 1890; 2002 (2) CCR 214; 2002 (2) Crimes 271; 2002 CRLJ 2790; 2002 (81) ECC 1; 2002 (102) ECR 522; 2002 (2) JCC 876; JT 2002 (4) SC 263; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 602; 2002 (50) RLT 3; 2002(4) SCALE 1; 2002 (3) SCJ 479; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 268; 2002 (3) SLT 588; 2002 (3) Supreme 421

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Detention Order, Any Person

Summary: A person who violates the provisions of the Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999 to a large extent can be detained under Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 since Section 3 of the COFEPOSA Act, which is not amended or repealed, empowers the authority to exercise its power of detention with a view to preventing any person inter alia from acting in any manner prejudicial to the conservation or augmentation of foreign exchange.
