State of Rajasthan v Sarjeet Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 713; (2006) 8 SCC 508; 2006 (6) AWC 5428; JT 2006 (9) SC 302; 2006(10) SCALE 417; 2006 (6) SLR 834; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 614

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 11A, 2(oo)(bb), 25G, 25H - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Appointment - Services terminated as Scheme came to an end - Finding that mandatory requirements of ss.25-G, 25-H were not complied with and consequently an award of reinstatement with continuity of service was passed - Held, Respondent No 1 was appointed under a Scheme - He was appointed for a specific purpose - Fact that his initial appointment was for a period of six months is not disputed - Concept of there being 'dual employer' although may not be unknown in industrial jurisprudence but Labour Court, misdirected itself in holding that termination of his services by Appellant was illegal - Appeal allowed.
