P. Punnaiah v Jeypore Sugar Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1560; (1994) 4 SCC 341; AIR 1994 SC 2258; [1994] 81 Comp Cas 1; JT 1994 (3) SC 508; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 385

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 and 399(3) - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 88 - Whether consent given by first appellant's daughter's, to first appellant as her GPA holder (General Power of Attorney), for and on her behalf and not by her personally, is a valid consent for filing application u/s. 397/398, within meaning of s. 399(3)? - S. 399(3) does not either expressly or by implication indicate that consent to be accorded thereunder should be given by member personally - First appellant could have filed, or joined as an applicant in an application u/s. 397/398 in name of and for and on behalf of Smt Rajeshwari as her GPA holder - No question of 'consent' would have and could have arisen in such a case - Accordingly first appellant could give consent on behalf of Smt Rajeshwari, member, u/s. 399(3) as well - Held, consent given by first appellant for and on behalf of Smt V Rajeshwari, as her GPA holder, is a valid consent within meaning of s. 399(3) therefore, preliminary objection to maintainability of application filed u/s. 397/398 is unsustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
