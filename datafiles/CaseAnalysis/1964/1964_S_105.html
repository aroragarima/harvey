Â A. Hajee Abdul Shukoor and Company v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar, J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 56; AIR 1964 SC 1729; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 39; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 217; 1964 (15) STC 719

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Discriminatory Legislation

Summary: Indirect Taxes - Constitution - Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax (Special Provisions) Act, 1963, s. 2(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 245, 246, 286(3), 301, 302, 303 and 304 - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 3(3) - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax (Turnover, Assessment) rr. 16(1) and 16(2) - Essential Goods (Declaration and Regulation of Tax on Sale or Purchase) Act, 1952, s. 3 - (A) Whether s. 2(1)of T N General Sales Tax (Special Provisions) Act, 1963 is valid? - Held, it is not valid - Provision cannot be changed in respect of sales during subsequent period - Discriminates against imported hides and skins which were sold up to 18-1957 on which tax on sale of raw hides and skins was at rate of 19/16th percent - This however does not mean that sub-section is valid with respect to sales which took place subsequent to 01-08-1957 - In view of invalidity of s. 2(1), other provisions of section also become unenforceable - (B) Whether Madras Act is affected by provisions of arts. 301 to 304 of Constitution? - Further held, yes, it is - Act makes provision for a period subsequent to commencement of Constitution and therefore is to be subject to provisions of Constitution - (C) Whether invalidity of r. 16(2) of T N General Sales Tax Act, 1939 and s. 3(3) of T N General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules affects r. 16(1) - Further held, it does not - Raw hides and skins and dressed hides and skins constitute different commodities of merchandise and they can, therefore, be treated as different goods for purposes of taxation under Act - As statute treats both these kinds of hides and skins as different commodities provision of r. 16(1) providing for levy of tax on raw hides and skins at a certain point, even in absence of any provision for taxation of dressed hides and skins, cannot be said to be discriminatory and invalid - Articles to be taxed were not same and legislature could provide different taxation - R. 16(1) did not become invalid on Supreme Court declaring r. 16(2), as invalid - (D) Whether amendments in pre-constitutional act requires President's assent - Validity - Further held, it does not require so - Did not affect legislative competency of state to enact provision relating to pre-Constitution Act - Section not invalid for want of President's assent - When Madras Act, 1963 was enacted by Madras Legislature, art. 286(3) had been amended and Central Act 52 of 1952 had also been repealed - Consequently there was no constitutional requirement for Act being reserved for assent of President before it could be enforced - State legislature is free to enact laws which would have retrospective operation - Its competence to make a law for a certain past period, depends on its present legislative power and not on what it possessed at period of time when its enactment is to have operation - 1939 Act required no assent of President - State Legislature was doing in 1963 what legislature enacting 1939 Act was supposed to have enacted and therefore its enactment was not governed by constitutional requirement for an Act to be enacted during period - Petitions allowed.
