Western U.P. Electric Power and Supplycompany v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 251; (1969) 1 SCC 817; AIR 1970 SC 21; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 865

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14 , 31(2) and 226 - Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 3(2)(e), 3(1), 3(2)(e)(ii) - (A) Grant of licence to a company to supply electrical energy for certain area - Whether debarrs State Govt. from granting licence within that area - (B) Scope of art.14 of constitution - Plea of denial of equal treatment - Onus on person setting up such plea - (C) Administrative order - Supply of energy to consumers within area of licensee - Exercise of power u/s, 3(2)(e) of 1910 Act (Amended by U.P. Act of 1961) - Satisfaction of Govt. that supply is necessary in public interest - Judicial review, if allowed - (D) Grant of Licence under Act to a company for certain area - Right of State to authorise supply of electrical energy to consumer through State Electricity Board in same area - Held, on that account State Govt. is not debarred from granting licence within that area to another person or to supply electrical energy to consumer if necessary in public interest - Onus on person setting up such plea - Satisfaction is in appropriate cases not excluded from judicial review - There is no infringement of art. 31(2) - Appeal Dismissed.
