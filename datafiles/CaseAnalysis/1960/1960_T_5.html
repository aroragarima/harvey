Income Tax Officer, Alwaye v Asok Textiles Limited, Alwaye

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 292; AIR 1961 SC 699; 1961 (1) AnWR 121; 1985 (15) E.L.T. 482; [1961] 41 ITR 732; 1961 KerLJ 399; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 121; 1961 (63) PLR 577; 1961 (1) SCJ 487; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1952, misa

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47, r. 1 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 35 and 18A(8) - Finance Act - Question as to applicability of s. 35 of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Held, language and scope of s. 35 of Act cannot be equated with that of O. 47, r. 1, CPC, 1908 - Language of two is different because according to s. 35 of Act which provides for rectification of mistakes power is given to various income Tax authorities within four years from date of any assessment passed by them to rectify any mistake 'apparent from record' and in CPC, 1908 words are 'an error apparent on face of record' and two provisions do not mean same thing - ITO, can, u/s. 35 of Act, examine record and if he discovers that he has made a mistake he can rectify error and error which can be corrected may be an error of fact or of law - Restrictive operation of power of review under O. 47, r. 1, CPC, 1908 is not applicable in case of s. 35 of Act - Further held, in s. 18A(8) is mandatory - Therefore ITO was required to calculate interest in manner provided under provisions of that sub-section and had to add it to assessment - Order of High Court was erroneous - Appeal allowed.
