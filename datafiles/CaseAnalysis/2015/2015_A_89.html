Ashok v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 174; (2015) 4 SCC 393; 2015 CRLJ 2036; 2015 (2) JCC 1280; JT 2015 (3) SC 545; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 253; 2015(3) SCALE 530; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 375

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Gas, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 201, 498A - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed murder of his wife and 2 kids - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 201 an 498A IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeal before HC - HC upheld the conviction and sentence of accused - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, prosecution has failed to discharge its initial burden itself. Therefore, the question of burden of proof shifting to the accused to explain the happening of incidents does not arise. The delay of one month in filing FIR at the very face of it makes the entire case of the prosecution as concocted and an afterthought. There is no explanation as to why did the parents of deceased not make any complaint or FIR immediately after the recovery of her dead body. It is surprising that nowhere in the case of the prosecution this delay has been explained. Further, accused had put a very consistent story at all stages of the case starting from the missing report to the s. 313 statement without any inconsistency. Lack of justified motive would adversely affect the case of the prosecution as the present case is solely based on circumstantial evidence. With respect to previous incidents, all that is proved is demand of dowry by the in-laws and the accused- appellant. The prosecution has not brought any clinching evidence in support of last seen together theory so as to shift the burden of proof on the accused-appellant. In light of this, the prosecution has evidently failed to prove the guilt of the accused-appellant beyond doubt. Hence, judgment and order passed by the HC as also by the Trial Court are set aside. Appeal allowed.
