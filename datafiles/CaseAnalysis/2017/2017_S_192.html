Southern Power Distribution Company of Telengana Limited, through its CMD and others v Gopal Agarwal and others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Navin Sinha

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 507

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Auction, BANKING & FINANCE, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 - Purchase in Auction - power supply connection - Denial of - Respondent no.1 purchased house in auction conducted by respondent no.2/bank and applied for electricity connection to appellants/electricity board - HC allowed writ petition of respondent no.1 on ground that corporation could not denied power supply connection due to non-payment of arrears payable by previous owner of property - DB of HC upheld judgment of Single Judge on ground of failure to show/prove that respondent no.1 had undertaken to discharge liability of previous owner - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the purchaser in an auction sale conducted by the official liquidator on 'as is where is' and 'whatever there is' basis was found not liable for payment of the electricity arrears. The respondent no.1 applied for a fresh connection and he is in no way connected to the past owner. He has also not undertaken to pay the past arrears of the previous owner. He has also not undertaken to pay the past arrears of the previous owner. Hence, power connection to an auction purchaser cannot be withheld for the dues of the past owner. Appeal dismissed.
