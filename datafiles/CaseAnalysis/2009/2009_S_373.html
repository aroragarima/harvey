Gamini Bala Koteswara Rao & Ors v State Of A.P. Thr. Secretary

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1460; (2009) 10 SCC 636; AIR 2010 SC 589; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1708; 2009 (4) JCC 2657; JT 2009 (12) SC 416; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 170; 2009(12) SCALE 611; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Chance Witness

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - All accused, 20 in number, armed with lethal weapons such as axes, knives, sticks and stones attacked deceased - Incident was witnessed by PW.1 (nephew of deceased) and PW.5, a vegetable hawker - Trial Court acquitted all accused - On appeal, HC held that eye witnesses account of PW.1 and PW.5 fully corresponded with medical evidence; that presence of two witnesses had been fully explained and that so called improvements and inconsistencies referred to by Trial Judge were innocuous and did not go to root of matter and could, therefore, be ignored - HC convicted A.1 to A.3 - Hence, present appeal - Held, injuries found on dead body correspond fully with weapons that had been used - It is clear from FIR recorded by PW.1 and his statement in Court that PW.5 had been present at time of incident - HC fully justified in interfering in matter and was well within its jurisdiction to do so - Appeals dismissed.
