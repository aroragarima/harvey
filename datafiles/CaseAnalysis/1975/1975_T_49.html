Babulal Das v The State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 90; (1975) SCC (Cr) 138; (1975) 1 SCC 311; AIR 1975 SC 606; 1975 CRLJ 585; 1975 CrLR(SC) 166; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 193; 1975 UJ 202

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3(1)(a)(ii), 15 - Deyention - (A) Power to detain - Fact that the petitioner (detenu) was discharged by a Court for the same crime - (B) Grounds for detention - Solitary incident - Organised dacoity in third class compartment in running train by armed gang with fire -arms - Order of detention against member for such solitary incident - Validity - (C) Need by Government of recourse to parole power - Fact held, does not bear on the power to detain - Not bad - Need by Government of recourse to parole power in exceptional situations emphasized - Rule Discharged.
