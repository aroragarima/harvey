A.N. Roy, Commissioner of Police and Anr v Suresh Sham Singh

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 290; (2006) 5 SCC 745; AIR 2006 SC 2677; 2006 CRLJ 3614; 2006 (2) JCC 1177; JT 2006 (6) SC 89; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 539; 2006(6) SCALE 446; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 20(1), 20(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 23, 35 - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, ss. 18, 20 - Notification - Conferring of Powers - Striking of Notification - Sustainability - State issued Notification dt. 1-10-1999 conferring on Commissioner of Police, powers of District Magistrate within metropolitan area, or purposes of ss. 18 and 20 of the Act - Notification was issued in exercise of powers conferred by s. 20(5) r/w ss. 20(1), 20(2) CrPC and of all other powers enabling it in that behalf - Pursuant to aforesaid notification, Police Commissioner constituted task force, which conducted effective raid and ordered eviction or closure of brothels - HC by impugned order set aside order of Commissioner of Police and held that notification dt. 1-10-1999 pursuant to which order of eviction was passed was ab initio bad in law - Hence, instant appeal - Whether notification dt. 1-10-1999 issued by State empowering Commissioner of Police, powers of District Magistrate for purposes of ss. 18 and 20 of the Act, had been validly made - Held, from notification, it clearly appeared that State invoked power conferred upon State by s. 20(5) r/w ss. 20(1), 20(2) CrPC - U/s. 20(1), State had got power to appoint as many persons as it thinks fit to be Executive Magistrates in every district and in every metropolitan area and should appoint one of them to be District Magistrate - S. 20(5) CrPC stated that nothing in section should preclude State from conferring under any law for time being in force, on Commissioner of Police, all or any of powers of Executive Magistrate in relation to metropolitan area - Court should not enlarge scope of legislation or intention when language of statute was plain and unambiguous - Reading ss. 20(1), 20(2) and 20(5) in conjunction, State had power to appoint Commissioner of Police as Executive Magistrate and further appoint him as Additional District Magistrate, who should have powers of District Magistrate for purposes of ss. 18 and 20 of the Act - Hence, status quo ante as on 28-6-2004 should be maintained till Commissioner of Police was appointed by State, hence, would mean Commissioner of Police after necessary appointment should revive case from stage of order of eviction - Appeal partly allowed.
