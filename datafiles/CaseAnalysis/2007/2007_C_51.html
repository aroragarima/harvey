Rajinder Singh Katoch v Chandigarh Administration and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1308; (2007) 10 SCC 69; AIR 2008 SC 178; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3281; 2007 (3) ALT 382; 2008 (1) CGLJ 501; 2008 CRLJ 356; 2007 (144) DLT 241; 2008 (1) JCC 246; JT 2007 (13) SC 534; 2007 (4) KLT 877; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3421; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 762; 2007(12) SCALE 288; 2007 (8) SCJ 14; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 246

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: House Trespass

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 339 - Appellant and respondent No. 4 are brothers and co-sharers - Jointly possess some properties - Restrained by his brother from entering into family house - Filed an application under s. 482 CrPC before High Court - Dismissed - Held, although officer-in-charge of a police station is legally bound to register a FIR if allegations made by them gives rise to an offence which can be investigated without obtaining any permission from Magistrate concerned same by itself, however, does not take away right of competent officer to make a preliminary inquiry - Criminal proceedings cannot be taken recourse to for enforcing such a civil right - Appeal dismissed.
