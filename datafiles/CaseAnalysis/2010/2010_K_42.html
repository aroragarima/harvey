State Of Haryana & Anr. v Kartar Singh (D) Through Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1506; (2013) 11 SCC 375

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 18 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984, ss. 23(1-A) and 23(2) - Compensation - Enhancement - Benefit of amendment decree - Entitled to - Appellant (State Govt.) issued land acquisition notification u/s. 4 of the Act, followed by declaration made u/s. 6 of the Act and an Award was passed fixing market value of acquired land @ Rs.270/- per Biswa - Respondent's land was part of acquisition - Respondent sought reference u/s. 18 of the Act for determination of compensation - Reference Court enhanced compensation @ Rs.22/- per sq.yard - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed SLP, which was dismissed by SC - Thereafter, the 1894 Act was amended and respondents filed an application u/s. 151 and 152 of CPC before HC, in disposed of appeal, and sought benefit of amended ss. 23(1-A) and 23(2) of 1894 Act, which was allowed granting benefit said amended sections - Appellant raised objection that executing Court could not go behind decree, which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before HC, which was dismissed by impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, an award and decree having become final under the Act could not be amended or altered seeking enhancement of statutory benefits under amended provisions brought in by Amendment Act in 1894 Act by filing petitions u/ss. 151 and 152 of CPC - Award and decree passed by HC was without jurisdiction and nullity - It goes without saying that a plea of nullity of a decree could always be set up before the executing Court - Any judgment and order which was a nullity never acquire finality and was thus open to challenge in executing proceedings - Execution petition filed by respondents seeking execution of award and decree dt.28-4-1989 stood dismissed - Impugned orders were liable to be set aside - Appeals disposed of.
