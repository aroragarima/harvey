Hari Shanker Jain v Sonia Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19829; (2001) 8 SCC 233; AIR 2001 SC 3689; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 119; JT 2001 (7) SC 629; 2001(6) SCALE 233; 2001 (3) SCJ 396; 2001 (6) SLT 513; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 38; 2001 (7) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election

Keywords: Delimitation Of Constituencies, Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975, Jurisdiction Of The High Court

Summary: Election - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss. 5(1)(c), 5, 6, 9, 9(2), 9(1), 13, 15 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 10, 36(7), 80-A, 82, 82(1)(a), 83, 84, 86, 87, 98, 99, 100(1), 100, 100(1)(d)(iv), 116-A - Election - Challenged citizenship of returned candidate - Validity - Elections were held for Amedhi constituency and was contested by 27 candidates and respondent was declared elected as member of parliament - Appellants and another defeated candidate filed 3 election petitions in HC challenging election of respondent disputing citizenship etc - Respondent filed applications supported by affidavit submitting that election petitions lack cause of action and pleadings were vague and did not raise any triable issue and hence to be dismissed and was opposed by appellants - HC by common order dismissed all three petitions holding that none of petitions disclosed any cause of action or triable issue and as such none was maintainable u/s. 86 of the 1951 Act - Other petitioner did not file appeal and only appellants herein filed instant appeals - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, SC in Bhagwati Prasad Dixit Ghorewala Vs. Rajeev Gandhi,1986 Indlaw SC 642 held that HC could declare validity of Indian citizen acquiring foreign citizenship based on declaration by Central Govt. produced before it and so long as such declaration was not forthcoming HC should proceed on ground that candidate concerned had not ceased to be Indian citizen - HC could hear citizenship issues in election petition only when based on factual matrix and not by merely vague and bald allegations - S. 83(1)(a) of the 1951 Act mandates that election petition should contain concise statement of material facts relied by petitioner relied and constitute cause of action - Omission of single material fact leads to incomplete cause of action and statement of claim becomes bad - HC alone was competent to hear election petitions and while declaring election to be void must conform requirements of s. 100 and operative part of HC Order must conform requirements of ss. 98 and 99 of the 1951 Act - SC in Samant N. Balakrishna etc. Vs. Geroge Fernandez and Ors. etc, 1969 Indlaw SC 558 and Jitender Bahadur Singh Vs. Krishna Behari ,1969 Indlaw SC 103 held that petitioner should present full picture of cause of action with detailed information enabling opposite party to understand case and merely quoting words of section like chanting of mantra would not amount to stating material facts - SC in V.S. Achuthanandan Vs. P.J. Francis & Anr, 1999 Indlaw SC 1018 held that based on several judgments of SC material facts were preliminary facts which must be proved by petitioner to establish existence of cause of action and failure to plead material facts was fatal to election petition and no amendment of pleadings was permissible at later stage - Court should examine petition irrespective of any written statement or denial and reject it if it did not disclose cause of action - U/s.57(1) of the 1872 Act Court should take judicial notice of all laws in force in India and foreign laws were not included therein - As SC did not take judicial notice of foreign law it should be pleaded like any other fact if petitioner wants to rely on same and instant petitions do not satisfy this requirement - Petitioners admitted that respondent was granted Indian citizenship and it should be held that respondent was citizen of India by virtue of certificate granted u/s. 5(1)(c) of the 1955 Act which was existing as it was not cancelled or withdrawn or annulled till date - Petitions were filed nearly two decades after grant of citizenship to respondent and at no point of time did petitioners even challenge inclusion of respondent name in electoral roll - Making vague and bald allegations without giving any material facts after losing elections show that proper care was not taken in gathering and stating all material facts - Factual matrix for bald assertion was completely missing from election petition - Bald and vague averments made in election petitions did not satisfy requirement of pleading material facts as required by s. 82(1)(a) of the 1951 Act r/wrequirements of O. 7 r. 11 CPC - Decision of HC dismissing 2 election petitions at preliminary stage was sustained - Appeals dismissed.
