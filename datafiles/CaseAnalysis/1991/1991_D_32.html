Delhi Judicial Service Association, Tis Hazari Court, Delhi v State Of Gujarat And Ors. Etc-Etc.

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 99; (1991) 4 SCC 406; AIR 1991 SC 2176; 1991 CRLJ 3086; JT 1991 (3) SC 617; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Court Martial, Legal Advisor

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 41, 43 and 300 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 15 and 19 - Appointment of Commr. by Court in criminal case to hold enquiry - Initiation of contempt proceedings against police officials during pendency of criminal proceedings - Issue of notice of contempt and asking them to file their version before Commr. appointed by Court - Effect thereof - Held, Commr. considered evidence as well as circumstances and it was well reasoned findings by him - Commr. was acting on behalf of Supreme Court and have full authority to cross-examine witnesses record and collect evidence on behalf of Court - His report can be accepted in absence of convincing material for rejection - Mere issue of notice of contempt cannot be said to be violative of art. 20(3) - Order accordingly.
