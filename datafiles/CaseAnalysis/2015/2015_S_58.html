Vinod Kumar Subbiah v Saraswathi Palaniappan

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 295; (2015) 8 SCC 336; AIR 2015 SC 2504; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 176; 2015(4) ALL MR 984; 2015 (111) ALR 253; 2015 (4) ALT(SC) 53; 2015 (3) AWC 2887; JT 2015 (4) SC 336; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2297; 2016 (2) MahLJ 1; 2015 (4) MLJ 374; 2016 (1) MPLJ 561; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 1008; 2015 (4) RLW 3341; 2015(5) SCALE 551; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 800; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 130

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 2 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1)(ia) - Alleged Cruelty - Divorce - Justification - Appellant and Respondent were married on 28-6-2004 and moved to U.S. on 9-7-2004 - Further, a male child was born from said wedlock on 5-12-2006 - Appellant filed suit for divorce u/s. 13(1) (ia) of Act on 30-4-2007 on grounds of cruelty alleging that respondent was verbally abusive; she would insult his family; she would threaten to lodge false police complaints; and she would threaten to commit suicide placing blame on appellant and his family - Further, it was stated that respondent left for her parental home in June 2006 and appellant attempted to bring her back to her matrimonial home but she refused -Trial Court passed order of dissolution of marriage in favor of appellant and maintenance was fixed at Rs. 25,000 p.m. - However, HC held that allegations in appellant's divorce petition were no more than ordinary wear and tear that takes place in marriage and reversed order of Trial Court - Whether order passed by HC against appellant husband is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, appellant had duly pleaded instances of mental cruelty which he proved in evidence and documents. Further, examination of divorce petition makes it abundantly clear that various allegations of cruelty were made out and number of incidents were mentioned therein. Further evidence was submitted during course of Trial to substantiate these allegations, which is in keeping with O. 6 VI r. 2 of CPC. Further, SC held that Trial Court examined evidence at great length and came to reasoned conclusion that actions of respondent wife amounted to cruelty. SC held that if spouse abuses other as being born from prostitute, this cannot be termed as 'wear and tear' of family life. Summoning police on false or flimsy grounds cannot also be similarly viewed. Making it impossible for any close relatives to visit or reside in matrimonial home would also indubitably result in cruelty to other spouse. Hence, SC held that order passed by HC is not justified and is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Cruelty done one party towards other in a marital relationship cannot be regarded as wear and tear of family life and is a valid ground for divorce.
