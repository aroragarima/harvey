Â Bidi Supply Company v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1956 SC 479; [1956] 29 ITR 717; 1956 SCJ 492; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 267

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 22(2), 5(7A) - A/ys 1948-49, 1949-50 and 1955-56 - Transfer of assessment proceedings - Legality - Petitioner firm was assessed for a/ys 1948-49 and 1949-50 to income-tax by Income-tax Officer, Calcutta - Notices u/s. 22(2) of the Act were issued to petitioner on different dates by Income-tax Officer, Calcutta - Petitioner received a letter from Income-tax Officer, Calcutta, informing assessment records were transferred from current office to Income-tax Officer, Ranchi - Thereafter Income-tax Officer, Ranchi, called petitioner to submit its return for a/y 1955-56 - Hence, instant writ petition - Whether constitutionality of s. 5(7A) should be supported on principle of any reasonable classification laid down by SC or whether the Act lays down any principle for guiding or regulating exercise of discretion by Commissioner or Board of Revenue or whether sub-section confers an unguided and arbitrary power on those authorities to pick and choose an individual assessee and place that assessee at a disadvantage in comparison with other assessees - Held, order of transfer was certainly calculated to inflict considerable inconvenience and harassment to petitioner - Further, power of transfer could only be conferred if it was hedged round with reasonable restrictions, absence or existence of which could in last instance be determined by Courts and exercise of power should be in conformity with rules of natural justice - However, there was no such order of transfer as contemplated or sanctioned by s. 5(7A) of the Act and, therefore, present assessee still had right, along with all other merchants carrying on business in Calcutta, to have his assessment proceedings before Income-tax Officer of area in which his place of business was situated - Thus, Income-tax authorities by an executive order, unsupported by law, picked out petitioner and transferred all his cases by an omnibus order unlimited in point of time - Hence, s. 5(7A) of the Act was ultra vires of art. 14 of Constitution and so was s. 64(5)(b) of the Act in so far as it makes an order u/s. 5(7A) of the Act as it now exists, inviolate - Impugned order of transfer was set aside - Petition allowed.
