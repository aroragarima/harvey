Kushum Lata v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 320; (2006) 6 SCC 180; 2006 (5) ADJ 530; AIR 2006 SC 2643; 2006 (4) AWC 3356; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 159; JT 2006 (6) SC 293; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 298; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 604; 2006(7) SCALE 41; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 462; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2386

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 1963, r. 23 - Alleged irregularities in auction proceedings - Writ was filed styled as PIL for direction to authorities for investigating into alleged irregularities - HC held that appellant cannot file PIL when she herself claimed to be an intending bidder - Appeal against - Held, appellant has styled petition as PIL though it relates to a tender where she herself claims to be a tenderer - In another petition, questioning legality of auction, she is herself a party - HC was perfectly justified in dismissing Writ styled as a PIL - Appeal dismissed.
