Delhi Cloth and General Mills Company Limited and v Rajasthan State Electricity Board And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 330; (1986) 2 SCC 431; AIR 1986 SC 1126; 1986 CRLR 420; 1986(1) SCALE 416; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 633; 1986 (2) UJ 390

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Electricity (Supply) Amendment Act, 1966, Rajasthan Amendment Act 1976, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Electricity Act,1948 - Whether revising of tariff and applying uniform tariff was valid-Whether Board to revise the special rate of tariff agreed upon- impugned demand made by Board against the appelants is violative of art.19 or confiscatory in nature- Held, revising of tariff and applying uniform tariff was valid-It was lawful by Board to revise the special rate of tariff agreed upon-that there was no violation of art. 19 - It cannot be said that the impugned demands made by the Board as against the appellants were confiscatory in nature.
