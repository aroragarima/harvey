Â Ram Gopal Reddy v Additional Custodian Evacuee Property, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 393; AIR 1966 SC 1438; 1966 (2) AnWR 90; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 90; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 46 and 7 - On 15-11-1946, appellant claimed to have purchased certain patta lands from one Abdul Aziz Khan - Before, however, any transfer was made, Abdul Aziz Khan migrated to Pakistan - Appellant received notice from Deputy Custodian in December 1950 u/s. 7 of Act asking him to show cause why land should not be declared evacuee property - On appeal, Custodian held there was no registered sale deed duly executed by Abdul Aziz Khan in favour of appellant - Whether suit was barred u/s 46 of Act? - Held, where title of evacuee himself is in dispute, there can be no doubt that where property admittedly belonged to evacuee and person filing suit claims to be a transferee from evacuee, suit would certainly be barred in view of s. 46 of Act - Act, by s. 46, bars jurisdiction of civil or revenue courts to entertain or adjudicate upon any question whether any property or any right to or interest in any property is or is not evacuee property - Where therefore property or any right to or interest in any property undoubtedly belonged to evacuee and any transferee from evacuee claims property or any right to or interest therein he has to avail of remedies provided under Act - No registered sale deed was executed in this case and therefore property did not pass from Abdul Aziz Khan to appellant - Appeal dismissed.
