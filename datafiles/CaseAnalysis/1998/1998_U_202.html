Upen Chandra Gogoi v State of Assam and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2106; (1998) 3 SCC 381; (1998) SCC (L&S) 872; AIR 1998 SC 1289; 1999 (2) CLR 300; JT 1998 (2) SC 577; 1998 LabIC 1074; 1998 (2) LLJ 63; 1998 (2) LLN 416; 1998(2) SCALE 404; 1998 (3) SLJ 230; 1998 (2) SLR 258; 1998 (3) SLT 531; 1998 (3) Supreme 210

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Assam Legislative Assembly Secretariat Rules, 1986, r. 3, Sch 3 - Assam Judicial Service Rules, 1967, rr. 5 and 6 - Appointment - Post of Officer on Special Duty in Assam Legislative Assembly - Eligibility criteria - Judicial officer qualified to be appointed as District Judge - Advertisement before enforcement of rules - Candidate applying in response to advertisement - Held, candidate had to meet qualifications as in advertisement - Cannot validate an action which was not lawful at inception - Judicial Magistrate Grade III is not eligible for appointment as District Judge or Addl. District Judge under 1967 Rules - He is also not eligible to be appointed as Officer on Special Duty.
