Purshottam Govindji Halai v Additional Collector of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1956 SC 20; 1956 (58) BomLR 498; 1956 CRLJ 129; [1955] 28 ITR 891; 1956 (1) MLJ 51; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; 1956 SCJ 75; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 887

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 46(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13(1), 14, 21 and 245 - Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876, s. 13 - Appellant, sole proprietor of a business- Assessee is said to have some connection with two companies- Assesee did not pay up assesed amount - No payment having been made, Addl. Collector attached goodwill and tenancy rights in said premises by a warrant- Sale proceeds not being sufficient to satisfy assessed amount - Notice issued for appearence - (A) Whether arrest and detention for recovery of arrears of tax amount to infringement of art. 21 of Constitution? - Arrest and detention of assessee is procedure of law as per provisions mentioned both in art. 21 and s. 46(2) of Income-tax Act, 1922 - Held, in case assessee is arrested and detained in jail in execution of a warrant of arrest issued u/s. 13 of Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876 for recovery of tax u/s. 46 (2) of Income-tax Act, 1922, it does not infringe art. 21 of Constitution -(A1) Whether s. 46(2) of Income-tax Act,1922 prescribes two alternative modes of recovery which infringe art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, s. 46(2) of Income-tax Act, 1922, does not however indicate two different, alternative modes of recovery of amount of tax but only provides additional powers to collectors for more effective application - Hence no scope for discrimination violating art. 14 - (A2) Whether a difference in procedure in various states for recovery of arrears of tax contravenes art. 14 of Constitution? - The grouping of Income-tax defaulters into separate categories or classes statewise is certainly a territorial classification which is based on an intelligible differentia - Further held, s. 42(2) of Income Tax Act authorises collectors in different states to adopt machinery which differs from state to state as to treat defaulters differently in different states - Such discrimination is however permissible and it does not offend art. 14 of Constituion Of India - (B) Whether duty of court is to enter upon a discussion as to what the law should be? - Held, court is only concerned with interpretation of law and does not enter upon a discussion as to what the law should be as laid down by art. 245 of Constitution of India (D) Whether Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876, has become void on advent of constitution u/art. 13(1)? - Effect of art. 13(1) is that law could not stand in way of enjoyment of fundamental rights and law is not dead - Further held, s. 13 of Bombay City Land Revenue Act, 1876, became void under art. 13(1) in that it denied to Bombay defaulter equality before law in comparison with defaulter outside city of Bombay, it is not obliterated from statute book for all times or for all purposes or for all - Application dismissed.
