Bar Council of Uttar Pradesh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 330; (1973) 1 SCC 261; AIR 1973 SC 231; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 1073; 1973 UJ 513

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Right To Practise, Enrolment As An Advocate, Bar Association, Bar Council

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246, sch. 7, list 1, entries 77, 78 and 96, sch. 7, list 2, entry 63 and sch. 7, list 3, entries 44 and 26 - Advocates Act, 1961, u/s 22 - Stamp Act, 1899, s. 11(c), ch. 1 B, art. 17 A, ch. 1 B, art. 30 - (A) Relative scope of certain entries under sch. 7 of Constitution - Constitutionality of imposition of stamp duty on certificate of enrolment u/s 22 of 1961 Act - By amendments made in art. 30 of sch. 1-B of 1899 Act - (B) Question of repugnancy u/art. 254 - When arises? - (C) Applicability of art. 14 of Constitution - Held, State Legislature is competent to legislature and imposes stamp duty on certificate of enrolment u/s 22 of 1961 Act to be issued by State Bar Council of Uttar Pradesh hence, s. 11(c), ch. 1-B, art. 17 A, ch. 1-B , art. 30 of Stamp Act as amended from time to time in its application to Uttar Pradesh are constitutionally valid - Can only arise in matters where both Parliament and State Legislature have legislative competence to pass laws - And art. 14 can have no application where sources of authority of Parliamentary and State Legislation are different - Appeal dismissed.
