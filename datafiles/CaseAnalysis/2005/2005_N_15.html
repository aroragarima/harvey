N. Selvaraj v Kumbakonam City Union Bank Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1749; (2006) 9 SCC 172; 2007(6) SCALE 579

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Second Enquiry, Shops and Establishments Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Denial of back wages - Validity - Criminal case was instituted against appellant (working with respondent bank) on a complaint filed by the cashier of the Bank alleging that the appellant had stolen a bundle of notes from the cash counter - Appellant was placed under suspension in contemplation of a departmental enquiry - Appellant did not participate in the departmental enquiry, resulting in passing of an order of ex parte enquiry - Enquiry officer found him guilty of the charge and he was dismissed from the service - Writ petition filed thereagainst was allowed - Respondent Bank filed a writ appeal before the HC, which was dismissed - However, HC directed that appellant would not be entitled get the benefit of back wages for the period out of employment including continuity of service for retirement benefit at later stage in the event of succeeding in the departmental enquiry initiated against him - Whether HC was justified in denying benefit of back wages to appellant? - Held, payment of back wages, if any, should be subject to the final outcome of the decision of the disciplinary authority on the basis of the second enquiry report - No infirmity in the order passed by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
