M/S Payal Vision Ltd. v Radhika Choudhary

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 302; (2012) 11 SCC 405; 2012 (95) ALR 606; 2012 (6) AWC 5456; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 262; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 41; 2012 (5) CTC 887; JT 2012 (9) SC 214; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 741; 2013 (1) OLR 108; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 816; 2013 (118) RD 378; 2012(9) SCALE 105; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 1160; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Relationship of landlord & tenant, Landlord & Tenant

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 12 r. 6 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Suit for possession and recovery of mesne profit - Plaintiff filed a suit under O. 12 r. 6 of CPC against defendant for possession and recovery of mesne profit before trial court - Trial court decreed the suit in favour of a plaintiff - HC allowed appeal filed against said order and remanded the matter back to the trial court for disposal in accordance with law - Hence, instant appeal - Plaintiff contended that HC had fallen in error in holding that there was no clear admission by the defendant either regarding the existence of a relationship of landlord and tenant between the parties or the service of notice of termination of tenancy upon the defendant - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, the averments made in the plaint and the written statement clearly spell out an admission by the defendant that lease agreement dt. 10-10-2001 was indeed executed between the parties - It was also evident that the monthly rent was settled at Rs.50,000/- which fact too was clearly admitted by the defendant - Suffice it to say that the averments made in the written statement clearly accept the existence of the jural relationship of landlord and tenant between the parties no matter the lease agreement was not duly registered - Moreover, defendant did not have the benefit of a secure term under a registered lease deed as the tenancy was only a month to month tenancy that could be terminated upon service of a notice in terms of s. 106 of Act - Defendant has not denied the service of a notice upon the defendant - Hence, trial Court was, perfectly justified in decreeing the suit for possession filed by the plaintiff by invoking its powers under O. 12 r. 6 of CPC - Impugned order of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
