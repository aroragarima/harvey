(1) New Delhi Bar Association and Another; (2) v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, B.N. Agrawal, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 882; (2008) 13 SCC 628; AIR 2009 SC 693; 2008 (151) DLT 610; JT 2008 (6) SC 586; 2008(7) SCALE 539; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 898; 2008 (6) SLR 8

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Solicitor General, Government Of National Capital Territory Of Delhi Act, 1991, Quasi-Judicial Functions, Judicial Functions, Legislative Function, Bengal General Clauses Act, 1899, National Capital Territory of Delhi Act, 1991, Constitution (Sixty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1991

Summary: Punjab Courts Act, 1918 - Whether notification under the provisions of s. 19 of the Punjab Courts Act, 1918 as extended to the region of Delhi issued by Delhi Government, through the Lt. Governor of the National Capital Territory of Delhi dividing Delhi into nine civil districts was validly issued? - Held, not only the policy decision taken by the NCT of Delhi is founded on prolonged and in-depth deliberation between the NCT of Delhi, the Lt. Governor and the Delhi High Court which is directly concerned with the division of Delhi into judicial districts, but is also a result of directions issued by High Court by its order dated 01.05.2000 - Lt. Governor of Delhi being the representative of the National Capital Territory of Delhi was competent to divide the territory of Delhi under his administration into civil districts - In the absence of any provision in the Government of National Capital Territory of Delhi Act or in the absence of any other notification, order or legislation, the Punjab Courts Act, 1918, has continuous application to Delhi along with the laws made by the Delhi Legislative Assembly - On the extension of the Punjab Courts Act, 1918, to the U.T. of Delhi, it becomes a Central Act or an Act of Parliament as it is made by virtue of powers of Parliament to legislate for the U.T. of Delhi by virtue of cl. (4) of art. 246 of the Constitution hence, the notification issued by the Lt. Governor u/s. 19 of the Punjab Courts Act, 1918 has been authorized by a central legislation - Petitions disposed of.
