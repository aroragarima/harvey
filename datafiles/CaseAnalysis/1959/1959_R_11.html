Ram Gopal v Anant Prasad and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 239; AIR 1959 SC 851; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 116; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 64(a) - (A) Whether order granting renewal of permit to one amounts to refusal of permit to other u/s. 64 ,cl. (a) of Motor Vehicles Act? - Held, if there are two applicants for same permit then order granting renewal of permit to one amounts to automatic refusal of permit to other - When there are two applications in respect of same permit, one of which is by way of renewal to which objections have been filed and other is a fresh application and an order granting a renewal of permit is made, order amounts in fact to a refusal to grant permit to person making fresh application and he would be a person aggrieved within s. 64, cl. (a) of Motor Vehicles Act - (B) Whether appeal u/cl (f) can set aside grant of permit u/cl. (a) of s. 64 of Motor Vehicles Act? - Further held, it cannot be said that in an appeal which is competent u/cl. (a) of s. 64, order renewing or granting a permit cannot be set aside unless case was such that an appeal u/cl. (f) would have also been competent - Cl. (f) deals with a case where an objection had been filed against fresh grant or renewal of a permit but permit has none less been granted or renewed - If an appeal lies under any of other clause, that of course must be an effective appeal and appellate authority must therefore have all powers to give relief to which appellant is found entitled u/cl. (f) - Both cls. (a) and (f) are independent under Act - Appeal dismissed.
