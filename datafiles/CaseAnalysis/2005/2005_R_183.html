Rite Approach Group Limited v Messrs Rosoboronexport

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 727; (2006) 1 SCC 206; AIR 2006 SC 401; 2006 (1) ALD(SC) 66; [2006] 129 Comp Cas 771; JT 2005 (9) SC 527; 2005(9) SCALE 361; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(6), 11(8), 34, 9 - Application for appointment of Arbitrator - Supply of helicopters to Govt. of India - Whether this Court has jurisdiction to entertain this petition? - Held, as per Agency Agreement jurisdiction has been conferred on Chamber of Commerce and Trade of Russian Federation as authority before whom dispute shall be resolved - In view of specific arbitration clause conferring power on Chamber of Commerce and Trade of Russian Federation, it is that authority which alone will arbitrate matter - This Court has no jurisdiction and Chamber of Commerce and Trade of Russian Federation alone has jurisdiction to act as an arbitrator and resolve dispute - Application dismissed.
