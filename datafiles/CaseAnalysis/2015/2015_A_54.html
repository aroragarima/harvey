Lal Babu Priyadarshi v Amritpal Singh

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 957; AIR 2016 SC 461; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 29; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 218; 2016 (66) PTC 18; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 1033; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 1009; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 146

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, Trade Marks Act, 1999, Trade Marks Bill, 1993

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade Marks Act, 1999, ss. 11(a), 11(b), 11(e), 12(1), 18(1), 9, 9(2) - Trade mark on name of Hook Book - Rejection - Justification - Appellant trader made application to Registrar of Trade Marks to register trade mark by name 'RAMAYAN' in respect of incense sticks (agarbattis, dhoops) and perfumeries etc. - Respondent filed Notice of Opposition to oppose registration of trade mark in question u/ss. 9, 11(a), 11(b), 11(e), 12(1), 12(3) and 18(1) of Act claiming that impugned mark, being name of religious book, cannot become subject matter of monopoly for individual - Lower Authority holding that impugned trade mark consists of device of crown and word 'RAMAYAN' is capable of distinguishing goods and is not included in list of marks not registrable under Act, by order dt. 31-3-2004, dismissed application filed by respondent - Further, Board vide order dt. 10-1-2005 set aside order dt. 31-3-2004, passed by Lower Authority - Whether registration of word 'RAMAYAN' as trade mark, being name of Holy Book of Hindus, is prohibited u/s. 9(2) of Act.

Held, word 'RAMAYAN' represents title of book written by Maharishi Valmiki and is considered to be religious book of Hindus in India. Hence, using exclusive name of book 'RAMAYAN', for getting it registered as trade mark for any commodity could not be permissible under Act. If any other word is added as suffix or prefix to word 'RAMAYAN' and alphabets or design or length of words are same as of word 'RAMAYAN' then word 'RAMAYAN' may lose its significance as religious book and it may be considered for registration as trade mark. Further, it is case that appellant is seeking registration of word 'OM's RAMAYAN' as trade mark. Further, from photographs given by appellant, photographs of Lord Rama, Sita and Lakshman are also shown in label which is clear indication that appellant is taking advantage of Gods and Goddesses which is otherwise not permitted. Further, 'RAMAYAN' is being used as mark for similar products by more than 20 traders in Patna and in different parts of country, hence, it has become public juris and common to trade. SC held that any person cannot claim name of holy or religious book as trade mark for his goods or services marketed by him. Further, appellant has not been able to establish that word 'RAMAYAN' for which he has applied trade mark had acquired reputation of user in market as there are more than 20 traders in city using word 'RAMAYAN' as mark for similar products and also in different parts of country.Hence, impugned order passed by Board is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Registration of trade mark, being name of Holy Book, is prohibited u/s. 9(2) of Act and authority is justified to reject application for registration of such trade mark.
