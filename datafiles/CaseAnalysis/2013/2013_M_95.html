S. Manickam v Metropolitan Transport Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 390; (2013) 12 SCC 603; 2013 (4) ACC 264; 2013 ACJ 1935; AIR 2013 SC 2629; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 116; 2013(5) ALL MR 477; 2013 (100) ALR 1; 2013 (4) AWC 4258; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 528; 2013 (3) KLT 248; 2013 (2) OLR 722; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 696; 2013 (4) RLW 3116; 2013(8) SCALE 179; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 351

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Reduction of compensation - Challenged - Appellant, aged 45 years, was alighting from the bus owned by the respondent (Transport Corporation), the conductor of the bus blown the whistle without noticing him - Due to the sudden movement of the bus, appellant fell down and the rear wheel of the bus rammed over on his right leg and he sustained severe injuries on his head, right hand and chest - After treatment, right leg of appellant below the knee was amputated - Appellant claimed a sum of Rs. 21,00,000/- as compensation before Tribunal who awarded a sum of Rs. 9,42,822/- as total compensation by adopting the multiplier of 13 in terms of the second schedule to the Act - On appeal, HC reduced the compensation from Rs. 9,42,822/- to Rs. 6,72,822/- - Hence, instant appeal - Whether compensation in a motor vehicle accident case was payable to a claimant for both heads, viz., loss of earning/earning capacity as well as permanent disability - Held, there was no difficulty in holding that the HC had committed an error in setting aside the award amount of Rs. 1,00,000/- under the head 'permanent disability' on the ground that substantial amount had been fixed under the head 'loss of earning' and 'loss of earning capacity' - It was not in dispute that at the time of the accident, the appellant was aged about 45 years and he was the proprietor of X Furniture Mart and 15 persons were working under him - Based on the evidence, the Tribunal had determined his income as Rs. 8,000/- per month - Further, it was seen from the evidence that appellant lost his earnings during the period of treatment from 28-1-1997 to 31-12-1997, and because of severe injuries, his right leg below the knee was amputated - Considering appellant's age, avocation and the fact that appellant cannot do the same work as he was doing prior to the accident due to amputation of his right leg, Tribunal was fully justified in fixing a sum of Rs. 1,00,000/- towards 85% permanent disability - Impugned order of HC setting aside the compensation under the said head cannot be sustained - Accordingly, in addition to the amount determined by the HC, a sum of Rs. 1,00,000/-, as awarded by the Tribunal, towards 85% permanent disability - HC, while computing the loss of earning capacity, without any acceptable reason, applied the multiplier of 10 and fixed a sum of Rs. 3,20,000 (Rs. 8000/- x 10x12x1/3 ) as against Rs. 4,00,000/- determined by the Tribunal - Though multiplier method cannot be mechanically applied to ascertain the future loss of income or earning power, depending on various factors such as nature and extent of disablement, avocation of the injured whether it would affect his or her employment or earning power, the loss of income or earnings may be ascertained by applying the same as provided under the second Schedule to the Act - Inasmuch as in the present case, the age of the claimant, i.e., 45 years, on the date of the incident had not been disputed by the respondent, the proper multiplier in terms of the second Schedule was 13 which was rightly applied by the Tribunal - Accordingly, while modifying the quantum under the loss of earning capacity, namely, Rs. 3,20,000/- as fixed by the HC, SC restored the amount to Rs. 4,00,000/- as determined by the Tribunal - Hence, appellant was entitled to a total compensation of Rs. 8,52,822/- with interest at the rate of 9% from the date of claim petition till the date of deposit - Appeals partly allowed.
