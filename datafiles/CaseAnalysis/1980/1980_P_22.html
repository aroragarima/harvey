Niranjan Singh and Another v Prabhakar Rajaram Kharote and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 201; (1980) SCC (Cr) 508; (1980) 2 SCC 559; AIR 1980 SC 785; 1980 CRLJ 426; 1980 CrLR(SC) 265; 1980 LLJ 428; 1980 MLJ 573; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 15; 1980 UJ 428

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 439 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Application for bail - Police convict - Maintainability of - Held, constitutional mandates lose credibility if, in practice, protectors of law and minions of State become engines of terror and panic people into fear - No person accused of an offence can move court for bail u/s. 439 CrPC, 1973 unless, he is in custody - A person in custody when he is in duress either because he is held by investigating agency or other police or allied authority or is under control of court having been remanded by judicial order, or having offered himself to court's jurisdiction and submitted to its orders by physical presence - Custody is physical control or at least physical presence of accused in court coupled with submission to jurisdiction and orders of court - A responsible Govt. responsive to appearances of justice, would have placed police officers against whom serious charges had been framed by a criminal court under suspension unless exceptional circumstances suggesting a contrary course exist - It is directed that a copy of judgment be sent to Home Ministry in Govt. of India for suitable sensitized measures to pre-empt recurrence of error highlighted in the present judgment - Order accordingly.
