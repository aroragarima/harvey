Sanichar Sahni v The State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

26 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 736; (2009) 7 SCC 198; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 347; AIR 2010 SC 3786; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2826; JT 2009 (13) SC 192; 2009(8) SCALE 680; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 112; 2009 (2) UC 1058

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Joinder Of Charges, Misjoinder Of Charges

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302 r/w 34, 394 r/w 34- Criminal conspiracy - Framing of charge and conviction of appellant u/s. 120B, and other co-accused u/ss. 302/394 r/w 34, 412 and u/s. 27 of Arms Act - Conviction confirmed for appellant and one other accused and the third accused was given benefit of doubt - (A) Conviction of appellant u/s. 120B IPC, whether proper? - Held, Yes - Evidence of impeccable character of two witnesses, PW2 and PW5 on record - Full opportunity given to defend only on the point of conspiracy as there was no other allegation against the appellant - (B) Framing of charge u/s. 120B against appellant and non-framing of the charge of conspiracy against other co-accused, on which contention was raised that it is not permissible in law to convict the appellant under the said charge as he has also not been charged for any other offence as other co-accused persons where not charged with s. 120B further contention that no person can conspire with himself - Contention whether acceptable? - Held, No - HC had taken up the issue but in view of the sufficient material on record to prove the guilt, it abstained to consider the question - Settled position vide decisions of Court referred and deliberation entered into - Unless the convict is able to establish that defect in framing the charges has caused real prejudice to him and that he was not informed as what was the real case against him and that he could not defend himself properly, no interference is required on mere technicalities; conviction order in fact is to be tested on the touchstone of prejudice theory - No prejudice shown by appellant; he never raised any grievance against the same at the time of framing of charge or during the course of trial or by filing any petition for quashing the charge; nor the issue was agitated before the HC - Held further, no prejudice caused to the appellant - Being without any force - Appeal dismissed.
