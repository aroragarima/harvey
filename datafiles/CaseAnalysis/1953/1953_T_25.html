Â Kedar Nath Bajoria Hari Ram Vaid v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 17; AIR 1953 SC 404; 1953 CRLJ 1621; 1953 SCJ 580; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950, West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949, Bombay Public Safety Act

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 (2) - West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949, ss. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 9(1), 10 and 11 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Sch. 11 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B and 420 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 5 - Bombay Public Safety Act, s. 12 - (A) Whether prescribing a special procedure of trial, can be said to be unreasonable or arbitrary and, therefore, violative of art. 14? - Held, legislative classification must not be arbitrary but should be based on an intelligible principle having a reasonable relation to object which legislature seeks to attain - If classification on which legislation is founded fulfils this requirement, then differentiation which legislation makes between class of persons or things to which it applies and other persons or things left outside purview of legislation cannot be regarded as a denial of equal protection of law - (B) Whether Govt. can make a discriminatory choice of selecting among persons, who are charged with same class or classes of offences, to be tried differently? - If legislative policy is clear and definite and as an effective method of carrying out that policy, a discretion is vested by statute upon a body of administrators or officers to make selective application of law to certain classes or groups of persons, statute itself cannot be condemned as a piece of discriminatory legislation - Classification must have a rational relation to object of Act as recited in preamble - It is therefore, not an arbitrary power - Appeals dismissed.
