Union Of India And Others v V.N. Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 437; (2010) 5 SCC 579; AIR 2010 SC 3116; 2010 (5) AWC 4781; 2010 (168) DLT 605; JT 2010 (3) SC 587; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 777; 2010(3) SCALE 618; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 454; 2010 (7) SLR 19

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Army Rules, Corps, Severe Reprimand

Summary: (A) Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950. ss. 122 and 123 - Period of limitation for trial - Held, s. 122 is a complete Code in itself so far as the period of limitation is concerned for not only it provides in sub-section (1) the period of limitation for such trials but specifies in sub-section (2) thereof, the offences in respect of which the limitation clause would not apply - S. 122 of the Army Act in substance prescribes that no trial by Court Martial of any person subject to the provisions of the Act for any offence shall be commenced after the expiration of a period of three years - It further explains as to when period of three years shall commence - It provides that the period of three years shall commence on the date of the offence or where the commission of the offence was not known to the person aggrieved by the offence or to the authority competent to initiate action, the first day on which such offence comes to the knowledge of such person or authority whichever is earlier - Since the Section is in absolute terms and no provision has been made under the Act for extension of time, it is obvious that any trial commenced after the period of limitation will be patently illegal - Further, question of limitation to be determined u/s. 122 of the Act is a mixed question of fact and law and therefore in exercise of writ jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution, ordinarily the HC will not interfere with the findings of Court Martial on question of limitation decided u/s. 122 of the Army Act - Appeal allowed.

(B) Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950. ss. 122, 122(1)(b) and 123 - Period of limitation for trial - Person aggrieved within the meaning of s. 122 - Held, term "the person aggrieved by the offence" would be attracted to natural persons i.e. human beings who are victims of an offence complained of, such as offences relating to a person or property and not to juristic persons like an organisation as in the present case - Therefore, a meaningful reading of the provisions of s. 122(1)(b) makes it absolutely clear that in the case of Government organisation, it will be the date of knowledge of the authority competent to initiate the action, which will determine the question of limitation - Appeal allowed.

(C) Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950. ss. 122 and 123 - Period of limitation for trial - Respondent, Lt. Col. was posted as Officiating Commandant in 4 Reserved Petroleum Depot ('4 RPD') - During the inspection of 4RPD, certain irregularities were noticed with regard to local purchases in the month of May 1993 - Preliminary investigation was carried out and report was submitted to Brigadier, Deputy Director, Supply and Transport, Headquarters (DDST), who in turn forwarded the report to Major General of Army Supply Corps (ASC) - In that report, the DDST recommended closure of the case - However, Major General, ASC forwarded the papers to the Headquarters Western Command - A show-cause notice was issued to respondent - DDST accepted the explanation given by the respondent and again recommended closure of case - On January 8, 1994 a Technical Court of Inquiry was convened - On May 7, 1994 a Staff Court of Inquiry was convened pursuant to the orders of the General Officer Commanding-in-Chief, ('GOC-in-C') Western Command - Staff Court of Inquiry concluded its proceedings and submitted its recommendations - After examining the recommendations of SCI, the GOC, Delhi area, Major General recommended on October 19, 1994, disciplinary action against the respondent - Thereafter, the GOC-in-C Western Command, directed to initiation of disciplinary action against the respondent vide communication dated December 3, 1994 - On December 11, 1996, the General Officer Commanding issued an order convening General Court Martial (GCM) - By order dated April 3, 1997, the GCM found the respondent guilty of some charges and imposed the punishment - HC quashed GCM proceedings as well as the sentence imposed upon the respondent after holding that GCM proceedings were initiated after expiry of the period of limitation prescribed by s. 122(1) (b) of the Army Act, 1950 - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Brigadier or Major General were competent to initiate action against the respondent? - Held, no - When an offence or misconduct is alleged to have been committed by a person subject to the Army Act, then the Officer in chain of command is required to take action for investigation of the charges and trial by court martial - These powers are vested in the officers in chain of command - Those powers are not vested with staff Officers - Since the respondent was commanding 4 RPD, his next officer in command was GOC, Delhi Area and the power to take disciplinary action was vested with him - Power to initiate action in terms of s. 122(1)(b) of the Army Act was only with GOC Delhi Area who is next superior authority in chain of command - (B) Whether GCM commenced the trial against the respondent within the period of limitation of three years? - Held, GOC Delhi Area i.e. the next Authority in chain of command to the respondent recommended on October 19, 1994 initiation of disciplinary action against the respondent whereas the GOC-in-C Western Command gave directions on December 3, 1994, to initiate disciplinary action against the respondent - Therefore, the date of commencement of the period of limitation for the purpose of GCM of the respondent, commenced on December 3, 1994 when direction was given by GOC-in-C Western Command to initiate disciplinary action against the respondent - Period of limitation for the purpose of trial of the respondent commenced on December 3, 1994 when the GOC-in-C Western Command being the competent authority directed disciplinary action against the respondent in terms of Section 122(1)(b) of the Army Act - Period of three years from the direction dated December 3, 1994 would expire on December 2, 1997, whereas the GCM commenced the trial against the respondent on December 17, 1996 which was well within the period of limitation of three years - Therefore the impugned Judgment is legally unsustainable and will have to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
