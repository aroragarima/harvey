Â Charan Lal Sahu v Neelam Sanjeeva Reddy

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 328; (1978) 2 SCC 500; AIR 1978 SC 499; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 1; 1978 UJ 134

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 71(1), 71(3) - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 14, 14-A, 14(3), 5-B and 5-C - Nomination - Rejection - Locus Standee - Exercise of Jurisdiction - Election of President was called in question by an election petition, either by candidate or by electors/petitioner u/s. 14-A of the Act, 1952 - Petitioner not deposited sum of money as required u/s. 5-C of Act, he was not duly nominated - His nomination paper was rejected by Returning Officer acting u/s. 5-E of Act - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioner had locus standee to maintain election petition, or, he was duly nominated candidate u/ss. 5-B and 5-C Of Act, 1952 - Held, petition was barred as petitioner had no locus standee to maintain it u/ss. 14-A, 14(1) and (3) r/w. 5-B and 5-C of the Act and Order 39, rr. 2 and 5 of the Rules mentioned in s. 14(3) of the Act - Hence, petition was not maintainable - Petition dismissed.
