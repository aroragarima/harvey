Moran Mar Basselios Catholicos and Another v Most Rev. Mar Poulose Athanasius and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1954 SC 526; 1954 KLT 385; 1954 SCJ 736; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General, Aliens

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Convention of Meeting - Without Invitation - Validity of Election Objected - Competency - President of appellants/Association, convened meetings of Association - No invitation of meeting was given to churches under control of respondent No. 1 as they were already members of a new Church - They were not entitled to any invitation and were ignored - Respondents objected validity of election conducted by appellants No. 1, 2 and 3 as a result of meeting - Appellants filed suit for possession of church properties - Lower Court's concluded that meeting was formed only by a minority of church, which was erroneous and meeting was not convened by competent persons - District Judge hold that Patriarch of church had no temporal authority of jurisdiction or control and had no right to interfere in administration of temporalities and properties of Church and notice of said meeting had not been given to all churches mainly to respondents - On further appeal to HC, also rejected review petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellants have proved validity of their meeting and Whether acts and conduct of appellants were found guilty - Held, Lower Courts were not justified in setting and using part of admission in support of appellants and rejecting that of respondents, was not sustainable in law - HC, had not considered implication of respondents claim, that Patriarch alone had got power of ordination and Catholics had not that power could not be regarded as a departure from Canon - Court opined that appellants had made out a valid ground for allowing their application for review and accordingly allowed appeal, and set aside judgment of HC and admitted review -Jurisdiction and power regarding review application, had become in fructuous - Appeal allowed.
