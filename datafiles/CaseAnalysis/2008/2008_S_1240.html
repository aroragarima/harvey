Ormi Textiles and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 733; (2008) 5 SCC 194; 2008 (5) ADJ 109; AIR 2008 SC 2177; 2008(4) ALL MR 408; 2008 (3) AWC 2772; 2010 (1) BC 517; [2008] 144 Comp Cas 580; 2008 (4) CTC 649; 2008 (2) DRTC 156; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 849; 2008(8) SCALE 185; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Industrial Concerns

Summary: State Financial Corporations Act, 1951 - Interpretation of s. 29 of the Act - Appellant contended that u/s. 29 only mortgaged property could be the subject matter of sale - Held, Corporation is a 'State' within the meaning of Article 12 of the Constitution of India - For the purpose of invoking s. 29 of the Act, the borrower must have a liability to the Corporation under an agreement, it must make a default in repayment of any loan or advance, etc., Corporation in such a situation shall inter alia have the right to take over the management or possession or both of the industrial concerns; this power is in addition to the power of the right to transfer by way of lease or sale and realize the property pledged, mortgaged, hypothecated or assigned to the Corporation - Powers contained in two parts of Section 29 of the Act are separate and distinct - Corporation can also realize the mortgaged property which would mean that when a property had been sold, only the mortgaged property can be realized and not any other property which was not the subject matter of mortgage - Power to take over the management or possession is a statutory power - As and when the debt is realized, the Corporation would be bound to handover the management or possession of the property, as the case may be, back to the industrial establishment - Whereas s. 29 confers a power to sale the property unilaterally, s. 31 provides inter alia for the same power only through the intervention of the court - Right to sell the property by the Corporation must be exercised only in respect of the mortgaged property and not the one which is not the subject matter thereof - Appeal allowed.
