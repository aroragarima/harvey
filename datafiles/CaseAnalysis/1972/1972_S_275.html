Mulk Raj v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, A.N. Grover, D.G. Palekar, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 621; (1972) 3 SCC 839; (1973) SCC (Cr) 24; AIR 1972 SC 1197; 1972 CRLJ 754

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 4 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Contempt - Apology when should be tendered ?- Apology is an act of contrition - Held, unless apology is offered at earliest opportunity and in good grace, apology is shorn of penitence and, hence is liable to be rejected - If offered at a time when contemnor finds that court is going to impose punishment, it ceases to be an apology and it becomes an act of a cringing coward - Appeal dismissed.
