Asrumati Debi v Kumar Rupendra Deb Raikot and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, M. Patanjali Sastri, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 101; AIR 1953 SC 198; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 710; 1953 SCJ 300; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 1159

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 2 (9) - Suit filed for recovery of large estate by respondent - High Court held appeal not competent in law - Whether an order for transfer u/cl. 13 of Letters Patent is a 'judgment' for purposes of appeal? - Judgment must be final pronouncement which puts an end to proceeding so far as court dealing with it is concerned - Decision on any and every point in dispute between parties to a suit is not necessarily a 'judgment' - Held, there is difference between an order refusing to rescind leave granted u/cl. 12 of Letters Patent and u/cl. 13 directing removal of a suit from one court to another - Order for transfer cannot be placed in same category as an order rejecting a plaint or one dismissing a suit on a preliminary ground - Appeal dismissed.
