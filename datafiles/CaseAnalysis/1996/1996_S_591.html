National Human Rights Commission v State of Arunachal Pradesh and Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1172; (1996) 1 SCC 742; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 442; AIR 1996 SC 1234; AIR 1996 SCW 1274; 1996 (1) CCC 69; JT 1996 (1) SC 163; 1996(1) SCALE 155; 1996 (2) SCJ 135; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 278; 1996 (1) Supreme 295; 1996 (1) UJ 370

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Foreigners

Keywords: Citizenship By Registration

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, arts. 21 and 32 - Human Rights Act, 1993, s. 18 - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss. 5(1)(a) and 6-A - Public interest petition filed, alleging that Chakmas, settled mainly in the State of Arunachal Pradesh, are being persecuted by sections of the citizens of Respondent State-Arunachal Pradesh - Whether grant of citizenship is entirely governed by the Citizenship Act, 1955 and the Central Govt. is the sole authority to grant citizenship, with State Govt. having no jurisdiction in the matter? - Held, the collector has merely to receive the application and forward it to the Central Govt., who is the only the authority constituted under r. 8 empowered to register a person as a citizen of India, thus it follows that only that authority can refuse to entertain an application made u/s. 5 of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
