State of Haryana v Pala and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3165; (1996) 8 SCC 51; (1996) SCC (Cr) 526; AIR 1996 SC 2962; 1996 (1) CCR 205; 1996 (1) Crimes 107; 1996 CRLJ 1872; JT 1996 (2) SC 416; 1996 (4) KarLJ 662; 1996(2) SCALE 179; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1022; 1996 (2) Supreme 221

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Acquittal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304 Part II - Murder - Cause of death - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent (accused) alleged killed deceased - Trial Court convicted respondents, applying cl. 3 of s. 300 of IPC and u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal, HC had applied exception 4 to s. 300 of IPC and converted the offence of murder into culpable homicide not amounting to murder and convicted u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment to period already undergone and accordingly got them released - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, medical evidence on record do clearly establish that Septicaemia was not the primary cause and death was due to injuries caused to deceased and they were sufficient to cause death in the ordinary course of nature - Septicaemia would, thus, not be taken into account - When accused emerged from their house and beat with deadly weapon on head and other parts of the body and death occurred as a result of injuries, it should be inferred that the attack on vital parts of body was intended to be caused with an intention to cause death - Intention was locked up in the heart of assailant and inference was to be drawn from acts and attending circumstances - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and accused was convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Appeal allowed.
