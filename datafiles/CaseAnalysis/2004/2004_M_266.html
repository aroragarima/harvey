Metroark Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 128; (2004) 12 SCC 505; AIR 2004 SC 3142; 2004 (164) E.L.T. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Finding

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35L - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 Appellate orders - Binding nature - If a Court or Tribunal records something then that has to be taken as correct unless party gets same clarified from that Court or Tribunal itself - Tribunal is final fact finding authority - Unless it is shown that there is something perverse in its finding, SC would not interfere, on facts held Dimethicone held to be Silicone in primary form by Appellate Tribunal - Assessee made no effort to get this position clarified from Tribunal nor taken any ground in Memo of Appeal - Product classifiable under sub-heading 3910.00 and not under sub-heading 3003.20.
