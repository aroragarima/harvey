Lt. Col. Sawai Bhawani Singh Etc v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3812; (1996) 3 SCC 105; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 62; JT 1996 (2) SC 132; 1996(1) SCALE 733; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 145; 1996 (2) Supreme 323; 1996 (1) UJ 746

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Taxation - Rajasthan Urban Lands Tax Act, 1964, ss. 3 and 4 - Amending Act 15 of 1973, ss. 4 and 5 - Amending Act 18 of 1971 - Amending Act 18 of 1973 - Whether or not Principal Act of 1964 was by itself invalid, more so after its amendment by Amending Act 15 of 1973 rendering it further void being beyond legislative competence of State Legislature? - A land or building or both of a person may be subjected to direct tax by State Legislature under Entry 49 of List II and may also be subject-matter of direct tax as a component of his total assets by Union Legislature under Entry 86 of List I; these two taxes are distinct in nature and cannot be said to be overlapping or that State Legislature was not competent to levy such tax on lands and buildings merely because they have been subjected to another tax as a component of concerned person's total assets - Principal Act could stand on its own and amendment caused to it by Amending Act 15 of 1973 by itself brought a blot by way of substitution, offending portion of which was later sliced off as much and nothing was so inextricably mixed up so that extricable parts were not severable - Held, charging s. 3 and supportive s. 4, as salvaged, are part of a scheme which was within legislative competence of Rajasthan State Legislature - Appeals dismissed.
