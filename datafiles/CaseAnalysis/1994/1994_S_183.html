State Bank Of India v S.S.Koshal

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 398; (1994) Supp2 SCC 468; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1019; AIR 1994 SCW 2901; 1994 (27) ATC 834; 1995 (5) SLR 181

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Summary: Banking & Finance - Service - Enquiry officer - Charge - Removal from service - Respondent was the Branch Manager in the State Bank of India - Disciplinary inquiry was held against him in respect of six charges - Enquiry Officer held charges 1 and 5 as established but held that charges 2, 3, 4 and 6 were not established - After perusing the report of the Enquiry Officer the disciplinary authority agreed with the Enquiry Officer that charges 1 and 5 were established and charges 3 and 4 were not established - So far as charge 2 was concerned he disagreed with the Enquiry Officer - Disciplinary authority held that the said charge was fully established - Punishment of removal from service was imposed by an order - Respondent filed an appeal to the appellate authority - Appellate authority dismissed the appeal - Respondent approached HC by way of W.P. - HC allowed the W.P. on three grounds - Whether it was obligatory on the part of the appellate authority to say more than this as the order as it is, showed application of mind - Held, HC could take the view that the rule required the appellate authority to pass a speaking order even if it was an order of affirmance - Court had already extracted the appellate order in full which showed that it considered at length the facts of the case including the fact that the appellate authority had differed from the findings of the Enquiry Officer in respect of two charges - Appellate authority then stated that it considered the relevant grounds of appeal and after considering the facts of the case came to the conclusion that there was no substance in the appeal - It was an order of affirmance - Thus, it was not obligatory on the part of the appellate authority to say more than this as the order as it is, showed application of mind - Order won't be characterised as a non-speaking order - Appeal allowed.
