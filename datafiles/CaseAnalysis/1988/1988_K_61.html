State of Uttar Pradesh v Krishna Gopal and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 76; (1988) 4 SCC 302; (1988) SCC (Cr) 928; AIR 1988 SC 2154; 1988 (2) AWC 1328; 1988 (3) Crimes 195; 1989 CRLJ 288; JT 1988 (3) SC 544; 1988(2) SCALE 632; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Respondents killed deceased by attacking him with knives - Whole incident was witnessed by prosecution witness from a distance of 20 paces - Motive for murder was previous enmity between appellant and deceased - Whether acquittal of respondents was proper? - Evidence must be tested for its inherent consistency and inherent probability of story; consistency with account of other witnesses held to be credit-worthy; consistency with undisputed facts 'credit' of witnesses; their performance in witness-box; their power of observation etc. - Held, no - Testimony of eye witness trustworthy and acceptable - Guilt further established on basis of medical evidence - Guilt of respondents proved beyond reasonable doubt - Appeal disposed of .
