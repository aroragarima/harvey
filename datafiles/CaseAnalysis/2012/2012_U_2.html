U. Sree v U. Srinivas

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 534; (2013) 2 SCC 114; AIR 2013 SC 415; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 162; 2013(1) ALL MR 409; 2013 (96) ALR 303; 2013 (1) AWC 193; 2013 (1) Bom.C.R. 485; 2013 (1) CTC 113; JT 2012 (12) SC 358; 2013 (1) MLJ 527; 2013 (1) OLR 905; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 883; 2012(12) SCALE 98; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 9, 13(1)(i-a), 26,27 - Family Courts Act, 1984, s. 7 - Dissolution of Marriage - Conjugal Rights - Custody of Child - Maintenance - Sustainability - Appellant-wife instituted case u/s. 9 of Act, for restitution of conjugal rights and respondent-husband filed petition u/ss. 13(1)(i-a), 26,27 of Act, r/w s. 7 of 1984, Act praying for dissolution of marriage, custody of child and return of jewellery and other items - Family Judge jointly tried both cases and on basis of evidence on record, dismissed application for restitution of conjugal rights preferred by wife and allowed petition of husband for dissolution of marriage and held that child would remain in custody of mother on principle that welfare of the child was paramount, and that husband was not entitled to return of jewels or any other item from wife in absence of any cogent evidence in that regard - Dissatisfied by order appellant and respondent preferred CMA's before High Court and DB, concurred with Family judge and dismissed appeals and directed respondent-husband to pay maintenance to appellant wife and her son - Hence instant appeals - Appellant contended that respondent dumped her without any reason after birth of their child and inspite of several efforts did not take her back - Respondent pleaded that there was total incompatibility in marital relationship and that appellant picked up quarrels on trivial issues - Whether Family Judge was justified in dismissing petition for conjugal rights of appellant and dissolving marriage of respondent with appellant - Held, factual matrix would reveal, both Courts have proceeded on base that wife had not endeavored to reunite herself with husband and there had long lapse of time since they had lived together as husband and wife, conclusion had been drawn that there was an animus descerendi on part of wife and to test tenability of conclusion, SC perused petition for divorce from which it was evident that there was no pleading with regard to desertion and it needed no special emphasis to state that a specific case for desertion had to be pleaded - It was also interesting to note that petition was not filed seeking divorce on ground of desertion but singularly on cruelty, in absence of a prayer in that regard, SC, was constrained to hold that conclusion arrived at as regards desertion by Family Judge which had been concurred with by HC was absolutely erroneous and, accordingly, overturned - From analysis, it was established that husband had proved his case of mental cruelty which was foundation for seeking divorce, therefore, despite dislodging finding of desertion, SC conclude and held that respondent-husband had rightly been granted a decree of divorce - Hence, decree for dissolution of marriage was affirmed only on ground of mental cruelty which eventually lead to dismissal of appeals - Appeal dismissed.
