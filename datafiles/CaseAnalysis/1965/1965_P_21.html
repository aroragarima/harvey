Â Prabhakar Rao N. Mawle v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1965 SC 1827; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 65; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Vexatious Litigation (Prevention) Act 1949, Madras Vexatious Litigation (Prevention) Act (Act 8 of 1949)

Summary: Constitution - Government of India Act, 1935, Entries 2 of List II and 2 and 4 of List II - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19 - Madras Vexatious Litigation (Prevention) Act, 1949, ss. 2(i) and (ii) - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 119 and 65 - Andhra State Act,1953, s. 30 - (A) Whether Madras Vexatious Litigation (Prevention) Act is constitutionally valid? - Held, valid - The Act does not contravene arts. 14 and 19 - Does not seek to create an unreasonable distinction between litigant and litigant - Litigants who seek High Court sanction before reaching court are a class by themselves, persons who 'habitually and 'without reasonable cause' file vexatious actions, civil or criminal - Thus, the Act promotes public good - Though the Act does not prevent him from bringing genuine and bona fide actions - So the Act is constitutional - (B) Whether the Madras Act 1949 is applicable to Telangana area of State of Andhra Pradesh? - Held, no, it does not - The Act conferred jurisdiction upon the Madras HC - This jurisdiction is not an inherent jurisdiction - Whether as a court of record or otherwise - By virtue of Andhra State Act ,1953, s. 30 of the Act continued to be an Act in force in the Andhra state and the Andhra HC possessed the same jurisdiction as the former Madras HC - The Act was applied by the legislature only to the Madras Presidency - U/s. 119 of the States Reorganistion Act clearly no law of one of the amalgamating states is to be extended to the area of the other amalgamating states except by a competent legislative or other competent authority and further that the law shall be construed as restricted to the territories within each state immediately before the reorganization - Thus, Madras Act cannot apply in the State of Andhra at least in so far as the Presidency Town mentioned in s. 2(1)(i) is concerned - The Act is not extended to the area known as Telengana area and, till extended, s. 119 of the States Reorganisation Act expressly prohibits an extension to the Telengana area by judicial construction - Appeal allowed.
