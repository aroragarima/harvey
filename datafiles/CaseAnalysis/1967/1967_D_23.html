Johrimal v Director of Consolidation of Holdings, Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 119; AIR 1967 SC 1568; 1967 (69) PLR 824; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948, ss. 42, 36, 19 and 20 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, - Scheme for consolidation of land holdings in village - Power of consolidation officers of state - Procedural requirements - (A) Whether power of State Govt. under s. 42 was controlled by procedure prescribed under s. 36 if it involved a variation of confirmed scheme? - Power conferred on State Govt. u/s. 42 of Act of 1948 is a separate power independent of s. 36 of Act which deals with power of authority confirming scheme - Scheme of consolidation can be varied by State Govt. u/s. 42 of Act except in accordance with s. 36 of Act- When a scheme is to be varied by State Govt. itself under s. 42 of Act, there is no requirement of statute that varied scheme should be published, for State Govt. is required to give notice and to give an opportunity to interested parties to be heard before variation is made - Held, power conferred on State Govt. by s. 42 is not controlled by s. 36 and procedure of publication and hearing objections contemplated by ss. 19 and 20 of Act is not necessary - (B)Whether order of Director D/- 08-03-1957 is illegal because it violates s. 18(c) of Act r/w. Rule 16(ii)? - Power of State Govt. to make reservation of land for common purposes under s. 42 is coterminus with power of Consolidation Officer under s. 18(c) - Consolidation Officer has power under u/s. 18( c) to take land out of common pool of village only according to rateable share from proprietors and other right holders for any common purpose including extension of village abadi - Further held, order of Director D/- 08-03-1957 is illegal and ultra vires and must be quashed by grant of a writ in nature of certiorari under art. 226 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
