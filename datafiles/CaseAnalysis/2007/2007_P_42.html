Sumtibai and Other v Paras Finance Company Regd. Partnership Firm Beawer (Rajasthan) Through Mankanwar (D) and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1393; (2007) 10 SCC 82; AIR 2007 SC 3166; 2007 (69) ALR 497; 2007 (4) AWC 4101; JT 2007 (11) SC 479; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 733; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 524; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 471; 2008 (104) RD 80; 2007(11) SCALE 496; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Application For Impleadment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.1, r. 10, O. 22, r. 4(2) - Suit for specific performance of contract - Death of a party - Filing of additional written statement by LRs (Appellants) - Whether it is allowable? - Held, prima facie LRs found to be co-owners of property in dispute - Entitled to file defense by way of additional written statement - LRs cannot be disallowed to file additional written statement merely because of rejection of some applications earlier - Allowing LRs to be impleaded but not allowing them to file an additional written statement will clearly violate natural justice - It cannot be laid down as an absolute proposition that whenever a suit for specific performance is filed by A against B, a third party C can never be impleaded in that suit - If C can show a fair semblance of title or interest he can certainly file an application for impleadment - Taking a contrary view would lead to multiplicity of proceedings because then C will have to wait until a decree is passed against B, and then file a suit for cancellation of decree on ground that A had no title in property in dispute - Impugned orders of High Court and Trial Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
