Madhavi Amma & Ors. v S. Prasannakumari & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 177; (2013) 4 SCC 77; AIR 2013 SC 1384; JT 2013 (8) SC 85; 2013 (2) KLT 114; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 181; 2014 (1) RCR(Rent) 244; 2013(4) SCALE 644; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Kerala Rent Control Act, 1963, Rights of kudikidappukaran

Summary: Rent Control - Kerala Rent Control Act, 1963, s. 125 (3) - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, ss. 80, 80A, 80B and 102 - Eviction - Rights of kudikidappukaran - Maintainability - Respondents landlord filed petition for eviction of tenant, sub-tenant and other occupants under the provisions of the 1965 Act - When that eviction petition was pending, at the instance of one of the tenants, who was predecessor of appellant(s) filed a petition u/s. 125 (3) of the 1963 Act by which predecessor of appellant(s) claimed rights as a Kudikidappukaran - Matter was referred to Land Tribunal - Tenant also filed an application u/s. 80B of the 1963 Act for purchase of Kudikidappu under his occupation of the lands before the Land Tribunal - Tribunal returned a finding in the Reference made by the learned Rent Control Authority to the effect that the predecessor-in-interest of appellant(s) did not possess any Kudikidappu rights - In the application filed u/s. 80B of the Act also such a claim came to the rejected - Rent Controller held that tenant's right as a Kudikidappukaran was not maintainable and thereafter the eviction petition was also ordered on merits in favour of the respondent - Aggrieved on appeal against the rejection of the application u/s. 80B of the 1963 Act Appellate Authority allowed the appeal - As against the order of Rent Control Authority an appeal was preferred before Rent Control Appellate Authority was dismissed - DB passed the impugned order confirming the order of eviction and also set aside the order of the Appellate Authority - What was the scheme of the Act in regard to the decision as to the status of a person as Kudikidappukaran, his rights and entitlements on the one hand and the effect of the decision of the Civil Court or any other authority in deciding an issue relating to the rights of a landlord as against a tenant in which any question was raised by tenant claiming rights as a Kudikidappukaran - Held, in contradistinction to s. 80, what was provided u/ss. 80A or 80B were the consequential benefits such as the right to purchase the Kudikidappu and the procedure to be followed for effecting the purchase by approaching concerned authorities and thereby ascertain his ownership rights after such purpose - By no stretch of imagination, right to purchase provided u/s. 80A and the procedure prescribed for purchase of such right u/s. 80B could be invoked, by a person whose status as Kudikidappukaran was yet to be ascertained earlier - Approach made by appellant(s) by invoking s. 80B of the Act in order to assert his right as Kudikidappukaran even without getting his status ascertained in the appropriate proceedings u/s. 80 of the 1963 Act was wholly invalid and was rightly rejected by original authority in its order - Unfortunately, Appellate Authority that decided the appeal as against the said order failed to understand the scope, power and jurisdiction of Appellate power u/s. 102 of the 1963 Act as against the order passed u/s. 80B of the 1963 Act which unfortunately resulted in the passing of the order dt. 13-11-1995 - It had to be stated in uncontroverted terms that the said order of the Appellate Authority was, therefore, wholly without any jurisdiction and was not in tune with the powers vested with the said Appellate Authority u/s. 102 of the 1963 Act while examining the order passed u/s. 80B of the 1963 Act - It had to be stated that the said order was far in excess of the jurisdiction vested in the said authority and, therefore, the said order was rightly set aside by the DB - Rent Control Authority decided the eviction petition on the merits of ground of eviction, namely, the alleged default in payment of rent, necessity for demolition and re-construction and the claim for own-occupation - In those aspects, as the conclusion was arrived at by the Rent Control Court based on a detailed consideration of the merits which were mainly based on facts and in the absence of any legal error in the said conclusion arrived at by the Rent Control Authority as well as the Rent Control Appellate Authority - There was no scope to find fault with the ultimate decision of the DB in dismissing the revision preferred by appellant(s) - Impugned judgment of DB was justified - Appeals dismissed.
