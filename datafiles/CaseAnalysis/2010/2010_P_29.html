Patai @ Krishna Kumar v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 252; (2010) 4 SCC 429; AIR 2010 SC 2254; JT 2010 (3) SC 536; 2010(3) SCALE 496; 2010 (2) UC 885

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Allegedly, appellants shot dead the deceased at the railway station - Trial Court convicted all the accused persons u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the order of conviction and sentence - Hence, present appeal - (A) It was submitted that two separate First Information Reports (FIR) lodged with the police on the same date - Alleged FIR stated to have been lodged by the Assistant Station Master was a telephonic message - Held, a message or communication to be qualified to be a FIR, there must be something in the nature of a complaint or accusation or at least some information of the crime given with the object of setting the police or criminal law into motion - A cryptic message recording an occurrence cannot be termed as a FIR - In the present case, alleged report was cryptic and without any details regarding the manner in which the incident had taken place or mentioning the name of the deceased - Considering the contents of the said message, it cannot be said that there was any possibility of recording a FIR on the basis of said message - Contention without merit - (B) Conviction - Held, evidence adduced by P.W. 1 and P.W. 3 clearly corroborate each other with respect to the fact that the appellants shot dead the deceased - A pre-concerted mind and a common intention to commit the offence are apparent on the face of the record - Prosecution was able to establish by leading cogent and reliable evidence, the guilt of appellants - Conviction and sentence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, IPC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
