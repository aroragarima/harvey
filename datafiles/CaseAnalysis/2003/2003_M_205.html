Commissioner of Sales Tax and others v Messrs Subhash and Company

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 189; (2003) 3 SCC 454; AIR 2003 SC 1628; 2003(2) SCALE 317; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 44; 2003 (2) SLT 280; 2003 (130) STC 97; 2003 (2) Supreme 842

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Rules, 1959, Madhya Pradehs General Salestax Act, 1958

Summary: Indirect Tax - M P General Sales Tax Act, 1958 - M P General Sales Tax Rules, 1959 - Rule 63 - Certificate of registration - Application for cancellation - Allowed - Re-assessment in respect of previous assessments years on ground of escapement - Notices issued and re-assessment done - Service of notice done by affixture because of non-availability of respondent/assessee - Notice and orders made challenged on ground that they never reached respondent/assessee - Challenge whether proper? - Held, (i) Non-issue of notice or mistake in the issue of notice or defective service of notice does not affect the jurisdiction of the assessing officer, if otherwise reasonable opportunity of being heard has been given; (ii) Issue of notice as prescribed in the Rules constitutes a part of reasonable opportunity of being heard (iii) If prejudice has been caused by non-issue or invalid services of notice the proceeding would be vitiated But irregular service of notice would not render the proceedings invalid: more so, if assessee by his conduct has rendered service impracticable or impossible (iv) In a given case when the principles of natural justice are stated to have been violated it is open to the appellate authority in appropriate cases to set aside the order and require the Assessing Official to decide the case de novo - Learned Single Judge was justified in directing de novo assessment by an order of remand - Appeal allowed.
