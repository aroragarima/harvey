Â Sawatram Ramprasad Mills Company Limited v Baliram Ukandaji and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 264; AIR 1966 SC 616; 1965 (11) FLR 394; 1966 (1) LLJ 41; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 764

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, s. 33C(1) - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25J(2) - C P and Berar Industrial Disputes (Settlement) Act, 1947 - (A) Whether compensation for lay offs can be determied in accordance with Industrial Disputes Act instead of C P and Berar Industrial Disputes (Settlement) Act, 1947? - Held, compensation for lay off can only be determined under Chap.V-A of Industrial Disputes Act, 1956 - This follows from s. 25J(2) as it is so stated there - (B) Whether claim which can be brought before Govt. or its delegate u/s 33C(1) must always be for a predetermined sum? - Further held, this argument has been considered on more than one occasion and it was rejected in light of Kays Construction Co. Pvt. Ltd v State of U P and Ors. (1964 Indlaw SC 45) - Appeal dismissed.
