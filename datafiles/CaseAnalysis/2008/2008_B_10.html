State of Kerala v B. Renjith Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 918; (2008) 12 SCC 219; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 465; 2008 (4) AWC 3567; 2008 (3) ESC 424; 2008 (118) FLR 322; JT 2008 (8) SC 559; 2008 (3) KLT 33; 2008 (3) LLJ 853; 2008 (3) LLN 508; 2008(9) SCALE 557; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 1078; 2009 (2) SLR 211; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 121

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Kerala Amendment) Act

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 39(d), 136, 233, 234 and 236 - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 , ss. 7 and 7A - Judicial Services - Equal pay for equal work - Revised pay scales recommended by the National Judicial Commission (Shetty Commission) - Whether Presiding Officers of the Industrial Tribunals are entitled to the same revised scale as one for the members of the Higher Judicial Services - On the ground that prior to 1998 they were getting the same scale at par with the District Judges - No qualitative difference in the nature of functions or duties attached to the categories of posts - This parity having been recognised by the State Government for over 30 years - High Court directed to treat the writ petitioners at par with the District Judges in the matter of time scale as well as the selection grade - Held, mere fact that the Constitution refers to the appointment of District Judges alone cannot, by itself, be a good ground for treating them differently - Having regard to the Constitutional mandate of equality and inhibition against discrimination in arts. 14 and 16, in service jurisprudence, the doctrine of 'equal pay for equal work' has assumed the status of fundamental right - Supreme Court decline interference in as much as no illegality, infirmity or error of jurisdiction shown - Appeal dismissed.
