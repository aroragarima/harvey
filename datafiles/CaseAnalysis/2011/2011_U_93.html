Shanker Raju v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 11; (2011) 2 SCC 132; 2011 (8) ADJ 79; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 69; 2011 (1) AWC 584; 2011 (271) E.L.T. 492; 2011 (1) ESC 83; JT 2011 (1) SC 49; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 181; 2011(1) SCALE 70; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1; 2011 (1) SLR 621; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 342

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Administrative Tribunals (Amendment) Act, 2006

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Administrative Tribunals (Amendment) Act, 2006, ss. 8, 10A - Judicial Member of Central Administrative Tribunal (CAT) - Reappointment - Entitlement - Petitioner was appointed as a Judicial Member of the CAT - After completion of petitioner's five-year term, he was reappointed for another term of five years and was due to complete his second term of five years on 09-12-2010 - Petitioner again applied to the vacancy of Judicial Member in CAT advertised on April, 2010 - Respondent/Authority rejected petitioner's application, vide impugned communication dated 12-08-2010, on the premises that the petitioner had already completed 10 years of tenure on the same post - Hence the present petition challenging the impugned communication - Petitioner contended that after completion of a tenure of 10 years, he was eligible to apply for the post afresh and must be considered on merits for his appointment as a Member of the Tribunal and should not be disqualified for appointment merely because he had completed 10 years in that office - Whether the petitioner entitled to get fresh appointment as Judicial Member in CAT - Held, reference of s. 8 in the proviso to s. 10A of Amendment Act clearly provides that the Chairman and Members appointed prior to the Amendment Act on completion of either their term of service or on attainment of 65 years in the case of Chairman or 62 years in the case of Members of the Tribunal, whichever is earlier, may be considered for fresh appointment - If they are eligible in terms of s. 8 of the Amendment Act that only means if a member has not completed 10 years term as a member of the Tribunal, he is eligible for fresh appointment, provided he has not completed 65 years of age - Proviso makes it abundantly clear that such fresh appointment could be done provided they satisfy the criteria prescribed under the amended s. 8 of the Act and further, it is made subject to the condition that the total term of office of the Chairman shall not exceed 5 years and that of the Member, ten years - Words of the amendment, on their plain reading, are sufficient to hold that the term of office of a Member of a Tribunal is 10 years and after completion of 10 years, he does not superannuate but he goes out of the office - Language of s. 10A is plain and unambiguous - Further, constitutional validity of the proviso to s. 10A pertaining to the eligibility of a Member for being considered for a fresh appointment after completing his term of office as a member was specifically pleaded in A.K. Behra v. Union of India, (2010) 5 SCALE 472, and the constitutional validity of the said proviso has been upheld by the said decision in para 16 of the Judgment - Further, allowing petitioner's stand would lead to a situation where a person who has been a Member of the Tribunal for 10 years would have to start at the bottom of the ladder as a fresh appointee and that would lead to an anomalous situation where a person who would have presided over a Bench in the Tribunal for years, would suddenly become the junior Member on the same Bench - Therefore, there is no merit in the present writ petition - Petition dismissed.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Judicial Process - Doctrine of binding precedent - Scope - Held, doctrine of binding precedent has the merit of promoting certainty and consistency in judicial decisions - Pronouncement of law by a larger Bench of the SC is binding on a DB of SC, especially where the particular determination by SC not only disposes of the case, but also decides a principle of law - Further, it would be inappropriate to re-agitate the very issue or a particular provision, which SC had already considered and upheld - Petition dismissed.
