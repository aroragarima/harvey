S. Varadarajan v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 177; AIR 1965 SC 942; 1965 CRLJ 33; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 40; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Abandon

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 361 and 363 - College girl nearly a major - She telephones accused, meets him and goes to Sub Registrar's Office along with him for registering marriage agreement - No threat or inducement on part of accused - Whether situation where minor abandons custody on her own volition comes within ambit of s. 361 of IPC? - Held, no - Take out of keeping of lawful guardian - Girl continues to be in fathers guardianship even when she has been kept by her father at somebody else's house - Taking or enticing away a minor out of keeping of a lawful guardian is an essential ingredient of offence of kidnapping - No suggestion that act was done by force or blandishment or anything like that on part of accused - It is clear from evidence that insistence of marriage came from her side, accused by complying with her wishes can by no stretch of imagination be said to have 'taken' her out of keeping of her lawful guardianship, that is, father - She willingly accompanied him - Distinction between 'taking' and allowing a minor to accompany a person - Two expressions are not synonymous though it cannot be laid down that in no conceivable circumstances can two be regarded as meaning same thing for purposes of s. 361 - She had voluntarily joined accused - It could not be said that accused had taken her away from protection of her lawful guardian within meaning of 361 of Code - Appeal allowed.
