Indus Mobile Distribution Private Limited v Datawind Innovations Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 281; (2017) 7 SCC 678; AIR 2017 SC 2105; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 120; 2017 (3) BC 54; 2017 (3) Bom.C.R. 726; 2017 (2) CompLJ 401; 2017 (4) CTC 213; JT 2017 (4) SC 338; 2017 (2) KLT 665; 2017 (3) RCR(Civil) 256; 2017(5) SCALE 60

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitral Tribunal, Jurisdiction, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Conciliation, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 9,11 - Exclusive jurisdiction - Parties entered into agreement dt.25-10-2014 (Agreement) and later, as disputes arose between parties, respondent no.1 invoked cl.18 of the Agreement and appointed Sole Arbitrator - However, appellant objected to appointment of said Arbitrator and also averments made in notice were denied in toto - Later, respondent no.1 filed petition u/s.9 of the Act praying for various reliefs and also filed another petition u/s.11 of the Act for appointment of Arbitrator - HC vide impugned judgment held that exclusive jurisdiction clause would not apply on facts, as the courts in Mumbai would have no jurisdiction at all and that since Delhi Court was the first Court that was approached, it would have jurisdiction in the matter and proceeded to confirm interim order dt.22-9-2015 - Further, court also appointed Sole Arbitrator in the proceedings and conduct of arbitration is taken to be at Mumbai - Hence, instant appeal by appellant contending that even though no part of cause of action arose at Mumbai, yet the seat of arbitration being at Mumbai, courts in Mumbai would have exclusive jurisdiction in all proceedings - Whether courts at Mumbai alone have jurisdiction in respect of disputes arising under the agreement.

Held, as seat of arbitration is Mumbai and also cl.19 of the Agreement vested jurisdiction exclusively in Mumbai courts, only courts at Mumbai would have jurisdiction to the exclusion of all other courts in the country. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside. However, to enable respondents to take necessary steps u/s.9 in Mumbai court, injunction confirmed by impugned judgment would continue for period of four weeks from date of instant judgment. Appeals disposed of.
