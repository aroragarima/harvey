State Of Punjab v Jasbir Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 869; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1; (1996) 1 SCC 288; AIR 1995 SCW 4426; 1996 (1) CCR 51; 1995 (4) Crimes 765; JT 1995 (9) SC 308; 1995(6) SCALE 740; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Acquittal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 50 - Mandatory requirements - Not complied with - Trial judge did not accept evidence of police officers on ground that it was open to them, at time of search, to take in writing from accused that they had no objection to search being conducted and that they were informed of their right and they waived search in presence of a gazetted officer - Held, evidence collected in breach of mandatory requirement does not become inadmissible - View expressed by trial judge cannot be said to be unwarranted or unjustified - Order of acquittal confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
