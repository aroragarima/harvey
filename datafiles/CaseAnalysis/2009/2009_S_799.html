Ram Phal v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1257; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 72; (2009) 3 SCC 258; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1410; 2009 (4) AWC 3921; 2009 (121) FLR 735; 2009(2) SCALE 285

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appellant working as constable filed petition against respondents to grant salary and to revise pay - Held, High Court without examining any one of the issues raised dismissed the writ petition - In Raj Kishore Jha vs. State of Bihar and Ors. 2003 INDLAW SC 819, u/a 226 or 227 of Constitution, while dismissing a plaint, court has to give the reason, "Reason is the heartbeat of every conclusion, without the same, it becomes lifeless - Matter remitted to HC for fresh consideration - Appeal disposed of.
