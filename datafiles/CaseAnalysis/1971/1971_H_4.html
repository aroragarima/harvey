Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation of The City of v Haji Abdulgafur Haji Hussenbhai

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 683; (1971) 1 SCC 757; AIR 1971 SC 1201; [1971] S.C.R. 63; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, s. 141 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3 and 100 - CPC, 1908, O. 21, r. 94 - (A) Muncipalities - Enforcement of charge against property - Property being in the hands of transferee for consideration without notice - (B) Question of constructive knowledge or notice - Determination of - (C) Purchaser at acution sale - Applicability of doctrine caveat emptor - Held, the Municipality cannot by virtue of s.141of 1949 Act enforce its charge against the property in the hands of a transferee for consideration without notice - Question of constructive knowledge or notice has to be determined on the facts and circumstances of each case and has to be approached from equitable considerations - Purchaser at acution sale takes the property subject to all the defects of title and the doctrine caveat emptor applies to such purchaser - Appeal Dismissed.
