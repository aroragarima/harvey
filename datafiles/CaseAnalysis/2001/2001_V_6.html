Vasantha Viswanathan and Others v V. K. Elayalwar and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19782; (2001) 8 SCC 133; AIR 2001 SC 3367; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 107; 2002 AllCJ 642; 2001 (45) ALR 206; 2001 (3) ARBLR 110; 2001 (4) AWC 2789; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 220; JT 2001 (6) SC 622; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 469; 2001(5) SCALE 483; 2001 (6) SLT 150; 2001 (6) Supreme 442; 2001 (2) UC 464

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: (1) Sale of Goods Act, 1930- Section 19-Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 (since repealed)-Section 31- Transfer of Motor Vehicles-When takes place?-Section 19 of Sale of Goods Act to apply-And property in vehicle to pass to defendant No. 1 at such time as parties to contract intend it to be transferred-Parties intending that after registration formalities price of vehicles covered by permits to be ascertained-And thereafter same to be paid by defendant No. 1 entitling him to take possession of vehicles-Thus, parties intending property in vehicles to pass only after possession of vehicles delivered after completion of aforesaid formalities - After completion of registration formalities value of vehicles covered by permits not ascertained, much less paid- Rather possession forcibly taken by defendant No. 1-Hence, property in vehicles not passing to first defendant as required by Section 19 of Sale of Goods Act-As defendant No. 1 took illegal possession-High Court right in holding that plaintiff entitled to profits earned by first defendant and defendant Nos. 4 to 7 for plying vehicles.

(2) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order XLI, Rule 27-Appeal-Additional evidence-Admissibility-Document sought be filed as additional evidence not put to plaintiff deposing as witness in suit-Copy produced not certified copy-Hence, additional evidence rightly refused by High Court to be admitted.
