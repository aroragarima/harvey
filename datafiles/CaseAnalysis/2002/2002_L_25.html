State of Rajasthan and Others v Lata Arun

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1736; (2002) 6 SCC 252; (2002) SCC (L&S) 859; AIR 2002 SC 2642; 2002 (48) ALR 493; JT 2002 (5) SC 210; 2002(5) SCALE 233; 2002 (4) SLR 775; 2002 (4) SLT 424; 2002 (5) Supreme 33; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2001

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions, Eligibility - Qualifications

Summary: Education - Indian Medicine Central Council Act, 1970 - Qualification for admission - Whether respondent had eligibility qualification for admission in general nursing and midwifery and staff nurse course? - Prescribed eligibility qualification for admission to a course or for recruitment to or promotion in service are matters to be considered by appropriate authority, not courts - Held, on date of submission of application for joining course respondent did not possess prescribed qualification - Respondent as a candidate did not possess prescribed educational qualification - Appeal allowed.
