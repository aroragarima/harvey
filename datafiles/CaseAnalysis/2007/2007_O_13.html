Manjuri Bera v Oriental Insurance Company Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 283; (2007) 10 SCC 643; 2007 (2) ACC 365; AIR 2007 SC 1474; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 55; 2007 (68) ALR 148; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2395; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 128; JT 2007 (5) SC 78; 2008 (2) KLT 873; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 906; 2007 (146) PLR 611; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 674; 2007(5) SCALE 193; 2007 (4) SCJ 760; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 590; 2007 (2) UC 898

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: West Bengal Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Appeal against the judgement of High Court allowing claim petition of married daughter under Section 166 of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 but disentitling compensation - Held, even if there is no loss of dependency the claimant if he or she is a legal representative will be entitled to compensation, the quantum of which shall be not less than the liability flowing from Section 140 of the Act - Appeal allowed.
