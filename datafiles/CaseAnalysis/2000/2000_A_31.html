Food Corporation of India, Hyderabad and Others v A. Prahalada Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2804; (2001) 1 SCC 165; (2001) SCC (L&S) 186; AIR 2001 SC 51; 2001 (1) AWC 96; 2001 (1) CLR 294; 2000 (87) FLR 899; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 266; 2001 LabIC 23; 2002 (4) LLJ 791; 2001 (1) LLN 808; 2000(7) SCALE 286; 2001 (2) SLJ 204; 2000 (5) SLR 600; 2000 (7) SLT 685; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 330; 2000 (7) Supreme 426

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971, regns. 54, 58, 59 and 60 - Whether regn. 60 of Food Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1971, prescribes holding of detailed enquiry by the disciplinary authority before imposing minor penalty as provided in regn. 54 in the case where the employee disputes his liability after receipt of the show-cause notice? - Regn. 60(1)(b) mandates the disciplinary authority to form its opinion whether it is necessary to hold inquiry in a particular case or not, but that would not mean that in all cases where an employee disputes his liability, a full-fledged inquiry should be held, if the discretion given u/regn. 60(1)(b) is misused or is exercised in an arbitrary manner, it is open to the employee to challenge the same before the appropriate forum - Held, the High Court erroneously interpreted the regulation, as per the regulation, holding of regular departmental enquiry is a discretionary power of the disciplinary authority which is to be exercised by considering the facts of each case and if it is misused or used arbitrarily, it would be subject to judicial review - Appeal partly allowed.
