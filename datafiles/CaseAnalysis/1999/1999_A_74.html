Allahabad Bank and Others v Bengal Paper Mills Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1099; (1999) 4 SCC 383; AIR 1999 SC 1715; [1999] 96 Comp Cas 804; 1999 (3) CompLJ 287; JT 1999 (3) SC 168; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, s. 457 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 273 - Winding up - Sale of assets and properties of company - Unsecured creditors - Valuation of properties suspicious - No adequate publicity of sale - Sale conducted with undue haste for inadequate price - Sale had not procured best possible price - Purchaser allowed to take possession of assets and properties on very next day of sale and to incur expenditure after sale - Held, are not ground to set aside sale especially when appeals against sale were filed within limitation - Re-employment of 1700 ex-employees of company by purchaser - Would not over weigh interests of unsecured creditors.
