C. O. Arumugam and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 157; (1991) Supp2 SCC 199; 1991 (17) ATC 402; JT 1989 (4) SC 377

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Tamil Nadu State of Subordinate services Rules 1955

Summary: Service - Tamil Nadu State and Subordinate Services Rules, rr. 4 and 39(d) - Promotion - Right thereto - Temporary promotion to cadre of Deputy Transport Commissioners - Respondents not considered for reason that disciplinary proceedings or criminal cases were pending against them - Thus their promotion cases was overlooked by Govt. - Whether such non-consideration proper? - Held, every civil servants has a right to have his case considered for promotion according to his turn and it is a guarantee flowing from art. 14 and 16(1) of Constitution of India, 1950 - Consideration of promotion could be postponed only on reasonable grounds - Uniform principles should be followed to avoid arbitrariness - Promotion of persons against whom charge has been framed in disciplinary proceedings or charge-sheet has been filed in criminal case may be deferred till proceedings are concluded - They must however be considered for promotion if they are exonerated or acquitted from charges - If found suitable, they shall then be given promotion with retrospective effect from date on which their juniors were promoted - Appropriate directions issued in each respondent's case - In respect of respondent no. 3 it had come out that he was exonerated from criminal case, thus Court directed that if he has been acquitted, he must be considered for promotion from date on which his junior was promoted and if he is found suitable, he should be promoted with all consequential benefits - Similar directions issued in respect of rest of respondents - Appeals disposed of.
