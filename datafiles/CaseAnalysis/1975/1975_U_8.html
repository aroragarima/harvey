Lachmi Narain Etc. Etc. v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 283; (1976) SCC (Tax) 213; (1976) 2 SCC 953; AIR 1976 SC 714; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 785; 1976 (37) STC 267; 1976 TAXLR 1467

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Withdrawal, Practice, Amending Act, 1903

Summary: Union Territories (Laws) Act, 1950, s. 2 - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 6(2)- Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - (A) Notification D/- 07-12-1957 under 1950 Act - Purporting to amend s. 6(2) of 1941 Act, as extended to Delhi - Invalid - Statutory limit to powers of Central Govt. u/s. 2 - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Provision whether mandatory or directory - Rule of construction - Held, power exhausts itself on extension of the enactment and it cannot be excercised repeatedly or subsequently to such extension - Notification transgresses limits firstly as the power has not been exercised contemporaneously with the extension or for the purposes of the extension of the Bengal Act to Delhi and as the alteration purports to change the essential features of s. 6(2) and the legislative policy inherent therein - Appeal Allowed.
