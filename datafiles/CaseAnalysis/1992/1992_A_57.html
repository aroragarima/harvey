State of Karnataka v Appa Balu Ingale and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 205; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1762; (1995) Supp4 SCC 469; AIR 1993 SC 1126; 1992 (3) Crimes 1104; 1993 CRLJ 1029; 1993 CRLR 72; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 588; 1992(3) SCALE 339; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 284

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 17 - Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, ss. 1, 4 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1973, s. 401 - Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955 - Harijans stopped from taking water from well on ground of they being untouchables with threat of using gun - Revisionary powers of High Court as to concurrent findings - Held, although ordinarily HC do not interfere by reappreciating evidence but in instant case an offence u/s. 4 of Act is made out - And court has to keep in mind constitutional goals and purpose of Act and interpret provisions of Act in light thus shed, to annihilate untouchability - Appeal allowed.
