G.D.A. v Delhi Auto & General Finance Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1866; (1994) 4 SCC 42; AIR 1994 SC 2263; AIR 1994 SCW 2135; JT 1994 (3) SC 275; 1994(2) SCALE 357; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 248; 1994 (2) UJ 497; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1373

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: National Capital Region Planning Board Act, 1985

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - National Capital Region Planning Board Act, 1985, s. 27 - U.P. Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973, ss. 13, 15 and 57 - (A) Bye-laws under 1973 Act - Draft bye-laws awaiting approval of State Govt. - Authority following procedure indicated therein - Sanction of a plan under certain draft bye-law - (B) Constitution, art. 14 - Doctrine of reasonable expectation - Applicability - Original land use for certain area in Master plan amended from recreational to residential - Original use restored subsequently -Claim that first amendment gave rise to expectation that they could construct colonies in area owned by them - If justified - (C) Right to construct - Amendment of permissible land use and restoration of original use subsequently - If first amendment of use confers any right on owners of land in area to construct - Held, (A) Are not bye-laws framed u/s. 57- Cannot be deemed a sanction - (B) Not justified - Doctrine confers no independent right - (C) Does not confer any right to construct - Appeal allowed.
