New India Assurance Company Limite v C.M. Jaya and others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S.S.M. Quadri, U.C. Banerjee, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 27; (2002) 2 SCC 278; (2002) SCC (Cr) 325; 2002 (1) ACC 299; AIR 2002 SC 651; 2002 (1) AllCJ 175; 2002 (47) ALR 197; 2002 (1) AWC 588; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 6; 2002 (1) CRC 298; 2002 (95) DLT 500; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 383; JT 2002 (1) SC 198; 2002 (1) KLT 596; 2002 (2) MahLJ 6; 2002 (1) MPLJ 578; 2002 (131) PLR 171; 2002(1) SCALE 183; 2002 (1) SCJ 348; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 298; 2002 (1) SLT 290; 2002 (1) Supreme 170; 2002 (1) UC 283

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act (4 of 1939), S. 95(2) - Liability of insurer - Non-payment of higher premium for higher liability - Insurer liable only to the extent limited under section 95(2) - Not liable for entire amount of compensation as awarded.

In the case of Insurance Company not taking any higher liability by accepting a higher premium for payment of compensation to a third party, the insurer would be liable to the extent limited under section 95(2) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 and would not be liable to pay the entire amount. The liability of the appellant-Insurance Company limited to Rs. 50,000/-, as held by the Accident Claims Tribunal. (1995) 2 SCC 539, Approved. No conflict between (1995) 2 SCC 539 and (1998) 3 SCC 744. (Paras 14 and 17)
