Kulwant Singh and others v Oriental Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 736; (2015) 2 SCC 186; 2014 (4) ACC 640; 2014 ACJ 2873; 2015(1) ALL MR 481; 2014 (6) AWC 6045; 2014 (6) Bom.C.R. 531; JT 2014 (12) SC 110; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4538; 2015 (4) MahLJ 61; 2015 (3) MPLJ 1; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 977; 2014(12) SCALE 356; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 160

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Accidental claim - Breach of insurance policy - Recovery rights - Entitlement - Claim petition was filed before the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) by the dependents of the deceased who died in a road accident - Tribunal held that the death was on account of negligence of the driver of the offending Tempo and the claimants were entitled to compensation - Vehicle was insured with the Insurance Company - On appeal, HC held that there was a breach of the policy condition and driver of the vehicle did not have a valid and effective driving licence at the time of the accident - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Insurance Company is entitled to recovery rights on the ground of breach of conditions of insurance policy when the driver possesses valid driving licence for driving light vehicle but fails to obtain endorsement for driving goods vehicle.

Held, HC committed grave error of law in holding that the insurer is not liable to pay compensation because the driver was not holding the licence to drive the commercial vehicle. There was no breach of any condition of insurance policy, entitling the Insurance Company to recovery rights. S. Iyyapan v. United India Insurance Company Limited and Another, 2013 Indlaw SC 377, relied on. Appeals allowed.
