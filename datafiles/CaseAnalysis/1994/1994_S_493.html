State of Orissa and Others v Dipitimala Patra and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1458; (1995) Supp4 SCC 49; 1996 (32) ATC 68

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Regularisation - Whether State Public Service Commission was right in holding that respondents were entitled to regularisation of their services and obtained interim stay for continuance of service? - Regular appointees alone have right to be accommodated in regular posts - There was no warrant to obligate State to create additional posts and responsibility to fill respondents in manner suggested - Held, respondents, unless wanted by State to continue, would have to vacate their posts unless already vacated - This order would not stand in way of State otherwise in some manner accommodating respondents, or such of them as can be accommodated on compassionate ground - Appeals allowed.
