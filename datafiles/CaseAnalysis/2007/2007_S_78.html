Coal India Limited And Others v Saroj Kumar Mishra

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 441; (2007) 9 SCC 625; AIR 2007 SC 1706; 2008(1) ALL MR 458; 2007 (6) AWC 6122; 2007 (104) CutLT 569; 2008 (1) ESC 111; JT 2007 (6) SC 6; 2007 (3) LLN 601; 2007(5) SCALE 724; 2007 (6) SCJ 532; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 233; 2007 (4) SLR 75; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1797

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Service

Keywords: Reduction Of Pay

Summary: Appeal against the judgment of High Court wherein the respondents were granted notional promotion with effect from the date their juniors were promoted - Respondents on the premise that the General Manager (Vigilance) intimated to the concerned authority that vigilance cases were pending against them- Interpretation of the office memorandums dated 27.6.1979 and 8.1.1981 - Held, rules operating in the field do not provide that only because some allegations have been made as against an officer of the company, the same would itself justify keeping a valuable right to be considered for promotion of an employee in abeyance - Appeal dismissed
