Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v S. Raman Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 1965 SC 1031; [1965] 55 ITR 630; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 883

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, s.22 - Failure to pay returns - Sanction - Taxable Limit - Invalid notice - Confusion - Respondent/assessee was a Hindu undivided family and has not filed returns u/s.22 of Act for a/ys 1944-45 and 1945-46- Income Tax Officer issued notices u/s. 34 for both assessment years - At that time it was not necessary to obtain sanction of Commissioner of Income Tax and none was obtained - Assessee filed a return for the assessment year 1944-45 showing an income of Rs. 4,053 which was below the HUF taxable limit of Rs. 7,200 - Determining the net taxable income as Rs.1,20,603 - Assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and then appealed to Appellate Tribunal who allowed the appeal in part and observed that out of a total profit of Rs. 79,760 arising from the sale of certain properties, only Rs. 33,000 was assessable in the assessment year 1945-46 and Rs. 46,760 was assessable in the assessment year 1944-45 - HC answered the question in negative and notwithstanding that return filed by assessee was the result of an invalid notice, the return itself could not be ignored or disregarded by the Department -Department cannot issue a further notice u/s.34(1) (a) of Act on assumption that there had been an omission or failure on the part of the assessee to make a return of his income u/s.22 of Act - Whether s.34 (1) (a) of Act could be invoked by Revenue, affirmed the assessment on ground that the second proviso to s. 34(3) of Act, as amended, applied - Held, assessee had never filed a return u/s.22 of Act - First return was filed in response to notice u/s.34 of Act, but filed this return even without a notice u/s.34 of Act, for the 4 years prescribed by s.34(3) and not expired - A per referred case return showing income below taxable limit was a good return and the Income Tax Officer could not choose to ignore the return and issue a notice u/s.34 of Act - Confusion in branch of Income Tax Law by use of words 'voluntary return' and a' non-voluntary return - S. 22(3) of Act does not use this expression and whatever the impelling cause or motive if a return otherwise valid was filed by an assessee before the receipt of a valid notice u/s.34 of Act, it was to be treated as a return within s.22(3) of Act for it falls within the language of sub-section - Agree with HC that the question referred to HC answered in negative - Appeal allowed.
