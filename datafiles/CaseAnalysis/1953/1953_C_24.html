Commissioner of Income Tax/Excess Profits Tax, Bombay City v Messrs. Bhogilal Laherchand

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 1954 SC 155; 1954 (56) BomLR 280; [1954] 25 ITR 50; 1954 SCJ 122; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4,4(1)(b)(i),12-B,14,18,42, 42(1)(3), 66(1) - Accounts - Assessment - Non-resident - Taxable territory - Hindu undivided family was carrying on business in Bombay, Madras and Mysore, being treated as a single assessee and its relevant accounting period was dt.10-10- 1941 to 8-11- 1942 - During this period, Mysore branch purchased goods from Bombay head office and Madras branch of value of Rs. 2 lakhs odd - Income Tax Officer estimated purchases of Mysore in British India at 3 lakhs and its profits at Rs 75,000 on the sale of these goods in Mysore - S.42 of 1922 Act, half of this profit to extent of Rs.37,500 was deemed to accrue or arise in British India because of the business connection of non-resident branch in British India - Whether Income Tax Officer was justified in applying provisions of s.42 (1) of 1922 Act - Held, Income-tax Officer was right in applying the provisions of s. 42(1) of 1922 Act and holding that Rs.37,500 were deemed to accrue in British India and in including in the assessment a portion - Foreign income of resident will not be applicable to income deemed to accrue within taxable territory - S.42(1) of 1922 Act, would not be liable to assessment regarding income accruing to them in Part 'B' States, even if there was business connection in taxable territory - Only purpose in deleting these words could be to bring residents within ambit of section - Appeal allowed.
