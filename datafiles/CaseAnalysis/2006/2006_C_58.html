Chatar Singh v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 853; (2006) 12 SCC 37; AIR 2007 SC 319; 2007 CRLJ 796; 2006 (3) JCC 2135; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 465; 2006(12) SCALE 381; 2007 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 808; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 31 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 201, 302, 364, 364A, 365, 71 - Sentence to undergo 20 years' Rigorous Imprisonment - Interpretation and application of s.31 of CrPC, 1973 - Held, in view of proviso appended to s.31 HC committed a manifest error in sentencing appellant for 20 years' Rigorous Imprisonment - Maximum sentence imposable being 14 years and having regard to fact that appellant is in custody for more than 12 years - Appellant is directed to be sentenced to period already undergone - Appeal partly allowed.
