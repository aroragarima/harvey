Pawan Kumar @ Monu Mittal and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 173; (2015) 7 SCC 148; AIR 2015 SC 2050; 2015 CRLJ 2418; 2015 (3) JCC 1591; JT 2015 (3) SC 1; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 212; 2015 (3) RLW 2697; 2015(3) SCALE 511

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability, Revolver, Association

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 404, 411, 302 and s. 201 r/w s. 34 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, ss. 30, 25 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant A1 allegedly committed murder of deceased - A2 to A6 were carrying the dead body of the deceased in his car, to dispose of the same at an unknown place - Accused were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 and s. 201 r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced all accused and added additional charged u/ss. 404, 4122 411 IPC and u/ss. 30, 25 of the Act and A1 was sentenced to death - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeals before HC - HC partly allowed the appeal of A1 and modified his death sentence to life imprisonment and appeals of other accused were dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, Court was in full agreement with the Courts below that the accused conspired to commit the offence of murder of the deceased. The nexus between the accused to do away with the deceased, has been established by the prosecution beyond all reasonable doubt. A1 being the owner of petrol pump where the incident took place, is an interested party in the crime to do away with the deceased, because at his instance, the supplies were suspended and only upon paying fine, the supplies were restored. A4 and A7 were caught by PW1 Head Constable and another police constable, while they were trying to dispose of the dead body of the deceased in his own car. There were nothing on record to suggest that the accused were unnecessarily implicated by the police. Merely because all the bullets fired from the gun did not hit the target and were not recovered from the scene of offence, is no ground to conclude that the incident did not take place. There were no major contradictions in the prosecution witness statement which require attention and consideration. In instant case, the evidence adduced by the prosecution clearly proves the chain of events connecting the accused to the guilt of the commission of the offence. The entire evidence brought on record by prosecution, is not only convincing, but is also trustworthy. Even if the confession of A 4 and A7 made before PW1 and PW2, which is barred by s. 25 of the 1872 Act, is not taken into account, the other evidence on record adduced by prosecution is sufficient to hold the accused guilty of the offence. Thus, on scrutiny of evidence on record, Court is convinced that prosecution had established beyond reasonable doubt the complete chain of events which points at the guilt of the accused. Hence, in the light of circumstances coupled with the complete chain of events, SC has no manner of doubt to hold that prosecution has succeeded in proving its case against the accused beyond all reasonable doubt. There is no merit in instant appeals which require interference. Appeals dismissed.
