Ashok Kumar Ratilal Patel v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 211; (2012) 7 SCC 757; 2012 (6) AWC 5936; 2012 (134) FLR 755; 2013 (1) G.L.R. 126; 2013 (1) MahLJ 22; 2013 (1) MPLJ 3; 2012(6) SCALE 448; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 545; 2012 (5) SLR 249

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Cancellation of appointment - Legality - Appeal was preferred against the order passed by HC whereby HC dismissed the writ petition and affirmed the order of cancellation of offer of appointment as was issued to the appellant - Appellant contended that the case of the appellant was not a case of transfer on deputation but was a case of appointment on deputation after following all due procedures for appointment and selection - In absence of any illegality in selection, it was not open to the respondents to cancel the offer of appointment - Such action was arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution of India -

Held, instant case was not a case of transfer on deputation - It was a case of appointment on deputation for which advertisement was issued and after due selection, the offer of appointment was issued in favour of the appellant - In such circumstances, it was not open for the respondent to argue that the appellant had no right to claim deputation and the respondent could not refuse to accept the joining of most eligible selected candidate except for ground of unsuitability or unsatisfactory performance - In the advertisement dt. 13-9-2009, the pre-revised scale of pay of the post of Director was shown at Rs.14,300-400-18,300 - It was mentioned that the said pay scale would be revised to the pay band + grade pay of Rs.37,000-67,000 + 8700 - No stipulation was made that a person receiving higher pay of scale or higher qualification was ineligible for appointment on deputation - Once such terms and conditions for deputation was intimated by respondent No.2 to the North Gujarat University, it was for the appellant to decide whether he would accept the scale of pay as was offered or would continue to receive his fixed pay on deputation as per Cl. 3 - Appellant by his letter dt. 20-2-2010, accepted the offer and had shown his desire to join the post of Director, AICTE with the pay as mentioned in the said letter; such acceptance being in consonance with the terms and conditions of deputation and the offer of appointment dt. 15-2-2010, it was not open for respondent No.2 to cancel and withdraw the offer of appointment - Going by the principles, SC was constraint to state that HC failed to appreciate the difference between 'transfer on deputation' and 'appointment on deputation' and erred in holding that the appellant had no right to claim entitlement to the post of Director - As the appellant was selected after due selection and was offered appointment on deputation, and, in absence of any valid ground shown by the respondents, HC held that the appellant had a right to join the post and the respondents were bound to accept his joining - Appeal allowed.
