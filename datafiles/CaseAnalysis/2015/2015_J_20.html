Jasbir Singh @ Javri @ Jabbar Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 239; (2015) 5 SCC 762; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2009; 2015 CRLJ 2476; 2015 (3) JCC 2061; JT 2015 (4) SC 106; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 228; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 523; 2015(4) SCALE 541; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 399, 402 - Benefit of reasonable doubt - Police apprehended six accused and arrested four accused while two others escaped - Further, police recovered country made pistol with two live cartridges from appellant and also recovered knife etc., from other accused and later FIR was lodged - Trial Court held that all six accused were guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 399 and 402 of IPC and appellant and another was found guilty and convicted u/s. 25 of the Act - On appeal, HC affirmed conviction but reduced the sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether prosecution proved charges against appellant beyond reasonable doubt.

Held, HC erred in law in not taking note of facts apparent from evidence on record, such as, firstly, that in day light, there was no public or any other independent witness of arrest of appellant and co-accused from place of incident nor that of the alleged recovery of fire arm said to have been made from two of them; secondly, complainant himself investigated crime and as such, credibility of investigation is doubtful; thirdly, it was not natural that none of accused offered resistance or caused injury to any of police personnel before they were apprehended by police when four of accused were armed with deadly weapons; fourthly, it was strange all accused were wearing blue shirts and lastly, appellant and others had not tried to run away at time of noon when they could have easily noticed that some policemen are coming towards them. Hence, from evidence on record, none of the charges against appellant were proved beyond reasonable doubt by prosecution. Hence, appellant is entitled to benefit of reasonable doubt and so, conviction and sentence is set aside and appellant is acquitted. Appeal allowed.
