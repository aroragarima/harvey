State of Andhra Pradesh v V. Vasudeva Rao

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 997; (2004) 9 SCC 319; (2004) SCC (Cr) 968; AIR 2004 SC 960; AIR 2003 SCW 6555; 2003 (4) CCR 361; 2003 (4) Crimes 519; 2004 CRLJ 620; 2004 (1) JCC 170; JT 2003 (9) SC 11; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 690; 2004 (1) KLT 526; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 917; 2003(9) SCALE 566B; 2003 (1) SCW 6555; 2003 (7) SLT 100; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 500; 2003 (8) Supreme 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Examination Of Accused

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d) - Accused demanded bribe for not transferring the complainant - High Court acquitted the accused reversing the judgment of the Trial Court - Held, when the factual position is examined in the background of legal principles culled out from various decisions of the Court, the inevitable conclusion is that the High Court's judgment is indefensible. The judgment is set aside. The accused has been rightly convicted under s. 161 and s. 5(2), IPC, 1860 r/w s. 5(1)(d) of the Act by the Trial Court - Further held, considering the age of the accused, the sentence is reduced.
