West Bengal State Electricity Board v Gajendra Haldea And Ors

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 428; (2009) 12 SCC 315; 2009(5) SCALE 486; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 838

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity and Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 121, 60, 66 and 111 - Appellate Tribunal for Electricity - Revisional Supervisory Power thereof, u/s. 121 - Whether Tribunal has power to determine tariff in exercise of its revisional supervisory powers u/s. 121 of Act? - In appeal against judgement of Electricity Regulatory Commission, Tribunal gave finding that Commission had no power to determine tariff for trading in electricity, after giving such finding Tribunal issued directions u/ss. 60 and 66 to Commission for fixing trading margins - Contention raised that such direction of Tribunal was uncalled for, on ground that only Regulatory Commissions can invoke s. 60 upon arriving at a finding that a particular licensee or generator had conducted himself in the specified manner which has an adverse effect on competition in the electricity industry further contention was raised that respondent was not having any locus standi in the matter - In support of contentions strong reliance was placed upon 'Grid Corporation of Orissa Ltd. v. Gajendra Haldea, (2008 (11) Scale 313)' - Judgement and provisions perused - Contentions upheld - Judgement applied - Appeal allowed.
