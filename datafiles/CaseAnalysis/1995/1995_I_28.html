Municipal Commissioner of Dum Dum Municipality and Others v Indian Tourism Development Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 905; (1995) 5 SCC 251; 1995 (60) DLT 723; JT 1995 (5) SC 610; 1995(4) SCALE 611B; 1995 (3) SCJ 454; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Land & Property

Keywords: International Airports Authority Act, 1971, Bengal Municipal Act, 1932

Summary: Land & Property - Air Law - International Airports Authority Act, 1971 - Airports Authority of India Act, 1994 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - In the case of the instant appeals, question before the court dealt with the properties so vested in the respondents under the provisions of 1971 Act and taxation policies with regard to that properties - Different HCs took different views regarding the issues - Hence, instant appeals for the consideration of that matter - Whether, the properties vested in the International Airport Authority of India under the provisions of the 1971 Act could be called the properties of the Union of India within the meaning of art. 285 of the Constitution and, therefore, exempt from all taxes imposed by a State or by any authority within a municipality - Held, in the view of provisions of the 1994 Act, the International Airports Authority of India was a statutory corporation distinct from the Central Govt. and that the properties vested in it by s. 12 of the Act could not be said to have been vested in it only for proper management - After the date of vesting, the properties so vested were no longer the properties of the Union of India for the purpose of and within the meaning of art. 285 of the Constitution and hence, the immunity could not be invoked which was created by art. 285(1) of the Constitution - Appeals disposed of.
