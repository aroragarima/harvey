Kishore Amarsingh Maheshkar, Ravindra @ Ravi Bansi Gohar v The State Of Maharashtra & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 535; (1998) 6 SCC 609; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1527; AIR 1998 SC 3031; 1998 (5) Bom.C.R. 920; 1998 (3) CCR 159; 1998 (3) Crimes 107; 1998 CRLJ 4059; 1998 (2) JCC 107; JT 1998 (5) SC 292; 1998 (3) RCR(Criminal) 766; 1998(4) SCALE 381; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 978; 1998 (6) SLT 513; 1998 (6) Supreme 203

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s.9; Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.300 - Murder - Proof - Identification parades - Legitimacy - Eye-witness, police constable - Not disclosing details and complete identity of accused - Not asserting that person named in FIR and before court is same - Showing photograph to witness before identification parade - Identification parade held in lock up - Legality - Held, conviction of accused on basis of sole identification of such witnesses - Not sustainable - Identification parade not valid.
