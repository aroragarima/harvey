Udit Narain Singh Malpaharia v Additional Member, Board of Revenue, Bihar

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 479; AIR 1963 SC 786; 1963 BLJR 512; 1964 (43) ILR(Pat) 13; 1964 (1) SCJ 151; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Board Of Revenue Act, 1913 - (A) Whether in a writ in nature of certiorari filed u/art. 226 of Constitution party or parties in whose favour a tribunal or authority had made an order, which is sought to be quashed, is or are necessary party or parties? - Held, a necessary party is one without whom no order can be made effectively; a proper party is one in whose absence an effective order can be made but whose presence is necessary for a complete and final decision on question involved in proceeding - (B) What is nature of a writ of certiorari? - Certiorari lies to remove for purpose of quashing proceedings of inferior courts of record or other persons or bodies exercising judicial or quasi-judicial functions - Appeal fails.
