Mohan Singh Gill and others Etc. v State of Punjab and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 232; (2015) 8 SCC 345; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 23; 2015(3) ALL MR 906; 2015 (110) ALR 488; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 21; 2015 (3) CTC 758; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1881; 2016 (2) MahLJ 525; 2016 (2) MPLJ 1; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 705; 2015 (128) RD 389; 2015(4) SCALE 196; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquired, Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995, s. 77 - Acquisition of Land - Validity of Notifications - Appellants filed petitions for challenging acquisition of land which were dismissed - As a consequence, validity of the notifications had been upheld, holding that acquisition for public purpose for development of Missing Link-II from Dhandra Road to Sidhwan Canal Ludhiana as well as for development of residential urban estate along proposed road from Dhandra Road to Sidhwan Canal Ludhiana, was just and proper - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, hidden purpose for acquiring 'surplus' land, i.e. the land apart from what is required for constructing Missing Link-II, was to develop it as commercial area (which is not stated in the acquisition notification) so that the finances could be arranged for construction of road or for some other purpose. This cannot be treated as public purpose. In regard to validity of second notification, notification acquiring land for the Missing Link road is for an area of approx. 74.52 acres. Yet, more than 55.41 acres of land has been acquired for adjusting the oustees of the said road. Thus, the acquisition is for a far greater area than what was required even as per the stated public purpose inasmuch as 55.41 acres of land was sought to be given to those from whom 74.52 acres of land was taken. Such an action was arbitrary and illegal which also amounted to depriving the appellants of their livelihood. Attempt was made by the appellants to show that there is vacant agricultural land nearby which is more suitable for the purpose for which appellants land is sought to be acquired. Liberty was given to the appellants to make a suitable representation to the respondents in this behalf within a period of 30 days. Appeals partly allowed.
