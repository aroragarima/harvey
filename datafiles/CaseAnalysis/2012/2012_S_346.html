Sumit Tomar v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 385; (2013) 1 SCC 395; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 4157; 2012 (4) CGLJ 554; 2013 (1) JCC (Narcotics) 6; JT 2012 (10) SC 609; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 948; 2012(10) SCALE 507; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 404

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotics Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8, 15, 60, and 61 - Narcotic substances - Official witnesses - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant (accused) was allegedly transporting contraband and thereby allegedly committed offences u/s. 8, 15, 60, and 61 of the Act - Trial Court (Special Court) convicted appellant u/s. 15 of the Act and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that no independent witness was examined - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, prosecution examined official witnesses (PW1, 2, 3 and 6) - Trial court and HC relied on evidence of official witnesses, forensic report and others documents accepted the case of prosecution - It was better if prosecution examined at least one independent witness to corroborate its case - However, in absence of any animosity between appellant and official witnesses, there was nothing wrong in relying on their statements and accepting the documents placed for basing conviction - It was not disputed that appellant was driving the car in which 2 bags of contraband were loaded - Merely because different punishments were prescribed under the Act depending on quantity of contraband, mixing of two bags, has not caused any prejudice to appellant - Even after taking 2 samples, quantity measured was more than commercial quantity - Impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Appeal dismissed.
