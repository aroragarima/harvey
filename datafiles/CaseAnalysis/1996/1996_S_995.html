P.Kannadasan v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2915; (1996) 5 SCC 670; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 237; AIR 1996 SC 2560; AIR 1996 SCW 3189; JT 1996 (7) SC 16; 1996(5) SCALE 596; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Sugarcane Cess (Validation) Act, 1961, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Cess and Other Taxes on Minerals (Validation) Act, 1992, Orissa Cess Act, 1962

Summary: Cess and Other Taxes on Minerals (Validation) Act, 1992, s. 2(1) - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Levying cess/tax on minerals - State Acts invalidated as power to levy exclusively vested in Parliament - Parliament, thereafter enacting impugned Act with retrospective effect - Held, impugned Act valid - Exception to rule of uniformity of rates of tax can be created by Parliament - Cannot be temporary statute only because levy was limited to a particular period.
