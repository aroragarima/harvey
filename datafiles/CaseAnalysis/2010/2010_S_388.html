State of Bihar and others v Mithilesh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 682; (2010) 13 SCC 467; 2011 (1) AWC 288; 2010 (4) ESC 569; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 113; JT 2010 (9) SC 11; 2010(8) SCALE 458; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 161; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2669

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Appointment - Change of government policy - Entitlement - Petitioner/State introduced two new vocational training courses for training persons with disabilities - Petitioner's proposal was approved by the Empowered Committee of State Public Service Commission (SPSC) and funds were sanctioned for such training - SPSC, subsequently, advertised the recruitment of Assistant Instructors for such training and the respondent applied for the post and attended interview - Empowered Committee, in the meantime, decided to train the students through professionally established NGOs/institutions - Respondent was declared successful in the interview and SPSC recommended the name of respondent for appointment - Since there was no response from the authorities, respondent filed a writ petition before the HC seeking appointment - Single Judge directed the SPSC to appoint respondent as Assistant Instructor - Petitioner/State challenged the Single Judge order before the DB - DB dismissed petitioner's appeal - Whether SPSC was justified in recommending the name of respondent for appointment even after the de-requisition of the said post by Empowered Committee - Held, change in the norms of recruitment could be applied prospectively and could not affect those who had been selected for being recommended for appointment after following the norms as were in place at the time when the selection process was commenced - Respondent was selected for recommendation to be appointed as Assistant Instructor in accordance with the existing norms - While a person may not acquire an indefeasible right to appointment merely on the basis of selection, in the instant case, the fact situation is different since the claim of the respondent to be appointed had been negated by a change in policy after the selection process had begun - Therefore, no interference called for in the impugned DB order and respondent is entitled to the appointment - Petition dismissed.
