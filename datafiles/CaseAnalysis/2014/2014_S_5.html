Inbasegaran and another v S. Natarajan (Dead) through Lrs and others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 743; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 135; 2015 (108) ALR 471; 2015 (1) AWC 928; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 468; 2014 (6) CTC 445; JT 2014 (12) SC 293; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 486; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 872; 2015 (127) RD 13; 2014(12) SCALE 435; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 1202

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Maintainability

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.2 r.2 - Suit for specific performance - Maintainability - Plaintiff filed a suit for permanent injunction and specific performance of agreement for sale in respect to suit schedule property against defendant - Defendant also filed suit seeking a decree for injunction restraining the purchaser from interfering with his possession and enjoyment of suit property - Trial Court decreed the suit filed by plaintiff for specific performance with direction to defendant to execute and register sale document in favour of plaintiff - Defendant filed appeals before HC - Appeals were allowed - Hence instant appeals - Whether suit filed by plaintiff is maintainable in view of O. 2 r. 2 CPC.

Held, when precise cause of action upon which the previous suit for injunction was filed because of imminent threat from the side of the defendant of dispossession from the suit property then subsequent suit for specific performance on the strength and on the basis of the sale agreement cannot be held to be the same cause of action. instant case, from pleading of both the parties in the suits, particularly the cause of action as alleged by plaintiff in the first suit for permanent injunction and cause of action alleged in the suit for specific performance, it is clear that they are not the same and identical. Suit for injunction filed by plaintiff, there is nothing to show that plaintiff intentionally relinquished any portion of his claim for reason that suit was for only injunction because of threat from side of defendant to dispossess him from the suit property. It was only after the defendant in his suit for injunction disclosed the transfer of the suit property by the Housing Board to the defendant and thereafter denial by defendant in response to the legal notice by the plaintiff, the cause of action arose for filing the suit for specific performance. In instant case, suit for injunction was filed since there was threat given from side of defendant to dispossess him from suit property. The plaintiff did not allege that the defendant is threatening to alienate or transfer the property to a third party in order to frustrate the agreement. Thus, conclusion arrived at by HC that the suit is barred under O. 2 r. 2 CPC cannot be sustained in law and matter needs to be remanded back to HC to consider and decide other points formulated by it. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - If the two suits and relief claimed are based on same cause of action then only subsequent suit will become barred under O. 2 r. 2 CPC.
