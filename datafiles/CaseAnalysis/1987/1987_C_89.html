Wazid Ali Abid Ali and another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Lucknow and another

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28430; (1988) Supp SCC 193; AIR 1988 SC 757; 1988 (67) CTR 43; [1988] 169 ITR 761; JT 1987 (4) SC 349; 1987(2) SCALE 1078; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 917; 1988 TAXLR 844; [1987] 35 TAXMAN 180

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Contract and Commercial - Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Income Tax - Partnership Firm - (A) Continuation of registration of firm after death of Partner when deed of partnership providing for continuation of firm with remaining partners and son or LRs of deceased partner - But son joining partnership before expiry of previous year - (B) Whether there is any dissolution of the partnership on the date of the death of partner and there should be two separate assessments or whether s.187(2) apply to the facts of this case - Held, firm is entitled to continuation of registration up to death of partner - The firm dissolved by the manner in which the parties acted so there is no question of the same firm being continued and there should be two assessments, i.e. up to death of partner and from death to expiry of previous year and the provisions of section 187(2) could not be said to apply in the light of the facts - Appeal dismissed.
