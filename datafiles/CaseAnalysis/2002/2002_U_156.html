Khazan Singh (Dead) by Lrs v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 45; (2002) 2 SCC 242; AIR 2002 SC 726; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 1; 2002 (47) ALR 215; 2002 (1) AWC 620; 2002 (1) CRC 394; JT 2002 (1) SC 262; 2002 (1) KLT 644; 2002 (2) MahLJ 259; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 175; 2002 (2) MPLJ 2; 2002 (131) PLR 669; 2002(1) SCALE 316; 2002 (1) SCJ 446; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 431; 2002 (1) SLT 387; 2002 (1) Supreme 250; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1536

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Reference by Collector to Civil Court

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151, O. 9 r. 9 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.18, 20, 26 - Reference by Collector to Civil court - Dismissal - Legality - Deceased/claimant moved application u/s. 18 of the Act for making reference to Civil Court - Civil Court dismissed reference on premise of non-appearance by claimant - Application quoting O. 9 r. 9 and s. 151 of CPC for restoration of reference filed by appellants was dismissed on ground that there was no sufficient cause for absence of appellant - On appeal by appellants, Single Judge dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether reference made by Collector u/s. 18 of the Act can be dismissed for default.

Held, if the application for reference is in order the Collector is bound to make a reference of it to the court. The Civil Court has to pass an award in answer to the reference made by the collector u/s. 18 of the Act. If any party to whom notice has been served by the civil court did not participate in the inquiry it would only be at his risk because an award would be passed perhaps to the detriment of the concerned party. But nonparticipation of any party would not confer jurisdiction on the civil court to dismiss the reference for default. Order passed by the Civil Court by which the reference was dismissed for default. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Application for restoration of reference can be entertained u/s. 151 of CPC if same is filed quoting O. 9 r. 9 of CPC.
