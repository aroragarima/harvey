Brij Mohan And Others v Haryana Urban Development Authority And Another

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 2; (2011) 2 SCC 29; 2011 (1) AWC 579; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 3; JT 2011 (1) SC 100; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 261; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 224; 2011(1) SCALE 1; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana Urban Development Authority Scheme

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Scheme for allotment to oustees - Allotment rates - Determination of - Haryana Urban Development Authority (HUDA) formulated a Scheme vide Circular dated 10.9.1987 for allotment of plots to land losers/oustees at normal allotment rates - Land of appellants were acquired for development and utilization of land as residential and commercial area - Thereafter, appellants made an application to HUDA for allotment of plots under the oustees policy - However, as the appellants were not allotted plots, they filed a writ petition which was disposed of after recording the statement of the respondents that the appellants case was under consideration and they will be allotted plots - HUDA allotted plot - Appellants challenged the same before HC contending that that the allotments should be made at cost plus development charges basis and not at market price - Writ petition was dismissed on the ground that the matter was governed by the policy and that under that policy, the oustees-allottees were liable to pay the normal allotment rate, meaning the prevailing rate that was being charged from any ordinary allottee to whom plots were allotted in that sector - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HUDA should charge only the actual land cost plus development charges for the plots allotted to oustees/land losers, and not the market price/normal allotment price? - Held, where the scheme applicable specifies the price to be charged for allotment, its terms cannot be ignored - If any land loser has any grievance in regard to such scheme, he may either challenge it or give a representation for a better or more beneficial scheme; But he cannot ask the court to ignore the terms of an existing or prevailing scheme and demand allotment at cost price - In the present case, scheme of HUDA contemplates allotment of plots only in terms of the scheme, that is at normal allotment rates - The allotment and the price to be charged, will have to be strictly in accordance with such HUDA Scheme - Therefore, appellants cannot claim allotment of a plot at acquisition cost of land plus development cost or at any other lesser price - (B) Meaning of the words 'normal allotment rate' used in the scheme for allotment to oustees - Held, policy clearly states that 'claims of the oustees shall be invited before the sector is floated for sale' - The prices to be charged will be the rate which is equal to the rate that is fixed when the sector was first floated for allotment - Appellants should therefore be allotted plots under the scheme at the initial price at which the Layout/Sector plots were first offered for sale after the acquisition - Hence, having regard to the terms of the scheme which clearly requires that the land losers shall be invited to apply for allotment before the sector is floated for sale, it is clear that the initial price alone should be applied provided the land losers had applied for allotment at that time - Appeal partly allowed.
