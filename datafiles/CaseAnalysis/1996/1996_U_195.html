Common Causea Registered Society v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2489; (1996) 6 SCC 530; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 189; AIR 1996 SC 3538; 1996 (4) CLT 76; JT 1996 (8) SC 613; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 65; 1996(7) SCALE 156; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 719; 1996 (7) Supreme 109; 1996 (2) UJ 802

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Petrol & Gas - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 32, 311 and 226 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 35 - Allotment of petrol pumps by Minister of State for Petroleum and Natural Gas - (A) Largesse - Discretionary quota of Govt. - No application invited - Allotted to relations of his personal staff etc. - Guidelines not observed - (B) Govt. policy - Allotment of petrol pumps, oil agencies, gas agencies - Procedure - It is a matter of policy for Govt. to lay down - (C) Mala fide acts in discharge of duties by public servant - (D) Powers of court as regard it - (E) Public Interest Litigation against allotment - Able assistance to court by petitioner - Costs - Allotments, held, were arbitrary, mala fide - Held, court declined to impose any procedure on Govt., however it directed that any procedure laid down by Govt. must be transparent, just, fair and non-arbitrary - It is high time that they should be held personally liable for such acts - Allotment set aside by court, Minister directed to show cause - Directed to be paid by concerned Minister of State personally - Order accordingly.
