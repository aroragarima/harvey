P.P. Abdulla & Anr. v The Competent Authority & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 962; (2007) 2 SCC 510; AIR 2007 SC 1057; 2007 CRLJ 1449; 2007 (1) JCC 527; 2007 (1) KLT 956; 2007 (5) RCR(Criminal) 179; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 954; 2006(14) SCALE 67; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 983

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Fema; Indirect Tax

Summary: Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976 - Allegation that certain properties purchased by appellant and building constructed thereon attracted the provisions of 1976 Act - Proceeding for forfeiture of the property - Held, contents of the notice, even if taken on face value, do not disclose any sufficient reason warranting the impugned action against the appellant - Condition precedent for exercising the power under the Act did not exist - It has not been alleged therein that there is any such link or nexus between the property sought to be forfeited and the alleged illegally acquired money of the appellant - Notice is illegal - Order passed in pursuance of the said notice is declared as null and void - Appeal allowed.
