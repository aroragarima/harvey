ABL International Limited and Another v Export Credit Guarantee Corporation of India

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1403; (2004) 3 SCC 553; [2004] 118 Comp Cas 213; 2004 (109) DLT 415; JT 2003 (10) SC 300; 2003(10) SCALE 815

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Trade; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Insurance - Trade - Agreement - Consideration - Maintainability - Respondent no. 4 entered into contract with Corporation for supply of 3,000 Metric Tons of tea - Agreement was entered into and as per original agreement payment for such tea exported was to be made by Corporation by barter of goods mentioned in Schedule to agreement, within 120 days of date of delivery by exporter - Appellant claimed that payment of consideration by barter of goods could not be finalized between appellant and Corporation, therefore, Corporation agreed to pay consideration amount for goods received by it in US dollars and also paid certain sums of money in US dollars as part payment but failed to pay balance amount due under contract - It was also sated by appellant that even Kazakhstan Govt. though admitted its liability to pay balance of consideration amount did not fulfill its part of guarantee given in contract - It was on failure of Kazakhstan Govt. to fulfill its guarantee appellants made claim on respondent no. 1 which had covered said risk of compensating loss suffered by it by non-payment of consideration amount for supply of tea made to Corporation -Respondent no. 1 as per its letter dated 14-12-1994 repudiated claim of appellants stating that appellants had changed terms of contract of payment without first consulting it, therefore, it had no obligation to compensate appellants for loss suffered - This alleged change of terms of contract, according to respondent no. 1, was due to fact that appellants had rejected barter offer made by Corporation and had opted for cash payment in US dollars which, according to respondent no. 1 was not mode of payment contemplated in contract - On further correspondence between appellants and respondent no. 1 latter reiterated its right to repudiate claim of appellants by its second letter dated 26 -5-1995 contending that refusal of barter offer by appellants without first consulting it, amounted to change in mode of recovery of dues, hence, loss suffered by such change in mode of recovery took away liability of respondent no. 1 to pay for such loss - Having failed to persuade respondent no. 1to adhere to contract of insurance appellant filed writ petition before HC- HC concluded that though dispute between parties arose out of contract respondent no. 1 being State for purpose of Art. 12, was bound by terms of contract, therefore, for such non-performance, writ was maintainable allowed writ petition and issued writ and directions as prayed for by appellants - In appeal filed by respondent no. 1 before Appellate Bench of same HC said Bench reversed findings of learned Single Judge and held that claim of appellant involving disputed questions of fact could not be adjudicated in writ proceeding u/art. 226 of Constitution, hence, set aside judgment of learned Single Judge -Hence, instant appeal -Whether order passed by Appellate Bench of HC was maintainable - Held, respondent no. 1insured export risk of appellants in regard to non-payment of consideration for tea exported whether it arose from non-fulfillment of barter clause or for non fulfillment of cash payment clause - Respondent no. 1 claimed that appellants refused to accept barter by goods offered by respondent no. 1 which amounted to default under contract on part of appellants has no legs to stand in view of clear language of amended cl.6 of agreement which stated that obligation of Corporation to pay for goods received by it in US $ arised when payment by barter failed for 'any reason whatever' - Court concluded that amended cl. 6 of agreement between exporter and importer does not give room for second or another construction than one already accepted - It was also noted that reliance placed on sub-cl. (d) of proviso to insurance contract by Appellate Bench was also misplaced which was clear from language of clause itself - Thereforeit does not require any external aid much less any oral evidence to interpret clause - Merely because respondent no. 1 wants to dispute this fact Court opined that it does not become disputed fact - If such objection as to disputed questions or interpretations were raised in writ petition courts could go into same and decide that objection if facts permit same as in instant case -It was further noted decisions of this court which in clear terms had laid down that mere existence of disputed questions of fact ipso facto does not prevent writ court from determining disputed questions of fact - Hence, Appellate bench of HC was not justified in reversing judgment of learned Single Judge - Impugned judgment of Appellate bench of HC was set aside and learned Single Judge was restored - Appeal allowed.
