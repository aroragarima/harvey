Shiv Chand v Ujagar Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 288; (1978) 4 SCC 152; AIR 1978 SC 1583; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 520; 1978 UJ 705

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951 - Practice and Procedure - Non Joinder - Election Petition by an unsuccessful candidate - Scope of ss. 86 (1) and 82 (b) of the Act - Allegation of corrupt practice - Effect of application u/s.86 (4) for being impleaded as respondent another unimpleaded unsuccessful candidate - Whether petition can be dismissed for non-compliance with s. 82 (b) - Held, right u/s. 86 (4) cannot be defeated and once he comes on record as party, the petition is in order and cannot be dismissed for non-joinder - Appeal allowed.
