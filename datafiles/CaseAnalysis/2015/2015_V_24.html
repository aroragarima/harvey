Mainuddin Abdul Sattar Shaikh v Vijay D. Salvi

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 471; (2015) 9 SCC 622; AIR 2015 SC 2579; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4914; 2015 (3) BC 348; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 1; 2015 (4) CompLJ 347; 2015 CRLJ 3618; 2015 (4) CTC 699; 2015 (2) DRTC 464; 2015 (3) JCC (NI) 236; JT 2015 (6) SC 120; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2505; 2016 (3) MahLJ 67; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 624; 2016 (2) MPLJ 266; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 711; 2015(7) SCALE 518; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 1033; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 210

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 378 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonor of cheques - Liability of party - Determination - Appellant booked flat proposed to be developed by respondent company by paying them consideration - However, respondent failed to deliver flat to appellant and issued cheques in individual capacity and not in capacity of Director of company in favor of appellant - However, after presentment of cheques it was unpaid - Appellant filed complaint u/s. 138 of Act - Trial Court dismissed suit on ground that respondent company was not made accused and instead respondent was made accused in his personal capacity - Further, appellant filed application for leave to appeal u/s. 378 of CrPC which was dismissed by HC - Whether respondent can be made liable in his personal capacity when Company has not been made party to complaint.

Held, drawer of cheque was respondent, who had drawn cheques, on bank account maintained by him towards refund of booking amount. P.J. Agro Tech Limited and Ors. v. Water Base Limited, 2010 Indlaw SC 807, relied on where SC has dealt with scope of s. 138 of Act and held that it is very clear that in order to attract provisions thereof cheque which is dishonoured will have to be drawn by person on account maintained by him with banker for payment of any amount of money to another person from out of that account for discharge, in whole or in part of any debt or other liability. It is only such cheque which is dishonoured which would attract provisions of s. 138 of Act against drawer of cheque. Further, about liability u/s. 138 under Act, where cheque drawn by employee of appellant company on his personal account, even if it be for discharging dues of appellant-company and its Directors, appellant-company and its Directors cannot be made liable u/s. 138 of Act. Hence, personal liability was upheld and Company and its Directors were absolved of liability because Section itself makes drawer liable and no other person. Further, as respondent was Managing Director of company and sole proprietor there is no need of specific averment that accused was person incharge of and responsible for affairs of Company. Hence, respondent be made liable u/s. 138 under Act, even though company had not been named in notice or complaint. Hence, compensation is awarded along with interest. Respondent is sentenced to simple imprisonment. Impugned order passed by lower courts are set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Person, who draws cheque on an account maintained by him, for paying payee, alone attracts liability.
