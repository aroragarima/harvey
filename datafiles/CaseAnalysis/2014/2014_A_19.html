Paschimanchal Vidyut Vitran Nigam Limited v Adarsh Textiles and another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 821; AIR 2015 SC 511; 2016 (116) ALR 521; 2015 (2) AWC 2028; JT 2014 (13) SC 429; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 584; 2014(13) SCALE 521

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1999

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 108, 62 and 65 - Uttar Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1999, s. 12 - Electricity supply - Subsidy to HV-2 category consumers - Powers of Regulatory Commission - Whether policy decision dt. 14-06-2006 issued by the Govt. of UP regarding supply of the electricity to power loom bunkers on the flat rate could have been applied by the U.P. Electricity Regulatory Commission to the industries availing HV-2 category connection.

Held, it can be culled out from order dt. 14-06-2006 that the State Govt. intended the benefit to be extended to power loom 'weavers' alike farmers. The activity of manufacturing textile is generally understood as the weaving of such textile and man who is engaged in such power loom activity is known as weaver. Weaving means to form a fabric by interlacing yarn on a loom. It also means the method of pattern of weaving or the structure of a woven fabric. The State Govt. thus, never intended the benefit to be given to big industries like HV-2 industries. In the circumstances, it was incumbent upon the Commission to consult the State Govt. before passing clarification order dated 14-15-9-2006 while applying its order dated 11-7-2006 to HV-2 consumers. When the State Govt. has written to the Commission on 6-10-2006, thereafter there was no justification for the Commission not to recall the clarification issued on 14-15-9-2006 as it was the prerogative of the State Govt. to extend the benefit of subsidy to a class or particular class of consumers and subsidy being a concession could not have been enforced as a matter of right. The Commission was bound to act as per such directives of State Govt. Commission and the Corporation had no jurisdiction in the matter of subsidy which is the domain of the State Govt. Appeals dismissed.
