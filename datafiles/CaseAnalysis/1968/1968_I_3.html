In The Matter of Madhu Limaye and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 450; (1969) 1 SCC 292; AIR 1969 SC 1014; 1969 CRLJ 1440; 1969 MLJ 636; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 22(1) - Detention - Validity of - Non-compliance with provisions of art. 22(1) of Constitution - Held, if their detention in custody could not continue after their arrest because of violation of art. 22(1) of Constitution they were entitled to be released forthwith - Therefore, they are entitled to be released on this ground alone - Further held, once it is shown that arrests made by police officers were illegal, it was necessary for State to establish that at stage of remand Magistrate directed detention in jail custody after applying his mind to all relevant matters - This State has failed to do - Remand orders are patently routine and appear to have been made mechanically - Appeal allowed.
