Subbhash Chand v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 663; (1988) SCC (Cr) 259; (1988) 1 SCC 717; AIR 1988 SC 584; 1988 (2) CLR 214; 1988 (1) Crimes 598; 1988 CRLJ 907; 1988 CRLR 145; 1988 CrLR(SC) 145; JT 1988 (1) SC 109; 1988(1) SCALE 99; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 556

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Punjab Borstal Act 1926 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Petitioner accused was convicted and sentenced for offence of murder - Petitioner filed instant petition and claimed the benefit of the Act and placed reliance on a decision of SC in the case of Hava Singh v State Of Haryana And Anr 1987 Indlaw SC 28843.

Held, in Hava Singh v State Of Haryana And Anr 1987 Indlaw SC 28843, the definition was not placed for consideration before the Court and, therefore, the conclusion which has been reached is not correct. The Act does not have application to offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC. Therefore, conclusion in Hava Singh's case is not correct. The petitioner is not entitled to the benefit of the Act as he has been sentenced to imprisonment for life for the offence of murder punishable u/s. 302 IPC for which the sentence of death is prescribed as an alternate. Petition dismissed.
