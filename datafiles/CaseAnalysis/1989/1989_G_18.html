Sushil Kumar Metha v Gobind Ram Bohra

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 260; (1990) 1 SCC 193; 1989 (3) CCC 623; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 329; 1990 (1) PLR 182; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 423; 1990 (1) RentLR 428; 1989(2) SCALE 1104; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 149

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities and Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, ss. 3 and 13 - (A) Whether civil court lacked inherent jurisdiction to entertain suit for ejectment of appellant/tenant and decree so passed is a nullity? - Open land is a part of frontage of shop and belongs to municipality which landlord had taken on lease from municipality - On construction of building covering a portion of municipal land landlord becomes landlord and appellant his tenant for purposes of Act - Shop in question stands mainly on land and a small portion is located on municipal land - Building was governed by provisions of Act and exemption accorded by Govt. u/s. 3 was not attracted to premises - S. 13 apply to building leased out to appellant by landlord and Controller was competent authority to pass a decree of ejectment against appellant and civil court lacked inherent jurisdiction to take cognizance of cause - (B) Whether impugned decree is nullity and whether plea can be raised in execution and further whether decree in suit does not operate as res judicata? - Decree passed by a court without jurisdiction is a nullity and plea can be set up whenever decree is sought to be enforced or relied upon even at stage of execution or in collateral proceedings - Doctrine of res judicata does not apply to case of decree of nullity - Appeal allowed.
