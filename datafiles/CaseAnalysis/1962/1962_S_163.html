Banwari v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 224; AIR 1962 SC 1198; 1962 CRLJ 278; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 180; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Joint Trial, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Consolidate

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 234 and 239 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 307 - (A) Whether joint trial is justified of two offenders who committed offences u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC, 1860? - Held, u/s. 234 of CrPC, 1898, trial allowed of a person accused of 3 or less number of offences of same kind committed within the space of 12 months - Offences of same kind are those which are punishable with same amount of punishment under same section of IPC, 1860 or of any special or local law - Two offenders are charged with those three offences, offences being of same kind, one joint trial of those offences is justified u/s. 234, CrPC, 1898 - (B) Whether Sessions Judge is competent to try at one trial several persons? - Further held, trial depends on provisions of ss. 233 to 239 of CrPC, 1898 - One trial of several persons is justified if no prejudice is caused to accused - Appeal partly allowed.
