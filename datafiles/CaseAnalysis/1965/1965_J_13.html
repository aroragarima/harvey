Â Jan Mohammad Noor Mohammad Begban v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 174; AIR 1966 SC 385; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Gujarat Agricultural Produce Marketing Act 1964

Summary: Constitution - Indirect Tax - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Service - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 31 - Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960 - Gujarat Agricultural Produce Marketing Act, 1964 - Whether the Gujarat Agricultural Produce Markets Act 20 of 1964 was ultra vires? - The Act was enacted with a view to provide satisfactory conditions for the growers of agricultural produce to sell their produce on equal terms and at reasonable prices by eliminating the middlemen and bringing the consumers in direct contact with the producers while preventing adulteration and promoting grading and standardisation of agricultural produce - Authority has been conferred on the director so as to ensure the aims of the act mentioned above - It is only after the objections and suggestions are received and considered by the Director and he is satisfied that it is in the interest of the producer and the general public that there should be regulation of the trade in agricultural produce in the specified area that he may proceed to declare the market area - Reasonable restrictions on the right of a citizen to carry on trade, retail and wholesale, may be placed by legislation
