Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation v Lakshmidevamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 395; (2001) 5 SCC 433; AIR 2001 SC 2090; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 99; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 623; 2001 (90) FLR 35; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2577; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 461; 2001 LabIC 1777; 2001 (2) LLJ 199; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 76; 2001(3) SCALE 600; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 284; 2001 (2) SLR 549; 2001 (4) SLT 295; 2001 (3) Supreme 652

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 11, 15 and 33 - Whether right of a management to lead evidence before Labour Court or Industrial Tribunal in justification of its decision under consideration by such Tribunal or Court is not a statutory right? - Held, procedure for exercising the right of leading evidence by the management which we have held is neither oppressive nor contrary to the object and scheme of the Act - Shambhu Nath Goyal's case (1983 Indlaw SC 126) represents highly technical view, considering rule of convenience, expediency and prudence there is no statutory prohibition, procedure which promotes cause of both employer and workman deserves to be laid down - Appeal dismissed.
