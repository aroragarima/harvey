Kanhai Mishra Alias Kanhaiya Misar v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20256; (2001) 3 SCC 451; (2001) SCC (Cr) 537; AIR 2001 SC 1113; 2001 (1) CCR 272; 2001 (1) Crimes 313B; 2001 CRLJ 1259; 2001 (2) JCC 5; JT 2001 (3) SC 191; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 110; 2001(2) SCALE 338; 2001 (2) SCJ 261; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 160; 2001 (2) SLT 340; 2001 (2) Supreme 180; 2001 (1) UC 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 320 - Rape and Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Reliability of - Whether circumstantial evidence had established guilt of accused? - Delay in lodging FIR - Non-examination of an important witness - Held, only circumstance which prosecution has proved against appellant was that on date of occurrence appellant came to house of informant in morning and gave a proposal to his daughter for going to orchard for plucking flowers which cannot be said to be inconsistent with innocence of appellant, especially in view of fact that FIR was not recorded at police station when informant had gone there, but at his house after two hours from time informant visited police station - The investigating officer suppressed fact that informant went to police station and narrated incident to him at police station, rather investigating officer stated that he received confidential information that some murder had taken place in village of occurrence and further, non-examination of an important witness, who was most material witness to unfold truth - Circumstantial evidence do not form a complete chain and a solitary circumstance cannot be made basis of his conviction - Appeal allowed.
