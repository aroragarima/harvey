Akula Ravinder and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 910; (1991) Supp2 SCC 99; (1991) SCC (Cr) 990; AIR 1991 SC 1142; 1991 CRLR 852

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.304B and 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants A1, A2 and A3 allegedly committed offence of dowry death - Appellants were convicted by Trial court u/ss.304B and 498A of IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants u/s.304B IPC was maintainable - Held, perusal of the s.304B IPC showed that one the of the essential ingredients that had to be established was that death was otherwise than in normal circumstances - In instant case no doubt, the other circumstance namely that the death occurred within seven years of the marriage and that before her death they have harassed her for demand of dowry were established - Coming to the other ingredient SC found that the prosecution had miserably failed to establish that death was otherwise than in normal circumstances - Respondent/State, however, submitted that the deceased was young and the death was not due to natural cause nor it was due to an accident and the only inference that could be drawn was that it was otherwise than under normal circumstances - In a case of such nature where the prosecution had failed to establish that it was an unnatural death it could not be surmised that death should be due to unnatural circumstances - Framing of the charge and the circumstances that were put to appellants u/s.313 CrPC also assumed importance - Charge was u/s.302 IPC and the contents of the charge were to the effect that death was only due to asphyxia - In the examination of s.313 CrPC it was not even indicated that the death could be due to poisoning - In any event in view of the facts and circumstances regarding their death, it had become very difficult, rather impossible, to hold that the death was otherwise than in normal circumstances and consequently SC was constrained to hold that the important aspect of s.304B IPC was not met out - Consequently the appellants were entitled to acquittal of the said offence - SC did not propose to examine the other submission namely that in the absence of charge u/s.304B IPC conviction could not be awarded since SC acquitted the appellants of the charge of the offence punishable u/s.304B IPC - So far as offence u/s.498A IPC was concerned the evidence of PWs 1, 2, 4 and 7 would show beyond a reasonable doubt that the three appellants were harassing the deceased and making the demand for dowry - Evidence would show that the deceased was subjected to cruelty within the meaning of s.498A IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
