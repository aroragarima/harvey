Raja Soap Factory and Others v S. P. Shantharaj and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1965 SC 1449; 22 PTC 434; 1966 (1) SCJ 116; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 800

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss. 105 and 2(e) - Civil Procedure Codee, 1908, ss. 151 and 24, Mysore Act, 1962, s. 105, Mysore Act, 1884, s. 12 - (A) Whether in given case High Court has power to pass order u/s 105 of Mysore Act, 1962 and u/s. 12 of Mysore Act, 1884 ? - (A) u/s. 2(e) of Mysore Act if HC possesses ordinary original civil jurisdiction then only it can act like District Court - So in this situation HC is not competent to entertain passing off actions u/s. 105 Mysore Act, 1962 and u/s. 12 of Mysore Act, 1884 - (B) CPC, 1908 - (B) Whether HC is competent to assume to itself jurisdiction which is otherwise possessed by District Court, when it is closed for vacation under ''extraordinary situation' u/s. 151? - Held, (B) s. 151 preserves inherent power of Court as may be necessary for ends of justice or to prevent abuse of process of Court - Power may be exercised where there is a proceeding lawfully before HC - Although it does not authorize HC to invest itself with jurisdiction where it is not conferred by law - It does not involve a power to entertain a proceeding which is not otherwise within cognizance of HC - Appeal allowed.
