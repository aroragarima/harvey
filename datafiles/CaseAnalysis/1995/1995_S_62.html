Lachhman Dass v Santokh Singh

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 542; (1995) 4 SCC 201; AIR 1995 SCW 2766; JT 1995 (7) SC 437; 1995 (3) PLR 276; 1995 (2) RCJ 271; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 480; 1995 (2) RentLR 254; 1995(3) SCALE 704; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 484; 1995 (2) UJ 475

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, s. 15(6) - Whether the Learned Single Judge of the High Court was justified in re-assessing the value of the evidence and substitute his own conclusions in respect of the concurrent findings of fact recorded by the two courts below, in exercise of his revisionary powers vested in the HC? - Legislature has provided for a single appeal against the order passed by the Rent Controlling Authority - Legislature has made a provision for discretionary remedy of revision u/s. 15(6) - Legislature has created two jurisdictions different from each other in the form of an appeal and revision - Both cannot be said to be one and the same but distinct and different in the ambit and scope - But in the case of revision whatever powers the revisional authority may have, it has no power to reassess and reappreciate the evidence unless the statute expressly confers on it that power - That limitation is implicit in the concept of revision - From the use of the expression 'Legality or propriety of such order or proceedings' occurring in s. 15(6) of the Act, the revisional power of the HC under the Act is wide, but not so wide as to embrace within its fold all the attributes and characteristics of an appeal and disturb a concurrent finding of fact properly arrived at without recording a finding that such conclusions a re perverse or based on no evidence or based on a superficial and perfunctory approach - If the HC proceeds to interfere with such concurrent findings of fact ignoring the aforementioned well recognised principles, it would amount to equating the revisional powers of the HC as powers of a regular appeal frustrating the fine distinction between an appeal and a revision - Held, that Learned Single Judge of HC was unjustified in re-assessing the value of the evidence and substituted his own conclusion in exercise of his revisional power - Impugned judgment and order of HC set aside - Order of the two courts below restored - Appeal allowed.
