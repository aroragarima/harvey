Tharumal v Masjid Hajum Pharosan Va Madarsa Talimul Islam, Mirza Izsmail Road, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1441; (1994) 3 SCC 375; JT 1994 (4) SC 137; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 230; 1994 (1) RentLR 358; 1994(2) SCALE 414; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 263; 1994 (2) UJ 129

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954

Summary: Land & Property - Trust & Associations - Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 111(g) and 114-A - Wakfs Act, 1954, ss. 2 and 5 - Suit for eviction filed by respondent against appellants - Disallowed - In appeal, District Judge held case of determination of tenancy so appellants not entitled to benefit - HC dismissed second appeal - Exemption granted by State Govt. vide notification by which all premises owned by wakfs registered under Wakfs Act were exempted from the operation of the Act - Whether benefit of exemption given by notification to registered wakfs could have been taken advantage of by respondent? - Held, wakf at hand is a registered wakf, as would appear from notification issued by office of Rajasthan Board of Muslim Wakf, Jaipur, as contemplated by s. 5(2) of Wakfs Act, 1954, therefore, premises were exempted from provisions of Act - (B) Whether case of forfeiture and not of determination of tenancy? - Held, notice shows that tenancy at hand was a monthly tenancy as per English calendar and it is because of this that vacant possession was demanded, therefore, case of forfeiture but of determination of tenancy - Appeal dismissed.
