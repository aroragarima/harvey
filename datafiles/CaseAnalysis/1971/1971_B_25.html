Bankey Lal and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 727; (1971) 3 SCC 184; (1971) SCC (Cr) 253; AIR 1971 SC 2233; 1971 CRLJ 1540; 1971 UJ 330

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, art. 134 - (A) Evidence - Appreciation of - Prosecution witnesses have not deposed truly in all respects - Court should scrutinse their evidence with care - Prosecution did not explain injuries on the person of accused - (B) Constitution, art. 134 - Review of evidence by Supreme Court in criminal appeals - Held, simply because prosecution did not explain injuries on the person of accused, Court cannot discard the entire prosecution evidence - SC in criminal appeals does not review evidence afresh unless it is shown that High Court failed to take into consideration any material evidence or approached the case from a wholly wrong point of view - Appeal Dismissed.
