Â State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Thakur Bharat Singh

Supreme Court of India

23 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, K. Subba Rao, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 131; AIR 1967 SC 1170; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Movement

Summary: Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Public Security Act, 1959, s. 3(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(d), 19(1)(e), 352 and 358 - Prohibitive and restrictive directions against individual under Act - Whether violative of fundamental rights u/art. 19(1)(d) and (e) and violative of principles of natural justice? - Directions threesome - First was that respondent shall not be in any place covering a particular district, second that respondent shall reside in municipal limits of a particular town of another district, third that he shall notify his movements and report himself personally every day at 8 a.m. and 8 p.m. to respective police station of such town - Held, Act does not give any opportunity to person concerned of being heard before place where he is to reside or remain in is selected - Place selected may be one in which person concerned may have no residential accommodation, and no means of subsistence - It may not be possible for person concerned to honestly secure means of subsistence in place selected - No provision made which provides for residence, maintenance or means of livelihood in place selected - Cl. (b) of s. 3(1) thus, is unreasonable - Further held, all executive action which operates to prejudice of any person must have authority of law to support it and terms of art. 358 do not detract from that rule - Art. 358 expressly authorises State to take legislative or executive action provided such action was competent for State to make or take, but for provisions contained in part III of Constitution - Order by State in exercise of authority conferred by s. 3(1)(b) of Act to be invalid and for acts done to prejudice of respondent after declaration of emergency u/art. 352, no immunity from process of court could be claimed u/art. 358 of Constitution, since order was not supported by any valid legislation - Appeal dismissed.
