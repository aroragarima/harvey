Â Mohammad Ghouse v State of Andhra

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 61; AIR 1957 SC 246; 1957 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 1957 SCJ 225; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madras Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Rules, 1948, r. 13 - Andhra Civil Service Rules, r. 4 - Whether order of suspension of appellant was illegal? - Order passed by High Court is merely one of suspension pending final orders by Govt., and such an order is neither one of dismissal nor of removal from service within art. 311 of Constitution - Held, u/ r. 13 of Madras Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, it is HC of Judicature at Madras that is constituted as authority which may impose suspension pending enquiry into grave charges u/r. 17(e) against Members of State Judicial Service - Order in question, therefore, falls within this rule, and is perfectly intra vires - Appeal dismissed.
