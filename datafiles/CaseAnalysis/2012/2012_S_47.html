V.D. Bhanot v Savita Bhanot

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 47; (2012) 3 SCC 183; AIR 2012 SC 965; 2012 (187) DLT 295; 2012 (3) JCC 1854; JT 2012 (2) SC 647; 2012 (1) KLT 637; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 361; 2012(2) M.P.H.T. 244; 2012 (1) OLR 764; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 972; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 834; 2012 (2) RLW 1836; 2012(2) SCALE 367; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Women & Children - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, ss. 3, 12, 18, 19 - Denial of right of residence in matrimonial home - Domestic Violence - Respondent wife filed petition before Magistrate u/s. 12 of the Act seeking various reliefs - Magistrate granted interim relief and passed a protection/residence order u/ss. 18 and 19 of the Act protecting right of respondent to reside in her matrimonial home - However, petitioner filed application for respondent's eviction from his service accommodation - Magistrate directed petitioner to make available a reasonable accommodation to respondent - Respondent filed appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court dismissed appeal holding that since cause of action arose prior to coming into force of the Act, Court could not adjudicate upon merits of respondent's case - Respondent appealed to HC wherein HC set aside Appellate Court order and directed Appellate Court to consider respondent's appeal - Hence instant petition - Whether petition under provisions of the Act, was maintainable by a woman, who was no longer residing with her husband or who was allegedly subjected to any act of domestic violence prior to coming into force of the Act - Held, in looking into a complaint u/s. 12 of the Act, conduct of parties even prior to coming into force of the Act, could be taken into consideration while passing an order u/ss. 18, 19 and 20 thereof - HC also rightly held that even if a wife, who had shared a household in past, but was no longer doing so when the Act came into force, would still be entitled to protection of the Act - Situation in the instant case came squarely within ambit of s. 3 of the Act which defined 'domestic violence' in wide terms, and, accordingly, no interference was called for with impugned HC order - Hence, direction issued to petitioner to provide respondent a right of residence where petitioner was residing, by way of relief u/s. 19 of the Act - Petition disposed of.
