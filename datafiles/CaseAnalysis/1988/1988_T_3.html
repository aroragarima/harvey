T. S. Vasudavan Nair v Director of Vikram Sarabhai Space Centre and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 541; (1988) Supp SCC 795; (1989) SCC (L&S) 121; 1988 (1) KLT 582A; 1988 (1) LLN 962

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Challenged

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Appointment - Challenged - Held, appellant should not have been denied employment on the sole ground that he had not disclosed that during emergency he had been convicted under Defence of India Rules for having shouted slogans on one occasion. Thus, judgment of HC and also the order cancelling the offer of appointment was set aside. Respondents shall issue the order of appointment to appellant within 3 months appointing him as Lower Division Clerk, if he is not otherwise disqualified, with effect from date on which he assumes duty. It is open to respondents to employ appellant at any place of their choice. Appeal disposed of.
