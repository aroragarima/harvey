Titan Medical Systems Pvt. Ltd v Collector Of Customs, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1891; (2003) 9 SCC 133; 2003 (87) ECC 13; 2003 (110) ECR 661; 2003 (151) E.L.T. 254; JT 2002 (10) SC 431; 2003 (54) RLT 248

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Misrepresentation, Customs Duty, Grant Of Licence, Customs Tariff, Exempted From Duty, Penalty

Summary: Customs - Exemption Notification No. 116 of 1988 - Advance licence was for import of certain components for manufacture of ultrasound scanners granted - Authorities held that appellants were not entitled to benefit of exemption notification as they misrepresented to licensing authority - Whether Collector of Customs erred in levying duty and penalty on Nicolian Brothers and penalty on Titan Medical Systems Pvt Ltd? - Whatever may be operation, it is effect of operation on commodity that is material for purpose of determining whether operation constitutes such a process which will be part of 'manufacture' - Any process or processes creating something else having a distinctive name, character and use, would be manufacture - Licensing authority have not claimed that there was any misrepresentation - Held, once an advance licence was issued and not questioned by licensing authority, customs authorities cannot refuse exemption on an allegation that there was misrepresentation - If there was any misrepresentation, it was for licensing authority to take steps in that behalf - Impugned order of Collector set aside - Appeals allowed.
