Sivayogeswara Cotton Press, Devangere and Others v M. Panchaksharappa and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 428; AIR 1962 SC 413; 1962 (2) SCJ 85; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Tenant at will - Whether or not lease deed evidences a perpetual grant to lessee on terms and conditions contained in lease deed? - Held, terms and conditions of lease which created rights and obligations between lessor and lessee were specifically declared to be binding on heirs and successors in interest of lessor and lessee - Nature of tenancy created by any document must nevertheless be determined by construing document as a whole - A stipulation entitling lessee to surrender possession of premises at his will is not wholly inconsistent with tenancy being permanent - On a true and proper construction of lease deed, presumption in favour of transaction creating a permanent lease cannot be held to have been rebutted by a stipulation in favour of tenant having right to surrender lease at his choice - Appeal allowed.
