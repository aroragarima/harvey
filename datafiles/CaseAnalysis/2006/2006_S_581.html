Bablu @ Mubarik Hussain v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1273; (2006) 13 SCC 116; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 590; AIR 2007 SC 697; 2007 CRLJ 1160; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 296; 2006(14) SCALE 15; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning, Mitigating Circumstance, Extra Judicial Confession, Specific Plea

Summary: Criminal - Death sentence - Rarest of rare cases - Intoxication - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant murdered his wife and children - Trial Court and High Court held him guilty of offence u/s. 302 and imposed death sentence - Whether death sentence was justified in such a case? - Held, yes - In rarest of rare cases when collective conscience of community is so shocked that it will expect holders of judicial power center to inflict death penalty irrespective of their personal opinion as regards desirability or otherwise of retaining death penalty, death sentence can be awarded - Present case squarely fell under rarest of rare category to warrant death sentence - Appellant took defence of drunkenness - Further held, defence of drunkenness can be availed of only when intoxication produces such a condition as accused loses requisite intention for offence and that intoxication must have been against his will and/or thing which he intoxicated was administered to him without his knowledge - In instant case, plea of drunkenness can never be an excuse for brutal, diabolic acts of accused - Appeal dismissed.
