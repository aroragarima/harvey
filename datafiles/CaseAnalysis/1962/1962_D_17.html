Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 310; AIR 1963 SC 791; 1990 (27) ECR 151; 1977 (1) E.L.T. 199; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 586

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Taxes - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944, sch. 1, items 12 and 13 - (A) Can 'Raw Oils purified in process but not deodorised' be covered under item 12 as 'refined oil' in Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944? - Held, not even covered by expression 'non-essential vegetable oils - Or by 'all sorts' in item 12 - Not covered as 'refined oil' under item 12 - (B) Can Excise duty be levied on 'Raw Oils purified in process but not deodrised'? - Further held, no new substance known to market is brought into existence - Does not become at any stage 'refined oil' - Excise duty - Chargeability is on manufacture, and not on sale, of goods - No legal basis for demand of excise duty thereon under Item 12 - Appeals dismissed.
