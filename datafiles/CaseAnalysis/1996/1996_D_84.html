Union of India and Others v Dhanwanti Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2318; (1996) 6 SCC 44; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 47; AIR 1996 SCW 4020; JT 1996 (8) SC 306; 1996 (2) RentLR 343; 1996(6) SCALE 431; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 32; 1996 (7) Supreme 51

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act 1968

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Solatium - Land of three villages was acquired for public purpose u/s. 7 of 1894 Act - Respondent-claimants claimed of enhanced compensation - Arbitrator allowed the enhanced compensation at the rate of fifteen percent solatium and interest on enhanced compensation - Order of arbitrator was allowed by HC and appeal of appellants was dismissed, hence present appeal by special leave was filed - Whether, the respondents were entitled to solatium and interest under the 1968 Act - Held, award of solatium and interest on the compensation awarded was set aside and compensation was upheld - 1952 Act did not provide for payment of solitium and interest - Principles of unjust enrichment were absent in omission by a statute and were made by the competent Legislature to award interest or solatium, in addition to compensation - Appeal allowed.
