Dr. Balram Prasad and others v Dr. Kunal Saha and another

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 696; (2014) 1 SCC 384; 2013 (4) ACC 378; 2014 (1) AWC 385; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 397; 2013 (4) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2013 (6) KarLJ 161; 2013 (7) MLJ 781; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 946; 2014 (1) RLW 54; 2013(13) SCALE 1; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Consumer Protection Rules, 1987, Hospitals, Medical Profession, Prescription, Consumer Protection Regulations, 2005, Antibiotics

Summary: (1) Consumer Protection Act, 1986-Section 2 (1) (g)-Deficiency in service-Medical negligence-Award of compensation in relation to proved medical negligence for death of wife of claimant-Compensation paid by National Commission-Inadequate-Required to be enhanced substantially in given facts and evidence on record-Deceased wife was citizen of U.S.A. and permanently settled as child psychologist and claimant was AIDS researcher in U.S.A.-National Commission ought to have taken relevant factual aspect of case into consideration regarding status and standard of living of deceased in U.S.A. to determine just compensation under head of dependency-Finding on contributory negligence by National Commission on part of claimant-Set aside-Direction of National Commission to deduct 10% of awarded amount of compensation on account of contributory negligence-Set aside-Compensation amount enhanced from R 1,34,66,000 to R 6,08,00,550 with 6% interest p.a.-Decision of Apex Court acts as deterrent and a reminder to those doctors, hospitals and nursing homes and other connected establishments who do not take their responsibility seriously.

(2) Consumer Protection Act, 1986-Section 2 (1) (g)-Deficiency in service-Medical negligence-Doctors, hospitals and nursing homes and other connected establishments to be dealt with strictly-If they found to be negligent with patients who come to them pawning all their money with hope to live better life with dignity-Patients irrespective of their social, cultural and economic background-Are entitled to be treated with dignity which not only forms their fundamental right but also their human right-Institutions and individuals providing medical services to public at large educate and update themselves about any new medical discipline and rare diseases so as to avoid tragedies such as the instant case where valuable life could have been saved with little more awareness and wisdom from part of doctors and hospital-Central and State Governments to consider for enacting laws for effective functioning of private hospitals and nursing homes.
