Central Bureau of Investigation v Anupam J. Kulkarni

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1268; (1992) 3 SCC 141; (1992) SCC (Cr) 554; AIR 1992 SC 1768; AIR 1992 SCW 1976; 1992 AllCriC 512; 1992 (2) AnWR 15; 1992 (3) AWC 1781; 1992 (3) Bom.C.R. 562; 1992 (2) CCR 113; 1992 (2) Crimes 310; 1992 CRLJ 2768; 1992 CrLR(SC) 533; JT 1992 (3) SC 366; 1992(1) SCALE 1024; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Procedural Law - Investigation was entrusted to C.B.I. relating to abduction of respondent - It emerged during investigation that respondent was one of the associates of accused - On the basis of some available material respondent was arrested and was produced before the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate(Magistrate) - On the request of the C.B.I. respondent was remanded to judicial custody - An application was moved by the IO seeking police custody of respondent which was allowed - When he was being taken on the way respondent pretended to be indisposed and he was taken to the Hospital where he remained confined on the ground of illness - Respondent was again remanded to judicial custody by Magistrate and thereafter was sent to Jail - In view that Police could not take him into police custody all these days the IO again applied to the court of Magistrate for police custody of respondent - Magistrate refused police remand - Revision was filed before HC - HC granted him bail - Hence, the present appeal - Whether a person arrested and produced before Magistrate as required u/s. 167(1) 1973 Act could still be remanded to police custody after the expiry of the initial period of 15 days - Held, if arrested accused was in judicial custody in connection with the investigation of the earlier case he can formally be arrested regarding his involvement in the different case and associate him with the investigation of that other case and the Magistrate could act as provided u/s. 167(2) of the CrPC and the proviso and could remand him to such custody during the first period of fifteen days - If the investigation was not completed within the period of ninety days or sixty days then accused had to be released on bail as provided u/s. 167(2) of the CrPC - Period of ninety days or sixty days had to be computed from the date of detention as per the orders of the Magistrate and not from the date of arrest by the police - Consequently, the first period of fifteen days mentioned in s. 167(2) of the CrPC had to be computed from the date of such detention and after the expiry of the period of first fifteen days it should be only judicial custody - Appeal dismissed.
