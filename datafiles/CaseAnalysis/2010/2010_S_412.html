Rameshbhai Pandurao Hedau v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 194; (2010) 4 SCC 185; AIR 2010 SC 1877; 2010 CRLJ 2441; JT 2010 (3) SC 254; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 489; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 375; 2010(3) SCALE 166; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 522; 2010 (1) UC 656

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 120-B and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 200, 202(2) and 202(3) - Magistrate power's to direct investigation - Dead body of appellant's brother was found near a high way - In the post-mortem examination, death was found to be a result of natural causes and investigation was closed - Dissatisfied with the closure of the investigation, the appellant filed a complaint before the Metropolitan Magistrate praying for an order to be passed for an inquiry u/s. 156(3) CrPC for taking action against the accused for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 114 r/w s. 120-B, IPC - Metropolitan Magistrate postponed the issuance of process and kept the complaint for Court inquiry, in accordance with s. 202, CrPC - Criminal Writ Petition filed thereagainst was also dismissed - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the Magistrate committed any error in refusing the appellant's prayer for an investigation by the police u/s. 156(3), CrPC and resorting to s. 202, CrPC instead? - Held, power to direct an investigation by the police authorities is available to the Magistrate both u/s. 156(3) CrPC and u/s. 202 CrPC, the only difference is the stage at which the said powers may be invoked - Power u/s. 156(3) CrPC to direct an investigation by the police authorities is at the pre-cognizance stage while the power to direct a similar investigation u/s. 202, CrPC is at the post-cognizance stage - In the present case, Magistrate chose to adopt the latter course and treated the protest petition filed by the appellant as a complaint u/s. 200, CrPC and has thereafter proceeded u/s. 202, CrPC and kept the matter with himself for an inquiry in the facts of the case - There was nothing irregular in the manner in which the Magistrate proceeded and if at the stage of Sub-section (2) of s. 202, the Magistrate deems it fit, he may either dismiss the complaint u/s. 203 or proceed in terms of s. 193 and commit the case to the Court of Sessions - Appeal dismissed.
