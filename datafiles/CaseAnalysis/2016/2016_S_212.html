Richa Mishra v State of Chhattisgarh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 131; (2016) 4 SCC 179; AIR 2016 SC 753; 2016 (2) AWC 1718; JT 2016 (2) SC 106; 2016 (1) MLJ 851; 2016(2) SCALE 247; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 316; 2016 (3) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Age, Rule, Examination, Appeal, Public Service Commission, Relaxation, Applicability, Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2005, Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Special Provision for Appointment of Women) Rules, 1997, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, State Services Examination Rules, 2003, Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2000

Summary: (A) Service - Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2000, r. 8 - Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2005 - Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Special Provision for appointment of Women) Rules, 1997 - State Services Examination Rules, 2003 - Application of Rule to Govt. servant - Relaxation of age limit - Determination - Appellant participated in selection process for appointment for post of Deputy Superintendent of Police (Dy.S.P.) where she had qualified at each stage of examination process, however, her name was still not included in list of successful candidates for said post on grounds that as per Rules, 2000, upper age limit for appointment to post of Dy.S.P. was 25 years and she had already crossed said age limit, hence, she was rendered ineligible for post in question - Appellant contended that she was entitled to benefit of age relaxation on account of being Govt. Servant as she was appointed as Excise Sub Inspector, hence, she was entitled to claim age relaxation as per r. 8 of Rules, 2000- HC dismissed petition filed by appellant holding that she entered Govt. job vide appointment order dt. 21-4-2006 which was after cut-off date as 1-1-2006 for post of Dy.S.P., hence, was not entitled to benefit of age relaxation - Further, appellant filed appeal before DB and claimed benefit of age relaxation under Rules, 1997, however, DB dismissed appeal filed by appellant vide impugned order - Whether Rules, 1997 are applicable, which make special provision for relaxation in upper age limit by 10 years in respect of women candidates.

Held, process of selection started before Rules, 2005 were promulgated with requisitions dt. 27-9-2004 and 26-3-2005 sent by State Govt. to authority. SC held that at that time, Rules, 2000 were in vogue. Hence, even in requisition it was mentioned that appointments are to be made under Rules, 2000. Further, it is also admitted fact that vacancies in-question which were to be filled were for period prior to 2005 and such vacancies needed to be filled in as per those Rules, i.e. Rules, 2000. Further, State sent requisition specifically mentioning that recruitment has to be under Rules, 2000 which was also provided in advertisement. SC held that appellant never challenged advertisement and contended that after promulgation of Rules, 2005 recruitment should have been under Rules, 2005 and not Rules, 2000. Hence, SC held that appellant is even precluded from arguing that recruitment should have been made under Rules, 2005. SC held recruitment was rightly made by as per Rules, 2000. Appeal allowed.

(B) Service - Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2000, r. 8 - Chhattisgarh Police Executive (Gazetted) Service Recruitment and Promotion Rules, 2005 - Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (Special Provision for appointment of Women) Rules, 1997, r. 4a - State Services Examination Rules, 2003 - Relaxation of age limit - Grant of benefit of relaxation - Determination - Whether Examination Rules, 2003 which specifically contain provision for applicability of Rules, 1997 would be treated as applicable for examination in-question.

Held, r. 8 of Rules, 2000, which, inter alia, lays down provision pertaining to upper and lower age of candidates, does not make any specific provision for relaxation of age in respect of women candidates. However, SC held that Rules, 1997 read with Rules, 2003 would get attracted and as these Rules make specific provision for providing of age relaxation upto ten years that is to be given to women candidates, hence, appellant shall be entitled to said benefit on ground that when all Rules, 2000, 1997, 2003 are seen in juxtaposition and in conjunction with each other, intention of rule making authority becomes apparent and is clearly ascertained. SC held that intention of rule making authority was, and it continues to be so, to give benefit to age relaxation to women candidates. Otherwise very purpose of such Rules is defeated. Hence, SC held that appellant was entitled to age relaxation as per r. 4 of Rules, 1997 read with Rules, 2003. Hence, directions are issued to respondents to appoint appellant as Dy.S.P. w.e.f. date her juniors in merit list are appointed. Her seniority and pay shall be fixed on that basis. However, appellant will not be allowed to make any claim for salary for intervening period otherwise intervening period shall count for all other purposes. Order passed by first judge of bench is affirmed by second judge. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Rules made by legislature should be interpreted accordingly as denial of benefit would make Rule wholly nugatory.
