Santosh Hazari v Purushottam Tiwari.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 266; (2001) 3 SCC 179; AIR 2001 SC 965; AIR 2001 SCW 723; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 40; 2001 (1) AllCJ 556; 2001 (42) ALR 749; 2001 (42) ALR 794; 2001 (1) ARC 449; 2001 (1) AWC 824; 2001 (170) CTR 160; [2001] 251 ITR 84; JT 2001 (2) SC 407; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 69; 2001(3) M.P.H.T. 71; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 243; 2001 RD 336; 2001(1) SCALE 712; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 948; 2001 (1) SLT 933; 2001 (1) Supreme 642

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure , 1908, s. 100 - Whether High Court can dismiss second appeal u/s. 100 CPC by passing a brief order that matter stood concluded by findings of fact and no substantial question of law arose for determination? - Existence of a substantial question of law is sine qua non for exercise of jurisdiction under amended s. 100 of Code - Held, HC cannot proceed to hear a second appeal without formulating substantial question of law involved in appeal and if it does so it acts illegally and in abnegation or abdication of duty cast on court - Matter remitted back to HC - Appeal allowed.
