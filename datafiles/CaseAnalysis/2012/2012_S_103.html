Salaha Begaum, Etc. v Special Land Acquisition Officer

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 519; (2013) 11 SCC 426; 2013(1) ALL MR 466; 2013 (97) ALR 200; 2013 (1) AWC 385; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 607; 2013 (119) RD 88; 2012(11) SCALE 730; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 30

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 54 - Acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement - Entitlement of - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act - Special Land Acquisition Officer passed award and fixed market value of the acquired land at the rate of Rs. 65,000/- per acre - On a reference made by the Collector u/s. 18 of the Act, the Reference Court determined the amount of compensation at the rate of Rs.1,00,000/- per acre - Appellants filed appeals u/s. 54 of the Act and pleaded that they were entitled to compensation at the rate of Rs. 2,20,000/- per acre - DB of the HC enhanced the amount of compensation from Rs.1,00,000/- to Rs.1,70,000/- per acre - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in enhancing compensation - Held, once HC accepted sale deed dt. 7-1-1993 as the touchstone for determination of the compensation payable for identically situated land, there could be no justification for awarding less compensation to appellants - Another error committed by the HC was that it has not given the benefit of principle of escalation of price to appellants - SC had repeatedly held that the exercise undertaken for fixing market value and determination of compensation payable to the landowner should necessarily involve consideration of escalation in land prices - SC in ONGC Ltd. v. Rameshbhai Jivanbhai Patel, 2008 Indlaw SC 2625, relied on - Appellants were entitled to compensation at the rate of Rs. 2,20,000/- per acre with benefit of 10 per cent increase for the time gap of two years between the notification issued for the acquisition of X land and the notification issued for the acquisition of their land - Competent authority was directed to pay to appellants the enhanced compensation together with other statutory benefits - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
