M.M. Quasim v Manohar Lal Sharma & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 308; (1981) 3 SCC 36; AIR 1981 SC 1113; 1982 (1) RCJ 212; 1981 (2) RCR(Rent) 74; 1981(1) SCALE 747; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 367; 1981 UJ 396

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Bihar Building (Lease Rent and Eviction) Control Act 1977 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Default in payment of rent - Eviction - Legality - Respondents No. 1 and 2 were brother's sons of respondent No. 3 - Respondents filed suit before Trial Court for ejectment of tenant/appellant from shop in default of payment of rents - Trial Court decreed the suit in favor of respondents - Appellant appealed to Appellate Court, when said appeal was pending appellant filed an application contending that shop belongs to firm and same was dismissed - Appellant filed second appeal before HC - HC found difficult to accept contention that during pendency of appeal if house in question allotted to share of one of co-sharers of decree the decree which had been passed in their favor becomes nullity and was liable to be set aside by Appellate Court on this ground alone and second appeal was also HC dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, on examining and evaluating contents of certified copy of decree in partition it was established conclusively that property had been exclusively allotted to 'P' who had not applied to be joined as party to these proceedings though he had filed some affidavit in this appeal before SC and if no reservation was made in decree for continuation of proceedings for recovering possession on ground of nonpayment of rent in favor of present respondents nor had present respondents undertaken any liability to continue proceedings on behalf of 'P' for the purpose of recovery of rent, in our opinion it would be extremely doubtful if respondents could still maintain action for recovering rent and for possession on grounds mentioned in s. 11(1)(c) & (d) - Aspect had not at all been examined either by Appellate Court or by HC - If 'A', a landlord commences action for eviction against his tenant on only ground of non-payment of rent and during pendency of proceedings transfers property lock stock and barrel to third person and if third person was not before SC, without finally expressing any opinion because remand was contemplated, it was just unthinkable that such landlord could continue suit even after he had no interest in property - Aspect might have to be examined in background of the contract between landlord who commenced action and his transferee, or transferee having reserved some right came to SC for being impleaded as party to continue action and his, right to continue, might be examined - These aspects were not examined by any Court though decision on them goes to root of matter - Therefore, a remand was inevitable in circumstances of this case - Decree passed by HC and Appellate Court was set aside and case was remanded to Appellate Court to decide - Appeal allowed.
