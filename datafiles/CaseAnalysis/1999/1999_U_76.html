Common Cause, A Registered Society v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, K. Venkataswami, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 503; (1999) 6 SCC 667; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1196; AIR 1999 SC 2979; AIR 1999 SCW 2899; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 538; 1999 (4) CompLJ 208; 2000 (1) G.L.R. 748; JT 1999 (5) SC 237; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1279

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Corporate - Socio-Economic - Tort - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 53, 73 and 77 - Whether action could have been initiated under public law and whether exemplary damages could have been awarded in proceedings related to the question of jurisdiction? - High Court has jurisdiction not only to grant relief for the enforcement of fundamental rights but also for "any other purpose" which would include the enforcement of public duties by public bodies- Supreme Court has the jurisdiction to issue prerogative writs for the enforcement of fundamental rights guaranteed to a citizen under the Constitution of India, 1950 - Every executive or administrative action of the State or other statutory or public bodies is open to judicial scrutiny and the HC or the Supreme Court can, in exercise of the power of judicial review under the Constitution, 1950, quash the executive action or decision which is contrary to law or is violative of fundamental rights guaranteed by the Constitution, 1950 - Arbitrary and unreasonable decisions of the govt. authorities while acting in pursuance of a contract would also be amenable to writ jurisdiction - "Authority" if arbitrary, unreasonable or contrary to law, amenable to the writ jurisdiction of this Court can be validly scrutinised on the touchstone of the constitutional mandates.
