Balbir Kaur and Others v New India Assurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 497; (2009) 13 SCC 370; AIR 2009 SC 2460; 2009 (75) ALR 829; 2009 (3) AWC 2151; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 898; 2009(6) SCALE 257; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 327

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166, Chapter XI - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64VB - Insurance of motor vehicles against third party risks - Award of compensation by MACT - Set aside by High Court - Ground, cover note of the insurance issued on 15-03-1996 which was to take effect from 19-03-1996 and accident took place on 18-03-1996, thus insurer was not liable - Judgement of HC whether proper? - Compensation awarded by MACT was deposited with the Court and was also withdrawn by the appellant - HC gave its finding relying on decisions in 'National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Jikubhai Nathuji Dabhi' and 'J. Kalaiveni v. K. Sivshanker' cases - Chapter XI of MV Act and s. 64VB of Insurance Act, looked into - Court made assumption that an insurance policy in law could be issued from a future date - A policy, however, which is issued from a future date must be with the consent of the holder of the policy - Insurance company cannot issue a policy unilaterally from a future date without the consent of the holder of a policy - No material produced to show as to why the policy was issued from a later date - In peculiar facts of the case Court exercised its jurisdiction u/art. 142 of Constitution and directed insurance company not to recover the amount from the appellants - Further directions issued - Appeal allowed.
