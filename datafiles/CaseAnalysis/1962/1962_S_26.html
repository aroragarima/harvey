Banarsi Das v Seth Kanshi Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 415; AIR 1963 SC 1165; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Contracts & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 17 - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 43 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 20, r. 15 - (A) Whether admission made by a person is binding on him? - Held, an admission in so far as facts are concerned would bind maker of admission but not in so far as it relates to a question of law - (B) Whether filing of suit for dissolution amount to notice of termination u/s. 43 of Partnership Act? - Further held, it does not - In case of a partnership at will mere filing of a suit for dissolution of partnership does not amount to a notice of dissolution of partnership within meaning of s. 43(1), Partnership Act - In a partnership at will, if one of partners seeks its dissolution, what he wants is that firm should be wound up, that he should be given his individual share in assets of firm (or may be that he should be discharged from any liability with respect to business of firm apart from what may be found to be due from him after taking accounts) and that firm should no longer exist - (C) When can a partnership be dissolved? - Further held, date of dissolution will be date fixed in preliminary decree passed u/o. 20, r. 15 of CPC - S. 43(2) of that Act contemplates mentioning of a date in notice from which firm would stand dissolved - Date of service of a summons accompanied by a copy of a plaint in suit for dissolution of partnership cannot be regarded as date of dissolution of partnership - Petition dismissed.
