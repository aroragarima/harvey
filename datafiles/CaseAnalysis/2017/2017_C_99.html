Commissioner of Income Tax v Balbir Singh Maini

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Sanjay Kishan Kaul

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 797

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Immovable Property, Capital Asset, Capital Gains, Earnest Money, Registration, Contract, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Registration Act, 1908, Capital Gains Tax, Finance Act, 1987, Accrual, Registration and Other Related Laws (Amendment) Act, 2001, Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(47)(ii), 2(47)(v), 2(47)(vi), 260A, 45, 48, 53A - Capital gains - Taxability - Challenged - Respondents/Assessees filed return of income which included capital gains - AO held that since physical and vacant possession was handed over under Joint Development Agreement(JDA) the same would tantamount to "transfer" within the meaning of ss. 2(47)(ii), (v) and (vi) of the Act - On appeal, Commissioner dismissed appeal upholding order passed by AO - Aggrieved, assessee filed appeal before Tribunal which was dismissed - On further appeals before HC - HC allowed appeals holding that Tribunal and authorities were not right in holding assessee to be liable to capital gains tax in respect of remaining land measuring 13.5 acres for which no consideration was received and which stood cancelled and incapable of performance due to various orders passed by SC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment of HC.

Held, income accrues when it becomes due but it should also be accompanied by a corresponding liability of other party to pay the amount. Only then could it be said that for the purposes of taxability that income was not hypothetical and it has really accrued to assessee. In instant case, even if it was assumed that assessee was entitled to the benefits under the advance licences as well as under the duty entitlement passbook, there was no corresponding liability on Customs Authorities to pass on benefit of duty-free imports to assessee until the goods are actually imported and made available for clearance. It was clear that income from capital gain on a transaction which never materialized was at best, a hypothetical income. It was admitted that for want of permissions, the entire transaction of development envisaged in JDA fell through. In point of fact, income did not result at all for the aforesaid reason. Such being the case, it was clear that there was no profit or gain which arises from the transfer of a capital asset, which could be brought to tax u/s. 45 r/w s. 48 of the Act. Assessee did not acquire any right to receive income, inasmuch as such alleged right was dependent upon the necessary permissions being obtained. Such being the case, there was no debt owed to assessees by the developers and assessees have not acquired any right to receive income under JDA. Therefore, no profits or gains "arose" from the transfer of a capital asset so as to attract ss. 45 and 48 of the Act. Impugned order of HC was upheld. Appeals dismissed.
