Karam Chand Thapar and Brothers Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 406; 1974 CTR 365; [1971] 82 ITR 899

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - In the instant case appellant sold the shares by which it suffered a loss - Appellant claimed that the loss was a trading loss - The Tribunal agreed with the Appellate Assistant Commissioner's conclusion that the loss in question was a capital loss and rejected the claim - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the Tribunal erred while holding its decision - Held, on the facts and circumstances of the case, that shares sold were shown in the account books of the assessee and in the balance sheet as investment shares, that they were not sold when the prices of those shares were high, depicted that assessee was not acquiring those shares as a trading activity - The Tribunal was hence, justified in drawing the inference that the loss in question was a capital loss - Appeals dismissed.
