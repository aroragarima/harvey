Commissioner of Income Tax v Govinda Choudhury and Sons

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 980; (1995) Supp4 SCC 515; 1994 (116) CTR 61; [1993] 203 ITR 881; [1994] 74 TAXMAN 331

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income - Interest - Assessment - Assessee/Respondent was a partnership firm engaged in the business of contractors - Dispute relates to certain amounts awarded by arbitrators as interest payable to assessee - Amount of interest about which there was dispute between parties was mentioned by Income Tax Officer as Rs.4,30,549 but Tribunal had found that interest can be taken into account for assessment year under consideration was only Rs.2,77,692 - Assessee contended that amount received by him by way of interest was really in nature of damages and was not taxable as a revenue receipt - Contention was rejected by Income Tax Officer as well as Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Assessee contended that amount of Rs.2,77,692 was not all taxable in its hands - Tribunal assumed that amount of interest was fully taxable as income from other sources - HC answered in favour of assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs.2,77,692 received by assessee could be treated as income from other sources - Held, that interest payable certainly partakes of same character as receipts for payments of which he was entitled under contract and which payment has been delayed as a result of certain disputes between parties - It cannot be separated from other amount granted to assessee under the awards and treated as income from other sources - Appeal disposed of.
