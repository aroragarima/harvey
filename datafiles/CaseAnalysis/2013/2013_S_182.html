K.S. Panduranga v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 117; (2013) 3 SCC 721; AIR 2013 SC 2164; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1485; 2013 CRLJ 1665; 2013 (3) CTC 631; JT 2013 (3) SC 514; 2013 (3) KarLJ 685; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 80; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 219; 2013(3) SCALE 152; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Adjourn

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 20, 7 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused working as Superintendent who was incharge of dispatch dept. demanded Rs.5000/- from PW1 as illegal gratification to give him more transport loads - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - Trial Court convicted accused for offence punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act and sentenced to undergo 1 year RI - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal, confirmed judgment and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Courts below were sustainable - Held, prosecution had established factum of recovery and had also proven demand and acceptance of amount as illegal gratification - Therefore, conviction recorded against accused was unimpeachable - In State of Maharahstra v Dnyaneshwar Laxaman Rao Wankhede 2009 Indlaw SC 930 relied on - Thus, explanation offered by accused did not deserve any acceptance and finding recorded on that score by Trial Court and stamp of approval given to same by HC could not be faulted - However, in facts and circumstances of case, age of accused and ailments he had been suffering, which was highlighted before SC, sentence of imprisonment imposed u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act was reduced to 1 year and maintain sentence u/s. 7 of the Act - Appeal disposed of.
