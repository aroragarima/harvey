Nani Gopal Sarkar and 151 Others v Heavy Engineering Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 758; (1990) 3 SCC 173; (1990) SCC (L&S) 458; AIR 1990 SC 1391; 1990 (14) ATC 699; 1990 (61) FLR 105; JT 1990 (2) SC 259; 1990 LabIC 1221; 1990 (2) LLJ 289; 1990 (2) LLN 706; 1990(1) SCALE 520; 1990 (2) SLR 423; 1990 (1) UJ 577; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1530

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 and 227 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 A - Non-implementation of award - Issuance of writ of mandamus - Legality of - (A) Whether writ of mandamus can be issued for implementing an Industrial Award? - Held, High Court found that no writ of mandamus could be issued to the respondents as the award did not comply and fulfil the procedure prescribed u/s. 10-A of the Act - The HC committed error in taking the said view in asmuch as the respondents themselves had not taken the stand that such award was not binding on them or that they did not enforce the same - The question of the validity of the award has not been canvassed before the Supreme Court - Therefore, court assumed that the award is binding on the parties - (B) Promotion - Entitlement of - Whether respondents acted discriminatively by refusing to promote appellants as Junior Executives as was done in case of accountants? - Held, it would be evident that posts of Office Superintendents/Personal Assts. (OS/PA) on Administrative and Personnel side and Accountant on Finance and Accounts side were almost identical - Both were in supervisory cadre and there was no valid justification in not promoting OS/PA to post of Junior Executive and to promote them on post of APO/PS which was again in supervisory cadre, pay scale also goes to show that there was no real difference - OS/PA who have already worked on such post for more than seven years have now been promoted on post of APO/PS and they would only get chance of further promotion as Junior Executive again after seven years, whereas accountants having worked for seven years on such post have already been promoted as Junior Executive - Appeal allowed.
