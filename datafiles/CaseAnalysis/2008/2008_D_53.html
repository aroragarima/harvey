Godavari Finance Company v Degala Satyanarayanamma and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 693; (2008) 5 SCC 107; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 531; 2008 (3) ACC 57; AIR 2008 SC 2493; 2008 (3) ALD(SC) 18; 2008(3) ALL MR 851; 2008 (3) AWC 2161; 2008 (4) CPJ(Sc) 30; 2008 (4) G.L.R. 3383; JT 2008 (5) SC 371; 2008 (2) KLT 429; 2008 (4) MahLJ 686; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 748; 2008 (151) PLR 257; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 875; 2008 (3) RLW 1912; 2008(6) SCALE 364; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 231; 2008 (2) UC 1322

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Accident Claims Tribunals, Registered Owner, Representation of People Act, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Use Of Vehicle

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - s. 2(30) - Whether a financer would be an owner of a motor vehicle within the meaning of s. 2(30) of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Held, definition of "owner" is a comprehensive one; interpretation clause itself states that the vehicle which is the subject matter of a Hire Purchase Agreement, the person in possession of vehicle under that agreement shall be the owner, thus, the name of financer in the Registration Certificate would not be decisive for determination as to who was the owner of the vehicle - Motor vehicle which is subjected to a hire purchase agreement, the financer cannot ordinarily be treated to be the owner; person who is in possession of the vehicle, and not the financer being the owner would be liable to pay damages for the motor accident - Appeal allowed.
