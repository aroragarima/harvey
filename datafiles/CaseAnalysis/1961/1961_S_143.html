Â State of West Bengal v Messrs B. K. Mondal and Sons

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1962 SC 779; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract Act, Indian Contract Act, Government of India Act 1935, Tacit promise

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 175(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 65 and 70 - Construction of temporary storage godowns at Arambagh, District Hooghly for Civil Supplies Department of State of Bengal - Offer accepted by department vide letter dated February 12, 1944 - Additional Deputy Director of Civil Supplies visited Arambagh on April 20, 1944, and instructed respondent to proceed with construction as per estimates submitted - Further constructions of storage sheds completed - Two bills unpaid - Whether High Court was right in passing a decree u/s. 70 of Contract Act? - Held, materials from record show that Govt. urgently needed work which was done by respondent and that Govt. accepted it as soon as it was done and used it for its benefit - This is a case where Province of Bengal was under a moral obligation to pay respondent - An obligation of this kind which is apart from provisions of s. 70 of Indian Contract Act a moral and natural obligation is by provision of that section converted into a legal obligation - S. 70 can be invoked by respondent against appellant - Courts below were right in decreeing respondent's claim - Appeal dismissed.
