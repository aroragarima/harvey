Dularey Lodh v The Iiird Addl. District Judge, Kanpur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 123; (1984) 3 SCC 99; AIR 1984 SC 1260; 1984 (10) ALR 498; 1984 (2) RCR(Rent) 132; 1984 (2) RentLR 356; 1984(1) SCALE 793; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 743; 1984 UJ 662

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction (Amendment) Act, 1976, s. 26 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 13 - Tenancy - In executable ejectment decree under Act of 1972 , becoming executable by virtue of Amendment Act, 1976 - Legality - Held, doctrine of eclipse applied and once the bar placed by the 1972 Act is removed, by virtue of the doctrine of eclipse the decree will revive and become at once operative and executable decree could be executed - Appeal dismissed.
