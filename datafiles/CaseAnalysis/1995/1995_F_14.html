N. Sukumaran Nair v Food Inspector, Mavelikara

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1513; (1997) 9 SCC 101; (1997) SCC (Cr) 608; AIR 1995 SCW 3229; 1995 CRLJ 3651; 1996 (2) FAC 21; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 248

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Criminal Procedure Code, s. 433(d) - Prevention of Food Adulteration Rules, 1955, r. 18 - Sample of ice cream purchased by Food Inspector from appellant was reported by Public Analyst to be adulterated because of reduction of milk-fat and total solids in product - Court acquitted appellant on ground that r. 18 was not complied - High Court reversed decision of Trial Court - Present appeal - Food Inspector as PW 1 was categoric that he had sent specimen impression of seal separately to Public Analyst under sealed cover - As is obvious, Food Inspector deposed to observance of the requirement of r. 18 but, at best, can be said not to have introduced corroborative evidence to his word, but, if word of Food Inspector is not challenged in cross examination and is otherwise found corroborated from report of Public Analyst wherein necessary recitals, even though in printed form, are available, compliance of r. 18 becomes obvious - Such report by Public Analyst is ex facie evidence - Held, view that HC was justified in upsetting order of acquittal on aforesaid ground - Further held, u/s. 433(d) the appropriate Govt. is empowered to commute sentence of simple imprisonment for fine - This would be an appropriate case for commutation of sentence where almost a decade has gone by - Appeal disposed of.
