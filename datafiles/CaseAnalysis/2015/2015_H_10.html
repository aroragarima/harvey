Union of India and others v Hanuman Industries and another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 339; (2015) 6 SCC 600; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 227; JT 2015 (5) SC 571; 2015 (4) MLJ 470; 2015(6) SCALE 185; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 302

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Closure

Summary: Administrative - Incentive Scheme - Withdrawal of scheme - Availment of benefit - Policy decision was taken by Govt. for promotion of industries in North East Region and scheme was launched granting incentives to newly set up industries - In response to scheme, respondents on various dates submitted their applications accompanied by necessary documents for setting up their industries and fulfilling various other conditions - However, implementing authorities were adopting pick and choose methods in matter of disbursement of financial assistance to selected few by overlooking their worthy claims - Lower Court dismissed petitions of respondents on ground of delay and laches, however, decision was reversed in appeals filed by respondents, by common judgment and order impugned whereby direction, in earlier proceedings to implementing authority for processing applications for investment subsidy under scheme in accordance with law was reiterated vis-Ã -vis present respondents - Appellant contended that scheme in question was withdrawn, hence respondent was not liable to avail benefits of scheme - Hence, present appeal.

Held, vide letter/notification dt. 4-5-2010 issued by authority confirmation of decision of withdrawal and closure of scheme w.e.f.23-2-2007, was notified. Hence, letter/notification dt. 4-5-2010, did relate back to 23-2-2007 for all intents and purposes. Further, by letter dt. 1-10-2007, as consequential step, proposal which had remained unprocessed as per standard procedures of scheme were returned to State Govts which also included proposal of respondents. Hence, on 1-10-2007, claims of respondents had not been accepted and in view of closure of scheme, were returned to respective State Govts. Further, there was no promise on part of public functionaries in charge of implementation of scheme to respondents to extend benefits thereunder, inspite of decision to withdraw or close same with effect from 23-2-2007 . Further, SC held that respondents in view of their deliberate laches, negligence and inaction have disentitled themselves to benefit of adjudication in earlier lis. In accompanying facts and circumstances, it would be iniquitous and repugnant as well to public exchequer to entertain belated claim of respondents on basis of doctrine of promissory estoppel which is even otherwise inapplicable to case in hand. Hence impugned order passed in favor of respondent is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Benefits of scheme cannot be availed by party when same has been withdrawn by concerned authorities.
