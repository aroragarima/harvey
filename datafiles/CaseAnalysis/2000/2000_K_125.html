Kamala Bakshi v Khairati Lal

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4169; (2000) 3 SCC 681; AIR 2000 SC 1808; JT 2000 (3) SC 556; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 400; 2000(2) SCALE 689; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 773

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Madras Rent Recovery Act, 1865

Summary: (A) Rent Control - Land & Property - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 14(1)(a) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 52 - Mandatory injunction - Recovery of rent arrears - Deceased Appellant and respondent executed document to join in business carried on in suit premises - Appellant filed suit claiming mandatory injunction in which Trial Court decreed suit - Respondent filed appeal before District Judge who reversed decree of Trial Court - Appeal filed before HC who held that Appellant was landlord and respondent was tenant was upheld but quantum of rent payable by respondent was modified - Meanwhile Appellant died leaving legal representatives who claimed title to suit premises on basis of family settlement - Appellant issued notice to respondent demanding rent but as respondent disowned his liability to pay arrears appellant filed petition before Additional Rent Controller who dismissed petition and held that as respondent paid arrears of rent immediately preceding demand notice and arrears of rent for rest period was not legally recoverable -Appellant filed revision before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether arrears of rent was legally recoverable - Held, order of eviction by any Court/Controller in favor of landlord and against tenant for non-payment of arrears of rent legally recoverable within two months of service of notice of demand, claiming arrears of rent, on tenant by landlord - If claim of appellant for recovery of arrears of rent was not enforceable in court of law for having become barred by limitation, amount ceases to be legally recoverable - Appellant would be entitled to recover only that much of arrears of rent for which she could sue in court - For recovery of arrears of rent art. 52 of 1963 Act from date arrears become due - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Rent Control - Land & Property - Recovery of arrears - Validity of - Whether Judgment passed by lower Court was valid - Held, trial Court held that respondent was trespasser and appellant could not claimed any rent till same was set aside by appellate court holding that respondent was tenant of suit premises which was confirmed by HC - Cause of action was accrued to appellants father to claim rent from respondent from inception of tenancy on basis of true state of affairs which they camouflaged but which was discovered and declared by court - Hence, judgment of HC did not suffer from any illegality - Appeal dismissed.
