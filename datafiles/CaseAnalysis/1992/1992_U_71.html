Union of India and Others v Secretary, Madras Civil Audit and Accounts Association and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 954; (1992) 2 SCC 1; (1992) SCC (L&S) 382; 1992 (20) ATC 176; 1992 (64) FLR 813; JT 1992 (1) SC 586; 1992 (2) LLJ 749; 1992 (1) LLN 894; 1992(1) SCALE 257; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 530; 1992 (1) SLJ 193; 1992 (1) SLR 667

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Service - Equal pay - Reasonable classification - Govt., Ministry of Finance, Department of Expenditure, issued OM dt. 12-6-1987 - Benefits in OM were to be implemented on different dates because of bifurcation of Accounts Wing and Audit Wing in 1983 - Respondents contended that implementation of benefits on different dates offended the Constitution - Tribunal allowed petition - Whether different dates of implementation of recommendations of the Pay Commission offended arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Held, there was clear distinction between the Accounts Wing and Audit Wing - Submission that giving two different dates of implementation in respect of two categories of personnel offended arts. 14 and 16 was rejected - Court was unable to agree with Tribunal - SC decision in All India Station Masters 'and Assistant Station Masters' Association & Others v. General Manager, Central Railways and Others, 1959 Indlaw SC 117, followed - SC decision in Unikat Sankunni Menon v. The State of Rajasthan, 1967 Indlaw SC 124, followed - SC decision in State of Punjab v. Joginder Singh, 1962 Indlaw SC 467, followed - Appeal allowed.
