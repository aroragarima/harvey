Tarun Bora Alias Alok Hazarika v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, B.P. Singh, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1644; (2002) 7 SCC 39; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1568; AIR 2002 SC 2926; 2002 (3) CCR 169; 2002 (3) Crimes 181; 2002 (6) CRJ 226; 2002 CRLJ 4076; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 172; JT 2002 (6) SC 78; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 194; 2002(5) SCALE 624; 2002 (4) SLT 750; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 458; 2002 (5) Supreme 407; 2002 (2) UC 589

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s.365 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(1)(2)(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Abduction - Corroborative Evidence - Conviction -_ Sustainability - PW1 was taken away from his home by United Liberation Front of Assam (ULFA ) extremist appellant with help of 3-4 members by blindfolding PW1 and released after 3 days - Respondent/State approached Addl. Judge, Designated Court which convicted appellant u/s.365 IPC and ss.3(1) and 3(5)of the Act and other accused were discharged - Hence, instant appeal - Whether act of accused/appellant fall under the Act - Held, in instant case credible and trustworthy statement of PW 1 and prosecution had established its case - It should be remembered that statement in-chief of PW 1 remained impeached - Therefore there was no reason to doubt credit worthy evidence of PW1 and PW4 apart from other lending circumstances - Further, stated that Human consideration was no ground for showing leniency to perpetrator of crime against organized civilized society, which was abhorrent to concept of rule of law - In fact, prayer had already been considered by designated court and lenient punishment of 5 years R.I. had been awarded - Hence, Court opined that offence of kidnapping in any form impinge upon human rights and right to life enshrined in art. 21 of the Constitution and such acts not only strike terror in mind of people but had deleterious effects on civilized society and had to be condemned by imposing deterrent punishment - Appeal dismissed.
