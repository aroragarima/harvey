Lakshmanasami Gounder v Commissioner of Income Tax Selvamani and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 974; (1992) 1 SCC 91; AIR 1992 SCW 551; 1991 (100) CTR 274; JT 1992 (2) SC 298; 1992 (1) MLJ(SC) 23; 1991(2) SCALE 956B; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Tamil Nadu Revenue Recovery Act, 1864, s. 36 - Appellant alleged to have misappropriated sum of Rs. 12,163,50 - For recovery his coffee estate was brought to sale - Respondent No. 1 purchased estate but did not deposited balance consideration within 30 days from date of auction - Appellant filed application Addl. District Collector set aside sale but High Court quashed order of Addl. District Collector - Whether HC right in confirming purchase made by respondent No. 1? - Held, s. 36 obligates Sale Officer (Tahsildar) that he shall publish date and place of sale - Sales Officer should conduct sale strictly in conformity with prescribed procedure under statute and rules - Specification of date and place sale shall be mandatory - Omission of specification of place of sale in form renders sale not merely irregular but also invalid - It is obligatory on purchaser to deposit amount within 30 days as u/s. 30 of 1864 Act unless he is prevented by order of court or tribunal from so making deposit - Sale is vitiated by manifest error of law due to non-compliance of s.30 of 1864 Act - Order of Addl. District Collector restored and order of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
