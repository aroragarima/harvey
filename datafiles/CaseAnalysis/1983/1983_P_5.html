Pandurang Dattatreya Khandekar v Bar Council Of Maharashtra, Bombay & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 317; (1984) SCC (Cr) 335; (1984) 2 SCC 556; AIR 1984 SC 110; 1984 (1) Bom.C.R. 422; 1983(2) SCALE 495; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 414; 1984 UJ 88

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 35(1), 38 - Disciplinary proceedings - What amounts to 'Professional misconduct' - And scope of interference with concurrent findings in writ jurisdiction - Held, improper legal advice may amount to professional misconduct but not wrong legal advice - Generally no interference with the concurrent finding of fact by the Disciplinary Committee of the Bar Council of India and the State Bar Council unless the finding is based on no-evidence or it proceeds on mere conjectures and surmises - Appeal partly allowed.
