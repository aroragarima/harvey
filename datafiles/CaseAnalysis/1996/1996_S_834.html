Rashtriya Mill Mazdoor Sangh v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 275; (1996) 5 SCC 542; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 21; AIR 1996 SC 2797; JT 1996 (7) SC 630; 1996 LabIC 2518; 1996(6) SCALE 303; 1996 (3) SLJ 132; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 458; 1996 (6) Supreme 457

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(c) and 21 - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25 - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 3 - Maharashtra Central India Spinning, Weaving and Manufacturing Company Limited, Empress Mills, Nagpur (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986 - Central India Spinning, Weaving and Manufacturing Company Limited, Empress Mills, Nagpur (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1986, ss. 9(2), 10(2), 12(1) and 26 - Whether there has been violation of art. 21 of Constitution by nationalisation of Industry? - Held, employees rendered unemployed - Unemployment of employees was not because of nationalisation - Act of nationalisation in this manner saved majority of employees from unemployment - Argument based on art. 21 is misplaced - Appeal dismissed.
