State of Maharashtra and Another v Super Max International Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1052; (2009) 9 SCC 772; AIR 2010 SC 722; 2009(5) ALL MR 1001; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 556; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 246; 2009(11) SCALE 794; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 801

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Residential Purposes, Delhi Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1976, Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1947, Statutory Tenants, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control (Amendment) Act, 1978, Bombay Rent Restriction Act, 1939, Bombay Rents, Hotel Rates and Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1944

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss.13(3)(a) and 13(1)(g) - Interim order - Payment of Monthly rent at a rate higher than contractual rent - Legality - Appellant, was in occupation of building on rent - Trial Court and Appellate Court decreed suit for ejectment - Appellant filed civil revision before HC challenging orders of ejectment - HC stayed execution of decree subject, however, to condition that appellant would deposit a sum of Rs. 5,40,000/- every month commencing from date of decree passed by Trial Court - Aggrieved by condition imposed appellant filed present appeal - (A) Imposition of condition - Legality - In an appeal or revision preferred by a tenant against a order or decree of an eviction passed under the Rent Act it is open to the appellate or the revisional Court to stay the execution of the order or the decree on terms, including a direction to pay monthly rent at a rate higher than the contractual rent - Appellant may or may not accept the order as a whole, but it is not open to appellant to accept the order in so far as it stays the execution of the decree and to question the condition attached to it - Held, order of HC just and proper - Atma Ram Properties (P) Ltd. vs. Federal Motors (P) Ltd 2004 INDLAW SC 1078 followed - (B) Position if tenant's appeal/revision allowed and eviction decree set aside - Held, in that event, status quo ante would be restored and tenant would be entitled to get back all amounts that he was made to pay in excess of contractual rent - For said purpose, amount fixed by the court over and above the contractual monthly rent, ordinarily, should not be directed to be paid to landlord during pendency of appeal/revision - Appeal dismissed.
