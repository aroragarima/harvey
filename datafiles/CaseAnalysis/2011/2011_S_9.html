Citicorp. Maruti Finance Limited v S. Vijayalaxmi

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 792; (2012) 1 SCC 1; AIR 2012 SC 509; 2012 (92) ALR 211; 2012 (1) AWC 26; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 110; 2012 (1) CompLJ 51; 2011 (4) CPJ(Sc) 67; 2012 (1) JCC 613; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 876; 2012 (1) RLW 245; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 1050

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Hire Charges

Summary: Consumer Protection - Banking & Finance - Hire-Purchase - Default - Recovery - Settlement - Hire-Purchase Agreement was entered into between appellant bank and respondent in respect of vehicle - Respondent failed to pay hire charges - Appellant bank sent legal notice to respondent recalling entire hire- purchase facility and made one-time offer of settlement for liquidating outstanding dues - Respondent failed to respond to offer - Appellant bank recovered and sold impugned vehicle to X - Respondent filed complaint before Consumer Forum against appellant alleging deficiency in service on their part - District Forum directed appellant to pay sum along with interest - On appeal, State Commission enhanced the amount - National commission set aside order of State Commission in part and reduced the volume of payment to be made by appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the District and State commissions were right in holding that vehicles had been illegally and/or wrongfully recovered by use of force from loanees - Held, recovery process should be in accordance with law and such recovery to be effected in due process of law and not by use of force - In the instant case, situation was little different, since after vehicle was seized, same was also sold and third party rights had accrued over the vehicle - It was possibly on such account that appellant Bank chose to comply with directions of District Forum notwithstanding pendency of case - Since the appellant bank had already accepted the decision of District Commission no relief could be granted to appellant - Appeal disposed of.
