Sakuru v Tanaji

Supreme Court of India

10 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 13; (1985) 3 SCC 590; AIR 1985 SC 1279; 1985 (2) CCC 432; 1985 ECR 2597; 1985 (22) E.L.T. 327; 1985(2) SCALE 304; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 109; 1985 UJ 894

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, Pre. - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950, s.90 - Whether the provisions of s.5 of the Limitation Act can be invoked for condoning the delay in the filing of an appeal before the Collector u/s. 90 of the 1950 Act - Applicability of provisions of limitation Act (s.5) to proceeding under special Acts (1950 Act) by s. 93 - Held it could not be invoked by appellate authority under the special Act - Appeal dismissed.
