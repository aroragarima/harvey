Tikaram v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1563; (2007) 15 SCC 760

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused persons shot dead 7 members of one family and injured some others - Sessions Judge came to conclusion that prosecution had established charges leveled against them and convicted them for offence u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced them to death and referred matter to HC for confirmation of sentence - So far as appellant was concerned, Sessions Judge found him guilty of offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC but taking into consideration his role in crime sentenced him only to life imprisonment - Said convicted accused filed appeal before HC which came to be dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of life imprisonment passed against accused for his conviction u/s. 302 of IPC was sustained - Held, there was no principle in law that evidence of single witness could not be relied upon to base conviction if that evidence was reliable and acceptable - Evidence of PW48 who was eyewitness to incident established role of appellant in incident - Both Courts below considered evidence of PW 48 and chosen to place reliance on same and it was rightly so - It was case of prosecution that brother of deceased having come to know of incident came to place of occurrence and having seen only part of incident informed police, therefore, in that process if he failed to mention name of appellant, it could not be held that circumstance alone would not be sufficient to discard evidence of PW 48 who was specifically identified accused and had narrated role played by him - Therefore, complaint made as to non-mentioning of appellant's name in FIR should be rejected - Thus, Courts below had rightly came to conclusion that appellant was guilty of offence punishable u/ss. 302 r/w. 149 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
