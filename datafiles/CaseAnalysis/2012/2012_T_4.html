Markio Tado v Takam Sorang & Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 45; (2012) 3 SCC 236; AIR 2012 SC 993; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 92; 2012 (3) CalLT 76; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 280; JT 2012 (2) SC 447; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 338; 2012(2) SCALE 280; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 661

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Booth Capturing, Order For, Polling Station, Offence of booth capturing

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(8) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 93(1) - Double voting - Production of Register of voters' counterfoils - Permissibility - 1st respondent filed election petition challenging election of appellant (returned candidate) on the ground of booth capturing - After the evidence of 1st respondent, he moved an application seeking direction to District Returning Officer (DRO) to produce record of Register of voters' counterfoils (in Form 17A) of 38 polling stations on the ground of double voting - Single Judge/Election Tribunal allowed application - Hence instant appeal - Whether Single Judge was right in allowing 1st respondent's application for getting additional record from DRO at the stage of evidence - Held, ground of impersonation or double voting was not pleaded in election petition, nor was any issue framed thereon for trial - Further, 1st respondent had not mentioned names of persons allegedly involved in booth capturing - Even with respect to impersonation, only instance pointed out in application was not stated in election petition or in evidence as to who did double voting - Having failed to place any material with respect to either booth capturing or impersonation, 1st respondent was trying to make fishing and roving inquiry to improve his case by calling for record of voters register from polling stations in question, in support of his grievance of double voting - In the absence of any evidence with respect to persons who at the instance of appellant allegedly captured booths or made double voting or impersonation in Constituency in question, no such inference could have been drawn against appellant - Single Judge, therefore, was clearly in error in allowing 1st respondent's application - Further, appellant had won with a margin which was more than total number of votes polled in two polling stations in question - Hence, 1st respondent's application could not have been entertained even on the ground of improper reception in absence of prima facie case that result of election was materially affected - Impugned order of Single Judge was illegal and unsustainable, hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
