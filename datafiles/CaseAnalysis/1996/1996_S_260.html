Prabhu Shankar Jaiswal v Sheo Narain Jaiswal and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1804; (1996) 11 SCC 225; 1996 (2) ARBLR 677; 1996 (3) AWC 1848; JT 1996 (10) SC 11; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 44; 1996 (8) Supreme 153

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 34 and 8 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69 - Application u/s. 8 - Maintainability - Whether an arbitration clause in a partnership deed of an unregistered partnership, can be enforced for purpose of securing, inter alia, a dissolution and accounts of partnership or for enforcing any right or power for obtaining property of a dissolved firm? - Held, where arbitration is sought under arbitration clause in a partnership deed of an unregistered firm for purpose of dissolution and accounts of partnership firm, partners can maintain all applications/petitioners under Arbitration Act for purpose of enforcing their right to secure dissolution and accounts of partnership firm through arbitration - In present case suit for dissolution and accounts of partnership firm has been stayed u/s. 34 of Arbitration Act at instance of respondent 1, therefore, petition of appellant u/s. 8 of Arbitration Act is maintainable - Appeal allowed.
