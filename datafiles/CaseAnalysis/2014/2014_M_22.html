MSP Infrastructure Limited v Madhya Pradesh Road Development Corporation

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 849; AIR 2015 SC 710; 2015(1) ALL MR 952; 2015 (1) AWC 392; 2015 (1) BC 395; 2015 (6) CTC 889; 2015 (1) JabLJ 100; JT 2014 (14) SC 101; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 66; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 601; 2014(13) SCALE 601; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 157

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Madhyastham Adhikaran Act, 1983

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Appeal u/s. 34 of the Act - Amendment - Permissibility - Appellant entered into a development agreement with respondent Corporation - Dispute arose between parties - Matter referred to arbitration - Arbitral Tribunal passed award - Respondent challenged award u/s. 34 of the Act - Respondent filed amendment application to amend original petition u/s. 34 of the Act after 2 years - District Court dismissed amendment application - However, HC set aside District Court order allowing amendment application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, all objections to jurisdiction of whatever nature must be taken at the stage of the submission of the statement of defence, and must be dealt with u/s. 16 of the Act. The mandate of s. 34 clearly prohibits such a cause. A party is bound, by virtue of s. 16 (2), to raise any objection it may have to the jurisdiction of the Tribunal before or at the time of submission of its statement of defence, and at any time thereafter it is expressly prohibited. Suddenly, it cannot raise the question after it has submitted to the jurisdiction of the Tribunal and invited an unfavourable award. It would be quite undesirable to allow arbitrations to proceed in the same manner as civil suits with all the well-known drawbacks of delay and endless objections even after the passing of a decree. Hence, the amendment application raised a ground which was contrary to law and ought not to have been allowed by the HC. Impugned HC order was set aside. Order accordingly.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Madhya Pradesh Madhyastham Adhikaran Act, 1983 - Dispute under 1983 Act - Jurisdiction of arbitral tribunal - Respondent contended that if the dispute under the 1983 Act was decided by tribunal constituted under 1996 Act, that would amount to violation of public policy - Whether the tribunal constituted under the Central Act (1996 Act) has the jurisdiction to adjudicate a dispute submitted under the State Act (1983 Act).

Held, intention of providing that the award should not be in conflict with the public policy of India is referable to the public policy of India as a whole i.e. the policy of the Union of India and not merely the policy of an individual state. Though, it cannot be said that the upholding of a state law would not be part of the public policy of India, much depends on the context. Where the question arises out of a conflict between an action under a State Law and an action under a Central Law, the term public policy of India must necessarily understood as being referable to the policy of the Union. It is well known, vide art. 1 of the Constitution, the name 'India' is the name of the Union of States and its territories include those of the States. Order accordingly.

Ratio - U/s. 16 (2) of the Act, there is a prohibition on the party from raising a plea that the Tribunal does not have jurisdiction after the party has submitted its statement of defence.
