Â Rohtak Hissar District Electricity Supply Company Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 408; AIR 1966 SC 1471; 1966 (12) FLR 290; 1966 (2) LLJ 330; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 863

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Closure, Certification, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, item 10, O. 47, 49 and 48, s. 4(K) - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6(K) and (R) - Central Act, ss. 25(J), (2) - (A) Whether Standing Orders submitted for certification conformed to Model Standing Orders or not? - Held, s. 3(2) provides that these Standing Orders shall be, as far as practicable, in conformity with such Model Standing Orders - S. 15 which deals with powers of appropriate Govt. to make rules, authorises, by cl. (2)(b), appropriate Govt. to set out Model Standing Orders for purposes of this Act - (B) Whether Standing Orders reasonable or fair? - Held, decision of Certifying Officer is made appealable to Appellate Authority u/s. 6 at instance of either party - When certification proceedings are held before certifying authorities, reasonableness or fairness of provisions contained in draft Standing Orders falls to be examined - (C) Whether it refers to conditions of employment or not? - Held, s. 15(1) confers wide powers on appropriate Govt. to make rules to carry out purposes of Act and s. 15(2) specifies some of matters enumerated by cls. (a) to (e), in respect of which rules may be framed - It is well-settled that enumeration of particular matters by sub-s. (2) will not control or limit width of power conferred on appropriate Govt. by sub-s. (1) of s. 15 - (D) Whether it would be fair or reasonable to retain other part of certified S.O. 54 without provision as to payment of pension? - Held, it would not be fair or reasonable to introduce a term of retirement in conditions of service without making any provision for a suitable retrial benefit, but such a provision cannot be made suo moto by appropriate authority under item 11 C - It has to be made either at initiative of employer, or by consent of parties - Whole of certified S.O. No. 54 should be deleted and matter of retirement of employees should be left to be determined under existing practice - Appeal allowed partly.
