Ram Shankar Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1956 SC 441; 1956 CRLJ 822

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 392 and 395 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 145, 167 and 342 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Appellant along with three other - Trial for dacoity - All convicted and sentenced - Appeal to Allahabad High Court - Judge acquitted three and - Maintained - Conviction and sentence, three appellants - Whether HC appears to have erred in making a distinction between case of those three accused and of appellants? - Held, only six persons named in the first information report were placed on their trial, though 14 to 16 persons are said to have participated in the dacoity - None of the unknown dacoits was placed on trial, apparently because none could be identified by any of the eye-witnesses during the investigation - The charge as framed does not refer to any other person besides the six accused to have concerned with the occurrence - There are no sufficient reasons for differentiating case of appellants from that of other accused who were acquitted by HC - Appeal allowed.
