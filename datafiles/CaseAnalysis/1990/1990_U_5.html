U. Ponnappa Moothan Sons, Palghat v Catholic Syrian Bank Ltd. And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 449; (1991) 1 SCC 113; AIR 1991 SC 441; 1990 (3) CCC 402; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 1; 1990 (88) CTR 192; JT 1990 (4) SC 94; 1990(2) SCALE 579; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 542; 1991 (1) ShimLC 348; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Holder For Value

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Negotiable Instruments Act,1881,ss. 9 and 118(g) - Partners in a firm availed credit facilities from the banker with a security of a promissory note drawn in favour of their mother - The banker purchased cheques drawn by one of the purchaser in favour of the firm for consideration and proceeds were received by the firm - Cheques dishonoured - Firm filed suit for recovery - Firm had to dispatch a consignment before presentation of cheques - (A) Whether the bank was only a collecting agent 'Holder in due course' and the said purchase of cheques was without consideration - (B) Whether the Bank acted negligently and in disregard of the provisions of law - Held, mere failure to prove bona fide or absence of negligence by holder would not negative bank's claim of being a 'holder in due course' - Since the cheques were not 'not negotiable' so Bank could purchase the cheques for a valuable consideration u/s. 118(g) - No negligence or illegality observed - Appeal dismissed.
