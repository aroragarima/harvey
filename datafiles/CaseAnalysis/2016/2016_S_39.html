Dokiseela Ramulu v Sangameswara Swamy Varu and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 863; (2017) 2 SCC 69; JT 2016 (11) SC 324; 2017 (1) MLJ 284; 2016(12) SCALE 367; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 459

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Agricultural Land, Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987, Madras Estates Land Act, Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari), Act 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari), Act, 1948, ss. 3, 11 - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987, s. 82 - Tenancy - Ryotwari patta - Respondent no.1 claimed that as any lease of agricultural land belonging to or given, or endowed for the purpose of any institution or endowment, subsisting on the date of commencement of the Act, stands cancelled, asserted that all existing rights in appellant would automatically stand terminated on the coming into force of the 1987 Act basing on s.82 of the Act - However, appellant rested his claim on collective reading of ss.3 and 11 of the Act - By impugned order, HC passed judgment in favour of respondent no.1 and hence, instant appeal by appellant.

Held, consequent upon issuance of a notification u/s. 3 of 1948 Act, the agricultural land in question in the revenue Estate of Sangam Agraharam village, was duly declared as an Inam Estate. The right of the appellant in said 'Inam Estate' is dependent on determination of the tenancy claim of the appellant prior to 17-1-1959 i.e., the notified date. The Original suit filed by appellant was decreed and was duly declared that appellant was in possession of the land in question and also appellant and his ancestors were held to be in continuous possession of the land in question, before the notified date - 17-1-1959. As such, in terms of s. 11 of 1948 Act, appellant automatically became entitled to 'ryotwari patta'. Hence, s. 82 of 1987 Act is inapplicable because appellant could not be treated as a lease holder of agricultural land belonging to or given, or endowed for purpose of any institution or endowment, subsisting on the date of commencement of 1987 Act namely on 21-4-1987. The said position also emerged from dismissal of suit no. 73/1974 filed by Estate Officer, wherein the assertion made on behalf of Sri Sangameswara Swamy Varu, that there existed a landlord tenant relationship with appellant on the basis of an alleged kadapa (rent-deed) dt.29-11-1970, was rejected. Further, in view of judgment of SC in Ramalinga Samigal case, it could not be accepted that judgment and decree dt.31-10-1977 passed in common for Original suit filed by appellant and suit of estate officer, was not binding on Sri Sangameswara Swamy Varu. Further, it could not be accepted that claim raised by appellant was barred by limitation. Further, on the issue of limitation, it emerged from the fact, that the appellant had preferred Execution Application no.18/2007 when respondent no.1 allegedly tried to interfere with the possession of the agricultural land in question, on 6-7-2005. There was no justification for determining limitation, with reference to the date when the decree in suit no.32/1974 was passed. The relevant date for determining limitation was 6-7-2005, when the appellant's possession was allegedly threatened. As such, the claim raised by appellant, was certainly not barred by limitation. Hence, impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Only when the possession and occupation of the agricultural land is subsequent to the first day of July 1945, that the State Govt. would examine the circumstances of each case, and thereupon, in an appropriate case, issue a direction, that 'ryotwari patta' was to be extended to the tenant of such agricultural land.
