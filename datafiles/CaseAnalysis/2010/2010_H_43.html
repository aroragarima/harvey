Dinesh Chandra Pandey v High Court Of Madhya Pradesh & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 503; (2010) 11 SCC 500; AIR 2010 SC 3055; 2010 (127) FLR 760; JT 2010 (7) SC 386; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 199; 2010(7) SCALE 59; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 37; 2010 (5) SLR 554

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Nurses Act, 1966, M.P. Judicial Service (Classification, Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1955, Govt. Servant Conduct Rules, 1985

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - M.P. Judicial Service (Classification, Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1955, r. 14(8) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Removal from service - Charge-sheet served upon appellant-Civil Judge alleging possession of disproportionate money/assets - Enquiry proceeding initiated against appellant - During the course of enquiry, the appellant made an application for permission to engage a legal practitioner to assist him in the departmental enquiry - Said request was declined by the HC - Enquiry officer submitted his report and returned the finding of guilt against the appellant - Relying on the enquiry officer's report, disciplinary authority removed appellant from service - Appellant preferred an appeal against this order before the Governor which also came to be dismissed - Said order of removal from service, as confirmed by the appellate authority, was challenged by the appellant before HC - HC dismissed said writ petition which was upheld by DB - Appellant challenged said order submitting that he had asked for assistance of a legal practitioner which had been unfairly denied to him - Appellant also submitted that the enquiry officer as well as the HC have not appreciated the evidence in its proper perspective - (A) Whether denial of a legal practitioner to appellant was violation of principles of natural justice as well as M.P. Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1966? - Held, on principle of fairness it cannot be said that the order has caused any prejudice to the appellant - Request of the appellant has been rightly rejected by the disciplinary authority - (B) Whether there is perversity in appreciation of the evidence in the impugned judgment by the enquiry officer as well as the HC? - Held, the finding of facts arrived at by the enquiry officer was not interfered with by the Single Judge as well as the DB, it is hardly permissible for this Court to disturb such findings of fact in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 136 of the Constitution of India - Moreover, department showed that the deposits have been made and the bank balance of the appellant, on a particular date, was beyond the known sources of his income to which, the appellant has raised a defence that he owned land and the income received was an agricultural income - However, he produced no evidence during the departmental enquiry to show that some person was making payment to him and/or some person was depositing the money in the Bank so received from agricultural activity - Having failed to produce relevant documentary evidence as well as examine the witnesses, the appellant cannot argue that the Disciplinary Authority or the Courts have not appreciated the evidence in its correct perspective - Appeal dismissed.
