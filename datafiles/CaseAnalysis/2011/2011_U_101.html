Trans Mediterranean Airways v Universal Exports and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 688; (2011) 10 SCC 316; 2012 (1) AWC 277; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 336; 2011 (4) CPJ(Sc) 13; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 570; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 472; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Carriage by Air Act, 1934, Air Cargo Tariff Rules

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 4, 12, 23, 29 - Carriage by AIR Act, 1972, ss. 2, 2(ii), 3, 4, 4A, 5, 6, 6A, 7, rr. 1, 12, 13, 14, 18, 19, 28, 29, 29(2), 33 - Wrong consignment - Compensation - Sustainability - Agent made three airway bills for shipping of garments to Spain on behalf of consignor through appellant-carrier - Airway bills were dated and consignment were sent to Madrid - Appellant-carrier informed consigner that on finding full name and complete postal address of consignee as Appellant-carrier delivered consignment to 'X' - Consignor claimed that consignee of said consignment was 'Y' - Appellant carrier had wrongly delivered consignment to address mentioned in Block column instead of routing it through 'Y' and there was deficiency of service - Consignor instituted complaint before National Commission claiming compensation for deficiency of service by appellant-carrier and agent for not delivering said consignment to consignee - National commission concluded that services rendered by appellant-carrier were deficient - Hence instant appeal - Whether National Commission had jurisdiction to entertain and decide a complaint filed by consignor claiming compensation for deficiency of service by carrier and

Whether appellant could be directed to compensate consignor for deficiency of service in facts and circumstances of case - Held, National Commission had jurisdiction to decide dispute between parties and it was a Court and that there was deficiency in service by appellant-carrier - Further, persons entitled to delivery should make a complaint to carrier in writing non- delivery of goods within 120 days from date of issue of airway bill - There was no question of delivery of goods to shipper/Complainant -

Rule 29(2) specifically provides that questions of procedure shall be governed by law of Court seized of the case- Right to damages should be extinguished if an action was not brought within 2 years, reckoned from date of arrival at destination, or from date on which aircraft ought to have arrived, or from date on which carriage stopped - Thus, method of calculating period of limitation should be determined by law of Court seized of case -

Complainant entrusted goods carrier and goods reached Madrid complaint was filed within a period of 2 years - Contract entered into before damage occurred by which parties purport to infringe rules laid down by schedule, whether by deciding law to be applied, or by altering rules as to jurisdiction, should be null and void - Hence, cl.12 of airway bill would not be of any ground for holding that petition filed by Complainant was barred by period limitation - - Appeal dismissed.
