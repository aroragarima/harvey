Karnataka Power Corporation Limited Through Its v K. Thangappan and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 114; (2006) 4 SCC 322; AIR 2006 SC 1581; 2006 (2) AWC 2021; JT 2006 (4) SC 312; 2006(4) SCALE 56; 2006 (4) SCJ 608; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 783; 2006 (3) SLR 27; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1552

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 12(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226, art. 32 - Respondent did not report for duty since February, 1979 and accordingly his name was removed from nominal muster roll - In October 1997, respondent No 1-workman addressed a letter to Corporation and sought employment as a Mason - Order of Single Judge directing appellants to appoint respondent No 1 in an appropriate vacancy in terms of cl. 4 of Settlement - Appeal against - Held, when writ jurisdiction is invoked, unexplained delay coupled with creation of third party rights in meantime is an important factor which also weighs with HC in deciding whether or not to exercise such jurisdiction - Whether cl (4) of Settlement was applicable to respondent No 1-workman could not have been adjudicated in a Writ - Respondent No 1-workman was not entitled to any relief - Appeal allowed.
