State of Punjab and Others v Gamdoor Singh

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1365; (1998) 8 SCC 366; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1689

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115 (7) - Punjab Civil Services (Premature Retirement) Rules, 1975, r. 2 (1) - Voluntary retirement - Competent authority - Acceptance - Legality - Respondent was appointed as Nazir Niabat Amolh on 1-5-1947 by Registrar of HC - Subsequently, said HC was merged with Pepsu State on 1-9-1948 - Later on, it was merged along with Pepsu with Punjab State - Disciplinary action for unauthorised absence from duty was initiated against respondent - At that time respondent voluntarily addressed letter on 6-7-1977 to Sub-Judge under whom he was serving, sought permission to retire voluntarily - Sub-Judge granted permission and respondent was retired from service w.e.f.31-8-1977 - Surprisingly respondent challenged permission so granted by Sub-Judge and consequential retirement order u/s. 115 (7) of the Act on the ground that authority to grant such permission was not Sub-Judge but Chief Justice/Registrar of HC - Respondent filed suit challenging said order of voluntary retirement - Trial Court did not accept said contention and dismissed suit - On appeal, Appellate Court accepted contention of respondent and allowed appeal - HC also confirmed it on the ground that appointing authority so far as respondent was concerned was Chief Justice/Registrar of HC - Hence instant appeal - Held, it was not in dispute that at the relevant time viz. 31-8-1977, appointing authority for post of Nazir Niabat was Senior Subordinate Judge - As a matter of fact HC had noticed this position - HC committed mistake by noticing provision in the Act without noticing the rules framed under the proviso to that section changing the service conditions concerning premature retirement - It was fairly stated by appellant that the Rules were not brought to the notice of HC - Combined reading of proviso to s. 115 (7) of the Act and r. 2(1) of the Rules, SC was of view that acceptance of letter of voluntary retirement given by respondent and consequential order retiring him from service prematurely by Senior Subordinate Judge was within his power and finding of HC that acceptance and consequential order should have been by HC was not correct - Appeal allowed.
