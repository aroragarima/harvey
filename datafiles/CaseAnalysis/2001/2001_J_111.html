Christ The King Cathedral v John Ancheril and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20104; (2001) 6 SCC 170; AIR 2001 SC 2543; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 6; 2002 (47) ALR 225; JT 2001 (5) SC 604; 2001 (2) KLT 946; 2001 (2) RCJ 230; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 155; 2001(4) SCALE 491; 2001 (4) SCJ 288; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1112; 2001 (5) SLT 359; 2001 (5) Supreme 248

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Madras Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1949

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Administrative - Tamil Nadu Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss. 29, 25 - Madras Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1949 - Constitution of India,1950, art. 14 - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, ss. 4, 5, 7, 8, 11, 13, 25 - Notification of exemption by State Govt. - Challenged - Validity - State Govt. had published notification in Gazette exempting buildings of all Churches/Mosques of all minority religions from provisions of ss.4, 5, 7, 8, 11 and 13 of the 1965 Act in public interest and amended it by second notification S.R.O. No. 769/96 exempting buildings of all Dioceses, Arch- dioceses, Monasteries, Convents, Wakfs and Madarsas from provisions of ss. 4, 5, 7, 8, 11 and 13 of the 1965 Act - Batch of writ petitions were filed in HC challenging validity of these notifications - Writ petitions were allowed by HC holding that it was arbitrary as buildings of Churches or Mosques were commercially used and also held that granting of such blanket exemptions would defeat secular nature of the 1950 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, SC in P.J. Irani v. State of Madras, 1961 Indlaw SC 69 held that similar powers vested under the 1949 Act exempting buildings from provisions of Madras Act was not violative of Article 14 of the 1950 Act and held that charitable and religious trusts or endowments fall into separate class - Similarly SC in S. Kandaswamy Chettiar v. State of Tamil Nadu & Anr., 1984 Indlaw SC 354 upheld grant of total exemption in respect of buildings belonging to public trusts religious or charitable in nature and indicated guidelines to be followed while granting exemption - HC had unnecessarily gone at tangent on various constitutional and administrative law questions which were unnecessary and it ought to have riveted its attention only on whether there was due application of mind in issuing notifications in question - Notifications could be upheld if they were issued in public interest - Kerala HC in several cases upheld grant of exemption to charitable bodies if issued in public interest even though it was not stated in notification granting exemption it would be enough if stated in counter-affidavit - Notifications granting exemption were indisputably being in public interest same could not be challenged - Order of HC was set aside and restored notifications - Appeals of State Govt. were allowed and other appeals were dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
