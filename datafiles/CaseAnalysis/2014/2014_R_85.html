Additional District and Sessions Judge 'X' v Registrar General, High Court of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 872; (2015) 4 SCC 91; AIR 2015 SC 645; 2015 (2) AWC 1580; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 73; JT 2014 (14) SC 146; 2015 (1) MLJ 188; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 983; 2014(14) SCALE 238

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Women & Children

Keywords: Member, Sexual Harassment, Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968, Madhya Pradesh Dekaiti Aur Vyapaharan Prabhavit Kshotra Adhiniyam, 1981

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Allegation against sitting Judge - In-house procedure - Guidelines - Instant writ petition filed by a former Additional District and Sessions Judge of the Madhya Pradesh Higher Judicial Service - Petitioner alleged sexual harassment, at the behest of a sitting Judge of the HC of Madhya Pradesh ('the HC'), who was impleaded by name as respondent no.3 (Justice 'A')- Authenticity of the allegations levelled by the petitioner, were disputed by respondent no.3 - Petitioner submitted that she having been subjected to victimization and harassment, the petitioner submitted her resignation from the post of Additional District and Sessions Judge - Chief Justice recommended the acceptance of the resignation to the State Govt., which was accepted - Having suffered extreme vilification at the hands of her superiors, for having not been afforded even an opportunity of being able to express her difficulties, the petitioner sent a representation to the President of India, the Chief Justice of India and the Chief Justice of the HC - 'Two-Judge Committee', was constituted - Petitioner submitted that the proceedings being conducted in the matter, were not in consonance with the 'in-house procedure' adopted by SC for taking suitable remedial action against judges, who by their acts of omission or commission, do not follow the accepted values of judicial life - Whether proceedings being conducted in the matter, were not in consonance with the 'in-house procedure' adopted by SC for taking suitable remedial action against judges.

Held, existence of the 'in-house procedure' is now an established means for inquiring into allegations levelled against a judge of a superior court, through his peers. It is a confidential inquiry for institutional credibility under the charge of the Chief Justice of India. In the instant case, by not strictly abiding by the procedure contemplated under the 'in-house procedure' evolved by SC, the Chief Justice of the HC, introduced serious infirmities in the investigative process. The Chief Justice of the HC, by constituting a 'two-Judge Committee', commenced an in-depth probe, into the allegations levelled by the petitioner. The procedure adopted by the Chief Justice of the HC, forms a part of the second stage (contemplated under steps four to seven). The second stage of the 'in-house procedure' is to be carried out, under the authority of the Chief Justice of India. The Chief Justice of the HC by constituting a 'two-Judge Committee' clearly traversed beyond his jurisdictional authority, under the 'in-house procedure'. In order to ensure, that the investigative process is fair and just, it is imperative to divest the concerned judge (against whom allegations have been levelled), of his administrative and supervisory authority and control over witnesses, to be produced either on behalf of the complainant, or on behalf of the concerned judge himself. The Chief Justice of the HC is accordingly directed to divest respondent no.3 - Justice 'A', of the administrative and supervisory control vested in him, to the extent expressed above. Petition disposed of.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Allegation against sitting Judge - In-house procedure - Guidelines.

Held, 'in-house procedure' framed by SC, can be adopted, to examine allegations levelled against Judges of HCs, Chief Justices of HCs and Judges of the Supreme Court of India. The investigative process under the 'in-house procedure' takes into consideration the rights of the complainant, and that of the concerned judge, by adopting a fair procedure, to determine the veracity of allegations levelled against a sitting Judge. At the same time, it safeguards the integrity of the judicial institution. Even though the said procedure, should ordinarily be followed in letter and spirit, the Chief Justice of India, would have the authority to mould the same, in the facts and circumstances of a given case, to ensure that the investigative process affords safeguards, against favouritism, prejudice or bias. In view of the importance of the 'in-house procedure', it is essential to bring it into public domain. The Registry of the Supreme Court of India, is accordingly directed, to place the same on the official website of the Supreme Court of India. Petition disposed of.
