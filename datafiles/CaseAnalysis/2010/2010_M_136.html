Har Narain (Dead) By Lrs. v Mam Chand (Dead) By Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 844; (2010) 13 SCC 128; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 78; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 34; 2011 (1) AWC 756; JT 2010 (11) SC 642; 2010 (4) KLT 359; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 723; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 853; 2011 (112) RD 184; 2010(10) SCALE 617; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Registration Act, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Doctrine of lis pendens - Applicability - Respondent No.1 mortgaged suit land in favour of the predecessor-in-interest of the appellant and he was also put in possession of the said land in the year 1970 - Later, respondent No.1 entered into an agreement for sale of the said property with the appellant - However, the respondent No.1 also executed the sale deed in favour of respondent nos.2 to 6 in respect of suit property - Thereafter, appellant filed a suit for specific performance against the respondent no.1 for executing the sale deed of the land in question - Trial Court held that respondents 2 to 6 were bona fide purchasers for consideration without notice and therefore, the sale deed in their favour was to be protected - Order was upheld by First Appellate Court - On appeal, HC also upheld the proposition laid down by the courts below that principles of lis pendens would not apply in the facts and circumstances of present case as the sale deed was executed before the filing of the suit though, the same was got registered subsequent to the institution of the suit - Hence, present appeals - (A) Whether in the fact-situation of present case, the sale deed executed by the respondent No.1 in favour of respondent nos.2 to 6 could be subject to the doctrine of lis pendens? - Held, sale deed in question requires registration - Further, in spite of the fact that the registration of the sale deed would relate back to the date of execution, the sale cannot be termed as complete until its registration and it becomes effective only once it stands registered - Hence, sale executed by respondent No.1 in favour of respondent Nos. 2 to 6 could not be termed as a complete sale until the document got registered - Sale stood completed during the pendency of the suit - Hence, doctrine of lis pendens would apply in the present case as the registration of the sale deed was subsequent to filing of the suit and subsequent purchasers i.e. respondent nos. 2 to 6 cannot claim benefit of the provisions of s. 19(b) of the Act, 1963 - (B) Whether respondent nos.2 to 6 can be held to be vendees without notice of an agreement to sell in favour of the appellant by the respondent no.1? - Held, sale deed in favour of the respondent Nos. 2 to 6 clearly disclosed that the suit land had been mortgaged to the appellant and it was in his possession since 1970 - Hence, respondents were fully aware that the suit land was in possession of the appellant and therefore question of respondent Nos. 2 to 6 being bonafide purchasers for value and paid money in good faith without notice does not arise - Judgment and decree of the courts below set aside - Appeals allowed.
