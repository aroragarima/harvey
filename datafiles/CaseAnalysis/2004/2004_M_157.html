Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai v Mahindra and Mahindra Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, P.K. Balasubramanyan, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1490; (2005) 10 SCC 253; 2005 (179) E.L.T. 21; 2005 (66) RLT 553

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173Q - Whether CEGAT erred in setting aside order of Commission of Central Excise on ground that Modvat credit was available to respondent-assessee and on that basis intention alone coming to conclusion that there could be no intention to evade payment of duty and thus allegation of suppression so as to invoke larger period of limitation under proviso to aforesaid s. 11A? - There can be number of eventualities where extended period of limitation in terms of proviso to s. 11A may be available to department despite availability of Modvat credit to an assessee - Availability of Modvat credit to an assessee by itself is not conclusive or decisive consideration - Held, tribunal has not examined any other consideration except availability of Modvat credit to appellant - Impugned judgment set aside - Matter remitted back to tribunal.
