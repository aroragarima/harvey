Kasturi and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1396; (2003) 1 SCC 354; AIR 2003 SC 202; 2002 (5) AWC 3513; JT 2002 (9) SC 299; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 278; 2002(8) SCALE 426; 2002 (6) SLT 456; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 117; 2003 (1) UC 218; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 131

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Ss. 4, 6 and 18 - Contention that the cut of 20% on the amount of compensation was not at all justified having regard to the fact that the acquired land was in a fully developed area - held, the fact that an area is developed or adjacent to a developed area will not ipso facto make every land situated in the area also developed to be valued as a building site or plot, particularly when vast tracts are acquired, as in this case, for development purpose - May be the acquired land with potentiality for construction of residential and commercial buildings had some advantages, which aspect is taken note of by the High Court in giving cut of only 20% as against 1/3 normal deduction.
