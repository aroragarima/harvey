Col. Sir Harinder Singh Brar Bans Bahadur v Bihari Lal and another

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2226; (1994) 4 SCC 523; JT 1994 (3) SC 1; 1994(2) SCALE 285; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, s. 18(2),(3) and (4) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1),11,16,17(1), 18, 30,31(2) - Deemed Owner - Acquisition of Land - Compensation - Entitlement - Respondent no. 1, became deemed owner on 12-3-1963, u/s. 18(4) of the 1953 Act - Land was acquired as per notification and whole compensation was paid to respondent/tenant in first instance - Appellant filed application, before Reference Court which was allowed stating that landowner alone was entitled to whole compensation - Respondent/deemed owner filed appeal which was partly allowed and compensation awarded was apportionable in ratio of 3:1 - Appellant filed appeal before DB - DB allowed appeal partly, holding appellant/land owner entitled to limited amount of compensation - In second instance, compensation for land acquired was paid to appellant/land owner - Reference Court held that entire amount of compensation was liable to be paid to respondent/tenant - Appellant/land owner filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - (A) Whether appellant cease to owner of land which could be purchased by tenant u/s. 18 of the 1953 Act and thereby cease to had any interest in it , on payment of purchase price by deemed owner - Held, object of tenancy legislation was to make tenant, tiller of land, its owner, beneficial provisions which were made to confer ownership rights on tenants could not receive a construction which results in either postponing or defeating right of ownership conferred in respect of their tenanted lands - Therefore, land owner of land which could be purchased by tenant ceases to be its owner and consequently cease to had any interest in such land - (B) Whether land owner who, as owner of tenanted land before its acquisition and vesting under the 1894 Act, was entitle to claim whole component of compensation - Held, amount of compensation to which landowner becomes entitled could only be purchase price, which he would be entitled u/s. 18(2) and (3) of the 1953 Act, for his land, which tenant had right to purchase there under - In instant tenant became deemed owner on 12-3-1963 was acquired and vested in State on 8-4-1968, which was subsequent to tenant becoming its deemed owner - Therefore, landowner had no right to make claim for compensation payable for that land under the 1894, Act as he had lost all his interest in land well before acquisition proceedings for acquiring that land had commenced - On second instance of acquisition amount of compensation should had been paid in its entirety on being deemed owner of acquired land - In instant case, appellant was liable to pay entire amount received by him from L.A.O to make good loss suffered by tenant - Appeals dismissed.
