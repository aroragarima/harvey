Manohar Lal v Vinesh Anand and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19955; (2001) 5 SCC 407; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1322; AIR 2001 SC 1820; 2001 ALJ 921; 2001 (2) AllCriR 1393; 2001 (2) ARBLR 160; 2001 (3) AWC 1712; 2001 (2) CCR 143; 2001 (2) CGLJ 349; 2001 (7) CLC 1036; 2001 (2) CompLJ 207; 2001 CRLJ 2044; JT 2001 (4) SC 573; 2001 (1) RAJ 536; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 475; 2001(3) SCALE 270; 2001 (4) SCJ 478; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1036; 2001 (3) SLT 481; 2001 (3) Supreme 279; 2001 (1) UC 726B

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Defence Of India Act, 1971, Prize Competitions Act, 1955

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Criminal - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 195(3) and 340 - Whether arbitrator can be identified as Court? - Court includes Civil, Revenue, Criminal and Tribunal constituted under Central or State Act, if declared Court - Courts to be in capacity to deliver definite judgment - Procedure adopted by arbitrator has some legal character but arbitrator cannot be given status of Court - Held, arbitrator cannot be regarded as Court within meaning of s. 195(3) of CrPC, 1973 - Question of applicability of s. 340 CrPC, 1973 in proceeding before arbitrator does not and cannot arise - Appeal dismissed.
