Â Dhirajlal Girdharilal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1955 SC 271; [1954] 26 ITR 736

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 66 - Hindu undivided family - Arrangement - Inheritance - Transfer of shares - Suspicions and surmises - Inadmissible material - Appellant was Hindu undivided family comprised of 'A' and his two brothers 'B' and 'C' - 'A' was karta of family - On death of 'X' his three sons and his widow became entitled to amount stood to his credit in firm and by arrangement made after his death Hindu undivided family got shares of value from firm towards payment of its liability to Hindu undivided family as part of inheritance of 'X' and for balance Hindu undivided family shown as creditor of new firm - Appellant contended that family was not doing any business in stocks and shares - Appellate Assistant Commissioner accepted contention and gave reduction of Rs.1, 42, 025 in income as assessed - Commissioner of Income Tax filed an appeal and succeed - Tribunal allowed appeal and restored order of Income Tax Officer and concluded that moving spirit in new firm being 'A' who was also karta of Hindu undivided family knowing conditions obtaining in market effected transactions of transfer of shares to enable Hindu undivided family to realize profits - Appellant made application to HC u/s. 66(2) but application was dismissed on the ground that no question of law arose out of order of Tribunal - Whether finding of Tribunal was vitiated by reason of suspicions and surmises not supported by any evidence on record or upon partly inadmissible material - Held, when court of fact acts on material, partly relevant and partly irrelevant it's impossible to say to what extent mind of court was affected by irrelevant material used by it in arriving at its finding such finding vitiated because of use of inadmissible material and issue of law arises - Tribunal and HC were in error that no issue of law arose and Tribunal could not be called upon to state case and to refer to HC any issue of law - Questions framed by appellant are comprehensive enough to embrace issue of law arises out of order of Tribunal - Order of HC dismissing the application of appellant u/s. 66(2) of 1922 Act, set aside - Appeal allowed.
