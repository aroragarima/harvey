Kanwar Singh Saini v High Court of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 601; (2012) 4 SCC 307; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 58; 2012 (90) ALR 24; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 327; JT 2011 (11) SC 544; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 409; 2012 (1) OLR 116; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 402; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 407; 2012 (115) RD 291; 2011(10) SCALE 725; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 972

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 32, O. 39 r. 2A - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss.10, 11, 12 - Failure to obey the undertaking given in civil suit - Contempt proceedings - Legality - Plaintiff filed suit alleging that defendant/appellant had tried to dispossess the suit property after executing sale deed and handing over the possession thereof - Appellant filed written statement denying the allegations - Trial Court disposed of the suit on the basis of appellant's undertaking that he would not attempt to dispossess the plaintiff - Plaintiff filed an application before the HC under the provisions of 1971 Act alleging the violation of the undertaking given by the appellant to the civil court - HC directed the plaintiff to approach the civil court - Plaintiff filed an application under O. 39 r. 2A CPC r/w ss. 10, 11, 12 of the Act before the Trial Court on the ground that appellant and his family had trespassed into the suit property and had broken the locks of the main door - Trial Court referred the application to HC - HC, by impugned order, held that the appellant guilty of criminal contempt on the basis of inconsistent pleas taken by him and also for the breach of undertaking and imposed simple imprisonment for four months - Whether the impugned order that imposed punishment on appellant for violating the undertaking given by him to civil court was legal - Held, in case there was a grievance of non-compliance of the terms of the decree passed in the civil suit, the remedy available to the aggrieved person was to approach the execution court under O. 21 r. 32 CPC which provided for elaborate proceedings in which the parties could adduce their evidence and could examine and cross-examine the witnesses as opposed to the proceedings in contempt which were summary in nature - Application under O. 39 r. 2A CPC was not maintainable once the suit stood decreed - Law did not permit to skip the remedies available under O. 21 r. 32 CPC and resort to the contempt proceedings for the reason that the court had to exercise its discretion under the 1971 Act when an effective and alternative remedy was not available to the person concerned - Thus, when the matter related to the infringement of a decree or decretal order embodied rights, as between the parties, it was not expedient to invoke and exercise contempt jurisdiction, in essence, as a mode of executing the decree or merely because other remedies would take time or were more circumlocutory in character - Thus, the violation of permanent injunction could be set right in executing the proceedings and not the contempt proceedings - Further, the appellant was subjected to unfair procedure from the institution of the suit itself - Suit had been 'disposed of' in great haste without following the procedure prescribed in CPC - Once the suit had been decreed, the court could not entertain the application under O.39 r. 2A CPC as the suit had already been decreed and such an application was maintainable only during the pendency of the suit in case the interim order passed by the court or undertaking given by the party was violated - In the instant case, no interim order had ever been passed and the undertaking given by the appellant/defendant not to dispossess the plaintiff culminated into a final decree and thus, if any further action was required, it could be taken only in execution proceedings - Hence, impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
