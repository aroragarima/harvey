Laxmikant V. Patel v Chetanbhat Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19796; (2002) 3 SCC 65; AIR 2002 SC 275; AIR 2001 SCW 4989; 2002 (1) ALD(SC) 45; 2002 (46) ALR 324; 2002 (1) CLC 1; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 518; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 997; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 293; JT 2001 (10) SC 285; 2002 (24) PTC 1; 2002 (1) RAJ 184; 2001(8) SCALE 350; 2002 (1) SCJ 56; 2001 (8) SLT 284; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 435; 2001 (8) Supreme 558; 2002 (1) UC 182

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trade Marks Act, 1999 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, rr. 1, 2 - Passing off - Plaintiff, started business of colour lab and studio in name and style of Muktajivan Colour Lab and Studio - In order to develop his business plaintiff has promoted his wife to open a new Colour Lab in name and style of Muktajivan Colour Studio - Defendant No. 1 who was carrying on his similar business in name and style of 'Gokul Studio' is intending to commence business through his wife, defendant No. 2 by adopting name and style of Muktajivan Colour Lab and Studio - Passing off action was initiated by plaintiff by filing a suit seeking issuance of permanent preventive injunction against defendants restraining them from passing off their business, services and goods as of and for business, services and goods of plaintiff - Whether High Court and Trial Court was justified in refusing grant of injunction, plaintiff-Appellant? - Neither Trial Court nor HC have kept in view and applied their mind to relevant settled principles of law governing grant or refusal of interlocutory injunction in trade mark and trade name disputes - A refusal to grant an injunction in spite of availability of facts, are prima facie established by overwhelming evidence and material available on record justifying grant thereof, occasion a failure of justice and such injury to plaintiff as would not be capable of being undone at a latter stage - Discretion exercised by trial Court and HC against plaintiff, is neither reasonable nor judicious - Held, grant of interlocutory injunction to plaintiff could not have been refused, therefore, an ad interim injunction u/rr. 1 and 2 of O. 39 of CPC shall issue in favour of plaintiff-appellant restraining defendant-respondents from using directly or indirectly word 'Muktajivan' in their trade name associated with business and services of Colour Lab and Studio and any other similar word or name which may be identical or deceptively similar to plaintiff's trade name - Appeals allowed.
