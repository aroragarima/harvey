Dr. A. K. Jain and Others v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28778; (1987) Supp SCC 497; (1988) SCC (L&S) 222; JT 1987 (4) SC 445; 1987(2) SCALE 1002; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 335; 1989 (1) SLJ 188

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Petitioners were appointed as Assistant Medical officers with initial appointments for period of six months - Respondents/railway extended their tenure from time to time - Petitioners contended that respondents had threatened to terminate their services - Respondents terminated services of petitioners as they had not availed of chances stated in their appointment order of Commission - Petitioners filed petitions against respondent - Respondent contended petitioners belonged to category of ad-hoc doctors were appointed as temporary measure for specified period - Whether service termination of petitioners was justified - Held, services of petitioners had terminated except on resignation or disciplinary grounds, should be considered for regularization with continuity of service but without back wages - Respondent was directed that Assistant Divisional Medical officers selected by Commission should be first be posted to available vacant posts - All Assistant Medical officers working on ad hoc basis should be paid same salary and allowances as Assistant Divisional Medical officers on revised scale with arrears - No ad hoc Assistant Medical officer should be replaced by newly appointed - Commission should grant relaxation in age, to extent of period of service rendered by them as ad hoc doctors in Railways - Petitions disposed of.
