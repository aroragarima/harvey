State of Orissa v Dibakar Naik and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 259; (2002) 5 SCC 323; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1128; AIR 2002 SC 2148; 2002 (2) CCR 205; 2002 (2) Crimes 279; 2002 (4) CRJ 171; 2002 CRLJ 2826; 2002 (2) JCC 835; JT 2002 (4) SC 303; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 159; 2002(4) SCALE 62; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 307; 2002 (3) SLT 577; 2002 (3) Supreme 456; 2002 (2) UC 103A

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Since, the High Court was wrong in acquitting the accused on surmises and conjectures ignoring the trustworthy evidence of PW1 and reaching at perverse conclusions regarding the facts of the case, the case can be interfered with by the Supreme Court under Article 136 of the Constitution. Further, culpable homicide is murder if the act by which the death is caused is done with the intention of causing death and is not covered by any of the exceptions of Section 300 of the Indian Penal Code.
