Principal, Mehar Chand Polytechnic and Another v Anu Lumba and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 413; (2006) 7 SCC 161; AIR 2006 SC 3074; JT 2006 (7) SC 322; 2006 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2483; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 17; 2006(7) SCALE 648; 2006 (6) SCJ 185; 2006 (5) SLR 430; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(4), 162, 21, 309, 335 - Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act, 1959 - Legal right of regularization of Respondents in services, although appointed for a fixed period in a project - Held, respondents did not have legal right to be absorbed in service - Prior to their appointments, requirements of provisions of art.14 and 16 had not been complied with - There did not exist any sanctioned post - Project undertaken by Union of India although continued for some time was initially intended to be a time bound one - In absence of any legal right in respondents, HC could not have issued a writ of or in nature of mandamus - Appeals partly allowed.
