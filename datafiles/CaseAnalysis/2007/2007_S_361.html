Jameel v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 28; (2007) 11 SCC 420; AIR 2007 SC 971; 2007 (3) ALT 89; 2007 CRLJ 1425; JT 2007 (3) SC 237; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 738; 2007(2) SCALE 32; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 946

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 376 r/w 511, 377; Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, deposition of the prosecutrix, clearly shows that she was absolutely an innocent girl - Having regard to the fact that the appellant was known to the prosecutrix and her family members and she having identified him before lodging of the F.I.R., it would have been futile to hold a test identification parade - Substantive evidence is the evidence of identification in court - Appeal dismissed.
