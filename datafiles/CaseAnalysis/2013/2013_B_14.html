Jitendra Raghuvanshi & Ors. v Babita Raghuvanshi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Jagdish Singh Khehar, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 142; (2013) 4 SCC 58; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 221; 2013 (3) G.L.R. 1875; 2013 (2) JabLJ 128; 2013 (2) JCC 1365; JT 2013 (4) SC 98; 2013 (2) KLT 47; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 736; 2013 (2) OLR 97; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 427; 2013 (2) RLW 1416; 2013(3) SCALE 537; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non-Compoundable Offences

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 406 r/w s. 34 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 320 - Non-compoundable offences - Quashing of - Inherent powers - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 498A, 406 r/w s. 34 of IPC and u/ss. 3 and 4 of 1961 Act - Pending trial, appellants and respondent (wife of appellant no. 1) amicably settled their matrimonial dispute - Respondent filed an application stating that she did not wish to pursue criminal proceedings against appellants - However, Trial Court dismissed application - Aggrieved appellants filed an application u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC to quash criminal proceedings pending against them - HC by impugned judgment dismissed application holding that HC has no power to quash criminal proceedings in respect of offences where were non-compoundable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC had inherent powers u/s. 482 of CrPC to quashing of criminal proceedings in non-compoundable offences related to matrimonial disputes - Held, it was duty of Courts to encourage genuine settlements of matrimonial disputes, particularly, when same were on considerable increase - Even if offences were non-compoundable, if they relate to matrimonial disputes and Court was satisfied that parties have settled the same amicably and without any pressure, for the purpose of securing ends of justice, s. 320 of CrPC would not be a bar to exercise of power of quashing of FIR, complaint or subsequent criminal proceedings - In instant case, impugned order of HC Court shows that because mutual settlement arrived at between parties related to non-compoundable offence, Court proceeded on a wrong premise that it could not be compounded and dismissed petition filed u/s. 482 of CrPC - Perusal of petition filed before HC u/s. 482 of CrPC show that application filed by appellants was not for compounding of non-compoundable offences but for the purpose of quashing criminal proceedings - HC ought to have quashed criminal proceedings by accepting settlement arrived at - It was duty of Courts to encourage genuine settlements of matrimonial disputes and s. 482 of CrPC enabled HC and art. 142 of Constitution enabled SC to pass such orders - S. 320 of CrPC did not limit or affect powers of HC u/s. 482 of CrPC - Impugned criminal proceedings were quashed - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
