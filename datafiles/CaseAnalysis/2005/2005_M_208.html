National Insurance Company Limited v Mastan and another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 947; (2006) 2 SCC 641; 2006 (1) ACC 1; AIR 2006 SC 577; 2006 (62) ALR 328; 2006 (1) AWC 404; [2006] 129 Comp Cas 81; 2006 (1) CTC 222; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 17; JT 2005 (10) SC 440; 2006 (1) KarLJ 342; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 53; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 1; 2006 (2) RLW 1319; 2005(10) SCALE 124; 2006 (1) SCJ 40; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 149, 165, 167 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Whether an insurer, while defending an action initiated under 1923 Act is precluded from raising ay defence as envisaged in u/s. 149(2) of 1988 Act? - Held, once a leave has been granted to insurer to contest claim on any ground as envisaged in s.170 of 1988 Act, an appeal shall also be maintainable as a matter of right, wherein HC can go into all contentions - Appeals allowed.
