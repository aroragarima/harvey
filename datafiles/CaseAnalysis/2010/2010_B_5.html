S. Nagaraj (Dead) By Lrs. And Others v B. R. Vasudeva Murthy And Others Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 96; (2010) 3 SCC 353; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 551; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 433; JT 2010 (2) SC 185; 2010 (3) KarLJ 513; 2010(2) SCALE 232; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 586

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Amenity, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, U.P. (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, Mysore (Personal & Miscellaneous) Inam Abolition Act, 1954, Karnataka Inam Abolition Laws (Amendment) Act, 1979, Karnataka Land Grants Rules, 1969

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Karnataka Inam Abolition Laws (Amendment Act) 1979 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, 79-A, 79-B and 80 - Mysore (Personal & Miscellaneous) Inam Abolition Act, 1954 - Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964, s. 95 - Allocation of land - Inam Abolition Act was enacted for abolition of personal Inams and other miscellaneous Inams in the State of Mysore, except Bellari District - On the Inam Abolition Act, all rights, title and interests vested in the Inamdars ceased and vested absolutely in the State of Mysore free from all encumbrances - Every Inamdar was entitled to be registered as an occupant of land and could make an application before the Special Deputy Commissioner, Inam Abolition, for such registration as an occupant - Two Inamdars, filed applications for registration as occupants in respect of certain lands before the Special Deputy Commissioner - Meanwhile, State Government accorded sanction for grant of the land in favour of the Sangha for providing house sites to the Members of the Sangha subject to the decision in the dispute pending before the Special Deputy Commissioner, Inam Abolition - One of the Inams filed a suit questioning the grant made by the State Government in favour of the Sangha - However, the Inams entered into an agreement with the Sangha to withdraw the suit on receipt of some amount - Meanwhile, the Inam Abolition Act was amended providing that the Tribunal constituted u/s. 48 of the Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 would decided the claim instead of the Special Deputy Commissioner - Thereafter, Tribunal ordered the confirmation of the occupancy rights in the suit land in favour of said two Inamdars jointly - However, Sangha got the layout plan of the land of 34 acres 3 guntas allotted to the Sangha sanctioned from the Bangalore Development Authority (BDA) and allotted sites to its members and the members of the Sangha built houses on some of these sites and some members also transferred their house sites to others - Writ petition was filed challenging order granting the land in favour of the Sangha - DB of HC held that the order of the State Government was valid and that the Inamdars were only entitled to the price payable for the land - Writ Appeal was filed against the said order - DB of the HC after deciding various issues raised by the parties dismissed the writ appeal - SLP filed against the said was disposed of - Minister, Revenue, Government of Karnataka passed the order issuing directions for cancellation of grant made in favour of the Sangha and directions were issued to the BDA to handover the vacant civic amenity sites to the Inamdars free of cost - Said order was challenged before the HC - HC decided said writ petitions by a common judgment - It was held that Minister, Revenue, Government of Karnataka, was justified in passing the order cancelling the grant in favour of the Sangha and further order of the State Government sanctioning the grant of the land in favour of the Sangha for allotment of house sites to its members was void ab initio - Present appeals filed against common judgment of the DB - (A) Whether findings recorded by the DB of HC in earlier writ petition had become final and binding on the parties, namely, the LRs of the Inamdars, the State of Karnataka and the Sangha or Teachers' Colony Residents Association? - Held, decisions on the issues in the earlier judgment of the DB of HC were not disturbed by SC in the SLP and, therefore, the decisions on the those issues became final and binding on the parties, namely, the legal representatives of the Inamdars, the State Government and the Sangha and its members - Order refusing special leave to appeal does not stand substituted in place of order under challenge and all that it means is that SC was not inclined to exercise its discretion so as to allow the appeal being filed - (B) It was submitted that order of the State Government sanctioning granting land in favour of Sangha for house sites was void ab initio because of the prohibitions in ss. 79-A, 79-B and 80 of the Land Reforms Act - Held, as per provisions of sub-sections (2) and (7) of s. 95 of the Karnataka Land Revenue Act, 1964 land held for agricultural purpose can be permitted to be diverted for other purposes on payment of fine - Law permitted the grant of the agricultural land in favour of the Sangha for house sites on payment of conversion fine and the grant made by the State Government in favour of the Sangha was not void ab initio on this count - (C) Order passed by the Minister, Revenue, Government of Karnataka - Legality - Held, Minister, Revenue, while considering the application of the Inamdars, ignored the findings of the DB of the HC and took the view in his order that on the competent authority granting occupancy right to the Inamdars, the Inamdars had become the rightful owners of the land and action would have to be taken to cancel the grant made in favour of the Sangha - Further, civic amenity sites and the road, had become properties of the BDA and it was the BDA only which was empowered to deal with such properties - Order regarding civic amenity sites was thus without the authority of law - Directions of the Minister, Revenue, Government of Karnataka, set aside - Impugned common judgment HC set aside - Appeals disposed of.
