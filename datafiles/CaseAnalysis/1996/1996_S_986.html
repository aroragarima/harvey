N. Rajarathinam v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2156; (1996) 10 SCC 371; (1997) SCC (L&S) 90; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 327; 1996 (74) FLR 2394; JT 1996 (8) SC 447; 1997 (1) LLJ 224; 1996(7) SCALE 21; 1997 (1) SLJ 10; 1996 (6) SLR 696; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 593; 1996 (7) Supreme 635; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 152

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Dismissal - Petitioner, while working as Asst. Commr. of Commercial Taxes, demanded and accepted illegal gratification - Suspended - Enquiry - Dismissed - Tribunal upheld order of dismissal from service - Petition - Evidence Act, 1872 has no application for disciplinary proceedings - If all relevant facts and circumstances and evidence on record are taken into consideration and it is found that evidence establishes misconduct against a public servant, disciplinary authority is perfectly empowered to take appropriate decision as to nature of findings on proof of guilt - Also what should be nature of punishment to be imposed is for disciplinary authority to consider - Held, there is no allegation of misconduct against officer during his earlier career, but it does not mean that proved allegation is not sufficient to impose penalty of dismissal from service - No illegality in order passed by Tribunal warranting an interference - Petition dismissed.
