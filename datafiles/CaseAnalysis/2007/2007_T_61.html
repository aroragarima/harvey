The Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madurai Etc. Etc v M/S Saravana Spinning Mills Pvt.Ltd

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1032; (2007) 7 SCC 298; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 741; [2007] 293 ITR 201; JT 2007 (10) SC 111; 2007(9) SCALE 697; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals allowed., Repairs And Insurance

Summary: Income Tax - Section 31(i), Income Tax Act, 1961 - Extent and scope - Repairs and insurance of machinery, plant and furniture - The assessee is a textile mill engaged in the manufacture of yarn - Claimed deduction on account of "modernisation and replacement expenses" - Claim of the assessee was disallowed on the ground that the expenditure was on capital account; that it was not a revenue expenditure as the assessee had obtained enduring benefit by replacing the old machine with new machine and that the machine replaced was an independent machine by itself and that it was not a part or portion of the other textile machinery (plant) as claimed by the assessee - Whether the expenditure incurred by the assessee for modernisation and replacement came within the connotation of the words "current repairs" in s. 31(i) - Held, whether an expenditure is revenue or capital in nature would depend on the facts of each case - Each machine including the Ring Frame was an independent and separate machine capable of independent and specific function and, therefore, the expenditure incurred for replacement of the new machine would not come within the meaning of the words "current repairs" - It is not the case of the assessee that a part of the machine needed repairs, but the entire machine had been replaced - Expenditure incurred by the assessee did not fall within the meaning of "current repairs" in s. 31(i) - Even if the expenditure incurred is revenue in nature, still it may not fall in the connotation of the words "current repairs" - Assessees were not entitled to claim allowance under s. 31(i).
