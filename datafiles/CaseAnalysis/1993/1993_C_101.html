Salem Cooperative Central Bank Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1336; (1993) Supp4 SCC 200; AIR 1993 SC 1517; 1993 (111) CTR 394; [1993] 201 ITR 697; JT 1993 (3) SC 181; 1993(2) SCALE 460; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 997; [1993] 68 TAXMAN 33; 1993 (2) TLR 495

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961- A/y 1963-64 - Assessee was a cooperative society engaged in the business of banking - Its business income was exempt under the provisions of s. 81(1) of the Act as it then stood - During the said accounting year, the assessee received a sum of Rs. 19 being the interest on the deposit made by it with the Salem-Erode Electricity Distribution Company - Income Tax Officer treated the said amount of Rs. 19 as 'income from other sources' - On that basis, he levied additional surcharge, in a sum of Rs. 81,920, under the provisions of the relevant Finance Act - On Appeal, the AAC and the Tribunal upheld the assessee's contention that it was business income and therefore the liability of surcharge was not attracted - HC was of the opinion that the legal submission urged by the Revenue before the HC, no doubt for the first time, did call for serious consideration - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, if, additional surcharge was chargeable according to the Finance Act even in case the said sum of Rs. 19 represented business income, the HC cannot be called upon to act on the assumption that it is not so chargeable and answer the question stated. Such a course would neither be in the interest of law or justice. That the Revenue was also a party to the erroneous assumption of law makes little difference to the principle. Appeal dismissed
