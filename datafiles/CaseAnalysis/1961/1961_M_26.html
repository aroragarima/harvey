Mallsappa Bandeppa Desai and Others v Desai Mallappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1961 SC 1268; 1962 (2) MLJ 154; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 154; 1962 (2) SCJ 589; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Appellant's case was that respondent No. 1 who has been manager of family for many years has been trying to deprive them of their legitimate share in property and refused their request for partition - This claim was resisted by respondent 1 principally on ground that father of respondent 1 and grandfather of appellants had effected a partition of joint family properties - High Court agreed with learned judge that alleged partition had not been proved - Appellant's claim in respect of this property is based on principle of blending or throwing into common stock which is recognised by Hindu law - Whether this principle apply in regard to a property held by a Hindu female as a limited owner? - Held, a Hindu female who has acquired immovable property from her father as a limited owner is not a coparcener and as such has no interest in coparcenary property - She holds property as a limited owner, and on her death property has to devolve on next reversioner - A Hindu female owning a limited estate cannot circumvent rules of surrender and allow members of her husband's family to treat her limited estate as part of joint property belonging to said family - Such a result would be inconsistent with basic notion of blending and basic character of a limited owner's title to property held, by her - Appeal partly allowed.
