State Bank Of India v M/S B.S. Agricultural Industries (I)

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 2009 SC 2210; 2009 (3) AWC 3132; 2009 (1) CCC 146; 2009 (2) CPJ(Sc) 29; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 101; JT 2009 (4) SC 191; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 126; 2009 (4) MPLJ 309; 2009 (154) PLR 496; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 628; 2009(4) SCALE 467; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 762; 2009 (1) UC 669

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Legal Notice, Pump Set, Business Profits Tax Act, 1947, Collection Charge

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Alleged deficiency in service - Limitation period for admission of a complaint - Held, consumer forum must deal with the complaint on merits only if the complaint has been filed within two years from the date of accrual of cause of action and if beyond the said period, the sufficient cause has been shown and delay condoned for the reasons recorded in writing - Complaint is barred by time and ought to have been dismissed as such but curiously this aspect was not examined by any of the consumer fora although specific plea to this effect was taken by the Bank - Appeal allowed.
