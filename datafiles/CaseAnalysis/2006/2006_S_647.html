Radha Mohan Singh @ Lai Saheb and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, K.G. Balakrishnan, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 250; (2006) 2 SCC 450; 2006 (2) ADJ 388; AIR 2006 SC 951; 2006 CRLJ 1121; 2006 (2) CTC 762; 2006 (1) JCC 345; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 692; 2006(1) SCALE 369; 2006 (3) SCJ 466; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 519

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 323, 324, 302 and 149 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 429 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 161, 392 and 174 - Five accused of murderous assault - Convicted - Appeal dismissed by High Court - (A) whether findings recorded against appellants suffer from any infirmity? - While hearing an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution this Court will normally not enter into review of evidence unless Trial Court or High Court is shown to have committed an error of law or procedure and conclusions arrived at are perverse - Witnesses corroborated by medical evidence - Held, that records do not suffer from any infirmity - (B) Whether third judge who was called to express his opinion in matter under appeal in HC due to difference of opinion between two judges ought to have leaned in favour of view taken by Judge who had recorded opinion for acquittal of accused? - S. 392, CrPC lays down that when an Appeal under Chapter XXEX is heard by a HC before a Bench of Judges and they are divided in opinion, Appeal with their opinions, shall be laid before another Judge of that Court, and that judge, after such hearing as he thinks fit, shall deliver his opinion and judgment and order shall follow that opinion - Further held, that third Judge is under no obligation to accept view of one of Judges holding in favour of acquittal of accused either as a rule of prudence or on score of judicial etiquette - (C) Inquest Report does not contain particulars of murder - An investigation u/s. 174 CrPC is confined to ascertainment of apparent cause of death - Details of overt acts are not necessary to be recorded in Inquest Report - Further held, that no requirement in law of mentioning details of FIR, names of accused or names of eye witnesses or gist of their statement nor it is required to be signed by any eye-witness - (D) whether conviction of remaining accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 149, IPC is legally sustainable? - First part of s. 149, IPC means that offence committed in prosecution of common object must be one which is committed with a view to accomplish common object - Expression 'know' in second part does not mean a mere possibility, but one members of assembly knew to be likely to be committed in prosecution of common object - Further held, that in view of features of case, it cannot be held that common object of unlawful assembly was to commit murder of deceased or that members of unlawful assembly knew that murder is likely to be committed in prosecution of common object of assembly - However, as members of unlawful assembly carried deadly weapons, knowledge that grievous injury may be caused can be attributed to them - Appeals disposed of.
