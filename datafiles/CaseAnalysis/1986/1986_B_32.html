Bhagwati Prasad Dixit 'Ghorewala' v Rajeev Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 642; (1986) 4 SCC 78; (1986) SCC (Cr) 399; AIR 1986 SC 1534; 1986 (2) CCC 714; 1986(1) SCALE 606; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, Citizenship Rules, 1956 (As amended upto 20-4-2000)

Summary: Election - Representation of the people Act, 1951, s. 116-A - Appeal against dismissal of election petition for failure to disclose a cause of action - Election of respondent (Rajiv Gandhi) challenged on the grounds : (A) Citizenship - He ceased to be an Indian citizen and, therefore, was disqualified to be a candidate - As he had been married to an Italian lady and had acquired properties in his own name as well as in the name of his wife in Italy the he must be deemed to have acquired Italian citizenship as per the Italian law and ceased to be an Indian citizen u/s. 9 of the Citizenship Act, 1955 - Held, allegations were wholly inadequate since it is not shown that there is any provision in our law which provides that a person would automatically lose his Indian citizenship on his marriage with a person who is a citizen of a foreign country or by acquiring, even if true, property in a foreign country - (B) Office of profit - At the time of election he was a MP and was holding an office of profit within the meaning of art. 102(1)(a) of the Constitution and, therefore, was disqualified for being chosen as a Member of parliament - Held, membership of Parliament is not an office under the Govt. - (C) Chief Election Commissioner was not qualified to be appointed as the Chief Election Commissioner and entire elections held through out the country were void - Held, however liberal the construction of art. 354 (5) of Constitution may be, it is not necessary that a person to be appointed as a Chief Election Commissioner should satisfy the qualifications prescribed for a Judge of the Supreme Court hence plea rejected - And further held that HC was right in dismissing the petition on the ground that it does not disclose a cause of action -

Appeal dismissed.
