Mahar Jahan and others v State of Delhi and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1918; (2004) 13 SCC 421; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 80

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: S. 145 proceedings, Civil courtâs decision

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal procedure, 1973, ss. 145, 482 - S. 145 proceedings - Civil court's decision - Validity - Plaintiffs/appellants sons and daughters, filed suit against defendant/respondent no. 2 father, for permanent preventive injunction - Respondent no. 2 initiated proceedings u/s. 145 of CrPC and the SDM allowed the proceedings - Appellants preferred revision before Sessions Judge and same was allowed by reversing SDM's order - Respondent no. 2 preferred petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC, HC partly allowed the SDM's order - Hence instant appeal.

Held, the order of the criminal court would always be subject to decision by the civil court for termination of proceedings u/s.145 of CrPC. The parties are already before the civil court, it is proper to let the civil suit be decided and therein appropriate interim order be passed taking care of the grievances of the parties by making such arrangement as may remain in operation during the hearing of the civil suit. Appeal allowed.
