Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Bombay v Messrs Walchand and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1967 SC 1435; 1967 (2) ITJ 198; [1967] 65 ITR 381; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) and 33(4) - Constitution, art. 226 - Income Tax - Appellate Tribunal - (A) Judicial order , s.33(4) of Income Tax Act -Not in and judicial spirit and without reasons - (B) Claim for allowance u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Test of commercial expediency - Reasonableness of expenditure to be determined from businessman'spoint of view - Increments in remuneration of top employees and directors due of increase in business of assessee - Claim for allowance allowed - Whether rule that there should be increase in profits to justify increase in remuneration is correct to be applied here - Held, decisions must he supported by reasons - Rule , is erroneous and claim allowed - Appeal Dismissed.
