Speaker Haryana Vidhan Sabha and others v Kuldeep Bishnoi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 452; AIR 2013 SC 120; 2012(6) ALL MR 399; 2012 (6) AWC 5704; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 1; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3474; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 392; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 607; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 672

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Haryana Legislative Assembly (Disqualification of Members on ground of Defection) Rules, 1986

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Civil & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Haryana Legislative Assembly (Disqualification of Members on Ground of Defection) Rules, 1986 - Merger of Party - Disqualification of Membership - Violation of Provisions - Jurisdiction of Writ - Sustainability - In Assembly elections INC emerged as single largest party and in collaboration with 7 independent and 1 MLA from BSP formed Govt. in State - Respondents-MLA's of BL party had written to speaker and requested to accept merger and to recognize them as members of INC in Vidhan Sabha, Appellant-Speaker accepted merger holding that it was in consonance with para 4(1) of Tenth Schedule to Constitution - Respondent No.1 filed 5 separate petitions against other respondents before speaker u/art. 191 r/w Tenth Schedule to Constitution and Rules for disqualification on ground that they had voluntarily given up membership of their original political party and joined INC in violation of provisions of para 4(1) of Tenth Schedule to Constitution - Speaker issued notice to respondents demanding explanation and granted time on request - Pending application, respondent No. 1 filed writ petition before HC, seeking quashing of orders passed by Speaker on 9th and 10th November 2009 and also for declaration that respondents were disqualified from membership of Vidhan Sabha and in alternative for a direction on Speaker to dispose of disqualification petitions with 3 months - HC, allowed petition and directed Speaker to decide disqualification petitions pending within 4 months - Disqualification petitions filed by respondent No. 1 were pending for production of witnesses before Speaker, meanwhile appeal before DB, was filed by Speaker challenging order of SJ - DB, upheld directions of SJ, further stayed operation of orders passed by speaker on merger of BL party with INC - Hence instant appeal - Appellant-Speaker contended that order of DB, was in violation of principles of natural justice - Whether orders passed by HC directing appellant-speaker to decide disqualification application within 34 months and further declaring respondents were disqualified from membership of Vidhan Sabha was sustainable - Held, decision of Speaker on a petition under paragraph 4 of Tenth Schedule concerns only a question of merger on which Speaker was not entitled to adjudicate, HC could not have assumed jurisdiction under its powers of review before a decision was taken by Speaker under paragraph 6 of Tenth Schedule to Constitution - It was in fact in a proceeding under paragraph 6 that Speaker assumed jurisdiction to pass a quasi-judicial order which was amenable to writ jurisdiction of HC, it was in such proceedings that question relating to disqualification was to be considered and decided - Accordingly, restraining Speaker from taking any decision under paragraph 6 of Tenth Schedule was, in our view, beyond jurisdiction of HC, since Constitution itself had vested Speaker with power to take a decision under paragraph 6 and care had also been taken to indicate that such decision of Speaker would be final, it was only thereafter that HC assumed jurisdiction to examine Speaker's order - HC had no jurisdiction to pass such an order, which was in domain of Speaker - SC, upheld order of DB only to extent that it directed Speaker to decide petitions for disqualification of 5 MLAs within a period of 4 months, remaining was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
