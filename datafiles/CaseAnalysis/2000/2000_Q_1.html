Quarry Owners' Association v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2839; (2000) 8 SCC 655; AIR 2000 SC 2870; JT 2000 (8) SC 539; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Mineral Concession, Religious Endowments Act, 1863, Prohibition Act, Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988, Fixation Of Royalty, Delegated Legislation Provisions (Amendment) Act, 1983., Gujarat Minor Minerals Rules, 1966

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Commercial - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 2, 3, 4A, 5 to 15, 17, to 21, 23-C, 26, 28(3), 29 and 30 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949, r. 4 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - Gujarat Minor Minerals Rules, 1966, r. 21 - Gujarat Minor Minerals (Amendment) Rules, 1979, r. 22 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1949 - Bihar Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1972 - Religious Endowments Act, 1863, s. 8 - Indian Christian Marriage Act, 1872, s. 83 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 151 and 338(5) - Essential Commodity Act, 1955, s. 3 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 5(7A) - Press and Registration of Book Act, 1867, s. 20 - Registration Act (Amendment) 1908, ss. 69 and 91(1) - Whether delegation of power by the Parliament to the state Government u/s. 15 comes under its ambit? - Held, there is no substance in the contention that no guidelines are provided in the 1957 Act for the exercise of the rule making power of the State Government under s. 15(1). - Court upheld the validity of sub-s. (1) of s. 15 by holding that power conferred upon the state governments does not amount to excessive delegation of any essential legislative power. - It further held, there are sufficient guidelines for the exercise of rule-making power which are to be found in the object for which such power is conferred, namely, for regulating the grant of quarry leases, mining leases or mineral concessions in respect of minor minerals and for the purposes connected therewith. - It also held that power to make rules under s. 15(1) includes to amend the rules so as to enhance the rates of royalty and dead rent - Appeal dismissed
