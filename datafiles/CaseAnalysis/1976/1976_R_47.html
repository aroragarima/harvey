Chanan Singh v Registrar, Co-Operative Societies, Punjab and

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 115; (1976) 3 SCC 361; (1976) SCC (L&S) 446; AIR 1976 SC 1821; 1976 (32) FLR 310; 1976 LabIC 1188; 1976 (2) LLJ 98; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 685; 1976 SLJ 428; 1976 (2) SLR 20; 1976 UJ 349

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Misconduct - Notice - Revival proceedings - Appellant, employee of respondent no.2 was issued show cause notice to him for certain items of misconduct imputed to him - After enquiry another notice was issued by Secretary as to why appellant's increment should not be stopped by way of punishment and thereafter explanation of appellant was accepted and proceedings were dropped - Thereafter, Managing Director had issued fresh memorandum by holding that Secretary of Bank had no power to inflict punishment and proceedings were held invalid and appellant was suspended - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC and challenged revival of proceedings against him as illegal and opposed to natural justice, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, that instant writ was in any case premature and no punitive action has yet been taken. It is difficult to state, apart from speculation, what the outcome of the proceedings will be. In case the appellant is punished, it is certainly open to him either to file an appeal as provided in the relevant rules or to take other action that he may be advised to resort. Therefore, instant court need not dwell on problems which do not arise in the light of the view taken that there is no present grievance of punitive action which can be ventilated in court. After all, even the question of jurisdiction to re-open what is claimed to be a closed enquiry will, and must, be considered by the Managing Director. Hence, respondent has not been able to show any power to suspend an employee pending an enquiry and the suspension of the appellant is plainly without the pale of law and he would be entitled to his salary during the period till final orders are passed. Order accordingly.
