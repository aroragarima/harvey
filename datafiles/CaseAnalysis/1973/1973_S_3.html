State of Punjab v S. P. Sharma and another

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 430; (1973) 2 SCC 466; (1973) SCC (L&S) 527; AIR 1973 SC 2468; 1973 LabIC 1467; 1974 SLJ 294; 1973 UJ 722

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Promotions, Passing of examination as pre-requisite

Summary: Service - State Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115(7) - Promotions - Passing of examination as pre-requisite - Legality - Respondents (employees) of appellant (State Govt.) and their history of service was same was of the services whose cases were dealt within by SC in State of Haryana, Etc., Etc v Shamsher Jang Bahadur, Etc., 1972 Indlaw SC 37 - Appellant filed an application in instant appeals for amendment of written statement and for urging additional grounds in the appeal. Appellant contended that since the post of Asst., for which the passing of examination which was struck down in said judgment was made a necessary qualification under the Rules, was newly created and there was no such post earlier there was no question of changing the service conditions of respondent to his disadvantage.

Held, the question that arises in instant appeals is the same as arose in 1972 Indlaw SC 37, on the basis of that judgment appeals have to be dismissed. There was no post of Asst. in Pepsu Secretariat would not affect the merits of the question. The post of Asst. is not the only step in the official hierarchy nor is it the final step. Therefore if the passing of an examination is made a pre-requisite for a clerk to be promoted as an Asst., he can never hope to act any promotion except by passing an examination and becoming an Asst. There is, therefore, clearly a change in the conditions of service of the respondents contrary to the provisions of s. 115(7) of the Act. Appeals dismissed.
