Marwadi Kishor Parmanand v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1395; (1994) 4 SCC 549; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1294; AIR 1994 SCW 2474; 1994 (1) AWC 479; 1994 (2) CCR 456; JT 1994 (2) SC 640; 1994(2) SCALE 251; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 378 and 386 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Appellants accused of murder - Acquitted - On appeal convicted by High Court - (A) Whether conviction can be based on testimony of sole witness? - Generally speaking, oral testimony may be classified into three categories, (1) wholly reliable, (2) wholly unreliable, and (3) neither wholly reliable nor wholly unreliable - Most often there are situations where only a single person is available to give evidence in respect of a disputed fact - Naturally in such a situation court has to weight carefully such a testimony and if it is satisfied that evidence is reliable and free from all taints which tend to render oral testimony open to suspicion, it becomes its duty to act upon such testimony - Held, in present case prosecution case rest on testimony of sole eyewitness and therefore, testimony must be examined with greater care and caution - In statement of solitary eyewitnesses, no infirmity or any unusual features from which it may be inferred that witness is a got-up witness and not a truthful person found - (B) Whether conduct of witness at time of occurrence was unnatural? - Different persons react differently in different situations and circumstances - No hard and fast rule of universal application with regard to reaction of a person in a given circumstances can be laid down - Most often when a person happens to see or come across a gruesome and cruel act being perpetrated within his sight then there is a possibility that he may lose his equilibrium and balance of mind and therefore he may remain as a silent spectator till he is able to reconcile himself and then react in his own way - There may be a person who may react by shouting for help while others may even choose to quietly slip away from place of occurrence while others may be so daring, enough to come forward unhesitantly and jump in fray at peril - Held, that solitary witness is a wholly reliable witness and his evidence in itself - Moreover corroboration is also not lacking in present case - Appeal dismissed.
