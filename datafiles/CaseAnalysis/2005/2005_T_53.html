Triloki Nath and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 705; (2005) 13 SCC 323; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 178; AIR 2006 SC 321; 2006 (1) JCC 41; JT 2005 (9) SC 370; 2005(9) SCALE 76; 2006 (2) SCJ 326; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 100, 105, 141, 147, 149, 304, 323, 352, 425, 96, 99 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Plea of self-defence - Held, concurrent finding of fact has been arrived at by both courts - When a large number of persons assembled with a gun and other weapons having in mind dispute over land in question, they must be held to have found common knowledge that by reason of their act, somebody may at least be grievously injured - Appellants were not entitled to raise plea of self-defence both in respect of property as also person being themselves aggressors - Appeals dismissed.
