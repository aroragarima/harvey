The State Of Bihar v The Tata Iron Steel Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 591; (1995) Supp2 SCC 4; AIR 1995 SC 1170; JT 1995 (3) SC 479; 1995(1) SCALE 792; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Colliery Control Order, 1945

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Contract & Commercial - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Colliery Control Order, 1945, cl. 2(2) - Whether company owner of captive 'coking coal mines' installing 'coke oven plants' within factory premises can come under definition of 'colliery' - Held, definition of colliery makes it clear that it specifically includes 'a plant for production of coke or for washing of coal' - Since hard coke also comes within definition of 'Coal' under order and is subject to control by Central Govt. authorities, coke oven plants which produce hard coke have been rightly included in definition of 'colliery' - Therefore, company owning captive 'coking coal mines' and having also installed 'coke oven plants' within factory premises is 'colliery' under order, 1945 - Appeal allowed.
