Officer On Special Duty (Land Acquisition) and Another v Shah Manilal Chandulal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3354; (1996) 9 SCC 414; AIR 1996 SCW 941; 1996 (27) ALR 358; 1996 (1) CCC 254; JT 1996 (2) SC 278; 1996 (1) MahLJ 609; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 113; 1996 (1) RentLR 299; 1996(2) SCALE 153; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 366; 1996 (2) Supreme 315

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18(1), 18(2) and 29 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 5 - Whether Collector is a court for purpose of s. 18(1) of Act? - Act maintains distinction between Collector and court and Collector/LAO performs only statutory duties under Act, including one while making reference u/s. 18 - It is difficult to construe that Collector/LAO while making reference under section is, as statutory authority still acts as a court for purpose of s. 5 of Limitation Act - Held, s. 29(2) cannot be applied to proviso of s. 18(2) - Collector/LAO is not a court when he acts as a statutory authority u/s. 18(1) - Therefore, s. 5 of Limitation Act cannot be applied for extension of period of limitation prescribed under proviso to 18(2) - High Court was not right in its finding that Collector is a court u/s. 5 of Limitation Act - Thus applications are barred by limitation and Collector has no power to extend time for making an application u/s. 18(1) for reference to court - HC order set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
