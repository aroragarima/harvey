Rajneesh Aggarwal v Amit J. Bhalla

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20417; (2001) SCC (Cr) 229; (2001) 1 SCC 631; AIR 2001 SC 518; 2001 (1) AllCJ 342; 2001 (42) ALR 528; 2001 (1) AWC 567; 2001 (1) CCR 61; 2001 (1) CGLJ 341; 2001 (2) CLC 211; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 332; 2001 (1) Crimes 104; 2001 CRLJ 708; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 2499; 2001 (1) JCC 87; JT 2001 (1) SC 325; 2001 (1) KLT 484; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 605; 2001(1) SCALE 36; 2001 (1) SCJ 13; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 54; 2001 (1) SLT 288; 2001 (1) Supreme 24

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 202 and 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Three cheques had been given to appellant representing different amounts - Cheques were presented by appellant for encashment, but same were returned with endorsement payment stopped by drawer - Appellant, therefore, served notices on respondent - Magistrate proceeded to hold inquiry and thereafter, took cognizance of offence and directed issuance of process - Respondent challenged order of magistrate - Petitions dismissed by High Court - Thereafter respondent filed application before magistrate for recalling issuance of process - Dismissed by Magistrate - Filed petitions before HC - HC allowed application - (A) Whether HC justified in coming to conclusion that drawer has not been duly served with notice for payment? - Mere dishonour of a cheque would not raise a cause of action unless payee makes a demand in writing to drawer of cheque for payment and drawer fails to make payment of said amount of money to payee - Notwithstanding service of notice, amount was not paid - Held, after cheques were dishonoured by bank payee had served due notice and yet there was failure on part of accused to pay money, who had signed cheques - Thus order of HC quashed - (B) Whether deposit of entire amount covered by three cheques, while matter is pending in this court, would make any difference? - As far as criminal complaint is concerned, once offence is committed, any payment made subsequent thereto will not absolve accused of liability of criminal offence, though in matter of awarding of sentence, it may have some effect on court trying offence - A criminal proceeding could not be quashed on account of deposit of money in court - Further held, deposit of money by respondent in this court is of no consequence - Appeal allowed.
