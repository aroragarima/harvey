Chemicals And Fibres Of India Limited Etc. v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 861; (1991) 2 SCC 10; 1991 CRLR 414; 1991 CrLR(SC) 414; 1991 (33) ECR 439; 1991 (54) E.L.T. 3; JT 1993 (1) SC 405; 1991(1) SCALE 165; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 288; 1991 (2) ShimLC 97; 1991 (1) UJ 700

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 25 and 75 - Customs and Central Excise duty Drawback Rules, 1971 - (A) Whether appellant companies are entitled to full 'drawback' of customs duty which they had paid on import of di-methyl-terephthalate for manufacture of polyester staple fibre yarn? - A manufacturer is not automatically entitled to a drawback of entire customs and excise duties paid by him if terms and conditions of s. 75 are fulfilled - Rules do not envisage a refund of an amount arithmetically equal to customs duty or central excise duty which may have been actually paid by an individual importer-cum-manufacturer - High Court right in concluding that rate of drawback in respect of goods in question was fixed after taking into consideration aspect of customs duty payable in respect of di-methyl-terephthalate and that it was available in country itself - Held, order of Central Govt. rejecting assessees' application was well founded and cannot be interfered with - Appeals dismissed.
