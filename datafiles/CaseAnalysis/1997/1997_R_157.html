Ashok Hurra v Rupa Bipin Zaveri

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2568; (1997) 4 SCC 226; AIR 1997 SC 1266; 1997 (3) AWC 1843; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 70; 1997 (1) DMC 491; 1997 (1) HLR 621; JT 1997 (3) SC 483; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 61; 1997(2) SCALE 582; 1997 (1) SCJ 529; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 875; 1997 (3) Supreme 35

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Tort; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13B - Constitution, art. 142 - Divorce by mutual consent - (A) Time limit for withdrawal of consent - Observations in the predecided case infer that it can be withdrawn at any time before decree is passed - (B) Proceedings pending for long period - But wife not withdrawing her consent within 18 months from presentation of petition - Marriage irretrievably broken and both filed civil as well as criminal proceedings - Husband has married and begot a child during pendency of divorce proceedings - Observations are too wide and require reconsideration - Held, circumstances warrant exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 142 and grant a decree of divorce by mutual consent , but considering the conduct of husband and circumstances of case wife directed to be paid Rs. 10 lakhs as condition precedent for decree - Appeals disposed of.
