Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited v M. Chandrasekhar Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 47; (2005) SCC (L&S) 282; (2005) 2 SCC 481; AIR 2005 SC 2769; 2005 (5) AWC 4198; 2005 (1) CLR 959; 2005 (104) FLR 806; JT 2005 (1) SC 474; 2005 (1) LLN 1051; 2005(1) SCALE 547; 2005 (3) SLR 396; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1483

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11-A - Guilty of misconduct - Termination of service - Labour Court set aside termination - Whether reinstatement of respondent sustainable? - Held, Labour Court itself came to conclusion that management has lost confidence in respondent - Question of it exercising its jurisdiction to alter or reduce punishment does not arise - No finding given by courts below including that either fact of loss of confidence or quantum of punishment is so harsh as to be vindictive or shockingly disproportionate - Without such finding based on records, interference with award of punishment in a domestic inquiry is impermissible - Dismissal of respondent made by appellant pursuant to inquiry is upheld - Appeals allowed.
