Sridevi and Others v Jayaraja Shetty and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 53; (2005) 2 SCC 784; AIR 2005 SC 780; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 99; 2005 (58) ALR 662; 2005 (1) AWC 799; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 179; JT 2005 (1) SC 547; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 795; 2005 (98) RD 613; 2005(1) SCALE 577; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Partition suit - Whether will under consideration had been duly executed and propounders of will had dispelled suspicious circumstances surrounding it? - Held, assertion of two attesting witnesses has not been controverted by either of daughters by appearing in witness box - From their presence in house at time of execution of will, it can reasonably be inferred that they had knowledge about execution of will - Under these circumstances, it cannot be held that execution of will had not been brought to notice of appellants - Appeal dismissed.
