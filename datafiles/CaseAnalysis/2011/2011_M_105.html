Medley Pharmaceuticals Ltd. v Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs, Daman and another

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 33; (2011) 2 SCC 601; 2011 (263) E.L.T. 641; JT 2011 (2) SC 54; 2011(1) SCALE 480; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 741

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Valuation Rules, 1975, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000, Drugs Control Act

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Valuation Rules, 1975, rr. 4 and 6(b)(ii) - Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, rr. 8 and 11 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945, rr. 65(18), 95 and 96 (1) (ix) - Assessable value - Physician's samples for free distribution - Commissioner, while passing the order-in-original has held that the value of physician's samples should be determined u/r. 4 of Rules, 1975 - Tribunal dismissed the appellant's appeal and upheld the order passed by the Commissioner and held that for the purpose of payment of Excise duty, Physician samples have to be valued for the period post 1.07.2001 upto December, 2001 on pro-rata basis on the value of trade packs u/r. 4 r/w. r. 11 of Rules 2000 - Tribunal also held that merely because a product is statutorily prohibited from being sold, would not mean that the product is not capable of being sold - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that the physician samples of patent and proprietary medicines are statutorily prohibited from being sold by virtue of rr. 65(18), 95 and 96 (1) (ix) of the Drugs Rules - (A) Whether 'Physician Samples' are excisable goods in view of the fact that they are statutorily prohibited from being sold under the Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 and the Rules made thereunder? - 'Physician Samples' supplied to the Doctor are excisable goods - Held, the prohibition on the sale of Physician Samples intended for distribution to medical practitioners as free samples by r. 65 (18) of the Drugs Rules shall have no bearing or effect upon the levy of excise duty under the Act, since excise is a duty on manufacture, duty is payable whether or not goods are sold - Excise duty is payable even in case of free supply, since sale is not a necessary condition for charging duty under the Act - Moreover, main object or real purpose of the Drugs Act, 1940 and Rules made thereunder, is to regulate the manufacture of drugs in order to maintain the standard or quality of drugs for sale and distribution as a drug - It is not mandatory for the pharmaceutical company to distribute free physician samples of every drug they manufacture - This choice made by the pharmaceutical companies in terms of r. 96 (1) (ix) of the Drugs Rules by overprinting words 'Physician's sample- Not to be sold' on the label of the drugs will not come in the way of the Revenue from levying excise duty on the drugs so manufactured - (B) What is the appropriate method of valuing physician samples for the purpose of excise duty? - Held, SC in Commissioner of Central Excise, Bangalore-I vs. Bal Pharma Ltd. 2010 INDLAW SCO 214, held that the physician's samples have to be valued on pro-rata basis - Hence, following said SC judgment physician samples have to be valued on pro-rata basis for the relevant period - Appeal disposed of.
