Regional Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax, Indore v Malwa Vanaspati and Chemical Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 409; AIR 1968 SC 894; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 431; 1968 (21) STC 431

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950, ss. 7(1), 8 and 10 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 9 - Show cause notice issued to respondent - Petition filed contending that the assessment was barred by s. 10 - High Court quashed proceeding for assessment and directed the appellant to forbear from proceeding with the assessment - Whether justified? - Notice u/s. 11(2) is only a step in the proceeding for assessment and does not disturb the continuity of the proceeding - Therefore, when the Sales Tax Officer issued a notice against the respondent u/s. 8(2) of the Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950, a fresh proceeding to assess turnover which has 'escaped assessment was not commenced, and s. 10 of the Act was not attracted thereto - Since in the present case the proceeding for assessment had already commenced when the respondent filed the return, that proceeding could be completed by the assessing authority at any time, and the issue of a notice u/s. 8(2) does not attract the bar of s. 10 - Held, HC had erred - Order passed by HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
