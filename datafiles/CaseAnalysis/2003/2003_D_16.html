Ramakant Dattatraya Deshpande v Dadu Bhagoji Patil (D) through LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1184; (2004) 1 SCC 460; AIR 2004 SC 1324; 2003(10) SCALE 941; 2004 (2) SLT 443; 2003 (8) Supreme 751

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Default In Payment Of Rent

Summary: Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948 - ss. 29, 32G, 43A - Possession - Tillers Day - Landlord minor - Original respondent-tenant defaulted in payment of rent consecutively for more than three agricultural years - Held, the High Court was clearly in error in observing that not all the lands have been found to have been leased for sugarcane cultivation. It also committed a serious error in overlooking the fact that all proceedings undertaken by the landlord for terminating tenancy, obtaining possession under s. 29, declaration under s. 43A and exemption certificate under s. 88C of the Act had attained finality - Further held, the High Court grossly erred in quashing all orders of the authorities under the Act and remanding the matter to the original authority of proceeding afresh under s. 32G of the Act.
