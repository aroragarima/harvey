Challamma v Tilaga and others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2097; (2009) 9 SCC 299; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 166; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 70; 2010 (1) AWC 533; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 129; JT 2009 (10) SC 246; 2010 (2) KarLJ 588; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 587; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 944; 2009 (108) RD 705; 2009(10) SCALE 511; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 831

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Township Committee Rules

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 5 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 372 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 50 and 114 - Marriage - Validity - Respondents nos. 1 and 3 filed an application u/s. 372 of 1925 Act for grant of succession certificate after the death of 'X' stating that deceased and respondent no. 1 was married on 3-12-1984 and respondent nos. 2 and 3 were their children - Appellant, mother of the deceased opposed said application stating that deceased was not married at all and she was shown as the sole nominee in life insurance policies obtained by deceased - Trial court held that deceased and respondent no. 1 lived together for a period over 3 years and 9 months and the society accepted them as husband and wife, hence, presumption of valid marriage should be drawn and on the basis thereof the application for grant of succession certificate filed by the respondents was allowed - On appeal, first appellate court held appellant was entitled to 1/4th share in the estate of the deceased while upholding the judgment and order of the Trial Judge - Appellant filed revision petition against said order which was dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether a valid marriage had taken place between the deceased and the first respondent? - Held, question as to whether a valid marriage had taken place between the deceased and the first respondent is essentially a question of fact - In arriving at a finding of fact indisputably the Trial Judge was not only entitled to analyse the evidences brought on record by the parties hereto so as to come to a conclusion as to whether all the ingredients of a valid marriage as contained in s. 5 of the Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 stand established or not - A presumption of a valid marriage having regard to the fact that they had been residing together for a long time and has been accepted in the society as husband and wife, could also be drawn - It is also well-settled that a presumption of a valid marriage although is a rebuttable one, it is for the other party to establish the same - Such a presumption can be validly raised having regard to s. 50 of the Indian Evidence Act - A heavy burden, thus, lies on the person who seeks to prove that no marriage has taken place - Order of HC and Trial Judge upheld - Appeal dismissed.
