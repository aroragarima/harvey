Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 635; (2009) 13 SCC 448; 2009 (238) E.L.T. 3; JT 2009 (7) SC 314; 2009(8) SCALE 231; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Recovery Of Duties, Levy Of Duty, Refund Of Duty, Adjustment Of Duty, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000, Mis- Statement

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11A (1), 11A (2), 11AC - Penalty - Mandatory penalty - Tribunal held that there was no warrant for levy of penalty since assessee had deposited balance amount of excise duty even before issuance of show cause notice - Revenue by relying upon a SC judgement in case of Union of India Vs. Dharamendra Textile Processors, contended that mere non payment or short payment of duty (without anything else) would inevitably lead to imposition of penalty equal to amount by which duty was short paid - Whether payment of differential duty before show cause notice is issued, can alter liability for penalty? - Application of s. 11AC would depend upon existence or otherwise of conditions expressly stated in section, once section is applicable in a case concerned authority would have no discretion in quantifying amount and penalty must be imposed equal to duty determined under sub-section (2) of s. 11A of Act - Held, if notice u/s. 11A (1) states that escaped duty was result of any conscious and deliberate wrong doing and in order passed u/s. 11A(2) there is a legally tenable finding to that effect then provision of s. 11AC would also get attracted and in absence of such a finding in order passed u/s. 11A (2) there would be no application of penalty provision in s. 11AC of Act - Interpretation of Dharamendra Textile case is misconstrued by Revenue as in almost every case relating to penalty, decision is referred to on behalf of Revenue as if it laid down that in every case of non-payment or short payment of duty penalty clause would automatically get attracted and authority had no discretion in matter - Impugned orders passed by Tribunal set aside and matters remanded to respective Tribunals for fresh consideration, in accordance with law, and in light of this judgment - Appeals allowed.
