Ramakant Rai v Madan Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 818; (2003) 12 SCC 395; AIR 2004 SC 77; 2003 (4) CCR 158; 2003 (4) Crimes 310; 2004 (1) CRJ 345; 2004 CRLJ 36; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 28; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 344; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 83; 2003(8) SCALE 243; 2003 (1) SCW 5633; 2003 (6) SLT 102; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 17; 2003 (7) Supreme 326

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 302, 440 - Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 136 - Powers of court under art. 136 - Trial Court convicted the accused - High Court reversed the judgment of the Trial Court - Held, in view of the unsatisfactory nature of disposal of the appeal and the inherent improbabilitises and incongruities in the conclusions, the unreasoned impugned judgment of the High Court warrants reversal.
