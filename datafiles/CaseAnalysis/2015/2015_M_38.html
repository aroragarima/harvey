Asha Verman and others v Maharaj Singh and others

27 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 403; 2015 (2) ACC 730; 2015 ACJ 1286; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 24; 2015 (110) ALR 445; 2015 (3) AWC 3061; 2015 (3) CGLJ 557; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 219; 2015 (2) JabLJ 209; JT 2015 (3) SC 361; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1895; 2015(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 520; 2015(4) SCALE 329; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 36

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Accident - Death - Enhancement of compensation - Deceased aged 35 years met with accident when truck driven by respondent no.1 rashly and negligently collided with back of his motorcycle - Deceased suffered injuries on is hand and was underwent operations and plastic surgery, however, due to severity of injuries caused to him in accident, decease died - Claim of compensation was filed by appellants being his wife, children and parents of deceased - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs.3,75,500/- at interest rate of 6.5% p.a. to appellants - Further, compensation was enhanced by HC to Rs. 5,35,000/- under all heads with interest at rate of 8% p.a. - Review petition filed before HC is dismissed - Whether appellant claimants are entitled to enhanced compensation.

Held, courts below have erred in calculation of loss of dependency by wrongly ascertaining income of deceased at time of his death. It is clear that deceased at time of his death was working in operation theatre as technician in permanent post at Hospital and was earning Rs.4,617/- p.m. (rounded off to Rs.4,600/-). On applying principles as laid down in earlier case, 50% of salary must be added to income of deceased towards future prospects of income, which comes to Rs.6,900/- pm., i.e. Rs.82,800/- p.m. Deducting 1/4th for personal expenses and applying appropriate multiplier taking into consideration age of deceased at time of his death, total loss of dependency comes to Rs.9,93,600/- . Further, Rs.1,40,000/- was spent by appellant-wife for medical purposes of her husband during period of treatment before his death. Accordingly, Rs.1,40,000/- towards medical expenses incurred for treatment of deceased is awarded. Further, SC held that HC erred in awarding only Rs. 5,000/- each towards loss of estate, funeral expenses and loss of consortium. Which is enhanced to Rs.1,00,000/- towards loss of estate. Further, Rs.1,00,000/- is awarded each to appellant-children towards loss of love and affection due to loss of their father. Further, sum of Rs.50,000/- is awarded to each of appellant-parents towards loss of love and affection of their deceased son. SC held that HC erred in awarding interest at rate of 8% p.a. only, instead of 9% p.a. on compensation amount is awarded based on principle laid down in earlier case. Hence, appellants are entitled to total compensation of Rs. 16,58, 600/-. Amount of compensation is to be divided between all claimants. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Quantum of compensation in case of accident cases should be awarded after taking into consideration age of deceased, medical expenditure, loss of dependency and other relevant factor.
