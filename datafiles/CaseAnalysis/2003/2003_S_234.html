Commissioner of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Srivallabh Glass Works Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 431; (2003) 11 SCC 341; 2003 (108) ECR 642; 2003 (153) E.L.T. 494

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - claim for differential duty and penalty - CEGAT felt constrained by the Constitution Bench Judgment of the Supreme Court in the case of Collector of Central Excise, Baroda vs. Cotspun Ltd - held, what Cotspuns case lays down is that so far as classification list subsists the differential duty cannot be claimed on the same product. In the present case, what was being cleared is not the product, which has been shown in the classification list. A different product, in respect of which no classification list had been filed, was being cleared and sold. Therefore, the principle laid down in Cotspuns case has no application. - On the findings given by the Tribunal, the demand is justified.
