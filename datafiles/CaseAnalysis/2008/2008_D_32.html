The New Indian Insurance Company v Darshana Devi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 262; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 98; (2008) 7 SCC 416; 2009 (2) ACC 269; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1639; 2008(2) ALL MR 757; 2008 (70) ALR 913; 2008 (2) AWC 1154; JT 2008 (2) SC 430; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 810; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1175; 2008 (151) PLR 471; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 86; 2008(2) SCALE 432; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Tribunal awarded a sum of Rs.2, 04, 000/- by way of compensation in favour of the claimants - Appeal bi Insurance Company - Held, in a case where the driver has no licence to drive a particular category of motor vehicle, the insurance company would not be liable - Tribunal has found that besides being a labourer, deceased also used to deal in Safeda wood - He was the owner of the 'Safeda' wood which was being transported to the market for its sale - It is not a fit case where this Court should exercise its discretionary jurisdiction under art. 136 - Appeal dismissed.
