Bhagwan Dass Jagdish Chander v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A. Alagiriswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 406; (1975) SCC (Cr) 410; (1975) 1 SCC 866; AIR 1975 SC 1309; 1975 CRLJ 1091; 1975 CrLR(SC) 311; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Prevention Of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 2(xiii), 7, 14 and 20A - Joint trial of vendor, distributor and manufacturerer - Appeal arises out of prosecution of M/s. Bhagwan Das Jagdish Chander, Ghee Merchants and Commission Agents at Delhi, u/ss. 7 and 16 of Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Appellant was prosecuted jointly with Laxmi Narain, vendor of 450 gms of ghee to a Food Inspector - (A) Whether power of Court to implead manufacturer in seperate trial u/s. 20 A? - Power of Court to implead manufacturer can be exercised only during course of trial s. 20A of Act of 1954 contemplates action which can only be taken during course of trial - Held, a separate trial would require a 'written consent of Central Govt. or State Govt. or a local authority or of a person authorised in this behalf by general of or special order by Central Govt. or State Govt. or a local authority', unless it is a complaint by a purchaser, other than a Food Inspector, who could rely upon s. 12 of Act of 1954 - But, an addition of an accused u/s. 20A constitutes an expressly laid down exception to requirement of a sanction u/s. 20(1) of Act of 1954 - (B) Whether a joint trial of vendor, distributor and manufacturerer for offences under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 is illegal? - In a suitable case, a vendor, a distributor, and a manufacturer could be tried together provided the, allegations made before Court show that there are connecting links between their activities so as to constitute same transaction - Connecting links, in a case such as one before us, could be provided by, firstly, fact that a sale at an anterior stage could be viewed as cause of subsequent sale; secondly, allegation that each of accused parted with article of food when it was in an adulterated state; and, thirdly, by common object of manufacturer distributor, and vendor, that article should reach consumer to be used as food - Held, joint trial of appellant with Laxmi Narain was not illegal, we think that, on special facts, of this case, interests of justice will be better served by quashing such a stale charge because appellant's defence will suffer if he is called upon to answer it now - Appeals allowed.
