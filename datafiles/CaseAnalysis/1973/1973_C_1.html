State of Gujarat v C. G. Desai and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 200; (1974) SCC (L&S) 116; (1974) 1 SCC 188; AIR 1974 SC 246; 1974 LabIC 187; 1974 (1) LLJ 252; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 255; 1974 SLJ 490; 1974 (1) SLR 420; 1974 UJ 57

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Summary: Service - Engineering Service Rules, 1960 r. 7(ii) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Service - Selection - Promotion - Petitioner/ respondent no.1 was working as deputy engineer till 03-12-1959, later on he was selected under competitive examination and appointed in B.S. E. Class II service - On completion of probation he was confirmed as Deputy Engineer in Class II from 03-12-1961 - Committee prepared list of Deputy Engineers for promotion as Executive Engineers but respondent no.1 was not considered as he had not put in 7 years of service requisite u/r.7(ii) of the Act and respondent nos. 2 and 3 were promoted and they were also directly recruited from competitive examination - Aggrieved respondent no.1 filed writ before HC contending pre selection service should be taken into account which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether action of State Govt. in treating differently promotees and direct recruits in Class II for computing period of their eligibility service requisite for promotion violates art.14 of constitution.

Held, direct recruits and promotees in Class II constitute two distinct groups or classes. This classification has a historical background and a rational basis and promotees from the lower ranks have only one chance of getting into Class II service, as against three available to the direct recruits. Further, for a considerable time, recruitment by promotion from the ranks of Temporary officiating Deputy Engineers etc. to Class II Service remained frozen with consequent stagnation and loss of incentive in the service and at the time of their entry into Class II service they are promotees, far older than the direct recruits; and, many of the promotees may have less than 7 years to go before attaining the age of superannuation. The classification is thus based on intelligible differentia and select-list prepared on the basis of seniority-cummerit, and the inter-se seniority of the selected officer in the lower rank is ordinarily to be maintained in the promoted rank. Further, respondents were denied in their rejoinder that the lists were prepared keeping the criteria laid down by the rules, but it is significant that they did not possess the required length of service in Class II, for them to be entitled to promotion when the respondents were included in the list and promoted as such they cannot challenge the appointments made as being in violation of art. 14 or art.16 of Constitution. Hence, HC erred in holding that the impugned action of the Govt. suffered from the vice of discrimination and as such was violative of art. 16 of Constitution. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - in applying the wide language of Arts. 14 and 16 to concrete cases, doctrinaire approach should be avoided and the matter considered in a practical way.
