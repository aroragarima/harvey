Gulamhussain Kutubuddin Maner v Abdulrashid Abdulrajak Maner and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2664; (2000) 8 SCC 507; JT 2000 (10) SC 425; 2001 (1) MPLJ 198; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 428; 2000 (91) RD 661B

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Registered gift deed - In favour of minor grandson - Minor's mother appointed guardian - Challenged by other son - Suit for letter of administration and possession - (A) Whether under Mohammedan law, a mother can be appointed to act as guardian of her minor son during lifetime of minor's father? - Held, under Mohammedan law, gift is a donation conferring right of property without exchange - Gift is in nature of contract where there must be a tender of property, acceptance of property by donee and delivery of possession of property - It is only when these three ingredients are satisfied, a gift is completed - In Valia Peedikakkandi Katheess Umma case (1963 Indlaw SC 370) it has been held that where father of a minor is alive mother of minor cannot act as a guardian of her minor son to accept gift on his behalf - In view of said legal position, in present case also where father of a minor is alive mother of minor cannot be appointed as a guardian of minor to accept gift on his behalf - (B) Whether decision of this Court in Valia Peedikakkandi Katheess Umma case is applicable to case of defendant-respondent? - Further held, view of Court in that case can be applied where father or grandfather of minor are not alive, and mother and in her absence any other person could be appointed as a guardian of minor which is not case here - Here, father of minor was alive and, therefore, mother could not have been appointed to act as a guardian of minor and to accept gift on his behalf and, therefore, gift executed by donor in favour of minor donee was invalid - (C) Whether a husband can appoint his wife as his agent to act as a guardian of his minor son? - Further held, it is not disposed to go into wider question as to whether husband can appoint his wife as his agent to act as guardian of his minor son in view of fact that such a case was not pleaded by defendant-respondents in their written statement - Besides that, no evidence at all was led by defendants that husband appointed his wife as his agent to act as guardian of his minor son - (D) Whether suit filed by plaintiff - appellant is maintainable? - Further held, plaintiff - appellant prayed for a relief of letter of administration in respect of estate of plaintiff's deceased father, which included other property also - Since there was no dispute in regard to any other property, trial court dealt with property which was in dispute - Under such circumstances, suit filed by plaintiff-appellant was maintainable - Appeal allowed.
