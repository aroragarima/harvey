(1) S. V. L. Murthy; (2) P. Jayakumar; (3) v State of Andhra Pradesh, By CBI, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1464; (2009) 6 SCC 77; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 941; AIR 2009 SC 2717; 2009 CRLJ 3930; JT 2009 (7) SC 385; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 696; 2009(6) SCALE 758; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 784

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 467, 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Negotiable Instrument Act, s. 138 - Conspiracy - Cheating - Conviction - Charge sheet filed alleging inter alia that there was criminal conspiracy between accused persons during 1988-89 to cheat SBI - Circumstances considered sufficient to bring home charges against appellant were: cheques of accused Nos. 1, 2 and 3 discounted after purchasing cheques; cheques deposited after a gap of 1 to 4 days; only later amounts deposited in account - Special Judge found A1 to A6 gulity u/s. 120-B and 420 IPC - A4 to A6 (employees of Bank)found guilty for offence u/s. 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Conviction upheld by HC - Present appeal - Held, prosecution failed to bring on record any evidence of conspiracy and wrongful gain so as to attract provisions of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Prosecution did not lay down any foundational facts to arrive at finding of dishonest intention on part of appellants, nor any such finding arrived at by Trial Court or HC - Circumstances considered not sufficient to hold appellants guilty for commission of offence u/s. 420, IPC as all actions on part of bank officers were in consonance with long standing banking practice - Appellants acted on instructions by higher authority - Impugned judgment and order of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
