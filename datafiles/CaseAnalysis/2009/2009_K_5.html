(1) New India Assurance Company Limited; (2) v (1) Kusum and Others; (2) Darshan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1028; (2009) 8 SCC 377; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 879; 2010 (2) ACC 518; 2010 (2) AWC 1818; 2009 (2) CCC 440; 2010 (1) MPLJ 337; 2010 (159) PLR 174; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 941; 2009(10) SCALE 592; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 343

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Arrears Of Land Revenue, Claim Tribunal, Excess Amount Paid

Summary: Insurance - Accident claim - Compensation - Liability of Insurance Company - Execution of decree - Deceased died while traveling in bus - Claim petition filed - Tribunal held that driver of the bus was not been possessing a valid driving licence - Primary liability to pay compensation was held to be that of driver and owner of bus - Insurance Company was directed to deposit amount - It was further directed that insurance company may recover same from driver and owner of bus - Insurance company deposited amount - In terms of order of Tribunal, Execution Petition was filed - Executing Court held that execution application not maintainable, however, Insurance Company has independent right to recover amount from insured (Owner and Driver) as per award through regular civil suit - Whether said order justified? - Owner of a vehicle in terms of provisions of Motor Vehicles Act is legally obligated to get vehicle insured - Rights and liabilities of parties to contract of insurance would be governed thereby subject to provisions of Motor Vehicles Act - One of conditions which would make insurance company liable to reimburse owner of vehicle in respect of payment of amount of compensation in favour of a claimant is that driver of vehicle must possess a valid driving licence - In present case, licence of driver of said bus was proved to be invalid - Insurance Company was not liable to pay claimed amount as the driver was not possessing a valid licence - Held, it would be travesty of justice, if Insurance Company which is directed to pay amount and then face immense difficulties in executing a decree - Impugned judgments set aside - Executing Courts directed to proceed with execution - Appeals allowed.
