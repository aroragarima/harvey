State Of U.P. v Laxmi Brahman and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 160; (1983) 2 SCC 372; (1983) SCC (Cr) 489; AIR 1983 SC 439; 1983 ALJ 419; 1983 (1) Crimes 797; 1983 CRLJ 839; 1983 CrLR(SC) 279; 1983(1) SCALE 274; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 537

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Adjourn, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 2(g), 167, 170, 207, 209, 309 and 439, 437(1) - (A) Jurisdiction of Magistrate - Question as to power to release on bail or remand to custody after charge-sheet and before commitment order High Court holding that after the accused is brought before the court alongwith the police report, the Magistrate must forthwith commit the accused to the Court of Sessions because the Magistrate would have no jurisdiction in the absence of any provision to remand the accused to custody till the order committing the case to Court o f Sessions is made - Held, it is incorrect to say that Magistrate have no jurisdiction in the absence of any provision to remand the accused to custody - Order of High Court granting bail on the short ground that they could not be remanded to the custody before the order committing them to the Court of Sessions is made, is set aside - (B) Determination of right of accused to be released on bail on non-submission of charge-sheet within the period of 60 days - Held, accused would be entitled to an order for being released on bail if he is prepared to and does furnish bail - Appeal allowed.
