Goli Eswariah v Commissioner of Glft Tax, Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 326; (1970) 2 SCC 390; AIR 1970 SC 1722; [1970] 76 ITR 675; 1971 (1) MLJ 105; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 105; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 522

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Gift Tax Act, 1958 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Interpretation of the term 'transfer' - Appellant was karta of the joint Hindu family - Appellant threw some of his self acquired property into the common stock of the joint Hindu family firm - HC decided that such act of the appellant amounted to the transfer so as to attract the provisions of the 1958 Act - Whether, the HC was right in holding its decision that the disputed act of the appellant was transfer within the meaning of the 1958 Act - Held, s. 2(xxiv) (d) of the 1958 Act, contemplates a 'transaction entered into' by one person with another and it was fact that appellant had not entered into any transaction while performing disputed act - Instant court opined that HC erred while holding its decision that act of the appellant in dispute was transfer and hence, the order of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
