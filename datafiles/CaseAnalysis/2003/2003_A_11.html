D.S. Parvathamma v A. Srinivasan

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 307; (2003) 4 SCC 705; AIR 2003 SC 3542; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 37; 2003 (51) ALR 273; JT 2003 (6) SC 249; 2003 (1) RCJ 140; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 773; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 623; 2003(3) SCALE 543; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 197; 2003 (4) SLT 144; 2003 (3) Supreme 88

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Rent Control - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961 - s. 21(1)(a) and (h) - Eviction under - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - s. 53A - Whether the appellant is entitled to protect his possession u/s.53A of the Act and hence not liable to suffer eviction based on landlord-tenant relationship which has ceased to exist on account of subsequent events? - Held, the appellant can be permitted to raise the plea of part performance only when, (i) the transferee has, in part performance of the contract, taken possession of the property or any part thereof, or the transferee, being already in possession, continues in possession part performance of the contract and has done some act in furtherance of the contract, (II) that the transferee has performed or is willing to perform his part of the contract, and (III) that the plea of part performance is not available to be raised against a transferee for consideration who has no notice of the contract or of the part performance thereof - Appellant cannot be permitted to raise the plea of part performance and seek shelter thereunder - Appeal dismissed.
