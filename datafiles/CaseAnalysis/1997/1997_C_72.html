Kuil Fireworks Industries v Collection Of Central Excise & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 712; (1997) 8 SCC 109; AIR 1997 SC 3559; 1997 (72) ECR 529; 1997 (95) E.L.T. 3; JT 1997 (8) SC 74; 1997 (22) RLT 327; 1997(6) SCALE 105; 1997 (8) Supreme 243

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Warehouse

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A - Customs Act, 1962 - Excise duty - Notification - Exemption from duty - Eligibility - Appellant was engaged in manufacturing of fireworks which would fall under Central Excise Tariff - By notification No. 167/86 dt. 1-3-1986 exemption from excise duty was granted in respect of certain goods provided that no process in or in relation to manufacture of said goods was ordinarily carried on with aid of power - Appellant claimed exemption in respect of fireworks on basis of said notification - Excise authorities on 2-9-1987, detained fire works valued at Rs. 39,83,698 and sum of Rs. 5,97,555 was demanded as excise duty payable on said goods on ground that goods were not entitled to exemption under said notification - Authorities permitted appellant to clear goods for sale without payment of excise duty on basis of interim orders by HC - Meanwhile notification no. 167/86 dt. 1-3-1986 was amended by notification no. 222/87 dt. 17-9-1987 whereby exemption in respect of fireworks was withdrawn - Since goods had been permitted to be cleared on basis of interim order, Assistant Collector, Central excise, by show cause notice dt. 16-2-1988 u/s. 11A of the 1944 Act demanded duty of Rs. 5,97,555 being duty on goods detained on September 2, 1987 on the ground that the said goods were cleared after 17-9-1987 when exception from duty was not available - Said order was affirmed, in appeal, by Collector (Appeals) by Order dt. 1-8-1991 - Further appeal by appellant was rejected by Tribunal which held that, as per SC decision, rate of duty applicable would be the one applicable on date of clearance of goods - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant could claim exemption from excise duty in respect of goods detained on basis of Order dt. 2-9-1987 - Held, by order dt. 18-12-1990, Assistant Collector held that paper tubes, paper cones were made by use of power and, therefore, appellant was not entitled to exemption under notification No. 167/86 dt. 1-3-1986 - Said order was set aside in appeal by Collector (Appeals) by order dt. 29-8-1991 and the order of Collector (Appeals) was not challenged by department and had become final - In view of the order dt. 29-8-1991, impugned judgement of Tribunal was set aside and demand raised by Assistant Collector of Central Excise on the basis of show cause notice dt. 16-2-1988 was quashed - Appeal allowed.
