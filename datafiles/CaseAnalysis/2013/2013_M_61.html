Prabhudas Damodar Kotecha and others v Manhabala Jeram Damodar and another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 504; (2013) 15 SCC 358; AIR 2013 SC 2959; 2013(6) ALL MR 399; 2013 (100) ALR 378; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 147; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 47; JT 2013 (11) SC 208; 2013 (2) RCR(Rent) 599; 2013(10) SCALE 242; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 52

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent Act, 1925, Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, Bombay Housing Board Act, 1948, Bombay Rent Act, 1944, Bombay Rents, Hotels and Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1947, Bombay Rent Act, 1939, Bombay Rent, Hotel and Lodging House Rates (Control) Act 1944, Bombay Rent Protection Act, 1939

Summary: (A) Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882 - Bombay Rents, Hotels and Lodging House Rates (Control) Act, 1947 - Respondents filed suit for eviction against appellants - Small Causes Court decreed eviction as appellants were gratuitous licensee - Appellants' appeal before Appellate Bench was dismissed - Respondents challenged jurisdiction of Small Causes Court before HC - Single Judge referred matter to Full Bench - Full Bench held that a suit by a licensor against a gratuitous licensee was not tenable before Presidency Small Causes Court u/s. 41 (1) of the 1882 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether suit filed by licensor against gratuitous licensee u/s. 41(1) of the 1882 Act was maintainable before Small Causes Court - Held, if provisions of s. 5 (4A) and s. 5 (11) r/w s. 15A of the 1947 Act were attracted, s. 41 (1) of the 1882 Act could not be resorted to institute a suit between licensor and licensee - Thus, if a licensee was covered by s. 15A r/w s. 5(4A) of the 1947 Act, suit u/s. 41(1) of 1882 Act would not be maintainable - S. 41(1)of 1882 Act, therefore, took in its compass 'licensees' who did not fall within the ambit of s. 5(4A) r/w s. 5(11) and s. 15A of the 1947 Act - Further, 1882 Act did not define expression 'licensor' and 'licensee' - However, both these expressions found a place in s. 41(1) of the 1882 Act - S. 41(1) conferred jurisdiction on Court of Small Causes to entertain and try all suits and proceedings between a 'licensor' and a 'licensee' relating to recovery of possession of any immovable property or relating to recovery of license fee - Expression 'licensee' used in 1882 Act did not derive its meaning from expression 'licensee' as used in s. 5 (4A) of 1947 Act and that the expression 'licensee' used in s. 41(1) was a term of wider import intended to bring in a gratuitous licensee as well - Hence, reasoning of Full Bench of HC was completely agreed with - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Interpretation of law - Golden rule - Held, a statute should prima facie be given their ordinary meaning when the language or phraseology was precise and plain - It was necessary to fall upon legislative history, statement of objects and reasons, frame work of statute etc. only when the words were unintelligible, ambiguous or vague - Appeals dismissed.
