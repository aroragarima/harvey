Mukand Limited v Mukand Staff and Officer's Association

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 643; (2004) 10 SCC 460; (2004) SCC (L&S) 798; AIR 2004 SC 3905; 2004 (5) AWC 4365; 2004 (101) FLR 219; JT 2004 (3) SC 474; 2004 (2) KLT 1; 2004 (2) LLJ 327; 2004 (2) LLN 122; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 6; 2004(3) SCALE 116; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 951; 2004 (3) SLR 16

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, r. 62 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 23 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(4) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k) and 2(s) - Whether the Industrial Tribunal was justified in adjudicating upon the service conditions of employees, who are not "workmen" under the Act and are hence clearly outside its jurisdiction? - It was not necessary for employees who are 'workmen' to agitate also for the revision of the emoluments of those who are not 'workmen' - 'Workmen' have no direct or substantial interest in the revision of emoluments of employees who are not 'workmen', nor could the workmen be held to be vitally interested in the terms of employment of the non-workmen - 'Workmen' as well as non-workmen being in the same grade did not imply that the distinction between the two categories ceased to exist, or that they belonged to the same class - High Court has erred in relying on the various settlements concluded between the parties regarding the service conditions of the employees including the welfare scheme and failed to appreciate that none of the said settlements contained any provision for waiver by the appellant-company of its rights with regard to the status of the employees - Held, remit the matter back to the Industrial Tribunal for adjudication according to law since errors in assessing financial capacity burden etc. in the award of the Tribunal - Held, appeal is allowed.
