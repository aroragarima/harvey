Adalat Pandit and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 398; (2010) 6 SCC 469; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 95; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 678; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 178; 2010(5) SCALE 658; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (nos. 10) allegedly murdered the deceased persons - Trial Court convicted appellants for murder - Appellants filed appeal before the HC - HC dismissed the appeals - Whether conviction by the Trial Court is sustainable - Held, on account of 1st, 5th, 9th appellants' presence even if they were armed with weapons, cannot be attributed to a common object - Prosecution failed to prove that the 1st, 5th, 9th appellants had common intention to commit the murder - However, overt act of dragging the bodies of the deceased persons from spot to another location would certainly make the 2nd and 6th appellants part of unlawful assembly - 4th, 7th and 10th appellants also participated in the unlawful assembly - Evidence of the prosecution witnesses, more particularly the eye-witnesses, clearly testified the role attributed by the 2nd, 6th, 7th and 10th appellants in committing the murder of deceased persons - Plea of alibi raised by the 10th appellant cannot be accepted since the prosecution led direct evidence with regard to his involvement - Trial Court and the HC were right in rejecting the defence of alibi - Appeals of 1st, 5th and 9th appellants are allowed and appeals of 2nd, 3rd, 4th, 6th , 7th and 10th appeals are dismissed - Order accordingly.
