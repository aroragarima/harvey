Haryana Power Generation Corporation Limited and others v Harkesh Chand and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 8; (2013) 2 SCC 29; AIR 2013 SC 403; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 113; 2013(1) ALL MR 938; 2013 (2) AWC 1917; 2013 (136) FLR 666; JT 2013 (1) SC 327; 2013 (1) LLJ 716; 2013 (2) MLJ 77; 2013(1) SCALE 127; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 593; 2013 (1) SLR 755

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 79 - Apprentices Act, 1961 - Assured Career Progression Scale - Trainees - Clam of scale - Entitled to -Respondents (employees) were working with appellant (Corporation) - Respondents claimed Assured Career Progression (ACP) scale on the ground that they joined service as trainees and first ACP scale was granted them on completion of 10 years from the date of they entered the service and that respondent were absorbed into cadre - However, while conferring benefit of second ACP period of training as apprentice was not considered - Aggrieved respondents filed a petition before HC - HC allowed petition holding that respondent were entitled for grant of ACP on completion of 10 years of service which included training as apprentice - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents were entitled for ACP scale as claimed - Held, first ACP scale was withdrawn during the pendency of writ petition where grievance pertained to non-grant of second ACP Scale in terms of Scheme dt.27.2.1998 introduced by Corporation (earlier Board) - It is apt to note that respondents were already granted second ACP Scale w.ef. 1.11.2008 - Thus, only grievance was that period should differ in respect of each respondent if training period was not computed - Appellant in exercise of power u/s. 79 of the Act, issued a notification on 14.3.1990 by bringing certain amendments in recruitment and promotion for employees working in Thermal Power Projects, and use of word 'training' therein after appointment to a post and stipulation related to grant of increment - In context of that notification, policy relating to ACP Scale granted under ACP Scheme and clarificatory communications were to be understood -In continuation to clarificatory memorandum dt.27.3.1991, appellant issued another memorandum on 22.11.1991, which clarification related to grant of regular pay scale to trainees of all categories - But benefit of grant of annual increment under provisions as contained in letter dt.27.3.1991 has to be given to trainee(s) of all categories whose services was regularized on 29.1.1991 or thereafter - It was stated that consequential benefits would accrue only from the date on which regular pay scale was granted to trainees of all categories - Appellant introduced ACP scheme on 27.1.1998 and u/cl. 2 of scheme excluded certain categories of employees, namely, appointed on ad hoc basis, work charged basis, part time paid out of contingencies and a daily wager from getting the benefit of Scheme and cl. 3 denied 'direct recruit fresh entrant' - Admittedly respondents were appointed as apprentice trainees for 2 years, after completion of training, appointed on temporary basis - Under 1961 Act nature and character of apprentice was nothing but that of a trainee and he was supposed to enter into a contract and by virtue of that contract, he was to serve for a fixed period on a fixed stipend and that did not change character of apprentice to that of a workman under employer where he was undergoing apprenticeship training - On perusal of said notification it was clear that it related to 2 categories of direct recruits who should undergo training for a period of 2 years in regular pay scale - Thus, said notification had no application to apprentices who avail the training - In clarification dt.27.3.1991, there was a mention with regard to regular pay scale in notification dt.13.9.1990 - It was clarified that period spent by apprentice of all categories should be treated as duty for all intents and purposes - Appellant issued further clarification that benefit of grant of annual increment under provisions as contained in letter dt.27.3.1991 was to be given to trainees of all categories whose services were regularized on 29.1.1991 or thereafter, and consequential benefit should accrue only from the date on which regular pay scale was granted to trainees of all categories - U/cl. 5 of ACP Scheme, regular satisfactory service would mean continuous service counting towards seniority under Corporation including continuous service in Board before reorganization - It was clearly stated that period spent on ad hoc basis, work charged basis, contingent basis and daily wages would not be counted for the purpose of counting prescribed length of regular satisfactory service for scheme - As evident, respondents were appointed on different dates as technicians Grade-II in pay scale on regular basis - Their period of probation was for 2 years - Respondents were not recruited under said policy and were appointed as apprentices trainee and were not given any kind of post - Respondents were not working on a post and they did not belong to any cadre - Thus, term trainee which was referred to in various clarificatory letters was misconstrued by HC - Financial benefit availed by respondents, should not be recovered, but their dates for grant of ACP Scale should remain as determined by appellants - Appeal disposed of.
