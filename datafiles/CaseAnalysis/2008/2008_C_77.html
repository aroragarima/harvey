Sahiti And Others v Chancellor, Dr. N.T.R. University of Health Sciences and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1688; (2009) 1 SCC 599; AIR 2009 SC 879; JT 2008 (12) SC 91; 2008(13) SCALE 464; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1032; 2008 (7) SLR 321; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 1024

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Revaluation, N.T.R. University of Health Sciences Act 1986

Summary: N.T.R. University of Health Sciences Act 1986 - Education - Decision of the Executive Council to cancel the result of re-verification of answer scripts and asking 294 students, who were declared passed on re-verification of answer scripts to re- appear in examination of first year MBBS, is upheld - Appeals against - Held, there may be several instances wherein re- evaluation of the answer scripts may be required to be ordered and this Court need not make an exhaustive catalogue of the same - If the authorities are of the opinion that re-evaluation of the answer scripts is necessary then the Court would be slow to substitute its own views for that of those who are expert in academic matters - No ground is made out by the appellants to interfere with the ultimate conclusion reached by the Division Bench - Appeals disposed of.
