Darshan Singh v State Of Punjab And Another

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 35; (2010) 2 SCC 333; AIR 2010 SC 1212; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 622; 2010 CRLJ 1393; 2010 (3) JCC 2217; JT 2010 (1) SC 306; 2010 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 336; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 587; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 751; 2010 (1) RLW 935; 2010(1) SCALE 467; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 642

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 96, 97 and 100 - Right of private defence - Petitioner shot dead his uncle - Before trial court petitioner pleaded right of private defence - Trial court acquitted petitioner on the ground that petitioner exercised his right of self defence u/ss. 96 and 97 of IPC - On appeal, HC reversed the acquittal and sentenced accused to life imprisonment -Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, in order to justify act of causing death of assailant, accused has simply to satisfy court that he was faced with an assault which caused a reasonable apprehension of death or grievous hurt - Question whether apprehension was reasonable or not is a question of fact depending upon facts and circumstances of each case and no formula can be prescribed in this regard - Weapon used, manner and nature of assault and other surrounding circumstances should be taken into account while evaluating whether apprehension was justified or not - Law does not require a law-abiding citizen to behave like a coward when confronted with an imminent unlawful aggression - There is nothing more degrading to human spirit than to run away in face of danger - Right of private defence is thus designed to serve a social purpose and deserves to be fostered within prescribed limits - In present case, petitioner had serious apprehension of death or at least grievous hurt when he exercised his right of private defence to save himself - Moreover, in a case of acquittal, if trial court's view is a possible or plausible view, then Appellate Court or HC would not be justified in interfering with it as it is settled legal position that there is presumption of innocence and that presumption is further fortified with acquittal of accused by trial court - Impugned judgement of HC set aside - Judgment of acquittal of trial court is restored - Appeal allowed.
