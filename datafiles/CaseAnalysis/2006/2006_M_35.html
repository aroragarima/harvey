M.P. State Electricity Board v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1536; (2006) 10 SCC 736; JT 2006 (8) SC 629; 2006(9) SCALE 194; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 133

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000, ss. 2, 37, 42, 43, 58, 58(1), 58(2), 58(3), 58(4), 58(5) and 75 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Bifurcation of State of Madhya Pradesh - Constitution of new Electricity Boards in the two bifurcated States on different dates - Madhya Pradesh State Electricity Board (MPSEB) was established in 1957 - A new State of Chhattisgarh was carved out of the State of Madhya Pradesh and formed on 1.11.2000 by virtue of Madhya Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000 - In furtherance of s. 58 of 2000 Act, State of Chhattisgarh constituted its own Electricity board w.e.f. 15.11.2000 and started collecting revenues w.e.f. the said date - However, it remitted all the revenues collected to the MPSEB from 15.11.2000 to 30.11.2000 - The State of Madhya Pradesh constituted a new board w.e.f. 1.1.2001 - On a complaint from the State of Madhya Pradesh alleging that Chhattisgarh State Electricity Board (CSEB) was illegally collected revenue, Government of India clarified that no unilateral action was to be taken by any State, and revenues realized within the State relating to distribution of power should be remitted without fail to MPSEB till the Board was bifurcated on a date as certified by the competent authority - Government of India issued a notification approving constitution of successor Boards w.e.f. 15.4.2001 - MPSEB filed a Writ Petition against CSEB for remittance of revenues illegally retained from 1.12.2000 to 14.4.2001 - Government of India, vide letter dt. 4.12.2001 provisionally confirmed the apportionment of assets and liability w.e.f. 15.4.2001 and opined that the revenue collected by CSEB before the said date should be remitted to MPSEB - State of Chhattisgarh filed a writ petition questioning the orders of Government of India dt. 12.4.2001 and 4.12.2001 - Said writ petition was dismissed by the HC - Hence, the instant appeal - Held, the cut-off date fixed by the Central Government cannot be said to be so arbitrary so as to attract the wrath of art. 14 of the Constitution of India - The logical corollary of our finding would be that the said date has been fixed in supersession of the earlier orders - the order impugned in the writ petition was admittedly passed by the Central Government upon giving an opportunity of hearing to both the parties - Factually the CSEB started functioning w.e.f. 15.11.2000 although it had remitted all the revenues collected to the MPSEB from 15.11.2000 to 30.11.2000 - It had started independent collection of revenue with effect from 1.12.2000 - Although MPSEB had all along been aware that the CSEB had been realizing revenue from the consumers, a protest was not made - Sub-section (1) of s. 58 of the 2000 Act contemplates only a situation where the existing Board was dissolved after the appointed day and it was continued till its dissolution - The Act to that effect was anomalous but it did not lead to an absurdity - Although the writ petitioner-Board came into force from 1.1.2001, having regard to the fixation of the appointed day as 1.11.2000, there cannot be a vacuum and with a view to avoid an absurd situation, all attempts must be made to iron out the creases - Illegality, if any, committed by CSEB in taking over of the assets of the Madhya Pradesh Electricity Board without there being any formal order of the Central Government in terms of s. 58(2) of the 2000 Act by itself may not be enough to arrive at the conclusion that the cut-off date fixed would be vitiated in law - Hence, writ petitions filed by the CSEB questioning the validity of the orders dt. 4.12.2000 and 12.4.2001, therefore, become infructuous - Writ petition dismissed.
