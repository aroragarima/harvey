C.V. Raman, Etc. v Management Of Bank Of India And Another, Etc.

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 719; (1988) 3 SCC 105; (1988) SCC (L&S) 687; AIR 1988 SC 1369; 1988 (2) BankCLR 67; [1988] 64 Comp Cas 358; 1988 (57) FLR 219; JT 1988 (2) SC 167; 1988 (1) KLT 759; 1989 LabIC 254; 1988 (2) LLJ 423; 1988 (2) LLN 156; 1989 (1) MLJ(SC) 23; 1988(1) SCALE 800; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 662; 1988 (2) SLR 758; 1988 (2) UJ 10

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Shops and Establishments Act 1960, Kerala Shops & Commercial Establishments Act, 1960, State bank of India Act 1958

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.10 and 109 - (A) Splitting up of unity and integrity of tenancy is not allowed exception u/s.109 of the 1882 Act - Lease of two shops by co-owners - On partition one of the them evicting the tenant from the shop - Also impleading another co-lessor - Determination of need of - (B) Transfer - Contended that what flows from a 'transfer' u/s. 5 r/s. 109 of T. P. Act cannot be predicated of a partition as partition is no 'transfer' It - Held, not necessary - Not actually a transfer of property, but would be covered by s.109 so principle underlying the section would in any event apply - Appeal allowed.
