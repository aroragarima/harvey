Amrit Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1304; (2006) 12 SCC 79; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 397; AIR 2007 SC 132; 2007 CRLJ 298; 2006 (3) JCC 2138; JT 2006 (10) SC 1; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3340; 2007 (1) MLJ 634; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 956; 2006(11) SCALE 309; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Rape - Circumstantial evidence - Death Penalty - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 376 - Awarding death penalty to appellant u/s. 302 and 376 IPC justified - Offence of rape took place on an agricultural field - Deceased might have suffered lot of pain, might have resisted, might have been gagged - Possibilities of some assault on her person cannot be ruled out but would be improper to hold that he killed her intentionally - Some marks of violence not only on neck but also on her mouth found - Submission that appellant might not have had an intention to kill deceased may have some farce - Medical report confirms that death could not have happened due to neck injuries - Deceased had bleeded half a litre of blood and death occurred as a consequence of and not because of any specific overt act on part of Appellant - Imposition of death penalty thus, improper and cannot be said to be a rarest of rare cases - Appellant had no pre-meditation for commission of offence - Offence may look as heinous, but under no circumstances, it can be said to be a rarest of rare cases - Appellant is an accused u/s. 376(2)(f) of IPC,1860 - Appellant deserves imposition of rigorous imprisonment for life - Appeal allowed.
