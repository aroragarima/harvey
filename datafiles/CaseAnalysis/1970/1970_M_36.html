Mathura Prasad Rajgarhia and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 409; (1970) 3 SCC 730; AIR 1971 SC 465

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Calcutta Improvement Act, 1911, s. 77A - Land acquisition - Compensation - Determined by the Tribunal - Reduced by High Court - Proprietary of - Whether HC's decision to award to appellants Rs. 11,59,430/- as compensation is vitiated the provision of Land Acquisition Act? - Held, award made by HC reducing amount awarded by tribunal cannot be justified on evidence on record, and law applicable thereto - In view of tribunal there was a general rise in price level of land all over town, and with that view HC agreed - But HC still held that in respect of land benefited by Schemes Nos. IV-M1 and IV-M2 rise in land values should be taken to be 80% above 1941 level - Finding of tribunal that rise of 80% in value of lands in town of Calcutta between 1941 and 1947 was a finding on a question of fact, was accepted by HC, as it was bound to accept in an appeal under Act - HC was also bound to accept finding of tribunal that additional 25% was attributable to two Schemes - HC recorded no reasons for assuming that increase in price of lands served by impairment schemes and not served by schemes will be same - In effect HC discarded, without assigning any reasons, appreciation resulting from application of improvement schemes - Tribunal also relied upon Ext. 35 which furnished some evidence of value of land - Tribunal accepted Exts. 42 and 35 as forming basis for calculating market value of land under acquisition - Sitting in appeal u/s. 77-A of Calcutta Improvement Act, HC could not set aside conclusion reached by tribunal on a matter of valuation - Order passed by HC is set aside and order of tribunal restored - Appeal allowed.
