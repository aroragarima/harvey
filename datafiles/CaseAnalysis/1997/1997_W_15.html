Wild Life Institute of India, Dehradun v Vijay Kumar Garg

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1780; (1997) 10 SCC 528

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Contract for construction of building - Contract terminated - Final payment made - After almost one year of signing of this receipt, respondent filed a suit u/s. 20 - Court directing appointment of arbitrator - Challenged - Held, final payment was accepted by respondent in full satisfaction of all his claims under contract and that there was no dispute outstanding - Arbitration clause under contract clearly provides that if contractor does not make any demand for arbitration in respect of any claim in writing within 90 days of receiving intimation from appellants that bill is ready for payment, claim of contractor will be deemed to have been waived and absolutely barred and appellants shall be discharged and released of all liabilities under contract in respect of these claims - Respondent has not made any claim within 90 days of even receipt of amount under final bill - High Court was not right in referring alleged dispute to arbitration - Appeal allowed.
