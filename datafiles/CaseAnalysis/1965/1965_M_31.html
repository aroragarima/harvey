Supreme Court of India

26 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1966 SC 153; 1965 MahLJ 764; 1965 MPLJ 852; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 102

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1939 - Respondents claim in application made by them under Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1939 for adjustment of their debt due to appellants - High Court set aside orders passed by courts - Whether HC can interfere with decision of subordinate court under revisional jurisdiction? - Held, while exercising its jurisdiction u/s. 115, it is not competent for HC to correct errors of fact however gross they may, or even errors of law, unless, said errors have relation to jurisdiction of court to try dispute itself - It is only in cases where, subordinate court has exercised a jurisdiction not vested in it by law, or has failed to exercise a jurisdiction so vested, or has acted in exercise of its jurisdiction illegally or with material irregularity that ,revisional jurisdiction of HC can be properly invoked - Appeal allowed.
