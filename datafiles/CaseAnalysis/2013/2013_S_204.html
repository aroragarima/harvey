State Of Maharashtra And Others v Nowrosjee Wadia College And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 53; (2013) 11 SCC 762; AIR 2013 SC 1192; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 421; JT 2013 (2) SC 283; 2013(2) SCALE 79; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 303; 2013 (2) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Leave Encashment, Poona University Act, 1974, Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Government Servants, Poona University Act, 1974 - Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994 - Maharashtra Civil Services (Leave) Rules, 1981, SS. 50,52,54 - Superannuation - Leave encashment - Government Servant - Reimbursement - Eligibility - Retired professors of respondent No.1 College filed applications before University Grievance Committee for encashment of earned leave, Committee passed order and recommended payment of amount in lieu of earned leave - Respondent No. 1 did not act due to which writ petition was filed for issuance of mandamus to respondent No. 1 to pay amount - DB, disposed petition directing respondent No. 1 to pay amount with liberty to seek reimbursement from State - Enactment of 1994, Act resulted in repealing various statutes of 1974, Act, State Govt. issued instruction to Universities to discontinue payment of leave encashment to teachers by pointing out that they fell in categories of employees working in Vacation Department and directed University Statutes should be amended with retrospective effect till then concerned University should bear expenses incurred in payment of leave encashment - Aggrieved by directives by State Govt. respondents filed writ petition for issue of mandamus to State to reimburse total amount paid and for grant of declaration that State was liable to reimburse amount paid to other teachers by way of leave encashment - DB, disposed petition by taking cognizance of statement made by Assistant Government Pleader that amount paid to teachers would be reimbursed by way of grant - Aggrieved instant SLP - Whether HC, was justified in directing State Govt. to reimburse amount paid in lieu of leave encashment by way of grant - Held, although, r. 54 had caption 'Earned leave for persons serving in Vacation Departments', sub-rule (1) thereof declares that a Govt. servant serving in a Vacation Department shall not be entitled to any earned leave in respect of duty performed in any year in which he availed full vacation - Sub-rule (2) of r. 54 deals with a situation in which a Govt. servant availed himself of a portion of vacation, in that event he was entitled to earned leave in such proportion of 30 days as number of days of vacation not taken bears to full vacation, Cl. (b) of r. 54(2) laid down that if a Govt. servant does not avail himself of any vacation in any year, earned leave shall be admissible to him in respect of that year in terms of r. 50 - provisions contained in 1981 Rules were not applicable to university teachers and teachers of affiliated colleges because they were not Govt. servants but this could not lead to an inference that affiliated colleges were entitled to reimbursement of amount paid to teachers in lieu of earned leave, though Statutes framed by University under 1974 Act entitled teachers of affiliated colleges to get benefit of leave encashment, there was no provision either in that Act or in 1994 Act, which obligated State Govt. to extend benefit of leave encashment to university teachers or to teachers of affiliated colleges and mere fact that Statutes of particular university provided for grant of leave encashment to teachers, does not entitle concerned university or college to claim reimbursement from State Government as of right - Criticism of directives issued by State Govt. to universities to amend Statutes under which teachers were given benefit of leave encashment was wholly misplaced - State Govt. was perfectly justified in issuing directives to universities to amend their Statutes, No doubt, in some of the communications reference has been made to Rules 50, 52 and 54 of the 1981 Rules but this does not detract from the fact that the State Government is empowered to issue such directives - Appeals allowed.
