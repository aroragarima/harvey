Delhi Administration v Bal Krishan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 577; (1972) 4 SCC 659; AIR 1972 SC 3; 1972 CRLJ 1; 1972 UJ 103

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 9 and 27 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - After a lapse of a long period, witnesses if able to identify dacoits - (B) Confession - Statement by way of confession - Distinctly relates to fact discovered - Admissibility - Held, it is not a proposition of law that after a lapse of a long period, witnesses would, in no case, be able to identify dacoits they had seen in course of a dacoity committed during night, however, Courts must be extremely cautious when such evidence is before them - Further held, s. 27 is by way of a proviso of ss. 25 and 26 and a statement even by way of confession which distinctly relates to fact discovered is admissible as evidence against accused - Appeal dismissed.
