B.S.N.L. v Reliance Communication Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1006; (2011) 1 SCC 394; 2016 (4) CompLJ 314; JT 2010 (13) SC 97; 2010(12) SCALE 586

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Access Providers, Carrier, Wll, TRAI's IUC Regulation

Summary: Telecom - Contract & Commercial - Masking of International Calls - Levy of pre-estimate damage - Justifiability - Respondent/Company entered into a BSO Interconnect Agreement with appellant/Department - Subsequently, BSO regime was replaced by Unified Access Services regime which granted the licence to service providers for both basic and mobile telephony services as part of a single unified licence - Appellant issued a circular specifying that Calling Line Identification (CLI) cannot be tampered with under any circumstances and also gave directions to service providers on how to prevent such tampering - Appellant, after an enquiry, found that the wrong routing of International Long Distance (ILD) calls was being done by one of the respondent's subscribers - Appellant, consequently, raised a bill levying penalty on respondent - Respondent referred the matter to TDSAT ( Telecom Dispute Settlement Appellate Tribunal) - TDSAT set aside the demand - Hence the present appeal - Whether clause 6.4.6 of the Interconnect Agreement between appellant/Department and respondent/Company is penal or a pre-estimate of damages - Held, u/cls.2.9.2 and 2.9.3 of the Interconnect Agreement, the establishment of proper screening function at its Gateway should be the obligation of the respondent/Unified Access Services Licensee (UASL) so as to detect signals outside the inter-working specification of TEC - As a corollary to the said clauses, cl. 6.4.6(a) inter alia provides that calls on non-specified trunk groups (like international calls landing on the local POIs), if detected, for which the IUC rate applicable is higher, then the higher IUC rate would be applicable for such unauthorized calls - Similarly, u/cl. 6.4.6(b), if the UASL masks or disguises the international call as local call that UASL will have to pay the higher IUC rate meant for international calls to appellant/Department from the date of provisioning of that POI or for preceding two months, whichever is less - U/cls. 6.4.6(a) and 6.4.6(b), the same economic and financial consequences flows to the respondent and appellant and that is the reason why cl. 6.4.6 provides for reasonable pre-estimate of damage - Thus, if there is masking of CLI for the calls generated and forwarded from the telephone of UASL, then it would be the primary duty of that UASL to prevent such misuse and failing which appellant/Department would be free to invoke cl. 6.4.6 - Further, it is not possible to trace each such unauthorized call, particularly its nature, as to from which place it originated and if it was possible the cost of tracing such call(s) may be much more than actual damage, if ascertainable, and therefore, a 'rough and ready measure' is provided in cl. 6.4.6 which measure is a reasonable pre-estimate of damage - Further, cls. 6.4.6(a) and 6.4.6(b) restrict the higher IUC rate made applicable for calls only for last two preceding months and not for last three years or the longer period - Said time lines is an indicia showing that clause 6.4.6 is not penal but a pre-estimate of reasonable compensation for the loss foreseen at the time of entering into the agreement - Therefore, cl. 6.4.6 represents pre-estimate of reasonable compensation for the loss suffered by the appellant/Department - Impugned judgment of TDSAT is set aside and the matter remitted back to TDSAT to decide afresh - Appeal allowed.
