Primella Sanitary Products Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Goa

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 839; (2005) 10 SCC 644; 2005 (184) E.L.T. 117; 2005 (68) RLT 708

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Tariff - Item Nos. 48.18 and 56.01 - In the appeal filed against the order of the Collector (Appeals), the counsel appearing for the appellants made a concession that their products were similar to one M/s. Patsan Hygiene Industries Pvt. Ltd. and that as in the case of M/s. Patsan Hygiene Industries Pvt. Ltd. it has already been held by the tribunal that the product was classifiable under tariff item No. 48.18, he had instructions not to press the appeal - On such concession, the appeals were dismissed by the tribunal - Whether sanitary towels manufactured by the appellants are classifiable under tariff item No. 48.18 or under tariff item No. 56.01? - Appellants knew that their appeal was dismissed on this concession - Appeals against adjudicating orders came up for hearing later - If concessions were not under instructions the party would have now agitated - On the contrary the same counsel is again permitted to make the concession in the other appeal - Impossible to believe that the counsel made this concession without instructions from the client - Held, in the order D/- 22-04-1999, it has been correctly recorded that the concession had been made under instructions - Matter of classification proceeded on a matter of concession of facts and thus, appellants not permitted to withdraw from that concession - Now not permitted to argue on the question of classification - Appeal disposed of.
