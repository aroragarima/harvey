Manjit Singh @ Mange and others v State of Uttar Pradesh, through SP, CBI and others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 57; (2011) 11 SCC 578; AIR 2011 SC 806; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 250; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 765; 2011(1) SCALE 766; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 997

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984, TADA Rules, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(1), 3(2) and 3(3) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 302 - Delhi Police Special Establishment Act, 1946, s. 3 - Admissibility of confession against co-accused not charged under TADA Act - Conviction - Challenged - Case u/ss. 302 r/w 120-B of IPC and ss. 3(2) and 3(3) r/w s. 3(1) of TADA Act was registered - Charge sheet against 'K' and 'M' was filed in the Designated Court both under the provisions of the IPC and the TADA Act - Subsequently, 'B' was arrested in Singapore - Since, there was no law in Singapore which corresponds to the TADA Act, though 'B' was extradited, he could only be tried u/s. 120-B and 302 of the IPC and, therefore, no charge u/s. 3 of TADA Act was framed against 'B' - Designated Court (TADA) relying on s. 12 of TADA Act, held that 'B' was rightly charged for an offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B of the IPC and tried him jointly with the accused 'K' and 'M' - It was further held that since the investigation was handed over to Superintendent of Police, CBI, by the State of Uttar Pradesh by issuing notification, prior approval from Superintendent of Police, CBI, was sufficient compliance of s. 20A of TADA Act - On the issue of the admissibility of the confessional statement of the accused 'K' and 'M' against the co-accused 'B', the confessional statements were held to be admissible against the co-accused - All the three accused were convicted u/s. 120B r/w s. 302 of the IPC but were acquitted of charges under TADA Act - Hence, present appeals - (A) Admissibility of confession against co-accused not charged under TADA Act - Whether the confessional statement of the co-accused admissible against 'B', who was not charged under the TADA Act? - Held, s. 15 of TADA Act, after its amendment, authorizes the Designated Court to use the confession statement of one accused against another accused only when the co-accused is charged in the same case along with the confessor and is tried together with the confessor in the same case - Hence, confessional statement made by a person u/s. 15 of TADA Act shall be admissible in the trial of a co-accused for offence committed and tried in the same case together with the accused who makes the confession - (B) Whether apart from confession of the co-accused against 'B', there was any other evidence against him to sustain the conviction and sentence u/s. 302 r/w s. 120-B of IPC? - Held, from the evidence on record, it can safely be inferred that 'B' was the mastermind of the whole incident and 'M' and 'K' committed the offence at the behest of 'B' - Hence, there was independent incriminating evidence against 'B' - (C) Whether there was breach of mandatory requirements provided in s. 20A(1) of TADA Act while recording the commission of an offence under the TADA Act? - It was contended that u/s. 20A of TADA Act, the sanction of the District Superintendent of Police was required to be obtained before the police record any information about the commission of an offence under the TADA Act and since the same was not been obtained, the conviction of the accused cannot be sustained - Held, in the present case, the State Government, in exercise of the power conferred by s. 3 of the Delhi Police Special Establishment Act handed over the investigation to CBI - Since investigation was done by Superintendent of Police, CBI could authorize the Police to record the information about the commission of the offence under the Act - Therefore, 'prior approval' by the Superintendent of Police, CBI would adequately satisfy the requirements u/s. 20A(1) - Further, no prejudice was caused to the accused as a result of authorization being granted by Superintendent of Police, CBI - (D) Whether conviction of 'K' and 'M' for offences under the provisions of the IPC sustainable with the available evidence on record? - Held, evidence on record presents an unimpeachable evidence against the accused, clearly indicating the modus operandi and the motive - Hence, Designated Court (TADA) was justified in convicting and sentencing 'K' and 'M' for the offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - (E) Whether Designated Court (TADA) was justified in acquitting all the accused persons for the offences charged and tried under the TADA Act? - Held, person who is alleged to be involved in a terrorist act can be charged u/s. 3(1) of TADA Act only when the prosecution has been successful in establishing that the same was committed with the intent to awe the government or to achieve one or the other ends mentioned u/s. 3(1) of TADA Act - In the present case, prosecution was not successful in proving that this particular murder was committed with the intention to cause terror - Designated Court (TADA) was justified in dismissing the charges framed under the TADA Act - Appeals dismissed.
