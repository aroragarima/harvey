S. Nagaraj and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 589; (1993) Supp4 SCC 595; (1994) SCC (L&S) 320; 1994 (26) ATC 448; JT 1993 (5) SC 27; 1994 (1) LLJ 851; 1993(3) SCALE 548; 1994 (1) SLJ 61; 1993 (5) SLR 1; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Karnataka Civil Service Rules, Karnataka Civil Services (Special Recruitment) Rules, 1982

Summary: R. M. SAHAI, J.

Service - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Civil Services (General Recruitment) Rules, 1977 - Karnataka Civil Services (Special Recruitment) Rules, 1982 - Karnataka Civil Services Special (Amendment) Rules 1987 r. 3(4) - Amendment in rules - Non compliance - Challenge - State Govt. framed scheme for employment of unemployed graduates, postgraduates and diploma holders - Rules were amended, in 1987 and three important changes were made - Govt. first issued order for stipendiary graduates to be absorbed against posts which required minimum graduate qualification and then issued another order creating certain stipendiary posts Govt. order was not implemented so association of Stipendiary Graduates approached HC by way of petition seeking declaration that they were regular employees under Govt. and that petition was transferred to Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed claim of association - Against order of Tribunal, association filed special petition and some of stipendiary graduates who had not approached HC or Tribunal approached SC directly by way of Petition in which Court order to comply eith direction but that was also not followed - Hence, instant Petition - Whether application for modifying order that modified and stipendiary graduates could be directed to be appointed as Second Division Assistants - Held, all confusion arose because of r. 3(4) of 1987 Rules as provision abolishing any test enlarged field of eligibility leaving scope for pick and choose arbitrarily - However, in absence of any guideline to appoint one against another, selection was bound to generate extraneous consideration - Further, it was informed that recruitment under 1977 scheme had been stopped since 1980 but if same was still in operation it needed immediate attention - Reservation of 40% posts for stipendiary graduates in direct recruits quota itself needed fresh look - Thus, framing of scheme in keeping with constitutional goal to provide jobs to unemployed could not be construed as right of such incumbents to enter civil service as matter of right either due to length of service or because allowance paid was lower than others who had entered civil service under the rules - Therefore, application for modifying order that modified and stipendiary graduates could be directed to be appointed as Second Division Assistants was allowed with certain directions - Petition disposed of.

B. P. JEEVAN REDDY, J. -

(A) Service - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Civil Services (General Recruitment) Rules, 1977 - Karnataka Civil Services (Special Recruitment) Rules, 1982 - Justifiability of order - Whether it was just, proper and consistent with law to direct Govt. to absorb all remaining five thousand S.Gs. in regular group 'C' Govt. service- Held, S.Gs. were appointed without any test or process of selection on ad hoc basis - However, original scheme was measure of unemployment relief as idea was to provide them small amount by way of stipend till they got employed in Govt. service or elsewhere - Further, stipend was raised from time to time under orders of Court it had been raised - Thus, number of those persons runs into several thousands as about five thousand persons remained unabsorbed in regular Govt. service - In pursuance of orders of Court, Govt. appeared to have 'absorbed' them in Govt. service but paying them only stipend of Rs. 960 per month - Those were all persons, it was relevant to remember, who either did not apply or did not appear in regular selection for group 'C' posts or having applied and appeared for regular selection for group 'C' posts, they could not be selected - Petition disposed of.

(B) Service - Practice & Procedure - Karnataka Civil Services (Special Recruitment) Rules, 1982 - Karnataka Civil Services Special (Amendment) Rules 1987 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 16 - Power to enforce - Whether Court should enforce 1982 Rules as amended in 1987 - Held, 1987 amendments had effect of smuggling in thousands of persons into Govt. service by back-door without complying with requirements of arts. 14, 16 of the Constitution - However, it was true that no one had questioned 1987 amendments as parties who were affected namely persons awaiting employment under Govt. probably did not even knew what was happening - But where as unconstitutional provision of such vast impact was brought to notice of Court and it was asked to enforce that, it was constitutional duty of Court to refuse to do so - Therefore, Court should refuse to make any orders directing implementation of rules as amended in 1987 - Petitions disposed of.
