Union of India and Others v Indian Charge Chrome and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 670; (1999) 7 SCC 314; AIR 1999 SC 3504; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 596; 1999 (84) ECR 273; 1999 (112) E.L.T. 753; JT 1999 (6) SC 306

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 25 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Project Imports (Registration of Contract) Regulations, 1965 - Notification No. 71/85 D/-17-3-1985 - Notification No. 306/86 D/-1- 5-1986 - Notification No. 67/87 D/-1-3-1987 - (A) Whether High Court had erred in holding Notification No. 67/87 ultra vires art. 14? - If legislature takes care to reasonably classify persons for legislative purposes, so long as classification is founded on an intelligible differentia which lays down a perceptible differentiation between two groups and differentiation has a rational relation with object sought to be achieved, such a classification does not fall foul of art. 14 of Constitution - Held, notification is only clarificatory and not violative of art. 14 - (B) Whether Asst. Collector was justified in refusing to register respondent's contract with M/s Asea Stal in view of amendment notification? - Mere making of an application for registration does not confer any vested right on applicant - Further held, no fault can be found with refusal to register contract - (C) Whether HC had erred in holding that that Asst. Collector of Customs did not have power to cancel any registration once granted? - New regulations having specifically saved things done or omitted to be done under superseded regulations authority empowered to register contract could not have cancelled registration already done, more so without affording respondent any opportunity of hearing - Further held, HC was therefore justified in setting aside communication by Asst. Collector of Customs cancelling registration of contract - Appeal partly allowed.
