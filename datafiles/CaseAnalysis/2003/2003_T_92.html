Prohibition & Excise Supdy. Andhra Pradesh and v Toddy Tappers Coop. Society, Marredpally and

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1013; (2003) 12 SCC 738; AIR 2004 SC 658; JT 2003 (9) SC 178; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 73; 2003(9) SCALE 643; 2003 (1) SCW 6271; 2003 (7) SLT 561; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 576; 2004 (2) Supreme 593

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977, Andhra Pradesh Excise (Arrack and Toddy Licenses General Conditions) Rules, 1969

Summary: Excise - A.P. Excise (Arrack and Toddy Licenses General Conditions) Rules, 1969, r. 24(As amended) - Validity of amended r. 24 - HC struck down the amended r. 24, quashing the suspension of licences and show-cause notices issued for cancellation with regard to the various toddy shops of the respondents - HC's order whether justified? - Held, the HC is not justified in striking down the retrospective effect of r. 24 in the facts and circumstances of the case, which was amended in the larger public interest, and that the amended rule is procedural in nature and as such retrospective effect can be given to the said rule - Further held, the HC is not justified in quashing the cancellation of licenses and show cause notices for cancellation of licence in the facts and circumstances of the case - Further, the appellants are at liberty to place the amended r. 24 before the state legislature in accordance with ss 72(3) and (4) of the said Act TheGovt.is also at liberty to proceed further pursuant to the show cause notices issued and dispose of the matter in accordance with law and after affording opportunity to the respondents herein - Appeals allowed.
