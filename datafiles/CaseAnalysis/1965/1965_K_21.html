Karsandas H. Thacker v Messrs Saran Engineering Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 513; AIR 1965 SC 1981

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 73 - Iron and Steel (Scrap Control) Order (1943), cl. 8 - The Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, s. 3(1)- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Whether the appellant committed breach of contract or not? - Held, the High Court was right in coming to the conclusion that the defendant-respondent did not know that the appellant was purchasing scrap iron for export - The appellant, on the breach of contract by the respondent, was entitled, u/s. 73 of the Contract Act, 1872, to receive compensation for any loss by the damage caused to him which naturally arose in the usual course of business from such breach - Loss which could have naturally arisen in usual course of things from the breach of contract by the respondent in the present case would be nil - We therefore hold that the HC was right in holding that the appellant suffered no such damage which he could recover from the respondent - Appeal dismissed .
