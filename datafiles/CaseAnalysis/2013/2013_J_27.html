State of Jharkhand and others v Jitendra Kumar Srivastava and another

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 507; (2013) 12 SCC 210; 2014 (1) AWC 159; 2013 (139) FLR 74; JT 2013 (11) SC 351; 2013 (3) KLT 782; 2013(5) M.P.H.T. 212; 2014 (1) OLR 341; 2013 (4) RLW 3498; 2013(10) SCALE 310; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 177; 2013 (5) SLR 448; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2369

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Leave Encashment, West Bengal Services (Death-cum-- Retirement Benefit) Rules, 1971

Summary: (1) Employment - Pension-Withholding of pension/gratuity-In absence of any provision in Pension Rules, State Government cannot withhold part of pension and/or gratuity during pendency of departmental/criminal proceedings-Attempt of appellant (State Government) to take away part of pension or gratuity or even leave encashment without any statutory provision and under umbrage of administrative instruction-Cannot be countenanced-On basis of circular, which is not having force of law, appellant (State Government) cannot withhold part of pension or gratuity-In instant case, so far as statutory rules are concerned, there is no provision for withholding pension or gratuity in given situation-Had there been any such provision in Pension Rules, position would have been different-High Court rightly directed appellant to release withheld dues to respondent-Impugned order of High Court is without blemish.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 300A-Right to property-Right to receive pension-Is recognised as right to property-Pension/gratuity is hard earned benefit which accrues to employee and is in nature of "property"-Person cannot be deprived of pension/gratuity without authority of law, which is constitutional mandate enshrined in Article 300A.

(3) Constitution of India-Article 300A-"Law" within meaning of Article 300A-Executive instructions-Are not having statutory character and, therefore, cannot be termed as "law" within meaning of Article 300A.

(4) Employment-Pension and gratuity-Are not bounties-Employee earn these benefits by dint of his long, continuous, faithful and unblemished service-These benefits are had earned benefit which accrues to employee and is in nature of "property"-Person cannot be deprived of these benefit without authority of law-Which is constitutional mandate enshrined in Article 300A of the Constitution.
