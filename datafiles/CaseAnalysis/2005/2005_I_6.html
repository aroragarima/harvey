Icici Bank and Another v Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 447; (2005) 6 SCC 404; AIR 2005 SC 3315; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 319; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 165; JT 2005 (6) SC 570; 2005(6) SCALE 110; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 62

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Corporation Act, Bombay Municipal Corporation Act 1888

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act 1888, ss. 28A(b)(c), 328, 328-A - Illuminated signboards of ATM centers - Notice requiring appellant to make payment of amount - Whether notices issued by Corporation to appellant-ICICI Bank are per se illegal or without authority of law as putting up sign boards of ATM enters at different places by bank could out-rightly be said not to be an advertisement and thus does not attract provision of s. 328 A? - Held, no exercise was undertaken by municipal authorities or Bombay HC before HC had reached to conclusion that sign boards of ATM center put up by ICICI bank at different locations would be an advertisement within s.328 A - Corporation shall decide question of advertisement - Appeal allowed.
