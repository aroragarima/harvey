Â Kays Construction Company Private, Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 45; AIR 1965 SC 1488; 1965 (11) FLR 328; 1965 (2) LLJ 429; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33C - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 6 H(1), 6 H(2) - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, s. 20 - (A) Whether back wages for period of forced unemployment should be given under s. 6H(1) and (2)? - Held, where money due is back wages for period of unemployment case is covered by first sub section and not second - Some calculation enters in determination of amount for which certificate will eventually issue but this calculation is not of type mentioned in second sub-section and cannot be made to fit in elaborate phrase 'benefit which is capable of being computed in terms of money' - Mere arithmetical calculations of amount due are not required to be dealt with under elaborate procedure of second clause - Appeal Nos. 1108 and 1109 of 1963 dismissed.
