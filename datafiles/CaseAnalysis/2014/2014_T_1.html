T. N. Generation and Distribution Corporation Limited v PPN Power Gen. Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 219; (2014) 11 SCC 53; 2014(6) ALL MR 476; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 86; 2014(4) SCALE 560; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 667

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Supply of electricity, Rebate on part payment, Dispute regarding invoices

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Supply of electricity - Dispute regarding invoices - Rebate on part payment - Entitled to - Respondent generating company entered into Power Purchase Agreement (PPA) with appellant for supply of entire Electricity to be generated by respondent for period of 30 years - According to appellant, invoices of respondent inter alia included interest on debt sanctioned but not disbursed, charges towards energy consumed at the residential quarters at the generating station etc - Appellant claimed that substantial payments towards monthly invoices raised by respondent for every month were paid against admitted amount in invoice - Further dispute arose between respondent and appellant with accounting details provided by each other - Since dispute was not resolved, respondent filed petition before State commission payment of sum of Rs. 1,89,91,17,264 - State Commission by order allowed petition filed by respondent for refund of excess rebate availed by appellant - Appellant filed appeal before APTEL - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to avail rebate of 2.5 % on part payment of monthly invoice within 5 business days from date of presentation of monthly invoice -

Held, APTEL concluded that merely because substantial payment were made in relation to monthly invoices would not entitle appellant to claim rebate of 2.5% on invoice amount - There was no reason to interfere with findings recorded by APTEL - Thus it would be evident that even if amount of invoice was disputed, appellant was obliged to make full payments of invoice when due and then raise dispute - Early payment was encouraged by offering rebate of 2.5% if paid within 5 days of date of invoice - Similarly, 1% rebate would be available if payment of entire invoice was made within 30 days - Rebate was in form of incentive and was exception to general rule requiring payment in full on due date - Therefore, appellant had no legal right to claim rebate at rate of 2.5% not had paid entire invoice amount within 5 days - Similarly, appellant would be entitled to 1% rebate if payment was made within 30 days of invoice - Thus, findings of APTEL on this issue did not call for any interference - Appellant illegally arrogated to itself right to adjudicate by unilaterally assuming jurisdiction not available to it - It was true that reconciliation was to be done annually but payment was to be made on monthly basis, this should not even be disputed by appellant in its claim for rebate at rate of 2.5% for have made part payment of invoice amount within 5 days - Therefore, benefit of interest was given on such annual invoices - In regard to interest on late payment, APTEL considered entire matter and concluded that interest was payable on compound rate basis in terms of the agreement - Central Bank of India v Ravindra and Others 2001 Indlaw SC 19930 relied on - Appeal dismissed.
