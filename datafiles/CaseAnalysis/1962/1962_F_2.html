Fine Knitting Company, Limited v Industrial Court and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 29; 1962 (4) FLR 289; 1962 (1) LLJ 275; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 196; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 2 and 11- Whether knitting and hosiery sections in appellant's establishment should be treated as one concern? - Evidence on record show that spinning section assumed major importance than hosiery - Functional interdependence could not be proved by appellant - Held, strength of employees, pattern for payment of wages to employees, distinguished treatment to employees, were different and proved that two activities were separate and as such must be separately recognized - (B)Whether respondent erred in recognizing two different undertakings? - Further held, if a company operates different types of industrial works, it amounts to separate enterprises or undertakings and it should be recognised as such - Appeal dismissed.
