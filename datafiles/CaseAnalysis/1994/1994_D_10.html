Corporation Bank v D.S. Godwa

Supreme Court of India

20 June 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 345; (1994) 5 SCC 213; AIR 1994 SCW 2721; 1995 (3) AWC 1760; 1994 (2) BC 613; 1994 (2) CCC 543; [1994] 81 Comp Cas 842; JT 1994 (7) SC 87; 1994(3) SCALE 46; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 170

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Banker And Customer, Quarterly Rest, Usurious Loans Act, 1918, Mortgage Deed, Excessive, Overdrafts, Banking Laws (Amendment) Act, 1983

Summary: Banking & Finance - Usurious Loans Act, 1918 - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 21 and 35-A - (A) Whether bank is entitled to claim interest with periodical rests, e.g., a monthly rest, a quarterly rest, a six-monthly rest, or a yearly rest, or compound interest in any other manner, from a borrower who has obtained a loan or an advance for agricultural purposes? - Held, referring various circulars issued by Reserve Bank from time to time in exercise of power u/s. 21/35-A of Banking Regulation Act, provide that agricultural advances should not be treated on a par with commercial loans insofar as rate of interest thereon is concerned because farmers do not have any regular source of income except sale proceeds of their crops which income they get once a year - Therefore recovery of interest with quarterly or six-monthly rests from farmers is, therefore, not feasible - (B) Whether banks are bound to follow directives/circulars issued by RBI in exercise of power u/s. 21 of Banking Regulation Act, 1949? - Held, wide powers are conferred on Reserve Bank to enable it to exercise effective control over all banks u/ss. 21 and 35-A enable it to directions to all other banks in regard to loan policies with a view to control charging of interest on loans or advances made from time to time - Appeal dismissed.
