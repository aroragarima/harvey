Â Rajahmundry Electric Supply Corporation Limited v State of Andhra

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 53; AIR 1954 SC 251; 1954 (1) MLJ 493; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 493; 1954 SCJ 310; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Constitution - Electricity & Energy - Defence & Security Forces - Madras Electricity Supply Undertakings (Acquisition) Act, 1949 - Govt. of India Act,1935, ss. 104 and 299 - (A)Whether Parliament has granted any power to federal and provincial legislature to make law with respect to compulsory acquisition of a commercial or industrial undertaking ? - Held, Parliament has expressly entrusted provincial legislature with power to make a law with respect to compulsory acquisition of land but no such power has been entrusted to make a law with respect to compulsory acquisition of a commercial or industrial undertaking - It has however been left at discretion of Governor-General to empower either of legislatures to enact such a law - (B) Whether right to acquisition of property by state is exercised under a law? - Further held, compulsory acquisition of property is an important sovereign right of State but this right however has to be exercised under a law - Appeal allowed.
