Tukaram Kana Joshi and others through Power of Attorney Holder v M.I.D.C. and others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 413; (2013) 1 SCC 353; AIR 2013 SC 565; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 7; 2013 (96) ALR 226; 2013 (1) AWC 10; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 103; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 333; 2013 (2) CTC 222; JT 2012 (11) SC 246; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 515; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 293; 2013 (118) RD 616; 2012(11) SCALE 4; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 29; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 2939

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4,6 - Acquisition of Land - Compensation - Proceedings Lapsed - Discrimination - Validity - Predecessors-in-interest of appellant were illiterate farmers and owners of disputed land - Govt. issued notice u/s. 4 of Act, but no subsequent proceedings were taken up due to which acquisition proceedings lapsed - Respondent authorities handed over physical possession of land to Industrial Development Corporation - Appellants had been regularly persuading with authorities to complete deemed acquisition proceeding even a declaration u/s. 6 of Act was not issued and appellants were not compensated for land acquisition - Appellant approached Special Land Acquisition Officer as well as Revenue Authorities without any success - Aggrieved they approached HC and filed Writ Petition, HC, dismissed on ground of delay and non-availability of certain documents - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellants contended that they were deprived of immovable property which was their fundamental rights as per Constitution and statutory right - Whether respondents authorities were justified in acquiring land of appellants without following due process of acquisition - Held, appellants had been seriously discriminated against qua other persons, whose land was also acquired, some of them were given benefits of acquisition, including compensation in year 1966 - This kind of discrimination not only breeds corruption, but also dis-respect for governance, as it leads to frustration and to a certain extent, forces persons to take law into their own hands - Findings of HC, that requisite records were not available, or that appellants approached authorities at a belated stage were contrary to evidence available on record and thus, could not be accepted and excused as it remained a slur on system of governance and justice alike, and an anathema to doctrine of equality, which is soul of our Constitution - Even under valid acquisition proceedings, there was a legal obligation on part of authorities to complete such acquisition proceedings at earliest, and to make payment of requisite compensation - Appeals etc. were required to be decided expeditiously, for sole reason that, if a person was not paid compensation in time, he would be unable to purchase any land or other immovable property, for amount of compensation that was likely to be paid to him at a belated stage - Appeal disposed of.
