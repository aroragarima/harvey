Chairman, State Bank of India and Another v All Orissa State Bank Officers Association and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 563; (2003) 11 SCC 607; (2004) SCC (L&S) 115; AIR 2003 SC 4201; 2003 (3) CLR 281; 2003 (98) FLR 971; JT 2003 (6) SC 339; 2003 LabIC 3759; 2003 (3) LLJ 751; 2003 (3) LLN 784; 2003(5) SCALE 675; 2003 (1) SCW 3760; 2003 (5) SLR 78; 2003 (4) SLT 844; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 27; 2003 (5) Supreme 406; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 1997

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Rules for Verification of Membership and Recognition of Trade Unions Rules, 1994 - r. 24 - Unrecognised Trade Union - Grievance that the bank had adopted a policy of hostile discrimination against the Union - Held, there is no common law right of a trade union to represent its members, whether for purposes of collective bargaining or individual grievances of members. This is an inroad made into the common law by special statutes. Either the special statute operates proprio vigore, or it does not - Further held, denying such a right of representation to the minority union, when such a right is not conceded even to the majority union does not amount to discrimination requiring redressal at the hands of the High Court. It is also not possible for the High Court to exercise its powers under art. 226 to direct an employer to bring into existence such a system of representation in grievance procedure.
