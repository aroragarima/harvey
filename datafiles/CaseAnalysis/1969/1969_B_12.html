Badri Pershad v Smt. Kanso Devi

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 122; (1969) 2 SCC 586; AIR 1970 SC 1963; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 95

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family and Personal - Women and Children - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, s. 3 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s.14(1)(2) - Suit filed by appellant praying for perpetual injunction against injunction - Appellant alleged that respondent was trying to alienate properties in which appellant had life estate only - Trial Court and HC held that respondent was full owner u/s. 14(1) and there was no act of waste - Whether HC erred in coming to conclusion that respondent was full owner of properties u/s. 14(1) or case of respondent falls u/s. 14(2)? - Respondent possessed rights in joint properties before commencement of 1956 act - After act came into force her pre-existing limited ownership converts into full and absolute ownership - Held, s.14(1) will be applicable and respondent is full owner thereof - Appeal dismissed.
