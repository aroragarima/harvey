New India Insurance Company Limited v Smt. Shanti Misra and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 349; (1975) 2 SCC 840; 1976 ACJ 128; AIR 1976 SC 237; [1977] 47 Comp Cas 453; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, ss. 9, 110, 110A and 110F - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Whether an application for compensation filed under s. 110A of the Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 arising out of an accident which occurred more than 60 days before the constitution of the Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal under s. 110 could be entertained by the Tribunal or the remedy of the aggrieved person was to institute a civil suit? - Change of law operates retrospectively and the person has to go to the new forum even if his cause of action or right of action accrued prior to the change of forum - He will have a vested right of action but not a vested right of forum - It is legitimate to think that the legislature did not think it necessary to affect the pending suits but wanted the cheap remedy to be available is soon as the Tribunal was constituted by the State Govts., in all cases irrespective of the date of the accident, provided the remedy of going to the court was not barred on the date of the constitution of the Tribunal - Held, the jurisdiction of the civil court is ousted as soon as the Claims Tribunal is constituted and the filing of the application before the Tribunal is the only remedy available to the respondent - Remedy available to the respondents was to go before the Claims Tribunal and since the law was not very clear on the point, the time of about four months taken in approaching the Tribunal after its constitution can be held to be either a reasonable time or the delay of less than 2 months could well be condoned - Appeal dismissed.
