Shambhu Prasad Sharma v Charandas Mahant and others

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 181; (2012) 11 SCC 390; AIR 2012 SC 2648; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 148; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 33; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 761; 2012(6) SCALE 166; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Representation of the People (Third Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11 - Nomination - Rejection of Plaint - Mandatory Affidavit - Cause of Action - Sustainability - Appellant filed Election Petition before HC, for declaration about his having been elected unopposed apart from declaration to effect that nomination papers filed by remaining 17 candidates had been improperly and illegally accepted - Appellant alleged that mandatory affidavit of 17 candidates which were along with nomination papers were incomplete as it did not contain averment about due outstanding against candidate towards financial institution or Govt. - Returning Officer overlooked and accepted nominations - Respondent filed petition under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC for rejection of plaint for no cause of action - HC, allowed petition and dismissed election petition - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in dismissing Election Petition - Held, appellant alleged that affidavit did not in prescribed format stated whether candidates had any outstanding liabilities qua financial institutions or the Govt., a departure from format would assume some importance if appellant alleged that there were such outstanding liabilities which were concealed by candidates - Any departure from prescribed format for disclosure of information about dues, if any, payable to financial institutions or Govt. would not be of much significance, especially when declaration made by returned candidate in his affidavit clearly stated that no such dues were recoverable from deponent - Departure from format was not, in circumstances, of a substantial character on which nomination papers of returned candidate could be lawfully rejected by returning officer - Appellant was required to not only allege material facts relevant to such improper acceptance, but further assert that election of returned candidate had been materially affected by such acceptance but there was no such assertion in election petition - Mere improper acceptance assuming that any such improper acceptance was supported by assertion of material facts by appellant, would not disclose a cause of action to call for trial of election petition on merit unless same was alleged to have materially affected result of returned candidate - Appeal dismissed.
