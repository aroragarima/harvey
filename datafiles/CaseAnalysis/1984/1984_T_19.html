A.L.Kalara v The Project & Equipment Corporation Of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 289; (1984) 3 SCC 316; (1984) SCC (L&S) 497; AIR 1984 SC 1361; 1984 (49) FLR 119; 1984 LabIC 961; 1984 (2) LLJ 186; 1984 (2) LLN 200; 1984(1) SCALE 798; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 646; 1984 (2) SLJ 82; 1984 (2) SLR 446; 1984 UJ 733

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Project and Equipment Corporation House Buildings Advance (Grant and recovery) Rules, PEC Conveyance Advance (Grant and Recovery) Rules, PEC Employees' (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1975, PEC House Building Advance (Grant and Recovery) Rules

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12,13, 14, 16, 14, 226, 311 and Part III and Part XIV - Industrial Project and Equipment Corp. of India Ltd. Employees' (Conduct, Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1975, rr. 4 and 5 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, sch.2, item 3 - Public Sector Undertaking - Dismissal of Employee - For lapse/ failure to return advances for House Building and for purchasing vehicle - (A) Whether taking the findings of facts as recorded by the inquiry officer and accepting for the present purpose that they are not open to a judicial review, do they constitute misconduct so as to invite penalty? - Held, advance, if not utilised within the prescribed period or if not refunded within the same time, will expose the drawer to a liability to penal interest - (B) Determination of jurisdiction over the corporation in light of art. 12 - Held, it is 'other authority' within the meaning of art. 12 and amenable to writ jurisdiction - (C) Validity of disciplinary order - Held, order of removal passed by Disciplinary Authority is illegal and invalid for the reasons: (1) that the action is thoroughly arbitrary and is violative of art. 14, (2) that the alleged misconduct does not constitute misconduct within the 1975 Rules, (3) that the inquiry officer himself found that punishment was already imposed for the alleged misconduct by withholding the salary and the appellant could not be exposed to double jeopardy, and (4) that the findings of the inquiry officer are unsupported by reasons and the order of the Disciplinary Authority as well as the Appellate Authority suffer from the same vice - (C) What relief the appellant is entitled ? Once the order of removal from service is held to be illegal and invalid and the appellant being in public employment, the necessary declaration must follow that he continues to be in service uninterruptedly - Appeal allowed.
