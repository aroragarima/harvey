Babita Prasad and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 551; (1993) Supp3 SCC 268; (1993) SCC (L&S) 1076; 1993 (25) ATC 598; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 135; 1993 (1) SLJ 1; 1993 (1) SLR 44; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - Appointment of Assistant-teachers in primary schools - Circular-letter D/- 02-07-1989, issued by State of Bihar pursuant to judgment of High Court in case of Anil Kumar v Chief Secretary - (A) Whether appointment of appellants have been rightly quashed in Civil Appeal No's 3218 of 1991, 3219 of 1991, 3220 of 1991? - It is fairly submitted by counsel for State that teachers who had been appointed till 02-07-1989, before or after Anil Kumar judgment and were continuing in service by virtue of stay orders, issued by courts, would be retained in service - Held, their retention in service is directed and continuity of service throughout for all other purposes is granted, however since they did not serve for interregnum of about 4 months, relief of back wages is declined - (B) C A No's 3216 of 1991, 2082 of 1991 and W P (C) No. 911 of 1991 - (i) Panels - Vested right - Empanelment was not as a result of any process of selection either and none of qualified trained teachers were excluded from being brought on panel - Panel by very nature of its preparation was, therefore, no more than a list of all eligible qualified teachers arranged according to merit in a chronological order year-wise which was utilised to make appointments of teachers as and when vacancies arose - Held, a panel of type prepared in present case cannot be equated with a panel which is prepared having co-relation to existing vacancies or anticipated vacancies arising in near future and for a fixed time and prepared as a result of some selection process and therefore cannot be treated as conferring any vested or indefeasible right to teachers to be appointed - (ii) Whether GO D/- 02-07-1989 and concession made by State Counsel in favour of candidates who had been appointed, both before and after judgment in Anil Kumar discriminated against candidates from panel who were unfortunate in not having received letters of appointment? - Fundamental right of equality implies that persons in like situations, under like circumstances, are entitled to be treated alike; reasonable classification according to some principle to recognize intelligible inequalities or to avoid or correct inequalities is permissible - Held, these candidates did acquire some right to so continue and action of State Govt. in protecting their services cannot be said to infringe art. 14, which even though all pervasive, has to be considered in facts and circumstances of each case; appointed and non-appointed teachers formed separate and distinct classes and in saving appointments of those who stood already appointed and were serving there was no arbitrariness whatsoever on part of respondents - (C) Civil Appeal No. 3217 of 1991 - Seniority - Determination of - Held, taking note of concession made by State in other cases, it is a fit case which should be remitted to HC for limited purpose to render a judgment on question of seniority of appellants vis-a-vis those who stand appointed and whose appointments have not been disturbed in view of concession made in other appeals in Supreme Court - Order accordingly.
