Behari Prasad Etc.Etc. v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3663; (1996) SCC (Cr) 271; (1996) 2 SCC 317; 1996 (33) ACC 160; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 469; AIR 1996 SC 2905; 1996 (2) AWC 620; 1996 (3) CCR 173; 1996 (1) Crimes 11; 1996 CRLJ 1653; JT 1996 (1) SC 93; 1996(1) SCALE 162; 1996 (2) SCJ 64; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 262; 1996 (1) Supreme 347

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Close Range

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 172 and 154 - Criminal case - Murder - (A) Detailed account of firing on deceased not given in FIR lodged within half an hour by injured witness - Adverse inference in the light of injury and killing of close relation - (B) Effect of non examination of investigating officer on prosecution case when involvement proved by evidence of eye witnesses and confirmed by medical evidence - Held, no adverse inference should be drawn against prosecution case for the omission - Does not per se vitiate a Criminal trial - Appeal dismissed.
