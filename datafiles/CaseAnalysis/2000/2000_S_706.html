Tamil Nadu P. G. T. Association v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3489; AIR 2002 SC 72; JT 2001 (2) SC 451; 2002 (35) LabIC 100; 2001(1) SCALE 60; 2001 (8) SLR 795; 2001 (1) SLT 657

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Grievance of appellants, being Post-Graduate Teachers(PGT's), having Post - Graduate Degree as qualification, is prescription of a ratio in rules framed under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution, for promotion to post of Headmasters of Higher Secondary Schools in ratio of 2:5:2 - Tribunal by impugned order concluded that there has been no discrimination in providing a quota for inducted teachers to be appointed as Principal of Higher Secondary Schools and dismissed application filed by PGT's - Appeal against order passed by Tribunal? - Held, having regard to period-taken by such inducted teachers to get into promotional avenue of Headmasters of Higher Secondary Schools and having regard to nature and duties of Headmasters of such Higher Secondary Schools, if rule-making authority prescribes a quota ratio of 5:2 between Post-Graduate Teachers of Higher Secondary Schools and inducted teachers of Higher Secondary Schools, same cannot be struck down as arbitrary or irrational - Court finds no infraction of any constitutional provisions in prescribing a quota for inducted teachers in view of documents placed - Appeals dismissed.
