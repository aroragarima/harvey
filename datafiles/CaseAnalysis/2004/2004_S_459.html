State of Andhra Pradesh v C. Uma Maheswara Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1420; (2004) 4 SCC 399; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1276; AIR 2004 SC 2042; 2004 CRLJ 2040; JT 2004 (4) SC 167; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 451; 2004(4) SCALE 106

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 4, 7 and 13 - Respondents faced trial for alleged commission of offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13 of Act, 1988 - Trial court found each to be guilty and convicted u/s. 120B of IPC, 1860 - On appeal, conviction,, were, aside - Present appeal - Proof of fact depends upon degree of probability of its having existed standard required for reaching supposition is that of a prudent man acting in any important matter concerning him - Presumption is an inference of a certain fact drawn from other proved facts - While inferring existence of a fact from another, court is only applying a process of intelligent reasoning which mind of a prudent man would do under similar circumstances - Presumption is not final conclusion to be drawn from other facts - But it could as well be final if it remains undisturbed later - Held, keeping in view legal principles, applying fact situation to them evitable conclusion is that High Court was not justified in directing acquittal - Not only correct legal position was not kept in view but analysis of factual position is also found to be erroneous - Judgment of HC set aside - Appeals allowed.
