State Of W.B. v National Builders (Sahai, J)

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, A.S. Anand, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 708; (1994) 1 SCC 235; AIR 1994 SC 200; 1994 (1) ARBLR 5; 1994 (1) BLJR 205; 1994 (2) CalLT(SC) 39; 1993 (3) CCC 612; JT 1993 (6) SC 144; 1993(4) SCALE 187; 1994 (1) UJ 81

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8(1)(b) - Constitution of India, 1953, art. 227 - (A) Whether refusal of an arbitrator to resign while not accepting joint request of parties to extend time for arbitration and leave it to them to decide their future course of action amounts to refusal to act by arbitrator within meaning of s. 8(1)(b)? - Refusal to act may be express or implied - Held, (A) once court is satisfied that arbitrator has refused to discharge his obligations then it has statutory duty to intervene and act in accordance with s. 8(1)(b) - If an arbitrator resigns or informs parties his inability to act it would be express refusal and even courts cannot force him to arbitrate - (B) Whether power to appoint next arbitrator vests in Court or it has once again to be in accordance with procedure provided in agreement - Further held, Court shall exercise jurisdiction to appoint another arbitrator except where it is specifically debarred from doing so - Appeal dismissed.
