Nityananda M. Joshi & Ors. v Life Insurance Corporation Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 181; (1969) 2 SCC 199; AIR 1970 SC 209; 1970 (20) FLR 153; 1970 LabIC 269; 1969 (2) LLJ 711; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33C(1) and 33C(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137, ss. 4 and 5 - (A) Application u/s. 33C of 1947 Act - (2) Applicability of art.137 of Limitation Act - (B) Provision of s-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 33C of 1947 Act - Relative scopes of - Held, art. 137 does not apply - It is plain from the wording of s-ss. (1) and (2) of s. 33C that the former sub-section deals with cases where money is due to a workman from an employer under a settlement or an award or under the provisions of chap. VA, while the latter with cases where a workman is entitled to receive from the employer any money or any benefit which is capable of being computed in terms of money - Appeal Allowed.
