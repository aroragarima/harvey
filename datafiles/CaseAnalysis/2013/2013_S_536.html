Sucha Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 June 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 881; (2013) 14 SCC 552; JT 2013 (9) SC 284; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 394 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed murder of deceased - Accused was charged for offences punishable u/ss. 302, 394 IPC - Trial court convicted appellant - On appeal before HC, HC confirmed conviction and dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, pursuant to the information furnished by the appellant the Kassi, Khes and Bed-sheet were recovered from the pit under the road pulia. The recovery has also been witnessed by PW3, who has clearly stated in his evidence that the accused got recovered Kassi, one Khes and a bed-sheet. These articles which were recovered were sent to FSL and the results are that the Kassi, Bed-sheet and Khes were stained with large and small blood stains. These recoveries of the aforesaid articles pursuant to the disclosure statement made by the appellant clearly point to the guilt of the appellant and statement of the appellant and the recoveries made pursuant to the statement of the appellant are of no evidentiary value. Appellant submitted that the FIR has not been proved through HC, the policeman who received the FIR. PW1 informant, has been examined and he has stated that he lodged the FIR on 1-2-1997 and PW 11 has stated that on the basis of the information furnished by PW1 he registered the FIR which was written by HC. Hence, FIR has been duly proved. Motive of the appellant to kill the deceased has not been established by the prosecution is misconceived in facts. From the extra judicial confession made by appellant to PW8, it is clear that the motive of the appellant was to take possession of the mule cart and sell the same and make money. Hence, there is no infirmity in the judgment of Trial court and HC. Appeal dismissed.
