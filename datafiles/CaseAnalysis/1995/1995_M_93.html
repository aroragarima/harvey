New India Assurance Co. Ltd. v Mandar Madhav Tambe & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1915; (1996) SCC (Cr) 307; (1996) 2 SCC 328; 1996 ACJ 253; AIR 1996 SC 1150; AIR 1996 SCW 702; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 199; 1996 (1) MahLJ 935; 1996(1) SCALE 400; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 687; 1996 (1) UJ 302

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s.96 (2) (b) (ii), s. 2 (5A); Bombay Motor Vehicles Rules (1959), r.16 - Driving licence - Insurance company liable only when vehicle in question was driven by person holding valid driving licence - - Whether Insurance company liable for paying compensation when vehicle driven by person holding learner's licence ? - Held, not liable - It cannot be regarded as duly licenced - Appeal allowed.
