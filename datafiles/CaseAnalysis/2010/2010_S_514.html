Jasbir Singh Chhabra and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 156; (2010) 4 SCC 192; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 682; JT 2010 (2) SC 637; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1688; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 607; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 474; 2010(2) SCALE 754

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995 - Allotment of residential plots - State Government acquired some land and handed over the same to the Corporation for developing infrastructure necessary for industrialization of different areas of the State - Land placed at the disposal of the Corporation was meant to be used for industrial purposes - Corporation allotted land to those interested in setting up industrial units - In December 2001, the State Government approved the proposal of the Corporation for earmarking 20-30% of the land for Industrial Housing - However, Plan Approval Committee of the Corporation had, while approving layouts of residential pockets in Phases VIII-A and VIII-B made it subject to approval of change of land use under the Act - Advertisement was issued inviting applications for residential plots in Phases VIII-A and VIII-B, focal point, Mohali under the Industrial Housing Scheme - Writ petitioners were declared successful in the draw - Meanwhile, allotment Committee unequivocally opposed the idea of allotment of land for housing purposes - Issue was then considered by the State Government and an unequivocal decision was taken not to allow change of land use from industrial to residential - Therefore, allotment letters were issued only to those who had applied for plots in focal point - A batch of writ petitions was filed - Writ petitioners invoked the doctrines of promissory estoppel and legitimate expectation and pleaded that on being declared successful in the draw of lots, they have acquired a right to be allotted plots in focal point - DB of HC held the decision contained in memo whereby it decided not to be desirable to change the land use from industrial to residential was vitiated due to malafides - HC also refused to invoke the doctrine of promissory estoppel in favour of writ petitioners - Hence, present appeals - (A) Whether writ petitioners entitled to allotment of residential plots in Phases VIII-A and VIII-B, Mohali, Punjab developed by Corporation? - Held, decision taken by Plan Approval Committee of the Corporation to approve the layouts of residential pockets in Phases VIII-A and VIII-B, Mohali was subject to sanction of change of land use in accordance with the provisions of the Act - Writ petitioners were very much aware of the tentative character of the initial advertisement as also the advertisement issued for holding draw of lots - HC rightly refused to invoke the doctrine of promissory estoppel in favour of the writ petitioners - Further, writ petitioners cannot claim that they had a legitimate expectation in the matter of allotment of plots despite the fact that change of land use was yet to be sanctioned - (B) Whether the State Government's refusal to sanction change of land use from industrial to residential was vitiated by arbitrariness and malafides? - Held, while exercising power of judicial review, the superior courts should not readily accept the charge of malus animus laid against the State and its functionaries - Burden to prove the charge of malafides is always on the person who moves the Court for invalidation of the action of the State and/or its agencies and instrumentalities - However, in the absence of any tangible or substantive evidence to show that the State Government had taken a conscious decision to allot the surplus land in Phases VIII-A and VIII-B, Mohali for any purpose other than industrial, the DB of the HC was not at all justified in recording a finding that the decision contained in memo was vitiated due to malafides - Further, in view of ss. 79 and 81 of the Act, State Government was well within its power to take appropriate decision on the proposal made by the Corporation to change the land use from industrial to residential and no fault with its decision not to sanction such change - Appeals disposed of.
