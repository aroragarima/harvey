Vishwa Mitter v O. P. Poddar And Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 150; (1983) 4 SCC 701; (1984) SCC (Cr) 29; AIR 1984 SC 5; 1983 ARBLR 394; 1983 BBCJ 164; 1984 (2) Crimes 834; 1984 CRLJ 1; 1983 CRLR 621; 1983 CrLR(SC) 621; 1984 (4) PTC 30; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, ss.78, 79 - CrPC, 1973, ss.4(2), 190 - Appeal against dismissal of complaint by a dealer and constituted attorney of owners of registered trade mark in matter of infringement of Trade Mark - Complaint was dismissed on the ground that complainant has no cause of action to file the complaint (as he was not owner of impugned trade mark) - Legality - Held, illegal - To say that the owner of the registered trade mark can alone file the complaint is contrary to t he provisions of the statute and commonsense and reason - Appeal allowed.
