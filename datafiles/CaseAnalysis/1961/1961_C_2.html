C.Mohammed Yunus v Syed Unissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 393; AIR 1961 SC 808; 1962 (1) MLJ 162; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 162; 1962 (1) SCJ 449; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Family & Personal - Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937, s. 2 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 120 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 42 - (A)Declaration of right and consequential relief - Injunction - Held, a suit for declaration with a consequential relief for injunction is not a suit for declaration simpliciter, it is a suit for declaration with further relief - A suit for a declaration of a right and an injunction restraining defendants from interfering with exercise of that right is governed by art. 120 of Limitation Act - Under article there can be no right to sue until there is an accrual of right asserted in suit and its infringement or at least a clear and unequivocal threat to infringe that right - Right to sue not affected if defendants have not denied rights - (B) Retrospectivity of Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937 - Further held, though provision affected vested rights of parties intention of legislature was clear and Act applied to all suits and proceedings pending even in appeals on date when Act was brought into operation - Appeal dismissed.
