Himachal Pradesh Financial Corporation v Anil Garg and others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Navin Sinha, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 2017 SC 1953; 2017 (123) ALR 274; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 324; 2017 (4) CTC 795; 2017(4) SCALE 95B; 2017 (2) WLN(SC) 76

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Abandonment, State Financial Corporations Act, 1951, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Civil Procedure, Himachal Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1973, H.P. Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 2000

Summary: Banking & Finance - Himachal Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act, 1973, s. 85 - Withdrawal of suit - Abandonment of claim - Appellant filed money suit for recovery but later withdraw the same as it wanted to proceed under the Act and then recovery certificate was issued under the Act on 19-4-1996 - Respondent filed suit thwarting Certificate proceedings but the same was ultimately dismissed on 21-5-2001 - While so, in respect of another loan obtained by respondent, Recovery certificate was issued on 3-9-1994 - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging both the Recovery proceedings and the petition was allowed on technical grounds with liberty to appellant to proceed afresh in accordance with law - Later, fresh show cause notices and recovery certificates were issued in respect of two loans - Respondent again filed writ petition before HC wherein HC passed impugned orders inter alia holding that suit having been withdrawn unconditionally and without any liberty granted under CPC for initiating appropriate legal proceedings, amounted to abandonment of claim and had set aside auction notice issued by appellant u/s. 85 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether withdrawal of suit amounted to abandonment of claim by appellant.

Held, proceedings in suit were essentially different from proceedings under the Act. The withdrawal of the Suit was no bar to proceedings under the Act and there was no bar under the Act to the proceedings. Further, there was no abandonment of claim by appellant and it would be contrary to public policy to prevent appellant from recovering the loan. The recovery proceedings were not time barred and HC factually erred in holding that trunk loan was time barred as appellant took no steps for recovery of dues from 1996 till 2002 overlooking Certificate dt.3-9-1994. Hence, order of HC is not sustainable and the same is set aside. Further, auction notice dt.13-1-2005/15-1-2005 u/s.85 of the Act is to be proceeded in accordance with law and concluded at the earliest. Appeal allowed.
