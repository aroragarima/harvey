Pradeep Naikanth Paturkar v S. Ramamurthi and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 139; (1993) Supp2 SCC 61; (1993) SCC (Cr) 392; AIR 1994 SC 656; AIR 1993 SCW 4066; 1994 CRLJ 620; JT 1992 (3) SC 261

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers and Drug Offenders Act, 1981, s. 3(1) - Govt. Order Home Department (Special) No. DDS/1391/1/SPL-3 (B) D/-26-07-1981 - Impugned detention order issued by Commr. of Police, Greater Bombay on basis of some criminal cases registered against detenu and also on basis of statements of witnesses - Order was in question as it was passed after 5 months and 8 days from registration of last case and more than 4 months from submission of proposal, moreover statements of witnesses referred to in grounds of detention was obtained after detenu was released on bail in all cases - Whether delay in passing detention order has vitiated said order? - Held, in instant case, taking into consideration of unexplained delay whether short or long especially when appellant has taken a specific plea of delay - Court was constrained to quash detention order - Appeal allowed.
