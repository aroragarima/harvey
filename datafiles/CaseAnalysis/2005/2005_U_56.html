Union of India and Others v Ashok Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 615; (2005) 8 SCC 760; AIR 2006 SC 124; 2005 (6) AWC 5831; JT 2005 (12) SC 515; 2005(8) SCALE 397; 2005 (6) SLR 832; 2006 (8) SLR 299; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 317; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 528

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Non-application Of Mind, Deputy Inspector General, Inspector General

Summary: Border Security Force Act, 1968, s. 10; Border Security Force Rules, 1969, r. 20 - Procedure to be followed to terminate services of an officer on account of misconduct - Director General recorded his satisfaction that it was neither expedient nor practicable to conduct the trial - High Court held that removal of delinquent officer from service was in violation of provisions of the Act and Rules - Appeal against - Held, both Director-General and Central Government can act in different situations and consideration by Director- General is not ruled out - High Court's conclusions that Central Government is the only authority to consider the matter whether holding of trial is inexpedient or impracticable is clearly indefensible - Appeals disposed of.
