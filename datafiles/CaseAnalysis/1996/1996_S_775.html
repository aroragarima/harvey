Dr. J. S. Chhabra and another v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1994; (1997) 3 SCC 203; (1997) SCC (L&S) 713; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 279; 1997 (1) ESC 532; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 11; 1996(8) SCALE 648; 1997 (1) SLJ 167; 1997 (1) SLR 132; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 313; 1996 (8) Supreme 562; 1997 (1) UJ 432

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Regularisation of Ad Hoc Appointment Rules, 1986 - Principle of continuous officiation - Error of law - Direction contrary to statutory rules - Respondent was appointed as Demonstrator in Surgery - However vide Govt. order, he was appointed along with some other doctors to officiate temporarily as Casualty Medical Officer (CMO) - Respondent made representation to Govt. to be absorbed in post of lecturer and for his seniority to be counted - Representations were ignored, thus respondent filed a petition in the Madhya Pradesh HC - During pendency of petition services of seven Ad-hoc CMOs (Lecturer Grade), including respondent, were regularised under the Rules, and he was appointed on same post of temporary basis - Govt. passed order that declared him lecturer in Surgery - Accordingly, HC dismissed petition as infructuous - However, respondent's seniority as lecturer and name was not included in gradation list of lecturers published - Respondent filed W.P. in HC - Petition was transferred to Tribunal - Appellant intervened in application as respondent challenged recognition of appellant and others as lecturers - Tribunal found that Govt. was not entitled to change position to prejudice of CMOs - Tribunal held that after regularising their services and giving them the status of full-fledged lecturers it was improper and unfair on the part of the Govt. not to treat them as lecturer - Principle of continuous officiation was invoked, it further held that CMOs became entitled to seniority from date of their continuous officiation and accordingly directed authorities - Tribunal order was challenged - Appellant challenged order of tribunal on ground that as recruitment to post of lecturer was by direct selection through Public Service Commission, it was not open to State Govt. to declare CMOs (Lecturer Grade) as lecturers and thus act in contravention of relevant recruitment rules - Hence, instant petition - Whether tribunal had committed a grave error of law that required reconsideration - Held, appellant had contended that post of lecturer was lowest post in set up of teaching staff of medical colleges in State of M.P. and that posts of CMOs were not part of cadre of lecturers - However, required qualification for both posts (lecturers and CMO's) were same, as CMOs had teaching experience it was open to Govt. to redesignate posts or merge them into cadre of lecturers - As appellant was not able to point out how it was beyond power of Govt. to declare CMOs as lecturers, contention raised on was rejected - Respondent had accepted regularisation under the Rules - Neither at time of regularisation of his services nor at any subsequent time had he challenged validity of the Rules - Respondent was not given seniority as CMO (Lecturer Grade) earlier than order of his regular appointment - Tribunal wrongly directed that respondent's seniority as lecturer was to be counted from date he had started working as CMO (Lecturer Grade) - Tribunal's direction was contrary to statutory rules and for that reason no benefit on basis of principle of continuous officiation was applicable to him - No order as to costs - Appeal allowed.
