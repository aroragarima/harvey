ACG Associated Capsules Private Limited (Formerly Associated Capsules Private Limited) and another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central-IV, Mumbai and another

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, S.H. Kapadia, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 33; (2012) 3 SCC 321; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 96; JT 2012 (2) SC 362

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Memorandum to the Finance (No.2) Bill 1991

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28(iiid) and 80HHC - A/y 2003-2004 - Deduction - Assessment - Entitlement - Sustainability - AO passed assessment order deducting 90% of gross interest and gross rent received from profits of business while computing deduction u/s. 80HHC against return filed of income by assessee claiming deduction u/s. 80HHC of Act - Assessee appeal before Commissioner of Income-Tax (Appeals) which was confirmed, assessee filed appeal before Tribunal - Tribunal remanded issue of netting of rent to AO with direction to find out whether the assessee has paid rent on same flats against which rent has been received from staff and if such rent was paid then such rent is to be reduced from rental income for purpose of exclusion of business income for computing deduction under Section 80HHC - Revenue preferred appeal in HC, HC directed that on remand AO will decide issue in accordance with judgment of HC wherein while determining profits of business, ninety per cent of gross receipts towards interest and not ninety per cent of net receipts towards interest on fixed deposits in banks received by assessee would be excluded for purpose of working out deduction u/s. 80HHC of Act - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that it will be clear that ninety per cent of any receipts by way of brokerage, commission, interest, rent, charges or any other receipt of a similar nature included in such profits will be excluded for determining profits and gains of business or profession - Revenue contended that HC rightly held that ninety per cent of gross amount received towards interest and rent have to be excluded from profits and gains of business for computing profits of business as defined in Explanation (baa) to Section 80HHC of the Act - Whether HC was justified in passing orders - Held, rent, commission, interest and brokerage do not possess any nexus with export turnover and, therefore, inclusion of such items in profits of business would result in a distortion of figure of export profits - Appeal allowed. Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28(iiid) and 80HHC - A/y 2003-2004 - Deduction - Assessment - Entitlement - Sustainability - AO held that 90% of gross interest has to be excluded from profits of business of assessee as per s. 80HHC of Act and deducted 90% of gross interest - Commissioner of IT(Appeals) dismissed appeal, but ITAT allowed appeal of assesse - Revenue preferred appeal in HC, HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in sustaining order of Tribunal - Held, ninety per cent of not gross interest but only net interest, which has been included in profits of business of assessee as computed under heads 'Profits and Gains of Business or Profession' is to be deducted under cl (1) of Explanation (baa) to Section 80HHC for determining profits of the business - Appeal dismissed.
