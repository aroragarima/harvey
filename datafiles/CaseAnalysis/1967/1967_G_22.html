Mohan Lal and Another v Grain Chamber Limited, Muzaffarnagar and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1968 SC 772; [1968] 38 Comp Cas 543; 1968 (1) CompLJ 275; 1968 (2) ILR(All) 1; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 252

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Substratum, Sugar (Futures and Options) Prohibition Order 1949

Summary: Corporate - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Companies Act, 1913 - Sugar (Futures and Options) Prohibition Order, 1949 - Is respondent bound to be wound up? - Company was carrying on extensive business in 'futures' in gut, but Company was formed not with object of carrying on business in 'futures' in gut alone, but in several other commodities as well - There is no evidence that Company was unable to pay its debts - Held, in present case object for which Company was incorporated has not substantially failed, and it cannot be said that Company could not carry on its 'business except at a loss, nor that its assets were insufficient to meet its liabilities - There were no creditors to whom debts were payable by Company - Appeals dismissed.
