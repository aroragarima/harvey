Mohd. Hashim v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 976; (2017) 2 SCC 198; AIR 2017 SC 660; 2017 ALL MR (Cri) 1338; 2017 CRLJ 1454; 2017 (1) JCC 537; JT 2017 (2) SC 200; 2017 (1) KLT 104; 2017 (1) OLR 241; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 152; 2016(12) SCALE 636; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 953

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, Age, Imprisonment For Life, Minimum Sentence, Dealing, Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, Character, Defence of India Act, 1962, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Corruption, Probation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 498A - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 3, 4, 6 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Dowry harassment - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent nos. 2 to 10/accused were prosecuted for the offences punishable u/ss. 498A and 323 IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the 1961 Act - Trial Court convicted respondent nos. 2 and 3 u/s. 498A IPC and sentenced to undergo RI for 2 years further, respondent nos. 4 to 10 were convicted for the offence punishable u/s. 498A IPC and sentenced to undergo simple imprisonment of 6 months - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 323 IPC and s. 4 of the 1961 Act and sentenced to undergo RI for 6 months - On appeal, Appellate Court dismissed appeal and extended the benefit u/s. 4 of the 1958 Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed revision before HC - HC dismissed revision and upheld order of Appellate Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether approach of Appellate Court which was concurred by HC was legally sustainable.

Held, in Dalbir Singh v. State of Haryana and others, 2000 Indlaw SC 255, it was held that Parliament has made it clear that only if Court forms the opinion that it was expedient to release the convict on probation for the good conduct regard being had to the circumstances of the case and one of the circumstances which cannot be sidelined in forming the said opinion was "the nature of the offence". Court has further opined that though the discretion was vested in Court to decide when and how Court should form such opinion, yet the provision itself provides sufficient indication that releasing the convicted person on probation of good conduct should appear to Court to be expedient. Court before exercising the power u/s. 4 of the 1958 Act has to keep in view the nature of offence and conditions incorporated u/s. 4 of the 1958 Act. SC highlighted those aspects for the guidance of Appellate Court as it has exercised the jurisdiction in a perfunctory manner and HC should have been well advised to rectify the error. There was no minimum sentence and provisions of the 1958 Act would apply. SC also opined that Court has to be guided by the provisions of the 1958 Act and the precedents of SC. It would be open for parties to raise all points before Appellate Court on merits including seeking release under the 1958 Act. Impugned judgment and order passed by HC and Appellate Court were set aside and the matter was remitted to Appellate Court for disposal in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.
