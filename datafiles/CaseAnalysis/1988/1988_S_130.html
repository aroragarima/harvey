Continental Construction Company Limited v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 498; (1988) 3 SCC 82; AIR 1988 SC 1166; 1988 (1) ARBLR 400; 1988(1) SCALE 721; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 103; 1988 (1) UJ 741

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Contract Act, 1872 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss.30, 33 - Setting aside of Award - Claim for extra cost incurred by contractor on non-completion of contract within time - Objection as to - (A) Misconduct on part of arbitrator - Cost awarded, without considering objection by State Government - Legality - (B) Question about specific reference on a question of law - District Judge framing specific issues and referring to arbitrator, who passed non-speaking award - And District Judge set aside it after looking into the terms of contract - Legality - Held, illegal - Now illegal, even though contract was not forming part of award or appended to it - Petition dismissed.

.
