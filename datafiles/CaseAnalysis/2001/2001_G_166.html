Kulwant Kaur and Others v Gurdial Singh Mann (Dead) by LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20224; (2001) 4 SCC 262; AIR 2001 SC 1273; JT 2001 (4) SC 158; 2001 (128) PLR 492; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 277B; 2001(2) SCALE 634; 2001 (2) SCJ 429; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 525; 2001 (3) SLT 47; 2001 (2) Supreme 556

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 4, 100, 101 and 103 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1976, s. 97 - Punjab Courts Act, 1918, s. 41 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Plaintiffs suit for partition and rendition of accounts decreed by lower appellate court but in second appeal High Court allowed appeal and set aside decree of lower appellate court - Perspective of s. 4(1) having regard to s. 97 of CPC (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Whether HC can, in absence of a substantial question of law interfere with findings of lower appellate court? - Held, neither savings clause in s. 100(1) or s. 4 of CPC, 1908, can come to rescue of respondents in view of s. 97(1) of Amendment Act because of clarification rendered by legislature in s. 101, CPC, 1908, which provides that no second appeal shall lie except on ground mentioned in s. 100, that substantial question of law - Appeal allowed.
