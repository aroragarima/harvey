Electrical Manufacturing Company Limited v D. D. Bhargava

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1968 SC 247; 1968 CRLJ 236; 1967 (3) DLT 699; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Trade

Keywords: Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Trade - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947, s. 6 - Whether complaint filed by Deputy Chief Controller of Imports and Exports is valid? - S. 6 only insists that complaint is to be in writing and that it must be made by an officer, authorised in that behalf - Complaint, in this case, has been made by respondent in writing, and that he is an authorised officer, in this behalf, has not been challenged - Limitation contained in s. 6 is only regarding particular officer who could file a complaint and, when once he satisfies those requirements, bar is removed to taking of cognizance by a court, on a complaint, made in accordance with s. 6 - Complaint in this case, filed by respondent, can be considered to be in conformity with provisions, contained therein - Held, complaint, filed by respondent, on 31-12-1962, satisfies requirements of s. 6 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
