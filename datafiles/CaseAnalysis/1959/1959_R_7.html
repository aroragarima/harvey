Rai Bahadur Seth Teomal v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1959 SC 742; [1959] 36 ITR 9; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 63; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Place of assessment - Appellant's case before an Income-tax Officer (ITO) within area in charge of the Commissioner, Bengal (Mofissil) was withdrawn from him and was assigned to Commissioner, Calcutta - ITO issued notice - Tribunal ordered against appellant that objection as to place of assessment by ITO could not be challenged in appeal - Hence, the present appeal - Whether objection as to place of assessment by ITO could be challenged in appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner and then before Appellate Tribunal - Held, that no Scheme of the Act showed that no appeal in regard to objection to place of assessment contemplated under the Act - Question as to place of assessment when it arose determined by Commissioner - Further, any such order could not be made a ground of appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner u/s. 30 of the Act which provided for appeals against orders of assessment and other orders enumerated u/s. 30 of the Act but no appeals provided against orders made u/s. 64(3) of the Act - Question as to place of assessment did not arise out of order of Tribunal and therefore no question of law could be referred nor could HC make such order under s. 66(2) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
