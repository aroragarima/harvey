Harish Chandra Bajpai v Triloki Singh

Supreme Court of India

21 December 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1957 SC 444; 1957 SCJ 297; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 370

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Civil Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 8 and 83 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. VI, r. 17 - Amendment of petition - Held, Election Tribunal has power to allow particulars in respect of illegal or corrupt practices to be amended - Further held, tribunal has power to order amendment of a petition, but cannot permit new grounds or charges to be raised so as to alter its character as to make it in substance a new petition - Appeal allowed.
