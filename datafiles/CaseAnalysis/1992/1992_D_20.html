Patel Naranbhai Marghabhai And Ors. v Deceased Dhulabhai Galbabhai And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 476; (1992) 4 SCC 264; AIR 1992 SC 2009; AIR 1992 SCW 237; JT 1992 (4) SC 381; 1992(1) SCALE 1304; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 384; 1992 (2) UJ 119

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1947, ss. 32(2)(iv), 38(3) - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 2, 3 and 27 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - (A) Evidence - Withholding best available evidence by a party - Effect - Mortgagee in possession of mortgaged property - Plea, he purchased property in auction sale and became owner - Best evidence namely notification to conduct sale, sale proceeding, certificate of sale not placed on record - (B) Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act - (1) Award for delivery of possession of mortgaged property - Execution of - No limitation prescribed - Execution application - Not a 'sui' - Provision of s. 27, Limitation Act not attracted - Absence of proof of adverse possession by mortgagee - (2) Execution Court - Power to grant mesne profits - It is concomitant of its power to pass order of delivery of possession of mortgaged property to mortgagor - Mortgagee becomes liable to pay mesne profit from date of discharge of debt by mortgagor - Held, gives rise to adverse inference, his possession must be deemed to be as mortgagee and not as owner - Mortgagor entitled to possession - Mesne profits for 3 years preceding date of filing application awarded - Appeal dismissed.
