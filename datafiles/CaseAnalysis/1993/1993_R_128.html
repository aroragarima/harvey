Major Genl. B.M. Bhatracharjee (Retd.) Andanother v Russel Estate Corporation And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 156; (1993) 2 SCC 533; AIR 1993 SC 1632; 1993 (1) CCC 595; 1993 CRLJ 2251; JT 1993 (2) SC 109; 1993 (2) MLJ 1; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1993 (1) RentLR 339; 1993(1) SCALE 444; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2 and 10 - Meaning of word 'allot' was in question - Disobedience of Court's interim order directing a party not to allot flats to anybody - Delivery of possession and execution of sale deed in favour of third parties pending interim order would amount to contempt or not - Held, word 'allot' would mean handing over of flats - Delivery of possession and execution of sale deed in favour of third parties pending interim order would amount to contempt notwithstanding that agreements of sale were already executed.
