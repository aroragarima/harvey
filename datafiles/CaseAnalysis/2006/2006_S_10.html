S. Narayana v Md. Ahmedulla Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 793; (2006) 10 SCC 84; AIR 2006 SC 2224; 2006(5) SCALE 448; 2006 (6) SCJ 537; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Regularization - Service benefits - Legality - Instant appeal was filed by appellant for benefit of service - Held, it is contended that regularization is same as confirmation in service, and therefore, lien would operate from said date. SC are afraid that SC cannot accept this contention in face of clear authority to contrary, to which SC have already referred. Counsel for respondent no. 1 also contended that any relief given, to appellant should not affect any benefit of service, emoluments, allowance and pension etc., available to respondent no. 1. SC did not see how this apprehension can arise. Order of Tribunal does not in any way show that respondent's no. 1 service benefits are affected. It ensures is that justice is rendered to appellant. Counsel for State submitted that State would submit to any orders passed by this Court. SC also notice that State Govt. has not filed any counter-affidavit in opposition to appeal. In the circumstances, SC find merit in contentions urged on behalf of appellant. SC are also of view that HC erred in interfering with order of Tribunal. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
