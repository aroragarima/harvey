Supreme Court of India

12 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, U.C. Banerjee, S.N. Variava, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21373; AIR 2001 SC 453; 2002 (139) E.L.T. 3; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1354; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 403; JT 2001 (10) SC 456; 2001(8) SCALE 479; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 607

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss.3 and 37 B - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1986, Tariff item 26 AA - Interpretation - 'On which appropriate amount of duty of excise has already been paid' - Held, correct interpretation of said phrase has not been placed in SC judgment Usha Martin Industries v. CCE 1997 INDLAW SC 2986 - Exemption notification that uses said phrase applies to goods which have been made from duty paid material - For purposes of getting benefit of exemption under notification, goods must be made from raw material on which excise duty has been paid, and has been paid at the 'appropriate' or correct rate - Unless manufacturer has paid the correct amount of excise duty, he is not entitled to the benefit of exemption notification - Appeals disposed of.
