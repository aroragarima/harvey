Bimlesh and others v New India Assurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 571; (2010) 8 SCC 591; AIR 2010 SC 2591; 2010 (6) AWC 5942; JT 2010 (8) SC 94; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 1067; 2010 (4) MPLJ 588; 2010(7) SCALE 732; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 605; 2010 (3) WLN(SC) 75

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 163-A - Maintainability of claim petition against insurance company - Deceased, owner of the vehicle, died while driving his vehicle - Vehicles was insured with Insurance company - Claimant (wife and children of deceased) filed claim petition u/s. 163-A of the Act - Insurance Company contended that the claim petition was not competent because the deceased was not a third party being the driver and owner of the vehicle and under the Act and terms of the policy of the insurance, the Insurance Company is only required to indemnify the owner in case of third party loss - Claims Tribunal held that the claim petition was maintainable - On appeal, HC held that claim petition was not maintainable against the Insurance Company - Hence, present appeal - Held, Claims Tribunal is required to dispose of all issues one way or the other in one go while deciding the claim application - Objection raised by the Insurance Company about maintainability of claim petition is intricately connected with its liability which in the facts and circumstances of the case is dependent on determination of the effect of the additional premium paid by the insured to cover the risk of the driver and other terms of the policy including terms of the policy - Matter needs to be sent back to the Claims Tribunal - Order passed by the HC as well as the order passed by the Claims Tribunal set aside - Claim petition to be decided by the Claims Tribunal - Appeal partly allowed.
