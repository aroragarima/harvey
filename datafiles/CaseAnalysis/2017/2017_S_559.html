State of U. P. and another v Sunil

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Rohinton Fali Nariman

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 353; AIR 2017 SC 2150; JT 2017 (5) SC 4; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 267; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 925; 2017 (2) RLW 1430; 2017(5) SCALE 489

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Weapon, Circumstantial Evidence, Corroboration, Chain, Inference, Adverse Inference, Discovery, Admissible, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 429 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent/accused allegedly committed murder of complainant's parents/deceased - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 302, 429 r/w s. 34 IPC and awarded death sentence - On appeal, HC allowed appeal, set aside the order of conviction and sentence passed by Trial Court and acquitted accused - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, in a case where there was no direct witness to prove the prosecution case, conviction of accused could be made on the basis of circumstantial evidence provided the chain of circumstances was complete beyond all reasonable doubt. However, basic foundation of prosecution had crumbled down in instant case by not connecting respondent with the incident in question. Further, when basic foundation in criminal cases was so collapsed, circumstantial evidence becomes inconsequential. In such circumstances, it was difficult for Court to hold that a judgment of conviction could be founded on sole circumstance that recovery of weapon and other articles were made. Therefore, prosecution has failed to connect the occurrence with respondent. Impugned judgment and order passed by HC setting aside of conviction order passed by the Trial Court upheld. Appeals dismissed.
