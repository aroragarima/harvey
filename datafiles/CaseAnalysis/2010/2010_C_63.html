Chunni Lal v State Of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 451; (2010) 7 SCC 496; 2010 (6) ADJ 371; AIR 2010 SC 2467; 2010 CRLJ 3836; JT 2010 (6) SC 400; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 680; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 480; 2010(6) SCALE 204; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 410; 2010 (2) UC 1190

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant convicted u/s. 302 of IPC - Conviction upheld by HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Evidence of interested witnesses - PWs 1 and 2 were the sons of the deceased - Held, having considered the evidence of PWs 1 and 2 who were the eye-witnesses to the occurrence there was no doubt that they were present at the place of occurrence in a usual and natural manner when the incident had taken place and they had actually seen the occurrence - Both PWs 1 and 2 have given a vivid account of the incident and the manner in which the incident occurred - Further, ocular evidence, fully corroborates the medical evidence - In that view of the matter it cannot be said that the evidence of PWs 1 and 2 should be discarded as they are interested witnesses particularly when their evidence adduced could not be shaken by the defence in the cross-examination - (B) Delay in lodging the FIR - Held, there was well reasoned and proper explanation for the delay both in the lodging of the FIR as also in starting of the investigation by the Investigating Officer - Delay which was caused due to reasonable factual situation cannot destroy the prosecution case nor creates any suspicion with regard to the prosecution case - Also fact of the appellant absconding immediately after the occurrence indicate that the appellant was guilty of the offence alleged against him - Appellant rightly convicted of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
