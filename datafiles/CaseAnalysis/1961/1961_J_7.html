Bhagat Singh and Others v Jaswant Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 515; AIR 1966 SC 1861; 1961 (1) KerLR 539; 1962 (1) SCJ 162

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Adoption - Punjab Customs (Power to Contest) Act, 1920 - Plaintiff-respondent alleged that plots were owned and possessed by K who had adopted respondent about two years before his death - Defendants, it is alleged, got mutation made over land in suit collusively with revenue officials and secured possession over them - Defendants accepted allegation that K owned and possessed land in suit and further alleged K deceased never adopted plaintiff as his son; nor is this admitted that plaintiff was sister's son of K; nor yet was he ever taken in lap in presence of Baradri; nor were any ceremonies of adoption performed; nor could plaintiff according to Riwaji-am of District Jullundur or Riwaj-am of Punjab State, be adopted as son - Whether adoption is valid? - Held, it is true that evidence was led by the parties both about the custom and about the ancestral nature of the land in suit - But, in view of the absence of any specific issue about the custom, it is possible that adequate evidence bearing on the question might not have been led - Case more to point is Privy Council case relied on by courts below, viz, Siddik Mahomed Shah v. Mt. Saran (1953 Indlaw SC 6), where it was held, that, where a claim has been never made in defence presented no amount of evidence can be looked into upon a plea which was never put forward - Appeal dismissed.
