Malabar Industrial Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 727; (2000) 2 SCC 718; AIR 2000 SC 1855; 2000 (159) CTR 1; [2000] 243 ITR 83; JT 2000 (2) SC 15; 2000 (2) KLT 38; 2000(1) SCALE 495; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 744; [2000] 109 TAXMAN 66

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 263(1) - A/y 1983-84 - Appellant entered into an agreement for sale of the estate of rubber plantation for consideration of Rs. 210 Lakhs with 'X' - Said agreement provided inter alia, for payment of the consideration in instalments - However, 'X' could not adhere to the schedule and on his request the parties agreed to extension of time for payment of the instalments on condition of his paying compensation/damages for loss of agricultural income and other liabilities in a sum of Rs. 3,66,649/- - Assessee mentioned that income as compensation and damages for loss of agricultural income - Income Tax Officer (ITO) accepted the same and endorsed nil assessment for that year - On appeal, Commissioner held that the nil assessment order passed by the ITO was erroneous and it was prejudicial to the interests of the Revenue - Tribunal dismissed appeal filed against said order - HC upheld the order of the tribunal - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether tribunal was justified in holding that there was evidence before the CIT that the assessment order was erroneous and prejudicial to Revenue? - Held, every loss of revenue as a consequence of an order of AO cannot be treated as prejudicial to the interests of the Revenue - In the present case, Commissioner noted that the ITO passed the order of nil assessment without application of mind - It appears that the resolution passed by the board of the appellant-company was not placed before the AO - Thus, there was no material to support the claim of the appellant that the said amount represented compensation for loss of agricultural income - Hence, conclusion that the order of the ITO was erroneous is irresistible - (B) Whether tribunal was justified in holding that Rs. 3,66,649 was a taxable receipt for the a/y 1983-84? - Held, said amount was paid in modification/relaxation of the terms of the contract, hence, said amount was a taxable receipt under the head income from other services - Appeal dismissed.
