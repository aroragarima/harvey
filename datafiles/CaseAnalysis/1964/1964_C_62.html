Commissioner of Income Tax, Patna v Rani Bhuwaneshwari Kuer

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 74; AIR 1965 SC 6; [1964] 53 ITR 195; 1964 (2) SCJ 361; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 920

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.16(1) (c) - Liquidation of debts - Exemption - Settlement - Power of revocation - Estate found by assessee was heavily encumbered and with a view to arrange for liquidation of debts assesses executed an indenture of trust whereby 'R' and certain zamindari properties owned by the assesses were conveyed to certain named trustees to be field in trust, subject to conditions - Principal beneficiaries under the deed after payment of debts were assesses, her husband and her five sons - Income Tax Officer rejected the contention raised by assessee that income under trust was taxable in hands of trustees under deed of settlement and applying provision of s.16(1)(c) of 1922 Act, brought the income of the trust to tax as part of assessee's income - Whether income received by beneficiaries deemed to be income of assessee - Held, HC was right in holding that by virtue of third proviso to s. 16(1)(c) of 1922 Act, income received by beneficiaries under the deed of trust other than assessee could not until power of revocation arose to assessee, be deemed to be income of assessee for purpose of assessment to Income Tax - Appeal dismissed.
