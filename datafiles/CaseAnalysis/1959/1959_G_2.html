Radha Prasad Singh v Gajadhar Singh & Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1960 SC 115; 1960 (1) AnWR 33; 1960 (1) MLJ(SC) 33; 1960 MPLJ 1; 1960 SCJ 73; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Sale deed - Forgery - Partial pre-emption - Performance of ceremonies - Right of pre-emption - Plaintiff's alleged 4 annas 5 gandas odd share of 'M' was sold was not correct and 8 annas odd share, entire interest of vendor in property was sold by deed - After registration of sale deed was tampered and by act of forgery 8 annas odd share was altered fraudulently to 4 annas 5 gandas was without substance examined evidence as - Plaintiff brought suit for pre- emption in respect of 5 items of property certain other properties were sold by Defendant - Trial Court detained that plaintiff had failed to prove - Hence, instant appeal - Whether share of vendor was sold and ceremonies essential for exercising right of pre-emption were properly performed - Held, plaintiff failed to prove that information of sale was conveyed to him - HC assumed that plaintiff had failed to prove words of second demand of Talabs were sufficient to draw attention of witnesses to specific properties in respect of right of pre-emption - Pre-emption in respect of 8 annas odd share of 'M' instead of 4 annas odd share as originally claimed to fail because there was no prayer for pre-emption - Suit was rightly dismissed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
