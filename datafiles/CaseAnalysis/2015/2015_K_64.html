Khumbha Ram v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 714; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4533; 2016 CRLJ 181; JT 2015 (9) SC 377; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 9; 2015(11) SCALE 581; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 888

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Leave To Appeal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 304B, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry death - Acquittal - Refusal to grant leave - Charge-sheet was filed against accused persons u/ss.302, 304B and 498A of IPC, but, after perusal of evidence, Trial Court held that prosecution failed to prove that accused persons harassed deceased in connection with demand of dowry prior to her death and that there was no medical evidence as to how deceased died and acquitted all accused - Aggrieved, State filed leave to appeal and appellant, who was father of deceased, filed criminal revision petition before HC but both appeal and revision were dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned order of HC is sustainable.

Held, even though deceased died within seven years of marriage in unnatural circumstances, still, Trial Court had not examined the evidence adduced by prosecution in light of statutory presumption to be raised u/s. 113B of the Act. In such circumstances, HC ought to have granted leave to appeal and thereafter re-appreciated evidence and recorded its findings independently as regards guilt or otherwise of the accused. Further, FSL report dt.16-4-2009 was received after disposal of case by Trial Court which showed positive report for presence of organo phosphorous insecticide in the viscera. Hence, HC should have considered FSL report in proper perspective and as First Appellate Court, should have independently examined matter and recorded its findings objectively. Furthermore, HC has not given any reason for refusing to grant leave to file appeal against acquittal. Hence, impugned order is set aside and matter is remitted to HC and even though State had not filed appeal, yet, as impugned order is common order, leave to appeal is granted to State. Appeal filed by State and criminal revision filed by appellant are to be taken on file by HC and dispose of the same. Order accordingly.

Ratio - HC while refusing leave should indicate reasons for refusal to grant leave as refusal of leave to appeal has the effect of foreclosing the right once for all.
