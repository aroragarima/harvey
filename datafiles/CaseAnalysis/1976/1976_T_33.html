Paradip Port Trust, Paradip v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 187; (1977) SCC (L&S) 253; (1977) 2 SCC 339; AIR 1977 SC 36; 1976 (33) FLR 243; 1977 LabIC 1770; 1976 (2) LLJ 409; 1977 (1) LLN 5; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 537

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Representation Of Parties, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Bar Councils (Validation of State Laws) Act, 1956

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 36(2)(4) - Industrial Dispute - Representation by legal practitioner - Holding power of Attorney - S. 36 is not exhaustive but only supplemental - Consent of other party and leave of court necessary - Interpretation of word 'and' in s. 36(4) of Act - It cannot be held that 'and ' in s. 36(4) can be read as 'or' - However advocate can plea only by consent of parties and tribunal except when he is an office bearer - Petition dismissed.
