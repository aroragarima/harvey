State of Maharashtra v Chandrabhan Tale

Supreme Court of India

07 July 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 110; (1983) 3 SCC 387; (1983) SCC (L&S) 391; (1983) SCC (Cr) 667; AIR 1983 SC 803; 1984 (48) FLR 57; 1983 LabIC 1128; 1983 (2) LLJ 256; 1983 (2) LLN 644; 1983(1) SCALE 690; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 337; 1983 (2) SLJ 227; 1983 (2) SLR 493; 1983 UJ 673

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 21, 300 A and 311(2) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Bombay Civil Services Rules, 1959, r. 151(i)(ii)(b), Second Proviso - (A) Constitutionality of - (B) Applicability, to civil servant under suspension - (C) Whether right to equal opportunity to public employment is a private property - Held, violative of arts. 14, 16, 21 and also, 311(2) of Constitution - Entitled to normal subsistence - Further held, it is national wealth in which all citizens are equally entitled - Appeal dismissed and petition allowed.
