R. Ravindra Reddy and Others v H. Ramaiah Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 124; (2010) 3 SCC 214; AIR 2010 SC 991; JT 2010 (2) SC 314; 2010 (3) KarLJ 161; 2010(2) SCALE 459; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 943

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Recognised Private Schools (Regulation) Act, 1973, Mysore Religious and Charitable Institutions Act, 1927, Karnataka Land Reforms (Amendment) Act, 1978, Karnataka Land Reforms Rules, 1977

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961, ss. 48 and 132(2) - Karnataka Land Reforms Rules, 1977, r. 17 - Limitation - Partition of Joint Hindu Property - Partition of properties occurred between 'X' and 'Y' in respect of ancestral properties - 'Z' filed an application on 30-12-1974 for grant of tenancy rights in respect of suit schedule lands u/s. 48 of 1961 Act claiming occupancy rights on the ground that he had been cultivating the suit lands - In 1996 'Z' sold some of the lands in favour of respondent nos. 2 to 5 - Petitioners contended that they had no knowledge of grant of occupancy rights in favour of 'Z' - Petitioners filed suit for a declaration that sale deeds executed by 'Z' in favour of the respondent nos.2 to 5 were illegal and not binding on the petitioners - Trial Court dismissed petitioner's suit and held that suit was barred by limitation and was also not maintainable in view of the bar of s. 132(2) of 1961 Act - HC dismissed the appeal endorsing the view taken by the trial court - Hence, present petition - Whether order of tribunal upheld by HC could be upheld? - Held, order of the tribunal, was passed on 11-12-1975, whereas the suit was filed by the petitioners in 2005 seeking declaration, partition and permanent injunction in respect of impugned properties contending that respondent nos. 2 to 5 had tried to disturb the possession of the petitioners during the year 2004-05 - It was on record that notice of the proceedings before the tribunal had been given in the village in respect of the application filed by 'Z' - It is also on record that the petitioners' father was quite aware of the orders of the tribunal - HC has observed that inspite of the same, the petitioners' father did not question the correctness of the order of the tribunal - It is on that basis that the courts below held that the petitioners had knowledge of the concession made in favour of 'Z' and negated their contention that they were not aware of the same till they signed the compromise petition before this Court in another appeal - Lower court's order upheld - Special Leave Petition dismissed
