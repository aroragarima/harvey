Calcutta Municipal Corporation and Another v Anil Ratan Banerjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1148; (1995) Supp2 SCC 56; AIR 1995 SC 659; JT 1994 (7) SC 270; 1995 (1) RentLR 84; 1994(4) SCALE 817; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 191

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bengal Municipal Act, 1932, Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Bengal Municipal Act, 1932, ss. 319 and 325 - Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1951- Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1980, s. 635(ii)(g) - On 04-01-1984, South suburban municipal area along with certain other municipal areas was merged with Calcutta Municipal Corporation area and Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1980 was brought into force in place of Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act, 1951- New Act governed and applied only to original Calcutta Municipal Corporation area but not to newly added areas including South suburban area - Act, 1980, was extended to said newly added areas with effect from 20-02-1989 - Respondents entered into an agreement with owner of site concerned herein to purchase same - On 27-04-1985, Municipal Corporation sanctioned plan for construction of an eight-storied building - However, no construction was made within two years of permission - On 28-09-1987 Respondents applied for permission to construct a building comprising of twelve floors on basis of a new plan - No orders were passed by Corporation authorities within thirty days of their respective submission - Respondents assert that permission applied for by them on 28-09-1987 must be deemed to have been sanctioned on expiry of thirty days by virtue of s. 319 of BMA, 1932 - Writ Petition of Respondents allowed - Whether Respondents acquired necessary sanction for construction? - 1985 permission was granted for construction of an eight-storey building on premises in question - But no construction could be made within period of two years therefrom - By virtue of s. 325 of BMA Act, no construction can be carried on after expiry of two years from date of grant of permission - Assuming that an application for renewal was indeed made and even if by virtue of fiction contained in s. 319 said renewal permission is deemed to have been granted, even then this renewed permission expired on 16-04-1987 - And, admittedly, even by this date no construction has been made on premises - On 24-08-1987 respondents had applied for permission to construct a twelve-storey building and even if it assumes that such a permission must be deemed to have been granted by virtue of s. 319, this deemed permission too came to an end on 24-09-1989 - Even by 24-9-1989, no construction was made by Respondents on said premises - Even if any construction was commenced before that date, it cannot be continued thereafter according to s. 325 of Bengal Municipal Act - New Building Rules issued under Calcutta Municipal Act, 1980 came into force on and from 12-12-1990 - No construction could have been carried on by respondents after 24-09-1989, unless they obtained a fresh permission according to law - Appeal allowed.
