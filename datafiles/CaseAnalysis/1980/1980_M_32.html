Jai Singh Jairam Tyagi Etc. v Mamanchand Ratilal Agarwal And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 135; (1980) 3 SCC 162; AIR 1980 SC 1201; 1980 (2) RCJ 159; 1981 (1) RCR(Rent) 653; 1980 (1) RentLR 644; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rate Control Act, 1947 - Cantonments (Extension of Rent Control Laws) Amendment Act, 1972 - Non payment of rent - Decree for possession - Maintainability - Respondents who were the landlords of premises filed civil suit against the appellant/tenant for recovery of possession and arrears of rent under the provisions of 1947 Act - Suit was decreed - There was an appeal by the tenant - It resulted in a compromise decree by which some time was given to the tenant to vacate the premises - As the tenant failed to vacate the premises within the time given to him, the landlords were compelled to take out execution taking advantage of the decision of HC in one case - Indu Bhusan v. Rama Sundari Devi & Anr, 1966 Indlaw 121, relied on - Appellant filed application for a declaration that the decree obtained against him was a nullity and incapable of being executed - Application was allowed by the Court on 15-11-1971 - But, after the enactment of 1972 Act, the landlords filed Darkhast to execute the decree in their favour - Tenant raised various objections - One of the objections was that subsequent to the compromise decree there was a fresh agreement of lease between the landlords and himself - It was denied by the landlords - Another objection was that the provisions of 1972 Act were not extensive enough to save the decree - Third objection was that in any case the decision in miscellaneous application holding the decree to be a nullity operated as res judicata between the parties - First of the objections was left open by all the Courts for future adjudication - Second and third objections alone were considered, for the time being - In the judgment under appeal, HC over-ruled the second and third objections of the tenant - Hence instant appeal - Whether the compromise decree was saved by 1972 Act and whether decision of the executing Court in application declaring the decree to be a nullity separated as res judicata between the parties- Held, executing Court had refused to exercise jurisdiction and to execute the decree on the ground that the decree was a nullity as 1947 Act, had no application to buildings in Cantonment areas - That defect having been removed and all decrees obtained on the basis that the Bombay rent law applied to the Kirkee Cantonment area having been validated by 1972 Act, it could not be said that the earlier decision holding that the decree was a nullity operated as res judicata - If the earlier decision in application was to be regarded as res judicata it would assume the status of a special rule of jurisdiction applicable to the parties in derogation of the law declared by the legislature - HC, therefore, saw no substance in the second submission - Mathura Prasad Bajoo Jaiswal & Ors. I v. Dassibai N.B. Jeejeebhoy, 1970 Indlaw SC 562, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
