Jacob Mathew v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 452; (2005) 6 SCC 1; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1369; AIR 2005 SC 3180; 2005 (5) ALD(SC) 52; 2005 (3) AWC 2756; 2005 (12) CCC 370; 2009 (2) CompLJ 367; 2005 (3) CPJ(Sc) 9B; 2005 CRLJ 3710; 2005 (122) DLT 83; 2005 (3) G.L.R. 2126; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 184; JT 2005 (6) SC 584; 2005 (3) KLT 965; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 673; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 836; 2005 (4) RLW 2790; 2005(6) SCALE 130; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal

Keywords: Medical Profession, Gas Cylinder, Reckless

Summary: Consumer Protection - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 304A, 336, 337, 338, 34 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Death of a patient - Medical negligence - Charges against doctors - Whether a different standard is applicable for recording a finding of negligence when a professional, in particular, a doctor is to be held guilty of negligence? - Held, a professional may be held liable for negligence if either he was not possessed of requisite skill which he professed to have possessed, or, he did not exercise, with reasonable competence which he did possess - It is a case of non- availability of oxygen cylinder either because of hospital having failed to keep available a gas cylinder or because of gas cylinder being found empty - Hospital may be liable in civil law but accused appellant cannot be proceeded against u/s. 304A IPC on parameters of Bolam's test - Prosecution of accused appellant u/s. 304A r/w s. 34 is quashed - Appeal allowed.
