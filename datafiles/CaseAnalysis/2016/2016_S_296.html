Ravi Prakash Refineries Private Limited v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 369; AIR 2016 SC 2564; 2017 (349) E.L.T. 5; JT 2016 (5) SC 218; 2016 (5) MLJ 328; 2016(5) SCALE 200; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 565; [2016] 93 VST 441

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, Gujarat Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1963

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 8(5), 9(2) - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s 12A - Grounds for Re-assessment - AO passed order of assessment u/s.9(2) of the Act expressing view that inter-State sales of Sunflower De-oiled Cake (SF DOC) were liable to tax at 2% and further granted benefit on production of 'C' Form in terms of notification dt.31-5-2002 - However, succeeding AO opined that inter-State sales of SF DOC was liable to tax at 4 % and not at 2 % and levied tax at 4 % on inter-State sales of SF DOC and passed reassessment order - On appeal, Appellate Authority held that change of opinion of assessing authorities could not be ground for reopening of assessment in exercise of power u/s.12-A of 1957 Act and set aside order of re-assessment - Assessee still preferred appeal before Tribunal for reason that Appellate Authority had not expressed any opinion with regard to rate of tax on oil-cake and de-oiled cake - Tribunal passed order modifying rate of CST levied at 4 % to 2% and modified reassessment order accordingly - Aggrieved, Revision petition before HC was preferred assailing said order of Tribunal and HC passed impugned judgment holding that there is distinction between oil cake and de-oiled cake and that they are two different commodities and not one and the same - Hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether impugned judgment could be upheld.

Held, competent authority while exercising power u/s.8(5) of the 1956 Act, has kept the reduction of tax qua de-oiled cake from the purview of Notification dt.31-5-2002 and had only provided oil cake to be taxed at the reduced rate of tax. Hence, said goods have distinct and different identity. Further, assessing authority expressed opinion with regard to rate of tax on de-oiled cake while scrutinizing 'C' Forms which is expression of opinion on available materials brought on record and hence, First Appellate Authority as well as Tribunal was justified in concurring with said order. However, revenue had not challenged order of Appellate Authority setting aside re-assessment and HC had not expressed any opinion on re-assessment and hence, there should not be reopening of assessment. Hence, appeal of assessee is partly allowed and finding of HC regards oil-cake and de-oiled cake being different products as per notification dt.31-5-2002 is upheld. However, assessee would reap benefit of initial assessment as the same could not have been reopened. Appeal partly allowed. '

Ratio - s.12(a) of1957 Act permits re-opening of an assessment on the ground that if assessee has been assessed at a rate lower than the rate at which it is assessable under Act.
