State of Maharashtra and Others v Uttam Vishnu Pawar

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1091; (2008) 2 SCC 646; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1368; 2008(3) ALL MR 447; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 429; 2008 (116) FLR 788; JT 2008 (2) SC 80; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 911; 2008(1) SCALE 538; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 817; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1861

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Respondent before Tribunal, sought a direction that his services which have been rendered by him in earlier department may be counted for computing period of 12 years service for Time Bound Promotion as per Govt. Resolution D\- 08-06-1995 - Tribunal allowed claim of respondent - Aggrieved by Tribunal, State appellant filed a petition before High Court - Division Bench of HC affirmed order of Tribunal - Whether interference by Apex Court is justified or not? - In view of consistent approach of Apex Court, it is no more res integra that incumbent on transfer to new department may not get seniority but his experience of past service rendered will be counted for purpose of other benefits like promotion or for higher pay scale as per Scheme of Govt. - In this view of matter, courts are of opinion that view taken by Tribunal and affirmed by Division Bench of HC is correct and there is no ground to interfere with impugned judgment and order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
