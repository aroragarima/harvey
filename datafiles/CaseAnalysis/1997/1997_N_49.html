Chandigarh Administration and Others v Naurang Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2697; (1997) 4 SCC 177; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1050; 1997 (1) CLR 832; 1997 (76) FLR 96; JT 1997 (3) SC 536; 1997 (2) LLJ 799; 1997 (2) LLN 554; 1997(3) SCALE 38; 1997 (1) SCJ 617; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 965; 1997 (2) SLR 230; 1997 (3) Supreme 116; 1997 (1) UJ 748

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Equal pay for equal work - Whether the decision of tribunal to upheld the claim of the respondents of equal pay for equal work, justified? - Higher pay scale then given to storekeeper was the pay scale actually given to assistants, which is a promotion post for storekeepers - This mistake was corrected by the pay Revision Committee - Proceedings of the administration D/- 19-08-1982 show that the higher pay scale was treated as personal to the then existing incumbents - An evident mistake cannot constitute a valid basis for compelling the administration to keep on repeating that mistake - Personal pay is granted to employees on various grounds - Personal pays cannot furnish a ground for invoking the doctrine of equal pay for equal work - That was the pay scale admissible to the post of assistants which was a promotion post to storekeepers - Both these posts cannot be given the same pay scale - Held, claim of the respondents not have been allowed by the tribunal - Doctrine of equal pay for equal work has no application in present case - Appeal allowed.
