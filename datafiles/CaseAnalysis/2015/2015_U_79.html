Common Cause v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 350; (2015) 7 SCC 1; AIR 2015 SC 2286; 2015 (112) ALR 5; 2015 (4) AWC 3457; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 206; 2016 (3) KarLJ 193; 2015 (2) KLT 739; 2015(6) SCALE 302; 2016(6) SCALE 302; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Ombudsman

Summary: Civil Procedure - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 - Filing of PIL - Use of Public funds for advertisement by Govt. - Determination - Appellant registered body filed writ petition to restrain respondent and all State Govts. from using public funds on Govt. advertisements which are primarily intended to project individual functionaries of Govt. or political party - Petitioner contended that in garb of communicating with people, in many instances, undue political advantage and mileage is sought to be achieved by personifying individuals and crediting such individuals or political leaders as being responsible for various Govt. achievements and progressive plans. Further, such advertisements results in gross wastage of public funds and constitute misuse of governmental powers besides derogating fundamental rights of large section of citizens as guaranteed by arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution - Hence, present petition.

Held, Committee has been appointed for this purpose which have laid down various recommendations, SC held that recommendation of Committee with regard to publication of photographs; appointment of Ombudsman; carrying out independent audit and embargo on advertisements during election time that will have to be specifically dealt with in some details, however, remaining recommendations of Committee appear to be comprehensive and based on analytical approach of best practices prevailing in other jurisdictions and said recommendations, would serve public interest by enabling dissemination of information and spreading awareness amongst citizens not only of Govt. policies; achievements made and targets to be reached but also rights and entitlements of citizens including availability of host of welfare measures. Hence, such recommendations are accepted by SC. Further, SC held that photographs of individuals have potential of developing personality cult and image of one or few individuals which is direct antithesis of democratic functioning, hence, SC held that in departure to views of Committee which recommended permissibility of publication of photographs of President and Prime Minister of country and Governor or Chief Minister of State along with advertisements, there should be exception only in case of President, Prime Minister and Chief Justice of country who may themselves decide question. Advertisements issued to commemorate anniversaries of acknowledged personalities like father of nation would of course carry photograph of departed leader. Further, with respect to appointment of Ombudsman, SC held that for ironing out creases that are bound to show from time to time in implementation of present directions and to oversee such implementation government should constitute three member body consisting of persons with unimpeachable neutrality and impartiality and who have excelled in their respective fields. Further, with respect to special audit, SC held that there is no necessity of any such special audit inasmuch as machinery available is adequate to ensure due performance as well as accountability and proper utilization of public money. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - PIL is entertained by SC when interest of public is affected by action of authorities and there is violation of fundamental rights of citizen.
