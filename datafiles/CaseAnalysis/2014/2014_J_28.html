Karam Kaur v Jalandhar Improvement Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 313; (2014) 6 SCC 409; 2014(4) ALL MR 401; 2014 (4) AWC 4139; 2014 (6) MahLJ 542; 2014 (4) MPLJ 522; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 83; 2014 (124) RD 718; 2014(5) SCALE 767; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 708; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 17

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Family Settlement, Displaced Person

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 r. 3, s. 151 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Condonation of delay - Denied - Challenged - Land of the X (Applicant's husband) was acquired by the respondent - X made many requests to the respondent for allotting him a plot in lieu of the acquired land - Respondent ultimately allotted the disputed plot to X - Thereafter, X requested the respondent to accept instalment of Rs. 10,000/- and deliver vacant possession of the plot but to no effect - X, therefore, filed Civil Suit for declaration that he was entitled to deposit Rs.10,000/- towards first instalment of plot No.456 and balance price thereof - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of X - On appeal, First Appellate Court reversed the judgment of the Trial Court - X then filed second appeal - During the pendency of the said appeal, X died leaving behind applicant (his widow) along with 2 sons and 3 daughters - However, no petition for substitution was filed for years - After six and half years of the death of X, an application was filed in the second appeal on behalf of the respondent Nos.2 and 3 before the HC for bringing on record the following legal heirs of the deceased - Since the legal representatives of the X had chosen not to come forward to pursue the appeal, the second appeal was dismissed for non-prosecution - Applicant filed appeal for setting aside the order dismissing the appeal for non-prosecution claiming herself to be the sole legal heir in whom the right to sue survived on the basis of a Deed of Family Settlement executed between the Legal Representatives of X and other legal heirs of X - An application u/s. 5 of Act was also filed for condonation of delay in filing the restoration application and delay in bringing on record the Legal Representatives - Applicant submitted that she was an illiterate lady and was informed by her husband that his appeal was not likely to be taken up for the next 20 years and their counsel would intimate the date whenever it was listed - Applicant was not aware that the Legal Representatives were required to be brought on record after the death of her husband - Whether plea of the applicant could be accepted and appeal filed by the applicant could be allowed -

Held, applicant's plea that she was told by her husband that counsel would inform about the hearing of the application, could not be a ground to entertain the application for condonation of delay of more than 7 years for preferring the petition for substitution - Apart from the fact that the aforesaid family settlement was not brought on record by respondent Nos.2 and 3 before the Second Appellate Court while the petition for substitution filed, so called family settlement could not be relied upon to exclude the other legal heirs who had a right to be substituted due to the death of the X - Appeals dismissed.
