State of Orissa v Debendra Nath Padhi

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1025; (2005) SCC (Cr) 415; (2005) 1 SCC 568; AIR 2005 SC 359; JT 2004 (10) SC 303; 2005 (1) KLT 80; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 297; 2004(10) SCALE 50; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 460; 2005 (1) UC 438

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 173, 207A, 209, 227, 228, 239, 240, 482, 91 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 226 - Income-Tax Act, 1961 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Whether trial court can at time of framing of charge consider material filed by accused? - Held, no provision in Code grants to accused any right to file any material or document at stage of framing of charge - Appeals disposed of.
