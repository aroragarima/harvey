M.P. Gangadharan and Another v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 244; (2006) 6 SCC 162; AIR 2006 SC 2360; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 25; 2006 (64) ALR 467; 2006 (3) AWC 2660; JT 2006 (11) SC 79; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 899; 2006(6) SCALE 194; 2006 (5) SCJ 71; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 649

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: General Clauses Act, 1925

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Family Courts Act, 1984 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 21 - General Clauses Act, 1925 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 235, 236 - Kerala Civil Courts Act, 1957 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 16(1)(a) - Interpretation of provisions in matter of shifting of a family court from one place to another - Tenanted premises lacking in space and necessary infrastructure - Held, if, according to HC, present building is not suitable so as to meet requirements of litigants and because of it, it had taken decision to shift court to a better place, no fault can be found with said decision only because proposed site is situated at a distance of 12 kms from existing court building - Appeal dismissed.
