Raghuram Rao and Others v Eric P. Mathias and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 69; (2002) 2 SCC 624; AIR 2002 SC 797; AIR 2002 SCW 451; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 26; 2002 (46) ALR 695; JT 2002 (1) SC 494; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 40; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 525; 2002(1) SCALE 522; 2002 (1) SCJ 579; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 759; 2002 (1) SLT 621; 2002 (1) Supreme 433; 2002 (1) UC 505

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Covenant Not To Assign

Summary: As there is no express condition restraining partial alienation of the leasehold property, it would not be open to the transferee of the lessor's right to invoke the forfeiture clause for determining the perpetual lease and such conditions cannot be inferred by implication. Further, without the presence of lessees, lease cannot be determined and decree for possession of the property cannot be passed in favour of the lessor.
