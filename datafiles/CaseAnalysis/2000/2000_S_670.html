Thakur Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3896; (2003) 9 SCC 208

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Tort - Rash and negligent driving - Indian Penal Code, s. 304-A - Probation of Offenders Act - Doctrine of res ipsa loquitur comes into play and burden shifts on to man who was in control of automobile to establish that accident did not happen on account of any negligence on his part - Negligence - Petitioner himself was driving vehicle at relevant time and bus was driven over a bridge and then it fell into canal - Petitioner failed to show that accident happened due to causes other than negligence on his part - Whether probation of Offenders Act can be applied in present case? - Held, Supreme Court in Dalbir Singh v. State of Haryana( 2000 Indlaw SC 255) held that Probation of Offenders Act cannot be invoked in cases involving rash or negligent driving of bus resulting in death of human beings - Dalbir Singh Case followed - Appeal dismissed.
