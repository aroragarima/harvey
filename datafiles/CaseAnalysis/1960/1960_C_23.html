Â Tungabhadra Industries Limited, Kurnool v Commercial Tax Officer, Kurnool

Supreme Court of India

18 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 118; AIR 1961 SC 412; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 45; 1961 (1) SCJ 272; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 14; 1960 (11) STC 827

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Gross Turnover

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939, rr. 4, 5(1)(g) and 18 - A/y 1949-50 - Deduction u/r. 18(2) - Appellant purchased groundnuts out of which it manufactured groundnut oil - Appellant also refined said oil and hydrogenated it and convert it into Vanaspati - Appellant claimed deduction u/r. 18(1) in respect of the purchase price of the groundnuts from the proceeds of the sale of all groundnut oil - Tribunal as well as the HC held that the hydrogenated oil (or Vanaspati) ceased to be groundnut oil by reason of the chemical changes which took place and which resulted in the acquisition of new properties including the loss of its fluidity - Hence, Vanaspati or hydrogenated oil was not 'groundnut oil but a product of groundnut oil manufactured out of groundnut oil and therefore not entitled to the benefit of the deduction u/r. 18(2) of Rules 1939 - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, hydrogenated groundnut oil continued to be 'groundnut oil' notwithstanding the processing which was merely for the purpose of rendering the oil more stable - To be groundnut oil two conditions had to be satisfied it must be from groundnut and it must be oil - Impugned hydrogenated oil was from groundnut and its essential nature remained an oil - Mere fact that hydrogenation made it semisolid did not alter its character as an oil - Appellant entitled for benefit of the deduction u/r. 18(2) of Rules 1939 in respect of hydrogenated oil - Appeal partly allowed.
