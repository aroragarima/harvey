Kailash Prasad Yadav And Another v State Of Jharkhand And Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 443; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 14; (2007) 5 SCC 769; AIR 2007 SC 2626; 2007 (2) ALT 153; 2007 CRLJ 3722; 2007 (1) FAC 253; 2007 (2) JCC 1347; JT 2007 (6) SC 369; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 24B; 2007(6) SCALE 461; 2007 (4) SCJ 82; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Fair Price Shop, Ration Cards, Vehicle Carrying

Summary: Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Appeal against the judgement of High Court dismissing the appeal filed against the dismissal order of Additional Session judge in a petition filed against the order of confiscation passed by Deputy Commissioner - Confiscation proceedings initiated against a truck hired for transportation of food grains while carrying food grains allegedly wheat - Held, there is no provision for search of a vehicle (in the order); the power of search is confined to fair price shop or any premises relevant to transaction of business of the fair price shop, the power of such authorities causing a search is confined to Sub-clause (3) of Clause 10 of the 2001 Order to search, seize or remove such books of accounts or stocks of essential commodities where such authority has reason to believe that these have been used or will be used in contravention of the provisions of the Order; a valid seizure, as is well known, is a sine qua non for passing an order of confiscation of property; this aspect of the matter has not been considered by any of the authorities and the courts - Appeal allowed.
