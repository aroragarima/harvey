Mohan Soni v Ram Avtar Tomar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 316; (2012) 2 SCC 267; 2012 ACJ 583; AIR 2012 SC 782; 2012 (90) ALR 883; 2012 (2) AWC 1263; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 842; 2012 (4) CGLJ 405; 2012 (2) CTC 435; 2012 (1) JabLJ 217; 2012 (2) LLJ 554; 2012 (4) MahLJ 47; 2012(4) M.P.H.T. 158; 2012 (3) MPLJ 3; 2012 (1) OLR 526; 2012 (166) PLR 420; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 431; 2012 (1) RLW 625; 2012(1) SCALE 135; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988, s.166 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Accident - Amputated - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Appellant/Victim used to earn his livelihood as cart puller and met with accident hit by tanker - Appellant's left leg was amputated below knee - Appellant claimed compensation u/s.166 of the Act before Tribunal - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs. 2,03,400/- Aggrieved appellant preferred appeal before HC which raised compensation amount from Rs.2,03,400/- to Rs.2,58,000/- - Hence, instant appeal - Whether compensation awarded by lower courts was justifiable - Held, there should be correlation between physical disability suffered in accident and loss of earning capacity resulting from it - Percentage of loss of earning capacity, arise from permanent disability would be different from percentage of permanent disability - Ascertainment of effect of permanent disability on actual earning capacity involved three steps - First, ascertain what activities claimant could carry on in spite of permanent disability and what he could not do as result of permanent disability - Second step was to ascertain his avocation, profession and nature of work before accident, and also his age - Third step was to find out whether claimant was totally disabled from earning any kind of livelihood, or in spite of permanent disability - Lower amount of compensation for lesser scale of activities should plead and show before Tribunal that victim enjoyed some legal protection under 1955 Act or in case of vast multitude who earn their livelihood in unorganized sector by leading cogent evidence that victim had in fact changed his vocation or means of his livelihood and by virtue of such change he was deriving certain income - Loss of earning capacity of appellant should be as high as 100 percent but in no case it would be less than 90 percent - Hence, compensation would amount to Rs.3,56,400/- and after addition of a sum of Rs.30,000/- and Rs.15,000/- total amount would be Rs.4,01,400/- - Additional compensation amount would carry interest at rate of 9 percent per annum from date of filing of claim petition till date of payment - Appeal disposed of.
