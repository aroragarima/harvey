Ashok Chintaman Juker and Others v Kishore Pandurang Mantri and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20123; (2001) 5 SCC 1; AIR 2001 SC 2251; 2002 (47) ALR 37; 2001 (4) Bom.C.R. 31; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 177; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 581; 2001(4) SCALE 46; 2001 (4) SCJ 141; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 627; 2001 (4) SLT 252; 2001 (4) Supreme 154; 2001 (2) UC 225B

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Rent Control Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Bombay Rent Control Act, 1947, s. 5(11)(c) - Whether member of family of original tenant claiming to have been residing with tenant at time of his death can resist execution of a decree passed against other member of tenant's family who undisputedly was accepted by landlord as a tenant on death of original tenant? - There are two requisites which must be fulfilled before a person is entitled to be called a 'tenant'; firstly, he must be a member of tenant's family and secondly, he must have been residing with tenant at time of his death; also he must have been agreed upon to be tenant by members of tenant's family, in default of such agreement decision of court shall be binding on such members; in case of joint tenancy, notice on any one of tenants is valid and suit impleading one of them as a defendant is maintainable and decree passed in such suit is binding on all tenants - Held, in this case, on death of original tenant, rent bills were issued in name of his elder son and on his death, in name of his widow, also appellant's plea is that he was paying rent through her, thus tenancy being one, all members of family of original tenant residing with him at time of his death, succeeded to tenancy together, therefore, widow who was impleaded as a tenant in suit represented all tenants and compromise decree passed can neither be said to be invalid nor inexecutable against any person claiming to be a member of family residing with original tenant - Appeal dismissed.
