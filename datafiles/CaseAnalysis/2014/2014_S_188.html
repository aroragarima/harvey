Nand Kumar v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 121; (2014) 5 SCC 300; AIR 2015 SC 133; 2014 (3) AWC 2378; JT 2014 (4) SC 227; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 334; 2014(3) SCALE 63; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 193; 2014 (3) SLR 73

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Regularization, Bihar Agriculture Produce Market (Repeal) Act, 2006, Daily wages employee

Summary: (1) Employment-Regularisation -Termination/removal - Daily-wagers-Not in dispute that some of appellants worked as daily-wagers for long period-Not in dispute that some of appellants received monthly salary in minimum pay scale with usual allowances-No advertisement issued by Government to regularise service of appellants-No steps taken to safeguard service of appellants-Appellants cannot be treated as permanent Government employees-Projects in which daily-wagers/ appellants were engaged, having come to end, their appointment necessarily terminated for want of work-Committee of Secretaries not faulted in treating daily-wage employees/appellants on different footing and deciding for removal of their services.

(2) Employment-Regularisation/ absorption-Is not matter of course-It would depend upon facts of case following rules and regulations and cannot be de hors rules for such regularisation/absorption.

(3) Employment-Appointment-Daily-wagers-Are not appointees in strict sense of term 'appointment'-They do not hold post-Appointment on daily-wage basis is not appointment to post according to rules-Scheme of alternative appointment framed for regular employees of abolished organiaation cannot, therefore, confer similar entitlement on daily-wagers of abolished organisation to such alternative employment.
