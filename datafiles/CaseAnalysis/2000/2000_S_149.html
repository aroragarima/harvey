Shama Prashant Raje v Ganpatrao and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 476; (2000) 7 SCC 522; AIR 2000 SC 3094; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 10; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 318; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 59; 2001 (1) MLJ 206; 2000 (2) RCR(Rent) 431; 2000(6) SCALE 531; 2000 (7) SLT 147; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 448; 2000 (6) Supreme 444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: Constitution - Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949, s.13(3)(ii), (iii) and (vi) - Habitual defaulter - Sublet premises - Bona fide - Erroneous - Respondent/landlord filed an application before Rent Controller u/s.13(3)(ii), (iii) and (vi) of 1949 Order sought permission to determine tenancy of appellant, inter alia on ground that tenant was a habitual defaulter and had sublet premises - Landlord needs premises for bona fide use - Appeal hold that tenant could not be held to be a habitual defaulter, that landlord had failed to establish tenant had sublet premises and that bona fide need had vanished - Hence, instant petition - Whether HC was justified in interfering with findings of Appellate Court under Control order, 1949 - Held, that Appellate Authority under 1949 Order was erroneous - HC was fully justified in correcting error by interfering with finding of lower Appellate Authority of applicability of s.13(3) (ii) of 1949 order - HC was entitled to correct error which was based upon a construction of document - S.13(3) (ii) of 1949 Order Controller had satisfied that tenant was habitually in errors with rent - Appeal dismissed.
