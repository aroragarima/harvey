Puspa Sen Gupta v Susma Ghose

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 800; (1990) 2 SCC 651; 1990 (2) CalLT(SC) 42; JT 1990 (2) SC 167; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 674; 1990(1) SCALE 804; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 564; 1990 (1) UJ 727

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Non-payment of Rent - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 8(3) - Trial Court agreed with the tenant and dismissed the suit while ADJ reversed decision and passed a decree for eviction - High Court confirmed the decree in second appeal - Whether rent includes additional amount payable in lieu of electricity, if so then appellant cannot be treated a defaulter in the matter of payment of rent? - Held, although expression 'rent' has not been defined, there are indications in the 1956 Act to suggest that the world 'rent' includes not only what is strictly understood as rent, but also payment in respect of amenities or services provided by landlord under the terms of the tenancy - The Act deals with the fixation and revision of fair rent and s. 8(3), takes into account, furniture if supplied or fittings affixed in the tenement for the use of the tenant, indicating that an agreement between the landlord and the tenant in respect of the additional amenities comes within the scope of the Act - Decision of HC was confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
