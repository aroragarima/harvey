Welfare Association of Absorbed Central Government Employees in Public Enterprises v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1924; (1996) 2 SCC 187; (1996) SCC (L&S) 444; AIR 1996 SC 1201; 1996 (33) ATC 188; 1996 LabIC 1042; 1995(7) SCALE 295; 1996 (1) SCJ 475; 1996 (1) SLR 618; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 748; 1996 (1) UJ 220

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 32 - Government Servant - Commutation of pension - Petitioners were Central Govt. servants - Public undertakings/enterprises was initiated by the Govt. of India - Depending on their experience and expertise, several officers were absorbed in the public undertakings - Retirement of such petitioners was deemed to be one in 'public interest' but the officers were denied the retiral benefits to the extent of 'one-third commutation' - Petitioners aggrieved - Hence, instant W.P.s - Whether, the retiral benefit regarding one-third commutation could be restored - Held, that after the expiry of 15 years, the pensioners who had opted for one-third commutation were entitled for restoration of pension on the ground that the lumpsum amount paid had got adjusted before the said period - Denial of benefits to the petitioners was violative of arts. 14, 16 of the Constitution - The contention of the Govt. that the petitioners ceased to be Central Govt pensioners on commuting their pension in full, was not acceptable - Petitioners were entitled for the same benefits as those derived by the Common Cause petitioners - Common Cause, Registered Society and Ors vs. Union of India, (1987) 1 SCR 497 1986 Indlaw SC 321, relied on - The denial of restoration of one-third commuted pension was not justified - Petitions allowed.
