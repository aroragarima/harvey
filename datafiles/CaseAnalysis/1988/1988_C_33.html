Collector Of Central Excise, Calcutta v Jay Engineering Works Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 292; (1989) Supp1 SCC 128; (1989) SCC (Tax) 165; AIR 1989 SC 488; 1989 (2) CalLT(SC) 32; 1989 CRLR 133; 1989 CrLR(SC) 133; 1989 (19) ECC 189; 1989 (20) ECR 137; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 169; JT 1988 (4) SC 664; 1988(2) SCALE 1529; 1989 (75) STC 313; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 998

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 3, Chap. 1, Item 68 - Exemption of excise duty was in question - Name plates to be affixed on electric fans before marketing - Held, dealer was entitled to benefit of proforma credit under notification providing exemption to excisable goods in manufacture of which any goods falling under Tariff Item 68 is used.
