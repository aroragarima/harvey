S. Ganesan v Rama Raghuraman And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 6; (2011) 2 SCC 83; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 662; 2011 (3) JCC 1542; JT 2011 (1) SC 1; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 181; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 283; 2011(1) SCALE 12; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 302 and 304 - Acquittal - Challenged - Respondents allegedly committed the murder of deceased by hitting him with a hammer on his head - Trial Court held that chain of circumstances was complete and each circumstance pointed out towards the guilt of the respondents and therefore convicted respondents for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - On appeal, HC reversed the judgment of Trial Court and acquitted respondents - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC committed an error by reversing the well reasoned judgment and order of the Trial Court? - Held, unless there are substantial and compelling circumstances, the order of acquittal is not required to be reversed in appeal - In the present case, HC did not deal with any of the incriminating circumstances considered by the Trial Court for the purpose of conviction of the respondent and did not address itself to the relevant issues involved in the appeal - Judgment and order of HC cannot be held to be sustainable in law and it suffers from perversity - (B) Acquittal - Held, prosecution did not establish any motive to commit the crime - There were mitigating circumstances in favour of the respondents to show that in spite of the fact that they had committed the offence they did not intend to kill the deceased - Thus, they were liable to be convicted u/s. 304 Part-II IPC read with s. 34 of IPC - Order passed by the Trial Court modified to the extent that respondents held guilty for offence punishable u/s. 304 Part-II r/w s. 34 of IPC -Appeal allowed.
