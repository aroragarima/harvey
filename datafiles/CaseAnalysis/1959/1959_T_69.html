Associated Cement Companies Limited, Chaibassa Cementwo v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1960 SC 56; 1960 (1) LLJ 1; 1960 SCJ 1164; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 703

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Factories Act, Mines Act, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25E r/w. 25C - Mines Act, 1952 ss. 17, 2 - Plantations Labour Act, 1951, s. 2 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1953 - One establishment - Rejected - Disqualification - Lay-off compensation - Company was owner of both factory and limestone quarry, it had also factories and limestone quarries at other places in India and Pakistan - All factories and limestone quarries of Company wherever situtate would be one establishment - Union made certain demands on management on behalf of labourers in the limestone quarry, but these were rejected by management - Industrial Tribunal apprehended limestone quarry was not part of establishment and workmen in latter were not disentitled to lay-off compensation by reason of s.25E of cl.(iii) - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interpretation in matter of disqualification for lay-off compensation u/s. 25E r/w s.25C of 1947 Act - Held, chairman of Industrial Tribunal wrongly detained that limestone quarry at Rajanka and factory at Jhinkpani were separate establishments - They constituted one establishment within meaning of cl. (iii) of s.25E of 1947 Act - Respondent workmen that lay-off in factory was due to non- supply of limestone by reason of strike in limestone quarry and strike was decided on by same Union which consisted of workmen at factory and quarry - Disqualification in cl. (iii) clearly applied and workmen at factory were not entitled to claim lay-off compensation - Appeal allowed.
