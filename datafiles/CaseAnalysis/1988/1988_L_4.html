State Of West Bengal And Anr. v Laisalhaque And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 706; (1989) 3 SCC 166; (1989) SCC (Cr) 513; AIR 1989 SC 129; 1988 BBCJ 139; 1989 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 1989 CRLJ 865; 1988 CrLR(SC) 672; JT 1988 (4) SC 32; 1988(2) SCALE 1090; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 870

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 218, 215, 221, 374(2), 386 and 454 - (A) Exception contemplated by s. 221 to general rule in s. 218 - When applicable? - (B) Error, omission or irregularity in charge - Question of prejudice - Decision as to - (C) Sessions trial against several persons - Appeal against conviction - No appeal by State against order of acquittal - Order of retrial - Maintainability - Held, (A) Applicable only when there is doubt regarding inference to be drawn from facts - (B) Factors to be considered by Court - (C) Not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
