U.P. State Road Transport Corporation v Subhash Chandra Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 216; (2000) 3 SCC 324; (2000) SCC (L&S) 349; AIR 2000 SC 1163; 2000 ALJ 847; 2000 (2) AWC 1482; 2000 (2) CLR 13; JT 2000 (3) SC 184; 2000 LabIC 1026; 2000 (1) LLJ 1117; 2000 (2) LLN 402; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 84; 2000(2) SCALE 371; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 451; 2000 (2) SLR 396; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1199

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act 1947, s. 11A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Quantum of punishment - Interfered with - Driver of STC demanding money in drunken state from cashier - Abuse and threat to assault - Punishment of removal - Charge of grave misconduct proved by Labour Court - But, relaxing punishment - Ground, not proportional - Discretion of - Writ petition against - High Court dismisses - Jurisdiction of - Held, capricious and arbitrary exercise of discretion by Labour Court - Dismissal of writ petition by HC was improper exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 226.
