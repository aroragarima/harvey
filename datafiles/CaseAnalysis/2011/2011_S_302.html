State Of U.P. & Ors. v Bharat Singh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 150; (2011) 4 SCC 120; JT 2011 (3) SC 457; 2011(3) SCALE 392; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 525; 2011 (4) SLR 436; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1109

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Clubbing, U.P. Secondary Education Services Commission and Selection Boards Act, 1982, U.P. Higher Education Services Act, 1980, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission (Procedure for Selection of Teachers) Regulations, 1983, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Rules, 1981

Summary: (A) Education - Service - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Act, 1980 - Selection of Principals in affiliated colleges - Validity - State Higher Education Commission selected respondent candidates as Principals in various affiliated colleges in State through a selection process - Since number of complaints were received alleging large scale irregularities and malpractices of serious nature in selection procedure, impugned direction appointing Divisional Commissioner to conduct an inquiry into allegations was issued by appellant State - Appellant, consequently, directed Director of Higher Education to withheld issuance of placement orders - Respondent candidates challenged impugned direction before HC - HC quashed direction and directed appellant to issue placement orders to respondent - Appellant issued placement orders to respondent in the meantime - Hence instant appeal by State - Whether HC was justified in quashing appointment of Divisional Commissioner (enquiry officer) appointed to look into allegations of malpractice allegedly committed in course of selection process - Held, if HC on basis of whatever material was placed before it by parties came to conclusion that there was nothing wrong with selection process, any enquiry made by State would be wholly unnecessary - However, if HC came to conclusion that selection was vitiated by any illegality or irregularity, State Government could exercise its power and institute an enquiry for removal of any member who might have committed any misconduct by being a party to any such illegality or irregularity - Since legality of selection process was pending adjudication before HC in various other writ petitions where all parties concerned would have an opportunity to present their respective cases - Respondent candidates, however, in meantime, undertook before SC to effect that in case they failed in their case before HC they would stand reverted to earlier posts and difference of salary amount drawn by them as Principals recovered and paid back to State - Such undertaking, however, did not mean that appointed candidates would not be entitled to claim full benefit of post admissible to incumbent to which they had been appointed during period such appointments continue to remain in force - Directions under which appointments were allowed to be made also did not permit appellant State to withhold benefits legitimately flowing from such appointments - If any additional financial benefits by way of allowances became payable to appointed candidates same should be allowed to be drawn by them - Enjoyment of all such benefits would also remained subject to undertakings which appointed candidates had filed before SC - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Education - Service - Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and other Backward Classes) Act, 1994 - Posts of Principals - Applicability of reservation policy - Whether posts of Principals in different affiliated/aided Degree and Post-Graduate institutions constituted a single cadre and were, therefore, subject to reservation as prescribed under provisions of 1994 Act - Held, merely because Government supported educational institutions which were in all other respects autonomous in their functioning, and were managed by individual managements could not by any stretch of reasoning be taken as a circumstance constituting posts in such colleges into a single cadre - Terms and conditions of service of teachers serving in different colleges including Principals were similar on account of such colleges being affiliated to same university and being governed by same set of Statutes, Rules and Regulations also did not have anything to do with creation or existence of a single cadre comprising such posts - Common features in maintaining posts of Principals did not in any way impinge upon autonomous character of affiliated educational institutions nor did payment of salaries and similarity of conditions of service of employees provided a test for holding that although serving in different institutions totally independent of each other Principals appointed in such institution form a common cadre - Further, attribute of inter-changeability and transferability was missing in case of Principals - Reservation of such a post was clearly impermissible not only because 1994 Act provided for reservation based on 'cadre strength' in aided institutions but also because such strength being limited to only one post in cadre was legally not amenable to reservations - Post of Principals in each of aided/affiliated institutions being a single post in cadre was, not amenable to any reservation - Appeal disposed of.
