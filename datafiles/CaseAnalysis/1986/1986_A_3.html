State of Kerala v A. Lakshmikutty and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 207; (1986) 4 SCC 632; AIR 1987 SC 331; 1986 (12) ALR 699; 1986 ATC 735; 1986 (53) FLR 763; JT 1986 SC 819; 1987 LabIC 447; 1987 (1) LLN 31; 1986(2) SCALE 773; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 136; 1987 (1) SLJ 245; 1987 (1) SLR 17

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Legislative Powers, Backward Classes, Advocate-General, Affirmative Action

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 163, 226 and 233 - Kerala State and Subordinate Services rules, 1958, r. 14 - Appointment of District Judges - Direction of the High Court to Governor of State - Validity - Whether issuance of a writ of mandamus by HC directing Governor to act on recommendation of HC to fill up five vacancies in posts of District Judges reserved for direct recruitment from practising members of bar u/art. 233(1) of Constitution, 1950 was constitutionally impermissible? - Held, choice of candidates lies entirely with HC, exercise of power of Governor u/art. 233(1) in matter of appointment of District Judges is conditioned by consultation with exercise of power that power can only be exercised in consultation with HC - The judgment and order of the HC directing the issuance of a writ of mandamus commanding the State Govt. to appoint respondents Nos. 1 and 3-6 as District Judges u/art. 233(1) of the Constitution, 1950 is set aside - Appeal disposed of.
