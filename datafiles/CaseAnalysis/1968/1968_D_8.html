Dalpat Singh and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 394; AIR 1969 SC 17; 1969 CRLJ 262; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Extortion - In discharge of official duty - Appellant no. 1 was a Havaldar and Appellant no. 2 was a Subedar in the Rajasthan Armed Constabulary - Appellants conspired to extort money as well as other valuable things from villagers by using force or threat of force or by harassment - Trial Court held appellants liable for offence and awarded punishment - HC affirmed convictions in respect of all charges leveled against appellants, however reduced sentence awarded by Trial Court as regards other offences - Whether both or any of the appellants could in law be convicted under ss. 120 B and 161 of IPC, as well as u/s. 5(2) read with s. 5(1)(a) of the Act, for offences with which they were charged - Held, appellant no. 2 was mainly responsible for extortions and appellant no. 1 was aiding him in activities - Acts complained against appellants could not be held to constitute offences u/s. 161 of IPC - If an offence did not fall under s. 161 of IPC, it could not come within s. 5(1)(a) of the Act - However, to bring offence under s. 5 (1)(d)of the Act it was not necessary to prove that the acts were done by appellants in discharge of their official duties - Appellants were liable to be convicted u/s. 5(2) read with s. 5( 1 )(d) of the Act - Court partly allowed appeal and acquitted appellants under ss. 120 B and 161 of IPC, as well as u/s. 5(2) read with s. 5(1)(a) of the Act - Appellant no. 2's conviction u/s. 5(2) read with s. 5 (1)(d) of the Act was sustained - Conviction of appellant no. 1 was altered to one u/s. 5(2) of the Act read with s. 114 of IPC - SC decision in Dhaneshwar Narain Saxena v. The Delhi Administration, 1961 Indlaw SC 178, followed - Appeal partly allowed.
