Gopika Chandrabhushan Saran and Another v Xlo India Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 176; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 85; (2009) 3 SCC 342; AIR 2009 SC 2032; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2802; [2009] 148 Comp Cas 130; 2009 (2) CompLJ 1; 2009 (2) CTC 639; JT 2009 (3) SC 431; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 195; 2009(3) SCALE 225; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Technical Advisor, Withholding Of Property

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Question with regard to the scope of and ambit of the provisions of s. 630 - Whether the proceedings under the said provision would cover within its purview only the employee of the company or also the persons claiming a right through him or under him? - Held, capacity, right to possession and the duration of occupation are all features which are integrally blended with the employment - Once the right of the employee or the officer to retain the possession of the property, either on account of termination of services, retirement, resignation or death, gets extinguished, they (persons in occupation) are under an obligation to return the property back to the company and on their failure to do so, they render themselves liable to be dealt with under s. 630 for retrieval of the possession of the property - S. 630 will cover within its ambit not only the employee or officer but also the past employee or the past officer or the heirs of the deceased employee or anyone claiming under them in possession of the property - Respondent company was within its jurisdiction to get the suit premises vacated - Appeal dismissed.
