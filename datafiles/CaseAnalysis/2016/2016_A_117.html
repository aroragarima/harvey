Arvind Kumar Sharma v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 594; AIR 2016 SC 3904; 2016 (5) AWC 5206; JT 2016 (8) SC 247; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1038; 2016(8) SCALE 6

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Licensing Authority, CONSTITUTION, Army Act, 1950, Arms Act, 1959, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Defense & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959 - Army Act, 1950 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 465, 468, 471 - Bore weapons - Illegal sale - Prohibition - Petitioner filed instant writ petition with regard to illegal selling of 'Prohibited/NSP bore-weapons' obtained by Army Personnel through Central Ordinance Depot (COD), on the basis of order passed by Allotment Committee - Petitioner alleged that weapons have been sold to general public including to persons with criminal records, in breach of relevant Rules and provisions of the 1959 Act.

Held, District Magistrate is licensing authority for non-prohibited bore weapons. As per the extant regulations, the licensing authority is obliged to seek report of the officer in-charge of the nearest police station to verify the antecedents of applicant and only those who fulfill the prescribed norms are granted license. While processing applications, District Magistrate is also obliged to consider such report and grant arms license for non-prohibited weapons only to persons who may be residing within the local limits of District Magistrate including after due verification of antecedents of applicant. Considering the reports and the affidavits filed by the respective authorities from time to time in instant proceedings and being satisfied that suitable action has been taken by appropriate authority of State Govt. as well as Central Govt. against officers whose involvement has been noted in independent enquiries made by concerned dept. and that as there is already a firm procedure prescribed for issuance of prohibited and non-prohibited weapons in the shape of provisions of the 1959 Act and the Rules framed thereunder and including periodical instructions issued by Dept. in that behalf, nothing more needs to be done. Therefore, appropriate Authorities/Court must dispose of pending matter(s) if any, expeditiously in accordance with law. Thus, mechanism for sale of NSP weapons must be under strict scrutiny and supervision of Competent Authority in accord with the provisions of the 1959 Act and the Rules framed thereunder including the Regulations without any exception. Petition disposed of.
