Jai Chand Sawhney v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 228; (1969) 3 SCC 642; 1970 (20) FLR 119; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 222; 1969 SLR 879

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, art. 102 - Dismissal - Arrears of salary - Validity - Respondents by an order removed plaintiff from service - Plaintiff filed suit challenging said order - Trial Court held that dismissal was illegal and void and decreed claim for Rs. 9,335-35 for arrears of salary - On appeal, HC awarded arrears of salary for 3 years prior to date of suit - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgment and order of HC - Held, if order of dismissal was set aside public servant was deemed to be in service throughout period during which order of dismissal remained operative, and his right to sue for salary arises at end of every month in which he was unlawfully prevented from earning salary which he could, but for illegal order of dismissal have earned - HC was right in holding that plaintiff's claim was governed by art. 102 of the Act that remuneration payable to him accrued due month after month, and that plaintiff's claim for salary beyond period was barred by limitation - However, a slight modification should be made in decree of HC - Therefore, plaintiff was entitled to salary for 3 years and 2 months prior to date of suit - Appeal dismissed.
