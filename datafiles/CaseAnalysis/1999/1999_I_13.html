Indian Airports Employees Union v Ranjan Chatterjee & Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 952; (1999) 2 SCC 537; (1999) SCC (L&S) 558; AIR 1999 SC 880; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 82; 1999 CRLJ 1332; JT 1999 (1) SC 213; 1999 (1) LLJ 661; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 326; 2000 (1) SLJ 265

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(b) - Wilful disobedience - Whether non-absorption of six sweepers by corporation is contrary to order by Supreme Court regarding abolition of contract labour system and regularisation of persons working with Corporation and hence amounts to 'wilful disobedience' of orders of Supreme Court? - Held, non-absorption of six sweepers by Corporation though their names were shown in Annexure to writ petition filed in High Court was bona fide and based on an interpretation of orders of Court and notification etc. and hence cannot be said to amount to 'wilful disobedience' of orders of Supreme Court.
