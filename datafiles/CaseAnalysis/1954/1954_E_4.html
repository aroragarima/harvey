Â Edward Mills Company Limited, Beawar, and Others v State of Ajmer and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1955 SC 25; 1954 (2) LLJ 686; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1955 SCJ 42; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Minimum Wages Act,1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Petitioners prayed for declaration that notification issued by State Govt. of Ajmer fixing minimum rates of wages in respect of employment in textile industry is illegal and ultra vires - Held, Committee appointed u/s. 5 is only an advisory body - Govt. is not bound to accept any of its recommendations - Procedural irregularities could not vitiate final report which fixed minimum wages - Appeals dismissed.
