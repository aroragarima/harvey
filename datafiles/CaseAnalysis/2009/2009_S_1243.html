Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited v Sunil Bansal And Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1155; (2009) 10 SCC 446; 2009 (4) ACC 444; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 175; 2010(2) ALL MR 484; 2009 (4) AWC 4186; JT 2009 (12) SC 335; 2009(12) SCALE 604; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 711

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Writ Jurisdiction, CPC, Appeal allowed, Stage Carriage, Rta, Registration Certificate, Transport Department, Inter-State Route, Registration Of Vehicle, Inter-State Permit, Tourist Permits, National Capital Region Planning Board Act, 1985, Central Motor Vehicles (Fourth Amendment) Rules, 2004, Central Motor Vehicles (First Amendment) Rules, 2009

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Central Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, r.90(7) and 115(14) - Pursuant to directions of SC in case of M.C. Mehta v. Union of India & Others 1998 INDLAW SC 1078, Central Govt. issued various notifications to achieve object of reducing pollution in NCR - Appellant (Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited) issued notice inviting tenders for transportation of LPG Cylinders within NCR - Prescribed that trucks offered for Delhi should comply with BS-III emission norms if manufactured on or after 1.4.2005 and age of truck should not be more than 12 years from month of floating of notice inviting tenders - Respondent no.1 challenged eligibility criteria of trucks - HC held that respondent no.1 is entitled to participate in tender process initiated by appellant - Hence, present appeal - Appellant submitted that respondent no.1 purchased vehicles which do not conform with Bharat Stage-III norms even though they had been manufactured after the notification dated 20.10.2004 and sought to circumvent it by getting national permits - Held, notification no. GSR 686 (E) dated 20.10.2004 was issued for purpose of controlling pollution within NCR in phased manner - Vehicles manufactured prior to 1.4.2005 being permitted to ply does not anyway militate or discriminate against prohibition of plying vehicles manufactured after 1.4.2005 which were not Bharat Stage-III compliant as they clearly fell in different classes - Further, other notification GSR 37(E) dated 20.1.2009 which inter-alia inserts proviso to sub-rule (7) in r.90 clarifies that where such vehicle is registered in NCR , it shall not pick up or set down goods between two points situated in NCR unless it conforms to mass emission standards (Bharat Stage-III) - Said classification is essential in view of fact that all vehicles could not have been prohibited from plying on road in one stroke - Therefore, there is a clear nexus of classification with objects sought to be achieved by legislation - Further held, in absence of any challenge to validity of proviso to sub-rule (7) of r.90, said provision has to be held valid and must be given full effect - View taken here is only possible and intended view which can be inferred from a reading of amended provisions which is of clarificatory nature - HC misread and misconstrued notification - Impugned judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
