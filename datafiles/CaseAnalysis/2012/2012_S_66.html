State of Haryana v Shakuntla and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 131; (2012) 5 SCC 171; AIR 2012 SC 2123; 2012 CRLJ 2850; JT 2012 (4) SC 287; 2012 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1911; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 106; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2012(4) SCALE 526; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148,149,325,302 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Murder - Contradictions in Statement - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondents-accused were charged with offences punishable u/ss. 148, 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Trial Court convicted respondents u/ss. 148, 325 and 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Aggrieved respondents preferred appeal before HC - HC, noticing contradiction in statement of witnesses upheld conviction of accused No 1 to 4 and 9 while acquitting accused Nos. 5 to 8 - Aggrieved respondents and petitioner filed separate appeals - Whether HC, was justified in reversing judgment of Trial Court - Held, HC, had acquitted some accused while accepting plea of alibi taken by them, against judgment of acquittal, onus was on prosecution to show that findings recorded by HC was perverse and required correction by SC, in exercise of its powers u/art. 136 of Constitution - SC, had repeatedly held that an appellate Court must bear in mind that in case of acquittal, there was a double presumption in favour of accused, Firstly, presumption of innocence was available to such accused under fundamental principles of criminal jurisprudence, i.e., that every person shall be presumed to be innocent unless proved guilty before court and Secondly, that a lower court, upon due appreciation of all evidence had found in favour of his innocence - Merely because another view was possible, it would be no reason for SC, to interfere with order of acquittal - Appeals dismissed.
