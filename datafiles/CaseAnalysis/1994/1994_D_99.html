Noorduddin v Dr. K.L. Anand

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 468; (1995) 1 SCC 242; AIR 1994 SCW 5093; 1994 (3) CCC 548; JT 1994 (7) SC 652; 1994(4) SCALE 960; 1995 (1) SCJ 76; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 322

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 21, rr.97, 98, 100, 101, 103, 104 and 151 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, s. 20 - Auction sale of evacuee properties - Respondent being highest bidder granted certificate of sale - Appellant resisted execution u/O. 21, rr. 97 and 98 r/w s. 151 of CPC, 1908 - Executing Court and High Court on revision dismissed application that dispute was adjudicated earlier by HC - Whether Executing Court and HC had properly appreciated scheme u/O. 21, r 97 of CPC, 1908 and declined to entertain and adjudicate claim of appellant? - Held, appellant claimed independent right, title and interest and resisted execution, decree-holder or appellant should make an application u/r. 97(1) and Court, in that event, is enjoined to adjudicate claim and record a finding, allowing or rejecting claim - Appeal allowed.
