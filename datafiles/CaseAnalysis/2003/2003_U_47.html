Sahadeo Singh & Ors. v Union of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 170; (2003) 9 SCC 75; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1010; AIR 2003 SC 1568; 2003 (2) AWC 1037; 2003 (19) CLR 709; 2003 (2) ESC 102; 2003 (96) FLR 1045; JT 2003 (1) SC 665; 2003 LabIC 1199; 2003 (1) LLN 815; 2003(1) SCALE 705; 2003 (2) SLR 415; 2003 (2) SLT 450; 2003 (1) Supreme 987; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 812; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3426

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Service

Summary: Service - Railway Protection Force Rules, 1959 - Rule 47 - appellants removed from service on ground of serious misconduct and negligence of duty - While passing the order of removal, the officer, satisfied that no independent evidence will be available in the departmental enquiry against these appellants in view of the prevailing fear amongst the witnesses, dispensed with the requirement of holding an inquiry - appeal - Held, the facts narrated in the internal reports clearly go to show that these appellants were in league with certain desparate miscreants, therefore, the locals who witnessed the theft were not willing to come forward to give any evidence, therefore, the disciplinary authority, rightly came to the conclusion that it would be impracticable for the Railways to hold an enquiry where witnesses could be examined to establish the misconduct of the appellants. - Material found in the 3 internal reports sufficient to dispense with the enquiry.
