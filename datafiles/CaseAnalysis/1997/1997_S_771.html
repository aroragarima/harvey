Parsion Devi and Others v Sumitri Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2991; (1997) 8 SCC 715; JT 1997 (8) SC 480; 1998 (1) MLJ(SC) 89; 1997(6) SCALE 432; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 470; 1997 (9) Supreme 25; 1997 (2) UJ 820

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47, r. 1 and s. 114 - Limitation Act, arts. 181 and 182 - Review of findings recorded by another Single Judge - Review Proceedings are to be strictly confined to ambit and scope of O. 47, r. 1, it cannot be an appeal in disguise - There is a clear distinction between an erroneous decision and an error apparent on face of record - While first can be corrected by higher forum, latter only can be corrected by exercise of review jurisdiction - However, mechanical use of statutorily sanctified phrases cannot detract from real import of order passed in exercise of review jurisdiction - Held, recourse to review petition in facts and circumstances of case was not permissible - Order set aside - Appeal allowed.
