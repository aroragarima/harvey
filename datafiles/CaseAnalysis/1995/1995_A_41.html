Union of India v Ajoy Kumar Patnaik

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1135; (1995) 6 SCC 442; (1996) SCC (L&S) 28; AIR 1996 SC 280; 1995 (31) ATC 730; JT 1995 (7) SC 30; 1995 LabIC 2712; 1996 (2) LLN 855; 1995(5) SCALE 490; 1996 (1) SLJ 138; 1995 (6) SLR 27; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 449

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service - Adverse entries - In character rolls of 'doubtful integrity' - Order of competent authority - Validity of - Held, when it is a misconduct, competent authority is equally entitled to take a decision whether an officer has impeccable integrity and absolute devotion to duty for further continuation in service - Would be free to consider material, particularly latest one, and form a bona fide decision in public interest to compulsorily retire an officer from service - Since competent authorities at different levels had considered material and ultimately had decided to compulsorily retire respondent from service, it cannot be said that it is an arbitrary decision - It is true that pending proceedings respondent has already retired from service on attaining age of superannuation, but that would not provide a ground to dispose of this matter without giving any finding on action taken by competent authority - Otherwise, in all cases it would cause grave damage to public justice - Employee would get away with it due to pending proceedings - Mere retirement of officer by efflux of time pending proceedings would not be a ground to close matter - Order of Tribunal is set aside and that of appellant is upheld - Appeal allowed.
