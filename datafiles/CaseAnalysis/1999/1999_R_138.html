State of W. B v Rashmoy Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1284; (2000) 1 SCC 76; (2000) SCC (Cr) 103; AIR 2000 SC 228; 1999 (4) CCR 306; 2000 (1) Crimes 36; 2000 (1) JCC 26; JT 1999 (9) SC 337; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 135; 1999(7) SCALE 293; 2000 (1) SCJ 132; 1999 (10) SLT 204; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 80; 1999 (9) Supreme 539

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 167(5) - Bengal Excise Act 1909, s. 92(1) - Cognizance of offence - Investigation - Sanction for Prosecution - Expiry Period - Search was conducted by appellants/officers of Excise Dept. and huge quantity of spirit was seized there from and three respondents were arrested and they were liable to be prosecuted for various offences under 1909 Act - Respondents were produced before Judicial Magistrate, who later released them on bail - Respondents filed petition before Sessions Court for anticipatory bail, which granted an order in their favor u/s. 167(5) of CrPC - As Excise Officers received report from Chemical Examiner after a period of six months they seek order of Magistrate for continuing investigation and State Govt.'s order sanctioning prosecution -Respondents moved magistrate to discharge them from said case, as appellant/State failed to launch prosecution within a period of six months from date of alleged commission of offence - Magistrate dismissed petition and on appeal to HC, which disposed of petition as per order and all proceedings against respondents were quashed - Whether order of HC, quashing all proceedings against respondents was maintainable - Held, Reasoning adopted by Lower Court, that for obtaining sanction should be adopted before expiry of first six months period had no support u/s. 92 or any other provision of the Act - No scope for suggesting that officer should have commenced proceedings for securing sanction, before expiry of period of six months - So need for obtaining sanction would arise only after expiry of period of six months - Appeal allowed.
