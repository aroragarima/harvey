Secretary, Department of Excise & Commercial v Sun Bright Marketing Private Limited Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 104; (2004) 3 SCC 185; AIR 2004 SC 2743; 2004 (3) CHN 91; JT 2004 (2) SC 570; 2004(2) SCALE 435; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 313; 2004 (2) SLT 778; 2004 (2) Supreme 180

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Excise Act, Uttar Pradesh Excise Licenses (Tender-cum-Auction) Rules, 1991

Summary: [A.] Excise - Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1915, ss. 24, 24(2), 32, 38 - Closure of liquor shops for maintenance of public peace - Deduction by way of remission from amount of licence fee payable for three different periods -in terms of cl (3) of Rule II of General Licence Conditions, a remission or abatement in licence fee cannot be claimed save and except in cases which would come within purview of Section 32 of Act or Rule VIII of General Licence Conditions - Section 32 of Act has no application in instant case - Act and Rules are required to be harmoniously read - Political agitation resulting in unlawful assembly would clearly attract proviso appended to Sub-section (2) of Section 24 - In case of a riot or unlawful assembly, a licensee is statutorily enjoined to close his shop - Proviso appended to Sub-Section (2) of Section 24 is mandatory in nature In so far as claim of respondent for period 1 st April, 2000 to 3 rd April, 2000 for non-grant of licence is concerned - Same does not come within purview of Act, General Conditions or conditions of Sale Memo - Respondent for aforementioned purpose must avail other remedies, if any, in relation thereto -appeal partly allowed.
