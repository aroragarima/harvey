S. Chinnasamy v Seed Inspector, Coimbatore & Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1309; (2006) 11 SCC 486; AIR 2007 SC 109; 2006 CRLJ 4761; JT 2006 (9) SC 1; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 645; 2007 (5) RCR(Criminal) 255; 2006(10) SCALE 7; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 918

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Seeds (Control) Order, 1983

Summary: Trade - Administrative - Seeds (Control) Order, 1983, clauses 3(1), 8 and 18(1) - Sale of seed - Without valid licence - Seed Inspector on inspection found 2kgs. of cotton seeds and 2 kgs. of tomato seeds in the shop of appellant no. 1 - Seed Inspector filed complaint against appellant no. 1 that he had transacted business in seeds without any valid permit - Appellant No. 1 stated that he was dealing only in cement and not in seeds - Trial Court on the basis of evidence held that the appellants had violated clauses 3(1), 8(a) and (b) and 18(1) of the Order, 1983 - Special Court found the appellants guilty of the aforesaid clauses of the Seeds (Control) Order, 1983 and convicted them thereunder and sentenced each of the appellants to undergo 3 months simple imprisonment - HC upheld the order of the Special Judge - Appellants filed appeal against said order contending that it was the first case registered in the State of Tamil Nadu for violation under the Seeds (Control) Order, 1983 and the sentence imposed by the courts below is disproportionate, excessive and harsh - Whether order of the Special Bench as affirmed by the HC could be upheld? - Held, the ends of justice would be met, if the sentence of the appellants was reduced to the period already undergone by them - Appeals partly allowed.
