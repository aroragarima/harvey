Supreme Court of India

01 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 399; (2003) 12 SCC 551; AIR 2003 SC 3995; 2003 (51) ALR 625; 2003 (3) AWC 2394; JT 2003 (5) SC 34; 2003 (1) RCJ 178; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 607; 2003(4) SCALE 513; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1073; 2003 (1) SCW 2902; 2003 (3) SLT 588; 2003 (3) Supreme 701

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Transfer of Property Act, 1961

Summary: Transfer of Property Act - s. 106 - suit for eviction - whether the tenancy was terminated in accordance with the provisions of Section 106 of the Act. - The receipt of notice by the defendant is admitted in the written statement. The defendant has not raised any specific objection as to the validity of the notice. - Held, an objection as to invalidity or infirmity of notice under Section 106 T.P. Act should be raised specifically and at the earliest; else it will be deemed to have been waived even if there exists one. It cannot, therefore, be said that the notice in the present case suffered from any infirmity.
