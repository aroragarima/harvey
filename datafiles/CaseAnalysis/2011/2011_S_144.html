Ranu Hazarika & Ors. v State Of Assam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 132; (2011) 4 SCC 798; 2011 (2) ESC 346; JT 2011 (2) SC 497; 2011(3) SCALE 37; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 280; 2011 (2) SLR 392

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: National Council for Teachers Education (Determination of Minimum Qualifications for Recruitment of Teachers in Schools) Regulations, 2001, Assam Elementary Education (Provincialization) (Amendment) Rules, 2005, Assam Elementary Education (Provincialization) Rules, 1977

Summary: Education - Service - Assam Elementary Education (Provincialization) Rules, 1977 - Assam Elementary Education (Provincialization) (Amendment) Rules, 2005 - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - National Council for Teachers Education (Determination of Minimum Qualifications for Recruitment of Teachers in Schools) Regulations, 2001 - Recruitment process - Legality - Appellants completed two year diploma course in teachers training and became eligible for posts of Assistant Teachers - Respondent State amended 1977 Rules and issued an advertisement for recruitment of Assistant Teachers giving preference to candidates who had completed two years pre-service teacher's training course to two year diploma course candidates - Appellants challenged amendment Act before HC on ground that amendment Rules were ultra vires of 1993 Act and 2001 Regulations - HC struck down amendment Rules, however, did not incline to interfere on recruitment process initiated by respondent under amendment Rules - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent was allowed to complete recruitment process under amendment Rules - Held, decision of HC, permitting respondent State to continue with recruitment process initiated on basis of amendment Rules which had been declared by it to be illegal was clearly indefensible - Having failed to sustain amendment Rules before HC, it would be improper for respondent State to go ahead with recruitments under said amended Rules which had been declared null and void, particularly when HC decision on that issue had not been questioned by it - Leave granted by HC to respondent State to complete selection process in terms of advertisement in question was set aside - Appeal allowed.
