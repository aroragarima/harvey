VIR Rubber Products Private Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Mumbai-III

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 425; 2015 (318) E.L.T. 578; 2015 (318) E.L.T. 583; 2015(5) SCALE 206; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Exemption - Brand name of other companies on goods manufactured by SSI unit - Appellant was engaged in manufacture of bushes for use in motor vehicles under its own brand name 'VIR' and was also engaged in manufacture of spare parts on job orders placed by automobile companies and marked such goods as 'HM', 'PAL', 'KH', etc. - Further, goods supplied to said automobile companies were cleared by appellant on payment of excise duty but for own goods under its brand name, appellant claimed SSI benefit in terms of Notification No.1/93 - However, on interpretation of said notification, Department included value of goods supplied to automobile companies and concluded that total value of goods cleared by assessee in previous financial year was much more than Rs.3 crore - On contrary, appellant contended that turnover in respect of goods supplied to automobile companies should not be considered for arriving at figure of Rs.3 crore - Appellate Tribunal rejected contention of appellant and hence instant appeal - Whether appeal could be allowed.

Held, on the goods manufactured by appellant for the automobile companies, the appellant had been using their brand names or trade names on those goods. In such cases, in respect of those goods which are manufactured for the other person, no exemption is to be given as Notification does not apply to those specified goods. Hence, in respect of those goods where brand name of other party is used on manufactured goods and that other party is not a SSI unit, exemption is not available, it would lead to inevitable result that the value of such goods could not be added as well, while considering the value of the goods cleared by assessee in the previous year. Hence, when value of the goods meant for automobile companies, with names 'HM', 'PAL','KH', etc. could not have been included while considering as to whether the appellant is entitled to the benefit of the Notification or not and when that is excluded and the case is confined to the brand name 'VIR' which is the appellant's own brand name and in respect of which the appellant had claimed exemption, the value of goods cleared in the previous year was less than Rs.3 crore. Hence, appellant is entitled to claim exemption under Notification and so, order of Appellate Tribunal is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per Notification, exemption contained therein is not to apply to those goods bearing brand name or trade name whether registered or not of another person, except in certain cases mentioned therein.
