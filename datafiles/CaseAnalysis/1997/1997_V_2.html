State Rep. By Dy. Supdt. of Police, CBI v V. Jayachandra @ Ezhu Viral and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1066; (1997) 10 SCC 70; (1997) SCC (Cr) 783; 1997 (1) CCR 255; 1997 (2) Crimes 9; JT 1997 (4) SC 313; 1997(3) SCALE 25; 1997 (1) SCJ 630; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1063; 1997 (3) Supreme 379; 1997 (1) UJ 818

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; International; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 19 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 6 - Common intention - Acquittal - Sustainability - It was case of prosecution that one vessel/ship on high seas, which was about 440 nautical miles South-East of Madras was found drifting and was not responding to radio calls - Informant informed that Master of that vessel did not give correct name of vessel or call sign and other details regarding the vessel - Therefore, entertained suspicion about nationality and intentions of that ship and demanded boarding for verification - It was alleged that Master of that ship threatened informant of dire consequences if an attempt for boarding was made, by saying that it was carrying 110 tonnes of explosives - It then started fleeing away by taking a zig-zag course - Subsequently, accused no.1 revealed that name of the ship and it was carrying 10 A.K. 47 rifles, one FNC rifle, one Rocket Propelled Gun and about 25 hand grenades and huge quantity of oil and explosives - Accused denied inspection of said ship, started fired shots from Rocket Propelled Gun launcher and small arms and after some time set fire to their ship by using explosives - Accused nos.1 to 9 jumped over board and rescued by Indian Naval and Coast Guard vessles - Vessel got badly damaged by fire - When it was in the danger of sinking, the Naval Commandos boarded vessel and recovered 2dead bodies, 2assault rifles and a hand grenade - Accordingly charges were framed against all accused u/s. 120B r/w ss. 201, 353 and 438 IPC, s. 27 of 1959 Act, ss. 3, 4 and 6 of 1908 Act and ss. 3 (2) and (3) of 1987 Act and r. 11 (a) of TADA Rules - Designated Judge held that prosecution had failed to establish any of charges levelled against accused - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that evidence led in the case clearly established offences u/ss. 353 and 437, both r/w s. 34 of IPC and contrary finding recorded by Designated Judge was incorrect - Whether prosecution had established offences punishable u/ss. 353 and 437 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Held, on perusal of evidence of PWs 9, 13 and 22 closely on this point and SC found no reason to disbelieve the same - Reasons given by Designated Judge for not believing this part of prosecution evidence were not at all proper and sufficient - Therefore, SC hold that prosecution had satisfactorily established that accused had used criminal force against Indian Naval Officers while they were performing their duty and that was done with an intention to prevent or deter them from discharging their duty - Therefore, they were held guilty of having committed offence punishable u/s. 353 r/w s. 34 of IPC - In view of facts and circumstance of the case it could be reasonably inferred that accused, finding that it was no longer possible to avoid boarding of their vessel by Indian Naval Officers, thought it proper to destroy their ship in order to avoid detection of true state of affairs and consequential action - Hence, prosecution could be said to have satisfactorily established that accused had, in further of their common intention, destroyed their ship - Therefore, SC hold that accused thereby committed and offence punishable u/s. 438 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Appeal partly allowed.
