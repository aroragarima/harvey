Nathani Steels Limited v Associated Constructions

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2118; (1995) Supp3 SCC 324

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Contract - Construction of sheds - Non completion of work on or before due date - Arbitration clause in contract - Settlement of disputes by parties through settlement deed - Respondent found there was a calculation mistake in regard to amount in question on his part in settlement and thereupon invoked Arbitration clause - Held, once parties have arrived at a settlement in respect of any dispute or difference arising under a contract and that dispute or difference is amicably settled by way of a final settlement by and between parties unless that settlement is set aside in proper proceedings, arbitration clause cannot be invoked - Sanctity of contract, settlement also being a contract, would be wholly lost and it would be open to one party to take benefit under settlement and then to question same on ground of mistake without having settlement set aside - Appeal allowed.
