Devendra Pratap Narain Rai Sharma v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1962 SC 1334; 1962 ALJ 437; 1962 (1) ILR(All) 636; 1962 (1) LLJ 266; 1962 (2) SCJ 282; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 315; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 315

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Second Enquiry

Summary: Service - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 2,r. 2 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 132(1), 133(1)(b), 226, 310 and 311 - Whether State Govt. had power to reopen enquiry concluded by decision of High Court? - Held, appellant was not in earlier proceedings exonerated by H1C in respect of alleged misconduct charged against him, and, in any event, charge against him in second enquiry was different from charge in first enquiry - State has made a wholly unjustifiable claim to fix salary of a public servant wrongfully prevented from performing his duties, even after he is reinstated in consequence of a decision of civil court declaring his dismissal as wrongful - Appeal dismissed.
