Anant R. Kulkarni v Y. P. Education Society and others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 268; (2013) 6 SCC 515; AIR 2013 SC 2098; 2013(3) ALL MR 952; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 93; 2013 (138) FLR 168; 2014 (3) MahLJ 535; 2013 (4) MLJ 364; 2013(6) SCALE 425; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 1124; 2013 (5) SLR 20

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Employees of Private School Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Statutory Rules 1981 - Inquiry - After retirement - Sustainability - Appeal was preferred against the impugned judgment and order of HC in Letters Patent Appeal arising out of Writ Petition by way of which DB of HC upheld the judgment of Single Judge, as well as that of Tribunal quashing the enquiry against appellant, while giving liberty to respondent Nos.1 and 2 to hold fresh enquiry on the charges leveled against the appellant - Appellant contended that the charges were found to be vague, and that the enquiry was conducted in violation of Rules, and further that none of the charges reflected embezzlement or mis-appropriation, and cast no doubt upon the integrity of the appellant whatsoever - As the appellant stood retired on 30-9-2002, the question of holding fresh enquiry in 2011 could not arise - Court did not lack competence to decide the case on merits even if it comes to the conclusion that there was violation of statutory rules, principles of natural justice or the order also stood vitiated on some other technical ground - There was no statutory rule permitting the Management Committee to hold enquiry against person who had retired decade ago, particularly when the school was a government-aided school, and the appellant received pension from the State - Thus, the appeal deserved to be allowed - Held, there was no allegation of misappropriation/embezzlement or any charge which might cast doubt upon the integrity of the appellant, or further, anything which might indicate even the slightest moral turpitude on the part of the appellant - Charges related to accounts and to the discharge of his functions as the Headmaster of the school - Appellant had provided satisfactory explanation for each of the allegations leveled against him - Moreover, appellant had retired in the year 2002 - Question of holding any fresh enquiry on such vague charges was therefore, unwarranted and uncalled for - Appeal allowed.
