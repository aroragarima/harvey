Ram Pratap Chandel v Chaudhary Lajja Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2775; (1998) 8 SCC 564

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 79, 82, 99, 123(4) and 123(8) - Whether individuals after withdrawal of nominations can be impleaded as respondents? - It is clear that a candidate who is duly nominated continues to be a candidate for purposes of s. 82(b) in spite of his withdrawal and, if allegations of corrupt practice are made against him, he must be impleaded as a party to election petition or election petition must fail - S. 82(b) in clear, peremptory terms obligates an election petitioner to join as a respondent to his petition a candidate against whom allegations of corrupt practice are made - A respondent cannot by consent waive these provisions or condone them - Even inaction, laches or delay on part of respondent in pointing out defect of non-joinder cannot relieve court of its statutory obligation of dismissing such an election petition - Appeal dismissed.
