P.C. Chacko And Another v Chairman, Life Insurance Corporation of India and

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1150; (2008) 1 SCC 321; 2007 (4) ACC 773; AIR 2008 SC 424; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 30; 2008(1) ALL MR 408; 2008 (70) ALR 189; 2007 (7) AWC 7090; 2007 (1) CCC 634; 2008 (3) CPJ(Sc) 78; 2008 (1) CTC 152; 2008 (1) KLT 698; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1256; 2008 (149) PLR 257; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 126; 2007(13) SCALE 329; 2007 (8) SCJ 620; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 352

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uberrima Fides, Duty To Disclose Material Facts, Misstatement, Marine Insurance, Warranty Clause, Policy Holder, Utmost Good Faith, Policy Of Life Insurance, Statement Made In The Proposal For Insurance, Innocent Misrepresentation, Conditions Imposed, Life Corporation Of India, Policy-Holder, Repudiation Of Policy

Summary: Insurance Act, 1938 - Application of s. 45 - Suit for recovery of the amount of insurance - Held, it has not been shown in this case that repudiation of the contract of insurance was not done by the respondent with extreme care and caution or was otherwise invalid in law - This is not a case where the contract of insurance or a clause thereof is unreasonable, unfair or irrational which could make the court carried the bargaining powers of the contracting parties - It is also not the case of the appellants that in framing the aforesaid questionnaire in the application/proposal form, the respondents had acted unjustifiably or the conditions imposed are unconstitutional - Appeal dismissed.
