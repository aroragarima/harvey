K. Nanjappa (Dead) by Lrs. v R. A. Hameed alias Ameersab (Dead) by Lrs. and another

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 596; (2016) 1 SCC 762; AIR 2015 SC 3389; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 41; 2015 (113) ALR 307; 2015 (6) ALT(SC) 49; 2015 (5) AWC 4942; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 346; 2016 (4) CTC 76; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3974; 2016 (4) MahLJ 1; 2015 (6) MLJ 756; 2016 (3) MPLJ 1; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 259; 2016 (130) RD 278; 2015(9) SCALE 457; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 822; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 180

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable, Processes, Mysore Usurious Loans Act, 1923

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Specific performance - Discretion of Court - Plaintiff filed suit for specific performance alleging that defendant nos. 1 and 2 and another failed to execute sale deed in accordance with terms of agreement dt.2-9-1967 entered into by defendant no.1 for himself and on behalf of defendant no. 2 and another in respect of suit schedule item no.1 - Trial Court framed issues and held that relief for specific performance could not be granted - However, on appeal, HC re-appreciated evidence and concluded that findings recorded by Trial Court are perverse in law and set aside the same - Hence, instant appeal by legal representatives of defendant no. 1 - Whether judgment of HC is sustainable.

Held, in agreement dt.2-9-1967, there was reference of earlier agreement dt.29-11-1965 where under Rs.18,000 was paid to defendant which was denied and disputed but said agreement dt.29-11-1965 was neither filed nor exhibited to substantiate the case of plaintiff. HC relied on agreement dt.2-9-1967 because the same was produced before Magistrate in a criminal proceeding. However, HC is not correct in holding that there is no reason to disbelieve the execution of document although it was executed on a quarter sheet of paper and not on proper stamp and also written in a small letter. Further, HC also misdirected in law in holding that there was no need of plaintiff to have sought for the opinion of an expert regarding execution of the document. Further, even though various documents including order-sheets in the earlier proceedings including execution case were filed to nullify the claim of the plaintiff regarding possession of the suit property, yet, said documents have not been considered by HC. Furthermore, HC failed to consider scope of s. 20 of the Act and law laid down by SC. Hence, it is not fit case where the discretionary relief for specific performance is to be granted in favor of plaintiff. Hence, appeal is allowed and impugned judgment of HC is set aside and that of Trial court is restored and consequently suit dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Evidence and finding recorded by criminal courts in a criminal proceeding could not be the conclusive proof of existence of any fact, particularly, existence of agreement to grant a decree for specific performance without independent finding recorded by the Civil Court.
