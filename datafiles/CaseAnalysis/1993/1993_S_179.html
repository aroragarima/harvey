Royal Hatcheries Pvt. Limited v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1527; (1994) Supp1 SCC 429; AIR 1994 SC 666; 1994 (53) ECR 200; JT 1993 (6) SC 248; 1993(4) SCALE 147; 1994 (92) STC 239

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Live-stock Importation Act, 1898

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Rules, 1957, r. 5(1) - Exemption - Appellants sell day-old chicks to poultry farms - The Sales Tax authorities levied tax on the sales of day-old chicks effected by the appellants - Appellants contended that inasmuch as day-old chicks fall within cl. (xxvi) of r. 5(2) of Rules, tax must be levied at the purchase point and, therefore, no tax can be levied or collected from the appellants - Appellants' contention was rejected by the authorities whereupon they approached the HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, cl. (xxvi) of r. 5(2) of Rules does not cover all 'livestock'. It covers only that 'livestock' which answers the description "domestic animals". Chicks sold by the appellants are not included within cl. (xxvi) of r. 5(2) of the Rules. Appeals dismissed.
