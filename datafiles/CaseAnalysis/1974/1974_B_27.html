State of Punjab v Bhajan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 122; (1975) 4 SCC 472; (1975) SCC (Cr) 584; AIR 1975 SC 258; 1975 CRLJ 282; 1974 CrLR(SC) 595; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 747; 1974 UJ 597

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 and 24 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Murder - Lack of evidence - No eye-witness - Circumstantial evidence suffering from a number of infirmities - Doctor unable to find cause of death - (A) Whether accused can be made to suffer as a result of omission on part of investigating authority? - Doctor did not send dead bodies to an anatomy expert - Failure of doctor cannot be a ground for drawing an inference adverse to accused - Held, accused cannot be made to suffer because of that omission - Accused and not prosecution would be entitled to get benefit of any gap or lacuna in prosecution evidence - (B) Whether in an appeal against judgment of High Court would interfere appraisement of evidence? - Held, in an appeal against judgment of HC recording a finding of acquittal, Supreme Court does not interfere with appraisement of evidence by HC unless that appraisement be vitiated by some glaring infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
