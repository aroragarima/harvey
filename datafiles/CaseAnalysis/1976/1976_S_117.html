Ramaswami Ayyangar And Ors. v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 126; (1976) SCC (Cr) 518; (1976) 3 SCC 779; AIR 1976 SC 2027; 1976 CRLJ 1563; 1976 CrLR(SC) 227; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 147, 148 and 149 - Supreme Court(Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Code of Criminal Code, 1973, ss. 423 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Requisited for application of s. 34, IPC, 1860 - Interference by High Court in appeal against acquittal - Six accused u/ss. 147, 148 and 149, IPC, 1860 - Acquittal of three - (A) Whether A-2 was guilty of offence u/ss. 147, 148 and 149, IPC, 1860? - S. 34, IPC, 1860 implies simultaneous consensus of minds of persons participating in criminal action of bringing about a particular result - Held, on facts and circumstances of case that A-2 obviously was acting in concert with A-3 and A-4 in causing murder of deceased, when he prevented P W 1 from going to relief of deceased and s. 34 was therefore fully attracted and A-2 was equally responsible for murder of deceased, though he did not physically participate in fatal beating administered by A-4 to deceased - (B) Whether HC is justified in convicting accused? - Further held, where view taken by trial court was reasonably possible to be taken and reasons given by it for acquittal were not such as to justify an interference - HC was not justified in convicting accused - Appeal partly allowed.
