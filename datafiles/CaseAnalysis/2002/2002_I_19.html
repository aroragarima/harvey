Parveen Mehta v Inderjit Mehta

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1315; (2002) 5 SCC 706; AIR 2002 SC 2582; AIR 2002 SCW 2886; 2002 (5) ALD(SC) 6; 2002 (48) ALR 485; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 668; 2002 (2) JCC 1251; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 11; JT 2002 (5) SC 159; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 82; 2002 (132) PLR 492; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 529; 2002(5) SCALE 165; 2002 (4) SLT 381; 2002 (4) Supreme 596

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Appellant wife of respondent - Unable to consummate marriage as there was no co-operation from side of wife for sexual intercourse - Suffering from some ailment - Dissolution of marriage on ground of cruelty and desertion - What is meaning and import of expression cruelty as a matrimonial offence is core question on determination of which depends result and fate of this case? - Held, under statutory provision, cruelty includes both physical and mental cruelty - Legal conception of cruelty and kind of degree of cruelty necessary to amount to a matrimonial offence has not been defined under Act - Provision in cl. (i-a) of s. 13(1), which was introduced by Marriage Laws (Amendment) Act 68 of 1976, simply states that treated petitioner with cruelty - Object, it would seem, was to give a definition exclusive or inclusive, which will amply meet every particular act or conduct and not fail in some circumstances - By amendment legislature must, therefore, be understood to have left to courts to determine on facts and circumstances of each case whether conduct amounts to cruelty - Mental cruelty is a state of mind and feeling with one of spouses due to behaviour or behavioural pattern by other - Unlike case of physical cruelty, mental cruelty is difficult to establish by direct evidence - It is necessarily a matter of inference to be drawn from facts and circumstances of case - A feeling of anguish, disappointment and frustration in one spouse caused by conduct of other can only be appreciated on assessing attending facts and circumstances in which two partners of matrimonial life have been living - Inference has to be drawn from attending facts and circumstances taken cumulatively - In case of mental cruelty it will not be a correct approach to take an instance of misbehaviour in isolation and then pose question whether such behaviour is sufficient by itself to cause mental cruelty - Approach should be to take cumulative effect of facts and circumstances emerging from evidence on record and then draw a fair inference whether petitioner in divorce petition has been subjected to mental cruelty due to conduct of other - Right from beginning matrimonial relationship between parties was not normal, spouses stayed together at matrimonial home for a short period of about six months, respondent had been trying to persuade appellant and her parents to agree to go for proper medical treatment to improve her health so that parties may lead a normal sexual life, all such attempts proved futile - Appellant even refused to subject herself to medical test as advised by doctor - After 21-6-1987 she stayed away from matrimonial home and respondent was deprived of her company - In such circumstances, respondent who was enjoying normal health was likely to feel a sense of anguish and frustration in being deprived of normal cohabitation that every married person expects to enjoy and also social embarrassment due to behaviour of appellant - Further, conduct of appellant in approaching police complaining against her husband and his parents and in not accepting advice of superior judicial officer and taking a false plea in case that she had conceived but unfortunately there was miscarriage, are bound to cause a sense of mental depression in respondent - Cumulative effect of all these on mind of respondent, in our considered view, amounts to mental cruelty caused due to stubborn attitude and inexplicably unreasonable conduct of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
