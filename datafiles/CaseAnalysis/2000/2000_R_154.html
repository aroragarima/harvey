Rohtash Singh v Ramendri Smt. and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 124; (2000) 3 SCC 180; (2000) SCC (Cr) 597; AIR 2000 SC 952; 2000 ALJ 629; 2000 (39) ALR 113; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 236; 2000 (1) CCR 268; 2000 (1) Crimes 293; 2000 CRLJ 1498; 2000 (1) DMC 338; 2000 (1) JCC 309; JT 2000 (2) SC 553; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 286; 2000(2) SCALE 194; 2000 (2) SCJ 40; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 58; 2000 (2) SLT 385; 2000 (2) Supreme 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Maintenance allowance - Once decree for divorce has been passed in favor of wife on ground of desertion - She continues to be wife for claiming maintenance allowance u/s. 125, CrPC, 1973 unless she suffers from any of disabilities indicated in s. 125(4) - Plea of desertion by wife cannot come in aid of husband to allow him to refuse to pay her same - As a divorced woman, she is again entitled to claim maintenance from person of whom she was once wife, if she cannot maintain herself or remains unmarried - However, since decree of divorce was passed on ground of desertion by wife, she would not be entitled to maintenance for any period prior to passing of decree u/s. 13, HMA, 1955.
