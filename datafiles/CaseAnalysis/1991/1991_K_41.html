Khujji @ Surendra Tiwari v The State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Ramaswamy, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 695; (1991) 3 SCC 627; (1991) SCC (Cr) 916; AIR 1991 SC 1853; 1991 CRLJ 2653; JT 1991 (3) SC 151; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Chance Witness, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 313, 34 and 324 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Trial Court acquitted the co-accused but convicted the appellant u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 - High Court confirmed that conviction - Present appeal - Held, on an independent appreciation of the evidence of the three eye-witnesses come to the conclusion that several persons had participated in the commission of the crime - Failure on the part of the prosecution witnesses PWs 3 and 4 to identify the others does not alter the situation - Safe to confirm the conviction of the appellant with the aid of s. 34, IPC, 1860 - Cannot agree with the submission of the learned counsel for the appellant that at best the conviction can be recorded u/s. 324, IPC, 1860 - Appeal dismissed.
