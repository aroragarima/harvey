Santoshi Tel Utpadak Kendra v Deputy Commissioner of Sales Tax

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 240; (1981) SCC (Tax) 253; (1981) 3 SCC 466; AIR 1981 SC 1617; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1651; 1981(3) SCALE 1094; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 97; 1981 (48) STC 248; 1981 TAXLR 3018; 1981 UJ 810

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Appellate Jurisdiction, Revisional Jurisdiction

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, ss. 57, 57(1)(a), 57(1) - Assessment - Tax deduction - Revisional jurisdiction - Validity - Sales Tax Officer levied sales tax at Rs. 73,198.62 and a penalty of Rs. 36,197.64 on appellant/firm - On appeal, Commissioner reduced quantum of turnover and tax liability to Rs. 30,494.67 and penalty to Rs. 11,745.71 - During pendency of appeals before Tribunal, Commissioner issued two show cause notices to appellant stating why appellate orders passed by Commissioner should not be revised u/s. 57 of the Act - However, Tribunal dismissed appeals - Appellant filed writ petition in HC against order of Commissioner and Tribunal - HC, dismissed appeal holding that it was always open to Commissioner to interfere in revision with an order prejudicial to Revenue notwithstanding that such order might be already under appeal before Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Commissioner could revise u/s. 57(1)(a) of the Act, an appellate order passed by Commissioner when assessee's second appeal against that order was pending before Tribunal - Held, it was open to Revenue to invoke that power in a pending second appeal filed by dealer before Tribunal - Thus, HC was in error in concluding that power to enhance an assessment should be discovered only in revisional jurisdiction of Commissioner - However, compulsion which drove HC to construction placed by it on s. 57(1) of the Act did not have substance and entire sub-stratum underlying HC judgment should give way - Hence, absence of an express provision could not detract from conclusion reached by SC - Commissioner being a subordinate authority to Tribunal, should not interfere with an order pending in appeal before Tribunal - Further, that interest of Revenue was protected by power of enhancement vested in Tribunal while disposing of a second appeal filed by a dealer - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
