Chittaranjan Mirdha v Dulal Ghosh and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 722; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 303; (2009) 6 SCC 661; 2009 CRLJ 3430; JT 2009 (13) SC 198; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 613; 2009(8) SCALE 40; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 412

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Gunshot

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173(2), 173(8), 190(1)(b), 319 and 482 - Report of police officer on completion of investigation - After police report u/sub.s. (2) of 173, further investigation u/sub.s. (8) - Protest petition - Complaint filed by appellant, after incident of gunshot firing which injured appellant's son - Completion of investigation - Chargesheet filed - Taking of cognizance by Sub-Divisional Judicial Magistrate vide its order 31-08-2001, directing issuance of warrants - After expiry of 27 months application moved by appellant/complainant for direction u/s. 173(8) to cause further investigation - Vide its order D/- 27-02-2003 sought for direction issued and vide its order D/- 09-06-2005 warrant of arrest issued against respondent 1 - Aggrieved by such order revision application moved, which was rejected - Afterwards petition was moved u/s. 482 before High Court against the two orders - HC set aside order of cognizance and issued direction for considering relevant materials as well as the charge sheet which was filed initially - HC's order whether proper and justified? - Held, Yes - Deliberation entered into on the aspect of 'protest petition' and right of informant to get an opportunity of being heard in case after considering police report, Magistrate is not inclined to take cognizance of the offence and issue process - There is no provision in the Code to file a protest petition by the informant who lodged the first information report but such aspect was considered in 'Bhagwant Singh vs. Commissioner of Police (AIR 1985 SC 1285)' and such right was recognized in the case - Position settled, that the informant is entitled to a notice and an opportunity to be heard at the time of consideration of the report - In instant case no material placed which showed that by the order of HC appellant is being prejudiced; HC has directed that all procedural safeguards be followed; further it has also referred to applicability of s. 319 of CrPC where appropriate - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
