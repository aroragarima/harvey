S. N. Dube v N. B. Bhoir and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 560; (2000) 2 SCC 254; (2000) SCC (Cr) 343; AIR 2000 SC 776; AIR 2000 SCW 322; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 449; 2000 CRLJ 830; JT 2000 (1) SC 220; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 848; 2000(1) SCALE 175; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 200; 2000 (1) SLT 386; 2000 (1) Supreme 163

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(3) - 'Terrorist Acts' - Murder by firing shot from pistol - Confessional statements of accused that gangs of some groups had created terror in areas of their operation - Evidence of prosecution witnesses though not specific and sufficient to prove terrorist acts, providing corroboration to confessional statements of accused - Finding based on such evidence can be recorded that accused were engaged in committing terrorist acts and accordingly be convicted.
