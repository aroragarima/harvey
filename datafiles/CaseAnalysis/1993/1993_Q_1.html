Commissioner of Sales Tax v Qureshi Crucible Center

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 974; (1993) Supp3 SCC 495; AIR 1994 SC 25; 1993 (89) STC 467

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - U.P.Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 8 - Payment and recovery of tax - Respondent-assessee filed a return for the a/y 1975-76 - Goods in which he was dealing fell within the category of unspecified goods - For unspecified goods, the rate of tax was revised to 7% from December 1973 - However, respondent-assessee arrived at the tax admittedly payable on the turnover disclosed by him, by applying rate of 3.5% - Authorities held that in as much as he has not paid the tax admittedly payable within the meaning of s. 8(1) of the Act, he must be held to have failed to comply with the requirement of s. 8(1) of the Act and accordingly, interest as prescribed by the said section was levied - Appellate authority as well as the Tribunal affirmed the said levy - Revision filed thereagainst was allowed and it was held that the there has been no finding by the Tribunal that the assessee acted male fide in not depositing the tax at the rate on 7% and the demand of interest was not justified - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 8(1) of the Act does not say that the non-payment should be mala fide. This is a case where the dealer calculated the tax at an inapplicable rate. He did not and could not plead ignorance of the change in rate of tax effected two years earlier. In the circumstances, the concept of mala fides was not relevant in the context. Single Judge was not justified in deleting the interest levied by the authorities. Appeal allowed.
