Calcutta Guj. Education Society & Anr. v Calcutta Municipal Corporation & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 673; (2003) 10 SCC 533; AIR 2003 SC 4278; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 52; 2003(6) SCALE 802; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 915

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980

Summary: Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980 - ss. 170, 171, 171(2) - Property tax - Validity and proper interpretation of impugned provisions of Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980 contained in Part IV, Chapter XII - Appellants challenged the provisions of ss. 184, 189, 194, 230, 231 and 234A as unworkable, impractical and hence unconstitutional - Held, there is no vice in any of the provisions of the Act, although it has been considered necessary to interpret the provisions harmoniously for better application of the provisions of the Act and the Tenancy Act.
