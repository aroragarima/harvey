Â Ranjit Singh and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 168; AIR 1965 SC 632; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964 - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948 - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1954 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellants in other appeals are owners of lands in District Hissar - Proceedings for consolidation of holdings are going on in these villages under East Punjab Holdings (Consolidation and Prevention of Fragmentation) Act, 1948 - Whether distribution of shamlat lands was illegal and such lands, if they had to be redistributed, could only be distributed among proprietors but could not be given to non-proprietors and several provisions of law allowing this to be done are ultra vires - Held, High Court was also right in its view that proposed changes in shamlat deh and abadi deh were included in general scheme of planning of rural areas and productive utilisation of vacant and waste lands - Scheme of rural development today envisages not only equitable distribution of land so that there is no undue imbalance in society resulting in landless class on one hand and a concentration of land in hands of a few on other, but envisages also raising of economic standards and bettering rural health and social conditions - If agrarian reforms are to succeed, mere distribution of land to landless is not enough - There must be a proper planning of rural economy and conditions and a body like village Panchayat is best designed to promote rural welfare than individual owners of small portions of lands - Further, village Panchayat is an authority for purposes of Part III as was conceded before us and it has protection of art. 31-A because of this character even if taking over of shamlat deh amounts to acquisition - Four Acts, namely, Consolidation Act, Village Panchayat Act, Common Lands Regulation Act and Security of Tenure Act are a part of a general scheme of reforms and any modification of rights such as present had protection of art. 31-A - Appeals dismissed.
