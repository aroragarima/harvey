Manam Saraswathi Sampoorna Kalavathi And Ors. v The Manager Apsrtc, Tadepalligudem A.P. And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 219; (2010) 5 SCC 785; 2010 (2) ACC 776; 2010 ACJ 1245; AIR 2010 SC 1609; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 93; 2010 (80) ALR 246; 2010 (3) AWC 2511; 2010 (3) CTC 346; 2010 (126) FLR 59; JT 2010 (3) SC 335; 2010 (2) LLJ 604; 2010 (6) MLJ(SC) 108; 2010 (2) OLR 936; 2010 (159) PLR 294; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 547; 2010(3) SCALE 193; 2010 (2) UC 1038

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 166 and 173 - Accident due to rash and negligent driving - Compensation - Deceased died due to rash and negligent driving of the driver of the impugned vehicle - Petitioners filed joint claim petition for compensation before tribunal - Tribunal accepted the testimony of PW-2 - pillion rider and clearly found that the deceased died because of the rash and negligent act of the driver of the APSRTC bus - Tribunal also awarded compensation of Rs. 4 lakhs to petitioners towards loss of future earnings or loss of dependency plus Rs.2,000/- towards the funeral expenses - On appeal, HC discarded the evidence of PW-2 and held that there is no evidence that the deceased died because of serious injuries received due to rash and negligent driving of the driver of the APSRTC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, no - HC was totally unjustified in weaving out a new case which is not borne out from the evidence on record - Entire analysis of evidence by the HC is erroneous and faulty as HC on the one hand, came to the clear conclusion that the deceased did not die because of the rash and negligent act of the respondents and on the other hand, it awarded compensation of Rs.75,000/- - If the HC was clearly of the view that the deceased did not die because of the serious injuries sustained on account of rash and negligent act of the driver, then no compensation ought to have been awarded - Findings of the HC are totally contradictory and unsustainable - Impugned order of HC set aside - Judgement passed by tribunal restored - Appeal allowed.
