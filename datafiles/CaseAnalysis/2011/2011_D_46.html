Union Of India v Dhiraj (Dead) Through Lrs

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 270; (2011) 15 SCC 507; 2011 (86) ALR 448; 2011 (4) AWC 3623; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 265; 2011 (113) RD 421; 2011(4) SCALE 754

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Notification u/s. 4 of Act was issued proposing to acquire suit land for public purpose and declaration u/s. 6 of Act was also made - Land Acquisition Collector, after following due procedure of law, passed award - Dissatisfied with compensation awarded, application for reference u/s. 18 of Act was made by respondent landowners - Reference Court answered reference partially in favour of respondents and enhanced compensation - Still dissatisfied with compensation awarded, respondents preferred appeals before HC - HC, relied upon judgment of another DB of that Court in case of Anil Kumar Sharma v. Union of India, 2000 INDLAW DEL 1109, further enhanced compensation - Appellant State contended that HC judgment in the case of Anil Kumar Sharma was set aside by SC in the case of Delhi Development Authority v. Bali Ram Sharma, 2004 INDLAW SC 573 and compensation granted by HC in that case was reduced by SC - Whether HC was right in enhancing compensation by relying on Anil Kumar Sharma's case - Held, Anil Kumar Sharma's case was set aside by SC mainly for reason that HC had failed to notice SC judgment in Karan Singh v. Union of India, 1997 INDLAW SC 2989, wherein a part of land was acquired under same notification - Instant case was squarely covered by SC judgment in Bali Ram Sharma's case - Reasoning given by SC in cases of Karan Singh and Bali Ram Sharma were adopted in the instant case and thereby, impugned HC judgment was set aside - Compensation awarded, therefore, was reduced to the extent of amount determined in Bali Ram Sharma's case with 10% acceleration and other statutory benefits available to respondents under law - Appeal partly allowed.
