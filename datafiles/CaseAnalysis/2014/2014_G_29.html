Biswanath Ghosh (Dead) by Lrs. and others v Gobinda Ghosh Alias Gobindha Chandra Ghosh and others

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 169; (2014) 11 SCC 605; AIR 2014 SC 1582; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 32; 2014 (104) ALR 184; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 1; 2014 (3) AWC 2759; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 72; JT 2014 (4) SC 132; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2319; 2014 (1) OLR 1074; 2015 (177) PLR 138; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 407; 2014 (123) RD 675; 2014(3) SCALE 630; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 1097

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Performance Of Contract, Bengal Money-Lenders Act, 1940

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963-Section 16 (c)-Suit for specific performance of contract of reconveyance-Absence of specific words in plaint would not result in dismissal of suit if sufficient fact and evidence are brought on record to satisfy court readiness and willingness to perform his part of contract-From sequence of facts and events it can safely be inferred that plaintiffs-appellants were always ready and willing to discharge their obligation and perform their part of agreement-Sufficient compliance of requirements of Section 16 (c)-High Court committed error of law in setting aside judgment and decree of trial court and first appellate court-Impugned judgment of High Court-Unsustainable in law-Impugned judgment by High Court set aside-Judgment and decree of first appellate court confirming judgment and decree passed by Munsif-Restored.
