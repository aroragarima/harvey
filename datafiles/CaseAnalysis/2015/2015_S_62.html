Commissioner of Income Tax-19, Mumbai v Sarkar Builders

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 375; (2015) 7 SCC 579; [2015] 375 ITR 392; JT 2015 (6) SC 32; 2015 (4) MLJ 715; 2015(6) SCALE 463; 2016(6) SCALE 463; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 56; [2015] 232 TAXMAN 731

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: DC Rules

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act,1961, ss.80IB(10), 80IB(10)(d) - Deduction - Application of - Assessees-respondents had claimed benefit of s.80IB of the Act on ground that their cases were covered by s.80IB(10) of the Act - HCs held that these assessees would be entitled to deduction u/s.80IB(10) of the Act - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether s.80IB(10)(d) of the Act applies to housing project approved before 31-03-2005 but completed on or after 1-4-2005.

Held, the approval of the housing project, its scope, definition and conditions, all are decided. Provisions of s.80IB(10) of the Act mention not only a particular date before which such a housing project is to be approved by the local authority, even a date by which housing project is to be completed, is fixed. This planning, in the context of facts in these appeals, had to be much before 1-4-2005. The basic objective behind s.80IB(10) of the Act is to encourage developers to undertake housing projects for weaker section of the society, inasmuch as to qualify for deduction under this provision, it is an essential condition that the residential unit be constructed on a maximum built up area of 1000 sq.ft. A housing project includes shops and commercial establishments also. But from the day said provision was inserted, they wanted to limit the built up area of shops and establishments to 5% of the aggregate built up area or 2000 sq.ft., whichever is less. Therefore, in year 2010, the Parliament has further amended this provision by providing that it should not exceed 3% of the aggregate built up area of the housing project or 5000 sq.ft., whichever is higher. This is a significant modification making complete departure from the earlier yardstick. Therefore, HC take correct view that the assesees were entitled to the benefit of s.80IB(10) of the Act. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - In order to be entitled to have the deduction under s.80IB(10) of the Act provision, housing project is to be approved by a local authority.
