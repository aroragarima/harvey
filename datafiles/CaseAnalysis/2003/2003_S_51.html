Deva (Dead) Thr. L.Rs. v Sajjan Kumar (Dead) By L.Rs.

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 671; (2003) 7 SCC 481; AIR 2003 SC 3907; 2003 (53) ALR 163; 2003 (5) AWC 4488; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 28; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 109; 2004 (136) PLR 657; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 546; 2003(7) SCALE 31; 2003 (5) SLT 109; 2003 (6) Supreme 391; 2004 (1) UC 126

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - s. 100 - Adverse possession - Limitation - Suit dismissed by the lower courts on the ground of limitation - Judgment reversed by the High Court - Held, mere long possession of defendant for a period of more than 12 years without intention to possess the suit land adversely to the title of the plaintiff and to latter's knowledge cannot result in acquisition of title by the defendant to the encroached suit land - Further held, the limitation for such a suit is governed by art. 65, Limitation Act, 1963. The plaintiff's title over the encroached land could not get extinguished unless the defendant had prescribed title by remaining in adverse possession for a continuous period of 12 years.
