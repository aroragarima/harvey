Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab Jammu and Kashmir, Himachal v Punjab Distilling Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1964 SC 1709; [1964] 53 ITR 75; 1964 (2) SCJ 248; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Trade - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - (A) Whether 'empty bottles return security deposit' is a trading receipt? - Held, yes it is - Assessee - Distiller and seller of bottled country liquor, started collecting from its customers from year 1945 besides price of liquor and bottles in which liquor was sold, a further charge called 'empty bottles return security deposit' - (B) Whether provision creates an obligation on wholesaler to return bottles? - Further held, there was no right in distiller to return of bottles u/r. 40(14)(v)(f) of Act - In absence of a right in wholesaler to a return of bottles from retailer it would be insensible to read that provision as creating an obligation on wholesaler to return bottles - He had no means under rules to perform that obligation - That rule must be read as intending only to lay down that if wholesaler could not return bottles, his deposit was liable to be confiscated u/r. (vi) - Rules did not lay down any procedure by which distiller might enforce return of bottles to him - Rule by its own force did not compel a deposit to be made - Trade in liquor is largely controlled by Govt. regulations - Deposit was actually taken under a contract - Though contract was authorized by statutory rules - Appeal allowed.
