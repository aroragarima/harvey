National Insurance Co. Ltd. v V. Chinnamma & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 678; (2004) 8 SCC 697; 2004 (3) ACC 1; AIR 2004 SC 4338; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 57; 2004 (4) AWC 2961; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 50; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 199; JT 2004 (7) SC 167; 2004 (3) KLT 397; 2004(7) SCALE 366; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - s. 147(1) - Deceased used to carry on business in vegetables - Died when travelling in the trailer of tractor - Held, even if it be assumed that the trailer would answer the description of the 'goods carriage' as contained in s.2(14) of the Act, the case would be covered by the decisions of this Court in Asha Rani's case (2002 Indlaw SC 1460) and other decisions following the same, as the accident had taken place much prior to coming into force of 1994 amendment - Impugned judgment liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
