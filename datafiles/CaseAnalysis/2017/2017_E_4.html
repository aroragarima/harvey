Aravali Power Company Private Limited v ERA Infra Engineering Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 764

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Presumption, Arbitration Agreement, Jurisdiction, Contract, CONTRACT & COMMERCIAL, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Arbitration, To Perform, Arbitration and Conciliation (Amendment) Act, 2015

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 11(6), 11(6)(a), 12, 13, 14 - Arbitral award - Termination of mandate - Challenged - Appellants awarded construction work for Power Project to respondent - Disputes aroused between parties and retired judge of HC was appointed as Arbitrator who passed an award - Respondent filed petition u/s. 14 of the Act seeking termination of mandate of Arbitrator - HC by its judgment and order set aside appointment of Arbitrator holding that apprehension entertained by respondent was reasonable and directed appellant to suggest names of three panel Arbitrators from different departments to respondent - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in passing impugned order.

Held, exercise was undertaken by HC, "in order to make neutrality or to avoid doubt in the mind of petitioner" and ensure that justice should not only be done and also be seen to be done. In effect, HC applied principles of neutrality and impartiality which have been expanded by way of Amendment Act, even when no cause of action for exercise of power u/s. 11(6) of the Act had arisen. The procedure as laid down in unamended s. 12 of the Act mandated disclosure of circumstances likely to give rise to justifiable doubts as to independence and impartiality of Arbitrator. It was not the case of respondent that the provisions of s. 12 of the Act in unamended form stood violated on any count. In any case the provision contemplated clear and precise procedure under which arbitrator could be challenged and objections in that behalf u/s. 13 of the Act could be raised within prescribed time and in accordance with the procedure detailed therein. The record shows that no such challenge was raised within time and in terms of the procedure prescribed. As a matter of fact, respondent had participated in the arbitration and by its communication had sought extension of time to file its statement of claim. HC was clearly in error in exercising jurisdiction in instant case and it ought not to have interfered with the process and progress of arbitration. The arbitration, in pursuance of the appointment of Arbitrator should proceed in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.
