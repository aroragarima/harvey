Modern Steel Industries v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2012; (2001) 10 SCC 491

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Recovery certificate was challenged on two grounds - (1) connected additional load was reduced and (2) regn. 17 (ii) is ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution - Whether revenue recovery certificate issued by 1st respondent on behalf of State for recovering minimum guarantee charges as sought to be recovered by respondent Electricity Board from appellant Industry could be legally sustained or not? - Held, High Court in impugned judgment without addressing itself to vital questions has observed that appellant may seek remedy by way of arbitration before a person nominated by Chairman of Electricity Board - Main contentions canvassed by appellant before HC were required to be examined by HC and there was no question of asking appellant to go for arbitration, especially when question of vires of regn. 17(ii) was on anvil for scrutiny - Appeal allowed.
