Koopilan Uneen’S Daughter Pathumma & Ors. v Koopilan Uneen’S Son Kuntalan Kutty Dead By Lrs. & Ors,

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 229; (1981) 3 SCC 589; AIR 1981 SC 1683; 1981(32) SCALE 1240; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 183; 1981 UJ 594

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 21(1) - Preliminary decree - Redefined territorial limits of courts - Jurisdiction - Plaintiff filed suit for partition and Trial court passed preliminary decree for partition - Later on HC passed order redefining territorial limits of courts of which said Trial court was one - Plaintiff filed application praying that final decree in suit be passed which was allowed by Manjeri Court - Appeal was filed against said final decree order before appellate court which confirmed order of lower court - Further appeal was preferred before HC which upheld objection and ruled that Manjeri Court had no territorial jurisdiction and Parappanangadi Court had territorial jurisdiction to entertain application praying for final decree - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, provisions u/s. 21(1) of CPC made it imperative for the District Court and the HC not to entertain the objection whether or not it was otherwise well founded. Therefore, court refrain from going into the question of the correctness of finding arrived at by the HC that the Manjeri Court had territorial jurisdiction to take cognizance of the application praying for final decree. Thus, appeal is allowed and judgement of HC is set aside and remand the case back to it for deciding on merits the appeal which culminated in that judgment and as proceedings for the final decree have been pending since 1966, further direct that the HC shall decide the appeal last mentioned at the earliest possible and, in any case, within three months from the receipt of the records from this Court. Appeal allowed.
