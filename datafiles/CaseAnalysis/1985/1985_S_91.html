I.T.C. Ltd. Etc. v State Of Karnataka & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 531; (1985) Supp SCC 476; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Doctrine Of Pith And Substance, Tobacco Board Act, 1975, Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, Tobacco Board Rules, 1976, Kerala Abkari Act, 1967, Cardamom Act, 1965, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) (Amendment) Act, 1980, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) (Amendment) Act, 1982, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Amendment Act, 1973, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) Amendment Act, 1975, Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) (Second Amendment) Ordinance, 1979

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 245, 246, 254, 254(1) - Tobacco Board Act, 1975 - Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation and development) Act, 1966, ss. 158, 1, 63, 63(2), 65, 65(1), 65(2), 65(3), 134, 148, 148(1), 148(2)(xxxiii), 150 - Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation) (Amendment) Act, 1980, ss. 65(1), 19(1), 19(2), 2, 20, 3, 4, 42 - Market committee - Fees - Maintainability - State had levied fee on agricultural produce brought to market for sale in that State - S. 65(1) of the 1966 Act directed market committees in State to levy and collect market fee from buyers in respect of specified agricultural produce - Pursuant to S. 65(1) of the 1966 Act market fee appears to have been fixed by all market committees in State thirty paise per one hundred rupees of price paid to buyers - Writ petitions were filed challenging validity of s. 65(1) of the 1966 Act - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether Govt. was entitled to levy fee on goods or various products and sub-products of tobacco - Held, when Parliament took over tobacco industry without any pre-conditions or permutations and combinations and established Tobacco Board for regulating sale and purchase of tobacco under Entry 52 of List I entire field of tobacco industry was fully occupied and nothing remained for States to do, and thus neither doctrine of entrenchment nor that of pith and substance would have any application - (B) Whether State had any jurisdiction to encroach upon limits of Entry 52 of List I - Held, encroachment or entrenchment amounts to defeating very object sought to be sub served by Central legislation then Central legislation should prevail - Matter in dispute falls within four corners of Entry 52 of List I and Entries 28 and 66 of List II - Thus, when Central and State legislation cover same field then Central legislation would prevail - It was well settled that where two Acts, one passed by Parliament and other by State Legislature, collide and there was no question of harmonising them, then Central legislation should prevail - Hence, once Centre takes over an industry under Entry 52 of List I and passes an Act to regulate legislation, State ceases to have any jurisdiction to legislate in that field and if it did so, that legislation would be ultra vires powers of State Legislature - Art. 254(2) applies only to matters contained in Concurrent List and had nothing to do with matters enumerated in List I or List II - Thus, Act of 1980 would have absolutely no application to Entry 52 of List I which was fully occupied by Central Act of 1975 - Appeals disposed of.
