Ganapathy and Company, Bangalore v Commissioner, Income Tax Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 35; AIR 2016 SC 422; [2016] 381 ITR 363; JT 2016 (1) SC 250; 2016 (1) MLJ 555; 2016(1) SCALE 333; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 187; [2016] 237 TAXMAN 587

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Service Charges, Donation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 35(2) - Disallowance of service charges - Loss in film business - Donation to Ashram - On reference, HC rendered its opinion on disallowance of service charges paid to UTC, disallowance of loss in film business and claim for deduction in respect of donation to Ashram, against assessee and in favour of revenue - Hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether opinion rendered by HC under reference could be upheld.

Held, assessee failed to adduce evidence in support of services claimed to have been rendered to it by UTC during A/Y 1984-85. Hence, HC was justified in reversing eventual conclusion of Appellate Tribunal on the basis that findings and conclusions recorded in course of assessment proceedings of previous year could foreclose the findings that are required to be arrived at for the A/Y in question i.e., 1984-85. Further, having taken into regard facts and circumstances in which 'investment' was made and 'loss' claimed, view taken by HC with regard to losses in film business, that entire transaction was sham transaction and was calculated device to avoid tax liability could not be faulted with. Furthermore, assessee had not furnished necessary certificate showing that Aparna Ashram, donee, had complied with conditions subject to which registration was granted to it u/s. 35(2A) of the Act, and so, assessee is not entitled to claim deduction of donation made. Hence, view taken by HC in regard to disallowance of deduction of donation could not be faulted with. Further, by the impugned order, HC had not transgressed position of law in relation to reference jurisdiction as difference in approach between Tribunal and HC, is not one relating to determination of new or additional facts but was merely one of emphasis on facts on which there is no dispute. It was an exercise that was within the jurisdiction of HC in exercise of its reference power under the provisions of the Act as it then existed. Hence, view taken by HC is upheld. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - It is the Tribunal which is the final fact finding authority and it is beyond the power of HC in the exercise of its reference jurisdiction to reconsider such findings on a reappraisal of the evidence and materials on record unless a specific question with regard to an issue of fact being opposed to the weight of the materials on record is raised in the reference before HC.
