Jamaat-E-Islami Hind v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 542; (1995) 1 SCC 428; AIR 1995 SCW 8; 1995 ALJ 153; JT 1995 (1) SC 31; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 45; 1994(5) SCALE 107; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Deem, Constitution (Sixteenth Amendment) Act, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Administrative - Constitution (Sixteenth Amendment) Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 153-A and 153-B - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967, ss. 2(f) and (g), 3(1), 4, 5, 7, 8 and 9 - Exercising powers conferred u/s. 3(1) of Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, Central Govt. declared 'Jamaat-E-Islami Hind' to be an unlawful association and notification referred to Tribunal u/s. 4(1) for purpose of adjudicating whether or not there is sufficient cause for declaring Association unlawful - Tribunal confirmed the decision of Govt. - Correctness of declaration of Tribunal challenged - Held, material on which Tribunal can place reliance for deciding existence of sufficient cause to support declaration, must be of kind which is capable of judicial scrutiny - Tribunal has merely proceeded to accept version of Central Govt. without taking care to know even itself source from which it came or to assess credibility of version and not properly appreciate and fully comprehend its role in scheme of statute and nature of adjudication required to be made by it - Therefore order of Tribunal cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
