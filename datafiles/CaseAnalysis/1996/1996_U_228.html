Murari and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1594; (1997) 1 SCC 15; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 577; 1996 (4) CCC 95; 1997 (1) CLT 250; JT 1996 (9) SC 742; 1996(8) SCALE 105; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 313; 1996 (7) Supreme 712

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution (Forty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1978 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1967 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5 and 23 - Land acquisition proceedings - Legality - Appellants had challenged the acquisition proceedings in respect of their respective lands acquired under the 1894 Act - Whether the acquisition proceedings were illegal and unfair - Held, that in the case of an acquisition of large areas of land belonging to different persons, the specification of public purpose could only be with reference to the acquisition of the whole area for it could be difficult to specify the particular purpose for which each and every item of land comprised in the area was needed but unlike the case of an acquisition of a small area - 1894 Act being a pre constitution Act its provisions were not liable to be challenged on the ground that they were not in conformity with the requirement of art. 31(2) of the Constitution - Land owners, therefore, could not complain about the payment of compensation computing with reference to the market value of the land as on the date of notification u/ss. 5 and 23 of the 1894 Act could not be held to be bad, as such a challenge was precluded in view of art. 31(5) of the Constitution - Court further held that it was true that there could be no planned development of Delhi except in accordance with the provisions of 1957 Act after that Act came into force but there was no inhibition in the acquisition of land for planned development of Delhi under the 1894 Act before the master plan was ready - Appeal dismissed.
