State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Shardul Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 57; (1970) 1 SCC 108; 1970 (20) FLR 81; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 302; 1970 SLR 101

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 24 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Central Provinces and Bihar Police, regns. 228 and 229 - Scope of art. 311 - Whether High Court right in opining power of dismissal and removal referred to in art. 311(1) implies that authorities mentioned in that article must alone initiate and conduct disciplinary proceeding culminating in dismissal or removal of a delinquent officer? - Art. 311(1) provides that no person who is a member of civil service of union or of an All India Service or civil service of a state or holds civil post under union or state shall be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed - This Article does not in terms require that authority empowered under that provision to dismiss or remove an official, should itself initiate or conduct enquiry preceding dismissal or removal of officer or even that that enquiry should be done at its instance - Only right guaranteed to a civil servant tinder that provision is that he shall not be dismissed or removed by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed - Held, view taken by HC not corrrect - Appeal allowed.
