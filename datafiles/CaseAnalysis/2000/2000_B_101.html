Dena Bank v Bhikhabhai Prabhudas Parekh and Company

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 740; (2000) 5 SCC 694; AIR 2000 SC 3654; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 93; 2000 (1) BC 651; 2001 (2) CLC 118; [2001] 107 Comp Cas 157; 2001 (166) CTR 86; [2001] 247 ITR 165; JT 2000 (5) SC 307; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 729; 2000(4) SCALE 125; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 509; 2000 (120) STC 610

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Commercial Transaction, Interest And Penalty, Appeal By Special Leave, Karnataka Sales-Tax Act, 1964

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 13 and 15(2A) - Suit for recovery of money against firm and its partners by appellant bank - Claim of state that it's right to recover arrears of sales tax has precedence over right of bank - (A) Whether recovery of sales-tax dues (amounting to Crown debt) shall have precedence over right of bank to proceed against property of borrowers mortgaged in favour of bank? - Held, State shall have a preferential right to recover its dues over rights of appellant bank and property of partner shall also be liable to be proceeded against -(B) Whether property belonging to partners can be proceeded against recovery of dues on account of sales-tax assessed against partnership firm under provisions of Karnataka Sales-tax Act, 1957? - s. 188A of the IT Act, 1962 declares a partner and his legal representatives jointly and severally liable along with the firm to pay any tax, penalty or sum payable for the year in which he was a partner it was observed that s. 188A explicitly provides what was implicit hitherto - Held, in the case in hand the partners are being held liable by reason of s. 15(2A) of the Karnataka Sales-tax Act, 1957 - Appeal dismissed.
