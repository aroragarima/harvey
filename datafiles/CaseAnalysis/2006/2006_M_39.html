Union Bank of India and Others v M.T. Latheesh

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 792; (2006) 7 SCC 350; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1646; 2006 (6) AWC 6410; 2006(8) SCALE 145; 2006 (6) SCJ 510; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 21 - Whether respondent was entitled to be employed by appellant on compassionate grounds? - It is settled law that principles regarding compassionate appointment that compassionate appointment being an exception to general rule appointment has to be exercised only in warranting situations and circumstances existing in granting appointment and guiding factors should be financial condition of family - Held, respondent is not entitled to claim relief under new scheme because financial status of family is much above criterion fixed in new scheme - Appeal allowed.
