Om Hemrajani v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1015; (2005) SCC (Cr) 443; (2005) 1 SCC 617; AIR 2005 SC 392; [2005] 123 Comp Cas 234; 2005 CRLJ 665; JT 2004 (10) SC 185; 2005 (2) KarLJ 364; 2005 (1) KLT 111; 2005 (2) MahLJ 442; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 293; 2004(9) SCALE 655; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 365

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Non-bailable Warrant

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 177, 180, 181(1), 186, 187, 188 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 415, 417, 418, 420 - Interpretation of expression 'at which he may be found' in s.188 - On whom, u/s.188, does responsibility to find accused lies-the complainant Police or Court? - Held, if an accused person is found anywhere in India, offence can be inquired into and tried by any Court that may be approached by victim - Victim who has suffered at hands of accused on a foreign land can complain about offence to a Court, otherwise competent, which he may find convenient - Convenience is of victim and not that of accused - It is not requirement of s.188 that victim shall state in complaint as to which place accused may be found - It is possible for a complainant to file a complaint against an accused in any Court in country.
