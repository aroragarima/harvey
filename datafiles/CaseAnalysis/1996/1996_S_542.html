Puran Singh And Ors. v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1108; (1996) 2 SCC 205; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 639; AIR 1996 SC 1092; 1996 (27) ALR 217; 1996 (1) CCC 125; 1996 (1) CLT 376; JT 1996 (1) SC 362; 1996 (4) KarLJ 47; 1996 (1) RentLR 532; 1996(1) SCALE 380; 1996 (2) SCJ 16; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 730; 1996 (1) Supreme 430

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.226 and 227; Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s.141, Expln., o.22 - Writ proceedings - Death of respondent during pendency - Held, order 22 of C.P.C. not applicable - Petitioner have to substitute legal heirs of respondent within reasonable time - Delay in filing application for substitution - Does not result in automatic abatement of writ proceedings - High Court can consider on facts and circumstances of case for purpose of condoning delay - Power of Writ Courts not limited by provisions of C.P.C. in view of Section 141.
