Tamil Nadu Housing Board v The Service Society & Anr

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 352; (2011) 11 SCC 13; JT 2011 (7) SC 13; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 669; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 546; 2011(5) SCALE 231; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Fixation of final price - First respondent (Society) requested the state government (second respondent) to provide a Low Income Group housing scheme for the benefit of its members who were the employees of Tamil Nadu Electricity Board - State government directed the Tamil Nadu Housing Board, the appellant (Board) to execute the said scheme - Land was acquired for said purpose - Board formulated a scheme for development of the land and construction of 145 LIG Houses and 120 LIG flats therein - In pursuance of it, in the year 1976, the Board allotted to several members of the society, LIG Houses - Tentative allotment price was fixed by the Board as Rs.8,000 and each allottee was required to make an initial deposit of Rs. 3000 and pay the balance in agreed monthly instalments - Board also entered into a lease-cum-sale agreement in November 1977 with the allottee containing the terms and conditions of lease and the option for sale - Though clause 17 contemplated the final price being fixed within three years from the date of allotment, the Board did not fix the final price within that period - Board determined the final price only in the year 1988, nearly 12 years after the allotment and sent a demand letter informing the allottee that the final cost of the LIG House was Rs. 34,770 as against the tentative price of Rs.18,000 - Board also clarified that the increase in the cost was mainly on account of payment of increased compensation for the acquisition of land - Feeling aggrieved, the society, acting on behalf of its members who were the allottees of the LIG houses, filed an appeal before the state government challenging the said demand - Appeal was dismissed - Society thereafter filed writ petition - Single judge quashed the appellate order of the state government and the demand by the Board for increase in price - On appeal, DB held that the interests of justice would be met if each allottee was directed to pay an additional sum of Rs. 13,780 towards the increased cost of the plot and Rs. 5,148 towards interest in all Rs. 18,928 as on 30.11.1991 with further interest at 9% per annum - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether Board was barred from fixing the final price on the expiry of three years from the date of allotment, resulting in the tentative price become the final price? - Held, there was no term or provision in the contract that if the Board did not determine the final price within three years from the date of allotment, the Board would lose the right to determine the final price thereafter or that the tentative price would become the final price - (B) Even if the Board could fix the final price beyond three years, whether only the land cost component could be increased out of the tentative price and not the cost of development or cost of construction? - Held, where the final price had not been fixed, the Board could, after ascertainment of various costs, determine the final price even after three years, and the finality in regard to cost of development and amenities and the cost of construction, referred under clause 17, would not apply - As compensation in regard to the land was pending in courts and was finally determined in or about 1985 - Therefore it was not possible to say that when the final price was fixed in the year 1988, it could be only with reference to increase on account of land and not with reference to increase in the development cost or construction cost - (C) Whether the Board failed to justify the increase demanded by it? - Held, HC had not appreciated the controversy in the correct perspective nor decided the matter in issue - Finding of the Single Judge that the Board was not entitled to any increase was contrary to the terms of allotment - Letter of allotment and the lease-cum-sale agreement make it clear that the price mentioned in the letter of allotment was only tentative and final price was to be determined taking into account, the final cost of acquisition, cost of development and amenities, and cost of the building - Demand for increase in price on account of final cost made by the board but confirm that the interest payable on the increase should be only 9% per annum as directed by the HC upheld - Board would now calculate the amounts due accordingly and after giving credit to the amounts already paid, demand only the balance due - Appeals disposed of.
