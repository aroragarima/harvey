Fakir Mohd. (Dead) By Lrs v Sita Ram

Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19909; (2002) 1 SCC 741; AIR 2002 SC 433; 2002 (46) ALR 523; JT 2001 (10) SC 530; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 91; 2001(8) SCALE 466; 2001 (5) SCJ 352; 2001 (8) SLT 361; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 575; 2001 (8) Supreme 618

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955

Summary: Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, ss.13, 19A(3) - Rajasthan General Clauses Act, 1955, s. 30 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114(f) - Default in payment of rent - Suit for eviction - Validity of deposits - Requirement of notice in writing - Landlord owned house in which there were four shops on ground floor, one of which was in occupation of tenant on monthly rent - Suit was filed for seeking eviction of tenant for his failure to pay or tender amount of rent due from him but same was dismissed on account of tenant having earned protection from eviction by making payment/deposit u/s. 13(4) and (6) of 1950 Act - Once again tenant fell into arrears of and suit was filed on ground of second default and again tenant had deposited in Court 6 months' rent u/s. 19A of 1950 Act - Trial Court held deposits to be valid deposits u/s. 19A(3) of 1950 Act while First Appellate Court and HC held deposits not to be valid and hence tenant to be defaulter - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether deposits made by tenant were valid deposits and tenant had at all asked for particulars of bank account by giving notice in writing u/s. 19-A(3)(b) of 1950 Act - Held, s.19-A(3)(a) and (b) of1950 Act were separated by word 'or' which was disjunctive and failure of payment by any of two methods for fault of landlord would enable tenant to deposit rent in Court and such deposit could valid deposit so as to be deemed to be payment or tender of rent due within meaning of s.19A(4) of 1950 Act - It was well settled that 'and' was capable of being read as 'or', if context demands it to be so read - Rule of homogenous construction dictated said 'and' being read as 'or' failing which there could be apparent conflict between 19-A(3) (a) and (b) of 1950 Act - Thus, tenant's right to deposit rent due in Court u/s. s.19A(3) (c) of 1950 Act arose if such deposit was preceded by tenant having adopted one of two methods contemplated by s.19-A(3) (a) and (b) of 1950 Act - When notice in writing was to be sent by post and mode of service was not specified, s. 30 of 1955 Act came into play - Notice should be sent by properly addressing, pre-paying and posting same by registered post which tenant had failed to do - Thus, presumption u/s. 114(f) of 1872 Act would be safe to draw in favour of tenant and to hold that requisite notice was sent by tenant to landlord - Therefore, tenant had defaulted in payment of rent and thus ground for his eviction u/s. 13(1) (a) of 1950 Act was made out - Appeal dismissed.
