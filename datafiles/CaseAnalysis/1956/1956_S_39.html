Pershadi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1957 SC 211; 1957 CRLJ 328

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Appellant sentenced to transportation for life for offence of murder - Whether conviction of appellant sustainable? - Motive for appellant to commit the murder - Motive for appellant to commit murder - Recovery of deceased's clothes at instance of appellant - No evidence that appellant was seen mixing with deceased on the day he disappeared - Evidence of accused to police admissible in evidence - No reason for disbelieving Police Officer regarding statement made by appellant - Fact that appellant hid clothes of deceased clearly indicated his guilty knowledge and is consistent only with his having murdered deceased - Held, that circumstantial evidence is consistent only with guilt of accused and that it is inconsistent with any other rational explanation - Appellant rightly convicted - Appeal dismissed.
