Â Thakur Raghubir Singh and Others v State of Ajmer (Now Rajasthan) and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1959 SC 475; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 478; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 478

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Land & Property - Ajmer Abolition of Intermediaries and Reforms Act, 1955, ss.8 and 38 - Govt. of part C States Act, 1951 - Ajmer Tenancy and Land Records Act, 1950 - Constitution Of India(Seventh Amendment) Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - (A) Whether 8 and 38 of Abolition Act are invalid? - Held, s. 8 provides.for cancellation of certain leases granted on or after 01-06-1950, where lease is for a period of three years or more with respect to matters dealt with in cls.(a) and (b), where lease is for any period in respect of matters dealt with in cl.(c) - Argument is that there can be no retrospective cancellation of leases is untenable - Legislature was certainly competent, under entry 18 of List 11 of Seventh Schedule to Constitution relating to Land, to make this provision - It cannot be disputed that legislature has power in appropriate cases to pass even retrospective legislation - S. 38 is another ancillary section, like s. 8, and is meant to subserve purposes of Act, namely, abolition of all intermediaries and encouragement of self-cultivation of land - S. 38 is also protected u/art. 31-A(l)(a) of Constitution as an ancillary provision necessary for purposes of carrying out objects of Act - (B) Whether Ajmer legislature was not competent to pass Act? - Further held, s. 21 of Govt. of Part C States Act created a Legislative Assembly for Ajmer and gave that legislative assembly power to make laws for whole or any part of State with respect to any of matters enumerated in List II or List III of seventh Schedule to Constitution - Ajmer legislature was thus given power to pass laws with respect to acquisition of property for purposes other than those of Union - Act was passed by State legislature for acquiring estates within State and it could only have been for purposes of State - (C) Whether Act does not apply to jagirdars? - Further held, 'intermediary' as defined in s. 2 (viii) of Act is a holder of an estate and includes a jagirdar - U/s. 4 all estates held by intermediaries vest in State Govt. on issue of a notification - Therefore, if jagirdars are intermediaries, that is holders of estates, their estates will vest in State Govt. u/s. 4 of Act - Petitions dismissed.
