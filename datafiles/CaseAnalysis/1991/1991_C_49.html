Navnit Lal Sakar Lal v Commissioner Of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.D. Ojha, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 540; (1992) 1 SCC 185; AIR 1992 SC 466; 1991 (100) CTR 125; [1992] 193 ITR 16; JT 1991 (4) SC 186; 1991(2) SCALE 893; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 585; [1992] 63 TAXMAN 518; 1992 (2) TLR 226

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Residuary Legatee

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 168 - Income Tax - Executor - Assessment - Completion of administration of estate - Inference of - (A) Categorical finding by Tribunal as to distribution of assests among legatees on certain date - Correctness of finding not challenged - By raising specific question of law - Completion date - (B) Part of estate duty liability outstanding - Inference in question - (C) Movable and immovable properties of deceased to be divided into two equal shares as per will - Executor deriving share of deceased i.e. half share , in certain firm - Executor not discharging his duty of making arrangements regarding devolution of said half share by having two legatees taken in as partners in respect of one fourth share each in firm -

Held, (A) Administration cannot be considered to be complete before said date - (B) Not warranted - (C) Administration cannot be said to be complete - Appeal allowed.
