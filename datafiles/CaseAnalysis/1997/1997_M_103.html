Swastic Industries v Maharashtra State Electricity Board

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 288; (1997) 9 SCC 465; AIR 1997 SC 1101; 1997 (4) CLT 94; 1997 (1) CompLJ 353; 1997 (5) CTJ 97; JT 1997 (2) SC 328; 1997(1) SCALE 622; 1997 (1) SCJ 363; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 532; 1997 (2) Supreme 439

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Indirect Tax

Summary: Electricity Act, 1910, s. 24 - Disconnection of electric supply due to non - payment of charges by consumer - Does Electricity Board have right to discontinue supply without filing a suit? - Held, Electricity Board was right in exercising power of disconnection of supply when consumer had neglected to pay bill for additional sum without filing of suit to recover same.
