Bhawani Prasad Sonkar v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 174; (2011) 4 SCC 209; 2011(2) ALL MR 967; JT 2011 (3) SC 293; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 187; 2011(3) SCALE 513; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 630; 2011 (3) SLR 225

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compassionate appointment - Entitlement - Appellant's father was declared medically unfit as de-categorized employee - Standing Committee decided to retire appellant's father without offering any alternate employment and he retired from service - Appellant's father moved an application requesting compassionate appointment to appellant that was rejected by respondent authority - Appellant's application before Tribunal and subsequent writ petition before HC were dismissed - Whether appellant was not entitled to compassionate appointment on ground that he did not fulfil conditions envisaged in Railway Board Circular dated 29-11-2001 - Held, respondents had not placed any material on record to establish that appellant's father was offered any alternative employment in terms of circular dated 29-4-1999 - Having denied appellant's father benefit of circular dated 29-4-1999, respondents should not claim that circular dated 29-11-2001 was applicable to appellant's father, disentitling him from seeking employment on compassionate ground for his son as he was not totally incapacitated and had sought voluntary retirement - Retirement order clearly showed that appellant's father was retired from service pursuant to recommendation of Standing Committee, however, earlier circular dated 22-9-1995 that was in force in relevant time was applicable in instant case - Appellant would be entitled to employment on compassionate ground as circular dated 22-9-1995 contemplated compassionate employment for wards of those employees who had been medically de-categorized, and had retired, without being offered an alternative suitable job - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside and direction issued to respondents to grant employment on compassionate ground to appellant - Appeal allowed.
