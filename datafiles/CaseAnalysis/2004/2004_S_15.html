State of Madhya Pradesh v S. P. Sales Agencies and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1102; (2004) 4 SCC 448; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1313; AIR 2004 SC 2088; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 134; 2004 (2) CGLJ 41; 2004 CRLJ 1832; JT 2004 (4) SC 245; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 493; 2004(4) SCALE 52

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Forest Produce - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 2(4) and 52 - Madhya Pradesh Transit (Forest Produce) Rules, 1961, rr. 3 and 29 - Proceedings initiated against respondent Nos. 1 and 3 for violation of provisions of r. 3 of Transit Rules - Seizure of stock of kattha and cutch under provisions of s. 52 of Forest Act - Quashing of proceedings by High Court - Present appeal - (A) Whether 'kattha' and 'cutch' are forest produce within meaning of s. 2(4) of Act? - Held, cutch and kattha come within sweep of expression 'catechu' which has been enumerated in definition of forest produce, as such kattha and cutch are forest produce within meaning of s. 2(4) of Act - (B) Whether confiscation proceedings can be initiated u/s. 52 of Act only after launching of criminal prosecution or it is open to Forest Authorities upon seizure of forest produce to initiate both or either? - U/s. 52 of Act when a forest officer or a police officer has reasons to believe that a forest offence has been committed in respect of any forest produce, he may seize same whereupon confiscation proceeding can be initiated - In present case, allegations are that by committing breach of r. 3, a forest offence within meaning of s. 2(3) of Act has been committed for which a criminal prosecution u/r. 29 of Transit Rules as well as a confiscation proceeding u/s. 52 of Act could be initiated - From scheme of Act, it would appear that for contravention of r. 3, two independent actions are postulated, one criminal prosecution and other confiscation proceeding - Power of confiscation, exercisable u/s. 52 of Act, cannot be said to be in any manner dependant upon launching of criminal prosecution as it has nowhere been provided therein that forest produce seized can be confiscated only after criminal prosecution is launched, but condition precedent for initiating a confiscation proceeding is commission of forest offence, which, in case on hand, is alleged to have been committed - Appeals dismissed.
