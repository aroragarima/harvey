Â State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Dr. Vijay Anand Maharaj

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 1963 SC 946; 1962 ALJ 819; 1962 (2) ILR(All) 695; [1962] 45 ITR 414; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Income Tax act, 1948, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - U.P. Act XIV of 1956 - Whether an appeal lay against the order of Mehrotra J. rejecting the application for review filed by the appellants to a division bench of the High Court? - Held, the question of the fresh right conferred upon a party to the proceeding and the jurisdiction of the court to enforce the said right would be in issue and any decision thereon could legitimately be said to be a decision determining the rights of parties - But for the amending Act, the order of the HC admittedly would have become final, but, because of the amending Act there was a controversy whether the binding decision could be reopened and the rights of the parties decided in accordance with the amending Act - The decision of Mehrotra J. dismissing the application was certainly a decision denying the right of the appellants alleged to have been conferred under the amending Act - We, therefore, hold that the order of Mehrotra J. dismissing the application, filed for review of his earlier order, on the ground that s. 11 of the Act (U.P. Act XIV of 1956) did not confer any such right on the appellants was a "judgment" within the meaning of cl. 10 of the Letters Patent as well as Chapter VIII, r. 5 of the Rules of Court - If so, we must hold that the division bench of the HC went wrong in holding that no appeal lay against the order of Mehrotra J. - Even so, the appellants would not be entitled to succeed - It cannot be contended that a petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution, 1950, is a continuation of the proceedings under the Act - The application was filed only u/s. 11 of the Act and no attempt was made either before Mehrotra J. or before the division bench of the HC to ask for an amendment or to sustain the petition under Order XLVII of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 ; nor did the appellants raise this plea in the petition filed for special leave or even in the statement of case as originally filed by them - After the case was argued for sometime on an observation casually made by the Court, time was taken and for the first time this plea was taken in the additional statement of case filed by the appellants - This is, therefore, a highly belated attempt to convert the application filed on one basis into that on another - We hold that the order of the HC is correct - Appeal dismissed.
