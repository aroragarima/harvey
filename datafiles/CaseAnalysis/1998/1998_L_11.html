Yallappa Basappa Googyala 'D' by Lrs. v Land Tribunal, Bagalkot and others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2303; (2000) 10 SCC 569

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Agricultural Debtors Relief Act, 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Land Reforms Act 1961, s. 121A - Registration - Occupancy right - Appeal arose out of an order passed by the HC in a revision petition filed u/s. 121A of the Act, which in turn was directed against the order made by the appellate authority in a proceeding arising under the Act, for registration of the occupancy rights in respect of 4 acres 38 guntas - Hence instant appeal.

Held, inasmuch as the respondent had not filed the application for declaration before the Mamlatdar that the respondent was not a tenant within one year, after the Act coming into force and his possession is protected. These aspects were duly considered by the HC and rejected. It was noticed by the HC that the effect of this decision was that it is applicable only if the appellant was a tenant in respect of the land. The transaction between Chabbi and the respondent was one of mortgage without possession and hence no tenancy rights could be created. It is also on record that interest was paid by the respondent, which when allegedly was not paid, possession of land was taken by Chabbi. Since possession is not lawful the appellant having obtained possession in those circumstances cannot be treated as a tenant. The view of the HC is, therefore correct. Appeal dismissed.
