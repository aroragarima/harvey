State of Orissa v Dhaniram Luhar

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 94; (2004) 5 SCC 568; AIR 2004 SC 1794; 2004 (1) CCR 344; 2004 (2) Crimes 24; 2004 CRLJ 1385; 2004 (1) JCC 396; JT 2004 (2) SC 172; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 868; 2004(2) SCALE 202; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 68; 2004 (2) SLT 278; 2004 (1) Supreme 947

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land and Property - CrPC, 1973, s. 378(3) - Orissa Forest Act, 1972, s. 27(1)(a) - Leave to appeal against acquittal - Refused by HC - No reasons given - Held - Trial Court was required to carefully appraise the entire evidence and then come to a conclusion- Did not perform its duties - HC ought to have in such circumstances granted leave - Re-appreciated the entire evidence on the record independently and returned its findings objectively as regards guilt or otherwise of the accused - Important questions involved - HC directed to entertain appeal - Appeal allowed.
