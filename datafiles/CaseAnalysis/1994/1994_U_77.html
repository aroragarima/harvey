Sarwan Singh Lamba v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1393; (1994) 4 SCC 152; (1994) SCC (L&S) 919; 1994 (27) ATC 541; JT 1994 (3) SC 637; 1994 (2) LLN 386; 1994(2) SCALE 779; 1994 (2) SLR 657; 1994 (2) UJ 138; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 1362

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Constitution (Forty-Second Amendment) Act, 1976 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 323-A(1) and 323-A(2)(d) - Recruitment to Central Administrative Tribunal - Constitutional validity of provisions of Act was considered by a Constitution Bench - In S P Sampath Kumar v. Union of India - Divergent view - It is suggested that appointment of Vice Chairman and members should be made by a High Powered Committee in consultation with Chief Justice of India - It was clarified by Constitution Bench in order made in case of S P Sampath Kumar v. Union of India - Parliament chose to adopt mode suggested by Constitution Bench made earlier - In present case, High Court held that non compliance of direction made by Constitution Bench in Sampath Kumar Case renders selection void and illegal - Held, approval granted on administrative side by Chief Justice of India cannot be used to dilute or defeat a decision rendered on judicial side by Constitution Bench of Supreme Court - Matters referred for consideration by a Constitution Bench.
