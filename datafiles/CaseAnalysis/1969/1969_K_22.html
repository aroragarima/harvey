Kantilal Chandulal Mehta v State Of Maharashtra And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 204; (1969) 3 SCC 166; (1970) SCC (Cr) 19; AIR 1970 SC 359; 1970 CRLJ 510; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 742; 1970 UJ 23

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 231, 423(1)(d) and 535 - Constitution, art.136 - (A) Criminal Procedure - - Charge can be altered at appellate stage - Charge altered and case remanded for fresh argument - Accused given opportunity to adduce evidence - No new trial - No prejudice - Interference by Supreme Court, if any - (B) Appeal by special leave u/ art. 136 - No ground taken in special leave petition - If can be raised in arguments - SC held, will not interfere with judicial exercise of discretion - Cannot be allowed to be raised in argument - Appeal Dismissed.
