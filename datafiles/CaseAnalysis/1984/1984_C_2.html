Central Coal Fields Limited Etc. v Bhubaneswar Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 193; (1984) 4 SCC 429; AIR 1984 SC 1733; 1985 (1) CCC 149; 1984 (3) CompLJ 158; 1984(2) SCALE 299; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 618; 1985 UJ 49

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Coking Coal, Coking Coal Mines

Summary: Civil Procedure - Corporate - Mines & Minerals - Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 - Coking Coal Mines (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971 - The issue concerns the stock of coal raised between taking over of the Colliery for its management and its nationalisation - Whether that stock was liable to be taken into account for the purpose of determining the amount payable to the owner in respect of the period when the mine was under the management of the Custodian? - Under the Nationalisation Act the books had to be balanced with a view to finding out whether the Govt. company which was in management for the relevant period on behalf of the owner was to pay anything to the owner or the Govt. company having spent for the owner was entitled to recover any sum from the owner - Nationalisation Act contemplated a balance-sheet according to the commercial procedure to be drawn up which necessarily required stock in trade to be reflected - Submission advanced on behalf of the appellants is so much opposed to common sense logic of the matter that in the absence of a legislative mandate we have no hesitation in rejecting it - Much of the controversy could have been avoided if reference had been made to the statutory form - Statement 8 in the prescribed form clearly indicates that the stock as on 30-04-1972, had to be taken into account - HC omitted to make a reference to it, and are equally sorry to note that the Govt. companies have failed to do their duty as cast on them by law - Appeal dismissed.
