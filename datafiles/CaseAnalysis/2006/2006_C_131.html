Hindustan Poles Corporation v Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 181; (2006) 4 SCC 85; 133 ECR 190; 2006 (196) E.L.T. 400; JT 2006 (4) SC 185; 2006 (73) RLT 547; 2006(3) SCALE 601; 2006 (3) SCJ 645; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 461; 2006 (145) STC 625

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether process undertaken by appellants for bringing into existence resultant Stepped Transmission Poles amounts to manufacture under provisions of s. 2(f)? - Process carried out by appellants do not change basic identity or original character of M S Welded Pipes to make it a new marketable product leading to manufacture as defined u/s. 2(f) - Burden to prove manufacture is always on revenue - It is settled law that when one particular item is covered by one specified entry, then revenue is not permitted to travel to residuary entry - Held, activity of appellants of merely joining of three pipes, one with other, of different dimensions to obtain a desired length can by no stretch of imagination be brought within category of 'manufacture' - Appeal allowed.
