K.K. Sidharthan v T.P. Praveena Chandran and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2265; (1996) 6 SCC 369; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1340; [1996] 87 Comp Cas 685; 1996 (4) Crimes 102; JT 1996 (9) SC 191; 1996(7) SCALE 454; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 248; 1996 (7) Supreme 453

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Banking & Finance - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190 and 200 - Whether if cheque dishonoured for insufficient funds or excess amount gets attracted s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act? - Yes - S. 138 gets attracted in terms if cheque is dishonoured because of insufficient funds or where amount exceeds arrangement made with bank - Also attracted for 'stop payment' instruction to bank - Not attracted, if prior notice given to not to present cheque for encashment - Held, in present case, 'stop payment' instruction issued by appellant had become known to respondent - Complaint quashed - Appeal allowed.
