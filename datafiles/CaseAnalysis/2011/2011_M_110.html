CMD/Chairman, B. S. N. L. and another v Mishri Lal and another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 268; (2011) 14 SCC 739; 2011 (8) ADJ 39; 2011(3) ALL MR 896; 2011 (5) AWC 4471; 2011 (2) ESC 371; 2011(4) SCALE 659; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 317; 2011 (3) SLR 168

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Raj Bhasha Adhikari Recruitment Rules, 2005

Summary: (A) Service - Raj Bhasha Adhikari Recruitment Rules, 2005 - Respondents filed writ petition before HC seeking to quash 2005 Rules as well as letters by which they were told to appear in Limited Internal Competitive Examination for promotion to post of Raj Bhasha Adhikari AD(OL) and thereby restraining appellants authorities from interfering in working of respondents as AD(OL) on their respective posts and to continue to pay them their salaries - HC allowed writ petition - Whether allowing writ petition at preliminary stage of admission was proper - Held, when rules were challenged it was necessary to have matter gone into in depth by inviting a counter affidavit and examining matter in detail - A summary disposal of a writ petition by allowing it without even calling for a counter affidavit and quashing rules, was totally against any established procedure of law - Appeal allowed.

(B) Service - Raj Bhasha Adhikari Recruitment Rules, 2005 - Promotion - Denial - Legality - Whether respondents were entitled to be promoted without appearing Limited Internal Competitive Examination - Held, respondents were never regularly promoted as Hindi Officer at any point of time either under old Rules or new Rules - Respondents were not appointed on basis of recommendation of Departmental Promotion Committee duly approved by Union Public Service Commission - Respondents were appointed purely on a local officiating basis under powers delegated to Heads of Telecom Circles on basis of administrative instructions dated 28-4-1994,thus, they were never regular appointees and hence had no vested rights for promotion to post of Hindi Officer under erstwhile Rules (Recruitment Rules of 2002), which, in fact, were never in operation at any point of time - Further, when revised Recruitment Rules 2005 were formulated, 120 posts were classified as Executive, and for Executive cadre posts, mode of recruitment was changed and it was then to be filled up by a Limited Internal Competitive Examination - It could not be allowed to be filled up by promotion of persons working on officiating basis - A conscious decision was taken in 2005 providing that all posts in question should be filled up by Limited Internal Competitive Examination - HC could not have found fault with policy decision of appellants authority - Appeal allowed.

(C) Service - Constitution - Raj Bhasha Adhikari Recruitment Rules, 2005 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Whether an enactment made u/art. 309 of Constitution could be amended retrospectively - Held, a rule made under proviso to art. 309 was a legislative act (though made by executive) - It was not a piece of delegated legislation like a rule made under a statute, hence it could be amended retrospectively - When Rules were framed u/art. 309 of Constitution, no undertaking should be given to anybody and Rules could be changed at any time - Further, there was no question of equity in instant case since law prevailed over equity if there was a conflict - Equity could only supplement law, and not supplant it - Impugned HC judgement was set aside - Appeal allowed.
