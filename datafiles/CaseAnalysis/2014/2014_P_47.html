Donesh Rajput and others v Pradeep Kumar Shukla and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 907; (2015) 1 SCC 628; 2014 (6) AWC 6476; 2014 (143) FLR 1024; JT 2014 (11) SC 476; 2014(11) SCALE 495; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 617

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) (First Amendment) Rules, 2003, U.P. Procedure for Direct Recruitment of Group 'C' Posts (Outside the Purview of Public Service Commission) Rules, 2000, Uttar Pradesh Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980

Summary: (1) Employment - Correct number of vacancies available for the post of Pharmacist earlier required by Supreme Court to be furnished by Principal Secretary, Law, Government of U. P.-Petition filed for recall of order of Principal Secretary and fresh order to be passed by Principal Secretary after giving fresh notice to parties-State Government came forward with valid reasons as to why the correct number of vacancies is not reflected in the report of Principal Secretary-Held, that the State to be allowed to verify the available vacancies by calling for particulars from the department from all the Districts matching with the information available in the Headquarters-Further, the Government orders regarding the number of post sanctioned also to be considered.

(2) Employment - U. P. Pharmacists Service Rules, 1980-Rules 14 and 15-Appointment of pharmacists - Clarification/ modification of order dated 15.7.2014 passed by Supreme Court sought-Order in question stated existing vacancies shall be filled up by the State Government from those candidates whose application forms were received by the department pursuant to the advertisement of the year 2007 and who were found to be eligible-Earlier, the Supreme Court held that benefit to be extended to similarly placed persons-Held, that for a person to claim himself to be similarly placed, i.e., at par with the writ petitioners primarily he should have applied in response to the advertisement made on 12.11.2007-Any candidate who did not apply in response to advertisement dated 12.11.2007 claiming himself to be a member of the same batch cannot seek for consideration of his claim at par with the private respondents.

(3) Employment - Contempt petitions filed alleging failure of State Government to fill up the vacancies pursuant to orders passed by Supreme Court-Held, that the orders passed on issues raised before the court the allegation of contempt cannot survive.
