Apollo Tyres Limited v C. P. Sabastian

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1903; (2009) 14 SCC 360; 2009 (77) ALR 413; 2009 (4) AWC 3656; 2010 (1) LLJ 621; 2009(8) SCALE 748; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 336; 2010 (7) SLR 47

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Personal Service

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Jurisdiction of Industrial Tribunal - Plaintiff filed a suit for declaration that he is still a workman and continues to be a workman under Defendant and hence entitled to wages and all other consequential benefits of service from Defendant - Defendant contended that Civil Court has no jurisdiction in such matter - Trial Court allowed said application and dismissed suit filed by plaintiff - On appeal, First Appellate Authority held that Civil Court has jurisdiction to entertain dispute - HC confirmed order of First Appellate Authority - Hence, present appeal - Whether Civil Court has jurisdiction in service matters? - Held, power to enforce contracts of personal service, to create contracts, to change contracts etc. can only be done by Labour Court or Industrial Tribunal and cannot be done by a Civil Court - Reliefs claimed by plaintiff were clearly seeking enforcement of a contract of personal service and civil Court has no jurisdiction to grant such reliefs - Appeal allowed.
