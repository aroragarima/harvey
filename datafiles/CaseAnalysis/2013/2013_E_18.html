Commissioner of Income Tax v Excel Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, R.M. Lodha, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 688; (2014) 13 SCC 459; 2014 (309) E.L.T. 386; [2013] 358 ITR 295; 2013(12) SCALE 582; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 490; [2013] 219 TAXMAN 379

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 28 - Taxability of income - Assessee maintained its accounts on a mercantile basis - In its return (revised on 31-3-2003) the assessee claimed a deduction of Rs.12,57,525/ under the head advance licence benefit receivable - Assessee also claimed a deduction in respect of duty entitlement pass book benefit receivable amounting to Rs.4,46,46,976/ - These benefits related to entitlement to import duty free raw material under the relevant import and export policy by way of reduction from raw material consumption - It was submitted that the amounts were excluded from its total income since they could not be said to have accrued until imports were made and the raw material consumed - Assessing Officer did not accept the assessee's claim - On appeal, Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals) referred to an earlier appellate order in the case of the assessee and it was held that the advance licence benefit receivable amounting to Rs.12,57,525/ and duty entitlement pass book benefit of Rs.4,46,46,976/- ought not to be taxed in this year - Appeal was filed before Income Tax Appellate Tribunal (Tribunal), which was dismissed - Tribunal observed that in the assessee's own cases starting from the a/y 1992-93 onwards these issues had been consistently decided in its favour - By the impugned order, the HC declined to admit the appeal filed by the Revenue - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the benefit of an entitlement to make duty free imports of raw materials obtained by the assessee through advance licences and duty entitlement pass book issued against export obligations was income in the year in which the exports were made or in the year in which the duty free imports were made - Held, income accrued when it became due but it should also be accompanied by a corresponding liability of the other party to pay the amount - Only then could it be said that for the purposes of taxability that the income was not hypothetical and it really accrued to the assessee - In the instant case, no real income but only hypothetical income had accrued to the assessee and s. 28(iv) of the Act would be inapplicable to the facts and circumstances of the case - Also, in several a/yrs, the Revenue accepted the order of the Tribunal in favour of the assessee and did not pursue the matter any further but in respect of some a/yrs the matter was taken up in appeal before the HC but without any success - Furthermore, in the subsequent accounting year, the assessee did make imports and did derive benefits under the advance licence and the duty entitlement pass book and paid tax thereon - Therefore, it was not as if the Revenue had been deprived of any tax - Appeals dismissed.
