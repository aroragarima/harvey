State Of Himachal Pradesh v A Parent Of A Student Of Medical College. Simla And Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 286; (1985) 3 SCC 169; AIR 1985 SC 910; 1985 (11) ALR 487; 1985 (2) CCC 239; 1985 CRLR 496; 1985(1) SCALE 758; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Eductation - Practice and Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.32, 226 - Supreme Court Rules 1966, O. 8, r. 1 and O. 35, r. 7 - Ragging - PIL - Division Bench of the High Court treated the letter of the guardian of the student along with the letter addressed to the guardian by the student as constituting a memo of Writ Petition - Directions issued to Govt. for taking steps against ragging - Letters should not be placed on the record of the proceedings and the identity of the guardian and the student should not be disclosed - Legality challenged - Held, direction which has the effect of mandating Govt.to initiate legislation against ragging is illegal - Procedure of non-disclosure is illegal - Appeal allowed.
