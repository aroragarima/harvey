R. Vijayakumar and Others v Commissioner of Excise and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 487; (1994) Supp2 SCC 47; AIR 1994 SCW 181; 1994 (1) CCR 70; JT 1993 (6) SC 325; 1993 (2) KarLJ 952; 1994 (1) KLT 10; 1993(4) SCALE 386

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Socio-economic - Kerala Foreign Liquor Rules, 1974, s. 13(3) - Cancellation of licences for foreign liquor to appellants - On ground of violation of fundamental right to carry on trade in liquor - Held, the classification which can be sustained must have a reasonable nexus with objective sought to be achieved by the impugned action - The reason for not renewing the licence of the appellants was the prohibition policy of the state - This is a valid ground for reducing the number of licences in the state - On the one hand the Govt. is taking the realistic view by permitting import of arrack which is consumed more by common man and its quota in 1992-93 was one crore bulk litres and on the other cancelling licence of 21 persons in the entire State of Kerala who were granted licence for promoting tourism as it would help in achieving the prohibition policy - The appellants who were granted licence in 1992-93 and those who are granted licence and are operating from before are hoteliers and are required under rules to conform to two star hotel standard - Both are required to promote tourism - In all respects their licence are same - Further the State does not appear to follow a consistent and uniform policy - In June 1992 it announced its intention not to issue any licence, 'afresh' from 18-09-1991 but it did not adhere to it and within a month it issued another order in February 1992 deciding to grant the privilege of selling liquor for promotion of tourism - In November 1992 it decided to cancel all licences issued in current year - If the licences issued in 1993-94 to licences operating from before and to the appellants were issued afresh as the rules do not make distinction between renewal and fresh grant then all licences were on same footing and the attempt to pick and choose the appellants was contrary to rules without any valid justification - Appeal allowed.
