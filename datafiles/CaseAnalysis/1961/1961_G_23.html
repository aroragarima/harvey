Gouri Prasad Bagaria and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 287; [1961] 42 ITR 112

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Sale proceeds - Undisclosed source - Justifiability - ITO found an amount credited in name of assessee - Assessee stated that amount was sale proceeds of gold purchased by him and sold in A/Y - ITO treated amount as income from undisclosed source - Order of ITO was confirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Tribunal ordered amount to be excluded - HC held there was no material before Tribunal to base its decision - Whether there was any material before Tribunal to hold that assessee purchased gold, which he sold in the year under consideration and the amount credited into account of assessee represented sale proceeds of such gold - Held, when Tribunal believed assessee's statement, there was an end of matter, so far as fact was concerned - If finding was based upon statement which was good material on which it could be based, no question of law really arose - Court said if question was treated as one of law, still answer was that there was material, viz., statement of assessee, on which finding could be given - Answer given by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
