State of Punjab and others v Shreyans Indus Limited and etc.

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 194; (2016) 4 SCC 769; AIR 2016 SC 1185; 2016 (2) AWC 2065; JT 2016 (2) SC 624; 2016 (2) MLJ 548; 2016(3) SCALE 127; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 896; [2016] 91 VST 23

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Assessment Year, Review, Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 11, 11(10), 11(3), 133(2) - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 12(5), 12(6) - Failure to File Returns - Denial of Exercise of Powers Challenged - Respondent/assessee filed quarterly returns, however failed to file returns for AY's 2000-01, 2001-02, 2002-03 and 2003-04 with in stipulated time - Appellants/AO issued notice for said AY's after expiry of three years - Assessee approached Tribunal, which upheld assessment order of AO and HC in appeal by assessee held that once period of limitation for passing assessment order expires, Commissioner was debarred from exercising powers - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, power to extend time was to be necessarily exercised before normal expiry of said period of three years run out.

Held, the assessment order is to be normally passed within a period of three years. At the same time, power is given to the Commissioner u/s. 11(10) of the Act, to extend the said period of three years. Once such an extension is given, the order is passed even beyond the period of three years and significantly, no upper limit is fixed while giving such extension. Further, when the Commissioner passes such an order and give reasons, not only he would have to justify his action of extending time but also the period by which the time is extended. However, upon the lapse of the period of limitation prescribed, the right of the Department to assess an assessee gets extinguished and this extension confers a very valuable right on the assessee. In other words, a valuable right has accrued in favour of the assessee when the period of limitation expires. Thus, the only way to interpret the s. 11(10) of the Act, is that by holding that power to extend the time is to be exercised before the normal period of assessment expires. Hence, impugned order passed by HC, does not suffer from any error. Appeals dismissed.
