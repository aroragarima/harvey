RAMESH KUMAR v KESHO RAM

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1027; (1992) Supp2 SCC 623; AIR 1992 SC 700; AIR 1992 SCW 336

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 151, O. 41, r. 23, O. 6, r. 2 - Houses and Rents - Bona fide requirement - Proof based on subsequent events - (A) Whether Court can take them into consideration - (B) Subsequent events when present must be proved - Tenant's counsel failing to appear - Held, (A) Court can take into consideration if same has material bearing on relief - (B)There can be no hard and fast rule governing matter - Procedure is not to be burdened with technicalities - Case remanded to High Court - Matter remitted to HC.
