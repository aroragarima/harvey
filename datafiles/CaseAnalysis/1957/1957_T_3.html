Raja Ganga Pratap Singh v Allahabad Bank Limited,Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1958 SC 293; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 156; 1958 SCJ 431; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1150

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 113 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 228 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindar's Debt Reduction Act, 1952, s. 2(f) - (A) Whether severability of an impugned provision can be determined as a preliminary question of law without going into merits of case? - Held, no, question of severability arises only after impugned part has been declared as invalid - (B) Whether tribunals and other lower courts have power to decide constitutional validity of Acts? - Held, no, such power and obligation vests with High Court and Supreme Court only - Appeal allowed.
