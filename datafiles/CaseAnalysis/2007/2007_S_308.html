Harishchandra Prasad Mani & Others v State Of Jharkhand & Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 57; (2007) 15 SCC 494; AIR 2007 SC 1117; 2007 (3) ALT 226; 2007 (1) JCC 606; JT 2007 (3) SC 229; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 852; 2007(2) SCALE 370; 2007 (7) SCJ 401; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 26

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, s. 482 - FIR under ss. 302, 201, 328 and 120-B Indian Penal Code, 1860 against the appellants - Order taking cognizance of the offence and issuing summons to the accused-appellants - Held, there is not even an iota of material indicating the guilt of the accused persons - Cognizance cannot be taken merely on the basis of suspicion - Allegation that wife of deceased was having an affair with accused No. 2, is only a suspicion and cannot be the basis of a conviction - Since there is no material on basis of which cognizance was taken, quashed order taking cognizance of the offence - Appeal allowed.
