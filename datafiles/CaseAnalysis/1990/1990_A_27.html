Municipal Corporation Of Delhi v Ajanta Iron And Steel Co. (Pvt.) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 733; (1990) SCC (Cr) 370; (1990) 2 SCC 659; AIR 1990 SC 882; 1990 (1) CCC 624; 1990 CRLR 557; 1990 CrLR(SC) 557; 1990 (40) DLT 440; JT 1990 (2) SC 94; 1990(1) SCALE 409; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 733; 1990 (1) UJ 642

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Electricity Act, 1910

Summary: Electricity Act 1910, s. 39 - Electricity Rules, 1956, r. 138 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Disconnection of supply of electricity to consumer on allegation of theft which were denied by him and also no notice for disconnection was issued to him as contemplated by agreement - Held, question whether allegations were true or not could be decided in appropriate proceedings and until then restoration of electric supply was ordered as it would not prejudice appellant.
