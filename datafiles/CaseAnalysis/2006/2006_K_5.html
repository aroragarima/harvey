Chairman-Cm/w-M.D., T.N.C.S. Corporation Limited and Others v K. Meerabai

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 133; (2006) 2 SCC 255; AIR 2006 SC 3522; 2006 (1) CTC 348; JT 2006 (1) SC 444; 2006 (1) LLJ 826; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 2006(1) SCALE 546; 2006 (2) SCJ 403; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 540; 2006 (2) SLR 358; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 798

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Service - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 409 and 477-A - Constitution - Whether High Court has gravely erred in interfering with punishment awarded to respondent who was found in departmental enquiry guilty of misappropriation and other heinous malpractices? - Charged employee holds a position of trust where honesty and integrity are inbuilt requirements of functioning and, therefore, matter should be dealt with firmly with firm hands and not leniently - Held, in instant case, respondent deals with public money and engaged in financial transactions or acts in a fiduciary capacity and, therefore, highest degree of integrity and trustworthiness is must and unexceptionable - Judged in that background, conclusion of learned single judge as affirmed by division bench of HC do not appear to be proper - Restore order passed by disciplinary authorities upholding order of dismissal - Appeal allowed.
