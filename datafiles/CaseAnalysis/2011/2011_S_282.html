State Of Rajasthan v Shera Ram @ Vishnu Dutta

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 716; (2012) 1 SCC 602; AIR 2012 SC 1; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2012 (2) RLW 1144; 2011(13) SCALE 140; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 485

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 84, 302, 295 and 449 - Murder - Committed under insanity - Acquitted - Challenged - Respondent abruptly hurled a stone on head of 'X' resulting into his instantaneous death - Additional Judge charged respondent for committing an offence u/ss. 302, 295 and 449 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - On appeal, HC acquitted respondent on the ground that at the time of incident, he was a person of unsound mind within the meaning of s. 84 of IPC - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether respondent was entitled to the benefit of the general exception contained in s. 84, Chapter IV of the IPC? - Held, to commit a criminal offence, mens rea was taken to be an essential element of crime - Once, a person was found to be suffering from mental disorder or mental deficiency, which takes within its ambit hallucinations, dementia, loss of memory and self-control, at all relevant times by way of appropriate documentary and oral evidence, the person concerned would be entitled to seek resort to the general exceptions from criminal liability - In the instant case, oral and documentary evidence clearly shows that the respondent was suffering from epileptic attacks just prior to the incident - Immediately prior to the occurrence, he had behaved violently and had caused injuries to his own family members - Moreover, according to the statement of the doctor and the prescription, the respondent was suffering from Epilepsy and after the epileptic attack, a patient behaves like an insane person and he was unable to recognise even the known persons and relatives - Hence, impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
