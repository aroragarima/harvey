U.P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad and others v Om Prakash Sharma

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 251; (2013) 5 SCC 182; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 495; 2013 (5) AWC 4566; JT 2013 (5) SC 548; 2013(6) SCALE 202; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 199; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 1806

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: U. P. Avas Evam Vikas Parishad (Delegation of Powers by the Board and Housing Commissioner) Rules, 1968

Summary: EditorsNote: (A) Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, s. 12 - U.P. Avas Avam Vikas Parishad (Delegation of Powers by the Board and the Housing Commissioner) Rules, 1968 - Auction - Authority to accept bid - Appellant/defendant no. 1 on 4-3-1977 published in local newspapers for auction of 9shops on 4-3-1977 and a plot earmarked for Cinema Hall - It was case of respondent/plaintiff that reserved price of Cinema plot was fixed at Rs.1,80,200/- and auction of property was conducted on 11-3-1977 under supervision of one 'R', then Assistant Housing Commissioner of appellant/defendant no.1 - In auction, respondent/plaintiff offered highest bid of Rs.1,31,500/- and as per terms and conditions of auction, he had deposited Rs.26,300/- i.e. 20% of bid amount, plus Rs.500/- as earnest money - Respondent made representation to appellant asking for issuance of allotment in his favour - Asst. Commissioner informed respondent vide letters dt.26-5-1977 and 8-7-1977 that defendant no.3/Housing Commissioner had rejected bid amount deposited by respondent and the same was refunded by way of demand draft - Aggrieved by that respondent/plaintiff filed suit challenging the action of appellant/defendant no. 1 with regard to allotment of plot - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of respondent - On appeal, First Appellate Court allowed appeal and set aside decree of Trial Court - On appeal, HC allowed appeal and set aside judgment of First Appellate Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent/plaintiff proved that Assistant Housing Commissioner had authority to accept bid in relation to the plot in question which was put to auction and was empowered to allot plot in favour of respondent/plaintiff being highest bidder - Held, it was undisputed fact that public auction was held in relation to the property of defendant no. 1 vide public notice dt.4-3-1977 published in local newspapers for auction of 9shops and plot earmarked for cinema hall - Auction was conducted on 11-3-1977 and it was also admitted fact that respondent was highest bidder as he had quoted Rs.1,31,500/- in relation to plot and he had deposited sum of Rs.26,500/- that was 20% of the amount of bid plus Rs.500/- as earnest money - It was also undisputed fact that offer of respondent was highest as per the terms and conditions of sale of plot in question by public auction were concerned, 20% of bid amount deposited by him that by itself did not amount to accepting his bid by competent authority for grant of lease hold rights of plot in his favour - In absence of acceptance of bid offered by respondent to competent authority of defendant no.1, there was no concluded contract in respect of plot in question, which was evident from letters dt.26-5-1977 and 8-7-1977 wherein defendant no. 3 had rejected bid amount deposited by respondent and the same was refunded to him by way of demand draft, which was undisputed fact and it was also not his case that then Assistant Housing Commissioner who conducted public auction had accepted bid of respondent/plaintiff - Therefore, HC answered in favour of defendants - Appeals allowed.

(B) Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, s. 12 - U.P. Avas Avam Vikas Parishad (Delegation of Powers by the Board and the Housing Commissioner) Rules, 1968 - Institution of suit - Non-issuance of notice - Legality - Whether Trial Court was right in holding that non-issuance of notice to appellant/defendant no. 1 as provided u/s. 88(2) of the Act for institution of suit and not taking plea in this regard by defendant in initial stage rather taking plea subsequently amounted to a waiver of defence of defendants - Held, to institute suit against appellant/defendant no.1, respondent/plaintiff was required to issue notice u/s. 88(2) of the Act which was mandatory in law - Undisputedly, no such notice was issued to defendant no. 1 - Plea taken by plaintiff that defendants had waived their right in urging their plea that suit was not maintainable for non-issuance of notice u/s. 88(2) to defendant no. 1 for institution of suit by plaintiff was wholly untenable in law and finding recorded by Trial Court in judgment that defendants did not take this plea in its original written submissions was also wholly untenable in law - Also plea that after remand order said plea was taken belatedly by defendant no. 1, therefore, it had waived its right, was erroneous finding recorded by Trial Court - Said finding of Trial Court, which was accepted by HC also suffered from error in law - Maintainability of suit on the ground of non-issuance of a statutory notice to defendant no.1 prior to institution of suit was legal ground, which could be raised at any point of time, even in second appeal - Therefore, plaintiff had no right to institute a suit in absence of the notice u/s. 88(2) of the Act, which was mandatory in law - Appeals allowed.

(C) Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, s. 12 - U.P. Avas Avam Vikas Parishad (Delegation of Powers by the Board and the Housing Commissioner) Rules, 1968 - Absence of allotment letter - Maintainability of suit - Whether suit for declaratory relief on basis of cause of action as pleaded by plaintiff, in absence of allotment letter issued by competent authority in relation to plot in question as provided u/s. 12(1) of the Act, was maintainable - Held, it was an undisputed fact that final bid was not accepted by defendant no.3 - It was also an undisputed fact that Asst. commissioner did not accept bid of plaintiff in writing and communicated the same to him - Therefore, there was no concluded contract in favour of plaintiff in relation to offer made by him, whose offer was highest in public auction held on 11-3-1977 - Hence, suit filed by plaintiff seeking for declaratory relief as prayed in plaint was wholly misconceived and was not maintainable in law - Thus, order passed by HC was wholly unsustainable in law and was liable to be set aside - Further, there was no concluded contract in favour of plaintiff in respect of plot in question and plaintiff could not claim any legal right and question of enforcement of said right as provided u/s. 34 of Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Appeals allowed.

(D) Administrative - Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, s. 12 - U.P. Avas Avam Vikas Parishad (Delegation of Powers by the Board and the Housing Commissioner) Rules, 1968 - Highest bidder - Absence of concluded contract - Suit - Maintainability - Whether findings in second appeal were erroneous in law - Held, in view of the fact that no legal right accrued in plaintiff favour in absence of a concluded contract which was said to have existed by mere offering of highest bid in relation to property in question to obtain property on lease for a period of 90 years amounting to disposal of property of defendant no. 1 being authority u/art. 12 of the Constitution, no right was accrued upon bidder in relation to property in question - Therefore, suit itself was not maintainable and suit filed on basis of alleged cause of action did not arise - Hence, Trial Court could not have granted any relief by not framing relevant and proper issue and answering the same - This aspect of matter was not considered by Trial court - Therefore, impugned judgment was set aside by First Appellate Court - Orders passed by Trial Court and HC were totally erroneous both on facts and in law and therefore impugned judgment was set aside - Appeals allowed.
