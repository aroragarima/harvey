Burn and Company, Limited v Their Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 16; AIR 1959 SC 529; 1959 (1) LLJ 450

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fair Wage

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11 and 26 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Central Rules, 1946, r. 14 - (A) Whether habitual absentism amounts to gross violation of discipline? - Held, yes, even union secretary cannot be immune - Defaulter can be suspended - (B) Whether mere participation in illegal strike would justify punishment to a workman where there is absence of credible evidence against him? - Held, no - Mere participation is not punishable - (c) What is effect of suspension ?- Held, wages, allowances free medical aid and free medicines can be withheld - Appeal 1: Partly allowed; Appeal 2: dismissed.
