Padma v Hiralal Motilal Desarda and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1794; (2002) 7 SCC 564; AIR 2002 SC 3252; 2003 (3) Bom.C.R. 265; JT 2002 (7) SC 198; 2002(6) SCALE 383; 2002 (5) SLT 276; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 179; 2002 (6) Supreme 339

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Holiday, Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Whether order of High Court in PIL challenging procedure adopted by City and Industrial Development Corporation (CIDCO) for disposal of land by bulk sale, is valid? - Held, allotment for any price less than Rs. 1500/- per sq meter except by approval of Board of Directors of CIDCO or any other authority competent in this regard and except by affording others an opportunity of competing with Tapadia in matter of allotment of land at rate at which it was allotted to Tapadia cannot be sustained - Very sale of bulk land by an institution like CID CO is an anathema to its objective and purpose of its establishment - By no stretch of imagination it can be contemplated that it was or could have been intended either at time of acquisition or at time of sanction of development plans by state Govt. that at some point of time in future develop able land would be available to be disposed of by bulk sale and that too in favor of professional builders - If only proposal would have been placed before Board of CID CO in all probability it would have been discarded - Even if development plan for develop able land was not approved by state Govt. there is nothing which had prevented CID CO from carving out small middle level and larger plots which those who can afford would have certainly been prepared to take and build small, middle level or spacious houses or bungalows for their own residential requirements - On principle and in substance, order of HC is maintained - Impugned sales of land in favour of respondent nos. 5, 6, 9 and 15 are vitiated and hence not binding on CIDCO - Petition dismissed.
