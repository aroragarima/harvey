Indian Council For Enviro-Legal Action v Union Of India & Others

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 508; (2011) 8 SCC 161; 2012 (1) CompLJ 360; JT 2011 (8) SC 375; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 779; 2011(7) SCALE 768; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Keywords: Fertilizer, Heap, Comprehensive Response Compensation and Environmental Liability Act, 1980, U.K. Supreme Court Rules, 2009

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Socio-Economic - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Industries (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1974, ss. 24, 25, 26, 33, 33A, 43, 44 - Untreated toxic sludge - Causing pollution - Water was unfit for cattle to drink and irrigating land - Compensation for restoration of environment and ecology - Sustainability - Instant case was taken from judgment delivered on dt. 13-2-1996 in writ petition no. 967 of 1989, relied on in Indian Council for Enviro Legal Action v. Union of India & Others 1995 Indlaw SC 2354 - Held, State Pollution Control Board was directed to seal all factories, plants of respondent nos.4 to 8/chemical industries forthwith and State Board in compliance of aforesaid direction sealed plants of respondent nos.4 to 8 as directed by SC - Hence, instant case - Even after final judgment of SC, review petition was dismissed, thereafter, curative petition had also been dismissed in instant case, controversy between parties should come to an end at some stage and judgment of SC must be permitted to acquire finality, it would hardly be proper to permit parties to file application after application endlessly - Finality of judgment was absolutely imperative and great sanctity was attached to finality of judgment - Hence, instant applications - Plaintiff contended that permitting parties to reopen concluded judgments of SC by filing repeated IAs was clearly an abuse of process of law and would have far reaching adverse impact on administration of justice and applications were blatant disregard towards complying with the directions of SC and they have made mockery with environmental justice delivery system by filing these applications - Whether IAs of respondent nos. 4 to 8 were succeed - Held, applicants certainly could not be provided an entry by back door method and permit unsuccessful litigant to re agitate and reargue their cases and applicants have filed instant applications merely to avoid compliance of order of Court - Applicants had been successful in their endeavor and had not permitted judgment delivered on dt. 13-2-1996 to acquire finality till date - Hence, in view of legal position, how could final judgment of SC be reopened by merely filing interlocutory applications where all possible legal remedies have been fully exhausted, when revert to facts of this case, it becomes abundantly clear that SC delivered final judgment in this case way back in 1996 and said judgment had not been permitted to acquire finality because respondent nos. 4 to 8 had filed multiple IAs and has ensured non-compliance of judgment of SC - Hence, judgment of SC should not be disturbed particularly in a case where review and curative petitions have already been dismissed - Therefore, applicants/chemical industries had deliberately not complied with orders of SC since dt. 4-11-1997 and thousands of villagers hadbeen adversely affected because no effective remedial steps hadbeen taken so far - Hence, directed applicants to pay Rs. 37.385crores along with compound interest @12% per annum from dt. 4-11-1997 till amount was paid or recovered - Applications disposed of.
