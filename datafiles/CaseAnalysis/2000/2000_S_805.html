Ranadhir Basil v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 344; (2000) 3 SCC 161; (2000) SCC (Cr) 587; AIR 2000 SC 908; 2000 (3) CalLT(SC) 74; 2000 (1) CCR 195; 2000 (2) Crimes 41; 2000 CRLJ 14CrPC7; 2000 (1) JCC 205; JT 2000 (1) SC 618; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 761; 2000(1) SCALE 483; 2000 (2) SCJ 267; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 646; 2000 (2) SLT 75; 2000 (2) Supreme 534

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Cyanide

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, s. 306(4) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Whether person who is granted pardon must be examined in presence of accused and that accused has right to cross-examine in neither inquiry nor trial and whether inconsistencies/improvements in evidence of approver regarding events that took place over a period of three years are natural? - Plea was that approver should be examined as witness in open Court and not in chamber - Held, plea was not tenable and minor inconsistencies in evidence can be regarded as natural, but elaborate details are not improvements as it would create doubt regarding trustworthiness.
