Nasib Kaur And Ors. v Col. Surat Singh (Deceased) and another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 79; (2013) 5 SCC 218; 2013 (3) ALD(SC) 25; 2013 (97) ALR 722; 2013 (2) AWC 1098; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 235; 2013 (3) CTC 550; JT 2013 (3) SC 37; 2013 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 538; 2013 (5) MahLJ 24; 2013 (3) MLJ 104; 2013 (3) MPLJ 520; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 423; 2013 (119) RD 545; 2013(2) SCALE 409; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 984; 2013 (4) WLN(SC) 66

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Suit for injunction - Suit for declaration - Material evidence - Appeals - Maintainability - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration that the plaintiff was the owner and was in possession of suit land - Trial Court found that the areas of land sold under the sale deed dt. 17-7-1978 by plaintiff was less by 1 Biswas than the area in the mutation entries and similarly the area of land sold by plaintiff as Attorney of NS was less than the area shown in the mutation entries - Trial Court, therefore, ordered for correction of the mutation entries, but directed that the corrections to be carried out would have no effect as regards the possession of the suit property - On appeal filed by Col. SS Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - Col. SS. also filed a suit for permanent injunction - Trial Court dismissed the suit, on appeal filed by him was dismissed by Appellate Court - Both the parties filed appeals - HC allowed the appeals and set aside the judgments and decrees of the Trial Court and the First Appellate Court in the two suits and decreed the suit of the plaintiff for possession after declaring the plaintiff to be the owner of the said property - HC also held that plaintiff was entitled for relief of permanent injunction restraining defendants from raising any construction in the said property or alienating the said property - Hence instant appeals - Whether the Courts below have failed to consider the material evidence on record -

Held, in the suit for declaration of title, plaintiff was unable to produce any evidence to prove his ownership over and possession over the suit land - Moreover, in the suit for injunction, the First Appellate Court had held that plaintiff had admitted in plaint that Col. GS, the predecessor-in-interest of defendants, had purchased the land from him and NS while the same was joint and hence there was no necessity for framing the issue (issue No.2) that defendants were owners and were in possession of the suit land - Thus, appellants were in lawful possession of the said areas of land by virtue of the two sale deeds and plaintiff was not able to establish that he was the owner of the suit land and consequently he was entitled to declaration of his title, recovery of possession and injunction - Having framed the substantial question of law, the HC should have pointed out in the impugned judgment the material evidence which was not considered by First Appellate Court, which if considered, would have established ownership of the plaintiff to the suit property - Instead of pointing out the material evidence which was not considered by the First Appellate Court, the HC made its own assessment of the entire evidence as if it was the First Appellate Court and held that plaintiff was the owner of the suit property and was entitled to possession of 17 karams X 45 karams of land depicted in letters EHGF in the site plan and that he was also entitled to the relief of permanent injunction restraining plaintiff from raising any construction in the said property or alienating the said property - Appellants had taken possession of the land from Col. GS and his family members in 1987 - Appellants were, thus, in legal possession of the suit property and the HC in exercise of its powers u/s. 100 CPC could not have reversed the findings of the Trial Court and the First Appellate Court and decreed the suits for declaration of title and for recovery of possession and injunction in favour of respondents so as to adversely affect such legal possession of appellants - Impugned common judgment and decree of the HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
