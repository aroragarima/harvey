Hasan Ali Raihany v U. O. I and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1373; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 33; (2006) 3 SCC 705; AIR 2006 SC 1714; 2006 (63) ALR 332; 2006 CRLJ 2106; 2006 (128) DLT 759; 2006 (1) JCC 556; JT 2006 (3) SC 533; 2006(3) SCALE 293; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1158

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Foreigners - Constitution - Cancelling residence visa - Without information of order of deportation - Succeed - Petitioner was born in India to parents who were Iranian citizens, he educated in India and thereafter intended to stay in India and applied for grant of Indian Passport - But on night intervening 7-10-2005 petitioner was deported to Tehran - Hence, instant petition - Petitioner grievance was that he was never informed as to why such action was taken against him and he applied again to India Embassy in Tehran to grant him entry visa enabling him to enter this Country so that he could settle his pending matters in this Country - A Single Entry Permit was issued to petitioner by Indian Embassy in Iran which was valid for period from 8- 11- 2005 to 8- 2- 2006, on that basis, petitioner has re- entered this Country - Whether authorities intend to deport him again and if so, whether they were obliged to disclose to petitioner reasons for his proposed deportation - Relied on in National Human Rights Commission v. State of Arunachal Pradesh and Anr.,1996 Indlaw SC 1172 - Held, petitioner had entered this Country legally upon Single Entry Permit issued to him, it was only fair that competent authority should inform him reasons for his deportation, if such decision was taken, petitioner should be given an opportunity to submit his representation against his proposed expulsion and competent authority might thereafter consider his representation and pass appropriate order - Hence, had not so far noticed any fact which might provide compelling reason for State not to observe this procedure - Therefore, directions to competent authority, to communicate to petitioner reasons why he was sought to be deported from this Country and reasons disclosed should be sufficient to enable petitioner should be given 2 week's time to make representation which should be considered by competent authority as soon as possible - Petition disposed of.
