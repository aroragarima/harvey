Dr. Chiranji Lal (D) By Lrs v Hari Das (D) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 376; (2005) 10 SCC 746; AIR 2005 SC 2564; 2005 (60) ALR 166; 2005 (2) AWC 1793; 2005 (120) DLT 83; JT 2005 (5) SC 329; 2005 (2) KLT 1018; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 272; 2005 (99) RD 272; 2005(5) SCALE 247; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 45,136 - CPC, 1908, o. 20, r. 6A, o. 41, r. 1 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 2(14), 2(15), 33, 35 - Period of limitation for execution of a decree passed in a suit for partition - Whether period of limitation begins to run from date suit is decreed or from date when decree is actually drawn up and signed by judge? - Held, starting of period of limitation for execution of a partition decree cannot be made contingent upon engrossment of decree on stamp paper - Engrossment of decree on stamp paper would relate back to date of decree - Execution application filed was time barred having been filed beyond period of twelve years prescribed - Appeal allowed.
