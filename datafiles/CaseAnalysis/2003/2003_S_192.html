Smt. Amiya Bala Paul v Commissioner of Income Tax, Shillong

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 491; (2003) 6 SCC 342; AIR 2003 SC 2702; 2003 (182) CTR 489; [2003] 262 ITR 407; JT 2003 (5) SC 384; 2003(5) SCALE 122; 2003 (4) SLT 397; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 388; 2003 (4) Supreme 577; 176 TAXATION 221; [2003] 130 TAXMAN 511

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 55(A), 131(1), 131(1)(d), 133(6), 142(2) - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(r), 8, 12 A, 16 A, 16 A(3)-(6), 23 (ha), 23(3 A), 35(aaa), 37(1), 38 - Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1972, 55A - CPC, 1908, o.26, s.75 - Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, Tribunal erred in law by holding that Assessing Officer cannot refer matter to Valuation Cell for estimating cost of construction of house property - Held, a valuation Officer is appointed under Wealth Tax Act and can discharge functions within statutory limits under which he is appointed - He cannot be called upon nor would he have jurisdiction to give a report to Assessing Officer under Income Tax Act, except when a reference is made under and in terms of s 55 A or to a competent authority except u/s. 269 L - Further held, Tribunal had not erred in holding that Assessing Officer cannot refer matter to valuation Officer for estimating cost of construction of house property - Appeal allowed.
