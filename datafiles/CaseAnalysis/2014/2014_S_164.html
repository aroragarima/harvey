B. Jayaraj v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 204; (2014) 13 SCC 55; 2014 CRLJ 2433; 2014 (3) JCC 1552; JT 2014 (7) SC 381; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 358; 2014 (1) OLR 1014; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 410; 2014(4) SCALE 81; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d)(i)(ii), 13(2), 20, 7 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused/appellant u/ss. 13(1)(d)(i)(ii), 13(2), 20, 7 of the Act - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC affirmed conviction order passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Trial Court and HC was justified -

Held, mere possession and recovery of currency notes from accused without proof of demand would not bring home offence u/s. 7 of the Act - Above also would be conclusive in so far as offence u/s. 13(1)(d)(i)(ii) of the Act was concerned as in absence of any proof of demand for illegal gratification, use of corrupt or illegal means or abuse of position as a public servant to obtain any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage could not be held to be established - In so far as presumption permissible to be drawn u/s. 20 of the Act was concerned, such presumption could only be in respect of offence u/s. 7 of the Act and not offences u/s. 13(1)(d)(i)(ii) of the Act - It was only on proof of acceptance of illegal gratification that presumption could be drawn u/s. 20 of the Act that such gratification was received for doing or forbearing to do any official Act - Proof of acceptance of illegal gratification could follow only if there was proof of demand - As same was lacking in case primary facts on basis of which legal presumption u/s. 20 of the Act could be drawn were wholly absent - Court could not sustain conviction of appellant either u/s. 7 or u/s. 13(1)(d)(i)(ii) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - Conviction and sentences imposed on appellant by Trial Court as well as HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
