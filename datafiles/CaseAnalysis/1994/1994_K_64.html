Morgan Stanley Mutual Fund v Kartick Das

Supreme Court of India

20 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 342; (1994) 4 SCC 225; AIR 1994 SC 2; AIR 1994 SCW 2801; 1995 (1) CalLT(SC) 18; 1994 (2) CLC 1; [1994] 81 Comp Cas 318; 1994 (2) CPJ(Sc) 7; 1994 (2) CTJ 385; JT 1994 (3) SC 654; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 97; 1994(2) SCALE 1121; 1995 (1) ShimLC 329; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 136

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Securities Exchange Board of India; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Capital Issue (Control) Act, 1947, Capital Issues (Control) Act,1947

Summary: Corporate - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Companies Act, 1956 - Capital Issue (Control) Act, 1947 - Securities And Exchange Board of India Act, 1992 - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - (A) Whether a prospective investor could be a consumer within meaning of Consumer Protection Act, 1986? - Consumer as term implies is one who consumes - As per definition, consumer is tile one who purchases goods for private use or Consumption - Meaning of word 'consumer' is broadly stated in above definition so as to include anyone who consumes goods or services at end of chain of production - Comprehensive definition aims at covering every man who pays money as price or cost of goods and services - Consumer deserves to get what he pays for in real quantity and true quality - In every society, consumer remains center of gravity of all business and industrial activity - He needs protection from manufacturer, producer, supplier, wholesaler and retailer - Held, shares can never be called goods - There is no purchase of goods for a consideration nor again could he be called hirer of services of company for a consideration - (B) Why respondent chose to come at eleventh hour and where was need to pass an urgent order of injunction, are matters which are not discernible? - There is no power under Act to grant any interim relief of (sic or) even an ad interim relief - Only a final relief could be granted - If jurisdiction of Forum to grant relief is confined to four clauses mentioned u/s. 14, it passes our comprehension as to how an interim injunction could ever be granted disregarding even balance of convenience - C A No. 4584 of 1994 allowed.
