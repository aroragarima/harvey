Iti Limited v Siemens Public Communications Network Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1292; (2002) 5 SCC 510; (2002) SCC (L&S) 801; AIR 2002 SC 2308; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1391; 2002 (48) ALR 250; 2002 (47) ARBLR 246; 2002 (4) CompLJ 186; 2002 (94) FLR 618; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 104; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 466; 2002 LabIC 2624; 2002 (2) LLJ 1153; 2002 (3) LLN 746; 2002 (2) RAJ 380; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 745; 2002(4) SCALE 655; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1122; 2002 (5) SLR 277; 2002 (4) SLT 137; 2002 (4) Supreme 444

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Exercise Of Powers In Appeal

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 37(2)(b) - Code of Civil Procedure , 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 227 - (A) Whether a revision petition u/s. 115 of CPC lies to High Court as against an order made by a Civil Court in an appeal preferred u/s. 37 of Act if so, whether on facts and circumstances of this case, such a remedy by way of revision is an alternate and efficacious remedy? - Held, appellant should present a revision petition within 30 days from today, same will be entertained by HC without going into question of limitation - By inference it should be held that code is in applicable - An efficacious alternate remedy is available to petitioner by way of filing a revision in HC u/s. 115 of CPC, a second appeal against an appellate order is barred by provisions of sub-s. (3) of s. 37 - (B) Whether revision is not an efficacious alternate remedy because appeal involved a very sensitive issue pertaining to security of country and which, according to appellant, he requires extreme urgency and which will not be possible if appellant has to approach HC? - Arbitration proceedings have started as far back as in year 2001 and parties instead of getting arbitration concluded, have been litigating on interim applications till date - Further held, there is no fact of urgency - Order accordingly.
