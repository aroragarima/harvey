Popular Muthiah v State Represented By Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 283; (2006) 7 SCC 296; 2006 (2) JCC 1018; JT 2006 (6) SC 332; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 527; 2006(6) SCALE 417; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 100

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Abuse Of Process Of Law

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Jurisdiction - Statutory power - Investigating officer - Criminal appeal was brought to HC where a person was fatally attacked and thus died - Appellants were not parties to criminal appeal - FIR in respect of incident was lodged - No charge sheet was filed against appellant - Copy of charge sheet was not sent to first informant - Magistrate also did not inform first informant - In sessions court all witnesses took names of appellants as having played active roles in incidence and judge was neither called upon to exercise nor suo motu exercised jurisdiction u/s. 319 of CrPC - Accused was tried and convicted for commission of offence u/s. 302 of IPC - HC directed CB, CID to take up matter and reinvestigate and specified provisions under which appellant was to be charged - Appellants contended that HC wrongly exercised power in terms of s. 482 of CrPC, also, while exercising said jurisdiction HC could neither exercise revisional jurisdiction u/s. 397 nor inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482 of the CrPC - Whether inherent jurisdiction of HC was available while dealing with criminal appeal filed by accused - Held, HC was not correct in issuing direction to take advice of state public prosecutor as to under what s. appellant was to be charged or directing CB, CID to take up matter and reinvestigate and prosecute appellant - Such power did not come within purview of s. 482 of CrPC - HC went beyond its jurisdiction in directing prosecution of appellant - When power u/s. 173(8) of CrPC was exercised court should not have interfered with statutory power of investigating agency - HC could not issue directions to investigate case from particular angle or by particular agency - HC should have given opportunity of hearing to appellants - Judgment was set aside and matter was remitted to HC for fresh consideration - Appeals allowed.
