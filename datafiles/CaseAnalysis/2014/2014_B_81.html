Rishi Kiran Logistics Private Limted v Board of Trustees of Kandla Port Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 297; AIR 2014 SC 3358; JT 2014 (6) SC 575; 2014(6) SCALE 4; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 411

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Plots on lease, Prolonged time lag, Doctrine of promissory estoppels, Concluded contract

Summary: (A)Trusts and Associations - Plots on lease - Prolonged time lag - Validity - Respondent Port Trust have number of plots - Respondent decided in the year 2005, to allot these plots on leasehold basis for period of 30 years for purpose of enabling allottees thereof to put up construction of liquid storage tanks - It was stated that successful bidder was required to pay premium within period of 3 months from issuance of formal letter of allotment or CRZ clearance whichever was earlier - Appellant submitted its bid and prolonged time lag resulted in taking decision by respondents in form of Resolution No. 108, deciding to cancel tender process started in year 2005 - Appellants challenged validity of Resolution No. 108 of respondent and filed petitions before HC - Petitions were dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether decision contained in resolution no. 108 was arbitrary and malafide -

Held, since there was concluded contract between parties, cancellation of such contract amounted to arbitrariness - It should hardly be ground to test validity of decision in administrative law - Even if there was conclusion contract, mere termination thereof should not be dubbed as arbitrary - Concluded contract if terminated in bonafide manner, that might amount to breach of contract and certain consequences might follow thereupon under law of contract - However, on touch stone of parameters laid down in administrative law to adjudge decision as were arbitrary or not, when such decision was found to be bonafide and not actuated with arbitrariness, such contention in administrative law was not admissible namely how and why concluded contract was terminated - Appeal dismissed.

(B)Trusts and Associations - Plots on lease - Prolonged time lag - Doctrine of promissory estoppels - Whether doctrine of promissory estoppel applies -

Held, apart from paying EMD of Rs. 3 lakhs alongwith tender documents, appellant did nothing more and in fact, no occasion for same even occurred - Total premium amount in respect of each of three plots was not to be paid on issuance of said LOIs - Reason for this was that formal LOI or leased documents were to be executed only after CRZ clearance - For this reason it was specifically mentioned in the LOI itself that premium amounts were to be paid by appellant only after receipt of CRZ clearance in general and after issuance of allotment letter as well as individual CRZ clearance and on execution of these documents - Before these events could happen, respondent decided to cancel entire process - Thus, except making payment of Rs. 3 lakhs by way of earnest money appellants did not incur any other expenses or suffered any liabilities or took any steps to implement project of construction and maintenance of tanks - HC, therefore, rightly remarked that even if it was assumed that issuance of LOI tantamounted to promise given by respondent, appellants did not alter its position to its prejudice pursuant thereto to such extent which could inspire Court to take the decision that holding promissory to its representation was necessary to do justice between parties - Impugned decision of respondent was not arbitrary, unreasonable or malafide and further that doctrine of promissory estoppel had no application - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Trusts and Associations - Plots on lease - Prolonged time lag - Concluded contract - Whether there was concluded contract between the parties -

Held, there was no interference was required, case of appellant was that with issuance of LOI concluded contract was entered into - Appellant submitted that only CRZ clearance was required and even if LOI, which amounted to acceptance of author given by appellant in his bid was contingent based on CRZ clearance, even that clearance was granted by competent authority ultimately - Entire process was that said clearance was delayed by period of 5 years - Because of that neither any final LOI could be issued, nor possession of plots given or payments received - It was to be borne in mind that apart from general CRZ clearance, specific clearances on individual basis in this behalf were also to be obtained - Letter of intent was not intended to bind either party ultimately to enter into any contract - It was no doubt true that letter of intent might be construed as letter of acceptance if such intention was evident from its terms - It was not uncommon in contracts involving detailed procedure in order to save time to issue letter of intent communicating acceptance of offer and asking contractor to start work with stipulation that detailed contract would be drawn up later - If such letter was issued to contractor, though it might be termed as letter of intent it might amount to acceptance of offer resulting in concluded contract between parties - It appeared that a letter with intention to enter into contract which could take place after all other formalities were completed - However, when completion of these formalities had taken undue long time and prices of land, in interregnum, shot up sharply, respondent had right to cancel process which had not resulted in concluded contract - Appeal dismissed.
