Sait Tarajee Khimchand and Others v Yelamarti Satyam Alias Satteyya and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 653; (1972) 4 SCC 562; AIR 1971 SC 1865; 1971 UJ 644

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Mortgage, Suit of recovery

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Suit of recovery - Mortgage - Legality - Appellants (plaintiffs) was a registered firm filed a suit for recovery on the basis of mortgage bond executed by defendant no. 1 and his sons defendants nos. 2 to 4 in favour of appellant/firm - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal, HC by impugned judgment allowed appeal by decree by giving defendants credit for payment of Rs.19,000 towards mortgage bond - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it is established beyond any doubt that the plaintiff obliterated the writing made by the defendant no. 1 and thereon brought into existence the writing in Marwari script. The defendant no. 1' evidence that Rs.19,000 was paid on 31-12-1953 is proved first by oral evidence, secondly contemporaneous books of account, and, thirdly, the obliteration of the writing, which lends direct support to the defendant no. 1's evidence that he made an endorsement for payment of Rs.19,000 on 31-12-1953. The evidence of the defendants is correct that Rs.19,000 was paid on 31-121953. It will follow that there could not therefore be an endorsement on 12-1-1955, for acknowledgment of liability for Rs. 26,000. The endorsement on 12-1-1955, contains intrinsic evidence to nullify the plaintiffs' version that the defendants acknowledged liability for Rs.26,000. On behalf of the plaintiffs there were three witnesses. The two attestors deposed that when the endorsement was made it was for Rs.26,000 and there was a vertical line immediately after the figures Rs.26,000 and that there were no dots in between the figures. If that were so the amount in figures would be Rs.26,000. On the contrary, the defendant's version is that the amount was Rs.2,600. A question would arise as to whether there was any mutilation of the digits. No such case is found in the pleadings. Even after inspection of the documents by the parties, the plaintiffs did not allege that it was tampered by the defendants. The document was all along in the possession of the plaintiffs. When the document was produced in court it was the plaintiffs' case that the figures were Rs.26,000. The HC rightly upheld the contentions of defendant, that the mortgage decree would be modified by giving the defendant's credit for payment of Rs.19,000 paid on 31-12-1953. Appeal dismissed.
