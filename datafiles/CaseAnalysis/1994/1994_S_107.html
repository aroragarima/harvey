State Of Maharashtra v Sharadchandra Vinayak Dongre

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh, Mr.Justice R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 829; (1995) SCC (Cr) 16; (1995) 1 SCC 42; 1995 (32) ACC 25; AIR 1995 SC 231; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 4; 1994 (4) CCR 775; 1995 CRLR 114; JT 1994 (6) SC 562; 1995 (1) MahLJ 432; 1994(4) SCALE 438; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 378

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Charge - Sheet

Summary: Criminal - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Criminal Procedure Code , 1973 , ss. 173, 190(1)(b), 468 and 482 - Raids in a company on basis that offences had been committed by respondent relating to manufacture and selling of beer without payment of excise duty - (A) Cognizance on police report - Whether power of Magistrate to take cognizance is lost when police report is termed as 'incomplete' by investigating officer? - (B) Whether delay in launching prosecution can be condoned? - Held, (A) Magistrate is not bound by label put on report/charge-sheet given by investigating officer and power to cognizance is not lost - (B) Delay cannot be condoned without notice to accused - Appeal allowed.
