Â State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Pawan Kumar Divedi and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, J. Chelameswar, R.F. Nariman, J. S. KHEHAR, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 573; (2014) 9 SCC 692; 2014 (106) ALR 805; 2014 (6) AWC 6276; JT 2014 (10) SC 190; 2014 (6) MLJ 739; 2014(10) SCALE 27; 2014 (6) SLR 34; 2015 (2) UPLBEC 890

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Salaries, Eligibility

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972 - Uttar Pradesh Junior High Schools (Payment of Salaries of Teachers and Other Employees) Act, 1978, ss. 10, 2(j) - Salaries - Eligibility - Respondents appellants got permission from District Inspector of Schools to management to run Classes I to VIII - Respondents filed petition before HC seeking direction for payment of salary and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeals - Whether teachers of privately managed primary schools and primary sections of privately managed high schools are eligible to receive their salaries from the State Govt. -

Held, recognized Junior High Schools can be of three kinds: (one) having Classes I to VIII (two) a school as above and upgraded to High School or intermediate standard and (three) Classes VI to VIII (Senior Basic School) initially with no Junior Basic School (Classes I to V) being part of the said school. First two categories of Junior High Schools, applicability of s. 10 of 1978 Act does not create any difficulty. Debate which has centered round in this group of appeals is in respect of third category of the schools where Classes I to V are added after obtaining recognition to schools which are recognized and aided for imparting education in Classes VI to VIII. Whether teachers of primary section Classes I to V in such schools are entitled to the benefit of s. 10 of 1978 Act is the moot question. Constitutional obligation of state to provide for free and compulsory education of children till they complete age of 14 years is beyond doubt now. If Junior Basic School (Classes I to V) is added after obtaining necessary recognition to recognized and aided Senior Basic School (Classes VI to VIII), then surely such Junior Basic School becomes integral part of one school, i.e., Basic School having Classes I to VIII. It is not appropriate to give narrow meaning to expression 'Junior High School' as contended by appellant/state. Legislature used the expression Junior High School and not the Basic School as used and defined in 1972 Act, is insignificant. View, which has taken, is fortified by fact that in s. 2(j) of 1978 Act, the expressions defined in 1972 Act are incorporated. Merit in argument of 'R' that the schools having the Junior Basic Schools and the Senior Basic Schools either separately or together are under same Board, i.e., the Board of Basic Education, as per 1972 Act. Junior High School necessarily includes Classes I to V when they are opened in a Senior Basic School (Classes VI to VIII) after obtaining separate recognition and for which there may not be a separate order of grant-in-aid by the Government. SC affirmed view taken by three-Judge Bench in 1998 Indlaw SC 1561. Appeals dismissed.
