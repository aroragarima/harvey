Dwarkanath, Hindu Undivided Family v Income Tax Officer, Special Circle, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1966 SC 81; 1965 (2) ITJ 218; [1965] 57 ITR 349; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12B, 33-A and 29 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income Tax Officer (ITO) determined the assessee's (HUF) income as capital gains u/s. 12B of Act, 1922 - Assessee raised an objection that the said income was not liable to be taxed u/s. 12B of the Act under the head 'capital gains' and that in any case in order to determine the amount of capital gains the market value of the shares only should be taken into consideration - ITO rejected the said contentions of the assessee and redetermined the assessable income of the assessee under the heading 'capital gains' but did not issue a demand notice as prescribed in s. 29 of the Act - Assessee filed petition before the Commissioner u/s. 33A(2) of the Act for revising the computation made by the ITO relying on HC decision in Baijnath Chaturbhuj and Another vs. CIT, Bombay 1956 INDLAW MUM 18 - Commissioner dismissed said petition as not maintainable as well as on merits ignoring the HC decision - Meanwhile, assessee filed an application requesting the ITO to issue a demand notice u/s. 29 of the Act to enable him to file an appeal but ITO declined to do so - Assessee filed writ petition for issuing appropriate writs to the Commissioner and ITO but HC dismissed it in limine - Assessee filed appeal against said order of the HC - Department raised a preliminary objection that as the order of the Commissioner was an administrative act, Art. 226 of Constitution could not be invoked - (A) Whether order of Commissioner u/s. 33A of the Act was an administrative act and, therefore limits High Court's jurisdiction u/art. 226? - Held, art. 226 was couched in comprehensive phraseology and it exfacie confers a wide power on HC to reach injustice wherever it was found, even from a quasi-judicial body - Constitution designedly used a wide language in describing nature of power, purpose for which and person or authority against whom it can be exercised - Hence, in the instant case, assessee rightly invoked art. 226 of Constitution - (B) Whether revision lies to Commissioner u/s. 33-A(2) of Act against order of ITO ? - Held, a revision does not lie to Commissioner against an order where an appeal lies to Appellate Asst. Commissioner but has not been made and time has not expired or where an appeal against order has been made and was pending - If no appeal lies against order, an officer to Appellate Asst. Commissioner can revise that order - (C) Whether ITO should have issued a demand notice u/s. 29 of Act ? - Held, u/s. 29 of the Act, if a tax was due in consequence of an order from an assessee, ITO was under a duty to serve on him a notice of demand - In not doing so, it must be held that ITO did not discharge his duty which he was bound to do under Act - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
