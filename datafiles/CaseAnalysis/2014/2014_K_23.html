P. S. Meherhomji v K. T. Vijay Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 707; (2015) 1 SCC 788; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4494; JT 2014 (13) SC 605; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 731B; 2014(12) SCALE 104; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Entitled to, Quashing of proceedings

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 403, 405, 406, 408, 415, 420 r/w s. 34, 499, 500 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Cheating - Quashing of proceedings - Entitled to - Appellant company secretary of respondent No. 4 Company issued a letter on 14-12-2006 alleging Respondent no.1 routed stocks clandestinely - Respondents found imputations made in letter dt. 14-12-2006 were defamatory - Hence, respondent Nos. 1 and 2 filed private complaint before Trial Court - Trial Court took cognizance of offence against appellant and respondent company and held that prima facie case was made out u/ss. 499 and 500 IPC and made respondent Company and appellant as necessary parties for cause of action and they were accordingly summoned as accused nos.1and 2 - Appellant and respondent Company filed petition before HC to quash complaint initiated against them - Petition dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether complaint against appellant was liable to be quashed.

Held, Court should be circumspect and judicious in exercising discretion and should take all the relevant facts and circumstances into consideration before issuing process lest it would be instrument in the hands of private complainant as vendetta to harass the persons needlessly. If the allegations set out in the complaint do not constitute the offence of which cognizance is taken by Magistrate it is open to HC to quash the same in exercise of power u/s. 482 IPC. Appellant tried to make out case that the letter dt. 14-12-2006 which is the basis of taking cognizance was allegedly sent from the office of respondent No.4 Company under the signature of one colleague of appellant without the knowledge or prior permission of appellant and the said letter was never signed by appellant. Thus, if that is so, it is open to the appellant to take a defense and prove their contention during trial. Trial Court shall consider the said contention during trial and record its findings. HC rightly refused to quash the criminal proceedings in exercise of power u/s. 482 CrPC. There was no merit in instant case. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Summoning of accused in criminal case is serious matter and order taking cognizance by Magistrate summoning accused must reflect that he has applied his mind to the facts of the case and the law applicable thereto.
