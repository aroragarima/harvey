Bahadursinh Lakhubhai Gohil v Jagdishbhai M. Kamalia and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1158; (2004) 2 SCC 65; AIR 2004 SC 1159; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 189; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 933; JT 2005 (6) SC 60; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 503; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 508; 2003(10) SCALE 806; 2004 (1) SLT 933; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 1023; 2004 (1) UC 569

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 136 - Public interest litigation - Property - Lease - Municipal register - Incorporation of name of the respondent - Auction - No written document to prove that possession has been delivered - Held, the statutory authorities are bound to pass orders in writing. If possession of the plot had been delivered, the respondent-corporation was bound to prepare document in respect thereof whereof at least a receipt was required to be obtained from the auction purchaser showing that such possession had been delivered. If the possession had been delivered, the date on which the same was done could have been found out from the records of the municipal corporation - Further held, it is not a fit case where this Court should exercise its discretionary jurisdiction under art. 136 of the Constitution.
