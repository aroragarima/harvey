Union Of India & Anr. v Arulmozhi Iniarasu & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 422; (2011) 7 SCC 397; AIR 2011 SC 2731; 2011(5) ALL MR 469; 2012 (6) AWC 6090; 2011 (4) ESC 538; 2011 (130) FLR 1076; JT 2011 (7) SC 502; 2011(7) SCALE 340; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 1; 2011 (4) SLR 697; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3398

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Relaxation - Age - Respondents were engaged as part-time contingent casual labourers purely on temporary basis in the office of the Commissioner of Central Excise in the year 1999 - On 14.01.2008, the Chief Commissioner of Central Excise issued a notice inviting applications for recruitment to 40 (37 General Category and 3 OBC) posts of sepoy - As per the recruitment rules, the age limit prescribed for the post as on 1.01.2008, was 27 years for general candidate, 32 years for SC/ST candidates and 30 years for OBC - Respondents applied for said posts, however, their applications were rejected as age barred - Respondents filed application before tribunal who held that the ratio of the decision of SC in Nagendra Chandra & Ors. vs. State of Jharkhand & Ors. 2007 INDLAW SC 1165 was applicable to case of the respondents and therefore, they were entitled to the same relief as was granted in that case - On appeal, HC modified the order of the tribunal to the effect that relaxation in the age limit could be up to 3 years for OBC candidates and 5 years for SC/ST candidates, subject to the condition that it would be applicable to those candidates who were actually erstwhile employees of the department - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether in the matter of relaxation of age limit, prescribed as eligibility criteria for appointment on a particular post, any principle of law has been laid down in the decision of this Court in Nagendra Chandra's case and if so, whether it could be applied to the facts of the instant case for directing the afore-stated relaxation in age limit? - Held, the relied upon observation of Nagendra Chandra case does not appear to be consistent with the ratio of the decision of the SC in Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others vs. Umadevi and Others 2006 INDLAW SC 125 as in the said decision it had clearly been held that the courts were not expected to issue any direction for absorption/regularisation or permanent continuance of temporary, contractual, casual, daily wagers or ad-hoc employees merely because such an employee was continued for a long time beyond the term of his appointment - Therefore, the said observation cannot be said to be an exposition of general principle of law on the point that a long length of service, dehors the relevant recruitment rules for the post, was a relevant factor for waiver or relaxation of any eligibility criterion, including age limit, for future regular selections for the post - In the instant case, the respondents were engaged as part time contingent casual labourers in the office of the Commissioner of Central Excise for doing all types of work as may be assigned to them by the office - Their part time engagement was need based for which they were to be paid on hourly basis - Though stand of the respondent was that many a times they were required to work day and night but it was nowhere stated that they were recruited or ever discharged the duties of a 'sepoy' for which recruitment process was initiated - Hence, the engagement of the respondents as casual labourers even for considerable long duration did not confer any legal right on them for seeking a mandamus for relaxation of age limit - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
