Hukam Chand v Om Chand and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 1304; (2001) 10 SCC 715

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949, Bombay Rent Restriction Act

Summary: Land & Property - East Punjab Urban Rent Restrictions Act, 1949 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Bombay Rent Restriction Act - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Power of Attorney - Registered lease deed was executed between appellant and respondent - On expiry of period of lease, deed provides tenants shall settle with landlord about construction so raised on rented land and if landlord does not wish to purchase building/construction raised by tenants, then tenants shall be liable to remove material/debris of construction at their own cost and hand over possession of rented land to landlord - On expiry of period of lease, landlord - respondent filed a suit against tenants - Trial court passed a decree in favor of respondent and High Court upheld same - Issues arise - (A) Whether suit property, being 'rented land' as defined in 1949 Act, suit for eviction could have been filed only before Rent Controller and therefore, suit filed before civil court was not maintainable? - Held, in view of finding arrived was based on appreciation of evidence and construction of clauses of lease deed, property forming subject matter of lease is not 'rented land' - Suit was, therefore, maintainable before civil court - (B) Whether decree passed in respondent's favour by trial court and maintained by HC cannot be sustained and should be set aside as he has parted with his interest in property? - Further held, in case of any assignment or creation of any interest during pendency of a suit, under O. 22, r. 10 of CPC, 1908 suit may, by leave of court, be continued by or against person to or upon whom such interest has come or devolved - Such assignment or devolution of right during pendency of litigation will not arrest progress of litigation - (C) Whether decree cannot be sustained unless and until persons, having now interest in property, come before court for purpose of settling amount of compensation? - Further held, decree of trial court consistent with terms of lease allows tenant appellant liberty of making such settlement - That liberty is still available to tenant appellant and he may settle compensation with present landlords - Appeal dismissed.
