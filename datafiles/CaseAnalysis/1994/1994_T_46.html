Sahib Ram v The State Of Haryana And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 146; (1995) Supp1 SCC 18; (1995) SCC (L&S) 248; AIR 1995 SCW 1780; 1994 (28) ATC 747; 1995 (1) CLR 226; JT 1995 (1) SC 24; 1995 (1) LLJ 488; 1994(4) SCALE 605B; 1995 (1) SLJ 151; 1994 (5) SLR 753; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 674

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether appellant was entitled to get revised pay scale? - Minimum educational qualification required was first or second class M.A., M.Sc., M.Com. plus a first or second Class B Library Science or a diploma in Library Science - Relaxation given was only as regards obtaining first class or second class not in educational qualification - Appellant did not possess required educational qualifications - Appellant would not be entitled to relaxation - Appeal partly allowed.
