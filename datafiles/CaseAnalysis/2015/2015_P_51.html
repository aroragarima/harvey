Prakash Chand Meena and others v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 385; (2015) 8 SCC 484; 2015 (3) AWC 2701; 2015(3) SCALE 799; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 729; 2015 (6) SLR 685

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Education Subordinate Service Rules, 1971

Summary: Service - Education - Rajasthan Education Subordinate Service Rules, 1971 - Recuritment process - Equalization of educational decrees - Justification - Respondent Commission published advertisement inviting applications for recruitment for which education qualification was specified - Examination was held for posts and candidates were directed to submit their testimonials - Further, it was advertised that State Govt. has directed Commission to treat such candidates also eligible for appointment to post who possess Diploma in Physical Education (D.P.Ed.) and/or Degree of Bachelor in Physical Education (B.P.Ed.) on premise that these qualifications should be treated as higher than or equal to Certificate in Physical Education (C.P.Ed.) - Accordingly result was declared by Commission - Applicants for post having qualification of C.P.Ed. were aggrieved with clarification issued by State Govt. and result declared by Commission on its basis, hence preferred petitions which were allowed on three different occasions - Recruitment Rule were amended vide notification in 2011 - DB based on amendment notification vide impugned order set aside order passed by lower Court - Hence, present appeal.

Held, recruitment process must be completed as per terms and conditions in advertisement and as per rules existing when recruitment process began. Further, DB has gone to great lengths in examining issue whether B.P.Ed. and D.P.Ed. qualifications are equivalent or superior to C.P.Ed. qualification but such exercise cannot help cause of respondents who had option either to cancel recruitment process or to complete it as per terms of advertisement and as per rules. They chose to continue with recruitment process, hence they cannot be permitted to depart from qualification laid down in advertisement and in rules which were suitably amended only later in 2011. Hence, respondents acted against law and against terms of advertisement in treating such applicants successful for appointment to post who held other qualifications but not qualification of C.P.Ed. Such candidates had not even submitted separate OMR application form for appointment to post which was essential as per terms of advertisement. Further, candidates who were aware of advertisement and did not have qualification of C.P.Ed. also had two options, either to apply only for post if they had necessary qualification for that post or to challenge advertisement that it omitted to mention equivalent or higher qualification along with qualification of C.P.Ed. for post. Having not challenged advertisement and having applied for other post, they could not have subsequently claimed or be granted eligibility on basis of equivalence clarified or declared subsequently by State Govt. SC held that process was initiated through advertisement inviting application which did not indicate that equivalent or higher qualification holders were eligible to apply nor equivalent qualifications were reflected in recruitment Rules or Govt. Orders of relevant time. Hence, SC held that DB erred in interfering with judgment of lower Court who had correctly allowed petitions filed against result declared by Commission on basis of State Govt's. letter. Impugned order passed by DB is set aside and order passed by lower Court is restored. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Recruitment process should be conducted in accordance with advertisement issued and as per rules laid down.
