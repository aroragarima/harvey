J. K. Trust, Bombay v Commissioner of Income Tax, Excess Profits Tax Bombay

Supreme Court of India

22 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1957 SC 846; [1957] 32 ITR 535; 1957 SCJ 845; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 , s. 66(1) - Excess profits tax - Managing agents - Acquisition - Exemption from Tax - whether a business carried on behalf of a trust profits derived was exempt from taxation - Held, income, profits or gains shall not be included in total income of person receiving them - Any income derived from business carried on behalf of a religious or charitable institution when income was applied solely to purposes of institution and business carried was carrying out of a primary purpose of institution and work in connection with business is mainly carried on by beneficiaries of institution - Profits of business would be exempt only if conditions laid down - Public charity was perpetual in character and capable of enforcement so long as there was any property left which can be appropriated for its objects and even if some or all of objects become incapable of fulfillment trust properties will be devoted to performance of similar or allied charitable purposes on doctrine of cy pres - Any income derived from property held under trust or other legal obligation wholly for religious or charitable purpose and in case of property held in part only for purpose income applied or finally set apart - Appeal allowed.
