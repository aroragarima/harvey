State of Rajasthan v Santosh Savita

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 879; (2013) 12 SCC 663; 2013 CRLJ 4611; 2013 (3) JCC 2123; JT 2013 (12) SC 168; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 765

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Cod,1860, s.302 - Murder - Acquittal - It was alleged that respondent poured kerosene on deceased by lifting container and ignited fire to her saree with matchbox - Respondent was charged for offence punishable u/s.302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted respondent for offence charged - HC acquitted appellant of charge u/s.302 of IPC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC was justified in acquitting respondent by impugned judgment.

Held, perusal of first dying declaration shows that deceased has clearly stated that respondent poured kerosene on her from can and ignited fire by match stick on her saree and as result she got burnt. First dying declaration was recorded in presence of Medical Jurist, when deceased was in condition to make statement. On a reading of second dying declaration it was found that deceased reiterated that there was quarrel between her and respondent and respondent poured kerosene oil on her and ignited fire and as result she got burnt. Two dying declarations of deceased are corroborated by recovery of a plastic can with some kerosene oil, burnt pieces of saree, blouse and bangles as well as broken matchsticks from room where incident took place. PWs2,3 and 8 have corroborated statements of deceased in two dying declarations. Hence, HC was not justified in acquitting respondent by impugned judgment. Therefore, impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.300,302,304 - Determination of nature of offence - Whether respondent should be held guilty of offence u/s.302 of IPC or s.304 IPC.

Held, intention to cause death or to cause bodily injury is an essential ingredient of offence of murder. Under fourth clause of s.300 of IPC, knowledge of act committed by accused that it is so imminently dangerous that it must, in all probability, cause death or such bodily injury as is likely to cause death, is a necessary ingredient for offence of murder. PWs2,3 and 8 have not seen what exactly happened inside room in which incident took place. However, deceased has stated in two dying declarations that respondent poured kerosene on deceased and ignited fire on saree of deceased. Two dying declarations are very sketchy and do not narrate details as to how incident took place except stating that there was quarrel between deceased and respondent. Therefore, it is difficult to record finding that respondent had any intention to cause death of deceased or had any intention to cause any bodily injury or committed act knowing that it is so imminently dangerous that it must, in all probability, cause death of deceased. Death of deceased has been caused by a reckless act of respondent with knowledge that it is likely to cause death and for this act respondent is guilty of culpable homicide not amounting to murder u/s.304, Part-II of IPC. Appeal allowed.
