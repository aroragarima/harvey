P.S. Sairam and Another v P.S. Rama Rao Pisey and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 95; (2004) 11 SCC 320; AIR 2004 SC 1619; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 108; 2004 (54) ALR 709; 2004 (2) AWC 998; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 912; 2004 (2) CHN 184; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 98; JT 2004 (2) SC 114; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 825; 2004 (2) RD 36; 2004(2) SCALE 233; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 98; 2004 (2) SLT 150; 2004 (1) Supreme 900; 2004 (1) UC 503

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Family Business, Coparcener, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family and Personal - Hindu Succession Act, s. 6 - Karnataka State Amendment - Insertion of s. 6A - Whether mere user of the joint family property, as a business premises by defendant No.1, who was karta of the joint family, for running his separate business can be said to be in any manner detrimental to the joint family property? - Proposition well settled, that there is no presumption under Hindu Law that a business standing in the name of any member of the joint family is a joint family business even if that member of the manager of the joint family, unless it could be shown that the business in the hands of the coparcener grew up with the assistance of the joint family property or joint family funds or that the earnings of the business were blended with the joint family estate, the business remains free and separate - Undisputed facts noted by Court as to that joint family had not invested a single farthing in the busines at any point of time as it was started by defendant No.1 by raising loans from the market, so far as the other portion of the said property is concerned, undisputedly, in one part only, defendant No.1 was carrying on business - Facts amply prove that joint family property was being used as business premises not only by karta but also by junior member of the joint Hindu family - There is no material whatsoever to show that user of the same as business premises by defendant no.1 was in any manner detrimental to the joint family property - Held, that the business carried on by defendant No.1 in the property described as item No.1 in the Schedule cannot be treated to be joint family business and the same remained his separate business throughout - Appeals disposed of.
