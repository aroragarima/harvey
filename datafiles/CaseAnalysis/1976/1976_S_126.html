Mohd-Ashfaq v State Transport Appellate Tribunal U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 197; (1976) 4 SCC 330; AIR 1976 SC 2161; 1985 ECR 2597; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 5 and 29(2) - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 57, 58 and 68 - (A) Whether time limit specified in s. 58(2), proviso was not applicable in case of an application for renewal of a permit u/s. 68 F(10), proviso and Regional Transport Authority was not entitled to reject application of appellant for renewal of his permit as time-barred? - Held, yes - It was applicable - There is no reason in principle why provisions enacted in s. 57 and s. 58(2), proviso should not apply in case of an application for renewal of a permit - If procedure set out in s. 57 does not apply in such a case, there is no other procedure prescribed by Act which can possibly be invoked and result would be that there would be no procedure for dealing with such an application for renewal - If time limit specified in s. 58(2), proviso does not apply, there would be no time limit for making such an application for renewal and it would be possible to make it any time, even after expiry of period of permit and Regional Transport Authority would be bound to consider it - (B) Whether s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 is applicable ? - This criterion can legitimately be imported from s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 - However, delay must not be condoned beyond 15 days - Appeal dismissed.
