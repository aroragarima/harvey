Rivadeneyta Ricardo Agustin v Govt. Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1262; (1994) SCC (Cr) 354; (1994) Supp1 SCC 597

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Customs Act, s. 135 - Petitioner is a foreign national - He was apprehended and on being searched, substantial quantity of gold was found concealed in VCR - He made a statement admitting that he was smuggling gold - He was arrested and investigation commenced - Magistrate took cognizance of an offence u/s. 135 - Impugned order of detention was made - Whether preventive detention order was valid? - In Kamarunniss v Union of India 1990 Indlaw SC 85 it was held that in no uncertain terms that detention orders can validly be passed against detenus who are in jail, provided officer passing order is alive to fact of detenus being in custody and there is material on record to justify his conclusion that they would indulge in similar activity if set at liberty - Held, in view of law laid in Kamarunniss case detention order is liable to be quashed - Petition disposed of.
