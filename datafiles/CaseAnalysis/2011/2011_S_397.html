Godavari Sugar Mills Limited v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 60; (2011) 2 SCC 439; 2011 (1) KLT 656; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 753; 2011(1) SCALE 588; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence of India Act, 1962

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holdings) Act, 1961, ss. 21, 24, 26 - Declaration of surplus land - Payment of compensation - Rate of interest - Determination - Land in question of appellant was declared as surplus u/s. 21 of the Act and was taken over by the State Government - Appellant submitted its claim u/s. 24(1) of the Act before the 2nd respondent/Consolidation Officer - 2nd respondent, after a long delay, passed an award with an interest at the rate of 3% per annum from the date of taking possession to the date of actual payment - Appellant accepted the award under protest - Appellant filed a writ petition seeking interest at the rate of 9% instead of awarded of 3% - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition as non-maintainable on the ground that the prayer in the writ petition was for only for payment of money - (A) Whether the writ petition was for 'recovery of money' and therefore not maintainable - Held, HC, erroneously relying upon the decision of SC in Suganmal v. State of MP, 1964 INDLAW SC 235, held that the prayer in the writ petition being one for payment of interest, it should be considered to be a writ petition filed to enforce a money claim and therefore, not maintainable - However, observations in Suganmal related to a claim for refund of tax and HC should have understood with reference to the nature of claim made in the writ petition - Reliance upon Suganmal by the HC was misplaced to hold that the writ petition filed by the appellant was not maintainable - Writ petition of appellant was, therefore, maintainable - (B) Whether the 2nd respondent was justified in awarding interest only at the rate of 3% per annum on the compensation payable u/s. 25 of the Act - Held, s. 26 contemplates payment of compensation within 20 years from the date of taking possession with interest at 3% per annum; and for the period beyond 20 years, the said provision regarding interest would cease to apply and the general equitable principles relating to interest would apply; and interest can be awarded at any reasonable rate, in the discretion of the court - Therefore, in the instant case, interest at the rate of 6% per annum, beyond 20 years would be appropriate and payable, on equitable principles - Direction issued to the respondents to pay interest on the compensation amount from the date of taking possession to date of payment, at the rate of 3% per annum for the first twenty years and thereafter at the rate of 6% per annum - Appeal partly allowed.
