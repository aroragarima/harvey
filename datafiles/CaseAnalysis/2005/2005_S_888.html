tate of Rajasthan v M/s Nav Bharat Construction Company

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 218; (2005) 11 SCC 197; AIR 2005 SC 2795; 2005 (3) ALD(SC) 58; 2005 (1) ARBLR 495; 2005 (3) AWC 2930; [2005] 125 Comp Cas 1; JT 2005 (3) SC 558; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 85; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 368; 2005(3) SCALE 402

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Practice and Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Dispute concerning civil construction work - Arbitrator awarded a sum of Rs. 6,68,058 16 with interest @ 18% - Civil Court reduced interest to 12% - Whether HC right in confirming amount ? - Held, State is estopped on doctrine of acquiescence and waiver from raising objection to competence of substituted arbitrator and validity of arbitration proceedings by taking recourse to cl 23 of agreement on basis of which initial reference was made to sitting Chief Engineer - No interference in rate of interest awarded by civil court treating it to be a reasonable rate - Appeals Dismissed.
