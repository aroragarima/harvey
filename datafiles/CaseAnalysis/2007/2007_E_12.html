Elgi Equipments Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Coimbatore

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1241; (2007) 8 SCC 84; 2007 (215) E.L.T. 348; JT 2007 (10) SC 511; 2007(10) SCALE 156; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 1040

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Appeals allowed, Sale Price, Normal Price, Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975, Place Of Removal, Trade Discount

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 4(1)(1) - Central Excise (Valuation) Rules, 1975 - Whether assessee was entitled to 20% trade discount? - Sales were effected at factory gate directly to distributor allowing 20% uniform discount on list price - In respect of sales to sub-dealers discount given was 8% and balance 12% was given to the distributors as commission - Held, word 'ordinarily' in s. 4(1)(a) indicated that if sale pattern adopted by an assessee indicate that a large part of total production was sold at wholesale price at the factory gate and that assessee had given benefit of trade discount to large number of its dealers at a particular rate, then, it would constitute 'normal practice of wholesale trade' - Assessee gave discount of 20% to majority of its dealers - Hence, assessee was entitled to a trade discount of 20% - Appeal allowed.
