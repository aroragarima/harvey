Â Himat Lal K. Shah v Commissioner of Police, Ahmedabad and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 757; (1973) 1 SCC 227; (1973) SCC (Cr) 280; AIR 1973 SC 87; 1973 CRLJ 204; 1973 CrLR(SC) 326; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Public Meeting

Summary: Defence and Security Forces - Bombay Police Act, 1951, s.33(1)(o) - Constitution, srts.14, 19(1)(a), 19(1)(b), and 19(1)(d) - Rules framed by Commissioner of Police Ahmedabad D/- 21-10-1965. rr. 7, 13, 14 and 15 - (A) Validity of rules - Scope of word 'regulate' in s.33(1)(o) - (B) Constitutionality of s. 33(1)(o) enabling Police Commissioner to make rules to regulate the assemblies and processions - (C) Validity of r. 7 - Rules held, are intra vires and regulate includes power to prescribe for taking of prior permission in writing for holding meeting - Not violative of rights u/art. 19(1)(b) & (d) of Constitution - Held, confers arbitrary powers and is violative of art. 19(1)(b) also - Appeal allowed.
