Guru Nanak Foundation v Rattan Singh and Sons

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 268; (1981) 4 SCC 634; AIR 1981 SC 2075; 1981(3) SCALE 1543; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 842; 1981 UJ 886

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 5, 11, 14 (2) and 31(4) - (A) Whether direction given by newly appointed arbitrator to commence arbitral proceedings afresh means that evidence led before previous arbitrator will be ignored? - Held, that arbitration proceedings will commence from stage where same was left by previous arbitrator - Further held, that parties may place before him supplementary pleadings if any, as well as additional evidence if any but earlier evidence shall not be ignored - (B) Which is court having jurisdiction in which award should be filed by arbitrator? - Held, Supreme Court has jurisdiction to examine validity of award - Taking harmonious construction of ss. 2 and 14 it is clear that ordinarily that court will have jurisdiction to deal with questions arising under Act, except one in Chap. IV, in which a suit with regard to dispute involved in arbitration would be required to be filed under provisions of CPC, 1908 - Where an application is made in any reference to a court competent to entertain it, that court alone will have jurisdiction over arbitration proceedings and all subsequent applications arising out of that reference and arbitration proceedings shall have to be made in that Court alone and in no other court- (C) Whether application u/s. 31(4) is confined to pendency of reference made? - Phrase 'in any reference' used in s. 31(4) means 'in course of any reference' - S. 31(4) would vest exclusive jurisdiction in court in which an application for filing of an award has been first made u/s. 14 - Held, it is not - Appeal dismissed.
