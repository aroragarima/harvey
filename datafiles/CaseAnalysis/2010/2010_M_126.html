Union of India and others v Mahaveer C. Singhvi

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J.M. Panchal, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 669; (2010) 8 SCC 220; AIR 2010 SC 3493; 2010 (6) AWC 5903; 2010 (171) DLT 110; 2011 (128) FLR 1; JT 2010 (8) SC 193; 2010 (4) LLJ 821; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 684; 2010(7) SCALE 623; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 246; 2011 (1) SLR 33

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Foreign Service (Recruitment, Cadre, Promotion, Seniority) Rules, 1961

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Discharge from service of a probationer during his period of probation - Respondent was discharged from Indian Foreign Service during his probation period - Respondent challenged said order before the Tribunal contending that after the expiry of his period of probation, he stood confirmed and his services could not have been terminated without an enquiry in view of the provisions of art. 311(2) of the Constitution - It was also contended that the order was passed in complete violation of the principles of natural justice as the respondent was not given a opportunity to defend himself against the allegations which formed the foundation of the said order - Application was dismissed - On appeal, HC quashed the order of discharge with a direction to reinstate the respondent - HC held that a one sided inquiry had been conducted at different levels - Hence, present petition - Held, the order of discharge was punitive in character and had been motivated by considerations which were not reflected in the said order - That being so, having regard to the consistent view taken by SC that if an order of discharge of a probationer is passed as a punitive measure, without giving him an opportunity of defending himself, the same would be invalid and liable to be quashed, and the same finding would also apply to the respondent's case - Further, since the HC has considered the matter in depth after perusing the relevant records there is no reason to interfere with the judgment and order of the HC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
