Maniben Devraj Shah v Municipal Corporation Of Brihan Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 123; (2012) 5 SCC 157; AIR 2012 SC 1629; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 35; 2012(3) ALL MR 450; 2012 (92) ALR 460; 2012 (3) AWC 3027; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 247; JT 2012 (4) SC 55; 2012 (5) MahLJ 584; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 317; 2012 (4) MPLJ 1; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 73; 2012 (116) RD 392; 2012(4) SCALE 248

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Law Officer

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 314 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Appeal - Condonation of delay - Sustainability - Appellants filed suits for grant of a declaration that notices issued by respondent-Corporation u/s. 314 of 1888 Act for demolition of properties were illegal and not binding on them - Corporation objected about maintainability of suit on ground that notice u/s. 527 of Act was not issued - Trial Court, considering pleadings and evidence produced, decreed suits - Corporation filed appeal along with petition for condonation of 7 years and 108 days delay before HC - HC allowed delay petition - Aggrieve instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in condoning delay of 7 years and 108 days in filing appeal - Held, HC altogether ignored gaping holes in story concocted by Corporation about misplacement of papers and total absence of any explanation as to why nobody even bothered to file applications for issue of certified copies of judgment for more than 7 years -Cause shown by Corporation for delayed filing of appeals was -wholly unsatisfactory and reasons assigned by HC, for condoning more than 7 years delay could not but be treated as poor apology for exercise of discretion by Court u/s. 5 of 1963 Act - Impugned order was set aside and appeals filed by Corporation against judgments of Trial Court were dismissed - Appeals allowed.
