Khenyei v New India Assurance Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 337; (2015) 9 SCC 273; 2015 (2) ACC 657; 2015 ACJ 1441; AIR 2015 SC 2261; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 98; 2015 (3) AWC 2945; 2015 (4) Bom.C.R. 21; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 162; JT 2015 (9) SC 501; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2306; 2016 (2) MahLJ 514; 2015 (4) MLJ 364; 2016 (2) MPLJ 18; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 1019; 2015 (4) RLW 3258; 2015(6) SCALE 194; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Composite Negligence, Indemnity, Apportionment Of Compensation, Apportionment, Proximate

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Contributory Negligence - Liability to pay compensation - Determination - Injuries were sustained by claimants when two vehicles i.e. bus and trailor-truck collided with each other - Respondent assurance company is insurer of bus, however, on basis of additional evidence adduced, HC held that respondent assurance company is not insurer of trailor-truck, hence is not liable to satisfy 2/3rd of award - Whether it is open to claimant to recover entire compensation from one of joint tort feasors, particularly when in accident caused by composite negligence of drivers of trailor-truck and bus has been found to 2/3rd and 1/3rd extent respectively.

Held, in case of composite negligence, claimant is entitled to sue both or any one of joint tort feasors and to recover entire compensation as liability of joint tort feasors is joint and several. However, apportionment of compensation between two tort feasors vis- -vis claimant is not permissible and he can recover at his option whole damages from any of them. Further, if all joint tort feasors have been impleaded and evidence is sufficient, it is open to Court to determine inter se extent of composite negligence of drivers. However, determination of extent of negligence between joint tort feasors is only for purpose of their inter se liability so that one may recover sum from other after making whole of payment to claimant to extent it has satisfied liability of other. In case both of them have been impleaded and apportionment/ extent of their negligence has been determined by Court, in main case one joint tort feasor can recover amount from other in execution proceedings. Further, it would not be appropriate for Court to determine extent of composite negligence of drivers of two vehicles in absence of impleadment of other joint tort feasors and in such case, impleaded joint tort feasor should be left, in case he so desires, to sue other joint tort feasor in independent proceedings after passing of decree or award. Hence, order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Claimant is not justified to recover entire compensation from one of joint tort feasors, when in accident caused by composite negligence of driver.
