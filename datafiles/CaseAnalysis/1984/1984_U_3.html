P.S.Mahal & Ors. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 May 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 157; (1984) 4 SCC 545; (1985) SCC (L&S) 61; AIR 1984 SC 1291; 1984 LabIC 1176; 1984 (2) LLJ 282; 1984 (2) LLN 479; 1984(1) SCALE 949; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 847; 1984 (2) SLJ 197; 1984 (2) SLR 498

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority

Summary: Service - Central Engineering Service (Class I) Recruitment Rules, 1954, r. 4 - Central Engineering and Central Electrical Engineering Service (Group A) (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1976, rr. 2(iii) and 2(iv) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Writ petition marks another round of litigation between two groups of Executive Engineers in Central Public Works Department of the Ministry of Works and Housing, Government of India, one group consisting of promotees from the grade of Assistant Executive Engineers and the other consisting of promotees from the grade of Assistant Engineers - (A) Service Rules - G. M. D/- 22-12-1959, Para 5(ii) and Para 6 - (1) Inter se seniority - To be fixed on basis of length of continuous officiation - AEE and AE promoted up to 11-12-1974 - To the grade of Executive Engineers - Carrying forward of posts of EEs allocable to AEE - Promotees given 'carried forward' posts - Permissibility - (2) Constitutionality of rr. 2(iii) and 2(iv) of 1976 Rules - (B) Applicability of quota r. 4 of Central Engineering Service (Class I) Recruitment Rules - (A) Held, impermissible and memorandum inapplicable - Both r. 2 (iii) and r. 2(iv) are violative of arts. 14 and 16 - (B) it is attracted whenever there is long term vacancy in the grade of Executive Engineer - Petition allowed.
