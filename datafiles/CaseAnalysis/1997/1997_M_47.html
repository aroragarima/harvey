B. L. Gupta and Another v M.C.D

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1369; (1998) 9 SCC 223; (1998) SCC (L&S) 532; JT 1998 (7) SC 225; 1999 LabIC 612; 1999(1) SCALE 297

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Promotion - Rules of 1978 - Rules amended in 1995 - Appellants are all employees of Delhi Electric Supply Undertaking - Question relates to appointment to post of Asst. Accountant - Where, however, statutory rules exist, appointments and promotions have to be made in accordance with statutory rules specially where it has not been shown that rules gave power to appointing authority of relaxing said rules - Held, vacancies which occurred prior to amendment rules of 1978 prescribe mode in which promotions can be made - This mode has to be followed before appointments could be made - In absence of any such power of relaxation, appointment as Asst. Accountant could only be made by requiring candidates to take examination which was method which was prescribed by 1978 Rules - Appeals disposed of.
