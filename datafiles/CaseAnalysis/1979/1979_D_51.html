Dalbir Singh and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 556; (1979) 3 SCC 745; (1979) SCC (Cr) 848; AIR 1979 SC 1384; 1979 CRLJ 1058; 1979 CrLR(SC) 705; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1059

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, s. 302 - Murder - Death sentence or life imprisonment - Appellants found guilty of four murders - Whether death sentence of accused is liable to be reduced to life imprisonment? - Counting casualties is not main criterion for sentencing to death nor recklessness in act of murder - Sole focus on crime and total farewell to criminal and his social personal circumstances mutilate sentencing justice - Held, that in circumstances death sentence awarded was liable to be reduced to one of life imprisonment - The earlier provocation came from deceased's side by beating up one accused - There was no pre-planned, well laid attack, hell-bent on liquidating enemy - Appeal dismissed.
