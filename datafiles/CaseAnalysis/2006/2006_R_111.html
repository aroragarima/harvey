State of Maharashtra v Rashid B. Mulani

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 5; (2006) 1 SCC 407; AIR 2006 SC 825; 2006 CRLJ 794; 2006 (1) JCC 156; JT 2006 (1) SC 76; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 421; 2006 (2) RLW 1140; 2006(1) SCALE 107; 2006 (3) SCJ 367; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 4(1), 5(1)(a), 5(1)(b), 5(1)(d), 5(2) - IPC, 1860, s. 161 - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Special Judge convicted and sentenced respondent to undergo RI for one year - HC set aside conviction - Appeal against acquittal - Held, it is no doubt true that if two views are possible and view of HC acquitting accused cannot be said to be wholly improbable, this Court will not interfere with decision of HC - But where material on record leads to only one conclusion viz, guilt of accused, judgment of HC will not be sustained - HC was not justified in accepting explanation regarding receipt of Rs. 300/- By accused as being towards Tagai loan - Conviction upheld - Appeal disposed of.
