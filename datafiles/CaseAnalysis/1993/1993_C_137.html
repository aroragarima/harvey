Indian Oil Corporation v Consumer Protection Council, Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 202; (1994) 1 SCC 397; 1994 (4) Bom.C.R. 37; 1994 (1) CLC 146; [1994] 79 Comp Cas 577; 1994 (2) CPJ(Sc) 21; 1993 (1) CTJ 1025; JT 1993 (6) SC 645

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Indirect Tax

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(g) - Liquified Petroleum Gas (Regulation of Supply and Distribution) Order, 1988, s. 3(2) - Whether there is deficiency in services of appellant? - Possession of an LPG gas cylinder by 'D' in this case has not been proved to be authorised - Therefore, on strength of obtaining possession by means of an unauthorised connection it is not open to first respondent to foist a contract on corporation - In so far there is no privity of contract between appellant and consumer no 'deficiency' as defined u/s. 2(1)(g) arises - Therefore, action itself is not maintainable before Consumer Forum - Appeal allowed.
