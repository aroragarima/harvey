Ashutosh Gupta v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Brijesh Kumar, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1953; (2002) 4 SCC 34; (2002) SCC (L&S) 465; AIR 2002 SC 1533; 2002 (93) FLR 1134; JT 2002 (3) SC 219; 2002 LabIC 1457; 2002(3) SCALE 203; 2002 (2) SCJ 555; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 649; 2002 (2) SLJ 497; 2002 (3) SLR 18; 2002 (2) SLT 638; 2002 (2) Supreme 561; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1567

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Administrative Service Rules, 1954 - Rajasthan Administrative Service (Emergency Recruitment) Rules, 1976, r. 25 - Emergency recruitment - Direct recruits vis-a-vis emergency recruits in matters relating to determining their seniority and promotions - R. 25 provides for seniority formula - Whether formula u/ r. 25 constitutionally valid on touchstone of art. 14? - In order to pass test of permissible classification, two conditions must be fulfilled, namely : (i) that classification must be founded on an intelligible differentia which distinguishes persons or things that are grouped together from others who are left out of group, and (ii) that differentia must have a rational relation to object sought to be achieved by Act - Provision of s. 25 dealing with seniority has been specifically designed to meet all situations under which people from different walks of life could be recruited to Rajasthan Emergency recruits to Rajasthan Administrative Service form a class by themselves, they being neither direct recruits under Recruitment Rules of 1954, nor are they promotees - For purpose of their seniority in cadre, in Emergency Recruitment Rules of 1976, a formula has been adopted - Said formula cannot be held to be discriminatory in nature - In facts of case cadre under emergency recruits were on verge of superannuation - Emergency recruits to Rajasthan Administrative Service form a class by themselves, they being neither direct recruits under Recruitment Rules of 1954, nor are they promotees - Held, formula for seniority of emergency recruits not discriminatory and thus is constitutionally valid - Appeal dismissed.
