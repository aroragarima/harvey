Afjal Imam v State Of Bihar And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 509; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Maharashtra Sales Tax on the Transfer of the Right to use any Goods for any purpose Act, 1985, Howrah Municipal Corporation Act, 1980, Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, Patna Municipal Corporation Act, 1951, Goa Municipalities Act 1968, M.P. and Chattisgarh Municipalities Act, 1961, Mizoram Municipalities Act, 2007, West Bengal Municipal Corporation Act, 2006, Bihar Municipal Empowered Standing Committee Conduct of Business Rules, 2010

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Bihar Municipal Act, 2007, ss. 21(3),23, 25, 27 - Election of Mayor - Empowered Standing Committee - Vote of no confidence - Anomalous situation - After election of Municipal Corporation, councilors elected Mayor and Deputy Mayor, 2 years later, no confidence motions were moved against both of them as far as motion against Mayor was concerned - Mayor challenged decision on no confidence motion by filing writ petition before Single Judge, which was allowed - On appeal before DB, DB set aside that order - Aggrieved, Mayor filed SLP, in view of order passed by SC, notice was given and meeting was convened and appellant/new Mayor was elected as new Mayor of Municipal Corporation and on his election he nominated 7 councilors to be members of Empowered Standing Committee of Municipal Corporation as per s. 21(3) of the Act and he requested DM to give them oath of secrecy as per s. 21(4) r/w s. 24 of the Act but DM declined to do so and held nomination by Mayor was only a one time act and notwithstanding change of Mayor, Empowered Standing Committee as nominated earlier should continue - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 27 of the Act should be read as it was and without reference to other connected sections, meaning thereby whether members of Empowered Standing Committee would continue to hold office even if nominator Mayor was no longer in office or whether such reading of s. 27 of the Act treats newly elected Mayor dissimilarly and whether s. 27 of the Act was ultra vires of Constitution - Held, in Jitendra Kumar Vs. State of Bihar 2007 Indlaw PAT 77 relied on - New Mayor was not permitted to have his nominees on Empowered Standing Committee, collective functioning would be under jeopardy, thus there was clear omission in the Act, in this behalf - Hence, result would be incongruous to democratic functioning in as much as nomination on Empowered Standing Committee would be one time act and newly elected Mayor would be at mercy of other members of Empowered Standing Committee, such reading would be contrary to s. 21 of the Act and newly elected Mayor would be treated dissimilarly as against earlier elected Mayor for no justifiable reason, thereby s. 27 of the Act would be ultra vires to art. 14 of Constitution - Hence, s. 27 of the Act should be read down harmoniously with and subject to ss. 25(4), 23(3), 21(3), and 21(4) of the Act and respondent no. 3 was consequently directed to administer oath of secrecy u/s. 24 of the Act to 7 Municipal Councilors nominated by appellant to Empowered Standing Committee and appellant as well as members of Empowered Standing Committee should be entitled to exercise all powers as Mayor and members of Empowered Standing Committee as provided in the Act, in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
