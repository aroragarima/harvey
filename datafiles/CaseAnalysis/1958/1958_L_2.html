Keshavlal Lallubhai Patel v Lalbhai Trikumlal Mills

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1958 SC 512; 1958 (60) BomLR 948; 1958 SCJ 866; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Practice & Procedure - Appellant entered into a contract with respondent for purchase of certain goods within certain time - Respondent fail to deliver goods in stipulated time - Whether there was enforceable agreement between parties for extension of time for performance of contract or barred by limitation? - No evidence to show acceptance by appellant of said condition - Held, contract must be based upon agreementso not open to promise by his unilateral act to extend time for performance of his own accord for his own benefit - No valid time agreement for extension of time - Appeal dismissed.
