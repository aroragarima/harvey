Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs v Venus Castings Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 319; (2000) 4 SCC 206; AIR 2000 SC 1568; 2000 (1) CLC 1121; 2000 (90) ECR 9; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 273; JT 2000 (4) SC 77; 2000 (38) RLT 1; 2000(3) SCALE 64; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 988; 2000 (4) SLT 25; 2000 (3) Supreme 257

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act 1944, s. 3 A(4) - Central Excise Rules (1944), r. 96 ZO(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Method of assessment of excise duty - Grant of special leave by manufacturer under s. 3 A(4) - Normal method of assessment is on basis of actual production capacity of mills and furnaces under s. 3 A(4) - But manufacturer also exercised his option in terms of r. 96 ZO(3) - Held, he cannot avail benefit of assessment in normal mode under s. 3 A(4) - Schemes contained in s. 3 A(4) and r. 96-ZO(3) or r. 96 - ZP(3) are alternative procedures to be adopted at option of assessee - These two procedures do not clash with each other - An assessee cannot have two procedures and thus state of law is uncertain - High Court's decision to remand of matter to Tribunal for fresh consideration was improper.
