Nichhalbhai Vallabhai and Others v Jaswantlal Zinabhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 500; AIR 1966 SC 997

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Appeal against order of High Court allowing application of plaint and remanded suit for retrial - Suit by respondent no 1 for partition of joint family properties - Appellant contended that suit not maintable as no consent taken by father defendent No.14 - Trial Court rejected application - HC allowed appeal - Whether HC was right, that suit brought by plaintiff was merely suit for partition by metes and bounds and not a suit for severance of Joint family status? - On question of interpretation of plaint it is important to consider all averments made by plaintiff in plaint - Held, HC right in holding that suit was not suit brought for severance of joint family status but suit merely for partition by metes and bounds - Appeal dismissed.
