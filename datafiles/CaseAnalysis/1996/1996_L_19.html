M/S. Aliji Momonji And Co. v Lalji Mavji And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3057; (1996) 5 SCC 379; AIR 1997 SC 64; 1997 (1) Bom.C.R. 464; 1996 (3) CCC 213; 1996 (3) CLT 171; JT 1996 (7) SC 53; 1996 (2) RentLR 315; 1996(5) SCALE 485; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 542; 1996 (6) Supreme 23

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10 - Necessary party - Appellant lease laid the suit for perpetual injunction against the Municipal Corporation of Bombay restraining them from demolishing a portion of the building - The Municipal Corporation had issued notice for demolition of the building on the ground that the appellant had made unauthorised structures - The contesting respondent Nos. 2 to 6 sought to come on record under O. 1 r. 10 of CPC contending that they have direct interest in the property and the motion taken out by the respondent was ordered by the Trial Court and the HC by the impugned order was upheld the same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the contesting respondents were necessary or proper party under O. 1, r. 10 of CPC

Held, the only reason which makes it necessary to make a person a party to an action is that he should be bound by the result of the action and the question to be settled, therefore, must be a question in the action which cannot be effectually and completely settled unless he is a party. The line has been drawn on a wider construction of the rule between the direct interest or the legal interest and commercial interest. In the event of building being demolished, right, title and interest of the landlord would directly be affected, the landlord would be a proper party, though no relief has been sought for against the landlord. HC, therefore, was right in refusing to interfere with the order passed by the trial Court impleading the landlords. Appeal dismissed
