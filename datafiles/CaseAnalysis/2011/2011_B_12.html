R.Vijayan v Baby & Anr

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 721; (2012) 1 SCC 260; AIR 2012 SC 528; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1325; 2013 (4) BC 601; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 335; 2011 (5) CompLJ 1; 2012 CRLJ 846; 2012 (1) CTC 791; 2011 (4) KLT 355; 2012 (2) MahLJ 584; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 204; 2012 (2) MPLJ 19; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 834; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 743; 2012 (2) RLW 1476; 2012 (2) RLW 1586; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 712

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s.138 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 29, 357(3) - Dishonor of cheque - Compensation - Sustainability - Respondent allegedly issued cheque in respect of repayment of loan, which was dishonored - Appellant filed complaint u/s. 138 of the Act - Trial Court found respondent/accused guilty and convicted - On appeal, Appellate Court , acquitted respondent/accused - Appellant filed criminal appeal before HC, which was partly allowed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that ss. 29 and 357 of CrPC and s. 138 of the Act should be read harmoniously and complementary to each other; and if so done, compensation could be awarded in cases u/s. 138 of the Act to meet loss sustained by dishonour and that if compensation could not be awarded for any reason, fine could be levied up to twice cheque amount - Held, in instant case Trial court thought fit to impose only a fine and not imprisonment - When conviction was set aside, appellant filed a revision, challenging non-grant of compensation of Rs.20,000/- but not non-imposition of sentence of imprisonment - Once sentence consists of only fine, power u/s. 357(3) CrPC could not be invoked for directing payment of compensation - Therefore order of HC did not call for interference - Appeal dismissed.
