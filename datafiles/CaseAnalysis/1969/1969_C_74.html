Commissioner of Income Tax, Madra v S. Chenniappa Mudaliar

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 287; (1969) 1 SCC 591; AIR 1969 SC 1068; [1969] 74 ITR 41; 1969 (2) MLJ 90; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 90; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 818

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 33(4) - Appellate Tribunal Rules, 1946, r.24 - Compensation - Assessment - Notification - Ultra vires - Assessee owned 1674 shares in A and 9 out of 20 shares in B - B company was managing agents of A - Assessee was a Joint Managing Director of B along with P - Assessee sold his entire holding in two companies to A and some of his relations - Those sales resulted in a profit of Rs.72,515/- and Rs. 3,14,100/- respectively - Income tax Officer assessed those amounts to tax for A/Y 1956-57 u/s.10(5A) of Act as compensation earned for parting with effective power of management - Assessment was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Assessee appealed to Appellate Tribunal - Tribunal dismissed appeal for default of appearance - Tribunal did not consider that there was sufficient cause for restoration and rejected petition - Assessee applied for a reference u/s.66(1) of Act on 2 questions of law but that application was rejected by Tribunal - Assessee approached HC u/s. 66(2) of Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether dismissal of appeal to Tribunal u/r. 24 of 1946 rules, was ultra vires of provisions of s.33 of 1922 Act - Held, there was no escape from conclusion that u/s. 33(4) Appellate Tribunal had to dispose of appeal on merits and could not short circuit same by dismissing it for default of appearance - S. 33(4) restricts jurisdiction of Tribunal to subject matter of appeal and included all powers which were conferred upon Appellate Assistant Commissioner by s. 31 of Act - R.24 provided for dismissal of an appeal for failure of appellant to appear, rules at material time did not contain any provision for restoration of appeal - Event of repugnancy between substantive provisions of Act and a rule it was rule which must give way to provisions of Act - Appeal dismissed.
