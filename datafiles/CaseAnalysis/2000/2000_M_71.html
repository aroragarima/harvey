M. V. Al Quamar v Tsavliris Salvage (International) Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2660; (2000) 8 SCC 278; AIR 2000 SC 2826; 2000 (5) ALD(SC) 97; JT 2000 (9) SC 184; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 212; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security, Foreign Court, Foreign Judgment, Foreign Territory

Summary: Civil Procedure - Admiralty Court Act, 1861, ss. 15, 44-A and 140 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 112, O. 21, r. 10 - Colonial Courts of Admiralty Act, 1890 - Andhra State Act, 1953 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 4 and 122(2) - States - Whether CPC can possibly be made applicable to any matter of criminal or admiralty or vice-admiralty jurisdiction? - If intent of legislation was to do away with applicability of provisions of CPC, in terms of s. 112(2) of Code then and in that event, question of continuance of s. 140 of Code would not have arisen - Jolly George Varghese v Bank of Cochin 1980 Indlaw SC 140 followed - S. 4, CPC being a general provision does not specifically excludes operation of CPC in matters of admiralty jurisdiction - Held, provisions of CPC are applicable to matters pertaining to admiralty jurisdiction - (B) Whether admiralty jurisdiction is available by a proceeding in rem and not in personam? - M V Elisabeth 1992 Indlaw SC 902 followed - Vessel is a juridical person, a maritime claim can be enforced against vessel - Further held, legal fiction created by s. 44-A makes High Court, court which passed decree and as such competency of HC to entertain execution proceeding cannot be doubted in any way - Appeals dismissed.
