P.P. Fathima v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 910; (2003) 8 SCC 726; 2003 (4) CCR 344; 2003 (4) Crimes 390; 2003 (90) ECC 779; JT 2003 (8) SC 527; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 700; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 81; 2003(9) SCALE 247; 2004 (1) ShimLC 166; 2003 (7) SLT 32; 2003 (8) Supreme 62

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 2(11), 21, 50 - Conviction - 750 mg of brown sugar seized from appellant - Whether brown sugar was a contraband article under Act - Held, a perusal of definition section clearly shows that brown sugar contains heroin, which is a prohibited drug, possession of which is punishable u/s. 21 of Act - Further held, at relevant point of time, distinction between small quantity and commercial quantity not being there, benefit of a subsequent notification would not be available to appellant because she was convicted by Special Judge of offence as constituted before notification in question came into force - Further, mere fact that a Panch witness does not support prosecution case by itself would not make prosecution case any less acceptable, if otherwise, court is satisfied from material on record and from evidence of seizing authority that such seizure was genuinely made.
