Mani Subrat Jain v Raja Ram Vohra

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 94; (1980) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1980 SC 299; 1980 (1) RCJ 337; 1980 (1) RCR(Rent) 325; 1980 (1) RentLR 25; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 141; 1980 UJ 60

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Punjab Rent Restriction Act 1947, Immunity under the Act

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13 - Eviction - Immunity under the Act - Legality - Respondent (landlord) sued appellant (tenant) for possession of tenanted building, and entreated into a compromise and appellant agreed to vacate by a certain date on certain terms - A decree in terms thereof was passed on 9-10-1972, thereafter the Act came into force w.e.f 4-11-1972 - However, appellant filed instant appeal against an order in execution for eviction of appellant, affirmed by Court after Court - Whether appellant was entitled to the immunity provided u/s. 13 of the Act - Held, s. 13 of the Act convincingly included ex-tenants against whom decrees or eviction might have been passed, whether on compromise or otherwise - In instant case, effect of the compromise decree, in respondent's submission, was that tenancy was terminated - Nobody had a case that appellant was not continuously in possession - Conclusion was inevitable that appellant remained a tenant and enjoys immunity u/s. 13(1) of the Act - Execution proceedings should, thus, failed because the statutory road-block could not be removed - Both parties produced an enlighten settlement under an agreement to sell property covered by instant appeal by respondent to appellant - Appeal disposed of.
