Pimpri Chinchwad Municipal Corporation And Ors. v M/S Gayatri Construction Company And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1230; (2008) 8 SCC 172; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 211; 2008 (6) AWC 6587; 2008 (4) BC 542; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 13; JT 2008 (9) SC 1; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 200; 2008(11) SCALE 142; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Answer To Question

Summary: Contract - Writ petition - Maintainability - Respondent No.1 was awarded a contract for the work of improvement and widening of part of the Thermax- Telco-Bhosari road - Writ petition challenging action of appellants in releasing the advertisement and inviting fresh tenders on for the very same work which is part of the contract awarded to the respondents - High Court directed the Corporation not to complete the work and to maintain status quo in respect of Phase-III of Telco Road as well as the tenders received for the said work - Appeal against - Held, High Court ought not to have entertained the writ petition - It is open to the respondents-writ petitioners to seek such remedy, if so advised, as is available in law - Appeal allowed.
