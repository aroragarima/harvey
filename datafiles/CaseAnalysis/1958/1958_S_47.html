Sri Baru Ram v Smt. Prasanni and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1959 SC 93; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1403

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Indian Evidence Act, Different Constituency, Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act

Summary: Election - Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 45, 47, 67 and 76 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 33, 116 A(2) and 123(7)(c) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 136 - Whether appellant had appointed Puran Singh as his polling agent for polling booth in question? - Held, s. 46 authorises a contesting candidate to appoint in prescribed manner such number of agents and relief agents as may be prescribed to act as polling agents of such candidate at each polling station provided u/s. 25 or at place fixed u/s. 29(2) for poll - No legal evidence to show such appointment - Respondent has failed to prove that appellant had committed a corrupt practice u/s. 123(7)(c) of Act - Appeal allowed.
