State of Uttar Pradesh v Singhara Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 167; AIR 1964 SC 358; 1963 ALJ 1093; 1964 CRLJ 263B; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 485; 1964 (1) SCW 57

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 91 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 120 B, 109 and 114 - Seven persons including three respondents prosecuted for murder - Acquittal of other accused persons - High Court allowed appeal against conviction of Respondents - Present appeal - Issue: Question of admissibility of certain oral evidence of magistrate who recorded confession? - When a statute confers a power on certain judicial officers, power can obviously be exercised only by those officers, no other officer can exercise that power - Power conferred by s. 164 on certain magistrates of higher classes, obvious, it was not intended to confer power on magistrates of lower classes - If, therefore, a proper construction of s. 164, a magistrate of a higher class is prevented from giving oral evidence of a confession made to him because thereby safeguards created for benefit of an accused person by s. 164 would be rendered nugatory - Object of s. 164 was not to give prosecution advantage of ss. 74 and 80 of Evidence Act but to provide for evidence being made available to prosecution subject to due protection of interest of accused - Held, HC rightly rejected oral evidence of magistrate who recorded confession - Appeal dismissed.
