Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Messrs Dwarkadas Khetan and Company

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 134; AIR 1961 SC 680; [1961] 41 ITR 528; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 42; 1961 (2) SCJ 187; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Indian Partnership Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26A and 2(6B) - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 30 - Whether a deed of partnership admitting a minor to full partnership is a valid document for registration u/s. 26 A of Income Tax Act, 1922? - Held, definition of 'Partner' in s. 2(6-B) does not render a minor to be a competent and full partner - S. 30 of Partnership Act - Minor cannot become a partner but can be admitted to benefits of partnership - Registration can only be granted of a document between persons who are parties to it and on covenants set out in it - Such a partnership deed is not a valid document for registration - Appeal allowed.
