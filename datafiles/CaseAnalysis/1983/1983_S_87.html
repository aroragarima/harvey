Bharwada Bhoginbhai Hirjibhai v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 103; (1983) 3 SCC 217; (1983) SCC (Cr) 728; AIR 1983 SC 753; 1983 (2) Crimes 232; 1983 CRLJ 1096; 1983(1) SCALE 665; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Wrongful Confinement

Summary: Constitution, 1950, art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Penal Code,1860, ss. 375, 376 and 511 - (A) Concurrent finding of facts in appeal by special leave before Supreme Court - Interfere if - (B) Discrepencies in appreciation of evidence - (C) Rape - Evidence - Necessity and extent of corroboration of testimony of prosecutrix - On principle the (D) Leniency in sentence for attempt to commit rape in special circumstances - Held , interference by SC - Overmuch importance cannot be given to minor discrepancies - Necessity and extent specified (Evidence of a victim of sexual assault stands on par with Evidence of an injured witness - But subject to the specified qualification ) - Taking into account the cumulative effect of these circumstances sentence reduced - Appeal Dismissed.
