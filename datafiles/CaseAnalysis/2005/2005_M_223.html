Supreme Court of India

11 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, B.N. Agrawal, H.K. Sema, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 326; (2005) 4 SCC 370; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1101; AIR 2005 SC 2119; 2005 CRLJ 2161; 2005 (118) DLT 329; JT 2005 (3) SC 195; 2005 (3) MahLJ 530; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 178; 2005(3) SCALE 93; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court, Finance Act, 1972

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.190, ss. 195 (1)(b)(i), 195(1)(b)(ii) - Interpretation of s.195(1)(b)(ii) - Question whether will was a genuine document or a forged one, was an issue before District Judge in probate proceedings where will had been filed - Whether ss.195 (1)(b)(i) and (ii) CrPC operated as a bar for taking cognizance of offences under ss.192, 193, 463, 464, 471, 475 and 476 IPC? - Held, Sachida Nand Singh has been correctly decided and view taken therein is correct view - s.195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC would be attracted only when offences enumerated in said provision have been committed with respect to a document after it has been produced or given in evidence in a proceeding in any Court - Appeal dismissed.
