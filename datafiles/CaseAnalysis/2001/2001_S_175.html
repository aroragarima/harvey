Her Highness Maharani Shantidevi P. Gaikwad v Savjibhai Haribhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S.P. Bharucha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19875; (2001) 5 SCC 101; AIR 2001 SC 1462; 2001 (3) GLR 2097; JT 2001 (4) SC 43; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 535; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 481; 2001(2) SCALE 647; 2001 (2) SCJ 650; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 590; 2001 (3) SLT 80; 2001 (2) Supreme 516

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Constitution - Power of attorney - Agreement entered into between defendant as owner and plaintiff as licensee in respect of a portion of property authorising plaintiff to administer property of declarant and to put Housing Scheme for constructing houses for weaker sections of society - (A) Whether agreement subsisting and binding on defendant? - Held, in draft development plan of 1979 which was finalised during pendency of suit, land in question is reserved for open space etc - It cannot be doubted that agreement had been entered into between parties mainly and rather only with object of construction of residential houses under scheme u/s. 21 of ULC Act for accommodation of weaker sections of society - It was not possible in view of what was provided in master plan - No interest can be said to have been created on account of plaintiff being permitted to prepare scheme and take ancillary steps - As per contract it could be unilaterally rescinded before delivery of possession - (B) Whether plaintiff entitled to seek specific performance of agreement or is he entitled to sue only for damages? - Further held, one of grounds on which court may decline to decree specific performance is where it would be inequitable to enforce specific performance - Under scheme as postulated by Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, it is not permissible to construct dwelling units for residence of weaker sections of society - It is not a contract for transfer of property but is a contract to carry out scheme which is incapable of being carried out at this stage on account of reservation in master plan and also repeal of Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - At best plaintiff could pray for damages - Appeal allowed.
