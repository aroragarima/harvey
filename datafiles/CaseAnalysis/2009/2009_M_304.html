Urban Improvement Trust, Bikaner v Mohan Lal

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1524; (2010) 1 SCC 512; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 504; 2010 (1) AWC 994; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 42; 2010 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 3639; JT 2009 (14) SC 355; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1547; 2009(13) SCALE 671; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 550

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Land & Property

Keywords: Illegal, Public Authorities

Summary: Consumer Protection - Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Deficiency of service - Illegal encroachment of plot - Petitioner trust allotted plot to respondent and thereafter without any notice to respondent illegally encroached the same - District Forum directed refund of allotment price paid with interest - On appeal, State Commission directed petitioner to allot an alternative plot and also awarded compensation - National Commission dismissed revision petition filed by petitioner - Hence, present Special leave petition - Whether National Commissioner's order could be upheld? - Held, petitioner did not offer any explanation for its negligence or action of taking over allotted plot without notice, acquisition, or consent of respondent - National Commission justified in not interfering with decision of State Commission - No case made out to grant special leave u/art. 136 of Constitution - Special leave petition dismissed.
