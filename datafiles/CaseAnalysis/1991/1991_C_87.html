Â In Re : Cauvery Water Disputes Tribunal

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Ranganath Misra, K.N. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1094; AIR 1992 SC 522; JT 1991 (4) SC 361; 1991(2) SCALE 1049; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Water

Keywords: Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, Assessors, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Ajmer-Merwara (Extension of Laws) Act, 1947, Karnataka Cauvery Basin Irrigation Protection Ordinance, 1991, River Boards Act, 1956

Summary: (A) Constitution - Water - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 143(1) and 262 - Inter-State water dispute - In exercise of the powers conferred by s. 4 of the Act, the Central Government constituted a Water Disputes Tribunal called "the Cauvery Water Disputes Tribunal" for the adjudication of the Water Dispute regarding the Inter-State River Cauvery - Tribunal passed an interim Order - Differences arose with regard to certain aspects of the Order - Meanwhile, the Governor of Karnataka promulgated the Karnataka Cauvery Basin Irrigation Protection Ordinance, 1991 - Doubts have been expressed with regard to the constitutional validity of the Ordinance and its provisions - In view of this, in exercise of the powers conferred u/art. 143(1) of the Constitution of India, President of India, made the present reference - Whether the Ordinance and the provisions thereof are in accordance with the provisions of the Constitution? - Held, provisions of the Act clearly show that apart from its title, the Act is made by the Parliament pursuant to the provisions of art. 262 of the Constitution specifically for the adjudication of the disputes between the riparian States with regard to the use, distribution or control of the waters of the inter-State rivers or river valleys - The Act is not relatable to Entry 56 and, therefore, does not cover either the field occupied by Entry 56 or by Entry 17 - Since the subject of adjudication of the said disputes is taken care of specifically and exclusively by art. 262, by necessary implication the subject stands excluded from the field covered by Entries 56 and 17 - It is not, therefore, permissible either for the Parliament under Entry 56 or for a State legislature under Entry 17 to enact a legislation providing for adjudication of the said disputes or in any manner affecting or interfering with the adjudication or adjudicatory process of the machinery for adjudication established by law under Art. 262 - Therefore, Karnataka Cauvery Basin Irrigation Protection Ordinance, 1991 passed by the Governor of Karnataka was beyond the legislative competence of the State and, therefore, ultra vires the Constitution - State legislature would even otherwise be incompetent to provide for adjudication or to affect in any manner the adjudicatory process or the adjudication made in respect of the inter-State river waters beyond its territory or with regard to disputes between itself and another State relating to the use, distribution or control of such waters - Any such act on its part will be extraterritorial in nature and, therefore, beyond its competence - Further, admittedly, the effect of the Ordinance is to affect the flow of the waters of the river Cauvery into the territory of Tamil Nadu and Pondicherry which are the lower riparian States - Ordinance has, therefore, an extra-territorial operation - Hence the Ordinance is on that account beyond the legislative competence of the State and is ultra vires the provisions of art. 245(1) of the Constitution - Order accordingly.

(B) Constitution - Water - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 262 - Adjudication of disputes relating to waters of interstate rivers or river valleys - Held, an exclusive power is given to the Parliament to enact a law providing for the adjudication of such disputes - Disputes or complaints for which adjudication may be provided relate to the "use, distribution or control" of the waters of, or in any interstate river or river valley - Provisions clearly indicate the amplitude of the scope of adjudication inasmuch as it would take within its sweep the determination of the extent, and the manner, of the use of the said waters, and the power to give directions in respect of the same - Order accordingly.

(C) Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 262 - Distinction between Art. 262 and Entry 56 - Held, whereas art. 262(1) speaks of adjudication of any dispute or complaint and that too with respect to the use, distribution or control of the waters of or in any inter-State river or river valleys, Entry 56 speaks of regulation and development of inter-State rivers and river valley - Thus the distinction between art. 262 and Entry 56 is that whereas former speaks of adjudication of disputes with respect to use, distribution or control of the waters of any inter-State river or river valley, Entry 56 speaks of regulation and development of inter-State rivers and river valleys - Entry 17 likewise speaks of water, that is to say, water supplies, irrigation and canals, drainage and embankments, water storage and water power subject to the provisions of Entry 56 - Order accordingly.

(D) Constitution - Water - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 262 - Whether the Order of the Tribunal constitutes a report and a decision within the meaning of s. 5(2) of the Act and required to be published by the Central Government in order to make it effective? - Held, sub-section (1) of s. 5 expressly empowers the Central Government to refer to the Tribunal not only the main water dispute but any matter appearing to be connected with or relevant to it - A request for an interim relief whether in the nature of mandatory direction or prohibitory order, whether for the maintenance of status quo or for the grant of urgent relief or to prevent the final relief being rendered infructuous, would be a matter connected with or relevant to the main dispute - Interim orders passed or reliefs granted by the Tribunal when they are not of purely procedural nature and have to be implemented by the parties to make them effective, are deemed to be a report and a decision within the meaning of Sections 5(2) and 6 of the Act - Hence the order of the Tribunal will be a report and decision within the meaning of s. 5(2) and would have, therefore, to be published u/s. 6 of the Act in order to make it effective - Order accordingly.

(E) Constitution - Water - Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 262 - Whether the Water Disputes Tribunal constituted under the Act is competent to grant any interim relief to the parties to the dispute? - Held, a Water Disputes Tribunal constituted under the Act is competent to grant any interim relief to the parties to the dispute when a reference for such relief is made by the Central Government - Further, question whether the tribunal has power to grant interim relief when no reference is made by the Central Government for such relief is a question which does not arise in the facts and circumstances under which the Reference is made - Order accordingly.
