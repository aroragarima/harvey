State Of Orissa v Chandrika Mohapatra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 176; (1976) 4 SCC 250; (1976) SCC (Cr) 584; AIR 1977 SC 903; 1977 CRLJ 773; 1977 CrLR(SC) 327; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Withdrawal from prosecution - CrPC, 1973,s.494 - Before trial could commenced, prosecution filed for withdrawal from prosecution which was allowed by Magistrate - Suo-motu proceedings were undertaken by HC against order of Magistrate and it was set aside - Whether setting aside of order of Magistrate by HC was justified? - Held, No - Public Prosecutor has to make out some ground which would show that the prosecution is sought to be withdrawn because inter alia the prosecution may not be able to produce sufficient evidence to sustain the charge or that the prosecution does appear to be well founded or that there are other circumstances which clearly show that the object of administration of justice would not be advanced or furthered by going on with the prosecution - For withdrawal of prosecution, ultimate guiding factor is interest of administration - No hard and fast rules or any categories can be prescribed - Present case, evidence not sufficient to prove charges against appellant - If State is of view that elimination of social or economic cause of crime would be better served by not prosecuting, it should clearly be at liberty to withdraw from prosecution - Appeal allowed.
