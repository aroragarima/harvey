Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education v K. S. Gandhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 880; (1991) 2 SCC 716; AIR 1991 SCW 79; JT 1992 (2) SC 296; 1991(1) SCALE 442; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 772; 1991 (2) SLR 682

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Questionnaire, West Bengal Panchayat Act, 1973, Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Boards Act, 1965, Maharashtra Secondary and Higher secondary Education Boards regulations 1977, Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Educations Board Regulations, 1977

Summary: Education - Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Boards Act, 1965, s. 19 - Higher Secondary Examination - Tampering of Moderators' mark sheet - Enquiry - Power of the Standing Committee constituted by the Divisional Board - Held, State Board is empowered to constitute Divisional Boards and the Standing Committees - State Board is also empowered to make regulations to conduct examinations and to deal with the matter of using unfair means at the examinations conducted by the Board - Similarly, Divisional Board is empowered to conduct examination within its area and to deal with the cases of unfair means according to the procedure laid down by State Board u/s. 19 of the Act - State Board is empowered to constitute the Standing Committee - The same way, the Divisional Board is also empowered to constitute Examination Committee or Standing Committee - Standing Committee is an executive arm of the Divisional Board - When the enquiry was conducted by some members of Divisional Board and the Standing Committee was taking the decision thereon, it is acting on behalf of the Divisional Board - Thus under the scheme of the Act, Standing Committee is clearly within its power to take final decision - Education Standing Committee of the Divisional Board, itself a statutory body, acted on behalf of the Divisional Board and is not a delegate of the Divisional Board - (B)Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Higher Secondary Examination - Fabrication of Moderators' Mark sheets - Privy of the candidates or their parents or guardians to such fraudulent fabrication - Question of fact - Investigation is in progress - U/arts. 226 or 136, High Court or Supreme Court cannot sit as court of appeal on the findings of facts recorded by the Standing Committee nor have the power to evaluate the evidence - If the conclusions reached by the Board fairly supported by the evidence on record, HC or Supreme Court would upheld the decision though as appellate court of facts, may be inclined to take a different view - (C)Education - Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Educations Board Regulations, 1977 - Higher Secondary Examination - Fabrication of Moderators' mark sheet - Enquiry - Denial of assistance of an advocate for students - Allegation of violations of principles of natural justice - Held, assistance to the delinquent in domestic enquiry is not a part of principles of natural justice - It depends nature of the enquiry and facts and circumstances of the particular case - Regulations and rules under the Act specifically deny the assistance of an advocate in enquiries - Show cause notice provided the opportunity to each students to give explanation and to give oral and documentary evidence - Answer sheets and marks were perused by the students and asked to testify its veracity - Students answered positively and admitted that Moderators' mark sheets were fabricated - Questionnaire was also given to indicate their educational background in the previous school years and the expected mark at the final examination - Questionnaire can be answered by the students without any assistance - No case of coercion to answer in a particular manner - It is evident from the records, they had prior consultation with their counsel - Procedure adopted at the inquiry is fair and just, not vitiated by any procedural irregularity or violation of principles of natural justice - (D) Education - Higher Secondary Examination - Fabrication of Moderators mark sheet - Enquiry - Allegation of Enquiry Report only contains conclusion bereft facts and reasons and handwriting of more than one persons - Held, in the enquiry of 283 students, enquiry officer might had the assistance of some other staff for writing the Report - Therefore, Report cannot be jettisoned on the ground that the conclusion drawn mechanically without applying mind to the facts - Hence, Enquiry Report is valid - (E) Education - Higher Secondary Examination - Fabrication of Moderators' mark sheet - Enquiry - Omission of recording reasons - Validity - Held, unless rules excludes specifically, principles of natural justice or fair play does require recording of reasons - In administrative decisions, the record should at least disclose reasons - It may not be like a judgment but precise - Factum of fabrication admitted by the students - Not expedient to reiterate all the admissions made by 283 students - Facts are not disputed - In the circumstance of the admission of facts, need for reiteration obviated, conclusion have been stated in the reports - Omission to record reason is neither illegal nor violative of principles of natural justice - Enquiry is valid.

SLP allowed.
