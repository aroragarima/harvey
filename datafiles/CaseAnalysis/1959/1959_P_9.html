Kedar Nath Motani and Others v Prahlad Rai and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 220; AIR 1960 SC 213; 1960 SCJ 1072; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Benami Transaction - (A) Whether maxims in pari delicto etc is applicable in present case ? - Where both parties do not show that there was any conspiracy to defraud a third person or to commit any other illegal act, maxim, in pari delicto etc,can hardly be made applicable - Held, appellants and respondents were not in pari delicto, respondents claimed to be innocent parties who had acquired lands themselves, and appellants stated that respondents knew nothing about matter and were not even consulted, therefore, application of maxim was erroneous - (B) Whether fraud was effected upon Bettiah Raj, and whether it was successful ? - Appellants' evidence show that Bettiah Raj was informed about benami nature of transaction and Bettiah Raj's Assistant Manager, under Court of Wards, was informed about this; Narain Lal deposed that in his presence Radhumal had told Moti Lal Basu that he was taking settlements in farzi names of his relations; further, in 1936 when dispute went to District Magistrate, all these facts were set out in rival cases of parties and Magistrate knew by 1936 that tenancies were taken benami by Radhumal, but after expiry of lease, did not enforce cl. 16 in spite of this knowledge - Further held, it appears that fraud was not effected because person or authority said to be defrauded knew all facts, and elected not to take any action and in view of fact that fraud cannot be said to have been effected, appellants who have clearly established benami nature of transactions cannot be deprived of their judgment - (C) Whether maxim ex turpi causa non oritur actio is applicable in present case ? - What one has to see is whether illegality goes so much to the root of the matter that plaintiff cannot bring his action without relying upon illegal transaction into which he had entered, if illegality be trivial or venial, and plaintiff is not required to rest his case upon that illegality, then public policy demands that defendant should not be allowed to take advantage of position, a strict view, of course, must be taken of plaintiff's conduct, and he should not be allowed to circumvent illegality by resorting to some subterfuge or by mis-stating facts, if, however, matter is clear and illegality is not required to be pleaded or proved as part of cause of action and plaintiff recanted before illegal purpose was achieved, then, unless it be of such a gross nature as to outrage conscience of court, plea of defendant should not prevail - Further held, in present case, object of benami transaction was merely to keep property from being resumed by benami on expiry of lease in favour of benami by marriage, information about benami transaction was, however, not withheld from benamidar, and even with that knowledge, benamidar took no action against benami by marriage or benamidars, plaintiffs recanted inasmuch as they asserted their true title and true facts before occasion for Benamidar to act arose, thus, fraud, though intended, was not perpetrated, because fraud could only be effected at the end of lease term, and locus poenitentiae which lessee possessed was duly used long before expiry of lease, further, plaintiffs could make out a case of benami nature of transaction, without having to rely upon additional fact that signatures of another benamidar and others were made upon application and forms without their knowledge, therefore, there is no room for application of maxim in all its rigour, and exceptional case applied, appellants were entitled to a decree in their favour - Appeal disposed of.
