Nand Kishore Gupta and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 720; (2010) 10 SCC 282; AIR 2010 SC 3654; 2011 (2) AWC 1788; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 186; JT 2010 (10) SC 521; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 518; 2010(9) SCALE 175; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 356; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 710

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh, Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, U.P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1996

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 and 6 - U.P. Industrial Area Development Act, 1996 - In year 2001, State Government took a decision to construct Yamuna Expressway - This was to be done on Build, Operate and Transfer (BOT) basis and the builder was to get the rights to collect the tolls for a period of 36 years from the date of commencement of commercial operations - Award of contract was given to the Company-respondent No. 5 and a concession agreement dated 7.2.2003 entered - Essential features of transaction was (i) Project was to be implemented on Build Operate and Transfer model, (ii) Project conceived of construction of Expressway as well as development of land parcels at five different locations and (iii) land for development was to be provided to selected bidder on a lease of 90 years upon payment of acquisition cost and necessary lease rentals - However, on account of public outcry, State Government appointed a Commission of Enquiry - Public Interest Litigation was also filed - Concession Agreement was also challenged on ground that award of contract to the Company was activated by mala fides and that the tender process itself was faulty - Project was cleared in enquiry and Public Interest Litigation also ended in favour of Government of Uttar Pradesh - Thereafter, notification dated 20.2.2009 was issued by Government of Uttar Pradesh u/s. 4(1) r/w ss. 17(1) and 17(4) of Act for acquisition of land for a public purpose, namely, construction of interchange under Yamuna Expressway Project in District Agra through Yamuna Expressway Industrial Development Authority (YEIDA) - Writ Petitions was filed by land owners challenging notification u/ss. 4 and 6 of Act challenging application of urgency u/ss. 17(1) and 17(4) of Act to these acquisitions, thereby depriving appellants of an opportunity to be heard u/s. 5A of Act - HC declined to give any direction to State Government to consider to exempt 21.03 hectares of land relating to 35 petitioners on account of fact that construction of road had to be made in an alignment and that alignment could not be changed - HC dismissed writ petition holding that (a) entire process of acquisition was in accordance with provisions of Act and this was not a colourable exercise of powers, (b) land in instant case was indeed acquired for public purpose, namely, construction of Yamuna Expressway Project, (c) land was not acquired for respondent No.5-Company and as such procedure under Chapter VII was not applicable - Present appeals - (A) Whether object of acquisition was not covered by definition of 'public purpose' in s. 3(f) of Land Acquisition Act? - Held, where acquisition of land is for companies, it cannot amount to a public purpose - In present case, this acquisition was not for Company but was for public purpose - Expressway is a work of immense public importance - State gains advantages from construction of an Expressway and so does general public - Therefore, it cannot be said that acquisition was not for a public purpose - (B) Whether acquisition itself must be considered to be under Part VII of Act since it virtually amounts to acquisition of land for company-respondent No.5? - Held, project conceived and justified by State Government, while concessionaire was to be chosen only to implement Project and there was going to be no vesting of land - Further, there was full transparency in whole process leaving no scope to infer any bias in favour of Company-respondent No. 5 - Further, it is not as if power of acquisition was exercised by State Government for work or Project of Company - Lastly, it is not a case where power was exercised by State Government so that acquired land was to belong or vest permanently in Company-respondent No. 5 for its own purpose - Company-respondent No.5 would get no proprietary or ownership rights over Project assets - Assets to revert back to acquiring body or, as case may be, Government - (C) Whether there was necessity to apply ss. 17(1) and 17(4) of Act and, therefore, Government could not have dispensed with enquiry u/s. 5A of Act? - Held, there was necessity to dispense with enquiry u/s. 5A of Act in this Project considering various reasons like enormousness of Project, likelihood of encroachments, number of appellants who would have required to be heard and time taken for that purpose, and fact that Project had lingered already from 2001 till 2008 - Challenge by appellants on ground that there was no urgency and, therefore, enquiry u/s. 5A of Act should not have been dispensed with, cannot be accepted - Appeals dismissed.
