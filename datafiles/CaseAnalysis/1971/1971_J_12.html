Jage Ram And Ors v State Of Haryana And Ors

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 132; (1971) 1 SCC 671; AIR 1971 SC 1033; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 871

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(f), 6(3), 17, 17(2) and 38 - (A) Industrialisation of an area - Whether in public interest - (B) Whether the purpose for which the land was needed was a public purpose or not - (C) Govt. contributed money towards cost of land sought to be acquired - Need to proceed with the acquisition under Part VII of the Act - (D) Fact that State Govt. or the party was lethargic at an earlier stage is not very relevant for deciding whether on the date of notification there was urgency or not - (E) Clause (c) - Interpretation of - Held, is in public interest - Unless it is shown that there was colourable exercise of power, the Court cannot go behind the declaration of the Govt. and find out in a particular case whether the purpose for which the land was needed was a public purpose or not - it is not necessary to proceed with the acquisition under Part VII of the Act - Conclusion of Govt. that there was urgency though not conclusive is entitled to great weight - Cannot be interpreted, by the application of the rule of ejusdem generis, as applying to cases similar to those mentioned in Clauses (a) and (b) - Appeal Dismissed.
