Sub Inspector Sadhan Kumar Goswami And Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1788; (1997) 2 SCC 225; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 532; JT 1996 (10) SC 236; 1996(8) SCALE 295; 1997 (2) SLJ 29; 1997 (1) SLR 262; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 814; 1996 (8) Supreme 216; 1997 (1) UJ 215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 32 and 141 - Admitted facts are that petitioners who joined service under Special Security Bureau (SSB) in North - Eastern Region of India, claimed special duty allowance as per order of Central Govt. - Petitioners are relying upon Office Memorandum which provided that 'it is not applicable from one station to another within region of Groups A and B staff will further continue to get facilities - They have filed writ petition contending that while Groups C and D employees have been denied benefit of above judgment, special duty allowance benefit is being granted to Groups A and B; it tantamounts to violation of art. 14 - Held, petitioners are Groups C and D employees and are bound by above declaration of law made by Supreme Court - Merely because they were not parties to judgment, they cannot file writ petition u/art. 32 - Thus, petitioners are not entitled to payment of special duty allowance irrespective of whether or not they were parties to judgment rendered in Vijayakumar case [(1994 Supp (3) SCC 6491994 (S3) SCC 6491994 Indlaw SC 1055 : 1995 SCC (L and S) 1891995 SCC(L and S) 1891994 Indlaw SC 1055 : (1994) 28 ATC 5981994 (28) ATC 5981994 Indlaw SC 1055; they cannot be permitted to raise new grounds, though not raised or argued in earlier case, to canvass correctness of judgment by filing writ petitioner u/art. 32 - It is seen that Govt. have limited payments already made after date of judgment of this court; payments made prior to that date are not being recovered - Under those circumstances, court does not find any substance that there will be any justification to direct respondents not to recover amount from petitioner after date of judgment of this court - Appeal allowed.
