State of Orissa v Arjun Das Agrawal and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 629; (1999) 8 SCC 154; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1378; AIR 1999 SC 3229; 1999 CRLJ 4078; JT 1999 (6) SC 14; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 302, 149, 451, 427, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Respondents along with 14 other accused persons were charged of murdering deceased - Trial Court after elaborate enquiry held that deceased met with homicidal death and convicted respondents along with 7 other accused and acquitted remaining accused - Aggrieved all convicted accused filed appeals in HC - Appeals of these respondents were allowed and other appeals were dismissed by DB - Hence instant appeal by State Govt. - Whether HC was justified in acquitting respondents - Held, Courts below relied on evidence of eye witnesses which was corroborated with medical evidence - Minor contradictions were not considered by HC as they could not demolish prosecution case - Prosecution witnesses proved presence and active participation of respondent no.2 during offence and resisted PW 1 from rescuing deceased - There was no evidence on record to show respondent no.1 active participation in incident or by his words accused gave more blows to deceased - HC had recorded reasons for acquittal of respondent no. 1 and did not record any reasons on respondent no.2 acquittal - SC held that accused charged under different sections could be convicted u/s. 34 IPC following principle laid down by SC in in Mahabir Gope Vs. State of Bihar, 1962 Indlaw SC 162 and in Jagir Singh Vs. State of Punjab, 1967 Indlaw SC 116 and in Bharwad Mepa Dana and another Vs. State of Bombay, 1959 Indlaw SC 187 holding that all persons of unlawful assembly were liable of guilt even if act was done by one person of that unlawful assembly - Settled law was that s.34 IPC would not create distinct offence and it would be attracted if participation of accused with common intention of committing crime was established - Respondent no.2 participation in crime by giving fatal blows to deceased was established by prosecution and hence appeal against respondent no.2 was allowed and convicted him u/ss. 302 and 34 IPC - As there was no evidence of participation of respondent no.1 in crime except instigating but that too was not proved and hence appeal against respondent no.1 was dismissed as rightly held by HC - Appeal partly allowed.
