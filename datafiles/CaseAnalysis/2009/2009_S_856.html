Raghavendra Rao Etc v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 164; (2009) 4 SCC 635; 2009 (2) ESC 216; JT 2009 (2) SC 520; 2009(2) SCALE 554; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 223; 2011 (4) SLR 394

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Karnataka Civil Services (Special Recruitment of Local Candidates) Rules, 1986

Summary: Service - Karnataka State Civil Services (Direct Recruitment to Class-III Posts) (Special) Rules, 1973 - Regularisation - Held, appellants claimed their right to be appointed and/or consequential regularization in the services on the basis of the hereditary right - Having been appointed by a person who had no authority, the offers of appointment made in their favour must be held to be nullities - Such illegal appointments cannot be brought within the purview of r. 3(2) - Merely because an employee had continued under cover of an order of Court, he would not be entitled to any right to be absorbed or made permanent in the service - Appeals dismissed.
