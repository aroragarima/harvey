Ajay Kumar Garg v Gaurav and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 391 - Recording of additional evidence - Instant appeal filed against the order passed by HC, whereby, during the course of hearing of the appeal preferred by the convict, the HC has ordered that X, who according to the evidence of PWs2 and 6 had apprehended the convict be summoned for giving evidence - HC also summoned the officer-in-charge of Pushpanjali Hospital where the two deceased ladies had been examined/treated, with all the material records for its examination - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, admission of additional evidence should not operate in a manner prejudicial to the prosecution or the defence. Though wide discretion is conferred on the Court, the same has to be exercised judicially. Thus, recording of reasons is a condition precedent for exercise of power u/s. 391 of the CrPC and an order bereft of reasons would tantamount to non-application of mind, rendering the exercise of power under the Section, bad in law. In the instant case, impugned order suffers from the vice of non-application of mind. The impugned order is set aside and the matter is remanded to the HC. Appeal allowed.
