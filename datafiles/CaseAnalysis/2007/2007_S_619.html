Namdeo v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 204; (2007) 14 SCC 150; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 100; 2007 (2) ALT 237; 2007 CRLJ 1819; JT 2007 (4) SC 618; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 893; 2007(4) SCALE 337; 2007 (4) SCJ 724; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 939; 2007 (4) UC 228

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Chance Witness

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - s. 302 - Appeal against the judgment of High Court dismissing the writ petition filed against the conviction order of Session Judge - Validity of solitary testimony of eye witness, the deceased son questioned claiming him to be the interested party - Held, if on such scrutiny, his evidence is found to be intrinsically reliable, inherently probable and wholly trustworthy, conviction can be based on the 'sole' testimony of such witness, close relationship of witness with the deceased or victim is no ground to reject his evidence - Appeal dismissed.
