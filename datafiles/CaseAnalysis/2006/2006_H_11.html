Hamza Haji v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 398; (2006) 7 SCC 416; AIR 2006 SC 3028; JT 2006 (8) SC 215; 2007 (2) MLJ 304; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 304; 2006(8) SCALE 75; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 604

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971, ss. 3(2), 3(3), 8, 8A, 8B - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 215, 226 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 44 - Kerala Land Reforms Act, s. 82 - Application seeking a declaration that application scheduled property was not a private forest liable to be vested in Govt. - Claim of appellant was upheld by Tribunal u/s. 3(3) of Act by rejecting plea of absence of title in appellant based on a pending litigation as set up by State - HC directed State to take back 20 acres of land said to have been put in possession of appellant - Appeal against - Held, when he made claim, appellant neither had title nor possession over land - Order of Forest Tribunal was procured by appellant by playing a fraud and said order is vitiated by fraud - Appeals dismissed.
