Acharaparambath Pradeepan & Anr v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 956; (2006) 13 SCC 643; 2007 (1) JCC 828; 2007 (1) KLT 249; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 928; 2006(13) SCALE 600; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1101

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 143, 147, 148, 342, 449, 302 r/w 149 - All accused were sentenced to death - Appeal against - Whether we should uphold the death sentence imposed upon A1? - Held, why the Investigating Officer took a long time for arranging a test identification parade has not been disclosed - Certain factors are required to be considered as regards reliability of the testimony of the child witnesses but it is also an accepted norm that if after careful scrutiny of their evidence the court comes to the conclusion that there is an impress of truth in it, there is no obstacle in the way of accepting the evidence of child witnesses - It cannot be said to be a rarest of rare case warranting imposition of the extreme punishment - So far as A1 is concerned converted the death penalty to rigorous imprisonment of life - Other accused persons are given benefit of doubt and they are acquitted - Appeals disposed of.
