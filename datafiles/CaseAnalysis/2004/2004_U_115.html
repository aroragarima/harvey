Union of India and others v Ramchander and another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1836; (2005) 9 SCC 365

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act,1947, ss.25-F,25-G - Termination from service - Reinstatement - Violation of provisions - Respondents were appointed as casual labourers mazdoors in stores maintained by Army - Respondents were terminated from their service and challenged same before Tribunal - Respondents alleged that they had completed requisite 240 days but for intermittent termination of their services and their termination was violative of ss.25-F and 25-G of Act - Tribunal did not accept submission of respondents and refused to grant any relief - Respondents preferred petitions before HC which directed that they should be reinstated in service - Hence instant appeals.

Held, it is found that respondents were appointed as against selection posts and they were also sponsored by employment exchange and they had also undergone medical examination and that termination orders served on respondents were to see only that these respondents did not continue in service for period of 240 days. Respondents were appointed against casual labourers but nevertheless they continued in service for four spells and that too their reappointments were made immediately within few days of termination on completion of 89 days. It shows that sufficient work was available with employer and had there been no termination on completion of 89 days, they would have completed 240 days of continuous employment. In that view of matter appellants had violated s.25-G of Act. Court does not find any error or illegality in decision rendered by HC. Court directs appellants to re-employ respondents as daily-wagers. However, direction of HC for payment of entire salaries and allowances for period they were out of service was not justified under circumstances. Therefore, Court directs appellants to re-employ respondents with 50% back wages from date of their termination till their re-employment. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - As per s.25G of 1947 Act, where any workman in industrial establishment and in absence of any agreement employer shall ordinarily retrench workman who was last person to be employed in that category, unless for reasons to be recorded employer retrenches any other workman.
