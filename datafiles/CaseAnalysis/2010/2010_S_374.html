Raj Transport Co. Pvt. Ltd., Amritsar v State Transp. Appellate Tribunal Pb. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 984; (2010) 13 SCC 460; 2011 (84) ALR 737; JT 2010 (13) SC 12; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 212; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 379; 2010(12) SCALE 241; 2010 (3) UC 2117

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Regional Transport Authority, Enhancement Compensation, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, State Transport Undertaking, Notified Area

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicle Act, 1988 - Grant of stage-carriage permits - Pursuant to a notification issued inviting applications for grant of four stage-carriage permits for plying two return trips daily, appellants and one of the respondents applied for the same - Thereafter, only two stage carriage permits with one return trip daily were granted to one of the respondents and remaining two permits were withheld - Appeals were filed thereagainst - During the pendency of proceedings, Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 was repealed by the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 which came into force effective from 01.07.1989 - Appeal filed by the Indian Bus Service was dismissed as being barred by limitation, whereas the appeals filed by the appellants were allowed and it was held that since two permits were available, therefore, the same be granted to four appellants - Writ petitions were filed thereagainst by Indian Bus Service wherein HC remitted the matter back to the Appellate Tribunal - After the remand, the Appellate Tribunal, dismissed all the five appeals including the appeals filed by the appellants - Writ petitions were filed before HC which were dismissed - It was held that after coming into force of the Transport Scheme as notified on 09.08.1990, which was subsequently modified vide notification dated 21.10.1997, it has to be give effect to, as the same was applicable and in light of the same, the route could not have been granted to the private operators because a stretch of 83 Kms. of the route in question falls on the monopoly route of Punjab Roadways and PRTC and therefore it was held under the Transport Scheme that the appellants were not entitled to grant of any permit - Hence, present appeals - Whether appellants entitled to the benefit provided by the substitution of a new clause in place of old clause (4) brought in the year 1997? - Held, scheme was introduced in the year 1990 and the substitution of a new clause in place of old clause (4) was brought about in the year 1997 and in the said modification/provision it was clearly provided that only those permits which were granted by the Regional Transport Authority before coming into force of the scheme to the private operators for operating on monopoly routes, wholly or on portion thereof would remain unaffected - The relevant date is the date of the grant of the permit and such date cannot relate back to any earlier period, particularly in view of the amendment which gives emphasis on the date of grant of the permit, which factually came to be granted only after promulgation of the Scheme - In the present case, the date when the Appellate Tribunal passed the order granting permits in favour of the two appellants would be the relevant date on which the appellants were granted permits - As on the said date, when the aforesaid provision was inserted, the appellants had no permit granted in their favour by the Regional Transport Authority and therefore it must be held that the appellants had no permit in their favour granted by the Regional Transport Authority before coming into force of the scheme - Consequently, the aforesaid scheme does not give any protection or benefit to the appellants - Appeals dismissed.
