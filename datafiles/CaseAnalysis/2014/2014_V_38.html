Vikram Singh and another v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 302; (2014) 11 SCC 238; JT 2014 (7) SC 194; 2014 (124) RD 14; 2014 (2) RLW 1573; 2014(5) SCALE 636

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1980

Summary: Land & Property - Rajasthan Imposition of Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings Act, 1973 - Rajasthan Tenancy Act, 1955 - Rajasthan Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1980 - Ceiling on Agricultural Holdings - Chapter III-B of 1955 Act was introduced by s. 3 of 1980 Act - Agriculturists were required to surrender excess land which should vest in the State Govt. - As per the provisions of Chapter III-B of 1955 Act, appointed date was fixed as 1-4-1966 by the agriculturists in accordance with the provisions of Chapter III-B of 1955 Act - Petitioners surrendered their land in pursuance of the said order and no appeal was preferred - Hence, the said order became final - 1955 Act was repealed by an Ordinance and thereafter by the 1973 Act, the State Govt. was given power to avail the remedies under 1973 Act against the case decided under the old ceiling law of Chapter III-B by making provision u/s. 15(2) of 1973 Act - An application was filed before the District Collector u/s. 232 of 1955 Act with a prayer to re-open the mutation made in accordance with the decision in Ceiling Case No.13/68 of the predecessors of the petitioners and prayed for cancellation of the said decision and to refer the matter before the Board of Revenue - District Collector issued notice fixing a date - Issuance of such notice had been challenged on the ground of inordinate delay in initiation of such proceedings u/s. 232 of 1955 Act - Petitioners filed writ petition challenging said notice - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC rightly dismissed appeal filed by the petitioners -

Held, the facts which have been pleaded by the respondents in the counter would show that on the basis of the misrepresentation, the order passed in the land ceiling cases, in particular Ceiling and the declaration which was filed by the ancestors of the petitioners, would reveal that the declarations which have been given by the predecessors of the petitioners, suffered from suppression of material facts - It was also revealed from the facts that there was an allegation of fraud which requires an enquiry - Therefore, the notice had been issued only to make an enquiry in the matter - Hence, in the given facts, such notice could not be said to be bad at this stage - Petitioners would only face the enquiry - In view of that, Court does not intend to interfere with the order passed by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
