H. L. Gulati v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 389; 2015 (6) AWC 6121; JT 2015 (4) SC 39; 2015(4) SCALE 353; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 722; 2015 (3) SLR 711

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punishing Authority

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 9 - Alleged fraudulent activities - Awarding of punishment - Justification - Appellant was holding charge of post of Senior Accounts Officer when it was discovered that 36 fraudulent claims came to be authorized by him, resulting in unauthorised release of amount - Further, investigative process, indicated involvement of large number of officers - FIR was registered and appellant was charge sheeted - Further, punishing authority held departmental enquiry and vide order punishment authority held appellant guilty and punished him u/r. 9 of Rules whereby 50 % of pension and gratuity was withheld by punishing authority - Further, review petition filed by appellant was rejected - However, Trial Court vide judgment held appellant not guilty and appellant came to be discharged from criminal case - Tribunal held that enquiry report did not record any finding of grave misconduct and punished imposed on appellant u/r. 9 of Rules is not sustainable - Further, HC set aside order passed by Tribunal and review petition filed was rejected - Whether punishment imposed by authority upon appellant is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, responsibility vested on enquiry officer is limited to determination of innocence or guilt of delinquent employee, with reference to charges levelled against him. It is on establishment of charges, that punishing authority will record finding, whether conclusions lead to further inference, that delinquent has committed acts of 'grave misconduct' or 'grave negligence'. It is on such determination by punishing authority that r. 9 of Rules can be invoked, in case delinquent employee has, in meantime, retired on attaining age of superannuation. SC held that when punishment was inflicted upon appellant by order dt. 30-11-2005, appellant had already retired from service having superannuated on 30-6-2002. Further, SC held that appellant was not guilty of any 'grave misconduct' because enquiry report only found that appellant was negligent in discharge of his duties. Further, appellant was discharged from criminal prosecution initiated against him with reference to same sequence of facts. Hence, appellant may have been negligent in discharge of his duties, but it is not possible to conclude, that appellant was guilty of 'grave misconduct'. Hence, finding recorded in impugned order that appellant had committed acts of 'grave misconduct' cannot be accepted. Hence, impugned order passed by punishing authority is set aside. Further, SC held that keeping in view delinquency proved and established against appellant in enquiry report which was accepted by punishing authority, ends of justice would be met if punishment of withholding 50% of his gratuity is maintained/sustained. Further, with respect to permanent withholding of 50% of appellant's pension, it would be just and appropriate to sustain same till end of current month, and to order release of 100% of appellant's pension w.e.f. 1-3-2015. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Punishment can be awarded when charges are proved against accused and is accepted by punishing authorities.
