K.S. Paripoornan (Ii) v State of Kerla

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Bharucha, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 511; (1995) 1 SCC 367; AIR 1995 SC 581; 1995 (1) AnWR 5; 1994 (3) CCC 540; 1995 (1) RentLR 90; 1994(4) SCALE 951; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Land Acquisition Act, 1961, s. 3(1) and 11 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1894, ss. 15 (b), 18, 23(1-A) and 28 - Appellant received enhanced compensation under Kerala Act, and High Court disallowed interest u/ss. 28 and 23(1-A) of the Land Acquisition Act, as amended under Amendment Act - Whether the appellant is entitled to the enhanced interest as envisaged u/s. 28 as amended u/s. 18 of the Amendment Act? - Held, if the sum which the Collector ought to have awarded as compensation, is in excess of the sum which the Collector did award as compensation, the court shall direct the Collector to pay interest on such excess at the rate of 9% per annum from the date on which the Collector took possession of the land to the date of payment of such excess into the court - Appeals allowed.
