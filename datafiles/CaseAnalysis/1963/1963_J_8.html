Jagdish Mitter v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 83; AIR 1964 SC 449; 1964 (1) LLJ 418

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311, 311(2) - Government of India Act, 1935, S. 240(1), 240(3), r. 55(b) - Posts and Telegraphs Manual, r. 126 - Quasi-permanent servant - Absence of Enquiry - Disciplinary Proceeding - Termination of Services - Appellant/Temporary servant, was working in status of quasi-permanent servant - Impugned order was passed by respondent/Union terminating services of appellant in terms of his contract, based on Disciplinary Proceeding - Appellant filed a suit claiming a declaration that termination of his services was illegal u/r. 126 of Posts and Telegraphs Manual and no enquiry was made after he was furnished with charge sheet - Sub-Judge hold that since appellant was a temporary Government servant, his service could be terminated without holding any enquiry u/s. 240(3) of Act, 1935 and dismissed appellant's suit - On appeal to District Judge, hold by appellant was allowed - Respondents were aggrieved by this and preferred appeal which was allowed - Appellant then applied to HC, which passed an impugned order against appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether order of termination of services of appellant amounted to his dismissal u/s. 240(1) of Act, 1935 and Whether appellant/temporary servant could continue his service - Held, Every order terminating services of a public servant, who was either a temporary servant, or a probationer, would not amount to dismissal or removal from service u/art. 311 of Constitution - Temporary public servant or a probationer could not be dismissed or removed from service without affording him protection guaranteed by u/art. 311(2) of Constitution - Act of fairness on part of authority to make such an enquiry and public servant should be given a chance to explain his conduct before such authority reaches a conclusion in that matter - HC committed error in concluding that appellant was not dismissed, but merely discharged - Appellant had been denied protection guaranteed to temporary servants u/s. 240(3) of the Act, 1935 or Art. 311(2) of Constitution and so said order of dismissal could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.
