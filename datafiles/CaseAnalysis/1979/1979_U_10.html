Supreme Court of India

20 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 271; (1979) 3 SCC 431; (1979) SCC (Cr) 691; AIR 1979 SC 898; 1979 CRLJ 773; 1979 CrLR(SC) 219; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Duty, Repugnancy, State Law, Madras City Municipal (Corporation) Act, 1919

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Ss. 21(12), 239 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, Art. 254(2) - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1952 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, Ss. 161, 468 and 471 - Madras City Municipal (Corporation) Act, 1919 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Ss. 5(1)(d), 5(2) - Master And Servant - Public Duty - Repugnancy - Letter was written by Chief Secy. to Govt. to DIG of Police, requesting to make a detailed investigation of abuse of their official position in purchase of wheat, against appellant/former Chief Minister and others - Sanction u/s. 197 of Code was granted by Governor for prosecution of appellant u/ss. 161, 468 and 471 of Code and s. 5(2) r/w. 5 (1)(d) of 1988 Act - Appellant submitted that there was no relationship of master and servant between him and Govt. and he was acting as a constitutional functionary and therefore, not a public servant by s. 21(12) of 1860 Code - Appellant filed an application for discharging him u/s. 239 of the Code and Special Judge rejected application and on appeal to HC was also rejected - Hence instant appeals - Whether there was real repugnancy resulting into irreconcilable inconsistency between State Act and Central Acts u/art. 254 of Constitution and Central Act stood repealed - Held, provisions of a Central Act and a State Act in Concurrent List were fully inconsistent and were absolutely irreconcilable, Central Act would prevail and State Act would become void in view of repugnancy u/art. 254(2) of Constitution - Court was in complete agreement with the view taken by HC, that State Act created new and distinct offences and was not in any way repugnant to any provisions of Central Acts and consequently overruled - Hence, appellant could be prosecuted only under Corruption Act and under Code, 1860 and not under any other Act - Court, agree with s. (12)(a) of 1988 Act in which "in the service of the Government' undoubtedly signified a relationship of master and servant - Appellant/Chief Minister undoubtedly performed a public duty of the nature u/art. 167 (a) to (c) of the Constitution - Appellant/Chief Minister was a public servant and holder of public office under Constitution - Court was satisfied that appellant/Chief Minister or a Minister was undoubtedly a public servant u/s. 21(12)(a) of 1860 Code and HC was absolutely correct in law - Appeals disposed of.
