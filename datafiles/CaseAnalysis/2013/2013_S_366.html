Kashi Vishwanath v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 422; (2013) 7 SCC 162; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 415; 2013 CRLJ 3655; 2013 (3) JCC 2076; JT 2013 (11) SC 136; 2013 (4) KarLJ 497; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 420; 2013(8) SCALE 620; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 627

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 498A - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed offence punishable u/ss. 498-A and 302 r/w 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 498-A and 302 r/w 34 IPC and sentenced to undergo life imprisonment - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was affirmed the order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting the appellant - Held, in first dying declaration, deceased stated that her husband instigated her to pour kerosene on her body, therefore, she poured kerosene on her body and her husband further poured kerosene on her and put on fire using a match box - In second dying declaration deceased stated that her husband along with L poured kerosene on her body and put on fire by using match stick - In third dying declaration deceased stated that her husband poured kerosene on her and L lit the match stick and thrown upon her body - However, the credibility of 3 dying declarations was to be doubted - In first dying declaration, thumb impression of victim was shown - Whereas in second dying declaration taken on same day and third dying declaration given on next day, the victim had stated that she had not given her signatures since her hand was completely burnt - Prosecution had failed to state as to why 3 dying declarations were recorded in Kannada, if deceased was talking in Telugu - It was unable to come to the conclusion that prosecution in instant case had established its case beyond reasonable doubt to base a conviction on appellant - Both Courts below have erred in coming to contra conclusion - Conviction recorded by Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
