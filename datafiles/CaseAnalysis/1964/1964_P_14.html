New Asiatic Insurance Company Limited v Pessumal Dhanamal Aswani and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1964 SC 1736; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 693; 1964 (2) SCJ 428; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 867

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 94, 95 and 96(2) - Whether driver, Pessumal comes within meaning of persons indemnified in para 3 of policy issued by company? - Held, yes - Company indemnified any person who is driving motor-car on insured's order or with his permission - Pessumal was driving car with permission of insured, and therefore company undertook to indemnify Pessumal in accordance with this provision of para 3 - Company, by agreeing with person who affects policy, to insure him against liability to third parties, takes upon itself entire liability of person effecting insurance - It is open to insurer not to extend this idemnity to insured, to other persons but if it extends it to other persons, it cannot restrict it vis a vis right of third party entitled to damages, to recover them from insured, a right which is not disputed - Company had insured Pessumal in view of para 3 of s. II of policy and that it comes within expression 'insurer' in s. 96 - Company did agree under policy to indemnify drivers who drove car with insured's permission - Appeals dismissed.
