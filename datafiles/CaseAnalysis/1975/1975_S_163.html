State of Karnataka and Others v Gunjahalli Nagappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 546; (1976) 1 SCC 204; AIR 1975 SC 1708; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 57; 1975 UJ 568

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Municipalities Act, 1964, ss. 13, 14(2)and 38 - Mysore Municipalities (Election of Councilors) Rules, 1965, r. 75 - Representation of People Act, 1950, s. 22 - Whether, in present case, State Govt. had power u/r. 75 to make impugned order cancelling calendar of events fixed by Returning Officer, thereby in effect setting at naught entire election process which had proceeded upto stage of poll? - Plain reading of s. 14(1) shows that electoral roll for territorial area comprised in a division is to be list of voters for such division; there is no separate qualification laid down for being placed in list of voters for a division - What is required by this sub-section is that list of voters of a division should correspond with electoral roll for territorial area included in division - If there is any mistake in electoral roll, it cannot be corrected by Returning Officer while preparing list of voters for each division - Only way in which mistakes, if any, can be corrected is by applying for rectification of electoral roll u/s. 22 of Representation of People Act; so long as such rectification is not made, entries in electoral roll would stand and they would necessarily be reflected in list of voters for division, but they would not constitute mistakes, so far as preparation of list of voters for division is concerned; it is only if list of voters for division does not correspond with electoral roll for territorial area comprised in division that it can be said that list of voters is defective - Held, in present case, list of voters for each division corresponded fully and completely with electoral roll for territorial area comprised in such division; only defect which Returning Officer noticed on physical verification was that voters shown in electoral roll as residing in territorial area of one division were in fact residing in another, but, that cannot be regarded as a defect in division-wise lists of voters and it would not stamp them with vice of not being in conformity with requirements of Act - Further held, State Govt. was not entitled to make impugned order u/r. 75 on ground that divisional lists of voters were defective and election held on basis of such lists of voters would not be in accordance with provisions of Act - Poll in present case must, therefore, be taken on basis of list of voters for each division prepared with reference to electoral roll in force on date on which calendar of events was published by Returning Officer - Appeal dismissed.
