Shiv Kant Yadav v Indian Oil Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1218; (2007) 4 SCC 410; AIR 2007 SC 1534; 2007 (3) ALJ 523; 2007 (6) AWC 6078; JT 2007 (5) SC 257; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 841; 2007(5) SCALE 402; 2007 (5) SCJ 404; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Whether furnishing of incorrect information or concealment of facts would result in rejection of application and violation of appellants own declaration? - Object of requiring information in form and certification thereafter by candidate was to ascertain and verify character and antecedents to judge his suitability to continue in service and a candidate having suppressed material information and/or giving false information cannot claim right to continue in service - Employer having regard to nature of employment and all other aspects had discretion to terminate his services, which is made expressly clear in offer of appointment - Held, if any factual mis-statement or declaration is made, that permits cancellation of allotment - Appeal dismissed.
