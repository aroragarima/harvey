State of Jharkhand and another v Harihar Yadav and others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 752; (2014) 2 SCC 114; 2014 (2) ALD(SC) 29; 2013(14) SCALE 133; 2014 (5) SLR 685

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Bihar Reorganisation Act, 2000, s. 65 - Bifurcation of State - Liability to pay salary to employees - Govt. company was floated by the State of Bihar in the name of Bihar Hill Area Lift Irrigation Corporation Limited (BHALCO) in public interest - On 9-1-2002 a memorandum of cabinet was drawn - Proposal of acquisition and adoption of BHALCO as Jharkhand Hill Area Lift Irrigation Corporation (JHALCO) was placed - Thereafter JHALCO issued notices in the local newspaper calling for applications from the employees of BHALCO for absorption of their services in JHALCO - However, due to disagreement and difference between the two States, a decision was taken to initiate the liquidation proceedings and the Central Govt. in exercise of its power u/s. 65 of the Act directed the State of Bihar to initiate liquidation proceedings - In the State of Bihar many a Govt. corporation and companies were not paying the salaries to the employees - At that stage, a writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution was filed before SC asserting, inter alia, that various Govt. companies/public undertakings situate in the State of Bihar have not paid salaries to their workmen and other employees for a long time resulting in deaths and insurmountable miseries befell on large number of families dependant on such employee - SC held that Govt. of the State of Bihar had a constitutional obligation to protect the life and liberty of the employees of the Govt.-owned companies/corporations who were the citizens of India - Directions were issued - In the said writ petition application was filed, wherein the principal relief sought was for issue of a direction to the respondent State/JHALCO to immediately comply with the SC order and pass the order of absorption in JHALCO with respect to 213 employees - Thereafter, Single Judge of HC took up the hearing of the writ petition and dealt with the issue as to Whether BHALCO in view of the provision as enshrined in s. 65 of the Act would be considered to be the same entity only with a change in its name as JHALCO or JHALCO was a separate entity than BHALCO - It was held that BHALCO and JHALCO could not be said to be two separate entities rather BHALCO in terms of s. 65 of the Act could be said to have been functioning as JHALCO - It was further held that the petitioners were entitled to be absorbed in the services of JHALCO from the date when they have made applications for their absorption, pursuant to direction given by the SC and they were entitled to get their salaries from the date of their absorption which was to be paid by the JHALCO as other employees whose services were accepted by the JHALCO were also paid from the date of joining - On appeal, HC held that the relationship between the employees and the employer had not become extinct by any proceeding or operation of law and directions were issued - HC directed the State of Bihar to pay the salary till 16-6-2011 and directed JHALCO to absorb the unabsorbed employees - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, when a State was bifurcated by a Parliamentary legislation, both the States and the Centre were required to take certain decisions under the Act and they were required to be taken in quite promptitude and not leaving the poor employees high and dry and suffer for no fault of theirs - In the instant case, State of Jharkhand took a decision to take over BHALCO and thereafter resiled - DB correctly opined that such a decision could not have been taken unilaterally by the State of Jharkhand - Their action reflected the attitude of emotionlessness, proclivity of impassivity and deviancy with cruel impassibility - Neither of the States nor the Corporations thought about the livelihood of the employees Directions issued to State Govts. - Directions issued - Appeals disposed of.
