Sri Abani Kanta Ray v State Of Orissa And Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2139; (1995) Supp4 SCC 169; (1996) SCC (L&S) 175; 1996 (32) ATC 107; JT 1995 (7) SC 467; 1996 LabIC 982; 1995 (2) LLN 1089; 1995(6) SCALE 41; 1995 (5) SLR 638; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 333

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Transfer - Validity of - (A) Whether Tribunal was justified in interfering with order of transfer? - Incident of service is not to be interfered with by courts unless it is shown to be clearly arbitrary or vitiated by mala-fides or infraction of any professed norm or principle governing transfer - Held, there was no permissible ground available to Tribunal for quashing it - (B) Whether critical comments and adverse remarks made against appellant in impugned order sustainable? - Proper discharge of his responsibilities by appellant to ensure proper functioning of tribunal is sufficient to indicate total lack of justification for any adverse command against appellant - Further held, observations made by Division Bench of Tribunal against appellant as Chairman of Tribunal were wholly unwarranted, illegal and improper - All disparaging remarks in impugned order against appellant are, therefore, expunged - Appeal allowed.
