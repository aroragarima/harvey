State of Uttar Pradesh v Chandrika

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 802; (1999) 8 SCC 638; (2000) SCC (Cr) 16; AIR 2000 SC 164; 1999 (4) CCR 216; 1999 (4) Crimes 137; 2000 CRLJ 384; 2000 (2) JCC 645; JT 1999 (8) SC 481; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 730; 1999(6) SCALE 689; 2000 (1) SCJ 385; 1999 (9) SLT 25; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 239; 1999 (8) Supreme 723

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 320 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304 Part I and 34 - Plea bargain - High Court allowed appeal of accused on his confessing guilt and sentenced him to sentence already undergone - Held, it is settled law that on basis of plea bargaining court cannot dispose of criminal cases - Court has to decide it on merits - If accused confesses his guilt, an appropriate sentence is required to be imposed - Further, approach of court in appeal or revisions should be to find out whether accused is guilty or not on basis of evidence on record - If he is guilty, an appropriate sentence is required to be imposed or maintained - If appellant or his counsel submits that he is not challenging order of conviction, as there is sufficient evidence to connect accuse with crime, then also court's conscience must be satisfied before passing final order that said concession is based on evidence on record - In such cases, sentence commensurating with crime committed by accused is required to be imposed - Mere acceptance or admission of guilt should not be a ground for reduction of sentence - Nor can accused bargain with court that as he is pleading guilty, sentence be reduced - Acquittal set aside - HC directed to decide appeals on merits - Appeal allowed.
