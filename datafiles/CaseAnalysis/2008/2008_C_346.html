Rikhabsao Nathusao Jain v Corporation of the City of Nagpur and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2661; (2009) 1 SCC 240; 2009 (1) Bom.C.R. 681; JT 2008 (11) SC 532; 2009 (2) MahLJ 601; 2008(13) SCALE 726; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1091; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 58

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - City of Nagpur Corporation Act, 1948, s. 286(5) - Jurisdiction of District Judge - Power to grant mandatory injunction - Whether District Judge have limited jurisdiction u/s. 286(5) and an order of mandatory injunction could not have been passed by it? - Held, jurisdiction of the District Judge can be invoked if a building is erected or re-erected in contravention of any town planning scheme or building bye-laws - Hence, keeping in view the nature of jurisdiction conferred upon the District Judge as also in view of the fact that the Civil Court's jurisdiction has been excluded in determining the said question, there is no other option but to hold that the jurisdiction of the District Judge is limited - Appeal allowed.
