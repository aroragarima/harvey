Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat Ii v Kurban Hussain Ibrahimji Mithiborwala

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 342; (1972) 4 SCC 394; 1973 (2) CTR 454; [1971] 82 ITR 821; 1972 TAXLR 1013; 1972 UJ 65

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 22(2)and 34 - Whether notice issued u/s. 34 was irregular and if so, whether such irregularity vitiated the proceedings taken under the said notice? - ITO's jurisdiction to reopen an assessment u/s. 34 depends upon the issuance of a valid notice - In the notice issued u/s. 34 the ITO sought to reopen the assessment of the assessee for the assessment year 1948-49 but in fact he reopened the assessment of the year 1949-50 - Held, the notice in question was invalid and ITO had no jurisdiction to revise the assessment for the year 1949 - Appeal dismissed.
