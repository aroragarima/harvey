Federal Bank Limited and Others v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 317; (2007) 4 SCC 188; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 44; 2007 (2) DRTC 33; JT 2007 (5) SC 91; 2007 (3) KLT 106; 2007(4) SCALE 613; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 313; [2007] 6 VST 736

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930, Kerala Finance Act, 1998

Summary: Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - whether banks are "dealers" under s. 2(viii) read with Explanation I? - Held, banks, in selling the goods pledged to them, did not act as agents of the borrowers/pledgors and that their sale was in exercise of statutory power under the 1949 Act - Dealing in non-banking assets is a banking business and the exception made in s. 8 which allows banks to realize security and which allows banks to engage in trade in order to realize security, falls within the banking business whenever undertaken - Once such transactions fall under s. 2(viii)(g) of the 1963 Act, banks become "dealers" and they are liable to pay sales tax under the said 1963 Act - Appeals dismissed.
