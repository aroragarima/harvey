Mustafa Shahadal Shaikh v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 354; (2012) 11 SCC 397; AIR 2013 SC 851; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 3785; 2012 CRLJ 4763; 2012 (4) JCC 2677; JT 2012 (9) SC 20; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 23; 2013 (2) RLW 1568; 2012(8) SCALE 692; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 560

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B, 306 and 498-A and 34 - Cruelty - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused and deceased marriage was solemnized and after marriage, deceased was staying with appellant in her matrimonial home - Deceased committed suicide by consuming poison - PW4 lodged an FIR alleging torture and harassment faced by the deceased on account of demand for dowry - Charge sheet against appellant was filed u/ss. 306, 304-B and 498-A r/w s. 34 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant - Aggrieved on appeal HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that all the witnesses relied on by the prosecution were close relatives and no outsider had been examined to prove the case - Whether the prosecution had made out a case in respect of the charges leveled against appellant relating to ss. 304B and 498A IPC - Held, SC could not expect outsiders to come and depose what had happened in the family of deceased - It had been already highlighted that the death occurred within a period of 7 months from the date of marriage and deceased died at her matrimonial home - It has also come in evidence from the prosecution witnesses that on the date of the death, appellant and his parents alone were in the house - Evidence of all the witnesses was corroborated with each other - Prosecution evidence, clearly proved the ingredients of cruelty - Prosecution had clearly established the offence u/s. 304B IPC and the same had been rightly accepted by the Trial Court and confirmed by the HC - Prosecution had established its case beyond reasonable doubt by placing acceptable evidence and of the fact that minimum sentence of 7 years had been prescribed, it could not be possible to award sentence less than 7 years - Impugned judgment and order of Courts below were upheld - Appeal dismissed.
