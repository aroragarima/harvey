Vijay Ranglal Chourasia and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 483; (2014) 12 SCC 400; 2014 CRLJ 3946; JT 2014 (7) SC 186; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 731; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 755

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Kidnap

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 341, 342, 343, 346, 364A - Kidnap - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused persons/appellants u/ss. 120B, 341, 342, 343, 346, 364A r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, HC ought to have addressed questions falling for determination before it, viz., whether evidence recorded in Trial Court was or could be transferred to case against appellant and read against him and if such evidence recorded in Sessions Case was not or could not be transferred, was there any other evidence to support an order of conviction against him - These questions having escaped attention of HC, case would, in our opinion, call for remand to HC to enable it to hear and dispose of matter afresh - Order passed by HC was set aside and matter was remitted back to HC for fresh hearing and disposal in accordance with law keeping in view observations made above - Appellant should be released from custody on furnishing bail bonds in sum of Rs. 50,000/- with two sureties to the satisfaction of HC - Appeal disposed of.
