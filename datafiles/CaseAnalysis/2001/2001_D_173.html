Union of India and Others v Dinesh Engineering Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21324; (2001) 8 SCC 491; AIR 2001 SC 3887; AIR 2001 SCW 4779; 2001 (45) ALR 443; 2001 (3) ARBLR 438; JT 2001 (8) SC 84; 2001(6) SCALE 390

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, art. 14 - Tender - Rejection of Offer - Justifiability - Respondents responded to tender of appellant and was later informed that Railway board had reviewed policy of purchase - Hence, tender was not accepted - Respondent filed writ petition seeking writ of mandamus commanding appellant to finalize offer and issuance of writ of certiorari quashing order with regard to purchase of spare parts - HC held that respondent was sole competitor to 'X' for supply of parts - Further policy of appellant created monopoly in favor of 'X', was wholly arbitrary & discriminatory and order of Railways with regard to purchase of spare parts was quashed - Hence, instant appeal- Appellant contended that respondents did not had requisite nor capacity to manufacture a governor or its genuine spare parts and HC was erred in passing impugned order - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justifiable - Held, in instant case, perusal of letter does not show that Board was either aware of existence of respondent or its capacity or otherwise to supply spare parts required by appellants/railways for replacement in governors used by it, an ignorance which was fatal to its policy decision - Thus, any decision be it simple administrative or a policy decision, if taken without considering relevant facts could only be termed as arbitrary decision, which would be violative of mandate of art. 14 of Constitution - Status of 'X' vis-a-vis respondents would be same in regard to the supply of spares to GE-governors - Further power of appellant u/cl. 16 of Guidelines which could be exercised on existence of certain conditions which in opinion of appellant was not in interest of Department to accept offer - No such ground had been taken while rejecting tender of respondent - Therefore, merely because authority had certain elbow room available for use of discretion in accepting offer in contracts, same would had to be done within 4 corners of requirements of law, especially art.14 of Constitution - HC was justified in quashing order of appellant/railway board and barred Board from purchasing spare parts under proprietary basis from 'X'without calling for tenders and to consider offers received on merits - Appeals dismissed.
