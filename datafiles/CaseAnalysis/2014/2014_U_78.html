Velaxan Kumar v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 852; (2015) 4 SCC 325; AIR 2015 SC 1462; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 171; 2015 (109) ALR 242; 2015 (1) AWC 194; 2015 (1) Bom.C.R. 370; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 41; JT 2014 (13) SC 592; 2015 (6) MahLJ 24; 2015 (4) MPLJ 284; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 404; 2015 (127) RD 345; 2014(14) SCALE 37

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Entitlement of, Applicability of beneficial provisions of 2013 Act

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 11, 17 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(2) - Land acquisition - Applicability of beneficial provisions of 2013 Act - Entitlement of - Land acquisition notification u/ss. 4, 17 of the Act was issued acquiring the land in question belongs appellant (landowner) and others - Appellant filed a petition before HC and challenged the Award passed by the Land Acquisition Officer, which was dismissed by impugned common judgment - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal and pending appeal the 1894 Act was repealed and its place the 2013 Act was enacted which came into force with effect from 1-1-2014 and thus appellant sought applicability of beneficial provision of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act - Appellant contended that more than 5 years prior to the commencement of 2013 Act, Award was passed and physical possession of land appellant was neither taken and he was still in physical possession by making construction of one room and boundary wall much prior to issuance of notifications over his acquired land.

Held, from facts and circumstances of case, the Award passed u/s. 11 of 1894 Act was passed on 3-4-2002 in respect of the disputed land, therefore, it is an undisputed fact that it was passed 5 years prior to the commencement of 2013 Act and the compensation for the acquisition of the appellant's land has not been paid to the appellant. It is clear from the facts and circumstances of the case that actual physical possession of the land in question has not been taken by the respondents. Even if, for the sake of argument it is accepted that possession of the land was taken by the respondents, it is clear that due procedure has not been followed by the Acquisition Authority by way of preparing proper 'Panchnama' in the presence of independent witnesses and the land-holders, and therefore it is contrary to the principles law laid down by SC. Therefore, the acquisition proceedings of the land of the appellant are lapsed in view of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act as both the conditions under the said provision are fulfilled in the present case. Neither compensation has been paid by the respondents to the appellant for the said acquisition even though more than 5 years have elapsed from the date of Award when 2013 Act came into force nor physical possession of the land belonging to the appellant has been taken by the respondents. Therefore, the acquisition proceedings in respect of the appellant's land have lapsed in terms of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act. The acquisition proceedings in respect of the appellant's land have lapsed. Appeal allowed.
