Ram Kumar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28159; (1988) 1 SCC 306; (1988) SCC (L&S) 329; AIR 1988 SC 390; 1987 (5) ATC 404; 1988 (1) CCC 81; JT 1987 (4) SC 534; 1988 LabIC 942; 1989 (2) LLJ 72; 1988 (1) LLN 331; 1987(2) SCALE 1189; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 138; 1989 (1) SLJ 102; 1988 (1) SLR 677; 1988 (1) UJ 276

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 39(d) and 311 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Writ petition - Railways - Temporary railway servants and casual labour acquiring temporary status - (A) Railway Establishment Manual, para 2511 - Entitlement to pension - (B) Equal treatment and pay for equal work and absorption in regular cadre in permanent category - When some empanelled for being granted temporary status, some already joined and some continuing on temporary status - Held, not admissible to them - Supreme Court directed Railways to take prompt steps to screen those who were not tested for purpose of regularising their service - Petition disposed of.
