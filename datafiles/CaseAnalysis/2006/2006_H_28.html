Meera Chauhan v Harsh Bishnoi and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1244; (2007) 12 SCC 201; 2007 (1) ADJ 216; 2007 (1) AllCJ 749; 2007 (1) ARC 336; 2007 (1) AWC 469; 2007 (1) CTC 89; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 155; JT 2007 (1) SC 458; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 597; 2006(13) SCALE 581; 2007 (7) SCJ 798; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 965; 2007 (1) UC 414

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Forcible Dispossession, Restoration Of Possession, Application For Impleadment, Saving Of Inherent Powers Of Court

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Possession of the property - Order of restoration - Legality of - Respondent No. 1 held an interim injunction restraining respondent No. 2 from only transferring, alienating or encumbering Suit property - No interim injunction was granted against respondent No. 2 from interfering with possession of respondent No. 1 - When said injunction was in force, Appellant purchased suit property from respondent No. 2 and was put into possession of same - Writ Petition was filed by respondent No. 1 in HC claiming forcible dispossession during his absence and praying for restoration of possession - Appellant got herself impleaded in original suit for declaration for title which was filed by respondent No. 1 against respondent No. 2 in respect of suit property - Application u/s. 151, CPC, 1908, was moved by respondent No. 1 for restoration of possession on allegations that he was dispossessed from suit property during pendency of suit and while injunction was in force - Trial Court allowed said application directing Respondent No. 2 and Appellant to restore possession of suit property - Appellant filed a revision application which was rejected - Order challenged in this Special Leave Petition - Whether HC and Trial Court were justified in directing restoration of possession upon application u/s. 151? - Held, approach of HC was unsustainable - It failed to notice that there was no order of injunction restraining respondent No. 2 from interfering with possession of respondent No. 1 - HC was not justified in rejecting revision application without going into propriety of Trial Court order - (B) Whether an application to pass order of injunction in form of restoration of possession u/s. 151 can be entertained by Court when specific provision under O. 39 existed? - Held, s. 151 confers wide powers on Court to make such orders as may be necessary for ends of justice or to prevent abuse of process of Court - Power of s. 151 to pass order of injunction in form of restoration of possession of Code is no longer res integra following Manohar v. Hira Lal (1961 Indlaw SC 266) - Trial Court came to a finding that respondent No. 1 was in possession of suit property till respondent No. 2 had forcibly dispossessed him and therefore he was entitled to get his possession restored as he was evicted without following any legal procedure - Respondent No. 1 simply prayed for an order of injunction restraining respondent No. 2 from transferring, alienating or encumbering suit property till disposal of application for injunction - While allowing Application for restoration of possession, HC and Trial Court failed to notice pendency of Writ Petition in which prayer for restoration for possession was main issue and fact of pendency of suit u/s. 6 of Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Order of HC and Trial Court set aside and Trial Court directed to decide application for restoration afresh and thereafter coming to conclusion of fact as to who was in actual possession of suit property at relevant time - Appeal is allowed.
