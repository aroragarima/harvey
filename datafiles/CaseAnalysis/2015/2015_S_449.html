V. Krishnakumar v State of Tamil Nadu and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 443; (2015) 9 SCC 388; 2015 ACJ 2028; AIR 2015 SC 2836; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 123; 2015(5) ALL MR 474; 2015 (113) ALR 227; 2015 (4) AWC 4257; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 345; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 332; 2015 (3) CPJ(Sc) 15; JT 2015 (6) SC 503; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2853; 2016 (2) MahLJ 555; 2015 (5) MLJ 613; 2016 (2) MPLJ 52; 2015 (3) RLW 2316; 2015(7) SCALE 275; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 100

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Medical Negligence - Complainant wife delivered premature baby in Govt. hospital and baby was administered oxygen at time of birth and underwent blood exchange transfusion - Later, when baby was taken to pediatrician for giving DPT shots, doctor suspected Retinopathy of Prematurity (ROP) and after examination by doctors, it was reported that ROP reached stage 5 - Complainant filed case against hospital and doctors therein who treated baby for medical negligence and NCDRC held that complainant at no stage, was warned or told about possibility of occurrence of ROP by respondents even though it was their duty to do so and held respondents liable for medical negligence and awarded compensation to complainant - Hence, instant appeals, one by State against judgment of NCDRC and another appeal by complainant for enhancement of compensation - Whether medical negligence could be attributed to hospital or doctors who treated baby.

Held, respondents were negligent in their duty and were deficient in their services in not screening the child between 2 to 4 weeks after birth for ROP when it is mandatory to do so and especially since child was under their care. Thus, the negligence began under the supervision of respondent no. 2-hospital. The respondent nos. 3 and 4, who checked baby at his private clinic and at the complainant's home, respectively were also negligent in not advising screening for ROP. Hence, respondents are liable to pay compensation for their negligence and deficiency in service. Further, compensation is computed at Rs.1,38,00,000 to be payable by respondents and further sum of Rs.42,87,921 in lieu of past medical expenses was also granted. Appeal of complainant is allowed and appeal of State dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - A premature baby who was administered supplemental oxygen and has been given blood transfusion is prone to higher risk of ROP and as per medical literature, ROP starts developing 2 to 4 weeks after birth when it is mandatory to do the first screening of the child.
