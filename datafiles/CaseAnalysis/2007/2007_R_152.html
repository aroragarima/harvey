Basanti Devi v Raviprakash Ramprasad Jaiswal

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1435; (2008) 1 SCC 267; AIR 2008 SC 295; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 50; 2007 (69) ALR 914; 2008 (1) AWC 182; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 786; 2007 (1) CTC 698; JT 2007 (12) SC 273; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1263; 2008 (104) RD 478; 2007(12) SCALE 542; 2007 (8) SCJ 1; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 444

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: General Rules Of Succession, Heirs Of The Father, Heirs Of The Mother, Bombay High Court Rules

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 263 and 283 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Bombay High Court Rules - Probate - Grant of - Whether an application u/s. 263 of Indian Succession Act, 1925 for revocation of grant of probate would be maintainable, inter alia, on premise that appellant's name was not cited in said application for grant of probate? - Provisions contained in sub-s. (3) of s. 283 are mandatory in nature - Once statutory requirements are found to have not been complied with, an application for revocation of grant of probate would be maintainable in terms of s. 263 of Act, apart from fact that non-publication of citation could be one of ground to revoke grant of probate - Explanation (c) appended thereto in a case of this nature would be attracted - Revocation or annulment of probate for just cause - Grant of probate or letters of administration may be revoked or annulled for just cause - Held, it is well settled that an application for grant of probate is a proceeding in rem - A probate when granted not only binds all parties before Court but also binds all other persons in all proceedings arising out of Will or claims under or connected therewith - Being a judgment in rem, a person, who is aggrieved thereby and having had no knowledge about proceedings and proper citations having not been made, is entitled to file an application for revocation of probate on such grounds as may be available to him - Thus application for revocation of grant of probate should have been entertained - Appeal allowed.
