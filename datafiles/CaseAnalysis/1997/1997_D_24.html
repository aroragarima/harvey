Balwant Singh and Another v Daulat Singh (Dead) By L.Rs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1656; (1997) 7 SCC 137; AIR 1997 SC 2719; 1997 (3) CLT 159; JT 1997 (5) SC 703; 1997(4) SCALE 388; 1997 (6) Supreme 385; 1997 (2) UJ 96

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Hindu Succession Act, 1956; Evidence Act,1872, s.35- Revenue records do not convey or extinguish any title - Widow mutated lands in favour of her adopted sons - No title acquired by the adopted sons nor widows title in the property would get extinguished - No title as such was passed on under alleged 'mutation gift,' the limited right of widow in property would get enlarged on coming into force of Hindu Succession Act - Recovery of possession of the suit land - Held, appeal stands abated only in respect of properties given to deceased under gift deed -Possession and enjoyment as one on behalf of the minors in whose favour the mutation was earlier effected on 19.7.54 - By mutation the widow has not divested herself of title and possession.
