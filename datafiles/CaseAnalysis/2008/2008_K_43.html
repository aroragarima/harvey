Bollepanda P. Poonacha & Anr v K.M. Madapa

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 397; (2008) 13 SCC 179; AIR 2008 SC 2003; 2008 (71) ALR 899; 2008 (2) AWC 1817; 2008 (2) CTC 523; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 34; 2008 (4) KarLJ 489; 2008 (2) KLT 9; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 150; 2008(4) SCALE 141; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1011; 2008 (2) UC 1017

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Form Of Pleading, Set- Off

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, O. 8 r. 6A - Appellant filed suit claiming title and possession over the property - Whether a counter claim is permissible to be filed after filing of a written statement? - Held, a right to file counter claim is an additional right - It may be filed in respect of any right or claim, the cause of action therefor, however, must accrue either before or after the filing of the suit but before the defendant has raised his defence - Civil Judge was not correct in allowing the application for amendment of the written statement - Appeal allowed.
