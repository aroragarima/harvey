N.P. Thirugnanam (D) By Lrs v Dr. R. Jagan Mohan Rao And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1786; (1995) 5 SCC 115; AIR 1996 SC 116; AIR 1995 SCW 3803; 1996 (2) CLT 191; JT 1995 (5) SC 553; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 118; 1996 (2) RCJ 490; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 648; 1995 (2) RentLR 396; 1995(4) SCALE 465; 1995 (3) SCJ 386; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 53

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22, r. 6 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 16(c)- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Finding of fact - Suit for specific performance of contract - Dismissal - Finding that plaintiff was not 'ready and willing to perform' his part of contract - Evidence - Plaintiff was never ready with resources and money to fulfill his part of contract - Dismissal of suit for specific performance of contract - Held, it is finding of fact - No infirmity in Judgment warranting granting of leave - Justified - When arguments already heard and judgment reserved by time when party died - Decree passed by court against dead party - Not nullity.
