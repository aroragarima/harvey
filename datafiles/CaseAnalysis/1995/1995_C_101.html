B. P. L. Pharmaceuticals Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Vadodara

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 254; (1995) Supp3 SCC 1; 1995 (59) ECR 1; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 485; JT 1995 (4) SC 459; 1995 (8) RLT 569; 1995(3) SCALE 222; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 1235; 1997 (104) STC 164

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Soap, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955

Summary: Excise - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940, s. 3(b) - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Trade And Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Whether CEGAT erred in upholding classification of product manufactured by appellants as a cosmetic under sub-heading 3305.90? - Selenium sulphide has an unpleasant odour and to get rid of it an insignificant amount of perfume is used and made it acceptable to consumers; addition of an insignificant quantity of perfume to suppress smell will not take away character of product as a drug or medicine - Held, product 'Selsun' will fall under tariff item 3003.19 - Appeal allowed.
