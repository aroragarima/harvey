D. P. Gulati, Manager Accounts Jetking Infotrain Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 411; AIR 2015 SC 3760; 2015 CRLJ 4886; 2015 (2) JCC (NI) 129; JT 2015 (5) SC 445; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 515; 2015(4) SCALE 373; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of proceedings - Abuse of process of law - Criminal complaint was filed against respondent no.2 as cheques issued on behalf of his company to appellant company were dishonored - Later, respondent no.2 filed criminal complaint against appellant and other directors of appellant company relating to offences punishable under IPC - Aggrieved, appellant filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC for quashing of proceedings but the same was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether proceedings initiated against appellant were liable to be quashed.

Held, after examination of criminal complaint filed against respondent no. 2 and complaint filed by respondent no. 2 against appellant, it was clear case of abuse of process of law on part of respondent no. 2. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and impugned proceedings of criminal complaint filed against appellant and others by respondent no. 2 are also quashed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Litigants should not be allowed to file vexatious complaints to otherwise settle their scores by setting the criminal law into motion, which is a pure abuse of process of law and the same should be interdicted at the threshold.
