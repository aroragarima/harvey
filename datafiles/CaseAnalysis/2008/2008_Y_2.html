T. Nagappa v Y.R. Muralidhar

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 747; (2008) 5 SCC 633; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 677; AIR 2008 SC 2010; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1945; 2010 (1) BC 500; 2008 (2) JCC (NI) 211; JT 2008 (6) SC 153; 2008 (5) KarLJ 112; 2008 (3) KLT 158; 2008 (4) MPLJ 455; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 926; 2008(6) SCALE 642; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 959

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, s. 138 - Appellant filed an application under s. 243 of the Code of Criminal Procedure wrongly mentioned as s. 293 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 for referring the cheque in question for examination by the Director of Forensic Science Laboratory for determining the age of his signature - Application dismissed - Appeal dismissed - Held, non-mentioning or wrong mentioning of provision of law would not be of any relevance, if the Court had the requisite jurisdiction to pass an order - Appeal allowed.
