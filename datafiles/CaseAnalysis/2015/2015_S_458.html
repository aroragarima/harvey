State of Uttar Pradesh and another v Om Prakash

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 418; (2015) 4 SCC 467; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2025; 2015 CRLJ 1706; JT 2015 (1) SC 342; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1173; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 814; 2015(1) SCALE 380; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 667

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Rarest Of Rare, Advocate General, Aggravating, Revenge

Summary: (A) Criminal - Arms Act, 1959 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013, s. 7 - Sustainability of conviction - Complaint was lodged against accused persons alleging that accused carrying different types of arms attacked complainant and others - Trial Court held accused persons guilty for various offences under IPC, and u/s. 7 of 2013 Act and s.4 read with s.25 of 1959 Act and passed various sentences including death sentence against 12 of accused persons - On appeals by accused against conviction and reference by State for confirmation of death sentence, DB of HC affirmed conviction of accused and upheld sentence passed by Trial Court against all except 12 accused persons who were awarded death penalty and modified death penalty to imprisonment for life - Aggrieved, State and accused filed instant appeals - Whether conviction of accused could be upheld.

Held, in view of statements of PWs corroborated by medical evidence assessed with the facts and circumstances of the case, there are no reasons to disbelieve participation of accused in the criminal offence of killing five villages besides causing injuries to several others. Further, the recovery of deadly weapons from the possession of the accused strongly affirms the role played by each accused in the deadly act. Hence, judgments of Trial Court and HC with regard to conviction of accused could not be interfered as prosecution proved the guilt of accused beyond all reasonable doubts. Hence, appeals by accused against conviction are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Imposition of Death penalty in Rarest of rare cases - Whether offence committed by accused persons warranted imposition of death penalty.

Held, even though accused persons indulged in acts of most gruesome nature, yet, accused were on rampage and running berserk with the only sense triggered by the thrust of avenge. Further, even though incriminating circumstances proved by prosecution unmistakably and unerringly lead to guilt of accused, yet, after balancing all mitigating and aggravating circumstances of the case, it does not fall under the category of rarest of the rare cases. Further, the repetition of such criminal acts at their hands making the society further vulnerable are also not apparent and so, accused could be awarded lesser punishment than death penalty. Hence, HC was right in modifying death sentence awarded by Trial Judge to that of imprisonment for life. Hence, appeal of State is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Imposition of death sentence is an exception and should be awarded only in rarest of rare cases. Further, rarest of the rare case is when the collective conscience of the community is so shocked that it will expect the holders of judicial power to inflict death penalty, irrespective of their personal opinion as regards desirability or otherwise of retaining death penalty.
