Uday Shankar Triyar v Ram Kalewar Prasad Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 729; (2006) 1 SCC 75; AIR 2006 SC 269; 2006 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2006 (62) ALR 308; 2006 (1) AWC 9; 2006 (2) Bom.C.R. 636; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 24; JT 2005 (9) SC 454; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 192; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 18; 2006 (100) RD 253; 2005(9) SCALE 302; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 3, rr. 4C, 4(2), o. 41, r. 1, o. 6, r. 14 - Eviction decree against District Congress Committee - Appeal dismissed on ground that Vakalatnama accompanying memorandum of appeal was signed only by A N Singh and no Vakalatnama had been filed on behalf of DCC - Whether appeal by DCC against eviction decree was defective or invalid? - Whether such defect could be permitted to be rectified? - Held, non-compliance with any procedural requirement relating to a pleading, memorandum of appeal or application or petition for relief should not entail automatic dismissal or rejection, unless relevant statute or rule so mandates - Procedural defects and irregularities which are curable should not be allowed to defeat substantive rights or to cause injustice - HC was justified in setting aside dismissal and restoring first appeal to file of Additional District Judge with a direction to decide matter on merits - Appeal dismissed.
