Gimik Piotr v State Of Tamil Nadu & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1543; (2010) 1 SCC 609; AIR 2010 SC 924; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 308; 2010 CRLJ 877; 2009 (248) E.L.T. 7; JT 2009 (14) SC 273; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 643; 2009(13) SCALE 706; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Past Conduct, Single Act, Solitary Incident, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Prevention of Black Marketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980, Foreign Exchange Management (Export and Import of Currency) Regulations, 2000, Terrorism and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 (COFEPOSA), s. 3(1)(i) - Preventive detention - Appellant-Polish citizen detained by Tamil Nadu Government authorities u/s. 3(1)(i) COFEPOSA on apprehension that if released he would indulge in smuggling of foreign currency - On detention appellant was found to be in possession of a large amount of Indian and foreign currency - Appellant filed a writ petition before HC against the detention which was set aside by HC - Whether detention of appellant is correct under preventive detention? - Detention of person irrespective of his nationality without valid reasons amounts to infringement of fundamental rights - Held, preventive detention essentially deals with curtailment of a person's liberty and is therefore a potential weapon for human rights abuses - As facts reveal, there was no pressing need to pass a preventive detention order - Foreign currency cannot be smuggled as person cannot move out of country on account of his passport being impounded - Higher standard of proof required in circumstances involving life and liberty of a person - Material provided by respondents is not enough to justify curtailment of liberty of appellant under an order of preventive detention - Appeal allowed.
