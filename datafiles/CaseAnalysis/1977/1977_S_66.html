Dinesh Chandra Sangma v State Of Assam & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 82; (1977) 4 SCC 441; (1978) SCC (L&S) 7; AIR 1978 SC 17; 1977 LabIC 1852; 1978 (1) LLJ 17; 1978 (1) LLN 23; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 607; 1977 SLJ 622; 1978 (1) SLR 25; 1977 UJ 682

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Fundamental Rules, r. 56(c) - Defence and Internal Security of India Rules, 1971, r. 119 (3) - Voluntary retirement - Countermanding earlier order - Validity - Appellant was District Judge, attained the age of 50 years after serving for about 20 years under the Govt. - Appellant, served a notice u/r. 56 of F.R on the Govt. for voluntary retirement from service - Governor of State by a notification allowed appellant to retire from State Govt. service - HC also allowed appellant to go on one month's leave preparatory to retirement - Later, Govt. sought to retrace its steps and passed an order countermanding its earlier order of allowing him to retire from service - Appellant was transferred and asked him to join there immediately after the expiry of his leave - Appellant did not join, as he voluntarily retired from service on and from 2-8-1976, u/r. 56(c) of the Fundamental Rules - HC directed appellant to join his post on pain of disciplinary action - Appellant filed writ petition for quashing the order of Govt. - HC dismissed the application holding that F.R. r. 56(c) was subject to compliance with r. 119 (3) of 1971 Rules - Hence instant appeal challenging the impugned order of HC - Whether order passed by HC was valid - Held, appellant had voluntarily retired by 3 months' notice, not in accordance with an express or implied term of his contract of employment, but in pursuance of a statutory rule - Explanation 2 to r. 119 of 1971 Rules makes no mention of retirement under a statutory rule and hence the same was clearly out of the way - Submission that r. 119 of 1971 Rules was super-imposed on F.R. r. 56 had no force in instant case - HC committed an error on law in holding that consent of the Govt. was necessary to give legal effect to the voluntary retirement of appellant under F.R. r. 56 (c) - Since the conditions of F.R. r. 56(c) were fulfilled in instant case, appellant should be hold to have lawfully retired as notified by him with effect from 2-8-1976 - Permission accorded by the Govt. to retire and its subsequent order of 28-7-1976, revoking the permission, were ineffectual in law and were therefore null and void - Since appellant voluntarily retired in accordance with F.R. r. 56(c), the HC's order of 31-7-1976, on the administrative side, transferring him was invalid and was quashed - Impugned judgment and order of the HC were set aside and the Writ Petition was allowed - Appeal allowed.
