Rajahmundry Electric Supply Corporation Limited v A. Nageswara Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 72; AIR 1956 SC 213; 1956 ALT(SC) 279; [1956] 26 Comp Cas 91; 1956 (1) LLJ 227; 1956 (1) MLJ 95; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 95; 1956 SCJ 218; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1066

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Indian Companies Act

Summary: Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1913, ss. 153 C and 162 - (A) Whether facts found make this a case for passing a winding up order u/s. 162 or 153-C on ground of mismanagement and misappropriation of fund by directors? - Held, where nothing more is established than misappropriation of funds by directors, an order for winding up would not be just or equitable, provided it is a sound concern as it would be harsh on shareholders - But if circumstances render it in the interests of shareholders then nothing bars the order of winding up - Chairman either actively co-operated with Vice-Chairman in misconduct and maladministration or abdicated entire management to him and as the company is in a state of confusion, it is necessary to take action u/s. 153C - (B) Whether appointment of administrators in suppression of directorate and vesting power to manage company was an interference with its internal management? - Held, no - If a liquidator can be appointed to manage affairs of a company when an order for winding up is made u/s. 162, administrators could also be appointed to manage its affairs, when action is taken u/s. 153 - Appeal dismissed.
