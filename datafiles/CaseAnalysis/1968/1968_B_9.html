Bai Radha v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

20 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 171; (1969) 1 SCC 43; AIR 1970 SC 1396; 1970 CRLJ 1279; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 799

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic; Women & Children

Keywords: Woman

Summary: CrPC, 1898, s. 537 - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, ss. 15, 15(1) and 15(2) - Suppression of Immoral Traffic in Women and Girls Act, 1956 - Power to search - (A) Power is conferred by statute and not derived from recording of reasons - Omission to record reasons before or after search - (B) Non-compliance with s-ss. (1) and (2) - It is mere irregularity - (C) Non-compliance with s. 15 of 1956 Act - (D) Scope of s. 537 of CrPC, 1898 - Held, trial is not vitiated unless it is shown that prejudice is caused - Trial is not vitiated unless it is shown that prejudice is caused by non-compliance - It is irregularity curable u/s. 537, CrPC, 1898 - Held, section governs investigation, inquiry and trial of offences under 1956 Act - Appeal Dismissed.
