Babu and another v State Rep. By Inspector Of Police, Chennai

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 331; (2013) 4 SCC 448; AIR 2013 SC 1769; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1897; 2013 CRLJ 2176; 2013 (2) JCC 1340; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 304, 324 - Murder - Culpable homicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/A1 to A4, A5 and A6 assaulted deceased with sharp cutting weapons, due to which he received severe head injuries and succumbed to the injuries on spot - Charge sheet against A1 to A6 was filed - Trial Court convicted A1, A2 and A3 u/ss. 148 and 324 r/w s. 149 IPC and s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and also convicted A6 u/ss. 147 and 324 IPC r/w s. 149 and s. 302 IPC - A5 was acquitted from all the charges - Aggrieved on appeal HC maintained conviction of A1 to A4 and acquitted A6 - Hence instant appeals - Whether Courts below were justified in convicting appellants for the offences charged against them and whether instant case falls u/s. 304 (4) IPC - Held, language of Exception 4 to s. 300 was, thus, clear that culpable homicide was not murder if it was committed without premeditation in a sudden fight in the heat of passion upon a sudden quarrel provided the offender had not taken undue advantage or acted in a cruel or unusual manner - In instant case, there was no evidence to show that the deceased was armed in any manner when he questioned A1 as to why he had threatened his wife - Appellants were armed with knives and attacked deceased on his head and face even after he fell down - Thus, A1 to A4, who were the offenders, had taken undue advantage and acted in a cruel and unusual manner towards deceased who was not proved to have been armed - From the evidence of PW7/doctor who conducted the post mortem of deceased he found as many as 6 injuries on the head and face of deceased - PW7 had further stated that due to these injuries sustained on his head and face, deceased would have died as had been expressed by him in the post mortem report - Considering the nature of the injuries and, in particular, injury nos. 5 and 6, SC did not have doubt that the common intention of A1 to A4 was to cause the death of deceased - Appellants were guilty of the offences u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Appeals dismissed.
