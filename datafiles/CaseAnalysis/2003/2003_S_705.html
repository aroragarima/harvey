Chittarmal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 48; (2003) 2 SCC 266; (2003) SCC (Cr) 514; AIR 2003 SC 796; 2003 (1) CCR 122; 2003 (1) Crimes 363; 2003 (4) CRJ 681; 2003 CRLJ 889; 2003 (1) JCC 176; JT 2003 (1) SC 91; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 442; 2003(1) SCALE 55; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 49; 2003 (1) SLT 249; 2003 (1) Supreme 221; 2003 (2) UC 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 34, 447 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s.319 - Whether charge u/s. 149 overlaps ground covered by s.34 - Held, a clear distinction is made out between common intention and common object in that common intention denotes action in concert and necessarily postulates existence of a pre-arranged plan implying a prior meeting of minds, while common object does not necessarily require proof of prior meeting of minds or pre-concert - Though there is substantial difference between two sections, they also to some extent overlap and it is a question to be determined on facts of each case - Overt act and active participation is indicative of common intention of persons perpetrating crime, and in facts and circumstances, it cannot be doubted that appellants shared common intention to commit murders of deceased and acting in concert they executed their pre-arranged plan to eliminate them - Therefore, their conviction u/s. 302 r/w s.34 can be safely recorded - Appeal dismissed.
