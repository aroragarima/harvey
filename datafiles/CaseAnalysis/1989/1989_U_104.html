Ratan Lal Adukia v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 653; (1989) 3 SCC 537; 1989 (2) ACJ 1080; AIR 1990 SC 104; 1990 (1) CalLT(SC) 25; JT 1989 (3) SC 148; 1989(2) SCALE 28; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 440; 1989 (2) UJ 382

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legislative Intent, Animals, Place Of Suing, Exhaustive Code, Railway Act 1890, Presidency Small Cause Courts (West Bengal Amendment) Act 1980

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Presidency Small Cause Courts Act 1882, s. 18 - Indian Railway (Amendment) Act 1961, s. 14 - Whether s. 80 is a complete, self-contained, exhaustive code in regard to place of suing respecting suits constituting a special law for such suits excluding, by necessary implication, operation of provisions of s. 20 of CPC, 1908 and s. 18 of Presidency Small Cause Courts Act, 1882 ? - Held, s. 80 is a self-contained provision - No appeal could be made to s. 21-A of State Amendment to Small Cause Courts Act, inasmuch as that provision cannot be under stood to have been intended to cover a situation of present type - It does not exclude a special law applicable to and governing a distinct class of subject matter intended to be covered by that special law - Appeals dismissed.
