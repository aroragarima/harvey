Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 188; (2015) 9 SCC 150; 2015 (2) ACC 76; 2015 ACJ 1239; 2015 (110) ALR 540; 2015 (3) AWC 2177; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 517; JT 2015 (4) SC 26; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2120; 2015 (3) MLJ 373; 2015(3) SCALE 668; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 1; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 35

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Returns

Summary: Insurance - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 168 - Just and reasonable Compensation - When driver of motor cycle applied sudden brakes, driver and deceased who was pillion rider over the same, both fell down and succumbed to injuries - Appellants filed claim petition before Tribunal claiming Rs.33,90,000 as compensation for death of deceased - However, Tribunal awarded only Rs.7,85,000 and on appeal, HC enhanced compensation to Rs.14,69,372 - Yet, not satisfied with amount of compensation, appellants preferred instant appeal - Whether amount of compensation awarded by HC is just and reasonable.

Held, in view of age of deceased and nature of business carried by deceased, HC was not justified in taking average of income of two A/Ys. Further, deceased also owns agricultural lands and as per evidence adduced, was involved in agricultural work also. Hence, considering age of deceased and nature of business deceased was involved, income of deceased as stated in income tax return for year 2006-07 i.e. Rs.2,02,911 should be taken as income of deceased for calculation of compensation. Further, compensation awarded is also enhanced on other heads and in total, compensation awarded to appellants is enhanced to Rs.19,32,310 and impugned judgment of HC is modified to such extent. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per s. 168 of the Act, courts/tribunals are obligated to award compensation which is just and reasonable.
