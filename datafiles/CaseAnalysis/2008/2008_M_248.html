Gulbarga University v Mallikarjun S. Kodagali and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1649; (2008) 13 SCC 539; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1281; 2008 (72) ALR 795; 2008 (3) AWC 2952; 2008 (5) CompLJ 107; 2008 (4) CTC 531; 2008 (3) ESC 555; 2008 (6) KarLJ 701; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 249; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 116; 2008(11) SCALE 79; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 703

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Claims And Counter Claims, Arbitration and Conciliation Act

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure,1908,s.47 - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, s.34 - Limitation Act,1963,s.14 - Allotment of Plot - Condone delay - Limitation - Challenged - Respondents entered into contract of construction of indoor stadium with appellant said contract contained clause pertaining to resolution of dispute between parties by Superintending Engineer, PWD, Gulbarga contained in cl 30 of contract - Appellant, respondents filed claims and counter claims before Authority which passed award - However copy thereof was not supplied to respondent - Aggrieved respondent filed writ petition before HC which directed Authorities to supply said copy which was supplied by said Authority/ Superintending Engineer - Appellant filed application in terms of s. 34 of the Act before Principal Civil Court which was barred by limitation - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by lower Court was justifiable - Held relied on State of Goa Vs. Western Builders, 2006 Indlaw SC 291 opined that s. 29(2) thereof would apply to arbitration proceedings and consequently s. 14 of the 1963 Act would also be applicable and bound by said decision - However, as to whether period spent by appellant in prosecuting aforementioned proceedings should be excluded or not was matter which should fall for decision before Civil Court -Therefore, necessary corollary of aforementioned finding was that as to whether appellant had been prosecuting, with due diligence another proceeding or not would fall for consideration before Principal Civil Court - Hence, set aside impugned order of HC and remitted matter back to Principal Civil Court for consideration afresh in light of observations made - Appeal allowed.
