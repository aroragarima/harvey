State of Punjab and Others v Bakshish Singh

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1125; (1998) 8 SCC 222; (1999) SCC (L&S) 183; AIR 1999 SC 2626; 1999 (1) AWC 245; 1998 (4) CLT 150; 1999 (82) FLR 761; JT 1998 (7) SC 142; 1999 (1) LLJ 1208; 1998(5) SCALE 580; 1999 (3) SLJ 1; 1998 (5) SLR 625; 1998 (8) SLT 224; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 478; 1998 (8) Supreme 128

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 107, o. 41, r. 33 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 143 - Appeal from original decrees - Power of appellate Court - Categorically recording of finding on fact by trial Court about absence of proper opportunity of hearing in departmental proceedings - Also that unauthorised absence from duty having been regularised charge of misconduct does not survive - Lower appellate court confirming that charge of misconduct did not survive - But did not say anything about finding relating to opportunity of hearing - Held, said findings shall be treated to have been affirmed - Lower appellate court cannot remand case to punishing authority - High Court dismissing second appeal summarily without adverting to inconsistent judgment - Not proper - Appellate court cannot enlarge scope of appeal - Supreme Court u/art. 142 in exercise of power of doing complete justice set aside order of HC remanding matter to punishing authority - And upheld judgment and decree passed by trial Court setting aside dismissal.
