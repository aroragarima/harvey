State of Uttar Pradesh v Boota Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 291; (1979) 1 SCC 31; (1979) SCC (Cr) 115; AIR 1978 SC 1770; 1978 ALJ 1156; 1978 CRLR 557; 1978 CrLR(SC) 557; 1979 MLJ 290; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 298

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Committing Court

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 288 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Confessional Statement - Conviction - Respondent No. 1 and his brother entered into an agreement with P.W. 16 and his father, for purchasing land - Earnest money was paid by Respondent No. 2 and 4 at time of agreement and balance amount was not paid - Hence, four respondents entered into conspiracy to obtain money and deceased was killed by respondents - Sessions Court convicted respondents u/ss. 120-B, 302 r/w. 34 , 364 r/w. 34, 394 r/w. 34, 419, 471 r/w.465 and 471 r/w. 466 of Code, 1860 and sentenced to RI - On appeal to HC, which reversed judgment of Sessions Court and acquitted all respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether acquittal of respondents were justifiable and proved - Held, court observed that HC had not marshaled evidence correctly based on confession of respondent No. 1 and judgment of HC could not be sustained - Statements of respondents were wholly inconsistent and differed from court to court and was absolutely false and incredible - Circumstances proved by appellant/prosecution, not only amounted to complete corroboration of confession, but provided additional circumstantial evidence against prosecution, which were conclusive in nature - Offence of conspiracy was also proved - Confession of respondent No. 1 was proved by evidence of P.Ws. 2, 8 and 14 - Appeal against Respondent No. 3 was dismissed and order of HC acquitting him was confirmed - Court accordingly allowed appeal of appellant/State against respondent No. 1 and convicted of offences u/ss. 364 r/w. 34, 394 r/w. 31, 302 r/w. 34, 471 r/w. 465, 471 r/w. 466 and 120-B of Code and sentenced him to undergo RI and life imprisonment concurrently - Order accordingly.
