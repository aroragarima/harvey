Arundhati Mishra Smt v Sri Ram Charitra Pandey

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 667; (1994) 2 SCC 29

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.6 r.17 - Plea of adverse possession - Legality - Appellant/plaintiff basing on title laid the suit for possession and mesne profits against the respondent - Respondent reiterated in his written statement that he was the real owner and remained in possession as owner of the suit house and the appellant was only benamidar - Respondent also later filed an application under O.6 r.17 CPC claiming alternatively compensation for the improvements made by him - Framing appropriate issues and on adduction of evidence, Trial Court found that the appellant had title to the property, by denial of the title, the respondent forfeited his tenancy and decreed the suit - Pending first appeal, respondent filed another application on 303-1989 for amendment of the written statement setting up the plea of 'adverse possession' - Appellate court rejected the application, considered the case on merits and confirmed the decree of Trial Court - In the second appeal Single Judge of HC considered and allowed the application for amendment, set aside the findings of the courts below and remitted the case to Trial Court for fresh trial - Hence instant appeal - Whether the plea of adverse possession sought to be set up by the respondent could be permitted to be raised - Held, pleas based on title and adverse possession were mutually inconsistent and the latter did not begin to operate until the former was renounced - It is respondent's own case that he came into possession of the suit house in his own right and remained in possession as an owner - Appellant was only benamidar - Therefore, respondent's plea was based on his own title - Respondent never denounced his title nor admitted the title of the appellant - Respondent never renounced his character as an owner asserting adverse possession openly to the knowledge of the appellant and the appellant's acquiescence to it - Thereafter, respondent remained in open and peaceful possession and enjoyment to the knowledge of the appellant without acknowledging/or acquiescing the right, title and interest of the appellant - Plea of adverse possession, though available to the respondent, was never raised by him - Only on receipt of the first notice respondent denied title of the appellant and made it known to him for the first time through the reply notice got issued by him - Even then the plea of adverse possession was not raised in the written statement - No explanation for the belated plea was given - Even assuming that the reply dt. 15-3-1971 constituted assertion of adverse possession, the limitation would start running against the appellant only from 15-3-1971 and not earlier - Suit was filed in 1978 within 12 years - Under these circumstances, the HC was not justified in permitting the respondent to raise the plea of adverse possession - Appeal allowed.
