Deonarayan Singh & Ors. v The Commissioner Of Bhagalpur & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1150; (1997) 10 SCC 51; AIR 1997 SC 3680; JT 1997 (4) SC 662; 1997(3) SCALE 656; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 941; 1997 (5) Supreme 186

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Santhal Parganas Tenancy (Supplementary Provisions) Act, 1949, ss. 20 (5), 42 - Santhal Parganas Settlement Regulation, 1872 - Valid title over land - Eviction - Sustainability - It was case of appellants that as entire family of 'B' was heavily indebted and was in need of money, the 8 annas interest in Mool Raiyat comprising 38 acres 9 decimals representing his share in Nij Jote came to be sold by said 'B' and his brothers to one 'K' on 22-3-1939 - Said vendors were in possession of 38.09 acres of land in lieu of their 8 annas interest in Mool Raiyat by family arrangement with their co-sharers - After said purchase 'K' got his name mutated in revenue records in respect of said land - Subsequently 'K' appointed as 16 annas sarbarakar of said mauza - 'K' by sale deed dt.26-6-1950 sold his entire right, title and interest in Mool Raiyat Ka Jote to 'R', Father or appellants, vendee 'R' got his name mutated in revenue records - Said order of mutation was passed after service of notice on all opposite parties, respondent nos.4 to 15 - Vendee 'R' during his lifetime remained in possession of aforesaid 38.09 acres of land and was also acting as sharer of a annas Mool raiyat Ka jote and 16 annas sarbarakar or said mauza - Further, it was case of appellants that respondents sought to disturb possession of 'R' proceedings u/s. 145 CrPC were initiated - Sub-Divisional Officer by his order dt.31-8-1951 declared possession of appellants' father - On revision, Sessions Court rejected said revision -After death of 'R' appellant no. 4 was appointed as Mool Raiyat to the extent of his interest in said Jote amounting to 8 annas and as 16 annas sarbarakar of the said mauza - It was only thereafter that in year 1970-71 respondent nos.4 to 15 claiming to be original co- sharers of mauza filed an application before Sub- Divisional Officer against appellants for their eviction from 38.09 acres of land of Jamabandi No. 65 alleging that the same was illegally alienated - Sub-Divisional Officer rejected said application - On appeal, Additional Deputy Commissioner allowed said appeal and ordered eviction of appellants and held that original sale transaction by 'B' in favour of 'K' dt.22-3-1939 was violative of provisions of s. 27(1) of the Regulation and consequently subsequent sale by 'K' in favor of appellants' father was equally violative of provisions of s. 20 (1) of said Act - On appeal, Commissioner dismissed said appeal and confirmed eviction order passed by Additional Deputy Commissioner - Aggrieved by said order, appellant filed writ petition - Full bench of HC did not find fault with decision rendered by lower authorities against appellants - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that by a family partition prior to the transaction of sale 8 annas share in Mool Raiyat comprising of 38 acres and 9 decimals feel to the share of 'B' and it was his entire share in the Mool Raiyat that was transferred by transaction - Hence s. 27 (1) was fully complied with - Held, it was clear from the record that possession of appellants as heirs of deceased vendee 'R' could be said to have been validly obtained and a valid title that was convoyed in land admeasuring 38.09 acres, to their father 'R' under second sale transaction dt.26-6-1950, got legally transmitted to appellants by rules of succession - Consequently on these facts no action could have been taken by authorities under relevant provisions of the Act against appellants, therefore, appeal was required to be allowed - Order of HC was quashed and set aside - Appeal Allowed.
