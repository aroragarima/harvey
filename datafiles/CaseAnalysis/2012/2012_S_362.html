Azim Ahmad Kazmi and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 209; (2012) 7 SCC 278; 2012 (8) ADJ 252; 2012(4) ALL MR 524; 2012 (94) ALR 245; 2012 (6) AWC 5941; 2012 (117) RD 424; 2012(6) SCALE 434; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Government Grants Act, 1895 - Cancellation of lease deed - Challenged - Lease deed of the demised premises was executed by the State Govt. in favour of the appellants on 19-3-1966 followed by a renewal of lease dt. 17-7-1998 - Later, the State Govt., cancelled the lease deed and proceeded to resume the demised premises which was informed to the appellants by the District Magistrate - The objection preferred by the appellants was rejected - Appellants preferred a writ petition against the order passed by the State Govt. and the order passed by the District Magistrate which was dismissed but with the observation that the State Govt. was not entitled to take forcible possession though it may take possession of the demised premises in accordance with the procedure established by law - Hence, the instant appeals - Appellants were aggrieved against the dismissal of the writ petition whereby the order of cancellation of lease deed was affirmed, whereas the State Govt. was aggrieved against the last portion of the order where under it was mentioned that the State Govt. was not entitled to take forcible possession though it may take possession in accordance with the procedure established by law - (A) Whether the order passed by the State Govt. for cancellation of lease and resumption of possession was legally valid - Held, State Govt. was resuming the leased property for his or for any public purpose, which under the terms of the grant it has absolute power to do, the order passed by the State Govt. was perfectly valid and does not suffer from any illegality - (B) Whether the State Govt. can dispossess the lessee in accordance with the Act without resorting to other procedure established by any other law - Held, under cl. 3(C) of the lease deed, the State Govt. was permitted resumption of the land which required for its own use or for public purpose and after giving 1 month's notice in writing was entitled to remove any building standing at the time on the demised premises and within 2 months of the receipt of the notice to take possession thereof subject to the condition that if the lessor was willing to purchase the building of the demised premises required to pay the lessee the amount for such building as may be determined by the Secretary to Govt. in the Awas Department - In the instant case, such procedure was followed - Therefore, there was no other procedure or law required to be followed, as a special procedure for resumption of land has been laid down under the lease deed - As a special procedure for resumption of land was prescribed under the lease deed, the HC was not correct in holding that the State Govt. cannot dispossess the appellants but can take possession according to the procedure adopted by any other law - The finding of the HC to such extent was set aside but the rest portion of the judgment affirming the order of the State Govt. and an order passed by the District Magistrate was upheld - Appeals disposed of.
