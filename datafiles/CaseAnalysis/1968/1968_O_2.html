P. S. L. Ramanathan Chettiar and Others v O. Rm. P. Rm. Ramanathan Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 390; AIR 1968 SC 1047; 1969 (1) MLJ 11; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 11; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 367

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, - Deposit of money - Decree holder - Respondent's father made a deposit of certain sum of rupees with appellant's father with interest - Demand was made for repayment - Suit was instituted for recovery of amount - Subordinate court decreed the suit in respondent's favour - Appellant's father appealed to HC - HC reversed the order of the Subordinate court - Hence this appeal - Whether the HC was right in reversing the order of the Subordinate Judge - Held, on a deposit being made, the depositee incurred a liability although the time for repayment would come only when a demand was made and the cause of action for the suit would arise on such a demand - Plea of the decree-holder which succeeded before the HC cannot therefore be accepted - Real effect of deposit of money in court as was done was to put the money beyond the reach of the parties pending the disposal of the appeal - Decree holder could only take it out on furnishing security - Deposit was not in terms of O. 21 r. 1 of CPC and as such there is no question of the stoppage of interest after the deposit - Appeal allowed.
