Â P. Sambamurthy And Ors. Etc. Etc. v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra, V. Khalid, G.L. Oza, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 251; (1987) 1 SCC 362; AIR 1987 SC 663; 1987 (2) ATC 502; 1987 (1) CCC 547; JT 1987 (1) SC 20; 1987 (1) LLJ 221; 1987 (1) LLN 488; 1986(2) SCALE 1168; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 879; 1987 (1) UJ 279

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Public Employment (Requirement As To Residence) Act, 1957, Constitution (Thirty-Second Amendment) Act, 1973

Summary: Constitution, art. 371D(5) - Constitutional validity of art. 371D(5) - Power Conferred on state govt. to modifying or annull an order of the Administrative Tribunal - Held, power of is violative of the rule of law as the exercise of power by the executive or any other authority must not only be conditioned by the Constitution but must also be in accordance with law and the power of judicial review - Hence, art. 371D (5) is therefore declared unconstitutional and void - Petition allowed.
