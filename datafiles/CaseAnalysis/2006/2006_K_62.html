Kerala Samsthana Chethu Thozhilali Union v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 98; (2006) 4 SCC 327; AIR 2006 SC 3480; JT 2006 (5) SC 41; 2006(3) SCALE 534; 2006 (4) SCJ 63; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Abkari Workers Welfare Fund Board Act

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25(FFF), 25(J) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(6), 311, 368, 39, 42, 43, 47 - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 3 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 23 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Abkari Workers Welfare Fund Board Act, ss. 18A, 24(c), 24(d), 29(1), 3(6A), 3(8), 68A, 69 - Kerala Abkari Shops Disposal Rules, 2002, rr. 4(2), 7(38), 9(10)(b) - On or about 1/4/1996, State banned sale of arrack - After a lapse of about six years, State inserted impugned Rules, inter alia directing that one arrack worker each must be employed in all toddy shops - Whether rr. 4(2) and 9(10)(b) are ultra vires Abkari Act? - Held, rehabilitation of workers, being not a part of legislative policy for which Act was enacted - To grant employment to one arrack worker in each toddy shop in preference to toddy workers neither promotes policy nor secures object of Act - Rehabilitation of employees is not within statutory scheme and, thus, Rules are ultra vires provisions of Act - r.4(2) declared ultra vires - Appeals allowed.
