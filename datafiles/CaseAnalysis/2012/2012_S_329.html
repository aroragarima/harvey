Salim Gulab Pathan v State of Maharashtra through SHO

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 163; (2012) 6 SCC 606; AIR 2012 SC 2176; 2012 CRLJ 2645; 2012 (3) JCC 1905; JT 2012 (5) SC 351; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 270; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 161; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 778; 2012 (4) RLW 3144; 2012(5) SCALE 249; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Dying declaration - Challenged - Appellant allegedly poured kerosene on the deceased and set her on fire - Trial court found appellant guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the trial court - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that principal basis of the conviction recorded against the appellant was the statement of the deceased recorded by PW 6, the police constable which has been treated by the courts below as a dying declaration - However, PW 1 (Father of deceased) has categorically stated that the deceased had not spoken to anybody while in the hospital and, in fact, the police had not come to meet the deceased at any time after her admission in the hospital till her death - Hence, the alleged dying declaration becomes unworthy of credence - Whether order of trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, PW 2, the doctor, has clearly deposed that the deceased had narrated the history of the injuries suffered by her in the course of which she had implicated her husband - PW 2 has also deposed that the police constable (PW 6) had visited the burn ward and had recorded the statement of the deceased - PW 2 and PW 6 cannot be attributed with any intention to falsely implicate the appellant - Moreover, PW 2 has clearly certified, both at the time of commencement of the recording of the statement of the deceased as well as at the conclusion thereof, that deceased was fully conscious and in a fit mental condition to make the statement - There was no occasion not to accept the said opinion of the doctor who was present with the deceased at the relevant time - Conviction of the appellant as recorded by the courts below has been rightly made - Appeal dismissed.
