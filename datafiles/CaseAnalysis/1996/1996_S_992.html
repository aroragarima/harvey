K. Kuppusamy and Another v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Venkataswami, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3305; (1998) 8 SCC 469; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1694

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Govt. indicated its intention to amend relevant rules - Whether relevant? - Held, statutory rules cannot be overridden by executive orders or executive practice - Merely because Govt. had taken a decision to amend rules does not mean that rule stood obliterated - Till rule is amended, rule applies - Tribunal was, therefore, wrong in ignoring rule - Order of tribunal set aside - Matter remitted to tribunal for disposal in accordance with law - Appeals allowed.
