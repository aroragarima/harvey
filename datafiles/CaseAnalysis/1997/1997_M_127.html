Malpe Vishwanath Acharya and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M. Srinivasan

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1250; (1998) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1998 SC 602; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 321; JT 1997 (10) SC 311; 1998 (2) MLJ 66; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 66; 1998 (1) RCJ 369; 1998 (1) RCR(Rent) 16; 1997(7) SCALE 786; 1998 (1) SLT 596; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 717; 1998 (1) Supreme 124

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Keywords: Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1976

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Bombay Rent Act, 1947, ss. 5(10)(B), 11(1) and 12(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 21 - Maharashtra Housing and Area Development Act, 1976 - Writ petitions in High Court challenging constitutional validity of ss. 5(10)(B), 11(1) and 12(3) of Bombay Rent Act, 1947 - HC dismissed writ petitions - Whether, provisions pertaining to standard rent were ultra vires arts. 14, 19 and 21 of Constitution and consequently void? - Held, no just principle is applied while providing for increase in standard rent and increases made even in 1987 are not adequate, fair or just and provisions continue to be arbitrary in today's context, therefore existing provisions of Bombay Rent Act relating to determination and fixation of standard rent can no longer be considered to be reasonable - Appeal allowed.
