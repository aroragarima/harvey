Jasvinder Saini and others v State Government of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 399; (2013) 7 SCC 256; AIR 2014 SC 841; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3006; 2013 (202) DLT 61; 2013 (3) JCC 2168; JT 2014 (1) SC 373; 2013 (2) OLR 992; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 316; 2013(8) SCALE 377; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302, 304B an d498A - Dowry death - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 34, 304B and 498A IPC - Trial Court convicted the appellants u/ss. 34, 302, 304B and 498A IPC and sentenced them accordingly - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC, which was affirmed the order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in framing a charge u/s. 302 IPC against appellants - Held, if main charge of murder was not proved against accused at trial, Court could look into evidence to determine whether alternative charge of dowry death punishable u/s. 304B IPC was established - Ingredients constituting the 2 offences were different, thereby demanding appreciation of evidence from perspective relevant to such ingredients - Trial Court acted mechanically for it framed an additional charge u/s. 302 IPC without adverting to evidence adduced in case - Order passed by Trial Court and so also that passed by HC were clearly untenable and should be set aside - That would not prevent Trial Court from re-examining question of framing a charge u/s. 302 IPC against appellant and passing an appropriate order if upon a prima facie appraisal of evidence adduced before it, Trial Court comes to conclusion that there was any room for doing so - Impugned order passed by HC and Trial Court framing charge u/s. 302 IPC were set aside and matter was remitted back to Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
