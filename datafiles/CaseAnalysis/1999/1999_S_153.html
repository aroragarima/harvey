Regional Provident Fund Commissioner v Shiv Kumar Joshi

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 264; (2000) SCC (L&S) 37; (2000) 1 SCC 98; AIR 2000 SC 331; 2000 (38) ALR 205; 2000 (1) AWC 575; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 526; 2000 (1) CLC 578; 2000 (1) CLR 415; [2000] 99 Comp Cas 347; JT 1999 (10) SC 70; 2000 (33) LabIC 232; 2000 (1) LLJ 55; 2000 (1) LLN 323; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 11; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 89; 2000 (124) PLR 655; 2000 (3) SLR 634; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 ss. 2(1)(d), 2(1)(o) - Deficiency in service - Damages and cost order - Maintainability -Respondent/employee filed complaint before District Consumer Dispute Redressal Forum on 26-8-1992 alleging deficiency in service of appellant/Provident Fund Commissioner and claimed damages along with costs for alleged delay in payment of his provident fund - District Forum vide its order dt. 4-11-1992 directed appellant to pay interest @ 18% on delayed payment and costs of Rs. 1,000 - Appellant filed appeal before State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission, which was dismissed on 1-3-1994 - Revision filed before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent was not 'consumer' and facilities provided by the scheme were not 'service' - Whether impugned order of State as well as National Commission was justified - Held, SC could not accept argument that Regional Provident Fund Commissioner, being Central Govt. could not be held to be rendering 'service' within meaning and scheme of the Act - Regional Provident Fund Commissioner, under the Act and the scheme discharges statutory functions for running the scheme - It had not, in any way, been delegated with sovereign powers of State so as to hold it as Central Govt. being not authority rendering 'service' under the Act, Commissioner was separate and distinct entity - It could not legally claim that facilities provided by the 'scheme' were not 'service' or that benefits under scheme being provided were free of charge - Further, definition of 'consumer' under the Act includes not only person who hires 'services' for consideration but also beneficiary, for whose benefit such services were hired - Even if it was held that administrative charges were paid by Central Govt. and no part of it was paid by employee, services of Provident Fund Commissioner in running the scheme should be deemed to have been availed of for consideration by Central Govt. for benefit of employees who would be treated as beneficiary within meaning of that word used in definition of consumer - Perusal of the scheme clearly and unambiguously indicated that it was 'service' within meaning of s. 2(1)(o) of the Act and member a 'consumer' within meaning of s. 2(1)(d) of the Act - It was, therefore, without any substance to urge that services under the scheme were rendered free of charge and, therefore, the scheme was not 'service' under the Act - Both State as well as National Commission had dealt with this aspect in detail and rightly came to conclusion that Act was applicable in case of the scheme on ground that its member was 'consumer' u/s. 2(1)(d) of the Act and the scheme was 'service' u/s.2(1)(o) of the Act - Thus, no ground was, therefore, made out for interference with impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
