Special Land Acquisition Officer Bangalore and Others v B. M. Krishnamurthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 507; (1985) 1 SCC 469; 1985(1) SCALE 1170; 1985 UJ 344

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - All three cases arise out of land acquisition proceedings - Result of SLP 3450 of 1974 is entirely dependent on result of Civil Appeal 1127 of 1974 while result of Civil Appeal 992 of 1971 is partly dependent on result of Civil Appeal 1127 of 1974 - In first two appeals, High Court reduced compensation to Rs. 15,000 per acre in case of agricultural lands and Rs. 22,000 per acre in case of lands whose use for non-agricultural purposes was sanctioned - Whether compensation awarded by HC was sufficient? - Held, HC took notice of circumstances and was totally justified in awarding compensation at rate of Rs. 15,000 per acre for agricultural lands - Further, making a provision for a 20% rise in price on basis of documentary evidence produced before court, HC thought that compensation at rate of Rs. 22,000 per acre was adequate and reasonable for land user of which had been permitted to be converted from agricultural to non-agricultural - Conclusion of HC is unassailable - In Civil Appeal 992 of 1971, HC affirmed judgment of Trial Court awarding compensation at rate of Rs. 20 per square yard - However, acquired land was only land available in vicinity for industrial purposes and had therefore, acquired a special value - Hence, Court may perhaps be justified in awarding compensation for this bit of land roughly about two and half times compensation awarded for land which was subject matter of Civil Appeal 1127 of 1974 - Appeal filed by special land acquisition officer of Bangalore is allowed to this extent that compensation awarded is reduced from Rs. 20 per square yard to Rs. 12.50 per square yard - Appeal partly allowed.
