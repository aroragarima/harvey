B. Raghuvir Acharya and another v Central Bureau Of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 365; (2014) 14 SCC 693; 2014 CRLJ 1380; 2013 (3) MLJ(Crl) 465; 2013(7) SCALE 722; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 132

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Falsification of accounts, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 120-B, 420,405, 411, 477-A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) - Cheating - Criminal Breach of trust - Falsification of accounts - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-accused No.1 was Trustee and General Manager and accused No.2 was Fund Manager and accused No.3 as approved broker for Canbank Mutual Fund (CMF) - Accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 120-B, 420,405, 411, 477-A IPC - Accused No.1 and accused No.2 being public servants were also charged for offences punishable u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - Accused No.1 and accused No.3 was found guilty and convicted and sentenced by Trial Court - Trial Court acquitted accused no.2 - Hence instant appeals - Whether accused No.3 could be convicted for alternative charge u/s. 420 IPC for which substantive charge had been framed against - Held, prosecution proved that accused No.3 deceived CMF by making false representation and dishonestly induced official of CMF to deliver Rs.32.50 lakhs in his favour and dishonestly received amount and thereby committed offence u/s. 420 IPC - Accused No.3 was originally charged for the offence of cheating, criminal breach of trust for receiving stolen property/falsification of accounts u/ss. 120-B, 420/409 IPC apart from ss. 411, 477-A IPC - Therefore, conviction from s. 409 IPC was altered to s. 420 IPC and convicted for the offence u/s. 420 IPC - Prosecution successfully established ingredients of theft for receiving stolen property from concerned bank of Rs.32.50 lakhs against accused No.3 - Order of conviction and sentenced passed by Trial Court u/s. 411 IPC were upheld - Order accordingly.
