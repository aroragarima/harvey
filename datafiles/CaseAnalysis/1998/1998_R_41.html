Silverline Forum Pvt. Ltd. v Rajiv Trust And Another

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 668; (1998) 3 SCC 723; AIR 1998 SC 1754; 1998 (33) ALR 183; 1998 (2) CLT 37; JT 1998 (3) SC 1; 1998 (1) RCR(Rent) 394; 1998(2) SCALE 550; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 587; 1998 (4) SLT 97; 1998 (3) Supreme 555

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s .151, O. 21 r.97 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956 - Sub-tenancy - Period of lease - Eviction - Interim order - Ownership of building changed from 'A' and it now vests with appellant as per registered conveyance deed - Appellant filed a suit for ejectment of tenant against respondent No.1 under provisions of Premises 1956 Act - Respondent No.1 who sublet building to a company/sub-tenant No.1- Sub-tenant No.1/respondent No.2 created another sub-tenancy under it in favour of respondent No.2 - Before appellant set out with execution proceedings respondent No.2 - Sub-tenant filed a suit against appellant and some others for a declaration and consequential injunction orders - Though initially respondent No.2 got an interim order injunction against ejectment it was subsequent vacated - Sub-tenant was not party to a decree for eviction, resisted execution of decree and court ordered an inquiry u/s.151 of CPC - HC upheld that order and that was challenged appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether a sub-tenancy created with consent of landlord during subsistence of tenancy would continue to be lawful even after expiry of period of lease - Held, respondent No.2 that HC went wrong in holding that controversy of respondent No.2 could not be considered under O. 21 r.97 of CPC - Claim of a landlord for eviction on ground that he reasonably requires premises for purposed of renovation, re-building - Respondent No.2 had no claim that he had notified landlord as envisaged in s.16(1) of 1956 Act - Consent given by landlord to his tenant for creation of sub-tenancy was valid - Consent could not be used by a sub-tenant to create another sub-tenancy under him so as to bind landlord - Appellant was entitled to deliver of possession by removing obstruction/resistance made by respondent No.2- Appeal allowed.
