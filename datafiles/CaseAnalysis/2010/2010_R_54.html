Rakesh Kumar Goel etc. v Uttar Pradesh State Industrial Development Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 477; (2010) 8 SCC 263; 2010 (8) ADJ 189; AIR 2010 SC 2451; 2010 (5) AWC 5284; JT 2010 (6) SC 465; 2010 (110) RD 762; 2010(6) SCALE 514; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, 1952

Summary: Land & Property - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - U.P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, 1952, 285-D - Private respondents had government dues therefore two proclamations were issued one for attachment u/ss. 284/286 of the Act and the other restraining 'the defaulter' from selling or transferring the plot in any manner to anyone or from creating any charge on it - Copies of the proclamations were affixed to the gate of the premises in presence of witnesses - Sale Proclamation was issued by the Additional Collector - Thereafter, a petition was filed for quashing the Sale Proclamation fixing the auction sale of plot - HC granted ad interim stay - Despite this fact, auction was held - Five bidders, including the appellants took part in the auction - Appellant made the highest bid and his bid was finally accepted - Writ petitions were filed by the UP State Industrial Development Corporation (respondent no.1-owner of property) - HC set aside the auction sale of two pieces of land made in favour of the appellants - HC held that the auction sale of the two plots was a nullity as in violation of r. 285-D of the Rules the appellants did not deposit one fourth of their bid amounts immediately on the conclusion of the auction - Present appeal - Held, r. 285-D of the Rules requires the highest bidder to deposit immediately twenty five percent of the amount bid by him - In the present case, twenty five percent of the bid amounts were not deposited immediately after the auction as claimed by the appellants - Further, no notice or information about the attachment or sale of the two plots was given to the respondent no. 1 - Sale Proclamations did not indicate the area of the plots or the nature of the rights therein that would be the subject matter of the sale - There was practically no public advertisement to give wider information to the general public about the auction - No reserve price for the plots in question was indicated either in the Sale Proclamations or even in the Memos of Auction - Information about the stay order passed by HC was completely ignored - Nothing to show that after the government dues were satisfied, the balance of the sale price was refunded to the land holders, the two private respondents - Hence, in view of these facts it was clear that auction proceedings of the two plots was illegal, fraudulent and collusive - Appeal dismissed.
