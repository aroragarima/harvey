Tej Kaur and Ors. etc. v State of Punjab and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 228; (2003) 4 SCC 485; AIR 2003 SC 2414; 2003 (51) ALR 77; 2003 (5) AWC 4371; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 132; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 244; 2003 (94) RD 674; 2003(3) SCALE 5; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 707; 2003 (2) SLT 536; 2003 (2) Supreme 542

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - S. 5A - contentions that there was no Section 5A of the Land Acquisition Act inquiry and the appellants were not given personal hearing regarding the objections filed by them; and secondly the lands owned by them were liable to be exempted as the acquired lands were agricultural lands - Held, the facts and circumstances of the case show that the objection raised by the appellants was considered and partly allowed by the Collector. Further, the appellants did not raise any objection within a reasonable time after Section 6 Declaration was made, which fact indicates that the appellants did not have a genuine grievance against Section 5A inquiry held by the Collector. - No reason to interfere with the judgment on the grounds now advanced by the appellants.
