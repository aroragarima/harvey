Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa v Messrs Patney and Company

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1959 SC 1070; 1960 (26) CutLT 96; [1959] 36 ITR 488; 1962 (1) SCJ 316; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Tax accrued outside Jurisdiction - Tax exemption - Cheque transaction - Tax Liability - Income-tax Officer assessed taxable income that entire amount was received in cheque by appellant in British India and not at Secunderabad - Further, against this order respondents took an appeal to Tribunal which altered order of IO which was affirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether amounts paid by cheques have been received in British India or at Secunderabad - Held, according to course of business usage to which as part of surrounding circumstances and parties must intended cheques should be sent by post which was usual and normal agency for transmission of such articles - Further, when there was an express or implied request for cheque for amount being sent by post - Hence, inferred from course of conduct of parties that amount of commission to be paid at Secunderabad and respondent not liable to pay Tax - Appeal dismissed.
