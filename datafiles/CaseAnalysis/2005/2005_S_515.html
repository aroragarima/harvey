K.R. Purushothaman v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 911; (2005) 12 SCC 631; AIR 2006 SC 35; 2006 (2) CalLT(SC) 78; 2005 CRLJ 4648; JT 2005 (9) SC 38; 2005 (4) KLT 842; 2005(8) SCALE 618; 2006 (2) SCJ 483; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(c), 13(1)(d), 13(2) - IPC, 1860, ss. 120A, 120B, 403, 409, 477A - Misappropriation of gold utilized for making ornaments for adorning deity in temple - Appeal against conviction - Held, entrustment of property or control of property is a necessary ingredient of s. 13(1)(c) - Property was neither entrusted nor was under control of accused-appellant and thus accused-appellant could not have been convicted - Prosecution has failed to prove that accused has obtained for himself or for any other person any valuable thing or pecuniary advantage - No evidence found on record to convict accused-appellant under ss 403, 477-A - Appeal allowed.
