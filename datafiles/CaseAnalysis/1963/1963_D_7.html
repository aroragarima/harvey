Santa Sila Devi and Another v Dhirendra Nath Sen and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 276; AIR 1963 SC 1677; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 410

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Companies Act, 1956 - (A) Whether all disputes which had been referred to arbitrator had not been disposed of by award, and therefore was incomplete and had to be set aside? - A Court should approach an award with a desire to support it, if that is reasonably possible - Held, since award now impugned or expressly states that it is made 'de praemissis' i.e., of and concerning all matters in dispute referred to arbitrator, there is a presumption that award is complete - (1) Whether award had given no direction regarding rendition of accounts and profits with reference to a lease of Glass Works Ltd., which the award had declared was invalid and not binding on Company in which all shares were owned by parties ? - Further held, if lease were held to be void because of technical informality it need not necessarily involve any accounting since accounting postulates lease being for an improperly low rental - If lease be set aside for such a reason, it would not necessarily follow that relief of accounting was implicit in declaration of invalidity of lease - (2) Whether fact that award had not decided claim on ground of misappropriation moneys of company made it incomplete and therefore invalid? - Further held, silence of award is a clear indication, having regard to adjudication completed and that claim in that respect was not upheld - Appeal dismissed.
