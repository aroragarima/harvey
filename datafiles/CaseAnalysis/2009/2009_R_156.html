Ram Singh & Ors. v Ram Niwas & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 698; (2009) 14 SCC 25; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 501; 2009(8) SCALE 284; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 878

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court, New Accused, Court's Jurisdiction

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Power to proceed against other persons appearing to be guilty of offence - Scope and application of s. 319 - Trial Court refused to issue summons against appellants on application moved by respondent no. 1(R1) u/s. 319 - High Court allowed petition filed thereagainst by R1 - HC's findings esp. on s. 319, whether proper? - Held, No - Trial Court while disposing of application u/s. 319 of CrPC had examined and cross-examined large number of witnesses on behalf of accused persons - Scope and application of s. 319 deliberated upon, by referring to decisions of the Court - Impugned judgment of HC examined - Application u/s. 319 would be maintainable not only during pendency of an inquiry but also in the course of a trial; if it appears from the evidence that any person, not being an accused, has committed any offence for which he could be tried together with the accused, the Court may proceed against him for the offence which he appears to have committed; provision of s. 319 confers an extraordinary power upon the Court to summon a person who, at the relevant time, was not being tried as an accused, subject, to fulfillment of the condition that it 'appears' to the Court that he committed an offence; a finding to that effect must be premised on the evidence that had been brought on record - Power u/s. 319 must be exercised very sparingly and not as a matter of course - Word 'appears', its essence noted by Court; HC proceeded on the premise that mere existence of a prima facie case would be sufficient to exercise the Court's jurisdiction u/s. 319; such premise found to be seriously erroneous by the Court; 'appears', what is necessary for the Court is to arrive at a satisfaction that the evidence adduced on behalf of the prosecution, if unrebutted, would lead to conviction of the persons sought to be added as accused in the case - Court further noted that HC failed to take into consideration that when order of Trial Court, which was impugned before the HC, was passed, the Judge therein was in a position to consider the evidence brought on record including the cross-examination of the prosecution witnesses; HC had not given any finding that a case has been made out for exercising jurisdiction u/s. 319, the jurisdiction which has to be exercised very sparingly - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
