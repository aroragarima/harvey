Mohamed Iqbal Madar Sheiri And Ors. v The State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4165; (1996) 1 SCC 722; 1996 (2) AWC 633; JT 1996 (1) SC 114; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Temporary Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3 (2)(i) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167, 437(5) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Bail - Rejection - Sustainability - Appellants along with others assembled in front of victims house and poured kerosene oil and set house on fire and they also threatened and prevented persons from coming to rescue of victims, which resulted in death of several children, ladies and males - After investigation, charge-sheet was submitted and appellants were put on trial for different offences including u/s. 3(1)(2)(i) of the 1987 Act- Designated Court rejected prayer for bail - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that the 1987 Act was temporary Act which had lapsed, there was no question of appellants being tried for offences under any of sections of the 1987 Act and conditions prescribed by s. 20(8) of the 1987 Act in respect of grant of bail now have to be ignored - Whether rejecting prayer for bail of appellants was sustainable - Held, in State of Orissa v. Bhupendra Kumar Rosa 1961 Indlaw SC 395 relied on, s. 6 of 1897 Act, in s. 1 (4) of the 1987 Act desired that even after expiry of such temporary Act, proceedings initialed under the 1897 Act, should not come to end without final conclusion and determination, they were to be continued in spite of expiry of the 1987 Act - Hence, petition for grant of bail on merit had been filed which was rejected on dt. 22-3-1993, but admittedly no petition for grant of bail after expiry of statutory period for submission of charge-sheet had been filed and there was no statement that any application for grant of bail had been filed on behalf of appellants u/s. 167(2)(a) CrPC after expiry of statutory period and appellants have forfeited their right to be released on bail - Appeals dismissed.
