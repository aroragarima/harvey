Â Supreme Court Bar Association v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S.C. Agrawal, G.N. Ray, S.P. Bharucha, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 688; (1998) 4 SCC 409; AIR 1998 SC 1895; AIR 1998 SCW 1706; 1998 (2) CLT 53; 2000 (5) CompLJ 18; 1998 (2) JCC 142; JT 1998 (3) SC 184; 1998(2) SCALE 745; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 795; 1998 (4) SLT 573; 1998 (4) Supreme 251; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 1320

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Contumacious Conduct, Contempt of Courts Act, 1926, Contempt Of Courts Act, 1952, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.142 and 129; Advocates Act, 1961, ss.38 and 35 - Contempt of Court - Powers of Court - Held, not to be exercised to protect dignity of individual Judge but to protect administration of justice being maligned - Power to punish - Though wide, yet limited - Not includes power to determine 'professional misconduct' of advocate - Powers under art.142 - To be used with restraint.
