Madhu Limaye v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.K. Goswami, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 299; (1977) 4 SCC 551; (1978) SCC (Cr) 10; AIR 1978 SC 47; 1978 CRLJ 165; 1977 CRLR 494; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 749; 1977 UJ 733

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 199(2), 199(4)(a), 397 (1) and (2) and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 500 - Statement of defamation - Trial challenged by appellant, but Sessions Judge rejecting all contentions framed charge - Revision dismissed by High Court on maintainability of revision application - Whether revision to HC against order of Subordinate Judge is expressly barred u/s. 397(2) CrPC, 1973? - Held, situation which is an abuse of process of Court or for purpose of securing ends of justice interference by HC is absolutely necessary, then nothing contained in s. 397(2) can limit or affect exercise of inherent power by HC, therefore, case falls for exercise of power u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 - Even assuming, although not accepting, that invoking revisional power of HC is impermissible - Appeal allowed.
