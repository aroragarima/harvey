Baldev Singh v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1246; (2005) 8 SCC 747; AIR 2006 SC 531; 2005 (6) AWC 5898; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 15; JT 2005 (10) SC 213; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 696; 2006 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 173; 2006 (2) RLW 1244; 2005(9) SCALE 73; 2005 (8) SLR 636; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 961

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appellant, enrolled in the Indian Army was convicted in a criminal case - However, on appeal he was acquitted - Appellant was discharged from service - Appellant claimed to have completed the requisite period of service from the date of enrolment to the date of discharge and claimed entitlement for the release of arrears of salary for the period when he was facing trial for criminal case as also pension for the subsequent periods - HC held that appellant had neither worked nor offered to work during said period and that appellant was entitled to salary for the period for which he actually rendered service and not for earlier periods - Hence, present appeal - Held, appellant was in custody while facing trial and was not in actual service for the period he was in custody - Merely because there was an acquittal would not automatically entitle him to get salary for the concerned period - Appellant having not rendered service, the question of inclusion of the period, does not arise and if the said period is excluded then the inevitable conclusion is that the appellant has not rendered the requisite period i.e. service of 15 years in order to be entitled to pension - No infirmity in HC order - Appeal dismissed.
