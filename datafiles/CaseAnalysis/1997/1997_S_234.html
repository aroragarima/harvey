Smt. Mehrunnisa & Ors. v Smt. Visham Kumari & Anr

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1012; (1998) 2 SCC 295; AIR 1998 SC 427; AIR 1998 SCW 3; JT 1997 (9) SC 616; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 89; 1998 (1) RCJ 67; 1997(7) SCALE 456; 1998 (2) SLT 34; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 81; 1998 (1) Supreme 29

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Bona fide requirement - Respondent-landlady issued a notice to the tenant terminating the tenancy stating inter alia that the suit premises, a non-residential one, was required for using it as an office for her husband, a practising lawyer - It was also stated that the tenant defaulted in payment of rent and sub-let the premises without written consent of the landlady - However, no action was taken pursuant to the three notice - Landlady filed a suit for eviction - Trial Court decreed the suit only on the ground of bona fide requirement by the landlady for starting the cloth business - On appeal, lower appellate reversed the Judgment and decree of the Trial Court on the ground that the landlady issued a first notice on entirely different ground for eviction - Lower appellate court held that change in the stand of the landlady exposed her intention to evict the tenant for some other motive - HC allowed the second appeal and decreed the suit for eviction - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the lower appellate court before reversing the finding of the trial court on the issue of bona fide requirement of the landlady for starting a cloth business failed to read the entire evidence and take into consideration all the documents placed before the trial court. The lower appellate court has also failed to give due importance to the fact that the landlady has not taken any steps to file suit for eviction pursuant to the notice issued on two earlier occasion and the ground stated in the notice preceding the suit are relevant for the purpose of deciding the issue. The lower appellate court has failed to take into account document necessary for giving a finding on the issue of bona fide requirement. There is no fault with the High Court in interfering with the reversing judgment of the lower appellate court. Appeal dismissed
