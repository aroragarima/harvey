Kedarnath Jute Mfg. Co. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Central Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 841; (1972) 3 SCC 252; AIR 1971 SC 2145; 1974 CTR 149; 1983 (32) CTR 221; [1971] 82 ITR 363; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 277; 1971 (28) STC 672

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: It Act, 1922, Appeal By Special Leave

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(xv) and 10(1) - A/y 1955-56 Deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) - Unpaid amount of sales tax - Business Expenditure - Assessee claimed deduction of Rs. 1,49,776, on account of sales tax determined to be payable by the sales tax authorities on the sales made by the assessee during a/y - Income Tax Officer held that the assessee was not entitled to claim the deduction of the aforesaid amount of sales tax inasmuch as it had denied its liability to pay that amount and had made up provision in its books with regard to the payment of that amount - The Appellate Assistant Commissioner confirmed the order of the Income-tax Officer - Tribunal dismissed appeal filed against said order - On appeal, HC held that unpaid and disputed sales tax liability could not form the basis of a claim for deduction for the purposes of income-tax - Hence, present appeal - Whether the amount of Rs. 1,49,776, which was claimed by the assessee as a deduction on account of sales tax, was deductible as a business expense? - Held, assessee who was maintaining accounts on the mercantile system was fully justified in claiming deduction of the sum of Rs. 1,49,776 being the amount of sales tax which it was liable under the law to pay during the relevant accounting year - Liability remained intact even after the assessee had taken appeals to higher authorities or courts which failed - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
