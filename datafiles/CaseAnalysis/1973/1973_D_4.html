Nagindas Ramdas v Dalpatram Ichharam @ Brijram And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, D.G. Palekar, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 286; (1974) 1 SCC 242; AIR 1974 SC 471; 1975 RCJ 48; 1974 RCR(Rent) 148; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 544

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12, 13, 28 - CPC, 1908, s. 47 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 58 - Tenancy - (A) Decree for eviction - Grounds for - Need of - (B) Consent decree for possession - Presumption as to Court's satisfaction - About existence of statutory ground - if can be drawn - Held, existence of one of the statutory grounds mentioned in ss. 12 and 13 is a sine qua non for passing a decree for eviction - Can be presumed from the compromise incorporated in the decree - Appeal dismissed.
