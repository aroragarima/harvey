Union Of India v Naveen Jindal & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Brijesh Kumar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 25; (2004) 2 SCC 510; AIR 2004 SC 1559; 2004 (3) CHN 30; 2004 (109) DLT 717; JT 2004 (2) SC 1; 2004(1) SCALE 677; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 1038; 2004 (1) SLT 832; 2004 (1) Supreme 880

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Intellectual Property

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 13(3), 14, 162, 166(3), 19, 19(1), 19(1)(a), 19(2), 21, 22, 48, 5, 51, 77 - Police Act, 1861, s. 12 - Emblems And Names (Prevention Of Improper Use) Act, 1950, s. 3 - National Assistance Act, 1948 - Prevention Of Insults To National Honour Act, 1971 - Government of India (Allocation of Business) Rules, 1961 - Fundamental right - Right to fly national flag by Indian citizen - Held, right to fly National Flag freely with respect and dignity is a fundamental right of a citizen within meaning of art 19(1)(a), being an expression and manifestation of his allegiance and feelings and sentiments of pride for nation; (ii) fundamental right to fly National Flag is not an absolute right but a qualified one being subject to reasonable restrictions under cl 2 of art 19; (iii) Emblems and Names (Prevention of Improper Use) Act, 1950 and Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act, 1971 regulate use of National Flag; (iv) Flag Code although is not a law within meaning of art 13(3)(a), for purpose of cl (2) of art 19 thereof, it would not restrictively regulate free exercise of right of flying national flag However, Flag Code to extent it provides for preserving respect and dignity of National Flag, same deserves to be followed; (v) For purpose of interpretation of constitutional scheme and for purpose of maintaining a balance between fundamental/legal rights of a citizen vis-Ã -vis, regulatory measures/restrictions, both Parts IV and IVA of Constitution of India can be taken recourse to.
