Hind Construction and Engineering Company Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1965 SC 917; 1965 (10) FLR 165; 1965 (1) LLJ 462; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Holiday

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether Tribunal was justified in interfering with punishment of dismissal after it had come to conclusion that workmen had gone on a strike even though strike was not illegal? - Tribunal is not required to consider propriety or adequacy of punishment or whether it is excessive or too severe - But where punishment is shockingly disproportionate, regard being had to particular conduct and past record or is such, as no reasonable employer would ever impose in like circumstances, Tribunal may treat imposition of such punishment as itself showing victimization or unfair labour practice - Held, interference was justified in this case because punishment was not only severe and out of proportion to fault, but one which no reasonable employer would have imposed - Appeal dismissed.
