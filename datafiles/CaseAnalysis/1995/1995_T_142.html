Santosh Kumar Singh v The State Of U.P. And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1903; (1996) 2 SCC 45; (1996) SCC (L&S) 400; 1996 (32) ATC 832; JT 1995 (9) SC 530; 1996 LabIC 935; 1995(7) SCALE 281; 1996 (1) SLR 602; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 568; 1996 (1) Supreme 33

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ad Hoc Appointment

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, ss. 16, 31B, 31C - Adhoc appointment - Termination - Regularization of service - Appellant while pursuing his final year studies was appointed as a lecturer - Service Commission issued fresh advertisement for filling up the post on adhoc basis - Appellant unanimously selected - 1985 Ordinance was promulgated for regularization of the adhoc teachers, permanent job status and salary was to be at par with other regularized and permanent employees - Management stopped his salary since his appointment was invalid and later terminated appellant from the services - Without approval of Vice-Chancellor appellant could not be terminated and order was passed in favour of appellant - W.P.s of appellant for executing the order of Vice-Chancellor dismissed on grounds of illegal appointment - W.P. of State allowed - Appellant aggrieved - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, the dismissal of the W.P.s of the appellant by the HC justifiable - Held, the initial adhoc appointment of the appellant as a lecturer was wholly without jurisdiction and such appointment did not confer the right of regularization on the appellant by virtue of the Regularization Ordinance which was later on replaced by an Act by inserting s. 31B of the 1980 Act into the Service Commission Act - Adhoc appointment could be made u/s. 16 of the Act only when the Management had notified the vacancy to the Commission and Commission had failed to recommend the name of suitable candidate within three months from the date of the notification - Since this condition as well as condition of required academic qualification of the appellant not being fulfilled, his appointment was held to be invalid and inoperative - Subsequent appointment wherein the appellant had acquired the qualification and Management could make adhoc appointment, could not be regularized u/s. 31B of the Act, since it was not made within the stipulated time-period - Benefit of s. 31C of the Act could not be availed since the appellant was not in service on the day s. 31C of the Act become enforceable - Order passed by the Vice-Chancellor cancelling the termination of the appellant was erroneous and HC had rightly dismissed the W.P.s of the appellant - Appeals dismissed.
