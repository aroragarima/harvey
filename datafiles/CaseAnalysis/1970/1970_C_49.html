Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Mysore v Segu Buchiah Setty

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 59; (1970) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1971 SC 854; [1970] 77 ITR 539; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 352

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 ss. 22(2), 22(4), 23(4), 27 - Assessment - Notice - Non compliance - Assessee was served with notices u/ss. 22(2), 22(4) of the Act for A/Y's 1953 - 1954, 1954 - 1955 - Assessee failed to file returns in compliance with notices and failed to appear before ITO but sought adjournment - ITO dissatisfied with reasons given for adjournments, proceeded to make assessment u/s. 23(4) of the Act - Assessee filed application before ITO u/s. 27 of the Act to reopen assessment on grounds given in application which was declined - Appellant filed appeal against said order before Tribunal, which was dismissed and upheld view of ITO - On appeal HC answered in favour of assessee - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 27 of the Act has to be read in a reasonable and harmonious way and in conjunction with s. 23(4)of the Act. It is inconceivable that the legislature could ever intended that in case of' multiple defaults for each one of which an ex-parte judgment and if assessment has to be made the assessee can ask for cancellation of' the assessment by merely showing cause for one of such defaults. Therefore, in case of Chiranjilal Tibrewala v.. The Commissioner of Income tax, Bombay City II, 1965 Indlaw MUM 33, held HC was right in holding that in circumstances similar to instant case the assessee cannot ask for cancellation u/s. 27 of the Act of an assessment made u/s. 23 (4)of the Act. Hence, judgment of the HC is set aside, and question answered against the assessee and in favour of the appellant. Appeals allowed.
