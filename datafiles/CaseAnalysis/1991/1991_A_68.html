Apar Private Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 605; (1992) Supp1 SCC 1; 1991 (4) Bom.C.R. 409; 1991(2) SCALE 805

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Petitioners purchased some land at Ulhasnagar in 1957, area of which, according to deed of conveyance r/w prior agreement of sale was 39, 200 sq yds - Area of encroachment by petitioners was determined at 25, 915 sq yds by order dated May 26, 1967 passed by Managing Officer and Administrator, Ulhasnagar Township - By same order a direction was given for removal of encroachment over Govt. land - No appeal or any other statutory remedy was restored to against this order by petitioners - (A) Whether area of encroachment was less that what was determined? - Held, binding effect of order dated 26-05-1967 concludes this controversy and extent of encroachment by petitioners has to be taken as 25, 915 sq yds on date of that order as a concluded finding of fact - (B) Whether area of encroachment could be reduced subsequently and a fresh determination of existing encroachment could be made? - Further Further held, it has not been petitioners' case till now that petitioners restored possession of any part of encroachment area to State Govt. at any time subsequent to aforesaid order - Without taking any such plea and proving restoration of possession of any part of encroached area - Petitioners cannot contend that area of encroachment is reduced subsequently to less than 25, 915 sq yds - Petition dismissed.
