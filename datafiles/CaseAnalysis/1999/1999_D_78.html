Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited and Another v Dolly Das

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1073; (1999) 4 SCC 450; JT 1999 (3) SC 61; 1999(2) SCALE 584

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Undertakings in India of Caltex (India) Ltd. Act, 1977, ss. 5 and 7(1) (3) - Lease Deed - Renewable and determinable as provided in lease deed - M/s Caltex (India) Ltd. was taken over by Govt. of India and was amalgamated with petitioner - Exercised option of renewal of lease for a further term of 10 years from expiration of present tenure of lease in question - Quashing of - Notice of renewal - Whether invocation of jurisdiction of HC in a contractual matter between two private parties though having a statutory flavour or adjudication of rights constitutes abuse of process of court? - In absence of constitutional or statutory rights being involved a writ proceeding would not lie to enforce contractual obligations even if it is sought to be enforced against State or to avoid contractual liability arising thereto - Exercise of powers u/s. 7 - Delay in filing writ - Delay, by itself, may not defeat claim for relief unless position of appellant had been so altered which cannot be retracted on account of lapse of time or inaction of other party - Period for which option of renewal has been exercised had not come to an end - During subsistence of such a period certainly respondent could make a complaint that such exercise of option was not available to appellants and, therefore, jurisdiction of HC could be invoked even at a later stage - No hardship in any manner caused to respondent by reason of delay in approaching HC - Where interpretation of a contract arises in relation to immovable property and in working such a contract or relief thereof or any other fallout thereto may have effect of giving rise to an action in tort or for damages, appropriate remedy would be a civil suit - Interpretation of clause of lease deed - A covenant for renewal is not treated as a part of terms prescribing period of lease but only entitles a lessee to obtain a fresh lease - Held, renewal of lease could only be for one term and no more, but nevertheless it could be contended that covenant for renewal was also part of lease and, therefore, stood incorporated in renewed lease arising under Act - Appeal disposed of.
