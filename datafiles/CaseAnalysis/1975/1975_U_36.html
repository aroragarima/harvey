Â Sabhajit Tewary v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Alagiriswami, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 106; (1975) SCC (L&S) 99; (1975) 1 SCC 485; AIR 1975 SC 1329; 1975 (30) FLR 212; 1975 LabIC 819; 1975 (1) LLJ 374; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 616; 1975 SLJ 410; 1975 (1) SLR 422; 1975 (255) UJ 2

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 16 - Recommendation - Remuneration - Petitioner was working as junior stenographer in council of Scientific and Industrial Research asked for order declaring two letters which relate to recommendations of finance sub-committee of said council with regard to remuneration of stenographers - Petitioner filed instant writ alleging that he should be granted same number of advance increments as approved and granted to new persons in order to entitle the petitioner to impeach the circular on the ground of infraction u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution.

Held, Society does not have a statutory character like the Oil and Natural Gas Commission, or the Life Insurance Corporation or Industrial Finance Corporation. It is a society incorporated in accordance with the provisions of the Societies Registration Act. The, fact that the Prime Minister is the President or that the Govt. appoints nominees to the Governing Body or that the, Govt. may terminate the membership will not establish anything more than the fact that the Govt. takes special care that the promotion, guidance and co-operation of scientific and industrial research, the institution and financing of specific researches, establishment or development and assistance to special institutions or departments of the existing institutions for scientific study of problems affecting particular industry in a trade, the utilisation of the result of the researches conducted under the auspices of the Council towards the development of industries in the country are carried out in a responsible manner. Hence, the Council of Scientific and Industrial Research is not an authority within the meaning u/art. 12 of Constitution. Petition dismissed.
