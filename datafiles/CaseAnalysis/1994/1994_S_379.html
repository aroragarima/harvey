Jashubha Bharatsinh Gohil and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1475; (1994) 4 SCC 353; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1193; AIR 1994 SCW 2360; 1994 (3) CCR 710; 1994 (2) Crimes 92; JT 1994 (3) SC 250; 1994(2) SCALE 534; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 471

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Enhancement of Sentence - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 354 - Indian Penal Code, 1860. ss. 120 B, 148, 149, 302 and 307 - 12 persons, appellants, were members of unlawful assembly caused death of 10 persons by firearms - During appeal, sentence of appellant no. 11 was enhanced to death sentence by High Court - Whether HC was justified in imposing death sentence upon appellant? - Held, no - No hard and fast rule laid down for enhancement of sentence and same has to be sparingly exercised - Each case dependant upon facts and a variety of factors - Death penalty to be imposed for special reasons only - HC imposed death sentence taking a different view whereas findings of Trial Court were not perverse - Sentence of death of appellant No. 11 commuted to imprisonment for life - Appeals of the remaining accused A-1, A-5, A-7, A-8, A-9 and A-12 are dismissed and their conviction and sentences are maintained - Order accordingly.
