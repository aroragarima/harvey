Â Rama Sugar Industries Ltd. v State Of Andhra Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 215; (1974) 1 SCC 534; AIR 1974 SC 1745; 1974 (3) CTR 44; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 787; 1974 (33) STC 277; 1974 TAXLR 2036

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Exemption From Payment, Andhra Pradesh Sugar cane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act (45 of 1961)

Summary: A.P. Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961, s. 21(3) - Constitution, arts. 14 and 133 - (A) Tax on purchase of cane - Powers of State Govt. to give exemption from payment - Exercise of discretion - Govt. if justified in laying down a policy of granting exemption only to co-operative sugar factories - (B) New Plea - State allowing exemption from purchase tax to co-operative sugar factories consisting of sugarcane growers - Plea that out of 1280 shares 1247 shares of appellant factory were held by cane growers not raised in the High Court - Permissibility, at argument stage in Supreme Court - Held, it has properly exercised the discretion conferred on it by the statutet - It cannot be allowed in SC at argument stage - Appeal And Petition Dismissed.
