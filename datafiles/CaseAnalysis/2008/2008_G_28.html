State of Rajasthan v Gajendra Singh

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2409; (2008) 12 SCC 720; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 744; 2008 (3) JCC 2061; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 943; 2008(11) SCALE 9; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 323 and 341 - Rape - Conviction u/s. 376 - Reduction of sentence - Respondent faced trial for alleged commission of offences punishable u/ss. 376, 323 and 341 of IPC and was sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years, six months and six months respectively - Respondent filed appeal praying for reduction of sentence - HC directed the sentence to be reduced to a period of five years (below the minimum prescribed) rigorous imprisonment for the offence relatable to s. 376 IPC and also reduced the sentence of six month's simple imprisonment to one month's simple imprisonment in the case of s. 341 IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was right in reducing the sentence, as imposed by the trial court on respondent? - Held, in order to exercise the discretion of reducing the sentence the statutory requirement is that the court has to record 'adequate and special reasons' in the judgment - HC has not recorded any reason, much less any adequate and special reasons for reducing the sentence - HC was, therefore, not justified in reducing the sentence below the prescribed minimum - Impugned judgement of HC set aside in respect of the offence punishable u/s. 376 IPC - Appeal allowed.
