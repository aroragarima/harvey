Deputy Registrar, Cooperative Societies, Faizabad v Sachindra Nath Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1557; (1995) 3 SCC 134; (1995) SCC (L&S) 648; AIR 1995 SCW 3028; 1995 (29) ATC 538; 1995 (1) CLR 596; 1995 (70) FLR 970; JT 1995 (2) SC 407; 1995 (1) LLJ 1069; 1995 (1) LLN 786; 1995(1) SCALE 848; 1995 (4) SLR 17; 1995 (1) UJ 485; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 977

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cooperative Federal Authority (Business) Regulations, 1976

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Cooperative Federal Authority (Business) Regulations, 1976, regn. 68 - Dismissal - Validity of - Whether inquiry and dismissal of respondent is illegal and vitiated? - Held, charges are very serious - High Court has not dealt with submissions and facts in support of submission of appellant - That in spite of being given a number of opportunities first respondent has failed to avail of them - If appellant's allegations are true then appellant cannot be faulted for not holding a regular inquiry - HC has assumed even without referring to regn. 68, held that an oral inquiry was obligatory - In the circumstances, the Writ Petition could not have been allowed unless it was held that the appellant's version of events is not true and that the first respondent's version is true - Set aside the order under appeal - Matter remitted back to HC - Appeal allowed.
