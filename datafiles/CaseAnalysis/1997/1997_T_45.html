Rajesh Kumar and Another v Institute of Engineers (India)

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1908; (1997) 6 SCC 674; AIR 1998 SC 5; AIR 1997 SCW 3946; 1998 (1) AWC 158; JT 1997 (7) SC 220; 1997(5) SCALE 309; 1997 (4) SLR 805; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 90; 1997 (7) Supreme 71

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Whether the order of the institute in cancelling the results of the appellants' examination and disqualifying them for two succeeding examinations, is justified? - The overall consideration of the institute reflected that its members thought that they would be put to an embarrassment if the plea of the two appellants were to be accepted and therefore, thought of declining relief to the appellants, which cannot be permitted to follow from the deliberation of the institute - Held, the impugned order of the High Court is set aside - The impugned communication D/- 11-12-1990 is quashed - Held, the respondent-institute is ordered to declare the result of the appellants forthwith - Appeal allowed.
