Ethiopian Airlines v Ganesh Narain Saboo

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 572; (2011) 8 SCC 539; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 114; 2011 (88) ALR 498; 2011 (5) AWC 5398; 2011 (4) CPJ(Sc) 43; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 71; 2011 (2) OLR 833; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 93; 2011 (4) RLW 2929; 2011(8) SCALE 549; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Trader

Summary: Consumer Protection - Civil Procedure - Carriage By Air Act, 1972, ss. 2(ii), 3, 7, 9 - Carriers Act, 1865 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 9, 86, 86(1), 86(2), - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 3, 13, 13(4), 13(5), 13(6), 24(A), 30(A) - Indian Carriage of Goods By Sea Act, 1925 - Indian Railways Act, 1890, s. 80 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1965 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Complaint in Consumer Forum - Proceedings - Claim for Sovereign Immunity - Sustainability - Respondent booked consignment of Reactive Dyes with appellant - Respondent filed complaint before Maharashtra State Consumer Dispute Redressal Commission ('State Commission') for gross delay in delivery of goods by appellant - State Commission held that complaint filed by respondent was not maintainable - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission ('National Commission') - National Commission categorically observed in impugned judgment s. 86 CPC was not applicable since case in dispute was covered under provisions of the 1986 Act - Moreover, National Commission set aside order passed by State Commission and remitted it to State Commission - Hence, instant appeal - Whether proceedings before Consumer Forum were suits according to CPC and appellant was entitled for sovereign immunity - Held, s. 3 of the 1972 Act r/w. s. 7 left no room or doubt that state carrier or legally constituted public body of international carrier was deemed to have submitted to jurisdiction of courts in India, including for purpose of CPC - The 1986 Act sets forth exhaustive list of procedures, distinguishable from those required under CPC, that consumer redressal should follow - Consumer Protection Act did not state that s. 86 CPC applicable to consumer fora's proceedings, s. 86 CPC should be held to be not applicable - CPC itself did not claim to make s. 86 applicable to proceedings before consumer forum - CPC specifically referred to District Courts, HCs, and SC and made little if any reference to other, quasi- judicial fora like consumer redressal bodies at issue in instant case - S. 86 CPC was inapplicable because legislative intent was deem to exclude older and more general statute by more recent and special statutes viz. the 1986Act and the1972Act - The 1986Act and the 1972Act, which came long after CPC, were more focused and specific statutes, and therefore should be held to exclude s. 86 - S. 86 CPC was inapplicable to instant case because older and more general statute had been excluded by more recent special statute, namely, the 1986 Act and the 1972 Act - Both the 1986 Act and the 1972 Act, which came long after CPC were more focused and specific statutes and therefore should be held to supersede s. 86 CPC - Countries who participate in trade, commerce and business with different countries ought to be subjected to normal rules of market - Hence, appellant should not claim sovereign immunity - Appeals disposed of.
