Shrimant Dattaji Raobahirojirao Ghorpade v Shrimant Vijayasinhrao and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1960 SC 1272; 1961 (1) SCJ 434; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949 - Bombay Revenue Jurisdiction Act, 1876 - One Bhujangarao Daulatrao Ghorpade was holder of Saranjam estate - In 1932 Saranjam was resumed and re-granted to said Bhunjangarao by Govt. of Bombay - One of younger branches of Ghorpade family was Babasaheb - He had an undivided brother called Dattojirao, who is appellant before us - On Babasaheb's death he left a widow named Abayabai and appellant, his undivided brother - On 1007-1941, Abayabai adopted Vijayasinhrao, plaintiff who brought suit and is now principal respondent before us - On Babasaheb's death Abayabai asked for sanction of Govt. to her taking a boy in adoption, this application was opposed by appellant - On 17-12-1941, Govt. of Bombay passed a Resolution stating that appellant would be new Potgidar of Saranjam potgi, that he should give an annual maintenance allowance of Rs. 300 to Bai Abayabai - Claim of plaintiff-respondent was that on his adoption estate of his deceased adoptive father devolved on him by the, rule of lineal primogeniture in preference to appellant - (A) Whether plaintiff has proved his title to suit property? - Held, Saranjam Rules furnish no basis for claim of plaintiff respondent - Abayabai asked for sanction to her taking a boy in adoption - No such sanction was given - On death of Babasaheb, it was open to Govt. to resume grant, and by its Resolution of 17-12-1941, Govt. directed that Saranjam potgi holding of village Dindur and Survey No 302 of Unachgeri should be continued to appellant - This really amounted to a resumption and fresh grant - (B) Whether Govt. Resolution (D G ) No. 8969 of 17-12-1941, is ultra vires and null and void? - Held, if Resolution dated 17-12-1941, is read as a whole it is clear that potgi of village Dindur and Survey field No. 302 of Unachgeri was granted to present appellant - It was open to Govt. to pass such an order, and no reasons to hold that it was null and void - Adoption took place on 10-07-1941, and Resolution was passed on 17-12-1941, though it took effect retrospectively from date of death of Babasaheb - No reasons why a valid order made by Govt. will cease to have any effect because of an adoption made by Abayabai without sanction of Govt. - (C) Whether suit is barred u/s 4 of Revenue Jurisdiction Act? - Held, Civil Court has no jurisdiction to determine any claim against Govt. in matter of Resolution of 17-12-1941, relating to Saranjam lands, and suit was barred u/s 4 of Bombay Revenue Jurisdiction Act, 1876 - (D) Is alleged custom set up in para 6(b) of plaint proved? - Held, plaintiff-respondent set up a family custom of lineal primogeniture different from ordinary law of inheritance; it was incumbent on him to allege and prove custom on which he relied and to show its precise extent and how far it prevailed over ordinary Hindu law - In our opinion, he failed to plead or prove any family custom by which properties devolved on him - Appeal allowed.
