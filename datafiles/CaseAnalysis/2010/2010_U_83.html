State of Assam v Union of India and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 875; (2010) 10 SCC 408; 2011 (1) CTC 375; 2011 (128) FLR 568; JT 2010 (10) SC 592; 2011 (1) LLJ 609; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 838; 2010(10) SCALE 352; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: ROP Rules, 1990

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Direction to non-party to the suit - Mis-joinder of parties - 1st respondent/Union of India appointed private respondents as 'Volunteers' in its Family Welfare Scheme - One of the private respondents filed a writ petition seeking parity of pay as of regularly appointed post of Ward Girls, which had higher pay scale - Single Judge allowed writ petition and directed the appellant/State to pay the minimum pay scale - Subsequently, number of other private respondents also sought the same relief along with the relief of regularisation - Single Judge allowed the writ petitions to the extent of parity of pay, however, did not grant any benefit with regard to the regularizations - 1st respondent/Union of India, being aggrieved by the Single Judge order filed appeal before the DB, however, 1st respondent did not implead appellant/State in that petition - 1st respondent contended that the Volunteers were not their employees and, therefore, Single Judge ought not to have issued any direction to the 1st respondent, much less for payment of minimum of pay scale - DB absolved 1st respondent of the responsibility of making any payment more than the minimum pay scale to Volunteers, but fixed the liability on the appellant/State Government - Hence the present appeal by the appellant/State Government - (A) Whether 1st respondent should have impleaded appellant in the petition before the DB - Held, appellant/State should have been joined as a necessary party in the petition before the DB in view of grounds raised and relief sought in the memorandum of writ appeal filed by the 1st respondent/Union of India - Appellant was the 1st respondent in that writ petition that was filed by the private respondents before the Single Judge - Main grievance of the 1st respondent before the DB was against the direction issued by the Single Judge to pay minimum pay scale to the volunteers, since it was their stand in the said writ appeal that under the scheme, their liability was only to the extent of payment of minimum scale as honorarium to Volunteers and anything over and above, requires to be paid by the appellant - DB imposed the burden of payment of the salary/wages as directed by the Single Judge on appellant/State in view of the fact that the appointments were made by appellant - Therefore, omission or default in impleading the appellant cannot be characterized as technical breach nor just an irregularity, since said omission has resulted in a party suffering an adverse order without getting a fair hearing - DB, while allowing the appeals filed by the 1st respondent and shifting the liability of payment of salary/wages of Volunteers on appellant, should have taken a little more care and caution to find out whether the State of Assam is arrayed as a party to the proceedings and whether they are served with the notice of the appeals and in spite of service, whether they have remained absent - Therefore, impugned DB order is set aside and matter remanded back to DB for de-novo hearing - However, direction issued to 1st respondent to pay the minimum wages under the provisions of Minimum Wages Act, as notified in their official Gazette to the private respondents during the interregnum - Appeal allowed.
