Ratanchand Darbarilal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 9; (1985) 4 SCC 183; AIR 1985 SC 1572; 1985 (48) CTR 349; 1985 (22) E.L.T. 635; [1985] 155 ITR 720; [1985] 23 TAXMAN 45; 1986 (1) UJ 242

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Partial Partition

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26A - Income Tax - Appeals by special leave from decision High Court on the three references made to it u/s. 66 of the Act - Legality of grant of registration of firm by Tribunal - Partnerships formed by members of two HUFs to look after businesses at two locations - Finding that requisite conditions were satisfied - Consideration of every aspect which weighed with ITO and AAC for refusing registration by Tribunal - Held, registration not illegal - High Court obviously fell into an error in proceeding on the footing that without a partition or a partial partition some of the members belonging to the HUF could not constitute themselves into a partnership firm - Appeal allowed.
