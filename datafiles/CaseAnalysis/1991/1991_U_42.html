Ex. Naik Sardar Singh v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 553; (1991) 3 SCC 213; (1991) SCC (Cr) 503; (1991) SCC (L&S) 975; AIR 1992 SC 417; 1992 (19) ATC 325; 1991 (2) Crimes 674; 1992 CRLJ 289; 1991 CrLR(SC) 489; 1991 (63) FLR 261; JT 1991 (3) SC 1; 1992 (2) LLJ 155; 1991 (2) LLN 424; 1991(1) SCALE 899; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 676; 1992 (8) SLR 788; 1991 (2) UJ 466

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Punishments

Summary: Defence & Security Forced - Army Act, 1950, ss. 63, 71, 72 and 73 - Accused an army jawan was found to have extra 7 bottles of rum within area under prohibition while going to home town - Accused had a valid permit to carry 5 bottles but he pleaded that extra bottles were purchased from Army Canteen on chit given by higher authority - In court martial proceedings he was awarded punishments of three months R I and dismissal from service - Whether sentence in instant case is justified?- Held, sentence was liable to be set aside as it was severe, arbitrary and violative of s. 72 of Act of 1950, case therefore remanded to court martial - Appeal disposed.
