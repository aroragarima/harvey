K. G. Ashok and Others v Kerala Public Service Commission and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20139; (2001) 5 SCC 419; (2001) SCC (L&S) 845; AIR 2001 SC 2010; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 154; 2001 LabIC 1769; 2001(3) SCALE 747; 2001 (4) SCJ 173; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 453; 2001 (4) SLR 243; 2001 (5) SLT 263; 2001 (4) Supreme 337

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 32, 136, 285, 309 -

Kerala State and Subordinate Services Rules, 1958, rr. 2(4), 2(12), 3, 4, 5A, 8, 22, 40 -Notification - Applied- Rejected - Challenged - Respondent by notification in official Gazette and by publication in news paper invited applications for Junior Health Inspector in 14 districts with restriction to apply for one district only and would be rejected if applied for more than one district - 436 persons including this appellants applications were rejected either having applied for more than one district or giving false declaration - Writ petitions filed by appellants in HC challenging orders of appellant were dismissed by Single Judge and confirmed by DB in appeal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Orders of appellant and HC were justified - Held, appellant had very clearly intimated terms and conditions and Part-II of notification contained application form and general conditions wherein clearly intimated that applications applied for more than one district and applications with false declarations would be liable to be rejected summarily and if appointments were made they would be terminated and liable for prosecution proceedings etc - Restriction contained in Note II was not violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Right of candidate was not curtailed as he/she was not prevented from choosing district of his/her choice - If every person was permitted to apply for all districts number of applications received by respondent/commission would be 14 times more and heavily burdened in selection process in all 14 districts and after all candidate could join in one district only and in order to lessen its burden restriction was imposed - Restriction did not tantamount to denial of opportunity to candidate for applying to any post - In concluding portion of short notification dt.11.4.1996 it was specifically mentioned that for more details candidate should refer concerned notification means notification dt. 2-4-1996 - Rejection of applications of these appellants was justified having rejected for making false declarations and having applied for more than one district in contravention of gazette notification - Orders of appellant and HC were justified - Appeals dismissed.
