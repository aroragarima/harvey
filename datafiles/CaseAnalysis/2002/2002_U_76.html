Sher Bahadur v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1989; (2002) 7 SCC 142; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1028; AIR 2002 SC 3030; 2002 (4) AWC 2877; 2002 (95) FLR 11; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 12; JT 2002 (6) SC 152; 2002 LabIC 2974; 2002 (3) LLJ 848; 2002(5) SCALE 616; 2002 (5) SLR 600; 2002 (4) SLT 768; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 568; 2002 (5) Supreme 330

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1986, r. 6 (vii) to (ix) - Railway Services (Conduct) Rules, 1966, r. 3 1(i)(ii), (iii) - Dismissal - Sufficiency of evidence - Whether High Court in dismissing writ petition on ground of sufficiency of evidence against appellant by disciplinary authority is justified? - Held, it is clearly a case of finding appellant guilty of charge without having any evidence to link appellant with alleged misconduct - Sufficiency of evidence completely misunderstood by High Court and order of disciplinary authority under challenge set aside - Compensation to appellant equal to average salary for two years - Appeal allowed.
