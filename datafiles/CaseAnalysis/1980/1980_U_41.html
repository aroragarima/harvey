Col. A.S. Sangwan v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 610; (1980) Supp SCC 559; (1981) SCC (L&S) 378; AIR 1981 SC 1545; 1981 LabIC 831

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Competing claims - Both petitioner and third respondent claims to be promoted as Brigadiers in Directorate of Military Farms - Both had their turn as Directors of Military Farms and when 'X' retired, post fell vacant and an appointment had to be made - Core of contention before High Court was that once a policy had been made in exercise of general executive power of Union of India and made known and acted upon, it would be arbitrary - HC issued a writ forbidding any fresh selection in variance of 1971 selection - Policy challenged? - Held, a policy once formulated is not good for ever; it is perfectly within competence of Union of India to change it, rechange it, adjust it and readjust it according to compulsions of circumstances and imperatives of national considerations - There is no bar to its changing policy formulated in 1964 if there are good and weighty reasons for doing so - It is entirely within reasonable discretion of Union of India - It may stick to earlier policy or give it up - But one imperative of Constitution implicit in art. 14 is that if it does change its policy, it must do so fairly and should not give impression that it is acting by any ulterior criteria or arbitrarily - Writ Petition and Civil Appeal disposed of.
