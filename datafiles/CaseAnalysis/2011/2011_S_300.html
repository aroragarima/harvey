J.S. Yadav v State Of U.P. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 272; (2011) 6 SCC 570; 2011 (8) ADJ 77; 2011 (2) AllCJ 1546; 2011 (86) ALR 765; 2011 (4) AWC 4023; 2011 (3) CTC 532; 2011 (2) ESC 380; JT 2011 (5) SC 186; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 998; 2011(4) SCALE 733; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 460; 2011 (4) SLR 465; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2036

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Protection of Human Rights (Amendment) Act, 2006, U.P. Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1975

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - The Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, ss. 21, 23, 25 and 26 - The Protection of Human Rights (Amendment) Act, 2006 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appellant, Principal Secretary and Legal Remembrancer, was appointed as a Member of the Commission on 29.6.2006 for a period of 5 years i.e. till 30.6.2011 - Subsequently, the Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993 stood amended vide the Protection of Human Rights (Amendment) Act, 2006 and the State of U.P. issued Notification dt. 28.5.2008 under Amendment Act 2006 to the effect that appellant ceased to hold the office as a Member of the Commission - Appellant challenged said notification dt. 28.5.2008 by filing writ petition on the ground that he had been appointed for a tenure of 5 years and that period could not be curtailed - Appellant also contended that Amendment Act, 2006 could not take away the accrued rights of the appellant as he had been appointed prior to the said amendment - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the State could issue the Notification making a declaration that the appellant ceased to be the member of the Commission and whether the said Notification could take away the accrued rights of the appellant? - Held, the Legislature was competent to unilaterally alter the service conditions of the employee and that can be done with retrospective effect also, but the intention of the Legislature to apply the amended provisions with retrospective effect must be evident from the Amendment Act itself expressly or by necessary implication - In the instant case, Amendment Act, 2006 does not expressly or by necessary implication suggest that Amendment Act, 2006 would apply retrospectively - Moreover, appellant had joined as a member of the Commission under the Act, 1993 - Section 26 of the Act, 1993 specifically provided that neither the salary and allowances nor other terms and conditions of service of a member shall be varied to his disadvantage after his appointment - Hence, service conditions of the appellant could not be curtailed vide Amendment Act, 2006 as he had been appointed prior to the said amendment - Notification dt. 28.5.2008 was illegal - Accrued rights of appellant cannot be taken away by repealing the statutory provisions arbitrarily - Appeal allowed.
