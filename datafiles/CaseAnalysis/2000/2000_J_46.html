Ravinder Singh v Janmeja Singh And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2826; (2000) 8 SCC 191; AIR 2000 SC 3026; 2002 (1) GLR 567; JT 2000 (10) SC 583; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 743; 2000(6) SCALE 424(B); [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Allegations Of Corrupt Practice

Summary: Election- Representation of People Act, 1951,s.83 ,s.123 ,s.83(1) ,s.123(1) ,s.123(4) ,s.123(1)(A)(b) ,s.100(1)(d)(iv) - Corrupt practice - Bribery - Publication of false statement about character/conduct of candidate - (A) Election petition - Averment that returned candidate has published it knowing it to be false and/or not believing it to be true, if mandatory - (B) Determination of effect of non disclosure of name of informant and election petition not being supported by affidavit in prescribed form - Held, mandatory , in its absence charge u/s. 23(4) cannot be put to trial - Defect is fatal and allegation cannot be put to trial - Appeal Dismissed.
