Dr. (Mrs.) Gurjeewan Garewal v Dr. (Mrs.) Sumitra Dash & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 300; (2004) 5 SCC 263; (2004) SCC (L&S) 747; AIR 2004 SC 2530; 2004 (2) CLR 632; 2004 (4) ESC 449; 2004 (101) FLR 999; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 176; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 140; 2004 (2) LLN 808; 2004(4) SCALE 680; 2004 (3) SLR 682; 2004 (3) Supreme 467; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2285

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Health & Drug; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: [A.] Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 311, 311(2) - Post-Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh, Act, 1966, s. 4 - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 14 - PGIMER Regulations, 1967, regns. 35, 36 - PGIMER Rules, r. 36 - Whether HC's approach that Respondent No 1 was not given an opportunity of hearing, is correct - Applicability of art.311 - Only persons employed in civil capacities under Union of India or a State can claim protection under art.311 - PIGMER is a separate entity and employees of a legal entity separate from state, cannot claim to be holders of civil posts under State - PIGMER may complete enquiry as expeditiously as possible.
