State of Andhra Pradesh and Another v Concap Capacitors and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1322; (2007) 8 SCC 658; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 478; 2007 (217) E.L.T. 481; JT 2007 (12) SC 62; 2007(12) SCALE 473; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 140; [2007] 10 VST 204

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Finding of fact, Unjust Enrichment, Functional Test, Appeal disposed of, Central Act, Rate Of Tax, Concessional Rate, Concessional Rate Of Tax, Rate, Collected, Setting Aside, Relevant Provisions, Government Orders

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether item 'capacitors' is 'electronic goods' or 'electric goods'? - Held, from G O Ms and circular issued by Commr., it is clear that in pursuance of several representations received from dealers requesting to clarify specific items falling under 'electronic goods' that classification memo was issued by Govt. and circular by Commr. on basis of list prepared by Electronic Commission - Said list expressly contained an item 'capacitors' - In view of specific reference to 'capacitors', HC was right in relying on said item and in holding that 'capacitors' could be said to be 'electronic goods' and was covered by a concessional rate of tax under Act - Item 'capacitors' has been expressly included in list prepared by Electronic Commission and hence it was not open to Tribunal to apply 'operating principle' or 'user test' and High Court was wholly justified in interfering with order passed by Tribunal - Appeal disposed of.
