Messrs Guruswamy and Company Etc v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 321; AIR 1967 SC 1512; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Excise Regulation

Summary: Constitution Indirect Tax Constitution Of India, 1950, art.226, 245, 246(3) and 265 and sch.7, List.2, Entry 51 of List II - Mysore Health Cess Act, 1962, ss.1, 3, 3(ii), 4, ch. A, Item I - Mysore Excise Act, 1901, ss.12, 15, 16, 17, 18, 18(c)(2) and 28 - Writ petitions - One dealing with licences for sale of Toddy and other dealing with licences for sale of Arrack - Petitioner alleged that as a result of Mysore Health Cess Act, 1962, it would have to pay Rs. 86,518 more as health cess for year 1964-65 - Challenged impugned Act as ultra vires - (A) Whether levy on factors which have close relation to sale of goods is an excise duty ? - Levy is in respect of business of carrying on sale of Toddy - There is no connection of any part of levy with any manufacture or production of any goods - Held, meaning of excise duty cannot include a levy which has close relation to sale of excisable goods - (B) Whether State legislature is competent to make a law levying a surcharge on shop rent under Entry 51, List II? - As a result of public auctions held subject to terms and conditions notified by State Govt. appellants were granted exclusive privileges of selling liquor in certain arrack shops, beer taverns and toddy shops in consideration of their agreeing to pay specified shop rents and health cess thereon at rate of nine naya paise in rupee for State submitted that shop rent is a duty of excise - Mysore Cess Act, 1962 levied a surcharge on this duty - Further held, State Legislature was competent to make this levy, having regard to Entry 51, List II of Seventh Schedule to Constitution - Appeal allowed.
