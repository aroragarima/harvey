Surinder Pal Jain v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 195; (1993) Supp3 SCC 681; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1096; AIR 1993 SC 1723; 1993 (3) AWC 1638; 1993 CRLJ 1871; JT 1993 (2) SC 206; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 226

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 203 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3, and 9 - Murder - Evidence - Held, contradictory - Evidence of convenient witnesses and investigation officer not trustworthy - During investigation dogs of police dog squad pointed towards accused - Further held, proof is not sufficient to connect him with crime - Appeal allowed.
