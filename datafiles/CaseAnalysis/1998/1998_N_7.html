K. Muthuswami Gounder v N. Palaniappa Gounder

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, B.N. Kirpal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1183; (1998) 7 SCC 327; AIR 1998 SC 3118; 1999 (1) Bom.C.R. 508; 1999 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 1998 (4) CLT 117; JT 1998 (6) SC 174; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 1998(5) SCALE 17; 1998 (7) SLT 258; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 206; 1998 (7) Supreme 59

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Furnishing Of Security

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 11, O. 41, r.33 - Redemption of suit - (A) Whether High Court can dismiss suit by setting aside decree for redemption in absence of an appeal by defendant in redemption suit? - Held, in exceptional cases, Rule enables appellate court to pass such decree or order as ought to have been passed even if such decree would be in favour of parties who have not filed any appeal - Power though discretionary should not be declined to be exercised merely on ground that party has not filed any appeals - (B) Whether a decree for redemption became final and operated as res judicata by not having been appealed against? - Held, entire matter is still in appeal and any part of finding could be varied by appellate court, it is idle to contend that same had become final, so also when matter had not attained finality and was still in dispute, principle of res judicata could not arise - Appeals dismissed.
