State of W. B. and Others v Niranjan Singha

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2610; (2001) 2 SCC 326; 2001 (1) AWC 440; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 610; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 24; 2000(8) SCALE 263; 2001 (1) SLT 86; 2000 (8) Supreme 247; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 333

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contention of respondent before High Court was that he was appointed as an agent for collection of toll/taxes from vehicles plying over a Setu vide an agreement - Respondent sought for extension of agency for a period of another one year in terms of cl. 5 of agreement - Appellant invited fresh bids for appointment of agent to collect toll or taxes instead of extending period of agency in favour of respondent - Respondent sought for quashing of notification calling for fresh bids and to consider representation of respondent for extension of period of agency before HC - Single Judge allowed Petition - Division Bench took view that cl. 5 of agreement provided for extension of period of agency though not renewal, as conditions imposed in respect of such extension had been fulfilled, it was not a case involving grant of a fresh agency but extension of existing one and upheld order made by ld Single Judge - Appeal against finding of Division Bench? - In a case where agency is granted for collection of toll or taxes, as in present case, it can be easily discerned that claim of respondent for extension of period of agency would not come in way of Govt. if it is economically more beneficial to have a fresh agreement by enhancing consideration payable to Govt. - In such an event, it cannot be said that action of Govt. inviting fresh bids is arbitrary - Moreover, respondent can also participate in tender process and get his bid considered - Held, Court does not think that view taken by HC can be justified - Thus, order made by Division Bench is set aside and order of Single Judge in writ proceedings is affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
