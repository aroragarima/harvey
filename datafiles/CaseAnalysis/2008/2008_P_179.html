State of Punjab v Prem Sagar and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 997; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 183; (2008) 7 SCC 550; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 261; 2008 CRLJ 3533; 2008 (3) JCC 1588; JT 2008 (7) SC 66; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2113; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2008(9) SCALE 590; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mitigating Factor, Punjab Excise Act, Criminal Justice Act 2003, Crime and Disrder Act 1998, Sentencing Reform Act of 1984

Summary: Punjab Excise Act, s. 61(1) - High Court, taking into consideration the fact that the offence was committed in the year 1987 and the appeal was dismissed in the year 1992, directed respondents to be released on probation on their executing a bond of Rs. 20, 000/- with one surety each of the like amount to the satisfaction of the Trial Judge - Appeal against - Held, High Court does not rest its decision on any legal principle - No sufficient or cogent reason has been arrived - respondent is sentenced to undergo simple imprisonment for a period of six months and a fine of Rs. 5, 000/- is imposed, in default to undergo imprisonment for a further period of one month - Appeal partly allowed.
