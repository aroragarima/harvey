Madras Industrial Investment Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2824; (1997) 4 SCC 666; AIR 1997 SC 2063; 1997 (1) BC 639; 1997 (139) CTR 555; [1997] 224 ITR 802; JT 1997 (4) SC 463; 1997(3) SCALE 436; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 593; 1997 (4) Supreme 173; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 340; 1997 (2) TLR 581

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Deferred Interest

Summary: Income tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Business expenditure - Debentures issued at discount by company - Whether liability to pay discount is incurred by company for business? - Held that said expenditure would be revenue expenditure - It cannot however be allowed in its entirety in year in which it was incurred - Payment is for secure benefit over a number of years - Claim for deduction by company should therefore be spread over period of debentures - Answered in favour of Assessee.
