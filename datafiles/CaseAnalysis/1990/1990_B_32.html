Bhagwan Dass v Jiley Kaur Smt. and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 1017; (1991) Supp2 SCC 300; AIR 1991 SC 266; 1992 (1) RCJ 612

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Application For Release

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972, r. 16(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 57 - Application for release of premises - Writ petition - Procedure - Finding of fact - (A) Whether finding of District Judge that certain premises could not be used for purposes of business by sons of landlord a finding of fact - (B) Whether refusal of High Court to interfere with it justified - Appellate authority granted release on basis of existence of outweighing circumstances in favour of landlord - (C) Whether order can be challenged - (D) Whether alternative accommodation is available or not is a matter of which judicial notice can be taken - Held, (A) it is finding of fact - (B) Non interference, justified - (C) Order not open to challenge - (D) Not a matter - It is to be proved by evidence - Appeal partly allowed.
