Workmen Of The Food Corporation Of Indla v M/S. Food Corporation Of India

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 190; (1985) 2 SCC 136; (1985) SCC (L&S) 420; AIR 1985 SC 670; 1985 (50) FLR 442; 1985 LabIC 876; 1985 (2) LLJ 4; 1985 (2) LLN 20; 1985(1) SCALE 344; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 1065; 1985 (1) SLR 625; 1985 UJ 775

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Public Sector, Intermediary, Food Corporations Act, 1964, Piece Rate, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970, Direct payment system, Reintroduction of contract system, Discontinuation of employment

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 9A and 31(2) - Direct payment system - Reintroduction of contract system - Discontinuation of employment - Legality - Respondent Corporation adopted different methods at different places for employing labour for handling food grains - One depot had setup by respondent number of workmen designated as handling Mazdoor were employed S Depot - However, 464 workmen were attached to S depot and initially a contractor was engaged by respondent for handling storage and transit of food grains S Depot - By negotiations and settlements contract system was abolished and appellants workmen were directly paid wages, presumably at piece rate for service rendered by them by respondent - Method adopted was that bills for piece rate wages payable to handling Mazdoors should be prepared by Depot Staff, and Sardar/Mondal should accept payment and sign bills on their behalf and distribute wages to handling labour - System of payment was implemented and was in vogue, till 27-1-1975 - On account of some other industrial dispute, appellants attached to S Depot went on strike - Respondent changed the method of payment superseding direct payment system and reintroducing contractor system and that too without giving any notice of change as contemplated by s. 9A of the Act - Respondent discontinued employment of 464 workmen attached to S Depot and brought in intermediary contractor and treated appellant as workmen employed by contractor - Aggrieved, appellants filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal negatived the claim of appellants and made award to that effect - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in passing the impugned award - Held, s. 9A of the Act makes it obligatory upon an employer who proposes to effect any change in conditions of service applicable to any workman in respect of any matter specified to give a notice of desired or intended change - It could not do so without giving to workman likely to be affected by change, a notice in prescribed manner of nature of change proposed to be effected and within 21 days of giving such notice - After introducing direct payment system agreed to between parties, if respondent or employer wanted to introduce a change in respect of any of matters, it was obligatory to give a notice of change - Therefore, obviously a notice of change should give before introducing change, otherwise it should be an illegal change - Any such illegal change invites a penalty u/s. 31(2) of the Act - Such a change which was punishable as a criminal offence should obviously be an illegal change - It should be held that without anything more such an illegal change should be wholly ineffective - Award of Tribunal rejecting reference and denying benefit should be quashed and set aside and an award made that 464 workmen who had become workmen of respondent continued to be workmen employed by Corporation and should be entitled to all rights, liabilities, obligations and duties as prescribed for workmen by respondent - Appeal allowed.
