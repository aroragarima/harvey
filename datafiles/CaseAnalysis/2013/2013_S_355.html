Samaj Parivartana Samudaya and others v State of Karanataka and others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 257; (2013) 8 SCC 154; JT 2013 (6) SC 425; 2013(6) SCALE 90; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Karnataka (Prevention of Illegal Mining, Transportation and Storage of Minerals) Rules, 2011

Summary: (A) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Illegal mining operations - Reports of CEC - Credibility - Petitioner filed instant writ petitions seeking to direct respondent/States to stop all mining and other related activities in forest areas of State and initiate penal action against violators - Whether report of Central Empowered Committee (CEC) was credible - Held, CEC had continued to function under orders of Court submitting its reports from time to time in various environmental issues pending before SC - It was on consideration of such Reports that Court was passing its orders from time to time - Hence, questions concerning credibility of CEC was absolutely unfounded, particularly in absence of any materials to substantiate apprehensions, if not allegations, that was leveled - However, said body was performing such tasks as was assigned by SC by its orders passed from time to time - Directions on basis of which CEC had proceeded and had submitted its Reports were within framework of terms of reference of CEC as determined by SC - Therefore, acceptance of recommendations made by CEC on basis of which orders of Court were formulated was on satisfaction of Court - Petitions disposed of.

(B) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Illegal mining - Recommendations by CEC - Jurisdiction - Whether exercise of jurisdiction u/art. 32 of Constitution on basis of facts revealed by Reports of CEC i.e. large scale damage to forest wealth of country due to illegal mining on an unprecedented scale vis-Ã -vis resort to remedies under the provisions of the 1957 Act, the 1980 Act, and the 1986 Act - Held, power u/arts. 32 and 142 of Constitution ought not to be exercised in instant case and instead remedies should be sought within relevant statutes - Even mechanism provided by any of Statutes in question would neither be effective nor efficacious to deal with extraordinary situation that had arisen on account of large scale illegalities committed in operation of mines in question resulting in grave and irreparable loss to the forest wealth of country besides colossal loss caused to national exchequer - However, situation being extraordinary remedy indeed should also be extraordinary - SC did not saw how any of recommendations of CEC, if accepted, would come into conflict with any law enacted by legislature - It was only in above situation that Court might consider necessity of placing recommendations made by CEC on a finer balancing scale before accepting same - Therefore, feel uninhibited to proceed to exercise our Constitutional jurisdiction to remedy enormous wrong that had happened and to provide adequate protection for future, as might be required - Petitions disposed of.

(C) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Lease fixed by Joint team - Sanctity - Whether Sanctity of process of survey undertaken by Joint Team constituted by SC's order and determination of boundaries of leases on basis of said survey was valid - Held, SC accept findings of survey conducted by Joint Team constituted by orders of SC and boundaries of each of leases determined on that basis - SC directed that in supersession of all orders either of authorities of State or Courts, as might be, boundaries of leases fixed by Joint Team would be boundaries of each of leases who would have benefit of lease area as determined by Joint Team - All proceedings pending in any Court with regard to boundaries of leases involved in present proceeding should stand adjudicated by means of present order and no such question would be open for re-examination by any body or authority - Petitions disposed of.

(D) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Recommendations by CEC - Acceptability - Whether acceptability of recommendations of CEC was valid - Held, Once result of survey undertaken and boundaries of leases determined by Joint Team was accepted by Court and basis of categorization of mines was found to be rational and Constitutionally permissible it would be difficult for SC to visualize as to how Category 'C' mines could be allowed to reopen - There was no room for compassion, fervent pleas for clemency could not have even a persuasive value - As against individual interest of Category 'C' leaseholders, public interest at large would require Court to lean in favour of demonstrating efficacy and effectiveness of long arm of law - Therefore, order for complete closure of Category 'C' mines and for necessary follow up action in terms of recommendations of CEC in that regard - Petitions disposed of.

(E) Mines & Minerals - Administrative - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Mining licenses - 'C' to 'B' Category - Upgradation - Validity - Whether impugned order passed by SC an embargo was placed on grant of fresh mining licenses was valid - Held, operation of 7 leases placed in 'B' category situated on or nearby inter-State boundary would remain suspended until finalisation of inter-State boundary dispute whereupon question of commencement of operations in respect of 7 leases would be examined afresh by CEC - Having considered facts on which two lessees had sought upgradation from 'C' to 'B' Category, such upgradation could not be allowed - Both lessees, in fact, accept results of survey by Joint Team which findings have already been accepted by SC - Petitions disposed of.
