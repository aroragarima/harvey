Vimal Kanwar and others v Kishore Dan and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 289; (2013) 7 SCC 476; 2013 (2) ACC 752; 2013 ACJ 1441; AIR 2013 SC 3830; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 156; 2013 (99) ALR 462; 2013 (4) AWC 3282; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 905; [2013] 354 ITR 95; JT 2013 (8) SC 234; 2013 (2) KLT 748; 2014(2) M.P.H.T. 459; 2013 (171) PLR 776; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 945; 2013 (3) RLW 2153; 2013(6) SCALE 705; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 223; [2013] 216 TAXMAN 300

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: (A)Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Enhancement - Entitled to - Appellants claimants (wife, daughter and mother of deceased ) filed application before Tribunal alleging that negligent and rash driving by non-applicant No. 1/jeep driver caused the death of deceased - Appellants claimed compensation of Rs. 80,40,160/- - Tribunal awarded total amount of compensation Rs. 14,93,700/- - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal before HC for enhancement of compensation, which was upheld the order of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Provident Fund, Pension and Insurance receivable by claimants come within periphery of the 1988 Act to be termed as 'Pecuniary Advantage' liable for deduction - Held, in Helen C. Rebello (Mrs) and others vs. Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation & Anr, 1998 Indlaw SC 1215, SC held that Provident Fund, Pension, Insurance and similarly any cash, bank balance, shares, fixed deposits, etc. were all a 'pecuniary advantage' receivable by heirs on account of one's death but all that had no correlation with amount receivable under a statute occasioned only on account of accidental death - Such amount would not come within periphery of the 1988 Act to be termed as 'pecuniary advantage' liable for deduction - Appeal allowed.

(B) Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Compensation - Pecuniary Advantage - Deduction - Legality - Whether salary receivable by claimant on compassionate appointment comes within periphery of the 1988 Act to be termed as 'Pecuniary Advantage' liable for deduction - Held, 'Compassionate appointment" could be one of the conditions of service of an employee, if a scheme to that effect was framed by employer - In case, employee dies in harness i.e. while in service leaving behind dependents, one of dependents might request for compassionate appointment to maintain family of deceased employee dies in harness - Compassionate appointment might have nexus with death of an employee while in service but it was not necessary that it should had a correlation with accidental death - However, an employee dies in harness even in normal course, due to illness and to maintain family of deceased one of dependents might be entitled for compassionate appointment but that could not be termed as 'Pecuniary Advantage' that comes under periphery of the 1988 Act and any amount received on such appointment was not liable for deduction for determination of compensation under the 1988 Act - Appeal allowed.

(C) Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 192(1) - Liability of income tax - Deductions in compensation - Legality - Whether the income tax was liable to be deducted for determination of compensation under the 1988 Act - Held, in case the income of victim was only from 'salary', presumption should be that employer u/s. 192 (1) of the 1961 Act had deducted tax at source from employee's salary - In instant case, none of respondents brought to notice of Court that income-tax payable by deceased was not deducted at source by employer/State Govt. - Tribunal or HC on perusal of Last Pay Certificate, had not noticed that income-tax on estimated income of employee was not deducted from salary of employee during said month or Financial Year - In absence of such evidence, it was presumed that salary paid to deceased as per Last Pay Certificate was paid in accordance with law i.e. by deducting income-tax on estimated income of deceased for that month or Financial Year - Appellants stated that Assessment Year applicable in instant case was 1997-98 and not 1996-97 as held by HC - HC was wrong in deducting 20% from salary of deceased towards income-tax, for calculating compensation - As per law, the salary as shown in Last Pay Certificate at Rs.8,920/- should be accepted which if rounded off comes to Rs.9,000/- for calculating compensation payable to dependent(s) - Appeal allowed.

(D) Carriers & Transportation - Motor accident claim - Quantum of compensation - Justifiability - Whether the compensation awarded to appellants was just and proper - Held, date of birth of deceased being 1-2-1968, submission that he should have continued in service up to 1-2-2026 - Deceased was only 28 years 7 Â½ month old at time of death - In normal course, deceased should have served State Govt. minimum for about 30 years - It could not be denied that pay of deceased should have doubled if he should continue in services of State till date of retirement - Hence, it was a fit case in which 100% increase in future income of deceased should have allowed by Tribunal and HC, which they failed to do - Normal compensation should then work out to be Rs.1,44,000/- x 17 = Rs.24,48,000/- to which added the usual award for loss of consortium and loss of estate by providing a conventional sum of Rs. 1,00,000/-, loss of love and affection for daughter Rs.2,00,000/-, loss of love and affection for widow and mother at Rs.1,00,000/- each i.e. Rs.2,00,000/- and funeral expenses of Rs.25,000/- - In all sum of Rs.29,73,000/- should be a fair, just and reasonable award in circumstances of instant case - Respondent No.3 was directed to pay total award with interest - Impugned award passed by Tribunal and HC was modified - Appeal allowed.
