Sorabkhan Gandhkhan Pathan and another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1676; (2004) 13 SCC 608

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Possession of charas

Summary: Criminal; Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 20(2), 21, 27, 29, 42, 50, 8(c) - Possession of charas - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused persons/appellant were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 8(c), 20(2), 21, 27, 29 of the Act - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, Courts below found no merit in case and Court agreed with same because information received by police officer was not in regard to any contraband article as contemplated under the Act - Information was in regard to certain illicit liquor, therefore, search by officer concerned was not under provisions of the Act - In course of search for illicit liquor officer concerned found 1960 grams of charas, therefore, it could be treated as chance recovery for which it was not necessary to follow procedure contemplated u/ss. 42, 50 of the Act - Contraband in question was seized from appellants possession and, prosecution had established case against said accused and Courts below rightly convicted appellant - Regard to appellant no. 2, it was prosecution case itself that he was travelling in autorickshaw, along with three other persons - Prosecution had not produced any material whatsoever to establish that either this appellant had knowledge that appellant no. 1 was carrying contraband or was in any manner, conniving with said appellant in carrying contraband - Courts below had rightly acquitted other two accused on similar ground and said benefit ought to have gone to appellant no. 2 also - Prosecution had failed to establish its case against appellant no. 2 - Appellant no. 2 if in custody, should be released - Appeal of appellant no. 1 was dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
