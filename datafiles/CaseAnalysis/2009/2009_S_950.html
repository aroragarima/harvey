Godrej and Boyce Manufacturing Company Limited and another v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2172; (2009) 5 SCC 24; 2009 (57) BLJR 755; 2009 (3) Bom.C.R. 187; JT 2009 (2) SC 283; 2009 (6) MahLJ 354; 2009(2) SCALE 286; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 1016; 2009 (2) UJ 499

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, Development Control Regulations for Greater Bombay, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, s. 126(1)(b) - Development Control Regulations for Greater Bombay, 1991 - Maharashtra town planning law was evolved, with a view to promote planned development and de-congest the highly congested areas - It was open to the landowner to surrender the plot of land free of cost (and free from all encumbrances) to the municipal authorities who may acquire the land by granting to the landowner Floor Space Index or Transferable Development Rights against the area of the surrendered land & Property - Appellants voluntarily surrendered their lands - In addition, appellants constructed on their respective pieces of land the Development Plan roads at their own cost and as per the specifications stipulated in the relevant rules - Dispute arose between parties regarding extent of Floor Space Index or Transferable Development Rights for the roads constructed on the surrendered lands at the owners' cost - Landowners claim that for constructing the roads they were entitled to Floor Space Index or Transferable Development Rights for the whole of the surface area of the roads - Municipal authorities, however, granted them additional Transferable Development Rights only to the extent of 15% of the road area on the basis of circular dated April 9, 1996 issued by the Municipal Commissioner - Circular envisages a graded scheme for grant of Additional Development Rights for construction of amenities by the landowner - Held, conditions, that is to say, the mutual rights and obligations subject to which the land owner may offer to surrender the designated plot of land to municipal authority and the latter may accept the offer are enumerated in detail in the statutory provisions; beyond those conditions there can be no negotiations for surrender of the land, particularly in derogation to the land owner's statutory rights - The surrender of the land in terms of s. 126(1)(b) of the Act cannot be subjected to any further conditions than those already provided for in the statutory provisions - Though, it is open to the legislature to add to the conditions provided for in the statute but it cannot be left in the hands of the executive to impose conditions in addition to those in the statutes for accepting the offer to surrender the designated land - Further, s. 126(1)(b) of the Act uses the word 'against': it speaks of granting FSI or TDR 'against the area of land surrendered' and further additional FSI or TDR 'against the development or construction of amenities on the surrendered land' - Hence, both s. 126(1)(b) of the Act and paragraph 6 of Appendix VII to the Regulations provide for additional FSI or TDR for construction or development of amenity - Hence, stand of municipal authorities that law provides for grant of additional Floor Space Index or Transferable Development Rights commensurate to the value of the amenity constructed by the landowner was contrary to the laws - Appeals allowed.
