Abdul Rahman v Prasony Bai and another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1440; (2003) 1 SCC 488; AIR 2003 SC 718; 2003 (1) AllCJ 696; 2003 (2) AWC 1678; 2003 (2) LCD 934; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 236; 2002(8) SCALE 479; 2003 (1) SCW 14; 2002 (7) SLT 83; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 260; 2002 (8) Supreme 253

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC - s.24 - Board of Revenue - Escheat proceedings - Authorities under Revenue department of State - Matter in issure travelled from 'Tehsildar' to 'Additional Collector' and than in appeal to 'Board of Revenue' and than in writ to High Court - What is the jurisdiction of HC to withdraw the suit and dispose of civil revision application u/s.24 of CPC - HC has the requisite jurisdiction to withdraw any suit pending in any court subordinate to it and try or dispose of the same inter alia on its own motion, wherefor even no notice is required to be issued - Application of principles of res judicata - Once it is held that the revenue authorities had the requisite jurisdiction to determine the said question subject, of course, to adjudication of the legality of validity thereof in an appropriate civil suit, the issues which could and ought to have been raised in the said proceedings but not raised would be barred by the principles of constructive res judicata, particularly when the validity or legality of the said proceedings had not been questioned in the civil suit, as a result whereof, the same attained finality - Further, the appellant must be held to have taken recourse to abuse of process of court underlying the principle that the litigation should be allowed to attain finality in public interest Although the concept of issues estoppel or estoppel by records are distinct and separate from the concept of abuse of process in public interest, the court may refuse the plaintiff from pursuing his remedy in a court of law - Further, having regard to the fact that the appellant himself was the tenant of the 1st Respondent, he could not have raised the plea of adverse possession - Appeal dismissed.
