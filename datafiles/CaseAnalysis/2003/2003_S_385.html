Kishorebhai Khamanchand Goyal v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 920; (2003) 12 SCC 274; AIR 2004 SC 1006; 2004 (1) CLR 516; 2003 (99) FLR 1167; 2004 (4) G.L.R. 3167; JT 2003 (8) SC 399; 2004 (1) LLJ 428; 2004 (1) LLN 97; 2004 (3) MahLJ 568; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 829; 2003(9) SCALE 119; 2003 (1) SCW 6638; 2003 (6) SLT 574; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 1; 2003 (7) Supreme 559

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Motor Transport Workers Act, 1961 - Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948 - Whether the obligation to comply with the requirement of the Bombay Shops and Establishments Act, 1948 gets wiped out due to enactment in Motor Transport Workers Act, 1961 - Held, the operation of the law as contained in the Establishments Act would stand curtailed, if on any particular aspect, there is a contra stipulation or provision in the Act in respect of a motor transport worker, and not otherwise. This would be the proper, reasonable and inevitable consequence of a harmonious construction of the provisions of the two welfare legislations, so that the best of the both would be available to the worker concerned.
