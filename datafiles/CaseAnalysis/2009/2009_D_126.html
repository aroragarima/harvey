State Of West Bengal v Dipak Halder & Anr

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 744; (2009) 7 SCC 288; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 392; AIR 2009 SC 1883; 2009 (3) CalLT(SC) 76; JT 2009 (7) SC 444; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 477; 2009(7) SCALE 596; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Witness, Subsequent Conduct, Ill Treatment, Mother - In-Law

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 109 and 498A r/w 34 - Conviction by Trial Court - Acquittal by High Court - Acquittal whether proper? - Held, No - Accused person three in numbers, respondent no.1 was husband and the other accused persons were brother and mother of R1 - All accused persons were charged u/s. 498A r/w 34, R1 was charged u/s. 302 and co-accused were charged u/s.302 r/w 109 - R1 was convicted u/s. 302 and all accused persons were convicted u/s. 498A but co-accused were acquitted u/s. 302 r/w 109, by the TC - Convictions awarded was reversed by HC - Judgment of TC examined - Demand of dowry at the time of marriage was not met but articles were handed over; ill treatment by all the accused; physical and mental torture; deceased was burned and half an hour before the incident there was a quarrel between the deceased and R1/husband - Nothing on record that R1 had tried to put off the fire and made any inquiry in the hospital as to condition of his burnt wife; he was in a position to make appropriate arrangement of treatment of his wife but he did not do so; no reason assigned as to why he did not accompany his deceased wife in the hospital alongwith neighbours - Doors and windows were closed during the fire and TC posits that 'does it not indicate that the victim was not allowed to go out of the house during the incident which was also accompanied by physical assault; reason was that no body could witness and that the victim could not go out to avoid assault - Severity of burning was noted and deduction was made that unless a combustible substance like kerosene is given to the body at the time of fire, such severity could not have been caused - Smell of kerosene oil on burnt saree and blouse; no sign of busting or any other damage to the kerosene stove - Deceased was in good mood on the morning of incident and had took breakfast, thus no possibility of setting fire to herself - Neighbours were trying to put out the fire and accused was then calling names to burnt Rimu and her parents - Court noted that case was based on circumstantial evidence and circumstances indicated by Trial Court points to only one conclusion, i.e. guilt of the accused/R1 - Impugned judgement of HC, set aside and of Trial Court restored - Appeal allowed.
