Ram Swaroop v State (Govt. Nct) Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 338; (2013) 14 SCC 235; AIR 2013 SC 2068; 2013 CRLJ 2997; 2013 (201) DLT 174; 2013 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 129; JT 2013 (8) SC 579; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 946; 2013 (3) RLW 1870; 2013(7) SCALE 407; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 791

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contraband, Possession of poppy straw powder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 50 - Contraband - Possession of poppy straw powder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly found with sitting on 2 bags contained 64 Kgs of poppy straw powder packed in 32 bags of polythene - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 15 of the Act and sentenced him to undergo RI for 10 years - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was no compliance of s. 50 of the Act and that no independent witness was examined - Whether impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Held, evidence of PW7 (Sub-inspector) was supported by PW5 (constable), and other witnesses - It has come in evidence of PW7 that he had asked the passerby to be witnesses but none of them agreed and left without disclosing their names and addresses - From the version of PW5 and 7 there was nothing noticed by which their evidence could be treated to be untrustworthy - On the contrary it was absolutely unimpeachable - There was no absolute rule that Police officers could not be cited as witnesses and their depositions should be treated with suspect - In instant case, there was no reason to hold that non-examination of independent witnesses affect the prosecution case - It was established by adducing cogent and reliable evidence that bags belonged to appellant - Compliance of provisions of s. 50 of the Act was not mandatory - State of U.P. v. Anil Singh, 1988 Indlaw SC 28, referred to - Appeal dismissed.
