Western Electricity Supply Company of Orissa Limited v Baba Baijanath Roller and Flour Mill Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 2014 SC 1605; 2014(3) ALL MR 975; 2014 (105) ALR 763; 2014 (3) AWC 2353; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 135; JT 2014 (4) SC 597; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2489; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 841; 2014(4) SCALE 141; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tampering of electricity meter, Penal Charges, Quashed

Summary: Electricity Act, 1910-Section 26-Orissa Electricity Regulatory Commission Distribution (Conditions of Supply) Code, 1998 (Code)-Regulation 64-Theft of electricity-Penal bill raised on respondent on ground of unauthorised consumption by way of tampering meter or meter-ing equipment-Applicability of Section 26 of Electricity Act-If there is question of fraud in tampering with meters, in that case there is no ques-tion of applicability of Section 26-Matter not referred to Electrical Inspector on ground of defective me-ter-Ingredients of Section 26 (6) of Electricity Act not followed by re-spondent to meet necessity of check-ing meter in question in accordance with said provision-High Court wrong in bringing matter within scope of provision of Section 26 (6) of Electricity Act-High Court miscon-strued facts and provisions of law in dealing with matter-Provision of law which deals with tampering of meter-ing equipments, i.e., Clauses 56, 64 and 105 of Code not considered by High Court-High Court accepted po-sition submitted on behalf of respon-dent that it was case of defective me-ter and there is no question of any tampering with meter in question-High Court erred in quashing penal charge and directing refund of penal charges-Impugned order of High Court without any reason-Cannot be sustained in eyes of law-Hence, same set aside.
