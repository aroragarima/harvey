Agnani (W. M.) v Badri Das and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 46; 1963 (6) FLR 440; 1963 (1) LLJ 684

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Misconduct - Legality - Whether High Court was justified in entertaining plea of respondents that tribunal had committed an error of law apparent on face of record? - On record, it appeared that respondents had formally affirmed charges before sending it to enquiry committee - In absence of standing orders, question of misconduct was not proved convincingly - Held, keeping in view nature of quarrel, time and place and its essential features, it is difficult to accept HC's conclusion that incident amounted to misconduct - No action was taken against appellant in time - Order of HC is set aside - Writ petition filed by respondents is dismissed - Appeal allowed.
