Raj Kumar v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 353; (1975) SCC (L&S) 1; (1975) 4 SCC 13; AIR 1975 SC 1116; 1975 (30) FLR 370; 1975 LabIC 120; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 963; 1975 SLJ 615; 1975 (1) SLR 774; 1975 UJ 343

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 309 - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1965, r. 5 (1) - Termination - Validity - Termination of service on basis of amended rules - (A) Whether appellant entitled sum referred in rule? - R. 5 (1) is a valid rule because it is well established that rules made under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution are legislative in character and therefore can be given to retrospectively - Held, Govt. servant concerned is only entitled to claim sums referred in rule - (B) Whether termination offends art. 14? - Held, termination of appellant's services was not on ground of retrechment - No offending of art. 14 - Held, no allegation of those persons exercised their influence and had petitioner's services terminated in order to provide them with posts - Not malafide - Appeal dismissed.
