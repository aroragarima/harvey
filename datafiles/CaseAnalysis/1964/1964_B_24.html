Â Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P v Bijli Cotton Mills, Hathras

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 408; AIR 1964 SC 1594; 1964 (2) SCJ 509; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 383; 1964 (15) STC 656

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Law Applicable

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - U P Sales Tax Act, 1948, s. 31 - (A) Whether High Court has power to consider laws which after amendment has retrospective effect? - Held, when question has been referred to HC and in meanwhile law has been amended with retroactive operation, it would be duty of HC to apply law so amended if it applies - By taking notice of law which has been substituted for original provision, HC is giving effect to legislative intent - Question should cover an enquiry into question in light of amended law and enquiry does not necessitate investigation of fresh facts - If question is not so couched as to invite HC to decide question in light of law as amended or if it necessitates investigation of facts which have not been investigated, HC may refuse to answer question - Appeal allowed.
