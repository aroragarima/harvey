Sanjeev Lal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Chandigarh and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 431; (2015) 5 SCC 775; AIR 2014 SC 3589; [2014] 365 ITR 389; JT 2014 (8) SC 22; 2014 (5) MLJ 758; 2014 (175) PLR 769; 2014(8) SCALE 432; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 795; [2014] 225 TAXMAN 239

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Liability, Income tax on capital gain

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(47), 260A, 45, 54, 54(1) - Income tax on capital gain - Liability - Appellants entered into an agreement to sell house on 27-12-2002 for consideration of Rs. 1.32 crores, out of said amount, sum of Rs. 15 lakhs was received by appellants - Appellants decided to purchase another residential house so that sale proceeds, including capital gain, could be used for purchase of another house - Said house was purchased on 30-04-2003 - 'R' filed suit before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - Appellants did not disclose said long term capital gain in their return of income filed for Assessment Year 2005-2006 - In assessment proceedings, Assessing Officer was viewed that appellants were liable to pay income tax on capital gain u/s. 45 of the Act - Appellants filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether agreement to sell which had been executed on 27-12-2002 could be considered as a date on which property i.e. residential house had been transferred -

Held, consequences of execution of agreement to sell were also very clear and they were to effect that appellants could not have sold property to someone else - In practical life, there were events when person, even after executing an agreement to sell an immoveable property in favor of one person, tries to sell property to another - Such an act would not be in accordance with law because once an agreement to sell was executed in favor of one person, said person gets right to get property transferred in his favor by filing suit for specific performance - Some right, in respect of said property, belonging to appellants had been extinguished and some right was created in favor of vendee/transferee, when agreement to sell had been executed - A right in respect of capital asset, viz. the property in question had been transferred by appellants in favor of vendee/transferee on 27-12-2002 - Sale deed could not be executed for reason that appellants had been prevented from dealing with residential house by an order of competent Court, which they could not have violated - Appellants were entitled to relief u/s. 54 of the Act in respect of long term capital gain which they had earned in pursuance of transfer of their residential property and used for purchase of a new asset/residential house - Impugned judgments were quashed and set aside and Authorities were directed to re-assess income of appellants for Assessment Year 2005-2006, after taking into account fact that appellants were entitled to relief, subject to fulfilment of conditions - Appeals allowed.
