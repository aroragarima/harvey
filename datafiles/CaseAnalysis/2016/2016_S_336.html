HDFC Securities Limited and others v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 882; (2017) 1 SCC 640; AIR 2017 SC 61; 2017 (1) BC 212; 2017 CRLJ 1033; JT 2017 (2) SC 190; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 207; 2016(12) SCALE 657; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Prosecution, Vicarious Liability, FIR, Jurisdiction, Stock, Contract, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Managing Director, General Clauses Act, 1897, Abuse, Cognizance, Cognizable, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156, 156(3), 200, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 409, 420, 465, 467 - Criminal complaint - Cognizance - Validity - Respondent no. 2 filed a criminal complaint against appellants/company u/s. 156(3) CrPC before Magistrate alleging execution of unauthorized trades in her account without her consent - Magistrate on 4-1-2011 directed registration of FIR against appellants and ordered for a report after investigation - FIR was registered against appellants u/ss. 409, 420, 465, 467 r/w ss. 34 and 120-B IPC - Appellants filed a writ petition before HC praying for quashing of FIR - HC dismissed writ petition holding that filing of writ petitions was premature and there was no need for exercising the powers either u/art. 227 of Constitution or u/s. 482 CrPC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in instant case a fact finding investigation was directed by impugned order. Accused under Indian Criminal Legal System, unless proved guilty should always be given a reasonable space and liberty to defend himself in accordance with the law. Further, Courts, taking cognizance of offence or conducting a trial while issuing any order were expected to apply their mind and the order should be a well-reasoned one. Appellants approached HC even before the stage of issuance of process. In particular, appellants challenged the order dt. 04-01-2011 passed by Magistrate u/s. 156(3) CrPC. However, it appears to SC that such order u/s. 156(3) CrPC requiring investigation by police cannot be said to have caused an injury of irreparable nature which at that stage requires quashing of investigation. Further, stage of cognizance would arise only after the investigation report was filed before Magistrate. Therefore, at that stage HC has correctly assessed the facts and the law in such situation and held that filing of the petitions u/art. 227 of Constitution or u/s. 482 CrPC, at that stage were nothing but premature. HC correctly came to the conclusion that the inherent powers of Court u/s. 482 CrPC should be sparingly used. In those circumstances, SC do not find that there was any flaw in impugned order or any illegality was committed by HC in dismissing the petitions filed by appellants. SC affirm the order so passed by HC. Appeal dismissed.
