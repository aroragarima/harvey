Sobha Hymavathi Devi v Setti Gangadhara Swamy and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 54; (2005) 2 SCC 244; AIR 2005 SC 800; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 53; 2005 (58) ALR 630; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 236; JT 2005 (1) SC 496; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 799; 2005(1) SCALE 626; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute

Summary: [A.] Election - Representation Of The People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 116A, 81 - Andhra Pradesh (Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribe and Backward Classes) Regulation of Issue of Community Certificate Act, 1993 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 15(4), 16(4), 332 - Whether appellant belongs to a Scheduled Tribe and hence qualified to contest election from a constituency reserved for a Scheduled Tribe? - Held, there was nothing to show that marriage of appellant with a person belonging to Bhagatha Community took place in customary mode followed by Bhagatha Community - Reservation would benefit only those who belong to a Scheduled Caste or Scheduled Tribe and not those who claim to acquire status by marriage - High Court right in setting aside election of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
