State of Madras v Cement Allocation Co-Ordinating Organisation

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 105; (1971) 2 SCC 587; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 548; 1972 (29) STC 114; 1972 UJ 51

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Packing Charges, Packing Materials

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act 1959,ss. 2(g) and 3 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules, r. 6 - Whether assessee is entitled to claim exemption in respect of packing charges if his principal could have claimed it had it sold cement itself? - Agents are considered as dealers under tax so as to effectively enforce provisions of Act, but that provision does not convert an agent into a principal for all purposes under Act - Excepting to extent otherwise provided in Madras General Sales Tax Act, the agent must be, held to represent his principal while dealing with goods of his principal, he merely steps into shoes of his principal - He is entitled to same exemptions as his principal would have got, had he dealt with concerned goods himself - Held, assessee was entitled to exemption - Appeal dismissed.
