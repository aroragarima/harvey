Saraswat Co-Operative Bank Limited and Another v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 422; (2006) 8 SCC 520; JT 2006 (7) SC 445; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 290; 2006(8) SCALE 53; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 258; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 567

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949, Central Provinces and Berar Regulation of Letting of Accommodation Act, 1946, Delhi Rent Control (Amendment ) Act, 1988

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999, ss. 3, 3(1)(b) - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Central Provinces and Berar Regulation of Letting of Accommodation Act, 1946 - Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 32, 39(b) - Constitutionality of3 (1) (b) - Held, it is quite clear that it is within legislative competence of State to enact laws for protection of certain sections of society on basis of economic criteria and so long as it does not result in unreasonable classification, it is for Legislature to decide whom it should include or exclude from application of such laws - No fault can be found with findings arrived at by HC - Provisions of s 3(1)(b) are intra vires and as has been held by Bombay HC, they do not violate art.14 of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
