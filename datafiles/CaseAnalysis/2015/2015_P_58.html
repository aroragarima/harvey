Pratap Kishore Panda v Agni Charan Das

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 721; 2016(2) ALL MR 461; 2016 (1) AWC 187; JT 2015 (11) SC 95; 2015 (7) MLJ 743; 2015(11) SCALE 609

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Locus Standi, Employment Exchange, Examination, Selection Committee, Public Service Commission, Ad Hoc, Quota, Inter Se Seniority, Relaxation, Gradation List, Reservation, Orissa Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services (For S.C. & S.T.) Act, 1975, Orissa Ministerial Service (Method of Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Lower Division Assistant in the Office of Department of Secretariat) Rules, 1951

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 320(4) - Orissa Reservation of Vacancies in Posts and Services (For S.C. & S.T.) Act, 1975, s. 9(4) - Orissa Ministerial Service (Method of Recruitment and Conditions of Service of Lower Division Assistant in Office of Department of Secretariat) Rules, 1951, r. 3 - Examination for recruitment - Alternate method to fill reserved seats - Justification - Orissa Public Service Commission (OPSC) issued advertisement for Competitive Examination for recruitment of candidates for which certain seats were reserved for Schedule Caste and Schedule Tribes, however, there were inadequate person who were recruited under reserved category, hence State Govt. decided to fill these remaining seats on ad hoc basis - Respondent was appointed on such ad hoc basis - Further, ad hoc reserved candidates including respondent were deemed as regular appointments - Further, respondents were declared senior to appellant who were appointed who are OPSC appointed general category candidates - Tribunal declared fixation of inter se seniority and promotions of regularized candidates over OPSC appointed recruits illegal and contrary to law - Further, services cannot be regularized by Resolution, and accordingly recruitment made by State Govt. contrary to Rules cannot be upheld - Further, HC set aside order passed by Tribunal and resolutions passed by Govt. was upheld - However, HC partly modified resolution, directing that candidates selected by Selection Committee and subsequently regularized should be kept below candidates selected by OPSC under reserved category quota, but should be placed in Seniority List according to then roster in accordance with Act and Rules framed thereunder - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, subject challenged recruitment was neither capricious nor arbitrary but on contrary was carried out in consonance with known method of selection, viz. r. 9(4) of Act. Further, incumbents were sponsored by Employment Exchange and over 400 candidates were found suitable by duly constituted Selection Committee which interviewed them. It was not relaxation of Rules in order to favour few, but was consequence of following alternate method of selection intended to remedy malady in recruitment of SC/ST candidates. It is well within powers of State to organize alternative recruitment drive when insufficient SC/ST candidates are available, and u/art. 320(4) of Constitution and OPSC was not required to be consulted. Further, at time of appointment of respondents, prevailing law regarding appointment of SC/ST candidates to surplus vacancies was contained in s. 9(4) of Act and it does not contain or prescribe any limitation regarding method of fresh recruitment except that it be restricted to SC/ST candidates. Further, fresh recruitment would not have been possible by means of competitive examination as r. 3 of Rules required that these be conducted once year, and examination for 1974-1975 had already been conducted, yielding meager number of 20 SC/ST candidates. Hence, method of appointment of respondents to be valid in eyes of law; their regularization with effect from date of appointment cannot be faulted. Hence, SC held that respondents were appointed in legal and legitimate manner. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is confirmed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - It is within powers of State to organize alternative recruitment drive when insufficient reserved candidates are available.
