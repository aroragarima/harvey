Sayed Darain Ahsan @ Darain v State Of West Bengal & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 95; (2012) 4 SCC 352; AIR 2012 SC 1286; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1767; 2012 CRLJ 1980; JT 2012 (3) SC 428; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 91; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 54; 2012(3) SCALE 582; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 546

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B and 302 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(1B) (a), 27 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed an offence punishable u/s. 120B r/w s. 302 of IPC and ss. 25(1B) (a)/27 of Arms Act - Trial Court found guilty of appellant for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced him to life imprisonment along with fine of Rs.5,000/- - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal affirming conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that ocular evidence of eyewitness ought not to have been believed because it was inconsistent with medical evidence - Further contended that no Test Identification Parade was held at the time of investigation and it was not possible for witnesses to identify the appellant as one of the persons who fired at deceased - Whether HC had rightly sustained the conviction of appellant on the evidence of four eyewitnesses - Held, there was no doubt that the deceased was not shot by a rifle from a long distance but by improvised or country-made handguns capable of firing from a short distance - Appellant and 4 eyewitnesses belonged to the same locality and 4 eyewitnesses knew the appellant before the incident and were able to immediately identify the appellant at the time of the incident - It was only if the appellant was a stranger to the eyewitnesses that Test Identification Parade would have been necessary at the time of investigation - HC rightly sustained the conviction of appellant on the evidence of 4 eyewitnesses as corroborated by medical evidence - Appeal dismissed.
