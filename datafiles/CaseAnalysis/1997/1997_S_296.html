Madan Lal v State Of Jammu And Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1460; (1997) 7 SCC 677; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1151; AIR 1998 SC 386; AIR 1997 SCW 4371; 1997 (3) CCR 90; 1997 (3) Crimes 218; 1998 CRLJ 667; JT 1997 (7) SC 357; 1997(5) SCALE 461; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 337; 1997 (7) Supreme 556

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.376, 511 and 354 - Rape - Attempt of - School headmaster and rustic girl in his school - Sending her to his home for cooking - Evidence showing absence of both in school during the particular time - Accused unclothing himself and prosecutrix and rubbed forcibly on her private parts - Ejaculation but no penetration - Plea of preparation - Held, gone beyond the stage of preparation - Neither amounts to attempt nor assault - Conviction for rape - Not interfered.
