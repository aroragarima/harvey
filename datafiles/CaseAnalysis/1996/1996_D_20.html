Union of India and Others v D.R.R. Sastri

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1951; (1997) 1 SCC 514; (1997) SCC (L&S) 555; 1997 (75) FLR 76; JT 1996 (10) SC 637; 1997 (2) LLJ 678; 1996(8) SCALE 574; 1996 (3) SLR 795; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Pension Scheme - Whether respondent was entitled to opt for Liberalised Pension Scheme introduced by Railway Board? - Held, respondent had served for about 22 years and he should not be deprived of pensionary benefit when Govt. itself had come forward with Liberalised Pension Scheme and gave option to persons already retired to come over to pension scheme - Pension is to be calculated as on 31-7-1972 in accordance with Railway Board's letter D/- 23-07-1974 and in compliance with all necessary formalities by respondent in accordance with said circular - Appeal dismissed.
