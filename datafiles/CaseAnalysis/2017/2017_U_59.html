Rishabh Choudhary and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 45; (2017) 3 SCC 652; AIR 2017 SC 609; 2017 (122) ALR 11; 2017 (1) AWC 1039; 2017(1) SCALE 632; 2017 (1) UPLBEC 363

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical Council Of India, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 1997 - Admission to MBBS Course - Rule of law - Non-compliance - College conducted CGMAT-2016 examination under State monitoring - Petitioner qualified in examination, therefore, was granted admission for MBBS course by College - Medical Council of India (MCI) published notification amending Regulations prescribing time schedule for completion of admission process - CGMAT-2016 examination conducted by College and consequent admission granted to petitioner were in defiance of that Notification as well as schedule approved by SC in Ashish Ranjan v. UOI - Hence, instant petition - Whether, admission of petitioner ought not to be disturbed. Held, in Sankalp Charitable Trust v. UOI, 2016 Indlaw SCO 167, it was made clear that no examination shall be permitted to be held for admission for MBBS studies by any private college or association or any private/deemed University. Subsequently SC declined to modify that order. Order was also passed making it clear that all such candidates who could not appear in NEET-I and those who had appeared but had apprehension that they had not prepared well, would be permitted to appear in NEET-II subject to option from these candidates to give up their candidature for NEET-1. It was further clarified that only NEET would enable students to get admission to MBBS studies. In view of all these orders passed by SC from time to time, it is more than abundantly clear that notification dt. 21-12-2010, mentioning that admissions to MBBS course shall be based solely on marks obtained in National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET), stood resurrected and that admissions to MBBS course could only through NEET-I and NEET-II. No other process of admission was permissible. No ground has been made out for granting relief to petitioner. Petition dismissed.
