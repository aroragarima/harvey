Smt. Malkani v Jamadar and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28654; (1987) 1 SCC 610; AIR 1987 SC 767; JT 1987 (1) SC 419; 1987(1) SCALE 342; 1987 (1) UJ 382

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family and Personal- Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Will - Appeal by special leave from suit for declaration and possession - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Concurrent findings, as to due execution and attestation of will - High Court dismissed the second appeal in limine as there was no question of law much less a substantial question of law to attract s.100 of CPC, 1908 - Interference sought - (B) Validity of will i.e. testamentary capacity of the executrix or the genuineness of the will challenged as beneficiaries under will taking active part in its execution - Supreme Court held, declined to interfere with findings of fact - Further held that will was genuine one and it was intended to be acted upon - Appeal dismissed.
