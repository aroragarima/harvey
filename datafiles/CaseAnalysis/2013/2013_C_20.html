Amitbhai Anilchandra Shah v Central Bureau of Investigation and another

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 206; (2013) 6 SCC 348; AIR 2013 SC 3794; 2013 CRLJ 2313; JT 2013 (4) SC 333; 2013 (2) OLR 269; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 819; 2013(5) SCALE 407; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Joint Trial

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 20, 21 and 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 173(2) and 173(8) - Filing of second FIR - Challenged - Sustainability - Petitioner had filed petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution of India owing to the filing of fresh FIR by the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) and charge sheet arraying him as accused in view of the directions given by HC to the Police Authorities to handover the case relating to the death of material witness to the killings of Sohrabuddin and his wife Kausarbi to the CBI - Petitioner claimed such act being violative of his fundamental rights u/arts. 14, 20 and 21 of the Constitution and contrary to the directions given by SC in one case - Whether filing of second FIR was in violation of fundamental rights of petitioner - Held, second FIR was nothing but consequence of the event which had taken place on 25-11-2005 and 26-11-2005 - SC had already concluded that having reposed faith in the CBI accepted their contention that Tulsiram Prajapati encounter was part of the same chain of events in which Sohrabuddin and Kausarbi were killed and directed the CBI to take up the investigation - For vivid understanding, considering situation in which Mr. 'A' having killed 'B' with the aid of 'C', informed the police that unknown persons killed 'B' - During investigation, it revealed that 'A' was the real culprit and 'D' abetted 'A' to commit the murder - As result, police officer filed the charge sheet u/s. 173(2) of CrPC with the Magistrate - Although, in due course, it was discovered through further investigation that the person who abetted Mr. 'A' was 'C' and not 'D' as mentioned in the charge sheet filed u/s. 173 of CrPC - In such scenario, uncovering of the later fact that 'C' was the real abettor would not demand second FIR rather supplementary charge sheet u/s. 173(8) of CrPC would serve the purpose - Likewise, in the case on hand, initially the respondent took stand that the third person accompanying Sohrabbuddin and Kausarbi was Kalimuddin - However, with the aid of further investigation, it unveiled that the third person was Tulsiram Prajapati - Thus second FIR in the given facts and circumstances was unwarranted - Instead filing of supplementary charge sheet in that regard would suffice the issue - SC accepting the plea that killing of Tulsiram Prajapati was part of the same series of cognizable offence forming part of the first FIR directed the CBI to "take over" the investigation and did not grant the relief prayed for i.e., registration of fresh FIR - Accordingly, filing of fresh FIR by the CBI was contrary to various decisions of SC - of the Narmada Bai vs. State of Gujarat & Ors., 2011 Indlaw SC 244, relied on - Petition allowed.
