Comptroller and Auditor General of India, Gian Prakash, New Delhi and Another v K. S. Jagannathan and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, R.S. Pathak, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 806; (1986) SCC (L&S) 345; (1986) 2 SCC 679; AIR 1987 SC 537; 1986 (1) ATC 1; 1986 (2) LLN 11; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 17; 1986 (2) SLJ 1; 1986 (1) SLR 712

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 148, 16(4), 226, 311, 335, 37, 46 - Equal Opportunity - Qualification - Maintainability - Respondents belonged to SC community and were working as Selection Grade Auditors - Next promotional post was Section Officer and for such promotion they were required to pass SAS Examination - Respondents failed to secure aggregate minimum marks - Respondents filed petition u/art. 226 of Constitution praying for writ of mandamus directing appellants to grant suitable relaxation for respondents in qualifying standard of marks and declare them as having passed SAS examination - Petition was dismissed - Respondents filed appeal which was allowed by DB directing appellants to give suitable relaxation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, in instant case candidates selected to appear for SAS Examination, condition had already been satisfied by reason of their selection as candidates - If it was considered that respondents would not be able to discharge duties they would not had been given relevant certificate required under Manual - They were given such certificates and it was not open to appellants to take stand contrary to what certificates given to respondents state - Appeal dismissed.
