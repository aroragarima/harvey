Ramnath International Construction Private Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1335; (2007) 2 SCC 453; AIR 2007 SC 509; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 111; 2007 (2) ALT 59; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 709; 2006(14) SCALE 49; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Award Damages

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Execution of work was delayed on account of breaches on part of employer and employer is liable to compensate contractor for all losses and extra cost on account of such delay and extended execution - Award - In contracts, employer was not released of his liability for damages on account of delays, by granting extension of time - (A) Whether claims are unsustainable being in derogation of cl. 11(C) of contract, which prohibits any compensation as a result of extension of time granted by department? - (B) Whether Arbitrator committed a legal misconduct? - Held, (A) time is of essence of contract and is specified in contract documents or in each individual works order - When extensions of time, were granted to complete work, respondents made it clear that no claim for compensation would lie - Further held, (B) arbitrator not acting in terms of cl. 11(C) of contract though pleaded and submitted before him clearly misconducted himself in awarding compensation - Appeals dismissed.
