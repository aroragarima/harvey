Â Roman Catholic Mission v State of Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 390; AIR 1966 SC 1457; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 119; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Board of Revenue Rules, r.54 (1) - Madras Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1926, s. 44 B - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 299 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 31 and 296 - Government of India Act, 1915, s. 45-A, 129-A - Inam lands were resumed and regranted to Devasthanam by an order passed by Revenue Divisional Officer, Madurai u/s 44 B of Madras Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1926 - At that time lands were in possession of Roman Catholic Mission of St. Mary's Church, Madurai, and were so held by Mission since October, 1894 - (A) What was nature of Inam - Whether it comprised both Warams? - Held, there is, of course, no dispute that Inam must have comprised Melwaram at least - Inamdars did not themselves claim in Inam enquiry anything more than Melwaram rights - There is no other evidence of inclusion of Kudiwaram in Inam and dealings were with Melwaram which alone inamdars claimed at Inam Enquiry - (B) Whether it was a grant to temple or a grant for an office to be remunerated by use of land or a grant of land burdened with service? - Further held, it was an Inam where land was held subject to payment of an amount as quit rent - It was granted to archakas and was recorded in their name - That they alienated lands is without any doubt and question is whether inam could be resumed or not - Question was whether s. 44-B(1) covered a grant of land burdened with service as against a grant for an office to be remunerated by use of land but resumable when service was not performed s. 44-B(1), in spite of width of its language is only open to a restricted interpretation and includes in resumable Inams those in which whole of income or a very great part is required for service and not large personal Inams with a small or slight service - On other hand grant of land made to an office-holder to remunerate him for service is always resumable if he ceases to hold office or to perform service - Inam was always considered as remuneration for archaka service of temple and on its alienation it is liable to resumption under s. 44 B - (C) Whether s. 44 B is ultra vires and void? - Further held, power of provincial legislature to enact s. 44 B was challenged under Govt. of India Act, 1915 and Govt. of India Act, 1935, respectively - Powers of Provincial legislatures extended over land tenures, land revenue administration and religious and charitable endowments - They must obviously furnish adequate scope for undertaking most comprehensive legislation on subject of Inams in general and Inams connected with religious and charitable endowments in particular -s. 44 B was thus fully within competence of Provincial Legislature - (D) Whether claim of adverse possession by petitioner is maintainable? - Further held, there is no period of limitation prescribed by any law within which alone Govt. should exercise its prerogative of imposing assessment on land liable to be assessed with public revenue - Appeals dismissed.
