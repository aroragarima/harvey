State of Bihar v Radha Krishna Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 278; (1983) 3 SCC 118; AIR 1983 SC 684; 1983(1) SCALE 789; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Possession of property, Revisionary right, Entitlement

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Possession of property - Revisionary right - Entitlement - Respondents/plaintiffs based their title as nearest revisioners of Maharaja and claimed to be entitled to immediate possession after the death of widows of Maharaja - Respondents claimed to be direct descendants of G and R, which was the branch of BS's son (Maharaja's son) which remained after extinction of line of Maharaja - Trial Court held that respondents had not proved any linkage or connection between R and BS - However, majority judgment disagreed with this finding and held that all links were clearly proved by respondents and it was proved to their satisfaction that R was son of BS - Although respondents produced oral and documentary evidence to show that R was the son of BS this position was not accepted by Trial Court as also by M.M. Prasad, J. - However, common factor between majority and dissenting judgments and it was that respondents proved beyond reasonable doubt their connection with G - Since the appellant State was the Court of Wards of the suit estate, appellant filed instant appeal - Aggrieved, appellant State preferred appeal before HC, which had negatived the plea of appellant and accepted that of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents proved their case that they were also direct descendants of BS so as to claim the properties in dispute on death of Maharaja - Held, respondent had no doubt proved that he was a direct descendent of G but that was of no assistance to him so long as it was not shown that missing links-the relationship of G with R, and R with BS, and that BS was one of the sons of H was established - Respondent had miserably failed to prove that G was in any way connected with BS, or that R was son of BS - However, documents, transactions judgments, rebkars, plaints, written statements, etc. produced by respondents were either inadmissible or irrelevant - Neither documentary nor oral evidence adduced by respondents was sufficient to prove their case and hence respondents failed to discharge the initial onus which lay on them to prove their case - Majority judgment was wrong in law and on facts and arrived at factually wrong and legally incorrect conclusions and, therefore, could not be upheld - It was entirely agreed with judgment of M.M. Prasad, J. so far as respondents case was concerned - Respondents not proved that they were next and nearest reversioners of late Maharaja - M.M Prasad, J. had rightly pointed out that as appellant did not enter arena as a respondent to claim properties by pleading that late Maharaja had left no heir at all - Hence, properties should vest in State, it should be difficult to hold that merely in event of failure of respondent's case properties should vest in State - Appellant satisfied by appearing to oppose claims of respondents - Even if they succeed in showing that respondents were not nearest reversioners of late Maharaja, it did not follow as a logical corollary that failure of respondents claim should lead to irresistible inference that there was no other heir who would at any time come forward to claim properties - Trial Court was wrong in accepting case of escheat put forward by appellants - Dissenting judgment of M.M. Prasad, J. was affirmed - Appeals allowed.
