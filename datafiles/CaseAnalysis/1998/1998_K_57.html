C. Rangaswamaiah and Others v Karnataka Lokayukta and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 330; (1998) 6 SCC 66; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1448; AIR 1998 SC 2496; 1998 (2) CLR 52; JT 1998 (5) SC 64; 1998 (4) LLN 60; 1998 (3) RCR(Criminal) 547; 1998(4) SCALE 266; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 837; 1998 (6) SLT 249; 1998 (6) Supreme 68

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Karnataka Lokayukta Act, 1984, s. 15 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 17 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Lokayukta - Staff - (A) Post of Director General in office of Lok Ayukta created by administrative order - Effect of non amendment of recruitment rules accordingly - (B) General and overall control of Lok Ayukta over staff and its supervising Director General of Police wing by notification - If it can be treated as threat to independence of Lok Ayukta - (C) Police Officers on deputation - If continue to remain as public servants in service of state Govt. - (D) Deputed officers entrusted with power u/s. 17 of 1988 Act by state Govt. - If entrustment can be questioned by public servants, against whom they investigate by tacit approval of Lok Ayukta? - Held, still post must be treated as part of staff of Lok Ayukta - Cannot be treated so - Can continue, as long as they are not absorbed in Lok Ayukta - Cannot question such entrustment - Petition dismissed.
