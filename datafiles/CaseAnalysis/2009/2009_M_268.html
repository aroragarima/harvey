National Insurance Company Ltd. v Meghji Naran Soratiya & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 336; (2009) 12 SCC 796; 2009 (2) ACC 289; 2009 (75) ALR 471; 2009 (3) AWC 2995; 2009 (1) CCC 136; 2009 (2) G.L.R. 1640; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 150; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 558; 2009(4) SCALE 50; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Award Of Interest, Compensation Claim, f Ob, Third Party Risks, Material Particular, Notice To The Insurer

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - High Court dismissed the appeal of insurer on the sole ground that the Tribunal while granting permission to the insurer to contest the claim under s. 170 did not assign reasons for granting permission - Appeal against - Held, grant of permission by the Tribunal to the insurer to contest the proceedings does not call for interference - Wnder no circumstances, the Tribunal's permission to contest the claim, can be equated to or treated as denial of permission to contest the claim, merely on the ground that reasons were not recorded - Appeals allowed.
