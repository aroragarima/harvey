Â State of Mysore v M. H. Krishna Murthy and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 45; (1973) SCC (L&S) 190; (1973) 3 SCC 559; AIR 1973 SC 1146; 1973 LabIC 791; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 575; 1973 SLJ 168; 1972 SLR 832

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1), 133 and 309 - (A) Division into two classes of members of same service belonging to same cadre for purpose of creating difference in promotional opportunity - Constitutionality - (B) Power to make rules u/art. 309 - Whether can be used to validate discrimination in promotional chances of Govt. servants who belong to same category ?- Held,Supreme Court will not allow new points to be raised for first time particularly when they involve investigation of questions of fact - Violates arts. 14 and 16 (1) - Cannot be used to validate unconstitutional discrimination in promotional chances - Appeal dismissed.
