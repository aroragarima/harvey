Raj Kumar v Sardari Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 84; (2004) 2 SCC 601; 2004 (2) ALD(SC) 95; 2004 (54) ALR 505; 2004 (2) AWC 1186; 2004 (2) CHN 13; 2004 (2) CLR 340; JT 2004 (2) SC 196; 2004 (1) LCD 803; 2004(2) SCALE 17; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 838; 2004 (1) SLT 926; 2004 (1) Supreme 532

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 146 and O. 9 r. 13, O. 22 r. 10 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Doctrine of lis pendens - Maxim 'ut lite pendente nihil innovetur' - Proceedings by or against representatives - A lis pendens transferee - Though not brought on record under o. 22 r. 10 - Entitled to move under o. 9 r. 13 to set aside a decree passed against his transferor - Appeal dismissed.
