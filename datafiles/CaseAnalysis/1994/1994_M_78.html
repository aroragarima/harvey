Managing Director Ecil v B. Karunakar

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 397; (1994) Supp2 SCC 391; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1009; 1994 (27) ATC 767

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Complaint - Inquiry - Dismissal - Affidavit - Respondent was an Engineer and was charged with passing of spurious EC T.V made by Electronics Corporation (employer) under the brand name 'Ajanta' - On the basis of the complaint made by the purchaser, an inquiry was instituted against the respondent - At the conclusion of the inquiry, he was dismissed from service by the Corporation by its order - Respondent challenged the same in the W.P. - Single Judge remarked that even if the witness was tendered for cross-examination, he couldn't have made more beneficial statements than those contained in the said affidavit in favour of the respondent - W.P. dismissed - Respondent filed writ appeal stating that the enquiry was vitiated - Whether there was ground for interference with the order of dismissal - Held, it was held by the Constitution Bench that the principles of natural justice do require that a copy of the enquiry report was supplied to the charged officer though it was not necessary to give him a notice indicating the proposed penalty - Order of dismissal of the respondent was far earlier to the said date - In the circumstances, the dismissal order won't be said to be vitiated by no furnishing of the Enquiry Officer's report - Therefore, there was no ground for interference with the order of dismissal - Appeal allowed.
