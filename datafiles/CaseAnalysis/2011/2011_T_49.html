Mahant Jawala Singh Chela Of Mahant Bishan Singh (Dead)Through Legal Representative v The Shiromani Gurdwara Prabhandhak Committee, Amritsar

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 46; (2011) 2 SCC 457; AIR 2011 SC 732; JT 2011 (2) SC 305; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 577; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 770; 2011(1) SCALE 341; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Sikh Gurdwaras (Supplementary) Act, 1925

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925, ss. 7 (1), 7(3) and 16 - Declaration of Sikh Gurudwara - Challenged - 53 persons claiming to be Sikh worshippers submitted a petition to the State Government u/s. 7(1) of the Act declaring the institution in question as a Sikh Gurdwara - All the petitions were forwarded by the State Government to the Tribunal constituted u/s. 14(1) of the Act to get the issue decided - Respondent- Shiromani Gurdwara Prabandhak Committee got itself impleaded as party to the proceedings pending before the Tribunal, filed written statement questioning the very maintainability of the petition filed by the petitioners on the ground that he was not a hereditary office-holder and the petition does not disclose the custom relating to devolution of Mahantship in this Gurdwara - Tribunal upheld the claim of the petitioners and declared the institution to be a State Gurdwara relying upon the entries in the revenue records - Said finding of tribunal was affirmed by the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the declaration made by the Tribunal as affirmed by the HC that the institution in question is a Sikh Gurdwara was legally correct? - Held, no - As per s. 16 of the Act, Tribunal can declare an institution to be a Sikh Gurdwara u/s. 16(2)(iii) of the Act, it must be satisfied that (a) the institution was established for use by Sikhs for the purpose of public worship, and (b) was used for such worship by Sikhs before and at the time of presentation of the petition u/s. 7(1) of the Act - These two conditions are required to be fulfilled separately and conjointly and unless that is done, the Tribunal cannot declare an institution to be a Sikh Gurdwara - It is also a settled law that the onus to prove that an institution is a Sikh Gurdwara lies on the person who asserts the same - In the present case, the findings recorded by the Tribunal and the HC on the question of use of the institution for worship by Sikhs are too sketchy as the only statement made by the witnesses examined by the respondent was that sometimes the residents go for worship of Guru Granth Sahib - Hence, in the absence of any evidence to show that the institution was established for use by Sikhs for the purpose of public worship, the Tribunal did not have the jurisdiction to declare it to be a Sikh Gurdwara by simply relying upon the entries in the revenue records or the fact that Prakash of Guru Granth Sahib is done and on some occasion people come to worship Guru Granth Sahib and the HC committed serious error by dismissing the appeal - Moreover, none of the 53 persons who submitted petition u/s. 7(1) of the Act for declaring the institution in question as a Sikh Gurdwara responded to the notice issued by the Tribunal or appeared before it to support their plea - Rather, some of them filed petition u/s. 8 of the Act asserting that their signatures were obtained by fraud - Hence, declaration made by the Tribunal that the institution in question is a Sikh Gurdwara is set aside - Impugned judgment as also the order passed by the Tribunal are set aside - Appeal allowed.
