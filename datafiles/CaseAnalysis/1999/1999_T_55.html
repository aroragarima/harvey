State of Maharashtra v Tapas D. Neogy

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 719; (1999) 7 SCC 685; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1352; AIR 1999 SCW 3389; 1999 (39) AllCriC 784; 1999 (4) AWC 3385; [1999] 98 Comp Cas 626; 1999 CRLJ 4305; 2000 (159) CTR 247; JT 1999 (7) SC 92; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 609; 2000 (159) TAXATION 370

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13 and 16 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 467, 468, 471 and 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 102 and 457 - Whether a police officer investigating into an offence can issue prohibitory order in respect of bank account of accused in exercise of power u/s. 102 of CrPC? - Within meaning of s. 102 of CrPC and a police officer in course of investigation can seize or prohibit operation of said account if such assets have direct links with commission of offence for which police officer is investigating into - Held, bank account within meaning of 'property' and if police officer is able to show nexus between amount in bank account and illegal gratification, there is no bar in exercising powers of prohibition in respect of operation of that bank account - As far as this present case is concerned, impugned order is given effect, no need to interfere with same - Order accordingly.
