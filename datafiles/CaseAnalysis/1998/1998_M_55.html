S.G.P. Committee v M.P. Dass Chela (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 724; (1998) 5 SCC 157; AIR 1998 SC 1978; JT 1998 (3) SC 604; 1998(3) SCALE 351; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 119; 1998 (5) SLT 27; 1998 (4) Supreme 411

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Socio-Economic - Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 2(4)(iv), 7(1) and 16(2 - (A) Letters Patent (Pun), cl. 26 - Reference - Difference of opinion between two Judges as to decision to be given on disputed point - Whether reference of matter to third Judge invalid - (B) Succession to Office of Mahant in accordance with particular scheme or definite usage or custom - Whether, jt is a case of Hereditary Succession - Nomination of chela by bhek after death of office holder - Whether nominated chela would be hereditary office holder - (C) Declaration of institution as Sikh Gurudwara - None of the persons presenting petition furnished evidence in support thereof - On analysing materials on record institution in question found to be not Sikh Gurudwara - Reliance on evidence that the Guru Granth Sahib is worshipped therein - Whether it would be helpful to applicant - (D) Appreciation of Evidence - Whether it is open to any Court to sift the deposition of any witness and accept a part thereof while rejecting the other part - Held, (A) Not invalid - (B) Is case of Hereditary Succession - Chela would be hereditary office holder - (C) Evidence that Guru Granth Sahib is worshipped therein could not be relied on - (D) Court has power to accept a part thereof while rejecting the other part - Appeal dismissed.
