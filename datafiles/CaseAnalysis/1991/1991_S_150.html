Sardara Singh And Ors. Etc. v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1009; (1991) 4 SCC 555; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1357; AIR 1991 SC 2248; 1992 (19) ATC 77; 1992 (64) FLR 25; JT 1991 (4) SC 154; 1991 LabIC 2404; 1992 (1) LLN 4; 1991(2) SCALE 616; 1991 (5) SLR 620; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 152; 1991 (2) UJ 640

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 309 and 311 - Punjab Revenue Patwari Class III Service Rules (1963), r. 4 - Recruitment to post of Patwari and District Committee constituted for that purpose - Direction issued by Govt. not to solicit candidates afresh and to give preference to priority categories - District Collector calling fresh application from priority categories - Vacancies were not advertised in newspaper - Educational qualifications, apparent from record - Validity of selection process challenged - Held, objection cannot be raised by persons who had applied for said post but were not appointed - Selection could not be assailed on ground that no proper opportunity was given in interview - Relaxation cannot be given on mere ground that petitioners are carrying on litigation - Appeal dismissed.
