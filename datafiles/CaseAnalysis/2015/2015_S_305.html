State of Kerala and another v Arun George and others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 60; JT 2015 (2) SC 252; 2015 (1) KLT 833; 2015(1) SCALE 775; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1034; 2015 (3) SLR 611

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Pre-degree Course (Abolition) Act, 1997, Entitlement of, Appointments by Statutory Selection Committee, Direct payment of salaries

Summary: Service - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Appointments by Statutory Selection Committee - Direct payment of salaries - Entitlement of - Respondent nos. 1 to 7 who were appointed by respondent no. 8 (management) to various new courses sanctioned by Govt., management forwarded the proposal for approval of their appointment to the University; but the same was rejected -Aggrieved by non-approval of appointment of respondent nos. 1 to 8, respondents filed petition before HC to quash the staff fixation orders - Single Judge held that Govt. was liable to pay salary and other allowances to the teachers appointed to new courses by managements - Aggrieved Govt. filed appeal before DB - DB by common judgment allowed the appeals - Aggrieved respondents filed various review petitions, which were allowed by impugned common judgment by DB - Aggrieved State filed instant appeals - Whether State Govt. was bound to pay the salary to teachers, who were appointed for newly commenced courses for the relevant period.

Held, initially approval was not granted for the appointments of respondent nos. 1 to 7, the University granted approval to these appointments vide order dt.29-10-2005. Sanction of new courses led to the increase of work load and the services of respondent nos. 1 to 7 were utilised by management. The courses are purely aided courses and therefore, the provisions of Direct Payment Agreement are undoutedly applicable. The State administration cannot shirk its responsibility of ensuring proper and quality education in Schools and Colleges on the plea of lack of resources. It is evident that respondent nos. 1 to 7 were appointed only against sanctioned posts. It is not the case of the Govt. that management violated the terms of the Direct Payment Agreement. For many years i.e. from 2002, services of respondent nos. 1 to 7 have been utilized for imparting instruction, invigilation and other duties. By perusal of the information obtained under 2005 Act that the Govt. has paid salaries and emoluments to some of the lecturers appointed in other private colleges. When the respondent nos. 1 to 7 were appointed by the Statutory Selection Committee, Court find no reason as to why respondent nos. 1 to 7 should be denied the payment of salary. When respondent nos. 1 to 7 have been appointed by the Statutory Selection Committee, it becomes obligatory for the Govt. to honour these appointments and pay the salary. Single Judge and DB in review petitions rightly held that respondent nos. 1 to 7 are entitled to the payment of salary for the relevant period. Appeals dismissed.
