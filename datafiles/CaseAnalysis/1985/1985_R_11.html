Collector (Distt. Magistrate) Allahabad and Another v Raja Ram Jaiswal Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 417; (1985) 3 SCC 1; AIR 1985 SC 1622; 1985 ALJ 887; 1985(1) SCALE 1044; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Cinemas (Regulation) Act, 1955, Uttar Pradesh Cinematograph Rules 1951

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963 - Validity - Respondent moved W.P. u/art. 226 of the Constitution in the HC questioning the validity of the Notification issued u/s. 4(1) of the 1894 Act as also a notice served upon him pursuant to mentioned notification - Impugned notification was published in the U.P. Govt. Gazette - By this impugned notification, land bearing Plot was sought to be acquired as being needed for a public purpose namely for extension of Sammelan - A substances of this notification was published in the locality where the land sought to be acquired was situate - Corrigendum was published by which the impugned notification was to stand corrected - After the publication of corrigendum petitioner sought amendment of the petition which was granted - Validity of the amended notification was challenged on diverse grounds - Negativing all the challenges except the one that as there was failure to cause public notice of the substance of notification u/s. 4(1) to be published at convenient place in the locality on this short ground, impugned notification was quashed - Hence, the present appeal - Whether impugned notification issued was valid - Held, after analysing the fact, it was concluded that the land was not needed for a Mandi which was the ostensible purpose for which the land was sought to be acquired but Mandi need was hijacked to reach the private destination of depriving an enemy of his land through back-seat driving of the statutory engine - Notification was declared invalid on the ground that it suffered from legal mala fides - Therefore, disagreeing with HC, the power to acquire land was exercised for an extraneous and irrelevent purpose and it was colourable exercise of power, namely, to satisfy the chagrin and anguish of the Sammelan at the coming up of a cinema theatre in the vicinity of its campus, which it vowed to destroy - Therefore, impugned notification had to be declared illegal and invalid - Validity of the impugned notification was also challenged on the ground that even though the acquisition was for the Sammelan, a company, the notification was issued without first complying with the provisions of r. 4 of the 1963 Rules - HC had negatived this challenge - Appeal dismissed.
