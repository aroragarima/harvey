G. Ramachandra Reddy and Company v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1989; (2009) 6 SCC 414; AIR 2009 SC 2629; JT 2009 (7) SC 655; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 977; 2009(7) SCALE 234; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 917

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Reasoned Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Claims of additional payment over quoted items, escalation cost and higher minimum wages - Appellant-Company entered into a construction contract with respondent-Union - Contract terminated and dispute referred to 2nd respondent-arbitrator - Appellant preferred claims against the respondent - 2nd respondent-arbitrator allowed 4 claims of appellant and published the award - Respondent filed application before the Single Judge to set aside the award - Single Judge dismissed the application - Respondent filed Letters Patent Appeal before DB - DB allowed appeal in part with respect to 3 claims - (A) Whether appellant entitled to get the amount towards additional payment over the quoted rates of items with respect to claim no. 1 - Held, conclusion of the contract has been taken place only after the consideration of a letter written by the appellant relating to the additional payment - Arbitrator held that such letter, therefore, formed part of the contract, hence claim no.1 of additional payment was sustained - (B) Whether appellant entitled to the amount under the head of escalation cost with respect to claim no.2 - Held, matter of escalation referred by the appellant to respondent as covering letter along with the tender bid - Since the tender bid has accepted by the respondent without any modification, said covering letter is also becoming the part of tender bid - Therefore, claim no. 2 with respect to escalation cost was sustained - (C) Whether appellant entitled to the higher minimum wages paid to the workmen with respect to the claim no. 3 - Held, the same workmen may have to work in different parts of the same building at different times - It would lead to an absurd situation if the workmen at one point of time are not paid the 20% of the excess amount and then paid the same and yet again denied the same benefit - Therefore, appellant's claim no. 3 with respect to the payment of higher minimum wages to his workmen is sustained - Appeal dismissed.
