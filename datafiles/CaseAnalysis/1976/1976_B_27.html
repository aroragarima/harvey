Bhagwati and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 525; (1976) 3 SCC 235; (1976) SCC (Cr) 388; AIR 1976 SC 1449; 1976 CRLJ 1171; 1976 CRLR 204

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 286 and 423 - (A) Power of appellate court to review evidence in an appeal against acquittal - (B) Effect od failure to examine a witness mentioned in FIR - Held, appellate Court should be slow in disturbing the finding of fact of the trial Court, and if two views are reasonable possible, it is not interfere because it feels that it would have taken a different view if the case had been tried by it - Held, It would be unsound to lay down a general rule - Appeal Dismissed.
