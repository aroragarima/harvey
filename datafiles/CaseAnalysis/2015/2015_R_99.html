Ratnesh Kumar Choudhary v Indira Gandhi Institute of Medical Sciences, Patna, Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 740; AIR 2016 SC 467; 2016 (149) FLR 125; JT 2015 (9) SC 363; 2016 (2) RLW 1496; 2015(10) SCALE 740; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 717; 2015 (6) SLR 746

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Departmental Enquiry, Competent Authority, Preliminary Enquiry, Discharge, Stigma

Summary: Service - Failure to possess requisite qualification - Termination from Service - Justification - Appellant, in pursuance of advertisement applied for post of Physiotherapist under Class-II Post in medical institute - Selection committee of institute selected him for appointment in post as Chest Therapist, however screening committee observed that post of Physiotherapist and Chest Therapist are of similar nature and hence, post of Chest Therapist may be considered from applications received for post of Physiotherapist - Further, when appellant was continuing on post of Chest Therapist, complaint was received by Vigilance Department, relating to illegal appointment of appellant on post of Chest Therapist on ground that that advertisement for Physiotherapist and Chest Therapist were different because streams are different and appointment of appellant was absolutely illegal as he did not possess required qualification - Inquiry was conducted and show cause notice was issued to appellant - Further, vide order, respondent institute terminated service of appellant - Lower Court quashed order of termination and directed that appellant should be treated in service with all consequential benefits on ground that appellant was not informed on what grounds his service had been terminated and further he was not furnished with necessary documents which formed part of enquiry conducted by authority - However, revered order passed by Lower court and passed order in favor of respondent institute - Hence, present appeal.

Held, authorities after issuing notice to show cause and obtaining reply from delinquent employee did not supply documents. Further, no regular enquiry was held and he was visited with punishment of dismissal. Hence, when enquiry commenced and thereafter without framing of charges or without holding enquiry delinquent employee was dismissed, there is clear violation of principles of natural justice. It cannot be equated with situation of dropping of disciplinary proceedings and passing order of termination simpliciter. Further, it is also not case that he was terminated solely on ground of earlier punishment and he continued in service thereafter. Further, SC held that it was not enquiry only to find out that he did not possess requisite qualification but allegations in report of authority pertain to his misbehaviour, conduct and his dealing with officers which is supported by stand taken in counter affidavit. Hence, it cannot be accepted that it can be accepted that it is termination simpliciter. Hence, impugned judgment passed by DB is set aside and order passed by lower Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to terminate service of employee when principles of natural justice has not been followed and no regular enquiry is held.
