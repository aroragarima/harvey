State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Sanjai Pratap Gupta @ Pappu and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1414; (2004) 8 SCC 591; AIR 2004 SC 4703; 2004 CRLJ 4600; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 568; 2004(8) SCALE 75; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 561

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 5A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - National Security Act, 1980, s. 3(2) - Detention order - Disturbance of public order - Respondent no. 1 was detained pursuant to order of detention passed u/s. 3(2) of the National Security Act, 1980 - According to order and grounds of detention, activities of respondents were considered to be prejudicial to public order, specifically firing by respondent no. 1 and his associates that had caused death of deceased - Case was registered for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC - Criminal antecedents of respondent was highlighted - Detention order was passed to prevent respondent no. 1 from committing similar prejudicial acts and to maintain public order - Respondent no. 1 filed habeas corpus petition questioning validity of order of detention - HC by impugned judgment accepted prayer and quashed order of detention - Appellant submitted that HC lost sight of scope and ambit of s. 5-A of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether activities of respondent no. 1were disturbance of current life of community that was prejudicial to public order and whether respondent no. 1 had to return to detention, after it was held that judgment of HC was not sustainable - Held, Law and order and public order was differentiated - Appellant rightly contended grounds of detention was not law and order situation but was public order situation - S. 5A of the Act was introduced to take care of situations when one or more of grounds were separated from other grounds that justified detention - Hence, inevitable conclusion was that s. 5A was applicable to case and HC was not justified in holding contrary - HC's judgment was therefore clearly indefensible - Respondent no. 1 had taken nearly three months to file his counter to special leave petition filed, thus it was clear that live link was not snapped and apprehension of detaining authority about respondent's prejudicial activities was not to be faulted - Accordingly, impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Respondent no. 1 was to surrender forthwith to serve remainder of period of detention - Appeal allowed.
