Jay Mahakali Rolling Mills v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1248; (2007) 12 SCC 198; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 674; 2007 (215) E.L.T. 11; JT 2007 (9) SC 629; 2007(9) SCALE 603; 2007 (6) SCJ 94; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Misstatement, Tariff Item

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 6 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 9(1), 49, 52A, 53, 173 and 174 - Notification no. 208/83-CE D/- 01-08-1983 - High Court by the impugned order held that there was no basis to accept the contention that the notification was intended to be given retrospective effect - By the amendment relatable to notification on 27-03-1987, items which were earlier not included were specified as inputs have been included - That being so, the contention that the amendment merely clarified the notification as it stood prior to the amendment, is not untenable - Held, a bare reading of the circular clearly shows that it was intended to have prospective effect - High Court and Tribunal order does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeal dismissed.
