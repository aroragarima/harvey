Ahmed v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 424; (2000) 7 SCC 477; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1407; AIR 2000 SC 2790; AIR 2000 SCW 2969; 2000 (3) CCR 142; 2000 (3) Crimes 188; 2000 CRLJ 4008; 2000 (2) JCC 723; JT 2000 (9) SC 416; 2000 (3) RCR(Criminal) 759; 2000(6) SCALE 94; 2000 (3) SCJ 354; 2000 (6) SLT 357; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 642; 2000 (5) Supreme 731

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sustainability, Possession of Contraband articles, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 20 and 50 - Mandatory requirement - Non-compliance of - Effect thereof - Appellant was tried for the offence u/s. 20-B(2) of the Act, as he was found in possession of 9 gms. of Charas, which he was selling outside his house - Trial Court held that the prosecution established beyond reasonable doubt that Charas was found from the pocket of the trouser of the appellant and the appellant was convicted - On appeal, HC affirmed the conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that the mandatory requirements of s. 50 of the Act have not been complied with inasmuch as notwithstanding the fact that the appellant himself requested for being taken to the Magistrate for the purpose of search, the Police did not accede to the same and, therefore, the conviction was null and void.

Held, when an empowered officer or a duly authorised officer, acting on prior information is about to search a person, it is imperative for him to inform the person concerned of his right u/s. 50 of the Act being taken to the nearest gazetted officer or the nearest Magistrate for making the search. In the instant case, appellant himself having wanted to be searched before a gazetted officer or a Magistrate and the same having been denied, there cannot be any doubt that failure on the part of the prosecution in complying with the provisions of Section 50, renders the recovery of illicit article suspect and vitiates the conviction and sentence of the accused, since the conviction in the case in hand is based solely on the alleged possession of Charas, which was recovered from his person, during a search conducted in violation of the provisions of s. 50 of the Act. The conviction and sentence is set aside. Appeal allowed.
