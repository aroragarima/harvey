K. K. Singhal and others v Steel Strips Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 837; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 343; 2015 (1) Bom.C.R. 55; 2015 (2) CompLJ 599; JT 2014 (13) SC 399; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 249; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 232; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 236; 2014(13) SCALE 661; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Quashing of complaint - Appellants filed application u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing of complaint - Appellants submitted that Judicial Magistrate, Chandigarh had no territorial jurisdiction since the agreement between the parties was entered into on 24-7-1998 at Faridabad - Further contended that the appellants having its place of business at Faridabad, it was the Court at Faridabad, which would have jurisdiction - HC dismissed said application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the agreement was entered into at Chandigarh to withdraw the criminal proceedings, the complainant having its head office at Chandigarh and further nowhere the appellants have made out a case that all the cheques were payable at Faridabad. Therefore, SC do not have any hesitation to hold that the question of jurisdiction was of no consequence, which would be decided by the trial court. The second point, which was urged by appellant that the dispute was of civil nature, cannot be allowed to stand at thwas stage after taking into account the conduct of the appellants. No reason to interfere with the orders passed by the HC. Appeal dismissed.
