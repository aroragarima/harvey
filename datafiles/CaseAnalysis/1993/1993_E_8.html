Wellman (India) Pvt.Ltd v Employees State Insurance Corpn

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 465; (1994) 1 SCC 219; (1994) SCC (L&S) 504; AIR 1994 SC 1037; 1994 (1) CompLJ 248; 1993 (67) FLR 1208; JT 1993 (6) SC 227; 1994 LabIC 954; 1994 (1) LLJ 545; 1993 (2) LLN 645; 1993(4) SCALE 353; 1993 (5) SLR 793

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(22) and 39 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - (A) Attendance bonus payable as per settlement reached in concilliation proceedings - Whether constitutes 'wages'? - (B) Definition of wages - Expression 'other additional remuneration' - Meaning thereof - Held, constitutes wages - Means remuneration not payable under contract of employment but it refers to remuneration payable at will of employer - Appeal dismissed.
