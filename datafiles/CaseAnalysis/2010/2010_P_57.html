Satheedevi v Prasanna and another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 363; (2010) 5 SCC 622; AIR 2010 SC 2777; JT 2010 (5) SC 118; 2010 (2) KLT 642; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 153; 2010(5) SCALE 164; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1955, Kerala Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1959, Madras Survey and Boundaries Act, 1923, Mysore Court Fees Act, Andhra Pradesh Court-fees and Suits Valuation Act, Travancore Survey and Boundaries Act, 1094, Cochin Survey Act, 1074

Summary: (A) Practice & Procedure - Kerala Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1959, s. 40 -Valuation of suit property - S. 40 of the Act - Interpretation of - Appellant executed a power of attorney in favour of 1st respondent with respect to the suit properties - 1st respondent sold the suit properties to 2nd respondent - Appellant filed a suit against respondents seeking to cancel the sale deed executed by the 1st respondent in favour of 2nd respondent - Trial Court directed appellant to pay court fees on the market values of the suit property - Appellant challenged the Trial Court order before HC - Single Judge dismissed appellant's writ petition - Held, deeming clause contained in the substantive part of s. 40(1) makes it clear that in a suit filed for cancellation of a document which creates any right, title or interest in immovable property, the court fees is required to be computed on the value of the property for which the document was executed and not on its market value - If the legislature intended that fee should be payable on the market value of the subject matter of the suit filed for cancellation of a document which purports or operates to create, declare, assign, limit or extinguish any present or future right, title and interest, then it would have, instead of incorporating the requirement of payment of fees on value of subject matter, specifically provided for payment of court fee on the market value of the subject matter of the suit - If the interpretation placed by the Trial Court and the HC on the expression 'value of the property for which the document was executed' is accepted as correct then the word 'value' used in s. 40(1) of the Act would have to be read as 'market value'- S. 40 of the Act certainly contains a special rule for valuing the property for the purpose of court fee and there is no reason why the expression 'value of the property' used in s. 40(1) should be substituted with the expression 'market value of the property' - Therefore, appellant is liable to remit the court fee only on the value of property mentioned in the documents and hence impugned orders of HC and Trial Court are set aside - Direction issued to the Trial Court to proceed with the case decide the same in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Interpretation of laws - Held, if the words used are capable of one construction, only then it would not be open to the courts to adopt any other hypothetical construction on the ground that such hypothetical construction is more consistent with the alleged object and policy of the Act - Words used in the material provisions of the statute must be interpreted in their plain grammatical meaning and it is only when such words are capable of two constructions that the question of giving effect to the policy or object of the Act can legitimately arise - Appeal allowed.
