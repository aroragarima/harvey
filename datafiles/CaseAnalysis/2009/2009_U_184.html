Union of India v Saraswat Trading Agency and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 891; (2009) 16 SCC 504; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 839; 2010 (1) AWC 362B; 2009 (3) JhrCR(SC) 133; JT 2009 (9) SC 648; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 715; 2009(9) SCALE 668; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1063

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34 and 11 - Escalation clause - Interest - Railway work contract - For three years starting from 03-08-1991 - One of the clauses of the contract provided for, that contractor shall pay to the labourers engaged by him not less than the fair wages - Fair wages were revised by the Railway authorities, vide respective circulars/guidelines, from retrospective dates - Respondent/contractor demanded enhancement of rates under the contract, on the ground that the rates stated in the agreement were based on the circular prevalent at the time of entering into the contract which had undergone a number of revisions, resulting into unrealistic and unviable rates - Demand rejected - Respondent/contractor terminated the contract by giving three months' notice as provided for in respective clause - In order to avoid any dislocation appellant requested the respondent/contractor to carry on the work on the same terms and conditions, promising that its claim would receive due consideration; respondent continued the work, under protest, till August, 1994 - At the time of final settlement Railway authorities offered certain sum which was unacceptable to respondent; committee was appointed for the purpose, and the amount fixed by it was still unacceptable - Departmental arbitrator was appointed - Award was challenged by respondent u/s. 34 of Act before High Court - HC upheld award on items 1 and 2 but set aside in respect of items 3, 4 and 5, and appointed an advocate for the three items - Application was moved u/s. 11, and earlier appointed advocate was substituted - Award made by arbitrator who was appointed u/s. 11, challenged by appellant u/s. 34; challenge accepted by single Judge of HC on items 1 and 3 but item 4 was upheld - Item 4 pertained to the claim of after termination period - Respondent preferred an appeal thereagainst - No appeal preferred against upheld item 4, thus it attained finality - Appeal by respondent was allowed by DB of HC wherein award of arbitrator was upheld in its entirety -P /&gt;(A) Escalation clause - Contention was raised by appellant that in the absence of any escalation clause in the agreement, claim for enhanced payments for the period during which agreement was in force is unfounded - Contention whether acceptable? - Held, No - Decision in 'Tarapore & Co. vs. State of MP, (1994) 3 SCC 521' relied - 'By asking the appellant to give tender by taking into account the fair wages notified at the time of inviting tenders, the authorities did give an impression that fair wages to be paid would be the one then notified/prescribed...; 'in such a situation, if rates of fair wages were raised afterwards, the tendered sum cannot be taken to be agreed amount for completing the contract, in the face of the directions of the authorities requiring the appellant to pay wages at rates higher than those prescribed or notified at the time of inviting tenders; '...the State had by necessary implication agreed to reimburse this increased payment.' -P /&gt;(B) Grant of Interest - Arbitrator granted pre-reference, pendente lite and post-award interest on both its claims under items 3 and 4 - Whether proper? - Held, partly - Appellant raised contention that vide cl. 31 of agreement, claim of interest was expressly barred - Cl. 31 perused - Decided cases on point of interest for different periods, referred - 'Bhagawati Oxygen Ltd. vs. Hindustan Copper Ltd., 2005 (6) SCC 462' - Arbitrator has power to grant interest for pre-reference period provided there is no prohibition in the arbitration agreement excluding his jurisdiction to grant interest - 'Arbitrator is competent to award interest for the period from the date of the award to the date of decree or date of realization whichever is earlier', 'Hindustan Construction Co. Ltd vs. State of J & K, 1992(4)SCC 217' - 'Interest pendente lite is not a matter of substantive law, like interest for the period anterior to reference; for doing complete justice between the parties, such power has always been inferred' - In instant case claim pertained to two periods; one during which agreement was subsisting and the other when the agreement was terminated - Court opined that the two periods must receive different treatments - Cl. 31 barred payment of any interest or damage to the contractor for any reasons whatsoever - Item no. 3 pertained the agreement period, thus no pre-reference or pendente lite interest could have been awarded on such item in view of bar of cl. 31 - Item no. 4 pertained to the post agreement period; thus no bar u/cl. 31 attracted; thus award of pre-reference interest and pendente lite interest for such item, upheld - Directions issued - Appeal disposed of.
