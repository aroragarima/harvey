Â Messrs Builders' Association of India v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.H. Kania, Yogeshwar Dayal, A.S. Anand, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 744; (1993) 1 SCC 409; AIR 1993 SC 991; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 516; 1992(3) SCALE 198; 1993 (88) STC 248; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 88

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Karnataka Sales Tax Rules, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Tax - Unconstitutional - Appellant was an association of contractor engaged in the work of construction - Appellant contested the Rules in the W.P. introduced after the Constitution (Forty-sixth Amendment) whereby clause (29-A) was introduced in art. 366 of the Constitution which was dismissed - Hence this Appeal - Whether the State Legislature had contravented art. 14 of the Constitution in prescribing different rates of tax for particular types of works contract in the Sixth Schedule - Held, tax was not levied on the value of the works contract - Taxable turnover on which tax was leviable was arrived at after deducting from the value of the works contract the expenses which are incurred by the contractor towards labour charges and other expenses - It cannot be said that s. 5B of Act provides for levy of tax not on the value of the goods involved in the execution of a works contract but also on something which was not part of that value - It cannot be held that State Legislature has contravened the provisions of art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
