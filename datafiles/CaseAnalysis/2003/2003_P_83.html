Laxman Singh v Poonam Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 735; (2004) 10 SCC 94; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1514; AIR 2003 SC 3204; 2003 (4) CCR 1; 2003 (4) Crimes 1; 2004 (1) CRJ 62; 2003 CRLJ 4478; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 61; 2004 (1) MahLJ 317; 2004 (1) MPLJ 93; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 560; 2003(7) SCALE 334; 2003 (1) SCW 4566; 2003 (5) SLT 331; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 528; 2003 (6) Supreme 644; 2004 (1) UC 298

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 100, 101, 304, 323, 447, 96, 97, 98, 99 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Acquittal - Questioning correctness and legality of judgment of HC of Rajasthan - Exercise of right of private defence - Held, in moments of excitement and disturbed mental equilibrium, it is often difficult to expect parties to preserve composure and use only so much force in retaliation commensurate with danger apprehended to him where assault is imminent by use of force, it would be lawful to repel force in self-defence and right of private-defence commences, as soon as threat becomes so imminent - Due weightage has to be given to, and hyper technical approach has to be avoided in considering what happens on spur of moment on spot and keeping in view normal human reaction and conduct, where self-preservation is paramount consideration - But, if fact situation shows that in guise of self-preservation, what really has been done is to assault original aggressor, even after cause of reasonable apprehension has disappeared, plea of right of private-defence can legitimately be negatived Court dealing with plea has to weigh material to conclude whether plea is acceptable - It is essentially a finding of fact - Further held, it has been significantly noted by both Courts below that attacks were not premediated - On contrary, for several hours, parties were discussing their respective stands (may be with some amount of verbal aggression), and that aspect has indelible importance while assessing basic issue regarding exercise of right of private defence.
