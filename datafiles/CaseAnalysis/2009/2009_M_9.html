(1) Manoj Narain Agrawal; (2) Shashi Agrawal and v (1) Shashi Agrawal and Others; (2) State of

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 490; (2009) 6 SCC 385; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1096; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1711; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 803; 2009(5) SCALE 535; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 976; 2010 (1) UC 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Revenue Record, Court's Jurisdiction

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 452, 323, 427, 506, 307 - Cr.P.C, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Jurisdiction to exempt the accused from personal appearance before the Court - Held, High Court can interfere with an order of summoning an accused by the Magistrate inter alia in the event if a finding is arrived at that the accused were being prosecuted mala fide and/or even if the allegations contained in the FIR are given face value and taken to be correct in their entirety, no case has been made out for taking cognizance of the offence - High Court should not have, for all intent and purport, issued the direction for grant of exemption from personal appearance - Appeals disposed of.
