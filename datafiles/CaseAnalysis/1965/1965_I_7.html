Union Of India And Anr. v India Fisheries (P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 185; AIR 1966 SC 35; 1965 (67) BomLR 944; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 669; 1965 (2) CompLJ 71; 1965 (2) ITJ 132; [1965] 57 ITR 331; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution - Corporate - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(c) and 226 - Indian Companies Act, 1913, ss. 179 and 180 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 48 and 49E - (A) Whether s. 49E is subject to Insolvency Rules contained in ss. 228 and 229 of Companies Act, 1913 ? - Held, on face of s.49E there is no doubt that this section is not subject to any other provision of law - But, this power cannot be exercised in a way to defeat provisions of Companies Act, 1913 - Effect of ss. 228 and 229, Companies Act, 1913 is, inter alia, that an unsecured creditor must prove his debts and all unsecured debts are to be paid pari passu - (B) Whether ITO and Commr. were equally in error? - Further held, ITO was in error in applying s. 49E and setting off refund due - Commr. was equally in error in affirming this order - Appeal dismissed.
