M. R. Prabhakar and others v Canara Bank and others

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 366; (2012) 9 SCC 671; 2013 (1) AWC 204; 2012 (135) FLR 694; JT 2012 (10) SC 178; 2013 (1) MahLJ 565; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 384; 2013 (1) MPLJ 242; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 1072; 2012 (6) SLR 619; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 2625

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Canara Bank (Employees') Pension Regulations, 1995, Canara Bank (Officers) Service Regulations, 1979

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, ss. 2(p) and 18(1) - Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1957, s. 58 - Canara Bank Pension Regulations, 1995 - Bank Officers Regulations, 1979, regns. 20(2) - Voluntary retirement - Resignation - Claim - Pension - Retiral benefits - Entitlement of - Appellants were officers of the Bank, who had resigned and stood relieved from their respective service between 24-7-1986 and 3-6-1993 - Indian Bank Association (IBA) representing 58 banks and their workmen had entered into a Memorandum of Settlement on 29-10-1993 u/ss. 2(p) and 18(1) of the 1947 Act r/w r. 58 of the 1957 Rules - IBA agreed to introduce a pension scheme in banks for the workmen employees in lieu of employers' contribution to the provident fund - Pension scheme agreed to by IBA was to be broadly based on Central Govt./Reserve Bank of India pattern, details of the scheme were worked out later - Joint Note was also made with regard to the introduction of pension as a second retiral benefit in lieu of Contributory Provident Fund (CPF) - Legality of the claim for pension in lieu of CPF - Hence instant appeals - Whether appellants were entitled for retiral benefits as they claimed -

Held, appellants, did not retire from the service, but resigned from the service - Appellants tried to build up a case that in the absence of a legal definition of 'voluntary retirement' or in the absence of legally prescribed consequences of 'resignation', it should be understood in the sense of voluntary relinquishment of service - It was pointed out that there could be no distinction between 'voluntary retirement' and 'resignation' and those expressions were to be understood in their ordinary literal sense - There was no ambiguity in the definition u/regn. 2(y) 1995 Regulations, which has statutorily brought in the 'voluntarily retirement' as 'retirement' - Though the concept of 'resignation' was well known in Service Jurisprudence, the same had not been brought within the definition of 'retirement' u/regn. 2(y) 1995 Regulations - Appellants, when tendered their letters of resignation, were governed by the 1979 regns. - Regn. 20(2) of 1979 Regulations, dealt with resignation from service and they tendered their resignation in the light of that provision - Appellants failed to show any pre-existing rights in their favour either in the Statutory Settlement/Joint Note or under the 1995 regns. - Appellants had resigned from service prior to 1-11-1993 and, therefore, were not covered by the statutory settlement, Joint Note dated 29-10-1993 and the 1995 Regulations - Appellants could not establish any pre-existing legal, statutory or fundamental rights in their favour to claim the benefit of 1995 Regulations - Consequently, the reliance placed by the appellants either on regns. 29 or 22 1995 Regulations in support of their contentions, could not be accepted, since they were not covered by the scheme of pension introduced by the banks with effect from 1-11-1993 - Appeals dismissed.
