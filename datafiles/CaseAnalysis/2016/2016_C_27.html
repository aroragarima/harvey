Central Industrial Security Force and others v Abrar Ali

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, T.S. Thakur, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 995; AIR 2017 SC 200; 2017 (152) FLR 431; JT 2017 (2) SC 213; 2016(12) SCALE 853

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Desertion, C.I.S.F. Rules, 1969, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - C.I.S.F. Rules, 1969, r. 36 - Disciplinary proceedings - Dismissal - Reinstatement - Respondent was appointed as a Constable in Central Industrial Security Force (CISF) - An inquiry was proposed u/r. 36 of the Rules for allegations of misconduct and misbehavior against respondent - Disciplinary Authority dismissed respondent from service - Punishment of dismissal from service imposed on respondent was found to be proportionate by both Appellate and Revisional Authorities - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned orders - HC allowed writ petition and directed respondent to be reinstated as Constable with notional seniority in his rank - Hence, instant appeal - Whether finding of Disciplinary Authorities holding respondent guilty of charges was justified and penalty of dismissal was proportionate.

Held, SC was in agreement with the findings and conclusion of Disciplinary Authority as confirmed by Appellate and Revisional Authorities on Charge no. 1. It was clear that respondent had intentionally disobeyed the orders of his superiors and deserted the Force for a period of 5 days. Such desertion was an act of gross misconduct and respondent deserves to be punished suitably. SC disagree with the finding of HC as respondent was not being tried again for previous misconduct. As the Respondent did not improve in spite of being punished earlier and had become habitual in indiscipline and disorderliness, Disciplinary Authority rightly found Charge no. 3 as proved. HC found that any penalty imposed under Charge no. 3 would amount to double jeopardy. SC was of view that HC ought not to have interfered with the order passed by Disciplinary Authority, penalty of dismissal from service was not commensurate with delinquency. Respondent was found guilty of desertion of the Force for a period of 5 days and not improving his conduct in spite of imposition of penalties on three occasions earlier. For the above delinquencies, the penalty of dismissal from service was excessive and harsh. However, penalty of compulsory retirement would meet the ends of justice. SC direct that respondent should be entitled for notional continuity of service till the date of completion of minimum service required to make him eligible for pension and would not be entitled for payment of salary and allowances for that period. Appeal allowed.
