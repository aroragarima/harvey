Sulochana Amma v Narayanan Nair

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 18; (1994) 2 SCC 14; AIR 1994 SC 152; AIR 1993 SCW 3792; 1993 (3) CCC 230; 1994 (53) ECR 195; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 785; JT 1993 (5) SC 448; 1993 (2) RentLR 589; 1993(3) SCALE 880; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 520; 1993 (2) UJ 774

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - CPC (Amendment) Act, 1976, s. 11, Explanation 8 - Whether, remedy of injunction is an equitable relief and in equity, doctrine of res judicata cannot be extended to a decree of a court of limited pecuniary jurisdiction? - Held, later suit of injunction to which appellant was a party also binds appellants, therefore, even decree founded on equitable relief in which issue was directly and substantially in issue decided, and attained finality would operate as res judicata in a subsequent suit based on title where same issue directly and substantially arises between parties - Appeal dismissed.
