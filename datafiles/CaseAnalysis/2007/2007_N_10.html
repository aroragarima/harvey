Indian Bank And Another v N. Venkatramani

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 864; (2007) 10 SCC 609; AIR 2007 SC 3159; 2007 (7) AWC 7329; 2007 (115) FLR 190; JT 2007 (10) SC 530; 2007 (3) LLJ 829; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 723; 2007(10) SCALE 475; 2007 (8) SCJ 72; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 570; 2007 (5) SLR 805

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Bank Employee's Pension Regulation 1995

Summary: Indian Bank Employee's Pension Regulation 1995 - Meaning of the term "broken period" for the purpose of grant of pension while implementing a voluntary retirement scheme - Held, an employee was entitled to pension provided he has completed the specified period of service - How such a period of service would be computed is a matter which is governed by the statute - It is one thing to say that a statute provides for completion of fifteen years of minimum service, but if a provision provides for measurement of the period, the same cannot be lost sight of - Provision of the Regulations which are beneficial in nature should be construed liberally - No case has been made out for interference - Appeal dismissed.
