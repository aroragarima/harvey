Ram Murti v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 500; (1970) 3 SCC 21; (1970) SCC (Cr) 371; AIR 1970 SC 1029; 1970 CRLJ 991; 1970 (6) DLT 537; 1970 (72) PLR 1000; 1970 UJ 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 366 and 376 - Evidence Act, 1972, ss. 35 and 133 - Rape - (A) Age of prosecutrix - Evidence as to - (1) Credibility - Unproved and unexhibited school certificate - In the light of provisions of s. 366 of IPC, 1860 and s. 35 of 1872 Act - (2) Importance ( for ss. 366, 376 of the Code ) - (B) Case u/s. 366 of the Code - Prosecutrix found to be used to sexual intercourse - Her statement that she was compelled, threatened or otherwise induced to go with accused who was alleged to have raped her without her consent - Statement to form base of conviction - Held, unproved and unexibited school certificate cannot be relied upon - Age of prosecutrix is always of importance - Her statement, must be corroborated in some material particulars from independent source - Appeal Allowed.
