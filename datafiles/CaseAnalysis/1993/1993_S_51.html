U. P. Rajya Krishi Utpadan Mandi Parishad and Others v Sanjiv Rajan

Supreme Court of India

29 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1012; (1993) Supp3 SCC 483; (1994) SCC (L&S) 67; 1993 (22) ALR 138; 1993 (25) ATC 764; 1993 (3) AWC 1703; 1993 (66) FLR 1058; JT 1993 (2) SC 550; 1993 (2) LLJ 95; 1993 (2) LLN 11; 1993 (2) MLJ(SC) 110; 1993 (4) SLR 543; 1993 (3) UPLBEC 1569

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Respondent employed as cashier of Agricultural Market Committee - Alleged that lakhs or rupees credited in accounts of Market Committee were not deposited in its bank account - Suspended from service - High Court quashed suspension order - Special auditors were appointed to audit accounts of Market Committee - Upon preliminary inquiry found that respondent either actively involved or done with his connivance and guilty of dereliction of duty for not scrutinising accounts - Again suspended - HC stayed order of suspension - Whether HC should have stayed suspension order? - Whether employees should or should not continue in their officer during period of inquiry is a matter to be assessed by authority concerned and ordinarily, Court should not interfere with orders of suspension unless they are passed mala fide and without there being even a prima facie evidence on record connecting the employees with the misconduct in question - No restriction on authority to pass a suspension order second time - Ordinarily, when there is an accusation of defalcation of monies, delinquent employees has to be kept away from establishment till charges are finally disposed of - No conclusion can be arrived at without examining entire record and hence advisable to allow disciplinary proceedings to continue unhindered - Held, HC misconstrued nature and purpose of power of suspension vested in management - Charges are grave and authorities come to conclusion that during disciplinary proceedings, officers should not continue in employment to enable them to conduct proceedings unhindered - Appeal allowed.
