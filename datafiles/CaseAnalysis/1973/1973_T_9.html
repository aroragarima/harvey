Kelvinator of India Limited v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 180; (1973) 2 SCC 551; AIR 1973 SC 2526; 1973 (2) CTR 406; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 463; 1973 (32) STC 629; 1973 TAXLR 2558

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax Levied, Inter State Sales, Liability, Distribution agreement

Summary: Sales Tax - Contract & Commercial - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Inter state sales - Distribution agreement - Agreement of sale - Tax levied - Liability - Appellant a registered dealer under the 1948 Act and the 1956 Act - Refrigerators and other articles were manufactured by appellant under a collaboration agreement with a company known as 'K' - Appellant entered into a distribution agreement with other companies too - Assessing authority vide its order held, that the transactions between the parties were inter State sales and liable to be taxed as such the movement of refrigerators from one place to another was held to be occasioned by the sales to the distributors - Sales tax-payable by assessee was assessed by the Sales Tax Officer to be Rs. 8,14,112.25 at the rate of 10 per cent of the 'transactions amounting to Rs. 81,41,142.45 - On review, application the tax liability was reduced to Rs. 1,59,691.19 - On appeal filed by appellant, Deputy Excise and Taxation Commissioner held that the distribution agreements were not contracts of sale - It was further held that the refrigerators were transported to D prior to their sale to distributors - Transactions in question were consequently held not to constitute interstate sales - Excise and Taxation Commissioner thereafter took suo motu action and held that as soon as the refrigerators were manufactured they were appropriated to contracts and that movement from F to D was under the agreement to sell - On further appeal Tribunal took the view that the agreements with the distributors were agreements of sale and that the sales in question were inter-State sales - Appellant thereafter filed review application but the same was dismissed by the Tribunal - Application was thereafter filed before the, Tribunal praying, inter alia, that the, questions of law be referred to the High Court - HC in answering the question in favour of department found that the machines moved from F to D in pursuance of agreements of sale which had been termed distribution agreements - Whether HC was justified in answering the question in favour of department - Held, there was no material on the record to show that an authority was given by distributors to appellant to appropriate the goods at F - There was no material to show that appellant was under the terms of contract authorised to do some act or thing with reference to the refrigerators which could not be done until the refrigerators were appropriated - Observation that 'until that time any act or thing done with reference to the goods towards appropriation by the party authorised was revocable' would show that there was no legal bar to the changing of name plates by appellant company till such time as orders with regard to the refrigerators were placed by distributors after inspection of those, refrigerators - Three agreements between appellant and distributors were merely agreements for the distribution of goods and were not agreements of sale between the parties - It could not be said that there was any movement of refrigerators from F to D under a contract of sale - Question in the circumstances was answered against the department - Transactions between appellant and the distributors did not, in SC's opinion, constitute sale in the course of inter-State trade or commerce - There was no liability to pay tax under the Act - Answer given by the HC to the question referred to it was discharged - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
