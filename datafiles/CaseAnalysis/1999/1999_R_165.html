Pawan Kumar Gupta v Rochiram Nagdeo

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1101; (1999) 4 SCC 243; 1999 (2) ACJ 1397; AIR 1999 SC 1823; 1999 (36) ALR 185; 1999 (3) AWC 1793; JT 1999 (3) SC 191; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 62; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 767

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, s. 11 - Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, s. 2(a) - Whether dismissal of earlier suit on account of extinguishment of cause of action or any other similar cause would act as Res Judicata ? - Held, if dismissal of prior suit was on a ground affecting maintainability of suit any finding in judgment adverse to defendant would not operate as res judicata in a subsequent suit - But if dismissal of suit was on account of extinguishment of cause of action or any other similar cause a decision made in suit on a vital issue involved therein would operate as res judicata in a subsequent suit between same parties - It is for defendant in such a suit to choose whether judgment should be appealed against or not - If he does not choose to file appeal he cannot thereby avert bar of res judicata in subsequent suit - (B) Meaning of ' Paid and Provided' in description of a benami transaction u/s. 2(a) Act - Held, word 'provided' in said clause cannot be construed in relation to source or sources from which real transferee made up funds for buying sale consideration - Words 'paid or provided' are disjunctively employed in clause and each has to be tagged with word 'consideration ' - Correct interpretation would be to read it as 'consideration paid or consideration provided ' - Appeal allowed.
