Sadananda Halo and others v Momtaz Ali Sheikh and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2608; (2008) 4 SCC 619; JT 2008 (3) SC 74; 2008(3) SCALE 209; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 497; 2008 (2) SLR 697

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: RVSP Act, 1978

Summary: (A) Service - Selection process - Legality of - Appellants conducted a District wise recruitment drive for the post of Armed Constables - Respondent/unsuccessful candidates challenged the selection list prepared by the appellants - Single Judge set aside the selection list on the ground that appellants had interviewed more than 250 candidates per day - Appellants challenged the Single Judge order before DB - DB confirmed Single Judge order - Whether impugned selection process in valid in law - Held, HC cannot give any concrete finding that the selection board could interview only 250 candidates per day and not more - Selection cannot be rejected only on the reason that selected candidates got higher marks in viva-voce - Number of candidates interviewed on a particular day exceeded above 250 cannot make the selection bad - Courts below have not recorded any finding that they found any mala fides on the part of any of the State officials who headed the interviews - No deviation from the rules or no inherent defect in the selection process which would render the whole selection illegal have either been alleged or proved - A mere expression of doubts only on the ground of large number of candidates appearing and they are not being objectively and properly tested without any further material cannot by itself render the whole selection process illegal - Selection of large number of candidates could not have been set aside on the basis of sample survey by HC, therefore, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Writ jurisdiction of HC - Scope of - Held, a roving inquiry on the factual aspect is not permissible in writ proceedings - HC cannot place itself into a position of a fact finding commission - HC should restrict itself to the pleadings in the writ petition and the say of the parties - HC cannot convert a writ petition into a public interest litigation without any justification - Appeal allowed.
