Messrs Shriram City Union Finance Corporation Limited v Rama Mishra

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3526; (2002) 9 SCC 613; AIR 2002 SC 2402

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata, Suit For Injunction

Summary: Civil Procedure - Suit for injunction - Territorial jurisdiction - Maintainability - Respondent obtained bus on lease, lease period came to end and respondent defaulted in making payment of installments and in spite of demand payment was not been made - Respondent filed suit for injunction in Court of Civil Judge of Bhubaneswar (junior division) and suit was dismissed - Civil Judge of Trial Court of Bhubaneswar through its order allowed respondent's application for repayment of outstanding installment to appellant - Appellant preferred appeal before District Judge who allowed appeal by setting aside judgment of trial Court - HC allowed revision of respondent, confirming order passed by trial Court - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Court of Calcutta or Court of Bhubaneswar had jurisdiction to decide issues in dispute - Held, impugned order of HC and order passed by appellate Court arises out of order passed by Civil Judge, Bhubaneswar - There was difference between inherent lack of jurisdiction of any Court on account of some statute and other where parties through agreement bind themselves to have their dispute decided by any one of Court having jurisdiction - However, party was bound either by provision of the Constitution, statutory provisions or any rule or under terms of any contract which was not against public policy - Therefore, it was open for party for his convenience to fix jurisdiction of any competent Court to have their dispute adjudicated by that Court alone and if one or more Court had jurisdiction to try any suit, it was open for parties to choose any one of two competent Courts to decide their dispute - Though, in case parties under their own agreement expressly agrees that their dispute should be tried by only one of them then party could only file suit in that Court alone to which they have so agree - Thus, parties have bound themselves that any matter arising between them under said contract, it was Courts in Calcutta alone which would have jurisdiction u/cl. 34 of lease agreement - Hence, suit filed by respondent in Civil Court at Bhubaneshwar would not be valid, in view of said agreement - Appeal allowed.
