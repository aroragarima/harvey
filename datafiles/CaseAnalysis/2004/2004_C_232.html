Hindustan Lever Limited v Contract Laghu Udyog Kamgar Union and others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1994; (2004) 13 SCC 501

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Employee - Benefits - Claim - Validity - Respondents claimed to be employees of appellant - Industrial Court dismissed complaint of respondents on finding that respondents were unable to establish relationship of employer and employee as claimed by them - HC quashed decision of Industrial Court and declared that respondents were employees of appellant and entitled to all benefits, as claimed for by them and also to pay all arrears of wages and benefits on such basis - DB allowed appeal of appellant and set aside decision of Single Judge - Moreover, DB directed State to refer disputes raised by respondent no. 1 to Industrial Tribunal u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act - Appellant challenged direction of DB to extent that it had directed reference u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act and also with regard to interim order passed by DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interim order and direction of DB to extent that it had directed reference u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act was sustainable - Held, parties agreed that complaint of respondents would be treated as statement of claim and written statement to complaint made by appellant would be treated as its affidavit-in-reply to claim statement - Pending determination of dispute by Industrial Tribunal, by way of interim arrangement, DB had directed that direction issued by Single Judge to continue subject to final outcome of reference - Hearing before Industrial Tribunal had been proceeded with over period of time and order under appeal had to large extent worked itself out - Hence, any observation of various fora in earlier round of proceedings would not in any way influence Industrial Tribunal to dispose of and deal with reference in such manner as it might think fit - Appeal disposed of .
