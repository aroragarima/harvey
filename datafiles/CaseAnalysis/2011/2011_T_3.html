Wakkar and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 76; (2011) 3 SCC 306; 2011 CRLJ 1639; JT 2011 (2) SC 502; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 26; 2011(2) SCALE 198

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 201 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25(4) - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellants for murder and sentenced them to death - HC, in appeal, confirmed the conviction, however, commuted the death sentence to that of imprisonment for life - Whether the circumstances and the chain of events conclusively established the involvement of all the appellants in committing the crime of murder of the deceased - Held, prosecution clinchingly established the strong circumstance that the dead body was found in the shop belonging to appellants where some business was being carried on by them - PW3, wife of the deceased, who in clear and categorical terms stated that the deceased received a telephone call on the fateful day from 1st appellant to come over to the shop - PW3's evidence received complete corroboration from the statement of PW 1 that he was informed by PW3 that the deceased left home on receiving telephone call from the 1st appellant but did not return thereafter - Evidence of PW3 read together with that of PW1's statement, unerringly established that the deceased had gone to the shop belonging to the appellants on receiving a telephone call from 1st appellant - Murder of the deceased took place during that fateful night only at the shop belonging to the appellants where all the appellants were seen coming from the scene of offence and hence, a very strong circumstance which unerringly pointed towards the culpability and involvement of appellants in the instant case - HC on re-appreciation of the evidence available on record, believed the evidence of PWs 1 and 3 and rightly taken circumstances into consideration leading to an irresistible conclusion that appellants were involved in the commission of the crime - Trial Court and as well as the HC had properly appreciated the evidence of PW1 and there was nothing on record to disagree with the view taken by the Courts below - Further, blood stained weapons allegedly used in the crime were recovered on the disclosure made by the appellants to the police - Trial Court as well as the HC took into consideration the recovery of articles used in the commission of offence together with other incriminating circumstances brought on record by the prosecution - Findings recorded by the Trial Court and confirmed in appeal by the HC finding the appellants to be guilty of the charged offences along with 1st appellant were unassailable and hence confirmed - HC, as far as the sentence was concerned, having taken all relevant factors into consideration rightly came to the conclusion that the case was not one which fell in the category of 'rarest of rare' - Conclusion arrived at by the HC and its decision to award life imprisonment instead of death sentence, therefore, was proper - Appeal dismissed.
