Jodhan v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 381; 2015 CRLJ 3291; JT 2015 (5) SC 133; 2015(4) SCALE 577; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Material Witness, Interested Witnesses, Explosive Substances Act, 1908

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 323, 324 - Alleged Attack - Conviction - Justification - On day of incident, when PW 7 was at home, accused appellants armed with weapons assaulted PW 7 and his family members - Further, bomb was thrown by accused on deceased - FIR was lodged against appellant accused - Trial court framed charges u/ss. 302, 323, 324 read with ss. 149 and 148 of IPC against all accused persons and additional charge u/s. 324 of IPC against accused and u/ss. 3 and 4 of Act, however, appellant accused was acquitted as it expressed doubt about testimony of witnesses who had deposed in favour of prosecution, referred to cases pending in Court, free fight between parties, absence of satisfactory explanation by prosecution as regards injuries sustained by accused persons, absence of independent evidence on record, hence, disbelieved story of prosecution and acquitted all accused persons - However, acquittal order passed by Trial Court is reversed by HC and appellants are convicted - Hence, present appeal.

Held, there is absolutely no inconsistency which will compel court of law to discard version of witness. SC held that Trial Court has attached immense emphasis to such omissions and contradictions which, according to HC which is accepted by SC are absolutely insignificant and trivial. Further, submission that when other witnesses have turned hostile, version of these witnesses also should have been discredited does not commend acceptance, for there is no rule of evidence that testimony of interested witnesses is to be rejected solely because other independent witnesses who have been cited by prosecution have turned hostile. SC held that testimony of injured witness has its own significance and it has to be placed reliance upon unless there are strong grounds for rejection of his evidence on basis of major contradictions and inconsistencies. Further, injured witness has been conferred special status in law and injury sustained by him is inbuilt- guarantee of his presence at place of occurrence. Further, prosecution has been able to establish not only appellant's presence but also his active participation as member of unlawful assembly. He might not have thrown bomb at deceased, but thereby he does not cease to be member of unlawful assembly as understood within ambit of s. 149 of IPC and there is ample evidence on record to safely conclude that all accused persons who have been convicted by HC had formed unlawful assembly and there was common object to assault deceased who succumbed to injuries inflicted on him. Further, HC on scrutiny of evidence has found that there is ample evidence on record to prove that accused persons were aggressors and it is they who arrived at place of occurrence and accused hurled abuses and threw handmade bomb on chest of deceased. Further, witnesses have deposed about genesis of occurrence, participation and involvement of accused persons in crime and injuries inflicted on deceased, and on each of them, hence, non-examination of any other witnesses who might have been available on scene of occurrence, would not make case of prosecution unacceptable. Hence, conviction order passed by HC reversing acquittal order passed by Trial Court is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.
