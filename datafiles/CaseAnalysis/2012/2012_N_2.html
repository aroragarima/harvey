Union of India and others v N. R. Parmar and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 490; (2012) 13 SCC 340; 2013 (3) AWC 2313; 2013 (136) FLR 134; JT 2012 (12) SC 99; 2012(11) SCALE 437; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 555; 2013 (1) SLR 435; [2013] 212 TAXMAN 97

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Promotion - Recruitment - Appointment Date - Seniority Basis - Sustainable - Appointment to cadre of Income Tax Inspectors in Income - Tax Department was made by way of promotion, by direct recruitment in ratio of 2:1 respectively, 66-2/3 by promotion and 33-1/3 by direct recruitment - Vacancies for year 1993-94 which were identified to be filled up by way of promotion were referred to Departmental Promotion Committee, whereas, those identified to be filled up by direct recruitment, were referred to Staff Selection Commission, based on recommendations made by DPC, Income-tax Department promotions were made, all these promotions were ordered against promote vacancies - Series of legal battles between Promotee Income Tax Inspectors and Direct recruit Income Tax Inspectors were held before CAT - Tribunal held that seniority of direct recruits would have to be determined with reference to date of their actual appointment and not date of arising of direct recruit vacancies, or date of initiation of process of recruitment, or date when SSC had made recommendations for filling up direct recruit vacancies, which were inconsequential for determination of seniority of direct recruits - Writ Petitions filed before HC, were disposed of with common order setting aside orders passed by Tribunal and remitted matter back for further consideration - Department held that seniority dispute between promotee and direct recruit Income Tax Inspectors of Income Tax Department was liable to be determined on basis of office memoranda dated 7-2-1986 and 3-7-1986, read with the clarificatory office memoranda and office notes - Whether Department was justified in determining seniority on basis of office memoranda and office notes - Held, advertised vacancies were filled up in original/first examination/selection conducted, none of direct recruit Income Tax Inspectors could be stated to be occupying carried forward vacancies, or vacancies which came to be filled up by a later examination/selection process - Facts only reveal, that examination and selection process of direct recruits could not be completed within recruitment year itself, for this, modification/amendment in manner of determining inter-se seniority between direct recruits and promotees, carried out through OM dt. 7-2-1986, and compilation of instructions pertaining to seniority in OM dt. 3-7-1986, leave no room for any doubt, that 'rotation of quotas' principle, would be fully applicable to direct recruits in present controversy, direct recruits herein would therefore have to be interspaced with promotees of same recruitment year - Appeals allowed.
