State Bank Of India & Ors. v Jaspal Kaur

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 60; (2007) 9 SCC 571; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 578; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 414; 2007 (5) AWC 4480; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 244; 2007 (1) CLR 947; 2007 (1) ESC 66; 2007 (112) FLR 1037; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 300; JT 2007 (3) SC 35; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 709; 2007 (2) LLJ 385; 2007 (1) LLN 613; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 396; 2007 (146) PLR 754; 2007(2) SCALE 397; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 101; 2007 (2) SLR 1; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1424

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Appointment of dependents of deceased employees on compassionate grounds - Declined appointment to the respondent on compassionate ground after taking into consideration the financial condition of the family - High Court held that Rs. 4, 57, 607/- granted to the family on account of terminal benefits could not be accepted to be sufficient - Appeal against - Held, major criterion while appointing a person on compassionate grounds should be the financial condition of the family the deceased person left behind - Unless the financial condition is entirely penury, such appointments cannot be made - Financial condition of the respondents family is not one of destitution - Appeal allowed.
