Collector of Customs and Excise, Cochin and Others v A. S. Bava

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 243; AIR 1968 SC 13; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 333; 1967 KLT 935; 1968 (1) SCJ 658; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s. 129 - Excise and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 12 and 36 - Central Excise and Salt Rules, 1944, r. 40 - Notification No. 68/63 D/- 04-05-1963 - (A) Whether petitions under art. 226 were not maintainable as petitioner did not avail himself of remedy of revision provided by s. 36 'of Excise and Salt Act, 1944? - Held, it is settled that existence of a remedy by way of revision does not bar jurisdiction of High Court to entertain a petition u/art. 226 - (B) Whether petitioner having availed of remedy u/s. 12 of Customs Act was debarred from challenging impugned notification, D/- 04-05-1963? - Further held, appellants did not contended that petitions under art. 226 were not maintainable as if petitioner had not applied for dispensation of deposit of duty - (C) Whether provisions of s. 129 of Customs Act can be said to be provisions relating to procedure in relation to appeals' within s. 12 of Excise and Salt Act? - Further held, right that amended section gives is certainly less than right which was available before - S. 129 of Customs Act whittles down substantive right of appeal and accordingly it cannot be regarded as 'procedure relating to appeals' within s. 12 of Excise Act - Appeals dismissed.
