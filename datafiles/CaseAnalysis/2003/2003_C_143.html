Alpine Industries v Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

14 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 10; (2003) 3 SCC 111; AIR 2003 SC 935; 2003 (85) ECC 491; 2003 (107) ECR 451; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 16; JT 2003 (1) SC 130; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 361; 2003 (54) RLT 369; 2003(1) SCALE 118; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 313; 2003 (1) SLT 319; 2003 (131) STC 9; 2003 (1) Supreme 353

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944 - S. 35L(b) - The Tribunal by majority opinion held that the appellant's manufactured product with trade name 'Lip Salve' is classifiable for payment of excise duty under Heading 33.04 of the Act as 'a preparation for care of skin' and not as a 'medicament' under Heading 30.03 of the Act. - Held, in interpreting tariff entries in taxation statute like Excise Act where the primary object is to raise revenue and for that purpose various products are differently classified, the entries are not to be understood in its scientific and technical meaning. The terms and expressions used in tariff have to be understood by their popular meaning that is the meaning that is attached to them by those using the product. - The product 'Lip Salve' is essentially a preparation for protection of lips and skin and is not 'medicament'.
