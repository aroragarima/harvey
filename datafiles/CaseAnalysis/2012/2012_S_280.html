Dr. Mehmood Nayyar Azam v State of Chattisgarh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 365; (2012) 8 SCC 1; 2012 ACJ 2239; AIR 2012 SC 2573; 2012 (5) AWC 4353; 2012 CRLJ 3934; 2012 (4) CTC 781; 2012 (2) OLR 525; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 925; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Human Rights; Tort

Keywords: Dignity

Summary: Constitution - Human Rights - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 427,379 - Electricity Act, 2003 - Judicial Remand - Harassment - Self-Humiliating - Violation of Human Rights - Compensation - Validity - Appellant was a social activist bring awareness among downtrodden people about their rights, which was not appreciated by some sect of people, hence to silence him false cases were filed and appellant was arrested for alleged offence under 420, 427,379 of IPC and under provisions of Act - Appellant was sent to judicial remand by Magistrate, instead of taking him to jail, next day, police brought him to police station, and self-humiliating words were written on placard and he was asked to hold it and photographs were taken and photographs were circulated in general public and were also filed by one of respondents in a revenue proceeding - Appellant filed writ petition for compensation alleging that action of respondent was a complete transgression of human rights which affected his fundamental right and right to live with dignity as enshrines u/art. 21 of Constitution - HC, held that appellant was harassed but did not pass any compensation - Aggrieved instant appeal - whether Court should grant compensation to appellant on bedrock that he had been harassed in police custody or whether appellant should be asked to initiate a civil action for grant of damages on foundation that he had been defamed - Held, it was luculent that appellant had undergone mental torture at hands of insensible police officials, He might have agitated to ameliorate cause of poor and downtrodden, but, social humiliation that had been meted out to him was quite capable of destroying heart of his philosophy - It had been said that philosophy had power to sustain a man's courage, but courage was based on self-respect and when self-respect was dented, it was difficult even for a very strong minded person to maintain that courage - Initial invincible mind paves path of corrosion, as was perceptible, mindset of protectors of law appeared to cause torment and insult and tyrannize man who was helpless in custody, there could be no trace of doubt that he was bound to develop stress disorder and anxiety which destroyed brightness and strength of will power -When torment was added, it creates commotion in mind and slow poisons get activated, inhuman treatment could be well visualized when appellant came out from custody and witnessed his photograph being circulated with self-condemning words written on it which withers away very essence of life as enshrined u/art. 21 of Constitution, taking note of totality of facts and circumstances, SC, disposed to think that a sum of Rs.5.00 lacs should be granted towards compensation to appellant - Appeal disposed of.
