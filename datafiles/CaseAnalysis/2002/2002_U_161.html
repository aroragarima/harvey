Shyama Charan Agarwala and Sons v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1734; (2002) 6 SCC 201; AIR 2002 SC 2659; 2002 (2) ARBLR 641; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 619; JT 2002 (5) SC 444; 2002 (2) RAJ 628; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 515; 2002(5) SCALE 193; 2002 (4) SLT 400; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 148; 2002 (5) Supreme 15

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Proceedings

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 30, 33 and 39 - Whether High Court decide questions on interpretation of contract and interfere with an arbitral award? - Held, only in some cases - From a reading of decisions of Supreme Court referred it is clear that when an award is made plainly contrary to terms of contract not by misinterpretation but which is plainly contrary to terms of contract it would certainly lead to an inference that there is an error apparent on face of award which results in jurisdictional error in award - In such a case courts can certainly interfere with award made by arbitrator - Relied on N Chellappan v Secy,Kerala SEB 1974 Indlaw SC 197 - Further held, approach of HC cannot be said to be contrary to well-settled principles governing scope of interference with an award of arbitrator under Act - Though HC may not be justified in observing that arbitrator took undue interest in trying to ascertain classification of strata, HC is well justified in holding that view taken by arbitrator is not at all a reasonably possible view and in fact he ignored one of relevant clauses - Order accordingly.
