Messrs Lipton Limited and Another v Their Employees

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 133; AIR 1959 SC 676; 1959 (1) LLJ 431; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 150

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Wage, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Living Wage, Available Surplus, Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules, 1947, Industrial Disputes (Central) Rules

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Bonus - Fixation Grades and Scales of Pay - Maintainability - Appellant was Company - Respondents were employees of appellant company formed union and represented for increase in pay, which was not acceded by appellant - Respondents filed conciliation proceedings before Appellant which proved futile - Aggrieved respondents referred matter before Tribunal which disallowed claim of bonus and allowed increase of 20 percent in fixation of grades and scales of pay - Aggrieved appellant and respondents filed appeal before Appellate Tribunal which upheld decision of lower Tribunal and awarded extra 2 months salary as bonus - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of Tribunal was justifiable - Held, Tribunal had rightly pointed out that moderate increase in wage scale proposed by it would only be a very small fraction of overall cost of production of packet of tea and would had very little repercussion in its price - Further, only point which required consideration was question of date from which new scales of pay should come into effect - Tribunal fixed 1-1- 1954, on ground that Union had presented its charter of demands to appellant for first time towards end of December 1953 - Therefore, unable to agree with Tribunals below that circumstance that charter of demands was presented in December 1953 was good ground for giving retrospective effect to new scales of pay - Thus, charter of demands presented by Union consisted of 20 items and in matter of wage scale what Union demanded was in some cases more than 50 to 75 perecent increase on existing scales of pay - Demands were exorbitant and management was justified in refusing to accept demands in toto - Hence, unable to agree that retrospective effect should be given to new scales of pay from 1-1- 1954 , award was made on 18-8- 1955, and it was published on 6-10- 1955 and it would be more just to bring new scales of pay with effect from 1-11- 1955 - Appeals disposed of.
