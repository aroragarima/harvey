D.R. Yadav and Another v R.K. Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 520; (2003) 7 SCC 110; (2003) SCC (L&S) 993; AIR 2003 SC 3935; 2003 (4) AWC 2600; 2003 (4) ESC 427; 2003 (98) FLR 744; JT 2003 (5) SC 555; 2003 LabIC 2634; 2003 (3) LLN 909; 2003(5) SCALE 307; 2004 (1) SLJ 19; 2003 (4) SLR 753; 2003 (4) SLT 508; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 583; 2003 (5) Supreme 57; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1903

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973, ss.4, 5(2), 55, 59(1), 59(3), 5A, 5A(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 309 - Uttar Pradesh Planning and Development Ordinance, 1973 - Uttar Pradesh Municipal Corporations Act, 1959 - Uttar Pradesh (Regulation of Building Operations) Act, 1958 - Uttar Pradesh Palika (Centralized) Services Rules, 1956 - Uttar Pradesh Government Servants Seniority rules, 1991, r. 6 - Uttar Pradesh Development Authorities Centralised Service Rules, 1985, rr. 28, 3, 7, 7(1) - Two different rules relating to determination of seniority were operating in field - (1) Dispute of inter se seniority - (2) applicability of general rules vis-is special rules - Held, (1) case of appellants vis-is respondent no 2 although may be governed by special rules, in terms of r 7, same has to be determined on criteria of continuous length of service including service rendered in a Development Authority, Nagar Mahapalika, Nagarpalika or Improvement Trust on similar posts - Further held, appellants, it will bear repetition to state, although were promoted at one point of time on purely ad hoc basis to posts of Assistant Executive Engineer, as said posts even in their parent authority were not of similar type, same would not be relevant for purpose of determining inter se seniority - If rule of continuous service in same and similar posts were to be resorted to, date of initial appointment would be a relevant criteria - (2) rules made under proviso to art.309 are for a transitory period and same would give way to special rules once framed - However, if a statute or rules made thereunder was/were already operating in field, general rules made under proviso to art.309 would not apply to services created thereunder - In event of two conflicting rules operating in same field, doctrine of generalia specialiabus non derogant shall apply.
