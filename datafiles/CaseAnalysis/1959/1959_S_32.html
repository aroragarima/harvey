State of Delhi v Shri Ram Lohia

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 270; AIR 1960 SC 490; 1960 CRLJ 679

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Criminal - Indian Official Secrets Act, ss. 5(4)and 5(2) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 164 - Conviction by Trial Court - Confirmed by Addl. Sessions Judge - Reversed by High Court - Reversal whether proper? - HC reversed conviction on ground that only evidence upon which respondent-accused was convicted on evidence of PW 10 - Court observed that evidence of witness PW 10, if is trustworthy, then a conviction can be sustained on it, otherwise not, and this is irrespective of correctness of finding of HC - Issue is whether evidence of PW 10 is trustworthy - Addl. Sessions Judge relied on statement u/s. 164 also while upholding conviction - Statement u/s. 164 referred to was not specifically put to PW 10 even to contradict him - Statements recorded u/s. 164 are not substantive evidence in a case and cannot be made use of except to corroborate or contradict witness - Addl. Sessions Judge thus committed error in law in using statement of PW 10 u/s. 164 - Court observed that if that statement is excluded from consideration it is a matter of pure guess that PW 10 had been won over after his examination-chief was over - Held, from statements given by PW 10 that they clearly demonstrates him to be utterly untrustworthy witness - Conviction cannot be based on it - Impugned judgment of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
