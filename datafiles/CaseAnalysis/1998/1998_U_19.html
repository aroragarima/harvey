Union of India and Others v Udai Narain

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1592; (1998) 5 SCC 535; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1418; 1998 (4) LLN 641; 1998 (9) SLT 70

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - CCS (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10(1)(b) - Respondent serving as Addl. Commr. Central Excise was arrested on allegation of demanding and acceptance of bribe - Thereafter, he was released on bail but was placed under suspension u/r. 10(1)(b) - Respondent application for revocation of suspension was allowed holding that ground mentioned in suspension order 'no longer survives' - Whether suspension is liable to be quashed? - Held, delinquent cannot be considered to be any better off after charge-sheet had been filed against him in Court after completion of investigation, than his position during investigation of case itself - Further held, sanction for prosecution has already been obtained and case has been fixed for framing of charges by trial court - Thus, view taken by Tribunal in impugned order is not sustainable and order of suspension was not liable to be quashed on ground that case was neither at stage of investigation or enquiry or trial - Appeal disposed of.
