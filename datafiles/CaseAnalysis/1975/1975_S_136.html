Pedda Narayana and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 625; (1975) 4 SCC 153; (1975) SCC (Cr) 427; 1975 (12) ACC 201; AIR 1975 SC 1252; 1975 CRLJ 1062; 1975 CRLR 335; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 84

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 154, 162, 174 and 417 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 299, 300 and 364 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Supreme Court Act, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134 - Appeal against acquittal to Supreme Court - Untenable reasons given for acquittal by lower court - Admissibility of evidence - Held, use of a statement recorded by police during investigation is not at all admissible in evidence according to s. 162, CrPC, 1973 - CrPC, 1973, s. 174 - Scope of proceedings is very limited - Details of overt acts in inquest report not necessary - Object was to determine if a person has died under suspicious circumstances - In circumstances, held, it was not possible for SC to convict accused for first time u/s. 201, IPC.
