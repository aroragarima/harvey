National Fertilizers Ltd. v Tuncay Alankus & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 192; (2013) 9 SCC 600; AIR 2013 SC 1299; JT 2013 (8) SC 375; 2013 (4) MLJ 453; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 168; 2013(5) SCALE 255; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Violation of contract, Restrain from withdrawing amount - Contempt of Court - Respondent was manager of K/Turkish company - Petitioner

Summary: International - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120 B, 409 and 420 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 11, 13(1)(c) and (d) - Violation of contract - Criminal proceedings - Restrain from withdrawing amount - Contempt of Court - Respondent was manager of K/Turkish company - Petitioner company was entered into agreement dt.9-11-1995 with K, which presented itself as producer of urea - In terms of contract, petitioner company was to pay to K full contract value in advance by way of two remittances i.e., US$ 380,000 towards insurance premium before entering into contract and US$ 37,620,000 towards cost of urea after entering into contract - Despite making full payment of contract money, petitioner did not received a single grain of urea and it later came to light that insurance cover taken out in connection with contract did not provided any protection against loss suffered by petitioner - CBI lodged FIR u/ss. 120 B, 409 and 420 IPC r/w. ss. 7, 11, 13(1)(c) and (d) of the 1988 Act against respondent No.1 - Prosecution filed petition for examining 63 persons, living in 10 different countries, through video-conferencing - Trial Court gave permission for examination of only 6 out of 63 witnesses - Respondent filed revision before HC, which was allowed him to examine, in addition to 6 witnesses allowed by Trial Court, 21 more witnesses, of whom a list was placed on record before HC - Petitioner filed leave petition before SC, which was set aside the order of HC and remanded the matter for a fresh consideration by HC - SC by order dt.4-9-2006 held that respondent was restrained from withdrawing amount from accounts in Swiss Bank till decision of matter - Hence, instant contempt petition was filed by petitioner alleging violation of order dt.4-9-2006 - Whether respondent had committed contempt of Court by withdrawing money from his bank account in Swiss bank in violation of Court's order dt.4-9-2006 - Held, it was clear that attachment against respondent's account was lifted on 1-6-2006 when Swiss SC dismissed petitioner's appeal and petitioner was able to obtain next attachment order only on 15-12- 2006 - There was, thus, a period of slightly over 6 months when there was no attachment order in respect of account - According to bank's statement, amount was withdrawn on 21-6-2006 (i.e., 20 days after attachment order was lifted) and account was closed on 25-7-2006 - It was clear that on 4-9-2006 when SC passed order prohibiting respondent No.1 from withdrawing any money from account there was actually no money in the account - There could be no question of committing any violation of Court's order by respondent No.1 - It would not be reasonable to hold that respondent withdrew large amounts from his account with Pictet in violation of Court's orders - Petition dismissed.
