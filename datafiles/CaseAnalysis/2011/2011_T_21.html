Tatipamula Naga Raju v Pattem Padmavathi

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 124; (2011) 4 SCC 726; AIR 2011 SC 1499; 2011(3) ALL MR 957; 2011 (86) ALR 66; 2011 (3) AWC 2303; 2011 (2) BC 563; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 652; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 228; 2011 (2) CTC 806; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1738; JT 2011 (5) SC 370; 2011 (164) PLR 115; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 308; 2011 (113) RD 291; 2011 (3) RLW 1892; 2011(2) SCALE 727

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Suit for recovery of money - Tampering of Promissory note - Respondent filed a suit for recovery of money against appellant on basis of a Promissory note - Appellant contended that he had already settled due amount with help of mediators, however, respondent did not return back Promissory note and had tampered the same for filing instant suit - Trial Court, on basis of mediators and handwriting expert's evidence, dismissed suit holding that Promissory note was tampered with by appellant - Respondent filed appeal, which was allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether Trial Court order was justifiable - Held, evidence of mediators and hand-writing expert was duly considered and appreciated by Trial Court and Trial Court had come to a right conclusion - HC should not have come to conclusion that amount was payable by appellant especially when there was an expert's evidence that promissory note was tampered with - Mediators deposed to effect that Promissory notes was not returned to appellant by respondent - In such a set of circumstances, respondent ought not to have been saddled with a liability to pay amount in pursuance of tampered Promissory note for which no consideration had ever passed from respondent to appellant - Trial Court order was restored - Appeal allowed.
