Village Panchayat, Calangute v The Additional Director Of Panchayat-Ii And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 186; (2012) 7 SCC 550; AIR 2012 SC 2697; 2012(4) ALL MR 494; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 481; 2012(6) SCALE 45; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 277

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Goa Panchayat Raj Act, 1994

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, 1992 - Goa Panchayat Raj Act, 1994 , ss. 3, 8, 64, 66 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 40, 136, 226, 227 - Mineral Concession Rules, 1960 - Respondent no. 4/company was granted permission by appellant/Panchayat for raising construction on property - Company said to have illegally constructed wall and blocked access to water well situated and chapel as also existing water drains - Appellant Panchayat revoked occupancy certificate and cancelled permission granted to company for raising construction - Resolution was recalled apparently because rules of natural justice had not been followed - Thereafter, Sarpanch issued notice u/s.64 and directed company to stop further construction - Company challenged notice and succeeded in persuading respondent No.1 to pass an ex-parte interim order - Application made by company for permission to use property for running a guest house was rejected - In both cases, respondent No.1 set aside resolutions passed by appellant as also notice issued by Sarpanch - Further, Block Development Officer based on complaint made by residents passed an injunction order initially and later on dismissed complaint - Appellant challenged orders passed by respondent No.1 and order passed by Block Development Officer in Writ Petition No.16/2010 on ground that respondent No.1 had no jurisdiction to entertain appeal against notice issued u/s. 64 of Act and such notice could not be stayed u/s. 178 - Pendency of Writ Petition No.16/2010, respondent No.1 passed final order in Panchayat Appeal No. 12/2009 and directed appellant to reconsider application made by the company for grant of permission to use the property for running a guest house - Appellant challenged that order in Writ Petition No. 312/2010 - Single Judge relied upon the order passed in Writ Petition No.620/2009 and dismissed both as not maintainable -Hence, instant appeals - Whether Village Panchayat established u/s. 3 of Act, or any other statutory dispensation existing prior to the enactment of Act had locus to file a petition u/art. 226 and/or 227 of Constitution for setting aside an order passed by designated officer exercising the power of an appellate authority qua the Action/decision/resolution of the Village Panchayat - Held, orders passed by respondent No.1 did not refer to particular provision under which the concerned officer was exercising appellate power - Power vested in appellate authority u/s. 201could not have been exercised because source of power of resolutions passed by appellant and notice issued by Sarpanch could not be traced in ss. 76, 77, 84, 104 and 105 of Act - Respondent No.1 could not be said to have exercised power u/s. 201-A because under that provision, only Block Development Officer was competent to entertain an appeal in a miscellaneous matter which was dealt with by Panchayat or Village Panchayat Secretary or Sarpanch and against which no appeal had been specifically provided under the Act - Therefore, it was reasonable to infer that respondent No.1 had exercised power u/s. 178(1) - However, instead of suspending execution of resolutions passed by appellant or notice issued by Sarpanch and sending matter to State Govt. for confirmation, concerned officer suo-moto annulled resolutions and notice by assuming that he had the power to do so - It was thus evident that while appellant and Sarpanch had exercised their respective powers in public interest, respondent No.1 nullified that exercise because he felt that resolution/action was contrary to law and was unjustified - While exercising power under Act, Panchayat was not acting as a subordinate to respondent No.1 but as a body corporate in terms of s. 8 of Act - Hence, it had locus to challenge orders passed by respondent No.1 and HC was clearly in error holding that writ petition was not maintainable - Appeals allowed.
