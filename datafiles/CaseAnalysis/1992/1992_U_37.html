K.T.M.S. Mohd. And Anr. v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1143; (1992) 3 SCC 178; (1992) SCC (Cr) 572; AIR 1992 SC 1831; 1992 (3) CCR 439; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 321; 1992 (2) Crimes 314; 1992 CRLJ 2781; 1992 (108) CTR 84; [1992] 197 ITR 196; JT 1992 (3) SC 129; 1992(1) SCALE 1006; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 879; [1992] 65 TAXMAN 130

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 39 and 40 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 and 228 - Income Tax Act 1961, s. 277 (A) Applicability of putting ss. 39 or 40 of FERA under ambit as 'judicial proceeding' within meaning of ss. 193 and 228 of IPC - Held, every investigation or proceeding u/s. 40 is deemed to be a judicial proceeding by a legal fiction embodied in sub-section (4) of that section though proceedings are neither in nor before any Court at that stage - But there is no such deeming provision u/s. 39 of FERA bringing every investigation or proceeding in its ambit as 'a judicial proceeding' within meaning of ss. 193 and 228 of IPC - (B) significance of a statement recorded under provisions of FERA during investigation or proceeding under said Act so as to bring them within meaning of judicial proceeding must be examined only qua provisions of FERA but not with reference to provisions of any other alien Act or Acts such as Income Tax Act -

(C) Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 277 - Unexplained money - Seizure of currency of Rs. 6 lakhs from assessee/appellant - Whether appellant liable u/s. 120 B of Code r/w s. 277 - Further held, appellant not liable for punishment u/s. 120 B of IPC r/w s. 277 and s. 277 (simpliciter) of I T Act as very basis of prosecution was completely nullified by order of Tribunal - (D) s. 120 B of IPC - Conspiracy - Holding unexplained money - No agreement can be said to have hatched among all accused - Accused merely related to main accused and had claimed ownership of currency seized by authorities - Whether sufficient enough to held him liable for conspiracy - Further held, not sufficient to infer that there was conspiracy among all accused - Evidence direct or circumstantial lacking to bring all three accused under charge of conspiracy - Conviction of accused under s. 120-B set aside - (E) Code of Criminal Procedure , 1974, s. 220 - Misjoinder of charges and misjoinder of parties - Can misjoinder of charges be said to be mere irregularity - Whether accused would be entitled to acquittal - Further held, cannot be said to be mere irregularity - Accused, entitled to acquittal - Appeals allowed.
