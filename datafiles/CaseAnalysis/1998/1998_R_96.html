Ravindra Kumar Rai v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.C. Agrawal, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1512; (1998) 3 SCC 183; AIR 1998 SC 1227; 1998 (3) Bom.C.R. 325; JT 1998 (2) SC 131; 1998(2) SCALE 66; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 1146; 1998 (2) SLR 270; 1998 (3) SLT 317; 1998 (2) Supreme 218

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33 - Regulation on Graduate Medical Education, 1997, Regn. 5 - Education - Combined entrance examination for admission in medical college - Plea, conducting a common entrance examination will delay admission process or it will be extremely difficult to conduct examination, even if number of students is large - Held, sufficient time is available for conducting common entrance examination for 1998 years and for other steps in that behalf - Plea rejected - State Govt. and its Medical Education Department directed to conduct it as per Regulation framed under Act.
