Bolin Chetia v Jogadish Bhuyan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 191; (2005) 6 SCC 81; AIR 2005 SC 1872; 2005 (59) ALR 446; JT 2005 (3) SC 267; 2005 (2) KLT 418; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 207; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 184; 2005(3) SCALE 156; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984, Bombay High Court Rules

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Consumer Protection - Corporate - Criminal - Customs - Election - MRTP - Practice and Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Appeal - Merely on its being filed should respondent be necessarily and in routine put on notice, forgoing application of judicial mind to merits of appeal, at that stage? Does this Court not have power to summarily throw out an appeal howsoever worthless it may be? - Held, though an appeal under s.116 A is preferred as of right, yet inherent power of this Court to summarily dismiss appeal at admission stage is not taken away - Such power would be exercised only by way of exception such as, on Court feeling convinced that appeal does not raise any such question of fact or law as would persuade this Court to put respondent on notice before hearing - Order accordingly - Order accordingly.
