Sarva Shramik Sangh v Messrs Indian Smelting & Refining Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 890; (2003) 10 SCC 455; AIR 2004 SC 269; 2003 (3) CLR 949; JT 2003 (8) SC 243; 2003 (3) LLJ 1156; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 87; 2003(9) SCALE 60; 2003 (1) SCW 5989; 2003 (6) SLR 414; 2003 (6) SLT 618; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1003; 2003 (7) Supreme 523

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946 Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971, ss. 7, 28, 32, 59 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether basic question of existence of relationship of workmen vis-Ã -vis principal employer is within purview of Maharashtra Act - Whether industrial dispute could be raised for abolition of contract labour system in view of provisions of Maharashtra Act - Held, it is relationship existing by contractual arrangement which is sought to be abandoned and negated and in its place complainant's claim is to effect that there was in reality a relationship between employer and complainant directly - It is establishment of existence of such an arrangement which decides jurisdiction - Further held, decision in CIPLA's case was taken not only in tune with earlier decisions of this Court in General Labour Union (Red Flag) Bombay's case (supra) and Vividh Kamgar Sabha's case (supra) but quite in accordance with subject of enactment and object which legislature had in view and purpose sought to be achieved by Maharashtra Act and consequently, there is no scope or necessity to reconsider question once over again by a larger Bench.
