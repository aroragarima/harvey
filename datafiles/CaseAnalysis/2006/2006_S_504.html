Sadashiv Ramrao Hadbe v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1484; (2006) 10 SCC 92; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 161

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Attempts to sexually assault - Conviction - Validity of - Benefit of doubt - Prosecutrix went to Police Station and gave FIR Statement and case was registered against appellant for attempt to sexual assault - PW-11 took over investigation and thereafter filed final report - Sessions Court as well as HC mainly relied on oral evidence given by complainant, held that evidence of prosecutrix's alone was sufficient to convict appellant as it had ring of truth and finding was upheld by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether HC and Sessions Court erred in finding that appellant was guilty and appellant was entitled to benefit of those doubts - Held, on careful scrutiny of entire evidence, prosecution evidence was so many contradictions and whole incident seems to be highly improbable - However, if version given by prosecutrix was unsupported by any medical evidence or whole surrounding circumstances were highly improbable and belie case set up by prosecutrix, court should not act on solitary evidence of prosecutrix - Therefore, it was highly improbable that prosecutrix could not make any noise or get out of room without being assaulted by doctor as she was able bodied person with ordinary physique and absence of injuries on body improbably prosecution version - Thus, Sessions Court as well as HC was not taken into consideration FI statement - Prosecutrix was not given full description of incident allegedly took place but when she was examined in Court she had improved her version - Hence, on consideration of entire evidence, there was serious doubt regarding offence allegedly committed by appellant on prosecutrix - Appellant was entitled to benefit of doubts and HC and Sessions Court erred in finding appellant guilty - Appeal allowed.
