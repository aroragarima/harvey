Centre for Public Interest Litigation v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 694; (2013) 16 SCC 279; AIR 2014 SC 49; 2013(6) ALL MR 955; 2014 (1) AWC 690; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 70; 2013(13) SCALE 210; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 1103

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Health & Drug

Keywords: Advertising Standards Council of India Code

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 21, 47 and 32-Food Supply and Standards Act, 2006-Writ petition-For constituting independent Expert/Technical Committee to evaluate harmful effects of soft drinks on human health, particularly on health of children and also for direction to Union of India to put in place regulatory regime which could control and check contents in particular chemical additive in foods, including soft drinks-Held-Any food article which is hazardous on injurious to public health is potential danger to fundamental right to life guaranteed under Article 21-Paramount duty cast on States and its authorities to achieve appropriate level of protection to human life and health which is fundamental right guaranteed to citizens under Article 21 read with Article 47-Authorities obliged to maintain system of control and other activities as appropriate to circumstances, including public communication on food safety and risk, food safety surveillance and other monitoring activities covering all stages of food business-Provisions of Food Supply and Standards Act, 2006 as well as Rules and Regulations framed thereunder to be strictly followed-Writ petition disposed of with directions.
