Haryana Urban Development Authority and others v Orchid Infrastructure Developers Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 53; (2017) 4 SCC 243; AIR 2017 SC 882; 2017 (121) ALR 467; JT 2017 (1) SC 503; 2017 (2) MLJ 519; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 145; 2017 (135) RD 433; 2017(1) SCALE 699

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1963, Review, Administrator, Reports, Tender, Delegation, Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1977, Haryana Urban Development (Disposal of Land and Buildings) Regulations, 1978, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land & Property

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Rejection of bid - Civil suit - Maintainability - Respondent/ plaintiff filed suit for declaration with consequential relief as against appellants/defendants with regard to rejection of bid relating to commercial tower - Trial Court held that Administrator, HUDA was not competent to reject bid of plaintiff, thus decreed suit - However, First Appellate Court reversed judgement of Trial Court - HC on second appeal restored judgment and decree passed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, suit filed by plaintiff was maintainable in absence of concluded contract. Held, plaintiff has prayed for declaration that rejection of bid was illegal. Merely by that, plaintiff could not have become entitled for consequential mandatory injunction for issuance of formal letter of allotment. Court while exercising judicial review could not have accepted bid. Bid had never been accepted by concerned authorities. It was not a case of cancellation of bid after being accepted. Thus even assuming as per plaintiff's case that Administrator was not equipped with power and Chief Administrator had power to accept or refuse bid, there had been no decision by Chief Administrator. Thus, merely by declaration that rejection of bid by Administrator was illegal, plaintiff could not have become entitled to consequential relief of issuance of allotment letter. Thus suit, in the form it was filed, was not maintainable for relief sought in view of the fact that there was no concluded contract in absence of allotment letter being issued to plaintiff, which was sine qua non for filing civil suit. Highest bidder has no vested right to have auction concluded in his favour. Appeal dismissed. Municipalities & Local Governments - Administrative - Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1977, s. 51 - Rejection of bid - Competency of Administrator - Delegation of power - Whether, Administrator was empowered to accept or reject bid. Held, plaintiff has come to Court with case that there was no delegation of power to Administrator. Undoubtedly, delegation of power made by HUDA u/s. 51 of Act has not been placed on record before Courts below. It is apparent that there had been delegation of power by HUDA to Administrator with respect to power to accept auction bids for commercial/residential/industrial sites provided highest bid is more than reserve price and minimum of three bids have been received. Administrator has also power if site is not sold in 3 attempts, to revise price downwards up to maximum of 10% of reserve price. Thus plaintiff has not come to Court with clean hands and has suppressed order of HUDA by which delegation of power has been made. Fact that there was delegation of power is also crystal clear from communication exchanged between Administrator and Chief Administrator. Admittedly, Presiding Officer was Administrator, HUDA. Thus, as per terms of auction also, Administrator was having power to accept or reject bid. Appeal dismissed. Contract & Commercial - Administrative - Rejection of bid - Legality - Whether, Administrator rejected bid in illegal or arbitrary manner. Held, Administrator had right to reject bids and he had rejected it on sufficient ground, duly considering materials on record as is apparent from communication dt. 21-9-2004. In interest of public, revenue of State and in interest of HUDA huge property was saved from being plundered. SC in State of Uttar Pradesh v. Vijay Bahadur Singh, 1982 Indlaw SC 281, has laid down that there is no obligation to accept highest bid. Govt. is entitled even to change its policy from time to time according to demands of the time. In Laxmikant v. Satyawan, 1996 Indlaw SC 3623, SC has laid down that in absence of completed contract when public auction had not culminated to its logical end before confirmation of bid, no right accrued to highest bidder. Appeal allowed.
