Â Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and A Others v R. S. Jhaver and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 428; AIR 1968 SC 59; 1967 (2) ITJ 919; [1967] 66 ITR 664; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 43; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 148; 1967 (20) STC 453

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Sales Tax

Summary: Criminal - Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madras General Sales Tax, 1959 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Search and seizure - Officers of the Commercial Tax Department raided the premises of the Respondent company - Suitcase was seized and forcibly removed by the officers who made the raid - In spite of the fact that they were informed that the box did not contain any papers or documents belonging to the Company and its contents consisted merely of personal effects of one of the Managing Directors - HC held that s. 41(2) did not allow search being made thereunder - It only provided for inspection - Search was a different thing altogether from inspection - If s. 41 (2) provided for search it would be within the legislative competence of the State Legislature - power of seizure and confiscation of goods contained in s. 41(4) could not be said to be ancillary and incidental to the power to tax sale or purchase of goods - Provision was beyond the legislative competence of the State Legislature - S. 41 (2)(3) and (4) were unconstitutional as they were unreasonable restrictions on the fundamental rights guaranteed under Art. 19(1) (f) and (g) of the Constitution - HC allowed all the petitions and directed that the documents, things and goods covered by the petitions should be returned to the petitioners - Whether HC was right in allowing the W.P. of the appellants - Held, legislature has power to provide for search and seizure in connection with taxation laws in order that evasion may be checked - Anything recovered from the search of the residential accommodation on the basis of this defective warrant must be returned - Anything confiscated must also be returned - Safeguards provided u/s. 165 of the CrPC do not appear to have been followed when the search was made - anything recovered on a defective search of this kind must be returned - Final order of the HC allowing the W.P.s must stand - Appeal dismissed.
