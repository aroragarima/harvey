Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal I, and Another v Anwar Ali

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 492; (1970) 2 SCC 185; AIR 1970 SC 1782; [1970] 76 ITR 696; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 28(1)(c) - Penalty u/s. 28(1)(c) - Assessee was a partner in the firm on 'X' - Income Tax Officer while making the assessment discovered an undisclosed bank account of the assessee with the Central Bank of India - It was found that a cash deposit of Rs. 87,000 had been made by the assessee on 21.11.1946 in that bank - Assessee was asked to explain the source of the amount of deposit - Explanation given by the assessee was not accepted by the Income Tax Officer who held that the sum of Rs. 87,000 represented income from undisclosed sources and added the amount to the total income of the assessee in his personal assessment - Income Tax Officer also imposed penalty amounting to Rs. 66,000 on the assessee u/s. 28(1)(c) of Act for concealing income and deliberately furnishing inaccurate particulars - On appeal, Asst. Commissioner upheld the order of the Income Tax Officer - Tribunal held that no penalty could be imposed as Income-tax Officer must find some material apart from the falsity of the assessee's explanation to support his finding that the receipt from undisclosed sources was income - HC upheld the order of the Tribunal - Hence, present appeal - Whether the Income Tax Authorities were justified in imposing a penalty on the assessee u/s. 28(1)(c) of the IT Act? - Held, it was neither suggested before the HC nor has it been contended before SC that, apart from the falsity of the explanation given by the assessee, there was cogent material or evidence from which it could be inferred that the assessee had concealed the particulars of his income or had deliberately furnished inaccurate particulars in respect of the same and that the disputed amount was a revenue receipt - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
