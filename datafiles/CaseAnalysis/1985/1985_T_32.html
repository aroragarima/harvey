State of Orissa and Others v Titaghur Paper Mills Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, V.D. Tulzapurkar, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 436; (1985) Supp SCC 280; (1985) SCC (Tax) 538; AIR 1985 SC 1293; 1986 (1) ARBLR 135; 1985(2) SCALE 410; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 26; 1985 (60) STC 213; 1985 TAXLR 2948

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Forest Contract Rules, Sales Tax Authorities, Practice, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Profit a Prendre, Madhya Pradesh Abolition of proprietary Rights (Estates, Mahals, Alienated Lands) Act, 1950, Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1951, Orissa General Clauses Act, 1937, Orissa Sales Tax Rules, Sale of Goods Act, 1893, Orissa Forest Contract Act, 1972, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1978, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1958, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act 1974, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1959, Orissa Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1979, Law Reform (Enforcement of Contracts) Act, 1954, Madhya Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1958, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax (Amendment And Validation) Act, 1971, Sale of Goods Act, 1939, Orissa Forest Contract Rules

Summary: - Indian Easements Act, 1882,s.4 - Orissa Sales Tax Act 1947,s.9 - Sale of Goods Act, 1893,s.4 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882,s.3 ,s.3-B - Sale of Goods Act, 1939,s.64A - Madhya Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1958,s.8(1) ,s.4(2) ,s.5(1) - Registration Act, 1908,s.2(9) - Sale of Goods Act, 1930,s.2(7) - Constitution Of India, 1950art. 142 ,art. 226 ,art. 286 ,art. 366 - General Clauses Act, 1897,s.3(36) - Orissa General Clauses Act, 1937,s.2(21) ,s.1(13) ,s.12(5) - Government of India Act, 1935,s.100(3) - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957,s.5(2)(a) ,art. 246(3) - Constitution, sch.7 list 2 entry 54, arts.246 and 136 - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 3B, 5(1) and 12(8) - Orissa Government Notification S.R.O. Nos. 372/77, 373/77 D/- 23-05-1977 and 900/77, 901/77 D/- 29-12-1977 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 3(26), 20 - Contract Act , 1872, s. 10 - (A) Notifications - Levy of sales tax on bamboos agreed to be severed and standing trees agreed to be severed liable to tax on turnover of purchase - Constitutionality - Considering the various ancilllary issues - (1) Notification - Tax on said purchase @ 10 % - Validity - (2) Use of word 'supersession' in the same sense as the words 'repeal and replacement' - Effect - (3) Applicability of notification to Timbers Contract - (4) Reopening of assessment - Permissibility - Rate of tax increased from 6 % to 10 % - Levy on sales of logs by forest contractor at 10 % - Tax assessed @ 6 % during 01-06-1977 to 31-12-1977 as assumed - (5) Bamboo contract is grant of profits a prendre - Constitutionality of levy of sales tax on bamboo contract - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Meaning of words - Absence of definition - Aid of dictionaries - Permissibility - (C) New plea - Appeal against decision allowing writ petition challenging levy of purchase tax by notifications - Supreme Court found notifications valid - Question of exigibility - Only an interpretation of documents is required - Objection that SC should not go into such question - Question raised for first time in petition for special leave - Maintainability - (D) Rules of construction for determining whether the document amounts to grant of benefit arising out of land - (A) Held, notifications are constitutional and valid - It does not have the effect of wiping out tax liability under previous notifications - Held, not applicable to Timbers Contract - By reason of period of limitation prescribed by s.12 (8), the assessment of forest contractor for the said period could not now be reopened to reassess such sales at 10 percent -Not only ultra vires the Act, but also unconstitutional - (B) Held aid of dictionaries may be taken - (C) Held, appellant could not raise such a plea for the first time in this appeal - (D) Rules Laid down - Order Accordingly.
