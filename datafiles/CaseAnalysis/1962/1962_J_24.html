Bench

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), (b), (c), (g) - Central Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1955, rr. 4(A), 4(B) - Misconduct - Departmental proceedings - Legality - Respondent in service of Govt of India in Audit and accounts Department at Bombay was served with charge-sheet for committing breach of rr. 4(a) and 4(b) of the 1955 as he refused to dissociate himself from activities of All India Non-Gazetted Audit and Accounts Association (AINGAAA) despite withdrawal of recognition of AINGAAA - Respondent filed petition before HC praying for writ of certiorary to quash charge-sheet issued against him and writ of prohibition prohibiting appellant/authority from proceeding further with departmental proceedings - HC held that r. 4A was valid and hence departmental proceedings could not be impeached - HC, however held that r. 4(B) of the 1955 Rules was invalid - Hence instant appeal by Govt. of India - Respondent also filed appeal challenging validity of r. 4(a) of the 1955 Rules - Whether departmental proceedings against respondent could be sustained - Held, as per SC decision in Kameshwar Prasad's case r. 4A in so far it prohibited any form of demonstration it would be invalid - It's not invalid in so far as it may prohibit participation in strike - Charge against respondent was not that he participated in any strike but he participated in various demonstrations and that was charge based upon that part of rule which prohibited demonstrations altogether - Particular charge against the respondent being on the basis of that part of the rule which was invalid, it should follow that departmental proceedings based on that charge were invalid - As for validity of r. 4(b) of the 1955 Rules, art. 19(4) of the Constitution would provide that art. 19(1) of Constitution would not affect operation of any existing law in so far as it imposed, in interests of public order or morality, reasonable restrictions - Restriction should be said to be in interests of public order only if the connection between restriction and public order was proximate and direct - It would be difficult to see any direct or proximate or reasonable connection between recognition by Govt of the Association and discipline amongst, and efficiency of, members of the said Association - It would also be difficult to see any connection between recognition and public order - HC rightly held that r. 4 (b) of the 1955 Rules was invalid - Departmental proceedings instituted against respondent for alleged contravention by him of rr. 4(a) and 4(b) of the 1955 Rules should be quashed - Kameshwar Prasad and Others v. The State Of Bihar and another, 1962 Indlaw SC 230, relied on - Appeals disposed of.
