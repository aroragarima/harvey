Leela Hotels Ltd v Housing & Urban Development Corporation Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 710; (2012) 1 SCC 302; AIR 2012 SC 903; 2012 (3) ALD(SC) 96; 2012 (91) ALR 909; 2012 (1) AWC 39; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 386; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 641; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 434; 2011(12) SCALE 573; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 36 - Payment towards arbitral award - Adjustment against interest - Permissibility - Appellant company was granted lease for land in question on the condition that in the case of default of any instalment, the same could be paid along with interest at the rate of 20% per annum and further, the allotment would automatically stand cancelled and in such event 50% of the amount paid up to that date would stand forfeited and the balance 50% would be refunded without interest - Appellant defaulted in payment of third instalment - Lease agreement was cancelled and as per the terms of the agreement 50% of the total amount paid by the appellant was refunded and 50% was forfeited - Appellant invoked arbitration clause and matter was referred to arbitratration - Arbitrator passed award - Appellant filed objections u/s. 34 of the Act before the HC - Single Judge dismissed objections and in the meantime, respondent deposited the award amount in the court - Appellant, in an appeal before the DB, contended that the amount deposited should be adjusted towards the payment of interest and not towards the principle amount - DB rejected appellant's contention - Whether the amounts deposited and/or paid by respondent to appellant in terms of the arbitral award, was first to be appropriated towards payment of the interest due on the principal sum or whether the same was to be appropriated against the principal sum itself - Held, there was no agreement between the parties as to how the amounts to be paid in terms of the award were to be appropriated by the appellant - In such cases it was for the creditor to appropriate such payment firstly against the interest payable - Creditor should not be denied its dues on a unilateral stipulation that the amount due was being deposited as against the principal sum due in terms of the award - Since the said amount was accepted by the appellant on protest, it would be entitled to appropriate the same against the interest which was due and payable till that date on the principal amount - DB erred in presuming that the said amount had been accepted by the appellant on account of good business sense in view of the uncertainty of the final outcome of the case - DB should have proceeded on the basis of the principles of law as laid down by SC in M/s I.C.D.S. Ltd. v. Smithaben H. Patel & Ors., 1999 INDLAW SC 409, keeping in mind the earlier decisions of the Privy Council in both Meka Venkatadri Appa Rao Bahadur Zamindar Garu & Ors. v. Raja Parthasarathy Appa Rao Bahadur Zamindar Garu, AIR 1922 PC 233 and Rai Bahadur Seth Nemichand v. Seth Radha Kishen, 1921 INDLAW PC 74, in interfering with the judgment of Single Judge - (B) Whether the award of the arbitrator amounted to a decree or not - Held, language used in s. 36 of the Act made it very clear that an arbitral award had to be enforced under the Code of Civil Procedure in the same manner as it were a decree of the Court - Language of s. 36 left no room for doubt as to the manner in which the award of the arbitrator was to be accepted - Appeal allowed.
