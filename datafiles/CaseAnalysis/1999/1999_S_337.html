State of Haryana and Others v S. K. Singhal

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1616; (1999) 4 SCC 293; (1999) SCC (L&S) 859; AIR 1999 SC 1829; 1999 (1) CLR 1148; 1999 (2) ESC 1182; JT 1999 (3) SC 140; 1999 LabIC 2072; 1999 (2) LLN 660; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 714; 1999 (3) SLJ 194; 1999 (2) SLR 249; 1999 (3) UPLBEC 2246

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Service Regulation (Vol II), rr. 2.2(a) and 5.32 (B)(2) - Voluntary retirement - Conditions of - Whether respondent is deemed to have retired on expiry of three months of notice issued after completion of 20 years of qualifying service under the Regulations? - Held, on 16-8-1995 respondent issued a notice seeking voluntary retirement - No response from concerned authorities till 16-11-1995 - Long after expiry of 3 months on 16-11-1995, a telegram was sent on 5-12-1995 asking respondent to join duty - It will be noticed that r. 2.2 does not obstruct voluntary retirement to come into force automatically on expiry of 3 months and it only enables withdrawal or withholding of pension subject to certain conditions, to a retired employee - Respondent must be deemed to have retired - Under r. 5.32 B, Govt. employee who has completed 20 years of qualifying service may, by giving notice of not less than 3 months in writing to appointing authority, retire from service - Retirement shall become effective from date of expiry of period - Therefore, it is clear that if a person has completed 20 years qualifying service and has given a notice u/r. 5.32 B of 3 months (or if his request for relaxation of 3 months is accepted), then request 'shall' be accepted subject to invoking provision of r. 2.2 of Punjab Civil Service Regulation (Vol II) - Sub-cl. (1) of r. 5.32 B contemplates a 'notice to retire' and not a request seeking permission to retire - Further 'request' contemplated by sub-s. is only for seeking exemption from 3 months period - Proviso to sub-cl. (2) makes a positive provision that where appointing authority does not refuse to grant of permission for retirement before expiry of period specified in sub-r. (1), retirement shall become effective from date of expiry of said period - Appeal dismissed.
