Commissioner of Customs (Import), Mumbai v Jagdish Cancer and Research Centre

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20060; (2001) 6 SCC 483; AIR 2001 SC 3161; 2001 (132) E.L.T. 257; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 140; JT 2001 (6) SC 244; 2001 (46) RLT 121; 2001(5) SCALE 11; 2001 (5) SLT 630; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 245; 2001 (5) Supreme 656

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Land & Property

Summary: Customs - Market Value - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 25(1), 28(1), 111(o), 112, 124 and 125(2) - Notification no. 64/88Cus D/- 01-03-1988 - Whether respondent was entitled to exemption under notification no. 64/88Cus D/- 01-03-1988? - Order for payment of duty u/s. 125(2) would be an integral part of proceedings relating to confiscation and consequential orders thereon, on ground as in this case that importer had violated conditions of notification subject to which exemption of goods was granted, without attracting provisions of s. 28(1) of Customs Act - A notice is to be read as a whole to find out as to whether person concerned is made aware of various grounds on basis of which action is proposed to be taken as well as nature of action - A perusal of condition in notification indicates that on an average, at least 40 per cent of all outdoor patients should be provided free treatment - Held, responded not entitled to benefit under notification - Appeal allowed.
