Jagdish v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21047; (2003) 9 SCC 159; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1171; AIR 2002 SC 2540; 2002 (80) ECC 3; 2002 (1) JCC 54; JT 2001 (10) SC 330; 2002 (1) SLT 31

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 8/18 - Whether any opium was being smuggled or if anybody was found in possession of same? - Held, search and seizure is not proper - PW's testimony suffers from various infirmities - In case prosecution had some prior information, he was obliged to make a record of it and send its copy to higher officials before proceeding for checking duty - Prosecution did not establish its case beyond reasonable doubt and appellant is entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
