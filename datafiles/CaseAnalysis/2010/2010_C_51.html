Afcons Infrastructure Limited and another v Cherian Varkey Construction Company (Private) Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 688; (2010) 8 SCC 24; 2010 (6) ALD(SC) 155; 2010 (5) AWC 5409; JT 2010 (7) SC 616; 2010 (8) MLJ(SC) 369; 2010(5) M.P.H.T. 1; 2010 (2) OLR 889; 2011 (112) RD 199; 2010(7) SCALE 293; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Settlement Agreement, Processes, Mediation Rules, 2003

Summary: Civil Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 89 and O. 10 r. 1A - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996 - Second respondent entrusted the work of construction of certain bridges and roads to the appellants under an agreement dt. 20.4.2001 - Appellants sub-contracted a part of the said work to the first respondent under an agreement dt. 1.8.2001 - It is not in dispute that the agreement between the appellants and the first respondent did not contain any provision for reference of the disputes to arbitration - Dispute arose - First respondent filed an application u/s. 89 of the Code before the trial court praying that the court may formulate the terms of settlement and refer the matter to arbitration - Trial court allowed said application - On appeal, HC confirmed the order of the trial court and held that s. 89 permitted the court in appropriate cases, to refer even unwilling parties to arbitration - Hence, present appeal - Whether s. 89 of CPC empowers the court to refer the parties to a suit to arbitration with the consent of both parties? - Held, no - The court should first ascertain whether the parties are willing for arbitration - The court should inform the parties that arbitration is an adjudicatory process by a chosen private forum and reference to arbitration will permanently take the suit outside the ambit of the court - Only if both parties agree for arbitration, and also agree upon the arbitrator, the matter should be referred to arbitration - If parties are not agreeable for arbitration and conciliation, the court should, keeping in view the preferences/options of parties, refer the matter to any one of the other three other ADR processes - If the reference to the ADR process fails, on receipt of the Report of the ADR Forum, the court shall proceed with hearing of the suit - Hence, court has no power, authority or jurisdiction to refer unwilling parties to arbitration u/sec. 89, if there is no arbitration agreement - Therefore, where there is no pre-existing arbitration agreement between the parties, the consent of all the parties to the suit will be necessary, for referring the subject matter of the suit to arbitration u/s. 89 of CPC - Impugned order of the trial court and HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
