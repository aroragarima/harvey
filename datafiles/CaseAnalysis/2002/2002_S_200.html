Alamgir v State (National Capital Territory, Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1395; (2003) 1 SCC 21; (2003) SCC (Cr) 165; AIR 2003 SC 282; 2002 (4) CCR 241; 2003 (1) Crimes 212; 2003 (2) CRJ 263; 2003 CRLJ 456; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 249; JT 2002 (9) SC 347; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 834; 2002(8) SCALE 373; 2002 (6) SLT 469; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 88; 2002 (8) Supreme 604

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Evidence Of Handwriting Expert

Summary: IPC - S. 302 - Cr.PC - S. 161 - whether the circumstances form a complete chain of events without any snap and pointedly point to the accused as the guilty person and to no-one else - held, on a plain look at the evidence one would take to conclude its acceptability in creditworthiness rather than its rejection - the events pointedly point out the guilt of the accused person - the handwriting expert's opinion simply corroborates the circumstantial evidence.
