Saharanpur Light Railway Company, Limited v Shahdara (Delhi)-Shahdara-Saharanpur Railway Worker's Union

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 15; AIR 1969 SC 513; 1969 (18) FLR 315; 1969 LabIC 837; 1969 (3) LLJ 734; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 131

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Order) Act, 1946, ss. 6, 10(2) - Standing orders certified - Modification applied for - Appellant was employer of respondents - Regional Labour Commissioner certified Standing Orders of appellant - Thereafter, respondent applied for modification of Standing Orders, u/s. 10(2) of the Act - Some modifications were permitted - Respondent's appealed, Appellate Authority further allowed some more modifications - Appellant objected at first to all modifications, but later limited objections to four modifications - Appellants challenged impugned order on two facets: it's scope of power of modification u/s. 10(2) of the Act, and on merits on ground that modifications had not stood test of reasonableness and fairness - Grounds urged were that authorities under the Act were to certify modifications of existing Standing Orders when change of circumstances was established - Further, appellant urged that principles analogous to res judicata were applicable to application for modification unless such application was occasioned by new circumstances that arose or was based on new facts - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 10(2) of the Act empowered modifications that were allowed - Held, s. 6 of the Act conferred finality on certified Standing Orders or modifications thereof - However, Act had not restricted right to apply for modification or power of authorities to allow modification based on proof of change of circumstances - Court was unable to accept interpretation that when application u/s. 10(2) of the Act was made, Certifying Officer was permitted to modify Standing Orders already certified, if request was not made based on material which existed at earlier stage when Standing Orders were certified, as finality of orders made under s. 6 of the Act by Appellate Authority was adversely affected - Impugned order passed by Chief Labour showed that he had not relied on any fresh facts, material or circumstances which were not available at earlier stage when Standing Orders were first certified or first modified - Present order amounted to passing orders, different from earlier orders passed by Appellate Authority, on reconsideration of same material which was available to both Authorities - Modification at item No. 1 in Standing Order 9(a) had been specifically disallowed in appeal by Chief Labour Commissioner in his earlier order u/s. 6 of the Act and confirmed certification of original Standing Orders - However, present Chief Labour Commissioner permitted modification because he considered it reasonable and fair, even though, on same material, his predecessor had disallowed modification based on reason that draft Standing Order was fair and reasonable - Clear that order of Chief Labour Commissioner, allowing all four modifications, which was not based on fresh facts, material or circumstances, was liable to be set aside - Accordingly, order of Chief Labour Commissioner (Central) that permitted modifications mentioned in his Order which related to Standing Orders 9(a), 12(A) and Il(vii) was set aside - Appeal partly allowed.
