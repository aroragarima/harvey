Sudarshan Rajpoot v Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 782; (2015) 2 SCC 317; 2015(1) ALL MR 485; 2015 (1) AWC 745; 2015 (144) FLR 7; JT 2014 (13) SC 141; 2014(13) SCALE 122; 2015 (1) SLR 533; 2015 (1) UPLBEC 355

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Retrenchment Compensation, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Rights and Full Participation) Act, 1995 - Removal from service - Legality - Appellant workman was removed from service - Appellant raised industrial dispute - Labour Court directed respondent management to reinstate appellant - Respondent challenged Labour Court award before HC - HC set aside award, however, awarded consolidated damages to appellant - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, finding of fact was recorded by the Labour Court accepting the evidence of EW1 that the appellant-workman has worked continuously from 11-3-1997 to 29-7-2000 in the respondent. Therefore, the Labour Court has rightly come to conclusion and held that the appellant has rendered more than 240 days continuous service from the date of his appointment till the date of passing the termination order. The finding of fact recorded by the Labour Court in its award on proper appreciation of undisputed facts and evidence on record, has been rightly held that the termination order amounts to retrenchment and non-compliance of the statutory provisions u/ss. 6-N, 6-R and 6-Q of the U.P.I.D. Act has rendered the order of termination void ab initio in law. Therefore, the Labour Court was justified in passing the award of reinstatement. The HC has erroneously exercised its supervisory jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution. The finding of the HC in setting aside the finding of fact recorded by the Labour Court in its award by applying Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others v Umadevi and Others, 2006 Indlaw SC 125, is wholly untenable in law. The action of respondent amounts to an unfair labour practice as defined u/s. 2(ra) of the 1947 Act r/w s. 25T which is prohibited u/s. 25U, Chapter VC of the 1947 Act. The appellant sustained injuries in the accident that occurred on account of the mechanical defect of the vehicle involved in the accident. The plea taken by the respondent that the order of termination was passed against the appellant as the accident occurred on account of negligent driving of the vehicle by the appellant is not proved by the respondent in order to justify the same. This aspect of the matter has not been discussed either by the Labour Court or by the HC. Therefore, the respondent is statutorily obliged u/s. 47 of the 1995 Act to provide alternate equivalent job to the appellant in place of the post of driver. The impugned Judgment and orders are set aside. Appeals allowed.
