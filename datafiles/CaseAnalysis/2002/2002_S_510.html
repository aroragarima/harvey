Balu Sonba Shinde v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1923; (2002) 7 SCC 543; (2003) SCC (Cr) 112; AIR 2002 SC 3137; 2002 (3) CCR 331; 2002 (4) Crimes 200; 2003 (1) CRJ 58; 2002 CRLJ 4650; 2002 (3) JCC 1968; JT 2002 (6) SC 611; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 95; 2002(6) SCALE 281; 2002 (5) SLT 165; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 135; 2002 (6) Supreme 194; 2003 (1) UC 3

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial Evidence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Relationship between appellant and deceased, who were brothers, was strained due to land dispute - Prosecution witness saw them arguing before incident and moved closer - After few moments, he heard cries and discovered that deceased was grievously injured - Whether keeping in mind such circumstances, appellant is liable to be convicted? - Held, no - No direct evidence and entire prosecution hinges on circumstantial evidence - Chain of evidence is not complete - No eye witness to assault on deceased - Appellant entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed
