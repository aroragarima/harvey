Commissioner of Sales Tax, M. P v Popular Trading Company, Ujjain

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 693; (2000) 5 SCC 511; AIR 2000 SC 1578; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 81; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 531; JT 2000 (4) SC 253; 2000(3) SCALE 85; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 983; 2000 (118) STC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Madhya Pradesh Sthaniya Kshetra Me Mal Ke Pravesh Par Kar Adhiniyam 1976, Sch. 1, entry 5 - Whether watery coconut is oil seed and eligible for entry tax? - Evaluation - 'Watery coconut' falls within scope of entry to attract entry tax - Expression 'that is to say' is clear and extensive and defines to a great extent - An oil seed botanically means a seed which is a flowering plants' unit of reproduction or germ capable of developing into another such plant - Seed which can yield oil is an oil seed - Held, 'Watery coconut'' while yielding oil merely because it yields some watery substance does not cease to be an ''oil seed'' - Falls within entry.
