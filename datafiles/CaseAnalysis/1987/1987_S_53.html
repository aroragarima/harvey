Raghunath Prasad v Secretary, Home (Police) Department and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28104; (1988) Supp SCC 519; (1988) SCC (L&S) 927; AIR 1988 SC 1033; 1988 (8) ATC 220; JT 1988 (1) SC 22; 1988 (2) LLJ 209; 1988 (1) SLR 347

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Bihar Police Act, 1861 - Regularisation - Option - Exercise of - Whether decision of a Division Bench of High Court rejecting writ petition of appellant who had claimed for being absorbed in regular Police Force on basis of exercise of option 2 is correct? - Held, benefit of option was confined to recruits prior to 6-5-1970 and since appellant had been recruited long after that date, he was not entitled to exercise of benefit of option - HC was right in finding against appellant that option in terms of instructions D/- 1-10-1974 was available to those who came within ambit of earlier judgment - Appellant being a recruit of 1972-73, not in combined cadre but in wireless organisation, was not entitled to benefit of option - Appeal dismissed.
