Purushotama Reddy v Sateesh

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2148; (2008) 8 SCC 505; AIR 2008 SC 3202; 2008 (4) BC 269; 2008 (3) JCC (NI) 341; 2008 (3) KLT 590; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 1067; 2008 (152) PLR 1; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 616; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 660; 2008(11) SCALE 73; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 738; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: New India Assurance Co.

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 200 and 357(1)(b),(5) - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 142 - Loan through promissory notes - In discharge of loan issuance of cheques in question - Dishonour of cheque - Complaints u/s. 200 of CrPC, 1973 r/w. 138 and 142 of Negotiable Instruments Act - Judgement of conviction and sentence - Whether in a suit for recovery of money on a cheque issued by defendant but dishonoured, amount received by plaintiff-creditor in a criminal proceeding should be adjusted? - A suit for recovery of money due from a borrower indisputably is maintainable at instance of creditor and for same cause of action a complaint petition under terms of s. 138 of Negotiable Instruments Act would also be maintainable - Held, yes, such amount which has been received by plaintiff-creditor in a criminal proceeding is to be adjusted, as a duty has been cast upon civil courts to take into account sum paid or recovered as compensation in terms of s. 357 of Code - Impugned judgement directed to be modified - Appeal allowed.
