State of Maharashtra v Dattatraya Digamber Birajdar

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1399; (2007) 12 SCC 172; AIR 2007 SC 3056; 2007 (7) AWC 7351; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 699; 2007 (3) CLR 806; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 92; JT 2007 (10) SC 459; 2007 (3) LLJ 838; 2007 (4) LLN 524; 2007(10) SCALE 442; 2007 (7) SCJ 304; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 504; 2007 (5) SLR 631; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 169

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Respondent was working as a daily wager in Public Works Division (PWD) - Respondent then joined as Surveyor in office of Divisional Soil Conservation Officer with effect from 03-04-1986 on consolidated pay - On 25-09-1986 Deputy Divisional Soil Conservation Officer transferred respondent to Paranda with effect from 06-10-1986 - On 05-08-1987 Divisional Soil Conservation Officer abolished all 52 posts of surveyor - Service of respondent as surveyor came to be terminated on 20-08-1987 - After about eight years, respondent submitted an application for reference in terms of s. 10 of Industrial Disputes Act - Whether, in view of facts of this case, did appellant terminate service of respondent from PWD? - Held, it is crystal clear that ample material and evidence were placed before Labour Court to justify stand that with effect from 03-04-1986 respondent was not in employment of appellant - Respondent himself had voluntarily left department to join another department - In any event, claim was stale and was filed after about eight years of alleged order of termination - Further held, Labour Court and High Court erroneously held that burden to prove engagement of 240 days lies on employer - Appeal allowed.
