C.B.I v V. K. Bhutiani

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1305; (2009) 10 SCC 674; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 572; 2009(13) SCALE 64

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Conspiracy, Appeal allowed, Co-Accused

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 13(1)(d) - Exoneration - Criminal conspiracy - Statement of witnesses - Non examination of charge sheet - Appellant-Central Bureau of Investigation had come up in appeal against order passed by HC in criminal revision where respondent had challenged order on charge passed by Special Judge for offence punishable under ss. 120-B, 420, 467, 468, 471 of IPC and ss. 13(2), 13(1)(d) of the Act - Respondent who was Senior Manager of Bank was in fact exonerated by Central Vigilance Commission and in its report, he was found to be innocent - It was apparent from charge that respondent along with other co-accused had entered into criminal conspiracy to defraud bank by granting loans which should never be granted - HC in its judgment held that petitioner was not actually involved in any corrupt practice or in conspiracy of other accused persons intended to cheat bank - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified in non examining charge-sheet - Held, though report of Central Vigilance Commission could be relevant factor, it could not be held to be 'be all or end all' of matter for prosecuting accused persons of such serious offences - There was no such reasoning having given by HC who did not bother to examine charge-sheet and allegations made therein nor did it examine statement of witnesses and/or reports of handwritten experts which were part of charge-sheet - However, perusal of impugned order suggested that HC did not examine material available by way of charge-sheet and had proceeded to follow - Therefore, impugned order passed by HC was set-aside and matter was remanded - Appeal allowed.
