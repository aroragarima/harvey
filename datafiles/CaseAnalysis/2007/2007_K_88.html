Dilip S. Dahanukar v Kotak Mahindra Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 353; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 209; (2007) 6 SCC 528; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1345; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1775; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 28; [2007] 137 Comp Cas 1; 2007 CRLJ 2417; 2007 (2) JCC (NI) 153; JT 2007 (6) SC 204; 2007 (2) KLT 488; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 636; 2007(5) SCALE 452; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1122; 2007 (4) UC 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Recovery Of Fine

Summary: Interpretation of s. 357 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 vis-'-vis the provisions of the Negotiable Instruments Act, as regards power to impose sentence of fine - Held, amount of compensation not only must be reasonable one, the conditions for suspending the sentence should also be reasonable - It is only with that intent in view, the doctrine of purposive construction should be applied - S. 357 (2) of the Code of Criminal Procedure would be attracted even when Appellant was directed to pay compensation - Reasonable to direct the appellant to deposit a sum of Rs. 1 lakh within a period of four weeks - Appeals partly allowed.
