Northern Plastics Limited v Collector of Customs and Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 726; (2000) 1 SCC 545; AIR 1999 SC 3643; 1999 (84) ECR 826; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 3; JT 1999 (7) SC 311; 1999 (34) RLT 591; 1999(6) SCALE 9; 1999 (9) SLT 207; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 679

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Customs; Indirect Tax; Tort

Keywords: Wrongful

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 17 and 111 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Customs duty - Liability to pay - Arises as soon as goods land on land mass of India - Plea that party whose goods are not cleared and subsequently confiscated by authority is not liable to pay duty tenable - Confiscation and sale of goods by custom authorities - Found to be illegal liability of customs authorities - Not tenable - Is to refund money value of goods as on date goods ought to have been cleared - Money value of goods - Cannot be value shown in import document - It would at least be equal to CIS value plus duty payable - Authorities directed to refund CIS value with 12% interest.
