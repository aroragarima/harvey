Om Prakash Goel v Himachal Pradesh Tourism Development Corporation

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 665; (1991) 3 SCC 291; (1991) SCC (L&S) 911; AIR 1991 SC 1490; 1991 (17) ATC 250; 1991 (2) CLR 350; 1991 (63) FLR 245; JT 1991 (3) SC 6; 1991 LabIC 1414; 1992 (1) LLJ 469; 1991 (2) LLN 420; 1991(1) SCALE 892; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 701; 1991 (2) SLR 796; 1991 (2) UJ 176; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 967

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Labour & Industrial law - Constitution - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 14, chap. 2, Item 3 and Item 6 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - (A) Termination of service - Appellant, accountant in H P T D C - Charge sheet framed against him - Terminated before enquiry concluded - Reason, that service is no longer required - Juniors still in service - Contended that termination is violative of arst. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Held, that order is illegal and all backlogs are to be cleared - (B) Whether fact that employee working as lawyer ever since his termination is reason for withholding his back wages? - Further held, no - Entitled to full backwages up to date of his enrollment as lawyer - Backwages for a period from date of enrollment to date of reinstatement granted at rate of half subsistence allowance with deduction of amount earned by him as lawyer - Appeal allowed.
