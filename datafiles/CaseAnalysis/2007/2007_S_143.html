Commissioner, Prohibition and Excise, Andhra Pradesh and another v Sharana Gouda

Supreme Court of India

05 June 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1617; (2007) 6 SCC 42; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 50; JT 2007 (9) SC 329; 2007(8) SCALE 439; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 935

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968, s. 45 - Confiscation - Legality - Respondent's vehicle was confiscated for carrying contraband liquor - 2nd appellant/authority registered a case - Although the custody of the vehicle was given to respondent, the same was again directed for confiscation - Respondent filed writ petition - Respondent contended that he was the owner of the vehicle and had no knowledge that the vehicle was being used for carrying contraband liquor - HC allowed the writ petition on the ground that there was no mens rea involved - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Revenue - Whether mens rea was required to be established by the appellant - Held, proviso to s. 45 of the Act provided that there was a prohibition on confiscation if owner of the property in question had no reason to believe that alleged offence was being or was likely to be committed - However, such proviso to s. 45 was omitted from the statute and the effect of such omission of the proviso was not considered by the HC - Impugned judgment, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
