Moti Lal Saraf v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 561; (2006) 10 SCC 560; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 180; AIR 2007 SC 56; 2007 (1) ALT 312; 2006 CRLJ 4765; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 637; 2006(10) SCALE 12; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - Jammu and Kashmir Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(2) - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Speedy trial as read into art.21 as an essential part of fundamental right to life and liberty guaranteed and preserved under our Constitution is main issue - Removal from service - Repeated filing of challans - Held, appellant has been facing criminal prosecution for almost more than two and a half decades - In last twenty six years, not even a single prosecution witness had been examined - Proceedings taken by respondents against appellant were clearly an abuse of process of law - Permitting State to continue with prosecution and trial any further would be total abuse of process of law - Criminal proceedings are quashed - Appeal allowed.
