Suresh Prasad Singh v Dulhin Phulkumari Devi and others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 380; (2010) 6 SCC 441; 2010 (82) ALR 350; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 33; 2010(5) SCALE 771; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 1047

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1961, s. 16(3) - Right of pre-emption - Impugned land was sold by 'X' and 'Y' to respondent no. 1 by a registered sale deed on 04-08-1980 - Soon thereafter, appellant filed an application before the Deputy Collector u/s. 16(3) of 1961 Act claiming that he was a co-sharer and a boundary raiyat in respect of the land and that the land be transferred to him - Deputy Collector, however, rejected the application of the appellant - However, Additional Collector allowed appeal filed by appellant against said order - Board of Revenue who set aside the order passed by Additional Collector - Order of Board of Revenue was confirmed by Single Judge as well as DB - Appellant filed present appeal submitting that DB failed to appreciate that the right of pre-emption u/s. 16(3) of the Act was a statutory right and that he had filed an application u/s. 16(3) of the Act within three months of the date of registration of the sale deed as provided in s. 16(3) of the Act - On the other hand respondent No.1 contended that a claim for pre-emption made by a co-sharer will not be available u/s. 16(3) of the Act against the transferee who holds the land adjacent to the transferred land - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether appellant was a co-sharer of the transferor in the land which was transferred by way of sale to respondent No.1? - Held, yes - As per the records, the appellant and the transferors were co-sharers of the land transferred to respondent No.1 - (B) Whether appellant being a co-sharer of the transferor in land transferred to respondent No.1 had a statutory right of pre-emption u/s. 16(3) of the Act? - Held, yes - As the language of s. 16(3)(i) shows, any co-sharer 'shall be entitled' within three months of the date of registration of the document of the transfer, to make an application before the Collector for the transfer of the land to him on the terms and conditions contained in the transfer deed - It is not disputed that the appellant in fact made such an application within three months of the date of registration of the sale deed executed by the transferors in favour of respondent No.1 and also deposited the purchase money together with sum equal to 10% thereof in the prescribed manner within the period of three months as provided in the proviso of s. 16(3)(i) of the Act - Deputy Collector, therefore, had no discretion but to allow the application considering the mandatory nature of the right of pre-emption conferred by s. 16(3) of the Act - (C) Whether respondent no. 1 claim regarding boundary raiyat could be allowed? - Held, no - Section 16(3) confers the right of pre-emption not only on the co- sharer but also on the raiyat holding land adjoining to the land transferred - Complete stranger who was not originally a raiyat holding land adjoining to the land transferred cannot be allowed to defeat the right of pre-emption of a co-sharer by first purchasing an adjoining plot of land and thereafter claiming to be a raiyat holding land adjoining to the land transferred - Appeal allowed.
