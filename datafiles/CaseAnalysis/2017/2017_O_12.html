Manju Devi v Onkarjit Singh Ahluwalia @ Omkarjeet Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 438; AIR 2017 SC 1583; 2017 CRLJ 2879; 2017 (2) JCC 1296; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 421; 2017(3) SCALE 699

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Anticipatory Bail, Single Judge, Cognizance, Caste, Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 354, 452 - Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, ss.18, 3, 3(1), 3(1)(xi) - Anticipatory bail in offences against scheduled caste and scheduled tribes - Basing on complaint filed by complainant against respondents, FIR was registered u/ss. 323,354 and 452 of IPC and u/s.3(1)(xi) of the Act but after investigation, closure report was filed - However, dissatisfied with the report, Trial Court took cognizance of offence and process was issued against respondents for commission of offences under IPC and the Act - Aggrieved, respondents filed revision but the same was dismissed and orders of Trial Court were affirmed - Aggrieved, respondents filed petition before HC but Single Judge of HC confirmed orders of appellate court and on another petition for anticipatory bail, Single Judge of HC granted anticipatory bail to respondents to the satisfaction of Judicial Magistrate, 1st Class, Begusarai - Hence, instant appeal by complainant - Whether HC is justified in granting anticipatory bail to respondents.

Held, after careful consideration of materials on record, Trial Court and HC have found that prima facie case for taking cognizance against respondents is made out. S. 3(1)(xi) of the Act which deals with assaults or use of force to any woman belonging to SC or ST with the intent to dishonor or outrage her modesty is an aggravated form of the offence. The only difference between s.3(1)(xi) and s.354 is essentially the caste or the tribe to which the victim belongs. If she belongs to a Scheduled Caste or Schedule Tribe, s.3(1)(xi) applies. The other difference is that in s. 3(1)(xi) dishonor of such victim is also made an offence. Hence, in the light of specific averments in the complaint made by complainant, s.18 of the Act creates bar for invoking s.438 of the CrPC and HC committed grave error in granting anticipatory bail to respondents. Hence, order of HC granting anticipatory bail is set aside and respondents are granted four weeks time to surrender before court. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - S.438 of CrPC, which is available to an accused in respect of offences under IPC, is not available in respect of offences under the Act. The offences enumerated under the Act fall into a separate and special class.
