Susetha v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 804; (2006) 6 SCC 543; AIR 2006 SC 2893; 2007 (1) AWC 41; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 1; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 124; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 231; 2006(7) SCALE 640; 2006 (7) SCJ 252; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environmental Legislation

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 52-A - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Construction of shopping mall on site of disuse water tank - Doctrine of principle of sustainable development - Applicability of - Whether construction of a shopping complex at site of water tank in village is to be allowed? - Held, development of doctrine of sustainable development indeed is a welcome feature but while emphasizing need of ecological impact, a delicate balance between it and necessity for development must be struck - Whereas it is not possible to ignore inter-generational interest, it is also not possible to ignore dire need which society urgently requires - Respondents categorically denied and dispute that there is any water shortage in village - Village is situated on both sides of National highway - It is situated near a sea and having five water tanks in or around therein - It is, therefore, difficult to accept that there had been acute water shortage in village - It is to be directed that State and Gram Panchayat to see that other tanks in or around village are properly maintained and necessary steps are taken so that there is no water shortage and ecology is preserved - Appeal dismissed.
