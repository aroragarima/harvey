Parimisetti Seetharamamma v Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 294; AIR 1965 SC 1905; 1965 (2) ITJ 247; [1965] 57 ITR 532; 1965 (2) MLJ(SC) 100; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 8

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3, 4 and 66 - (A) On whom is burden of proof to establish that receipts are income? - Held, whatever is received by a person is not to be regarded as income liable to tax - In cases where receipt is sought to be taxed as income, burden is upon Revenue to prove that it is within taxing provision - (B) Whether there lies an appeal to Supreme Court under Income Tax Act?- Further held, when High Court has not considered evidence, normally Supreme Court would remand case for disposal - Supreme Court may hear and decide reference on merits - When proceeding has been pending for a very long time - It is in enforcement of orders of assessment that entire property of assessee has been attached - Appeal allowed.
