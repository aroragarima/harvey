Valliammal (D) By Lrs. v Subramaniam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2094; (2004) 7 SCC 233; AIR 2004 SC 4187; 2004 (57) ALR 439; 2004 (4) AWC 2971; JT 2004 (8) SC 442; 2004 (3) KLT 587; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 153; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 311; 2005 (98) RD 77; 2004(7) SCALE 297; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 966

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Title - Benami Transaction - Entitlement - Suit land was purchased by appellant/original plaintiff's wife for consideration of Rs.500/- on 5-12-1933 in court auction for sale consideration of Rs.500/- - It was alleged that original plaintiff got sale deed executed in favour of his wife as benami as thought it would not be safe for him to get sale deed executed in his name as some creditors might not create problem in future - Same suit land was executed as Will in favour of her daughters/defendants/respondents -Original plaintiff made averments that property was all along in his possession and that he continued to encumber property as its owner -After death of original plaintiff's wife on 2-1-1979 daughters started claiming right over suit property and tried to trespass into same - Plaintiff filed suit to establish his title over suit land and to get an injunction restraining defendants from disturbing his peaceful possession before Trial Court which gave order in favour of plaintiff - Aggrieved, defendants on appeal before Appellate Court which affirmed Trial Courts order - Defendants filed second appeal before HC which set aside Trial Court and Appellate Court's order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether sale in favour of original plaintiff's wife name was benami transaction, justifiable - Held, in Heirs of Vrajlal J. Ganatra Vs. Heirs of Parshottam S. Shah, 1996 Indlaw SC 3527 that question whether particular sale was benami or not, depends upon six circumstances which could be taken as guide to determine nature of transaction -i) Source which purchase money came - ii) Nature and possession of property of purchase - iii) Motive , if any for giving transaction benami colour - iv) position of parties and relationship, if any, between claimant and alleged benamidar - v) custody of title deeds after sale and conduct of parties concerned in dealing with property after sale - It was well settled that intention of parties was essence of benami transaction and money must had been provided by party invoking doctrine of benami - Evidence showed clearly that original plaintiff did not had any justification for purchasing property in name of his wife and reason given by him was not at all acceptable - Source of money was not at all traceable to appellant/plaintiff- No person named in plaint or anyone else was examined as witness, failure of plaintiff to examine relevant witnesses - Since appellant/original plaintiff failed to prove that he had provided money for purchase of land and reasons why he purchased property benami in name of his wife - Hence, HC had come to right conclusion that wife of original plaintiff did not hold property as benami on behalf of her husband - Appeal dismissed.
