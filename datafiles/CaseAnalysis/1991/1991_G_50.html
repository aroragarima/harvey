State Of Punjab And Ors. v Gurdev Singh, Ashok Kumar

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 224; (1991) 4 SCC 1; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1082; AIR 1992 SC 111; 1991 (17) ATC 287; 1991 (63) FLR 529; JT 1991 (3) SC 465; 1991 LabIC 2409; 1992 (1) LLJ 283; 1992 (1) LLN 38; 1991(2) SCALE 365; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 663; 1991 (5) SLR 1; 1992 (1) UPLBEC 50

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 120 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 2(J) and 3, arts. 113 and 120 - Dismissal - Validity of - Limitation of - Whether there is no limitation to bring a suit for declaration when dismissal of employee is illegal, void and in contravention of mandatory provisions of any rules or conditions of service? - Termination order was against principles of natural justice, terms and conditions of employment, void and inoperative and he continues to be in service - State resisted suit barred by time - Trial Court accepted plea of limitation and dismissed suit - On appeal Addl. District Judge, decreed suit holding that no limitation is prescribed for challenging an illegal order - High Court affirmed principle - S. 2(J) of Limitaion Act defines, 'prescribed period' - Period of limitation computed in accordance with provisions of Limitation Act - If suit is not covered by any of specific articles prescribing a period of limitation, it must fall within residuary article - Art. 113 of Limitation Act, 1908 is a residuary article for cases not covered by any other provisions in Act - Residuary article is applicable to every variety of suits not otherwise provided for - Suit for declaration that an order of dismissal or termination from service passed against plaintiff is wrongful, illegal or ultra vires is governed by art. 113 of Limitation Act, 1908 - Appeal allowed.
