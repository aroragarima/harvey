Messrs Tashi Delek Gaming Solutions Limited and v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 851; (2006) 1 SCC 442; AIR 2006 SC 661; 2006 (1) CTC 208; JT 2005 (10) SC 458; 2006 (1) KarLJ 417; 2005(10) SCALE 104; 2006 (1) SCJ 399; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 670

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Socio-Economic; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Socio-Economic - Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 131,14, 226, 6(1) - Lotteries (Regulation) Act, 1998, s. 5 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 182, 185, 186, 202, 230 - Declaration that State of Karnataka would be free zone from online and internet Lotteries - Prohibition of sale of all computerized and online lottery tickets marketed and operated through vending machines, terminals, electronic machines and through internet in State of Karnataka - Writ Petition by States of Sikkim and Meghalaya challenging legality of said notification on ground that State of Karnataka having itself organized lotteries could not have imposed said ban - Although HC accepted position that Appellants herein were statutory agents but it evidently failed to take into consideration status of Appellants vis-a-vis their contractual rights and obligations with their principal coupled with their individual rights to maintain their Writ Petitions in proper perspective - Had State of Sikkim or State of Meghalaya intended to sue State of Karnataka independently u/art. 131, only forum where dispute between them could have been resolved was this Court alone but when such a lis was brought by State jointly with their agents who had also independent cause of action and had a legal right to maintain writ application questioning legality and/or validity of said notification issued by State, a suit in terms of art. 131 would not have been maintainable - Appellants herein were not busy bodies - They had an interest in subject-matter of writ - They were, not merely strangers having no right whatsoever in matter - HC, unjustified in holding that states of Sikkim and Meghalaya had no personal right to enforce despite fact that they would suffer injuries or would otherwise be prejudiced by notification issued by State of Karnataka - Appeal allowed.
