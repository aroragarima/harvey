Prof. K. V. Rajendran v Superintendent of Police, CBCID South Zone, Chennai and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 520; (2013) 12 SCC 480; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 508; 2013 CRLJ 4464; 2013 (5) CTC 310; JT 2013 (12) SC 37; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 745; 2013(10) SCALE 513; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 199

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Liability, Transfer of case to CBI

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of case to CBI - Appellant was taken forcefully by Revenue Divisional Officer (RDO) and obtained signatures in blank papers and handed over to local police with statement written by officials concerned - Magistrate remanded appellant to judicial custody - Request for medical examination was rejected - On bail appellant filed complainant against RDO officials - Appellant filed petition before HC to direct registration of FIR based on complaint - Confidential report of DSP, SBCID revealed that preliminary enquiry was conducted in proper manner - HC did not transfer investigation to CBI, however, allowed appellants petition issuing direction to register case - Appellant filed petition to transfer case to CBI - HC ordered to transfer the case to CBI - DSP, SBCID filed petition before SC thus, transferring investigation to CBI by HC was set aside - Appellant again filed petition before HC to transfer investigation to CBI which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the case was liable to be transferred to CBI - Held, matter originated in 1998 and period of 15 years had been lapsed - Then RDO, against whom allegations were made, might have been transferred to various districts of State - Allegations of malafide had been made against police in general without impleading any person by name - During period of 15 years, investigation could have been carried out by many police officers - It should not be presumed that each of them could be influenced - SC gave liberty to appellant to approach HC for transferring investigation to CBI provided there was sufficient material available subsequent to earlier orders passed by HC - Even if investigating agency did not proceed promptly and was in deep slumber for long time, appellant did not make any attempt to move Court for issuance of appropriate direction to transfer case to CBI - It was at belated stage when HC was approached where final report was submitted before competent criminal Court - In view of earlier order SC, no subsequent development had been brought to notice of Court which could warrant interference by superior Courts and transfer investigation to CBI - Instant appeal lacked merit - Appeal dismissed.
