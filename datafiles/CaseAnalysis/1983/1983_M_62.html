Kunwar Ram Nath and Others v Municipal Board, Pilibhit

Supreme Court of India

02 June 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 272; (1983) 3 SCC 357; AIR 1983 SC 930; 1983 (2) Crimes 216; 1983 CRLJ 1289; 1983 CrLR(SC) 403; 1983(1) SCALE 672; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 321; 1983 UJ 728

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Uttar Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1916, ss. 155, 120, 128, 114, 133 and 157(3) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 561(A) - (A) Whether on relevant date appellants were liable in law to pay octroi on sugarcane brought by them by railway into sugar factory which was situated within municipal limits of Pilibhit? - Held, sum received as octroi is also dealt with like any other tax - There is no element of quid pro quo between person who pays octroi and Municipal Board - Hence, octroi being a tax it was competent to Provincial Govt. to make an order u/s. 157(3) of Act exempting railborne sugarcane from payment of octroi - (B) Whether appellants having failed in an earlier writ petition can now challenge levy again in these proceedings? - Held, yes - In year 1960, by a notification published in Official Gazette D/- 23-04-1960 Municipal Board of Pilibhit promulgated new rules relating to levy of octroi - Appellants who were liable to pay octroi on many of those articles challenged in a petition filed under art. 226 of Constitution - That petition was dismissed by High Court - HC was in error in holding that order of exemption granted u/s. 157(3) was same as any general rule or special order of State Govt. referred to in sub-s.(1) of s. 128 subject to which Municipal Board may impose any tax referred to therein - An order u/s. 157(3) operates only after a tax is validly imposed with sanction of State Govt. or of Commr. as stated in s. 133 of Act - It is, therefore, open to appellants to contend even after dismissal of their earlier petition that they are entitled to limited exemption granted by order of 1936 - Appeal allowed.
