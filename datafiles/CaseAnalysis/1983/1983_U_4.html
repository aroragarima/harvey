Shiv Dutt Rai Fateh Chand and Others v Union Of India & Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 54; (1983) SCC (Tax) 204; (1983) 3 SCC 529; AIR 1984 SC 1194; [1984] 148 ITR 664; 1983(1) SCALE 590; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 198; 1983 (53) STC 289; 1983 TAXLR 2917

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Constitutionality, Inter-State sales or purchases, Imposition and collection of penalties

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 20(1), 19(1)(f) and (g) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 9(2-A) - Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Haryana General Sales Tax Act, 1973, s. 48 - Haryana General Sales Tax Rules, 1975, r. 4(1) - Inter-State sales or purchases - Imposition and collection of penalties - Validity - Petitioners were dealers and having business in different States - Petitioners filed instant batch petitions u/art. 32 of Constitution questioning Constitutional validity of s. 9(2-A) of 1956 Act (Act No. 74 of 1956) as amended by 1976 Act (Act No. 103 of 1976), Amending Act, and s. 9 of Amending Act Validating the levy of penalties under the Act with retrospective effect - Petitioners contended that introduction of s. 9(2-A) of 1956 Act did not have the effect of making the provisions related to penalties leviable under the general sales tax laws of States applicable to proceedings under the 1956 Act - Parliament could not adopt the provisions related to penalties in general sales tax laws, of States for enforcing the charge under the Act, as such a course would amount to an abdication of its essential legislative function by Parliament - Further contended that provision giving retrospective effect to s. 9(2-A) of 1956 Act and provision validating all the penalties levied prior to coming into force of Amending Act were violative of cl. (1) of art. 20 of Constitution and that levy of penalties with retrospective effect was violative of art. 19(1)(f) and (g) of Constitution - Whether impugned provisions of 1956 and 1976 Act were constitutionally valid - Held, there was no dispute in instant case about validity of tax payable under 1956 Act during 1-1-1957 and the date of commencement of Amending Act, 1976 - There was ni dispute that law required the dealers to pay tax within specified time and dealers had the knowledge of provisions relating to penalties in general sales tax laws of their respective States - Word 'penalty' used in art. 20(1) of Constitution could not be construed as including a 'penalty' levied under sales tax laws by departmental Authorities for violation of statutory provisions penalty imposed by sales tax authorities was only a civil liability, though penal in character - S. 9(2-A) of 1956 Act specifically referred to certain acts and omissions which were offences for which a criminal prosecution would lie and provisions relating to offences were not given retrospective effect by s. 9 of Amending Act - imposition and collection of penalty with retrospective effect did not amount to an imposition of an unreasonable restriction on fundamental right of petitioners to own property and to carry on business guaranteed u/art. 19(1), (f) and (g) of Constitution - In circumstances it becomes necessary to levy penalties with retrospective effect and to validate all proceedings relating to levy of penalties and recovery thereof - S. 48 of 1973 Act itself suggested that the levy to be made under it was in nature of a penalty which required the authority concerned to apply his mind to all relevant aspects of the default alleged to have committed by a dealer - First the default committed by dealer should be established at an enquiry after giving the dealer concerned an opportunity of being heard - Degree of remissness involved in default was a relevant factor to be taken into account while levying penalty - Section provided both minimum and maximum amount of penalty leviable and it was correlated to amount of tax which would have avoided if the turnover returned by such dealer was accepted as correct - It was not possible to hold that s. 48 of 1973 Act conferred an uncanalised, unguided and arbitrary power on authority levying penalty - Impugned order of penalty was passed by Assessing Authority-cum-Excise and Taxation officer, by State Govt. u/s. 2(a) r/w s. 3 of 1973 Act and r. 4(1) of the Rules - Amending Act expressly made the levy of penalties as per general sales tax laws in force in States permissible with retrospective effect - Khemka and Company v. State of Maharashtra, 1975 Indlaw SC 416, referred to - Petitions dismissed.
