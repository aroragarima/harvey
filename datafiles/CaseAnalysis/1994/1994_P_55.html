Commissioner, Food And Civil Supplies v Prakash C. Saxena

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1529; (1994) 5 SCC 177; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1042; 1994 (27) ATC 817; 1994(3) SCALE 12; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 930; 1995 (1) SLJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination Simpliciter

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Whether termination of respondent by way of punishment and in violation of art. 311 of Constitution? - Held, upholding appellant's power to terminate services of a temporary Govt. servant under rules, without holding an enquiry, Court do not propose to disturb reinstatement of Respondent, since he was in service as a temporary Govt. servant for nearly 20 years before his services were terminated and he has since been retired as well from service - Taking these facts into consideration, it was held by Court that respondent must be deemed to have been in service from date of termination till date of his superannuation, but he is not entitled to back wages from date of termination till date of his filing petition in Services - Appeal allowed.
