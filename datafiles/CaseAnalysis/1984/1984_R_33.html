Â A. R. Antulay v Ramdas Sriniwas Nayak and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 337; (1984) SCC (Cr) 277; (1984) 2 SCC 500; AIR 1984 SC 718; 1984 (2) ALT 272; 1984 (1) Bom.C.R. 345; 1984 (86) BomLR 228; 1984 (1) Crimes 547; 1984 CRLJ 647; 1984(1) SCALE 239B; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 914; 1984 (1) SLR 666

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Private Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Indian Companies Act, 1956, s. 621 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952, ss. 6 and 8 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, s. 61 - Gold Control Act, 1968, s. 97 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 271 and 279 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5 A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 161 and 165 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 6, 24, 167, 190, 193, 200, 202 and 321 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 251 A and 256 - Import and Export Control Act, 1947, s. 6 - Electricity Supply Act, 1948, s. 77 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s. 187 A - Whether a private complaint can be entertained by a Special Judge in respect of all or any of offences enumerated u/s. 6 of Criminal Law Amendment Act? - Held, there is no merit in contention that by entertaining a private complaint, purpose of speedy trial would be thwarted or that a pre-process safeguard would be denied - Further when cognizance is taken on a private complaint or to be precise otherwise than on a police report, Special Judge has to try case according to procedure prescribed for trial of warrant cases instituted otherwise than on police report by a Magistrate - Appeal dismissed.
