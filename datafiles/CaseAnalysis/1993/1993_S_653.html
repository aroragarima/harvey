General Manager, Kerala S.R.T.C v Susamma Thomas

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1302; (1994) 2 SCC 176; (1994) SCC (Cr) 335; 1994 (1) ACC 346; 1994 ACJ 1; AIR 1994 SC 1631; AIR 1994 SCW 1356; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 573; 1994 (3) KarLJ 39; 1994 (1) KLT 67; 1993(4) SCALE 643

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Actio Personalis Moritur Cum Persona, Assessment Of Damage

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicle Act, 1939 - Motor Vehicle Act, 1939 - Determination of quantum of compensation - Deceased, aged 38 years, employed in the newspaper establishment on a monthly salary of Rs. 1032 died in an motor-accident - Claim petition filed - Motor Accidents Claims Tribunal awarded a compensation of Rs. 58,760 to the respondents-claimants - On appeal, HC enhanced the compensation to Rs. 2,64,000 - HC, in addition to the usual award for loss of dependency, made award for special damages for funeral expenses, journey to the hospital, treatment expenses etc. - HC also determined the amount of compensation on account of loss of dependency to the tune of Rs. 1,80,000 - Present appeal - (A) Determination and calculation of compensation in fatal accident actions by multiplier method - Held, multiplier method involves the ascertainment of the loss of dependency or the multiplicand having regard to the circumstances of the case and capitalizing the multiplicand by an appropriate multiplier - Choice of the multiplier is determined by the age of the deceased (or that of the claimants whichever is higher) and by the calculation as to what capital sum, if invested at a rate of interest appropriate to a stable economy, would yield the multiplicand by way of annual interest - In, ascertaining this, regard should also be had to the fact that the capital sum should also be consumed-up over the period for which the dependency is expected to last - Further, multiplier represents the number of years' purchase on which the loss of dependency is capitalised - Multiplier method is the appropriate method, a departure from which can only be justified in rare and extraordinary circumstances and very exceptional cases - (B) Ascertainment of damages - Held, assessment of damages to compensate the dependants is beset with difficulties because from the nature of things, it has to take into account many imponderables, e.g., the life expectancy of the deceased and the dependants, the amount that the deceased would have earned during the remainder of his life, the amount that he would have contributed to the dependants during that period, the chances that the deceased may not have lived or the dependants may not live up to the estimated remaining period of their life expectancy, the chances that the deceased might have got better employment or income or might have lost his employment or income altogether - Further, the manner of arriving at the damages is to ascertain the net income of the deceased available for the support of himself and his dependants, and to deduct therefrom such part of his income as the deceased was accustomed to spend upon himself, as regards both self-maintenance and pleasure, and to ascertain what part of his net income the deceased was accustomed to spend for the benefit of the dependants - Then that should be capitalised by multiplying it by a figure representing the proper number of year's purchase - (C) Whether claimants were over-compensated and that the principles of assessment of compensation do not support the award of Rs. 1,80,000? - Held, deceased was 39 years of age and his income was Rs. 1032 per month - Further, having regard to the prospects of advancement in the future career, monthly income should be ascertained at Rs. 2000 as the gross income - Hence, Rs. 1,400 per month or Rs. 17,000 per year as the loss of dependency and if capitalized on a multiplier of 12 which was appropriate to the age of the deceased, the compensation would work out to (Rs. 17,000 x 12= 2,04,000 rupees) to which is added the usual award for loss of consortium and loss of the estate each in the conventional sum of Rs. 15,000 - In all, a sum of Rs. 2,25,000 should be fair, just and reasonable award in the circumstances of this case - Claim made for loss of future earnings of Rs. 50,000 on the prospects of future employment in USA was rightly negatived by the Tribunal as award under this head is clearly unjustified in the facts of the case - Judgment of the HC modified - Appeal partly allowed.
