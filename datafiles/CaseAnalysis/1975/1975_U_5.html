Krishna Chandra Gangopadhyaya Etc. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 171; (1975) 2 SCC 302; AIR 1975 SC 1389; 1975 CRLR 375; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mines And Minerals

Summary: Constitution - Mines & Minerals - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 24 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Bihar Mines Mineral Concession Rules, 1964 - Whether a statute and a rule earlier declared by Court unconstitutional or otherwise invalid, can be retroactive through fresh validating legislation enacted by competent Legislature? - If a notification had been issued properly under provisions of earlier Act and its validity could not have been impeached if said provisions were themselves valid, it would be deemed to have been validly issued, provided, of course, it is not inconsistent with other provisions - It is a saving and validating provision and it clearly intends to validate actions taken under relevant provisions of earlier Act which was invalid from start - Fact that s. 24 does not use usual phraseology does not alter position that second part of section - Held, Parliament desired to validate retrospectively what Bihar legislation had ineffectually attempted - It has used words plain enough to implement its object and therefore validating Act as well as consequential levy are good - Further held, source of authority for rule-making being of Parliament, it is indubitable that power to give it life retrospective exists - Writ Petition dismissed.
