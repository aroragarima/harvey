Â New Maneck Chowk Spinning and Weaving Company, Limited v Textile Labour Association, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1961 SC 867; 1961 (1) LLJ 521; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Minimum Bonus, Rehabilitation, Labour Appellate Tribunal, Living Wage, Available Surplus, Maximum Bonus, Full Bench Formula, Trade Union Act, 1926, Bombay Industrial Relations act, misa

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1947, ss.73A, 42 - Notice of change - Demand for bonus - Justifiability - Appellants owned textile mills, entered into agreement with respondent for payment of bonus by appellants to their employees - Agreement was made for period of five years from 1953 - 1957 - Disputes arose when agreement had ended and notice of change u/s. 42 of the Act was given by respondent - Aggrieved appellants made reference u/s. 73A of the Act before tribunal - Tribunal had ordered for extension of agreement for the year 1958 and directed appellants to file calculation of bonus payable for said year - Hence, instant appeals - Whether tribunal was right in law in extending agreement for another year - Held, appeals should be allowed and matter should be sent back to tribunal for disposing of issue before it in accordance with law - And directed that tribunal should proceed to try question whether any bonus should be awarded to employees of appellants on the basis of Full Bench formula interpreted in case of Associated Cement Companies, 1959 Indlaw SC 21 - Hence, according to opinion of majority appeals were allowed and matter was remitted back to Tribunal - Order accordingly.
