Maya Devi (Dead) Through Lrs. v Smt. Raj Kumari Batra (Dead) Through Lrs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 815; (2010) 9 SCC 486; 2011 (84) ALR 674; 2010 (6) AWC 5441; JT 2010 (9) SC 602; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 866; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 831; 2011 (1) MPLJ 326; 2010 (160) PLR 410; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 795; 2011 (112) RD 372; 2010(9) SCALE 262; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1113

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 rr. 72 and 84 - Execution of Mortgage decree - Plaintiff filed a suit against defendant for recovery of a sum of Rs. 60,000/- - Said suit was decreed in favour of plaintiff - In execution of the said decree, residential property of defendant was attached and finally sold in a public auction - for a sum of Rs.82,000/- in favour of the decree holder - Judgment debtor filed his objections challenging the legality of the auction, but while the same were pending consideration, the parties put in a written compromise provided that the decree holder would deposit a sum of Rs.35,000/- for payment to the judgment debtor, whereupon the latter shall handover to the decree holder the vacant possession of the property aforementioned that stood attached - Executing Court made an order whereby it confirmed the sale in favour of the decree holder in accordance with the compromise between the parties - Judgment debtor filed objection submitting that impugned property was exempt from attachment and sale as the same being a residential premises and the decree in question being a simple money decree - Executing Court dealt with these objections and noted that the issues raised by the judgment debtor had already been decided against him by the earlier orders of the Executing Court which orders had attained finality - Said order was assailed by the judgment debtor in Execution First Appeal which was dismissed by a Single Judge - A Letters Patent Appeal preferred against the said order also dismissed by DB - Hence, present appeal assails the correctness of the said order, submitting that Executing Court had committed a serious irregularity in the matter of directing attachment of property of the judgment debtor and issuing a sale proclamation - Whether order of the DB could be upheld? - Held, yes - In the present case the appellate Court appears to have decided against remanding the matter to the Single Judge on the ground of absence of reasons in the order passed by the latter because any such remand would have only prolonged the agony of the parties as litigation had been prolonged for many years - It, therefore, decided to resolve the matter on merits rather than remitting the same back for a fresh disposal by the Single Judge - In as much as the appellate Court adopted that approach it did not, commit any mistake to warrant any interference - Impugned order of HC upheld - Impugned order of DB upheld - Appeal dismissed.
