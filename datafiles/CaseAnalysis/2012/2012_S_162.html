Guru Basavaraj @ Benne Settappa v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 262; (2012) 8 SCC 734; 2012 (3) ACC 782; 2013 ACJ 216; 2012 CRLJ 4474; 2012 (4) JCC 2515; JT 2012 (8) SC 246; 2013 (2) KarLJ 544; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 263; 2012(8) SCALE 47; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal

Keywords: Acquitted

Summary: Criminal - Carriers & Transportation - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 279, 337, 338 and 304A - Death by negligence - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was driving an unregistered new tractor on national highway - The tractor turned turtle towards the left side and caused simple injuries to many people who were sitting inside the trailer of the tractor and grievous injuries to 3 persons - 1 injured succumbed to the injuries sustained in the accident - Trial court convicted appellant for the offences punishable u/ss. 279, 337, 338 and 304A of the IPC - Appellate Judge concurred with the view of the Trial court and sustained the conviction in respect of all the offences but set aside the sentence imposed for the offence punishable u/s. 279 of the IPC - On appeal, HC upheld the order of the Appellate Judge - Hence, the instant appeal - Appellant contended that the said accident occurred due to mechanical failure and not because of rash and negligent driving - Further contended that when appellant has been acquitted u/s. 279 of the IPC, he cannot be punished in respect of the other offences as the allegation of rash and negligent act cannot be treated to have been proven - (A) Plea of accident occurred due to mechanical failure - Held, the manner in which the accident occurred due to detachment of the trailer from the tractor and the distance to which the tractor moved vividly reveals that the vehicle in question was driven recklessly at a high speed - The plea of mechanical failure as put forth by the appellant was not even suggested to the Inspector - The analysis of the factual score in this regard cannot be regarded to be perverse and, therefore, not liable to be unsettled by SC - (B) Rash and negligent driving not proven - Held, the Appellate Court held that a separate sentence u/s. 279 of the IPC was not necessary and, accordingly, the said sentence has been set aside - The reading of the entire judgment makes it graphically clear that the conviction u/s. 279 of the IPC has not been annulled - It was noticeable that the rash and negligent driving by the appellant that resulted in the causation of injuries to the persons travelling in the trailer has been proved - There was no cavil that some have been seriously injured and 1 person who was grievously injured breathed his last - (C) Compassion on the ground of young age - Held, compassion was being sought on the ground of young age and mercy was being invoked on the foundation of solemnization of marriage - The date of occurrence was in the month of March, 2006 - The scars on the collective cannot be said to have been forgotten - Weighing the individual difficulty as against the social order, collective conscience and the duty of the Court, SC was disposed to think that the substantive sentence affirmed by the HC does not warrant any interference - Impugned order of the HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
