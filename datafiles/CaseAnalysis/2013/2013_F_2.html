Fakhruzamma v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 826; (2013) 15 SCC 552; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 354; 2014 CRLJ 681; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2131; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 364; 2014 (1) RLW 166; 2013(15) SCALE 159; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 824

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mysore Police Act, 1908, Sanction to prosecute

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 456, 323, 504, 506, 342, 386, 201, 120B and 304 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Jharkhand Police Manual, r. 824 - Criminal conspiracy - Cognizance - Sanction to prosecute - Legality - Appellant (accused) was Sub-inspector of police; had allegedly committed offences u/ss. 456, 323, 504, 506, 342, 386, 201, 120B and 304 of IPC - Trial Court took cognizance of against appellant - Aggrieved appellant challenged the same before HC, stating that Magistrate could not take cognizance of offences in absence of previous sanction of State Govt. as per s. 197 of CrPC - HC by impugned order held that competent Authority had removed the appellant from service, sanction to prosecute u/s. 197 CrPC was not warranted - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether appellant was liable to be prosecuted without prior sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC - Held, impugned order of HC was upheld - Nagraj v. State of Mysore, 1963 Indlaw SC 159, followed - Appeal dismissed.
