Sales Tax Officer and Another v Shree Durga Oil Mills and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1219; (1998) 1 SCC 572; AIR 1998 SC 591; 1998 (97) E.L.T. 202; JT 1997 (10) SC 155; 1997(7) SCALE 726; 1998 (1) SLT 488; 1998 (108) STC 274; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 488; 1997 (10) Supreme 418

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Promissory estoppel - Applicability against Govt. - Withdrawal of tax exemption contrary to Industrial Policy announced for particular period - Plea of estoppel - Held, not tenable - As Industrial Policy Resolution of Govt. and notification under Sales Tax Act are subject to change of policy in public interest at any time - Govt. could not back out from commitments made in resolution is no ground to dismiss appeal - Point involved in appeal is of general public importance.
