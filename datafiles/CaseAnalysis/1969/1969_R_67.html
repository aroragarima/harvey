Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Rn. Ar. Ar. Veerappa Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 199; (1970) 1 SCC 174; [1970] 76 ITR 467; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 67; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 307; 1970 UJ 225

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(3)(vii) and 66(1) - Liability to Income Tax on share of assessee in amount of interest paid on estate duty which was refunded by Ceylon Govt. after Judicial, Committee set aside order levying the estate duty - Whether High Court erred in holding that assessee's share in amount of interest received from Ceylon Govt. was of capital nature? - Held, after death of 'A' property was held by three widows as members of HUF - Under Hindu Law it is not predicted of a Hindu Joint family that there must be a male member in existence - Even after death of sole male member, so long as property which was originally of Joint Hindu family remains in hands of widows of members of family and is not divided among them, joint family continues - Payment of estate duty was doubtless made out of joint family fund and interest which accrued due, also acquired character of joint family property when receive - Joint family status came to an end only on 17-02-1947 - On severance of joint status assessee became entitled to a share in family estate - Amount of interest on estate duty accrued as income to joint family but it was income of joint family and not of individual members - But when a share out of estate which included interest on estate duty was received by assessee it had not character of income - Once income was received by joint family, amount lost its character of income, it became merged in joint family assets and became capital of family - Share received by assessee was therefore a share in capital of family - Share in joint family property which included interest on estate duty which accrued prior to 17-02-1947 was rightly held by HC to be not of nature of revenue and accordingly not taxable - Appeal dismissed.
