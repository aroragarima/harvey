Paradip Port Trust v Sales Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1706; (1998) 4 SCC 90; AIR 1999 SC 552; JT 1998 (4) SC 483; 1999 (114) STC 178; 1998 (9) Supreme 149

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 366(29-A)(d) - Interpretation of the words "transfer of the right to use any goods" - Whether assessment for sales tax has been made in respect of matters which do not fall within the legislative power of the State to impose sales tax? - High Court dismissed the writ petitions filed by the appellant on the view that the alternative remedy of an appeal against order of assessment is available under the sales tax law - Held, having regard to the question involved, HC should have entertained the writ petitions and should have considered the said question instead of requiring the appellant to avail the remedy of appeal under the Sales Tax Act - Appeal allowed.
