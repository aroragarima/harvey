Harshadsingh Pahalvansingh Thakore v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 368; (1976) 4 SCC 640; (1977) SCC (Cr) 26; AIR 1977 SC 710; 1977 CRLJ 352; 1976 CRLR 481; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 626; 1976 UJ 891

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, arts. 134 and 136 - Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 300 - (A) Scope of Supreme Court's jurisdiction in a Criminal Appeal - (B) Murderous assault by more than one - Plea of extrication - (C) Acquittal of 3 out of 4 accused - Whethar s. 34 can be invocated - (A) SC cannot be persuaded, to go over the ground of reading the evidence and interpreting it anew so as to uphold that which appeals to it among possible alternative views - If there is perversity, miscarriage of justice, shocking misreading or gross-misapplication of the rules, procedural and substantive, SC will interfere without hesitation - Of Course, other exceptional circumstances also may invoke the SC's review jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed .
