Raj Kumar Singh v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 512; (1986) 4 SCC 407; (1986) SCC (Cr) 481; AIR 1986 SC 2173; 1986 (3) Crimes 252; 1986 CRLJ 2042; 1986 CrLR(SC) 462; JT 1986 SC 555; 1986(2) SCALE 541; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 913; 1986 (2) UJ 614

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Bihar Control Of Crimes Act, 1981 - Women And Girls Act, 1956 - Arms Act, 1959 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 142, 149, 302, 307, 326, 353, 333, 395, 224, 225 and 34 - Arms Act, s. 27- Preventive Detention - Petitioner detained by order of Magistrate as he was an anti-social element and habitually committed offences - Whether detention proper? - Proximity would be relevant in order to determine whether an order of detention was arrived at irrationally or unreasonably - When power is given to an authority to act on certain facts and if that authority acts on relevant facts and arrives at a decision which cannot be described as either irrational or unreasonable, in a sense that no person instructed in law could have reasonably taken that view, then order is not bad and court cannot substitute its decision or opinion, in place of decision of authority concerned on necessity of passing order - Preventive detention as reiterated is hard law and must be applied with circumspection rationally, reasonably and on relevant materials - Held, petitioner a hardcore habitual offender, detention order proper - Preventive detention a necessary evil for social protection - Appeal dismissed.
