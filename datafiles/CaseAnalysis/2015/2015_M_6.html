Canara Bank and another v M. Mahesh Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 371; (2015) 7 SCC 412; AIR 2015 SC 2411; 2015(4) ALL MR 419; 2015 (146) FLR 401; JT 2015 (5) SC 156; 2016 (1) MahLJ 594; 2015 (4) MLJ 479; 2016 (1) MPLJ 262; 2015(6) SCALE 540; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 724; 2015 (5) SLR 243

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Dying in Harness Scheme, 1993

Summary: Service - Dying in Harness Scheme,1993, cl.3.2 - Dying in Harness Scheme, 2005 - Dying in Harness Scheme,2014 - Compassionate appointment - Ex-gratia payment - Applicability - Respondent's father, was clerk in appellant-bank, who was died while on duty - Respondent applied for compassionate appointment on account of 'Dying in Harness Scheme' which was rejected by appellant - Respondent gave his representation to General Manager of appellant for reconsideration of his claim; but nothing fruitful happened - Respondent filed petition before HC which was allowed and Single Judge directed to appellant to reconsider claim of respondent for appointment - Appellant filed appeal wherein DB of HC upheld order of Single Judge and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether Scheme passed in 2005 providing for ex-gratia payment or Scheme then in vogue in 1993 providing for compassionate appointment is applicable to respondent.

Held, 2005 Scheme providing only for ex-gratia payment in lieu of compassionate appointment stands superseded by 2014 Scheme which has revived the scheme providing for compassionate appointment. Appellant is not justified in contending that the application for compassionate appointment of the respondent cannot be considered in view of passage of time. Insofar as the contention of the appellant that since the respondent's family is getting family pension and also obtained the terminal benefits, is of no consequence in considering the application for compassionate appointment. Cl.3.2 of 1993 Scheme says that in case the dependant of deceased employee to be offered appointment is a minor, the bank may keep the offer of appointment open till the minor attains the age of majority. This would indicate that granting of terminal benefits is of no consequence because even if terminal benefit is given, if the applicant is a minor, the bank would keep the appointment open till the minor attains the majority. Thus, considering the scope of the 1993 Scheme then in force HC rightly directed the appellant to reconsider the claim of the respondent for compassionate appointment in accordance with law and as per 1993 Scheme then in existence. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - When person is entitled to compassionate appointment as per provisions of scheme, no authority is entitled to deny his appointment.
