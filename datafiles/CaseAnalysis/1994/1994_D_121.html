Ram Dular v Dy. Director Of Consolidation

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1593; (1994) Supp2 SCC 198; 1994 (1) HLR 360; JT 1994 (3) SC 341; 1994 RD 290; 1994(2) SCALE 338; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 735

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953, s. 48 - Deputy Director held on revision that fabricated revenue records for 1306 and 1308 Fasli cannot be relied on - He held that entries in revenue records for 1307 Fasli only name of X was found - So appellant cannot be said to be legal representative from branch of Y - As a result entire ancestral property would pass on to X's legal representative - Whether Deputy Director of Consolidation was legally justified in upsetting finding recorded by Consolidation Officer and Settlement Officer? - Held, Consolidation Officer recorded genuineness of entries for year 1308 Fasli which was not even disputed by respondents - In entries for 1308 Fasli name of Y was found as son of Z and was mutated - This vital aspect was omitted to be taken into consideration by Deputy Director - Deputy Director on other hand concluded that for year 1308 Fasli also name of Y was fabricated - Further held, thus it is an obvious error committed by Deputy Director and HC refused to correct it on plea that it is only a finding of fact - Appeal allowed.
