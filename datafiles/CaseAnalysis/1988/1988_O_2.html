Odyssey Communications Pvt. Limited v Lokvidayan Sanghatana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 520; (1988) 3 SCC 410; AIR 1988 SC 1642; 1988 (4) Bom.C.R. 194; JT 1988 (3) SC 66; 1988(2) SCALE 34; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 486; 1988 (2) UJ 325

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: Constitution - Media & Entertainment - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, rr. 1 and 2 - Freedom of expression - Telecast of serial on Doordarshan - Denied as it was against public interest - Alleged of promoting blind faiths, superstitious beliefs in stories of ghosts, rebirth, precognition etc. - High Court in an interim order stayed telecast - Held, that prima facie no evidence of grave prejudice likely to be caused to public by exhibition or to endanger public morality, and not allowing telecast would infringe fundamental right of freedom of expression of producer - Interim orders of HC are set aside - Appeal allowed.
