Tamil Nadu Housing Board and Others v Sea Shore Apartments Owners Welfare Association

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1466; (2008) 3 SCC 21; AIR 2008 SC 1151; 2008 (1) AWC 514; 2008 (1) CCC 33; 2008 (3) CGLJ 101; 2008 (1) CompLJ 260; 2008 (1) CPJ(Sc) 45; 2008 (1) CTC 245; JT 2008 (1) SC 318; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 278; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 517; 2008 (3) RLW 2073; 2008(1) SCALE 201; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 370; 2008 (1) UC 415

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Land & Property

Keywords: Illegal, Tamil Nadu Housing Board Act, 1961

Summary: Constitution - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(i)(g) - Land and Property - Appeal filed against order to refund the payment with interest passed by State Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission and confirmed by National Commission - Deficiency in by Housing Board - Ultimate cost of flat was subject to the outcome in the award of compensation in land acquisition proceedings - Price mentioned in advertisement was tentative - Allotter want to go behind the concluded contract between the parties - Board contended that allottees were estopped from raising the contention that additional amount could not have been recovered from them - Held, it was open to the allottees not to pay the additional amount and not to take possession - Orders passed by both the Fora are, liable to be set aside, remitting the cases to the State Commission - Order accordingly.
