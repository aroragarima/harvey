L. I. C. of India and others v Krishna Murari Lal Asthana and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 263; (2016) 6 SCC 515; AIR 2016 SC 2003; 2016(3) ALL MR 937; 2016 (149) FLR 735; JT 2016 (3) SC 564; 2016 (3) MLJ 608; 2016(3) SCALE 689; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 837; 2016 (3) SLR 725

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Conditions Of Service, Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, Relief, High Courts, Pay, Pension, Rule, Date, Validity Of, Appeal, Dearness Relief, Scheme, Cut-Off Date, Life Insurance corporation of India (employees) Pension Rules, 1995, Writ Petition, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Service - Life Insurance Corporation Act, 1956, s. 48 - Grant of pensionary benefit - Approval of Central Govt. - Justification - Life Insurance Corporation of India (Employees) Pension Rules, 1995, r. 55 - Respondent corporation decided to grant dearness relief to its employees for which employees were divided into three categories regard being had to date of retirement - However, there were three different rates prescribed for groups depending upon their date of retirement, hence, resolution was passed for amendment of Rules for upgradation of basic pension - However, after passing of resolution, amendment to Rules did not take place - Lower Court directed Corporation to take decision for implementation of resolution and every retied employee may get same benefit - DB vide impugned order held that Central Govt. did not file any appeal against resolution of Board, hence, it is considered that approval has been granted - Whether approval of Central Govt. is necessary for grant of benefit to retired employees.

Held, taking into consideration r. 55 of Rules, SC held that it does not confer power on Chairman of Corporation to issue any instructions which are beyond Rules and Chairman is authorised to issue instructions which are necessary and expedient for implementation of Rules. Further, SC held that Board can pass resolution and Chairman can be head of Board, but it does not authorize Board to take decision with regard to certain matters which are within domain of rule-making authority. Further, as per s. 48 of Act, conferment of benefit, either pension or anything ancillary thereto has to be conferred by Rules and same has to be tabled before Parliament. Hence, SC held that in absence of Rule, no benefit can be granted on basis of resolution passed by Corporation. Hence, SC held that lower Court could not have held to contrary on basis of concession given by counsel for Union of India. However, in view of grant of interim measure, SC directed that Corporation shall pay 40% as per Para 3A of Appendix to each of employees and payment shall be subject to final results in writ petitions. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Resolution passed for granting benefit without jurisdiction and without taking into consideration Rules frames is liable to be quashed aside.
