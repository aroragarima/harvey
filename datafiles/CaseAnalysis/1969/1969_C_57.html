Raja Bahadur Kamakhya Narain Singh v Commissioner of Income Tax Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 96; (1969) 3 SCC 791; AIR 1971 SC 794; [1970] 77 ITR 253; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Government securities, Debenture Holder, Question Of Fact And Law

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Determine whether contemplated transactions in gold and shares were by way of realisation of investments or were adventures in nature of trade or business - Assessee was at material time a landholder of a large estate - Transactions were not diversified nor were gradual according to opportunities offered by fluctuating market prices, but were in bulk and almost at a time, which ordinarily were not characteristics of dealings of a person carrying on trade or business in them - Gold was not purchased with intention of trading in it as it was held by assessee for long and was not disposed of when market was at maximum and he could have made considerable gain - Shares were acquired with intention of obtaining control over a company's management and not for dealing in them - Held, transactions as contemplated were not in nature of trade or business - Appeal allowed.
