Â Sardar Syedna Taher Saifuddin Saheb v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 423; AIR 1962 SC 853; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; International

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Human Rights - Universal Declaration of Human Rights - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 26, Cl. (b) and art. 25(2) - Bombay Prevention of Excommunication Act, 1949 - (A) Whether religious rights are violated by excommunication under art. 26(b) and art. 25(1) of Constitution? - Held, exercise of rights under art. 26(b) of Constitution does not effect civil rights involving excommunications prejudicial to public order - Art. 25(1) includes religious freedom and excommunication of a person only restricts performing certain rights within community which are of civil nature and not religious - (B) What is purpose of an excommunication? - Further held, excommunication must ensure preservation of religious practices in a community - Petition allowed.
