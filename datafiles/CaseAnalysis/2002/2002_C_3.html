Michael B. Fernandes v C. K. Jaffar Sharief and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1944; (2002) 3 SCC 521; AIR 2002 SC 1041; 2002 (47) ALR 338; 2002 (5) AWC 4333; 2002 (1) CRC 437; JT 2002 (2) SC 87; 2002(2) SCALE 157; 2002 (2) SCJ 68; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1053; 2002 (2) SLT 39; 2002 (2) Supreme 1; 2002 (1) UC 536

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute, Any Other Candidate

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 61, 61A, 79, 80, 81, 82, 83, 86, 100(1)(d), 101 and 116A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 324 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Whether deletion of respondents 6, 7 and 8 from array of parties was justified? - On a plain reading of s. 82, which indicates as to person who can be joined as a respondent to an election petition, conclusion is irresistible that returned candidate, candidate against whom allegations of any corrupt practice have been made are to be joined as party respondent when declaration is sought for holding election of returned candidates to be void and when a prayer is made as to any other candidate to be declared to be duly elected, then all contesting candidates as required to be made party respondents - Relied on Jyoti Basu and others v. Debi Ghosal and others (1982 Indlaw SC 110) - It was held that contest of election petition is designed to be confined to candidates at election and all others are excluded and, therefore, only those may be joined as respondents to an election petition, who are mentioned in ss. 82 and 86(4) and no others - Public policy and legislative wisdom both seem to point to an interpretation which does not permit joining, as parties, of persons other than those mentioned in ss. 82 and 86(4) - Court also considered consequences if persons other than those mentioned in s. 82 are permitted to be added as parties and held that necessary consequences would be an unending, disorderly election dispute with no hope of achieving goal contemplated by s. 86(6) of Act - Appeal dismissed.
