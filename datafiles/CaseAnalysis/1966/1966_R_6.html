Lallan Prasad v Rahmat Ali and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1967 SC 1322; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 233

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Pawnee, Pawn

Summary: Contract - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 172, 173, 174, 175 and 176 - Appellant advanced money to first respondent against a promissory note and a receipt - First respondent also executed an agreement to pledge as security for debt said aeroscraps and to deliver them at appellant's house - Appellant's claim that first respondent failed to deliver said goods to him, stored them in a plot adjacent to aerodrome at Allahabad and therefore said agreement did not ripen into a pledge - (A) Whether first respondent pledged certain quantity of aeroscraps purchased by him from military authorities and delivered possession thereof to appellant under an agreement of pledge entered into between them? Held, in view of these facts, said goods were delivered to appellant, that he was a pledgee thereof and that said agreement did not rest at stage of a mere agreement to pledge - (B) Whether appellant was entitled to any relief when his case was that first respondent never delivered to him said goods and said agreement never ripened into a pledge? - Pawner has right to redeem goods so pledged by payment of debt - If pawnee is not in a position to redeliver goods he cannot have both payment of debt and also goods - Held, appellant would not be entitled to a decree against said promissory note and also retain said goods found to have been delivered to him and therefore in his custody - For, if it were otherwise first respondent as pawner would be compelled not only to pay amount due under promissory note but lose the pledged goods as well - Appeal dismissed.
