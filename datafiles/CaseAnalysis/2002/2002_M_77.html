Collector of Central Excise, Bombay v Maharashtra Fur Fabrics Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1108; (2002) 7 SCC 444; AIR 2002 SC 3482; 2002 (83) ECC 738; 2002 (105) ECR 1; 2002 (145) E.L.T. 287; JT 2002 (7) SC 435; 2002 (52) RLT 629; 2002(7) SCALE 70; 2002 (5) SLT 587; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 544; 2002 (6) Supreme 568

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - whether the respondent is entitled to the benefit of Notification No. 109/1986-C.E. dated 27th February, 1986, as amended by Notification No. 3/1988-C.E. dated 19th January, 1988? - The notification discloses that the benefit available to silver pile fabrics falling under Heading 58.01 or 60.01 of the schedule to the Act is lost if the product is subjected to the process of bleaching, dyeing, printing, shrink proofing, tentering, heat-setting, crease-resistant processing or any other process or any two or more of these processes. - Held, the benefit of the notification cannot be availed by the respondent since, the process adopted by the respondent is analogous to stentering, as admittedly, the respondent is drying the fabric by passing through the hot air stenter.
