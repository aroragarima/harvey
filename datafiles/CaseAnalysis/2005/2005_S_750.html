Rajesh Kumar Gupta and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 380; (2005) 5 SCC 172; (2005) SCC (L&S) 668; AIR 2005 SC 2540; 2005 (5) AWC 4764; 2005 (2) ESC 291; 2005 (106) FLR 411; 2005 (106) FLR 840; JT 2005 (6) SC 12; 2005(4) SCALE 657; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1171; 2005 (5) SLR 1; 2005 (4) Supreme 262

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution - Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 15(3), 16, 16(2), 21, 45, 226, 350-A - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education Act, 1972 - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993, ss. 3, 16 - Uttar Pradesh Basic Education (Teachers) Services Rules, 1981 - Quashing of certain govt. orders relating to selection for training of candidates for appointment as Asst. Teachers in the primary schools run by the U.P. Basic Education Board (the 'Board') in question - Whether Petitioners having applied for Special BTC training course and having failed to get their name in merit list are estopped from challenging advertisement and selection made pursuant thereto? - Whether State Govt. preparing merit list at District level instead of State level is violative of arts.15 and 16? - Whether provisions of art 350-A are attracted in present case? - Nothing was established to show that they altered their position to their detriment by applying pursuant to the advertisement - Action of the State govt., restoring the preparation of merit list from State level to district level without any relevant and justifiable reason, arbitrary and violative of arts. 15(1) and 16(2) - Restriction of the selection and preparation of merit list at the district level was arbitrary and violative of arts.15(1) and 16(2) - No material on record for the High Court to show that the case of any linguistic minority was involved - Held, no fault can be found with impugned judgment of HC that special BTC training course formulated by State Govt. was contrary to provisions of impugned Act and Rules and 1993 Central Act - Judgment under appeal is justified and sustainable - Appeal dismissed.
