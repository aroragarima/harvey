Manuelsons Hotels Private Limited v State of Kerala and others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 378; (2016) 6 SCC 766; AIR 2016 SC 2322; 2016 (4) AWC 3919; JT 2016 (5) SC 96; 2016 (2) KLT 694; 2016 (4) MLJ 463; 2016(5) SCALE 120; [2016] 3 S.C.R. 718

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Mandamus, Customs Act, 1962, Sales Tax, Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, Subordinate Legislation, General Clauses Act, 1897, Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Constitution of India, 1950, Kerala Buildings Tax Act, 1975, Kerala Buildings Tax Amendment Act, 1990, Kerala Buildings Tax Rules, 1974

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Kerala Buildings Tax Act, 1975, s. 3A - Promissory Estoppel - Payment of building tax - Relying on G.O. dt.11-7-1986 issued by State Govt. inter alia granting exemption for classified hotels, from 1 to 5 stars from payment of Building Tax, and also on approval from Govt. of India for its hotel project, appellants started construction of hotel building and the same was completed in 1991 - However, notices were issued to appellant for filing returns under the Act and the same was challenged before HC wherein orders were passed to appellants to pursue their claim before Committee set up under G.O. dt.11-7-1986 - However, by letter dt.6-2-1997, exemption promised by said G.O. was denied to appellants and notices were again issued for filing of returns under the Act and the same was challenged before HC again wherein orders were passed to consider matter afresh in light of judgments of SC - However authorities again rejected application of appellants and aggrieved by the same, appellants challenged said orders before HC vide writ petition and but the same was rejected by HC holding that exemption for appellants from building tax would not arise as s.3A itself was omitted w.e.f.1-3-1993 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellants are entitled for property tax exemption.

Held, s.3A was enacted by State Legislature by suitably amending the Act in order to give effect to representation made by G.O. dt.11-7-1986 and said provision continued on statute book and was deleted only with effect from 1-3-1993. Hence, from 6-9-1990 to 1-3-1993, the power to grant exemption from building tax was statutorily conferred by s.3A on Govt. Further, as per statements and objects of the Act, reason for introducing s.3A was to fulfill one of the promises contained in G.O.dt.11-7-1986. Hence, as appellants, relied on said G.O., and constructed hotel building by 1991, non-issuance of a notification u/s. 3A was an arbitrary act of Govt. and the same is to be remedied by application of doctrine of promissory estoppel. Further, ministerial act of non issue of notification could not possibly stand in the way of appellants getting relief under the said doctrine for it would be unconscionable on part of Govt. to get away without fulfilling its promise. Furthermore, no other consideration of overwhelming public interests exits in order for Govt. to justify rescinding from its promise. Hence, for period that s. 3A was in force, no building tax is payable by appellants. However, for period post 1-3-1993, no statutory provision for grant of exemption being available, no relief could be given to appellants as doctrine of promissory estoppel has to yield when it is found that it would be contrary to statute to grant such relief. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Promissory estoppel could be the basis of an independent cause of action in which detriment does not need to be proved and it is enough that a party has acted upon representation made.
