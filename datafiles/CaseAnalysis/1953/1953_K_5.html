Kalipada Chakraborti and Another v Palani Bala Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1953 SC 125; 1953 (1) MLJ(SC) 597; 1953 SCJ 208; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 503

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation Act, 1859

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 124, 141, 3 and 2(8) - Limitation Act, 1859 - Alienation of shebait right by widow challenged in suit by reversioner against alienee - Whether plaintiff's suit is barred by limitation u/art. 124?- Intention of legislature is obviously to treat hereditary office like land for purpose of barring suits for possession of such office and extinguishing right to possession thereof after a certain period - Held, art. 124 relates to a suit for possession of a hereditary office and period of limitation prescribed for such suit is 12 years from date when defendant takes possession of office adversely to plaintiff/appellant - Appeal allowed.
