N. S. K. Nayar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 420; (1992) Supp2 SCC 508; (1992) SCC (L&S) 954; AIR 1992 SC 1574; 1992 (21) ATC 695; 1992 (64) FLR 354; JT 1991 (6) SC 525; 1992 LabIC 1532; 1992 (2) LLN 644; 1991(2) SCALE 1340; 1991 (6) SLR 155; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 402; 1992 (1) UJ 467; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1348

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Telegraphic Engineering Service (Class I), Rules, 1965, r. 27(b) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 309 and 311 - Officiating appointment - When ceases to be so? - Rule providing for officiating appointments, to meet administrative exigency of short tenure - Appointment made under rule continued for 10-15 years - Cannot be considered as officiating/temporary appointments - Denial of right of regularisation and consequential benefits - If arbitrary - Held, denial to such appointees is arbitrary - Petition allowed.
