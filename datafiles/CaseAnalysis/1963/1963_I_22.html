Meenglas Tea Estate v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 113; AIR 1963 SC 1719; 1963 (7) FLR 106; 1963 (2) LLJ 392; 1964 (1) SCJ 98; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 134 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 112 - (A) Whether procedure of tribunal would be erroneous as contravening s. 134 Evidence Act, when it would insist on corroboration of testimony given in an enquiry conducted by assaulted officers themselves? - Held, yes, it is erroneous - A person who is required to answer a charge must know not only accusation but also testimony by which accusation is supported - Given a fair chance to hear evidence in support of charge and to put such relevant questions by way of cross-examination as he desires - Rebut evidence led against him - Against natural justice - (B) Whether Supreme Court can enter into evidence to find facts in an appeal from award of Industrial Tribunal? - Further held, no - In appeal by special leave from an award of Industrial Tribunal in respect of dismissal of certain workmen on grounds of misconduct, it is not practice of Supreme Court to enter into evidence with a view to finding facts for itself - Appeal dismissed.
