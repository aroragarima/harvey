High Court of Judicature For Rajasthan v Ramesh Chand Paliwal and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 161; (1998) 3 SCC 72; (1998) SCC (L&S) 786; AIR 1998 SC 1079; 1998 (2) AWC 1085; 1998 (1) CLT 155; JT 1998 (2) SC 1; 1999 (1) LLJ 885; 1999 (1) LLN 290; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 11; 1998(1) SCALE 636; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 961; 1998 (3) SLJ 65; 1998 (2) SLR 200; 1998 (3) SLT 23; 1998 (2) Supreme 249

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 229 - Rajasthan High Court (Conditions of Service of staff) Rules, 1953 - Posts in HC - Directions of HC - Sustainability - Chief Justice made the Rules, which were amended from time to time by administrative orders - Respondent no. 2 was promoted as Deputy Register post in accordance with the Rules as amended on 28-2-1992, and, not in accordance with Rules prevalent on 1-2-1992 therefore, said promotion was challenged before HC by respondent no. 1 - HC vide judgment dt. 28-9-1993, passed by 2 judges, gave directions to Registrar to submit report whether posts on which officers from Rajasthan Judicial Service were appointed on deputation could be manned by HC staff and further directed such report to be placed before Full Court for consideration of other Judges on administrative side - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned directions of HC were justified - Held, Judge of HC individually or all Judges sitting collectively, as in Full Court, could not either alter Constitutional provisions or the Rules made by Chief Justice, they had no jurisdiction even to suggest any constitutional amendment or amendment in the Rules made by Chief Justice - Nor could they create any avenue of promotion for HC staff so as to be appointed on posts meant for Officers from Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service or Rajasthan Judicial Service - Further, it should not be lost sight that Registrars, under Rules of various HCs, have also to perform some limited judicial functions which could not be done by officer other than Judicial Officer in HC establishment - Judges had attempted to indirectly exercise power of appointment on certain posts in HC establishment on which appointment could be made only by Chief Justice - Judges who disposed of matter were themselves of opinion that this question was not required to be decided for effective decision of Writ Petition pending before them, as such, they should have stopped there and should not have proceeded to give impugned direction to Registrar of HC particularly as it was difficult to believe that Cadre strength of Rajasthan Judicial Service or Higher Judicial Service was so weak or depleted that no substitute could be provided for 8 officers (maximum under Rules) placed on deputation in HC - Impugned direction whether posts in HC on which officers on deputation were appointed could be managed by HC staff was patently contrary to mandate of art. 229 vesting HC Administration in Chief Justice and purports to encroach upon his authority - Judgment dt. 28-9-93 passed by 2 Judges, in so far as it related to direction to Registrar was set aside, judgment in all other respects was upheld - Appeal allowed.
