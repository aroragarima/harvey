M/S. Ghcl Employees Stock Option Trust v India Infoline Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 178; (2013) 4 SCC 505; AIR 2013 SC 1433; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4423; [2013] 177 Comp Cas 527; 2013 CRLJ 2044; 2013 (2) JCC 1347; JT 2013 (2) SC 567; 2013 (2) MLJ(Crl) 534; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 519; 2013 (3) RLW 1965; 2013(4) SCALE 598; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: prima facie, Issuance of summons

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 415, 409, 34, 120B - Issuance of summons - Prima facie - Sustainability - Appellant/complainant had filed a complaint alleging commission of offences u/ss. 415, 409, 34, 120B IPC - Trial Court satisfied that, prima facie case was made out and directed issuance of summons against respondents to face trial u/ss. 415, 409, 34, 120B IPC - Aggrieved respondents filed separate petitions before HC - HC by the impugned order held that issuance of summons against respondents Nos. 2 to 7, were set aside and in case of respondent No. 1 issuance of summons u/s. 415 IPC also could not be sustained - Trial Court was directed to proceed with the trial against respondent No. 1 u/ss. 409, 34, 120B IPC - Hence instant appeals by appellant - Whether averments constitute offences u/ss. 406, 409, 420, 477A, 34, 120B IPC - Held, in the entire complaint there were bald and vague allegations against respondent Nos. 2 to 7 - There was no dispute with regard to the legal proposition that the case of breach of trust or cheating were both a civil wrong and a criminal offence, but under certain situations where the act alleged would predominantly be a civil wrong, such an act did not constitute a criminal offence - Be that as it may, summoning of accused in a criminal case was a serious matter - Hence, criminal law could not be set into motion as a matter of course - Order of Trial Court summoning accused should reflect that he had applied his mind to the facts of case and law applicable thereto - Trial Court had to record his satisfaction with regard to the existence of a prima facie case on basis of specific allegations made in complaint supported by satisfactory evidence and other material on record - Trial Court had not recorded his satisfaction about prima facie case as against respondent Nos. 2 to 7 and the role played by them in the capacity of Managing Director, Company Secretary or Directors which was sine qua non for initiating criminal action against them - Impugned order of HC was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
