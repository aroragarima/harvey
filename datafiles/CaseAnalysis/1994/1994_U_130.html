Delhi Domestic Working Women's Forum v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1120; (1995) SCC (Cr) 7; (1995) 1 SCC 14; 1995 (1) BLJR 1; 1994 (3) Crimes 597; JT 1994 (7) SC 183; 1994(4) SCALE 608; 1995 (1) SCJ 94; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Sexual Assault - Compensation - Six women, domestic servants, were travelling by train - Army men who were traveling in the same train sexually assaulted them - Victims registered FIR at the police station u/s. 376-B r/w s. 341, IPC - Members of petitioner Delhi Domestic Working Women's Forum was very much concerned as the victims were its members, to get the needed social, cultural and legal protection - Neither the Central Govt. nor the State Govt. had bestowed any serious attention as to the need for provision of rehabilitatory and compensatory justice for women - Prayer of petitioner that Respondents nos. 1 to 3 would have to engage themselves in framing an appropriate scheme to provide inter alia compensation and rehabilitation to the victims of such crimes of violence - This stand was opposed by respondent no. 3 - Hence, instant petition - Whether compensation payable by the offender - Held, that the Criminal Justice Act, 1991 contained a number of provisions which directly or indirectly encouraged an even greater role for compensation - Having regard to these provisions, respondent no. 3 was required to take necessary steps to evolve such a scheme - Petition dismissed.
