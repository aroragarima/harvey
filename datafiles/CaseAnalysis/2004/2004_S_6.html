State of Punjab and Others v S. C. Chadha

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1385; (2004) 3 SCC 394; (2004) SCC (L&S) 469; 2004 (2) AWC 1879; 2004 (3) CHN 14; 2004 (3) CLR 9; 2004 (102) FLR 636; 2004 (2) LLN 793; 2004 (3) MahLJ 688; 2004 (2) MLJ(SC) 177; 2004 (3) MPLJ 134; 2004(2) SCALE 337; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 216; 2004 (2) SLR 741

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Punjab Civil Services Rules Vol. I, Part I - Alteration of date of birth - Whether change in date of birth made within period permitted by amended rules justified? - Held, opportunity granted to correct date of birth did not take away effect of inaction and continued silence for more than three decades, which dehors laches on respondents part seriously reflecting on bona fide nature of claim itself - No contemporaneous document produced to show recording of date of birth wrong - High Court unjustified in directing correction of birth - Relied on State of Orissa and Ors v Ramanath Patnaik, (1997 Indlaw SC 818) - When entry made in service record and no attempt done to correct record, any evidence produced subsequently of no consequence - Appeal allowed.
