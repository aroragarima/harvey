Â State Trading Corporation of India Limited and Others v State of Mysore and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1963 SC 548; 1963 (14) STC 416

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Mysore Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 3(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(2) - Constitution (Sixth Amendment) Act, 1956 - Assessment year 1956-57 - Sales of cement - Sales took place between 01-04-1956, and 31-03-1957 - Goods were moved from a place outside Mysore into State of Mysore as a result of contracts of sale - Whether certain sales were inter-State sales so that it was beyond power of State Legislature to tax them? - Inter-State sales contemplated by art. 286(2) were same as those defined in s. 3(a) of Central Sales Tax Act - Held, goods moved from outside State of Mysore into it under procedure which prevailed in present case were all inter-state sales - Therefore, State of Mysore had no power to tax these sales either - Petition allowed.
