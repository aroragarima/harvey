Matukdhari Singh and Others v Janardan Prasad

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1966 SC 356; 1966 CRLJ 307; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 255

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, ss. 210, 417(3) and 423(1)(a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420, 486, 467, 468, 461, 465 and 471 - Five appellants were tried on a complaint by respondent for offences u/ss. 420, 468, 406 and 465/471, IPC, 1860 - They were acquitted - First class magistrate framed charges against accused persons - Failure to frame charge u/s. 467 IPC, 1860 - Magistrate tried case himself under other sections and acquitted accused - (A) Whether magistrate is to ignore offences on which he had no jurisdiction and choose trial for others ? - Held, it was improper for Magistrate to ignore offences over which he had no jurisdiction and to choose for trial only such offences over which he had - It was clearly his duty to frame a charge u/s. 467 IPC, 1860 and to commit accused to Court of Session - (B) Whether High Court had jurisdiction to order retrial by framing a charge u/s. 467? - Held,the HC acted within its jurisdiction when it set aside acquittal of appellants and made an order for their retrial in terms it did - Appeal dismissed.
