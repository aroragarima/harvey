Â Shri Ram Prasad (Deceased) By His Legal Representative v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 15; AIR 1966 SC 1607; 1967 (15) FLR 443; 1967 (1) LLJ 438; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 486

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 357(2) - Patiala Regulation and Management Order, 1954 - Service - Promotion - Termination - Legality - Appellant entered into service and was promoted on various post at various times and then he was compulsorily retired from service but was subsequently reinstated by Govt. as order was legally defective - Board passed order compulsorily retiring appellant from service - Appellant filed suit and challenged order of compulsory retirement passed by Board in which Subordinate Judge granted decree substantially allowing claims made by him - Respondent filed appeal in HC which allowed appeal and set aside judgment of Subordinate Judge - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of compulsory retirement passed by Board against appellant was invalid - Held, Regulation Order was not published in Official Gazette, but cl. 1(b) of Order provided that it should come into force at once and repeal all, previous Orders and instructions - However, by reason of fact that Order itself provided for its commencement as date on which it was made, it was clear that Order came into operation on such date, though it was published at later date - Thus, it should be held that all clauses of Regulation Order, including cl. 4(1)(iii), came within purview of saving clause occurring in art. 357(2) of the Constitution and they continued to be in operation after period specified in that article - Further, delegation made by President in favour of Board under Regulation Order did not lapsed on termination of Presidents rule in PEPSU - Therefore, it was clear that extension of rules governing conditions of service of Govt. servants to employees of State Bank was result of extension made by Maharaja pursuant to executive power vested in him - Hence, it was clear that what was changed or superseded was extension and not rules - Appeal dismissed.
