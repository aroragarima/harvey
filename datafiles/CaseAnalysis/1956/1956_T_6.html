Â Bhagubhai Dullabhabhai Bhandari v District Magistrate, Thana and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 25; AIR 1956 SC 585; 1956 (58) BomLR 1007; 1956 CRLJ 1126; 1956 SCJ 565; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Tort

Keywords: City of Bombay Police Act, 1902

Summary: Tort - Constitution - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. (2) of s. 10, 56, 56(b), 59 - Externment - Applicability - Maintainability - Petitioner was citizen of India and carried on trade in grass - Deputy Superintendent of Police and Sub-Divisional Police Officer served notice u/s. 56 of the Act and petitioner was called upon to appear before police officer - petitioner appeared, before the police officer aforesaid and the hearing of his case took place - petitioner claimed to have examined seven "respectable persons" to testify on his behalf - Order was passed by District Magistrate externing petitioner outside District - order of externment was petition and contained recitals that after considering evidence and explanation offered by petitioner District Magistrate was satisfied that petitioner - order was challenged as illegal and ultra vires and against which petitioner had moved this Court for appropriate writ, direction or order against respondents - petitioner had preferred an appeal to Government against said order of externment - Appeal was dismissed - petitioner moved HC u/art. 226 of Constitution - Application was also dismissed in limine by the High Court - Hence, instant petition - Whether order of externment passed against petitioner was maintainable - Held, in Gurbachan Singh v. State of Bombay, 1952 Indlaw SC 20 it was held that after examining constitutionality of s. 27(1) of the Act operative words of that section were almost exactly same as those of s. 56 of the Act - It was not necessary to re-examine constitutionality of those very provisions - It was enough to point out that no attempt was made in this Court to bake authority of that decision - If petitioner had only taken course indicated by law he could have avoided prosecution and conviction - s. 56 of the Act was held not unconstitutional and that orders passed against petitioners were not invalid - Petition dismissed.
