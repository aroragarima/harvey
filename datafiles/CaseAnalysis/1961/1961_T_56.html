Â Chandi Prasad Chokhani v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 73; AIR 1961 SC 1708; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 193; [1961] 43 ITR 498; 1962 (1) SCJ 138; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 276; 1961 (12) STC 506

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Practice

Summary: Sales Tax- Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1944, ss. 4, 5, 13, 23, 24 and 25(1) - Whether as a matter of practice of this Court, appellant is entitled to be heard on merits in three appeals when special leave was neither asked for nor granted in respect of subsequent orders of High Court relating to assessments in question which have now become final between parties thereto? - Held, there must be exceptional and special circumstances to justify exercise of discretion u/art. 136 - It would be inappropriate on part of Supreme Court to enter upon an adjudication of these appeals on merits - Appeal dismissed.
