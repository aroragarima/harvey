Â K.N.Shukla v Navnit Lal Manilal Bhat and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1967 SC 1331; 1967 CRLJ 1200; 1967 (2) LLJ 261; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 290; 1967 SLR 213

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Railway Act 1890, Allocation of Business Rules, 1961, Indian Tramways Act, 1886

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1872, ss. 166, 167 and 182 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 195(1)(a) and 197 - General Clauses Act, 1872, s. 3(8)(b) - Railway Board Act, 1905, s. 2 - Prosecution of railway employee without sanction of central Govt. - Whether prosecution can be instituted on a Railway Officer officiating in Class I of Transportation without sanction of Central Govt.? - Railway Officer officiating in class I of transportation (traffic and commercial) department is not an officer under Central Govt. but is under Railway Board - Held, Prosecution can be instituted without sanction of Central Govt., since official is an employee under Railway Board - Appeal dismissed.
