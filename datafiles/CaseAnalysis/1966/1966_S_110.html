Nagarajan (B. N.) and Others v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 3; AIR 1966 SC 1942; 1967 (15) FLR 332; 1967 (1) LLJ 698; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 682

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Summary: Administrative - Service - Mysore Public Service Commission Functions Rules, 1957 rr. 3 , 4 - Mysore Public Works Engineering Department Services Recruitment Rules, 1960 - Appointment - Recruitment Rules - Maintainability - Mysore Public Service Commission issued notification for appointment of assistant engineers, 40 per cent by direct recruitment by Public Service Commission after interview and oral test, 50 per cent by promotion from cadre of Junior Engineers, and 10 per cent by promotion from cadre of Supervisors and interviewed list of 80 candidates and sent list to Govt. - Appellants filed writ petition challenged appointment of candidates on ground that on 3-12-1960, under proviso to art. 309 of the Constitution Governor had made rules regulating recruitment to posts of Assistant Engineers, and that under those rules, forty per cent of appointments alone could be made by Public Service Commission after interview and oral test which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appointments made on 31-10-1961 could be upheld - Held, Court had come to conclusion that said appointments could be considered to had been validly made in exercise of executive power of State u/art. 162 of Constitution - Notifications issued by Public Service Commission was with consent of State Govt. and said notifications were not rules made u/art. 309 of Constitution, they were mere executive notifications - Therefore rules purported to be made by Mysore State Govt. had no retrospective validity, there were no statutory rules to govern appointment of 88 persons as Assistant Engineers - Thus, the Rules, 1957, did not debar Govt. from making appointments without making statutory rules - Hence, appointments were valid - Appeals allowed.
