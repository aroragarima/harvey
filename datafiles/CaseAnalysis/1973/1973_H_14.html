Â Hariprasad Mulshanker Trivedi v V. B. Raju And Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, S.M. Sikri, M. Hameedullah Beg, S.N. Dwivedi, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 311; (1974) 3 SCC 415; AIR 1973 SC 2602; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1950, ss. 16, 19 and 30 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 2(1)(e), 3 and 100(1) - Constitution, arts. 13, 326 and 327 - (A) Bar to jurisdiction of Civil Court - Election petition - (B) Constitutionality of statute - Practice of Supreme Court - Held, Court cannot go behind electoral roll to see if elector is ordinarily resident of the constituency in which his name is enrolled - It is a wise tradition with court not to adjudicate a constitutional question unless it is absolutely necessary to the disposal of the case in hand - Appeal partly allowed.
