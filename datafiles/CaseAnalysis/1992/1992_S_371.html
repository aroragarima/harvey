Polaki Motors and Others v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Veeraswami Ramaswami, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 672; (1993) Supp2 SCC 674; 1993 (75) CLT 1; JT 1992 (6) SC 51; 1992(2) SCALE 780; 1993 (88) STC 259; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 994

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Orissa Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2, 3-B, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 8 - Orissa Additional Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1983, ss. 3 and 17 - Orissa Additional Sales Tax Act, 1975,ss. 2, 3, 4 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s.15 - Orissa Additional Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1979,s. 3 - Additional Sales Tax Rules, 1975, r. 5 - (A) Whether Orissa Additional Sales Tax (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1983 is constitutionally valid as it now levied additional tax on gross turnover and not on taxable turnover which will amount to a multi-point tax against very scheme of Principal Act? - Successive transactions would become liable for levy of additional tax and levy becomes multi-point; in order to give effect to multi-point scheme, s. 3 as amended provides that levy of additional sales tax would be 'notwithstanding anything contained in ss. 4, 5 and 8' of Principal Act - Held, impugned Act cannot be held to be beyond legislative competence; by choosing some transactions of sale of goods alone for levy of sales tax in first instance legislature does not exhaust its legislative power in field of tax on sale of goods; law does not require entire gamut under topic of tax on sales to be covered in first or one enactment itself on peril of losing its legislative competence on that topic or field; there being no legal or constitutional bar for a combination of single-point levy and a multi-point levy and levying of additional tax, there is no infirmity or constitutional inhibition which would invalidate impugned Validation Act - (B) Whether, Validating Act was rightly given retrospective operation by s. 3? - Held, by enacting retrospectively a valid and legal taking provisions law creates a fiction that assessments made and tax collected are under new law; s. 3(5) of Validating Act made all provisions of Principal Act including those relating to appeal, revision, reference and penalty as applicable in relation to additional sales tax payable; r. 2(c) of Rules defines 'Assessing Authority' as meaning Assessing Authority under Orissa Sales Tax Act; r.5 requires assessing authority assessing tax under Principal Act to simultaneously assess amount of additional tax payable by a dealer and r 7 states that provisions of Orissa Sales Tax Rules shall mutatis mutandis apply in respect of all procedural and to matters incidental to carrying out of purposes of Act; therefore, all assessment and collection provisions under Principal Act and rules framed there under are attracted and would apply for assessment and collection of additional tax as well; notification issued u/s. 17 of Principal Act delegating powers of Commissioner therefore would automatically apply insofar as officers authorised to assess and collect are concerned; there was no need for a further delegation of power in respect of Orissa Additional Sales Tax are concerned - Appeal dismissed.
