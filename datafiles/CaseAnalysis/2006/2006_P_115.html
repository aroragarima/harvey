Jai Narain Parasurampuria (Dead) and others v Pushpa Devi Saraf and others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1450; (2006) 7 SCC 756; 2006 (65) ALR 163; 2007 (1) CompLJ 69; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1224; 2006(8) SCALE 477; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 325; 2006 (Supp) RD 430; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 325; 2006 (3) UC 1901

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Fraudulent, Unfair Advantage, Frivolous, U.P. Rent Control Act, Housing Act, 1996, Cantonments (House-Accommodation) Act, 1923, Lord Cairns' Act, Orissa House Rent Control Act, 1967

Summary: Corporate - Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Companies Act, 1956 - Property in question was purchased by Promoters of company, namely, 'S', the Directors and shareholders of Company since on date of execution of deed of sale company was not registered - On its registration, company paid a sum of money to 'S' - Suit was filed by company through 'S' praying for a declaration that property belonged to company - Said suit was decreed declaring that company was absolute owner thereof - Thereafter, Board of Directors of company adopted a resolution for sale of said property in favour of appellant - Company had also adopted another resolution for leasing out property to 'M' - Deed of lease in favour of 'M' was also signed by 'S' - Dispute arose regarding ownership of property - In the backdrop of several litigations between parties and allegations and counter allegations made by one party against other therein, Trial Court decreed appellants' suit for specific performance of contract - On appeal, DB of HC allowed appeal on premise that Trial Court wrongly exercised its discretionary jurisdiction u/s. 20 of Specific Relief Act, 1963; as appellants were guilty of demolition of existing structures on land - Hence, present appeals - (A) Ownership of property - Held, for all intent and purport Company was owner of property and at all material times 'S' had made representations as such to appellants as also to others thereabout - In balance sheet, income tax return, annual report, audit accounts and other relevant documents, company had been shown to be owner of property - Company had taken a loan from Bank upon mortgaging property as a security - Only because at time of acquisition of property by 'S', Company was unincorporated, same would not mean that no title could have been passed in favour of Company - Further, in view of their conduct, 'S' were estopped and precluded from denying and disputing title of Company over property in dispute - (B) Unincorporated Corporation - Held, in terms of s. 15(h) of Specific Relief Act, Promoters of a company before its incorporation could enter into a contract for benefit of company and such contract may be warranted by terms of incorporation of company - Said provision is subject to proviso that company should accept said transaction - In instant case, it was done - Further, s. 19(e) of Act applies to present case as it provides for grant of a decree of specific performance of a contract against a company when promoters of a company before incorporation entered into a contract for purpose of company and such contract is warranted by terms of incorporation - (C) Lifting Corporate Veil - It was submitted that Court failed to apply doctrine of lifting corporate veil, as same was necessary for determining real issue between parties - Held, application of doctrine becomes relevant in view of fact that in Memorandum of Association of company 'S' alone were shown to be subscriber members of company - In Article of Association they were inducted as first Directors - Subsequently they included their son as a Director; and it was all three of Directors who executed agreement for sale - There had, thus, been no shareholder except 'S' - Since, they had been attempting to use personality of company for furthering their own personal object doctrine of lifting veil is applicable - They did so in furtherance of their dishonest and fraudulent design - (D) Demolition of house - Held, prima facie demolition of house took place at instance of appellants - However, it was not a case where appellants entitled to a decree for specific performance of contract - Respondents should refund amount of advance with interest and furthermore pay compensation - Appeals disposed of.
