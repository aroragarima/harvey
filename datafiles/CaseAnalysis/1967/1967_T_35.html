National Iron and Steel Company Limited and Others v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 473; AIR 1967 SC 1206; 1967 (14) FLR 356; 1967 (2) LLJ 23; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 391

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 18(1), 18(3), 25 F and 33 -Appellants are four registered public limited companies against whom tribunal has passed awards -(A) Whether orders are valid as all companies were not concerned in all items of dispute? - Held, that orders were valid as National Iron and Steel Co. Ltd. played a major part in adjudication and other three concerns were content to abide by same - (B) Whether scheme of gratuity be binding on all concerns? - Held, that scheme of gratuity framed was a reasonable one and hence would apply on all companies - (C) Whether retrenchment of certain employee was justified? - Held, it was justified as retrenchment could only be struck down if it was mala fide or if it led to victimisation of workmen - (D) Whether abolishing system of contract labour is fundamentally wrong? - Held, that Industrial Tribunal can abolish system depending on facts - Appeal dismissed.
