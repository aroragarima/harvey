Neelamma and others v Sarojamma and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1607; (2006) 9 SCC 612

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss.12, 16, 16(3) - Joint Hindu family property - Right of illegitimate child - Respondent was an illegitimate child belonging to appellant family - Respondent claimed her share in which HC held that u/s. 16(3) of Act, illegitimate child would be entitled to succeed/claim share in joint Hindu family property as well - Whether an illegitimate child can acquire/claim as of right a share in joint Hindu family property.

.

Held, HC relying upon judgment of a Single Judge of HC in G. Nirmalamma v G. Seethapathi case has held that u/s. 16(3) of Act, illegitimate child would be entitled to succeed/claim a share in joint Hindu family property as well. This view of HC is contrary to law laid down by this Court in Jinia Keotin v. Kumar Sitaram Manjhi case. In said case this Court, interpreting same provisions of Act, has come to conclusion that an illegitimate child cannot succeed/claim a share in joint Hindu family property. Such illegitimate child would only be entitled to a share in self-acquired property of parents. In said case, Court held that so far as s. 16 of Act is concerned, though it was enacted to legitimise children, who would otherwise suffer by becoming illegitimate, at same time it expressly provides in s.16 (3) of Act by engrafting a provision with a non obstante clause stipulating specifically that nothing contained in s.16 (1) of Act or s.16 (2) of Act shall be construed as conferring upon any child of a marriage, which is null and void or which is annulled by a decree of nullity u/s. 12 of Act, 'any rights in or to property of any person, other than parents, in any case where, but for passing of Act, such child would have been incapable of possessing or acquiring any such rights by reason of his not being legitimate child of his parents'. In light of such an express mandate of legislature itself, there is no room for according upon such children who but for s. 16 of Act would have been branded as illegitimate any further rights than envisaged therein by resorting to any presumptive or inferential process of reasoning, having recourse to mere object or purpose of enacting s. 16 of Act. Any attempt to do so would amount to doing not only violence to provision specifically engrafted in s.16 (3) of Act but also would amount to Court relegislating on subject under guise of interpretation, against even will expressed in enactment itself. In view of said decision of this Court, impugned order is set aside, appeal is accepted and suit filed by respondent-plaintiffs is dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Illegitimate child cannot succeed/claim share in joint Hindu family property. Such illegitimate child would only be entitled to share in self-acquired property of parents.
