Gurgaon Gramin Bank v Smt. Khazani & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 269; (2012) 8 SCC 781; AIR 2012 SC 2881; 2013(2) ALL MR 448; 2013 (96) ALR 258; 2012 (5) ALT 53; 2012 (5) AWC 5314; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 481; 2012 (4) CPJ(Sc) 5; 2012 (5) CTC 345; 2012 (2) DRTC 761; JT 2012 (8) SC 201; 2012 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3183; 2013 (1) MahLJ 19; 2013 (1) MPLJ 9; 2013 (119) RD 23; 2012(8) SCALE 471; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Banking & Finance - Claim - Respondent No. 1 availed of a loan from the appellant-bank to purchase a buffalo and the same was insured for Rs.15000/ - Buffalo unfortunately died - Respondent No. 1 lodged a claim for insurance money through the appellant-bank - Since no steps had been taken either by the appellant-bank or by the insurance company, Respondent No. 1sent a notice to the bank as well as to the insurance company, which yielded no results - Respondent No. 1then filed a complaint before District Consumer Disputes Redressal Forum, which was allowed - Appeal filed thereagainst was rejected - Revision filed thereagainst before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission was also dismissed - Hence, instant petition - Whether claim to be allowed -

Held, no error in the decisions taken by all fact finding authorities including the National Disputes Redressal Commission - Further, for a paltry amount of Rs.15000/, appellant-bank had already spent a total amount of Rs.12,950/ - Also, District Forum had awarded Rs.3,000/ towards cost of litigation and compensation for the harassment caused to respondent No. 1 - Hence, these types of litigation should be discouraged - Appeal dismissed.
