Messrs Tribhovandas Bhimji Zaveri v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 528; (1993) Supp4 SCC 718; 1993 (115) CTR 411; [1993] 204 ITR 368; JT 1993 (5) SC 177; 1993(3) SCALE 712; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 207; [1993] 71 TAXMAN 309; 1993 (2) TLR 939

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 132 - Voluntary Disclosure Of Income And Wealth Act, 1976, ss. 3(1), 3(2)(ii), 4 and 14(1) - Whether because accounting year 1975-76 was still running during period for which Voluntary Disclosure Ordinance/Act was to operate, neither sub-clause (a) nor (b) nor (c) of sub-section (2) of s. 3 could apply to that accounting year and whether appellant's declaration was rightly held not to be in order u/s. 3(2) and ought to be filed u/s. 14? - In first place, words 'in respect of a previous year in which a search has been made' cannot be so read as to mean that, for provisions of sub-section (2) of s. 3 to apply, search and seizure should have taken place before Ordinance/Act came into force - Object of said Act is to motivate voluntary declaration of concealed income and with that object in mind schedule to Act prescribes concessional rates of tax, a declaration of concealed income made after books of account or other documents or valuable assets have been seized cannot be said to be a voluntary disclosure; provisions of sub-section (2) must be read as substantive provisions which debar an assessee who has declared concealed income for a previous year during which a seizure of his books, documents or valuable assets has been made or for any previous years prior thereto from getting advantage of concessional rates of tax set out in schedule; such assessee is, however, not entirely excluded from ambit of legislation for he may file a declaration u/s. 14 and obtain comparatively limited, but still substantial, benefits prescribed therein - Held, to those assessees who have declared concealed income, subsequent to a seizure during a previous year, for that previous year prior thereto, more beneficial provisions of s. 3 do not apply, for declaration is not really voluntary, and less beneficial provisions of s. 14 do; classification between those who make truly voluntary disclosures of concealed income and those who are impelled to make such disclosures because seizures of their books, documents and assets have already been effected is real and valid and bears a nexus to object to be won; appellant's declaration of concealed income was rightly rejected u/s. 3 - Appeal dismissed.
