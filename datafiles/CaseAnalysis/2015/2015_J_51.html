Jt. Collector Ranga Reddy District and another v D. Narsing Rao and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 18; (2015) 3 SCC 695; AIR 2015 SC 1021; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 3; 2015(2) ALL MR 962; 2015 (2) ALT(SC) 1; JT 2015 (1) SC 201; 2015 (127) RD 25; 2015(1) SCALE 274; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 437

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Assigned Lands (Prohibition of Transfers) Act, 1977, Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, Orissa Estates Abolition Act, 1951, Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1976, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Land Revenue Act, 1317F, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Record of Rights in Land Regulation, 1358F, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition of Jagirs Regulation, 1358F

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Land Revenue Act, 1317, s. 166B - Correction of Revenue entries - Exercise of Suo-motu power - Govt. allotted extent of Acres 477 of land in Survey Nos. 36 and 37 of Gopanpally village for grant of house sites to govt. employees vide Govt. order dt.10-7-1991 and 24-9-1991 - Hence, respondents, who were in possession of lands to extent of Acres 90 in said Survey Nos. 36 and 37 by virtue of purchase from pattadars under registered sale deeds, filed writ petition before HC - Later, impugned notice dt.31-12-2004 was issued to respondents fixing date of hearing for enquiry u/s. 166B of the Act, to show cause why Khasra Pahani entries in respect of land in Survey Nos. 36 and 37 should not be cancelled - Aggrieved, respondents filed another writ petition before HC - HC allowed both writ petitions, by common order, and set aside impugned Govt. order dt.24-9-1991 in so far as they are related to land of writ petitioners and also set aside impugned notice dt.31-12-2004 holding that exercise of revisional powers, even where not period of limitation is prescribed, should be within reasonable period - Appeals filed before DB being dismissed by common judgment, appellant-State filed instant two appeals and another independent appeal by respondent no. 13 - Whether govt. is justified in its exercise of suo motu power after period of 50 years.

Held, names of predecessors in title of the respondents are found mentioned in Khasra Pahani of the year 1954-55 pertaining to Survey Nos. 36 and 37 of Gopanpally village. The purchase of the said lands by the respondents from them under registered sale deeds is also not disputed and, they have been regularly paying land revenue continuously since 1954. Govt. passed order reserving 477 acres of land in Survey Nos. 36 and 37 of Gopanpally village for house-sites to govt. employees and govt. had every occasion to verify the revenue entries pertaining to the said lands while passing the said govt. order but no exception was taken to entries found. Further, even though respondents filed petition challenging said govt. order, yet, even at that point of time no action was initiated pertaining to entries in the said survey numbers. Furthermore, by impugned notice dt.31-12-2004, the suo motu revision power was sought to be exercised after five decades and if the same is allowed, it would lead to anomalous position leading to uncertainty and complications seriously affecting rights of the parties over immovable properties. Hence, DB was right in affirming view of Single Judge that the suo motu revision undertaken after long lapse of time, even in absence of any period of limitation was arbitrary and opposed to concept of rule of law. Hence, all appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
