Church of North India v Lavajibhai Ratanjibhai and others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 365; (2005) 10 SCC 760; AIR 2005 SC 2544; JT 2005 (5) SC 202; 2005(4) SCALE 633; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1037

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Madras Hindu Religious And Charitable Endowments Act, Madras Hindu Religious Endowments Act, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946, Industrial Disputes Act and Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946

Summary: [A.] Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, ss. 17, 2(13), 21(2), 22(3), 26, 31, 36, 41(2), 50, 51, 51(4), 79, 79(2), 80 (the Act) - Societies Registration Act, 1860, ss. 13, 17, 5 - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 254(2) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 7, r. 11, ss. 92, 93 - Extent of bar of jurisdiction of Civil Court u/s. 80 of the Act is the question herein - Whether, in relation to its affairs, a Society managing or governing the trust having a separate entity, the jurisdiction of the civil court is barred? - The Act is a complete code in itself - It provides for a complete machinery for a person interested in the trust to put forward his claim before the Charity Commissioner who is competent to go into the question and to prefer appeal if he feels aggrieved by any decision - Bar of jurisdiction created u/s. 80 clearly points out that a third party cannot maintain a suit so as to avoid the rigours of the provisions of the Act - Matter would be different if the property is not a trust property in the eyes of law - If a property did not validly vest in a trust or if a trust itself is not valid in law, the authorities under the Act will have no jurisdiction to determine the said question - To determine the question regarding exclusion of jurisdiction of civil court, the court has to consider what, in substance, and not merely in form, is the nature of the claim made in the suit and the underlying object in seeking the real relief - If for the purpose of grant of an appeal, the court concludes that the question is required to be dealt with by an authority under the Act, the jurisdiction of the civil court stands ousted - The questions which are required to be determined are within the sole and exclusive jurisdiction of the authorities whether simple or complicated - S. 26 specifically refers to those questions wherewith a court of competent jurisdiction can deal with and if the same is not expressly or impliedly barred - Once a decision is arrived at, having regard to the nature of the claim and reliefs sought for, that civil court has no jurisdiction, s. 26 to have no application - Held, having regard to nature of lis, jurisdiction of Civil Court was clearly barred - Suit has rightly been held to be not maintainable by HC - Appeal dismissed.
