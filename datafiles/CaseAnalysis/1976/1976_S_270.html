Hardayal v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 536; (1976) SCC (Cr) 317; (1976) 2 SCC 812; AIR 1976 SC 2055; 1976 CRLJ 1578; 1976 CRLR 185

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 363 and 364 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 342 and 354(3) - Whether the appellant wrongly convicted for deceased's murder in view of (i) absence of motive as he could has no conceivable grievance against the father of deceased, Lok chand; (ii) delay by Lok Chand in going to Konch in search of the boy; (iii) impossibility of identification of deadbody? - (i) The appellant, during his examination u/s. 342, himself, included Lok Chand in the category of those persons who wanted to force the appellant to repudiate his wife; in his eyes, Lok Chand was as much responsible for wrecking his matrimonial home as his parents; also the witnesses were unanimous in their evidence that they had seen the appellant taking away the boy with him; all these were independent witnesses and had no axe of their own to grind against the appellant; further the confession or admission made by the appellant before witnesses with regard to the act of kidnapping the boy is not of a type which is generally introduced as a padding to bolster up a weak prosecution case; even an extra-judicial confession, if cogently proved to have been made truly and voluntarily, is an efficacious proof of guilt; (ii) the delay was explained by Lok Chand in that when they learnt from the neighbours that the boy had been taken away by the appellant, they did not immediately suspect anything wrong in the matter and it was only from appellant's confession made at Konch and his conduct in slipping away that he was convinced of foul play on appellant's part; (iii) as to the identification, the very fact that the medical officer found marks of injuries from which he could definitely opine about the cause of death shows that the decomposition was not so advanced as to defy identification; the clothes and the shoes found on the deadbody were identified by Lok Chand as same which the deceased was wearing on the day of his disappearance - Held, the circumstances established by prosecution had made the chain so complete that there was no escape from the conclusion that the deceased was kidnapped, murdered and his deadbody thrown into the well by the appellant; under the new Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 life imprisonment for murder is the rule and capital sentence an exception, hence, appellant's death sentence is commuted to that of life imprisonment - Appeal dismissed.
