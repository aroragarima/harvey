Royal Western India Turf Club Limited v E. S. I. Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 175; (2016) 4 SCC 521; AIR 2016 SC 1143; 2016 (3) AWC 2448; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 289; 2016 (149) FLR 136; JT 2016 (2) SC 491; 2016 (2) LLJ 1; 2016(3) SCALE 57; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 1045; 2016 (3) SLR 216

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Rates, Employees' State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950, Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950, Sickness, Employees State Insurance Act, 1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(9), 2(22), 2(23) - Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950, r. 2A - Employees' State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950, regns. 29, 30, 31 - SC vide order held that appellant Club would fall within meaning of word 'shop' as mentioned in notification issued under Act, hence, provisions of Act would extend to appellant - Appellant club contended that that temporary staff engaged on race-days for issue of tickets, would not be covered by definition of 'employee' u/s. 2(9) of Act - Further, in view of consent terms, casual labour engaged on race track were not to be covered under - Further, in view of r. 2A of Rules, contribution is required to be made for period as may be prescribed in regns. and in view of regns. 29 and 31 of Regulations, it would be difficult to calculate contribution for employees who work casually on racing days - Whether casual workers are covered under definition of employee as defined in s. 2(9) of Act and pertaining to period for which appellant is liable to pay from 1978-1979 or from 1987.

Held, ss. 2(22) , 2(23) of Act makes it clear that it would cover 'casual employees' employed for few days on work of perennial nature and wages as defined in s. 2(22) of Act and wage period as defined in s. 2(23) of Act does not exclude wages payable to casual workers. Hence, they cannot be deprived of beneficial provisions of Act. Further, SC held that r. 2(2A) of Rules cannot be interpreted to mean that if employee has worked for lesser period he is not entitled for coverage under Act. SC held casual workers working with appellant Club are covered under Act. Further, after issuance of notification dt. 18-9-1978 by State Govt., remaining departments of appellant which were left out earlier were specifically covered under purview of Act. Hence, no benefit can be derived by consent terms which related to earlier period when notification dt. 18-9-1978 had not been issued. SC held that notification has statutory force and agreement cannot supersede it. SC held that HC on facts of case has erred in quashing demand for contribution with effect from 1978 till 1987. Hence, appellant Club is liable to make contribution as per notification dt. 18-9-1978 along with interest at such rate as provided in Act and Rules till date of actual payment. Let amount be contributed within period of three months from today. Appeal preferred by Corporation is allowed and appeal preferred by appellant Club is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Casual workers working under establishment are covered under definition of employee as defined in s. 2(9) of Act and are entitled to claim benefits under Act.
