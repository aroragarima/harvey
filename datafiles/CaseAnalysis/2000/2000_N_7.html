State Of Rajasthan v N. K. Accused

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 240; (2000) 5 SCC 30; (2000) SCC (Cr) 898; AIR 2000 SC 1812; AIR 2000 SCW 1407; 2000 (2) CCR 14; 2000 (2) Crimes 84; 2000 CRLJ 2205; 2000 (1) JCC 354; JT 2000 (3) SC 643; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 471; 2000(2) SCALE 652; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 818; 2000 (3) SLT 389; 2000 (3) Supreme 70

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 154 - IPC, 1860 ss. 375, 157 , 376 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872,s.8 -- Rape - Married woman and was virgin prior to commission of crime - (A) FIR - Delay in lodging - Communal tinge - (B) Consent, when can be inferred - Medical examination delayed, injuries abrasions, testimony supported by evidences, just crossing 16 years - (C) Incident in 1993, but no bail since then - Release after two years when acquitted by HC - Held, delay was satisfactorily explained - Consent on her part could not be inferred, absence of injuries on her person not necessarily evidence of consent - Sentence of imprisonment reduced to period already undergone - Appeal allowed. .
