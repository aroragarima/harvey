Transport Manager, Pune Municipal Corporation Transport Undertaking v Vasant Gopal Bhagwat (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1175; (1998) 7 SCC 574; AIR 1998 SC 2789; 1999 (1) Bom.C.R. 560; JT 1998 (6) SC 62; 1998 LabIC 3505; 1999 (3) LLJ 1078; 1998(4) SCALE 705; 1998 (7) SLT 154; 1998 (6) Supreme 566

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, Poona Municipal Transport Service Regulations

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Pension scheme - Differential application - Legality - Pension scheme introduced by Municipal Corporation for its employees - Employees of Transport Undertaking however excluded therefrom - Separate pension regulations framed subsequently for Transport Undertaking employees - Corporation later on resolving to give retrospective effect to pension scheme for its employees - Employees of Transport Undertaking cannot claim similar benefit - (A)Whether pension scheme applied to transport undertaking employees was entirely different from that of Municipal employees? - Pension regulations fixing date of agreement as cut off date - (B)Whether fixation is arbitrary? - Held, since in view of difference in nature of duties and service rules and of availability of provident fund benefit, (A)pension scheme applied to transport undertaking employees was entirely different from that of Municipal employees - (B)Not arbitrary - Petition dismissed.
