State Of Punjab And Another v Shamlal Murari & Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 189; (1976) SCC (L&S) 118; (1976) 1 SCC 719; AIR 1976 SC 1177; 1976 LabIC 777; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: High Court Rules

Summary: Constitution, arts. 136 and 311 - Punjab and Haryana High Court Rules and Orders, Vol.5, chap. 2C, r. 3 - (A) Service Matter - (1) Increment - Withholding for failure to pass departmental examination after long service - Legality - (2) Discretionary exercise of power - Interference by Court, if - (B) Whether Vol.5, chap. 2C, r. 3 of the Order is mandatory - Effect of minor deviation in fulfilling the requirements - Held, failure cannot stand in the way of his enjoying the benefits of increment, etc., particularly when he had been accorded exemption - Discretionary exercise of power by a Court cannot be lightly interfered with by a Court of appeal, therefore, the SC refused to upset the order of the HC declining to condone the delay, there being nothing perverse or irrational in the exercise - Even taking a stern view, every minor detail in r. 3 cannot carry a compulsory or imperative import - Appeal Dismissed.
