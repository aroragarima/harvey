Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh v Jaswant Singh Charan Singh

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 464; AIR 1967 SC 1454; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 720; 1967 (19) STC 469

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958, s. 18(6) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 14 - Rate of taxation on coal - Appellant carrying on business and a dealer in firewood and charcoal - For the period from 29.03.1962 to 29.04.1962, appellant was assessed to sales tax u/s. 18(6) of 1958 Act as he did not have any registration certificate in respect of this period - Subsequently, Addl. Sales Tax Officer and Addl. Appellate Asst. Commissioner held that charcoal in which the appellant was dealing was not covered by Entry I of Part III of Sch. II to the Act, but that it fell under the residuary Entry I of part VI of that Schedule; and consequently was liable to be assessed at the rate of 4 % of the price of charcoal - On appeal, Board of Revenue held that the charcoal would be included in the term 'coal', and, therefore, Entry I in Part III of Schedule II would Apply and the tax chargeable would be at 2 % only - HC also held that the charcoal would be covered by Entry I of Part III of Sch. 11 and was taxable at 2 % - Hence, the present appeal - Whether charcoal is taxable at rate of 2% or will be taxable at rate of 4% under Entry I of Part VI of sch. II to 1958 Act? - Held, 'Charcoal' is covered by Entry 1, Part III of sch. II to Act and is liable to be taxed at 2% and not under Entry 1 of Part VI as charcoal is one of the species of coal - It would not be possible to discard meaning of word 'coal' in this statute as understood in its commercial or popular sense and to adopt its connotation from other statutes passed for different purposes or in context of different objects - Charcoal would be taxable at rate of 2% only - Appeal dismissed.
