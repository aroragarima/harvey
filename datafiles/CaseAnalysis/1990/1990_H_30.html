Manibhai v Hemraj

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 761; (1990) 3 SCC 68; 1990 (3) CCC 594; 1990(1) SCALE 574; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O 20 r. 12 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Father/husband of plaintiffs aliennated his ancestral property in favour of respondents and it was challenged by plaintiffs on ground that father was a drunkard, gambler and satoria and alienations made by him were not invalid - Whether, when sons join with their father and debts have been contracted by father provided for his personal benefit, sons are liable to pay debts? - Held, liability to pay debt has been described as pious obligation of son to pay father's debt not tainted with illegality or immorality, and even if any loan is taken by father for his personal benefit which is found as vyavaharik debt and not vyavaharik, sons are liable to discharge their father's debt under doctrine of pious obligation and in this view of matter if any alienation of joint family property is subsequently made to discharge such antecedent debt or loan of father, such alienation would be binding on sons - Appeal partly allowed.
