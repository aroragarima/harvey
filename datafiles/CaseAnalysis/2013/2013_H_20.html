Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation Limited v Udal and others Etc.

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 396; (2013) 14 SCC 506; AIR 2013 SC 3111; 2013 (5) AWC 4790; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 186; JT 2013 (9) SC 451; 2013 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2441; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 896; 2013(8) SCALE 601

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Frontage

Summary: Land &Property - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - Sustainability - State Govt. acquired huge land to establish Industrial Model Township (IMT) under various phases - Instant appeals were filed by State Govt. challenging impugned order of HC, where in HC enhanced compensation in lieu of acquisitions made for Phases-II and III of IMT payable to landowners from Rs.28,15,356 per acre to Rs.37,40,000 per acre - Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation v. Pran Sukh and others, 2010 Indlaw SCO 1 relied on - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, on careful scrutiny of impugned judgment shown that while determining amount of compensation payable to landowners other than 'K'/company HC did make a reference to Ext. P38 (paragraph 30) but did not rely upon the same for the purpose of determination of amount of compensation - Instead of adopting a holistic approach and examining documents produced before Reference Court, HC simply referred to judgment of Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation v. Pran Sukh and others, 2010 Indlaw SCO 1 and granted a flat increase of 12% for the time gap of about 7 years and 3 months between 2 acquisitions, i.e., 1994 and 2002 and determined market value at the rate of Rs.37,40,000 per acre - In case of 'K', HC squarely relied upon Ext. P38 for the purpose of fixing market value of acquired land, granted an increase at a flat rate of 15% p.a. on price of land specified in Ext. P38 with an addition of 30% on account of special locational advantage and held that particular landowner was entitled to compensation at the rate of Rs.2119 p.sq.yads. (Rs.1,02,55,960 per acre) - However, no discernible reason was given for granting benefit of annual increase at different rates to 'K' on one hand and remaining landowners on other - Therefore, SC found merit in argument of remaining landowners that their clients were subjected to discrimination in the matter of grant of annual increase - Other error committed by HC was that he granted annual increase at a flat rate of 12/15% - Further there was merit in contention of landowners that while fixing market value of acquired land HC committed serious error by not considering an important piece of evidence, i.e., Ext. PW9/A vide which HSIIDC had allotted land to 'H' at the rate of Rs.1254.18 per square yard - Although, this document was produced before Reference Court but the same was not taken into consideration while determining amount of compensation - The same error was repeated in impugned judgment - If this document was taken into consideration, then market value of acquired land would come to Rs.60,69,360 per acre - By making deduction of 50% towards development cost and granting annual increase of 12/15% (cumulative), market value of land would be much higher than Rs.37,40,000 per acre - In view of the same, impugned judgment was set aside and matter was remitted HC for fresh disposal of appeals - Appeals allowed.
