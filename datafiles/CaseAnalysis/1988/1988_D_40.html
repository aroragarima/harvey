Fasih Chaudhary v Director General, Doordarshan and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.H. Kania, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 241; (1989) 1 SCC 89; AIR 1989 SC 157; 1988 (36) DLT 375; JT 1988 (4) SC 50; 1988(2) SCALE 1079; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 282; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Denied, Serialising the script

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Media & Entertainment - Serialising the script - Contract - Denied - Justifiability - Petitioner filed a petition before HC seeking direction to UOI to accept the script of petitioner and eventually to give him the contract - HC by impugned judgment dismissed petition holding that there was no substance in the allegation that respondent no. 2, was preferred over petitioner by practicing discrimination - Aggrieved petitioner filed instant SLP to appeal from decision of HC - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, it appeared from records that respondent no. 1 (Doordarshan Authorities) found that project submitted by petitioner was not 'attractive or interesting' and from the letter that Doordarshan Authorities did not find the proposal of petitioner to be 'attractive or interesting' enough - There was one more proposal given by another person apart from petitioner and respondent no. 2 - Respondent no. 2 submitted his proposal and Doordarshan, though the proposal was not complete, asked respondent no. 2 to give further details and after they were submitted by respondent no. 2, it was examined by the Committee - It appeared to be corroborated by records that before the proposal of respondent no. 2 was considered, script and proposal of the petitioner and that of other person were considered and not found to be 'attractive or interesting' enough - It appeared that though the idea was presented by respondent no. 2 by a letter but the matter was decided only after the entire proposal was submitted by respondent no. 2 - Thus, all the proposals were duly considered by Committee - Respondent no. 1 did not transgress the limits of fair-play in action - All proposals were duly considered - Those were considered by a Committee of eminent persons - Committee did not find proposal of petitioner and the other person either attractive or interesting enough in awarding the TV serial on the aspect of national integration of lifetime of 'Mirza Ghalib' - There was objectivity in actual consideration of different proposals and that there was fairness in decision and that no malice or ill-will coloured the decision-making process in instant case - Impugned order of HC was justified - Petition dismissed.
