Ramesh B. Desai and others v Bipin Vadilal Mehta and others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1440; (2006) 5 SCC 638; AIR 2006 SC 3672; 2006 (5) ALD(SC) 18; 2007 (1) AWC 299; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 574; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 2495; JT 2006 (6) SC 253; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 174; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 921; 2006(7) SCALE 62; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 414

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Companies (Amendment) Act, 1988, Reduction Of Capital, Limitation Act, 1939

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Practice - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 77 and 155 - Companies (Amendment) Act, 1988 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11(d) and O. 6 r. 4 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 17 - Rectification of register of members - Limitation period - Company Petition was filed by shareholders of the Company seeking rectification of the register and for deletion of names of respondents nos. 2 to 11 in accordance with s. 155 of the Companies Act - Petitioner's case was that in view of the fact that the funds of the Company were utilized for purchase of shares by respondent no. 2, the whole transaction was in violation of s. 77 of the Companies Act, and consequently the register of the Company required to be rectified in accordance with s. 155 of the Companies Act - It was also pleaded that the petitioners had got no knowledge of the fraud played by the respondents in May, 1987 - Respondent nos. 1 and 2 filed application before the Company Judge to dismiss the Company on the ground that the same was barred by limitation - Application was allowed - On appeal, said order was affirmed by a DB of the HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Plea of limitation - Held, a plea of limitation is a mixed question of law and fact - Therefore, unless it becomes apparent from the reading of the Company Petition that the same was barred by limitation, the petition cannot be rejected under O. 7 r. 11(d) of CPC - In the present case, having regard to the provisions of s. 17(1) of the Limitation Act, the limitation for filing the Company Petition had not begun to run until May, 1987 when the petitioners claim to have got knowledge of the alleged fraud committed by the respondents in utilizing the funds of the Company for purchase of its shares, which is a clear violation of s. 77 of the Companies Act - Thus the Company Petition cannot be rejected at the preliminary stage as being barred by limitation and the view to the contrary taken by the Company Judge and also by the DB was clearly erroneous in law - (B) Respondents submitted there was no compliance of O. 6 r. 4 of CPC in the Company Petition - Held, O. 6 r. 4 of CPC requires that complete particulars of fraud shall be stated in the pleadings - Particulars of alleged fraud, which are required to be stated in the plaint, will depend upon the facts of each particular case and no abstract principle can be laid down in this regard - Where some transaction of money takes place to which 'A', 'B' and 'C' are parties and payment is made by cheques, in normal circumstances a third party 'X' may not get knowledge of the said transaction unless he is informed about it by someone who has knowledge of the transaction or he gets an opportunity to see the accounts of the concerned parties in the Bank - In such a case an assertion by 'X' that he got no knowledge of the transaction when it took place and that he came to know about it subsequently through some proceedings in court cannot be said to be insufficient pleading for the purpose of O. 6 r. 4 of CPC - In such a case 'X' can only plead that he got no knowledge of the transaction and nothing more - In the present case, approach adopted by HC was clearly illegal as no finding on the point of knowledge could have been recorded until the parties had been given opportunity to lead evidence and in such circumstances dismissal of the Company Petition at a preliminary stage on the finding that it was barred by limitation was clearly erroneous in law - (C) Benefit of s. 17 of the Limitation Act - Effect of fraud or mistake - Petitioners relied upon s. 17 of the Limitation Act in support of their claim that the limitation will start running only when they got knowledge of the fraud committed by the contesting respondents, i.e., in May or June, 1987 - Held, s. 17(1)(b) will apply when the plaintiff or applicant is claiming any kind of right or title to any moveable or immoveable property etc - In view of the facts pleaded in the Company Petition, the case is covered by s. 17(1)(a) of the Limitation Act and not by s. 17(1)(b) as the petitioners are not claiming any right or title over the shares of the Company, which according to them were purchased out of the funds of the Company - In view of the pleadings, it is s. 17(1)(a) of the Limitation Act which would govern the situation and not s. 17(1)(b) of the Limitation Act - Appeal allowed.
