Indian National Congress (I) v Institute of Social Welfare and others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 341; (2002) 5 SCC 685; AIR 2002 SC 2158; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 398; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 164; 2002(4) SCALE 627; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1040; 2002 (3) SLT 693; 2002 (4) Supreme 181

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968, Representation of the People (Second Amendment) Bill, 1994

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Section 29A - whether the Election Commission of India has power to de-register or cancel the registration of a political party? - Election Commission is a quasi-judicial authority - Neither under the Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968 nor under Section 29A of the Act, the Election Commission has been conferred with any express power to de-register a political party registered under Section 29A of the Act on the ground that it has either violated the provisions of the Constitution or any provision of undertaking given before the Election Commission at the time of its registration. - However, there are three exceptions where the Commission can review its order registering a political party.
