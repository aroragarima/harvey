Â M. K. Gopalan and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 38; AIR 1954 SC 362; 1954 CRLJ 1012; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 76; 1954 SCJ 534; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 168

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 14, 197(1) and 197(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120-B, 409 and 420 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - (A) Whether s. 14 of Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, is violative of art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, no discrimination arises when Special Magistrate under s. 14 of CrPC, 1898 has to try case entirely under normal procedure - Not hit by art. 14 - (B) Whether a lacuna in sanction can be made good? - Further held, lacuna, if any, in sanction can be remedied in course of trial by specific evidence in that behalf - (C) Whether there is any distinction between s. 14(1) and s. 197(2) of CrPC, 1898? - Further held, powers under s. 197(2) relates to court and those under s. 14(1) relate to person - Under s. 197(2) sanctioning Govt. may specify a court for trial of case but is not bound to do so - If it does specify a court, such specification does not mention who person to function in such court is - That is to be decided by Provincial Govt. of area where trial is to take place - Word 'Court' in s. 197(2), cannot be treated as being same as a 'person' in s. 14(1) - (D) What is implication of non-exercise of power under s. 197 (2) - Further held, s. 197(2) is by any policy of protection of concerned public servant - Power under s. 197(2) appears to be vested in appropriate Govt. - It cannot be held that it is for very Govt. which sanctioned prosecution under s. 197(1) to specify court before which trial is to be held - If in an individual case, power under s. 197(2) is not exercised, it cannot under s. 197(2) be taken that appropriate Govt. did not feel called upon to allot case to any special court, and it cannot also be taken that such allotment by another Govt. u/s. 14 would affect or nullify power of appropriate Govt. under s. 197(2) - Power to specify a court for trial in such cases is a permissive power - No implication arises from non-exercise of power - (E) What is effect of making a direct petition to Supreme Court without appeal from decision under art. 226 - Further held, nothing that Supreme Court said was intended to be a pronouncement as to correctness or otherwise of orders, nor to encourage practice of direct approach to Supreme Court in matters which had been taken to High Court and found against - Without obtaining leave to appeal there from - Petition dismissed.
