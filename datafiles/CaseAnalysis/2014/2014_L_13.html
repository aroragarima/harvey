Nandlal Wasudeo Badwaik v Lata Nandlal Badwaik and another

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 5; (2014) 2 SCC 576; AIR 2014 SC 932; 2014 (102) ALR 666; 2014 CRLJ 1098; 2014 (5) CTC 680; 2014 (1) JCC 392; JT 2014 (1) SC 266; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 255; 2014 (1) MLJ(Crl) 246; 2014(3) M.P.H.T. 326; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 761; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 655; 2014 (2) RLW 1662; 2014(1) SCALE 99; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 120

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 482 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 112 - Maintenance - Legality - Appellant was husband of respondent no. 1 and father of respondent no. 2 - Respondent no. 1 filed an application for maintenance before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - Respondent no. 1 resorted to fresh proceeding before Trial Court u/s. 125 of CrPC claiming maintenance for herself and respondent no. 2 alleging that she started living with appellant - Trial Court allowed said petition - Appellant filed revision before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that after respondent no. 1 left matrimonial home, she did not return and thereafter, he had no access to her - Whether DNA test would be sufficient to hold that appellant was not biological father of respondent no. 2 as provided u/s. 112 of the Act - Held, result of DNA test was scientifically accurate - Although s. 112 of the Act raised presumption of conclusive proof on satisfaction of conditions enumerated therein but same was rebuttable - Interest of justice was best served by ascertaining truth and Court should be furnished with best available science and should not be left to bank upon presumptions, unless science had no answer to facts in issue - When there was a conflict between conclusive proof envisaged under law and proof based on scientific advancement accepted by world community to be correct, latter should prevail over former - Appellants plea that he had no access to respondent no. 1 when respondent no. 2 was begotten stands proved by DNA test report and in face of it, it could not compel appellant to bear fatherhood of respondent no. 2, when scientific reports proved to contrary - Court was conscious that respondent no. 2 should not be bastardized as marriage between respondent no.1 and appellant was subsisting at time of her birth, but in view of DNA test reports and Court could not forestall consequence - Impugned judgment so far as it directed payment of maintenance to respondent no. 2 was set aside, however, payments already made should not be recovered from respondents - Appeal allowed.
