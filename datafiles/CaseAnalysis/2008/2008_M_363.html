(1) V.S. Krishnan and Others; (2) Dr. P.P v Messrs Westfort Hi-Tech Hospital Limited and

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 277; (2008) 3 SCC 363; 2008 (2) BC 433; [2008] 142 Comp Cas 235; 2008 (2) CompLJ 1; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1192; 2008(3) SCALE 184; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 184

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indemnity Bond, Re-Appointment, Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 - Whether petitioners were successful in making out a case for interference by the CLB by invoking ss. 397 and 398? - Held, claim 'legitimate expectation' cannot be extended to and there is no specific promise that the petitioners would be given directorship permanently - Even otherwise, the same cannot be accepted in view of the mandate of the statute that 1/3rd of the directors have to retire in a year by rotation - High Court verified the notice dated 24.08.2005 sent for AGM dated 29.09.2005 wherein the names of the retiring directors were subsequently mentioned - Proper advertisement in the Indian Express and Deepika were given in terms of s. 157(1)A - Judgment of the High Court is fair to both sides and safeguards the interest of the directors and shareholders; hence there is no valid ground to interfere under art. 136 - Appeals dismissed.
