D. Macropollo and Company Private, Limited v Their Employees' Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 11; AIR 1958 SC 1012; 1958 (2) LLJ 492

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act (14 of 1947) ss. 2(oo) and 25 F - Constitution Of India, art. 136 - Appellant selling agency of various cigarette manufacturing concerns - Appellant closed down said outdoor salesman department and to retrench outdoor salesmen, to operate through distributors - On realisation of drop in sales offered reinstaement to salesman - Respondent-union raised an industrial dispute with appellant in respect of termination of services of its fourteen outdoor salesmen - (A)Whether discharge of workers due to country wide re-organisation was actuated by malice? - Held, it is fully proved by evidence on record that employer has adopted re-organisation scheme and same has been implemented in all areas of country where his business is conducted - Though re-organisation scheme had a very unfortunate consequence - Employer's act was bona-fide which he was entitled to adopt - (A1)Whether by an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution of India it is open to appellant to challenge facts recorded by a labour court? - Held, an appeal u/art. 136 would not be open to appellant to challenge correctness or finding of fact recorded by a labour court - But findings not supported by any legal evidence may be set aside by Supreme Court - Appeal allowed.
