Babu Ram and Another v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1192; (2009) 10 SCC 115; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 285; 2010 (1) ALD(SC) 59; 2010 (1) AWC 68; JT 2009 (13) SC 99; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2759; 2010 (157) PLR 232; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 908; 2010 (109) RD 684; 2009(13) SCALE 162; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5-A, 6, 17(2)(c), 17(4) - Land Acquisition Proceedings - Right of citizen to file objections u/s. 5-A - Construction of Sewage Treatment Plant (STP) - Notification issued for acquisition of certain lands u/s. 4 r/w s. 17(2)(c) of Act for construction of STP - Provisions of s. 5-A were dispensed with - Thereafter, notifications u/s. 6 and 9 of Act issued - Whether provisions of s. 17(4) of Act had been validly invoked by respondents for purpose of acquiring lands in question for Sewage Treatment Plant? - Great care to be taken by authorities before resorting to s. 17(4) of Act and had to satisfy themselves that there was an urgency of such nature as indicated in s. 17(2) of the Act - Proposed STP is to be situated in vicinity of a school and grain market which are both hubs of community activities - Provisions of s. 17(4) were invoked in relation to a project which could have serious consequences to health of general public of said area - Held, opportunity should have been given to appellants to file objections u/s. 5-A of Act to proposed acquisition - Site is still lying unutilized - Appellants can file objections u/s. 5-A of Act and respondents would be at liberty to take consequential steps after disposal of same - Appeal disposed of.
