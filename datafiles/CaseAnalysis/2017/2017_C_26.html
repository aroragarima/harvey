Central Bank of India v M. Sethumadhavan and others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 456; 2017 (153) FLR 999; 2017 (3) LLJ 178; 2017(6) SCALE 142

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Gratuity, Pay, Date, Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, Cut-Off Date, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1947, art. 142 - Payment of Gratuity - Order Challenged - Appellants/banks filed instant appeals against impugned order of HC whereby HC declined to upheld regulation passed in respect of payment of gratuity - Whether appellants were justified in fixation of cut-off date for payment of gratuity.

Held, fixing of cut-off date has been a well accepted principle and do not find that the same needs to be supported by any judgment since it has been the consistent view taken by Apex Court. In the peculiar facts of the case, having regard to the background of the regularisation making process, the cut-off date fixed by the appellants in the regularisation was not arbitrary, unjust or unfair. However, in view of retired employees been fighting for around a quarter of a century and taking note of the fact that they are only a few in number it is a fit case to invoke our jurisdiction u/art. 142 of Constitution. Hence, direct the banks to pay an amount of Rs. 2,00,000 in full and final settlement of all their claims including the expenses which they have incurred for litigation. Appeals disposed of.
