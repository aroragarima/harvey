Motor Industries Company Limited v Commissioner Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2184; 2009 (244) E.L.T. 4

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, s. 14(1) - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr 5 and 8 - Assessable value - Transaction value - Original Authority rejected declared value/transaction value and determined assessable value in Free on Board (FOB) terms in terms of r. 8 of Rules - On appeal, Commissioner relied on SC judgement in Eicher Tractors Ltd., Haryana vs. Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai 2000 INDLAW SC 2778 and held that transaction value in r. 4 of Rules is limited to transaction in question and that this mandate is only subject to certain exceptions specified in r. 4(2) of Rules - Tribunal allowed appeal of Revenue and reject transaction value as determined by first appellate authority by using depreciation method of calculating transaction value of goods - Hence, present appeal - Whether Tribunal's order could be upheld? - Held, as per above mentioned judgement transaction value has to be accepted subject to exceptions contained in r. 4(2) of Rules by recording special or extraordinary reasons - No special or extraordinary reasons have been recorded for rejecting transaction value - It is settled law that unless transaction value is rejected for extraordinary or special reasons, same has to be accepted - Tribunal has fallen in error in taking a decision contrary to principle laid down by this court in case of Eicher Tractors - Impugned order of Tribunal and adjudicating authority set aside and order of first appellate authority restore - Appeal allowed.
