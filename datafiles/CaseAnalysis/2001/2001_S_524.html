Ram Deo Chauhan Alias Raj Nath Chauhan v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20670; (2001) 5 SCC 714; (2001) SCC (Cr) 915; AIR 2001 SC 2231; AIR 2001 SCW 2159; 2002 (1) Bom.C.R. 665; 2001 CRLJ 2902; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 300; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 702; 2001(4) SCALE 116; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 669; 2001 (4) SLT 130; 2001 (4) Supreme 363

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1978, Adjourn, Haryana Children Act, 1974

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Death Sentence - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 432, 433 and 433A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 149 and 302 - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986, s. 22(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 137 and 145 - (A) Death sentence - Age - Held, awarding of lesser sentence only on ground of petitioner being a youth at time of occurrence cannot be considered as a mitigating circumstance when case is rarest of rare requiring maximum penalty, imposable under law - Power to commute a sentence of death is independent of s. 433 - Restriction u/s. 433-A comes into operation only after power u/s. 433 is exercised; s. 433-A is applicable to two categories of convicts: (a) those who could have been punished with sentence of death and (b) those whose sentences have been converted into imprisonment for life u/s. 433 - Despite bar of third proviso to sub-s. (2) of s. 309, court in appropriate case, can grant adjournment for enabling accused persons to show cause against sentence proposed on him particularly if such proposed sentence is sentence of death - (B) Review - Further held, power of review is not inherent in court as it must be specifically conferred by law - No application for review can be entertained except on ground of an error apparent on face of record which limits grounds for review - A Court is not precluded from recalling of reviewing its own order if it is satisfied that it is necessary to do so for sake of justice - Mere fact that two views on same subject are possible is no ground to review earlier judgment passed by a Bench of same strength - Review cannot be treated an appeal in disguise - (C) Plea of petitioner being juvenile - Further held, it is not only an after-thought but a concoction of his imagination at a belated stage to thwart course of justice by having resort to wrangles of procedures and technicalities of law - Petitioner was not a juvenile within meaning of Act nor did he seriously claim to be a juvenile for purposes of getting benefit of s. 22 of Act - Petition dismissed.
