International Contractors Limited v Prasanta Kumar Sur

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 506; AIR 1962 SC 77; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Specific performance - On 04-02-1941, respondent-plaintiff sold property in dispute to appellant-defendant for a sum of Rs. 10, 000 On 10-02-1941, there was an agreement for reconveyance within a period up to 10-02-1943, for a sum of Rs. 10, 001 - When respondent approached appellant for reconveyance with required money, appellant denied that he had entered into any such agreement, respondent filed a suit for specific performance and in alternative for redemption on footing that transaction was in reality a mortgage - Whether answer sent on behalf of appellant amounted to an enequivocal refusal to carry out its part of contract? - Held, principle laid down in Hunter v Daniel stated position - 'The practice of Courts is not to require a party to make a formal tender where from facts stated in Bill or from evidence it appears tender would have been a mere form and that party to whom it was made would have refused to accept money' - Hence, answer on behalf of appellant did amount to a refusal - Appeal dismissed.
