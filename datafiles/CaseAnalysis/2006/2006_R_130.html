Regional Manager, S.B.I v Rakesh Kumar Tewari

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 570; (2006) SCC (L&S) 143; (2006) 1 SCC 530; AIR 2006 SC 839; 2006 (5) AWC 4473; JT 2006 (1) SC 252; 2006(1) SCALE 59; 2006 (1) SCJ 747; 2006 (2) SLR 265; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 2(ra), 33-C(2), 78, 25-G, 25-H and 17-B - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether the tribunal had correctly found that the termination of the respondent's service in 1982 was violative of s. 25-G of the Industrial Disputes Act? - Tribunal ignored the fact that there was no pleading by the respondent in support of an alleged violation of s. 25-G - Order of reference by the Central Govt. did not also refer to s. 25-G but only to s. 25-H - It was not open to the tribunal to conclude that the termination of service of the respondent was invalid - Tribunal did not consider the plea that there was no vacancy against which the respondent had been appointed and that it was an ad hoc arrangement - No procedure whether in law or under any award or settlement was followed in appointing either of the respondents - No condition of services were agreed to and no letter of appointment was given, nature of the respondents' employment was entirely ad hoc - They had been appointed without considering any rule - It would be ironical if the person who have benefited by the flouting of the rules of appointment can rely upon those rules when their services are dispensed with - Held, High Court's view that the termination was invalid cannot in any event be sustained - HC holding is set aside - Held, The factual basis for this conclusion was erroneous - Both appeals are allowed.
