Rakesh Kapoor v State Of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 482; (2012) 13 SCC 552; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 324; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 211; 2012(11) SCALE 255; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(2) - Bribery - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused working as Divisional Tourism Development Officer, Dharamshala, allegedly fixed tariff of Rs. 10,000 for providing registration certificate to complainant - Charge sheet was filed against accused u/ss. 7, 13(2) of the Act - Trial Court convicted accused guilty and convicted him for offences and sentenced him to undergo 2 years RI and to pay a fine of Rs.10,000 - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal and set aside conviction u/s. 7 of the Act and confirmed the same u/s. 13(2) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in convicting accused - Held, in instant case though prosecution heavily relied on evidence of PW1/complainant that demand was made to him over mobile phone, admittedly call details had not been summoned - No doubt, statement of PW1, was corroborated by PW3 who stated that he overheard PW1 saying that he had brought money, when the latter went to the office of appellant in evening -However, except oral testimony of PWs 1 and 3, there was no other proof in respect of demand of bribe money and I.O. could not collect call details as stated by PW1 from department concerned - In Banarsi Dass vs. State of Haryana, 2010 Indlaw SC 24 SC held that in absence of demand and acceptance, accused was entitled to benefit of doubt - Therefore, prosecution had miserably failed to prove accused guilty of offence u/s. 7, 13(2) of the Act - Hence, impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
