Tata Motors Ltd. v Talathi Of Village Chikhali & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 425; (2011) 7 SCC 594; AIR 2011 SC 2884; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 820; JT 2011 (7) SC 407; 2011(7) SCALE 93; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 863

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, Pimpri-Chinchwad New Town Development Authority (Disposal of Land) Regulations, 1973

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, ss. 39, 115, 168, 168(1), 168(1)(a), 168(1)(c), 168(2), 174 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 113, 113(2), 113(3A), 114, 118, 159 - - Lease - Demand notice - Validity -Development Authority granted lease on land to appellant - Appellant utilized plot and adjoining plot obtained on lease from MIDC for construction of factory - Demand notices were served against appellant for nonpayment of non-agricultural cess and additional non agricultural cess on land leased - Appellant filed writ petition before HC for quashing of demand notices which was allowed in part - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was Govt. lessee and not liable to pay non agricultural assessment or Whether appellant being tenant of Development Authority non agricultural assessment could be made only on Development Authority and not against tenant - Held, lands for which non-agricultural assessment had been demanded, were not leased by Collector to appellant besides lease deed states that lands leased were held by Development Authority and it was lessor - Further, leased lands were not Govt. lands and lessor was not government and there was nothing to show that lands belonged to government and that Development Authority granted lease in favour of appellant, acting as an agent of State Govt. - Further Court, said that lessee from Development Authority was not a Govt. lessee as Development Authority was not Govt. and lease lands were not Govt. lands - Further, primary liability to pay land revenue which includes non-agricultural assessment is on occupant, u/s. 39 of the Code and definition of 'occupant' excludes not only 'Govt. lessee' but also every tenant - Whenever person in actual possession of land is tenant, land holder or superior landlord who granted lease to such tenant was deemed to be an occupant - Whereas, in instant case appellant had taken lease from Development Authority and therefore Development Authority as landlord and occupant would be primarily liable to pay land revenue - Regn. 16 provides that in event of conflict between Regulations and provisions of a lease deed entered into by Development Authority, provisions of Regulations would prevail - There was a statutory liability upon appellant as lessee to pay non- agricultural assessment to State Govt. - Thus, s. 168(2) would operate where tenant was not primarily liable u/s. 168(1) of the Code - Further, where liability of lessee was statutory liability, there was no reason why that recovery should be delayed and protracted by requiring a demand by State Govt. on lessor and a consequential demand by lessor on lessee - Further it was noted that if lessee commits default in paying land revenue, it might amount to breach leading to re-entry under cl. (4) of lease deed - However, having regard to pendency of these proceedings, if the payment of land revenue dues was made within four months from today it should not be treated as a default or breach of the terms of the lease deed for the purpose of re-entry - Appeal dismissed.
