State of Madhya Pradesh and Another v Anshuman Shukla

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 819; (2008) 7 SCC 487; AIR 2008 SC 2454; 2008 (72) ALR 498; 2008(8) SCALE 425; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record, Arbitration and Conciliation Act, Revisional Order, Administrators, Rent Act, Bihar Administrative Tribunal Act, Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1944

Summary: Madhya Pradesh Madhyastham Adhikaran Adhiniyam, 1983 - Applicability of s. 5 of the Limitation Act, 1963 in the matter of entertaining a revision application before the High Court in terms of s. 19 of 1983 Act - Held, a Court for the purpose of application of the Limitation Act should ordinarily be subordinate to the High Court - High Court exercises its jurisdiction over the subordinate courts inter alia in terms of s. 115 of the Code of Civil Procedure - While the High Court exercises its revisional jurisdiction, it for all intent and purport exercises an appellate jurisdiction - Matter requires consideration by a Larger Bench.
