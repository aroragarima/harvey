Murarilal Mahabir Prasad and Others v B. R. Vad and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 79; (1975) SCC (Tax) 432; (1975) 2 SCC 736; AIR 1976 SC 313; 1975 (4) CTR 34; 1976 CTR 34; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 689; 1976 (37) STC 77

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Dissolution Of Firm

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, ss. 2(6), 5, 14, 15 and 26 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Whether under Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, and Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, a dissolved firm can be assessed or reassessed to sales tax in respect of its pre-dissolution turnover? - Held, it is indisputable that first appellant-firm was liable to be charged to sales tax on its business turnover and charging provisions are contained in Chap. III of Act of 1953 and Chap. II of Act of 1959 - Construction which has been placed on machinery provisions of 1953 Act will give meaning and content to charging sections, in sense that construction will effectuate provision contained in charging sections - Resourcefulness and ingenuity which go into well-timed dissolution of firms ought not to be allowed to be used as convenient instruments of tax evasion - View taken by a full bench of Madras High Court in Sales Tax Officer (XIX), Enforcement Branch, Bombay v K M S Mari Chettiar 1974 Indlaw MAD 79 that a dissolved firm can be assessed under Act of 1959 is correct - Appeal dismissed.
