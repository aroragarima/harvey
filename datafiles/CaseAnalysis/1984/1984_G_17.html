State of Uttar Pradesh v Gokaran and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, M.P. Thakkar, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 27; (1984) Supp SCC 482; (1985) SCC (Cr) 41; AIR 1985 SC 131; 1984 (2) Crimes 761; 1985 CRLJ 511; 1984 CRLR 428; 1984(2) SCALE 602

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 307 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, 157 and 159 - Appeal again acquittal to Supreme Court - Circumstances relied by High Court for acquittal inferential and immaterial - Not detracting from ocular evidence by eye-witness - Interference by Supreme Court warranted - Held, HC had clearly erred in relying upon circumstances for purpose of rejecting prosecution story - Acquittal of accused was not proper and he was liable to be convicted.
