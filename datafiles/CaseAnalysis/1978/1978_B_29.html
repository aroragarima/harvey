Shambu Nath Goyal v Bank of Baroda, Jullundur

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 130; (1978) 2 SCC 353; (1978) SCC (L&S) 357; AIR 1978 SC 1088; 1978 (36) FLR 195; 1978 LabIC 961; 1978 (1) LLJ 484; 1978 (1) LLN 340; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 793; 1978 UJ 168

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k) and 10(1) - (A) What is the scope of industrial dispute u/s. 2(k) of the Act? - Held, where there is a dispute or difference between the parties contemplated by the definition and the disputes or difference is connected with the employment or non-employment or the terms of employment or, with the conditions of labour of any person there comes into existence an industrial dispute - The Act nowhere contemplates that the dispute would come into existence in any particular, specific or prescribed manner - (B) Whether the Labour tribunal is competent to sustain the preliminary objection that the reference made by the Government was incompetent? - Held, power conferred by s. 10(1) on the Government to refer the dispute can be exercised not only where an industrial dispute exists but when it is also apprehended - It will not be competent for the Court to hold the reference bad and quash the proceedings for want of jurisdiction merely because in its opinion there was no material before the Government on which it could have come to an affirmative conclusion of those matters - Appeal allowed.
