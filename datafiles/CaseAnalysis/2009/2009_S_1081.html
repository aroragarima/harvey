Santosh Kumar Dubey v State of Utar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U.P. Recruitment of Dependents of Government Servants Dying in Harness Rules, 1974

Summary: Service - U.P. Recruitment of Dependents of Government Servants Dying in harness Rules, 1974, r. 5 - Compassionate appointment - Father of appellant was a constable, untraceable since 1981 - Writ petitions filed by appellant and his mother - Observations made that since whereabouts of the father of appellant are not known for last seven years therefore the concept of deemed death be invoked; thus direction was issued as to payment of service benefits of the father - Contention before High Court raised as to that as benefits had been given, a right has accrued to the appellant to seek for a compassionate appointment - HC in its single Bench rejected claim of compassionate appointment which is confirmed by Division Bench - Whether case for compassionate appointment made out? - Held, No - R. 5 of Rules perused, contentions heard - Time limit is prescribed for making such application, which is five years from the date of death of Govt. servant - Court raised presumption that compassionate appointment can be given in cases of deemed death also without deciding such question on merits, and observed that undisputedly father of appellant as untraceable from 1981 and seven years from 1981 is 1988 from which five years have to be counted which comes to 1993 - Court further pointed that very concept of giving compassionate appointment is to tide over the financial difficulties that is faced by the family of the deceased due to the death of the earning member of the family; there is immediate loss of earning for which the family suffers financial hardship; it cannot be another source of recruitment; it cannot be treated as bonanza and also as a right to get an appointment in Govt. service - In instant case for 18 years, family could survive and successfully faced and overcame the financial difficulties that they faced on missing of the earning member - Held further, instant is not a fit case for exercise of Court's jurisdiction - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
