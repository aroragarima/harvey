Harchand Singh and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 186; (1973) SCC (Cr) 962; (1974) 3 SCC 397; AIR 1974 SC 344; 1974 CRLJ 366; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: 12 Practice & Procedure -Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.3 -Murder - Prosecution witness contradicting each other - Validity of evidence- Whether evidence produced by prosecution in instant case was reliable? - The evidence of one set consisted of testimony of three eye-witnesses who were not present at the time of occurrence according to fourth eye-witness who according to prosecution case was with deceased at the time of assault. This fourth eye-witness was also shown to be an unreliable witness by other evidence produced by prosecution - Held, it was a case wherein one set of evidence condemned other set leaving Court with no reliable and trustworthy evidence upon which conviction of accused might be based - Appeal allowed.
