Messrs Shanti Vijay and Company and Others v Princess Fatima Fouzia and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 370; (1979) 4 SCC 602; AIR 1980 SC 17; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 459

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, s. 2(h) - Trust Act, 1882, ss. 47, 48 and 49 - Whether there was concluded contract effected between appellants and other successful bidders of one part, and Board of Trustees of other, for sale of 37 items of jewellery? - Held, there is no such clause in trust deed authorising execution of trusts to be carried out not by all but by one or more or majority of trustees and in absence of such a specific provision, general law envisaged in s. 48 of Act would govern rights of parties, therefore alleged contracts of sale entered into by four trustees were not binding and of no legal effect, and could not be enforced - Appeal disposed of.
