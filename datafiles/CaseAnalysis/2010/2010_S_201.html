Gangula Mohan Reddy v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1; (2010) 1 SCC 750; AIR 2010 SC 327; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 615; 2010 CRLJ 2110; 2010 (1) JCC 493; JT 2010 (1) SC 17; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 410; 2010 (1) OLR 779; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 603; 2010 (1) RLW 639; 2010(1) SCALE 1; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 7; 2010 (1) UC 216

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Suicide Act, 1961

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Abetment to suicide - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 306, IPC and his conviction on appeal was confirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Held, in order to convict a person u/s. 306, IPC there has to be a clear mens rea to commit offence - Without a positive act on part of accused to instigate or aid in committing suicide, conviction cannot be sustained - In the light of the provisions of law, conviction of appellant cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
