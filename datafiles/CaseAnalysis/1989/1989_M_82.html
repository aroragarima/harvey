Krishna Ram Mahale (Dead), By His Lrs v Mrs. Shobha Venkat Rao

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 218; (1989) 4 SCC 131; AIR 1989 SC 2097; 1989 (3) Bom.C.R. 364; JT 1989 (3) SC 489; 1989 MPLJ 767; 1990 (1) RCR(Rent) 525; 1989(2) SCALE 424

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20, r. 9 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Appeal by special leave - Licensee and licensor - Unlawful dispossession before expiry of licence period - Counsel for a party made statement on instructions before Supreme Court that opposite party is dead - (A) Decree for recovery of possession - (B) Suppression or falsity of facts before Court - Held, licensee was entitled to, notwithstanding that period of licence had expired long back during pendency of legal proceedings - Considering conduct leave not granted, as instruction appeared to have been given recklessly and art. 136 also not intended to come to assistance of such party - Appeal dismissed.
