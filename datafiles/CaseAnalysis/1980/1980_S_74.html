Commissioner of Income Tax, Kerala v Smt. P.K. Kochammu Amma, Peroke

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 598; (1981) 1 SCC 241; AIR 1980 SC 2124; 1980 CTR 196; [1980] 125 ITR 624; 1980 LabIC 1291; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 781; [1980] 4 TAXMAN 11

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Respondent-assessee was a partner in two partnership firms - Respondent's husband and minor daughter were also the partners of those firms - Respondent filed return of income but did not show the amounts representing shares of her husband and minor daughter in the return - Income Tax Officer held that respondent concealed particulars of income by not disclosing income of her husband and minor daughter alongwith her income - Assistant Appellate Commissioner issued notice u/s. 274 of Act and imposed a penalty on respondent - The HC accordingly answered the question referred to it in favour of the respondent and against the appellant - The appellant thereupon preferred the instant appeal with special leave obtained from instant Court - Whether, the respondent in fact concealed the income as alleged by the appellant, by non disclosure of income of her husband and minor daughter - Held, within the meaning and the purview of the s. 34(1) (a) of the Act respondent was not said to had concealed her income, by not disclosing the income of her husband and minor daughter in return filed by her - Appeal dismissed.
