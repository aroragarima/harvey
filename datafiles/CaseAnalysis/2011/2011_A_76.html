Arundhati Ashok Walavalkar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 43; (2011) 11 SCC 324; 2011 (2) Bom.C.R. 14; 2011 (128) FLR 554; JT 2011 (1) SC 165; 2011 (2) LLJ 206; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 320; 2011(1) SCALE 424; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 355; 2011 (1) SLR 614

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Maharashtra Civil Services Conduct Rules, 1979

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline & Appeal) Rules, 1979, r. 8(25)(e) - Misconduct - Compulsory retirement - Validity - Appellant/Metropolitan Magistrate allegedly travelled in a local train in three occasions without ticket - Memo of charges was issued to the appellant, which was replied by her - Disciplinary Authority having not been satisfied with the appellant's reply, initiated a departmental inquiry - Inquiry Officer found that the appellant was guilty of misconduct - Respondent/Authority issued a show cause notice to appellant asking her to explain as to why a major penalty including a penalty of dismissal from service would not be imposed on the appellant - Appellant gave reply to the show case notice, however, Disciplinary Authority, by impugned order, imposed the penalty of compulsory retirement - Appellant challenged the order of penalty before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Appellant contended that she had not travelled by train on those three alleged days of ticketless travelling - (A) Whether the charges levelled against the appellant were proved - Held, specific stand of the Railway and also of the departmental authority in the inquiry was that the appellant when accosted for her ticketless travelling, she simply passed her identity card to the hands of the ticket collector and went away and giving no opportunity to the ticket collector to detain her - No justifiable reason that the identity card of appellant was recovered at the Railway Station if she had not at all travelled by train on the first alleged occasion - In the second occasion also the appellant could not produce any valid ticket or pass when she was accosted and asked to produce her valid ticket/pass - Defence of the appellant was that she had lost ticket while boarding the train could always be taken by anybody, however, there must be some basic facts supporting such statement which could not be produced by the appellant - On third alleged occasion, when the appellant was caught without ticket and when she was asked to produce the ticket, she could not do so nor was she prepared to pay the charges on the ground that she was a Magistrate and therefore has a right to travel without ticket - Railway records established that appellant had paid the fine amount for ticketless travelling - Appellant, subsequently, wrote a letter to the General Manager of Central Railway stating that at times she was unable to buy tickets because of shortage of time for which she had been harassed by the ticket collectors, therefore, she should be provided a free passage in a First Class compartment of local trains for the purpose of reaching the courts in time during such emergencies - A letter written immediately after the alleged incident clearly indicated that she had travelled without ticket on various occasions and she had taken offence for demanding a ticket from her as she was a Magistrate and she had made complaint against the ticket collectors - Offence as alleged against the appellant in the memo of charges, therefore, is established on her own showing and therefore, the Inquiry Officer was justified in coming to the conclusion that the charges levelled against her stood proved - (B) Whether the inquiry officer was justified in recommending punishment to the appellant and such punishment is proportionate to her delinquency - Held, r. 8(25)(e) of the Rules provided and permitted an inquiry officer to recommend for the punishment to be provided in the facts of the case - Disciplinary authority considered the records and thereafter came to an independent finding that the appellant was guilty of the charges framed against her of misconduct and that in the facts and circumstances of the case, a major penalty like compulsory retirement from service could only be imposed on her and consequently such a punishment was decided to be imposed - Further, present case is a case of judicial officer who was required to conduct herself with dignity and manner becoming of a judicial officer - A judicial officer must be able to discharge his/her responsibilities by showing an impeccable conduct - In the instant case, appellant not only travelled without tickets in a railway compartment thrice but also complained against the ticket collectors who accosted her, misbehaved with the Railway officials and in those circumstances, the punishment of compulsory retirement awarded to her could not be said to be disproportionate to the offence alleged against her - No interference on impugned HC order - Appeal dismissed.
