Maharashtra State Board of Secondary and Higher Secondary Education v Paritosh Bhupesh Kumar Sheth Etc

Supreme Court of India

17 July 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 322; (1984) 4 SCC 27; AIR 1984 SC 1543; 1984 (86) BomLR 428; 1984(2) SCALE 30; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Maharashtra Secondary and Higher Secondary Education Boards Regulations, 1977, regns. 104(3), 36, 3(1), 18, 19, 38 and 14(3) - (A) Whether under law a candidate has a right to demand such an inspection, verification and revaluation of answer books? - Held, no candidate shall be entitled to disclosure or inspection of answer books or other documents as these are to be treated as most confidential is ultra vires on ground of its being in excess of regulation-making power of Board - In opinion of DB, said provision cannot be said to serve any purpose of Act, but is, on contrary, 'defeasive' of same - (B) Whether cl. (3) of regn. 104 is ultra vires regulation-making powers of Board? - Held, Prohibition against inspection or disclosure of answer papers and other documents and declaration made in impugned clause that they are 'treated by Divisional Board as confidential documents' do not serve any of purposes of Act and hence these provisions are ultra vires - Appeal allowed.
