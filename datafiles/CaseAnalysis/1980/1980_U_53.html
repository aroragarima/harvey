Lallubhai Jogibhai Patel v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 44; (1981) 2 SCC 427; (1981) SCC (Cr) 463; AIR 1981 SC 728; [1982] 52 Comp Cas 543; 1981 CRLJ 286; 1981 CRLR 99; 1981 CrLR(SC) 99; 1981 MLJ 350; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 352

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Summary: Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art.22(5), 32, 226 and 359- Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3 and 3(3)- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Petitioner was served with an order of detention by the Deputy Secretary to Govt. of Gujarat (Home Department) - Grounds of detention also served on same day the petitioner challenged order by Writ Petition -Petition dismissed - Reasons announced later - Additional grounds filed after dismissal -(A) Whether doctrine of constructive res judicata applies to a subsequent petition for a writ of habeas corpus on a ground which he 'might and ought' to have taken in his earlier petition for same relief? - Application of doctrine of constructive res judicata is confined to civil actions and civil proceedings - Held, this principle of public policy is entirely inapplicable to illegal detention and does not bar a subsequent petition for a writ of habeas corpus u/art.32 of Constitution on fresh grounds, which were not taken in earlier petition for same relief - (B)Whether continued detention of detenu was illegal? - Materials not supplied to detenu were evidently a part of those materials which had influenced mind of detaining authority in passing order of detention - Representation made by detenu and forwarded to Central Govt. at his instance was not disposed for a long time and grounds of detention in English language were forwarded to him though he did not know English - Further held, continuation of his detention was illegal - (C) Whether not conveying grounds of detention in a language known by accused infringed art.22(5) of Constitution? - Whole purpose of communicating 'ground' to detenu is to enable him to make a purposeful and effective representation - Further held, If 'grounds' are only verbally explained to detenu and nothing in writing is left with him, in a language which he understands, then that purpose is not served - Constitutional mandate in art.22(5) of Constitution is infringed - Petition allowed.
