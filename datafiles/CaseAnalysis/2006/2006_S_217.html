Sasi Thomas v State & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 850; (2006) 12 SCC 421; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 72; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 240; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 695; 2006(12) SCALE 507; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 450

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 173(8), 216, 311, 391, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 306 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 32 - Appellant filed an application purported to be u/s. 482 of CrPC inter alia praying for further investigation in aforementioned case by CBI - Held, in event of open marshalling of evidence, it comes to opinion that a case has been made out for alteration of charge, it indisputably can do so in exercise of its power u/s. 311 of CrPC - It is not beyond jurisdiction of this Court to direct further investigation by CBI - Declined to do so keeping in view fact that 47 witnesses including appellant himself have already been examined and recourse thereto can be taken if during trial a case therefor is found to be have been made out - Appeal disposed of.
