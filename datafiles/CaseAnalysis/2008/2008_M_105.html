Tamil Nadu Electricity Board v M/S Rasipuram Textile(P) Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2078; (2008) 17 SCC 285; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 758; [2009] 147 Comp Cas 315; JT 2008 (13) SC 400; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 71; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 609; 2008(16) SCALE 387; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 11

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Apparatus, Offences By Companies, Theft Of Electrical Energy, Daily Fine

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 39(1), 44(1)(c), 49A - Inspection - Theft of energy - Responsibility - Absence of evidence - One executive engineer of State electricity Board made surprise inspection of premises of respondent no.1, where, theft of electrical energy was detected - Criminal prosecution was lodged not only against company but also against its managing Director and other Directors u/ss. 39(1) and 44(1)(c) of the Act - Trial Judge, convicted accused and Directors of company - Managing Director of company and another Director of company expired during pendency of proceedings - Thereafter, Additional District and Sessions Judge, setting aside judgment of trial Court - Revision application filed by appellant was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether private respondents and/or any of them was in charge and/or responsible for conduct of business of company in absence of any evidence brought on record by complainant to satisfy requirements of s. 49A of the Act - Held, Trial Judge had misconstrued and misinterpreted provisions of s. 49A of the Act - In terms of s. 49A(1) of the Act it was for complainant to prove that Director of company at time when theft was committed was in charge of and/or was responsible for conduct of its business - Only in event such an averment was made and sufficient and cogent evidence was brought on record to prove said allegations, proviso appended to s. 49A of the Act would be attracted - It was proved that Director or Group of Directors of company were in charge of and/or were responsible for conduct of business of company, burden would shift on accused to establish ingredients contained in proviso appended to s.49A of the Act - Thus, Additional Sessions Judge as well as HC were right in holding that in absence of any averment made in complaint petition as in absence of any evidence brought on record by complainant to satisfy requirements of s.49A of the Act, respondents could not convicted - Appeal dismissed.
