Sree Narayana Dharma-Sangam Trust v Swami Prakasananda and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 949; (1997) 6 SCC 78; 1997 (30) ALR 332; 1998 (1) AWC 365; 1997 (2) CLT 388; JT 1997 (5) SC 100; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 135; 1998 (89) RD 155; 1997(3) SCALE 745; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 799; 1997 (4) Supreme 651; 1997 (2) UJ 32

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 , 136 and 226 - Whether, the dismissal of special leave petition in limine operates as a final order between the parties and any order passed by the High Court subsequently operates as a res judicata? - Whether, the dismissal of the writ petition u/art. 32 operates as res judicata on jurisdiction of the HC u/art. 226? - Held, dismissal of special leave petition in limine operates as a final order between the parties and any order passed by the HC subsequently operates as a res judicata between the parties - Dismissal of the writ petition u/art. 32 does not operate as res judicata on jurisdiction of the HC u/art. 226 - Appeal dismissed.
