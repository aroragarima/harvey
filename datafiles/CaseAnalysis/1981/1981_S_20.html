Sarkari Sasta Anaj Vikreta Singh and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 607; (1981) 4 SCC 471; AIR 1981 SC 2030

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Consumer Protection; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Socio-Economic

Summary: Socio-Economic - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Madhya Pradesh Foodstuffs (Civil Supply Distribution) Scheme, 1981 - (A) Whether amendment to Control Order requires it to be placed before Legislature within six months to validate same? - Held, what is required to be placed before Parliament u/s. 3, sub-s. (6) of Essential Commodities Act is an order made by Central Govt. or by any officer or authority of Central Govt. - An order made by State Govt. in exercise of powers delegated to it by Central Govt. u/s. 5 is not required to be placed before Parliament - (B) Whether amendment of Control Order which had effect of doing away with 'appointed retailers' without notice to them is violative of principles of natural justice? - Held, amendment of Control Order being a legislative function there is no requirement to afford an opportunity to those who were to be affected by it - (C) Whether formulation of scheme by State Govt. was an exercise of power by delegate of a delegate? - Held, executive power of State Govt. u/art. 162 of Constitution is co-extensive with legislative power of State Legislature Entry 33 (b) of List III (Concurrent List) is 'trade and commerce in, and production, supply and distribution of foodstuffs, including edible oil-seeds and oils' - Govt., therefore, has undoubted right to make a scheme for distribution of foodstuffs, without being vested with any special authority under any order made under Essential Commodities Act - (D) Whether new scheme prioritising Co-operative Societies are discriminatory? - Held, there was a reasonable classification and a nexus with object intended to be achieved, which was a fair and assured supply of rations to consumer - Further, fundamental right of traders to carry on business in foodstuffs was in no way affected - Hence Scheme prioritising Co-operative Societies is not discriminatory - Petitions dismissed.
