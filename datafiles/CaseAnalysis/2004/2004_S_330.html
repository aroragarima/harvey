Inder Parkash Gupta v State Of Jammu & Kashmir & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 330; (2004) 6 SCC 786; AIR 2004 SC 2523; 2004 (2) CLR 637; 2004 (102) FLR 226; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 338; 2004 (3) LLN 51; 2004(5) SCALE 90; 2004 (3) SLR 658; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 14, 309, 320 - Jammu and Kashmir Constitution, ss. 124, 133 - Jammu & Kashmir, Medical (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1979, rr. 8, 133 - Jammu & Kashmir Public Service Commission (Business & Procedure) Rules, 1980, r. 51 - Selection and appointment of Respondents - While going through selection process Commission must scrupulously follow statutory rules operating in field - Rule 8 mandates that while selecting teaching wing of service, Commission must have regard to academic qualification of candidate, teaching experience, research experience and previous record of work, if any - HC was correct in holding that Rule 51 providing for 100 marks for viva voce against 40 for other criteria is contrary to law laid down by this Court - For allocation of marks for viva voce test, no hard and fast rule of universal application which would meet requirements of all cases can be laid down - When allocation of such mark is made with an intention which is capable of being abused or misused in its exercise, it is liable to be struck down as ultra vires art.14 of Constitution of India - Having regard to Rule 8 of 1979 Rules higher marks for viva voce test could not have been allotted as has rightly been observed by HC - Interest of justice would be sub-served if State is directed to fully comply with directions of HC by giving all benefits to appellant herein including monetary benefits and seniority by placing him in select list above Respondents 3 and 9 and further direct that if any respondent has been promoted to higher post in meantime same would be subject to direction.
