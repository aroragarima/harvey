Rameshbhai Chandubhai Rathod v The State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, P. Sathasivam, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 62; (2011) 2 SCC 764; AIR 2011 SC 803; 2011 CRLJ 1458; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 278; JT 2011 (2) SC 35; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 682; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 728; 2011(1) SCALE 757; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 829

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Rape and Murder - Conviction - Death Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly raped and murdered the deceased/minor - Trial Court, after the appreciation of circumstantial evidences, convicted the appellant for murder and sentenced him to death - HC, in death reference, confirmed the conviction and death sentence - Appellant filed appeal before the SC - DB of SC delivered two judgments whereby the conviction of the appellant was unanimously maintained, however, a difference of opinion arose as to the death sentence - Hence the present reference by the DB - Whether the award of death sentence to appellant ought to be maintained - Held, DB extensively relied on the case of Dhanonjoy Chatterjee v. State of West Bengal, 1994 INDLAW SC 1743, however, there was a distinction of facts between Dhanonjoy Chatterjee case and the present one - Appellant was a young man and only 27 years of age, hence, it was obligatory on the Trial Court to have given a finding as to a possible rehabilitation and reformation and the possibility that he could still become a useful member of society in case he was given a chance to do so - At the same time the gravity of the offence, the behaviour of the appellant and the fear and concern such incidents generate in an ordered society, cannot be ignored - Therefore, a via-media ought to be adopted in the light of judgments of SC in Ramraj v. State of Chhattisgarh, 2009 INDLAW SC 2087 and Mulla & Anr. v. State of Uttar Pradesh, 2010 INDLAW SC 87 - In the said two cases, SC has held that the term imprisonment for life which is found in s. 302 of the IPC, would mean imprisonment for the natural life of the convict subject to the powers of the President and the Governor u/arts. 72 and 161 of the Constitution of India or of the State Government u/s. 433-A of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Therefore, death sentence awarded to appellant was commuted to life imprisonment and directed that the life sentence must extend to the full life of the appellant but subject to any remission or commutation at the instance of the Government for good and sufficient reasons - Order accordingly.
