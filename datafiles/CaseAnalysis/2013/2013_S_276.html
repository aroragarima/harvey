Bhaikon @ Bakul Borah v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 282; (2013) 9 SCC 769; JT 2013 (10) SC 373; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 952; 2013(6) SCALE 496; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Remission Of Sentence, Rape and murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 - Rape and murder - Conviction - Remission of sentence - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offence u/ss. 302 and 376 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment convicted appellant and sentenced him to death - Thereafter, Death reference was made to HC for confirmation of death sentence - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment disposed of death reference and appeal filed by appellant by commuting the sentence of death to imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, entire prosecution case rests on solitary evidence of eye-witness PW1 - According to PW1, appellant engaged him as a labourer in his farm house and deposed that he saw appellant and his friend following the deceased and on seeing same, PW1 also followed them and saw that appellant and his companion behaving indecently with the girl, committed rape on her and, thereafter, appellant assaulted the girl by throttling her neck - PW1 noticed that because of acts of appellant, the girl died on the spot and appellant along with accomplice dragged her to the nearby place surrounded by shrubs and bushes and left the body there - Trial Court and HC rightly observed that there was no reason to disbelieve the version of PW1 and corroborative evidence of PW2 (father of deceased) - However, HC, took note of fact that appellant was is a young man of 33 years of age and finding that case did not come under purview of 'rarest of rare' category, declined to confirm the sentence of death and altered the same to imprisonment for life while upholding conviction under both counts - When death sentence was commuted to imprisonment for life by Appellate Court, concerned Govt. was permitted to exercise its executive power of remission cautiously, taking note of gravity of offence - Impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
