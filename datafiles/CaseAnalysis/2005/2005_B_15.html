Babba Alias Shankar Raghuman Rohida v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1349; (2005) 11 SCC 569

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Entitlement of Bail - Petitioner was involved in offence punishable under TADA and he was in jail since dt.16-7-1992 and bail was refused to him - Hence, instant Petition - Whether petitioner is entitle for Bail.

Held, there is no regular TADA Court at Bombay and District Judge is put in charge of trial of TADA cases for time being and State want some more time to commence trial of accused. Thus, bail is granted to petitioner on certain stringent conditions. Petitioner shall report to Police Inspector, Ulhas Nagar Unit, Crime Branch once in a week and shall not leave jurisdiction of Thane Police Station without previous permission of TADA Special Judge. If petitioner has got any passport or any other travel documents, he shall surrender same before TADA Special Judge. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - Considering nature and circumstances f case and gravity of offence, Court can grant bail to accused on certain conditions.
