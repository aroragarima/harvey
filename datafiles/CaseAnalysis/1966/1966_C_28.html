S. Narayanappa and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1967 SC 523; [1967] 63 ITR 219; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1) - A/y 1951-52 - Jurisdiction - Income Tax Officer - Appellant was carrying on business in jewellery, copper-wire and money lending - A scrutiny of the wealth statement and the Bank account and the extensive nature of the business carried on by the appellant led the Income-tax Officer to entertain a belief that the income of the a/y 1951-52 had been under-assessed - Income Tax Officer issued a notice u/s. 34(1) of 1922 Act and after examining the return made, he assessed the income of the appellant at Rs. 89,002/- - Appellant filed an appeal against the assessment order of the Income Tax Officer to the Asst. Commissioner but the appeal was dismissed - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that Income Tax Officer has no jurisdiction to initiate the proceedings u/s. 34(1) of 1922 Act - Tribunal over-ruled the objection and dismissed the appeal - On appeal, HC held that the Income-tax Officer had jurisdiction to initiate proceedings against the appellant u/s. 34(1) (a) of the 1922 Act for the a/y 1951-52 - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the Income-tax Officer had jurisdiction to initiate proceedings for the a/y 1951-52 under the provisions of s. 34(1) (a) of 1922 Act? - Held, appellant contended that the Income Tax Officer should have communicated to him the reasons which led him to initiate the proceedings u/s. 34 of 1922 Act - The proceedings for assessment or reassessment u/s. 34(1) (a) of 1922 Act start with the issue of a notice and it is only after the service of the notice that the assessee, whose income is sought to be assessed or re-assessed, becomes a party to those proceedings - The earlier stage of the proceeding for recording the reasons of the Income-tax Officer and for obtaining the sanction of the Commissioner are administrative in character and are not quasi-judicial - The scheme of s. 34 of 1922 Act is that, if the conditions of the main section are satisfied a notice has to be issued to the assessee containing all or any of the requirements which may be included in a notice u/s. 22(2) of 1922 Act - There is no requirement in any of the provisions of the Act or any section laying down as a condition for the initiation of the proceedings that the reasons which induced the Commissioner to accord sanction to proceed u/s. 34 of 1922 Act must also be communicated to the assessee - Hence, HC rightly held that the Income-tax Officer had reasonable grounds for thinking that there was non-disclosure on the part of the appellant and that there was under-assessment for the a/y 1951-52 - Income Tax Officer has jurisdiction to initiate proceedings against the appellant u/s. 34(1) (a) of the 1922 Act for the a/y 1951-52 - Appeal dismissed.
