Â Cumbum Roadways Private Limited v Somu Transport Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 431; AIR 1966 SC 1366; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 17; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 7

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Writ Proceedings

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Grant of permit - Permission for stage carriage route - Review of Appeals - Regional Transport Authority granted stage carriage permit on certain route to respondent no.1 out of large number of applicants - Appellate Tribunal set aside order of grant of Transport Authority - Respondent no.1 filed petition in HC before Single Judge who allowed petition and quashed order of Appellate Tribunal leaving it free to dispose of appeal afresh - Letters Patent Appeal was filed by appellant which was disposed of by Division Bench of HC holding that Appellate Tribunal should consider all appeals that had been filed before it - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether order of HC reviving other appeals before Appellate Tribunal besides two between parties which went to HC was correct - Held, Appellate Tribunal was plainly influenced by Govt. Order, it dealt with appeals before it and could not be said to be case decision of Appellate Tribunal was not influenced by Govt. Order in question - Remand should only be confined to those parties which came to HC and not extend to others, as HC could have no jurisdiction to interfere with orders of Appellate Tribunal either in favour of or against parties which have not came to it - Thus, order of Appeal Court could have to be modified and remand confined to reconsideration of appeal of appellant alone as against claims of respondent, and Appellate Tribunal should decide between those two only who should be granted permit for routes in question - Further, it appeared that there were two petitions before HC and they gave rise to two appeals - Appeal was only from one of appeals, in which appellant was appellant and respondent No. 1 was respondent No. 1 - Thus, reconsideration before Appellate Tribunal would only be confined to parties which went to HC in writ proceedings and respondents - Appeals partly allowed.
