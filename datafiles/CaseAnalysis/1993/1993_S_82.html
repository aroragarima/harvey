T. S. Thiruvengadam v Secretary To Government of India, Ministry of Finance, Department of Expenditure, New Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 892; (1993) 2 SCC 174; (1993) SCC (L&S) 495; 1993 (24) ATC 102; 1993 (1) CLR 581; JT 1993 (1) SC 609; 1993 LabIC 863; 1993 (1) LLJ 1101; 1993 (1) LLN 755; 1993(1) SCALE 625; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 1078; 1993 (3) SLJ 41; 1993 (2) SLR 34; 1993 (1) UJ 689; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 910

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Defence Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 37 - Appellant was in the service of Central Govt. for a period of 15 years - Appellant was not given the pro-rate pension and death-cum-retirement gratuity - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the appellant was eligible for pro-rate pension and death-cum-retirement gratuity based on the length of his qualifying service - Held, that the appellant should be granted pro-rata pension and other benefits, under the Office Memorandum, dt.1 6-6-1967 - R. 37 of the 1972 Rules thus, provided that a govt. servant who had been permitted to be absorbed in service in a Central Govt. public undertaking in public interest, would be deemed to have retired from service from the date of such absorption and should be eligible to receive retirement benefits in accordance with the orders of the Govt. applicable to him - When the Rule specifically provided that all the persons who fulfilled the pre-conditions prescribed therein should be deemed to have retired from govt. service from the date of absorption and should be eligible to receive retirement benefits then the govt. while granting benefits could not deny the same to some of them on the basis of arbitrary classification - Appeal allowed.
