M/S. J. K. Cotton Spinning & Weaving Nulls Co. Ltd. v Sales Tax Officer, Kanpur And Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1965 SC 1310; 1998 (75) ECR 473; 1997 (91) E.L.T. 34; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 900; 1965 (16) STC 563

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Processing

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 7 and 8(3)(b) - Amendment of Certificate of Registration - Assessee-manufacturer of textile goods, tiles and other commodities, applied for registration u/s. 7 of 1956 Act and requested that certain goods be specified in its certificate of registration for the purpose of getting benefit u/s. 8(1) of the Act - Sales Tax Officer at first accepted the assessee's claim in respect of all the goods as requested by the assessee but later on directed that certain goods, namely, 'drawing material, photographic material, building materials including lime and cement, electricals, iron and steel, and coal', be deleted from the assessee's certificate of registration - Assessee filed petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution which was dismissed by the HC who also held that the drawing materials, photographic materials, colour, chemicals, electricals machinery and building materials such as cement and lime were not comprehended in the expression in the manufacture or processing of goods for sale' within the meaning of s. 8(3) (b) of 1956 Act - Hence, present appeal - Whether drawing instruments, photographic materials, colours, chemicals, electricals, machinery and building materials such as cement, lime are comprehended in expression in manufacture or processing of goods? - Held, where any particular process is so integrally connected with the ultimate production of goods that but for that process, manufacture or processing of goods would be commercially inexpedient, goods required in that process would, fall within the expression 'in the manufacture of goods' - It is not necessary that they must of necessity be goods which are used as 'ingredient or commodity in the creation of goods', or which are 'directly and actually needed for turning out or making of the goods - Drawing and photographic materials falling within description of goods intended for use as 'equipment' in process of designing which is directly related to actual production of goods and without which commercial production would be inexpedient must be regarded as goods intended for use 'in manufacture of goods' - Building materials including lime and cement not required in manufacture of tiles for sale cannot be regarded within meaning of s. 8(3) of 1956 Act, as raw materials in manufacture or processing of goods or even as 'plant' - However, if, having regard to normal conditions prevalent in the industry, production of finished goods would be difficult without use of electrical equipment, equipment would be regarded as intended for use in manufacture of goods for sale - Order of Sales Tax Officer modified - Appeal allowed.
