Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes (Asst.) v Dharnendra Trading Company, Etc

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 924; (1988) SCC (Tax) 432; (1988) 3 SCC 570; AIR 1988 SC 1247; 1988 (17) ECR 652; [1988] 172 ITR 395; JT 1988 (2) SC 606; 1988(1) SCALE 973; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 946; 1988 (70) STC 59

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 8A - Govt. adopted a policy of rapid industrialisation and issued directions indicating the incentives and concessions to entrepreneurs starting new industries - Subsequent order putting ceiling on incentives on ground of misuse of scheme - (A) Writ petitions claiming that the industrial undertakings started between the two orders could not be deprived of the concessions as the said grant of concessions constituted a promissory estoppel against the Govt. on the basis of which they had acted by starting new industries requiring investment of considerable funds and the Govt. was not entitled to go back or that promise - (B) Ultravires - Plea raised by State or its functionaries - Maintainability - (C) Benefit by way of refund of sales tax paid on purchase of raw material granted - Refund is in nature of exemption - Held, it cannot resile from the promise and withdraw the concessions - Not open for sales tax officers and the State itself to contend that the scheme itself is ultra vires statutory provisions - Scheme is intra vires s.8A and its non mention in the scheme is not material - Appeal dismissed.
