Damodar v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 757; (2004) 12 SCC 336; AIR 2003 SC 4414; 2003 (4) CCR 92; 2003 (4) Crimes 98; 2004 (1) CRJ 243; 2003 CRLJ 5014; 2003 (3) JCC 1567; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 234; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 354; 2003(7) SCALE 627; 2003 (1) SCW 5050; 2003 (5) SLT 583; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 904; 2003 (6) Supreme 722

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 114, 120B, 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - s. 162 - Perpetrators of homicidal death of an octogenarian Sadhu - Conviction of accused confirmed by High Court - Application of s. 120B - Held, the evidence on record is too scanty and meagre to bring in application of s. 120B, IPC, 1860. It is also improbable that PW-15 could have heard about the exhortation by accused Balak Dass to run over the vehicle consideration the distance from which the statement is said to have been made and the speed of the vehicle. Merely because accused-Damodar is the son of Balak Dass, who had a dispute with the deceased over properties, is not sufficient to establish the charge of conspiracy. That being so, the conviction of all the three appellants under s. 302 r/w s. 120B, IPC, 1860 cannot be maintained.
