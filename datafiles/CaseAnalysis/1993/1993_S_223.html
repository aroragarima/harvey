Naniram v State Of M. P.

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1493; (1994) Supp2 SCC 539; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1487; AIR 1994 SC 840; AIR 1994 SCW 211; 1994 CRLJ 946

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC,1860,s.300 - The accused was alleged of burning his wife and was convicted on the basis of dying declarion by the high court but the dying declaration recorded by Sub-Inspector was in nature of F.I.R. and no attestation from doctor taken to the effect whether patient was conscious or not - There was also failure to take signature or thumb impression of deceased - Whether the sessions judge was justified in acquitting the accused ? - There is no other evidence against the appellant except this dying declaration which is of highly doubtful nature - Held,the learned Sessions Judge has given the good reasons for acquitting the accused and the view taken by him is quite reasonable and there is no ground for reversing the same by the High Court - Conviction set aside - Appeal Allowed
