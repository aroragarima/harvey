Sudarsha Avasthi v Shiv Pal Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 887; (2008) 7 SCC 604; 2008 (6) ADJ 434; AIR 2008 SC 2724; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 37; 2008 (4) AWC 3550; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 857; 2008(8) SCALE 415; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 116

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Ballot Paper, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Contents Of Petition, Corrupt Practice, Election Matter, Satisfaction Of The Returning Officer

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 123, 83 - Allegation of bribing Returning Officer for contesting the election and to another candidate for withdrawing, that also in full public view - Whether the election petition deserved to be dismissed on ground that it did not disclose any cause of action of corrupt practice of bribing and material facts and the particulars were lacking? - Held, as per s. 83 of the RP Act, 1951 it is the duty of the person who files the election petition and levels the allegation of corrupt practice, he has to disclose the material facts on which he relies and that should set forth the full particulars of a corrupt practice that the petitioner alleges including the full statement as far as possible disclosing the names of the parties alleged to have committed such corrupt practice and the date and place of commission of each such practice and the same shall be filed by the petitioner and verified in the manner as laid down in the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Allegations are not precise allegations so as to show that these are serious allegations to be tried in the election petition; case lacks in particularity any allegation of bribery as contained in s. 123 r/w s. 83 of the RP Act, 1951; material particulars disclosed are not sufficient to be gone into for trial - Appeal dismissed.
