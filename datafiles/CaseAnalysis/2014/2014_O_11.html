Swiss Timing Limited v Organising Committee, Commonwealth Games 2010, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 387; (2014) 6 SCC 677; AIR 2014 SC 3723; 2014 (4) AWC 4314; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 338; 2015 (1) CompLJ 286; 2014 (3) CTC 830; JT 2014 (7) SC 574; 2014 (6) MahLJ 662; 2014 (4) MPLJ 527; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 805; 2014(7) SCALE 515; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 514

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Nominee arbitrator

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11, 11(4), 11(6), 16, 5, 8 - Nominee arbitrator - Appointment - Instant petition was filed by petitioner/company u/s. 11(4) r/w s. 11(6) of the Act to appoint nominee arbitrator of respondent/Organizing Common wealth Games and to further constitute arbitral Tribunal, by appointing presiding arbitrator in order to adjudicate disputes between parties - Petitioner contended that respondent defaulted in making payment without any justifiable reasons, not only amount was not paid to petitioner, respondent sent a letter asking petitioner to extend Bank Guarantee and there was no provision in service agreement for extension of Bank Guarantee -

Held, it would be appropriate to await decision of criminal proceedings before arbitral tribunal was constituted to go into alleged disputes between parties - SC did not accept aforesaid submission made by respondents - Balance of convenience was tilted more in favor of permitting arbitration proceedings to continue rather than to bring same to grinding halt - Defense of contract being void was now-a-days taken routinely along with other usual grounds, to avoid/delay reference to arbitration - Such ground needs to be summarily rejected unless there was clear indication that defense has reasonable chance of success - Plea was never taken till petition was filed in SC - Respondents were only impressing upon petitioners to supply certain information - It would be appropriate, let Arbitral Tribunal examine whether there was any substance in plea of fraud now sought to be raised by respondents - Purpose of aforesaid solitary rule was to avoid embarrassment to accused - In contrast, findings recorded by arbitral Tribunal in its award would not be binding in criminal proceedings - SC has indicated earlier in case award was made in favor of petitioner, respondents would be at liberty to resist enforcement of same on ground of subsequent conviction of either Chairman or officials of contracting parties - Petitioners has nominated S.N. Variava, former Judge of SC as their arbitrator - SC nominate B.P. Singh, former Judge of SC as second Arbitrator and Justice Kuldip Singh, former Judge of SC as Chairman of Arbitral Tribunal to adjudicate disputes - Petition allowed.
