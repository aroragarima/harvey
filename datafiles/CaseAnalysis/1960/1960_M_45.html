Messrs Swadeshi Cotton Mills Company, Limited, Kanpur v Rajeshwar Prashad and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 1961 SC 429; 1961 (2) FLR 26; 1960 (2) LLJ 707; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, United Provinces Industrial Disputes Act 1947 (UP 28 of 1947) (ENG), Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Rules 1957

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(t), 6-B and 6-C - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Rules, 1957, r. 5(1) - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 15, 16 and 23 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether Compromise settlement recorded can be implemented without recourse to Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(t) or Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Rules, 1957, r. 5(1) when matter is in appeal in Supreme Court - Held, need not be resorted to s. 2(t) or procedure prescribed by r. 5(1) cannot have any application to a compromise agreement which has been entered into between parties pending appeal in this court - Compromise in question is intended to be filed in this court for purpose of enabling parties to request this court to pass an, order in terms of said compromise - Procedure for obtaining such an order which has to be followed is procedure prescribed by rules of this court, just as if a compromise was reached before tribunal procedure to be followed before it would be, procedure prescribed by its rules - Result is that finding recorded by tribunal that compromise in question is valid is obviously right and must be confirmed - Since it is found that compromise in fact has taken place and is otherwise valid - An order to be drawn in terms of said compromise in present appeal - Order of tribunal confirmed - Appeal allowed.
