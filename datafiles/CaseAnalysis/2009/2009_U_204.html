Ningamma and Another v United India Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 807; (2009) 13 SCC 710; 2009 (2) ACC 804; AIR 2009 SC 3056; 2009 (5) ALD(SC) 106; 2010(1) ALL MR 441; 2009 (76) ALR 173; 2009 (3) AWC 3045; 2009 (6) KarLJ 62; 2009 (156) PLR 796; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 435; 2009(8) SCALE 244; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Appeals Disposed Of, Public Service Vehicle, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Use Of Vehicle, Welfare Legislation, Non Obstante Clause, Heads Of Compensation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 163A, 166 and 147 - Motor cycle colliding with bullock cart carrying iron sheets - Motor cycle was driven by the deceased who borrowed it from his friend who owned the motor cycle - Claim of compensation u/s. 163A - Award by MACT partly allowing the claim, which was received by LRs of the deceased - Appeal preferred by insurance company before High Court - HC allowed the appeal holding that petition was not maintainable as there was no tort-feasor involved and further that income of the claimaint was stated to be above Rs. 40,000/-p.a. which makes the petition barred u/s. 163A; HC issued direction for refund of compensation - (A) Whether the LRs of a person, who was driving a motor vehicle, after borrowing it from the real owner meets with an accident without involving any other vehicle, would be entitled to compensation u/s. 163A or under any other provision(s) of law? - Held, No u/s. 163A - Liability u/s. 163A is on the owner of the vehicle; so a person cannot be both, a claimant as also a recipient, with respect to claim; thus heirs of the deceased could not have maintained a claims in terms of s. 163A, 'Oriental Insurance Company Ltd. V. Rajni Devi, (2008) 5 SCC 736' - In instant case, deceased was not the owner of the motorbike in question as he had borrowed the same from its real owner; deceased cannot be held to be employee of the owner of the motorbike although he was authorized to drive the said vehicle by its owner; thus deceased would step into the shoes of the owner of the motorbike - Held further, even if in the pleadings no specific claim was made u/s. 166 a party should not be deprived from getting 'just compensation' in case the claimant is able to make out a case under any provision of law; MV Act is a beneficial and welfare legislation; Court is duty bound and entitled to award 'just compensation' irrespective of the fact whether any plea in that behalf was raised by the claimant or not - Court further observed that on essential issues like whether or not the claimants would be governed with the terms and conditions of the insurance policy and whether or not provisions of s. 147 would be applicable in present case and further whether or not there was rash and negligent driving on the part of the deceased, which are factual are required to be considered and answered by HC - Matter remanded back to HC for its decision on such issues - Appeals disposed of.
