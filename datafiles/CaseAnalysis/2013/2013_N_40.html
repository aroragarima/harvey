Nagendrappa Natikar v Neelamma

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 152; (2014) 14 SCC 452; AIR 2013 SC 1541; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 59; 2013(2) ALL MR 952; 2013 (98) ALR 699; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 616; 2013 CRLJ 2060; 2013 (2) CTC 440; JT 2013 (4) SC 120; 2013 (2) OLR 69; 2013 (172) PLR 55; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 469B; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 424; 2013 (2) RLW 1350; 2013(3) SCALE 561; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 426; 2013 (2) WLN(SC) 38

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 3 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, s. 18 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 25 - Consolidated amount - Permanent alimony - Grant of maintenance - Marriage between petitioner (husband) and respondent (wife) took place - Petitioner was not maintaining his wife, and respondent filed application u/s. 125 of CrPC for grant of maintenance before First Additional JMFC - While matter was pending, application was preferred by parties under O. 23 r. 3 CPC stating that parties had arrived at compromise, by which respondent had agreed to receive amount towards permanent alimony and that she would not make any claim for maintenance in future or enhancement of maintenance - Respondent then filed application u/s. 127 of CrPC before Family Court for cancellation of earlier order and also for awarding future maintenance - Family Court dismissed application and ordered that compromise entered into between parties in proceeding u/s. 125 of CrPC. would not be bar in entertaining suit u/s. 18 of 1956, Act - Petitioner took up matter before HC by filing appeal which was dismissed - Hence, instant Petition - Whether compromise entered into by petitioner and respondent under O. 23 r.3 of CPC, for consolidated amount towards permanent alimony, and giving up any future claim for maintenance, accepted by Court u/s. 125 of CrPC, would preclude wife from claiming maintenance in suit filed u/s. 18 of 1956, Act - Held, s. 125 of CrPC was piece of social legislation which provides for summary and speedy relief by way of maintenance to wife who was unable to maintain herself and her children - However, s. 125 of CrPC was not intended to provide for full and final determination of status and personal rights of parties, which was in nature of civil proceeding, though were governed by provisions of CrPC and order made u/s. 125 of CrPC was tentative and was subject to final determination of rights in civil Court - It was noticed that s. 25 of the 1872, Act provided that any agreement which was opposed to public policy was not enforceable in Court of Law and such agreement was void, since object was unlawful - Therefore, proceeding u/s.125 of CrPC was summary in nature and intended to provide speedy remedy to wife and any order passed u/s.125 of CrPC by compromise or otherwise could not foreclose remedy available to wife u/s. 18(2) of 1956, Act - Hence, agreement with reasoning of Family Court and confirmed by HC that suit u/s. 18 of 1956, Act was perfectly maintainable, in spite of compromise reached between parties under O. 23 r.3 of CPC and accepted by Court and no error in view taken by Family Court, which was affirmed by HC - Petition dismissed.
