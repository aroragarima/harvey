Vigneswara Coop. Housing Society Limited v K. Balachandramouli and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1888; (2005) 13 SCC 506

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure,1908, O.7 r.11 - Specific performance - Validity of plaint - Plaintiff filed suit for specific performance - Additional Chief Judge rejected plaint under O.7 r.11 of CPC on ground that land was notified to be acquired, but plaint itself shows that acquisition proceedings have been dropped - DB confirmed order of Additional Chief Judge passed in suit for specific performance â Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, in the plaint it has been stated that permission was not granted as respondent (the defendant) withdrew his application. However, if petitioners succeed in their suit they could apply again or they may be able to challenge rejection even now. At highest could be ground on which relief may not be ultimately granted to plaintiff in suit but at this stage the plaint cannot be rejected on this ground. It has finally been held that as there is no alternate prayer for damages and for refund plaint stands rejected. Even if refund cannot ultimately be granted, rejection of plaint can only be on the four grounds made under O.7 r.11 of CPC. Thus, none of grounds set out in O.7 r.11 of CPC have been made out. Thus, both orders are set aside and remit matter back to Trial Court. Appeal disposed of.
