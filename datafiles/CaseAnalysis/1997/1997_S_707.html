Shiv Ram and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1607; (1998) 1 SCC 149; (1998) SCC (Cr) 278; AIR 1998 SC 49; 1998 (3) CCR 70; 1997 (4) Crimes 96; 1998 CRLJ 76; JT 1997 (8) SC 500; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 778; 1997(6) SCALE 466; 1998 (5) SLT 471; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 531; 1997 (9) Supreme 34

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ghastly and brutal murders

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147, 148, 307, 436, 452, 404 r/w s. 149 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 366 - Ghastly and brutal murders - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302, 147, 148, 307, 436, 452, 404 r/w s. 149 of IPC and u/s. 25 of the Act - Trial Court awarded death sentence to 4 accused, life imprisonment to 12 accused and acquitted 7 accused - Thereafter, Trial Court made a reference u/s. 366 of CrPC; convicted accused filed appeals including those who had been awarded capital punishment - Respondent (State) filed 2 appeals; one against order of acquittal of 7 accused and another for enhancement of sentence in respect of other accused before HC - HC by impugned judgment confirmed death sentences awarded to 4 accused and allowing State appeal for enhancement, awarded death sentence to 3 accused - State appeal filed against order of acquittal was allowed and accused were convicted for various offences including substantive offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and sentenced each one of them to suffer life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, it was well settled that evidence of witnesses could not be discredited only on the ground that they were close relatives of deceased persons - In instant case, PW1 was son and grandson of deceased persons and PW2 was neighbor of deceased persons - Evidence of both witnesses was absolutely straightforward, unblemished and without any infirmity - FIR was lodged within 4 hours, naming all accused also lends assurance that evidence of those witnesses was trustworthy and could not be discarded - Police machinery reached place of incident within a short and evidence of PW14 (sub-inspector of police) would show that wheels of investigating machinery started moving during the same night - There was nothing in evidence of PW14 to indicate that he had antedated ail documents to suit complainant's version - It was only a figment of imagination to contend that investigation carried out by investigating machinery was antedated at the instance of complainant - It was true that complainant at the relevant time was posted at a far-off place but he testified that he had come to village on 2 days' leave as he had not come till then to his village after he joined service - That explanation given by witness was quite plausible and Courts below were right in accepting his presence at the time of occurrence - There was no manner of doubt that accused persons formed an unlawful assembly and object of that assembly was not only to take revenge against deceased persons but also to take revenge upon family members of deceased persons and accused persons were under the belief that victims were giving shelter to their rivals - All appellants formed an unlawful assembly with an object to take revenge against their rivals and to commit mass murder in that process to prove their supremacy and create terror in minds of family members of deceased - Occurrence was full of revenge which was deep-rooted in minds and actions of accused persons and they were determined to take revenge in same manner in which deceased was murdered - There was no difficulty in holding that unlawful assembly could develop a common object on the spur of moment to commit massacre of family members of deceased - Evidence did not prove plea of alibi set up by accused nos. 11 and 12 - Evidence even on test of probability could not prove the plea of alibi set up by accused nos. 1, 11 and 12 - In view of proved facts death sentence awarded to accused no. 6 was not proper and instead he should undergo life imprisonment - HC had committed no error whatsoever in awarding death sentence to accused nos. 1, 2, 4, 5, 8 and 13 - Impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Appeals disposed of.
