Kumara Nand v Brijmohan Lal Sharma

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 136; AIR 1967 SC 808; 1967 CRLJ 823; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Statement Of Fact

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(4) - Whether a statement of fact was made with respect to respondent? - Held, proved that statement of fact was made with respect to him - He has further proved that that statement was false and related to his personal character or conduct - There can be no doubt that a statement of this nature calling one candidate a thief or greatest-of-all thieves is reasonably calculated to prejudice prospects of this election - Appeal dismissed.
