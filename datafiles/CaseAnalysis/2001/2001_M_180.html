Laxman Dundappa Dhamanekar and Another v Management of Vishwa Bharata Seva Samithi and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20561; (2001) 8 SCC 378; (2002) SCC (L&S) 63; AIR 2001 SC 2836; AIR 2001 SCW 3786; 2001 (3) CLR 1018; 2001 (91) FLR 599; JT 2001 (8) SC 171; 2001(6) SCALE 522; 2001 (4) SLR 570; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Karnataka Private Educational Institutions (Discipline and Control) Act, 1975, ss. 3, 6 and 15 - Karnataka Private Educational Institutions (Discipline and Control) Rules, 1978 - Whether there was any requirement of law for management to obtain approval in regard to appointment of teachers in institution? - It appears that omission to obtain approval of head of department in case of a regular teacher under rules is deliberate - Held, rules do not contemplate for obtaining approval of head of department i.e. director of public instructions where appointment is to be made on basis of recommendation of selection committee constituted u/s. 6(2) - Breach of non statutory r. 16 would not render appointments of appellant invalid - Appeals allowed.
