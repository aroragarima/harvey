Chandigarh Housing Board v Devinder Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1209; (2007) 9 SCC 67; AIR 2007 SC 1723; JT 2007 (8) SC 567; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1332; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1366; 2007 (147) PLR 49; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 640; 2007(5) SCALE 145; 2007 (4) SCJ 353; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 1049

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: By-Laws, Residential House, Haryana Housing Board Act, 1971, U.P. Co-operative Societies Act, 1965, Chandigarh Housing Board (Allotment, Management and Sale of Tenements) Regulations, 1979

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Haryana Housing Board Act, 1971, s. 74 - Chandigarh Housing Board (Allotment, Management and Sale of Tenements) Regulations, 1979 - Whether by being members of AHWO, respondents did not satisfy eligibility criteria of appellant board under regn. 6? - Held, respondents satisfied condition - Keeping in view settled principles of interpretation of deed/ Statute, Court of opinion that condition of eligibility in question must be construed literally - If a plain meaning can be given effect to there is no reason why should not be applied - Acquisition of any property through any other source or through any other agency is not prohibited - Right to acquire property is a human right - A deed must be construed reasonably and in its entirety - If acquisition of any property through any agency other than specified therein is not prohibited, evidently, restriction clause in condition of eligibility will have no application - Same, must be construed strictly - Appeal dismissed.
