Akkode Jumayath Palli Paripalana Committee v P. V. Ibrahim Haji and others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 472; AIR 2013 SC 3530; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2013(6) ALL MR 423; 2013 (5) ALT(SC) 10; 2013 (5) AWC 4684; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 187; 2013 (3) KLT 521; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 10; 2013(9) SCALE 622; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 1033; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 85

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Jurisdiction - Wakf Tribunal - Appellant, a society, stated to be formed for the management and administration of wakf property including a Mosque, filed a suit for an injunction before the Court of Munsiff, which was transferred to the Court of Wakf Tribunal - Said suit was contested by the respondents on merits and ultimately it was decreed by the Wakf Tribunal and appellant was given a decree for a perpetual injunction restraining the respondents and their men from interfering in any manner in the administration, management and peaceful possession and enjoyment of the Mosque, madrassa run by it and all the assets attached to the Mosque - On appeal, HC set aside the judgment and decree passed by the Wakf Tribunal holding that a suit for injunction was not maintainable before a Wakf Tribunal - Review petition filed by the appellant was also dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the Wakf Tribunal had got jurisdiction to entertain a suit for injunction restraining the respondents from interfering with the administration, management and peaceful enjoyment of the Mosque and madrassa run by it and all the assets attached to the Mosque - Held, the dispute that arises for consideration in instant case was with regard to the management and peaceful enjoyment of the Mosque and madrassa and the assets which relate to Wakf - Nature of the relief clearly shows that the Wakf Tribunal had got jurisdiction to decide those disputes - No error in the Wakf Tribunal entertaining suit filed by the appellant and HC had committed an error in holding otherwise - Impugned order passed by the HC was set aside and the matter was remitted to the HC to consider the revision on merits - Appeals disposed of.
