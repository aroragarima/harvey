Dove Investments Private Limited and Others v Gujarat Industrial Inv. Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 127; (2006) 2 SCC 619; AIR 2006 SC 1454; 2006 (2) BC 1; [2006] 129 Comp Cas 929; 2006 (2) CTC 233; JT 2006 (2) SC 164; 2006(2) SCALE 234; 2006 (2) SCJ 697; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1087

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Instrument Of Transfer

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Refusal to register transfer of shares - Held, appellant were not prejudiced - In fact, they had no objection in registering shares - Only objection was with regard to value thereof - It is also not in dispute that they, in fact, registered 2,99,800 pledged shares, although they were also presented after a period of two months without any demur whatsoever - Appellants, therefore, must be held to have waived their right - Pledge of shares is not in dispute - Appeal dismissed.
