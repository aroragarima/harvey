H.M.M. Limited v Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3713; (1996) 11 SCC 332; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 378; 1996 (87) E.L.T. 593; JT 1996 (9) SC 444; 1996(7) SCALE 687; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 535; 1997 (5) Supreme 451

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Whether appellants are entitled to set off entire duty paid on inputs against excise paid on part of goods cleared from its manufacturing unit? - Where strict construction of an exemption will defeat purpose of exemption, it will be construed in a manner to serve purpose - Where rules set out by Govt. did not show necessity of direct correlation between inputs and manufactured goods, or between duty payable on inputs and amount set off against excise on manufactured, entire amount of duty payable on inputs could be taken credit of even though excise on entire amount of goods manufactured were not cleared - Held, appellants were entitled to set off entire input duty on malt barley against excise duty paid for part of stock of Horlicks cleared at Rajahmundry factory - Appeal allowed.
