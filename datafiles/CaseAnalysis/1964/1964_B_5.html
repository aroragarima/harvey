Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Bagyalakshmi and Company, Udamalpet

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 143; AIR 1965 SC 1708; [1965] 55 ITR 660; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 22

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Association - Contract & Commercial - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26 A - (A) When can an ITO reject registration of a firm? - Held, if registration of a firm is not genuine or valid and if application for registration has not complied with rules made under Act, it can be rejected - (B) What is nature of contract of partnership? - Further held, a contract of partnership has no concern with obligation of partners to others in respect of their shares of profit in partnership - It only regulates rights and liabilities of partners - This deed is used to specify shares of partners - Appeal dismissed.
