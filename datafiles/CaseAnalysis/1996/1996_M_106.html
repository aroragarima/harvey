Employees’ State Insurancecorporation v M/S. F. Fibre Bangalore Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 289; (1997) SCC (L&S) 190; (1997) 1 SCC 625; AIR 1997 SC 2441; 1997 (1) CLR 403; 1997 (75) FLR 524; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 52; 1997 (2) LLJ 739; 1997 (1) LLN 528; 1996(8) SCALE 652; 1997 (1) SLR 18; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 1(4), 1(5), 1(6), 39(1), 40, 40(2), 41, 42, 43, 44, 45, 45A, 45B, 45C, 70 and 75 - Whether the view taken by the Full Bench of the High Court is correct in law? - Full Bench of the HC held, where the order u/s. 45-A becomes final, there is no need for the Corporation to seek adjudication before the Insurance Court, in all other cases, the Corporation is required to go to the Insurance Court - When the employer denies the applicability of the provisions of the Act, it is for him to approach the Insurance Court and seek adjudication - Corporation to not to go to the Insurance Court for adjudication, every time there is a dispute - Full Bench judgment of the HC is unsustainable - Held, appeal allowed.
