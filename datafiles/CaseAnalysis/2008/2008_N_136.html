Deokar Exports Pvt. Ltd. v New India Assurance Company Ltd

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2116; (2008) 14 SCC 598; 2009 (1) ACC 93; AIR 2009 SC 2026; 2009 (1) ALD(SC) 15; 2009(3) ALL MR 443; 2009 (1) AWC 20; 2009 (1) CPJ(Sc) 6; JT 2008 (12) SC 26; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 941; 2008(14) SCALE 283; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fire Insurance Policy, Cover Note, Premium Paid, Re Insurance, Re Insurance Policy

Summary: Insurance - Practice & Procedure - Insurance Act, 1938, ss. 1, 64-VB - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Insurance - Retrospective effect - Legality - Appellant imported De-hydration Machine financed by MSFC and was insured by with respondent through MSFC, against risk of fire for period 12-9-1986 to 12-3-1988 - After expiry of policy, MSFC sent cheque on 25-8-1988 on behalf of appellant for renewal of policy - Insurer issued insurance policy dated 30.6.1989 extending insurance cover for period 26-8-1988 to 25-8-1989 - Insurer sent insurance policy to MSFC as required - MSFC did not raise any objection about period of cover when policy was received by it - On 10-2-1990, machine was damaged in fire accident - Appellant when claim was rejected by Commission filed civil suit claiming said amount - Appellant submitted that suit was in time, if period spent in prosecuting claim before Commission was excluded u/s. 14 of the 1963 Act - Trial Court upheld contention of appellant that insurance cover could only be prospective, that was for period of one year from date of issue of policy and that as insurance policy was issued on 30-6-1989, it should be deemed to have been issued to cover a period of one year commencing from that date; and that therefore, as on date of fire accident 10-2-1990, machine must be deemed to have been insured - However, Trial Court dismissed suit as barred by limitation - On appeal, HC held suit was not barred by limitation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether insurer was justified in issuing the policy showing the period of insurance cover as 26-8-1988 to 25-8-1989 - Held, s. 64-VB of the 1938 Act, provides that no risk could be assumed unless premium was received in advance - There was nothing illogical or arbitrary about insurance of policy specifying period of insurance cover as one year effective from date of receipt of premium, that is from 26-8-1988 to 25-8-1989 - If appellant wanted insurance cover prospectively it should have so specified in proposal and having failed to do so and having sought retrospective cover, appellant could not make grievance when insurance cover was issued retrospectively from date of receipt of the premium - Further, there was no protest or objection from MSFC that policy was issued for a wrong period nor did it return policy to insurer with request to make it prospective from date of policy - Appellant did not choose to examine policy or cross-check with MSFC about currency of insurance policy or about need to further renewal of policy - In fact, it would appear from record that MSFC had written on 31-7-1989 to appellant that insurance policy was due to expire in August, 1989 - It is, thus, clear that both appellant and MSFC were aware of fact that insurance cover under policy was for period 26-8-1988 to 25-8-1989 but neither of them objected to it - Nor was any premium paid for further renewal of policy beyond 25-8-1989 - Obviously, therefore, insurer could not be made liable for loss which occurred on account of a fire accident on 10-2-1990 - Appeal dismissed.
