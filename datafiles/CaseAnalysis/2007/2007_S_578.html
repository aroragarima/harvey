Naresh Giri v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1127; (2008) 1 SCC 791; 2008 (3) ACC 115; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1190; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3550; 2008 (2) CGLJ 519; 2007 (4) JCC 3285; JT 2007 (12) SC 433; 2007 (4) KLT 1115; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 1038; 2007(13) SCALE 7; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 987

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specific Offence

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, 325 and 323 - Applicability of s. 302 - Held, as rightly observed by the High Court charges can be altered at any stage subsequent to the framing of charges - But the case at hand is one where prima facie s. 302 IPC has no application - Charges stand altered to s. 304-A IPC along with ss. 279 and 337 IPC - Appeal allowed.
