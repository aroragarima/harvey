Â Accountant General, Bihar and Another v N. Bakshi

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 223; AIR 1962 SC 505; 1962 (2) SCJ 243; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 505

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Govt. of India Act 1919, ss. 96 B(2) and 96 B(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 314 - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3 - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 247(1) - Indian Independence Act, 1947, s. 10(2) - Superior Civil Services (Revision of Pay and Pension) Rules 1924 - All India Services (Overseas Pays, Passage and Leave Salary) Rules 1957, r. 3 - Whether Govt's decision to cancel passage benefits to members of Indian Civil Services was valid? - Held, right to passage benefits was statutory, and was under Indian Independence Act in nature of an allowance, or in any event privilege or facility paid in kind - It was expressly made part of remuneration earned by numbers of Indian Civil Service - Conditions of service as to remuneration having been guaranteed, right to this benefit remained guaranteed to those members of Indian Civil Service who were entitled to it before Constitution - Central Govt. in exercise of Rule making power was incompetent to destroy or cancel a constitutional guarantee - Appeal dismissed.
