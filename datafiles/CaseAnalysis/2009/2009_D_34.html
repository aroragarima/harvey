Secretary, Ministry of Defence and Others v Damodaran A. V. (Dead) Through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1032; (2009) 9 SCC 140; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 586; 2009(6) ALL MR 493; 2009 (5) ESC 724; JT 2009 (12) SC 188; 2009 (3) KLT 876; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1475; 2009(11) SCALE 648; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 416; 2009 (7) SLR 171

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Armed Forces, Active Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations for Army, 1961, regn.173 - Guide to Medical Officers (Military Pensions) 1980 - Disability pension - Entitlement to - Question of attributability or aggravation from military service - Respondent No. 1 was enrolled in Indian Army - Respondent No. 1 was declared non-suitable for continuance in Army as he was suffering from Schizophrenia - Respondents filed writ petition for grant of disability pension, which was allowed - Writ appeal filed thereagainst by dept. was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondents entitled to grant of disability pension under provisions as contained in regn.173 of Pension Regulations for Army, 1961 and such other enabling provisions? - A person is, under law, entitled to disability pension provided his disability is certified by appropriate medical authority as being attributable to or aggravated by or connected with military service - Opinion given by doctors or medical board to be given weightage and primacy in matter for ascertainment as to whether or not injuries/illness sustained was due to or was aggravated by military service which contributed to invalidation from military service - Held, in present case, opinion of Medical Board ruled out possibility of disease of respondent No. 1 being attributable to or aggravated by military service - Respondent cannot claim for payment of any disability pension - Single Judge and Division Bench of HC were not justified in allowing claim of respondents - Appeal allowed.
