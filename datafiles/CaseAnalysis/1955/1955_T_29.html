Â Thaivalappil Kunjuvaru Vareed v State of Travancore-Cochin

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 62; AIR 1956 SC 142; 1956 CRLJ 315; 1956 SCJ 166; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1022

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 72, 161, 238, 362 and 372(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 401, 402 and 402 A - Appellant convicted of murder - Sentenced to death - Filed mercy petitions to Raj-Pramukh of Travancore, Cochin - Order staying execution of sentence issued - (A) Whether continuance of prerogative of Maharaja of Cochin relating to execution of death sentences with reference to ex-State of Cochin would be inconsistent u/arts. 72, 161 and 238 of Constitution? - Held, in light of provisions contained in arts. 72, 161 and 238 continuance of prerogative of Maharaja of Cochin relating to execution of death sentences with reference to ex-State of Cochin would be inconsistent with new Constitution - Ss. 401, 402 and 402-A of CrPC, 1898 relating to commutation of sentences having been made specifically applicable by CrPC (Amendment) Act, 1951, to all part-B States, prerogative under old Cochin law must in any case be deemed to have been repealed or abrogated by competent legislative authority after coming into force of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
