Shri Malaprabha Coop. Sugar Factory Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3328; (1997) 10 SCC 216; JT 1997 (2) SC 361; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Batch, Producer Of Sugar

Summary: Administrative - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3(3-C) - Sugarcane (Control) Order, 1966, cl. 5 - Challenge to notification - Order for issuance of additional notification - Non compliance - Various appeal, special leave petitions, transfer petitions and transferred cases challenging fixation of price of levy sugar for relevant years by orders issued u/s. 3(3-C) of Act, was disposed of by SC and held that impugned orders/notifications were bad as price was not fixed in accordance with relevant provisions of law - However, it did not quashed notification as they would have led to nebulous situation during interregnum till re-fixation of price - Instead of quashing said notifications it directed UOI to amend notifications taking into account liability of producers of sugar under cl. 5A of the Order having regard to factors mentioned in s. 3(3-C) of the Act and Govt. was directed to issue notification - UOI filed Review Petition and that resulted into dismissal - Hence, instant Application - Held, Whether there was need of further order to Govt. to issue additional notification - order that was passed by SC earlier in Transferred Case was in respect of levy sugar price for later year - Therefore, it could not have any bearing on fixation of price of levy sugar for years relevant years - However, even if Govt. had omitted to take into consideration one unfavourable element, namely, mopping up of excess realization it could not justify its omission to take into consideration another relevant element which was favourable to producer of sugar - Hence, Govt. was directed to issue additional orders/notifications in terms of directions given by SC in earlier batch of cases - Applications allowed.
