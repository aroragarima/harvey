Nasiruddin and Ors v Sita Ram Agarwal

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 462; (2003) 2 SCC 577; AIR 2003 SC 1543; 2003 (2) AWC 1087; JT 2003 (2) SC 56; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 35; 2003 (1) RCJ 97; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 337; 2003(1) SCALE 658; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 634; 2003 (2) SLT 165; 2003 (2) Supreme 50

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963 - s.5 - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1950 - Default in deposit of rent - Two issues: (1) Interpretation of word 'shall' occurring in the Act; (2) Applicability of s.5, Limitation Act, 1963 in the matter of default in deposit of rent under the Act - Held: (1) Use of the expression 'shall or may' is not decisive for arriving at a finding as to whether statute is directory or mandatory. The intention of the legislature must be found out from the scheme of the Act. Held, since the present Act does not provide for extension of time or condonation of default, the Court does not have the power to either extend the period to deposit the rent or to condone the default in depositing the rent; (2) s.5, Limitation Act, 1963 is not applicable where there is a default in depositing the rent by the tenant under s.13(4) of the Act
