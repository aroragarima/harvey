State of Uttar Pradesh v Raja Yadvendra Datt Dube

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 360; AIR 1966 SC 727; 1966 ALJ 370; 1966 (1) ITJ 494; [1966] 60 ITR 221; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 161

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Agricultural income Tax Act, 1948, ss.15, 16, 25 - Assessment - Payment of agricultural income - Reassessment - Exercise of power - Sub-Divisional Officer assessed respondent u/s.16(3) of the Act to pay agricultural income-tax - Being of view that part of income of respondent had escaped assessment, Collector recomputed tax u/ss.25 r/w 16(4) of the Act - In Appeal by respondent against impugned order, Agricultural Income-tax Commissioner set aside orders of Collector and also of Sub-Divisional Officer and directed that fresh assessment of income be made - Respondent moved Board of Revision against impugned order - Board agreed with Commissioner - Board then referred question regarding its power to direct fresh assessment to HC which was answered in negative - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Board had authority to make order directing reassessment - Held, Sub-Divisional Officer had power to decide whether gross agricultural income of respondent did or did not exceed Rs.1 lakh - Notice u/s.15(3) of the Act was issued to respondent by Sub-Divisional Officer - Board of Revision did not expressly set aside notice issued by Sub-Divisional Officer but Board agreed with Commissioner that original order of assessment passed by Sub-Divisional Officer was without jurisdiction and directed that entire case be reopened by Collector and fresh assessment of income be made - Mere issue of notice u/s. 15(1) of the Act could not come to aid of Collector in commencing fresh assessment proceedings many years after date on which that notice was issued, on return which was not made to him - Collector issued notice u/s.25 of the Act for reassessing income which had escaped assessment and assessed income of respondent but proceeding of Collector was set aside as unauthorized and Collector was directed to start fresh proceeding for assessment - Thus, on account of diverse orders passed by authorities from time to time without correct appreciation of scheme of the Act, respondent escaped liability to pay tax which was due by him - Hence, commencement of fresh proceeding for assessment contrary to provisions of Statute could not be justified - Appeal dismissed.
