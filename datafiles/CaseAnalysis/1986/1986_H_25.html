Commissioner Of Income-Tax,West Bengal-Ii, Calcutta v Hindustan Housing And Land Developmenttrust Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 180; (1986) 3 SCC 641; (1986) SCC (Tax) 705; AIR 1986 SC 1805; 1986 (58) CTR 179; [1986] 161 ITR 524; JT 1986 SC 2; 1986(2) SCALE 342; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 390; 1986 TAXLR 1274; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 450; 1986 (2) UJ 493

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Defence of India Rules, r. 75A(1) - Defence of India Act, 1939, s.19 - Requisition of Land (Continuance of Powers) Act, 1951, s.5 - Acquisition -Compensation - Enhancing amount - Security bond - Assessee/respondent was a limited company dealing in land - It maintains its accounts on mercantile system - By an order under r. 75A(1) of Rules r/w s. 19 of 1939 Act certain plots of land and belonging to assessee, were requisitioned by Govt. - Subsequently land was acquired permanently in State Govt. u/s.5 of 1951 Act by a notice of acquisition published in Gazette - AO awarded Rs.24,97,249 as compensation payable to assessee was not satisfied with amount of compensation and preferred an appeal before Arbitrator made an award whereby he fixed amount of compensation at Rs.30,10,873 on account of the permanent acquisition of the land - Thus enhancing the original amount of compensation by Rs.5,13,624 on which he directed interest at 5% per annum from the date of acquisition to date of payment - Further recurring compensation at Rs.6272/10/4 should be paid to assessee from date of requisition till the date of acquisition - State Govt. appealed to HC and during pendency of appeal deposited Rs.7,36,691, which the assessee was permitted to withdraw on May 9, 1956 on furnishing security - On receipt of amount assessee credited it in its suspense account on same date - Whether Revenue can claim that Rs.7,24,914 payable to assessee as compensation can be have accrued to it as income during previous year ended relevant to assessment year 1956-57 - Held, award was made by Arbitrator enhancing amount of compensation payable to assessee, the entire amount was in dispute in appeal filed by State Govt. - Because assessee was not permitted to withdraw Rs.7,36,691 deposited by State Govt. without furnishing a security bond for refunding amount - There was no absolute right to receive amount at that stage - Appeal dismissed.
