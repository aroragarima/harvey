State Of Haryaka And Another v Haryana Civil Secretariat Personal Staff Association

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2269; (2002) 6 SCC 72; (2002) SCC (L&S) 822; AIR 2002 SC 2589; 2002 (3) AWC 2477; JT 2002 (5) SC 189; 2002 (3) LLN 840; 2002(5) SCALE 138; 2002 (4) SLR 754; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 118; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2081

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Parity of pay scale - Equal pay for equal work - Petitioner filed writ petition alleging that post of P.A. in Civil Secretariat of the State of Haryana is comparable with the post of P.A. in Central Secretariat and they discharge similar duties and responsibilities as those of their counterparts in the Central Secretariat but P.A. in Central Secretariat were granted the pay scale of Rs. 2000-3200 whereas the P.A.s in Civil Secretariat were placed in the scale of Rs. 1640-2900 only - Petitioner submitted that such action on the part of the State Government, was arbitrary, discriminatory and irrational - Respondents contended that though the State Government on principle accepted the report of the Fourth Central Pay Commission it did not entitle the members of the petitioner to claim post to post and scale to scale parity of pay - HC allowed writ petition filed by the petitioner and held that P.A.s of Civil Secretariat were entitled to the scale of Rs. 2000-3500 from 1.1.1986 - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld and petitioner was entitled to higher pay scale? - Held, claim of equal pay for equal work is not a fundamental right vested in any employee though it is a constitutional goal to be achieved by the Government - Fixation of pay and determination of parity in duties and responsibilities is a complex matter which is for the executive to discharge - While taking a decision in the matter several relevant factors, are to be considered keeping in view the prevailing financial position and capacity of the State Government to bear the additional liability of a revised scale of pay - Moreover, in the present case HC has not even made any attempt to compare the nature of duties and responsibilities of the two sections of employees - HC was in error in allowing the writ petition and directing the appellants to grant the scale of Rs. 2000-3500 to the P.A.s of the State Civil Secretariat w.e.f. 1-1-1986 - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
