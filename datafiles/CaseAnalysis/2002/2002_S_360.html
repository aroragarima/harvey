Sunil Kumar Rana v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1566; (2003) 2 SCC 628; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 439; 2002(9) SCALE 713; 2003 (1) SLT 190; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 568; 2003 (1) Supreme 330

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Haryana Municipal (Amendment) Act, 1994 - S. 13A(1)(c) - the proviso contains disqualification for membership of municipality who has more than two living children on or after the expiry of one year of the commencement of the Act - nomination paper of the appellant rejected on ground that at the time of filing his nomination, the respondent had four children and that of the said four children, two were born after the coming into force of the Act - The High Court held that the disqualification will operate after 5.4.1995, on the expiry of the period of one year from 5.4.94, the date of coming into force of the Amendment Act of 1994 - Held, the High Court could not be said to have erred in the construction adopted, which not only accords with the intention of the legislature but avoids uncertainty and friction as well as repugnance, which otherwise would result in accepting the stand of the appellant.
