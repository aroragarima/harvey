Liquidators of Pursa Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 158; AIR 1954 SC 253; [1954] 25 ITR 265; 1954 SCJ 294; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 767

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(vii) - Whether surplus arising out of sale of plant and machinery of sugar factory chargeable u/s. 10(2)(vii)? - Held, intention of assessee company was to discontinue its business and sale of machinery and plant was a step in process of winding up of its business - Machinery and plant which were sold had not at all been used for purpose of business carried on in accounting year and consequently second proviso to s. 10(2)(vii) could have no application to sale proceeds of such machinery and plant - Answered in favour of assessee.
