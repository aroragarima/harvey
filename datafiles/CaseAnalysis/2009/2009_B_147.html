Bhola @ Paras Ram v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 200; (2009) 11 SCC 460; AIR 2009 SC 1338; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1598; 2009 CRLJ 1885; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 166; 2009(3) SCALE 186; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against order of conviction High Court setting aside the acquittal - Held, even if there are some omissions, contradictions and discrepancies the entire evidence cannot be discarded - After exercising care and caution and sifting the evidence to separate the truth from untruth, exaggeration, embellishments and improvements the court can come to a conclusion as to whether the residual evidence is sufficient to convict the accused - High Court has analysed the evidence and has rightly come to the conclusion that the guilt of the accused persons has been established - Appeal dismissed.
