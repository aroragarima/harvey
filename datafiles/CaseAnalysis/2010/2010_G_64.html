Greater Mohali Area Development Authority & Anr. v Manju Jain & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 648; (2010) 9 SCC 157; AIR 2010 SC 3817; 2010(6) ALL MR 462; 2011 (1) AWC 292; JT 2010 (9) SC 17; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2817; 2010 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3407; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 1052; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 224; 2010(8) SCALE 275; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 134; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2673

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Cancellation of the allotted flat - Respondent no. 1 applied for allotment of a flat under a hire purchase scheme along with application money of Rs.20,000/- - A draw of lots was held and an M.I.G. flat was allocated to the respondent No.1 and respondent no. 1 was informed vide letter dt. 19.11.1997 about the said allocation - Appellant (Authority) issued the letter of allotment dt. 9.03.1999 in favour of respondent no. 1 - Respondent No.1 did not make any response to the said letter nor did she deposit any amount - Subsequently, appellant informed the respondent No.1 that the allotment made in her favour stood cancelled, as she did not deposit any amount in pursuance of the allotment letter dt. 9.03.1999 - Respondent no. 1 preferred an appeal challenging said order of cancellation submitting that greater injustice has been done to her by the authorities, as in spite of the order of allotment, the allotment had been cancelled without issuing any show cause notice to her or sending any information whatsoever - Said appeal was dismissed - Revision petition was also dismissed by Revisional Authority - HC allowed writ petition quashing all the orders passed by the authorities of the appellants - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, respondent No.1, did not make any response whatsoever after applying for allotment - No explanation could be furnished by respondent No.1 for why she kept quiet for 4 years after receiving the allocation letter and why she did not make any attempt to find out what had happened to her application - Respondent No.1 did not send her acceptance of the allotment; did not deposit the amount which became due in 1999 itself; and did not execute the required hire-purchase agreement with the appellant - Thus, it is solely for her that no concluded contract came into existence between the parties - In such a fact-situation, the respondent No.1 could not be handed over possession of the flat - The forfeiture of the earnest money is in terms of the statutory provisions - Impugned judgement of the HC set aside and the orders passed by the authorities under the statute are restored - Appeal allowed.
