Vikas Pratap Singh and another v State of Chhattisgarh and another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 431; (2013) 14 SCC 494; AIR 2013 SC 3414; 2013 (138) FLR 567; JT 2013 (9) SC 562; 2013(8) SCALE 713; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 1114; 2013 (5) SLR 141

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Examination Conduct Rules

Summary: Service - Merit list - Revised merit list - Cancellation of appointment - Legality - Appellants/candidates aggrieved by cancellation of the first merit list and the redrawal of the second revised merit list by respondent, whereby their appointments to the posts were cancelled - Instant appeals were directed against the judgment and order passed by HC, whereby and HC had dismissed the petitions filed by appellants and confirmed the revised merit list drawn after the selective re-evaluation of the answer scripts of all the candidates who had appeared in the Main Examination for the posts of Subedars, Platoon Commanders and Sub-Inspectors in respondent/State - Hence instant appeals - Whether the decision of respondent in directing re-evaluation of the answer scripts had caused any prejudice to appellants appointed qua the first merit list - Held, error was committed by respondent in the matter of evaluation of the answer scripts could not be attributed to appellants as they have neither been found to have committed any fraud or misrepresentation in being appointed qua the first merit list nor has the preparation of the erroneous model answer key or the specious result contributed to them - Had the contrary been the case, it would have justified their ouster upon re-evaluation and deprived them of any sympathy from HC irrespective of their length of service - Appellants have successfully undergone training and were efficiently serving respondent for more than 3 years and undoubtedly their termination would not only impinge upon the economic security of the appellants and their dependants but also adversely affect their careers - It would be highly unjust and grossly unfair to appellants who were innocent appointees of an erroneous evaluation of the answer scripts - However, their continuation in service should neither give any unfair advantage to appellants nor cause undue prejudice to the candidates selected qua the revised merit list - Respondent was directed to appoint appellants in the revised merit list placing them at the bottom of the said list - Candidates who have crossed the minimum statutory age for appointment should be accommodated with suitable age relaxation - Impugned order passed by HC modified - Appeals disposed of.
