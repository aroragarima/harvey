Canbank Financial Services Ltd. v The Custodian & Others

Supreme Court of India

03 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 725; (2004) 8 SCC 355; AIR 2004 SC 5123; [2004] 122 Comp Cas 263; 2004 (4) CompLJ 129; JT 2004 (7) SC 266; 2004(7) SCALE 495; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 60

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1975

Summary: Banking and Finance - Civil Procedure - Contract and Commercial - Corporate - Exchange Control and FDI - Fema - Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Indirect Tax - Land and Property - Practice and Procedure - Sales Tax - Securities Exchange Board of India - Trusts and Associations - Water - Special Courts (Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities) Act, 1992 - Appellant having paid a consideration of Rs 33 crores in relation to the CANCIGOS in question had a just right to possess the same to the exclusion of other respondents - Special Court could not have directed the Appellant to hand over the same to the Custodian - Held on facts, the transaction was not a benami transaction and cannot be labeled illegal, although it may appear unethical - Appeals allowed - Appeal allowed.
