Sayarabano @ Sultanabegum v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 92; (2007) 12 SCC 562; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 303; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 398; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 835; 2007 CRLJ 1458; JT 2007 (3) SC 106; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 968; 2007(2) SCALE 564; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 354

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, criminal cases are decided on facts and on evidence rather than on case law and precedents - There is ample evidence to show that even prior to the incident in question, the appellant used to beat the deceased and ill-treat her - Both the Courts were right in relying upon the second dying declaration of the deceased treating it as true disclosure of facts by the deceased - Appeal dismissed.
