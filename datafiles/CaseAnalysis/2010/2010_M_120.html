Maharashtra Land Development Corporation & Ors. v State Of Maharashtra & Anr

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 954; (2011) 15 SCC 616; 2010 (6) Bom.C.R. 755; JT 2010 (12) SC 305; 2010(11) SCALE 675

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maharashtra Private Forests (Acquisition) Act, 1975, Bombay Salsette Estate Abolition Act, 1951

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Maharashtra Private Forests (Acquisition) Act, 1975 - Bombay Salsette Estate Abolition Act, 1951, s. 4 - Land in question was originally owned by the Village Dahisar and after his demise, HC was appointed as Receiver for the land - Land in question, subsequently, was sold by the Receiver to a private Company, however, land was declared as 'forest' and vested in the 1st respondent/State u/s. 4 of the 1951 Act - In the meantime, 1975 Act came into force and land in question stood acquired and vested in the 1st respondent/State on the appointed day of the Act - Sub Divisional Officer (SDO) declared land in question as 'private forest' and issued a notice u/s. 5 of the Act to the Company for handing over the possession of entire land to the 1st respondent/State - Company challenged the SDO order before the Revenue Tribunal - Revenue Tribunal confirmed the SDO order - Since a part of disputed land was in possession of appellant/Corporation, 1st respondent sought handing over the possession from appellant also - Appellant challenged the SDO order before the HC - HC directed the SDO to conduct a fresh enquiry u/s. 6 of the 1975 Act - SDO, after the enquiry, held that disputed land was neither a 'forest' nor a 'private forest', hence land did not stand acquired and vested in the 1st respondent/State - 1st respondent challenged the SDO order before the Revenue Tribunal - Tribunal, in pursuance of a direction of the SC, considered the issue and held that land in question was not a 'private forest land' - 1st respondent challenged the Tribunal order before the HC - HC set aside the Tribunal order and allowed 1st respondent's writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Whether on the appointed day under the 1975 Act the appellant's land was a 'private forest' - Held, subsequent to proceedings initiated under the 1951 Act it was clearly established that the entire land in dispute was a 'forest' - Appellant contended that disputed land which was a forest before the appointed day might not be a 'forest' after the enactment of 1975 Act, however, proceedings under the 1951 Act were concluded closer to the appointed day of the 1975 Act, and the character of land as forest cannot be said to have changed over such a relatively short period of time - Revenue records of the past 50 years and the conveyance deed which was executed by the Receiver to the appellant clearly described the land as 'piece or parcel of forest land with structures' - Further, overwhelming documentary evidence and also contemporaneous evidence on record proved and established that the land in question even in recent times was considered as forest land and also retained its character as such - Hence, in light of the legislative scheme of the 1975 Act and provisions therein, the land in question is to be vested with the respondent/State as a 'private forest land' - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Administrative - Land & Property - Maharashtra Private Forests (Acquisition) Act, 1975 - Whether the action of vesting appellant's land under the provisions of the 1975 Act to 1st respondent/State amounted to be irrational or disproportionate - Held, 1st respondent's decision was guided by the provisions of the 1975 Act, which seek to conserve and protect private forests in the State that had been facing severe depletion and exploitation - Therefore, the 1975 Act, which provides for the vesting of private forests with the Government, does so in the general interests of the public in tune with principles of environmental protection and sustainable development - 1st respondent/State was only acting in accordance with the principles envisaged in the 1975 Act - Action of vesting cannot in any way said to be disproportionate or irrational solely because it divests the land of appellant/Corporation which was the part of the land in dispute - Appeal dismissed.
