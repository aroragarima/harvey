State of Andhra Pradesh v A.S. Peter

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1348; (2008) 2 SCC 383; AIR 2008 SC 1052; 2008 (1) JCC 235; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 131; 2007(14) SCALE 372; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 517

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1972

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 199, 200 - Respondent (Accused No. 1) carries on business in Red Sanders hard wood - A fire broke out resulting in destruction of red sanders hard wood - False declaration regarding the stock shown in the go down - Application for quashing of criminal proceedings on the ground that prior permission of Magistrate was not obtained for further investigation as also on the ground that the same was conducted entirely by a different investigating agency allowed - Appeal against - Held, law does not mandate taking of prior permission from the Magistrate for further investigation - Carrying out of a further investigation even after filing of the charge sheet is a statutory right of the police - CID is a part of the investigating authorities of the State - S. 36 of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1972 empowers a police officer, superior in rank to an officer in charge of a police station, to exercise the same powers throughout the local area to which they are appointed, as may be exercised by such officer within the limits of his station - Appeal allowed.
