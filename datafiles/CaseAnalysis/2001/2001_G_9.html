G. Mallikarjunappa and Another v Shamanur Shivashankarappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19884; (2001) 4 SCC 428; AIR 2001 SC 1829; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 414; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 643; 2001(3) SCALE 217; 2001 (3) SLT 587; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 98; 2001 (3) Supreme 276

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83(2), 86(1) and 117 - Whether Single Judge was justified in its finding that there was 'difference of identity' of appellant 2 in election petition and election agent form, rendering election petitions as not maintainable? - Defect in verification and affidavit is a curable defect - What other consequences, if any, may follow from a 'defective' affidavit, is required to be judged at trial of an election petition - Election petition challenging election of a returned candidate can be filed not only by other candidate/candidates at election but also by a voter - No dispute that name of appellant 2 as given in election petition tallies with his name as appearing in voters' list, thus, no discrepancy in name of appellant 2 in election petition, let alone any 'difference of identity' - Held, High Court erred in finding that since there was difference in name of appellant 2 as given in election petition and voters' list from one given in form for his appointment as an election agent, defect was 'fatal' - Impugned order set aside - Appeals allowed.
