Gaon Sabha and Another v Nathi and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, AR. Lakshmanan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1467; (2004) 12 SCC 555; 2004 (110) DLT 549; JT 2004 (4) SC 36; 2004 (3) RCR(Civil) 11; 2004(4) SCALE 3

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Land Reforms Act, 1954, ss. 7(2), 8, 154 and 185 - (A) Whether respondents are owners of land which was notified to be acquired, being co-sharers of Thok Pudhori, Patti Moharaja and therefore entitled to compensation? - Held, legal position is absolutely clear that a person can be either a Bhumidhar or an Asami of agricultural land in a village, he can also be an owner of property of type enumerated in s. 8 - Respondents neither claimed to be Bhumidhar nor an Asami of land and acquired land does not come within purview of s. 8, therefore, land stood vested with Gaon Sabha on date of commencement of Act and it was Gaon Sabha which was owner thereof and entitled to compensation - (B) Whether res judicata applicable in present case in view of decision in second appeal? - Further held, same would depend upon whether Civil Court had jurisdiction to entertain suit giving rise to second appeal - When land was acquired, rights of individual persons were not finally determined by Deputy Commr. as directed by HC and all reliefs claimed by respondent in present suit were within competent jurisdiction of Revenue Asst., Civil Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit, hence, decree passed in said suit including that of HC is wholly without jurisdiction - (C) Whether no notification u/s. 7(2) could be issued with regard to land in dispute as it was not a waste land? - Further held, definition of 'waste land' in Explanation (i) s. 7 is inclusive definition and only exception is 'uncultivated land included in holding of such proprietors'; expression 'such proprietor' here refers to such types of persons who are described in main part of sub-s. (1) of s. 7, since dispute land was not included in holding of respondents, therefore, a notification u/s. 7(2) could validly be issued - Appeals allowed.
