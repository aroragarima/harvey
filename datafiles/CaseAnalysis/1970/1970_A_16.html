Agarwal and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 241; (1970) 2 SCC 48; AIR 1970 SC 1343; [1970] 77 ITR 10; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Registration of firm - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 26A - Indian Companies Act, 1913, s. 4 - Whether, on facts and in circumstances of case, registration u/s. 26A of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 was rightly refused to appellant firm on ground that partnership in question violated provisions of s. 4 of Indian Companies Act, 1913? - Authorities under Act as well as High Court of Allahabad have answered that question in affirmative - Assessee challenges that conclusion - When a firm makes an application u/s. 26A for registration, ITO can reject same if he comes to conclusion that partnership is not genuine or instrument of partnership does not specify correctly individual shares of partners - But once he comes to conclusion that partnership is genuine and a valid one, he cannot refuse registration on ground that one of partners is a benamidar of another - Benamidar is a mere trustee of real owner and he has no beneficial interest in property or business of real owner - Held, in present case persons who are shown in partnership deed as partners, appeared to have joined same in their individual capacity - There is nothing in partnership deed to indicate that they have joined partnership as kartas of their respective families - It was not open to ITO to go behind deed and find out, for purposes of registration u/s. 26A, whether partners mentioned in deed have joined partnership in their own right or as representing others - Hence, partnership must be held to have been validly formed as law did not at relevant time prohibit anyone, otherwise competent to contract, from entering into a contract of partnership, even though beneficial interest in his share may vest in others - Application made for registration complies with requirements of s. 26A and rules framed thereunder - Therefore, ITO was bound to register partnership - Appeal allowed.
