Ghanshyam Dass Relhan v State Of Haryana & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 906; (2009) 14 SCC 506; AIR 2009 SC 2412; 2009 (2) CLR 1071; 2010 (124) FLR 103; JT 2009 (12) SC 86; 2009 (4) LLN 86; 2009(9) SCALE 626; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 603; 2009 (6) SLR 196

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Leave Encashment, BSF Rules, 1969, Haryana Civil Services (Safeguarding of National Security) Rules, 1971, West Bengal Services (Death- cum-Retirement Benefit) Rules, 1971, RBI Regulations, 1948, Special Leave Petition dismissed

Summary: (A) Service - Punjab Civil Services Rules, rr. 4.19 and 5.32(b) - Pension - Denial of - Challenged - Pension was denied to petitioner in view of R. 4.19 and Note 1 of R. No.5.32(b) of Punjab Civil Services Rules - Contention that case of petitioner covered u/r. 6(16)(2) - Whether can be accepted? - Held, no - Rule cannot be divided into two separate compartments - Second part of said Rule is a consequence of first part, which deals with retirement upon superannuation and not resignation, as in instant case - In order to be eligible for pension Govt. employee at the time of superannuation would have to complete qualifying service of not less than 33 years or more - Exception allows benefit of pro- rata pension to employees who had rendered 10 years service or more - Petitioner not having superannuated from govt. service, petitioner cannot come within the said category and his case would instead be governed by r. 5.32-A, which deals with resignation - Resignation to take up with proper permission another appointment, would have to be in a service, which would count towards pension in Government service - Subsequent appointment must also be in public service and in such a case resignation would not amount to resignation of public service - Further held, since petitioner did not completed qualifying service of 30 years and since service rendered by him with Bank would not be counted towards Govt. service, petitioner not entitled to benefit of pension u/r. 6.16(2) - Special Leave Petition dismissed.P /&gt;(B) Service - Punjab Civil Services R.s, rr. 4.19 and 5.32(b) - Expression 'resignation from public service' in r. 4.19(a) - Interpretation of - Held, it have to be read disjunctively from 'dismissal or removal from it' - Expression 'resignation from public service' will not be qualified by subsequent references relating to anti-national activities - Expression 'dismissal or removal from it' will be qualified by said expression which would in both cases entail forfeiture of past service and disqualification so far as payment of pension is concerned - Read disjunctively, resignation simpliciter from public service would entail forfeiture of past service and no pension is to be granted in aforesaid circumstances - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
