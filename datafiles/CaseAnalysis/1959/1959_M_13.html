Maharajadhiraj Sir Kameshwar Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

15 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1959 SC 1303; [1959] 37 ITR 388; 1960 MLJ 83; 1960 SCJ 145; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bihar Agricultural Income Tax Act,1938, ss. 25(1), 26 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - A/y 1944-45 - Assessment - Exempt - Re assessment - Challenged - Appellant/assessee filed return for A/y1944 -45 and was assessed by ITO and approved by Assistant Commissioner of Agricultural Income-tax - Agricultural Income-tax Officer assessed appellant to tax u/s. 26 of the 1938 Act which was challenged by appellant by appeal and was reversed by Commissioner holding that agricultural income from property was exempted - Respondents referred two questions to HC and were answered by HC in favour of respondents - Hence, instant appeal by assessee - Whether answers of HC were justified - Held, ITO had powers to serve notice within 8 years and assess or re-assess assesses income and profits if ITO had believed that assessee had concealed particulars of his income - HC was right in holding that ITO was competent to assess tax u/s. 26 of the 1938 Act which was omitted earlier even though that income was included in return and then thought by ITO that it was exempt - Thika profits were clearly agricultural income derived from lease hold properties and were taxable as rightly held by HC - Appeal dismissed.
