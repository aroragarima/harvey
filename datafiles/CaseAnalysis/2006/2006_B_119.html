Dresser Rand S.A v Messrs Bindal Agro Chem Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 577; (2006) 1 SCC 751; AIR 2006 SC 871; 2006 (126) DLT 437; JT 2006 (1) SC 262; 2006(1) SCALE 218; 2006 (2) SCJ 366; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 308

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961 - (A) Whether there is an arbitration agreement between Dr. and BINDAL? - Held, in absence of acceptance of standby letter of credit and performance guarantee by parties, no enforceable agreement could be said to have come into existence - (B) Whether there is an arbitration agreement between DR and KGK? - Further held, neither general conditions of purchase forming part of invitation of bid nor revision, nor letters of intent contain any arbitration agreement - There is also no other document or correspondence which can be read as containing a provision that can be interpreted as an agreement to resolve disputes by arbitration - (C) Whether BINDAL and KGK are estopped from contending that there is no arbitration agreement, in view of their counsel having stated in his telex D/- 11-04-1993, that his clients were in process of jointly appointing an arbitrator? - Further held, fact that at some point of time, BINDAL or KGK had stated that they would appoint an arbitrator will not come in way of their demonstrating that there is no arbitration agreement when matter comes up before court u/s. 3 of Foreign Awards Act 1961- Therefore, there is no question of either waiver or acquiescence - Appeals dismissed.
