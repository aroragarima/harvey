Rattan Singh and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 812; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 135; 2016 (114) ALR 717; 2016 (1) AWC 657; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 614; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 33; 2016 (1) CGLJ 356; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 65; 2016 (2) CTC 691; JT 2015 (12) SC 1; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 866; 2015(13) SCALE 594; 2015 (4) UPLBEC 3172; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 153

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 11 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(2) - Respondent/state govt. issued notification u/s.4 of 1894 Act, followed by declaration u/s. 6 and an award was finally passed - However, appellant/land owners failed to chose comply with procedure of making application to Collector for payment of compensation - Further, on commencement of 2013 Act, appellants invoked deemed lapsed of proceedings u/ s. 24(2) of 2013, Act - Hence, instant appeals and application.

Held, where a power is given to do a certain thing in a certain way, the thing must be done in that way or not at all. Other methods of performance are necessarily forbidden. S. 31 of 1894, Act mandates the Collector to make payment of compensation to such persons unless prevented by one of the contingencies contemplated in sub-s. (2), namely (i) the persons interested entitled to compensation do not consent to receive it, (ii) there is no person competent to alienate the land, and (iii) there is dispute as to the title to receive compensation or as to the apportionment of it. In case of above mentioned contingencies, the Collector is required to deposit the compensation in the Court to which reference u/s. 18 of 1894, Act may be made. Thus, compensation can be regarded as 'paid' if the compensation has literally been paid to the person interested, or after being offered to such person, it has been deposited in the Court. The deposit of the award in a Government Treasury would not amount to compensation being paid to the person interested. In instant case, compensation was neither paid to the appellants nor deposited in the appropriate Court. Since the award predated the commencement of the 2013 Act, by well over five years and compensation has not paid to the appellants, s. 24(2) of 2013 Act, comes into operation in favour of the Appellants. Whether possession was taken by the respondent need not be dilated upon nor need it detain us any further. Hence, the acquisition is deemed to have lapsed in the circumstances of the case and respondent may initiate fresh acquisition proceedings in accordance with the provisions of 2013 Act. Appeals allowed.
