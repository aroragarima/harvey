M/S. Govind Impex Private Limited And Others v Appropriate Authority, Income Tax Department

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1055; (2011) 1 SCC 529; JT 2010 (13) SC 748; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 123; 2010(13) SCALE 124; [2011] 196 TAXMAN 425

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Criminal - Income Tax Act, 1961, chapter XXC, ss. 269UA(f) and 269UC - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Application of s. 269UA - Appellants, owners of impugned property, let out the same at a monthly rental of Rs.2,50,000/- w.e.f. 1.06.1991 for a period of nine years renewable for a further period of nine years - Respondent issued show cause notice to the appellants alleging that since the lease is for a period of nine years extendable for a further period of nine years, it was a lease for a period of more than 12 years and hence the provision of Chapter XXC of the 1961 Act would be attracted and the lessor and the lessee were obliged to submit Form 37-1 within 15 days of the draft agreement - Appellants contended that the lessee had an option to renew the lease by giving three months' notice prior to the expiry of the lease and further a fresh lease deed was required to be executed and registered, hence the provision of Chapter XXC of the 1961 Act shall not be attracted - Respondent rejected said contention and held that the appellants guilty of not complying with the provisions of s. 269UC of 1961 Act - Accordingly, a complaint was filed and Magistrate took cognizance of the offence and issued process against the appellants - Appellants filed writ petition for quashing the said order - Single Judge dismissed said writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether impugned lease was for a period of more than 12 years and the provision of Chapter XXC of the 1961 Act would be attracted? - Held, clause 1 of said lease in no uncertain term provides for extension of period of lease for a further period of nine years and clause 12 of said lease thereof provides for renewal on fulfilment of certain terms and conditions - Therefore, when the document is constructed as a whole, it is apparent that it provides for the extension of the term - If that is taken into account the lease is for a period of not less than twelve years - Once it is held so the explanation to s. 269UA(f)(i) of 1961 Act is clearly attracted - The lessor and the lessee were obliged to submit Form 37-1 within 15 days of the draft agreement - Appeal dismissed.
