Suraj Mal v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 475; (1979) 4 SCC 725; (1980) SCC (Cr) 159; AIR 1979 SC 1408; 1979 CRLJ 1087; 1979 CRLR 717

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 354 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 9 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(1)(d) - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Credibility of two inconsistent statements by witness at one or two stages - (B) Bribery case - Whether recovery of money from accused, if sufficient evidence and proof - When substantive evidence is not reliable - Held, testimony of such witnesses becomes unreliable and unworthy of credence and in absence of special circumstances no conviction can be based on evidence of such witnesses - Held, not sufficient - Appeal allowed.
