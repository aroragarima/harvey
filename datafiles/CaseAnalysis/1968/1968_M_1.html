Third Income Tax Officer, Mangalore v M. Damodar Bhat

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 274; AIR 1969 SC 408; 1969 (1) ITJ 482; [1969] 71 ITR 806; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Garnishee Order

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 156, 220(6), 226(3), 297(2)(j) - Assessment - Issuing Notice - Statutory Discretion - Appellant assessed tax of respondent for assessment year 1960-61 amounting to Rs. 7,056 and sent notice - Respondent contended notice issued in an arbitrary manner - HC found statutory discretion was used in an arbitrary manner by appellant and notice issued - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was right in taking view that Income-tax Officer did not properly exercise statutory discretion in issuing impugned notice - Held, no - Respondent has not alleged before HC any specific particulars in support of his case that Income-tax Officer has exercised his discretion in an arbitary manner - Further, respondent had merely said the order of the Income-tax Officer made u/s. 220 of the Act was arbitrary and capricious - Hence, respondent unable to substantiate that impugned notice was in any way defective - Appeal allowed.
