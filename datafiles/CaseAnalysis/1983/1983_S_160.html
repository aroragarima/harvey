Bhagat Ram v State of Himachal Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 9; (1983) SCC (L&S) 342; (1983) 2 SCC 442; AIR 1983 SC 454; 1983 (47) FLR 95; 1983 LabIC 662; 1983 (2) LLJ 1; 1983 (1) LLN 810; 1983(1) SCALE 864; 1983 (2) SLJ 323; 1983 (1) SLR 626; 1983 UJ 297

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 15(5) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 311 and 311(2) - Service matter - Natural justice - (A) Determination of effect of not providing opportunity to be represented by officer of delinquent's choice in joint disciplinary enquiry - Whereas co-delinquent represented by Officer of his choice - (B) Determination of Jurisdiction of High Court to interfere under writ petition with perverse finding - (C) Determination of validity of quashing of penalty imposed by HC - Proceeding, held, would be vitiated - HC can interfere - SC may not permit fresh enquiry and may itself impose proper penalty - Appeal allowed.
