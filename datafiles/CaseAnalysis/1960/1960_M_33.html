Â Messrs Hatisingh Manufacturing Company Limited And Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1960 SC 923; 1960 (1) FLR 402; 1960 (2) LLJ 1; 1961 (1) SCJ 22; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 528

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, General Public

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Disputes (Amended) Act, 1953, s. 25FFF(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 20 - Closure of unit - Compensation - Entitlement - Petitioner business units were closed due to losses incurred by them - 1947 Act was amended by 1953 Act inserting s. 25FFF(1) - S. 25FFF(1)?? provided provision for payment of compensation for lay-off and retrenchment, and certain incidental provisions enunciating and regulating liability for payment of compensation - Being aggrieved, petitioners challenged validity of s. 25FFF(1) of the 1953 Act in instant petition - Whether impugned s. 25FFF(1) of the 1953, Act infringes freedom of petitioners guaranteed u/arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 20 of Constitution - Held, restrictions imposed by the impugned provision including the proviso are not unreasonable restrictions on exercise of fundamental right of employers to conduct and close their undertakings - Provision required employers to pay compensation to their employees though restrictive of fundamental freedom guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) of constitution was evidently in interest of general public, and was therefore saved u/art. 19(6) of Constitution from challenge that it infringed fundamental right of employers - Therefore, impugned s. 25FFF(1) of the 1953, Act including proviso and explanation thereto were not unconstitutional as infringing the freedom guaranteed u/art. 19 (1)(g) of Constitution or as infringing u/arts. 14 or 20 of Constitution -- Petitions dismissed.
