Shankar Gopinath Apte v Gangabai Hariharrao Patwardhan

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 181; (1976) 4 SCC 112; AIR 1976 SC 2506; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 411; 1976 UJ 823

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 107 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 221 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 53A and 105 - Easements Act, 1882, s. 60(b) - (A) Summary dismissal of appeal - In a matter wher construction of written instrument was involved - Rival interpretations having more than mere plausibility - Dismissal of appeal summarily without indicating reasons by Highj Court - Legality - (B) Suit for eviction from lands in possession of appellant under power of attorney - Improvements made by appellant to property - Recovery of value of improvement - Held, is illegal - Held, appellant liable to be ejected, further, could not claim to recover the value of improvements u/s. 221 of Contract Act as the same was incurred without reference whatsoever to the owner - Appeal Dismissed.
