Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Sony Cheriyan

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 207; (1999) 6 SCC 451; AIR 1999 SC 3252; 2000 (1) ALD(SC) 17; 1999 (37) ALR 10; 1999 (4) AWC 3337; 1999 (2) CCC 42; 1999 (4) CompLJ 39; 1999 (2) CPJ 13A; JT 1999 (6) SC 149; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 8; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 622

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 147 - Central Motor Vehicles Rules 1989, r. 137, Table 3 - Extent of liability of insurer - Terms of policy have to be strictly construed - Permit granted for carrying unhazardous goods by Motor Vehicles - Ethyl Ether specified as hazardous substance in Table 3 of r. 137 - Truck carried Ethyl solvent in contravention to terms of Insurance Policy caught fire - Held, insurer not liable to compensate loss suffered - Respondent was specifically prohibited from carrying a hazardous and flammable substance in his vehicle with permit - Truck of respondent carrying Ether solvent which is flammable substance caught fire - Claim of respondent is liable to be dismissed on grounds that it was not covered by terms of insurance policy.
