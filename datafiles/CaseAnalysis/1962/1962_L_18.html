T. P. Daver v Lodge Victoria No. 363, S. C. Belgaum

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 578; AIR 1963 SC 1144; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 100; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Votes

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Rule of expulsion - Respondent No. 2 file a complaint against the appellant, a member of the lodge, alleging that the appellant was guilty of Masonic offences - It was alleged therein that, as the appellant had committed Masonic offences, he should be tried by the Lodge for the charges levelled against him under Law 198 of the Constitution - Notice of the complaint was issued to the appellant - Thereafter, special meeting of the Lodge was held and each charge was put to vote and the members present unanimously held that every one of the charges levelled against the appellant was established - A resolution was passed excluding the appellant from the Lodge until the exclusion was confirmed by the District Grand Lodge under Law 199 of the Constitution - Appeal filed thereagainst to the District Grand Lodge was dismissed - Thereafter, appellant instituted a suit in the Court of the Civil Judge for a declaration that the resolution of the Lodge was illegal and void and that he continued to be a member of the Lodge despite the resolution, for an injunction - Suit was dismissed - Appeal filed by the appellant to the HC of Mysore was also dismissed - Present appeal - (A) Whether Lodge has jurisdiction to decide on the question whether a member committed a Masonic offence? - Held, Law 198 expressly confers a jurisdiction on a Daughter Lodge to try a member if he commits an offence - If the allegations against the appellant amounted to ""offences" Law 198 was immediately attracted - If that be so, neither Law 128 nor Law 56, which deal with the jurisdiction of a District Grand Lodge in respect of "Masonic complaints", can justify the jurisdiction expressly conferred on the Daughter Lodge - In the present case, all the parties concerned in the matter accepted the position that if the acts alleged to have been committed by appellant were established, he would have committed "offences" under the laws - Hence, Daughter Lodge had jurisdiction to entertain the complaint filed by the respondent No. 2 against the appellant and decide it on merits - (B) Whether Law 198 was strictly complied with? - It was contended that the notice of the special meeting issued to the members was not in strict compliance with the said Law - Held, as per the Law 198, in the special meeting convened for the purpose or at a regular meeting of the Lodge, the matter of the complaint shall be brought for consideration and judgment - In the present case, prescribed notice was given to the members and at the meeting all of them had considered the complaint as well as the answer lodged by the appellant - Therefore, the law in this regard was strictly complied with - (C) Whether trial against appellant was vitiated on the ground that he was not given the additional period after he filed his answer? - Held, appellant could have taken objection for his being given a shorter period of notice than prescribed under the Law for his appearance before the meeting of the Lodge - However, he did not do so - Hence, appellant by his conduct, waived his right under the said Law - Having waived it, he now precluded from relying upon the said defect - Hence, it was not open to the appellant to rely upon the said defect for invalidating the proceeding - (D) Jurisdiction of a civil court - Whether civil courts have jurisdiction to decide on the merits of a decision given by a private association like a Lodge? - Held, civil courts have no jurisdiction to decide on the merits of a decision given by a private association like a Lodge - Further, jurisdiction of a civil court is rather limited; it cannot sit as a court of appeal from decisions of such a body; it can set aside the order of such a body, if the said body acts without jurisdiction or does not act in good faith or acts in violation of the principles of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
