Banaras Hindu University and Another v Dr. N. N. Pandita

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 799; (1994) Supp3 SCC 303; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1435; 1994 (28) ATC 389; 1994 (69) FLR 862; JT 1994 (5) SC 537; 1994(3) SCALE 1001; 1994 (5) SLR 247; 1994 (2) UJ 634; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1886

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Education - Service - University Grants Commission - Merit promotion - University Grants Commission introduced a Merit Promotion Scheme for University Teachers in order to provide reasonable opportunities for career advancement of teachers - Respondent made application for same - Recommendation of Selection Committee, in respect of respondent was considered by Executive Council, in its meeting dated 3-2-1984/4-2-1984 and he was appointed as Professor - Respondent filed representation stating that he should be promoted w.e.f. 1910-1983 instead of 6-2-1984 - Representation was rejected - High Court by impugned order directed appellant to give seniority to respondent w.e.f. 30-09-1983/1-10-1983 - Present appeal - Whether after receipt of communication dated 11-11-1983, from UGC, respondent should have been appointed under Scheme w.e.f. 30-09-1983? - Held, communication dated 1983 issued by UGC had to be considered by Executive Council, which was actually considered on 3/4-2-1984 and thereafter respondent was promoted under scheme - HC was not required to interfere with said order - Impugned order of HC quashed - Appeal allowed.
