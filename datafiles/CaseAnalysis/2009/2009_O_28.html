Baby Radhika Gupta and Others v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1577; (2009) 17 SCC 627; 2010 ACJ 758; 2010 (78) ALR 235; JT 2009 (14) SC 457; 2010 (159) PLR 125; 2010 (157) PLR 625; 2009(14) SCALE 182

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Accident - Award of Compensation - Reduction - Sustainability - Tribunal awarded compensation of Rs. 45, 000, 00/- to dependents of deceased, who died in motor accident - Aggrieved respondent-Insurance Company filed appeal before HC - HC reduced compensation to Rs.5,82,132/- - Appellants-dependents of deceased had filed appeal by way of special leave - Appellants contended that HC had erred in applying multiplier of 14, when, according to second schedule to the Act, correct multiplier should be 17, because at time of death, deceased was 32 years' of age - Hence, instant appeal - Whether claim of appellants that multiplier of 17 should be applied to calculate compensation was valid - Held, out of total income, HC deducted two-third of amount as personal expenditure of deceased, however, it should be 1/3rd because of settled legal position of number of cases - Deducting 1/3rd as personal expenditure from annual income of Rs.1,18,314/- of deceased, then it came to Rs.39,438/- and remaining amount would be Rs.78,876/- and if it was multiplied by 17, then amount would work-out to be Rs.13,40,892/- - Hence, respondent was directed to pay balance amount to appellants within four weeks from today with interest at rate of nine per cent per annum - Appeal disposed of.
