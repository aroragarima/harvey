Purushottam Umedbhai and Company v Messrs Manilal and Sons

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 454; AIR 1961 SC 325; 1961 (1) KerLR 164; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 38; 1961 (1) SCJ 283; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Amendment of plaint - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10, O. 6 r. 17, O. 30, O. 23 r. 1 - Breach of contract - Suit for compensation - Petition for amendment of plaint was filed by plaintiff - Amendment sought was to effect that name of firm Manilal and Sons as plaintiff be struck off and in its place names of office person who were partner may be entered - An objection would be taken on behalf of defendant that suit, as framed, was null and void and not maintainable - Single Judge held that original plaint was no plaint in law and therefore was a mere nullity of a process - Proper course, when there is such a mistake, is not to amend, disregarding conditions of O. 1, r. 10 of CPC, 1908, but to seek Court's permission to withdraw suit with liberty to file a fresh suit under O. 23 r. 1 CPC, 1908 on ground of formal defect and which should be done before limitation - But DB allowed amendment - Whether such amendment of plaint is allowable? - Held, O. 30 CPC, 1908 permits two or more persons carrying on business of firm to sue or be sued in name of firm but firm must be carrying on business in India - A suit filed in name of a firm is still a suit by all partners of firm unless it is proved that all partners had not authorized suit - Even if provisions of O. 1, r. 10 and O. 6, r. 17 did not strictly apply amendment could be permitted u/s. 153 of CPC, 1908 because it was not a case of either adding parties or substituting parties - O. 30, being enabling provisions, do not prevent partners of a firm from suing or being sued in their individual names - Since, a firm is not a legal entity privilege of suing in name of a firm is permissible only to those persons who, as partners, are doing business in India - O. 1, r. 10(1) has no application to a case of this kind because suit has not been instituted in name of a wrong person, nor is it a case of there being a doubt whether it has been instituted in name of right plaintiff - Provisions of O. I, r. 10(2) also do not apply because it is not a case of any party having been improperly joined whose presence before court is necessary in order to enable court effectually and completely to adjudicate upon settle all questions involved in suit - Suit has been from its very inception a suit by partners of firm and no question of adding or substituting any person arises, partners collectively being described as a firm with a particular name - One of partners M was Manager of firm Manilal and Sons - That partner had executed a Power of Attorney in favour of four persons including one D and by this Power he authorized any one of these persons to sue for recovery of moneys due to firm - In these circumstances, it cannot be said that at time plaint was filed it was defective because Power of Attorney in favour of D was not a Power of Attorney on behalf of firm and its partners - As High Court has pointed out, there is on record now Powers of Attorney on behalf of all partners of firm - It seems to that DB of HC took a correct view in holding that plaint was not a nullity - It was a case of a suit instituted by all partners of a firm - Appeal dismissed.
