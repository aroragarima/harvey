V. K. Surendra v V. K. Thimmaiah and others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 230; (2013) 10 SCC 211; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 1114; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 166; 2013 (98) ALR 300; 2013 (3) ALT 59; 2013 (3) AWC 3159; 2013 (3) CLT(SC) 76; JT 2013 (8) SC 424; 2014 (2) MahLJ 1; 2014 (1) MPLJ 539; 2013 (171) PLR 702; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 64; 2013 (120) RD 533; 2013(5) SCALE 645; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 386

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Self-acquired properties

Summary: Civil Procedure - Family & Personal - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Suit for partition - Self acquired property - Share - Entitled to - Appeal was preferred by defendant No. 3 against judgment passed by the HC - By the impugned judgment and decree the HC allowed the appeal, set aside the judgment and decree of Trial Court and decreed the suit declaring that defendant Nos. 1,2,3 and 4 were entitled to 11/50th share each and the plaintiff, defendant Nos.5,6,7,8 and 9 were entitled to 1/50th share each in the suit schedule properties - Hence instant appeal - Whether the suit schedule properties were joint family properties or self-acquired properties of late. K -

Held, appellant examined himself as DW4 but he failed to produce either documentary or oral evidence to show the lands at items Nos.2, 3 and 5 were self-acquired properties - In absence of any division in the family of K and his sons, SC hold that the family of K continued to be the joint family - If a co-parcener of a joint family claims that properties were his self-acquired properties, the burden was on him to prove that the same were the self-acquired properties - In that background HC has rightly held that K had no right to change the character of the joint family properties by transferring the same either under a Will or a gift to any party without the consent of the other co-parceners - It was clear that even defendant No. 3/DW4 admitted that the lands sold under Ex.D5 were the joint family properties and if lands were not sold he and his brothers would have been entitled for a share - SC hold that those suit schedule properties were joint family properties of K along with 4 sons and the co-parceners have equal shares in the properties - 4 sons and K were entitled to 1/5th share of the total properties - So far as 1/5th share of K was concerned, apart from 4 sons, i.e., defendant Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4, the daughters of K were entitled to 1/50th share each whereas the sons, i.e., defendant Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4 were entitled to 11/50th share each, inclusive of their respective shares - Defendant No. 9 who was the son of the first daughter having succeeded the estate of his mother, a co-parcener was also entitled to 1/50th share - In that background no interference with the impugned judgment was called for - Appeal dismissed.
