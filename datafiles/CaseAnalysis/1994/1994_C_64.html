Charanjit Kaur v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1269; (1994) 2 SCC 1; 1994 ACJ 499; AIR 1994 SC 1491; 1994 (1) CLR 434; JT 1994 (1) SC 131; 1994 (1) LLJ 987; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 200; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 907

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Whether authorities could refuse grant of inquiry report in regard to death of petitioners husband? - Difficult to believe that when he was being airlifted at his own request, he had refused to board plane and instead had left for his said alleged private business - All that thereafter was found was his charred body with 98% burns - No inquiry whatsoever seems to have been made and if made, its results being kept a secret - Case has been handled with culpable negligence and cynical indifference - This is a matter which requires investigation at highest level - Held, this matter be personally looked into by Chief of Army Staff - Petitioner is, therefore, entitled to suitable compensation as well as Special Family Pension and Children allowance according to relevant rules - Rules made absolute.
