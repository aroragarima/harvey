Ramnagar Cane and Sugar Company Limited v Jatin Chakravorty and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1960 SC 1012; 1960 CRLJ 1384; 1960 (1) FLR 411; 1961 (1) LLJ 244; 1960 SCJ 1246; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 22(1) (d), 24(1)(1) - West Bengal Security Act, 1950, ss. 11, 2(9)(e) - Pendency of conciliation proceedings - Strike - Legality - During pendency of conciliation proceedings workers union commenced strike - Appellant filed criminal complaint u/s. 11 of the 1950 Act, against eleven respondents/workmen - Trial Court upheld respondent's plea and acquitted - Aggrieved appellant challenged said order before HC which upheld order of Trial court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC had misconstrued provisions u/s. 22(1)(d) of the Act by holding that strike was not illegal - Held, if conciliation proceeding was pending between one union and employer and it relates to matters concerning all employees of employer, pendency of said conciliation proceeding would be a bar against all employees of employer employed in public utility service to go on strike during pendency of said proceeding u/s. 22 (1)(d) of the Act - Therefore, during pendency of conciliation proceedings between appellant and employees union attracts the, provisions u/s. 22(1)(d) of the Act to strike in question and made said strike illegal u/s. 24(1)(1) of the Act, and if strike was illegal it followed that respondents had took part in subversive activity as defined u/s. 2(9)(e) of the 1950, Act and had committed offence punishable u/s. 11 of the 1950, Act - Hence, order of acquittal passed by HC in favour of respondents was set aside and convicted them for said offence - Appeal allowed.
