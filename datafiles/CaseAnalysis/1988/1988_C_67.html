Coffee Board, Karnataka, Bangalore v Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, Karnataka and

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 673; (1988) 3 SCC 263; (1988) SCC (Tax) 308; AIR 1988 SC 1487; 1988 (2) CompLJ 161; JT 1988 (2) SC 448; 1988(1) SCALE 1055; 1988 (70) STC 162; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Coffee Act, 1942, Madras Plantations Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1955

Summary: Sales Tax - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4(1) - Constitution, art. 300A - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 5 - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 2(t), 5 and 6 - Coffee Act, 1942, ss. 11, 12, 17, 25 and 34 - Export of Coffee - Compulsory delivery - By Growers to Coffee Board under Coffee Act - Not compulsory acquisition but sale - Element of consensuality present in the transaction - (A) Whether purchase tax is leviable (B) Agreement at time when transaction of sale takes place is necessary - Whether exemption from tax u/s. 5 of 1956 Act is available - Purchase tax held, is leviable - Exemption from tax - Order accordingly.
