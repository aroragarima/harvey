Harjeet Singh and another v Amrik Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1554; (2005) 12 SCC 270

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Finding of fact

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Suit for specific performance - Finding of fact - Jurisdiction - Appellants (plaintiffs) filed a suit against respondents for specific performance of an agreement to sell land in question - Trial Court dismissed the suit - On appeal, First Appellate Court allowed the appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC Court should have entered into a finding of fact arrived at by First Appellate Court u/s. 100 of CPC.

Held, questions framed by HC did not raise any question of law at all. It was not a case of finding of perversity, but a case of appreciation of the evidence by the HC. This is patent from the HC holding that the appellants had not 'fully' proved that they were not performing their part of the contract. No reason was given by the HC for discarding the reasoning given by the First Appellate Court. The HC has clearly overstepped its jurisdiction prescribed u/s. 100 of CPC. Impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
