Mahesh Chandra Verma and others v State Of Jharkhand & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 299; (2012) 11 SCC 656; AIR 2013 SC 862; 2012 (135) FLR 1049; JT 2012 (9) SC 334; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 720; 2012(9) SCALE 113; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 6; 2012 (6) SLR 705

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Practicing, U.P. Subordinate Civil Courts Inferior Establishment Rules, 1955, Fast Track Court Scheme

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Service - Jharkhand Superior Judicial Service (Recruitment, Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2001, rr. 4(a), 9, 25 -Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Ad-hoc appointments - Exhaust of Select list - Appointments - Challenged - HC issued an advertisement dt.23-5-2001 inviting applications to fill-up vacancies in the post of Additional District Judges (ADJs) - 17 candidates were appointed as ADJs in regular cadre of Higher Judicial Services - Selection process pursuant to said advertisement should have come to an end, but subsequent appointments of 10 & 15 ADJs in Fast Tract Courts (FTCs) in year 2002 by way of direct recruitment from amongst the members of Bar were in violation of rules of fairness, equality and fair play as enshrined in arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Aggrieved respondents (Judicial Officers) who were members of Subordinate Judiciary of State, challenged said appointment of appellants (ADJs) to posts of ADJs, FTC, before HC - DB by impugned judgment allowed writ petition - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants (ADJs, FTCs) contended that they were appointed u/r. 4(a) of the Rules and that appellants passed orientation course, which 17 candidates have undergone the same course were working as District Judges in the cadre and that appellants were appointed on anticipated and contemplated vacancies and that appellants were in select list of examination process conducted in pursuance to advertisement dt.23-5-2001 - Further contended that appellants were included in select list of State Superior Judicial Service for appointment as ADJs, but appointment should, initially be on ad hoc basis in regular scale of ADJs and that contesting respondents who were from Subordinate Services could not participate in the process of direct recruitment from Bar - Whether impugned order of DB was justified - Held, State Govt. constituted 89 FTCs of Additional District & Sessions Judges for 5 years with immediate effect and on 14-12-2001, 20 promotee officers whose names were recommended by HC were appointed by the State as FTC Judges on ex cadre temporary posts - On 15-12-2001, 17 candidates whose names were found at S.Nos. 1 to 17 of the merit list were appointed as ADJs in regular cadre of Higher Judicial Services - Appointments of those persons could not be faulted, because it was stated on oath that there were 17 clear cut vacancies - When letter dt.14-6-2001 was received by HC from the Law and Judiciary Department of State giving state-wise break-up showing 89 FTCs against the State, only 70 officers from sub-judge cadre were available and, as such, FTCs could not have filled up by ad hoc promotion from service cadre - It was the case of HC that since State was lagging behind in so far as creation of FTCs was concerned and there was persistent request from Central Govt. to establish FTCs as soon as possible, it was felt that if fresh examination was conducted for appointments to be made to FTCs from the Bar, much time would be consumed and, therefore, it was decided to appoint officers from the merit list who had appeared in examination for recruitment of regular ADJs - Full Court Resolutions of HC and the correspondence of Chief Justice with Law Ministry indicated that HC was ill-equipped to put FTCs Scheme in action in State because of several difficulties, prominent amongst them being cadre bifurcation not having been completed and unavailability of officers from service cadre - HC was bona fide trying to comply with the Central Law Ministry's desire and in that it overstepped its limits - Appointments made on 2-2-2002 and 12-8-2002 were irregular, made in ignorance of settled principles underlying service law, in an anxiety to comply with desire expressed by Law Ministry and to set up FTCs to deal with the problem of pendency of cases - Once it was held that appointments of appellants were ad hoc, ex-cadre and not made as per 2001 Rules and that they were made in a scheme of temporary duration, wait list prepared while selecting regular ADJs could not be used to appoint FTC Judges - In instant case, select list got exhausted when 17 ADJs were appointed and persons from select list prepared for recruitment to the post of regular ADJs could not be appointed as FTC Judges - Appointments of appellants, ADJs FTCs, were made on ad hoc ex-cadre basis in a scheme of temporary duration and their appointments were irregular - Appellants were not appointed on any permanent post - Notification of their appointment dt.12-8-2002 clearly states they were appointed against temporary and ex-cadre posts on ad hoc basis - They were not appointed under the Rules - Appellant's case clearly covered by Brij Mohan Lal v. Union of India & Ors, 2012 Indlaw SC 319 - State was directed to comply with directions to appoint the appellants in regular cade in Higher Judicial Services in the State strictly in the manner laid down in Brij Mohan Lal v. Union of India & Ors , 2012 Indlaw SC 319, referred to - Appeals disposed of.
