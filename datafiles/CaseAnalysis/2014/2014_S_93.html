Singh Ram (D) Through Lrs. v Sheo Ram and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Usufructuary Mortgage, Right Of Redemption

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 61 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 62 - Mortgaged property - Right to Redemption - Predecessor of respondents mortgaged suit property on 11-8-1903 to predecessor of appellants for sum of Rs.80/- - Appellant-plaintiffs filed suit for declaration that suit land had not been redeemed for period of more than 60 years, defendants lost all rights, title and interest therein and appellants became owners by prescription - Trial Court held that limitation started running from date when mortgagee demanded money and mortgagor refused the same - Appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court affirmed the order passed by Trial Court - Appeal before HC, matter was directed to be placed before Full Bench - Full Bench held that in case of usufructuary mortgage, limitation for recovery of possession u/art. 61 of the 1963 of the Act started on payment of mortgage money as provided u/s. 62 of the 1882 Act and not from date of mortgage - Hence instant appeal - Whether right to seek redemption would arise on date of mortgage itself in case of usufructuary mortgage when no time limit was fixed to seek redemption -

Held, possession remained with mortgagor and it was not case of usufructuary mortgage - Court need not to multiply reference to other judgments - Reference to judgments clearly spell out the reasons for conflicting views - In cases where distinction in usufructuary mortgagor's right u/s. 62 of the 1882 Act had been noted, right to redeem had been held to continue till mortgage money was paid for which there was no time limit while in other cases right to redeem had been held to accrue on date of mortgage resulting in extinguishment of right of redemption after 30 years - Thus, SC held that special right of usufructuary mortgagor u/s. 62 of the 1882 Act to recover possession commences in manner specified therein, when mortgage money was paid out of rents and profits or partly out of rents and profits and partly by payment or deposit by mortgagor - Until then, limitation did not start for purposes of art. 61 of the Schedule to the 1963 Act - Usufructuary mortgagee was not entitled to file suit for declaration that he had became owner merely on expiry of 30 years from date of mortgage - Hence, view taken by HC would stand affirmed - Appeals allowed.
