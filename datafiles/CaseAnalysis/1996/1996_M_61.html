M. Govindaraju. v V. K. Munisami Gounder [Dead] And Others.

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2274; (1996) 5 SCC 467; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 548; AIR 1997 SC 10; 1996 (3) CCC 322; 1997 (1) HLR 445; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 629; 1996 (6) Supreme 489; 1996 (2) UJ 551

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Legitimacy of child - Woman deserting her husband and living with another man - When begotten no valid marriage between parents - Appellant has been denied his share in joint Hindu family property owned by his father on sole ground that when begotten no valid marriage subsisted between his parents - Challenged - As per Hindu law if a non-Dwija woman is turned out of the house by her husband, or she willfully abandons him and is not pursued to be brought back as wife, a divorce in fact takes place, sometimes regulated by custom, and then each spouse is entitled to rearrange his/her life in marriage with other marrying partners - Hence, there was a valid divorce in the present case - Held, paternity of appellant having not been denied, he was treated as a son of his father - Trial Court was right in giving appellant status as a son of his father - Fact was corroborative of valid acknowledgment of paternity and legitimacy - If people, especially relatives, had treated and acknowledged appellant as legitimate son of his father by forging a bond of matrimony of sort aforementioned, it is a strong piece of evidence to hold that the appellant was a legitimate offspring of his father - High Court clearly fell in error in illegitimating appellant - Appeal allowed.
