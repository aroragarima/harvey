Debotosh Pal Choudhury v Punjab National Bank and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1815; (2002) 8 SCC 68; AIR 2002 SC 3276; 2002 (95) FLR 338; JT 2002 (7) SC 187; 2002 LabIC 3264; 2002 (3) LLJ 1082; 2002(6) SCALE 469C; 2003 (1) SLJ 93; 2002 (6) SLR 222; 2002 (5) SLT 382; 2002 (6) Supreme 398

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab National Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations

Summary: Service - Punjab National Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1977, regn. 6(18) - Petitioner was dismissed from service by an order made on basis of an enquiry conducted by an Enquiry Officer - Challenged - Whether petitioner had reasonable opportunity? - Held, regn. 6(5) of regulations requires disciplinary authority shall, where it is not inquiring authority, forward to Inquiry Authority certain documents - Fulfillment of some of requirements of this regulation is purely procedural in character - Petitioner had been given documents for inspection - Having participated fully in enquiry and on sitting having confirmed that he did not intend to say anything further, it would not be appropriate for petitioner to contend that he had not been given any reasonable opportunity to put forth his case - Further held, petitioner was not provided with a copy of enquiry report by disciplinary authority before imposition of punishment of dismissal, but that circumstance has no bearing on dismissal of petitioner in view of decisions of Supreme Court in Managing Director, ECIL, Hyderabad & Ors. v. B. Karunakar & Ors. 1993 Indlaw SC 430 held that no order of punishment before date of decision in Ramzan Khan's case 1990 Indlaw SC 577 would be challengeable on ground that there is failure to furnish inquiry report before imposing punishment by disciplinary authority - In present case, punishment had been imposed upon petitioner by disciplinary authority long before decision of SC in Ramzan Khan's case - Petition dismissed.
