State, Represented by Inspector of Police, Central Crime Branch v R. Vasanthi Stanley and another

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 857; (2016) 1 SCC 376; AIR 2015 SC 3691; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4960; 2015 (4) CalLT(SC) 20; 2015 CRLJ 4767; JT 2015 (8) SC 486; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 772

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: CBI, Assistant Commissioner, Credit, Criminal Justice, Justification, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 406, 420, 467, 468 - Alleged Forgery - Criminal Proceeding quashed - Justification - Respondent no. 1 i.e. accused no.2, along with her husband submitted application for home loan to three banks depositing sale deed in which wife i.e. respondent no.1 was also guarantor - Further, all three banks founds documents forged and complaint was filed by accused persons - FIR was lodged by complainant against accused person u/ss. 120, 406, 467, 368 of IPC - HC held that 'No due certificate' had been issued by respective banking institutions and further settlements had been arrived at under scheme, hence, continuance of prosecution would be exercise in futility, hence, criminal proceedings was quashed to prevent abuse of process of law - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, it was inapposite on part of HC to quash criminal proceedings as allegation by prosecution pertain to availing of loan by depositing documents which were forged. Further, plea of respondent accused that she was unaware of any transaction does not remotely appeal to common sense and, in any case, such plea cannot be entertained for purpose of quashing criminal cases. Further, SC held that assertions as regards ignorance are mere pretence and sans substance given facts. Lack of awareness, knowledge or intent is neither to be considered nor accepted in economic offences. Further, submission presented on gender is not acceptable for offence under criminal law is offence and it does not depend upon gender of accused. SC held that person getting involved in financial scam or forgery of documents, cannot claim discharge or acquittal on ground of her gender as that is neither constitutionally nor statutorily valid argument. Hence, impugned order passed by HC in favor of accused is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Court is not justified to quash criminal proceedings against accused person when there is evidence on record against accused person.
