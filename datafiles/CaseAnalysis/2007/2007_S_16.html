P. Chandrasekharan & Others v S. Kanakarajan & Others

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 427; (2007) 5 SCC 669; AIR 2007 SC 2306; 2007 (4) ALD(SC) 70; 2007(3) ALL MR 826; 2007 (68) ALR 304; 2007 (3) AWC 2646; 2007 (5) Bom.C.R. 419; 2007 (104) CutLT 585; JT 2007 (6) SC 347; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 842; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 115; 2007 (103) RD 243; 2007(6) SCALE 342; 2007 (7) SCJ 449; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 967

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Issue of 'substantial question of law' - Appellants claimed title of property by reason of sale deed - Contention of the appellant that only because there existed a dispute in regard to the description of the property in a document, the same by itself would not give rise to a substantial question of law - Held, a substantial question of law is different from a question of law, interpretation of a document which goes to the root of the title of a party to the lis would indisputably give rise to a question of law , the courts below misread and misinterpreted a document of title read with other documents and the plan for the identification of the suit lands whereupon the plaintiffs themselves relied upon, a substantial question of law arose for determination of the High Court in between the parties to the suit - Appeal dismissed.
