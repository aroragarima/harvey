Om Prakash Chautala v Kanwar Bhan and others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 62; (2014) 5 SCC 417; AIR 2014 SC 1220; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 32; 2014 (3) AWC 2195; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 197; 2014 (3) CTC 310; JT 2014 (2) SC 417; 2014 (5) MahLJ 498; 2014 (4) MPLJ 46; 2014 (1) RCR(Civil) 974; 2015 (1) RLW 920; 2014(1) SCALE 742; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 939; 2014 (2) SLR 573

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Employment - Disparaging remarks/adverse remarks-Expunction of-Held-Disparaging remarks as recorded by single Judge not necessary for arriving at decision which he has rendered-Same being not integral part and further that could not have been done when appellant was not party before court and also he was never afforded opportunity to explain his conduct-Affirmation of same by Division Bench of High Court on foundation that it has not caused any prejudice and he can fully defend himself

when subsequent litigation is instituted-Legally unacceptable-Accordingly, any remarks which are likely to affect reputation of appellant expunged.
