Viveka Nand Sethi v Chairman, J & K., Bank Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 363; (2005) 5 SCC 337; (2005) SCC (L&S) 689; 2005 (2) CLR 527; 2005 (2) ESC 260; 2005 (106) FLR 207; JT 2005 (5) SC 193; 2005 (2) LLJ 1034; 2005 (3) LLN 57; 2005(4) SCALE 587; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1095; 2005 (4) SLR 676; 2005 (4) Supreme 232; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 2036

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17-B - Interpretation of a bipartite settlement - Held, appeal can not be entertained in view of fact that workman did not file an affidavit before Single Judge in support of his contentions and as required under law - It is not necessary to remit matter to Tribunal as it would not serve any purpose - Appeal preferred by Bank should be allowed and that of workman should be dismissed.
