State Of U.P. And Others v Rekha Rani ..

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 198; (2011) 11 SCC 441; 2011 (4) ADJ 527; AIR 2011 SC 1893; 2011 (2) AllCJ 1042; 2011 (5) AWC 4774; 2011 (129) FLR 950; JT 2011 (4) SC 6; 2011 (3) LLJ 17; 2011(4) SCALE 97; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 1154; 2011 (2) SLR 787; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1126

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization - Entitlement - Respondent was appointed as Medical Officer under a temporary scheme formulated by appellant State - Respondent petitioned to HC for issuance of writ contending that in an earlier HC judgment, HC directed the appellant to regularise the service of those ad hoc employees who were allowed to continue in service by providing artificial breaks - HC, by relying on the said earlier judgment, allowed respondent's writ petition - Hence the instant appeal - Whether the respondent was entitled to get regularised in the service - Held, a regular appointment could only be made after selection by the Public Service Commission - Respondent was only a temporary employee and had not worked after a particular point of time - Merely because some others had been regularized did not give any right to the respondent since an illegality should not be perpetuated - Respondent's service was not terminated as a measure of punishment, hence no opportunity of hearing was necessary for terminating her service - Impugned direction for respondent's reinstatement was not sustainable as she was only a temporary employee and hence had no right to the post - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
