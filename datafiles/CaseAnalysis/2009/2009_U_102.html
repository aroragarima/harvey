Union of India and Others v Muralidhara Menon and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 991; (2009) 9 SCC 304; JT 2009 (12) SC 571; 2009(11) SCALE 416; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 540; 2009 (8) SLR 501

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Sources Of Recruitment

Summary: Service - Inter-charge transfer - Reversion - Validity - Respondent sought inter-charge transfer - Appellant allowed the transfer on the basis of an undertaking of respondent that he would be reverted to the post of LDC from UDC - Respondent filed application before Tribunal to restore his post of UDC - Tribunal dismissed the application - Respondent filed writ petition before HC - HC allowed writ petition of respondent - Legality - Appellant contended that respondent expressed his willingness to reversion at the time of transfer - Respondent contended that appellant allowed number of similarly placed employees to resume their original post -Held, respondent was volunteered to be transferred to the post of LDC on the basis of his undertaking - Respondent could not have been accommodated the UDC in absence of power - Respondent could not have resiled the undertaking - Circular (CBDT) dt. 14-5-1990 suggested that transfer order made after the representation would be final and no re-transfer could be passed - Principle of 'estoppel' applicable - Allegation of similarly placed employee was allowed to resume the original post, not brought on record - Respondent could either continue on the same post or seek transfer to the original post at the earlier place of employment - Therefore, impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
