National Insurance Company Ltd v Sehtia Shoes

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 252; (2008) 5 SCC 400; 2008 (70) ALR 910; 2008 (2) AWC 1550; 2008 (1) CCC 172; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 16; JT 2008 (3) SC 301; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 584; 2008(3) SCALE 135; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 451

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Insurance Amount, Loss Assessor

Summary: Respondent obtained a shopkeeper insurance policy of appellant - Respondent without demur accepted the sum of Rs.2, 72, 301/- in full and final settlement - Complaint before District Forum claiming that his claim was Rs.9 lacs and he should be indemnified to the extent of Rs.9 lacs less Rs.2, 72, 301/- which had been received by him - District Forum awarded a sum of Rs.4, 95, 000/- - Appeal against - Held, filing of a complaint is not barred; but it has to be proved that agreement to accept a particular amount was on account of coercion - This relevant factor has not been considered specifically by the District Forum, State Commission and the National Commission - Remitted the matter to the District Forum.
