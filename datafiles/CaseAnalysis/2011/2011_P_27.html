Pesara Pushpamala Reddy and another v G. Veera Swamy and another

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 185; (2011) 4 SCC 306; JT 2011 (3) SC 210; 2011(3) SCALE 253; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Rules, 1988

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982, ss. 7-A, 8 - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Rules, 1988, rr. 6, 7 - Cognizance of a case under 1982 Act by Special Tribunal - Report from Mandal Revenue Officer - Requirement of - Special Tribunal declared respondents as land grabbers with regard to appellants land - Respondents challenged Special Tribunal order before HC - HC allowed writ petition on grounds that Special Tribunal had not called for a report of Mandal Revenue Officer u/r. 6 of Rules and had also not issued a notification u/r. 7 of Rules in State Gazette after taking cognizance of case - Whether it was mandatory for Special Tribunal or Special Court to call for a report of Mandal Revenue Officer before taking cognizance of a case under Act - Held, ss. 7-A and 8 of Act contemplated that neither of two Sections required Special Tribunal or Special Court to refer any application or a case for local inspection or verification or both by Mandal Revenue Officer having jurisdiction over area - R. 6(1), however, provided that every application filed u/s. 8(1) of Act or every case taken cognizance of suo motu by Special Court or an application filed u/s. 7-A(1) of Act, before Special Tribunal, 'may' be referred for local inspection or verification or both by Mandal Revenue Officer having jurisdiction over area or by any other Officer of Government authorized by Court in that behalf - Object of r. 6 of Rules was to assist Special Tribunal or Special Court to arrive at a correct decision on claims and allegations made in application u/s. 7-A(1) and s. 8(1) of Act to Special Tribunal or Special Court and if that very object could be achieved without referring application of case to Mandal Revenue Officer, it may not be necessary for Special Tribunal or Special Court to make a reference to Mandal Revenue Officer and therefore there was no compelling duty on Special Tribunal or Special Court to refer application u/s. 7-A (1) or u/s. 8 to Mandal Revenue Officer - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Act, 1982 - Andhra Pradesh Land Grabbing (Prohibition) Rules, 1988 - Cognizance of a case under 1982 Act by Special Tribunal - Publication of notification in State Gazette - Requirement of - Whether it was mandatory for Special Tribunal or Special Court to publish a notification in Gazette notifying fact of cognizance of a case under Act - Held, notification or publication of notice of fact that cognizance of a case had been taken in State Gazette as required by proviso to s. 7-A(4) and proviso to s. 8(6) and r. 7(1)(2) was mandatory and should not be dispensed with by Special Tribunal and Special Court - Such requirement of a notification or publication of notice in State Gazette of fact that cognizance of a case had been taken by Special Tribunal or Special Court had been made mandatory by Act and Rules not in public interest but in interest of persons who may claim title, ownership or lawful possession of land which was subject-matter of proceedings u/s. 7-A or s. 8 of Act before Special Tribunal or Special Court - If, therefore, a person who claimed title, ownership or lawful possession of any such land was already a party in proceedings u/ss. 7-A or 8 of Act in Special Tribunal or Special Court and he had notice of such proceedings and had due opportunity to participate in said proceedings and asserted his title, ownership or lawful possession over land, he should not challenge proceedings of Special Tribunal or Special Court on ground that notification or publication of notice had not been made in accordance with Act and Rules - Respondents in instant case not only had notice of application u/s. 7-A of Act before Special Tribunal but also filed their replies to application and got opportunity to adduce evidence in support of their case and had not suffered any prejudice for non-compliance of provisions of proviso to s. 7-A(4) of Act or r. 7 of Rules - HC, therefore, was not right in quashing proceedings before Special Tribunal in instant case on ground that a notification or notice in terms of r. 7(2) of Rules had not been issued after case was taking cognizance of by Special Tribunal - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
