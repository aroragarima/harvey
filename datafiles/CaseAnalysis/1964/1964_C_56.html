Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Mir Mohd. Ali, Bus Owner, Vellore

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 1964 SC 1693; [1964] 53 ITR 165; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 66; 1964 (2) SCJ 354; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,ss.10(2),66A(2) - Income Tax Rules,1962, r.8 - Assessment - Allowance - Depreciation - Replacement - Assessee was the owner of a fleet of buses, replaced the petrol engines in two of his buses by new Diesel engines incurring an expenditure of Rs. 18,544/- in this connection, during the year of account ending with dt.31-3-1950 - For the relevant assessment year assessee claimed depreciation allowance u/s.10(2) of 1922 Act - Whether assessee was entitled to extra depreciation u/s.10(2) of 1922 Act - Held, assessee was entitled to extra depreciation u/s.10(2) of 1922 Act in respect of the diesel oil engine fitted to the motor vehicles in replacement of the existing engines - A diesel engine by itself may undoubtedly be used in a business other than that of a transport operator, for working a pump to draw underground water and may for that purpose be regarded as a self-contained unit - It would be fruitless to refer to the schedule u/r. 8 of 1962 Rules, for computing the allowance in respect of depreciation u/s.10(2)(vi) of Act - Schedule catalogues different items in respect of which depreciation was admissible at the rates prescribed - Appeal dismissed.
