Durga Prasad and another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 589; (2010) 9 SCC 73; 2010 (3) CGLJ 22; 2010 (3) JCC 1852; JT 2010 (8) SC 228; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 511; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 219; 2010(6) SCALE 18; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly caused the death of deceased - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 498A, 304B - HC upheld the conviction - Whether conviction is sustainable - Held, no evidence available on record to prove that the victim had committed suicide on account of cruelty and harassment to which she was subjected just prior to her death - Further, no charges were framed against the appellants under the provisions of the Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Evidence against the appellants for conviction u/ss. 498-A and 304-B IPC is insufficient - No case is made out by the prosecution against appellants - Therefore, impugned judgments of Trial Court and HC are set aside and appellants are acquitted - Appeal allowed.
