Navjot Singh Sidhu v Om Parkash Soni and others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 827; AIR 2016 SC 4965; 2017 (1) Bom.C.R. 481; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 185; JT 2016 (10) SC 323; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 899; 2016(10) SCALE 352

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Expenses, Expenditure, Candidate, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: (A) Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16, O. 7 r. 11 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 123(7), 77(3), 83, 83(1)(a), 83(1)(b) - Election petition - Corrupt practices - Dismissal - Appellant/returned candidate filed an application under O. 6 r. 16 and O. 7 r. 11 CPC for dismissal of election petition contending that on account of deficiencies in pleadings no triable issues was disclosed to justify a regular trial of allegations made - HC directed that a regular trial of the same was ordered - Hence, instant appeal - Whether incurring of expenditure in contravention of the limit prescribed u/s. 77(3) of the Act was valid.

Held, it cannot be said that full particulars of allegation of corrupt practice were set out by respondent/election petitioner. The dates on which the advertisements had appeared, particulars of newspapers in which such advertisements were published, cost incurred for each type of advertisement in each newspaper, were mentioned. When details to the above extent have been mentioned in election petition, it cannot be said that full particulars as required u/s. 83(1)(b) of the Act have not been furnished by election petitioner. Respondent by giving details of expenditure incurred by appellant in connection with public meetings held on different dates and in different venues, has contended that the expenses incurred on public meetings was much more than what was shown in return of election expenses under the said head (Rs.1,83,466/-). While the details of meetings i.e. the time, date and venue were mentioned and so was the number of persons who claimed to have attended the meetings, SC do not find any basis as to how election petitioner had arrived at the quantum of expenses which he alleges to have been incurred by returned candidate in holding each of the said meetings. All such particulars that were an integral part of allegation of corrupt practice alleged were absent. Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 16, O. 7 r. 11 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 123(7), 77(3), 83, 83(1)(a), 83(1)(b) - Returned candidate - assistance received - Validity - Whether assistance received by appellant as returned candidate from gazette officer was valid.

Held, the allegation in election petition was that the post to which gazette officer was transferred from State Electricity Board was under State Govt. and assistance received by returned candidate from said person was while he was rendering service as Addtl. Superintending Engineer, namely, while he was performing the duties in State Govt. If that be so, the aforesaid issue also would have to go for a full trial as ordered by HC. Appeal partly allowed.
