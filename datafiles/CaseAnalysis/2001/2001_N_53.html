G. C. Kapoor v Nand Kumar Bhasin and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20375; (2002) 1 SCC 610; AIR 2002 SC 200; 2001 (45) ALR 808; 2002 (1) AWC 73; JT 2001 (9) SC 558; 2002 (131) PLR 251; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 407; 2001(8) SCALE 222; 2001 (8) SLT 100; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 282; 2001 (8) Supreme 329; 2002 (1) UC 197

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972

Summary: Rent control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21(1)(a) - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Rules, 1972, r. 16(2)(d) - Bonafide requirement - Whether court below were justified, keeping in view facts of case, in negating eviction on ground of bona fide requirement? - S. 21(1)(a) provides that an application for eviction can be filed by landlord if building is bona fide required by landlord for occupation by himself or by any member of family or by any person for whose benefit premises is held by him, either for residential purposes or for purposes of any profession, trade or calling - It is irrelevant to consider question of necessary funds to start business withdrawal of franchise from one institution and technical know how of son of landlord, in view of facts - Held, courts below were not justified in rejecting eviction petition - Courts below completely overlooked cl. (d) of r. 16(2) while deciding eviction petition - From rule, court has to ascertain whether son of landlord has completed technical education and is not employed in Govt. and wants to engage in self-employment - All criteria laid down in said clause have been proved and, therefore, appellant is entitled to get decree for eviction - Petition allowed.
