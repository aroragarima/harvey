International Airports Authority Employees' Union and Another v Airport Authority of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2223; (1997) 10 SCC 754; 2001 (1) CLR 585; 1997 (2) CLT 446; 1997 (76) FLR 390; JT 1997 (4) SC 757; 1997 (2) LLJ 361; 2001 (1) LLJ 549; 1997 (3) LLN 78; 1997(3) SCALE 683; 1997 (2) SCJ 282; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 868; 1997 (4) SLR 24; 2001 (1) SLT 206; 1997 (4) Supreme 325; 1997 (1) UJ 804

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract, Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970, s. 10 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - (A) Whether the abolition of the contract labour system with effect from 09-12-1976 in the light of the judgment of this Court in Air India Statutory Corpn. v. United Labour Union, is covered in the present circumstances of case? - Held, though the regularisation of their services with effect from the date of judgment was given by this Court since they have come in appeal by virtue of that part of the judgment in these cases, viz., they are not entitled to the benefit from the date of the abolition of the contract labour system, the same benefit may be given from the date of the judgment of the High Court - Where the matter is covered by the judgment of the HC, the regularisation will be with effect from the respective dates - Appeals disposed off - (B) Whether three types of workmen, namely, sweepers, canteen workers and cabin catering cleaners covered by the judgment Air India Statutory Corpn. v. United Labour Union? - Held, as far as the sweepers are concerned, it is covered by the judgment of this Court - With regard to canteen workers the matter requires remittance for reconsideration by the HC in the light of the judgment - Appeal disposed of - (C) Whether the regularisation of the employees engaged in various fields covered by the said judgment? - Held, their case is that they are employed as contract labour by the various employers on behalf of the principal employer, namely, Air India - The Notification D/- 09-12-1976 relates to the abolition of the contract labour engaged in sweeping, cleaning, dusting and watching of buildings owned by Air India - They are also entitled to be appointed on regular basis - Instead of directing Central Advisory Board to go into the question, the HC would go into and decide the matter in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
