S. Vanathan Muthuraja v Ramalingam Aliaskrishnamurthy Gurukkal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 865; (1997) 6 SCC 143; 1998 (1) AWC 396; 1997 (2) CLT 385; JT 1997 (5) SC 110; 1997(3) SCALE 726; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 581; 1997 (4) Supreme 439

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Tamil Nadu Minor Inams (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1963, s. 8(2)(ii) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - One Ayyasamy Gurukkal was common ancestor in respect of the suit property - He had four sons - Ramani is the grand-son of Ayyasamy Gurukkal who was impleaded as first defendant - First son died unmarried, the second son died leaving behind four grandsons and his son's widow (D-3 to D-7), the third son died leaving behind his widow and daughter (D-8 and D-9) - Widow D-8 had sold her 1/3rd share to the plaintiff no 1 under sale deed, Ex A-7 D/- 26-06-1970 - After the purchase of the property under Ex A-7, the suit came to be filed for partition of the 2/3rd share and separate possession thereof - On appeal, it was reversed with the holding that they did not acquire title of adverse possession against co-owner - When the appeal was pending, the Tehsildar conducted a suo motu enquiry and was found that the ryotwari patta was granted to the institution - Learned Judge held that by virtue of the provisions contained under the Act, the suit is not maintainable - Whether, the suit of the appellant is not maintainable? - Held, the patta granted u/s. 8(2)(ii) of the Act by the Tehsildar was confirmed - On appeal, it was confirmed the order has become final - Thus, the title to the property was vested in the institution and thereby, none of the parties has any right, title and interest in the property - Therefore, the suit of the appellant with out impleading the institution is not maintainable - In this case, the Act concerned extinguishes the pre-existing right, creates new rights, creates new rights under the Act and requires tribunals to enquire into the rival claims and a forms of appeal has been provided against the order of the primary authority - Thereby the right and remedy made conclusive under the Act are given finality by the orders passed under the Act - Thereby, by necessary implication, the jurisdiction of the Civil Court stands excluded - Appeal dismissed.
