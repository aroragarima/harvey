New Okhla Industrial Development Authority v Harkishan (Dead), Through Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 79; (2017) 3 SCC 588; AIR 2017 SC 854; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 29; 2017 (121) ALR 487; 2017 (2) AWC 1145; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 388; JT 2017 (2) SC 42; 2017 (4) MahLJ 30; 2017 (1) RCR(Civil) 993; 2017 (135) RD 539; 2017(2) SCALE 69; 2017 (2) UPLBEC 1232

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Review, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 11, 11A, 17, 4, 5A, 6, 48(1) - Acquisition of Land - Order Challenged - Respondents/landowners land was acquired by appellant/development authority in emergency against denial of right to file objection - In first round of litigation HC dismissed writ petitions and the same was confirmed by SC, however, liberty was granted to make representation to state govt. for release of land - HC dismissed writ petitions filed by respondent in second round of litigation against rejection of representation by state govt. - HC allowed writ petition of respondent on ground that award was passed beyond limitation and direct issuance of fresh notification - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, filing fresh writ petition challenging the validity of the award for the first time in the year 2004 would, therefore, not only be barred by the provisions of O. 2, r.2 of CPC but would also be barred on the doctrine of laches and delays as well. If the respondents wanted to challenge the validity of the award on the ground that it was passed beyond the period of limitation, they should have done so immediately and, in any case, in the second round of writ petitions filed by them. Further, once possession is taken u/s.17(1) of the Act, s. 11A of the Act is not even attracted and, therefore, acquisition proceedings would not lapse on failure to make award within the period prescribed therein. In instant case, appellant acquired land by invoking urgency clause. The approach of the HC in entertaining the writ petitions, which were clearly barred in law, and allowing the same is not right. Hence, judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
