Anjani Kumar Chaudhary v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 754; (2014) 12 SCC 286; AIR 2014 SC 2740; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4166; 2014 CRLJ 3798; 2014 (3) JCC 1774; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 737; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 504, 323, 384, 324, 307 and 341 - Framing of charges - Case was registered u/ss. 147, 148, 504, 323, 384, 324, 307 and 341 of IPC against seven named persons and 10-15 unknown persons - Accused No. 1 filed an application stating that no offence u/ss. 307 or 386 of IPC was made out and hence prayed for the discharge - Sessions Court held that no charge can be framed u/ss. 307, 386 of IPC against the accused, except the rest of the charges levelled against him - Revision filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the instant case, as per the statements, the weapons used were Lathi, rod, Farsa, Talwar etc. and the injuries were caused by using sharp cutting weapons and also with hard blunt substance. Open declaration by the accused that a person would be killed, indicates his intention and the question as to whether there was intention to kill or knowledge that death will be caused is a question of fact. Hence, facts would indicate that the ingredients of s. 307 of IPC are made out. Appeal allowed.
