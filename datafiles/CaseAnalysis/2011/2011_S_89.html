National Insurance Co. Ltd v Shyam Singh And Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 405; (2011) 7 SCC 65; 2011 (3) ACC 291; 2011 ACJ 1990; 2011 (4) AWC 3942; 2011 (3) JabLJ 91; JT 2011 (7) SC 135; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 551; 2011 (164) PLR 113; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 679; 2011(6) SCALE 723; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Enhancement of compensation - Challenged - Deceased, age 19 years, died in a motor accident - Respondent nos. 3 and 4 (Parents of deceased) filed claim petition submitting that deceased was drawing salary of Rs. 4,500/- per month - Tribunal applied the multiplier of 9 and awarded a total compensation of Rs. 1,72,000/- along with 6 % interest per annum from the date of claim petition considering deceased was earning Rs. 3000/- per month and deducted 50% therefrom towards personal expenses, as he was a bachelor - On appeal, HC enhanced the amount of compensation from Rs. 1,72,000/- to Rs. 3,39,000/- on the ground that the income of the deceased was Rs. 4,500/- and not Rs. 3,000/- - HC also relied on the SC judgment in Sarla Verma (Smt.) and Others v. Delhi Transport Corporation and Another 2009 INDLAW SC 488 and held that a multiplier of 18 instead of 9 was supposed to be applied - Petitioner filed appeal against said order contending that as per SC judgment in Sarla Verma case, where an unmarried young man dies, the average age of the parents will be taken for determining the multiplier and not the age of the deceased - Whether HC rightly enhanced the compensation awarded to the respondents? - Held, SC in Vijay Shankar Shinde and Others vs. State of Maharashtra 2008 INDLAW SC 43, held that the selection of multiplier cannot in all cases be solely dependent on the age of the deceased - If a young man was killed in the accident leaving behind aged parents who may not survive long enough to match with a high multiplier provided by the 2nd Schedule, then the Court has to offset such high multiplier and balance the same with the short life expectancy of the claimants - The dictum laid down in Vijay Shankar Shinde case was applicable to the instant case - Accordingly, Tribunal had rightfully applied the multiplier of 8 by taking the average of the parents of the deceased who were 55 and 56 years - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
