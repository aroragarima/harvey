Dina Nath (D) by Lrs. and another v Subhash Chand Saini and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 256; (2014) 11 SCC 20; 2014 (107) ALR 145; 2014 (5) ALT(SC) 8; JT 2014 (5) SC 326; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 951; 2014 (1) RCR(Rent) 486; 2014(5) SCALE 147

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Delhi Rent Act, 1995, Delhi Rent (Repeal) Bill, 2013

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s.15(7) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.227 - Eviction - Validity - Appellants/tenants rented a shop wherein the monthly payable rent was Rs. 66 - Respondents/landlords jointly owned the tenanted premises - Rent for the shop was paid to respondent no. 1, who held a power of attorney to collect rent (on behalf of the respondents) - In November 2007, the respondents filed an eviction petition u/s. 14 (1) (a) (b) (c) and (j) of Act seeking repossession of the rented premises, for a variety of reasons - By an order dt.14-9-2009 the Rent Controller allowed the application filed by the respondents u/s. 15(7) of Act, and thereby, struck off the defence of the appellants in the pending eviction petition - Dissatisfied with the order passed by the Rent Controller, the appellants approached Tribunal - By an order dt. 24-5-2010, Tribunal dismissed the appeal preferred by the appellants - Dissatisfied, the appellants approached HC by filing a petition u/art. 227 of the Constitution of India, wherein, the appellants assailed the order passed by the Rent Controller dt. 14-9-2009, as well as, the order of the Tribunal dt. 24-5-2010 - HC dismissed the petition filed u/art. 227 on 10-5-2011 - Whether it was just and appropriate for the succeeding courts (the Rent Controller, Tribunal and HC) to have accepted the prayer made by the respondents, for striking out the defence of the appellants, in the eviction proceedings -

Held, facts of the case depicted a recalcitrant, as well as, a negligent and careless behaviour, at the hands of the appellants - It was not a case of a single lapse, but of persistent repeated and unrelenting default in the payment of future rent, for all the months intervening the date when the order u/s. 15(1) of Act was passed, and the date when the application u/s. 15(7) of Act was filed by the respondents - It was not possible to condone such indifference, insensitivity, disinterest and apathy to judicial directions - Judicial discretion in such a matter, taking into consideration the defaults committed by the appellants, was legitimately exercised by the Courts below, by striking out the defence of the appellants-tenants - Furthermore, payment voluntarily made by the tenant on 21-4-2008 towards arrears of rent, could not be attributable or assignable or creditable, towards future rent - Said payment was made, in exercise of free discretion, towards arrears of rent - It should be deemed to be a deposit by the tenant for that purpose, and for no other purpose - Respondents filed an application u/s. 15(7) of Act on 28-4-2009 praying for striking out the defence of the appellants for non-compliance of the order of the Rent Controller dt. 21-4-2008 - Payments made by the appellants, for future rent payable upto 15-4-2009 (for the month of March, 2009), after the date of filing of the application (on 29-4-2009), was not relevant, for determining the issue in hand - Date on which the Rent Controller passed the order striking out the defence of the appellants, i.e. 14-9-2009, had absolutely no nexus to the consideration contemplated in s. 15(7) of Act - Offer made by the appellants to raise the rent by ten times of the current amount, and to pay the same in advance for a period of five years, was nothing but an act of frustration, and was only aimed to prejudice the Court's mind - S. 15(7) of Act did not contemplate condonation of payments made in violation of the directions issued by the Rent Controller, by subsequent payments, even where the appellant accepted to make a voluntary payment, many folds more than what was due to the landlord - Only exception was when there was a reasonable explanation for delayed payment - Unfortunately, there was no such explanation on behalf of appellant, in instant case - Therefore, the action of the appellants in not complying with the schedule of payment expressed in Rent Controller's order dt. 21-4-2008 (for paying future rent), consecutively and repeatedly for 12 months, was nothing but a contumacious failure to comply with the directions of the Rent Controller - Instant controversy actually demonstrated how a tenant had effectively frustrated the legislative intent contemplated in s.15(7) of Act - Legislative purpose was, to curb tendencies of tenants, from abusing the legal process - Respondents filed an eviction petition in November, 2007 - Based on the non-compliance of the directions issued by the Rent Controller (on 21-4-2008), the respondents moved an application on 28-4-2009, praying for striking out the defence of the appellants-tenants - After the appellants filed their reply on 17-8-2009, the Rent Controller allowed the application, and struck off the defence of the appellants, by an order dt. 14-9-2009 - Order of the Rent Controller dt. 14-9-2009 was assailed by the appellants before Tribunal - Prayer made by the appellants was rejected by Tribunal on 21-4-2010 - Appellants then approached the HC by filing a petition u/art. 227 of the Constitution of India - HC dismissed the petition on 10-5-2011 - Said order was assailed by filing a Petition for Special Leave to Appeal - Matter was pending disposal in HC ever since - Appellants, despite their contumacious disobedience, of the directions contained in the order of the Rent Controller dt. 14-9-2009, have frustrated the process of law successfully, for about five years (from 28-4-2009, i.e., the date on which the application u/s. 15(7) of Act was filed, till the disposal of instant Appeal) - Appellants had achieved, what the legislation aimed to avoid - HC was of the considered view, that the order passed by the Rent Controller dt. 14-9-2009, which was upheld by Tribunal (on 24-5-2010) and the HC (vide order dt.10-5-2011) called for no interference whatsoever - Appeal dismissed.
