Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K. Ramaswamy, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 430; (1993) 4 SCC 727; (1993) SCC (L&S) 1184; AIR 1994 SC 1074; AIR 1994 SCW 1050; 1993 (25) ATC 704; 1993 (67) FLR 1230; JT 1993 (6) SC 1; 1994 LabIC 762; 1994 (1) LLJ 162; 1993(3) SCALE 952; 1993 (3) SLJ 193; 1993 (5) SLR 532; [1993] Supp2 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 141, 226, 309 and 311 - Delinquent employee is entitled to copy enquiry report, before disciplinary authority takes decision regarding guilt or innocence - Constitutionality of refusal to furnish copy - Held, amounts to denial of reasonable opportunity - Copy has to be furnished to employee, even if (1) Statutory rules are silent or against it - (2) Punishment imposed is other than major punishment of dismissal, removal or reduction in rank - (3) Irrespective of whether he asks for it or not - (4) Inquiry held by person other than Disciplinary Authority and this applies to employees in all establishments whether Govt. or non-Govt., public or private - Tribunal should furnish him with copy and afford opportunity to show if and how prejudice is caused - Order accordingly.
