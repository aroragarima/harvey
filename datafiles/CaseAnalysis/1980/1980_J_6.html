State Bank of Haryana and Others v Jage Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 483; (1980) 3 SCC 599; (1982) SCC (Tax) 130; AIR 1980 SC 2018; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 746; 1980 TAXLR 2362

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution, arts. 226, 299, 265, sch. 7, list 2, entry 51 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, ss. 36 and 58 - Punjab Liquor Licence Rules, 1956, rr. 36(1), 36(16), 36(22A), 36(22)(2) and 36(23)(2) - Excise Duty - Liquor Licence Rules - Bids in respect of country liquor vends at annual auctions - (A) Bidding - Acceptance of bids by State Authorities - Whether bidders can be permitted to wriggle out - When they had full knowledge of the Terms an Conditions - (B) Amount which bidders agree to pay to State Government under auction terms - What it is - Held, bidders cannot be permitted to wriggle out of their voluntarily incurred contractual obligations by invoking writ jurisdiction - Amount is neither fee nor excise duty on undrawn liquor but price of privilege which State parted in their favour - Matter Remitted To High Court.
