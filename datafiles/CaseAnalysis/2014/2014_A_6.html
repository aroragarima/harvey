Zarina Siddiqui v A. Ramalingam alias R. Amarnathan

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of Attorney

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 65, 66 - Suit for specific performance - Power of attorney - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) filed a suit for specific performance seeking for a direction to respondents (defendants) to execute the sale deed in respect of 1/3rd share in suit property - Trial Court decreed the suit - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Single Judge gravely erred in law in holding that the Power of Attorney was not proved as required u/ss. 65 and 66 of the Act.

Held, although defendant no. 2 held a registered power of attorney on behalf of defendant no. 1 to sell and dispose of the property, but the defendants not only made a false statement on affidavit that the power of attorney had authorized the second defendant only to look after and manage the property but also withheld the said power of attorney from the Court in order to misguide the Court from truth of the facts. Further, by registered agreement the defendants agreed to sell the suit premises after receiving advance consideration but they denied the existence of the agreement in their pleading. Such conduct of the defendants, disentitle them to ask the Court for exercising discretion in their favour by refusing to grant a decree for specific performance. Further, if a party to a lis does not disclose all material facts truly and fairly but states them in distorted manner and mislead the Court, the Court has inherent power to exercise its discretionary jurisdiction in order to prevent abuse of the process of law. In the facts and circumstances of case and considering the phenomenal increase in price during the period the matter remained pending in different Courts, impugned order under appeal be set aside but with a condition imposed upon the appellant (plaintiff) to pay a sum of Rs.15,00,000/- (Rupees Fifteen Lacs) in addition to the amount already paid by the appellant to the respondent. On deposit in trial court of aforesaid amount by the appellant, for payment to the respondent, within 3 months, the respondent shall execute and register the sale deed in favour of the plaintiff in respect of the suit property. In the event said condition of deposit of Rs.15 lacs is fulfilled within the time stipulated but the defendant fails to comply with the direction, then the appellant shall be entitled to execute the decree in accordance with the procedure provided in law. Impugned judgment passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
