State of Rajasthan and Another v Mohammed Ayub Naz

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 134; (2006) 1 SCC 589; AIR 2006 SC 856; 2006 (1) CLR 401; 2006 (1) CTC 361; JT 2006 (1) SC 162; 2006 (1) LLJ 742; 2006 (2) RLW 1084; 2006(1) SCALE 79; 2006 (1) SCJ 348; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 138; 2006 (1) SLR 832

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Rajasthan Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1958, rr. 16 and 86(3) - (A) Whether the High Court can interfere with the decision of imposing punishment once the HC finds that finding of the delinquent being absent for a period of 3 years as correct? - Held, the impugned order of removal from service is the only proper punishment to be awarded to the respondent herein who was willfully absent for 3 years without intimation to the Govt. - Opportunity was given to the respondent to contest the disciplinary proceedings - Respondent himself has admitted that he was absent for about 3 years the authorities have not omitted any relevant materials nor any irrelevant fact taken into account nor any illegality committed by the authority - Om Kumar and Others v. UOI, (Indlaw SC 2713) - (B) Whether the HC is right in converting the punishment of removal into compulsory retirement with consequential retrial benefits after endorsing that the respondent did remain absent for about 3 years and that there was no satisfactory explanation to justify absence of 3 years? - Held, the respondent was deliberately absent for a period of about 3 years and, therefore, he has violated r. 86(3) of the Service Rules which contemplated removal from service and, therefore, he will not be entitled to any back-wages or any other emoluments for the period for which he was absent - Respondent himself that he was wilfully absent for 3 years, the punishment of removal imposed on him is absolutely correct - A Govt. servant who has wilfully been absent for a period of about 3 years has no right to receive the monetary/retiral benefits during the period in question - Appeal allowed.
