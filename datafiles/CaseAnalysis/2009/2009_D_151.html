State Of Maharashtra v Dnyaneshwar Laxman Rao Wankhede

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 930; (2009) 15 SCC 200; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3127; 2009 CRLJ 4425; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 335; 2009(10) SCALE 355; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 (1), 20 - Bribery - Respondent-police constable alleged to be taken bribe from the complainant - Trial Court sentenced respondent for 6months imprisonment and fine - Prosecution filed Appeal before HC - HC reversed the conviction on the ground of failure to prove the ingredients of s. 20 - Legality - Held, the sole independent witness died during the pendency of trial - Only witness, PW1, declared hostile - No possibility of visiting the place of complainant by respondent frequently for collecting a small amount of bribe - However, prosecution was repeatedly conducting raids in the premises of respondent during that time - Therefore, allegation of receiving bribe by respondent in that relevant time cast serious doubt over prosecution case - Respondent alleged to be received bribe in a public place instead of at his home or at police station, is indigestible - Details of raids at the home of respondent and at the police station were not disclosed by prosecution - Prosecution failed to prove even the foundational facts of the case - No merit and acquittal upheld - Appeal dismissed.
