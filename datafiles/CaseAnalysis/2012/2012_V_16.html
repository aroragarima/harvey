Vijay Kumar Kaul And Others v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 145; (2012) 7 SCC 610; AIR 2012 SC 2274; 2012 (134) FLR 8; JT 2012 (5) SC 474; 2012(5) SCALE 665; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 128; 2013 (1) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Fixation of seniority - Belated approach - Sustainability - Appellants participated in a selection process conducted in the year 1984 conducted by the Second Field Ordnance Depot (2 FOD) for the post of Lower Division Clerks (LDCs) - Despite their selection for the post in question they were not issued appointment letters on the pretext that there was a ban on appointments - In December 1993, pursuant to the order passed by the Central Administrative tribunal (Tribunal), respondent was issued an appointment letter - Appellant were given appointment in May, 1996 on the basis of the directions issued on 24-7-1995 by the HC - 'X' and others, whose names, had figured in the select list, being aggrieved due to non appointment, filed application before the Tribunal, which was allowed directing the respondent to issue appointment letters to them - Respondents appointed them w.e.f. 1-1-1990 - 'X' and others filed second application with a prayer to issue a direction to the respondents to appoint them as LDCs w.e.f. 1-5-1985 - Tribunal allowed the application - On appeal, HC upheld said direction - After the HC passed the order, respondents conferred the benefit on said 'X' and others - Thereafter, appellants submitted a series of representations to extend to them the similar benefits on the foundation of parity - Said prayer was negatived by the respondents - Application was filed before Tribunal - Tribunal held that the decision rendered in the case of 'X' and others could not be treated as judgment in rem but a judgment in personam - On appeal, HC on considering the factum of the delay and laches on the part of the appellants, and that they had not been superseded as the select list was prepared in order of merit, and appreciating the fact that the appointments had been made strictly in accordance with the merit declined to interfere with the order - Hence, instant appeal - Held, a litigant who invoked the jurisdiction of a court for claiming seniority, it was obligatory on his part to come to the court at the earliest or at least within a reasonable span of time - In the instant case, the appellants knew about the approach by 'X' and others before the Tribunal and the directions given by the Tribunal but they chose to wait and thereafter further consumed time submitting representations and eventually approached the Tribunal after quite a span of time - In the meantime, the beneficiaries of HC were promoted to the higher posts - Hence, to disturb the seniority position at this stage would be extremely inequitable - Further, neither before the Tribunal nor before the HC, 'X' and others were arrayed as parties - In their absence, if any direction was issued for fixation of seniority, that was likely to jeopardise their interest - Tribunal and HC correctly declined to exercise their jurisdiction - Appeals dismissed.
