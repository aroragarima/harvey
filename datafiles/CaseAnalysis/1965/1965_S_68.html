Union of India v Sree Ram Bohra and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1965 SC 1531; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 830

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corporate Body

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Law - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Plaintiffs carried on business of cloth - Plaintiffs had filed suit in their capacity as kartas of same joint family- Thereafter one of plaintiffs dies - Appeal against order obtained - (A)Whether any other member or members other than manager of joint Hindu family, carry on business, etc, on behalf of family, does so as kartas of family? - Karta of joint Hindu family is certainly manager of family property but undoubtedly possesses powers which ordinary manager does not possess - Held, fact that any other member or members other than manager of joint Hindu family, carry on business, etc, on behalf of family, does not mean that such members who act for family do so as kartas of family - (B)Whether appeal against second plaintiff Sree Ram Bohra is competent ? - For purpose of suit, there were two plaintiffs and on death of one of them it was necessary for opposite party to implead his heirs and LRs. within time - It failed to do so and therefore appeal against those heirs and representatives of Bilas Rai Bohra was rightly held to have abated - Result of such abatement makes this appeal against other respondent incompetent as decree against both respondents - There was nothing in decree to indicate for whose benefit it was passed or in what proportions two decree-holders were to get decretal amount - Further held, appeal against Sree Ram Bohra was incompetent - Appeal dismissed.
