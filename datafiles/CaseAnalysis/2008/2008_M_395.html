Vidyodaya Trust v Mohan Prasad R and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 318; (2008) 4 SCC 115; AIR 2008 SC 1633; 2008 (72) ALR 10; 2008 (2) AWC 1591; 2008 (2) CalLT(SC) 44; 2008 (3) CTC 868; JT 2008 (3) SC 119; 2008 (2) KLT 68; 2008 (4) MahLJ 18; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 967; 2008 (3) MPLJ 133; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 229; 2008(3) SCALE 273; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 569; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2178

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - High Court held that leave had been rightly granted in terms of s. 92 - Appeal against - Held, if in a given case notice has not been given and leave has been granted, it is open to the Court to deal with an application for revocation and pass necessary orders - Though the color of legitimacy was sought to be given by projecting as if the suit was for vindicating public rights the emphasis was on certain purely private and personal disputes - Courts have to be careful to eliminate the possibility of a suit being laid against public trusts under s. 92 by persons whose activities were not for protection of the interests of the public trusts - High Court was wrong in holding that the grant of leave was legal and proper - Appeal allowed.
