Divya Exports v Shalimar Video Company and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 616; 2012 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2011 (89) ALR 476; 2012 (1) CGLJ 114; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 154; 2012 (2) RLW 1073; 2011(9) SCALE 630

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Media & Entertainment; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Broadcasting

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Indian Trust Act, 1882, ss. 73 and 74 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.41 r.24 - Copy Right - Assignment - Non-joinder - Justifiability - Respondent no.1 published notice declaring that it had purchased rights for 15 films produced by M/s.Vijaya production - Respondent no.3 called upon respondent no.1 to stop production of DVDS and VCDs - Respondent no.1 filed suit for grant of declaration of rights and also to restraint appellant and respondents no. 2 and 3 from producing or selling VCDs/DVDs or any other video format of those films in any form of exploitation - Trial Court found that suit was bad for non-joinder of assignor - Respondent filed appeal before HC - HC allowed appeal and found that agreement executed by respondent/defendant no.3 in favour of defendant no.1 there in was not valid - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment of HC was justifiable - Held, evidence available on ground was not at all sufficient for deciding question, yet HC had decided by drawing inferences from statements made by witnesses and formed a negative finding on validity of agreement - HC was not at all justified in invoking O.41 r. 24 CPC - If at all HC felt that Trial Court should have framed specific issue on validity of agreement, then it should have remanded matter to Trial Court with direction to frame such issue and decide suit afresh - Omission on part of HC to adopt that course had resulted in manifest injustice to appellant - Hence , impugned judgment was set aside and case was remitted to Trial Court -Appeal allowed.
