Ram Singh and others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 194; (2015) 4 SCC 697; 2015 (3) AWC 2485; JT 2015 (3) SC 159; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1376; 2015 (3) MLJ 339; 2015(3) SCALE 570; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 670

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Affirmative Action, Scheduled Castes, National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993, National Commission for Backward Classes (Power to Review Advice) Rules, 2011

Summary: Administrative - National Commission for Backward Classes Act, 1993, ss. 9, 11 - Inclusion of Jat community in Backward Class - Instant writ petitions were filed challenging notification dt.4-3-2014 whereby Jat community was included in list of backward classes for States of Bihar, Gujarat, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh, Madhya Pradesh, NCT of Delhi, Bharatpur and Dholpur districts of Rajasthan, Uttar Pradesh and Uttarakhand rejecting advice of National Commission for Backward Classes (NCBC) to the contrary - Whether Union Govt. is bound by advice rendered by NCBC .

Held, report dt.26-2-2014 of the NCBC was made on a detailed consideration of the various reports of the State Backward Classes Commissions, other available literature on the subject and also upon consideration of the findings of the Expert Committee constituted by the ICSSR to examine the matter. Hence, the decision not to recommend the Jats for inclusion in the Central List of OBCs of the States in question could not be said to be based on no materials or unsupported by reasons or characterized as decision arrived at on consideration of matters that are, in any way, extraneous and irrelevant. Further, something more would be required in order to bypass the advice tendered by the NCBC which judicially and statutorily would be binding on the Union Govt. in the ordinary course. An impossible or perverse view would justify exclusion of the advice tendered but that had not happened in instant case. Hence, notification dt.4-3-2014 is not justifiable and the same is set aside and quashed allowing writ petitions. Petitions disposed of.

Ratio - As per observations made in Indra Sawhney and Others v. Union of India and Others, 1992 INDLAW SC 735, and the expressed provisions contained in s. 9 of the Act, advice tendered by NCBC is ordinarily biding on the Govt., meaning thereby that the same could be overruled or ignored only for strong and compelling reasons available in writing. Further, even when the exercise undertaken by the Central Govt. is one under s. 11 of the Act, the views expressed by the NCBC in the process of the consultation mandated by s. 11, would have a binding effect in the normal course.
