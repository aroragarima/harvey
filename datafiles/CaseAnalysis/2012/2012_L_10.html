Office Of The Chief Post Master General & Ors v Living Media India Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 55; (2012) 3 SCC 563; AIR 2012 SC 1506; 2012 (91) ALR 879; 2012 (3) AWC 2651; 2012 (3) CalLT 65; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 338; [2012] 174 Comp Cas 387; 2012 (277) E.L.T. 289; [2012] 348 ITR 7; JT 2012 (2) SC 483; 2012 (116) RD 167; 2012(2) SCALE 782; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1045; [2012] 207 TAXMAN 163; [2012] 54 VST 188

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Indian Post Office Rules, 1933

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Publishers - Transmission - Concession - Limitation - Validity - Respondent publishers of magazines made application to appellants stating that they are entitled for transmission by post under concessional rate of postage - Appellants rejected their representation - Respondent filed writ petition in HC, HC allowed Writ petition - Respondent filed LPA before DB, DB upheld decision of HC - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant filed present appeal with delay of 427 days - Respondent seriously objected to the conduct of appellants in approaching Court after enormous and inordinate delay of 427 days in filing above appeals, and that petition filed does not contain acceptable and plausible reasons to condone delay - Whether reasons for justifying such a huge delay are acceptable or not - Held, department itself had mentioned that it is aware of date of the judgment of DB of HC, Department nor person in-charge had filed any explanation for not applying for certified copy within prescribed period or why such delay had occasioned, it was stated that delay was due to unavoidable circumstances and genuine difficulties, fact remains that from day one Department or person/persons concerned had not evinced diligence in prosecuting matter to this Court by taking appropriate steps - It is not in dispute that person(s) concerned were well aware or conversant with issues involved including prescribed period of limitation - They cannot claim that they have a separate period of limitation when Department was possessed with competent persons familiar with court proceedings - In absence of plausible and acceptable explanation, Department cannot take advantage of various earlier decisions as law of limitation undoubtedly binds everybody including Government - Appeals dismissed.
