Mattapalli Chelamayya and Another v Mattapalli Venkataratnam and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 314; (1972) 3 SCC 799; AIR 1972 SC 1121; 1972 UJ 579

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 30, 33 -

Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 132, 133 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Stamp Act, 1899, s. 35 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 49 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882- Property - Personal Liability - Maintainability - Respondents filed u/s. 14 of Arbitration Act for filing of award and for decree in terms of that award - Application was registered as suit - Defendants filed written statements contesting suit and also applied by I.A. for setting aside award u/ss. 30 and 33 of Arbitration Act - It was contended that award was unstamped and unregistered and was inadmissible in evidence - Trial Court embodied partition of immovable properties worth more than Rs. 100/- and was compulsorily registrable u/s. 17 of Indian Registration Act - Being compulsorily registrable award could not be admitted in evidence for purpose of passing of decree - Trial Court further held that award was not duly stamped and was inadmissible in evidence and dismissed suit - Plaintiff's appealed to HC - Appeal was placed before Full Bench and Court held that award was not inadmissible - Since other points raised in Trial Court had not been considered case was remanded to Trial Court - Application for certificate u/arts. 132 and 133 of Constitution were filed by defendants in HC and same was rejected - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, instant case involved two distinct matters - One was personal liability to pay amount, and second was additional relief to recover that amount from immovable property of appellants in case they failed to pay as ordered - Court held that two do not form one transaction but two severable transactions - HC was right in directing that second transaction could be validly ignored and to extent that it declared personal obligation to pay transaction, not being required to be compulsorily registered award was admissible in evidence - Court further held that award was inadmissible in evidence in view of s. 35 of Stamp Act - It was true that award in original was not engrossed on stamp paper - HC had rightly pointed out that intention of arbitrators in engrossing copy of award on stamp paper and producing same attached to original award was merely to show that required stamp duty and penalty had been paid - It was not disputed that actual value of stamp used covers more than stamp duty and penalty required for document and, therefore, there was no difficulty in holding that award was admissible in evidence and could not be rejected on ground that proper duty and penalty had not been paid - Appeal dismissed.
