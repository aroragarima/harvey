Controller of Estate Duty Madras v Alladi Kuppuswamy

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 273; (1977) 3 SCC 385; AIR 1977 SC 2069; 1977 CTR 297; 1977 HLR 490; [1977] 108 ITR 439; 1977 (2) MLJ(SC) 30; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 721

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance, Coparcenary Interest, Aliyasantana Law, Coparcenary By Birth

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, s. 3 - Estate Duty Act, 1953, ss. 7 and 39 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Ambit and scope of ss. 7(1) and (2) of the Act - 'Y' died some time before the passing of the 1953 Act, but during his life time he had settled certain properties absolutely on his wife 'X' and he had also declared certain other properties to form part of the joint family properties - 'Y' was a member of the Hindu coparcenary consisting of himself, his wife and three sons - 'X' died few months before the passing of the 1956 Act - Revenue assessed the estate duty on the footing that as 'X' was a member of the Hindu coparcenary her interest in the joint family properties passed on her death to the other three sons and the value of this interest being 1/4th the heirs would be liable to pay estate duty on the value of the 1/4th share assessed at Rs. 2,02,271/ - Writ petition was filed - HC held that the interest of 'X' was not a coparcenary interest and as the said interest was incapable of valuation was not exigible to estate duty - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, on the facts and in the circumstances of the case, one-fourth share of the deceased in the joint family properties, to which she was entitled u/s. 3 of the 1937 Act, was correctly included in her estate as property deemed to pass on her death u/s. 7 of the 1953 Act.

Held, once a Hindu widow is held to have a coparcenary interest, then there would be no difficulty in treating her as a member of the Hindu coparcenary, in which case her interest could be easily valued according to the relevant provision. By virtue of the provisions of the 1937 Act a Hindu widow undoubtedly possesses a coparcenary interest as contemplated by s. 7(1) of the 1953 Act and she is also a member of a Hindu copercenary as envisaged by s. 7(2) of the 1953 Act. On the death of 'X', therefore, there was clearly a cesser of her interest and her interest merged in the copercenary property and by reason of the inclusive part of s. 7(1) of 1953 Act, it must be taken to have passed on her death and was hence exigible to estate duty. Since 'X' was a member of the copercenary, this interest of her which passed on her death was liable to be valued in accordance with the method provided by s. 39 of the 1953 Act. The judgment of the HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
