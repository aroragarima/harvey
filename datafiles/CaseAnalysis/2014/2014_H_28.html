Aayush Buildwell Private Limited v Haryana Urban Development Authority

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 120; (2014) 12 SCC 93; 2014 (104) ALR 209; 2014 (4) AWC 3814; JT 2014 (5) SC 136; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 598B; 2014 (123) RD 687; 2014(3) SCALE 82; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 181

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Institutional plots, Freehold

Summary: Land & Property - Minicipalities & Local Governments - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Institutional plots - Freehold - Entitled to - Respondent/authority issued advertisement for allotment of freehold institutional plots for Corporate Offices - Appellant applied for half an acre plot - Appellant gave earnest money and project report was submitted - Appellant's money was refunded without giving any reasons - Allotments were challenged before HC and interim stay was granted - Subsequently petition was allowed to be withdrawn by an order - Delhi Assam Roadways Corporation Ltd.,/applicant filed application in respect of allotments made by authority - Since no reply was received - Applicant moved to Central Information Commission - Respondent provided requisite information - Aggrieved by action on part of respondent appellant filed petition before HC - HC set aside allotments made to private respondents - Respondent filed petitions before SC and the same were disposed of - Hence instant appeals - Whether appellant was eligible to have allotment of plot in its favor, and further while setting aside process for allotment of plots, could it direct process afresh allowing ineligible candidates/parties to participate in said fresh process -

Held, it appeared that said order was passed by Court since Additional Solicitor General, on instructions, who appeared on behalf of HUDA, conceded to effect that half an acre of plot, as far as possible, should be made available to Delhi Assam Roadways Corporation Ltd - Order was passed on concession granted on behalf of HUDA - It appeared that in view of facts and circumstances of case and submissions made on behalf of respondents, respondent did not agree to concede it to that extent in this case - There was distinction in situation in passing said order and present order so passed by Court - Right of appellant has not been crystallized - No right could be conferred on appellant, granting allotment as has been prayed - Appellant has to comply with process followed by HUDA to allot plots in favor of the allottees and accordingly, if the appellant fulfills all criteria laid down by HUDA in process of allotment, HUDA was directed to consider its case for such allotment - Steps taken by appellant in accordance with process of HUDA and if criteria was being fulfilled by appellant, HUDA was directed to take necessary steps in matter for allotment in accordance with provisions of law - Appeals disposed of.
