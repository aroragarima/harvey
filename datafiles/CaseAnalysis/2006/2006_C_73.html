Midnapore Peoples Co-op. Bank Limited and Others v Chunilal Nanda and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 254; (2006) 5 SCC 399; AIR 2006 SC 2190; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 53; 2006 (3) CalLT(SC) 53; 2006 CRLJ 2903; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 209; JT 2006 (11) SC 203; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 804; 2006(6) SCALE 308; 2006 (8) SLR 708; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 986

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 17, 19, 19(1), 20 - Limitation Act, 1963, s.5, - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 215 - CPC, 1908, o. 43, r. 1, s. 2(9) - Enquiry proceedings - Single Judge held Enquiry Officer had not proceeded with due diligence - Appeal dismissed as not maintainable - S. 19 provided for appeals only against orders punishing a contemnor - Appeal dismissed by merely stating that delay of 728 days had not been properly explained - Appeal against - Where HC, in a contempt proceedings, renders a decision on merits of a dispute between parties, either by an interlocutory order or final judgment, whether it is appealable u/s.19 of Contempt of Courts Act, 1971? - Held, If HC decides an issue or makes any direction, relating to merits of dispute between parties, in a contempt proceedings - Order is open to challenge in an intra-court appeal or by seeking special leave to appeal under art.136 - No justification for direction by Single Judge in contempt proceedings, that too by an interlocutory order, that complainant should immediately and forthwith be reinstated into service of Bank - Order accordingly.
