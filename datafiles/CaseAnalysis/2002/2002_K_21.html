K. Varadharaj v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1647; (2002) 6 SCC 735; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1514; AIR 2002 SC 2953; 2002 (3) CCR 205; 2002 (3) Crimes 149; 2002 (6) CRJ 442; 2002 CRLJ 4089; JT 2002 (6) SC 189; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 801; 2002(6) SCALE 10; 2002 (4) SLT 799; 2002 (5) Supreme 369

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Tamil Nadu Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug Offenders, Forest Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum Grabbers Act, 1982 - Whether detaining authority is bound to take into consideration any bail application filed by detenu and any order passed by criminal court on such application? - Held, no - Placing of application for bail and order made are not always mandatory and would depend upon facts of each case - In present case, fact of detention was taken note of by detaining authority by reference to his remand order - Contents of bail application do not contain any vital material notice of which detaining authority had to take - However, public prosecutor had no objection to grant of bail and this opinion expressed by State was vital and should have been taken note of - Non-consideration of this fact, vitiates order of detention - Detention quashed - Appeal allowed.
