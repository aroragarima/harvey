S. N. Dhingra and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20317; (2001) 3 SCC 125; (2001) SCC (L&S) 539; AIR 2001 SC 1535; 2001 (90) FLR 500; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 258; JT 2001 (2) SC 362; 2001 LabIC 723; 2001(1) SCALE 532; 2001 (1) SCJ 586; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 770; 2001 (1) SLT 809; 2001 (1) Supreme 685; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 805

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Recruitment Rules, rr. 7 and 8(2) - Petition u/art. 32 by direct recruits to Delhi higher judicial service (DHJS), assails inclusion of respondents 5 to 8 in gradation list drawn up by High Court of Delhi - (A) Whether it was open for administrator to upgrade post of chief metropolitan magistrate (CMM) and include those upgraded posts in DHJS, so as to form a part of cadre post? - (B) Whether such appointments though nomenclature as "stopgap and fortuitous" can at all be held to be such in light of enunciation of those terminology in Rudra Kumar case 2000 Indlaw SC 2837? - (C) Whether fact that respondents though were appointed to DHJS, but having been posted as CMMs against whose orders appeal lay to court of district and sessions judge would make any difference? - Held, (A) Administrator by upgrading five posts of CMM to rank of DHJS and by including them in service has merely exercised his power u/r. 16, and therefore, appointees like respondents to those posts in service from Delhi judicial service must be held to be born in service from date of their appointment by virtue of order D/- 16-01-1986 - (B) Said case clearly indicated that whether a particular appointment is really fortuitous or stopgap has to be decided in facts and circumstances of case and any universal principle cannot be made for purpose - It would be a travesty of justice if their continuous appointment in service is not taken into account for purpose of their seniority, merely because of use of expression "stopgap and fortuitous" in order D/- 16-01-1986 - (C) Once appointment is made to higher judicial service, as in case in hand, then subsequent posting against some posts born in higher judicial service will not deprive appointees of benefits of continuous appointment against post merely because at a given point of time against their order an appeal lay to district and sessions judge, which might have been occupied by petitioners on being directly recruited in year 1988 - Continuous length of service of these respondents right from their appointment to higher judicial service in January 1986 should be basis on which their seniority has to be determined and HC therefore, was fully justified in including names of these respondents in gradation list that had been drawn up on 22-08-2000 - Petition dismissed.
