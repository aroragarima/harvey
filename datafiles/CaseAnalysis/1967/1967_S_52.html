Sowdagar Ahmed Khan v Income Tax Officer, Nellore

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 355; [1968] 70 ITR 79

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure To Disclose Material Facts

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Failure to disclose - Assessee failed to disclose facts in course of assessment proceedings for relevant A/Y - ITO detected that in relevant returns assessee had not shown income from property in names of his family - By reason of omission or failure on part of assessee to disclose fully and truly all basic and material facts necessary for assessment, income chargeable to tax had escaped assessment or was under-assessed - ITO wrote letter to assessee to provide details of cash credits for which assessee was required to give explanation as well as properties whose incomes was to be assessed in his hands - HC dismissed petition - Whether proceedings could be restrained by notices issued u/s. 34(1)(a) of the 1922 Act - Held, ITO had reason to believe that information material to assessment had been withheld, and on account of withholding information, income liable to tax had escaped assessment - It could not be inferred from mere production of books of account that there had been full disclosure of material facts necessary for assessment - It was duty of assessee to bring to notice of ITO particular items in books of account or portions of documents which were relevant - Appeals dismissed.
