Mahanadi Coal Fields Ltd. & Anr. v Mathias Oram & Ors

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 509; (2010) 11 SCC 269; 2010 (5) Bom.C.R. 675; JT 2010 (7) SC 352; 2010(7) SCALE 186; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 750

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Act, 1957, s. 11 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Land was acquired by Central Government - Thereafter, Central Government issued notification u/s. 11 of the Act vesting the acquired land and all rights therein in the petitioner company - However, respondents and others whose lands were acquired were never paid any compensation for their lands - Therefore, they filed writ petition before HC, claiming compensation for their lands - By the impugned judgment HC directed the Central Government and the petitioner to proceed forthwith in accordance with the provisions of the Act to determine the compensation payable to the land owners - Present SLP filed - Petitioner challenging HC order on the plea that it has no liability to pay compensation for the lands acquired by the Central Government since they are of no use for the purpose of mining operations nor are they likely to be needed in the near future as per the mining plan approved by the Central Government - Held, SLP remained pending for the last three years and respondents remain unpaid of the compensation for their lands - It was in these circumstances, SC requested Solicitor General for India, to assist the court on behalf of the Central Government - Scheme proposed by Solicitor General for India was agreed upon by the Central Government and the Coal Company and respondents also - Scheme approved but with certain clarifications and modifications - Order accordingly.
