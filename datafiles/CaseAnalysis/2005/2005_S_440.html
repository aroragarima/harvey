Harbans Kaur and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 157; (2005) 9 SCC 195; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1213; AIR 2005 SC 2989; 2005 CRLJ 2199; JT 2005 (3) SC 233; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 773; 2005(2) SCALE 457; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - IPC, 1860 - Sentencing - Common intention - Conviction under ss 323, 325 and 326 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction - S.34, IPC is applicable even if no injury has been caused by the particular accused himself - For applying s.34 it is not necessary to show some overt act on the part of the accused - Held, trial Court and HC were right in relying on evidence of prosecution witnesses - Custodial sentence of 4 years in place of 7 years as recorded by Courts below for offence relatable to s.326 r/w s.34 would meet ends of justice - - Appeal disposed of.
