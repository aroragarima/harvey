Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited and Others v Super Highway Services and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 137; (2010) 3 SCC 321; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 703; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 326; JT 2010 (2) SC 446; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 278; 2010(2) SCALE 508; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 1053

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Termination of agreement - Petitioner Corporation entered into an agreement with the respondent no.1 for the retail sale or supply of petrol, diesel, motor oils, grease and such other products - On checking, petitioner found that sample of High Speed Diesel (HSD) was contaminated and failed the marker test - Petitioner gave notice to respondent no. 1 for nozzle test which was not attended by respondent no. 1 - Petitioner cancelled the dealership on account of the failed marker test - Respondent no. 1 filed a writ petition praying for quashing entire proceedings arising out of the market test - Single Judge allowed the respondent no. 1 writ petition and held that since the retesting had been done without proper notice to the respondent no.1, same had caused severe prejudice to the respondent no.1 and the order of termination of the dealership agreement could not, therefore, be sustained - Hence, present petition - Whether Single Judge's order could be upheld and termination of dealership agreement could not be sustained? - Held, yes - In order to justify the action taken to terminate such an agreement, the concerned authority has to act fairly and in complete adherence to the rules/guidelines framed for the said purpose - Non-service of notice to the aggrieved person before termination of his dealership agreement also offends the well- established principle that no person should be condemned unheard - It was the duty of the petitioner to ensure that the respondent no.1 was given a hearing or at least serious attempts were made to serve him with notice of the proceedings before terminating his agreement - Termination of the dealership agreement of the respondent no. 1 was arbitrary, illegal and in violation of the principles of natural justice - HC's order upheld - Petition dismissed
