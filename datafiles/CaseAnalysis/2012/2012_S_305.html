Om Prakash and another v State of Jharkhand and another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 315; (2012) 12 SCC 72; JT 2012 (9) SC 642; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 433; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 662; 2012(9) SCALE 291; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 203 and 302 r/w s. 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197, 197(2) and (3), 482 - Fake encounter - Cognizance - Prosecution - Challenged - Appellant (complainant) filed a complaint before Trial court against respondent (accused) (Dy. Superintendent of Police (Dy.SP)) and other Police personnel, alleging that deceased son of appellant was killed in a fake encounter by respondents (accused) and allegedly committed offence u/ss. 120-B, 203 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Aggrieved respondents filed a petition u/s. 482, before HC to quash criminal proceeding pending against them, as no prior sanction as required u/s. 197 CrPC was not obtained - Respondents submitted before HC that 'N', who lodged a FIR against deceased and others, alleged that deceased had at his house to threaten him and to force him to yield to their ransom demand - Dy.SP traced decease and others and asked them to surrender, but they fired at Police - HC by impugned order allowed petition filed by Dy.SP and dismissed petition of other respondents (Police personnel) holding that no notification issued u/s. 197(3) of CrPC was produced by respondents to show that they were protected against prosecution - Aggrieved appellant and respondents filed instant appeals - Appellant (complainant) contended that deceased had received injuries on chest, from close range and that falsity of encounter theory was evident because none of the members of police party received injuries -Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, National Human Rights Commission (NHRC) directed CID to conduct an inquiry - CID had conducted the inquiry and recorded statements of brother of deceased and independent witnesses and submitted report - Doctors' report stated that there was no charring, blackening etc. found on body which confirmed that bullets were fired from some distance - 'N' (complainant), who was attacked by deceased detailed account as to how criminals fired at his house and fled away from there - After considering inquest report, postmortem report, forensic laboratory report, statements of independent witnesses, statement of complainant 'N', statement of brother of deceased and antecedents of deceased and other attendant circumstances, report concluded that encounter was genuine - There was no dispute about the fact that NHRC has accepted said report and come to a conclusion that instant case was not a case of fake encounter - There was on record the detailed seizure memo which speaks about the recovery of blood stained soil - Inquest report, which was reproduced in report of CID confirmed that deceased had received bleeding injuries - Deceased was wanted in cases of 2002 and 2003 - He was absconding when he died - A report was required to be filed to inform Court that he was dead - In those circumstances three challans were prepared and filed, which were final forms - That was not done purposely with mala fide intention to create record against deceased - Magistrate conducted the inquest and CID had fully investigated and submitted its report stating that it was a genuine encounter - NHRC was satisfied with the postmortem - Thus, it was not possible to infer that post-mortem was not video-graphed because Police wanted to suppress something - It was cleared that instant case was not a case of false encounter - Case of complainant that police were guilty of killing deceased in cold blood in fake encounter was rejected - In instant case, Police should get protection given u/s. 197 of CrPC because the acts complained of were so integrally connected with or attached to their office as to be inseparable from it - It was not possible to conclude that protection granted u/s. 197 of CrPC was used by Police personnel in instant case as a cloak for killing deceased in cold blood - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 203 and 302 r/w s. 34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197, 197(2) and (3), 482 - Prosecution - Protection - Entitled to - Whether prior sanction was required to prosecute Police personnel - Held, notification dt.16-5-1980 issued by State which extended protection of ss. (2) of s. 197 of CrPC to all the members of Police force as it included both officers and men - whether sanction was necessary or not might be determined from stage to stage - If, at the outset, defence established that act purported to be done was in execution of official duty, complaint would have to be dismissed on that ground - whether sanction was necessary or not had to be decided from stage to stage - That question might arouse at any stage of proceeding and it might arouse at the inception - There might be unassailable and unimpeachable circumstances on record which might establish at the outset that Police officer or public servant was acting in performance of his official duty and was entitled to protection given u/s. 197 of CrPC - It was not possible to hold that in such a case, Court could not look into any documents produced by accused or concerned public servant at the inception - Previous sanction was a precondition for taking cognizance of offence and, therefore, there was no requirement that accused should wait till the charges were framed to raise that plea - Instant case was not a case where Police might be held guilty of killing deceased in cold blood in a fake encounter -Police have to do their legal duty of arresting criminals, they have to protect themselves against attacks - Unless unimpeachable evidence was on record to establish that Police action was indefensible, mala fide and vindictive, they could not be subjected to prosecution - Sanction Should be a precondition to their prosecution - It affords necessary protection to such police personnel - Plea regarding sanction could be raised at inception - Instant case was one of such cases where proceedings initiated against police personnel need to be quashed - Appeal of complainant was dismissed - Appeal of respondent was allowed - Impugned order of HC was upheld - Appeals disposed of.
