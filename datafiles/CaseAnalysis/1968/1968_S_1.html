Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation v Balwant Regular Motor Service, Amravati and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 489; AIR 1969 SC 329; 1969 (71) BomLR 438; 1969 MahLJ 890; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 808

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39, O. 47, r. 1, r. 9, O. r.1, O. r.9, O.39 O.47 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226- Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 46, 48, 57, 58(1)(a) - Status Quo - Statutory Rule - Maintainability - Appeals were brought by certificate from judgment of HC in Special Civil Applications Nos. 540-570 to 572, 575 to 596 and 634 of 1967 filed u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution - Appellant was State Road Transport Corporation - Respondent No. 1 who was private stage carriage operator, along with other such private operators, had applied for renewal of stage carriage permits which they were holding and which permits were to expire on 31-3- 1961 - Provincial Transport Services had been also operating stage carriage service in adjoining and nearby areas and had made applications sometime in January, 1961 for grant of substantive permits for same routes - Provincial Transport Services had published scheme u/s. 68-D of the Act under which it was proposed to take over several routes in region including routes in respect of which renewal applications were made by appellant and private operators - Scheme was approved by CM of State - Approval was challenged by private operators in Special Civil Application No. 86 of 1962 in HC - HC by its order dt. 29-8-1963 ,30-8-1963 passed in that case, HC quashed scheme with direction that matter should be reconsidered by approving authority - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, RTA did not make any modification and it must therefore be deemed that grant of permit was made in accordance with application of appellant which expressly declared period of validity of permit applied for to be of five years - Reference was made to r. 80 of Bombay Motor Vehicles Rules which provided for forms of application for permits and to Form P. St. S. A. prescribed under that Rule which required application to mention for what period stage carriage permit was to be granted - Court opined that order of RT Adt. 10-5-1965 could not be held to be illegal merely because period of validity of permit had not been expressly mentioned therein - Court further held that HC was in error in holding that order of R. T. A. was legally invalid either because period of validity of permit or date of commencement was not mentioned - Appeals allowed.
