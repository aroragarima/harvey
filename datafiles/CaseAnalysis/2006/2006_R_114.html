Bharat Coking Coal Limited v Rashtriya Colliery Mazdoor Sangh

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1428; (2006) 2 SCC 329; AIR 2006 SC 946; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 25; 2006 (5) AWC 4568; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 250; JT 2006 (1) SC 411; 2006(1) SCALE 284; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 391; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 792

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: CLRA Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10, 2(a) - Workmen - Reinstatement in service with back wages - Succeed - Central Govt. in exercise of power u/s. 10 of the 1947 Act referred disputes 'Whether Action of management in denying employment to 'X' and 21 others with effect from dt. 9-7-1977 was justified, if not, to what relief concerned workmen were entitled', for adjudication to Central Govt. Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal held, in favor of workmen declaring them to be workmen of principal employer, namely, Management and directing for their reinstatement in service with effect from dates of references with 75% back wages - Aggrieved by this order management filed petitions before HC, which were dismissed - Management filed appeals before DB of HC - DB held, decision of Single Judge was unexceptionable and relied on in Secretary, Haryana State Electricity Board v. Suresh & Ors. etc. 1959 Indlaw SC 102 to hold that where engagement of workmen by contractor was camouflage to conceal real relationship between principal employer and workmen, then also workmen employed through unlicensed contractor were liable to be treated as workmen of principal employee - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order of DB was justified - Held, delay in case at hand has been so culpable as to disentitle appellants for any relief, although HC has opined that there was delay of 7 to 9 years in raising dispute before Tribunal but SC find HC factually not correct and employment of appellants was terminated sometime in 1985-86 or 1986-87, relied on in Daily Rated Casual Employees Under P & T Department v. Union of India 1987 Indlaw SC 28447 - Dept. was formulating scheme to accommodate casual laborers and appellants were justified in awaiting outcome thereof, on dt. 16-1-1990 they were refused to be accommodated in scheme - Hence, on dt. 28-12-1990 they initiated proceedings under the Act followed by conciliation proceedings and then dispute was referred to Industrial Tribunal cum-Labor Court and SC do not think that appellants deserve to be non suited on ground of delay - Appeals allowed.
