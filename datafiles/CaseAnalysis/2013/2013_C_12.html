T. P. Vishnu Kumar v Canara Bank, Tiruppur and others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 745; (2013) 10 SCC 652; 2013 (97) ALR 753; 2013 (2) AWC 2116; JT 2013 (3) SC 73; 2014 (2) MahLJ 436; 2014 (1) MPLJ 626; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 977

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Summary: Banking & Finance - Constitution - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 17, 20 - Constitution of India, 1950, s. 226 - Invoking of art. 226 of constitution - Legality - Respondent No. 1 filed an OA for a decree directing defendants therein for recovery of amount - Petitioner and respondent Nos. 2 to 6 sought direction to produce the extract of accounts as well as documents relating to banking transactions - Tribunal rejected the petitions - Against the said order petitioner filed writ petition - Single Judge passed an elaborate order and allowed the writ petition - On appeals, those were allowed holding that petitioner had not availed of the alternative remedy available u/s. 20 of the Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was not justified in invoking jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution to examine that the rejection of the applications by the Tribunal was correct -

Held, powers, which were conferred on the civil court, now conferred on a Tribunal u/s. 17 of the Act thereby it could deal with applications from banks and financial institutions for recovery of debts due to such banks and financial institutions - SC was of the view when a specific remedy was made available to the aggrieved party u/s. 20 of the Act, Single Judge in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution, was not justified in interfering with the orders passed by the Tribunal - Powers of the HC u/art. 226 could not be invoked in the matter of recovery of dues under the Act, unless there was any statutory violation resulting in prejudice to the party or where such proceedings or action was wholly arbitrary, unreasonable and unfair - When the Act itself provided for a mechanism, by an appeal u/s. 20 of the Act, HC was not justified in invoking jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution to examine that the rejection of the applications by the Tribunal was correct or not - Petitioner and the contesting respondents have no case that either the bank or the Tribunal had violated any statutory provisions by rejecting their applications - Writ petition was preferred against the rejection of applications and the same were entertained by Single Judge and decided on merits and which in our view was impermissible while exercising its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution - If the correctness or otherwise of each and every interim order passed by Tribunal, was going to be tested in a writ court, it would only defeat the object and purpose of establishing such Tribunal - Due to the intervention of writ court, the matter got delayed for four years defeating the very purpose and object of the Act - Petitions dismissed.
