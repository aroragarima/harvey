Arun Nivalaji More v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 373; (2006) 12 SCC 613; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 221; AIR 2006 SC 2886; 2006 CRLJ 4057; 2006 (3) JCC 1388; JT 2006 (7) SC 299; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 19; 2006(7) SCALE 532; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 299, 300, 302, 304 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 313, 315 - Appellant was convicted u/s. 304 Part I and sentence of 7 years RI and a fine of Rs. 200/- imposed by Additional Sessions Judge - HC altered conviction from 304 Part I to s.302 and he was sentenced to imprisonment for life - Appeal against - Whether offence committed by an accused would fall under one of clauses of s. 304 or u/s. 302? - Held, prosecution has established beyond any shadow of doubt that appellant caused an injury by knife on left hypochondrium - Injury inflicted by appellant was clearly intended by him and it was not an accidental or unintentional injury - Medical evidence established that injury was sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - Offence committed by appellant is clearly covered by clause Thirdly of s.300 - Offence committed by appellant is one u/s. 302 and not u/s. 304 Part I - Appeals dismissed.
