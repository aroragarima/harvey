Union Public Service Commission v Girish Jayanti Lal Vaghela and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 26; (2006) 2 SCC 482; AIR 2006 SC 1165; 2006 (5) AWC 4794; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 640; 2006 (2) ESC 183; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 360; JT 2006 (2) SC 137; 2006 (1) MLJ(SC) 156; 2006(2) SCALE 115; 2006 (2) SCJ 207; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1006; 2006 (2) SLR 298; 2006 (2) Supreme 221; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 699

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1925, Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 16, 309, 310, 311(2) - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1925 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Central Civil Service (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 2(h) - Upper age limit for making direct recruitment - Respondent was appointed as Drugs Inspector on short term contract basis on a fixed salary for a period of six months from date of joining or till the date candidate selected by UPSC joined duty on regular basis - Refusal of Administrator to grant age relaxation certificate - Whether a person working on a short term contract basis can be said to be a Govt. servant? - Held, respondent no 1 cannot be said to be a Govt. servant as he was working on contract basis and, therefore, he was not eligible for any relaxation in upper age limit - Appeal allowed.
