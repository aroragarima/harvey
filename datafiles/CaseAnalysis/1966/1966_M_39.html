State of Gujarat v Messrs Raipur Manufacturing Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 297; AIR 1967 SC 1066; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 618; 1967 (19) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1953, s. 2(6) - Appellant engaged in manufacturing and selling cotton textiles - Sale of (1) Old containers, cans, boxes etc., discarded stores, machinery and iron scrap and miscellaneous discarded items, such as, cotton ropes', chindis (rags) etc.; (2) Kolsi (cinders), waste caustic liquor; (3) Coal - Whether turnover from turnover of goods sold by the Company was taxable? - Mere sale of commodity which a Company requires for the purpose of its business and which has been purchased for use in that business will not justify an inference that a business of selling that commodity was intended, unless there are circumstances existing at the time when the commodity was purchased or which have come into existence later which establish such an intention - Burden of proving that the Company was carrying on business of selling coal lay upon the Sales Tax authorities and if they made no investigation and have come to the conclusion mere because of the frequency and the volume of the sales, the inference cannot be sustained - Held, in disposing of miscellaneous old and discarded items such as stores, machinery, iron scrap, cans, boxes, cotton ropes, rags etc. the Company cannot be called as carrying on business of selling those items of goods and hence turnover not taxable - Further held, 'Kolsi' and 'waste caustic liquor' are by-product or a subsidiary product in the course of manufacture and the sale thereof is incidental to the business of the Company and the turnover in respect of both 'kolsi' and 'waste caustic liquor' would be liable to sales tax - Appeal partly allowed.
