Banchhanidhi Rath v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 546; (1972) 4 SCC 781; AIR 1972 SC 843; 1972 (38) CutLT 267; 1972 LabIC 431; 1971 (1) SLR 333; 1972 UJ 350

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Authority to superannuate, Privity of contract

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Authority to superannuate - Privity of contract - Legality - Appellant (petitioner/headmaster) filed a petition for quashing the order passed by Inspector of Schools, which stated that date of birth of appellant as recorded in service registers was 1-1-1908 and since the appellant exceeded the age of 58 years he was requested to hand over charge of his duties by 24-5-1969 to 'M', senior-most trained graduate Asst. teacher - HC by impugned judgment dismissed the petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it is manifest that the appellant could not enforce a contract of employment in an application u/art. 226 of Constitution. The appellant did not rely on any rule of the management of the institution that the appellant would continue in service as long as the appellant was physically fit and mentally alert. The appellant alleged a custom to that effect in the petition. Custom of such a nature cannot be enforced in an application u/art. 226 of Constitution. The management of the school was by resolution passed over to the Govt. The Government was giving grant-in-aid to the school. The Govt. therefore managed the School. The Govt. made uniform policy that teachers should be retired on attaining the age of 58 years. There is no right to remain in service. If a right is claimed in terms of a contract such a right cannot be enforced in a writ petition. HC was justified in dismissing the application. Appeal dismissed.
