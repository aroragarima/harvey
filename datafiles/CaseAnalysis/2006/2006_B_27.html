State of Karnataka and Others v Balaji Computers and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 991; (2007) 2 SCC 743; 2006(14) SCALE 240; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Contemporanea Expositio, Tax Payable, Exemption Notification, Rate Of Tax, Single Point Tax, Turnover Tax, Power Of State Government, Market Fee, Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam Act, 1964

Summary: Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 6-B, 8-A - Turnover tax is imposed - The respondents are registered dealers - Held, parts of computer by employing legal fiction need to be treated as computer under Sr. No.20 (i) of the pt. 'C' of the Sch. 2 of the Act - When language of the statute is plain and unambiguous, the method of contemporanea expositio need not be employed - Plain construction of the statute leads to a clear conclusion that the legislature intended to exempt computer and parts of computer and computer peripherals from levy of turnover tax - No interference is called for in the well reasoned impugned judgment of the High Court
