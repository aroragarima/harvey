State of West Bengal v Gaurangalal Chatterjee

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1693; (1993) 3 SCC 1; 1994 (23) ALR 33; 1993 (2) ARBLR 95; 1993 (2) CCC 173; JT 1993 (3) SC 394; 1993(2) SCALE 798; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 640; 1993 (2) UJ 67

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 39(1) and 39(2) - Second appeal - Single Judge by order revoked the authority of the Chief Engineer to act as an arbitrator and directed the person suggested by the respondent to act as the sole arbitrator - Whether appeal was maintainable against order passed by Single Judge u/s. 39(1) either u/s. 39(2) or under Letters patent jurisdiction? - Held, question answered in case of Union of India v. Mohindra Supply Company that no second appeal lay u/s. 39(2) against a decision given by a Single Judge u/s. 39(1) - Also in view of bar created by s. 39(2) debarring an second appeal from an order passed in appeal under sub-s.(1) the 'conclusion was inevitable that it was so done with a view to restrict the right of appeal within strict limits defined by s. 39' - High Court has appointed a retired Chief Engineer and not a non-technical man - No allegation has been made against him - Therefore, the order of the Single Judge also does not suffer from any infirmity - View taken by the Division Bench dismissing the appeal as not maintainable appears to be well founded - Appeal dismissed.
