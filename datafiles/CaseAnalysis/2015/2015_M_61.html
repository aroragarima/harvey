Bhanushali Housing Cooperative Society Limited v Mangilal and others

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R.K. Agrawal, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 492; (2015) 10 SCC 277; AIR 2015 SC 3016; 2015(5) ALL MR 898; 2015 (112) ALR 451; 2015 (3) JabLJ 76; JT 2015 (7) SC 13; 2016 (2) MPLJ 367; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 787; 2015(8) SCALE 209; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 689; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 170

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Contract For Sale, Co-operative Societies Act, 1912, Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, Deceased Member, Past Member, Andhra Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1964, Madhya Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1957

Summary: Trusts & Associations - M.P. Cooperative Societies Act, 1960, s. 64 - Business transactions - Disputes - Deputy Registrar decreed specific performance of contract entered between parties and aggrieved by said order, respondents preferred appeal but the same was dismissed by Joint Registrar - Aggrieved, respondents filed second appeal before Tribunal wherein orders passed by Deputy Registrar and Joint Registrar were set aside holding that dispute raised by purchaser/appellant-Society could not be made subject matter of proceeding u/s. 64 of the Act - Aggrieved, purchaser/appellant-Society filed writ petition but the same was dismissed by DB of HC concurring with view taken by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal by purchaser/appellant-Society - Whether dispute arising out of contract for sale and purchase of immovable property owned by respondents was amenable to adjudication u/s. 64 of the Act.

Held, purchase of land for being used in the manner set out in objects of Society is one of the facets of the business that the Society undertakes. Such purchase is directly linked to the object of developing the acquired land for allotment of house sites to the members of the Society. Hence, there is a clear and discernible nexus between acquisition/purchase of land and the object of providing house sites to the members which under the circumstances happens to be the main business of the society. Hence, dispute arising out the purchase of the land owned by the respondents was, a dispute touching the business of purchaser/appellant-Society. However, it was single transaction where under the respondents had agreed to sell to the purchaser/appellant-Society a parcel of land to the Society, for use by the Society in terms of the objects for which it is established. Further, respondents were not in the business of selling land as a commercial or business activity and hence, such transaction without any business element in same could not constitute 'business transaction' within the meaning of s. 64(1)(c). Hence, appeal is dismissed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Any activity in order to constitute business should be systematic and continuous and 'business transactions' are not just solitary transaction between a society, on the one hand, and a third party, on the other, which would bring any dispute arising out of any such transaction within the purview of s. 64(1)(c). The dispute should be between parties who have had a series of transaction, each one constituting a business transaction in order that the provisions of s. 64 are attracted and a dispute arising out any such transaction brought within its purview.
