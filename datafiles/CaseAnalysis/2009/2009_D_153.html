Bharat Amratlal Kothari And Another v Dosukhan Samadkhan Sindhi & Others

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1530; (2010) 1 SCC 234; AIR 2010 SC 475; 2010 CRLJ 379; JT 2009 (14) SC 102; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 102; 2009(13) SCALE 563; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 662; 2010 (1) UC 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preliminary Hearing, Postgraduate, Bombay Animal Preservation Act, 1954

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960, s. 11(1)(d) and 35(4) - Bombay Animal Preservation Act, 1954, ss. 5, 6 and 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 279 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 451 and 457 - Custody of cattle - Appellant No. 1 (Animal Right Activist) filed complaint for alleged commission of offences punishable u/s. 279 of IPC, s. 11(1)(d) of Prevention of Cruelty to Animals Act, 1960 and ss. 5, 6 and 8 of Bombay Animal Preservation Act, 1954 - Respondent Nos. 1 to 6, claiming to be owners of live stock filed application u/s. 451 and 457 of CrPC for custody of live stock - Application rejected - Petition u/art. 226 of Constitution filed before HC - Single judge convicted respondent Nos. 1 to 6 u/s. 11(1)(d) of Act and imposed fine and quashed complaint registered by appellant No. 1 and also issued certain directions - (A) Whether HC justified in convicting respondent No. 1 to 6? - Respondent Nos. 1 to 6 were not accused of commission of any offence before HC - Held, Single Judge convicted respondent Nos. 1 to 6 though none of them allegedly committed any offence either under Act or under IPC or under Bombay Act while on the other hand HC quashed the complaint - Findings contradictory - Conviction of respondent Nos. 1 to 6 would have to be regarded as without jurisdiction, unauthorized, unwarranted and illegal and set aside - (B) Quashing of complaint by HC - Legality - Held, complaint filed by appellant No. 1 was quashed without taking into account contents thereof or discussing them - A relief for which the accused had not approached the HC not asked for could not have been granted in a petition filed seeking custody of live stock notwithstanding wide amplitude of power available u/art. 226 of Constitution - Single Judge quashed the complaint contrary to well settled principles governing quashing of a complaint - (C) Whether HC justified in directing appellant No. 1 to pay by way of compensation and cost? -Respondent Nos. 1 to 6 had not prayed that appellant No. 1 be directed to pay compensation and cost to each of them - Held, every petition u/art. 226 of Constitution must contain a relief clause - Approach of the HC in granting relief not prayed for cannot be approved by SC - No justification in directing appellant No. 1 to pay compensation and cost to respondents - Direction set aside - (D) Whether respondent Nos. 1 to 6 entitled to relief of interim custody of live stock seized pursuant to filing of complaint? - Held, respondent Nos. 1 to 6 are owners of the live stock seized - Keeping live stock in custody of respondent No. 8 would not serve any purpose - Respondent Nos. 1 to 6 would be entitled to interim custody of live stock seized in case during pendency of trial, of course, subject to certain conditions - Appeal partly allowed.
