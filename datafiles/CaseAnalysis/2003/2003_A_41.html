Jamal Uddin Ahmad v Abu Saleh Najmuddin and another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 252; (2003) 4 SCC 257; AIR 2003 SC 1917; 2003 (3) AWC 1744; JT 2003 (2) SC 320; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 180; 2003 (2) KLT 638; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 215; 2003(2) SCALE 518; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 473; 2003 (2) SLT 591; 2003 (2) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Practice and Procedure - Representative of the People Act, 1951, ss.80, 81, - Constitution, arts. 329, 214, 216 - Election petition - Whether, the HC is at all competent to frame rules making provision for receiving the election petitions and whether, in the absence of any provision specifying the person who is competent to receive election petitions presented to the HC, no petition can be presented? - Held, the HC and the Chief Justice, as the case may be, have the power to frame rules and issue directions regulating their own affairs and conduct of business in the HC Even in the absence of rules or directions if there is any practice prevailing under which the administrative or ministerial functions of the HC are being performed, unless there be something inherently objectionable or impermissible about it the same should be allowed to prevail if it satisfies the test of being incidental and essential to the performance of the main judicial functions of the HC - Therefore, presentation of an election petition to the Stamp Reporter of the HC of Gauhati is a valid presentation - Appeals Dismissed.
