Hari Shankar Sharma and Others v Artificial Limbs Manufacturing Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20539; (2002) SCC (L&S) 120; (2002) 1 SCC 337; AIR 2002 SC 226; 2002 (1) AWC 21; 2002 (1) CGLJ 94; 2002 (1) CLR 13; 2002 (100) FJR 102; 2002 (92) FLR 14; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 282; JT 2001 (9) SC 650; 2002 LabIC 131; 2002 (1) LLJ 237; 2002 (1) LLN 703; 2001(8) SCALE 282; 2002 (1) SLR 228; 2001 (8) SLT 185; 2001 (8) Supreme 458

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, s. 46 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Respondent No. 1 is a Govt. of India undertaking - Respondent No. 1 set up a canteen for its employees - Agreements were entered into between respondent No. 1 and different contractors (at relevant time respondent No. 2) for canteen - Appellants were employed by several of contractors and had been serving in canteen for several years - Appellants raised industrial dispute claiming to be regular workmen of respondent No. 1 - Labour Court held that appellants were not employees of respondent No. 1 but were employees of respondent No. 2 - High Court dismissed writ petition - Hence, present appeal - Whether appellants are employees of respondent No. 1? - Even if agreement had contained a condition that contractor must retain old employees, it would not necessarily mean that those employees were employees of establishment - Terms of agreement are inconclusive - Respondent No. 2 (contractor) stated that he used to supervise and control his employees and pay their salaries - Prescribed procedure for appointing employees of respondent No. 1 was not applied to them - Labour Court found that appellants were unable to prove that respondent No. 1 exercised any control or supervision over employees of contractor - Held, finding of Labour Court that appellants were not employees of respondent No. 1 cannot be termed to be perverse - It would have been inappropriate for HC u/art. 226 to re-appreciate evidence and come to a different factual conclusion - Appeal dismissed.
