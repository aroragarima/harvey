Haryana State Agricultural Marketing Board v Subhash Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 329; (2006) SCC (L&S) 455; (2006) 2 SCC 794; AIR 2006 SC 1263; 2006 (5) AWC 5162; JT 2006 (3) SC 393; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1053; 2006(2) SCALE 614; 2006 (3) SCJ 701; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 640; 2006 (2) SLR 780; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1226

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Agricultural Produce Markets Board (Officers and Staff Establishment) Regulations, 1984

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1) (c), 2 (oo) (bb), 25-G - Respondent was appointed on contractual basis - Termination of services - Held, if legal right has been derived by respondent herein to continue in service in terms of provisions of Act under which he is governed, then only, question of depriving him of any status or privilege would arise - According to appellant he has worked only for 208 days - Appeal allowed.
