Dharam Deo Yadav v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 242; (2014) 5 SCC 509; 2014 CRLJ 2371; JT 2014 (5) SC 31; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 435; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 396; 2014(4) SCALE 730; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 650

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 27, 8 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 34, 364, 366, 394, 411 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused/appellant u/ss. 366, 302, 201, 394, 364, 411 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court found appellant guilty of offences and awarded him death sentence - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed appeal and confirmed death sentence awarded by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Courts below were sustainable -

Held, DNA sample got from the skeleton matched with the blood sample of father of the deceased and all sampling and testing was done by experts whose scientific knowledge and experience was not been doubted in proceedings - No reason to discard evidence of PW19, PW20 and PW21 - Prosecution had succeeded in showing that skeleton recovered from house of appellant was that of victim/'D' daughter of complainant and it was none other than appellant, who had strangulated 'D' to death and buried dead body in his house - Appellant in his examination had denied prosecution case completely, but prosecution had succeeded in proving guilt beyond reasonable doubt - Often, false answers given by appellant in the statement might offer an additional link in chain of circumstances to complete chain - Both Trial Court as well as HC have correctly appreciated oral and documentary evidence in case and correctly recorded conviction - SC laid down three tests, namely, Crime Test, Criminal Test and RR Test - So far as case was concerned, both Crime Test and Criminal Test was satisfied as against appellant - It was pointed out that it would not be possible for SC to come to conclusion that crime was committed in barbaric manner and, hence instant case would not fall under category of rarest of rare - Due to lack of any evidence with regard to manner in which crime was committed, case would not fall under category of rarest of rare case - SC inclined to commute death sentence to life and award 20 years of rigorous imprisonment, over and above period already undergone by appellant, without any remission, would meet ends of justice - Death sentence was altered to life - Appeal disposed of.
