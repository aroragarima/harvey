Parmar Kanaksinh Bhagwansinh (Dead) By L.R’S. v Makwana Shanabhai Bhikhabhai and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 691; (1995) 2 SCC 501; AIR 1995 SCW 188; JT 1995 (1) SC 103; 1995 (2) RCJ 401; 1995 (1) RCR(Rent) 381; 1995 (1) RentLR 227; 1994(5) SCALE 169; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 423

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 2, 4, 70 and 85 A - (A) Whether leasehold of a tenant (lessee) in a property merge in mortgage security possible? - Doctrine of merger - Held, there cannot be a merger of a lease and a mortgage in respect of same property since neither of them is a higher or lesser estate than other - (B) Whether plea of tenancy on an agricultural land can be raised by a defendant under Act and seeks a reference of that issue by Civil Court ? - Further held, s. 70 imposes a duty on Mamlatdar to decide whether a person is an agriculturist or a tenant or a protected tenant or a permanent tenant when such person claims to be so under that Act - Therefore, provisions in Act give no scope or room to think that plea of tenancy if raised by defendants in a suit in a Civil Court, same could be decided by Civil Court - Appeal dismissed.
