Union of India and Another v T.V. Patel

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 391; (2007) 4 SCC 785; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1331; 2007 (6) AWC 6182; 2007 (4) KLT 370; 2007(6) SCALE 9; 2007 (8) SCJ 419; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 373; 2007 (5) SLR 430

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Summary: Whether a copy of the advice tendered by the UPSC is to be furnished along with the order of penalty or before the passing of an order imposing final penalty? - Appeal against the order of High Court's holding that the advice tendered by the UPSC, a copy of which should be made available to the delinquent officer so as to enable him to afford an effective representation - Held, the provisions of Article 320(3)(c) of the Constitution of India are not mandatory and they do not confer any rights on the public servant so that the absence of consultation or any irregularity in consultation process or furnishing a copy of the advice tendered by the UPSC, if any, does not afford the delinquent government servant a cause of action in a court of law - Appeal allowed.
