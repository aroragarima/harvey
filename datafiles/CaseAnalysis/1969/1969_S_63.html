Â Shri Prithvi Cotton Mills Limited and another v Broach Borough Municipality and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vishishtha Bhargava, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 296; (1969) 2 SCC 283; AIR 1970 SC 192; 2000 (123) E.L.T. 3; [1971] 79 ITR 136; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 388

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f),(g) and 265 - Gujarat Imposition of Taxes by Municipalities (Validation) Act, 1963, r. 350 A - Gujarat Municipalities Act, s. 99 - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, ss. 8, 7 and 73 - Whether Gujarat Imposition of Taxes by Municipalities (Validation) Act, 1963 and Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act are valid? - Held, if legislature has power over subject-matter and competence to make a valid law, it can at any time make such a valid law and make it retrospectively so as to bind even past transactions - Validity of a validating law depends upon whether legislature possesses competence which it claims over subject matter and whether in making validation it removes defect which courts had found in existing law and makes adequate provisions in validating law for a valid imposition of tax - Gujarat Municipalities Act, s. 99 of Act examined - Held, exercise of power by legislature was valid because legislature does possess power to levy a tax on lands and buildings based on capital value thereof and in validating levy on that basis - Appeal dismissed.
