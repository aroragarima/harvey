Borosil Glass Works Limited Employees Union v D. D. Bambode and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2946; (2001) 1 SCC 350; (2001) SCC (L&S) 997; AIR 2001 SC 378; 2001 (88) FLR 343; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 278; 2001 LabIC 99; 2001 (1) LLJ 171; 2001 (1) LLN 811; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 144; 2000(7) SCALE 713; 2000 (6) SLR 382; 2000 (8) SLT 574; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 187; 2000 (8) Supreme 166; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 28

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Trade Union Act, 1926, ss. 10(b) and 28(1A) - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1956 - Whether High Court was justified in disposing of petition of appellant by interpreting s. 28(1A) and holding that even a person who has applied to become a member can apply u/s. 28(1A)? - S. 28(1 A) has been incorporated to ensure that internal disputes in a trade union get decided - It can be only invoked by a person who has been a member of such registered trade union for a period of not less than 6 months - Held, interpretation given by HC leads to requirement of a person being a member for 6 months being rendered nugatory - A dispute whether a person should or should not be admitted as a member is not a dispute falling within s. 28(1A), it would always be open to such persons to approach a Civil Court for resolution of their dispute - Judgment of HC cannot be sustained and is set aside - Appeal allowed.
