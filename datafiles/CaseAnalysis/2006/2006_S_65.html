Sanjay Kumar And Others v Narinder Verma And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1403; (2006) 6 SCC 467; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 197; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 183; 2006(5) SCALE 441; 2007 (3) SLR 64; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 59

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Jammu &Kashmir Persons with Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection of Right & Full Participation) Act, 1998, Jammu & Kashmir Power Development Department (Subordinate) Service Recruitment Rules, 1981

Summary: Service - Jammu & Kashmir Power Development Department (Subordinate) Service Recruitment Rules, 1981 - Selection process - Recruitment - Minimum qualification - Higher starting pay - Succeed - Advertisement was issued inviting applications for post of Junior Engineers (Elect) Grade-II in grade of Rs. 1400-2300/- Several candidates holding either Diploma or Degree, as prescribed applied for post, called for interviews for selection - Result of selection process was published, out of 300 selected candidates, 153 were Degree holders and 147 were Diploma holders - Some of unsuccessful Degree holders challenged Selection list by writ petition before Single Judge - Single Judge held, criteria adopted had been made applicable uniformly to all candidates, 80 points had been given for educational qualifications and 20 marks had been assigned for viva voce, which was proper method of selection and no objection could be taken thereto and dismissed writ petitions - On appeal, DB allowed appeals and held, Degree holders being higher in qualification and that Executive while prescribing eligibility qualification has not prescribed method and procedure to assess suitability and merit of candidates possessing un-equal or superior qualification to that which could make post functional - Degree holders could not thus be equated with Diploma holders and Criterion of 80% marks could not be applied uniformly to both differently situated - Therefore, selection to extent could not be maintained - Hence, instant appeals - Whether judgment of DB was justified - Held, Single Judge was justified in applying Rules and upholding selection process made by State authorities, it was wholly unjustified on part of DB to have interfered with selection process on basis of criteria which were not laid down in Rules and that too on erroneous appreciation of the Rules, 1981- Hence, between Degree holders and Diploma holders at stage of recruitment for purpose of minimum qualifications and in other words, no distinction was made between two categories at stage of recruitment, but greater weight age was given to Degree holders in post-recruitment period in form of higher starting pay and also lesser number of years service requirement for qualifying for promotion to higher post - Therefore, there was sufficient inbuilt balance maintained between two categories of candidates and the impugned judgment of the High Court completely throws the Rules out of balance and judgment of DB of HC was set aside and judgment of Single Judge dismissing writ petitions was affirmed - Appeals allowed.
