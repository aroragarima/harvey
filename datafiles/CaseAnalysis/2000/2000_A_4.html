David Joseph Guido v A. C. Fernandes

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3989; (2000) 9 SCC 179; JT 2000 (7) SC 551

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rent (Amendment) Act, 1986

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13A(1) - Appellant was a member of armed forces of UOI - Suit for eviction - Whether certificate granted u/s. 13-A(1) is conclusive proof of facts stated therein and that, therefore, it was not open for competent authority or High Court to go into question of ownership of appellant and relationship of landlord and tenant between them and record findings thereon? - Before passing an order of eviction, in case of a member of armed forces of Union, competent authority has to be satisfied that he has produced a certificate signed by authorised officer to effect that : (i) he is a member of armed forces of Union or that he was such a member and has retired as such; and (ii) he does not possess any other premises suitable for residence in local area where premises are situated - Such a certificate is conclusive evidence of facts stated therein - Held, a plain reading of certificate shows that authority has certified only two facts, namely, that appellant was a member of armed forces of Union and had retired and that he did not possess any other suitable house in local area where he or any member of his family could reside - Presumption of conclusive evidence applies to facts stated in certificate and to no other fact - Evidently ownership of flat in dispute and relationship of landlord and tenant are not facts stated therein, so it is futile to contend that presumption extends to those facts also - Appellant failed to prove his ownership of flat in question and relationship of landlord and tenant between him and respondent - Appeal dismissed.
