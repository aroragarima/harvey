Ashwin Nanubhai Vyas v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 156; AIR 1967 SC 983; 1967 (69) BomLR 308; 1967 CRLJ 943; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 807

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 4(1)(h), 198, 206, 208, 247, 259, 431 and 495 - Complaint u/s. 493 and 496 of Indian Penal Code, 1860 but therafter complainant died of a heart attack - Complaint's mother applied to Court for substitution as, a fit and proper complainant - Whether on death of complainant proceedings ipso facto came to an end or could be continued in manner ordered by Presidency Magistrate? - Held, there is nothing in Code or in Chap. XVIII which says that, if any, consequence would follow if complainant remains absent at any subsequent hearing after filing complaint - Further held, Presidency Magistrate was right in proceeding with inquiry by allowing mother to carry on prosecution, and u/s 495 mother may continue prosecution herself or through a pleader - Appeal dismissed.
