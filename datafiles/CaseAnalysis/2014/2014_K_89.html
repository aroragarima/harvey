Kumod Kumar and another v State of Jharkhand and others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 871; (2015) 4 SCC 646; AIR 2015 SC 732; JT 2014 (14) SC 183; 2014(14) SCALE 205; 2015 (1) SLR 551

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reversion, Police Act, 1861, Seniority claim, Bihar Police Manual, 1978, Bihar Fire Service Act, 1948, Common cadre

Summary: Service - Bihar Police Manual, 1978, chap. 1A, rr. 7A(a), 7A(b) - Seniority claim - Common cadre - Legality - Appellants joined police service by way of direct recruitment - Appellants claimed seniority in general line of Sub-Inspectors of Police (on reversion) with effect from the date of their initial appointment, as Steno Sub-Inspectors of Police - Authorities chosen to determine their seniority with effect from the dates of their appointment to general line of Sub-Inspectors of Police, with effect from 26-8-1989/11-6-1991 - Aggrieved appellants filed petitions before HC, seeking seniority in general line, with effect from the date of their appointment into police service, which was dismissed by DB - Appellants sought a review of DB order, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether posts of Sub-Inspector of Police in general line of Police dept. as also, posts of Steno Sub-Inspector of Police belong to a common cadre.

Held, the comparative analysis of selection and appointment of Sub- Inspectors/Asst. Sub-Inspectors of Police in the general line, and that of Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors, leaves no room for any doubt, that those inducted into the general line of the police force, constitute a separate and distinct cadre, as against those recruited as Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors in the âMiscellaneous Force" of the Police dept.. The duties which Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors discharge, are totally dissimilar from the responsibilities assigned to Sub-Inspectors/Asst. Sub-Inspectors in the general line of the police force. While the former are assigned only stenography work, the latter perform traditional police duties. Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors are a part of a different cadre vis-vis Sub-Inspectors/Asst. Sub-Inspectors of the general line. Therefore the view expressed by HC, in the impugned orders is endorsed. The cadre of Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors was created out of the existing cadre of Stenographer-Clerks. Therefore, terms and conditions postulated (in the letter dt.4-9-1953) with reference to the Steno-Clerks, cannot be extended to Steno Sub-Inspectors/Steno Assistant Sub-Inspectors. Therefore, per se para 11 of the letter dt.4-9-1953 is inapplicable to the controversy in hand. Because the appellants cannot, describe themselves as "existing" Steno-Clerks, when the said communication was issued, the said communication, is wholly irrelevant/inapplicable to the claim raised on behalf of the appellants. Para 11, at best, afforded a protection to Steno-Clerks who had been inducted into the service of the Police dept. prior to the creation of a separate cadre of Steno Sub- Inspectors/Steno Asst. Sub-Inspectors. It is apparent, that the said benefit would not flow to those appointed to the Stenographers' cadre after the issuance of the said communication, or alternatively, after the incorporation of the proposals accepted by the State Govt. (in the said communication), through the suggested amendment in the existing provisions of the Police Manual. The appellants' appointment (on reversion) to the general line, is for the benefit of the appellants (who belonged to the Stenographers' cadre), on account of lack of promotional avenues. Having participated in the process of selection, the appellants will be deemed to have sought and opted for their appointment (on reversion) to the general line of the police force. It is only on the determination of the incumbent's suitability, through a selection process, that an individual from the Stenographers' cadre, will gain entry into the general line of Sub-Inspectors/Asst. Sub-Inspectors of Police. Such appointment is neither in public interest nor in the exigency of service. It is not possible for Court to accept, that appointment (on reversion) contemplated in the instant case, can be equated with an appointment by way of transfer, as generally understood. The general principles of seniority applicable to transfers, are inapplicable to the facts and circumstances of the instant case. Appeals dismissed.
