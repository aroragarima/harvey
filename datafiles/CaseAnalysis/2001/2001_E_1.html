P. Mohan Reddy v E. A. A. Charles and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20199; (2001) 4 SCC 433; (2001) SCC (L&S) 718; AIR 2001 SC 1210; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 57; JT 2001 (3) SC 1; 2001 LabIC 1087; 2001(2) SCALE 105; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 1068; 2001 (1) SLR 787; 2001 (2) SLT 168B; 2001 (2) Supreme 10

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Determination Of Seniority, Retrospectivity, Andhra Pradesh Revenue Subordinate Services Rules, 1961, Uttar Pradesh Service of Engineers (Building and Roads Branch) Class II Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 309 - Andhra Pradesh Revenue Subordinate Services Rules, 1961 rr. 16, 3(b), 4(e), s. 33 - Uttar Pradesh Service of Engineers (Building and Roads Branch) Class II Rules s. 23 - Promotion - Retrospective effect - Validity - Appellants were directly recruited Deputy Tehsildars and their service conditions were governed by Special Rules - Under Special Rules appointment to cadre of Deputy Tehsildars could be made either by direct recruitment or by transfer - It also further provides that substantive vacancies in cadre would be filled up by direct recruitment and recruitment by transfer in proportion of 1:1 - Special Rule was amended inserting r. 4(e) and giving it retrospective effect with effect from promulgation of rules on 12 October 1961 - Amended r. 4(e) provided that inter se seniority between direct recruits to category of Deputy Tehsildars and promotees to category of Deputy Tehsildars should be determined from date of their confirmation in substantive vacancy in that category in proportion of 1:1, as provided in r. 3(b) - Validity of amended Rule was subject matter of challenge in this Court in case of K. V. Subba Rao and others v. Government of Andhra Pradesh and others 1988 Indlaw SC 866 This Court came to hold that amended Rules can operate only prospectively and shall not have any retrospective effect - Further direction was given that State shall within 4 months from date of judgment would compute substantive vacancies in cadre and determine quota of direct recruits to rank of Deputy Tehsildars and after working out of vacancies available to be filled up by direct recruits on basis of 50 per cent of total number, fill up same by making direct recruitment within a period of 4 months - State Govt. was further directed to draw up seniority list on basis of r. 4(e) on or before 31 December 1988 - Pursuant to aforesaid direction seniority list were prepared but alleging that list have not been prepared strictly in accordance with judgment of this Court, applications were filed before Administrative Tribunal - Special Rules of 1961 were further amended by limiting direct recruits to 30 per cent of approved substantive vacancies and further providing that notwithstanding r. 4(e), seniority of person appointed as Deputy Tehsildar should be governed by General Rule 33 in Part II of Service Rules, according to which continuous service and not confirmation by following ratio of vacancies 1:1 between direct recruits and promotees would be criteria - Promotee Tehsildars, however, approached Tribunal and prayed for direction that seniority list be re-drawn up as per criteria under amended Rules dated 24 September 1992 , of all those who continue in cadre and who have not been promoted to any higher post and necessarily therefore, persons appointed as Deputy Tehsildars between period 09 October 1980 and 23 September 1992, their seniority has to be determined on the basis of remanded Rules - Tribunal dismissed applications filed by promotees with direction that seniority of persons directly appointed as Deputy Tehsildars between 09 October 1980 and 23 September 1992 be drawn up in accordance with r. 4(e) since amendment brought about in September 1992 had not been given any retrospective effect, and on other hand was prospective in nature - Against order of dismissal by Administrative Tribunal promotees approached HC and HC having set aside judgment of Tribunal and having held that seniority has to be re-drawn up in accordance with amended Rules on basis of total length of service without reference to date of confirmation and without reference to r. 4(e), which had been inserted by amendment of 09 October 1980 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in setting aside order of Tribunal - Held, question of re- determination of seniority in cadre on basis of any amended criteria or Rules would arise only when amendment in question was given retrospective effect - If retrospectively of Rule was assailed by any person then Court would be entitled to examine same and decide matter in accordance with law - If retrospectivity of Rule was ultimately struck down, necessarily question of re-drawing of seniority list under amended provisions would not arise, but if however, retrospectivity was up held by a Court then seniority could be re-drawn up in accordance with amended provisions of employees who were still in cadre and not those who have already got promotion to some other cadre by that date - Further particular Rule of seniority having been considered by Court and some directions in relation thereto having been given, that direction has to be followed in matter of drawing up of seniority list until and unless valid Rule by Rule Making Authority comes into existence and requires otherwise - It might be further stated that if any Rule or Administrative Instruction mandate drawing up of seniority list or determination of inter se seniority within any specified period then same must be adhered to unless any valid reason was indicated for non-compliance of same - HC therefore, was clearly in error and said judgment of HC was thus set aside - Tribunal was fully justified in dismissing applications filed by promotee Deputy Tehsildars - Appeals disposed of.
