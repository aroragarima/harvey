Sumitomo Heavy Industries Limited v Ongc Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1147; (1998) 1 SCC 305; AIR 1998 SC 825; 1998 (1) ARBLR 10; 1998 (1) CLT 18; [1998] 91 Comp Cas 349; JT 1997 (9) SC 666; 1998 (1) RAJ 1; 1997(7) SCALE 338; 1998 (1) SLT 443; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 186; 1997 (10) Supreme 157

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Curial Law, Enforcement Of Foreign Judgments/ Decrees

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14 and 47 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 9 - Enforceability of arbitral awards - Held, law which would apply to filing of award, to its enforcement and to its setting aside would be law governing agreement to arbitrate and performance of that agreement - Having regard to clear terms of cl. 17 of contract between appellant and first respondent, there is no doubt that law governing contract and law governing rights and obligations of parties arising from their agreement to arbitrate, and in particular, their obligations to submit disputes to arbitration and to honour award, are governed by law of India; nor is there any dispute in this behalf - Appeal dismissed.
