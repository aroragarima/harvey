Chhaganbhai Norsinbhai v Soni Chandubhai Gordhanbhai and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 534; (1976) SCC (Cr) 349; (1976) 2 SCC 951; AIR 1976 SC 1909; 1977 CrLR(SC) 39; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 786; 1976 UJ 387

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 2(b) - Appellant in previous revision proceedings before High Court had given a solemn undertaking to Court to hand over certain premises in his possession and violated same - Whether violation of an undertaking to Court to hand over certain premises in appellant possession leads to comtempt of Court? - Undertaking was on record - There was a breach of undertaking - There was nothing in conditions of undertaking to imply that it was merely a consent order passed upon an agreement between parties to which order of Court had been superadded - It was clearly a case of express undertaking to Court incorporated in order - Held, case being a case of a deliberate violation of an undertaking to Court effect was same as that of breach of an injunction - Hence it amounted to Contempt of Court - Appeal dismissed.
