Â Associated Cement Companies Limited v P. N. Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 1965 SC 1595; 1965 (11) FLR 77; 1965 (1) LLJ 433; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 366

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Arbitration Act, Punjab Welfare Officers Recruitment and Conditions of Service Rules, 1952, Eastern Bengal And Assam Excise Act, 1910, Sea Customs Act 1878

Summary: Service - Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Arbitration & ADR - Punjab Welfare Officers Recruitment and Conditions of Service Rules (1952), rr. 6 (5), 6 (6) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 (1) and 227 - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 s. 10 A - Arbitration Act, 1940 - (A) Whether State Govt, when it exercises appellate jurisdiction u/r. 6(6) of Punjab Welfare Officers Recruitment and Conditions of Service Rules (1952), is a Tribunal? -Further held, it is Within meaning of art. 136 (1) of Constitution of India, Punjab Govt. will be considered as state - (B) Whether state Govt. is competent to transfer judicial power to tribunals?- Further held, it can - Govt. can transfer a part of its judicial powers and functions to tribunals by entrusting to them task of adjudicating upon special matters and disputes between parties -(C) What is criteria in considering question about status of any body or authority as a tribunal u/art. 136 (1)? - (C) Main test to be applied is whether body or authority has been constituted by State and has been clothed with State's inherent judicial power to deal with disputes between parties and determine them on merits fairly and objectively - (D) Whether power exercised by authority which possesses courts trappings, is judicial power of State or not? -Further held, main and basic test, is whether adjudicating power which a particular authority is empowered to exercise, has been conferred on it by a statute and can be described as a part of State's inherent power exercised in discharging its judicial function - Applying this test, there can be no doubt that power which State Govt. exercises under r. 6 (5) and r. 6 (6) is a part of State's judicial power, it has to be held that it is a Tribunal within meaning of art. 136 (1) - (E) Whether u/s. 10 A private arbitrators can be said to be tribunals? - Further held, an authority or body deriving its power of adjudication from an agreement of parties, such as a private arbitrator or a tribunal acting u/s 10 A of IDA, 1947, does not satisfy test of a tribunal within art. 136 - Arbitration act, 1940 vests an arbitrator with some of trappings of a court and yet, such bodies and authorities are not tribunals -(F) What does word 'tribunal' in art. 227 of Constitution means? - Further held, word 'tribunal' in art. 227 have same meaning as in art. 136 - (G)Whether terms of termination of service falls u/r 6(3)? - Further held, it is - Under what circumstances an employee's services can be terminated and subject to what conditions, can well be, subject-matter of a contract of employment, because conditions of service would take in termination of services and incidentally, conditions subject to which such termination could be brought about- Statutory rule imposing obligation on management as prescribed by second proviso in question should be said to fall outside s. 49 (2) of Act - (H) Whether order of termination is a punishment u/r. 6(3)? - Further held, it is not- Where management has terminated services of Welfare Officer for his refusal to obey order of his transfer and termination is in accordance with terms of his appointment by giving him one month's pay in lieu of notice without holding any enquiry against him, order of termination is an order of simple discharge and as such is not a punishment, contemplated by r. 6 (3) (v) and is outside r. 6 (3) altogether - Hence no appeal can be filed u/r. 6 (6) and order of reinstatement passed by State Govt. was without jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
