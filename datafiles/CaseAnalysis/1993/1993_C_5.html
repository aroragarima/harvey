Bengal Iron Corporation v C. T. O

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 883; (1994) Supp1 SCC 310; AIR 1993 SC 2414; 1993 (47) ECR 23; 1993 (66) E.L.T. 13; JT 1993 (3) SC 134; 1993(2) SCALE 702; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 433; 1993 (90) STC 47

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970

Summary: Sales Tax - Limitation Act, 1908 - A.P.General Sales Tax Act, 1957,s. 42 ,sch. 3 item 2 ,s. 9 - Evidence Act , 1872, s. 115 - Central Sales Tax Act 1956 ss.14 (iv)(i) and 15 - Dealer is manufacturer of cast iron pipes etc. by casting of cast iron - As per ss. 14 and 15 pf 1956 Act , the Cast iron does not include cast iron castings - Item no 2 in sch. 3 of 1957 Act - Notification issued under by State Govt. declaring that cast iron castings are covered within term 'cast iron' - Later state levy tax on such goods - Question arise as to : (A) Validity of s. 42 pf 1957 Act - (B) Whether any clarification or circulars issued by Govt. regarding taxability of certain item do not prevent State from recovering tax leviable according to law ( s. 115 of the Evidence Act ) - (C) Whether tax levied on cast iron casting w as not effective - Held, s. 42 is valid - Such circular do not prevent State from recovering tax leviable according to law - And the levy of tax on the basis of ss. 14 and 15 is not binding - Petition dismissed.
