Palure Bhaskar Rao etc v P. Ramaseshaiah and others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 305; (2017) 5 SCC 783; AIR 2017 SC 1805; 2017 (4) ALD(SC) 134; 2017 (4) ALT(SC) 10; 2017 (4) AWC 3320; 2017(4) SCALE 482

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Date Of Appointment, Transfer, Selection, Pay, Promotion, Seniority, Rule, Date, Grade, Merit, Qualifications, Recruitment, Cadre, Quota, Eligibility, Police, Lien, Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Appointment, Andhra Pradesh Police Subordinate Service Rules

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966, rr. 3, 5, 6(a) - Andhra Pradesh Police Subordinate Service Rules, r. 15 - Promotion - Seniority - Service rendered - Appellant filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of Tribunal and HC whereby it was held that 6 years service required for appointment as Inspector under State Police Service should be as Sub-Inspector of Police (Civil) and the same does not include the service rendered as Reserve Sub-Inspector - Whether a Reserve Sub-Inspector of Police who was transferred on selection as Sub-Inspector of Police (Civil) should have 6 years of completed service as Sub-Inspector of Police (Civil) or a total service of 6 years including service as Reserve Sub-Inspector.

Held, State Police Subordinate Service and State Police Service were two distinct and separate services. Further, as and when a Reserve Sub-Inspector was selected and appointed by transfer to the post of Sub-Inspector (Civil), though there might be Sub-Inspectors of Police (Civil) already available in that category working for more than 4 years but less than 5 years yet Reserve Sub-Inspector transferred as Sub-Inspector of Police (Civil) would be placed above those existing Sub-Inspectors of Police recruited from other channels without the benefit of 'carry on' seniority. But that does not mean that on such placement in seniority he would be entitled to claim appointment as Inspector of Police in State Police Service. Though Reserve Sub-Inspector selected and appointed on transfer as Sub-Inspector (Civil) might be senior most in the category of Sub-Inspector of Police, but still he would be ineligible for consideration of appointment as Inspector in case he does not have 6 years of service as Sub-Inspector of Police (Civil). All his juniors who have 6 years of service as Sub-Inspector of Police and having been recruited to that post from different categories were entitled to steal a march over him as the rule now stands. The rule making authority in its wisdom has provided such a classification and SC do not find any material on record to upset the said wisdom. Appeals dismissed.
