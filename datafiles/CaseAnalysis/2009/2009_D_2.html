(1) Subhash Chandra and Another; (2) Sarv Rural v (1) Delhi Subordinate Services Selection Board

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1071; (2009) 15 SCC 458; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 517; 2010 (166) DLT 659; 2010 (1) ESC 128; JT 2009 (10) SC 645; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 625; 2009(11) SCALE 278; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 978; 2009 (5) SLR 612

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, Affirmative Action, Employment Under The State, Freedom Of Association, Reservation In Services, Special Provisions Relating To Certain Classes, Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, Constitution (Scheduled Castes) (Union Territories) Order, 1951, Constitution (Sixty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1991

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 (4), 16 (4), 341, 342, - Schedule Caste/Tribes (SC/ST) - Migrant SC/ST members - Caste status in Union Territory (UT) - Determination of - Ministry of Home Affairs issued a circular on 2-5-1975 with regard to verification of caste status of migrant SC/ST members - Govt of Delhi issued a clarification dt. 22-3-1977 in respect of said circular - Govt issued another circular on 8-4-1994 in this regard - Delhi Subordinate Service Selection Board issued employment notice by stipulating that SC/OBC candidates must furnish certificates from competent authority of Govt of Delhi before closing date - Writ sought against - Single Judge allowed writ petition of appellants by relied upon decision of SC in Marri Chandrasekhar Rao v. Dean, Seth Medical College & Ors (1990 Indlaw SC 695) - DB dismissed intra court appeal of respondents by relying decision of SC in S. Pushpa & Ors v. Sivachanmugavelu & Ors (2005 Indlaw SC 19) - Legality - Held, arts. 341 and 342 would applicable to State as well as UT - Once a person comes within purview of presidential promulgation, he would be entitled to constitutional and other statutory or administrative benefits attached thereto - Socio political rights created by Constitution cannot be segregated keeping in view of administrative exigencies - Power of president u/art.16(4) cannot be made applicable to the purpose of grant of benefit of reservation for SC/ST members in one State/UT to migrants in another State/UT - No material showed that migrant SC/ST members having same socio economic conditions in their state as well as in Delhi - Migrant SC/ST members would not come within purview of backward class specified in art. 14(4) - Notification of caste status to be done as per arts. 341 (1) and 342 - No alteration can be done by disregarding the procedure laid down u/art. 341 (2) - Intensive study required for considering migrants SC/ST members at par with backwards class existing in Delhi - Anyhow, circulars like in present case would deserve strict scrutiny - Decision of S. Pushpa (supra) rendered Marri chandrasekhar Rao (supra) had no application to UT, was not correct - Constitutional scheme contained in arts. 341 (1) and 342, can be putting State and UT in same bracket - Administrative circular cannot disregard procedure laid down in art. 341 (2) - Decision of S. Pushpa (supra) could not have ignored decision of Marri Chandrasekhar Rao (supra), hence dicta of decision in S.Pushpa did not lay down any binding ratio - Decision of SC in Official Liquidator v. Dayanand & Ors. (2008 Indlaw SC 1715) followed in that respect - Impugned judgments set aside - Appeal allowed.
