Jagbir Singh v Haryana State Agriculture Marketing Board and

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 893; (2009) 15 SCC 327; AIR 2009 SC 3004; 2009 (3) CLR 628; 2009 (4) ESC 686; 2009 (122) FLR 665; JT 2009 (9) SC 396; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2504; 2009 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3139; 2009 (4) LLJ 336; 2009 (4) LLN 24; 2009(9) SCALE 611; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 908; 2009 (5) SLR 606

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25F - Reinstatement - Grant of back wages - Legality of - Appellant engaged as a daily wager and was paid consolidated wages - Service was terminated - Industrial dispute raised - Labour Court held that respondent No. 1 violated provisions of s. 25F of Act by not giving him notice, pay in lieu of notice and retrenchment compensation before his termination and directed reinstatement with continuity of service and full back wages from date of demand notice - On appeal, HC set aside award of Labour Court - P /&gt;(A) Whether HC, in a case where termination of appellant was in contravention of S. 25F, was justified in upsetting award of Labour Court? - In catena of decisions in recent time, SC clearly laid down that an order of retrenchment passed in violation of s. 25F although may be set aside but an award of reinstatement should not, however, be automatically passed - Award of reinstatement with full back wages in such a case not found proper by SC and instead compensation has been awarded - SC distinguished between a daily wager who does not hold a post and a permanent employee - Held, view of HC that Labour Court erred in granting reinstatement and back wages in facts and circumstances of present case cannot be said to suffer from any legal flaw -P /&gt;(B) Award of Back wages - While awarding compensation, lots of factors, inter-alia, manner and method of appointment, nature of employment and length of service are relevant - Held, HC erred in not awarding compensation to appellant while upsetting award of reinstatement and back wages - In present case, where total length of service rendered by appellant was short and intermittent and that he was engaged as a daily wager, a compensation of Rs. 50, 000/- to appellant shall meet ends of justice - Appeal partly allowed.
