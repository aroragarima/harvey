Associated Cement Companies Limited v Commissioner of Customs

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Doraiswamy Raju, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 451; (2001) 4 SCC 593; AIR 2001 SC 862; 2001 (2) CLC 280; 2001 (95) ECR 6; 2001 (128) E.L.T. 21; JT 2001 (2) SC 141; 2001 (42) RLT 937; 2001(1) SCALE 436; 2001 (2) SCJ 204; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 608; 2001 (1) SLT 763; 2001 (124) STC 59; 2001 (1) Supreme 380

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Gatt, Importation, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules 1988

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(22), 12, 14, 24, 28(1) and 156 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 2 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 3, 4 and 9 - Whether Tribunal had erred in holding that drawings, designs, etc., relating to machinery or industrial technology were goods which were leviable to duty of customs on their transaction value at time of their import? - Value of goods imported would depend upon quality of same and would be represented by transaction value in respect of goods imported - Held, provisions of Chap. 98 were rightly applied on facts of these cases - Order passed against appellant levying customs duty and penalty is, therefore, to be set aside - Order accordingly.
