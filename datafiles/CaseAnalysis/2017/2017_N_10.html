HAFFKINE Bio-Pharmaceutical Corporation Limited and another v NIRLAC Chemicals, through its Manager and others

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Gupta, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 536; JT 2017 (8) SC 111

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract, Bulk Drug, Tender Of

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Health & Drug - Tender floating process - Violation of CVC guidelines - In pursuance of tender notice issued by Haffkine for supply of bulk drugs, Nirlac took part in tender but their tender was not found to be technically qualified - Aggrieved, Nirlac filed WP before HC challenging rejection of their bid and the award of tender in favour of Bionet by contending that CVC guidelines had been violated since tender of Bionet was not opened in the presence of representatives of Nirlac - HC held that Haffkine violated CVC guidelines and also concluded that Nirlac complied with all tender conditions, and so, tender of Nirlac was wrongly rejected - HC quashed tender dt.20-1-2016, letter dt.22-6-2016 and also set aside award of contract in favour of Bionet - However, HC permitted Haffkine to procure bulk drug from Bionet till fresh tender was finalized and State Govt. was directed to float fresh tender - Aggrieved, Haffkine and Bionet filed instant appeals and Nirlac also filed appeal contending that when they were found to be eligible, the tender should have been awarded to them instead of directing floating of fresh tender.

Held, when a technical bid is opened, it is the right of the rival bidders to see whether the documents attached by a bidder met the technical requirements or not and the same could be done only if the documents attached to the bid are shown to the other side. However, no record is produced to show that bid of Bionet was opened in the presence of representatives of Nirlac. Hence, entire tender opening process is vitiated since CVC guidelines have not been followed. Further, violation of CVC guidelines is itself sufficient to vitiate the entire tender process and hence, appeals of Haffkine and Bionet are dismissed. Further, Nirlac was not qualified because it did not satisfy condition 'j' of tender and so, HC erred in holding that Nirlac satisfied all tender conditions. Hence, though tender was wrongly awarded to Bionet, still tender could not have been awarded to Nirlac and as such, appeal of Nirlac is also dismissed. However, in the interest of justice, Nirlac is allowed to do bulk supply of drugs as per price quoted by it for manufacture of 120 million doses of oral polio vaccine. Further, Haffkine is restrained from purchasing any further bulk drug from Bionet pursuant to the contract in dispute but Haffkine could supply and Bionet could purchase 66 million doses of oral vaccine as per confirmed order. Further, HC erred in directing State Govt. to float separate tender since, Haffkine though Govt. undertaking, is still a separate entity and so, Haffkine is permitted to float fresh tender for supply of bulk drugs. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - A party could claim restitution only when its bid is technically qualified and wrongly rejected.
