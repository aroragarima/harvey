Regional Provident Fund Commissioner v Bhavani

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 684; (2008) 7 SCC 111; AIR 2008 SC 2957; 2008 (5) AWC 5210; 2008 (1) CCC 158; 2008 (2) CPJ(Sc) 9; 2008 (4) G.L.R. 2912; JT 2008 (6) SC 56; 2008 (2) LLJ 1079; 2008 (3) LLN 45; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 931; 2008 (4) MPLJ 121; 2008(6) SCALE 773; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 767; 2008 (5) SLR 430; 2009 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 496; 2008 (3) UC 1724

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Employees' Pension Scheme Act, 1995

Summary: Employees' Provident Funds and Misc. Provisions Act, 1952; Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Refusal of the Regional Provident Fund Authorities to release pension to respondent - District Forum came to a finding that since respondent was eligible for the benefits of the 1995 Scheme, denial of the same amounted to deficiency of service which would attract the provisions of the Consumer Protection Act - Appeal against - Held, dates of birth of the respondents as recorded in their service records with the company are the correct dates of birth of the employees and not the dates of birth as entered in the records of the appellant - Reasoning given by the District Forum in accepting the entries in the company's record while rejecting those in the records of the appellant/Regional P.F. Commissioner are based on sound logic and the materials on record - Respondent comes squarely within the definition of 'consumer' within the meaning of s. 2(1)(d)(ii), inasmuch as, by becoming a member of the Employees' Family Pension Scheme, 1971, and contributing to the same, she was availing of the services rendered by the appellant for implementation of the Scheme - Appeals dismissed.
