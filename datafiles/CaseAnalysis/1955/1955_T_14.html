Topandas v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1956 SC 33; 1956 ALJ 161; 1956 (58) BomLR 492; 1956 CRLJ 138; 1956 (1) MLJ 60; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; 1956 SCJ 86; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 120 A and 120 B - Criminal conspiracy - Appellant and others accused were charged that they were parties to a criminal conspiracy by agreeing to do certain illegal acts - There were also charges against all accused u/s. 471 r/w s. 465 and ss. 34 and 420 IPC, 1860 in respect of each of three illegal acts aforesaid -(A) Whether one person alone can be convicted for criminal conspiracy? - Definition of criminal conspiracy itself states that there ought to be two or more persons who must be parties to a conspiracy - Held, one person alone can never be held guilty or convicted of offence of criminal conspiracy as for simple reason that one cannot conspire with oneself - Appeal allowed.
