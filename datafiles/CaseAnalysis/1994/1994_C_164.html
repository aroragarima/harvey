Srikant K. Jituri v Corpn . Of The City Of Bengauam

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, A.M. Ahmadi, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 793; (1994) 6 SCC 572; AIR 1995 SC 288; AIR 1994 SCW 4453; JT 1994 (6) SC 496; 1994 MLJ 739; 1994(4) SCALE 447; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Karnataka Municipal Corporation Act, s. 108(2), r. 25 - (A)Whether suit against enhancement of property tax maintainable- (B) Whether writ petition is maintainable if alternative remedy under an Act is not efficacious - Held, (A) no grounds on which assessment made by corporation can be challenged in civil court - Suit is not maintainable - (B) onerous provision may be a ground for entertaining writ petition on ground that alternative remedy provided by statute is not efficacious remedy but that can never be ground for maintaining civil suit - Both jurisdictions are different and are governed by different principles - Finality clause in statute is not a bar to exercise of constitutional power whereas jurisdiction of civil court arises from another statute - Appeal dismissed.
