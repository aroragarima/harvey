Deokuer and Another v Sheoprasad Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 465; AIR 1966 SC 359; 1966 (1) MLJ 12; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 12; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, s. 42 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 145 - Suit brought by appellants for a declaration that respondents had acquired no right or title to a property under certain deeds and that deeds were inoperative and void - High Court held that as appellants were not in possession of property at date of suit, their suit must fail under proviso to s. 42 of Act as appellants had failed to ask for further relief of recovery of possession from respondents - However, fact was that at date of suit property was under attachment by a magistrate under powers conferred by s. 145 of CrPC, 1898 and was not in possession of any party - Whether in view of attachment, appellants could have in their suit asked for relief for delivery of possession to them? - Held, in a suit for declaration of title to property filed when it stands attached u/s. 145 of Code, it is not necessary to ask for further relief of delivery of possession - Where defendant is not in possession and not in a position to deliver possession to plaintiff it is not necessary for plaintiff in a suit for a declaration of title to property to claim possession - Case remanded - Appeal allowed.
