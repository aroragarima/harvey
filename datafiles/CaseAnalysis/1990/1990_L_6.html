Suresh Mohan Chopra v Lakhi Prabh Dayal and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 359; (1990) Supp SCC 696; 1991 (1) ACJ 1; AIR 1990 SC 1979

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Tort

Summary: Tort - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110D - Finding of tribunal - Reversal - Plea of driver having no licence - Driver, witness of insurer claiming to have licence - Same, not produced - Explanation was that he destroyed it same being of only one year's duration - Explanation not shown to be wrong - No record indicating that driver had no licence at relevant time, produced - Tribunal had no option but to accept statement of driver, he being insurer's witness - Reversal, whether proper? - Held, reversal is not proper - Appeal allowed.
