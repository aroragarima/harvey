Uttaranchal Forest Development Corporation and another v K. B. Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1400; (2005) 11 SCC 449

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17-B - Employment - Discontinuance of services - Reinstatement - Petitioner/employer was State forest development corporation and respondents were employees under employer Corporation - Later employees were dismissed from their service - Employees approached Labour Court for reinstatement in service but same was rejected by Labour Court - Employees filed Writ petition and claimed for same relief which was allowed by Writ Court - Aggrieved by said decision of Writ Court petitioner filed petitions and sought for appropriate reliefs - Hence instant petitions.

Held, Court found from employerâs prayer which states that such workmen who had directly approached by writ petitions to HC for seeking relief of reinstatement, cannot be granted benefit of s. 17-B of 1947 Act as there was no evidence before Tribunal or Labor Court about their non-employment or gainful employment elsewhere after discontinuance of their services. After hearing parties Court direct that only such workmen in whose favor there are awards of reinstatement and who have filed affidavits of their not being in gainful employment, shall be entitled to be granted reinstatement or in lieu thereof paid wages last drawn by them on respective dates of their terminations from services. Their entitlement for such wages would be from respective dates by filing affidavits by each of them in Court in compliance with s. 17-B of 1947 Act. As per office report, makes a statement that on basis of service position, he seeks deletion of names of pro forma respondents and prayer is allowed. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Benefit of s. 17-B of 1947 Act provides that direction of reinstatement in service or payment of last wages drawn in lieu thereof can be granted only in favor of such workmen who have obtained awards in their favor from Industrial Tribunal/Labor Court and in support of their claims.
