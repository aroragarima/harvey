Parkash Singh Badal And Anr v State Of Punjab And Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 941; (2007) 1 SCC 1; AIR 2007 SC 1274; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 8; 2007 (1) JCC 236; JT 2007 (1) SC 89; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 142; 2007 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 218; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2006(13) SCALE 54; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification, Old Act

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973; Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988; Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Questioning validity of proceedings initiated - Held, an investigation should not be shut out at the threshold because a political opponent or a person with political difference raises an allegation of commission of offence - Plea of mala fides as raised cannot be maintained - Obligation to register a case is not to be confused with the remedy if same is not registered - If any information disclosing a cognizable offence is laid before an officer incharge of a police station satisfying the requirements of s. 154(1) of the Code, the said police officer has no other option except to enter the substance thereof in the prescribed form, that is to say, to register a case on the basis of such information - Appeal dismissed.
