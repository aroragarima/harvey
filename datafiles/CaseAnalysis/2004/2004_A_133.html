Smt. Swarnam Ramachandran & Another v Aravacode Chakungal Jayapalan

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 659; (2004) 8 SCC 689; 2004 (57) ALR 730; 2004 (4) AWC 3028; 2005 (2) Bom.C.R. 887; JT 2004 (7) SC 53; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 245; 2005 (98) RD 181; 2004(7) SCALE 211; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 806

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Agreement For Sale

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Specific Relief Act, s. 16 - Specific Performance - Decreeing of suit for specific performance, whether proper? - Notwithstanding that a specific date was mentioned in the agreement, one has not only to look at the letter but also at the substance of the contract - Whether time is of essence is a question of fact and the real test is intention of the parties - Facts and circumstances of each case is one of the deciding factors - Held on facts - Time was not the essence in this case - Respondents have proved that they were ready and willing to perform their part of the agreement - Courts below correct in decreeing the suit for specific performance - Appeal dismissed.
