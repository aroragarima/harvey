Som Mittal v Govt. Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 122; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 73; (2008) 3 SCC 753; AIR 2008 SC 1126; 2008 CRLJ 1610; JT 2008 (2) SC 41; 2008 (2) LLN 690; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 880; 2008(2) SCALE 108; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 323; 2008 (1) ShimLC 115

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Karnataka Shops And Commercial Establishments Rules, 1963

Summary: Karnataka Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1961; criminal Procedure Code - Alleged violation of ss. 25 and 30(3) - Petition to quash the complaint and cognizance was dismissed - Appeal against - Held, inherent power of the High Court should not be exercised according to whims and caprice and it has to be exercised sparingly, with circumspection and in the rarest of rare cases - Power under s. 482 is not intended to scuttle justice at the threshold - Concurring Judgment making strong recommendation to the U.P. Government to immediately issue an Ordinance to restore the provision for anticipatory bail by repealing Section 9 of U.P. Act No. 16 of 1976, and empowering the Allahabad High Court as well as the Sessions Courts in U.P. to grant anticipatory bail - Appeal dismissed.
