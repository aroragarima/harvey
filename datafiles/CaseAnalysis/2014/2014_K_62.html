Khim Singh v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 448; (2014) 12 SCC 562; AIR 2014 SC 2810; 2014 CRLJ 3713; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 9; 2014(8) SCALE 506; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly killed his wife and was charged with offence u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court found appellant guilty of the offence u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether circumstances relied upon by prosecution formed a series of events and whether chain of circumstantial evidence was complete, which could be sufficient to show involvement of appellant in the commission of murder -

Held, from the narration of testimony of PW1, 2, 4 and 5, it could be concluded that appellant was unhappy with his wife (deceased/victim) and that resulted in quarrels between them off and on - Quarrel took place even in the evening preceding the date of death - In the night intervening 17th and 18th July, 1987 deceased was killed by means of a Kulhari - Except appellant nobody was residing in the house - Thus, deceased could not be killed as a result of assault by anybody else other than appellant - Conduct of appellant in the morning of 18-7-1-1987 was unnatural - Appellant failed to explain as to where he remained on the fateful night - Prosecution successfully proved that there was a quarrel between the appellant and his wife in the preceding evening - conduct of appellant was unnatural in not disclosing the place where he remained in the fateful night, making it clear that his statement u/s. 313 of CrPC was not believable - From the testimony of real mother of appellant (PW1) as well as PW4 (Pradhan of village), it was fully established that appellant was very much present in the house on the fateful night and there was a quarrel between the appellant and his wife - In the absence of any reason for leaving his house, it could be held that appellant remained in his house in that night - Homicidal death of deceased was corroborated by the conduct of appellant in the morning of 18-7-1987 - PW5 stated that when the accused was found in the morning, he was asked about his whereabouts in the night and he was not able to explain it - Even PW2 (wife of the brother of appellant), stated that accused was outside the house in the morning wandering here and there - Narration of chain of circumstantial evidence relied upon by prosecution in instnat case lead to the inference that appellant was guilty for the offence of murder of deceased as all the circumstances taken together lead to only hypothesis of the guilt of appellant - Chain of circumstantial evidence relied upon by prosecution was complete to hold the accused guilty of offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Appellant was rightly convicted and sentenced u/s. 302 of IPC for life imprisonment by Trial Court as affirmed by HC - Impugned judgment under appeal was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
