Â Khyerbari Tea Company Limited and Another v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 361; AIR 1964 SC 925; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 975

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Ex Post Facto Law

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Prohibition Act - River Boards Act, 1956 - Tea Act, 1953 - Legislative Powers - Evasion Of Tax - Constitutional Validity - Competence of Legislature - Retrospective Effect - According to petitioners, tea was delivered in packed form by Respondent/Railways, who took goods by ferries on inland waterways - Petitioner No. 2/Management found that provisions of Act were invalid, as taxes directly and immediately impeded and freedom of trade were mischief u/art. 301 of Constitution and they constitute an unreasonable restriction on freedom of trade guaranteed and their petitioner No. 2's run mental right guaranteed by Art. 19(1) (g) of Constitution - Hence instant petition - Whether impugned tax imposed a restriction on movement of trade, and whether u/art. 304(b) of Constitution restrictions imposed by respondents were reasonable and in public interest and validity of constitution were challengeable - Held, carriage of goods as taxable event u/ss. 3(1) and 3(2) made producer liable to pay tax only on goods carried - Legislature had adopted machinery of making producer responsible for payment of tax and liable - Hence Act, 1922 passed u/art. 304(b) of Constitution could be valid - Fundamental right to carry on trade was not an absolute right and it could be regulated and controlled by law which impose restrictions on said right - Court opined that exercise of legislative power in passing Act, 1922 could not be a colorable - Therefore, goods entrusted to respondent/railway through carriage, and so, same should be outside purview of s. 3 of Act, could not be sustained - Court hold that State Legislature had no power to levy a tax on tea which was carried over to a part of area of State - Hence, notification could not be issued with retrospective effect and incompetent - Petition dismissed.
