T.A. Abdul Rahman v State Of Kerala And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 205; (1989) 4 SCC 741; (1990) SCC (Cr) 76; AIR 1990 SC 225; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 578; 1989 (3) Crimes 186; 1990 CRLJ 78; 1990 CrLR(SC) 15; 1989 (25) ECR 296; JT 1989 (3) SC 444; 1989(2) SCALE 388; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 945; 1990 (1) UJ 116

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3(1)(iii) and 3(1)(iv) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Validity of detention order - Unexplained delay of three months in securing arrest of detenu - Genuineness of subjective satisfaction of authority - Representation submitted by detenu considered after delay of 72 days - Casual and indifferent attitude displayed by authorities - In such circumstances subjective satisfaction of authority becomes doubtful therefore detention is vitiated - Appeal allowed.
