Union of India and Anr. v S.B. Vohra and Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 37; (2004) 2 SCC 150; (2004) SCC (L&S) 363; AIR 2004 SC 1402; 2004 (5) AWC 4035; 2004 (109) DLT 266; 2004 (1) ESC 168; 2004 (100) FLR 1029; JT 2004 (1) SC 38; 2004 (1) LLN 462; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 132; 2004(1) SCALE 131; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 36; 2004 (3) SLR 353; 2004 (1) SLT 400; 2004 (1) Supreme 471; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 474

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Service

Keywords: Administrative Discretion, Attorney-General

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 146(2), 16(4), 226, 229, 229(2) - Delhi High Court Establishment (Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1972 - Delhi High Court (Original Side) Rules - Fixation of pay of officers in HCs - Must either be examined by an expert body like Pay Commission - In absence of such a body - HC itself to undertake task - In terms of art.229 - No doubt whatsoever that recommendations of Chief Justice should ordinarily be approved by State - Refusal - Only for strong and adequate reasons - Fixation of a revision of scale of pay of HC being within exclusive domain of Chief Justice of HC - State is expected to accept same recommendations - Writ of Mandamus - Statutory duties should be allowed to be performed by statutory authorities at first instance - Chief Justice of HC and State are not ad idem - Matter should be discussed and an effort should be made to arrive at a consensus - Writ should normally not issue - Only in exceptional cases HC may interfere on judicial side - Jurisdiction of HC should be exercised with care and circumspection - Held on facts - Not a fit case for exercise of discretionary jurisdiction.
