U. P. Income Tax Department Contingent Paid Staff v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28225; (1987) Supp SCC 658; (1988) SCC (L&S) 344; AIR 1988 SC 517; 1987 (5) ATC 434; 1988 (56) FLR 121; JT 1987 (4) SC 585; 1988 LabIC 958; 1988 (1) LLJ 396; 1988 (1) LLN 667; 1987(2) SCALE 1222; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 170; 1988 (2) SLJ 38; 1988 (7) SLR 804

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Payment of minimum wages - Regularisation of service - Petitioner serving Income tax department for 8 years or more - Contended that they were paid on daily wage basis, less than class IV employees - Whether petitioner are entitled to pay scale equal to regularly employed workers? - Held, respondents are directed to pay wages to workmen who are employed as contingent paid staff of Income Tax Department throughout India, doing work of class IV employees at rates equivalent to minimum pay in pay scale of regularly employed workers in corresponding cadres - Petition Allowed.
