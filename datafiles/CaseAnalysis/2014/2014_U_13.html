Major General H. M. Singh, VSM v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 16; (2014) 3 SCC 670; AIR 2014 SC 1128; JT 2014 (1) SC 465; 2014(1) SCALE 135; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 270; 2014 (2) SLR 142; 2014 (2) UPLBEC 877

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Denial of Promotion, Onward promotion, Delay in consideration

Summary: Constitution - Defence & Security Forces - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Army Rules, 1954, r. 16A - Onward promotion - Delay in consideration - Denial of promotion - Justifiability - Appellant (Major General) claimed that he might be considered for onward promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General - However, appellant was not considered eligible for promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General as he had not completed 2 years' service in the rank of Major General at that time - Thereafter, appellant addressed a representation to the Director General (DRDO) and 2 days prior to appellant's retirement on superannuation, a meeting of Selection Board for promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General was convened and appellant's name was cleared for promotion and President of India by an order dt.29-2-2008, was pleased to grant the appellant extension of service, for a period of 6 months - However, Appointments Committee had not approved the promotion of appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed by Single Judge - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB - DB by impugned judgment held that unless it was shown that determination of Appointments Committee of Cabinet suffered from arbitrariness or malafides and capriciousness, the same could not be interfered with and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that determination of Appointments Committee of the Cabinet, was not supported by justifiable reasons - Whether non- consideration of claim of the appellant would violate the fundamental rights vested in him u/arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Held, Appointments Committee of the Cabinet did not in any manner upset the finding recorded by Selection Board, in respect of merit and suitability of appellant for promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General - On the aspect of instant matter, Appointments Committee of the Cabinet has maintained a sullen silence - Even in pleadings filed on behalf of respondents UOI and Director General DRDO), there was an ironic quiescence - Thus, all other issues apart, appellant should be deemed to have been found suitable for promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General, even by Appointments Committee of the Cabinet - Factual position depicted in counter affidavit revealed, that respondents indeed were desirous of filling up said vacancy - In view of matter, if appellant was the senior most serving Major General eligible for consideration (which he undoubtedly was), he most definitely had the fundamental right of being considered against the above vacancy, and also the fundamental right of being promoted if he was adjudged suitable - Failing which, he would be deprived of his fundamental right of equality before the law, and equal protection of laws, extended by art. 14 of Constitution -By extension orders, respondents desired to treat appellant justly, so as to enable him to acquire the honour of promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General, (in case the recommendation made in his favour by Selection Board was approved by Appointments Committee of Cabinet), stood affirmed- Action of authorities in depriving the appellant due consideration for promotion to the rank of the Lieutenant General, would have resulted in violation of his fundamental right under Art. 14 of Constitution - Such an action at the hands of the respondents would unquestionably have been arbitrary - Extension in service granted to appellant, for all intents and purposes, would be deemed to satisfy the parameters of exigency of service, stipulated in r. 16A of Rules - Selection Board had singularly recommended name of appellant for promotion, out of a panel of 4 names - In such an eventuality, Court have no other alternative but to strike down action of authorities as discriminatory and violative of art. 16 of Constitution - Denial of promotion to appellant mainly for the reason, that appellant was on extension in service, was unsustainable besides being arbitrary, especially in the light of fact, that vacancy for which appellant was clamoring consideration, became available, well before the date of his retirement on superannuation - Thus, no hesitation in rejecting the basis on which the claim of appellant for onward promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General was declined, by Appointments Committee of Cabinet - Appellant deserves promotion to the rank of Lieutenant General, from the date due to him and would also be entitled to continuation in service till the age of retirement on superannuation stipulated for Lieutenant Generals - Operative part of impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
