B.P. Khemka Pvt. Limited v Birendra Kumar Bhowmick And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28671; (1987) 2 SCC 407; AIR 1987 SC 1010; 1987 (1) CalLT(SC) 23; JT 1987 (1) SC 665; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 256; 1987 (1) RentLR 659; 1987(1) SCALE 537B; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 559; 1987 (2) UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 17(1), 17(2 A), 17(3) and 17(4) Proviso - Tenancy - Provisions of s. 17 of Act - (A) Availability of benefit of s. 17(2)(A) - (B) Claim for relief u/s. 17(4) - (C) Expression 'shall' u/s. 17(3) of Act, if meant for striking out defence - Held, benefit available to all tenants notwithstanding bar of time limit u/s. 17(1) - Tenant can claim relief as provided by proviso - Directory and not mandatory provision - Appeal allowed.
