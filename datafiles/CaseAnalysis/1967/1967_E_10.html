P.S.N.S. Ambalavana Chettiar and Company Limited and Another v Express Newspapers Limited, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 180; AIR 1968 SC 741; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 34; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 239

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax; Water; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 176 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 18 and 54 - Whether the respondent had the right to resell the goods under s. 54(2) of the Sale of Goods Act, 1930? - The statutory power of resale u/s. 54(2) arises if the property in the goods has passed to the buyer subject to the lien of the unpaid seller - Where the property in the goods has not passed to the buyer, the seller has no right of resale under s. 54(2) - It is a condition precedent to the passing property under a contract of sale that the goods are ascertained - The condition is not fulfilled where there is a contract for sale of a portion of a specified larger stock - Held, decree in favour of the respondent against the appellants for Rs. 10,980,128 with interest thereon @ 6% p.a. from 30-07-1952, decrees for 'costs passed by Courts below affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
