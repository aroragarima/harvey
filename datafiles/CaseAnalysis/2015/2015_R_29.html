Hindustan Zinc Limited v Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 499; 2015(6) ALL MR 432; JT 2015 (9) SC 585; 2015(6) SCALE 706; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 1104

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Generating Company, Transmission Lines, Laying, Live, Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Renewable Energy Certificate and Renewable Purchase Obligation Compliance Framework) Regulations, 2010, Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Renewable Energy Obligation) Regulations, 2007

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 265 - Electricity Act, 2003, ss. 7, 9, 61, 66, 86(1)(a), 86(1)(e), 181 - Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Renewable Energy Certificate and Renewable Purchase Obligation Compliance Framework) Regulations, 2010, regn. 9 - Rajasthan Electricity Regulatory Commission (Renewable Energy Obligation) Regulations, 2007 - Obligation to purchase energy - Violation of Act and fundamental rights - Determination - Appellant companies are engaged in business of production, manufacturing, selling of metals and have established their own captive generation power plants in terms of Act - Commission in exercise of its power u/ss. 61, 66, 86(1)(e) and 181 of Act vide notification framed 2007 and 2010 Regulations which imposed Renewable Energy (RE) obligation on Captive Gencos and other obligated entities including appellants to purchase minimum energy from renewable sources and to pay surcharge in case of shortfall in meeting RE obligation - HC upheld validity of 2007 and 2010 Regulations directing appellants to purchase minimum energy for renewable sources and comply with their liability under said Regulations - Appellant contended that impugned Regulations are ultra vires to ss. 7, 9, 86(1)(a) and (e) and 181 of Act and also fundamental rights guaranteed to appellants u/arts. 14, 19(1)(g) 265 of Constitution of India, National Electricity Policy, 2005 and Tariff Policy, 2006 - Whether impugned Regulation framed by respondent is in accordance with Act and fundamental rights.

Held, framing of regn. 9 by RERC is in exercise of its statutory power u/s. 181 of Act, relevant entry to frame impugned Regulation as provided u/s. 86(1)(e) of Act is valid and legal, keeping in view National Electricity Policy, 2005 and Tariff Policy of 2006 which are framed by Union of India, International obligation under Kyoto Protocol to which our Country is signatory and also to discharge Constitutional obligations as mandated u/art. 21 of Constitution i.e. Fundamental Right of citizens and art. 48 of Constitution i.e. Directive Principles of State Policy and to discharge Fundamental Duties by respondents as envisaged u/art. 51A (g)of Constitution. Further, with respect to imposing surcharge upon generating companies if company commit default of impugned Regulations it can be exercise its power u/s. 86(1)(g) of Act which empowers State Commission to 'levy fee for purposes of Act'. Further, penalty imposed by impugned Regulations is not in nature of 'tax' but to achieve object and intendment of Act. SC held that provisions requiring purchase of minimum percentage of energy from renewable sources of energy have been framed with object of fulfilling Constitutional mandate with view to protect environment and prevent pollution in area by utilizing renewable energy sources as much as possible in larger public interest. Further, SC held that HC has considered submissions of appellants and has rightly rejected same on ground that RE obligation imposed on captive gencos under impugned Regulations is neither ultra vires nor violative of provisions of Act and cannot in any manner be regarded as restriction on fundamental rights guaranteed to appellants under Constitution. Order passed by HC is upheld. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Regulations framed by authority cannot be challenged when same is in accordance with power conferred under Act and Constitutional mandate.
