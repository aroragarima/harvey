Meena Jayendra Thakur v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 739; (1999) 8 SCC 177; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1392; AIR 1999 SC 3517; 1999 CRLJ 4534; 2000 (120) E.L.T. 37; JT 1999 (7) SC 336; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 98

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs

Keywords: COFEPOSA, Detention Order, Person Concerned

Summary: FEMA - Customs - Constitution - National Security Act, 1980 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Customs Act, 1962 ,s.108 - Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 ,s.3,9(i), 8 (f) - Detention Order - Challenged - Customs authorities on reliable information searched vessel and recovered 350 pieces of silver ingots each weighing 35 KGs and arrested persons present in ship - Detenu was not present in vessel during seizure but arrested persons confronted to appellant authorities that silver in question was meant for detenu and was to be handed over to him - Detaining authority based on statements of arrested persons satisfied u/s. 3(i) ?? and issued impugned detention order dt. 5-2-1992 and as detune was not available could serve it only dt. 13-8-1993 after knowing that detenu was in custody in other criminal case and was properly explained by affidavit - Case was referred to and on advise of Advisory Board State Govt. confirmed order of detention u/s. 8 (f) of the 1974 Act - Detenu filed writ petition in HC but was dismissed - Hence instant appeal by detenu's wife - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, SC in Kamleshkumar Ishwardas Patel V. Union of India and Ors,1995 Indlaw SC 1014 held that right to representation u/art. 22(5)?? against order of detention could be made to Advisory Board and also to detaining authority and as such detaining authority had obligation to inform detunu his right to make representation - Non intimation of his right makes notification invalid and continued detention were vitiated - Non-compliance of procedure prescribed under law makes further detention illegal but that would not affect validity of orginal detention Order passed u/s. 3(i) of the Act - Detention of detune after original detention order would be unjust and improper - Hence, detention Order of appellants husband could not be set aside - SC in Shibapada Mukherjee Vs. The State of West Bengal,1972 Indlaw SC 646 held that detention order could be passed only for 3 months but could be extended on Advisory Board decision - SC in Shri Jagprit Singh V. Union of India & Ors,1990 Indlaw SC 776 held that detunu was informed his right after one month and hence detention of detenu after original period of one year was unjustified - Detaining authorith acted properly in serving detention order and getting it executed - Detention Order was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
