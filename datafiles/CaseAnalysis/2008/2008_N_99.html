Harman Electronics Private Limited and Another v National Panasonic India Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1963; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 610; (2009) 1 SCC 720; AIR 2009 SC 1168; 2009(1) ALL MR 479; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 280; 2009 (1) CompLJ 29; 2009 CRLJ 1109; 2009 (156) DLT 160; 2009 (2) G.L.R. 1467; 2009 (1) JCC (NI) 18; JT 2008 (13) SC 671; 2009 (2) KLT 113; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 889; 2009 (2) MPLJ 549; 2009 (153) PLR 525; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 435; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 458; 2009 (1) RLW 645; 2008(16) SCALE 336; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 487; 2009 (1) UC 173

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Civil Case

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Territorial jurisdiction of a court to try an offence under s. 138 - Whether sending of notice from Delhi itself would give rise to a cause of action for taking cognizance under the Negotiable Instruments Act? - Held, a Court derives a jurisdiction only when the cause of action arose within his jurisdiction - The same cannot be conferred by any act of omission or commission on the part of the accused - Issuance of notice would not by itself give rise to a cause of action but communication of the notice would - Delhi High Court has no jurisdiction to try the case - Appeal allowed.
