Sk. Yusuf v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

14 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 385; (2011) 11 SCC 754; AIR 2011 SC 2283; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2365; JT 2011 (6) SC 640; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 127; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 762; 2011 (4) RLW 3221; 2011(6) SCALE 511; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Extra-judicial confession - Reliability of - Appellant was convicted for the offence punishable u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - Case was based on the circumstantial evidence and extra-judicial confession made by appellant to PW.11 (maternal uncle of the deceased) - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Last seen theory - Whether Courts below were right in holding that there was sufficient material on record to show that the deceased and the appellant were seen together at the place of occurrence? - Held, last seen theory was applicable where the time gap between the point of time when the accused and deceased were last seen alive and when the deceased was found dead was so small that possibility of any person other than the accused being the author of the crime becomes impossible - In the instant case, there was no evidence to the extent that the deceased and appellant were seen together at the place of occurrence or nearby the same in close proximity of time - (B) Extra-judicial confession allegedly made by the appellant before PW.11 and PW.13 - Held, Court while dealing with a circumstance of extra-judicial confession must keep in mind that it was a very weak type of evidence and require appreciation with great caution - Extra-judicial confession must be established to be true and made voluntarily and in a fit state of mind - Words of the witness must be clear, unambiguous and clearly convey that accused was the perpetrator of the crime - In the instant case, PW.11 deposed about extra-judicial confession made by the accused in presence of others, though he was not able to explain who were the other persons as no other person was examined in this respect - Theory of extra-judicial confession revealed by PW.11 did not get corroboration from the statement of PW.13 or any other independent witness or police personnel - (C) Abscondance - Courts below have considered the circumstance of abscondance of the appellant as a circumstance on the basis of which an adverse inference could be drawn against him - Held, appellant absconding could not be taken as a circumstance which give rise to draw an adverse inference against him - Appellant was given benefit of doubt and acquitted of the charges of offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 201 IPC - Appeal allowed.
