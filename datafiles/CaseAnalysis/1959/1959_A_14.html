Asiatic Steam Navigation Company, Limited v Sub-Lt. Arabinda Chakravarti

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 255; AIR 1959 SC 597; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 979; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments; Water

Keywords: Ground Of Appeal

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Municipalities & Local Government - Prohibition Act - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1898, r. 28 - (A) Whether Supreme Court can allow appellant to place entire evidence of appellant's witnesses in support of his contentions in an appeal? - Held, if there being no concurrent findings, lower courts taking different views of evidence, Supreme Court will allow appellant to place entire evidence of appellant's witnesses in support of his contentions - (B) Whether in purely nautical matters court is bound to follow advice of nautical assessors? - Further held, function of nautical assessors is to advice court upon nautical manners and their advice is expert evidence - Decision of case, however, rests entirely with court and even in purely nautical matters court is not bound to follow advice of assessors - But on questions of nautical science and skill great attention must obviously be paid to opinion of assessors since they are only source of information on these points and some reason should be given for disregarding them -(B) Whether nautical assessors in an appeal court are substituted for those previously consulted? - Further held, assessors in an appeal court are not substituted for those previously consulted, they are additional to them - If one adviser or two advisers are to be preferred, it is because in judgment of court advice given is such as, in itself, is more acceptable - There can be no question of any appeal from one set of assessors to another - (C) Whether ship Nizam was responsible for collision by act of negligence? - Further held, act of negligence was primarily responsible for collision - Responsibility for collision lay with Nizam - Kalawati ship took avoiding action by turning hard to port and gave a signal to Nizam to take cognizance of distance between them - Nizam continue steering to port and then took starboard action to get back to right side of channel and get out of way of Kalawati - When Master of Nizam McLure, came on bridge he ordered full speed astern but it was too late and collision took place - Appeal dismissed.
