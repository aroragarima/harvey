Usmanbhai Dawoodbhai Menon And Ors. Etc. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 405; (1988) 2 SCC 271; (1988) SCC (Cr) 318; AIR 1988 SC 922; 1988 CRLJ 938; 1988 CrLR(SC) 234; JT 1988 (1) SC 539B; 1988(1) SCALE 494; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 225

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Fetters, Defence And Internal Security Of India Act, 1971, The Special Courts Act, 1979

Summary: Criminal - TADA Act, 1987, ss. 3 and 4 - (A) Scope and applicability of Act - (B) Prosecution for offence u/ss. 3 and 4 - Bail - (1) HC's jurisdiction to entertain application - (2) Grant of - Considerations for designated court - (3) Grant or refusal of - Order is interlocutory and not final - Appeal against, to SC - Maintainability - (A) Held, it should not ordinarily be resorted to unless Government's law enforcing machinery fails - (B) Held, has no jurisdiction to entertain application - Designated Court should not act mechanically but should examine every case carefully - Held, is not maintainable - Appeal partly allowed.
