Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh v Stesalit Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 133; (2017) 5 SCC 231; AIR 2017 SC 2485; 2017(3) SCALE 274

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Act, 1944, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Central Excise Rules, 1944

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11AB, 11AC - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 173C, 173Q - Penalty imposed - Reduced - Challenged - Appellants/Commissioner filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of Tribunal whereby Tribunal partly allowed appeal of respondent/assessee and reduced the amount of penalty imposed by appellants from Rs.2,06,000/- to Rs.50,000/-.

Held, Tribunal erred in reducing the amount of penalty from Rs.2,06,000/- to Rs.50,000/-. Tribunal, failed to take into consideration the law laid down in case of Union of India v. Dharamendra Textile Processors, 2008 Indlaw SC 1837, which Tribunal was bound to take while deciding the appeal and instead Tribunal wrongly placed reliance on its own decision in case of Escorts JCB Ltd. v. CCE, 2000 (118) ELT 650. SC found that Tribunal gave no justifiable legal reasons for reducing the penalty amount. Therefore, SC was unable to concur with the reasoning and conclusion arrived at by Tribunal which was not legally sustainable and same was set aside and that of adjudicating authority was restored. Appeal allowed.
