Board of Muslim Wakfs v Hadi Begum Smt. and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 930; (1993) Supp1 SCC 192; AIR 1992 SC 1083; JT 1992 (2) SC 385; 1992(1) SCALE 706; 1992 (1) UJ 627

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Jaipur Land Acquisition Act, 1943 - Acquisition - Compensation - Challenged - Proceedings for acquisition of the land comprised were initiated under the provisions of 1943 Act - Petitioners filed a petitions

wherein he claimed that he was in possession of the land in dispute and that the State was threatening to disturb his possession - State contended that the possession over land in dispute had been taken as far back as in the years 1945 and the land had vested in the State according to law in view of the acquisition proceedings and that the Urban Improvement Board, Jaipur, was justified in taking steps to sell the land - Single Judge set aside the order passed by the Commissioner of Wakfs and declared that the inclusion of the disputed property in the list of wakf property in the Rajasthan as amended by the subsequent order of the Board could not be binding on the petitioners in the writ petitions - DB upheld the order of the Single Judge - Hence, instant appeals.

Held,

the Wakfs Commissioner was vested with the jurisdiction to arrive at a decision on this question on a consideration of the said evidence and such a determination by Wakfs Commissioner could not be held to be vitiated on the ground of lack of jurisdiction for the reason that the Wakfs Commissioner had committed an error in construing the documentary evidence produced before him. The order dt. 9-7-1962 passed by the Wakfs Commissioner and the list of wakf properties as amended by notification dt. 29-6-1967, published under sub-section (2) of Section 5 of the Act were not open to challenge before the HC on the ground that the finding recorded by the Wakfs Commissioner was not supported by the evidence on record. The judgment of the DB setting aside the order of the Wakfs Commissioner and declaring as invalid the inclusion of the land measuring 31 bighas and 3 biswas, in excess of land measuring 1 bigha and 17 biswas on which the mosque and the makbara are situated, in the list of wakf properties as amended by the subsequent notification dt. 29-6-1967, cannot be upheld and must be set aside. Impugned order is set aside. Appeals allowed.
