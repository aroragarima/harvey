Jaipal Singh v Smt. Sumitra Mahajan And Anr

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 237; (2004) 4 SCC 522; AIR 2004 SC 2066; 2004 (5) AWC 4207; 2004 (4) ESC 549; JT 2004 (4) SC 542; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 562; 2004(4) SCALE 129; 2004 (3) SLT 219; 2004 (3) Supreme 3; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2423

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, Fundamental Rules, 1922, All India Services (Conditions of Service- Residuary Matters) Rules, 1960

Summary: [A.] Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 102(1), 102(1)(c) - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 116-A, 83, 83(1), 83(1)(a) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Election Petition - Material facts - Whether appellant, member of IAS, held an office of profit on date of scrutiny - His request for waiver of notice period; that appointing authority had received notice of specified date and that his request for waiver stood granted on date of scrutiny and he ceased to be a Govt. servant - All constitute material facts which should have been pleaded - Returned candidates should not be taken by surprise - HC was right in dismissing election petition for want of material facts - Appeal dismissed.
