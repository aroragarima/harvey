Suresh Prasad Yadav v Jai Prakash Mishra & Ors

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A. Alagiriswami, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 184; (1975) 4 SCC 822; AIR 1975 SC 376; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 21

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Representation of the People Conduct of Elections Rules, 1961, rr. 20, 38, 53, 56, 56(7), 57(2), 63, 93 and 93(1) - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Election - (A) Inspection and recount of ballot papers - When to be ordered - (B) Production and inspection of election papers - Custody contemplated by r. 93(1) is the post-election custody - Objection raised at counting of votes that some unused ballot papers in the packet did not bear mark or signature as required by r. 38 (1) - (C) Counting of votes - Preparation of result sheet - Form 20 - No need of preparing final result sheet as table wise, also - Held, conditions stated - Act of Returning Officer in opening the packet and in inspecting and counting the unused ballot papers does not amount to an illegality - It is sufficient if the final result sheet is candidatewise and roundwise - Appeal Dismissed.
