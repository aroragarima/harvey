Sunder Das and Others v Gajananrao and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2026; (1997) 9 SCC 701; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 286; AIR 1997 SC 1686; 1997 (1) CLT 140; JT 1996 (11) SC 255; 1996(9) SCALE 336; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 66; 1997 (5) Supreme 519

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Hindu Law - Ancestral property - Alienation by Karta - Legal necessity - Existence of - Sale deed mentions that sale is for family necessity - Alienator's son was minor when transaction took place - Father serving as Upper Division Clerk in Court - Sound moral character - Being Clerk in Court must have well versed in ways of world and disposed of property, house which was a ruin - Vendees also making all possible enquiry as to legal necessity of family - Suit refiled before Court of competent jurisdiction - Held, family necessity not doubted - Father could have disposed of share of minor sons - Sale is binding on minor sons - Plaintiff was entitled to exclude period from date of filing of suit to date when High Court returned for representation to competent court - What is to be seen is whether plaintiffs were bona fide prosecuting suit before a court, which from lack of jurisdiction was unable to entertain it.
