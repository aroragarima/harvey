Bimlendu Kumar Chatterjee v Dipa Chatterjee and others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 203; (2001) 8 SCC 5; AIR 2002 SC 2528; 2001 (45) ALR 256; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 317; JT 2001 (8) SC 1; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 118; 2001(6) SCALE 403; 2001 (6) SLT 636B; 2001 (7) Supreme 83; 2001 (2) UC 575

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Custody of child - Appellant is the husband of respondent No. 1 - Dispute raised in the case relates to the right of appellant to have custody or at least a right to visit regularly his daughter who is now residing with respondent No. 1 - Appeal was allowed by DB and that part of the order by which the Single Judge had directed that the child will remain with the mother with liberty to the father to go and see the child at least once a week, was set-aside - Respondent No. 1 having failed to restore custody of the child to him, appellant filed an application before the HC seeking implementation of the order of the Family Court giving custody of the child to him - Considering the said application, DB passed the order under challenge - Hence instant appeal.

Held, order under challenge is clearly unsustainable and has to be set aside. Counsel appearing for the respondents fairly accepted the position that the impugned order is wholly unsupportable.

Then the questions arises what will be an appropriate order to be passed on the facts and circumstances of the case. The appellant, who is the father of the child, submitted that after long years of litigation, he has not yet got even a right to see his child for some time at regular intervals. The grievance of the appellant cannot be brushed aside. A humanitarian approach is necessary for solving the problem. In the result, the appeal is allowed. Leave is granted to the appellant to file an application for appropriate interim arrangement for getting custody of his child or for making an arrangement enabling him to see his child at regular intervals before the Court in which the suit/appeal stated to have been filed by him is pending. If such an application is filed, the Court will dispose of the same as expeditiously as possible. Appeal allowed.
