State Of U.P. v Durga Prasad

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 129; (1974) SCC (Cr) 525; (1974) SCC (Cr) 828; (1975) 3 SCC 210; AIR 1974 SC 2136; 1974 CRLJ 2465; 1974 CrLR(SC) 553; [1975] 1 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Railway Act 1890, Railway Protection Act XXIII of 1957

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 155, 156, 162 and 160 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 193 - Railway Protection Act XXIII of 1957, ss. 8(1), 3(a), 7, 2(c), (e), 6, 9(1), 9(3), 11 and 14 - Issues arise (A) Whether inquiry which an officer of Railway protection Force holds u/s. 8(1) of Act is an investigation within meaning of CrPC? - Right and duty of an investigating officer to file a police report or a charge-sheet on conclusion of investigation is hallmark of an investigation under CrPC - Officer conducting an inquiry u/s. 8(1) of Act cannot initiate court proceedings by filing a police report as is evident from two Provisos to s. 8(2) of Act - On conclusion of an enquiry u/s. 8(1) of Act, therefore, if officer of Force is of opinion that there is sufficient evidence or reasonable ground of suspicion against accused, he must file a complaint u/s. 190(1)(a) in order that Magistrate concerned may take cognizance of offence - Thus an officer conducting an inquiry u/s. 8(1) of Act does not possess all attributes of an officer-in-charge of a police station investigating a case under Chapter XIV of CrPC - He possesses but a part of those attributes limited to purpose of holding inquiry - (B) Whether entire trial is vitiated if signatures of witnesses are obtained on statements made by them during course of inquiry? - By s. 9(4) of Act every inquiry u/s. 8(1) of Act is deemed to be a 'judicial proceeding' within meaning of s. 193 of IPC - In order that prosecution u/s. 193 of IPC may not be rendered illusory and duty to state truth should have a real and practical sanction for its enforcement, officer conducting inquiry must have right to obtain signature of person making statement - Apart from statements made by witnesses during inquiry which were brought on record of case by learned Magistrate, there was before him evidence of witnesses who were examined in court and therefore, entire trial could in any case not be said to have been vitiated - Held, evidence shows clearly that respondent was in possession of Railway property and had thereby committed an offence u/s. 3(a) of Act - Order accordingly.
