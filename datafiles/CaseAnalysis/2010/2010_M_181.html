Punjab And Haryana High Court At Chandigarh … v Megh Raj Garg And Another

Supreme Court of India

20 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 433; (2010) 6 SCC 482; AIR 2010 SC 2295; 2011 (1) AWC 383; 2010 (126) FLR 149; 2010 (3) JhrCR(SC) 154; JT 2010 (6) SC 334; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1993; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2266; 2010 (5) MLJ(SC) 953; 2010(6) SCALE 61; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 172; 2010 (4) SLR 755

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Service

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, Punjab Civil Service (First Amendment) Rules

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Change in date of birth after joining service - Respondent No.1 joined service as Sub Judge-cum-Judicial Magistrate, II Class in March, 1973 - His date of birth was recorded in the service book as 27-3-1936 - After 10 years of joining the service, respondent No.1 submitted an application to the concerned authority of Punjab University for amendment of the date of birth recorded in the matriculation certificate by asserting that his correct date of birth was 27-3-1938 but by mistake the same was recorded as 27-3-1936 - University made the necessary change in the matriculation certificate of respondent no. 1 - Subsequently, respondent no. 1 represented to the State Government for making corresponding change in the date of birth recorded in the service book - Said prayer was rejected by State Government - Respondent No.1 challenged the said decision of the State Government in civil suit - Trial court decreed the suit and declared that rejection of the representation made by respondent No.1 for correction of his date of birth was illegal and void - On appeal, lower appellate Court agreed with the trial Court on all the issues and dismissed the appeal preferred by the appellant - Single Judge held that the decree passed by the trial Court, which was confirmed by the lower appellate Court was legally correct and justified - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld and date of birth of respondent no. 1 be rightly changed after 10 years of joining the judicial service? - Held, no - Being a law graduate, respondent No.1 must have been aware of the date of birth i.e., 27-3-1936 recorded in his matriculation certificate and this must be the reason why he mentioned that date in the application form submitted to the Public Service Commission - If the correct date of birth of respondent No.1 was 27-3-1938 and this was supported by the certificates issued by the schools, then he would have immediately after joining the service made an application to the University for change of date of birth recorded in the matriculation certificate and persuaded the concerned authority to decide the same so as to enable him to move the State Government and the HC for making corresponding change in the date of birth recorded in his service book - However, respondent No.1 waited for more than ten years after entering into service and submitted an application dt. 27-10-1983 to the University for effecting change in the date of birth recorded in the matriculation certificate by citing the school certificates as the basis for his claim - All the courts overlooked the stark reality that respondent No.1 had made application for change of date of birth recorded in the matriculation certificate after more than ten years of his entry into government service - Suit filed by respondent No.1 for correction of the date of birth recorded in his service book was clearly misconceived and the trial Court committed a serious error by passing a decree in favour of respondent No.1 and the lower appellate Court and the HC repeated the same error by refusing to set aside the decree passed by the trial Court - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
