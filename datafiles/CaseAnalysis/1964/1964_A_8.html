A. Venkata Subba Rao v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 363; AIR 1965 SC 1773; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 42; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 577

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: Trade - Practice & Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, s. 3 - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 62 - (A) Whether description of appellants as 'procuring agents' would make them agents of Govt. so as to enable Govt. to claim any profit made by them over and above 'remuneration' permitted to them ? - Held, they are not agents of Govt. as (1) there is no remuneration paid to agents - Margin between procurement price and price at which procured paddy or rice could be sold could not be termed as 'remuneration' as there was also a similar margin between price at which a wholesaler could buy rice and that at which he should sell and so, it was case of retail dealer - There is no relationship of agent and principal (2) If theory of principal-agent relationship was discarded as untenable, there would be no legal basis at all for surcharge - It would then be in effect a tax imposed by an executive fiat without any legislative sanction - On facts that procuring agents never stood as agents of Govt. or in a fiduciary capacity to Govt. so as to make Govt. entitled to claim excess profits earned by them - Appeal dismissed.
