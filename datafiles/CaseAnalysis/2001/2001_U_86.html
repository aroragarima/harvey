Union Public Service Commission v A. Cletus and others

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21490; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 321; 2002 (1) SLR 331

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Backward Classes, Vacancies, Interview, Procedure, Rule, Examination

Summary: Service - Rules for Competitive Examination for Civil Services 1995, rr. 3, 15, 16 - Minimum qualifying marks - Reservation rule - Legality - Respondent approached Tribunal challenging the rr. 15, 16 of the Rules contending that the said Rules have to be r/w r. 3 of the Rules, which provides for reservation - Tribunal allowed the petitions - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, interpretation placed before Tribunal appears to be perfectly in order inasmuch as the Screening test done through a preliminary examination is applicable to all the candidates irrespective of the group to which they belong. There is no need to categorise the candidates as to whether they belong to different reserved categories or not and, thereafter, find out the group to which they belong before they qualify in the preliminary examination. Appeals dismissed.
