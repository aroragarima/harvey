Kabul Singh v Kundan Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 354; (1969) 2 SCC 452; AIR 1970 SC 340; 1970 (1) SCJ 469; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 845

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 62 - Representation of the People Act, 1950, s. 23(3) and 30 - Election - (A) Inclusion of name in electoral roll - After prescribed date - (B) Person's name included in electoral roll as on last date of making nomination paper - Entitlement to vote - (C) Finality of electoral roll cannot be challenged in a proceeding challenging validity of election - Right to vote being purely a statutory right, validity of any vote is to be examined on basis of provisions of the Act - Held, such inclusion is prohibited whether application for inclusion was made before or after that date - He is entitled to vote unless prohibited by any provision - Entries in electoral roll held, are final and are not open to challenge - Appeal Dismissed.
