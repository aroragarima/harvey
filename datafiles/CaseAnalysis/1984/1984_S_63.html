Amar Kant Choudhary v State Of Bihar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 117; (1984) SCC (L&S) 173; (1984) 1 SCC 694; AIR 1984 SC 531; 1984 (10) ALR 110; 1984 (48) FLR 241; 1984 LabIC 291; 1984 (1) LLN 249; 1984(1) SCALE 10; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 299; 1984 (1) SLJ 356; 1984 (1) SLR 470; 1984 UJ 1007

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Indian Po) ice Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulation 1955a, Indian Police Service (Recruitment) Rules 1954

Summary: Service - Constitution, arts. 311 and 309, proviso - Police Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1954, r. 9(1) - Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, regn. 4 - Service Rules - Police - (A) Employee's case not considered for promotion to IPS Cadre on ground of ue to adverse remarks - Effect of non-communication of remarks to him - Subsequently expunged by the State Govt. - (B) Entry of adverse remarks in confidential roll - Determination of proper procedure - Held, decision of Selection Committee vitiated - The follwing two systems suggested : (1) Make remarks, in the roll in presence of officer concerned with right to make representation to higher authorities and (2) Serve copy of such remarks on the officer before submission of confidential roll - Appeal allowed
