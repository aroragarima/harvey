Murugesan S/o Muthu and others v State through Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 360; (2012) 10 SCC 383; AIR 2013 SC 274; 2013 (1) JCC 118; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 515; 2013 (1) OLR 500; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 791; 2012(10) SCALE 378; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302,120B,34,149148,332 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 378, 379 - Murder - Incomplete Dying Declaration - Hostile Witnesses - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 302,120B,34,149148,332 of IPC - Prosecution case was that appellants had enmity due to land with deceased persons and were involved in several litigations and when both parties came to attend court, appellants party attacked and killed D1,D2 and D3 - Prosecution on complaint given by D1 registered case u/s. 302 - Prosecution examined 30 witnesses and exhibited large number of documents besides 20 material objects, but Trial Court that charges leveled against appellants were not proved beyond reasonable doubt and acquitted all 23 accused - Aggrieved appeal before HC, acquittal of trial court was reversed and convicted accused No. 1 to 19 and maintained order of acquittal against remaining accused - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that none of witnesses supported case of prosecution and further case u/s. 302 of IPC was registered even before death of any persons - Whether HC, was justified in convicting appellants - Held, failure on part of PW.5 and PW.7 to use fire arms issued to them despite an assault committed by as many as 23 persons resulting to death of 3, as prosecution had alleged, was both mysterious and inexplicable - Registration of FIR u/s 302 IPC when deceased persons were still alive, efficacy of dying declaration when maker thereof had slipped into a coma even before completing statement would had a serious effect on capacity of D1 to make such a statement - Certification made by PW. 21 with regard to condition of deceased was definitely not last word, though ordinarily and in normal course such an opinion should be accepted and acted upon by court, in cases, where circumstances so demand such opinions must be carefully balanced with all other surrounding facts and circumstances - Conviction of accused appellants recorded by HC under different provisions of IPC and sentences imposed could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.
