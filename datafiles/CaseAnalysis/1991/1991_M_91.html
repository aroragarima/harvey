U.P.State Road Transport Corporation And Anr. v Mohd. Ismail And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Veeraswami Ramaswami, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 864; (1991) 3 SCC 239; (1991) SCC (L&S) 893; AIR 1991 SC 1099; AIR 1991 SCW 1095; 1991 (17) ATC 234; 1991 (2) CompLJ 170; 1991 (62) FLR 841; JT 1991 (2) SC 292; 1991 (2) LLJ 332; 1991 (2) LLN 406; 1991(1) SCALE 734; 1991 (1) SCJ 521; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 274; 1991 (2) SLR 600; 1991 (2) UJ 61; 1991 (2) UPLBEC 855

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation Employees (Other than Officers) Service regulation 1981

Summary: Service - Road Transport Corporations Act ,1950, s. 45 - Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation Employees (other than Officers) Service Regulations (1981), Regn.17(3), Proviso - Service matter - Proviso to Regn. 17(3) is not proviso but substantive provision - Discretion conferred on the corporation to offer alternative job to retrenched driver - (A) Whether it confer vested right on driver to get alternative job - (B) Whether it is with in the jurisdiction of the court to issue direction to offer alternative job - (C) Whether any denial of, by Corporation to itself by issuing notifications to Regional Managers directing that retrenched employees be paid benefits like retrenchment compensation under U.P. Industrial Disputes Act is warranted - (D) Is the corporation under any obigation to offer job to a driver found medically unfit - Held, provides discretion to Corporation to offer alternative job to retrenched driver - It is beyond jurisdiction of Court.to issue such directions -denial not warranted - Discretion is not absolute ,it has to be guided by consideration that public service rendered by it has to be efficient and effective - Appeal allowed.
