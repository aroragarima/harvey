Delhi Water Supply And Sewage Disposalcommittee And Ors. v R.K. Kashyap And Or8.

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 89; (1989) Supp1 SCC 194; (1989) SCC (L&S) 253; AIR 1989 SC 278; 1989 (9) ATC 784; 1988 (36) DLT 378; JT 1988 (4) SC 421; 1989 (1) LLJ 171; 1989 (1) LLN 248; 1988(2) SCALE 1390; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 633; 1988 (6) SLR 33; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 633; 1989 (1) UJ 8

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Union Public Service Commission (Consultation by Delhi Municipal corporation) Regulation, 1959 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, s. 92 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Ad hoc appointment - Seniority - Entitled to - Whether decision of the DB that determination of seniority of officers is not governed by any statutory rule and continuous officiation in post should be basis is correct? - Held, in regard to officers of Delhi Municipal Corporation where there was ad hoc appointments followed by regularisation of service, Delhi High Court has taken a consistent view that such persons should get their service in ad hoc appointment for determining seniority in absence of any specific rule to contrary - If ad hoc appointment or temporary appointment is made without considering claims of seniors in cadre, service rendered in such appointment should not be counted for seniority in cadre - Length of service in ad hoc appointment or stop-gap arrangement made in exigencies of service without considering claims of all eligible and suitable persons in cadre ought not be reckoned for purpose of determining seniority in promotional cadre - To give benefit of such service to a favored few would be contrary to equality of opportunity enshrined in arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
