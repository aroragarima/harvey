Prashant Bharti v State Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 34; (2013) 9 SCC 293; AIR 2013 SC 2753; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1123; 2013 CRLJ 3839; JT 2013 (2) SC 240; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 399; 2013 (4) RLW 3155; 2013(1) SCALE 652; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 328, 354 and 376 - Rape - Outraging modesty - Proof of allegations - Farming of Charges - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly raped complainant (prosecutrix) and committed offences u/ss. 328, 354 and 376 - Trial Court framed charges against appellant u/ss. 354, 328 and 376 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Whether charges framed against appellant would culminate into his conviction - Held, evidence revealed complainant's marriage was subsisted till 23.9.2008, when divorced by mutual consent - In supplementary statement dt.21.2.2007, complainant accused appellant of having had physical relations during with her on 23.12.2006, 25.12.2006 and 1.1.2007, and for a period of more than 1 year and 6 months thereafter, she had remained married to 'L' - In such a fact situation, assertion made by complainant, that the appellant had physical relations with her, on the assurance that he would marry her, was per se false and unacceptable - Complainant was clearly aware of fact that she had a subsisting valid marriage with 'L' - If judgment and decree dt.23.9.2008 produced before Court by complainant herself was taken into consideration along with factual position depicted in supplementary statement dt.21.2.2007, it would clearly emerge, that complainant was in a relationship of adultery on 23.12.2006, 25.12.2006 and 1.1.2007 with appellant, while she was validly married to her previous husband 'L' - Assertion made by complainant, that she was induced to a physical relationship by appellant, on the basis of a promise to marry her, stands irrefutably falsified - Medical evidence revealed that alleged sexual relationship with appellant, was made more than 1 month after alleged occurrences and in absence of any scientific evidence, proof of sexual intercourse between complainant and appellant would be based on an assertion made by complainant - A consensual relationship without any assurance, obviously would not substantiate offence u/s. 376 of IPC, alleged appellant - Allegations leveled by complainant, which culminated in registration of a FIR and supplementary statement, would never lead to appellant's conviction - Appellant was not at the place of occurrence, as alleged in complaint - Verification of mobile phone call details of complainant revealed, that on the date of occurrence, no calls were made by appellant to complainant, and that, it was complainant who had made calls to appellant - Complainant, on and around the time referred to in complaint, was at different places, as per verification of IO on the basis of her mobile phone call details - At the time when complainant alleged, that appellant had misbehaved with her and had outraged her modesty, she was actually in conversation with her friends (as per the verification made by IO on the basis of her mobile phone call details - Through supplementary statement dt.21.2.2007, complainant leveled allegations against appellant for offence of rape - Complainant's subsequent marriage with 'M' on 30.9.2008 was evidenced by a 'certificate of marriage' dated 30.9.2008 - It was apparent that complainant could not have been induced into a physical relationship, based on an assurance of marriage - Physical relationship between complainant and appellant was admittedly consensual - Acknowledged consensual physical relationship between parties would not constitute an offence u/s. 376 of IPC -FIR, charge sheet and impugned order framing charges against appellant was quashed - Appeal allowed.
