Pabitra Kumar Roy & Anr. v Alita D’ Souza

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 527; (2006) 8 SCC 344; AIR 2006 SC 3355; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 417; 2006(9) SCALE 606; 2006 (8) SCJ 175; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 678

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 3(2) - Registration Act, 1908 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 111(g), 114 - Question relates to applicability of s.3(2) to leases which were executed for periods of over twenty years but containing a clause allowing prior determination at instance of either lessor or lessee - Held, lessee/tenant had fully enjoyed period of lease of twenty years - Mere inclusion of a clause for prior determination of a lease, which is otherwise for a fixed period of more than twenty years, will not ipso facto bring it within exception contemplated in proviso to s.3(2) - If lease was allowed to run its full course, both lease and conditions contained therein would come to an end and would cease to be operative and clause for prior determination would no longer be available as a defence against eviction - Respondent is granted time to vacate premises - Appeal allowed.
