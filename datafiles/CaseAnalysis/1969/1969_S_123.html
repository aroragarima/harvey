Heavy Engineering Mazdoor Union v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 343; (1969) 1 SCC 765; AIR 1970 SC 82; [1969] 39 Comp Cas 905; 1969 (19) FLR 27; 1970 LabIC 212; 1969 (2) LLJ 549; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 995; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 995

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(a),(g) and 10 - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 34(2) and 617 - Industrial Employees (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industry - Corporation incorporated under Companies Act - But not directly by Central Govt. or any of its departments - All shares are owned by President of India and some officials - Reference made to tribunal - Application for modifying standing order actually pending before certifying officer under 1946 Act - (A) Whether Industry is carried by Central Govt. in considering s. 2(a) - (B) Meaning of 'Under authority of' in light of ss. 2(a), 2(g) and 10 - (C) Whether reference u/s. 10 can be precluded - (D) Whether company is an agent of Central Govt. - In light of s. 34(2)and 617 of Companies Act and s. 2(a) of 1947 Act - Held, industry is not one carried by Central Govt. - Controlled wholly or partially by govt. - Reference not precluded and is valid - Co. not an agent - Appeal dismissed.
