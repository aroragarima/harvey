Tamil Nadu Housing Board, Chennai v M. Meiyappan and others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 917; 2011 (84) ALR 760; 2011 (2) AWC 1459; 2011 (1) CTC 714; JT 2010 (12) SC 29; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 171; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 12; 2011 (112) RD 493; 2010(11) SCALE 411; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 1184

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition proceedings - Challenged to - Delay and Laches - Land was acquired for providing housing facilities to people of low income group - Award in relation to the said lands was made and the compensation was awarded - After 16 years, respondents Nos.1 to 17 filed a writ petition before the HC challenging the acquisition of their lands, which was allowed - On appeal, DB without examining the question of delay and laches dismissed the appeal - Hence, present appeal - Whether writ petition was to be dismissed on the ground of delay and laches on the part of respondent Nos.1 to 17? - Held, respondents did not furnish any explanation as to why it took them 16 years to challenge the acquisition of their lands, when admittedly they were aware of the acquisition of their lands and had in fact participated in these proceedings before the Land Acquisition Collector - Hence, HC ought not to have entertained the writ petition of the respondents after 16 years of the passing of the award - HC should have dismissed the writ petition at the threshold on the ground of delay and laches on the part of respondent Nos.1 to 17 - Impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
