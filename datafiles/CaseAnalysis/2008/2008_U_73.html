Mukund Swarup Mishra v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2705; (2008) 15 SCC 243; AIR 2009 SC 1684; JT 2009 (1) SC 281; 2008(13) SCALE 698

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Contract & Commercial - Allotment of LPG distributorship - Cancellation - News daily carried a news reports alleging irregularities in allotment of Retail Outlets, LPG distributorship and SKO-LDO dealerships, by selection of relatives/associates of political functionaries - 1st respondent/Union of India directed the Ministry of Petroleum and Natural Gas to initiate steps to cancel all allotments made on the basis of recommendations of Dealer Selection Boards - Ministry, consequently, by vide order 9-8-2002, cancelled all the allotment - Cancellation order was challenged by various allottees from different States - SC upheld the cancellation of all allotments except 413 cases - SC, subsequently, constituted a SC Committee to examine 413 cases and submitted its report - Committee, out of 413 cases, rejected 8 cases and considered 409 cases wherein 112 cases were upheld and remaining cases were rejected on the ground of political patronage - Several allottees filed objection to the Committee report - Whether the objections of the rejected allottees would be sustainable - Held, Committee had considered in detail individual cases and submitted the report - SC therefore would consider a complaint of an allottee who can successfully put forth his complaint and satisfy the court that in the facts and circumstances of the case, the finding of the Committee that the allotment was not on merit, was not correct but only in those individual cases the court would consider him and grant relief to such applicant - It however could not be said that the report of the Committee was without power, authority or jurisdiction or was uncalled for and liable to be ignored - SC, hence, considered and disposed of the cases relating to various States - Further, approval by the Committee in respect of 112 allotments was accepted and consequently, the cancellation of allotments in those cases was set aside - Wherever the Committee had not approved the allotment as not being on merits, and the allottees had not filed objections to the Committee's report or filed objections belatedly (which were not accepted), the non- approval of selection/allotment were upheld - Petitions disposed of.
