Gulzari Lal Agarwal v Accounts Officer

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 256; (1996) 10 SCC 590; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 569; AIR 1996 SCW 3926; 1996 (4) CCC 39; [1996] 87 Comp Cas 1001; 2000 (5) CompLJ 96; 1996 (3) CPJ 12; 1996 (4) CTJ 851; JT 1996 (9) SC 103; 1996(7) SCALE 323; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 708; 1996 (7) Supreme 505; 1996 (2) UJ 761

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 14(2A) and 18 - Consumer Protection Rules, 1987, rr. 6(9) and 6(10) - Complaint filed by appellant regarding inflated telephone bills - Order made in favour of appellant - On appeal, National Commission held that order passed by State Commission is manifestly contrary to a mandatory provision contained in s. 14(2 A) r/w s. 18 of Act as it was made by two other members of said Commission without 'junction' of President - Whether impugned order passed by National Commission is legal? - It is well settled that every provision in Act needs to be construed harmoniously with a view to promote object and spirit of Act but while doing so, no violence would be done to plain language used in section - Only harmonious construction that could be given to sub - section (2) and (2A) of s. 14 r/w sub - rr. (9) and (10) is that as and when President of State Commission is functional, he along with at least one member sitting together shall conduct proceeding but where President being non - functional, sub - rr. (9) and (10) of r. 6 will govern proceedings - Held, National Commission committed an error in holding that order passed by two members of State Commission without junction of President is 'illegal and void' - Impugned order to that extent is set aside - Further held, National Commission was right in applying law laid down in Morgan Stanley Mutual Fund v. Kartick Das, 1994 Indlaw SC 342 that Consumer Forum has no jurisdiction or power to pass any interim order pending disposal of original complaint filed before it - Impugned order in this behalf is confirmed - Appeal partly allowed.
