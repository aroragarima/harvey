Tara Chand Vyas v Chairman and Disciplinary Authority and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1037; (1997) 4 SCC 565; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1241; 1997 (30) ALR 389; 1997 (1) CLR 809; 1997 (76) FLR 176; JT 1997 (3) SC 500; 1997 (2) LLJ 26; 1997 (4) LLN 5; 1997(2) SCALE 569; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 472; 1997 (2) SLR 378; 1997 (3) Supreme 312

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 38, 39 and 46 - The petitioner was imputed with the charges that while working as a Branch Manager of the respondent-Gramin Bank he derelicted in the performance of the duties in making payment of loans without ensuring supply of implements to the loanees and deposit of adequate security from the dealers as a consequence of which the respondent-Bank was put to loss - On the basis thereof, the disciplinary authority found that the charges were established and imposed the proposed punishment - Impugned order came to be passed, on appeal, by the Board - The writ petition filed by the petitioner was dismissed - Thus, this special leave petition - Whether the respondents were justified was right in holding that the appellant derelicted in the performance of his duties in making payment of loans as a consequence of which the Bank was put to loss? - Charge against the appellant is based upon the documentary evidence on record and copies thereof was supplied to him - Respondents had discussed each charge and given reasons which were considered by the disciplinary authority thereby concluding that the charges were proved - There is no error apparent on the face of the record, warranting interference - The order of the High Court is upheld - Appeal dismissed.
