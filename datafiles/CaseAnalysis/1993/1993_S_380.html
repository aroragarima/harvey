Asha Kaul (Mrs) and Another v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1800; (1993) 2 SCC 573; (1993) SCC (L&S) 637; AIR 1993 SCW 2314; 1993 (24) ATC 576; 1993 (1) CLR 966; JT 1993 (2) SC 688; 1993 (2) LLN 64; 1994 (1) MLJ(SC) 29; 1993(2) SCALE 545; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 94; 1993 (2) SLR 560

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Judicial) Recruitment Rules, 1967

Summary: Service - Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Judicial) Recruitment Rules, 1967, rr. 39 and 41 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 320(1), 317, 318, 319 and 323 - Matter pertains to approval and publication of select list of District Munsifs prepared by J and K Public Service Commission - High Court intimated Govt. ten vacancies in category of Munsifs - HC requested Govt. to select twenty candidates instead of ten - Three lists prepared - Govt. approved names of thirteen persons out of list - Petition to direct Govt. to approve and publish list - Statement that approval of remaining persons in list under active consideration - Petition withdrawn - No further names approved by Govt. -Direction issued to State Govt. to approve and publish list of remaining candidates - On appeal held, that State Govt. cannot alter or tamper with order of merit determined by Commission but can refuse to appoint a candidate who is not meritorious - Appeal - Whether once Public Service Commission prepares a select list, Govt. is bound to approve it? - Govt. is appointing authority for Munsifs but in matter of selection and appointment Govt. has no absolute power - Govt. is directed to consult Public Service Commission on all matters relating to methods of recruitment to civil services - Constitution contemplates Commission to be an independent and effective body outside Govt. control - R. 39 does not confer an absolute power upon Govt. to disapprove or cancel select list sent by Public Service Commission - However, if Govt. is satisfied, after due inquiry that selection has been vitiated it can refuse to approve select list - Must provide reasons for doing so - Held, by merely approving list of twenty, there was no obligation upon Govt. to appoint them forthwith - Appointment depended upon availability of vacancies - List remains valid for one year from date of its approval and publication - If Govt. wanted to disapprove or reject list, it ought to have done so within a reasonable time - When it approved list to extent of thirteen, it ought to have approved entire list - Further held, appears that Govt's action in not approving rest of seven names in select list is unsustainable - However, during period of one year from date of approval of thirteen names no vacancy had arisen - Any direction at this stage to approve list would be futile - Appeal dismissed.
