Calcutta Port Trust and others v Anadi Kumar Das (Capt.) and others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 827; (2014) 3 SCC 617; 2014 (2) AWC 1176; 2013(14) SCALE 212; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 862; 2014 (1) SLR 379

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Ex Gratia Payment, Contributory Provident Fund Scheme

Summary: (1) Employment-Pension Scheme-Whenever employer introduces Pension Scheme or makes same applicable to retired employees and give them opportunity to exercise option-Circulars/instructions issued for that purpose should either be communicated to the retirees or made known to them by some reasonable mode-Mere display of notice/instructions on notice board of Head Office cannot be treated as intimation thereof to retired employees/officers.(2) Employment-Pension scheme-Exercise of option to switch over from C.P.F. scheme to Pension Scheme-Respondent No. 1 availed benefits under C.P.F. scheme-He submitted application for exercise of option in terms of circular dated 19.2.1986 after 18 years of his retirement-Not possible for any person of ordinary prudence to believe that respondent No. 1 was not aware of Pension Scheme and opportunities given to retired employees/officers to exercise option to switch over from C.P.F. scheme to Pension Scheme-Not possible to accept specious argument of respondent No. 1 that he had no knowledge of Pension Scheme and circulars issued in 1984 and 1986-Impugned order of Division Bench of High Court-Set aside-Order of single Judge-Restored-However, in view of peculiar facts of the case, appellant directed to allow respondent No. 1 to exercise option in terms of circular dated 19.2.1986-Direction not to be treated as precedent for other cases pending before High Court.
