Bakshi Ram and Others v Brij Lal

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 874; (1994) Supp3 SCC 198; AIR 1995 SC 395; 1994 (2) CCC 489; 1994 (2) DMC 345; JT 1994 (5) SC 422; 1994(3) SCALE 352; 1995 (1) SCJ 64; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 795; 1994 (Supp) ShimLC 343; 1994 (2) UJ 439

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 4 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s.35 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Hindu Law - Succession - Rights of reversioners - Suit - By remote reversioners after death of a lienor for their share under compromise decree - To set aside alienation of property - Decree becoming final - Held, a liencee cannot take objection that next reversioners being alive - Suit not maintainable.
