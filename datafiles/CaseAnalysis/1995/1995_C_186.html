Secretary, Ministry of Information and Another v Cricket Association of Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, P.B. Sawant, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2353; (1995) 2 SCC 161; AIR 1995 SC 1236; AIR 1995 SCW 1856; JT 1995 (2) SC 110; 1995(1) SCALE 539; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 1036

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Freedom Of Press, Prasar Bharati (Broadcasting Corporation of India) Act, 1990, Morality, Licences, Madras Maintenance of Public Order Act, 1949, East Punjab Public Safety Act, 1949, Television Act, 1954, Import Control Act, Federal Communications Act, 1934, Wireless Telegraph Act, 1933, Broadcasting Act, 1990, Italian Constitution, 1947, Radio Act, 1927, Broadcasting Act, 1991, Cinematograph Rules

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19 - Madras Maintenance of Public Order Act, 1949 - Government of India Act, 1935 - East Punjab Public Safety Act, 1949 - Drugs and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisements) Act, 1954 - Newspaper (Price and Page) Act, 1956 - Cinematograph Act, 1952 - Federal Communications Act, 1934 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Wireless Telegraph Act, 1933 - Prasar Bharati (Broadcasting Corporation of India) Act, 1990 - Television Act, 1954 - Broadcasting Act, 1990 - Italian Constitution, 1947 - Radio Act, 1927 - Broadcasting Act, 1991 - Whether Govt. or the Govt. agencies have a monopoly of creating terrestrial signals and of telecasting them or refusing to telecast them? - Court held, right of free speech and expression includes right to receive and impart information - For ensuring the free speech right of the citizens of this country, broadcasting media should be under the control of the public as distinct from Government and this is command implicit in art. 19(1)(a) - Appeals, petition and applications disposed of.
