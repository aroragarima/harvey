Rajpati v Bachan and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 450; (1980) 4 SCC 116; (1980) SCC (Cr) 927; 1980 (17) ACC 274; AIR 1981 SC 18; 1980 CRLJ 1276; 1980 CrLR(SC) 540; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 92; 1980 UJ 918

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preliminary Order

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 ss. 145, 145(5) and 482 - Proceedings u/s. 145 was started by Magistrate against respondents on basis of a police report - After a full enquiry as provided by s. 145 Magistrate passed final order declaring appellant to be in possession of land in dispute - Petition filed u/s. 482 CrPC for quashing order of Magistrate - High Court allowed petition - Present appeal - Whether HC erred in holding that final order of Magistrate was vitiated in absence of a finding that breach of peace existed at time order was passed? - Once a preliminary order drawn up by Magistrate sets out reasons for holding that a breach of peace exists, it is not necessary that breach of peace should continue at every stage of proceeding unless there is clear evidence to show that dispute has ceased to exist so as to bring case within ambit of s. 145(5) - Held, mere absence of finding by Magistrate in final order in circumstances as mentioned in present case cannot be such a manifest defect so as to attract extraordinary jurisdiction of HC u/s. 482 of CrPC - It was therefore not correct on part of HC to have interfered with order of Magistrate on a purely technical ground when aggrieved party had a clear remedy in civil court - Order passed by HC is legally erroneous and cannot be allowed to stand - Appeal allowed.
