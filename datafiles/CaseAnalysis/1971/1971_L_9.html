Lallubhai Devchand Shah and Others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 569; (1971) 3 SCC 767; (1972) SCC (Cr) 13; AIR 1972 SC 1776; 1972 CRLJ 828; 1972 UJ 177

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Murder - Dying declaration - Evidentiary value of - Held, person who records a dying declaration must be satisfied that dying man was making a conscious and voluntary statement with normal understanding - Responsibility of Court is greater in holding that it was so made when in fact it is found that man dies a few minutes afterwards - There can be hardly any doubt that the deceased was not only conscious and able to speak but also that she was consciously and voluntarily making statement - All points raised before the Supreme Court on facts had been raised before HC and they have been satisfactorily discussed and dealt with - There was any misapprehension or misreading of evidence on part of Trial Court or HC resulting in failure of justice - Appeal dismissed.
