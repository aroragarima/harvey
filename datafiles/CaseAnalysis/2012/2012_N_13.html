Rajesh Awasthi v Nand Lal Jaiswal And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 626; (2013) 1 SCC 501; 2013 (1) AWC 265; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 883; 2012 (6) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Kerala Agriculturists Debt Relief Act, 1970, U.P. Electricity Regulatory Commission (Appointment and Conditions of Service of the Chairperson and Members) Rules, 1999

Summary: K. S. Radhakrishnan, J.

(A) Service - Electricity Act, 2003, s. 85(5) - Appointment - Fulfillment of requirement - Exercise of powers - Legality - Post of Chairperson of Commission fell vacant and Govt. constituted selection committee vide notification consisting of three members for finalizing selection of chairperson - Govt. appointed appellant as chairman of commission and appellant on that date sent letter to State Govt. stating that he had resigned from his previous assignments and severed all his links with private sector as required u/s. 85 of the Act - Respondent no. 1 challenged appointment of appellant - HC allowed petition and quashed appointment of appellant holding that selection committee had failed to follow provisions of s. 85 (5) of the Act, hence appointment was vitiated and appellant had no authority to hold post of chairperson - Further, held that selection committee had no power to delegate powers conferred on it u/s. 85(5) of the Act to State Govt. - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appointment of appellant for post of chairperson was in accordance with statutory provisions of law - Held, powers conferred u/s. 85(5) of the Act had to be exercised by selection committee and committee alone and not by Govt. - Suitability of candidate for appointment did not fall within realm of writ of quo warranto and there could not be any quarrel with that legal proposition - Though selection committee had not discharged its functions u/s. 85(5) of the Act, it was only omission which could be cured by giving direction to selection committee to comply with requirement - Non-compliance of mandatory requirements resulted in nullification of process of selection unless it was shown that performance of that requirement was impossible or it could be statutorily waived - Expression 'before recommending any person' clearly indicated that it was mandatory requirement to be followed by selection committee before recommending name of any person for post of Chairperson - Expression 'before' clearly indicated intention of Legislature - Therefore, view of HC was accepted that appointment of appellant was in clear violation of u/s. 85(5) of the Act and, thus, he had no authority to hold post of chairperson of commission and HC had rightly held so - Appeal dismissed.

Dipak Misra, J.

(B) Service - Appointment - Non-compliance of statutory provision - Justifiability - Whether order of HC that appellant had no authority to hold post of chairperson was justified - Held, responsibility of selection was conferred on high powered selection committee - Selection committee was legally obliged to record that it had satisfied that candidate did not have any financial or other interest which was likely to affect prejudicially his functions as chairman or member - Said satisfaction had to be reached before recommending any person for appointment - Abdication of said power tantamounted to breach of Rule of Law because it not only gave go by to warrant of law but also created dent in basic index of law - Therefore, selection was vitiated and it could never come within realm of curability, for there was statutory non-compliance from very inception of selection - Thus, there was total non-compliance of statutory provision by selection committee which made decision making process vulnerable warranting interference by constitutional courts and so HC was justified in holding that appointment was nonest in law - Appeal dismissed.
