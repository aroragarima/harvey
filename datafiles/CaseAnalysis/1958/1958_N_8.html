Sm. Saila Bala Dassi v Sm. Nirmala Sundari Dassi and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 20; AIR 1958 SC 394; 1958 (2) MLJ 99; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 99; 1958 MPLJ 473; 1958 SCJ 743; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1287

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Govts - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 146, O. 22 r. 10 and 151 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - (A) Whether O. 22, r. 10 of CPC will sustain application to Appellate Court when transfer in favour of appellant made prior to filing of appeal?- Held, even if a suit is pending when transfer in favour of appellant was made, that would not affect result when no application had been made by her to be brought on record in original court during pendency of suit - Application made to Appellate Court cannot be sustained under O. 22 r. 10, when transfer in favour of appellant was made prior to filing of that appeal and not during its pendency - (B) What is scope and object of s. 146 of CPC? - Further held, s. 146 was introduced for first time in CPC, 1908 with object of facilitating exercise of rights by persons in whom they come to be vested by devolution or assignment, being a beneficent provision should be construed liberally, so as to advance justice, not in a restricted or technical sense - Right to file an appeal must be held to carry with it right to continue an appeal which had been filed by person under whom applicant claims and petition of appellant to be brought on record as an appellant in an appeal must be held to be maintainable under s. 146 -(C) Whether purchaser pendente lite u/s. 53 of Transfer of Property Act is given an opportunity to protect her rights?- Further held, as a purchaser pendente lite, a person will be bound by proceedings taken by party in whose favour decree is passed in execution of her decree, and justice requires that she should be given an opportunity to protect her right u/s. 53 of Transfer of Property Act - (D) Whether Court has jurisdiction to transpose appellant as respondent in appeal? - Further held, Court can, if necessary, take action suo motu either under O. 1 r. 10 of CPC or in its inherent jurisdiction and transpose an appellant as respondent in appeal -(E) Whether execution proceeding can be taken without certified copy of decree u/r. 27 chap. 16 of Calcutta High Court Original Side Rules, 1914? - Further held, importance of r. 27 is that until a decree is drawn up no certified copy thereof would be issued to party and without such a certified copy no execution proceedings could be taken - Appeal allowed.
