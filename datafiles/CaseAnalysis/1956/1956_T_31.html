Burn and Company, Calcutta v Their Employees

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, P. Govinda Menon, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 66; AIR 1957 SC 38; 1957 (1) LLJ 226; 1957 SCJ 28; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cost Of Living, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, s. 19(3) - Jurisdiction - Bonafide requirement - Maintainability - Disputes had arisen between Company, and section of their employees Union - State Govt. issued notification referring same to Tribunal - Tribunal, held that there were no grounds for revising scale of pay of clerical and sub-staff that pay of checkers should be increased and that they should be paid according to scale as set out in his award that profits of Company did not warrant grant of any bonus in addition to what had been paid by Company and that of four employees should be re- employed - Union preferred appeal to Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal modified award of in favour of Union - Against Appellate Tribunal decision Company preferred Appeal by special leave - Union had preferred Appeal the leave being limited in latter to four points raised by Company in its appeal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by Appellate Tribunal was maintainable - Held, ground of discharge was continued absence of employee, and his inability to do work, and it was difficult to see what purpose would be served by formal charge being delivered to him and what conceivable answer he could give - Order of Appellate Tribunal was manifestly erroneous and was set aside - Minimum pay of clerical staff would be Rs. 65 per mensem with modifications as to ceiling level and increments as set out SNCwould be reinstated with compensation as provided in order of Appellate Tribunal - Union would pay half costs of appellant - Appeals disposed of.
