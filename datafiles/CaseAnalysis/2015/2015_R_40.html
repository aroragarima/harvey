Om Prakash Sharma @ O. P. Joshi v Rajendra Prasad Shewda and others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 704; 2015 (4) CalLT 36; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 26; JT 2015 (12) SC 104; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 758; 2015(10) SCALE 389; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Benami Transactions (Prohibition) Act, 1988, Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32(5), 32(6) - Title of property - Legality of adoption - Determination - Original plaintiff, (since deceased), contended that suit property was purchased by her father-in-law with his funds in name of his wife - Further, original plaintiff's husband was adopted sons of owner of property - Original plaintiff stated that after death of her father-in-law, one half of suit property devolved on his wife and remaining half on deceased plaintiff as widow of predeceased son - Further, on death of her mother-in-law, her half share in suit property devolved on her daughter and on death of her daughter i.e. plaintiff's sister-in-law, her half share in property devolved on original deceased plaintiff - Hence, plaintiff became absolute owner of entire suit property and it is stated that respondent no.1 who claimed to be adopted son of plaintiff's sister- in-law had no basis to make any claim as no such adoption took place - Trial Court held that on death of plaintiff's sister-in-law, her half share in property devolved on her adopted son respondent no.1. Hence, plaintiff and respondent no.1 were held to be entitled to equal shares in suit property - However, HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and held that adoption of plaintiff's husband is not proved, hence, entire suit property had devolved on her daughter - Whether impugned order passed by HC against plaintiff is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, HC is justified in coming to conclusion that property though purchased from funds of deceased father-in-law was really for benefit of his widow; hence, his wife was real owner of property. Further, with respect to legality of adoption of husband of plaintiff, SC held that evidence of PW 2 cannot be considered in view of certain inherent inconsistencies therein testimony of said witness is not worthy of acceptance. PW 2 though had stated that adoption of plaintiff's husband took place 40 years back she could not recollect her own age; she had no recollection of number of years prior to adoption when she got married and was unable to recall when her sons got married and most surprisingly age of her elder son at time of his marriage; present age of elder son or even present calendar year. Further, evidence of three witnesses examined in other suit is be inadmissible u/ss. 32(5), 32(6) of Act inasmuch as on date when said evidence was recorded controversy with regard to adoption of plaintiff's husband had already occurred. Further, letter dt. 20-7-1945 does not lead to any clear conclusion with regard to adoption of plaintiff's husband and had been rightly discarded by HC. Hence, SC held that it cannot be concluded that plaintiff's husband was adoptive son of owner of property in question. Further, SC held that claim of respondent no.1 to be adopted son of plaintiff's sister-in-law could have been challenged only by such legal heirs on whom property would have devolved following her death in event adoption of respondent no. 1 is to be held to be invalid. However, no such challenge was made by legal heir who had better/preferential claim. Hence, SC held that HC was fully justified in not entering into issue of validity of adoption of respondent no.1 or gift deed executed in his favor as said issues had become redundant. Hence, impugned judgment passed by HC against plaintiff is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Title of property cannot be declared in favor of person who is not legal heir of deceased and has no legal right to claim property in his name.
