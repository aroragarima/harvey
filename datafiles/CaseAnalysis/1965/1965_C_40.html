Â Keshav Mills Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1965 SC 1636; 1998 (1) Crimes 143; 1965 (1) ITJ 853; [1965] 56 ITR 365; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 908

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques, Debt Laws

Summary: Constitution - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 31(2), 66(1), 66(4) and 66(2) - Income Tax (Appellate Tribunal) Rules, 1963, rr. 29 and 39 - Whether Supreme Court can review and revise its own decisions? - Held, Supreme Court has inherent jurisdiction to reconsider and revise its earlier decision - It would impose certain reasonable limitations - Reluctant to entertain pleas for reconsideration unless in interest of public good or for any other valid and compulsive reasons - Depends upon nature of infirmity or error - (B) Whether High Court is competent to direct tribunal to call for additional evidence? - Further held, power of HC is limited to requiring tribunal to add to or alter statement of a case, material and evidence already on record - When tribunal has disposed of matter and is preparing a statement of case either u/s. 66(1) or s. 66(2), there is no scope for any further or additional evidence - Appeal allowed and matter remanded.
