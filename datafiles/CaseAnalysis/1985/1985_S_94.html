Narayan Singh And Ors. v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 223; (1985) 4 SCC 26; (1985) SCC (Cr) 460; AIR 1985 SC 1678; 1985 (2) Crimes 604; 1985 CRLJ 1862; 1985 CrLR(SC) 404; 1985(2) SCALE 76; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 319

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 2, 24 - Supreme Court (Enlargement Of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Appeals against conviction u/ss. 148 and 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC, 1860 - (A) Appreciation of evidence of sole eye-witness - Witness not immediately disclosed the names of the accused to the inmates of the family of the deceased when he went to the house - Held, not a fatal defect in the prosecution case, as witnesses to murder almost lose their sense of balance and remain dumbfounded until they are able to compose themselves - (B) What is the evidentiary value of extra-judicial confession - Held, it vary as per circumstances etc. of each case - (C) What is the scope for interference in appeal against acquittal - Judgment of the Sessions Judge was absolutely perverse, legally erroneous and based on wrong assumptions - Supreme Court held that where two views are reasonably possible, the order of acquittal should not be disturbed, however it is a fit and proper case for interference by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
