Jammu Tehsil v Hakumar Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1831; (2006) 12 SCC 193

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Civil Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 17A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 9 r. 13 - Services of the respondent no. 1 were terminated by order dt. 11.7.1989 - Said order was challenged before Registrar who dismissed said appeal - Respondent no. 1 preferred a revision before tribunal who also dismissed said revision - Subsequently, a dispute was raised under the Act in the year 1991 and an ex parte award was passed against appellant - Appellant filed an application praying for setting aside of said ex parte award on 31.3.1994 submitting that it came to know of the award when it received a letter from the Registrar and immediately thereafter he filed an application to set aside the ex parte award - The Labour Court issued notice on the application and stayed execution of the said award - On appeal, HC held that the provisions of o. 9 r. 13 of CPC would be applicable only when the application to set aside the ex parte award made under the 1947 Act preferred within 30 days from the date of publication of award - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, an authority for the proposition that while an Industrial Court will have jurisdiction to set aside an ex parte award, but having regard to the provision contained in s. 17-A of the Act, an application therefore must be filed before the expiry of 30 days from the publication thereof - Till then the Tribunal retains jurisdiction over the dispute referred to it for adjudication, and only up to that date, it has the power to entertain an application in connection with such dispute - In the instant case, HC found that the application for setting aside the award was filed before the Labour Court after 1 month of the publication of the award - Hence, impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
