Commissioner of Income Tax, Jalpaiguri v Om Prakash Mittal

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 143; (2005) 2 SCC 751; 2005 (194) CTR 97; 2005 (184) E.L.T. 3; [2005] 273 ITR 326; JT 2005 (2) SC 538; 2005 (3) MLJ(SC) 48; 2005(2) SCALE 309; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 263; [2005] 143 TAXMAN 373

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 245D(4), 245D(6), 245C - Settlement order - Revision of application for settlement - Whether Commission is bound to proceed with any application filed under s.245 C? - Held, if revenue has material to show that order was obtained by fraud or misrepresentation of facts it certainly can move Commission for decision on that issue - Voluntary disclosure and making a full and true disclosure of income are necessary pre-conditions for invoking Commission's jurisdiction - No provision for revision of an application made in terms of Section 245C - Applicant for settlement has to make a true and fair declaration from the threshold - Commission to re-hear matter - Appeal disposed of.
