Jaffar Hussain Dastagir v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, S.M. Sikri, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 489; (1969) 2 SCC 872; AIR 1970 SC 1934; 1970 CRLJ 1659; 1970 MLJ 13; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 379 - Evidence Act, ss. 25, 26 and 27 - Charge u/ss. 379 and 34 - Appreciation of Evidence - Scope of s. 27 - In order that s. 27 may apply prosecution must establish that information given by appellant led to discovery of some fact deposed to by him - Whether statement in question was really a discovery of a fact deposed to or whether there was no discovery within meaning of s. 27 of Evidence Act because police was already in possession of fact that accused no. 3 was a person who had diamonds? - Held, there was no discovery of any fact deposed to by appellant within meaning of s. 27 - Although statement might otherwise have been admissible in evidence, there was no discovery of a fact connecting appellant with receipt of diamonds which were stolen, within meaning of s. 27 of Evidence Act because police already knew that third and/or fourth accused had diamonds - Appeal allowed.
