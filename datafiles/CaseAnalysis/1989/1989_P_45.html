Prabha Manufacturing Industrialco-Operative Society v Banwari Lal

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 462; (1989) 2 SCC 69; AIR 1989 SC 1101; JT 1989 (1) SC 397; 1989 (1) RCJ 303; 1989 (1) RCR(Rent) 421; 1989(1) SCALE 383; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 647; 1989 (2) UJ 15

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Appurtenant Land

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 44 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 5 and 6 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111 - Constitution - Premises - Permission for repair Premises - Rent controller's finding that only plot of land was originally allotted to society become final - (A) Subsequent suit to recover possession of property from tenant society - Plea by society that it was not tenant of only open plot - Maintainability - (B) Review of entire evidence by Supreme Court, if - Held, barred by res judicata - Further held, property allotted to society in respect of which it was a tenant, was only a plot of land and that plaintiffs were justified in attempting to recover possession thereof by a suit for possession in Civil Court - Appeal dismissed.
