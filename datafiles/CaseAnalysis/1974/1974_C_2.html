C. Krishna Prasad v C. I. T. Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 180; (1975) 1 SCC 160; AIR 1975 SC 498; 1975 (1) ALR 17; 1975 CTR 7; [1974] 97 ITR 493; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 709; 1975 UJ 59

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Coparcenary, Hindu Law

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 ss. 256(1), 2(31) - Indian Income-tax Act, 1922 s. 25(A) - Assessment - HUF - Assessee/appellant belonged to HUF where partition was made between his father and brother - Assessee got some house properties in said partition and was recognized by department and order u/s. 25(A) of 1922 Act was passed - Till A/Y 1963 -64 assessee was unmarried and assessed as individual and for A/Y 1964-65 he claimed to be assessed as HUF - ITO had rejected his claim and on appeal said order of ITO was affirmed by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and also by Tribunal - Reference was made before HC which answered in favour of Revenue against assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether unmarried male Hindu on partition can be assessed as HUF even though no other person besides him is member of alleged family.

Held, s. 4 of the Act provides for the charging of income-tax on the total income of every person subject to the conditions prescribed in that s. and 'Person' has been defined u/s. 2(31) of the Act and includes, inter alia, an individual and HUF. Therefore, inherent fallacy of the case set up on behalf of the assessee-appellant, is that according to him a single individual can constitute a HUF and be assessed as such. Thus, 'Family' connotes a group of people related by blood or marriage and it is well settled that a HUF consists of all persons lineally descended from a common ancestor and includes their wives and unmarried daughters. Hence, a Hindu coparcenary is a much narrower body than the joint family; it includes only those persons who acquire by birth an interest in the joint or coparcenary property, these being the sons, grandsons, and great-grandsons of the holder of the joint property for the time being. Appeal dismissed
