K.P.Sudhakaran and Another v State of Kerala and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 213; (2006) 5 SCC 386; AIR 2006 SC 2138; JT 2006 (11) SC 101; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 329; 2006(6) SCALE 92; 2006 (4) SCJ 723; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Kerala Public Services Act, 1968, s. 3 - Kerala State and Subordinate Service Rules, 1958, rr. 27, 27(a) - Seniority - Whether a Govt. servant who is transferred from one district to another on his own request, can claim seniority from his initial date of recruitment in post or from date on which he is transferred on his own request to new district? - Whether DB was justified in holding that GO dated 2/1/1961 and proviso to r 27(a) of Rules should only be applied prospectively in case of transferred LDCs? - Held, where a Govt. servant is so transferred on his own request, transferred employee will have to forego his seniority till date of transfer, and will be placed at bottom below junior-most employee in category in new cadre or department - HC has no power to direct that a Rule which has been in force for several years, will be operated only prospectively - Revised seniority lists under which seniority of transferred LDCs (inter-district transferees) is counted only from date of their joining new district, excluding previous service, are proper and do not call for interference - Appeals allowed.
