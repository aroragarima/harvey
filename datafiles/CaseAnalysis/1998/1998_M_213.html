Nagulapati Lakshmamma v Mupparaju Subbaiah

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2168; (1998) 5 SCC 285; AIR 1998 SC 2904; 1998 (33) ALR 287; 1998 (4) AWC 854; 1998 (2) CLT 82; 1998 (146) CTR 506; JT 1998 (3) SC 253; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 129; 1998(2) SCALE 705; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 723; 1998 (4) SLT 320; 1998 (Supp) RD 502; 1998 (3) Supreme 614

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: (1) Indian Succession Act, 1925-Section 63-Will-Attestation-Absolutely necessary that attesting witness should either sign or affix his thumb mark himself-Section 63 does not permit attesting witness to delegate function to another-If witness not himself signing or affixing his thumb impression-He is not attesting witness-Section 63 making vital distinction between testator and attestor for obvious reason.

(2) Evidence Act, 1872-Section 68-General Clauses Act, 1897- Section 3 (56)-Word 'sign'-Meaning.
