Cheema Engineering Services v Rajan Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1574; (1997) 1 SCC 131; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 567; 1997 (3) Bom.C.R. 36; [1997] 88 Comp Cas 400; 1997 (5) CTJ 1; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 90; 1996(8) SCALE 643; 1998 (6) SLT 20; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 340; 1997 (1) Supreme 51

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(d) - Scope of s. 2(1)(d) - If any goods are purchased for consideration including any user of such goods other than person who has paid consideration and when such use is made with approval of such person, purchaser is 'consumer' within meaning of Act - Self- employment is an exception to Explanation to s. 2(1)(d) and implies that purchaser alone uses machinery for purpose of manufacture by employing himself in working out or producing goods for earning his livelihood - Held, burden of proof of whether respondent is using machine exclusively by himself and family members for preparation, manufacture and sale of bricks or whether he employed any workmen and if so, how many, are matters of evidence and lies with respondent - Appeal allowed.
