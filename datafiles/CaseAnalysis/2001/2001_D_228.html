Central Council For Research In Ayurveda and Siddha and Another v Dr. K. Santhakumari

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20135; (2001) 5 SCC 60; (2001) SCC (L&S) 772; AIR 2001 SC 2306; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 411; 2001 (2) KLT 775; 2001 (34) LabIC 2073; 2001 (3) LLN 39; 2001(4) SCALE 9; 2001 (3) SCJ 260; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 519; 2001 (4) SLR 599; 2001 (4) SLR 651; 2001 (4) SLT 341; 2001 (4) Supreme 97

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research

Summary: Service - Seniority - Validity of - Respondent, an Asst. Research Officer (Ayurveda) - Departmental promotion committee prepared a panel of eligible candidates for being promoted as research officers - Contention of respondent is that promotion had to be effected on principle of seniority cum fitness and therefore, placing of respondent at sl. no. 15 for being promoted as research officer was illegal - Petition before High Court in this regard allowed but was dismissed in appeal by division bench - Sustainability of judgment of division bench of HC? - Even if appellants had mistakenly contended in HC that principle of seniority-fitness was to be followed for promotion to post of research officer, departmental rules clearly show that promotion was in respect of a 'selection post' and promotion was to be made on basis of inter se merit of eligible candidates - In that view of matter, respondent was not entitled to get promotion to post of research officer on strength of her seniority alone - Seniority list prepared by departmental promotion committee was not challenged by respondent on other grounds and so court do not find any ground to assail that select list - Thus, writ petition is liable to be dismissed by setting aside orders made therein and in writ appeal arising there from - Appeal allowed.
