R. C. Sood v High Court of Judicature At Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.S. Anand, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 983; (1998) 5 SCC 493; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1389; AIR 1999 SC 707; AIR 1999 SCW 168; 1998 (2) CLR 20; JT 1998 (4) SC 4; 1999 LabIC 453; 1999 (1) LLJ 912; 1999 (1) LLN 304; 1998(3) SCALE 468; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 352; 1998 (3) SLJ 292; 1998 (4) SLR 365; 1998 (5) SLT 145; 1998 (4) Supreme 506

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, Art. 32, 226 - Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules - Preliminary Enquiry - Complaint - Resolutions - Departmental Enquiry - Petitioner joined as District and Sessions Judge and was posted as Registrar - To fill up vacancies in Higher Judicial Service by way of direct recruitment, advertisement was published with age limit of candidates - Committee of two Judges observed that petitioner was responsible for forgery committed on record - Departmental enquiry was initiated by a resolution of Full Court, which was challenged by petitioner before this Court, which quashed said disciplinary proceedings and resolution - It recommended that a regular enquiry be made in accordance with rules and that petitioner should be placed under suspension - Hence instant petition - Whether for good and sufficient reasons Full Court could over-rule or recall earlier decision of Chief Justice and Whether allegations against petitioner were any way warranted holding of disciplinary proceeding - Held, preliminary report was not brought to notice of Full Court, which Chief Justice was not bound do could not be reason for recalling order - Chief Justice by his order decided that no complaint against a judicial officer should be entertained, unless supported by an affidavit - Court hold that there was a complete lack of bona fides on part of HC when it decided to institute disciplinary proceedings against petitioner and petitioner was entitled to succeed, on this ground alone - Observations of HC was arbitrary and unwarranted and entire disciplinary proceedings initiated by against petitioner together with Full Court's resolutions were quashed - Petition allowed.
