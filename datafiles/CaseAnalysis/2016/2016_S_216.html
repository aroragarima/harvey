Krishan Chander v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 13; (2016) 3 SCC 108; AIR 2016 SC 298; 2016 CRLJ 1079; 2016 (226) DLT 292; 2016 (1) JCC 495; JT 2016 (1) SC 263; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 837; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 806; 2016 (2) RLW 1173; 2016(1) SCALE 143

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bond, Credit, Conversation, Hostile Witness, Contradiction, Police Statement, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Challenged - Complainant alleging that appellant sought bribe for release of his brother on bail, made written complaint to ACB - Later, appellant was caught accepting money and was arrested - Upon trial, appellant was convicted and sentenced by Trial Court for offences punishable u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d) r/w s.13(2) of the Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal but same was dismissed upholding decision of Trial Court and hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment of HC is sustainable.

Held, approach of Trial Court and HC is erroneous as both courts relied upon evidence of prosecution on the aspect of demand of illegal gratification from the complainant by appellant though there is no substantive evidence and appellant was erroneously convicted for the charges framed against him. Further, prosecution failed to prove factum of demand of bribe money made by appellant from complainant and hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is not only erroneous but also suffers from error in law and so, same is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Demand and acceptance of bribe is sine qua non for constituting offence u/ss.7 and 13(1)(d) read with s. 13(2)of the Act.
