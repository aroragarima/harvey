Special Deputy Collector and Another Etc v Kurra Sambasiva Rao and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2969; (1997) 6 SCC 41; AIR 1997 SC 2625; 1997 (3) CLT 38; JT 1997 (5) SC 580; 1997(4) SCALE 326; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1107; 1997 (6) Supreme 150

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 11, 18, 23(1-A) - Notification - Land acquisition - Compensation - Enhancement - Award - Validity - Govt issued notification, on 9-12-1980, u/s. 4(1) of the Act for acquiring land for development of colony for weaker sections - Land acquisition officer awarded compensation of Rs. 22,940 per acre for levelled up land and Rs. 21,700 per acre for unlevelled land - On reference, Trial Court, Tenali by its decree dt. 26-2-1991 awarded uniform compensation at the rate of Rs. 1,00,000 - On appeal by appellants as well as respondents/claimants, HC by its judgment dt. 23-3-1994 enhanced compensation to Rs. 23.50 per sq.yd allowing appeals of respondent/claimants - Hence, instant appeals - Whether decree of HC enhancing compensation could be sustained - Held, on the basis of evidence adduced before land acquisition officer and contents of award and material evidence, it would be difficult to accept contention of appellants that lands possessed potential value for being used for building purpose as, except small fraction of land, lands were agricultural lands - HC therefore, was clearly in error in treating lands as fit for building purpose and on that basis determining compensation - As for the question of market value said lands were capable to fetch, burden of proof would always be on claimants to establish that lands were possessed of advantageous features and were, therefore, capable of fetching higher market value than what was determined by land acquisition Officer in his award u/s. 11 of the Act - In view of the fact that HC itself had rejected all sale deeds except Ex. A-12 which this court was constrained to reject, and as no other evidence was available, these appeals should not be allowed - In view of material collected by land acquisition officer himself as referred to in award, and after taking into consideration all relevant factors, reasonable compensation should be Rs. 50.000/- per acre and the compensation was accordingly awarded - Claimants would be entitled to additional amount at 12 per cent per annum u/s. 23 (1-A) from date of notification till that date of taking possession - Claimants would be entitled to compensation at rate of Rs.50,000/- per acre in respect of all acquired lands with solatium at 30% on enhanced compensation and interest @ 9% for one year and on expiry thereof @ 15 per cent till date of deposit into court and additional amount - Appeals disposed of.
