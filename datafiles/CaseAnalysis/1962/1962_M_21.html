Management of Ritz Theatre Private Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1963 SC 295; 1962 (5) FLR 216; 1962 (2) LLJ 498; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11- (A) What is jurisdiction and powers of Industrial Tribunal in a dispute arising from order of dismissal passed by employer? - Held, an employer serves relevant charge or charges - Holds a proper and fair enquiry - It is open to him to act upon report submitted to him by Enquiry Officer and to dismiss employee concerned - If enquiry properly held, order of dismissal passed can be challenged, if it is shown that conclusions reached at departmental enquiry were perverse or impugned dismissal is vindictive or malafide, and amounts to an unfair labour practice - In such an enquiry before tribunal, it is not open to tribunal to sit in appeal, over findings recorded at domestic enquiry - Where in departmental enquiry proper charge had not been served or proper or full opportunity had not been given to employee to meet charge, or other grave irregularities vitiating it, then position would be that tribunal would deal with merits of dispute - Same result follows if no enquiry has been held at all - Though open to employer to adduce additional evidence - Must necessarily follow that he has given up his stand based on previous departmental enquiry and tribunal is entitled to examine dispute on merits for itself and on principle of fair play and justice, said proposition is unsound - Tribunal is in error if he proceeds to examine evidence for itself in coming to conclusion that dismissal of an employee was not justified on merits - Appeal partly allowed.
