Howrah Municipal Corporation and Others v Ganges Rope Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1274; (2004) 1 SCC 663; JT 2003 (10) SC 355; 2003(10) SCALE 1067; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 1212

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Howrah Municipal Corporation Act, 1980, Howrah Municipal Corporation Building Rules, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Howrah Municipal Corporation Building Rules, 1991 - Whether by order of court in which a period was fixed for Corporation to take a decision on application for sanction for construction of additional floors, any vested right is created in favour of a company to seek sanction for construction of additional three floors irrespective of subsequent amendment to Building Rules and resolution of Corporation putting restrictions on height of high-rising buildings on G.T. Road? - Held, in none of the orders of High Court, there is a mandate issued to Corporation to grant a sanction - What was directed by HC in first order was merely a 'liberty' or option to the company to seek sanction for additional three floors - In subsequent order, an 'expectation' was expressed for decision of pending applications within a period of four weeks - There was, thus, in favour of company an order of HC directing Corporation to decide its pending applications for sanction within allotted period but non-compliance therewith by Corporation cannot result in creation of any vested right in favour of company to obtain sanction on basis of Building Rules as they stood on date of making application for sanction and regardless of amendment introduced to Building Rules - Neither provisions of Act nor general law creates any vested right, as claimed by applicant Company for grant of sanction or for consideration of its application for grant of sanction on then existing Building Rules as were applicable on date of application - Order accordingly.
