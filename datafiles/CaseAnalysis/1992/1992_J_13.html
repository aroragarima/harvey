Jagdish Prasad and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 141; (1995) SCC (Cr) 160; AIR 1994 SC 1251; 1994 (2) BLJ 345; 1994 CRLJ 1106

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 34 - Evidence Act 1872, s. 3 - Murder - Single inimical eye-witness whose credibility is in question as he had enmity with one accused - Eye-witness charged one of accused on prior occasion which is a proximate motive of assault on deceased-witness accompanying deceased, fleeing, hiding and witnessing entire occurrence of assault could not be accepted - Witness helped acquittal of one of accused by non-mentioning of his name in his evidence - Held, testimony of witness clouded with grave suspicion and disparity in material particulars - Conviction on his testimony would be unsafe.
