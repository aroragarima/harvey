Disha v State Of Gujarat & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 453; (2011) 13 SCC 337; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2669; 2011 CRLJ 4615; 2011 (3) G.L.R. 2744; 2011 (3) JCC 2178; JT 2011 (7) SC 548; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 694; 2012 (2) RLW 1037; 2011(7) SCALE 736; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 359

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Criminal investigation - Transfer of investigating agency - Petitioner engaged in commercial/business activities along with her husband - Petitioner's husband and his maternal uncle appointed a large number of agents/franchises for their firms all over the State and the said agents allegedly collected huge amount from large number of persons/investors giving them assurances of high return - Petitioner's husband committed suicide by jumping from the 22nd floor of a Hotel and died spontaneously - Investors lodged complaints against partners/agents/franchises of the firm - Police registered a case and filed charge-sheets against 13 persons including petitioner - Petitioner filed the instant petition seeking transfer of investigation of the case to CBI - Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case, where in case of cheating, a charge sheet had been filed, the matter was required to be transferred for investigation/further investigation to the CBI - Held, petitioner herself was an accused in the instant case - A huge amount had been collected from innocent persons giving them false assurances that their amount would have a high premium - It had not been alleged in the petition that any of the investor was very powerful or capable to manage the investigation against the petitioner or that the case of suicide of her husband was not properly investigated - It was no body's case that the police had unnecessarily harassed the petitioner; rather, the record of the case revealed that it was only after completing the investigation, that the charge sheet had been filed against 13 persons including the petitioner - No allegation of mala fide or bias had been alleged against any investigating authority nor had it been pleaded that charge sheet had been filed against the petitioner without investigating the case or having any vindictive attitude towards the petitioner - In fact, the instant petition was based purely on mere apprehension by the petitioner - None of the grounds taken by the petitioner for transfer was tenable - No cogent reason to interfere in the matter - Petition dismissed.
