Nizam and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 601; (2016) 1 SCC 550; AIR 2015 SC 3430; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4087; 2015 CRLJ 4655; JT 2015 (8) SC 87; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 79; 2015 (4) RLW 3598; 2015(9) SCALE 513; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 786; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 19

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302 - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - Deceased victim was helper on truck who went along with PW 1 and 2 driver to destination - Further, accused appellant were driver and cleaner of other truck and reached to same destination - Further, PW 2 got into quarrel with some local persons as result of which he was detained by police along with his truck - Amount was handed to t deceased by PW2 who left with accused persons - Further, death body was found of deceased - Accused persons were arrested and charge sheet u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC was filed - Trial Court based on 'last seen theory' convicted appellant accused for murder of deceased and found accused guilty u/s. 201 and 302 of IPC - Order passed by Trial Court is confirmed by HC - Whether conviction of accused by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, body of deceased was recovered after three days. SC gap between time when deceased is alleged to have left in truck of accused and recovery of body is not so small, to draw inference against appellants. SC held that in view of time gap between deceased left in truck and recovery of body and also place and circumstances in which body was recovered, possibility of others intervening cannot be ruled out. In absence of definite evidence that appellants and deceased were last seen together and when time gap is long, it would be dangerous to come to conclusion that appellants are responsible for murder of deceased and are guilty of committing murder of deceased. Where time gap is long it would be unsafe to base conviction on 'last seen theory'; it is safer to look for corroboration from other circumstances and evidence adduced by prosecution. From facts and evidence, SC found no other corroborative piece of evidence corroborating last seen theory. Further, neither weapon of murder nor money allegedly looted by appellants or any other material was recovered from possession of appellants. Hence, SC found many loopholes in case of prosecution. Further, SC held that none of circumstances relied upon by prosecution and accepted by courts below can be said to be pointing only to guilt of appellants and no other inference. Hence, conviction order passed by lower Courts is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - For establishing guilt on basis of circumstantial evidence, circumstances must be firmly established and chain of circumstances must be completed from facts.
