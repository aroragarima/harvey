Air India Statutory Corporation and Others v United Labour Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Controlled, International Airports Authority Act, 1971, Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970 - Contract, Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Appellants were engaged as contract labour, of the respondent union's members, for sweeping, cleaning, dusting and watching of the building owned and occupied by the appellant - Notification was issued, dt. 9-12-1976, prohibiting the employment of contract labour - Notification was not enforced, dt. 9-12-1976, so W.P.s were filed to enforce forthwith the notification - HC directed the appellant to enforce the notification - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the contract labour system should be abolished - Held, that on abolition of the contract labour system, by necessary implication, the principal employer was put under statutory obligation to absorb the contract labour, that to do complete justice, direction should be given to absorb the workmen with effect from the date abolition, i.e. 9-12-1976 u/art.142 of the Constitution - Linkage between the contractor and the employee stood snapped and direct relationship stood restored between principal employer and the contract labour as its employees - All the workmen in the respective services working on contract labour were required to be absorbed in the establishment of the appellant - Workmen immediately were kept out of job to endlessly keep waiting for award and thereafter resulting in further litigation and delay in enforcement - Management would always keep them at bay for absorption, it would be difficult for them to work out their right - Appeals dismissed.
