Suresh Alias Pappu Bhudh-Armlal Kalani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20688; (2001) SCC (Cr) 621; (2001) 3 SCC 703; AIR 2001 SC 1375; 2001 (1) CCR 336; 2001 (2) Crimes 125; 2001 CRLJ 1697; 2001 (1) JCC 258; JT 2001 (3) SC 386; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 278; 2001(2) SCALE 421; 2001 (2) SLT 584; 2001 (2) Supreme 289

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 5, 18 and 19 - Indan Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 120 - B, 302 and 307 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227 and 228 - Application to transfer suit from special court to session court in absence of any ground under TADA, 1987 - Other offences alleged to have been committed are not triable by special court - Whether charge should be framed against accused and he should be called to face trial or he is to be discharged from case should be decided at stage of inquiry and evidence collection? - Held, at stage of framing of charge court has to consider material with a view to find out if there is ground for presuming that accused has committed offence or that there is not sufficient ground for proceeding against him - Court is not required to appreciate evidence to conclude whether materials produced are sufficient or not for convicting accused - Court has to prima facie consider whether there is sufficient ground for proceeding against accused or not - S. 18 of TADA (P) Act, 1987 provides that where, after taking cognizance of any offence, a designated court is of opinion that offence is not triable by it, it shall, notwithstanding that it has no jurisdiction to try such offence, transfer case for trial of such offence to any court having jurisdiction under Code - In this case learned special judge has recorded finding that a prima facie case u/s. 5 of TADA (P) Act, 1987 has been made out - Therefore petition filed by accused u/s. 18 of TADA (P) Act, 1987 was rightly rejected - Appeals dismissed.
