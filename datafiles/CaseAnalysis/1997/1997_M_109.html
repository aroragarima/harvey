Omwati Smt. and Others v Mahendra Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1403; (1998) 9 SCC 81; (1998) SCC (Cr) 984; AIR 1997 SC 4220; 1998 ALJ 82; 1998 (1) CCR 130; 1997 (4) Crimes 270; 1998 CRLJ 401; JT 1997 (9) SC 47; 1997(6) SCALE 681; 1998 (2) SLT 110; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 88; 1997 (9) Supreme 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 148, 149, 302 and 307 - (A) Whether proof of motive is necessary to sustain a conviction? - Held, no - Proof of motive is not necessary to sustain a conviction but when prosecution puts forward a specific case as to motive for crime, evidence regarding same has got to be considered in order to judge probabilities - Motive for a crime is a satisfactory circumstance of corroboration when there is convincing evidence to prove guilt of an accused person but it cannot fill up a lacuna in evidence - (B) Whether non examination of important and material witnesses effects credebility of evidence? - Further held, yes - When presence of two witnesses in village itself is questioned, prosecution ought to have examined witnesses - Further held, prosecution has not been proved beyond doubt - Accused entitled to benefit of same - Appeals dismissed.
