Escorts Farms Limited, Previously Known As Messrs Escorts Farms (Ramgarh) Limited v Commissioner, Kumaon Division, Nainital, U. P and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1157; (2004) 4 SCC 281; AIR 2004 SC 2186; JT 2004 (2) SC 481; 2005 (98) RD 158; 2004(2) SCALE 607; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Society

Summary: A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - UP Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, s. 3(9)(17) - Govt. Grants Act, 1895 - Govt. Grants (UP Amendment) Act, 1960 - Acquisition of land - Applicability of Act - Govt. decided to release land to ruler on lease under 1895 Act as amended in its application to State by Govt. Grants 1960 Act - In terms of Govt. Grants, ruler and company had to develop lands cultivable but as company was unable to develop land they entered into agreement to form third company - 1960 Act enforced which declared land as surplus with holder of lands - Appeal filed before District Judge who remanded case to prescribed authority and authority passed fresh order determining land as surplus and holder was allowed to retain land which included 250 acres of land claimed to be used for running Farm School - Under amended Govt. Grants Act, fresh ceiling proceedings were initiated but transferees filed their objections - Authority declared Land of 250 acres exempted under earlier order of ceiling authority was left undisturbed and land for school, was held to be barred by principle of res judicata - Transferees and Farm filed appeals before Commissioner - Commissioner dismissed appeal holding that exemption in favor of school of land was wrongly granted and plea of Res Judicata could not be raised under provisions of 1960 Act and directed that surplus land inclusive of 250 acres of land wrongly exempted in favor of school vested in State under the Ceiling Act - Firm filed Petition before HC - HC dismissed all Petitions - Hence instant Appeal - Whether 1960 Act was applicable to farm - Held, farm was only ostensible holder of land and company of which Ruler was share holder continued to be real holder - Notices issued by ceiling authority were responded by submitting statements and returns before ceiling authority by Farm - Limited purpose of s. 5(4) was clear to treat land as being held in proportion to respective shares of shareholders in case of firm, co-operative society or other society and association of persons - Exclusion of public company from. 5 (4) was with intention to keep out public companies from application of section in matter of distribution of land holdings amongst shareholders - Exclusion of public company in matter of distribution of shareholding of land was not indication that public company was not deemed to be a 'holder' of land or legal 'person' as defined in s. 3(9)(17) of 1960 Act r/w s. 4(33) of the U.P. General Clauses Act - Therefore ceiling proceedings could not be initiated and concluded against company through farm and they were all invalid and non est, was rejected - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Govt. Grants (UP Amendment) Act, 1960 - Validity of sale deed - Execution - Whether sale-deeds were executed in anticipation of Amendment Act was valid - Held, Resolution of Board of Directors quoted and heavily relied by appellate authority and HC in their orders clearly showed that sale-deeds were executed in anticipation of Amendment Act and at time when reduction of ceiling limit was already made public - Oral leases followed by sale-deeds were in favour of persons closely connected with managing partners of firm - Consideration received was not duly accounted for in balance sheet of company - HC confirmed conclusion that all transfers were made to related parties and only to evade effect of impending amendment to ceiling law - Concurrent findings in judgment of appellate authority and of HC of lack of good faith on part of Company and Firm in executing sale-deeds after cut off was not vitiated by consideration of any irrelevant circumstances and essentially finding of fact was not liable to be interfered with - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - UP Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960 UP Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings (Amended Act), 1976 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Principal of resjudicata - Applicability - Whether principal of resjudicata was applicable to firm and land was held to running of farming school - Held, bar of res judicata was not available to holder Company or Farm - Their own subsequent conduct of effecting transfers of school land estopped them from raising plea of res judicata on apparently erroneous finding recorded in order of Prescribed Authority in course of proceedings under original un amended Act - Res judicata was plea available in civil proceedings in accordance with s. 11 of CPC - It was doctrine applied to give finality to lis in original or appellate proceedings - Plea of res judicata was barred in proceedings under Ceiling Law - Amendments made to 1960 Act justified reopening of proceedings undertaken under the 1960 Act prior to amendment and s. 38-B of Amended Act of 1976 bars plea of res judicata to parties on basis of findings and decisions in earlier ceiling proceedings - Land of school as includible for determination of ceiling area, in appeals instituted against order of Prescribed Authority by holder of land and transferees and without any appeal by State - Appeal dismissed.

(D) Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 136 - Principal of natural jusrice - Denial of hearing - Whether denial of opportunity of hearing to transferees of land/Breach of Principles of Natural Justice - Held, large scale transfers were effected to defeat Ceiling Law - Transfers made after cut-off date were not in good faith hence liable to be ignored for determining extent of surplus land with holder - Entire land being held under Govt. Grant were not transferable without permission of Govt. and transfers were invalid being in clear breach of the conditions of the Grant - Right of hearing to necessary party was valuable right of Denial such right was serious breach of statutory procedure and violation of rules of natural justice - Terms of Govt. Grant did not permit transfers of land without permission of State as grantor - Remand of cases of group of transferees who were not heard, would, therefore, be of no legal consequence - Rules of natural justice was to be followed for doing substantial justice therefore, refrained from remanding those cases in exercise of discretionary powers u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.

(E) Land & Property - UP Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, ss. 16, 18A - Imposition of cost - Validity - Whether cost imposed as damages was valid - Held, provisions of ss. 16 r/w 18-A of the Act required separate proceedings for determination and quantification of amount of damages for use and occupation of surplus land - However, HC should not awarded lump sum damages by imposing heavy costs - Managing partner was found active part in formation of partnership firms and obtaining transfers for favoured parties - Managing partner did not in individual capacity but as managing partner of one of partnership firms and on being invited by holder Company in meeting to help out company from effect of ceiling law - In event of default of payment of costs by company, direction made by HC to managing partner to pay cost was not justified - therefore part of order imposing costs on Farm and directing its payment by Farm was set aside - Appeals dismissed.
