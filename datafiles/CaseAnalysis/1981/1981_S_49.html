Harcharan Singh v Shiv Rani and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 421; (1981) 2 SCC 535; AIR 1981 SC 1284; 1981 ALJ 504; 1981 (7) ALR 206; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 962

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Indian Evidence Act, s. 114 - Serving of notice of ejection - Mode of - Whether, when landlords' notice demanding arrears and seeking eviction is sent by registered post and is refused by tenant, latter could be imputed knowledge of contents thereof that upon his failure to comply with notice tenant could be said to have committed willful default in payment of rent? - Held, s. 27 of Act deals with topic 'Meaning of service by post' and says that where any Central Act or Regulation authorises or requires any document to be served by post, then unless a different intention appears, service shall be deemed to be effected by properly addressing, pre-paying and posting it by registered post, a letter containing document, and unless contrary is proved, to have been effected at time at which letter would be delivered in ordinary course of post - Under section thus arises a presumption of due service or proper service if document sought to be served is sent by properly addressing, pre-paying and posting by registered post to addressee and such presumption is raised irrespective of whether any acknowledgement due is received from addressee or not - It is obvious that when section raises presumption that service shall be deemed to have been effected it means addressee to whom communication is sent must be taken to have known contents of document sought to be served upon him without any thing more - Similar presumption is raised under Illustration (f) to s. 114 of Act where under it is stated that Court may presume that common course of business has been followed in that particular case, that is to say, when a letter is sent by post by pre-paying and properly addressing it same has been received by addressee - Undoubtedly, presumptions both u/s. 27 of General Clauses Act, 1897, as well as u/s. 114 of Act are rebuttable but in absence of proof to contrary, presumption of proper service or effective service on addressee would arise - In instant case, additionally, there was positive evidence of postman to effect that registered envelope was actually tendered by him to appellant on 10-11-1966 but appellant refused to accept - In other words, there was due service effected upon appellant by refusal - Appeal dismissed.
