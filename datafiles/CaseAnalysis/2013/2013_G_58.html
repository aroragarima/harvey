Guddu @ Dwarikendra v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, M.Y. Eqbal, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 683; (2013) 16 SCC 596; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 615; 2013(12) SCALE 290; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 293

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Death sentence - Sustainability - Appellant was tried for the offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court relied on primarily the evidence of eye- witnesses PWs5 and 7, whose evidence was corroborated by the evidence of PWs4, 8, 9 and 18 and the medical evidence of PWs16 and 14 and the post-mortem report of the deceased persons and the medical report of A2 to reject the defense version - Trial Court held that the appellant had invited the deceased family for lunch and upon a quarrel thereat, attacked 'S' with a knife and thereafter, assaulted 'S', his wife and his child to death - Motive of the appellant was recorded as the suspicion of the appellant on the fidelity of A2 and her continuous engagement with 'S' even after his warnings - Trial Court convicted the appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to death - On appeal, HC confirmed the judgment and order passed by the Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether conviction of appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Held, testimony of the two eye-witnesses was natural, convincing and well corroborated by the evidence of PW4, 8, 9 and 18 and the medical evidence - Evidence of the two eye-witnesses was credible and trustworthy - Also, prosecution case was well supported and established by the evidence of PW5, 7, 9 and 18 coupled with the evidence of Doctors, the post-mortem report and medical evidence and did not leave any room for doubt as to the guilt of the appellant - Therefore, the Courts below had not committed any error in convicting the appellant for the murder of the three persons u/s. 302 of the IPC and the conviction of the appellant was upheld - (B) Whether instant case fell into the category of 'rarest of the rare' to justify the imposition of death penalty - Held, it fell upon the Court to award the sentence having due regard to the nature of offence such that neither was the punishment disproportionately severe nor was it manifestly inadequate, as either case would not sub-serve the cause of justice to the society - In the instant case, appellant was a young man of about 35 years and neither did he had any criminal antecedents nor was it stated that he was or had been an anti-social element - Future possibilities of his reform also could not be ruled out - Hence, having regard to the nature of the offence and the methodology adopted by the appellant, instant case did not fells into the category of 'rarest of the rare' to justify the imposition of death penalty - Also, brutality as evinced by the appellant would not fall within the ambit of the 'rarest of the rare' cases - Therefore, sentence of death imposed on the appellant be commuted to imprisonment for life - Appeals disposed of.
