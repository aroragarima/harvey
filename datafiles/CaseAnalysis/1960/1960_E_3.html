East and West Steamship Company, George Town, Madras v S. K. Ramalingam Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 465; AIR 1960 SC 1058; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 820

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Carriage Good Act, 1925, art. III(6) - Limitation Act, art. 31 - Compensation - High Court discharged shipper from liability of compensation for non-delivery - Whether suit for compensation brought within one year? - Whatever be proper mode of ascertaining date when delivery 'ought to be made' u/art. 31 of Limitation Act - Whether that be reasonable time for delivery in circumstances of case or date when after correspondence carrier intimates its inability to deliver or date of final repudiation of claim on a claim for compensation having been made or in case of part delivery date when bulk of consignment was delivered date when goods should have been delivered? - For purpose of third clause of 6 th paragraph of art. III of Act is date when ship by which goods were contracted to be carried has left port at which delivery was to be made - Held, date of delivery is date when ship by which carried goods left port for delivery - None of suits were brought within a year from date when ship carrying goods left port of discharge - Appeal dismissed.
