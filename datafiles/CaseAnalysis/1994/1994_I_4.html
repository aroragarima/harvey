Inder Prashad v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1714; (1994) 5 SCC 239; JT 1998 (4) SC 440; 1994(2) SCALE 553

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Appellant admittedly was a lessee of Nazul land - Had perpetual lease from Govt. of India - Appellants with permission of lessor constructed a building on demised land and was in its quiet enjoyment, complying with covenants - By a notification u/s. 4(1) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894, demised land together with building, along with other lands was acquired for a public purpose - Appellant being aggrieved for apportionment of 25% of compensation in favour of Govt. filed this appeal by special leave - Whether Govt. being owner of land cannot acquire its own interest therein and appellant is entitled to total compensation determined by award? - Held, when land of Govt. is granted on lease in favour of a lessee, its power to resume land is subject to non-fulfillment of terms and conditions of lease by lessee - But when land is required for public purpose, Govt. should get absolute title thereof free from all encumbrances - Compensation become payable for leasehold right or interest held by lessee or grantee when land is acquired - High Court by its judgment and decree under present appeal has modified apportionment of compensation payable for land as 75 per cent for lessee and 25 per cent for lessor - It cannot be said that Land Acquisition Collector had determined compensation only towards leasehold interest held by appellant and that, therefore, appellant is entitled to entire compensation determined by Collector - Appeal dismissed.
