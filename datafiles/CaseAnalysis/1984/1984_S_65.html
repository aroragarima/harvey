Vijay Narain Singh v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 230; (1984) 3 SCC 14; (1984) SCC (Cr) 361; AIR 1984 SC 1334; 1984 (1) Crimes 914; 1984 CRLJ 909; 1984 CrLR(SC) 240; 1984(1) SCALE 736; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Defence Regulations, communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Bihar Control of Crimes Act, 1981, ss. 2(d) and 12 - Constitution, arts.22(5) and 226 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Detention - (A) Question of interpretation of the provisions of the Bihar Control of Crimes Act, 1981 - Definition of anti-social element - Whether alleged incidents constitute different offences or same offence committed after long interval - Held, order of detention could not have been passed u/s.12(2) of the Act which authorises the detention of anti-social elements only - (B) Question of the effect of the order of grant of bail in the criminal proceeding arising out of the incident constituting one of the grounds of detention - Held, adequacy or sufficiency may not be a ground of challenge but relevancy and proximity are certainly grounds - Petition allowed.
