State of Gujarat and others v Essar Oil Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 395; (2012) 3 SCC 522; AIR 2012 SC 1146; JT 2012 (1) SC 536; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 810; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 667; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 1127; [2012] 48 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Administrative - Sales Tax - Practice & Procedure - Capital Investment Incentive to Premier/Prestigious Unit Scheme, 1995-2000 - Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Indian Forest Act, 1927 - Sales Tax Incentives - Violation of Statutory Provisions - Eligibility - Govt. in Industries and Mines Department vide Resolution dt. 11-09-1995, introduced Scheme envisaged grant of Sales Tax incentives by way of Sales Tax Exemption or Sales Tax Deferment or Composite Schemes, for Premier/Prestigious Units according to location, investment and status of project - Respondent No. 1 was given benefit of Sales Tax incentive as its investment was more than Rs. 1000 Crores - Respondent No. 1 due to several litigations being filed against it for violation of statutory provisions could not set up its establishments and commence production - Govt. has issued notification that 'industry units in pipelines cases which had been registered should start production within two years from January 1, 2000 failing which such units shall be rendered ineligible for sales tax incentive - Several cases were filed against officials of respondent No. 1 and after much delay respondent No. 1 commenced commercial production and started paying sales tax on products sold by it under protest - Respondent No. 1 filed writ petition as there was no communication on representation made by respondent No. 1 for granting of Sales Tax incentive - Respondent No.1 contended before HC, that delay was caused due to delay caused by Govt. officials in granting permission/sanctions and also due to injunction orders passed by Courts, hence it was eligible for sales tax incentive - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in holding that respondent No. 1 was eligible for sales tax incentive - Held, respondent No. 1 was categorically told by letter dt. 28-05-2002, which was much prior to expiry of period, that time for availing exemption could not be extended, admittedly, respondent No. 1 failed to meet deadline - In factual scenario, exercise undertaken by HC in impugned judgment by directing various adjustments which virtually re-wrote State's exemption scheme, was an exercise which was, with great respect, neither warranted in law nor supported by precedents - There was no question of equity here, an exemption was a standalone process, either an industry claiming exemption comes within it or it does not, hence, HC, judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
