Dinesh Tiwari v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 441; (2014) 13 SCC 137; AIR 2014 SC 3502; 2014 CRLJ 4192; JT 2014 (8) SC 365; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2921; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 36; 2015 (1) RLW 26; 2014(8) SCALE 427; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Framing of charges, Quashing, Denied

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323, 504, 506 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 226, 227, 228, 482 - Framing of charges - Quashing - Denied - Legality - Appellant (accused) was charge sheeted for alleged offence u/ss. 302, 323, 504, 506 of IPC and Trail Court by its order framed charges against appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed an application u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC for quashing the order of framing the charge u/s. 302 of IPC by Trial Court - HC dismissed the application by its impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that he was not given an opportunity of being heard before framing of charge u/s. 302 of IPC and that neither any charge sheet was submitted by investigating agency against appellant for the offence u/s. 302 of IPC nor any cognizance was taken by Magistrate against him for the said offence - But Sessions Judge after committal framed the charge u/s. 302 of IPC which was not permissible -

Held, from s. 228(1) of CrPC, it was clear that after consideration and hearing, as given u/s. 227 of CrPC, if Judge forms an opinion that there was a ground for presuming that the accused has committed an offence, Judge may frame the charge(s) - From s. 228 of CrPC it was clear that no separate hearing was required to be given for framing the charge if the accused was not discharged upon consideration of the record of the case and documents and after hearing the submissions u/s. 227 of CrPC - In instant case, it was not alleged that Sessions Judge had not followed ss. 226 and 227 of CrPC before framing the charge - Further, it was not the case of appellant that Court had not given him hearing at the stage of discharge u/s. 227 of CrPC - For framing of charge u/s. 228 of CrPC, Judge was not required to record detail reasons as to why such charge was framed - On perusal of record and hearing parties at the stage of discharge u/s. 227 of CrPC if the Judge was of opinion that there was ground for presuming that the accused has committed an offence, he was competent to frame charge for such offence even if not mentioned in charge sheet - Appeal dismissed.
