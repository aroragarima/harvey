U.P. State Road Transport Corporation v Man Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 516; (2006) 7 SCC 752; 2006 (6) AWC 5603; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 282; 2006(9) SCALE 230; 2006 (7) SCJ 52; 2007 (3) SLR 874; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 121; 2006 (3) UC 1978

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Road Transport Corporation Act - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11A, 25F - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Respondent appointed on temporary basis - Termination - Labour Court finding that in retrenching respondent appellant failed to comply with mandatory requirements of s.25 F - Held, appellant is a statutory Corporation - Respondent was appointed on a temporary basis, it was unlikely that he remained unemployed for such a long time - It would be wholly unjust at this distance of time i e after a period of more than 30 years, to direct reinstatement of respondent in service - Appellant is directed to pay a sum of Rs. 50, 000/- To him - Appeal partly allowed.
