United India Insurance Company Limited and Others v Patricia Jean Mahajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1726; (2002) 6 SCC 281; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1294; 2002 (2) ACC 460; AIR 2002 SC 2607; 2002 (4) ALD(SC) 118; 2002 (3) AWC 2410; 2002 (2) CRC 291; 2002 (98) DLT 410; 2002 (4) G.L.R. 2900; JT 2002 (5) SC 74; 2002 (132) PLR 281; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 534; 2002(5) SCALE 56; 2002 (3) SCJ 232; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1176; 2002 (4) SLT 262; 2002 (4) Supreme 518

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Death Or Permanent Disablement, Indian Fatal Accidents Act, 1855

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Oil, Natural Gas Commission - Tort - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 140, 163-A, 165(1), 166 and 168 - One Dr Mahajan, who had set up a hospital in U.S.A., had a good practice, received injuries, died because of rash, negligent driving of trailer No. HR 29-D-1125 - Learned single judge of High Court applied multiplier of 10 and disallowed any deductions on account of social security system - Division bench, however, applied multiplier of 13 according to Sch. 2 to Act referable to s. 163-A of Act - (A) What should be appropriate multiplier in facts and circumstances of case? - Held, under explanation to s. 165(1), it has been indicated that provision includes claims for compensation u/ss. 140 and 163-A but it is nowhere provided that amount of compensation is to be assessed or calculated according to Sch. 2 - On other hand, s. 168 provides key leading to determination of amount of compensation u/s. 166 of Act - Court must adhere to system of multiplier in arriving at proper amount of compensation, and also with a view to maintain uniformity and certainty - Use of higher multiplier has been deprecated and it is emphasized that it cannot exceed 18 - Multiplier of 10 as applied by learned single judge should be restored instead of multiplier of 13 as applied by division bench - (B) Whether while assessing amount of compensation, benefits which have accrued to claimants by reason of death must also be taken into account? - Further held, there is no correlation between compensation payable on account of accidental death and amounts receivable irrespective of such accidental death, which otherwise in normal course one would be entitled to receive - HC has rightly disallowed any deduction on account of receipts under insurance policy and other receipts under social security system which claimant would have also otherwise been entitled to receive irrespective of accidental death of Dr Mahajan - Appeal partly allowed.
