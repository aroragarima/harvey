Â N.K. Mohammad Sulaiman v N. C. Mohammad Ismail and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 254; AIR 1966 SC 792; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 10; 1967 (1) SCJ 24; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 937

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 50 and 52, O.22, rr.3 and 4, O.34, r.1 - Khader Miran, Muhammad Abdul Kassim and Muhammad Labhai mortgaged immovable property in favour of Narsimha Reddy to secure repayment of certain amount - Khader Miran, Narsimha Reddy commenced an action for enforcement of mortgage against Muhammad Abdul Kasim, Muhammad Labhai, and three widows of Khader Miran - A preliminary mortgage decree passed and in execution of decree properties mortgaged were sold at a court auction and were purchased by mortgagee Narsimha Reddywho trasferred it to P Chinnamma Reddi - Plaintiff,Mohammad Sulaiman, claiming that he was son of Khader Miran instituted suit for a decree for partition of mortaged properties by metes and bounds - (A) Whether a decree obtained by a creditor against heirs of a deceased muslim is binding upon entire estate or only of those who were impleaded to be a nominee? - Plaintiff seeking relief institutes his suit against a person who is not representing estate of a deceased person against whom plaintiff has a claim - Held, in absence of fraud or collusion or other ground which taint decree, a decree passed against persons impleaded as heirs binds estate, even though other persons interested in estate are not brought on record - This principle is a part of law of procedure which regulates all matters going to remedy and applied to all parties irrespective of their personal law - (B) On whom does principle of representation apply? - Further held, principle of representation of estate by heirs who were joined as parties applied to case and decree was binding on persons who claimed to be sons of deceased mortgagor and sued for a declaration that mortgage decree was not binding on them - Appeal dismissed.
