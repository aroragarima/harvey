State of Karnataka v Azad Coach Builders Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 272; (2006) 3 SCC 338; JT 2006 (2) SC 599; 2006(2) SCALE 487; 2006 (3) SCJ 375; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 245; 2006 (145) STC 176

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956,s. 5(3) - Constitution of India, 1950 - Whether since sale is only for bus-body and not for complete bus by assessee to exporter in India, assessee is not entitled to benefit of s. 5(3) of Act? - Held, there cannot be a bus without bus-body - Subject matter of inter-state movement and subject matter of export is a 'bus' and not a 'bus-body' - It cannot be denied that sale of bus-body by assessee to exporter is in course of export of bus to Sri Lanka - What is delivered to exporter by assessee is a complete bus - It is true that for accounting purpose, there is a bifurcation between bus-body and a complete bus - Decisions of this court cited hereinabove in case of Sterling Foods v State of Karnataka and Vijaylakshmi Cashew Co. and Others v Dy Commercial Tax Officer need reconsideration by a larger bench - Appeal disposed of.
