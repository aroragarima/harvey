M.L. Binjolkar v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 573; (2005) 6 SCC 224; (2005) SCC (L&S) 827; 2005 (6) AWC 5510; 2005 (106) FLR 924; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 110; JT 2005 (6) SC 461; 2005 (3) LLN 1035; 2005(5) SCALE 657; 2005 (5) SLR 379; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 765; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2988

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Compulsory retirement - In four cases HC found that orders passed were not supportable and ordered re-instatement with 50% of amount payable - In certain cases, HC found that there was no infirmity in orders passed directing compulsory retirement - Appealed herein - Held, order of HC not to be interfered with as concerned employees already retired and it would not be in interest of anybody to go into merits - No reason to interfere with quantum of 50% fixed by HC - Appeals disposed of.
