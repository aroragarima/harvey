State Bank of India v Shri A.N. Gupta Etc

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 634; (1997) 8 SCC 60; AIR 1998 SC 159; 1997 (77) FLR 794; JT 1997 (8) SC 336; 1998 LabIC 396; 1998 (1) LLN 9; 1997(6) SCALE 303; 1999 (1) SLJ 60; 1997 (5) SLR 601; [1997] Supp4 S.C.R. 383; 1997 (8) Supreme 526; 1997 (3) UPLBEC 2109

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Imperial Bank of India Act, 1920 - Rules and Regulations of the Imperial Bank of India Pension and Guarantee, r. 11 - Imperial Bank of India Employee's Provident Fund Rules, r. 20 - Pension Rules, rr. 10, 11 and 20 - When can the pension of an employee be withheld? - Held, r. 10 of the Pension Rules provides for forfeiture of pension if an employee is dismissed from service for willful neglect or fraud - R. 26 of the Service Rules clearly mandates the retirement of an employee on his attaining the age of superannuation - R. 20 of the Employees' Provident Fund Rule will become applicable only if an employee retiring from the service of the Bank is under a liability incurred by him to the Bank - In that case trustees administering the Provident Fund can pay to the Bank out of the balance to the credit of the employee in the Fund any amount due by him to the Bank - It cannot accept the proposition that the trustees of the Bank should be allowed to withhold the Provident Fund till they have had an opportunity to determine the amount, if any, due from the respondents - Appeals partly allowed.
