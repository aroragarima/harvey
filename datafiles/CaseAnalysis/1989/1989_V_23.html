Sau Ashabai Kate v Vithal Bhika Nade

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 265; (1989) Supp2 SCC 450; AIR 1990 SC 670; 1989 (15) ALR 785; 1990 (3) Bom.C.R. 229; 1989 (3) CCC 311; 1990 (1) DMC 45; 1990 (1) HLR 334; JT 1989 (4) SC 163; 1989(2) SCALE 866; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 464; [1989] SUPP1 S.C.R. 464

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance, Coparcenary rights, Suit for possession of joint property, Revival of adoption rights

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Hindu Women's Rights to Property Act, 1937, s. 3(2) - Coparcenary rights - Suit for possession of joint property - Revival of adoption rights - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) filed a suit for possession of properties with mesne profits and a decree for money as expenses of her marriage and challenged the power of her grand-mother respondent (defendant no. 2) to adopt the defendant no. 1 on the ground that her right to adopt was lost on the death of her grand-father leaving behind his widow respondent (defendant no. 2) - Trial Court upheld adoption of defendant no. 1 as valid and dismissed suit - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was allowed - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment held that a Hindu widow's power to adopt was revived the moment there was nobody to continue the line, and since mother of appellant incapacitated herself in doing so by her remarriage, the right of her mother-in-law to adopt a son to her husband revived - Adoption of defendant no. 1 was, thus, found legal and valid - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether on the remarriage of son's widow the power of the son's mother to adopt revives - Held, on the death of father of appellant the responsibility for the continuance of family line fell on his widow mother of appellant by the power of adoption vesting in her, and the power of defendant no. 2 to adopt was extinguished permanently and did not revive even on remarriage of mother of appellant - Consequently the adoption of defendant no.1 was invalid in the eye of law and he did not get any interest in suit properties - Properties belonged to joint family of which husband of defendant no. 2 was a coparcener and defendant no. 2 got u/s. 3(2) of the Act, the same interest as husband of defendant no. 2 had in joint family properties - Interest which initially devolved on defendant no. 2, was limited in nature known as Hindu Woman's estate - Appellant was entitled to a decree for half share in suit properties, as prayed - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
