Senior Divisional Manager, Life Insurance Corporation of India Limited and others v Shree Lal Meena

Supreme Court of India

26 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 985; AIR 2016 SC 1394; 2016 (149) FLR 609; 2016(2) SCALE 526; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 158

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Voluntary Retirement, provident fund, Termination Of Service, Competent Authority, Age, Retrenchment, Superannuation, Resignation, Leave, Salary, Pension, Employment, Rule, Date, Qualifying Service, Appeal, Interest, Retiral Benefits, Amendment, Option, Relationship, Scheme, Employer, Pension Scheme, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Arrears, Life Insurance Corporation of India (Staff) Regulations, 1960, Central Bank of India (Employees) Pension Regulations, 1995, Reserve Bank of India Pension Regulations, 1990, Employees' Pension Scheme, 1995, Notice, Employee, Constitution of India, 1950, Life Insurance Corporation (Employees) Pension Rules, 1995

Summary: Service - Life Insurance Corporation (Employees) Pension Rules, 1995, rr. 23, 3, 31, 34 - Voluntary retirement - Resignation - Entitlement to pension - Respondent submitted resignation from services of Corporation on 14-7-1990 and the same was acceded by Corporation vide letter dt.11-1-1991 - While so, Corporation brought into force Rules with retrospective effect from 1-11-1993 and then respondent submitted representation for grant of pension under the Rules but Corporation rejected the same on ground that respondent resigned from service and not retired, so not entitled for grant of pension as per the Rules - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC and Single Judge of HC allowed writ petition and directed grant of retiral benefits to respondent as per the Rules - Aggrieved, Corporation preferred intra-court appeal but the same was dismissed by DB of HC affirming views of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent is entitled for grant of retiral benefits under the Rules.

Held, retrospective operation of the Rules in entirely is limited to the employees, who had retired in normal course of superannuation. Resignation has effect of termination of employee and though voluntary retirement has effect of termination of employee yet it has different consequences. In former case, ex-employee could not be entitled to pension, whereas in case of voluntary retirement, employee would be entitled to pension depending upon terms postulated in the regulations or rules or scheme. Further, r.23 of the Rules specifically provides that on resignation, dismissal, removal, termination or compulsory retirement, the employee has to forfeit the entire past service and he would not qualify for pensionary benefit. Thus, resignation given under the Rules would not entitle an employee to get pension. Further, in Asger Ibrahim Amin. 2015 INDLAW SC 705, retrospectivity has been given to r.31 and for said purpose amendment to 1960 Regulations, specially regn.19(2A) was taken recourse to. However, r.31 covers field of voluntary retirement and does not make it retrospective, there being a real difference between resignation and retirement, it is not seemly to read the amended regulations to the Rules to make the same retrospective. Hence, view expressed by SC in Asger Ibrahim Amin v. Life Insurance Corporation of India, 2015 INDLAW SC 705 could not be concurred with. Hence, matter is placed before CJI for constitution of larger Bench to look into the matter. Matter referred to larger Bench. Order accordingly.
