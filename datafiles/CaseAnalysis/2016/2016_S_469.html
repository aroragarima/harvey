Â Muthuramalingam and others v State, Represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.K. Sikri, Sharad Arvind Bobde, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 517; (2016) 8 SCC 313; AIR 2016 SC 3340; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 3575; 2016 CRLJ 4165; 2016 (2) ILR(Cut) 231; 2016 (4) JCC 2279; JT 2016 (7) SC 1; 2016 (3) KLT 480; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 827; 2016(7) SCALE 129; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 30; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 85

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Imprisonment For Life, Proviso, Prisoner, Remission, Commutation, Rigorous Imprisonment, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 31, 31(2), 427, 427(2), 432, 433 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 207, 302, 306, 354, 376, 376(2)(f), 45, 498, 71 - Conviction - Consecutive punishments - Sustainability - Appellants were convicted for offences punishable u/s. 302 IPC for several murders allegedly committed by them in a single incident - Trial Court found appellants guilty and sentenced to suffer varying sentences, including imprisonment for life for each one of the murders committed by them - On appeal, HC affirmed the judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant reference - Whether consecutive life sentences can be awarded to a convict on being found guilty of a series of murders for which he has been tried in a single trial.

Held, while multiple sentences for imprisonment for life can be awarded for multiple murders or other offences punishable with imprisonment for life, life sentences so awarded cannot be directed to run consecutively. Such sentences would, however, be super imposed over each other so that any remission or commutation granted by competent authority in one does not ipso facto result in remission of sentence awarded to the prisoner for the other. Therefore, Court can legitimately direct that prisoner should first undergo term sentence before commencement of his life sentence. Such a direction shall be perfectly legitimate and in tune with s. 31 CrPC. The converse however may not be true for if Court directs the life sentence to start first it would necessarily imply that the term sentence would run concurrently. That is because once the prisoner spends his life in jail, there is no question of his undergoing any further sentence. Whether or not the direction of Court below calls for any modification or alteration is a matter with which SC is not concerned. The Regular Bench hearing the appeals would be free to deal with that aspect. Reference answered.

Ratio - If a person is sentenced for conviction of several offences the proviso to s. 31(2) CrPC shall come into play and no consecutive sentence can be imposed.
