Rameshwari Devi v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 16 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 5(i) and 11 - Dispute concerns payment of family pension and death cum retirement gratuity to two wives of deceased - From first marriage he had one son and from second marriage four sons - High Court held that even though second marriage was void their children would be legitimate and thus would be entitled to claim share in family pension and death retirement gratuity of deceased - Second wife was not entitled to anything - In appeal by first wife it was argued that there was no marriage between deceased and second wife therefore children were not legitimate - Held, it cannot be disputed that marriage between deceased and second wife was in contravention of cl.(i) of s. 5 of Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 and was a void marriage u/s. 16 of this Act, children of a void marriage are legitimate under Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Appeal dismissed.
