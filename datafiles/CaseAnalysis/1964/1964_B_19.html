Â State of Madhya Pradesh v Bhopal Sugar Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 300; AIR 1964 SC 1179; [1964] 52 ITR 443; 1964 JabLJ 369; 1964 MLJ 697; 1964 MPLJ 857; 1964 (1) SCJ 555; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution - Reorganization of States Act, 1956, s. 119 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Bhopal State Agricultural Income Tax Act, 1953 - Whether Bhopal Act, 1953 observing that Act was 'in clear contravention of petitioner's right u/art. 14 of Constitution and must be declared void? - High Court observed that even though state had enacted Madhya Pradesh Extension of Laws Act, 1958, and had removed diversity in some of laws of component regions, no attempt was made to remove discrimination between territory of former Bhopal State and rest of territories of State of MP in matter of levy of agricultural income-tax - Held, this view of HC was unlawful because state had since enactment of States Reorganisation Act sufficient time and opportunity to decide whether continuance of Bhopal State Agricultural Income Tax Act in Bhopal region would be consistent with art. 14 of Constitution - It would be impossible to lay down any definite time-limit within which state had to make necessary adjustments so as to effectuate equality clause of Constitution - Case remanded to HC for re-trial - Appeal allowed.
