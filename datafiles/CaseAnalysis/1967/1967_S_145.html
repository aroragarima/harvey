Sunderlal and Son v Bharat Handicrafts Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 395; AIR 1968 SC 406; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contracts & Commercial - Forward Contracts (Regulation ) Act, 1952, s. 15(4) - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 33 - Appellants applied to High Court u/s. 33 of Act for an order declaring that 'there exists a valid arbitration agreement contained in contract between petitioners' and respondents - With regard to the existence of contract and also its validity, HC dismissed application holding that contract was invalid in that it did not comply with requirements of s. 15(4) Act - Whether that appellants were not at relevant time members of recognized Association and that requirements of s. 15(4) of Act were not complied with and contract was on that account invalid? - Held, respondents in paragraph 6 of plaint filed in Civil Court for a declaration that there was in fact no contract between them and appellants in which respondents had averred that they had discovered that appellants 'at all material times were members of said association - In view of this evidence, counsel for respondents did not seek to support decision of HC - For second issue by s. 15(4) Parliament has then imposed a prohibition upon every member of a recognized association against entry into a contract on his own account with a non-member in respect of specified goods and prohibition is lifted when in memorandum, agreement of sale or purchase or in bought and sold notes it is expressly disclosed that contract is by member on his own account and that he has secured consent or authority of other person who is a party to contract, and if such consent or authority be not in writing, member has obtained it within 3 days from date of contract - It is contemplated that for an enforceable contract to arise there shall be a writing evidencing or confirming consent or authority of such person - Prohibition imposed by cl. (4) is not imposed in interest of revenue, clause is apparently conceived in large interest of public to protect them against malpractice indulged by members of recognized associations in respect of transactions in which their duties as agents conflict with their personal interest - Parliament has clearly made a writing evidencing or confirming consent or authority of a non-member as a condition of contract, if member has entered into a contract on his own account - So long as there is no writing as is contemplated by s. 15(4) or proviso thereto, there is no enforceable contract - Appeal dismissed.
