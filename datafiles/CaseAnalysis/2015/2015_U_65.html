Ramesh Kumar v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 544; AIR 2015 SC 2904; 2015 (5) AWC 4559; 2015 (147) FLR 211; JT 2015 (7) SC 363; 2015 (6) MLJ 243; 2015(8) SCALE 426; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 138; 2015 (5) SLR 628

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, ss. 164, 39(a), 41(i), 54(b), 63 - Imposition of Punishment - Promotion granted - Failure to grant pay and allowance - Appellant joined Indian Army on 19-3-1983 and was promoted on 1-8-1989 - Further, Summary Court Martial for offences u/ss. 41(i), 39 (a) and 63 of Act was initiated against him and after completion of inquiry and on proved charges by order appellant was sentenced - However, Central Govt. vide order commuted punishment modifying it to one of severe reprimand and further remitted sentence of dismissal from service directing reinstatement in service - Further, it was held that appellant was not entitled to any pay and allowances for period between date of dismissal and date of reinstatement in service - Appellant was reinstated in service w.e.f. 29-10-1994 and was again put to Summary Court Martial for committing offence u/s. 54(b) of Act and by order dt. 18-2-1995; appellant was awarded severe reprimand/red ink entry - Appellant was promoted and his seniority was maintained w.e.f. 1-8-1997, however, no direction was issued regarding any pay and allowances to appellant from back date - HC dismissed petition of appellant on ground that appellant has no legitimate claim for payment of pay and allowances from retrospective date i.e. from 1-8-1997 to 13-11-2000 on principle of 'no work no pay' - Further, review petition was dismissed - Hence, present appeal.

Held, when case of appellant came up for consideration on 1-8-1997, first punishment/red ink entry had already expired i.e. on 3-6-1997 and only one red ink entry made on 18-2-1995 was on record; but authority appears to have erred in ignoring same. SC held that when appellant was granted ante-dated seniority w.e.f. 1-8-1997 alongwith his batchmates, SC held that there is no reason as to why he should be denied pay and allowances in promotional post as w.e.f. 1-8-1997 till date of his actual promotion on 13-11-2000. SC held that HC has not properly appreciated these aspects and erred in holding that on 1-8-1997, appellant was not eligible to be considered for promotion. When respondents themselves have taken view that order of Government would be deemed to have taken from date of original sentence was passed i.e. 3-6-1992 and not from 17-8-1994, date on which commutation/remission was granted by Govt., HC was not right in holding that appellant was not eligible to be considered for promotion on 01.08.1997 and impugned order cannot be sustained. Further, SC held that principle of 'no work no pay' would not be attracted where respondents were in fault in not considering case of appellant for promotion and not allowing appellant to work on post of carrying higher pay scale. SC held it would be unjust to deny appellant higher pay and allowances in promotional position. Hence, impugned orders passed by HC are set aside. Respondents shall release arrears of pay and allowances to appellant for period from 1-8-1997 till date of his actual promotion that is 13-11-2000 in promotional post. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to grant pay and allowance retrospectively when promotion is granted to employee from retrospective effect.
