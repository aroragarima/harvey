Union of India and Others v Rekha Majhi

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2887; (2000) 10 SCC 659; AIR 2000 SC 1562; JT 2000 (4) SC 379; 2000 LabIC 1468; 2000 (2) LLJ 449; 2000 (2) LLN 484; 2000(3) SCALE 203; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1058; 2000 (3) SLJ 289; 2000 (2) SLR 642; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1739

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 16 - Railway Services (Pension) Rules, 1993, r. 21(ii) - Dearness relief on family pension - Widow of employee on compassionate grounds - Drawing pension of deceased - Re-employed - Held, has wide meaning - Includes first regular appointment in service - Is immaterial whether employment of pensioner is first regular or temporary appointment or re-employment if she is a pensioner - To be treated as re-employed - Rule 21 applies and disentitles for - Denying dearness reliefs on her family pension is justified.
