Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18 and 28A - (A) (1) Whether dismissal of application seeking reference u/s. 18 on ground of delay amounts to 'not filing an application' within meaning of s. 28-A? - Held, dismissal of application seeking reference u/s. 18 on ground of delay would tantamount to not filing application within meaning of s. 28 - When an application of a landowner u/s. 18 is dismissed on ground of delay, then said landowner is entitled to make an application u/s. 28-A, if other conditions prescribed therein are fulfilled - (B) whether person who has received compensation without protest pursuant to award of Land Acquisition Collector and has not filed an application seeking reference u/s. 18 is 'a person aggrieved' within meaning of s. 28-A? - Further held, if a person has not filed an application u/s. 18 to make a reference, then irrespective of fact whether he has received compensation awarded by Collector with or without protest, he would be a person aggrieved within meaning of s. 28-A and would be entitled to make an application when some other landowner's application for reference is answered by reference court - Appeal disposed of.
