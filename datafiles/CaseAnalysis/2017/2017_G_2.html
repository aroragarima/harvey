Medical Council of India v G. C. R. G. Memorial Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 921

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Admissions to medical courses - Instant matter regarding admissions to Medical courses in the respondent institution - Instant appeal by MCI assailing orders of DB of HC quashing order dt.19-8-2017 and also order dt.31-5-2017 passed by Central Govt. and eventually granting permission to respondent no.1 to admit students for academic session 2017-18.

Held, as per order dt.28-8-2017 of SC, HC was not allowed to pass any interim order pertaining to academic session 2017-18 but the DB of HC, for some unfathomable and inscrutable reason, referred to certain judgments of SC and allowed prayer. Further, judgment was delivered by HC on 1-9-2017 without waiting for reply from Central Govt. or MCI. As such, judgment of HC shows unnecessary and uncalled for hurry, unjustified haste and an unreasonable sense of promptitude being oblivious of the fact that the stand of MCI and Central Govt. could not be given indecent burial when they were parties on record. Further, in the absence of a reply filed by MCI and Central Govt., it could not have been possible to answer the factual matrix of the case. DB of HC had no reason to abandon the concept of judicial propriety and transgress the rules and further proceed on a path where it was not required to. Hence, earlier direction that if Institution had admitted students they would be barred from continuing the course is confirmed. Further, respondent-institution is directed to pay Rs.10,00,000 to each of the students who had taken admission apart from refunding their fee. Further, for absolutely blameworthy conduct of respondent no.1, costs of Rs.25 Lakh is imposed and prayer of institution as regards academic session 2018-19 does not deserve consideration and so, is rejected. Appeal allowed.
