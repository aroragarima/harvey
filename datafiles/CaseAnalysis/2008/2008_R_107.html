Chander Kanta Bansal v Rajinder Singh Anand

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 381; (2008) 5 SCC 117; AIR 2008 SC 2234; 2008(4) ALL MR 423; 2008 (71) ALR 465; 2008 (3) AWC 2277; 2008 (149) DLT 253; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 75; JT 2008 (4) SC 496; 2008 (4) MPLJ 269; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 801; 2009 (106) RD 763; 2008(4) SCALE 546; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 748

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Payment Of Cost, Mutual Consent, Filing Of Applications

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 r/w s.151 - Suit for mandatory injunction - Application for amendment of written statement and permission to file an agreement , dismissed - Appeal - Held, no application for amendment shall be allowed after the commencement of trial, unless the court comes to the conclusion that in spite of due diligence, the party could not have raised the matter before the commencement of trial - Which further depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case - Fact that nothing was mentioned regarding the document or partition when appellant gave evidence as D.W.1- Although it was asserted that partition was oral - Supreme Court did not find any ground for interference in the High Court order - Appeal dismissed.
