FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Imtiyaz Ahmad v State of U. P. and others

Supreme Court of India

02 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur, L. Nageswara Rao

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 24; (2017) 3 SCC 658; 2017 (1) ADJ 695; JT 2017 (1) SC 443; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 229; 2017(1) SCALE 164

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Final Report, Review, Allocation, Access, Pendency, Ministers, Judiciary, Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, Facilitate, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397, 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 226 - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987 - Pendency of proceedings - Directions issued - Appellant filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC wherein writ petition of appellant was pending in HC for 6 years further that similar cases were pending before HC where proceedings were stayed at the stage of registration of an FIR, investigation, framing of charges or during trial, in exercise of the power conferred by art. 226 of Constitution or ss. 397/482 CrPC.

Held, until National Court Management Systems Committee (NCMSC) formulates a scientific method for determining the basis for computing the required judge strength of district judiciary, the judge strength should be computed for each state, in accordance with the interim approach indicated in the note submitted by Chairperson, NCMSC. NCMSC was requested to endeavour the submission of its final report by 31-12-2017. A copy of the interim report submitted by Chairperson, NCMSC should be forwarded by Union Ministry of Law and Justice to Chief Justices of all HCs and Chief Secretaries of all States within 1 month so as to enable them to take follow-up action to determine the required judge strength of the district judiciary based on the NCMSC interim report. State govts. should take up with HCs concerned the task of implementing the interim report of Chairperson, NCMSC and take necessary decisions within a period of 3 months for enhancing the required judge strength of each State judiciary. State govts. should cooperate in all respects with HCs in terms of the resolutions passed in the joint conference of Chief Justices and Chief Ministers in April 2016 with a view to ensuring expeditious disbursal of funds to State judiciaries in terms of the devolution made under the auspices of the Fourteenth Finance Commission. HCs should take up the issue of creating additional infrastructure required for meeting the existing sanctioned strength of their State judiciaries and the enhanced strength in terms of interim recommendation of NCMSC. The final report submitted by NCMSC might be placed for consideration before Conference of Chief Justices. The directions in above should then be subject to the ultimate decision that was taken on receipt of final report and a copy of order should be made available to the Registrars General of each HC and to all Chief Secretaries of States for appropriate action. List the proceedings for disposal of appeals before appropriate bench in the third week of July 2017. Order accordingly.
