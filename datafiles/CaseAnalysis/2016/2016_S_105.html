Shanti Conductors Private Limited and another v Assam State Electricity Board and others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 640; JT 2016 (9) SC 115; 2016(8) SCALE 485

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Presumption, Jurisdiction, Precedent, Contract, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Review, Payment Of Interest, Factors, Interest On Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Income-tax Act, 1961, Delayed Payment to Small Scale Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Ordinance, 1992

Summary: V. Gopala Gowda, J.

(A) Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Delayed Payment to Small Scale Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Ordinance, 1992 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, ss. 1, 10, 2, 2(b), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 25 of the Schedule, s. 19 - Suit for recovery - Retrospective effect - Validity - Respondent/State Electricity Board placed an order on 31-03-1992 for supply of Aluminium Electrical Conductors from appellants for a total consideration of Rs. 1.22 crores - On 13-05-1992 another order was placed by Electricity Board to appellants for supply of various types of conductors for a total consideration of Rs. 32.49 lacs - Meanwhile, the 1993 Act was enacted and came into force w.e.f. 23-09-1992 - On 05-03-1994, the entire payment of Rs. 2.15 crores against aforesaid supply orders was received by appellants - Appellants filed a suit for recovery on 10-01-1997 of Rs.53.68 lacs on account of interest on delayed payments and future interest at the rate of 27% p.a. - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal before HC, DB referred the matter to Full Bench - Full Bench answered reference in affirmative by holding that a suit for interest alone could be filed and that the 1993 Act is applicable to contracts entered into prior to 23-09-1992, matter was sent back to DB - DB allowed appeal relying on judgment of SC in Purbanchal Cables and Conductors Private Limited v.Assam State Electricity Board and another, (2) Shanti Conductors Private Limited and another, 2012 Indlaw SC 405, set aside judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether provisions of the Act are retroactive in nature and non-consideration of such aspect of matter renders the decisions of SC in M/S. Modern Industries v. M/S. Steel Authority Of India Limited And Others, 2010 Indlaw SC 268 and Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405, as sub silentio.

Held, s. 3 of the 1993 Act provides that liability of buyer to make payment accrues after the supplier supplies goods or renders any services to buyer. The date of entering into the agreement or the date of supply order were not in contemplation of the legislature. Therefore, it is clear from a bare reading of s. 3 of the 1993 Act that for the purpose of the 1993 Act it does not matter when the contract was entered into, as long as the supply of the goods was after the 1993 Act came into force on 23-09-1992. Further, even on the issue of retrospectivity, what was required to be examined by SC was whether by reading the relevant statutory provisions ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of the 1993 Act, a vested statutory right is conferred. The aforesaid provisions of the 1993 Act are retroactive in nature therefore, non-consideration of such aspect in Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405, and cases mentioned therein, renders the said judgment sub silentio on that question. It is in that sense that the question of retrospective application of the 1993 Act does not arise at all. The 1993 Act has been enacted for the benefit of small scale and ancillary industries at large, decision in case of Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405, does not correctly lay down the position of law with respect to the nature of the 1993 Act and its effect on its prospectivity as well. In SC view, Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405, and other decisions of SC referred did not consider the important aspect of the matter namely as to whether the provisions of the 1993 Act are retroactive or not. They merely held that the provisions of the 1993 Act have no retrospective effect. Thus, judgments have been rendered sub silentio on such aspect. Therefore, question referred is answered in favour of appellants. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Delayed Payment to Small Scale Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Ordinance, 1992 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, ss. 1, 10, 2, 2(b), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 25 of the Schedule, s. 19 - Suit for recovery - Barred by limitation - Legality - Whether the judgment rendered in Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405, operates as res judicata in instant case and suit filed by appellants is barred by limitation.

Held, SC was only concerned with maintainability of a suit with regard to interest on the basis of statutory provisions of the 1993 Act, in relation to those agreements which had been entered into prior to coming into force of the 1993 Act. The issue of whether an appellant is entitled to prefer a claim on the interest as provided u/s. 4 of the 1993 Act was not the issue decided in Purbanchal Cables & Conductors Pvt. Ltd., 2012 Indlaw SC 405. Therefore, the decision in the same cannot be said to operate as res judicata taking into consideration the supply order against actual supply of goods with payment made, the last payment was made on 05-03-1994. Thus, time began to run from that date. Taking into consideration the fact that the date of institution of suit is 10-01-1997, the suit has been filed within the period of limitation as prescribed in the 1963 Act. Though on such aspect of matter no finding has been recorded either by Trial Court or HC, SC answer the question in favour of appellants. Further, question of maintainability of appeal filed by appellants questioning the correctness of the judgment and order passed in the review petition, SC hold the same to be maintainable in law. Appellants were directed to pay an amount of Rs.38,70,000/- back to respondents. Respondents shall refund the amount to appellants with 9% interest p.a. within 6 weeks. Appeals disposed of.

Arun Mishra, J.

(C) Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 34 - Delayed Payment to Small Scale Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Ordinance, 1992 - Interest on Delayed Payments to Small Scale and Ancillary Industrial Undertakings Act, 1993, ss. 1, 10, 2, 2(b), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 25 of the Schedule, s. 19 - Delayed payments - Retrospective in operation - Effect to - Whether SC was inclined to give any order in favour of appellant.

Held, taking note of various provisions of the 1993 Act, it is apparent that the 1993 Act is not retrospective in operation. SC has clearly laid down that when payments are not made in terms of s. 3 of the 1993 Act, s. 4 of the 1993 Act would apply. The 1993 Act came into force w.e.f. 23-9-1992 and would not apply to transactions which took place prior to that date and interest at the rate of 23 per cent was disallowed on the transactions 'entered' into between June, 1991 and 23-9-1992. Further, the 1993 Act cannot be said to be retrospective in operation or having retroactive operation. The question stands answered affirmatively beyond pale of doubt and decisions are binding on a Co-ordinate Bench. It cannot be said that decisions are sub silentio or per incuriam in any manner whatsoever and it is not open to Co-ordinate Bench to take a different opinion. There is no confusion with respect to meaning of transaction, supply order and agreement. SC while deciding aforesaid cases was not in oblivion of aims and objects of beneficial legislation, considered same and it has affirmatively pronounced on all the aspects. However, considering the scheme of the 1993 Act, various provisions of the 1993 Act it cannot be said to have retrospective operation or retroactive operation and where a supply order has been placed before the date of commencement of the 1993 Act, that is before 23-9-1992, the beneficial provisions of the 1993 Act regarding higher interest would not be applicable. HC has rightly followed the decisions in Purbanchal Cables & Conductors, 2012 Indlaw SC 405, and finding recorded by SC in remand order is final and binding on appellant. Appeals disposed of.
