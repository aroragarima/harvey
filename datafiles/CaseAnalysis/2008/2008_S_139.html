Sardar Jogendra Singh (D) By LRs. v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2514; (2008) 17 SCC 133

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, ss. 28, 23(1A) - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Market value - Increasing rate of interest - Excising market value - Certain lands in village were acquired for scheme, on outskirts and preliminary notification u/s. 28 of 1965 Act was issued and final declaration was issued - Land Acquisition Officer by award made offer of Rs.1.88 per sq.ft. as compensation - Tribunal to which claims of land owners for higher compensation was referred, determined market value as Rs. 3 per sq.ft - Tribunal determined market value as Rs.3 per sq.ft and allowed reference holding that claimants were entitled to compensation and interest on unpaid compensation amount - Parishad and Claimants filed appeals - HC disposed of said appeals by common judgment affirming judgment of Tribunal in regard to market value and increased rate of interest payable from date of expiry of one year from date of taken possession - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether said market value was excessive or low - Held, it was showed that lands covered by second award were clearly superior or situated in more advantaged position however compared to lands covered by award - Tribunal and HC held that acquired lands were similar to lands considered in first award (Ex.C-141) and therefore, they accepted award first and rejected award second (Ex.C-146) - However, claimants wanted to ignore award first and preferred award second, they must demonstrate with reference to oral or documentary evidence, that their lands were on par with lands considered in award second, and that lands considered in award first were inferior to their lands - There was no such evidence - Claimants merely chose to produce both awards - Fact that claimants themselves had produced and relied on first award apart from second award showed that they were satisfied if market value under first award was adopted as basis - However, on consideration of evidence, both Tribunal and HC have chosen to rely on first award, thus, no reason to interfere with such decision, in absence of special circumstances or evidence to choose second award as basis - Other evidence relied on by claimants did not establish market value of Rs.5/- per sq.ft - Therefore, there was no reason to increase market value - Appeal dismissed.
