Narayanlal Bansilal Pittie v Tarabai Motilal (Dead) By L. Rs

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 422; (1970) 3 SCC 293; 1971 UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Hyderabad Limitation Act, s. 13 - States Reorganization Act, 1956 - Whether partnership agreement was extended by mutual agreement? - Held, we are of the view that the High Court was in error in holding that the settlement alleged between Tarabai and Pittie in November, 1927, in which Pittie agreed to continue to remain a partner in the business of the Jalna firm to the extent to which it related to the business in cotton, cotton-seed and cotton bales and that he agreed to pay in November, 1930, Rs. 93,82,746/- is not proved - Decree passed by HC is set aside and the decree passed by the Trial Court is restored - Tarabai failed to prove that the partnership agreement was by mutual consent extended for a period of three years after the first two years - Appeal allowed.

Legislation Cited

Indian Limitation Act, 1908

States Reorganisation Act, 1956
