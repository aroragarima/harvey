Uniply Industries Limited v Unicorn Plywood Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19952; (2001) 5 SCC 95; AIR 2001 SC 2083; 2002 (47) ALR 102; 2001 (2) CLC 760; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 218; 2001 (21) PTC 417; 2001 (2) RAJ 31; 2001(3) SCALE 642; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 307; 2001 (3) SLT 786; 2001 (3) Supreme 605

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Intellectual Property

Summary: Corporate - Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act,1958, s. 12(3) - - Disputes regarding trade mark - Validity - Counter suits by appellant and respondent against each other - Ex-parte injunction was granted to appellants and denied to respondents - Appellants were into plywood business since 1996 and respondent registered its plywood unit in 1993 - Both of them were claiming rights on brand name of plywood - Trial Court dismissed suit of appellant following principle of prior user holding that respondent was using this brand name since 1993 and appellant could not claim it as had started his business only in 1966 - Appellant preferred appeal to HC and was dismissed by DB accepting finding of trial court - Hence instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, it was true that respondents issued advertisement of their plywood with disputed brand name in 1993 itself but whether it had started its production in 1993 or not was not discussed by Trial Court -- HC found declarations made before excise authorities were dubious - Trial Court should have noted above discrepancies and should have been very cautious in granting injunction which would affect trade and business of another person using identical trade mark - As both appellant and respondents had applied for registration of their respective trade marks before Registrar under the 1958 Act it would be proper for Register to investigate their rights and claims while granting registration - There were many precedents to show that ownership was governed by priority of usage of such marks for inherently distinctive marks - First user in sale of goods or service would be owner and senior user - These marks were given legal protection against infringement immediately upon adoption and use in trade if two companies make use of same trade mark and gist of passing off in relation to goodwill and reputation to goods - Courts below had tried to decide matter on prima facie issue without considering various other aspects - Judgment of HC was set aside - Matter remitted back to Registrar to decide rights of parties - Appeal disposed of.
