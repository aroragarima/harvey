Joseph Salvaraj A v State Of Gujarat & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 404; (2011) 7 SCC 59; AIR 2011 SC 2258; JT 2011 (7) SC 53; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 918; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 632; 2011(6) SCALE 731; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Tort - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406, 420 and 506(1) - Complainant lodged an FIR complaining that the appellant had committed offences u/ss. 406, 420 and 506(1) of IPC - Complainant submitted that he had got in touch with the appellant so as to extend the benefit of appellant's Channel 'GOD TV' to residents in Ahmedabad - For the said purposes, complainant had met 'X', the owner of Siti Cable and negotiated a settlement for a sum of Rs. 10 lacs on behalf of the appellant's company as fee to be paid to 'X' by appellant for telecast of channel 'God TV' in Ahmedabad - Complainant further submitted that despite the telecast of 'GOD TV', the appellant, as promised, failed to pay a sum of Rs. 10 lacs to 'X' - Appellant filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before HC praying for quashing of the FIR lodged by the complainant - Single Judge dismissed the petition filed by the appellant - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether any of the ingredients u/ss. 406, 420 or 506 (1) of the IPC have been made out to enable the Court to take cognizance thereof against the appellant or not? - Held, from the general conspectus of the various sections under which the appellant was being charged and was to be prosecuted would show that the same were not made out even prima facie from the complainant's FIR - The matter appears to be purely civil in nature as there appears to be no cheating or a dishonest inducement for the delivery of property or breach of trust by the appellant - The impugned FIR was an abuse of process of law as the purely civil dispute, was sought to be given a colour of a criminal offence to wreak vengeance against the appellant - The FIR under the circumstances deserves to be quashed at the threshold - Appeal allowed.
