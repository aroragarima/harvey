Hussein Ghadially @ M.H.G.A Shaikh and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

18 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 459; (2014) 8 SCC 425; 2014 CRLJ 3984; 2015 (1) G.L.R. 559; JT 2014 (8) SC 391; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 734; 2014(8) SCALE 598; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fax, U.P. Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 307, 326, 325 and 324 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3 and 20 - Bomb blast - Riots - Conviction - Violation of mandatory provision - Challenged - Blast took place at various locations - Blasts that took place were, according to the prosecution, the culmination of the conspiracy hatched by the accused and the efforts made by them including their active participation in the sordid sequence leading up to grievous injuries to several persons including the killing of child - Accused persons were arrested - Arrest of accused persons and the seizure of arms and ammunition in turn led to invocation of provisions of 1987, Act by orders passed by the Additional Commissioner of Police - Chargesheet in both FIRs were filed before the Designated Court - Trial Court found some of the accused persons (appellants) guilty while some others were acquitted giving them the benefit of doubt - Appellants were convicted u/s. s. 3(2)(ii) of 1987, Act r/w s. 120B of IPC, s. 5 of 1987, Act, ss. 307, 326, 325 and 324 r/w s. 120B of IPC, ss. 3,4,5 of 1908, Act and s. 25(1) A of the 1959, Act - Hence, instant appeals - Whether conviction of the appellants for offences with which they were charged was vitiated for breach of the mandatory provisions of s. 20-A(1) of 1987, Act -

Held, provision start with a non obstante clause and was couched in negative phraseology - It forbid recording of information about the commission of offences under 1987, Act by the Police without the prior approval of the District Superintendent of Police - That being so, no one except the authority so designated, could exercise that power - In the instant case, requirement of a mandatory statutory provision having been violated, the trial and conviction of the petitioners for offences under the 1987, Act should be held to have been vitiated on that account - Further, recovery of the weapons was not satisfactorily proved by cogent and reliable evidence - Conviction of the appellants could not be sustained and was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
