Ram Jivan v Smt. Phoola (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 408; (1976) 1 SCC 852; AIR 1976 SC 844; 1976 (2) ALR 262; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Statutory tenant - Ejectment -Possession - Preferential heir - H died leaving property behind three sons, G, A and R - A died issueless but R died leaving widow/M and daughter P/respondent No. 1 - G died leaving son J/Appellant - M/tenant was to continue in occupation of lands obtained status of statutory tenant with effect of ss. 3(18), 36 of 1886 Act - Court of Wards claimed tenancy from M, that property had escheated to State - Revenue Courts declared M had acquired independent rights of statutory tenant and was, not liable to be ejected - M acquired status of hereditary tenant, U/s.29(a) of Tenancy Act and status of Bhumidhar under s.18(1) of Abolition Act - M died and P as legal heir filed suit u/s. 209 of Abolition Act for ejectment of J and P filed application to Consolidation officer - Consolidation officer struck off name of J and directed P's name to be mutated in respect of property - J appealed to Settlement officer was dismissed - Aggrieved J appealed to District Deputy Director of Consolidation was dismissed - J filed Revision was dismissed by Joint Director of Consolidation - J filed W.P was allowed by HC - P filed special appeal to Division Bench of HC was allowed - Hence instant S.L.P - Whether J entitled to possession of property - Held, division Bench of HC had applied erroneous view of law - M inherited property from her husband and erred in law in by not applying provisions of s. 172 of Abolition Act - J being her husband's brother's son was entitled to succeed to estate in preference to P - There could be no doubt that M's occupation of tenancy on death of R was by way of inheritance - It was true that interest of M was very limited one and she could had ejected by landlord - Hence, J was preferential heir according to order of succession u/s. 171 of Abolition Act - Appeal allowed.
