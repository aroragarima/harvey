Uttar Pradesh Power Corporation Limited v National Thermal Power Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2169; (2009) 6 SCC 235; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1931; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 139; JT 2009 (3) SC 462; 2009(3) SCALE 620; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1060; 2009 (2) UPLBEC 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (Terms & Conditions of Tariff) Regulations, 2001, U.P. Electricity Regulatory Commission Act, 1998

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (Conduct of Business) Regulations, 1999, regns, 92, 94, 110 to 117 - Electricity Regulation Commissions Act, 1998 - Tariff - Revision by Central Regulatory Commission (CRC) - Jurisdiction - Government of India issued guidelines for revision of salary to the employees of the Public Sector Undertakings with effect from 1-7-1997 whereby a High Powered Committee was constituted - Appellant/Corporation revised pay scales of its employees having accepted the recommendations of High Powered Committee with effect from 1-1-1997 but was implemented during the period 2000-2001 - CRC made a tariff order for appellant/Corporation covering the revised cost of pay to employees, which was to be paid by the 1st respondent/Corporation for the purpose of implementation of the recommendations of High Powered Committee - CRC in exercise of its suo motu jurisdiction passed an order to inquire into the actual escalation factor which was found to be less than 6% - 1st respondent/Corporation, subsequently, filed a review petition before the CRC for revising the tariff - CRC dismissed 1st respondent's review petition - 1st respondent filed appeal before the Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal allowed 1st respondent's appeal directing amounts of arrears paid by the appellant in the year 2000-2001 on account of employees cost, incurred in the respective years, be considered in the tariff fixation for re-imbursement to 1st respondent/Corporation - Hence the present appeal by appellant/Corporation - (A) Whether the CRC has the jurisdiction to entertain or carry out revision in the tariff order on the basis of purported subsequent events or otherwise - Held, CRC has the exclusive jurisdiction to frame not only tariff but also any amendment, alterations and additions in regard thereto - CRC in terms of the 1998 Act as also the Regulations framed thereunder exercise diverse powers - CRC exercises legislative power, power of enforcement of the Regulations as also the adjudicatory power - Each of CRC's functions although are separate and distinct but may be overlapping, is extensive - Further, regn. 92 of the 1999 Regulations provides for revision of tariff and regns. 110 to 117 also provide for extensive power to be exercised by the CRC in regard to the proceedings before it - Having regard to the nature of jurisdiction of the CRC even principles of res judicata will have no application - CRC has a plenary power and its inherent jurisdiction is saved - Having regard to the diverse nature of jurisdiction, CRC may for one purpose entertain an application so as to correct its own mistake but in relation to another function its jurisdiction may be limited - Provisions of the 1998 Act do not put any restriction on the CRC in the matter of exercise of such a jurisdiction and it is empowered to lay down its own procedure - Further, regns. 92 and 94, do not restrict the power of the CRC to make additions or alterations in the tariff - Making of a tariff is a continuous process and it can be amended or altered by the CRC, if any occasion arises thereof - Said power of CRC can be exercised not only on an application filed by the generating companies but by the Commission also on its own motion - While the CRC exercises a suo motu jurisdiction, the period of limitation prescribed in regn. 103 shall not apply - Therefore, CRC has sufficient jurisdiction to revised the tariff once fixed - (B) Whether the order of revision of tariff made by the CRC in the instant case is reasonable - Held, statute does not provide for the manner in which a petition is to be filed before the CRC or the manner in which the tariff order is to be passed or revision or non-revision thereof, however, while exercising such jurisdiction the CRC must act within a reasonable time - For the purpose of making tariff the actual costs required for payment to the employees being a part of the operation and maintenance cost, which were to be paid by way of extra amount, could fall for determination by the CRC but, such an application ordinarily could have been filed within the period during which the tariff order was in force - Thus, 1st respondent/Corporation was not justified as the CRC should not have been asked to revisit the tariff after five years and when everybody had arranged its affairs - (C) Whether the Appellate Tribunal has the jurisdiction to interfere with the order of CRC - Held, jurisdiction of the appellate tribunal is wide - Appellate Tribunal is also an expert tribunal and, thus, it can interfere with the finding of the CRC both on fact as also on law - Appeals allowed.
