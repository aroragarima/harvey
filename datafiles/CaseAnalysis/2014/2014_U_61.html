 Pramati Educational and Cultural Trust ® and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra, R.M. Lodha, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 333; (2014) 8 SCC 1; AIR 2014 SC 2114; 2014 (5) AWC 5048; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 496; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 145; JT 2014 (7) SC 46; 2015 (2) KarLJ 70; 2014 (2) KLT 547; 2014 (4) MLJ 486; 2014 (3) RLW 2007; 2014(7) SCALE 306; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 712; 2014 (5) SLR 139; 2014 (3) UPLBEC 1775

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Scheduled Castes, Constitution (Ninety Third Amendment) Act, 2005, Constitution (Eighty-third Amendment) Bill, 1997, Right of Children to Free and Compulsory Education (Amendment) Act, 2012

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution (Eighty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 2002, art. 21A - Constitution (Ninety-third Amendment) Act, 2005, art. 15 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Constitutional validity of cl. (5) of arts. 15 and 21A of Constitution - Minority Education Institution - (A) Whether by inserting cl. (5) in art. 15 of the Constitution by the 2005 Act, Parliament had altered the basic structure or framework of the Constitution -

Held, object of cl. (5) of art. 15 of Constitution was to enable the State to give equal opportunity to socially and educationally backward classes of citizens or to the Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes to study in all educational institutions other than minority educational institutions referred in cl. (1) of art. 30 of the Constitution - None of the rights u/art. 14, 19(1)(g) and 21 of the Constitution have been abrogated by cl. (5) of art. 15 of the Constitution and the view taken in Ashoka Kumar Thakur v. Union of India 2008 Indlaw SC 596 that the imposition of reservation on unaided institutions by the Ninety-third Amendment had abrogated art. 19(1) (g) of Constitution, a basic feature of the Constitution was not correct - 2005 Act of the Constitution inserting cl. (5) of art. 15 of the Constitution was valid - (B) Whether by inserting art 21A of the Constitution by the 2002 Act, Parliament had altered the basic structure or framework of the Constitution -

Held, power u/art. 21A of the Constitution vesting in the State could not extend to making any law which would abrogate the right of the minorities to establish and administer schools of their choice - 2002 Act inserting art. 21A of the Constitution does not alter the basic structure or framework of the Constitution and were Constitutionally valid - Hence, 2009 Act was not ultra vires art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution - 2009 Act insofar as it applies to minority schools, aided or unaided, covered under cl. (1) of art. 30 of the Constitution was ultra vires the Constitution - Petitions disposed of.
