Dayaram Asanand Gursahani v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 335; (1984) 3 SCC 36; (1984) SCC (L&S) 341; AIR 1984 SC 850; 1984 BomLR 206; 1984 LabIC 608; 1984 (1) LLN 643; 1984(1) SCALE 316; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 703; 1984 (1) SLJ 370; 1984 (1) SLR 602; 1984 UJ 386

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Bombay Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1956, r. 5(2)(1)(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 235 - Selection grade pay - Whether the appellant is entitled to get the salary and allowances in the selection grade scale or to be posted as an Inspecting Judge - High Court refused to sanction the selection grade pay scale to the appellant when it become due automatically on the ground that he was not found fit to be sanctioned that scale of pay - Held, is erroneous; HC cannot refuse to sanction it u/art. 235 of Constitution - Appeal partly allowed.
