Chandradhar Goswami and Others v Gauhati Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 1967 SC 1058; [1967] 37 Comp Cas 108; 1967 (1) CompLJ 98; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 898

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s.34 ; Bankers' Books Evidence Act, 1891, s.4 ; Limitation Act, 1908, Arts. 85, 1 Bankers' Books Evidence Act .s.4 - Evidence u/s.34 of 1872 Act - Bank - Entry in bank's books of account - Account witth the bank - Last payment in November of the relevant year - Subsequent entries showing only interest due to bank - (A) Whether mere entries from bank's books of account or mere copies thereof are sufficient evidence to charge person with liability - (B) Determination of commencement of period of limitation and mutuality - Held, not sufficient to charge person with liability except where person concerned accepts correctness of entries - Held mutuality had come to an end in 1949 and limitation ran from end of 1949 - Appeal allowed
