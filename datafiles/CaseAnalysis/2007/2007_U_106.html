Aashirwad Films v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 594; (2007) 6 SCC 624; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 1; 2007 (3) CTC 596; JT 2007 (8) SC 398; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 170; 2007(8) SCALE 288; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Entertainment Tax Act, 1939, Kerala Land Tax Act

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Entertainment Tax Act, 1939 - Constitutionality of a notification issued by the Government of the Andhra Pradesh levying different rates of entertainment tax - Differentiation on the basis of language - Held, this is definitely derisive of social attributes of the polity and Article 14 in its basic form i.e. equality before law - If any classification seeks to take refuge of exception under reasonable differentia category under art. 14, it must stay clear of the broad constitutional mandate - Classification solely on the basis of language, fails in its initiative to be called reasonable and thus is arbitrary and as such violative of art. 14 of the Constitution of India - Impugned levy cannot be sustained being discriminatory in nature - Petition allowed.
