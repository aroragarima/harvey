Subhash Chand v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 92; (2002) SCC (Cr) 256; (2002) 1 SCC 702; AIR 2001 SCW 4209; 2001 (4) CCR 254; 2001 (4) Crimes 315; 2001 (91) FLR 761; 2001 (2) JCC 332; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 67; JT 2001 (8) SC 505; 2001 LabIC 3715; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 496; 2001(7) SCALE 246; 2001 (4) SCJ 51; 2002 (1) SLJ 110; 2001 (7) SLT 325; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 163; 2001 (7) Supreme 585

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963

Summary: Each of the pieces of incriminating circumstantial evidence must be carefully scrutinized so as to find out if each one of the circumstantial evidence is proved individually and collectively in forging such a chain of incriminating circumstances as would fasten the guilt on the accused beyond any shadow of reasonable doubt. Where accused has not taken a plea of alibi the question of adverse inference against him, does not arise at all.
