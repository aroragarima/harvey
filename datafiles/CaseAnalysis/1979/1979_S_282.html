Dhanabal and Another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 169; (1980) 2 SCC 84; (1980) SCC (Cr) 340; 1980 (17) ACC 115; AIR 1980 SC 628; 1980 CRLJ 439; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, 164, 388 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.157, - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 302- Murder - Power of Attorney - Admission of Evidence - Conviction - Appellant No.2's mother in law cultivated in her own land, in spite of execution of power of attorney, in favor of appellant No. 2 and she was also leading a immoral life - As there was misunderstanding between parties, deceased/mother in law of appellant No.2 was murdered - Evidence was admitted by P.W. 1, 2, 3 and 5 u/s. 288 of Code - Appellant No. 1 was found guilty u/s. 302 of Code and Appellant No. 2 and 3 were found guilty of an offence u/s. 302 r/w. 149 of Code and sentenced to imprisonment for life - On appeal to HC, Appellant No. 3 was acquitted, as he had not instigated appellant No.1, u/s. 164 of Code, but Appellants No.1 and 2 were convicted - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction of appellants were maintainable - Held, appellant No. 2 instigated appellant No. 1 and caused fatal injury to deceased - Hence, appellant No. 2 was entitled to benefit of doubt - His appeal was allowed and his conviction and sentence were set aside - HC was right that s. 157 of the Act makes it clear that the statement recorded u/s. 164 of the Code could be relied for corroborating statements made by witnesses in the Committal Court - HC was right in relying on statement of witnesses under S. 164 as corroborating their subsequent evidence before Committal Court - Appeal of appellant No. 1 was rejected and his conviction and sentence confirmed - Appellant No. 3 was allowed and his conviction and sentence set aside - Appeals disposed of.
