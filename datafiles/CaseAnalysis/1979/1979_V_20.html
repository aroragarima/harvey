State of Maharashtra v Vishwanath Tukaram Umale and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 550; (1979) 4 SCC 23; (1979) SCC (Cr) 893; AIR 1979 SC 1825; 1979 CRLJ 1193; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 190; 1979 UJ 584

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Framing of Charge - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 379, 461 and 34 - Railway Property (Unlawful Possession) Act, 1966, s. 3 - Whether High Court justified in taking view that it was sufficient of frame charges u/ss. 379/34 and 461/34 IPC, 1860 against accused persons as in its view allegation against them did not attract application of s. 3 of Act? - It is true that gravamen of offence u/s. 3 of Act is possession of property, but it need not necessarily be a subsisting possession, and it is sufficient if accused was proved to have been in possession of that property at any point of time - Essential requirements of s. therefore are that (i) property in question should be railway property, (ii) it should reasonably be suspected of having been stolen or unlawfully obtained, and (iii) it should be found or proved that the accused was or had been in possession of that property - Held, allegation against accused was to effect that they had been in possession of railway property in question, and that was sufficient to attract application of s. 3 of Act - HC erred in taking view that it was necessary, for purpose of bringing a case under that section, to prove that accused were found to be in possession of railway property at time of its seizure, and that it would not be attracted in case of an allegation that railway property was subject-matter of dacoity or theft by accused - Trial Court directed to frame a charge u/s. 3 of Act against accused 1, 2, 5 and absconding accused in addition to charge u/ss. 379/34 and 461/34 IPC, 1860 - Appeal allowed.
