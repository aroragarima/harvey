P. P. Beeran v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20902; (2001) 9 SCC 571; AIR 2001 SC 2420; 2001 (2) CCR 38; 2001 (3) Crimes 171; 2001 CRLJ 3281; 2001 (1) JCC 202; JT 2001 (4) SC 184; 2001 (2) SLT 779; 2001 (4) Supreme 446

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 27 - Whether appellant be afforded an opportunity to adopt a defence u/s. 27? - S. 27 of NDPS, Act, 1985, says that whoever is in possession of any narcotic drug or psychotropic substance of 'a small quantity' which is proved to have been intended for his personal consumption and not for sale or distribution, offence would run down to a much lesser rigorous one as court would then be in a position to consider awarding imprisonment as sentence for a term which may extend to one year or with fine or with both - Held, in a case where Parliament has afforded very substantial benefit to a person who possesses only a small quantity of narcotic drug for his personal consumption,in interest of justice, that appellant should be given one more opportunity at trial court to advance a pleading on strength of s. 27 of Act - Appeal disposed of.
