Ajit Singh and Others (Ii) v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 725; (1999) 7 SCC 209; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1239; AIR 1999 SC 3471; AIR 1999 SCW 3460; JT 1999 (7) SC 153; 2000(1) M.P.H.T. 1; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 521; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 195

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion, Roster Points, Punjab Secretariat Class Iii Service Rules, 1976

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, Arts.14 and 16(1) - Seniority - Promotion - Roster points - Matter concerned dispute related to seniority of reserved candidates and general candidates - Reserved candidates got promotion at roster points not entitled to claim seniority at promotional level against senior general candidates promoted at later point of time to same level - Seniority at promotional level decided by applying provisions of Arts.14 and 16(1) of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether roster point promotees entitled to count their seniority in promoted category from date of their continuous officiation vis-a-vis general candidates senior to them in lower category - Held, roster point promotees (reserved category) cannot count their seniority in promoted category from date of their continuous officiation in promoted post vis-a-vis general candidates senior to them in lower category and who were later promoted - If senior general candidate at lower level reached promotional level later but before further promotion of reserved candidate, he would have to be treated as senior at promotional level to reserved candidate even if reserved candidate was earlier promoted to that level - Virpal and Ajit Singh have correctly decided and Jagdishlal was not correctly decided - Seniority at level 4 refixed on basis of when reserved candidate at level 3 would got his normal promotion treated him as junior to senior general candidate at level 3 - Any promotions made wrongly in excess of any quota were to be treated as ad hoc - Order accordingly.
