District Magistrate, Haridwar and another v Harish Malhotra

Supreme Court of India

09 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 932; 2015 (2) AWC 2128; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 430; 2014(13) SCALE 683

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Cable Television Networks (Regulation) Act, 1995, Cinematograph Act, 1952, Uttar Pradesh Reorganisation Act, 2000, Uttar Pradesh Cinemas (Regulation) Act, 1955, Uttar Pradesh Entertainments And Betting Tax Act, 1979, Uttar Pradesh Cinemas (Regulation of Exhibition by means of Video) Rules, 1988

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Administrative - Uttar Pradesh Cinemas (Regulation) Act, 1955 - Uttar Pradesh Cinemas (Regulation of Exhibition by means of Video) Rules, 1988, r. 17(2) - Uttar Pradesh (Entertainment and Betting) Tax Act, 1979 - Licence fee - Exhibition by means of Video - In pursuance of directions of HC, respondent-cable operator applied for license under 1955 Act and District Magistrate, after considering application, directed respondent-cable operator to pay Rs.11,88,500 as licence fee u/r. 17(2) of the Rules - However, respondent-cable operator complied with payment of licence fee u/r. 17(1) of 1988 Rules by paying Rs.2,400 but refused to pay licence fee of Rs.11,88,500 u/r. 17(2) - HC quashed part of order of District Magistrate dt.30-4-2010 whereby licence fee of Rs.11,88,500 was demanded from respondent u/r. 17(2) - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether respondent-cable operator is liable to pay licence fee u/r. 17(2) of the Rules.

Held, notification dt.17-8-2005 would not apply to respondent-cable operator as it seeks to tax 'Exhibition by means of Video' whereas respondent's activities are not covered by said expression. Further, even though said notification is issued under 1979 Act, yet, for definition of said expression, 1955 Act has to be referred. The provisions of 1955 Act do not apply to respondent and hence, said expression within meaning of 2(aa) of Act is not applicable to respondent and thus, r. 17 is also not applicable to respondent. As per plain reading of s. 2(aa) of the Act, section would not apply to respondent who is a cable operator telecasting video channels 'to the public' and not 'in public'. Hence, from plain reading of relevant provisions it is clear that the same are not applicable to respondent and hence demand as well as recovery certificate dt.6-8-2011 issued u/r.17(2) of the Rules are bad in law. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is upheld. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Expression 'Exhibition by means of Video' is in Pari Materia, and so, definition of said term under one Act, could be used to interpret provisions of rules under other Act. Further, as per principles of statutory interpretation, plain meaning should be given to words contained in statute.
