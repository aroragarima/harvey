Rambhau and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20151; (2001) 4 SCC 759; (2001) SCC (Cr) 812; AIR 2001 SC 2120; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 638; 2001 (2) CCR 271; 2001 CRLJ 2343; JT 2001 (5) SC 121; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 721; 2001(3) SCALE 539; 2001 (4) SCJ 561; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 210; 2001 (4) SLT 316; 2001 (3) Supreme 544

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 313 and 391 - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - High Court reversed finding of acquittal and convicted for offence punishable u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) - Appeal - Whether rectification of irregularity by an additional evidence goes to root of issue and instead of subserving ends of justice, same runs counter to concept of justice? - Additional evidence cannot and ought not to be received in such a way so as to cause any prejudice to accused - It is not a disguise for a retrial or to change nature of case against accused - Irregularity covers any case where a thing has not been done in manner laid down by statute, irrespective of what that manner might be - Powers being an exception shall always have to be exercised with caution and circumspection so as to meet ends of justice - HC thought it fit to conduct an additional examination of both accused persons - Held, HC on basis of relevant evidence on record held that prosecution has fully established demand by accused 1 on 23-01-1989 - It is demand of 24th January which was said to have not been put to accused but factum of demand on an earlier day stands proved and concluded together with seizure of tainted notes on 24th January, completes offence, as such omission to put to accused, demand on 24th cannot be said to be of such a nature which would go to root of matter - It is not a defect incurable in nature but a mere irregularity which HC thought it fit to cure - Irregularity has been cured - Prosecution has clearly established that accused 1 is a public servant and in discharge of his official duties made a demand of illegal gratification - Accused 2 also has played a very significant role and substantially abetted crime - Accused persons are guilty of offence for which they were charged - Appeal dismissed.
