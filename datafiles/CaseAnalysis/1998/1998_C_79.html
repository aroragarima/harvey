Calcutta Chromotype Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1610; (1998) 3 SCC 681; AIR 1998 SC 1631; 1998 (76) ECR 385; 1998 (99) E.L.T. 202; JT 1998 (2) SC 747; 1998 (25) RLT 866; 1998(2) SCALE 576; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 570; 1998 (4) SLT 120; 1998 (3) Supreme 403

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Companies Act, 1956 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4(4)(c) - Whether the Appellate Tribunal rightly observed that in view of s. 4(4)(c) of the Act that if a person is so associated with the assessee that they have interest in the business of each other then the person was a related a person of the other within the meaning of the section? - Held, the authorities and the Appellate Tribunal did address themselves to the basic question as to the shareholdings of both the assessee and the buyer, inasmuch as they found that the Managing Director of both the companies was the same and one more Director was common - The shares of both the companies were held by the members of the Sharma family but that is quite a vague expression - The Appellate Tribunal was partly right in giving the direction to ascertain the break-up of the shares of each member of the family in the two companies - No presumption of such mutuality of interest in the business of each other could have been drawn without the factual data - Held, the matter pertains to the year 1976 - Order of the Assistant Collector is of the year 1978 - At this late stage any purpose will be served to inquire into the shareholdings of the assessee, the appellant and its sole distributor as directed by the Appellate Tribunal - Therefore, inclined to hold that no effect be given to the judgment of the Appellate Tribunal - Appeals allowed.
