Onkarlal Nandlal v State of Rajasthan and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 148; (1985) 4 SCC 404; (1986) SCC (Tax) 34; AIR 1986 SC 2146; 1985(2) SCALE 1217; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 1075; 1985 (60) STC 314; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 1075

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954, s. 10, 2(t), 2(p), 26, 2(o), Expln.II and 2(s), cl. (iv), Proviso 2 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3 and 4 - (A) Case - Grant of Special leave - Direct appeal to Supreme Court against order of Commercial Tax Officer - Ordinarily, not entertainable - Exception - (B) Construction of expression 'resale within state' in Form No. ST 17 - Effect of incorporation by reference - Purchase of poppy seeds against Declaration in Form No. ST 17 for purpose of sale within state - At time of resale, goods were specific ascertained and lying at a Mandi inside state - Fact that resales were in course of inter-State trade or Commerce - Held, can be entertained when appeal to High Court would be futile as it has already taken particular view - Resales must be deemed to have taken place inside state and fact immaterial - Appeal allowed.
