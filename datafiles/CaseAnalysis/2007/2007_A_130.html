Rahul Builders v Arihant Fertilizers and Chemical and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1385; (2008) 2 SCC 231; (2008) 2 SCC 321; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3564; 2009 (4) BC 218; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 572; 2008 (1) CGLJ 470; [2007] 140 Comp Cas 600; 2008 (2) CompLJ 83; 2008 CRLJ 452; 2007 (5) CTC 876; JT 2007 (12) SC 495; 2007 (4) KLT 977; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 973; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 951; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 59

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonoured Cheque

Summary: Criminal - Private complaint - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s.138 Proviso - Whether ongoing criminal proceeding under the Act should be quashed on failure of complainant to serve a proper notice strictly in terms of s. 138 proviso? - When the statute prescribes for service of notice specifying a particular period, it should be expressly stated - In the absence of any such stipulation, it is difficult to hold that 15 days' notice was thereby contemplated - Operation of s. 138 of the Act is limited by the proviso - When the proviso applies, the main section would not - Statute envisages application of the penal provisions - An omnibus notice without specifying as to what was the amount due under the dishonoured cheque would not subserve the requirement of law - In the instant case no demand was made for payment of the cheque amount after cheque was returned by the bank, instead demand was made with respect to entire sum - Held, notice to be bad - Impugned judgment not liable to be interfered with - Appeal dismissed.
