Union of India and Others v Motion Picture Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, K. Venkataswami, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 584; (1999) 6 SCC 150; AIR 1999 SC 2334; JT 1999 (4) SC 520; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; International; Media & Entertainment; Tort

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Sovereignty And Integrity Of India, European Convention On Human Rights

Summary: Constitution - Media & Entertainment - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1), 19(1)(g) and 265 - Delhi Cinematograph Rules, 1981, Conditions 15 and 22 - (A) Freedom of speech - Compelled speech or must carry provision in statute - Whether would infringe freedom of speech of exhibitor of cinema? - (B) Freedom of trade - Cinema exhibitors - Compulsion to show scientific, educational and documentary films - Whether unreasonable restriction on freedom of trade? - (C) Restriction to exhibit approved films - Films Division charging one per cent of net recoveries for supply of approved films - Whether amounts to levy of tax u/art. 265 - (D) Requirement to exhibit films or lantern slides - Whether condition 15 wide or unrelated to object? - Unreasonable restriction - Whether condition 15 redundant since it is covered by Condition 22? - Held, (A) the requirement under 'must carry' provisions in various cinematograph legislations permitted u/art. 19(2) - (B) the expenses required to be incurred by cinema exhibitors to show these films is not a high or unreasonable expense - Requiring a cinema theatre to show for a short duration of its programme, educational films cannot be considered as an unreasonable - (C) Payment is under an agreement and for services provided - It is in nature of fee - (D) Condition read in light of s. 12(4), Cinematograph Act will have a direct nexus with object sought to be achieved - Not unreasonable - Conditions 15 and 22 do not overlap, but refer to different sizes and types of short films, shorter films or lantern slides - Appeal allowed.
