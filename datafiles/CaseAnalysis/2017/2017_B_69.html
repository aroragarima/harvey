Secretary Mahatama Gandhi Mission and another v Bhartiya Kamgar Sena and others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 26; AIR 2017 SC 505; 2017(1) ALL MR 947; JT 2017 (1) SC 569; 2017(1) SCALE 325

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Keywords: Conditions Of Service, Educational Institutions, Review, Relief, Pay, Salary, Re-Employment, Employment, Rule, Terms And Conditions, Date, Examination, Examinations, Pay Commission, Posts, Service Conditions, Revision Of Pay, Cadre, Criteria, Grounds, Policy, Eligibility, Amendment, Relationship, Scheme, Allowances, All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, Pension Scheme, Classification, Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950, Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994, Liberalised Pension Scheme, Maharashtra Non-Agricultural Universities and Affiliated Colleges Standard Code (Terms and Conditions of Service of Non-Teaching Employees) Rules, 1984, Applicability, Universities, Writ Petition, Constitution of India, 1950, Right to, Training, Indian Universities Act, 1904, Maharashtra Non-Agricultural Universities and Affiliated Colleges Standard Code (Non Teaching Employees Revised Pay) Rules, 2009

Summary: (A) Education - Service - Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994, ss. 8(3), 14(8) - Maharashtra Non-Agricultural Universities and Affiliated Colleges Standard Code (Non-Teaching Employees Revised Pay) Rules, 2009, rr. 17, 2 - Non teaching staff - Payment of salaries - Denial - Respondent no. 1 and others (non-teaching members in the employment of appellant) filed writ petitions before HC seeking that respondent/authorities of State Govt. be directed to extend the benefits of revised pay scales as recommended by Fifth Pay Commission set up by Central Govt. to the employees of appellant - DB allowed writ petitions holding that relief could be granted only with reference to Sixth Pay Commission Report but not the other two Pay Commission Reports in view of laches and directed appellants that the pay scales suggested by Sixth Pay Commission for non-teaching staff be given - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants were obliged to pay the salaries and other allowances to its employees whether teaching staff or non-teaching staff in accordance with the pay structure recommended by Sixth Pay Commission.

Held, the Sixth Pay Commission appointed by UOI was only a body entrusted with the job of making an assessment of the need to revise the pay structure of employees of Central Govt. and to suggest appropriate measures for revision of pay structure. The recommendations of the pay commission were not binding on UOI, much less any other body. They were only meant for administrative guidance of UOI. UOI might accept or reject the recommendations either fully or partly, though it has never happened that the recommendations of the pay commission are completely rejected by Govt. so far. The fact that UOI accepted the recommendations of Sixth Pay Commission (for that matter any pay commission) does not either oblige States to follow the pattern of the revised pay structure adopted by UOI or create any right in favour of employees of State or other bodies falling within the legislative authority of State. UOI decided to extend the benefit of revised pay scales to the employees of various central universities etc. on the basis of the recommendations of University Grants Commission (UGC). While accepting the recommendations of UGC, UOI chose to extend the benefit of revised pay scales only to the teachers and other equivalent cadres of Library and Physical Education etc. in all the Central Universities and Colleges thereunder etc. UOI was not oblivious of the fact that various other categories of employees were working with those institutions. GR dt. 12-8-2009 could be safely construed to be one made in exercise of the power u/s. 8(3) of the Act conferring a legal right on the teaching staff of the affiliated colleges irrespective of the fact whether they were aided or not. The colleges run by the appellants were admittedly colleges affiliated to Universities functioning under the Act. Therefore, their teaching staff would be entitled to the revised pay scales in terms of GR dt. 12-8-2009. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994, ss. 8(3), 14(8) - Maharashtra Non-Agricultural Universities and Affiliated Colleges Standard Code (Non-Teaching Employees Revised Pay) Rules, 2009, rr. 17, 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 162, 21, 43 - Un-aided college - Pay commission - Statutory obligation - Whether appellant which was an unaided college cannot be compelled to pay the salary and other allowances to its employees in terms of recommendations of Sixth Pay Commission in the absence of any express statutory obligation to make such payment.

Held, GR dt. 12-8-2009 was specific in its declaration that the elaborate Rules contained therein dealing with the pay scales of various cadres of teaching staff of educational institutions mentioned, it does not make any distinction between aided and un-aided colleges. However, GR does not purport to be one made in exercise of the power u/s. 8(3) of the Act. Coming to the non-teaching staff working in the colleges run by appellant, the Rules purport to be the rules revising the pay-scales of non-teaching staff of only the affiliated aided colleges. Therefore, textually colleges administered by appellants were not governed by the Rules. Therefore, the object sought to be achieved by the Rules was required to be identified and that it was required to be examined whether the classification made bears any reasonable nexus to such object. The decision of State Govt. in issuing the two GRs revising the pay scales of teaching staff of all the educational institutions and non-teaching staff of aided educational institution was proof of such endorsement. Thus, SC see no justification in excluding non-teaching employees of the unaided educational institutions while extending the benefit of revised pay scales to the non-teaching employees of the aided educational institutions. Such a classification was violative of art. 14 of Constitution. Appeals dismissed.

(C) Service - Maharashtra Universities Act, 1994, ss. 8(3), 14(8) - Maharashtra Non-Agricultural Universities and Affiliated Colleges Standard Code (Non-Teaching Employees Revised Pay) Rules, 2009, rr. 17, 2 - Revised pay scale - Payment denial - Challenged - Whether since appellant does not receive any financial aid from State, calling upon appellants to pay its employees in terms of the revised pay scales would be compelling them to perform an impossible task as their only source of revenue was fee collected from students.

Held, s. 8(3) of the Act clearly authorises State Govt. to frame rules dealing with the service conditions of employees (both teaching and non-teaching) of various educational institutions. While exercising such power was that State drew an artificial distinction between aided and unaided educational institutions. Further, insofar non-teaching staff were concerned, appellants have no excuse for making such a submission because in the earlier round of litigation non-teaching employees of appellants though succeeded both before HC and SC in obtaining appropriate directions to appellant and other authorities to revise the pay scales of employees in tune with Fifth Pay commission, entered into a settlement dt. 30-1-2006, the terms of which have been taken note in instant judgment. Under the said agreement, the management agreed to revise the pay scales from time to time in tune with the revision of pay scales of the employees of State. Thus, it was for appellants to work out the remedies and find out the ways and means to meet the financial liability arising out of the obligation to pay the revised pay scales. Appeals dismissed.
