Messrs Quinn India Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 210; (2006) 9 SCC 559; 2006 (198) E.L.T. 326; JT 2006 (5) SC 614; 2006(5) SCALE 620; 2006 (6) SCJ 596; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ad Valorem, Excisability of goods

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 11A, s. 35L - Penetrator 4893 - Tribunal held that goods manufactured by assessee were not commercially and popularly known as service active agents and not covered under tariff item No 3402.90 - Appeal against - Held, onus or burden to show that product falls within a particular tariff item is always on Revenue - Revenue has failed to adduce contrary evidence in support of its claim that classification of penetrator manufactured by assessee is not covered under tariff item No 3402.90 - Appeal allowed.
