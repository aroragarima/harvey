Indian Oil Corporation Limited and Another v Ashok Kumar Arora

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, S.C. Sen, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3145; (1997) 3 SCC 72; (1997) SCC (L&S) 636; AIR 1997 SC 1030; 1997 (1) CLR 659; 1997 (2) CompLJ 15; 1997 (75) FLR 949; JT 1997 (2) SC 367; 1997 LabIC 1284; 1997 LLR 335; 1997(1) SCALE 775; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 980

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Reimbursement

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Departmental enquiry - Interference - Powers of High Court - Reimbursement of forged medical bills by employee - Disciplinary authority findings against employee - As main culprit behind act - Forged for other employees also - Other employees admitting facts against him - Imposition of minor penalty to other employee while dismissing him from service - Interference by HC upon findings - Held, is bad in law.
