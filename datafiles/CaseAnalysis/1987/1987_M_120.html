State Of Gujarat v Mohanlal Jitamaljiporwal And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28411; (1987) 2 SCC 364; AIR 1987 SC 1321; 1987 (1) BankCLR 226; 1987 (2) Crimes 1; 1987 CRLJ 1061; 1987 CrLR(SC) 325; 1987 (13) ECC 12; 1987 (11) ECR 353; 1987 (29) E.L.T. 483; JT 1987 (1) SC 783; 1987(2) SCALE 598; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 677; 1987 (2) UJ 129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs

Summary: Criminal -Gold Control Act, 1968,ss.85 and 123 -CrPC,1973,s.391 - A passenger traveling by a train, had adorned his waistline with a waist chain (kandora) weighing 820 grammes - Superintendent of Customs made the seizure on the basis of a "reasonable belief" that the article in question was made of smuggled gold - As per the prosecution, it was made of pure gold and was coated with mercury so as to give an appearance of being made of silver - Relying on the evidence of a licensed gold dealer, the trial court holding that what was seized was an 'ornament' and not 'primary gold' acquitted the accused of the charge for an offence under s.85 of the Gold (Control) Act, 1968 - Whether request for adducing additional evidence can be rejected on the grounds of delay? - Held, mere fact that six years had elapsed, for which time lag the prosecution was in no way responsible, not a good ground for refusing to act in order to promote the interests of justice - Application of the Assistant Public Prosecutor granted - Appeal allowed
