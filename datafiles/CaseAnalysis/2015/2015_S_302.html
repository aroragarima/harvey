Selvaraj v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 613; (2015) 10 SCC 230; AIR 2015 SC 3834; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4941; JT 2015 (8) SC 516; 2016 (1) RLW 19; 2015(9) SCALE 691; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 381; 2015 (6) SLR 13

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Illegal gratification - Conviction - On complaint by complainant regarding demand of illegal gratification by appellant for securing refund of loan subsidy, trap was laid and appellant was caught - However, Trial Court acquitted appellant and on appeal, HC reversed judgment of Trial Court and convicted appellant - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether judgment of HC convicting appellant is sustainable.

Held, acceptance of the bribe has not been established by prosecution by adducing cogent evidence. Hence view taken by Trial Court was plausible one and could not have been interfered with by HC, more so, without coming to the close quarters of the reasoning and re-appraisal of the evidence. Further, judgment of HC is not only cryptic but also no attempt was made to look into evidence, both oral and documentary. Hence, impugned judgment and order of HC is set aside and that of Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is incumbent upon HC while reviewing evidence and reversing the order of acquittal, to consider all matters on record including the reasons given by Trial Court in respect of order of acquittal and consider all the circumstances in favour of accused.
