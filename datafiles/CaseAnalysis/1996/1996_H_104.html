Dr.D.C. Saxena v Hon’Ble The Chief Justice Of India

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 927; (1996) 5 SCC 216; (1996) SCC (L&S) 496; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 404; AIR 1996 SC 2481; 1996 (3) CLT 140; 1996 CRLJ 3274; 1996 (63) DLT 492; JT 1996 (6) SC 529; 1996(5) SCALE 233; 1996 (5) Supreme 564

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Duties

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.19, 129, 215 and 124(4); Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss.2(c) and 4 - Freedom of expression - Criminal contempt - Public interest litigation for directions against former Prime Minister - Dismissal, by Bench headed by CJI - Allegation that CJI 'wilfully' and 'advertently' violated petitioner's and peoples' fundamental right to redressal by wrongful Dismissal of petition and CJI lost constitutional protection of not being prosecuted - Held, ex facie amounts to criminal contempt - Freedom of expression does not give licence to make unfounded allegations against any institutions, much less the judiciary.
