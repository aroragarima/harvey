State Of Maharashtra v Maharau Srawan Hatkar

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1556; (1995) 3 SCC 316; 1995 (1) CCC 624; JT 1995 (2) SC 583; 1995 (1) MLJ 111; 1995 (1) MLJ(SC) 111; 1995 (1) RentLR 626; 1995(2) SCALE 100; 1995 (3) SCJ 127; [1995] 2 S.C.R. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land and Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23 (1) - Enhanced compensation for land acquisition - Whether civil court has power and jurisdiction to award benefits of Amendment Act 68 of 1984? - A decree having been made u/s. 26(2), civil court is left to correct only either clerical or arithmetical mistakes - Inherent power of court is intended only to prevent abuse of process of court or to meet ends of justice - Present is not a case of such nature - Held, civil court has inherent lack of jurisdiction and it was devoid of power to entertain application to award additional benefits under Amendment Act - High Court and civil court order set aside - Appeal allowed.
