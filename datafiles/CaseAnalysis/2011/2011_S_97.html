Ashish Chadha v Smt. Asha Kumari & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 768; (2012) 1 SCC 680; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 311; 2012 CRLJ 773; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 94; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 239 and 240 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 218, 467, 468, 471 r/w. s. 120-B - Illegal grabbing - Government's land - Challenged - 'X' filed complaint alleging that respondent had connivance with Revenue Officials and manipulated revenue records, forged documents and transferred Government land on their names - Special Judge, framed charges u/ss. 420, 218, 467, 468, 471 r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Revision petition was filed by respondent before HC - HC set aside said order and transferred the matter, further directed that matter be proceeded, accordance with provisions of ss. 239 and 240 of CrPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned orders - Held, HC should not have transferred case to Special Judge, and such transfers ordered merely on say-so of a party have a demoralizing effect on trial courts - Unless very strong case based on concrete material was made out, such transfers should not be ordered - HC had quashed charge not only against respondents but also against all accused when no such prayer was made - It was improper for HC to go beyond scope of prayers made by respondents and quash even charges framed against all other accused - Impugned order has resulted in miscarriage of justice, accordingly was set aside - Charges framed by Special Judge was confirmed and Trial Court was directed to proceed further in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
