U.P. Electric Supply Co. Ltd. v R. K. Shukla Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 135; (1969) 2 SCC 400; AIR 1970 SC 237; 1969 (19) FLR 268; 1970 LabIC 276; 1969 (2) LLJ 728; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Closure, Demotion, Industrial Disputes (Amendment And Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956, Coal Mines Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1948, Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 6R(2), 6H(1) and 6H(2), 6-O - Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 6 and 7 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25FF, 33C(2), 33C(1), 25J(2) - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 57, 57A, sch. VI, cl. V(2), Proviso - Constitution, s. 254(2) - (A) Industrial Dispute - (1) Provision of s. 6R(2) of the U.P. Act - Whether overides s. 25J(2) of Industrial Disputes act, 1947 - (2) Powers of Labour Court - Question whether there has been retrenchment - (3) Scope of s. 6-O of U.P. Act - Order awarding retrenchment compensation - Without recording finding that there was retrenchment and compensation was payable - (4) Earned leave not availed of, by workmen before closure or transfer of undertaking - Absence of any provision in statute - Compensation if - (B) Electricity Act, 1910, ss.6, 7 - Purchase of undertaking by Board - Liability to pay retrenchment compensation arising on transfer - It attaches to purchase money payable to company in substitution for the undertaking and is not enforceable against the Board - Provision of ss. 57 and 57-A of Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Effect - (C) Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, sch.VI, cl.V(2), Proviso - Contingencies Reserve out of which retrenchment compensation is payable to employees of company handed over to Board - Charge for payment of compensation -

Held, Act having received assent of President s.6-R (2) would prevail over s. 25-J(2) of Industrial Disputes act, 1947 by virtue of art. 254(2) of Constitution - Held, cannot be decided by Labour Court , it can only compute compensation claimed to be payable to workmen when retrenchment is conceded - Cannot be made without recording finding Labour Court cannot award compensation without determining whether conditions of proviso to s. 6-O have been fulfilled - Compensation is not payable in absence of any provision in statute governing right to such compensation - Would make no difference, held that under sch.VI , cl.V, sub-cl. (2) proviso, compensation payable to employees of company would be charged upon Contingencies Reserve of the Company and balance alone would be handed over to Board - Charge for payment of compensation amount may attach to that amount - Appeal Allowed.
