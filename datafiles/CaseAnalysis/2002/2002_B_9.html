A.V. Murthy v B.S. Nagabasavanna

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 78; (2002) 2 SCC 642; AIR 2002 SC 985; AIR 2002 SCW 694; 2002 (2) AllCriC 390; 2002 (1) AllCriR 786; 2002 (48) ALR 378; 2002 (2) BC 1; 2002 (3) Bom.C.R. 13; 2002 (1) CCR 150; 2002 (1) CGLJ 260; [2002] 108 Comp Cas 838; 2002 (1) Crimes 306; 2002 CRLJ 1479; 2002 (1) JCC 397; 2002 (1) JIC 665; JT 2002 (1) SC 605; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 745; 2002(2) SCALE 65; 2002 (1) SCJ 675; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 906; 2002 (1) SLT 755; 2002 (1) Supreme 517

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Summary: The Sessions Judge and the Single Judge of the High Court were in error in quashing the complaint proceedings at the threshold, if, according to the respondent the loan was advanced four years prior to the issuance of the cheque, and therefore, at that stage of the proceedings, to say that the cheque drawn by the respondent was in respect of a debt or liability, which was not legally enforceable, was clearly illegal and erroneous.
