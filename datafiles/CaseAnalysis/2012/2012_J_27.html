Zelia M. Xavier Fernandes E. Gonsalves v Joana Rodrigues & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 21; (2012) 3 SCC 188; AIR 2012 SC 988; 2012(2) ALL MR 48; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 528; JT 2012 (2) SC 373; 2012 (4) MahLJ 686; 2012(2) SCALE 328; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Goa Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, Central Provinces and Berar Municipalities Act, 1922, Portuguese Civil Code, 1860

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Goa Panchayat Raj Act, 1994, ss. 10(f) and 11 - Portuguese Civil Code, 1860, art. 1098 and 1108 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 5A - Disqualification - Monetary interest - Sustainability - Respondent No. 1 made representation to Deputy Director Panchayat that appellant Panchayat member was liable for disqualification u/s. 10(f) of 1994 Act and also filed petition u/s. 11 of 1994Act before State Election Commission for disqualification on ground that appellant had directly or indirectly a share or monetary interest in contract given by respondent no. 2 Village Panchayt to her husband - Commission held that appellant had indirectly a share or monetary interest in contract and ordered to disqualify appellant as Panch Member of village in terms of cl. (f) s. 10 of 1994 Act - Appellant filed writ petition before HC - HC dismissed writ petition - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that mere relationship of husband and wife would not create that type of interest which was contemplated by s.10(f) of the Act - Respondent No 1 contended that marriage between appellant and respondent No 4 was governed by system 'Communiao Dos Bens' and under such marriage each spouse was entitled to one-half income of other spouse unless contracted otherwise - Whether appellant could be said to have any indirect share or monetary interest in contract of her husband with the Village Panchayat and thereby, she had incurred disqualification as a Panch member from Village Panchayat u/s. 10(f) of 1994 Act - Held, provisions contained in art. 1098 and 1108 of the 1860 Code and s. 5A of 1961 Act gave appellant a participation in profits of contract and advantages like apportionment of income from contract - Appellant, by operation of law, became entitled to share in profits of contract awarded to her husband by Village Panchayat, - Appellant's participation in profits of contract did constitute an 'indirect monetary interest in contract for collection of market fee awarded to her husband within s. 10(f) prohibiting the member of the Village Panchayat from having such an interest - Prohibition in s. 10(f) should not receive unduly narrow or restricted construction - Hence appellant had indirect share or monetary interest in contract of her husband and as a result, she had incurred disqualification u/s. 10(f) of the 1994 Act - Appeal dismissed.
