Gian Singh v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1532; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 141

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Compoundable, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 120B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 320, 482 - Conspiracy & Cheating - Denial of Quashing of FIR Challenged - Petitioner was convicted by Trial Court for commission of offence u/ss. 420 and 120B of IPC - During pendency of appeal, Trial Court allowed application and directed for compounding offence - However, HC dismissed petition u/s. 482 of Cr.P.C for quashing of FIR on ground of compounding offence - Hence, instant SLP for appeal.

Held, s. 320 of Cr.P.C mentions certain offences as compoundable, certain other offences as compoundable with the permission of the Court, and the other offences as non- compoundable vide s. 320(7) of Cr.P.C. S. 420 of IPC is compoundable, but s. 120B of IPC is non-compoundable. Hence, something which cannot be done directly cannot be done indirectly. The above three decisions do not appear to us to be correctly decided. Hence, direct that this matter be placed before a larger Bench to reconsider the correctness of the aforesaid three decisions. Order accordingly.

Ratio: Non-compoundable offences cannot be permitted to be compounded by the Court, whether directly or indirectly.
