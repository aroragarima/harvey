N. Madhavan v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 205; (1979) 4 SCC 1; (1979) SCC (Cr) 883; AIR 1979 SC 1829; 1979 CRLJ 1197; 1979 CrLR(SC) 606; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 228; 1979 UJ 793

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 17, 517(452(1)) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - Murder - Licensed Gun - Confiscation - Restoration -Appellant/accused was charged u/s. 302 of IPC, for shooting deceased with his licensed gun - Sessions Court hold that appellant/accused was entitled to protection and had not committed any offence - Appellant filed Revision petition before HC, against direction of confiscation of gun, which was dismissed - Aggrieved by this appellant appealed u/art. 136 of Constitution - Hence instant appeal - Whether Lower Courts were right in confiscating licensed gun instead of restoring it to appellant - Held, acquittal of appellant on ground that gun was used in causing fatal injury to deceased only in self-defense necessarily involved a finding, that gun was not used in commission of any offence for which he was tried - Sessions Judge did not give any reason, for directing confiscation of licensed gun of appellant/accused - Nor there was anything on record to show that Sessions Court, before passing Order of confiscation, had given an opportunity of being heard to appellant - There was absolutely no material before HC to show that appellant had been in lawful possession of gun under a license - Court opined that impugned order of confiscation of gun was arbitrary, unjust and could not be sustained - Hence, impugned order was set aside and Court directed for restoration of gun to appellant - Appeal allowed.
