Harbilas Rai Bansal v The State Of Punjab And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 719; (1996) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1996 SC 857; AIR 1996 SCW 238; 1996 (1) CLT 1; 1995 CPJ 1; JT 1995 (8) SC 458; 1995 (2) RCJ 641; 1996 (1) RentLR 1; 1996(6) SCALE 717; 1996 (1) SCJ 160; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 178; 1996 (1) UJ 464

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13 - Constitution, art. 14 - Amendment has taken away right of landlord to evict tenant from non-residential premises for his bona fide requirement - Whether it is discriminatory - Act, initially provided conforming to its objects and reasons bona fide requirement of premises by landlord whether residential or non-residential as ground of eviction of tenant - Whether classification created by Amendment has a nexus with object sought to be achieved by Act - Held, It is discriminatory - No nexus with object of the Act - Petition allowed.
