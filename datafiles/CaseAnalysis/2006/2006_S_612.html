Lata Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 299; (2006) 5 SCC 475; 2006 (5) ADJ 417; AIR 2006 SC 2522; 2006 CRLJ 3309; JT 2006 (6) SC 173; 2006 (144) PLR 218; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 738; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2006(6) SCALE 583; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 32 - IPC, 1860, ss. 366, 368 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 164, 482 - Prayer for issuing a writ of certiorari and /or mandamus for quashing Sessions Trial - Held, petitioner is a major and was at all relevant times a major - She is free to marry anyone she likes or live with anyone she likes - No bar to an inter-caste marriage under Hindu Marriage Act or any other law - No offence was committed by petitioner, her husband or her husband's relatives - Petition allowed.
