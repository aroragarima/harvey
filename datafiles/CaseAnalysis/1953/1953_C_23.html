Turner Morrison and Company, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 108; AIR 1953 SC 140; [1953] 23 ITR 152; 1953 SCJ 181; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940,s.21 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.3,4(1)(a), 4(1)(c),40, 42, 42(1), 42(3), 43, 66(1),66A - Assessment - Excess Profits tax - Accounting periods - Sale - 'M' incorporated in UK carries business of manufacturing salt - Salt manufactured sent for sale in any country where there was a suitable market - 'T' Company paid commission at rate of 2 1/2 per cent generally on all the sales except in some cases where 1 1/4 per cent paid - Sale proceeds collected by 'T' Company and credited to account after deducting their commission - Income-tax Officer treated 'T' Company as agents of Association u/s.43 and assessed them to income-tax for two assessment years - They were also assessed to excess profits tax for four chargeable accounting periods - 'T' Company preferred appeals which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the appointment of a person as a statutory agent u/s.43 of 1922 Act would apply or not - Held, person who comes within one or other of three categories u/s.43 of Act, be treated by Income-tax Officer as agent of non-resident and such person was for all purposes of this Act to be deemed to be such agent - Appointment of a person as a statutory agent u/s.43 of 1922 Act, only attracts s.42 of 1922 Act, for such appointment for all purposes of Act and not only for purposes of s.42 of 1922 Act - Appeal dismissed.
