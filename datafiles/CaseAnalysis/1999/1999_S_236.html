P. Nallammal Etc v State Rep. By Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 171; (1999) 6 SCC 559; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1133; AIR 1999 SC 2556; 1999 (1) CLR 1170; 1999 CRLJ 3967; 1999 (80) DLT 818; 1999 (84) ECR 22; 1999 (82) FLR 291; JT 1999 (3) SC 208; 1999(2) SCALE 685; 1999 (4) SLT 122; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 135; 1999 (4) Supreme 151

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Abetment - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 2, 11, 12 and 13(1)(e) - Whether offence u/s. 13(1)(e) of Act is unabettable? - Undoubtedly s. 11 deals with a case of abetment of offences defined u/ss. 8 and 9, and also that s. 12 specifically deals with case of abetment of offences u/ss. 7 and 11 - However, that is no ground to hold that Act does not contemplate abetment of any offences specified in s. 13 of Act - Held, legislative intent is manifest that abettors of all different offences u/s. 13(1)(e) of Act should also be dealt with along with public servant in same trial held by Special Judge - Appeals dismissed.
