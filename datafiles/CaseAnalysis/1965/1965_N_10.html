Kedar Pandey v Narain Bikram Sah

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1966 SC 160; 1966 (45) ILR(Pat) 878; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Election - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 5 - (A) whether High Court was right in its conclusion that respondent was a citizen of India u/art. 5 of Constitution on material date? - Held, place of birth of respondent has lost its importance in this case in view of concurrent findings of both HC and tribunal that for a period of 5 years preceding commencement of Constitution he was ordinarily resident in territory of India - Domicile of choice is India - Only intention required for a proof of a change of domicile is an intention of permanent residence - What is required to be established is that person who is alleged to have changed his domicile of origin has voluntarily fixed habitation of himself and his family in new country, not merely for a temporary purpose, but with a present intention of making it his permanent home -The domicile of an independent person is constituted by factum of residence in a country and animus manendi - Intention to reside in that country for an indefinite period - Events and circumstances of respondent shows respondent has acquired domicile of India - Therefore requirement of art. 5(C) of Constitution is fulfilled - It is manifested that requirements of art. 5(C) of Constitution are satisfied - Appeal dismissed.
