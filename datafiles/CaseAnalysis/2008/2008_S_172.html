Satya Narain Yadav v Gajanand And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1217; (2008) 16 SCC 609; AIR 2008 SC 3284; JT 2008 (8) SC 432; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 954; 2009 (1) RLW 263; 2008(10) SCALE 728; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 607; 2008 (3) UC 1535

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Self-Defence, Civil Case, Finding Of Facts, Right Of Self-Defence, Hearing Of Appeal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Trial Court convicted the respondent - High Court reversed the judgment and ordered acquittal - Appeal against - Alleged exercise of right of private defence - Held, a plea of right of private defence cannot be based on surmises and speculation - While considering whether the right of private defence is available to an accused, it is not relevant whether he may have a chance to inflict severe and mortal injury on the aggressor - To claim a right of private defence extending to voluntary causing of death, the accused must show that there were circumstances giving rise to reasonable grounds for apprehending that either death or grievous hurt would be caused to him - Burden is on the accused to show that he had a right of private defence which extended to causing of death - An appellate court has full power to review, re-appreciate and reconsider the evidence upon which the order of acquittal is founded - Appeal dismissed.
