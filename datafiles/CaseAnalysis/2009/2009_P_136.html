Capt. Amarinder Singh v Prakash Singh Badal and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 640; (2009) 6 SCC 260; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 971; JT 2009 (7) SC 187; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1627; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 943; 2009(7) SCALE 382; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Police Record, List Of Witnesses, Re Examination, Transfer Of Case, Power Of Supreme Court

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 406 and 407 - Transfer of Criminal proceedings u/s. 406 - FIR D/-24-06-2003, registered against respondent no. 1 and 2 i.e. current Chief Minister and Deputy Chief Minister of State of Punjab respectively, for offence u/ss. 420, 467, 468, 471 and 120B of IPC r/w ss. 7, 8, 9, 10, 13(1)(2) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 which were alleged to have been committed during the period between 1997-2002 when R1 was the CM of the State - Petitioners contended that after filing of charge sheet wherein 138 witnesses were to be examined and after further filing of supplementary chargesheet wherein some more witnesses were to be examined, on R1 coming to power in 2007 as CM, out of 138 only 59 were examined, out of which 35 turned hostile; aspect of selective calling up of witnesses, dropping crucial witnesses put forth; further contentions, most of the witnesses turning hostile one after another, non-examination of witnesses connected with the transaction and not citing certain witnesses hostile or cross examining them when they resile from the earlier statements has virtually rendered the trial farcical - Fact that complainant himself resiled from his affidavit which was filed in support of his private complaint, noted by Court; further noted the fact that Investigation Officer in his examination-in-chief resiled from every aspect of the investigation done by him - Whether transfer of case made out or whether petitioners have made out a case that they have reasonable apprehension of not availing justice in the State of Punjab? - Held, No - Proposition of law on the subject vide decisions of Court, deliberated upon - A criminal prosecution, if otherwise, justifiable and based upon adequate evidence does not become vitiated on account of mala fides or political mandate of the informant or the complainant; if justifiable and reasonable apprehension of miscarriage of justice and likelihood of bias is established, the proceeding has to be transferred elsewhere by exercise of power u/s 406 CrPC; apprehension should be reasonable - Other than R1 and R2 and one or two others who were relatives of R1, others were businessmen, agriculturalists, employed as special inspectors in Chandigarh, one of them a teacher; finding thus made as to difficulties that would be faced by other accused persons to attend Court proceedings as also by other witnesses from prosecution and defence sides if transfer of case is made - Material on record analysed, submissions heard - Transfer not called for - Complainant disowning the complaint, all important witnesses particularly Govt. officials informing that what they said before the Court alone is absolute truth and they were forced to make false statement at the time of registering the complaint, noted by Court - Further fact noted as to that similar petition was filed before High Court which was dismissed and had attained finality - Court said that 'we are satisfied that the presiding officer of the Special Court is conscious of his power and how to conduct fair trial at the same place' - Court opined that the public prosecutor cannot act on the dictates of the State Govt., he has to act objectively as he is also an Officer of the Court - Held further, apprehension not reasonable as to that justice will not be done - Petitions dismissed.
