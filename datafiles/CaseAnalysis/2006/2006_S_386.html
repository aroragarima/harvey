Sube Singh v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 32; (2006) 3 SCC 178; AIR 2006 SC 1117; 2006 (2) CGLJ 217; 2006 CRLJ 1242; 2006 (1) JCC 285; JT 2006 (2) SC 235; 2006 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 697; 2006 (143) PLR 746; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2006(2) SCALE 161; 2006 (3) SCJ 754; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 67; 2006 (1) UC 685

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: TADA Act

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 226, 32 - IPC, 1860, ss. 120-B, 120B, 186, 302, 307, 323, 342, 343, 352, 353, 365, 384, 392, 395, 399, 401, 402, 86 - CPC, 1908, s. 357 - Alleged illegal detention, custodial torture and harassment to family members of petitioner - Whether, in addition to directing CBI inquiry and prosecution of officers concerned, on facts and circumstances of this case, compensation should be awarded to petitioner and his family members, as a public law remedy for violation of their fundamental rights under art.21? - Whether compensation should be awarded under art 32/226, for every violation of art.21 where illegal detention or custodial violence is alleged? - Held, there is no clear or incontrovertible evidence about custodial torture, nor any medical report of any injury or disability - Grievance of petitioner and his relatives is against different officers in different Police Stations at different points of time - Several of allegations are proved to be exaggerated and false and not considered this to be a fit case for award of compensation - Petition disposed of.
