Kiran Singh and Others v Chaman Paswan and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1954 SC 340; 1954 ALJ 546; 1954 ALJ 551; 1965 KLT 1195; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 60; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 21, 103, 578 and 100 - Suits Valuation Act, 1887, s. 11(1), (a) and (b) - Construction of s. 11 of Suits Valuation Act, 1887 - What position in law is when a Court entertain a suit or an appeal over which it has no jurisdiction and what is effect of s. 11 of Suit Valuation Act, 1887 is on that position? - Held, it is a fundamental principle well-established that a decree passed by a Court without jurisdiction is a nullity and that even consent of parties could not cure defect - S. 11 provides that objections to jurisdiction of a Court based on over-valuation or under-valuation shall not be entertained by an Appellate Court except in manner and to extent mentioned in section - It is a self-contained provision complete in itself - Appeal dismissed.
