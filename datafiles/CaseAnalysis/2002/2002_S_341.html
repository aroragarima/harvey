Sunderbhai Ambalal Desai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1453; (2002) 10 SCC 283; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1943; AIR 2003 SC 638; 2002 (4) CCR 345; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1337; 2003 (1) JCC 86; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 153; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 273; JT 2002 (10) SC 80; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 380; 2002(9) SCALE 153; 2002 (7) SLT 131; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 39; 2002 (8) Supreme 525

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC - s. 451 - Charge against police inspectors of misappropriation and tampering with valuable articles, vehicles, Narcotic drugs, liquor while kept in police custody - Prosecution against petitioner/accused/inspectors - Prosecution seeking police remand for purpose of collection of evidence - Right to ask for remand challenged on ground of petitioners being granted anticipatory bail - Prosecution also sought directions from Court for proper management of mudammal articles - Procedure to be followed by police upon seizure of property - Held, s.451, Cr P C clearly empowers the Court to pass appropriate orders with regard to such property - Powers u/s.451 Cr P C must be exercised expeditiously and judiciously - In no set of circumstances, the Investigating Officer should keep such articles in custody for a longer period for the purpose of investigation and identification - Concerned Magistrate to take immediate action for seeing that powers u/s.451 Cr P C are properly and promptly exercised and articles are not kept for a long time at the police station, in any case, for not more than fifteen days to one month - Further, there must be proper supervision by the Registry of the concerned HC in seeing that the rules framed by the HC with regard to such articles are implemented properly - Adjourned for three weeks.
