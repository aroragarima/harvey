Lala Hari Chand Sarda v Mizo District Council and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1967 SC 829; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 1012

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Constitution - Socio-Economic - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Lushai Hills District (Trading by Non Tribals) Regulation (2 of 1963), s. 3 - Whether s. 3 and consequently said order was invalid which infringed appellant's fundamental right to carry on business at Aijal under art.19(1)(g) and that refusal to allow him to carry on his business amounted to an unreasonable restriction by exercise of an arbitrary power? - In State of Madras v V G Row ([1952] S C R 597) court laid down an elaborate test of reasonableness - Held, nature of right alleged to have been infringed, underlying purpose of restriction imposed, extent or urgency of evil sought to be remedied thereby, disproportion of imposition, prevailing conditions at time should enter into judicial verdict - It is well established that where a provision restricts any one of fundamental rights it is for State to establish reasonableness of such restriction - It is clear from Regulation and Rules made thereunder that there is no right of appeal to any superior authority against a refusal to grant or renew a licence - A perusal of regulation shows that it nowhere provides any principles or standards on which Executive Committee has to act in granting or refusing to grant licence - S. 3 and consequently order passed under it are ultra vires - Appeal allowed.
