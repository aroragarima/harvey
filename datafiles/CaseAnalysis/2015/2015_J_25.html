Reserve Bank of India and others v Jayantilal N. Mistry and others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 901; (2016) 3 SCC 525; AIR 2016 SC 1; 2016 (1) CTC 771; JT 2015 (12) SC 426; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4620; 2016 (1) MLJ 55; 2016 (1) RCR(Civil) 568; 2016 (1) UPLBEC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Reserve Bank Of India Act, 1934, Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, State Bank Of India (Subsidiary Banks) Act, 1959, Representation Of The People Act, 1951, State Bank of India Act, 1955, Credit Information Companies (Regulation) Act, 2005, Right to Information Bill, 2004

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Banking Regulation Act, 1949 - Right to Information Act, 2005, ss. 8(1)( a), 8(1)(d), 8(1)(e) - Information sought under RTI Act - Information upheld under public interest - Justification - Statutory inspection of banks were conducted by RBI under 1949 Act - Further, respondent sought information under 2005 Act - However, information sought under 2005 Act was denied by authority - RBI contended that it carries out inspections of banks and financial institutions on regular basis and inspection reports prepared by it contain wide range of information that is collected in fiduciary capacity - First Appellant Authority passed order against respondent - However, in second appeal, Commission by impugned order directed RBI to provide information as per records to respondent - HC vide notice stayed operation of order passed in second appeal - Petitioner contended that information sought for by respondents from Bank have been denied on ground that such information is exempted from disclosure u/s. 8(1)(a), (d) and (e) of 2005 Act - Whether RBI has rightly refused to disclose information on ground of its fiduciary relationship with banks.

Held, RBI does not place itself in fiduciary relationship with Financial institutions as reports of inspections, statements of bank, information related to business obtained by RBI are not under pretext of confidence or trust. Further, SC held that neither RBI nor Banks act in interest of each other. SC held that RBI is supposed to uphold public interest and not interest of individual banks. RBI is clearly not in any fiduciary relationship with any bank. RBI has no legal duty to maximize benefit of any public sector or private sector bank; hence, there is no relationship of 'trust' between them. SC held that RBI has statutory duty to uphold interest of public at large, depositors, country's economy and banking sector. Hence, RBI ought to act with transparency and not hide information that might embarrass individual banks. Further, SC held that RBI is duty bound to comply with provisions of 2005 Act and disclose information sought by respondent. SC held that contention of RBI that disclosure would hurt economic interest of country is totally misconceived. Further, SC held that exemption contained in s. 8(1)(e) of 2005 Act applies to exceptional cases and only with regard to certain pieces of information, for which disclosure is unwarranted or undesirable. If information is available with regulatory agency not in fiduciary relationship, there is no reason to withhold disclosure of same. However, where information is required by mandate of law to be provided to authority, it cannot be said that such information is being provided in fiduciary relationship. SC held that Financial institutions have obligation to provide all information to RBI and such information shared under obligation/ duty cannot be considered to come under purview of being shared in fiduciary relationship. Further, SC held that legislature's intent was to make available to general public such information which had been obtained by public authorities from private body. Further, SC held that not all information that Govt. generates will or shall be given out to public. Hence, impugned order passed by authority in favor of respondent is justified and in accordance with law. Petition dismissed.

Ratio - Authority is not authorized to upheld information sought under RTI Act when same is in private interest and would not affect economic interest of country.
