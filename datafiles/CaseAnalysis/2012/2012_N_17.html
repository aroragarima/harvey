Archaeological Survey of India and another v Narender Anand and another

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 458; (2012) 2 SCC 562; 2012 (3) AWC 2735; 2012 (187) DLT 763; JT 2012 (1) SC 386; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 260

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Delhi Development Authority Act, 1957, Indian Treasure-Trove Act, 1878, Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1904, Ancient and Historical Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains (Declaration of National Importance) Act, 1951, the DDA Act

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1904, 3(2) - Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958, ss. 20A, 20B, 39(2) - Construction of building - Issuance of notice - Justifiability - After building plan was sanctioned by Corporation, respondent nos. 1, 2 had started digging foundation for new building according to notification of Central Govt. on 15-5-1991, which was published in Gazette of India dt. 25-5-1991 - Further, Archaeological Survey of India, informed Corporation that sanction given by it was contrary to notification - Corporation issued notice to respondent nos. 1, 2to stop construction and obtained requisite permission from Archaeological Survey of India - Respondent nos. 1 , 2 challenged letter of Corporation in suit - Respondent filed application for injunction and application was allowed by Single Judge - Application was filed by Archaeological Survey of India for review of injunction order which was disposed of by learned Single Judge - Archaeological Survey of India challenged order of Single Judge - During pendency of appeal Heritage and Culture Forum, filed writ petition for stopping construction of multistoried building on plot owned by respondent nos. 1, 2 - Division Bench of HC held that prohibition respondent nos. 1, 2 were not entitled to raise construction - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether Archaeological Survey of India was fully justified in making grievance that Division Bench of HC was not justified in directing Central Govt. to review prohibition contained in notification on 16-6-1992 - Held, HC's anxiety to maintain balance between dire necessity of protecting historical monuments of national and international importance and development of infrastructures was understandable, but it was not possible to approve fiat issued to Central Govt. to review prohibition contained in notification - Govt. must have issued notification on 16-6-1992 after consulting experts in field and keeping in view object of 1958 Act - HC was not justified in directing Central Govt. to review or reconsider notification on 16-6-1992 and to that extent, impugned judgment was liable to be set aside - Insertion of ss. 20A, 20B, direction given by HC for review of notification on 16-6-1992 had become infructuous and Govt. was no longer required to act upon same - Appeal of respondent nos.1, 2 was wholly meritless - HC had rightly held that even though notification did not become effective as same was not published in Official Gazette, earlier notification issued remained effective and same was saved by s. 39(2) of 1958 Act - Even though notification on 3-5-1957 was issued in supersession of notification on 4-10-1956, same remained alive because of non compliance of s. 3(2) of 1904 Act - HC's interpretation of prohibition contained in notification on 16-6-1992 was correct and distance of 100 meters had to be counted from outer boundary wall of Jantar Mantar which had protected area and not physical structures of observatory - HC had given detailed reasons for rejecting plea of respondent nos.1, 2 that provisions of DDA Act would prevail over those contained in 1958 Act - Appeal disposed of.
