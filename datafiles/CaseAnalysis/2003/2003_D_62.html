Oriental Insurance Company Ltd. v Devireddy Konda Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 46; (2003) 2 SCC 339; (2003) SCC (Cr) 540; 2003 (1) ACC 214; AIR 2003 SC 1009; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 133; 2003 (50) ALR 585; 2003 (2) AWC 1255; 2003 (6) Bom.C.R. 386; [2003] 113 Comp Cas 532; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1608; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 1; JT 2003 (1) SC 372; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 455; 2003 (1) KLT 583; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 115; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 748; 2003(1) SCALE 441; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 537; 2003 (1) SLT 515; 2003 (1) Supreme 732; 2003 (1) UC 687

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contractual Liability

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act - cases of passengers traveling in goods vehicles which met accident resulting in death of such person or bodily injury - High Court held that compensation is payable by the insurer even if the deceased persons in respect of whom claims are made were gratuitous passengers - appeal - Held, provisions of the Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on the owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger traveling in a goods carriage and the insurer would have no liability therefore. - The Tribunal and the High Court were not justified in holding that the insurer had the liability to satisfy the award.
