Brij Mohan and Others v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 142; (1994) 1 SCC 413; (1994) SCC (Cr) 527; AIR 1994 SC 739; 1994 (1) Crimes 288; 1994 CRLJ 922; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 203

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 9 and 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 396 and 397 - (A) Whether ID parade within 24 hours of arrest can be rejected on ground that it was not possible to identify after three months from incident - (B) Death sentence for 'Rarest of rare cases' in dacoity with gruesome murders - Two accused persons alleged to be armed with fire arms did not even enter house - Held, cannot be rejected - It is not rarest to rare cases, death sentence reduced to imprisonment for life - Order accordingly.
