Manzar Sayeed Khan v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, K.G. Balakrishnan, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 312; (2007) 5 SCC 1; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 417; AIR 2007 SC 2074; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1701; 2007 (3) ALT 5; 2007 CRLJ 2959; JT 2007 (5) SC 335; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 883; 2007(5) SCALE 445; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 907; 2007 (4) UC 297

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Civil Action, Communal Tension, Customs (Amendment) Act, 1969

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 153, 153A and 34 - High Court directed the Crime Branch of the State of Maharashtra to complete the investigation in FIR No.10 of 2004 registered at the Deccan Police Station, Pune, against the appellants - Appeal against - Held, gist of the offence is the intention to promote feelings of enmity or hatred between different classes of people - Intention to cause disorder or incite the people to violence is the sine qua non of the offence under s. 153A and the prosecution has to prove prima facie the existence of mens rea on the part of the accused - One cannot rely on strongly worded and isolated passages for proving the charge nor indeed can one take a sentence here and a sentence there and connect them by a meticulous process of inferential reasoning - Respondents shall not proceed against Professor James W. Laine, the author of the book, for offences under ss. 153, 153A and 34 - Appeals disposed of.
