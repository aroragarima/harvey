Sadhu Singh v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

01 June 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 453; 1965 (2) ACC 258; AIR 1966 SC 91; 1966 MLJ 153; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 243

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 30(1), 30-A(6)(b), 30-A(8) - Anti-social activity - Detention order - Scope of Judicial review - In exercise of powers conferred by r. 30(1) of the Rules, District Magistrate ordered that petitioner be detained in Central Jail - It was alleged that petitioner was indulging in anti-social activities and that detention order was reviewed and confirmed by Administrator - Hence, instant Petition - Whether order of detention was amenable to Judicial Review and there had been no confirmation of order of detention by Administrator in manner provided by r.30-A(6)(b) of the Rules - Held, r. 30(1) of the Rules was enacted as emergency measure which authorized appropriate Govt. or Administrator or authorities empowered by Govt. or Administrator with view to prevent person from acting to detriment of public order and safety, to detain him without Trial in larger interests of security of State - Satisfaction of authority which justified use of power u/r.30 of the Rules and confirmation of order of detention were not subject to judicial review, for order of detention without Trial was executive act - However, that did not exclude Court's power to investigate into compliance with procedural safeguards imposed by statute or into existence of prescribed conditions precedent to exercise of power or into plea that order was made mala fide or for collateral purpose - Thus, it could not be said that decision to continue detention upon review of order of detention could still be regarded as subject to judicial review - Further, it was pursuant to detention order so confirmed that any person would remain detained and review which was intended to be made u/r.30-A(8) of the Rules was of that order which was confirmed - Hence, it was clear from order of Administrator that detention order was confirmed by Administrator - Petition dismissed.
