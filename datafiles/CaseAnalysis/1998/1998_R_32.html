Rahabhar Productions Private Limited v Rajendra K. Tandon

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1582; (1998) 4 SCC 49; AIR 1998 SC 1639; JT 1998 (2) SC 674; 1998 (1) RCJ 409; 1998 (1) RCR(Rent) 482; 1998(2) SCALE 497; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 470; 1998 (4) SLT 78; 1998 (3) Supreme 473

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Rent Control

Summary: Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 1 and 14 C- Whether Act is beneficial as well as restrictive? - Harmonious construction to keep a balance in rights of landlord and obligations of tenant is necessary - Whether landlord who has retired or is about to retire from service, has special right to recover immediate possession? - Whether proceedings of eviction by landlord who has retired or is about to retire, can be contested by tenant? - Held, Act is beneficial as also restrictive - Available only when requirement of owner is bona fide - It restricts right of landlords to evict tenants at their will - Can be contested by tenant.
