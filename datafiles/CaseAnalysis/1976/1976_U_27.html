S. B. Gurbaksh Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 55; (1976) 2 SCC 181; AIR 1976 SC 1115; 1976 (5) CTR 311; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 247; 1976 (37) STC 425; 1976 TAXLR 1619

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Bihar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Delhi Sales Tax Rules, 1951

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Appellant was assessed to sales tax under the Act - Assistant Commissioner of Sales Tax held that the assessment of the income of the assessee for the first two quarters of the concerned year was invalid having been made out of time and remanded to the Sales Tax officer for a fresh assessment in respect of the third and fourth quarter of the year - Commissioner, however, revised the appellate order of the ACST in exercise of his power u/s. 20(3) of the Act and held that no part of the assessment for the concerned year was barred and directed a fresh assessment to be made for all the four quarters - Appellant then filed the W.P.s challenging the order of the Commissioner and against the fresh assessment order passed by the Sales Tax officer in pursuance thereof - HC in letters patent appeal decided in favor of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the Commissioner in the instant case acted without due or reasonable delay as alleged by the respondent - Held, there was no undue or unreasonable delay made by the Commissioner, since, time limit had been prescribed for the assessee to move in revision and appellate or the revisional powers in an appeal or revision filed by an assessee could be exercised in due course, no time limit had been prescribed for it - Appeals dismissed.
