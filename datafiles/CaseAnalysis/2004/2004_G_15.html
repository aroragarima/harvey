K.G. Shivalingappa (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v G.S. Eswarappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 498; (2004) 12 SCC 189; AIR 2004 SC 4130; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 45; 2004 (56) ALR 556; 2004 (4) AWC 3617; 2004 (2) CLR 599; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 43; JT 2004 (6) SC 17; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 371; 2004 (2) RD 305; 2004(6) SCALE 262

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Coparcener, Evidence Of Partition

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17(1)(b) - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Partition - Whether there is prior partition of joint family property - Whether unregistered document can be relied upon to establish severance of status in joint family - First Appellate Court's finding of fact that there was a prior partition of joint family property and document though unregistered could be relied upon to establish severance of status in joint family and respective branches were enjoying properties and its usufruct separately - In second appeal judgments and decrees of courts below set aside only because Partition Deed required to be registered - Held, while setting aside judgment in second appeal of courts below which is limited to substantial questions of law, HC should have recorded proper reasons - Single Judge not adverted to or set aside any of findings recorded by first Appellate Court - Judgment and decree passed by HC set aside and that of courts below restored.
