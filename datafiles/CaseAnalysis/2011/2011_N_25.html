Narwinder Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, B. Sudarshan Reddy

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 22; (2011) 2 SCC 47; AIR 2011 SC 686; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 632; 2011 (1) JCC 334; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 228; JT 2011 (1) SC 89; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 280; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 100; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 427; 2011 (1) RLW 573; 2011(1) SCALE 114; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304-B and 306 - Abetment to suicide - Conviction - Challenged - Charge u/s. 304-B of the IPC was framed by the Trial Court against appellant and his parents - Trial Court held that the ingredients of s. 304-B of IPC were satisfied and accordingly convicted them for an offence punishable u/s. 304-B of IPC - On appeal, HC held that the deceased had not committed suicide on account of demands for dowry but due to harassment caused by the husband (appellant), in particular - HC acquitted the parents of the appellant but conviction of the appellant was converted from one u/s. 304-B of IPC to s. 306 of IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC could have convicted the appellant u/s. 306 of IPC as the charge had been framed u/s. 304-B of IPC? - Held, cruelty or harassment sans any dowry demands which drives the wife to commit suicide attracts the offence of 'abetment of suicide' u/s. 306 of IPC and not s. 304-B IPC which defines the offence and punishment for 'dowry death' - In the present case, HC recorded a firm finding that the harassment was not for or in connection with any demands for dowry; but, at the same time, the HC concluded that the wife committed suicide due to the harassment of the appellant, in particular - There was no evidence of any demand for dowry soon before the death - Thus, mere omission or defect in framing charge would not disable the Court from convicting the accused for the offence which has been found to be proved on the basis of the evidence on record - In such circumstances, the HC was, therefore, fully justified in convicting the appellant u/s. 306 of IPC - There was no failure of justice in the conviction of the appellant u/s. 306 IPC by the HC, even though the specific charge was not framed - Appeal dismissed.
