Tulsidas Paul v Second Labour Court, W. B. and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 729; (1972) 4 SCC 205B; 1971 (22) FLR 148; 1971 (1) LLJ 526; 1971 UJ 308

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - National bank - Industrial Dispute - Rivalery between unions - Workers involved in throwing brickbats and missiles were refused employment on request of other workers - Whether refusal to give them work amounts to dismissal? - It is true that workmen could not have lien on posts to which they were employed - There was evidence of firm following practice of engaging same workmen when they presented themselves for work at commencement of next season - Held, appellants having failed to lead evidence as to a reasonable possibility of recurrence of trouble if concerned workmen were to be reinstated or of their not being entitled to be employed at beginning of next season, it was impossible to say that case of an exception to general rule of reinstatement was made out - Appeal dismissed.
