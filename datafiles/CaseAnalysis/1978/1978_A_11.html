Mohan Lal and Another v Ajit Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 473; (1978) 3 SCC 279; (1978) SCC (Cr) 378; AIR 1978 SC 1183; 1978 CRLJ 1107; 1978 CrLR(SC) 268; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 823

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 302, 392 and 397 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - High Court acquitted respondent of offences u/ss. 302, 392 and 397, IPC, 1860, for which he was convicted by Additional Sessions Judge - Present appeal - As evidence on record disproves exculpatory part of respondent's statement in Trial Court, it is clearly permissible to accept that part of statement which accords with evidence on record, and to act upon it - Held, important piece of evidence against respondent was his statement u/s. 27 of Evidence Act, 1872, and recoveries which were made in pursuance thereof - Further held, incriminating articles were acquired by respondent at one and same time and that it was he and no one else who had robbed deceased of money and ring and had hidden them at a place and in a manner which was known to him - All these facts were not only proof of robbery but were presumptive evidence of charge of murder as well - Impugned judgment of HC set aside and respondent convicted of offences u/ss. 302 and 392/397 IPC, 1860 - Appeal allowed.
