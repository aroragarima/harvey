Chimanlal Hargovinddas v Special Land Acquisition Officer, Poona and

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 525; (1988) 3 SCC 751; AIR 1988 SC 1652; 1988 (14) ALR 566; 1988 (2) CCC 476; JT 1988 (3) SC 106; 1988(2) SCALE 43; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 531; 1988 (2) UJ 318

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service rules 1975 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss.18, 23 - Land Acquisition - Valuation - (A) Disposal of reference by Court - Relevancy of material relied on by Acquisition Officer in his award - (B) Determination of market value of land - (C) Large block of agricultural land between underdeveloped land - Deduction of 25% on ground of largeness of block and also depressing of valuation as development would reach the lane after 12 years - Challenged - Held, cannot be relied upon unless same is produced and proved - It must be determined as on crucial date of publication of the notification u/s.4 , methodology of determination indicated - Deduction justified, depressing held not open to challenge, however further depressing not warranted - Appeal partly allowed.
