M.D., H.S.I.D.C. and Others v Hari Om Enterprises and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 936; (2009) 16 SCC 208; AIR 2009 SC 218; JT 2008 (8) SC 184; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3009; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 3876; 2008(9) SCALE 241; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Doctrine Of Proportionality, Industrial Concerns, Actual Possession, Solicitor, Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery) Act, 1959

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 226 - Whether action for resumption of land or forfeiture had rightly been taken by Corporation? - Whether High Court was right in entertaining writ petition in a matter arising out of a contract qua contract? - Held, in a case where the allottee had complied with the terms of allotment in the matter of payment of instalments, the same would be a relevant factor for exercising the enabling clause of resumption by a 'State'; further opportunity was required to be given to the allottee even if there was some default on its part inasmuch as the appellant itself provides for levy of penalty; power of resumption, thus, must be resorted to only in a case where despite grant of the opportunities contemplated in terms of the letter of intent were violated - Oordinarily in a matter of enforcement of a contract qua contract, a writ court shall not exercise its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India, but, it is also trite that where the action of a State is violative of art. 14 of the Constitution of India as being wholly unfair and unreasonable, the writ court would not hesitate to grant relief in favour of a person, where both law and equity demands that such relief should be granted - Appeals disposed of.
