Mushtak Hussein v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Vivian Bose, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 10; AIR 1953 SC 282; 1953 CRLJ 1127; 1953 SCJ 338; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Misdirection, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Conjectures

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, ss. 421, 423(2),297 and 537(d) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Appellant convicted on a charge u/s. 366, IPC, 1860 for having kidnapped at Poona a minor girl Shilavati in order that she may be forced or seduced to illicit intercourse and was sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for two years -(A)Whether Appellate Court to substitute its own verdict ? - The statute law in India in certain circumstances permits an appeal against a jury's verdict and authorize Appellate Court to substitute its own verdict on its own consideration of evidence ? - Unless it is established in a case that there has been a serious misdirection by Judge in charging jury which has occasioned a failure of justice and has misled jury in giving its verdict, verdict of jury cannot be set aside - (A1)Whether jury has been misdirected regarding being asked to solve problem by resorting to speculative reasoning?- Judge gave jury a carte blanche to come to its conclusion on basis of its own conjectures, if necessary- These propositions placed before jury were repugnant to all notions of criminal jurisprudence and they must necessarily have had affected its mind in arriving at conclusion- Appeal allowed.
