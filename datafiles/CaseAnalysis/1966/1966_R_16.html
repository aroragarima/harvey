Ram Prasad Dagduram v Vijay Kumar Motilal Mirakhanwala and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 87; AIR 1967 SC 278; 1967 (69) BomLR 20; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 188

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Wrong Plaintiff, Foreclosure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 (1) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 22 - Whether claim of respondents to enforce mortgage is barred by limitation? - Addition of plaintiff could not be made under O. 1, r. 10(1) unless necessary for determination of real matter in dispute - In view of s. 22, suit as regards parties added under O. 1, r. 10(1) deemed to have been instituted when they were added - If parties are added with claim which is antagonistic to original plaintiff's claim, that would not constitute a case where original suit should be deemed to have been continued - Held, it is not a case of a continuation of original suit - Parties added to press their own rights, in conflict with and antagonistic to those which were being pressed in suit as originally framed - Suit is barred by limitation - Decree passed by High Court be set aside - Decree passed by trial court restored - Appeal allowed.
