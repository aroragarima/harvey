Govindaraju v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 685; (2009) 14 SCC 236; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2784; 2009 CRLJ 3457; JT 2009 (7) SC 521; 2009 (5) KarLJ 428; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 183; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 73; 2009(8) SCALE 607; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1074

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304B - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dowry death - Presumption as to dowry death - Acquittal by Trial Court - Conviction by High Court - Whether proper? - Held, yes - Deceased married to appellant on 25-05-1986, died due to burn injuries in her matrimonial home on the night of 28/29-01-1987 - Deceased had no father and marriage was arranged by her matrimonial uncle(complainant) - Judgment of TC and HC closely examined - HC found to have appreciated the evidence very deeply - Evidence of witnesses esp. of PW1 and PW18 found to be unshakable on the aspect of demand of dowry; TC has discarded their testimonies on a contradiction completely ignoring the fact that PW18 was an illiterate woman and could not expected to remember the details regarding the date on which the initial amount of Rs. 2000/- out of total demanded Rs. 5000/- was paid to the appellant - It is a basic principle that the evidence of witness has to be appreciated as a whole, when the evidence of an ordinary witness, who is not much educated and comes from a poor strata of society not having the advantage of education; the Court has to keep in mind all these aspects - PW5 and PW6 had supported the case of the dowry harassment; PW6 was the immediate neighbour of appellant - Judgment of HC found to be wholesome as it dealt with each and every necessary aspect like its power to review the evidence and to arrive at its own independent conclusion, whether the appeal is against conviction or acquittal, for it HC relied on 'Dhanna v. State of MP, 1996 (10) SCC 79', aspect that it was considering an acquittal judgement wherein accused's presumed innocence has been further reinforced - All aspects such as the cruelty meted out to deceased, her unnatural death within the time span of seven years and constant demands of dowry proved to the hilt, satisfying requirement of s. 113B of Evidence Act - HC's judgement commendably upheld - Being without merits - Appeal dismissed.
