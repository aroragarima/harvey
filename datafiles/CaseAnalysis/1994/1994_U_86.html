Puranjit Singh v Union Territory of Chandigarh and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1062; (1994) Supp3 SCC 471; (1995) SCC (L&S) 95; AIR 1994 SC 2737; 1994 (28) ATC 518; 1994 (69) FLR 1027; JT 1994 (6) SC 239; 1994(4) SCALE 269; 1994 (5) SLR 280; 1994 (2) UJ 629

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Housing

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 and 311 - Punjab Service of Engineers, Class II P. W. D. (Buildings and Roads Branch) Rules (1965), r. 12.5 - Seniority and promotion - (A) Engineer in Irrigation Department opted to join P.W.D. (Buildings and Roads) Department - Appointed as direct recruit to post in later department - Was sent on deputation to various organizations - His service prior to his fresh career as direct recruit cannot be counted for seniority - Held, it cannot be counted for seniority - He cannot also claim promotion on basis of post or posts held on deputation - (B) Govt. service - Official procedure - Notings in departmental files - Production of said notings by him in Court proceedings - If proper - They do not create rights in favour of employee - Further held, it is not proper - Appeal dismissed.
