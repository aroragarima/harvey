Vidhya Viswanathan v Kartik Balakrishnan

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 681; AIR 2015 SC 285; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 187; 2014(6) ALL MR 922; 2014 (107) ALR 394; 2014 (6) ALT(SC) 31; 2014 (6) AWC 5909; 2014 (6) Bom.C.R. 154; JT 2014 (10) SC 490; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4393; 2014 (2) OLR 907; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 563; 2015 (2) RLW 1082; 2014(11) SCALE 71; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 1164

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, ss. 13(1)(ia) and 25 - Divorce - Cruelty - Appellant (Husband) filed a petition u/s. 13(1)(ia) of Act against respondent (wife) for dissolution of marriage - Appellant alleged that respondent used to insult him and used to get violent and hysterical - Further alleged that respondent used to send him e-mails which were derogatory and in bad taste - It was also alleged by the appellant that respondent refused to join his company even after his best efforts - HC allowed the divorce petition, and dissolved the marriage between the parties - Hence, this appeal with special leave petition.

Held, no ground to interfere with the decree of divorce passed by the HC on the ground of cruelty. However, as respondent, was doing a job before her marriage, and at present she is not doing any work. It was just and proper to direct the appellant to pay to the respondent one time lump sum amount of alimony. Directions was given to the appellant to pay Rs. 40 Lakhs as one time alimony to the respondent. Appeal disposed of.
