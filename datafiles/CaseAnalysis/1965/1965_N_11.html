R. Venkataswami Naidu and Another v Narasram Naraindas

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 144; AIR 1966 SC 361; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Madras City Tenants' Protection Act, 1921, ss. 9, 2 (4), 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 7 A, 8, 9, 10, 12, 13 and proviso to s. 12 - Transfer of Property Act, 1872, s. 108(h) - Presidency Small Cause Courts Act, 1882 ,s. 41 - By an unregistered instrument of lease respondent let out a piece of vacant land to appellants for term of one year - Tenants held over ,after expiry of term reserved and tenancy was continued - Lease provided that tenants 'shall not raise any building whatsoever in vacant site' - They committed a breach of covenant by putting up a building on land - Scope of ss. 3 and 9 of Act of 1921 - (A)Whether tenants in present case were entitled to compensation u/s. 3 of Act of 1921? - A section has only one interpretation and one scope - No resort to Preamble would be justified - Word 'tenant' in s. 3 must be understood only in sense that word is defined in Act- Section in general applies to every tenant and would include all and sundry tenants - Appellants are tenants as contemplated by s. 3 of Act of 1921 - Covenant not to build affects right to claim compensation u/s. 3 of Act of 1921, such a covenant would be of no effect, for u/s. 12 nothing in any contract shall take away a tenant's rights under Act of 1921 - Held,the tenants must be held entitled to their right to compensation u/ss. 3 and 9 of Act of 1921 in spite of covenant not to build and a breach of it by them - Order accordingly.
