Arun v State By Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudarshan Reddy, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1947; (2008) 15 SCC 501; AIR 2009 SC 1256; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1245; 2009 (1) JCC 609; JT 2008 (12) SC 571; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 83; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 902; 2008(16) SCALE 361; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: By-Standers, Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, Mother- In-Law

Summary: IPC, 1860, s. 302 r/w 32 - Trial Court acquitted the appellants - High Court reversed the acquittal and recorded conviction - Appeal against - Whether appellant entered the premises armed along with A-4, who killed the deceased?; Even if he entered the premises armed, will that by itself establish common intention to commit murder? - Held, appellate court has full power to review, re-appreciate and re-consider the evidence upon which the order of acquittal is founded and its power to review and re-appreciate the evidence and come to its own conclusion is not controlled by any provisions of the Cr.P.C., 1973 - Accused having secured his acquittal, the presumption of innocence is further reinforced, reaffirmed and strengthened by the trial court - If two reasonable views are possible on the basis of the evidence on record and one favourable to the accused has been taken by the trial court, it ought not to be disturbed by the appellate court - Burden lies on prosecution to prove that actual participation of more than one person for commission of criminal act was done in furtherance of common intention of all at a prior concert - Accusations made against the appellant making him constructively liable for the criminal act of murder committed by A-4 with the aid of Section 34 IPC were not established - Judgment of the High Court convicting the appellant under s. 302 r/w s. 34 is set aside and affirmed the conviction of the appellant under s. 457 (1) - Appeal partly allowed.
