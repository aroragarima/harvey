Gopabandhu Biswal v Krishna Chandra Mohanty and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1660; (1998) 4 SCC 447; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1147; AIR 1998 SC 1872; 1998 (79) FLR 396; JT 1998 (3) SC 279; 1998 (3) LLN 68; 1998(3) SCALE 226; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 1108; 1998 (3) SLJ 102; 1998 (2) SLR 557; 1998 (4) SLT 556; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 74; 1998 (4) Supreme 201

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Civil Procedure - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 22(3)(f) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 47, r. 1 - Review - SLP filed against judgment of tribunal, dismissed - Judgment of tribunal becomes final and binding on parties - Review petition by aggrieved person - (A) If permissible, even by person who was not a party to petition or SLP - (B) Maintainability - Held, not permissible and aggrieved person who is not party to main petition and SLP cannot also challenge judgment of tribunal by filing review petition - Mere chance in future not making them aggrieved person, petition not maintainable - Appeal allowed.
