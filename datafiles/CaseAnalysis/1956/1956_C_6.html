Â Ch. Tika Ramji and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1956 SC 676; 1956 SCJ 625; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Sugarcane Control Order, 1955, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Sugar-Cane Act, 1934, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Uttar Pradesh Sugar Factories Control Act, 1938, Sugar Control Order, 1955, Essential Commodities Ordinance, 1955, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Rules, 1954, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane Regulation Of Supply And Purchase Order, 1954, Rubber (Production and Marketing) Amendment Act, 1954

Summary: Constitution - Agriculture & Agri. Products - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14, 19(1)(c), 31, 32, 254, 254(2) and 30, Entry 33 List III sch. 7, Entry 52 of List I - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, ss. 15, 16 1(a) 2(b), 17 and 28 - Sugarcane Control Order, 1955, cls.(3) and (4) - Constitutional validity of 1953 Act and notifications D/-27-09-1954 and 09-11-1955 issued by State Govt. - Held, Privy Council in Commonwealth of Australia v Bank of New South Wales,Indlaw, [1950] AC 235 relied on - Court held, restrictions imposed by alleged notifications are reasonable restrictions imposed on petitioners in public interest - Therefore, impugned Act and notifications issued thereunder were intravires State Legislature and are binding on petitioners - Further Court held, it comes well within protection of art.19(6) and impugned notification cannot be challenged as violative of fundamental right guaranteed under art.19(1)(f) and (g) - If these impugned notifications are, therefore, intravires State Legislature, they cannot be challenged also u/art. 31 as none of petitioners is being deprived of his property, if any, save by authority of law - Petitions dismissed.
