Commissioner of Income Tax v Willamson Financial Services and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1363; (2008) 2 SCC 202; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1271; [2008] 297 ITR 17; JT 2007 (13) SC 581; 2007(14) SCALE 337; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 376; [2007] 165 TAXMAN 638

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Taxes On Income

Summary: Income-tax Act, 1961; Income-tax Rules, 1962 - At what stage s. 80HHC Deduction is to be allowed i.e. before the 60 : 40 apportionment under Rule 8(1) or from 40% profits on sales taxable as Business Income? - Held, r. 8(1) segregates agricultural income which is exempted income from business income which is chargeable to tax - For that purpose it need to apply the ratio of 60 : 40 - To the extent of 40% only chargeability and computability - Assessee cannot claim 80HHC(3)(a) Deduction against the entire tea composite income - It can be claimed only against proportionate income - Appeals disposed of.
