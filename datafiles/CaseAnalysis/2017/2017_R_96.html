Unichem Laboratories Limited v Rani Devi and others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 279; AIR 2017 SC 2050; 2017 (3) AWC 2200; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 311; 2017 (153) FLR 867; JT 2017 (4) SC 330; 2017(5) SCALE 14

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Corporate, Jurisdiction, Contract, Allotment, Accommodation, Quarters, Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887, Uttar Pradesh Industrial Housing Act, 1955, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land & Property, Companies Act, 2013

Summary: Civil Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Housing Act, 1955 - Eviction of employee from quarters allotted - Maintainability of suit for want of locus - In pursuance of application made by husband of respondent no.1 (deceased employee), appellant granted quarters to deceased employee vide order dt.12-5-1971 - While so, after retirement, deceased employee had not vacated the quarters but sought time and appellant granted time to vacate quarter on or before 30-6-1992 - Later, deceased employee died and his wife respondent no.1 continued to remain in occupation of quarter along with her family members without vacating the quarter - Appellant filed suit in year 2001 for eviction of respondents from quarters and also claimed damages payable from 30-6-1992 - Upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court vide judgment dt.30-7-2012 decreed appellant's suit and passed eviction decree against respondents by holding inter alia that suit is maintainable and that there existed relationship of landlord and tenant between appellant and deceased employee - Aggrieved, respondents filed revision before HC and by impugned order, HC set aside judgment and decree of Trial Court on grounds, inter alia, that civil suit at instance of appellant is not maintainable for want of appellant's locus and that such suit could be filed by State Govt. or/and Labour Commissioner and that there was no relationship of landlord and tenant between appellant and deceased employee - However, HC passed directions to the concerned to take action against erring officials who failed to take any action to obtain possession of quarters from illegal occupants - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether HC is justified in holding that appellant has no locus to file suit against respondents and that only State or/and Labour Commissioner are entitled to file suit for eviction of respondents.

Held, State allotted quarters to appellant under the Act and in turn, appellant allotted said quarters to their workers for their use and occupation, who were in their employment and appellant deducted the rent every month from monthly salary of workers under the Act. Further, there existed relationship of employer and employee between appellant and deceased employee due to which only, the workers were eligible to secure the quarter under the Act as a part of their service conditions. Under such circumstances, it could be held that there was tenancy agreement between the parties and so, appellant was competent to file civil suit against worker for his eviction from the quarter allotted to him on strength of such agreement by taking recourse to the provisions of the Act. The breaches alleged by appellant against respondents in the suit rendered the worker and all those claiming through him liable to suffer the eviction order because such breaches were rightly held proved by Trial Court. Further, possession of quarters by deceased employee became illegal on and after 12-1-1992 when he retired from service because on said date, tenancy in relation to suit quarter also came to an end and thereafter it became unauthorized on and after 30-6-1992. The respondents also had no independent right to remain in occupation of the quarter in question because they were neither in the employment of the appellant and nor were the allottees under the Act so as to entitle them to remain in possession on their own rights. Hence, Trial Court was justified in recording findings against respondents and passing decree for eviction and recovery of rent by way of damages against respondents and so, same are upheld and impugned order of HC is set aside and that of Trial Court is restored. Appeals disposed of.
