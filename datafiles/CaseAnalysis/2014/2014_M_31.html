Mahesh Dhanaji Shinde v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 126; (2014) 4 SCC 292; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1535; 2014 CRLJ 1739; JT 2014 (3) SC 449; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 90; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 947; 2014(3) SCALE 96; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, death penalty, Mitigating Circumstance, Commutation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, 120-B, ss. 120-B, 302, 364A - Arms Act, 1959 - Murder - Conviction - Death penalty - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) were charged for the offence of kidnapping and murder and also under the Act - Trial Court convicted appellants u/ss. 302/120B of IPC and imposed death penalty on the appellant - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment upholding that conviction imposed by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that in absence of any direct evidence the prosecution not only has to prove that circumstances incriminating to accused had been laid before the Court but further that the sum total of such evidence unerringly points to the commission of alleged offence by accused leaving no room for any other view and that the parts of evidence of material witnesses were not free from doubt and ambiguity and were tainted on account of embellishments and improvements and that no circumstance that implicates the appellants, much less a chain of circumstances which admits of no other possibility except the guilt of accused, was established by prosecution -

Held, evidence and other materials on record made it clear that appellants were known to each other - It was deliberately circulated and spread by appellants that appellant (accused no. 1) was gifted with super-natural powers of causing money showers i.e. multiplying money - Evidence on record established that accused had been persuading people, including the victims, to arrange for cash money and bring the same to them at 'M' or 'K' so that the same could be multiplied - Accordingly, victims, including the deceased persons, after obtaining cash money from different sources, had come to 'M' or 'K' and they were put up in different lodges/hotels by accused - Prosecution had also established that while staying in hotels/lodges the victims and accused did not use their real names - Specifically, the prosecution evidence shows that accused no. 2 arranged for conveyance and stay of the victims whereas accused no. 2 3 had assisted accused no. 2 in shifting the victims from the lodges to the place where the crimes were committed - In instant case prosecution succeeded in proving a series of highly incriminating circumstances involving the accused all of which, if pieced together, could point only to one direction, namely, that it was the appellants and nobody else who had committed the crimes in question - Appellants were guilty of commission of offences alleged including the offence u/s. 302 of IPC r/w s. 120-B of IPC - Appellants have committed the murder of as many as 9 innocent and unsuspecting victims who were led to believe that accused no. 1 had magical powers to multiply money - Deceased, after being killed, were robbed of the cash amounts that they had brought with them for the purpose of 'money shower' - Criminal acts of accused were actuated by greed for money and such acts were the result of a carefully planned scheme - Crimes were committed over a period of nearly 2 months in 3 different episodes - The assaults on some of the victims were merciless and gruesome - Balancing 2 sets of circumstances , one favouring commutation and the other favouring upholding the death penalty, in instant case option of life sentence was not 'unquestionably foreclosed' - Thus, sentence of death awarded to accused should be commuted to life imprisonment - Appellants should undergo imprisonment for life for commission of offence u/s. 302/120B of IPC - Appeals disposed of.
