Â Illias v Collector of Customs, Madras

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 4; AIR 1970 SC 1065; 1970 CRLJ 998; 1985 ECR 2252; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1427; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 133; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 613

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962, s. 104 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 162 and 163 - Question was asked whether statements made by appellants and other accused under new Act were admissible in evidence u/s. 120 B of IPC r/w s. 135 of new Act - Held that a Custom's Officer is not a Police Officer - Powers of officer were increased under new Act - Statements made before him by a person who is arrested or against whom an inquiry is made are not covered by s. 25 of Evidence Act - Appeal dismissed.
