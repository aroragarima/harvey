Thandi Ram v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1166; (2000) SCC (Cr) 189; (2000) 1 SCC 318; AIR 2000 SC 468; 1999 (1) AllCriC 850; 1999 (2) AllCriR 990; 1999 (2) AllCriR 1950; 1999 (3) AWC 1986A; 2000 CRLJ 588; JT 1999 (3) SC 231; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 857; 1999 (5) SLT 321

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18,50, 52, 55 and 57 - Possession of opium without licence - Whether provisions of ss. 50, 52, 55 and 57 of the Act had not been complied with? - High Court was of the opinion that the officer concerned having accidentally recovered the article in question, the question of following s. 50 did not arise - Appellant relied on Balbir Singh case (1994 Indlaw SC 79) and Mohinder Kumar v. State, Panaji - Held, conclusion of the HC
