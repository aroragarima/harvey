Munshi Singh Gautam (D) and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 970; (2005) 9 SCC 631; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1269; AIR 2005 SC 402; 2005 CRLJ 320; JT 2004 (10) SC 547; 2004 (3) KLT 971; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 361; 2004(9) SCALE 390; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 1092

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Custodial Death, Death In Police Custody

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Custodial Torture and Death - IPC, 1860, ss. 330, 302 and 201 - Constitution, art. 21 - On the night intervening 19th and 20th June, 1984 to extort a confession from one Shambhu Tyagi (deceased), he was brought to the police station where he was beaten as a result of which he died and thereafter to remove the traces of the crime and conceal the acts, the dead body was thrown near a Nala - Presence of two persons in the police station they being complainant in relation to one scooter theft, on their disclosure police officers/accused reached deceased's home at 1:30 a.m., from where he was brought to the police station - Per prosecution, accused-police officers forged the Rojnamacha report to conceal the crime by recording that they received an information that some person was lying in the Nala bed and the said person was intoxicated badly - Trial - Trial Court after recording the evidence and hearing the parties found each of the accused persons guilty and sentenced them - The trial Court convicted each of the accused persons for offences punishable under Sections 304 Part I, 330 and 201 IPC, sentencing each to undergo RI for 7 years, 3 years and 2 years respectively - Appeal preferred dismissed by High Court - Precedents referred on custodial death, deliberations - One accused expired during pendency of appeal before HC, thus appeal against him was held to have been abated - Conviction by Trial Court and upheld by HC, whether proper? - Evidence of witnesses PW12 and PW14, not found to be cogent and thus insuficient to fasten guilt on the accused persons/police officers - Court noted contradictory pleas taken by one of the accused police officer, wherein he took definite plea as that deceased was lying injured near the Nala and information to that effect was received at the police station and the statement made by him before PW1 Doctor was entirely different - Effect of a false stand being taken in case of custodial death was considered by this Court in Sahadevan alias Sagadevan v. State rep. by Inspector of Police, Chennai - Plea that the deceased had come to the police station in a severe condition and after telling his name has collapsed gets falsified by the categorical statement made by the accused in his statement u/s 313 of CrPC to the effect that on receiving information where the deceased was lying unconscious in injured state - Held, the case being one of custodial torture, accusations have been established so far as accused/appellant Gulab Singh Chaudhury(GSC) is concerned -Nature of injuries taken note of - Held further, conviction u/s 304 Part II to be awarded - Appeal of GSC dismissed - In respect of other accused persons appeal allowed in view of prosecution has not been able to bring home the accusations - Appeals disposed of.
