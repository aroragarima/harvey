Kulwinder Pal Singh and another v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 367; (2016) 6 SCC 532; AIR 2016 SC 2281; 2016 (4) AWC 3913; 2016 (150) FLR 211; 2016 (5) MLJ 132; 2016(5) SCALE 193; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 439; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Backward Classes, Vacancy, Selection, Vacancies, Representation, Examination, Merit, Posts, Public Service Commission, Advertisement, Temporary, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Right to, Appointment, Punjab Scheduled Castes and Backward Classes (Reservation in Services) Act, 2006., Constitution vide Eighty First (Amendment) Act, 2000

Summary: Service - Filling up of vacancies - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC contending that three vacancies that remained unfilled due to non-joining of three candidates could have been offered to them as they were next in order of merit - However, HC held that appellants had no right to be appointed against said vacancies and also held that candidates much more than vacancies advertised were permitted to join and thus select list of 2007-2008 stands exhausted and dismissed appeals - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellants could claim right to get appointed against three vacancies.

Held, three resultant vacancies of the year 2007-2008 stood consumed with the joining of the 17 candidates in pursuance of orders of SC, and so, the same could not be filled up from the select list of the year. The decision of Administrative Committee observing that the three resultant vacancies stood consumed is based on factual situation arising there and is not arbitrary. Further, HC was right in finding fault with the de-reservation of the seven posts which were filled by candidates belonging to general category and interference in the same is not warranted. Merely because some persons were granted benefit illegally or by mistake, it does not confer right upon appellants to claim equality. Hence, HC rightly held that candidates much more than vacancies advertised have already been permitted to join and thus the appellants could not claim any legal right in respect of the posts of reserved category remaining unfilled. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Merely because the name of a candidate finds place in the select list, it would not give him indefeasible right to get an appointment as well.
