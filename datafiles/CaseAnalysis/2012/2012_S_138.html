Rameshbhai Dabhai Naika v State Of Gujarat & Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 48; (2012) 3 SCC 400; 2012(2) ALL MR 70; 2012 (91) ALR 251; 2012 (3) AWC 2380; 2012 (3) Bom.C.R. 139; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 192; JT 2012 (1) SC 515; 2012 (1) KLT 383; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 332; 2012 (1) OLR 602; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 254; 2012(1) SCALE 434; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Tribal Certificate - Cancelled - Entitlement - Sustainability - Appeal was preferred against orders passed by Scrutiny Committee cancelling Tribal certificate obtained by appellant on sole ground that his father was non-tribal belonging to Hindu caste Kshatriya - HC did not refer to any evidences adduced by appellant, on the strength of decisions passed by SC in previous cases upheld decision of Scrutiny Committee - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that his mother was undeniably a Nayak and he and his other siblings were married to Nayaks and further, HC did not refer to evidences adduced on question of his upbringing as a member of Nayak community - Appellant further contended that HC considered only fact that his father was non-tribal and declined to record any findings - What would be status of a person, one of whose parents belonged to scheduled castes/scheduled tribes and other came from upper castes - Held, SC had held that offspring of an inter-caste marriage or a marriage between tribal and non-tribal would take his/her caste from father, but it did not rule out possibility of child from an inter-caste marriage taking his/her caste status from mother - Presumption by no means was conclusive or irrebuttable and it was open to child of such marriage to lead evidence to show that he/she was brought up by mother who belonged to scheduled caste/scheduled tribe -- In the instant case, tribal certificate was taken away from appellant without adverting to any evidences and on sole ground that he was son of a Kshatriya father - Orders passed by HC and Scrutiny Committee, therefore, could not be sustained and hence, set aside - Case was remitted to Scrutiny Committee to take a fresh decision on the basis of evidences that might be led by two sides - Appeal allowed.
