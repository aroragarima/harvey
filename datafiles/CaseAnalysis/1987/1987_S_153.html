Sri K. Prasad And Ors. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28431; (1988) Supp SCC 269; (1988) SCC (L&S) 710; AIR 1988 SC 535; 1988 (7) ATC 632; JT 1987 (4) SC 731; 1988 (1) LLJ 485; 1989 (1) LLN 575; 1987(2) SCALE 1343; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 285; 1988 (1) SLR 549

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3 - Forest Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1966, rr. 1, 4(3-A), 4(1) and 4(2) First Proviso - Initial Recruitment Regulation, Regns. 4, 5 and 6 - Forest Service Cadre Rules, 1966, r. 4(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - All India Forest Service - Initial recruitment - Quashing of first selection - Boards set up - Fixation of cadre strength Regulations u/r. 4(1), Regn. 2 and Sch. (A) Comparatively more selections by new - But restricted to those eligible as on 01-10-1966 - Challenged - (B) Cadre strength - Expansion - Inference of - (C) Restriction on number of officers to be appointed under schedule to Cadre Regulations - Applicability of - (D) Appointment to posts, junior or senior, in excess of stated number - Validity of - (E) Seniority of officers - Fixation of - All officers adjudged suitable for appointment at initial recruitment take precedence over persons appointed u/r. 4(2)of Recruitment Rules - Officers adjudged suitable exceeding number of posts available - (F) Whether fresh selection can be done only once - (G) Selection Board did not consider all eligible Officer but some and made omnibus observation that others were found unsuitable - (H) Constitution - Laches - Officers of State Forest Service eligible for being considered for initial recruitment to All India Forest Service, not considered, guilty of laches in challenging non-consideration - It is not open to challenge - Cannot be inferred merely on basis of appointment of number of officers in excess of prescribed strength as prescribed procedure of consulting State Govt. and amending regulations must be followed - Applies to such recruitment also - Excess appointments are not affected - Immaterial - Not restricted to once, can be repeated twice - This is not in proper compliance with rules, selection set aside - Held, having regard to complicated nature of issues involved, they should not be put out of court - Order accordingly.
