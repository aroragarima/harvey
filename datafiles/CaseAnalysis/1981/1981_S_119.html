Laxmi Khandsari Etc. Etc. v State Of U.P. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 558; (1981) 2 SCC 600; AIR 1981 SC 873; 1981(1) SCALE 455; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Trade - Agriculture and Agri Products - Prohibition Act - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 39 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - U P Khandsari Sugar Manufacturers Licensing Order, 1967 - Sugar-cane (Control) Order, 1966, cl. 8 - In order to facilitate production by sugar-mills most of whom were controlled by state, a reserved area of fields growing sugar-cane was fixed throughout State - Sugar cane (Control) Order, 1966 applied only to reserved areas of a mill and not to any other areas - Owners of power crushers of khandsari units had taken out regular licenses under U P Khandsari Sugar Manufacturers Licensing Order of 1967 - Therefore, power crushers owned or worked by Petitioners were governed by conditions of licenses under which they were working crushers - (A) Whether notification violative of art.19(1)(g)? - Held, Fundamental rights are neither absolute nor unlimited but are subject to reasonable restrictions which may be imposed by State in public interest under cls. (2) to (6) of art.19 - Reasonable restrictions would depend on nature and circumstances of case - Further, restrictions may be partial, complete, permanent or temporary but they must bear a close nexus with object in interest of which they are imposed - Care should be taken to see that real purpose which is sought to be achieved by restricting rights of citizens is subserved - In determining reasonableness of restrictions imposed by law in filed of industry, trade or commerce, mere fact that some of persons engaged in a particular trade may incur loss due to imposition of restrictions will not render them unreasonable - Price prevailing in free market must be taken into account in formula of fixation of price for essential commodities - On a parity of reasoning, therefore, a restriction on right of a trader dealing in essential commodities like ban in instant case or fixation of prices aimed at bringing about distribution of essential commodities keeping consumers interests as prime consideration, cannot be regarded as unreasonable - Furthermore, as power is vested in a very high authority, it cannot be assumed that it is likely to be abused - Moreover, power cannot be said to be arbitrary or unguided because impugned notification derives its course from S 3 of Essential commodities Act of 1955 which clearly lays down sufficient guide-lines and existence of certain conditions for proper distribution of an essential commodity - (B) Whether violative of art.14 as it differentiates between vertical and horizontal power crushers without any rhyme or reasons? - Held, as far as word vertical used in impugned notification is concerned, it must be struck down as being violative of art.14 - This, however does not render entire notification avoid because word vertical used in notification is clearly severable from other portions of notification - All that has to be done is to read notification without word vertical as a result of which exemption from ban will include all owners of power crushers whether vertical or horizontal - (C) Whether Control Order violates principles of natural justice inasmuch as it was passed without hearing Petitioners whose valuable rights were involved? - Held, procedural pre-condition of fair hearing, however minimal even post decisional has relevance to administrative and judicial gentlemanliness - Need to insist on observance of natural justice in area of administrative decision making so as to avoid devaluation of this principle and rationale of audi alteram partem - If hearing was to be given to so many owners of power crushers, it would have completely defeated and frustrate very object not only of notification but also of Essential Commodities Act of 1955 and created complication which may have resulted in a further deterioration of an already serious situation - After all notification directed stoppage of operation of petitioners crushers only for a very short period - Petitioners were, therefore, not seriously prejudiced and have rushed to this Court rather prematurely - Petition dismissed.
