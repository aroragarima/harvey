Yusufbhai Noormohammed Jodhpurwala v Mohmmed Sabir Ibrahim Byavarwala

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 890; (2015) 6 SCC 526; AIR 2015 SC 1131; 2015(1) ALL MR 974; 2016 (1) G.L.R. 377; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 839; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 393; 2015 (1) RCR(Rent) 33; 2014(12) SCALE 589

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Decree For Eviction, Bombay Rent Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act 1947, s. 12 - Right of tenant - Petitioner issued a legal notice to the respondent-tenant stating that the tenant was in arrears of rent for more than six months - Eviction suit was filed under various grounds including arrears of rent u/ss. 12(3)(a), 12(3)(b) of the Act, bonafide requirement, alteration in the suit premises, and causing nuisance to the landlord - Suit was dismissed - In appeal, the appellate bench held that the ground of eviction u/s. 12(3) (b) of the Act was made out - HC by the impugned judgment reversed the judgment of the First Appellate Court and it was held that there was substantial compliance of s. 12(3)(b) of the Act to escape eviction on the ground of non payment of arrears of rent - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the right that is conferred upon tenant by the legislature can be availed of only twice. In the instant case, tenant was in arrears of rent prior to the filing of the suit and continued to be so. On the date of the first hearing of the suit, the rent that was paid fell short by Rs.270/. Therefore, HC was wrong in interpreting s. 12(3)(b) of the Act purposively holding that so long as the HC, in its discretion, feels that there is a readiness and willingness on the part of the tenant to pay rent, the HC can in its discretion say that substantial compliance of s. 12(3)(b) of the Act is good enough for the tenant to escape eviction on the ground of non payment of arrears of rent. The order of the First Appellate Court restored. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - Right conferred upon tenant u/s. 12(3)(b) of the Act by the legislature can be availed of only twice.
