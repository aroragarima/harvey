Shaji & Ors. v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 301; (2011) 5 SCC 423; AIR 2011 SC 1825; 2011 (4) CGLJ 121; 2011 CRLJ 2935; 2011 (3) JCC 1989; JT 2011 (6) SC 623; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 114; 2011 (3) RLW 2200; 2011(5) SCALE 278; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 210

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143, 147, 148, 149, 302 and 449 - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court held A-1 to A-4 guilty of the offences punishable u/ss. 143, 147, 148, 342, 449 and 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Conviction and sentence was confirmed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether in order to bring home the charge u/s. 149 of IPC, it was not necessary that five or more persons must necessarily be brought before the Court and convicted? - It was contended that in the instant case out of six named persons, two were acquitted by the Trial Court and only four were convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC - Held, in view of SC decision in Mohan Singh & Anr. Vs. State of Punjab, 1962 INDLAW SC 149, in the instant case, even after acquittal of two accused from all the charges leveled against them, if there was any material that they were members of the unlawful assembly, the conviction u/s. 302 could be based with the aid of s. 149 of IPC - (B) Whether the prosecution established the conviction of the appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC? - Held, in order to attract s. 149 of IPC, it must be shown that the incriminating act was done to accomplish the common object of unlawful assembly and it must be within the knowledge of other members as one likely to be committed in prosecution of the common object - In the absence of such a finding as also any overt act on the part of the accused persons, mere fact that they were armed would not be sufficient to prove common object - In the instant case, ingredients of s. 149 of IPC have not been fulfilled or established by the prosecution insofar as the accused other than A-1 - Trial Court and the HC committed an error in convicting the A-2 to A-4 u/s. 302 with the aid of s. 149 of IPC - Conviction and sentence set aside - Appeals disposed of.
