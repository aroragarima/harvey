Thungabhadra Industries Limited v Government of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 195; AIR 1964 SC 1372; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Refusing, Supreme Court Rules 1950

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Constitution, 1950, art. 133(1) - Supreme Court Rules, 1950 - Turnover and Assessment Rules, rr. 5(k), 5(1)(g) and 18 - Whether common order passed by High Court of Andhra Pradesh rejecting applications to review an earlier order by that court, is correct on facts of this case? - Held, whether 'hydrogenated groundnut oil' was 'groundnut oil' entitled to deduction of purchase turnover u/r. 18(2) of Turnover and Assessment Rules - Where without any elaborate argument one could point to error and say here is a substantial point of law which stares one in face, and there could reasonably be no two opinions entertained about it, a clear case of error apparent on face of record would be made out - Learned Judges were in error in rejecting application for review and petitions for review should have been allowed - Appeal allowed.
