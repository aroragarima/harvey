Syscon Consultants Private Limited v Primella Sanitary Production Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 664; (2016) 10 SCC 353; AIR 2016 SC 4564; 2017 (1) ALD(SC) 38; 2016 (119) ALR 529; 2016 (6) AWC 6091; 2016 (4) CLT(SC) 60; JT 2016 (9) SC 267; 2017 (2) MahLJ 124; 2016 (7) MLJ 820; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 590; 2017 (134) RD 167; 2016(9) SCALE 26

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Agreement For Sale, Vendor, Auction, Contract, Specific Performance, CONTRACT & COMMERCIAL, Performance, Specific Performance Of, Suit For Specific Performance, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, To Perform, Marketable Title, Portuguese Civil Code, 1867, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Portuguese Civil Code, 1867 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 44 - Suit for specific performance - Preemption of rights - Validity - Plaintiff/respondent no. 1 filed suit for specific performance of agreement dt. 04-09-1985 made with defendant nos. 1 to 6 for conveyance of suit property - Defendant no. 7 was the Bank where defendants had mortgaged suit property - Plaintiff's suit was dismissed for default and was restored on 05-02-1994 - By an amendment on 4-11-1995 defendant nos. 7 and 8 sold suit property in inventory proceedings to defendant no. 9 - Plaintiff sought amendment in suit for cancelling the sale - Trial Court disposed of both suits upholding right of defendant nos. 7 and 8 and directed defendant nos. 1 to 6 to refund the advance of Rs. 50 thousand with interest @ 21 per cent p.a. - Defendant nos. 1 to 8 were further directed to refund an amount of Rs.17 lakhs to plaintiff with interest @ 6 per cent p.a. - On appeal, HC partly allowed appeal to the extent of share of defendant nos.1 to 6 in suit property permitting defendants to withdraw the amount deposited in Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether defendants no.7 and 8 prove that they have right of preemption in respect of suit property and that agreement dt. 4-9-85 entered between plaintiff and defendant nos. 1 to 6 was null and void.

Held, defendant nos. 1 to 6 failed or neglected to complete the sale even after clear title was made out when the obstacle of mortgage was removed. Having clearly opted throughout to enforce specific performance, SC was of view that justice of the case requires that Cl. 9 must be applied in favour of plaintiff. After inducing plaintiff as per PW-1/F letter to pay Rs.17 lakhs to bank to clear the dues on understanding that defendant nos. 1 to 8 would thereafter execute the sale deed they cannot go back. The clear title stood made out at that stage and agreement was enforceable thereafter. Therefore, defendant nos. 1 to 8 had already given up on their right in suit property by not taking steps to avoid the distress sale at the instance of Bank. Despite specifically getting reserved a liberty to proceed further after the redemption of property by plaintiff nothing was done by defendants. They also did not exercise their right of preemption available under the Portuguese Law. Conspicuously, none of the defendants entered the witness box despite the voluminous and clinching evidence tendered by plaintiff to avoid inconvenient questions, particularly, based on PW1/F. Therefore, no substantial or grave injustice is caused to defendants on contrary justice of the case, on facts, is in favour of plaintiff, and no interference u/art. 136 of Constitution was required. Once, it is found that justice of the case on facts does not require interference, SC even at appellate stage, is well within its discretion to stay its hands off, as held in Taherakhatoon (D) by Lrs. v. Salambin Mohammad, 1999 Indlaw SC 983. Thus, viewed from any angle, justice was done to plaintiff as per the decree granted to them by HC and no injustice is caused to defendants, in particular, defendant no. 9, who, with open eyes, purchased litigation. SC decided not to interfere with the judgment of HC and also dismiss plaintiff's appeal against impugned judgment seeking the entire property. Appeals dismissed.
