Radhey Shyam Ratanlal and Another v Commissioner of Customs (Adjudication), Mumbai

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 645; (2009) 13 SCC 157; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1411; 2009 (238) E.L.T. 14; JT 2009 (13) SC 36; 2009(7) SCALE 713; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 954

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Place Of Importation, Undervaluation, Bill Of Export, Delivery At The Time And Place Of Importation, Rate Of Exchange, Special Discount, Time And Place Of Importation Or Exportation, Out Of Charge Order

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 14(1) (a), 47, 130 E - Customs Valuation Rules, 1988, rr. 4, 5, 10A - Confiscation - Penalty - Assessable value - Transaction value - Undervaluation - Show cause notice issued alleging undervaluation as appellants imported cloves, invoiced by M/s. Ketan Trading Co.at Unit Price of US Dollars 2600 PMT CIF, although prevailing international price of cloves during relevant period was around US Dollars 5500 - Appellant contended that transaction value was based on price agreed upon which was contractual in nature, and therefore, said price should have been accepted as value of goods - Respondent contended that appellant could not produce original contract said to have been entered into between M/s. Ketan Trading Company and appellant in which price of goods was allegedly agreed upon at US Dollars 2600 PMT and document of M/s. Ketan Trading Co. is not a contract and therefore no reliance could and should be placed on said document - Respondent further contended that document of M/s. Ketan Trading Co also not accepted as it is indicated from comparable rate that other persons and firms purchased same goods almost at same relevant time at a higher rate- On appeal, Commissioner held that value for purpose of assessment of cloves imported under four Bills of Entry should be US Dollars 5500 PMT and for balance six Bills of Entry, value for assessment of cloves imported by Appellants should be @US$ 5600 PMT (CIF) - Appellants preferred an Appeal in Tribunal which was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether price of goods at time of computation should be deciding factor in arriving at value of goods or it is price agreed upon by parties? - Deemed value contemplated u/s. 14(1) would prevail when price declared does not reflect price at which such or like goods are ordinarily sold or offered for sale for delivery at time and place of importation - Held, appellant although claimed a transaction value, but such value could not be supported by production of original contract or invoices relating to procurement of cloves - Alleged contract dated 23-11-2000 cannot be termed as a contract between parties and it is merely a certificate issued by M/s. Ketan Trading Co - Moreover, documents like Weekly Bulletin of Spices Market and also Public Ledger also indicates that price of Zanzibar cloves had reached Rs.6100/- PMT - It is also established that nature of business of M/s. Ketan Trading Co. is primarily dealing with sport goods and not cloves - Hence, Department correctly imposed proper assessment value on goods in question imported by appellant - Confiscation and penalty upheld - Appeal dismissed.
