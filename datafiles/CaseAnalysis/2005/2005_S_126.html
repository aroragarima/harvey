State of Punjab v Sawaran Singh

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 426; (2005) 6 SCC 101; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1359; AIR 2005 SC 3114; 2005 CRLJ 3445; JT 2005 (6) SC 456; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 785; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 889; 2005(5) SCALE 601; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Narcotics, Drugs & Psychotropic Substances, 1985, s. 18 - Conviction u/s. 18 - Challenged - Accused was found carrying a plastic bag which contained opium - Trial Court held that the recovery of opium was fully proved and convicted appellant - Said conviction order was challenged by the accused before HC who set aside conviction on the ground that contents of affidavits of 2 official witnesses not put to accused while he was examined u/s 313 Cr.P.C - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld? - Held, accused was not in any way prejudiced by not giving him an opportunity to answer specifically regarding the evidence of PW 1 and PW 4 as evidence of PW 1 and PW 4 was recorded in his presence, he had the opportunity to cross-examine the witnesses but he did not specifically cross-examine these two witnesses in respect of the facts deposed by them - HC erred in holding that the evidence of PW 1 and PW 4 could not have been used against the accused - Hence, acquittal of the accused was improper as the evidence in this case clearly established that the accused was in possession of 5 Kg of opium and thereby committed the offence u/s. 18 of the NDPS Act - Appeal allowed.
