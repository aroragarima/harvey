Rameshchandra Rampratapji Daga v Rameshwari Rameshchandra Daga

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1048; (2005) 2 SCC 33; AIR 2005 SC 422; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 62; 2005 (58) ALR 296; 2005 (1) AWC 150; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 834; 2005 (2) G.L.R. 939; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 306; JT 2004 (10) SC 366; 2005 (1) KLT 188; 2005 (2) MLJ(SC) 49; 2005 (1) RCR(Civil) 615; 2004(10) SCALE 391; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 888; 2005 (1) UC 586

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, s. 125 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Matrimonial proceedings - Whether second marriage is null and void? - whether wife is entitled to maintenance after Court held that marriage was nullity? - Held, a Hindu marriage can be dissolved only in accordance with provisions of Act by obtaining a decree of divorce from Court - A bigamous marriage may be declared illegal being in contravention of provisions of Act but it cannot be said to be immoral so as to deny even right of alimony or maintenance to a spouse financially weak and economically dependant - HC's judgment granting decree of declaration of marriage as nullity and granting maintenance to wife and daughter are maintained.
