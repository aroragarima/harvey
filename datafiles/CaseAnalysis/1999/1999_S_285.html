State of Bihar and another v Dr. Braj Kumar Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 489; (1999) 9 SCC 546; AIR 2000 SC 106; JT 1999 (8) SC 634; 2000 (2) SLJ 119; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 333

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Education - University - Promotion to post of Professor under time bound promotion scheme - What is effective retrospective date of promotion? - Respondent 1(R1) appointed as lecturer on 16 - 08 - 1967 and obtained Phd degree on 14 - 02 - 1974 - Promoted to post of reader on 1991 w. e. f. 14 - 11 - 1980 - R1 than selected by Screening Committee under 16 years scheme for promotion to post of professor in 1990 with retrospective effect and vide notification in that behalf D/ - 15 - 04 - 1990 he was promoted on provisional basis to such post with effect from 01 - 02 - 1985 subject to approval of Bihar State University (Constituent Colleges) Services Commission(Commission) - Commission had not taken any decision on promotion of R1, thus Commission sought new Screening Committee to be constituted - R1 was compelled to apply once again with new Screening Committee, for his promotion to post-R1 was again selected but only under 25 years scheme - Hence grievance - Whether R1 eligible for such promotion with effect from 01-02-1985 after completion of 16 years of continuous service or with effect from 16-09-1992 on basis of 25 years of continuous service? - Two schemes for time bound promotion to post viz 15 years scheme and 25 years scheme noticed and perused - HC accepted case of R1 - Primary contention of appellant was that that HC should have only set aside petition and it should have remitted matter to Commission for its consideration as to whether R1 possessed requisite qualification or not - Contention found to be having force, but in view of peculiar circumstances of case Court perused requisite qualifications for post of professor, and gave finding that appellant was unable to put forth material which negated that R1 was not having such qualification - No reason found by Court in resolution of Screening committee as to why 25 years criteria was taken and not 16 years - Held, R1 eligible with effect from 01 - 02 - 1985 - Impugned judgment held to be passed in peculiar facts of case and not be be treated as precedent - Appeal dismissed.
