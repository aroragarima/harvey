Rajendra Pratap Singh Yadav and others v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 407; (2011) 7 SCC 743; AIR 2011 SC 2737; 2012 (6) AWC 6096; JT 2011 (7) SC 255; 2011(7) SCALE 22; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 910; 2011 (4) SLR 676; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3387

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Armed Forces, Uttar Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1942, Uttar Pradesh Non-Technical (Class II/Group-B) Services (Appointment of Demobilised Officers) Rules, 1980, U.P. Non-Technical (Class-II) Services (Reservation of Vacancies for Demobilised Officers) Rules, 1973

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - U.P. Non-Technical (class-II) Services (Reservation of Vacancies for Demobilised Officers) Rules, 1973 - Seniority - Appellants filed writ petition submitting appellants and respondent no. 4 were direct recruits to the U.P. Provincial Police Service and appellants were 4 to 10 years senior to respondent no. 4, who was selected and appointed in the year 1994 as Deputy Superintendent of Police - Appellants also submitted that respondent no. 4 was given benefit of his past service in the Indian Army as a Short Service Commissioned Officer of 8 years as per 1973 Rules, issued by the State Government - Appellants further submitted that the persons who joined the Army service after cessation of the foreign aggression and revocation of emergency like respondent no. 4 cannot be treated like persons who have joined the Army during emergency due to foreign aggression like appellants and similar benefits cannot be given to such persons - HC dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether a Short Service Commissioned Officer who was commissioned in the Army during the normal period was entitled to the certain benefits given to the Army officers who were commissioned during the emergency when the nation was at war with the foreign enemy? - Held, respondent no. 4 and similarly placed employees could not have been given the benefit of the 1973 Rules as said Rules ceased to exist after 5 years i.e. on 5.8.1978 and were not in existence when they were appointed - Therefore, they could not have derived any benefit from the 1973 Rules - State was directed to prepare fresh seniority list - Appeals disposed of.
