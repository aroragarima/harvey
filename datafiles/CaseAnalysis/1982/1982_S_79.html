Hari Ram v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 76; (1983) SCC (Cr) 159; (1983) 1 SCC 193; AIR 1983 SC 185; 1983 CRLJ 346; 1983 CRLR 122; 1982(2) SCALE 1337

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction of - Legality - Held, the facts do not make out an offence u/s. 302 against appellant and a conviction under second part of s. 304 is called for instead of - In heat of altercation between Ran Singh on one hand and appellant and his comrades on other, appellant seized a jelli and thrust it into chest of the deceased and it was preceded by his remark that the deceased must be beaten to make him behave - On evidence it does not appear that there was any intention to kill the deceased - Conviction u/s. 302 cannot be sustained and, on contrary, facts make out an offence under second part of s. 304 - Appeal allowed.
