Buddhadeb Ruidas and others v State of West Bengal and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 506; (2013) 12 SCC 221; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 45; 2013 (139) FLR 487; JT 2013 (11) SC 370; 2013(10) SCALE 306; 2013 (5) SLR 467

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: West Bengal Regulation of Recruitment in State Government Establishments and Establishments of Public Undertakings, Statutory Bodies, Government Companies and Local Authorities Act, 1999

Summary: Service - West Bengal Regulation of Recruitment in State Government Establishments and Establishments of Public Undertakings, Statutory Bodies, Government Companies and Local Authorities Act, 1999, s. 4 - Cancellation of selection - Validity - Deputy Secretary (Rev.), Irrigation and Waterways directed the Director of Personnel and Ex-Officio Chief Engineer, Irrigation and Waterways, to issue instructions to call for names of eligible candidates for recruitment to 1446 group 'D' posts - In pursuance of the said direction the departmental authorities in accordance with s. 4 of the Act requisitioned names from employment exchange - 57,437 candidates took part in the selection process - 24520 candidates were sponsored by the employment exchange - Select list of the selected candidates was prepared and the same was published - Respondent Nos. 4 and 5 filed application before the Tribunal assailing the process of selection - Tribunal held that as the Dept. had not advertised the posts in the newspapers and the entire recruitment process had taken place by calling names from the employment exchange and hence, the selection was vitiated - Writ petitions filed before HC were also dismissed - Instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in cancelling the selection on a singular ground, namely, there was no advertisement for direct recruitment - Held, Act provided that the persons were to be selected from the candidates sponsored by the employment exchange - However, it was clarified that though the names were called for from the employment exchange, the process of selection was not restricted to only the sponsored candidates - Further, when thousands of candidates had appeared, though not sponsored by the employment exchange, the panel was prepared after following due procedure - However, vacancies had not been filled up because of pendency of litigation - Hence, State Govt. directed to fill up the posts available from among the select list - Appeals disposed of.
