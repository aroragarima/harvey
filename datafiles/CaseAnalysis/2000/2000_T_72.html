Datar Switchgears Limited v Tata Finance Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 28; (2000) 8 SCC 151; AIR 2000 SCW 3925; 2000 (3) ARBLR 447; 2001 (1) Bom.C.R. 778; 2000 (1) CLC 2141; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 226

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Corporate; Trade

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - No failure of procedure for appointment of Arbitrator - No refusal to appoint Arbitrator - (A) Whether for purposes of s. 11(6), party to whom a demand for appointment is made, forfeits his right to do so if he does not appoint an Arbitrator within 30 days considering arbitration clause gives an unfettered discretion to 1st respondent - lessor to appoint, no notice period is prescribed, and arbitration cl does not speak about any concurrence or consent of other party? - Held, in cases arising u/s. 11(6), if opposite party has not made an appointment within 30 days of demand, right to make appointment is not forfeited but continues, but an appointment has to be made before former files application u/s. 11 seeking appointment of an Arbitrator - Appointment of Arbitrator by respondent is valid - (B) Whether first respondent, in appointing second respondent as Arbitrator, failed to follow procedure, contemplated under agreement or acted in contravention of Arbitration clause? - Further held, it is settled law that Court cannot interpose and interdict appointment of an Arbitrator, whom parties have chosen under terms of contract unless legal misconduct of Arbitrator's fraud, disqualification etc. - Appeal dismissed.
