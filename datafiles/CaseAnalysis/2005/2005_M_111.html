Rajendra Sail v Madhya Pradesh High Court Bar Association and

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 321; (2005) 6 SCC 109; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1401; AIR 2005 SC 2473; 2006 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; 2005 CRLJ 2585; JT 2005 (4) SC 548; 2005 (140) PLR 829; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 718; 2005(4) SCALE 295; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Criticism, Fair Comment

Summary: [A.] Media & Entertainment - Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Contempt - Criticism of judgment - Whether there is contempt or not? - Held, speech that judgment is rubbish and deserves to be thrown in a dustbin cannot be said to be a fair criticism of judgment - Publication amounts to a gross contempt of court - It has serious tendency to undermine confidence of society in administration - Contemptuous statements by a law graduate cannot be regarded as an ill- Tempered or emotional outburst of an uninformed person - Sentence of six month reduced to sentence of one week simple imprisonment - Appeal disposed of.
