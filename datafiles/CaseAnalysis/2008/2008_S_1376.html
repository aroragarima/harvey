Atul Singh and Others v Sunil Kumar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 23; (2008) 2 SCC 602; AIR 2008 SC 1016; 2008 (1) AWC 757; 2009 (6) CompLJ 559; 2008 (2) CTC 856; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 39; JT 2008 (1) SC 279; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 908; 2008(1) SCALE 84; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Continuing

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Prayer for referring the dispute for arbitration under s. 8 - Held, there is no whisper in the petition that the original arbitration agreement or a duly certified copy thereof is being filed along with the application - There was a clear non- compliance of s. 8(2) of 1996 Act which is a mandatory provision and the dispute could not have been referred to arbitration - No order for referring the dispute to arbitration could have been passed in the suit - Appeal allowed.
