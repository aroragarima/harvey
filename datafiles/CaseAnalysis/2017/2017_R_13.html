Orissa Lift Irrigation Corporation Limited and another v Rabi Sankar Patro and another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 883

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Interim Orders, Experience, Award, Review, Promotion, Employment, Date, Examinations, Qualifications, EDUCATION, Regular, Admission, Interest, Policy, All India Council For Technical Education Act, 1987, University Grants Commission Act, 1956, Statutory Bodies, Indira Gandhi National Open University Act, 1985, Universities, UGC (institutions deemed to be universities) Regulations, 2010, Orissa Service of Engineers Rules, 1941, Writ Petition, Notice, Post, Training, Affidavit, Bharathiar University Act, 1981, All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) Grant of Approval for starting new technical institutions, introduction of courses or programmes and increase/variation of intake capacity of seats for the courses or programmes and Extension of approval for the existing technical institutions and maintenance of norms and standards in Universities including Deemed to be Universities Regulations, 2005, UGC (the minimum standards of instructions for the grant of the first degree through formal education in the faculties of Arts, Humanities, Fine Arts, Music, Social Sciences, Commerce and Science) Regulations, 1985, AICTE (Grant of Approval for starting new Technical Institutions, introduction of courses or programmes and approval of intake capacity of seats for courses or programmes) Regulations, 1994

Summary: (A) Service - Education - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, ss. 10, 10(1)(k), 10(k), 2(g), 2(h), 20(1) - AICTE (Grant of Approval for starting new Technical Institutions, introduction of courses or programmes and approval of intake capacity of seats for courses or programmes) Regulations, 1994 - Promotion - Deemed Universities degree - Validity - Respondent no.1 joined as Junior Engineer and while in service acquired B.Tech (Civil) Degree from Deemed to be University - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC seeking that he being an in-service graduate Engineer was entitled to be promoted as Assistant Engineer by appellant no. 1 - HC allowed petition and directed appellant no. 1 to consider the case of concerned candidates as in-service graduate Engineers - Aggrieved, appellant no. 1 filed review petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether concerned Deemed to be Universities in instant case could start courses through distance education in subjects leading to award of degrees in Engineering without prior approval under the Act.

Held, if according to All India Council for Technical Education (AICTE) such courses ought not to be taught in distance education mode that was the final word and was binding unless rectified in a manner known to law. Even National Policy on Education laid down that AICTE would be responsible for planning, formulation and maintenance of norms and standards including maintenance of parity of certification and ensuring coordinated and integrated development of technical and management education. Therefore, without Guidelines having been issued in that behalf by AICTE expressly permitting degree courses in Engineering through distance education mode Deemed to be Universities were not justified in introducing such courses. None of the Deemed to be Universities was conferred such status for its excellence in the field of Engineering. Their fields were completely un-related yet, they started courses in Engineering through distance education mode without the approval of AICTE. Deemed to be Universities were required to abide by the provisions of the Regulations and could not introduce courses leading to award of degrees in Engineering without the approval of AICTE. AICTE was directed to devise within one month modalities to conduct appropriate test/tests both in written examination as well as in practicals for concerned students admitted during the academic sessions 2001-2005 covering all the concerned subjects. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Service - Education - All India Council for Technical Education Act, 1987, ss. 10, 10(1)(k), 10(k), 2(g), 2(h), 20(1) - AICTE (Grant of Approval for starting new Technical Institutions, introduction of courses or programmes and approval of intake capacity of seats for courses or programmes) Regulations, 1994 - Distance education - Deemed Universities - Permissibility - Whether Development and Education Committee (DEC) on its own, was competent to grant permission to the concerned Deemed to be Universities to start such courses through distance education.

Held, permissions granted by DEC in the first instance allowing Deemed to be Universities in question to introduce courses leading to the award of degrees in engineering were illegal and opposed to Law. The illegality in the exercise of power was to such an extent that it could not be cured by ex post facto approvals granted later. Further, the exercise of grant of ex post facto approvals, as a matter of fact, was only superficial and perfunctory. Such exercise was done in the face of declared policy statements governing the field and even when specific complaints were received about concerned Deemed to be Universities. The only thing in favour of concerned Deemed to be Universities is the fact that Joint Committee of AICTE and DEC had endorsed the decision though such exercise was also completely flawed. The concerned Deemed to be Universities had gone far beyond their limits and to say the least, had violated binding policy statements. Even when they did not have any experience in the concerned field and had no regular faculty or college in Engineering, they kept admitting students through distance education mode. However, without causing any inspection such power was exercised and the exercise of power was found be suffering from illegality and infirmity. SC restrain all Deemed to be Universities to carry on any courses in distance education mode from academic session 2018- 2019 onwards until it was permissible to conduct such courses in distance education mode and specific permissions were granted by concerned statutory/regulatory authorities in respect of each of those courses and unless the off-campus Centres/Study Centres are individually inspected and found adequate by concerned Statutory Authorities and approvals have to be course specific. Impugned judgments of HC were set aside. Appeals disposed of.
