Â Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Assam v Nandlal Aggarwal & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, P. Satyanarayana Raju, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 216; AIR 1966 SC 899; [1966] 59 ITR 758; 1967 (2) MLJ 8; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 612

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Family Property, Guardian

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 40 and, 66 - Whether Tribunal was justified in assessing income of minors in hands of guardians as income of a Hindu undivided family ? - If a guardian carries on business on behalf of minors and receives income on their behalf, s. 40 of Act must be applied - Till some positive action was taken to have a partition of property, it would remain joint family property - Court under Guardianship Act has no jurisdiction to partition property belonging to a joint Hindu family there are no words in order to warrant such a finding - Held, s. 40 of 1922 Act plainly applies to facts of this case and consequently guardians have to be assessed, treating minors as constituting a Hindu Undivided Family - Appeal allowed.
