Messrs Shree Ram Mills Limited v Messrs Utility Premises Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 225; (2007) 4 SCC 599; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 200; 2007 (2) AWC 1848; JT 2007 (4) SC 501; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 720; 2007(4) SCALE 571; 2007 (6) SCJ 171; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11(6) - Appeal against the order appointing Arbitrators passed by the Designate Judge of the Bombay High Court - Pleas that there was no live issue in existence in between the parties and claim had become barred by limitation between the parties - Whether the Judge was right in passing the order? - Held, Chief Justice or his Designate has to decide those issues because otherwise the arbitration can never proceed - Chief Justice has to decide about the territorial jurisdiction and also whether there exists an arbitration agreement between the parties and whether such party has approached the court for appointment of the Arbitrator - There was no final settlement of the issue regarding 1, 20, 000 sq.ft. of FSI even by the MoU - There was live issue in between the parties - Designate Judge was right in appointing the Arbitrator - Appeal dismissed.
