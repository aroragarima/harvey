Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure (Amendment) Act, 2002, Trial Of Election Petitions, Punjab Municipal Corporation Act, 1976

Summary: [A.] Election - Representation Of The People Act, 1951, s.79 , s.80 , s.87 - Punjab Municipal Corporation Act, 1976, s.384 , s.117 - Constitution Of India, 1950art. 225 , s.86(6) , s.87(1) , s.90(3) - .Representation of People Act, 1951 - Challenge to election of appellant - Delay in filing written statement not condoned - whether rules framed by HC governing trial of election petitions would override provisions of CPC and permit a written statement being filed beyond period prescribed by CPC?; whether time limit of 90 days as prescribed by CPC is mandatory or directory in nature? - Held, Representation of People Act shall prevail over CPC - HC felt satisfied that reason assigned by defendant-appellant in support of prayer for extension of time was good and valid - Written statement shall be taken on record but subject to payment of Rs. 5000/- by way of costs [B.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.129, O. 1 r. 10 , s.O. 6 r. 17 , O. 8 r. 1 - Whether O 8 r.1 of CPC is applicable to trial of an election petition under Chapter II of Act? -Held, rules of procedure contained in CPC apply to trial of election petitions under Act with flexibility and only as guidelines - Appeal allowed.
