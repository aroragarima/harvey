Jai Singh Dalal and Others v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1224; (1993) Supp2 SCC 600; (1993) SCC (L&S) 846; 1993 (24) ATC 788; 1993 (2) CLR 671; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 505; 1993 LabIC 844; 1993 (1) SLR 422; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Eligibility Conditions

Summary: Service - Punjab Civil Services (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930, r. 5 - Appointment - Denial - (A) Whether candiadates have right of appointment on basis of earlier notification, denied same by subsequent notification? - Candidates were selected for appointment to vacancies pursuant to an advertisement did not confer any right to be appointed to post in question to entitle selected to a writ of mandamus or any other writ compelling authority to make appointment - Held, no right had vested in candidates on their names having been entered on select list and it was open to Govt. for good reason not to make appointments therefrom and fill in vacancies - Further held, notification merely amounts to an invitation to qualified candidates to apply for recruitment and on their selection they do not acquire any right to post - (B) Whether State Governemnt has power to withdraw or rescind earlier notification in which appointment was at advance stage? - Yes - Selection by itself would not confer a right to appointment and Govt. may refuse to make appointment for valid reasons - At best Govt. may be required to justify its action on touchstone of art. 14 of Constitution - Further held, State Govt. which has power to specify method of special recruitment by notification has inherent power to revise same if it for good reasons considers same necessary - Appeal dismissed.
