Employers Management West Bokaro Colliery of Tisco Limited v Concerned Workman,Ram Pravesh Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 132; (2008) 3 SCC 729; AIR 2008 SC 1162; 2008 (2) CLR 220; 2008 (2) ESC 246; 2008 (116) FLR 1107; 2008 (2) JhrCR(SC) 5; JT 2008 (2) SC 272; 2008 (2) LLJ 309; 2008 (2) LLJ 892; 2008 (2) LLN 42; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 61; 2008 (3) RLW 2083; 2008(2) SCALE 158; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 247; 2008 (6) SLR 625; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 583

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Show-Cause

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Order of dismissal - Industrial Tribunal held that Management had failed to substantiate charges brought against the concerned workman beyond reasonable doubt - Respondent was ordered to be reinstated with 50% back wages - Writ Petition against dismissed - Appeal against - Held, Tribunal has interfered with the findings recorded by the domestic Tribunal as if it was the Appellate Tribunal - There was evidence present on record regarding indecent, riotous and disorderly behaviour of the respondent towards his superiors - Industrial Tribunal fell in error in discarding the evidence produced by the Management only because the independent witnesses were not produced - While the standard of proof in a criminal case is proof beyond all reasonable doubt, the standard of proof in a departmental proceeding is preponderance of probabilities - Appeal allowed.
