Veruareddi Ramaraghava Reddy and Others v Konduru Seshu Reddy and 2 Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 93; AIR 1967 SC 436; 1967 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 270

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Declaratory Suit, Attorney-General

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Madras Hindu Religious Endowment, ss. 6(17) and 20 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 42 - (A) Whether Act, 1926, Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951 suit is barred u/s. 42 of Specific Relief Act? - Declaration that compromise decree was null and void sought - Worshipper of a Hindu temple is entitled, in certain circumstances, to bring suit for declaration that alienation of temple properties by de jure Shebait is invalid and not binding upon temple - If Shebait has improperly alienated trust property a suit for declaration can be brought, by any person interested, that such alienation is not binding upon deity but no decree for recovery of possession can be made in such a suit unless plaintiff has present right to possession - Worshippers are cestuui que trustent or beneficiaries in a spiritual sense - Since worshippers do not exercise deity's power of suing to protect its own interests, they are not entitled to recover possession of property improperly alienated by Shebait, but a declaratory decree can be granted that alienation is not binding on deity - S. 42 is not exhaustive of cases in which a declaratory decree may be made - Courts have power to grant such a decree independent of requirements of section - Held, suit seeking declaration that compromise decree is not binding upon deity is in itself a substantial relief and thus falls outside purview of s. 42 and will be governed by general provisions of CPC - It is maintainable - (B) Whether compromise decree is invalid for reason that Commr. did not represent deity? - U/s. 20 Commr. is vested with power of superintendence and control over temple but does not have authority to represent deity - Only person who can represent deity or can bring a suit on behalf of deity is Shebait - Where Shebait is negligent or is guilty against whom deity needs relief it is open to worshippers or other persons interested in religious endowment to file suits for protection of trust properties - It is open to deity to file a suit through next friend for recovery of possession of property improperly alienated or for other relief - Next friend may be a worshipper or prospective Shebait legally interested in endowment - Where Shebait has denied deity's right to dedicated properties, deity should file suit through a disinterested next friend, nominated by court - Further held, as there was no representation of deity, compromise decree cannot be binding upon deity - Further held, decree passed by trial court be set aside and plaintiff/respondent No. 1 be granted declaratory decree that compromise decree is not valid and binding on temple - Appeal dismissed.
