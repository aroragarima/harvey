General Manager (P) Punjab and Sind Bank and others v Daya Singh

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 691; (2010) 11 SCC 233; 2010 (6) AWC 5805; 2011 (1) DRTC 18; 2010 (127) FLR 605; JT 2010 (8) SC 436; 2011 (1) LLJ 255; 2010(7) SCALE 521; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Punjab and Sind Bank officer/ employees (Discipline and appeal) Regulation, 1997, Punjab & Sind Bank Officers Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1981

Summary: Service - Punjab & Sind Bank Officers Employees (Conduct) Regulations, 1981 - Dismissal from service - Misconduct - Respondent-Manager of appellant's Branch allegedly has misappropriated Rs.16.48 lac by way of sanctioning demand loans against non-existent FDRs without any security and is absconding from the services since 09.03.1999 - After departmental inquiry respondent was dismissed from services for misconduct - Respondent challenged said order before HC who set aside said order on the ground that no specific finding has been recorded on the basis of the evidence to establish the guilt of the respondent and the absence of good reason was held to be in breach of the principles of natural justice - HC also directed appellant to reinstate the respondent though for the limited purpose of holding the inquiry afresh - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, no - There was a clear documentary evidence on record in the handwriting of the respondent which established his role in the withdrawal of huge amounts in the name of fictitious persons - The ledger entries also clearly showed that whereas the FDRs were in one name, the withdrawals were shown in the name of altogether different persons and they were far in excess over the amounts of FDRs - Respondent had no explanation and, therefore, it had to be held that the respondent had misappropriated the amount - In spite of a well reasoned order by the Inquiry Officer, the HC has interfered therein by calling the same as sketchy - HC has completely overlooked the role of the bank manager - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
