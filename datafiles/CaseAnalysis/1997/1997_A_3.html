Union of India through secretary Ministry of Home Affairs New Delhi and others v A. Ajit Singh S/o S. Chet Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1994; (1997) 6 SCC 50; AIR 1997 SC 2669; 1997 (3) CLT 29; JT 1997 (5) SC 623; 1997(4) SCALE 335; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 1117; 1997 (6) Supreme 39; 1997 (2) UJ 144

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - (A) Apportionment of compensation - Determination of - Whether High Court is correct in affirming apportionment of enhanced compensation to Rs. 28,387 per bigha in 75% to tenant and 25% to landlord? - Held, court is required to take into consideration relevant factors, viz, duration of lease, nature of right to enjoyment of leasehold interest and improvements tenant made on land etc. - It is equally settled law that if Govt. is owner of land, before initiating acquisition, it is entitled to terminate lease and take possession of lands in terms of lease - Apportionment of compensation in ratio of 60% to tenant and 40% to landlord would be a reasonable ratio and payment should accordingly be made - (B) Enhanced solatium - Determination of - Whether HC is correct in applying provisions of Amendment Act 68 of 1984 and awarded enhanced solatium, interest and additional amount? - Held, HC in judgment has noted that lands are situated in developed area and are very near to developed localities belonging to private parties and Govt. - Therefore, it possesses potential value for use for building purposes - Accordingly, it has determined compensation @ Rs. 28,387 per bigha - In view of fact that award of Reference Court is of 02-11-1973, Amendment Act would (sic not) apply and, therefore, claimants are entitled to solatium at 15% and interest at 6% on enhanced compensation from date of taking possession till date of deposit in Court - Further held, determination of market value on basis of above consideration is not vitiated by any error of principle - Appeal allowed.
