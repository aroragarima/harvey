Jhapsa Kabari and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20667; (2001) 10 SCC 94; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1071; AIR 2002 SC 312; 2001 (4) CCR 342; 2002 (1) Crimes 205; 2002 (3) CRJ 384; 2002 CRLJ 587; 2002 (1) JCC 51; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 347; 2001(8) SCALE 372; 2001 (8) SLT 250; 2001 (8) Supreme 536; 2002 (1) UC 407

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148, 302, 364, 380, 449 - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused had committed murder of three persons in a faction ridden village - Accused persons were part of unlawful assembly of about 50 persons and were armed with deadly weapons with common object, they caused death - Seventeen persons were placed on trial having been charged u/s. 302 r/w ss. 34 and 148 IPC for intentionally and knowingly causing death in furtherance of their common intention - Except two accused, who were acquitted, rest were convicted for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC for causing death - HC had confirmed conviction and sentence awarded by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in confirming order of conviction by Trial Court - Held, there was no bar in basing conviction on testimony of solitary witness so long as said witness was reliable and trustworthy - SC had gone through testimony of PW-1 and opined deposition was most natural, reliable and trustworthy - In view, of circumstances there was no infirmity in conviction and sentence of 'B' and 'I' for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC - Insofar as other appellants were concerned, finding of HC was that there was no specific allegations against them - There was no evidence whatsoever to establish that any criminal act was done by them in furtherance of common intention - As above noticed, there were about 50 persons - Nothing was shown before Trial Court & HC or even before SC to establish common intention of remaining accused - Common intention was different than common object - Charge against them was not under s. 149 IPC - Even otherwise, it was not shown that ingredients of s. 149 had been established - There was total absence of evidence both in respect of s. 34 as well as s. 149 IPC - Therefore, it was not possible to confirm conviction of appellants other than 'B' and 'I' - Appeals disposed of.
