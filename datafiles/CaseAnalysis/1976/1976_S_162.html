Bahal Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 541; (1976) SCC (Cr) 461; (1976) 3 SCC 564; AIR 1976 SC 2032; 1976 CRLJ 1568; 1976 CRLR 151

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: CrPC, 1973, s. 386 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Appeal against acquittal - High Court's power to interfere - When view taken by trial Court not perverse but reasonably possible -Guidelines - (B) Evidence of evidence - (1) Criminal trial - Motive - (2) Chance witness - HC held not justified in interfering with it - Even if the genesis or the motive of the occurrence was not proved the ocular testimony of the witnesses as to the occurrence could not be discarded only on that account, if otherwise it was reliable - Such a piece of evidence is not necessarily incredible or unbelievable but does require cautious and close scrutiny - Appeal Allowed.
