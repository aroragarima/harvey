M. M. Malhotra v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 2007; 2005(8) SCALE 202; 2005 (6) SLR 526; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1026

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Air Force Rules, 1969, Court-Martial, Air Force Act, 1950, Air Force (Revised Edition) Regulation, 1964

Summary: Service - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s.11 - Mis-deed - Initiation of enquiry - Compulsory retirement - Proof of plural marriage - Appellant was appointed as Pilot Officer in Indian Air Force - Appellant was married as per Hindu rites and marriage was registered with Registrar of Marriage - Appellant wife lodged complaint with then Chief of Air Staff against mis-deeds of appellant and prayed for maintenance as well as appropriate action against him - On basis of said complaint, Court of enquiry was initiated against appellant by concerned authority - Enquiry continued and after certain stage appellant did not effectively participate - On consideration of materials, it was observed that there was irrefutable evidence of plural marriage and disgraceful conduct but also use of criminal force against his wife - Reference note was submitted to Ministry of Defence and order of compulsory retirement was passed - Same was challenged before HC -who held that he was guilty of act which was prejudicial to good order and discipline in Air Force - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether there was need for remand to authorities to re-consider question of punishment once it was held that plural marriage was not established - Held, there was decision rendered by competent jurisdiction to effect that marriage was subsisting at time when appellant undisputedly married her - For appreciating status of Hindu woman marrying Hindu male with living spouse some of provisions of the Act have to be examined - However, s. 11 of the Act declared such marriage as null and void - Therefore, effect of decree passed was that marriage was void and there was no marriage in eyes of law - Thus, appellant's subsequent marriage could not be said to be case of plural marriage - Further, when foundation for order was partially held not in accordance with law, reconsideration of quantum of punishment could be directed, but that was not invariable rule - Though, scheme of disciplinary rules in general was to identify conduct which was made punishable and then to provide for various punishments which should be imposed for the Acts which were inconsistent with such conduct - Therefore, range of activities which may amount to acts which were inconsistent with interest of public service and not befitting status, position and dignity of public servant were so varied that it would be impossible for employer to exhaustively enumerate such acts and treat categories of misconduct as closed - Hence, order of compulsory retirement could not be said to be one which was shockingly disproportionate to warrant interference and charge of plural marriage had not been established - Appeal dismissed.
