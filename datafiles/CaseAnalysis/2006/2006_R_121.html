Central Bureau of Investigation v Ravi Shankar Srivastava, Ias and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 374; (2006) 7 SCC 188; AIR 2006 SC 2872; 2007 (2) ALT 64; 2006 CRLJ 4050; 2006 (3) JCC 1404; JT 2006 (7) SC 313; 2006(7) SCALE 618; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 482 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1), 13(2) - IPC, 1860, ss. 120B, 167, 168, 177A - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, s. 6 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 166 - S. 482 merely recognise and preserves inherent powers - Does not confer any new power on High Court - To be exercised as an exception and not as a rule and as per tests laid down in Section - Should not be exercised to stifle a legitimate prosecution - HC not to embark upon an enquiry whether evidence adduced is reliable - In cases relating to cognizable offences - Categories of cases where Court may prevent abuse of process of Court by exercising powers under S. 482 is set out - Mala fides of informant , cannot be the sole basis to quash proceedings - Under the Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, extension to areas in State by consent of State, a general notification would suffice - Notification revoking the earlier notification granting consent not produced - Plea that there was notification rescinding earlier notification, not tenable - Therefore quashing of FIR was not proper - Appeal allowed.
