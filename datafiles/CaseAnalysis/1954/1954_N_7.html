Naresh Charan Das Gupta v Paresh Charan Das Gupta

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 117; AIR 1955 SC 363; 1956 (1) MLJ 183; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 183; 1955 SCJ 293; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1035

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 61 and 63 - Application filed by respondent No. 1 for probate of will - ADJ refused probate - High Court allowed appeal and grant probate - (A) Whether HC was right in granting probate to respondent No. 1? - It was alleged that will was executed under undue influence of respondent No. 1 - The burden is on appellant to establish that will was result of undue influence - Terms of will cannot be relied on as intrinsic evidence of undue influence - Held, it is proved that first respondent had no act or part in preparation, execution, or registration of will - Cumulative effect of evidence clearly establish that will represents free volition of testator and it is not result of undue influence by respondent No. 1 - (B) Whether there was a due attestation of the Will? - Held, HC were of the opinion that as the execution and attestation took place at one sitting at the residence of P.W. 1, where the testator and the witnesses had assembled by appointment, they must all of them have been present until the matter was finished, and as the witnesses were not cross-examined on the question of attestation, it could properly be inferred that there was due attestation - It cannot be laid down as a matter of law that because the witnesses did not state in examination-in-chief that they signed the will in the presence of the testator, there was no due attestation - It will depend on the circumstances elicited in evidence whether the attesting witnesses signed in the presence of the testator - This is a pure question of fact depending on appreciation of evidence - The finding of the Court below that the will was duly attested is based on a consideration of all the materials, and must be accepted - Appeal dismissed.
