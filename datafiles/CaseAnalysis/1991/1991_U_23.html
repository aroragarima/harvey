Â Sub-Committee Of Judicial Accountability v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, S.C. Agrawal, B.C. Ray, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 786; (1992) 4 SCC 97; AIR 1992 SC 63; JT 1991 (3) SC 659

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Judges (Inquiry) Act, 1968

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 129 and 145 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 94 - (A) Interlocutory order - Supreme Court's power to pass - Scope - Order, if passed, having effect or tending to be susceptible of an inference of pre-judging some important and delicate issues in main matter - (B) Choice of Forum - Supreme Court - Bias against judge - Procedure - Held, (A) Court will abstain from passing interlocutory order - (B) Can be exclusively entertained by that Judge or that bench of which he is member - Another co-ordinate bench would not interfere - Petition allowed.
