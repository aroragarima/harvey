Man Singh v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 413; (1979) 3 SCC 425; (1979) SCC (Cr) 528; AIR 1979 SC 1455; 1979 CRLJ 1118; 1979 CRLR 625; 1980 (4) ShimLC 121; 1979 UJ 798

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 5(1)(d), 5(2) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Corruption - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was convicted u/ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) of the Act r/w s. 161 of IPC and sentenced to RI for 1 year on each count - On appeal, HC dismissed the same by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there are intrinsic circumstances in the case which fully probabilise the defence of the appellant and show that the explanation given by him is reasonable. In instant case, from the evidence, the defence of the appellant has been clearly proved u/s. 3 of 1872 Act. Therefore, conviction as also the sentence passed on the appellant cannot be sustained. Appellant is acquitted of the charges framed against him. Appeal allowed.
