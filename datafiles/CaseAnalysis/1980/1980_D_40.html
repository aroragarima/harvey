Land Acquisition Collector & Anr. v Durga Pada Mukherjee & Others

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 180; (1980) 4 SCC 271; AIR 1980 SC 1678; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 ss. 4, 5A, 9 - Notification - Purpose of acquisition - Challenged - Notifications were issued u/s. 4 of the Act and respondents raised objections against proposed acquisition u/s. 5A of the Act contending that real purpose of acquisition was to benefit 'X' Company - Later on third notification was issued and previous notifications were cancelled - Declaration was made u/s.6 of the Act and notices were issued u/s. 9 of the Act - Aggrieved respondents filed writ before HC, SB which was dismissed - LPA was filed before DB which reversed order of HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, DB of HC seriously erred in accepting the LPA in view of the finding arrived at by them that malafides or a colourable exercise of power on the part of the State could not be held established. Further, acquisition could be struck down only if the declaration contained in the third notification was proved to be vitiated by malafides or colourable exercise of the power. Thus, it could not be held merely on the strength of the absence of production of documentary evidence by the State that the onus to prove malafides or colourable exercise of power on the part of the State has been discharged. Even the respondents have produced no material to show that the assertion about the public purpose as stated in the third notification was incorrect for the reason that the acquired land was not suitable for any industry or that no industrial activity except that by the Company had been undertaken in the neighbourhood of the acquired area. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside and the petitions made by the respondents to the HC are dismissed. Appeals allowed.
