Soma Bhai v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 559; (1975) 4 SCC 257; (1975) SCC (Cr) 515; AIR 1975 SC 1453; 1975 CRLJ 1201; 1975 CRLR 421

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: CrPC, 1898, ss. 154, 162, 286 and 290 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Duty of prosecution to examine material witnesses - Effect of non-examination - (B) Appreciation of evidence - Duty of Court - Credibility of witnesses- Portion of evidence unreliable - (C) Defence of alibi - Plea of alibi has to be proved to the satisfaction of court - (D) What is 'First Information Report' - First information reduced to writing after making telephone call - Admissibility - Held, non-examination did not at all destroy the fabric of the prosecution case which was proved by the evidence of other eyewitnesses and corroborated by the evidence of other witnesses - Entire evidence need not be rejected - Plea held not established on facts of the case - Held, undoubtedly constituted FIR and HC was in error in treating FIR lodged in the present case as inadmissible in evidence - Appeal Dismissed.
