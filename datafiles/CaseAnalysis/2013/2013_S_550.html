Sunil Damodar Gaikwad v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 572; (2014) 1 SCC 129; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 781; JT 2013 (12) SC 310; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 160; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 261; 2013(11) SCALE 232; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly caused the death of his wife and two minor sons - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 r/w s. 307 IPC and sentenced him to death - On reference, HC confirmed death sentence - Whether conviction recorded against appellant was sustainable - Held, Trial Court and HC discussed in detail the conduct of appellant - Courts below also considered appellant's main contention that he was not involved in the incident - Courts below found that it was not at all possible to appreciate appellant's contentions since normal conduct of a father in such circumstances would be first to help child to obtain treatment either by himself or with assistance of those residing in neighbouring rooms and nearby - Evidence available on record established beyond doubt that appellant alone was involved in the commission of offences - Hence, conviction was confirmed - Appeal partly allowed.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - Murder - Conviction - Death sentence - Sustainability - Whether instant case would fall under rarest of the rare category - Held, poverty, socio-economic, psychic compulsions, undeserved adversities in life were some of mitigating factors to be considered, in addition to those indicated in Bachan Singh v State of Punjab and Others, 1980 Indlaw SC 586 and Machhi Singh and Others v State of Punjab, 1983 Indlaw SC 116 - In the instant case, appellant accused suffered from economic and psychic compulsions - Possibility of reforming and rehabilitating appellant should not be ruled out - Appellant had no prior criminal record - Further, appellant was not likely to be menace or threat or danger to society - There was nothing to show that appellant had any previous criminal background - Appellant had intended to wipe out whole family including himself on account of abject poverty - This aspect of the matter was not properly appreciated by both Trial Court and HC which held that appellant had intention to only wipe out others and had not even attempted, and he was not prepared either, for suicide - On analyzing of facts and circumstances of the case, and on applying crime test and criminal test, it was fairly clear that case did not fall under rarest of rare category of cases so as to warrant a punishment of death - Thus, while upholding conviction of appellant u/ss. 302 and 307 of IPC, death sentence was modified to life imprisonment - Appeal partly allowed.
