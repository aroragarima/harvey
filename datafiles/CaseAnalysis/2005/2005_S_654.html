Jaswant Gir v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1670; (2005) 12 SCC 438; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 202

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Benefit of doubt - Exonerated of charges - Accused no. 2/appellant was charged u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC along with other two accused for the murder of cashier/deceased in Bank - Other two accused were acquitted by Trial Court holding that there was no satisfactory evidence of their involvement in murder - However, appellant was convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment and said conviction and sentence had been confirmed by HC by impugned judgment - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether benefit of doubt could go to appellant and he was got to be exonerated of charge - Held, all three accused were involved and PW 9 stated before police and as per statement made by PW 9 to police, all three accused made confession before him but accused gave different version in court and that was accused was treated as hostile witness and leading questions were put to him by prosecution - Thus, credibility of that witness was in doubt - However, alleged statement of appellant that deceased was in drunken condition could not be correct as doctor found no evidence of consumption of alcohol by deceased - Therefore, there were nothing found in assurance from all angles that alleged confession attributed to appellant by PW 9 was correct - It was not safe to base conviction on doubtful testimony of PW 9 who gave different versions before police and Court - HC omitted to evaluate evidence of PW 9 and failed to take into account doubtful features of his evidence - Thus, benefit of doubt should go to appellant and he was got to be exonerated of charge - Appeal allowed.
