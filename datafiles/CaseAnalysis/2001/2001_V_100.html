State of Jammu and Kashmir v Vinay Nanda

Supreme Court of India

16 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20705; (2001) 2 SCC 504; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1293; AIR 2001 SC 611; 2001 (1) CCR 151; 2001 (1) Crimes 185; 2001 CRLJ 957; JT 2001 (2) SC 351; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 532; 2001(1) SCALE 202; 2001 (2) SCJ 31; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 399; 2001 (1) SLT 513; 2001 (1) Supreme 187

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 313 - Jammu and Kashmir Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(1)(b) - Jammu and Kashmir Probation of Offender Act, 1966, s. 1 - Ranbir Penal Code, 1989, s. 409 - (A) Whether benefit of Jammu and Kashmir Probation of Offenders Act can be extended to convicts under Jammu and Kashmir Prevention of Corruption Act, 2006? - Held, respondent, when posted as cashier in Procurement Department of State of J and K, tampered record regarding carriage of store articles to and fro through RTC vehicles - Respondent was further accused of embezzlement of a substantial amount - Trial Court convicted accused/respondent, High Court held him guilty of committing offence, however, he was given benefit of Jammu and Kashmir Probation of Offenders Act - (B) Whether such leniency was justified and legal? - (1)

Whether 'special reasons' for awarding minimum sentence shown in case? - (i) Whether established for awarding minimum sentence? - Held, in view of unambiguous and categoric provisions of s. 18 of Jammu and Kashmir Probation of Offenders Act, HC was not justified in giving respondent benefit of Jammu and Kashmir Probation of Offenders Act as said Act was not applicable to offences under Jammu and Kashmir Prevention of Corruption Act - None of circumstances, stated by respondent, by itself constitute a 'special reason' - However, keeping in view general aspects of case, respondent has made out a case for invoking proviso to sub-s. (2) of s. 5 of Act - Respondent sentenced to undergo six months imprisonment - Appeal allowed.
