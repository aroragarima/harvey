State of Manipur and Others v Y. Token Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 272; (2007) 5 SCC 65; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 107; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 145; 2007(6) ALL MR 439; 2007 (5) AWC 5355; 2007 (3) ESC 206; 2007 (114) FLR 912; JT 2007 (3) SC 606; 2007 (3) LLN 111; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 934; 2007(3) SCALE 319; 2007 (6) SCJ 458; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 965; 2007 (2) SLR 760; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1165

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Law Officer, Process Server, Character Roll, M.L.R. Act, 1960

Summary: Service - Offers of appointment issued in favour of the Respondents - Order of cancellation of their appointments - Held, appointing authority, in absence of any delegation of power having been made in that behalf, was the State Government - Government Order dated 12.01.1998 did not delegate the power of appointment to the Commissioner - He, therefore, was wholly incompetent to issue the appointment letters - Respondents were not entitled to hold the posts - Not see any arbitrariness on the part of the State in its action directing cancellation of appointments - Appeals allowed.
