State Of U.P. v Ram Krishan Burman (Dead) By L. Rs. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 351; (1970) 1 SCC 80; AIR 1971 SC 87; 1971 ALJ 1; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 588

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Court Fees Act, 1870 s. 7(iv)(A), cl. 17(iii) - Property - Declaration - Suit - Court Fee - 'X' had filed suit for declaration that he was owner in possession of property left by 'Y' and defendant was impleaded in said suit which was decreed ex parte - Defendant filed suit against 'X' claiming that he was appointed as heir by 'Y' - 'X' represented defendant that compromise decree would be obtained and took advantage of ignorance obtained ex parte decree - Plaintiff valued properties at Rs. 5,99,503-63 and claimed court fees under sch.II, cl. 17(iii) of the Act - Inspector of Stamps reported that case fell u/s.7(vi)(A) of the Act and liable to pay Rs.3,528-80 as court fees on plaint, Trial court ordered to pay remaining fees - Plaintiff filed appeal against said order before High court which set aside order of Trial court - Hence instant appeal by State.

Held, decree for declaration of title to money or other property is not a decree for money or other property. Expression 'decree for money or other property' means only a decree for recovery of money or other property and it does not include a decree concerning title to money or other property. A consent decree in certain cases may be regarded as an instrument securing money or other property, where the decree proceeds upon a contract which had that effect, but that is only because a consent decree is a record of the contract between the parties to which is superadded the seal of the Court. Thus, High Court was right in holding that the court-fee paid on the plaint was proper and plaintiff had claimed nothing more than a declaration with regard to certain properties. Therefore, rigid attitude of the State which has by insisting upon a comparatively small claim, held up the proceedings for all these long years by raising contentions which had no merit. Hence, lower court is directed to take up this suit for hearing with the least practicable delay and dispose of according to law. Appeal allowed.
