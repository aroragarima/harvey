Nanakram Etc v Kundalrai Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, V. Balakrishna Eradi, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 641; (1986) 3 SCC 83; AIR 1986 SC 1194; 1986 ALJ 1027; 1986 (3) Bom.C.R. 98; 1986 (2) CCC 533; 1986 MahLJ 506; 1986 MLJ 506; 1986 (2) RCR(Rent) 253; 1986 (2) RentLR 260; 1986(1) SCALE 916; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 839; 1986 (2) UJ 535

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949 cls. 13, 22, 23, 30 - Lease -Transaction void - Maintainability - In 2 different cases respondents/landlords leased non-residential buildings to appellants/tenants resulting in common question of law - HCs concluded in respective cases that lease in favour of appellants were hit by cl. 22(2) of the Order and were void - Hence, instant appeals - Whether lease concluded between landlord and tenant in contravention of cl. 22 of the Rent Control Order could be assailed by landlord as void transaction in proceeding between parties to lease - Held, nowhere did the Rent Control Order mandate that Deputy Commissioner should eject person who had entered into possession of house in violation of cl. 22 of the Order - If upon view of circumstances prevailing then the Deputy Commissioner takes no action in matter, there was no reason why lease between landlord and tenant, although inconsistent with cl. 22 of the Order, should not be binding as between parties thereto, it was not void transaction - There was nothing in the Rent Control Order declaring it to be so - If lease was not void then it was not open to either party to avoid lease on ground that it was inconsistent with cl. 22 of the Order - Parties would be bound, as between them, to observe conditions of lease, and it could not be assailed by either party in proceeding between them - Thus, SC set aside appellate order dt. 17-2-1981 of Appellate Authority under the Rent Control Order and judgment and order of Bombay HC in writ petition filed by appellant insofar as they proceed on finding that lease was void - Further, SC set aside judgment and decree of Bombay HC and dismissed suit filed by respondent - Appeals allowed.
