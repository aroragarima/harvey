T. N. Godavaraman Thirumulpad and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1607; (2008) 2 SCC 222; 2008 (1) MLJ 997; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 997; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 9; 2007(3) SCALE 430; 2007(13) SCALE 430; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Corporate - Contract & Commercial - Use of land - Clearance of proposal - Setting up of project - Company/Applicant filed application for clearance of proposal for use of land for setting up Alumia Refinery - Project consisted of setting up of large integrated aluminium complex by company - Hence, instant Applications - Whether company should be allowed to set up its Refinery/Project known as 'Alumina Refinery Project' - Held, company was subsidiary of another company and that could provide jobs on permanent basis to tribals, particularly, land-losers - However, there was no positive statement as to number of persons who would get jobs on permanent basis - Statement referred only to potentiality to employ - There was no study made in regard of work - There was no statement as to in which category they would be fitted - Project was funded by U.K. based company and according to newspaper reports that U.K. based company had banned from Norway for non-compliance of labour laws and for violation of human rights - However, risk could not be taken of handing over important asset into hands of company unless its credibility would be satisfied - Under agreement, name of contractor was company and stating that company was subsidiary of another company, whereas Financial Statements annexed to affidavit of that another company showed that company was associate company and not subsidiary of that another company - On going through Financial Statements of another company, it was found that operating profits from aluminium segment was negligible for years ending - Company was joint venture partner and nothing prevented company from terminating joint venture agreement - Court did not have list of assets and even Accounts of company - Therefore, as if company was agreeable to Rehabilitation Package then they could be at liberty to move Court by initiating proper application - Applications disposed of.
