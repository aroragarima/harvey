Municipal Council, Bhopal v Sindhi Sahiti Multipurpose Transport Co-Operative Society Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 521; (1973) 2 SCC 478; AIR 1973 SC 2420; 1973 (2) CTR 373; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 274

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Motor Vehicles, Levy Of Fee, Permissibility, Use of municipal bus stand

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Carriers & Transportation - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, ss. 356.(7)(f) and (x) r/w s. 349(ii) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 68(2) (r) and (s), 76 - Use of municipal bus stand - Levy of fee - Permissibility - Appellant (Municipal Board) made bye-laws u/s. 356.(7)(f) and (x) r/w s. 349(ii) of the Act in respect of a Municipal bus stand, which provided that no person in charge of a motor-bus plying for hire shall for the purpose of taking up or setting down of passengers, park or stop his bus anywhere within the limits of Municipality except at Bus Stand - The other bye-laws provided for a levy of a fee of Re. 1/for every 8 hours or part thereof in respect of the, use of the bus stand by such buses and for the issue of a permit on such payment - Respondents filed a petition before HC challenging said bye-laws - HC held that byelaws 3 to 7, which provided for payment of fee and the giving of a permit etc., as invalid, and restrained the appellant and was directed to refund the fee collected from respondents - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, neither party has been able to show that there is any power in the State Govt. to delegate their power u/s. 68(2)(r) and (s) of 1939 to the District Magistrate nor have Court been shown any notification by the District Magistrate specifying the Municipal Bus Stand as one under the provisions of s. 68(2) (r) and (s) of 1939 Act. Apparently both the parties proceeded on a misapprehension. If at all the District Magistrate had taken any action it could only be u/s. 76 of 1939. But that section does not enable him to specify places for setting down or picking up of passengers as we pointed out earlier. Therefore, HC was in error in holding bye-law 2 valid. Nobody has a fundamental right to use a land belonging to another without that persons permission or paying for it if necessary. While the appellant has no power to compel persons plying motor buses for hue to use only the Municipal bus stand for the purpose of taking up and setting down passengers, there can be no objection to its providing a bus stand for anybody who chooses to use it, voluntarily and to such person being required to pay for such use. The bylaws compel persons in charge of motor buses to use the Municipal bus stand, which the Municipality has no power to do. The bye-law 2 as not valid and with it go the other bye-laws. Appeal dismissed.
