Mr. Louis De Raedt And Ors. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 204; (1991) 3 SCC 554; (1991) SCC (Cr) 886; AIR 1991 SC 1886; AIR 1991 SCW 2113; 1991 CRLR 660; JT 1991 (3) SC 306; 1991(2) SCALE 127; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 149

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners

Keywords: Acquisition Of Citizenship, Domiciled In India

Summary: Foreigners - Constitution of India, 1950 - Citizenship Act, 1955 - Foreigners Act, 1946 - Acquisition of Citizenship - Dismissal - Justification - Petitioners were foreign nationals - Petitioners were staying in India continuously since 1937 excepting on two occasions when he went to Belgium for short periods in 1966 and 1973 - Petitioners filed petition before HC for further extension of period of their stay in India and same was dismissed and they were ordered to leave the country - Hence instant petition - Whether at commencement of Constitution petitioners had an intention of staying here permanently - Held, available materials on record leave no room for doubt that petitioners did not have such intention - It could be said that they were in certain about their permanent home - During relevant period very significant and vital political and social changes were taking place in this country, and those who were able to make up their mind to adopt this country as their own, took appropriate legal steps - So far three petitioners were concerned, they preferred to stay on, on basis of their passports issued by other countries, and obtained from time to time permission of Indian authorities for their further stay for specific periods - None of applications filed by petitioners in this connection even remotely suggests that they had formed any intention of permanently residing here - Impugned order passed by SP, who was not authorized to so, was also devoid of any merit - Order was not passed by SP, decision was of Central Govt. which was being executed by SP, as was clear from order itself - Petitions dismissed.
