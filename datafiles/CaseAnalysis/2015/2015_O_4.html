Petromarine Products Limited v Ocean Marine Services Company Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 103; (2015) 7 SCC 229; AIR 2015 SC 1487; 2015 (3) ALT(SC) 43; 2015 (3) CTC 92; JT 2015 (2) SC 351; 2015 (2) MLJ 452; 2015(2) SCALE 443; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 1086

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Madras High Court Original Side Rules, Admiralty suit for recovery, Disbursement of sale proceeds

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 52 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958, s. 3(15) - Madras High Court Original Side Rules, O. 42 r. 11 - Admiralty suit for recovery - Disbursement of sale proceeds - Legality - Respondent no. 1 (company) filed a suit under Admiralty Jurisdiction of Madras HC, for recovery of US$ 22,705.84 against respondent no. 3 along with an application praying for an order of arrest of the vessel in question, which arrived at Port of 'M' - Appellant (execution creditor) filed another admiralty suit in Bombay HC for recovery of amount of US$ 39,712.97 i.e. the security of Appellant's suit claim - Bombay HC while passing the order of attachment was not aware about the fact that the vessel was seized by the Madras HC much prior to the filing of the suit by appellant in Bombay HC - Before the decree transmitted by Bombay HC was received by the Madras HC on 24-1-1998, Single Judge of Madras HC confirmed the sale in favour of 'J' (company) on 24-10-1997 - In execution petition moved by appellant in February, 1998, Bombay HC issued notice under O. 21 r. 52 of CPC, requesting the Madras HC to hold the decretal sum in an aggregate amount of US$ 58,325.64 from and out of the funds deposited by 'J' - However, Madras HC confirmed sale in favour of 'J' company and ordered reimbursement of cost of sale, payment to the crew members and charges to the statutory authorities - Aggrieved appellant challenged the same before DB of Madras HC in an application, which was dismissed holding that once suit was filed invoking admiralty jurisdiction of Madras HC, the suit in rem, it decides the interest of not only parties to suit but also other parties who were interested in the property under arrest or in the fund - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that on service of notice issued by Bombay HC under O. 21 r. 52 of CPC, appellant was entitled to decretal amount alone and amount attached ought not to have been disbursed to third parties and custody Court (Madras HC) has no authority to make rateable distribution.

Held, the appellant without approaching the admiralty proceedings sought a declaration that it is not entitled to priority. Being fully aware of the development of the proceedings and suits in the Madras HC, the appellant did not raise any objection. In the result, Single Judge of Madras HC after hearing all the parties, who had approached the Court, passed the order. Once the decree was transferred and transmitted by the Bombay HC to the Madras HC, the appellant could not have moved the Bombay HC and obtained an order without notice to the creditors and claimants. When the property was in the custody of Madras HC, being the transferee Court in question of title of priority arisen between the person having decree in his favour and person not being the judgment debtor is to be determined by the transferee Court. Court is unable to accept the submission of appellant that after order of attachment under O. 21 r. 52 of CPC, the Registry of Madras HC had to remit the amount to Bombay HC ignoring the pendency of proceedings in the Madras HC. It has not been disputed by the appellant that the Bombay HC while passing the order of attachment was not aware about the fact that the vessel was seized by the Madras HC much prior to the filing of the suit by the appellant in Bombay HC. The DB of Madras HC in impugned order has recorded the finding that Madras HC while deciding the issues in the suit filed under admiralty jurisdiction had considered the interest and also priorities of all interveners and also parties to the suit. It was held that the appellant ought to have made claim under O. 42 r. 11 the Rules. The DB rightly held that no Court is so prestige conscious that it will stand in the way of legitimate legal proceedings for redressal or relief sought for by the litigant. The Court also took notice of the fact that the necessary parties who had led their claims had not been impleaded by the appellant in the proceedings. Appeal dismissed.
