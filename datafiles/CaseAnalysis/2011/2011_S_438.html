Rafiq Ahmed @ Rafi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 589; (2011) 8 SCC 300; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1303; 2011 CRLJ 4399; 2012 (1) JCC 108; JT 2011 (9) SC 279; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1066; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 389; 2011 (4) RLW 2868; 2011(8) SCALE 272; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 907

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Manslaughter, Criminal Law Act, 1967

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 233(2), 313, 464 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201, 300, 302, 304B, 306, 323, 325, 326, 364, 391, 392, 396, 406, 411- Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Dacoity - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Five accused, allegedly committed dacoity in car - Accused had committed murder of deceased and thereafter thrown his body in sugarcane field situated in Village with intention of screening themselves from punishment for committing any offence - Trial Court had acquitted four accused but convicted appellant for an offence u/ss. 302 and 201 IPC while convicting another accused, , for an offence punishable u/s. 411 IPC - On appeal, HC upheld acquittal of four accused for offences u/s. 396 IPC was concerned as well as conviction of appellant u/s. 396 IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether accused was deprived of fair opportunity of defence and whether it caused him serious prejudice - Held, conjoint reading of ss. 396 , 302 IPC, it was clear that offence of murder had been lifted and incorporated in provisions of s. 396 IPC - Further, provisions were clear and admit no scope for application of any other principle of interpretation except 'golden rule of construction', to read statutory language grammatically and terminologically in ordinary and primary sense which it appears in its context without omission or addition - Hence, that no prejudice had been caused to appellant by his conviction for an offence u/s. 302 IPC though he was initially charged with an offence punishable u/s. 396 r/w s. 201 IPC - Circumstances which constitute an offence u/s. 302 were literally put to him, as s. 302 IPC itself was an integral part of an offence punishable u/s. 396 IPC - Appellant had not been able to demonstrate any prejudice which he suffered in his right to defence, fair trial and in relation to case of prosecution - Therefore, appellant had not suffered any prejudice, much less a serious prejudice, then conviction of appellant u/s. 302 IPC could not be set aside merely for want of framing of a specific/alternate charge for an offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC - It was more so because dimensions and facets of offence u/s. 302 IPC were incorporated by specific language and were inbuilt in offence punishable u/s. 396 IPC - Thus, on application of principle of 'cognate offences', there was no prejudice caused to rights of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
