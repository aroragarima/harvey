Â Sardar Indra Singh and v Commissioner of

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 27; AIR 1953 SC 453; [1953] 24 ITR 415; 1953 SCJ 628; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 167

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Profits and Gains - Securities - Taxable income - Income-tax authorities rejected appellants claim and taxed surplus in each a/y as profits and gains of company's business of dealing in shares - On appeal, Tribunal confirmed assessment orders - Appeal was filed aggrieved from judgment of HC answering question referred to it by Tribunal u/s. 66 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether surplus realized by company on sales of shares and securities was taxable income - Held, principle applicable was well settled and question always was whether sales which produced surplus were so connected with carrying on of assessee's business that it could be said that surplus was profits and gains of such business - It was not necessary that surplus should have resulted from course of dealing in securities as by itself would amount to carrying on of business of buying and selling securities - It would be enough if such sales were effected in usual course of carrying on business - Appeal dismissed.
