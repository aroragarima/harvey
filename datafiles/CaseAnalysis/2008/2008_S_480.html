Pulin Das @ Panna Koch and Another v State Of Assam

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 232; (2008) 5 SCC 89; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 520; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1315; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1134; 2008 CRLJ 2070; JT 2008 (2) SC 574; 2008(2) SCALE 729; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 257

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Self-Defence, TADA Act, Speedy Disposal

Summary: Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Whether prosecution has established the charge under s. 3(2) (ii)? - Held, evidence clearly show that they did not know about the activities of the accused persons particularly whether they are members of ULFA - S. 3 (1) as well as s. 20A mandates that no information about the commission of an offence under this Act shall be recorded by the police without prior approval of the D.S.P, and no court shall take cognizance of any offence under this Act without previous sanction of the Inspector General of Police or Commissioner of Police, P.W.9 D.S.P. ought to have explained all the details about the ULFA organization its activities and the alleged connection of the accused persons - It is the bounden duty of the prosecution to examine highest police officer of the district about the relevant materials - Prosecution has miserably failed to establish the charge levelled against both the accused - Appeals allowed.
