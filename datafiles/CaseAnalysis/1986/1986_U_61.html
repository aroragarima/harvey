Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra, V. Khalid, G.L. Oza, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 193; (1987) 1 SCC 124; AIR 1987 SC 386; 1987 (2) ATC 82; 1993 (1) CompLJ 266; 1989 (20) ECR 146; 1987 (27) E.L.T. 1; 1987 (54) FLR 310; JT 1986 SC 996; JT 1986 SC 1064A; JT 1986 SC 996B; 1987 LabIC 222; 1987 (1) LLJ 128; 1987 (1) LLN 294; 1986(2) SCALE 960; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 435; 1987 (1) SLJ 1; 1987 (1) SLR 182; 1987 (1) UJ 191

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 6(1)(c) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 and 236 - Issue arise - (A) Whether exclusion of jurisdiction of High Court u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution, 1950 in service matters specified in s. 218 of Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 and vesting of exclusive jurisdiction of such matters to Administrative Tribunal is void? - Held, impugned Act does not provide for an effective alternative institutional mechanism or authority for judicial review - Hence, it is void - (B) Whether Tribunal set up under impugned Act u/s. 6(1)(c) is equally efficacious as HC, so far as power of judicial review over service matter is concerned? - Held, it is not - Substituting Chief Justice of a HC by a chairman who has merely held post of a secy. to Govt. and who has no legal or judicial experience is less effective and efficacious mechanism than HC - (C) Whether appointment of Judges by sole discretion of Govt. is valid? - Held, members of tribunal will be partial as their promotion depends solely on Govt. discretion which will affect independence and impartiality of members of Tribunal - It is not valid -(D) Whether impugned tribunals should have jurisdiction in respect of employees of Supreme Court or members of subordinate judiciary and employees working in such establishments? - It should not have any such jurisdiction - In as much as exercise of jurisdiction of Tribunal would interfere with control absolutely vested in respective HCs in regard to judicial and other subordinate officers u/art. 235 of Constitution, 1950 - Answered in favour of petitioner.
