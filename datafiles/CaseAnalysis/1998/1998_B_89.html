State Of Haryana v Brij Lal Mittal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 723; (1998) 5 SCC 343; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1315; AIR 1998 SC 2327; AIR 1998 SCW 2240; 1998 CCR 246; [1998] 93 Comp Cas 329; 1998 (2) CompLJ 1; 1998 CRLJ 3287; JT 1998 (3) SC 584; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 104

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Health & Drug

Summary: Drugs And Cosmetics Act, 1940, s.25, s.34(1) - Right to get sample examined - Report of public analyst - Accused failing to notify inspector about their intension to adduce evidence in contoversion - Effect of - Offence committed by company - Respondent, Directors of manufacturers - No allegation indicating Directors were in-charge of the company and responsible to the conduct of company of it's business - Complaint was filed delaying till expiry of shelf life of drugs - Held, delay is not a ground to quash - Prosecution against Directors liable to be quashed - Right to examine the sample extinguished and report becomes final.
