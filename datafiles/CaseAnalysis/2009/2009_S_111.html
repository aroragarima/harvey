Santosh Kumar Shivgonda Patil and Others v Balasaheb Tukaram Shevale and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1571; (2009) 9 SCC 352; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 664; JT 2009 (13) SC 69; 2009(12) SCALE 278; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Land Revenue Act, 1879, Bombay Tenency and Agricultural Lands Act, 1976

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Land Revenue Code, 1966, s. 257 - Time limit for revision - Whether power of revision in s. 257 of Code can be exercised at any time although no time has been prescribed for exercise of such power? - Held, if a statue does not prescribe time limit for exercise of revisional power, it does not mean that such power can be exercised at any time - Law does not expect a settled thing to be unsettled after a long lapse of time - Reasonable period within which power of revision may be exercised would be three years u/s. 257 of Maharashtra Land Revenue Code subject, of course, to exceptional circumstances in a given case - But exercise of revisional power after a lapse of 17 years is not a reasonable time - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
