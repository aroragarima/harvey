Param Hans Yadav And Sadanand Tripathi v State Of Bihar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28077; (1987) 2 SCC 197; (1987) SCC (Cr) 322; AIR 1987 SC 955; 1987 BLJ 531; 1987 BLJR 486; 1987 (1) Crimes 894; 1987 CRLJ 789; 1987 CrLR(SC) 346; JT 1987 (3) SC 595; 1987(1) SCALE 426; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Evidence Act, s. 30 - Explosive Substance Act, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 120-B - Murder - District Magistrate was murdered in his office - Circumstantial evidence - Whether case against appellants proved beyond reasonable doubt? - Held, so far as first appellant is concerned his involvement is proved beyond reasonable doubt and his confession statement supports prosecution evidence - Second appellant not proved to be part of conspiracy and entitled for benefit of doubt - Being District Magistrate's murder, better investigation was expected but it is unfortunate that lapses occurred - Order accordingly.
