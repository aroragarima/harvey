Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti and Another v Metal Craft and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2157; (2008) 7 SCC 780; 2008 (6) ADJ 717; AIR 2008 SC 2883; 2008 (4) AWC 3758; 2008 (106) CutLT 606; JT 2008 (7) SC 472; 2008 (105) RD 166; 2008(10) SCALE 26; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 176; 2008 (3) UPLBEC 2660; [2008] 17 VST 152

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Water; VAT; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Condition Precedent, Contract For Sale, Bill Of Lading, Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930, Passing Of Property, Sale Of Goods Act, U. P. Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, U. P. Krishi Utpadan Mandi Niyamavali, 1965

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - U P Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, s. 17(iii)(b)(1), (2), (3) and (4) - U P Krishi Utpadan Mandi Niyamavali, 1965, r. 66 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Whether appellant is entitled to levy market fee u/s. 17(iii)(b) of U P Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964 if agricultural produce is neither brought nor taken out of market place? - A plain reading of s. 17(iii)(b) of Act shows that Committee is empowered to levy and collect market fee which shall be payable on transaction of sale of agricultural produce in market area words 'specified agricultural produce in market area' have great relevance manner of realisation of market fee has been enumerated in sub-clauses (1), (2), (3) and (4) of s. 17(iii)(b) - Reference is to 'produce' - This apparently shows that physical presence of agricultural produce within market area is necessary for levy of market fee - Explanation to s. 17(iii)(b) appended at end of section lays down that unless contrary is proved any specified agricultural produce taken out or proposed to be taken out of a market area by or on behalf of licensed traders shall be presumed to have been sold within such area - Explanation has application only if agricultural produce is physically present within market area - Explanation becomes redundant if stand of appellant that s. 17(iii)(b) is applicable even in cases where agricultural produce is neither physically brought nor is in existence within market area - Held, admitted position was that rice was never brought or was in existence within market area, Mandi Samiti, Ghaziabad or for that matter within State of UP - HC recorded a categorical finding that sale took place only when rice was loaded on sea at port in terms of agreement - That being so, there was no transaction of sale within market area of Mandi Samiti, Ghaziabad - Therefore, HC rightly held that Mandi Samiti was not entitled to levy any market fee - Appeal dismissed.
