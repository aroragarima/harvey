Hafazat Hussain S/O Mubarak Hussain v Abdul Majeed S/O Wali Mohd. Alias Sheikh Ballan and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20044; (2001) 7 SCC 189; AIR 2001 SC 3201; 2002 AllCJ 227; 2001 (45) ALR 50; 2001 (4) AWC 2782; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 228; JT 2001 (6) SC 591; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 12; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 334; 2001(5) SCALE 104; 2001 (5) SLT 679; 2001 (5) Supreme 706; 2001 (2) UC 397

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Muslim Waqfs Act, 1936 - Whether learned single judge in High Court has transgressed permissible limits u/s. 100 CPC, 1908? - Concurrent findings recorded by trial judge as well as 1st appellate judge on proper appreciation of materials on record should not be disturbed by HC, while exercising second appellate jurisdiction, but at same time, it is not an absolute rule to be applied universally and invariably - Findings recorded by trial court as well as first appellate court were shown to be not only vitiated due to perversity of reasoning but also due to surmises and misreading of materials on record - Held, plea that any findings of fact concurrently recorded were mechanically interfered with without justification or by transgressing limitations on exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 100 CPC, 1908 not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
