Scindia Employees' Union v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2974; (1996) 10 SCC 150; 1996 (3) CCC 123; 1996 (3) CLT 78; 1996(5) SCALE 75; 1996 (3) SLJ 91; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 647; 1996 (5) Supreme 481

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Service - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 5-A - Whether the land sought to be acquired is needed for a public purpose or not? - Acquisition of land for expansion of dockyard for defence purpose is a public purpose - Publication of declaration u/s. 6 accords conclusiveness to public purpose - It is for the appropriate Govt. to take a decision whether a particular land is needed for a public purpose or not and the court cannot substitute its opinion on the public purpose to that of the appropriate Govt. - Held, the petitioner, therefore, is not a person interested - Notice and hearing of it u/s. 5-A(2) is not mandatory - Publication of declaration u/s. 6 is conclusive evidence of public purpose - The Govt. has exercised the power of eminent domain and had got published notification u/s. 4(1) - After conducting the enquiry u/s. 5-A, declaration u/s. 6 was published which is conclusive evidence of public purpose - Need to give notice u/s. 5-A(2) nor to hear the petitioners - Appeal dismissed.
