Management of Hindustan Steel Limited v Workmen and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A. Alagiriswami, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 142; (1973) SCC (L&S) 195; (1973) 3 SCC 564; AIR 1973 SC 878; 1973 (26) FLR 136; 1973 LabIC 461; 1973 (1) LLJ 42; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Closure, Housing

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25 F, 25 FFF and 258 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Bihar Shops and Establishments Act, 1963, s. 25 - Retrenchment - Validity of - Whether retrenchment of Shri Venkatesan, Ex-Overseer, Housing Colony Construction Scheme of Hindustan Steel Ltd., Ranchi is proper and justified? - If not, what relief is he entitled to? - Held, it was within managerial discretion of an employer to organise and arrange his business in manner he considered best and that if a bona fide scheme for such re-organisation results in surplusage of employees, no employer is expected to carry on burden of such economic deadweight and retrenchment has to be accepted as inevitable, however unfortunate - U/s. 25 FFF(1), Shri Naidu was entitled to, a notice and compensation in accordance with provisions of s. 25 F if he had been retrenched - Retrenchment notice, D/- 22-06-1968, quite clearly, complies with this requirement - Appeal allowed.
