Sabeeha Faikage and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 406; (2013) 1 SCC 262; 2012 (4) ACC 768; AIR 2013 SC 189; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 277; JT 2012 (10) SC 371; 2012 (8) MLJ(SC) 126; 2012(10) SCALE 317; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 862

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Shipping Act, 2004, Merchant Shipping (Recruitment and Placement of Seafarers) Rules, 2005

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21,32,142 - Merchant Shipping (Recruitment and Placement of Seafarers) Rules, 2005 - Shipping Act, 2004 - Merchant Shipping Act, 1958 - Violation of Right to Life - Compensation - Eligibility - Petitioners are family members-deceased of Seafarers on tugboat Jupiter-6 carrying flag of Saint Vincent and Grenadines, who were presumed dead by declaration of Govt. in marine casualty - Petitioners filed instant petition u/art. 32 of Constitution complaining breach of fundamental right to life u/art. 21 of Constitution - On 5-9-2005, Jupiter-6 went missing in high seas and GOI after 35 days without taking part in marine casualty investigation certified that petitioners-husbands/sons were presumed dead and that owners of vessel were coordinating with underwriters for nomination of local P & I correspondent who would deal with them for requisite compensation package - Petitioners filed inter alia a writ of mandamus/direction to respondents to conduct an investigation into mysterious disappearance of their husbands/sons who were on board Jupiter6 - Court directed, Directorate to collect, analyze and prepare a report with reference to following information and file in court - Petitioner submitted that under r. 4 (3)(a) of Rules 2005 read with Form-III prescribed by Rules 2005, it was mandatory for Recruitment and Placement service providers to provide insurance cover to seafarers and further that insurance amounts of 40,000 US Dollars for each of officers and 25,000 US Dollars for each of non-officers deposited by respondent nos. 4 and 5 in were not adequate and compensation amounts could have been much higher as indicated in Model Collective Bargaining Agreements - Respondent No. 4 pleaded that there was no delay in informing GOI about missing of Jupiter-6 - Whether GOI was liable for violation of right to life guaranteed u/art. 21 of Constitution and was liable for any compensation to petitioners for not causing a marine casualty investigation - Held, that right to life and personal liberty guaranteed u/art. 21 of Constitution was only available against State and that art. 21 was not intended to afford protection to life and personal liberty against violation by private individuals - All ships on which seafarers were recruited and placed were covered adequately by P & I Insurance, Jupiter-6 was a ship bearing flag of Saint Vincent and Grenadines and was also covered by insurance and insurers had deposited Forty Thousand Dollars (40,000 Dollars/ Rs.18,14,800/) for each deceased officer seafarer and Twenty Five Thousand Dollars (25,000 Dollars/ Rs.11,34,250/) for each deceased non-officer seafarer - It was difficult to hold that amount of compensation was not adequate in absence of sufficient materials produced to show age, income of seafarers and all other factors which were relevant for determination of compensation in case of death of seafarers, hence SC, could not direct respondent nos.3 and 4 to pay compensation as per Collective Bargaining Agreements in absence of any materials placed before Court to show that respondent nos. 4 and 5 were bound by Collective Bargaining Agreements - Petition disposed of.
