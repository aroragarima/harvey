M/S. L.K.Trust v Edc Ltd. & Ors. Respondent(S)

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 330; (2011) 6 SCC 780; AIR 2011 SC 2060; 2011 (87) ALR 513; 2011 (2) BC 655; 2011 (2) DRTC 305; JT 2011 (6) SC 470; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 753; 2011 (4) MPLJ 9; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 600; 2011(5) SCALE 531; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 60 - Right to redemption - Respondent No. 1 was an investment company - Main objects of the respondent No. 1 were providing financial assistance to the industrial enterprises - Respondent No. 3 was a Private Limited Company and was engaged inter alia in the business of development/operation of hotel and tourism - Respondent No. 1 granted term loan of Rs.7.00 crores to respondent No. 3 against mortgage of hotel property - Respondent No. 2 also granted a loan of Rs. 5 crores to the respondent No. 3 against pari pasu charge of the hotel property - Subsequently because of global recession, development of the project was affected - Respondent No. 3 was not able to repay the loan amount - Respondent No. 1 initiated action for the recovery of loan amount and attached the property of respondent No. 3 - Offer made by respondent No. 3 for financial restructuring and/or one time settlement by payment of Rs.12.00 crores was rejected by the respondent No. 1 and the respondent No. 2 - Thereafter, respondent No. 1 had accepted the proposal of appellant-trust to sell the property in question for a sum of Rs.12.99 crores - Offer received by the respondent No. 1 from the appellant was subject matter of Writ Petition filed by the respondent No. 3 before the HC - Respondent No. 3 had prayed for a writ of mandamus directing the respondent No. 1 to consider and accept the proposal of the respondent No. 3 and restrain respondent No. 1 from proceeding to sell the property attached to the appellant-trust - Respondent No. 1 had relied upon the resolution of the Board of Directors indicating that the Board of Directors had accepted the offer of the appellant and acceptance was communicated to the appellant - HC, therefore, relying upon the stand taken by the respondent No. 1, held that there was a concluded contract between the respondent No. 1 and the appellant and in view of the said conclusion dismissed the petition filed by the respondent No. 3 - Special Leave Petition filed before SC was dismissed - Again writ petition was filed challenging the action of the respondent No. 1 in selling the property to the appellant-trust - Respondent no. 3 submitted that its right of redemption of the mortgaged property u/s. 60 of the Transfer of Property Act was not defeated by mere agreement to sell the property between the respondent No. 1 and the appellant-trust nor by the Judgment of the HC which was confirmed by the SC because the said question was never raised before the Court and was, therefore, not considered - Meanwhile, Board of Directors of respondent no.1 passed a resolution deciding that the respondent No. 1 would conclude the sale transaction with the appellant-trust - Thereupon, the respondent No. 3 filed writ petition before the HC praying for writ of mandamus against the respondent Nos. 1 and 2 inter alia directing them to permit the respondent No. 3 to exercise the rights of redemption of mortgaged property by accepting the offer towards full and final settlement of the liability of the respondent No. 3 - HC directed the parties to maintain status quo - HC by the impugned order held that the order of status quo would not come in the way of respondent nos. 1 and 2 in considering the proposal of respondent No. 3 - Thereafter, the respondent no. 1 passed a resolution accepting the offer of the respondent No. 3 to redeem the mortgage - HC accepted the same - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether respondent No.1 and 2, were all precluded by principles of res judicata and principles of constructive res judicata, from re-opening the matter to overcome the sale of the mortgaged property in favour of the appellant-trust under a concluded contract, as affirmed by SC? - Held, HC had dismissed the Writ Petition holding that the respondent No.1 had already entered into an agreement with the appellant for the sale of the assets and, therefore, there was no question of the same being cancelled or set aside since it represented a concluded contract between the parties - SC also did not record any finding that a concluded contract had come into existence between the appellant and the respondent No. 1 - SC had never recorded any finding to the effect that sale of the property mortgaged by respondent No.3 was concluded between the appellant and the respondent No.1 - Hence, the contention that respondent No.1 and 2, were all precluded by principles of res judicata and principles of constructive res judicata, from re-opening the matter to overcome the sale of the mortgaged property in favour of the appellant-trust under a concluded contract, as affirmed by SC was rejected - (B) Whether the right to redeem the mortgaged property conferred by s. 60 of the Transfer of Property Act upon the mortgager, i.e., respondent No.3 could be exercised? - Held, mortgagor under Indian law was the owner who had parted with some rights of ownership and the right of redemption was the right which he exercised by virtue of his residuary ownership to resume what he had parted with - Right of redemption was an incident of a subsisting mortgage and subsists so long as the mortgage itself subsists - In the instant case, no sale worth the name of the mortgaged property had taken place in favour of the appellant because there was no agreement of sale on the record of the case nor the facts indicate that the same was registered - Right to redeem the mortgage property which was available to the respondent No.3 had never extinguished at all and, therefore, the acceptance of proposal of the respondent No. 3 by the respondent No. 1 to permit it to redeem the property could not be said to be illegal in any manner - Appeals dismissed.
