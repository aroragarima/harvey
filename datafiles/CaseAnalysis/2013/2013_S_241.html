State Of U.P. & Ors. v Mahesh Narain Etc.

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 125; (2013) 4 SCC 169; AIR 2013 SC 1778; 2013 (2) AWC 1776; 2013 (137) FLR 316; JT 2013 (3) SC 546; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 534; 2013 (2) SLR 784; 2013 (1) UPLBEC 593

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Public Services (Tribunal) Act, 1976, U.P. Forensic Science Laboratories Technical Officers Service (First Amendment) Rules, 1990, U.P. Forensic Science Laboratories Technical Officers Service Rules, 1987

Summary: Service - Administrative - Uttar Pradesh Forensic Science Laboratories Technical Officers Service Rules, 1987, r. 5 - Uttar Pradesh Forensic Science Laboratories Technical Officers Service (First Amendment) Rules, 1990 - Appointment - Promotion - Entitled to - Respondent No. 1/employee promoted and working as Scientific Officer - State Govt. published the 1987 Rules and r. 5 of the Rules laid down that 75% posts should be filled through direct recruitment and remaining 25% posts should be fulfilled by promotion from permanent scientific officers, who had 5 years of experience - State sanctioned 15 posts of Assistant Directors in department, when the 1987 Rules came into force - However, the 1987 Rules were subsequently amended by the 1990 Rules, which was published in Govt. Gazette dt. 20-10-1990 - Pursuance to the 1990 Rules, State Govt. notified 11 vacancies for direct recruitment through notification - Since promotion was not granted to respondent on post of Asst. Director even after 5 years of service against 4 vacancies which were available to be filled under promotion quota, respondent filed claim petitions before Tribunal - Claim petition was allowed by Tribunal and directed the authorities to consider case of respondents for promotion - Aggrieved, appellant/State filed petitions before HC, which was dismissed holding that the 1990 Rules, were published in Gazette on 20-10-1990 and they should be deemed to be enforced from date when they were duly published in Gazette and not from date when the Rules were prepared by State Govt. as a result of which respondents were eligible to be considered for promotion as they had requisite experience - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, the 1987 Rules were amended in year 1990 which was published in Govt. Gazette dt. 20-10-1990 laying down that subsequent promotion should be made only by direct recruitment - But this amendment could not be allowed to affect respondents' claim for promotion as a rule could not work to prejudice of an employee who was holding post of his eligibility prior to enactment and enforcement of the Amended 1990 Rules - Since respondents were eligible and entitled to promotion for post of Scientific Officer in terms of the 1987 Rules, their experience could not be ignored on said post so as to deny them the benefit of consideration for subsequent post of Assistant Director on basis of the 1990 Rules which should be made effective for vacancies which arose after 1990 - Respondents had already confirmed on next lower post when they were promoted to post of Scientific Officers and as they were entitled to benefit of proviso which laid down that even temporary scientific officers who were permanent on next below post might also be considered for purpose of promotion - Respondents had right to be considered for promotion since they were continuing on post of Scientific Officer and had completed 5 years even before the Amended Rules came into effect on 20-10-1990 which laid down that all post of Asst. Directors should be filled by direct recruitment - Appellant/State was directed to consider respondent eligibility for promotion to post of Asst. Director - Impugned orders passed by Tribunal and HC was affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
