Mithabhai Pashabhai Patel And Others v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 743; (2009) 6 SCC 332; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1047; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1658; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2460; JT 2009 (7) SC 666; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 171; 2009(7) SCALE 559; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1126

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Specially Empowered

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167(2), 309(2), 173(8) and 482 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 and 142 - 'Re-investigation' vis-a-vis 'further investigation' - Whether with the change of an investigating authority, police custody of the accused on remand can be sought for, although cognizance of the offence had already been taken? - Held, No - Appointment of 'Special Investigation Team' by the Court vide its Order D/- 26-03-2008 and constitution of SIT by the State pursuant to such Order for the purpose of investigating into cases arising out of Godhra incident and communal riots erupted thereafter in the year 2002 - Incident occurred on 20-08-2002; during investigation six appellants were arrested; prosecuted for commission of offence under various provisions of Indian Penal Code - Indisputably they were remanded to police custody u/s. 167(2) of CrPC; completion of investigation, submittal of charge sheet, committal of case to Sessions Court, cognizance of offence taken; bail applied for before High Court which was granted - Thereafter in 2008 present Court appointed SIT - Application moved by one H. Shukla, ASP on 22-05-2008 seeking remand of the accused for a period of 14 days; application was rejected by the Sessions Judge on ground that HC had already granted bail to the appellants and unless it is cancelled, remand cannot be granted - Revision application filed thereagainst before HC; accepted and Order of Sessions Judge was reversed - Hence the present appeal - (A) Whether in the facts and circumstances of the case the High Court was correct in directing custodial remand of the appellants? - Held, No - Order of the Court D/-22-09-2008 noted wherein interalia Court ordered, '..petitioner may be summoned by the SIT appointed by this Court on the days fixed by it without taking the petitioners into custody..' and further noted the facts that appellants had given appearance before the concerned Sessions Judge as also the SIT, they had submitted a written representation expressing their willingness to cooperate with the further investigation, etc. - Court further deliberated upon difference between 're-investigation' and 'further investigation' as they stand on different footing - Direction of a re-investigation being forbidden in law, no superior could would ordinarily issue such a direction; '...it is evident that even after completion of investigation u/sub-s.(2) of s. 173 of the Code, the police has right to further investigate u/sub-s. (8), but not fresh investigation or reinvestigation..','Ramachandran v. R. Udhayakumar, (2008) 5 SCC 413' - Court thus proceeded further on the ground that Order appointing SIT was not for re-investigation but for further investigation - Power to remand as vested in Magistrate u/s. 167(2) of CrPC, further deliberated upon by perusing the section - Power of remand is to be exercised when investigation is not complete; once charge-sheet is filed and cognizance of the offence is taken, the Court cannot exercise its power u/s. 167(2); its power of remand can then be exercised in terms of sub/s. (2) of s. 309; s. 309(2) further perused - Appellants had been granted bail; they are not in custody of the court; they could not be taken in custody ordinarily unless their bail was not cancelled - HC thus was not correct in holding that as further investigation was required, s. 167(2) CrPC gives ample power for grant of police remand - Impugned judgement unsustainable, set aside - Appeal allowed.
