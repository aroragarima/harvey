Madhya Pradesh Industries Limited v Income Tax Officer, Special Investigation Circle "B", Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 300; [1965] 57 ITR 637

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Whether High Court acted improperly in refusing to investigate a plea raised by company that in issuing a notice u/s. 34(1)(a) of Income Tax Act, 1922 ITO acted without jurisdiction and for a colourable purpose? - Held, jurisdiction to issue prerogative writs is designed to invest HC with authority to superintend exercise of Governmental or other powers by tribunals, authorities, bodies, or persons - Jurisdiction is discretionary and HC is not bound to issue a writ merely because it is lawful to do so - If petition on face of it does not disclose a right to relief, HC has undoubtedly power to dismiss it in limine - Whereas in present case, claim made is that ITO had no power to issue notice u/s. 34, and that power is exercised not for any legitimate purpose for which it may be used, but for purpose of making a fishing enquiry and to review a previous order passed in favour of company, a rule upon ITO to show cause why order should not be set aside and an opportunity to authority whose action was challenged either to accept or deny facts alleged and to set out such other material facts as have a bearing on question, was at least called for - Appeal allowed.
