Gautam Kundu v Manoj Kumar, Assistant Director, Eastern Region, Directorate of Enforcement (prevention of Money Laundering Act) Government of India

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 906; AIR 2016 SC 106; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 453; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 133; [2016] 195 Comp Cas 186; 2016 CRLJ 666; 2016 (334) E.L.T. 195; JT 2016 (1) SC 8; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 200; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 398; 2016 (1) RLW 764

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Stock, Dealing, Overriding Effect, Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, Prevention of Money-laundering (Amendment) Act, 2009, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Disclosure and Investor Protection) Guidelines, 2000 (Updated till January 20, 2006), Prevention of Money-laundering (Amendment) Act, 2012

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 439 - Prevention of Money Laundering Act, 2002, ss. 3, 4, 45A - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 24, 12A - Alleged no offence under Act - Failure to grant bail - Justification - Appellant was arrested in relation to offence alleged to have been committed u/s. 3 of 2002 Act - Further, while appellant was in custody, his father expired upon which he moved application before HC for interim bail to perform rituals for his deceased father - Further, HC vide its order directed release of appellant on provisional bail for two weeks on conditions mentioned in order - Further, appellant filed fresh bail application u/s. 439 of CrPC - HC vide impugned order has rejected appellant's application for bail u/s. 439 of CrPC on ground that no order has yet been passed by any competent Court of law that no offence is made out against appellant u/s. 24 of 1992 Act - Appellant contended that there is no offence made out under 2002 Act against appellant as s. 24 of 1992 Act is not separate scheduled offence under 2002 Act - Further, neither complaint filed by SEBI nor complaint filed by respondent prosecutes appellant for violation of s. 12A read with s. 24 of 1992 Act - Hence, present appeal.

Held, s. 24 of 1992 Act is penal provision of inclusive nature, hence, it clearly reflects legislative intent of scheduled offence under 2002 Act. SC held that complaint was filed by SEBI against appellant on allegation of committing offence punishable u/s. 4 of 2002 Act. Further, there is no order as yet passed by competent court of law, holding that no offence is made out against appellant u/s. 24 of 2002 Act - Further, criminal revision praying for quashing proceedings initiated against appellant u/s. 24 of 1992 Act is still pending for hearing before HC Further, SC held that conditions laid down u/s. 45A of 2002 Act, would bind HC as provisions of special law having overriding effect on provisions of s. 439 of CrPC for grant of bail to any person accused of committing offence punishable u/s. 4 of 2002 Act, even when application for bail is considered u/s. 439 of CrPC. SC held that appellant floated as many as 27 companies to allure investors to invest in their different companies on promise of high returns and funds were collected from public at large which were subsequently laundered in associated companies of Group and were used for purchasing moveable and immoveable properties. Further, HC at time of refusing bail application, duly considered fact and further considered statement of Assistant General Manager of RBI, seizure list, statements of directors of company , statements of officer bearers of Rose Valley, statements of debenture trustees of company, statements of debenture holders of company, statements of AGM of Accounts of company and statements of Regional Managers of company for formation of opinion whether appellant is involved in offence of money laundering and on consideration of said statements and other materials collected during investigation, HC held that appellant cannot be granted bail. SC concurred with view expressed by HC and held that HC has called for all relevant papers and duly taken note of that and thereafter after satisfying its conscience, refused bail. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Rejection of bail application by court is justified when Court has taken into consideration relevant documents and is of view that accused's case is not a fit case to grant him bail.
