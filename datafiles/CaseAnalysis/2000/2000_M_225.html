Collector of Central Excise, Jaipur v Messrs Raghuvar (India) Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 682; (2000) 5 SCC 299; AIR 2000 SC 2027; 2000 (1) CLC 1462; 2000 (90) ECR 414; 2000 (118) E.L.T. 311; 2001 (1) G.L.R. 877; JT 2000 (7) SC 99; 2000 (38) RLT 777; 2000(4) SCALE 732; 2000 (6) SLT 26; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 236; 2000 (4) Supreme 243

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Modvat Scheme

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 11 A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57I - MODVAT Credit - Whether reversal of credit was wrongly taken? - Argued action need not be initiated within 6 months as limitation of 6 months provided under s. 11 - A is not applicable to action for reversal of credit under r. 57 - It will be attracted only to cases where any duty of excise has not been levied or paid or has been short - levied or short - paid or erroneously refunded - Held, provisions of s. 11 - A prescribing limitation of 6 months for recovery of duty not levied, or short levied or wrongly refunded have no application to any action taken under r. 57 - I of Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Action of reversal of credit is not same thing as recovery of duty.
