Â Mervyn Continho and Others v Collector of Customs, Bombay, and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 6; AIR 1967 SC 52; 1967 (15) FLR 226; 1967 (1) LLJ 749; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 600; 1967 SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Seniority - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16(1) - Whether seniority list prepared in 1963 should be struck down as violative of art. 16(1) and a fresh seniority list for cadre of appraisers, and system being followed in matter of seniority should be struck down? - Where there is only one source of recruitment, normal rule will apply, namely that a person promoted to a higher grade gets his seniority in that grade according to date of promotion subject always to his being found fit and being confirmed in higher grade after period of probation is over - Present method clearly denies equality of opportunity where grade of principal appraiser has only one source of recruitment, namely, from grade of appraisers - Held, seniority in grade of principal appraisers must be determined according to date of continuous appointment in that grade irrespective of whether person promoted to that grade from appraisers' grade is a direct recruit or a promotee - Petition allowed.
