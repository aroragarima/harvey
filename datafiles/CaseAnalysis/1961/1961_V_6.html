Patneedi Rudrayya v Velugubantla Venkayya and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 206; AIR 1961 SC 1821; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 186; 1961 (2) SCJ 662; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Easements Act, 1882 - Plaintiff who is appellant before us is owner of survey No. 159 of village Vemulavada while defendants 1 and 2 are owners of survey No. 158 lying to north of survey No. 159 and adjoining - Defendant No. 3 is owner of a field lying to north of survey No. 158 - To south of survey no. 159 is survey No. 160 belonging to brother of plaintiff - Immediately beyond this field and to south are a 'parallel drain', into which flow waters of Vakada drain, and Tulyabhaga drain both running west to east - Defendants 1 and 2 constructed a bund running approximately east-west on their own land - Plaintiff's grievance is that as a result of what defendants 1 and 2 have done flood water flowing from his field in northerly direction cannot find an outlet and stagnates on his land thus doing damage to his crops - Plaintiff claims right of drainage of all water falling on or invading his land including flood water on basis of immemorial user - Defendants contended that stagnation was a result of closing of some vents in Vakada drain - High Court, however, came to conclusion that flooding of fields because of swelling of Vakada drain is not something which has been happening from time immemorial - According to HC plaintiff had no right to prevent defendants from taking steps that they are taking and that a custom to allow flood water to flow over neighbour's land has not been so far established - Whether HC erred in its judgment? - Held, in coming to conclusion that plaintiff has not been able to establish immemorial user in respect of right claimed by him of draining of flood waters from his field on to defendants HC has ignored clear finding of lower appellate court on this point - Even in revised finding appellate court has not been able to fix precise year of commencement of phenomenon - It would, therefore, follow that upon evidence available in this case proper inference to be drawn would be that this phenomenon has been known from time immemorial - Basis of plaintiff's claim is not natural right of owner of higher land to drain off water falling on his land on to lower lands but basis is that this right was being exercised with respect to land of defendants 1 and 2 from time immemorial - Finding of fact of lower appellate court being in his favour on this point, his suit must succeed - Appeal allowed.
