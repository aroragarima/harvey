Hero Vinoth (Minor) v Seshammal

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 204; (2006) 5 SCC 545; AIR 2006 SC 2234; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 28; 2006 (64) ALR 194; 2006 (4) AWC 3299; JT 2006 (5) SC 436; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 121; 2007 (1) MPLJ 17; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 677; 2006 (1) RCR(Rent) 457; 2006 (101) RD 269; 2006(5) SCALE 477; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - CPC, 1908, ss. 100, 109 - Indian Easements Act, 1882, s. 41 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133(1)(a) - Suit filed by appellant for permanent prohibitory injunction to restrain defendant from causing obstruction in plaintiff putting up compound wall in his portion of property - Trial Court held that right of way provided to defendant's husband under partition deed was an easement of necessity and when appellant has got other access situated on northern side necessity has ceased to exist - HC in second appeal held that right of way given was one of grant and not an easement of necessity and plaintiff's suit was dismissed - Appeal against - Held, when approach was fundamentally wrong HC cannot be faulted for having gone into question as to what was proved intention of party as culled out from Partition deed - HC was right in holding that parties clearly provided for a right of access to backyard of defendant's house when Partition deed was executed and shares were allotted to various sharers taking into account various factors and it is a matter of contractual arrangement between them - If a right of way is provided to a particular sharer, it cannot be extinguished merely because such sharer has other alternative way - Appeal dismissed.
