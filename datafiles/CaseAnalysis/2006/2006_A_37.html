Adityapur Industrial Area Development Authority v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 358; (2006) 5 SCC 100; AIR 2006 SC 2375; [2006] 283 ITR 97; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 202; JT 2006 (11) SC 67; 2006(5) SCALE 321; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 757; [2006] 153 TAXMAN 107

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bihar Industrial Areas Development Authority Act, 1974

Summary: Banking & Finance - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(20 A) - Finance Act, 2002 - Bihar Industrial Areas Development Authority Act, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 289 - Cantonments Act, 1924 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950 - Notice issued by Deputy Commr. of Income Tax to Manager of Bank bringing to notice that Bank is obliged to deduct income tax at source from interest accrued on fixed deposit receipts of authority - Adityapur Industrial Area Development Authority challenged the same by a writ petition - Whether a Local Area Development Authority is a state under art. 289 of Constitution of India, 1950 after amendment in s. 10(20 A) of Income Tax Act, 1961? - A mere perusal of art. 289(1) discloses that a claim of exemption under it must proceed on the foundation that the exemption is claimed in respect of property and income of a State - Once it is held that the property and income is that of the State, a question may well arise whether it is still taxable in view of the provision of cl. (2) of art. 289 which dominantly is in the nature of a proviso - Cl. (2) empowers the Union to impose any tax to such extent as Parliament may by law provide, in respect of a trade or business of any kind carried on by, or on behalf of, the Govt. of a State, or any operation connected therewith - Thus, even the income of the State within the meaning of cl. (1) of art. 289 may be taxed by law made by the Parliament, if such income is derived from a trade or business of any kind carried on by or on behalf of the Govt. of a State or any operations connected therewith - Cl. (1) of art. 289, therefore empowers Parliament to frame law imposing a tax on income of a State which is earned by means of trade or business of any kind carried by or on behalf of the State Govt. - In view of amendment, explanation to s. 10(20 A) local authority to include only authorities enumerated in explanation, which does not include Local Area Development Authority - Exemption u/art. 289(1) also not available as it is a distinct legal entity - Properties, funds and dues are those of Authority itself and not of State - Held, notice was, therefore, valid and legal - Appeal dismissed.
