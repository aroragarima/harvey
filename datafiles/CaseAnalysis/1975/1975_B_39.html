State Of Assam v Bhubhan Chandra Datta & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 123; (1975) 4 SCC 1; (1975) SCC (L&S) 186; AIR 1975 SC 889; 1975 LabIC 581; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 854; 1975 SLJ 346; 1975 (1) SLR 569; 1975 UJ 299

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Assam and Nagaland High Court services (Appointment Conditions of service and Conduct) Rules 1955, Assam And Nagaland High Court Services (Appointment, Conditions Of Service)

Summary: Assam and Nagaland High Court Services (Appointment, Conditions of Service and Conduct) Rules, 1956, rr. 3(1) and 11- Fundamental Rules, r. 19 - Constitution, art. 229 - Service matter - Pay - Appointment of Registrar of HC - At initial pay of Rs. 1500/- with special allowance of Rs. 250/- per month without approval of Governor - Validity - Held, merely because the initial pay of the Registrar in 1967 became Rs. 1200/- and with five increments the salary would be Rs. 1500/- no implication could arise on the power of the Chief Justice to appoint the Registrar at the initial pay of Rs. 1500 - Appeal Allowed.
