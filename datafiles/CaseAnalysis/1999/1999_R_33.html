Smt. Beni Bai v Raghubir Prasad

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1187; (1999) 3 SCC 234; AIR 1999 SC 1147; 1999 (4) ALD(SC) 8; 1999 (36) ALR 481; 1999 (2) AWC 1384; 1999 (2) CalLT(SC) 66; 1999 (157) CTR 585; [1999] 236 ITR 898; JT 1999 (2) SC 54; 1999 (90) RD 317; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 889

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Women & Children

Summary: Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1)and 14(2) - Will of husband - Life time interest to wife and after her death absolute ownership to his son over property in dispute - Widow subsequently gifting to her daughter - Validity - Held, limited interest was given in lieu of maintenance as her pre-existing right - Right transformed into an absolute right by virtue of s. 14(1) of Act - Said right not conferred on her for first time - Therefore s. 14(2) of Act not applies to present case - Widow became absolute owner - Competent to execute gift deed in favour of her daughter.
