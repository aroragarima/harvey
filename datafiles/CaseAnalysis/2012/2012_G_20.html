Darshan Lal Nagpal (Dead) By L.Rs. v Government of NCT of Delhi And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 7; (2012) 2 SCC 327; AIR 2012 SC 412; 2012 (91) ALR 213; 2012 (2) AWC 1474; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 712; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 1; 2012 (186) DLT 243; JT 2012 (1) SC 20; 2012 (4) MahLJ 17; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 574; 2012 (3) MPLJ 8; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 858; 2012 (115) RD 612; 2012 (2) RLW 1525; 2012(1) SCALE 8; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 17(1) and (4), 5A - Land acquisition - Public utility - Dispense - Maintainability - Delhi Telecom Limited stressed need for allotment of land for public purpose, establishment of electric sub-station - Officers of Delhi Development Authority repeatedly expressed their inability to allot particular site by pointing out that same was reserved for other purpose - After common survey land was selected and was

informed that as per Master Plan, Agriculture/Green area can be utilized for Utilities and to acquire Land by invoking Section 17 of Act - As per direction given by Lieutenant Governor, notification u/s. 4(1) r/w ss. 17(1) and (4) for acquisition of 80 bighas 15 biswas land was issued - Appellants learnt about proposed acquisition of their land made representation to Member of Legislative Assembly that as per Master Plan of Delhi-2021 only 29.6 bigha land was required for sub-station and that barren land available in area could be utilized for that purpose leaving out their land, but no result of representation, so they filed writ petition for quashing of notifications in HC - Appellant pleaded that more than 4 years' time was spent and there was no urgency for acquisition of land justifying invoking s. 17(1) and (4) of Act - Government of NCT of Delhi and Lieutenant Governor of Delhi averred that with a view to provide power to the city power plant was being constructed and that plant was scheduled to be commissioned in a time-bound manner - HC rejected challenge of appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that acquisition of land for establishment of sub-station did not warrant invoking of urgency provision contained in Act, because proposal was initiated more than 5 years ago - (A) Whether Government should have invoked Section 17(1) and (4) of Act and dispensed with rule of hearing embodied in Section 5A(2) for purpose of acquiring land - Held, that Government did not produced any material to justify its decision to dispense with application of Section 5A of Act, documents produced by parties including the noting's recorded in file and approval accorded by Lieutenant Governor do not contain anything from which it can be inferred that a conscious decision was taken to dispense with application of Section 5A - (B) Whether the Division Bench of HC had rightly negative appellants' challenge to acquisition of their land - Held, that if what HC has observed is treated as a correct statement of law, then in all such cases acquiring authority will be justified in invoking Section 17 of Act and dispense with inquiry contemplated under Section 5A, which would necessarily result in depriving owner of his property without any opportunity to raise legitimate objection - Appeal allowed.
