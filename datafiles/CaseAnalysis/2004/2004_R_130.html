Commissioner of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Ramesh Food Products

Supreme Court of India

04 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1491; 2004 (174) E.L.T. 310; 2005 (66) RLT 4

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Exemption - Central Excise Rules, r. 57A - Whether respondent can avail full exemption on products falling under one head in terms of Notification 175/86 and avail modvat credit on full duty on goods falling under different chapter heading? - Exemption envisaged for specified goods accrues through instrumentality of manufacturer - Notification clearly demarcated two categories of manufacturers - A clear cut distinction is explicit between a manufacturer availing Modvat credit u/r. 57A and another not opting for Modvat Scheme - As is statutorily provided, input duty relief is given under scheme to manufacturers who opt to operate under scheme by applying for it in prescribed manner - Ultimately manufacturers have choice of choosing one of two concessions, ie. either Modvat Scheme or Notification 175/86 - There is no one to one correlation between inputs and final products under Modvat Scheme - Held, it would therefore not possible to allow manufacturer to simultaneously avail Modvat for some products and avail full exemption for others under small-scale exemption scheme - Appeal allowed.
