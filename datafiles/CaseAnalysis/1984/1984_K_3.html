A. Madan Mohan v Kalavakunta Chandrasekhara

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 339; (1984) 2 SCC 288; AIR 1984 SC 871; 1984(1) SCALE 294; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 894; 1984 UJ 414

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 81(3), 82, 83(2) and 86 - Petitioner declared elected to Assembly - Respondent filed election petition in High Court alleging corrupt practices - HC dismissed application of petitioner for throwing out election petition of respondent in limine - Whether or not election petition was liable to be dismissed in limine u/s. 86 of Act in breach of mandatory provisions contained in s. 81 (3) of Act? - Held, no - There was no requirement that documents or schedules should also have been served on petitioner because if they were filed in Court it is always open to petitioner to inspect them and find out allegations made in petition therefore documents or schedules do not form an integral part of election petition - Petition dismissed.
