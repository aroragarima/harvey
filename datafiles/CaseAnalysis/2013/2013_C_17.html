Nimmagadda Prasad v Central Bureau Of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 312; (2013) 7 SCC 466; AIR 2013 SC 2821; 2013 CRLJ 3449; JT 2013 (8) SC 18; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 175; 2013(7) SCALE 25; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant Of Bail, Continuation of investigation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120-B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173(8) - Economic offences - Continuation of investigation - Grant of bail - Justifiability - Pursuant to order of HC dt.10-8-2011, respondent, Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) registered a case against appellant (accused no. 3) and 73 others - Appellant was charge sheeted for alleged offences u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 420, 409 and 477-A of IPC and u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(c) and (d) of the Act - Appellant was arrested for his involvement and complicity in case and was in judicial custody - Respondent filed two successive bail applications before Trial Court, which were dismissed - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC for grant of bail - HC by impugned order dismissed the same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant has made out a case for bail - Held, according to CBI, appellant was Director of limited company, which was awarded many projects/contracts by Govt. of Andhra Pradesh during period between 2004 and 2009 -According to CBI, based on various materials, further investigation u/s. 173(8) of CrPC was still continuing in respect of other aspects of case - It was brought to notice of Court by CBI that a sitting Minister in-charge of Ports had nexus with transactions - Considering developments, various details furnished in status report dt.30-4-2013, though appellant was in custody for nearly 11 months, at the same time, claim of the premier investigating agency could not be underestimated - If ultimately it was established, it was a grave economic offence of alienating prime lands to selected private companies/individuals under the garb of development using deceptive means resulting in wrongful ownership and control of material resources detrimental to common good - In order to establish all those events, it was the claim of CBI that documents have to be obtained from different banks, other private companies/individuals, who facilitated diversion of funds - Economic offences constitute a class apart and need to be visited with a different approach in the matter of bail - Economic offence having deep rooted conspiracies and involving huge loss of public funds needs to be viewed seriously and considered as grave offences affecting the economy of country as a whole and thereby posing serious threat to financial health of country -Appellant could not be released at instant stage, however, CBI was directed to complete investigation and file charge sheet(s) as early as possible preferably within 4 months - Appeal dismissed.
