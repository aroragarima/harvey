T.N. Dadha Pharmaceuticals v Collector of Central Excise, Madras

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 179; (2003) 3 SCC 574; AIR 2003 SC 1556; 2003 (86) ECC 44; 2003 (107) ECR 441; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 251; 2003 (55) RLT 20; 2003(2) SCALE 1; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 897; 2003 (2) SLT 424; 2003 (1) Supreme 974

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s.11-A(1) - Assessee manufactures, among other pharmaceutical formulations, Darzamol Injection I V - Claims exemption under notification - (A) Whether withdrawal of exemption was unjustified - Held, in view of the technical report of the Drugs Controller of India confirming the view of the Drugs Controller, Tamil Nadu that Dextrose in Darzamole Injection is not a pharmaceutical necessity and Dextrose cannot be considered as therapeutically inert, the assessee has no case in support of the claim for exemption - (B) Whether the CEGAT erred in confirming the order of the Collector, Central Excise invoking the proviso to s.11-A(1) of the Act - Held, to invoke the proviso three requirements have to be satisfied, namely (i) that any duty of excise has not been levied or paid or has been short-levied or short-paid or erroneously refunded; (ii) that such a short-levy or short-payment or erroneous refund is by reason of fraud, collusion or wilful mis-statement or suppression of facts or contravention of any provisions of the Central Excise Act or the rules made thereunder; and (iii) that the same has been done with intent to evade payment of duty by such person or agent - Since, the requirements have not been satisfied, the proviso cannot be attracted - Appeals allowed.
