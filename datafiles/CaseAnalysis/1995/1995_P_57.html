Patel Natwarlal Rupji v Kondh Group Kheti Vishayak and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Faizan Uddin, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 777; (1996) 7 SCC 690; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 572; AIR 1996 SC 1088; 1996 (1) CLT 235; JT 1995 (9) SC 479; 1996(1) SCALE 149; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 289

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, s. 53 A - A sum of Rs. 1, 31, 596 07 was due from one Nagindas Tarachand as Secretary of first respondent-Society and an award for recovery thereof was made against him - Petitioner filed a suit for a declaration that suit land was not liable to attachment and sale by public auction to execute award made in favour of first respondent-Society - According to petitioner, Bai Leelawati, wife of Nagindas, as General Power of Attorney had executed an agreement on 2906-1969 to sell said land in his favour for a consideration of Rs. 35, 000 - He paid Rs. 30,000 and balance amount of Rs. 5000 was agreed to be paid on or before 29-1971 - Pursuant thereto, he was put in possession and ever since he was in enjoyment thereof in his own right as owner - Whether by operation of s. 53-A of Act, petitioner is entitled to retain possession of land? - Held, s. 53-A confers no title on transferee but imposes a statutory bar on transferor to seek possession of immovable property from transferee - Evidence establishes that petitioner did not have consideration of Rs. 30, 000 said to have been paid to Bai Leelawati whose husband was absconding at relevant time - It is a fraudulent agreement and no consideration has been passed thereunder - High Court had rightly adverted to all relevant facts and petitioner was denied right to avail of statutory rights u/s. 53- Petition dismissed.
