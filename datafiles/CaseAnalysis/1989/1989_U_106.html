Gujarat Water Supply And Sewerage Board v Unique Erectors (Gujarat) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 412; (1989) 1 SCC 532; AIR 1989 SC 973; 1990 (1) ARBLR 299; 1989 (1) CCC 389; JT 1989 (1) SC 285; 1989(1) SCALE 170; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 318; 1989 (1) UJ 452

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Counter-Affidavit, Error Of Law, Interim Award, Arbitral Award

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Plea that Arbitrator had passed award without deciding on preliminary issue of arbitrability of various claims and counter claims - Parties had agreed that pre-issue of arbitrability need not be decided - Non-speaking award - Arbitrator is not obliged to give reasons for his decision - Question of legality pending adjudication before Constitution Bench - Plea that Non-speaking award is per se bad, not agitated before High Court - Reasonableness of an award - Not a matter for Court to consider - Award of interest for period prior to commencement of arbitration proceedings - Award of pendente lite interest - Award of interest from date of award to date of decree - Held, arbitrator not to have acted arbitrarily in not deciding arbitrability as pre-issue - Not obligatory to give detailed judgment - Reference of matter to Constitution Bench held would be unjust - Pendente lite interest cannot be granted by Arbitrator - Interest from date of award to date of decree can be granted by Court.
