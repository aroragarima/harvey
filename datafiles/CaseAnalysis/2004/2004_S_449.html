Muthu Kutty and another v State by Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1001; (2005) 9 SCC 113; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1202; AIR 2005 SC 1473; JT 2004 (10) SC 538; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 639; 2004(9) SCALE 520; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 222

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - IPC, 1860, ss.498 A, 304 B - Trial Court and HC convicted accused and sentenced to imprisonment - Appeal against conviction - Conviction based on dying declaration - Held, no material to show that dying declaration was result of product of imagination, tutoring or prompting - On contrary, same appears to have been made by deceased voluntarily - It is trustworthy and has credibility - Courts below have rightly relied upon dying declaration.
