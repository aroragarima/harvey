President Board of Secondary Education, Orissa and Another v D. Suvankar & Anr

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 757; (2007) 1 SCC 603; 2007 (1) ADJ 277; 2006 (7) AWC 7628; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 757; 2006 (4) CPJ(Sc) 21; 2007 (2) ESC 270; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 122; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 198; JT 2006 (10) SC 103; 2006(12) SCALE 24; 2006 (6) SLR 570; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1143

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Revaluation

Summary: [A.] Education - Examination - HC held that there was no provision under any rule or regulations of Appellant-Board for revaluation, a sum of Rs. 20, 000/- Was awarded for wrong intimation about total marks actually received by respondent No 1 - Appeal against - Held, it is Board which has to ensure that correct marks sheet is issued to candidates since candidates who appear at High School Certificate are of tender age - Wrong entry of marks by computer firm - Ssum of Rs. 20, 000/- Has to be paid to respondent no 1 by Board out of which it shall recover Rs. 15, 000/- From computer firm - Appeal disposed of.
