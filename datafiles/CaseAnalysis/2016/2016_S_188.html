V. Sejappa v State by Police Inspector Lokayukta, Chitradurga

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 300; AIR 2016 SC 2045; 2016 CRLJ 2589; 2016 (3) JCC 1488; JT 2016 (4) SC 431; 2016 (2) OLR 155; 2016(4) SCALE 308; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Presumption, Currency Notes, Public Health, Proof, Secretary, Bribe, Trap, Version, Raiding Party, Diary, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant, Plea, Corruption, gratification

Summary: Criminal - Illegal gratification - Proof of demand - Complainant made oral complaint before Police Inspector alleging that accused demanded sum of Rs.5,000 as illegal gratification for handing over NOC to process his pension papers and other retiral benefits - Based on said complaint, FIR was registered and trap was laid to nab accused and later, accused was caught and amount of Rs.5,000 was recovered and upon completion of investigation, charge sheet was filed against accused - However, upon consideration of evidence, Trial Court acquitted accused of all charges holding that prosecution failed to prove demand and acceptance of illegal gratification by accused from complainant and also that there was no valid sanction to prosecute accused - However, on appeal by State, HC reversed findings of Trial Court and held that valid sanction order was obtained by prosecution and also held that prosecution proved demand and acceptance of illegal gratification and found accused guilty of offences and passed sentence of imprisonment - Hence, instant appeal by accused - Whether HC was justified in interfering with order of acquittal passed by Trial Court.

Held, considering the evidence of PWs 4,5,7 coupled with the attendance register, defence version that the accused was not present in the office at Chitradurga from 8-12-1997 to 10-12-1997 and that he was attending the seminar in Bangalore on 9-12-1997 is highly probablised. Further, absence of proof of demand on 9-12-1997 coupled with evidence of PW2 that the amount was paid by complainant to appellant towards purchase of diesel, raises serious doubts about the amount being paid as illegal gratification. However, HC neither considered the defence plea of alibi nor it held that the decision of the Trial Court was erroneous or perverse. Further, in evaluation of evidence made by Trial Court while recording an order of acquittal there is no infirmity or illegality or manifest error. Hence, HC was not justified in interfering with order or acquittal and so, impugned judgment is set aside and order of Trial Court acquitting appellant of charges is restored. Appeal allowed.
