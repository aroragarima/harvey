Â Nagpur Improvement Trust and Another v Vithal Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, A.N. Ray, D.G. Palekar, M. Hameedullah Beg, S.N. Dwivedi, J.M. Shelat, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 421; (1973) 1 SCC 500; AIR 1973 SC 689; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 39

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 131(c), 132(1), 226 and 227 - Nagpur Improvement Trust Act, 1936, ss. 3, 17-A, 26, 27, 39, 43, 44, 45 and 59 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Land acquierd under Nagpur Improvement Trust Act - Validity of Act challenged u/arts. 226 and 227 and High Court set aside award made under Act - Whether state is acquiring authority or it is Improvement Trust which is acquiring authority? - Held, state, in view of s. 17-A as inserted by para 6 of schedule, the acquisition will be by Govt. and it is only on payment of cost of acquisition to Govt. that lands vest in Trust -(B) Whether HC is right in holding that provisions of Act hit by art. 14 of Constitution? - Held, discrimination is writ large on Amending Act and it cannot be sustained on principle of reasonable classification, therefore, Amending Act clearly infringes art. 14 of Constitution and is void - Appeal dismissed.
