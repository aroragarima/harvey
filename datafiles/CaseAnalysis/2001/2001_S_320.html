Â Shyam Sunder and Others v Ram Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, N. Santosh Hegde, Y.K. Sabharwal, Shivaraj V. Patil, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21401; (2001) 8 SCC 24; AIR 2001 SC 2472; JT 2001 (6) SC 94; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 754; 2001(4) SCALE 710; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 115; 2001 (5) Supreme 492

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana Amendment Act, 1995, Punjab Amending Act, 1960, Punjab Pre-emption Repealed Act, 1973, Bihar Money-Lenders Act, 1939

Summary: (A) Constitution - Haryana Amendment Act, 1995 - Punjab Pre-emption Act - Effect of substituted s. 15 introduced by the Amendment Act in the parent Act i.e. Punjab Pre-emption Act - Conflict in the view taken in Didar Singh etc. etc. vs. Ishar Singh (dead) by Lrs. etc. etc. [1995 (1) Scale 1] (wherein it was held that in a suit for pre-emption, any loss of right or subsequent change in law after the date of adjudication of the suit and during pendency of appeal would not affect the decree of the first court and Ramjilal & Ors. etc. vs. Ghisa Ram etc. 1996 INDLAW SC 3146 wherein it was laid down since the Amending Act came into force during pendency of appeal, the right and remedy of the plaintiff stood extinguished and as a result suit must fail - Hence, present appeal - Held, amending Act being prospective in operation does not affect the rights of the parties to the litigation on the date of adjudication of the pre-emption suit and the appellate court is not required to take into account or give effect to the substituted Section 15 introduced by the amending Act - View of law taken in Didar Singh etc. vs. Ishar Singh (dead) by Lrs. etc. approved - Decision in the case of Ramjilal vs. Ghisa Ram does not lay down the correct view of law - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Constitution - Land & Property - Haryana Amendment Act, 1995 - Punjab Pre-emption Act - Effect of substituted s. 15 introduced by the Amendment Act in the parent Act i.e. Punjab Pre-emption Act as applicable to the State of Haryana whereby the right of a co-sharer to pre-empt a sale has been taken away during the pendency of an appeal filed against a judgment of the HC affirming the decree passed by the Trial Court in a pre-emption suit - Held, where a repeal of provisions of an enactment is followed by fresh legislation by an amending Act such legislation is prospective in operation and does not affect substantive or vested rights of the parties unless made retrospective either expressly or by necessary intendment - There is a presumption against the retrospective operation of a statute and further a statute is not to be construed to have a greater retrospective operation than its language renders necessary, but an amending Act which affects the procedure is presumed to be retrospective, unless amending Act provides otherwise - In the present case, in new substituted s. 15 brought in the parent Act by Amendment Act 1995 in the absence of anything in it to show that it is retrospective, does not effect the right of the parties which accrued to them on the date of suit or on the date of passing of the decree by the Court of first instance - Present appeals are unaffected by change in law in so far it related to determination of the substantive rights of the parties and the same are required to be decided in light of law of pre-emption as it existed on the date of passing of the decree - Appeals dismissed.

(C) Constitution - Land & Property - Haryana Amendment Act, 1995 - Punjab Pre-emption Act - Applicability of rule of benevolent construction - Substituted s. 15 introduced by the Amendment Act in the parent Act i.e. Punjab Pre-emption Act - Section 15 being a beneficial legislation enacted for general benefit of citizens, SC while construing it, is required to apply rule of benevolent construction and on application of the said rule of construction the substituted s. 15 has to be given retroactive operation - Held, when the Court finds that by application of rule of benevolent construction it would be re-legislating a provision of statute either by substituting, adding or altering the words used in the provision of the Act, Courts have refrained themselves from applying the rule of benevolent construction - Amending Act neither expressly nor by necessary implication is retrospective - If it is held that the amending Act is retrospective in operation, it would be re-legislating the enactment by adding words which are not to be found in the amending Act either expressly or by necessary intendment and it would amount doing violence with the spirit of the amending Act - Application of rule of benevolent construction inapplicable while construing substituted s. 15 of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(D) Constitution - Haryana Amendment Act, 1995 - Punjab Pre-emption Act - Retrospective operation - It was contended that the amending Act whereby new s. 15 of the Act is substituted is declaratory and, therefore, has retroactive operation - Held, function of a declaratory statute is to supply an omission or explain previous statute and when such an Act is passed, it comes into effect when the previous enactment was passed - Substituted s. 15 of the amending Act neither expressly nor by necessary implication intended to supply an omission or to clear up a doubt as to the meaning of previous s. 15 of the parent Act - Previous s. 15 of the parent Act was precise, plain and simple, there was no ambiguity in it - Meaning of the words used in s. 15 of the parent Act was never in doubt and there was no omission in its phraseology which was required to be supplied by the amending Act - Moreover, the amending Act either expressly or by implication was not intended to be retroactive - Amending Act is not a declaratory Act and, therefore, it has no retrospective operation - Appeals dismissed.
