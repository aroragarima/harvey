Land Acquisition Officer-cum-RDO, Chevella Division Ranga Reddy District v A. Ramachandra Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 34; (2011) 2 SCC 447; AIR 2011 SC 662; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 30; 2011(2) ALL MR 470; 2011 (84) ALR 796; 2011 (2) AWC 2171; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 27; JT 2011 (1) SC 132; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 205; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 557; 2011 (112) RD 520; 2011(1) SCALE 298; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 17 - Land acquisition - Compensation - Impugned land was acquired by the State Government vide a preliminary notification issued u/s. 4(1) r/w. s. 17 of 1894 Act published on 3.1.1990 - Possession of the acquired lands was taken on 18.9.1991 by invoking the urgency clause u/s. 17 of the 1894 Act, however, award was not be passed within the stipulated 2 years - The State government, being of the view that as a consequence, the acquisition had lapsed, published a fresh preliminary notification dt. 9.9.1993 u/s. 4(1) of the Act in the A.P. Gazette dt. 19.11.1993, followed by a fresh final declaration u/s. 6 of the Act published in the Gazette dt. 16.2.1994 - Land Acquisition Officer made an award passed with reference to the market value as on the date of issue of the first preliminary notification dt. 3.1.1990 and offered compensation at the rate of Rs. 24,000/- per acre with 30% solatium u/s. 23(2), additional market value at 12% per annum u/s. 23(1A) and interest at the rate of 9% per annum - On appeal, the Reference Court held that the relevant date of determination of market value was 3.1.1990 and that there was no evidence about the market value as on 3.1.1990 - However having regard to the situation and potential of the land, it concluded that approximately double the amount offered by the Land Acquisition Officer would be the appropriate market value and therefore, awarded compensation at the rate of Rs.50,000 per acre - On appeal, HC held that the relevant date for determination of compensation was not 3.1.1990 as the said preliminary notification was superseded by notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act published on 19.11.1993 and therefore the compensation had to be determined with reference to the said date - HC relied upon a sale deed relating to sale of a land at a distance of about 30 yards from the acquired land and awarded compensation at the rate of Rs. 1,50,000/- Hence, the present appeal - (A) Whether relevant date for determination of market value is 3.1.1990 as contended by the appellant (or 19.11.1993)? - Held, under sub-section (5) of s. 17 of the Act, if the Collector does not take possession of the land within 3 months from the date when State Government directs under sub-section (4) of s. 17 that the provisions of s. 5A shall not apply, the effect would be that the provisions of s. 5A would apply and the period of 30 days referred to in s. 5A shall be reckoned from the date of expiration of 3 months - In these peculiar circumstances, the government with a view to avoid further challenge, issued a fresh notification dt. 9.9.1993 followed by final declaration dt. 16.2.1994 - The State Government did not subsequently cancel/rescind/withdraw the notifications dt. 9.9.1993 and 16.2.1994 - Therefore, appellant cannot contend that the second preliminary notification is redundant or that first preliminary notification continues to hold good - Hence, HC was justified in holding that the compensation should be determined with reference to the date of publication of the second preliminary notification, namely 19.11.1993 - (B) Whether the compensation determined at Rs.1,50,000/- per acre requires interference? - Held, if quantum of compensation awarded by the HC would be examine with reference to the date of gazetting of the second preliminary notification, i.e. 19.11.1993, it found that the compensation award is not excessive and does not call for interference - It has been determined with reference to a sale transaction dt. 12.11.1993, just a few days prior to the publication of the second preliminary notification in the gazette dt. 19.11.1993 - HC has also made a deduction of 40% in the market value disclosed by the said sale transaction - Hence, compensation determined at Rs. 1,50,000/- per acre does not requires interference - Appeals dismissed.
