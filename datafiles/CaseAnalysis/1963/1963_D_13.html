Dr. Raghubir Sharan v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 350; AIR 1964 SC 1; 1964 CRLJ 1; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; 1964 (2) SCJ 143; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Expunge

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 423, 435, 439 and 561 (A) - (A) Whether High Court can expunge irrelevant remark from a judgment or order without matter being brought before it in appeal or revision ?- Held, yes it can - HC has inherent power to make any order for purpose of securing ends of justice - This power extends to expunction or ordering expunction of irrelevant remarks made against a person who is neither a party nor a witness to proceeding, from a judgment or order of a subordinate court - Although matter has not been brought before it in regular appeal or revision - It would be exercised by it in appropriate cases for securing ends of justice - This power will not be pressed in aid except for remedying a flagrant abuse by a subordinate court of its powers such as by passing comment upon a matter not relevant to controversy before it and which is unwarranted or is likely to harm or prejudice another - (B) Whether HC under its inherent power can expunge remark made by tribunals?- Further held, yes, it can do so in a limited manner - Judgment of a tribunal empowered by law to adjudicate upon and decide any matter affecting rights of parties is inviolable unless law allows it to be questioned or interfered with - In such a case judgment can be challenged only and interfered with only by specified authority and to extent permissible by express provisions of law - HC cannot exercise its inherent power because what is made final or inviolable by law is beyond purview of such power - However inviolability which attaches to a judgment must necessarily be confined its verdict and reasons - HC can expunge such matters which though ostensibly a part of judgment are not in reality its integral parts - Ss. 423 and 439 which deal with appellate and revisional powers, are silent about such matters - They do not exclude such power - Bearing in mind paramount importance of securing ends of justice, HC must be deemed to have such power - Appeal dismissed.
