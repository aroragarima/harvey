Dharmarajan and Others v Valliammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1289; (2008) 2 SCC 741; AIR 2008 SC 850; 2008 (70) ALR 169; 2008 (1) AWC 74; JT 2008 (1) SC 113; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 974; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 278; 2008 (104) RD 246; 2007(14) SCALE 79; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Suit claiming a decree for declaration of ownership as also for the injunction against the present appellants and appellants filed suit claming Eastern one half portion of suit property of which he claimed ownership through the sale deed in the earlier suit - Appellate Court decreed appellant's suit only to the extent of the decree of declaration of title - High Court set aside the decree - Appeal against - Held, High Court has gone into a dangerous area of appreciation of evidence, that too on the basis of non existent substantial questions of law - In the absence of pleadings, High Court gravely erred in finding out an entirely new case on the basis of unpleaded facts and non existent rights - Appellate court has rightly granted the declaration in that suit and has also restricted the relief only to the declaration since Dharmrajan and the other defendants had not terminated or revoked the licence of Doraiswamy or his wife Valliammal or daughter Palaniammal - Appeals allowed.
