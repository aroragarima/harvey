Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay v Abdullabhai Abdulkadar

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 1961 SC 701; [1961] 41 ITR 545; 1961 (2) SCJ 172; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 949

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 42(1), 10(2)(xi) and 10(2)(xv) - Whether amount involved was allowable as deduction? - Respondent firm had claimed dues from its non-resident principal as bad debts - Liability was imposed upon respondent firm because it was treated as an agent within meaning of s. 42(1) - It was not considered as business loss as liability arose because of business of another person - Held, amount was not allowable as deduction - Decision of High Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
