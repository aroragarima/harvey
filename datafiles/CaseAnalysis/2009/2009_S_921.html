Bhagwati Prasad v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, R.V. Raveendran, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1779; (2010) 1 SCC 697; AIR 2010 SC 349; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 284; 2010 CRLJ 528; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 129; JT 2009 (14) SC 560; 2009(14) SCALE 314; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 126

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Blood- Stained Earth

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302 and 304 Part II - Murder - Conviction - Trial Court acquitted all accused person and held that there was no direct evidence for common object - Trial Court found two irregularities, first was relating to spot of occurrence not being properly identified and second was about inquest panchnama which was found to be torn - On appeal, HC review all evidence and held that participation of five persons was not proved beyond doubt and there could not be common intention - HC further held that offence on part of appellant could not be u/s. 302 IPC and it was covered u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, once evidence of two eye-witnesses, who themselves were injured eye-witnesses, was accepted by HC after detailed consideration and when they asserted that incident took place in field of complainant and when placement of field of complainant was fixed by evidence, evidence becomes immediately acceptable and then such minor discrepancy whether it was spot 'A' or spot 'B', would be pushed to background - Such minor discrepancy cannot affect whole prosecution story - HC absolutely right in upsetting Trial Court judgement and convicting accused persons - Appeal dismissed.
