Aneeta Hada v M/S Godfather Travels & Tours Pvt.Ltd

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1015; (2008) 13 SCC 703; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1849; 2008(3) ALL MR 881; 2012 (5) CompLJ 138; 2008 (3) CTC 840; 2008 (4) JCC (NI) 415; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 11; 2008(8) SCALE 25; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1054

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonoured Cheque, Control Order, Banking Operations, Offences By Companies, Artificial Person, Indian Merchandise Marks Act, 1889, Employees Provident Fund and Miscellaneous Provisions Act

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Prayer for quashing the proceedings refused - Appeal against - Held, a company has to be made an accused but applying the principle "lex non cogit ad impossibilia", i.e., if for some legal snag, the company cannot be proceeded against without obtaining sanction of a court of law or other authority, the trial as against the other accused may be proceeded against if the ingredients of ss. 138 as also 141 are otherwise fulfilled - Difference of opinion between judges - Matters be placed before three-Judge Bench.
