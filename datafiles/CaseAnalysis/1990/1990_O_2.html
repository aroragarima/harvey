Oil and Natural Gas Commission and Another v Association of Natural Gas Consuming Industries of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 221; (1990) Supp SCC 397; AIR 1990 SC 1851; 1990 (2) CompLJ 89; JT 1990 (2) SC 516; 1990(1) SCALE 900; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas

Keywords: Oil and Natural Gas Commission, Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, Industrial (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951, Oil! and Natural Gas Commission Rules 1960

Summary: Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Industrial Disputes Act, 1938, s. 2(n) - Constitution, 1950, art. 12 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 3 (3-C) - Industrial (Development and Regulation) Act, 1951 - Oil And Natural Gas Commission - Oil and Natural Gas Commission Act, 1959, ss. 2(f), 14, 15, 16, 17 and 23 - Oil and Natural Gas Commission Rules, 1960 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) whether the ONGC is at liberty to fix its own price for the gas or should be directed to fix the price in any particular manner? - Court suggesting modes of price fixation held, if the last of the three courses is adopted by the ONGC for deciding the price structure afresh, it would be in their interest to give hearing to the persons likely to be affected so that the possibility of a new round of litigation is avoided also reiterate that as far as the petitioners are concerned, they are amenable to any of the three modes which the ONGC may choose to adopt - Further set aside the prices demanded by the ONGC from petitioners leaving it open to the ONGC to deal with the question of price fixation in any one of the three modes suggested - Petitions partly allowed.
