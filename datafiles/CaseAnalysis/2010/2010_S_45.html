Sandur Manganese and Iron Ores Limited v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 730; (2010) 13 SCC 1; JT 2010 (10) SC 157; 2010(9) SCALE 492; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 240

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Minerals Concession Rules, 1960, Bihar Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1964, Land Reforms Act, 1950

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution - Mines & Minerals (Development and Regulation) Act, 1957, s. 11 - Minerals Concession Rule, 1960, rr. 59(1) and 60 - Grant of mining lease - Appellant made an application to State Government for grant of mining lease and also sought relaxation of conditions specified in r. 59(1) of MC Rule - Subsequently, respondent 'J' also made an application for grant over same area - State Government made a recommendation to Central Government for grant of lease to appellant and sought relaxation of conditions set out in r. 59(1) - However, Central Government returned proposal of State Government directing it to await an environmental study being carried out by National Environmental Engineering Research Institute - Thereafter, State Government issued a Notification u/r. 59(1) of MC Rule notifying availability of a large area for re-grant of mining lease which was referred to as "Held Area Notification" - Pursuant to same, appellant made a fresh application for grant of mining lease over notified area - Respondent 'k' and 88 other applicants also applied pursuant to said Notification - Respondent 'J' did not reapply pursuant to "Held Area Notification - Thereafter, State Government made a recommendation to Central Government u/s. 5 of MMDR Act for approval of proposed grant of mining lease to respondents - Challenging recommendation dated 06.12.2004, writ petitions were filed by aggrieved companies before HC - During pendency of writ petitions, Central Government gave its approval for grant of mining lease in favour of respondents - Single Judge allowed the writ petitions and quashed the grant on the ground that respondent 'J''s application prior to "Held Area Notification" could not have been entertained in view of s. 11(4) of MMDR Act and rr. 59 and 60 of MC Rule - On appeal, DB reversed judgment passed by Single Judge and held that applications for grant of mining lease made prior to notification u/r. 59 of MC Rule could be considered for grant along with applications filed pursuant to notification - DB upheld recommendations dated 06.12.2004 of State Government together with proceedings of Chief Minister which were basis for recommendation u/s. 5(1) of MMDR Act to Central Government for approval of grant of mining lease in favour of respondents - Hence, present appeals - (A) Whether State Government's recommendation dated 06.12.2004 and proceedings of Chief Minister are contrary to provisions of s. 11 of Act and rr. 59 and 60 of MC Rule and not valid in law? - It was contended that decision of State Government is violative of s. 11(4) of Act which permits only applications made pursuant to Notification to be taken into account and not applications made prior to Notification - Held, exercise has to be done strictly in accordance with statutory provisions and if there is any deviation, same cannot be sustained - It was not open to State Government to justify grant based on criteria that are de hors to MMDR Act and MC Rule - In the present case, evaluation of all applications was done in three successive stages in a manner not envisaged by s. 11 - (B) Whether respondent 'J''s application made prior to Notification dated 15.03.2003 is capable of being entertained along with applications made pursuant to said notification? - Held, respondent 'J''s application made prior to Notification cannot be entertained along with applications made pursuant to Notification dated 15.03.2003 because it is s. 11(4) which covers said Notification along with r. 59(1) and not first proviso to S. 11(2) as contended by respondents - Further, proceedings of Chief Minister and recommendation are contrary to Scheme of MMDR Act as they were based on s. 11(5) which had no application at all to applications made pursuant to notification dated 15.03.2003 - It is s. 11(4) which would apply to a Notification issued u/r. 59(1) - Also, s. 11(4) is consistent with Rule 59 and 60 when it provides for consideration only of applications made pursuant to a Notification - Consideration of applications made prior to Notification, as required by first proviso to s. 11(2), is clearly inconsistent with rr. 59 and 60 - Hence, DB clearly erred in concluding that applications made prior to notification u/r. 59(1) which are premature and cannot be entertained u/r. 60 would revive upon issuance of Notification and this conclusion goes against basic principles of statutory interpretation - (C) Preferential rights of certain persons - Whether r. 35 of MC Rules justify recommendation of State Government in favour of respondents? - Held, r. 35 permits State Government to differentiate between "end use" of minerals for purpose of sub-s. (2) of S. 11 in addition to matters in s. 11(3) - R. 35 requires its application only in cases covered by s. 11(2) and not by s. 11(4) - Therefore, to extent that it is s. 11(4) that covers Notification u/r. 59(1) and not s. 11(2) and therefore, State Government committed an error in relying on r. 35 to exclude appellants - (D) Whether criterion of "captive consumption" referred to in Tata Iron and Steel Co. Ltd. vs. Union of India, 1996 INDLAW SC 2229, have any application in present case despite not being one of factors referred to in s. 11(3) of MMDR Act or R. 35 of MC Rules? - Held, TISCO's case concerned solely on interpretation of s. 8(3) of MMDR Act in context of a second renewal of a mining lease in favour of TISCO, and not a fresh grant covered by s. 11 of MMDR Act - Criterion of captive consumption referred to in TISCO's case does not have any application in present case - (E) Permitting grant of mining lease based on past commitments - Whether factors such as past commitments by State Government to applicants who have already set up steel plants, matter for consideration for grant of lease despite MMDR Act and MC Rule constituting a complete Code? - Held, State Government is purely a delegate of Parliament and a statutory functionary, for purposes of s. 11(3) of Act, hence it cannot act in a manner that is inconsistent with provisions of S. 11(1) of MMDR Act in grant of mining leases - Further, having notified area, State Government certainly could not thereafter to honour an alleged commitment by ousting other applicants even if they are more deserving on merit criteria as provided in s. 11(3) - (G) Whether recommendation in favour of respondents saved by operation of Law of Equity? - Held, Law of Equity cannot save the recommendation in favour of respondents as it is a well settled principle that equity stands excluded when a matter is governed by statute - (F) Whether Division Bench justified in arriving at such conclusion? - Held, DB erred in concluding that respondent 'J' application made prior to Notification can be entertained along with applications made pursuant to said Notification because it is not S. 11(4) which covers said Notification u/r. 59(1) but first proviso to S. 11(2) - DB also faulted in concluding that applications made prior to Notification u/r. 59(1) which are premature and cannot be entertained u/r. 60 would revive upon issuance of Notification which was not the case - (G) Whether it is advisable to remit it to Central Government? - Held, as recommendation of State Government dated 06.12.2004 is not valid with reference to provisions of MMDR Act and Rule, hence invalid recommendation cannot be looked into by Central Government - Impugned order of DB of HC and decision of Central Government quashed - State Government directed to consider all applications afresh in light of interpretation of s. 11 of Act and r. 35, 59 and 60 of MC Rule and make a recommendation to Central Government - Appeals disposed of.
