Sushil Murmu v State of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1088; (2004) 2 SCC 338; (2004) SCC (Cr) 529; AIR 2004 SC 394; 2004 (1) CCR 99; 2004 (1) Crimes 125; 2004 CRLJ 658; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 193; JT 2003 (10) SC 340; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 353; 2003(10) SCALE 670; 2003 (1) SCW 6782; 2004 (1) SLT 275; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 702; 2003 (8) Supreme 699

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bride Burning

Summary: Indian PEnal Code, 1860 - ss. 201, 302 - Young child sacrificed by the appellant - Extra judicial confession - Death sentence - Death sentence imposed by the Trial Court confirmed by the High Court - Whether 'rarest of the rare case' - Held, superstition cannot and does not provide justification for any killing, much less a planned and deliberate one. No amount of superstitious colour can wash away the sin and offence of an unprovoked killing, more so in the case of an innocent and defenceless child - Further held, this is an illustrative and most exemplary case to be treated as the 'rarest of rare cases' in which death sentence is and should be the rule, with no exception.
