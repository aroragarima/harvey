Indian Metals And Ferro Alloys Ltd and M/s Ferro v Union Of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 998; (1992) Supp1 SCC 91; AIR 1991 SC 818; JT 1991 (5) SC 236; 1990(2) SCALE 634; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 27; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate

Summary: Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act ,1957, ss. 10 and 11(4) -

Mineral Concession Rules (1960), r. 59 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Grant of mining lease by State Govt. - (A) Applications received - Lease should be given to applicant who can exploit mineral most efficiently - Whether the State Govt. is bound to dispose of applications only on 'first come, first served' basis - (B) Some areas for mining lease are reserved for public sector exploitation - However State Govt. can grant private leases also - Whether r. 59 of 19600 Rules can be challenged by any instrumentality in public sector - (C) Industry allowed to be set up in State by grant of industrial licence and / or certain other concessions -Whether merely because of that , the industry would be entitled to captive mine to cater its needs - Held, State Govt. is not bound to apply 'first come, first served' basis - Such grant of mining lease to private sector cannot be challenged by public sector - Merely because of that , the industry would not be entitled to captive mine - Appeal allowed.
