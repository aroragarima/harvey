Satish Kumar Gupta etc. v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 128; (2017) 4 SCC 760; AIR 2017 SC 1072; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 45; 2017 (122) ALR 19; 2017 (2) AWC 1734; 2017 (2) CLT(SC) 170; JT 2017 (2) SC 477; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 74; 2017 (135) RD 791; 2017(3) SCALE 53; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 162

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Local Authority, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10, O. 1 r. 10(2), O. 41 r. 27 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 3(b), 3(f), 50, 54 - Post-acquisition allottee - Compensation enhancement - Entitled to - State Govt. acquired lands for public purpose for setting-up Industrial Model Township by Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation (HSIDC) - Further, substantial part of acquired land was allotted by HSIDC to Maruti Suzuki India Limited (MSIL)/allottee - Land owners as well as HSIDC filed appeals u/s. 54 of the Act - Subsequently, MSIL filed an application for its impleadment to act as intervenor before HC - HC allowed application holding that allottee had right to be impleaded as a party and it was not possible for HC to assess compensation as there was no site plan showing the location of transactions relied - Hence, instant appeals - Whether a post-acquisition allottee of land was necessary or proper party or has any locus to be heard in the matter of determination of compensation under the scheme of the Act.

Held, mere fact that Govt. chooses to determine allotment price with reference to compensation price determined by Court does not provide any locus to an allottee to contest the claim for enhancement of compensation. The only justification relied upon by respondents was lack of sincerity on part of State authority for whose benefit the acquisition was made viz. HSIDC, which by itself cannot be a valid ground to permit post-acquisition allottee to be treated as a necessary or proper authority under O. I r. 10 CPC to proceedings for determination of compensation. The view taken in impugned judgment cannot be sustained on any principle or precedent. SC hold that post-acquisition allottee has no locus to be heard in the matter and was neither a necessary nor a proper party. The other part of impugned order permitting additional evidence and remanding the case for fresh decision was uncalled for. No case was made out for permitting additional evidence on settled principles under O. XLI r. 27 CPC. It was clear that neither Trial Court has refused to receive the evidence nor it could be said that the evidence sought to be adduced was not available despite the exercise of due diligence nor it could be held to necessary to pronounce judgment. SC had given liberty to MSIL to file an application for impleadment or to act as an intervenor which implied that such application was to be accepted. It cannot be held that any right was crystalised by the said observation and such prayer had to be considered according to law. Therefore, post-acquisition allottee had no right in the matter. Impugned order of HC was set aside and remand the matter to HC for fresh decision in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.
