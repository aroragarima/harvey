Mahadeo Savlaram Shelke and Others v Pune Municipal Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1483; (1995) 3 SCC 33; AIR 1995 SCW 1439; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 441; 1995 (1) CCC 352; JT 1995 (2) SC 504; 1995(1) SCALE 829; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 39 - (A) Whether there was a prima facie triable issue and balance of convenience for granting ad interim injunction pending suit? - Acquisition proceedings having become final, necessarily possession has to be taken by Corporation for public purpose for which acquisition was made - Orders of eviction having been passed against appellants by due process of law and becoming final, no right was created in their favour to remain in possession and their possession is unlawful - Held, no prima facie right or title to appellants to have perpetual injunction restraining Corporation from taking possession of building - (B) What are relevant considerations for granting of injunction? - Further held, courts should necessarily consider effect on public purpose thereof and possibility of mitigation of damages caused to defendant by grant of injunction; in event of plaintiffs losing suit ultimately they should necessarily bear consequences, namely escalation of cost or damages suffered on account of injunction issued by courts - Appeal dismissed.
