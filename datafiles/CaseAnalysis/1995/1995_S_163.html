Shri Ganesh Narayan Hegde v Shri S. Bangarappa And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1034; (1995) 4 SCC 41; (1995) SCC (Cr) 634; AIR 1995 SCW 2364; 1995 (4) CCR 20; 1995 (2) Crimes 306; 1995 CRLJ 2935; JT 1995 (4) SC 124; 1995(2) SCALE 748; 1995 (2) SCJ 583; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 549; 1995 (2) UJ 560

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of Charges - High Court quashed proceedings in respect of appellant as well as other co-accused, who had not filed any proceedings before him - Whether order of HC holds good ? - Held, no - HC is empowered to exercise its inherent powers to prevent abuse of process of court which is called for only in cases where no offence is disclosed or complaint is frivolous, vexatious or oppressive - If complaint is bonafide and discloses an offence, there would be no justification for interference by HC - Non-application of mind by HC - HC proceeded to analyze case in light of all probabilities to determine whether a conviction would be sustainable and arrived at a conclusion that proceedings are to be quashed against all respondents - Appeal allowed.
