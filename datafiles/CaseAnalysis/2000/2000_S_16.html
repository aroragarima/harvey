State By Central Bureau of Investigation v S. Bangarappa

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2771; (2001) SCC (Cr) 152; (2001) 1 SCC 369; AIR 2001 SC 222; 2000 (4) CCR 271B; 2000 (4) Crimes 276; 2001 CRLJ 111; 2001 (1) JCC 3; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 818; 2000(7) SCALE 557; 2001 (1) SCJ 37; 2000 (8) SLT 331; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 726; 2000 (7) Supreme 633

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of Charge sheet - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 4, 13 and 17 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 482 - Accumulation of wealth beyond known sources of income - Central Bureau of Investigation chargesheeted respondent, ex - chief misnister of Karnataka - Trial court found prima facie case made out but High Court quashed charges against respondent - Held, special judge was empowered to try case - HC wrongly weighed evidence at early stage and quashed proceedings - Proper course was to allow trial and let trial reach at its logical terminus - HC order set aside, trial court to proceed with trial in accordance with law and dispose it as expeditiously as possible - Order accordingly.
