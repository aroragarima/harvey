N.D.P. Namboodripad v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 688; (2007) 4 SCC 502; AIR 2007 SC 1782; 2007 (5) AWC 5339; 2007 (4) ESC 532; 2007 (115) FLR 63; JT 2007 (5) SC 32; 2007 (2) KLT 248; 2007 (3) LLN 99; 2007(4) SCALE 361; 2007 (7) SCJ 101; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 769; 2007 (3) SLR 123

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals partly allowed, Special Pay, Dearness Relief, High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, Kerala Service Rules, High Court Judges, Kerala Public Services Act, 1968, Kerala Service (Amendment) Rules, 2004, Kerala Service (Amendment) Rules, 2005

Summary: High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954 - Kerala Service Rules - r. 62 -Special leave petitions filed against the Order of Division Bench wherein the Bench set aside the Single judge order entitling the appellant to a pension of Rs.35, 100/- per annum (that is Rs.2, 925 x 12) from 1.1.1986 and Rs.47, 900/- per annum (that is Rs.35, 100 + Rs. 12, 800) from 1.11.1986 - Appellant contended that average emolument of the appellant had to be taken as Rs.4237/- instead of Rs. 3500/ - per month for calculating the ordinary pension and that the special additional pension to be calculated without any ceiling - Held, R.62 does not enable the addition of clearness allowance and special allowance to the pay for purposes of pension, the 'emolument' of the appellant was rightly taken as Rs.3500/- per month; neither the ceiling of Rs.8, 000/- introduced with effect from 1.11.1986, nor the earlier ceiling of Rs.3500/- is valid - Appeals partly allowed.
