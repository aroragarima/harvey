Zahid Hussein and Others v State of West Bengal and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20686; (2001) SCC (Cr) 631; (2001) 3 SCC 750; AIR 2001 SC 1312; AIR 2001 SCW 1139; 2001 (2) CCR 23; 2001 (2) Crimes 42; 2001 CRLJ 1692; JT 2001 (3) SC 525; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 206; 2001(2) SCALE 479; 2001 (2) SCJ 393; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 442; 2001 (2) SLT 721; 2001 (2) Supreme 385

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Human Rights - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 32 and 302 - West Bengal Rules for Superintendence and Management of Jails, rr. 591(4) and (29) - West Bengal Correctional Services Act, 1992, s. 61 - Life convicts - Premature release - Whether petitioner entitled to premature release as of right if total period of his imprisonment is more than 20 years? - Held, no - R. 591(4) and (29) of rules do not provide for automatic release of a life convict after he has completed 20 years of detention including remission - Under these sub-rules only right which a life convict can be said to have acquired is a right to have his case put up by prison authorities in time to state Govt. for consideration for premature release and in doing so Govt. would follow guidelines mentioned in sub - rule (4) - Further held, discretion is to be exercised by taking into account conduct of petitioner in jail, his potentiality of to commit crime, danger to locality due to his premature release, age cannot be a factor for consideration, possibility of regrouping for anti - social activities, socio - economic condition of their families - Matter to be decided afresh - Petitions allowed.
