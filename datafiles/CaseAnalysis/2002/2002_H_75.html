Dhruv Green Field Limited v Hukam Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1751; (2002) 6 SCC 416; AIR 2002 SC 2841; 2002 (132) PLR 472; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 690; 2002(5) SCALE 487; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 449; 2002 (5) Supreme 257

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Act, 1961, ss. 10-A and 13 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 9 - Punjab Village Common Lands (Regulation) Rules, 1964, r. 6 - Whether provisions of s. 13 r/w s. 10-A of Act bar jurisdiction of a Civil Court to entertain suit filed by respondents? - Held, from a combined reading of s. 10-A(1) and 10-A(2) it is clear that any matter which raises issue of violation of provisions of Act or Rules made thereunder including question of lease being entered into as a result of fraud or concealment of facts or even otherwise detrimental to interests of Panchayat as prescribed, is required to be decided by Asst. Collector of First Grade, therefore provisions of s. 13 of Act, bar jurisdiction of Civil Court to entertain and adjudicating such questions - Appeal allowed.
