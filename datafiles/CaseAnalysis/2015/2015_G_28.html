Agricultural Income Tax Officer and another v Goodricke Group Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 234; (2015) 8 SCC 399; [2015] 373 ITR 166; 2015(4) SCALE 422; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 612; 2016 (3) SLR 168; [2015] 79 VST 450

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Education Cess, Application For Revision, West Bengal Primary Education Act, 1973, West Bengal Rural Employment and Production Act, 1976, West Bengal Taxation Laws (Second Amendment) Act, 1989

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - West Bengal Primary Education Act, 1973, s. 78C - West Bengal Rural Employment and Production Act, 1976, s. 4B - West Bengal Taxation Laws (Second Amendment) Act, 1989 - Amendment - Levy of Cess - HC found, based on a reading of the interim orders passed in both Buxa Dooars Tea Company Ltd.'s case and Goodricke Group Ltd.'s case, that for the period prior to the Amendment Act of 1989, the respondent was entitled to a refund of the cess paid by it together with interest at 12 per cent per annum, and had further found that insofar as interest payable after the Amendment Act concerned, such interest would only be payable after assessment orders were passed - Whether rural employment cess and education cess continue to be the same cess before or after the Amendment Act.

Held, it is clear from a reading of ss. 4B and 78C that where any sum is paid by or collected from an owner of a tea estate during a period commencing from 1-4-1981 or 14-4-1984, as the case may be, up to the date of the Amendment Act as rural employment cess or as education cess, such portion of the said sum as may become payable under the provisions of the Amendment Act shall, notwithstanding any judgment, decree or order of any court, be deemed to have been validly levied, paid or collected under the Amendment Act. Also, what would have been payable under the Act as unamended, is now payable only under the 1989 Amendment Act which has come into force with retrospective effect. Thus, refund will have to be made to respondent together with 12 per cent interest. Respondent have made payment of interest from time to time to the State. These payments will be adjusted against any sum that would become payable as a result of instant judgment. Appeal disposed of.
