Dr. Ramakrishna Rawat v District Magistrate, Jabalpur and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 340; (1975) 4 SCC 164; (1975) SCC (Cr) 457; AIR 1975 SC 90; 1975 CRLJ 46; 1974 CRLR 651

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Hunger Strike

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3(1)(a)(ii) - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 117 and 151 - Detention order - Validity of - Grounds - Relevancy - (A) Detention order served when petitioner was in jail custody - Whether valid? - Held, petitioner at time of service of order of detention was in jail neither as an under trial prisoner for an indeterminate period nor as a convict serving a long sentence, but as a detenu in proceedings u/ss. 151/117 CrPC, that custody was obviously of a short duration; mere service of detention order on petitioner in jail would not therefore invalidate order - (B) Grounds in schedule are same on basis of which previous order of detention was found - Held, past events recited in paragraphs 2, 3, 4, and 5 constitute one chain culminating in previous detention of petitioner; they constitute only background and not grounds of detention on which present order is based - (C) Whether matter is not germane to public order? - Held, petitioner has been painted in all incidents as prime-mover of gear which resulted in disturbances accompanied by violence, looting and mischief on a wide scale; particulars are neither vague, nor are they irrelevant to object of detention; on basis of these activities, detaining authority could reasonably gauge tendency of petitioner to act in a manner prejudicial to maintenance of public order in future - Petition dismissed.
