Brijendra Nath Bhargava and Another v Harsh Wardhan and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28160; (1988) 1 SCC 454; AIR 1988 SC 293; JT 1987 (4) SC 538; 1988 (1) RCJ 467; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 86; 1988 (1) RentLR 228; 1987(2) SCALE 1394; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 124; 1988 (1) UJ 397

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 13(1)(c) - (A) Suit for eviction - On the ground of material alteration - Validity - Held, construction of wooden dochatti or a balcony is not a material alteration within meaning of s. 13(1)(c) of Act - It could not give a cause of action to respondent-landlord for filing a suit of eviction - No other question was pressed - (B) Finding about the construction without consent of the landlord is finding of fact - Interference by Supreme Court u/art. 136 - Legality of - Held, the Courts below while coming to a conclusion of fact have omitted to consider material pieces of evidence and drawn inferences without looking into the material pieces of evidence which proved circumstances on the basis of which a contrary inference could be drawn - Such findings are not binding upon Supreme Court - Appeal allowed.
