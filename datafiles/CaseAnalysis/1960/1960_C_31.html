Raja Bahadur Visheshwara Singhand Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, Biharand Orissa

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 294; AIR 1961 SC 1062; [1961] 41 ITR 685; 1962 (1) SCJ 704; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Indirect Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961- Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(2) - Assessment - Business profits - Overdraft - Yearly allowance - Shares & Securities - Presumption - Appellant son of Ma and brother of present M - Father died and appellant was given by way of maintenance - Also given yearly allowance , appellant invested his cash surplus in shares and securities - Appellant sold shares and securities ,made profits on sales - Appellant arranged overdraft and actually withdrew Rs. 10,000 for purchase of shares - Income-Tax Officer held these to be liable to income-tax as business profits - On appeal, Appellate Assistant Commissioner excluded the profits for certain years - Appellant applied for a case to be stated u/s.66(1) - Application dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal -

Whether profits and transactions of sale and purchase of shares and securities amount liable to be taxed - Held, Profit was capital accretion and not profit derived from an adventure in nature of trade but if purchase made solely and exclusively with intention to resell it at profit and purchaser never had any intention to hold property for himself there would be strong presumption that transaction was in nature of trade but that was also a rebuttable presumption - Appellate Tribunal produced that the activities of appellant showing buying and selling and magnitude of holdings and it cannot be said therefore that it was not open to Appellate Tribunal to give finding that it did - Appeals dismissed.
