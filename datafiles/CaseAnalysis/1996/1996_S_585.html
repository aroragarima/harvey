C. Chenga Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3059; (1996) 10 SCC 193; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1205; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 445; AIR 1996 SC 3390; 1996 (3) Crimes 119; 1996 CRLJ 3461; JT 1996 (6) SC 739; 1996(5) SCALE 318; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 479; 1996 (6) Supreme 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Urgent

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(d) and 5(2) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 377A/34 and 420/34 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 142 - (A) Criminal Appeal - No direct evidence available on record connecting any of accused persons with commission of crimes - Case based on circumstantial evidence - Re appreciation of evidence by Supreme Court - (B) Charge against some Govt. officers and contractors that they conspired to commit offences u/ss. 377A/34 and 420/34, IPC, 1860 and u/s. 5(1)(d) of 1947 Act - Case based purely on circumstantial evidence - No evidence of clinching nature proved - Chain of evidence not leading to irresistible conclusion of guilt - Whether they are entitled benefit of reasonable doubt and acquittal - (C) Scope of art. 142 in anti-corruption case - Supreme Court setting aside acquittal giving them benefit of doubt - Their acts and omissions, however, resulting in administrative lapses and violations of departmental circulars and instructions thus making them vulnerable to departmental inquiry thereby - Exercise of powers u/art. 142 of Constitution - Held, (A) Supreme Court examined evidence led in cases - (B) They are entitled to benefit of reasonable doubt and acquittal - (C) Supreme Court directed that no departmental enquiry be held, accused be reinstated with continuity in service but will not be entitled to back wages or other monetary benefits - Appeal allowed.
