Vijay Kumar Arora v State Govt. Of Nct Of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 27; (2010) 2 SCC 353; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 944; 2011 (1) CGLJ 82; 2010 (166) DLT 569; 2010 (1) JCC 550; JT 2010 (1) SC 277; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 383; 2010(1) SCALE 437

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Conviction based on circumstantial evidence - Sustainability of - Appellant allegedly burnt the deceased to death - Trial Court convicted appellant for murder and sentenced him to life imprisonment - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC dismissed the appeal - Whether conviction by Trial Court is legally sustainable - Held, Medical evidence showed that the death of the deceased was due to shock and toxaemia due to burns by fire, however, defence had not cross-examined the doctor - No evidence showed that the deceased had left any suicidal note - Presence of kerosene oil on the body and clothes of the deceased and presence of extensive burn injuries rules out the theory of accidental fire - Forensic record showed that kerosene oil stove was found in normal working order - Evidence relating to cruelty meted out by the appellant to the deceased for bringing insufficient dowry and his extra-marital relations would show that he had a strong motive to murder the deceased - Truthful testimony of the relatives and neighbours of the deceased which unerringly established that the deceased had made dying declaration before those witnesses implicating the appellant - Circumstances from which the conclusion about the guilt of the appellant is to be drawn are fully proved and proved circumstances are conclusive in nature - All the established facts establish guilt of the appellant - Therefore, impugned orders of Trial Court and HC are upheld - Appeal dismissed.
