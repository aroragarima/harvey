Â Pu Myllai Hlychho and Others v State of Mizoram and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna, G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 744; (2005) 2 SCC 92; AIR 2005 SC 1537; JT 2005 (1) SC 263; 2005(1) SCALE 239; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 145(3), 163, 311(2), sch. 6 para2(1), 6-A and 20-BB - Nomination -Governor power - (A) Discretionary power of governor in nominating and removing person to district council of mizoram u/arts. 145(3) and sch. 6 para2(1), 6-A and 20-BB challenged - Relied on Rai Sahib Ram Jawaya Kapur v. State of Punjab 1955 Indlaw SC 16, A. Sanjeevi Naidu v. State of Madras, 1970 Indlaw SC 297, U. N. R. Rao v. Indira Gandhi 1971 Indlaw SC 148 and Edwingson Bareh v. State of Assam 1965 Indlaw SC 202 - Held, application of para 20-BB of sch. 6 validly made - (B) Is governor bound by aid and advice of council of minister in tribal area for termination and appointment of officer - Held, governor bound by aid and advice of council of minister and termination of members of MADC is valid in sch. 6 of constitution - (C) Is there any violation of principle of natural justice in termination without hearing other side - Relied on Rash Lal Yadav (Dr.) v. State of Bihar, 1994 Indlaw SC 1577 - Held, not accepted as office held at pleasure of governor - Appeal dismissed without any costs.
