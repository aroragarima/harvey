Jai Narain Mishra and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

D.G. Palekar, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 570; (1971) 3 SCC 762; (1972) SCC (Cr) 40; AIR 1972 SC 1764; 1972 CRLJ 469; 1972 UJ 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 108, 307 and 324 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 239 - (A) Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Attempt to murder - (1) Where four or five persons attack a man with deadly weapons - Presumption as to intention to cause death - (2) Accused causing simple injury by Farsa - Possibility of its sharp edge not being used - Applicability of s. 307 of IPC, 1860 - (3) Conviction of accused u/s 307 r/w s.109 on ground that he instigated his companions to kill a person - Evidence showing that he instigated them to assault only - (B) CrPC, 1898, s. 239 - Joint trial - Offence committed in course of same transaction - (A) Held, it may well be presumed that intention is to cause death and he is liable to be convicted u/s 326 and not u/s 307 - Offence committed is not one u/s. 307 but u/s. 324 as injury is caused by a weapon which was likely to cause death - Further held, conviction was not proper and he should have been convicted u/s. 324 r/w s. 109 - (B) Held trial was not vitiated on ground of misjoinder of charges - Appeal dismissed.
