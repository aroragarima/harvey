M/S. Hind Wire Industries Ltd. v The Commissioner Of Income Tax, West Bengal-V

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 28; (1995) 3 SCC 136; AIR 1995 SC 1133; 1995 (124) CTR 219; 1998 (99) E.L.T. 25; [1995] 212 ITR 639; JT 1995 (2) SC 317; 1995(1) SCALE 475; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 519; [1995] 80 TAXMAN 79

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 154(7) - Appellant-assessee was assessed for Income Tax originally under assessment order - ITO dismissed assessee's claim which was confirmed by Appellate Asst. Commr. - In appeal, tribunal allowed application and High Court reversed order - Interpretation of expression 'from date of order sought to be amended'? - Entitlement of assessee's claim on ground that request for rectification of mistake 'from date of order' to rectified order has been exercised after four years of limitation given u/s. 154(7) of 1961 Act? - Word 'order' does not mean original order but also include amended or rectified order - Held, asessee's claim cannot be disallowed on ground that second application for rectification was filed after four years from date of original assessment order - Period of four years shall be counted from date of fresh assessment order passed on first application of rectification sought by assesee - Appeal allowed.
