Ahmedabad Pvt. Primary Teachers' Association v Administrative Officer And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 4; (2004) SCC (L&S) 306; (2004) 1 SCC 755; AIR 2004 SC 1426; 2004 (6) Bom.C.R. 485; 2004 (3) CHN 21; 2004 (1) CLR 495; 2004 (109) DLT 374; 2004 (1) ESC 194; 2004 (100) FLR 601; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1828; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 72; JT 2004 (2) SC 27; 2004 (1) KLT 470; 2004 (1) LLJ 596; 2004(1) SCALE 368; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 470; 2004 (1) SLR 705; 2004 (1) SLT 490; 2004 (1) Supreme 276; 2004 (2) UPLBEC 1494

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Education; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Minimum Wages Act, 1948, ss. 2(1), 2(i), 27, 3 - Payment Of Bonus Act, 1965, s. 2(13) - Payment Of Gratuity Act, 1972, ss. 1(3)(c), 2(e) - Employees' Provident Funds Act, 1952, s. 2(f) - Teachers - Schools of Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation - Whether fall within definition of 'employee' - Held, gratuity is an amount paid unconnected with any consideration and not resting upon it, and has to be considered as something given freely, voluntarily or without recompense - Further held, on plain construction of words and expression used in cl 2(e) of Act, 'teachers' who are mainly employed for imparting education are not intended to be covered for extending gratuity benefits under Act - Teachers do not answer description of being employees who are 'skilled', 'semi-skilled' or 'unskilled' - These three words used in association with each other intend to convey that a person who is 'unskilled' is one who is not 'skilled' and a person who is 'semi-skilled' may be one who falls between two categories meaning he is neither fully skilled nor unskilled - Further held, it is for Legislature to take cognisance of situation of such teachers in various establishments where gratuity benefits are not available and think of a separate legislation for them in this regard.
