Commissioner Of Central Excise, Noida v M/S. Accurate Meters Ltd

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 271; (2009) 6 SCC 52; 2009 (235) E.L.T. 581; JT 2009 (3) SC 481; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 666; 2009(4) SCALE 333; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 1146

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Place Of Removal, Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000, Central Excise (No 2) Rules, 2001

Summary: Indirect Tax - Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944; Central Excise Valuation (Determination of Price of Excisable Goods) Rules, 2000 - Whether 'freight' and 'insurance charges' constitute the value of the goods for the purpose of computation of Excise Duty - Held, where, in pursuance of a contract of sale, the seller is authorized or required to send the goods to the buyer, delivery of the goods to a carrier, whether named by the buyer or not, for the purpose of transmission to the buyer, or delivery of the goods to wharfinger for sale custody, is prima facie deemed to be a delivery of the goods to the buyer - Authority in appeal as also the Tribunal were correct in their view that the amount claimed by way of transportation charges and insurance cannot be considered for determining the value of the electric meters supplied - Appeal dismissed.
