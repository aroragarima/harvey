Anant Madaan v State Of Haryana And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 350; (1995) 2 SCC 135; AIR 1995 SC 955; AIR 1995 SCW 914; JT 1995 (1) SC 612; 1995(1) SCALE 396; 1995 (1) SLR 714; 1995 (1) UJ 320

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution, art. 136 - Criteria for admission to Medical College - Validity of - Criteria requiring that candidates should have studied their 10th, 10+1 and 10+2 classes as regular candidates in recognised institution of State itself - Condition introduced to curb practice of availing admission by producing forged domicile certificates - Condition reserved for 85% of seats and excludes 15% to be filled in All India basis - (A) Whether criteria arbitrary or unreasonable - (B) Whether relaxation can be extended to students who completed their 10th, 10+1, 10+2 from outside State - No relaxation of condition can be extended to them - (C) Whether exception in favour of children/wards of personnel of defence services or employees of State Govt./ All India Services discriminatory - Held, (A) criteria cannot be said to be arbitrary or unreasonable - (B) no relaxation of condition can be extended these students - (C) issue not raised, discussed or decided by in HC judgment therefore SC would not examine question in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
