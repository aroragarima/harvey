Â Firm A. T. B. Mehtabmajid and Company v State of Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 300; AIR 1963 SC 928; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 115; 1964 (1) SCJ 355; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 435; 1963 (14) STC 355; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 435

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Madras General Sales Tax Rules 1939, Madras General Sales Tax Act 1908 (IX of 1908)

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act 1908, ss. 3, 5 and 19 - Constitution Of India ,1950, arts. 301 and 304 - Madras General Sales Tax Rules 1939, r. 16 - Whether r. 16 of Madras General Sales Tax Rules is ultra vires of Constitution? - Art. 301 provides that trade shall be free throughout territory of India, it means that flow of trade shall run smooth and unhampered by any restriction either at boundaries of States or at any other points inside States themselves - If any Act imposes any direct restrictions on very movement of such goods it attracts provisions of art. 301, and its validity can be sustained only if it satisfies requirements of arts. 302 or 304 - Held, provisions of r. 16 (2) discriminate against imported hides or skins which had been purchased or tanned outside State and that therefore they contravene provisions of art. 304(a) of Constitution - Petition allowed.
