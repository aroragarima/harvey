Lakhan Sao (Deceased) Through Legal Heirs v Dharamu Chaudhary

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 992; (1991) 3 SCC 331; 1991 (17) ALR 363; 1991 (2) BC 84; 1991 (1) CCC 586; 1991 (1) GLH 288; JT 1992 (2) SC 639; 1991(1) SCALE 240; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 544; 1991 (1) UJ 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 100 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 145 - Suit for possession by Plaintiff-respondent decreed by Trial Court and was confirmed in appeal - High Court set aside decree and remanded case to first appellate court - On remand Addl. District Judge uphold plaintiff's title - Second appeal dismissed in limine by HC - Whether HC failed to exercise jurisdiction u/s. 100, Civil Procedure Code, in dismissing appeal in limine? - Held, no error had been committed by HC in his approach, it recorded definite findings on a clear analysis of entire evidence and findings are fully supported by evidence on record - Appeal dismissed.
