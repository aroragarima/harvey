Bhim Singh and another v State of Uttarakhand

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 89; (2015) 4 SCC 281; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1270; 2015 CRLJ 1428; 2015 (2) JCC 1011; JT 2015 (2) SC 177; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 614; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 22; 2015(2) SCALE 280; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Autopsy

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 113A, 113B - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (husband and in-laws) allegedly caused the death of deceased wife - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellants u/ss. 304B, 498A IPC and u/ss. 3, 4 of the Act - On appeal, HC acquitted all other co-accused but upheld the conviction of appellants - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the circumstantial evidence leads to the guilt of the accused persons, as the prosecution has proved that the accused had the opportunity to administer the poison and the doctors in the medical examination have also reported that the deceased was a healthy woman who, along with her family, was trying to reconcile matters with the accused persons. The fact that the death occurred in the house of the accused persons, leads to their guilt. They have not discharged the onus of disproving the presumptions u/ss. 113A and 113B of 1872 Act. Thus, the question of suicide is ruled out. The Court in this case is obliged to take the presumption raised u/s. 113B of the 1872 Act. Further, there is no missing link in the circumstantial evidence put forth by the prosecution, and hence the accused are not entitled to benefit of doubt. The guilt of the accused appellants, u/s. 304B IPC has been successfully established. Hence, no infirmity was found in the impugned judgment passed by the HC. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - If the prosecution has proved by producing cogent evidence that soon before her death the lady was subjected to cruelty or harassment in connection with the demand for dowry, then, it becomes obligatory for the Court to raise a presumption that the death is a dowry death.
