Jaipal And Others v State Of Haryana And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 June 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 773; (1988) 3 SCC 354; (1988) SCC (L&S) 785; AIR 1988 SC 1504; 1988 (7) ATC 771; 1988 (3) CompLJ 93; 1988 (57) FLR 210; JT 1988 (2) SC 528; 1988 LabIC 1673; 1994 (3) LLJ 972; 1988 (2) LLN 580; 1988(1) SCALE 1198; 1988 (2) SLR 710; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 411; 1988 (2) UJ 367

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, Part IV, art. 39(d) - Applicability of doctrine of 'Equal work equal pay' - Nature of duties performed by instructors working in adult and non-formal education scheme in Haryana were similar to those performed by squad teachers in social education scheme of that state - Whether the instructors are entitled to same pay scale as sanctioned to squad teachers under the perview of art. 39(d) of the Constitution - Held to entitled to equal pay - Petition allowed.
