Krishna Priya Ganguly Etc v University Of Lucknow & Ors. Etc

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 113; (1984) 1 SCC 307; AIR 1984 SC 186; 1984 ALJ 180; 1984 ALJ 1220; 1983 CRLJ 1982; 1983(2) SCALE 877; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 302; 1984 UJ 68

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act 1973, Medical Council Act 1956

Summary: Education - Constitution, art. 226 - UP State Universities Act, 1973, s. 28(5) - Order D/- 3-12-1980 - Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33 - Admission to examination in PG Medical course - Challenged - (A) Direction by HC to admit provisionally - ( B) Rules u/s. 33 of 1956 Act, for selection to PG Courses, r. (c), Para 1- Admission Committee also making, prescribing and indiscriminately appling a rule - (C) Condition of 2 years compulsory work dispensed - Held, factors to be considered - Held, Rule being supplementary to Govt. order, HC should not have directed so - Held, HC can not re-write or relax rules - Direction given by SC that if candidate failed in examination he should be required to fulfil condition in question before he was admitted to final examination - Appeal allowed.
