Prabhakar Ramakrishna Jodh v A. L. Pande and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 195; 1971 (1) LLJ 26; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - University of Saugar Act 1946, ss. 6(6), 32 - Terminated - Legality - Appellant was lecturer in S.B.R. College, which was managed by respondents/Governing Body - On 2-6-1960, appellant was served charge sheet by College Principal consisting of few charges and was asked to submit explanation - Appellant submitted explanation denying all charges and requested Governing Body to supply particulars on which first charge was based - Later, Governing Body terminated services of appellant w.e.f 1-7-1960, without holding any enquiry - Aggrieved appellant made representation to Governing Body which was rejected - Aggrieved appellant filed petition before HC for grant of writ which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, appellant's counsel urged that there was violation of procedure prescribed in cl. 8(vi)(a) of the 'College Code' and order of Governing Body dt. 30-6-1960, terminating appellant's services was illegal and ultra-vires and might be quashed by grant of writ in nature of certiorari - Respondents Counsel contended that there was no violation of said Procedure nor order of Governing Body, was defective in law - Since, question had not been investigated by HC - SC might like to add that respondents Counsel raised two preliminary objections in course of argument was that appellant had an alternative remedy u/cl. 8(vi)(c) of the 'College Code' which provides that aggrieved teacher might request for reference of dispute to Tribunal of Arbitration consisting of Vice-Chancellor and two other persons appointed by Executive Council of University - Respondents Counsel also contended that Governing Body of College was not statutory body performing public duties and no writ in nature of mandamus might, therefore, be issued to Governing Body of College - Respondents Counsel also conceded that those objections were not pressed before HC - SC, therefore, unable to entertain those preliminary arguments at that stage and they might be over-ruled - For reasons already expressed, SC allowed appeal, set aside judgment of HC, and remanded case to HC for investigating question whether there was violation of procedure contained in cl. 8(vi)(a) of the 'College Code' and for final determination of case in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
