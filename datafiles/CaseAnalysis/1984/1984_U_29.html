Dr. Pradeep Jain v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 June 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 72; (1984) 3 SCC 654; AIR 1984 SC 1420; 1984 (2) LLJ 481; 1984(1) SCALE 894; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 942

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Education - Reservation - Validity of - Admissions to a medical college or any other institution of higher learning situate in a state - Confined to those who have their 'domicile' within State or who are resident within State for a specified number of years or can any reservation in admissions be made for them - To give them precedence over those who do not possess 'domicile' or residential qualification within State - Irrespective of merit - Constitutionality thereof - Held, in a broader perspective classification may serve interests of nation better, though interests of individual, states to a small extent may be affected - This distinction in case of super-specialities proceeds on basis that in these very important spheres criterion for selection should be merit only without and institutional reservations or any reservation on ground of residence - Orders and directions proposed in regard to admission to M.B.B.S. and Post Graduate courses are also to be read as applicable mutatis mutandis in relation to admission to M.D.S. and M.D.S. courses - Question of constitutional validity of reservation of seats within reasonable limits on basis of residence and also equations of institutionalised reservation of seats clearly appear to be concluded by various decisions of Supreme Court - These decisions are binding on Court and are to be followed - Constitutional validity of such reservations within reasonable limit must, be upheld - Petition disposed of.
