D.P. Maheshwari v Delhi Admn. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 137; (1983) 4 SCC 293; (1983) SCC (L&S) 527; AIR 1984 SC 153; 1983 (47) FLR 477; 1983 LabIC 1629; 1983 (2) LLJ 425; 1984 (1) LLN 1; 1983(2) SCALE 313; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 949; 1983 (2) SLJ 542; 1983 UJ 958

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 136 - Duty of Tribunal - (A) Whether High Court u/art.226 can stop proceedings before a Tribunal to decide a preliminary issue - Held, arts. 226 and 136 cannot be allowed to be used to break resistance of workmen - High Court was totally unjustified in interfering with the order of the Labour Court u/art. 226 of the Constitution - (B) Appellant was occasionally deputed by the Managing Director to undertake some important missions - Question is what were his main duties Held, finding of the Labour Court is that he was mainly discharging duties of a clerical nature - Order accordingly.
