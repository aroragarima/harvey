Sukhdev Singh v Delhi State (Govt. Of Nct Of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 679; (2003) 7 SCC 441; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1714; AIR 2003 SC 3716; 2003 (3) CCR 239; 2003 (3) Crimes 363; 2004 (1) CRJ 666; 2003 CRLJ 4315; 2003 (3) JCC 1638; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 94; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 972; 2003(7) SCALE 102; 2003 (5) SLT 134; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 224; 2003 (6) Supreme 477; 2004 (1) UC 202

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 80, 302, 304, 307, 308 - Non-applicability of s. 302 - Held, the possibility about the trigger being pressed unintentionally for the second time during the course of scuffle does not in any manner help the accused-appellant despite the factual position in this case indicating use of the gun by the appellant. The inevitable conclusion is that the accused-appellant is the assailant. The factual scenario as presented by the prosecution and the conclusions of the High Court, go to show that in course of sudden quarrel, the offender fired the shots - Further held, though the High Court was justified in holding that s. 302 was not applicable, it was not correct in holding that Exception I applied. In fact, Exception 4 to s. 300 applied. The conviction is altered to s. 300, Part II instead of s. 304, Part I.
