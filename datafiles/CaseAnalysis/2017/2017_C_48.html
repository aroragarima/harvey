Executive Officer, Arulmigu Chokkanatha Swamy Koil Trust Virudhunagar v Chandran and others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 105; (2017) 3 SCC 702; AIR 2017 SC 1034; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 37; 2017 (122) ALR 46; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 293; 2017 (2) CTC 678; JT 2017 (2) SC 295; 2017 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 833; 2017 (4) MahLJ 809; 2017 (2) MLJ 404; 2017 (2) RCR(Civil) 1; 2017 (135) RD 811; 2017(2) SCALE 747; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 118

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1963, Documentary, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 34 - Suit Property - Order Challenged - 'X'/owner transferred suit land in name of 'Y', who executed GPA in favour of 'Z' - Respondent /plaintiff purchased suit property vide sale deed from 'Z' and later came to know that suit land was recorded in name of appellant/defendant no.1, temple - TC dismissed suit of plaintiff for want of failure to prove by document that 'X' had any right as well as possession over suit property, thereby held that suit property belong to defendant no.1 and denied declaration and permanent injunction - First Appellate Court affirmed findings of TC and held that date on which plaintiff purchased suit property, survey number was already sub-divide - HC in second appeal set aside decree of Lower Courts and directed defendant nos. 4-5/revenue authorities to include name of plaintiff after exclusion to extent of 5 acres and 10 cents of land in name of defendant no.1 - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, s. 34 of the Act provides for discretion of Court as to declaration of status or right and states that any person entitled to any legal character, or to any right as to any property, may institute a suit against any person denying, or interested to deny, his title to such character or right, and the court may in its discretion make therein a declaration that he is so entitled, and the plaintiff need not in such suit ask for any further relief: Provided that no court shall make any such declaration where the plaintiff, being able to seek further relief than a mere declaration of title, omits to do so. Hence, the suit for mere declaration when the plaintiff was not in possession of the property was not maintainable and hit by s. 34 of the Act the plaintiff having not sought for recovery of possession. In instant case, the plaintiff who was not in possession claimed only declaratory relief along with mandatory injunction in suit. In instant case, suit filed by the plaintiff for a mere declaration without relief of recovery of possession was clearly not maintainable and the TC has rightly dismissed the suit. Further, the HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/s. 100 of CPC could not have reversed the decree of the Courts below without holding that the above reasoning given by the courts below was legally unsustainable. Hence, the judgment of the HC is set aside and judgment that of Trial court and the First Appellate Court are restored. Appeal allowed.
