Akhil Bharat Goseva Sangh v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 606; (2006) 4 SCC 162; 2006 (3) ALD(SC) 82; JT 2006 (4) SC 482; 2006(3) SCALE 617; 2006 (4) SCJ 15; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 543

Case Digest

Subject: Air Law; Constitution; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayat Act, 1964, Mysore Prevention of Cow Slaughter and Cattle Preservation Act, 1964, Andhra Pradesh Prohibition of Cow Slaughter and Animal Preservation Act, 1977

Summary: Air Law - Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Labour & Industrial Law - Trade - Water - Andhra Pradesh Prohibition of Cow Slaughter and Animal Preservation Act, 1977, ss. 5 and 6 - Environment (Protection) Rules, 1986, r. 3 - Mysore Prevention of Cow Slaughter and Cattle Preservation Act, 1964, ss.4, 8, 9, 10, 11 and 18 - Water(Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1974, ss. 25 and 26 - Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayat Act, 1964, s. 131(3) - (A)Whether the High Court erred in dismissing the petitions all-together after holding that the state Govt. must strictly implement the provisions of the 1964 Act? - Held, no - It proper at this stage to direct the state Govt. and its instrumentalities to strictly enforce and implement the provisions of ss. 4, 8 to 11 and 18 of the 1964 Act without going into this question in detail - (B) Whether the view taken in Mohd. Hanif Quareshi v. State of Bihar regarding implementation of art. 48 directive principle vis-a-vis fundamental right guaranteed requires modification in the light of larger bench decision in Keshavananda Bharti Case and the subsequent decisions of this court? - Held, the decision of this court in the case of Mohd. Hanif Quareshi and others v. State of Bihar [supra] has now been over-ruled on this point by the Constitution bench decision of this court in Mirzapur case - In Mohd. Hanif Quareshi and others v. State of Bihar [supra] the contention that a law enacted to give effect to directive principles cannot be held to be violative of fundamental rights was rejected - (C) Whether the terms in art. 48 are wide enough to include all categories of bovine cattle? - Held, the interpretation of art. 48 of the Constitution has now been widened and "milch and draught cattle" include cattle which have become permanently incapacitated to be used for milch and draught purposes - (D) Whether s. 5 of the 1964 Act is unconstitutional in so far as it does not impose a total prohibition of slaughter of bovine cattle and whether a writ must be issued directing the state to prohibit slaughter of all bovine cattle in the State of Karnataka? - Held, declaring s. 5 of the 1964 Act as unconstitutional and directing the state Govt. to impose a total ban on slaughter of bovine cattle - Therefore, the prayer of the appellants in issue No. 4 to issue a writ to the state Govt. to totally prohibit slaughter of bovine cattle is rejected - Appeal allowed.
