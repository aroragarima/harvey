IDBI Trusteeship Services Limited v Hubtown Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 817; (2017) 1 SCC 568; AIR 2016 SC 5321; 2017 (1) ALD(SC) 76; 2017 (120) ALR 715; 2017 (1) ALT(SC) 47; 2017 (2) BC 280; 2017 (1) CLT(SC) 350; 2016 (6) CTC 647; JT 2016 (11) SC 28; 2017 (2) MahLJ 770; 2016 (8) MLJ 390; 2017 (135) RD 60; 2016(12) SCALE 24

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Voting, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, FEMA Regulations, 1999

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37 r. 3, s. 115 - FEMA Regulations, 1999, regns. 4, 5 - Corporate guarantee - Enforcement of rights - Validity - Appellant/petitioner filed instant appeal challenging summons for judgment in a summary suit filed on original side of HC by petitioner/debenture trustee to enforce rights that arise out of a Corporate Guarantee executed by respondent/defendant - Whether judgment in Mechalec Engineers and Manufacturers v. Messrs Basic Equipment Corporation, 1976 Indlaw SC 352, case continues to be the law even after the amendment of O. 37 in 1976.

Held, Milkhiram (India) Private Limited and Others v. Chamanlal Bros, 1965 Indlaw SC 508, case was a direct authority on the amended O. 37 provision, as the amended provision in O. 37 r. 3 was the same as the Bombay amendment which SC was considering in aforesaid judgment. The principles stated in para 8 of Mechalec Engineers and Manufacturers, 1976 Indlaw SC 352, case would now stand superseded, given the amendment of O. 37 r. 3 and binding decision of four judges in Milkhiram (India) Private Limited and Others case, 1965 Indlaw SC 508. In instant case payment under the said Guarantee was to debenture trustee for and on behalf of another Indian company, so that prima facie again there was no infraction of the Regulations. Since FMO becomes a 99% holder of another Company after requisite time period has elapsed, FMO might that stage utilise the funds received pursuant to overall structure agreements in Country. If that was so again prima facie there was no breach of the Regulations. At the stage that FMO wishes to repatriate such funds, RBI permission would be necessary. If RBI permission was not granted then again there would be no infraction of the Regulations. It cannot be said that defendant has raised a substantial defence to claim made in suit. As held by Single Judge, even if a triable issue might be said to arise on application of the Regulations, nevertheless, SC left with a real doubt about defendant's good faith and genuineness of such a triable issue. Defendant would be granted leave to defend the suit only if it deposits in HC the principal sum of Rs. 418 crores invested by FMO, or gives security for the said amount of Rs. 418 crores within a period of 3 months. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
