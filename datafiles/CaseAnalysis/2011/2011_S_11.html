SBEC Sugar Limited and another v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 85; (2011) 4 SCC 668; 2011 (264) E.L.T. 492; JT 2011 (2) SC 187; 2011(2) SCALE 240; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 585

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Proper Officer, Warehoused Goods, Warehouse, Such Goods, Export Promotion Capital Goods Scheme

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962 - Levy of interest - The Appellant no. 1 (Importer) imported certain capital goods and opted for getting those goods warehoused under Bond - Bonds for these goods were about to expire - The Appellant no. 1 made an application for extension of the bond period by six months in respect of all the consignments - However, the said request was rejected by the Asst. Commissioner on the ground that the application was not received in the Bond department at least 15 days before the expiry of the current period of bond - In the meantime, vide notification no.29/97, the Central Government extended the Export Promotion Capital Goods Scheme for the period 1997-2002 to agro-based industries - The effect of the said notification was that the capital goods used in the manufacture of agro-products, like sugar were exempted from the payment of whole of the customs duty - Subsequently, the Superintendent directed the importer to clear the goods covered under bond on payment of full duty of customs and other charges within a period of 15 days - The appellant no. 1 executed a bond and furnished a bank guarantee for 100% of the duty saved as required under notification no. 29/97 and made a representation to the Deputy Commissioner stating that since zero duty was chargeable on the goods under the EPCG licence, there was no question of levy of interest thereon - The Deputy Commissioner rejected said request for waiver of interest - The Appellant no. 1 filed writ petition against said order which was dismissed by the HC - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether request for waiver of the interest was rightly rejected by the Deputy Commissioner as affirmed by the HC? - Held, condition 6 of the licence granted under the EPCG Scheme was valid against goods which had already been shipped but not cleared, but, the benefit of exemption granted under the Scheme to the already imported goods would be available only in respect of those goods which were cleared u/s. 68 of the Act - Moreover, it was manifest that warehousing was permissible for a limited period, as contemplated under sub-sections (1)(a) and (1)(b) of s. 61 of the Act and such period was extendable on showing sufficient cause for the same - However, by operation of sub-section (2) of s. 61 of the Act, interest on the amount of duty is payable from the period of expiry of the permissible period till the date of clearance from the warehouse, regardless of whether the goods have remained in the warehouse beyond the permitted periods by reasons of extension or otherwise - Hence, request for waiver of the interest was rightly rejected by the Deputy Commissioner as affirmed by the HC - - Appeals dismissed.
