Commissioner Of Income-Tax, New Delhi v M/S Eli Lilly & Company (India) Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1011; (2009) 15 SCC 1; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 333; [2009] 312 ITR 225; 2009(4) SCALE 384; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 20; [2009] 178 TAXMAN 505

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Advance Payment, Average Rate, Deducted At Source, Foreign Technician, Assessability, Collection And Recovery, Special Allowance, Average Rate Of Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950 , art. 245 - Finance Act, 1983 - Finance Act, 1999 - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 133A, 192, 192(1), 200, 201, 201(1), 203, 203A, 206, 271C, 9, 9(1)(i) - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 42(1) - Income-tax Rules, 1962 - Assessment - Tax deduction - Validity - Assessee was engaged in manufacturing and selling pharmaceutical products during financial years 1992-93 to 1999-00 - Post-survey operations revealed that no work stood performed for Foreign Company - AO found that total remuneration paid was only on account of services rendered in India and therefore in terms of s. 9(1)(ii) income derived by expatriates was taxable in India and subject to s. 192(1) of 1961 Act - Consequently, tax- deduct or-assessed was asked to explain why it should not be declared as "assessee-in-default" u/ s. 201(1) as it had failed to deduct tax at source on aggregate salary received by four expatriates - In reply, tax-deduct or-assessed submitted that four expatriates were seconded by Foreign Company to Joint Venture company in India; they were employed by joint venture; they continued to be on rolls of said Foreign Company and they received Home Salary outside India in foreign currency from said Foreign Company - Tribunal and HC, however, held that tax- Deduct or-assessed was not under statutory obligation to deduct tax at source on Home Salary paid by said Foreign Company u/s. 192 as it was not paid by Joint Venture Company in India and consequently said Joint Venture was not an "assessed-in-default" u/ s. 201(1) of the 1961 Act - Hence instant appeals - Whether TDS provisions in Chapter XVII-B, which are in nature of machinery provisions enabling collection and recovery of tax are at all applicable to payments made abroad by Foreign Company/HO who had seconded expatriate(s) for rendering services in India to tax-deductor- assessed (employer) - Held, TDS provisions in Chapter XVII-B relating to payment of income chargeable under head "Salaries", which were in nature of machinery provisions to enable collection and recovery of tax forms an integrated Code with charging and computation provisions under 1961 Act, which determines assess ability/taxability of "salaries" in hands of employee-assessed - Consequently, s. 192(1) had to be read with s. 9(1)(ii) read with the Explanation thereto - Therefore, if any payment of income chargeable under head "Salaries" falls within s. 9(1)(ii) then TDS provisions would stand attracted - In this batch of civil appeals, identification of recipient of salary was not in dispute - In our view, therefore, tax-deduct or- assessed (respondent(s)) were duty bound to deduct tax at source u/s. 192(1) from Home Salary/special allowance(s) paid abroad by foreign company, particularly when no work stood performed for foreign company and total remuneration stood paid only on account of services rendered in India during period in question - AO was directed to examine each case to ascertain whether employee-assessed (recipient) has paid tax due on Home Salary/special allowance(s) received from foreign company - Appeals partly allowed.
