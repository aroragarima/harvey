Hardeep Singh and Others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

11 June 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 941; (2008) 12 SCC 39; (2008) 7 SCC 46; AIR 2008 SC 3113; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 3291; 2008 (3) JCC 1818; JT 2008 (10) SC 593; 2008 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1761; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2320; 2008 (5) MahLJ 490; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 125; 2008(8) SCALE 686; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 1114

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 r/w s. 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Order challenged on ground of reliability of evidence of witnesses who were close relatives of deceased - Applicability of s. 34 IPC, 1860 also in issue - Held, relationship is not a factor to affect credibility of a witness - It is more often than not that a relation would not conceal actual culprit and make allegations against an innocent person - Ground that the witness being a close relative and consequently being a partisan witness, should not be relied upon, has no substance - Under s. 34 the essence of the liability is to be found in the existence of a common intention animating the accused leading to the doing of a criminal act in furtherance of such intention - When an accused is convicted u/s. 302 r/w s. 34, in law it means that the accused is liable for the act which caused death of the deceased in the same manner as if it was done by him alone - Appeal disposed of.
