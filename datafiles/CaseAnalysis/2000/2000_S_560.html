Inderpal v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3079; (2001) 10 SCC 736; 2002 CRLJ 926

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 and 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Dying Declaration - Reliability of - Whether statement of deceased could be admitted in evidence against accused for his conviction u/s. 498A, IPC, 1860? - Held, unless statement of a dead person would fall within purview of s. 32(1) of Indian Evidence Act, 1872 there is no other provision under which same can be admitted in evidence - In order to make statement of a dead person admissible in law (written or verbal) statement must be as to cause of her death or as to any of circumstance of transactions which resulted in her death, in cases in which cause of death comes into question - In this case, by no stretch of imagination can statements of deceased and those quoted by witnesses be connected with any circumstance of transaction which resulted in her death - That apart, when dealing with an offence u/s. 498A, IPC, 1860 disjuncted from offence u/s. 306, IPC, 1860 question of her death not an issue for consideration and on that premise also s. 32(1) of Evidence Act, 1872 will stand at bay so far as these materials are concerned - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
