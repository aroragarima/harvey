Chase Bright Steel Limited v Shantaramshankar Sawant

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2073; (1994) 4 SCC 89; AIR 1994 SC 2114; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 561; JT 1994 (2) SC 192; 1994 (1) RCJ 372; 1994 (1) RCR(Rent) 690; 1994 (1) RentLR 364; 1994(1) SCALE 832; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 287; 1994 (1) UJ 544

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12(3)(a) and 12(3)(b) - Suit for eviction - (A) Notice of demand of arrears of rent - Scope of demand - (B) Whether rent would cease to be payable every month as permitted increases were payable on year to year basis - (C) Tenant paying and depositing in court only standard rent but not permitted increases pendentelite - If he can avoid decree for eviction - Held, includes demand for permitted increases which are part of rent - Would not cease, especially when claim for permitted increases was for period of about 3 years - He is not relieved of obligation u/s. 12(3)(b) and, hence, entitled to avoid decree for eviction - Appeal dismissed.
