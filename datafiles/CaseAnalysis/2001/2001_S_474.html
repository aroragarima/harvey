Satvir Singh And Ors v State Of Punjab And Anr

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 224; (2001) 8 SCC 633; (2002) SCC (Cr) 48; AIR 2001 SC 2828; 2001 (4) CCR 75; 2001 (4) Crimes 45; 2002 (1) CRJ 182; 2001 CRLJ 4625; 2001 (2) JCC 274; JT 2001 (8) SC 208; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 355; 2001(6) SCALE 513; 2001 (6) SLT 803; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 353; 2001 (7) Supreme 267; 2001 (2) UC 645

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 498A, 304B, 116, 511 - Abetment to suicide - Dowry death - Conviction - Appellants were convicted for the offence punishable u/ss. 306, 498A IPC - On appeal HC substituted s. 306 IPC with 304B t/w s. 116 IPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the appellants can be convicted u/s. 511 r/w s. 304B IPC.

Held, PW5 in her evidence said that 4 or 5 months after her marriage, she was ill-treated on the ground of insufficiency of dowry and then she reported the matter to her father. But PW5 did not say one word in her evidence regarding any other ill treatment relating to dowry thereafter. It is true, she said in her evidence that in November 1995, a sum of Rs.20,000/was paid by her father. But neither PW5 nor PW6 testified that the said amount was paid as part of the dowry or in connection with the marriage. SC cannot overlook two important events which had happened in the family during the said long interregnum of three years. One is the birth of the elder son on 12-11-1993 and the other is the birth of the second son on 10-6-1995. SC have to bear in mind the payment of Rs.20,000/- was made five months after the birth of the second son. Even PW6 had no case that his daughter was subjected to any ill treatment in connection with the demand for dowry on any day after she reported to him about the demand for further dowry way back in the early 1993 months. All amounts paid by the in-laws of the husband of a woman cannot become dowry. There is dearth of evidence to show that PW5 was subjected to cruelty or harassment connected with the demand for dowry, soon before the attempt to commit suicide. When the position is such it is an unnecessary exercise to consider whether s. 116 IPC can ever be linked with the offence u/s. 304B IPC. SC, therefore, conclude that appellants cannot be convicted u/s. 116 IPC either by linking it with s. 306 or with s. 304B IPC. Hence the conviction and sentence passed on them u/s. 116 IPC is set aside. SC have no reason to interfere with the conviction passed on the appellants u/s. 498A IPC. Appeals disposed of.
