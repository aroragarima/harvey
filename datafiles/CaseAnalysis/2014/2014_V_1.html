R. Unnikrishnan and another v V. K. Mahanudevan and others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 19; (2014) 4 SCC 434; AIR 2014 SC 1201; 2014(2) ALL MR 468; JT 2014 (1) SC 450; 2014 (2) KLT 524; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1191; 2014 (4) MahLJ 1; 2014(1) SCALE 305; [2014] 1 S.C.R. 350

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Service

Keywords: Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976, Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order (Amendment) Act, 2007, Scheduled Castes & Scheduled Tribes Lists (Modification) Order, 1956, Scheduled Caste Certificate

Summary: Administrative - Service - Scheduled Castes & Scheduled Tribes Lists (Modification) Order, 1956 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Scheduled Caste Certificate - Legality - Respondent applied to Tehsildar for grant of Scheduled Caste Certificate - Tehsildar held an enquiry and denyed to grant the certificate - Respondent filed petition before HC and same was disposed of with direction to Tehsildar to issue caste certificate - Certificate was accordingly issued - Respondent was appointed as Engineer under special recruitment scheme for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes candidates - After certificate was issued HC observed that large number of applications change of caste name was received - HC ordered that all such certificates as were corrected on basis of such applications ought to be scrutinized by Scrutiny Committee - In course of scrutiny, it was found that respondent actually belonged to OBC category - Accordingly notice was issued to respondent - Aggrieved by said proceedings respondent filed petition before HC - HC allowed the petition and quashed the ongoing enquiry proceedings - Aggrieved State filed appeal before DB and the same was allowed - Respondent filed review petition before DB and the same was dismissed - After fresh enquiry was commenced it was proved that respondent belonged to OBC - Scrutiny Committee issued show-cause notice to respondent as to why certificate issued in his favor should not be cancelled - Aggrieved respondent filed petition before HC and same was disposed of with direction that report should be placed before State Govt. for appropriate orders - State accordingly passed order - Aggrieved by order passed by state, respondent filed petition before Single Judge and the same was allowed - State preferred appeal before DB and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, there was no evidence of lack of bona fide by respondent - Protection available under decision of Milind's case 2000 Indlaw SC 2635 could, be admissible even to respondent - It followed that even if on true and correct construction of expression 'Thandan' appearing in Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order 2007 did not include 'Ezhuvas' and 'Thiyyas' known as 'Thandan' and assuming that two were different at all relevant points of time, fact that position was not clear till 2007 Act made clear distinction between two would entitle all those appointed to serve State up to date of Amending Act came into force to continue in service - HC found cancellation of Caste Certificate issued in favor of respondent was legally bad inasmuch as Scrutiny Committee had not applied its mind to material which was relied upon by respondent in that case - No enquiry into validity of certificate was found to have been conducted nor was order passed by Scrutiny Committee supported reasons - There was no legal flaw in that reasoning much less any perversity that might call for Court's interference - Order passed by HC took fair view of matter and does not suffer from any illegality or irregularity of any kind - Court made it clear that benefit granted to respondent as a Scheduled Caste candidate should be remained undisturbed, any advantage in terms of promotion or otherwise which respondent might have been granted after said date solely on basis of his being treated as Scheduled Caste candidate might if so advised be withdrawn by Competent Authority - It was axiomatic that respondent should not be entitled to claim any benefit in future as scheduled caste candidate but no benefit admissible to him as an OBC candidate should be denied - Appeals dismissed.
