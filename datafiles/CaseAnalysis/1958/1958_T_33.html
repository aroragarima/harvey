Haji Mohammad Ekramul Haq v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1959 SC 488; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 922; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 922

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Defence of India Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 5 and 45 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 112 - Defence of India Act, 1939, s. 19 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 23 - (A) Whether an expert opinion is de facto admissible as evidence? - Held, an expert report becomes admissible only after expert is examined as a witness and has faced cross examination - Mere opinion of an expert unsupported by reasons is not admissible as evidence - (B) What are principles to be used for determining compensation under s. 19 of Defence of India Act, 1939? - Further held, same principles as provided in s. 23(1) of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 are applicable for determining compensation - Appeal partly allowed.
