Supreme Court of India

10 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 908; (2009) 8 SCC 492; 2009 (3) ACC 370; AIR 2009 SC 3041; JT 2009 (9) SC 355; 2009(9) SCALE 469; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 536

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Transport Department, Laying Of Rules And Notifications

Summary: Constitution - Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 162 and 166, sch. VI - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 138(2)(b), 114 and 211 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 23 - Memorandum by State of Meghalaya u/s. 138(2)(b) of MV Act - Was held to be illegal, and a writ of mandamus was issued by the High Court - Writ of mandamus was issued to make rules in exercise of its powers thereunder - Allegations were that trucks carrying cargo in the State were compelled to pay substantial amounts to various entities at various points, viz., weighbridges, local tribal chiefs, the authorities implementing the provisions of Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981 and police officers of the State - Contention before HC was raised on behalf of State that check posts have been established and fees were being collected without framing any Rules, purportedly in terms of the Memorandum - Whether the State Govt. is empowered to issue any executive order in respect of the matters required to be prescribed by Rules? - Impugned Memorandum examined, provisions perused - MV Act, examined - Undisputedly, Act in unequivocal terms provides to specify among other things the weight which a carrier of a given description may carry; such provisions are necessary not only for construction and maintenance of road but also to prevent accidents - Provisions of the Act mandate that the unladen weight and laden weight must be determined; without doubt weighing devices had to be provided for the said purpose; for it Rules may have to be framed - Position of law well settled, that even in a case where the statute provides for certain things to be done, subject to Rules, any action taken without framing the Rules would not render any action invalid; if a statute is workable even without framing of the Rules, the same has to be given effect to; the law itself except in certain situations does not envisage vacuum - Provision is penal in nature so compliance was imperative - The State for giving effect to the provisions of the statute may upon itself take the burden of providing for weighbridges and collection of fees, etc., in exercise of its powers u/art. 298 or art. 162; it may however permit to provide parties to install weighbridges, subject to regulations; Memorandum provided for broad terms and conditions under which private parties were authorised to set up weighbridges and collection of fees - State is otherwise also competent to impose such fees under respective item of List II of Seventh Schedule - Where the State or its agencies render services for the purpose of effectuation of the provisions of a Central Act, it is entitled to charge a reasonable amount in respect thereof - Impugned judgement unsustainable, set aside - Appeals allowed.
