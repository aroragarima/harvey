Messrs Gammon India Limited v Spl. Chief Secretary and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 56; (2006) 3 SCC 354; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 103; JT 2006 (2) SC 494; 2006(2) SCALE 414; 2006 (3) SCJ 83; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 304; 2006 (145) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Rate Of Tax, Rate, Relevant Provisions, Andhra Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2005, Andhra Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1891, English Interpretation Act, 1889, Workmen's Compensation Act, 1937

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 5B, 5B(2), 7A(2)(ii) - Andhra Pradesh Value Added Tax Act, 2005, ss.80, 80(3) - Andhra Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1891, s. 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 6, 6(c) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 19(1)(a) - Jurisdiction of Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, Warangal Division, Andhra Pradesh in initiating and completing penalty proceedings under APGS Tax Act after its repeal - Held, there is a simultaneous repeal and reenactment and APVA Tax Act clearly saves earlier provisions - Rights and liabilities accrued or incurred under APGS Tax Act shall continue even after it is repealed - Whenever there is a repeal of an enactment and simultaneous reenactment, reenactment is to be considered as reaffirmation of old law and provisions of repealed Act which are thus reenacted continue in force uninterruptedly unless, reenacted enactment manifests an intention incompatible with or contrary to provisions of repealed Act - Assistant Commissioner (Commercial Taxes), Warangal Division was fully justified in initiating and completing proceedings under APGS Tax Act even after it is repealed - Appeals disposed of.
