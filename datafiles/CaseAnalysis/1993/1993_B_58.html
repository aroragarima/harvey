Bhimappa Jinnappa Naganur v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 61; (1993) Supp3 SCC 449; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1053; AIR 1993 SC 1469; 1993 (3) Crimes 411; 1993 CRLJ 1801; 1993 CRLR 221; JT 1993 (2) SC 165; 1993(1) SCALE 705

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300- Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Reliability on evidence of eye-witnesses was in question - When First Information Report was lodged, it didn't mention presence of any persons as eye-witnesses plus statement of that eye-witness was inconsistent with medical evidence as to time of death of deceased - Whether accused is to be charged to murder based on evidence?- Held, presence of those eye- witnesses was rightly disbelieved - Behaviour of eyewitness found unnatural as she being wife of deceased not trying to nurse him or offering any help which would have shown her presence at time of incident - Conviction set aside- Appeal allowed.
