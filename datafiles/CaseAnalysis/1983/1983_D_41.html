State Of U.P. v Dr.G.K. Ghosh

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 141; (1984) SCC (Cr) 46; (1984) 1 SCC 254; AIR 1984 SC 1453; 1983 ALJ 1170; 1983 (2) Crimes 856; 1985 CRLJ 904; 1983 CrLR(SC) 490; 1983(2) SCALE 407; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 993

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(1)(d) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - CrPC, 1973, s. 378 - Doctor in a Govt. Hospital was found guilty of demanding and accepting illegal gratification from the father of a patient - Conviction u/s.5(1)(d) of 1947 Act and u/s.161 of IPC, 1860 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Oral evidence as to gratification corroborated by circumstantial evidence but fact that trap witnesses were not independent - (B) CrPC, 1973, s. 378 - Appeal against acquittal by High Court on the ground that that HC had acquitted on a point which was not even taken as defence plea - Held, no difficulty in upholding the prosecution case - Held, decision of HC not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
