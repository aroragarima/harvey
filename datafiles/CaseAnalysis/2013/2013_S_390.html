Ganga Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 402; (2013) 7 SCC 278; AIR 2013 SC 3008; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 3020; 2013 (3) CGLJ 320; 2013 CRLJ 3966; 2013 (3) JabLJ 249; 2013(8) SCALE 309; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 400

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.157 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.376 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.313 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed offence of rape - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court u/s.376 IPC - HC also upheld the decision of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Held, evidence of PW5 was corroborated by other evidence - S.157 of Act provided that in order to corroborate the testimony of a witness, any former statement made by such witness relating to the same fact at or about the time when the fact took place, or before any authority legally competent to investigate the fact might be proved - Evidence of PW5 was corroborated by the evidence of her mother-in-law (PW2) before whom she stated about the commission of the rape by the appellant soon after the incident the very same evening - Evidence of PW5 was also corroborated by the FIR before the Investigating Officer, PW10, before whom she lodged the complaint one day after the incident - Further, though the medical evidence of PW9 and the medical examination report did not give any definite opinion on whether or not PW5 suffered any sexual intercourse, soon after the medical examination, the petticoat and vaginal smear slides (which were prepared and sealed) were sent for further examination and the report of State Forensic Science Laboratory confirmed spots of semen and spermatozoa - Evidence confirmed that PW5 had been subjected to sexual intercourse some time before she lodged the complaint in the police station - Hence, the forensic evidence was not entirely in conflict with the evidence of PW5 so as to belie her story that she was raped by the appellant - SC further found that the appellant did not take a defence in his statement u/s.313 of CrPC that the sexual intercourse was with the consent of PW5 - Instead, appellant denied having had any sexual intercourse with PW5 and had taken a stand that he was falsely implicated on account of a quarrel between him and the husband of PW5 - Yet, Trial Court held that there was proof of sexual intercourse between the appellant and PW5, but the sexual intercourse was with the consent of PW5 - SC was of the considered opinion that as the appellant had not taken any defence of consent of PW5, the Trial Court was not correct in recording the finding that there was consent of PW5 to the sexual intercourse committed by the appellant and should have instead considered the defence of the appellant that he had been falsely implicated because of a quarrel between him and the husband of PW5 - SC had, however, considered this defence of the appellant but found that except making a suggestion to PW2, appellant did not produce any evidence in support of this defence - As PW2 had denied the suggestion, SC could not accept the defence of the appellant that he was falsely implicated on account of a quarrel between the appellant and the husband of PW5 - Appeal dismissed.
