C.S. D. Swamy v State

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 95; AIR 1960 SC 7; 1960 CRLJ 131; 1960 SCJ 160; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 461

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, ss. 5(1)(a) and 5(1)(d) - Appellant, holding a respectable position in Govt. office alleged to have taken bribe from a firm in connection with import of fertilizers - Whether appellant was liable to be acquitted on ground of failure to prove charge u/s. 5(1)(a)? - Held, no - Failure to prove charge u/s. 5(1)(a) does not amount to failure u/s. 5(1)(d) - Requirement of s. 5(3) is that accused person shall be presumed to be guilty of criminal misconduct in discharge of his official duties 'unless the contrary is proved' - Words of statute are peremptory, and burden must lie all time on accused to prove contrary - As soon as requirements of sub-s. (3) of s. 5, have been fulfilled, the court will not only be justified in making, but is called upon to make, presumption that accused person is guilty of criminal misconduct within meaning of s. 5(1)(d) - In order to succeed, in respect of the charge u/s. 5(1)(a), prosecution has to prove that accused person had accepted or obtained or agreed to accept or attempted to obtain from any person any gratification by way of bribe within meaning of s. 161, IPC, 1860 - Charge u/s. 5(1)(d) does not require any such proof - If there is evidence forthcoming to satisfy the requirements of earlier part of sub-s. (3) of s. 5, conviction for criminal misconduct can be had on the basis of the presumption which is a legal presumption to be drawn from the proof of facts in the earlier part of the sub-s. (3) aforesaid - Hence, failure of charge u/cl. (a) of sub-s. (1) of s. 5, does not necessarily mean failure of charge u/s. 5(1)(d) - To succeed in respect of charge u/s. 5(1)(a) prosecution has to prove that accused had accepted or agreed to accept from any person any gratification by way of bribe within meaning of s. 161 - Appeal dismissed.
