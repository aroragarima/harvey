New Horizon Sugar Mills Limited v Government of Pondicherry

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 427; (2012) 10 SCC 575; AIR 2013 SC 584; [2012] 175 Comp Cas 475; 2013 (1) CompLJ 272; 2012 (6) CTC 220; 2013 (1) DRTC 632; JT 2012 (9) SC 624; [2012] 8 S.C.R. 874

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maharashtra Protection of Interest of Depositors (in Financial Establishments) Act, 1999, Financial Establishments Act, 2004

Summary: Banking & Finance - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254(2) - Credit from Bank - Default in repayment - Recovery proceedings - Challenged - Appellant availed credit facilities from respondent bank - Appellant defaulted in repayment - Respondent bank initiated recovery proceedings under 2002 Act - Appellant challenged recovery proceedings before HC - HC ordered sale of secured property in auction - Auction was held - In the meantime, Govt. issued G.O.Ms. No.12 dt. 18-2-2006 under the provisions of 2004 Act attaching the property acquired by another claimant (PNL Ltd) - Due to the issuance of G.O.Ms. No.12, auction purchaser did not get Sale Certificate - Appellant challenged the validity of G.O.Ms. No.12 as well as the validity of 2004 Act before the HC - HC upheld the validity of the 2004 Act - Hence instant appeal - (A) Whether the subject matter covered by the Pondicherry Act was relatable to Entries 43, 44, 45 and 97 of the Union List or to Entries 1, 30 and 32 of the State List - Held, Entries relating to the State List referred to above, and in particular Entry 30, appeared to be more appropriate source of legislative authority of the State Assembly for enacting laws in furtherance of such Entry - Power to enact the Pondicherry Act, Tamil Nadu Act and Maharashtra Act was relatable to Entries 1, 30 and 32 of the State List, which involved the business of unincorporated trading and money-lending which fell within the ambit of Entries 1, 30 and 32 of the State List - Even if it was to be accepted that the Pondicherry Act was relatable to Entries 43, 44 and 45 of List I, it could be equally said that the said enactment was also relatable to Entries 1, 30 and 32 of List II, thereby leaving the field of legislation open, both to the Central Legislature as well as the State Legislature - In such situation, unless there was anything repugnant in the State Act in relation to the Central Act, provisions of the State Act would have primacy in determining the lis in instant case - Apart from the above, the provisions of the Pondicherry Act were also saved by virtue of art. 254(2) of the Constitution - (B) Whether the decision of SC in K.K. Baskaran's case 2011 Indlaw SCO 201, upholding the validity of the Tamil Nadu Act, would also be applicable for determining the validity of the Pondicherry Act - Held, in HC's view the decision rendered by the Madras HC in K.K. Baskaran's case 2011 Indlaw SCO 201, relied on - SC had to bear in mind that the validity of the Tamil Nadu Act and the Maharashtra Act was upheld by the Madras HC and SC - Objects of the Tamil Nadu Act, Maharashtra Act and Pondicherry Act being the same and/or similar in nature, and since the validity of the Tamil Nadu Act and the Maharashtra Act was upheld, decision of Madras HC in upholding the validity of the Pondicherry Act should also be affirmed - Appeals dismissed.
