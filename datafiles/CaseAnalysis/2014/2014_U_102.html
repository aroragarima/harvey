Ram Daan (Dead) Through Lrs. v Urban Development Trust

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 546; (2014) 8 SCC 902; 2014 (106) ALR 705; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 158; 2015 (1) MahLJ 34; 2015 (1) MPLJ 3; 2014 (125) RD 738; 2014(9) SCALE 80; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 360

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Suit For Permanent Injunction

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Suit for permanent injunction - Sustainability - Appellant filed suit before Trial Court for decree of permanent injunction restraining respondent from dispossessing appellant of plot of land and further not to demolish construction existing over said property - Appellant pleaded uninterrupted possession of suit scheduled property from year 1942 - Respondent disputed claim of uninterrupted possession from the year 1942 - Suit was dismissed by Trial Court - Appeal before Appellate Court - Appeal was dismissed - Appeal before HC - HC dismissed appellants appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by HC was just and proper -

Held, there was no assertion that respondent was real owner of property though it was very boldly submitted that State of Rajasthan was owner of the property - However, respondent was not the State of Rajasthan - What exactly was legal character of respondent was not known - Assumed that State of Rajasthan was real owner of property in question, whether respondent was legally authorized by State of Rajasthan to recover possession of suit scheduled property was not clear from record - Assumed that respondent had some authority to evict appellant, eviction should be by process known to law, that's either by filing suit to evict appellant or by resorting to some other procedure duly authorized by law - However, from written statement it did not appear that respondent took any steps contemplated u/s. 91 of the said Act - It appeared that respondent was under the belief that once s. 91 was invoked (in 1965), such invocation for evicting appellant summarily could eternally be relied upon by anybody for evicting appellant - SC opined that Law did not sanction such process - Even according to respondent, subsequent to alleged eviction in 1965, appellant re-entered possession of suit scheduled property - In which case if respondent desired to evict appellant again, same should be done in accordance with law by initiating appropriate proceedings - Until such process of eviction in accordance with law was initiated, appellant's possession of suit scheduled property was required to be protected - Thus, there was no clear pleading in case on hand as to who was real owner of suit schedule property and absence of any assertion that respondent was owner of property, SC opined that appellant was entitled for injunction, as prayed, subject, of course, to right of true owner of property (whoever it was) to evict appellant in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
