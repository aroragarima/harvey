Ramesh Kumar Soni v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 118; (2013) 14 SCC 696; AIR 2013 SC 1896; 2013 CRLJ 1738; 2013 (2) JabLJ 207; JT 2013 (3) SC 375; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 271; 2013(3) SCALE 51; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 1129

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1994, Code of Criminal Procedure (Madhya Pradesh Amendment) Act, 2007

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 408, 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, sch. I - Code of Criminal Procedure (Madhya Pradesh Amendment) Act of 2007 - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 408, 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC - On the date of registration of case, offences in question were triable by a Magistrate of First Class in terms of sch. I of CrPC - By introduction of Amendment Act, which amended sch. I of CrPC and among others made offences u/ss. 467, 468 and 471 of IPC triable by Court of Sessions instead of a Magistrate of First Class - Full Bench of HC in the reference of Amendment of First Schedule of Criminal Procedure Code by Criminal Procedure Code (M. P. Amendment) Act, 2007, held that all cases pending before Judicial Magistrate First Class as on 22-2-2008 remained unaffected by amendment - Appellant relied on reference and filed an application before Trial Court contending that amendment was not retrospective, thus sought direction for remission of case for trial by Magistrate - Trial Court dismissed appellant's request - Aggrieved appellant filed revision before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant could be tried by Judicial Magistrate, First Class, for offences u/ss. 408, 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC notwithstanding the fact that sch. I of CrPC as amended by Act of 2007, made offences u/ss. 467, 468 and 471 of IPC Code triable only by Court of Sessions - Held, no case was pending before the Magistrate against appellant as on the date the Amendment Act came into force - Magistrate on receipt of a charge-sheet which was tantamount to institution of a case against the appellant was duty bound to commit the case to Sessions as offences with which appellant was charged were triable only by the Court of Sessions - Case having instituted after Amendment Act had taken effect, there was no need to look for any provision in Amendment Act for determining whether amendment was applicable even to pending matters as on the date of amendment no case was instituted against appellant nor was it pending before any Court to necessitate a search for any such provision in Amendment Act - Trial Court and HC were, in that view, perfectly justified in holding that order of committal passed by Magistrate was a legally valid order and appellant could be tried only by Court of Sessions to which case stood committed - Amendment to CrPC in instant case has effect of shifting the forum of trial of accused from Court of Magistrate First Class to Court of Sessions - Apart from fact that as on the date the amendment came into force no case was instituted against appellant nor Magistrate had took cognizance against appellant, any amendment shifting the forum of trial had to be on principle retrospective in nature in absence of any indication in Amendment Act to contrary - Appellant could not claim a vested right of forum for his trial for no such right was recognized - View taken by the Full Bench in reference holding amended provision to be applicable to pending cases was not correct on principle - Decision rendered by Full Bench would, thus, stand overruled but only prospectively since trial of cases that were sent back from Sessions Court to Court of Magistrate First Class under the orders of Full Bench might have concluded or might be at an advanced stage - Any change of forum at that stage in such cases would cause unnecessary and avoidable hardship to accused in those cases if they were to be committed to Sessions for trial in the light of amendment - Overruling of Full Bench decision of Madhya Pradesh HC would not affect cases that already tried or were at an advanced stage before Magistrates - In Re: Amendment of First Schedule of Criminal Procedure Code by Criminal Procedure Code (M. P. Amendment) Act, 2007, overruled - Appeal dismissed.
