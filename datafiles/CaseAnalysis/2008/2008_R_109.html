Sulochana v Rajinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2589; (2008) 15 SCC 538; AIR 2008 SC 2611; JT 2008 (9) SC 216; 2008 (2) RCR(Rent) 3; 2008(8) SCALE 304; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 429

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: (A) Rent Control - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 2(b), 23-J, 45 - Composite suit for eviction - Jurisdiction of civil court - Respondent was a tenant in the disputed premises which were purchased by appellant - Appellant demanded rent from respondent - Respondent neglected to make payment of rent - Appellant terminated the tenancy and requested the respondent to vacate the premises - Respondent did not vacate the premises, appellant filed a composite suit on various grounds i.e., default in payment of rent, bona fide requirement of tenanted premises and denial of landlord's title by tenant - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of appellant - Respondent filed appeal before the First Appellate Court - First Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - Respondent preferred Second Appeal - Having held that the Trial Court had no jurisdiction to entertain the composite suit of appellant, HC allowed the Second Appeal of respondent - Whether the civil court has jurisdiction to entertain a composite suit for eviction - Held, respondent was inducted as tenant by predecessor-in-interest of disputed premises and not by appellant, therefore, appellant was a landlord within the meaning of s. 2(b) of the Act - Further, appellant was not a 'specified landlord' u/s. 23-J since she was not a widow or divorced wife - Jurisdiction of the civil court can be excluded only if the matter comes within the purview of s. 45 of the Act of Chapter III-A thereof - S. 45 has no application to the facts and circumstances of the present case since eviction contemplated under Chapter III-A relates to an eviction of the tenant by the landlord as defined in s. 23-J of the Act - HC failed to notice that the definition of 'landlord' as contained in s. 2(b) and s. 23-J are different and failed to notice the limited application of Chapter-IIIA of the Act - Since the appellant is a landlord within the meaning of s. 2(b), the civil court has the jurisdiction to entertain the composite suit, therefore, impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 2(b), 23-J - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Definition of 'specified landlord' - Interpretation of - Held, definition of 'specified landlord' as contained in s. 23-J of the Act is not as broad as the definition of the same term as contained in s. 2(b) thereof - A statute must be read, keeping in view the constitutional scheme of equality as adumbrated in art. 14 of the Constitution - Once a special benefit has been conferred on a special category of landlord, the same must receive strict construction - Appeal allowed.
