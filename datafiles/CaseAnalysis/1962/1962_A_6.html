Abdul Mateen v Ram Kailash Pandey and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 501; AIR 1963 SC 64; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 64 A, 64, 57, 48 and 48(3) - (A) Whether permits can be issued beyond limits fixed u/s. 47(3) Regional Transport Authority ? - Held, where a limit has been fixed u/s. 47(3) by Regional Transport Authority and thereafter said authority proceeds to consider applications for permits u/s. 48 r/w s. 57 of Regional Transport Authority must confine number of permits issued by it within those limits - On an appeal u/s. 64 or revision u/s. 64-A as inserted by Bihar Act by an aggrieved person, Appellate Authority or Revisional authority must equally be confined to issue of permits within limits fixed u/s. 47(3) not open to issue permits beyond limits fixed u/s. 47(3) - (B) Whether revisional power of Regional Transport Authority is subject to s. 48?- Further held, Regional Transport Authority can revise general order passed by it u/s. 47(3) - Revision is a separate power in authority and not a power arising when it is dealing with individual permits - When an appeal is taken from an order u/s 48 and a revision is taken by an aggrieved person u/s. 64 A, power of Appellate Authority as well as of revisional authority is as much subject to s. 47(3) as power of Regional Transport Authority u/s. 48 - (C) Whether number of vacancies shown in an advertisement issued by Regional Transport Authority calling for applications for vacancies is number fixed u/s. 47(3)? - Further held, it may not be generally possible to conclude from number of vacancies shown in an advertisement issued by Regional Transport Authority calling for applications for vacancies that it is number fixed u/s. 47(3) by Regional Transport Authority - There is one exception to this general rule and that is when a new route is being advertised for first time - Appeal dismissed.
