C. Magesh and Others etc. v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 327; (2010) 5 SCC 645; AIR 2010 SC 2768; JT 2010 (4) SC 467; 2010 (4) KarLJ 161; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 666; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 382; 2010(4) SCALE 520; [2010] 5 S.C.R. 623

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Criminal - Murder - Dying declaration - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/Union members allegedly caused the death of deceased/workers - Trial Court acquitted appellants - State preferred appeal before the HC - HC reversed the Trial Court judgment and convicted appellants on the basis of two dying declarations - Whether HC order is sustainable - Held, possibility of the complaint being dictated by the management of appellants cannot be negated - Doctors certified that the deceased persons were in a fit state of health to have their statements recorded only at the end of recording of their statements and not prior to the statement being recorded - HC had no occasion to have passed order of conviction, that too without removing the doubts with regard to correctness, legality and propriety of two dying declarations - Prosecution witnesses alleged that appellant had attacked them with clubs, however, Doctor's evidence does not disclose anywhere that the injuries sustained by any of the injured persons could have been caused with clubs - Therefore, appellants were acquitted from the charges and impugned orders modified to that extent - Appeals allowed.

(B) Practice & Procedure - Criminal trial - Appraisal of ocular evidence - Scope - Held, evidence of the eye witness requires a careful assessment and must be evaluated for its creditability in criminal trial - Since the fundamental aspect of criminal jurisprudence rests upon the stated principle that 'no man is guilty until proven so', hence utmost caution is required to be exercised in dealing with situations where there are multiple testimonies and equally large number of witnesses testifying before the court - Therefore, there must be a string that should join the evidence of all the witnesses and thereby satisfying the test of consistency in evidence amongst all the witnesses - Appeals allowed.
