Sikkim Subba Associates v State of Sikkim

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19812; (2001) 5 SCC 629; AIR 2001 SC 2062; 2001 (2) ARBLR 17; JT 2001 (5) SC 186; 2001 (2) RAJ 52; 2001(3) SCALE 721; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 261; 2001 (4) SLT 164

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Avoid multiplicity of proceedings - Retrospective deletion - 'Misconduct' in s. 30 (a) - Whether arbitrator acted arbitrarily and irrationally on a perverse understanding or misreading of materials but also found to have misdirected himself on vital issues before him so as to render award to be one in utter disregard of law and precedents? - Held, a careful scrutiny of award discloses total non-application of mind to actual, relevant and vital aspects and issues in their proper perspective - Arbitrator could not have awarded any damage whatsoever and, at any rate, such a fabulous and astronomical sum on mere conjectures and pure hypothetical exercises, absolutely divorced from rationality and realities, inevitably making law, equity and justice, in process, a casualty - Award of arbitrator set aside - Appeal disposed of.
