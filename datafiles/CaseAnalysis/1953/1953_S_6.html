Â Satish Chandra Anand v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 4; AIR 1953 SC 250; 1953 SCJ 323; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Service - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14, 16(1), 32(1) and 311 - A civil servant, who had been engaged on basis of a special contract for a certain term, was, on expiry of term, re-appointed by a further contract on a temporary basis - Later on discharged - (A) Whether there has been infringement of rights conferred by art. 311? - Held, art. 311 had no application because there was neither a dismissal nor a removal from service nor a reduction in rank - No question of discrimination arose - (B) Whether termination of contractual service by notice has led to breach of fundamental rights u/arts. 14 and 16 (1)? - Further held, art. 16(1) was inapplicable - Petitioner had not been denied any opportunity of employment or of appointment - Treated just like any other person to whom an offer of temporary employment was made - There can be no grievance against an offer of temporary employment on special terms as opposed to permanent employment - Remedy of a writ was misconceived - Petition dismissed.
