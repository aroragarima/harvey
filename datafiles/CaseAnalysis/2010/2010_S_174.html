May George v Special Tahsildar and others

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 418; (2010) 13 SCC 98; 2010 (5) ALD(SC) 34; 2010 (81) ALR 241; 2011 (2) AWC 1369; 2010 (4) CTC 724; JT 2010 (5) SC 526; 2010 (2) KLT 866; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 114; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 270; 2010(6) SCALE 71; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 204

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Haryana (Control of Rent & Eviction) Rules, 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 9(3) and 11 - Appellant's land was acquired by government for development of Electrical/Electronics Industrial Estate and award u/s. 11 of Act was made - Appellant challenged said award submitting that she had purchased the said land on 27-9-1961 and mutation had taken place, thus her name stood recorded in the revenue record - Appellant further submitted that she had never been aware of the acquisition proceedings as she was not served with notice u/s. 9(3) of the Act and she got the information first time that a part of her land had been acquired only on receiving the notice dt. 8-12-1986 issued by Respondent-Department to the effect - Single Judge dismissed said petition - On appeal, Court has given liberty to the appellant to move an application for making reference u/s. 18 of the Act and further directed the Land Acquisition Collector to make a reference, if such an application is filed - Appellant contended that provisions of s. 9 are mandatory in nature and non-compliance thereof would vitiate the Award and all other consequential proceedings - Hence, present appeal - Whether the provisions of s. 9(3) of Act are mandatory in nature and non-compliance thereof, would vitiate the Award and subsequent proceedings under the Act? - Held, in order to declare a provision mandatory, the test to be applied is as to whether non-compliance of the provision could render entire proceedings invalid or not - Court may find out as what would be the consequence which would flow from construing it in one way or the other and as to whether the Statute provides for a contingency of the non-compliance of the provisions and as to whether the non-compliance is visited by small penalty or serious consequence would flow therefrom and as to whether a particular interpretation would defeat or frustrate the legislation and if the provision is mandatory, the act done in breach thereof will be invalid - In the present case, court was fully satisfied that notice had been affixed on the impugned land, satisfying the requirement of law and the Award had been made within limitation - Though appellant was aware of the proceedings conveniently, chose to remain silent and made use of the notice, asking her removal from the unauthorised occupation as the basis of challenging the Award and land acquisition proceedings after inordinate delay of 10 years and vesting of land in the State itself - Moreover, HC has granted the relief to the appellant to make the application for making a reference u/s. 18 of the Act and further directions have been issued to the Collector to make the reference and further to the Tribunal to decide the same within the stipulated period, instead of approaching this Court in appeal, the appellant ought to have pursued that remedy - Appeal dismissed.
