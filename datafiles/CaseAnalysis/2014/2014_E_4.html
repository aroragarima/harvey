Jafar Imam Naqvi v Election Commission of India

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 2014 SC 2537; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 146; 2014 (105) ALR 706; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 38; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 127; 2014(7) SCALE 95; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, Religious Institutions (Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1988

Summary: Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Hate speeches during election campaign - Whether SC in exercise of power u/art. 32 of the Constitution should enter into the arena of effect and impact of election speeches rendered during the election campaign in a PIL (Public Interest Litigation) -

Held, PIL was initially used by SC as a tool to take care of certain situations which related to the poor and under-privileged who were not in a position to have access to the Court - Thereafter, from time to time, the concept of PIL expanded with the change of time and the horizon included the environment and ecology, the atrocities faced by individuals in the hands of the authorities, financial scams and various other categories including eligibility of the people holding high offices without qualification - But a PIL pertaining to speeches delivered during election campaign, could not be put on the pedestal of a real PIL - There were laws to take care of it - In the name of a Constitutional safeguard entering into this kind of arena, in would not be within the Constitutional parameters - Petition dismissed.
