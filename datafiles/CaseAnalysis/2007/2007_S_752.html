R.N. Jadi and Brothers and Others v Subhashchandra

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1173; (2007) 6 SCC 420; AIR 2007 SC 2571; 2007 (68) ALR 451; 2007 (4) AWC 3430; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 386; 2007 (3) CHN 143; 2007 (3) CHN 183; 2007 (4) CTC 326; JT 2007 (9) SC 165; 2007 (6) KarLJ 161; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 59; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 587; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 139; 2007(9) SCALE 202; 2007 (5) SCJ 785; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 241; 2007 (3) UC 1857

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 8, r. 1 - Delay in filing written statement - Written statement filed beyond 90 days - Written statement accepted by Trial Court - Whether O. 8, r. 1 was mandatory and trial judge could not have accepted written statement filed beyond 90 days from date of service? - Held, post amendment O. 8, r. 1 casts an obligation on defendant to file written statement within 30 days from date of service of summons and within extended time falling within 90 days - O. 8, r. 1 does not deal with power of court and also does not specifically take away power of court to take written statement on record though filed beyond time as provided for - Nature of provision contained in O. 8, r. 1 is procedural and not substantive - Courts, when called upon to interpret nature of provision, may, keeping in view entire context in which provision came to be enacted, hold same to be directory though worded in negative form - Impugned orders of High Court dismissing review petition and writ appeal set aside - Written statement already filed shall be duly taken note of by Trial Court - Appeal allowed.
