Ruby Sales and Services Private Limited v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1369; (1994) 1 SCC 531; 1994 (1) BC 495; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 518; JT 1993 (6) SC 217; 1993 (2) RentLR 749; 1993(4) SCALE 280; 1994 (1) UJ 41

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Banking & Finance - Bombay Stamps Act, 1958, ss. 2(g) and 2(1) - Whether a consent decree conveying title in property falls under the definition of 'conveyance' u/s. 2(g) or 'instrument' u/s. 2(1) Act? - Terms, and not label, of the document is decisive - Held, the consent decree, on its true interpretation, is a conveyance - Also, consent decree being a creature of the agreement on which it is founded is liable to be set aside on the grounds which will invalidate the agreement - Such a consent decree is liable to stamp duty - Appeals dismissed.
