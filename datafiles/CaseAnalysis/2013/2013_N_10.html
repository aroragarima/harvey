Nagappan v State by Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 447; (2013) 15 SCC 252; AIR 2013 SC 3298; 2013 CRLJ 3878; 2013 (4) JCC 2555; JT 2014 (1) SC 331; 2013(9) SCALE 363; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 99; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly murdered deceased on account of enmity between deceased and Accused no.1 - Trial Court convicted appellants/accused for offences punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and confirmed order of conviction passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal was filed by accused no. 3 - Appellant/accused no. 3 contended that conviction was solely based on evidence of PW nos. 1 and 3, who were brothers and interested/related eye- witnesses, could not be sustained in absence of corroboration from other witnesses - Further both courts below failed to notice the fact that medical evidence did not support version of prosecution in respect of appellant/accusedno.3 and in fact contrary to evidence of PW nos. 1 and 3 and, therefore, conviction and sentence of appellant was liable to be set aside - Held, evidence of PW nos. 1 and 3 was clear, cogent and without much contradiction - PW nos. 1 and 3 mentioned that appellant/accused no. 3 attacked deceased with stick, however, evidence of PW1 and PW3 clearly implicated accused nos. 1 and 2 and Courts below had rightly accepted the case of prosecution - Insofar as role of appellant/accused no. 3 was concerned, even according to eye witnesses, , he attacked deceased with a stick - There was no specific assertion about exact blow on head by use of stick by appellant - They merely stated that appellant used stick and hit on back - There was not even a whisper that stick used by appellant hit on neck or head of deceased - SC was satisfied that evidence of PW1 and PW3 were not sufficient to convict appellant u/s. 302 of IPC - Both courts below failed to take note of fact that medical evidence had not supported version of prosecution in respect of appellant and in fact contrary to evidence of PW nos. 1 and 3 - Impugned order of conviction was set aside - Appeal allowed.
