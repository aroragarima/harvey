Harmony Innovation Shipping Limited v Gupta Coal India Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 166; (2015) 9 SCC 172; AIR 2015 SC 1504; 2015 (2) CompLJ 213; 2015 (2) CTC 310; JT 2015 (3) SC 63; 2015 (3) MLJ 107; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 978; 2015(3) SCALE 295; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 697

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Curial Law, International Commercial Arbitrations, English Arbitration Act, 1996, SIAC Rules

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 9, 47, 49 - Dispute - Order passed by HC - Jurisdiction - Agreement was entered into between parties on 20-10-2010 in respect of 24 voyages of coal shipment belonging to appellant from Indonesia to India - Appellant undertook only 15 voyages and that resulted in disputes and was referred to arbitration - Further, addendum to contract was executed as regards remaining voyages on 3-4-2013 when disputes arose in respect of main agreement - Further, award was passed and appellant filed application u/s. 9 before Trial Court for its enforcement u/ss. 9, 47, 49 of Act - Further, when dispute arose relating to addendum, arbitration proceedings were initiated and appellant moved Trial Court u/s. 9 of Act seeking attachment of cargos as interim relief - Trial court issued conditional order of attachment - However, HC set aside order passed by Trial Court directing appellant to furnish security or to show cause and as interim measure conditionally attaching cargo belonging to appellant on foundation that s. 9 of Act is limited to applications to arbitration that takes place in India and has no applicability to arbitration which takes place outside India in view of pronouncement in case in question - Whether order passed by HC against respondent was justified and in accordance with law.

Held, stipulations in arbitration clause shows that if any dispute or difference would arise under charter, arbitration in London to apply; that arbitrators are to be commercial men who are members of London Arbitration Association; contract is to be construed and governed by English Law; and that arbitration should be conducted, if claim is for lesser sum, in accordance with small claims procedure of London Maritime Arbitration Association. There is no other provision in agreement that any other law would govern arbitration clause. Further, there is no other clause anywhere in contract. Further, it is also postulated that if dispute is for amount less that USD 50000 then, arbitration should be conducted in accordance with small claims procedure of London Maritime Arbitration Association. SC held that when aforesaid stipulations are read and appreciated in contextual perspective, 'presumed intention' of parties is clear as crystal that juridical seat of arbitration would be London. Hence, it would be appropriate to interpret clause that it is proper clause or substantial clause and not curial or procedural one by which arbitration proceedings are to be conducted, hence, SC is disposed to think that seat of arbitration will be at London. Hence, SC held that DB has allowed petition on foundation that case in question would govern field, hence, court below had no jurisdiction is not correct. SC held that courts in India will not have jurisdiction as there is implied exclusion. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Courts in India would not have jurisdiction to pass order in case of dispute when their jurisdiction has been expressly excluded in the agreement executed between parties.
