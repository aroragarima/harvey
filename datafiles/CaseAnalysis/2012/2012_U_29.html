Bar Council Of India v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 242; (2012) 8 SCC 243; AIR 2012 SC 3246; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 58; 2012 (94) ALR 483; 2012 (6) AWC 5953; 2012 (6) Bom.C.R. 98; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 117; 2013 (1) KarLJ 353; 2012 (3) KLT 557; 2012 (6) MahLJ 483; 2012 (6) MLJ(SC) 758; 2012 (4) MPLJ 503; 2012 (4) RLW 3298; 2012(7) SCALE 69; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1094; 2012 (3) UPLBEC 2416

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Companies (Second Amendment) Act, 2002, Legal Services Authorities (Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32,39A - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, ss. 22-A,22-CB,22-C,22-D,22-E - Permanent Lok Adalat - Pre-empting - Award - Ultra Vires - Sustainability - Petitioner filed petition u/art. 32 of Constitution challenging vires of Sections 22-A, 22-B, 22-C, 22-D and 22-E of Act - Petitioner contended that as per amended s. 22 of Act Lok Adalat were substituted by Lok Adalat or Permanent Lok Adalat and that dispute before Permanent Lok Adalat could be raised by moving an application to it unilaterally by any party to dispute before dispute is brought before any court for settlement - Petitioner contended that public utility service provider, could play mischief by pre-empting an aggrieved consumer from going to consumer fora or availing other judicial process for redressal of his grievance and enforcement of his rights and while deciding case on merits, Permanent Lok Adalat was not required to follow provisions of CPC or Indian Evidence Act and pleaded that provisions under challenge as arbitrary and irrational - Respondent contended that impugned provisions are in conformity with the objectives of Article 39A and intended to provide an affordable, speedy and efficient mechanism to secure justice and was previously decided as such petition was liable to be dismissed - Whether provisions of Act were ultra vires to constitution - Held, Court found that provisions of Chapter which contemplated setting up of permanent Lok Adalats, for deciding disputes in which public utility services was one of matters involved, it was quite obvious that effort of legislature was to decrease work load in Courts by resorting to alternative disputes resolution - Lok Adalat was a mode of dispute resolution which had been in vogue since over two decades - Court did not find any constitutional infirmity in legislation, act ensured that justice will be available to litigant speedily and impartially - Court emphasised that persons who were appointed on Permanent Lok Adalats should be person of integrity and adequate experience, appropriate rules, inter alia in this regard, ought to be framed, if not already in place - Hence Court upheld validity of Act and hope Permanent Lok Adalats will be set up at an early date - Petition disposed of.
