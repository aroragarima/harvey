Smt. Shanti and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 29; (1991) SCC (Cr) 191; (1991) 1 SCC 371; AIR 1991 SC 1226; 1991 (2) AWC 795; 1991 CRLJ 1713; 1991 CRLR 4; 1991 CrLR(SC) 4; 1991 (1) DMC 187; 1991 (1) HLR 409; JT 1991 (1) SC 118; 1990(2) SCALE 988; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 675; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 675; 1991 (1) UJ 135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B and 498A - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113A - Dowry death - Accused, in laws of deceased - Evidence with regard to dowry demand by accused - Cruel treatment to deceased proved - Death within seven years of marriage - Body cremated hurriedly without informing her parents - Part played by them in bringing about death not proved - Circumstances indicates that death was unnatural - Question whether unnatural death was homicidal or suicidal is irrelevant - (A) Whether accused is liable to be convicted u/s. 304B? - (B) Whether charges u/s. 304B and s. 498A are mutually exclusive? - Held, accused liable to be convicted - No separate sentence u/s. 498A, is necessary in view of substantive sentence u/s. 304B - Sentence of life imprisonment reduced to 7 years' Rigorous Imprisonment - (B) Both sections are not mutually exclusive - A person charged and acquitted u/s. 304B can be convicted u/s. 498A without charge being there, if such a case is made out - Order accordingly.
