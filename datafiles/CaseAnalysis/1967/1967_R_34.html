Chief Mining Engineer East India Coal Company Limited v Rameswar and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 244; AIR 1968 SC 218; 1967 (15) FLR 457; 1968 LabIC 197; 1968 (1) LLJ 6; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Domestic Servant, Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950, Industrial Provident Fund and Bonus scheme Act 1948

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Mines & Minerals - Practice & Procedure - Service - Coal Mines Provident Fund And Bonus Schemes Act, 1948 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33 C(1), (2), Chap VA - (A) Whether Labour Court had jurisdiction to try applications for bonus u/s. 33 C(2)? - Scope of s. 33 C(2) is wider than s. 33 C(1) and is not confined to cases arising under an award, settlement or under Chap VA - Benefit provided by a statute or scheme made thereunder, without there being anything contrary to such statute or s. 33 C(2), can fall u/s. 33 C(2) - Benefit provided in Bonus Scheme remaining to be computed must fall u/s. 33 C(2) and Labour Court had jurisdiction to entertain and try claim, in respect of an existing right arising from relationship of industrial workman and his employer - Held, contention that Labour Court had no jurisdiction because claim arose under scheme or benefit was monetary or it involved any substantial question between company and workmen, fails - (B) Whether applications were barred by limitation set by bonus scheme and/or due to laches on part of workmen? - Further held, considerations like delay or laches are irrelevant to claims under a statutory provision unless such provision lays down any period of limitation - S. 33 C(2) does not lay down any limitation and such a provision can only be made by legislature if it thinks fit - Period of three years of limitation applies to applications for payment by Commr. from deposit made in treasury and has no application to claims u/s. 33 C(2) - (C) Whether workmen are entitled to bonus being employed allegedly as domestic servants and performing domestic and personal works during relevant period? - S. 1 of Bonus Scheme entitles every employee of Company except as therein provided to bonus - Exception excludes a person, though an employee in a colliery for period during which he is employed, working as mali, sweeper or domestic servant and on domestic and personal work - Further held, Company having failed to bring case within exception, Company's contention fails - (D) Whether direction to pay bonus for period prior to dates of employment was valid? - Further held, contention fails as Company did not dispute quantum of relief claimed by workmen as regards bonus, railway fares and leave wages - Appeal dismissed.
