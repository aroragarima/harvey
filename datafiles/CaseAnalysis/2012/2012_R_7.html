Bharat Heavy Electricals Ltd. v R.S. Avtar Singh & Co.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 344; (2013) 1 SCC 243; AIR 2013 SC 252; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 44; 2012(6) ALL MR 459; 2013 (99) ALR 716; 2013 (1) AWC 232; 2012 (4) BC 299; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 401; 2012 (193) DLT 714; JT 2012 (10) SC 152; 2013 (2) MahLJ 542; 2013 (2) MPLJ 8; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 252; 2013 (119) RD 751; 2012(10) SCALE 61; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Dispute regard to payment, Decretal amount

Summary: Civil Procedure - Arbitration & ADR - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 1, O. 21 r. 4, O. 21 r. 5, O. 21 r.1, O.21 r.4, O.21 r.5, ss. 34, 47 - Interest Act, 1978, s. 3(3)(c) - Dispute regard to payment - Decretal amount - Legality - Respondent undertook contract work with appellant in respect of which dispute arose as regards the payment to be made by appellant - Dispute was referred before Arbitrator - Arbitrator accordingly passed award - Respondent filed Execution Petition before Single Judge and the same was dismissed as entire award amount was fully paid - Appeal was filed before DB - DB affirmed order passed by Single Judge - Hence instant appeal - Respondent contended that payment of decretal amount was not satisfied as stipulated under O. 21 r. 1(1) and consequently operation of O. 21 r. 1 (4)(5) could ot be held to have operated upon until such satisfaction of payment of decretal amount was not made by appellant -

Held, Single Judge as well as DB has applied rule of construction, in regard to rule of appropriation, was also approved, SC did not find any illegality in said judgment of DB while affirming order of Single Judge - Contention based on s. 34 of CPC regard to general rule of appropriation in cases of this nature where there was short payment made pursuant to decree, SC did not find any conflict with said provision in so far as it related to payment of interest to be payable by appellant - Submission based on s. 3(3)(c) of the Act was concerned, said provision only states de hors substantive part of said s. 3, Courts were not empowered to award interest upon interest - SC did not find any scope to apply said section to case on hand where controversy was subsequent to decree where direction for payment of interest on award amount was spelt out - Issue related to correctness of interest calculated as per decree of Court which made award its rule - Challenge was not to decree on footing that it was in violation of s. 3(3)(c) of the Act - SC did not find any support in submission based upon said s. 3(3)(c) of the Act - Appeal dismissed.
