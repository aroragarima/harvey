Commisioner of Central Excise, Navi Mumbai v Amar Bitumen and Allied Products Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 784; (2010) 13 SCC 76; 2006 (202) E.L.T. 213; 2010(4) SCALE 481

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, s. 11 AC - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173 Q - (A) Whether article under consideration is to be classified under Chapter Heading 68.07, as contended by revenue or under Chapter Heading 59.09, as contended by assessee - Tribunal relying upon an earlier decision of in Bitumen Products case 1998 Indlaw CEGAT 1044 held that 'Bituminised Hessian based felt' is covered under Chapter Heading 59.09 as contended by assessee and no appeal was filed by revenue against said decision and same has become final - Held, article under consideration would be classifiable under Chapter Heading 59.09 and assessee would be entitled to exemption from payment of excise duty under notifications in question - (B) Whether process of making blown grade bitumen out of straight grade bitumen amounts to manufacture? - Tribunal decided in favour of assessee, relying upon decisions CCE, Vadodara v. Tikitar Industries 2000 Indlaw CEGAT 2704 which have been confirmed by Supreme Court - Further held, no merit in appeals - Appeal dismissed.
