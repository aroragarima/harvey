Lalu Prasad @ Lalu Prasad Yadav v State Of Bihar Through Cbi (Ahd) Patna

Supreme Court of India

06 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 997; (2007) 1 SCC 49; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1873; JT 2007 (1) SC 183; 2007 (1) KLT 47; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 305; 2006(13) SCALE 91; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 251

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Aiding And Abetting

Summary: Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13(1)(e) r/w s. 13(2), 19(1)(b); Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Validity of sanction to prosecute the appellants - Plea relating to cognizance of the offence is that previous sanction is necessary under the Act if the public servant does not hold the same office which he allegedly abused on the date when the cognizance was taken by the Court - Held, where the question of jurisdiction is raised and the trial Court is required to adjudicate that issue, it cannot be said that reasons are not to be recorded - Reasons relate to question of jurisdiction and not necessarily to the issue relating to framing of charge - Appeals dismissed.
