Col. Anil Kak (Retd.) v Municipal Corporation, Indore and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 566; (2005) 12 SCC 734; AIR 2007 SC 1130; 2005 (61) ALR 200; 2005 (4) AWC 3539; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 216; JT 2005 (8) SC 412; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 283; 2005 (99) RD 667; 2005(7) SCALE 356

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, o. 39 rr. 1, 2, s. 115 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Suit was for permanent injunction - Trial Court granted injunction to a limited area - Additional District Judge granted injunction to entire plaint schedule property - In revision HC held that no revision u/s. 115 could be entertained against an order of injunction since order was purely interlocutory in nature and allowed to convert revision into a petition u/art. 227 of Constitution and restored order of trial court - Appealed herein - Held, HC rightly decided to permit revision petitioners to convert petition as a proceeding u/art. 227 - An educational institution is located, and is functioning in suit property, including in disputed portion - Proposed demolition of structures outside area of 16, 000 Sq ft remains stayed - Appeal disposed of.
