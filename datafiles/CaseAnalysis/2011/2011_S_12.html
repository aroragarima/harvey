Indian Oil Corporation Limited v SPS Engineering Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 99; (2011) 3 SCC 507; AIR 2011 SC 987; 2011 (2) AWC 1884; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 287; 2011 (2) CTC 317; JT 2011 (2) SC 553; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 174; 2011 (3) MPLJ 73; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 335; 2011(2) SCALE 291; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 512; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 33

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.11 - Application for appointment of arbitrator - Adjudication - Scope and extent - Appellant Corporation awarded a construction contract to respondent Company, however, respondent failed to complete the work within the stipulated time - Parties invoked the arbitration clause and consequently, HC appointed an arbitrator - Respondent and appellant raised various claims - Arbitrator awarded the claims of appellant and respondent and adjusted the due amounts of each other resulting in appellant having to pay an amount to the respondent - Appellant, subsequently, invoked arbitration clause by sending a notice to respondent claiming risk execution cost - Respondent rejected appellant's request for invocation of arbitration clause again - Appellant filed application u/s. 11 of the Act before the HC for appointment of an arbitrator, which was dismissed by the HC - Hence the instant appeal - (A) Whether the Chief Justice or his designate could examine the tenability of a claim, in particular whether a claim was barred by res judicata, while considering an application u/s. 11 of the Act - Held, if the Chief Justice or his designate was of the view that in addition to examining whether there was an arbitration agreement between the parties, he should consider the issue whether the claim was a dead one (long time barred) or whether there had been satisfaction of mutual rights and obligation under the contract, he should record his intention to do so and give an opportunity to the parties to place their materials on such issue - Unless parties were put on notice that such an issue would be examined, they would be under the impression that only questions of jurisdiction and existence of arbitration agreement between the parties would be considered in such proceedings - Question whether a claim was barred by res judicata, did not arise for consideration in a proceedings u/s. 11 of the Act and such an issue would have to be examined by the arbitral tribunal - A decision on res judicata required consideration of the pleadings as also the claims/issues/points and the award in the first round of arbitration, in juxtaposition with the pleadings and the issues/points/claims in the second arbitration - Limited scope of s. 11 of the Act did not permit such examination of the maintainability or tenability of a claim either on facts or in law - It was for the arbitral tribunal to examine and decide whether the claim was barred by res judicata and there could be no threshold consideration and rejection of a claim on the ground of res judicata, while considering an application u/s. 11 of the Act - (B) Whether the Designate was justified in holding that the claim was barred by res judicata and that application u/s. 11 of the Act was misconceived and mala fide - Held, Designate should have avoided the risks and dangers involved in deciding an issue relating to the tenability of the claim without necessary pleadings and documents, in a proceeding relating to the limited issue of appointing an Arbitrator - Designate committed a jurisdictional error in dismissing the application filed by the appellant u/s. 11 of the Act, on the ground that the claim for extra cost was barred by res judicata and by limitation - Consideration of an application u/s. 11 of the Act, did not extend to consideration of the merits of the claim or the chances of success of the claim - Impugner order of Designate, therefore, was set aside - Application u/s. 11 of the Act filed by appellant before the Chief Justice of the HC was allowed and an arbitrator was appointed - Appeal allowed.
