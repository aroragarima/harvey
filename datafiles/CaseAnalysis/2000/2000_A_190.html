Murray and Company v Ashok Kr. Newatia and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 597; (2000) 2 SCC 367; (2000) SCC (Cr) 473; AIR 2000 SC 833; AIR 2000 SCW 389; 2000 (38) ALR 577; 2000 (1) AWC 823; 2000 (1) CLT 310; 2000 CRLJ 1394; JT 2000 (1) SC 337; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 729; 2000(1) SCALE 251; 2000 (2) SCJ 584; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 367; 2000 (1) SLT 550; 2000 (1) Supreme 251

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c) and 13 - Person making definite and deliberate false statement in affidavit but fact revealing that its advantage not taken - Whether liable for contempt of Court? - Held, cannot escape liability of being held guilty of contempt - Appeal disposed of.
