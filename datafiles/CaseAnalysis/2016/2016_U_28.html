Savelife Foundation and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 248; (2016) 7 SCC 194; 2016 (2) ACC 1; AIR 2016 SC 1617; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 206; 2016(4) ALL MR 941; 2016 (116) ALR 545; 2016 (3) AWC 3043; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 515; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 1; JT 2016 (3) SC 369; 2016 (3) KarLJ 237; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1553; 2016 (3) MLJ 366; 2016 (2) OLR 12; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 725; 2016 (4) RLW 2668; 2016(3) SCALE 522; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 45; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 82

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: CARRIERS & TRANSPORTATION, Assistance, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Social Action, Responsibility and Heroism Act, 2015, Civil Law (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 2011, Emergency Medical Aid Act, Civil Liability Act, 2002, Good Samaritan Act, Wrongs Act, 1958

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 32, 142, 144 - Legal framework for protection of people who help victims in road accidents - Instant petition u/art.32 of Constitution in public interest for development of supportive legal framework to protect bystanders and passersby (Good Samaritans) who render help to victims of road accidents and play significant role in saving lives of victims by either immediately rushing them to hospital or by providing immediate life saving first aid.

Held, Ministry of Road Transport and Highways issued notification on 12-5-2015 containing guidelines for protection of Good Samaritans and a further Notification has been issued on 21-1-2016 in accordance with para 1(7) and 1(8) of the guidelines which required standard operating procedures to be framed and issued for examination of Good Samaritans by the police or during trial. However, in the absence of any statutory backing, it would be difficult to enforce said guidelines and standard operating procedures. Further, as per guidelines 'acknowledgment' if desired by Good Samaritans, has to be issued as may be prescribed in standard format by State Govt. and so, till such time the format is prescribed, acknowledgment be issued on official letter-pad etc. and in the interregnum period, if so desired by Good Samaritan, mentioning the name of Samaritan, address, time, date, place of occurrence and confirming that the injured person was brought by said Samaritan. Hence, remaining guidelines in notifications dt.12-5-2015 and 21-1-2016 are approved and directions are passed to effect that guidelines with modifications made by SC are to be complied with by Union territories and all the functionaries of State Govt. as law laid down by SC u/art.32 read with art.142 of Constitution and the same to be treated as binding as per mandate of art.141. Further, courts are not to insist on appearance of Good Samaritans as that causes delay, expenses and inconvenience and concerned Court should exercise the power to appoint the Commission for examination of Good Samaritans in accordance with provisions contained in s. 284 of CrPC suo motu or on an application moved for that purpose, unless for reasons to be recorded personal presence of Good Samaritan in court is considered necessary. Petition allowed.

Ratio - SC could issue directions u/art.32 read with art.142 to implement and enforce guidelines which are necessary for protection of rights u/art.21 read with art.14 of Constitution so as to provide immediate help to the victims of the accident and at the same time to provide protection to Good Samaritans.
