Life Insurance Corporation Of India And Anr. v Gangadhar Vishwanath Ranade (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 181; (1989) 4 SCC 297; AIR 1990 SC 185; 1990 (1) BC 69; [1989] 66 Comp Cas 858; 1989 (80) CTR 213; 1989 (25) ECR 466; [1989] 180 ITR 1; JT 1989 (3) SC 637; 1989(2) SCALE 499; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 97; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 97; [1989] 47 TAXMAN 373; 1990 (1) UJ 90

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Insurance

Keywords: Liability To Pay Interest, Material Particular

Summary: Banking & Finance - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Insurance - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, u/s. 226(3) - Insurance Act, 1938 - Is Life Insurance Corporation's (LIC) liability for payment of interest on principal amounts at rate of 15% per annum excessive? - Held, assignment of policies was made by insured, petitioner and same was duly accepted and registered by LIC - LIC was bound to act on that assignment in favour of assignee - ITO had assumed that money payable on maturity of these policies belonged to insured/assessee/defaulter, overlooking duly registered assignment made much earlier in favour of assessee's wife - Question of revocation of notice u/s. 226(3)(vii) arose only after LIC made requisite statement on oath u/s. 226(3)(vi) in view of its consistent stand throughout that moneys due under policies were held by it for and on behalf of assignee and not defaulter - This statutory obligation was performed by LIC much later - There is no risk of personal liability of person making statement on oath unless any material particular mentioned in statement is false - Award of interest to assignee of policies to whom payment there under had to be made is justified - Appeal dismissed.
