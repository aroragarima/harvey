Associated Tanners Vizianagram, A.P v Commercial Tax Officer, Vizianagram, Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 415; (1986) 2 SCC 479; AIR 1987 SC 1922; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 969; 1986 (62) STC 1; 1986 (2) UJ 108

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Act, Rate Of Tax, Rate

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 8 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 304 - Declaration of item 9(b) in sch. III of State enactment - Validity - Whether item 9(b) of sch. III of State Act as unconstitutional and void? - Held, the effect of an imposition of tax might work differently upon different dealers namely, those who use imported tanned goods and those who purchase these locally and tan these locally and then sell in the course o f inter-state sales - But that effect cannot be said to be arising directly, or as an immediate effect of the imposition of the tax - Therefore there cannot be any question of violation of art. 304(a) of the Constitution - The imposition in this case was in implementation of the Central Act and it was submitted on behalf of the respondent that there was no prohibition u/art. 304 of the Constitution on the Parliament for imposition of any tax - The embargo that was placed by art. 304 of the Constitution was on the Legislature of a state - Therefore the prohibition was not on the Parliament - The High Court was therefore right in dismissing the writ petition - Appeal dismissed.
