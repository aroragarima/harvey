Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1950

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 106 - Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 28 - Eviction suit in absence of one month's notice to vacate suit premises - Maintainability of - Institution of two eviction petitions by landlords appellants against respondents tenants - Rent receipts in both cases had printed condition requiring one 'month's notice either oral or written - Objection filed by respondents that eviction petitions were not maintainable in absence of one month's notice - Validity - Terms and conditions of a lease result from conscious decision of parties - Since conditions printed at back of rent receipt cannot be said to be a conscious decision taken by parties governing lease of premises - Hence it could not be held that parties had entered into an agreement about service of one months' notice in advance to vacate premises before institution of eviction petition - In first case notice was issued terminating tenancy w.e.f 31-11-1991 calling upon tenant to vacate premises after said date - Hence a notice was served on tenant before institution of eviction petition - In second case there was no requirement of a written notice before institution of an eviction petition - Case of landlord in plaint that he had intimated to tenant to vacate premises before institution of eviction petition - Trial Court directed to decide issue after allowing parties to lead evidence with respect thereto - Appeals disposed of.
