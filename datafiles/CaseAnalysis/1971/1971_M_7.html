Ram Janki Devi & Anr v M/S. Juggilal Kamlapat

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 754; (1971) 1 SCC 477; AIR 1971 SC 2551; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Limitation Act, 1908, art. 60 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 34 - (A) Practiceand Procedure - Limitation - (1) Whether transaction is a deposit of money or loan - Decisive factors of - (2) Demand for return of part of the amount deposited - Limitation - Comencement of - (B) Evidence - Book of account - Proof of - In absence of its writer - Held, surrounding circumstances, relationship of parties, character of transaction and the manner in which parties treated the transaction are factors decisive of whether transaction is a deposit of money or loan - Limitation does not start to run on demand for return of part of the amount deposited - Book of account must be hold to have been proved even in absence of its writer on proof of its proper maintaining and keeping and of impossibility of arranging presence of its writer - Appeal Dismissed.
