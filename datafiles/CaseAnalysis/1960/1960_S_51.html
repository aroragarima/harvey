Â Smt. Shanno Devi v Mangal Sain

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, S.K. Das, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1961 SC 58; 1961 (1) SCJ 201; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 576

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Foreigners

Keywords: Migration, Registration of Foreigners Act

Summary: Foreigners - Citizenship Act, 1955 - Foreigners Act, 1946 - Registration Act, 1908 - Registration of Foreigners Act, 1948 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 6 and 5 - Migration - What do words 'has migrated to territory of India' in art. 6 of Constitution mean? - Election petition - Held, respondent was not a citizen of India and thus, was disqualified - (1) Whether migration u/art. 6 must take place after territory of India came into existence under Constitution? - Cls. (a) and (b) of art. 6 and sub-clauses (i) and (ii) of cl. (b), perused - Further held, act of migration must take place before commencement of Constitution and thus 'migrated to territory of India' means 'migrated' at any time before commencement of Constitution to a place now in territory of India - (2) Whether migrated to territory of India means merely to come to territory of India or it means coming to territory of India to remain here? - Meaning of word 'migration', construction to be given to it, position of law in other nations, discussed - At point of time a person migrates to a new place or country it can be said that he has not migrated to such a place, but in law he should be held to have migrated to this later place or country at later point of time when he forms intention of residing there permanently - Meaning of words 'has migrated to territory of India' thus answered through such findings - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal dismissed.
