Kanuji S. Zala v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1066; (1999) 4 SCC 514; (1999) SCC (Cr) 594; AIR 1999 SC 2269; 1999 CRLJ 3527; 1999 (2) GLH 415; 2000 (4) G.L.R. 3256; JT 1999 (4) SC 448

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985, s. 4(3) - Gujarat Prevention of Anti Social Activities Act, 1985, s. 4(3) - Preventive detention - Ground, detenu is bootlegger selling liquor - And, consumption of liquor by people of that locality is harmful to their health - Detenue indulging in violence for carrying activity of selling liquor - Statements of witnesses clearly showed that as a result of violence resorted to by detenu even tempo of public life is disturbed - Satisfaction recorded by detaining authority that activity of detenu is prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Held, is genuine - No quashment of detention order.
