Prestige Engineering(India) Ltd v C.C.E

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 757; (1994) 6 SCC 465; 1994 (54) ECR 519; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 497; JT 1994 (5) SC 514; 1994 (4) RLT 333; 1994(3) SCALE 957; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Notification No 199/75-C E dated 30-4-1975 - Whether manufacturing process undertaken by petitioner was in nature of 'job work' within meaning of notification? - Meaning of 'job work' having regard to present context is 'a piece of work especially one done for hire or profit - Intention of notification was to help job workers who contributed mainly their labour and skill, though done with help of tools, gadgets or machinery - Held, work done by appellant cannot be characterised as a job work - Appeal dismissed.
