Rashmi Metaliks Limited and another v Kolkata Metropolitan Development Authority and others

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 576; JT 2013 (12) SC 377

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Terms of tender - Income tax return - Offer - Disqualification - Effects of - Appellant-company failed to correspond to essential terms of tender and its offer contained in said tender was ineligible for consideration - As cl. (i) of Tender was concerned, Single Judge held that violation of cl. (j) of Tender was committed by Appellant-company inasmuch as it failed to file its latest income tax return along with its bid - DB held that cl. (j) of Tender could not be viewed as non-essential term and therefore, should be corrected before submission of tender - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether disqualification of appellant-company on ground of it having failed to submit its latest income tax return along with its bid was sufficient reason for disregarding its offer/bid - Held, so far as cl. (j) of Tender, detailed notice was concerned, it seemed contrary to conclusion in impugned judgment, clause was not essential element - Income tax return was filed by appellant-company and scrutinized - Gross income of appellant-company was Rs.15,34,05,627 although, for succeeding A/y 2012-2013, income tax was NIL, but substantial tax was deposited - Income tax return would have assumed character of essential term if one of qualifications was either gross income or net income on which tax was attracted - In many cases that was salutary stipulation, since it was indicative of commercial standing and reliability of tendering entity and that feature being absent, filing of latest income tax return was collateral term, and accordingly tendering authority ought to have brought that discrepancy to notice of appellant-company and if even thereafter no rectification was carried out, position could be appreciably different - Hence, impugned judgment was set aside - Therefore, disqualification of appellant-company on ground of it having failed to submit its latest income tax return along with its bid was not sufficient reason for disregarding its offer/bid - Appeal allowed.
