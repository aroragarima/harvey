Sarup Singh & Anr v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 998; (2011) 11 SCC 198; 2011(1) ALL MR 411; 2011 (3) AWC 2719; 2011 (2) CTC 340; JT 2010 (13) SC 69; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 317; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1014; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 837; 2011 (112) RD 445; 2010(12) SCALE 424

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition (amendment) Act, 1984 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151, 152 - Land acquisition - Benefit from Amendment Act, 1984 - Entitlement - Appellants' lands were acquired and award was passed by the Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) - Appellants raised objections with regard to the quantum of compensation - Matter referred to the Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation - Appellants filed appeal before the HC for further enhancement- HC enhanced compensation and granted solatium at 15% and interest at 6 % per annum - Appellants, subsequently, filed application u/ss. 151, 152 of CPC praying for solatium and interest at the enhanced rate as provided for by the Amendment Act, 1984- HC allowed appellants petitions by passing an order enhancing the payment of solatium from 15% to 30% and interest from 6% to 9% - Appellants, consequently, filed execution petition before the District Court for executing the award - District Court dismissed execution petition holding that appellants were not entitled to the enhanced rate of solatium and interest as the award of LAO and that of Reference Court were passed prior to 30-4-1982 - District Court further held that the order passed by the HC u/ss. 151 and 152 of CPC was without jurisdiction and as such a nullity - Appellants challenged the District Court order before the HC, however, HC dismissed appellants appeal - (A) Whether the benefit of enhancement in the rate of solatium and interest as introduced by the Amendment Act, 1984 could be given to such of the claimants whose cases for payment of compensation were finalized prior to coming into force of the Amendment Act, 1984 - Held, SC, in Union of India & Anr. v. Raghubir Singh (Dead) by Lrs., 1988 INDLAW SC 483, held that the award made by the Collector u/s. 11 of the Act made between 30-4-1982 and 24-9- 1984, i.e., the dates of introduction of the Land Acquisition Amendment Bill, 1982 in the House of the People and that of commencement of operation of the Amendment) Act, 1984 respectively, would be entitled to the enhanced rates under the Amendment Act - 5 Bench Judges decision of the SC in Reghubir Singh case squarely applies to the instant case and award of the Land Acquisition Officer/Collector or of the Reference Court must have been made between the said stipulated period, i.e., between 30-4-1982 and 24-9-1984 - However, in the instant case, award of the LAO and that of the Reference Court were passed prior to 30-4-1982, therefore, amendment brought in by the Amendment Act, 1984 to the concerned provisions for enhancement could not have been made applicable to the proceeding of the present cases - Hence, judgment and order passed by the HC giving the benefit provided by under the Amendment Act, 1984 cannot be made applicable in appellants' cases - (B) Whether the judgment and order given by the HC enhancing solatium from 15 % to 30% and interest from 6% to 9% per annum in view of the Amendment Act, 1984 could be negated by the District Court while acting as an Executing Court and whether the Executing Court could go behind the judgment and decree passed by the HC - Held, if a decree is found to be nullity, the same could be challenged and interfered with at any subsequent stage, say, at the execution stage or even in a collateral proceeding - If a particular Court lacks inherent jurisdiction in passing a decree or making an order, a decree or order passed by such Court would be without jurisdiction and the same is non-est and void ab initio - In the present cases the judgment and order passed by the HC before the advent of Amendment Act, 1984 became final and binding as no appeal was brought to SC thereafter - However, consequent to the Amendment Act, 1984, the appellants had filed applications for the grant of 30% solatium and 9% interest - A decree once passed and which has become final and binding cannot be sought to be amended by filing petition u/ss. 151 and 152, CPC - Therefore, Executing Court as also the HC were justified in holding that the orders passed by the HC granting enhanced solatium and interest as Amendment Act, 1984 is without jurisdiction and a nullity, hence the order passed by the Executing Court and the order confirming the Executing Court order by the HC are legal, valid and justified - Appeals dismissed.
