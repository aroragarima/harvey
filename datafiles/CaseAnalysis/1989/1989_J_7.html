Duli Chand (Dead) By L.Rs. v Jagmender Dass

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 189; (1990) 1 SCC 169; 1990 (40) DLT 1; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 389; 1990 (1) RCJ 1; 1990 (1) RentLR 445; 1989(2) SCALE 1331; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 465; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 465

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(b) and 16(2) - Indian Evidence Act 1872, s. 91 - Subletting - Whether it was necessary for tenant to obtain consent in writing to sub-letting premises? - Held, sub-s.(3) of s. 16 prohibits sub-letting of premises after commencement of Act of 1958 without 'previous' consent in writing of landlord - Use of word 'previous' in this sub-section shows that where it was intention of legislature that consent in writing should be obtained before sub-letting it said so specifically - Absence of word 'previous' in s. 16(2) shows that it was not intention of legislature that consent in writing could be obtained before sub-letting - It was necessary for tenant to obtain consent in writing to sub-letting premises - Mere permission or acquiescence will not do - Consent shall also be to specific sub-letting or parting with possession - There can be no waiver unless person against whom waiver is claimed had full knowledge of his right and of facts enabling him to take effectual action for enforcement of such rights - Appeal dismissed.
