Shree Vijay Cotton and Oil Mills Limited v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 276; (1991) 1 SCC 262; AIR 1991 SC 656; AIR 1991 SCW 221; 1991 (1) CCC 140; JT 1990 (4) SC 771; 1990(2) SCALE 1194; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 447; 1991 (1) UJ 237; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 194

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 28 and 34 - Land acquisition - Compensation and interest thereon - Determination of - (A) Whether compensation awarded by learned District Judge at rate of Rs. 3 per square yard was wrong and if so to what extent? - Held, according to High Court while fixing price of a large chunk of land on basis of comparable instances of small plots some allowance has to be made and for doing so there is no hard and fast rule and mathematical accuracy can hardly be achieved - That may be so but in facts of this case one gets impression that HC has given higher allowance than was warranted - Be that as it may HC, on appreciation of evidence and taking into consideration all facts and circumstances before it has come to conclusion that reasonable market price of acquired land would be Rs. 135 per square yard - No interference - (B) Whether u/ss. 34 and 28 of Act appellant was entitled to interest from date possession of land was taken over from him? - Held, there is inherent evidence in wording of s. 28 and 34 to show that framers of Act intended to assure payment of interest to person whose land was acquired and it was not intention to subject said payment to procedural hazards - Further held, it was not necessary for appellant-claimant to have filed separate appeal/cross-objections before HC for purposes of claiming interest u/s. 28 or s. 34 of Act - Appeal allowed.
