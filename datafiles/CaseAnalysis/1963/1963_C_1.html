C. Beepathumma and Others v V. S. Kadambolithaya and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 365; AIR 1965 SC 241; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1877, s. 3, art. 134 - (A) Whether suit for redemption was barred by limitation and whether doctrine of election will be applicable in present case ? - Held, there is no doubt that minor son was not directly bound by Exs. P-2 and P-2(a); he was a minor and no guardian signed on his behalf, thus Ex. P-2(a) cannot be used to show either an acknowledgment by him or an extension of term of original usufructuary mortgage; further doctrine of election was properly applied in respect of minor son's 1/4 th share now in possession of present appellants through defendant 8 - (B) Whether or not High Court was in error in awarding mesne profits till they were paid full price of redemption including compensation for improvements? - A mortgage had to be redeemed according to its own terms; mortgagors undertook that they would redeem properties by paying principal of mortgage amount and compensation for improvements in a lumpsum and cannot complain if mortgagees are not compelled to hand over property or to pay mesne profits till mortgagors have paid full amount - Further held, date 15/04/1946 was fixed for redemption and mortgagors put into court a sum of about Rs. 17,000, appellate decree was passed on 15/04/1946 and possession was delivered in 1957; mortgagees cannot claim to hold lands and use amount paid as price of redemption and even if they were not required to hand over possession till amount together with compensation for improvements was paid in full to them, they could not have use of money as well, therefore, mortgagees must pay interest on amount paid by mortgagors from date of withdrawal of amount till possession was delivered to mortgagors at 6% per annum simple and extra amount due to mortgagees by way of compensation will be deductible and accounts shall be adjusted between parties accordingly - Appeal partly allowed.
