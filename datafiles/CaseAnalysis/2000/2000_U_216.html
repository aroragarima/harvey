Karamchari Union, Agra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 729; (2000) 3 SCC 335; AIR 2000 SC 1226; 2000 ALJ 711; 2000 (159) CTR 148; [2000] 243 ITR 143; JT 2000 (2) SC 493; 2000 (2) LLJ 603; 2000(2) SCALE 161; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 33; [2000] 109 TAXMAN 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: City Compensatory Allowance, Recognised Provident Fund, Special Allowance

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Service - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 17 and 2(24) - Challenging inclusion of DA, HRA and CCA paid to them in their income for purpose of income tax - Equity or hardship would hardly be relevant ground for interpretation of tax law - CCA amount cannot be said to be profit, gain or additional salary - Taxation of income is not dependent upon its destination or manner of its utilisation - 'Income', 'Salary' and its taxability is to be in accordance with Act - Held that DA, CCA and HRA would be taxable income - Appeal nos. 1784-86 of 1988, 6054/1994 and 6058/1994 allowed and Appeal No. 1843/1989 and C A No. 1853-1856 of 2000 dismissed.
