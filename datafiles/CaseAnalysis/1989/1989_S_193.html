Ram Lal Sethi and Another v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 282; (1990) Supp SCC 11

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Insecticides Act, 1968 - Appellant's case is that out of an area of 1300 sq. yards. acquired from appellant only about 700 sq. yards. had been utilised for purpose of constructing a new road and balance of 600 sq. yards. had been given away by State Govt. to M/s Weldon Inks, who had set up their industry near plot in question - Whether it is not permissible for Govt. to acquire land ostensibly for a public purpose but to allot a portion thereof capriciously to some other private party? - Held, allegations on which contention is based have not been substantiated - Lands of appellant had in fact been acquired for a public purpose but that exigencies of development necessitated allotment of a part of it to another - Appellant has not been able to show that said allotment was an act of favoritism to M/s. Weldon Inks - So far as appellant is concerned he has already been awarded compensation - Moreover, it is not possible for us to direct restoration of land to petitioner without Weldon Inks being made a party to proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
