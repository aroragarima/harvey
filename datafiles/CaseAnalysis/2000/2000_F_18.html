Chairman, J and K State Board of Education v Feyaz Ahmed Malik and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 122; (2000) 3 SCC 59; AIR 2000 SC 1039; 2000 (1) CLT 318; JT 2000 (1) SC 398; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 212; 2000(1) SCALE 311; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 402; 2000 (1) SLR 569; 2000 (1) SLT 564; 2000 (1) Supreme 330

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Jammu and Kashmir Board of School Education Act, 1975, ss. 3, 4, 10, 13, 24, 25 and 33 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Concerned about menace of mass copying in examinations, the Jammu and Kashmir State Board of School Education made certain amendments to existing regulations governing cancellation of examinations on account of mass copying, outside interference or any other reason which vitiates sanctity of examination - Validity of notification in question - (A) Whether notifications D/- 27-01-1993 and 29-06-1993 are valid on grounds of jurisdiction of authorities passing them? - Held, the chairman acts as a delegate of board any action taken or order passed by chairman on strength of delegation made by board cannot be faulted on ground of lack of competence or authority - On careful consideration of provisions of Act and regulation of board under challenge, both board and its chairman were within their powers and authority in issuing notifications D/- 27-01-1993 and 29-06-1993 respectively - (B) Whether university is competent to cancel an examination without interference from court? - Held, if there is sufficient material on which it can be demonstrated that university was right in its conclusion that examinations ought to be cancelled, then academic standards require that university's appreciation of problem must be respected - It would not do for court to say that candidates or even their representatives should have been examined with a view to ascertaining whether they had received assistance or not - (C) Whether courts should interfere in decisions of conduct of examinations decided by authorities in charge of institution? - Held, in matters concerning campus discipline of educational institutions and conduct of examinations duty is primarily vested in authorities in charge of institutions - In such matters court should not try to substitute its own views in place of authorities concerned nor thrust its views on them - That is not to say that court cannot at all interfere with decisions of authorities in such matters - (D) Whether High Court was justified in striking down as notification in question as discriminatory? - Held, a notification cannot be struck down as discriminatory merely because in implementing same injustice is likely to be suffered by some candidates - In judging validity of a notification containing provisions regarding steps to be taken when a report of mass malpractice is received it is to be kept in mind whether provisions contained in notification are relevant for achieving purpose for which notification is issued and if it is found that notification is relevant for and has a nexus with purpose to be achieved then notification cannot be said to be arbitrary and discriminatory - Further held, impugned judgment of HC does not show that decision to strike down two notifications is based on grounds sound in law and justified on facts, hence liable to be quashed - Appeal allowed.
