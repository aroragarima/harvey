State Of Punjab v Wasson Singh And Five Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 200; (1981) 2 SCC 1; (1981) SCC (Cr) 292; AIR 1981 SC 697; 1981 CRLJ 410; 1981 CrLR(SC) 122; 1981(1) SCALE 146; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss.149,302,34 - Criminal Procedure Code,1973, s.342 - Respondents accused of double murder - Acquitted on appeal - Appeal - Whether HC correct in directing acquittal? - Held, reasons given by HC for holding that P.W. 2 was not an eye-witness of these murders, are utterly unsustainable - Mere fact that witnesses had succeeded in escaping unhurt, or that there are discrepancies in statements was no ground for holding that they were not eye witnesses - Moreover F.I.R. lodged by witness so promptly that he had practically no time to spin out a false story - Both witnesses are related to deceased, and, as such, are interested witnesses and their antecedents, also, are of a questionable nature - But their antecedents or mere interest not a valid ground to reject their evidence - Persons with such antecedents are not necessarily untruthful witness nor mere relationship with deceased a good ground for discarding their testimony, when their presence at scene of occurrence was probable - All that was necessary was to scrutinise their evidence with more than ordinary care and circumspection - Thus rejection of their evidence untenable - Appreciation of evidence - Examination of Ballistic Expert under s.342, Cr.P.C. - Circumstantial evidence corroborated by testimony of eye - Witnesses - Two respondents convicted while benefit of doubt given to others - Appeal partly allowed.
