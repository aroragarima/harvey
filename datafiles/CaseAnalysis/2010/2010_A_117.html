FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Bhai Jaspal Singh and another v Assistant Commissioner of Commercial Taxes and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 888; (2011) 1 SCC 39; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 1; JT 2010 (11) SC 562; 2010(11) SCALE 263; [2010] 35 VST 456

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: West Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1941, Demand Notice, West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, West Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1995, Small Scale Industrial Unit, Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1954

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, s. 10A - Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1941, r. 3(116) - West Bengal Sales Tax Act, 1994, s. 31 - West Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1995, r. 41 - A/y 01.04.1995 to 30.04.1995 and 01.05.1995 to 31.03.1996 - Exemption - Assessee engaged in manufacturing juice, jelly, jam etc., filed return for the aforesaid period, claiming exemption from payment of sales tax mainly relying on the exemption notification dt. 26.05.1994 and r. 41 of 1995 Rules submitting that its investment in plant and machinery in its units during said period was less than Rs. 5 lakhs - Asst. Commissioner disallowed said assessee's claim for exemption from payment of sales tax and also levied interest as provided u/s. 10A of the 1941 Act and s. 31 of the 1994 Act - Assessment order passed by the Tax Officer was confirmed by the Deputy Commissioner - Tribunal also confirmed the order passed by the First Appellate Authority - On appeal, HC confirmed the order passed by the Tribunal - Assessee filed appeal against said order submitting that word 'investment' requires to be understood as the investment made by a Small Scale Industrial Unit after giving depreciation on plant and machinery - (A) Investment made by a Small Scale Industrial Unit - Held, in computing the valuation of plant and machinery, only the cost price/purchase price of the equipment invested by the assessee will have to be taken into account - The expression 'investment' in plant and machinery is not subject to the impact of depreciation in the value of plant and machinery - Since the assessee's investment is more than Rs. 5 lakhs before the periods in question and since the investment continues to remain unchanged, the assessee is not entitled to exemption from payment of sales tax either under the Rules or under the notification - (B) Notification providing exemption should be liberally construed having regard to the purpose and object - Held, purpose of the notification as stated in the preamble is to promote industrial activity and development in the State of West Bengal - However, it is a necessary pre- condition that first the assessee should fall within the clear wording of the notification - The assessee in this matter falls outside the parameters of this Notification, since his investment is over Rs. 5 lakhs, therefore, there is no question of the Notification applying to him - Thus, there is no requirement of liberal construction as the notification does not apply to the assessee in the first place - (C) Interest on tax - Held, interest is compensatory in character and is imposed on an assessee who has withheld payment of any tax as and when it is due and payable - The interest is levied on the actual amount of tax withheld and the extent of delay in paying the tax on the due date - In the present case, it is the admitted position that tax due on the basis of quarterly return was not paid as required by sub-section (3) of s. 31 of 1994 Act and the appellant was, therefore, liable to pay interest on the amount of tax in respect of which default was committed at the rate prescribed in sub-section (2) of s. 31 of 1994 Act from the last date prescribed for filing quarterly return under the Act upto the date of payment - Appeals dismissed.
