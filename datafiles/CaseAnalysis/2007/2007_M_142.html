Union Of India And Another v Mahajabeen Akhtar

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1068; (2008) 1 SCC 368; AIR 2008 SC 435; 2008 (5) AWC 4495; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 807; 2007 (8) SLR 772

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: By Transfer On Deputation, Doctrine Of Equal Pay For Equal Work

Summary: Appeal to challenge order passed by Division Bench of High Court dismissing petition filed to question order passed by CAT directing grant of replacement pay scale with consequential benefits - Respondent appointed as Technical Assistant of Urdu Language in Bureau of Promotion of Urdu Language was promoted as Research Assistant but when National Council for Promotion of Urdu Language (NCPUL) was constituted in place of Bureau of Promotion of Urdu, she opted for Government service and was redeployed as Assistant Librarian but her pay scale was not upgraded as per recommendation of Fifth Pay Commission - Held, doctrine of equal pay for equal work has no application - Matter may have been different, had the scales of pay have been determined on basis of educational qualification, nature of duties and other relevant factors - Not only essential educational qualifications are different but nature of duties is also different - Article 39(d) as also Article 14 of the Constitution of India must be applied, inter alia, on premise that equality clause should be invoked in respect of the people who are similarly situated in all respects - Appeal dismissed.
