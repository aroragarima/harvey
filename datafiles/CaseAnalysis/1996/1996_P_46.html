Union of India v Paliwal Electricals Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 233; (1996) 3 SCC 407; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 447; AIR 1996 SC 3106; 1996 (2) CCC 29; 1996 (64) ECR 312; 1996 (83) E.L.T. 241; JT 1996 (3) SC 606; 1996(3) SCALE 113; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 845; 1996 (3) Supreme 213

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Industries (Development and Regulations) Act, 1951 - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, r. 8 - Whether Para 7 of Exemption Notification No. 175 of 1986, which was inserted by Notification No. 223 of 1987, violate art. 14 of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, Court must presume that amendment was found by Central Govt. to be necessary for giving effect to its policy [underlying notification] on basis of working of Notification and that such an amendment was found necessary to prevent persons from taking unfair advantage of concession, therefore Notification is not ultra vires Act, or art. 14 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
