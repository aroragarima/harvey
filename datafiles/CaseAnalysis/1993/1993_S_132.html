Anwari Basavaraj Patil and Others v Siddaramaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 863; (1993) 1 SCC 636; AIR 1994 SC 512; JT 1993 (1) SC 328; 1993(1) SCALE 235; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 313; 1993 (1) UJ 422

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Indian Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 97(1) - Whether s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 is applicable to a recrimination notice given u/s. 97 of Representation of the People Act, 1951? - Proviso to sub-s. (1) of s. 97 which requires recrimination notice to be given to High Court within fourteen days of 'date fixed for respondents to appear before HC to answer claim or claims' (reading the definition of 'commencement of trial' into it) has also a particular meaning and object behind it - Idea is that recrimination notice, if any, should be filed at earliest possible time so that both election petition and recrimination notice are tried at same time - Recrimination notice is thus comparable to an election petition - If s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963 does not apply to filing of an election petition, it does not equally apply to filing of recrimination notice - So far as decisions of HCs are concerned, this court cannot agree with them insofar as applicability of s. 5 of filing of an election petition and/or recrimination notice is concerned in view of decision of this Court in H.N.Yadav case (1973 Indlaw SC 359) - Appeal dismissed.
