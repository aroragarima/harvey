National Insurance Company Limited v Om Prakash Jain

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1217; (2010) 15 SCC 527

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Third Party Risk, Cause Of Accident

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Grant of Compensation - Legality - Validity of driving licence - Complaint filed before District Forum claiming compensation in lieu of damage caused to vehicle - On appeal, State Commission held that insurance company cannot be absolved of its liability to compensate owner of vehicle who would be unable to determine whether driver's driving license forged - Revision there against dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Whether State Commission justified in awarding compensation? - In National Insurance Company Limited vs. Laxmi Narain Dhut [2007 INDLAW SC 179], it was laid down that decision in case of National Insurance Company Limited vs. Swaran Singh & Anr. [2004 INDLAW SC 14] has no application to cases other than third-party risks and where originally license was fake, renewal thereof cannot validate the same - Held, in present case, complaint was filed for damage of vehicle of insured and not third party risk - District Forum and State Commission held that original license of driver was fake, hence, District Forum justified in dismissing complaint and State Commission committed error by awarding compensation to respondent - Appeal allowed.
