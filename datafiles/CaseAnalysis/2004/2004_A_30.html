Omar Usman Chamadia v Abdul and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 109; (2004) 13 SCC 234; (2005) SCC (Cr) 157; AIR 2004 SC 1508; 2004 (1) CCR 198; 2004 (1) Crimes 327; 2004 (2) CRJ 697; 2004 CRLJ 1364; 2004 (1) JCC 388; JT 2004 (2) SC 176; 2004(2) SCALE 193; 2004 (1) SLT 883; 2004 (1) Supreme 858

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 325, 326, 504, 506(2) - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 25(1), 27 - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3, 4 - Bail granted by HC - Nature of allegation made in this case and facts - At least 7 other cases involving very serious charges against this respondent are pending trial - First respondent herein has violated conditions of bail granted in earlier cases and in event of he being enlarged on bail there is every likelihood of he interfering with investigation of this case, threatening witnesses and may even go to extent of causing physical harm to complainant and others - Bail granted to first respondent by HC should be cancelled - Several occasions to notice that HCs by recording concessions shown by counsel in criminal proceedings refrain from assigning any reason even in orders by which it reverses orders of lower courts - Practice disapproved - Need for delivering a reasoned order is a requirement of law which has to be complied with in all appealable orders.
