Wassan Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1979; (1996) SCC (Cr) 119; (1996) 1 SCC 458; AIR 1995 SCW 4629; 1996 (1) ALT(SC) 17; 1996 (1) CCR 107; 1995 (4) Crimes 587; 1996 CRLJ 878; JT 1995 (8) SC 434; 1995(6) SCALE 653; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 703; 1996 (1) UJ 134

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 100, 302 and 304 - Private defence - According to the High Court the appellant had a right of private defence as he had sustained number of injuries in the incident - HC convicted the appellant for a lesser offence u/s. 304 - Whether HC was right in convicting appellant u/s. 304 and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 10 years? - Held, appellant had a right of private defence of body which extended to even causing death and in exercise of that right if he fired one gun shot which killed the deceased, it cannot be said that he was guilty of an offence even u/s. 304 Part of the IPC, 1860 on the ground that he had exceeded his right of private defence - Appeal allowed.
