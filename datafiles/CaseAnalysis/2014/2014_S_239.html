Ram Kishan and others v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 874; (2015) 4 SCC 347; AIR 2015 SC 440; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 29; 2015 (113) ALR 13; 2015 (1) AWC 816; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 17; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 259; 2016 (130) RD 101

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24 - Acquisition proceedings - Legality - Appellant nos. 24-28, owners and in possession of the land in question, were in continuous possession of the aforesaid land in question till date and harvesting crops - On 20-1-2003 the respondents published a notification u/s. 4 of the 1894 Act - Thereafter on 16-1-2004, the respondents issued notification u/s. 6 of the 1894 Act - Land Acquisition Collector passed an award bearing No.7 of the year 2006-2007 dt. 14-1-2006 - Appellants challenged the said notification - Petition was dismissed by HC on the ground that the same was not maintainable after passing of the award - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, neither the possession of the land in question has been taken by the respondents nor was the compensation paid to the appellants though more than five years have passed since the date of the award passed on 14-1-2006. Therefore, the acquisition proceedings of the land of the appellants have lapsed in view of s. 24(2) of 2013 Act. The acquisition proceedings quashed in so far as the land of the appellant Nos. 24-28 are concerned. Appeal allowed.
