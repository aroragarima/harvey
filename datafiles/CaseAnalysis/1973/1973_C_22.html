Prince Muffakham Jah Bahadur v Commissioner of Expenditure-Tax, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 237; (1973) 2 SCC 403; AIR 1973 SC 2346; 1973 (2) CTR 304; [1973] 89 ITR 41; 1973 TAXLR 1284

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Expenditure Tax Act, 1957, s. 5(o)(ii) - Trust deed - Claim of exemption - Challenged - Assesses/appellants (Grandsons of Nizam) grandfather who Constituted trust under which assesses and his daughter-in-law were given certain benefits and payment was required to be made in India - After execution of such trust, assesses as well as daughter-in-law went and resided in London and every year trustees were realized amount due from Govt. and remit amounts due to beneficiaries to London and those amounts were expended by them in London - Assesses claimed exemption for such amounts before Tribunal which was rejected but on assesses request Tribunal referred question before HC who rejected assesses question and affirmed Tribunal decision - Hence, instant appeals - Whether assesses were entitled for exemption u/s. 5(o) of the Act - Held, Tribunal opined that trustees should have been sending amounts in question to London under assesses instructions and finding of Tribunal in that regard was essentially finding of fact - Therefore, all that this Court had to see was whether that finding was either perverse or not based on any evidence - SC were unable to agree with assesses counsel that finding in question was either perverse or same was not based on any evidence - It was open to Tribunal to come to conclusion, on facts and circumstances of case, that trustees acted under instructions from assesses and that appeals to be reasonable inference - If trustees were sending amounts in question to assesses to London under instructions from assesses as found by Tribunal, in law it means that assesses had realized those amounts in India itself and, consequently, they were not entitled to exemption provided in s. 5(o) of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

Legislation Cited

Expenditure Tax Act, 1957

Expenditure Tax Act, 1957 s. 5(o)(ii)
