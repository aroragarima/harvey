Weston Electroniks and Another v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 877; (1988) SCC (Tax) 229; (1988) 2 SCC 568; AIR 1988 SC 2038; JT 1988 (2) SC 251; 1988(1) SCALE 902; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 768; 1988 (70) STC 52; 1988 (2) UJ 141

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958

Summary: Sales Tax - Lower rate of tax for local manufacturers - Levy of tax - Legality - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969, ss. 7 and 49(2) - Petitioners manufacture electronic goods including television sets, television cameras and television monitors - In 1981, while rate for electronic goods entering State for sale was maintained at 15%, rate in respect of locally manufactured goods was reduced to 6% by Notification No. (GHN-51) issued u/s. 49(2) of the Act - Notification introduced a new entry in Schedule dealing specifically with electronic goods manufactured in State - Thereafter, in 1986 rate of sales tax in respect of television sets imported from outside State was reduced from 15% to 10% and for goods manufactured within State of sales tax was reduced to 1% by Notification No. (GHN 22) - Hence, petitioner filed instant petition for quashing the above notices - Whether respondents were justified in passing the impugned notifications - Held, for purpose of ensuring same rate of tax between petitioners and local manufacturers, levy of higher rate of tax suffered by petitioners should be quashed and they be held entitled to levy of lower rate applied to local manufacturers or should higher rate imposed on petitioners be maintained and notifications imposing lower rate on local manufacturers be quashed - So long as higher rate of tax imposed on petitioners was also suffered by local manufacturers, no complaint was voiced by petitioners - However, rate levied on petitioners was rate prescribed u/s. 7 of the Act - It represented the normal standard of levy - Lower rate applied to local manufacturers was applied by invoking s. 49(2) of the Act - It represented a departure from, or exception to general norm - Impugned notifications prescribing a lower rate of tax for local manufacturers in respect of television sets and other electronic goods were quashed - Appeal allowed.
