Mool Chand Bakhru and another v Rohan and others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 63; (2002) 2 SCC 612; AIR 2002 SC 812; AIR 2002 SCW 471; 2002 (2) ALD(SC) 84; 2002 (46) ALR 763; 2002 (1) AWC 687; 2002 (1) CTC 433; JT 2002 (1) SC 465; 2002 (1) MLJ 161; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 161; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 853; 2002(1) SCALE 507; 2002 (1) SCJ 490; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 702; 2002 (1) SLT 528; 2002 (1) Supreme 385; 2002 (1) UC 532B

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Equitable Doctrine

Summary: A person claiming to be a proposed vendee, cannot protect his possession of an immovable property on the plea of part performance under Section 53-A of the Transfer of Property Act on the basis of an oral agreement, if, the terms have not been reduced in writing and unless, the written agreement is not preceded by putting the proposed vendee in possession of the property.
