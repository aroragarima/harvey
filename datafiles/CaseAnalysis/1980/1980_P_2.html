Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Lucknow v P. K. Banerjee (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 190; (1981) 1 SCC 63; AIR 1981 SC 401; 1981 ALJ 1; 1980 (19) CTR 376; [1980] 125 ITR 641; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 657; [1980] 4 TAXMAN 35; 1980 UJ 934

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Government securities, Estate, Net Wealth, Annual Income, Estate Duty, Yearly, Beneficiary, Annuity

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether interest of assessee in trust fund amounted to an annuity exempt u/s. 2(e)(iv) of Wealth Tax Act? - Held, no - Father of respondent executed trust deed by way of which certain directions were given as regards appropriation of income from trust fund - Perusal of intention of executor as inferred from deed was that assessee should get whole income of trust - Right of assessee is not an annuity - To constitute an annuity, payment should be periodical and a fixed or pre-determined one - Payment should not be liable to any variation depending upon or on any ground relating to general income of fund or estate which is charged for such payment - Question answered against assessee - Appeal allowed.
