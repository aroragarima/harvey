Avinash Kumar Chauhan v Vijay Krishna Mishra

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2007; (2009) 2 SCC 532; AIR 2009 SC 1489; 2009 (1) ALD(SC) 109; 2009 (74) ALR 912; 2009 (2) AWC 1272; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 334; 2008 (6) CTC 516; 2009 (2) G.L.R. 1202; JT 2009 (1) SC 656; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 409; 2009 (3) MPLJ 289; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 615; 2009 (106) RD 254; 2009(1) SCALE 80; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 944

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Indian Registration of Ships Act, 1841, Merchant Shipping Act 1894

Summary: Indian Stamp Act 1899 - Interpretation of ss. 33 and 35 - Held, where an instrument has been admitted in evidence, such admission shall not, except as provided in s. 21 thereof, be called in question at any stage of same suit or proceeding on the ground that the instrument has not been duly stamped - Although ordinarily an agreement to sell would not be subject to payment of stamp duty which is payable on a sale deed, but having regard to the purpose and object it seeks to achieve the legislature thought it necessary to levy stamp duty on an instrument whereby possession has been transferred - S. 33 of the Act casts a statutory obligation on all the authorities to impound a document - The court being an authority to receive a document in evidence is bound to give effect thereto - Appeal dismissed.
