Subedar Tewari and another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 211; (1989) Supp1 SCC 91; (1989) SCC (Cr) 218; AIR 1989 SC 733; 1989 (2) Crimes 724; 1989 CRLJ 923; 1989 CRLR 79; 1988(2) SCALE 1341

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 309 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 8 - (A) Bride burning - Murder or suicide - Licentious conduct of husband - Recently wed and highly educated wife burned at midnight - No kerosene etc. found in kitchen - Discord among spouses - She considering the prospect of separation and determined to start fresh life - Husband's conduct unnatural and absconding - (B) Murder - Motive - Determination of - Held, (A) It was a case of murder and not suicide - (B) Same is often than not within reach of others, including victim of crime - Order accordingly.
