Jamuna Singh and Others v Bhadai Sah

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1964 SC 1541; 1964 CRLJ 168; 1964 (2) SCJ 439; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 190, 193, 194, 423, 417, 417(3), 200, 202, 156(3) and 173 - (A) What is limitation in right of appeal in s. 417(3)?- Held, right of appeal u/s. 417(3) is limited only to cases instituted upon a complaint - (B) When should a magistrate take cognizance of an offence? - Further held, on examination of ss. 190, 193 and 194 - Its clear that a Magistrate takes cognizance of an offence upon receiving a complaint of facts which constitute such offence and when a Magistrate takes cognizance of any offence upon a report in writing of such facts made by any police officer - But if it is for ordering investigation u/s. 156(3) or issues a search warrant for purpose of investigation he cannot be said to have taken cognizance of any offence - If Magistrate had used words 'for investigation' instead of words 'for instituting a case' order would clearly be u/s. 202 of Code - Words 'for instituting a case' did not make any difference - (C) Whether Magistrate after taking cognizance can order for investigation u/s. 156(3)?- Further held, once he took cognizance he could order investigation by police only u/s. 202 and not u/s. 156(3) - (D) Whether fact that Sub Inspector of Police treated copy of petition of complaint as a F.I.R and submitted 'charge-sheet' against accused persons would make difference?- Further held, no, it could not - Report made by police officer though purporting to be a report u/s. 173 should be treated in law to be a report only u/s. 202 - Order of Magistrate asking police to institute a case and to send a report should properly and reasonably be read as one made u/s. 202 - When Magistrate asked police to institute a case he acted irregularly - Appeal dismissed.
