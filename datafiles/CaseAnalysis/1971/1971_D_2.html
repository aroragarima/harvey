Â D. A. V. College Etc. v State Of Punjab & Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, A.N. Grover, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 638; (1971) 2 SCC 269; AIR 1971 SC 1737; [1971] S.C.R. 688; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Competence Of The Legislature, Freedom Of Association

Summary: Education - Religious or linguistic minority educaional institutions - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(c), (f), 26, 29 (1), 30(1) and 50(1) - Punjab University Act, s. 72 - Guru Nanak University, Amritsar, ss. 4, 4(2), 4(3) and 5 - (A) Whether Arya Samaj sect is entitled to invoke arts. 29(1), 30(1) being a religious or linguistic minority? - Held, Arya Samaj is a religious minority, Arya Samaj have a distinct script of their own, namely Devnagri - They are therefore entitled to invoke right guaranteed u/art. 29(1) because they are a section of citizens having a distinct script and u/art. 30(1) because of their being a religious minority - (B) Whether s. 4(2), (3) of Guru Nanak University, Amritsar, Act ultra vires arts. 29(1) and 30(1)? - Further held, ss. 4(2),(3) of Guru Nanak University, Amritsar, Act does not in our view transgress guarantee u/art. 29(1) - (C) Whether ss. 4(2), 4(3) are violative of art. 14 of constituion? - Further held, ss. 4(2), 4(3) facilitates study and research of life and teachings of Guru Nanak and for study of Punjabi language but is not violative of art. 14 of Constitution - (D) Whether compulsory affiliation of petitioners to University affects their fundamental right of freedom of association as guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(c)? Further held, D A V College Trust and Management Society is not being interfered with, by any attempt to form an Association with Universityhence no infringement of art. 19(1)(c) - (E) Whether Cls. 2(1)(a), 17,18 r/w cl. 1(2) and (3)of University Act interferes with management of institutions and violates art. 30(1) of Constitution? - Further held, there is no possible justification for provisions contained in cl. 2(1)(a) and 17 of Chapter V of statutes which directly interfere with rights of management of petitioners colleges - These provisions cannot therefore be made as conditions of affiliation, non-compliance of which would involve disaffiliation and consequently they will have to be struck down as offending art. 30(1), however, cl. 18 does not suffer from same vice as cl. 17 - (F) Whether State Legislature is competent to enact s. 5 of Act which empowers State Govt. by notification to compulsorily disaffiliate from Punjab University all colleges? - Further held, when once an impugned law does not affect fundamental rights of petitioners as in this case, it is not necessary to go into question of legislative competence or to decide on validity of s. 5 - Petition allowed.
