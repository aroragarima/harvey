Marathwada Agricultural University And Others v Marathwada Krishi Vidyapith, M.S.K.S. And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 859; (2007) 8 SCC 497; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 928; AIR 2007 SC 2969; 2007 (7) AWC 7352; 2008 (1) Bom.C.R. 326; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 186; JT 2007 (10) SC 448; 2007 (3) LLJ 768; 2007 (4) LLN 500; 2007(10) SCALE 381; 2007 (7) SCJ 836; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 548; 2007 (5) SLR 621; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 157

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947; Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Grievance that qualification, nature of work, duties and responsibilities of the work of labourers who were daily rated labourers are same as that of permanent labourers employed by the University and even then the daily rated workers were getting far less wages than the emoluments which were being paid to permanent labourers - Held, a committee should be constituted for the purpose of rationalization of the wages to be paid to the concerned workers - State Government then shall take necessary action on the basis of the recommendation, after obtaining the view of the University and after giving all concerned parties an opportunity of stating their views - Appeals allowed.
