S. Rama Iyer v Sundarasa Ponnapoondar

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 371; AIR 1966 SC 1431; 1966 (2) AnWR 78; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 78; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Limited Jurisdiction

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Madras Cultivating Tenants Protection Act, 1955, s. 3(3) - Whether decision of Revenue Divisional Officer that respondent is not a cultivating tenant of appellant is subject to review by High Court in its revisional jurisdiction? - Tribunal can exercise its jurisdiction under 1955 Act only if relationship of landlord and tenant exists - Tribunal must enquire into existence of preliminary fact and if decision is not final then decision is subject to revisional jurisdiction by HC u/s. 6B - Held, HC had power to enquire into correctness of decision of tribunal and so HC had power to set-aside decision of tribunal u/s. 115 r/w s. 6 - Appeal dismissed.
