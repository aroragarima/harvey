Ranjit Hazarika v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3945; (1998) 8 SCC 635; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1725

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 164 and 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Whether conviction and sentence imposed on appellant proper? - Corroborative evidence is not an imperative component of judicial credence in every case of rape - Corroboration as a condition for judicial reliance on testimony of prosecutrix is not a requirement of law but a guidance of prudence under given circumstances - Held, both courts below have examined evidence carefully and relied on same - Evidence of prosecutrix sufficiently corroborated by other evidence - Though no injury found on private parts of girl offence of rape cannot be ruled out - No reason to interfere - Appeal dismissed.
