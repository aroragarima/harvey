Â Ganga Ram and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, A.N. Ray, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 277; (1970) 1 SCC 377; AIR 1970 SC 2178; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 481; 1970 SLR 755

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Service -Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Promotion - Validity - Whether procedure laid down for promotion violate petitioners' right as guaranteed by arts. 14 and 16? - Held, no - Direct recruits and promotees like petitioners, clearly constitute different classes and this classification is sustainable on intelligible differentia which has a reasonable connection with object of efficiency sought to be achieved and promotion to Grade I is guided by consideration of seniority cum merit - Therefore, no fault with provision - Petition dismissed.
