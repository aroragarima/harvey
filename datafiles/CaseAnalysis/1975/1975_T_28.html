Isha Beevi and Others v Tax Recovery Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Hans Raj Khanna, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 323; (1976) 1 SCC 70; AIR 1975 SC 2135; 1975 CTR 300; [1975] 101 ITR 449; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 681

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(44), 221 and 222 - Constitution, art. 226 - Travancore Income Tax Act, 1121 MEs. 66(3) - Finance Act, 1950, s. 13 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 8 - (A) Additional Collector or Collector - Whether competent to discharge duties of 'Peshkar' as Recovery Officer - (B) Prohibition-Issue of conditions essential - Existence of alternative remedy - Total absence of alternation remedy - Citing wrong provisions of law not sufficient if power exists otherwise - Held, duties automatically devolve as may be empowered by the State Govt. by a Special or general notification - Total absence of jurisdiction in the authority ought to be shown - Appeals Dismissed.
