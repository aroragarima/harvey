State Of Madhya Pradesh v Sheetla Sahai And Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1173; (2009) 8 SCC 617; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 901; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1744; 2009 CRLJ 4436; JT 2009 (10) SC 388; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1103; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 835; 2009(10) SCALE 632; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 1048

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Comments, Discharge Of Duty, Old Act, Mines And Minerals

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss.13(1)(D)(ii-iii), 13(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.120B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.197 - Criminal misconduct - Criminal conspiracy - Misuse of official position - Provided undue pecuniary advantage to contractors, alleged - Special Court charged respondents with criminal misconduct for misusing their position as public servants and thereby provided financial benefit to contractors in a dam construction project - Respondents filed Revision Petition before HC - HC allowed Revision - Whether prior sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC required for prosecuting respondents - Held, decision of allowing contractors to carry mining operation on some other quarries, was taken consciously by competent authorities of State - Authorities in-charge were aware of that such exigencies would be faced by contractors - Decision was not taken by one officer but collectively - No material showing that respondents caused any wrongful gains to themselves or loss to State - 1st respondent, being a Minister, constituted a Committee and obtained opinion of Financial advisor in this regard - 1st respondent formed an independent opinion only after consideration of entire materials on record - No material suggested that respondents nos. 1 to 7, being the public servants, either abused their position or had facilitated any pecuniary advantage to contractors - Respondents were in office for short time or retired long back, however, public servants involved in the matter still working in concerned departments have discriminately not been proceeded with - Respondents nos. 1 to 7 were performing their official duties at the relevant time - Sanction for prosecution u/s. 197 of CrPC not obtained before proceeding against respondents - Therefore, no criminal misconduct or criminal conspiracy proved - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
