Abhimanyoo Ram v State Of Up & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2704; (2008) 17 SCC 73; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1136; 2009 (3) AWC 2475; JT 2009 (1) SC 528; 2009 (3) MLJ 300; 2009(1) SCALE 107; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 1; 2009 (3) SLJ 67

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Appointment - The appellant was enrolled/registered with the Board of Indian Medicine, U.P. on 23.7.2003 - He got his name deleted from the State Register of Indian Medicine, U.P. so as to enrol himself in another State - The appellant again sought re-enrolment in U.P. which was granted with prospective effect from 23.8.2007 - The appellant was denied interview for selection by the respondents to the post of Medical Officer, as his re-enrolment did not fulfil the eligibility requirements - He therefore wanted his registration to be restored with retrospective effect from the date of original enrolment - The appellant filed a writ petition seeking a direction to respondents to call him for interview for the post of Medical Officer - In the said petition, an interim order was granted on 31.10.2007, permitting him to appear in such interview - By making use of the said interim order, the appellant secured an order from the Board of Indian Medicine, UP, restoring his enrolment from the date of initial registration - Consequently, he was called for the interview - Thereafter, the appellant moved the court for dismissal of the writ petition as not pressed - The HC, accordingly dismissed said writ petition and held that petitioner could not be permitted to draw the benefit from the interim order - The HC further directed state authority to revoke the selection/appointment of appellant, in case he had been selected/appointed because of participation in this process of selection under the interim order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, such explicit directions had become necessary to check the rising trend among litigants to secure the relief as an interim measure, and then avoid adjudication on merits, particularly in matters relating to examinations and recruitment - Particularly where the interim order granted was the same as the final relief prayed for- Therefore, appropriate essential directions cancelling or locating the interim order should be passed so as to restore status quo ante - Court cannot assume every such attempt to be dishonest - Order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
