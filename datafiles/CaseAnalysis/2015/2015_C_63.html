Chitra and another v State of Kerala and another

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 614; (2016) 1 SCC 685; 2015 (3) KLT 956; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 285; 2015(9) SCALE 129; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Foreign Liquor Rules

Summary: (A) Excise - Foreign Liquor Rules, r. 4 - Liquor licence - Appellant applied for FL3 licence but third party filed suit and was granted injunction in said suit, restraining Commissioner from issuing licence to appellant for user at her Hotel - Later, appellant's application for FL3 licence was rejected owing to amendments in Rules - Aggrieved, appellant filed petition before Single Judge and the same was allowed and in pursuance of orders of Single Judge, appellant was granted licence and demand was made for proportionate licence fee - However, on appeal, DB of HC reversed judgment of Single Judge and appellant was directed to pay entire licence fee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant is entitled to pay proportionate annual rental for year 1999-2000 instead of full annual fee of Rs.13 Lakh in respect of FL3 licence granted to her.

Held, licence to appellant could only be granted for the period from 21-12-1999 to 31-3-2000, till the close of financial year, owning to unforeseeable circumstances beyond the ken and control of the parties to instant litigation. Hence, appeal is allowed and respondent-State is directed to refund to appellant the balance amount along with interest. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Excise - Liquor licence - Appellant approached HC as its application for renewal of FL3 licence was rejected and HC issued direction to State Govt. to issue licence and also directed appellant to pay licence fee for years 2001-02 and 2002-03 - On appeal, DB of HC held that since licence was renewed in March 2003, even though appellant could conduct its business for less than fortnight in that licence years, yet, appellant was liable to pay full fee for year 2002-03 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was liable to pay full fee for year 2002-03.

Held, appellant would not be in a position to decline to accept the renewal of the licence even though it was for less than a fortnight, since that would have led to the licence being rendered defunct; which could have led to consequence of disentitlement for grant or renewal of FL3 licence in future. Hence, appellant is only liable to pay the proportionate licence fee for the period in which it could avail of the licence, i.e., 25-3-2003 to 31-3-2003 and consequently appeal allowed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - A party is entitled to seek remission in the payment of licence fee if it is precluded from transacting business on the strength of that licence because of factors and reasons extraneous to it and/or if it is granted the licence on the direction of a Court for only a portion of the financial year.
