Kedar Nath Agrawal (Dead) and another v Dhanraj Devi (dead) by Lrs. and another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 861; (2004) 8 SCC 76; 2004 (57) ALR 419; 2004 (4) AWC 3709; JT 2004 (9) SC 113; 2004 (2) RCR(Rent) 498; 2004(8) SCALE 739; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, s. 21(7) - Order of eviction - Ground of bonafide requirement - Whether subsequent event can be taken into consideration while entertaining an appeal? - Held, HC was in error in not considering subsequent event of death of both applicants - It was power as well as duty of HC to consider fact of death of applicants during pendency of writ petition - Since all three daughters got married and were staying with their in-laws, the fact was relevant and material - Where possession is sought by landlord on ground of bona fide requirement and during pendency of application, landlord dies, his LRs can prosecute such application on basis of their own need in substitution of need of deceased - Matter remitted to HC for consideration afresh as per law - Appeal allowed.
