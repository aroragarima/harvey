Uttaranchal Forest Rangers' Asson.(Direct Recruit) and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 519; (2006) 10 SCC 346; 2006 (6) AWC 5784; 2006(9) SCALE 577; 2007 (1) SLR 99; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16(1), 309 - Public Service Commission (Procedure) Rules, 1970 - Uttar Pradesh Govt. Servant Seniority Rules 1991, rr. 39, 8(1), 8(3) - Uttaranchal Subordinate Services Rules, 1951, r. 5(a) - Promotion - Appellants are direct recruits to post of Forest Rangers in State of U P - Held, promotees were appointed on 17/07/1991 and therefore cannot be given seniority over appellants who were substantively appointed prior in point of time i e in 1990 - No retrospective promotion or seniority can be granted from a date when an employee has not even been borne in cadre so as to be adversely appointed validly in meantime - Date on which vacancies arose cannot without more be made a basis of giving retrospective promotion and seniority - Directed State of Uttaranchal to revise seniority list of direct recruits in line with observations and conclusions made in this judgment - Appeals disposed of.
