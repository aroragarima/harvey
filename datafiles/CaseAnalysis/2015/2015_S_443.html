Mohan Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 285; (2015) 6 SCC 222; AIR 2015 SC 2098; 2015 CRLJ 2811; 2015 (2) JCC (Narcotics) 37; JT 2015 (4) SC 228; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 717; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 779; 2015 (3) RLW 2664; 2015(5) SCALE 330

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dominion, Standard Of Proof, Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 18 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 380, 457 - Possession of Contraband substance - Chargesheet u/s. 18 of the Act and u/ss. 457, 380 of IPC was filed against accused and Trial Court held accused guilty of charges and passed order of conviction and the same was affirmed by HC on appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, accused could be convicted under the Act.

Held, accused had requisite degree of control when, even if the said narcotic substance was not within his physical control at that moment. Further, accused was in possession of the prohibited or contraband substance which was an offence when the Act came into force and accused remained in possession of prohibited substance and as such offence u/s. 18 of the Act is made out. Furthermore, accused led to discovery of substance which was within his special knowledge, and hence, he was in possession of contraband article when the Act came into force. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - 'Possession' for the purpose of s. 18 of the Act means physical possession with animus, custody or dominion over the prohibited substance with animus or even exercise of dominion and control as a result of concealment. Further, if a person is in possession of the banned substance on the date when the Act was enforced, he would commit the offence, for on the said date he would have both the 'corpus' and 'animus' necessary in law.
