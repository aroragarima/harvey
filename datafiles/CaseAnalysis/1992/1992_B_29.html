Banaras Hindu University, Varanasi And Anr. v Dr. Indra Pratap Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, L.M. Sharma, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 864; (1992) Supp2 SCC 2; (1992) SCC (L&S) 645; AIR 1992 SC 780; 1992 (20) ATC 552; 1992 (64) FLR 690; JT 1992 (1) SC 320; 1992 (1) LLJ 624; 1992 (1) LLN 723; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 360; 1992 (2) SLJ 1; 1992 (1) SLR 636; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 190

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Education - Service - University Grants Commission Merit Promotion Scheme, para 2(a) - University teachers - Condition for Promotion - Continuous service of eight years - Lecturer denied promotion on ground that there was a break of three months twenty days in his service - Countervailing circumstance - Lecturer was reappointed on a permanent basis, on same post - Contemplation of para 2(a) for computing continuous service - Whether reasonable break in service can be condoned by - University - Held, para 2(a) itself expressly recognises eight years service having been put in more than one University - Such breaks should be condoned - Appeal dismissed.
