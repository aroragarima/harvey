Director of Income Tax v M/s Bharat Diamond Bourse

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1511; (2003) 1 SCC 741; 2002 (179) CTR 225; 2003 (179) CTR 225; [2003] 259 ITR 280; JT 2002 (10) SC 392; 2002(9) SCALE 453; 2002 (7) SLT 410; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 95; 2003 (1) Supreme 286; 173 TAXATION 1; [2002] 126 TAXMAN 365

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961 - Ss. 11, 2(15), 13(3)(a) and 13(1)(c)(ii) - Companies Act, 1956 - claim for benefits of S. 11 of I.T. Act on the ground that the institution was established wholly for 'charitable purposes' within the meaning of S. 2(15) and had been registered as such under Section 13 - Held, the dominant objects of the assessee are charitable as the dominant object is one of general public utility, and therefore, the assessee is entitled to be registered as an institution established for charitable purpose within the meaning of Section 2(15) of the Act. - However, the assessee would lose the benefit under Section 11 of the Act by falling the mischief of Section 13(3)(a) read with 13(1)(c)(ii) of the I.T. Act since during the previous years relevant to the assessment years 1989-90 and 1990-91, a substantial amount of money to the extent of Rs. 70 lakhs was lent to one Bharat Shah, who was a founder of the assessee institution, without adequate security or interest.
