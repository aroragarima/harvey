Bharat Parikh v Central Bureau of Investigation and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1693; (2008) 10 SCC 109; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 609; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 523; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2609; 2008 CRLJ 3540; 2008 (3) JCC 2088; JT 2008 (8) SC 163; 2008(10) SCALE 86; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 950

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Warrant Case

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 207, 227, 238 and 482 - Framing of charge - (A) Whether after charges are framed, a Magistrate has jurisdiction in law to recall such order on ground that prosecution had failed to comply with provisions of s. 207 of CrPC, 1973 and whether such failure would render framing of charges void? - (B) Whether in exercise of its inherent powers, High Court could quash charges framed and acquit accused on account of such non-compliance? - Held, no, once charges are framed it cannot be recalled or in other words after framing of charges order of discharge cannot be made by Magistrate - 'State of Orissa Vs. Debendra Nath Padhi' and with this, second ancillary question as to whether such failure on part of prosecution would render framing of charge void does not require answer - Further held, no, as after charges are framed evidence has to be led on behalf of prosecution to prove charge(s) if accused pleads not guilty and it is only in exceptional circumstances that a criminal proceeding may be quashed to secure ends of justice, and such stage will only come after evidence is led - Accused will get an opportunity to prove documents subsequently produced by prosecution on order of Court, but same cannot be relied upon to re-open proceedings once charge has been framed or for invocation of HC's power u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973 - Impugned orders upheld - Appeal dismissed.
