Â K. A. Abbas v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, C.A. Vaidyialingam, A.N. Ray, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 165; (1970) 2 SCC 780; AIR 1971 SC 481; 1971 (2) SCJ 242; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Examining Committee, Board Of Film Censors, Film Censor, Defamatory Material, Defamatory Articles

Summary: Constitution, arts.14, 19(1)(a) and 19(2) - Cinematograph Act, 1952, s. 5B(2) - Limitation Act, 1908 - (A) Treatment of motion picture on different footing - From other forms of art and expression - Validity of classification - (B) Validity - Censorship justified by Constitution - General principles in directions by Central Govt. cannot be said to be vague - (C) Law uncertain and prima facie taking away guaranteed freedom - Held, is valid classification - There is however absence of direction for preserving art-Some procedural safeguards are also needed - Law offends - Petition Allowed.
