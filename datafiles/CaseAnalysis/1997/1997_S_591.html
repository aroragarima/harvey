Tata Davy Limited v State of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3119; (1997) 6 SCC 669; AIR 1998 SC 2928; 1997 (3) CLT 318; [1998] 93 Comp Cas 1; 1997 (94) E.L.T. 477; JT 1997 (7) SC 216; 1997 (2) LLJ 989; 1997(5) SCALE 335; 1998 (111) STC 462; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 232; 1997 (7) Supreme 6

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Bombay Village Panchayat Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 22(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, sch. 7, List 2, Entry 54 - Suspension of legal proceedings against sick company - Scope of phraseology 'any other law' in s. 22 of Central Act - Whether includes State Acts? - Recovery of sales tax arrears cannot be made from sick company without consent of Board for Industrial and Financial Reconstruction - Words 'any other law' need not be so read as to exclude all State Laws - Appeals allowed.
