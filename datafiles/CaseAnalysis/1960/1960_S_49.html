Commissioner Of Income Tax, Bombay, North Kutch And Saurashtra, Ahmedabad v Smt. Indira Balakrishna

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 168; AIR 1960 SC 1172; 1961 (63) BomLR 197; 1973 CTR 279; [1960] 39 ITR 546; 1961 (1) KerLR 282; 1961 (1) SCJ 153; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 513

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.3 - Assessment - Inheritance - Enjoyment - Income - B left behind him three widows and two daughters - Widows as legal heirs inherited estate of deceased - For two a/ys 1950-51 and 1951-52 Income Tax Officer issued notices to legal heirs of B - For both years Income Tax Officer took status of assessee as an association of persons and on that footing made two assessment orders - Widows ought to have been assessed separately and not as an association of persons and that in any event income from property ought to have been assessed separately in the hands of three widows by reason of provisions in s.9(3) of Act - Tribunal assumed that entire estate of deceased B was inherited and possessed by three widows as joint tenants and its income was liable to be assessed in their hands in status of an association of persons - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessment made on widows in status of association of persons was valid in law - Held, no finding that three widows had combined in a joint enterprise to produce income - Only finding was that they had not exercised their right to separate enjoyment and except for receiving dividends and interest jointly, it had been found that they have done no act which had helped to produce income in respect of shares and deposits - Widows had status of an association of persons within meaning of s.3 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
