Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Gurbux Rai Harbux Rai

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 400; (1971) 3 SCC 654; AIR 1971 SC 2444; [1972] 83 ITR 86; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(b) - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, ss.10A, 15 - Partnership business - Chargeable accounting periods - Profit and loss - Jurisdiction - Assessee/registered firm carried on business in piece goods and commission agents - During accounting periods G and H were two partners of assessees with equal shares in profit and loss - In proceedings for assessment of 1940 Act for 2 chargeable accounting periods - Assessee informed Tax Officer that joint family dissolved and reconstitution of business of partnership - IO found that assessee was partition amongst the members of family could not be accepted - IO directed that income assessed as income of assessee and not as income of a separate firm - HC observed that Excess Profits Tax Officer was not competent to take proceedings u/s.15 of 1940 Act, it was not open to him to apply provisions of s. 10 in proceedings up reopening assessment u/s. 15 of 1940 Act, for in view of HC an order u/s. 10-A of 1940 Act passed only where Excess Profits Tax Officer was seized of jurisdiction in a pending assessment proceeding - As the proceedings initiated by Excess Profits Tax Officer u/s. 15 of 1940 Act were void, he had no power to make an order u/s. 10-A of 1940 Act - HC answered 2nd question also in negative - Hence instant appeal - Whether IO have jurisdiction to initiate proceedings u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - Held, IO have jurisdiction to initiate proceedings u/s. 34(1)(b) of 1922 Act - Appellate Assistant Commissioner proceeding related to assessment of income tax u/s. 10A of 1940 Act was a special provision which deals with transactions designed to avoid or reduce liability to excess profits tax - Information came into possession of Excess Profits Tax Officer of partial partition have been effected was relevant for purpose of s.15 of 1940 Act and once he had initiated proceedings under that section he was perfectly competent and had jurisdiction to examine for purpose of s.10A of 1940 Act - Appeals allowed.
