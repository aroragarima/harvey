Â Bhagirath and Others v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, E.S. Venkataramiah, O. Chinnappa Reddy, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 427; (1985) SCC (Cr) 280; (1985) 2 SCC 580; AIR 1985 SC 1050; 1985 (87) BomLR 400; 1985 CRLJ 1179; 1985(1) SCALE 719; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 743

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jail Manual

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 ss. 428, 432, 433, 433A - Murder - Conviction - Sentence set off - Maintainability -Appellants/accused were sentenced to life imprisonment for offence of murder - Accused filed petitions in Delhi HC asking that their case be referred for orders of Delhi Administration under paragraph 516-B of Punjab Jail Manual, petitions were rejected - Hence instant appeals - Whether accused were entitled to benefit of s. 428 CrPC - Held, there were no words of limitation either in the section or in its marginal note which would justify restricting plain and natural meaning of word 'term' so as to comprehend only sentences which were imposed for fixed or ascertainable period - Further, large number of cases in which accused suffer long undertrial detentions were cases punishable with imprisonment for life - Usually those who were liable to be sentenced to imprisonment for life were not enlarged on bail, to deny benefit of s. 428 to them was to withdraw application of benevolent provision from large majority of cases in which such benefit would be needed and justified - Therefore, SC directed that, period of detention undergone by 2 accused as undertrial prisoners, should be set off against sentence of life imprisonment imposed upon them, subject to provision contained in s. 433A Crpc and provided that order was passed by appropriate authority u/ss. 432 or 433 CrPC - Appeal allowed.
