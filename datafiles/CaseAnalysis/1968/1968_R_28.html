Hiralal Agrawal, Etc v Rampadarath Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 435; AIR 1969 SC 244; 1969 BLJR 186; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Act, 1962, s. 16(3) - Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling Area and Acquisition of Surplus Land) Rules, 1963, r. 19 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 61 - Application u/s. 16(3) of the Act - Maintainability of - Right of re-conveyance of the co-sharer - Whether application was premature as registration of sale deed was not then completed and, therefore there was not yet a completed transfer? - Held, sale there was completed only when registration of sale deed was completed as contemplated by s. 61 of Registration Act and, therefore, talab-i-mowasibat made before date of completion of registration was premature and a suit based on such a demand of right of preemption was premature and must, therefore, fail - Assuming, therefore, that application was presented on 26-11-1964, transferee's title having related back to date of execution of sale deed, transfer must be deemed to be complete on that date and, therefore, it was not correct that right of re-conveyance had not accrued to appellant on 26-11-1964 - (B) Jurisdiction of the Collector - To accept the application u/s. 16 of the Act - During the course of registration - Whether Collector had no jurisdiction to entertain such an application, his jurisdiction being dependent on a transfer having taken place? - Held, annexing a certified copy of sale deed where a copy of registered dead is not yet available, on account of process of registration not having been completed would be sufficient compliance of directory prescription so long as it furnishes information necessary for Collector to proceed with application - Fact that a copy of registered deed was not furnished along with application was, therefore, not fatal to application nor did such omission deprive Collector of his jurisdiction to entertain - Board of Revenue and HC were not right in dismissing appellant's application - Appeal allowed.
