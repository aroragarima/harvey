Sector-6, Bahadurgarh Plotholders Association (Regd.) And Others v Na

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 806; (1996) 1 SCC 485; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 23; JT 1995 (9) SC 167; 1995(6) SCALE 765; 1996 (1) SCJ 28; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Land & Property - Contract & Commercial - Punjab Urban Estates Act, 1964 - Punjab Urban Estates Rules, 1965 - Allotment of plot - Interest on unpaid installments - Offer was made for freehold sale of about 2200 plots on first come first serve basis - Applicants were informed that all modern amenities would be provided - Members of appellant-association, applied for allotment - Appellant no. 2 was informed about the allotment of plot by the Estate Officer on terms and conditions mentioned in the memo - The first installment became due as per condition no. 5 after the expiry of one year from the date of issue of the allotment order - Respondent no. 2, the Estate Officer, sent notices to the members of the appellant-association on installments not being paid to pay the installments including interest on the total price of the plot, failing which, it was stated, that action u/s. 10 of the 1964 Act would be taken which would result in resumption and forfeiture - Members of the association acted as required by the notices but without actually taking possession of the plots - It was so because the plots had not been developed as visualised by the advertisement seeking applications - Various representations were made to respondent no. 2 for early development and for delivering the possession of the plots - It had been alleged that the members of the association were being charged interest without actual delivery of possession of the plots which according to the members was not permissible - Respondents contended that possession of the plot would be offered to the appellants as soon as the development was completed and in case the appellant was interested in taking possession of the undeveloped plot, they were prepared - Appellants contended that what was offered for allotment was developed plots and not undeveloped ones and as the plots were yet to be developed fully, the respondents could not have charged interest because possession of developed plots was yet to be given - Respondents submitted that as per Rule 12(2) of the 1965 Rules, interest accrued from the date of the issue of the allotment order as had been mentioned in the aforesaid condition No. 5 - Whether appellants were liable to be charged interest - Held, interest in the present case had become due though most of the members of the appellant-association had paid the installments with interest as per the notice of respondent no.2 - It would be open to the members of the Association to claim refund if they had paid interest - A statement had been made on behalf of the respondents that possession of the developed plots would be given to the appellants within a period of six months failing which the respondents would not only be liable for contempt but the allottees would be exonerated from the liability to pay any interest whatsoever - Appeal disposed of.
