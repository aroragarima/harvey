Bhimasha v Special Land Acquisition Officer And Another

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, H.S. Bedi, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2581; (2008) 10 SCC 797; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 155A

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 18 - Acquisition of land - Enhancement of award - Lands belonged to appellant were acquired by Govt. - Land Acquisition Officer fixed market value of acquired land, which was assailed by appellant - On reference u/s. 18 of Act, Reference Court awarded compensation at rate of Rs.36,200/- per acre - On appeal, HC referred to yield notification and price list issued by competent authority as also average price of both crops in relation to relevant year and concluded that market price of land comes to Rs.66,550/- per acre - However, HC refused to award compensation at said rate on premise that appellant had claimed compensation at rate of Rs.58,500/- per acre - Hence instant appeal.

Held, in impugned order HC, after taking note of yield notification issued by Govt. and price list notified by competent authority for crops concluded that market value of land is Rs.66,550/- per acre. Appellant's omission to make appropriate claim before HC after paying requisite Court fee cannot be castigated as one lacking bona fide. HC should have, after taking note of, peculiar facts of case and market value determined by it, awarded higher compensation to appellants subject to condition of paying balance court fee. This, having not been done, Court feels that ends of justice could be met if impugned order is suitably modified. Impugned judgment is modified and market value of land fixed by HC is enhanced to Rs.66,550/- per acre. Appeal allowed.
