Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I v Ciba of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 367; AIR 1968 SC 1131; [1968] 69 ITR 692; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 696

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - Expenses - Admissible deduction - Scientific research - Secret processes - After incorporation of assessee, activities of company were bifurcated - Pharmaceutical part was taken over by assessee - Company made agreement with assessee in consideration of payment with regard to communication of results of research work with prior approval of company - During A/Ys, assessee contended that payment made by assessee was an admissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act and if not then u/s. 10 (2)(xii) of the Act, also, payment made for meeting expenses of suit was allowable expense u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - ITO disallowed claim - AAC confirmed order of ITO - Tribunal held that payments made by assessee were allowable u/s. 10 (2)(xii) and in any event under s. 10 (2)(xv) - HC disagreed with Tribunal as to admissibility of expenditure u/s. 10 (2)(xii) of the Act, but agreed on its admissibility u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Whether payment made by assessee in pursuance of agreement was admissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xii) of the Act, and if not then u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act, either in part or whole - Held, amounts paid by assessee were not laid out or expended by assessee on scientific research related to business of assessee - Company did not wholly parted with its Indian business - There was no attempt to part with technical knowledge absolutely in favour of assessee - The secret processes were not sold by the company to assessee - HC was right in rejecting claim for allowance u/s. 10(2)(xii) of the Act - Appeals dismissed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Obligation - Expenses of suit - Allowable expenses - Patent rights - Whether payment made in accordance with terms of agreements for meeting expenses of suit was allowable expense u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Held, contentions could not be accepted - It could not be assumed that rights to patents were available to assessee under agreement with company - No argument was advanced either before Tribunal or before departmental authorities that assessee was entitled to these patent rights and no investigation was permissible on that question in HC - By paying to company, share of costs in suit assessee was not seeking to protect its trading interest - There was no obligation on assessee to pay costs of suit - HC was right in answering against assessee - Appeals dismissed.
