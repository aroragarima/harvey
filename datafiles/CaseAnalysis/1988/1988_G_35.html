Municipal Corporation Of Delhi v Gurnam Kaur

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, B.C. Ray, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 703; (1989) 1 SCC 101; AIR 1989 SC 38; 1988 BBCJ 201; 1988 (36) DLT 194; JT 1988 (4) SC 11; 1988(2) SCALE 1155; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 929; 1988 (2) UJ 713

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Municipal Act, 1888

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21, 133 and 141 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 320 and 322 - Precedents - Binding effect of consent order - Supreme Court directed Municipal Corporation in earlier case on consent of parties - To construct stalls for pavement squatters - New plea raised for first time before SC - Held, not binding on subsequent judge, High Court should not follow it as precedent - Plea not allowed - Appeal allowed.
