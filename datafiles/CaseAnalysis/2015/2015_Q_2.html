Queen's Educational Society v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 192; (2015) 8 SCC 47; AIR 2015 SC 3253; 2015 (3) Bom.C.R. 1; [2015] 372 ITR 699; JT 2015 (9) SC 169; 2015 (3) MLJ 494; 2015(3) SCALE 641; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 838; [2015] 231 TAXMAN 286

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Educational institution - Surplus - Appellant-Educational society filed returns for A/Y 2000-2001 and A/Y 2001-2002 showing net surplus and claimed exemption u/s. 10(23C)(iiiad) of the Act - AO rejected exemption claimed but on appeal, appellate authority allowed exemption and further appeal by revenue to Tribunal was dismissed - However, on reference, Uttarakhand HC set aside judgment of Tribunal and affirmed order of AO - Hence, instant appeal by Appellant-Educational society - Further, by various impugned orders passed, appellate authority withdrew exemptions granted u/s. 10(23C) (vi) of the Act r/w r.2CA of the rules for various A/Ys in favour of other assessees but the same were set aside by Punjab and Haryana HC and hence, instant appeals by revenue - Whether exemption could be granted in favour of assesses.

Held, it is not necessary that there should be revision in the constitution of the Trust or institution that the activity should be carried on no profit or no loss basis or that profit should be proscribed. Even if there is no such express provision, the nature of the charitable purpose, the manner in which the activity for advancing the charitable purpose is being carried on and the surrounding circumstances might clearly indicate that the activity is not propelled by a dominant profit motive. What is necessary to be considered is whether having regard to all the facts and circumstance of the case, the dominant object of the activity is profit making or carrying out a charitable purpose. If it is the former, the purpose would not be a charitable purpose, but, if it the latter, the charitable character of the purpose would not be lost. Hence, judgment of Uittarakhand HC is set aside and decision of Tribunal is approved. Further, judgments of Punjab and Haryana, Delhi and Bombay HCs are approved. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Law common to ss. 10(23C)(iiiad) and (vi) is that firstly, where an educational institution carries on activity of education primarily for educating persons, fact that is makes surplus does not lead to the conclusion that it ceases to exist solely for educational purposes and becomes an institution for the purpose of making profit; secondly, distinction should be drawn between making of surplus and an institution being carried on 'for profit' and no inference arises that merely because imparting education results in making profit, it becomes an activity for profit. If after meeting expenditure, a surplus arises incidentally from the activity carried on by the educational institution, it will not cease to be one existing solely for educational purposes.
