Ghaziabad Development Authority & Anr v Ashok Kumar & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 195; (2008) 4 SCC 261; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1334; 2008 (117) FLR 1198; JT 2008 (2) SC 494; 2008 (1) LLJ 1013; 2008 (2) LLN 51; 2008(2) SCALE 593; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 1069; 2008 (3) SLR 366

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether Labour Court was justified in awarding the relief of reinstatement with full back wages in favour of the workman? - Held, proviso appended to clause (a) of s. 6-N provides that no notice would be necessary to be served, if the retrenchment has been in terms of an agreement which specified a date for the termination of service - Appellant has failed to prove that the services of the first respondent were taken under an agreement providing for a specific date for termination thereof - If there did not exist any post, Labour Court should not have directed reinstatement of the first respondent in service - Appellant should be directed to pay compensation to the first respondent instead and in place of the relief of reinstatement in service - Directed to pay a sum of Rs.50, 000/- - Order accordingly.
