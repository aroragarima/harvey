Wazirchand Mahajan and Another v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 62; AIR 1967 SC 990; 1967 (69) PLR 423; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 303

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 20 and 37(1) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, Sch. 1, art. 181 - Whether application of appellants for filing arbitration agreement barred u/art. 181? - S. 37(1) deals only with authority of arbitrator to deal with and decide any dispute referred to him - S. 37(1) does not confer authority upon court to reject application for filing of an arbitration agreement u/s. 20 because claim is not made within three years from date on which right to apply arose - Court must be satisfied only about existence of a valid and subsisting written agreement which has been executed before institution of any suit and that a dispute has arisen with regard to subject matter of agreement within jurisdiction of court - Court not concerned with question whether claim of a party to arbitration agreement is barred by limitation - That question is for arbitrator to decide - Held, Judicial Commr. erred in rejecting application of appellants for filing arbitration agreement as barred u/art. 181 - Appeal allowed.
