K. Venkamma v Govt. of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 279; (1977) 3 SCC 36; AIR 1977 SC 1170; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Nationalisation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Inter-state route - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 63(1) and 68 D(3) - Whether a route, whose termini lie within the same State but which traverses in its course one or more other States, be designated as inter-state route? - Relied on Khazan Singh case 1973 Indlaw SC 15 - Held, route is an inter state route - (B) Whether A.P. State transport ply its bus over TN enclave without counter signature exemption by second proviso to s. 63(1) of Act? - Further Held, A.P. may ply its buses over T.N. without counter signature exemption u/s. 63(1) having been granted in that behalf by the 2nd proviso to s. 63(1) - (C) Whether in nationalisation proposed by the respondent State cannot materialise into an 'approved scheme' unless as desiderated by the proviso to s. 68 D(3) the previous approval of the Central Govt. is secured - Further Held, the scheme of nationalisation is operative even in the absence of the previous approval of the Central Govt, so far as the portions which fall within Andhra Pradesh are concerned - Further held, nationalisation cannot become effective over the tiny strip in Tamil Nadu and private operators may still be permitted to ply their services over that strip by the concerned authority within Tamil Nadu State - Appeal dismissed.
