Subbu Singh v State By Public Prosecutor

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 578; (2009) 6 SCC 462; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1106; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3797; 2009 CRLJ 3433; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 405; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 40; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 823; 2009(6) SCALE 671; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 383; 2009 (2) UC 1064

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7 r/w 12, 13(2) r/w 13(1)(d) r/w 12 and 20 - Acquittal by Trial Court - Conviction by High Court - Appeal - Land dispute between PW 4 and PW 21 - Quarrel - Accused 1/appellant a sub inspector of police - Relative of PW 4 reached police station - PW 21 called for - PW 21 lying in injured condition where quarrel took place - A1 first conducted enquiry and than ask them to reach Muchalika(agreement) to settle the dispute in Civil Court - Demand of bribe from PW 21 - Complaint to Vigilance - Recovery of money not in dispute - Evidence of PWs found to be reliable as to prosecution case of PW4 reaching police station and subsequent enquiries and the warnings issued - Subsequent enquiry found to be mock one - Stand of A1 that money was kept for the purpose of falsely implicating him found to be without substance as accused was a police officer who knew the consequences of the bribe; no explanation by A1 as to why he took the money in his examination u/s 313 of Criminal Procedure Code - Once it is proved by the prosecution that the money was demanded as bribe and the same was received from PW2, s. 20 of the Act comes into play, and once there is presumption u/s. 20, it is for the accused to establish that the amount was not received as bribe - Minimum sentence has been imposed by HC; interference not called for - Appeal dismissed.
