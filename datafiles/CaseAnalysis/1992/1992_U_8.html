Satyanarain Dudhani v Uday Kumar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 738; (1993) Supp2 SCC 82; AIR 1993 SC 367; AIR 1992 SCW 3449; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 752; 1992(3) SCALE 221; 1993 (1) UJ 70

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 116A - Respondent challenged election of appellant by way of an election petition on the sole ground that counting of the votes was done illegally and against law - High Court allowed election petition and declared the petitioner-respondent elected to Legislative Assembly - Appeal by returned candidate - Held, when there was no contemporaneous evidence to show any irregularity or illegality in the counting ordinarily, it would not be proper to order recount on the basis of bare allegations in the election petition - Further held, it is settled proposition of law that the secrecy of the ballot papers cannot be permitted to be tinkered lightly - As order of recount cannot be granted as a matter of course - Secrecy of ballot papers has to be maintained and only when HC is satisfied on the basis of material facts pleaded in the petition and supported by contemporaneous evidence that recount can be ordered - As a consequence order of HC declaring the election of appellant Assembly Constituency as void and declaring respondent as duly elected is also set aside - Appeal allowed.
