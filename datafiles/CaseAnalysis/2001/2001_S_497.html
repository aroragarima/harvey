Ramachandra Reddy and Company v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Brijesh Kumar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19760; (2001) 4 SCC 241; AIR 2001 SC 1523; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 107; 2001 (1) ARBLR 643; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 406; 2001 (1) RAJ 469; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 346; 2001(2) SCALE 291; 2001 (2) SCJ 325; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 186; 2001 (2) SLT 490; 2001 (2) Supreme 220; 2001 (1) UC 609B

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, ss. 14, 16, 30, 33 and 39 - Remittance u/s. 16 on ground that Arbitrators have left undetermined claims of claimants on items nos. 1, 2 and 3 on an erroneous view of relevant clauses of agreement - (A) Whether claim item No. 1 is in legality of award in relation to claim items 2 and 3? - Held, discretion having been conferred on court, to re merit an award, said discretion has to be judicially exercised and an Appellate Court would not be justified in interfering with exercise of discretion unless discretion has been misused - An error of law on face of award would mean that one can find in award or a document actually incorporated thereto stating reasons for a judgment some legal propositions which is basis of award and when can be said to be erroneous - Documents not incorporated directly or indirectly into award cannot be looked into for purpose of finding out any alleged error - Courts are not to investigate beyond award of Arbitrators and documents actually incorporated therein as such when there would be no patent error on face of award, it would not be open for Court to go proceedings of award - Principle of integrity of contract and refusal to interfere with award - (B) Whether it would be permissible for interfering with an award which was a non-speaking one? - Further held, it is not permissible to probe into mental process of Arbitrator and then interfered with same - Appeal dismissed.
