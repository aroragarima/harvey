I. Chuba Jamir and Others v State of Nagaland and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 875; (2009) 15 SCC 169; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1418; 2009 (122) FLR 978; JT 2009 (9) SC 299; 2009 (4) LLN 80; 2009(9) SCALE 679; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 118; 2010 (2) SLR 614

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Compensatory Allowance, Nagaland Economics and Statistics Service Rules, 1973

Summary: Service - Nagaland Economics and Statistics Service Rules, 1973 - Merger of posts/cadres - Encadrement of post - Respondent no. 3 was given an appointment/promotion as assistant director(AD) in Nagaland Economics and Statistical Service, who was earlier posted as statistician-cum-economists in the PWD and Housing Department - Appointment was as a consequence of encadrement of the post previously held with the post in E & S service - Appellants/petitioners were direct recruits in E & S service, who were appointed on various dates in the year 1984; in 1990 five posts of ADs fell vacant and appellants/petitioners claimed that they had five years of service and were otherwise eligible for promotion as AD; but they were not considered for the post - High Court through its single Judge issued directions which were in favour of writ petitioners - Division Bench of HC reversed the judgment of single Judge in writ appeal - Whether proper? - Held, Yes - Petitioners had approached the HC after 7 or 8 years and such delay was ignored by the single Judge - Submissions by appellants/petitioners were based on the premise that respondent no. 3 was brought to the post of AD in E & S service by giving him promotion; premise was unfounded - Court observed that it was not a case of promotion but a case of merger of the post - In the interest of administration it is open to the State Govt., as a matter of policy, to bring about merger of posts/cadres and, ordinarily, the matter does not warrant any interference by the Court - Further observations made - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
