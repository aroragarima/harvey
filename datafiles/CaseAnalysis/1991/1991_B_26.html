State of Punjab v Balwant Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, M.H. Kania, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1045; (1992) Supp3 SCC 108; AIR 1991 SC 2301; 1991 (3) CCC 721; 1992 (1) DMC 569; 1991 (2) HLR 664; JT 1991 (4) SC 128; 1992 (2) PLR 102; 1991(2) SCALE 783; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 458; 1991 (2) UJ 754

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Land & Property; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 15 and 16 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922 - One Smt Mahan Kaur, wife of Jaimal Singh inherited from her husband certain agricultural land - Some of lands were under mortgage and are in possession of defendants 2 to 6 - After coming into force of Act, she died interstate - On being informed that there was no heir entitled to succeed to her property, Revenue Asst. Collector sanctioned mutation in favour of state - Balwant Singh claiming to be a legal heir of her brought suit - Suit was resisted by state on ground that intestate has left behind no heir to succeed and mutation effected in favour of state was valid - Defendants 2 to 6 contended that right to redeem mortgage has been extinguished and they have become owners of property by being in possession for more than sixty years - Trial Court held that plaintiff was not entitled to succeed to property of deceased since property was inherited from her husband - Second appeal preferred by plaintiff was, however, accepted by High Court - HC decreed suit for possession even against defendants 2 to 6 - That has been challenged by defendants 2 to 6 - Their grievance is that HC ought not to have decreed suit against them since plaintiff's right to redeem mortgage was not adjudicated by Trial Court and by agreement, question was expressly left open - Plaintiff is grandson of brother of Mahan Kaur - According to him he is entitled to get property of deceased - Case of state is that plaintiff is not her qualified heir under Act - Held, submission of defendants 2 to 6 appears to be correct and decree against them made by HC is plainly untenable - There is indeed no controversy on that aspect of matter - Sub-s. (2) of s. 15, was intended only to change order of succession specified under sub-s. (1) and not to eliminate other classes of heirs - Appeal dismissed.
