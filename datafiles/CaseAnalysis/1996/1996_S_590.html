S. Gopal Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4023; (1996) 4 SCC 596; (1996) SCC (Cr) 792; AIR 1996 SC 2184; 1996 (3) Crimes 35; 1996 CRLJ 3237; JT 1996 (6) SC 268; 1996(5) SCALE 78; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Women & Children - Dowry (Prohibition) Act, 1961, ss. 2 and 4 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 42 and 114 - Dowry - Adverse presumption - Alleged cancellation of marriage for non-fulfillment of dowry demand on basis of letter - Letter not produced and prosecution relying on some other letter for it - Credibility of expert evidence and validity of conviction for demand of dowry - Weak type of evidence, not conclusive and, therefore, not safe to rely upon it without seeking independent and reliable corroboration - Conviction cannot be based on such assumptions without offence being proved beyond a reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed.
