M/S Dhampur Sugar (Kashipur) Ltd v State Of Uttranchal And Ors

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 926; (2007) 8 SCC 418; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1635; 2007 (6) ALJ 631; JT 2007 (11) SC 209; 2007(11) SCALE 374; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 199; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2672

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Sugar-Cane Act, 1934, Central Government framed the Sugarcane (Control) Order, 1966, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, U.P. Sugarcane (Purchase Tax) Act, 1961, Uttar Pradesh Khandsari Sugar Manufacturers Licensing Order, 1967, Special Bearer Bonds (Immunities and Exemptions) Act, 1981

Summary: U.P. Sugarcane (Purchase Tax) Act, 1961 - Prayer for direction or order quashing and setting aside relaxation in Clause (ka) of Notification and for quashing an order issuing licence for Power Crusher issued in favour of respondent No. 4 rejected by High Court - Appeal against - Held, Court of Law is not expected to propel into the unchartered ocean of Government Policies - Once it is held that the Government has power to frame and reframe, change and rechange, adjust and readjust policy, the said action cannot be declared illegal, arbitrary or ultra vires the provisions of the Constitution only on the ground that the earlier policy had been given up, changed or not adhered to - It also cannot be attacked on the plea that the earlier policy was better and suited to the prevailing situation - Mere assertion, vague averment or bald statement is not enough to hold the action to be mala fide - High Court, was right in observing that the change of policy was not limited to the case of respondent No. 4 but it was uniformly applied to one and all - Appeal dismissed.
