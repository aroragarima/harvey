Murari Lal S/o Ram Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 302; (1980) SCC (Cr) 330; (1980) 1 SCC 704; AIR 1980 SC 531; 1980 CRLJ 396; 1980 CrLR(SC) 168; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 460 r/w ss. 34, 457, 380, 392, 394, 397 - Murder - Robbery - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed robbery and murder on deceased - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/ss. 302, 460 r/w ss. 34, 457, 380, 392, 394, 397 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 302 , 460 r/w ss. 34, 457, 380, 392, 394 and 397 IPC and sentenced to death - Appeal before HC - HC altered conviction from s. 302 IPC to s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and substituted sentence of imprisonment for life for sentence of death - Hence instant appeal - Whether conviction and sentence recorded by HC was sustainable - Held, circumstance against accused was recovery of deceased's watch at accused instance - Recovery was too remote in point of time to be linked with robbery and murder - It was true that there was considerable time-lag - SC would have found it difficult to link recovery of watch with robbery and murder had this been only circumstance - But, other vital circumstance that writing made by accused was left on deceased's table that night - That circumstance coupled with recovery of dead man's watch at instance of accused was sufficient - In absence of any acceptable explanation to hold accused guilty of offences of which he was convicted - Appeal dismissed.
