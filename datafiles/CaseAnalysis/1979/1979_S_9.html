S. P. Bhatnagar and another v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 546; (1979) SCC (Cr) 323; (1979) 1 SCC 535; AIR 1979 SC 826; 1979 CRLJ 566; 1979 CRLR 328; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 875

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409, 109, 120B - Instant appeals were filed against the decision of the HC maintaining the conviction of the appellants u/s. 120B read with ss. 409, 109 of the IPC and s. 5(2) of the Act and against the simple imprisonment awarded to the appellants - Whether, on the facts and the circumstances case, appellants should be granted with relief - Held, after considering the facts and the circumstances, instant court was of the view that, evidence adduced by the prosecution did not lead to an unerring certainty that both the appellants acted with any dishonest or corrupt motive or abused their position - And instant court further held, that non examination by the prosecution of the material witnesses for the unfolding the case had left some yawning gaps in the evidence which were difficult to bridge and if those material witnesses had been produce many of the points which remained obscured and hidden up would have been cleared up, hence, instant court discharged the appellants from the charges and conviction thereof was set aside - Appeals allowed.
