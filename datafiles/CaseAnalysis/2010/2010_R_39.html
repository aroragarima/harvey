Rajesh Kohli v High Court Of J. & K. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 793; (2010) 12 SCC 783; 2011 (263) E.L.T. 168; JT 2010 (10) SC 276; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 553; 2010(10) SCALE 134; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 699; 2010 (6) SLR 56

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Jammu & Kashmir Higher Judicial Service Rules

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Jammu & Kashmir Higher Judicial Service Rules, r. 15 - Petitioner appointed as District & Sessions Judge on probation basis for 2 years - A complaint was received against the petitioner on charges that he had misappropriated some amount fraudulently when he was an advocate - HC Registrar (Vigilance) held an enquiry in the matter and found that charge against the petitioner was proved - HC Chief Justice referred the matter to the Chairman, Disciplinary Committee - Petitioner, in the meantime, completed his 2 years of probation, however, petitioner's service records were place before the Full Court for confirmation or extension of his probation - Full Court passed a resolution recommending the dispensation of the service of petition - HC Chief Justice/1st respondent forwarded the resolution to 2nd respondent/State Government for termination of petitioner's service - Hence the present writ petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution - (A) Whether HC is the competent authority to recommend the dispensation of petitioner's service - Held, impugned resolution taken by the full court of HC on its administrative side clearly indicated that the matter regarding petitioner's confirmation or otherwise or extension of his probation period for another one year was considered by the full court but since his service was not found to be satisfactory on consideration of the records, full court decided not to confirm him in service and recommended for dispensation of his service - On the basis of the recommendation of the 1st respondent/HC, impugned termination order was passed by the 2nd respondent/Government - Facts clearly proved and established that the impugned termination order was not issued by 1st respondent/HC but it only recommended petitioner's termination as his service was not found to be satisfactory - Said recommendation was accepted by the 2nd respondent/Government which finally ordered the termination of petitioner's service - 2nd respondent's order was an order of the competent authority, hence it was a valid order and should be treated as such, although it was specifically not issued in the name of the Governor - (B) Whether impugned orders are stigmatic orders - Held, impugned orders were issued in view of the resolution of the full court meeting where the full court of the HC held that the service of the petitioner was unsatisfactory - Whether or not the probation period could be or should be extended or petitioner's service should be confirmed is required to be considered by the full court of the HC and while doing so necessarily the service records of the petitioner were required to be considered and if from the service records it was disclosed that the service of the petitioner was not satisfactory - 1st respondent has the liberty to record such dissatisfaction and even mentioning the same in the order would not amount to casting any aspersion on the petitioner nor it could be said that stating in the order that his service was unsatisfactory amounts to a stigmatic order - (C) Whether granting two increments during the probation of petitioner would implied that his service was satisfactory - Held, since the petitioner was continuing in service, therefore, the case for granting increment was required to be considered which was so granted - Mere granting of yearly increments would not in any manner indicate that after completion of the probation period the full court of the HC was not competent to scrutinize his records and on the basis thereof take a decision as to whether or not his service should be confirmed or dispensed with or whether his probation period should be extended - HC has a solemn duty to consider and appreciate the service of a judicial officer before confirming him in service - Petition dismissed.
