Â University of Mysore and Another v C. D. Govinda Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 169; AIR 1965 SC 491; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 575

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Education Norms

Summary: Service - Mysore University Act, 1956, s. 26(2) - University Grants Commission Act, 1956, s. 26 (1)(e) - Appointment - Disputed - Validity - Appellant no.1/university appointed appellant no.2 as 'Research Reader' - Order of appointment was challenged by respondent stating that he should be given 'Research Reader' as he was more qualified than appellant no.2 - Appellant no.1/university denied claim of respondent - Respondent filed writ petition and was allowed by HC setting aside appointment of appellant no.2 as invalid Hence instant appeals - Whether Order of HC was justified - Held, respondent did not follow procedure laid down in writ of quowarranto and failed to mention by what statutory rules and ordinances appointment of appellant no.2 was contravened - HC failed to consider fact that Board of experts constituted for selection, considered degree of appellant no. 2 as equivalent to high second class master's degree of Indian University - Appellant no. 2 published several works and his service record proved that he had more than prescribed 5 years of teaching experience - HC erred to note that appellant no.2 satisfied qualification as his degree was equivalent to high second class master's degree of an Indisan University - Appellant no. 2 satisfied all qualifications prescribed by appellant no.1/University - Appointment of appellant no.2 by appellant no.1/university was justified - Appellant was more qualified and experience than respondent - Findings of HC were against merit - Judgment of HC was set aside - Appeals allowed.
