State Of Rajasthan & Anr. v M/S. Mahaveer Oil Industries & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Mohapatra, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 373; (1999) 4 SCC 357; AIR 1999 SC 2302; JT 1999 (3) SC 212; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 798; 1999 (115) STC 29

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Rajasthan Sales Tax Act, 1954 s. 4(2) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 s. 8(5) - Incentive Scheme - Benefits - Maintainability - Appellant/State notified Sales Tax Incentive Scheme for Industries, 1987 under which it exempted new industrial units from payment of tax on sale of goods manufactured by them for sale within State of Rajasthan - Operative period of the scheme under that notification was from 5-3-1987 to 31-3-1992, it was subsequently extended to 31-3-1997 - By notifications issued on 7-5-1990 benefit of the Incentive Scheme was withdrawn from oil extracting and manufacturing industries, position was reviewed by notification dt. 26-7-1991 and benefit of exemption from Central Sales Tax was restored to oil extracting and manufacturing industry - Respondents on 2-4-1991 applied for eligibility certificate, which was rejected on 30-11-1991 - Respondents filed writ petition before HC challenging the 2 notifications of 7-5-1990 issued under the 1954 Act, and the 1956 Act - Single Judge granted relief to respondents setting aside notifications of 7-5-1990 and directed appellants to issue eligibility certificate to respondents - Appeal filed by appellant before DB was dismissed by impugned judgment dt. 14-8-1995 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment dt. 14-8-1995 was justified - Held, notification of 7-5-1990 issued under the Act withdrawing benefit of the scheme from oil extraction and manufacturing industries in respect of inter-state sales effected by them was already quashed by SC by its judgment dt. 23-2-1995, State of Rajasthan & Anr. v. Gopal Oil Mills & Anr 1995 Indlaw SC 253 relied on - Since respondents had started commercial production on 17-2-1991 during subsistence of the said scheme, they were entitled to benefit of the said scheme pertaining to exemption from Central Sales Tax from date of starting their commercial production, to this extent judgment of DB should be upheld - Further, even in case where party had acted on promise, if there was any supervening public interest which requires that benefit be withdrawn or the scheme be modified, that supervening public interest would prevail over any promissory estoppels - Thus judgment of HC, in so far as it sets aside notification of 7-5-1990 issued under the 1954 Actwas set aside and notification of 7-5-1990 issued under the 1954 Act was upheld as valid - Respondents, however, would be entitled to retain benefits received by them under the Incentive Scheme framed under Rajasthan Sales Tax Act upto 4-4-1994 - Appeal disposed of.
