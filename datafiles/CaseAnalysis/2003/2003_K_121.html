State of Rajasthan v Kheraj Ram

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 650; (2003) 8 SCC 224; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1979; AIR 2003 SC 3432; 2003 (3) CCR 219; JT 2003 (7) SC 419; 2003(6) SCALE 757; 2003 (5) SLT 69; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Extra-judicial confession, Bride Burning

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860 - ss.302, 307 - CrPC, 1973, ss. 354(3), 360, 361 - CrPC, 1898, s. 562 - Circumstantial evidence - Accused suspected chastity and fidelity of his wife - Death sentence awarded by trial court - Rarest of rare cases - Acquitted by HC on ground of insufficient evidence - Held, circumstance highlighted by prosecution present complete picture which completely rules out role of any other person and unerringly as well as inevitably point finger at accused and in that view of matter trial Court was justified in convicting accused, and consistently HC was in error in reversing conviction - Further held, when entire chain of events and circumstances are comprehended, inevitable conclusion is that accused acted in most cruel and inhuman manner and murder was committed in extremely brutal, grotesque, diabolical, revolting and dastardly manner victims were two innocent children and a helpless woman - Taking note of these factors, death sentence imposed by Trial Court is most appropriate.
