Canara Bank Bangalore v M. S. Jasra and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, L.M. Sharma, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1014; (1992) 2 SCC 484; (1992) SCC (L&S) 590; AIR 1992 SC 1341; 1992 (20) ATC 266; 1992 (1) BankCLR 555; 1992 (1) BC 627; 1992 (64) FLR 908; JT 1992 (2) SC 203; 1992 LabIC 1001; 1992 (1) LLJ 777; 1992 (1) LLN 914; 1992(1) SCALE 616; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 68; 1992 (2) SLJ 160; 1992 (2) SLR 233; 1992 (2) UJ 173

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Keywords: Qualification And Experience

Summary: Service - Banking & Finance - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, s. 45(4) and 45(5) - Whether cl. (i) of sub-s. (5) of s. 45 of Act r/w cl. 10 of amalgamation scheme confers a vested right on transferred employees of Lakshmi Commercial Bank in respect of their terms and conditions of service as a result of amalgamation with Canara Bank or by reason of this, a former employee of Lakshmi Commercial bank has a vested right to retire at age of 60 years instead of 58 years? - Scheme so framed under sub-section (4) may contain provisions for all or any of matters specified in sub-s. (5) so that it enables all or any of specified matters to be provided in scheme; they do not automatically get incorporated in such matter and such a scheme may or may not contain provision for continuance of services of all employees of banking company in transferee bank as is specified in cl. (i), however, if scheme does provide for this matter - Held, if scheme provides for continuance of services of employees in transferee bank, then beyond a period of 3 years from date on which scheme is sanctioned by Central Govt., transferee bank cannot discriminate between such employees and its other employees of corresponding rank or status; only right of such an employee whose service is so continued is, therefore, to claim parity with employees of transferee bank itself of corresponding rank or status subject to equivalent qualifications and experience and no more, thus respondent 1 could not claim to be governed by age of superannuation of 60 years in Lakshmi Commercial Bank; when his services were continued on amalgamation of two banks, he became an employee of Canara Bank and was, therefore, entitled only to right given by proviso (ii) to cl. (i) of sub-s. (5) of s. 45 which entitled him to same terms and conditions of service as employees of corresponding rank or status of Canara Bank and age of superannuation of employees in Canara Bank being 58 years only, respondent 1 could not claim to retire at 60 years - Appeal allowed.
