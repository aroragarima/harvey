U.P. Sales Tax Service Association v Taxation Bar Association, Agra And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 664; (1995) 5 SCC 716; AIR 1996 SC 98; AIR 1995 SCW 3759; 1995 (71) FLR 836; JT 1995 (6) SC 306; JT 1995 (6) SC 306B; 1996 (1) MLJ(SC) 70; 1995(5) SCALE 102; 1995 (3) SCJ 672; 1996 (100) STC 108; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 229; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 607

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Sales Tax

Keywords: Legal Practitioners Act, 1879

Summary: Advocates and Judges - Sales Tax - Constitution, art. 226 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 35 - Sales Tax matter - (A) Scope of Hgh Court's power when authority exercising statutory powers - (B) Conduct of advocate in attending Court with fire arms - Held, HC cannot prohibit it from discharging statutory functions or transferring these functions to another jurisdiction - Such conduct is not befitting to dignity of legal profession and is a distressing feature, deprecated - Petition dismissed.
