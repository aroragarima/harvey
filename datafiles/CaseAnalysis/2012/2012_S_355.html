Selvam and another v State of Tamil Nadu and another

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 362; (2012) 10 SCC 402; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 379; 2013 (1) CGLJ 63; JT 2012 (10) SC 256; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 612; 2012(10) SCALE 279; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 628

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 148,147,307, 326,325,34,304 - Murder - Beyond reasonable doubt - Conviction - Sustainability - Trial Court had convicted accused no.1 u/ss. 148,302 of IPC, accused no.2 u/ss. 148, 307, accused no.3 u/ss. 147, 307, accused no.4 u/ss. 148, 326, accused no.5 u/ss. 148, 326, accused no.6 u/ss. 148,302 and 34 of IPC, accused no.7 u/ss. 147, 302, 34, 325, accused no.8 u/ss. 147, 325, accused no.9 u/ss. 147,302, 34 of the IPC - Prosecution case was that appellants due to disputes with deceased family attacked deceased armed with aruvals and sticks, due to which deceased died - Trial Court convicted accused and on appeal, HC upheld conviction of Trial Court - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether Trial Court was justified in convicting appellants - Held, evidence of PW-1 and PW-2, established beyond reasonable doubt that accused no.1 used aruval to strike at head of deceased and also established beyond reasonable doubt that accused no.6 snatched aruval from accused no.1 and struck on head of deceased - Evidence of PW-1 and PW-2 also established that accused no.7 struck head of deceased by a stick, result of all these acts of accused nos. 1, 6 and 7 was death of deceased - S. 34, IPC, states that when a criminal act was done by several persons in furtherance of common intention of all, each of such persons was liable for that act in same manner as if it were done by him alone - Injuries caused by accused nos.1, 6 and 7 were all on head of deceased, including his parietal and temporal regions, accused nos.1, 6 and 7, thus, had intention of causing bodily injury as was likely to cause death and were liable for punishment for culpable homicide not amounting to murder u/s. 304 Part I, IPC - Appeals partly allowed.
