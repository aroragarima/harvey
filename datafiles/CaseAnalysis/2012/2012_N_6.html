N.V. Subba Rao v State, Through Inspector Of Police, Cbi/Spe, Visakhapatnam, A.P.

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 622; (2013) 2 SCC 162; 2013 CRLJ 953; 2013 (1) JCC 235; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 606; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 701

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 468, 471 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Defraud with bank - Conviction - Justifiability - Discretionary jurisdiction - Inspector of Police, Special CBI registered FIR against accused/ A-1, Branch Manager of bank and accused/ A-2, Proprietor of Enterprises for commission of offence punishable u/s. 120-B r/w s. 420 of IPC and ss.420, 468 and 471 r/w s. 468 of IPC and s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act, alleging that A-1 abused his official position as public servant and entered into criminal conspiracy with A-2 and defrauded Bank by sanctioning temporary over drafts and term loans to various individuals sponsored by A-2 - Special Judge for CBI cases sentenced A-1 and A-2 to undergo RI along with fine - Against said conviction and sentence, A-1 and A-2 filed criminal appeal before HC wherein HC dismissed appeals confirmed conviction passed by trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether orders for conviction passed by HC against A-1 and A-2 for abusing official position as public servant and entered into criminal conspiracy and defrauded Bank was justified - Held, it was seen that A-1 without any enquiry, allowed A-2 to represent higher value which was subsequently discovered by evidence which was much lesser to value as quoted - All details established joint role played by A-1 and A-2 and their connivance - It also established active collusion of A-1 and A-2 in cheating bank and borrowers - Further, it could not be claimed by A-2 that he had no fraudulent and dishonest intention to cheat bank - In view of statement by A-1, he remitted amount of all loanees into account of A-2 and of fact that A-2 had admitted same i.e., he received amount and not making house plots ready to remaining employees for purpose of allocation and execution of sale deed approached Bank for release of loan amounts with connivance of A-1 which showed that both were having intention to cheat bank at every stage - In so far as, charge u/s. 13(1)(d) r/w S. 13(2) of the Act was concerned, materials placed on record substantiated ingredients of that offence - Therefore trial Court and HC rightly recorded their findings and convicted A-1 and A-2 - Hence, it was not fit case where SC should exercise discretionary jurisdiction u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
