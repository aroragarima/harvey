Madhavan and others v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 650; JT 2017 (8) SC 38

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Two Courts, Explain, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 149, 304(2), 334 - Communal riot - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly assaulted deceased with "Thadi" (wooden log) who succumbed to injuries - Trial Court convicted accused for the offences punishable u/ss. 147, 324, 324 r/w s. 149, 355 r/w ss. 149, 506 (ii), 506 (ii) r/w ss. 149, 302 and s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC and sentenced them to undergo RI for 2 years each - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and confirmed conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, finding and conclusion reached by Courts below for convicting appellants for concerned offence was unexceptionable. Infirmity cannot belie the evidence produced by prosecution which was found to be truthful and reliable. The involvement of appellants was spoken by the eye witnesses including injured eye witnesses PWs1 and 2. Finding of guilt recorded against each of the appellants was in conformity with the evidence produced by prosecution. However, HC has not considered the issue of quantum of sentence and mechanically proceeded to affirm the sentence awarded by Trial Court. The incident in question happened all of a sudden without any premeditation after PW1 questioned appellants about their behavior. Both sides suffered injuries during the altercation. Considering the above and the nature of crime, subsequent conduct of appellants, nature of weapon used and no subsequent untoward incident was reported against appellants, SC was inclined to modify the sentence period of accused. The sentence period awarded to appellant nos. 2 and 4 for offences punishable u/ss. 147 and 334 IPC would stand reduced to period already undergone without disturbing the fine amount specified by Courts below. The sentence period awarded to appellant nos. 1, 3 and 5 for offences punishable u/ss. 304 part (2) r/w ss. 149 and 304 part (2) IPC would stand reduced to 5 years each without disturbing the fine amount awarded by Courts below. Appeal partly allowed.
