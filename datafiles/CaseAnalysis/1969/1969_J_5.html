Municipal Corporation Of Delhi v Jagdish Lal Son Of Radhakishan & Anr

Supreme Court of India

27 May 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 139; (1969) 3 SCC 389; AIR 1970 SC 7; 1970 CRLJ 1; 1970 (6) DLT 105; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923

Summary: Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, s. 476(1)(h) - Prevention Of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, s. 20(1) - CPC, 1908, Pre. - Contract Act, 1872, s. 226 - (A) Expression 'other legal proceedings' in s. 476(1)(h) of 1957 Act includes power to institute complaint before Magistrate - Power can be exercised only by the Commissioner - (B) Complaint u/s. 417(3) of CrPC, 1973 - Prosecution u/s. 20 of 1957 Act - Offence committed within the Delhi Municipal Corporation area - Complaint can be filed either by the Municipal Corporation or by a person authorised by it in that behalf by a general or special order - Municipal prosecutor authorised by resolution of Municipal Corporation to file complaint - (A) Act contains no provision which confers the power on any one else - (B) In filing the complaint he acts only in a representative capacity and the municipal corporation is the complainant within the meaning of s. 417(3) of the Code - Qui per alium facit per seipsum facere videtur (he who does an act through another is deemed in law to do it himself) - Hence petition for special leave for filing appeal against acquittal of accused and the appeal petition filed by the Municipal Corporation is properly instituted - Appeal Allowed.
