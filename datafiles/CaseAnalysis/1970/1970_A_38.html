J. S. Bajaj & Ors. v Arjandas Dayaram Vachhani & Ors

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 602; (1970) 1 SCC 382; AIR 1970 SC 1226; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 - Displaced Persons (C and R) Rules, r. 19(3) - Whether parties constituted a joint Hindu family and were entitled to apportionment and father and sons could not be treated as seperate? - Plain reading of r. 19(3) suggest that language is not susceptible of meaning that there should be in existence some member of joint family other than sons, against whom father should be entitled to claim partition, special provision embodied in r. 19 for paying compensation to joint Hindu families is intended to treat a joint Hindu family consisting only of a father and his sons as one unit for purpose of payment of compensation for joint family property left in Pakistan and such a joint family is not intended to be broken up by statutory scheme of Act and Rules - Held, sub-rule (3)(b) of r. 19 was correctly construed by Chief Settlement Commr. - Appeal allowed.
