Topanmal Chhotamal v Messrs Kundomal Gangaram and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 262; AIR 1960 SC 388

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Court, Foreign Judgment, Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Appellant filed suit for recovery of sum of money - Chief court decreed suit in favour of appellant - Chief court become foreign court after partition of India into two dominions so decree become inexecutable in India - Appellant filed suit in court of civil judge, Agra - Civil Judge decreed suit in favour of apellant with pendente lite interest - No appeal filed so decree was executed - Respondent 2 to 6 objected attachment on ground that shop and articles are not assests of firm - Civil judge held that assests sought to be attached not assests of firm except respondent No. 1 - Respondent no. 1 filed appeal to High Court - HC allowed appeal as decree was only against firm and assests and not executable against personal properties - Can executing court go behind decree and give relief to appellant which was expressly denied to him in suit? - Held, it is a well settled principle that a court executing a decree cannot go behind decree, it must take decree as it stands, for decree is binding and conclusive between parties to suit - Decree as construed by this court, has, directed that it should not be executed against personal properties of partners, executing court would be directing execution against said partners - While decree excluded personal liability, executing court would be imposing the same - This cannot obviously be done - Conclusion arrived at by learned Judges of HC is correct - Appeal dismissed.
