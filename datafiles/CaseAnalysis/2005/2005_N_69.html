Sanjay Singh Rawat and others v National Small Industries Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1434; (2005) 12 SCC 146

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Dismissal of appeal - Justification of - HC had dismissed appeal preferred by appellant summarily - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, first appeal filed in HC did raise questions of fact and law which called for a full-dressed hearing. In first appeal all the questions of fact and law decided by Trial Court are open for reconsideration. Disposal of appeal by HC, in the manner in which it has been done, is not satisfactory. Therefore, impugned order of HC dismissing appeal summarily is set aside and appeal is remanded for hearing and decision afresh. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - First appeal is valuable right of appellant and Court shall deal with it satisfactorily.
