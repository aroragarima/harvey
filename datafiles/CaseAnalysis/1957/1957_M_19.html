Mobarik Ali Ahmed v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1957 SC 857; 1957 CRLJ 1346; 1958 SCJ 111; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 203, 212, 216, 236, 415 and 420 - Extradition Act, 1870, s. 3(2) - Fugitive offenders Act, 1881, s. 8 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 179 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 45, 47 and 88 - Cheating - Appellant is Pakistani national and convicted for offence of cheating - Whether conviction of appellant is sustainable? - Appellant contended that during entire period of commission of offence he never stepped in India hence he cannot be tried by Indian Court - Statement of appellant shows that he continued to be in India till July 1950 - By virtue of art. 5 of Constitution of India, 1950 r/w art. 7 thereof, he was citizen of India on date of commencement of Constitution, 1950 and continued to be so at date of offence - Prima facie mere migration to Pakistan is not enough to show that he had lost Indian citizenship - Held, appellant must be held guilty and punished under IPC, 1860 notwithstanding his not being corporeally present in India at time - Appeal dismissed.
