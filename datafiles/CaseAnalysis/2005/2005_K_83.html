Kanwar Pal Singh Gill and another v State (Admn., U.T. Chandigarh) Through Secretary, and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 431; (2005) 6 SCC 161; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1420; AIR 2005 SC 3104; 2005 CRLJ 3443; JT 2005 (6) SC 452; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 772; 2005(5) SCALE 643; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 341, 342, 352, 354, 355, 509 - Conviction and sentence - Judgment of HC is challenged by accused as well as complainant - Held, in view of findings of fact recorded by two courts and affirmed by HC in revision, order of HC cannot be set aside on mere assertion by accused that whole incident was falsely foisted on him with ulterior motives - Appeals disposed of.
