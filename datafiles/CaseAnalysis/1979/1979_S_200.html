Pandit Ishwardas v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 543; (1979) 4 SCC 163; AIR 1979 SC 551; 1979 UJ 231

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 135 and 139 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 299 - Trial Court rejected plea of discharge from suretyship - Appeal in High Court - During pendency of appeal defendant was permitted to raise an additional plea that suit barred by res judicata - HC upheld plea of res judicata - Whether suit is barred by res judicata? - Held, yes - Res judicata is not necessary that all parties to two litigations must be common but all that is necessary that issue should be between same parties or between parties under whom they or any of them claim - Therefore issue in suit and earlier suit were between same parties namely appellant and State of MP - Appeal dismissed.
