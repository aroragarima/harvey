Peerless General Finance And Investmentco. Limited And Anr. v Reserve Bank Of India

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 332; (1992) 2 SCC 343; AIR 1992 SC 1033; 1992 (2) BankCLR 60; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 12; 1992 (1) CompLJ 351; JT 1992 (1) SC 405; 1992(1) SCALE 216; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Reserve Bank of India Act, Public Sector Banks, Reserve Fund, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Non-Banking Financial Companies and Miscellaneous Non-Banking Companies (Advertisement) Rules, 1977

Summary: Banking & Finance - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, ss. 45K, 45K(3), 45J and 45(2) - Residuary Non-Banking Companies (Reserve Bank) Directions of 1987 ; Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Residuary non-banking companies - Directions providing for manner in which deposits received are to be invested and its disclosure in their books of accounts - Validity - Held, directions are fully covered u/s. 45-K(3) of Act - Are reasonable and valid - Order accordingly.
