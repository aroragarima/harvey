Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P. v Dhanrajgirji Raja Narasingirji

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 234; (1974) 3 SCC 520; AIR 1974 SC 1366; 1973 (2) CTR 445; [1973] 91 ITR 544

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Indian Companies Act, s. 86F - Whether any part of the amounts spent by the assessee in connection with the criminal litigations deductible u/s. 10(2)(xv)? - Held, tribunal found that expenditure was a bona fide expenditure and it was incurred for the purpose of business - Basis of apportionment of such expenditure as allowed by the tribunal was challenged - High Court allowed the entire expenditure which was beyond the scope of the questions referred to it - HC order vacated and answered the question referred in affirmative - Order accordingly.
