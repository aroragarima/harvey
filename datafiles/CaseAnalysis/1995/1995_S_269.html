Gulabrao Keshavrao Patil & Ors. v State Of Gujarat & Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1928; (1996) 2 SCC 26; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 147; 1995 (4) CCC 362; 1996 (1) CLT 226; JT 1995 (9) SC 12; 1996 (1) RentLR 14; 1995(7) SCALE 121; 1996 (1) SCJ 98; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 97

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 5-A and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 163, 164, 166 and 167 - Revenue Department after submission of enquiry report by Land Acquisition Officer to State Govt. for decision, communicated its disapproval for acquisition and directed Land Acquisition Officer not to proceed u/s. 6 - Ministry of Urban Development, however, did not agree with view of Revenue Department and matter was referred to Chief Minister for re-examination - High Court, in writ petition, held that decision, as communicated by Minister of Revenue, was not taken u/s. 5-A(2) - Whether appropriate Govt. u/s. 5-A (2) has decided objections raised by claimants for further action u/s. 6 of Act? - Held,no - Before an order or action can bind Govt. it must be drawn in name of Governor as envisaged in art. 166 (1) and (2) r/w Business Rules and must be communicated to affected person - Uphelding decision of HC - Appeal dismissed.
