Yunus Ali (Dead) Through His L.Rs v Khursheed Akram

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 935; (2008) 7 SCC 293; AIR 2008 SC 2607; 2008 (5) ALD(SC) 47; 2008 (72) ALR 653; 2008 (4) AWC 3442; 2008 (5) CTC 188; JT 2008 (11) SC 110; 2008 (5) MahLJ 729; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 997; 2008(8) SCALE 702; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 1069

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rent Receipts, Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent & Eviction) Act, 1950

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Whether High Court u/s. 115 of CPC, 1908 could replace concurrent findings of facts with its own findings? - Held, u/s. 115 of CPC, 1980 the High Court cannot re- appreciate the evidence and cannot set aside the concurrent findings of the Courts below by taking a different view of the evidence - High Court is empowered only to interfere with the findings of fact if the findings are perverse or there has been a non-appreciation or non-consideration of the material evidence on record by the courts below - Simply because another view of the evidence may be taken is no ground by the High Court to interfere in its revisional jurisdiction - Appeal allowed.
