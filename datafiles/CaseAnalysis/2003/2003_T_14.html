P.T. Rajan v T.P.M. Sahir and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Brijesh Kumar, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 817; (2003) 8 SCC 498; AIR 2003 SC 4603; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 613; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 340; 2003 (3) KLT 1081; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 614; 2003(8) SCALE 165; 2003 (1) SCW 5396; 2003 (6) SLT 144; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 84; 2003 (7) Supreme 124

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void, Bye-Election, Council Of States

Summary: [A.] Election - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 324, 326 - Registration of Electors Rules, 1960 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss.100(1)(d), 116 A - Representation of People Act, 1950, s. 23(3) - Whether non-publication of a final electoral roll would render a general election invalid in law - Held, law requires a strict pleading and proof of factors enumerated in s.100 of 1951 Act for obtaining a declaration that election was a void one Such a declaration can only be made upon consideration of materials brought on records election tribunal will have jurisdiction to issue such a direction only in event one or other factors enumerated in sub-cls contained in s 100(1) of 1951 Act are fulfilled - Further held, appellant herein has not been able to show that names of how many persons were included after 3 p m on 23/4/2001 and how they have exercised their right of franchise - He has also failed to show by adducing any evidence that those persons whose names had been omitted would have voted for second respondent least appellant could do was to file an appropriate application before Tribunal praying for inspection of ballot papers to find out void votes which had been accepted so as to enable HC to arrive at a conclusion how casting of such void votes affected outcome of such election - For inspection of ballot papers, election petition must make out a strong prima facie case, only after setting out material facts in election petition and leading evidence to that effect.
