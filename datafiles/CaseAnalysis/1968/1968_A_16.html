Ambalal Purshottam Etc v Ahmedabad Municipal Corporation

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 220; AIR 1968 SC 1223; 1969 (1) ALT(SC) 101; 1968 (70) BomLR 722; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 207

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Govts - Bombay Municipal Boroughs Act, 1925, ss. 52, 63, 114 and 118 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5 A, 6(1)and 6(3) - (A) Whether notification issued by Govt. of Bombay, award made by Special Land Acquisition Officer, and proceedings subsequent to award were invalid as infringing arts. 19 and 31(2) of Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, land needed road widening in a town which is a public purpose - Lands were properly notified for acquisition - No infringement of fundamental rights - (B) What are rights of appellants in structures occupied by them as tenants? - Held, appellants are not owners of lands acquired but are tenants in occupation of structures permitted to be constructed upon lands after date of notification u/s. 4 on condition that owners of lands will not claim compensation for those structures - Thus, appellants have no interest in lands or compensation and they cannot hold up acquisition proceedings by preventing Special Land Acquisition Officer from taking over possession of lands - (C) Whether conditions precedent to exercise of power, to acquire lands have been fulfilled? - Held, for purpose of widening street, municipality had power u/s. 114 to purchase land, and u/s. 52 municipality could request local Govt. to take action for compulsory acquisition of land and for vesting same in municipality - S. 52 merely sets out alternative modes of acquiring property it does not provide that before a Municipal Borough may move Govt. to acquire land under Land Acquisition Act, 1894 Borough should have made attempts to purchase land by private treaty and have failed in that attempt - The scheme of Land Acquisition Act, 1894 is that whenever land is needed for a public purpose or is likely to be needed for a public purpose, Govt. may resort to machinery provided under Act for acquiring land - There is no other bar statutory or otherwise to acquisition of land for purposes of a municipality - Appeals dismissed.
