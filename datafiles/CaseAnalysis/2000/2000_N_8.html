Chinnamma George and Others v N. K. Raju and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 341; (2000) 4 SCC 130; (2000) SCC (Cr) 780; 2000 (1) ACC 577; AIR 2000 SC 1565; 2000 (3) ALD(SC) 121; 2000 (39) ALR 492; 2000 (2) AWC 1596; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 252; JT 2000 (4) SC 207; 2000 (2) KLT 155; 2000(3) SCALE 106B; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1050; 2000 (3) SLT 453; 2000 (3) Supreme 136

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Tortfeasors

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 149, 166 and 173 - Accidental claim - Quantum of compensation - Appellants were widow and minor children of deceased who died in a motor vehicle accident - Appellants were aggrieved by the judgment of DB which reduced the amount of compensation arising out of the accident from Rs.3,78,000/ awarded by the Motor Accident Claims Tribunal (Tribunal) to Rs.2,27,320/ - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the insurer cannot maintain a joint appeal along with the owner or the driver if defence on any ground u/s. 149(2) of the Act is not available to it. In that situation joint appeal will be incompetent. The impugned judgment does not reflect any grievance of the owner or even that of the driver of the offending bus against the award of the Tribunal. The insurer by associating the owner or the driver in the appeal when the owner or the driver is not an aggrieved person cannot be allowed to mock at the law which prohibit the insurer from filing any appeal except on the limited grounds on which it could defend the claims petition. Hence, the appeal by the insurer by joining the owner was not competent, as there was no ground available to the insurer to defend the claim petition. The impugned judgment of the HC set aside. Appeal allowed.
