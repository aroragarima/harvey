Resham Singh v Raghbir Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 663; (1999) 7 SCC 263; AIR 1999 SC 3087; 2000 (40) ALR 636; JT 1999 (6) SC 221; 2000 (1) RCJ 91; 1999(5) SCALE 149; 2000 (2) SCJ 419; 1999 (7) SLT 323; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 668; 1999 (7) Supreme 300

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Defaulter - Sub-letting - Appellant filed a petition for eviction of two respondents - Rent Controller decided the issue regarding defaulter against appellant-landlord but ordered eviction of respondent on the ground of sub-letting - Appeal filed by respondent was dismissed by Appellate Authority - HC by the impugned judgment set aside both the judgments and allowed the revision petition holding that there was no sub-letting and the respondents were not defaulters - Hence instant appeal.

Held, it is not disputed that both the respondents are brothers and respondent No. 1 who was the tenant was involved in some criminal proceedings and he was absconding for a considerable period. Being an absconder it does not possible for the tenant respondent No.1 be physically present in the premises in question. It is natural to allow his brother to look after the shop and this fact would not amount to sub-letting. It is settled position of law that burden of making a case of sub-letting is on the landlord/landlady. In the instant case there is no evidence regarding parting of possession of the suit premises by respondent No. 1 in favour of his brother respondent No.2 and that said was in an exclusive possession of the suit premises. There is also no evidence of relationship of lessee and lessor between the two brothers. Appeal dismissed.
