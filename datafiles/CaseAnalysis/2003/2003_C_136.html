M/s. Naturalle Health Products Private Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 974; (2004) 9 SCC 136; AIR 2004 SC 669; 2003 (90) ECC 505; 2003 (111) ECR 513; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 257; JT 2003 (8) SC 614; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 232; 2003(9) SCALE 542; 2003 (7) SLT 117; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 433; 2004 (2) Supreme 630

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Health & Drug; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Classification - Cough drops/Throat drops/Balms - Whether classifiable as ayurvedic medicines - CH 3003.30 or 3003.10 - Ad valorem duty - Held, it is clear that a patent Ayurvedic medicament could be one where all the ingredients find mention in the authoritative text books on Ayurveda, though the formula for preparation of the medicament is not in accordance with the formula given in those text books - Further held, all the products ought to be classified as Ayurvedic medicaments under sub-heading 3003.30, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985.
