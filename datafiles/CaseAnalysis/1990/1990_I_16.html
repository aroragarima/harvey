Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others v Indian Oil Corporation Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 592; (1991) Supp2 SCC 18; AIR 1991 SC 686; 1992 (3) Bom.C.R. 176; 1991 (91) CTR 135; 1991 (44) DLT 57; JT 1990 (4) SC 533; 1991 (2) RCJ 665; 1991 (1) RCR(Rent) 62; 1990(2) SCALE 1140; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 365; 1991 (1) UJ 287

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 3(r), 3(ss) and 139 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Exigibility from property tax - As per interpretation of definitions of 'Land' and 'building', petroleum storage tanks are 'structures' or 'things attached to land' within definition of s. 3(s) and s. 3(r) - Held, are exigible to property tax - Appeal allowed.
