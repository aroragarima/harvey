Sachchey Lal Tiwari v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 837; (2004) 11 SCC 410; AIR 2004 SC 5039; 2004 CRLJ 4660; JT 2004 (8) SC 534; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 697; 2004(8) SCALE 539; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 300 exception 4, 302 r/w 34 - HC altered sentence of one accused to life sentence from death sentence and acquitted other accused - Appeal against conviction by accused and appeal against acquittal by State - Held, for application of Exception 4, it is not sufficient to show that there was a sudden quarrel and there was no premeditation - It must further be shown that the offender has not taken undue advantage or acted in cruel or unusual manner - Exception 4 to s. 300 has no application to facts of case - If two views are possible on the evidence adduced in a case, the view which is favorable to accused should be adopted - No infirmity in HC's judgment - Appeals Dismissed.
