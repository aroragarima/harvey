Lt. Governor of Delhi and Others v Dharampal and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 217; (1990) 4 SCC 13; (1990) SCC (L&S) 568; AIR 1990 SC 2059; 1990 (14) ATC 831; 1990 (2) CLR 144; 1993 (66) FLR 972; JT 1990 (2) SC 432; 1990 LabIC 1660; 1991 (1) LLJ 605; 1990 (2) LLN 302; 1990 SCALE 138; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 93; 1991 (3) SLR 1; 1990 (2) UJ 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination - On the ground of participation in an agitation - High Court quashed order of termination - LPA of Respondent dismissed - Thereafter respondents filed a Writ Petition in HC - WP allowed and subsequently transferred to Central Administrative Tribunal, Delhi - CAT gave orders of re-instatement of employees and granted petitioners same relief as in C W P No's 270 and 937 - Validity of Order of Tribunal - Held, the Tribunal while rejecting plea of respondents that petitioners should be denied any relief because of delay and laches held that claims of petitioners (respondents in these appeals) was identical to claim of petitioners in CWP No's 270 and 937 of 1978 whose petitions were allowed by HC of Delhi - Upheld CAT order on similar grounds - Appeal dismissed.
