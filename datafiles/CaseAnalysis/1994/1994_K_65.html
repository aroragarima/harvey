Bhura v Kashi Ram

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1726; (1994) 2 SCC 111; AIR 1994 SC 1202; 1995 (1) CCC 307; 1994 (1) HLR 324; JT 1994 (1) SC 11; 1994(1) SCALE 17; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 16; 1994 (1) UJ 503

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Succession Act, 1925, s. 74 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Interpretation of will - Whether it grant of limited or absolute estate through will? - Testator while bequeating 'Sir' land in favour of daughter intended property to be retained in his family - Held, testator's intention is only creating a life interest which could not be enlarged into an absolute estate under 1956 Act - Appeal dismissed.
