Arun Vyas and Another v Anita Vyas

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 553; (1999) SCC (Cr) 629; (1999) 4 SCC 690; AIR 1999 SC 2071; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 233; 1999 CRLJ 3479; JT 1999 (4) SC 421; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Criminal - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 6 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 190(1), 153(3), 239, 240 and 468 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 498-A - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Appellant 1 married respondent in accordance with Hindu rites - Complaint filed alleging demand of dowry against in-laws - Charges framed - Submitted on behalf of appellants that complaint was barred by limitation and that referring case for investigation to police itself was bad, therefore, no charges could be framed against accused - Magistrate discharged appellants - On appeal, High Court set aside order - (A) Whether learned Magistrate can discharge an accused after taking cognizance of an offence by him but before trial of case? - S. 239 has to be read along with s. 240, CrPC, 1973 - If Magistrate finds that there is prima facie evidence or material against accused he may frame charge in accordance with s. 240, CrPC, 1973 - In a case where Magistrate takes cognizance of an offence without taking note of s. 468, CrPC, 1973 most appropriate stage at which accused can plead for his discharge is stage of framing charge - Held, Magistrate will be committing no illegality in considering that question and discharging accused at stage of framing charge if facts so justify - (B) Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 468(2)(c), 473, 469, 471 and 472 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 406 and 498-A - Whether learned Magistrate was right in discharging appellants on grounds that complaint was barred by limitation u/s. 468, CrPC, 1973? - Point of commencement of period of limitation in case of a continuing offence is embodied in s. 472 and in case other than a continuing offence is contained in s. 469 - Object is to protect persons from prosecution based on stale grievances and complaints which may turn out to be vexatious - At same time it is necessary to ensure that due to delays on part of investigating and prosecuting agencies and application of rules of limitation, criminal justice system is not rendered toothless and ineffective and perpetrators of crime are not placed in an advantageous position - Grave offences for which punishment prescribed is imprisonment for a term exceeding three years are not brought within ambit of Chap. XXXVI - S. 473 provides that if any court is satisfied on facts and in circumstances of case that delay has been properly explained or that it is necessary so to do in interests of justice, it may take cognizance of an offence after expiry of period of limitation - Essence of offence in s. 498-A is cruelty as defined in explanation appended to that section - It is a continuing offence and on each occasion on which respondent was subjected to cruelty, she would have a new starting point of limitation - Last act of cruelty was committed against respondent, on 13-10-1988 when, she was forced to leave matrimonial home - Having regard to provisions of s. 469 and 472, period of limitation commenced for offences u/ss. 406 and 498-A from 13-10-1988 and ended on 12-10-1991 - Held, as charge-sheet was filed on 22-12-1995, therefore, it was clearly barred by limitation u/s. 468(2)(c), CrPC, 1973 - But regarding offence u/s. 498-A Magistrate did not advert to second limb of second part in s. 473, CrPC, 1973 - Further held, Magistrate shall consider question of limitation taking note of s. 473, CrPC, 1973 - Appeal partly allowed.
