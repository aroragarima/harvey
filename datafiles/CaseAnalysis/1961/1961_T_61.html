Â Mohammad Hussain Gulam Mohammadand Another v State of Bombay and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1962 SC 97; 1961 (2) G.L.R. 21; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 659

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Constitution - Bombay Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1939, ss. 4, 5 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Constitutionality of 1939 Act and the Rules framed thereunder? - Challenge in respect of (i) the provisions in r. 53 of the said Act on the ground that they are ultra vires s. 11, there being no maximum fee prescribed by the State Govt., and (ii) the provisions in rr. 65, 66 and 67 on the ground that they are ultra vires the provisions in s. 5(a) r/w the proviso in s. 4(2) succeeds - Held, market in this case has not been properly established, the market committee cannot enforce any of the provisions of the Act or the rules or the bye-laws framed by it and cannot issue licences till the market is properly established in law for this area u/s. 5AAA and not to levy any fees u/s. 11 till the maximum is prescribed under the rules - Petition partly allowed.
