State, CBI v Sashi Balasubramanian and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1076; (2006) 13 SCC 252; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 337; 2006 (204) E.L.T. 193; [2007] 289 ITR 8; JT 2006 (9) SC 535; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 947; 2006(10) SCALE 541; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 914

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Special Bearer Bonds, Taxation Laws (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1965

Summary: Income Tax - Sections 87(a), 87(k)(xi), 88, Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998 - Interpretation and/or application of - No allegation of resorting to any device or camouflage - Accused had filed false returns of income before the Department which led to concealment of income to evade tax - (1) Whether the Scheme is applicable in relation to a public servant; (2) when does a prosecution start; (3) whether the offences enumerated under s. 95(iii) are excluded from immunity in terms of s. 91 - Held, (1) Ex facie public servants would not come within the purview of the Act - Public servants who can never file a declaration would not come within the purview thereof; (2) the term 'prosecution has been instituted' would not mean when charge-sheet has been filed and cognizance has been taken - Meaning of the word may vary from case to case. In its wider sense, the prosecution means a proceeding by way of indictment or information, and is not necessarily confined to prosecution for an offence; (3) immunity under the Scheme is not an absolute one, the designated authority may impose certain conditions while making an inquiry contained in s. 90 - Clause (iii) of section 95 would be attracted if, inter alia, any prosecution for any offence enumerated thereunder has been instituted on or before the filing of the declaration - Public interest is involved as interpretation of the provisions of the Act was in question - There cannot be any equality in illegality - Impugned order set aside.
