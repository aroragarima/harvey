C.N. Anantharam v M/S Fiat India Ltd. & Ors. Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 993; (2011) 1 SCC 460; 2011 (3) AWC 2748; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 26; JT 2011 (1) SC 202; 2011 (2) MPLJ 302; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 246; 2010(12) SCALE 359

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Carriers & Transportation - Deficiency in service - Petitioner filed complaint before District Forum submitting that he purchased a diesel vehicle from respondent no. 2 (an agent of respondent no. 1) and paid a sum of Rs. 7,69,187/- towards the Ex-showroom price of the vehicle, together with a sum of Rs.56,537/- towards lifetime road tax and Rs.28,964/- as insurance - Petitioner further submitted that when said vehicle was taken out for a drive, it was noticed that it had inherent defects - Accordingly, petitioner insisted that the vehicle be replaced with a new vehicle or the amount paid by him as sale price be refunded, together with expenses incurred in trying to rectify the defects in the vehicle - District Forum allowed said complaint and directed the respondent nos. 1 and 2 to refund a sum of sum of Rs.9,15,536/-, as claimed by the petitioner, together with interest at the rate of 12% per annum - On appeal, State Commission modified the order of the District Forum by directing respondent nos. 1 and 2 to replace the petitioner's vehicle with a brand new vehicle or on their failure to do so to refund Rs.7,69,187/-, along with life time tax paid and the monthly instalments which had been paid by the petitioner together with interest @ 12% per annum - National Commission held that there was no worthwhile evidence to indicate that the vehicle had suffered from any serious manufacturing defect and directed that the delivery of the vehicle to the petitioner after having the same properly checked by an independent technical expert who would have to certify that the vehicle was free from any defect when it is delivered - Hence, present petition - Whether order of the National Commission could be upheld? - Held, order passed by the National Commission does not appear to be unreasonable as except for a mere 800 kms the petitioner has not used the vehicle after it was delivered and has, on the other hand, made several complaints in an attempt to prove that there were manufacturing defects in the vehicle - Apart from the complaint relating to noise from the engine and the gear box, there was no other major defect which made the vehicle incapable of operation, particularly when the engine was replaced with a new one - Hence, it is directed that if the independent technical expert is of the opinion that there are inherent manufacturing defects in the vehicle, the petitioner will be entitled to refund of the price of the vehicle and the lifetime tax and EMI along with interest @ 12% per annum as directed by the State Commission - Petition disposed of.
