Bhagwant Singh v Commissioner of Police and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Amarendra Nath Sen, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 48; (1985) SCC (Cr) 267; (1985) 2 SCC 537; 1985 ACC 246; AIR 1985 SC 1285; 1985 AllCriC 246; 1985 (2) ALT(SC) 1; 1986 (12) AWC 26; 1985 (87) BomLR 421; 1985 (1) Crimes 994; 1985 CRLJ 1521; 1985 CrLR(SC) 296; 1985(1) SCALE 1194; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 942; 1985 (9) ShimLC 260; 1985 UJ 820

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 154, 157 and 173 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - (A) Whether Magistrate can drop proceedings if no offence is made out in chargesheet after completion of investigation initiated on basis of FIR, without issuing notice to first informant or injured? - (B) Whether injured person or a relative of deceased, who is not informant, is entitled to notice when report comes up for consideration by Magistrate? - Held, (A) in a case where Magistrate to whom a report is forwarded u/s. 173(2)(i) decides not to take cognizance of offence and to drop proceeding or takes view that there is no sufficient ground for proceeding against some of persons mentioned in FIR, Magistrate must give notice to informant and provide him an opportunity to be heard at time of consideration of report - Further held, (B) injured person or any relative of deceased, though not entitled to notice from Magistrate, has locus to appear before Magistrate at time of consideration of report - Order accordingly.
