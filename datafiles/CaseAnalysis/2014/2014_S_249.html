Dinesh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 307; (2014) 12 SCC 532; 2014 CRLJ 3092; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 506; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 882; 2014(5) SCALE 641; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 321

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Cruelty - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused/petitioner u/ss. 304B, 498A of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by Trial Court and HC was sustainable -

Held, from evidence of other witnesses, it was sufficiently established that there had been persistent demand for dowry from side of accused persons and for non-fulfilment of their demand deceased was being subjected to cruelty and harassment, because of persistent demand for dowry and continuous torture, harassment and cruelty meted out on deceased, she died by consuming pesticide - Considering evidence referred and conduct of accused persons, there could not be any difficulty in holding that deceased died because of cruelty, harassment and demand for dowry - There was proximate connection between cruelty, harassment and death of deceased - There were sufficient materials showing that accused persons started demanding television and gold chain etc. after marriage and that their demand continued and parents were not allowed to meet their daughter unless their demands were fulfilled - Trial Court and HC had come to correct finding that accused was guilty of offence u/s. 304-B of IPC and that presumption contained in s. 113-B of 1872 Act was fully applicable to facts of case - Judgment of conviction passed by Courts below needs no interference - Appeal dismissed.
