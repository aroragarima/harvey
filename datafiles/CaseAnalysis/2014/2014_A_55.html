Sunita Kachwaha and others v Anil Kachwaha

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 732; AIR 2015 SC 554; 2015 CRLJ 659; 2014 (3) JabLJ 404; JT 2014 (12) SC 107; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4382; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 831; 2014(12) SCALE 352

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Entitlement of, Grant of maintenance

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure -Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 125, 397 - Grant of maintenance - Entitlement of - Appellant (wife) filed a petition u/s. 125 of CrPC against respondent (husband) before Family Court and respondent was directed to pay Rs.3,0008/- p.m and Rs.2,500/- p.m to appellant and daughters, respectively - Aggrieved respondent filed revision before HC u/s. 397 of CrPC, which modified the order, disallowing the maintenance to appellant and affirming the award of maintenance to daughters - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal.

Held, HC was not right in going into the intricacies of dispute between the appellant-wife and the respondent and observing that the appellant-wife on her own left the matrimonial house and therefore she was not entitled to maintenance. Such observation by the High Court overlooks the evidence of appellant-wife and the factual findings, as recorded by the Family Court. Merely because the appellant is a qualified post graduate, it would not be sufficient to hold that she is in a position to maintain herself. In any event, merely because the wife was earning something, it would not be a ground to reject her claim for maintenance. Respondent is directed to pay maintenance as ordered by Family Court and also pay the arrears of maintenance payable to appellant. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - In a proceeding u/s. 125 of CrPC, it is not necessary for the court to ascertain as to who was in wrong and the minute details of the matrimonial dispute between the husband and wife need not be gone into.
