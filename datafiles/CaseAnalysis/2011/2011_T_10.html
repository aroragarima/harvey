Takdir Samsuddin Sheikh v State Of Gujarat And Another

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 698; (2011) 10 SCC 158; AIR 2012 SC 37; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 688; 2012 CRLJ 621; 2012 (1) JCC 158; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 840; 2011(12) SCALE 158

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly murdered the deceased - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellants for murder - Appellants' appeal before the HC was dismissed thereafter - Whether the conviction and sentence recorded by the courts below were sustainable - Held, courts below had discussed each and every factual and legal aspect of the case elaborately and it was not required to re-examine every point - Presence of the PW1 complainant along with deceased and appellants was natural as being partners, they had gone to see the land for their business purpose - PW4, brother of the deceased, had admitted that immediately after the incident he received the phone call from the complainant regarding the incident - Said fact made the prosecution case most probable - FIR was lodged promptly, thus, there was no time for any kind of manipulation - PW1 was an eye-witness and had accompanied deceased at the time when the incident had taken place - No ground was found on the basis of which any of the findings recorded by the courts below was improbable or did not require affirmation - No cogent reason to interfere with the impugned judgments and order - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
