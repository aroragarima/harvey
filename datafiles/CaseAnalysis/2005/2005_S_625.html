Gurpal Singh v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 386; (2005) 5 SCC 136; (2005) SCC (L&S) 636; AIR 2005 SC 2755; 2005 (106) FLR 1; JT 2005 (5) SC 389; 2005 (3) KLT 588; 2005 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 297; 2005 (3) LLN 110; 2005(5) SCALE 91; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Abuse Of Process Of Law

Summary: [A.] Service - Punjab Excise Act, 1974, s. 61(1)(a) - Punjab and Haryana Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, s. 42 - Punjab Market Committees (Class III) Rules, 1989 - Appointment as Auction Recorder of Market Committee, Patran, challenged - Scope of entertaining a petition styled as a public interest litigation - Appeal against order of HC in a public interest litigation holding that appointment of appellant is illegal - Whether order of HC sustainable? - Held, when a particular person is object and target of a petition styled as PIL, court has to be careful to see whether attack in guise of public interest is really intended to unleash a private vendetta, personal grouse or some other mala fide object - In service matters public interest litigation cannot be filed - HC was not justified in entertaining the writ petition - Appeal allowed.
