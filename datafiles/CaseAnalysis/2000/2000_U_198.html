Union of India v K.M. Shankarappa

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 812; (2001) 1 SCC 582; AIR 2000 SC 3678; 2001 (127) E.L.T. 8; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 209; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 146; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 521; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Media & Entertainment

Summary: Cinematograph Act, 1952, s. 6(1) - Constitutional validity - Tribunal constituted under Act is a quasi-judicial body consisting of retired High Court judges or other experts - Decision given by it should be taken to be final and binding, so far as executive or Govt. is concerned - Therefore, executive has got no power to review and/or revise that decision as that would amount to subjecting decision of a quasi-judicial body to scrutiny of Executive - Further, it is an Expert Body to consider impact of film on public and once cleared by it, it is no excuse to say that there may be a law and order situation and this cannot be a ground for Executive to review or revise a decision of Tribunal - Held, s. 6(1) under which Central Govt. retains with it power to make such orders as it thought fit and thereby exercise power of review or revision over decisions of Board or Tribunal, is liable to be struck down as unconstitutional.
