Harnek Singh v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 601; (1982) 1 SCC 116; (1982) SCC (Cr) 121; AIR 1982 SC 682; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 262; 1982 CRLJ 420; 1982 CRLR 26; 1981(3) SCALE 1883; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 139

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Exchange Control & FDI - Socio-Economic - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 307, 411 and 414 - Habeas corpus - Release from custody - Whether there is a nexus between the unlawful activities attributed to the detenu and his incarceration? - Held, the charge is so stale in relation to the detention as not to have any real connection with it - Gap between the two being well-nigh a year and a half - No explanation at all has been furnished on behalf of the State as to why action under the Act was not taken at the earliest possible after the alleged commission of the offences which are the foundation of the grounds for detention - Detention takes the character of punitive rather than preventive action and is therefore vitiated - Detenu directed to be released - Appeal allowed.
