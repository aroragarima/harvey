Supreme Court of India

03 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1964 SC 1419; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 654; 1964 (15) STC 468

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax; Water; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Assam Sales Tax Act, 1947, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Assam Sales Tax Act, 1947, ss. 2(3), 2(12), 3, 4, 17, 30, 31, 32 and 17 - Constitution of India , 1950, art. 132 - (A) Whether High Court had erred in rejecting petition of appellant? - High Court cannot be asked to assume role of an Appellate Authority over decision of Commr. on questions of fact or even of law - (B) Whether explanation to s. 2(12) of Assam Sales Tax Act, 1947 is ultra vires power of Assam legislature? - Held, under Sale of Goods Act, 1930, in absence of a contract to contrary a sale is complete when property in goods passes, but by Assam Sales Tax Act Legislature has attempted to locate sites of sale for purpose of levy of sales-tax by fixing upon actual situation of goods within province at date of contract, for purposes of levying tax on sales - Legislature has thereby not overstepped limits of its authority - Appeals dismissed.
