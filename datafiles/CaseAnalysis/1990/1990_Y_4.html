Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited and another v Yashwant Gajanan Joshi and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 249; (1991) Supp2 SCC 592; AIR 1991 SC 933; JT 1991 (1) SC 38; 1990(2) SCALE 1208; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 434; 1991 (1) UJ 259

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Petroleum Pipelines (Acquisition Of Right Of User In Land) Act, 1962

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Petroleum and Minerals Pipelines (Acquisition of Right of User in Land) Act, 1962, s. 2(a) - High Court removed competent authority appointed under Petroleum Act - (A) Whether special leave petition can be filed by Hindustan Petroleum Corp? - (B) Whether petition liable to be dismissed on ground that petition of UOI who appointed competent authority, was dismissed by Supreme Court? - Held, (A) petition can be filed by Corp as it has independent grievance - (B) Petition not liable to be dismissed on that ground - Petition dismissed.
