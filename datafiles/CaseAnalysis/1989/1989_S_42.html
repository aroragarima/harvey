Union of India and Others v Shaik Ali

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 266; (1989) Supp2 SCC 717; (1990) SCC (L&S) 199; AIR 1990 SC 450; 1991 (17) ATC 192; 1989 (59) FLR 752; JT 1989 (4) SC 123; 1990 LabIC 137; 1989 (2) LLN 956; 1989(2) SCALE 845; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 456; 1990 (1) SLJ 135; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Liberalised Pension Rules, 1950

Summary: Service - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Liberalised Pension Rules, 1950 - Indian Railway Establishment Code, Volume II Pension Rules, r. 2046 - Whether Division Railway Manager was competent to pass order of retirement of respondent? - Held, order was passed u/r. 2046(h)(ii) of Code without verifying whether or not incumbent had attained age of fifty-five years - Even if order was intended to be u/r. 2(2) of Pensions Rules, this requirement had to be satisfied - If service record of respondent was so bad as is now sought to be made out, he would not have been promoted to post of Asst. Yard Master on 22-08-1984 and later to post of Yard Master on 31-01-1986 - Impugned order of premature retirement is punitive in nature and having been passed in flagrant violation of principles of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
