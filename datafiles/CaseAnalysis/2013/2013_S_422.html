Sudam Charan Dash v State of Orissa and others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 731; (2014) 2 SCC 141; 2013 (4) JCC 2807; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 532; 2013 (2) OLR 936; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 337; 2013 (4) RLW 3680; 2013(13) SCALE 269; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 284

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Acquittal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 438, 439 - Murder - Acquittal - Sustainability - Appellant's son/deceased was allegedly murdered by respondent no. 2/accused - Appellant filed writ petition before HC - HC dismissed petition and directed Trial Court to release respondent no. 2 on bail after trial - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was sustainable - Held, when HC rejected application for anticipatory bail, it was sufficient indication that HC thought it fit not to put a fetter on investigating agency's power to arrest respondent no. 2 - However, investigating agency, if it so desired and if it thought that custodial interrogation of respondent no. 2 was necessary, could have arrested him - Therefore, after rejecting prayer for anticipatory bail, HC should not have negated its own order by directing that respondent no. 2 should be released on bail which dilutes order rejecting anticipatory bail - Such order was not legally sound - It overlooks scope and purport of ss. 438 and 439 CrPC - Appeal disposed of.
