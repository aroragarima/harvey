Â R. L. Arora v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 421; AIR 1964 SC 1230; [1964] 34 Comp Cas 487; 1964 (2) CompLJ 309; 1964 (2) MLJ 148; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 148; 1964 (2) SCJ 652; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 784

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 7, 40, 41 and 44B Etc. - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31(2), 14 and 19(1)(f) - Land - Acquisition for public purpose - Validity of - (A) Constitutionality of ss.40(1)(aa), 41, 7 and 44B - (B) Whether decided cases can be re-opened or notification struck down by courts can be revived - (C) Acquisition for construction of works for manufacture of textile machinery parts in light of art.31 of Constitution - (D) Whether nature of agreement u/s. 41 is required to be proved - Held, provisions do not contravene arts. 14, 19(1) and 31(2) - Can re-open decided cases and revive - Acquisition is for public purpose - Need not provide for control or regulation of products of company - Appeal dismissed.
