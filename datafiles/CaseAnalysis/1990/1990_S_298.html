Phagwara Improvement Trust v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 492; (1991) Supp2 SCC 753; JT 1990 (4) SC 184; 1991 (1) PLR 458; 1990(2) SCALE 785; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 227; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 227; 1991 (1) UJ 172

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922, ss. 24, 28, 36a, 42 and 101(1)(d) - Acquisition - Non publication of notification for raising objections agaist such acquisition - Sustainability - Appellant/Trust prepared a development scheme u/s. 24 r/w. s. 28 of Act in relation to an area of about 60 acres of land - Lands of the respondents fell within the said area - On 9-4-1976 a notice u/s. 36 of the Act was published in daily Tribune inviting objections within 5-5-1976 - Notice was published in the three consecutive weeks of the said newspaper dt. 9-4-1976, 16-4-1976 and 23-4-1976 - Very notice of the said scheme was also published under Section 36a of the said Act in the Punjab Government Gazette on three consecutive weeks i.e. 7-5-1976, 14-5-1976 and 21-5-1976 inviting objections till 5-5-1976 against the scheme framed - In accordance with the provisions of s. 38 of the said Act the Trust also served notice on every person who was occupier or owner of any immovable property falling within the area proposed to be acquired in executing the scheme within 30 days from the date of publication of the notice u/s. 36 of Act in order to enable the owners and occupiers of such premises to file objections to such acquisition and to state their reasons in writing within a period of 60 days of service of the notice - After completion of the acquisition formalities, notification u/s. 42 of the said Act was published on 26-3-1979 - Respondent No.2 and others assailed the appellant's scheme notified under the Act on the ground that they could not file objections against the scheme in terms of s. 36 of the Act till 5-5-1976 as the notification was published in the Punjab Government Gazette on 7-5-1976, 14-5-1976 and 21-5-1976 - These writ petitions were allowed by order dt. 25-2-1982 and the sanctioned scheme notified u/s. 42 of the Act was quashed - It was also mentioned in the said order that the appellant might, however, publish the scheme against either amended or unamended u/s. 36 of the said Act and proceeded further in the matter in accordance with law - It was against this order appeals were filed - DB of the HC affirmed the judgment and order of Single Judge and held that the provisions contained in s. 36 of the Act were mandatory and as it had not been complied with in instant cases, the illegality of non-compliance of the mandatory provisions contained in s. 36 of Act would not stand cured u/s. 101(1)(d) of the Act - Hence the letters patent appeals were dismissed - Whether plea of respondents that Gazette Notification was not duly published was sustainable - Held, insofar as the publication of the scheme in the newspaper Tribune in three consecutive weeks in April 1976 inviting objections thereto till 5-4-1976 was quite in accordance with the provisions of the said section - Gazette Notification published in three consecutive weeks was however, made after expiry or the period of filing objections against this scheme - It was the bone of contention on behalf of respondent No.2 and others that it resulted in violation of the provisions of s. 36 of the Act as their right to file objections against the scheme was set at naught - Contention in our considered opinion was totally devoid of merit inasmuch as admittedly individual notices u/s. 38 of the said Act were duly served on all owners and occupiers of the land falling within the said scheme and purported to be acquired and respondent No.2 and others admittedly filed objections against the proposed acquisition of their land - Said objections were duly considered after hearing respondent No.2 and others and notice was issued sanctioning the scheme by the State Govt. - In these circumstances, it would not lie in the mouth of respondent No.2 and others to challenge the scheme on the mere plea that the Gazette Notification was not duly published - Appeal allowed.
