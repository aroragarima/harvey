Secretary, Jaipur Development Authority, Jaipur v Daulat Mal Jain and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2456; (1997) 1 SCC 35; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 550; 1996 (4) CCC 65; JT 1996 (8) SC 387; 1996(7) SCALE 135; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 584; 1996 (7) Supreme 594

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Non allotment of land - Notification u/s. 4 of 1953 Act was issued to acquire land for urban development - Apart from compensation plots were also granted to land owners or nominees in the scheme - Further, the Ministers directed to deliver the possession of land at the rate of Rs. 50 per square yard and the lands were sub divided to escalate the net by reducing the area of allotment - Allotment was questioned in HC - HC stated that illegality was committed by the Land Acquisition Officers and the Minister since, such allotments has deflected the scheme and defeated the public purpose by abusing public office on inquiry it was castigated that there was blatant abuse of the power and action was accordingly initiated - Whether the HC was right to direct allotment of the lands to the respondents since allotment made to others had become final and denial to the remaining respondents would amount to violation of equality u/art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, art. 14 of the Constitution had no application or justification to legitimize an illegal and illegitimate action and that a citizen had legal and valid right enforceable at law and persons who had similar fight and persons similarly circumstanced, could not be denied of the benefit thereof - HC was clearly in error to direct the appellants to allot the land to the respondents - Court had the duty to correct ultra vires or illegal exercise of power or misuse of the same and failure of that gave wrong signals which legitimized wrong actions - If allotments would be cancelled it would be virtually impossible for the respondents to acquire residential plots any where in the city due the great increase in prices of land - Hence, it was ordered to allot plot measuring 250 square yards to each of the two respondents in some other scheme which would be at the rate which was prevailed when the allotments were first made to respondents - Appeal allowed.
