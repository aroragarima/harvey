Benilal (Dead) By LRs. v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 686; (1995) Supp1 SCC 235; 1996 (2) RCJ 455; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 691

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Central Province and Berar Letting of House and Rent Control Order, 1949 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appellant's tenancy was permitted to be terminated u/cls. 13(2) and 13(3) (ii) of Order - Whether cl. 13(3)(ii) was valid? - Held, cl. 13(3) (ii) of Order provides that a landlord who seeks to obtain permission u/sub-cl. (1) shall apply in writing to Controller in this behalf that tenant is habitually in arrears with rent - It has been alleged that words 'habitually

in arrears' being vague and indefinite, and exercise of power having been entrusted to an officer not judicially trained to construe provision of Act, it would lead to unbridled exercise of power without guidelines, offending art. 14 of Constitution - As to when an inference that a tenant is habitually in arrears disentitling him to protection of order could be drawn is a question of fact in each case - On that ground or circumstance itself, provision of Act cannot be declared to be ultra vires - Further, it being legislative policy, conferment of power on tribunal clothed with quasi-judicial power to decide disputes placed before them, on application of judicial mind and objective consideration of facts and circumstances cannot be faulted - Appeal dismissed.
