Â East India Tobacco Company v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 115; AIR 1962 SC 1733; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 404; 1962 (13) STC 529

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 286(1)(b) - Exemption from tax - Appellants are firms doing business in export of tobacco - Prior to 01-10-1953 area wherein appellants carried on business formed part of State of Madras and on that date State of Andhra was constituted, and area in question fell within that State - Govt. of Madras exempts sales of unmanufactured tobacco from sales tax - After Andhra State came into existence, Legislature of that State enacted Act XIV of 1955 which states that raw tobacco (except country variety thereof) whether cured or uncured, shall be liable to tax - (A) Is impugned Act repugnant to art. 14 for reason that it singles out Virginia tobacco for taxation? - Held, Virginia tobacco has features which distinguish it from country tobacco, and can be treated as a class in itself - It will therefore be within power of State to impose a tax on sales of Virginia tobacco while exempting country tobacco - (B) Is impugned legislation in contravention of art. 286(1)(b) as imposing a tax on sales in course of export? - Further held, sale under which export is made that is protected by art. 286(1)(b), and that a purchase which precedes such a sale does not fall within its purview though it is made for purpose of, or with a view to, export - Impugned legislation must accordingly be held not to contravene art. 286(1)(b) - Appeals dismissed.
