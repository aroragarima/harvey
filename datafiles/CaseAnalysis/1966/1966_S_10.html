State of Gujarat v Sakarwala Brothers

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 43; 1967 (19) STC 24

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Assam Sales Tax Act, 1947 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Whether 'patasa', 'harda' and 'alchidana' are forms of sugar within meaning of definition of sugar? - Held, words 'any form of sugar' are intended to cover sugar in any form, by whatever name called - Qualifying words are that it must contain more than 90 per cent sucrose - Word 'form' would connote a visible aspect in which thing exists or manifests itself - Sugar may manifest itself in form of 'patasa' as a result of certain simple processes being carried out - It may similarly manifest itself in form of 'alchidana' or small lumps of sugar or as 'harda' - If sugar so manifests itself, it cannot be said that sugar has ceased to be sugar merely because it takes a particular shape or form which appeals to certain class of people on festive occasions - All these articles could be put to same use to which sugar-candy or 'bura' sugar could be put - Term 'sugar' as used in entry 47 is wide enough to cover 'patasa','harda' and 'alchidana' - Appeal dismissed.
