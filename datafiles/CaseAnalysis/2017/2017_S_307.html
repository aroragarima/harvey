Umarmia Alias Mamumia v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

L. Nageswara Rao, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 67; (2017) 2 SCC 731; AIR 2017 SC 721; JT 2017 (2) SC 49; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1013; 2017(2) SCALE 131

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Customs Act, 1962, Arms Act, 1959, Explosive Substances Act, 1908, Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Bail - Rejection - Challenged - Designated Court rejected bail application filed by Appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, designated court erred in rejecting bail application filed by appellant was proper. Held, court has consistently recognized right of accused for speedy trial. Delay in criminal trial has been held to be in violation of right. Though appellant is involved in serious offences and has absconded for period of 10 years before he was arrested in 2004, no reason to confine him to jail as he has already suffered more than 12 years in custody and trial may not be completed in near future. Appellant will furnish bail bond. Appeal allowed.
