Krishna Bhagya Jala Nigam Ltd v G. Harischandra Reddy And Anr

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 18; (2007) 2 SCC 720; AIR 2007 SC 817; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 1; 2007(2) ALL MR 423; 2007 (2) AWC 1285; 2007 (1) CompLJ 209; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 87; JT 2007 (2) SC 447; 2007(2) SCALE 94; 2007 (4) SCJ 948; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 698

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Claims And Counter Claims

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Whether Jala Nigam could be allowed to raise the contention, that Clause 29 of the Contract(Agreement) is not an arbitration clause and due to want of jurisdiction of the arbitral tribunal to adjudicate upon the claims made by the contractor (respondent no.1), Award dated 25.6.2000 published on 14.11.2000 was a nullity - Held, both the parties accepted that there was an arbitration agreement, they proceeded on that basis and, therefore, Jala Nigam cannot be now allowed to contend that Clause 29 of the Contract did not constitute an arbitration agreement - Interest awarded by the Arbitrator at 18% for the pre-arbitration period, for the pendente lite period and future interest be reduced to 9% - Appeal partly allowed.
