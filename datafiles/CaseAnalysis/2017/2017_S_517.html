Senior Manager (P & D), RIICO Limited v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 842

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Final Report, Subordinate, Subordinate Court, Protest, Additional Sessions Judge, Signatures, Cognizance, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 195(1)(b)(ii), 482 - Forgery - Cognizance of offence - Sustainability - Appellant filed FIR u/s. 420 IPC alleging that letter was forged by respondent no. 2 and got it dispatched from the office by a Class IV employee - Trial Court relying on s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC held that letter having been filed in civil suit, cognizance cannot be taken - Appellant filed revision before Revisional Court challenging the same - Revisional Court held that when the document before producing in Court was prepared in a forged manner provision of s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC cannot be made applicable - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld order of Revisional Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, there was no case that forgery was committed after the letter was filed in Court. Provision u/s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC was not attracted. A perusal of Final Report was clear that Inspector after conducting an investigation concluded that in view of s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC. Police cannot investigate the matter. HC also came to the conclusion that no evidence is available on record to suggest that letter was prepared by respondent no. 2. Though, Final Report was submitted on the ground relying on s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC but before submitting the report investigation was conducted by Inspector and materials collected during the investigation were all referred in the Final Report. Holding that s. 195(1)(b)(ii) CrPC. was not attracted in instant case, Revisional Court directed Court below to pass an order in accordance with law on the basis of evidence available. Trial Court looked into the material on record and came to conclusion that there are no sufficient material for taking cognizance against accused. SC found no infirmity in impugned judgment. Appeal dismissed.
