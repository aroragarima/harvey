Gohil Vishvaraj Hanubhai and others v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Abhay Manohar Sapre

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 350; 2017(4) ALL MR 489; JT 2017 (6) SC 1; 2017(5) SCALE 433

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Review, Violation Of, Age, Selection, Procedure, Examination, Judicial Review, Posts, Recruitment, Appeal, Administrative Action, Administrative Law, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Administrative

Summary: (A) Service - Recruitment process - Violation of - Appellants/candidates appeared in the examination conducted by respondents for recruitment to the post of Revenue Talati however were not appointed - COMMITTEE probed into the matter in view of receipt of a large number of complaints - Govt. issued orders by a Resolution dt. 03-07-2015 (GR-II) cancelling recruitment process - Aggrieved, appellants filed writ petition seeking a declaration that GR was illegal and arbitrary - HC dismissed appeals holding that decision of committee was not unreasonable since there various allegations that have cast a shadow over the sanctity of recruitment process. Hence instant appeal - What are the principles which govern jurisdiction of Courts which exercise the power of judicial review of administrative action in instant case.

Held, there were allegations of large scale tampering with the examination process. Scrutiny of the answer sheets (OMR) revealed that there were glaring aberrations which provide prima facie proof of the occurrence of a large scale tampering of examination process. Denying power to State from taking appropriate remedial actions in such circumstances on ground that State did not establish the truth of those allegations in accordance with the rules of evidence relevant for the proof of facts in Court of law (either in a criminal or a civil proceeding), would neither be consistent with the demands of larger public interest nor would be conducive to the efficiency of administration. No binding precedent was brought to SC notice which compels to hold otherwise. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Service - Selection process - Elimination - Vitiated - Whether those legal principles were strictly followed by respondents while taking impugned decision.

Held, SC held that there were large scale malpractices at the examination process and State was entitled to take appropriate remedial action. Identifying all the candidates who were guilty of malpractice either by criminal prosecution or even by an administrative enquiry was certainly a time consuming process. If it were to be the requirement of law that such identification of wrong doers was a must and only the identified wrongdoers be eliminated from selection process and until such identification was completed the process cannot be carried on, it would not only result in a great inconvenience to the administration, but also result in a loss of time even to innocent candidates. On the other hand, by virtue of the impugned action, innocent candidates (for that matter all the candidates including the wrong doers) still get an opportunity of participating in the fresh examination process to be conducted by State. The only legal disadvantage if at all was that some of them might have crossed the upper age limit for appearing in fresh recruitment process which aspect of the matter was taken care of by State. Therefore, it cannot be said that impugned action was vitiated by lack of nexus with the object sought to be achieved by State, by herding all the candidates at the examination together. Appeals dismissed.
