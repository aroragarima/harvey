Â Fazal Bhai Dhala v Custodian-General of Evacue Property

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1961 SC 1397; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Evacuee Interest (Separation) Act - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Appellant and his brother Abdulla Dhala were partners in a business of hides and skins - On 10-08-1949, Abdulla Bhai Dhala executed a deed of sale in respect of some immovable properties at Jharsuguda in Orissa, and also certain properties, at Madras, in favour of appellant - A deed of dissolution of partnership was also executed on 12-08-1949 - On 30-12-1949, on receipt of information that Abdulla Dhala had migrated to Pakistan after transferring his properties to appellant, Assistant Custodian issued a notice u/s. 7 (1) of Ordinance XXVII of 1949 to appellant in respect of immovable properties at Jharsuguda including properties covered by sale deed of 10-08-1949, and business in hides and skins under name of Fazalbhoy Dhala and Co. and certain immovable properties standing in name of that firm - Custodian agreed with Assistant Custodian, in respect of these properties, and held that these had been rightly declared as evacuee properties - On revision application, Custodian-General refused to interfere - (A) Whether against Assistant Custodian's order in respect of hides business and sch. A III properties mentioned in notice, Custodian's department had not preferred any appeal, so that Custodian could not interfere with it, in exercise of his appellate jurisdiction? - Held, while it is true that order does not clearly mention that in respect of hides business and sch. A III properties it was being made in exercise of revisional jurisdiction, it is clear that only jurisdiction Custodian could exercise, in absence of any appeal against that portion of Assistant Custodian's order would be his revisional jurisdiction u/s. 26 - When we find that Custodian has made order it is proper and reasonable to hold that he passed it in exercise of only jurisdiction he had viz.,the revisional jurisdiction and fact that this was not clearly stated in order can be no ground for holding that he was not exercising revisional jurisdiction - (B) Whether Custodian-General should have held that Custodian acted without jurisdiction, and at any rate, irregularly in exercise of his jurisdiction as no notice of such intention to examine records in revision, had been issued to appellant? - Further held, u/s. 26 we find that there is no provision for service of any notice - Proviso secures requirements of principles of natural justice when it says that any order prejudicial to any person shall not be passed without giving such person a reasonable opportunity of being heard - No specific provision for service of notice in order that such a reasonable opportunity of being heard be given has however been made by any rule - Requirements of law as embodied in proviso to s. 26 (1) of Act were fully satisfied - Contention that Custodian acted without jurisdiction or irregularly exercised his jurisdiction must therefore, fail - Appeal dismissed.
