State of Haryana and others v Northern Indian Glass Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 729; 2015(6) ALL MR 951; 2015 (113) ALR 682; 2015 (6) AWC 6025; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 298; 2016 (130) RD 424; 2015(10) SCALE 518; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 697

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Investment, Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 - Notice of resumption - Failure to flloow principal of natural justice - Justification - Respondent approached appellant State in request of property in question for setting up industrial undertaking which was approved by State - Notification was issued u/ss. 4 , 6 of Act - Further, compensation was granted to landowners which was subsequently granted - Deed of conveyance was executed in favor of respondent - Further, land owners filed execution proceeding against respondent and appellant State for recovery of enhanced compensation awarded to them - Trial Court proceeded to attach acquired land and directed its sale by way of auction - Further, proposal of private sale of respondent was accepted by Trial Court - Further, HC quashed acquisition proceedings in its entirety and directed that land be returned to original landowners which was reversed by SC - Appellant State initiated resumption proceedings, pursuant to which Committee was constituted - However, HC set aside notice to resume land of respondent and appellant State has been directed to comply with principles of natural justice as perceived by HC and rehear respondent as well as subsequent purchasers, and thereafter to decide issue of resumption of subject land by appellant State - Whether impugned order passed by HC against appellant State is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, there was no permission either in law or as per Deed which authorised respondent to sell land. Further, respondent failed to establish and commence production in sheet-glass factory within time frame provided in Deed of Conveyance; that it used subject acquired land for purposes other than those allowed by Deed; that it sold acquired land, without written permission or any permission worth mentioning, to third parties. Further, respondent failed to make even single representation before Appellant State. SC held that conduct of respondent has not only been utterly unfair but, there is fraud, malpractice and malfeasance. Further, according to its own affidavit filed before HC, respondent has executed 118 Sale Deeds in favour of various third parties, with several sales being in 2004-2005. SC held that respondent cannot predicate that after paying cost of land to Appellant State and enhanced compensation to original landowners, it had become absolute owner of land and consequently it could use land in manner it liked. Further, SC held that in writ petition, challenge was predicated on perceived failure to adhere to audi alterem partem rule and not to correctness of decision to resume possession of land. However, SC held that circumstances of case warrant issuance of Resumption Notice of land by Appellant State and may be liable to make over to appellant State all profit that it has illegally and unjustifiably reaped in its misutilization of lands acquired for it for purpose of setting up industrial unit. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside.

Appeal allowed.

Ratio - It is not justified to set aside notice of resumption on basis of natural justice when other party has played fraud while acquiring land in question.
