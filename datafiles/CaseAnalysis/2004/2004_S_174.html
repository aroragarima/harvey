Chandrakant Uttam Chodankar v Shri Dayanand Rayu Mandrakar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1054; (2005) 2 SCC 188; AIR 2005 SC 547; JT 2005 (11) SC 408; 2004(10) SCALE 429; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bailiff

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Election - Representation Of The People Act, 1951 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - Whether copies of election petitions supplied by appellants and alleged to have been served upon counsel for respondent No 1 were true copies of election petitions? - whether appellants had served copies of election petitions to number of respondents mentioned in petitions in compliance with s 81 (3)? - whether verification of election petitions and document was made by appellant or not? - Held, HC ought not to have rejected election petitions for non-compliance of s.81(3) as defects shown by respondent no 1 cannot be said to be fatal and copies which were alleged to have been served or supplied to respondent no 1 were wholly and substantially same as original - Since true copies duly filed before preliminary hearing of Election Petitions defects even if there be any, were thus removed, election petitions could not be rejected on these grounds - Judgments of HC set aside and matters remitted back to HC for final disposal.
