M. Krishnan v Vijay Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21143; (2001) 8 SCC 645; (2002) SCC (Cr) 19; AIR 2001 SC 3014; 2001 CRLJ 4705; JT 2001 (8) SC 540; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 405; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 45; 2001 (2) UC 724

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code , 1860, ss. 193, 196, 197, 406, 467, 468 and 471 - Code of Criminal Procedure , 1973, s. 482 - Appellant filed complaint against respondent - Respondent filed petition u/s. 482 for quashing proceedings before Magistrate - High Court quashed proceedings on ground that where genuineness of documents relied upon by complainant, was in dispute, no criminal action could be initiated against accused - Whether HC has committed error by quashing proceedings u/s. 482 at initial stage without affording complainant an opportunity to prove his case against accused? - Onus of proving allegations beyond reasonable doubt in a criminal case can be decided merely on basis of probabilities with respect to acts complained of - Held, it cannot be said that complaint filed by appellant did not disclose commission of an offence or there existed any other circumstance which can be made basis for quashing proceedings - Allegations made in complaint required adjudication and complaint could not have been aborted in manner it has been done by HC vide impugned order - Impugned judgment not sustainable and set-aside - Appeal allowed.
