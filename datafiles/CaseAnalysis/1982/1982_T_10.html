L. Robert D’Souza v The Executive Engineer Southern Railway And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 79; (1982) SCC (L&S) 124; (1982) 1 SCC 645; AIR 1982 SC 854; 1982 (44) FLR 250; 1982 LabIC 811; 1982 (1) LLJ 330; 1982 (1) LLN 257; 1982(1) SCALE 466; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 251; 1982 (1) SLJ 319; 1982 (1) SLR 864; 1982 UJ 344

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, ss. 25F, 2(s) - Termination - Retrenchment - Appellant had rendered continuous service for a period of 26 years yet the Railway administration wrongfully denied him the status of a temporary and or regular workman and treated him a daily rated casual labourer - Appellant approached the Labour Court for recovering some of his dues which remained pending for a long time - Appellant and those similarly situated could not be treated as daily rated casual labour and under the relevant rules appellant and his co-workers would at least acquire the status of temporary railway servants and their services could not be terminated in the manner in which the appellant's service was terminated and that they would be entitled to all the retiral benefits -Petitioner came up before Single Judge who dismissed the same - Matter was taken in appeal before the DB - In the appeal it was contended that the termination of service of appellant would constitute retrenchment within the meaning of s. 25F of the Act, and therefore, the order of termination, inter alia, was invalid - DB referred the same to the Full Bench - HC answered the point referred to it against appellant holding that there was no retrenchment as contended for, on behalf of appellant and dismissed the petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that termination of his service for the reasons and in the manner brought about was illegal and invalid, that it was victimisation for trade union activities; that it was unfair labour practice and that it was mala fide - Termination of service constituted 'retrenchment' within the meaning of s. 25F of the Act and as the pre-condition to valid retrenchment having not been satisfied, the termination was illegal and invalid - Whether the termination of service in the circumstances mentioned by Railway administration constitute retrenchment under the Act - Held, S. 25F of the Act provided that no workman employed in any industry who had been in continuous service for not less than one year under an employer should be retrenched by that employer until the conditions set out in Act were satisfied - There was no dispute that appellant would be a workman within the meaning of the expression in s. 2(s) of the Act - Further, it was incontrovertible that he has rendered continuous service for a period over 20 years - Therefore, the first condition of s. 25F that appellant was a workman who has rendered service for not less than 1 year under the administration, an employer carrying on an industry, and that his service was terminated which for the reason hereinbefore given would constitute retrenchment - It was immaterial that he was a daily rated worker, he was either doing manual or technical work and his salary was less than Rs. 500/- and the termination of his service did not fall in any of the excepted categories - Therefore, assuming that he was a daily rated worker, once he has rendered continuous uninterrupted service for a period of one year or more, within the meaning of s. 25F of the Act and his service was terminated for any reason whatsoever and the case does not fall in any of the excepted categories - Notwithstanding the fact that r. 2505 would be attracted, it would have to be read subject to the provisions of the Act - Termination of service in instant case would constitute retrenchment and for not complying with pre-conditions to valid retrenchment, the order of termination would be illegal and invalid - Appellant continued to be in service and he would be entitled to full back wages and costs quantified at Rs. 2,000 - SC set aside order of HC and declared that the termination of service of appellant was illegal and invalid - Appeal allowed.
