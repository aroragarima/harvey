Â A.P. State Road Transport Corporation, Hyderabad and Another v Sri Satyanarayana Transports Private Limited, Guntur and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 456; AIR 1965 SC 1303

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Validity of orders passed by appellant No. 2 u/s. 68D(2) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 approving schemes for nationalisation of bus transport in area of Guntur - Schemes were duly notified under G.O.Ms. Nos. 2230 to 2239 in Andhra Pradesh Gazette - Whether High Court is right in coming to conclusion that Minister who dealt with ten schemes u/s. 68D(2) of Act was incompetent to deal with them because he had a personal bias in matter? - Nos. assigned to schemes are meant only for convenience and they show that whole scheme of nationalisation would be brought into force in phases; that is about all - Therefore, grievance made by parties that change of numbers itself amounted to discrimination u/art. 14 rejected - It is clear that when Minister heard objections to proposed schemes u/s. 68D(2), he was dealing with matter in a quasi-judicial manner and his inquiry had to conform to principles of natural justice - It is an elementary rule of natural justice that a person who tries a cause should be able to deal with matter before him objectively, fairly, and impartially - Held, no good grounds for reversing conclusion of HC - Appeals dismissed.
