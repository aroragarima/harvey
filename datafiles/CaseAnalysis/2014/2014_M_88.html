State of Rajasthan v Mohammad Muslim Tagala

Supreme Court of India

13 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 695; (2014) 10 SCC 658; 2015 CRLJ 252; JT 2014 (11) SC 408; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 428; 2014 (2) OLR 1066; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 755; 2014(12) SCALE 20; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 902

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Commutation

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 376, 307 and 109 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.433 - rape - Acquittal - Sustainability - Respondent committed offence of rape - Respondent was convicted by Trial Court u/ss. 363, 366, 376, 307 r/w. s. 109 IPC - However, HC gave a direction to the concerned authorities to give the appellant benefit of s. 433 of CrPC and disposed of the appeal - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC erred in giving a direction to the State Govt. to commute the sentence of the respondent. It could have only directed the State Govt. to consider the respondent's case for commutation of sentence. In any case, assuming the High Court could have given such a direction, since it was dealing with a conviction u/s. 376 of the IPC, it should have noted the extra- ordinary circumstances, if any, which persuaded it to give such a direction. Unfortunately, HC merely noted the request made by the counsel for the respondent and concession made by the State counsel. If the HC felt that the prosecution case was extremely weak and the respondent deserved to be acquitted, it should have discussed the evidence and acquitted him. But, it could not have adopted such a course. Before closing, we must express our extreme displeasure about the manner in which the Public Prosecutor made a concession in the HC. Firstly, the offence is grave and in such grave offence, the Public Prosecutor ought not to have made a concession that the court should direct the Govt. to commute the sentence. Besides, the Public Prosecutor made a concession without examining the legal position. The Public Prosecutor plays a very important role in a criminal case. It is distressing to note that in such a serious case, the Public Prosecutor should have shown such a casual approach. Since the appeal has become infructuous, we do not want to precipitate the matter further. Appeal disposed of.
