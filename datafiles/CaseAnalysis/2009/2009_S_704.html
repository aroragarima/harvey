Amalendu Pal @ Jhantu v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1525; (2010) 1 SCC 707; AIR 2010 SC 512; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3755; 2010 (1) JCC 133; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 82; JT 2009 (14) SC 195; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 109; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 643; 2009(13) SCALE 663; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 836; 2009 (3) UC 1753; 2010 (1) UC 359

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Distinct Offence, Specific Offence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 306 and 498A - Cruelty - Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant convicted u/ss. 306 and 498A, IPC - Conviction confirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - In cases of alleged abetment of suicide there must be proof of direct or indirect acts of incitement - Held, in the present case, no direct evidence on record to show that appellant had by his acts instigated or provoked the deceased to commit suicide and has not done any act which could be said to have facilitated commission of suicide by deceased - However, fact that cruelty was meted out to deceased by accused sufficiently proved from evidence on record - Conviction u/s. 306, IPC set aside - Conviction of appellant u/s. 498A, IPC upheld - Appeal partly allowed.
