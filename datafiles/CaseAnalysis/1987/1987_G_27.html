Special Land Acquisition Officer, Bombay, And Others. v Godrej And Boyce

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28448; (1988) 1 SCC 50; AIR 1987 SC 2421; 1988 BBCJ 10; JT 1987 (4) SC 218; 1987(2) SCALE 851; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 590; 1988 (1) UJ 195

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 48, 4, 6, 16 and 24 - State Govt. wants to withdraw proceedings for acquisition of lands initiated by it under Land Acquisition Act by exercising its powers u/s. 48 but owner of land is insisting that Govt. should be directed to go ahead with acquisition, take over lands and pay him compensation - Whether Govt. can be directed to acquire land in question? - Held, withdrawal permissible and need not be backed by reasons - Hearing not necessary - Order passed by State Govt. u/s. 48 upheld and release of lands from acquisition sustained - Appeal allowed.
