State of Tamil Nadu Represented By Secretary Housing Deptt., Madras v K. Sabanayagam and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1697; (1998) SCC (L&S) 260; (1998) 1 SCC 318; AIR 1998 SC 344; 1998 (1) CLR 132; 1998 (78) FLR 961; JT 1997 (9) SC 316; 1998 LabIC 421; 1998 (1) LLJ 214; 1998 (1) LLN 383; 1997(7) SCALE 170; 1998 (1) SLR 28; 1998 (1) SLT 137; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 345; 1997 (10) Supreme 324

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Minimum Bonus, Housing, Uttar Pradesh Town Areas Act, 1914

Summary: Labour & Industrial laws - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, ss. 32(v)(c) and 36 - Tamil Nadu State Housing Board Act, 1961, s. 8 - Whether Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 will be applicable to employees of Housing Board during relevant accounting years? - Held, yes - It is obvious that if the Housing Board was statutorily exempted u/s. 32(v)(c) there was no need for the Housing Board to invoke the powers of the State of Tamil Nadu u/s. 36 of the Act for getting exemption from the Act by satisfying the State of Tamil Nadu that it is an establishment which in public interest requires such exemption having regard to its financial position and other relevant circumstances - Simultaneous invocation of ss. 32(v)(c) and 36 of the Act during the course of present proceedings would indicate that the Housing Board tried to blow hot and cold at the same time by taking inconsistent positions - In fact the claim for statutory exemption u/s. 32(v)(c) of the Act cannot stand if the power of exemption of the State Govt. u/s. 36 is invoked by the Housing Board - Similarly s. 36 of the Act would get out of picture once s. 3(v)(c) of the Act was resorted to by the Housing Board - But both the provisions could not be simultaneously resorted to - Under these circumstances, therefore, the High Court was right in not considering the case of the Housing Board for statutory exemption u/s. 32(v)(c) of the Act for the relevant accounting years wherein the Housing Board had tried to obtain and actually got orders of exemption u/s. 36 of the Act - HC correctly held that notifications by State of Tamil Nadu were null and void - When Parliament in its wisdom has enacted such a beneficial piece of social legislation which already guarantees minimum statutory bonus to employees governed by it, if their employers are to be allowed to earn exemption from sweep of such a beneficial legislation which would ipso facto adversely affect entire class of their employees, conditions for exercise of such power of exemption have to be strictly and objectively fulfilled by repository of such a drastic power - A statutory right already accrues to employees under Act - Housing Board is statutorily established for undertaking beneficial activities far people of Tamil Nadu with a view to supplying them housing accommodation at reasonable costs and to save them from exploitation by builders - Appeals dismissed.
