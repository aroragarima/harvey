Subhendu Mishra v Subrat Kumar Mishra and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1675; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1508; AIR 1999 SC 3026; AIR 1999 SCW 2955; 1999 CRLJ 4063

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bail - Cancellation - Bail granted to appellant by Trial Court was cancelled by the HC on a petition filed by the brother of the deceased - Hence instant appeal -

Held, in Dolat Ram v. State of Haryana 1994 Indlaw SC 1812, the principles appear to have been in the case referred above totally lost sight of by the HC while cancelling the bail. The HC overlooked the distinction of the factors while cancelling the bail of the appellant in a rather mechanical manner. The order of the HC, under the circumstances, cannot be sustained. The same is set aside and that of the Trial Court is restored. Appeal allowed.
