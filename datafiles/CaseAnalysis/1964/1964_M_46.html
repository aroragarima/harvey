Â Muncipal Corporation For Greater Bombay v Lala Pancham of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 91; AIR 1965 SC 1008; 1965 (67) BomLR 782; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 542

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 - Bombay Rents Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 354 R and 354 RA - Plaintiffs are some of tenants of lands belonging to Colaba Land Mill Co.Ltd.,Bombay - Plaintiffs' contention is that buildings were in a dilapidated condition for a number of years and that between August 1951 and May 1956 as many as 138 notices were served on Company for effecting repairs to buildings but they took no action whatsoever in this regard - Plaintiffs admit that Corporation, in exercise of powers conferred by s. 354 R of Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 have declared area in which buildings stand as a clearance area - By amendment made by them in pursuance of order of HC they have shifted their ground by saying that landlords have fraudulently and wrongfully induced Corporation to make order - (A) Whether HC was justified in permitting amendment to plaint? - Held, plaintiffs are now making out a case of fraud for which there is not slightest basis in plaint as it originally stood - Mere use of word mala fide in plaint cannot afford any basis for permitting an amendment - It was alive to fact that in such a case a party should not be allowed to adduce fresh evidence at appellate stage and much less so at stage of letters patent appeal - HC ought not to have overlooked mandatory provisions of cl. (b) of sub-r. (1) of r. 27 which provision does not entitle HC to let in fresh evidence at appellate stage where even without such evidence it can pronounce judgment in a case - HC has quite clearly not proceeded under O. XLI, r. 25 because it has not come to conclusion that City Civil Court had omitted to frame or try an issue or to determine question of fact which was essential to right decision of a suit - For, HC has not indicated which issue was not tried by Trial Court - HC was in error in allowing amendment to plaint and in remitting suit to trial court for a virtual retrial - (B) Whether provisions of ss. 354 R and 354 RA do not contemplate an opportunity to be given to tenants before a clearance order is passed and, therefore, provisions are ultra vires? - Further held, a proceeding of nature contemplated by s. 354 R is not, strictly speaking, a proceeding between parties ranged on opposite sides - What is contemplated is exercise of certain powers by Corporation which will affect interests of a variety of persons or a class or classes of persons and cl. (2) of, Sch. GG gives a right to any of them to prefer an appeal if his legal right or interest is affected by any action of Corporation taken in pursuance of its powers - Upon a reasonable construction of s. 354 RA and Sch. GG it must, therefore, be held that they afford opportunities to tenants to object to clearance order - It follows from this that restrictions on tenants' right to hold property enacted by ss. 354 R and 354 RA are not unreasonable and that provisions are valid - Appeal allowed.
