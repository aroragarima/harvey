Kanwar Singh v Delhi Administration

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 78; AIR 1965 SC 871; 1965 CRLJ 1; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 7

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Criminal - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 332 and 333 - Revision petition of appellants dismissed by High Court summarily - Respondent impounded cattle of appellants, who in turn used force to free cattle - Whether appellants had right to private defence? - There is no right of private defence against an act which does not reasonably cause apprehension of death or of previous hurt, if done, or attempted to be done by direction of a public servant acting in good faith under colour of his office - Held, raiding party was acting within authority and at time appellants were not present near cattle and it was presumed abandoned - No right to private defence can be extended to appellants as there was no apprehension of death or grievous hurt - Appeal dismissed.
