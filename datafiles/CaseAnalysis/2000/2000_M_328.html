Lt. Col. PR. Chaudhary (Retd.) etc. v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 306; (2000) 4 SCC 577; AIR 2000 SC 2030; AIR 2000 SCW 2030; 2000 (85) DLT 223; JT 2000 (5) SC 266; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 711; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 474; 2000(4) SCALE 104; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 607; 2000 (4) SLT 52; 2000 (4) Supreme 717

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Land & Property - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, s. 116 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 4 and 6 - Property of appellant comprised of his house constructed on a plot of land - Assessment of rateable value of appellant's property for purpose of property tax u/s. 116 - Whether assessing authority rightly assessed rateable value of property? - For purpose of arriving at rateable value, basic principle is annual rent which owner of premises may reasonably expect to get if premises were let out to a hypothetical tenant - That cannot be in excess of standard rent which would be upper limit - Assessment records of rateable value of premises in locality are certainly available in records of Municipal Corporation of Delhi - Even notice for enhancement of rateable value has to be based on reasons which must exist on record and owner is entitled to be apprised of those reasons - Held, matter remitted back to Assessing Officer of Municipal Corporation of Delhi to arrive at rateable value in accordance with law keeping in view principles laid by this Court in case of Dr Balbir Singh v Municipal Corpn, Delhi, Indlaw(1985) 1 SCC - Appeals allowed.
