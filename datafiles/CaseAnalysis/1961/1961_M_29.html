Sakharam Bapusaheb Narayan Sanas and Another v Manikchand Motichand Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1963 SC 354; 1962 (64) BomLR 403; 1962 (1) SCJ 396; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 59

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939, s. 3 A (1) - Bombay Tenancy & Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, ss. 88(1)(c), 89(2)(b) - (A) What tenant has to do to bring his case u/s 89(2)(b) of Act? -Held, once status of a protected tenant u/s 3 A(1) of 1939 Act has been recognised by public authorities under Act - Proper entries is made in records and landlord does not raise objection - Nothing else needs to be done- (B) Whether s.88 (1) (c) of Act operates retrospectively? - Further held, no - It does not - Provisions of s.88 are entirely prospective - Apply to lands of description contained in cls. (a) to (d) of s. 88(1) from date on which Act came into operation - Not intended in any sense to be of a confiscatory character - No intention to take away what had already accrued to tenants acquiring status of protected tenants - So proceedings for ejectment upon notice after expiry of term of tenancy of tenant who has acquired status of a protected tenant under s. 3 A by Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939 as amended in 1946, in respect of lands falling within area mentioned in s. 88(2)(c) of 1948 Act, is governed by repealed Act of 1939 - Appeal allowed.
