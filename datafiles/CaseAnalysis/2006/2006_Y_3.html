Y.P. Sarabhai v Union Bank of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 248; (2006) 5 SCC 377; AIR 2006 SC 2316; JT 2006 (11) SC 218; 2006(6) SCALE 264; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 853

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Union Bank of India Officer Employee (Conduct) Regulations, 1976, regn. 13 - Violation of regn. 13 - Charge that appellant's services were transferred to Chennai but he did not carry out his transfer orders to Chennai and was remaining absent without sanction of leave - Dismissal from service - Held, appellant remained absent from his duty for a very long time ie from 3/6/1997 to 23/11/1997 without any reasonable cause and justification inspite of respondent's requests to join duty and inspite of respondent's granting him further time to join duty - Concurrent findings by three different Authorities including HC should not be disturbed by this Court - Directed Bank to pay to appellant by Demand Draft a sum of Rs. 1, 50, 000/- towards full and final settlement - Appeal dismissed.
