Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise v Hongo India Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, K.G. Balakrishnan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 800; (2009) 5 SCC 791; 2009 (4) CTC 390; 2009 (236) E.L.T. 417; [2009] 315 ITR 449; JT 2009 (7) SC 83; 2009(4) SCALE 374; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 1197; [2009] 24 VST 298

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Enactment, Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 35, 35B, 35EE, 35G and 35H - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Condonation of delay - Whether HC has power to condone delay in presentation of reference application under unamended s. 35H(1) of Act beyond prescribed period by applying s. 5 of Limitation Act, 1963? - Held, HC has no power to condone delay in filing 'reference application' filed by Commissioner under unamended s. 35H(1) of Act beyond prescribed period of 180 days and rightly dismissed reference on ground of limitation - HC's order upheld - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
