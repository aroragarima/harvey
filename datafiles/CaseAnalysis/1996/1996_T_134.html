Chief General Manager, Jagannath Area and Others v State of Orissa and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, M.K. Mukherjee, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2459; (1996) 10 SCC 676; 1996 (2) ACC 390; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 458; AIR 1996 SCW 3766; 1996 (4) CCC 53; JT 1996 (8) SC 530; 1996(7) SCALE 123; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 570; 1996 (7) Supreme 571; 1997 (1) UJ 131

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Orissa Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1975 - Mining activity - Imposition of tax - Petitioner was levied tax on dumpers used by him for the purpose of mining - HC dismissed the writ filed by petitioner hence, instant application was filed - Whether, the dumpers belonging to petitioners were taxable under the 1975 Act - Held, the dumpers were heavy and could not move on the roads without damaging roads - On the fact of situation therefore, it was held that dumpers were vehicles adapted or suitable for use on roads and being motor vehicles per se were liable to taxation due to their use on public roads - Relied on Central Coal Fields Ltd. vs. State of Orissa - 1992 Suppl. (3) SCC 133 1992 Indlaw SC 1083 - Proposition of the case relied on was that the dumpers were used solely on the premises of the owner or that they were in closed premises or permission of the authorities was needed to move them from one place to another or that they were not intended to be used or were incapable of being used for general purposes or that they had an unladen and laden capacity depending on their weight and size was of no consequence for dumpers were vehicles used for transport of goods and thus were liable to pay a compensatory tax for the availability of roads for them to run upon commission - If a statute had fixed charge for a convenience or service provided by the State or an agency of the State, and had imposed it upon those who chose to avail themselves of the service or convenience, or who could use the services provided for, the imposition assumed the character of remuneration or consideration charged in respect of an advantage sought or received or advantage which was received - Application dismissed.
