Vannattankandy Ibrayi v Kunhabdulla Hajee

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2620; (2001) 1 SCC 564; AIR 2003 SC 4453; AIR 2000 SCW 4592; 2001 (1) ALD(SC) 77; 2001 (43) ALR 298; JT 2001 (1) SC 96; 2000(8) SCALE 396; 2001 (1) SCJ 332; 2001 (1) SLT 61; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 630; 2000 (8) Supreme 553

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent control) Act, Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Poof Land - (A) Whether tenancy in respect of premises extinguished by destruction of subject - matter of tenancy i.e., premises by natural calamities? - Held, lease being a transfer to enjoy property transferred, with total destruction of property lease cannot be considered as continuing - There cannot be a lease subsisting in regard to a property not in existence - Where tenancy is exclusively for premises and not for land and on destruction of subject - matter tenancy stands extinguished - (B) Whether on destruction of property, civil court has jurisdiction to entertain and try suit for recovery of possession of land brought by landlord? - Further held, tenancy in present case was of a shop room which was let out to tenant - What is protected by State Rent Act is occupation of tenant in superstructure - S. 11 of State Rent Act does not provide for eviction of tenant on ground of destruction of building or superstructure - Thus, when there is no superstructure in existence landlord cannot claim recovery of possession of vacant site under State Rent Act - Only remedy available to him is to file a suit in a civil court for recovery of possession of land - Appeals dismissed.
