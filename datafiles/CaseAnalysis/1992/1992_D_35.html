Commissioner of Income Tax v Dhadi Sahu

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 753; (1994) Supp1 SCC 257; AIR 1993 SCW 3578; 1992 (108) CTR 444; [1993] 199 ITR 610; JT 1992 (6) SC 714; 1992(3) SCALE 255; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 168; [1992] 65 TAXMAN 502

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6(b) - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 256(1), 271(1)(c) and 274(2) - Taxation Laws (Amendment) Act, 1970, s. 49 - Whether, pursuant to amendment brought out in s. 274(2) w.e.f 01-04-1971, Inspecting Asst. Commr., to whom case was referred prior to 01-04-1971, had jurisdiction to impose penalty? - General principle is that a law which brings about a change in forum does not affect pending actions unless an intention to contrary is clearly shown one of modes by which such an intention is shown is by making a provision for change over of proceedings from court or Tribunal where they are pending to court or Tribunal which, under new law, gets jurisdiction to try them - Held, Amending Act did not make any provision that reference validly pending before Inspecting Asst. Commr. shall be returned without passing any final order if amount of income in respect of which particulars have been concealed did not exceed Rs. 25,000, hence, in pending references, Inspecting Asst. Commr. continued to have jurisdiction to impose penalty; previous operation of s. 274(2) as it stood before 01-04-1971, and anything done there under continued to have effect u/s. 6(b) of General Clauses Act, enabling Inspecting Asst. Commr. to pass orders imposing penalty in pending references; if reference was made before 01-04-1971, it would be governed by s. 274(2) as it stood before that date and Inspecting Asst Commr would have jurisdiction to pass order of penalty - Appeals allowed.
