Satish Kumar v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 659; (1990) Supp SCC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Bihar Ayurvedic Medical Science refutations

Summary: Education - Bihar Ayurvedic Medical Science Regulation, regn. 16 - Whether regn. 16 applied to compartmental examination also or not and whether petitioner entitled to be declared successful at BAMS (Bihar Ayurvedic Medical Science) First Examination on strength of regn. 16 ? - Petitioner appeared at First BAMS Examination but could not pass in two subjects - On second attempt, he was successful only in one subject - By reason of regn. 16 he was allowed to appear at Final BAMS Examination which he duly passed - Not included in list of successful candidates on ground that he was not entitled to benefit of regn. 16 - High Court held that regn. 16 is not applicable to compartmental examinations - Appeal - Held, there is no express provision in regulation to indicate that its application is confined to main examination, First of Final - One plain language it should be given a universal application - No reason for excluding regn. 16 from compartmental examinations - If intention of author of Regulations was otherwise it should have been clearly so indicate - Object of regulation is to adopt a liberal policy in favour of a candidate who has passed in all subjects except one, and petitioner clearly comes within that category - Petitioner is entitled to relief claimed - Impugned judgment set aside - Order accordingly.
