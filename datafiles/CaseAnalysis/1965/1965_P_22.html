Â Principal, Patna College v Kalyan Srinivas Raman

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 256; AIR 1966 SC 707; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 974

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Patna University Act 1951

Summary: Constitution - Pracice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Patna University Act, 1951, s. 34(b), regn. 4 - Respondent was a student of B A part-1 of Patna University - He was made eligible to appear for University examination - Later name removed from list stating it to be a clerical mistake - Respondent filed a writ in Patna High Court - (A) Whether issue writ in questions pertaining to regn. framed by Academic Council of a University muct be interefered with by Court? - Held, where question involved is one of interpreting a regn. framed by Academic Council of a University, High Court should ordinarily be reluctant to issue a writ of certiorari where it is plain that, regn. in question is capable of two constructions and it would generally not be expedient for HC to reverse a decision of educational authorities on ground that construction placed by such authorities on relevant regn. appears to HC less reasonable than alternative construction which it is pleased to accept - (B) Whether it was proper in instant case for HC to pass an interim order on that night itself? - Though if justice demands that court should receive a petition even on Sunday, court should and ought to accept petition - Having regard to fact that petitioner postponed filing of petition until Sunday (18-04-1965) night - Held,it would have been better if HC had not passed an interim order on said night as it had done - Petition dismissed.
