O.P. Sharma & Ors v High Court Of Punjab & Haryana

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 323; (2011) 6 SCC 86; AIR 2011 SC 2101; 2011 (4) ALD(SC) 165; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2069; 2011 CRLJ 2952; 2011 (4) CTC 98; JT 2011 (6) SC 275; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1921; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 884; 2011(5) SCALE 518B; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bar Council of India Rules, 1975

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(c), 12 and 15 - Criminal contempt - Conviction - Challenged - Contemnors (appellants), during proceedings in a case in which orders went against their client became enraged and started hurling abuses and derogatory remarks against the Civil Judge hearing the matter and uttered unparliamentary words and also threatened him with dire consequences - HC took up the matter suo motu and initiated contempt proceedings against the contemnors u/s. 2(c) of the Act - Appellants appeared before the Magistrate and expressed their regret and also tendered unconditional apology - HC found all of them guilty of criminal contempt within the meaning of s. 2(c) of the Act and imposed simple imprisonment with a fine - Hence, instant appeals - It was submitted that in view of the fact that the appellants after realizing their mistake, immediately offered unconditional apology by filing affidavits before the HC and also appeared before the Magistrate before whom the incident had occurred, tendered apology and regret for their action, prayed for leniency and setting aside the order of the HC sentencing the appellants to jail - Held, considering the plea made by Senior counsel and President of the Supreme Court Bar Association, in tendering unconditional apology, recorded even at the initial stage before the HC and before the Magistrate before whom the incident had occurred and the affidavits filed expressing unconditional apology and regret with an undertaking that they would maintain good behaviour in future and in view of the language used in 'proviso' and 'explanation' appended to s. 12(1) of the Act, affidavits filed by all the appellants were accepted and appellants were discharge - Appeals disposed of.
