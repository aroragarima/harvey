Institute of Human Resources Development and Others v T. R. Rameshkumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1380; (1995) 4 SCC 211; AIR 1995 SC 1587; JT 1995 (5) SC 181; 1995(3) SCALE 619; 1995 (3) SCJ 8; 1995 (2) SLR 733; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 447; 1995 (2) UPLBEC 1231

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Self-financing institutions - Applicability of Scheme - Two self-financing engineering colleges were established in State and societies that run the same were under control of State - State sought to depart from Scheme framed in Unni Krishnan J.P. and Others v. State of Andhra Pradesh and Others, 1993 INDLAW SC 1056, on ground that said two colleges are not private educational institutional set up for purpose of profit-making - Whether scheme framed by SC in case of Unni Krishnan apply to self-financing engineering colleges set up by IHRDE and LBS Centre, two Societies under control of State Govt.

Held, in terms, the Unni Krishnan scheme provides that it will not be applied to Govt. institutions and also it had not contemplated self-financing institutions set up by or sponsored by Govt. and scheme of Unni Krishnan applies only to purely private educational institutions which are self-financing. Further, material adduced clearly shows that societies which run said two engineering colleges are fully controlled by State and fee which was fixed in said colleges was also fixed by State Govt. and also State passed order that said two self-financing engineering colleges will be treated as Govt. colleges and undertakes to give financial support in future if necessity arises. Further, appellants made good case for being permitted to start two self-financing engineering colleges controlled by State. Hence, State is directed to fix fee of said two engineering colleges every year after taking into account financial needs of colleges and accounts of said two societies and colleges are to be audited in same manner as other State-run institutions. Further, NRI quota will be in future, in accordance with directions of SC as will be given from time to time under Unni Krishnan. Hence, scheme as propounded by appellants with modifications is sanctioned allowing appeals. Appeals disposed of.
