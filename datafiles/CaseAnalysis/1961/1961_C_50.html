Dafedar Niranjan Singh and Another v Custodian, Evacuee Property (Pb.) and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 388; AIR 1961 SC 1425; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, ss. 27 and 58(3) - Patiala Evacuees (Administration of Property) Ordinance IX of Samvat 2004 - Patiala and East Punjab States Union Ordinance XIII of Samvat 2006, s. 10 - Patiala and East Punjab State Union Ordinance XVII of 2006, s. 40 - Central Ordinance XXVII of 1949, ss. 27, 28 and 55 - (A) Whether operation of s. 58 (3) of Act apply only to administrative acts done by custodian under earlier ordinances and not to orders made by him in exercise of his judicial powers - Held, in view of decision in Indira Sohan Lal v Custodian of Evacuee Property, Delhi [1955 (2) SCR 11], said provision applied to judicial orders -(B) Whether order passed by custodian under Ordinance IX of 2004 can be deemed to be an order passed under Act ? - s. 10 (7) of Ordinance XIII of 2006 which provided that orders passed by claims officer under Ordinance IX of 2004 should be deemed to have been passed u/s. 10 (4) of Ordinance XIII of 2006 for purpose of appeal or revision, had introduced a fiction with two limitations-that original order under Ordinance IX of 2004 should have been made by claims officer and that it was only for purpose of appeal or revision, hence order of custodian could not be deemed to be an order under Ordinance XIII of 2006 as he was not claims officer and even if he was claims officer, his order must be deemed to be an order under said ordinance only for limited purpose of appeal or revision - Further held, order of custodian under Ordinance IX of 2004 could not be deemed to have been passed under successive Ordinances and Act -(C) Whether Custodian's order dated 06-1949, by process of fiction, shall be deemed to be an order made in exercise of powers conferred on him by Ordinance XXVII of 1949? - Subject to provision of s. 25 providing appeal to District Judge against custodian's order and s. 27 providing for revision of District Judge's order by Custodian-General, order of custodian was final u/s. 28 - Further held, by operation of provisions of said Ordinance, custodian's order made under Ordinance IX of 2004 but deemed to have been made under Ordinance XXVII of 1949 had become final since no appeal was filed against custodian's order to District Judge, thus order had become final u/s. 28 -(D) Whether s. 58(3) of Act expressly saves previous operation of Ordinance XXVII of 1949 and therefore, orders that had become final under said ordinance could not be revised u/s. 27 ? - Further held, in view of principle stated in case of Colonial Sugar Refining Co. Ltd. v Irving [(1905) A C 69], custodian's order which had become final under Ordinance XXVII of 1949 could not be affected retrospectively u/s. 58(3) of Act so as to deprive custodian's order of finality it had acquired under said ordinance not only said provision does not contain any positive indication giving it such retroactivity, but also in express terms it saves previous operation of that ordinance -(E) Whether u/s. 27 of Act, Custodian-General can revise without any limit of time any order made by any custodian under any previous law? - Further held, words in section 'any time' or 'any Custodian' must necessarily be confined only to orders of any one of custodians defined in Act and to orders of custodians deemed to have been made under Act but had not become final before Act came into force - Appeal allowed.
