Raj Kapoor S/O Prithviraj Kapoor v Laxman S/O Kishanlal Gavai

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 103; (1980) 2 SCC 175; (1980) SCC (Cr) 383; 1980 (17) ACC 8; AIR 1980 SC 605; 1980 CRLJ 436; 1980 CrLR(SC) 96; 1980 (17) DLT 186; [1980] 2 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Board Of Film Censors, Film Censor

Summary: Criminal- Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 79 and 292 - Cinematograph Act, 1952, s. 5 A- Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Allegation that title of film in question was misleadingly foul and beguiled guileless into degeneracy- Accusation of obscenity, indecency and vice are writ large on picture, constituting an offence u/s. 292, IPC, 1860 - Present petitioner, moved High Court u/s. 482 CrPC, 1973 on score that criminal proceeding was an abuse of judicial process and engineered by ulterior considerations - Whether it is justified for trial court in instant case to discharge accused as charge is groundless ? -S. 79, IPC, 1860 makes an offence a non-offence when offending act is actually justified by law or is bona fide believed by mistake of fact to be so justified - Trial court when it hears case may be appropriately apprised of certificate u/s 79 it is right for court to discharge accused as charge is groundless- Held, present case, prosecution is unsustainable because s. 79 of IPC, 1860 is exculpatory when r/w s. 5 A of Cinematograph Act,1952 and certificate issued thereunder - Appeal Allowed.
