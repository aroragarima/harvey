Commissioner of Customs, Mumbai v Messrs Toyo Engineering India Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 452; (2006) 7 SCC 592; 2006 (201) E.L.T. 513; JT 2006 (8) SC 210; 2006(8) SCALE 737; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: [A.] Customs - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Project Imports Regulations, 1986 - Respondent imported various special construction equipments to carry out all works, services, erection and commissioning of project on turn key basis for M/s Toyo Engineering Corporation - Application for grant of benefit under Project Import Scheme allowed - Held, construction equipments imported by respondent were used in initial setting up of plant - What is required under heading 98.01 Tariff Act is that machinery imported should be required 'for initial setting up of a unit, or substantial expansion of an existing unit' - This heading specifically mentions and includes 'auxiliary equipment' - Any equipment which aids or helps in setting up of an industrial plant would fall and be covered under heading 98.01 of Tariff Act - Mere possibility of its being used subsequently for other project would not debar respondent from availing facility of project import - Appeal dismissed.
