Â Mohan Lal Goenka and Another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

18 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 375; AIR 1961 SC 1543; 1961 CRLJ 713; 1961 (3) FLR 326; 1961 (2) LLJ 404; 1962 (1) SCJ 401; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 36

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 134(1)(c) - General Clauses Act, 1897, s.24 - Indian Mines Act, 1923, ss. 18, 29, 30, 30(bb) - Mines Creche Rules, 1946 Conviction - Legality - Mining regulations - Breach of liability - Appellants one of whom was the owner and other was manager of a colliery and were both convicted u/s. 73 of the 1923 Act, for contravention of r.7 of the 1946 Act by way of a complaint - r.3 of the 1946 Act required owner of every mine to construct a creche in accordance with plans prepared in conformity with rules which had been previously approved by competent authority - Further, owner of mine was required to appoint 'a creche in charge', who would be a woman who possessed required qualifications and training which would be approved by competent authority - There was no creche attendant appointed as required by r.3 of the 1946 Act in instant case - On appeal to Sessions Court appeal was dismissed - HC upheld decision of sessions court but sentences were reduced and a certificate under art. 134(1)(c) of the constitution was given - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that rules of 1946 Act were repealed as the 1923 Act under which those rules were framed could not be deemed to be rules made under s. 58(d) of the 1952 Act as requirements were different from those of s. 30(bb) of the 1923 Act - It was further contented that manager of colliery was not liable for contravention of r.7 of the 1964 Act - Held, that regulations framed under s.30 of the 1923 Act survived the repeal - Mines Creche Rules, 1946, covered a part of ground that was covered by rules under s. 58(d) of the 1952 Act - Provisions of s. 58(d) of the 1950 Act and s. 30(bb) of enactment overlapped and those rules would continue to be in force by virtue of s. 24 of the 1897 Act and operate on rules of 1952 Act - Contravention of r. 7 of Mines Creche Rules was in contravention of a rule under s. 58(d) of the 1952 Act - According to s.18 of the 1952 Act owner, agent and manager of every mine would be responsible for observance of rules which formed a part of conditions of employment of a female labour employed in mining operations- Each of them would be deemed to be guilty of contravention of any rule by any person whosoever, unless he had proved that he took all reasonable means to prevent such contravention - Mining industry defined a 'mining operation', as an operation undertaken for purpose of mining minerals and could not be extended to mean 'management of mines' such as employment of labour and providing amenities to employees - Manager of a mine could not be made vicariously liable for omission of owner to carry out his own duty under r. 7(1) of the Mines Creche Rules - s. 18(2) of the 1923 Act would also absolve manager from vicarious liability if he could show that he had taken all reasonable means by publishing and to the best of his power enforcing those provisions to prevent such contravention - There was nothing in the Act or the rules which empowered manager to enforce performance by owner of his duty under r. 7(1) of Mines Creche Rules and manager was therefore was not held liable for breach- Conviction of manager was set aside - Appeal dismissed.
