State of Haryana and Anr v Satyender Singh Rathore

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 651; (2005) 7 SCC 518; (2005) SCC (L&S) 977; 2005 (6) AWC 5158; 2005 (4) ESC 533; 2005 (107) FLR 252; JT 2005 (8) SC 192; 2005(7) SCALE 182; 2005 (5) SLR 716; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 126; 2005 (6) Supreme 161

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 131, 311 - Termination Order - Validity of - Appointment of Respondent as Medical officer on a fixed salary for a period of six months - Letter of appointment clearly stipulated that services of respondent being on contractual basis, could be terminated any time without assigning any reason - Termination of services of respondent - Challenged by filing a Writ on ground that termination order though appeared to be termination simplicitor was relatable to alleged misconduct - HC held that misconduct referred was foundation and not motive for termination - Validity - Non-consideration by HC of question of stigma or effect of any enquiry held before passing of termination order - Since question whether enquiry purportedly held provided motive or foundation was required to be considered by HC in detail and HC failed to do so - Hence HC was not justified in interfering with order of termination - Impugned judgment of HC being unsustainable, set aside - Appeal allowed.
