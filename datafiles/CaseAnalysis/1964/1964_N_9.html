Commissioner of Income Tax, U P v Nainital Bank Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 318; AIR 1965 SC 1227; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 259; [1965] 55 ITR 707; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.10(1) - Income Tax - Banking business - Factors to be considered - Question arises as to permissibility of deductions in computing profits of - Trade loss of a business - Loss of cash from Bank premises as a result of dacoity - Held, trading loss is deductible if it is incurred in carrying out operation of business and is incidental there to - Loss as a result of dacoity is an admissible deduction u/s. 10(1) of Income Tax Act - Appeal dismissed.
