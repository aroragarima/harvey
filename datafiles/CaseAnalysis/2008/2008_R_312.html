M/S. Medicamen Biotech Ltd. & Anr v Rubina Bose, Drug Inspector

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 423; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 20; (2008) 7 SCC 196; AIR 2008 SC 1939; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1768; 2008 (2) CalLT(SC) 76; 2008 DC 163; 2008 (2) JCC 1254; 2008 (3) JCC 1762; JT 2008 (4) SC 45; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 496; 2008(3) SCALE 563; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Practice & Procedure; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Government Analyst, Medical Store, Insecticide Analyst, Government Analyst's Report, Reports Of Government Analyst

Summary: Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Held, appellant had not once but on at least two occasions and within 28 days of the receipt of the show cause notice clarified that it intended to adduce evidence to show that the test report of the Government Analyst was not correct - There is no explanation as to why the complaint itself had been filed about a month before the expiry of the shelf life of the drug and concededly the filing of the complaint had nothing to do with the appearance of the accused in response to the notices which were to be issued by the Court after the complaint had been filed - Appellants have been deprived of a valuable right under ss. 25(3) and 25(4) of the Act which must necessitate the quashing of the proceedings against them - Appeal allowed.
