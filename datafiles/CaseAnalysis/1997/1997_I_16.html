State of Punjab and Others v Inder Singh and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 650; (1997) 8 SCC 372; AIR 1998 SC 7; AIR 1997 SCW 3949; 1998 (78) FLR 272; JT 1997 (8) SC 466; 1998 LabIC 133; 1998 (1) LLN 74; 1997(6) SCALE 442; 1998 (2) SLJ 113; 1997 (5) SLR 789; 1997 (9) Supreme 141

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Police Act, 1861, s. 12 - Punjab Police Rules, 1861, rr. 21 and 25 - (A) Whether repatriation to the parent department as constable of the respondent who was promoted during deputation to the post of sub inspector was valid order? - Held, it is valid - Respondent has not passed qualifying exam for promotion to post of Head Constable in parent department - The post in CID was only on ad hoc basis - Not entitled to be promoted as Head Constable in parent department - Exemption from appearing in qualifying test cannot be granted - The respondent can seek voluntary retirement from rank he holds - (B) Whether he is eligible to be absorbed in CID? - Held, no - There is no rank of Sub-Inspector in CID - Deputationist was given promotion as Sub-Inspector on ad hoc basis - Not entitled to be absorbed in CID as it has no cadre of its own - Appeal dismissed.
