Lahu Kamlakar Patil and another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 571; (2013) 6 SCC 417; 2013 CRLJ 603; JT 2012 (12) SC 607; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 354; 2013 (1) OLR 1018; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 393; 2012(12) SCALE 710; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147, 148, 149 and 452 - Murder - Conviction - Evidence of eye witness - Sustainability - Appellants/accused assaulted deceased with deadly weapons - Charge sheet against appellants and other accused persons was filed u/ss. 302, 147, 148, 149 and 452 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and acquitted other accused persons - Aggrieved on appeal HC affirmed the conviction of appellants - Hence instant appeal - Whether the conviction could be sustained if the version of PW2 was not accepted - Held, SC in Gopal Singh and others v. State of Madhya Pradesh 2010 INDLAW SC 395 and in Alil Mollah and another v. State of W.B, 1996 INDLAW SC 2895, relied on - From the said pronouncements it was vivid that witnesses to certain crimes might run away from the scene and may also leave the place due to fear and if there was any delay in their examination, the testimony should not be discarded - It was clear from the testimony of PW2 that seeing the incident, he was scared and frightened and ran away from the hotel - He was frightened and hid himself behind the pipes throughout the night and left for home the next morning - But his conduct not to inform his wife or any family member and left out of station and not telling anyone there defies normal human behavior - PW2 had also not stated anywhere that he was so scared that even after he reached home, he did not go to the police station which was hardly at any distance from his house - There was nothing in his testimony that he was under any kind of fear or shock when he arrived at his house - SC was not oblivious of the fact that certain witnesses in certain circumstances might be frightened and behave in a different manner and due to that, they may make themselves available to the police belatedly and their examination gets delayed - In instant case, regard being had to the evidence brought on record and, especially, non-mentioning of any kind of explanation for rushing away out of station the said factors made the veracity of his version doubtful - His evidence could not be treated as so trustworthy and unimpeachable to record a conviction against appellants - Trial court as well as the HC had made an endeavour to connect the links and inject theories like fear, behavioural pattern, tallying of injuries inflicted on the deceased with the Post Mortem report and convicted appellants - In the absence of any kind of clinching evidence to connect the appellants with the crime, SC disposed to think that it would not be appropriate to sustain the conviction - Impugned judgment of conviction was set aside - Appeal allowed.
