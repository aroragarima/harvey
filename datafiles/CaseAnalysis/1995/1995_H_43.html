Hindustan Steel Workersconstruction Ltd. v G.S. Atwal & Co. (Engineers)Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2051; (1995) 6 SCC 76; AIR 1996 SC 131; [1996] 85 Comp Cas 270; JT 1995 (7) SC 2

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Corporate - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41 - Contracts - Bank guarantees - Disputes arose between appellant and respondent regarding performance of contract resulting in a reference to arbitration - Court ordered Injunction restraining from enchashing bank guarantees - Hence, present appeal - Against agreement between respondent and bank that guarantee be only for mobilisation advance - Bank issued mobilisation advance-cum-performance guarantee in favour of appellant in a standard form which is unjustified - In case of confirmed bank guarantees/ irrevocable letters of credit Court will not interfere with same unless there is fraud and irretrievable damages are involved in case and fraud has to be an established fraud - Claim of appellant, regarding balance to be recovered on account of security deposit and other outstanding advances, is not less than amount covered by bank guarantees - Held, Judge acted illegally and without jurisdiction in affirming interim order of injunction against appellant restraining it from enforcing bank guarantees till disposal of arbitration proceedings - Appeal allowed.
