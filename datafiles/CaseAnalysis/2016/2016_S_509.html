Sekhar Suman Verma v Superintendent of N. C. B. and another

Supreme Court of India

29 June 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 456; AIR 2016 SC 3193; 2016 CRLJ 4182; 2016 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 190; JT 2016 (6) SC 375; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 747; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 1002; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 809

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Gazetted Officer, Offer, Search, Narcotic Drugs And Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, Heroin, Rigorous Imprisonment, Seizure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 29, 42 and 50 - Possession of Narcotics Drugs - Conviction - Sustainability -Appellant/accused allegedly found in possession of 250 grams of heroin - Accused was charged for offences punishable u/s. 21of the Act - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 21 of the Act and sentenced him RI for 10 years - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, appellant was sufficiently and duly represented throughout in these proceedings and it is not necessary to issue any fresh notice to appellant and give him another opportunity to engage a counsel of his choice. Further, offer to search appellant was given to him in writing and on his giving consent, he was searched. HC was right in upholding the procedure followed by raiding party for ensuring compliance of s. 50 of the Act. HC rightly held against appellant on this issue. Thus, no ground to take a different view than the one taken by HC and uphold finding on this issue against appellant. Impugned judgment of HC was upheld. Appeal dismissed.
