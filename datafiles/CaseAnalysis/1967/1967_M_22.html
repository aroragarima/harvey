Secretary, Madras Gymkhana Club Employees’Union v Management Of The Gymkhana Club

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 480; AIR 1968 SC 554; 1967 (15) FLR 411; 1968 LabIC 547; 1967 (2) LLJ 720; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 6; 1968 (2) SCJ 138; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 742

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Catering, Air Force Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Air Force Act, 1950 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Army Act, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2 (k) and (j) - Whether a Club is 'an industry' as liable to pay bonus? - Although there is no incorporation as such, the club has attained an existence distinct from its members - Members come and go but the club goes on forever - The club belongs to members on its list of members and that is what matters - Those members can deal with the club as they like - Therefore, the club is identified with its members at a given point of time - Thus, it cannot be said that the club has an existence apart from the members - Held, the Madras Gymkhana Club being a member's club is not an industry - Appeal dismissed.
