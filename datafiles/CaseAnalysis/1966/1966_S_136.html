State of Uttar Pradesh v Akbar Ali Khan

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1966 SC 1842; 1968 (16) FLR 29; 1967 (1) LLJ 708; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 821

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Subordinate Revenue Executive Service (Tahsildars) Rules, 1944, r.14 - Constitution of India,1950, art.311 - Termination of probation - Protection - Infringement of - Respondent was appointed as Naib Tahsildar in United Provinces Civil Service and was confirmed in that post - Respondent was selected for permanent promotion to post of Tahsildar and was placed on probation for period of two years - On report from Accountant General that respondent had drawn excess travelling allowance in respect of certain journeys, inquiry was held against respondent - Probation of respondent was ordered to be terminated and that he be reverted to post of Naib Tahsildar and that he should not be considered for promotion for period of seven years from date of reversion - Respondent submitted memorial to Governor who passed order cancelling that part of order which related to stoppage of promotion of respondent - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order made by Governor determining probation of respondent infringed protection of art. 311 of Constitution - Held, as per scheme of rules, confirmation in post which probationer was holding did not result merely from expiry of period of probation unless order of confirmation was made - However, inquiry against respondent was held for purpose of determining his probation - Further u/r.14 of the Rules, Governor was authorized to revert person appointed on probation, if it appeared at any time that person had not made sufficient use of his opportunities or had failed to pass departmental examination completely or had otherwise failed to give satisfaction - Thus, merely because inquiry was directed to ascertain whether person appointed on probation had failed to give satisfaction, that it was not intended to hold inquiry with view to impose punishment against that person - Reversion to substantive appointment could be directed u/r.14 of the Rules in conditions mentioned therein and for ascertaining existence or otherwise of those conditions, appointing authority could hold some inquiry - Thus, it could not be said that by terminating probation, any penalty was imposed - Hence, HC was in error in holding that order made by Governor determining probation of respondent infringed protection of art. 311 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
