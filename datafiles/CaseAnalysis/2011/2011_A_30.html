Agricultural Market Committee A.P. Etc. v M/S M.K. Exports, A.P. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 297; (2011) 13 SCC 290; JT 2011 (5) SC 353; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 783; 2011(5) SCALE 62; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 990

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Trade

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh (Agricultural Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Trade - A.P. Agricultural (Produce and Livestock) Markets Act, 1966, ss. 12-B(5) and 23 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Non-payment of market fee - Respondents were traders and were given licence by the appellants for doing business in prawns, a notified commodity under the Act - Appellants issued notices to the respondents to produce books of accounts for the years 1998-99 and 1999-2000 to enable them to assess the correct amount of market fees - Respondents failed to produce the books of accounts - Thereafter, show cause notices were issued to the respondents to show cause as to why the exemption given earlier on certain turnover for the assessment years 1998-99 and 1999-2000 be not disallowed; the re-assessment for these two years be not done and the market fees be not collected u/s. 12-B(5) of the Act - Writ petitions filed thereagainst was disposed of with a direction to respondents to respond to the show cause notices - Appellants re-determined the turnover for that period and, consequently, re-assessed the market fees and also levied penalty - Revisions filed thereagainst were dismissed - Thereafter, demand notices were issued by the appellants to the respondents to pay the market fees determined u/s. 12-B(5) of the Act - Respondents did not comply with the demand notices - Notices were then issued to the respondents to show cause as to why criminal proceedings be not initiated against them u/s. 23 of the Act - Respondents did not respond to the show cause notices nor made any payment of outstanding market fees - Appellants then filed criminal complaints against the respondents - Respondents questioned the complaints in the petitions u/s. 482 of the CrPC and prayed for quashing the criminal proceedings - HC quashed the criminal proceedings on the ground that the non-payment of market fees re-assessed u/s. 12-B(5) of the Act was not punishable u/s. 23 of the Act - Whether view of the HC was right? - Held, as per the scheme of the Act, it was the assessment of market fee u/s. 12-B(1) or re-assessment u/s. 12-B(5) which resulted in levy of fee u/s. 12(1) of the Act - HC overlooked the explanation appended to s. 12-A of the Act which clearly provides that for the purposes of ss. 12-A to 12-G, 'market fees' should mean fees levied under sub-section (1) of s. 12 of the Act - Question, whether assessment of market fees payable by a trader was made u/s. 12-B(1) or s. 12-B(5), the market fees so assessed means the fees levied u/s. 12(1) - Provisions being clear, non payment of the market fees assessed in the original proceedings u/s. 12-B(1) or in the proceedings for re-assessment u/s. 12-B(5) would mean default in payment of fee levied under sub-section (1) of s. 12 of the Act - S. 23 of the Act provides for penalty to be imposed against a person who contravened the provisions of s. 7 or who failed to pay fees levied under sub-section (1) of s. 12 - HC erred in quashing the criminal proceedings against the respondents - Appeals allowed.
