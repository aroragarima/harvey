Kil Kotagiri Tea and Coffee Estates Company, Limited and Others v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 435; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 803; 1965 (16) STC 467

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Sales Tax Authorities, Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, s. 64(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1) - Outside sale - Sale by public auction - Appellants are plantation companies which grow their own tea in tea estates and sell their products by public auction at Fort Cochin - Purchasers paid the consideration at Fort Cochin and obtained from the auctioneers delivery notes requesting the godown keepers at Wellingdon Island to deliver the goods - Whether the present sales are outside sales so far as the respondent is concerned? - Title to the goods passed to the buyer u/s. 64(2) of the Act as soon as the sale was completed by the auctioneer announcing its completion by the fall of the hammer - Initial auction cannot, be treated as an executory contract which became a conditional contract on the fall of the hammer - Held, High Court was in error in coming to the conclusion that the Sales Tax Authorities were justified in imposing sales tax against the appellants - Appeal allowed.
