Shimnit Utsch India Private Limited and another v West Bengal Transport Infrastructure Development Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 389; (2010) 6 SCC 303; 2010 (2) ACC 745; 2010 (5) AWC 4762; 2010 (2) CLT(SC) 289; JT 2010 (5) SC 237; 2010(5) SCALE 617; [2010] 6 S.C.R. 1110

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Motor Vehicles [New High Security Registration Plates (Amendment)] Order, 2001, Motor Vehicles (New High Security Registration Plates) Order, 2001, Bihar Excise Act

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, r. 50 - Motor Vehicles (New High Security Registration Plates) Order, 2001 - Motor Vehicles [New High Security Registration Plates (Amendment)] Order, 2001 - Tender Notification - Change of eligibility condition - Validity - Central Government laid down guidelines for incorporating necessary conditions in the Notices Inviting Tenders (NIT) to be issued by the various States/Union Territories (UTs) for manufacturing and supply of High Security Registration Plates (HSRP) for motor vehicles - Several States/UTs included the conditions in the NIT, namely, experience in the field of manufacturing the registration plates in 5 foreign countries, a minimum annual turnover of 30 crores in such business and the contract would be for 15 years - Said conditions of the NIT was challenged before the SC - SC in Association of Registration Plates v. Union of India and Ors., 2004 INDLAW SC 1031, upheld the said conditions as valid and dismissed the writ petitions - Appellant/Company's bid was selected after the evaluation, however, respondent/State cancelled the NIT - Respondent issued another NIT wherein clauses pertaining to experience in foreign countries and the minimum prescribed turnover from such business were done away with and period of contract was reduced from 15 years to 10 years - Appellant filed its tender pursuant to the issuance of second NIT - Appellant, in the meantime, filed a writ petition before the HC challenging the conditions of second NIT, principally on the ground that the essential conditions having been approved by the SC in Association of Registration Plates' case could not have been done away with - Single Judge dismissed appellants writ petition - Appellant challenged the Single Judge order before the DB - DB dismissed appeal - Held, due to various litigation, no substantial progress took place for two years in finalization of process for which first NIT issued - In interregnum, considerable number of indigenous manufacturers obtained the requisite Type Approval Certificate (TAC) from the approved institutions as per the provisions of 1988 Act and thereby acquired capacity and ability to manufacture HSRP - Respondent/State, in the backdrop of the said reasons, formed an opinion that by increasing competition, greater public interest could be achieved and, accordingly, decided to cancel first NIT and issued second NIT doing away with conditions like experience in foreign countries and prescribed minimum turnover from that business - Since the circumstances changed in some material respects, departure from the earlier policy cannot be held to be legally flawed, particularly when there is no challenge to the changed policy reflected in second NIT on the ground of Wednesbury reasonableness or principle of legitimate expectation or arbitrariness or irrationality - Government policy can be changed with changing circumstances and only on the ground of change, such policy would not be vitiated - Government has a discretion to adopt a different policy or alter or change its policy calculated to serve public interest and make it more effective - Further, SC had neither laid down as an absolute proposition that manufacturer of HSRP must have the foreign experience and a particular financial capacity to fulfil the contractual obligations nor it was held that those conditions must necessarily be insisted upon in the NIT in Association of Registration Plates' case - No justification in denying the State authorities latitude for departure from the conditions of the NIT that came up for consideration before the SC in larger public interest to broaden the base of competitive bidding due to lapse of time and substantial increase in the number of persons having TAC from the approved institutes without compromising on the quality and specifications of HSRP as set out in Rule 50, Order 2001 and Amendment Order, 2001 - Central Government clarified the position in its communication with the States/UTs that draft tender conditions circulated by them were only suggestive - Therefore, cancellation of clauses pertaining to foreign experience and turn over in the second NIT is valid and hence, no case for judicial review or intervention in the said NIT is made out - Appeal dismissed.
