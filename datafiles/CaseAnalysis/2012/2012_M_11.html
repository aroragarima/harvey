P.A. Mohammed Riyas v M.K. Raghavan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 460; (2012) 5 SCC 511; AIR 2012 SC 2784; 2012 (93) ALR 448; JT 2012 (4) SC 427; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 637; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 929; [2012] 4 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Purity

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951,ss. 100,101,123 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 15 - Corrupt Practices - Election Petition - Complete Cause of Action - Dismissed - Sustainability - Appellant contested for parliamentary elections in 2009 for Lok Sabha and lost and is challenging election of respondent who was returned candidate by way of Election Petition filed u/s. 81, r/w ss. 100,101,123 of Act, on ground that respondent had published false statement with regard to appellant and thereby committed corrupt practice within meaning of s. 123 of Act - Respondent raised objection with regard to maintainability of petition for want of complete cause of action, HC accepted objections and dismissed election petition - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in dismissing election petition for want of complete cause of action - Held, Election Petitioner was required to set forth full particulars of any corrupt practice that alleges and names of parties involved therein and it further provides that same was to be signed by Petitioner and verified in manner laid down in CPC for verification of proceedings - Although allegations as to corrupt practices alleged to have been employed by respondent had been mentioned in body of Petition, Petition itself had not been verified in manner specified in O. 6 r. 15 of CPC, and s. 123(4) of Act which defines 'corrupt practice' and publication of various statements against respondent which were not supported by affidavit, could not, therefore, had been taken into consideration by HC while considering Election Petition, in absence of proper verification, it had to be accepted that Election Petition was incomplete as it did not contain a complete cause of action - Although, s. 83 had not been mentioned in s. 86(1), in absence of proper verification, it must be held that provisions of s. 81 had also not been fulfilled and cause of action for Election Petition remained incomplete, Petitioner had opportunity of curing defect, but it chose not to do so - Appeal dismissed.
