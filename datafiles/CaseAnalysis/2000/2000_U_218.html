Om Kumar and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2713; (2001) 2 SCC 386; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1039; AIR 2000 SC 3689; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 92; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 693

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Affirmative Action

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 311 - Wednesbury principle and principle of proportionality - Delinquent officer in Delhi Development Authority - Misconduct proved - Disciplinary Authority imposing minor penalty of censure - Considering complicated stage at which delinquent was required to handle case and absence of mala fides - Held, not violative of principles.
