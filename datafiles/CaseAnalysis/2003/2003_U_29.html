Balram Kumawat v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 664; (2003) 7 SCC 628; AIR 2003 SC 3268; 2003 (106) DLT 392; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 177; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 1; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 194; 2003(6) SCALE 724; 2003 (5) SLT 279; 2004 (134) STC 626; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 24; 2003 (6) Supreme 264

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Customs Rules, Offences against the Person Act, 1861

Summary: Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 - Whether 'mammoth ivory' imported in India answers the description of the words 'ivory imported in India' contained in Wild Life (Protection) Act, 1972 (hereinafter referred to as 'the said Act') as amended by Act No. 44 of 1991 - Held, the Parliament has enacted the Amending Acts of 1986, 1991 and 2003 not only for the purpose of banning a trade in elephant ivory but with a view to create a blockade of the activities of poachers and others so that a complete prohibition in trade in ivory is achieved. By reason of the Amending Acts, the Parliament was anxious to plug the loopholes and impose a ban on trade in ivory so that while purporting to trade in imported ivory and carvings therefrom, poaching of Indian elephants and resultant illegal trade by extracting their tusks may not continue.
