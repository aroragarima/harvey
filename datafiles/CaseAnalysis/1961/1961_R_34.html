Â Ramdhandas And Another v The State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1961 SC 1559; 1961 (3) FLR 48; 1961 (2) LLJ 102; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 852

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 arts, 19(6), 32 - Punjab Shops and Commercial Establishments Act, 1958 ss. 7, 9 & 10 - Factories Act, 1948 - Imposition of restriction under trade - constitutionality - Petitioners filed instant petition u/art. 32 of Constitution challenging validity of laws passed under the Act of 1958 - Petitioners were into business where practically they have to keep open their shop for whole day & night i.e. for all 24 hours - Petitioners contended that impugned provisions of the Act are against fundamental right u/art. 19(6) and imposed unreasonable restriction on right to carry on trade or business - Restriction imposed under the Act of 1958 was s. 7. Hours of employment: Subject to provisions of this Act, no person shall be employed about business of an establishment for more than forty-eight hours in any one week, and ninehours in any one day, s. 9. Opening and closing hours: No establishment shall save as otherwise provided by this Act, open earlier than ten o'clock in morning or close later than eight o'clock in evening, s. 10. Close day: Save as otherwise provided by this Act, every establishment shall remain close on every Sunday: Provided that, in case of an establishment attached to a factory, employer may substitute close day of such establishment so as to correspond to substituted close day of factory in same manner and subject to same conditions as are laid down in this behalf in the Act of 1948 - whether provision in impugned law, which was enacted to avoid physical overstrain of worker, and so as to afford him better conditions of work, and more regulated hours, thus ensuring to them reasonable amount of leisure-factors which would render restrictions in interest of general public was justified - Held, one of the petitioner before instant Court was a small trader who did not employ any person under him but who, like second petitioner before SC himself with members of his family attended to all requirements of his shop basing himself on this feature they challenged validity of provision which restricted their right to carry on his business in such manner as he chose on all the seven days in week - Hence HC had rested their decision on this part of instant case on reasoning that terms of impugned section might be justified on ground that it was designed under interest of owner of shop or establishment himself and that his health and welfare was matter of interest not only to himself but to general public restriction imposed, with a view to secure this purpose it would be clearly saved by Art. 19(6)... Apart from this constitutionality of impugned provision might be sustained on another ground also, viz., with a view to avoid evasion of provisions specifically designed for protection of workmen employed it might be pointed out that acts innocent in themselves may be prohibited and restrictions in that regard would be reasonable, if same were necessary to secure efficient enforcement of valid provisions inclusion of reasonable margin to ensure effective enforcement will not stamp a law otherwise valid as within legislative competence with character of unconstitutionality as being unreasonable - Thus impugned provisions were justified and valid - Petition dismissed.
