Jit Ram Shiv Kumar And Ors. Etc. v State Of Haryana And Anr. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 314; (1981) 1 SCC 11; AIR 1980 SC 1285; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 689

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Legislative Powers, Import Licence, Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, Levy Of Octroi

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948 - Resolution - Annulment - Ultra vires - Illegal - Respondent/Municipal Committee established Mandi to improve trade - Respondent passed resolution No. 8 that Mandi would remain exempt from payment of octroi - Respondent by its new resolution levied octroi duty , but it was annulled by P Govt. u/s. 236 of 1911 Act - Respondent started charging octroi duty on goods imported into Mandi and it was approved by State of H - Petitioners contended that resolution and order of approval granted by State was illegal and ultra vires - HC rejected petitions - Hence instant appeals - Whether order of Govt. was illegal and ultra vires - Held, no promissory estoppel against exercise of legislative power of State - Doctrine could not be invoked for preventing Govt. from acting in discharge of its duty under law - Govt. would not bound by act of its officers and agents who act beyond scope of their authority - Respondent not estopped as representation made by it was beyond scope of its authority - Levy of tax being for public purpose for augmenting revenues of Municipality - Hence, order of Govt. directing levy of octroi in pursuance of resolution of Municipality could not be challenged as it was in exercise of its statutory duty - Appeals disposed of.
