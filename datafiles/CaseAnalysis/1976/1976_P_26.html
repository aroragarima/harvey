Â State of Uttar Pradesh v Poosu and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Shingal, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 395; (1976) 3 SCC 1; (1976) SCC (Cr) 368; AIR 1976 SC 1750; 1976 CRLJ 1373; 1976 CrLR(SC) 232; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution, arts. 21, 136 and 142 - CrPC, 1898, s. 427 - Special leave to appeal - Against order of acquittal on a capital charge - (A) Whether Supreme Court can direct rearrest - (B) Constitutionality of - Held, SC can direct rearrest of accused as it can exercise the same powers which the HC has u/s.427, CrPC, 1898 - No infringement of art. 21 of Constitution - Reference answered against the accused/respondents - Order accordingly.
