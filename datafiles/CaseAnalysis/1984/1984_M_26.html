Messrs A.T. Brij Paul Singh and Bros v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 445; (1984) 4 SCC 59; AIR 1984 SC 1703

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Breach of contract - Appellant granted works contract to respondent by way of tender - Time was essence of contract - Respondent rescinded from contract when appellant failed to perform his obligation within specified time - Appellant filed suit for breach of contract - Dismissed by Trial Court - High Court reversed findings of Trial Court in appeal but granted damages for certain items - Whether respondent was guilty of breach of contract? - Held, respondent was not justified in rescinding from contract part of which was already performed - For performing remaining part of contract, appellant, had transported machinery and equipment from Rajkot - Appellant entitled to damages by way of loss of profit - Damages enhanced - Thus respondent was guilty of breach of contract - Appeal partly allowed.
