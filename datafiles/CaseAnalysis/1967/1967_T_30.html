Yusufalli Esmail Nagree v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 1968 SC 147; 1968 CRLJ 103; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 720

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Bribe - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - S. 165A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(3) - Appellant convicted for offering bribe to Govt. clerk for non-execution of warrant issued for violation of Building regulations against his wife - Trap laid down - Conversation between the appellant and decoy was tape recorded and appellant was apprehended - Whether the conviction of the appellant was justified? - Held, yes the dialogue between the appellant and police decoy was not a statement made to police in any manner - Plea of protection cannot be taken as conversation by appellant was not under any force or compulsion - Conviction u/s. 165A of IPC, 1860 justified - In view of old age and ill-health of appellant, sentence reduced to already undergone - Appeal dismissed.
