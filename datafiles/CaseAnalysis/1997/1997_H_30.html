Balwinder Kaur v Hardeep Singh

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1667; (1997) 11 SCC 701; AIR 1998 SC 764; 1998 (32) ALR 237; 1998 (1) AWC 709; 1998 (5) Bom.C.R. 274; JT 1997 (9) SC 157; 1997(7) SCALE 37; 1998 (1) SLT 26; 1998 (Supp) RD 33; [1997] Supp5 S.C.R. 202; 1997 (9) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955-Sections 13 and 23-Divorce petition by wife appellant-Ex parte decree by trial court-Fraud alleged by wife to have been committed by respondent husband in getting ex parte divorce-Petition for divorce-Not commercial suit-Stress should always be on preserving institution of marriage-Counsel for respondent stating that respondent at no time wanted divorce-Then why respondent allowed proceeding to go ex parte-Compromise between parties-Then what was reason for appellant to file divorce petition within six weeks of compromise-Duties of Court under Section 23-High Court should not have summarily dismissed appeal-By so doing, it has failed to exercise its superintendence under Article 227 of Constitution-Appeal allowed with costs-Matter remitted to trial court.
