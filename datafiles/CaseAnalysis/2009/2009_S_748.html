Subodh Kumar Yadav v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 897; (2009) 14 SCC 638; AIR 2010 SC 802; 2009 (4) G.L.R. 3504; 2009 (3) JCC 2362; JT 2009 (9) SC 408; 2010 (2) MLJ(Crl) 348; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 600; 2009(9) SCALE 621; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 574; 2009 (3) UC 1377

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A] Criminal - Cancellation of Bail - Relevant factors - Whether application for cancellation will not be maintainable with reference to what transpired prior to grant of bail? - Held, for cancellation of bail, conduct subsequent to release on bail and supervening circumstances will be relevant - If a superior court finds that court granting bail had acted on irrelevant material or if there was non- application of mind or failure to take note of any statutory bar to grant bail, or if there was manifest impropriety as for example failure to hear public prosecutor/complainant where required, an order for cancellation of bail can in fact be made - Superior court would also be justified in considering question whether irrelevant material were taken into consideration by court granting bail - Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;[B] Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 498A - CrPC, 1973, s. 437 - Bail - Cancellation of - Legality of - Complaint filed by respondent No. 2 for alleged commission of offence punishable u/s. 498A IPC - On application of bail, Judicial Magistrate First Class (Magistrate) granted bail to appellant - Application filed for cancellation of bail - Sessions Judge held that Magistrate enlarged appellant on bail on considerations other than judicial and cancelled bail granted to appellant - HC upheld order of Session Judge - Hence, present appeal - Whether Session Judge and HC justified in canceling bail granted to appellant? - Magistrate decided to hear bail application presented by appellant on same day and also called for record from superior court and granted bail to appellant without hearing complainant - Magistrate had taken into consideration totally irrelevant documents which were never referred to in complaint at all - By taking into consideration those documents Magistrate exhibited his anxiety to release appellant anyhow on bail - Held, judicial discretion was exercised by Magistrate in an arbitrary manner and with oblique motives - HC did not commit any error in confirming order of Sessions Judge cancelling bail which was arbitrarily granted to appellant by Judicial Magistrate First Class - Appeal dismissed.
