National Fertilizers Limited v Tuncay Alankus and others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1520; (2013) 9 SCC 616

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legality, Contempt proceedings

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 129 - Supreme Court Rules, 1996, O. XLVII r. 3(c ) - Contempt Proceedings - Legality - Petitioner filed instant petition initiating proceedings u/art. 129 of the Constitution r/w O. XLVII of the Rules and r. 3(c) of the Rules to Regulate Proceedings for Contempt of the Supreme Court, 1975 against the respondent No. 1for flouting the orders of SC dt. 4-9-2006 r/w O. dt. 14-12-2006 in criminal appeal - Trespondent was restrained from withdrawing any sums from his accounts in a Swiss Bank till the decision in the matter.

Held, allegations are correct and that respondent No.1- contemnor has willfully flouted the orders dt. 4-9-2006 and 14-12-2006 of SC and that he has no respect or regard for SC at all. For the allegations made in the contempt petition, a notice had been issued to the contemnor. In the notice it was specifically mentioned that the charge against him is that he has violated the order of SC dt. 4-9-2006. In fact, the respondent No. 1-contemnor has filed his reply thereto. However, from a perusal of the reply filed by contemnor it is clear that he has not denied the allegation of the petitioner that he has withdrawn money by flouting the order of SC dt. 4-9-2006. Thus, there is sufficient material on the record to suggest that contemnor has committed contempt of Court. Therefore, SC held the contemnor guilty of Contempt of Court. In fact, even today, the contemnor is not prepared to return the money which he has withdrawn from his accounts in the Swiss Bank. Respondent No. 1-contemnor has withdrawn about 11 million dollars. Thus, there is no option but to punish him for Contempt of Court. It is made clear that if by the said date, the contemnor deposits the amounts withdrawn by him, SC may consider recalling the order passed. Order accordingly.
