M/S. Kamal Trading Private Limited(Now Known As Manav Investment & Trading Co. Ltd.) v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 803; (2012) 2 SCC 25; AIR 2012 SC 823; 2012 (91) ALR 481; 2012 (2) AWC 1819; 2012 (3) CTC 102; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 826; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 630; 2012 (115) RD 821; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provision) Act, 1947

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - West Bengal Premises Requisition and Control (Temporary Provision) Act, 1947 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5(A), 6 - Notification - Public Purpose - Hearing rejected - Sustainability - Appellant was entrusted by seventeen joint owners of premises to look after day-to-day management and maintenance and as also to initiate proceedings for and on their behalf - Property was seized and possession of certain floors of said premises taken by respondents - State Govt. was obliged to release property under requisition after expiry of 25 years as per 1947 Act - State Govt. was planning to acquire premises in exercise of its powers under 1894 Act - Appellants filed Writ Petition for a writ of mandamus directing State to release said floors from requisition - Govt. issued notification u/s. 4 of 1894 Act stating that floors were needed for public purpose - Notification was published in Govt. Gazette - Appellant raised objections u/s. 5A of 1894 Act - Second Land Acquisition Officer issued notice fixing date of hearing of objections - Appellant requested for adjournment - Special Land Acquisition Officer did not accept appellant's request for further adjournment and proceeded to submit report - Govt. issued declaration u/s. 6 of 1894 Act - Appellants and other owners filed writ petition for quashing notification on ground that order was vitiated due to violation of rule of hearing enshrined in s. 5A(2) of 1894 Act and non-application of mind by concerned officer to objections filed u/s. 5A(1) of 1894, Act - Writ petition was dismissed - DB also dismissed Writ appeal thereafter - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether respondent authority was justified in passing orders without hearing appellants and other owners - Held, Second Land Acquisition Officer had only reproduced contentions of officers of Acquiring Body, objections taken by appellants were rejected on a very vague ground mere use of words 'for greater interest of public' did not lend report character of a report made after application of mind - Hence, declaration u/s. 6 of 1894 Act, ought to be set aside because appellant was not given hearing as contemplated u/s. 5A(2) of 1894 Act - Notification issued u/s. 4 of 1894 Act, was also set aside - It was, however, open to State Govt. to initiate fresh land acquisition proceedings in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
