State Of U.P. v Virendra Prasad

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 60; (2004) 9 SCC 37; AIR 2004 SC 1517; 2004 ALJ 673; 2004 (2) Crimes 30; 2004 CRLJ 1373; 2004 (1) JCC 370; JT 2004 (2) SC 89; 2004(2) SCALE 175; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 39; 2004 (2) SLT 875; 2004 (1) Supreme 962

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss 302 and 304 part II and 307 - Conviction and sentence u/s 302 - Alteration by HC of punishment and acquittal of accused u/s 307 - Whether proper? - Held, The HC was not justified in altering the conviction or directing acquittal so far as the offence punishable u/s.307 is concerned No reason whatsoever has been indicated for holding that a case u/s.307 was not made out Sentence should commensurate with the gravity of offence - Appeal allowed.
