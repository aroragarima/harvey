Messrs Widia (India) Limited and Others v The State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 646; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 110; JT 2003 (7) SC 237; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Mysore General Clauses Act, 1899, Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas for Consumption Use or Sale Therein Act, 1979, Karnataka Municipal Corporation Act, 1976, Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Second Amendment), Act 1992, Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Amendment) Act, 1994, Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Amendment) Act, 1992, Bombay Act, 1958, Hyderabad Amending Act, 1954, Mandal Panchayat and Nyaya Panchayats Act, 1983, Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Second Amendment) Act, 1992

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Karnataka Tax On Entry Of Goods Into Local Areas For Consumption Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1979, ss. 2(A)(5), 3, 3(1) - Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Second Amendment), Act 1992, s. 3 - Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Amendment) Act, 1994 - Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods (Amendment) Act, 1992 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 3 - Mysore General Clauses Act, 1899, s. 21 - Karnataka sales tax Act, 1957 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 246, 301, 303, 304, 304(a), 304(b) - Karnataka Tax on Entry of Goods Act, 1979 - Amending Act No 8 of 1993 - Constitutional validity - 'Retrospective' - Held, since no contention is raised to effect that levy of tax on goods by impugned notification discriminates between goods imported from other states and similar goods manufactured or produced within state, it would be difficult to accept contention that sanction of President was required to be obtained before amending and enacting Act No 8 of 1993, whereby for words 'by State Govt, by notification from time to time', words 'retrospectively or prospectively by State Govt. by notification and different dates' were submitted - A subsequently notification issued in exercise of powers conferred under said section may in some case amount to restriction to free trade and commerce, but simplicitor addition of words 'retrospectively or prospectively' would not require sanction of President as contemplated under art.304(b) - Hence, contention that amending Act No 8 of 1993, by which words 'retrospectively or prospectively' are added, requires sanction of President, is without any substance - Further held, establishment of industrial area is for limited purpose and s 3, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966 specifically provides that State Govt. may by notification declare any area to be an industrial area for purposes of said Act - But, it is nowhere provided that said area would cease to be part and parcel of either municipal corporation or area of municipality or panchayat.
