Â Kewal Krishan Puri and Another v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 222; (1980) 1 SCC 416; AIR 1980 SC 1008; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1217

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Ultra Vires, Constitutional Validity of a Provision, Government servant, Agricultural Produce Markets

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, Art. 110(2) of 199 - Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951 - Punjab Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1961, Ss. 3, 5, 6(3), 6(1), 8, 10, 11, 12, 23 - Levy of Market Fee - License - Agriculture Produce - Constitutional Validity - Maintenance - Respondents required appellants/Traders to take out licenses on payment of a license fee u/s. 23 of 1961 Act - Market Committee was required and authorized to levy on ad-valorem basis fees on agricultural produce bought or sold by licensees in notified market area - Ordinance 4 of 1974 was replaced and rate was raised - Appellants filed petitions before HC challenging increase in rate of market fee - HC upheld levy and increase of market fee and Petitions were dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether levy of increase in rate of market fee for agricultural produce bought or sold in notified market area was justifiable - Held, some licenses were imperative to be taken only by way of regulatory measure, some were in nature of grant of exclusive right or privilege of State - Respondent/Market Committees could not be competent under Act to impose any tax - Market was not found to have been properly established and it could not enforce any provisions of the Act or Rules or bye-laws - Market fee realized from traders on purchase of agricultural produce in market had to be utilized for purpose of rendering all sorts of facilities and services for benefit of agriculturists throughout area - Market Committees or Board to prepare balance-sheets for their accounts to be audited by Govt. auditors but for purposes of raising market fee - Then only there might be legal justification for raising rate of market fee further to a reasonable extent - Court concluded that there was no justification in raising fee and HC was wrong and erroneous - Appeals disposed of.
