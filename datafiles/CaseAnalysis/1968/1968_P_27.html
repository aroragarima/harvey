Pushkar Mukherjee and Others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 163; (1969) 1 SCC 10; AIR 1970 SC 852; 1970 CRLJ 852; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 635

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3(2), 7, 10 and 11(1) - Whether grounds served on appellant for detention are grounds which are relevant to maintenance of 'public order'? - Held, no - All grounds relate to cases of assault on solitary individuals either by knife or by using crackers and it is difficult to accept argument that these grounds have any relevance or proximate connection with maintenance of public order - Every act of assault or injury to specific persons does not lead to public disorder - When two people quarrel and fight and assault each other inside a house or in a street, it may be said that there is disorder but not public disorder - A mere disturbance of law and order leading to disorder is thus not necessarily sufficient for action under Preventive Detention Act but a disturbance which will affect public order comes within scope of Act - Even if any one of grounds or reasons that led to satisfaction is irrelevant, order of detention would be invalid even if there were other relevant grounds, because it can never be certain to what extent bad reasons operated on mind of authority concerned or whether detention order would have been made at all if only one or two good reasons had been before them - Petitions dismissed.
