Union of India v Mahadeolal Prabhudayal

Supreme Court of India

23 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 475; AIR 1965 SC 1755; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 80 - Indian Railways Act, 1890, ss. 72, 73, 74 and 77 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 152 and 161 - Carriers Act, 1865 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Respondent consignee claimed damages for non-delivery from railways - Appellant contended that they fall u/s. 77 of 1890 act - Absence of evidence establishing misconduct of railways - High Court allowed appeal and decreed suit in favour of respodents - Whether respondent entitled damages for non-delivery due to misconduct of servants? - Held, notice given by railways fulfills all requirements of s. 77 - Risk note Z provides complete immunity of railways except upon proof of misconduct - Railway administration required to disclose how consignment was dealt during time of its possession or control - No such demand was made by consigner - There is no evidence on behalf of respondent to prove misconduct and as misconduct cannot fairly be inferred from evidence produced on behalf of railway, suit must fail - Appeal dismissed.
