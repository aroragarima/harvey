Union of India v Sher Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 868; (1993) 1 SCC 608; AIR 1993 SCW 701; 1993 (2) AWC 1131; 1993 (1) CCC 306; JT 1993 (3) SC 693; 1993 (1) RentLR 273; 1993(1) SCALE 209; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 326; 1993 (1) UJ 710

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 11, 18 and 50(2) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether UOI through Deputy Inspector General, National Security Guard is a necessary and proper party to be impleaded when land in question was acquired by State of Haryana for purpose of National Security Guard as desired by UOI? - Held, definition of person interested must be liberally construed so as to include a body, local authority, or a company for shows benefit land is acquired and who is bound under an agreement to pay compensation - Central Govt. has no machinery of its own for acquiring of land and as such it had to depend upon State Govt. for above purpose - However, financial implication with regard to payment of compensation is to be borne by Central Govt. therefore necessary and proper party to be impleaded - Application of UOI for impleadment allowed - Relied on, (Himalayan tiles and Marbles [1980 Indlaw SC 500] - Appeal allowed.
