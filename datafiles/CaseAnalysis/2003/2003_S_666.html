Balram Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, Ashok Bhan, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 435; (2003) 11 SCC 286; (2004) SCC (Cr) 149; AIR 2003 SC 2213; 2003 (2) CCR 197; 2003 (3) Crimes 43; 2003 (5) CRJ 292; 2003 (2) JCC 893; JT 2003 (4) SC 569; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 255; 2003(4) SCALE 561; 2003 (1) SCW 2656; 2003 (3) SLT 601; 2003 (3) Supreme 749

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss.157, 319 - IPC, 1860, ss.148, 149, 302, 307, 324, 326, 34 - Held, while considering complaint of appellants in regard to delay in FIR reaching jurisdictional Magistrate, creditworthiness of ocular evidence adduced by prosecution will have to be borne in mind and if it is found that such ocular evidence is worthy of acceptance, element of delay in registering a complaint or sending same to jurisdictional Magistrate by itself would not in any manner weaken prosecution case - Applicability of s.34 - Further held, in regard to applicability of s.34, all that prosecution has to establish is that there was a prior concert or meeting of minds between accused persons and such prior concert or meeting of minds may be determined from conduct of offenders unfolding itself during course of action and declaration made by them just before mounting attack common intention can also be developed at spur of moment but there must be pre-arrangement or pre-meditated concert - In instant case, accused persons had shared common intention of causing death of deceased - HC was, therefore, justified in invoking s.34 to uphold conviction of appellants - Appeal dismissed.
