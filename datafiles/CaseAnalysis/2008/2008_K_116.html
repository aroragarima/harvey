Bhakra Beas Management Board v Kanta Aggarwal and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2154; (2008) 11 SCC 366; 2008 (4) ACC 764; AIR 2008 SC 3118; 2008(4) ALL MR 916; 2008 (4) AWC 3830; JT 2008 (7) SC 377; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 604; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 733; 2008(9) SCALE 740; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, Computation Of Compensation, f Ob, Assessment Of Damages

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - Whether benefits which claimant has received on account of death of her husband have to be deducted while computing compensation, if any, payable? - Held, deductions are admissible from amount of compensation in case claimant receives benefit as a consequence of injuries sustained, which otherwise he would not have been entitled to - It does not cover cases where payment received is not dependent upon an injury sustained on meeting with an accident - Thus, an amount earned out of one's labour or contribution towards one's wealth, savings, etc. either for himself or for his family which such person knows under law has to go to his heirs after his death could not be said to be 'pecuniary gain' only on account of one's accidental death - This of course is a 'pecuniary gain' but how this is equitable or could be balanced out of amount to be received as compensation under Motor Vehicles Act - There is no correlation between two amounts not even remotely - High Court lost sight of fact that benefits which claimant receives on account of death or injury have to be duly considered while fixing compensation - In view of background facts, it is just and proper that sum of Rupees five lakhs already deposited shall be permitted to be withdrawn by claimants in full and final settlement of claim relatable to death of deceased - Appeal partly allowed.
