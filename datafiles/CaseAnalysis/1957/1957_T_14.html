Â Central India Spinning And weaving and Manufacturing Comp v Municipal Committee, Wardha

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 72; AIR 1958 SC 341; 1958 SCJ 604; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1102

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indirect Tax - Practice and Procedure - Municipalities & Local Govts - Indian Stamp Act, 1899 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Central Provinces & Berar Municipalities Act, 1922, ss.66(1)(o), 83(2) - (A) What meaning has to be given to a taxing statute if two alternative interpretations are possible?- Held, in construing a taxing statute, effect has been given to interpretation which favours citizen and not one that imposes a burden on him - (B) Whether goods passing in transit through municipal limits, without loading or unloading or stopping within municipal limits are liable to terminal tax? - Further held, terminal tax is not leviable on goods which are in transit and are only carried across limits of Municipality and are neither loaded nor unloaded within municipal area - Appeal allowed.
