Nana Keshav Lagad and another v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 360; (2013) 12 SCC 721; AIR 2013 SC 3510; 2013 CRLJ 4011; JT 2013 (12) SC 59; 2013(8) SCALE 131; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 606

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Police Act, 1951, ss. 37(a), 135 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 324, 504, 506 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused no. 2, 3, 4 committed offence punishable u/ss. 34, 143, 147, 148, 149, 302, 324, 504, 506 of IPC - Trial Court prosecuted appellants and passed conviction order - On appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court confirmed the same - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC against conviction order - HC upheld conviction and sentence imposed against appellants - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by Trial Court was sustainable - SC found that Trial Court had analyzed every one of submissions relating to evidence of PW4 in detail and had found no substance in contention made on behalf of appellants - Therefore, based on said submissions, regarding evidence of PW4, SC did not find any scope to interfere with judgment impugned - In fact, as rightly noted by Trial Court, it was for appellants to have explained as to how clothes worn by them contained human blood, u/s. 313 questioning, no explanation was forthcoming from appellants - Appellants placed reliance upon Vadivelu Thevar v State of Madras 1957 Indlaw SC 71 to support contention that since conviction was based on solitary evidence of PW4, without proper corroboration, same could not be sustained - SC found that it was not based on solitary statement of PW4 alone, but was also supported by other eyewitness viz., PW5, whose evidence merited acceptance on par with evidence of PW4, apart from medical evidence fully supporting case of prosecution, said decision could had no application to facts of this case - State/respondent rightly contended that medical evidence substantially establishes intention of accused to eliminate deceased and injuries sustained by deceased discloses coordinated vengeance with which assault was caused by appellants, in order to ensure that deceased did not survive - Appeals dismissed.
