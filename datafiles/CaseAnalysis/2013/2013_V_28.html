Hari Dass Sharma v Vikas Sood and others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 272; (2013) 5 SCC 243; JT 2013 (6) SC 490; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 96; 2013 (1) RCR(Rent) 587; 2013(6) SCALE 464; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1029

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: H.P. Urban Rent Control Act, 1987, Himachal Pradesh Urban Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 2009

Summary: Rent Control - Himachal Pradesh Urban Rent Control Act, 1987, ss. 14(4), 14 - Eviction - Bonafide requirement - Addition and alteration - Legality -Appellant/landlord filed applications u/s. 14 of the Act before Rent Controller for eviction of respondents/tenants from building on grounds that he required building for purposes of addition and alteration of building - Rent Controller allowed applications - On appeal Appellate Authority dismissed appeals - Respondents filed revision petitions before HC - HC allowed revisions and directed appellants to produce valid revised/renewed sanctioned or approved building plan before Executing Court whereupon Executing Court should allow a reasonable time to tenants for vacating property and delivering possession to landlord and till then tenant should remain liable to pay charges for use and occupation of premises at the rate at which they were being paid earlier - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC could have directed that only on valid revised/renewed building plant being sanctioned by competent authority, order of eviction should be available for execution - Held, Rent Controller while determining bonafides of appellant had recorded finding that landlord had obtained sanction from Municipal Corporation, and had accordingly passed order of eviction and that order of eviction was not disturbed either by Appellate Authority or by HC - However, once HC maintained order of eviction passed by Rent Controller u/s. 14(4) of the Act, tenants were obliged to give vacant possession of building to landlord and could only ask for reasonable time to deliver vacant possession of building to landlord - Hence, direction of HC that order of eviction could only be executed on revised plan of building being approved was clearly contrary to provisions of s. 14(4) of the Act - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside however granted time to respondents to vacate building within 3 months - Appeals allowed.
