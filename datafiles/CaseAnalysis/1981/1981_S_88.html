Gokaraju Rangaraju Etc. v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 411; (1981) 3 SCC 132; (1981) SCC (Cr) 652; AIR 1981 SC 1473; 1981 BBCJ 187; 1981 CRLJ 876; 1981 CrLR(SC) 294; 1981 MLJ 528; 1981(1) SCALE 706; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 474

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Constitution, art. 233, 21 - Whether Judgments pronounced by a de facto judge is valid or not when his appoint is subsequently found to be defective? - Held, it is valid - De facto doctrine - The acts of the Officers de facto performed by them within the scope of their assumed official authority, in the interest of the public or third persons and not for their own benefit, are generally as valid and binding, as if they were the acts of officers de jure - Whatever be the defect of his title to the office, judgments pronounced by him and acts done by him when he was clothed with powers and functions of the office, albeit unlawfully, have the same efficacy as judgments pronounced and acts done by a judge de jure -Valid as the judgments and orders of the Court of Session - De facto doctrine is born of necessity and public policy - Prevents needless confusion and endless mischief - Appeals dismissed.
