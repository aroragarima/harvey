Binani Zinc Limited v Kerala State Electricity Board And Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2022; (2009) 11 SCC 244; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 417; 2009(3) SCALE 849; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity Regulatory Commission Act, 1998, ss. 49, 59 and 79(j) - Power to determine tariff - Parliament enacted Electricity Regulatory Commissions Act, 1998 which deemed to have come into force w.e.f. 25-04-1998 - Respondent No. 2 (KSEB) revised its tariff w.e.f. 15-05-1999 - State Government of Kerala, pursuant to its G.O. dt. 17-8-2001, entered into an MoU with Government of India and constituted Kerala State Electricity Commission (KSERC) on 14-12-2002 - Meanwhile, respondent no. 2 revised tariff w.e.f 1-10-2002 by issuing Kerala State Electricity Board Extra High Tension Tariff Revision Order, 2002 - Said order was challenged by appellant and when matter reached KSERC, it by an order dt. 30-4-2004 held, inter alia, that on day tariff revision Notification dt. 24-10-2002 was issued, Board was to determine tariff since Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 was still applicable - On appeal, HC upheld said order - Appellant contended that Electricity Act, 2003 having come into force w.e.f. 10-6-2003, KSERC functioning as such prior thereto continued to function as State Commission under 2003 Act and it must be held to have considered appellant's petition in terms of provisions therof - Whether appellant's contentions could be accepted? - Held, Electricity (Supply) Act, 1998 was not repealed by ERC Act, 1998 - It was only u/s. 185 of Electricity Act of 2003 that provisions of Indian Electricity Act 1010, Electricity (Supply Act 1948 and ERC Act 1998 were repealed - But at same time anything done or any action taken under Acts of 1910 or 1948 or 1998 Act have been saved in so far as they are not inconsistent with provisions of 2003 Act - Hence, State Electricity Board had requisite jurisdiction to revise a tariff till such time as Commission was constituted and purposes of 1998 Act could be achieved through it - Till time Regulatory Commission was not constituted by state of Kerela, power to determine tariff remained with Board under Electricity (Supply ) Act 1948 as it was not repealed by Electricity Regulatory Commission Act 1998 - Parliament could not have intended to bring about a situation where no authority would be empowered to determine tariff between date of coming into force of ERC Act, 1998 and constitution of commission - It is only after Regulatory commission is constituted that it will be sole authority to determine tariff - This clarification in regard to decision rendered by a two Judge Bench of this Court in BSES would be sufficient to answer reference - Reference answered.
