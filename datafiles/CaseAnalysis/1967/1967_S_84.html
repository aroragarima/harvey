Â Jagdev Singh v State of Jammu and Kashmir

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 246; AIR 1968 SC 327; 1968 CRLJ 387; 1969 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 197

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Defence and Security Forces - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 30A and 30(1)(b) - (A) Whether orders of review are sufficient for continuance of detention, even though earlier orders of review passed were not properly made in view of judgment of this Court in Lakhanpal's case (P. L. Lakhanpal v. the UOI, 1967 Indlaw SC 145)? - Held, no orders passed should have been reviewed after every six months in manner explained in Lakhanpal's case - Orders of detention passed fell after six months and there were no orders to continue thereafter - When State Govt. ordered continuance of detention orders on review in accordance with procedure, there was no order to be continued because in between reviews were not proper and detention had become illegal - There was no proper review as required by r. 30A(9) and detention for all that period was illegal and could not be saved by original order which must be deemed to have come to an end, after six months, in absence of a proper review u/r. 30 A(9) - Petitioners would be entitled to release - (B) Whether it is open to State Govt. to pass a fresh order in circumstances of present cases? - Further held, there is nothing in Defence of India Act and Rules which forbids State Govt. to cancel one order of detention and pass another in its place - There is nothing which will bar Govt. from passing a fresh order of detention on same facts, in case earlier order of detention or its continuance is held to be defective for any reason - There must be no malafide - Petitions allowed.
