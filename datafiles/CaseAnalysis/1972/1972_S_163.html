Ananta Mukhi, @ Ananta Hari v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 389; (1972) SCC (Cr) 344; (1972) 1 SCC 580; AIR 1972 SC 1256; 1972 CRLJ 885; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act, 1970, s. 3(2) - Arms Act, 1959 - (A) Scope of expression 'acting in any manner prejudicial to security of State or maintenance of public order' u/s 3(2) - (B) Detention - Order stating that it is necessary to detain detenu with view to prevent him from acting in any manner prejudicial to security of State or maintenance of public order - If lack of application of mind - Held, grounds that detenu with large number of associates armed with lethal weapons committed dacoity and dacoity with murder fall within expression - And it does not suffer from casualness or absence of due application of mind - Petition dismissed.
