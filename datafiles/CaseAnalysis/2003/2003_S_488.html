State of Karnataka v M.V. Mahesh

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 205; (2003) 3 SCC 353; (2003) SCC (Cr) 795; 2003 (1) CCR 447; 2003 (1) Crimes 459; 2003 (4) CRJ 315; 2003 (1) JCC 443; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 106; 2003(2) SCALE 553; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 553; 2003 (2) SLT 455; 2003 (2) Supreme 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Charge - Sheet

Summary: Criminal - Mystery over disappearance of wife of the respondent - Even in the absence of the corpus delicti it is possible to establish in an appropriate case commission of murder on appropriate material being made available to the court. - No such material available to the court. - Held, the whole prosecution case is a chain of circumstances connecting one with another with many missing links in between. The High Court was, therefore, right in not accepting the case put forth by the prosecution.
