Hydro-Electric Employees Union, U.P. and Others v Sudhir Kumar Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 838; (1998) 6 SCC 706; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1645; AIR 1998 SC 2970; 1998 (3) AWC 2273; JT 1998 (5) SC 631; 1999 (1) LLJ 153; 1999 (1) SLJ 152; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 105; 1998 (3) UPLBEC 1786

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, s. 79(c) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Electricity Supply Act - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, s. 4(1) - Whether judgments of Allahabad High Court dealing with question of amalgamation of posts of Meter Readers, Sub Station Operators and Switch Board Attendants under Uttar Pradesh State Electricity Board is valid? - Held, no relied on Reserve Bank of India case (1976 Indlaw SC 179) - Prior to enforcement of the service regulations the Board was fully competent to interchange the incumbents occupying the post of Meter Readers, sub Station Officers and Operators and Switch Board Officers in view of the administrative orders issued by the Board - Order of the HC is set aside - Appeals disposed.
