State of Gujarat and others v Gujarat Revenue Tribunal And Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 656; (1977) 1 SCC 46; AIR 1976 SC 1721; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition) Act, 1953, Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949, Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition Compensation Bonds) Rules, 1956

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition) Act, 1953, ss. 3, 8, 11, 11(2), 11(3), 13(1), 13(2), 15, 16, 17, 22, 25 - Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949, ss. 7(1), 7(2), 7(1)(a), 7(1)(b), 7(1)(b)(i), 7(1)(b)(ii), 7(1)(b)(iii) -Land Acquisition Act, 1894,ss. 11, 15, 23, 23(1), 23(2), 24, 26, 34 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 22 - Constitution of India, 1950,arts. 31(2), 31A, 136, 227 - Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition Compensation Bonds) Rules, 1956 - Jagirdars abolished - Compensation Award - Entitlement - The 1953 Act came into force w.e.f.1-8-1954 by which jagirs of jagirdars were abolished and certain properties comprised in jagirs vested in State Govt. - Jagir Abolition Officer was authorised to act as Collector under the 1953 Act - Jagirdars filed applications to Collector for award of compensation - Collector granted compensation to some properties and refused compensation to some other properties by award dt. 30-7-1963 - Jagirdars filed appeal in Tribunal - Award was modified by Tribunal - Jagirdars and State Govt. filed separate appeals in HC - HC had disposed them by common judgment dt. 27/28-8-1969 answering some points in favour of jagirdars and some points in favour of State Govt. and matter remanded back to Tribunal for fresh disposal - Aggrieved, instant appeals were filed by both parties - Whether directions of HC in remand order to Tribunal were justified and in accordance to law - Held, method of awarding compensation to jagirdars was mentioned in s. 13 of the 1953 Act - Every award u/s. 13 or 14 should be in form prescribed and provisions of the 1894 Act would apply while making award - - s. 11(3) of the 1953 Act provided that jagirdars would be entitled to compensation in respect of any property in which they had any right or interest, but in manner provided in s. 11 (i) to (iii) - - Hence u/s. 11(3) of the 1953 Act jagirdars were entitled to compensation for all unbuilt village site lands in which they had right or interest - s. 26(1) of the 1894 Act stated that every award should specify amount awarded u/s. 23(1) and also amounts if any awarded under other clauses - Solatium , interest and 15% of market value awarded were not part of compensation awarded and hence not to be mentioned in prescribed form of award u/s. 26(1) of 1894 Act - - Legislature never intended to provide solatium to jagirdars - Awarding interest on delayed payments was justified but not on entire amount of installments - Interest would be payable only on principal amount of installments - - SC in Satinder Singh and Others v Amrao Singh and Others, 1961 Indlaw SC 503 held that intention of legislature to award 3% interest was legitimate to hold that interest was payable on arrears of principal amount of instalments - Jagirdar were deprived of their properties after the 1953 Act came into force were entitled to interest on amount of delayed payment of compensation - Jagirdars would not get interest at 3% on total 10 installments of principal for 10 years but would get interest for 1 year only at 3%. - In s. 11(2) of the 1953 Act expression used 'equivalent to 3 multiples' meant 3 times and not 6 times - State had no right of appeal to revenue Tribunal and could not challenge compensation fixed by Collector - Tribunal orders were justified and could not be interfered -- Directions of HC in remand order to Tribunal were modified - Appeals disposed of.
