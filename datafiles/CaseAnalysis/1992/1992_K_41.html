Indian Aluminium Company Limited And Anr. v Karnattaka Electricity Board And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 467; (1992) 3 SCC 580; AIR 1992 SC 2169; 1992 (2) CompLJ 177; JT 1992 (3) SC 535; 1992(1) SCALE 1157; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Aluminium Control Order 1970, Electricity (Supply) (Karnataka Amendment) Act 1981

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 49(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14, 19(1)(g), 133 and 245 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Electricity - (A) Tariff rate - Increase of by virtue of amending Act - Constitutionality - (B) Legislative competence - (C) Electricity supply - Increase in tariff rate - Applicability of principle of promissory estoppel concessional rate - Agreement, not outcome of any unilateral promise or assurance but negotiations - Annulment by amendment of Act and imposition of uniform tariff rate on industries classified under one category - (D) Smelter Plant in aluminium industry - Supply of electricity - Question of - Held, (A) Amendment Act held does not suffer from any infirmity affecting its vires either on score of legislative competence or for offending art. 19(1)(g) or 14 of Constitution - (B) Question of Constitution should be interpreted broadly and liberally - (C) Not hit by promissory estoppel - (D) Supreme Court directed Govt. to consider it sympathetically - Order accordingly.
