Mackinnon Mackenzie And Co. Limited v Audrey D’Costa And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28200; (1987) SCC (L&S) 100; (1987) 2 SCC 469; AIR 1987 SC 1281; 1987 (1) BankCLR 31; 1987 (2) Bom.C.R. 662; 1987 (2) CompLJ 165; 1987 (54) FLR 530; JT 1987 (2) SC 34; 1987 LabIC 961; 1987 (1) LLJ 536; 1987 (2) LLN 10; 1987(1) SCALE 627; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 659; 1988 (1) SLJ 196; 1987 (2) SLR 690; 1987 (2) UJ 180

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Equal Remuneration Act, 1976

Summary: Service - Equal remuneration Act, 1976, ss. 1, 2(h) and 4 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14 and 39(d) - Service matter - Company - Stenographers of both sex working in it - Unequally remunerated - To determine: (A) Whether the petitioner had violated the provisions of s.4 of the 1976 Act - (B) Whether the work is of same or similar nature - (C) Whether the applicability of, s.1 depend upon financial ability of employer - Held, no, since after the settlement was arrived at between the Union and the Company there was a common pay scale both for men and women Stenographers and the discrimination was brought about while carrying out the fitment of the lady stenographers in the said scale of pay - Considerations for determination, stated and the work held to be of same and similar nature , irrespective of difference in pay scale - Applicability of Act does not depend upon financial ability - Petition Dismissed.
