Renuka Bai Alias Rinku Alias Ratan And Another v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1462; (2006) 7 SCC 442; AIR 2006 SC 3056; 2006 (3) JCC 1515; JT 2006 (8) SC 282; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 128; 2007 (1) RLW 273; 2006(8) SCALE 604; 2006(80) SCALE 604; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 643

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 306 - Kidnapping - Murder - Conviction - Death penalty - Challenged - Appellants (sisters) along with their mother co-accused with the help of approver used to commit thefts and were instrumental in kidnapping 13 children and caused the death of 9 out of them - Appellants were convicted for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 120B of IPC and the death penalty imposed on them by the Sessions Court was confirmed by the HC - Present appeal - (A) Evidence of approver - Held, when the Court suspected the evidence of the approver, the pardon given to him itself could be withdrawn and he could be tried along with the other accused - But the provisions contained in the CrPC do not enable the Court to take such a strong action - In the present case, entire evidence adduced by the prosecution clearly establishes that the approver's evidence was fully corroborated by other items of evidence and death of deceased was caused by the appellants - (B) Conviction - Held, evidence adduced by the prosecution proved beyond reasonable doubt that the appellants were responsible for series of kidnapping of children and murders and they have been rightly found guilty for these offences - Prosecution thus succeeded in proving that these appellants have committed a series of murders - No reason to interfere with the order of conviction passed by the sessions court and confirmed by the HC - (C) Death penalty - Held, no mitigating circumstances in favour of the appellant, except for the fact that they were women - Further, the nature of the crime and the systematic way in which each child was kidnapped and killed amply demonstrates the depravity of the mind of the appellants - These appellants indulged in criminal activities for a very long period and continued it till they were caught by the police - Appellants had not been committing these crimes under any compulsion but they took it very casually and killed all these children - Conviction and death penalty imposed on them confirmed - Order accordingly.
