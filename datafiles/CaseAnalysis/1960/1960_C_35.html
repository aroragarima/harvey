Haji Aziz and Abdul Shakoor Brothers v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City II

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 324; AIR 1961 SC 663; 1983 ECR 1942; [1961] 41 ITR 350; 1961 (1) SCJ 625; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Customs - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(xv) - Sea Customs Act, 1878 (Since repealed), ss. 167(8), 183 and 193(2) - Whether penalty in lieu of confiscation u/s. 167(8) r/w s. 183, Sea Customs Act, is a permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of Income Tax Act, 1922? - Held, a penalty incurred for the contravention of any specific statutory provision cannot be said to be a commercial loss - Expenditure is not deductible unless it is a commercial loss in trade - Penalty imposed is not a permissible deduction u/s. 10(2)(xv) of Income Tax Act, 1922 - Appeal dismissed.
