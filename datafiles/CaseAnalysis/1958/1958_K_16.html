Bishan Singh and Others v Khazan Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 May 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 183; AIR 1958 SC 838; 1958 SCJ 1234; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 878

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Pre-emption Act, ss. 17 and 28 - On January 6, 1951, vendees, i.e. defendants 1 and 2, and plaintiffs therein, i.e. defendants 8 to 11 (appellants in present appeal), entered into a compromise, under which, vendees admitted that they had received Rs. 1,700 from appellants and balance amount of Rs. 35,911 on 27-04-1951 - Balance amount was deposited on 23-01-1951, and appellants got possession of land on 17-05-1951 before appellants deposited amount, respondents herein, claiming to be owners of land in same patti, filed a suit, to enforce their right of pre-emption - HC upheld that part of decree of learned District Judge holding that plaintiffs were entitled to a share in suit property - (A) Whether respondents have got a statutory right u/s. 17 of Pre-emption Act to share land with appellants? - Held, provisions do not in any way enable pre-emptor to exercise his right without establishing his superior right over vendee or person substituted in his place or to prevent vendor or vendee, by legitimate means, to defeat his right by getting substituted in place of vendee a pre-emptor with a superior right to or an equal right with that of plaintiff - (B) Whether s. 28 precludes court from giving a decree for pre-emption in a case where two suits were not joined together but one of suits was decreed separately? - Further held,s. 28 enacts a convenient procedure, but it cannot affect substantative rights of parties - Court do not see that, if plaintiffs were entitled to a right of pre-emption, they would have lost it by appellants obtaining a decree before plaintiffs instituted suit, unless it be held that decree itself had effect of substituting them in place of original vendees - (C) Whether appellants having taken possession of property under decree can legitimately defeat rights of plaintiffs, who were only in position of pre-emptors of equal degree vis-a-vis appellants? - Further held, appellants right of pre-emption was clearly subsisting at time when appellants deposited amount and took possession of land, for they not only filed suit but obtained a decree therein and complied with terms of decree within time prescribed thereunder - Coercive process was still in operation - If so, it follows that appellants are not hit by doctrine of lis pendens and they acquired an indefeasible right to suit land, at any rate, when they took possession of land pursuant to terms of decree, after depositing in Court balance of amount due to vendors - Appeal allowed.
