M/S. Master Cables Private Limited v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 501; (2007) 5 SCC 416; 2007 (219) E.L.T. 41; [2008] 296 ITR 8; 2007 (3) KLT 408; 2007(7) SCALE 1; 2007 (8) SCJ 210; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 219; [2007] 162 TAXMAN 479; [2007] 7 VST 355

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Interest Tax Act, 1974, Expenditure Tax Act, 1987, Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme Rules, 1998

Summary: Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998; Finance Act, 1998; Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Legality of a notice issued by the Deputy Commissioner of Commercial Taxes vis-a -vis the provisions of the Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, 1998 - Assessment proceedings - Directed re- assessment for the year 1995-96 - Held, once it is found that a statutory authority had the jurisdiction to reopen a proceeding or set aside the order of the assessing authority, only the higher authorities can interfere therewith - Only because the appellant had taken recourse to the Scheme, the same would not attract either s. 90(3) of the Scheme or s. 91 thereof so as to cover a subject which is within the exclusive domain of the State Legislature - Appeal dismissed.
