S Chattanatha Karayalar v Central Bank of India Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 5; AIR 1965 SC 1856; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 610; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 318

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, s. 126 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 92 - Contract - Loan secured from Bank - Transaction contained in more than one document - Letter, promissory note and deed of hypothecation executed in favour of Bank by three persons including certain person Documents to be read and interpreted together - To determine - (A) That certain person's position in light of s. 126 of Contract Act - (B) Applicability of s. 92 of Evidence Act - Held, all documents read together satisfied conditions of s. 126 and that defendant 3 was surety and not co-obligant - And s. 92 did not apply - Appeal allowed.
