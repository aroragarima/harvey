N. Srinivasa v Kuttukaran Machine Tools Limited

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 208; (2009) 5 SCC 182; AIR 2009 SC 2217; 2009 (4) AWC 3837; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 112; JT 2009 (4) SC 440; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 142; 2009 (4) MPLJ 321; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 835; 2009(3) SCALE 762; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 852

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interim Measure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Held, even if an agreement ceases to exist, the Arbitration clause remains in force and any dispute pertaining to the agreement ought to be resolved according to the conditions mentioned in the Arbitration clause - High Court was not justified in setting aside the order of the trial Court directing the parties to maintain status quo in the matter of transferring, alienating or creating any third party interest in the same till the award is passed by the sole Arbitrator - Appeal partly allowed.
