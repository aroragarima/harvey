Oaramjit Kaur v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1247; (1999) SCC (Cr) 109; (1999) 2 SCC 131; AIR 1999 SC 340; 1998 (3) CCR 264; 1998 (3) Crimes 245; 1999 CRLJ 456; 1998 (2) JCC 117; JT 1998 (6) SC 338; 1998 (4) RCR(Criminal) 239; 1998(5) SCALE 219; 1998 (7) SLT 451; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 538; 1998 (7) Supreme 222

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Human Rights - Constitution, art. 32 - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1994, ss. 12, 17 , 18 and 36 (2) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Human rights - Violation - SC u/art. 32 entrusting NHRC to deal with matters - (A) Commission headed by a former Chief Justice is a unique expert body in itself - Determination of powers and jurisdiction in deciding matters referred by SC, Commission is not circumscribed by any conditions - (B) Period of limitation u/s. 36 has been expired, if it can still investigate - (C) Power of Commission to investigate when investigation by CBI already ordered - Held, matter is of a special nature not covered by enactment or law, and thus commission acts sui generies -Commission empowered to investigate - Commission empowered to investigate into complaints if filed before it in accordance with provisions of ss. 17 and 18 of 1994 Act - Petition dismissed.
