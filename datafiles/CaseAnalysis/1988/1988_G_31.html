Gulshan and Others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

G.L. Oza, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 20; (1990) Supp SCC 682; (1991) SCC (Cr) 218; AIR 1988 SC 2110; 1988 BLJR 467; 1989 CRLJ 120; 1989 CRLR 629; JT 1988 (2) SC 21A

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Sustainability, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34, 304I - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants A1 and A2 allegedly committed murder of deceased - Accused were convicted and sentenced.

Held, appellants were released on bail by orders of SC in August, 1978, about 10 years back. On the facts offence u/s. 302 IPC could not be attributed to them. Therefore, conviction of appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC could not be maintained. Appellants could only be convicted for offence u/s. 304- I and 304 -I r/w s. 34 IPC .

Sentence normally would be that of 7 years. But in the circumstances of instant case and looking to the age of the appellants and the time that has elapsed after they were released on bail sentence already undergone will meet the ends of justice. Appeal allowed.
