Maheshchandra Trikamji Gajjar v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 441; (2000) 3 SCC 295; AIR 2000 SC 1183; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 202; JT 2000 (3) SC 92; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 323; 2000(2) SCALE 295; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 195; 2000 (3) SLJ 380

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 5(1-A) and 15-B - Government allottee - Person allotted requisitioned property by Govt. for residence - Whether requisition under order of requisition D/- 17-4-1958 under Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948 is valid and legal? - Requisition cannot be for an indefinite period and continuance of an order of requisition for a period of 30 years was unreasonable - Class that is allotted, while in service, with accommodation which is requisitioned which class would get special benefit even on superannuation - This class will become tenant under original owner after retirement with benefits of all protections under Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rent Rates Control Act, 1947 - Their heirs and successors may also subject themselves to eviction only on proof of one or other ground of eviction provided in Act - Deletion of words, allowed by State Government to remain from cl. (b) of s. 5(1A) in 1997 also does not alter status of an occupant who has retired before 1996 - Word 'allowed' in aforesaid provision may mean some positive sanction and not mere slackness on part of Competent Authority in not taking action for getting premises vacated - It is evident that accommodation or possession of premises within meaning of cl. (b) by a person who when allotted was a Govt. employee has to be on account of some right to occupy or possess premises, continued occupation or possession without any such right would not confer on occupant status of a Govt. allottee simply on account of such person being in occupation or possession of requisitioned premises even after retirement - Held, continuing in occupation even after his retirement from service cannot be Govt. allottee u/s. 5(1A) and would not become deemed tenant u/s. 15-B - No right in favour of Respondent 3 to continue with possession of premises even after invalidity of order of requisition D/- 17-4-1958 and his retirement Respondent 3 cannot be treated as a deemed tenant - No justification in respondent 3 continuing with possession of premises in question - Respondent 3 is granted three months' time to vacate and hand over possession to respondent 4 - Appeal allowed.
