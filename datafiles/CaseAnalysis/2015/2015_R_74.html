Nanjappan v Ramasamy and another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 137; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 135; 2015(3) ALL MR 408; 2015 (111) ALR 212; 2015 (2) AWC 1707; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 1; 2015 (2) CTC 178; JT 2015 (2) SC 562; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2112; 2015 (1) OLR 771; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 224; 2015 (128) RD 627; 2015 (3) RLW 2187; 2015(2) SCALE 651; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 606; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 133

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 16(c), 20 - Suit for Specific performance -Maintainability - Respondents filed suit for specific performance of agreement alleging that appellant failed to execute sale deed even after receipt of part of sale consideration - Trial Court dismissed suit - Appeal before First Appellate Court, who allowed the appeal and passed decree for specific performance - Appellants filed appeal before HC, which was dismissing - Hence instant appeal - Whether lower courts were justified in decreeing suit for specific performance.

Held, recitals of agreements entered by parties had not reflected concurrent findings of First Appellate Court and HC that respondents were always ready and willing to perform their part of contract. As per recitals from second agreement, respondents were unable to pay balance sale consideration and get sale deed executed. Further, time for performance of contract was extended in total for period of 8 years and in view of passage of time that's 27 years from first agreement and escalation of value of property, grant of specific relief of performance would give unfair advantage to respondents whereas performance of contract would involve great hardship to appellant and his family members. Hence, in view of construction of residential house over suit property, sale consideration, passage of time and hardship caused to appellant, exercise of discretionary relief of specific performance could not be made and concurrent findings of First Appellate Court and HC decreeing suit for specific performance is set aside. However, appellant is directed to refund advance amount received from respondents along with interest and also pay compensation of Rs.2,00,000 to respondents. Appeal allowed.
