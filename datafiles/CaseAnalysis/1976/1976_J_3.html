Sriram Pasricha v Jagannath and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 374; (1976) 4 SCC 184; AIR 1976 SC 2335; 1977 (1) RCJ 495; 1976 RCR(Rent) 832; 1977 (1) RentLR 224; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 395; 1976 UJ 899

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 13(1 )(f) - Plaintiff, a co-sharer owner of the suit premises instituted a suit for eviction of the defendants - Whether a co-owner is as much an absolute owner as a sole owner is with reference to the interest held by him? - Held, the tenant cannot deny that the landlord had title to the premises at the commencement of the tenancy - Under the general law, in a suit between landlord and tenant the question of title to the leased property is irrelevant - Present case, on the facts found, is covered by the first part of s. 13(1 )(f), namely, where the premises are reasonably required by the landlord for his own occupation if he is the owner - Jurisprudentially it is not correct to say that a co-owner of a property is not it's owner - He owns every part of the composite property along with others and it cannot be said that he is only a part-owner or a fractional owner of the property - Position will change only when partition takes place - It is, therefore, not possible to accept the submission that the plaintiff who is admittedly the landlord and co-owner of the premises is not the owner of the premises within the meaning of s. 13(1)(f) - Appeal dismissed.
