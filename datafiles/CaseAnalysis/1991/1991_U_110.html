Mohan Kumar Singhania and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 814; (1992) SCC (L&S) 455; (1992) Supp1 SCC 594; AIR 1992 SC 1; 1992 (19) ATC 881; JT 1991 (6) SC 261; 1991 LabIC 2334; 1991(2) SCALE 565; 1992 (1) SLR 77; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 46

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Civil Services Examination Rules 1980, IAS (Appointment by Competitive Examination) Regulations 1955, Constitution (Twenty-Eighth Amendment) Act, 1972

Summary: (A) Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Civil Services Examination Rules 1980, r. 4 - IAS (Appointment by Competitive Examination) Regulations 1955, regn. 4(iiia) - Selection - Probationary training - Validity of rule - Applicant was selected for appointment to Central Service Group 'A' was held by UPSC - However, communication to that effect was sent to applicant in which applicant was drawn to r. 4 of the Rules pointing out that if he intended to appear in Civil Services (Main) Examination in 1988 he would not be allowed to join Probationary Training, along with candidates of 1987 group but would only be allowed to join Probationary Training along with candidates who would be appointed on basis of CSE 1988 - Appellants preferred instant appeals in which common question of law arose, against judgment of CAT upholding the validity of second provision to r. 4 of the Rules introduced by notification dt.13-12-1986 - Whether second proviso to r. 4 of the Rules was invalid for reason that it puts an embargo restricting candidates who were seeking to improve their position visa vis their career in Govt. service - Held, impugned second proviso to r. 4 of the Rules introduced by notification dt.13-12-1986 was legally and Constitutionally valid and sustainable in law and said proviso neither traveled beyond intent of main rule, namely, r. 4 of the Rules nor it was ultravires regn. 4 (iiia) of Regulations that it was neither arbitrary nor unreasonable and that there was a dynamic and rational nexus between impugned second proviso and object to be achieved - There is no discrimination whatsoever involved on account of introduction of second proviso in question and said proviso was not ultravires art. 14 or art. 16 of Constitution - It had become necessary to give a specific direction to respondents inclusive of UPSC in pursuance of earlier directions given in order dt.7-12-1990 which directions were given in pursuance of various interim orders passed by CAT - Order accordingly.

(B) Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16(4) - Civil Services Examination Rules 1980, rr. 4 and 6(b) - IAS (Appointment by Competitive Examination) Regulations 1955 - Civil services examination - Restriction - Constitutionality - Whether restriction imposed on number of attempts in pursuance of impugned proviso, in case of Scheduled Castes/Scheduled Tribes candidates who were since then availing any number of attempts subject to eligibility of age limit was unjustifiable and illegal and amounts to deprivation of right conferred on them by Constitution of India - Held, it was true that while substantive r. 4 of the Rules permits every candidate to appear for 3 attempts at examination which was now increased to 4 the first proviso to rule states that restriction on number of attempts at examination was not applicable in case of SC/ST candidates who were otherwise eligible - However, even in case of SC/ST candidates, there was a specific restriction so far as upper age limit was concerned as envisaged u/r. 6 (b) of the Rules - Constitution had laid a special responsibility on Govt. to protect claims of SC/ST in matter of public appointments under various Constitutional provisions of which should refer to a few - However, art. 16(4) of Constitution, as manifested from various decisions of Court was an enabling provision conferring a discretionary power on State for making any provision or reservation of appointments or posts in favour of any backward class of citizens - Hence, there was no question of severance of status as come to conclusion that restriction imposed by impugned proviso could not be said to be unjust, unreasonable or arbitrary or change of any policy and moreover, spirit of main rule was not in any way disturbed - Order accordingly.

(C) Service - Constitution - Recruitment - Rationality in recruitment - Justifiability - Whether reasons given by Govt. to justify introduction of impugned proviso had any rational nexus to object of scheme of recruitment to All India Services or/and whether such reasons were arbitrary, unfair and unjust - Held, civil service being top most service in country had got to be kept at height, distinct from other services since these top echelons have to govern a wide variety of departments - Therefore, persons joining higher service should have breadth of interest and ability to acquire new knowledge and skill since those joining service had to be engaged in multiple and multifarious activities as pointed out - In order to achieve that object, selectees of higher civil services had to undergo - Training in National Academy/ Training institutes wherein they have to undergo careful programme of specialized training as probationers - It was brought to notice of Govt. that probationers who had allocated to IPS and Group 'A' service were more often than not completely neglecting their training in academies/Training Institutes and also had gone on enmass leave thereby creating a complete vacuum in academy and Training Institutes for purpose of preparing for next CSE (Main) in hope of getting a better position and a more preferred service like IAS, IFS etc. without having a sense of involvement with service to which they had allocated and appointed on basis of earlier examination - Resultantly, matter was considered in consultation with Department of Personnel and Training and it was agreed that relevant rules should be amended so as to prevent IPS and Group 'A' probationers from joining training at academy in case they intend to take another CSE - There was a dynamic nexus between impugned second proviso and object to be achieved - Order accordingly.
