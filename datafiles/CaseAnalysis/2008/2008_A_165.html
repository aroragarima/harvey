Bharat Coking Coal Limited v Annapurna Construction

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 332; (2008) 6 SCC 732; AIR 2008 SC 2028; 2008 (3) AWC 2284; JT 2008 (4) SC 390; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 480; 2008(4) SCALE 316; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1124; 2008 (1) UC 596

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Applications Disposed Of

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Which would be the appropriate court for the purpose of filing of an award by the arbitrator? - Held, by consent of the parties jurisdiction cannot be assumed by this court - A right of appeal is a valuable right and unless there exist cogent reasons, a litigant should not be deprived of the same - It is a statutory right - This Court has no jurisdiction to entertain these applications - Applications disposed of.
