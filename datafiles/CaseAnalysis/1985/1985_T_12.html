Shambhu Charan Shukla v Thakur Ladli Radha Chandra Bmadan Gopalji Maharaj And Anr

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 197; (1985) 2 SCC 524; AIR 1985 SC 905; 1985 (11) ALR 289; 1985(1) SCALE 503; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 372; 1985 (9) ShimLC 249; 1985 UJ 802

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family and Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s.14(1) - Hindu Law - Shebaitship - No provision as to succession in endowment - Founder's widow succeeding to shebaiti right as limited owner - Becoming absolute owner u/s.14 (1) of the Act - (A) Transferability of right - Held, she can transfer that right by will - As the widow had succeeded to the shebaiti right held by him on his death as a limited owner and that right has become enlarged into an absolute right by the provisions of s. 4 (l) of the Act and she could transfer that right by a will in favour of a person who is not a non-Hindu and who could get the duties of shebait performed either by himself or by any other suitable person - (B) Interpretation of will - First sentence of the will makes an absolute bequest of shebaitship - Subsequent words only describe the rights and duties - Appeal Dismissed.
