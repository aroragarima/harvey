State of Madras v Swasthik Tobacco Factory, Vedaranyam

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 80; AIR 1966 SC 1000; 1966 (12) FLR 193; 1966 (1) MLJ(SC) 142; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 79; 1966 (17) STC 316

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939, Finance Act, 1894, Increase of Rent and Mortgage Interest (Restrictions) Act, 1920, Kerala Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Excise - Constitution of India, 1950 - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944 - Kerala Sales Tax Act - Madras General Sales Tax (Turnover and Assessment) Rules, 1939 - Whether raw tobacco was converted by a manufacturing process into chewing tobacco, a different commodity and that, therefore, u/r. 5(1) of Rules, as excise duty was paid only in respect of raw tobacco and not chewing tobacco, said duty was not deductible from turnover of assessee? - R. 5(1)(i) refers to duty paid in respect of goods to Central Govt., it necessarily refers to duty paid on goods mentioned in Schedule - As duty exempted from gross turnover is duty so paid under Central Act, r/w Schedule, expression 'in respect of' in context can only mean excise duty paid on goods - Expression 'in respect of goods' in r. 5(1)(i) means only 'on goods' - Even if word 'attributable' is substituted for words 'in respect of', result will not be different, for duty paid shall be attributable to goods - Held, if excise duty was paid by a dealer on certain goods, it would be deducted from gross turnover of dealer in regard to said goods, as otherwise, in effect, sale tax would have to be paid on amount paid towards excise duty - This concession could have no relevance if goods subjected to excise duty were different from goods sold - Duty on raw tobacco may have some effect on cost of manufactured product, but it cannot possibly be said that said duty is paid in respect of manufactured product - R. 5(1)(i) of Rules, therefore, permits deduction from gross turnover of dealer only excise duty paid by him in respect of same goods sold by him - Appeal partly allowed.
