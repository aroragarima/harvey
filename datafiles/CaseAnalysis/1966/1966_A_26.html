State of Madhya Pradesh v Azad Bharat Finance Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 106; AIR 1967 SC 276; 1967 CRLJ 285; 1967 JabLJ 153; 1967 MPLJ 14; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 473

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Opium Act, 1878, s. 11 - Possession of Narcotics Drugs - Acquittal - Sustainability - Accused(A1 to A5) were allegedly in possession of contraband articles - Trial Court convicted 3 persons and acquitted 1 person - Regarding truck, he ordered that final orders regarding disposal of truck would be passed later, on conclusion of trial of one of accused - Respondent applied for release of truck before Trial Court - Trial Court acquitted accused but ordered that truck be confiscated to State - Respondent filed a revision before HC - HC held owner of truck had neither authorised offender to transport opium, nor was there any reason to believe that owner knew that his vehicle was likely to be used for transporting contraband opium, conveyance should not be confiscated because confiscation in such circumstances would be tantamount to punishing one, who had not committed any offence under the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was sustainable - Held, HC was correct in reading s. 11 of the Act as permissive and not obligatory - It was well-settled that use of word 'shall' did not always mean that enactment was obligatory or mandatory, it depends on context in which word 'shall' occurs and other circumstances - If a statute leads to absurdity, hardship or injustice, presumably not intended, a construction might be put on it which modifies meaning of words, and even structure of sentence - Secondly, it was penal statute and it should, if possible, be construed in such a way that a person who had not committed or abetted any offence should not be visited with a penalty - S. 11 of the Act was not obligatory and it was for Court to consider in each case whether vehicle in which contraband opium was found or was being transported should be confiscated or not, having regard to all circumstances of case - Appeal dismissed.
