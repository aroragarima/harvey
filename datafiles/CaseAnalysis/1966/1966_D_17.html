Jahuri Sah and Others v Dwarka Prasad

Supreme Court of India

27 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 94; AIR 1967 SC 109; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 280

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, Bihar Act 3 1947

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Rent Control - Bihar Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1947, s. 2(2) - (A) Whether suit for partition is maintainable for non-joinder of a possible co-owner, evidence of whose adoption or otherwise by another is absent? - Adoption deed merely records fact of adoption and nothing more - Such a deed cannot be likened to a document which by its sheer force brings a transaction into existence - It is no more than a piece of evidence and failure of a party to produce such a document in a suit does not render oral evidence in proof of adoption inadmissible - Held, plaintiffs' suit for partition of their half share was not incompetent because of non-joinder - (B) Whether suit is maintainable under Act? - For s. 2(2) to apply, plaintiff and defendant should have a landlord-tenant relation - If they are co-owners of property and property is held by them as tenants-in-common no question of landlord-tenant relation arises - Each tenant-common is entitled to possession of entire property though its right to possession is limited to extent of its share - Further held, mere fact that defendants agreed to pay compensation to plaintiffs for their occupation of entire property would not bring into existence landlord-tenant relationship - Nor did parties intend to create such a relation - Act is therefore, inapplicable - (C) Whether contract under which plaintiffs claimed compensation enforceable? - No principle of law exists which excludes providing for in an agreement that those co-owners as are in actual occupation and enjoyment of property shall pay to other co-owners compensation - Further held, contract for payment of compensation is enforceable - Appeal dismissed.
