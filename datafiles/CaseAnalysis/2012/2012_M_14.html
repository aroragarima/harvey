Best Sellers Retail (India) Private Limited and another v M/S Aditya Birla Nuvo Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 615; (2012) 6 SCC 792; AIR 2012 SC 2448; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 140; 2012 (93) ALR 219; 2012 (4) AWC 4052; 2012 (2) CLT(SC) 308; JT 2012 (5) SC 357; 2013 (1) KarLJ 197; 2012 (5) MahLJ 769; 2012 (5) MLJ(SC) 435; 2012 (4) MPLJ 16; 2012 (2) OLR 376; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 857; 2012 (116) RD 778; 2012(5) SCALE 437; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 834

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 r/w. s. 51 - Specific performance - Injunction - Damages - Challenged -Respondent no. 1 was engaged in the business of readymade garments and accessories under various reputed brand names and had appointed 'X' as an agent to conduct its business of readymade garments and accessories - Thereafter, respondent no.1 entered into a fresh agreement with 'X' under which 'X' agreed to sell the products of the respondent no.1 in the suit property and also agreed to retain the possession of the suit property until the expiry of the term of agreement - Respondent no. 1 suffered huge financial losses due to various breaches of the terms and conditions of the agreement by the 'X' - Respondent no. 1 filed suit for specific performance of the agreement and in the alternative for damages for expenses and losses amounting to Rs.20,12,44,398/- if the specific performance of the agreement was refused by the Court - Respondent no. 1 also filed an application under O. 39 rr. 1 and 2 r/w. s. 151 of CPC praying for a temporary injunction restraining the 'X' from leasing, sub-leasing, alienating or encumbering the suit property in any manner pending disposal of the suit - Trial Court allowed the said application for temporary injunction - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - HC also directed that the respondent no.1 to give an undertaking that in case respondent no.1 fails in the suit, it will compensate the loss to the 'X' and its partners for not using the suit property - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether impugned order of HC could be upheld - Held, HC has held that if the impugned property was let out, the respondent no.1 will be put to hardship and the relief claimed would be frustrated and, therefore, it was proper to grant injunction and the trial court has rightly granted injunction restraining the partners of 'X' from alienating, leasing, sub-leasing or encumbering the property till the disposal of the suit - HC lost sight of the fact that if the temporary injunction restraining 'X' and its partners from allowing, leasing, sub-leasing or encumbering the suit schedule property was not granted, and the respondent no.1 ultimately succeeded in the suit, it would be entitled to damages claimed and proved before the court - Respondent no.1 will not suffer irreparable injury - Hence, the order of temporary injunction passed by the lower Courts set aside - Appeals allowed
