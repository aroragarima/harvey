People’S Union For Civil Liberties v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 344; (2005) 5 SCC 363; AIR 2005 SC 2419; 2005 (119) DLT 401; JT 2005 (5) SC 28; 2005 (3) LLN 29; 2005 (5) SLR 310; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1987

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Human Rights; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Human Rights - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14, art. 32 - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993, ss. 3(2)(d), 5-A, 8(1) - National Human Right Commission - Inclusion of a Police Officer in Commission - Appointment of respondent No 2 as a member of N H R C challenged - Ground that prior to appointment respondent was holding post of Director, Central bureau of Investigation and was also holding post of Vice-President (Asia) Interpol - Whether s 3 (2) (d) of Act requires any interpretation or a construction which would exclude Police Officers from becoming member of Commission? - Held, it is relevant to note that this section does not exclude any class of persons so long as they have knowledge of, or practical experience in, matters relating to human rights which is a requirement to be satisfied by Selection Committee - Petition dismissed.
