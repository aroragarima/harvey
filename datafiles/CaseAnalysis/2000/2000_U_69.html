East India Hotels Limited and Another v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Doraiswamy Raju, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3074; (2001) 1 SCC 284; AIR 2001 SC 231; 2000 (88) DLT 551; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 74; 2000(7) SCALE 564; 2001 (1) SCJ 146; 2000 (8) SLT 369; 2001 (121) STC 46; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 658; 2000 (7) Supreme 645

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 246(4) - Delhi Sales Tax Act, 1975, ss. 2(1), 3 and 4 - Whether appellant could be taxed for 'sales' made in his restaurants under Delhi Sales Tax Act? - When a customer goes to a restaurant and orders food and in respect of which he pays price indicated therein and said food items are supplied to him, it would clearly be a case of transfer of property in goods to customer - Moment dish is supplied and sale price paid, it would amount to a sale - Held, appellant liable to pay sales tax in respect of sales made in his restaurants - Appeal dismissed.
