Union of India v Jain Associates

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 447; (1994) 4 SCC 665; AIR 1994 SCW 2507; 1994 (1) ARBLR 494; 1995 (1) CalLT(SC) 44; JT 1994 (3) SC 303; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 551

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 20, 29, 30 and 33 - Indian Contarct Act, 1872, s. 73 - Appointment of joint arbitrators - Subsequently appointment of Umpire - Illegality in awarding damages - Non application of judicious mind - Non speaking award - Doctrine of severability - (A) Whether the HC could award interest pendente lite? - Held, HC was not right in granting interest pendente lite which the arbitrator himself had not granted - (B) Whether the umpire committed misconduct by non application of mind to the claims and counter-claims and of its consequence - Held, 'Misconduct' in s. 30(a) does not necessarily comprehend or include misconduct of fraudulent or improper conduct or moral lapse but does comprehend and include actions on the part of the arbitrator that on the face of the award are opposed to all rational and reasonable principles - The award becomes mechanical when arbitrator awards different amounts on each claim - Failing to consider counter-claim on the specious plea that it is belated counter-statement shows the non application of the mind by arbitrator - Appeal partly allowed.
