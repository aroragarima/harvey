Bajaranglal Shivchandrai Ruia v N. Shashikant Ruia and others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2103; 2004 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2004(3) SCALE 580

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (A) Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 s. 11 - Attachment of property - Principle of res-judicata - Commissioner of BMC issued warrant of attachment for recovery of property tax- Since notice of Municipal Corporation was addressed in name of dead person, it was returned unserved - Plaintiff filed suit for delivery of possession of property - One suit also filed before HC by Mahavirprasad but suddenly moved Single Judge for withdrawal of suit which was allowed - Defendant (appellant) made request that he be transposed as plaintiff in suit to enable him to prosecute suit which was rejected - Defendant filed appeal which was dismissed by Division Bench of HC- Division Bench of HC allowed plaintiffs set aside judgment and decreed suit for possession against defendant but application for speaking to minutes by defendant was not entertained- On petition moved by defendant against judgment of Division Bench interim stay was granted and petition was converted into Appeal - Defendant filed petition before HC for quashing sale made by BMC in favor of plaintiff after disclosing fact of pendency of appeal before HC - Petition was admitted by HC and was pending before HC - Appeal filed by D5 was dismissed for default in compliance with office report - Hence instant Appeal - Whether principal of res judicata applicable in case - Held, principle of res-judicata would apply to bar appeal - Leave was granted in defendants appeal while leave was granted in respondent no 5 case - Appeal of respondent no 5 was dismissed for default for non-removal of office objections - Thus, both in matter of filing petition and granting of leave defendants appeal was earlier and respondents no 5's was later in time - Order dismissing subsequent appeal for default could not operate as res-judicata in respect of earlier appeal neither s. 11 of CPC nor any principle derivable would bar appeal - Appeal allowed.

(B) Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Recovery of possession - Whether appeal should be dismissed for possible conflict of decrees - Held, respondents no. 5 own appeal failed for non-compliance with office objections could not have consequence of defeating appeal of appellant (defendant) - Reasoning of Division Bench was erroneous - Although, period of limitation prescribed in the Limitation Act, 1963, precludes plaintiff bringing suit which was barred by limitation as far as any defense, there was no such limitation - In reply to plaintiffs suit that she had derived title to suit property by virtue of auction sale and certificate of sale issued by BMC, it was open to defendants to contend to contrary - Burden of proving facts alleged in plaint was squarely upon plaintiff - If evidence showed that auction sale held by BMC was contrary to provisions of the BMC Act and Regulations made defendants were entitled to urge upon Single Judge to come to the conclusion recorded by Single Judge - Contention of respondent was misplaced - If title claimed by plaintiff was nullity and wholly void, there was no need for any of defendants to challenge it by way of substantive suit - They could always set up nullity of title as defense in any proceeding taken against them based upon such title - If sale was nullity, it was non est in eye of law and all that defendant had to do was point this out - Thus, finding of Division Bench that action of BMC in holding auction sale could not challenged appellant after withdrawal of suit and that right to challenge auction sale would not subsist in appellant by way of defense in the suit filed by plaintiff auction purchaser for recovery of possession was erroneous - Appeal allowed.

(C) Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Fraud - Whether there was fraud committed by plaintiff - Held, defendant no.8 specifically relied upon defence taken by defendant no 2 and he had also adopted and reiterated contentions urged by defendant no 2 - Thus, there were enough pleadings with regard to fraudulent manner in which property was sought to be grabbed by plaintiff and her husband who was plaintiff in suit - Single Judge pointed out as to how husband of plaintiff had connived with officers of BMC to grab suit property, which was worth lakhs of rupees for a paltry sum - Thus, conduct of plaintiffs husband and plaintiff as found from facts adduced before trial Judge, fully justify conclusions of single Judge as to fraud perpetrated - Appeal allowed.

(D) Civil Procedure - Municipalities & Local Governments - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888 s. 206 - Illegality of sale - Sale certificate - Whether there was illegality of sale conducted by BMC - Held, HC unable to accept reasoning of Division Bench that merely because certificate of sale was issued in name of plaintiff, it was conclusive of title of plaintiff and could not be impeached and it was for defendants to defend their possession - There was no provision in BMC Act or Regulations for conclusiveness of certificate of sale - Analogy drawn by Division Bench with court sale was wholly misconceived - Conclusiveness or presumption of sale was there, it could only arise if it was shown that certificate was issued strictly in accordance with s. 206 of the Act and Regulations - Where there was no compliance with law court was unable to appreciate reasoning of Division Bench that certificate of sale conveyed good title to Plaintiff - Sale was ab initio void and certificate of title was bad and null and void for complete violation of provisions of the BMC Act and Regulations - Conclusions drawn by single Judge were perfectly justified and in accordance with law - Division Bench erred on all counts in interfering with and setting aside judgment of single Judge - Judgment of Division Bench was set aside - Appeal allowed
