P. V. Sundara Rajan and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 692; (2000) 4 SCC 469; (2000) SCC (L&S) 660; AIR 2000 SC 3387; 2000 (86) FLR 59; JT 2000 (5) SC 175; 2000 LabIC 1857; 2000 (2) LLN 890; 2000(4) SCALE 48; 2000 (4) SLR 37; 2000 (5) SLT 454; 2000 (5) Supreme 83; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 1916

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Civil Pensions (Commutation) Rules, 1972, r. 37A - Dearness relief - Commuted pensioners - Entitlement - Whether those Govt. employees who have commuted 100% pension, are eligible to get dearness relief in par with other Govt. employees? - No - Dearness relief on pension has been granted to pensioners to compensate them for erosion in value of money due to rise in cost of living - What is commuted or given up is an amount and not right to receive pension or right to receive post - commutation revision and attendant benefits including dearness relief on gross entitled pension on dates they were granted to other Govt. pensioners, is only illusory - Held, those who commuted 100% pension continue to remain non - pensioners till their pension is restored - Those who commute 100% pension are not entitled to benefit of dearness relief on full pension or other benefits as claimed herein - No discrimination - Petition disposed of.
