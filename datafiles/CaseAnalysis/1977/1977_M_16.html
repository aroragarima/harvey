Mahant Bikram Dass Chela v Financial Commissioner, Revenue,Punjab, Chandigarh And Other

Supreme Court of India

03 August 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 56; (1977) 4 SCC 69; AIR 1977 SC 2221; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 262; 1977 UJ 550

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ejectment Suit

Summary: Punjab and Haryana High Court Rules and Orders, Vol.5, chap.1, rr. 4 and 5(1), chap. 2C, r. 3 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 and art.117 - Constitution, art. 133 - (A) Practice and Procedure - Since r. 3 is directory only one complete set of documents filed with memorandum of appeal within time - Remaining two sets filed in reasonable time - Whether s. 5 of Limitation Act necessary to be invoked - (B) Constitution - Discretionary Order - Order refusing to condone delay on fallacious supposition that appeal was time barred - Interference, if - Held, there was no occasion to invoke s. 5 of the Act or of r. 4 of chap. I of the Rules - Supreme Court does not normally interfere with discretionary orders, it will not stay its hands when the HC used its discretion in refusing to condone delay on an altogether fallacious supposition that the appeal was barred by time - Order Accordingly.
