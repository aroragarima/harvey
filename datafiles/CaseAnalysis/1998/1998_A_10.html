Sayyed Ali and Others v A. P. Wakf Board, Hyderabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1430; (1998) 2 SCC 642; AIR 1998 SC 972; 1998 (1) CLT 135; JT 1998 (1) SC 304; 1998(1) SCALE 277; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 398; 1998 (2) SLT 445; 1998 (3) Supreme 258

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Dedication, Charity

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Constitution - Wakf Act, 1995, ss. 5(2), 5(8), 6(4) - Andhra Pradesh Inams (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1956, s. 3 - Inam land - Wakf Property - Suit for possession - Dismissal - Sustainability - Respondent, Wakf Board, on 8-8-1967 filed suit for cancellation of various leases and sub-leases in respect of suit property claiming it as wakf property - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that inam was in favour of named individuals and property did not belong to Dargah - Before filing suit, Tahsildar made suo motu enquiry u/s. 3 of 1956 Act for the purpose of grant of pattas and by Order dt. 17-1-1967 held that suit land was inam land and was not held by any institution - Further appeal was dismissed by Revenue Divisional Officer on 21-8-1967 - On appeal, HC dismissed the same by Order dt. 22-4-1970 - Respondent, after dismissal of suit, preferred first appeal before HC which was allowed and suit was decreed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that findings recorded by HC regarding character of suit property in its judgment dt. 22-4-1970 constituted res judicata - Whether Order of HC could be sustained - Held, if there was any question as to whether particular property specified as Wakf property in the list of Wakf published under the Act, was Wakf property or not, Wakf Board or any person interested therein, might institute suit in Civil Court of competent jurisdiction for determination of the question and the decision of Civil Court in respect of such matter should be final - It was also provided therein that no such suit should be entertained by civil court after expiry of one year from date of publication of the list of Wakf u/s. 5(8) of the Act - S. 6(4) further would provide that list of Wakfs published u/s. s. 5(2) should, unless it was modified in pursuance of decision of civil court be final and conclusive - In instant case, suit property was shown as Wakf property in Andhra Pradesh Official Gazette on 30-11-1961 and no suit having been filed challenging Wakf property, entries in official gazette describing property as Wakf became final and conclusive - While making enquiry in instant case as to find out whether inam land was held by the Dargah, Tahsildar was not required to enquire into and adjudicate upon character of the Wakf property mentioned in list of Wakfs published in official gazette, as dispute in that regard as to its character could only be decided in the manner provided in s. 6 of the Wakf Act - Even if Wakf property was not found to be held by the Dargah u/s. 3 of 1956 Act, it was not open to Tahsildar to adjudicate upon character of the Wakf property - Thus, finding of Tahsildar that the property was not Wakf was wholly erroneous and beyond his jurisdiction - Consequently, finding of Tahsildar that the property was not Wakf property would not constitute res judicata in subsequent suit filed by Wakf Board - Appeal dismissed.
