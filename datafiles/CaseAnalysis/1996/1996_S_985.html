Dr. K. R. Lakshmanan and Others v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, B.L. Hansaria, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3510; (1996) 2 SCC 226; AIR 1996 SC 1153; AIR 1996 SCW 713; 1996 (1) CCC 77; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 66; 1996 CRLJ 1635; [1997] 223 ITR 601; JT 1996 (1) SC 173; 1996(1) SCALE 208; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 395

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Madras City Police Act, 1888, s. 49A - Tamil Nadu Gaming Act, 1930, s. 11 - Madras Race Club (Acquisition, Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1986 - Expression 'mere skill' in terms of s. 49A of 1888 Act and s. 11 of 1930 Act - (A) Meaning - Held, it would mean substantial degree or preponderance of skill - (B) Whether horse racing a game, where winning depends substantially and preponderantly on skill? - Is a game, where winning depends substantially and preponderantly on skill? - (C) 'Handicap horse races' - Whether changes natural horse racing from a game of skill to that of chance? - Further held, it does not change it - (D) Whether wagering or betting on horse - racing a game of skill comes within definition of 'gaming? - Further held, it does not come within definition of 'gaming' - (E) Wagering or betting on horse race - Taking place within club premises and on date on which such race is actually run on turf of club - Whether s. 49A and s. 4 would be applicable to it? - Further held, it would not be applicable to it - (F) Madras Race Club Act, Pre. s. 2 - Validity - Protection u/art. 31(c) of Constitution - Whether can be extended to Act? - It cannot be extended to Act - Reference to arts. 39(b) and (c) in aims and objects and in s. 2 of Act is nothing but a mechanical reproduction of Constitutional provisions in a totally inappropriate context - There is no nexus so far as provisions of 1986 Act are concerned with objectives contained in arts. 39(b) and (c) of Constitution - Therefore, protection u/art. 31(c) of Constitution cannot be extended to 1986 Act - (G) Madras Race Club (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act (26 of 1986), Pre., s. 2 - Race club - Acquisition and transfer under Act - Provisions as to - Are violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Policy of state Govt. as projected in all enactments on subject prior to 1985 shows that state Govt. considered horse racing as gambling and as such prohibited under law - 1985 Act on other hand declares horse racing as a public purpose and in interest of general public - There is apparent contradiction in two stands - It cannot be said that 1985 Act is a colourable piece of legislation, but at same time no public purpose is being served by acquisition and transfer of undertaking of club by Govt. - State Govt. can not acquire and take over functioning of race club when it has already enacted 1974 Act with avowed object of declaring horse racing as gambling - Having enacted a law to abolish betting on horse racing and stoutly defending same before Supreme Court in name of public good and public morality, it is not open to state Govt. to acquire undertaking of horse racing again in name of public good and public purpose - It is ex facie irrational to invoke 'public good and public purpose' for declaring horse racing as gambling and as such prohibited under law, and at same time speak of 'public purpose and public good' for acquiring race club and conducting horse racing by Govt. itself - Arbitrariness is writ large on face of provisions of 1985 Act - Therefore, provisions of 1985 Act are discriminatory and arbitrary and as such violate and infract right to equality enshrined u/art. 14 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
