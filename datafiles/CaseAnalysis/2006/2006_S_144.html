Union Of India & Ors. v Sheela Rani

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 931; (2007) 15 SCC 230; 2006 (7) AWC 7421; 2006(13) SCALE 394; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Contempt Of Court, Ad Hoc Appointment, Daily Wages, Employment Exchange

Summary: Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985 - Service - Tribunal considered the case of the respondent and the long continuous service rendered by her and held that the respondent is entitled to regularization from the date of her initial appointment - Whether services of a casual worker can be regularized with retrospective effect i.e. from the date of initial appointment? - Held, respondent's claim to be regularized from 17.11.1982 is not acceptable as she was engaged only as casual worker - Respondents who are employed on daily wages can not be treated on par with persons in regular service of the State holding similar post - If the ad hoc service is regularized from the back date in this manner, it will disturb the seniority of regularly appointed employees in the cadre and, therefore, ordinarily the regularization must take effect prospectively and not retrospectively - Appeal allowed.
