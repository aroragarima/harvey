Shri Ambica Mills Company, Limited v Shri S. B. Bhatt and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 291; AIR 1961 SC 970; 1961 (63) BomLR 496; 1961 (2) FLR 205; 1961 (1) LLJ 1; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 198; 1961 (1) SCJ 643; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. 15, 16 - Award - Agreement - Claimed Delayed wages - Appellant (Textile mill) at Ahmedabad and an award called Standardisation Award which covered appellant was pronounced by Industrial Tribunal, in Industrial Reference No. 18 of 1947, which fixed wages for different categories of workers working with appellant at Ahmedabad, but left over question of clerks for future decision, but was later considered by it and an award pronounced in that behalf but said award was later terminated by clerks in 1949, and that led to an agreement between Ahmadabad Mill Owners Association and Textile Labour Association in matter of wages payable to clerks - Cls. 2 and 5 of said agreement were material for purpose of instant appeal - Respondents moved to authority and urged that they were governed by cl. 5 of agreement and were entitled to increment provided by said clause - It was held that respondents were governed by Standardisation Award and does not fall under subsequent agreement - Aggrieved respondents appealed before District Judge who was appellate authority were dismissed - Aggrieved respondents filed petition before HC, which was allowed, and send case back to authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC was sustainable - Held, learned Attorney General had relied very strongly on decision of Bombay HC in Anthony Sabastin Almeda v. R. M. T. Taylor, 1956 Indlaw MUM 136 but SC do not proposed to express any opinion on correctness of view taken by Bombay HC on that question - Only point of dispute was do three workmen fall within category of cut-lookers or do they not, if they do then cl. 5 applies, if they do not Award would come into operation - That being so, SC did not see how decision in Anthony Sabastin Almeda v. R. M. T. Taylor, 1956 Indlaw MUM 136, could really assist appellant - In that connection SC may point out that it was common ground that in Ahmedabad textile mills do not had class of employees called cut-lookers as in Bombay and work of cut-looking along with other kind of work was done by bleach-folders and other folders - That was finding made by authority on an earlier occasion when Punamchand and Vishnuprasad had moved authority u/s. 15 of the 1936, Act - - If said finding amounts to res judicata it was in favour of two respondents and not in favour of appellant that was why learned Attorney-General did not seriously disputed correctness of decision of HC on question of res judicata - Appeal dismissed.
