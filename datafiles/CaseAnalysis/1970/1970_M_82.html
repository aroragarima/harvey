Ballabhadas Mathurdas Lakhani and Others v Municipal Committee, Malkapur

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 510; (1970) 2 SCC 267; AIR 1970 SC 1002; 1970 MLJ 561; 1970 UJ 516

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Constitution, arts.141, 276 and 276(2), Proviso - Contract Act, 1872, s. 72 - Prohibition Act - C.P. and Berar Municipal Act, 1922, s. 48(2) - CPC, 1908, Pre. - Govt. of India Act, 1935, s. 142A - Taxation - (A) Municipality collecting tax in excess of amount permitted by art. 276 of Constitution - Refund of tax illegally collected - Suit for, maintainability - (B) Provisions of art.141 of Constitution - Supreme Court decision is binding on High Court - If can be ignored for relevant provision was not brought to the notice - (C) Provision of s. 48(2) of 1922 Act - 'Bale and Boja' tax by Municipality by Notification of 1912 - Enhancement in 1940 beyond prescribed limits ( after incorporation of s. 142A in 1935 Act ) - Validity - (D) Notification of 1912 levying tax, in existence when s. 142A incorporated in 1935 Act - Notification after incorporation of s. 142A under 1922 Act - Enhancing tax - Legality - (E) Notification increasing tax after enacting s.142A of 1935, ineffective, is not revived under proviso to art. 276 (2) - Suit is maintainable - Suit for, is maintainable - Held, it cannot be ignored on ground that relevant provision was not brought to the notice of the SC - Notification held not saved by proviso to s. 142A , is ineffective and is not revived under proviso to art. 276(2) , as rate of Tax prescribed by 1912 Notification alone could be enforced so collection of tax at enhanced rate is illegal - Notification held not saved by s .142A(2) - Appeal Allowed.
