N. Padmamma and others v S. Ramakrishna Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 908; (2015) 1 SCC 417; JT 2014 (11) SC 567; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 981; 2014(11) SCALE 486; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 641

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: Hostile, Karnataka Village Offices Abolition Act, 1961, Inam Land, Inam Lands, Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition of Inams Act, 1955

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Abolition of Inams Act, 1955 - Grant of occupancy rights - Original owner was survived by his sons, namely, X and Y - Former passed away in the year 1968 leaving behind two wives, two daughters and a son - Appellants-plaintiffs were the first wife and her daughter, the defendants-respondents were the son, second wife and the daughter left behind by the deceased - Suit was filed by appellants-plaintiffs for a decree for partition of the property left behind by X - Respondents alleged that agricultural lands covered by Surveys No. 21 to 28 were inams lands which were acquired by the grandfather of respondent no.1 and enjoyed by him as holder by paying land revenue - Trial Court held that respondent no.1 was the absolute owner of property covered by Survey Nos.21-28 but had failed to establish that he was the absolute owner of the property covered by item 9-14 on the basis of the instrument of sale in his favour - Said properties were held by the Trial Court to be joint family properties partitioned - Trial Court further held that the plaintiffs-appellants were entitled to 1/5th share only in Schedule 'B' and 'C' properties - Appeal filed thereagainst was disposed of by a Single Judge with the modification that the appellants-plaintiffs would be entitled to 3/8th share in the property held jointly among the parties - Claim for allotment of a share in the inam lands over which respondent no.1 had acquired occupancy rights was held untenable as the grant of such occupancy rights was in favour of respondent no.1 in his individual capacity and not as a member of the joint family - DB affirmed the finding recorded by the Trial Court and the Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the civil court has jurisdiction to entertain a suit for partition for division of respective shares amongst the members of a joint family, when in respect of some of the lands, occupancy right has been granted in favour of one of them in terms of the provisions of the Act.

Held, demise of X, the original occupant of the land, could not on any juristic principle grant exclusivity to his son (respondent No.1) to claim the right to possession or cultivation of the land which X held in his individual capacity and which upon his demise would logically and as a matter of course devolve upon the legal heirs left behind by him in equal share. Grant of occupancy rights to respondent No.1 as the only male member of the family, could not result in the extinction of the rights of the appellants who had an equal claim in no way inferior to that of respondent No.1 to succeed to estate left behind by the deceased including succession to all such rights that may have been inchoate on the date of the demise of X but as could result in a beneficial grant in his favour based on his being an Inamdar. Further, the grant of occupancy rights in favour of respondent no.1 was for the benefit of all the legal heirs left behind by X. The judgment and order passed by the Courts below set aside to the extent the same hold that inam lands granted in favour of respondent no.1 upon abolition of the inam under the Act are not partible among the heirs left behind by X. Appeal allowed.
