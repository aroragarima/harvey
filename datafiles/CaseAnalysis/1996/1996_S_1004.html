Comorin Match Industries Private Limited v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 719; (1996) 4 SCC 281; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 11; AIR 1996 SC 1916; JT 1996 (5) SC 167; 1997 (1) MLJ(SC) 41; 1996(3) SCALE 538; 1996 (102) STC 1; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 349; 1996 (4) Supreme 23

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Validating Provision, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1969, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Gujarat Imposition Of Taxes By Municipalities (Validation) Act, 1963

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss.2(j) and 8; Constitution of India, art.245; Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s.11; Evidence Act , 1872, s.115 - 'Turnover' - Assessment under - Set aside by the High Court - Order for refund - Not carried out - Contempt petition - Order directing State to refund disputed amount - Amendment Act coming into force with retrospective effect validating assessment orders - Suit claiming amount under validated assessment orders - Held, no question of res judicata or estoppel - Cannot be challenged on ground that the legislature cannot nullify any judgment of the Court - This is a case of changing the law itself.
