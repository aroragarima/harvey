S. L. Kapoor v Jagmohan and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 287; (1980) 4 SCC 379; AIR 1981 SC 136; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 746

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Administrative - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Co-operative Societies Act, s. 77(2) - Punjab Municipal Act, 1911, ss. 12, 238(1), 11, 13, 18, 19, 56, 61, 16, 232, 236, 234 and 238 - Lt. Governor of Delhi, by a notification D/- 29-09-1979, appointed nine non-official members and four ex-officio members to New Delhi Municipal Committee to hold office for a period of one year - However, well before expiry of term committee suspended - (A) Whether rule of audi alteram partem is attracted? - Held, a committee so soon as it is constituted, at once, assumes a certain office and status and its subsequent loss would entail civil consequences so as to justify an insistence upon observance of principles of natural justice before an order of supersession is passed - S. 16 of Punjab Municipal Act expressly provided for an opportunity being given to member concerned whereas s. 238(1) did not provide for such an opportunity - It is not always a necessary inference that if opportunity is expressly provided in one provision and not so provided in another, opportunity is to be considered as excluded from that other provision - Silence of a statute has no exclusionary effect except where it flows from necessary implication - (B) Whether Committee was given an opportunity to make its representations? - Held, correspondence that passed was between Govt. of India and New Delhi Municipal Committee and not between Delhi Administration and New Delhi Municipal Committee regarding first charge - Authority competent to take action u/s. 238(1) was Delhi Administration and not Govt. of India - Based on material New Delhi Municipal Committee never put notice of any proposed action by Delhi Administration in regard to first of allegations made in impugned order - With regard to second charge Delhi Administration took to task and reprimanded New Delhi Municipal Committee - This letter cannot be construed as a notice to New Delhi Municipal Committee to come forward with its explanation - On a consideration of entire material New Delhi Municipal Committee was never put on notice of any action proposed to be taken and no opportunity was given to Municipal Committee to explain - Demands of natural justice are not met if information on which action is based is furnished in a casual way or for some other purpose - (C) Whether failure to observe natural justice does at all matter if observance of natural justice would have made no difference? - Held, where on admitted or indisputable facts only one conclusion is possible and under law only one penalty is permissible, court may not issue its writ to compel observance of natural justice, but because Courts do not issue futile writs - But it will be a pernicious principle to apply in other situations where conclusions are controversial, however, slightly, and penalties are discretionary - Requirement that justice should be seen to be done may be regarded as a general principle - It is recognition of importance of requirement that justice is seen to be done that justifies giving of a remedy to a litigant even when it may be claimed that a decision alleged to be vitiated by a breach of natural justice would still have been reached had a fair hearing been given by an impartial tribunal - Non-observance of natural justice is itself prejudice to any man and proof of prejudice independently of proof of denial of natural justice is unnecessary - Therefore, merely because facts are admitted or are indisputable it does not follow that natural justice need not be observed - Appeal allowed.
