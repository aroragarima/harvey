Bharat Damodar Kale and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 830; (2003) 8 SCC 559; (2004) SCC (Cr) 39; AIR 2003 SC 4560; 2003 (4) Crimes 420; 2003 CRLJ 4543; 2004 ILR(Kar) 174; 2003 (3) JCC 1831; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 569; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 924; 2003(8) SCALE 392; 2003 (1) SCW 5333; 2003 (6) SLT 440; 2003 (7) Supreme 736; 2004 (1) UC 339

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - ss. 468, 469, 473, 482 - Drugs and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisements) Act, 1954 - s. 8 - Cognisance of offence - Limitation - Competency of Drug Inspector under the Notification issued by the Government of Andhra Pradesh under the provisions of the Drugs and Magic Remedies (Objectionable Advertisements) Act, 1954 to initiate a complaint - Held, the notification in question is applicable to the entire State of A.P. and the complainant in this case had the necessary authority to seize and detain any material which would indicate the commission of an offence under the Central Act of 1954 as also to file a complaint as has been done in this case - Further held, applying the interpretation of Chapter XXXVI of the Code to the facts of the case, the complaint was well within the period of limitation.
