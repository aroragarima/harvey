Commnr. Of Central Excise, Chandigarh v M/S. Pepsi Foods Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1078; (2011) 1 SCC 601; 2010 (260) E.L.T. 481; JT 2010 (13) SC 544; 2010(13) SCALE 274

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Normal Price, Place Of Removal

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 4 and 11AC - Assessable value - Related person - Cost of freight between factory to depot of related person - (A) Whether cost of freight between factory to depot of related person to be included in the assessable value of impugned goods determined u/s. 4(2) of 1944 Act? - Held, SC in CCE, Belgaum vs. Akay Cosmetics Private Ltd. 2005 INDLAW SC 231, held that u/s. 4 (2) of 1944 Act, it was provided that where the price of the excisable product for delivery at the place of removal was not known and the value was determined with reference to the price for delivery at a place other than the place of removal, the cost of transportation from the place of removal to the place of delivery had to be excluded from such a price - Section 4 (2) of 1944 Act is a residuary section and applied only to cases where the price at the place of removal was not known and the taxable value of the excisable product had to be determined with reference to the price for delivery (sale) at a place other than the price of removal - Under s. 4 (2) of 1944 Act, the cost of transportation from the place of removal to the place of delivery was deductible, provided that the assessable value (taxable value) was not known at the factory gate and had to be determined with reference to another place - In the present case, contention of the assessee that it is justified in claiming exclusion of freight charges arising between the factory gate of the assessee to the depot of the related person, cannot be sustained as assessee himself admitted that the sale of the products occurred at its factory gate - Section 4(2) of 1944 Act does not apply in the present case - Hence, cost of freight between factory to depot of related person to be included in the assessable value of impugned goods - Impugned order of Tribunal set aside and order-in-original restored - (B) Whether penalty u/s. 11AC of 1944 Act could be imposed on assessee as it not included the freight charges in the assessable value of the goods? - Held, in order to attract the penalty u/s. 11 AC of 1944 Act, criminal intent or 'mens rea' is a necessary constituent - In the present case, in the reply to the show cause notice the stand which has been taken by the assessee is that it has been paying the duty and there is no malafide intention on its part to evade the payment of duty - This stand was not found to be incorrect in the order-in-original - As such the imposition of penalty of the equal amount of duty under the order-in-original cannot be sustained - Revenue's appeals partly allowed.
