Income Tax Officer Kolar and Another v Seghu Buchiah Setty

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 215; AIR 1964 SC 1473; [1964] 52 ITR 538; 1964 (1) SCJ 683; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1922 - Revenue Recovery Act, 1890 - Notices of demand u/s.29 of Act were issued in respect of dues - Assessee tiled appeals to Appellate Assistant Commissioner against assessment orders but did not pay tax as demanded by notices - On such failure to pay, the Income-tax Officer sent certificates to Deputy Commissioner u/s.46(2) of Act for recovery of the tax as arrears of land revenue and latter in the course of the same month attached various properties of the assessee under Act - Thereafter appeals filed by assessee which were till then pending were decided by Appellate Commissioner - He reduced the assessable income of the assessee to Rs.27,000/- for year 1953-54 and to Rs.45,000/- for year 1954-55 and directed Income-tax Officer to re compute the tax on basis of reduced income and to refund excess if any collected - Income-tax Officer informed assessee that his tax liability for 1953-54 150 had reduced to Rs.4,215-9-0 Rs.13,346-8-0 and called upon him to pay these amounts at once into the local treasury - Assessee filed further appeals against orders of Appellate Commissioner and asked that the recovery proceedings might be stayed pending decision of these appeals - HC observed invalid and quashed - Revenue authorities appealed to Court against that decision - Hence instant appeal - Whether proceedings for recovery of tax from the assessee under Act were invalid - Held, when tax, penalty or interest was determined and demanded, proceedings shall be commenced for recovery, and these proceedings may be commenced and continued, notwithstanding the presentation of an appeal - By failing to comply with the demand assessee becomes a defaulter, and it was not provided that he shall cease to be a defaulter on the disposal by appellate authority of appeal against order of assessment - In absence of any provision imposing an obligation upon Income-tax Officer to issue successive notices of demand from time to time for recovery of amount due during process of assessment - Notices of demand issued by Income-tax Officer in exercise of the power u/s.29 may be enforced in the manner provided by s.46 and within the period of limitation provided in cl.(7) of s.46, even after the appeal against the order of assessment by Income tax Officer was disposed of, subject to adjustment of the amount to be recovered in light of order of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Petition filed by respondent will stand dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
