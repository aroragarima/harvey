Ram Pyare Mishra v Prem Shanker and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1362; (2008) 14 SCC 614; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 1132; AIR 2009 SC 552; 2008 (4) JCC 2391; JT 2008 (9) SC 263; 2008(11) SCALE 607; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence, Faecal Matter, Civil Case, Blunt Injury, Non-Explanation, Right Of Self Defence, Injuries On Accused

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Appeal against acquittal - Right of self defence - Held, it is for the accused to place necessary material on record either by himself adducing positive evidence or by eliciting necessary facts from the witnesses examined for the prosecution - An accused taking the plea of the right of private defence is not necessarily required to call evidence; he can establish his plea by reference to circumstances transpiring from the prosecution evidence itself - Private defence exceeded - Respondents were convicted of offence punishable under s. 304 Part I - Appeals partly allowed.
