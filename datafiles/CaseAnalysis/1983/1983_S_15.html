Punjab Engineering College Etc. Etc. v Sanjay Gulati And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 89; (1983) 3 SCC 517; AIR 1983 SC 580; 1983 ALJ 1220; 1983 (9) ALR 411; 1983(1) SCALE 404; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 801; 1983 UJ 439

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution - Whether sixteen writ petitioners should be admitted to those sixteen seats or whether admission to those seats should be strictly in accordance with merit? - Held, following decisions in cases like State of Kerala v. Kumari T. P. Roshana, Arti Sapru v. State of Jammu and Kashmir &Others, it is directed that admission to the 16 additional vacancies for the academic year 1983-84 shall be made in accordance with merit on the basis of the lists which were prepared for the academic year 1982-83 for the Chandigarh group of students and the general group of students - Appeals disposed of.
