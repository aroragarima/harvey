Kiriti Pal and others v State of West Bengal and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 904; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2066; 2015 CRLJ 3152; 2015 (3) JCC 1559; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 617; 2015(5) SCALE 319; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 726

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Life Imprisonment, Conspiracy, Confessional Statement, Conversation, Telephonic Conversation, Subsequent Conduct

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25(i)(a), 25(i)(a)(b) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302, 376(2)(g) - alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - It is alleged that on day of incident , deceased was murdered by accused nos. 1 to 3 - FIR was lodged against appellant accused persons - Trial Court convicted appellant accused A1 to A4 u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced them to death - Appellants A1 to A4 were also convicted for offence u/s. 376(2)(g), 120B of IPC and s. 25(1)(a), (b) of Act - HC partly allowed appeal by setting aside conviction u/s. 376(2)(g) of IPC and for conviction u/s. 302 of IPC modified sentence of death to life imprisonment - Whether impugned order passed by HC against appellant nos. 1 to 4 is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to involvement of accused no.1 and his conviction, SC held that from evidence of PWs 6, 7 and 10, prosecution has thus established that deceased was last seen alive in company of appellant no.1. Further, appellant no.1 has failed to discharge onus and failed to offer any explanation as to how, as to when and how and in what manner he parted company of deceased. Further, subsequent conduct of appellant no.1 threatening appellant no.4 and PW7 is relevant circumstance as such conduct of accused indicates his nexus with offence. Further, recovery at instance of appellant no.1 accused is yet another incriminating circumstance in chain which leads to conclusion pointing to guilt of appellant no.1. SC held that circumstances relied upon by prosecution against appellant no.1 are well established by prosecution. Hence, upon appreciation of evidence, Trial court and HC rightly held that incriminating circumstances against appellant no.1 are firmly established and form complete chain pointing to guilt of accused . Hence, conviction and sentence u/s. 302 of IPC and 120B of IPC and u/s. 25(i)(a), (b) of Act imposed on appellant no.1 by HC is confirmed. Further, with respect to involvement of A2, A3 and A4 and their conviction, SC held that apart from telephonic conversation, no other evidence was adduced by prosecution to bring home that accused no.1 hatched conspiracy. There is no evidence to prove as to how appellant nos. 2 and 3 had gone to place of occurrence and what was their subsequent conduct. SC held that evidence adduced by prosecution against appellant nos. 2, 3, 4 do not form complete chain connecting accused with crime and conviction of appellants u/s. 302 of IPC read with s. 120B of IPC cannot be sustained and is set aside. Appellant nos. 2 to 4 are acquitted of all charges against them .Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Conviction of accused is sustained when it is based on evidence on record and prosecution should prove guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt.
