Ramji Rai and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 773; (1999) 8 SCC 389; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1446; AIR 1999 SC 3857; 2000 CRLJ 19; JT 1999 (8) SC 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Arms Act, 1959,s.27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ,ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 323, 324, 326, 307, 302, 380 ,452 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants /accused formed unlawful assembly and armed with deadly weapons like Kharia, Pharsa, Garasa, guns, rifles, sword, lathi etc. killed 3 persons and another 3 persons were grieviously injured - PW 16 son of deceased filed FIR and police chargesheeted 35 accused persons - Prosecution had examined 17 witnesses - Trial Court relying on evidence convicted 4 accused with capital punishment of death and convicted 21 accused on different provisions and 10 accused were acquitted on benefit of doubt - Aggrieved convicted accused filed appeal in HC and reference was made by Trial Court to confirm death sentence to 4 accused - HC dismissed appeals of convicted accused and rejected reference and modified it into life sentence - Hence instant appeals - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, prosecution proved its case by examining as many as 17 eye witnesses and also corroborated by medical evidence - Perused evidences and it could not be doubted that they were not eye witnesses and Trial Court had rightly relied them and convicted accused concluding that they had formed unlawful assemble with common object to cause death of deceased and murdered deceased brutally and also looted their property which was rightly confirmed by HC - Regarding death punishment Trial Court had given reasons but HC did not give any reasons in rejecting death sentence - Well settled principle of law was that penalty of death should be awarded only in rarest of rare cases when Court felt that lesser sentence of life imprisonment would not meet ends of justice - SC in Gentela Vijayavardhan Rao and Anr. v. State of A.P, 1996 Indlaw SC 2361 after considering law laid down by its earlier judgments held that no litmus were provided nor any test formulated to discern precisely what was rarest of rare case in which alternative option of awarding life sentence was foreclosed - Trial court reasons for awarding death sentence were not in accordance with principle laid down by SC in Machhi Singh v. State of Punjab,1983 Indlaw SC 116 in which SC laid down questions to be put and answered by Court before awarding death sentence - Instant case would not fall under rearest of rare cases to award death punishment and HC was justified in modifying it to life imprisonment - Conviction and sentences passed by Trial Court and HC were upheld - Appeals dismissed.
