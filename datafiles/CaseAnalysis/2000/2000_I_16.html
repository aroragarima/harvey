Indian Hotels Company Limited and Others v Income Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, Ruma Pal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 787; (2000) 7 SCC 39; AIR 2000 SC 2645; 2000 (162) CTR 310; [2000] 245 ITR 538; JT 2000 (9) SC 139; 2001 (34) LabIC 2142; [2000] Supp2 S.C.R. 261; [2000] 112 TAXMAN 46

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961 - S. 80J(4)(iii) - Taj Flight Kitchen held not to be a hotel activity of the assessee by tribunal - It would not come within the purview of s. 80J(6) which provides for approval by the Central Govt. - Whether the assessee entitled to benefit of s. 80J of Income Tax Act, 1961 - Held, no - Business of hotel and industrial undertaking separate provision prescribed u/s. 80J of the Act - Assessee carrying on trading activity of business of hotel cannot claim benefit granted to industrial undertaking merely because it produces foodstuff or food packets - Assessee not entitled to get benefit of s. 80J of the Act - Disposed accordingly.
