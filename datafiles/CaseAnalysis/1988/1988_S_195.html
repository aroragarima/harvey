Appabhai and Another v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 54; (1988) Supp SCC 241; (1988) SCC (Cr) 559; AIR 1988 SC 696; 1988 (1) Crimes 606; 1988 CRLJ 848; 1988 CRLR 178; JT 1988 (1) SC 249; 1988(1) SCALE 228; 1988 (2) UJ 27

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - (A) Grant of special leave - Scope - For parties, to contend - (B) Murder - (1) Effect of failure of prosecution to examine independent witnesses - (2) Credibility of evidence when there were contradictions in evidence of victim of assault - Held, parties cannot open out and argue whole case or contest all findings of Court below except substantial and grave injustice is done - Prosecution case cannot be thrown out on that ground alone - Further held, that is no ground to reject his entire testimony - Appeals allowed.
