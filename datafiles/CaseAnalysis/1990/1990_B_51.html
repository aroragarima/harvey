Vasantkumar Radhakisan Vora v Board Of Trustees Of The Port Of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 387; (1991) 1 SCC 761; AIR 1991 SC 14; JT 1990 (3) SC 609; 1990 (2) RentLR 271; 1990(2) SCALE 297; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879, Bombay Presidency Small Cause Courts Act 1882

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 109, 106 and 111(h) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Major Port Trust Act - Eviction - Tenants of Bombay Port Trust - (A) Transfer of right to successor-in-interest - Notice terminating lease - And Major Port Trust Act made applicable prior to expiry of notice period - Right to evict to Board of Trustees - Competency of suit - (B) Promissory estoppel - Representation to tenants by Estate Manager of Port Trust that on deposit of certain amount they would be allotted flats after reconstruction of building - (C) New plea, raised for first time u/art. 136 of Constitution - Claimed pure question of law going to roots of jurisdiction - Held, suit is competent as transfer by operation of law, so spirit behind s. 109 would apply - Ultra vires act, no promissory estoppel u/s. 115 of Evidence Act, though executive necessity not established - Plea not allowed to be raised being mixed question of law and fact - Appeal dismissed.
