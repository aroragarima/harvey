Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Gillanders Arbuthnot and Company

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 280; (1973) 3 SCC 845; AIR 1973 SC 989; 1973 (2) CTR 136; 1973 (1) ITJ 527; [1973] 87 ITR 407; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 437; 1973 TAXLR 167

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 12B and 34(1)(a) - (A) Whether proceedings u/s. 34(1)(a) have been validly initiated? - The Income tax officer had reason to believe that the assessee had not disclosed all the material facts necessary for its assessment - Held, the proceedings were validly initiated - (B) Whether any capital gains u/s. 12B arise by transaction involving transfer of the investments to the 'Co.' and admission of the 'Co.' as partner in assessee-firm and issue of shares of the Co. to the public? - The substance of the transaction was that there was sale of shares and securities and not merely a readjustment - Held, capital gain u/s. 12B arise only to transfer of investments to the Co. - Appeals dismissed.
