Railway Board, Representing The Union Of India, New Delhi and v Niranjan Singh

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 44; (1969) 1 SCC 502; AIR 1969 SC 966; 1969 CRLJ 1368; 1969 (18) FLR 300; 1969 LabIC 1368; 1969 (2) LLJ 743; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 548

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1) - Show Cause Notice - Dismissal from service - Maintainability - Respondent was holding permanent post in Northern Railways - Two charges were levied against him on 07-11-1956 - Show cause notice was issued and asked to submit his explanation - Enquiry committee was constituted to enquire into charges - Respondent submitted his explanation - Disciplinary Committee did not accept his explanation and by order of 20-08-194-57 he was removed from service - Respondent challenged decision before HC which was allowed - Aggrieved by said order appeal was made before DB which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether direction issued by Authority was violative of arts. 19(1)(a) to 19(1)(c) and 19(3) - Held, rule laid down in Marsh case does not apply to facts of this case - Further, premises which are concerned in this appeal unlike the roads and sidewalks of Chickasaw town were not open for use of general public and they were intended for certain specified public purposes - Therefore, they could not be used for any other purposes except with permission of concerned authority - Thus, neither language of art. 19(1) of Constitution nor purpose behind it lends support to contentions of respondent and their acceptance might lead to confusion in public offices - Appeal allowed.
