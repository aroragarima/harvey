R.S Dass Etc. Etc. v Union Of ’India And Ors.,

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 220; (1986) Supp SCC 617; AIR 1987 SC 593; 1987 (2) ATC 628; JT 1986 SC 1043; 1987 LabIC 476; 1986(2) SCALE 1012; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 527; 1987 (2) SLJ 55; 1986 (4) SLR 75; 1987 (1) UJ 150

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - All India Services Act, 1951, s. 3 - Indian Administrative Service (Recruitment) Rules, 1954, rr. 4 and 8 - Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, Regn. 5 - Constitution, arts. 14, 16, 226, 311 and 320 - Promotion of Members of State Civil Service to IAS - Merit - Validity of list selecting juniors in preference to seniors - Challenged on the grounds - (A) Regn. 5 , recording of reasons not necessary - (B) Failure of State Govt. to forward reasons u/regn. 6 to UPSC - (C) Applicability of principles of natural justice - (D) Method of selection, if unreasonable - (E) Arbitrariness in categorisation of Officers of State Service - (F) Alleged delay in forwading list with view to grant favour to officer promoted - Ancillay question of vires- Whether Regns. 3, 5, 7 of the 1955 regulation are ultra vires r. 8 of the 1954 Rules - Held, amended regn 5 is not violative of arts. 14, 16 - Requirement of giving reasons cannot be read into on basis of principles of natural justice - Not applicable to all cases - Select list is not vitiated on ground of failure to forward reasons - That cannot be characterised as unreasonable merely because service records contain remarks which are sometimes not fully justified - Categorisation not at free will, regns. 3, 5 are not discriminatory - He also found to be discharging duties of Development Commr. - Not ultravires , regulations do not impinge upon power of State Govt. to make recommendations to Central Govt. as per r. 8 - Appeal Dismissed.
