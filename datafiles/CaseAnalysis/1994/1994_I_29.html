Svenska Handelsbanken and Others v Messrs Indian Charge Chrome Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1274; (1994) 2 SCC 155; 1994 (1) ARBLR 205; 1994 (1) CCC 252; 1994 (78) CLT 1; JT 1994 (1) SC 240; 1994(1) SCALE 156; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 261

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; International; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award, Enforcement Of Foreign Judgments/ Decrees

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 34 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, ss. 3 and 9(b) - Applications filed by set of defendants for stay of suit filed by plaintiff invoking s. 3 of Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, dismissed by Trial Court - Civil revision dismissed by Single Judge of High Court - Whether the defendant complied with s. 3 of Foreign Awards Act, 1961? - Held, stay of suit could be granted notwithstanding cl. 18.03 for simple reason that agreement to submit to jurisdiction to court under cl. 18.03 relates to maintainability of suit in a court agreed to by both parties, but does not affect question whether proceedings should be stayed in view of arbitration clause - Plaintiff may well elect to have dispute decided in court or it may apply u/s. 3 of Foreign Awards Act, 1961 - Thus no obstacle in applications filed on behalf of defendant 4 and defendants 5 to 11 for staying suit filed by borrower/plaintiff under provisions of s. 3 of Foreign Awards Act, 1961 - Appeal allowed.
