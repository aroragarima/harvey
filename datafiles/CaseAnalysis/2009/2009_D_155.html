Kurapati Maria Das v Dr. Ambedkar Seva Samajan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 529; (2009) 7 SCC 387; AIR 2009 SC 2475; 2009 (2) AWC 1966; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 1030; 2009(6) SCALE 84; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 498

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Election Dispute, Scheduled Caste, Reserved Constituency, Reserved For Scheduled Caste, Andhra Pradesh (Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Backward Classes) Regulation of Issue of Community Certificates Act, 1993, Andhra Pradesh Municipalities (Decision on Election Disputes) Rules, 1967, Jurisdiction Of The High Court

Summary: Election - Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Andhra Pradesh Municipalities (Decision on Election Disputes) Rules, 1967, r. 1 - Constitution of India, 1950, Part IXA, art. 243ZG(b) - Election petition - Municipal Elections - Jurisdiction of High Court u/art. 226 - Ward elections for Councilor - Reserved for Scheduled Castes - Dispute as to caste - Writ petition file before High Court - Petition accepted, judgement delivered by Court - Division Bench further confirming the judgement - Creation of Bar u/art. 243ZG(b) - Bar whether absolute or whether HC had rightly exercised its jurisdiction u/art. 226? - Contentions heard - Provision of art. 243ZG(b) perused - Finding, article is couched in negative terms, bar created thereunder is absolute; 'Jaspal Singh Arora v. State of M.P. [1998 (9) SCC 594]' - Undisputed is the position that r.1 of Rules specifically provides for such disputes - Court noted that writ petition though termed as petition for writ of quo warranto, was in fact regarding challenge to the election - Held, art. 243ZG(b) attracts - Impugned judgements set aside - Writ petition dismissed - Appeal allowed.
