Satni Bai v State of Madhya Pradesh (Now Chhattisgarh)

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 70; (2010) 2 SCC 646; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 960; 2010 CRLJ 1459; 2010 (1) JCC 690; JT 2010 (1) SC 560; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 812; 2010(1) SCALE 694; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 186; 2010 (1) UC 385

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Conviction - Circumstantial evidence - Appellant was convicted u/s. 302, IPC for killing her own son with an axe - On appeal, the conviction was upheld by the HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Circumstantial evidence - Held, conviction can be based solely on circumstantial evidence, but it should be tested as per law relating to circumstantial evidence laid down by SC - In present case, it was proved beyond reasonable doubt that the appellant was standing with the blood stained axe near the dead body of the deceased - Post mortem report coupled with the testimony of the witnesses presents unerringly points towards the guilt of the appellant - (B) False implication - Held, there was no question of falsely implicating the appellant - Witnesses were her close relatives and had no enmity nor animosity against the appellant - Findings of the DB of the HC holding the appellant guilty of the offence u/s. 302, IPC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
