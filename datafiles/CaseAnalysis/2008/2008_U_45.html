Farhd K. Wadia v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1931; (2009) 2 SCC 442; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 174; 2009 (1) AWC 856; 2009 (4) Bom.C.R. 448; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 193; JT 2008 (12) SC 534; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 502; 2009(1) SCALE 293; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 140

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Noise, Suo Motu Cognizance, Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000

Summary: Noise Pollution (Regulation and Control) Rules, 2000 - Whether musical functions in an open theatre being Rang Bhavan should be allowed to be carried on or not despite the fact that it is situate within 100 meters of an educational institution and a hospital? - Held, an independent public interest litigation to obtain a relief which would be contrary to and inconsistent with the order of injunction passed by the court was not maintainable - Held, interference by the court in respect of noise pollution is premised on the basis that a citizen has certain rights being 'necessity of silence', 'necessity of sleep', 'process during sleep' and 'rest', which are biological necessities and essential for health - It is considered to be one of the human rights as noise is injurious to human health which is required to be preserved at any cost - State Government is bound by the order of Supreme Court besides the order passed by the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
