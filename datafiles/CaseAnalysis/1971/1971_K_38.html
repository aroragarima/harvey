Krishna Biharilal v Gulabchand Am Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 146; (1971) 1 SCC 837; AIR 1971 SC 1041; 1973 ALJ 94; [1971] S.C.R. 27; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 27; 1971 UJ 492

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Constitution , art. 136 - (A) Deed - Rule of construction - (B) Evidence Act, 1872, s.115 - If a person having full knowledge of his rights as a possible reversioner enters into a compromise which settles his claim as well as the claim of the opponent at the relevant time - (C) Constitution - Issue raised in the suit is broad enough to cover the plea of estoppel and that plea has been argued and considered by the lower Courts without any objection from the plaintiffs -Special leave petition u/art.136 - Contention that specific plea of estoppel not taken - (D) Hindu Law - Family arrangement - Whether parties to the compromise should all belong to one family - Ordinary rule of construction of a document is to give effect to the normal and natural meaning of the words employed in the document - Held, he cannot be permitted to go back on that arrangement when reversion actually opens - They cannot be permitted in an appeal by special leave, to contend that the defendant had not taken any specific plea of estoppel - To consider a settlement as a family arrangement, it is not necessary that the parties to the compromise should all belong to one family - Appeal Allowed.
