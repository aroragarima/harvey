State of Maharashtra v Champalal Punjaji Shah

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 4; (1981) 3 SCC 610; (1981) SCC (Cr) 762; AIR 1981 SC 1675; 1981 CRLJ 1273; 1981 CrLR(SC) 496; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1661; 1981(3) SCALE 1161; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 299

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution, art. 21 and 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Customs Act, 1962, s. 135 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 126P(2)(ii) and 126P(2)(iv) - CrPC, 1973, s. 386 - Offences u/s. 120 IPC, 1860 r/w s. 135 Customs Act and r. 126 P(2) (ii) & (iv) of Defence of India Rule - (A) Constitution - (1) Delayed trial is not necessarily an unfair trial - Powers of appellate Court as to quashing of conviction on ground of delayed trial only - (2) Appeal against acquittal - High Court ordered acquittal by reversing the conviction - (B) Evidence Act - Circumstantial evidence - Acceptability of exaggerated explanation - (C) Offence jeopardises economy of country - Light view of the offence - (A) Not open - Explanations fancied by High Court , wholly unreasonable, miscarriage of justice , held judgment of HC must be interfered with - (A) Need not be accepted - (C) Not to be taken - Delay in trial notwithstanding - Appeal Allowed.
