U.T. Chandigarh Administration & Anr. v Amarjeet Singh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 329; (2009) 4 SCC 660; AIR 2009 SC 1607; 2009 (2) AWC 2073; 2009 (1) CCC 97; 2009 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2009 (4) SC 135; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1058; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1399; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 401; 2009(3) SCALE 892; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Public Authorities, Non Compliance, Allotment Of Site, Chandigarh Leasehold Sites & Building Rules, 1973, Punjab Urban Estates Sale of Rights Rules, 1965

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Issue with regards to auction of 74 residential sites and 71 commercial sites in different sectors of Chandigarh, on leasehold basis subject to the General Terms and Conditions regarding auction - Held, Central Government is required to provide amenities by levying fees and taxes in respect of sites/plots on the transferees/ occupiers thereof - It is doubtful whether any proportionate reduction in penal/default interest could be made on the ground of non-provision of amenities - Rate of default interest mentioned in r. 12(3A) of Leasehold Rules as on the date of auction, would alone apply - If r. 12(3A) was not amended increasing the rate of default interest from 12% P.A. to 24% per annum as on the date of auction, then the rate of interest stipulated in r. 12(3A) as it stood on the date of auction will apply - Appeals disposed of.
