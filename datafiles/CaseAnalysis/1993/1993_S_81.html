State of Bihar and Others v Secretariat Assistant Successful Examinees Union 1986 and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 540; (1994) 1 SCC 126; (1994) SCC (L&S) 274; AIR 1994 SC 736; 1994 (26) ATC 500; 1994 (1) CLR 181; 1994 (68) FLR 73; JT 1993 (6) SC 462; 1994 LabIC 676; 1994 (1) LLJ 625; 1993(4) SCALE 38; 1993 (5) SLR 598; 1994 (1) UJ 235; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 951

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appointment - Empanelled candidates - Right of - Whether High Court decision to fill up of vacancies upto 1991 from list of selected candidates who had been empanelled after declaration of result in 1990 is correct? - Being empanelled alone, acquire any indefeasible right of appointment - Empanellment is at best a condition of eligibility for purposes of appointment, and by itself does not amount to selection or create a vested right to be appointed unless relevant service rule says to contrary - Directions given by HC for appointment of empanelled candidates according to their position in merit list against vacancies till 1991 were not proper and cannot be sustained - Appeal disposed of.
