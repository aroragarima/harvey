V. L. Tresa v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20291; (2001) 3 SCC 549; (2001) SCC (Cr) 573; AIR 2001 SC 953; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 80; 2001 (1) CCR 202; 2001 (1) Crimes 240; 2001 CRLJ 1171; 2001 (1) JCC 169; JT 2001 (2) SC 515; 2001 (1) KLT 781; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2001(2) SCALE 14; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 973; 2001 (2) SLT 74; 2001 (1) Supreme 654; 2001 (2) UC 5

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Deceased was husband of appellant - His death occurred during course of arguments with appellant over sale of property - Appellant convicted for concealing evidence - Death of deceased was due to injury on head caused by a crowbar - Crowbar with which injury was caused to deceased was concealed by appellant - Conviction challenged - Ingredients for offence u/s. 20: (i)committal of an offence; (ii) person charged with the offence u/s. 201 must have the knowledge or reason to believe that main offence has been committed; (iii) person should have caused disappearance of evidence or should have given false information regarding main offence; and (iv) act should have been done with intention of screening the offender from legal punishment - Held, yes - S. 201 prescribes punishment for concealing evidence or giving false evidence - Conviction supported by conduct of appellant - Deliberate concealment of crowbar by appellant - Conviction u/s. 201 upheld even if appellant was acquitted offence of murder - Appeal dismissed.
