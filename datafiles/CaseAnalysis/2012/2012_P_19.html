Ponnala Lakshmaiah v Kommuri Pratap Reddy and others

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 179; (2012) 7 SCC 788; AIR 2012 SC 2638; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 7; 2012(4) ALL MR 531; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 22; 2012 (6) MLJ(SC) 218; 2012 (3) RCR(Civil) 770; 2012(6) SCALE 207; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 851

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 81, 82 and 117 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 94 (A) - Election petition - Petitioner challenged the order of the HC whereby HC held that the election petition filed by respondent against the petitioner who happens to be the successful candidate in the election to the Assembly Constituency, disclosed a cause of action and could not therefore be dismissed at the threshold - Petitioner submitted that the election petition was liable to be dismissed as that the same was not accompanied by an affidavit which the respondent was obliged to file in terms of proviso to s. 83 (1) of the Act - Petitioner also submitted that the use of the word 'shall' in the proviso made it mandatory for the respondent to support the averments in the election petition with an affidavit in Form 25 prescribed u/r. 94 (A) of the Rules - Respondent contended that the non-filing of an affidavit in terms of proviso to s. 83(1) of the Act was never taken as a ground before HC, therefore, petitioner cannot urge that point before SC for the first time - Respondent further contended that even assuming that there was any deficiency in the affidavit sworn by the respondent, yet the same was not fatal to the election petition inasmuch as the Court trying the petition can at any stage of the proceedings direct the respondent to file a proper affidavit if it finds that the one already filed was deficient in any way - Whether HC was right in holding that the election petition filed by respondent against the petitioner, disclosed a cause of action and could not therefore be dismissed at the threshold - Held, the requirement of filing an affidavit along with an election petition, in the prescribed form, in support of allegations of corrupt practice was contained in s. 83(1) of the Act - Non-compliance with the provisions of s. 83 of the Act, however, does not attract the consequences envisaged by s. 86(1) of the Act - In the absence of any provision making breach of the proviso to s. 83(1) of Act, a valid ground of dismissal of an election petition at the threshold, SC see no reason why the requirement of filing an affidavit in a given format should be exalted by a judicial interpretation to the status of a statutory mandate - A petition that raises triable issues need not, therefore, be dismissed simply because the affidavit filed by the respondent was not in a given format no matter the deficiency in the format has not caused any prejudice to the successful candidate and can be cured by the respondent by filing a proper affidavit - Appeal dismissed.
