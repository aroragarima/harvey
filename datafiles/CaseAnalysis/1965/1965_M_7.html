Her Highness Maharani Mandalsa Devi and Others v M. Ramnarain Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 116; AIR 1965 SC 1718; 1966 (68) BomLR 31; 1966 MahLJ 273; 1966 MPLJ 289; 1965 (2) SCJ 853; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 421

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.21, r.50(2) and O.30 r/w ss. 86 and 87 - (A) Whether decree obtained under O. 30 of CPC, 1908 against firm is a decree against partners? - Held, yes - A suit by or in name of a firm is really a suit by or in name of all its partners - Decree passed in suit, though in form against firm, is in effect a decree against all partners - Such a decree may be executed against property of partnership and against all partners by following procedure of O. 21, r. 50 of CPC, 1908 - (B) Whether decree can be passed u/s. 86 r/w s. 87 B against a ruler of former Indian state? - Further held, it cannot - When suit was instituted without consent of central Govt. as required by s. 86 r/w s. 87 B no decree could be passed in suit against him - But suit against firm other than ruler was competent and a decree could be passed against firm other than ruler and such a decree could be executed against partnership property and against other partners by following procedure of O. 21 ,r. 50 of CPC, 1908 - Appeal dismissed.
