State of Jharkhand v Bhadey Munda and another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 677; (2014) 10 SCC 398; 2014(10) SCALE 473; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 765; 2014 (8) SLR 701

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Bihar Reorganization Act, 2000, ss. 72 and 73 - Promotion - Challenged - Respondents (Functional Manager in the Department of Industries in the erstwhile State of Bihar) filed writ petition before HC of Jharkhand praying that the State of Jharkhand be directed to promote the respondents with all consequential benefits from the post of Functional Manager to the post of General Manager/Deputy Director of Industries w.e.f. the date from which they had been working as officers-in-charge of the post of General Manager/Deputy Director (that is from 1998 and 1996 respectively, well before the bifurcation of the State of Bihar) - Single Judge directed the concerned authority to look into the matter and ensure that the respondents were not discriminated against - On appeal, DB held that a right had accrued in the favour of the respondents for being promoted and this right could not be taken away from them since it was protected by ss. 72 and 73 of the Act.

Held, Jharkhand HC could not have directed implementation of the recommendations of the DPC held on 15-2-1999 that is from a date anterior to the date of the Constitution of the Jharkhand HC and the State of Jharkhand and the exercise of accepting the recommendation of the DPC from a date prior to the date of formation of the State of Jharkhand could have been taken only by the State of Bihar and not by the State of Jharkhand. Appeal of the State of Jharkhand deserves to be allowed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Jharkhand HC could not directed implementation of the recommendations of the DPC held prior to the date of the Constitution of Jharkhand HC and the State of Jharkhand.
