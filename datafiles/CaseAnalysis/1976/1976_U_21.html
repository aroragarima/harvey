Union of India and Others v Sugauli Sugar Works Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 323; (1976) 3 SCC 32; AIR 1976 SC 1414; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 614; 1976 UJ 328

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Gross Negligence

Summary: Land & Property - Compensation - Claim of - Negligence of railway in delivery - Whether judgment and decree of High Court at Patna D/- 13-04-1966 and 3-09-1968 is correct? - Held, consignments were booked at railway risk and there was no explanation given for sinking of Barge - HC further found that if barge had been towed to Diara, it could not sink - Water near Diara must have been shallow so that wagons upon barge could not be submerged in water near it - On other hand, Rasul took steamer and barge to a much longer distance and passage must have taken a considerable time - Besides, water near jetty was undoubtedly deep and wagons were also submerged - The HC on these findings correctly came to conclusion that barge sank because of serious negligence of railway employees and railways did not take care which it was required to take as a bailee - One of principles for award of damages is that as far as possible he who has proved a breach of a bargain to supply what he has contracted to get is to be placed as far as money can do it, in as good a situation as if contract had been performed - Fundamental basis thus is compensation for pecuniary loss which naturally flows from breach - Therefore, principle is that as far as possible injured party should be placed in as good a situation as if contract had been performed - In other words, it is to provide compensation for pecuniary loss which naturally flows from breach - HC correctly applied these principles and adopted contract price in facts and circumstances of case as correct basis for compensation - For these reasons, judgment of HC is affirmed - Appeals dismissed.
