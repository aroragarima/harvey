Bhim Singh, Mla v State of Jammu and Kashmir and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 20; (1985) 4 SCC 677; (1986) SCC (Cr) 47; 1986 (2) ACJ 867; AIR 1986 SC 494; 1986 CRLJ 192; 1986 CRLR 66; 1985(2) SCALE 1117; 1986 (1) UJ 458

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Arrest

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1953, art. 21 and 22 (2), 32 - (A) Violation of personal liberty - Police obtaining remand of arrested person but not producing him before Magistrate - (B) Arrest with mischievous and malicious intent - Compensation - In appropriate cases - MLA arrested while en route to Assembly seat - Deprived of right to attend impending Assembly Session - (C) Personal liberty of citizen - Duty of Police officers - Held, there is gross violation of his rights u/arts 21 and 22 (2) by not producing him with in requisite time - Compensation of Rs. 50,000/- Awarded - Police officers must show greatest respect for it - Order accordingly.
