H. Dohil Constructions Company Private Limited v Nahar Exports Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 656; (2015) 1 SCC 680; 2014 (107) ALR 168; 2016 (1) ALT(SC) 33; 2015 (5) CTC 534; JT 2014 (12) SC 533; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4598; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 653; 2015 (126) RD 48; 2014(9) SCALE 503; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 424

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Shops and Establishments Act

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Delay in refiling of appeal papers - Condonation of delay - Justifiability - Instant appeal filed challenging the order passed by the HC, whereby HC condoned the delay of 9 days in filing the first appeals and also another order condoning the delay of 1727 days in refiling those appeals - Whether HC erred in condoning the long delay of 1727 days in refiling the appeals apart from condoning the delay of 9 days in filing the appeals.

Held, principle that the law of limitation is based on sound public policy and therefore in the absence of bona fide reasons the applications for condonation of delay should be strictly construed assumes significance. In the instant case, the failure of the respondents in not showing due diligence in filing of the appeals and the enormous time taken in the refiling can only be construed, in the absence of any valid explanation, as gross negligence and lacks in bonafides as displayed on the part of the respondents. Further, filing of an application for condoning the delay of 1727 days in the matter of refiling without disclosing reasons, much less satisfactory reasons only results in the respondents not deserving any indulgence by the Court in the matter of condonation of delay. There was total lack of bona-fides in HC approach and the impugned order of the HC in having condoned the delay in filing as well as refiling, of 9 days and 1727 days respectively, in a casual manner without giving any reason, much less acceptable reasons, cannot therefore be sustained. Appeals allowed.
