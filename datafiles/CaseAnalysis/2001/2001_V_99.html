Nirmal Chandra v Vimal Chand

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20127; (2001) 5 SCC 51; AIR 2001 SC 2284; 2001 (3) ALD(SC) 113; 2002 (46) ALR 532; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 183; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 8; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 123; 2001(4) SCALE 24; 2001 (4) SCJ 166; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 571; 2001 (4) SLT 124; 2001 (4) Supreme 59; 2001 (2) UC 135

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Surrender Of Lease, Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 83 - Madhya Pradesh Accommodation Control Act, 1961, s. 12 - Question regarding nature of possession of tenant mortgagee and what are obligations of respective parties in event of redemption of mortgage insofar as it relates to possession of such properties? - There is no automatic merger of two rights where mortgage is executed in favor of tenant and on redemption of mortgage, tenancy rights kept in abeyance would revive and entitle tenant to continue in possession even after redemption of mortgage - On execution of mortgage, tenancy rights would terminate only if it is clear, expressly or impliedly by conduct or other related circumstances that parties had intended so, thus as a normal rule except in intention being to contrary, mortgage and lease operate independent of each other and on mortgage coming to an end by redemption, tenancy would revive - Held, in present case, under condition 1 of mortgage deed, payment of rent is kept alive, sought to be adjusted by amount of interest payable by mortgagor - lessor to lessee, hence, element of tenancy and payment of rent operated throughout period of mortgage, tenant remained in actual possession and therefore there was no merger of two rights nor could surrender of tenancy be inferred on facts and circumstances - High Court rightly held that in view of s. 12, relief of possession of tenanted premises could not be granted to landlord lessor - Appeal dismissed.
