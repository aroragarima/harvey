Puran Ram v Bhaguram and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 296; (2008) 4 SCC 102; AIR 2008 SC 1960; 2008(3) ALL MR 843; 2008 (71) ALR 742; 2008 (3) AWC 2329; 2008 (2) CTC 224; 2008 (3) JhrCR(SC) 26; JT 2008 (4) SC 37; 2008 (4) KLT 233; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 784; 2008 (4) MahLJ 1; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 258; 2008 (3) MPLJ 273; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 499; 2008(4) SCALE 40; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 663; 2008 (2) UC 870

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, O.6 r. 17; Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Suit for specific performance of agreement for sale - Application for amendment of plaint seeking to correct a part of the description of the suit property rejected - Appeal against - Held, in a suit for specific performance of contract for sale, it is permissible to amend a part of the description of the suit property not only in the plaint but also in the agreement - S. 26 clearly says as to when a contract or other instrument can be rectified and provides that when through fraud or a mutual mistake of the parties, the agreement in writing does not express their real intention, it is open to the parties to apply for amendment of the instrument - Change in a part of the description of the suit property in the plaint cannot convert the suit for specific performance of the contract to a suit for declaration - Appeal allowed.
