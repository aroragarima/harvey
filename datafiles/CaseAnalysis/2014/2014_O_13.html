V. Kala Bharathi and others v Oriental Insurance Comopany Limited, BR. Chitoor

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 206; (2014) 5 SCC 577; 2014 (2) ACC 219; 2014 ACJ 1612; AIR 2014 SC 1563; 2014 (4) ALD(SC) 54; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 282; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 115; 2014 (3) CTC 35; 2015 (1) G.L.R. 343; JT 2014 (4) SC 467; 2014 (2) KarLJ 631; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1393; 2014 (6) MahLJ 1; 2014 (4) MPLJ 290; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 572; 2014 (124) RD 471; 2014 (2) RLW 1358; 2014(4) SCALE 449; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil procedure, 1908, O. 21 r. 1 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 173 - Interest Act, 1978, 3(3)(c) - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Challenged - Deceased died in road accident - Appellants claimants filed claim petition before Tribunal for Rs.2 crores - Tribunal awarded amount of Rs.98,40,500/- as compensation with interest at 12% p.a. - Aggrieved respondent insurance company filed appeal before HC u/s. 173 of the 1988 Act - DB partly allowed appeal and reduced compensation from s.98,40,500/- to Rs.56,40,000/- at 12% p.a. - Appellants filed Execution Petition before Tribunal and claimed amount of Rs.20,16,700/-, which was denied by respondent - Tribunal took view that amounts deposited by respondent from time to time were liable to be adjusted towards component of interest first and thereafter to portion of decretal amount - Respondent assailed order of Tribunal and appellants too filed petition and challenged that Tribunal could not have adjusted amount paid as costs towards decretal amount - Single Judge allowed both petitions filed by respondents and appellants - Hence instant appeal - Whether amount deposited by judgment debtor in decree was to be adjusted first towards interest or towards principal decretal amount -

Held, if there was shortfall in deposit, amount had to be adjusted towards interest and costs, then it had to be adjusted towards principal - HC failed to appreciate this fact and misdirected itself - There was no much difference in provisions prior to or subsequent to amendment, because in objects and reasons for amendment to O. 21 r. 1, as observed by Constitution bench in Gurpreet Singh v Union of India 2006 Indlaw SC 721, legislative intent in enacting rr. (4) and (5) was that interest should cease on deposit being made and notice given or on amount being tendered outside Court in the manner provided - Intent of rule making authority was to leave no room for any frivolous pleas of payment of money due under money decree - Thus, it was no way guide for appropriation of amount as contemplated under O. 21 r. 1 CPC - In Industrial Credit and Development Syndicate Now Called I. C. D. S. Limited v Smithaben H. Patel Smt. and Others 1999 Indlaw SC 409 which was subsequent to amendment to provision, Court categorically observed procedure to be followed and which squarely applied to case, but HC gave its own interpretation to judgment and failed to consider law laid down by Court in its proper perspective - In money suit, amount consisted of principal and interest till suit was filed, however , in case of award passed, question of inclusion of any interest on decretal amount did not arise - HC proceeded on assumption that it amounts to interest on interest which was prohibited u/s. 3(3)(c) of 1978 Act, - Decree passed by Trial Court or Appellate Court did not contain mode of appropriation and in absence of any such direction, decree-holder was entitled to appropriate amount deposited by judgment debtor first towards interest, then cost and thereafter towards principal - In view of Gurpreet Singh v Union of India 2006 Indlaw SC 721, it was held that if amount deposited by the judgment debtor falls short of the decretal amount, the decree-holder was entitled to apply rule of appropriation by appropriating amount first towards interest, then towards costs and subsequently towards principal amount due under decree, Appellants herein were entitled to amount awarded by Trial, as amounts deposited by judgment debtor fell short of decretal amount, after such appropriation, decree-holder was entitled to interest only to extent of unpaid principal amount - Hence, interest be calculated on unpaid principal amount - Appeal allowed.
