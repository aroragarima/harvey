Sikka Papers Limited v National Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1009; (2009) 7 SCC 777; 2010 ACJ 291; AIR 2009 SC 2834; 2009 (76) ALR 459; 2009 (3) AWC 3055; 2009 (3) CalLT(SC) 83; 2009 (3) CompLJ 395; 2009 (3) CPJ(Sc) 90; 2009 (3) CPR 272; 2009 (4) CTC 502; JT 2009 (7) SC 465; 2009(8) SCALE 593; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 1088; 2010 (1) ShimLC 205; 2010 (1) UC 207

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Loss Assessor, Time Of Loss, Consumer Protection Act, 1996, Insurance (Amendment) Act, 1968, Property Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 64UM(2) - General Machinery Insurance - 'Under Insurance' - Diesel Generating Set - Period of insurance 08-04-1999 to 07-04-2000, insurance for an amount of Rs. 35,00,000 - Set broke down on 25-12-1999 - Repair - Expenditure on repairs Rs. 25,00,000 - Insurance claim for such repairs made - Reimbursement to the tune of Rs. 8,07,110/- agreed upon by the insurer on the basis of surveyor's report D/- 15-05-2000 - Aggrieved thereby complaint made to the National Commission for a claim of Rs. 25,00,000 towards repairs and Rs. 10,00,000/- for mental harassment and damages - NC awarded Rs. 10,47,491/- as assessed by the third surveyor - Still aggrieved, present appeal preferred - (A) Whether the insurer was justified in accepting report D/- 15-04-2000 submitted by the surveyor who had assessed the loss of Rs.14,45,000/- after deducting about Rs.10,55,000/- from Rs.25,00,000/- i.e. actual amount paid by the complainant for repairing the diesel generating set? - Held, Yes - Terms of insurance policy and surveyor's report in question perused, contentions heard - General exceptions incorporated in the policy provided that insurer shall not be liable under the policy in respect of deterioration of or wearing away or wearing out of machine caused by or naturally resulting from normal use or exposure; in terms of policy insurer was required to reimburse the insured to the extent of moneys spent on repairs or replacement of the machinery to the kind of position that it was before the incident of damage - Court held that parts which had suffered due to wear and tear on account of constant use, although replaced could not form part of claim for reimbursement under the policy, thus surveyor's report had rightly rejected such claim - (B) Whether the insurer was justified in deducting an amount of Rs.3,71,509.50 (25.71%) as under insurance from the loss assessed at Rs.14,45,000/- by the surveyor in its report? - Held, Yes - 'Under insurance' as explained in Dictionary of Insurance by C. Bennett, noted - It occurs when the amount of insurance is less than the full value of property insured, means that the insured pays a smaller premium than that required as the rate is fixed on the basis of full values being insured; it leads to partial loss claims being scaled down by average - Word 'average' further discussed - Invoice for the whole machinery showing that it was purchased in 1997 for Rs. 45,25,000 and insurance cover was taken by valuing the machinery at Rs. 35,00,000, thus element of under insurance is present in the case, Court observed - Impugned judgment of NC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
