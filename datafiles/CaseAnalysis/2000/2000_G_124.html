State of Delhi v Gyan Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2645; (2000) 8 SCC 239; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1486; AIR 2001 SC 40; 2000 (4) CCR 154; 2000 (4) Crimes 164; 2001 CRLJ 124; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 635; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 517; 2000(7) SCALE 192; 2000 (7) SLT 506; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 270; 2000 (7) Supreme 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Quashing of charges - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 173(2) and 227 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 304 and 498-A - Whether High Court committed any illegality in quashing charge framed u/s. 304 r/w s. 34, IPC 1860 against respondents 1 to 3 by Sessions Judge in exercise of its powers u/s. 482 of CrPC, 1973? - Once trial court has framed a charge against an accused trial must proceed without unnecessary interference by a superior court and entire evidence from prosecution side should be placed on record - Any attempt by an accused for quashing of a charge before entire prosecution evidence has come on record should not be entertained sans exceptional case - Held, HC erred in its approach to case as if it was evaluating medical evidence for purpose of determining question whether charge u/s. 304/34, IPC, 1860 framed against respondents 1 and 2 was likely to succeed or not - This question was to be considered by Trial Judge after recording entire evidence in case - It is not an exceptional case in which immediate interference of HC by invoking its inherent jurisdiction u/s. 482, CrPC, 1973 is warranted in interest of justice - Order under challenge is vitiated on account of erroneous approach of HC and it is clearly unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
