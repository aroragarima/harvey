State of Andhra Pradesh v National Thermal Power Corporation Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, N. Santosh Hegde, Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 323; (2002) 5 SCC 203; AIR 2002 SC 1895; JT 2002 (Supp1) SC 254; 2002(4) SCALE 7; 2002 (3) SCJ 486; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 278; 2002 (3) SLT 394; 2002 (127) STC 280; 2002 (3) Supreme 400

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Sales Tax

Keywords: Tax On The Sale Or Purchase Of Goods, Collected, Situs Of Sale, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1972, Andhra Pradesh Electricity Duty Act, 1939, Madhya Pradesh Electricity Duty Act, 1949

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Electricity Duty Act, 1939 - Section 3 - Held, the sale of electricity is an inter-State sale, when - contracts have been entered into between parties to the transaction prior to the generation of electricity; there is no hiatus between generation, sale, supply, transmission, delivery and consumption; the inter-State movement of electricity is pursuant to contracts of sale; and the generation takes place in one State wherefrom it is supplied and it is received in another State where it is consumed.
