State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Nallamilli Rami Reddi and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20001; (2001) 7 SCC 708; AIR 2001 SC 3616; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 95; JT 2001 (7) SC 503; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 588; 2001 (2) RCR(Rent) 498; 2001(5) SCALE 582; 2001 (6) SLT 303; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 287; 2001 (6) Supreme 481

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Tenancy Act, 1974 - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Area) Tenancy and Agricultural Land Act, 1950, s. 38-E - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Tenancy Act, 1956, ss. 18(2), 3 to 7, 74(1)(e) - Constitution of India,1950,arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21, 31-A - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act,1966,ss. 80,82, 82(1) - Endowment Act - Tenancy rights - Challenged - Writ petitions were filed in HC challenging constitutional validity of s. 82 of the 1987 Act - Single Judge allowed writ petitions holding that s. 82 (1) of the 1966 Act was arbitrary and ultra vires under arts. 14 and 21 of the 1950 Act to extent that lessees who were marginal or small farmers were not excluded from its effect and s. 82 (2) was declared to be unconstitutional in its entirety - Respondent/ State Govt. filed appeal and was dismissed by DB holding that s. 82(1) of the 1966 Act was void as violative of art. 14 of the 1950 Act and holding that s. 82 (2) of the 1966 Act puts an end to tenancy rights of landless poor persons though purports to save them from cancellation and that object of Act would not be served as tenancy laws were not excluded - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Orders of HC were justified - Held, HC should have worked out how new tenancy Acts would effect new tenancies and Court could not guess its operation - Under new rules land would be taken away by Govt. from rich and powerful persons and would give leases to landless poor - Whether tenancy Acts should be applicable to religious institution or should be kept out was not matter to be decided by Courts and it was matter to be decided by legislature - Courts could not interfere with validity of the Act - Settled law was that religious and charitable institutions fall into separate category and tenants coming under them also form separate class and they could be treated differently from others to achieve object of protecting interests of religious institutions - S. 82 of the 1966 Act cancelled all leases of agricultural lands belonging to institutions and s. 82(1) of the 1966 Act defined land less poor as persons holding two-and-half acres of wetland or five acres of dry land either as owner or as cultivating tenant - S. 82(2) of the 1966 Act could not be held inconsistent with earlier provisions as held by HC because that was consequence flowing from fact that such landless poor person was either not anxious to purchase land or fails to do so - Validity of enactment could not be judged by fortuitous circumstances arising out of peculiar circumstances - Reasoning of Single Judge and DB were erroneous and were set aside - Appeals allowed.
