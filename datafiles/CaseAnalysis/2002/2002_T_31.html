Shashikant Singh v Tarkeshwar Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 260; (2002) 5 SCC 738; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1203; AIR 2002 SC 2031; 2002 (3) CalLT(SC) 55; 2002 (2) CCR 169; 2002 (2) CGLJ 176; 2002 (2) Crimes 350; 2002 (5) CRJ 452; 2002 CRLJ 2806; 2002 (2) JCC 884; JT 2002 (4) SC 386; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 191; 2002(4) SCALE 89; 2002 (3) SCJ 529; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 400; 2002 (3) SLT 434; 2002 (3) Supreme 566; 2002 (2) UC 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Proceedings against a person summoned pursuant to an order passed by a court under Section 319 (1) of the Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 have to be commenced afresh and the witnesses reheard under Section 319 (4). Further, on the magistrate committing the case under Section 209 Cr. PC to the Court of Session, the bar of Section 193 is lifted thereby investing the Court of Session complete and unfettered jurisdiction of Court of original jurisdiction to take cognizance of the offence which could include summoning of the person or persons whose complicity in the commission of crime can, prima facie, be gathered from the material on record.
