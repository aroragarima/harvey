National Insurance Corporation Limited v Kanti Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 406; (2005) 5 SCC 789; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1354; AIR 2005 SC 2850; 2005 (60) ALR 191; 2005 (2) AWC 1914; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 151; JT 2005 (11) SC 273; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 388; 2005(5) SCALE 69; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: [A.] Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 10(2), 149(2)(a)(ii), 166 - Tribunal ordered compensation of Rs. 2,24,800/- together with 8% interest from date of filing of claim petition and insurer was held liable to compensate claimant - Tribunal's order challenged - Ground that driver did not have requisite driving licence - Held, it will be for insurer to prove that insured did not take adequate care and caution to verify genuineness or otherwise of licence held by driver - Effect of evidence in this regard has to be considered by concerned Tribunal - Remitted matter to MACT for fresh consideration - Appeal disposed of.
