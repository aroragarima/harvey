Navalshankar Ishwarlal Dave And Anr. v State Of Gujarat And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 199; (1993) Supp3 SCC 754; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1126; AIR 1994 SC 1496; 1994 CRLJ 2170; JT 1993 (3) SC 421; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Gujarat Prevention of Anti-social Activities Act, 1985, ss. 3(2), 3(1) and 15 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 114 - Constitution, art. 22(5) - Preventive detention - (A) Validity of delegation of power to DM and specified Commr.of Police when law and order situation found prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Effect of absence of periodical review of order - (B) Cancellation of mutation of Govt. land in name of firm having detenus as partners and status quo directed but firm selling land - DM reaching satisfaction that it was case of property grabbing and ordering detention - (C) Whether mutation of name in revenue record is evidence of title - (E) State Govt. keeping representation of detenu without being considered and disposed and awaiting decision of Advisory Board that too after receipt of Board's decision - Held, delegation is not rendered invalid - Satisfaction warranted ,so not liable to interference - Authorised officer has no express power or general power u/s. 21 of General Clauses Act to revoke or rescind or modify order - Not evidence of title - Illegal - Appeal allowed.
