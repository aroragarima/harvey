Lily Thomas etc. v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 325; (2000) 6 SCC 224; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1056; AIR 2000 SC 1650; AIR 2000 SCW 1760; 2000 (6) ALD(SC) 16; 2000 (2) CCR 193; 2000 (2) CLT 226; 2000 CRLJ 2433; JT 2000 (5) SC 617; 2000 (3) RCR(Civil) 252; 2000(4) SCALE 176; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1081; 2000 (4) SLT 620; 2000 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 464; 2000 (3) Supreme 601

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Muslim Personal Law (Shariat) Application Act, 1937, Parsi Marriage and Divorce (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 47, r. 1, O. XL - Rules, O. XL - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 15(1), 32, 136 and 137 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 17 - Indian Penal Code, 494 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 198 - Writ petition - Wife alleging violation of fundamental rights guaranteed by art. 15(1) - Practice - Hindu males cannot get divorce from first wife - Convert to Muslim religion solely for purpose of second marriage - Whether marriage where a non - Muslim gets converted to Muslim faith without any real change of belief and merely with a view to avoid an earlier marriage or to enter into a second marriage, such marriage entered into by him after conversion would be void? - Held, conversion to Islam was not result of exercise of right to freedom of conscience, but feigned to get rid of first wife, and to marry a second time - S. 17 of Hindu Marriage Act provides punishment for bigamy - Latter part of this section makes 494 and 495 of IPC applicable to such marriages by reference - A perusal of s. 494 indicates that in order to constitute an offence under this section, following ingredients must be found to be existing (i) first marriage of accused, (ii) second marriage of accused, (iii) first wife or husband, as case may be, should be alive at time of second marriage, (iv) under law, such marriage should be void by reason of its taking place during lifetime of such husband or wife - Before an offence u/s. 494 can be said to have been constituted, second marriage should be shown to be void in a case where such a marriage would be void by reason of its taking place in lifetime of such husband or wife - Words husband or wife are also important in a sense that they indicate personal law applicable to them which would continue to be applicable to them so long as marriage subsists and they remain husband and wife - If a Hindu wife files a complaint for offence u/s. 494 on ground that during subsistence of marriage, her husband had married a second wife under some other religion after converting to that religion, offence of bigamy pleaded by her would have to be investigated and tried in accordance with provisions of Hindu Marriage Act - It is under this Act that it has to be seen whether husband, who has married a second time, has committed offence of bigamy or not - Since under Hindu Marriage Act, a bigamous marriage is prohibited and has been constituted as an offence u/s. 17 of Act, any marriage solemnised by husband during subsistence of that marriage, in spite of his conversion to another religion, would an offence triable u/s. 17 of Hindu Marriage Act r/w s. 494 IPC - Since taking of cognizance of offence u/s. 494 is limited to complaints made by persons specified in s. 198 of CrPC, it is obvious that person making complaint would have to be decided in terms of personal law applicable to complainant and respondent (accused) as mere conversion does not dissolve marriage automatically and they continue to be husband and wife - Conversion or apostasy does not automatically dissolve a marriage already solemnised under Hindu Marriage Act - It only provides a ground for divorce u/s. 13 - Prosecution under s. 494 in respect of a second marriage under Mohammedan law can be avoided only if first marriage was also under Mohammedan law and not if first marriage was under any other personal law where there was a prohibition on contracting a second marriage in lifetime of spouse - Petition disposed of.
