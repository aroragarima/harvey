Sharat Babu Digumarti v State Govt. of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 892; (2017) 2 SCC 18; AIR 2017 SC 150; 2017 CRLJ 977; 2016 (235) DLT 618; JT 2017 (2) SC 509; 2017 (3) KLT 20; 2017 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 643; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 86; 2017 (1) RCR(Criminal) 196; 2016(12) SCALE 736; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 1015

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Decency, Books, Reason To Believe, Computer, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Banking Regulation Act, 1949, Description, Believe, Corrupt, Attorney, Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985, Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, Dealing, Overriding Effect, Speech, Orissa Municipal Act, 1950, Slum Areas (Improvement and Clearance) Act, 1956, Information Technology Act, 2000, Obscene, Displaced Persons (Debts Adjustment) Act, 1951, Obscene Publications Act, 1925, Orissa Agricultural Produce Markets Act, 1956, Special Court Trial of Offences Relating to Transactions in Securities Act, 1992, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Transplantation of Human Organs And Tissues Act, 1994, Obscene Books and Pictures Act, 1856, Obscene Publications Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 292, 294 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 228, 482 - Information Technology Act, 2000, ss. 2(1), 2(1)(t), 66A, 67, 69, 79, 81, 85 - Obscene books sale - Conviction - Sustainability - Charge sheet was filed against appellant/accused for the offences punishable u/ss. 292 and 294 IPC and s. 67 of the Act - Appellant filed an application before Trial Court to drop the proceedings against him - Trial Court partly allowed application and dropped proceedings against appellant for offences u/s. 294 IPC and s. 67 of the Act and framed charge u/s. 292 IPC - On appeal, Single Judge declined to interfere on ground that there was sufficient material showing appellant's involvement to proceed against him for commission of offence punishable u/s. 292 IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant who was discharged u/s. 67 of the Act could be proceeded u/s. 292 IPC.

Held, s. 67 of the Act stipulates punishment for publishing, transmitting obscene materials in electronic form. S. 79 of the Act as has been interpreted was an exemption provision conferring protection to individuals. However, s. 292 IPC makes offence sale of obscene books, etc. but once the offence has a nexus or connection with the electronic record the protection and effect of s. 79 of the Act cannot be ignored and negated. SC inclined to think so as it is a special provision for a specific purpose and the Act has to be given effect to so as to make the protection effective and true to the legislative intent. Further, if legislative intendment was discernible that a latter enactment should prevail, the same was to be interpreted in accord with the said intention. Once the special provisions having the overriding effect do cover a criminal act and the offender, he gets out of the net of IPC and in instant case, s. 292 IPC. It was apt to note that electronic forms of transmission was covered by the Act which was a special law. It was settled position in law that a special law should prevail over the general and prior laws. When the Act in various provisions deals with obscenity in electronic form, it covers the offence u/s. 292 IPC. HC had fallen into error that though charge was not been made out u/s. 67 of the Act, yet appellant could be proceeded u/s. 292 IPC. Impugned orders passed by HC and Trial Court were set aside and criminal prosecution lodged against appellant stands quashed. Appeal allowed.
