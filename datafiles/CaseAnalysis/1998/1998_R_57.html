State of Punjab and Others v Ram Lubhaya Bagga and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1505; (1998) 4 SCC 117; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1021; AIR 1998 SC 1703; JT 1998 (2) SC 136; 1998 LabIC 1555; 1998 (2) LLJ 867; 1998 (2) LLN 973; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 1120; 1998 (2) SLJ 35

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Right To Work

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 21, 47, 133 and 144 - Whether Preliminary objection against maintainability of appeal, must be maintained? - Whether Govt. policy providing for treatment of employees,in private hospitals, and giving ex post facto sanction for reimbursement from board, can be objected? - Whether exclusion of private designated hospital from list, due to financial constraints, is violative of art. 21 or 47 - Held, prelimnary objections are to be maintained? - Policy cannot be objected - Not violative of arts. 21 or 47.
