Employees State Insurance Corporation and another v Mangalam Publications (India) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohan M. Shantanagoudar, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 709; AIR 2017 SC 4525; 2017 (5) AWC 5015

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Contract Of Employment, Period, SERVICE, Instructions, Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, Insurance

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2 (22) - ESI contribution - Interim relief to employees - Aggrieved by demand notices issued by appellant to pay contribution on interim wages paid by it to its employees during the period from 1-4-1996 to 31-3-2000, respondent moved application under the Act before Trial Court but the same was dismissed by holding that respondent was liable to pay contribution for interim relief paid since, interim relief was 'wages' as defined under the Act - Aggrieved, respondent filed appeal before HC and the same was allowed by impugned judgment by holding that amount paid as interim relief could not be treated as 'wages' or 'part of wages' and could only be treated as 'ex-gratia payment' - Hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether interim relief paid by respondent to its employees, during the period from 1-4-1996 to 31-3-2000, could be treated as 'wages' as defined u/s.2(22) of the Act.

Held, the interim relief paid by respondent to its employees is not a 'gift' or 'inam' but is a part of wages, as defined u/s.2(22) of the Act. As such, payment made by way of interim relief to the employees by the respondent for the period from 1-4-1996 to 31-3-2000 is 'wages', as contained in s.2(22) of the Act and so, respondent is liable to pay ESI contribution. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and that of Trial court is restored. Further, appellant is entitled to recover ESI contribution from respondent for the period from 1-4-1996 to 31-3-2000 as per demand notice dt.2-11-2000. Appeal allowed.
