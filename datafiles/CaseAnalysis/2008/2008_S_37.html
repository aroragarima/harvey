Collector and District Magistrate and Others v S. Sultan

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 565; (2008) 15 SCC 191; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 778; AIR 2008 SC 2096; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1715; JT 2008 (4) SC 369; 2008(4) SCALE 574; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 764

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: A.P. Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Dacoits, Drug Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers Act, 1986

Summary: A.P. Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Dacoits, Drug Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers Act, 1986 - High Court quashed the order of detention accepting contention that the instances referred to do not affect the public order at all and in any event since some of the grounds related to offences punishable under the Explosive Act, the detention under the Act was impermissible - Appeal against - Held, all instances indicated are in respect of offences covered by the definition of the expression 'goonda' - The test is whether the detenu is a "goonda" in terms of s. 2(g) - Reference to other provisions does not affect that conclusion - Appeals allowed.
