Mohan Lal Shamlal Soni v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 22; (1991) Supp1 SCC 271; (1991) 1 SCC 272; (1991) SCC (Cr) 595; AIR 1992 SC 1346; 1991 (1) Crimes 818; 1991 CRLJ 1521; 1991 CrLR(SC) 286; 1997 (68) ECR 783; 1991 (2) GLH 11; JT 1992 (3) SC 17; 1992(2) SCALE 401; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 712; 1991 (2) UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Practice & Procedure - Customs Act, 1962 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 211(7), 236, 245(3), 311, 397(2) and 397(3) - (A) Whether High Court gravely erred in allowing second revision petitions filed by respondent to examine three witnesses notwithstanding that case was pending before Trial Court for considerable length of time? - Held, second part of s. 311 of new code imposes upon Court an obligation of summoning or recalling and re-examining any witness and only condition prescribed is that evidence sought to be obtained must be essential to just decision of case - (B) Whether entertainment of second revision by HC was in violation of sub-ss. (2) and (3) of s. 397 of new Code? - Further held, no - Prosecution was launched under old Code and as such only provisions of old Code have to be applied as per s. 484 of new Code - Appeals dismissed.
