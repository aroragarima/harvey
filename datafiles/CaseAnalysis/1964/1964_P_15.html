Smt. Surasaibalini Debi v Phanindra Mohan Majumdar

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 209; AIR 1965 SC 1364; 1965 (1) ITJ 526; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 861

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Plaintiff had started a boarding house business with his own funds - By service rules governing his employment he was not permitted to start or carry on any trade or business of his own - He then arranged with defendant Gupta that latter be held out as nominal owner of said business - Plaintiff called upon Gupta to hand over possession and management of business - In breach of trust and confidence reposed in him, Gupta refused to hand over possession and management of business asserting that he was real owner of business - Appellate Court agreed with view of Trial Court and held that plaintiff was rightful owner - Whether plaintiff's suit should fail for reasons that arrangement between plaintiff and defendant was with a view to circumvent service rules and to evade liability to pay income-tax? - Held, purpose of arrangement was to circumvent service rules - It is true that having started business in name of defendant, plaintiff was able to evade payment of tax, which if true state of affairs was known, he would have been liable to pay - Court unable to hold that from inception object of arrangement was to enter into an unlawful arrangement - It is true that if plaintiff seeks assistance of Court to effectuate an unlawful transaction, Courts will refuse to assist him - Where, however, plaintiff is seeking to enforce his title to property and it is not an integral part of his pleading which he must prove to entitle him to relief that there was between him and defendant an unlawful transaction or arrangement which he seeks to enforce, plaintiff will be entitled to assistance of Court, even if initial title of plaintiff is rooted in an illegal transaction - Appeal dismissed.
