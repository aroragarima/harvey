Management of Aurofood Private Limited v S.Rajulu

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 601; (2008) 14 SCC 608; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 368; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1885; 2008 (6) AWC 6599; 2008 (117) FLR 770; 2008 (2) LLJ 1061; 2008(6) SCALE 109; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 1080; 2008 (5) SLR 3; 2008 (4) SLR 575

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Show-Cause

Summary: Labour case - Misconduct - Dismissal from service - High Court directed reinstatement of respondent with full back wages - Appeal against - Held, High Court has also found that the allegations against the workman even if taken to be true were trivial and could not justify an order of dismissal from service - Questions of fact which have been decided by the High Court call for no interference - Consequent upon the bitter relations between the parties and as even the High Court has found the charges proved though 'trivial' and the fact that the respondent has not been on duty with the appellant-management since the year 1981 directed that instead of reinstatement the respondent would be entitled to the payment of Rs.10, 00, 000/- as compensation as full and final settlement - Appeal dismissed.
