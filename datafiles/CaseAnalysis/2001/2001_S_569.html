Anil Rai and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20743; (2001) 7 SCC 318; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1009; AIR 2001 SC 3173; 2002 (3) Bom.C.R. 360; 2001 (3) Crimes 458; 2002 (1) CRJ 293; 2001 CRLJ 3969; 2009 (233) E.L.T. 13; JT 2001 (6) SC 515; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 722; 2001(5) SCALE 41; 2001 (3) SCJ 334; 2001 (5) SLT 594; 2009 (13) S.T.R. 465; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 298; 2001 (5) Supreme 617; 2001 (2) UC 514B

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156, 157, 159, 161, 353, 353(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 141, 142, 148, 149, 302 - Unlawful Assembly - Common object - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused were convicted for offences u/ss. 302, 149 of IPC - Prosecution case was that accused due to previous enmity committed murder of deceased - Prosecution to prove its case examined 14 witnesses and court on appreciation of evidence convicted accused - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal before HC, which confirmed order of conviction - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in confirming order of conviction of Trial court - Held, that on basis of ocular testimony and recovery of weapons from A-1 and A-2, existence of enmity between them with deceased and medical evidence - No ground to interfere with finding of conviction and sentence in so far as it relates to A-1, A-2 - Further held, scope of s. 149, IPC had been explained by this Court in various judgments holding that application of s. 149, I.P.C. would be highly unsafe unless it was positively proved that each one of accused shared common object and accordingly participated in occurrence - Further held that there was sufficient evidence on record to show that A-3 to A-7 had formed unlawful assembly with A-1 and A-2, common object of which was to use force and violence against deceased - It had also come in evidence that accused persons who had formed an unlawful assembly for offence of rioting were armed with deadly weapons which, when used as weapons of offence, were likely to cause death - Death of deceased was caused by A-2 who admittedly, was not part of unlawful assembly and was proved to have fired gun shot from his verandah - HC had not adverted to this aspect of matter so far as A-3 to A-7 were concerned but on similar reasoning acquitted A-8 and A-9 from offence of murder with help of s. 149, of IPC. - Prosecution had established that common object of unlawful assembly was to commit offence of rioting armed with deadly weapons punishable u/s. 148 of IPC - Causing of death of deceased persons was individual acts of A-1 and A-2 and prosecution evidence does not show that other accused persons shared said common object - Therefore, conviction of A-3 to A-7 for offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s. 149, I.P.C. was not sustainable - They were however, liable to be convicted u/s. 148, I.P.C. r/w s. 149, I.P.C - Further their conviction and sentence under Arms Act cannot be interfered with - Appeals disposed of.
