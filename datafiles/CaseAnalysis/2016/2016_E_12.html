Suryachakra Power Corporation Limited v Electricity Department and others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 718; 2017 (122) ALR 15; JT 2016 (12) SC 209; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 710; 2016(10) SCALE 46; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 108; 2016 (4) WLN(SC) 160

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Good Faith, Limitation Act, 1963, Limitation, Electricity Act, 2003

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Electricity Act, 2003, s. 125 - Limitation Act, 1963, ss. 14, 2(h), 4, 5 - Condonation of delay - Dismissal - Legality - Appellant filed instant appeal u/s. 125 of the Act in I.A. no. 1 of 2015 is for condonation of 161 days' delay in filing appeal on ground that the delay occurred on account of time taken by appellant in prosecuting a review petition before Tribunal for Electricity.

Held, after obtaining the certified copy of the order on 17-12-2014, the review petition was filed only on 25-02-2015, delayed by 37 days. Even after withdrawal of review petition on 07-05-2015, appeal was filed before SC only on 07-07-2015. Thus, there were few days left, before closing of Court for summer vacations for appellant to file appeal after withdrawal of review petition. Appeal was filed only after a few days of the reopening of Court on 01-07-2015. Therefore, appellant is not entitled even to the benefit of the principles u/s. 4 of the 1963 Act for exclusion of the period when Court was closed. Merely, Tribunal condoned the delay in filing the review petition, for the purpose of application of s. 14 of the 1963 Act before SC for exclusion of the period, in instant case it cannot be said that there was due diligence. U/s. 2(h) of the 1963 Act, nothing shall be deemed to be done in good faith which is not done with due care and attention. The facts would show lack of good faith on part of appellant in conducting its case. Thus, appellant having not prosecuted his case with due diligence and good faith is not entitled for the application of the principles u/s. 14 of the 1963 Act. Therefore, the order dt. 03-08-2015 condoning the delay of 161 days in filing the appeal is recalled. Since, SC cannot condone the delay beyond 60 days u/s. 125 of the 2003 Act and in the facts of instant case principles of s. 14 of the 1963 Act were not attracted, I.A. 2015 for condonation of delay is dismissed. Appeal dismissed.
