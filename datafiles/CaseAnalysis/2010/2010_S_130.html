V. K. Enterprises v Shiva Steels

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 592; (2010) 9 SCC 256; AIR 2010 SC 2885; 2010 (82) ALR 497; 2010 (5) AWC 4372; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 16; JT 2010 (8) SC 122; 2011 (1) MPLJ 1; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 365; 2010(7) SCALE 758; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 37 rr. 1, 2 and 3, s. 151 - Leave to defend suit - On account of business transactions, petitioner purchased iron sheets on credit from respondent on account whereof a sum became due and payable by petitioner to respondent - Petitioner acknowledged liability in respect of said amount - Thereafter, petitioner issued cheque towards part-payment of said dues - On presentation, said cheque was dishonoured and a legal notice was, therefore, sent by respondent to petitioner for payment of outstanding dues - Since, despite such notice petitioner failed to pay said dues, respondent filed suit under O. 37 rr. 1 and 2 of CPC - Petitioner filed application under O. 37 r. 3, r/w s. 151 of CPC which was dismissed - Whether District and Sessions Judge rightly dismissed petitioner's application under O. 37 r. 3, r/w s. 151 of CPC for leave to defend suit filed by respondent under O. 37 for recovery of money? - Held, what is required to be examined for grant of leave is whether defence taken in application under O. 37, r. 3 of CPC makes out a case, which if established, would be a plausible defence in a regular suit - In matters relating to dishonour of cheques, aforesaid principle becomes more relevant as cheques are issued normally for liquidation of dues which are admitted - In the present case, issuance of cheque had never been disputed on behalf of petitioner whose case was that same had been given on account of security - Defence raised by petitioner does not make out any triable issue - HC dealt with matter correctly and has justifiably rejected petitioner's application under O. 37, r. 3 of CPC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
