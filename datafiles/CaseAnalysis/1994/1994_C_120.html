Hind Plastics v Collector of Customs

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 442; (1994) 5 SCC 167; 1994 (52) ECR 336; 1994 (71) E.L.T. 325; JT 1994 (3) SC 258; 1994(2) SCALE 575; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Importation, Place Of Importation

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(41) and 25 - Notification No. 184 CUS D/- 2-8-1967 - (A) Whether the Division Bench was correct in holding that the Notification No. 184 CUS D/- 2-8-1967 does not provide for deduction of the value of packages from the invoice value of imported PVC? - Taxing of packing material twice, once at the rate applicable to the contents and then at the rate applicable to container, which would be the result if levy of duty on packing material were not to be exempted, may appear harsh, but it cannot be said to be illegal - The notification is designed to exempt levy of duty/additional duty on the packages separately, since in law there is also an import of packages and such import too is subject to ITC restrictions - Held, interpretation placed by the HC on Notification No. 184 is correct - Petitions dismissed.
