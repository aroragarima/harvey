Victoria v K.V. Naik and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2322

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Respondents-mortgagors had filed suit for redemption of mortgage - Petitioner-mortgagee claimed fixity of tenure in respect of entire extent of land u/ss. 4(1), 10 (b) and 13 of Kerala Land Reforms Act; in alternative, she claimed to be entitled to deemed Kudikidappu rights over 3 cents of total extent of 8 cents, by operation of explanation IV to s. 2(25) of Act - Trial Court negated contention - Final decree was passed - Court advanced by petitioner arises for consideration only at time of execution; thus, in this case, prima facie, they are not entitled to reference u/s. 125(3)of Act - When petitioner had carried matter to High Court, held that it operates as constructive res judicata - Whether the question of constructive res judicata was raised at time when final decree had been passed, the same can be in execution? - Held, deposit of mortgage money u/s. 83 does not extinguishes mortgage where mortgagee had refused to accept deposit - If deposit is not accepted, mortgage does not get extinguished; that means parties continue to have relationship of mortgagor and mortgagee - When ss. 2(25) and 125(3) of Land Reforms Act are to be considered in a suit of redemption claiming right of Kudikidappu as found, is not barred by principle of constructive res judicata in executing proceedings - If plea has not been raised, it operates as constructive res judicata on principle of 'might and ought' - If it is taken and rejected, it operates as res judicata and same cannot be raise in execution - Even if it is left open, inequity, justice and good conscience, it must not be extended to mortgagee - Appeal dismissed.
