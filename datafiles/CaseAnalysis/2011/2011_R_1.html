FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

V. S. Achuthanandan v R. Balakrishna Pillai and others

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 91; (2011) 3 SCC 317; AIR 2011 SC 1037; JT 2011 (2) SC 206; 2011 (2) KLT 486; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 403; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 538B; 2011(2) SCALE 346; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Kerala State Electricity Board Tender Regulations

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(c), 5(2) - Corruption - Acquittal by HC - Appeal by a person other than State - Maintainability - State Government completed the construction of a Dam for State Electricity Board (Board) wherein work relating to power tunnel was awarded at 188% above the Probable Amount of Contract and the work relating to surge shaft and allied works at 162% above the estimated amount with many special conditions, as requested by the 3rd accused contractor, involving heavy financial implications/advantages to him at the expense of the Board - Several leaks and cracks were noticed in the tunnel during the trial run itself, consequently, Public Undertaking Committee of the State Legislature, in its report, recommended a judicial probe - Commissioner of Inquiry, subsequently, came to the conclusion that materials placed before it prima facie disclosed commission of offences punishable under IPC and 1947 Act against persons responsible for the same and recommended for investigation into these offences - Special Team investigated the case and submitted its report to the Special Court - Special Court, subsequently, convicted the respondents/accused u/ss. 120B, 409 IPC and s. 5(1)(c) and 5(2) of 1947 Act r/w s. 120B IPC and sentenced them to rigorous imprisonment - Respondents/accused filed appeal - HC, by impugned order, acquitted the respondents - Hence the present appeal - Whether the present appeal by way of special leave by a person other than State was maintainable - Held, allegations against the 1st accused/Power Minister and higher officials of the Board in respect of award of contract with the ulterior motive, the appellant approached SC on earlier occasion when the State wanted to close the prosecution against all the accused including the Minister based on the order of the HC in respect of 5th accused - Further, when the very same appellant filed special leave petition before SC and later leave was granted by SC, neither of the respondents/accused raised any objection as to the maintainability of the petition - A Bench of three Judges of SC accepted the appellant's claim and set aside the HC order based on which the Special Court proceeded further and ultimately convicted and sentenced respondents/accused - In view of the said factual details, maintainability of the instant appeal could not be projected seriously - Appeal allowed.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(1)(c), 5(2) - Corruption - Acquittal by HC - Sustainability - Whether the order of acquittal recorded by the HC in the instant case was proper - Held, prosecution had established the relationship and friendship between 1st accused/Minister and 3rd accused/contractor by placing acceptable evidence - Evidence led in by the prosecution showed that 1st accused took initiative to include the name of 3rd accused in the list of nominees for constituting the Consultative Council for the construction project in question - Prosecution evidence further showed that the mandatory requirements contemplated u/s. 16 of the Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 regarding the constitution of Consultative Council was not adhered to by 1st accused who wanted to include 3rd accused contractor in the panel which stated that the State Government might constitute the Consultative Council considering all representatives of power generating companies and other persons in consultation with the representative bodies of various interests, namely, industry, commerce, agriculture, transport etc. - 1st accused and 3rd accused, even before execution of the contract in question, had prior understanding with regard to the award of contract - Prosecution witnesses testified that 1st accused/Minister had interfered with the award of the contracts and was awaiting for a probable contractor of his choice to undertake the project in question at exorbitant rates - Prosecution had also highlighted labour unrest at the worksite which was kept pending at the instance of 1st accused and other interested parties so as to make it appear that no contractor would come forward to undertake the contract, so much so that there was possibility of choosing a contractor of their choice for the execution of the work at exorbitant rate - Special Court, therefore, from the materials on record, rightly concluded that several out of way methods were adopted by the Board at the instance of 1st accused for achieving the object of conspiracy - HC failed to consider various instances and materials placed by the prosecution in respect of charge relating to conspiracy - Even though, the HC had admitted that 1st accused and 3rd accused belonged to the same political party and close relationship existed between the two, the nomination of 3rd accused in the Consultative Council of the Board was unfortunately not recognised by the HC as a material evidence proved by the prosecution - Though, several reports and minutes of the Board meeting were pressed into service by the respondents/accused in order to strengthen their case that all important decisions accepting the contract in favour of 3rd accused including several special conditions etc., it was clear that due to the pressure of 6th accused, the then member of the Board, who was close to 1st accused, as well as the desire of 1st accused in awarding the contract in favour of 3rd accused with higher rate, PW7/Official of Board had no other option except to execute the directions of 6th accused and 1st accused - Analysis of the evidence of PW7 coupled with the other prosecution witnesses and other notes and report prepared for the Board clearly indicated that though he reminded that certain things were not permissible, because of the fact that the beneficiaries of the contract were known to 1st accused and 6th accused, he had no other option except to prepare notes in such a way and ultimately the Board accepted the same - HC failed to appreciate in its proper sense the materials placed by the prosecution and brushed aside several important items of evidence adduced by the prosecution - HC wrongly concluded that, namely, 'the proved circumstances are not sufficient to hold that there was conspiracy as alleged by the prosecution' - Special Court after framing various points for consideration and after thorough discussion had accepted the case of the prosecution insofar as contract work in question awarded to 3rd accused contractor and the accused persons had abused their official positions - Special Court had also accepted the prosecution case finding that 1st accused along with other accused persons entered into criminal conspiracy and rightly convicted them - HC, however, committed a grave error in acquitting the accused without adverting to the reliable and acceptable evidence adduced by the prosecution - All the ingredients of criminal conspiracy were satisfied for convicting accused nos. 1, 3 and 6 for the offence charged against them and hence conviction against them by the Special Court was upheld - However, in view of the long pendency of the case, sentence awarded was modified by confining it to one year rigorous imprisonment with fine - Appeal allowed.
