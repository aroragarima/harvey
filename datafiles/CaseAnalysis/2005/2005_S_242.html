Technip Sa v Sms Holding Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 494; (2005) 5 SCC 465; 2005 (3) BC 56; [2005] 125 Comp Cas 545; 2005 (4) CompLJ 385; 2005 (2) DRTC 381; JT 2005 (5) SC 506; 2005(5) SCALE 123; 2006 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 227; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Moratorium, Evidence Act, 1972, French Companies Act 1966

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 139, 154, 155, 157 - Complaint -Non-Registration - Maintainability - Appellant was in possession of land - Stay Order was granted by HC protecting possession of appellant - However, respondent nos. 4 to 9 broke open lock and removed various articles - Information of cognizable offence had been filed by appellant before Station House Officer('SHO'), however, no case was registered by concerned SHO - Appellant filed criminal writ petition in HC - HC dismissed petition by holding that appellant had alternative remedy available to her, without indication alternative remedy available to appellant - Hence, instant appeal - Whether non-registration of complaint by SHO, filed by appellant, u/s. 154(1) CrPC was maintainable - Held, genuineness of information should be considered after registration of case - Genuineness or credibility of information was not condition precedent for registration of case - HC erred in law in dismissing petition solely on ground that Contempt Petition was pending and appellant had alternative remedy - Ground of alternative remedy nor pending of Contempt Petition would be no substitute in law not to register case, when citizen made complaint of cognizable offence against SHO - In State of Haryana and Ors. v. Bhajan Lal and Ors., 1992 Indlaw SC 821, it was held that Police Officer mandatorily should register case on complaint of cognizable offence by citizen u/s. 154 CrPC were no more res integra - Contempt Petition had been pending since 1997 and as such petition should be disposed of with sense of urgency otherwise petition itself would loose all its force and purpose for which contempt was initiated would be defeated - Hence, it was directed that CBI should register case and investigate of complaint filed by appellant and HC was directed to expedite disposal of Contempt Petition in any event not later than three months from today - Appeal disposed of.
