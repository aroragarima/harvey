Dr. Ashok Kumar Maheshwari v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1376; (1998) 2 SCC 502; (1998) SCC (L&S) 592; AIR 1998 SC 966; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 94; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 147; 1998 (3) SLJ 30; 1998 (2) Supreme 100; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 801

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Pharmacy

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 16 - Promissory estoppel - Promise contrary to law - Enforceable - Post of lecturer - By rule Direct recruitment - Whether State Govt. assured demonstrators-appellants to be promoted to post of lecturers? - No executive instruction or statutory provision either - Plea of said doctrine - Held, appellant had not acting upon such promise altering his position to his disadvantage - Post of lecturer is to be filled up by direct recruitment and not by promotion - Cannot be invoked for enforcement contrary to law.
