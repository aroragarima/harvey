Land Acquisition Officer and Asstt. Commissioner and another v Hemanagouda and others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1660; (2005) 12 SCC 443

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 34, 4(1) - Acquisition proceedings - Prior to acquisition period - Compensation - Entitlement of - WP filed by respondents for grant of interest u/s. 34 of the Act on account of appellants' possession of respondents' land for period prior to date of acquisition was allowed by HC - Aggrieved appeal by appellants was dismissed by DB - Hence, instant appeal - Whether interest u/s. 34 of the Act was payable to respondents - Held, SC, in R.L. Jain v. DDA, 2004 Indlaw SC 1141 held that if possession was taken prior to notification of s. 4(1) of the Act, it was dehors the Act and the phrase 'so taking possession' in s. 34 could under no circumstances mean such dispossession of owner of land which was done prior to publication of notification u/s. 4(1) of the Act - Where possession was taken prior to issuance of preliminary notification, it would be just and equitable that Collector would also determine rent or damages for use of property to which landowner was entitled while determining compensation amount payable to landowner for acquisition of property - In instant case, it would not be appropriate to deprive respondents of their rights, if any, to receive rent or damages from use of property prior to date of acquisition - Hence, decision of HC was set aside on ground that no interest on awarded amount was payable u/s. 34 in respect of possession taken prior to notification u/s. 4(1) - Matter was remanded back to relevant Land Acquisition Authority before whom respondents could raise claim for rent or damages for any use of property as claimed prior to the date of acquisition - Appeals allowed.
