State of Haryana v Satish Kumar Mittal and another

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 710; (2010) 9 SCC 337; AIR 2010 SC 3312; 2011 (1) AWC 950; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 272; 2010 (4) ESC 574; JT 2010 (9) SC 275; 2010 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3082; 2010 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3650; 2011 (1) MPLJ 302; 2010(9) SCALE 91; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Service

Keywords: Punjab Financial Rules, Punjab Financial Volume-I (Haryana First Amendment) Rules, 2001, Punjab Financial Volume-I (Haryana First Amendment) Rules, 2000, Punjab Civil Services (First Amendment) Rules, Punjab Civil Services Rules, 1994

Summary: Service - Civil Procedure - Punjab Civil Services Rules, 1994, r. 2.5 - Date of birth - Correction - Entitlement - 1st respondent/Assistant District Attorney joined the office of the Director of Prosecution on 1992 and he got the date of his birth recorded in the service book on the basis of his Matriculation Certificate - 1st respondent made a representation to appellant/Department on 2-7-2001 for correction of his date of birth - Representation was rejected on the basis of Notification dated 13-8-2001, which laid down that no application for correction in date of birth, submitted after two years from entry into service, can be entertained - 1st respondent gave a notice u/s. 80 CPC to appellant and consequently, filed a suit for declaration - Trial Court decreed the suit holding that the appellant was giving a retrospective effect to the Notification dated 13-8-2001 and that was not permissible, therefore, appellant's order was null and void - Appellant filed appeal - First Appellate Court and HC dismissed appellant's appeal and confirmed the Trial Court order - Hence the present appeal - Whether 1st respondent's claim for correction of date of birth would be sustainable - Held, amended r. 2.5 of the Rules requires an application for correction of date of birth to be submitted within two years from joining the service - 1st respondent, in the instant case, made application for correction of date of birth after nine years of joining into service - Even assuming that 1st respondent came to know in June 2001 that there was an error in his date of birth entered in the Matriculation Certificate, he took more than three years to issue the notice u/s. 80 of the CPC and then to file the suit - Therefore, the claim of the 1st respondent was belated and it has to be filed within the time provided or within a reasonable time - Courts should not have entertained the claim of the 1st respondent belatedly and beyond the period provided in the rules - HC as well as the courts below clearly erred in entertaining the claim of 1st respondent for correction in his date of birth at a belated stage - Impugned orders of HC and the courts below are set aside - Appeal allowed.
