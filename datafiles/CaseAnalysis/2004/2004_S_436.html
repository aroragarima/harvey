Sri Ram Saha v State Of West Bengal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

14 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 873; (2004) 11 SCC 497; AIR 2004 SC 5080; JT 2004 (9) SC 136; 2004(8) SCALE 800; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 459

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Orchard

Summary: West Bengal Land Reforms Act, 1955 - Whether any permission is required under ss. 4-B r/w 4-C of West Bengal Land Reforms Act, 1955 by owners of orchards to fell old trees for replacing them by new saplings having greater potential of yield? - Held, High Court not right in expanding scope and application of s. 4-A so as to apply it to areas in State of West Bengal other than area specified in three sub-divisions of Darjeeling District - When intention of legislature is clear to confine its application to limited area, court could not ignore it - High Court was also not correct in imposing further restrictions and conditions on appellant for felling trees in his private non-forest garden land - No permission is required for felling trees in non-forest private plantation / orchard/ bagan.
