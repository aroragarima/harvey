Surinder Singh v Kapoor Singh (Dead) Through Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, D.M. Dharmadhikari, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 768; (2005) 5 SCC 142; 2005 (4) ALD(SC) 106; 2005 (59) ALR 754; 2005 (2) AWC 1685; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 605; JT 2005 (11) SC 8; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 268; 2006 (100) RD 22; 2005(4) SCALE 596; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1084; 2005 (3) UC 1548

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Specific Relief Act, 1877, s. 12(3) - Balwant Singh, father of appellant herein - Entered into an agreement to sell said land on a consideration of Rs. 500 per bigha - Said Balwant Singh died on 11-2-1968 whereafter appellant herein entered into another agreement for sale on or about 17-10-1968 in relation to suit land - As appellant herein allegedly failed and/or neglected to perform his part of contract, a suit for specific performance of said agreement D/- 17-10-1968 was filed - High Court by reason of impugned judgment holding that as property was owned by appellant and his sister, Tajinder Kaur in equal share, a decree for specific performance could be granted in favour of plaintiff-respondents herein in respect of share of appellant, subject to his right to apply for partition of property for getting his share demarcated - Plaintiff-respondents later filed an application for amendment of plaint to grant decree for possession by part-performance of agreement to sell D/- 17-10-1968 of land qua share of Surinder Singh - It was submitted that Division Bench of HC could not have allowed plaintiff-respondents to amend plaint in relation to addition of plots which they failed to mention in schedule of original plaint - Whether since plaintiff-respondents did not file an appropriate application conforming to requirements of s. 12(3) of Specific Relief Act, 1963, impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Held, s. 12(3) of Act is a beneficial provision so far as purchasers are concerned - In view of findings of fact arrived at by HC, decree for specific performance of contract in respect of entire suit land could not have been granted as appellant herein was not authorised by his sister to enter into agreement for sale - Present is not a case of performance of a part of contract but of whole of contract so far as contracting party, namely, respondent is concerned - Under agreement, he had contracted to sell whole of his property - There was no contract between appellant and respondent's sister and only valid contract was with respondent in respect of his share in property - Further, an application for amendment of plaint relinquishing claim in respect of that part of contract, which cannot be performed can be filed even at appellate stage - Appellant misled plaintiff-respondents by representing that he had requisite authority to enter into an agreement for sale on behalf of his sister, which was found to be incorrect - In this situation, equity lies in favour of grant of decree for specific performance of contract in respect of share of appellant rather than refusing same - Appeal dismissed.
