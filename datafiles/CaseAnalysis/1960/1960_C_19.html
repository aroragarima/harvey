Â State of Kerala and Others v Cochin Coal Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1961 SC 408; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 219; 1961 (12) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Trade - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 286(1)(a) - Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125, s. 6 - Notification under - (A)Whether contract of sale of coal in State of Travancore? - Cochin to ultimate consumer as a direct result of sale falls within explanation of art. 286(1)(a) of Constitution? - Held, delivery of coal to ultimate consumer to use goods for his own purposes - Not for re-export or transactions of a commercial character in goods - Contract of sale falls u/art. 286(1)(a) of Constitution - (B)Whether assessee Co. was entitled to sales tax exemption by Notification D/-05-02-1954, u/s. 6 of Travancore Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1125? - Further held, notification designed to afford relief to non-resident dealers engaged in inter-State transactions - Assessee Co. is entitled to sales tax exemption - Appeal dismissed.
