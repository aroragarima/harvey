State of Karnataka and another v Associated Management of (Government Recognized unaided English medium) Primary and Secondary Schools and others

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 409; (2013) 11 SCC 72; AIR 2013 SC 2930; 2013 (4) CTC 338; JT 2013 (9) SC 579; 2013 (4) KarLJ 337; 2013 (5) MLJ 758; 2013(8) SCALE 290; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 446; 2013 (5) SLR 170

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Mother tongue as medium of instruction - Constitutionality - Appellants/State Govt. by way of G.O. dt. 19-6-1989 specifying mother tongue as medium of instruction at primary school level and making it mandatory for every child who had not opted for 'Kannada' as first language to take it as a second language - Appellants issued another G.O. dt. 29-4-1994 indicating language policy to be followed in State w.e.f Academic Year 1994-1995 - Aggrieved, respondent/association filed writ petition questioning Constitutional validity of G.O.'s - HC partly allowed writ petition while upholding G.O.'s and quashed cls. 2, 3, 6 and 8 of impugned G.O. dt. 29-4-1994 in its application to schools other than schools run or aided by Govt. - Hence, instant appeals - Whether imposition of mother tongue or regional language by State as medium of instruction at primary education stage was constitutionally valid - Held, two-Judge Bench of SC had already arrived at a decision as to question whether imposition of mother tongue or regional language by State as medium of instruction at primary education stage was constitutionally valid in English Medium Students Parent v State of Karnataka, 1993 Indlaw SC 1456 - Therefore, it was not appropriate to decide very same issue under different grounds by a Bench of same number of judges - If contention of respondent was accepted that a student or a parent or a citizen had a right to choose a medium of instruction at primary stage, it would be contradicting judgment in English Medium Students Parent v State of Karnataka, 1993 Indlaw SC 1456, which upholds mother tongue as medium of language - Matter was referred to larger bench - Order accordingly.
