Â Jai Shanker v Satate of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 260; AIR 1966 SC 492; 1966 (13) FLR 133; 1966 (2) LLJ 140; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 311 , 311(2) - Code Of Civil Procedure,1908, s. 80 - Jodhpur Service Regulations, regns. 7, 11, 12 and 13 - Jai Shanker was a Head Warden, Central Jail, Jodhpur in 1950 - He was a permanent servant of State - He proceeded on leave for two months - He applied for extension of leave on medical grounds for 20 days, as he had fallen ill, and again for 10 days - Later he asked for a an extension by a month - He received a communication of Deputy Inspector General, that he was discharged from service from August 13, 1950 - (A)Whether appellant entitled to an opportunity to show cause against proposed punishment as required by art. 311(2) of Constitution? - Even if a regulation is made, it is necessary that Govt. should give person an opportunity of showing cause why he should not be removed - Held, constitutional protection of art.311 of Constitution cannot be taken away from him by contending that under Service Regulations incumbent himself gives up employment and all that Govt. does is not to allow person to be reinstated - (B) Whether when a person removed from dervice without opportunity to shows cause be reinstated if his plea succeeds? - Further held, Govt. cannot order a person to be discharged from service without at least telling him that they propose to remove him and giving him an opportunity of showing causes why he should not be removed If this is done incumbent will be entitled to move against punishment for- If his plea succeeds, he will not be removed and no question of reinstatement will arise - Appeal allowed.
