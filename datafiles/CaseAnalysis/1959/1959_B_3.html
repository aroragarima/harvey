Kaushalya Devi and Others v Bachittar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 279; AIR 1960 SC 1168

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Allotment of land - Cancellation of allotment - Appeal - Deceased was temporary allottee in village and was allotted some land on quasi-permanent basis, his representatives were respondents in the instant case - Appellants were quasi-permanent allottee in another village and prayed to Revenue to be allotted land at respondent's village - Prayer of appellants was accepted and notice was issued by revenue to respondents that they would have to be ousted from that village - Further order was passed by the Deputy Commissioner, who was in charge of rehabilitation, canceling the allotment in favour of the respondent in village- In implementation of order the patwari made a report to the effect that the allotment of deceased in village had been cancelled and therefore consequential proposals were put forward to fit the appellants in village and to allot deceased's heirs alternative land to make up for the cancellation - On revision filed by respondents Deputy Custodian General came to the conclusion that the order of allotment in favour of the respondents had been cancelled on 6-5-1952 by the Deputy Commissioner in previous appeal, though that order was not actually available on the record, he gave certain reasons in support of the conclusion and eventually dismissed the revision - Aggrieved by that order respondents made an application and contended that canceling their allotment was illegal inasmuch as a rule had been made, which came into force on 22-7-1952, according to which no quasi-permanent allotment could be cancelled after that date except in certain circumstances, which admittedly had not existed in their case - HC held that cancellation of allotment was illegal hence, appellants filed the instant appeal on special leave - Whether, the order of cancellation of the allotment in favour of the respondents had been made before 22-7-1952 - Held, there was no error of law apparent on the face of the record which would justify interference by the HC with the order of the Deputy Custodian General, who undoubtedly had jurisdiction to deal with the matter and had not in any way exceeded that jurisdiction or failed to deal with the matter in accordance with the essential requirements of law which he was authorized and required to administer - Deputy Custodian General was entitled to take into account all the reports, proposals and orders that appeared on the record and if on a review of those he came to the conclusion that an order of cancellation was passed on 6-5-1952, though it did not appear on the record, it could not be said that conclusion of fact was based on no evidence - Appeal allowed.
