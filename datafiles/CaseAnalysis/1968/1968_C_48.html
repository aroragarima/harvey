Commissioner of Income Tax, Uttar Pradesh v Devi Prasad Vishwanath Prasad

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 260; [1969] 72 ITR 194

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.13 - Income - Profit -Assessment - Burden of proof - Respondent firm dealing in handloom cloth and silk fabrics - In proceedings for assessment for A/Y 1946-47 Income Tax Officer estimated the business profits of assessee by applying a flat rate of 12 1/2% to turnover of business and found in books of account an entry of a deposit of Rs.20,000 in the name of M - Order relating to the addition of Rs.20,000, in addition to the estimated income was challenged before the Tribunal - Tribunal found that Income Tax Officer was right in estimating turnover from sales and in applying a rate for determination of profit - HC hold that amount of Rs.20,000 was Income of assessee from the business and not from some other source - Whether burden of proof was established by assessee - Held, where there was an explained cash credit it was open to the Income Tax Officer to hold that it was income of the assessee and no further burden lies on the Income Tax Officer to show that that income was from any particular source - It was for the assessee to prove that even if the cash credit represents income it was income from a source which has already been taxed - Appeal allowed.
