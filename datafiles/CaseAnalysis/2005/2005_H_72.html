Bhagawati Oxygen Limited v Hindustan Copper Limited

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 968; (2005) 6 SCC 462; AIR 2005 SC 2071; 2005 (1) ARBLR 608; 2005 (2) BC 585; JT 2005 (4) SC 73; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 501; 2005 (2) RCR(Civil) 559; 2005(3) SCALE 646; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 232

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 30 and 33 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930 - Code of Civil Code, 1908 - (A) Whether on facts and in circumstances of case, arbitrator was right in allowing claim of BOL? - Held, no case has been made out by HCL as regards claim allowed by arbitrator in favour of BOL to extent of supply of oxygen gas to HCL - Hence, appeal filed by HCL deserves to be dismissed - (B) Whether arbitrator had misconducted himself in passing impugned award and by dismissing counter-claim of HCL and whether Learned Single Judge and Division Bench of High Court were right in setting aside that part of award by directing arbitrator to reconsider matter and decide it afresh? - Further held, if in light of evidence, arbitrator did not think it fit to allow counter-claim, it could not be said to a case of misconduct covered by s. 30 of Act - Learned Single Judge as also Division Bench were, therefore, not justified in setting aside award passed by arbitrator dismissing counter-claim and hence order of Learned Single Judge as confirmed by Division Bench deserves to be set aside by restoring dismissal of counter-claim of HCL by arbitrator - (C) Whether arbitrator had power to award interest at rate of eighteen per cent per annum for pre-reference period, pendente lite and post-reference, i.e., future interest from date of award till date of payment and whether Learned Single Judge and Division Bench were justified in reducing rate of interest from eighteen per cent to six per cent? - Further held, a relevant and germane factor weighed with arbitrator in awarding eighteen per cent interest that at that rate HCL had given advance to BOL - Even that part of award passed by arbitrator did not deserve interference and Learned Single Judge and Division Bench were not right in reducing rate of interest - Appeal dismissed.
