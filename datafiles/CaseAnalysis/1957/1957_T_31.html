Â Hansraj Moolji v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, B. Jagannadhadas, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1957 SC 497; 1957 CRLJ 599; 1957 SCJ 397; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 634

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Jammu and Kashmir (Extension of Laws) Act, 1956, Idia and Burmah (Emergency & Provision) Act 1940, High Denomination Bank Notes (Demonetisation) Ordinance 1946 (Ordinance 111 of 1946), High Denomination Bank Notes (Demonetization) Ordinance, 1946

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 109 - India and Burmah (Emergency & Provision) Act 1940 - Government Of India Act, 1935, s. 72 of the 9th sch. - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 134(1)(c), 136 - Offence - Maintainability - Appellant was accused No. 1 before Trial Court and was charged along with accused Nos. 2, 3, 5 and 6 with having transferred by sale 10 High Denomination Bank Notes of Denomination of Rs. 1,000 each to VLJ for Rs. 1,800 at rate of Rs. 180 per note and thus contravened provisions of s. 4 of Ordinance and committed offence punishable u/s. 7 of Ordinance rw/s. 109 IPC - Preliminary objection was urged and objection was overruled - Appellant along with co-accused was sentenced to pay fine of Rs. 8,000 - Appellant took appeal to HC - Respondent filed application for enhancement of sentence - Co- accused of appellant had also filed appeals against their convictions and sentences before DB of HC - HC agreed with Trail Court in regard to the finding of fact and upheld its decision - Appellant applied to HC for certificate under Art. 134 (1) (c) of the Constitution - Application was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, reading s. 72 in manner suggested would be tantamount to giving retrospective effect to section as it originally stood in regard to Ordinances which had been promulgated between 27-6-1940, and 1-4- 1946 - There was nothing to justify such retrospective operation - As regards such Ordinances period of their duration had to be determined having regard to provisions of s. 72 as they stood with omission of words ' for the space of not more than six months from its promulgation ' there from during period specified in s. 3 of India and Burma (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1940, and Ordinance in question was therefore not limited to the space of not more than six months from date of its promulgation but was perpetual in its duration with result that it continued in operation until it was repealed - There was no warrant for reading provisions of s. 72 with omitted words restored to their original position after 1-4-1946, while determining duration of Ordinances which had been promulgated between 27-6-1940, and 1-4- 1946 - Hence, High Denomination Bank Notes (Demonetization) Ordinance, 1946 (Ordinance No. III of 1946) was in operation on 11-7- 1953, date on which offence was committed by appellant and appellant was rightly convicted by both courts below - Appeal dismissed.
