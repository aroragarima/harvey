Muthu Karuppan v Parithi Ilamvazhuthi & Anr

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 264; (2011) 5 SCC 496; AIR 2011 SC 1645; 2011 CRLJ 2680; 2011 (3) CTC 520; JT 2011 (4) SC 268; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 542; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 829; 2011(4) SCALE 664; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madras High Court Contempt of Court Rules, 1975

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Madras High Court Contempt of Court Rules, 1975 - Contempt of Court - FIR was registered against Respondent No. 1 (Member of Legislative Assembly) alleging violence and several attempts of booth capturing were reported on the day of election - Respondent No. 1 was granted conditional bail by the Sessions Judge on the ground that he had to take oath as MLA - Inspector of Police-Respondent No. 2, filed an application for cancellation of bail before the HC and sought for stay of bail granted to Respondent No. 1 on the ground that victim, was in serious condition and the Respondent No. 1 was in police custody - Single Judge stayed the order of grant of bail and ordered notice to Respondent No. 1 - Respondent No. 1 filed a counter affidavit submitting that the statement of Respondent No. 2 regarding police custody was false - Respondent No. 2 admitted that it was a mistake by oversight - Petition for cancellation of bail was dismissed by the HC holding that no ground was made out for cancellation of the bail - Respondent No. 1 filed Contempt Application before the HC stating that on the direction, supervision and knowledge of the appellant, Respondent No. 2 moved an application to cancel the bail granted to him on the basis of false statement thereby prevented him from attending the Assembly - HC initiated contempt proceeding - HC held them guilty of the offence punishable u/s. 2(c) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether procedure prescribed under the Rules applicable to such proceedings were followed? - Appellant (Commissioner of Police) submitted that while sending notice, relevant documents were not enclosed and the consent of Advocate General was not obtained for initiating contempt proceedings against the appellant - Held, while dealing with criminal contempt in terms of s. 2(c) of the Act, strict procedures were to be adhered - Further s. 15 of the Act as well as the Madras High Court Contempt of Court Rules insist that, particularly, for initiation of criminal contempt, consent of the Advocate General was required - In the instant case, the above provisions had not been strictly adhered to - Order of the HC convicting the appellant u/s. 2(c) of the Act was set aside - Appeal allowed.
