Ammathayee Ammal and Another v Kumaresan and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 52; AIR 1967 SC 569; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 164; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 112 - Transfer - By way of gift - Validity - Whether plaintiff-respondent was son of Rangaswami Chettiar, and whether deed of gift was valid? - Held, a child born during continuance of a valid marriage between his mother and any man is legitimate son of that man, and this conclusive presumption can only be rebutted if it is shown that parties to marriage had no access to each other at any time when he could have been begotten - Hindu law does not permit a father-in-law to make a gift of ancestral immovable property to his daughter-in-law - Appeal dismissed.
