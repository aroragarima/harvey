Maula Bux v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 111; (1969) 2 SCC 554; AIR 1970 SC 1955; 1970 ALJ 783; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 61; 1970 (2) SCJ 249; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 928

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 74 - Deposit of amount for due performance of the contract - Nature of - Whether s. 74 of Contract Act, 1872 can be applied and amount deposited for due performance of a contract can be forfeited for breach? - Held, deposit made was not of a sum of money by purchaser to be applied towards part payment of price when contract was completed and till then as evidencing an intention on part of purchaser to buy property or goods - Here plaintiff had deposited amounts claimed as security for guaranteeing due performance of contracts - Such deposits cannot be regarded as earnest money - Further, forfeiture of earnest money under a contract for sale of property-movable or immovable, if amount is reasonable, does not fall within s. 74 - Appeal allowed.
