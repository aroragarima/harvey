B.V. Nagesh & Anr. v H.V. Sreenivasa Murthy

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 795; (2010) 13 SCC 530; 2010(6) ALL MR 928; 2011 (2) AWC 1810; 2010 (5) CTC 719; JT 2010 (10) SC 551; 2011 (2) KarLJ 577; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 415; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 802; 2011 (113) RD 127; 2010(10) SCALE 222; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 784

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 96 and O. 41- Appellant filed appeal against order of the HC submitting that HC without adverting to all the factual details and various grounds raised, disposed of the appeal in a cryptic manner - Whether order of the HC could be dismissed on the ground that said order is not proper? - Held, yes - Judgment of the appellate Court shall state points for determination, decision thereon, reasons for the decision and where the decree appealed from is reversed or varied, the relief to which the appellant is entitled - In present case, HC has failed to discharge the obligation placed on it as a first appellate Court as after narrating the pleadings of both parties, HC without framing points for determination and considering both facts and law, set aside the judgment and decree of the trial Court and modified the same without proper discussion and assigning adequate reasons - Hence, impugned order of the HC set aside - Matter remanded to the HC for its fresh disposal - Appeal disposed of.
