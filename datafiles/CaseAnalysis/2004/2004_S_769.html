K. Hashim v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

17 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 973; (2005) 1 SCC 237; 2005 CRLJ 143; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 982; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s 120B r/w s 489A, 489C, 489D - Counterfeiting currency notes or bank notes - Possession of forged or counterfeit currency notes or bank notes - Making or possessing instruments or materials for forging or counterfeiting currency notes or banknotes - Offences - Conviction and sentence - Appeal against conviction - Whether appellant is liable to be punished? - Held, possession and knowledge that currency notes were counterfeited notes are necessary ingredients to constitute offence under s. 489C and 489D - Where a person is found in possession of machinery, instrument or materials for purpose of being used for counterfeiting currency notes, even though machinery, instruments or materials so found were not all materials particular required for purpose of counterfeiting s. 489D is applicable - Appellants have been rightly convicted - Appeals Dismissed.
