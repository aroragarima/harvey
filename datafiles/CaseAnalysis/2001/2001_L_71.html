Kempaiah v Lingaiah and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19929; (2001) 8 SCC 718; 2002 (46) ALR 1; JT 2001 (9) SC 332; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 532; 2001(7) SCALE 567; 2001 (7) SLT 602; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 644; 2001 (8) Supreme 116

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Karnataka Rent Control Act, ss. 3, 14, 15, 16, 17, 21(1)(h), 29(4) and 50 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Appellant-landlord prayed for eviction - Trial Court allowed suit but in revision High Court set aside order of Trial Court - (A) Whether findings of fact arrived at by Trial Court could not be disturbed by HC in exercise of its revisional jurisdiction? - Held, revisional powers of HC under Act, are wider than powers conferred upon it u/s. 115 CPC, 1908 therefore HC not precluded to appreciate evidence for arriving at conclusion - Relied on Bhoolchand v Kay Pee Cee Investments 1990 Indlaw SC 490 - (B) Bona fide requirement - Held, word 'require' used in cl. (h) of s. 21(1) implies something more than a mere wish or impulse or desire on part of landlord - Real distinction between 'desire' and 'require' lies in the insistence of need - There is an element of 'must have' in case of 'require' which is not present in case of mere 'desire' - Ground mentioned in cl. (h) of s. 21(1) emphasises on genuineness of requirement of landlord - Appeals dismissed.
