Union of India and Others v Shakuntala Gupta (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

27 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1776; (2002) 7 SCC 98; AIR 2002 SC 3079; 2002 (49) ALR 206; 2002 (4) AWC 3225; 2002 (2) CRC 356; 2002 (99) DLT 396; JT 2002 (6) SC 371; 2002 (132) PLR 836; 2002 (4) RCR(Civil) 114; 2002(6) SCALE 131; 2002 (5) SLT 60; 2002 (5) Supreme 570

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6 and 17(1) - Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952 - Whether notification u/ss. 4 and 6 valid in present case? - Held, urgency sought to be expressed in impugned notification cannot be held to be sufficient for purposes of s. 17(1) in this case when it has already been held to be bad in earlier cases - It must follow that acquisition in respect of respondent's premises as mentioned in notification which were sought to be acquired on basis of such invalid expression of 'urgency' cannot be sustained - Review application dismissed.
