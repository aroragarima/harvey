V. M. Shah v The State Of Maharashtra And Anr.K. Ramaswamy, B.L.Hansaria

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 321; (1995) 5 SCC 767; (1995) SCC (Cr) 1077; AIR 1996 SC 339; AIR 1995 SCW 4140; [1996] 85 Comp Cas 465; 1995 (71) FLR 747; JT 1995 (6) SC 433; 1996 (2) RCJ 173; 1995(5) SCALE 191; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 79

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tenancy Rights

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, s.630 - Conviction for wrongful withholding of property - On basis of findings of criminal Court - Subsequent contrary finding of Civil Court - In suit for eviction that accused had independent tenancy rights from principal landlord -Held, appellant has been in wrongful possession of the property entailing his conviction and punishment under s.630 of the Act and requiring handing over of the possession of the flat - Improper.
