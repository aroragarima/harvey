British India Corporation Limited and Others v Industrial Tribunal, Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1957 SC 354

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial dispute - Tribunal - Jurisdiction - Publication of award - Certain industrial disputes arose between the management of the appellant and the workers of the said Mills as represented by the Dhariwal Mills Mazdoor Union, Dhariwal - Dispute was referred by the Punjab Govt. for adjudication to respondent, Industrial Tribunal, and hereafter referred as the first reference - Further disputes arose between the parties and the same were also referred to Tribunal, hereafter referred to as the second reference - After hearing the parties, Tribunal made an award on both the references and the same was submitted to the Punjab Govt. which was published in the Punjab Official Gazette - But before the publication in the Official Gazette further disputes arose between the parties and these were referred for adjudication to Tribunal, hereafter referred to as the third reference - Thereafter, the Tribunal a notice to the appellant to file written statement - The appellant refused to file the written statement and approached the HC u/art. 226 of the Constitution requesting for a writ in the nature of prohibition to be issued against respondent no 1 the Industrial Tribunal for proceeding with the third reference - Appellant's petition was dismissed by the HC following which the appellant applied for special leave u/art. 133(1)(c) of the Constitution to enable the appellant to place the matter before SC - Hence, the instant petition - Whether during the period of the operation of the said award it was competent for the Punjab Govt. to refer the very same disputes by the third reference for adjudication to Tribunal - Held, the decision of HC was apparently based on a mere comparison of the relative items in those references - Also the fact that there was an allegation of mala fades on the part of the Punjab Govt. stating that the terms of items of the third reference though differently worded from those of the relative items of the second reference were alleged to be identical with the second reference - HC had to accord a hearing of the parties after issuing notice to the Tribunal and record its decision after considering all the circumstances of the case which were brought to its notice - Matter was remanded back to the HC with a direction to determine the matter after hearing both the parties and considering the statements and evidence adduced by them before it - Order accordingly.
