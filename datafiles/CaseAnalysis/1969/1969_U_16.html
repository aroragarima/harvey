Â Harakchand Ratanchand Banthia and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 134; (1969) 2 SCC 166; AIR 1970 SC 1453; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Gold (Control) Ordinance, 1968

Summary: Constitution - Defence of India Rules, 1962, r. 1261 - Gold Control Act, 1968, ss. 5(2)(b), 27(2)(d), 27(6), 32, 46, 88 and 100 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Whether Gold (Control) Act, 1968 is constitutionally valid? - Held, provisions held to be invalid are not inextricably bound up with remaining provisions of Act - It is difficult to hold that Parliament would not have enacted impugned Act at all without including that part which is found to be ultra vires - Act still remains substantially Act as it was passed, that is, an Act to provide for control, production, manufacture, supply, distribution, use and possession of gold and gold ornaments and articles of gold - Ss. 5(2)(b), 27(2)(d), 27(6), 32, 46, 88 and 100 are invalid - Entries in three Lists are only legislative heads or fields of legislation; they demarcate area over which appropriate legislatures can operate - Widest amplitude should be given to language of entries, but some of entries in different lists or in same list may overlap or may appear to be in direct conflict with each other, it is then duty of this Court to reconcile entries and bring about a harmonious construction - It was argued that licensing of ID dealers and certification of goldsmiths are discriminatory and violate guarantee of equal protection u/art. 14, but it was held that licensed dealers and certified goldsmiths form separate classes and classification is a reasonable classification - Petitions partly allowed.
