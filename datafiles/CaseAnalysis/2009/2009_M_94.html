Authorized Officer, Indian Overseas Bank and v M/S. Ashok Saw Mill

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 902; (2009) 8 SCC 366; AIR 2009 SC 2420; 2009(6) ALL MR 971; 2009 (3) BC 640; [2011] 162 Comp Cas 324; 2009 (4) CompLJ 433; 2009 (2) DRTC 545; JT 2009 (9) SC 491; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 897; 2009(9) SCALE 649; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Law Of Limitation, Save As Otherwise Provided

Summary: Banking & Finance - Securitisation and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 13(2)(4) and 17(provisions as they originally stood, and as amended vide amending Act of 2004) - Demand notice u/s. 13(2) was issued by appellant bank against respondent/firm for the loans defaulted - Accounts had become non performing assets(NPA) - No response to the demand notice - S. 13(4) invoked by appellant/bank; possession of secured assets taken - Litigation ensued - Whether the Debt Recovery Tribunal(DRT) would have jurisdiction to consider and adjudicate with regard to post 13(4) events or whether its scope in terms of s. 17 of the SARFAESI Act would be confined to the stage contemplated u/s. 13(4), as contended on behalf of the appellants? - Held, DRT has such jurisdiction and it is not confined to pre-13(4) stage - History of litigation noted, object and scheme of SARFAESI deliberated upon; provisions of s. 13 and 17 before and after amendment, perused - 'Mardia Chemicals Ltd. vs. UOI (2004) 4 SCC 311' - Law is that action taken by a secured creditor in terms of s. 13(4) is open to scrutiny and cannot only be set aside but even the status quo ante can be restored by the DRT - Impugned judgement upheld - Appeal dismissed.
