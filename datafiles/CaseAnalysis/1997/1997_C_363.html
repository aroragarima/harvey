Vellore Electric Corporation Limited Etc v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3267; 1997 (141) CTR 398; [1997] 227 ITR 557; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 401

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Tax - Electricity & Energy - Income Tax Act 1961,u/s.261 - Electricity (Supply) Act,1948, ss. 57,80-I - Fixed deposit - Government securities - Development rebate - Central excise - Assessee/Company have licence under provisions of 1948 Act to distribute power - Assessee was required to invest sums appropriated in Contingencies Reserve in securities authorized under 1882 Act - Assessee claimed deduction in respect of amounts transferred to Contingencies Reserve and Development Reserve - ITO while making assessment disallowed deductions and added amounts in income of assessee and on appeal AAC allowed certain deductions but did not considered question of excluding amounts transferred to Contingencies Reserve and Development Reserve - On appeal Tribunal disallowed claim of assessee and held assessee was entitled to succeed in respect of claim relating to Contingencies Reserve - Tribunal referred to HC for opinion for regarding deductibility of sums transferred to Development Reserve - Assessee was not entitled to claim any deduction u/s.80-I of income tax act -Whether income earned had a direct and proximate connection with priority industry of generation and distribution of electricity - Held, Profits and gains can be said to be attributable to priority industry u/ s. 80-I of 1948 Act if there was a direct and proximate connection between profits and gains and business of priority industry - Interest received on investments of sums in Contingencies Reserve could not be considered profit of company attributable to business of assessee - There was a direct and proximate connection between carrying on business of generation and distribution of electricity by assessee as a licensee under Electricity Supply Act and income derived by way of interest from investments in securities of sums appropriated to Contingencies Reserve as required under provisions of Sixth Sch. to Electricity Supply Act which was one of conditions of license on basis of which assessee could carry on its business of generating and distributing electricity - Income earned by way of interest on sums appropriated to Contingencies Reserve which had been invested in securities could be profits and gains attributable to business of assessee - Appeal disposed of .
