Radha Kissan Chamria and Others v Keshardeo Chamria and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1957 SC 743

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Bengal Money Lenders Act, 1940, ss. 2(2), (12) and 30 - (A) Whether appellants are entitled to relief under Bengal Money Lenders Act? - Held, no - Borrower means a person to whom a loan is advanced and includes a successor -Interest or surety - Loan means in advance whether of money or in kind, made on condition of repayment with interest and includes any transaction which is in substance a loan - This is admittedly not a case of an advance in kind nor there was an actual advance of money - Moneys originally due as price had come to be treated as a loan - Appellants, therefore, cannot contend that they are 'borrowers' or are being made to pay in respect of a 'loan' as these terms are defined in Act - They cannot, therefore, claim any benefit u/s. 30 of Act - (B)What is transaction in this case which is in substance a loan? - Held, consent decree was itself a transaction - An unpaid vendor leaves purchase money outstanding and agrees to accept it later with interest, transaction amounts in substance to a loan - Original debt was not a loan and was only a part of unpaid purchase money but parties had treated purchase money as paid off in its entirety and amount equivalent to unpaid purchase money as being due by purchaser to vendor by way of a loan - On such basis, transaction may in substance be a loan - When money is due from one party to another not on an advance actually made but on other accounts a debt comes into existence but a debt is not necessarily a loan - Parties, may agree to treat money as a loan and then transaction may in substance be a loan for purpose of Bengal Money Lenders Act - Appeal dismissed.
