Mohammad Jafar v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1859; (1994) Supp2 SCC 1; AIR 1994 SCW 2839; 1994 (2) CCR 510; JT 1994 (2) SC 597; 1994(2) SCALE 267; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 111; 1994 (2) UJ 236

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Administrative - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 153-A and 153-B, - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967, ss. 2(g), 3(1) and (3), 4, 5, 9, 10, 11 and 13 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(c) and 19(4) - Central Govt. issued a notification declaring Jamaat-e-Islami Hind an unlawful association u/s. 3(1) of Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 and also gave immediate effect to notification u/s. 3(3) of Act - Notification of Govt. challenged by Jamaat-e-Islami Hind - Held, Govt. has failed to justify immediate ban imposed on Jamaat-e-Islami Hind u/s. 3(3) of Act - Further, part of notification that says, 'this notification shall have effect from date of its publication in Official Gazette', is bad in law and is struck down - Appeal allowed.
