Punjab University v Unit Trust of India and others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 451; (2015) 2 SCC 669; AIR 2014 SC 3670; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 27; 2014 (4) AWC 4194B; 2015 (1) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2014 (8) SC 438; 2015 (5) MahLJ 499; 2015 (4) MPLJ 74; 2014(8) SCALE 536; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 273

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1987, Punjab University Act, 1947, Institutional Investors Special Fund Unit Scheme-93, Institutional Investors Special Fund Unit Scheme, 1998, Unit Trust of India Scheme

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(d), 2(1)(d) (i) and (ii) - Consumer - Commercial purpose - Complaint - Maintainability - Complainant/university had a contributory Provident Fund Scheme for its employees and its fund was maintained and administered by the University - Complainant invested an amount of Rs.9.6 crores in the 'Institutional Investors Special Fund Unit Scheme-93' (IISFUS-93) of respondent/OP, which was an open ended scheme - Scheme guaranteed protection of original capital and assured a return of 16% per annum payable half yearly - Respondent sent the cheques for the maturity amount of Rs.30,45,23,910.23 and for Rs.7,13,81,520/- drawn on Bank and also furnished the details of the maturity payments against the investments - On receiving the cheque for Rs.30,45,23,910.23, being the maturity amount of Rs.19 crores from respondent, appellant University was surprised and shocked as according to the 'Terms of Offer' of IISFUS-98, the maturity proceeds would be Rs.48,76,88,935.12/- which is higher than the amount received - Appellant filed complaint, before National Commission - Commission dismissed the complaint - Hence instant appeals - Whether complainant-Universities fall within the ambit of the definition of 'consumer' as laid down in s. 2(1)(d) of the Act and that the 'services' hired by them are not for any 'commercial purpose' and whether the Commission correctly dismissed the complaint on merits -

Held, explanation given u/s. 2(1)(d), by means of an amendment in 2003 the term 'sub-clause (i)' was substituted with 'clause' to further widen the scope of the applicability of the explanatory clause - Quandary which existed thus in light of the amendments was whether services availed by complainants (being investment in the IISFUS-98 made through UTI) preclude them from being consumers under the Act by virtue of those being availed for 'commercial purpose' - S. 2(1)(d) (i) and (ii) of the Act should be interpreted harmoniously and in light of the same, find that Explanation following s. 2(1)(d)(ii) of the Act would be clarificatory in nature and would apply to the instant case - Thus, the words 'commercial purposes' would cover an undertaking the object of which was to make a profit out of the undertakings - In instant case the services of UTI were availed by complainant for the betterment of their employees, that such an investment was made, and it was to be made clear that no benefit by way of profit was to accrue to complainant, improving its balance-sheet, in view of the definition of the word 'commerce', under no circumstances, appellant could be said to be indulging in any 'commercial' activity, thus excluding him from the definition of 'consumer' as enshrined in the Act - Intent of the Universities in instant dispute was not profiteering and the same was for benevolent interest and there was no intention whatsoever that the investment was made for any commercial purpose or gain and therefore we find that the complainant Universities fall within the definition of 'consumer' under the Act and the complaints were maintainable before the National Commission - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Whether in terms of the offer, was there any deficiency of services -

Held, complainants have no case - It was clearly stipulated in the 'terms of offer' that the maturity amount would depend on the NAV and that the same was guaranteed not to be below the par value of Rs. 10 per unit - All investments were subject to markets risks and fluctuations and an investor had to exercise due caution while investing any amount in any Scheme just because the maturity amount was below their expectations they could not drag the service provider to Court for the same - National Commission correctly held that the University would come within the purview of 'consumer' as defined in s. 2(1)(d) of the said Act and correctly dismissed the claim of complainants on merits - Appeals disposed of.
