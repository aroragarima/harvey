Dalbir Singh v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 247; (2004) 5 SCC 334; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1592; AIR 2004 SC 1990; 2004 ALJ 1448; 2004 CRLJ 2025; JT 2004 (4) SC 455; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 497; 2004(4) SCALE 238; 2004 (2) SLT 1033; 2004 (3) Supreme 506; 2004 (2) UC 877

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 304 B, 498 A and 306 - whether in a given case is it possible to convict the accused under Section 306 IPC if a charge for the said offence has not been framed against him - conflicting decisions in Lakhjit Singh and Anr. vs. State of Punjab and Sangarabonia Sreenu vs. State of A.P. - appeals referred to three-judge Bench. Held on facts, in view of Section 464 Cr.P.C., it is possible for the appellate or revisional Court to convict an accused for an offence for which no charge was framed unless the Court is of the opinion that a failure of justice would be occasioned - convicted under s.306 IPC and is sentenced to the period already undergone.
