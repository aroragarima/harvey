Govt. Of T.N. v A. Rajapandian

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 158; (1995) 1 SCC 216; (1995) SCC (L&S) 292; AIR 1995 SC 561; 1995 (29) ATC 89; 1995 (1) CLR 167; JT 1994 (7) SC 492; 1995 LabIC 311; 1995 (1) LLJ 953; 1996 (2) LLN 459; 1994(4) SCALE 690; 1995 (2) SLJ 216; 1994 (5) SLR 745; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 707

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether the Administrative Tribunal was wrong in setting aside the dismissal order of respondent on reappreciation of evidence? - Held, Administrative Tribunal erred in going into the sufficiency of evidence - Standard of proof required is that of preponderance of probability and not proof beyond reasonable doubt - Tribunal cannot interfere with the findings of the inquiry Officer or competent authority - Appeal allowed.
