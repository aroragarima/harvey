Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Nanhe Lal Kushwaha

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1029; (2009) 8 SCC 772; 2009(5) ALL MR 1017; 2009 (3) CLR 184; 2009 (4) ESC 708; JT 2009 (12) SC 581; 2009(11) SCALE 35; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 331; 2009 (8) SLR 557

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Educational Institution, Back-Wages, Chargesheeted, U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, s.11A - Misconduct - Carrying passengers without tickets - Removal from service - Challenged before Labour Court - Labour Court also found the respondent guilty of carrying the passengers without tickets on two occasions - However, Labour Court opined that punishment of removal from service imposed by appellant on respondent is excessive - Labour Court directed reinstatement of respondent with 75% back-wages - HC passed operative portion of the order without discussing any materials on record - Legality of - If charged employee holds a position of trust where honesty and integrity are inbuilt requirements of functioning, it would not be proper to deal with matter leniently - Question as to whether an order of punishment is disproportionate to the gravity of charge on the basis whereof the workman was found to be guilty, must be spelt out in a clear and cogent manner - In instant case, respondent was appointed as a conductor and in that capacity was holding position of trust, it is not amount which would be very material for purpose of determining quantum of punishment - Labour Court did not assign any sufficient and cogent reason as to on what premise punishment imposed upon respondent by appellant can be said to be excessive; keeping in view seriousness of charges - HC also passed operative portion of order without discussing any materials on record - Held, impugned judgment cannot be sustained and set aside - Appeal allowed.
