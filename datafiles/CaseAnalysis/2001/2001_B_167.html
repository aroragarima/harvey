State of Maharashtra v Bharat Fakira Dhiwar

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19927; (2002) SCC (Cr) 217; (2002) 1 SCC 622; AIR 2002 SC 16; 2001 (4) CCR 272; 2002 (1) CHN 7; 2002 (1) Crimes 164; 2002 (2) CRJ 392; 2002 CRLJ 218; JT 2001 (9) SC 407; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 768; 2001(7) SCALE 602; 2001 (7) SLT 641; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 12; 2001 (8) Supreme 309; 2002 (1) UC 176

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 34, 302, 363 and 376 - (A) Evidence of child witnesses - Reliability - Held, relied on Panchhi v State of U.P. [1998 Indlaw SC 827] in which it was held that it cannot be said that evidence of a child witness would always stand irretrievably stigmatized - It is not law that his evidence shall be rejected, even if it is found reliable - Evidence of a child witness must be evaluated more carefully and with greater circumspection because a child is susceptible to be swayed by what others tell him - Child witness an easy prey to tutoring - Evidence of a child witness cannot be discarded only on ground of her being of tender age - Corroboration of testimony of a child witness not a rule but a measure of caution and prudence - Held, Trial Court had opportunity of watching conduct of two child witnesses and found them to be truthful - HC erred in coming to conclusion that it was not shown that two children stayed in locality - Also do not find any material contradictions between deposition given in Court and statement given by them to police - (B) Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Held, now well settled that discovery of fact referred to in s. 27 of Evidence Act is not object recovered but fact embraces place from which object is recovered and knowledge of accused as to it - In present case grinding stone was found in tall grass - Pants and underwear were buried - Were out of visibility of others in normal circumstances - Until they were disinterred, at instance of respondent, their hidden state had remained unhampered - Respondent alone knew where they were until he disclosed it - Under these circumstances, impugned judgment set aside - Judgment of Trial Court convicting accused restored - Appeal allowed.
