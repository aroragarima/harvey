Union of India v B.M. Jha

Supreme Court of India

24 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1494; (2007) 11 SCC 632; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1396; 2008 (5) AWC 4839; JT 2008 (1) SC 77; 2007 (8) SCJ 683; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 661; 2008 (1) SLR 488

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Retrospective promotion - Arrears of pay and allowances for higher post - When a retrospective promotion is given to an incumbent, whether he is entitled to all benefits flowing therefrom? - Relied on 1996 Indlaw SC 3382, wherein it was held that in case of a notional promotion from retrospective date, it cannot entitle employee to arrears of salary as incumbent has not worked in promotional post - Held, arrears of salary cannot be granted to respondent in view of principle of no work no pay in case of retrospective promotion - Appeal allowed.
