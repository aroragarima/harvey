Collector of Customs v Television and Components Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 174; (2000) 3 SCC 131; AIR 2000 SC 1114; 2000 (89) ECR 219; 2000 (116) E.L.T. 412; JT 2000 (2) SC 412; 2000(2) SCALE 35; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1139; 2000 (2) SLT 609; 2000 (4) Supreme 785

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Misdeclaration, Import (Control) Order, 1955, Valuation Rules, Import and Export (Control) Act. 1947

Summary: Customs Act 1962, s. 14(1) - Determination of value of imported Tape Deck Mechanisms (TDM) was in question - TDMs alllegedly imported at a gross undervalue which resulted in a non - payment of appropriate customs duty - Imported contrary to provisions of Import and Export (Control) Act, 1947 and Import (Control) Order - Violation of Import (Control) Order, 1955 - (A) Whether determination of price by Collector is justifiable u/s. 14(1-A)of Customs Act, 1962? - S. 14(1-A) of Act of 1962 provides for determination of price in accordance with Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988 made in this behalf subject to provisions of sub - section - Held, finding of Collector is also justifiable u/s. 14(1-A) of Act - Unit price of TDMs is for S $ 343 45 and also his further payment of differential duty is to be made - (B)Whether quantum of penalty imposed by Collector was justified? - That Collector had imposed a penalty of Rs. 40 lakhs on respondent 1 as being equivalent to redemption value of TDMs which were not available for confiscation and Rs. 5 lakhs each on respondent 1's Directors penalty was a composite one in sense that it was imposed both on account of violation of Import Control Order and because of misdeclaration of value and evasion of customs duty - Held, in circumstances of case quantum of penalty was justified - Appeal partly allowed.
