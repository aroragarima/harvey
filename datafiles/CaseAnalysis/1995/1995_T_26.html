Jagdish Prasad v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1872; (1996) 1 SCC 301; (1996) SCC (L&S) 303; 1996 (32) ATC 238; JT 1995 (9) SC 131; 1996 (1) LLJ 1105; 1995(6) SCALE 545; 1996 (1) SLR 7A; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 177

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Death in harness - Appointment on compassionate grounds - Held, the very object of appointment of a dependent of the deceased employees who die in harness is to relieve unexpected immediate hardship and distress caused to the family by sudden demise of the earning member of the family - Since the death occurred way back in 1971, in which year the appellant was four years old, it cannot be said that he is entitled to be appointed after he attained majority long thereafter - Appeal dismissed.
