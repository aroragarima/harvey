Sevantilal Karsondas Modi v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 6; (1979) 2 SCC 58; (1979) SCC (Cr) 360; AIR 1979 SC 705; 1979 CRLJ 645; 1979 CrLR(SC) 558; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1160; 1979 UJ 702

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal -Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B - Customs Act, 1962, s. 135- Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Appellant convicted u/s. 120-B,IPC r/w s. 135 (1) (a) and (b) of Customs Act, 1962 - Plea that confession made to Customs Officer under coercion - Whether sustainable - Ambit of s. 24 of Indian Evidence Act -Circumstances as per prosecution story not incompatible with innocence of accused - Circumstances cannot be regarded as incriminating circumstances - Whether confessions signed by appellant is voluntary and trustworthy? - Appellant, has shown existence of circumstances which make it appears to Court that confession may well have been obtained in a manner which would bring it within Ambit of s. 24 of Evidence Act, it being undisputed that Concerned officers of Department of Customs were 'persons in authority' within meaning of that expression as used in section - Held, Therefore it was extremely unsafe to regard confession signed by appellant as having been made by him voluntarily and therefore trustworthy- Appeal Allowed.
