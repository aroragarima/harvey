Kandapazha Nadar and Others v Chitraganiammal and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 388; (2007) 7 SCC 65; AIR 2007 SC 1575; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 94; 2007 (67) ALR 784; 2007 (3) AWC 2338; 2007 (2) CHN 246; 2007 (3) CTC 767; 2007 (3) JhrCR(SC) 51; JT 2007 (5) SC 605; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 383; 2007 (4) MPLJ 459; 2007 (148) PLR 210; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 794; 2007 (103) RD 112; 2007(5) SCALE 707; 2007 (5) SCJ 932; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 174

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Procedure In Case Of Assignment

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.100, o.23, r.1(4) (b), o.9, r.9, o.22, r. 10 - Respondents are the legal representatives of the original plaintiff - Defendants 1-3 fraudulently created a conveyance deed in their favour - Obtained orders of injunction and managed to enter into the suit properties without any right - Suit was permitted to be withdrawn without liberty to file a fresh suit on the same cause of action -The trial Court held that the plaintiff in the present suit is entitled to the suit property and is entitled to recover possession - Held, the petitioners were precluded from raising the plea in defence that sale deed executed, was not true and valid - The position in law is clear that when the court allows the suit to be withdrawn without liberty to file a fresh suit, without any adjudication, such order allowing withdrawal cannot constitute a decree and it cannot debar the petitioners herein from taking the defence in the second round of litigation as held in the impugned judgment - Civil appeal allowed.
