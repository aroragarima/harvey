High Court Of A.P. v N. Sanyasi Rao

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 764; (2012) 1 SCC 674; AIR 2012 SC 2118; 2012 (5) ALD(SC) 30; 2012(6) ALL MR 454; 2012 (90) ALR 888; 2012 (1) ALT 22; 2012 (132) FLR 384; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 403; 2012 (3) SLR 629

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Public Employment (Recording and Alteration of Date of Birth) Rules, 1984

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Administrative - Public Employment (Recording and Alteration of Date of Birth) Rules 1984, rr. 2, 2-A and 2(2) - Judicial Officer - Date of Birth - Correctness - Respondent was selected in judicial service and was appointed as District Munsif - Respondent mentioned his date of birth as 07-01-1949 in the application at the time of recruitment based on his date of birth shown in the Secondary School Leaving Certificate - However, by the time, he was selected and appointment letter came to be issued, he had a decree in his favour declaring his date of birth as 29-03-1953 - Respondent filed a suit seeking declaration that date of birth was 03-29-1953 and not 07-01-1949 and for direction to concerned authorities to make necessary alterations on school, college and University records - District Munsif held that respondent was entitled to the declaration of his date of birth as 03-29-1953 - Respondent made representation before HC for correct recording of his date of birth - Representation was pending for years - HC passed a Resolution on administrative side and rejected representation made by respondent - Respondent sought review of order of HC, which was not disposed till date - Respondent filed writ petition challenging the Resolution before HC - HC allowed said writ petition - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that direction of HC was contrary to r. 2-A of 1984 Rules and date of birth entered on service record was final and respondent was estopped from disputing the correctness of date of birth so recorded - Whether HC was justified in passing the Resolution on administrative side - Held, for about nine years, HC on administrative side, sat over the representation made by respondent - Further HC, considered respondent's date of birth as 07-01-1949, issued an order that respondent should retire from the service on attaining the age of 60 years and, hence, respondent had to retire on that date - HC had to determine on administrative side respondent's date of birth - This was unavoidable as nothing was shown that there had been determination of respondent's date of birth as contemplated and required in r. 2(2) of 1984 Rules - However, HC on administrative side should objectively determine respondent's age in accordance with the statutory provisions which was not shown to be done at any point of time, after giving an opportunity to respondent - Impugned order was modified - Appeal disposed of.
