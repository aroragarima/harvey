Jarnail Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 373; (2013) 7 SCC 263; AIR 2013 SC 3467; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 2946; 2013 CRLJ 3976; JT 2013 (9) SC 374; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 644; 2013(7) SCALE 764; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 1044

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366, 376(g), 120 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164 - Kidnap - Rape - Criminal Conspiracy - Conviction - Sustainability - Complainant/father of PW6/prosecutrix lodged a complaint against accused/appellant alleging that appellant has taken away PW6 - Charge sheet against appellant was filed u/ss. 366, 376(g) and 120 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant for the offences charged against him - Aggrieved on appeal HC dismissed the appeal and upheld the judgment and order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the Courts below was justified in convicting appellant for the offences charged against him - Held, it was not as if the prosecution version was entirely based on the statement of the prosecutrix, her recovery from the custody of appellant from the house of SB was sought to be established from the statement of PW3 - There could therefore be no room for any doubt, that after she was found missing from her father's residence and after her father had made a complaint to the police she was recovered from the custody of appellant - Thereafter, prosecutrix was subjected to medico-legal examination by PW1, in her independent testimony, affirmed that she had been subjected to sexual intercourse - Assertion that prosecutrix had accompanied appellant and had had sexual intercourse with him consensually was completely ruled out, because as per the substantiated prosecution version, the prosecutrix was not taken away by appellant alone, but also, by his three accomplices - All the four of them had similarly violated her person - Thereafter, each one of the accomplices had committed forcible intercourse with her in turns - Factum of commission of forcible intercourse by appellant, as also, his accomplices was reiterated by her during her testimony before the Trial Court as prosecutrix - Besides the aforesaid, there was a statement of her own father who also in material particulars had corroborated the testimony of the prosecutrix - Prosecutrix was not subjected to cross- examination on any of these issues - Nor was the prosecutrix confronted with either the statements made by her u/s. 161 or s. 164 of the CrPC prosecution, so as to enable her to explain discrepancies, if any - There was substantial material corroborating the statement of the prosecutrix for an unequivocal determination of the guilt of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
