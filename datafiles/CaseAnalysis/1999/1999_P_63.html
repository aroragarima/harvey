Management of M. C. D v Prem Chand Gupta and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 266; (2000) 10 SCC 115; (2000) SCC (L&S) 404; AIR 2000 SC 454; JT 1999 (10) SC 12; 2000 (33) LabIC 25; 2000 (1) LLJ 533; 2000 (1) LLN 358; 2000 (1) SLR 81; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 403; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 280

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 s. 25F - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1965 r. 5 - Industrial dispute - Order of reinstatement - Validity - Respondent no. 1/workman was appointed by appellant/Corporation on temporary post of Section Officer (Civil) on 5-5-1964 with condition that he would be considered for confirmation after 1 year of satisfactory service - However, respondent's services were terminated on 31-3-1965 and he was re-employed on 1-4-1965, thus, for about 18 months respondent was in continuous service - Therefore, respondent raised industrial dispute before Labour Court, which came to conclusion that respondent was not entitled to any relief - Respondent filed writ petition before HC, which was dismissed and Single Judge confirmed finding recorded by Labour Court - Respondent filed Letters Patent Appeal before DB, which allowed appeal by taking view that respondent's termination was contrary to r. 5 of the Rules - Whether impugned order of DB was justified - Held, what was condition precedent under un-amended proviso to r. 5 of the latter Rules became condition subsequent and consequently, after 1-5-1965, as per r. 5 of the latter Rules there remained no necessity for employer while forthwith terminating services of temporary government servant to offer him compensation simultaneously with termination order - Such service could be terminated forthwith and termination would immediately come into force, payment of appropriate compensation as per the proviso to r. 5 of the Rules on or after 1-5-1965 could be effected even later on though, of course, within reasonable time thereafter - This change in the Rule wef from 1-5-1965 directly got attracted on facts of instant case as respondent's services were terminated after this amendment came into force as his services were terminated on 29-4-1966 - Further, once it was held that termination of respondent on 29-4-1966 was null and void being violative of s. 25-F of the Act, logical consequence would be that he would be entitled to be re-instated in service with continuity and in normal course would be entitled to full back-wages - However, SC on peculiar facts of this case, it would not be appropriate to grant full back-wages to respondent even though he would be entitled to be re-instated in service of appellant with continuity and all further consequential benefits on that score, save and except grant of full back-wages - Therefore, impugned order of DB insofar as it holds that termination order of respondent dt. 29-4-1966 was violative of r. 5 of the Rules was set aside - However, final order passed by HC ordering re-instatement of respondent with continuity of service was upheld on alternative ground holding termination of services of respondent on 29-4-1966 to be violatiye of s. 25-F of the Act - So far as back-wages were concerned, impugned order of HC was modified by directing that respondent would be entitled to get 50% of back-wages from date of his termination i.e. from 29-4-1966 till his actual re-instatement in service of appellant with continuity of service - Appeal allowed.
