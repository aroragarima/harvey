Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Subhagwanti and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 355; 1966 ACJ 57; AIR 1966 SC 1750; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 649

Case Digest

Subject: Tort

Keywords: Danger

Summary: Tort - (A) Whether owner of a building is liable even when defect is patent or latent? - (B) Whether negligence on part of owner is established and thus liable for damages? - Legal position is that there is a special obligation on owner of adjoining premises for safety of structures which he keeps besides highway If these structures fall into disrepair so as to be of potential danger to passers-by or to be a nuisance, owner is liable to anyone using highway who is injured by reason of disrepair In such a case it is no defence for owner to prove that he neither knew nor ought to have known of danger In other words, owner is legally responsible irrespective of whether damage is caused by a patent or a latent defect - Held, appellant is guilty of negligence because of potential danger of Clock Tower maintained by it having not been subjected to a careful and systematic inspection which it was duty of appellant to carry out - Appeal dismissed.
