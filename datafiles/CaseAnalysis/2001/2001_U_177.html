Balco Employee's Union (Regd.) v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, P. Venkatarama Reddi, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20366; (2002) 2 SCC 333; AIR 2002 SC 350; AIR 2001 SCW 5135; 2002 (1) CGLJ 128; [2002] 108 Comp Cas 193; 2002 (1) CompLJ 205; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 339B; JT 2001 (10) SC 466; 2002 (1) LLJ 550; 2001(8) SCALE 541; 2002 (1) SCJ 123; 2001 (8) SLT 321; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 511; 2001 (8) Supreme 660

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Enterprises, Reimbursement, Retirement Scheme, Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Areas Land Transfer Regulation, 1959, Mining Concession Rules

Summary: Corporate - Constitution of India, 1950 - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Companies Act, 1956 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885 - Andhra Pradesh Cooperative Societies Act, 1964 - Mining Concession Rules - Andhra Pradesh Scheduled Areas Land Transfer Regulation, 1959 - Validity of the decision of the UOI to disinvest and transfer 51% shares of M/s. Bharat Aluminium Co. Ltd. - Economic policies are not amenable to judicial review unless the policy is contrary to any statutory provision - Every matter of public interest or curiosity cannot be the subject-matter of PIL - Courts are not intended to and nor should they conduct the administration of the country - Courts will interfere only if there is a clear violation of constitutional or statutory provisions - No ex parte relief by way of injunction or stay with respect to public projects and schemes or economic policies should be granted unless an irreparable and irretrievable damage has been done - Held, petitions dismissed.
