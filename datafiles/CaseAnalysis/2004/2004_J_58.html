State Of U.P. And Anr. v Johri Mal

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 423; (2004) 4 SCC 714; AIR 2004 SC 3800; 2004 (2) AllCriC 702; 2004 (5) AWC 4609; JT 2004 (5) SC 342; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 443; 2004 (3) LLN 13; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 732; 2004(5) SCALE 1; 2004 (3) SLR 734; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 560; 2004 (4) Supreme 149

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: [A.] Advocates & Judges - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 24, 320 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 124, 13, 14, 142, 166, 226, 309 - Bar Council of India Rules, r. 36 - Provisions of Legal Remembrancer's Manual relating to appointment and renewal of term of District Govt. Counsel - State of Uttar Pradesh deleted requirements to consult HC for appointment of Public Prosecutors for HC - Renewing tenure of Public Procecutor- Appointment of Public Prosecutors are governed by CrPC or executive instructions framed by State governing terms of their appointment - In appointing a counsel procedures laid down under CrPC are followed and a reasonable or fair procedure is adopted, Court will normally not interfere with decision - Action on part of State cannot be said to be wholly without jurisdiction requiring interference by HC in exercise of its power of judicial review - While making appointments of District Govt. Counsel, therefore, State should give primacy to opinion of District Judge - In matter of Counsel, choice is that of Govt. and none can claim a right to be appointed - Directed, State of U P either would suitably amend same or despite deletion shall consult HC with a view to ensure fairness in action.
