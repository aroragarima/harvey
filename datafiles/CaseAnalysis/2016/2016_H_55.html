Salam Samarjeet Singh v High Court of Manipur At Imphal and another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 714; (2016) 10 SCC 484; JT 2016 (10) SC 48; 2016(9) SCALE 738; 2016(9) SCALE 757; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Violation Of, Relief, Selection, Vacancies, Interview, Procedure, Rule, Date, Examination, Grade, Merit, Posts, Recruitment, Criteria, Advertisement, Eligibility, Scheme, Recruitment Process, Right To Information Act, 2005, Writ Petition, Notice, Constitution of India, 1950, Post, Right to, Appointment, Manipur Judicial Service (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2005, Competitive Examination of Manipur Judicial Services Rules, 2005

Summary: Shiva Kirti Singh, J.

(A) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 234, 309, 32 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Competitive Examination of Manipur Judicial Services Rules, 2005 - Manipur Judicial Service (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2005, r. 1(3) - Notification - Viva-voce disqualified - Retrospective effect - Advertisement for filling up a single vacancy in the post of District Judge (Entry Level) by way of direct recruitment was published on 15-5-2013 - HC issued a corrigendum and declared petitioner as successful in the written examination - By a letter dt. 29-01-2015 petitioner was informed that viva voce will be held on 12-02-2015 and petitioner undertook the said test - On 16-02-2015 a notice was issued by Joint Registrar of HC that petitioner had failed to qualify in the interview - Petitioner filed instant writ petition seeking quashing of notification dt. 16-2-2015 issued by HC whereby petitioner was declared unsuccessful in viva-voce conducted by HC for appointment to the post of District Judge (Entry Level) in Judicial Services Grade-I - Petitioner seeks further direction to declare his appointment to Judicial Services Grade-I with retrospective effect.

Held, once petitioner was declared as the lone candidate having passed in the written examination, it matters little whether minimum marks for interview were introduced before or after calling him for interview. Petitioner or any other person in his place, knowing fully well that there was no separate cut-off or pass mark for the viva voce, would not feel any pressure to be extra ready for the interview. In order to ensure fairness, after Full Court decision on 12-01-2015 to fix 40% as pass marks for viva voce, petitioner ought to have been informed of such development at least when intimation of date of interview was communicated to him through letter dt. 29-01-2015. Since the viva voce was held on 12-02-2015, he would have got some time to improve his preparations to meet the 40% cut-off newly introduced which was not done. HC did not have the power to change the scheme of the Rules which prescribed pass marks only for the written examination, deliberately omitted the same for viva voce examination and warranted final results after adding both the marks. Since the procedure was already prescribed by the Rules in instant case there was clear impediment in law in the way of HC in proceeding to lay down minimum pass mark for the viva voce test which was meant only for petitioner as he was the lone candidate successful in the written examination. Petitioner was prejudiced and although no case of bias has been pleaded, the impugned action would validly attract the criticism of malice in law. All actions of authorities must meet the test of reasonableness and in case petitioner is not offered appointment though being the only successful candidate, then respondents might have to justify their action, if challenged, on the basis of case of Kulwinder Pal Singh and another v. State of Punjab and others, 2016 Indlaw SC 367, and similar other judgments. Therefore, viva voce result of petitioner dt. 16-02-2015 showing him as 'unsuccessful' shall stand quashed. Respondents shall declare the result of petitioner for appointment to MJS Grade I within 4 weeks. A decision for appointment of petitioner to MJS Grade I with retrospective effect after a reasonable period from date of viva voce result which was 16-02-2015 or say w.e.f. 01-04-2015 should be communicated to petitioner within 4 weeks. In case petitioner is offered appointment and joins the service, he would get wages by way of salary etc. only from the date he starts working on the post. For the past period he would be entitled only for notional benefits of increment and length of service for pensionary benefits as and when occasion arises in future. Order accordingly.

R. Banumathi, J.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 234, 309, 32 - Right to Information Act, 2005 - Competitive Examination of Manipur Judicial Services Rules, 2005 - Manipur Judicial Service (Recruitment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2005, r. 1(3) - Minimum qualifying marks - Prejudice caused - Relief sought - Whether prescribing 40% marks as the minimum qualifying marks for the interview after holding written examination and before conducting viva-voce was within the power of respondents or whether it amounts to change in the criteria of selection in the midst of selection process.

Held, Full Court decision dt. 12-1-2015 prescribing minimum qualifying marks for viva-voce is a decision taken towards ensuring the fair and meritorious appointment on the post advertised and no mala fide can be attributed to respondents for such a decision. When the decision of Full Court was to ensure selection of meritorious candidate, it cannot be said that the decision of HC amounted to change in the criteria of selection after the selection process has started. No mandamus can be issued in favour of petitioner as no prejudice was caused to him. However, the 2013 advertisement was issued only for one 'unreserved' post. Had somebody else been appointed, the grievance of petitioner might have had substance. Further, nobody was appointed for the said post and the post remains vacant. Subsequent developments are also relevant and noteworthy. For filling up of three 'unreserved' posts of MJS Grade-I, fresh advertisement was issued on 12-08-2015 by HC. Petitioner also applied for the said post. Because of litigation and certain directions thereon, selection process pursuant to the said advertisement was cancelled. In supersession of the said earlier advertisement, a fresh advertisement was issued by HC on 4-08-2016 seeking applications for three 'unreserved' and one 'reserved' post of MJS Grade-I. The last date for the receipt of applications was 26-08-2016 and petitioner also applied for the said post. The counsel for HC submitted that the examinations are likely to be conducted in October, 2016. When the said post of 2013 examination has now been clubbed with other vacant posts and advertised seeking applications from the eligible candidates, petitioner cannot seek mandamus seeking for appointment for the said post with retrospective effect. Petitioner has no indefeasible right to seek appointment as District Judge (Entry Level) in Judicial Services Grade-I. Thus, petitioner is not entitled to the relief sought for. Matter may be placed before appropriate Bench for final adjudication after obtaining permission of the Chief Justice. Order accordingly.
