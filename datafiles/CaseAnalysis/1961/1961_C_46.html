Corporation of Calcutta v Sm. Padma Debi and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 448; AIR 1962 SC 151; [1973] 92 ITR 810; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Rent Control - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1923, ss. 127(a), 131 and 140 - Rent Control Act, ss. 2(10)(b), 3 and 33(a) - Calcutta Rent Act, 1920, s. 26(A) - (A) Can rental value of a building, to be let, be fixed higher than standard rent under Rent Control Act? - Held, by combined reading of ss. 2(10)(b), 3 and 33(a) of Rent Control Act - Contract for rent at a rate higher than standard rent is not enforceable and also that landlord would be committing an offence if he collected a rent above rate of standard rent - Omission of a specific provision, as in s. 26 of Calcutta Rent Act, 1920 - Prohibiting Calcutta Corporation from making assessment of any rent higher than rent fixed by Rent control in subsequent Acts does not inevitably lead to conclusion that omission implies conferment of such a power - Appeal dismissed.
