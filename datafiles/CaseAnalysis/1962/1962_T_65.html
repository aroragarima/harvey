Rajinder Kumar and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 163; AIR 1966 SC 1322; 1966 CRLJ 960; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 281

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201 and 302 - Conviction u/ss. 201 and 302 under challenge - Held, motive behind a crime is a relevant fact of which evidence can be given, absence of a motive is also a circumstance which is relevant for assessing evidence - Circumstances mentioned proved guilt of accused are however not weakened at all by the fact that motive has not been established - Convictions and sentence to death upheld - Appeal dismissed.
