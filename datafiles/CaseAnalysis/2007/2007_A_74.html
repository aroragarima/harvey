All Bengal Excise Licensees Association v Raghabendra Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 196; (2007) 11 SCC 374; AIR 2007 SC 1386; 2007 CRLJ 2147; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 79; 2007(4) SCALE 155; 2007 (7) SCJ 65; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 816

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Appeal against the judgment of High Court dismissing contempt application - Single Judge Bench of High Court passed an interim order directing respondent-authorities to be at liberty to process the applications in respect of grant of licenses for excise shops but restricted final selection in respect of such shops which could only be after obtaining specific leave of the Court - Extensions granted to interim order - Respondent authorities caused an advertisement to be published in the newspapers for holding lottery for final selection of excise shops - Respondents held guilty of contempt of court - Held, it is settled law that a party to the litigation cannot be allowed to take an unfair advantage by committing breach of an interim order and escape the consequences thereof by pleading misunderstanding and thereafter retain the said advantage gained in breach of the order of the Court - Appeal allowed.
