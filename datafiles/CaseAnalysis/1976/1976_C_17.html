Chaturdas Bhagwandas Patel v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 593; (1976) 3 SCC 46; (1976) SCC (Cr) 351; AIR 1976 SC 1497; 1976 CRLJ 180; 1977 CrLR(SC) 32; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 1052

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal- Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 4(1) -Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 233 and 537 - Presumption u/ss. 4(1) of Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947 - Rebuttal - Onus of proof - Essential ingredients of offence under s. 161, IPC - Charge against two accused u/ss. 161 and 34, IPC, 1860 - Acquittal of both by Trial Court - Appeal in High Court - HC acquitted one and convicted others under s. 161, IPC, 1860 - Whether capability or intention of a police officer to perform an act is requisite to constitute an offence u/s. 161, IPC, 1860? - To constitute an offence u/s. 161, IPC, 1860, it is enough if public servant who accepts gratification, takes it by inducing a belief or by holding out that he would render assistance to giver 'with any other public servant' and giver gives gratification under that belief - Held, when a public servant, being a police officer, is charged u/s. 161, IPC, 1860 and it is alleged that illegal gratification was taken by him for doing or procuring an official act, question whether there was any offence against giver of gratification which accused could have investigated or not, is not material for that purpose -If he has used his official position to extract illegal gratification, requirement of law is satisfied -It is not necessary in such a case for court to consider whether or not public servant was capable of doing or intended to do any official act of favour or disfavour - Appeal dismissed.
