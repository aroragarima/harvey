Â Narumal v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 283; AIR 1960 SC 1329; 1960 CRLJ 1674

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Prevention of Hindu Bigamous Marriages Act, 1946, ss. 5, 6, 4(b) and 8 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 177 and 258(1) - Magistrate held that appellant and his second wife had committed offences u/ss. 5 and 6 of Act - However, Magistrate upheld plea raised by appellant that he had no jurisdiction to try offence, that is why appellant and his second wife were acquitted u/s. 258(1) of CrPC, 1898 - High Court reversed conclusion of Magistrate on question of jurisdiction - Present appeal - Amending Act had provided for trial of offences u/s. 5 by enacting s. 8 and so original s. 8 has been numbered as s. 8A - Non-obstante clause in s. 8 which is identical with clause u/s. 8A should under rule of harmonious construction ordinarily bear same meaning - If non-obstante clause in s. 8A does not have effect of overriding provisions of s. 177 of CrPC, 1898 there is no reason why said result should necessarily follow from same non-obstante clause in s. 8 - Two non-obstante clauses were really intended to serve a similar purpose - Non-obstante clause of s. 8A was intended to exclude jurisdiction of Second and Third Class Magistrates to try offences u/ss. 6 and 7 and a similar object was intended to be achieved by non-obstante clause in s. 8 - As soon as offence u/s. 5 was made punishable by amending Act with seven year's rigorous imprisonment and fine it became triable by a Court of Session under Sch. II of CrPC, 1898 and by s. 8 Legislature wanted to enact that said offence may be tried by any Court of Presidency Magistrate or a Magistrate of First Class - Object of Legislature in enacting s. 8 by amending Act was not to depart from provisions of s. 177 of CrPC, 1898 but to modify effect of Sch. II under which offences u/s. 5 were triable by a Court of Session and provided that they may be tried by Courts specified in s. 8 - Order of conviction and sentence passed against appellant is set aside - Appeal allowed.
