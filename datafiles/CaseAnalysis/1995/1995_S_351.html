Yadu Nandan Garg v State Of Rajasthan And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 682; (1996) 1 SCC 334; AIR 1996 SC 520; AIR 1995 SCW 4420; 1995 (4) CCC 258; 1996 (1) CLT 138; JT 1995 (8) SC 179; 1996 (1) RentLR 111; 1995(6) SCALE 389; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 710; 1996 (1) UJ 351

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 4, 4(1), 9, 16 - Land acquisition - Notification - Exemption - Preliminary Notification u/s. 4 (1) of the Act was published - Declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was published after the improvement scheme had been finalized - Appellant had purchased the land under a registered sale deed - Notice was issued u/s. 9 of the Act - Appellant filed an application for exemption which was turned down - Since exemption was rejected he filed a writ petition in the HC - Single Judge dismissed the writ petition and the same was confirmed by the DB - Hence instant appeal.

Held, long after the notification u/s. 4 (1) of the Act was published in the Gazette, the appellant had purchased the property and constructed the house thereon. Therefore, as against the State his purchase was not lawful and it could not be used against the State to cloth it with a colour of title as against the State. It is an encumbrance against the State and when the acquisition was finalised and the possession is taken, the State u/s. 16 of the Act is entitled to have the possession with absolute title free from all encumbrances. The appellant cannot get any title much less valid title to the property. It is seen that after the publication of the notification u/s. 4 (1)of the Act, improvement scheme was prepared and sent to the Government for finalisation thereof. It has taken, as usual, certain time for approval and after the declaration u/s. 6 was duly published in the Gazette. Under these circumstances, SC does not find any inordinate delay in the issuance of declaration u/s. 6 and the valid notification u/s. 4 of the Act does not become invalid on that account due to inaction on the part of the subordinates. The wrong mention of Survey number in notice u/s. 9 of the Act cannot cast cloud on valid notification issued u/s. 4 (1) of the Act. It is enough that main survey number is mentioned in the notification u/s. 4 of the Act and the details thereof would be supplemented at the appropriate stage. Mention of the sub-division of the main survey number does not render the notification u/s. 4 of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
