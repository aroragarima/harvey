M.R. Satwaji Rao (D) By L.Rs v B. Shama Rao (Dead) By L.Rs and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 691; (2008) 5 SCC 124; AIR 2008 SC 2328; 2008 (5) AWC 5202; JT 2008 (5) SC 557; 2008 (3) KLT 265; 2008 (5) MLJ(SC) 406; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 827; 2008(6) SCALE 554; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 90

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Mortgagee In Possession, Preliminary Decree For Redemption, Mortgagor And Mortgagee, Money Advanced Or To Be Advanced, Performance Of An Engagement, Specific Immoveable Property, Mortgage Decree, Satisfaction Of Claim

Summary: Suit for redemption of the mortgage - Trial Court dismissed the suit - High Court allowed the appeal - Appeal against - Held, bringing the mortgaged property for sale by the appellants in execution of the decree passed in O.S. No. 120/51-52 and purchasing the same by the appellants in public auction is clearly barred under O.34 r. 14 CPC - Though the mortgagee purchased the mortgaged property pursuant to the decree in O.S. No. 120/51-52, as explained and interpreted the provisions of O. 34 r. 14 CPC and s. 90 of the Indian Trusts Act, in the absence of recourse to r. 14 of O. 34 relationship of mortgagor and mortgagee continues to subsist even thereafter, and his purchase is only in trust for the mortgagor - High Court was right in granting preliminary decree for redemption - Appeal dismissed.
