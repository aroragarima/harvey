Consumer Online Foundation, Etc. v Union Of India & Ors., Etc

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 289; (2011) 5 SCC 360; JT 2011 (5) SC 221; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 544; 2011(5) SCALE 193; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 911

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Airports Authority Of India (Amendment) Act, 2003, Airports Economic Regulatory Authority of India Act, 2008, Bombay Municipalities Act, 1901

Summary: Administrative - Carriers & Transportation - Contract & Commercial - Airports Authority of India Act, 1994, ss. 3, 12A(4), 22A - Airports Authority of India (Amendment) Act, 2003 - Airports Economic Regulatory Authority of India Act, 2008, ss. 13(1)(b), 22 - Airports Authority of India (AAI) constituted u/s. 3 of 1994 Act - 1994 Act was amended by 2003 Act by inserting s. 22A in 1994 Act whereby AAI, with approval of Central Government, was empowered to levy on, and collect from, embarking passengers at an airport, development fees at rate as may be prescribed - Amended s. 22A under 2008 Act provided for determination of rate of development fees for major airports u/s. 13(1)(b) of 2008 Act by Airports Economic Regulatory Authority (Regulatory Authority) - AAI leased out Delhi Airport to Delhi International Airport Private Limited (DIAL) and Mumbai Airport to Mumbai International Airport Private Limited (MIAL) - Government of India sent a letter dated 9-2-2009 to DIAL conveying approval of Central Government u/s. 22A of 1994 Act for levy of development fees by DIAL at Delhi Airport at rate of Rs.200/- per departing domestic passenger and at rate of Rs.1300/- per departing international passenger - Government of India sent another letter dated 27-2-2009 to MIAL conveying approval of Central Government u/s. 22A of 1994 Act for levy of development fees by Mumbai MIAL at Mumbai Airport at rate of Rs.100/- per departing domestic passenger and at rate of Rs.600/- per departing international passenger - Appellant association filed writ petition before HC contending that such levy of development fees u/s. 22A of 1994 Act should only be made by AAI and not by lessee and that until rate of such levy was either prescribed by Rules made under 1994 Act or determined by Regulatory Authority under 2008 Act as provided in s. 22A of Act before and after its amendment by 2008 Act, levy and collection of development fees were ultra vires 1994 Act - HC held that there was no illegality attached to imposition of development fees by two lessees with prior approval of Central Government and dismissed writ petitions by impugned judgment and order - Whether imposition and collection of development fees by lessees of AAI were ultra vires of 1994 Act - Held, since lessee of an airport should not be assigned function of AAI to establish airports or assist in establishing private airports in lieu of existing airports at which development fees was being collected, lessee could not u/s. 12A(4) had power of AAI u/s. 22A of 1994 Act to levy and collect development fees - HC was not correct in coming to conclusion in impugned judgment that development fees to be levied and collected u/s. 22A of 1994 Act was in nature of tariff or charges collected by AAI for facilities provided to passengers and airlines - Charges, fees and rent collected by AAI u/s. 22 were for services and facilities provided by AAI to airlines, passengers, visitors and traders doing business at airport - When AAI made a lease of premises of an airport (including buildings and structures thereon and appertaining thereto) in favour of a lessee to carry out some of its functions u/s. 12, lessee, who had been assigned such functions, would have powers of AAI u/s. 22 of 2008 Act to collect charges, fees or rent from third parties for different facilities and services provided to them in terms of lease agreement - However, legal basis of such charges, fees or rent enumerated in s. 22 of 2008 Act was the contract between AAI or lessee to whom airport had been leased out and third party, such as airlines, visitors and traders doing business at airport but there could be no such contractual relationship between passengers embarking at an airport and AAI with regard to up-gradation, expansion or development of airport which was to be funded or financed by development fees as provided in s. 22A(a) of 1994 Act - Passengers who embarked at airport after airport was upgraded, expanded or developed would only avail facilities and services of upgraded, expanded and developed airport - Similarly, there could be no contractual relationship between AAI and passengers embarking at an airport for establishment of a new airport in lieu of existing airport or establishment of a private airport in lieu of existing airport as mentioned in cls. (b) and (c) of s. 22A of 1994 Act - In absence of such contractual relationship, liability of embarking passengers to pay development fees had to be based on a statutory provision and for that reason s. 22A had been enacted empowering AAI to levy and collect from embarking passengers development fees for purposes mentioned in cls. (a), (b) and (c) of s. 22A of Act - Further, nature of levy u/s. 22A of 2004 Act, was not charges or any other consideration for services for facilities provided by AAI - Since rules had not prescribed rate at which development fees could be levied and collected from embarking passengers, levy and collection of development fees from embarking passengers was without authority of law - Thus, rate at which tax was to be levied was an essential component of a taxing provision and no tax should be levied until rate was fixed in accordance with taxing provision - Until rate of development fees was prescribed by Rules, as provided in s. 22A of 1994 Act, development fees could not be levied on embarking passengers at two major airports - HC erred in concluding that exercise of power to levy and collect development fees u/s. 22A was not dependent on existence of rules and, therefore, that power could be exercised even if rules had not been framed prescribing rate of development fees u/s. 22A of 1994 Act - S. 22A of 1994 Act before its amendment by 2008 Act specifically provided that development fees may be levied and collected at rate as may be prescribed by rules - Hence, rate of development fees could not be determined by Central Government in two letters dated 9-2-2009 and 27-2-2009 communicated to DIAL and MIAL respectively - U/s. 22A of 1994 Act, Central Government had only power to grant its previous approval to levy and collection of development fees but had no power to fix rate at which development fees was to be levied and collected from embarking passengers - Hence, levy and collection of development fees by DIAL and MIAL at rates fixed by Central Government in two letters dated 9-2-2009 and 27-2-2009 were ultra vires 1994 Act and two letters being ultra vires 1994 Act were not saved by s. 6 of General Clauses Act, 1897 - After amendment of s. 22A by 2008 Act with effect from 1-1-2009, rate of development fees to be levied and collected at major airports such as Delhi and Mumbai was to be determined by Regulatory Authority u/s. 13(1)(b) of 2008 Act and not by Central Government - Regulatory Authority constituted under 2008 Act had already issued a public notice dated 23-4-2010 permitting DIAL to continue to levy development fees at rate of Rs.200/- per departing domestic passenger and at rate of Rs.1,300/- per departing international passenger with effect from 1-3-2009 on an ad hoc basis pending final determination u/s. 13 of 2008 Act - Public notice dated 23-4-2010 had been issued by Regulatory Authority under 2008 Act long after impugned decision of HC upholding levy and it had not been challenged by appellants, hence, question of examining validity of said public notice did not arise but no such public notice had been issued by Regulatory Authority under 2008 Act pertaining to levy and collection of development fees by MIAL - Hence, MIAL could not continue to levy and collect development fees at major airport at Mumbai and should not do so in future until Regulatory Authority passed an appropriate order u/s. 22A of 1994 Act as amended by 2008 Act - Having held that levy and collection of development fees by DIAL and MIAL at rates fixed by Central Government in two letters dated 9-2-2009 and 27-2-2009 were ultra vires 1994 Act and that MIAL and DIAL could not continue to levy and collect of development fees at major airport at Mumbai without an appropriate order passed by Regulatory Authority, direction issued to DIAL and MIAL to credit the development fee collected so far to AAI and would be utilized for the purposes mentioned in cls. (a), (b) or (c) of s. 22A of 1994 Act in the manner to be prescribed by the rules which may be made as early as possible - Impugned HC judgment was set aside - Appeals allowed.
