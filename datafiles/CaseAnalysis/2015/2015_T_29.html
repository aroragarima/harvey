Tejram Patil v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 141; (2015) 8 SCC 494; 2015 CRLJ 1829; 2015 (3) JCC 2053; JT 2015 (2) SC 526; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1436; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 585; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 283; 2015 (3) RLW 2046; 2015(2) SCALE 698; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 912

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32(1) - Reliability of dying declaration - Appellant was allegedly poured kerosene on deceased and set her on fire - Further, in process of rescuing deceased, her mother and PW1-house owner sustained injuries - Deceased made dying declaration and later succumbed to injuries and her mother also made dying declaration and later died with 77% burn injuries - Trial Court held case of prosecution proved on relying upon dying declaration made by mother of deceased and held it to be admissible and genuine and held appellant guilty - On appeal, HC affirmed conviction and sentence of appellant but held dying declaration of mother of deceased to be inadmissible and accepted dying declaration of deceased which was not accepted by Trial Court - Instant appeal by appellant against order of HC upholding conviction u/s. 302 of IPC and sentence of rigorous imprisonment for life - Whether conviction of appellant could be upheld.

Held, there was no material for Court being satisfied that deceased was in fit condition to make declaration. The deceased was in hospital at time of her alleged statement but no effort was made by PSI to ascertain her medical condition or to certify that he had satisfied himself about the fitness of declarant. Further, the dying declaration had no signature or thumb mark of deceased. Hence, view of HC that in peculiarity of facts, authenticity of dying declaration of deceased could be accepted is not sound. Further, statement of pouring of kerosene on deceased, intervention of mother of deceased in the process and her receiving burin injuries resulting in her death are integral part of the same transaction. The said statement by mother of deceased which related to circumstances of transaction resulting in her death being admissible, it could be relied upon to show as to how death of deceased took place. The said statement is also corroborated by admission of appellant himself to the extent that the death of deceased was by burning and mother of deceased received burn injuries in the same incident. Hence, dying declaration of mother of deceased is admissible as to circumstances of transaction which included the circumstance of pouring of kerosene and lighting of fire by appellant resulting in death of deceased. Hence, death of deceased is proved beyond reasonable doubt to be homicidal death by burning and by pouring of kerosene and setting her on fire by appellant and thus, case of prosecution against appellant is proved beyond reasonable doubt. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per judgment of SC in Laxman v. State of Maharashtra, 2002 INDLAW SC 1921, even in absence of certification by Doctor as to fitness of mind of the declarants and even if the dying declaration is recorded by Police Officer, the same could be relied upon. However, Court should be satisfied that deceased was in fit mental condition to make the Dying declaration and that statement was faithfully recorded and was otherwise reliable. Further, when dying declaration relating to circumstances of the transaction which resulted in death of a person making the declaration are integral part of circumstances resulting in death of any other person, such dying declaration has relevance for death of such other person also.
