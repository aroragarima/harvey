Virender Chaudhary v Bharat Petroleum Corporation And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1719; (2009) 1 SCC 297; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1160; JT 2008 (12) SC 488; 2008(15) SCALE 67; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 713

Case Digest

Subject: Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Alternative Remedy, Appeals allowed

Summary: Grant of allotment of the LPG dealership - Cancellation of empanelment of the 5th respondent on the ground of involvement in criminal case and allotment made in favour of the appellant - High Court set aside the cancellation - Appeal against - Held, from May 2004 to October 2006, the respondent did not take any step to challenge insurance of the letter of intent granting dealership in favour of appellant - Considering the fact that starting of a business in LPG dealership requires a huge investment and infrastructure therefor is required to be provided and a large number of employees are to be appointed therefor, High Court committed a serious error in not taking these factors into consideration in proper perspective - Appeals allowed.
