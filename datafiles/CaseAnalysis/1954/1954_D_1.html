Vashist Narain Sharma v Dev Chandra and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1954 SC 513; 1954 ALJ 525; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 379; 1954 SCJ 717; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 509

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Margin Of Votes

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 33, 36(6) and 100(1)c) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - (A) Whether nomination of one of rival candidates, was improperly accepted by Returning Officer? Held, no - No apparent evidence to suggest that nomination is improperly accepted - (B) Whether burden of proof lies upon petitioner who objects to validity of election? - Further held, burden lies upon objector - (C) Whether result of election was thereby materially affected? - Further held, no - Result of election has not been materially affected by an improper acceptance of nomination - Appeal allowed.
