Guduthur Brothers v Income Tax Officer, Special Circle, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 366; AIR 1960 SC 1326; [1960] 40 ITR 298; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 71; 1961 (1) SCJ 320; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Encumbered Estates Act, 1934, ss. 15 and 14 - Interpretation of s. 15 of United Provinces Encumbered Estates Act, 1934 - Predecessor of appellant borrowed a sum of rupees one lakh from predecessor of respondents, on mortgage of a house and also entire Zamindari interest in a village Parsera - Interest was 8 percent per annum compound with six monthly rests - A suit for recovery of amount on account of principal and interest due on mortgage, by sale of mortgaged property was instituted - Suit was decreed - Revision sought - (A)Whether special judge can reject findings of decree passed by civil court? - Held that if they are inconsistent with any of sub-section 1, 2, 7 and 3 of s. 14 of Act of 1934 he has to reject findings to extent of such inconsistency - (A1) Whether special judge is bound to to follow provisions s. 14(4) (a) regarding amount of interest on principal? - Held that special judge is bound to follow provisions of s. 14(4)(a) so that amount of interest which he can hold to be due on date of application must not exceed portion of principal found to be due on date of application - Appeal allowed.
