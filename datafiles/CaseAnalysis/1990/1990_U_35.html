Mahesh Kumar Chauhan @ Banti v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 52; (1990) 3 SCC 148; (1990) SCC (Cr) 434; AIR 1990 SC 1455; [1991] 70 Comp Cas 782; 1990 (2) Crimes 472; 1990 CRLJ 1507; 1990 CrLR(SC) 428; 1990 (41) DLT 234; 1990 (30) ECR 309; JT 1990 (2) SC 592; 1990(1) SCALE 863; 1990 (2) SCJ 359; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 979; 1990 (2) UJ 391

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 22(5) - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Detention order against appellant by UOI to prevent smuggling - Whether representation made by appellant considered after an inordinate delay violative of art. 21? - Held, delay in disposing representation of a detenu violative of art. 21 - Continued detention illegal and impermissible - Undue and unexplained delay is in violation of constitutional obligation enshrined in art. 22(5) rendering impugned order invalid - Even huge enormity of offences does not permit prolonged detention - Order of High Court set-aside, appellant set at liberty - Appeal allowed.
