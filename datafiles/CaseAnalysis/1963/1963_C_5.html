Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v C. M. Kothari

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 236; AIR 1964 SC 331; 1963 (49) HLR 107; [1963] 49 ITR 107; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 57; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 531

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16(3)(a)(iii)(A) - Whether there should be a consideration in case of chain transfers in order to come within ambit of s. 16(3)(a)(iii) of Income Tax Act? - Held, in the case of chain of transfers, consideration in the technical sense is not necessary - If the two transfers are inter-connected and are parts of the same transaction in such a way that it can be said that the circuitous method has been adopted as a device to evade implications of this section, the case will fall within the s. 16 - Appeal allowed.
