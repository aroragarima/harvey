Messrs Hindustan Coca Cola Beverage Private v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudershan Reddy, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 811; (2007) 8 SCC 463; AIR 2007 SC 2930; [2007] 293 ITR 226; JT 2007 (10) SC 169; 2007(10) SCALE 29; 2007 (163) SCJ 355; 2007 (6) SCJ 411; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 1046; [2007] 163 TAXMAN 355

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure To Deduct Tax At Source, Appeal By Special Leave

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 194 C, 194-I, 201(1) and 201(1 A) - Circular No. 275/201/95- IT(B) D\-29-1-1997 - Assessee was held to be 'assessee in default' for deducting of tax at source on warehousing charges in accordance with s. 194 C at 2% instead of s. 194-I at 20% - Penalty u/s. 201(1 A) was also imposed for short deduction of tax - Whether fact that warehouser been assessed on its income and tax due has been recovered from it, should be considered to conclude that no further tax could have been collected from deductor-assessee - Held, said fact was not considered earlier - Tax due had been paid by deductee-assessee - Circular applicable - Assessee liable to pay interest u/s. 201(1 A) - Appeal allowed.
