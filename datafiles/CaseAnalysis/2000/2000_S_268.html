Mohd. Chaman v State

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3127; (2001) 2 SCC 28; (2001) SCC (Cr) 278; 2001 (1) CCR 88; 2001 (1) Crimes 21; 2001 CRLJ 725; 2001 (89) DLT 107; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 570; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 147; 2000(8) SCALE 218; 2001 (1) SLT 123; 2000 (8) Supreme 333

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 376 - Murder - Rape - Conviction of - Whether the case can be classified as 'rarest of rare' for imposition of maximum penalty of death? - Principles laid down in Bachan Singh case - Formulations made in Machhi Singh case (1983 Indlaw SC 116) - Nature of injuries on accused - Circumstantial evidence - All injuries ante-mortem in nature - Cause of death is hemorrhagic shock consequent to liver injury - Testimony of witnesses - Crime committed is undoubtedly serious and heinous and conduct of appellant is reprehensible - Reveals a dirty and perverted mind of a human-being who has no control over his carnal desires - Testing case on touchstone of guidelines laid down in Bachan Singh and other decisions and balancing aggravating and mitigating circumstances emerging from evidence on record, are not persuaded to accept that case can be appropriately called one of 'rarest of rare cases' deserving death penalty - Find it difficult to hold that appellant is such a dangerous person that to spare his life will endanger community - Also not satisfied that circumstances of crime are such that there is no alternative but to impose death sentence even after according maximum weightage to mitigating circumstances in favour of offender - Death sentence imposed set aside - Appeals dismissed.
