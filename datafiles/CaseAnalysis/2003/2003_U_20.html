Udham Singh v Ram Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1581; (2007) 15 SCC 529

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Himachal Pradesh Tenancy and Land Reforms Act, 1972

Summary: Civil Procedure - Suit for permanent injunction - Decree - Application for adducing evidence - Effects of - Appellant-plaintiff was owner of land in question and entry of possession and occupancy tenancy was found recorded in favour of defendants in revenue records and it was cause to filing of suit by appellant for declaration and permanent injunction against defendants which was dismissed in default - Plaintiff again filed another suit before Trial Court which was allowed - Appeal made by defendants before first Appellate Court was dismissed - Respondent-defendant moved application for adducing additional documentary evidence which was rejected - Second appeal was made against order passed by first Appellate Court before HC where findings of two Courts below were set aside and allowed application filed for adducing additional documentary evidence by defendant - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order passed by HC was liable to be quashed and set aside - Held, though document was brought on record but no request seems to be made for summoning plaintiff - However, HC was wrong in attaching much weight to averments made in earlier plaint and coming to conclusion that defendants were admitted to be tenants by plaintiff on land in question - According to observations of HC on basis of averment made in plaint suit was cognizable by Civil Court - Averments and prayers made in plaint were relevant for purpose of deciding forum where cause would lie - HC went wrong while holding otherwise on basis of findings ultimately arrived at by HC on facts that defendants were not trespassers - Jurisdiction was not to be decided on basis of ultimate findings arrived at by Court - HC was erred in upsetting concurrent findings of fact arrived at by Trial Court and First Appellate Court after detailed and elaborate discussion of oral as well as documentary evidence on record - Therefore, HC misread documents and thereby upset findings of Courts below - Hence, order passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
