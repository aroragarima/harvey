Shakuntala v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 770; (2007) 10 SCC 168; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 454; AIR 2007 SC 2709; 2007 CRLJ 3747; 2007 (3) JCC 2183; JT 2007 (9) SC 433; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 925; 2007(9) SCALE 423; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Match Box, Medico-Legal Report

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, dying declaration is only a piece of untested evidence and must like any other evidence, satisfy the Court that what is stated therein is the unalloyed truth and that it is absolutely safe to act upon it - If after careful scrutiny the Court is satisfied that it is true and free from any effort to induce the deceased to make a false statement and if it is coherent and consistent, there shall be no legal impediment to make it basis of conviction, even if there is no corroboration - No material to show that dying declaration was result of product of imagination, tutoring or prompting - On the contrary, the same appears to have been made by the deceased voluntarily - It is trustworthy and has credibility - Appeal dismissed.
