Kerala State Housing Bd. v Ramapriya Hotels

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 926; (1994) 5 SCC 672; 1994 (2) CCC 806; [1996] 85 Comp Cas 36; JT 1994 (5) SC 113; 1994 (2) KLT 529; 1994(3) SCALE 565; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Kerala Land Acquisition Act, 1961, s. 3(1), s. 16(1) - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Compensation by an award - Completion of passing of the award after the acquisition proceedings are being unduly delayed. It could leads to manifest injustice. Having had the power to make an award under the agreement and without any limitation the Collector would be left with his discretion to make the ward leisurely at his whim or he may delay the issuance of the notification under Section 3(1) or may issue successive declarations under Section 6. This arbitrary exercise of power would result in hardship and manifest injustice to the owner of the land. "such date" referred to in the proviso must be referable to the date of the agreement and not to the date of the notification published under Section 3(1) of the Act. - Held, after the expiry of four years from the date of the agreement the Collector ceased to have power to pass the award under Section 16(1) - Appeal dismissed.
