Sales-Tax Officer, Circle-1, Jabalpur v Hanuman Prasad

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1967 SC 565; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 831; 1967 (19) STC 87

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 19(1) - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Whether proviso to s. 19(1) is applicable and what should be period of limitation in respect of reassesment? - Mere enforcement of that Act by time order of assessment was passed by Sales Tax Officer cannot lead to conclusion that assessment of respondent was made under new Act and not under repealed Act - Though principal clause of s. 19(1) was amended, proviso to it was not deleted by Amending Act - It is well-recognised that a proviso is added to a principal clause primarily with object of taking out of scope of that principal clause what is included in it and what legislature desires should be excluded - Even if it be held that effect of Amending Act was that, under principal clause of s. 19(1), reassessment of under-assessed or escaped turnover in case of respondent could be taken up within a period of five calendar years, that provision became ineffective because of continued existence of proviso - Appeal dismissed.
