Gillanders Arbuthnot and Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 63; AIR 1965 SC 452; [1964] 53 ITR 283; 1964 (2) SCJ 395; [1964] 8 S.C.R. 121

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913

Summary: Income tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 3 and 4 - Assessee was carrying on variety of agencies - One distributing agency terminated - Compensation paid to assessee for three successive years, by way of commission based on past rates - Compensation to be treated as capital receipt or income - Whether amount received by appellant as compensation for loss of agency are of nature of capital or revenue? - Examining circumstances of present case High Court was correct in holding that what was received by appellant was income and not capital - Cancellation of tile contract of agency did not affect profit making structure of appellant, nor did it involve a loss of an enduring trading asset - It merely deprived appellant of a trading avenue, leaving him free to devote his energies after cancellation to carry on rest of business, and to replace contract lost by a similar contract - Compensation paid, therefore, did not represent price paid for loss of a capital asset - Held, there is no immutable principle that compensation received on cancellation of an agency must always be regarded as capital - In each case question has to be determined in light of attendant circumstances - Appeal dismissed.
