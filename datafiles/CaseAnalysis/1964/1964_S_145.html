Kunwar Trivikram Narain Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1965 SC 1267; 1965 (1) ITJ 558; [1965] 57 ITR 17; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - U.P. Agricultural Income-tax (Amendment) Act, 1956 - U.P. Agricultural Income-tax Act, 1948 - U.P. Agricultural Income-tax (Amendment) Ordinance, 1956 - Assessment of agriculture tax - Reviving - Permissibility - On 10-1-1953, for A/Y 1952-53, Addl. Collector assessed agricultural income-tax of appellant under 1948 Act - On 9-2-1956, 1956 (Amendment) Ordinance was passed enacting that the word 'Collector' should always be deemed to include Add. Collector - That Ordinance was later replaced by 1956 Amendment Act - On application filed by appellant, Collector by his order dt.9-5-1956, revoked his earlier order and directed Addl. Collector to proceed to assess appellant in accordance with law - Thereupon, Addl. Collector resumed proceedings and on 7-6-1956, passed a fresh assessment order imposing a tax of Rs. 42,761 on appellant, and on 4-7-1956, he issued a notice to appellant for payment of tax - On 7-8-1956, appellant filed a petition for quashing order of assessment - HC dismissed said petition - On appeal, DB also dismissed the same - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent's right to assess appellant to tax was barred by limitation and, therefore, the Act could not have effect of reviving said right - Further contended that amount of malikhana could not be in law the subject-matter of assessment - Held, proceedings were initiated by Addl. Collector on 10-1-1953, for the purpose of assessing appellant for A/Y 1952-53 - There was no flaw in said proceedings except that Addl. Collector was not authorized by 1949 Act, as it then stood, to make said assessment - Collector quashed those proceedings by his order dt.26-11-1955 - After amendment Act was passed, within 90 days therefrom appropriate income-tax authority had filed application before Collector to review his order - Collector reviewed order and set aside same - Result was that proceedings before Addl. Collector were restored - As by amendment Addl. Collector should be deemed to have been Collector from inception of Principal Act itself, said proceedings should be deemed to have been initiated before proper authority under Principal Act - In this view no question of limitation could possibly arise, for proceedings were initiated in time and should be deemed to have been pending throughout and fresh assessment was made in said proceedings - It had expressly conferred power on appropriate authority to review its previous order if an application was filed within time prescribed - When once that power of review was exercised, the proceedings were reopened - In this view, no question of application of amending Act to a barred claim would arise - Order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
