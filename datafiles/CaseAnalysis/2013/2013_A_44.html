Aneesh D. Lawande and others v State of Goa and others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 552; (2014) 1 SCC 554; 2013 (6) Bom.C.R. 278; JT 2013 (12) SC 6; 2013(11) SCALE 149; 2013 (6) SLR 190

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Goa (Rules for admission to Postgraduate degree and diploma courses of the Goa University at the Goa Medical College) Rules, 2004

Summary: Education - Goa (Rules for admission to Postgraduate degree and diploma courses of the Goa University at the Goa Medical College) Rules, 2004 - Admission to medical courses - Cancellation - Propriety - Respondent candidates, eligible as per Rules, applied for admission to various medical courses - National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test (NEET) was adopted by State for admission to medical courses - Petitioner candidates, who passed NEET, sought admission to medical courses - Petitioners were afforded admission - However, NEET was declared as invalid by SC in Christian Medical College Vellore and others v Union of India and others, 2013 Indlaw SC 452 - State Govt. validated respondents' admission as per Rules and cancelled petitioners' admission - Hence, instant writ petition - Whether petitioners were entitled to continue their studies - Held, in Christian Medical College case, majority had stated that quashment of notification should not invalidate action already taken under amended regulations including admissions already given on the basis of NEET conducted by Medical Council of India and Dental Council of India - HC, on several writ petitions, directed to hold counselling in respect of both categories of students and permit admissions to students who had passed NEET subject to further orders - However, HC should not have entertained writ petition on three counts, namely, (i) all writ petitions challenging notification from all HCs had been transferred to SC; (ii) SC had been passing interim orders from time to time; and (iii) any order passed by HC had potentiality to usher in some kind of anomaly - Admissions given on the basis of NEET examination had been protected by SC and hence, their admissions could not have been cancelled by State Govt. - However, State Govt. in spite of SC order took a decision to cancel provisional admissions given to students on the basis of NEET merit examination - Such an action was absolutely impermissible - Hence, petitioners, who were admitted on the basis of NEET examination, should be allowed to prosecute their studies - Petitions disposed of.
