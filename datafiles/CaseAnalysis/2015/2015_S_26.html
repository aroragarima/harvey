Maharaji Educational Trust and others v SGS Construction and Dev. Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, H.L. Dattu, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 522; (2015) 7 SCC 130; 2015 (8) ADJ 426; AIR 2015 SC 2684; 2015 (111) ALR 477; 2015 (4) AWC 3849; JT 2015 (5) SC 604; 2015(6) SCALE 574; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965, Uttar Pradesh Revenue Code, 2006

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Uttar Pradesh Avas Evam Vikas Parishad Adhiniyam, 1965 - Uttar Pradesh Revenue Code, 2006, s. 24 - Demarcation of land - Jurisdiction of HC - Determination - Appellant no.1 Trust has taken loan from appellant no.3 Corporation for which Trust had mortgaged property in question along with other property in favor of appellant no.3 - Further, appellant no.1 failed to repay loan amount - Appellant no.1 had exchanged land in area 21 acres from appellant no2. vide Exchange Deed and had also deposited deed of exchange of said land with appellant no.3 - As loan was not repaid by appellant no.1, appellant no.3 started proceedings before Tribunal - Objections were filed by respondent builder on grounds that sale deed was executed by appellant no.1 in his favor with respect to property in question - Respondent builder filed petition praying to demarcate land in question - HC vide impugned order has directed appellant no.2 to demarcate 42.45 acres of mortgaged land and 21 acres as unencumbered land out of total area of 63.45 acres - Whether HC had jurisdiction to pass impugned order in favor of respondent builder.

Held, writ petition filed for twin reliefs, was not maintainable before writ court as it is not within ken of HC in writ jurisdiction to declare any property as unencumbered one. Such rights between private parties cannot be made subject-matter of writ jurisdiction as has been ordered in impugned judgment and order that out of total of 63.45 acres of land, 21 acres be demarcated as unencumbered property and to maintain status quo. SC held that it was not open to HC to enter into aforesaid arena, which of property is encumbered and to be sold in realization of debt is outlook of Tribunal where recovery proceedings are pending, including objections preferred by Builder. SC held that it was for parties to agitate questions before Recovery Tribunal where recovery proceedings are pending at instance of appellant no.3 with whom property had been mortgaged by Trust. SC held that on strength of agreement to sell, particularly when possession had not been handed over to respondent builder, it was not open to him to file writ application for demarcation of property as unencumbered property or otherwise. Further such disputed questions with respect to properties inter se between Builder and Trust as to demarcation, writ petition could not be said to be appropriate remedy, particularly when order passed by Recovery Officer was not in question and order passed by Tribunal could not have been questioned before HC. Further, property was situated in district of Ghaziabad which is not under territorial jurisdiction of Bench at Lucknow, hence, writ petition was not maintainable at Lucknow Bench. Further, SC held that for purpose of demarcation remedy is available before concerned authority u/s. 24 of Code and recourse to provision of Adhiniyam of 1965 in such cases was not available. Hence, impugned order passed by HC in favor of respondent builder is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - HC cannot grant reliefs with respect to demarcation of land under writ petition which is not within its jurisdiction.
