State of Bihar and Others v Project Uchcha Vidhya Sikshak Sangh and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 573; (2006) SCC (L&S) 355; (2006) 2 SCC 545; 2006 (5) AWC 4479; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 254; JT 2006 (1) SC 195; 2006(1) SCALE 122; 2006 (2) SCJ 179; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 14

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Affirmative Action

Summary: Constitution of India-Articles 15 (3), 21A, 162, 166 and 300A-Education - Keeping in view constitutional scheme in Articles 15 (3) and 21A, policy decision by State of Bihar to establish project schools-In remaining four years of sixth five year plan, at least 4 High Schools, one out of which to be Girls High School to be established in every block-Schools recognised in 1985-Qualifications for teachers and strength thereof prescribed by Government letter dated 4.2.1989-Said circular letter challenged by teaching and non-teaching staff of several schools before Patna High Court as arbitrary and discriminatory -Different orders passed in writ petitions some of which conflicting with others-Supreme Court directing resolution of dispute by Full Bench of Patna High Court-Judgment of Full Bench challenged by State of Bihar and others-Held, in view of fact that recognition of Schools wrongly granted to such schools where buildings also not completed or process of selection not over-Necessary for State to have further look in matter-Accordingly, Chief Secretary of State of Bihar to constitute committee of two officers and one educationist of repute and/or retired judicial officer-Judicial officer, if appointed, to be Chairman-Committee to examine matter and submit its reports-Appropriate decision to be taken on report within 4 months.
