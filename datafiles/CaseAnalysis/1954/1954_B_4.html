Â Bacha F. Guzdar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 8; AIR 1955 SC 740; [1955] 25 Comp Cas 1; [1955] 27 ITR 1; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 27; 1955 SCJ 68; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 876

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(1)(a), 4(3)(viii) and 6(v) - Companies Act, 1913, ss. 2(1), 192 and 2(2), Chap.1, Table A, regn. 95 - Partnership Act, 1932, s. 4 - Shareholder in Tea Company claiming income tax exemption in regard to dividend income as agricultural income - In light of policy and object of Act along with position of share-holder with respect to company's assets, dividend as an effective source of income and comparison with partners in a firm and Interpretation using English decisions - Whether exemption to be allowed or not? - Held, exemption cannot be claimed - English decisions cannot be used - Claim not allowed - And court declined to give view on contention that, conclusion that dividend is not agricultural income also follows from provisions of s. 16(2) and proviso to - Appeal dismissed.
