R.S. Sujatha v State Of Karnataka And Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1028; (2011) 5 SCC 689; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 154; 2011(2) ALL MR 476; 2011 (4) AWC 4119; 2010 (5) ESC 721; JT 2010 (13) SC 133; 2012 (1) KarLJ 42; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1159; 2011 (1) OLR 565; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 365; 2010(12) SCALE 556

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contempt of Courts (C.A.T.) Rules, 1992

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Rules, 1992, rr. 7, 13 and 15 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 191, 193 and 197 - Disciplinary proceedings - Respondent-State initiated disciplinary proceedings against the appellant, an IAS Officer, on the allegation that she had committed certain irregularities in the allotment of wheat under a special programme at public distribution system - Appellant filed application before the tribunal for quashing the articles of charges and subsequent proceedings initiated against her - Tribunal held that the appellant was guilty of perjury, as well as of criminal contempt of the Tribunal and imposed the punishment of imprisonment till rising of the court and a fine of Rs.2,000/- HC dismissed writ petition filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the tribunal could be upheld? - Held, the case had been at a preliminary stage, thus, the Tribunal ought not to have initiated the criminal contempt proceedings at such a pre-mature stage making reference to the provisions of ss. 191, 193 and 197 of IPC - Moreover, a criminal contempt case has neither been registered nor numbered separately - No charge had ever been framed by the Tribunal as mandatorily required under the rules - Thus question of furnishing the copy of the same to the appellant did not arise - Therefore, the contempt proceedings had not been concluded in conformity with the 1992 Rules at all - Impugned judgment of the tribunal set aside - Appeal allowed.
