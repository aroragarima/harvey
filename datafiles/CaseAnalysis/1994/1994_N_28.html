N.M.Engineer v Narendra Singh Virdi

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1699; (1994) 5 SCC 261; AIR 1995 SC 448; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 376; JT 1994 (5) SC 454; 1994 (2) RCJ 113; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 453; 1994 (2) RentLR 169; 1994(3) SCALE 246; 1995 (1) UJ 745

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 12(3)(a) - Whether Single Judge of High Court's decision to dismiss suit as not maintainable is correct? - Held, appellant having failed in his case u/s. 12(3)(a), cannot seek to reply on s. 12(3)(b) - U/s. 12(3)(a), there was a dispute about amount of rent - There were no arrears for six months outstanding and there is no negligence on part of respondent-tenant in making payment thereof notice is bad on that account - Notice referred to u/s. 12(3)(b) is entirely different - Thus, no exception could be taken to impugned judgment - Arrears of rent in hands of transferee becomes debt was not taken either in written statement filed by respondent not was issue framed by courts below - Therefore, u/art. 227, such a point could not be raised - It is purely a question of law arising on admitted facts and hence u/art. 227 such a point could validity be raised - HC arrogated to itself powers of a Court of appeal, which it did not posses under law, and has exceeded it jurisdiction u/art. 227 of Constitution - So, it is purely a question of law - (B) Whether notice D/- 02-05-1967 is in conformity with s. 12(3)(a)? - Held, s. 12(3)(a) consists of following (i) rent is payable by month and there is no dispute regarding amount of standard rent or permitted increases; (ii) If such rent or increases are in arrears for a period of six months or more; (iii) If tenant neglects to make payment - If all these conditions are satisfied, a decree for eviction could be passed - Whatever might have been due prior to deed of lease dated 08-06-1976, could not constitute arrears of rent - It was mere actionable claim - That being so, notice does not satisfy requirements of s. 12(3)(a), more so in this case, arrears at rate of Rs. 87 had been deposited - It is not open to appellant to call upon s. 12(3)(b) - Appeal dismissed.
