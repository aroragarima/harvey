Sushil Kumar Sabharwal v Gurpreet Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 285; (2002) 5 SCC 377; AIR 2002 SC 2370; 2002 (1) AllCJ 671; 2002 (47) ALR 679; 2002 (2) AWC 1600; 2002 (3) CalLT(SC) 77; 2002 (2) CGLJ 134; JT 2002 (4) SC 489; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 35; 2002 (131) PLR 382; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 431B; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 602; 2002(4) SCALE 280; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 352; 2002 (3) SLT 510; 2002 (3) Supreme 668; 2002 (2) UC 361B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Under the provisions contained in Order 9 Rule 6 of the C.P.C., the date appointed for hearing in the suit for which the defendant is summoned to appear is a significant date of hearing and requires a conscious application of mind on the part of the Court to satisfy itself on the service of summons, as, any default or casual approach on the part of the Court may result in depriving a person of his valuable right to participate in the hearing and may result in a defendant suffering an ex-parte decree or proceedings in the suit wherein he is deprived of hearing for no fault of his.
