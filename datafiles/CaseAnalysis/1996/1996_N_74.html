State of Punjab v National Organic Chemical Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1791; (1996) 11 SCC 613; (1997) SCC (Cr) 312; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 97; 1997 (1) CCR 55; 1996 (4) Crimes 169; JT 1996 (10) SC 480; 1996(8) SCALE 224; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 807; 1996 (8) Supreme 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Health & Drug; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Insecticide Analyst

Summary: Health & Drug - Agriculture & Agri Products - Insecticides Act, 1968, ss. 21, 22 and 24 - Respondent is a manufacturer of Monocil, an insecticide - Insecticide Inspector visited factory of respondent and had taken two samples of Monocil - Sent one of samples to Public Analyst, and it was not in conformity with ISI specifications - Opined that it is adulterated - Respondents requested sample to be sent to Central Insecticides Laboratory for analysis, which was not done - High Court held that appellant had deprived respondent of its defence due to delay, and quashed proceedings - Whether appellant has complied with statutory requirements envisaged u/s. 22 r/w s. 24(3) and (4) of Act? - Held, respondent, without availing of remedy of report by Director, Central Insecticides Laboratory, may not be entitled to plead deprivation of statutory defence - But complaint should be lodged with utmost dispatch so that accused may opt to avail of statutory defence - Appellant had not given third sample to respondent - As a result, respondent has been deprived of his statutory opportunity to have sample tested by Central Insecticides Laboratory - Resultantly, respondent has been deprived of a valuable defence statutorily available to him - HC's conclusion to quash complaint is right - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
