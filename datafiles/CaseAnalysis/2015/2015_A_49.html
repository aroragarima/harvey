S. E. B. I. v Alliance Finstock Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 782; 2015 (4) BC 578; [2015] 193 Comp Cas 424; 2016 (1) CompLJ 342; 2015 (8) MLJ 94; 2015(12) SCALE 271; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Financial Year, Partnership, Stock, Conversion, Membership, Brokers, Finance Bill, 1997, Securities and Exchange Board of India (Stock-Brokers and Sub-Brokers) Regulations, 1992, Employee's [Workmen's] Compensation Act, 1923

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Companies Act, 1956 - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, ss. 15Z, 30 - Retrospective benefit granted by Tribunal - Justification - Respondent broker was previously member of Bombay Stock Exchange (BSE) in his individual capacity or as partnership firm - Further, respondent broker opted to form corporate entity under provisions of 1956 Act prior to 1-4-1997 and carried on brokers' business under name and style of new corporate entity by getting its membership converted through approval of BSE leading to registration by SEBI as corporate entity - Further, broker were entitled to pay requisite fees provided in Regulation - Further, Tribunal granted benefit of fee continuity to all entities retrospectively - Appellant SEBI stated that since Para 4 of Schedule III was introduced by amending notification dt. 21-1-1998 which states in Para 2 that amendment will be effective from date of notification i.e. 21-1-1998, annual fee payable by registered brokers would remain unaffected for earlier year ending 31-3-1997 and it can at best be effected only in respect of fees payable for year 1-4-1997 onwards - Hence, it is contended that by SEBI that Tribunal has erred in granting retrospectively to provisions of para 4 by granting benefit of fee continuity even to entities which acquired corporate membership on conversion even prior to 1-4-1997.

Held, para 4 inserted through amendment w.e.f. 21-1-1998 but it does not disclose, either explicitly or even by necessary implication, that although possessing required qualifications, corporate entity formed earlier to 21-1-1998 would not be exempted from payment of fee for period for which erstwhile individual or partnership members has already paid fees. Further, explanation to para 4 introduced w.e.f. 20-2-2002 takes complete care of any doubt, if at all it could exist, by providing deeming fiction that in case of conversion of entities having individual or partnership membership card into corporate entity, corporate entity shall be deemed to be continuation of entity in respect of collection of fees from converted corporate entity. Further, embargo has been created against collection of fees again from converted corporate entity. Further, explanation does not talk of making any refund nor does it render initial levy or assessment of fee as bad but forbids collection of such fees if converted corporate entity is entitled to fee continuation benefit in terms of paragraph 4 of Schedule III to Regulations. Further, Somaiya Organics v State of Uttar Pradesh, 2001 Indlaw SC 462, followed, SC agreed that 'levy' and 'collection' are not synonyms and generally they occur at different stages. SC held that in present case legislative intention is to put embargo on collection in future, in case converted corporate entity is found entitled to benefits of fee continuity. Such embargo is clearly to operate prospectively even if there existed some kind of liability in past on account of fees leviable prior to insertion of paragraph 4 of Schedule III to Regulations. Further, SC held that in present case, retrospectively application of law provisions do not create any obligation or liability. They only confer benefits by way of fee continuity on account of fees already paid by earlier entity before its conversion into new corporate entity. Further, since policy behind grant of benefits is to encourage corporatization of individual or partnership members of stock exchange, action of extending such benefits without any curb on basis of date of conversions cannot be held as unfair. Further, SC held that SEBI itself extended benefit to those converting not only from 21-1-1998 but from 1-4-1997 and there is nothing in paragraph 4 or in explanation to support stand of SEBI that benefits must be confined to conversions taking place after particular date when no such date finds place in Regulations. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal in favor of respondent broker is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Benefits granted retrospectively cannot be challenged when there is no bar of granting such benefit under Regulations or statute.
