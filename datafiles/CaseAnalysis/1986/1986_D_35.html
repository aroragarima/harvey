District Council of The Jowai v Dwet Singh Rymbai

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 409; (1986) 4 SCC 38; (1986) SCC (Tax) 768; AIR 1986 SC 1930; [1988] 169 ITR 468; JT 1986 SC 146; 1986(2) SCALE 240; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Jawai Autonomous District (Administration) Act 1967, United Khasi and Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Management and Control of Forests) Act 1958

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 244 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Jawai Autonomous District (Administration) Act, 1967, s. 3 - United Khasi and Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Management and Control of Forests) Act, 1958, s. 8 - Whether District Council had no constitutional authority to impose either royalty or tax or fee on private forests and that Notification dated 20-04-1968 issued u/s. 8 of Act was ultra vires and not sanctioned by sch. VI of Constitution? - Held, appellants have not been able to establish that the impugned royalty was leviable under any other provision - It was no doubt true that it was argued before the High Court that it was open to the District Council to levy fees as quid pro quo for the services rendered by it to the forest owners or contractors - HC erred in holding that even fees could not be levied under para 3 of the sch. VI to the Constitution - Though there is no express provision to levy such fees, the District Council can levy fees under para 3 - But that would not save the Notification since there is no material placed before the court to uphold the notification on that ground - No evidence is placed before the court showing the expenses incurred by the District Council towards the services rendered and the total amount of royalty realized by it - Appeal dismissed.
