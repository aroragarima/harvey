Juggi Lal Kamlapat Bankers & Anr. v Wealth Tax Officer. Special Circle C-Ward, Kanpur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 189; (1984) 1 SCC 571; AIR 1984 SC 564; 1984 (39) CTR 47; [1984] 145 ITR 485; 1983(2) SCALE 953; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 35; [1984] 16 TAXMAN 1; 1984 UJ 841

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act 1957 - Hindu undivided family - Valuation of Wealth tax - Validity - Appellant No. 1/firm and appellant No. 2 was one of the partners in firm in his capacity as 'Karta' of HUF up to 15-3-1972 - Appellant no. 2 was being assessed to wealth tax in the status of HUF and assets so assessed for wealth tax included interest of family in appellant No. 1 firm - For A/Ys 1967-68 to 1972-73 wealth-tax returns were submitted by appellant No. 2 in the status of HUF and therein family's interest in appellant No. 1 firm was included - Since appellant No. 1 firm owned number of buildings in Kanpur in returns so submitted the book-value of those buildings were adopted by appellant No.2 for valuing interest of family in appellant No. 1 firm - Respondent No. 1/Wealth Tax Officer felt that market value of those buildings was much more than such book-value - Therefore, respondent no. 1 referred the question of valuation of those buildings to respondents Nos. 2 and 3 u/s.16A of Act - Respondents Nos. 2 and 3 issued notices u/ s. 38A (1) (b) of the Act to appellant No. 2 intimating that they would inspect buildings for determining the fair market value thereof and requested him to afford necessary facilities for such inspection and to produce certain records connected with those buildings - On receiving notices appellant No. 2 realised that respondent No.1 had referred the question of valuation of concerned buildings to respondents Nos. 2 and 3 u/s. 16A of the Act and that notices issued by respondents Nos. 2 and 3 were in furtherance of such reference - Appellant No.2 addressed a letter to respondent No. 1 and contended that none of properties referred to the Valuation Officers belonged to him and that reference to them was un authorised and the same should be with-drawn - Further contended that reference made to them by respondent No.1 was invalid and requested each one of them to return the reference back to Wealth Tax Officer - Since these contentions were not accepted by respondents, appellants filed a writ petition in HC - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Appellants contended that there was no provision for the inclusion of a Karta's interest in a partnership firm in HUF's net-wealth for wealth tax purposes under the Act and this would be clear from sec. 4 of the Act - Further contended that s. 7 (1) which enabled Wealth-tax officer to determine value of any asset, other than cash, at the market price thereof on valuation date for purposes of the Act was inapplicable to the instant case and, therefore, s. 16A was not attracted and hence valuation reference made by Wealth-tax officer to Valuation officers regarding specific house properties was liable to be set aside - Held, it clear that wealth tax was levied on the net- wealth of an individual or a HUF meaning thereby the aggregate value of all assets belonging to such assessee minus all the debts owed by him - Primary method of determining value of assets for the purposes of the Act was the one indicated in s. 7 (1), inasmuch as it provided that value of any assets, other than cash, for purposes of Act should be estimated to be its market price on the valuation date - It was optional for Wealth tax officer to resort to either of methods even in the case where the net value of a business carried on by assessee was to be determined - It was clear that even where Wealth-tax officer had resorted to sec. 7 (2) for determining value of assets of a business as a whole written down values or book values of specific assets as appearing in balance-sheet were not sacrosanct and when market value exceeded the written down value or book value by more than 20 per cent, Wealth-tax officer had to adopt the market value of such assets for the purposes of the Act - Since in instant case Wealth tax officer was of the view that book values of specific house properties as indicated in returns filed by appellant No. 2 were far below their market values, he was justified in making a reference to the Valuation officers u/s. 16A of the Act and notices issued by Valuation officers in pursuance of such reference were also valid - Appeal dismissed.
