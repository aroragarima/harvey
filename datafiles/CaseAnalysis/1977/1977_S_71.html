Narpal Singh & Others v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

01 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 140; (1977) 2 SCC 131; (1977) SCC (Cr) 262; AIR 1977 SC 1066; 1977 CRLJ 642; 1977 CrLR(SC) 219; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 901

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure ,1973, ss. 235(2) and 326 - Murder - Appellant denied of 'audi alterm partem' on question of sentence - Was awarded death penalty - (A)Whether in instant case it is mandatory for Session's Judge to hold a de novo trial? - Once Judge who hears evidence delivers a judgment of conviction, one part of trial comes to an end second part of trial is restricted only to question of sentence and so far as that is concerned, when a case is remitted by Appellate Court (SC) to Sessions Court for giving a hearing on question of sentence u/s 235(2), CrPC, 1973, there would be fresh evidence and principle that Sessions Judge may not act on evidence already recorded before his predecessor and must conduct a de novo trial would not be violated - Held, when case was thus remitted to Sessions Judge to give an opportunity to both sides to lead fresh evidence only on question of sentence, it was not necessary for Sessions Judge to hold a de novo trial -(B)Whether examination of witness after a lapse of 20 days renders evidence unacceptable? Person, not an eye-witness, examined by Police 20 days after occurrence - Held, this lapse on part of Police not sufficient ground to reject evidence of witness, because there is no intrinsic defect in his evidence, evidence can be accepted - (C)Whether non examination of signatories of of inquest report renders evidence invalid? -It is not at all necessary in law to incorporate statements of witnesses in inquest report -The inquest report is to be made by Investigating Office just to indicate injuries which he has found on bodies of deceased persons It may be witnessed by one or two persons but it is not at all necessary for Investigating Officer to record statements of witnesses or to get statements of witnesses signed on inquest report and incorporate same in it which introduces an element of chaos and confusion and demanding an explanation from prosecution regarding statements made therein - Further held, merely because signatories had not been examined, prosecution case could not be held to be not proved, when evidence of eye-witnesses was believed - (D)Whether instant case of prosecution is to be rejected on grounds of conflict between ocular and medical evidence? - Further held, in cases where there is any direct conflict between ocular and medical evidence that Court has to reject prosecution case - In instant case there is no such inconsistency between direct and medical evidence to render prosecution case unbelievable - Appeal partly allowed.
