Gurudevdatta Vksss Maryadit and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19874; (2001) 4 SCC 534; AIR 2001 SC 1980; 2001 (3) Bom.C.R. 61; JT 2001 (4) SC 11; 2001(2) SCALE 670; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 654; 2001 (3) SLT 844; 2001 (3) Supreme 28

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Election; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Society

Summary: Election - Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Administrative - Maharashtra Co-operative Societies (Second Amendment) Act, 2000 - Maharashtra Co-operative Societies (Amendment) Act, 2000 - Acts Interpretation Act, 1901, s. 15AB - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Maharashtra Co-operative Society Act, 1960, ss. 27(3), 73 B, 27, 40(3), 40(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 123(1), 123, 136, 213, 348(3) - Ordinance - Voter list - Challenged - Validity - Respondent/State Govt. had amended the 1960 Act by inserting proviso to s. 27(3) by the 2000 Act by which voting rights to federal society was provided only to those member societies which had completed three years from their admission - Similar provision was also made in s. 27(3A) of 1960 Act giving voting rights to only such individual members who had completed two years from their admission as members in society elections w.e.f 23-8-2000 - After this amendment came into force elections were declared to some societies and confused while preparing voter lists as to whether names of societies and individuals who were already admitted as members before 23-8-2000 could be included or not and to get clarification some societies filed writ petitions in HC and later in SC - State Govt. submitted to SC that amended provisions would not affect voting rights of existing member societies or members who had become members prior to amendment and in order to remove all doubts State Govt. proposed to add clarificatory provision in 1960 Act and as both Houses of State Legislature were not in session Governor of Maharashtra issued Ordiance Maharashtra Ordinance X of 2001 amending the 1960 Act and was published in Gazette -

Petitioners challenged restrictive list of voters added as per amendment to s. 27 (3) of 1960 Act stating that list of voters was finalised on 30-6-2000 and introduction of amended proviso effected after finalisation of voters list and was dismissed by HC holding that inclusion of societies which were eligible on 30 -6- 2000 could not be faulted or denied right to vote based on subsequent amendment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, though legislature was law making authority in some circumstances Governor could pass ordinances to deal with emergency situations when legislature was not in session - Statements of objects and reasons in normal course could not be termed as main/ principal aid in construction of statute - It was permissible to look into statement of objects and reasons of Bill which actuated steps to provide remedy for existing malady - SC in Thangal Kunju Musaliar v. M. Venkitachalam Potti, 1955 Indlaw SC 45 considered statement of objects and reasons for judging reasonableness of classification made in enactment to see if it infringed or was contrary to Constitution - Cardinal principle of interpretation of statute was that words of statute must be understood in their natural ordinary or popular sense and construed according to their grammatical meaning - Golden rule was that words of statute must prima facie be given their ordinary meaning - Requirement of completion of 3 years would apply only to those societies which became members of federal society after 20-8-2000 as rightly held by HC - Judgment of HC was justified - Appeal dismissed.
