Prakash v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 894; (2006) 13 SCC 508; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 101; 2007 CRLJ 798; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 263; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 682; 2006(12) SCALE 635; 2006 (3) ShimLC 398; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 907

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian Penal Code - High Court set aside conviction and sentence of Appellant under s. 302 r/w s. 34 and appellant was found guilty for commission of an offence punishable under s. 304 Part-I r/w s. 34 - Appeal against - Held, Common intention on the part of Appellant is evident - All the accused armed with lathis - Proof of participation by acceptable evidence in certain circumstances would lead to a conclusion that the accused had a common intention to commit the offence - Presence or absence of community of interests may not of much significance - Circumstances existing herein categorically establish formation of common intention amongst the accused - Appeal dismissed.
