State Of Himachal Pradesh v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 833

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, Inter-State Water Disputes Act, 1956, State of Himachal Pradesh Act, 1970, Himachal Pradesh and Bilaspur (New State) Act, 1954, State Reorganisation Act, 1966

Summary: (A) Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 131 - Indian Limitation Act, 1963 - Cause of action - Barred by limitation - Challenged - Plaintiff/State filed instant suit u/art. 131 of Constitution against defendants/UOI and other States with regard to power generated in Bhakra-Nangal and Beas Projects - Cause of action for filing suit arose when defendant no. 1/Central Govt. failed to determine lawful claim of plaintiff - Since Central Govt. failed to determine share of plaintiff in power generated in two projects, it also claimed compensation from Central Govt. in addition to other reliefs - Whether suit was not maintainable being barred by limitation, delay and laches - Held, there had been no final allocation of share of power from Bhakra-Nangal Project and Beas project to plaintiff yet and whatever allocations of power from two projects to plaintiff had been made were only adhoc or interim - Until a final decision was taken with regard to allocation of power to plaintiff from two projects, claim of plaintiff to appropriate allocation of power from two projects was live and could not be held to be stale or belated - Hence, suit was not barred by limitation, delay and laches - Suit partly allowed.

(B) Constitution - Administrative - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Merger of State - Share of power - Justifiability - Whether after merger of State of Bilaspur with Dominion of India, plaintiff could still have any cause of action to file instant suit - Held, plaintiff would not have any cause of action to make any claim on basis of any right of Raja of Bilaspur prior to merger of Bilaspur State with Dominion of India - However, claim of plaintiff to share of power from Bhakra-Nangal and Beas Projects in suit was based on provisions of the Act and provisions of Constitution were not affected by merger of State of Bilaspur with Dominion of India - Suit partly allowed.

(C) Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 363 - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966 - Draft agreement - Allocation of power - Entitlement - Whether instant suit was barred by reasons of art. 363 of Constitution - Held, there were no provisions which have any relevance to share of plaintiff to power generated in Bhakra- Nangal and Beas projects - However, draft agreement dt, 7-7-1948, had provisions in cl. 13 for allocation of power to Bilaspur State, but it was not proved to have been executed on behalf of parties thereto and could not constitute a basis for allocation of power to plaintiff - Thus, SC already held that claim of plaintiff was based on the Act and provisions of Constitution and such claim was not barred u/art. 363 of Constitution - Suit partly allowed.

(D) Constitution - Administrative - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 23 r. 6(a) - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, s. 78(1) - Cause of action - Maintainability - Whether instant suit did not disclose any cause of action against defendant nos. 3 and 4 and therefore liable to be rejected under O. 23 r. 6(a) of the Rules - Held, plaintiff had no legal right to claim a share of power from Bhakra- Nangal and Beas projects from out of share of power of defendant no. 4, plaintiff had no cause of action to file suit against defendant no.4 - Since plaintiff had legal right to utilization of power out of total share of power of composite defendant no. 2 from Bhakra-Nangal and Beas projects as a successor State, plaintiff had cause of action to file suit and to maintain suit as against defendant nos. 2, 3 and 5 - Moreover, as u/s. 78(1) of the Act defendant no. 1 was required to determine by an order rights of plaintiff to utilization of power from Bhakra-Nangal and Beas projects and defendant no. 1 had not done so, plaintiff had cause of action to file suit against defendant no.1 - Suit partly allowed.

(E) Constitution - Administrative - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, ss. 78 to 80 - Utilization of power - Jurisdiction - Whether instant suit was not maintainable by virtue of scheme of the Act, in general and provisions of ss. 78 to 80 of the Act in particular - Held, plaintiff had a legal right as a successor State of composite State to receive and utilize power generated in Bhakra-Nangal and Beas projects and this right was recognized by law and capable of being enforced by power of State - Therefore, SC had jurisdiction to decide extent to which plaintiff would be entitled to receive and utilize power generated in Bhakra- Nangal and Beas projects - Thus, instant suit of plaintiff was not barred by scheme of ss. 78 to 80 of the Act - Suit partly allowed.

(F) Constitution - Administrative - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966, s. 78(1) - Documentary evidence - Adhoc and final - Sustainability - Whether any agreement was reached between party States as regards their share in power generated (rights to receive and to utilize power generated) in Bhakra project - Held, documentary evidence before SC, clearly establishes that allocation of power to plaintiff to extent of 2.45% of share of power of composite State from both Bhakra and Beas projects was 'tentative and ad hoc' and not final - However, no final agreement between successor States of composite State with regard to rights and liabilities of successor States including right to power generated in Bhakra and Beas projects in terms of s.78(1) of the Act - Suit partly allowed.

(G) Constitution - Administrative - Net power generated - Free of cost - Entitlement -Whether plaintiff was entitled to 12% of net power generated in Bhakra- Nangal and Beas Projects free of cost from date of commissioning of projects - Held, formula of making 12% free power from energy generated by a power station was purely a policy- decision taken by defendant no. 1 much after Bhakra-Nangal Project and Beas Project were executed and in any case did not find place in any provision of law so as to confer a legal right on plaintiff to claim same - Hence, plaintiff was not entitled to 12% power generated from Bhakra-Nangal and Beas projects free of cost from date of commissioning of projects - Suit partly allowed.

(H) Constitution - Administrative - Punjab Reorganisation Act, 1966 - Commissioning of projects - Power requirements -Maintainability - Whether plaintiff was entitled to an allocation of 7.19% in addition to 12% free power as claimed above, of total power generated in Bhakra-Nangal and Beas Projects from date of commissioning of projects or appointed date - Held, equal treatment warranted that plaintiff was allocated 7.19% of total power generated in Bhakra-Nangal and Beas projects from appointed day as defined in the Act - However, considering fact that Chandigarh was capital of defendant nos. 2 and 3, these two States should meet power requirements of defendant no. 5 out of their share - Hence, what was received by plaintiff in regard to Bhakra-Nangal and Beas had to be deducted for purpose of finding out amount due to plaintiff from defendant nos. 2 and 3 - Suit partly allowed.

(I) Constitution - Administrative - Excess power - Award - Entitlement - Whether plaintiff was entitled to award of any interest on amounts determined as its entitlement - Held, since defendant nos. 2 and 3 had utilized power in excess of what was due to them under law, plaintiff would be entitled to interest at rate of 6% on amounts determined by defendant no. 1 to be due from defendant nos. 2 and 3 - Suit partly allowed.
