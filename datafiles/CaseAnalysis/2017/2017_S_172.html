Union of India v Simplex Infrastructures Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 308; 2017 (3) CLT(SC) 530

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitral Award, Jurisdiction, Arbitration Act, 1940, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Letters Patent Appeal

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 34(3), 37, 42 - Arbitral award - Stay - Condonation of delay - Respondent was allotted a contract by appellant - Appellant issued show cause notices to respondent and rescinded the contract - Arbitrator held that rescindment order passed by appellant was illegal and directed appellant to pay final bill - Appellant filed an application before District Court for stay of Award - District Court held that petition filed by appellant u/s. 34 of the Act was not maintainable on account of territorial jurisdiction - On appeal, Single Judge allowed application for condonation - On intra Court appeal, DB reversed order of Single Judge by invoking its jurisdiction under Letters Patent Appeals - Hence, instant appeals - Whether an intra-Court appeal u/cl. 15 of Letters Patent of HC could be maintained against an order passed by Single Judge on an application for condonation of delay along with petition u/s. 34 of the Act.

Held, DB made a fine distinction by holding that judgment of Single Judge of condoning delay in filing of petition u/s. 34 of the Act was without jurisdiction and not in terms of provisions of the Act. Further, remedy of appeal was provided only against an order of setting aside or refusing to set aside an arbitral award u/s. 34(1)(c) of the Act. No appeal was provided against an order passed by Court of competent jurisdiction condoning delay in filing petition u/s. 34 of the Act. The issue was answered against respondent by decision of SC in Fuerst Day Lawson Limited; (2) Jindal Exports Limited; (3) Ite India P. Limited; (4) Shivnath Rai Harnarain India Company; (5) Tinna Finex Limited; (6) Sea Stream Navigation Limited v. (1) Jindal Exports Limited; (2) Fuerst Day Lawson; (3) Mukesh Sharma And Others; (4) Glencore Grain Rotterdam; (5) National Ability S.A. And Another. (6) Lmj International Limited National Ability S.A. And Another., 2011 Indlaw SC 433. In that judgment Single Judge passed on an application purported to be u/s. 34(3) of the Act for condoning delay in filing of petition for setting aside arbitral award. The remedy of Letters Patent Appeal against that decision was unavailable. For, even if Single Judge might have committed manifest error or wrongly decided application for condonation of delay, that judgment was ascribable to exercise of jurisdiction u/s. 34(3) of the Act. In other words, whether prayer for condonation of delay could be accepted or whether application deserves to be rejected was a matter well within the jurisdiction of that Court. However, SC cannot permit respondent to agitate that plea in instant appeal preferred by appellant challenging impugned decision of DB. SC deem it appropriate to leave all contentions available to both sides open and give liberty to respondent to challenge judgment of Single Judge if so advised. Impugned judgment of DB was set aside. Appeals allowed.
