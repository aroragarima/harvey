State Inspector of Police Visakhapatnam v Surya Sankaram Karri

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 446; (2006) 7 SCC 172; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1860; 2007 (2) ALT 50; 2006 CRLJ 4598; 2006 (4) ESC 404; JT 2006 (8) SC 177; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 53; 2006(8) SCALE 451; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 229; 2006 (6) Supreme 560

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 17, 6(1) - Jurisdiction - Conviction and sentence set aside by HC - Appeal against - Held, respondent had suffered miscarriage of justice as investigation made by PW41 was not fair - Trial Judge committed a serious error in so far as he failed to take into consideration that PW37, being a Senior Divisional Operations Manager, was not competent to accord sanction for prosecution of respondent herein - When a sanction is granted by a person not authorized in law, same being without jurisdiction, would be a nullity - Appeal dismissed.
