Srihari (Dead) Through Lrs. Ch. Niveditha Reddy v Syed Maqdoom Shah and others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 623; (2015) 1 SCC 607; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 70; 2014 (107) ALR 368; 2014 (6) AWC 6201; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 35; 2015 (3) CTC 679; JT 2014 (12) SC 199; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4542; 2015 (3) MahLJ 582; 2015 (2) MPLJ 654; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 557; 2015 (126) RD 245; 2014(10) SCALE 540; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 864; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 12

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC), ss. 151, 152 and 153 - Power of Amendment of Judgments, Decrees or Orders - Powers of Court - Scope thereof - Held, the section is meant for correcting the clerical or arithmetical mistakes in judgments, decrees or orders or errors arising therein from any accidental slip or omission. The powers u/s. 152 of the CPC are neither to be equated with the power of review nor can be said to be akin to review or even said to clothe the Court under guise of invoking after the result of the judgment earlier rendered. The corrections contemplated under the section are of correcting only accidental omissions or mistakes and not all omissions and mistakes. The omission sought to be corrected which goes to the merits of the case is beyond the scope of S. 152 of CPC.

Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 (CPC), Section 152 - In an appeal against order dismissing suit for partition, Appellate Court after reappreciating evidence decreed the suit - Later High Court vide its impugned order clarified on shares in the preliminary decree passed in exercise of powers under Sections 151 read with ss. 152 and 153 of the CPC - Whether by the impugned order, the High Court had only corrected the clerical, arithmetical or accidental omission in the decree passed or not.

Held, an unintentional mistake of the Court which may prejudice the cause of any party must be rectified. However, Court cannot go into the merits of the case to alter or add to the terms of the original decree or to give a finding which does not exist in the body of the judgment sought to be corrected. Had the appellate court, not decreed the suit with discussion of evidence after rejecting the plea relating to claim of ownership and had there been no pleadings for separation of shares with admission of share of the Plaintiff as decreed by the Appellate Court, it could have been said that the High Court erred in declaring shares of the Parties by resorting to S. 152 of the CPC. However, in the present case since there was a clear finding of shares of the parties in the judgment and order and by clarifying the decree by the impugned order, High Court committed no mistake of law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Court in exercise of Powers u/s. 152 of the Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 cannot go into the merits of the case to alter or add to the terms of the original decree which does not exist in the body of the judgment sought to be corrected
