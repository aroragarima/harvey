Kapurchand Shrimal v Tax Recovery Officer, Hyderabad and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 84; AIR 1969 SC 682; 1969 (1) ITJ 709; [1969] 72 ITR 623; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 691

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Tax Recovery Officer

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s. 46 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 220 and 222 - Hindu Undivided Family committed default, and ITO issued a certificate u/s. 46 of Act, 1922 for recovery of tax due - Pursuant to this, properties of HUF were attached - Held, any sum payable under Act, 1922 may be recovered under Act, 1961, but without prejudice to any action already taken for recovery under Act, 1922 - Proceedings could therefore be taken for recovery of tax due for assessment years 1955-56 to 1959-60 by HUF under Act, 1961 - As per s. 220 of Act, 1961 deals with payment of tax and conditions in which an assessee may be deemed to be in default - Further held, High Court should have allowed appellant's contention that he was not liable to be arrested and imprisoned for non-payment of tax arrears - Appeals allowed.
