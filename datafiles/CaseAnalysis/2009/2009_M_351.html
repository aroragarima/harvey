A. Abdul Farook v Municipal Council, Perambalur & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 939; (2009) 15 SCC 351; JT 2009 (10) SC 331; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1659; 2009(10) SCALE 325; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 727

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Highways Act, 2001, Tamil Nadu District Municipalities Act, 1920, Public Health Act, 1875

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Administrative - Carriers & Transportation - Tamil Nadu Highways Act, 2001, s. 26 - Notification, GOMs no. 32 - Interpretation and/or application of s. 26 of Act - Vide GOMs no. 32, Govt. of Tamil Nadu granted permission for installation of statues and erection of arches alongside the roads, for which there was a procedure - In exercise of power u/s. 3 of Act, State Govt. vide its notification declared some of the roads as State Highways, Major District Roads and other District Roads; roads in question were Major District Roads, which were declared to be highways belonging to the Govt. - Requisition was moved by one Ravichandran to respondent/Municipal Council for issuance of 'No Objection Certificate'(NOC) for erecting arches on occasion of Birthday of CM - NOC was issued; and further procedure was followed, and ultimately permission was granted to construct the arches - Writ petition by Ward Councillor was filed against issuance of NOC, which was dismissed, and writ appeal preferred thereagainst was also dismissed - Dismissal whether proper? - Held, No - Object and scheme of Act, noted, provisions of s. 26 and other relevant provisions, perused - Contentions heard - S. 26(1) is mandatory in character; with a view to prevent unauthorised occupation of highway or encroachment thereof, at the hands of third parties, provisions of s. 26 was enacted; it is applicable to third parties and not to the Highway authorities; power to grant permission for erecting any arch or any other constructions strictly lies with the Highway authorities; State, after coming into force of the said Act, is denuded of its power in the matter of grant of any permission; HC committed manifest error in holding that the State would exercise its jurisdiction in terms of GOMs no. 32 - On contention being raised on this behalf, Court raised assumption that any construction on the roads declared under the Act, shall not be an encroachment as defined under the Act, if it is undertaken with permission under the GOMs no. 32; and after raising such assumption, Court took surprise as to that why the State shall grant permission to erect such arches at the instance of a private party - State being the principal protector of the rights of its citizens, keeping in view the doctrine of public trust, should not have granted such permission, Court opined - With the coming into force of the Act, GOMs 32 must be held to have been repealed - State Govt. thus had no jurisdiction to pass the order impugned in writ application - Road authorities have full rights over acquired lands over which roads are laid out, free from all encumbrances; however in the interest of general public and pedestrians and others, they may grant permission to construct anything on the roads or alongside it, even if it is permanent in character, as it may seem fit; what is important is 'public interest' and not any private interest or interest of a political party - Doctrine of good governance, requires the Govt. to rise above their political interest and act only in public interest and for welfare of its people - Impugned judgement being unsustainable, set aside - Appeals allowed.
