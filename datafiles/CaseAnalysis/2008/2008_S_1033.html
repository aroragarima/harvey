Rajbabu and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1176; (2008) 17 SCC 526; AIR 2008 SC 3212; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2894; 2008 CRLJ 4301; 2008 (3) JCC 2013; JT 2008 (8) SC 250; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 890; 2008(10) SCALE 437; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 270

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ill- Treatment

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 306, 498A - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, a perusal of the dying declaration would prove and establish that there is nothing incriminating in the said statement against the appellant and, therefore, the said dying declaration, which was exculpatory in nature, so far as the prosecution is concerned is of no relevance and would rather help the accused appellants - After analyzing the evidence and statements made by PW1 and PW3 benefit of doubt should be granted to appellant No. 2. - Appeal allowed.
