Braithwaite and (India) Limited v Employees' State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1968 SC 413; 1968 (16) FLR 237; 1968 LabIC 364; 1968 (1) LLJ 550; 1968 (2) SCJ 858; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Indirect Tax - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - Whether the 'Inam' paid by the appellant is covered by the definition of "wages" as given in s. 2(22) of the Act - Promise on behalf of the appellant not to deny the payment of 'Inam' does not indicate that a right to receive the Inam had accrued to the employees as an implied condition of contract of employment - Held - The appeal is allowed with costs, the order passed by the High Court is set aside and the order passed by the Employees' Insurance Court it restored.
