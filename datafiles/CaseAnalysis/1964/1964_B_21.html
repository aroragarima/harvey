S. M. Karim v Bibi Sakina

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 466; AIR 1964 SC 1254; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 780

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 66 - Declaration of Title - Confirmation of possession - Appellant son of one 'S' and the respondent was transferee of the properties in dispute from son-in-law of 'S' - Appellant alleged that his father 'S' had purchased the suit properties at a court sale, benami in the name of his son-in-law 'H' and thereafter sold the property to appellant and 'H' also sold the property in his turn - Suit filed for declaration of title and confirmation of possession or in the alternative for delivery in respect of properties was decreed - On appeal, HC reversed the concurrent judgments of the two courts below, and ordered the dismissal of the suit of the appellant - (A) Whether suit was barred u/s. 66 of the CPC? - Held, s. 66 of the CPC provides that no suit shall be maintained against any person claiming title under a purchase certified by the Court on the ground that the purchase was made on behalf of the plaintiff or on behalf of someone through whom the plaintiff claims - Protection was thus available not only against the real purchaser but also against anyone claiming through him - In the present case, the appellant's claim based upon the benami nature of the transaction cannot stand as it was barred by s. 66 of CPC - (B) Plea of adverse possession - It was contended that the title of 'H' was extinguished by long and uninterrupted adverse possession of 'S' and after him of the appellant - Held, adverse possession must be adequate in continuity, in publicity and extent and a plea is required at the least to show when possession becomes adverse so that the starting point of limitation against the party affected can be found - In the present case, there was no evidence as to when possession became adverse - Appeal dismissed.
