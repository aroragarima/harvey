Bibi Saddiqa Fatima v Saiyed Mohammad Mahmood Hasan

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 306; (1978) 3 SCC 299; AIR 1978 SC 1362; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 886

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Women & Children

Keywords: Distributable Income, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Hindu Law Women's Rights Act, 1933 - Mysore Hindu Law Women's Rights Act, 1933 - Mussalman Wakf Validating Act, 1913 - Wealth Tax Act, 1957 - Whether property acquired by Raja for plaintiff was her personal property and not property of waqf? - Held, in this case that a valid waqf was created by Smt. Sughra Begum - Ultimate object of waqf was to spend income, if any, in service of Almighty God - Evidence is overwhelming on question as to what was source of money for acquisition of disputed property, either land or kothi - It came from waqf fund - Concurrent findings of Courts below that expenses for acquisition of lease were incurred from waqf estate funds could not be successfully assailed - Numerous documents are mentioned in judgments of Trial Court as also of High Court to show that every bit of expenditure in construction of Kothi came out of the waqf fund under direction of Raja - Power given to Raja u/cl. 18 had to be reasonably exercised within a reasonable limit of variation according to exigencies and special needs of a particular beneficiary - He had no power to spend money quite disproportionately for benefit of one beneficiary - There will be no escape from position that real owner of property was Raja in his capacity as Mutawalli and plaintiff was a mere benamidar - Property in reality, therefore, belong to waqf estate as concurrently and rightly held by two courts below - If property became acquired property of waqf a Mutawalli, as Raja was, by his mere declaration contained in clause 19 of Will could not make it a property of lady - Appeal dismissed.
