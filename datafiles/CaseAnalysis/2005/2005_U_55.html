Sarbananda Sonowal v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 404; (2005) 5 SCC 665; AIR 2005 SC 2920; JT 2005 (6) SC 79; 2005(5) SCALE 375; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 472

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners

Keywords: People, Migrated, Registration of Foreigners Act, 1939, Immigration Act, 1971, Immigrants (Expulsion From Assam) Act, 1950, Citizenship (Amendment) Act, 1985, Passport (Entry into India) Act, 1920, The Passport (Entry into India) Act, 1920

Summary: [A.] Foreigners - Criminal - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 13, 14, 19(1)(d), 19(1)(e), 21, 213, 29(1), 32, 355 - Illegal Migrants (Determination By Tribunals) Act, 1983,(the IMDT Act), ss. 25, 3(1)(c), 8 - Foreigners Act, 1946, ss. 12, 14A, 14B, 2(a), 21, 28, 3(2)(c), 4(2), 4(3), 5, 9 - Citizenship Act, 1955, ss. 6-A(1)(d), 6-A(3), 6A, 9 - Registration Of Foreigners Act, 1939 - Citizenship (Amendment) Act, 1985 - Passports Act, 1967 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Immigration Act, 1971 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 167(8)(c) - Immigrants (Expulsion From Assam) Act, 1950 - Passport (Entry into India) Act, 1920 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 176 - Illegal Migrants (Determination by Tribunals) Rules, 1984, r. 7 - Whether Act ultra vires Constitution of India, null and void? - Held, procedure under Foreigners Act and also under Foreigners (Tribunals) Order, 1964 is far more effective in identification and deportation of foreigners as compared to procedure under IMDT Act and Rules made there under - No corresponding provision like s. 9 of Foreigners Act which places burden of proof upon person concerned who claims to be an Indian citizen, which is absolutely essential in relation to nature of inquiry being conducted regarding determination of a person's citizenship - Provisions of IMDT Act and Rules clearly negate constitutional mandate contained in art. 355, where a duty has been cast upon Union of India to protect every State against external aggression and internal disturbance - IMDT Act is made applicable only to State of Assam has no rational nexus with policy and object of Act, it is clearly violative of art 14 and is liable to be struck down on this ground also - IMDT Act and Rules declared to be ultra vires Constitution of India and are struck down - Petition allowed.
