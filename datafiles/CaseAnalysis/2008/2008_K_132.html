T. O. Anthony v Karvarnan and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 280; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 738; (2008) 3 SCC 748; 2008 (1) ACC 706; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1646; 2008(3) ALL MR 902; 2008 (2) AWC 1151; 2008 (1) CCC 387; 2008 (1) CPJ(Sc) 84; 2008 (6) CTC 23; 2008 (4) JhrCR(SC) 47; JT 2008 (3) SC 297; 2008 (3) KLT 431; 2008 (5) MahLJ 7; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1124; 2008 (151) PLR 462; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 120; 2008(3) SCALE 174; [2008] 2 S.C.R. 291

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Personal Injuries, Loss Of Future Earning, Negligence Of Driver

Summary: Motor Accident Claims - Tribunal held that accident occurred due to the composite negligence of drivers of both vehicles and it could not be said that the accident occurred solely due to the negligence of the first respondent and determined the compensation as Rs.78, 500/- and deducted 50% for appellant's negligence, and awarded Rs.39, 250/-- High Court increased the compensation and directed payment of an additional compensation of Rs.39, 900/- - Appeal against - Held, evidence shows that the appellant was not diligent, as he neither slowed down the bus nor swerved to his left, on seeing the oncoming bus - Fix the responsibility at 25% on the appellant and 75% on the first respondent - Compensation awardable to the appellant will be Rs.1, 18, 725 - Appellant is entitled to an additional sum of Rs.39, 575 with interest @ 9% P.A. - Appeal partly allowed.
