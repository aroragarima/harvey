Union Of India & Anr v Hansoli Devi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

03 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1116; (2010) 15 SCC 483

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Re-determination of

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 28-A - Compensation - Re-determination of - Legality - DB allowed writ petition directing re-determination of amount of compensation payable to claimants/respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether it was not permissible for claimants to move another application u/s. 28-A of the Act for re-determination of amount of compensation after reference court has decided yet another land reference case.

Held, though, the HC was right in assuming to a certain extent that when there are more than one award arising out of the claims made in respect of the acquisition under the same notification, the claimants seeking redetermination may avail of anyone or other of such awards, apparently seems to have fallen into an error in over looking the fact that such scope to avail of the remedy u/s. 28-A of the Act on the basis of anyone or more than one awards made on different occasions or point of time by the reference court, cannot be also for more than once. Order of the HC is set aside and the writ petition filed before the HC by the claimants shall stand dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Only one application can be moved u/s. 28-A of the Act for redetermination of compensation by applicant.
