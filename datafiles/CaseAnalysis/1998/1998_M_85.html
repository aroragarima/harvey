Mrs. Helen C. Rebello & Ors v Maharashtra State Road Transport Corpn. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1215; (1999) 1 SCC 90; (1999) SCC (Cr) 197; 1999 ACJ 10; AIR 1998 SC 3191; 1999 (1) AWC 164; 1999 (3) Bom.C.R. 106; [1999] 95 Comp Cas 509; JT 1998 (6) SC 418; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 684

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit, English Fatal Accidents Act, 1846 (ENG)

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 110 B - Fatal Accidents Act, 1855, s. 1A - word 'just' - Meaning of - Compensation - Held, widens scope of determination of compensation - Should be interpreted in a way which confers benefit and not which usurp its benefit - Pecuniary advantage received by claimant to be deductible should have correlation with accidental death and no other form of death - Therefore, Life insurance amount received by claimants is not deductible.
