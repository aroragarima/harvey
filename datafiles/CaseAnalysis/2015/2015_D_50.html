FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

H. S. Sidhu v Devendra Bapna and others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 841; (2016) 1 SCC 495; 2015 (6) AWC 5838; 2015 (147) FLR 883; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Grade, Dpc, Inter Se Seniority, Gradation List, Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (General Conditions of Services) Rules, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Development Authority Services (Officers and Servants) Recruitment Rules, 1987

Summary: Employment - Promotion - To post of Director-Appellant and first respondent initially appointed as Assistant Directors as direct recruits through Public Service Commission and their service conditions governed by M. P. Fisheries (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1987 with certain incorporation from M. P. Services (General Conditions of Service) Rules, 1961-Appellant and first respondent promoted to post of Deputy Directors and in that cadre first respondent treated as senior to appellant-While appellant and first respondent officiating as Joint Directors, post of Director fall vacant and both of them were considered-D.P.C., considering merit and suitability of appellant recommended him to be promoted to post of Director-After appellant promoted temporarily to officiate as Director, same challenged by first respondent-On scrutiny of A.C.Rs. and other materials, D.P.C. found that appellant received more marks than first respondent-D.P.C. after due evaluation of A.C.Rs. and consideration of merit by ascribing reasons prepared merit list-Single Judge and Division Bench of High Court guided by principle of seniority-cum-fitness, but proviso to Rule 15 (3) of M. P. Fisheries (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1987 states position differently laying emphasis on exceptional merit and suitability-Single Judge and Division Bench of High Court not appreciated tenor and content of Rule 15 (3), especially proviso thereof-If assessment made by D.P.C. is perverse or not based on record or proper record has not been considered by D.P.C., it is open to High Court under Article 226 of the Constitution to remit matter back to D.P.C. for recommendation-But it cannot assess merit on its own on perusal of service record of one or other employee-Single Judge and Division Bench of High Court exercised their jurisdiction over proceedings of D.P.C. apart from taking note of seniority on erroneous perception-Impugned order wholly unsustainable and set aside.

Neither the appellant nor the 1st respondent was confirmed and, therefore, the rules relating to seniority as far as the confirmed employees are concerned, do not apply. The Rule that really applies is Rule 12 (c) of M.P. Services (General Conditions of Service) Rules 1961, which deals with seniority of Government servant. The proviso to Rule 12 (c) makes it quite vivid that if an officer has been selected for officiation from a list in which he is considered suitable for trial in a promotion and the said list has been arranged in order of merit, their inter se seniority shall be determined in accordance with the order of merit. In such a situation, there can be no scintilla of doubt that when the D.P.C. had drawn the list on the basis of inter se merit, the fixation of seniority could not be found fault with. It needs no special emphasis to state that while exercising the power under Article 226 of the Constitution, the High Court cannot sit in appeal over the assessment made by the D.P.C. In Union of India v. S. P. Nayyar, (2014) 14 SCC 370 : 2014 (4) SCCD 1899 (SC), it has been stated that if the assessment made by the D.P.C. is perverse or not based on record or proper record has not been considered by the D.P.C., it is open to the High Court under Article 226 of the Constitution to remit the matter back to the D.P.C. for recommendation, but it cannot assess the merit on its own on perusal of the service record of one or the other employee. Thus, analysed the view expressed by the High Court in the impugned order is wholly unsustainable.
