Union of India v ABN Amro Bank and others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 884; (2013) 16 SCC 490; 2014 (1) CompLJ 80; JT 2013 (10) SC 124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Keywords: Press Note, Foreign Exchange Regulation (Amendment) Act, 1993

Summary: (A)Criminal - FEMA - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act,1973, ss.19,29(1)(b),47,49,68 - Trade in gold coins - Grant of general permission - Special Director of Enforcement initiated proceedings against respondents-Company for violation of provisions of ss.19(1)(a),19(1)(d),29(1)(b), 47(1),49(i)(a) r/w s.68 of the Act - Respondents preferred four appeals before Appellate Tribunal for Foreign Exchange who allowed said appeals and set aside order of confiscation and penalty imposed - UOI preferred Appeal before HC which was dismissed, stating that neither any question of law nor any legal infirmity had been found in order passed by Tribunal - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether company has obtained any general permission u/s.29(1)(b) of the Act r/w notification and if so in what activity.

Held, there is no indication whatsoever that company was set up for trading, but only indicated 'consultancy for trading'. Further, Para IX (iii) called for description of products for export trading wherein company has stated as 'not applicable'. Purpose for which company had sought for foreign collaboration was not for trading in gold coins either for export or domestic purpose, but for activities mentioned in NIC Code 893. Company cannot go back from information already furnished by it in application, form which are declared as 'true and correct'. Based on that application RBI granted registration which pertains only to NIC code '893'. Thus, no permission was obtained by respondent no.2-company from RBI for 51% foreign equity induction, for trading, by way of export. RBI, on other hand, granted general permission only for dealing with activities mentioned in NIC Code 893 and not for any trading activities leading to import or export. Therefore, HC has committed error in holding that no questions of law arose for its consideration u/s. 54 of the Act and has completely misread and misinterpreted Industrial Policy, Press Notes and ss.19(1)(a),19(1)(b),29(1)(a),29(1)(b) etc of the Act. Appeals allowed.

(B)Criminal - FEMA - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act,1973, s.6 - Import of gold coins - Misuse of permission - Whether Bank has contravened s.6(5) of the Act and misused permission granted to it by RBI for importing gold coins.

Held, proceedings were initiated against company and others as per directions given by RBI and it was noticed that bank had also sold gold coins to company without being reasonably satisfied about nature of business of company. Adjudicating authority took view that Bank as authorized dealer, should have ascertained whether company had got necessary permission from RBI in dealing with gold coins. Bank, it is seen, had imported gold on its own behalf and sold same to company and if Bank was acting as agent of company, it would not have sold gold to company, but would have charged commission for acting as agent. No materials have been placed to show that Bank was acting as agent of company. On facts, Tribunal as well as HC took view that Bank had not misused permission granted by RBI for importing gold coins. Therefore, there is no error in view taken by Tribunal as well as HC that proceedings initiated against Bank that it had violated s.6(4),(5) of the Act was illegal. Thus, appeal filed by UOI so far as Bank is concerned, stands dismissed. Appeals allowed.
