M. Natarajan v State By Inspector of Police, Spe, Cbi, Acb

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1016; (2008) 8 SCC 413; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 507; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 785; 2008 (226) E.L.T. 679; 2008 (3) JCC 1552; JT 2008 (6) SC 451; 2008(8) SCALE 290; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998, Duty Exemption Entitlement Certificate, Under Trial, Interest Tax Act, 1974, Expenditure Tax Act, 1987, Kerala Sales Tax Act

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 120B r/w 420, 468, 471; Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) - Held, merely because there may be some overlapping in the two offences, it does not mean that the appellant cannot be tried under the offences covered under the Indian Penal Code - Merely because the appellant could be tried under s. 132 of the Customs Act, it does not mean that he could not be tried for the offence committed under the Indian Penal Code - Appeals dismissed.
