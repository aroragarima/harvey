Nicholas Piramal India Limited v Harisingh

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 311; (2015) 8 SCC 272; 2015 (145) FLR 1003; JT 2015 (4) SC 370; 2015 (2) LLJ 609; 2015(5) SCALE 701

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Reinstatement - Withholding of half back wages - Legality of - Respondent was employed as a workman at the drug manufacturing unit of appellant - Company issued two charge sheets against respondent, alleging that he has violated and disregarded orders of his senior officers - After conducting enquiry, order of dismissal of respondent was passed by appellant - Labour Court set aside the order of dismissal passed against respondent and appellant was directed to reinstate respondent in service with 50% back wages - Industrial Court dismissed appeal of appellant - HC confirmed finding of Industrial Court - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether concurrent finding of facts recorded by HC in not interfering with order of Industrial Court is legal and valid.

Held, Labour Court at the first instance has erroneously failed to exercise its jurisdiction by not re-appreciating evidence on record after holding that preliminary issue regarding domestic enquiry conducted by appellant is legal and valid. Said erroneous finding was challenged by respondent in Appellate Court after two remand orders were passed by Industrial Court. Thereafter, Labour Court has exercised its jurisdiction and on re-appreciation of facts and evidence on record, it has found fault with findings of Inquiry Officer which was endorsed by the Disciplinary Authority which has erroneously held that workman was guilty of misconduct. Further, Labour Court rightly held that punishment of dismissal is disproportionate and interfered with same by imposing the lesser punishment and same has been examined and rightly upheld by Appellate Court and HC. Hence, denial of 50% back wages to workman by Labour Court is itself a punishment imposed upon workman. There was no good reason to interfere with impugned judgment and awards of HC as well as Appellate Court and Labour Court. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Concurrent findings of Courts below shall not be interfered unless error is apperent on face of record.
