Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation and others v Madu Giri (Dead) and another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 265; (2013) 11 SCC 603; JT 2013 (9) SC 86; 2013 (2) LLJ 577; 2013 (3) RLW 2320; 2013(6) SCALE 385; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 464; 2013 (4) SLR 588

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Refund of CPF, Rejection of pensionary benefits

Summary: Service - Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation Employees Pension Regulations, 1989, cl. 3 - Refund of CPF - Rejection of pensionary benefits - Entitled to - Respondents employees of appellant corporation retired from service and were paid Contributory Provident Fund(CPF) - However, the 1989 Regulations, came into force - As per cl. 3(1) of the Regulations, option was given to existing employees as well as those employees who retired before coming into force of the Regulations but before acceptance of option and grant of benefit condition was placed on employees to refund employer's share of CPF with interest - Respondents exercised their option in favour of Pension Scheme under the Regulations, but did not deposit amount of employer's share of CPF with interest in lumpsum within stipulated time - However, respondents claim for grant of pensionary benefit was rejected by appellant - Respondents filed writ petitions, which were disposed by HC with direction to appellant to accept option submitted by respondents with regard to grant of pension - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, DB had considered the Regulations but failed to notice that there was apparent error in order passed by Single Judge - Indisputably, concerned employees respondents retired from service in 1991 and 1992 and after retirement they were paid CPF including share of employer's contribution - Hence, as per cl. 3 of the Regulations, no right accrued to respondents to claim pensionary benefits without first depositing amount and complying with the Regulations - Impugned orders passed by Single Judge and DB of HC could not be sustained in law - Appeal allowed.
