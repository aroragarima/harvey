Vinod v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 224; (2014) 5 SCC 678; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1915; 2014 CRLJ 2360; 2014 (3) JCC 1563; JT 2014 (8) SC 211; 2014 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1400; 2014 (2) MLJ(Crl) 227; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 440; 2014(4) SCALE 537; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 752

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 375, 376, 417, 419, 90 - Special Marriage Act, 1954 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused/appellant u/ss. 375, 376, 417, 419, 90 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved appellants appealed to HC - HC set aside conviction order u/ss. 417, 419 of IPC and reduced sentence to rigorous imprisonment for period of four years, while maintaining fine, appellant would suffer rigorous imprisonment for reduced period of six months - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was liable to set aside -

Held, in conspectus that unfolds itself in present case, prosecutrix was aware that appellant was already married but, possibly because polygamous relationship was not anathema to her because of faith which she adheres to, prosecutrix was willing to start a home with appellant - It could not be concluded beyond reasonable doubt that appellant was culpable for offence of rape, nay, reason relentlessly points to commission of consensual sexual relationship, which was brought to an abrupt end by appearance in scene of uncle of prosecutrix - Rape was indeed reprehensible act and every perpetrator should be punished expeditiously, severally and strictly - This was only possible when guilt was proved beyond reasonable doubt - There was no seduction just two persons fatally in love, their youth blinding them to futility of their relationship - Appellant was not an innocent man inasmuch as he had willy-nilly entered into relationship with prosecutrix, in violation of his matrimonial vows and his paternal duties and responsibilities - If appellant suffered incarceration for an offence for which he was not culpable, he should realize that retribution in another form had duly visited him - Conviction of appellant was set aside - Appeal allowed.
