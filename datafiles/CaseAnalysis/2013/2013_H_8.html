Board Of Trustees Of Port Of Kandla v Hargovind Jasraj & Anr

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 45; (2013) 3 SCC 182; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 173; JT 2013 (2) SC 312; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 451; 2013 (1) RCR(Rent) 362; 2013(1) SCALE 630; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r.2 - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 57 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 111,106 - Limitation Dispossession, Major Port Trusts Act, 1963, s. 120 - Lease Property - Default - Termination - Validity - Sustainability - Respondent No. 2-Lessee was granted land on a long term lease basis, she had defaulted in paying rents and appellant-lessor had issued notices calling upon lessee to pay outstanding amount - Respondent requested appellant-Port Trust to permit her to resell plots for a symbolic consideration and to obtain refund of installment amount already paid, failure of lessee to remit outstanding installment amount culminated in termination of lease by appellant - Lessee requested appellant-Port Trust to refund amount and in case a refund could not be made, to return possession of plot to her and filed suit for perpetual injunction, suit was dismissed for default - Respondent No. 1 claiming that respondent No. 2 sold plot to her filed suit for injunction against respondent No. 2 not including appellant - Pending disposal of second suit, respondent No. 1 filed another suit for declaration and permanent injunction questioning termination of lease by appellant-Port Trust - Suit was decreed and appeal before appellate court was partly allowed affirming decree passed by Court as Trial Court had declared that lease deed had not been validly terminated by lessor - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in affirming decree pass by Trial Court holding that lease was not validly terminated - Held, right to sue in present case first accrued to lessee on 13-12-1978 when in terms of order dt. 8-8-1977 lease in favour of lessee was terminated - Suit for declaration that termination of lease was invalid hence ineffective for any reason including reason that person on whose orders same was terminated had no authority to do so, could had been instituted by lessee on 14-12-1978 - For any such suit it was not necessary that lessee was dispossessed from leased property as dispossession was different from termination of lease, but even assuming that right to sue did not fully accrue till date lessee was dispossessed of plot in question, such a dispossession having taken place on 14-12-1978, lessee ought to had filed suit within 3 years of 15-12-1978 so as to be within time stipulated u/art. 58 extracted above, suit in instant case was, however, instituted in year 1996 i.e. after nearly eighteen years later and was, therefore, clearly barred by limitation - Courts below fell in error in holding that suit was within time and decreeing same in whole or in part - Appeal allowed.
