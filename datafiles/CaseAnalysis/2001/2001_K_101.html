Hinch Lal Tiwari v Kamala Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20074; (2001) 6 SCC 496; AIR 2001 SC 3215; 2001 ALJ 2035; 2001 (3) AWC 2398; JT 2001 (6) SC 88; 2001 (92) RD 689; 2001(4) SCALE 670; 2001 (3) SCJ 51; 2001 (5) SLT 564; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 23; 2001 (5) Supreme 646; 2001 (2) UC 247

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 136 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, ss. 4, 117 and 122-C - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Rules, r. 115-P - Whether alloted land forms part of pond and if so, can it be allotted u/s. 122-C(1)? - There is concurrent finding that a pond exists and area covered by it varies in rainy season - In such a case no part of it could have been allotted to anybody for construction of house building or any allied purposes - Appeal allowed.
