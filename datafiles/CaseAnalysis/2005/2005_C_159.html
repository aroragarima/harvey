Commissioner of Central Excise, Calcutta II v Andaman Timber Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1532; (2005) 12 SCC 151

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Reasoned order - Determination of - Respondent filed appeal before Tribunal, challenging order of lower Authority - Tribunal allowed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether order of Tribunal is sustainable in law.

Held, perusal of impugned judgment shows that Tribunal has merely set out arguments on behalf of the respondent and then after making reference to a judgment of SC allowed appeal with consequential relief. However, manner of disposal of appeal is unsatisfactory. Tribunal must decide appeal after hearing arguments of each party, on their own merits and give its reasons for accepting or rejecting submissions. Tribunal must also consider all contentions of parties and deal with each contention. Hence, impugned order is set aside and matter is remitted back to Tribunal for decision on merits. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Order must contain reason for arriving at such conclusion reached by Forum passing such order.
