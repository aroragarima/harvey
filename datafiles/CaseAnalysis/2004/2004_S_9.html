S. Jeevanantham v State Through Inspector of Police, T. N

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1115; (2004) 5 SCC 230; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1584; AIR 2004 SC 4608; 2004 CRLJ 3834; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 507; 2004 (3) RCR(Criminal) 333; 2004(5) SCALE 780; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 607

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c), 20(b)(ii) and 50 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 161 - Appellants searched by sub-inspector on basis of information and found to be in possession of narcotics - Whether it is illegal for officer to be complainant and to investigate case? - No principle or binding authority to hold that moment, competent police officer, on basis of information received, makes out an FIR incorporating his name as informant, he forfeits his right to investigate - Held, that officer in his official capacity gave information, registered case and as part of his official duty later investigated case and filed a charge-sheet - He was not in any way personally interested in case - Thus, no bias in process of investigation - Appeal dismissed.
