Maruti Udyog Limited v Susheel Kumar Gabgotra and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 104; (2006) 4 SCC 644; AIR 2006 SC 1586; 2006 (63) ALR 477; 2006 (5) AWC 5265; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 266; 2006 (2) CPJ(Sc) 3; JT 2006 (4) SC 113; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 437; 2006 (3) RLW 2519; 2006(3) SCALE 611; 2006 (3) SCJ 664; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 603; 2006 (2) UC 1209

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: J&K Consumers Protection Act, 1988

Summary: [A.] Consumer Protection - J&K Consumers Protection Act, 1988, s. 17 - Complainant purchased a Maruti Car from appellant through its authorized dealer, respondent No 2 - Clutch of car was not functioning properly as it developed unusual noise/jerks on running of engine - Appellant was directed to replace car or repay amount received by it as sale price with interest @ 18% p.a. w.e.f. 27-11-1996 with costs awarded by Commission - Appeal against - Whether Commission and HC correct in holding that there was an admission to replace car and/or admission of any manufacturing defect - Held, in various documents, more particularly letter dated 19-02-1997 written by respondent no 1 to appellant, it is clearly stated that appellant had indicated that downing of engine was necessary to trace problem - There was no agreement to replace engine system - It is not disputed by respondent no 1 that when appellant had asked vehicle to be brought for downing respondent no 1 had not done so - There was any manufacturing defect in said background is without any foundation - Defective part that is clutches assembly should be replaced - Respondent no 1 shall be entitled to receive a consolidated sum of Rs. 50, 000/- for inconvenience caused - Appeal partly allowed.
