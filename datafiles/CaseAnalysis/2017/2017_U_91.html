Ashish Ranjan and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

09 June 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 435; 2017(7) SCALE 122; 2017(7) SCALE 129

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College, Medical Council Of India, State Reservation Rules

Summary: Education - Admissions to PG Medical course - Extension of date - Instant applications are filed by Private Medical Colleges praying for direction to State via BCECE Board to hold fresh counseling for the seats available in applicants' colleges which are lying vacant and could not be filled up due to haphazard and arbitrary counseling conducted by Board - Further, applications by Private Universities seeking intervention in petition.

Held, in view of peculiar circumstances in which the State had to cancel first and second part of counseling and fix fresh counseling on 29/30-5-2017 and considering substantial vacant seats both in Govt. Medical Colleges and Private Medical colleges, State is right in asking for giving further time to take steps to fill up the vacant seats in PG Degree Courses and Diploma courses. Hence, time is extended for completing admission process by 15-6-2017 by holding an extended counseling by Board. Further, only those students are to be invited to participate in extended counseling whose roll numbers were reflected both in Govt. Medical Colleges and Private Medical Colleges in last notice for counseling and the eligibility is confined to those students only who were invited in last counseling and could not take admission. Further, circular dt.26-5-2017 issued by Govt. of India on the strength of order of SC dt.9-5-2017 was under misconception that said order is applicable to admission to PG courses. However, order dt.9-5-2017 does not relate to admission into PG Courses and is confined to admission in UG Courses. Hence, as said order is itself not applicable to PG Courses, circular dt.26-5-2017 is not effective and so, as the very basis of applications filed by applicants is unfounded, applicants are not entitled to any relief in the applications and so, are rejected. Order accordingly.
