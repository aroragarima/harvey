Ambya Kalya Mhatre (d) Through Legal Heirs and others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Gokhale, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 636; (2011) 9 SCC 325; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 37; 2012 (1) AWC 123; 2011 (6) Bom.C.R. 645; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 26; JT 2011 (11) SC 23; 2011 (4) KLT 1; 2012 (1) MahLJ 9; 2012 (1) MPLJ 19; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 767; 2011(10) SCALE 296; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 12, 18, 19, 20, 23, 25, 3(a), 4(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 17 - Limitation Act, 1973 - Limitation - Particulars - Amount awarded - Amount claimed - Apportionment - Land Acquisition officer - Lands situated at village, were acquired for New project in pursuance of preliminary notification - Compensation was awarded to owner of lands by Land Acquisition Officer ( 'LAO') - Not being satisfied with compensation awarded, owner made an application u/ s. 18 of the 1894 Act to LAO seeking a reference for enhancement of compensation - In pursuance of said request, a reference was made to Civil Court by LAO - During pendency of reference before Reference Court, owner died and his legal representatives came on record - Appellants made an application before Reference Court seeking amendments to application for reference - Said application was allowed by Reference Court and awarded compensation - Aggrieved by award, appeal was filed challenging increase in compensation -- Hence, instant appeal - (A) Whether in a reference made to Reference Court u/s. 18 of the 1894 Act, land owner was barred from amending amount claimed in reference application and seeking higher compensation; and even if he could seek amendment, whether such application should be made within period of limitation mentioned in s. 18 of the Act - Held, reference was filed within prescribed period of limitation - However, four years later reference was amended for enhancing claim of compensation for trees - If date of amendment of reference was taken into consideration, claim for further enhancement made by way of amendment was barred by limitation - (B) Where landowner sought increase in compensation for only land, in application u/s. 18 of the 1894 Act, whether he could seek increase in compensation for trees or structures also, before Reference Court - Held, time limit u/s. 18 of the 1894 Act was only for seeking reference by raising objection to amount of compensation or any of other three objections - Land owner or persons aggrieved would have to give only nature of objection to award, that was whether it was with reference to measurement or compensation or person to whom it was payable or apportionment, and briefly mention grounds in support of it - Though land owner could give details of his claim and quantum, he was not bound to do so - When reference was made, he could give particulars of claim for compensation or additional particulars or even increase claim - (C) Where compensation was awarded for land, whether no compensation could be awarded for trees or well separately - Held, if land value had been determined with reference to sale statistics or compensation awarded for a nearby vacant land, then necessarily, trees would have to be valued separately - But if value of land had been determined on basis of sale statistics or compensation awarded for an orchard, that was land with fruit-bearing trees, then there was no question of again adding value of trees - In instant case, value of trees could be added to value of land - Impugned orders/judgments were set aside - Appeal allowed.
