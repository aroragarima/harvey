Bhavesh D. Parish v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 625; (2000) 5 SCC 471; AIR 2000 SC 2047; AIR 2000 SCW 1965; [2000] 101 Comp Cas 459; 2000 (161) CTR 162; JT 2000 (6) SC 604; 2000(4) SCALE 616; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 291; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2140

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Writ Petition dismissed

Summary: RBI - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934, s. 45S - Constitution of India, arts. 32 and 226 - Economic Policy matters - Decision of expert bodies - Accepting deposits from public - (A) Imposing ban on unincorporated bodies, if unreasonable and validity of s. 45 of the Act - (B) Powers of court to interfere - (C)Tendency to grant stay of legislation - Held, reasonable, s. 45S valid and further, right to raise public deposit cannot be construed as fundamental right - Court while not jettisoning its jurisdiction to curb arbitrary action or unconstitutional legislation, should interfere only in those few cases where the view reflected in the legislation is not possible to be taken at all - Supreme Court sounded a word of caution, moreso in case of legislation relating to economic reform - Writ petition dismissed.
