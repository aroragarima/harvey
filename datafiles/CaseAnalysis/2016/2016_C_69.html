Commissioner of Central Excise, Ahmedabad v Gujarat Ambuja Exports Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 568; AIR 2016 SC 3692; 2016 (338) E.L.T. 481; JT 2016 (8) SC 5; 2016(7) SCALE 603; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Show Cause Notice, EXCISE, Customs Act, 1962, Rate Of Duty, Exemption Notification, Customs (Import of Goods At Concessional Rate of Duty For Manufacture of Excisable Goods) Rules, 1996

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 112, 114A, 28, 28AB - Customs (Import of Goods at Concessional Rate of Duty for Manufacture of Excisable Goods) Rules, 1996 - Crude palm oil import - Notification - Exemption - Assessee/respondent is the manufacturer of various edible oils - Assessee imported crude palm oil (industrial grade) and cleared the same on payment of customs duty under Notification No. 21/2002-Cus dt. 01-03-2002 r/w Notification No. 66/2004-Cus dt. 09-07-2004 - Appellant/Revenue issued show cause notice to assessee demanding custom duty of Rs. 7,89,89,868/- under para 8 of the Rules, and u/s. 28AB of the Act and also imposed penalty u/ss. 112, 114A of the Act - Adjudicating Authority confirmed demand raised by holding that assessee did not fulfill conditions contained in exemption notification - On appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal holding that as per HSN Explanatory Notes, fatty acids distillate are also covered by said chapter and benefit of notification was available to assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether assessee is entitled to avail the benefit of Notification No. 21/2002-Cus dt 01-03-2002 r/w Notification No. 66/2004-Cus dt. 09-07-2004 for import of crude palm oil which is not used in manufacture of Industrial Fatty Acid.

Held, Notification No. 21/2002 dt. 01-03-2002, as amended by Notification No. 66/2004 dt. 09-07-2004 is a general exemption to notification which enlist number of products that are given full or partial exemption from payment of custom or additional duty. In the instant case, crude palm oil which was imported was used for making edible products like refined oil/Vanaspati. In the process of said manufacture, 25% of fatty (palm) was produced and 75% was oil which was edible. Thus, when the main manufacturing activity relates to edible product which is 75%. If in the process 25% of fatty (palm) emerges as a by-product it cannot be said that first requirement of exemption notification is satisfied in instant case. Even if Industrial Fatty Acid is to be treated as separate manufacturing activity and it is non-edible, the same is only to the extent of 25%. That, according to SC, would not satisfy the requirement of exemption notification in question. SC is in agreement with the reasoning adopted by Commissioner that HSN Explanatory Notes, in case where exemption notification was to be construed, would only serve as guide and is not used to interpret the same. At the time of import, importer only gives declaration. It is the actual use, which event takes place much after import, from where it can be gathered as to where the import is made for the purpose for which it was done. As soon as the aforesaid information was gathered by DRI, show cause notice was issued. Therefore, show cause notice had been issued within a reasonable period and it cannot be treated as time barred. Impugned order of Tribunal was set aside and that of Adjudicating Authority was restored. Appeal allowed.
