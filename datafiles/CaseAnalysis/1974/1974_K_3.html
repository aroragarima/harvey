State of Andhra Pradesh and Another v K. Jayaraman and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 24; (1974) SCC (L&S) 547; (1974) 2 SCC 738; AIR 1975 SC 633; 1975 (30) FLR 1; 1975 LabIC 385; 1975 (1) LLJ 256; 1975 SLJ 464; 1975 (1) SLR 668; 1974 UJ 683

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - A.P. Secretariat Service Rules, r. 22 - Service Rules - Writ Petition - For quashing Govt. Order fixing gradation of employees - (A) Applicability of r. 22 (known as A.T.A. Rules ) involved but not its validity - (B) Maintainability of petition - Validity of Rule is not a pure question of law - Cannot be decided except by amendment of petition - petitioners having failed to show how they would be adversely affected by the application of Rule 22 were not aggrieved persons and therefore were not entitled to maintain the petition Appeal Allowed.
