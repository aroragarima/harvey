Jawaharlal Wadhwa And Another v Haripada Chakroberty

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 250; (1989) 1 SCC 76; AIR 1989 SC 606; 1989 (1) ARBLR 403; JT 1988 (4) SC 138; 1988(2) SCALE 1033; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 513; 1989 (1) UJ 111

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Anticipatory Breach

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Anticipatory breach of contract - Other party must keep contract alive by performing its part of contract and show readiness and willingness in order to claim specific performance - Otherwise can claim damages - Vendee in possession of premises - Could not claim protection under s. 53A - Contract repudiated once by a party - Not necessary for other party to tender amount payable under contract in manner provided in order to successfully claim specific performance of contract - Held, transferee stopped payment of monthly sum he could be said to have performed his part of contract - Not entitled to retain possession under protection afforded by s. 53 - Award of arbitrator could not be set-aside on ground of error of law apparent on face of award - Arbitrator not guilty of misconduct.
