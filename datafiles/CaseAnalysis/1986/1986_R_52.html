Ramveer Jatav v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 457; (1986) 4 SCC 762; (1987) SCC (Cr) 10; AIR 1987 SC 63; 1986 (3) Crimes 654; 1987 CRLJ 321; 1987 CRLR 109; JT 1986 SC 849; 1986(2) SCALE 837

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution, art.22(5) - National Security Act, 1980, s.3(3) - Detention - Writ of habeas corpus challenging the validity of the order of detention - Ground, detenu along with others jointly committed murder in broad day light - No other circumstances to draw inference that detenu would be likely to commit such act if left free - Order of detention passed against him - Held, ground of detention given in support of the order of detention was irrelevant and no reasonable inference could be drawn from that ground - Detention not sustainable - Petition allowed.
