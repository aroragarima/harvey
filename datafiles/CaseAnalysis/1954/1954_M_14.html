Marachalil Pakku and Another v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

25 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1954 SC 648; 1954 CRLJ 1668

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149 and 302 - Murder - Prosecution case is that on account of his active interest on behalf of Hindu community, Moplas wanted to put an end to his life - Appellants along with accused 3 to 7 proceeded to house of vakil and rushed into room where Kannan (X) was taking refuge - Serious stab injuries inflicted on X which brought about his death - After investigations, appellants along with accused 3 to 7, had been acquitted by High Court, committed to Court of Session - HC confirmed death sentences passed on appellants but benefit of doubt given to accused 3 to 7 - Conviction of accused set aside and were acquitted - Eye witnesses - Testimonies corroborated in all details - Whether there was any scope for applying theory of benefit of doubt by HC? - Held, Court opined that no scope was left for introducing the theory of benefit of doubt into case - Therefore, accused 3 to 7 were wrongfully acquitted - Though their acquittal stands, that circumstance cannot affect conviction of appellants u/s. 302 r/w 140, IPC - Appeal dismissed.
