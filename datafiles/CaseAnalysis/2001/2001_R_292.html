Regional Manager, R. S. R. T. C. v Ghanshyam Sharma

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21491; 2002 (1) SLR 341

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947,s.11 - Misconduct - Removal of service - Reinstatement - Respondent was punished on misconduct of not issuing tickets to passengers - Labour Court directed respondents reinstatement with continuity of service but without back wages - On petition by appellant, Writ Court set aside award of Labour Court - On appeal by respondent, order of Writ Court was reversed by Division Bench - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Court in B.S. Hullikatti case has held that in such cases where bus conductors carry passengers without ticket or issue tickets at a less rate than proper rate, said acts would inter alia amount to either being a case of dishonesty or of gross negligence and such conductors were not fit to be retained in service because such inaction or action on part of conductors results in financial loss to Road Transport Corporation. In cases like, orders of dismissal should not be set aside. Furthermore, Court agrees with observations of Writ Court in appellant case that Labour Court was not justified in interfering with punishment of dismissal. Though u/s.11 (A) of Act Labour Court has jurisdiction and powers to interfere with quantum of punishment, however discretion has to be used judiciously. When main duty or function of Conductor is to issue tickets and collect fare and then deposit the same with Road Transport Corporation and when a conductor fails to do so, then it will be misplaced sympathy to order his reinstatement instead of dismissal. For reasons, appeal is allowed, order of Division Bench is set aside and that of Writ Court restored. Appeal allowed.
