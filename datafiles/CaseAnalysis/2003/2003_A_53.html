Union of India v Ahmedabad Electricity Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 916; (2003) 11 SCC 129; AIR 2004 SC 11; 2003 (90) ECC 249; 2003 (111) ECR 274; 2003 (158) E.L.T. 3; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1257; JT 2003 (8) SC 153; 2003 (59) RLT 119; 2003(9) SCALE 123; 2003 (1) SCW 5529; 2003 (7) SLT 236; 2004 (134) STC 24; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1117; 2003 (8) Supreme 323

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excise Act, 1944 - ss. 2(d), 3 - Duty - Exigibility - Cinder - Whether inclusion of an item in the entries to the First Schedule to the Tariff Act per as makes the item exigible to excise duty; (2) does the item in question satisfy the test of being 'manufactured in India' and 'marketability' - Held, for being exigible to excise duty, excisable goods must satisfy the test of being produced or manufactured in India; (2) excise duty is an incidence of manufacture and, therefore, it is essential that the product sought to excise duty should have gone through the process of manufacture. Cinder cannot be said to have gone through any process of manufacture, therefore, it cannot be subjected to levy of excise duty - Further held, the result is that the contention of the Revenue that cinder is liable to payment of excise duty is hereby rejected.
