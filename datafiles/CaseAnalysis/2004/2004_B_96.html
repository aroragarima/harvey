State of Rajasthan v Bhanwar Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 771; (2004) 13 SCC 147; (2005) SCC (Cr) 73; AIR 2004 SC 4660; 2004 CRLJ 4886; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 154; 2004(7) SCALE 676; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s 302 - Culpable homicide amounting to murder - Convicted and sentence thereunder, by Trial Court - HC acquitted - Whether acquittal justified? - Appeal against acquittal - Held, combined effect of infirmities noticed by HC are sufficient to show that prosecution case has not been established - Unexplained delay of more than one day in lodging FIR casts serious doubt on truthfulness of prosecution version - Where medical evidence totally improbablises ocular version that can be taken to be a factor to effect credibility of prosecution version - Acquittal confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
