Dosa Satyanarayanamurty Etc v Andhra Pradesh State Roadtransport Corporation

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1961 SC 82; 1963 (1) MLJ(SC) 111; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 642

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Summary: Constitution - Carriers & Transportation - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) and 32 -Motor Vehicles Act 1939, s. 68 C - Writ of Certiorari - Word 'Service' - Enforcement of petitioner's fundamental right to carry on business of motor transport - (A) To what extent art. 19(6) secures validity of Chatper IVA of 1939 Act from attack that it offends against art. 19(1)(g)? - Law, contained u/art. 19(6), can provide for carrying on a service to exclusion of all citizens; it may, exclude some of citizens only; it may do business in entire State or a portion of State, in a specified route or a part thereof - Word 'service' is wide enough to take in not only general motor service but all species of motor service - Held, no limitations on state's power to make laws conferring monopoly on it in respect of an area, and person or persons to be excluded - In this view, it must be held that s. 68 C of Act, 1939 does not exceed limits prescribed by art. 19(6)(ii) - (B) Whether provisions of chapter IVA of 1939 Act, and s. 68 C, offend against art. 14? - Accepting construction suggested, court held that discretion to be exercised by State would be a regulated discretion guided by statutory rules - But in instant case, no liberal construction of provisions need be resorted to, for chapter IVA of Act in specific terms provides a complete and, satisfactory machinery for reasonably regulating exclusion of all or some of private operators from notified area or route - Further held, therefore, provisions of chapter IVA of Act do not infringe equality cls. enshrined in art. 14 - (C) Validity of scheme on ground that Govt. is actuated by bias against private operators - Held, it has not been established that Minister in charge of portfolio of transport had personal bias against operators of private buses and, therefore, it cannot be held that Govt. is actuated by bias against private operators - (D) Scope of s. 68 C? - Further held, s. 68 C empowers state transport undertaking to propose a scheme to include new routes - Petitions dismissed.
