P. N. Kaushal and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 295; (1978) 3 SCC 558; AIR 1978 SC 1457; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 122

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Eastern Bengal And Assam Excise Act, 1910, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution - Constitution, arts. 14 and 19 - Punjab Liquor Licence Rules, 1956, r. 37(9) Note - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, s. 59(f)(v) - Eastern Bengal and Assam Excise Act, 1910 - Excise - Licence Rules - (A) Prohibition on liquor sales - Excluding institutions run by Govt. u/r. 37, Note - Undertaking by Govt. - (B) Constitutional validity of s. 59 (f) (v) of Excise Act and r. 37of 1956 Rules , as unguided and arbitrary respectively - Held, point not pressed in view of this undertaking - Unchannelled and arbitrary discretion, violative of arts. 14 and 19, tested on the touchstone of principled pragmatism and living realism - Petitions dismissed .
