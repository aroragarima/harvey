Govt. (NCT of Delhi) and others v Jagjit Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 2015 SC 2683; 2015 (109) ALR 893; 2015 (2) AWC 2043; 2015 (2) CGLJ 181; 2015 (2) KarLJ 547; 2015 (2) KLT 180; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 674; 2015 (127) RD 785; 2015(3) SCALE 208; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 692

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Settlement Ordinance, 2014

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s.11 - Right to Fair Compensation and Transparency in Land Acquisition, Rehabilitation and Resettlement Act, 2013, s. 24(2) - Lapse of acquisition - Instant appeals assailing judgment and order of HC allowing writ petitions and declaring acquisitions as lapsed since possession was not taken and compensation was not paid.

Held, protection as envisaged u/s. 24(2) of 2013 Act could be granted to respondents since possession of land was not taken by appellants and compensation was not paid to respondents. Hence, appeals dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - In case Award was passed u/s. 11 of 1894 Act on or before 1-1-2009 and either possession of land was not taken or compensation was not paid, then acquisition proceedings are liable to be quashed u/s. 24(2) of 2013 Act.
