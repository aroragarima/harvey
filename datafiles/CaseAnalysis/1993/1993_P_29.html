Moti Ram v Param Dev And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1147; (1993) 2 SCC 725; AIR 1993 SC 1662; JT 1993 (2) SC 251; 1993 (2) LLJ 629; 1993 (2) LLN 589; 1993(1) SCALE 803; [1993] 2 S.C.R. 250; 1993 (2) SLR 295; 1993 (2) UJ 160

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Himachal Pardesh Khadi And Village Industries Board Act, 1966

Summary: Election - Constitution, art.133 - Himachal Pardesh Khadi And Village Industries Board Act, 1966, s. 4 and 7 - HP Khadi and Village Industries Board Rules, 1966, rr. 3, 7 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 86 - Election Petition challenging dissolution of House during pendency - Issues arise : (A) Whether petition become academic - (B) When resignation by chairman of Statutory Board becomes effective or Whether acceptance is necessary - (C) Candidate not holding office of profit on date of scrutiny - Rejection of his nomination was found to be not proper - Setting aside of election - Held, no as invalidation of election of candidate may give rise to liability to refund allowances - Being an unilateral Act it does nor require be accepted and notified - It becomes effective from date of communication - Proper - Appeal dismissed.
