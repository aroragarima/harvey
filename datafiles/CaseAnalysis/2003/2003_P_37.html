Baitarani Gramiya Bank v Pallab Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 739; (2004) 9 SCC 100; (2004) SCC (L&S) 715; AIR 2003 SC 4248; 2003 (99) FLR 514*; JT 2003 (8) SC 121; 2004 (1) LLJ 184; 2003 (4) LLN 173; 2003(7) SCALE 394; 2003 (6) SLR 664; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 571; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 229

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Service; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Eligibility - Merit

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 141, 16 - Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976 - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and other Employees) Rules, 1988 - Bank Officer/Field Supervisors - Bank had bona fide and genuine reasons for pruning down indent submitted to BSRB and same was communicated well ahead of publication of results - BSRB did not accommodate appellant-Bank's request and sent nomination letters to respondents based on original indent and also published results and intimated selection - Held, Bank had bona fide and genuine reasons for pruning down indent If Banks are forced to accommodate Officers and Field Supervisors more than their required indent, it will have a crippling effect on Bank and public interest will stand seriously prejudiced as several employees will have to be unnecessarily retained and public money will have to be expended on them - When public interest competes with private interest, private interest will have to give way to public interest - Further held, respondents/WPers had not acquired any indefeasible right and decision not to fill up all vacancies had been taken bona fide reasons and directions as sought for by respondent cannot, therefore, be issued - Directions issued.
