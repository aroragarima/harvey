Ganga Ram Sah and others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

27 January 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 54; AIR 2017 SC 655; JT 2017 (1) SC 563; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 73; 2017(1) SCALE 718

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Object, FIR, Assembly, Version, Informant, Arms Act, 1959, Unlawful Assembly, Lathi, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 325, 332 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25A, 26 - Unlawful assembly - Common object - Conviction - Trial Court convicted accused appellants for offences u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 325, 332 of IPC and u/ss. 25A and 26 of Act - HC affirmed conviction - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, order of conviction called for any interference. Held, FIR was registered on basis of fardbayan given by informant immediately after the incident. There is no time lag between incident and FIR. In said FIR, both appellant Nos. 1 and 3 are specifically named. Insofar as appellant No. 1 is concerned, specific allegation is made in FIR that it was exhortation of appellant No. 1 which led to assault. Role attributed to appellant No. 1 stands proved, as rightly held by Trial court as well as HC. All accused are convicted u/s. 149 of IPC as well. Appellant Nos. 1, 2, 3 and 4 are closely related. Reason for causing murder of one person and injuring other persons, all of whom were related and belonged to rival group, is obvious as stated in FIR itself. Appellants had come with clear motive in mind to bodily harm members of informant's family and with common objective. Calculated action was spearheaded. Courts below rightly held that ingredients for offence u/s. 149 of IPC stood proved. It is trite law that common object of unlawful assembly has to be inferred from membership, weapons used and nature of injuries as well as other surrounding circumstances. In instant case, repeated gun shots fired by one of appellant No. 3 on person of deceased and injuries caused by lathis by other accused persons on complainant and his second brother on their heads, clearly demonstrate objective to cause murder of these persons. Appeal dismissed.
