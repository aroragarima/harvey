Management of Messrs M. S. Nally Bharat Engineering Company Limited v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 852; (1990) 2 SCC 48; (1990) SCC (L&S) 189; 1990 (1) CLR 736; 1990 (60) FLR 785; JT 1990 (2) SC 96; 1990 (2) LLJ 211; 1990 (1) LLN 755; 1990(1) SCALE 156; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 290; 1991 (2) SLR 36; 1990 (1) UJ 500; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 521

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(c) and 33-B - Whether, Govt. before accepting representation of workman and transferring case from Labour Court, Dhanbad to Labour Court, Patna should have given an opportunity to management? - Expression 'may' in sub-s. (1) of s. 33-B only makes it discretionary in so far as appropriate Govt. taking a decision as to whether powers conferred thereunder has to be exercised or not, but when once a decision is taken to transfer a pending case then requirement of giving reasons becomes mandatory - Authority is under legal obligation to record reasons in support of its decision and failure to give reasons or giving reasons not germane would be fatal to decision - Fairness is a rule to ensure that vast power in modern State is not abused but properly exercised and authority is not misguided by extraneous or irrelevant consideration - Held, State in fairness ought to have got workman's representation verified by giving an opportunity to management which is a party to pending reference and denial of that opportunity is a fatal flaw to decision of Govt.; management need not establish particular prejudice for want of such opportunity - Appeal allowed.
