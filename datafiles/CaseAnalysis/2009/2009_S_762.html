Vakil Prasad Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2155; (2009) 3 SCC 355; AIR 2009 SC 1822; 2009 CRLJ 1731; JT 2009 (2) SC 113; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2009(1) SCALE 22; 2009(2) SCALE 22; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 517; 2009 (1) WLN(SC) 83

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 120B and 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Right to speedy trial - HC dismissed the petition filed by the appellant u/s. 482 of the CrPC seeking quashing of proceedings pending against him in the case for allegedly committing offences u/ss. 161, 109 and 120B of the IPC and s. 5(2) of the Act - Hence, present appeal - Appellant submitted that though a period of about twenty eight years, since the registration of the case against the appellant, elapsed, the trial according to law was yet to commence and thus, the appellant was deprived of his constitutional right to speedy investigation and trial - Held, right to speedy trial extends equally to all criminal prosecutions and is not confined to any particular category of cases - In every case, where the right to speedy trial is alleged to have been infringed, the court has to perform the balancing act upon taking into consideration all the attendant circumstances and determine in each case whether the right to speedy trial has been denied in a given case - Where the court comes to the conclusion that the right to speedy trial of an accused has been infringed, the charges or the conviction, as the case may be, may be quashed unless the court feels that having regard to the nature of offence and other relevant circumstances, quashing of proceedings may not be in the interest of justice - In such a situation, it is open to the court to make an appropriate order as it may deem just and equitable including fixation of time frame for conclusion of trial - In the present case, it was manifest from the facts that in the first instance investigations were conducted by an officer, who had no jurisdiction to do so and also the appellant cannot be accused of delaying the trial merely because he exercised his right to challenge an illegal investigation - Further, prosecution failed to show any exceptional circumstance which could possibly be taken into consideration for condoning a callous and inordinate delay of more than two decades in investigations and the trial - Thus, the stated delay clearly violates the constitutional guarantee of a speedy investigation and trial u/art. 21 of the Constitution - In these circumstances, further continuance of criminal proceedings, pending against the appellant unwarranted and despite the fact that allegations against him were serious, they deserve to be quashed - Appeal allowed.
