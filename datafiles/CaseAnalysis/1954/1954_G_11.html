Lakshminarayan Ram Gopal and Son Limited v Government of Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 80; AIR 1954 SC 364; [1954] 25 ITR 449; 1955 SCJ 595; [1955] S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Regulation, s.2(4) - Hyderabad Excess Profits Tax Regulation, s. 13, 48(2),48(3) - Agreement - Remuneration - Excess Profits Tax - Appellants were registered as a private limited company an agency agreement was entered into between Mills Company and appellants appointing appellants its agents for a period of 30 years on certain terms and conditions - Appellants throughout worked only as agents of Mills Company and for Fasli years received their remuneration under terms of agency agreement - Notice was issued u/s.13 of Hyderabad Excess Profits Tax Regulation by Excess Profits Tax Officer calling upon appellants to pay amount of tax appertaining to these chargeable accounting periods - Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax adjudging liability of appellants for excess profits tax in regard to amounts received by them as remuneration from Mills Company - Application made by appellants u/s.48(2) for statement of HC was rejected by Commissioner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether remuneration received from company was justify and Whether principle of personal qualification of Excess Profits Regulation was applicable to petitioner company - Held, appellants were rightly assessed for excess profits tax - Appellants were entitled though with sanction or ratification by board of directors with company by way of sales and purchases of various commodities Remuneration by way of commission of 21 percent of amount of sale proceeds of produce of company savored more of remuneration given by a principal to his agent in carrying out of general management of business of principals of wages or salary which would not normally be on such a basis - Appellants were agents of company and not merely servants of company remunerated by wages or salary - Nature of remuneration and assign ability of their rights, were sufficient - Appeals disposed of.
