Dr. Ram Deen Maurya v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2302; 2009 (4) SLR 544

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Transfer, Procedure, Rule, EDUCATION, Uttar Pradesh Higher Education Services Commission Act, 1980, State Universities Act, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Aided College Transfer of Teachers Rules, 2005, Writ Petition, Employee, Post

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Aided College Transfer of Teachers Rules, 2005 - Transfer application - Validity of recommendation - Quashment of transfer - Petitioner was working as Lecturer in Post Graduate College in Department of Economics - Post of Reader fell vacant in Department of Economics due to retirement of âAâ in said college in âLâ branch - Petitioner wanted to be transferred to said vacant post as well as applied for No Objection Certificate/NOC before management was allowed - At meantime respondent no.5 was also applied for transfer to said vacant post as well as applied for NOC was also allowed - Thereafter respondent no.5 was recommended by Secretary Higher Education for transfer - Petitioner so that filled application before State Govt. passed order in favour of petitioner - Respondent no.5 filed writ petition against order of State Govt. was allowed - Hence instant appeals.

Held, on request made by applicant NOC is granted and thereafter, routing through application through management is requirement under 2005 Rules, and its non-compliance thereof would not make her application invalid. In facts and circumstances of case, since both managements were fully aware that applicant intends to shift herself to college which may be helpful to her either to achieve better prospects in her profession or to suit her convenience. In Court view, since it does not involve any public interest nor it would affect interest of both managements in any manner whatsoever, and since there is substantial compliance with requirements of 2005 Rules. While considering non- compliance of procedural requirement, it has to be kept in view, that, such requirement is designed to facilitate justice and furthers its ends and, therefore, if consequence of non-compliance is not provided, requirement may be held to be directory. Respondent no.5 has obtained NOC from her parent college and also from college where she intends to be transferred. NOC issued by both colleges was earlier in point of time than that of NOC granted to petitioner. However, case of management authorities is that NOC granted to respondent no.5 was not by management of institution, but by President and Assistant Secretary, who were not authorized to issue such No Objection Certificate. HC while considering this issue, has looked into records maintained by college authorities and on facts has come to conclusion that NOC issued to respondent no.5 is in order and, therefore, was not justified in contending contrary to their own records. In Court view, finding on facts by HC, by no stretch of imagination can be criticized as perverse findings. Court is in full agreement with findings and conclusion reached by HC on facts. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - State Govt. need not accept recommendations of Director of Higher Education and it can take its own decision with material available and also to take such decision, collect material from respective managements.
