Smt. Isabella Johnson v M. A. Susai

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 430; (1991) 1 SCC 494; AIR 1991 SC 993; 1990 (3) CCC 676; JT 1990 (4) SC 406; 1990 RD 484; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 213; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 213

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Res judicata - Eviction suit - Civil Court having no jurisdiction as it lay exclusively within jurisdiction of rent controller - Can jurisdiction be conferred on Civil Court by raising plea of res judicata - On ground that earlier decisions of rent controller to effect that it was City Civil Court who had jurisdiction to entertain suit for eviction filed by appellant against respondent, constituted res judicata between parties on question of jurisdiction - And respondent could not be permitted to take inconsistent pleas as he was barred by principles of estoppel from taking up plea before Civil Court that it was rent controller who had exclusive jurisdiction to entertain suit when said decision of rent controller was given on specific plea raised to that effect by respondent - Held, jurisdiction cannot be conferred by raising plea of res judicata - There can be no estoppel on a pure question of law and question of jurisdiction is a pure question of law - Plea of estoppel also not available - Appeal dismissed.
