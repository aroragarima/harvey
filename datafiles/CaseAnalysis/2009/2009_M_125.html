Union Of India v M/S. Singh Builders Syndicate

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 337; (2009) 4 SCC 523; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1795; 2009 (3) AWC 2352; 2009 (4) CompLJ 447; 2009 (5) MahLJ 138; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 144; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 636; 2009(4) SCALE 491; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Whether the appointment of a the retired Judge of the High Court as sole Arbitrator should be set aside and an Arbitral Tribunal should again be constituted in the manner provided in terms of clause 64? - Held, invariably the court should first appoint the Arbitrators in the manner provided for in the arbitration agreement - Where the independence and impartiality of the Arbitrator/s appointed/nominated in terms of the arbitration agreement is in doubt, or where the Arbitral Tribunal appointed in the manner provided in the arbitration agreement has not functioned and it becomes necessary to make fresh appointment, the Chief Justice or his designate is not powerless to make appropriate alternative arrangements to give effect to the provision for arbitration - Appeal dismissed.
