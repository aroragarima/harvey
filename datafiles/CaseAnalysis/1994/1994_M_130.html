Messrs Spencer and Company Limited and Another v Messrs Vishwadarshan Distributors Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 649; (1995) 1 SCC 259; JT 1995 (1) SC 113; 1994(4) SCALE 93; 1995 (1) SLR 282; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 294

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Contempt - Judicial language - In the original suit, the petitioner was granted certain interim orders by the inter court appellate jurisdiction - Appeals were filed in the HC of which some were disposed while others were still pending - Application were filed for early hearing of the appeals - In the special leave petitions filed, the SC passed order on 14-1-1994, directing the parties to approach HC for early disposal of all the appeals pending in the petition - The matter was adjourned time to time - On 18-8-1994, HC passed order that the applications for early fixing of appeals were not so important to get precedence over the large number of other pending appeals in HC and the applications were rejected - Order of the SC was flouted by the HC - Hence, instant contempt petition - Whether, the HC was right and justified in flouting the order of the SC - Held, that the independence and majesty of the SC as the highest Court of Justice in Sovereign Republic of India had to be upheld and preserved for it was a pillar of the body politic, established under the Constitution, conferred with plenary powers u/arts. 141,142, 144 of the Constitution - Judicial language was always chaste - Order of the SC was neither commanding in nature nor explicitly in terms of a direction - There was a deliberate and conscious obstruction put and recorded by the HC in spite of the order of SC which supported the contention of early hearing of the appeal - Order dt. 14-1-1994 of SC was a judicial order and otherwise enforceable throughout the territory of India u/art. 142 of the Constitution - HC was bound to come in aid of SC and language of the request employed by the SC was to be read by the HC as an obligation in carrying out the Constitutional mandate - Order dt. 18-8-1994 of the HC had to be upturned and there had to be expeditious disposal of the pending appeals within one month from the date of communication of the order - Order accordingly.
