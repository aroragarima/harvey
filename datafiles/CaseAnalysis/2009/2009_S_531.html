Sayanna v State Of Maharashtra And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1354; (2009) 10 SCC 268; 2010(1) ALL MR 957; 2010 (78) ALR 211; 2010 (3) AWC 2545; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 684; 2010 (3) ESC 320; JT 2009 (12) SC 405; 2009(12) SCALE 530; 2009 (7) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Caste Certificate - Validity - Appellant wanted to contest election for post of President of Municipal Council which was reserved for candidate belonging to Scheduled Tribes - appellant contested election and got elected as President - Scrutiny Committee asked Collector to forward caste claims of elected candidates of Municipal Council for verification - Scrutiny Committee by order cancelled and confiscated certificate issued to appellant indicating that he belongs to "Mannerwarlu" Scheduled Tribe - Aggrieved, appellant invoked extraordinary jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 of Constitution by filing writ petition - DB had dismissed petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether order passed by DB was justified - Held, appellant had produced before Scrutiny Committee four Xerox copies of Caste Certificate issued by Talathi of Village and (C) Certificate issued by C.E.O.- Perusal of record showed that these documents were arbitrarily and lightly brushed aside by Scrutiny Committee by observing that documents were issued in casual manner and that too on personal knowledge without verifying facts - It was admitted position that none of officers, who had issued certificate concerned were either summoned or examined by Scrutiny Committee - Claim of appellant that he belonged to Mannerwarlu Scheduled Tribe could not have been negative on ground that he had no basic knowledge of traits, characteristics, customs and culture, etc. of Mannerwarlu Scheduled Tribe or that he failed to prove his affinity and ethnic linkage to Mannerwarlu Scheduled Tribe - Court held that decision of Scrutiny Committee to cancel and confiscate the caste certificate issued to appellant was based on irrelevant considerations and suffered from vice of non-consideration of relevant factors - Hence, decision as well as decision of HC confirming said decision were set aside - Appeal allowed.
