Chief Commissioner, Central Excise and Customs, Lucknow and others v Prabhat Singh

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 582; (2012) 13 SCC 412; 2013 (287) E.L.T. 393; 2013 (136) FLR 858; JT 2013 (1) SC 350; 2013 (170) PLR 441; 2013(1) SCALE 506; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 209; 2013 (3) SLR 709; 2013 (1) UPLBEC 357

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Appointment - Compassionate Ground - Rejected - Time Barred - Sustainability - Respondent applied for appointment on compassionate ground after death of his father was working as Sepoy with appellant, but was not appointed as there was no vacancy available to accommodate him and his application therefore remained pending - Respondent filed OA praying for direction to appoint him on compassionate ground - CAT disposed application of respondent with direction to Commissioner to take decision on representation of respondent within 3 months - Application of respondent was rejected as respondent was possessing lower than minimum prescribed qualification, against which respondent filed OA, which was disposed of with direction to reconsider appointment of respondent for post of Tax Assistant - An Office Memorandum was issued which spelled out policy that appointment of a dependent family member on compassionate ground, was permissible only within three years of the death of the bread-winner in harness, hence Review Committee excluded name of respondent from consideration, because more than three years expired after death of his father, hence it was rejected - Appellants preferred Writ Petition before HC, to assail order of CAT, which was disposed of with direction that respondent shall be given appointment either in department where is father was working or in other department - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in directing appellants to appoint respondent - Held, very object of making provision for appointment on compassionate ground, was to provide succour to a family dependent on a government employee, who had unfortunately died in harness, delay in seeking such a claim, was an ante thesis, for purpose for which compassionate appointment was conceived and was contradictory to object sought to be achieved - Respondent sought appointment on compassionate ground first time, by approaching CAT in 2005, by such time, there was no surviving right for appointment on compassionate ground under OM dt. 5-5-2003, which was clearly that appointment, was permissible within three years of death of bread winner in harness, sixteen years had passed by, and as such, there could be no surviving claim for compassionate appointment - Courts and Tribunals must understand, that every such act of sympathy, compassion and discretion, wherein directions were issued for appointment on compassionate ground, could deprive a really needy family requiring financial support, and thereby, push into penury a truly indigent, destitute and impoverish family, hence order of HC, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
