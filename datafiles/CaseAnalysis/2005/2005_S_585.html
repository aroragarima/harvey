Rajesh Vashdev Adnani v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1708; (2005) 8 SCC 390

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3(1)(i) and (iii) - Constitution of India, 1950, art.22 - Smuggling Activities - Detention - Legality - Appeal was directed against the judgment of HC wherein the prayer of the appellant for quashing the order of detention purporting to be u/ss. 3(1)(i) and (iii) of Act was dismissed - Appellant contended that from a perusal of the impugned order, it would appear that the same was almost the verbatim production of the proposal made to the detaining authority by the sponsoring authority only by altering the word 'he' with 'you' - Held, respondent No.2 directed obtaining of some documents when the proposal for detention of the detenu was submitted - Respondent No.2 also sought for the statement made by the detenu before the Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate - Respondent No.2 further took note of a purported pre-detention representation made by the detenu - Detention order was passed upon discussions made in that behalf by her with three officers including Deputy Director - It further appeared that the order of detention as well as grounds therefore were formulated and placed before respondent No.2 for approval - It appeared that only small changes were made by some officers - Perusal of the proposal made by the sponsoring authority and the order of detention passed by the detaining authority would show that except by substituting word 'he' by 'you' no other change was effected - Further, respondent No.2 submitted that from the records produced it would be evident that there had been due application of mind on the part of respondent No.2 in passing the order of detention - It might be so but keeping in view the safeguards envisaged u/art. 22 of the Constitution it was absolutely essential for respondent No.2 to apply her mind not only at the time of grant of approval to the proposal for detention but also when the actual order of detention and grounds thereof were prepared - Thus, there was no application of mind on the part of respondent No.2 and impugned order of detention could not be sustained - Jai Singh v. State of J & K 1985 Indlaw SC 32, relied on - Appeal allowed.
