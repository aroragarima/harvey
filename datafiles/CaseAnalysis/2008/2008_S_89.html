Steel Authority of India Limited v Sales Tax Officer, Rourkela-I Circle and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1078; (2008) 9 SCC 407; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 561; 2008(10) SCALE 223; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 655; [2008] 16 VST 181

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Stock Transfer, Inter-State Sale, Inter-State Trade, Opinion On Merits, Central Sales Tax (Orissa), Rules, 1957

Summary: Central Sales Tax (Orissa), Rules, 1957, r. 12(5); Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Division Bench disposed of the writ petition without any decision on merits because in respect of the assessment year in question, i.e. 2001-02, an order was earlier passed by Supreme Court on 31.03.2006 - Appeal against - Held, reason is the heartbeat of every conclusion - It introduces clarity in an order and without the same it becomes lifeless - Various important questions of law were raised - They were not dealt by the first appellate authority - Remitted the matter to Assistant Commissioner for a fresh consideration - Appeal partly allowed.
