Hussainara Khatoon and Others (Vi) v Home Secretary, State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 391; (1980) SCC (Cr) 57; (1980) 1 SCC 115; AIR 1979 SC 1819; 1979 CRLJ 1134; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1276; 1979 UJ 728

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Directions, Requirement of, Release of under-trial prisoner

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Release of under-trial prisoner - Directions - Requirement of - Petitioner filed instant petition seeking directions to release of under-trial prisoners - Respondent (State) contended that order made by SC on 19-4-1979 directing release of 'S' and 'G', being under-trial prisoners detained in Bhagalpur Central Jail and mentioned in the list furnished by petitioner was not correct and that do not fall within the category of under-trial prisoners who have been in jail for a period longer than the maximum term for which they could have been sentenced, if convicted.

Held, there is absolutely no reason why they should be allowed to continue to remain in jail for a moment longer, since such continuance of detention would be clearly volatile not only of human dignity but also of their fundamental right u/art. 21 of Constitution. It is direct that these under-trial prisoners be released forthwith. If the State wishes to contest the correctness of the information supplied by the HC or the validity of the proposal made by the HC, the State may file an affidavit in reply on or before 20-7-1979. Court will then decide, on the basis of the material placed before it, as to what directions are necessary to be given for setting up more Courts, appointing additional judges and providing more facilities by way of staff and equipment, so as to ensure fulfillment of the fundamental right of the accused to speedy trial u/art. 21 of Constitution. Order accordingly.
