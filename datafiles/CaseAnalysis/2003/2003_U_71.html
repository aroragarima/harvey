Dwarka Prasad and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 555; (2003) 6 SCC 535; (2003) SCC (L&S) 941; AIR 2003 SC 2971; 2003 (3) ESC 382; 2003 (98) FLR 896; JT 2003 (6) SC 113; 2003 LabIC 2564; 2003 (3) LLN 860; 2003(5) SCALE 461; 2004 (1) SLJ 376; 2003 (5) SLR 153; 2003 (4) SLT 656; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 784; 2003 (5) Supreme 166; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2082

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Service; Trade

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(1), 309 - Department of Revenue (Custom Appraisers Recruitment) Rules, 1988 - Whether a quota of 20 per cent fixed for Preventive Officers as compared to 75 per cent for Examining Officers for promotion to post of appraiser is arbitrary and discriminatory - Held, holders of two posts constitute two distinct classes with different conditions of service and nature of duties It is open to promoting authority to treat them differently in matter of providing avenues of promotion to Group 'B' posts There is no legal or constitutional infirmity in lower quota fixed for POs as compared to EOs for post of Appraiser Group 'B' - Further held, art.14 and 16, Constitution of India, 1950 cannot be pressed into service to describe fixation of lower quota for POs as discriminatory - It is well established in law that right to be considered for promotion on fair and equal basis without discrimination may be claimed as a legal and a fundamental right under art.14 and 16 of Constitution, but chances of promotion as such cannot be claimed as of right.
