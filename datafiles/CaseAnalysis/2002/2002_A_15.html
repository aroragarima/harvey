Commissioner Of Central Excise, Calcutta v A.S.C.U. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1896; (2003) 9 SCC 230; 2003 (90) ECC 438; 2003 (151) E.L.T. 481; 2003 (54) RLT 607

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 C(2) - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Validity of order passed in application u/s. 35 C(2)? - Under Act there is no power of review available to tribunal - Only power available is power of rectification of a mistake apparent from record - If a decision is based solely on material which is irrelevant or which could not have been used, then, possibly it could be said that there is a mistake apparent from record - However, if a decision is based on more than one material, then merely because in process of arriving at final decision, reliance was placed on some material which could not have been used it can never be said that in final decision there is a mistake apparent from record - Held, it is clear that there is no mistake apparent on record - In such a case there can be no rectification u/s. 35 C(2) -Impugned order set aside and restore original - Appeal allowed.
