Tamilnad Mercantile Bank Share Holders' Welfare v S. C. Sekar and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2071; (2009) 2 SCC 784; 2010 (1) CompLJ 357; 2008 (5) CTC 844; JT 2008 (13) SC 49; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 878; 2008(16) SCALE 205; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Court Of Record, Adjourned Meeting, Reappointment, Unlimited

Summary: Corporate - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Contempt petition - Aggrieved person - Annual General Meeting - Appellant was an Association of shareholders of a Bank - Suit filed by it involved the question as to whether the power of attorneys executed by the share-holders were required to be registered or not - Suit was filed at a point of time when 83rd Annual General Meeting of the Bank was to be held - In view of the orders of injunction passed by the Madras HC, admittedly the Annual General Meeting could not be held - Order by the Single Judge dt. 26-7-2006, Annual General Meeting was permitted to be held in respect of item nos. 1, 2 and 6 of the agenda, the same was adjourned in respect of agenda items nos. 3 to 5 - Appellant filed an application for injunction and also raised objections before the Chairman appointed by the Court in terms of the order dt. 27-3-2008 - Reports of the Chairman showed that consultative process had been taken recourse to - Appellant had participated in the election - Three reports submitted by the Chairman in sealed covers were read over and opened by the Single Judge, copies whereof were also handed over to the parties that no effect to the said Resolution be given which was passed on 29-4-2008 - Ad interim ex parte injunction on 12-6-2008 restraining the Managing Director of the Company from implementing the resolution or item of business in relation to the election of Directors passed at the Annual General Meetings held on 5-6-2008 till the disposal of contempt petition - Several proceedings were initiated by the respondents - Appeals were preferred u/s. 19(1) of the 1971 Act - DB of the HC held that the single judge's order holding that the contempt appeals were maintainable did not in any way adversely affect the right of the respondent - Hence, the instant appeals - Whether, the purported ad interim order passed by the Single Judge in contempt proceeding should have been interfered with or not by the Division Bench - Held, the Directors representing the Reserve Bank of India were not made parties in the contempt petition - Full disclosure as regards the subsequent proceedings was not made therein - Contempt petition was taken up by the Single Judge who was not acquainted with the fact of the matter - Appellant had not approached the Court with clean hands and was thus not entitled to any equitable relief - Respondents were thus prejudiced indisputably in view of the interim order - They were, thus, 'persons aggrieved' and could move the higher court - If an interim order was passed wholly without jurisdiction, an appeal would lie there against - Order passed by the Single Judge dt. 20-6-2008 was found to be illegal and without jurisdiction having been passed without any application of mind and obtained by the appellant by suppressing material facts and the proceedings between the parties - Hence, it was eminently a fit case where instant Court could refuse to interfere with the impugned order of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
