Girish Chandra Gupta and another v Uttar Pradesh Industrial Development Corporation Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 532; (2012) 13 SCC 452; AIR 2013 SC 352; 2013 (1) CompLJ 257; 2013 (1) CPJ(Sc) 9; JT 2012 (12) SC 423; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 928; 2012(12) SCALE 65; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 287

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP; Trade

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices, Trade Practice, Monopolies And Restrictive Trade Practices Commission, Competition (Amendment) Ordinance, 2009

Summary: MRTP - Competition - Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Act, 1969 - Competition Act, 2002 - Restrictive Trade Practice - Compensation - Maintainability - Dismissed - Sustainability - Appellants had filed compensation application before Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices Commission u/s. 12B of Act, but since Act was repealed and MRTP Commission was dissolved and all cases pertaining to monopolistic trade practices or restrictive trade practices were transferred to Competition Appellate Tribunal to be adjudicated by Tribunal with provision of Act as if Act had not been repealed - Respondents before Tribunal raised preliminary objections to maintainability of compensation application contending that appellants had not initiated separate proceedings either u/s. 10 or s. 36B of Act alleging unfair trade practices by respondent, hence application of appellants u/s. 12B of Act - Tribunal dismissed petition of appellant which was concurred by Competition Appellate Tribunal - Aggrieved instant appeals - Whether Tribunal and appellate Tribunal were justified in dismissing petition on preliminary objection of maintainability - Held, In fact, s. 12B was introduced in Act by Act 30 of 1984 as an independent remedy for a claimant in addition to a suit that he may file to claim any loss or damage that he may suffer by reason of any monopolistic or restrictive or unfair trade practice as would be clear from s. 12B(4) There was no reference at all in s. 12B of Act to provisions of either s. 10 or s. 36B of Act and if Parliament intended that power of MRTP Commission to award compensation u/s. 12B of Act was to be dependent on exercise of power of MRTP Commission either u/s. 10 or u/s. 36B of Act, Parliament would have made this intention clear in language of some provision in s. 12B of Act - There was also no reference in either s. 10 or in s. 36B of Act to any of provisions of s. 12B of Act and if Parliament intended to make s. 10, 12B, 36B of Act interdependent, there would have been some indication of this intention of Parliament in s. 10 or in s. 36B of Act - In absence of any such indication of this intention of Parliament to make provisions of s. 12B of Act dependent on initiation of an inquiry or proceeding u/s. 10 or s. 36B of Act, Competition Appellate Tribunal clearly erred in coming to conclusion that interdependence of provisions of s. 10 or s. 36B with s. 12B could not be lost sight of and in absence of a separate proceeding alleging unfair, monopolistic or restrictive trade practice, an application for compensation u/s. 12B of Act was not maintainable - Appeals allowed.
