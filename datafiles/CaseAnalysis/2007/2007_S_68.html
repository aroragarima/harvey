Shiv Kumar Sharma v Santosh Kumari

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 933; (2007) 8 SCC 600; AIR 2008 SC 171; 2007 (6) ALD(SC) 116; 2007(6) ALL MR 935; 2008 (2) Bom.C.R. 313; 2007 (5) CTC 453; JT 2007 (11) SC 260; 2007 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3858; 2008 (149) PLR 186; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 515; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 465; 2008 (104) RD 489; 2007(11) SCALE 303; 2007 (7) SCJ 353; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Decree for possession in favour of the Plaintiff-respondent - Held, High Court was not correct in framing the additional issues of its own which did not arise for consideration in the suit or in the appeal - High Court should have formulated the points for its consideration in terms of O. 41 r. 31 - On the pleadings of the parties and in view of the submissions made, no such question arose for its consideration - In any event, if a second suit was maintainable in terms of O. 2 r.4 of the Code no leave was required to be granted - Appeal disposed of.
