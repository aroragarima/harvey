Rashtriya Mill Mazdoor Sangh v National Textile Corporation (Southmaharashtra)

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1962; (1996) SCC (L&S) 306; (1996) 1 SCC 313; AIR 1996 SC 710; 1996 (1) AnWR 30; 1996 (72) FLR 114; JT 1995 (9) SC 186; 1996 (1) LLJ 787; 1995(6) SCALE 609; 1996 (1) SCJ 200; 1996 (1) SLR 12; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 641; 1996 (1) Supreme 61; 1996 (1) UJ 456

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Textiles

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, Textile Undertakings (Taking Over of Management) Act, 1983

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Textiles - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972, s. 3(7) - Resignation - Take over - Custodian - Respondent, an employee of the respondent textile mill, on his resignation, moved the Controlling authority for his claim for gratuity - By the promulgation of the 1983 Ordinance, management of the textile mill was taken over by the Central Govt. and vested with the National Textiles Corporation (NTC) - In the proceedings before the Controlling authority, NTC appeared and contested its liability towards the respondent and the textile mill was directed to deposit the amount of gratuity in the Court - Respondent filed appeal and claimed that NTC was liable to pay the gratuity amount to him and not the textile mill - Industrial court allowing the appeal directed that failure of the textile mill to deposit gratuity amount could be recovered from the NTC - W.P. of NTC was dismissed and HC held that textile mill was liable to pay gratuity and not the NTC - Rashtriya Mill Mazdoor Sangh (RMMS) had been impleaded as a party in the proceedings - Hence, instant appeal by special leave - Whether, NTC was liable for the payment of gratuity to the respondent under the Act - Held, that u/s. 4 of the Act, gratuity was payable to the respondent on his resignation on 22-3-1983 and gratuity under the Act was payable to him on 23-3-1983 which was before the taking over of the management of the textile mill by the NTC - S. 3(7) of the Act provided that any liability incurred by the textile company in relation to the textile undertaking before the appointed day was enforceable against the concerned textile company and not against the Central Govt. or the custodian - Liabilities for the period prior to the take over of the management were to be discharged from the amount payable to the owner of the textile undertaking for the acquisition of the undertaking and not by the NTC - NTC was not liable to pay to the respondent the gratuity amount under the Act - Appeal dismissed.
