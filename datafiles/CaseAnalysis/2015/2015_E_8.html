S. Satyanarayana v Energo Masch Power Engineering and Consulting Private Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 219; AIR 2015 SC 2166; 2015 (2) BC 348; [2015] 190 Comp Cas 1; 2015 (3) CompLJ 33; 2015 CRLJ 2874; 2015 (2) CTC 553; JT 2015 (5) SC 425; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 300; 2015 (1) OLR 1038; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 903; 2015(4) SCALE 214; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 1094

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 621, 628 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420 - Proceedings by Special Court - Interference by HC - Permissibility - Appellant complainant lodged criminal complaint against respondents accused alleging that they had induced appellant to invest into accused company - Further, respondents allegedly filed false declarations and thereby obtained loans and swindled that money - Special Court initiated proceedings u/s. 628 of the Act and u/ss. 120B and 420 IPC - Respondents challenged Special Court proceedings before HC - HC quashed proceedings - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, allegations are that the accused conspired with each other to cheat the complainant and a series of transactions gave rise to offence u/s. 120B r/w s. 420 of IPC as also s. 628 of the Act. It is, therefore, clear that if the Special Court has jurisdiction to try offences under both the aforesaid Acts then the trial can certainly continue in respect of the offences which do not require the complainant to belong to the categories specified u/s. 621 of the Act Thus the trial could certainly continue against those accused under the IPC. The HC completely overlooked the fact that the complaint made allegations against some of the accused only in respect of ss. 120B and 420 of IPC and there was no reason in law to quash a complaint against them on the ground that they were immune from prosecution u/s. 628 of the Act by virtue of s. 621 of that Act. Hence, findings of the HC was set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Even if a number of persons are accused of offences under a special enactment such as 'the Companies Act and as also the IPC' in respect of the same transaction or facts and even if some could not be tried under the special enactment, it is the special court alone which would have jurisdiction to try all the offences based on the same transaction to avoid multiplicity of proceedings.
