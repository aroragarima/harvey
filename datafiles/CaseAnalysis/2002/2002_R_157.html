R.D. Goyal and another v Reliance Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1417; (2003) 1 SCC 81; [2003] 113 Comp Cas 1; 2003 (1) CompLJ 202; 2002 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2002 (9) SC 594; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 76; 2002(8) SCALE 488; 2002 (6) SLT 535; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 231; 2002 (8) Supreme 301

Case Digest

Subject: MRTP; Trade

Keywords: English Companies Act, 1948 (ENG), Judgments Act, 1838, Companies Clauses Act, 1863, Monopolies and Restrictive Trade Practices (Amendment) Bill, 1991

Summary: MRTP Act, 1969 - S. 2(e) - whether shares or convertible debentures even before they are allotted can be considered to be "goods" within the meaning of S. 2(e) of the MRTP Act - a debenture would not come within the purview of definition of goods, in as much as, although the shares and stocks are included in the definition of goods but debentures are not - held, in company law 'allotment' means the appropriation out of the previously unappropriated capital of a company, of a certain number of shares to a person. Till such allotment the shares do not exist as such. It is on allotment in this sense that the shares come into existence - therefore, shares pending allotment in view of the provisions of law cannot be said to be goods.
