Ram Singh @ Chhaju v State of Himachal Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 75; (2010) 2 SCC 445; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 594; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1616; 2010 CRLJ 1655; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 23; JT 2010 (1) SC 666; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 230; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 851; 2010(1) SCALE 669; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 95; 2010 (2) ShimLC 33; 2010 (1) UC 324

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Rape - Conviction - Accused allegedly raped the victim - Trial court found that the prosecution had not been able to prove that the accused had sexual intercourse with the victim and therefore, acquitted the appellant - On appeal, HC set aside the acquittal order of trial court and convicted accused u/s. 376 of IPC - Accused contended that in the absence of any injury on the private parts of the victim, HC should have disbelieved the prosecution story - Hence, present appeal - Whether accused rightly convicted u/s. 376 of IPC by HC? - Held, yes - Doctor who has been examined victim has found that the victim was used to sexual intercourse and as such absence of injury on the private parts of the victim may not be very significant - Moreover, testimony of the victim inspires confidence - Her testimony is not only corroborated by other witnesses but also by the medical evidence - HC also observed that there is no contradiction in the testimony of victim and her son PW-7 - Investigating Officer's report also showed that at the site of incident, grass and plants of some crop were found damaged and ruffled - No inference in HC's order - Appeal dismissed.
