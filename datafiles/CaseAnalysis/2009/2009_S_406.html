Ash A Devi And Anr v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2275; (2010) 15 SCC 329

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s.113B - Conviction - Justifiability of order - Deceased was married with accused about 3-4 years before incident happened and was pregnant at time of occurrence - PW5, received information that deceased was being beaten by her husband and his parents and sisters - PW5 along with her relatives rushed to deceased home and was told by her mother-in-law that she was in process of delivering child - PW5 then rushed to police station and lodged FIR - Trial Court relying upon evidence of PW11, PW13 and PW15 and documents which had been produced on record, convicted accused u/ss.304-B and 498-A of IPC and sentenced to seven years RI u/s.304-B and to undergo imprisonment for life - Accused thereupon filed appeal in HC which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether ordered of HC was justified - Held, evidence against other accused however was unexceptional - Deceased mother-in-law ,father-in-law and husband for having brought inadequate dowry and repeated demands were made and further that sum had been given after marriage pursuant to demand - It was true that if statement from Panchayat member had come on record, case of prosecution would be strengthened but this omission would not mean that evidence available was insufficient to record conviction, more particularly, in back drop of s. 113-B of 1872 Act - However, primary villain was mother in law and that her husband and son were in fact acting as tools at her behest - Further, from reading of Ext. P.2 with statements of ocular evidence it appears that demands had been made over for period of time - Thus, presence of burn marks on abdomen of deceased indicate torture and cruelty meted out to deceased even on day when she met her death and from doctor's evidence it transpires that deceased was first strangulated, and, thereafter she had been hanged in bedroom to make it look as case of suicide - Hence, keeping in mind presumption u/s.113-B of 1872 Act, there was no merit in appeals - Appeal dismissed.
