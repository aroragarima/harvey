(1) Kuriachan Chacko and Others; (2) C. N v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2124; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 631; (2008) 8 SCC 708; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 893; JT 2008 (7) SC 614; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 272; 2008(9) SCALE 787; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Ordered accordingly, Moral Turpitude, Private Party, Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 (As Amended up to 2000), Lottery, Lottery Ticket

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 34, 415, 420 - Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, 1978, ss. 2, 2(c), 3, 4, 5 - Reserve Bank of India Act, 1934 - ss. 45I(bb), 58B, 845S - Deepasthambham Scheme - Collection of money - Cheating - Validity - Appellants/accused partners of LIS, were engaged in business of sale of lotteries and magazines and had floated scheme known as 'LIS Deepasthambham Scheme' - Membership fee collected were being paid to unit holders initially very promptly - but there was a jolt to scheme - FIR was registered against promoters for offence punishable u/s. 420 IPC and under 1978 Act and also under 1934 Act - Trial Court framed charge against appellants for offences punishable u/s. 420 r/w s. 34, IPC and u/ss. 4 and 5 r/w ss. 2(c) and 3 of 1978 Act and, discharged all accused for offences punishable u/ss. 4 and 5 r/w ss. 2(e) and (3) of 1978 Act and also under ss. 45I(bb), 8 45S and 58B of 1934 Act - Aggrieved by order passed by Trial Court, accused as well as State filed revision petitions in HC - Single Judge observed that there was no error on part of Trial Court in not framing charge against accused for offences punishable under Act of 1934- HC observed that though no express concession was made by State, it was not seriously challenged by prosecution that Trial Court had committed any error in discharging accused - HC, after considering rival contentions of parties and referring to relevant decisions on point, held that Trial Court was right in discharging accused for offences punishable u/ss. 4 and 5 r/w ss. 2(e) and 3 of Act and also under 1934, Act - HC held that Trial Court was also right in framing charge against accused for offences punishable u/ss. 4 and 5 r/w ss. 2(c) and 3 of Act and also u/s. 420 r/w Section 34, IPC - HC, therefore, confirmed order passed by Trial Court and dismissed both revisions - Hence instant appeals - Whether Trial Court and HC had committed any error in coming to such conclusion at stage of framing of charge - Held, Trial Court as well as HC considered facts of case and held that there was element of cheating inasmuch as representation was made by accused that every unit holder would get double amount invested by him, representation was false, maker of representation was aware that representation was not true and by such representation, he deceived victim to believe representation to be true and actuated him to act on such representation - Promoters induced common public to part with money on lure of doubling amount - Prima facie, Courts were satisfied that but for such representation and benefit sought to be given under scheme, victims would not have acted on such representation - It was, therefore, a case of application of s. 415, IPC - Prima facie case had been made out in absence of better explanation by accused - If it was so, it could be said to be a case for application of s. 420 r/w s. 34 IPC, of course, at this stage - Hence, Courts below had not committed any error in coming to such conclusion at stage of framing of charge and no interference by HC was therefore, called for - Appeals dismissed.
