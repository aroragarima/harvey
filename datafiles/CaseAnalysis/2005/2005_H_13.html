Guntaiah and others v Hambamma and others

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Venkatarama Reddi

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 423; (2005) 6 SCC 228; 2005 (5) ALD(SC) 78; JT 2005 (6) SC 347; 2005 (4) KLT 227; 2005(5) SCALE 580; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 770

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands), Act, 1978, ss. 4, 5 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f) - Mysore Land Revenue (Amendment Rules), 1960, rr. 43-C, 43-D, 43-E, 43-J, 43-G - Conditions regarding alienation - Lands were given permanently with a restriction that grantees shall not alienate these lands to third parties for a period of 15 years - Whether such conditions sustainable in law? - Held, grantee is not challenging condition - Third party is not entitled to say that conditions imposed by grantor to grantee were void - Conditions restricting alienation imposed by authorities are legally valid - Appeals allowed.
