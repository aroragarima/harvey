Ram Niwas v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 552; (2010) 12 SCC 250; JT 2010 (7) SC 429; 2010 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4127; 2010(7) SCALE 319; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 112; 2010 (3) UC 1427

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 452, 302 and 307 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court on the basis of evidence acquitted 'X' and 'Y' but convicted 'Z' u/s. 302 and 307 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life and to pay a fine of Rs.25,000/- on two counts - On appeal, HC affirmed the order of the trial court - Appellant filed appeal against said order contending that having disbelieved the prosecution story as given by PW7 with respect to the involvement of 'X' and 'Y', there was no justification whatsoever in relying on her evidence with respect to the murder of deceased by the appellant - Whether trial court rightly convicted appellant u/s. 302 and 307? - Held, yes - It is true, as contended, that the relations between the two parties were extremely strained on account of the murder of 'A' - While this fact could, undoubtedly, be a reason for false implication of the accused, but on the contrary, it could also be a motive for the commission of the crime - However, in the light of the fact that the FIR had been recorded within a reasonable time of the incident and the medical evidence fully supports the ocular version, and additionally, the trial court has given the benefit of doubt to some of the accused, as they had no active role to play, the possibility of false implication has also been examined and dealt with - The courts below have rightly assessed the evidence and being cognizant of the strained relations between the parties and the possibility of false implication, have accordingly given the benefit of doubt to two of the accused - No interference is thus called for in this appeal - Appeal dismissed.
