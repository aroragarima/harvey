Pravin C. Shah v K. A. Mohammed Ali and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19805; (2001) 8 SCC 650; AIR 2001 SC 3041; 2001 (45) ALR 776; 2001 (4) AWC 3212; 2002 (1) CompLJ 35; JT 2001 (9) SC 46; 2001 (3) KLT 820; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 408; 2001(7) SCALE 60; 2001 (5) SCJ 278; 2001 (7) SLT 153; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 675; 2001 (7) Supreme 386; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 140

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Advocates

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 12 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 34(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g) and 21 - When an advocate was punished for contempt of court, can he appear thereafter as a counsel in courts, unless he purges himself of such contempt ? If he cannot, then what is way he can purge himself of such contempt ? - A mere statement made by a contemnor before, court that he apologises is hardly enough to amount to purging himself of contempt - Court must be satisfied of genuineness of apology - If court is so satisfied and on this basis, accepts apology as genuine, court has to make an order holding that contemnor has purged himself of contempt - Till such an order is passed by courts delinquent advocate would continue to be under spell of interdict - Appeal disposed of.
