Som Dev and Others v Rati Ram and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 470; (2006) 10 SCC 788; AIR 2006 SC 3297; 2006 (65) ALR 484; 2006 (4) AWC 3926; 2006 (5) CTC 79; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 141; JT 2006 (8) SC 469; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 303; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 400; 2006(9) SCALE 31; 2006 (Supp) RD 639; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 778; 2007 (1) UC 32

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Code Of Civil Procedure, 1976, Registration and Other Related Laws (Amendment) Act, 2001, Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17(1), 17(1)(b), 17(2)(vi), 43 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 107, 123, 53A, 54 - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - CPC, 1976, o. 23, r. 3 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 44 - Suit for recovery of possession of suit property in enforcement of a right of pre-emption - Plea of defendant appellant that a right was created in present plaintiff by a decree which was one based on a compromise and since decree purported to create a right in plaintiff in a property in which he had no pre-existing right, compromise decree required registration in terms of s.17(1) - HC held that decree was based on a family settlement which did not require registration and that decree itself did not require registration in view of s.17(2)(vi) - Appeal against - Held, all decrees and orders of Court including a compromise decree subject to exception as regards properties that are outside subject matter of suit, do not require registration on ground that they are hit by ss., 17(1)(b), (c) - Appeal dismissed.
