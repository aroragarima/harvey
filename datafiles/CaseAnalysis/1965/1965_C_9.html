R. S. Madanappa and Others v Chandramma and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 471; AIR 1965 SC 1812; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Improvements

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Whether person concerned can plead that he was induced to hold an erroneous belief with respect of title to property? - Held, No he cannot - Person knowing true position relating to title in property in his possession cannot plead that he was induced to hold erroneous belief by reason of conduct of real owner of that property - (B) Whether s. 115 includes equitable estoppel? - Further held, law of estoppel by representation is confined to provisions of s. 115 and apart from provisions of this section there is nothing like what is called 'equitable estoppel' as evolved by English judge - Object of estoppel is to prevent fraud and secure justice between parties by promotion of honesty and good faith - Where one person makes misrepresentation to other about a fact he would not be shut out by rule of estoppel if that other person knew true state of facts and must consequently not have been misled by misrepresentation - (C) When can principle of estoppel be invoked? - Further held, person setting up an estoppel against another must show that his position was altered by reason of representation or conduct of latter, otherwise even general principle of estoppel cannot be invoked - Appeal dismissed.
