Rajasthan Housing Board And Another v G.S. Investments And Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 729; (2007) 1 SCC 477; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 45; JT 2006 (9) SC 612; 2006(11) SCALE 166; 2006 (Supp) RD 333; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 226 - Rajasthan Housing Board Act, 1970, s. 60 - Auction - Cancellation of auction - Rights of an auction bidder - Held, a person who had made highest bid in auction did not acquire any right to have auction concluded in his favour until Chairman of Housing Board had passed an order to that effect - Chairman of Housing Board could not exercise his power in an arbitrary manner but so long as an order regarding final acceptance of bid had not been passed by Chairman, highest bidder acquired no vested right to have auction concluded in his favour and auction proceedings could always be cancelled - HC erred in entertaining WP and in not only issuing a direction for consideration of representation but also issuing a further direction to appellant to issue a demand note of balance amount - Appeal allowed.
