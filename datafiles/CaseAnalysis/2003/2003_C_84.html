Dhananjay v Chief Executive Officer, Zilla Parishad, Jalna

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 141; (2003) SCC (L&S) 170; (2003) 2 SCC 386; AIR 2003 SC 1175; 2003 (2) AWC 998; 2003 (3) Bom.C.R. 547; 2003 (19) CLR 703; 2003 (2) ESC 111; 2003 (96) FLR 1002; 2003 (2) JhrCR(SC) 5; JT 2003 (1) SC 632; 2003 LabIC 748; 2003 (2) LLJ 179; 2003 (1) LLN 802; 2003(1) SCALE 585; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 744; 2003 (2) SLJ 46; 2003 (2) SLR 298; 2003 (1) SLT 678; 2003 (2) Supreme 120; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 761

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Central Civil Services

Summary: Service Law- Whether the order of termination of services is simpliciter or is punitive attaching stigma? - Held, It is not possible to hold that the order of termination of services was not simpliciter or the misconduct was the foundation for passing such order. Even if an enquiry was ordered to find out or verify the truth or otherwise and the allegation by itself does not establish that the respondent had any such design to somehow remove the appellant from services.
