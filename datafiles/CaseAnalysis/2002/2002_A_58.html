Chairman, State Bank of India and another v All Orissa State Bank Officers Association and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 299; (2002) 5 SCC 669; (2002) SCC (L&S) 805; AIR 2002 SC 2279; 2002 (2) CGLJ 145; 2002 (2) CLR 517; 2002 (94) FLR 338; JT 2002 (4) SC 537; 2002 LabIC 2153; 2002 (2) LLJ 562; 2002 (3) MLJ(SC) 48; 2002(4) SCALE 423; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 797; 2002 (4) SLR 48; 2002 (3) SLT 609; 2002 (4) Supreme 70; 2002 (3) UPLBEC 2052

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Trusts & Associations - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947, s. 2-A - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Trade Unions Act, 1926 - Verification of Membership and Recognition of Trade Unions' Rules, 1994, r. 23 - Circular - Non-recognized Association - Rights on par with Recognized Association - Entitlement - Respondent no. 1/Association filed petition against unfair, unjust and hostile treatment towards its members before HC - Further claim treatment at par with office-bearers of recognized association - HC allowed petition, quashed para 2 of circular as arbitrary and directed appellants to confer rights to respondents as available u/r. 24 of 1994, Rules - Appellants filed review petition which was partly allowed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that association did not satisfy criteria laid down by the 1994, Rules framed by Govt. - Further HC was erred in quashing para 2 of circular - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, very fact that certain rights were vested in a non-recognized union shows that Trade Union Act and the Rules framed there under acknowledge existence of a non-recognised union - Such a union was not superfluous entity and it had relevance in specific matters relating to administration of establishment - Therefore, management/employer could not out rightly refuse to had any discussion with a non-recognized union in matters relating to service conditions of individual members and other matters incidental thereto - It was also to be kept in mind that for sake of industrial peace and proper administration of industry it was necessary for management to seek cooperation of entire work force - In instant case, contents of para 2 of circular clearly gives impression that management had decided at threshold before being aware of nature of dispute raised that its representatives should had no discussion at all with office bearers of non-recognized associations, which was not only contrary to provision in r. 24 of the 1994 Rules but also counter to scheme of Trade union Act and Rules - Thus, HC had not conceded any right to non-recognized union to participate in discussion relating to general issues concerning all workmen - Hence, no illegality or infirmity in judgment and order passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
