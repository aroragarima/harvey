Baldev Singh Mann v Surjit Singh Dhiman

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1824; (2009) 1 SCC 633; AIR 2009 SC 1045; 2008(14) SCALE 568; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 540

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Corrupt Practices, Election Petition, Demeanour Of Witness, Material Particulars, Criminal Charge, Civil Dispute, Representation Of People Act, 1951, Revenue Officer, Any Other Person, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Purity

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 80, 81 r/w 98, 99, 100 - Plea that respondent's election was liable to be declared void as the respondent had committed corrupt practices by obtaining and procuring assistance, for the furtherance of the prospects of his election, from persons who were in the service of the government and were gazetted officers - Whether the evidence of the appellant on record is adequate to constitute corrupt practice within the meaning of section 123 (7)? - Held, charge of corrupt practice is a quasi-criminal in character and it has to be proved as a criminal charge and proved in the court - Appellant has failed to prove the basic ingredients of corrupt practices under s. 123(7) - Appeal dismissed.
