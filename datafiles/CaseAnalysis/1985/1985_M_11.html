Collector of Customs and Central Excise and v M/S. Oriental Timber Industries

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 201; (1985) 3 SCC 85; (1985) SCC (L&S) 365; AIR 1985 SC 746; 1985 (2) CCC 243; 1985 CrLR(SC) 341; 1985 ECR 665; 1985 (20) E.L.T. 202; 1985(1) SCALE 627; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 475; 1985 TAXLR 2511; 1985 UJ 574

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Central Excise Rules 1944 (contd), Manufacturer of plywood circles

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3, item 16-B - Manufacturer of plywood circles - Excise duty - Legality - Respondent was a manufacturer of plywood circles to be used as component parts of packing materials for wire and cables - Respondent used to be assessed to duty under the Act on basis of total area of circles manufactured and duty used to be collected when circles were issued out of factory premises - On 13-2-1967 an audit objection was taken to this mode of assessment of excise duty on ground that process of cutting out circles and punching of holes could not be considered as incidental or ancillary to completion of manufacture of plywood - However, audit objection pointed out that levy of excise duty should be on total area of blocks or panels of plywood that came out of press and not on area of circles made out of blocks or panels - Appellant No. 2 issued notice to respondent to furnish area of plywood manufactured at panel stage for taking clearance of plywood circles - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging the validity of notice - Single Judge disposed of the matter - Respondent filed appeal before DB, which was allowed and quashed the notices - Aggrieved, appellant filed instant appeal - Whether plywood manufactured by respondent and utilised by respondent in manufacturing plywood circles to be used as component parts of packing material for wire and cables was exigible to excise duty under the Act - Held, item 16-B of s. 3 of the Act, made it clear that plywood in sheets, blocks, boards like-attracts excise duty. - A special provision by way of exception was made only in case of plywood for tea chests when cut to in panels or shooks and packed in sets - There was nothing to indicate in item 16-B of s. 3 of the Act that plywood should be trimmed or sanded - Plywood was manufactured as soon as it comes out of press, though same might not be trimmed or sanded out of which circles were to be produced - There was nothing to indicate that plywood in panel stage, not trimmed and riot sanded, was not known in market as plywood - Plywood when it comes out of press at panel stage, therefore, clearly falls within item 16-B of First Schedule of the Act, and appellants were justified in seeking to levy duty on plywood at panel stage - HC was erred in allowing writ petition and in quashing the notices - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and notices issued by respondents were valid and lawful - Appeal allowed.
