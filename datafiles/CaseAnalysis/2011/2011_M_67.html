High Court of Judicature at Patna v Madan Mohan Prasad and others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 558; (2011) 9 SCC 65; 2013 (1) AWC 108; JT 2011 (10) SC 402; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 533; 2011(10) SCALE 15; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 972; 2011 (5) SLR 676

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Delhi and Ajmer Rent Control Act, 1947

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 235 - Promotion - Entitlement - 1st respondent Munsif was suspended from service and departmental proceedings were initiated against him - 1st respondent challenged the disciplinary action against him before the HC - HC dismissed 1st respondent's writ petitions - 1st respondent challenged the HC order before the SC - SC directed the HC (administrative side) to reinstate the 1st respondent, however, granted liberty to the HC (administrative side) to amend the charge sheet before initiating departmental proceedings and to consider the question of his suspension from service afresh - Notification was issued on 10-12-1981 posting 1st respondent as Additional Munsif - 1st respondent filed a petition before the HC for quashing Notification dated 10-12-1981 and prayed to direct the HC (administrative side) to give him promotions from the dates when his juniors named in the petition - In the meantime, HC had concluded departmental proceedings and served a show cause notice calling upon him to show cause as to why he should not be removed from service - HC, hence, dismissed 1st respondent's pending petition as infructuous - 1st respondent filed SLP (C) No.8923 of 1983 challenging the HC order, however, that was later withdrawn - 1st respondent, in the meantime, superannuated - HC (administrative side) recommended to the State Government for withholding 1st respondent's pension permanently, however, in 1st respondent's SLP (C) No.8621 of 1985, SC directed the State Government to restore the pension payable to him and pay arrears of his retirement benefits - After finalization of pension, 1st respondent sought lawful claims as were given to his juniors - Since the HC (administrative side) rejected 1st respondent's representation, he filed writ petition (CWJC No. 6538 of 1990) before the DB - DB directed the appellant HC (administrative side) to consider the case of promotion of the 1st respondent as also consequential benefit in accordance with law which had given rise to the instant appeal - Whether the 1st respondent was entitled to the promotion as that was claimed by him in his petition before the HC - Held, promotion was not a matter of right much less a fundamental right, more particularly when promotion in the subordinate judiciary was to be dealt with by the HC which had complete control over the subordinate judiciary in view of art. 235 of the Constitution - All rights and claims of the 1st respondent got crystallized when SC passed order dated 25-11-1986 in SLP (C) No.8621 of 1985 read with order dated 30-8-1983 passed by SC in SLP (C) No. 8923 of 1983 - If the 1st respondent had any other claim he ought to have made the same before SC when the above numbered Special Leave Petitions were disposed of - In fact both the Special Leave Petitions were dismissed and therefore all his claims stood finally rejected, except the direction given to pay him the pension etc. mentioned in order dated 25-11-1986 passed in SLP (C) No.8621 of 1985 - No grievance was made by the 1st respondent in the petition before the HC that the direction given by SC on 25-11-1986 in SLP (C) No.8621 of 1985 were not complied with by the appellant - Neither at the time of disposal of SLP (C) No.8923 of 1983 nor at the time of disposal of SLP (C) No. 8621 of 1985 the 1st respondent had claimed any other relief and had not obtained permission to claim relief of promotion in future - Hence, the relief claimed in CWJC No. 6538 of 1990 could not have been granted by the Court - It was evident that, CWJC No. 6538 of 1990 was filed for the same reliefs which were claimed in his earlier writ petitions and were rejected, and therefore, it could not have been entertained - Further SLP No. 8261 of 1985 which was filed by the 1st respondent against judgment and order of the HC in CWJC No. 2059 of 1984 was dismissed and the only relief granted by SC was to direct the State to restore pension payable to him with arrears due on the basis that he had superannuated from service from the date of superannuation and a further direction was issued to pay him Provident Fund, Gratuity and leave salary as might be admissible to him on superannuation - SC had never directed that the HC on its administrative side should consider the claim of the 1st respondent regarding deemed promotions - Hence, HC had erred in law in directing the appellant to consider the case of promotion of 1st respondent as also the consequential benefits in accordance with law by the impugned judgment - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal disposed of.
