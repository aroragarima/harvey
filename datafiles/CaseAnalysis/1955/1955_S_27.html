Jayaram Vithoba and Another v State of Bombay

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Vivian Bose, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 74; AIR 1956 SC 146; 1956 (58) BomLR 513; 1956 CRLJ 318; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 92; 1956 SCJ 150; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1049

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 423(1)(b), 423(1)(d) and 439(2) - Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act, 1887 - Magistrate found accused guilty under two provisions - High Court convicted under one and acquitted under the other - (A) Whether on language of s. 423(1)(b) of CrPC imposition of a sentence u/s. 5 and setting aside conviction u/s. 4(a) of Bombay Prevention of Gambling Act, 1887 by HC is justified? - S. 423(1)(b) of CrPC only provides for altering finding and maintaining sentence, and that can apply only to cases where finding of guilt under one section is altered to a finding of guilt under another - Held, when the HC set aside conviction u/s. 4(a) and affirmed that u/s. 5, there were two distinct findings, one of reversal and another of affirmance and there was no question of alteration - Hence, on language of s. 423(1)(b), imposition of a sentence u/s. 5 could not be justified - (A)(1) Whether HC was competent u/s. 423(1)(d) to have passed the sentence? - Power to pass a sentence u/s. 5 was reserved to Appellate Court expressly by s. 423(1)(d) - When a conviction was affirmed in appeal but no sentence had been awarded by Trial Magistrate, award of sentence was consequential on and incidental to affirmance of conviction and it was just and proper order to be passed under law - Such an order would not be an enhancement of sentence - Held, it was, within competence of HC u/s. 423(1)(d) to have passed sentence -(B) Whether awarding of sentence by HC u/s. 423(1)(d) of CrPC subjects to want of notice u/s. 439(2) of CrPC? - Law does not prescribe that any particular formalities should be complied with before action is taken u/s. 439(2) of CrPC - It only provides that accused should have an opportunity of showing cause against conviction and enhancement and as accused was heard on both these questions requirements of section were satisfied - Order of HC could accordingly be maintained u/s. 439 - Held, under these circumstances order of HC could not be held to be bad for want of notice u/s. 439(2) - Appeal dismissed.
