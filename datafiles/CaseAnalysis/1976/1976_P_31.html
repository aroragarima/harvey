Prem Chand v District Judge, Dehradun and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Y.V. Chandrachud, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 278; (1977) 1 SCC 254; AIR 1977 SC 364; 1977 (3) ALR 15; 1977 (1) RCJ 116; 1977 (1) RCR(Rent) 471; 1977 (1) RentLR 493; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 170; 1977 UJ 20

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - United Provinces (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947 s. 3 - U.P. Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Eviction - Bonafide requirement - Appellant was tenant of respondent no.2 occupying two rooms and other two rooms were occupied by sons of respondent - Respondent was old lady and filed application u/s. 3 of 1947 for permission to bring suit against appellant for eviction on ground of bona fide personal requirement - During pendency of this application, Act of 1947 was repealed and proceedings u/s.3 of 1947 Act was converted u/s. 21 of 1972 Act, which was rejected - Respondent filed appeal before Trial court which was allowed u/s. 21(1)(iv) of 1972 Act - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether explanation u/s.21(1)(iv) of 1972 Act has been correctly held to be applicable by lower courts.

Held, there was conclusive proof that the building is, bona fide, required by the Landlord and there is no need for the landlord to establish any other requirement. Explanation (iv) provides a conclusive and irrebuttable presumption of bona fide requirement once the conditions mentioned therein are established. Therefore, Trial court was right in his finding that Explanation (iv) u/s. 21 (1) of 1972 Act was applicable which was also later upheld by the HC and was, therefore, right in dismissing the writ application. Hence, there is no merit in this appeal which is dismissed and appellant may continue in possession of the suit premises till 30-4-1977 and he shall hand over vacant and peaceful possession thereof on 1- 5- 1977, to the respondent. Appeal dismissed.
