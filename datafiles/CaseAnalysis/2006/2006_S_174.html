Shyamal Kumar Roy v Sushil Kumar Agarwal

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 877; (2006) 11 SCC 331; AIR 2007 SC 637; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 38; JT 2006 (9) SC 483; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 617; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 321; 2007 (102) RD 295; 2006(11) SCALE 159; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 47

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Marwar Stamp Act, 1947, State of West Bengal by Indian Stamp (West Bengal Amendment) Act, 1990

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 33, 35, 36, 38, 39, 40 and 61 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 151 and 2(14) - Interpretation of s. 36 of 1899 as amended in State of West Bengal in question - Parties entered into agreement to develop suit property - Suit filed by respondent for declaration and permanent injunction - Interim order of injunction was passed which was set aside by revisional Court - Respondent filed suit for specific performance of contract - Appellant filed application for sending documents to collector for impounding thereof u/s. 38 of 1899 Act - Application was rejected and thereafter revisional application was also dismissed by High Court - Whether HC was right to dismiss revisional application? - Held, s. 33 of 1899 act as amended by State of West Bengal, duty is cast upon authorities concerned including Courts to impound document where instrument produced before it is insufficiently stamped - Party objecting to admissibility of document must raise objection so as to enable trial judge to determine issue upon application - If no objection had been made by appellant herein in regard to admissibility of said document, he, at a later stage, cannot be permitted to turn round and contend that said document is inadmissible in evidence - Once document has been admitted in evidence, it is not open either to Trial Court itself or to court of appeal or revision to go behind that order - HC committed no error in dismissing revisional application - Appeal dismissed.
