Vinod Kumar Sangal v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1059; (1995) 4 SCC 246; (1995) SCC (L&S) 963; 1995 (30) ATC 262; JT 1997 (10) SC 514; 1996 LabIC 760; 1995 (2) LLN 328; 1995(3) SCALE 55; 1995 (2) SCJ 272; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 734; 1995 (3) SLJ 143; 1995 (2) SLR 695; 1995 (2) UJ 582

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Whether Departmental Promotion Committee was wrong in reverting back appellant to his original post? - Held, Departmental Promotion Committee to meet every year - Departmental Promotion Committee did not meet from 1980 to 1984 - Separate selection made for vacancies in years 1980, 1982 and 1983 appellant would have had better chances of being selected - Departmental Promotion Committee to consider appellant for each year separately and decide his promotion accordingly - Appeal disposed of.
