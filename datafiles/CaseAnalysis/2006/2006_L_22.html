Lav Nigam v Chairman and MD, ITI Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1564; (2006) 9 SCC 440; 2007 (5) SLR 263

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Departmental Proceedings, Punjab National Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1977

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Civil Services (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1979, r. 9(2) - Punjab National Bank Officer Employees' (Discipline and Appeal) Regulations, 1977, reg. 7(2) - Departmental proceedings - Dismissal - Legality - Departmental proceedings were initiated against appellant/employee - Enquiry officer filed his report - Disciplinary authority passed dismissal order as two of three charges were proved - Appellant filed writ petition before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to notice from disciplinary authority to show cause against tentative decision of disciplinary authority to differ with finding of inquiry officer - Held, conclusion of HC was contrary to consistent view taken by SC that in case disciplinary authority differed with view taken by inquiry officer, he was bound to give notice setting out his tentative conclusions to appellant - It was only after hearing appellant that disciplinary authority would at all arrive at final finding of guilt - Thereafter, employee would again have to be served with notice relating to punishment proposed - It was clear that no notice was given before disciplinary authority recorded its final conclusions differing with finding of fact of inquiry officer - Notice to show cause was merely a show-cause against proposed punishment - Decision of HC could not be sustained and was set aside - Appeal allowed.
