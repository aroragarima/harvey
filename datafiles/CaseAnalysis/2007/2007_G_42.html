C. Gupta v Glaxo Smith Klin Pharmaceutical Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 623; (2007) 7 SCC 171; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 605; 2007(6) ALL MR 422; 2007 (2) CLR 836; 2007 (114) FLR 585; JT 2007 (8) SC 480; 2007 (4) LLN 134; 2007(8) SCALE 467; 2007 (8) SCJ 856; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 800; 2007 (4) SLR 745

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Provident Funds Act, 1925, Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Termination - Single Judge held that the appellant could not be said to be a workman within the meaning of s. 2(s) - Appeal against - Held, definition of workman as amended must, therefore, presumed to be prospective - It is only in respect of a workman who is then within the definition of s. 2(s) of the Act that the employer is required to follow the condition mentioned in s. 25-N, failing which, he will commit an offence - Impugned judgment of the Division Bench do not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Appeals dismissed.
