Saiyad Mohd. Saiyad Umar Saiyed & Ors. v The State Of Gujarat.

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, A.M. Ahmadi, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 385; (1995) SCC (Cr) 564; (1995) 3 SCC 610; AIR 1995 SCW 1852; 1995 (2) CCR 146; 1995 (2) Crimes 182; 1995 CRLJ 2662; 1995 (59) ECR 187; 1995 (78) E.L.T. 649; JT 1995 (3) SC 489; 1995(2) SCALE 576; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, ss. 65, 66, 50 and 54 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 20 - Conviction of appellant confirmed by High Court - Appellants found on possession of narcotic drugs - Whether in facts and circumstances of case conviction of appellants can be based ? - It is necessary that courts dealing with offences under then NDPS Act should be very careful to see that it is established to their satisfaction that accused has been informed by concerned officer that he had a right to choose to be searched before a Gazetted Officer or a Magistrate - Held, panch witnesses does not support prosecution version - If provisions of s. 50 of Act not complied, benefit should be given to accused - Convictions set-aside - Appeal allowed.
