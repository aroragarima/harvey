Hope Plantations Limited v Taluk Land Board, Peermade and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K.T. Thomas, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 896; (1999) 5 SCC 590; JT 1998 (7) SC 404; 1999 (1) KLT 331; 1998(6) SCALE 19; 1998 (9) SLT 33; [1998] Supp2 S.C.R. 514; 1998 (8) Supreme 309

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Cardamom Act, 1965

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Cardamom Act, 1965 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, S. 2(44), 2(3), 11, 81, 82 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Ceiling Area - Exemption - Substantial Claims - Predecessor-in-title of appellant filed return before respondent/Land Board in relation to tea plantation hold by it - Out of total area part of it was sought to be surrendered and for said area appellant claimed exemption - Respondent/Land Board disallowed substantial claims - On appeal to HC, which by order restored claims made by appellant and remanded case to respondent for re-determination of ceiling area - Respondent felt aggrieved - Hence instant appeal - Whether respondent/Land Board could exercise power u/s. 85(9) of Act and order of HC, restoring claim of appellant was maintainable - Held, present Court by judgment allowed appeal and restored orders of respondent/Land Board - S.11 of Code contained provisions of res judicator, which were not exhaustive - Court, therefore uphold judgment of HC, on grounds of res judicator and estoppels and dismissed appeal u/s. 81(3) of Act - Court allowed appellant to approach State Govt. to sought exemption under Act - Appeal partly allowed.
