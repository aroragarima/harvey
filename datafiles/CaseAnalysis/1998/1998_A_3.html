A. G. Varadarajulu and Another v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2169; (1998) 4 SCC 231; AIR 1998 SC 1388; 1998 (146) CTR 117; JT 1998 (2) SC 597; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 133; 1998(2) SCALE 441; [1998] 2 S.C.R. 390; 1998 (4) SLT 10; 1998 (3) Supreme 308

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Tamil Nadu Land Reforms (Fixation of Ceiling on land) Act 1961 ss. 3(19), 3(42), 5(4), 12, 18(1), 21A - Ceiling laws - Stridhana land interpretation - Scope - There was partition deed between appellant no. 1 and his son in 1959 and another partition deed dt. 24-9-1970 was executed on basis that appellant no. 2/wife of appellant no. 1 was not allotted any property towards her right to maintenance against joint family property - Land ceiling authorities started proceedings under the Act for computing admissible ceiling area which could be allocated to appellant no. 1 and held that appellant no. 2 was not in possession of allotted land at time of commencement of the Act on 15-2-1970 as required by s. 3(42) and hence same could not be treated as 'stridhana' - Final notification was published in Gazette on 12-2-1986 u/s. 18(1) of the 1961 Act, against which revision was preferred by both appellants before Land Commissioner at Madras, which was rejected on 25-9-1986 - Against order of Land commissioner writ petition was filed in HC which was transferred to Special Appellate Tribunal, which was dismissed by judgment dt. 25-4-95 - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment dt. 25-4-95 was proper - Held, section 21A was intended to treat as valid such partitions or transfers as were mentioned in section 21A even if such partitions or transfers would otherwise had been invalid - Now s. 3(42) of the 1961 Act did not deal with invalidity of partition or transfers but dealt with stridhana land - Subject matter of enacting part of section 21A did not have any connection with subject matter of s. 3(42) of the 1961 Act - Hence it was clear that non-obstante clause in section 21A was not intended to override any thing in s. 3(42) - Thus, unless 'definitions' in land ceiling laws themselves refer to personal laws, it was not permissible to resort to personal laws while interpreting 'definitions' in land ceiling laws, it might be that for purposes of computation of ceiling area, land ceiling law might itself refer to personal laws or it might be necessary to refer to personal laws but that was different - Therefore it was not permissible for appellants to introduce principles relating to maintenance of wife or mother into interpretation of word 'stridhana land' in s. 3(42) of the 1961 Act - Further, wife or mother in Hindu joint family did not basically had share in joint family property and she had on right to maintenance and mere existence of such right against joint family property as on 15-2-1970 could not, in law, be treated as being equivalent to 'holding' share in joint family property, as on that date - Hence, fact that land was reduced to possession of appellant no. 2 on 24-9-1970, could not be equated with her possession on 15-2-1970 - Appeal dismissed.
