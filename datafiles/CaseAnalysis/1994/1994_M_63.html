High Court Of M.P. v Mahesh Prakash And Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 803; (1995) 1 SCC 203; (1995) SCC (L&S) 278; AIR 1994 SC 2595; 1994 (28) ATC 787; 1994 (69) FLR 878; JT 1994 (5) SC 620; 1994 LabIC 2471; 1995 (2) LLJ 48; 1995 (2) LLN 318; 1994(3) SCALE 968; 1994 (3) SLJ 161; 1994 (5) SLR 180; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 128; 1994 (2) UJ 742

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Administrative order - Judicial orders - Respondent no. 1 was appointed as Civil Judge, Class - II and later confirmed in said Class- II with effect from 27-7-1974 - While so, respondent no. 1 made grievance to Registrar of HC that he had not been confirmed along with his batch mates and the same had effect of causing change in their inter se seniority - However, HC rejected said grievance based on recommendation of Grievances Committee - Respondent no. 1 made second representation concealing fact about his first representation but the same was again rejected - Hence, respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC and DB of HC passed order confirming respondent no. 1 in post of Civil Judge, Class - II with effect from 5-2-1973 and quashed decisions of Full Court dt.5-2-1973, 27-7-1974, 26-4-1976 and 5-5-1985 - Hence, instant appeal by HC - Whether appeal is maintainable.

Held, order that the respondent no. 1 challenged in writ petition filed by him before HC was order passed by HC on its administrative side. By reason of art. 226 of Constitution it was permissible to move HC on its judicial side to consider validity of the order passed by HC on the administrative side and issue a writ in that behalf. In the writ petition, respondent no. 1 was obliged to implead the HC for it was the order of the HC that was under challenge. Hence, it was permissible for HC to prefer petition for special leave to appeal to instant Court against the order on the writ petition passed on its judicial side. Further, HC moved SC to support its administrative order which its DB set aside but not to support judicial order passed by DB. Hence, appeal is maintainable. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Where the promotional post is a selection post, the most that Court could do is to order constitution of a committee to consider the case of the candidate for promotion as on the date on which his immediate junior had been promoted thereto.

(B) Advocates & Judges - Delay and Laches - Maintainability of petition - Whether HC ought to have dismissed writ petition on ground of delay and laches.

Held, averment of respondent no. 1 is significantly silent about dates of his communications to HC regarding first representation and more so, second representation does not mention about first representation. Further, denial of knowledge by respondent no. 1 that his first representation had been rejected by Full Court in May 1976 is false. Hence, his approach to writ court was grossly delayed and his writ petition ought to have been dismissed by HC on the ground of delay and laches.

(C) Advocates & Judges - Confirmation of services - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, this was a case of Civil Judges up for confirmation in the class to which they had been recruited, not for confirmation in the post in which they were officiating on promotion, nor for promotion on the basis of seniority-cum-merit. Each Civil Judge up for confirmation in Class-II, being the class to which he had been recruited, was liable to be judged individually on his own record. The Full Court could not be faulted for not having confirmed respondent no. 1 on 1-2-1973, based upon his own record. Further, unless there is clear indication that there had been misinterpretation of a legal position, it is undesirable and unsafe for one Full Court to revise the decision taken by an earlier Full Court, particularly in cases of promotion, confirmation, supersession and the like. Furthermore, DB could not pass orders directing HC and State Govt. to place respondent no. 1 in Class II as of that date and also in subsequent cadres of Civil Judge Class-I, Chief Judicial Magistrate and Additional District Judge. Hence, impugned judgment and order is set aside and writ petition dismissed. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Where the promotional post is a selection post, the most that Court could do is to order constitution of a committee to consider the case of the candidate for promotion as on the date on which his immediate junior had been promoted thereto.
