Bhoop Ram v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 37; (1989) 3 SCC 1; (1989) SCC (Cr) 486; AIR 1989 SC 1329; 1990 ALJ 65; 1989 AllCriR 276; 1989 (1) AWC 562; 1989 (2) Crimes 294; 1990 CRLJ 2671; 1989 CRLR 360; 1989(1) SCALE 799

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Women & Criminal - Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1952, s. 2(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 323 and 324 - Question for consideration in this appeal is whether appellant also accused is child or not? - School certificate produced by accused showing that he was below 16 years on date of offence whereas medical certificate showed that he was above 16 on relevant date - Accused below 16 years of age on date of offence was wrongly sentenced to imprisonment instead of being sent to approved school - Court held that school certificate cannot be rejected on surmise that it is not unusual for parents to understate age of their children at time of school admission and medical certificate is based on estimate and possibility of error cannot be ruled out and since accused crossed maximum age of detention in approved school in meantime, sentence imposed on him is liable to be quashed - Appeal partly allowed.
