Bishwanath Prasad Singh v Rajendra Prasad and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 417; (2006) 4 SCC 432; AIR 2006 SC 2965; 2006 (2) AWC 1763; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 168; JT 2006 (3) SC 221; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 231; 2006 (101) RD 416; 2007 (1) RLW 290; 2006(2) SCALE 699; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 566

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Bihar Money Lenders Act, 1974, s. 12 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, ss. 58(c), 58(c), 83, 91 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91, 92 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Respondent's suit for a declaration that transaction although ostensibly expressed in shape of a deed of sale, was in fact a transaction of usufructuary mortgage and for a further declaration that said transaction stands redeemed u/s. 12 - Relief granted in second appeal - Held, a mortgage by conditional sale must be evidenced by one document whereas a sale with a condition of re-transfer may be evidenced by more than one document - A sale with a condition of retransfer, is neither mortgage nor a partial transfer - By reason of such a transfer all rights have been transferred reserving only a personal right to purchaser, and such a personal right would be lost, unless same is exercised within stipulated time - Transfer is complete and not partial, no stipulation has been made that appellant cannot transfer property - Appeal allowed.
