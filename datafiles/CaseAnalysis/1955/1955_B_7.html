Â Bhikaji Narain Dhakras And Others v The State Of Madhya Pradesh And Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 31; AIR 1955 SC 781; 1956 (1) MLJ 37; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 37; 1956 SCJ 48; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Amending Act, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 13, 19(6), 19(1)(g) and 31 - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 - (A) What effect did art. 19 (6) as amended have on impugned Central Provinces and Berar Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1947? - Held, impugned Act was an existing law at time when Constitution came into force - That existing law imposed on exercise of right guaranteed to citizens of India by art. 19 (1)(g) restrictions which could not be justified as reasonable u/cl. (6) as it then stood and consequently, u/art. 13(1) that existing law became void 'to extent of such inconsistency' - Effect of Constitution (First Amendment) Act 1951 was to remove shadow and to make impugned Act free from all blemish or infirmity - If that were not so, then it is not intelligible what 'existing law' could have been sought to be saved from operation of art. 19 (1) (g) by amended cl. (6) in so far, as it sanctioned creation of state monopoly, for, ex hypothesis, all existing laws creating such monopoly had already become void at date of commencement of Constitution in view of cl. (6) as it then stood - It is true that as amended cl. (6) was not made retrospective impugned Act could have no operation as against citizens between 26-01-1950 and 18-06-1951 and no rights and obligations could be founded on provisions of impugned Act during said period, whereas, amended cl. (2) by reason of its being expressly made retrospective had effect even during that period - Notification declaring intention of State to take over bus routes to exclusion of all other motor transport operators was published on 04-02-1955 when it was perfectly constitutional for State to do so - (B) Whether impugned Act conflicts with provisions of art. 31 (2) of Constitution? - Held, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 came into force on 27-04-1955, by which art. 31-A was also amended - There can be no question that amended provisions, if they apply, save impugned law, for it does not provide for transfer of ownership or right to possession of any property and cannot therefore, be deemed to provide for compulsory acquisition or requisitioning of any property - It is quite true that impugned Act became inconsistent with art. 31 as soon as Constitution came into force on 26-01-1950 and continued to be so inconsistent right up to 27-05-1955 and therefore, u/art. 13(1) became void 'to extent of such inconsistency' - Nevertheless that inconsistency was removed on and from 27-04-1955 by Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 - Present writ petitions were filed on 27-05-1955, exactly a month after Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955 came into force, and, on a parity of reasoning, petitioners cannot be permitted to challenge constitutionality of impugned Act on and from 27-05-1955 and this objection also cannot prevail - Petitions dismissed.
