Harinagar Sugar Mills Limited v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1060; (2003) 11 SCC 40; AIR 2004 SC 682; 2004 (176) E.L.T. 48; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 88; 2003(9) SCALE 823; 2003 (1) SCW 6324; 2003 (7) SLT 264; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 794; 2004 (1) Supreme 460

Case Digest

Subject: Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Agricultural Produce Marketing, Bihar Agricultural Produce Market Act, 1960

Summary: Bihar Agricultural Produce Market Act, 1960 - Transactions of purchase of sugarcane and sale of sugar and molasses subject to payment of market fee - Whether the amount deposited in view of s. 27-B of the Act is deposit of the liability of dues of fee assessed or not - Held, there is no scope to treat the amount deposited as anything else except part of the fee assessed and due - Further held, merely because the amount deposited may have to be refunded in case the appeal succeeds, that alone does not mean that the nature of the deposit is changed or it is anything else except the amount of levy assessed and due, particularly looking to the language used and provision made under s. 27-B - Further, the amount which was in fact not liable to be paid but a part of it is being retained in pursuance of the arrangement made in exercise of powers under art. 142 of the Constitution and the remaining part which is not recoverable, no penalty is liable to be recovered and retained.
