Â S. P. Mittal etc. v Union Of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.B. Misra, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 188; (1983) 1 SCC 51; (1983) SCC (L&S) 61; AIR 1983 SC 1; 1982(2) SCALE 1001; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 729

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Morality, Universal Declaration of Human Rights, Religious Denomination, Constitutionality, Descent, Kerala University Act, 1969, Auroville (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1980

Summary: Constitution - Trusts & Associations - Auroville Act, 1980 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Kerala University Act, 1969 - Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951 - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - West Bengal Societies Registration Act, 1961 - Universal Declaration of Human Rights - Mismanagement - Dissension - Violation - Promulgation - 'M'/disciples and follower of S/Shri Aurobindo Society as mother - 'M' died and management of 'S' was taken by Govt. by promulgation of 1980 ordinance - There was dissension among petitioners/members of 'S', that 1980 Act should necessitated - Petitioners contended that 1980 Act was in violation of art.14 of Constitution - Petitioners filed petitions contended 1980 Act offend rights guaranteed by arts. 25 and 26 of Constitution was dismissed by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether enactment of 1980 Act was in violation of art.14 of Constitution - Held, after death of 'M', affairs of Society were not properly managed, and there was mismanagement of funds of Society and diversion of funds meant for Auroville to other purposes - Committee was appointed by Govt. and irregularities in management of Society, misutilization of funds, and diversion was confirmed - Govt. takeover management of Auroville project for limited period by legislation u/art. 31-A (1) (b) of Constitution by promulgation of 1980 ordinance - Hence, 1980 Act was not in violation of art. 14 of Constitution - Enactment was passed with procedure and it could not be said that enactment was passed at his behest - Petitions disposed of.
