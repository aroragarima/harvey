Â Sakal Papers Private Limited, and Others v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 429; AIR 1962 SC 305; 1962 (2) SCJ 400; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 842

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Freedom Of Press, Constitutionality, Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 and 19(1)(a) - Newspaper (Price and Page) Act, 1956, s. 3 - Daily Newspaper (Price and Page) Order, 1960 - Freedom of press - Constitutionality of 1956 Act - Whether Newspaper Act and Daily Newspaper Order amounts to abridgment of right to freedom of speech and expression guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(a)? - The law in question was made upon recommendation of Press Commission but since its object is to affect directly right of circulation of newspapers which would necessarily undermine their power to influence public opinion, it cannot but be regarded as a dangerous weapon which is capable of being used against democracy itself - In these circumstances, Act and Order cannot be sustained upon ground that it merely implements a recommendation of Press Commission and was thus not made with an ulterior object - Similarly, since Act taken in conjunction with order made thereunder operates as a restraint on freedom of speech and expression of newspapers, mere fact that its object was to suppress unfair practices by newspapers would not validate them - Carrying on unfair practices may be a matter for placing restrictions on right of circulation - Legitimacy of result intended to be achieved does not necessarily imply that every means to achieve it is permissible; for even if end is desirable and permissible, means employed must not transgress limits laid down by Constitution, if they directly impinge on any of fundamental rights guaranteed by Constitution it is no answer when constitutionality of measure is challenged apart from fundamental right infringed provision is otherwise legal - Such an object may be desirable but for attaining it, State cannot make inroads on right of other newspapers which art. 19(1)(a) guarantees to them - Held, therefore, s. 3(1) of Act, which is its pivotal provision is unconstitutional and, therefore, Daily newspaper (Price, Page) Order, 1960 made thereunder is also unconstitutional - Petition allowed.
