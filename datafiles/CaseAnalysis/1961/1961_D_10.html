Raja Harish Chandra Raj Singh v Deputy Land Acquisition Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 482; AIR 1961 SC 1500; 1961 ALJ 650; 1966 CRLJ 129; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 10; 1962 (1) SCJ 696; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 676

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5-A, 6, 9, 12 and 18 - Whether application filed by appellant on 24-02-1953 u/s. 18 of Act was barred by time when award was signed and delivered in his office by respondent 1 on 24-02-1953? - Proviso to s. 18 deals with question of limitation and appellant's case falls u/cl.(b) of proviso; 'the date of award' occurring in relevant section cannot be determined solely by reference to time when award is signed by Collector or delivered by him in his office but it must involve consideration of question as to when it was known to party concerned either actually or constructively; if award is treated as an administrative decision taken by Collector, it is clear that said decision ultimately affects rights of owner of property and in that sense, like all decisions which affect persons, it is essentially fair and just that said decision should be communicated to said party, thus considered, making of award cannot consist merely in physical act of writing award or signing it or even filing it in office of Collector, it must involve communication of said award to party concerned either actually or constructively; knowledge of party affected by award, either actual or constructive, being an essential requirement of fairplay and natural justice expression 'the date of award' used in proviso must mean date when award is either communicated to party or is known by him either actually or constructively - Held, where rights of a person are affected by any order and limitation is prescribed for enforcement of remedy by person aggrieved against said order by reference to making of said order, making of order must mean either actual or constructive communication of said order to party concerned, hence, High Court was in error in coming to conclusion that appellant's application was barred under proviso to s. 18 - Appeal allowed.
