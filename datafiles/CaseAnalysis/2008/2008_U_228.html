K. Janardhan v United India Insurance Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 895; (2008) 8 SCC 518; 2008 (3) ACC 44; 2008 (4) ACC 734; AIR 2008 SC 2384; 2008 (3) AWC 2469; 2008 (118) FLR 1185; 2008 (2) KLT 995; 2008 (2) LLJ 960; 2008 (3) LLN 472; 2008 (6) MLJ(SC) 147; 2008 (151) PLR 757; 2008(8) SCALE 240; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923, s. 2(1)(e); Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Whether the loss of claimant- appellant's right leg ipso facto meant a total disablement as understood in terms of s. 2(1)(e) of the Workmen's Compensation Act and whether he was entitled to have his compensation computed on that basis? - Held, appellant has suffered a 100% disability and incapacity in earning his keep as a tanker driver as his right leg had been amputated from the knee. additionally, a perusal of ss. 8 and 9 of MV Act, 1988 would show that the appellant would now be disqualified from even getting a driving licence - Appeal allowed.
