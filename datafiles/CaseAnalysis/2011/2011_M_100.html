Shipping Corporation Of India Ltd v Mare Shipping Inc

Supreme Court of India

13 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 521; (2011) 8 SCC 39; AIR 2011 SC 2608; 2011 (5) AWC 5196; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 73, 74 - Entitlement - Validity - Demurrage claim - Petitioners and Respondents entered into Charter Party in respect of respondents' vessel for carriage of tonnes of crude oil - Cl. 9 of Charter Party provided for settlement of all disputes arose out of Charter Party by arbitration under the 1996 Act, and Maritime Arbitration Rules of Indian Council of Arbitration - Vessel was delayed - Claim was disputed, arbitration was invoked by parties under provisions of the 1996 Act - Arbitrators made and published their Award by which they allowed respondents' demurrage claim - Award was challenged by Petitioners/Charterers in HC on ground that respondents had not proved that Notice of Readiness had been tendered and consequently respondents were not entitled to demurrage for period that vessel was detained - HC allowed remitted matter to Arbitration for proper finding - After remand, Arbitrators passed another Award admitting fresh evidence holding that service of Notice of Readiness by Master of vessel on Agents of Petitioners was proved in view of evidence of Petitioners' witness that asked Captain for copy of Notice of Readiness sent by Master to Petitioners Agents - Tribunal held that respondents/owners were entitled to receive demurrage with interest and costs, as awarded in earlier Award - Arbitration Petition was dismissed by single Judge which was upheld by HC - Hence, instant special leave petition - Whether Respondents/Owners of vessel were entitled to damages or demurrage - Held, it was agreed that demurrage would be settled as per terms of Charter Party - R/w cl. s. 6 of Charter Party, Addendum made clear that Charterers had accepted responsibility for failure of vessel to discharge cargo and had agreed to bear all expenses for delay in diversion of vessel that included time spent at port and expenses incurred towards pilotage, tugs and other port expenses - Cl. 4(1) of Part II of Charter Party specifically provided that extra expenses incurred on account of any change in loading or discharging ports, had to be paid by Charterers, and any time lost to vessel should count as used lay time - It was not accepted that clause had to be read in context of Cls. 4(a) and 4(b) which referred to ports other than Indian Ports in different context - Decisions relied upon by parties laid down certain propositions of law which were well-established and with which there should not be any disagreement, but for purposes of instant case they were basically academic - Therefore, there was no reason to interfere with Award of Arbitral Tribunal and decisions, of Single Judge and DB, confirmed Award of Arbitral Tribunal - Petition dismissed.
