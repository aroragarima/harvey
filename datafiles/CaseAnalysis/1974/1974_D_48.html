State of Bihar and Another v Dr. Asis Kumar Mukherjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.K. Goswami, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 56; (1975) SCC (L&S) 51; (1975) 3 SCC 602; AIR 1975 SC 192; 1975 LabIC 141; 1975 (1) LLJ 198; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 894; 1975 SLJ 47; 1975 UJ 42

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical Council Act 1956

Summary: Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Medical Council Act, 1956, s. 33 - (A) Power of High Court - To call for record - Call for sensitive materials in possession of Govt. - (B) Regulations u/s. 33 - Teaching experience in a teaching institution - Meaning of Experience as Registrar in British Hospitals - Power of Court, though wide, will have to be exercised judicially and judiciously, having regard to totality of circumstances, including impropriety of every disgruntled party getting an opportunity to pry into files of Govt. - Held it was duty of Govt. to be satisfied on reasonable materials that posts of Registrar in which he worked for 3 years involved teaching functions, question being looked at fairly, not by semantic hair splitting and quibbling on words like 'participating' in teaching - Appeal dismissed.
