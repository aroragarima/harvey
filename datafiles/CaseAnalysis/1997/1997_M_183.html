State of Orissa v Messrs Orissa Road Transport Company Limited, Chief Accounts Officer

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S.C. Sen, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 593; (1997) 7 SCC 104; AIR 1997 SC 3409; JT 1997 (7) SC 545; 1997(5) SCALE 589; 1997 (107) STC 204; [1997] Supp3 S.C.R. 592; 1997 (8) Supreme 48

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Casual Dealer

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Orissa Sales Tax, 1947 - ss. 2 (bb), 2(c), 12(5) - Transport business - Disposal of unserviceable parts - Registration as dealer - Assessment order - Levy of penalty - Challenge - Respondent was engaged in transport business by running buses - Along with these services, respondent had been disposing of unserviceable parts from its stores at yearly intervals - Respondent did not get registered as dealer under said the Act as he thought no business was being carried on in respect of which any sales tax could be levied - Sales Tax Officer considered that respondent was liable to pay tax and made order of assessment u/s. 12(5) of the 1947 Act seeking to tax respondent's turn-over on sale of unserviceable old parts - Assessing authority also levied penalty on respondent for failing to get registered itself as dealer under 1947 Act - Respondents appeal was dismissed by Assistant Commissioner - On further appeal, Tribunal allowed appeal and held that respondent could not be regarded as dealer within scope of 1947 Act for purpose of levying tax - Tribunal referred question of law to HC which answered question in favour of respondent - Hence instant appeals - Whether respondent was liable to pay sales tax and should be registered as dealer under relevant provisions of 1947 Act -

Held, as prudent business organization, respondent sold some obsolete and spare parts which were intended for use in its business as it did not require them - It would be difficult to accept that such sales should not even be regarded as occasional sales of business nature which would make respondent casual dealer within meaning of that expression occurring in s. 2[bb] of the 1947 Act - Definition of business had eliminated profit making element and keeping this in view this Court held that activity of appellant in selling unserviceable material and scrap would be business within cl. 1 of definition of word 'business' introduced by amending Act - SC held, even if it be assumed that activity involved in selling unserviceable material and scrap-iron might not amount to carrying on business in normal connotation of that term, it would still be business within meaning of expression 'business' occurring in the 1947 Act - Such sales were, therefore, to be held to eligible to sales tax - Therefore, respondent would have to be regarded as dealer carrying on business of selling spare parts and thereby become liable to pay sales tax on sale of such items - State of Tamil Nadu v. Burmah Shell Oil Storage & Distributing Co. of India Ltd. & Anr., 1972 Indlaw SC 205, relied on - Appeals allowed.
