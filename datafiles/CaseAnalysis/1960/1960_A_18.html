Associated Electrical Industries (India) Private, Limited, Calcutta v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 493; AIR 1967 SC 284; 1961 (2) LLJ 122

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33(B)(1) - Whether proceedings followed in this case is valid ? - Though appropriate Govt. was competent to transfer proceedings it could exercise its power only after complying with requirements of s. 33B, and one of requirements of said section is that before making order of transfer appropriate Govt. must record reasons for same - In circumstances of this case any reasons have not been stated as required by section, and so orders of transfer cannot be held to be justified u/s. 33B(1) - Held, objection raised by appellant as to validity of present proceedings must be affirmed and award passed by fourth industrial tribunal must be set aside - Appeal allowed.
