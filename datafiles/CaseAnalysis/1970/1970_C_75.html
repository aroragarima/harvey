Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab v Kulu Valley Transport Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 73; (1970) 2 SCC 192; AIR 1970 SC 1734; 1973 CTR 177; [1970] 77 ITR 518; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 452

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 22(1) and 24 - A/y 1953-54 and 1954-55 - Return of Income - Assessee did not file returns of income in respect of the a/y 1953-54 and 1954-55 within the period specified in the general notice u/s. 22(1) of the IT Act, 1922 - In January 1956 the assessee filed voluntary returns disclosing loss of income in the course of its business amounting to Rs. 1,51,520/- and Rs. 48,977 respectively for the two years in question - ITO refused to determine the loss on the ground that this is a loss case and the return has been filed after the statutory time, hence, assessee was not entitled to the benefit of carry forward of loss in the subsequent assessments - Asst. Commissioner dismissed appeal filed against said order - On appeal, Tribunal held that a voluntary return disclosing loss of income submitted after the expiry of the period for filing a return under sub-s. (1) of s. 22 of IT Act may be deemed to be a return under sub-s. (3) of s. 22 of IT Act, and the loss disclosed therein must be determined under sub-s. (2) of s. 24 of IT Act to qualify the assessee to carry it in the following year - HC held that a voluntary return filed after the expiry of the period specified in sub-s. (1) of s. 22 of IT Act but before the assessment is made must still be entertained as a return filed under sub-s.(3) of s. 24 of IT Act, even if it returns a loss of income under the head 'Profits and gains of business, profession or vocation' - Hence, present appeal - Whether the losses of Rs. 1,51,520/- and of Rs. 48,977/- returned by the assessee in January 1956 for the a/ys 1953-54 and 1954-55 respectively require in law to be determined and carried forward u/s. 24(2) of the IT Act? - Held, sub-s. (3) of s. 22 of IT Act applies to all returns whether disclosing profit or loss, and whether made voluntarily or pursuant to a notice under sub-s. (2) of s. 22 of IT Act, and on that account even if the return is filed beyond the period prescribed by s. 22(1) of IT Act, and discloses a loss the Income-tax Officer was bound to determine the loss so that it may be carried forward in the following year - A return whether it is a return of income, profits or gains or of loss must be considered as having been made within the time prescribed if it is made within the time specified in s. 22(3) of IT Act - Moreover, it cannot be overlooked that even if two views are possible the view which is favorable to the assessee must be accepted while construing the provisions of a. taxing statute - Impugned order of HC upheld - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
