Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others v M. A. Kareem and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 442; (1991) Supp2 SCC 183; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1206; 1991 (17) ATC 303; 1990(2) SCALE 493; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 482; 1991 (2) SLJ 14; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 482; 1991 (1) UJ 210

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: AP Ministerial Service Rules

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Ministerial Service Rules, r. 16 - Whether petitioners can claim consideration of their services in district police offices for purpose of seniority even though a memorandum preventing same is in existence? - Held, petition before Tribunal was filed by respondents after a period 13 years of their initial appointment in Chief Office, during which period many orders consistent with terms of service (preventing inclusion of service in district police) office must have been passed in favour of other incumbents of service - Respondent officers had joined office of Inspector General of Police after qualifying at general examination held for purpose, and since petitioners did not appear at examination, they cannot be equated with respondent officers - Petition dismissed.
