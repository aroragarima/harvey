Krishan Ballabh Prasad Singh v Sub-Divisional Officer Hilsa-Cumreturning Officer

Supreme Court of India

12 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 299; (1985) 4 SCC 194; AIR 1985 SC 1746; 1985(2) SCALE 1402; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 532; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 532; 1985 UJ 1067

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 66 and 169 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 66, form 22 and r. 64(a), form 21C - Constitution, arts. 329 (b) and 226 - Maintainability - in this petition for special leave against the majority judgment of the High Court, the only question is whether the bar enacted in cls.(b) of art.329 operates against the writ petition - Held, process of election came to an end after the declaration in Form 21C was made and the consequential formalities were completed - Bar of cls.(b) of art. 329 came into operation thereafter and an election petition alone was maintainable - Petition fails.
