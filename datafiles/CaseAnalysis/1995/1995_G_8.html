G.C. Kanungo v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 302; (1995) 5 SCC 96; AIR 1995 SC 1655; 1995 (2) ARBLR 277; JT 1995 (4) SC 589; 1995(3) SCALE 658; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 510

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Orissa Superior Judicial Service (Senior Branch), Arbitration (Orissa Second Amendment) Act, 1991, Arbitration (Orissa Amendment) Act, 1982

Summary: Arbitration & Adr - Constitution, arts. 50, Pre., 226, 245 and 254 - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 8, 14, 17 and 41A (Orissa) - CPC, 1908, s. 100 - Constitutionality of Arbitration (Orissa Second Amendment) Act, 1992 challenged on grounds of : (A) Legislative competence - When Central Act on subject already in existence and attribution of mala fides or ulterior motive to legislature - (B) Transgression of judicial power of State vested in Courts - Amendment nullifying judgements and decrees of Courts by which awards of Special Arbitration Tribunals are made 'Rule of Court' - (C) Ammended s. 41A nullifing the Awards made by SAT in exercise of State's judicial power - Held, State can still legislate on the subject but assent of President to be obtained - No transgression - Unconstitutional - Petition allowed.
