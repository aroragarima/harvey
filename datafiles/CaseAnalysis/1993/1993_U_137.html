Â R.C.Poundyal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, K. Jayachandra Reddy, S.C. Agrawal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1362; (1994) Supp1 SCC 324; AIR 1993 SC 1804; JT 1993 (2) SC 1; 1993(1) SCALE 489; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 891

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Electorate, Scheduled Caste, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Constitution (Thirty-Fifth Amendment) Act, 1974, Election Laws (Extension to Sikkim) Act, 1976

Summary: Constitution of India, Arts.2 and 371F(f) (Amendment 1975); Representation of the People Act, 1951, s.5A(2) - Political questions doctrine - Accession of border State into Union of India - Reservation of seats for 'Bhutias-Lepchas' and 'Sangha' in Sikkim Legislative Assembly - Constitutionality - Held, power of Parliament - To provide terms and conditions for admission of a new State under art.2 - Is subject to judicial review - Bhutias-Lepchas are tribes like other tribes - And would necessarily be governed by art.332(2) - 'Sangha' is not merely a religious institution but historically a political and social institution - Not unconstitutional.
