R. Kapilnath (Dead) through Lr. v Krishna

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1494; (2003) 1 SCC 444; AIR 2003 SC 565; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 27; JT 2002 (10) SC 271; 2003 (1) RCJ 36; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 82; 2002(9) SCALE 296; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 66; 2003 (1) Supreme 69

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Karnataka Rent Control (Amendment) Act, 1994

Summary: Rent Control - Practice and Procedure - Karnataka Rent Control Act, 1961, s.21(1)(h), 21(1)(p) - Decree for eviction granted by Court of Munsif - Upheld by the Additional District Judge and HC - (A) Challenge to the proceedings held before the Munsif for want of jurisdiction in view of an amendment made in the Act by Karnataka Act No 32 of 1994 - Held, a new law bringing about a change in forum does not affect pending actions, unless a provision is made in it for change over of proceedings or there is some other clear indication that pending actions are affected - (B) Challenge to the claim of respondent to be the owner of the premises who is a mere rent collector on behalf of the temple - Held, it cannot be doubted nor has it been disputed that the respondent is 'landlord' within the meaning of s.3(h) Though the appellant claimed himself to be an owner also so long as he has been found to be a landlord he is entitled to maintain the action for eviction u/s.21(1)(p) - Further, held, the plaintiff or petitioner may claim a higher right and may succeed in proving only a smaller right or entitlement to relief but that would not result in disentitling the plaintiff or petitioner from succeeding so long as the smaller right successfully substantiated by him is enough in law to entitle him to a relief against the defendant - Appeal dismissed.
