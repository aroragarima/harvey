Nahalchand Laloochand Private Limited v Panchali Co-operative Housing Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 725; (2010) 9 SCC 536; AIR 2010 SC 3607; 2010(6) ALL MR 430; 2010 (6) Bom.C.R. 74; 2012 (3) CPJ(Sc) 18; 2010 (5) CTC 678; JT 2010 (9) SC 414; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 316; 2010(8) SCALE 710; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 804

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulations of the Promotion of Construction, etc.) Rules, 1964, Maharashtra Apartment Ownership Act, 1970, Bombay Municipal Corporation Act

Summary: Land & Property - Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulation of the Promotion of Construction, Sale, Management and Transfer) Act, 1963 - Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulations of the Promotion of Construction, Etc.) Rules, 1964 - Development Control Regulations for Greater Bombay, 1991 - Maharashtra Apartment Ownership Act, 1970 - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966 - Parking spaces in the stilt portion - Promoter developed few properties and entered into agreements for sale of flats with flat purchasers - Flat purchasers were members of Co-operative Housing Society Ltd. - Promoter filed a suit for permanent injunction restraining the Society (defendant) from encroaching upon, trespassing and/or in any manner interfering with its possession in respect of 25 parking spaces in the stilt portion of the building - Defendant (Society) contended that the promoter has no right to sell or dispose off spaces in the stilt portion - Suit was dismissed - First appeal filed before HC was also dismissed - Present appeal - (A) Whether stand alone 'garage' or 'garage' as an independent unit by itself is a 'flat' within the meaning of s. 2(a-1) of Act, 1963 ? - Held, phrase, 'and includes a garage' occurring on s. 2(a-1) in the bracket does not bring in 'garage' by itself within the meaning of word 'flat' - If stand alone 'garage' (or a garage by itself) were intended by the legislature to be a 'flat' within the meaning of s. 2(a-1), that could have been conveniently conveyed by use of the expression 'or garage' after the word 'business' as preceding uses - Bracketed phrase is rather indicative of the legislative intention to include a 'garage' as appurtenant or attachment to a flat which satisfies the ingredients of s. 2(a-1) - Hence, stand alone 'garage' or in other words 'garage' as an independent unit by itself is not a 'flat' within the meaning of s. 2(a-1) - (B) Whether stilt parking space/open parking space of a building regulated by Act, 1963 is a 'garage'? - Held, a stilt area may be usable as a parking space but for the purposes of Act, 1963 , such portion could not be treated as garage - Hence, open to the sky parking area or stilted portion usable as parking space is not 'garage' within the meaning of s. 2(a-1) and, therefore, not sellable independently as a flat or along with a flat - (C) Whether stilt parking space/open parking space in such building is part of 'common areas and facilities'? - Held, looking to the scheme and object of Act, 1963, and there being no indication to the contrary, no justifiable reason to exclude parking areas (open to the sky or stilted portion) from the purview of 'common areas and facilities' under Act, 1963 - Hence, stilt parking space/open parking space of a building regulated by ACT, 1963 is nothing but a part of 'common areas' - (D) What are the rights of the promoter vis-'-vis society (of flat purchasers) in respect of open parking space/s/ stilt parking space/s? - Held, stilt parking space/s being part of 'common areas' of the building developed by the promoter, the only right that the promoter has, is to charge the cost thereof in proportion to the carpet area of the flat from each flat purchaser - Such stilt parking space being neither 'flat' u/s. 2(a-1) nor 'garage' within the meaning of that provision is not sellable at all - Further, promoter has no right to sell any portion of such building which is not 'flat' within the meaning of s. 2(a-1) and the entire land and building has to be conveyed to the organisation; the only right remains with the promoter is to sell unsold flats - Appeals dismissed.
