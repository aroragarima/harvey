Subhash Chander v Krishan Lal and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20876; (2001) 4 SCC 458; (2001) SCC (Cr) 735; AIR 2001 SC 1903; AIR 2001 SCW 1492; 2001 (3) Crimes 196; 2002 (1) CRJ 588; 2001 CRLJ 1825; 2001 (1) JCC 238; JT 2001 (4) SC 444; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 400; 2001(3) SCALE 130; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 864; 2001 (3) SLT 386; 2001 (3) Supreme 268; 2001 (2) UC 50B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1963, ss. 161, 313 and 401 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 57, 120-B, 148, 149, 302, 307 and 450 - Indian Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Design to kill whole of family - (A) Whether mere fact that some of accused persons were acquitted could be made a ground for converting death sentence into life imprisonment? - Held, no, if a case which could be termed as rarest of rare case, extreme penalty is warranted - Manner in which crime was committed should be looked into to justify grant of death sentence - (B) Whether death sentence can be converted to other penalty when two views are possible about quantum of sentence? - Further held, yes - A view which favours grant of life in comparison of death is generally accepted - (C) Whether transportation for life means twenty years imprisonment only? - Further held, unless life imprisonment is commuted or remitted by appropriate authority under relevant provisions of law applicable in case, a prisoner sentenced to life imprisonment is bound in law to serve life term in prison - Law does not say that transportation for life shall be deemed to be transportation for twenty years for all purposes - A sentence of transportation for life must prima facie be treated as transportation or imprisonment for whole of remaining period of convicted person's natural life - Appeals dismissed.
