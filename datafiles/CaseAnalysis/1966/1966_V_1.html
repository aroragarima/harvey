M. P. Shreevastava v Mrs. Veena

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 73; AIR 1967 SC 1193; 1967 ALJ 423; 1967 BLJR 487; 1967 CurLJ 19; 1967 (3) DLT 41; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Decree For Restitution Of Conjugal Rights

Summary: Civil Procedure - Municipalities and Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 47 and 224 - Appellant filed for a decree for restitution of conjugal rights against respondent who deserted appellant - Petition decreed ex-parte - Respondent returned to residence of appellant and offered to live with him - Appellant did not let respondent back to house - Respondent applied to District Court for order that decree be recorded as satisfied as appellant did not allow respondent to resume conjugal rights - District Court held that decree for restitution of conjugal rights against respondent stood satisfied - On appeal High Court confirmed order - Whether applications by judgment-debtors raising questions relating to execution, discharge or satisfaction not failing within o. 21, r. 2 was maintainable or not? - S. 244 only covers cases of proceedings initiated by decree-holder and does not include applications relating to execution, discharge or satisfaction of decree made by judgment-debtor - Held, no antithesis between s. 47 and o. 21, r. 2 as former deals with power of court and latter with procedure to be followed in respect of a limited class of cases relating to discharge or satisfaction of decrees - Appeal dismissed.
