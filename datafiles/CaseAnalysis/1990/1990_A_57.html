Assam Sillimanite Limited and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 748; (1990) 3 SCC 182; AIR 1990 SC 1417; JT 1990 (2) SC 248; 1990(1) SCALE 545; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 983

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Mines And Minerals (Regulation And Development) Act, 1957, ss. 4-A and 4-A(1) - Mining leases - Premature Termination of the mining leases - (A) Whether Govt. order not fulfill the requirements specified in s. 4-A justifying the premature termination of leases in pursuance thereof? - Court held, yes, provisions of s. 4-A gives no suggestion to deny the right of the affected persons to be heard and that the section must be interpreted to imply that the persons who may be affected by such a decision should be afforded an opportunity to prove that the proposed step would not advance the interest of mines and mineral development - It would be violative of the principles of natural justice, therefore lessee-respondents were entitled to be heard before a decision to prematurely terminate their leases was taken - (B) Whether petitioners have suffered any damages as a result of the premature termination of the three leases in their favor either in the shape of loss of profits for the unexpired periods of the leases or in any other material respect? - Held, yes, but compensation/damages should be restricted to a period of five years from the date of termination of the leases or up to the date of expiry of the original lease deeds referred to above whichever is less and not for the entire unexpired period of all the leases and referred the issue to arbitration - Petition disposed of.
