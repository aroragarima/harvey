State of Andhra Pradesh and Others v Chitra Venkata Rao

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 234; (1975) SCC (L&S) 349; (1975) 2 SCC 557; AIR 1975 SC 2151; 1976 (32) FLR 44; 1975 LabIC 1585; 1976 (1) LLJ 21; 1976 (1) LLN 45; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 521; 1975 SLJ 772; 1976 (1) SLR 653; 1975 UJ 774

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Act, 1960

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Disciplinary Proceedings Tribunal) Act, 1960, s. 5 - Departmental inquires - Jurisdiction of High Court - Nature and extent - (B) Domestic enquiry before Tribunal - Held, HC is not a court of appeal u/art. 226, not competent to declare tribunals order holding departmental inquiry invalid, it can only determine competency of authority and procedure, observance of natural justice - More over not to review evidence and arriving at independent finding as it has to deal with error of law only as adequacy or sufficiency of evidence are within exclusive jurisdiction of Tribunal - Not same as prosecution in a criminal case - Appeal allowed.
