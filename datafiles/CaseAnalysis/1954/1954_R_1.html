Â R. M. Seshadri v District Magistrate, Tanjore, and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Vivian Bose, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 17; AIR 1954 SC 747; 1954 (2) MLJ(SC) 717; 1954 SCJ 842; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 686

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Cinematograph Act, 1918, Madras Cinematograph Rules, 1933

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g) and 226 - Cinematograph Act, 1918, s. 8 - Whether condition 4(a) and special condition 3 imposed on licensee(for running a permanent theatre) in pursuance of notifications are unreasonable and violate Constitution? - Held, impugned conditions did not amount to reasonable restrictions within meaning of art. 19(6) - They were therefore void - Had no legal effect on fundamental right under art. 19(1)(g) - Appeal allowed.
