Gulam Hussain Shaikh Chougule v S. Reynolds, Supdt. of Customs, Marmgoa

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 423; (2002) SCC (Cr) 116; (2002) 1 SCC 155; AIR 2001 SC 2930; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 590; 2001 (4) CCR 211; 2002 (1) CRJ 69; 2001 CRLJ 4755; 2002 (79) ECC 228; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 3; 2001 (2) JCC 307; JT 2001 (9) SC 89; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 581; 2001 (47) RLT 377; 2001(7) SCALE 409; 2001 (7) SLT 466; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 476; 2001 (7) Supreme 846; 2001 (2) UC 701

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Code of Civil Procedure, s. 164 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108 and 135 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 25 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 and 228 - Whether confessional statements recorded u/s. 108 Customs Act admissible? - High Court declined to accept contention of appellant that confessional statement was made under inducement and threat and physical assault - Held, 1992 Indlaw SC 474 relied on - Statement recorded by customs officers u/s. 108 of Customs Act is admissible in evidence - Court has to test whether inculpating portions were made voluntarily or whether it is vitiated on account of any of premises envisaged in s. 24 of Evidence Act - Further held, HC rightly rejected contentions raised on behalf of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
