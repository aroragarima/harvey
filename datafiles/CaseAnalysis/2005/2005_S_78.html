State of N.C.T. of Delhi and another v Sanjeev @ Bittoo

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 257; (2005) 5 SCC 181; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1025; AIR 2005 SC 2080; 2005 CRLJ 2179; 2005 (118) DLT 710; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 228; JT 2005 (4) SC 109; 2005(3) SCALE 626; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 151

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Delhi Police Act, 1978, ss.47, 50, 51, 52 - IPC,1860, ss. 290, 498A- 489E - Scope, ambit of s. 51 - Externment - Held, order directing externment should show existence of some material warranting an order of externment - Court will be slow to interfere in such matters relating to administrative functions unless decision is tainted by any vulnerability enumerated above; like illegality, irrationality and procedural impropriety - Whether action falls within any of categories has to be established - Mere assertion in that regard would not be sufficient - Relevant aspects were considered and HC was not justified in holding to contrary - Appeal disposed of. [B.] Practice & Procedure - Bihar Civil Court Staff (Class-II and Class-IV) Rules, 1992, r.7 - Notices should be placed on the notice board of the respective Civil Court premises and in the local daily newspaper of the District - Directive was binding on the District Judge - Instructions of the High Court were disregarded with impunity and a procedure evolved for appointment which cannot be said to be in any way fair or above board - Appeals dismissed.
