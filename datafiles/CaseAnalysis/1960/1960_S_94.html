Â State of Uttar Pradesh v Deoman Upadhyaya

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 462; AIR 1960 SC 1125; 1960 CRLJ 1504; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 90; 1961 (2) SCJ 334; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 14

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Intentionally causing death - Constitutional validity - Conviction - Respondent was tried for murder of a person before Court of Session and convicted on consideration of evidence led by prosecution - Reference was made to HC by Court of Session for confirmation of sentence - Appeal was filed before HC by respondent - HC held that evidence was insufficient to prove guilt beyond reasonable doubt and acquitted respondent for offence of murder - At instance of State, HC granted a certificate that having regard to the general importance of the question as to constitutional validity of s. 27 of the 1872 Act, case was fit for appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was correct in deciding that circumstances were insufficient to prove the guilt beyond reasonable doubt of offender - Held, Trial Court correctly convicted accused for offence of murder - Offence appeared brutal, conceived and executed with deliberation - S. 27 of the 1872 Act and s. 162(2) of CrPC in so far as 'that section relates to s. 27 of the Indian Evidence Act', were intra vires and did not offend art. 14 of the Constitution - Order of HC acquitting respondent was set aside and order of Court of Sessions convicting the accused (respondent) u/s. 302 of IPC and sentencing to death was restored - Appeal allowed.
