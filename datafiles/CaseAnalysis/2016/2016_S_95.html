Senior Divisional Commercial Manager and others v S. C. R. Caterers, Dry Fruits, Fruit Juice Stalls Welfare Association and another

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 82; (2016) 3 SCC 582; AIR 2016 SC 668; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 40; 2016 (2) AWC 1784; 2016 (1) CLT(SC) 87; JT 2016 (1) SC 557; 2016 (1) MLJ 884; 2016(2) SCALE 38; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 447

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Railways Act, 1989

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Administrative - Catering policy - License renewal - Aggrieved by notification issued by Senior Divisional Commercial Manager, Vijayawada inviting sealed bids on Single Stage Two-Packet System from food and catering service providers for provision of catering services at various GMUs of Categories 'A' and 'B' Railways Stations in Vijayawada Division, and another notification for establishment of catering stalls/fruits and fruit juice stalls in SMUs in 'A1', 'A' and 'B' Category Railway Stations, respondent-Association filed writ petition before Single Judge of HC contending that in terms of Catering Policy, 2010, existing licensees were entitled for renewal of their licenses for a period of three years, subject to their satisfactory performance, payment of all dues and arrears and withdrawal of court cases, if any - Single Judge of HC held that members of Welfare Association are entitled for renewal of licenses of members subject to their satisfying conditions stipulated in Catering policy, 2010 - On appeal, DB of HC upheld judgment and order of Single Judge and hence, instant appeals by appellants - Whether respondents are entitled to get their licenses renewed under Catering Policy, 2010.

Held, as per Circular no.37 dt.9-8-2010, renewal of license is required to be granted to all the existing licensees of the Minor Units as per cls.16 and 17 of the Catering Policy, 2010. Further, existing licensees need not be included in the tender process and Circular dt.23-8-2011 issued by Chief Commercial Manager of South Central Railway directed all the Divisional Commercial Managers and other subordinate officers of the South Cetral Railway to confirm that the tenure of all GMUs and SMUs at 'A1', 'A' and 'B' category stations would be renewed after every 3 years on their satisfactory performance and payment of all dues and arrears as per the 2010 Policy. In view of the said circular, catering licenses of all the members of the respondent Association were renewed till July 2013. Hence, Catering Policy, 2010 is applicable to respondents and action of railways in not granting renewals of the licenses to the members of the respondents is arbitrary, unreasonable, unfair and discriminatory and unsustainable. The judgment and order of HC is upheld and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - State action should not be arbitrary and discriminatory and not guided by any extraneous considerations which are antithetical to equality.
