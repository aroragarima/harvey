Seth Pushalal Mansinghka Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi, Rajasthan and Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 323; AIR 1967 SC 1626; [1967] 66 ITR 159; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 961

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(1)(a) - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 23 and 25 - Whether assessee was entitled to any rebate under Part B States (Taxation Concessions) Order, 1950 in respect of income from the mining business - Profits accrued to the appellant at the place where property in the goods passed to the purchasers - Appellant has reserved the right of disposal over the goods at the time of despatch such that until the payment of the price the property in the goods does not pass to the purchaser - Appellant became entitled to the purchase money only on the passing of title to the purchasers in Part A and Part C States and therefore the income accrued to the appellant in Part A and Part C States - Held, assessee was not entitled to any rebate - Appeal dismissed.
