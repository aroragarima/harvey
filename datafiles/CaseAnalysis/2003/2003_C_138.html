Aman Marble Industries Private Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1198; (2005) 1 SCC 279; 2003 (157) E.L.T. 393; 2003 (58) RLT 595

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Process of manufacturing

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Process of manufacturing - Whether cutting of marble blocks into marble slabs amounted to manufacture for purpose of the Act - Held, SC in, Rajasthan State Electricity Board v. Associated Stone Industries and Anr., 2000 Indlaw SC 400, observed that word 'manufacture' generally and in ordinary parlance in absence of its definition in the Act should be understood to mean bringing to existence new and different article having distinctive name, character or use after undergoing some transformation - When no new product as such came into existence, there was no process of manufacture - Activity carried on by appellant did not amount to manufacture - Appeal allowed.
