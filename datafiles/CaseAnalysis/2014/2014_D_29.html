Dev Prakash Tewari v Uttar Pradesh Cooperative Institutional Service Board, Lucknow and others

Supreme Court of India

30 June 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 405; (2014) 7 SCC 260; 2014(6) ALL MR 448; 2014 (4) AWC 4244; 2014 (142) FLR 621; JT 2014 (10) SC 85; 2014(8) SCALE 216; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 11; 2014 (6) SLR 59; 2014 (3) UPLBEC 1749; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 167

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: U. P. Co-operative Societies Employees Service Regulations, 1975

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Employees Service Regulations, 1975, regn. 85 - Disciplinary proceeding - Post retirement - Legality - Disciplinary proceeding was initiated u/regn. 85 of the Regulations, against appellant by serving a charge-sheet and after inquiry he was dismissed from service - Appellant sought for quashing the said order, HC quashed the disciplinary proceeding and granted liberty to conduct fresh disciplinary inquiry - Fresh disciplinary proceeding was initiated, meanwhile appellant reached the age of superannuation and retired from service - Appellant challenged the continuance of disciplinary proceeding after his retirement - HC held that there was no ground to interfere with the disciplinary proceeding and directed to complete it - Appellant filed review petition and the HC dismissed the same - Hence instant appeals - Whether disciplinary proceedings could be continued after retirement -

Held, there was no provision in the Regulations, for initiation or continuation of disciplinary proceeding after retirement of appellant nor there was any provision stating that in case misconduct was established a deduction could be made from his retiral benefits - Once appellant had retired from service on 31-3-2009, there was no authority vested with respondents for continuing the disciplinary proceeding even for the purpose of imposing any reduction in the retiral benefits payable to appellant - In the absence of such an authority it should be held that the enquiry had lapsed and appellant was entitled to get full retiral benefits - Inasmuch as the inquiry had lapsed, it was, obvious that appellant would have to get the balance of the emoluments payable to him - Impugned judgment and order of HC were set aside and respondents were directed to pay arrears of salary and allowances payable to appellant and also to pay him his all the retiral benefits in accordance with the rules and regulations as if there had been no disciplinary proceeding or order passed therein - Appeals allowed.
