Rajendra and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1132; (1999) 2 SCC 317; (1999) SCC (L&S) 551; AIR 1999 SC 923; 1999 (81) FLR 645; JT 1999 (1) SC 278; 1999 LabIC 1106; 1999 (2) LLN 35; 1999(1) SCALE 256; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 408; 2000 (1) SLJ 119; 1999 (1) SLR 636; 1999 (1) SLT 513; 1999 (2) UPLBEC 889

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 32 - Absorption - Whether claim for by employees to continue working under schemes founded by State Govt. for posts temporarily created for fulfilling needs of particular project after completion of project is justified? - Held, decision to abolish posts, based on administrative and financial consideration is bona fide and employer cannot be directed to continue with displaced persons - Can, be preferred if additional posts are created in future.
