Paras Yadav and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 437; (1999) SCC (Cr) 104; (1999) 2 SCC 126; AIR 1999 SC 644; 1999 CRLJ 1122; JT 1999 (1) SC 25; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 55

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 r/w s.34 - Murder - Dying declaration - Conviction - Imposition of sentence - Accused no.1 caught hold of bicycle of deceased and accused nos.2 and 3 caught both arms of deceased and assaulted him with fists and slaps - Accused no.1 gave chhura blow in abdomen - Accused persons fled away - Deceased stated before witnesses and Police Sub-Inspector that he was surrounded by accused persons and accused no.1 stabbed him on abdomen - Sub-Inspector of Police arrived on spot and recorded u/s.307 of IPC against three appellants - Victim shifted to hospital was died - Offence u/s.302 of IPC was added - Lower Courts convicted appellants u/s.302 r/w s.34 of IPC and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment for life - Whether conviction of appellants based on dying declaration of deceased was sustainable - Held, no eye witness to incident - Prosecution relied upon statements made by deceased - Dying declarations established beyond reasonable doubt that deceased was conscious and was in a position to speak after received injuries - Giving of chhura blow by accused no.1 was corroborated by medical evidence - Evidence did not suffer from any infirmity would render dying declarations as doubtful or unworthy of evidence - Some witnesses deposed that deceased informed that accused nos.2 and 3 surrounded him while other witnesses deposed that deceased told that they gave fist blows or slaps - Inconsistencies made in dying declaration as deposed by witnesses with regard to part played by accused nos.2 and 3 - Conviction of accused no.1 confirmed - Conviction of accused nos.2 and 3 was set aside - Appeals partly allowed.
