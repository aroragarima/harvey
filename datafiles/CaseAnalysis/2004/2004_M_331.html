Adu Ram v Mukna and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 838; (2005) 10 SCC 597; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1635; AIR 2004 SC 5064; 2004 CRLJ 4674; JT 2004 (8) SC 545; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 64; 2004(8) SCALE 591; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 314

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302/304 Part I r/w 149, 148, 341 - HC altered conviction from ss 302 r/w 149, 148, 341 to s 304 Part I r/w s 149, 149, 341 - Appeal against alteration of conviction - Whether HC right in alteration? - Held, HC recorded adequate reasons for altering conviction - Number of injuries is always not determinative of offence - Assaults appear to have been made in course of quarrel - No serious infirmity is noticed in HC's view - Appeals Disposed Of.
