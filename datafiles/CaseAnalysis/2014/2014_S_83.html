Shamim Bano v Asraf Khan

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 272; (2014) 12 SCC 636; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2200; JT 2014 (6) SC 393; 2014 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2342; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 820; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 592; 2014(5) SCALE 299; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 844

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986, s. 3 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Muslim personal law - Maintenance after divorce - Entitled to - Appellant wife sought maintenance u/s. 125 CrPC - Parties got divorced - In the meantime, appellant filed petition u/s. 3 of the Act for mahr and maintenance during Iddat period - Trial Court dismissed petition u/s. 125 CrPC, however, allowed petition u/s. 3 of the Act - Appellant appealed to HC - HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant Muslim woman was entitled to get maintenance after divorce - Held, no maintenance was granted to appellant beyond Iddat period by Trial Court - It would be travesty of justice if appellant would be made remediless - Appellant's application u/s. 125 CrPC was continuing - Even if an application u/s. 3 of the Act for grant of maintenance was filed, parameters of s. 125 CrPC would have been made applicable - Further, application for grant of maintenance was filed prior to the date of divorce and hearing of application continued - HC was not correct in opining that when appellant filed application u/s. 3 of the Act, she exercised her option - Trial Court still retained the power of granting maintenance u/s. 125 CrPC to a divorced Muslim woman - No justification on the part of HC to hold that proceeding after divorce took place was not maintainable - Hence, impugned orders were set aside and matter was remitted to Trial Court for re-adjudication of controversy in question - Appeal allowed.
