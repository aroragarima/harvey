Â Associated Hotels of India, Limited and Another v R. B. Jodha Mal Kuthalia

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1961 SC 156; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 259

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Independence Act, 1947, Punjab Land Revenue Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Indian Independence Pakistan Courts (Pending Proceedings) Act, 1952, Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949, Pakistan (Administration of Evacuee Property) Act, Transfer Of Evacuee Deposits Act, 1954, Indian Independence (Legal Proceedings) Order 1947

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898 - Court Fees Act, 1870 - Indian Independence Act, 1947 - Pakistan (Administration of Evacuee Property) Act - Punjab Land Revenue Act - Transfer of Evacuee Deposits Act, 1954 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Administration of Evacuee Property Ordinance, 1949 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Indian Independence (Legal Proceedings) Order 1947 - By an agreement D/- 2-10- 1947, respondent had agreed to sell to appellants certain property known as Nedous Hotel at Lahore for Rs. 52,75,000 - In pursuance of terms of said agreement appellants had paid respondent Rs. 5,00,000 by, way of earnest money - Respondent's title to property in question was defective, and so sale could not be completed - Appellants had to file a suit in court of Senior Subordinate Judge at Lahore claiming to recover from respondent a sum of Rs. 5,10,000 - This amount included Rs. 5,00,000 paid by appellants to respondent as earnest money and interest accrued due thereon up to date of suit - (A) Whether decree of Federal Court of Pakistan is covered by words 'appellate jurisdiction' in cl. 2 of art. 4 of Order? - (B) Whether word 'effect' in cl. 3 of art. 4 is of wide connotation and is equivalent to 'being enforced' by suits on a foreign judgment? - Held, so long as Order remains in force and has neither been modified or repealed it is duty of courts in India to consider its provisions in a fair and reasonable manner and to give full effect to them - Considerations based on unilateral conduct adopted by Pakistan Legislature in departing from provisions of Order cannot have any bearing in relating to the question of construction of Order itself - It is also significant that s. 4 is confined to specified judicial proceedings pending in only three Provinces, that is to say, proceedings pending in competent courts either in Sind or in North West Frontier Province which are parts of Pakistan and in all States in India except Punjab, West Bengal and Assam do not attract provisions of Order - S. 4(1) does not apply to all proceedings pending at material time before specified courts but only such of them in respect of which jurisdiction of trial court would have been affected by passing of Act or by transfer of certain territories - The amplitude of language of art. 4 is not cut down by any words in article or in Order and therefore decree of courts of West Punjab passed in proceedings pending immediately before appointed day are not foreign judgments in East Punjab and limited interpretation contended for by respondent is not sustainable - Decree of Federal Court of Pakistan is covered by words 'appellate jurisdiction' in cl. 2 of art. 4 of Order - Word 'effect' in cl. 3 of art. 4 is of wide connotation and is not equivalent to 'being enforced' by suits on a foreign judgment - Cl. 3 of art. 4 is in nature of a deeming clause and makes decree of Pakistan court (West Punjab) a decree of a court of competent jurisdiction in East Punjab (India) - Situs of decree is not in Pakistan alone but legal fiction applies to that also - Evacuee A laws of Pakistan do not affect effectiveness of decree in India - Appeal dismissed.
