Pratap Singh And Anr v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 936; (2005) 13 SCC 624; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 284; AIR 2006 SC 514; 2006 CRLJ 301; 2006 CRLJ 310; 2006 (1) CTC 571; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 162; JT 2005 (10) SC 423; 2006 (2) RLW 1496; 2005(9) SCALE 553; 2006 (2) SCJ 594; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 439

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1972, ss. 161, 379 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970 - Order of acquittal set aside by HC - Appeal against - Held, it cannot be said that learned Trial Judge committed any illegality in coming to conclusion that PW 2 might have been a tutored witness - No reason found as to why adverse inference should not have been drawn for non-filing of statements before Court along with charge sheet - Judgment of HC cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
