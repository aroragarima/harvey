P. C. Thomas v P. M. Ismail and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1094; (2009) 10 SCC 239; AIR 2010 SC 905; 2009 (3) KLT 972; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 293; 2009(12) SCALE 235; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 125

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Agents, Order For, Decision Of The High Court, Religious Symbol, Absence Of Proof, Purity, Circulation

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss.100(1)(b), 116A, 123(3) and 123(5) - Election petition - Corrupt practices - Appellant was declared elected - Election petitioner got second highest number of votes - Election of appellant was challenged by election petitioner on ground that appellant committed corrupt practices as enumerated u/ss.123(3) and 123(5) of the Act - HC held that appellant committed corrupt practice u/s.123(3) of the Act by appealing to electors to vote on ground of his community and religion through printing and circulation of photo calendar and notice among electors - HC declared election of appellant to be void and set aside election of appellant to House of People (Lok Sabha) - Hence, present appeal - Whether findings recorded in judgment in appeal, holding corrupt practices u/ss.123(3) and (5) of the Act having been committed by appellant, justified? - To bring aforesaid appeal within ambit of s.123(3) of Act, it required proof that as per specific pleas in election petition, documents were got printed and published at Akshara Press by appellant through his election agent and distributed by agents or workers/campaigners as alleged - HC deduced, notice clearly contains an appeal in that, Holy Father, Pope, head of Roman Catholics does exhort them not to keep away from mainstream - Notice/statement did constitute an appeal to members of Christian community, particularly, Catholics to seek their votes for appellant, being a Catholic, on ground of religion, caste and community - Indeed, community members all over understood it as such - This is borne out from testimony of witnesses who all deposed about party workers, to which party appellant belonged to, giving notice and calendar to each one of them as a statement by known leader of Catholics - Consent of appellant for distribution of said exhibits among Catholic voters, though not directly proved was rightly inferred from fact that his agent, who got these printed, paid charges and circulated them - Held, circulation of said exhibits amounted to violation of s.123(3) of the Act - Calendar does not per se fall within mischief of s.123(3) of the Act but taken along with notice does serve as a statement to strengthen appeal in notice - Further held, election petitioner adduced cogent, satisfactory and reliable evidence to establish charge against appellant u/s.123(3) of Act - HC has not committed any grave error in appreciation and assessment of evidence on point - Appeals disposed of.
