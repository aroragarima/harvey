Ganga Saran And Sons Pvt. Ltd. Calcutta v Income Tax Officer & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 302; (1981) 3 SCC 143; AIR 1981 SC 1363; 1982 (2) CompLJ 511; 1981 (22) CTR 112; [1981] 130 ITR 1; 1981(1) SCALE 883; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 564; 1981 TAXLR 1069; [1981] 6 TAXMAN 14; 1981 UJ 533

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Failure To Disclose Material Facts

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147(a) - A/y 1949-50 - Escaped Assessment - Assessee took over the business of the trading company carried on by 'Y' - When business of 'Y' was taken over by the assessee, 'Y' was appointed Director of the assessee along with two other persons and was paid a salary of Rs. 1000 per month, commission at the rate of 1% and bonus equivalent to 3 months' salary - Assessee claimed deduction in respect of payments made to the Managing Director and other Directors on account of commission and bonus - Said deduction was allowed - Subsequently, Income Tax Officer (ITO) issued a notice seeking to reopen the assessment of the assessee on the ground that the income of the assessee had escaped assessment - However, Income Tax Officer did not state the reasons which had led to the belief that the income of the assessee had escaped assessment by reason of omission or failure to disclose material facts nor did he give any reasons though requested by the assessee - Assessee challenged the validity of the notice vide writ petition which was allowed by Single Judge and notice issued by the ITO was quashed - DB allowed appeal filed against said order - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the DB could be upheld? - Held, before the ITO can assume jurisdiction to issue notice u/s. 147(a) of IT Act, two distinct conditions must be satisfied i.e. he must have reason to believe that the income of the assessee has escaped assessment and he must have reason to believe that such escapement is by reason of the omission or failure on the part of the assessee to disclose fully and truly all material facts necessary for his assessment - Neither of the two conditions necessary for attracting the applicability of s. 147(a) of IT Act was satisfied in the present case and the notice issued by the ITO must be held to be without jurisdiction - Order of the DB set aside and order of the Single Judge restored - Appeal allowed.
