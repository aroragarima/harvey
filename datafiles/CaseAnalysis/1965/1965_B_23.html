Bhagwati Prasad v Shrichandramaul

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 244; AIR 1966 SC 735; 1966 ALJ 799; 1967 BLJR 158; 1966 (1) ILR(All) 796; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 286

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - U P (Temporary) Control Of Rent And Eviction Act, 1947 - (A) Whether High Court has made a new case for plaintiff, is permissible in law? - Held, mere fact that plea was not expressly taken in pleadings would not necessarily disentitle a party from relying upon it if it is satisfactorily proved by evidence - (B) Whether plaintiff is entitled for mesne profit? - Further held, once it is held that plaintiff is entitled to eject defendant, it follows that from date of decree granting relief of ejectment to plaintiff, defendant who remains in possession of property despite decree, must pay mesne profits or damages for use and occupation of said property until it is delivered to plaintiff - Appeal partly allowed.
