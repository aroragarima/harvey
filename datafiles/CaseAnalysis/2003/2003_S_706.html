Dr. (Mrs.) Chanchal Goyal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 161; (2003) SCC (L&S) 322; (2003) 3 SCC 485; AIR 2003 SC 1713; 2003 (2) CLR 949; 2003 (2) ESC 163; 2003 (97) FLR 7; JT 2003 (5) SC 144; 2003 LabIC 1141; 2003 (2) LLN 415; 2003(2) SCALE 264; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 112; 2003 (3) SLJ 1; 2003 (5) SLR 424; 2003 (2) SLT 269; 2003 (2) Supreme 810; 2003 (4) UPLBEC 3444

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Foreign Allowance

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Municipalities Act, 1959 - S. 308 - Rajasthan Municipal Service Rules, 1963 - Rules 26 and 27 - whether the appellants' termination from service is in order - an ad-hoc appointee cannot be replaced by another ad-hoc appointee and only a legally selected candidate can replace the ad-hoc or temporary appointee. - Held, when a person had been selected and the Service Commission had drawn up a list of selected candidates, then, non-joining of the selected candidate does not confer any right on the appellant. - Principle of legitimate expectation - held, the principle of legitimate expectation has no application as it has not been shown as to how any act was done by the authorities which created an impression that the conditions attached in the original appointment order were waived. - Clear statutory words override any expectation, however, founded.
