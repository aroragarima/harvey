Gurpal Tuli And Ors., Etc. v State Of Punjab And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 213; (1984) Supp SCC 716; (1985) SCC (L&S) 366; AIR 1984 SC 1901; 1985 LabIC 411; 1985 (2) LLJ 332; 1985 (1) LLN 590; 1984(2) SCALE 557; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 882; 1985 (3) SLR 259; 1985 UJ 243

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Determination of availability of benefits higher grades to Masters and Mistresses in govt. schools to those having PG degrees by Circular letter No. 2036-ED 167/2/67 D/- 29-07-1967, para 2 - Held, cannot be granted unless they are appointed as lecturers - The grades specified in para2 of the Circular letter D/-29-07-1967 are applicable only to those who specifically hold the posts of Lecturer- Appeal dismissed.
