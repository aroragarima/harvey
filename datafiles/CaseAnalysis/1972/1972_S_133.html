Union of India v Sri Sarada Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

28 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 345; (1972) 2 SCC 877; AIR 1973 SC 281; [1973] 43 Comp Cas 431; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 464

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Land & Property

Keywords: Government securities, Legal Proceedings, Contract Of Insurance, Actionable Claim, Contract Of Indemnity, Contract Of Marine Insurance, Fire Insurance, Fire Insurance Policy, Underwriter, Chose In Action, Rights Of Subrogation

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 135 and 135 A - Insurance - Transfer of Property - Assignment of rights - (A) Marine insurance policy - Subrogation - In whose name insurer is entitled to sue - (B) Fire insurance claim by Mill satisfied - Insurer subrogated to rights of Mill - Suit by Mill against Railway Administration, if barred - Held, insurer on subrogation is not entitled to sue in his own name and it can only sue in insurer - Not barred, but accountable to return from money recovered - Appeal dismissed.
