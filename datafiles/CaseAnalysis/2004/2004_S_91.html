Sarjas Rai & Ors v Bakshi Inderjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1555; (2005) 1 SCC 598; 2005 (58) ALR 332; 2005 (1) ARC 637; 2005 (1) AWC 131; JT 2005 (11) SC 93; 2005 (1) RD 350; 2004(10) SCALE 267

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Second Appeal dismissed, Validity, No substaintial question of law

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Second appeal dismissed - No substantial question of law - Validity - Respondent/landlord filed suit for perpetual and mandatory injunction against appellant alleging that they carried out certain structural changes in the premises let out to the appellant - Trial Court decreed the suit - On appeal before Appellate Court, Appellate Court dismissed the appeal - On appeal before HC, HC dismissed the appeal - Hence instant petition.

Held, findings recorded by the Courts below are either findings of fact or mixed question of law and fact. It has been found as a fact by the Courts below that the floor of the shop was sought to be changed and in that process the level of the floor has been brought down one feet below the road level. Similarly certain alterations were made in the structure by removing pillars with a view to give a wider frontage to suit the convenience of the appellant. The HC was justified in dismissing the Second Appeal. Appeal dismissed.
