Rajendra Singh v The State Of Madhya Pradesh& Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 972; (1996) 5 SCC 460; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 185; AIR 1996 SC 2736; 1996 (4) CLT 42; JT 1996 (7) SC 216; 1996(5) SCALE 793; [1996] Supp4 S.C.R. 393; 1996 (6) Supreme 658

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise

Summary: Constitution - Excise - Constitution of India, 1950 - Madhya Pradesh Excise Act, 1915 - For the year 1994-95, the appellant was the highest bidder for certain number of liquor shops, but failed to pay the monthly rental for the months July and August, 1994, so a notice was issued against him - Contentions of the appellant was rejected - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the appellant rightly contended that reasonable opportunity was not given - Held, that the contract provided for payment of monthly rental on or before a particular date, and if the amount of monthly rental was not paid before the due date, the licence was liable to be cancelled as provided by sub-s. (1) of s. 31, of the 1915 Act - While the opportunity to be given should be reasonable, the reasonableness or otherwise of the opportunity given should be judged keeping in view the time-frame available - Time given to the licencee to show cause would naturally be a short one for the reason that soon thereafter the next month's rental would fall due - Appeals dismissed.
