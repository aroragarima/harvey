Rajasthan Financial Corporation and Another v Official Liquidator and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 987; (2005) 8 SCC 190; AIR 2006 SC 755; 2005 (4) BC 551; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 531; [2005] 128 Comp Cas 387; 2005 (6) CompLJ 129; 2006 (1) DRTC 321; JT 2005 (12) SC 156; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 184; 2005(8) SCALE 255; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 1073

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Summary: Corporate - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 - Companies Act, 1956 - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Whether assets of a company in liquidation could be sold by a financial institution? - Held, right of a financial institution or of Recovery Tribunal or that of a financial corporation or court which has been approached u/s. 31 of SFC Act to sell assets may not be taken away, but same stands restricted by requirement of official liquidator being associated with it, giving company court right to ensure that distribution of assets in terms of s. 529A of Companies Act takes place - To ensure proper working out of scheme of distribution, it is necessary to associate official liquidator with process of sale so that he can ensure, in light of directions of company court, that a proper price is fetched for assets of company in liquidation - Appeal disposed of.
