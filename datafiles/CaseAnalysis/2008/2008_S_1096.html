Vijay v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1888; (2009) 2 SCC 87; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 674; AIR 2009 SC 1217; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1268; 2009 (1) JCC 113; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 315; 2008(15) SCALE 362; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Women & Children

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 304B, 306 and 498A - Construction of evidence (letter) - Alleged dowry death - Demand for ring and money in letter written by deceased - High Court convicted the accused/ appellant u/s. 498A, 304-B and 306 of Penal Code, by reversing the order of acquittal - Counsel for State submitted that there is some reference to money in the letter but the sentence is being read out of context - And deceased had written in the letter that she may have to get a gold ring but wherefrom will she get the money for buying it - Further no reference to any demand of money by the present appellant - Held, High Court was not justified in upsetting the judgment of the trial Court - Appeal allowed.
