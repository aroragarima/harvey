Â Lakhan Lal and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 146; AIR 1968 SC 1408; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, Bihar Agricultural Market Produce rules 1962

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Bihar Agriculture Produce Markets Act, 1960, ss. 2, 3, 4, 5, 6, 15 and 27 - Bihar Agricultural Market Produce rules 1962, rr. 59, 61, 67, 71 and 73 - (A) Whether notification declaring market area is void as notified area is too wide and whether Act and Rules are valid in absence of a market? - (B) Whether fees levied are illegal? - Held, market consisting of market proper and market yards were validly established after following procedure laid down by Act and Rules - Fees charged by market committee are correlated to expenses incurred by it for rendering services - Further, they form part of market committee fund which is set apart and ear-marked for purposes of Act - There is sufficient quid pro quo for levies and they satisfy test of 'fee' and hence, is valid - (C) Whether setting up of a market in a particular region is discriminatory and violative of art. 14 of Constitution as Act and Rules have not been implemented in all parts of State? - Held, it is not violative of art. 14 since State Govt. is not bound to implement Act and Rules in all parts of State at same time - It may establish markets regulating sale and purchase of Agricultural produce in different parts of State gradually and from time to time - Appeals and Petitions dismissed.
