Lakshmi Alias Bhagyalakshmi And Anr. v E.Jayaram (D) By Lr.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 71; (2013) 9 SCC 311; AIR 2013 SC 2939; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 1; 2013(2) ALL MR 468; 2013 (99) ALR 728; 2013 (2) AWC 1294; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 217; JT 2013 (2) SC 466; 2013 (3) KarLJ 439; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 180; 2013 (1) RCR(Rent) 295; 2013 (119) RD 794; 2013(2) SCALE 277; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 794; 2013 (2) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 53A - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2, O. 39, rr. 1,2 - Suit for Injunction - Failure to register Sale Deed - Interim Injunction - Sustainability - Appellants-plaintiffs had filed suit for permanent injunction along with application for interim injunction on ground that defendants-respondents had received entire sale consideration but failed to register sale deed as registration as suspended by Govt. and respondents could not get clearance from ULC authority - Respondents pleaded that appellants were their tenants and that they never intended to sell property - Appellants moved application for change of Kattas and came to know that respondents have concocted Gift Deed in favor of defendant No. 2, appellants issued notice and filed suit for permanent injunction - Trial Court passed interim order granting injunction to appellants - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal before HC, Single Judge instead of considering legality and propriety of interim injunction proceeded to decide effect of s. 53A of Act 1882, and that suit for bare injunction was filed without seeking leave under O. 2 r. 2 of CPC, and that if respondent was barred from appellants-respondents before HC, were barred from claiming any relief of specific performance, incidental relief of injunction would be unavailable to respondents - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in deciding scope of s. 53A of Act 1882 and O.2 r. 2 of CPC without looking into legality and propriety of interim injunction - Held, single judge had completely misconstrued provisions of O. 39 r. 1,2 CPC and had committed serious error in deciding scope of Section 53A of Act, 1882 and O. 2 r. 2 of CPC, while granting ad-interim injunction Trial Court had very categorically observed that respective rights of parties shall be decided at time of final disposal of suit - Fact that Plaintiff No.2 was in possession of property as a tenant under Plaintiff No.1 and possession of Plaintiff No.2 was not denied, interim protection was given to Plaintiff No.2 against threatened action of defendants to evict her without following due process of law, order passed by single judge could not be sustained in law - Appeal allowed.
