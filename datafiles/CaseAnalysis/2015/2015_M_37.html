Ajay Ramdas Ramteke and another v Mahanagar Sudhar Samiti, Akola and others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 71; 2015(2) ALL MR 950; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 719; JT 2015 (2) SC 116; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 235; 2015(2) SCALE 136; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 100

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Maharashtra Local Authority Members' Disqualification Act, 1986, Maharashtra Local Authority Members' Disqualification Rules, 1987

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Maharashtra Municipal Corporations Act, 1949, s. 31A(2) - Maharashtra Local Authority Members' Disqualification Rules, 1987 - Nomination to Standing Committee - Legality - Some elected members of Municipal Corporation in question formed respondent No.1 front - Respondent No. 1 sought registration - Divisional Commissioner rejected registration application by vide order dt. 28-8-2012 - Appellants were nominated to Standing Committee of Municipal Corporation by vide Resolution dt.29-4-2013 - On a writ petition by respondents, HC quashed Resolution dt.29-4-2013 - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the order dt. 28-8-2012 the Divisional Commissioner also referred to a serious infirmity in accepting the proposal, as he found that the affidavit was sworn to and attested on 22-2-2012, whereas the stamps were purchased on 23-2-2012 which the Divisional Commissioner held to be an incomprehensible act of the proposer. Such serious infirmities which weighed with the Divisional Commissioner in passing the order of rejection dt. 28-8-2012 cannot be found fault with. Further, it was incumbent upon the Divisional Commissioner to hold a meaningful exercise of scrutinizing the proposal for registration and pass a positive order of registration and then alone the exception carved out u/s. 31A(2) of the Act, even for the limited purpose to get rid of disqualification under the Rules can be allowed to operate. Viewed in that respect also the order dt. 28-8-2012 assumes greater significance and, therefore, unless and until the said order was set aside in the manner known to law, the formation of the front as claimed by respondent No.1 could not have come into effect. Further, no one challenged the order dt. 28-8-2012 passed by the Divisional Commissioner, as such the same has attained finality. That being so, respondent no.1 cannot be said to be a registered group as required under second proviso of s. 31A (2) of the Act. Thus, the HC has erred in law by ignoring the order of the Divisional Commissioner, and holding that respondent no. 1 stood registered. Had the writ petitioners challenged order dt. 28-8-2012 passed by the Divisional Commissioner, with the Resolution dated 29-4-2013, the situation would have been different. But in the present case, order of Divisional Commissioner rejecting application for registration has attained finality, and same cannot be ignored. As such, writ petition filed by respondent nos. 1 to 3 questioning validity of resolution dt. 29-4-2013 was liable to be dismissed. Appeal allowed.
