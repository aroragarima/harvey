State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v T.P. Lal Srivastava

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2460; (1996) 10 SCC 702; 1997 (1) ESC 39; JT 1996 (9) SC 301; 1997 (1) LLJ 831; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 623; 1997 (2) Supreme 579; 1997 (1) UPLBEC 539

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Charge-sheet served on respondent calling upon him to explain charges for committing gross irregularities in movement of wheat outside State of U P - Found guilty of misappropriation - High Court held that documents have not been supplied to respondent and, therefore, action was vitiated by error of law - Held, no justification in view taken by HC - Substratum of result is that appellants have not conducted any enquiry - Respondent has forgone his right to submit his reply - Appellants are not absolved of duty to hold an ex parte enquiry to find out whether or not charge has been proved - Disciplinary authority should communicate copy of enquiry report to respondent and seek an explanation for proposed action thereon - Respondents response to be taken into consideration - Respondent must be deemed to be under suspension - Appeal allowed.
