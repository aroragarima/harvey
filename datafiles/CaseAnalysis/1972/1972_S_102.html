Raj Narain v Smt. Indira Nehru Gandhi And Another

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 126; (1972) 3 SCC 850; AIR 1972 SC 1302; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Procure

Summary: Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 83 and 86(5) - Constitution, arts. 311 and 309 - CPC, 1908, O. 11, r. 1 - (A) Contents of election petition - Amendment of petition for better particulars - (B) Resignation of Government servant - When becomes effective - (C) Interrogatories - If required to be relate to any matters in question - Held, may be incorporated into the election petition with the permission of the Court even after the period of limitation and absence of better particulars did not per se invalidate the charge - Service of a Govt. servant normally stands terminated from the date on which the letter of resignation is accepted by appropriate authority unless there is any law or statutory rule governing the conditions of service to the contrary - The interrogatories served must have reasonably close connection with 'matters in question.' - Appeal Allowed.
