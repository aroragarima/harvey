Messrs Savita Chemicals Private Limited v Dyes and Chemical Workers Union

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1722; (1999) 2 SCC 143; (1999) SCC (L&S) 492; AIR 1999 SC 413; 1999 (2) Bom.C.R. 664; 1999 (81) FLR 932; JT 1998 (8) SC 552; 1999 LabIC 578; 1999 (1) LLJ 416; 1999 (1) LLN 637; 1998(6) SCALE 519; 1999 (3) SLR 688; 1998 (10) SLT 248; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 488; 1998 (9) Supreme 415

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Privilege Leave, Trade Union Act, 1926, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946

Summary: Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act 1972, ss. 24(1)(a), 61(1) and 44 - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Rules, 1975, r. 24 - Labour Courts (Practice and Procedure) Rules, 1975, rr. 50 and 51 - Strike - Legality of, challenged as being in respect of matters 'covered by settlement' - Held, pertains to matters posterior to settlement and has no connection with matter covered by settlement - Strike notice calling for proper implementation of settlement - Not hit by provisions - Cannot also be said to be illegal only on grounds that non - implementation of terms of settlement could have been treated as unfair labour practise.
