Â Gopal Narain v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 316; AIR 1964 SC 370; 1964 ALJ 479; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 869

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: House Tax

Summary: Municipalities & Local Govts - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f), art 265 - U.P. Municipalities Act, ss. 7, 8, 128, 128 (1), 131, 132 and 133 - Evidence Act, s. 114 - Notification by Bareilly Municipal Board imposing house tax and scavenging tax in civil lines area - (A)Whether taxes imposed by municipalities act is violative of art. 14 of Constitution - Held, power vested in Board to select part of municipality within which to levy a tax was not an arbitrary power but one which is controlled by purpose which was intended to be achieved by Act itself - Equality clause does not forbid geographical classification, provided difference between geographical units has a reasonable relation to object sought to be achieved - This principle applies to a taxation law - When such powers are conferred upon Municipality Board by act coming into force then levying such taxes is not violative of art. 14 - There is a presumption, when a statutory authority makes an order for imposition of tax, that it has followed prescribed procedure - Said presumption is not in any way weakened by long acquiescence in imposition by residents of locality - (B) Whether a time lapse in attacking a tax derogatory to statute can weaken a suit opposing same? -Further held, while long period of time that lapses between imposition of tax and attack on it may permit raising of certain presumptions where evidence is lost by efflux of time, it cannot exonerate statutory authority if it imposes a tax in derogation of statutory provisions - Therefore, time lapse cannot be cause to escape prosecution - Petition dismissed.
