FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Ramji Veerji Patel and others v Revenue Divisional Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 806; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 1; 2012 (1) AWC 7; 2012 (5) Bom.C.R. 584; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 271; 2012 (3) CTC 325; 2012 (2) MahLJ 522; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 795; 2012 (2) MPLJ 246; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 276; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 821; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 946

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4 (1), 5-A and 6 - Companies Act, 1956, s. 3 (e) and Part VII - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Compulsory acquisition - Alternative land - Extraordinary jurisdiction - Respondent/corporation made requisition for making available land for expansion of their workshop/depot - Respondent issued notification u/s. 4 (1), Act 1894 - Appellants filed objections to acquisition before Revenue Divisional Officer (RDO) - RDO submitted his report to Government on conclusion of enquiry u/s. 5-A, Act 1894 - Appellants sent representation to Government bringing to its notice that land belonging to Tamil Nadu Evengelical Lutheran Church (TELC) just behind existing depot was advertised for sale and, therefore, instead of resorting to compulsory acquisition of appellants' land, land of TELC might be acquired, which was ignored - Declaration u/s. 6, Act 1894 was issued - Appellants challenged notification and declaration u/ss. 4 (1) and 6, Act 1894 respectively in writ petition before HC - Single Judge dismissed writ petition - Appellants filed intra court appeal, which was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that land belonging to TELC was suitable as that land was situated just behind existing depot, which had already access to main road and also contended that corporation or successor Tamil Nadu State Transport Corporation (TNSTC) was 'Government Company', hence compliance with provisions of Part VII of Act, 1956 had to be made in order to lawfully acquire any land for its purpose and invoked jurisdiction u/art. 142, Constitution - Whether consideration of appellants' objections to acquisition of their land by Government suffered from any illegality or irrationality - Held, on perusal of records, land of TELC suggested by appellants was not found to be more suitable; Government gave reasons as to why appellants land was found to be more suitable for expansion of depot - Appellants land was adjacent to existing depot of respondent/corporation having easy access to main road and decision was taken by Government regarding suitability of appellants land for expansion of depot was not vitiated by any error of law nor it was irrational or founded on extraneous reasons - Further, Part VII (acquisition of land for companies) of the Act 1956 had no application to instant case as acquisition of land was not for a 'company' as defined in s. 3(e), which excludes Government Company - Corporation or its successor TNSTC did not fall within definition of 'company', hence it was not covered by Part VII of Act, 1956 at all - Further, instant case, nothing should be done that would result in frustrating acquisition of land which was completed long back by following procedure under the Act 1894 and after giving full opportunity to appellants u/s. 5-A, Act 1894 - Possession of land was also taken - Hence, application made by appellants seeking direction not to interfere with functioning of saw mill and permit them to use would not be granted as it was closed for more than ten years after possession was taken over from appellants - This was not a case fit for exercise of power u/art. 142 - Appeal dismissed.
