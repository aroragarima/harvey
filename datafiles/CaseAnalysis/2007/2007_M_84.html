M/S. Agri Gold Exims Ltd v M/S. Sri Lakshmi Knits & Wovens & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 46; (2007) 3 SCC 686; 2007 (2) AWC 1961; 2007 (1) CompLJ 309; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 407; JT 2007 (2) SC 602; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 984; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 851; 2007(2) SCALE 296; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 1161

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Arbitration and Conciliation Act

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Suit for recovery of some amount - Respondents in the suit filed an application praying for reference of the dispute to the arbitral tribunal contended that suit filed by the appellant was not maintainable - High Court allowed the application - Appeal against - Held, claim is not confined to the question regarding non-payment of the amount under the two dishonoured cheques - There existed a dispute between the parties - In a case where there exists an arbitration agreement, the court is under obligation to refer the parties to arbitration in terms of the arbitration agreement - High Court, was right in referring the dispute between the parties to arbitration - Appeal dismissed.
