Nestle India Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 797; (2009) 12 SCC 294; 2009 (235) E.L.T. 577

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Case Remanded

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Limitation - Suppression - Manufacture - (A) Whether process undertaken by appellant resulting in emergence of 'intermixture of vitamins' for 'Lactogen' and 'Cerelac' amounts to manufacture u/s. 2(f) of Act? - Held, Vitamin A, Vitamin D and Vitamin E etc. were bought out items which are marketable - Said vitamins were converted into a recipe which according to appellant was not marketable and, therefore, Note 11 is not applicable - No finding of Tribunal in this respect, hence matter remanded to Tribunal for de novo disposal - (B) Whether extended period of limitation could be invoked on ground of suppression? - Held, as per SC decision in Padmini Products Vs. Collector of C.Ex 1989 INDLAW SC 764 and in Collector of Central Excise Vs. Chemphar Drugs & Liniments 1989 INDLAW SC 755, extended period of limitation is applicable only when there is some positive act other than mere inaction or failure on part of manufacturer - There must be conscious or deliberate withholding of information by manufacturer to invoke larger period of limitation - No infirmity in Tribunal's order - Appeals allowed.
