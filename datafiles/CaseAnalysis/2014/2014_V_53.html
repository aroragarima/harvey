Vireshwar Singh and others v Municipal Corporation of Delhi and others

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 578; (2014) 10 SCC 360; 2014 (6) AWC 5641; 2014 (143) FLR 603; JT 2014 (10) SC 146; 2014(10) SCALE 52; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 372; 2014 (5) SLR 771; 2014 (3) UPLBEC 2376

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Delhi Municipal corporation Health Service Recruitment Regulations, 1982, Principle of counting seniority

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Health Service Recruitment Regulations, 1982 - Principle of counting seniority - Legality - Appellants were General Duty Medical Officers (GDMO) Grade-II who were appointed on ad hoc basis between 1986 and 1989 - Appellants were aggrieved by denial of their claim to regularization with effect from the dates of their initial appointments - Regularization granted from the date of the recommendations of Union Public Service commission (UPSC), namely, 24-7-1998 as approved by HC by means of impugned order was into question in instant appeals - Appellants contended that denial of benefit of long years of ad hoc service, in view of the ratio of the law laid down in Narender Chadha and Others v. Union of India and Others, 1986 Indlaw SC 661 and Keshav Chandra Joshi and Others v. Union of India and Others, 1990 Indlaw SC 270, would be contrary to arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution -

Held, both sets of GDMOs (between 1982 and 1986) Phase-I and (between 1986 and 1989) Phase-II were not appointed on the basis of a selection held by UPSC as mandated by the Regulations in force. Their appointments were recommended by a Specially Constituted Selection Committee. Their appointments were ad hoc; initially for a period of 6 months which was subsequently extended from time to time. Being similarly circumstanced, undoubtedly, both sets of GDMOs will have to be treated equally and evenly for the purpose of regularization. In Dr. Anuradha Bodi & Ors. Etc. after noticing the precise terms of appointment of Phase-I GDMOs, the entitlement of GDMOs (Phase-I) to regularization with effect from the date of their initial appointments was considered by SC in the light of the decision of Constitution Bench in Direct Recruit Class II Engineering Officers' Association case. In Narender Chadha, the lis between the parties was one relating to counting of ad hoc service rendered by the promotees for the purpose of computation of seniority qua the Direct Recruits. The basis of the decision to count long years of ad hoc service for the purpose of seniority is to be found more in the peculiar facts of the case as noted in para 20 of the report than on any principle of law of general application. However, in paras 15-19 of the report a deemed relaxation of the Rules of appointment and the wide sweep of the power to relax the provisions of the Rules, as it existed at the relevant point of time, appears to be the basis for counting of the ad hoc service for the purpose of seniority. The principle laid down in Narender Chadha, was approved by the Constitution Bench in Direct Recruit Class II, as the promotion of the officers on ad hoc basis was found to be 'without following the procedure laid down under the Rules'. Any departure from the views expressed and conclusions reached in Dr. Anuradha Bodi & Ors. Etc. will not be necessary or justified. Appeals dismissed.
