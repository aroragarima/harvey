Anil Kumar Vitthal Shete and Others v State of Maharashtra and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 624; (2006) 12 SCC 148; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 901; AIR 2006 SC 2018; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 705; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 502; JT 2006 (6) SC 24; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1851; 2006(5) SCALE 84; 2006 (4) SCJ 614; 2006 (4) SLR 660; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 553

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporations Act, 1949 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 233, 309, 32 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115(5) - Government servant - Equal opportunity - Pay commissions committees - Petitioners claimed that they belonged to a cadre of Judges of Small Causes Court, Bombay which was an independent, separate and distinct cadre filled up by promotion from Civil Judges (Senior Division) and also by direct recruitment - Their cases were considered by the Administrate Side of the HC of Bombay and a decision was taken by the Full Court to place them in Category 2 of the judicial hierarchy in the State of Maharashtra - Grievance of the petitioners was that, though they held higher post and formed higher cadre than Civil Judges (Senior Division) and were promoted from the post of Civil Judges (Senior Division) to the post of Judges, Small Causes Court, they had been illegally put on par with Civil Judges (Senior Division) virtually reverting them to the position of Civil Judges (Senior Division) from which cadre they were promoted to the higher cadre - Whether the placement for position of Assistant Judges of Small Causes Court in Maharashtra and in Gujarat by Committee of five judges was legal - Held, keeping in view the relevant provisions of the Constitution, Recruitment Rules and the powers exercised by Assistant Judges, the Committee felt that proper placement of Assistant Judges would be above Civil Judge (Senior Division) in Category 2 - Commission was of the opinion that the post of the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate should be placed in the cadre of District Judge - Considering the powers to be exercised, functions to be performed and duties to be discharged by Assistant Judges and keeping in view the provisions of the Constitution as also the relevant provisions of law, the Committee constituted by the HC of Gujarat considered the question and decided to place Assistant Judges in Category 2 above Civil Judges (Senior Division) - It was no doubt true that Assistant Judges were promoted from feeder cadre of Civil Judges (Senior Division), but hereinabove, while dealing with the placement of Judges of Small Causes Court in Maharashtra and in Gujarat that when all officers were to be placed within few cadres, some officers were required to be placed in one and the same cadre even though they were holding promotional posts - Their placement, however, must be properly done so that they were shown above the feeder cadre from which they have been promoted - This was the position of Judges of Small Causes Court and it was held that such an action wouldn't be held illegal - Application disposed of.
