Sambasivan & Ors. v State Of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 809; (1998) 5 SCC 412; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1320; AIR 1998 SC 2107; 1998 (3) CCR 5; 1998 CRLJ 2924; 1998 (2) JCC 227; JT 1998 (3) SC 742; 1998 (2) RCR(Criminal) 693; 1998(3) SCALE 462; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 280; 1998 (5) SLT 182; 1998 (4) Supreme 562

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - CrPC, 1973, s. 379 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, s. 3 - (A) Appeal against acquittal - Murder case - Appellate Court convicting accused b reversing order of acquittal - Approach of trial Court in dealing with evidence patently illegal - If the judgment of the Appellate Court is free from those infirmities - Whether interference by appellate Court in order of acquittal justified - (B) Murder - Proof - Trade Union rivalry - Throwing of bombs by members of one union on members of other union while they were relaxing due to no work available on that day - Testimony of eye-witnesses not shaken in cross-examination - Whether their testimony can be discarded because they belong to rival union - Medical evidence and Forensic Laboratory report corroborating prosecution case - No possibility of false implication as overt acts of accused mentioned in FIR lodged soon after incident - Whether accused liable to be convicted - Held, (A) Trial Court judgment warranted interference - Of the two reasonable views, the one in support of the acquittal alone has to stand - (B) Only because they belong to rival union their testimony cannot be discarded - Accused liable to be convicted - Appeal dismissed.
