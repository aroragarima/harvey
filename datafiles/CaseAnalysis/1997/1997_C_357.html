South India Viscose Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2850; (1997) 6 SCC 393; AIR 1997 SC 2943; 1997 (141) CTR 374; [1997] 227 ITR 286; JT 1997 (6) SC 394; 1997(4) SCALE 538; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 89; 1997 (6) Supreme 455; [1997] 93 TAXMAN 371; 1997 (2) TLR 766

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 32(1) - Is assessee entitled to extra shift allowance in respect of machinery and spares which were added during relevant previous year, on basis of double and triple shifts worked by entire concern? - Held, provisions governing extra shift depreciation allowance postulate that such allowance would be allowable only when concern works double shift or triple shift - It means that concern as a whole should have worked extra shift - If a small portion of industry works overtime whole unit will not get benefit for it - Answered in favour of appellant.
