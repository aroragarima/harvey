Republic of Italy and another v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 59; (2013) 4 SCC 721; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 1075; JT 2013 (2) SC 1; 2013 (1) KLT 367; 2013(1) SCALE 462; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 595

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; International

Keywords: Piracy, United Nations (Privileges and Immunities) Act, 1947, Territorial Waters, Territorial Waters, Continental Shelf, Exclusive Economic Zone and Other Maritime Zones Act, 1976, Maritime Zones of India (Regulation of Fishing By Foreign Vessels) Act, 1981, Geneva Conventions Act, 1960, High Seas, Theft Act, 1968, Anti-Hijacking Act, 1982, Diplomatic Relations (Vienna Convention) Act, 1972, Suppression of Unlawful Acts, Italian Penal Code

Summary: Altamas Kabir, J.

(A)Criminal - International - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Territorial Waters, Continental Shelf, Exclusive Economic Zone and Other Maritime Zones Act, 1976, s. 7(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Suppression of Unlawful Acts Against Safety of Maritime Navigation and Fixed Platforms on Continental Shelf Act, 2002, s. 3 - Maritime Zones Act, 1976 - United Nations Convention on Law of the Sea 1982 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Territorial jurisdiction - Sovereign rights - Maintainability - Petitioner nos. 2 and 3 were deployed along with 4 others, as 'Team Latorre', on board to protect said vessel and to embark thereon from Galle in Sri Lanka - While vessel, with Military Protection Detachment on board, was heading for Djibouti on 15-2-2012, it came across an Indian fishing vessel, which it allegedly mistook to be a pirate vessel and on account of firing from Italian vessel, two fishermen were killed - Enquiry was conducted and FIR was lodged u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC and petitioners nos. 2 and 3 were arrested - Petitioners nos. 2 and 3 filed writ petition before HC seeking to quash FIR as same was without jurisdiction, and was null and void and for release of petitioner nos.2 and 3 from case - Single Judge held that under Notification No. SO 67/E dt. 27-8-1981, entire IPC had been extended to Exclusive Economic Zone and territorial jurisdiction of State of Kerala was not limited to 12 nautical miles only - Single Judge also held that under provisions of the SUA Act, State of Kerala had jurisdiction upto 200 nautical miles from Indian coast, falling within Exclusive Economic Zone of India - Hence, instant special leave petition - Whether Single Judge was justified in holding that sovereignty of India would extend to Exclusive Economic Zone, which extends to 200 nautical miles from baseline of coast of State of Kerala - Held, incident took place within Contiguous Zone over which, both under provisions of the 1976 Act and the 1982 Act, India was entitled to exercise rights of sovereignty - However, as decided by SC in Aban Loyd Chiles Offshore Limited and Another v Union of India and Others 2008 Indlaw SC 664 s. 7(4) of the 1976 Act only provides for Union of India to have sovereign rights limited to exploration, exploitation, conservation and management of natural resources, both living and non-living, as well as for producing energy from tides, winds and currents, which could not be equated with rights of sovereignty over said areas, in Exclusive Economic Zone - Thus, India was entitled both under its Domestic Law and Public International Law to exercise rights of sovereignty upto 24 nautical miles from baseline on basis of which width of Territorial Waters was measured, it could exercise only sovereign rights within Exclusive Economic Zone for certain purposes - Therefore, incident of firing from Italian vessel on Indian shipping vessel having occurred within Contiguous Zone, Union of India was entitled to prosecute two Italian marines under criminal justice system prevalent in country - While, therefore, holding that State of Kerala had no jurisdiction to investigate into incident, till such time as it was proved that provisions of art. 100 of the 1982 Act apply to facts of instant case - However, it was Union of India which had jurisdiction to proceed with investigation and trial of Petitioner Nos.2 and 3 in writ petition - Hence, Union of India was directed, in consultation with Chief Justice of India, to set up a Special Court to try instant case and to dispose of same in accordance with provisions of the 1976 Act, IPC, CrPC and most importantly, provisions of the 1982 Act, where there was no conflict between domestic law and the 1982 Act - Thus, pending proceedings before Chief Judicial Magistrate should be transferred to Special Court to be constituted in terms of judgment and same should be disposed of expeditiously - Petition disposed of.

J. Chelameswar, J.

(B)Criminal - International - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Territorial Waters, Continental Shelf, Exclusive Economic Zone and Other Maritime Zones Act, 1976, s. 7(4) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 4(1)(2) - Suppression of Unlawful Acts Against Safety of Maritime Navigation and Fixed Platforms on Continental Shelf Act, 2002, s. 3 - Maritime Zones Act, 1976 - United Nations Convention on Law of the Sea 1982 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 188 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Territorial jurisdiction - Sovereign rights - Maintainability - Whether Single Judge was justified in holding that sovereignty of India would extend to Exclusive Economic Zone, which extends to 200 nautical miles from baseline of coast of State of Kerala - Held, Parliament always asserted its authority to make laws, which were applicable to persons, who were not corporeally present within territory of India (whether or not they were citizens) when such persons commit acts which affect legitimate interests of this country - In furtherance of such assertion and in order to facilitate prosecution of offenders contemplated u/s. 4(1)(2) IPC, s. 188 CrPC prescribes jurisdiction to deal with such offences - Such assertion was not peculiar to India, but was made by various other countries - Petition disposed of.
