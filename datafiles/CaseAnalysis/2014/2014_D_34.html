State of Punjab and Others v Dhanjit Singh Sandhu

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 2014 SC 3004; 2014 (104) ALR 485; JT 2014 (4) SC 123; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 415; 2014 (124) RD 76; 2014(3) SCALE 622; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 1121

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Refund, Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995, Extension fee

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Punjab Housing Development Board Act, 1972 - Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development Act, 1995, s. 183 - Punjab Regional and Town Planning and Development (General) Rules, 1995, r. 13 - Extension fee - Refund - Legality - Respondent/allotte was allotted plot of land vide allotment letter - Respondent failed to complete construction of building within time framed - Authority/appellant demanded payment of non-construction fee/extension fee in order to avoid resumption of plot - Respondent paid extension fee and availed benefit of extension - Respondent sent a letter to Estate Officer demanding refund of extension fee and the same was rejected - Respondent filed petition before HC - HC quashed the notice and directed appellant to calculate extension fee as per r. 13 of the Rules - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by HC was sustainable -

Held, respondents accepted terms and conditions of allotment letter and possession were taken but they did not raise any construction - There was specific condition that non-construction of building would lead to resumption of said plot under provisions of Acts and Rules - When respondents did not raise construction on plot, demand was raised for extension fee in order to avoid resumption of plot by Authority, respondent paid extension fee - After availing benefit of extension on payment of extension fee, respondent sent letter to Estate Officer demanding refund of extension fee on basis of amended r. 13 of the Rules - Said demand was rejected by Estate Officer by passing reasoned order in compliance of directions of HC - Defaulting respondents of valuable plots could not be allowed to approbate and reprobate by first agreeing to abide by terms and conditions of allotment and later seeking to deny their liability as per agreed terms - It was settled proposition of law that once an order was passed, it was complied with, accepted by other party and derived benefit out of it, he could not challenge it on any ground - HC totally overlooked facts of present case and allowed the petition - Impugned order could not be sustained in law and was set aside - Appeals allowed.
