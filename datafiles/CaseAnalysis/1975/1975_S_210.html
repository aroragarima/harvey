Balwant Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 271; (1976) SCC (Cr) 43; (1976) 1 SCC 425; AIR 1976 SC 230; 1976 CRLJ 291; 1976 CrLR(SC) 20; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 684; 1976 UJ 66

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 354(3) - Murder - Death sentence - High Court not assigning special reasons - Whether HC was justified in awarding death sentence? - Awarding of sentence other than sentence of death is general rule now under new Code and only special reasons, that is to say, special facts and circumstances in a given case, will warrant passing of death sentence - Held, since there were no special reasons HC was not justified in confirming death sentence by relying on some decisions of Supreme Court given under old Code - Appeal allowed.
