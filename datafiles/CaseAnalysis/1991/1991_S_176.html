Smt. Meera Gupta v State Of West Bengal And Ors

Supreme Court of India

22 October 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K. Ramaswamy, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 319; (1992) 2 SCC 494; AIR 1992 SC 1567; AIR 1992 SCW 1665; 1991 (18) ALR 533; 1992 (1) CalLT 28; 1992 (1) CalLT(SC) 28; 1991 (3) CCC 712; JT 1991 (4) SC 162; 1992 (2) RCR(Rent) 348; 1991(2) SCALE 836; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 501; 1992 (1) UJ 93

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act 1976

Summary: Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 2-A, 2 (g), 2(q), 3, 4(11), 5, 6(1), 6(2), 8(1) and 33 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 39(b), 39(c) and 252 - Building was constructed before enactment of Ceiling Act and owner entered into an agreement with appellant, to sell vacant property - What happens to lands over which buildings are commenced after 'appointed day' of Ceiling Act and building progresses to completion thereafter ? - Held, 'land appurtenant' as defined in s. 2(g) - When related to any building in an area where there are building regulations, additional extent as permitted is based on principle of contiguity ? - Expression applies to buildings constructed of which commenced before 'appointed day', and was in progress on that day - It, no doubt, applies to buildings, constructed thereafter too - When court import this understanding to s. 4(9) two different results discernibly follow, based on commencement of construction - If construction of a building with a dwelling unit therein had begun after appointed day, then it is all same 'any other land' to be reckoned for calculation extent of vacant land held by a person - And if construction of a building with a dwelling unit therein on land had been completed or was in progress by and on appointed day, then it is not 'any other land' to be reckoned for calculating extent of vacant land held by a person - On 'appointed day', these lands were vacant lands, but not so thereafter because of surface change - Appeal allowed.
