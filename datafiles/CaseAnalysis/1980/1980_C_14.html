Charles. K. Skaria v Dr.C.Mathew

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 320; (1980) SCC (L&S) 305; (1980) 2 SCC 752; AIR 1980 SC 1230; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 71

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Selection - Illegal - Violation - Equality - State runs three medical colleges with post-graduate degree and diploma courses in two of its universities - Selection was made from candidates guided by Selection Committee - Notification inviting applications was published in Gazette - Candidates were considered on basis of merit, including military service, membership of Scheduled Castes and Tribes with 10% of competitive marks - Appellants ousted for reason that certificate of diploma had not been produced together with application for admission - Appellants Nos. 1 to 3/Petitioners contended that students of Medical College were placed at disadvantage and selection process was in violation of arts. 14 and 15 of Constitution - HC declared that there was no illegality and notification was validly issued - On appeal, Full Bench of HC quash selections made on basis of list - Hence instant appeal - Whether selection process was in violation of art.14 of Constitution - Held, there was nothing improper in selection committee - There was nothing objectionable with Govt. directive to selection committee, nor in communication to selection committee by university, nor in allotment of credit for diplomas although authentic copies of diplomas were not attached to application for admission - Appellant Nos. 2 & 3 were entitled to admission - Appellant No. 1 had no legal claim to seat and on consideration State and Medical Council directed to permit him to complete his course with addition of one more seat - Allotment of 2% open seats made for candidates from all Universities counter to constitutional directive of equal opportunity - Selection formula bad for violation of art.14 of Constitution, and it was possible to reconstruct - Hence, appellant Nos. 2 and 3 would be entitled to continue their course and appellant No. 1would move two universities - Order accordingly.
