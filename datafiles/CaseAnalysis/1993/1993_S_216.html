Sangappa Gurulingappa Sajjan v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

03 December 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 390; (1994) 4 SCC 145; 1994(1) SCALE 20

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1984, ss. 4 and 6(1) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Whether notification u/s. 4(1) shall stand lapsed since the declaration u/s. 6 was not published within three years from the date of the notification u/s. 4(1)? - Held, since the petitoner failed to raise the said plea at the first instance, it operates as constructive resjudicata u/s. 11 CPC, 1908 - Further, explanation I to s. 6(1) which provides the method of computation of the period referred to in the first proviso, excludes the period occcupied by the order of stay made by a court - Therefore, the period during which the order of dispossession granted by the High Court operated, should be excluded in computation of period of three years u/cls. 1 of first proviso - When so computed the declaration is perfectly valid - Petition dismissed.
