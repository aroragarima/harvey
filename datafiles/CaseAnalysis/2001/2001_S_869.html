Suresh Chand Jain v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 296; (2001) SCC (Cr) 377; (2001) 2 SCC 628; AIR 2001 SC 571; 2001 (1) CCR 54; 2001 (1) CGLJ 451; 2001 (3) CompLJ 78; 2001 (1) Crimes 171; 2001 CRLJ 954; 2001 (1) JCC 78; JT 2001 (2) SC 81; 2001 (1) KLT 623; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 335; 2001(1) SCALE 93; 2001 (1) SCJ 605; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 257; 2001 (1) SLT 364; 2001 (1) Supreme 129; 2001 (1) UC 202

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act

Summary: Criminal - Prize Chits and Money Circulation Schemes (Banning) Act, s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3), 202 and 482 - Complaint forwarded by magistrate to police for registering FIR and conducting investigation - Appellant who was arrayed as accused moved in revision but same was dismissed and also petition u/s. 482, CrPC was dismissed by High Court - Whether Magistrate has powers to forward complaint to police and direct registration of FIR and start investigation? - Complaint disclosing cognizable offences may well justify a Magistrate in sending complaint u/s. 156(3) to police for investigation - There is no reason why time of Magistrate should be wasted when primarily, duty to investigate in cases involving cognizable offences is with police - Held, Magistrate was correct in sending complaint to police for registration of FIR and investigation as per Chap. XV of Code - 'Tula Ram V. Kishore Singh', 1977 Indlaw SC 397 followed - Appeal dismissed.
