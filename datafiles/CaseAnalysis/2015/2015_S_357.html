A. C. Narayanan and another v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 69; AIR 2015 SC 1198; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 781; 2015 CRLJ 1434; 2015 (1) CTC 853; 2015 (3) JCC (NI) 185; JT 2015 (2) SC 211; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1043; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 823; 2015(1) SCALE 698; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1016; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 88

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delegate, Oath

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 204 - Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Cognizance of offence - Appellant, Vice-Chairman and Managing Director of the Company, collected various amounts from various persons in the form of loans and in consideration thereof issued post-dated cheques either in his personal capacity or as the signatory of the Company which got dishonoured - Respondent No. 2, power of attorney holder of six complainants, filed complaint case against the appellant u/ss. 138 and 142 of the Act before the Trial Court - Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate issued process against the appellant u/s 204 of the CrPC for the offences punishable u/ss. 138 and 142 of the Act - Appellant, being aggrieved filed an application for discharge/recall of process in each of the complaints - Trial Court dismissed the applications filed by the appellant - On appeal, applications were dismissed by the HC - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Magistrate had taken cognizance of the complaint without prima facie establishing the fact as to whether the Power of Attorney existed in first place and whether it was in order. Further, the complaint against the appellant was not preferred by the payee or the holder in due course and the statement on oath of the person who filed the complaint has also not stated that he filed the complaint having been instructed by the payee or holder in due course of the cheque. Since the complaint was not filed abiding with the provisions of the Act, it was not open to the Magistrate to take cognizance. Furthermore, except mentioning in the cause title there is no mention of, or a reference to the Power of Attorney in the body of the said complaint nor was it exhibited as part of the said complaint. The impugned judgment passed by HC and the order passed by the Additional Chief Metropolitan Magistrate are set aside and the proceedings in question against the appellant are quashed. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - In case the complaint was not filed abiding with the provisions of the Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881, it was not open to the Magistrate to take cognizance.
