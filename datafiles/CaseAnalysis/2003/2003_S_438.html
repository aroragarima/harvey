Pawan Kumar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 556; (2003) 11 SCC 241; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2015; AIR 2003 SC 2987; 2003 (3) CCR 78; 2003 (3) Crimes 238; 2003 (6) CRJ 431; 2003 CRLJ 3552; 2003 (2) JCC 1242; JT 2003 (6) SC 445; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 839; 2003(5) SCALE 580; 2003 (1) SCW 3671; 2003 (4) SLT 700; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 710; 2003 (5) Supreme 301; 2003 (2) UC 1263

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.302, 304, 34 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts.72, 136, 142 - No substantive evidence against accused - Whether powers conferred under art 136 can be exercised suo motu to meet ends of justice in favour of non-appealing accused - Held, when apex Court comes to conclusion that conviction of appealing as well as non-appealing accused both was unwarranted, further detention of non-appealing accused, by virtue of judgment rendered by HC upholding his conviction, being without any authority of law, infringes upon right to personal liberty guaranteed to citizen as enshrined under art 21 of Constitution - Where there is either a flagrant violation of mandatory provision of any statute or any provision of Constitution, it is not that this Court has a discretion to exercise its suo motu power, but a duty is enjoined upon it to exercise same by setting right illegality in judgment of HC, as it is well settled that illegality should not be allowed to be perpetuated and failure by this Court to interfere with same would amount to allowing illegality to be perpetuated - Further held, non-appealing accused is also entitled to be extended same benefit which is granted in favour of appellant.
