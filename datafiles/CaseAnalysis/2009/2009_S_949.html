Zenit Mataplast Private Limited v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1117; (2009) 10 SCC 388; 2009 (4) AWC 4114; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 93; JT 2009 (12) SC 240; 2009(12) SCALE 432; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 403

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (Disposal of Land) Regulations, 1975

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art.14 - Maharashtra Industrial Development Act, 1961 - Maharashtra Industrial Development Corporation (Disposal of Land) Regulations, 1975 - Interim relief - Grant of - Allotment of land - Industrial purpose - Present appeal against order of HC rejecting application for interim relief while admitting writ petition against allotment of land by respondent No.2 in favour of respondents nos.4 and 5 - Whether HC was justified in not granting interim relief in favour of appellant? - Grant of temporary injunction, is governed by three basic principles, i.e. prima facie case; balance of convenience; and irreparable injury - Further, where case of a party is based on fundamental rights guaranteed under Constitution and there is an apprehension that suit property may be developed in a manner that it acquires irretrievable situation, Court may grant relief even at a belated stage provided court is satisfied that applicant has not been negligent in pursuing case - Held, whole case of appellant is based on violation of art.14 of Constitution as according to appellant it has been a case of violation of equality clause enshrined in art.14 - Facts establish that respondent No.2 and Govt. proceeded in haste while considering application of respondent No.4 which tantamount to arbitrariness, thus violative of mandate of art.14 of Constitution - No development could take place therein for more than two years of taking possession of land - In such a fact-situation submission made on behalf of respondents that interim stay cannot be granted at a belated stage in preposterous - Interim stay granted - Appeal disposed of.
