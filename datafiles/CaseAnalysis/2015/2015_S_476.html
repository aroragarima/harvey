Ramgarh Farms and Industries Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

21 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 902; 2015 (10) ADJ 765; 2015 (110) ALR 770; JT 2016 (1) SC 277; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 522; 2015 (128) RD 534; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 1104

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Forest Act, 1927, Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, U.P. Zamidari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 4, 20 - U.P. Zamidari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960, s. 10(2) - Surplus land - Failure to retain land under ceiling - Justification - Appellant was recorded as Bhumidar of various lands in question - In proceedings initiated under 1960 Act , appellant was served with notice u/s. 10 of 1960 Act and Prescribed Authority declared area of land to be surplus land vide order and appellant was held to be entitled to certain amount of compensation - However, order was reviewed and it was held that appellant was held to be not entitled to any compensation as appellant was not Bhumidar and notice issued to it u/s. 10 of 1960 was erroneous - Further, lower court passed order in favor of appellant - Further, Ceiling Act was amended and permissible extent of land to be held was reduced to 18 acres - Notice was issued in favor of appellant and land in excess of appellant was declared as reserved forest area - Appellant filed appeal for correction of revenue record, payment of compensation and returning of land to appellant - Appellant contended that notification u/ss. 4, 20 of 1927 Act were nullity as statutory procedure was not complied - HC held that appellant is entitled to 18.03 acres of land in terms of order dt. 29-11-1976 passed in ceiling proceeding re-determining surplus area held by Company after amendment came into effect in Act - Hence, present appeal.

Held, appellant came in possession of land by virtue of lease dt. 25-1-1951 which came into effect from 1-7-1951. By said lease deed about 8874 bighas of land was transferred by way of permanent lease. Further, before lease was executed in 1951, 1950 Act was enacted and said Act came into effect from 24-1-1951 putting restriction on transfer of land and, further all transfers made after 1-7-1948 were held not to be recognised. Hence, neither lease deed nor any authority would have recognised appellant as tenure holder. Moreover, lease of land was granted to appellant on payment of rent and on terms and conditions contained in said lease deed. Further, appellant was permitted to retain 428 bighas of land spread in three villages while determining surplus land u/s. 12 of 1960 Act. Further, 1960- Act was amended with effect from 8-6-1973 whereby ceiling area of land was reduced from 40 acres to 18.03 acres. As result, fresh ceiling proceeding was initiated under amended Act. SC held that once it is held that appellant came in possession of land from ex-landlord on basis of lease treating appellant as tenant, appellant cannot be held to be tenure holder. Hence, without going into other questions, hence, appellant would be entitled to retain land to extent of 18 acres which is ceiling limit. Hence, prescribed authority is under obligation to give opportunity to appellant to exercise its option as to which land it opts to retain to extent of 18 acres and not more than that. Impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Party is entitled to retain land when it is within ceiling limit prescribed under amended statute and possession of land is because lease agreement treating party as tenant.
