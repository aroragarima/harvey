Chinnam Kameswara Rao & Ors. v State Of A.P. Rep. By Home Secretary

Supreme Court of India

10 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 46; (2013) 12 SCC 689; AIR 2013 SC 3602; 2013 CRLJ 1540; JT 2013 (2) SC 398; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 245; 2013(1) SCALE 643; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 324 r/w s. 34 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, ss. 2(a) - Murder - Common intention - Acquittal - Reversal - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302, 324 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted appellants - Aggrieved State filed appeal before HC, which was partly allowed reversing acquittal of appellants and convicted them for said offences and sentenced them to undergo imprisonment for life - However, acquittal of accused no. 4 was confirmed - Aggrieved appellants filed instant appeal u/s. 2(a) of the Act - Appellants contended that HC erred in fresh appraisal of evidence and convicting appellants with the aid of s. 34 of IPC and that evidence did not support the charge of murder - Whether impugned conviction judgment passed by HC reversing acquittal order of Trial Court was justified - Held, nothing prevent Appellate Court from reversing view took by Trial Court and holding accused guilty - If view was not a reasonably possible view the Appellate Court was duty bound to interfere and prevent miscarriage of justice by suitably passing order by punishing offender - Mere omission of s. 34 of IPC from charge sheet did not ipso facto or ipso jure lead to any inference or presumption of prejudice having caused to accused in cases where conviction was recorded with the help of that provision - It was only if accused persons plead and satisfactorily demonstrate that prejudice had indeed resulted from the omission of a charge u/s. 34 of IPC that any such omission might assume importance - In instant case, there was no such prejudice caused - Absence of charge u/s. 34 of IPC did not affect the legality of conviction recorded by HC - Evidence on record sufficiently proved that appellants had confronted deceased and PW1 on the previous date which was defused with interference of PW3, a shopkeeper in vicinity who was, however, witnessed to threat extended by appellants to deceased of dire consequences - There was evidence to show that on the date of occurrence appellants were lying in wait near Reading Room for deceased - Appellants went behind Reading Room to fetch the stout sticks, which were hidden, to mount a surprise attack on deceased - That implied that appellants had made preparations for commission of offence and incident was premeditated as a sequel to confrontation that 2 parties had on previous date - Nature of injuries inflicted upon deceased on vital part of body resulting in fracture of skull, sufficient in ordinary course to cause death - There was no doubt that appellants shared common intention to kill deceased and that they had acted under a premeditated plan - Common intention might develop during course of commission of offence but fact that incident in instant case had a history behind it and that appellants had not only threatened the deceased previously but were lying in wait for his arrival at place of occurrence clearly showed that commission of offence was preconcerted - Trial Court erred in rejecting testimony of witnesses on minor contractions - Acquittal recorded by Trial Court was not a reasonably possible view - Impugned judgment passed by HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
