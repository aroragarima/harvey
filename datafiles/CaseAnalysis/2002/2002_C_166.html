Commissioner of Income Tax v Gujarat Electricity Board. (And Others Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

12 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1933; 2003 (181) CTR 28; [2003] 260 ITR 84; [2003] 129 TAXMAN 65

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss.143(1)(a), 260A - Appeals directed against orders of HC in dismissing appeals u/s.260A of Act - On ground that no substantial question of law arose - Whether Revenue was justifiable to issue intimation u/s.143 (1) (a) of Act after notice u/s.143(2) of Act - Held, that Legislature, intended that, where the summary procedure u/s.143 (1) has been adopted, there should be scope available for Revenue, either suo motu or at the instance of assessee to make a regular assessment u/s.143(2) - Regular assessment proceeding having been commenced u/s.143 (2) - No need for a summary proceeding u/s.143 (1) (a) - No infirmity in judgment of HC - Appeals disposed of.
