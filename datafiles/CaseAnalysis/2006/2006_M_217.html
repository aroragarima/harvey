Mineral Exploration Corporation Employees' Union v Mineral Exploration Corporation Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 796; (2006) 6 SCC 310; (2006) SCC (L&S) 1318; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1837; 2006 (6) AWC 6420; JT 2006 (7) SC 151; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1212; 2006(7) SCALE 374; 2006 (6) SCJ 567; 2006 (5) SLR 21; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 87

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fringe Benefits tax

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 12 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 226 and 227 - Whether,the appellant workmen who had completed a minimum of 8 years and maximum 20 years of service started demanding regular pay scales and their regularization in service? - Ample material was placed to show that the temporary/contingent employees have been doing the work of permanent nature and the temporary employees are required to do work which was used to be done by the skilled - Respondent/ Corporation has sufficient work and the financial condition of the corporation is also satisfactory - Employees engaged and continued for years together cannot be termed as temporary or casuals - Once an employee completes 240 days, he is deemed to be a permanent employee - The term contingent employee is not included in standing orders - Held, Tribunal is directed to scrutinize all the records already placed by the appellant-Union and also the records placed by the management and arrive at a conclusion in regard the authenticity of claim for regularization - Subject to the outcome of the enquiry, the respondent-Corporation should absorb permanently and regularize the services - Held, interim order shall be in operation - The appeals are disposed of.
