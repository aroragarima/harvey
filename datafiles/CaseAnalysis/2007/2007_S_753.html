Subhodkumar & Ors v Bhagwant Namdeorao Mehetre & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 62; (2007) 10 SCC 571; AIR 2007 SC 1324; 2007 (2) AWC 1937; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 863; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 44; JT 2007 (2) SC 523; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 1008; 2007 (146) PLR 53; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 855; 2007(2) SCALE 288; 2007 (3) SCJ 735; [2007] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Incompetent To Alienate

Summary: Whether on the facts and circumstances of the case the lower appellate court was right in holding that the issue of legal necessity even if decided in favour of defendant nos.1 to 5 (appellants herein) was not a "fact in issue" and was therefore not a relevant fact in a suit for possession? - Held, once it is found that the conveyance executed is true under which the plaintiffs were put in possession and later on disposed, in the suit for possession, in such an event, the issue of legal necessity becomes irrelevant - A mere declaration that transaction was imprudent or was not for legal necessity in such a suit cannot give any right to defendant nos.1 to 5 to get the demarcated portion of 2 acres 2 gunthas of land on the southern side without the said defendants taking appropriate proceedings in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
