State Government of Nct of Delhi v Sunil and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3131; (2001) SCC (Cr) 248; (2001) 1 SCC 652; AIR 2000 SCW 4398; 2001 (1) Crimes 176; 2001 CRLJ 504; 2000 (88) DLT 630; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 267; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 56; 2000(7) SCALE 692; 2000 (8) SLT 491; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 144; 2000 (7) Supreme 728

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 100(5) and 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 34, 302, 304 II, 364, 376 and 377 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - (A) Whether document prepared by Investigating Officer contemporaneous with such recovery must necessarily be attested by independent witnesses? - Held, there is no requirement either u/s. 27 of Evidence Act or u/s. 161 of CrPC, to obtain signature of independent witnesses on record in which statement of an accused is written that when recovery is effected pursuant to any statement made by accused document prepared by Investigating Officer contemporaneous with such recovery must necessarily be attested by independent witnesses - Such statement leads to recovery of any article it is open to Investigating Officer to take signature of any person present at that time, on document prepared for such recovery - If no witnesses was present or if no person had agreed to affix his signature on document, it is difficult to lay down, as a proposition of law, that document so prepared by police officer must be treated as tainted and recovery evidence unreliable - (B) Whether High Court erred in interfering with conclusion made by Trial Court in fact and circumstances of present case? - Further held, Trial Court had come to correct conclusion that two respondents were rapists - Reasons adopted by HC for such interference are very tenuous - Rapists cannot disclaim knowledge that acts done by them on a little infant of such a tender age were likely to cause its death - They cannot escape conviction from offence of culpable homicide not amounting to murder - Appeal disposed of.
