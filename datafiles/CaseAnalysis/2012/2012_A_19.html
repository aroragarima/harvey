Ajay Maken v Adesh Kumar Gupta and another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 536; (2013) 3 SCC 489; 2013 (2) ALD(SC) 122; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 615; 2013 (3) CTC 317; 2012 (195) DLT 565; JT 2013 (1) SC 587; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 165; 2012(12) SCALE 173; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 192

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Contesting Candidate, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Any Other Candidate

Summary: Chelameswar, J.

Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 101, 117, 123,127,81,82,83,84,86,99 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r.11 - Corrupt Practices - Dismissal - Rejection of Petition - Dismissed - Sustainability - Appellant was declared elected in election held in 2009, challenging election of appellant, voter of constituency filed election petition in HC, challenging his election on ground of commission of corrupt practices u/ss. 123 (1),(2),(5),(6),(7), 127(a) of Act, impleading only returning officer - Appellant filed interlocutory application invoking O. 7 r. 11 of CPC praying that election petition be dismissing in compliance with mandate contained in s. 86 of Act which stipulates HC, to dismiss election petition which does not comply provision of s. 81 or s. 82 or s. 117 of Act - HC, dismissed application of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that petition was liable to be rejected on ground of non-compliance with s. 81(3) and election petition did not reveal complete cause of action and does not contain all material facts and another candidate who contested election and who was necessary party as per s. 82 of Act - Whether election petition was liable to be dismissed or HC, was justified in rejecting application of appellant - Held, it could be seen that two propositions of law were settled by this Court, firstly, when an election petition was accompanied by annexures, whose content was completely described in election petition, failure to serve a copy of such an annexure along with copy of election petition on a respondent to election petition does not render copy served on respondent anything other than a true copy of election petition - Secondly, even in a case where content of annexure was not fully described in election petition, non-supply of such annexure along with copy of election petition to respondent does not violate mandate of s. 81(3) in those cases where annexure was only sought to be used as evidence of some allegation contained in election petition - Hence, Judge, held that election petition could not be dismissed on ground that another contestant was not made a party, but, in so far as question whether election petition was required to be dismissed on ground that copy served on appellant was not true copy of original within meaning of s. 81(3), matter to HC for disposal in accordance with law and in light of this judgment - Petition disposed of.

Altamas Kabir. J

Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 101, 117, 123,127,81,82,83,84,86,99 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r.11 - Corrupt Practices - Dismissal - Rejection of Petition - Dismissed - Sustainability - Whether election petition was liable to be dismissed or HC, was justified in rejecting application of appellant - Held, Considering fact that s. 83(1)(b) requires an Election Petition to contain full particulars of any corrupt practice alleged by petitioner, could a document which contains allegations of corrupt practice against a candidate against whom no allegation was made in Election Petition itself, be deemed to be a part of Election Petition, in order to apply decisions of this Court, to facts of this case, it would be necessary that some allegation of corrupt practice would have to be made in Election Petition itself against a person against whom allegations of corrupt practice may separately have been made - Hence, Judge was of view, that in absence of any such allegation in Petition, provisions of cl. (b) of S. 82 would not be attracted - Further non-impleadment of another candidate against whom there were no allegations in Election Petition was not fatal to Election Petition and matter was required to be re-examined by HC - Petition disposed of.
