State Bank of Travancore v A. K. Soumini and others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1793; (2003) 7 SCC 243

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Promotion - Minimum qualifying marks - Respondent was employed as clerk/typist in appellant/Bank - Written test and viva voce was contacted by bank for purpose of promotion - In viva voce candidate was required to obtain 6 1/2 marks to qualify for selection but respondent scored only 5 marks on which respondent was not selected for promotion - Aggrieved, respondent filed petition before HC which was allowed and held that prescription of minimum marks for viva voce introduced arbitrariness in process of selection and was not valid - Whether minimum qualifying marks can be prescribed for viva voce in matter of promotion of clerk/typist to Junior Management Grade in Bank.

Held, appellant submitted that prescription of minimum marks for interview is permissible and has placed reliance on decisions of this Court in Mehmood Alam Tariq case wherein this Court has upheld provisions prescribing minimum marks for interview in a selection. In view of decisions, requirement of 6 1/2 marks for qualifying in viva voce as prescribed in promotion policy of Bank cannot be held to be invalid and judgment of HC taking a contrary view cannot be upheld. Court, however, cannot lose sight of fact that in view of decision of HC in her favour whereby it has been directed that respondent should be treated as selected if aggregate total marks both in written and oral tests exceed that of anyone who is now selected, respondent did not appear in subsequent tests. She should not be made to suffer on account of the pendency of appeal in this Court for more than 10 years. Court, therefore, consider it appropriate in interest of justice to direct that decision of this Court reversing view of HC with regard to validity of requirement of minimum marks for qualifying in viva voce should not operate to prejudice of respondent and she should be given benefit of decision of HC. Appeal allowed.
