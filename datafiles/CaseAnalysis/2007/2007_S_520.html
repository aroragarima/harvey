Messrs Oriental Select Granite Private Limited v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1301; (2007) 14 SCC 73; 2007(14) SCALE 117; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 85

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Granite, Concession Rules, Mineral Concession, Minor Mineral Concession, Quarrying Operation, Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1969

Summary: Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1969; Karnataka Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1994 - By the impugned demand notice, petitioners were asked to pay the value of the granite excavated and transported by them during the period August 27, 1993 to January 18, 1996 - Held, petitioners, like all other lessees in Alankar Granites, carried on their quarrying operations on the basis of the interim order passed by this Court and the petitioners are fully covered by the decisions in Alankar Granites and Karnataka Rare Earth - It is basic and elementary that once the same issues between the same or similarly placed parties are decided by this Court no order or direction passed by the High Court contrary to or at variance with the decision of this Court would survive - Petitions dismissed.
