Vice-Chancellor, University of Allahabad and Others v Dr. Anand Prakash Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2036; (1997) 10 SCC 264; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1265; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 275; 1997(1) SCALE 301; 1997 (2) SLJ 97; 1997 (2) SLR 356; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 175; 1997 (1) UJ 778

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Education - Uttar Pradesh State Universities Act, 1973, s. 3(1) - Appointment of Reader in University - Right of the Chancellor (Governor) to issue direction for appointment - Held, since process of selection starts by screening of candidates eligible for consideration by Selection Committee, process could be said to have started prior to coming into force of Act - All appointments should be made in accordance with law applicable prior to coming into force of Act - It is settled law that the process of selection must be in accordance with the law existing as on the date of selection - Keeping a candidate in the waiting list does not confer any vested right in his favour much less indefeasible right - The appropriate appointing authority is not obliged to fill up the vacancies or to appoint any candidate/candidates waiting in the list to any resultant vacancy, due to the operation of law under the Act - The Vice-Chancellor, therefore, was obliged under the Act and vested with duty and right in taking action to have the vacancies notified applying s. 3(1) of the Act for recruitment in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
