S. C. Cambatta and Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 331; AIR 1961 SC 1010; [1961] 41 ITR 500; 1961 (2) SCJ 265; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 805

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(1), 66(2) - Goodwill - - Assessment - Justifiability - Appellants a subsidiary Company called Eros Theatre and Restaurant, Ltd - Paid up capital of subsidiary Company was Rs. 7,91,100 divided into 7,911 shares of Rs. 100 each 7,901 shares were allotted to subsidiary Company as consideration for assets, goodwill stock-in-trade and book debts which were taken over by subsidiary Company - Goodwill was not shown separately in appellants' account books, but only in accounts of subsidiary Company, accounting periods, each ending respectively on December 31, beginning with year, 1943 and ending with year, 1945 - Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax/respondent passed order against subsidiary Company - Subsidiary Company filed petition u/ss. 66(1), 66(2) r/w s. 21 of 1940, Act before Tribunal - Tribunal came to conclusion that no goodwill had been acquired by business of Theatre as such, and that whatever goodwill there was, related to site and building itself and dismissed the same - Aggrieved subsidiary Company filed petition before HC and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether goodwill of Eros Theatre and Restaurant Ltd, was calculated in accordance with law - Held, Tribunal seemed to have taken into account only value of leasehold of site to subsidiary Company, and rejected other considerations which go to make up goodwill of business - Goodwill of business depends upon a variety of circumstances or combination of them - Location, service, standing of business, honesty of those who run it, and lack of competition and many other factors go individually or together to make up goodwill, though locality always plays considerable part - Shift locality, and goodwill might be lost - At same time, locality was not everything - Power to attract custom depends on one or more of other factors as well - In case of theatre or restaurant, what was catered, how service was run and what competition was, contribute also to goodwill - It was manifest that matter of goodwill needs to be considered in a much broader way than what Tribunal had done - A question of law did arise in case, HC should have directed Tribunal to state a case upon it - HC would frame a suitable question, and ask for statement of case from Tribunal, and decide question in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
