Abdul Rajak Murtaja Dafedar v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 130; (1969) 2 SCC 234; AIR 1970 SC 283; 1970 CRLJ 373; 1969 MLJ 862; [1970] 1 S.C.R. 551

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 26 and 45 - CrPC, 1898, s. 164 - Evidence - (A) Accused kept in remand for fifteen days - Then in jail custody for three days - Produced before executive Magistrate for recording confession - Sent back to jail after preliminary questioning and a warning - And confession recorded on next day - Nature of confession - (B) Dog Tracking evidence - Admissibility - Confession, held, was voluntary as accused had spent four days in judicial custody and he was not under influence of investigating agency for at least four days - Opinion that in the present state of scientific knowledge evidence of dog tracking, even if admissible, is not ordinarily of much weight (But not expressed any conclusive opinion or lay down any general rule with regard to tracker dog evidence or its significance or its admissibility ) - Appeal Dismissed
