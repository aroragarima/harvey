Jagarnath Singh v B. S. Ramaswamy

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 258; AIR 1966 SC 849; 1966 CRLJ 697; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 885

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Indian Electricity Act, 1910, ss. 39 and 44 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 379 and 201- Indian Electricity Rules, 1956, rr. 56(2) and 138(b) - Power theft - Meter tampering - Direct and circumstantial evidence - Presumption - (A) Conviction u/s. 39 Electricity Act r/w 379 IPC, 1860 and s. 44 of Electricity Act r/w 138(b) of rules - Whether sustainable? - Held, prosecution failed to prove that appellant abstracted, consumed or used any energy without paying for it - Appellant charged with throwing acid on meter and attempted to remove evidence of tampering, but this charge was not pressed in High Court - Benefit of doubt to be given, conviction u/s. 39 r/w 379 IPC, 1860 set aside - Conviction u/s. 44 r/w 138(b) -Rules affirmed - (B) Tampering of meter - Attempt and destruction of evidence be throwing acid in meter - Further held, preventing authorities to inspect meter and to prevent disclosure of evidence - Reason to believe existed that checking of meter, if permitted by appellant, would have disclosed that after tampering meter was recording less than units actually consumed, and was used as an artificial means for abstraction of unrecorded energy - Conviction u/s. 201 of IPC, 1860, upheld - Appeal No 76 of 1963 partly allowed - Appeal No 130 of 1963 dismissed.
