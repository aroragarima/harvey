State Of Madhya Pradesh v Jiyalal

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1484; (2009) 15 SCC 72; AIR 2010 SC 1451; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 2846; JT 2009 (12) SC 425; 2009(11) SCALE 406; [2009] 12 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 7, 13(1)(d)(ii), 13(2) and 19 - Sanction - Special Judge convicted the Respondent for offences u/ss. 7 and 13(1)(d)(ii) r/w s. 13(2) of the Act - On appeal, HC set aside the conviction and the sentence on the ground that the requisite sanction order was not obtained properly - Whether HC was justified in interfering with the 'finding, sentence or order passed by a Special Judge' under the Act? - Held, no - In the absence finding that a serious failure of justice was caused to the respondent it was not correct for the HC to set aside the conviction and sentence given by the Special Judge - HC was also not justified to hold that the District Magistrate who had passed the sanction order should have been subsequently examined as a witness by the prosecution in order to prove the same - Judgment of HC set aside - HC directed to consider appeal filed by the respondent on merits - Appeal disposed of.
