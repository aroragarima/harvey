Gumpha v Jaibai

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1649; (1994) 2 SCC 511; 1995 (2) Bom.C.R. 24; 1994 (1) CCC 534; 1994 (1) HLR 292; JT 1994 (1) SC 535; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 86; 1994(1) SCALE 578; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 901; 1994 (2) UJ 121

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Rights Of Inheritance, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14(1), (2) and 30 - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Whether life estate of a widow under a will executed in 1941 gets enlarged into an absolute estate u/s. 14(1) of Hindu Succession Act, 1956 if succession opened after death of testator in 1958? - Held, possession must be founded on some basis which may be acceptable in law and right that she acquires u/s. 14 depends on nature of possession she enjoyed over property - Consequently if a female Hindu acquires possession after enforcement of Succession Act and that possession was traceable to an instrument or a document described in s. 14(2) then she could not get higher right than what is stipulated in document itself - Purpose and legislative intention which surfaces from a combined reading of ss. 14(1) and 14(2) is that it does not enlarge or enhance right which she gets under a will giving her a limited estate - Appeal dismissed.
