Shashikant Laxman Kale And Anr. v Union Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 894; (1990) 4 SCC 366; (1990) SCC (Tax) 428; AIR 1990 SC 2114; 1990 (86) CTR 201; [1990] 185 ITR 104; JT 1990 (3) SC 267; 1990(2) SCALE 71; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 441; 1990 TAXLR 877; [1990] 52 TAXMAN 352

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Classification For Taxation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 10(10 C) - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Classification - Discrimination between employees of public sector and private sector - For conferring benefit contemplated by cl. (10 C) - Constitutionality - (B) Interpretation of statutes - (1) Determination of object or purpose of statute - Circumstances necessitating enactment can be looked into - (C) Taxation statute - Determination of true object - Catchphrase used as populist measure to describe some provisions in Bill in explanatory memorandum - Held, (A) Not discriminatory nor arbitrary, it achieves object of streamlining public sector by curing it of one of its ailments of over staffing - (B) For this, statement of objects and reasons can be considered - (C) Not determinative and it cannot also camouflage true object - Petition dismissed.
