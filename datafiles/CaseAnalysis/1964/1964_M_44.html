Â Kultar Singh v Mukhtiar Singh

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 398; AIR 1965 SC 141; 1965 (1) SCJ 565; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 790

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 123(3) - Whether by publishing and distributing a poster in furtherance of his election, appellant, committed a corrupt practice u/s. 123(3)? - Held, democratic process should thrive and succeed, it is of utmost importance that elections to Parliament and different legislative bodies must be free from unhealthy influence of appeals to religion, race, caste, community or language - S. 123(3) wisely provides a check on this undesirable development by providing that an appeal to any of these factors made in furtherance of candidature of any candidate as therein prescribed would constitute a corrupt practice and would render election of said candidate void - In deciding question as to whether corrupt practice has been committed u/s. 123(3), care must be taken to consider impugned speech or appeal carefully and always in light of relevant political controversy - Appeal allowed.
