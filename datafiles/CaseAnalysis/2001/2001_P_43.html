A.P.State Fed. of Coop.Spinning Mills v P.V. Swaminathan

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 634; (2001) 10 SCC 83; 2001 (2) AWC 1291; 2001 (2) CLR 25; 2001 (89) FLR 332; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 427; JT 2001 (3) SC 530; 2001 (1) LLJ 1341; 2001 (6) SLR 570; 2001 (4) Supreme 215

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12 and 226 - Termination - Respondent had been appointed as General Manager (Finance) under State Federation Cooperative Spinning Mills Ltd., while he was continuing as such, his services stood terminated - Learned Single Judge dismissed the Writ Petition but was allowed by Division Bench of High Court - Division Bench came to the conclusion that a Writ would lie against State Federation of Co-operative Spinning Mills Ltd. as it would be a 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 - Whether conclusion drawn by Division Bench of HC was justified or not? - Legal position is fairly well settled that an order of termination of a temporary employee or probationer or even a tenure employee, simpliciter without casting any stigma may not be interfered with by Court - But the Court is not debarred from looking to the attendant circumstances - If the Court comes to a conclusion that the order was, in fact, the motive, then obviously the order would not be interbred with, but if Court comes to a conclusion that the so called inefficiency was the real foundation for passing of order of termination, then obviously such an order would be held to be penal in nature and must be interfered with since the appropriate procedure has not been followed - In fact, the letter of Commr. for Handlooms and Director of Handlooms and Textiles was the foundation of the employer to terminate the services and as such the Division Bench of HC was justified in holding that the order of termination is based upon misconduct, though on the face of it, it is innocuous in nature - Held, therefore Court does not find any infirmity with the said conclusion of the Division Bench of HC requiring Court's interference - More so, the respondent cannot seek enforcement of re-instatement by way of a Mandamus but all the same he would be entitled to all his benefits flowing from the terms of appointment - Appeal disposed of.
