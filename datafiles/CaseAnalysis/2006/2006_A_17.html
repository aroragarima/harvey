State of Maharashtra and Others v A.P. Paper Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 231; (2006) 4 SCC 209; AIR 2006 SC 1788; 2006 (2) AWC 2011; 2006 (2) BC 245; 2006 (3) Bom.C.R. 540; 2007 (4) CompLJ 63; 2006 (2) CTC 619; JT 2006 (4) SC 271; 2006 (3) RLW 2038; 2006(4) SCALE 28; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 719

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Govt. Tender - Tender Notice for sale of Bamboo Units at various places in Maharashtra - Terms and conditions of tender notice - Interpretation thereof - Tenders has to be filled in prescribed forms, to be submitted on a specified date as mentioned in tender notice - On such specified date itself sealed tenders were to be opened and earnest money was to be deposited - Within 30 days of specified date final sale result was to be declared for purpose of getting approval of competent authority - Offer under tender was valid for a period of 45 days from specified date - As per respondent case appellant did not declared final sale result within 30 days from specified date, and thus by sending a telegram it withdrew from tender - On withdrawal of tender earnest money deposited was forfeited by appellants - Respondent preferred writ petition before High Court - HC accepted petition and interpreted various conditions of tender notice - HC held that respondent had withdrawn bid before conclusion of contract and, therefore, was entitled to refund of earnest money deposited which it had paid to present appellants at time of making its offer in form of bid to tender notice - Interpretation made by HC whether proper? - Respondent withdrew from bid on 30th day as appellant failed to declare final sale result by then, but it withdrew before expiry of 45 days - Cl. 7 of tender notice perused - Held, bid once made remains operative for a period of 45 days and thus decision could be taken on bid on or before expiry of 45 days and as withdrawal was made before 45 days, forfeiture of earnest money is justified - Interpretation by HC on conditions of tender notice not proper - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
