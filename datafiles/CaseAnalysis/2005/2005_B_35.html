Balasaria Construction (Private) Limited v Hanuman Seva Trust and others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1827; (2006) 5 SCC 662

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.151, O.7 r.11 - Dismissal of suit - Conflict of judgments - Plaintiff/Respondents had filed suit before Trial Court for declaration that acquisition of suit property by defendants as illegal and void - Appellant/ Defendant No.18 instead of filing written statement, filed application under O.7 r.11 r/w s.151 of CPC seeking rejection of plaint which was termed as dismissal of suit - Trial Court dismissed application, aggrieved against which appellant filed revision application before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, appellant relies upon judgment of Court in âN.V.Srinivasa Murthy case for proposition that plaint could be rejected if suit is ex facie barred by limitation. Bench did not consider import of words âbarred by lawâ occurring in O.7 r.11 (d) of CPC exhaustively. This point has been specifically dealt with by different HCs wherein it has been held that plaint under O.7 r.11 (d) of CPC cannot be rejected on ground that it is barred by limitation. According to these judgments suit has to be barred by provision of law to come within meaning of O.7 r.11 of CPC. Contrary view has been taken in âJugolinija Rajia Jugoslavija caseâ, âNational Insurance Co. caseâ and âJ. Patel and Co. caseâ. Respondent also relied upon recent judgment of Court in âPopat and Kotecha Property caseâ decided on 29-8-2005 in support of proposition that O.7 r.11 (d) of CPC would not be applicable in case where question has to be decided on basis of fact that suit was barred by limitation. Keeping in view importance of question and conflict of opinion, Court deems it appropriate to direct that matter be placed before larger Bench. Registry is directed to place papers before Chief Justice for necessary orders. Order accordingly.
