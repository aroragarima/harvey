Y.Narayana Chetty and Another v Income Tax Officer, Nellore and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 80; AIR 1959 SC 213; [1959] 35 ITR 388; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 109; 1959 SCJ 250; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 189; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 189

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(2), 3, 22, 23 and 34 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 3(42) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Income Tax Rules, r. 6 b - (A) Whether notice prescribed under s. 34 of Income Tax Act is a mere procedural requirement - Held, proper issuance of notice under s. 34 of Income Tax Act is a mandatory requirement? - Non - Issuance or invalid issue of notice will render proceedings taken by ITO illegal and void - (1) Whether it is necessary for a notice under s. 34 to be served on all individual partners in case of a registered firm in order to be a valid notice? - Further held, a notice under s. 34 served on a (registered) firm itself and subsequent proceedings taken are not invalid on ground that notices have not been served on individual partners - A firm is treated as an assessee under ss. 3, 22 and 23 of Income Tax Act - Under s. 63(2) a notice or requisition may, in case of a firm, be addressed to any member of firm - No objections by partners to whom a notice under s. 34 has not been served would disqualify them from contending in writ proceedings that assessment proceedings were invalid on ground that notices were not served on individual partners proceedings subsequent to a notice under s. 34 served on a registered firm instead of individual partners are valid - Such proceedings are not invalid on ground that notices have not been served on individual partners - (B) Whether r. 6 B is inconsistent with s. 23(4) of Income Tax Act and thus invalid as ultra vires - Further held, while s. 23(4) provides for cancellation of a firm's registration as a punitive measure for non-compliance with requirements of law; r. 6 B deals with cases where Income-tax officer is satisfied that a certificate of registration has been granted under rr. 4 and 6 A - Without there being a genuine firm in existence - Rule has been made to authorize ITO to review his own decision in matter of registration of firm - R. 6 B is not inconsistent with s. 23(4) - Also, absence of provision for appeal does not invalidate rule - (C) Whether challenge to ITO in making orders of fresh assessment under s. 34 as irregular and illogical raise any question of jurisdiction under art. 226 - Further held, such grounds relate to merits of order and do not raise any question of jurisdiction under art. 226 - Appeal dismissed.
