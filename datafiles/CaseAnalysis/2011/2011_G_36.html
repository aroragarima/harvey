G. Reddeiah v Government of Andhra Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 629; (2012) 2 SCC 389; JT 2011 (10) SC 285; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 157; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 219; 2011(10) SCALE 224; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 454

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: A.P. Forest Act, 1967, Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot Leggers, Dacoits, Drug Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers Act, 1986, A.P. Sandal Wood and Red Sanders Transit Rules, 1969

Summary: Criminal - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Practice & Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Boot Leggers, Dacoits, Drug Offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers Act, 1986, ss. 3(1), 2(a), 2(g),3,2(b) - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 378, 379 - Andhra Pradesh Forest Act, 1967, ss. 20(1)(c)(iii)(ii) (vi) (x), s. 29(2)(b), 29A(1), 29 - Order of Detention - Validity - Detenue was allegedly found to be involved in felling, transporting, smuggling of red-sanders trees and committing theft of forest wealth in as many as 8 times within a period of 1 year - Detenue was arrested and order of detention was served by Collector and District Magistrate u/ss. 3(1) and 2(a) & (b) of the 1986 Act stating that activities of detenue were dangerous to forest wealth and forest eco-system and were prejudicial to maintenance of public order - Appellant, who was brother-in-law of detenue, challenged detention order before HC - HC dismissed petition holding that order of detention was not illegal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether detention order issued under the 1986 Act and as affirmed by HC was valid - Held, grounds of detention clearly indicate that detenue had been indulging in various activities in felling and smuggling red-sanders and he was habitually committing same and was unmindful of wastage of national forest wealth and public order - Conclusion of Detaining Authority that by invocation of normal procedure, activities of detenue could not be controlled was acceptable was satisfied - Further, detaining authority was well within its powers in passing impugned order of detention - Court also agreed with reasoning of HC which, by a detailed judgment, upheld order of detention - Court also satisfied that detenue was afforded adequate opportunity at every stage and there was no violation of any of safeguards - Appeal dismissed.
