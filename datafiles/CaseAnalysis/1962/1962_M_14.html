Madan Gopal v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 55; AIR 1963 SC 531; 1964 (1) LLJ 68; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 716

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - (A) Whether an enquiry made by Officer made with object of ascertaining whether disciplinary action should be taken against Govt. servant can be considered as an enquiry made for purpose of dismissal? - Held, it can not be considered so - It is an enquiry for purpose of taking punitive action including dismissal or removal from service if servant is found to have committed misdemeanor charged against him - (B) Whether protection u/art. 311(2) applies to permanent as well as temporary servant? - Further held, where employment of a temporary Govt. servant, even though liable to be terminated by notice of one month without assigning any reason is not so terminated, but instead superior Officer chooses to hold an enquiry into his alleged misconduct, termination of service is by way of punishment, because it puts a stigma on his competence and thus affects his future career - In such a case he is entitled to protection of art. 311(2) of Constitution - Protection of art. 311(2) of Constitution applies as much to a temporary public servant as to permanent public servants - Appeal allowed.
