State of Haryana and Others v Jasmer Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1679; (1996) 11 SCC 77; (1997) SCC (L&S) 210; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 649; AIR 1997 SC 1788; 1997 (75) FLR 776; JT 1996 (10) SC 876; 1997 (2) LLJ 667; 1997 (1) LLN 1023; 1996(8) SCALE 263; 1997 (1) SLJ 129; 1997 (1) SLR 143; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 592; 1997 (1) Supreme 137; 1997 (1) UJ 238

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Equality in wages - Whether High Court granting to persons employed by State of Haryana on daily wages same pay as those holding regular posts in Govt. service is correct? - Held, daily-rated workmen who were before court in that case were entitled to be paid minimum wages admissible to such workmen as prescribed and not minimum in pay scale applicable to similar employees in regular service - Unless employer had decided to make such minimum in pay scale applicable to daily-rated workmen - Rated workers are not required to possess qualifications prescribed for regular workers, nor do they have to fulfill requirement relating to age at time of recruitment - They are not selected in manner in which regular employees are selected - There are also other provisions relating to regular service such as liability of a member of service to be transferred, and his being subject to disciplinary jurisdiction of authorities as prescribed, which daily-rated workmen are not subjected to - HC was, therefore, not right in directing that respondents should be paid same salary and allowances as are being paid to regular employees holding similar posts with effect from dates when respondents were employed - If a minimum wage is prescribed for such workers, respondents would be entitled to it if it is more than what they are being paid - Appeal allowed.
