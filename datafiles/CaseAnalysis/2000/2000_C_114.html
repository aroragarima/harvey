Collector of Central Excise, Baroda v United Phosphorus Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 353; (2000) 4 SCC 18; AIR 2000 SC 3424; 2000 (90) ECR 6; 2000 (117) E.L.T. 529; 2001 (2) G.L.R. 1717; JT 2000 (4) SC 323; 2000 (38) RLT 239; 2000(3) SCALE 120; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 1062; 2000 (3) SLT 696; 2000 (3) Supreme 251

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1986, sch. 1, Heading 3808.10 - Excise Duty - Intermediate products are not excisable unless they taken to market and bought and sold - No facility existed for separation of three intermediate products including Mercuric Acetate which came out while manufacturing of insecticides etc. - Not in marketable form but mentioning of three items as 'goods' in Dictionary and excise tariff and Duty drawback Rules - If bring them to excisable category - Held, intermediate products, not excisable - Does not satisfy test of marketability - Appeal dismissed.
