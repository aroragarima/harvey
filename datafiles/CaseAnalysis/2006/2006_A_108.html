Muni Suvrat-Swami Jain S.M.P. Sangh v Arun Nathuram Gaikwad and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 682; (2006) 8 SCC 590; AIR 2007 SC 38; 2007 (3) Bom.C.R. 293; JT 2006 (9) SC 156; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 175; 2006(10) SCALE 122; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 118

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1988, s. 354A - Mumbai Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 337, 351, 351(2) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - MTP Act, ss. 44, 99 - Illegal Construction - HC directed Municipal Corporation to demolish entire illegal and unauthorized construction carried on by respondent Nos 3 -17 - Appeal against - Held, no notice under provisions of s.351 has been issued by Municipal Commissioner in this matter against appellant - If sufficient cause is not shown it is left to Commissioner's discretion whether or not to demolish unauthorized construction and, HC cannot impede exercise of that discretion by issuance of a mandatory order - Directed Commissioner to decide question as to whether he should pass an order for demolition or not - Appeal allowed.
