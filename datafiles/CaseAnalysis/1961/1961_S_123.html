Payare Lal v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1962 SC 690; 1962 CRLJ 688; 1962 (1) LLJ 637; 1962 (2) SCJ 492; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 350 and 537 - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(2) - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 1952(Act XLVI of 1952),s. 8 - (A) Whether conviction by successor judge u/s.5(2) of Prevention of Corruption Act, without calling witnesses is valid? - Held, no - U/s 350 of CrPC, 1973, successor special judge has no authority to proceed with trial of case from stage at which his predecessor left - Conviction could not be supported, as he had not himself heard evidence - Proceedings before successor judge is incompetent - Appeal allowed.
