Coastal Paper Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Visakhapatnam

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 526; (2015) 10 SCC 664; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 153; JT 2015 (6) SC 404; 2015(8) SCALE 64; 2016(8) SCALE 64; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 486

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Rule Of Construction, Finance Bill, 1984

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Use of Non-conventional raw material - Claim of exemption - Justification - Appellant assessee was paper mill which engaged in manufacture of paper for which he uses various conventional raw materials and also non-conventional raw materials, namely, waste gunny bags, jute waste etc. - Further, Govt. in order to encourage production of paper by use of non-conventional raw material issued notification dt. 1-3-1994 which assures concessional rate of duty at 5% for 'paper and paperboard or articles made from non-conventional material' on fulfilling certain conditions - Assessee stated that he is liable to pay concessional rate of excise duty as pulp of waste gunny bags or jute waste does not fall in any of materials mentioned in Notification - However, revenue held that pulp of waste gunny bags/jute waste is nothing but pulp of 'rags' and since notification in question disentitles benefit thereof if pulp is made from rags, assessee is not covered by said Notification - Further, Commission passed order in favor of assessee, however Tribunal reversed order passed by Commissioner and held hat assessee is not liable to claim benefit of notification in question - Whether pulp of waste gunny bags/jute waste is to be treated as pulp made from material 'rags'.

Held, all materials and notifications clearly suggest that Govt. itself distinguished between jute bags/gunny bags and rags and exemption was being extended to paper made from old jute/gunny bags. Further, SC held that pulp from waste of jute bags or gunny bags would not be covered by term 'rags' appearing in notification dt. 1-3-1994 as it could never be intention to exclude non-conventional material from benefit of aforesaid notification when that was precisely purpose for which notification in question was issued to encourage use of non-conventional material for purposes of manufacturing paper or paper products. Further, all books on subject uniformly define 'rag' or 'rag pulp' as one which is made from cotton waste or cotton textile material and revenue could not point out through any technical literature which even remotely suggests that jute gunny bags come under category of 'rags' in context of paper technology. SC held that Tribunal has simply brushed aside material on record with mere observation that it is not relevant and this approach of Tribunal cannot be justified. Hence, impugned order passed by Tribunal is set aside and order of Commissioner is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Assessee is eligible to avail exemption benefit provided under notification when goods used by assesee is exempt under notification.
