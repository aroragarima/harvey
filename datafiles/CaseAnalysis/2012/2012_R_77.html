Ritesh Sinha v The State Of Uttar Pradesh & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 524; (2013) 2 SCC 357; AIR 2013 SC 1132; 2013 CRLJ 1301; JT 2012 (12) SC 258; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 30; 2012(12) SCALE 10; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Deem, Criminal Procedure Act, 1977

Summary: Ranjana Prakash Desai

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 53 - Identification of Prisoners Act, 1920, SS. 2,5,6,7 - Fraud - Recruitment - Voice Sample - Self-incriminating - Violation of Rights - Sustainability - Incharge of Company had lodged FIR against accused alleging that accused in connivance with appellant had collected money from people on pretext of recruiting them in police department - Police arrested accused and recovered mobile from his possession and to verify whether recorded conversation was between accused and appellant approached Trial Court with application to summon appellant for recording voice sample for verification - Appellant approached HC, and filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC for quashing order, HC, rejected application - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that order passed by Trial Court violates his fundamental right of protection from self-incrimination as guaranteed u/art. 20(3) of Constitution - Further there was no provision in Code or in any other law which authorized police to make an application for an order directing accused to permit recording of his voice for voice sample test - Whether Trial Court and HC, were justified in summoning appellant for recording voice sample - Held, Judge was not inclined to give a narrow construction to provisions of Act and s. 53 of CrPC, Judicial note could be taken of fact that there was a great deal of technological advance in means of communication, criminals were using new methodology in committing crimes, use of landlines, mobile phones and voice over internet protocol (VoIP) in commission of crimes like kidnapping for ransom, extortion, blackmail and for terrorist activities was rampant - Therefore, in order to strengthen hands of investigating agencies, Judge, was inclined to give purposive interpretation to provisions of Act and s. 53 of CrPC, instead of giving a narrow interpretation to them. Judge, however, felt that Parliament needs to bring in more clarity and precision by amending Act and CrPC also needs to be suitably amended - Crime had changed its face and there were new challenges faced by investigating agency and it was necessary to note that many local amendments had been made in Act by several States - Technological and scientific advance in investigative process could be more effectively used if required amendments were introduced by Parliament which was necessary to strike a balance between need to preserve right against self-incrimination guaranteed u/art. 20(3) of Constitution and need to strengthen hands of investigating agency to bring criminals to book - Appeal dismissed.

Aftab Alam

Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 53 - Identification of Prisoners Act, 1920, SS. 2,5,6,7 - Fraud - Recruitment - Voice Sample - Self-incriminating - Violation of Rights - Sustainability - Whether Trial Court and HC, were justified in summoning appellant for recording voice sample - Held, s. 53 applies to a situation where examination of person of accused was likely to provide evidence as to commission of an offence, whether or not examination of person of accused would afford evidence as to commission of offence undoubtedly rests on satisfaction of police officer not below rank of sub-inspector, but, once police officer makes a request to registered medical practitioner for examination of person of accused, what other tests might be necessary in a particular case could only be decided by medical practitioner and not police officer referring accused to him - Hence, appellant, was right in his submission that any tests other than those expressly mentioned in Explanation could only be those which registered medical practitioner would think necessary in a particular case and further that in any event a registered medical practitioner could not take a voice sample - Appeal allowed.
