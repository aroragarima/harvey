Indian Bank v Manilal Govindji Khona

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 83; (2015) 3 SCC 712; AIR 2015 SC 1240; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 146; 2015 (109) ALR 692; 2015 (2) ALT(SC) 52; 2015 (1) BC 689; 2015 (1) CalLT(SC) 57; 2015 (1) DRTC 464; JT 2015 (2) SC 376; 2015 (6) MahLJ 1; 2015 (4) MPLJ 274; 2015(2) SCALE 85

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Aggrieved, Mortgaged Property

Summary: Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993, ss. 24, 31 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Auction as per HC direction - Jurisdiction - Respondent's mortgaged property was sold in public auction as per the direction of HC - Appellant lender bank purchased property through Court Receiver - Respondent moved application for rectification of recovery certificate with delay condonation application - DRT dismissed applications - DRAT dismissed respondent's appeal thereafter - However, HC, on respondent's writ petition, set aside orders of DRT and DRAT - Hence, instant appeal by lender bank.

Held, exercise of jurisdiction by the HC for giving the direction to the Court Receiver to sell the mortgaged property even after the DRT was established at Mumbai and in view of the fact that the proceedings before the HC were automatically transferred to it in view of s. 31 of the 1993 Act and therefore it was impermissible in law for the HC to direct the Court Receiver to sell the property of the respondent in public auction by executing the Court decree, which action of the Court Receiver is void ab initio in law. Further, the provisions of the 1963 Act are applicable to the proceedings of the DRT in view of s. 24 of 1993 Act and therefore, the provisions of s. 5 of the 1963 Act are applicable to the provisions of the said Act. The same has not been examined and considered by the DRT and the DRAT at the time of passing the impugned orders in the writ petitions. Therefore, the HC has rightly exercised its discretionary power keeping in view the rights of the respondent upon the immovable property involved in this case and it has condoned the delay in filing application and accordingly the orders of the DRT and the DRAT impugned in the writ petition are set aside by allowing the same. Appeal dismissed.
