M/S Atul Commodities Pvt. Ltd. & Ors v Commissioner Of Customs, Cochin-9

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 796; (2009) 5 SCC 46; 2009 (235) E.L.T. 385; JT 2009 (4) SC 23; 2009(3) SCALE 242; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Exchange Control & FDI; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Capital Goods, Fulfillment Of Export Obligations, Exim Policy, Machine Tools, Computer Software, Other Goods, Import Under Epcg Scheme, Epz Scheme, Assessees's appeal allowed/Department's appeals dismissed

Summary: Customs - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d) and 124 - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992, s. 5 - Import of Second Hand photocopier - Appellant imported in January, 2005 Photocopying Machines under category 'general imports' - Show cause notice issued alleging that used Photocopying Machines were restricted items for import under para 2.17 of current FTP 2004-09, consequently, they were liable to be confiscated u/s. 111(d) of Act - Tribunal allowed appeal against said order - On appeal, HC held that import of second-hand photocopier machines effected prior to 19-10-2005 would be governed by circular Nos. 16/03, 19/03 and 20/05 issued by DGFT and second-hand photocopier machines are not covered by definition of capital goods - Hence, present appeal - Whether, during period in question, Photocopying Machines (Photocopiers) were 'freely importable' or whether its import required a licence/permission/certificate? - Held, impugned item is restricted item for import under para 2.17 of FTP 2004-09 - By amendment Notification No. 31 dt. 19-10-2005, import of second-hand photocopying machines under 'general' category came under 'restricted' category of imports - Hence, after 19.10.2005, second-hand photocopying machines came within 'restricted' category - Impugned judgement of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
