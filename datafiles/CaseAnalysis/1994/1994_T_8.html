Punjab University and Another v T. P. Rahamatullah and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1363; (1995) Supp4 SCC 64; JT 1998 (4) SC 408

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Re evaluation - Denied - Legality - Respondent No.1 was a B.Sc. Part III student in the Govt. College - Respondent No.1 was stated to be short of lectures and had not complied with the condition regarding the passing of the house test - Principal of the college allowed the respondent to sit in the B.Sc. Part III examination held in April 1986 provisionally, at his own risk and responsibility, subject to the approval of University/appellant to which the college was affiliated - Principal recommended the case of the respondent for grant of relaxation in respect of the passing of the house test - Vice-Chancellor of appellant constituted a special committee - Committee accepted the recommendation of the Principal and relaxed the condition of passing the house test - Appellant, as a consequence, declared the B.Sc. Part III result of the respondent by a notification - Respondent was declared pass with compartment in the subject of Chemistry - Respondent No.1 was given a chance to qualify the subject of Chemistry by taking the examination which was to be held in the year 1990 - Respondent No.1 did not avail the chance and instead applied for re-evaluation of the paper in the subject of Chemistry in which he was placed under compartment - Appellant informed the respondent No.1 that the answer books relating to the examinations held in the year 1986 were destroyed two years thereafter and as such it was not possible to re-evaluate any paper pertaining to the examinations held in that year - Respondent No.1 filed a writ petition u/art. 226 of the Constitution before HC and sought a declaration that, appellant having failed to permit re-evaluation, he be declared to have qualified the examination - HC allowed the writ petition and declared that the respondent No.1 be deemed to have passed the Chemistry paper and as a consequence qualified B.Sc. Part III examination of the University - Whether HC erred by declaring that in absence of answer sheet respondent No.1 should be considered to have passed the examination - Held, HC fell into patent error in granting the declaration that the respondent be deemed to have qualified the paper of Chemistry without taking the examination - HC became totally oblivious to the facts and circumstances of instant case - It was the case of appellant before the HC that as a rule the answer books in respect of all the examinations were destroyed after a period of two years of the completion of the examination - SC found nothing wrong in having a rule or a practice of that type - No university or examining body could preserve the answer books of all the examinations for all the time - As per rules of appellant application for re evaluation of papers were to be submitted within 21 days of publication of result which the respondent Noi.1 did not do - HC was wholly unjustified in drawing an adverse inference against appellant in the facts of the case - Appeal allowed.
