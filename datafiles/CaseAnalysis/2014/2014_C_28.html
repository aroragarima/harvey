Chandigarh Administration and another v Jasmine Kaur and others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 583; (2014) 10 SCC 521; AIR 2015 SC 34; 2014 (6) AWC 6293; JT 2014 (10) SC 319; 2014 (7) MLJ 111; 2014(10) SCALE 1; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 1122; 2014 (6) SLR 377

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College

Summary: Education - Admission - Respondent was aggrieved of a clause in the prospectus issued by the appellants (Chandigarh Administration and the Govt. Medical College Chandigarh), which according to her was not valid - Respondent stated that she being a Canadian Citizen was an NRI, that, therefore, she was entitled to seek admission to the MBBS course in the NRI category quota but yet the definition of NRI as specified in the prospectus issued by the appellants for the academic year 2014-15 would denude her of such status and, therefore, it was liable to be struck down - Single Judge held that the impugned clause was totally impracticable, illegal, illogical and declared as such - Single Judge held that the contesting respondent was not entitled to any relief for getting admission into MBBS course - On appeal, DB held that when once the definition clause of NRI was found to be invalid by the Single Judge, the respondent ought to have been granted admission into MBBS course - Chandigarh Administration filed review - DB directed that the respondent be accommodated in the academic session 2014-15 instead of 2013-14 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the DB was justified in directing the admission of the contesting Respondent to the MBBS course in the academic year 2014-15 -

Held, respondent knew full well when the prospectus was issued in April 2013 that she did not fulfill the criteria for making an application under the first category of NRI quota as prescribed in paragraph 2 of the prospectus. But yet there was no immediate challenge to the said provision before the HC. There was total lack of diligence displayed by the respondent right from the stage when the submission of the application was made. Therefore, the ultimate direction of the DB in having directed the Chandigarh Administration and the Govt. Medical College to provide admission to the contesting Respondent without her participation in the admission process of the year 2014-15 and thereby causing prejudice to the rightful claims of the candidates who validly made their applications in the said academic year cannot be countenanced. The impugned orders of the DB in having issued such a direction cannot be approved by SC. A cumulative effect of the conduct of the respondent has only resulted in disentitling her to claim any equitable relief prejudicial to the interest of other eligible candidates of the year 2014-15 and whose rights came to be crystallized based on the process of selection made for the academic year 2014-15. Appeals disposed of.
