Bombay Gas Company Limited v Gopal Bhiva and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 177; AIR 1964 SC 752; 1964 CurLJ 83; 1963 (7) FLR 304; 1963 (2) LLJ 608; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 709

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.33 C(2) - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 181 - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, s. 22 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 48 - Claim of benefits - Disputed - Validity - Respondents /workmen raised industrial disputes in 1958 claiming monetary benefits from appellant as per earlier Award dt. 11-5-1950 and was opposed by appellant on several grounds such as limitation and resjudicata etc - Labour Court rejected appellant's preliminary objections and allowed claim of respondents and directed appellant to pay respective amounts to respondents - Aggrieved appellant/company filed instant appeals - Whether judgment of Tribunal was justified - Held, limitation and resjudicata pleas of appellant were rightly rejected by Trial Court - Legislature provided limitations in some Acts but did not provide any limitation to claims in Labour Court keeping in view illiteracy of workers - Limitation prescribed in other Acts could not be used to claims u/s. 33C(2) of the 1947 Act in Labour Court - Limitation prescribed by art. 181 of the 1963 Act could not be invoked in dealing with applications u/s. 33C (2) of the 1947 Act - Tribunal on earlier dispute considered discrimination between staff and daily rated workers with regard to weekly off and ordered payment to daily rated workers also for weekly off as their wages per month would mean 26 days - Labour Court was justified in allowing claims of respondents as based on earlier Award - SC in central bank of India ltd. v. P. S. Rajagopalan, 1963 Indlaw SC 136 held that Labour Court had jurisdiction for computation of monetary benefits - Award of Labour Court was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
