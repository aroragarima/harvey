Kuldeep Kumar Gupta and Others v H. P. State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2728; (2001) SCC (L&S) 253; (2001) 1 SCC 475; AIR 2001 SC 308; 2001 (1) AWC 536; 2001 (1) CLR 578; JT 2001 (1) SC 47; 2001 LabIC 409; 2001 (1) LLN 842; 2000(8) SCALE 311; 2001 (1) SCJ 372; 2001 (2) SLJ 310; 2000 (6) SLR 705; 2001 (1) SLT 107; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 572; 2000 (8) Supreme 392; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 273

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Electricity & Energy; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Efficiency

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Constitution - Service - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 79(c) and 15 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16 (1), 16 (4) and 16 (4-A) - Whether it is permissible for employer to frame regulations, providing a separate quota of promotional avenues for less qualified Junior Engineers in preference to claim of qualified diploma-holder Junior Engineers? - Held, if rule-making authority considers that stagnation at stage of Junior Engineer will not be conducive for administration and provides promotional avenue for them, by providing a quota in promotional cadre and service history itself indicates that such provision has been made right from inception, no constitutional infraction therein, so as to be interfered with - There is no bar for providing a quota in promotional post, even in favour of unqualified matriculate Junior Engineers - Appeal dismissed.
