State of Uttar Pradesh v Dayanand Chakrawarty and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 388; (2013) 7 SCC 595; AIR 2013 SC 3066; 2013 (6) AWC 6123; 2013 (139) FLR 272; JT 2013 (9) SC 494; 2013(8) SCALE 74; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 1023; 2013 (5) SLR 126; 2013 (3) UPLBEC 2125

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Age Of Retirement, Uttar Pradesh Water Supply and Sewerage Act, 1975, Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules, Corporations, Government Servant Rules, U.P. Jal Nigam Karamchari (Adhivarshita Par Seva Nivarti) Viniyamawali, 2005, Uttar Pradesh Fundamental (Amendment) Rules, 2002, Uttar Pradesh Service of Engineers (Public Health Branch) Regulations, 1977, Uttar Pradesh Jal Nigam Employees (Retirement on attaining age of superannuation) Rules, 2005, U.P. Jal Nigam Employees (Retirement on attaining age of Superannuation) Regulations, 2005, Uttar Pradesh Jal Nigam Services of Engineers (Public Health Branch) Regulations, 1978

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Article 14-U.P. Jal Nigam Employees (Retirement on attaining age of Superannuation) Regulations, 2005 ("Regulations 2005")-Regulation 4-U. P. Jal Nigam Services of Engineers (Public Health Branch) Regulations, 1978 ("Regulations 1978")-Regulation 31-Employment-Age of superannuation-Of employees of Jal Nigam-Whether "Regulations 2005" fixing two different age of superannuation for similarily situated employees of Jal Nigam discriminatory and ultra vires under Article 14 of the Constitution?-Held, "yes"-Solely on basis of source of recruitment no discrimination can be made and differential treatment would not be possible in matter of condition of service, including age of superannuation, in absence of intelligible differentia distinguishing them from each other-Two different age of superannuation of 58 and 60 years cannot be prescribed for similarily situated employees of Jal Nigam-No interference called for in impugned judgment, whereby High Court held "Regulations, 2005" unconstitutional, violative of Article 14 of the Constitution and set aside orders of retirement-Regulation 31 of Regulations 1978 applicable-By implication it cannot be inferred that Regulation 31 stands repealed in view of subsequent Regulations, 2005-Employees of Jal Nigam not to be guided by State Govt. Order prescribing uniform age of superannuation at 58 years-Employees of Jal Nigam to continue in services up to age of 60 years, in view of Regulation 31 of "Regulations 1978", having not yet amended or repealed-Consequential and pecuniary benefits followed to different sets of employees who were ordered to retire at age of 58 years-Impugned judgment of Division Bench of High Court and other impugned judgments modified with directions.

(2) Principle of "no pay no work"-Applicability-It employee prevented by employer from performing his duties-Employee cannot be blamed for having not worked-Principle of "no pay no work" shall not be applicable to such employees.
