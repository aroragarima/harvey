Thakur Kamta Prasad Singh (Dead) By L.Rs v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 322; (1976) 3 SCC 772; AIR 1976 SC 2219; 1976 (2) ALR 465; 1976 (2) ALR 721; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 585; 1976 UJ 326

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1984 - Compensation - Reduction of - Whether High Court was in error in reducing rate at which compensation had been awarded? - Held, HC in taking into consideration above three sale transactions relied upon by appellant and three sale transactions relied upon by respondent found mean price of land covered by six sale deeds to be a little more than Rs. 460 per katha - HC in circumstances came to conclusion that just and fair market value of land should be assessed at Rs. 475 per katha - Finding of HC is based upon consideration of evidence adduced in case, there is no ground to interfere with that finding - In opinion of HC, said sale deeds could not form a safe criterion for assessing market value of acquired land because they had been executed by claimant himself after notification - It was also observed that plots sold were quite suitable for shop or residential purposes -The Court finds no sufficient reason to take a contrary view - If judgment of HC reveals that it has taken into consideration relevant factors, its assessment of fair market value of acquired land should not be disturbed - No such infirmity has been brought to our notice that might induce us to disturb finding of HC - Appeal dismissed.
