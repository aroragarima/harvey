State of Maharashtra and Another v B.E. Billimoria and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 618; (2003) 7 SCC 336; AIR 2003 SC 4368; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 68; 2004 (54) ALR 148; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 694; 2003 (4) JhrCR(SC) 15; JT 2003 (7) SC 257; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 87; 2003(6) SCALE 441; 2003 (5) SLT 473; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 603; 2003 (5) Supreme 580

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Urban Land (Ceiling And Regulation) Act, 1976, ss. 10(3), 18, 2(1), 2(i), 2(q), 3, 4, 4(8), 4(9), 5, 6, 8 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 227 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 45 - Building Rules, r. 2(9) - Whether land and flats held by respondents was in excess of ceiling limit of Pune Agglomeration - Held, definition of 'vacant land' as given in s.2(q) clearly provides that land on which construction of a building is not permissible under building regulations in force in area has to be excluded - As under relevant rules in force in area, construction was not permissible on two-third of area of plot, HC was perfectly justified in holding that for determining vacant land, two-third portion of each of respondents had to be excluded and thus vacant land held by each one of them in said area was only 905 sq m - Further held, ownership or possession of land over which building stands is a necessary condition for applicability of any one of sub-sections of s 4 - Therefore, they cannot be held to be either owners of any land or in possession of any land and consequently s 4(8) or (9) would not apply - In these circumstances, view taken by competent authority and appellate authority that area of flats in Bombay had to be clubbed with vacant land held by respondents in Pune was patently wrong and HC has rightly set aside same.
