State of Maharashtra and Others v Nanded-Parbhani Z. L. B. M. V. Operator Sangh

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 594; (2000) 2 SCC 69; (2000) SCC (Cr) 318; 2000 (1) ACC 290; AIR 2000 SC 725; 2000 (38) ALR 555; 2000 (5) Bom.C.R. 669; 2000 (1) Crimes 174; 2000 (1) JCC 193; JT 2000 (1) SC 290; 2000 (125) PLR 558; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 153; 2000(1) SCALE 211; 2000 (2) SCJ 462; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 357; 2000 (1) SLT 458; 2000 (1) Supreme 204

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Criminal

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Criminal - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 207 and 207(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Whether u/s. 207(1) of Act, appropriate authority can be said to have power to detain a vehicle, vehicle being found to be used in contravention of conditions of permit relating to number of passengers which could be carried in vehicle? - It is a cardinal principle of rule of construction of a statute that when language of a statute is fairly and reasonably clear, then inconvenience or hardships are no considerations for refusing to give effect to that meaning - Held, purpose of section would only refer to a contingency when a vehicle having a permit of a stage carriage is used as a contract carriage or vice versa or where a vehicle having a permit for stage carriage or contract carriage is used as a goods vehicle and vice versa - But carrying passengers more than number specified in permit will not be a violation of purpose for which permit is granted - If legislature really wanted to confer power of detention on police officer for violation of any condition of permit, then there would not have been necessity of adding expression - Further held, detention of vehicle is unauthorized - Compensation awarded right - Appeal dismissed.
