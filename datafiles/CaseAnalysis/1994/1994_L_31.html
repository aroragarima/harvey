Lubri-Chem Industries Limited v Collector of Central Excise, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 762; (1994) Supp3 SCC 258; AIR 1994 SC 2604; 1994 (54) ECR 514; 1994 (73) E.L.T. 257; JT 1994 (5) SC 503; 1994(3) SCALE 928

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Central Excises & Salt Act 1944, s. 11 A, 35 and 35 B (A) Demand for excise duty beyond period of six months - Assessee not guilty of any fraud or collusion or willful misstatement or suppression of facts with intent to evade payment of excise duty - If sustainable - Held, demand of excise duty beyond six months, not sustainable - (B) Appellate powers u/ss. 35 and 35 B - Scope - Assessee manufacturing liquid paraffin - Assessee's paraffin classified under T I 8 for one period and under T I 14 E for another period - Appeal against both classifications - Appellate authority holding that paraffin on its manufacture was classifiable under T I 8 and on being packed in drums with monogram was classifiable under T I 14 E and thus assessee had to pay duty at two stages - Whether proper - Further held, Order is not proper - (C) s. 3, Sch T I 8 - Mineral oils - If Liquid paraffin is mineral oil - If covered by T I 8 - Whether exclusion of spindle oils from which paraffin is processed, from purview of T I 8 takes liquid paraffin out of purview of T I 8 - Further held, it is beyond controversy raised in appeal - Is mineral oil - Does not take liquid paraffin out of purview of T I 8 - Appeal allowed.
