Kirpal Kaur v Jitender Pal Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 485; (2015) 9 SCC 356; AIR 2015 SC 2967; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 110; 2015 (112) ALR 478; 2015 (6) ALT(SC) 14; 2015 (5) AWC 4737; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 315; JT 2015 (6) SC 314; 2016 (2) MahLJ 542; 2015 (5) MLJ 871; 2016 (2) MPLJ 93; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 826; 2015 (129) RD 381; 2016 (1) RLW 275; 2015(8) SCALE 38; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 51; 2015 (3) WLN(SC) 193

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Pendente Lite

Summary: Family & Personal - Land & Property - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Claim of joint owner of property - Share in property - Determination - Appellant filed civil suit before Trial Court against respondents for partition of property in question in favour of her late husband's share, contending thereby that all properties are jointly owned by family - Trial Court in respect to one property dismissed suit of appellant holding that said property is self acquired property of deceased respondent no.1 - Further, in respect to other property Trial Court partially decreed same in favor of appellant by granting 1/5th share in agricultural land - First Appellate Court confirmed order passed by Trial Court - Whether impugned order passed by lower courts against appellant in respect to one property is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, admissions made by deceased respondent no.1 in his statement of evidence deposed before Trial Court shows that he received money from appellant's husband while he was in Kuwait. He has also admitted that appellant's husband had share in ancestral property. Further, it was admitted in his statement that he had received amount of Rs.1 lakh by way of bank draft and cash from deceased husband of appellant , while he was working in Kuwait which amount was utilised by deceased-first defendant for reconstruction of property in question. Hence, SC held that in view of evidence elicited from deceased respondent no. 1, First Appellate Court was not right in making observation in impugned judgment that appellant is only entitled for refund of said amount from deceased respondent no.1 even though there is substantive and positive evidence on record to effect that amount sent by deceased husband of appellant was utilised by deceased respondent no.1 for purpose of construction of building upon suit property. Further, with respect to execution of alleged gift deed, SC held it to be invalid as it is evident from factual and legal aspect of case that the gift deed of property in question was executed by deceased respondent no.1 in favour of respondent no.2 during pendency of proceedings and the same could not have been acted upon by respondent as appellant has been in possession of second floor of said property in her husband's independent right. Further, same is also not acted upon by parties for reason that appellant has been in physical possession of second floor of property in question, hence, she could not have delivered possession to respondent no.2 and acted upon same, hence, s. 8 of Act would come into operation in respect of said property. SC held that property in question of deceased respondent no.1 would devolve upon deceased husband of appellant along with respondent no.2 and other daughters of deceased respondent no.1 as they are joint owners of property by virtue of being Class I legal heirs of deceased as per schedule to Act, upon death of the respondent no.1 Hence, appellant is entitled for 1/4th share in suit schedule. Impugned order passed by lower Courts is set aside with respect to property in question. Appeal allowed.
