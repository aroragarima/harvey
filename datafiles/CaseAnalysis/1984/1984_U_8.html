Delhi Veterinary Association v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 229; (1984) 3 SCC 1; (1984) SCC (L&S) 329; AIR 1984 SC 1221; 1984 (49) FLR 159; 1984 LabIC 860; 1984 (2) LLN 46; 1984(1) SCALE 663; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 429; 1984 (2) SLR 144; 1984 UJ 596

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, art, 14, 16, 32, 311 - Equal pay for equal work - Petition challenging alleged disparity of pay among Veterinary Assistant Surgeons in office of Development Commissioner, Delhi and those employed by the UT of Chandigarh or by the Central Govt. (in some para-military forces etc.) - Held, claim though prima facia ligitimate not dealt with by Court as 4th Pay Commission was to consider same question - (B)Whether the VASs were entitled to any retrospective benefit in respect of the period between 1973 and the date on which the new pay scales to be fixed - Urged that Pay Commission would not be making any recommendation in respect of that period - Held, having regard to the long delay in approaching Court after the fixation of their pay scale earlier, no relief granted in respect of that period - Petition dismissed.
