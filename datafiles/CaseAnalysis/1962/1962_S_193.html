Â Government of Andhra Pradesh v Syed Mohd. Khan

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1962 SC 1778; 1963 AllCriR 379; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 111; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 62; 1962 (64) PLR 889; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 288; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 288

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Foreigners

Summary: Constitution - Foreigners - Citizenship Act, 1955, s. 9 - Central Govt. power to hold inquiry with reference to r. 3 - Whether as a result of conduct of respondents in applying for and obtaining Pakistani passport, they had lost citizenship of India and had voluntarily acquired citizenship of Pakistan? - No automatic cesser of respondents citizenship by virtue of s. 9 - Held, question about status of respondents has to be tried by Central Govt. and it is only after Central Govt. has reached conclusion that respondents have acquired citizenship of Pakistan that appellant can issue orders of deportation against them - Writs issued by High Court restraining appellant from giving effect to impugned orders of deportation until question about respondent's status is determined by Central Govt. are confirmed - Appeal disposed of.
