J. C. Yadav and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, N.M. Kasliwal, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 716; (1990) 2 SCC 189; (1990) SCC (L&S) 218; AIR 1990 SC 857; 1990 (12) ATC 745; 1990 (60) FLR 523; JT 1990 (1) SC 278; 1990 LabIC 727; 1990 (2) LLJ 138; 1990 (1) LLN 732; 1990(1) SCALE 229; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 470; 1990 (1) SLR 860; 1990 (1) UJ 636; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 898

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Haryana Service of Engineers Class I PWD (Public Health Branch) Rules 1961, Punjab Police Service Rules, 1959

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Haryana Service of Engineers Class I PWD (Public Health Branch) Rules, 1961, rr. 5, 6(b), 9, 15 and 22 - Punjab Police Service Rules, 1959, r. 14 - Service - Promotion - Appellants promoted to Class I service - Respondent challenged validity of the appellants' in Writ Petition but dismissed - On appeal Division Bench set aside appellant's promotions - Whether relaxation granted by the State Government in favor of appellants is valid? - Court held, facts clearly show that in the absence of relaxation there could be no promotion to the post of Executive Engineer and the officers who were found suitable would have suffered great hardship - Also in 1973, State Government with a view to meet the particular situation exercised its power of relaxation in appellants' favor - Therefore no illegality in the appellants' promotions, pursuant to the relaxation granted by the State Government - Appeal allowed.
