Rural Litigation And Entitlementkendra And Ors. Etc. v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 244; (1986) Supp SCC 517; AIR 1987 SC 359; 1987 ALJ 95; 1987 (1) CCC 200; JT 1986 SC 1118; 1986(2) SCALE 1083; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 641; 1987 (1) UJ 132

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Practice & Procedure - Disturbance of ecology - Pollution - Instant Court had appointed a committee for the purpose of inspecting all the mines other than those belonging to the State - Committee classified the mines in the area into three groups being A, B and C - Committee was appointed to enquire and investigate into the question of disturbance of ecology and pollution and affectation of air, water and environment by reason of quarrying operations or working of stone crushers or limestone kilns - On submission of report SC directed closing down of mines in A category - SC directed that until further orders from instant Court on the basis of Committee report these mines should not be worked - Hence instant petition - Whether the schemes submitted by the mine lessees to the Committee under Courts order dt. 12-3-1985 was rightly rejected or not and whether under those schemes, the mine lessees could be allowed to carry on mining operations without in any way adversely affecting environment or ecological balance or causing hazard to individuals, cattle and agricultural lands - Held, SC had commended earlier to the State of UP also to the Union of India that afforestation activity might be carried out in the whole valley and the hills - SC was not oblivious of the fact that natural resources have got to be tapped for the purposes of social development but one could not forget at the same time that tapping of resources have to be done with requisite attention and care so that ecology and environment might not be affected in any serious way, there might not be any depletion of water resources and long-term planning should be undertaken to keep up the national wealth - It had always to be remembered that these were permanent assets of mankind and were not intended to be exhausted in one generation - Order accordingly.
