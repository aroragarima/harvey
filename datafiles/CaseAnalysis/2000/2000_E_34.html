K. R. Suraj v Excise Inspector, Parappananqadi and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2746; (2001) SCC (Cr) 133; (2001) 1 SCC 327; 2001 (1) CCR 21; 2001 (1) Crimes 1; JT 2000 (Supp3) SC 583; 2001 (1) KLT 169; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 202; 2000(8) SCALE 64; 2000 (8) SLT 670; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 333; 2000 (8) Supreme 87

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Kerala Abkari Act, ss. 31 and 51-A - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 482 - Whether proceedings initiated against appellants on basis of samples collected from their shops u/s. 31 (before its amendment in 1997) are liable to be quashed u/s. 482? - Under unamended s. 31, on basis of samples collected from shops of appellants, Excise Inspector could file report before Magistrate concerned - S. 50 enjoins that upon receipt of a report from Excise Inspector Magistrate shall inquire into such offence and try person accused thereof in a like manner as if complaint had been made before him as prescribed in CrPC - Further, held, that amendment of ss. 31 and 50 and insertion of s. 50-A has not changed law but has placed matter beyond controversy - In instant cases, on basis of samples of arrack collected while carrying out search under unamended s. 31, prosecution u/s. 57-A was rightly initiated by Excise Inspector - Prosecution has to make out a case under first limb of s. 31, which can be determined only on examination of Excise Inspector and decided on trial - In such a case if proceedings are not quashed u/s. 482, it cannot be said that High Court has committed any error in law - Appeal dismissed.
