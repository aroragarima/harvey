North Eastern Railway Administration, Gorakhpur v Bhagwan Das (D) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

11 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 613; (2008) 8 SCC 511; AIR 2008 SC 2139; 2009 (74) ALR 370; 2008 (3) AWC 2898; 2012 (281) E.L.T. 161; JT 2008 (4) SC 587; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 789; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 165; 2009 (106) RD 398; 2008(6) SCALE 254; [2008] 6 S.C.R. 416; 2012 (28) S.T.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Review Application

Summary: Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - O. 6, r. 17 - O. 41, r. 27 - Appeal to challenge order dismissing second appeal on ground that no substantial question of law arose for consideration of the Court and dismissing application filed under O. 6, r. 17 of CPC on ground that such application could not be entertained in second appeal and also dismissing review application filed under O. 41, r. 27 CPC - Appellant contended that High Court committed grave error in rejecting the amendment application as respondent had obtained decree by concealing material facts, the decree was a nullity and, therefore, non consideration of the additional material placed on record, had resulted in grave miscarriage of justice - Held, under clause (b) of sub rule (1) of r. 27 O. 41 C.P.C., evidence may be admitted by an appellate authority if it 'requires' to enable it to pronounce judgment 'or for any other substantial cause' - Question whether looking into the documents, sought to be filed as additional evidence, has to be considered by the Court at the time of hearing of the appeal on merits - High Court did not examine the record of the case with the thoroughness which was expected at the time of disposal of the pending applications; High Court was not even aware of the pendency of the application under O. 41, r. 27 C.P.C. seeking leave to adduce additional evidence - Judgment or decree by the first court or by the highest court obtained by playing fraud on the Court is a nullity and non est in the eyes of law - Appeal disposed of.
