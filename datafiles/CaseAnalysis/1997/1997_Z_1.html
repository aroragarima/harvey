State of Uttar Pradesh v Zakaullah

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1213; (1998) SCC (Cr) 456; (1998) 1 SCC 557; AIR 1998 SC 1474; 1998 ALJ 290; 1998 (1) CCR 49; 1998 (1) Crimes 58; 1998 CRLJ 863; JT 1997 (10) SC 54; 1998 (1) RCR(Criminal) 345; 1997(7) SCALE 620; 1998 (1) SLT 366; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 456

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1947 - Anti-corruption - Conviction and sentence - Acquittal - Respondent/ Revenue Inspector received Rs.400 as bribe from PW5 for doing an official act and he was caught red- handed with bribed amount by anti-corruption officials - After obtaining sanction from govt. respondent was challenged - Respondent govt. servant was convicted by Trial court u/s.161 of IPC and s.5(2) of 1947 Act and sentenced to substantive terms of imprisonment and fine - Respondent was acquitted by a single judge of HC and appealed against conviction and sentence - Assailing acquittal of a govt. servant from graft charge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent's conviction u/s.161 of IPC and s.5(2) 1947 Act was justified - Held, no doubt that HC had misdirected itself by such patently wrong and tenuous considerations and it resulted in unmerited acquittal of accused against whom prosecution succeeded in making out a fool-proof case u/s.161 of IPC and s. 5(2) of 1947 Act - Appeal allowed.
