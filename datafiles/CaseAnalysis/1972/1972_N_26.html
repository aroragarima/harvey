Assistant Controller of Central Excise v National Tobacco Company of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 716; (1972) 2 SCC 560; AIR 1972 SC 2563; 1972 (1) CTR 219; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 416; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 822; 1973 TAXLR 1607

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act of 1944 - Manufacturer - Excise duty - Notice - Validity - Excise duty was levied by appellant from respondent - After changes in basis of assessment, appellant send three notices to Company (respondent) demanding payment - Company applied to HC u/s. 226 of Constitution - Single Judge by order quashed notices - Case went back to Collector, he issued fresh notice - Company challenged validity of notice by second petition for Writs of Prohibition and Mandamus against Collector - Petition was allowed by Single Judge of HC which was confirmed by Division Bench of HC - Whether fresh notice issued by Collector was barred by time and was issued without jurisdiction - Held, r. 10A of the Central Excise Rules, 1944 indicated that there were residuary powers of making demand in special circumstances not foreseen by framers of the Act or rules - If assessee disputed for correctness of demand, an assessment became necessary to protect interests of assessee - Case fell more properly within residuary class of unforeseen cases - From provisions of s. 4 of the 1944 Act r/w r. 10A of the 1944 Rules, an implied power to carry out or complete an assessment, not specifically provided for by rules, could be inferred - No writs of prohibition or mandamus called for in circumstances of case - Orders of HC set aside - Collector might proceed to complete assessment - Appeal allowed.
