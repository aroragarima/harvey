Central Bureau of Investigation, Bank Securities and Fraud Cell and another v Ramesh Gelli and another

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 190; (2016) 3 SCC 788; AIR 2016 SC 1063; 2016 CRLJ 1576; 2016 (2) CTC 672; 2016 (2) DRTC 1; 2016 (2) JCC 1053; JT 2016 (3) SC 140; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 646; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 259; 2016(2) SCALE 579; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, FIR, CBI, Companies Act, 1956, Omission, Managing Director, Banking Regulation Act, 1949, Credit, Cognizance, Public Duty, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, ss. 120B, 420, 409, 467, 468, 471 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 35A, 46, 46A, 5(b) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 10, 11, 12, 13(1)(d), 13(2), 2(b), 30, 31, 46A - Wrongful Gain - Denial to Take Cognizance of Offence Challenged - Respondent no.1/accused as well as other co-accused were promoters of private bank, incorporated under companies and license was granted under 1949 Act - Accused raised loan from various individuals and companies, which were owned or managed by accused and conspired, manipulated accounts of bank thereby diverted large fund to its companies - Further, on amalgamation with other bank, FIR was lodged against wrongful gain by accused, however, Special Judge refused to take cognizance of offence punishable u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 3(1)(d) of 1988 Act, on ground that accused were not public servants - HC upheld order of Special Judge - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Chairman, Directors and officials of private bank could be said to be servants for purpose of their prosecution in respect of offence punishable under 1988 Act or not.

Held, the object of enactment of 1988, Act was to make the anti corruption law more effective and widen its coverage. In view of definition of public servant in s. 46A of 1949, Act, as amended the Managing Director and Executive Director of a Banking Company operating under licence issued by Reserve Bank Of India, were already public servants, as such they cannot be excluded from definition of 'public servant'. Thus, over general definition of 'public servant' given in s. 21 of IPC, it is the definition of 'public servant' given in the 1988, Act, r/w s. 46A of 1949, Act, which holds the field for the purposes of offences under the said Act. A law which is not shown ultra vires must be given proper meaning. S. 46-A of 1949 Act, cannot be left meaningless and requires harmonious construction. In instant case, the courts below have erred in law in holding that accused who were Chairman/Managing Director and Executive Director of GTB were not public servants for the purposes of 1988 Act. Hence, impugned order passed by lower courts are set aside and accused are public servants for the purpose of 1988 Act, by virtue of provision of s. 46A of 1949 Act and prosecutions launched against accused are maintainable in law. Appeals allowed.
