Janatha Textiles and Others v Tax Recovery Officer and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 873; (2008) 12 SCC 582; 2008 (3) BC 372; [2008] 301 ITR 337; JT 2008 (6) SC 653; 2008(8) SCALE 76; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 1148; [2008] 170 TAXMAN 221

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interest And Penalty

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 220(2) and 271(1)(c), sch. 2 rr. 60 and 61- Appeal - (A) Whether the Income Tax Department is justified in auctioning the attached property for recovery of debt - Partnership firm in arrears of tax - Agricultural lands owned by the partners attached and sold in public auction - No procedural irregularity or illegality alleged - Sale challenged on grounds of non disposal of objections and applications for waiver of interest and stay, failure to notice nature of land, failure to give notice of demand qua partner's share in firm - Department contended that alternate remedy by way of filing a petition u/rr. 60 and 61 of Second sch. availed - (B) Rights of the bona fide purchaser for value - Point that auction purchaser had purchased the said property in a valid auction and he cannot be disturbed according to the settled legal position - Principle of law that in a third party auction purchaser's interest in the auctioned property continues to be protected notwithstanding that the underlying decree is subsequently set aside or otherwise - Held, no interference called for - Held, law makes no distinction between a stranger who is a bona fide purchaser at auction sale and a decree holder purchaser at a court auction - Appeal Dismissed -
