State Bank of India v Anjan Sanyal and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 389; (2001) 5 SCC 508; (2001) SCC (L&S) 858; AIR 2001 SC 1748; AIR 2001 SCW 1674; 2002 (1) CGLJ 36; 2001 (2) CLR 930; 2001 (90) FLR 14; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 157; JT 2001 (5) SC 203; 2001 LabIC 1698; 2001 (1) LLJ 1687; 2001(3) SCALE 329; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1165; 2001 (3) SLR 639; 2001 (3) SLT 535; 2001 (3) Supreme 436; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1841

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - State Bank of India Officers Service Rules, rr. 47, 49, 50, 66 and 67 - State Bank of India Act, 1955, s. 43(1), r. 50(1) - Respondent appointed as probationary officer was transferred to Port Blair - Respondent remained in leave for two years - Respondent again transferred to Calcutta and then to Mumbai - Respondent filed representations but it was never allowed by bank authorities - Single Judge of Calcutta High Court set aside transfer order - Division Bench dismissed appeal of Bank - Whether findings of HC sustainable? - Held, valid order of transfer had been issued and employee concerned relieved of his duties but instead of joining new place of posting he went on representing authorities and openly disobeyed orders of transfer - No infirmity could be found with transfer order - Findings of HC unsustainable and set aside - Appeal allowed.
