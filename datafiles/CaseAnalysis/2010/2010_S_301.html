Asoke Basak v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 854; (2010) 10 SCC 660; JT 2010 (11) SC 123; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 388; 2011 (1) OLR 249; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 789B; 2010(10) SCALE 582; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 405 and 409 - Quashing of criminal complaint - Respondent No.2 entered into contracts with Maharashtra State Electricity Board (MSEB) and deposited an amount of Rs. 5 lakhs with MSEB as security deposit - Subsequently, on disputes arising between MSEB and respondent No. 2 and respondent No. 2 sought refund of the said amount - MSEB informed respondent No.2 that the deposit was adjusted by MSEB against the dues payable by respondent No.2 to MSEB - Respondents No.2 to 4 filed complaint against the appellant (Chairman of MSEB) and others for offences punishable u/ss. 405 and 409 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - Judicial Magistrate issued summons against all the accused named in the complaint - Appellant filed petition u/s. 482 of CrPC before the HC for quashing of the complaint, which was dismissed - Present appeal - (A) Whether ingredients of s. 409 of IPC were satisfied against appellant? - Held, in the present case, there was nothing in the complaint which may even remotely suggest that the respondent No. 2 had entrusted any property to the appellant or that the appellant had dominion over the said money of the respondent No. 2, which was dishonestly converted by him to his own use, so as to satisfy the ingredients of s. 405 of the IPC - Ingredients of s. 409 of IPC are not satisfied against the appellant - (B) Whether appellant was vicariously liable for the offence with the aid of s. 34 of IPC? - Held, essence of liability u/s. 34 IPC is the existence of a common intention - A common intention implies pre-arranged plan and acting in concert pursuant to the pre-arranged plan - Thus, in order to attract s. 34 of the IPC, the complaint in question must, prima facie, reflect a common prior concert or planning amongst the appellant and other accused - In the present case, complaint does not reveal any pre-concert or pre-planning whereby all the accused had decided to misappropriate the said amount - Therefore, s. 34 of IPC not attracted against appellant - (C) Quashing of complaint - Held, no prima facie case made out against the appellant in respect of offence u/s. 409 r/w s. 405, even with the aid of s. 34 of IPC - Therefore, it was a fit case where the HC should have exercised its powers u/s. 482 of the CrPC by quashing the complaint against the appellant - Impugned order set aside - Appeal allowed.
