General Secretary, South Indian Cashew Factories Workers' Union v Managing Director, Kerala State Cashew Development Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1427; (2006) 5 SCC 201; AIR 2006 SC 2208; 2006 (7) AWC 7034; 2006 (3) RLW 2540; 2006(6) SCALE 44; 2006 (6) SLR 461; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 488

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Industrial dispute - Misconduct - Conducted enquiry - Imposed punishment by reverting employee - Succeed - Appellant-union raised industrial dispute on behalf of one of its member charged for misconduct so questioning before Labour Court correctness of order passed by respondent no.1 imposed punishment by reverting employee as factory clerk in respondent no. 1's establishment - Labour Court preliminary order found that employee was workman as defined under the Act 1947 and Industrial dispute was validly raised, with regard to enquiry, it was found that enquiry was fair and proper and findings were not perverse, but Labour Court set aside enquiry report on ground that enquiry officer was biased as enquiry was conducted by employee of Corporation - Respondent no. 1 filed writ before HC and contending that preliminary order of the Labour Court in setting aside enquiry report was illegal - HC allowed petition and held employee was workman as defined under the Act and that there was valid preference for adjudication, it questioned conclusion - It was found that enquiry conducted was fair and proper, there was no scope for reappraising evidence or to consider adequacy of punishment and Labour Court had erred in holding that since enquiry was conducted by officer of Management, enquiry was vitiated and also because he made some observations against workman that did affect validity of enquiry - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that enquiry officer was officer of management itself affected fairness of enquiry, further his biased approach was evident from unnecessary observations made by him - Whether order of HC was justified - Held, relied on in Workmen of Firestone Tyre and Rubber Co. of India (Pvt.) Ltd. v. The Management 1972 Indlaw SC 315 - Powers of the Labour Court in the absence of Section 11A is illustrated by SC, when enquiry was conducted fairly and properly, in absence of any of allegations of victimization or malafides or unfair labour practice, Labour Court has no power to interfere with punishment imposed by management - Hence, s. 11A was not applicable, Labour Court had no power to re-appraise evidence to find out whether findings of enquiry officer were correct or not or whether punishment imposed was adequate or not - Therefore, Labour Court could interfere with findings, if findings were perverse, but, here there was clear finding that findings were not perverse and principles of natural justice were complied with while conducting enquiry - Appeal dismissed.
