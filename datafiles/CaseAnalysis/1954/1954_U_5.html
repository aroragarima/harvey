Â Madhya Bharat Cotton Association Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 191; AIR 1954 SC 634

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946, s. 2(a) - Essential Commodity - Cotton Control Order of 1950, cls. 4 and 6 - Business of hedge contracts in cotton - Notifications issued under Order wherein petitioner alleged to have been prevented from carrying on such business - Whether such prevention amounted to restraint of trade so as to attract art. 19(1)(g) and further, allowing another company to carry on trade in cotton whether amounted to discrimination so as to attract art. 14? - Cotton was listed as an 'essential commodity' u/s. 2(a) of Act, so right of State to control, and even to prohibit, transactions in it is evident - 'Hedging' is of vital importance in cotton trading, it not only acts as an insurance and protects cotton growers, manufacturers and merchants but also acts as a check on reckless speculation and gambling when properly controlled - Consequently, it is important to have this type of dealing under proper supervision and control, other-wise, as in case of banks and insurance companies, innocent persons may have to suffer for reckless gambling and speculation of handful of persons anxious to get rich quickly - Further, cotton being a commodity essential to life of community, it is reasonable to have restrictions which may, in certain circumstances, extend to total prohibition for a time, of all normal trading in commodity - Held, impugned notification under provisions of Control Order does not offend art. 19(1)(g) - Further held, 'Hedging' requires experience and stability, also, it so vitally affects welfare of a large section of people of India and India's economic stability in world markets that it cannot be lightly entrusted to inexperienced hands - Two associations cannot be said to be on a footing of equality, so no question of discrimination u/art. 14 - Petition dismissed.
