Balbir Singh and Another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 526; (2006) 12 SCC 283; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 715; AIR 2006 SC 3221; 2007 (2) ALT 5; 2006 CRLJ 4646; 2006 (3) JCC 1829; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2988; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 452; 2006(9) SCALE 537B; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 307, 34, 498 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 20 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held law does not provide that a dying declaration should be made in any prescribed manner or in form of questions and answers - Only because a dying declaration was not recorded by a Magistrate, same by itself, may not be a ground to disbelieve entire prosecution case - When a statement of an injured is recorded, in event of her death, same may also be treated to be a First Information Report - Keeping in view inconsistencies between two dying declarations, benefit of doubt should be given to Appellant No 2 - Conviction and sentence of Appellant No 2 u/s. 302 r/w s 34 is set aside and u/s. 498-A is upheld - Conviction and sentence of appellant no 1 confirmed - Appeals disposed of.
