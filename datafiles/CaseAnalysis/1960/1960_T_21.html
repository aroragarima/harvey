Chartered Bank, Bombay v Chartered Bank Employees' Union

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 161; AIR 1960 SC 919; 1960 (1) FLR 34; 1960 (2) LLJ 222; 1961 (1) SCJ 70; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 441

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: misa

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - All India Industrial Tribunal (Bank Disputes) Award of March, 1953, ss. 521 and 522(1) - Issue: the scope of the power of the Bank to act under para 522(1) of the Bank Award, particularly in the peculiar situation prevailing in the cash department of the Bank - Security of the cash department involved and bank deciding to use para 522(1) of the Bank Award to terminate the service of the respondent - Cannot be said that the Bank was exercising its power under para 522(1) in a colourable manner - Held (A): appeal allowed and the order of the Tribunal set aside - Dispute between the Bank and its workmen regarding the termination of the service, of respondent, an asst. cashier in the Bank - Peculiar system of working in the cash department - The chief cashier has to give security for the work of the cash department, all asst. cashiers employed upon the introduction of the Chief Cashier who guarantees each such employee - The Chief Cashier is unconditionally responsible to the Bank for any shortage which might occur in the cash department and no security is taken from the asst. cashiers working therein - The Chief Cashier reported to the management, that the respondent had been leaving the Bank without his permission for some time past before the cash was checked and locked up in spite of the issue of a departmental circular issued on 24-12-1956, by which all asst. cashiers (including the respondent) were reminded of the longstanding practice, that no asst. cashier should leave the Bank without the permission of the Chief Cashier, before the cash was checked and locked up - The Chief Cashier therefore, stated that he was unable to continue to guarantee the respondent and that unless the respondent's service was dispensed with, his conduct will affect the security of the cash department - Bank not prepared to change the system in force in the cash department, the management decided to dispense with the service of the respondent in accordance with the mode of termination prescribed by para 522(1) of the Bank Award - Notice required under para 522(1) was given and the amount due to the respondent including retrenchment compensation was paid to him and his service was terminated - Dispute was raised by the workmen of the Bank and the Industrial Tribunal held, the termination of the service of the respondent was illegal and improper and he was entitled to reinstatement with full back wages and other benefits.
