M. L. Sethi v R. P. Kapur

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 370; (1972) 2 SCC 427; AIR 1972 SC 2379; 1973 (1) ALT(SC) 230; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 697

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 10, O. 9, O. 11 rr. 12, 13 and 21, O. 33 rr. 5, 6, 7, 9, O. 39 - Respondent filed suit in forma pauperis - Court dismissed application for permission to sue in forma pauperis and directed to pay court fee within 15 days - Respondent challenged in revision before High Court, order directing discovery of documents and dismissing his application for permission to sue in forma pauperis - HC set aside order for discovery and order dismissing application for permission to sue in forma pauperis - Whether HC was correct in interfering u/s. 115 of CPC with order of lower court? - Held, rejection of application by Lower Court for time and consequent dismissal of petition for permission to sue in forma pauperis can hardly be said to sound in jurisdictional error and refusal to adjourn occasioned any failure of natural justice nor is there material irregularity in exercise of its jurisdiction - Therefore, set aside order of HC - Appeal allowed.
