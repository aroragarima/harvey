Ahsanul Hoda v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 379; (2013) 14 SCC 59; AIR 2013 SC 3463; 2013 (6) ALD(SC) 10; 2013(5) ALL MR 488; 2013 (99) ALR 895; 2013 (4) AWC 4263; 2014 (1) CLT(SC) 100; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 1013; 2013(8) SCALE 185; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 312

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition of land - Claim for higher compensation - Reduction of compensation - Legality - Appellant's land was acquired for construction of Orni-reservoir - Collector by award fixed the compensation of Rs.6513.60 for entire land based on market rate at Rs.16 per decimal - No amount was awarded towards damages of standing crops - Appellant claimed higher compensation before Reference Court, which was determined market value as Rs. 250/- per decimal i.e. Rs. 25,000/- per acre - Respondent preferred appeal before HC - HC by impugned order reduced the market rate from Rs.250/- per decimal to Rs.100/- - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, HC committed error in holding that sale deeds (Ext.1 and Ext.1/b) relate to homestead land, on ground that a 'road' and a 'masjid' were shown in boundary of one of exhibits - From copies of Ext.1 and Ext. 1/b on record, it was found no citation in Ext.1 showing land as homestead land - However, Ext.1/b specifically cited that land was an agricultural land for which annual revenue rent of Rs.25 was payable - HC disregarded evidence adduced by appellant in its entirety without any reason, it was relied on evidence of an officer of State and set aside compensation in relation to standing crops - Fact that Collector had not allowed any amount towards damage of standing crops and that no such amount was mentioned in Khatiyan did not mean that no standing crop was there at time of taking possession of land - Witnesses AW1 to AW5 appeared and supported statement of appellant that at time of possession, standing crops were there which were damaged causing loss to extent of Rs.10,000 to Rs. 12,000/- - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and restored the award passed by Reference Court - Respondents were directed to pay appellant compensation in terms of award passed by Reference Court - Appeal allowed.
