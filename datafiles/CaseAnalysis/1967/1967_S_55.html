Sonawati and Others v Sri Ram and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 214; AIR 1968 SC 466; 1968 ALJ 313; 1968 CurLJ 182; 1968 (1) ILR(All) 230; 1968 (2) SCJ 225; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 617; 1968 (21) STC 91

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Land Reforms (SUttar Pradeshplementary) Act 1952

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Zamidari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950, s. 20 - Uttar Pradesh Land Reforms (Supplementary) Act, 1952, s. 3 - Whether defendant No. 1 can acquire adhivasi rights by placing reliance merely upon entries in Khasra, or in alternative by placing reliance upon s. 3 of Reforms act, if so, its effect? - In order that a person may be regarded as an adhivasi of a piece of land, s. 20(b) of Act 1 of 1951 requires.that his.name must be recorded in khasra or khatauni for 1356 Fasli as an occupant - There is also strong evidence on record which shows that name of defendant No. 1 was surreptitiously entered in khasra for 1356 Fasli - Scheme of s. 3 of U.P. Land Reforms (Supplementary) Act, is different from scheme of s. 20(b) of U.P.Z.A. and L.R. Act - Whereas under latter entry is made evidence without further enquiry as to his right of status of person who is recorded as an occupant, u/s. 3 of Act, a person who claims status of an asami or an adhivasi must establish that he was in 'cultivatory possession' of land during year 1359 Fasli - Explanation clearly implies that claimant must have a lawful right to be in possession of land, and must not belong to classes specified in explanation - There is further no oral evidence in support of case of Pritam Singh that he was in actual 'cultivatory possession' of land and entry relied upon by him does not support his case - Held, HC was justified, and indeed bound, to avoid giving a fresh lease of life to defendants litigation - Appeal dismissed.
