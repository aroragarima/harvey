State of Maharashtra and Others v Maimuma Banu and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 625; (2003) 7 SCC 448; AIR 2003 SC 3698; 2003 (53) ALR 135; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 691; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 186; JT 2003 (7) SC 171; 2003 (3) MLJ(SC) 209; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 85; 2003 (95) RD 659; 2003(6) SCALE 479; 2003 (4) SLT 931; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 228; 2003 (6) Supreme 289; 2003 (3) UPLBEC 2666

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - ss.4, 6, 17(3 A), 23(1-A), 28 - Whether there can be any direction for interest on rental compensation once it is held that same has to be paid within time frame, notwithstanding fact that there is no statutory obligation - Held, it is crystal clear from a bare reading of provisions of Act that it does not provides for payment of any rental compensation - Therefore, appellants are correct in their stand to extent that liability for rental compensation does not have its source under Act - Therefore, logic of ss.17(3-A), 28, 34 has no application in law to rental compensation - Further held, though inevitable conclusion is that HC is not justified in directing grant of interest on logic of various provisions contained in Act, yet there is an element of equity in favour of land owners - It would therefore be appropriate if appellants pay interest till amounts payable as rental compensation are paid to concerned land owners.
