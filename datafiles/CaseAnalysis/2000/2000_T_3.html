Manjushree Pathak v Assam Industrial Development Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 435; (2000) 7 SCC 390; (2000) SCC (L&S) 939; AIR 2000 SC 2769; 2000 (87) FLR 190; JT 2000 (10) SC 9; 2000 (2) LLJ 1125; 2000 (4) LLN 580; 2000(6) SCALE 198; 2000 (5) SLR 256; 2000 (6) SLT 518; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 53; 2000 (6) Supreme 98; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2444

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16 and 12 - Voluntary retirement Scheme - Option under offered by employee cannot be withdrawn - Eligible employee seeking voluntary retirement in form prescribed - Held, application has to be accepted with immediate effect and it cannot be refused on extraneous consideration - Discretion to consider application has to be exercised judiciously - Corporation being an authority within art. 12 of Constitution cannot abdicate its duty to act reasonably and fairly in exercise of discretion.
