Manphul Singh Sharma v Ahmedi Begum

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1489; (1994) 5 SCC 465; 1994 (55) DLT 506; JT 1994 (5) SC 49; 1994 (2) RCJ 225; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 402; 1995 (1) RentLR 98; 1994(3) SCALE 712; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - General Caluses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1952, s. 20 - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 17, 18 and 25 - (A) Whether Appellate Bench was right in dismissing appeal on ground that appellant had not served notice on owner Ahmedi Begum thus protection available u/s. 18, making a subtenant a statutory tenant was unavailable to him after repeal of 1952 Act? - (B) Which one of these two Acts is to govern, whether 1952 Act or 1958 Act? - The first proviso to s. 57 (2) states inter alia that in the matter of eviction of a tenant from any premises to which s. 54 does not apply, the court or other authority shall have regard to the provisions o f the Control Act of 1958 - The first proviso must be read harmoniously with the substantive provision contained in sub-s. (2) - High Court was wrong in holding, that for failure of appellant to issue notice u/s. 17, benefit of s. 18 would be unavailable - 1952 Act was repealed by s. 57 of 1958 Act, however, proceedings under 1952 Act could be continued, as if 1958 Act had not been enacted - Appellant is a lawful subtenant and would be entitled to protection of s. 20 of 1952 Act - Ss. 17 and 18 of 1958 Act will have no application whatsoever - Appellant being a lawful subtenant had become a statutory tenant - Appeal allowed.
