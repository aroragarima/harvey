State of Uttar Pradesh v Nawab Hussain

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 63; (1977) SCC (L&S) 362; (1977) 2 SCC 806; AIR 1977 SC 1680; 1977 (3) ALR 311; 1977 LabIC 911; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 428; 1977 (2) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Constructive

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Whether suit before Civil Judge was barred by principle of constructive res judicata? - Same set of facts may give rise to two or more causes of action - A person cannot be allowed to choose and sue upon one cause of action at one time and to reserve other for subsequent litigation - This raises bar of res judicata, by suitably construing general principle of subduing a cantankerous litigant - If doctrine of constructive res judicata is not applied to writ proceedings, parties would be open to take one proceeding after another urging new grounds every time - That plainly is inconsistent with considerations of public policy - Held, respondent did not raise plea, in writ petition earlier filed in High Court, that u/art. 311(1) he could not be dismissed by Deputy Inspector General of Police as he had been appointed by Inspector General of Police - He only raised plea that he was not afforded a reasonable opportunity to meet case against him in departmental inquiry and that action taken against him was mala fide - It was therefore not permissible for him to challenge his dismissal, in subsequent suit, on other ground that he had been dismissed by an authority subordinate to that by which he was appointed - Being barred by constructive res judicata - HC erred in taking a contrary view - Appeal allowed.
