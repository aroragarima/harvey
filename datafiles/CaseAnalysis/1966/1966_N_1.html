Â State of Kerala v N. Ramaswami Iyer and Sons

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1966 SC 1738; [1966] 61 ITR 187; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 582; 1966 (18) STC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Constitution- Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, 1950 - Cochin General Sales Tax Rules - (A) Whether absence of an express provision in Act excluding jurisdiction of civil court, courts below were right in holding that suit was maintainable? - Held, it is true that even if jurisdiction of civil court is excluded, where provisions of statute have not been complied with or statutory tribunal has not acted in conformity with the fundamental principles of judicial procedure, Civil courts have jurisdiction to examine those cases - (B)Computation of taxable turnover - Held, in Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act at material time there was no express provision which obliged taxing authority to exclude from computation of taxable turnover amount of sales tax collected by dealer - Appeal allowed.
