Â State of West Bengal v Nripendra Nath Bagchi

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 1966 SC 447; 1968 (1) LLJ 270; 1966 (2) SCJ 59; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 771

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Fundamental Rules, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, West Bengal Service Rules

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - Govt. Of India Act, 1935 - Civil Services (Classification, Control And Appeal) Rules - West Bengal Service Rules, r.75(a) - (A) Whether provisions of r.75(a) could be utilised to extend service of Bagchi beyond normal age of retirement? - Held, r. 75(a) is intended to be used to keep in employment persons with a meritorious record of service who, although superannuated, can render some more service, whose retention in service is considered necessary on public grounds - (B) Whether Govt. or High Court should order, initiate, and hold enquiries into conduct of District Judges? - Further held, control which is vested in HC is a complete control subject only to power of Governor in matter of appointment (including dismissal and removal) and posting and promotion of District Judges - Within exercise of control vested in HC, HC can hold enquiries, impose punishments other than dismissal or removal, subject however to conditions of service, to a right of appeal if granted by conditions of service, and to giving of an opportunity of showing cause as required by cl.(2) of art.311 unless such opportunity is dispensed with by Governor acting under provisos (b) and (c) to that clause - HC alone could have held enquiry in this case - Appeal dismissed.
