Bhikhubhai Vithlabhai Patel and Others v State of Gujarat and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Sudarshan Reddy, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 527; (2008) 4 SCC 144; AIR 2008 SC 1771; 2008(3) ALL MR 816; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1531; JT 2008 (4) SC 10; 2008(4) SCALE 278; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 1051

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Gujarat Town Planning and Urban Development Act, 1976 - Held, State Government took the action proposing to make substantial modifications to the plan without forming of any opinion, which is a condition precedent for the use of power under proviso to s. 17(1)(a)(ii) - Power, to restrict the use of land by the owners thereof, is a drastic power - S. 17 and more particularly the proviso to s. 17 (1) (a) (ii) prescribes some of the conditions precedent for the exercise of power - Order proposing to make substantial modifications, in breach of any one of those conditions, will undoubtedly be void - Struck down both the notifications and declared them ultra vires - Appeal allowed.
