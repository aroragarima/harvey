Union of India and Another v Jain Sabha, New Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1931; (1997) 1 SCC 164; 1996 (9) AD(SC) 82; JT 1997 (10) SC 642; 1996(8) SCALE 520; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 1; 1997 (1) UJ 413

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Sabha applied to Land and Development Officer (L and DO) for allotment of a plot of land for locating school being run by them - An allotment was made of 1.363 acres in year 1967 and Sabha had remitted consideration of Rs. 7185 75 p in that year itself - But for one or other reason, possession of land could not be delivered and no steps were taken by Sabha thereafter to enforce its claim - About twenty years later, i e,on 14-10-1986, 2.15 acres was proposed to be allotted at a uniform rate of Rupees eight lakhs per acre - This offer was later revised in appellants' letter D/- 18-07-1990 - Sabha accepted same and deposited sum of Rupees ten lakhs towards part consideration - It only changed its stance two months later when it came to know of judgment of Delhi High Court in Lala Amar Nath [42 (1990) DLT 61990 DLT 6 51] and on that basis demanded that rate to be charged for additional land should be @ Rupees eight lakhs per acre only and not @ Rupees thirty-eight lakhs per acre - (A) Whether Sabha is entitled to a revision of price in view of aforesaid judgment? - Held, said judgment was in no way relevant to facts of this case and, therefore, it is clear that reversal of its stand by Sabha was neither justified as a fact nor justified in law - Even assuming that said judgment was relevant in some manner, Sabha could only request for revision of price but could not claim such revision as a matter of right, in view of its acceptance of terms of letter of allotment D/- 18-07-1990 - It is not and it cannot be case of Sabha that its acceptance aforesaid is vitiated by later judgment of HC between third parties and that it is not bound by said acceptance - (B) What is proper direction to be made in matter consistent with law, justice and public interest? - Further held, proper course in all circumstances of case is to leave it open to respondents to approach appellants with above request - It is open to respondents to place all relevant facts before appellants and ask for a reconsideration of matter - It is for UOI and L and DO to consider whether their orders contained in allotment letter D/- 18-07-1990 call for revision - Appeal disposed of.
