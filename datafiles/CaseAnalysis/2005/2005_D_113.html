Rakesh Vij v Dr. Raminder Pal Singh Sethi and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 614; (2005) 8 SCC 504; JT 2005 (12) SC 1; 2005 (141) PLR 676; 2005(8) SCALE 11; [2005] Supp3 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, s. 13(3), s. 15(5), s. 2(hh) - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act (Extension To Chandigarh) Act, 1974, s. 13(3)(a)(ii) - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction (Amendment) Act, 1956 - Punjab Reorganization Act, 1966 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction (Chandigarh Amendment) Act, 1982 - Eviction - Whether in Union Territory of Chandigarh a landlord can seek eviction of a tenant from a non-residential building on ground of his own use? - Held, to completely deprive a landlord of his right to seek eviction of a tenant from a non residential building even on ground of his own use for all times to come would be highly unjust and inequitable to him - A landlord can seek eviction of a tenant from a non-residential building on ground of his own use - Rent Controller and Appellate Authority recorded concurrent finding of fact that respondent landlord bona fide needs premises in question for his own use and this finding has been affirmed in revision by HC - No illegality in impugned orders - Appeal dismissed.
