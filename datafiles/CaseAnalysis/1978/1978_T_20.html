Hareram Satpathy v Tikaram Agar W Ala and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 72; (1978) 4 SCC 58; (1978) SCC (Cr) 496; AIR 1978 SC 1568; 1978 CRLJ 1687; 1978 CrLR(SC) 413; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 349; 1978 UJ 693

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Practice & procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190 and 482 - Power of magistrate - Issued process against those whose names were not mentioned in police report-(A) Whether sub- Divisional magistrate exceeded his power by issuing process against respondents? - In instant case Sub-Divisional Magistrate took cognizance of offence on police report, after taking cognizance of offence and perusal of record he appears to have satisfied himself that there were prima facie grounds for issuing process against respondents-Held, Magistrate did not exceed power vested in him under law -(B)Whether High Court was justified in setting aside order of Sub-Divisional Magistrate? -The Magistrate was restricted to finding out whether there was a prima facie case or not for proceeding against accused and could not enter into a detailed discussion of merits or demerits of case - Scope of revisional jurisdiction very limited HC could not launch on a detailed and meticulous examination of case on merit- Further held, as HC has clearly exceeded its jurisdiction in setting aside order of Sub-Divisional Magistrate- Appeal allowed.
