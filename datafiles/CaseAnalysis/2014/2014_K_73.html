Krishan Kumar v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 383; (2014) 6 SCC 664; 2014 CRLJ 3147; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 170; 2014(7) SCALE 467

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 18 and 50 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court acquitted appellant of the charge u/s. 18 of the Act - However, HC allowed the appeal of the State and convicted the appellant u/s. 18 of the Act, sentencing him to undergo rigorous imprisonment of 10 years and to pay fine of Rs. 1 lakh - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, half hearted attempt was made by X to dig loopholes in the prosecution story - X argued that though Chaukidar was also present at the time of search, he was not produced as a witness; the prosecution does not join any independent witness at the time of seizure of opium from the appellant even when witnesses were present near the spot; and that there was an inordinate delay in sending the sample to Forensic Science Laboratory - There was no merit in any of these submissions which were adequately taken care of by the Courts below - Prosecution had established the guilt of the appellant by leading cogent evidence and the guilt was proved beyond reasonable doubt - There was no scope of interference with the said findings - Appeal dismissed.
