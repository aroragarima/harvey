Â Union of India and Others v Gopal Chandra Misra and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta, N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 25; (1978) 2 SCC 301; (1978) SCC (L&S) 303; AIR 1978 SC 694; 1978 (4) ALR 233; 1978 (37) FLR 16; 1978 LabIC 660; 1978 (1) LLJ 492; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 12; 1978 (1) SLR 521

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 217(1), 132, 133(1), 124(4), 56(1) and 190(3) - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(3) - Whether a High Court Judge, who sends to President, a letter in his own hand, intimating to resign his office with effect from a future date, is competent to withdraw same before that date is reached? - Held, prospective resignation remains mute and inoperative till date on which it was intended to take effect is reached, and can be withdrawn and rendered non est at any time before such date - It must be remembered that doctrine of public policy is only a branch of common law and its principles have been crystallised and its scope well delineated by judicial precedents - Vacancy will only occur when resignation is effective, and if it is from future date both resignation and vacation of seat will be effective simultaneously - In Reichal v. Bishop of Oxford (1889 (14) AC 259), the House of Lords, affirming decision of Court of Appeal, held that resignation was voluntary, absolute, validly executed and irrevocable and that action could not be maintained - Letter, dated 7-05-1977 addressed by appellant 2 to President, both in point of law and substance, amounts but to a proposal or notice of intention to resign at a future date (1-8-1977) and not being an absolute, complete resignation operative with immediate effect, could be and, in fact, had been validly withdrawn by said appellant through his letter, dated 15-7-1977, conveyed to President - Appeal allowed.
