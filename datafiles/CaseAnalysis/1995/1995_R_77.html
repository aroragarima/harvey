The Administrator Municipalcommittee Charkhi Dadri Andanothe v Ramji Lal Bagla And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 474; (1995) 5 SCC 272; AIR 1995 SC 2329; AIR 1995 SCW 3462; JT 1995 (5) SC 486; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 97; 1995 (2) RentLR 201; 1995(4) SCALE 559; 1995 (3) SCJ 437; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 335

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Punjab Town Improvement Act, 1922, s. 44-A - Land acquisition - Nullification - Possession - Notification for acquiring land was published in the State Gazette - Proceedings were initiated and award was passed - Concerned persons were also given compensation and possession of the land - Some portions of the land had remained unutilised and the scheme was not implemented in full within the period of five years as per s. 44-A of the Act - Respondents filed W.P. on the ground that the scheme had failed and was liable to be quashed - Order granted in favour of the respondents - Petitioners aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, interpretation of s. 44- A of the Act by the respondents was justified for passing of an order in their favour - Held, that since possession was taken pursuant to the award and payment of compensation, the title to the land vested in the Trust and the title owners came to an end - S. 44-A of the Act was only directory in nature and not mandatory - It was one of the provisions which sought to safeguard the interest of the owners of the land required for executing the schemes framed under the Act - Non-completion of the scheme within the period of 5 years specified in s. 44-A could not have the effect of invalidating the scheme and/or nullifying the acquisition of the land which had become final - Only the land which had not been utilised for the scheme was liable to be restored to its erstwhile owners, but not the land which had already been utilised - If the trust had not executed the scheme within the period of five years and the Govt. had not seen sufficient reason to extend time , the Govt. could take any of the steps for appointment of Deputy Commissioner to manage all the working of the trusts - If the legislature intended to say that failure to execute the scheme within the time prescribed u/s. 44-A of the Act, led to nullification of acquisition, it would have been enacted in the other s. in a specific and clearer language - Impugned order of the HC favouring the respondents was liable to be set-aside - Appeal allowed.
