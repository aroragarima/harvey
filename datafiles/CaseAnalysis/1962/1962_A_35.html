Second Additional Income Tax Officer, Guntur v Atmala Nagaraj and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 349; [1962] 46 ITR 609

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23 B, 35 and 46(1) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(c) and 226 - Whether revised assessment u/s. 35 against respondents are correct? - Appellant was not permitted to apply provisions of s. 35(5) - Original assessment was before operation of sub-section (5) of s. 35, but assessment of firms and revised assessments were after that date - Assessment was final notwithstanding remark in order of assessment - It was not a provisional assessment u/s. 23 B of Act, and was not stated to be so - Being a final assessment, it could be rectified only under law, as it stood then - That law did not include fiction enacted by sub-section (5), which, when enacted, could not be used in those cases which had been finally closed before 1-04-1952 - Held, s. 35(5) could not be applied in this case - Appeal dismissed.
