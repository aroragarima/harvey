Sir Shadi Lal Sugar And General Millslimited And Anr. v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28822; (1987) 4 SCC 722; AIR 1987 SC 2008; 1987 (64) CTR 199; 1987 (13) ECR 489; 1987 (31) E.L.T. 325; [1987] 168 ITR 705; JT 1987 (3) SC 189; 1987(2) SCALE 153; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 692; [1987] 33 TAXMAN 460

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 256 and 274 r/w. s. 271(1)(c) - Penalty - Concealment of income - Assessee derived its income from the manufacture and sale of sugar and confectionery, filed return of income for a/y 1958-59 - Assessment for said a/y was completed under Income Tax Act, 1922 and Income Tax Officer made certain additions like Rs. 48,500/- in respect of cane, Rs. 67,500/- in respect of shortage in cane and Rs. 21,700/- in respect of salary of outstation staff - Assessee did not challenge the said assessment order passed by the Income Tax Officer in regard to additions - Subsequently, Income Tax Officer imposed a penalty of Rs.70, 000 u/s. 274 r/w. s. 271 of 1961 Act on the ground that there was concealment of income to the tune of Rs. 1,37,700/- and the maximum penalty of Rs. 1,06,317 was imposable in law but a sum of Rs. 70, 000 was imposed as penalty considering the facts and circumstances of the case - On appeal, Tribunal, after considering the entire matter, reduced the penalty to Rs. 5,000/- and held that the assessee had not concealed income to the extent of Rs.67,500/- and Rs. 21,700/- within the meaning of s. 271(1)(c) of the 1961 Act - HC allowed the appeal filed against order of the Tribunal - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the finding of the Tribunal that the assessee had not concealed income to the extent of Rs.67,500/- and Rs. 21,700/- within the meaning of s. 271(1)(c) of the 1961 Act is correct in law? - Held, the conclusions reached by the Tribunal should not be coloured by any irrelevant considerations or matters of prejudice and if there were any circumstances which required to be explained by the assessee, the assessee should be given an opportunity of doing so - In the present case, the Tribunal had taken into consideration the fact that the assessee had admitted the additions as its income when faced with non-disclosure in assessment proceedings - HC accused the Tribunal of not considering the time when the assessee admitted the additions - However, it was duly considered by the tribunal who held that the assessee admitted that these were the income of the assessee but that was not an admission that there was deliberate concealment - Moreover, factual appreciation by HC not permissible - Hence, HC was in error in so far as it held that the Tribunal had acted incorrectly - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
