Chhotu and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3221; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1143; AIR 1997 SC 3501; 1997 CRLJ 4394; JT 1997 (7) SC 565

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 34 - Murder - Common intention - Assault by three persons on deceased by giving repeated blows upon him for sometime - Held, evidence of two eye-witnesses establishing presence of other eye-witnesses, found to be acceptable by Court - Presence of such other eye-witnesses cannot be doubted only on ground that they were not residents of same locality where incident took place or on ground of failure of police to seize their blood, stained clothes or to promptly examine them u/s. 161, CrPC, 1973 - Such facts only indicate remissness on part of police but evidence of eye-witness would not be impaired thereby - One of accused seen standing with knife in his hand - No shared common intention, on ground of such fact only - Accused entitled to benefit of doubt.
