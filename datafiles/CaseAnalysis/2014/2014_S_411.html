Sukhjit Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 665; (2014) 10 SCC 270; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4480; 2014 (4) CGLJ 369; 2015 CRLJ 624; JT 2014 (11) SC 403; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4048; 2015 (1) OLR 94; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 902; 2014(11) SCALE 414; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 608

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Illiterate

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 364 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Abduction - Conviction - Challenged - Instant appeal filed challenging the judgment and order passed by the HC, whereby the Single Judge affirmed the conviction recorded by the Trial Judge u/s. 364 of IPC.

Held, no incriminating material has been brought to the notice of the accused while putting questions u/s. 313 of CrPC. Hence, when the requisite questions have not been put to the accused it has caused immense prejudice to him, more so, when there is no evidence to establish his complicity in the alleged abduction. The judgment of conviction and order of sentence recorded by the Trial Court and affirmed by the HC are set aside. Appeal allowed.
