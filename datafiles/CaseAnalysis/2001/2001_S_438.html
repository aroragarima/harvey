Sanjay Alias Kaka and Others v State (N.C.T. of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20700; (2001) 3 SCC 190; (2001) SCC (Cr) 449; AIR 2001 SC 979; 2001 (1) Crimes 268; 2001 CRLJ 1231; 2001 (89) DLT 745; JT 2001 (2) SC 461; 2001 (1) RCR(Criminal) 677; 2001(1) SCALE 695; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 904; 2001 (1) SLT 940; 2001 (1) Supreme 692

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120 B, 302, 342, 392, 394, 397, 398 and 411 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25, 27, 54 and 59 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Amendment Act, 1993, s. 5 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, ss. 313 and 162 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 25, 26, 27, 106, 114 and 114(a) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether in absence of direct evidence in form of eye-witnesses, Trial Court can convict accused? - Held, effort of Criminal Court should not be to prowl for imaginative doubts - Unless doubt is of a reasonable dimension which a judicially conscientious mind entertains with some objectivity no benefit can be claimed by accused - (B) Whether Trial Court committed a mistake of law for basing its findings and conviction on evidence which was not reliable but also inadmissible in evidence under relevant provisions of law? - Held, Court must ensure credibility of evidence by police because this provision is vulnerable to abuse - Fact is deposed to as discovered in consequence of information received from a person accused of any offence, in custody of a police officer, so much of such information, whether it amounts to a confession or not, as relates distinctly to fact thereby discovered, may be proved - Extent of information admissible must depend on exact nature of fact discovered to which such information is required to relate - (C) Whether any fact discovered in consequence of information received from a person accused of any offence in custody of a police officer would amount to confession, if no whether they may be proved against accused ? - Held, it cannot be discarded only on ground that it was made to a police officer during investigation - Court has to be cautious that no effort is made by prosecution to make out a statement of accused with a simple case of recovery as a case of discovery of fact in order to attract provisions of s. 27 - Information as relates distinctly to fact thereby discovered may be proved - Principle of admitting evidence of statements made by a person giving information leading to discovery of facts which may be used in evidence against him is manifestly reasonable - Appeals dismissed.
