Nehru Yuva Kendra Sangathan v Mehbub Alam Laskar

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 76; (2008) 2 SCC 479; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1459; 2008(2) ALL MR 812; 2008 (1) AWC 1056; 2008 (1) ESC 120; 2008 (116) FLR 896; JT 2008 (2) SC 163; 2008 (2) LLJ 868; 2008 (2) LLN 36; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 1073; 2008(1) SCALE 590; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 1069; 2008 (2) SLR 103; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 613

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Simpliciter Or Punitive

Summary: Appellant an autonomous body operating under the Department of Youth Affairs and Sports, Ministry of Human Resource Development - Respondent was appointed as a Youth Co-ordinator in terms of an offer of appointment - Termination - Order of reinstatement - Appeal against - Held, if the employee is found to have committed a misconduct, although an order terminating probation would appear to be innocuous on its face, the same would be vitiated, if in effect and substance it is found to be stigmatic in nature - Mere holding of a preliminary enquiry where explanation is called for from the employee, if followed by an innocuous order of discharge, may not be held to be punitive in nature but not when it is founded on a finding of misconduct - Not find any legal infirmity in the judgment of the High Court - Appeals disposed of.
