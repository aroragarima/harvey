A and G Projects and Technologies Limited v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1945; (2009) 2 SCC 326; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 122; 2008(16) SCALE 178; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 321; [2009] 19 VST 239

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Assessment Year, Collection Of Tax, Appeal allowed, Inter-State Sales, Registered Dealer, Tax Payable, Unregistered Dealer, Liability To Tax, Sales Tax Law, Inter-State Trade, Collected, Relevant Provisions, Intra-State Sale, Sale In The State, Second Sale, Subsequent Sale, Sale In The Course Of Export, Transfer Of Documents Of Title, Civil Works, Inter- State Sale

Summary: Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957; Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - If the sales stood covered under s. 3(a) and if they were not entitled to exemption under s. 6(2), whether the appellant could have been taxed by the Department by invoking the proviso to s. 9(1) of the CST ACT 1956? - Held, movement of the goods is only material for the purpose of deciding whether the sale took place in the course of inter-State trade or commerce or whether such sale was purely an intra-State transaction - Name given to a transaction by the parties concerned, does not decide the nature of the transaction - Every dealer liable to pay tax under the 1956 Act on all sales of goods other than electrical energy effected by him in the course of inter-State trade - In order to attract s. 6(2), it is essential that the concerned sale must be a subsequent inter-State sale effected by transfer of documents of title to the goods during the movement of the goods from one State to another and it must be preceded by a prior inter-State sale - Once the sales fall under s. 3(a) then under s. 9(1) the tax has got to be collected by the State of Tamil Nadu from which the movement of the goods commenced - Appeal allowed.
