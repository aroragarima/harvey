State of Madhya Pradesh v Shyamsunder Trivedi and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1216; (1995) 4 SCC 262; (1995) SCC (Cr) 715; AIR 1995 SCW 2793; 1995 (3) CCR 36; JT 1995 (4) SC 445; 1995(3) SCALE 343; 1995 (2) SCJ 451; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 149, 147, 201, 342 and 218 - Custodial death - Proof of - Held, doctor opined that cause of death was shock as a result of extensive external injuries and that death had taken place within 24 hours preceding postmortem examination - Respondents presence at police station was amply established by prosecution evidence, of offence of causing multiple external injuries to deceased which ultimately resulted in death - Respondents participation in removal of dead body to hospital with a view to screen offence - Testimony of witnesses who are independent persons and respectable members of bar established beyond any reasonable doubt that dead body of deceased was taken out from Police Station and transported in jeep of Fisheries Department for postmortem to hospital - Further held, torture in custody flouts basic rights of citizens recognised by Indian Constitution and is an affront to human dignity - Respondents 1 and 3 to 5 had participated in causing injuries to deceased while in police custody, directly or indirectly, and even if it is not possible to say that they intended to cause death, and they can certainly be clothed with knowledge that injuries which were being caused to deceased at police station were likely to cause his death though probably without any intention to cause his death or even to cause such bodily injuries to him as were likely to cause death - Their offence would, thus, squarely fall u/ss. 304 Part II/34 IPC - Respondents 3 to 5 are also guilty of offences u/ss. 201 and 342 IPC and holding them so guilty and thereby to be convicted - Appeal disposed of.
