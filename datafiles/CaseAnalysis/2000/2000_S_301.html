Manish Dixit And Ors. v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3182; (2001) SCC (Cr) 235; (2001) 1 SCC 596; AIR 2001 SC 93; 2000 (4) CCR 176; 2000 (4) Crimes 171; 2001 CRLJ 133; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 237; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 583; 2000(7) SCALE 218; 2000 (7) SLT 647; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 74; 2000 (7) Supreme 364

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Re-Examination

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 165, 462 and 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120B, 302, 364, 365 and 379 - Indian Evidence Act, 1873, ss. 34 and 165 - Abduction and murder - High Court confirmed conviction only as against A 2 and offence against A 1 was found u/s. 411 - Appeal - (A) Whether entry in register of hotel, by itself is of no avail on language of s. 34 of Evidence Act? - Held, expression 'books of accounts' means books in which merchants, traders or businessmen generally keep their accounts i.e., statements of debits and credits or receipts and payments - Register kept at counter of a hotel need not contain any statement of account - Until it is shown that such register also pertained to pecuniary transactions involving customers of hotel same cannot be treated as a book of accounts - Entry therein cannot become sole premise to charge a person with liability - (B) Whether proved circumstances are too scanty for coming to conclusion that A 1 was involved in abduction and murder? - Further held, circumstance has been established by prosecution but that is not decisive enough to point to involvement of that accused in murder of deceased - (C) Comments against witness - Further held, Trial Court and HC should have avoided making such unsavoury comments against a witness in such a manner as to entail serious implications on his career, merely because answers which were extracted from him through cross questions contained contradictions or inconsistencies - Disparaging remarks and direction to initiate departmental action against him could have very serious impact on his official career - Appeal disposed of.
