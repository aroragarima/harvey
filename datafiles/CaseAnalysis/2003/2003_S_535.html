Bharat v State of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

30 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 144; (2003) 3 SCC 106; (2003) SCC (Cr) 738; AIR 2003 SC 1433; 2003 (1) CCR 322; 2003 (2) Crimes 168; 2003 (4) CRJ 580; 2003 CRLJ 1297; 2003 (1) JCC 283; JT 2003 (1) SC 635; 2003 (3) MPLJ 292; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 637; 2003(1) SCALE 610; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 748; 2003 (1) SLT 724; 2003 (2) Supreme 116; 2003 (2) UC 860

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 394, 498 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 302, 394, 498 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified.

Held, there was neither proper and legal identification of ornaments nor recovery as a consequence of statement of appellant. Finding of Courts below that appellant must have killed deceased in greed of ornaments which were robbed by him and subsequently recovered from his house, on exclusion of evidence of recovery and identification of ornaments, cannot be sustained as SC is left with only circumstance of deceased having left with appellant only on 08-01-1981. It cannot be held that prosecution has established charge against appellant only on ground that appellant has failed to offer any explanation in his statement. SC have already come to conclusion as above that prosecution has failed to establish that death of deceased took place on 08-01-1981, earliest it could be on 10-01-1981. There is nothing to show as to what transpired between these dates. Mere non-explanation cannot lead to proof of guilt against appellant. Prosecution has to prove its case against appellant beyond reasonable doubt. Chain of circumstances is not complete so as to sustain conviction of appellant. No substance in contention urged on behalf of State that this Court may not interfere in concurrent findings of fact of Courts below. There has been a complete miscarriage of justice to appellant. Impugned orders passed by Courts below are set aside. Appeal allowed.
