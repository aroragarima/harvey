Sushmita Basu and Others v Ballygunge Siksha Samity and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 513; (2006) 7 SCC 680; 2006 (6) AWC 6033; 2006(9) SCALE 459; 2006 (6) SLR 30; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 506

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Delhi School Education Act, 1973, s. 10 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Prayer for issuance of writ of mandamus directing authorities of school to fix salaries of teaching and non-teaching staff of school and to remove all anomalies in scales of pay as recommended by Third Pay Commission as extended to other Govt. aided schools and Govt. schools - There has been just treatment of teachers by first respondent - Held, there is no reason to interfere even on ground that appellants are being treated unfairly by their employer, educational institution, or on basis that this is a case in which conscience of court is shocked, compelling it to enter arena to afford relief to teachers - To issue a writ of mandamus by court against a private educational institution like first respondent herein, would be justified only if a public law element is involved and if it is only a private law remedy no Writ Petition would lie - Appeal dismissed.
