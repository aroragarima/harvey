State of Madhya Pradesh v Anoop Singh

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 454; (2015) 7 SCC 773; 2015 (3) CGLJ 408; 2015 (3) JabLJ 102; 2015 (1) JCC 2450; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 358; 2015(7) SCALE 445; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 960

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Birth Certificate

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366 and 376 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, r. 12(3)(b) - Age of victim - Medical evidence - Charge-sheet was filed against accused for offences u/ss. 363, 366 and 376 of IPC and upon perusal of evidence, Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 363, 366 and 376 of IPC and passed sentence - However, on appeal, HC set aside judgment and order of conviction and held inter alia that prosecution failed to prove fact that girl was less than 16 years of age at time of incident and that prosecutrix was consenting party - Whether prosecutrix was below 16 years of age at the time of incident.

Held, documents, Birth certificate and Middle School Examination Certificate of prosecutrix could be used for ascertaining the age of the prosecutrix as per r. 12(3)(b) of the Rules. The difference of two days in the dates in said documents is immaterial and just on such minor discrepancy, the evidence in the form of said documents could not be discarded and so, Trial Court was correct in relying on said documents. Further, HC should have relied firstly on documents as stipulated u/r. 12(3)(b) and only in absence, the medical opinion should have been sought when Trial Court rightly held that ossification test is not the sole criteria for determination of date of birth of prosecutrix as her certificate of birth and also the certificate of her medical examination had been enclosed. Hence, in view of medical examination reports, the statements of PWs which inspire confidence and the certificates proving the age of the prosecutrix to be below 16 of years of age on the date of the incident, impugned judgment of HC is set aside and that of Trial Court is upheld. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - As per ruling of SC in case of Mahadeo v. State of Maharashtra and Another, r. 12(3) of the Rules is applicable in determining the age of victim of rape.
