Hans Raj Agarwal & Anr. v Chief Commnr. of Income Tax & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1561; (2003) 2 SCC 295; AIR 2003 SC 2112; 2003 (179) CTR 89; [2003] 259 ITR 265; 2002(9) SCALE 751; 2003 (1) SLT 128; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 648; 2003 (1) Supreme 94; 174 TAXATION 221

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Auction Sale, Transfer Of Immovable Property

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss.269UD(1), 269UC - Constitutional validity of Chapter XX-C and seeking the setting aside of the order passed u/s.269-UD(1) basically on the ground that it was passed in violation of natural justice and was otherwise unconstitutional - Held, it was the appellants and transferor's act of filing a fresh statement u/s.269-UC which gave rise to a fresh period of limitation Under the first proviso to s.269UD(1) time is to be computed from the receipt of 'the statement' under form 37-I In this case, 'the statement' was the second one - It was open to the Appropriate Authority, in such circumstances, to act on the second statement and pass the compulsory purchase order within a period of two months from the receipt of that statement - The first compulsory purchase order could not, in the circumstances, be said to be invalid on this ground - If the first purchase order has been set aside without protest, the appellants present grievance that the first purchase order is otherwise invalid is ill founded - Appeal dismissed.
