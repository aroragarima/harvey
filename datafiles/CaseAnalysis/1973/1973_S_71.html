Khashaba Maruti Shelke v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 170; (1973) 2 SCC 449; (1973) SCC (Cr) 863; AIR 1973 SC 2474; 1973 CRLJ 1607; 1973 CrLR(SC) 546; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Explosive

Summary: Criminal - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 302, 307, 324 and 333 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Lack of direct evidence - Appreciation of circumstantial evidence - Held, circumstantial evidence must be so complete as to exclude every hypothesis other than that of guilt of accused - In order to base conviction of an accused on circumstantial evidence court must be certain that circumstantial evidence is, of such a character as is consistent only with guilt of accused - If, however, circumstantial evidence admits of any other rational explanations, in such an event an element of doubt would creep in and accused must necessarily have benefit thereof - Circumstances relied upon should be of a conclusive character - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
