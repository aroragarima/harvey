Bench

J. Chelameswar, R.M. Lodha, A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 576; (2014) 9 SCC 737; 2014 CRLJ 4598; JT 2014 (10) SC 209; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 344; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 103; 2014(10) SCALE 84; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Review

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 40 r. 3 - Death sentence - Stayed - Review - Instant petitions were filed by a referral Order where in each of them execution of death sentence awarded to petitioners has been stayed - Hearing of cases in which death sentence has been awarded should be by a Bench of at least three if not five SC Judges and hearing of Review Petitions in death sentence cases should not be by circulation but should only be in open Court, and accordingly O. 40 r. 3 of the Rules, should be declared to be un-Constitutional inasmuch as persons on death row are denied an oral hearing.

Held, the circumstances do not warrant the death penalty, whereas another may feel it to be a fit case fully justifying the death penalty, a convict who has suffered the sentence of death and files a review petition, the necessity of oral hearing in such a review petition becomes an integral part of 'reasonable procedure' - 'Reasonable procedure' would encompass oral hearing of review petitions arising out of death penalties. Death penalty is irreversible in nature. Once a death sentence is executed, that results in taking away the life of the convict. If it is found thereafter that such a sentence was not warranted, that would be of no use as the life of that person cannot be brought back. This being so, if the fundamental right to life is involved, any procedure to be just, fair and reasonable should take into account the two factors mentioned above. A limited oral hearing even at the review stage is mandated by art. 21 of the Constitution in all death sentence cases. Review petitions arising out of death sentence cases is carved out as a separate category as oral hearing in such review petitions is found to be mandated by art. 21 of the Constitution. While awarding a death sentence, in most of the cases, SC would generally be affirming the decision on this aspect already arrived at by two Courts below namely the Trial Court as well as HC. After such an affirmation, the scope of review of such a judgment may be very narrow. At the same time, when it is a question of life and death of a person, even a remote chance of deviating from such a decision while exercising the review jurisdiction, would justify oral hearing in a review petition. Every cause, appeal or other proceedings arising out of a case in which death sentence has been confirmed or awarded by HC shall be heard by a Bench consisting of not less than three Judges. If a Bench of less than three Judges, hearing a cause, appeal or matter, is of the opinion that accused should be sentenced to death it shall refer the matter to the Chief Justice who shall thereupon constitute a Bench of not less than three Judges for hearing it. Petitions disposed of.
