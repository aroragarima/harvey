Vijay Kumar Sharma @ Manju v Raghunandan Sharma @ Baburam And Others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 211; (2010) 2 SCC 486; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 578; 2010 (79) ALR 237; 2010 (2) AWC 1969; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 365; 2016 (4) CompLJ 50; 2010 (1) CTC 854; JT 2010 (2) SC 155; 2010 (2) MPLJ 283; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 912; 2010(1) SCALE 688; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 582; 2010 (1) UC 469

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Civil Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 8, 11 and 15(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11 - First respondent filed a suit against the appellant alleging that their father died leaving a will bequeathing portions of property in possession of appellant to him - Appellant also filed a Civil Suit for partition and separate possession of his share in the ancestral properties and also sought a declaration that the will was fabricated, null and void - Respondent nos. 2 and 3 (executors of the will) filed an application u/s. 8 of the Act in the said suits alleging that as per the declaration made by deceased before his death any dispute in connection with the will should be decided by arbitration - Trial Court held that in view of the said provision for resolution of disputes by arbitration, its jurisdiction was barred and suits were dismissed - Appeal filed thereagainst still pending - First respondent filed an application for appointment of an independent arbitrator - Arbitrator was appointed - Hence, present appeal - (A) Pendency of appeal against dismissal of suits - Effect thereof - Held, mere fact that an appeal from an order dismissing the suit under O. 7 r. 11 CPC (on the ground that the disputes require to be settled by Arbitration) is pending before the HC, will not come in the way of the appointment of an arbitrator u/s. 11 r/w .s 15(2) of the Act, if the Authority u/s. 11 finds it necessary to appoint an Arbitrator - (B) Whether there is an arbitration agreement in existence between appellant and respondent no. 1? - Held, alleged Will, does not contain any provision for arbitration - A unilateral declaration by a father that any future disputes among the sons should be settled by an arbitrator named by him, cannot be considered as an arbitration agreement among his children, or such of his children who become parties to a dispute - There is no arbitration agreement between the parties and the Court committed a serious error in allowing the application u/ss. 11 and 15(2) of the Act and holding that there is an arbitration agreement between the parties to the dispute and appointing an arbitrator - Appeal allowed.
