Anil Sood v Presiding Officer, Labour Court Ii

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2910; (2001) 10 SCC 534; 2001 (2) CLR 18; 2001 (89) FLR 229; JT 2001 (3) SC 240; 2001 (1) LLJ 1113; 2001(2) SCALE 193; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1043

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11(1) and (3) - Central Rules, r. 2 - A reference was made under Industrial Disputes Act to Labour Court - Award was made - An application was filed by appellant on averring that he had no notice of proceedings in reference - That application came to be dismissed on ground that Labour Court has become functus officio having passed an award - Legality of award passed by High Court and Tribunal? - When matters are referred to Tribunal or Court they have to be decided objectively and they have to exercise their discretion in a judicial manner without arbitrariness by following general principles of law and rules of natural justice - Held, both HC and Tribunal fell into an error in stating that Labour Court had become functus officio after making award though ex parte - Appeal allowed.
