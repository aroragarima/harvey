Devender Kumar Singla v Baldev Krishan Singla

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 155; (2005) 9 SCC 15; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1185; AIR 2004 SC 3084; 2004 CRLJ 1774; JT 2004 (2) SC 539; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 15; 2004(2) SCALE 537; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 459; 2004 (2) UC 787

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Post Dated Cheque, Stop Payment

Summary: [A]Practice and Procedure - absence of any suggestion during cross examination cannot be made up by a statement under s.313 Cr.P.C., 1973 - the prosecution does not get an opportunity to question the accused about his stand in the statement under Section 313.[B]IPC, 1860, s.420, inter-linked appeals - held on facts - conviction against original respondent justified and upheld, sentence reduced - complainant's appeal dismissed.
