Commissioner of Income Tax, Patiala and Others v Shahzada Nand and Sons and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 236; AIR 1966 SC 1342; 1977 CTR 275; [1966] 60 ITR 392; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indirect Tax - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 34(1)(a), 34(IA) - A/y 1945-46 - Escaped Income - Assessment - Time Barred - Challenged - Respondent no.1 was undivided Hindu family firm and was assessed a/y 1945-46 meanwhile said undivided family was parted and new firm evolved - Income-tax Authorities issued notice to members of defunct Hindu undivided family u/s. 34(1)(a) of the 1922 Act in respect of a/y 1945-46 on ground that certain income of said family had escaped assessment - Respondent No.1/assessee took matter on appeal before Commissioner, who held that said notice was barred by time, though on merits confirmed order of Income-tax Officer - Income-tax Dept. as well as respondent no.1 preferred appeals against said order to Tribunal which held that notice was barred by time and, therefore, Income-tax authorities had no jurisdiction to give finding on merits - Meanwhile s. 34(1)(a) of the Act was amended by the 1956 Act, subject to certain conditions, notice u/s. 34(1)(a) of the Act could be issued any time - Income-tax Officer issued notice to respondents to file returns for a/y 1945-46 - Aggrieved respondents No.2 to 5 approached Central Board of Revenue for redress without any success - Respondent filed writ petition before HC which was allowed - Aggrieved appellants filed Letter patents appeal which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether notice could be issued at any time for reassessment u/s. 34(1)(a) of the Act as amended by 1956 Act, of 1922 Act in respect of concealed income to which s. 34(1A) thereof applied was just and proper - Held, relied on Debi Dutt v. T. Belan 1958 Indlaw CAL 13 and S. C. Prashar v. Vasantsen 1954 Indlaw MUM 12, new notices could be issued under subsection after 1-4- 1956 and notices already issued before that date were pending - They would be disposed of in manner prescribed by sub-ss. of s. 34 (IA), (1B), (1C) and (1D) - All the said sub-sections formed integral code and Legislature, presumably, intended to keep said-sub-sections where under proceedings had already been initiated and make available to said proceedings that sub-s. (1A) was kept in super-abundant caution - Hence, order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
