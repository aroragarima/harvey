East India Hotels Limited v Syndicate Bank

Supreme Court of India

12 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 888; (1992) Supp2 SCC 29; 1991 (3) CCC 412; 1993 (1) CompLJ 1; 1991 (45) DLT 476; JT 1991 (6) SC 112; 1991(2) SCALE 638

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Special Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 5 - Indian Easement, Act, 1882, s. 52 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Bombay Rents, Hotels and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 15-A - Bank executed a leave and licence agreement in favour of company in respect of 15,000 sq. ft. on mezzanine to ground floor, on a monthly compensation of Rs. 60,000 per month, belonging to company, for a period of 12 years - At end of 12th year company informed bank that request for renewal of licence was not acceptable and requested to hand over vacant possession on expiry of term - Bank did not hand over vacant possession of premises even after expiry of term - Bank started functioning its business at another place but its papers, furniture, fixtures etc. continued to remain in premises - Company did not permit bank staff to enter premises - Bank filed a suit u/s. 6 of Specific Relief Act, 1963 on original side of High Court - HC decreed suit - Whether bank had been dispossessed except in due course of law? - Held, stray or intermittent acts of possession does not constitute settled possession - Person in settled possession is entitled to resist attempt of even owner or persons claiming under him to dispossess trespasser - Only exception is that a recent or concealed possession does not enable a trespasser to defend his possession - Further, bank being a licensee and having become a trespasser after expiry of term has no right to claim possession from company who is admittedly owner of premises - Grant or not to grant relief under Specific Relief Act, 1963 lies in judicial discretion of Court - Bank is not entitled to any decree u/s. 6 of Act - Appeal allowed.
