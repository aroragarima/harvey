Ashish Batham v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1924; (2002) 7 SCC 317; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1718; AIR 2002 SC 3206; 2002 (3) CCR 337; 2002 (4) Crimes 220; 2003 (1) CRJ 71; 2002 CRLJ 4676; 2002 (3) JCC 1883; JT 2002 (6) SC 593; 2002(6) SCALE 289; 2002 (5) SLT 179; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 146; 2002 (6) Supreme 228; 2003 (1) UC 9

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 449 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted by Trial Court for offence u/s. 302 IPC on two counts on ground that he killed N and P daughters of PW-2 and sentenced to death and imposition of life imprisonment for offence u/s. 449 IPC for committing house trespass to commit murders - Appeal filed by appellant in HC and reference made to HC for confirmation in death reference came to be heard together by DB - While sustaining conviction u/ss. 302 and 449 IPC, HC altered and reduced sentence to imprisonment of life by making sentence to run concurrently - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, that on going through materials on record and judgments of courts below fact that at busy place like one in and nearby Hospital area thickly residential with surroundings murder of two girls could be said to have been executed without attracting attention of anyone nearby or regular passersby at that point of time in area was improbable prosecution version that appellant alone was and could have been culprit - Further, appellant seemed to have been roped in merely on suspicion and story of prosecution built on materials placed seemed neither truth nor wholly truth and findings of courts below, though seem to be concurrent, were not accepted having regard to infirmities and illegalities vitiating them and patent errors apparent on face of record, resulting in serious and grave miscarriage of justice to appellant - Judgments of Courts below were set aside - Appeal allowed.
