Vallabhai Nathabhai v Bai Jivi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 415; (1969) 1 SCC 392; AIR 1969 SC 1190; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 829; 1969 MahLJ 958; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 309

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1948, s. 15, 29(1)(2) and 84 - Appellant-tenant voluntarily handed over possession of the lands to the respondent-landlord - However, the surrender though voluntary thus was not in accordance with s. 15 and therefore was not, valid and binding on the appellant - It is not in dispute that respondent 1 thereafter personally cultivated the said lands - Appellant applied to the Deputy Collector u/s. 84 of the Act for summary eviction of respondent 1 - Whether s. 84 would apply to the facts of this case? - Held, u/s. 29(1) and (2), it is the duty of the Mamlatdar to restore possession to the tenant or to the landlord as the case may be - Thus, in all cases where a tenant is entitled to possession of land under any of the provisions of the Act, he has a right u/s. 29(1) to apply to the Mamlatdar for restoration of possession - While giving drastic powers of summary eviction to an administrative officer, the legislature was careful to restrict this power firstly because the result otherwise would be to deprive the person evicted u/s. 84 of his remedy of appeal before the Collector which he would have if the order were to be passed u/s. 29(1) and secondly, because it would enable a tenant to bypass a judicial inquiry by the Mamlatdar u/s. 29(1) by directly applying to the Collector u/s. 84 - Therefore, the contention that ss. 29(1) and 84 provide alternative remedies and a choice to the tenant cannot possibly be correct - High Court was correct in its interpretation of the two sections and the conclusion which it arrived at in holding on the facts of the present case that the Collector had no jurisdiction u/s. 84 to entertain the tenant's application - Appeal dismissed.
