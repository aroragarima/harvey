State Of Rajasthan v Smt. Kalki & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, A.P. Sen, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 296; (1981) SCC (Cr) 593; (1981) 2 SCC 752; AIR 1981 SC 1390; 1981 CRLJ 1012; 1981 CrLR(SC) 350; 1981(1) SCALE 645; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: . Practice and Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872,s.3 -IPC,1860,s.300 - Widow of the deceased - 'Interested' or 'related' witness - Deceased killed in presence of wife - Whether the wife of the deceased in the case is an 'interested' or 'related witness'? - A witness may be called 'Interested' only when he or she derives some benefit from the result of a litigation or in the decree in a civil case or in seeing an accused person punished-A witness who is a natural one and is the only possible eye witness in the circumstances of a case cannot be said to be 'Interested' - Held, that a widow is a related witness as she is wife of deceased and has no benefit in seeing an accused person punished, thus, convicting the accused of murder -Appeal Allowed.
