Sadashiv Shyama Sawant [D], Through L.Rs., and Others v Anita Anant Sawant

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 134; (2010) 3 SCC 385; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 798; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 24; 2010(3) ALL MR 410; 2010 (79) ALR 479; 2010 (2) AWC 1914; 2010 (2) Bom.C.R. 554; 2010 (3) CalLT 35; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 311; 2010 (3) CTC 663; JT 2010 (2) SC 305; 2010 (1) KLT 808; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 728; 2010(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2010 (3) MPLJ 265; 2010 (1) RCR(Rent) 237; 2010 (110) RD 628; 2010(2) SCALE 530; [2010] 2 S.C.R. 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - Rent Control - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 6 and 6(1) - Maintainability of suit by the landlord of immovable property in respect of property let out to the tenant who has been dispossessed forcibly by a third party - (A) Whether in a case where a tenant in exclusive possession is dispossessed forcibly by a person other than landlord, can landlord maintain suit u/s. 6 of the Act against such person for immediate possession? - Held, landlord by letting out the property to a tenant does not lose possession as he continues to retain the legal possession although actual possession, user and control of that property is with the tenant - As a matter of law, the dispossession of tenant by a third party is dispossession of the landlord - Word "dispossessed" in s. 6(1) of the Act must be read in this context and not in light of the actual possession alone - A landlord who holds the possession through his tenant is competent to maintain suit u/s. 6 of the Act and recover possession from a trespasser who has forcibly dispossessed his tenant - There is nothing in s. 6 of the Act to bar a landlord from suing a trespasser in possession even when, at the date of dispossession, the property is in actual occupation of a tenant entitled to possession - (B) Whether tenant is a necessary party in such suit? - Held, s. 6 of the Act provides that suit to recover possession under the said provision could be filed by the person who is dispossessed or any person claiming through him - Tenant having lost the possession though without his consent to a third party, may not be interested in recovery of possession - In such circumstances, if a landlord brings the suit to recover possession against trespasser u/s. 6, it cannot be laid down as an absolute proposition that tenant must necessarily be impleaded as party to such suit - Appeal dismissed.
