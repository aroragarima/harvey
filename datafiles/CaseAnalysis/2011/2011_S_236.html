Narmada Bachao Andolan v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 462; (2011) 12 SCC 333; 2011(5) ALL MR 388; 2012 (6) AWC 6212; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 225; 2011 (3) JabLJ 209; JT 2011 (8) SC 307; 2011(8) SCALE 38; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 84

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Administrative - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land acquisition - Grant of compensation in lieu of alternative land - Respondent State consturcted a dam after getting Environment and Forest clearance and approval from Government of India - Cabinet of Ministers approved payment of Special Rehabilitation Grant (SRG) to be paid to oustees whose lands were acquired - Construction was completed up to crest level - Respondent State issued Notification dated 5-3-2008 regarding submergence of four nearby villages - Appellant association filed writ petition claiming various reliefs, inter-alia, to stop further construction which may cause submergence so that displaced families were resettled and rehabilitated in 6 months before the submergence; to direct respondent State to provide irrigated agricultural land to eligible oustees including encroachers and landless labourers; to declare the order dated 7-6-1991 passed by Narmada Valley Development Department (NVDD) amending para 5.1 of the Re- settlement and Rehabilitation Policy, 1991 (R & R Policy) to be ultra vires and unconstitutional, being arbitrary and mala fides - HC disposed of writ petition by giving various directions holding that alternative remedy before respondent's Grievance Redressal Authority (GRA) was efficacious and no extraordinary situation prevailed warranting the HC to interfere at the present stage - HC further held that landless labourers were not entitled for allotment of agricultural land but entitled to the grant offered by respondent assessed under 1894 Act - HC further observed that person aggrieved, if any, could approach the GRA if he was not satisfied with the reliefs granted to him in terms of the R & R Policy - Whether the amendment of cl. 5.1 of the R & R Policy was valid - Held, procedure which led to the amendment of R & R Policy revealed that cl. 3 of the Policy provided for entitlement of oustees to get land in lieu of the land acquired - Cl. 5 prescribed only the procedure for allotment of land u/cl. 3 of the R & R Policy - Amendment of R & R Policy on 7-6-1991 which was under challenge by the appellant only facilitated those oustees who were not willing to take the land in lieu of the land acquired - Such an amendment was brought on demand of the oustees as an alternative, however, it did not take away the right of the oustees to claim land in lieu of the land acquired, for the simple reason that there was no amendment in year 1991 to cl. 3.2 of the R & R Policy and the amendment to the said cl. 3.2 incorporated on 27-4-2002 was not under challenge - Amendment under challenge simply facilitated an oustee to claim compensation instead of land for the reason that oustee may be willing to settle in another State or in urban area or wanted to adopt any other vocation/profession or wanted to start any other business - Hence, amendment to cl. 5.1 remained inconsequential so far as the right of an oustee to claim land in lieu of the land acquired was concerned - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Decision of Council of Ministers - Amendment by subordinate authority - Permissibility - Whether the State Council of Ministers was, as a matter of law, permitted to delegate its power to a subordinate authority to amend its own decision - Held, whether there could be further delegation by the minister to the officer subordinate to him depends on the provisions of the Rules of Business - Rules of Business operated even when a statute did not authorize sub-delegation - A provision permitting sub-delegation was merely supplementary and could be no ground for excluding the ordinary method by which the Government's executive business was carried on - However, requirement of the Rules of Business should be complied with in order to give validity to the action or decision taken - Hence, delegation was permissible and that Rules of Business were directory in nature - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Land & Property - Administrative - Land acquisition - Alternative land - Entitlement - Whether the landless labourers were entitled to the alternative land - Held, rehabilitation policy for the oustees provided that the displaced families would be rehabilitated maintaining existing structure of social groups as far as possible, in the command area or near the periphery of the affected areas in accordance with their preferences - Policy made it clear that there was no provision for allotment of agricultural land to the landless labourers - However, when the project was placed before the Ministry of Welfare, Government of India, it granted clearance providing for allotment of minimum 2 hectares of land for all landless labourers - Further, vide letter dated 5-4-1997, the NVDD wrote to the Ministry of Welfare informing it that landless labourers had been proposed for giving them minimum 2 hectares of land as per its clearance but action in that respect would be taken as per decision of the Government - Letter of approval mentioned 'allotting a minimum of 2 hectares of land for all landless labourers' and said that unmarried major daughters would be treated as separate entities for that particular purpose, i.e. of allotting 2 hectares of land - Appellant never plead that the cause of unmarried major daughters to be treated as separate entities for allotment of land and that issue was dealt with in negative in Narmada Bachao Andolan v. State of M.P., 2011 INDLAW SC 332 - Further, even if the allotment of land to landless labourers as a condition, the respondent State did not accept such a condition - Ministry of Welfare's clearance was not statutory, like any other statutory clearance e.g. clearance granted by Environment and Forest Ministry and nothing in that clearance as to what would be the consequence for non-compliance with those conditions - Subsequent thereto, it was evident from the record that representations had been filed on behalf of the oustees before the Ministry of Welfare - However, no action had ever been taken by the Ministry of Welfare that the terms incorporated by it while granting clearance were not being adhered to and in spite of writing several letters, the Ministry of Welfare did not consider it proper to take any action or even to refer those letters to the State Government or to the NVDD, thus, the said Authorities also treated the same as non-statutory - Even otherwise, it did not seem that landless labourer could be entitled for allotment of agricultural land admeasuring two hectares - Neither it had ever been contemplated nor it was compatible with R & R Policy - Further, nor such land had ever been allotted to landless labourers - Hence, landless labourers were not entitled to any alternative land in accordance with the R & R Policy - Appeal dismissed.
