Gujarat State Financial Corporation v Messrs Lotus Hotels Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 363; (1983) 3 SCC 379; AIR 1983 SC 848; 1983(1) SCALE 584; 1983 UJ 564

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Summary: Constitution, 1950, art. 12 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Banking and Finance - Gujarat State Financial Corporation entered into agreement to advance loan to a company - Acting on undertaking the company proceeded to undertake and execute project of setting up a 4-Star Hotel - And thereby incurred huge expenses and suffered liabilities - (A) Applicability of principle of promissory estoppel - (B) Maintainability of writ petition seeking madamus against the corporation - Held, principle would stop Corporation from backing out of its obligation - Held, since Corporation would be 'other authority' within art. 12 , writ of mandamus could be issued directing Corporation to perform its statutory duty - Appeal Dismissed.
