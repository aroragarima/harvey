S.Vinod Kumar and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2089; (1996) 6 SCC 580; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1480; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 768; 1996 (2) CLR 1090; 1997 (1) ESC 1; JT 1996 (8) SC 643; 1996 (2) LLN 1277; 1996(7) SCALE 396; 1997 (1) SLJ 121; 1996 (6) SLR 466; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 142; 1996 (7) Supreme 333

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Efficiency

Summary: Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 16(4), 335 - Provision or concession - Lower marks in examination - Indra Sawhney etc. v. Union & Ors. etc declared that that art. 16(4) of Constitution had not contemplated or permitted reservation in matter of promotions, but declared that reservations already made were to continue for five years from date of judgment - Para 829 directed that decision was to operate only prospectively and was not to affect promotions already made, on temporary, officiating or regular/permanent basis - It further directed that wherever reservations were already provided, authority or body falling under definition of 'State' in Art. 12 of Constitution such reservations was to continue in operation for period of five years thereafter - Tribunal was required to decide if reservations provided in Para 829 took within its purview provisions providing for lesser qualifying marks in qualifying examination for promotion - Tribunal held that expression 'reservation' provided in art. 16(4) of Constitution took within its fold concessions and facilities including provision for lesser qualifying marks in qualifying examination for promotion, provision was also saved by virtue of declaration contained in Para 829 - Office Memorandum led to Govt. issuing orders that provided lesser qualifying marks for passing qualifying examination prescribed for promotion, in case of Scheduled Caste/Scheduled Tribe - Hence, instant appeal - Whether provision or 'concession', as Tribunal called it, was saved by declaration in Para 829 of Indra Sawhney etc. v. Union & Ors.etc - Held, Tribunal decision was erroneous and unsustainable - Concession provided 'temporary exemption to members already in service belonging to any of Scheduled Castes or Scheduled Tribes from passing all tests (unified, special or departmental test) for period of two years, and they were required to pass tests within period of exemption - It was thus clear that so far as promotions were concerned, it was not permissible to provide lesser qualifying marks of evaluation in favour of OBCs/SCs/STs since that would compromise efficiency of administration, while same was provided in matter of direct recruitment - So far as provision for lower qualifying marks or lesser level of evaluation in matter of promotion was concerned, it was not permissible under art. 16(4) of Constitution in view of command contained in art. 335 of Constitution - Hence, even if reservation was permitted by art. 16(4) of Constitution in matter of promotions, provision for lower qualifying marks or lesser level of evaluation was not permissible in matter of promotions, by virtue of art. 335 of Constitution - Accordingly, there was no question of provision or 'concession', as it was called by Tribunal, being saved by declaration in Para 829 of said judgment - Order of Tribunal was set aside - No costs - Appeal allowed.
