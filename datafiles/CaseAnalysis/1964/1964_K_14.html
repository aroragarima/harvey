K. V. Narayanaswami Iyer v K. V. Ramakrishna Iyer and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 403; AIR 1965 SC 289; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 78; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Ramakrishna, eldest brother, Narayanaswami second brother and youngest brother, Mahadeva constituted a joint family - Narayanaswami brought this suit for partition and accounts - Whether acquisitions made by karta in name of his wife, son and grandson were from his own funds or family funds and whether they were to be included in family property? - Held, if in fact at date of acquisition of a particular property joint family had sufficient nucleus for acquiring it, property in name of any member of joint family should be presumed to be acquired from out of family funds and so to form part of joint family property, unless contrary is shown - On a consideration of evidence, it comes to conclusion that it does not appear that joint family had at date of acquisitions made in names of first defendant's wife, his son, and his grandson sufficient nucleus from which these properties could be acquired - On a consideration of all circumstances in this case, we are of opinion that High Court's conclusion as regards these properties also that they did not form part of joint family property is correct - Appeal dismissed.
