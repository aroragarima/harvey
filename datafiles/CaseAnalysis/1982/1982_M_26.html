Bhikoba Shankar Dhumal (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v Mohan Lal Punchand Tathed and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 141; (1982) 1 SCC 680; AIR 1982 SC 865; 1982 (2) Bom.C.R. 372; 1982 (2) Bom.C.R. 383; 1982 MahLJ 329; 1982(1) SCALE 127; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 218; 1982 UJ 273

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Administrative - Maharashtra Agricultural Land (Ceiling on holdings) Act, 1961, ss. 3, 4, 18, 19, 10, 13, 21, 2(16), 16, 21, 12(1)(a) and (b) - Appellant tenant concerned in this case, filed before Special Deputy Collector a return in prescribed form furnishing particulars of land - Special Deputy Collector found that appellant was in possession of surplus land - Appellant filed an appeal - Thereafter appellant died - Special Deputy Collector found that appellant was a surplus holder of land - Heirs prayed that proceedings which were commenced with return filed by appellant should be dropped as they had become infructuous - Accepted plea - Petition by respondents allowed by High Court - (A) Whether respondents had locus standi to file an appeal against order of Special Deputy Collector? - Respondents were former landlords of land - They cannot, therefore, be characterised as just strangers to these proceedings - It cannot, therefore, be said that respondents had no locus standi to file an appeal - (B) Whether proceedings have become infructuous? - Held, crucial date with reference to which extent of surplus land held by a person is to be determined is appointed day in case of persons holding land in excess of ceiling area at any time after 04-08-1959 but before appointed day and in case of those acquiring, holding or coming into possession of such excess land on or after appointed day - Similarly those who acquire land in excess of ceiling area on or after appointed day would become liable to surrender surplus land on date of taking possession of any land in excess of ceiling area - This liability to surrender surplus land would not in any way come to an end by reason of death of such holder before actual extent of surplus land is determined and notified u/s. 21 - S. 3 of Act expressly imposes a limit on holding of agricultural land on commencement of Act - Extent of surplus land which Govt. can acquire under Act from a holder cannot be made to depend upon date on which a declaration indicating extent of surplus land is notified in official gazette u/s. 21 and date on which such surplus land is taken possession of - It cannot also be made to depend upon holder who has incurred liability on relevant date being alive on date on which declaration is made u/s. 21 and possession of surplus land is taken - Held, proceedings commencing with return filed by appellant could not be dropped merely because he died before a notification was issued u/s. 21 - Proceedings have to be continued and surplus land in hands of appellant as on appointed day should be determined and taken possession of in accordance with law - Appeal dismissed.
