Nidamarti Maheshkkumar v Statf Of Maharashtra And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 311; (1986) 2 SCC 534; AIR 1986 SC 1362; 1986 (2) CCC 35; JT 1986 SC 501; 1986(1) SCALE 967; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 230; 1986 (2) UJ 434

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Admissions, Regional classification

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Admissions - Regional classification - Admissions to medical colleges were made subject to region wise classification inasmuch as student from school or college situated within jurisdiction of particular university could seek admission only in medical college or colleges situate within jurisdiction of that University and not in any other university - On filing of writ petition, HC held that r.B(2) of the Rules for admission to M.B.B.S. Course framed by State Govt. was therefore valid - Hence, instant appeal - Whether

Held, it is not possible to categorise the regions within the jurisdiction of the various universities as backward or advanced as if they were exclusive categories and in any event there is no material placed which would persuade to reach that conclusion. Even if the regions within the jurisdiction of the universities in Vidharbha and Marathwada can be said to be backward and regions within the Jurisdiction of the universities in Bombay and Pune can be said to be advanced, regionwise classification for admission to medical college's cannot be sustained. There is no reason why a brilliant student from a region which is within the jurisdiction of a university in Vidharbha or Marathwada area should be denied the opportunity of medical education in Bombay or Pune. It would plainly be violative of the mandate of the equality clause to compartmentalize the State into different regions and provide that a student from one region should not he allowed to migrate to another region for medical education and thus be denied equal opportunity with others in the state for medical education. The judgment of the HC set aside and r. B(2) of the Rules declared unconstitutional and void. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Primary consideration in selection of candidates for admission to medical colleges must be merit and object of any rules which may be made for regulating admissions to medical colleges must be to secure best and most meritorious students.
