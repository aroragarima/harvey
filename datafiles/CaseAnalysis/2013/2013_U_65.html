Dharamvir Singh v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 421; (2013) 7 SCC 316; AIR 2013 SC 2840; 2013 (5) AWC 5308; JT 2013 (12) SC 44; 2013(8) SCALE 58; [2013] 8 S.C.R. 1119; 2013 (7) SLR 376

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Armed Forces, Pension Regulations for the Army, 1961, Entitlement Rules for Casualty Pensionary Awards to Armed Forces Personnel, 1982, General Rules of Guide to Medical Officers (Military Pensions) 2002

Summary: Pension Regulations for the Army, 1961-Regulation 173-Employment-Army Service-Disability pension-Eligibility-Nothing on record to show that appellant was suffering from "Generalised Seizure (Epilepsy) at time of initial recruitment in Indian Army-Respondents failed to bring on record any document to suggest that appellant was under treatment for such disease or by hereditary he is suffering from such disease-No note of any disability or disease in service record at time of appellant's acceptance for military service-Incumbent on part of Medical Board to call for records and look into same before coming to opinion that disease could not have been detected on medical examination prior to acceptance for military service-Nothing on record to suggest that any such record called for by Medical Board or looked into-No reasons recorded in writing to come to conclusion that disability is not due to military service-Presumption would be that disability of appellant bore casual connection with service conditions-Disease deemed to be attributable to or aggravated by Army Services-In terms of Regulation 173 appellant eligible for disability pension-Respondents directed to grant disability pension to appellant in terms of order of single Judge of High Court-Impugned order of Division Bench of High Court-Set aside.
