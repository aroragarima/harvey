Shah Bhojraj Kuverji Oil Mills and Ginning Factory v Subbash Chandra Yograj Sinha

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 472; 1997 ACJ 926; AIR 1961 SC 1596; 1962 (64) BomLR 407; 1962 (1) SCJ 377; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 159

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12 and 50 - Bombay General clauses Act, ss. 7 and 12 - On 11-09-1942, appellants had executed a rent note, under which period of tenancy in favour of appellants was 15 years, and it expired by efflux of time on 14-03-1957 - Landlord thereupon filed a suit on 25-04-1957, for possession of premises - A notification was issued u/s.6 of Act, applying Part II of Act to area where property is situated - Appellants claimed protection of s. 12 in Part II of Act, which deprived landlord of right of possession under certain circumstances - Issues arise - (A) Whether by virtue of first proviso to s. 50 of Act, all provisions of Part II including s. 12 were not expressly made applicable to all suits? - Held, proviso which has been added to s. 50 of Act deals with effect of repeal - Substantive part of section repealed two Acts which were in force in State of Bombay - If nothing more had been said, s. 7 of Bombay General clauses Act would have applied, all pending suits, proceedings would have continued under old law, as if repealing Act had not been passed - Effect of proviso was to take matter out of s. 7 of Bombay General clauses Act and to provide for a special saving - It cannot be used to decide whether s. 12 of Act is retrospective - Proviso here saves pending suits and proceedings, and further enacts that suits and proceedings then pending are to be transferred to courts designated in Act and are to continue under Act and any or all provisions of Act are to apply to them - (B) Whether by virtue of s. 12(1) of Act, which applied independently by extension of Act to area where property is situated, suit was not rendered incompetent and landlord deprived of his remedy of possession? - Further held, s. 12(1) enacts a rule of decision, it says that a landlord is not entitled to possession if tenant pays or shows his readiness and willingness to pay standard rent, to observe other conditions of tenancy - Tenants, in present case, have expressed their readiness and willingness to pay, and it is clear that they fulfil requirements of s. 12(1) and landlord is, therefore, not entitled to relief of possession - Appeal allowed.
