K. A. A. Raja and Others v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1508; (1994) 5 SCC 138; 1994 (1) RentLR 610; 1994(2) SCALE 1059; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Kerala Land Acquisition Act, 1961 - Market value of the land - Determination of - Whether High Court's impugned decision which allowed appeal of State, dismissed cross-objections and determined market value of said land at Rs. 23,73,967.74 is correct? - Held, evidence nature of trees and their yield accepted by Income Tax Authorities and price fixed by HC - On an overall consideration of prevailing prices though for one year there may be higher prices, but on an average it was found that price would be at Rs. 220 per kg - To apply capitalisation method, on facts and circumstances, in assessing market value of plantation of a large extent of lands acquired in notification, suitable and proper multiplier could be taken as 7 years - Applying 7 years' multiplier with reference to year of acquisition for purpose of capitalising net income, just and proper market value would be round figure of 36 lakhs - Appellants, therefore, would be entitled to market value in a sum of Rs. 36 lakhs for total extent of 52 acres 88 cents - Appellants are entitled to market value of Rupees Thirty-six lakhs for their lands with everything thereon and usual benefits of 30% solatium on enhanced market value and interest at 9% for first year from date of taking possession and 15% interest from date of expiry of one year till date of making payment compensation - Entitlement with regard to payment of interest u/s. 23(1-A) would depend upon decisions to be rendered by Constitution Bench in K S Paripoornan case and authority depending upon judgment would work out that benefit also - Appeal allowed.
