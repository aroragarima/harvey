M/s. Cadila Laboratories Pvt. Ltd. v C.C.E. Vadodara

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 119; (2003) 4 SCC 12; AIR 2003 SC 1700; 2003 (86) ECC 25; 2003 (107) ECR 420; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 262; 2003 (2) G.L.R. 1706; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 76; 2003 (55) RLT 1; 2003(2) SCALE 63; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 1048; 2003 (2) SLT 72; 2003 (1) Supreme 981

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - S. 11A - whether excise duty is payable on these intermediate products? - Held, in order to be excisable, not only goods must be manufactured i.e. some new product brought into existence, but the goods must be marketable. It has not been established that the intermediate products manufactured by the Appellants are marketable. - Whether the Respondent were entitled to the extended period under Section 11A of the Act? - Held, there was no deliberate act of fraud, collusion, misstatement, suppression or contravention of the Act. Mere fact of not filing of the classification lists is not sufficient to bring into play the extended period or limitation. Therefore, the extended period of limitation, under the provision to Section 11-A, was not available.
