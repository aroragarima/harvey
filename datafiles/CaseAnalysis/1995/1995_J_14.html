Chief Conservator of Forests and another v Jagannath Maruti Kondhare and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 795; (1996) 2 SCC 293; (1996) SCC (L&S) 500; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 32; AIR 1996 SC 2898; AIR 1996 SCW 735; 1996 (1) CLR 680; 1996 (72) FLR 840; JT 1995 (9) SC 465; 1996 LabIC 967; 1996 (1) LLJ 1223; 1996 (1) LLN 475; 1995(7) SCALE 101; 1996 (1) SLR 56; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 259; 1996 (1) UJ 484

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.2(j); Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1972, s.3(7) - 'Industry' - Forest Dept. of State Govt. - State Govt. indulged in un fair labour practice visualised by item 6 of Schedule IV of the State Act - Permanency - Status of casual employees - Continuation of casuals for long years - Object to deprive them the status of permanent employees - Held, not a part of sovereign function of State - Forest Depts. is an industry - Burden does not lie upon workman to establish the object of employer - Relief to regularisation with all benefits of permanent worker - Cannot be refused on grounds of financial strain of State exchequer.
