Uttar Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation v Ram Singh and another

Supreme Court of India

14 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1526; (2008) 17 SCC 627

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Termination, Maintainability

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination - Maintainability - Respondent was appointed as Booking Clerk by appellant - Respondent was suspended during pendency of disciplinary proceedings initiated by appellant - Later respondent was terminated - Respondent filed appeal before deputy General Manager - Appellate authority stated that respondent was careless in his duty but that such carelessness did not warrant order of dismissal - However, respondent would not get benefit of his earlier service with appellant - After 13 years respondent raised industrial dispute challenging order of Deputy General Manager - Labour Court set down matter for ex parte - Application by appellant for recalling order was rejected - Ex parte award was passed by Labour Court setting aside order of Deputy General Manager, insofar as it directed that respondent would not be entitled to benefit of his earlier service with appellant - Being aggrieved with this award appellant filed petition before HC - Petition was rejected by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was maintainable - Held, HC erred in not setting aside award of Labour Court - Apart from unacceptable manner in which appellant was denied opportunity of participating in proceedings, including being debarred from cross-examining respondent, Labour Court should not have entertained industrial dispute given enormous delay - SC had in several decisions held that while delay should not by itself be sufficient reason to reject industrial dispute, nevertheless delay should not be unreasonable - In instant case delay of 13 years was unreasonable - Mere fact that respondent was making repeated representations would not justify his raising issue before Labour Court after 13 years - In any event last representation was made in 1983 and industrial dispute was admittedly raised in 1986 - Lack of diligence on part of respondent was apparent - Besides Labour Court had set aside order by which appeal of respondent had been allowed, logically original order of termination should have revived - Respondent could not accept part of order of appellate authority and reject other - Appellate Authority, while setting aside order of termination had directed reemployment on certain terms - Respondent had complied with terms and having acquiesced in same, had been given re-employment on that basis - Respondent could not turnaround and raise dispute on basis of which he had been re-employed with appellant again - Decision of HC as well as award of Labour Court should not be sustained and was set aside - Appeal allowed.
