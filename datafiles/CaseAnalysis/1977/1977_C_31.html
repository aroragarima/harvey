Carborandum and Company v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 209; (1977) SCC (Tax) 391; (1977) 2 SCC 862; AIR 1977 SC 1259; 1977 (6) CTR 209; [1977] 108 ITR 335; 1977 (2) MLJ(SC) 19; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Service fee - Taxable territories - Refund - Assessee company entered into agreement with Indian company - As per agreement assessee was to render certain technical and know-how services to Indian company - In lieu of services assessee was to receive service fee from Indian company - During relevant A/Y assessee received its service fee - Good slab of it was deducted at source on account of income tax and super tax - Assessee filed return of income with application for refund of entire tax deducted - ITO said that five per cent of fee paid was earned in India and only that small amount was assessable to income tax - Commissioner revised order of ITO and said that at least seventy five percent fee had accrued in India - Tribunal set aside order of Commissioner - HC answered question against assessee - Whether service fee in excess of five percent received by assessee company from Indian company during relevant A/Y had accrued or arisen in India - Held, HC went wrong in its approach to question raised before it and did not correctly appreciate scope and applicability of s. 42 of the Act - Assessee made services of foreign personnel available to Indian company outside taxable territory - Later took them as their employees, paid their salary and they worked under control of Indian company - Service rendered by assessee was wholly and solely rendered in foreign territory - Service fee received by assessee did not accrue or arise in India nor was deemed to have accrued or arisen in India - But since five percent of service fee was brought to tax by ITO and no appeal was filed against it, assessee could not interfere with addition of five percent - Fee in excess of five percent was not taxable - Appeal allowed.
