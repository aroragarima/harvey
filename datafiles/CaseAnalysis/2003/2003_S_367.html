Ram Udgar Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

03 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 928; (2004) 10 SCC 443; JT 2003 (8) SC 508; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 224; 2003(9) SCALE 179; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 34, 302 - Arms Act, 1959 - ss. 25A, 27 - Trial Court held the accused-appellant guilty, while giving the benefit of doubt to others, confirmed by the High Court - Held, the courts were justified in holding that appellant was the assailant, and accordingly convicted him. No exception could be taken to the well merited reasoning squarely found supported by overwhelming relevant, convincing and concrete evidence placed on record by the prosecution in the case, and no error could be made out or substantiated in them, to call for interference.
