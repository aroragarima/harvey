Â Cooverjee B. Bharucha v Excise Commissioner and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 159; AIR 1954 SC 220; 1954 SCJ 246; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 873

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Excise Regulation

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32 and 226 - Excise Regulation I of 1915 - Petition u/art. 32, Constitution - Infringement of fundamental rights - (A) Whether fundamental right to carry on trade or business in liquor u/art. 19(1)(g) been infringed by act of Collector of Excise? - Held, under rules every member of public who wishes to carry on trade in liquor is invited to make bids - This is only method by which carrying on of liquor trade can be regulated - When contract is thrown open to public auction, it cannot be said that there is exclusion of competition and thereby a monopoly is created - For all these reasons Courts are of opinion that, the contention that provisions of regulation are unconstitutional as they abridge rights of petitioner to carry on liquor trade freely, cannot be sustained - (B) Whether charge of fee by public auction is excessive and is not in nature of a fee? - One of purposes of regulation is to raise revenue - By provisions of s. 24, duties can be imposed on manufacture, import, export and transport of liquor and other excisable articles - Revenue is also collected by grant of contracts to carry on trade in liquors and these contracts are sold by auction - Grantee is given a licence on payment of auction price - Regulation specifically authorises this - Further held, thus, it is not a fee levied without authority of law as was situation in Rashid Ahmad's case Indlaw [1950] S C J 324 - (C) Whether irregularities committed in conducting auction sale can be said to have abridged fundamental rights of petitioners? - Further held, mere irregularities committed in conducting an auction sale cannot be said to have abridged petitioner's fundamental rights and so art. 32 is not attracted - It is open to petitioner u/art. 226 to approach High Court for a mandamus if officers concerned have conducted themselves not in accordance with law or if they have acted in excess of their jurisdiction - Petition dismissed.
