Satya Pal Anand v State of M.P. and others

Supreme Court of India

26 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 825; (2016) 10 SCC 767; AIR 2016 SC 4995; 2017 (2) ALD(SC) 144; 2017 (120) ALR 458; 2017 (1) Bom.C.R. 397; 2017 (1) CTC 414; JT 2016 (10) SC 393; 2017 (2) KarLJ 536; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 904; 2017 (134) RD 679

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Contract, Specific Relief Act, 1963, Limitation Act, 1963, Registration Act, 1908, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Irregularity, Assignee, Societies, Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, Constitution of India, 1950, Andhra Pradesh Registration Service Rules, 2009

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 300A - Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, s. 64 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17, 17(1)(b), 18, 34, 35, 36, 69,87 - Allotment of plot - Cancellation - Alternative remedy - Appellant's mother was allotted plot in question by respondent/Society vide a registered deed - Society executed a Deed of Extinguishment cancelling said allotment of appellant's mother on her death - Society executed and registered a deed in favour of respondent no.5 for same plot - Appellant challenged Society's action seeking for a declaration that he continues to be the owner of subject plot allotted by Society to his mother, having inherited the same - During pendency of dispute, Society permitted transfer of subject plot in favour of respondent nos. 6 & 7 vide registered Deed - Appellant moved an application before Sub-Registrar to cancel registration of Extinguishment Deed and subsequent two deeds which was rejected - Appellant approached Inspector General which was rejected on ground that powers conferred on him were limited to general superintendence of Registration Offices and making Rules - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC - DB dismissed writ petition holding that an alternative remedy before a competent Forum was available and was pending between parties, it was not feasible to invoke writ jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was duty bound to declare registered Deeds as void ab initio especially when appellant has already resorted to an alternative efficacious remedy u/s. 64 of the 1960 Act before competent Forum.

Held, appellant has not disputed execution of compromise deed nor has he paid any heed to notice given by other party to refund the amount accepted by him in furtherance of the compromise deed. No fault can be found with the approach of HC in refusing to exercise its writ jurisdiction due to the conduct of appellant in pursuing multiple proceedings for the same relief and also because appellant had an alternative and efficacious statutory remedy to which he has already resorted to. Question whether Society was competent to unilaterally cancel allotment of a plot given to its member and to cancel the membership of such member due to default committed by the member, was within the purview of business of Society. Any cause of action in that regard should be adjudicated by the procedure prescribed in that behalf. It was not open to presume that Society had no authority in law to take a decision in that behalf. The right of appellant qua the plot of land would obviously be subject to the final outcome of such action. Appellant being legal representative of original allottee cannot claim any right higher than that of his predecessor qua the Society, which was final authority to decide on the issue of continuation of membership of its member. The right of member to remain in occupation of plot allotted by Society would be entirely dependent on that decision. HC had justly, summarily dismissed writ petition with liberty to appellant to pursue statutory remedy under the provisions of the 1960 Act or by way of a civil suit. Thus, writ petition filed by appellant deserved to be dismissed as was rightly dismissed by HC. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Madhya Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1960, s. 64 - Registration Act, 1908, ss. 17, 17(1)(b), 18, 34, 35, 36, 69,87 - Andhra Pradesh Registration Rules 1960, r. 26 (k)(i) - Extinguishment Deed registration - Cancellation - Challenged - Whether Sub-Registrar and Inspector General had authority to cancel registration of any document including an Extinguishment Deed after it was registered/s. 69 of the 1908 Act.

Held, decision in case of Thota Ganga Laxmi & Anr. v. Government of Andhra Pradesh & Ors., 2010 (15) SCC 207, was dealing with an express provision as applicable to State and in particular with regard to registration of an Extinguishment Deed. In absence of such an express provision, in other State legislations, Registering Officer would be governed by the provisions in the 1908 Act. There was nothing to indicate that Registering Officer was required to undertake a quasi judicial enquiry regarding the veracity of factual position stated in the document presented for registration or its legality, if the tenor of document suggests that it requires to be registered. The validity of such registered document can indeed be put in issue before a Court of competent jurisdiction. In instant case document in question was termed as an Extinguishment Deed. However, in effect, it was manifestation of decision of Society to cancel allotment of subject plot given to its member due to non fulfillment of the obligation by member concerned. The subject document was linked to decision of Society to cancel membership of allottee of plot given to him/her by Society. Therefore, it was the decision of Society, which Society was entitled to exercise within the frame work of governing cooperative laws and Bye-laws which were binding on the members of Society. The case of Thota Ganga Laxmi, 2010 (15) SCC 207, besides the fact that it was dealing with an express provision contained in Statutory Rule namely r. 26 (k)(i) of the Rules was not a case of deed for cancellation of allotment of plot by Society that of a cancellation of registered sale deed executed between private parties which was sought to be cancelled unilaterally. Even for the latter reason the exposition in case of Thota Ganga Laxmi, 2010 (15) SCC 207, would have no application to the fact situation of instant casein absence of a specific Rule in that regard in the State of Madhya Pradesh. Thus, HC was justly dismissed writ petition filed by appellant with liberty to appellant to pursue statutory remedy resorted to by him under the 1960 Act or by resorting to any other remedy as might be advised and permissible in law. Appeal dismissed.
