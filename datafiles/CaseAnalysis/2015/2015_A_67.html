Rakesh Mohindra v Anita Beri and others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 786; 2016 (1) ALD(SC) 19; 2016 (2) AWC 1899; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 323; 2016 (1) CGLJ 46; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 229; JT 2015 (10) SC 588; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4585; 2015 (8) MLJ 200; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 1023; 2016 (130) RD 730; 2015(12) SCALE 412

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Copies

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 64, 65 - Secondary Evidence - In suit filed by plaintiffs-respondents for declaration that appellant-defendant had no right, title or interest over suit property and that plaintiffs-respondents are in possession of the same, appellant-defendant filed application in Trial Court u/s. 65 of 1872 Act and sought permission to prove Ex-DW-2/B-letter of disclaimer by way of secondary evidence - Trial Court allowed said application of appellant-defendant and granted leave of Court to lead secondary evidence of Ex-DW-2/B-letter of disclaimer - However, on revision preferred by plaintiff-respondent no. 1, HC had set aside said order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of HC setting aside order of Trial Court permitting defendant-appellant to lead secondary evidence in respect of Ex-DW-2/B- letter of disclaimer is sustainable.

Held, Trial Court noticed that the photocopy of Exhibit DW-2/B-letter of disclaimer came from the custody of DEO Ambala and the witness, who brought the record, has been examined as witness. Hence, there is compliance of the provisions of s. 65 of the Act. Merely because the signatures in some of the documents are not legible and visible that could not be a ground to reject the secondary evidence. Trial Court correctly appreciated the efforts taken by appellant-defendant for the purpose of leading secondary evidence. Hence, impugned order of HC is not sustainable and so, same is set aside. However, genuineness, correctness and existence of the document has to be established during trial and Trial Court is to record reasons before relying on those secondary evidences. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - If a party wishes to lead secondary evidence, the Court is obliged to examine the probative value of the document produced in the Court or their contents and decide the question of admissibility of a document in secondary evidence.
