State Of Punjab v Vishkarma And Co. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, L.M. Sharma, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1124; (1993) Supp3 SCC 62; JT 1993 (1) SC 448; 1993 (1) RentLR 336; 1993(1) SCALE 417; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 761; 1993 (1) UJ 659

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Punjab Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1964

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Punjab Land Revenue Act, 1887, ss. 31, 41, 42 and 42(2) - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, ss. 3 and 4 - Minor and Mineral Concession Rules, 1964, rr. 7, 20 and 21 - Whether State is entitled to demand payment of royalty for removal of brick-earth? - Held, ownership of brick-earth belongs to landowners and not to State Evidence to show ownership of brick-earth belongs to landowners and not State - Hence, no justification for State action to demand royalty and insist on obtaining license - Appeals dismissed.
