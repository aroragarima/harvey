Thanedar Singh v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 135; (2002) SCC (Cr) 153; (2002) 1 SCC 487; AIR 2002 SC 175; 2001 (4) CCR 242; 2001 (4) Crimes 382; 2002 (2) CRJ 735; 2002 CRLJ 254; JT 2001 (9) SC 293; 2001(7) SCALE 499; 2001 (7) SLT 545; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 579; 2001 (7) Supreme 880; 2002 (1) UC 217

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused appellant u/ss. 302, 148, 149 of IPC - Trial Court passed acquittal order - State filed appeal before HC - HC reversed verdict of acquittal and passed conviction order - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC was not justified in making comments that Trial Court did not give reasons on certain important aspects or misdirected itself in appreciation of evidence. Though judgment of Trial Court is somewhat perfunctory and lacking clarity in certain respects, on the whole, approach and conclusions of Trial Court cannot be said to be perverse or vitiated by any serious error warranting interference with verdict of acquittal. The view taken by Trial Court, is a reasonably possible view and, therefore, HC was not justified in reversing acquittal insofar as appellant is concerned. HC failed to address itself to certain crucial factors discussed above concerning credibility of eye witness account and correctness of FIR. Conviction and sentence of appellant is set aside. Appeal allowed.
