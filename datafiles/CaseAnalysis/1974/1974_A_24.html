Anil Dey v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.G. Palekar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 436; (1974) 4 SCC 514; (1974) SCC (Cr) 550; AIR 1974 SC 832; 1974 CRLJ 702; 1974 CRLR 327

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Single Act

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3(2) and 8(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5)and 22(6) - (A) Whether detention order of statutory functionary, if based on a single instance, is too flimsy, too alien or too remote to bear a reasonable nexus with two-fold requirement of subjective satisfaction and activity prejudicial to maintenance of supplies and services essential to community? - If grounds relied on have nothing to do with prejudicial purposes stipulated in statute, no nexus exists and order is bad; even if incident attributed to detenu has some connection with obnoxious activities, it should not be too trivial in substance nor too stale in point of time as to snap rational link that must exist between vicious episode and prejudicial activity sought to be interdicted - Held, ground stated is neither too distant nor too trifling to strain judicial credulity to breaking point; on contrary it is proximate and pernicious, if true - (B) Whether factual components constituting real grounds for detention have been fairly and fully put across to detenu so as to enable him to make an effective answer? - Prejudicial act petitioner is alleged to have committed, law ordinarily forbids judicial scrutiny of veracity thereof unless it could be shown to be patently incorrect; ground furnished speaks of a conjoint act of theft of signal materials, feed and transformers, from junction box, and like - Held, this single act cannot live in isolation and necessarily connotes a course of previous conduct whereby some specialisation has been acquired, some specialised agencies have been fabricated and some special mischief has been planned to be perpetrated; thus it cannot be said that there is insufficient communication or illegitimate reliance on materials - Petition dismissed.
