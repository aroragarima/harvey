Shobha Rani v Madhukar Reddi

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28508; (1988) SCC (Cr) 60; (1988) 1 SCC 105; AIR 1988 SC 121; 1988 (1) AWC 250; 1988 (1) CCC 209; 1988 CRLR 88; 1988 CrLR(SC) 88; 1988 (1) DMC 12; JT 1987 (4) SC 433; 1987(2) SCALE 1008; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 1010

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Civil Procedure - Criminal - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Women & Children - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1984 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1)(i-a) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498A - Appeal against order of High Court denying decree of divorce to appellant-wife - Meaning of 'cruelty' under Hindu Marriage Act - Cruelty if not admitted requires to be proved on preponderance of probabilities as in civil cases and not beyond a reasonable doubt as in criminal cases - Held, evidence of harassment to meet any unlawful demand for money is necessary to constitute cruelty in criminal law, being requirement of offence of 'cruelty' u/s. 498 - Conduct which is complained of as cruelty by one spouse may not be so for other - There may be instances of cruelty by unintentional but inexcusable conduct of any party - Cruel treatment may also result by cultural conflict of spouse - Intention is not a necessary element in cruelty - 'Intention' has to be understood in ordinary sense of term in matrimonial affairs - If intention to harm, harass or hurt could be inferred by nature of conduct complained of, cruelty could be easily established - Demand for dowry is prohibited under law - That by itself is bad enough and amounts to cruelty entitling wife to get a decree for dissolution of marriage - Decree for dissolution of marriage granted - Appeal allowed.
