Ved Prakash Gupta v Messrs Delton Cable India Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 58; (1984) SCC (L&S) 281; (1984) 2 SCC 569; AIR 1984 SC 914; 1984 (48) FLR 417; 1984 LabIC 658; 1984 (1) LLJ 546; 1984 (2) LLN 27; 1984(1) SCALE 474; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 169; 1984 (1) SLJ 569; 1984 (2) SLR 5; 1984 UJ 523

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(s)(iii) and 10(i)(c) - (A) 'Workman' - Definition - Whether appellant was a workman at relevant time? - Work done in audit department is important for proper and efficient functioning of bank it would be idle to elevate that work to status of officers who supervise work of everybody concerned with bank's establishment - Held, appellant clearly falls within definition of a workman in s. 2(s) of Act and that reference of dispute u/s. 10(i)(c) of Act is valid in law - (B) Whether termination of appellant's service is proper? - Inquiry Officer had not acted properly in proceedings and that he had not given full opportunity to appellant as required by law - Charge levelled against appellant is not a serious one and it is not known how charge even if proved would result in any much less total loss of confidence of management in appellant as management would have it in charge - Nothing on record to show that any previous adverse remark against appellant had been taken into consideration by management for awarding extreme penalty of dismissal from service to appellant even if he had in fact abused in filthy language - Further held, termination of appellant's service is invalid and unsustainable in law, and that he is entitled to reinstatement with full back wages and other benefits including continuity of service - Appeal allowed.
