U. Raghavendra Acharya and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1264

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Burmah Oil Company (India Trading) Limited) Act, 1981, Karnataka Civil Services Rules, 1957

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution - Karnataka Civil Services Rules, 1957 r. 296 - Constitution of India, 1950 arts. 14 & 16 - Revised pay - Retrospective Effect - Retrial Benefit - Entitlement - Respondent issued notifications extending UGC pay scales to Govt. aided colleges and engineering colleges, from 1-1-1996 - Appellants/teachers retired during period 1-1- 1996 to 31-3- 1998 - Appellants filed writ petitions before HC - HC held notification were arbitrary and appellants could not had been deprived of benefit - Respondent file appeal before DB , which set aside order of HC - Appellants filed review petitions, which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Appellants contended that they were given benefit of revised scales of pay w.e.f. 1-1-1996 - Thus, having acquired a vested right in relation thereto, quantum of their pensionary benefits should be computed on basis of 50% of last pay drawn and pensionary benefits could not had been directed to be given from 1-4-1998 - Whether appellants having been given benefit of revised pay scales from 1-1-1996, were entitle to retiral benefits calculated with same effect - Held, it was well settled that notification could be issued by State accepting recommendation of Pay revision committee with retrospective effect as it was beneficent to employees - Once such retrospective effect was given to recommendation, concerned employees despite their reaching age of superannuation in between dates or date of issuance of notification would be deemed to be getting said scales of pay as on 1-1-1996 - In instant case, appellants had been deprived of a vested right by reason of executive instructions - Thus, if appellants became entitled to benefits of revised scales of pay, and consequently to pension calculated on said basis in terms of impugned rules, there would be reduction of pension with retrospective effect which would be violative of Arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India - Appellants had retired from service and State therefore could not had amended statutory rules adversely affecting their pension with retrospective effect - Hence, judgment of DB was set aside - Appeals allowed.
