Imperial Chit Funds Private Limited v Income Tax Officer, Ernakulam

Supreme Court of India

19 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2731; (1996) 8 SCC 303; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 488; AIR 1996 SC 1887; 1996 (1) BC 659; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 555; 1996 (133) CTR 505; [1996] 219 ITR 498; JT 1996 (3) SC 410; 1996 (1) KLT 699; 1996(3) SCALE 20; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 640; 1996 (3) Supreme 225

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 178, 2, 3(b) and 4 - Companies Act, of 1956, s. 530 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 17 - Winding up of company - Tax claimed - Preferential/Secured creditor - Whether ITO should wait and prove his claim before official liquidator? - Held, effect of s. 178(3)(b) is that amount 'set aside' by liquidator is marked off as outside area of winding up proceedings - S. 178(2) r/w s. 178(3) of Act, provision should be made for any tax which is then or is likely thereafter to become payable - Chap. XV seeks to fasten liability to pay tax on income received and to catch income at earliest point of time - ITO need not wait to prove claim before liquidator.
