Nagappa Dondiba Kalal v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 448; (1980) Supp SCC 336; (1981) SCC (Cr) 278; AIR 1980 SC 1753; 1980 CRLJ 1270; 1981 CRLR 160; 1981 UJ 344

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 394 and 411 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103, 104 and 114 - Murder - Proof of offence - Recovery of ornaments at the instance of accused - Deceased last seen wearing them - Inference of murder - Conviction u/ss. 302 and 294 - Validity - Held, interence cannot be drawn in absence of any evidence to connect him with murder - Onus lies on prosecution to prove its case - Accused liable to be convicted u/s. 411 and not u/s. 302 or s. 394 - Appeal Allowed.
