Soyebbhai Yusufbhai Bharania and others v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 210; AIR 2017 SC 1606; 2017 (2) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2017 (2) WLN(SC) 47

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Object, Interfere, Knife, Deposition, Concurrent, Registered, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 147,148, 149 - Murder - Conviction Challenged - Appellant/accused no.1 along with A2-5 was charged for murder of 'X' - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 302, 147, 148 r/w 149 of IPC and awarded sentence - Trial Court acquitted A5 of all charges and HC in appeal confirmed conviction of A1-A3 and acquitted A4 thereby dismissed appeal of A1-A3 - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, once the principal eye-witness(es) have proved their credentials on the whole, it can be said to be believable that the prosecution can rest even on the testimony of a single eyewitness. Further, the improper admission or rejection of evidence shall not be ground of itself for a new trial or reversal of any decision in any case, if it shall appear to the Court before which such objection is raised that, independently of the evidence objected to and admitted, there was sufficient evidence to justify the decision, or that, if the rejected evidence had been received, it ought not to have varied the decision. In instant case,

albeit the murder is proved but the ingredients of the unlawful assembly remained elusive, as pre-requisite condition for an unlawful assembly i.e., minimum five persons, has not been met. However, the common object has been proved by the prosecution. The homicidal death was proved beyond all reasonable doubts. Hence, impugned order is devoid of any merit and does not warrant any interference. Appeal dismissed.
