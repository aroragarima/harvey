Jabal C. Lashkari and others v Official Liquidator and others

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 247; AIR 2016 SC 1601; 2016(3) ALL MR 197; 2016 (4) AWC 3612; [2016] 196 Comp Cas 486; JT 2016 (3) SC 406; 2016(3) SCALE 499; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Keywords: Corporate, Liquidator, Official Liquidator, Secured Creditors, Winding Up, Liquidation, Companies Act, 1956, Contract, Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Unpaid, Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Karnataka Rent Act, 1999

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, ss. 12, 13(2) - Companies Act, 1956 - Lease property - Company in Liquidation - Predecessor of appellants (Original Lessor) leased out land for period of 199 years to Prasad Mills Limited (Company) and while so, secured creditor of said Company filed Company Petition seeking winding up of Company - Pending winding up petition, legal heirs of Original Lessor filed suit in Small Causes Court seeking permanent injunction against sale of assets of Company, particularly, sale of leased property - While so, Company Court directed winding up of Company and appointment of Official Liquidator and another heir of Original Lessor filed application in winding up petition seeking direction to further prosecute suit pending before Small Causes Court - However, Company Court ordered that suit could be withdrawn and instead directions could be sought from Company Court for return of leased property - In pursuance of the said orders, application was filed for return of leased property and pending said application, Official Liquidator made advertisement for sale of leased property and against the same, appellant filed application for declaration that Official Liquidator had no right to sell leased property and another application was also filed seeking permission from Company Court to file suit before appropriate court for eviction of Official Liquidator from leased property - However, Company Court rejected all three company applications and aggrieved, appellant and other legal heirs of Original Lessor filed three separate appeals before DB of HC - However, DB of HC dismissed all appeals and hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned orders of HC could be upheld.

Held, while there is no doubt that rent has not been paid, also, there is no doubt that the secured creditors including SBI had all along been ready and willing to pay the rent and the reasons for non-payment appears to be lack of communication by the official liquidator to SBI of the precise amount of rent due. Further, no notice as contemplated by s.13(2) has been issued by the landlord and in such a situation, the readiness and willingness of the tenant to pay the rent, though could have continued for a fairly long time without actual payment, yet, same will not deprive the tenant of the protection under 1947 Act. Hence, finding of HC that the landlord is not entitled to seek eviction on the ground of non- payment of rent u/s. 12 of 1947 Act could not be said to be so inherently infirm so as to require interference of SC. Further, requirement of unjustified non-user for a period exceeding 6 months is not attracted in instant case in view of pendency of liquidation proceedings. Apart from that, cl.5 of lease deed dealing with non-user of leased land does not contemplate eviction on account of such non-user but merely entitles the lessor to receive rent for the period of such non-user of the land. Hence, order of HC is affirmed and connected appeals are dismissed. Since decision to affirm said judgment of HC is based on a consideration of the specific clauses in the lease deed between the parties to the case, hence, with regard to other appeals, effect of principles of law underlying instant order vis-Ã -vis specific clauses of lease deed between parties in said other appeals is question that is to be considered by HC in each of those appeals. Apart from that, whether order dt.17-7-2006 passed in Company Application attained finality in law and forecloses question raised and further whether constructions have been raised on such land by State Govt. for benefit of general public, are question that would require full and complete consideration by HC. Hence, to enable said exercise to be duly performed, impugned orders of HC in other appeals are set aside and all matters are remitted to HC for fresh consideration in accordance with observations and principles of law contained in instant order. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - The mere fact that the company has been ordered to be wound up could not be ground to direct official liquidator to handover possession of the land to the owners inasmuch as the company in liquidation continues to maintain its corporate existence until it stands dissolved upon completion of the liquidation proceedings in the manner contemplated by 1956 Act.
