Bablu Kumar and others v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 488; (2015) 8 SCC 787; 2015 (1) JCC 2454; JT 2015 (8) SC 208; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3047; 2015(8) SCALE 53; 2016(8) SCALE 53; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Jurisprudence, Defect, Criminal Justice

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 232 - Power of HC to direct Retrial - Fair Trial - FIR was lodged by informant against accused persons including appellants alleging that they armed with weapons took away her husband and that next day was found dead - Trial was conducted against accused and later, Trial Court passed orders u/s.232 of CrPC acquitting appellants - Aggrieved, informant approached HC by way of revision petition and Single Judge opined that there was no fair trial and remanded matter for retrial by Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal by appellants - Whether HC under revisional jurisdiction could direct for retrial of case.

Held, Trial Court had not taken pains to verify whether summons had really ben served on witnesses or not and HC rightly observed that Trial Court has also not tried to verify from the record whether warrants had been executed or not. Further, Trial Court had directed the prosecution to produce the witnesses and mechanically recorded that witnesses were not present and proceeded to direct the prosecution to keep them present. However, HC upon perusal of record held that notices were not served on witnesses. Hence, order passed by HC could not be regarded as faulty and so, order of HC is affirmed as views of HC are unexceptionable, for HC had annulled an order replete with glaring defects that led to miscarriage of justice. Hence, appeal is dismissed and Trial Court is directed to proceed with retrial. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - As per ruling of SC in K. Chinnaswamy v. State of Andhra Pradesh and another, 1962 INDLAW SC 192, HC would be justified to interfere with order of acquittal if Trial Court has wrongly shut out evidence which the prosecution wishes to produce.
