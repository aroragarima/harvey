Fatehchand Murlidhar And Anr v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 410; AIR 1967 SC 383; 1966 (2) ITJ 667; [1966] 62 ITR 323; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 453

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23(5)(a), 66(1) - Indian Partnership Act, 1890, s. 29 - Assessment - Business - Share - Deduction - References were made were common because real question raised by those references was income of appellant, from firm in which he was a partner should be included in his personal assessment or in assessment of firm of F, to which M had purported to assign profits and losses from firm - Facts from statement of case was made at instance of M - M was carrying on business and was a partner in registered firm, having /2/8 share - A deed of partnership was executed by M and his two sons, X and Y and a grandson - For A/Y 1955-56 Income Tax Officer(I.T.O.) included income from share in registered firm in individual assessment of M, M appealed to Appellate Commissioner - M appealed to Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal heard that appeal together with two appeals filed by F - Appellate Tribunal, agreed with views of Appellate Commissioner, dismissed appeal - Hence instant appeal - Whether firm was assessed to tax u/s. 23(5)(a) - Held, object of s. 23(5)(a) was not to assess firm itself but to apportion income among various partners - If it was real income of another firm, it was that firm which was liable to be assessed Act - Principle asserted in that case was that even in case of a partner in a registered firm, when question arises as to his individual assessment, what was to be considered is not income allocated to his share by employing machinery of s. 23(5)(a), but his real income, and that real income was what remained after deducting amounts which might be said to have been diverted and never constituted his real income and such amounts would have to be excluded before his real income was reached - Appeals allowed.
