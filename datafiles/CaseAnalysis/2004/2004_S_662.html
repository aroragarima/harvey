Kachrulal Bhagirath Agrawal and Others v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1499; (2005) 9 SCC 36; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1191; AIR 2004 SC 4818; 2004 CRLJ 4634; 2005 (4) MahLJ 963; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 415; 2004(8) SCALE 107; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 597

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Public Nuisance

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Nuisance - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 129 to 132 and 133 - Trade injuries to health or causing physical discomfort - Grievance of residents that due to loading and unloading of chillies, pollution caused in area - Whether said act leads to nuisance and is liable to be discontinued? - Held, yes - In order to bring a trade or occupation within operation of s. 133, i.e., injurious to health or physical comfort, it must be proved that interference with public comfort was considerable - Large number of people affected - In present case, residents of an entire locality were affected - Conduct of trade must be injurious in present to health or physical comfort of community - Appeal dismissed.
