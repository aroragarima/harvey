A. P. Public Service Commission, Hyderabad and Another v B. Sarat Chandra and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 866; (1990) SCC (L&S) 377; (1990) 2 SCC 669; 1990 (13) ATC 708; 1990 (1) CLR 768; 1990 (61) FLR 17; JT 1990 (2) SC 143; 1990 (2) LLJ 135; 1990 (1) LLN 773; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 463; 1990 (4) SLR 235; 1990 (1) UJ 690

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: AP Police Service Rules 1966, Rules of Procedure of Public Service Commission

Summary: Service - Andhra Pradesh Police Service Rules, 1966, r. 5 - Rules of Procedure of Public Service Commission, r. 3 - Selection - Validity of - Whether, age of a candidate for civil services should be considered on date of application or on date of final selection? - The candidate must have completed 21 years on the first day of July of the year in which the selection is made - Process of selection begins with the issue of the advertisement and culminates in forwarding the list to the appointing authority - The essence of the process lies in the preparation of the list - A selection can be said to have been done only when the list is prepared - There are different steps in process of selection, minimum or maximum age for suitability of a candidate for appointment cannot be allowed to depend upon any uncertain date i.e. date of final selection or preparation of list of selected candidates - If final stage of selection is delayed and more often, it happens for various reasons, candidates who are eligible on date of application may find themselves eliminated at final stage for no fault of theirs - Appeal allowed.
