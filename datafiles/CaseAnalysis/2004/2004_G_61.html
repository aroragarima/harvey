Vice-Chairman, Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan and v Girdharilal Yadav

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1121; (2004) 6 SCC 325; 2004(6) SCALE 224

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 58 - Whether further hearing as a principle of natural justice should have been given to respondent after he was guilty of fraud? - Held, no - In terms of s. 58 of Evidence Act, 1872 fact admitted need not be proved - It is also a well-settled principle of law that principles of natural justice should not be stretched too far and same cannot be put in a straitjacket formula - Furthermore, respondent herein has been found guilty of an act of fraud - No further opportunity of hearing is necessary to be afforded to him - Appeal allowed.
