Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams v K. Jotheeswara Pillai (D) By Lrs And Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 454; (2007) 9 SCC 461; (2007) 2 SCC 534; AIR 2007 SC 1771; 2007 (2) CLR 822; 2007 (114) FLR 12; JT 2007 (6) SC 447; 2007 (3) LLJ 13; 2007 (4) LLN 62; 2007(6) SCALE 603; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 1; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2530

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Tirumala Tirupathi Devasthanams Employees Service Rules, 1989, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Conduct) Rules, 1964, Andhra Pradesh Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1963

Summary: Appeal against the judgment and order of Division Bench of High Court affirming the directions issued by Single Judge to appellant to reinstate the respondents with continuity of service and full back wages - Single Judge allowed the writ petition mainly on the ground that on two earlier occasions the appellant had granted exemption from age and qualifications and had also issued a writ of mandamus to the appellant to consider whether writ petitioner No. 5 was entitled for exemption from the requirement of age limit having regard to certain GOs issued by the Revenue Department of the State Governments - No material placed before the High Court as to why such a discretion could not be exercised by the appellant in favour of the concerned employees - Held, merely because on two earlier occasions the appellant granted exemption from eligibility criterion in respect of some employees cannot be a ground to grant relief to the writ petitioners (respondents); even if some concession had been shown to some employees in the past it would not confer any right upon anyone seeking employment in future to claim exemption from eligibility criterion as a matter of right - Appeal allowed.
