Muluwa S/O Binda and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, M. Hameedullah Beg, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 647; (1975) SCC (Cr) 759; (1976) 1 SCC 37; AIR 1976 SC 989; 1976 CRLJ 717; 1975 CRLR 521; 1975 UJ 692

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - CrPC, 1898, s. 417 - CrPC, 1974, s. 378 - (A) Appreciation of evidence - Reliability of evidence of infirm witness - (B) When interference with appeal against acquittal is not open - Held, evidence of an infirm witness does not become reliable merely because it has been corroborated by a number of witnesses of the same brand; for, evidence is to be weighed not counted - Where two views are possible and in the absence of any material irregularity etc. HC should not interfere - Appeal allowed.
