Osmania University Represented By Itsregistrar, Hyderabad, A v Abdul Rayees Khan & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.P. Kurdukar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4163; (1997) 3 SCC 124; JT 1997 (10) SC 384; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 6

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Service

Summary: Education - Service - Evaluation of work - Promotion - Selection Committee - Compliance of guidelines - Vice-Chancel of University had nominated panel for evaluation of works presented by respondent no. 1 and respondent no. 2 - After consideration of respective papers placed before Expert Committee, Dean of Faculty of Law recommended both respondent nos. 1 as well as 2 for consideration of merit promotion as Readers - Professor and Head of Department of Legal Studies stated that respondent no. 1 was prima facie qualified for promotion, as regards respondent no. 2 it stated that Selection Committee should go into articles and other material, research papers placed for consideration by respondent no. 2; if they were found to be based on any substantial research, he should be promoted - Therefore, Committee was constituted for selection - Single Judge held that evaluation committee of two professors had not adopted any objective criteria in evaluating papers presented by respective candidates while giving their opinion for consideration by selection committee - Selection Committee had not adopted any procedure in awarding marks for considering claims, thus, selection of respondent no. 2 was bad in law - Decision of Single Judge was upheld by DB - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether selection of respondent no 2 was according to law - Held, it would be self-evident to show that appellant had followed procedure in accordance with guidelines laid down by UGC in referring claims of respondents no.1 and 2 for evaluating their papers - Professors of outside University recommended cases of respondent no.1 and 2 for consideration for merit promotion as reader - It was found that respondent no.1 was qualified for consideration for promotion, he did not make any specific recommendation by leaving it to Selection Committee to consider research papers submitted by respondent no.2 - Outside experts were unanimous in recommending promotion of respondent no.2 as reader - Procedure for promotion from post of lecturer to reader as enjoined in statute and guidelines laid down by UGC, was scrupulously followed and was strictly complied with and after interviewing candidates, Committee unanimously found respondent n0.2 to be eligible for promotion as reader - Single Judge was not right in concluding that there was no objective evaluation by two experts and was also not right in concluding that Committee should have adopted procedure of awarding marks for selection of candidates - When lecturer was selected for promotion as reader, respective academic preferences and performance were considered by Committee - Thus, generally Court could not interfere with selection, relating to educational affairs, and academic matters could be left to expert body to select best of talent on objective criteria - Hence, order of Single Judge and of DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
