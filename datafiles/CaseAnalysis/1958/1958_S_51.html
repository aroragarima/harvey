Â M. P. V. Sundararamier and Company and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1958 SC 468; 1958 (1) MLJ(SC) 179; 1958 SCJ 459; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1422; 1958 (9) STC 298

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Andhra State Act, 1953, Sales Tax Laws Validation Act, 1956, Essential Commodities Act, 1952, C.P. & Berar Motor Vehicles (Amendment) Act, 1947, Commonwealth Of Australia Act, 1900

Summary: Constitution - Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939 - Andhra State Act, 1953 - Constitution of India, 1950 - The petitioners were dealers carrying on business in the city of Madras in the sale and purchase of yarn - Instant petition was seeking order to issue writ for restraining the respondent-state from taking proceedings for imposing tax on certain sales effected by them in favour of merchants who were residing or carrying on business in respondent-state, on the ground, inter alia, that the said sales were made in the course of inter-State trade, and that no tax could be levied on them by reason of the prohibition contained in art. 286(2) of the Constitution - Whether, the contention of the appellant was sustainable and the respondent-state should be restrained from imposing the tax - Held, after detailed consideration of the facts and the circumstances of the case and in the view of provisions of sales tax Act with which instant case was concerned did not authorize the taxing of a sale under which goods were delivered in Andhra but the property in them was passed in Madras - However, instant court in view of the opinion of the majority, opined in the favour of respondent - Petitions dismissed.
