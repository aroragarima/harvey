Yograj Infrastructure Limited v Ssang Yong Engineering and Construction Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 632; (2011) 9 SCC 735; 2011(6) ALL MR 464; 2012 (3) AWC 2282; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 177; 2011 (3) JabLJ 417; JT 2011 (10) SC 588; 2011(9) SCALE 567; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 301

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; International; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Curial Law, Singapore International Arbitration Act, 2002, Singapore International Arbitration Centre Rules

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - International - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 9(b)- Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 9, 37(2)(b), 37, 42 - Companies Act, 1956 - Singapore International Arbitration Act, 2002 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Singapore International Arbitration Centre Rules, rr. 32, 24 - Arbitration - Jurisdiction - Sustainability -Appellant/company incorporated under the 1956 Act and respondent/company incorporated under laws of Republic of Korea with its project office at New Delhi - National Highways Authority of India, New Delhi (NHAI) awarded contract to Respondent, 'SSY' for National Highways upgradation to Four Laning - Total contract amount was 2,19,01,16,805/- - Respondent entered into sub-Contract with appellant-company for carrying out work in question - Work Order of entire project was granted to appellant by respondent on back-to-back basis - Respondent Company issued notice of termination of Agreement, inter alia, on ground of delay in performing work under agreement - Appellant filed an application before District Court u/s. 9 the 1996 Act praying for interim reliefs - Similar application was filed by appellant before same Court on 30-12-2009also for interim reliefs - Dispute between parties was referred to arbitration in terms of agreement and Sole Arbitrator was appointed by Singapore International Arbitration Centre - Aggrieved by interim order appellant who was respondent before Arbitrator, appealed before District Judge, u/s. 37(2)(b) of the 1996 Act for setting aside same - On behalf of respondent it was contended in said appeal that same was not maintainable before learned District Judge, since seat of arbitration proceedings was in Singapore and said proceedings were governed by laws of Singapore - District Judge dismissed appeal as not maintainable - Appellant then moved Civil Revision before HC same was dismissed- Hence, instant appeal - Whether arbitral proceedings was maintainable as seat of arbitration proceeding was in Singapore and said proceedings were governed by laws of Singapore - Held, cl. 27.1 makes it quite clear that curial law which regulates procedure to be adopted in conducting arbitration would be Rules - Therefore, no ambiguity that Rules would be Curial law of arbitration proceedings - Parties had agreed to make Singapore seat of arbitration - Clause 27.1 indicates that arbitration proceedings were to be conducted in accordance with Rules - It was no longer available to appellant to contend that 'proper law' of agreement would apply to arbitration proceedings - As noticed, r. 32 of Rules provides that law of arbitration would be International Arbitration Act, 2002, where seat of arbitration was in Singapore - With regard to effect of s. 42 the 1996 Act, same, it was applicable at pre-arbitral stage, when arbitrator had not also been appointed - Once Arbitrator was appointed and arbitral proceedings were commenced, Rules became applicable shutting out applicability of s. 42 and for that matter Part I of the 1996 Act, including right of appeal u/s. 37 thereof - Appeal dismissed.
