C. Gangacharan v C. Narayanan

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 893; (2000) 1 SCC 459; AIR 2000 SC 589; 2000 (38) ALR 262; 2000 (1) CLT 90; 2000 (158) CTR 97; [2000] 242 ITR 126; JT 1999 (9) SC 640; 2000 (1) KLT 445; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 44; 2000 (1) RCR(Civil) 687; 1999(7) SCALE 518; 1999 (10) SLT 441; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 332; 1999 (10) Supreme 239

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Benami Transactions (Prohibition of the Right to Recover Property) Ordinance, 1986 - Indian Trusts Act, ss. 82, 4, 4(3)(b) - Suit for possession - Benami transactions - Appellant filed suit for possession against respondents - Suit for possession was decreed with costs and mesne profits - Respondent filed an appeal to the HC which dismissed the same - Special leave petition filed by respondent was dismissed by this Court - Appellant then- filed an execution application before the Trial Court - Meanwhile Benami Ordinance 1986 was promulgated - Respondents filed objections that decree could not be executed in view of the provision of the Ordinance - Executing court disallowed the objections - Respondents filed revision which was allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC on 27-8-1987 had given a categorical finding to the effect that the respondent was only a trustee and the case was governed by s. 82 of the Act. S. 4 of the Act which contains the prohibition to recover the property held benami expressly provides in s. 4 (3) (b) of the Act that the said section is not to; apply, inter alia in a case where the property is held in the name of a trustee. In view of the finding of the HC in its judgment of 27-8-1987 that the property was being held in the name of the respondent as a trustee, the question of the respondent invoking the provisions of the Benami Transactions Ordinance or the Act did not arise. The provisions of the Act did not prohibit a suit being filed against a trustee for the recovery of the trust property. HC in instant case fell in error in setting aside the decision of the executing court and in holding that the right of the appellant to recover possession had come to an end fray virtue of the said Act. Judgment of the HC under appeal 2-8-1988 is set aside. Appeal allowed.
