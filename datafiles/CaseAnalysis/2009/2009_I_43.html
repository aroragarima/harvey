D. Ramakrishnan v Intelligence Officer Narcotic Control Bureau

Supreme Court of India

27 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 983; (2009) 14 SCC 603; AIR 2009 SC 2404; 2009 (240) E.L.T. 321; 2009 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 149; JT 2009 (14) SC 473; 2009(10) SCALE 138; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Summary: Customs - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940, s. 80 - Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Rules, 1985, rr. 58, 59 - Export of Schedule 'G' and 'H' Drug - Revenue alleged that appellant exported Alprazolam, Lorazepam, Nitrazepam, etc. without taking permission from competent authority - Appellant contended that all drugs being Schedule 'G' and 'H' drugs under Drugs and Cosmetics Rules, 1945, export of thereof would not attract provisions of r. 58 of Rules - Revenue contended that in terms of r. 58 and 59 of Rules, no export of any drug is permissible unless permit obtained for export thereof from Competent Authority - Whether said export of Alprazolam, Lorazepam, Nitrazepam is valid? - Held, appellant and his co-accused got licenses under Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Said Act does not deal with exports - Exports deals with provisions of Customs Act - Appellant neither applied for nor granted any authority to export by Narcotic Commissioner or any other Officer who is authorized in this behalf - Hence, export not valid - Appeal dismissed.
