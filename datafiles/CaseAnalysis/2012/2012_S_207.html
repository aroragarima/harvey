Rashmi Rekha Thatoi & Anr v State Of Orissa & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 456; (2012) 5 SCC 690; JT 2012 (4) SC 563; 2012 (2) OLR 90; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 870; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 674

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438 - Denial of bail - Direction of HC - Legality - Respondents/accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 341, 294, 506 and 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Accused moved applications for bail before HC - HC did not grant bail, however, directed accused to surrender before concerned Court for availing bail - Appellant contended that HC had gravely flawed in passing such kind of orders in exercise of power u/s. 438 of CrPC - Whether orders passed by HC were legally sustainable u/s. 438 of CrPC - Held, arrest may become inevitable part of the process of investigation, legality of proposed arrest could not be gone into in an application u/s. 438 of CrPC - Role of the investigator was well-defined and jurisdictional scope of interference by the Court in the process of investigation was limited - Court ordinarily would not interfere with investigation of a crime or with arrest of accused in a cognizable offence - An interim order restraining arrest, if passed while dealing with an application u/s. 438 of CrPC would amount to interference in the investigation, which could not, at any rate, be done u/s. 438 of CrPC - In instant case, direction to admit accused persons to bail on their surrendering had no sanction in law and, in fact, created a dent in the sacrosanctity of law - It was contradictory in terms and law did not countenance paradoxes - Passing such kind of orders the interest of collective at large and that of individual victims was jeopardized - That apart, it curtailed the power of regular court dealing with the bail applications - Hence, impugned orders directing enlargement of bail of accused persons by Magistrate on their surrendering were wholly unsustainable and bound to founder and accordingly said directions were set aside - Appeals disposed of.
