Bagalkot Cement Company Limited v R. K. Pathan and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 127; AIR 1963 SC 439; 1962 (4) FLR 284; 1962 (1) LLJ 203; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 697

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, Sch (5)4 - (A) What is meaning of word 'condition' in Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, Sch., Cl. 5?- Held, word 'conditions' in (5) of Sch. has to be reasonably construed in broad and liberal sense - Dictionary meaning of word 'condition' is a provision or a stipulation - Now a provision or a stipulation as to leave and holidays will necessarily include a provision for quantum of holidays and leave and this construction is consistent with meaning of word 'condition' as employed in preamble to act - To construe (5) as not including a provision for quantum and extent of leave and holidays is to construe word 'conditions' narrowly - To adopt narrow construction of word 'conditions' in s. (5) will defeat very purpose of (5) - It will not be inconsistent with scheme of Schedule to hold that substantive provision for granting of leave and holidays along with conditions in respect of them have to be made by Standing Orders u/cl. 5 of Schedule - (B) What is scope of s. 4 of Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946? - Further held, after amendment of this section made in 1956 Legislature has imposed upon Certifying Officer and appellate authority duty to adjudicate upon fairness or reasonableness of provisions of any standing orders - Appeal dismissed.
