Mohan Amba Prasad Agnihotri and Others v Bhaskar Balwant Aher (D) Through LRs

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 564; (2000) 3 SCC 190; AIR 2000 SC 931; AIR 2000 SCW 690; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 230; 2000 (1) CLT 365; JT 2000 (2) SC 558; 2000 (1) RCR(Rent) 275; 2000(2) SCALE 186; 2000 (2) SLT 500; 2000 (2) Supreme 49

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Order of eviction - Appreciation of evidence by High Court - Legality of - (A) Whether HC ought not to have re-appreciated evidence and set aside findings of facts found by courts below concurrently and that on this ground alone order under appeal is liable to be set aside? - Held, HC failed to notice both pleadings and proof on aspect of damage to property and erroneously came to conclusion that finding was not supported by any evidence and set aside same on a non-existent ground - This reason is sufficient to set aside order of HC and restore decree of eviction of respondents passed by Trial court and confirmed by Appellate Court - The court set aside impugned order of HC and restore order of eviction passed by Lower Courts on said ground - (B) Imprudent user - Whether 1st respondent was guilty of 'imprudent use', causing damage and waste to the property, being a ground for eviction? - Held, s. 13(1)(a) of the Rent Control Act, 1947 r/w s. 108 of the Transfer of Property Act shows that the lessee shall use the premises as a person of ordinary prudence as if they were his own and that he shall not damage the demised building - The Trial Court as well as the Appellate Court found that the use of the premises by the first respondent was imprudent which caused damage to the suit premises - The HC set aside that finding - Though the HC noted that the respondents had stored the spare parts of motorcycles in the backyard in the passage and also on the roof which amounted to imprudent use of the suit premises having noticed the photographs of the suit premises and perused the report of the Commr., which mentions that the tenants placed spare parts of motorcycles and old motorcycles in the backyard and on the roof, it, however, commented that no evidence was produced to show that it had resulted in any injury or damage to the property and that the appellants had not examined any witness for this purpose - HC failed to notice the para 7 of the plaint and testimony of PW 1- Therefore 'imprudent user' has been proved - Appeal allowed.
