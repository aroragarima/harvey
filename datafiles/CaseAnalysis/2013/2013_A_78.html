C.B.I. v Ashok Kumar Aggarwal and another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 789; (2013) 15 SCC 222

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Economic Offence, Application for approver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 164, 166A, 306, 397, 401, 482 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114, 133 - Economic offence - Application for approver - Case was registered against respondent no. 1 and 2 - Respondent no. 2 filed application before Special Judge seeking pardon and making him an approver - Special Judge allowed application - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC - HC quashed the order and matter was remitted to Special Judge - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified - Held, respondent no. 1 agitated issue regarding application filed by respondent no. 2 seeking pardon and had lost before HC as well as before SC - These facts had not properly been placed by appellant before HC, while passing impugned judgment - Material required to be considered had not even been placed before Court while disposing of application for grant of pardon - Manner in which application had been dealt with as respondent no. 2 and appellant had been playing hide and seek with Court and in spite of fact that Court had asked appellant to disclose criminal cases pending against respondent no. 2, no information was furnished to Court - In facts and circumstances of case, substantial justice should not be defeated on mere technicalities - No cogent reason was found to interfere with impugned order - Appeal dismissed.
