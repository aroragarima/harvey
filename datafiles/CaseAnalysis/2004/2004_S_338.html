(1) Rudrappa Ramappa Jainpur and others; (2) v State Of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

02 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 549; (2004) 7 SCC 422; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1954; AIR 2004 SC 4148; 2004 (2) CGLJ 374; JT 2004 (6) SC 273; 2004(6) SCALE 379; 2004 (2) UC 1093

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - IPC - ss. 302 r/w 149, 326 r/w 149 - Whether HC justified in coming to conclusion that A-1 to A-6 alongwith A-9 (since deceased) formed themselves into an unlawful assembly, object of which was to commit murder of deceased - Held, injuries not on vital parts of body - Does not appear that A-3, A-4 and A-6 shared common object to commit murder - Object of unlawful assembly could not be to cause death of deceased - Formed an unlawful assembly common object of which was to cause grievous hurt to deceased - Since none of injuries found on deceased was in itself sufficient in ordinary course to cause death - Found guilty of offence under ss 326, 149 not under s 302 - Appeals Disposed Of.
