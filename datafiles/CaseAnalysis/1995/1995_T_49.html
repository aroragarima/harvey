T. Shantharam v State Of Karnataka And Ors

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1427; (1995) SCC (L&S) 537; (1995) 2 SCC 538; AIR 1995 SC 1123; 1995 (29) ATC 383; 1995 (70) FLR 1063; JT 1995 (2) SC 642; 1995 LabIC 613; 1995(1) SCALE 320; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 274; 1995 (1) SLR 674; 1995 (1) UJ 611

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Repatriation of deputationist - Relevant service rule provides that employee cannot be transferred and deputed to another department on higher post than held in parent department - Appellant, second division clerk in Revenue Department mistakenly deputed to hold higher post in food wing of department - Appellant satisfactorily discharged his duties and responsibilities in food wing uninterruptedly for about 32 years - Whether repatriation to parent Department justified - Held, it is not justified - Appellant entitled to be absorbed on higher post in food wing - Appeal allowed.
