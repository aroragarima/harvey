Shriram Bearings Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Patna

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 147; (2003) 12 SCC 185; 1998 (79) ECR 913; 1997 (91) E.L.T. 255

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - (A) Value for the purpose of excise duty on ball bearings manufactured by the assessee - Revenue argued that the duty liability had to be determined at the time of clearance and the ball bearings had been actually removed from the appellant's factory fitted with accessories, therefore, the composite value thereof was the excisable value of the ball bearings - Tribunal upheld contention of Revenue - Held, it cannot be charged along with accessories - Tariff Entry at the relevant time (No. 49) read, 'Rolling bearings, that is to say, ball or roller bearings, all sorts' - Clearly, what fell under this entry were the ball bearings and not what, admittedly, are the accessories thereof - Conclusion of the tribunal on this issue must be set aside - Tribunals order set aside - (B) Issue of post-manufacturing expenses in form of wooden crates used for purpose of safe transportation and transit insurance - Covered in Govt. of India v Madras Rubber Factory Ltd 1995 Indlaw SC 937 (SC) - Matter remanded to Asst. Commr. of Central Excise for determination - Appeal allowed.
