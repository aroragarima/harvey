General Manager, Mysore State Road Transport Corporation and Others v Devaraj URS and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 58; (1976) SCC (L&S) 345; (1976) 2 SCC 862; AIR 1976 SC 1027; 1976 (32) FLR 16; 1976 LabIC 634; 1976 (2) LLJ 306; 1976 SLJ 264; 1976 (2) SLR 233; 1975 UJ 929

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Disciplinary Proceeding, Disciplinary Authority

Summary: Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950, ss. 34(1) and 14(3)(b) - Constitution, arts. 12 and 226 - State govt. directed transport corporation as to service conditions etc. by way of notification D/- 01-08-1961 - Still corporation adopted old rules - Standing Order XIII also kept alive - Validity of dismissal of conductor by Deputy General Manager , which was recruited by by Divisional Controller, Mysore - Held, disciplinary action against the conductor was a breach of the statutory duty and made the action of the Corporation amenable to the jurisdiction of the High Court u/art. 226 of Constitution - Appeal Dismissed.
