Synco Industries v State Bank of Bikaner and Jaipur and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, B.P. Singh, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 25; (2002) 2 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 568; 2002 (2) AllCJ 907; 2002 (48) ALR 22; 2002 (3) AWC 2372; [2002] 110 Comp Cas 48; 2002 (2) CompLJ 262; 2002 (1) CPJ 16; 2002 (1) CPJ(Sc) 16; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 52; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 396; JT 2002 (5) SC 23; 2002 (130) PLR 791A; 2002(1) SCALE 148; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 225; 2002 (2) SLJ 511; 2002 (1) SLT 214; 2002 (1) Supreme 122; 2002 (1) UC 494B

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Since the claim for damages amounts to a sum of Rupees fifteen crores and an additional sum of Rupees sixty lakhs for covering the cost of travelling and other expenses, it is obvious that very detailed evidence would have to be led, both to prove the claim and thereafter to prove the damages and expenses, and therefore, the National Commission was right in giving the appellate liberty to move the Civil Court.
