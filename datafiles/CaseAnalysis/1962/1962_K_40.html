Â Kunj Beharilal Agarwal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 205; AIR 1963 SC 518; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Defence Service, Right To Promotion

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Army Instructions of 1945 and 1946 and order of Govt. of India, D/- 19-8-1949 - (A) Whether discrimination between Extra Temporary clerks and Temporary clerks violates arts. 14 and 16? - Held, on date when Constitution came into force position was that for determination of relative seniority between Extra Temporary Clerks and Temporary Clerks, while in case of former, date from which they should be deemed to have come into regular establishment and common roll was 1-08-1949, in case of later it was from date when they entered service-on this basis petitioner (Ex Extra Temporary clerk) could obviously not claim that any rights as to seniority which he possessed on date when Constitution came into force were, in any way, restricted or denied to him by impugned order of 20-04-1955 - It would be apparent that order of Govt. of 20-04- 1955 impugned ,was really a concession in favor of petitioner and not any detraction from rights that he possessed at commencement of Constitution - No violation of fundamental right - (B) Whether order passed by Govt. before 26-01-1950 can be challenged as violative of fundamental right? - Further held, by reason of orders passed by Govt., rights of petitioner and those like him had become settled, petitioner could not invoke constitutional guarantees under Part III of Constitution for their enforcement, for challenging legality of orders passed before Constitution, as Constitution was not retrospective - Petition dismissed.
