K. P. G. Nair v Jindal Menthol India Limited

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2686; (2001) 10 SCC 218; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1038; 2001 (1) BC 243; 2000 (4) CCR 100; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 290; 2001 (2) CompLJ 258; 2 Crimes 132; 2000 (4) CTC 432; 2000 (2) JCC 795; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 519; 2000(6) SCALE 578; 2000 (7) SLT 189; 2001 (2) Supreme 311

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - High Court dismissed petition of appellant for quashing of proceedings - Whether ingredients of s. 141 prima facie made out in respect of appellant? - A person other than company can be proceeded against under those provisions, only if that person was in charge of and was responsible to company for conduct of its business - Held, allegations made in complaint do not, either in express words or with reference to allegations contained therein, make out a case that at time of commission of offence, appellant was in charge of and was responsible to company for conduct of its business - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
