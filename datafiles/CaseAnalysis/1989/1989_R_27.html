Lachhman Dass v Ram Lal And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 551; (1989) 3 SCC 99; AIR 1989 SC 1923; 1989 (2) ARBLR 165; 1989 (2) CCC 207; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 78; 1989(1) SCALE 921; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 250

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Regulation Act 1908

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Registration Act, 1908 - Award holding that half share in immovable property of a person is benami - Another party owner of entire property - Creates right in immovable property - Award, is not merely declaratory - Award not registered though required - Cannot be considered by court for pronouncing judgment in terms thereof - Registration of award during pendency of appeal before Supreme Court - Not relevant - Held, must be registered when value of property exceeds Rs. 100 - Award not registered though required cannot be considered by court for pronouncing judgment in terms thereof - No judgment upon award can be pronounced upon unregistered award.
