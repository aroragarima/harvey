Messrs Sasa Musa Sugar Works Private Limited v Shobrati Khan and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 77; AIR 1959 SC 923; 1959 (2) LLJ 388; 1960 SCJ 10; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 836

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33- Acts of misconduct -Suspended - Challenged - Appellant/company suspended respondents/workers for misconduct and applied for permission from Tribunal u/s. 33 of the Act to dismiss suspended respondents/workers - Tribunal rejected appellant's application - Appellant filed appeal and was dismissed by appellate Tribunal holding that permission u/s. 33 of the Act should be obtained before giving punishment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Order of appellate Tribunal was justified - Held, Tribunal was wrong in stating that action on respondents may be taken bases on evidence when Tribunal itself had admitted that acts of respondents amount to misconduct which was upheld by appellate Tribunal -Suspension notice clearly stated that suspension was pending further orders followed by application to Tribunal u/s. 33 of the Act - Appellate Tribunal erred in holding that suspension was punishment when notice clearly stated that suspension was interim measure pending enquiry and proceedings in Tribunal - Management was liable to pay wages to suspended workmen as it failed to conduct enquiry proceedings before applying permission u/s. 33 of the Act as laid down by SC in Management of Ranipur Colliery v. Bhuban Singh,1959 Indlaw SC 12 - Permission u/s. 33 of the Act was granted to appellant subject to wages for suspension period was paid to respondents/workmen - Impugned Orders of both Tribunals below were set aside - Appeals allowed.
