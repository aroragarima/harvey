Amarkant Rai v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 185; (2015) 8 SCC 265; 2015 (3) AWC 2556; 2015 (146) FLR 75; JT 2015 (3) SC 107; 2015 (2) LLJ 1; 2015(3) SCALE 505; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 542; 2015 (3) SLR 658

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Irregular Appointment, Entitlement of, Appointment on daily wages

Summary: Service - Bihar State Universities Act, 1976, ss. 10(6), 35 - Appointment on daily wages - Regularisation - Entitlement of - Appellant was appointed temporarily in Class IV post of Night Guard, on daily wages by Principal of College, which was affiliated to University - Thereafter, Principal recommended for absorption of appellant against vacant posts - However, claim for regularization of services was rejected as it was not in consonance with Recruitment Rules laid down by Constitution Bench in Secretary, State of Karnataka & Ors. v. Umadevi (3) & Ors., 2006 Indlaw SC 125 - Aggrieved appellant approached HC by way of writ petition, which was dismissed holding that it was clear case of violation of s. 10(6) and s. 35 of the Act - Order of Single Judge was confirmed in appeal Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that he served on post for 29 years on daily wages and even as per decision in Umadevi's case 2006 Indlaw SC 125, his case should be considered.

Held, the exception carved out in para 53 of Umadevi's case, 2006 Indlaw SC 125, is applicable to the facts of the instant case. There is no material placed on record by the respondents that the appellant has been lacking any qualification or bear any blemish record during his employment for over two decades. Appellant although initially working against unsanctioned post, the appellant was working continuously since 3-1-2002 against sanctioned post. Since there is no material placed on record regarding the details whether any other night guard was appointed against the sanctioned post, in the facts and circumstances of the case, Court is inclined to award monetary benefits be paid from 1-1-2010. Considering facts and circumstances of the case that appellant has served the University for more than 29 years and has served the College on daily wages, in the interest of justice, the authorities are directed to regularize the services of the appellant retrospectively with effect from 3-1-2002 (the date on which he rejoined the post as per direction of Registrar). Impugned order of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
