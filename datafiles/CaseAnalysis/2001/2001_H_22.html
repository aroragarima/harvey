Hamida and Others v Md. Khalil

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20128; (2001) 5 SCC 30; AIR 2001 SC 2282; 2002 (4) AWC 2925; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 173; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 60; 2001 (1) RCR(Rent) 564; 2001 (92) RD 783; 2001(4) SCALE 1; 2001 (4) SLT 277; 2001 (4) Supreme 21; 2001 (2) UC 113

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Eviction - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Trial Court dismissed eviction suit of plaintiff but decreed suit for specific performance by defendant - Plaintiff appealed against judgments and decrees in both suits - First Appellate Court reversed them but finally High Court dismissed suit by plaintiff for eviction - Whether HC erred in interfering with judgment and decree in absence of any substantial question of law? - Finding of fact recorded by First Appellate Court based on evidence could not be interfered with by HC, that too in absence of any substantial question of law that arose for consideration between parties - Held, HC neither framed a substantial question of law nor is any such question indicated in impugned judgment as required u/s. 100 of CPC, 1908, therefore HC's judgment is clearly and manifestly erroneous and unsustainable in law - Appeal allowed.
