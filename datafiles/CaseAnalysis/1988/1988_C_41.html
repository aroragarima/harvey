Indian Oxygen Limited v Collector Of Central Excise.

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Sabyasachi Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 799; (1988) Supp SCC 658; (1989) SCC (Tax) 9; AIR 1988 SC 1809; 1988 CRLR 522; 1988 CrLR(SC) 522; 1988 (18) ECC 168; 1988 (18) ECR 161; 1988 (36) E.L.T. 723; JT 1988 (3) SC 291; 1988(2) SCALE 291; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 687; 1988 (2) UJ 526

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Price List, Ascertainable

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4(1)(a) and 4(4)(d)(i) - Manufacturers of compressed oxygen and acetylene - Received show cause notices in respect of price - List submitted by them - Whether criteria for determining value of goods for imposing duty was proper? - Held, value of goods has to be computed under s. 4(1)(a), r/w s. 4(4)(d)(i) of Act - Wholesale price is ascertainable at factory gate - If ex-factory prices were not ascertainable and goods were to be assessable ex-depot, then it would be for manufacturer to claim on basis of actual evidence that deductions should be admissible from price list as per provisions of Act - Tribunal clearly made a finding that there was an ex-factory price which was ascertainable - Price should be basis upon which value is to be determined, other expenses, costs or charges must be excluded - Cost of transportation from factory gate to place of delivery and transit expenses were not be added to wholesale price at factory gate - Delivery and collection charges also had to be excluded - Loading charges incurred for loading goods within factory is to be included irrespective of who had paid for same - Loading expenses incurred outside factory gate were excludible - Appeal disposed of.
