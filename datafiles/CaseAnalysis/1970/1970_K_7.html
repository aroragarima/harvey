Dy. Chief Controller of I. and E., New Delhi v K. T. Kosalram

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 13; (1970) 3 SCC 82; (1970) SCC (Cr) 554; AIR 1971 SC 1283; 1971 CRLJ 1081; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 43; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Control Order

Summary: Constitution, art.136 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 16, r. 3 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 5 - Imports (Control) Order, 1955, cl. 5(4) - (A) Complaint - Filed by authority duly authorised by statute - Public Prosecutor shown as petitioner in petition for enhancement of sentence - Also filing of application (not leave to appeal to SC ) filed - Legality - (B) Import -Export - Two printing presses imported under actual user's licence - Under the condition that goods will be utilized only for consumption as raw materials or accessories in licence-holder's factory and no portion thereof be sold - However licensee negotiating for sale of one of them while import was being carried out and actually sorting it thereafter - (C) Interpretation of Statutes - Construction of word of Statute or instrument - (D) Appeal against acquittal - Contention that High Court having acquitted the accused, SC should not convert acquittal into conviction - View of HC found not to be sustainable on statutory language of 1947 Act and import control policy of which accused were fully aware - Held, original complainant was not disentitled to apply for special leave to appeal to SC and to prosecute the same on merit and Special leave already granted could not be revoked - Held, the condition of licence was applicable to the case and the breach thereof was fully intended and designed - Dictionary meanings Use of - Held, contention could not be accepted - Appeal Allowed.
