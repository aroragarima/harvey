Â M. Ramanatha Pillai v State of Kerala and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg, S.N. Dwivedi, Y.V. Chandrachud, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 182; (1973) SCC (L&S) 560; (1973) 2 SCC 650; AIR 1973 SC 2641; 1973 LabIC 1593; 1973 (2) LLJ 409; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 515; 1974 (1) SLR 225

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Abolition Of Post

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f), 31 and 311 - Doctrine of pleasure - Applicability of - (A) Whether Govt. has a right to abolish a post in service? - Held, yes - Power to create or abolish a post is not related to doctrine of pleasure - It is a matter of Govt. policy - Every sovereign Govt. has this power in interest and necessity of internal administration - Creation or abolition of post is dictated by policy decision, exigencies of circumstances and administrative necessity - (B) Whether abolition of post is dismissal or removal within meaning of art. 311? - Held, no - Abolition of post may have consequence of termination of service of a Govt. servant, but such termination is not dismissal or removal within meaning of art. 311 - Abolition of post is an executive policy decision - A Govt. servant cannot complain of a violation of arts. 19(1)(f) and 31 of Constitution, 1950 when post is abolished - Appeals dismissed.
