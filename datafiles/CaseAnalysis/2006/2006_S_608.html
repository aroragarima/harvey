State of Uttar Pradesh and Another v Om Prakash and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 333; (2006) 6 SCC 474; AIR 2006 SC 3080; 2006 (6) AWC 6382; JT 2006 (6) SC 452; 2006(7) SCALE 280; 2006 (5) SLR 104; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 793; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2291

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Health & Drug

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Homeopathic Medical Service Rule, 1990

Summary: [A.] Service - Uttar Pradesh Homeopathic Medical Service Rule, 1990, r. 8 - Controversy revolves around selection made by Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission for Medical Officers of Homeopathy - Held, requisite academic qualification for post of homeopathy as prescribed in advertisement was a recognized degree in Homeopathy or a recognized diploma in Homeopathy - Proviso has been added that preference will be given to degree holders - Preference would mean that when claims of all candidates who are eligible and who possess requisite educational qualification prescribed in advertisement are taken for consideration and when one or more of them are found equally positioned, then only additional qualification may be taken as a tilting factor - Preference does not mean en bloc preference irrespective of inter se merit and suitability - HC has misdirected itself by issuing such directions despite fact that respondents were not selected by Commission - Appeals disposed of.
