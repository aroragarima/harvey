Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Lucknow v Raja Vishwanath Pratap Singh

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2667; (1996) 8 SCC 122; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 30; 1996 (133) CTR 182; [1996] 222 ITR 189; JT 1996 (4) SC 62; 1996(3) SCALE 313; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 1150; 1996 (3) Supreme 423; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 673

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Uttar Pradesh Encumbered Estates Act, 1934, s. 4 - Wealth Tax Act, ss. 2(m), 17 and 27 - (A) Whether debts amounting to Rs. 30 lakhs and odd, more or less for each of assessment years under appeal, were rightly not allowed as a deduction in calculating net wealth of assessee? - Amounts invested in Govt. securities were investments made by Court of Wards out of savings from income of estate in its hands, it was held by Special Judge that this property was not available for payment of decretal dues obtained by creditors in proceedings under Estates Act, and High Court affirmed view, therefore, creditors of deceased could not proceed against these Govt. securities to recover decretal dues - Held, having successfully thwarted attempts of decree-holders to proceed against aforesaid Govt. securities and income arising therefrom, assessee cannot now be heard to say that decretal dues are his debts which are personally payable by him and question is answered in affirmative - (B) Whether provisions of s. 17 of Wealth Tax Act were applicable so far as Assessment Years for 1957-58, 1958-59 and 1959-60 are concerned? - Son's obligation to pay off debts contracted by his father is limited to properties inherited by son from his father and it is not assessee's case that he has inherited amount of Rs. 6,87,000 held in Govt. securities from his father, in any event, it was held by HC in its order D/- 25-03-1961 that a decree obtained by creditors could not be executed against these Govt. securities and question is answered in affirmative - Appeals allowed.
