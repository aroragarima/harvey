Collector Of Central Exise, Culcutta-Ii v Eastend Paper Industries Ltd. And Anr

Supreme Court of India

29 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 766; (1989) 4 SCC 244; AIR 1990 SC 1893; 1990 CRLR 11; 1990 CrLR(SC) 11; 1990 (40) DLT 35; 1989 (24) ECC 375; 1990 (26) ECR 10; 1989 (43) E.L.T. 201; [1990] 186 ITR 105; JT 1989 (3) SC 518; 1989(2) SCALE 456; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 1017; 1990 (77) STC 203

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Tariff Item

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excises Tariff Act, 1985, sch. 1, item 17(1) - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 9(1) - Exemption - Notification No. 18A/83-CE dt. 9.07.1983 - Wrapping paper - Respondent manufacture different varieties of printing paper including wrapping paper falling under item No. 17(1) of Central Excise Tariff - Department issued show cause notice to respondent alleging that they had removed 4, 000 kgs. of wrapping valued at Rs. 13, 200/- without payment of Central excise duty - Respondent submitted that finished product by wrapping paper is a process incidental and ancillary to the completion of the manufactured product and wrapping is used as a component part of the finished excisable goods attracting the benefit of the notification No. 18A/83-CE - Said submission of the respondent was rejected by the Superintendent (Technical) - Collector (Appeals) also rejected the claim to exemption in respect of such wrapping paper - Tribunal set aside the order of the Collector (Appeal) - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondent is entitled to benefit of exemption notification no. 18A/83-CE in respect of wrapping paper? - Held, anything required to make the goods marketable must form part of the manufacture and any raw material or any materials used for the same would be a component part for the end-product - Hence, tribunal was right in the view that wrapping paper is used as a component part of finished excisable goods, therefore, it would attract benefit of notification no. 18A/83-CE - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
