A. K. Singhania v Gujarat State Fertilizer Company Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 690; (2013) 16 SCC 630; AIR 2014 SC 71; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 4098; 2013 (4) BC 623; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 771; [2014] 182 Comp Cas 572; 2013 (4) CompLJ 486; 2014 CRLJ 340; 2013 (4) JCC (NI) 226; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 186; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 844; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 777; 2013(12) SCALE 673; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 1069

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881, s. 138 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Complaints were filed by against accused co., its Chairman, Managing Director and other Directors including accused Nos. 7 and 9 alleging commission of an offence u/s. 138 of the Act - It was alleged that 'all business and financial affairs of the accused co. were decided, organized, administered by accused Nos. 1 to 5' - It was further alleged that accused Nos. 1 to 5 did so with consultation of other Directors namely, accused Nos. 6 to 12 - Magistrate took cognizance of the offence, issued process to the accused persons to face trial for commission of the offence u/s. 138 of the Act - Accused No. 9, aggrieved by the order issuing summons to face trial u/s. 138 of the Act, filed applications u/s. 482 of the CrPC for quashing the order taking cognizance and issuing process - Applications were allowed - Accused No. 7 also, filed separate applications for quashing the entire prosecution - HC by the impugned order dismissed all the applications - Hence, instant appeals - Whether orders passed by HC were justified - Held, in the case of offence by Co., to bring its Directors within the mischief of s. 138 of the Act, it would be necessary to allege that they were in charge of and responsible to the conduct of the business of the Co. - In the instant case, there was no averment that accused Nos. 7 and 9 were in-charge of and responsible for the conduct of the business of the co. at the time the offence was committed - Hence, there was no essential averment in the complaints - Prosecution of accused Nos. 7 and 9 could not be allowed to continue - Order of the HC quashing the prosecution of the accused No. 9 was upheld - Also, order passed by the HC declining the prayer of accused no. 7 for quashing of the prosecution set aside - Appeals disposed of.
