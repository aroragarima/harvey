Lata Wadhwa and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20611; (2001) 8 SCC 197; 2001 (2) ACC 316; AIR 2001 SC 3218; 2003 (2) AWC 1486; 2001 (91) FLR 705; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 53; JT 2001 (6) SC 431; 2001 (2) LLJ 1559; 2001 (129) PLR 9; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 673; 2001(5) SCALE 286; 2001 (5) SCJ 68; 2001 (5) SLT 826; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 578; 2001 (6) Supreme 151; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2268

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Factories Act, 1948 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 144 - Celebrations in factory - Organizers had not taken adequate safety measures - Fire engulfed area and a number of people died - Petition against Co. - Co. did not wish to treat litigation as an adversarial one - Matter left to court for determining what monetary compensation should be paid - Court held, grant of compensation should be looked into by a person, having expertise and ultimately requested Y V Chandrachud, former Chief Justice of India to look into matter and determine compensation, payable to legal heirs of deceased as well as compensation payable to injured persons - Report, quantifying compensation payable prepared and submitted by Y V Chandrachud - Objection filed to report - Report consisted of two parts, Part I dealing with cases of death and Part II dealing with cases of burn injury - Shri Chandrachud came to conclusion that multiplier method is of universal application and it would be proper to apply said method for determining quantum of compensation - Multiplier method involved ascertainment of loss of dependency or multiplicand having regard to circumstances of case and capitalizing multiplicand by an appropriate multiplier - Choice of multiplier determined by age of deceased and by calculation as to what capital sum, if invested at a rate of interest appropriate to a stable economy, would yield multiplicand by way of annual interest - Held, Justice Chandrachud correctly arrived at basic figure as well as applied proper multiplier - In case of death of an infant, there may have been no actual pecuniary benefit derived by its parents during child's lifetime but this would not necessarily bar parents' claim and prospective loss - Valid claim provided that parents established that they had a reasonable expectation of pecuniary benefit if child had lived - So far as compensation to injured persons concerned though, on behalf of claimants, compensation on several heads had been claimed, but unfortunately, no materials had been placed, which could have been placed - A bare perusal of report bear testimony to fact that claimants did not discharge their obligations by putting relevant materials to enable Justice Chandrachud to arrive at quantum of compensation - Justice Chandrachud showed maximum sympathy and determined compensation to maximum extent possible, which was not objected to by Co. - Writ Petition disposed of.
