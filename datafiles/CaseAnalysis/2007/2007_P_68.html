Post Master General, Kolkata And Others v Tutu Das (Dutta)

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 450; (2007) 2 SCC 179; (2007) 5 SCC 317; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1126; 2007 (6) AWC 5727; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 101; JT 2007 (6) SC 340; 2007 (3) LLJ 163; 2007 (4) LLN 66; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 322; 2007(6) SCALE 453; 2007 (4) SCJ 95; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 1117; 2007 (3) SLR 583; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1921

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Irregular Appointment

Summary: Appeal against the judgment of High Court dismissing the petition filed against the order of the CAT for regularizing the services of the respondent - Services of the respondent had not been regularized, she filed a contempt application; extension was sought for by the appellant to comply with the said direction, which having been rejected, the respondent was asked to produce relevant documents showing the period during which she had worked as EDA substitute in different post offices ; there was nothing on record to show that she brought such materials on records - High Court did not come to a positive finding that she had worked for more than 240 days in a year - Held, completion of 240 days of continuous service in a year would be attracted only in a case where retrenchment has been effected without complying with the provisions contained in Section 25F of the Industrial Disputes Act , but would not be relevant for regularisation of service; only something that is irregular for want of compliance with one of the elements in the process of selection which does not go to the root of the process, can be regularised and that it alone can be regularised and granting permanence of employment is a totally different concept and cannot be equated with regularization - Appeal allowed.
