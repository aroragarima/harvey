Viklad Coal Merchant, Patiala, Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 158; (1984) 1 SCC 619; AIR 1984 SC 95; 1983(2) SCALE 619; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Keywords: Carrier, Patent, Railway Act 1890

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 14 and 19(1)(g) - Indian Railway Act, 1890, ss. 27A and 28 - Whether right to equality of opportunity of petitioners u/art. 14 has been violated due to fact that they have been denied use of railways for transport of coal from various coal fields? - Held, no - S. 27 A was introduced in Act which enabled it by a general or special order to direct railway administration to grant special facilities for or preference to transport of goods - Such a general or special order can be issued by Central Govt. if in its opinion it is necessary in public interest to do so - Goods consigned to Central Govt. or to Govt. of any State must obviously have a priority over what may loosely be described as private transporters, because it is well-settled that Central or State Govt. is in a class by itself - This view is founded on assumption that all activities of State are in public interest in sense that they are either undertaken on behalf of public or that loss or gain arising from them falls upon public - Action of railway administration in complying with such special or general order could not be said as tantamounting to giving undue or unreasonable preference or advantage to or in favour of any particular person or railway administration - Petitions dismissed.
