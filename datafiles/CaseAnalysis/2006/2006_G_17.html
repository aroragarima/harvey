State of Punjab and Others v Ganpat Raj

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 814; (2006) 8 SCC 364; AIR 2006 SC 3089; 2006 (6) AWC 5604; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 476; 2006 (4) JhrCR(SC) 131; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2919; 2007 (1) MPLJ 472; 2007 (145) PLR 18; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 497; 2006(9) SCALE 164; 2006 (7) SCJ 364; 2006 (6) SLR 57; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 25; 2006 (3) UC 2000

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Legal Services Authorities Act, 1987, s. 20 - Whether a pension matter could have been disposed of by Lok Adalat? - Meaning of 'compromise' and 'settlement' - 'Compromise' is a bilateral agreement reached by adjustment of conflicting or opposing claims by reciprocal modification of demands - 'Settlement' is termination of legal proceedings by mutual consent - Held, case at hand did not involve compromise or settlement and could not have been disposed of by Lok Adalat - Appeal allowed.
