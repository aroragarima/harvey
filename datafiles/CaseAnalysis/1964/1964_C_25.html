Indian Copper Corporation Limited v Commissioner of Commercial Taxes, Bihar, and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 436; AIR 1965 SC 891; 1965 (16) STC 259

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Processing

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Certificate of registration - Corporation applied for registration as a dealer under Central Sales Tax Act (74 of 1956) setting out a list of goods for specification in certificate of registration - High Court given certificates for locomotives and motor-vehicles, accessories and spare parts for motor-vehicles and loco-motives, tyres and tubes for motor-vehicles and Household, laboratory, hospital and general furnishings and fittings and rest was denied - All these items were required for mining operations and in manufacture of copper and for workmen employed in those operations - Whether items like medical supplies, stationery and cane baskets could be specified in certificate of registration? - Held, order passed by HC will be modified - Items Locomotives and motor vehicles, accessories and spare parts for motor-vehicles and locomotives,tyres and tyres for motor vehicles, laboratory fittings out of item (iii) used for sampling and analysis of ore and other raw materials in mining operations and in process of manufacture for sale and cane baskets out of item (vii) used for carrying ore and other materials used in course of manufacture, will be specified in certificate of registration - Household, hospital equipment with furnishings and fittings [part of item (iii)], medical supplies [item (iv)], stationery [item (v)] and cane baskets used for collecting refuse to protect health and cleanliness in colony of workmen [part of item (vii)] will not be included in certificate of registration - Appeal partly allowed.
