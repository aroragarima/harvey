Hazara Singh v Raj Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, M.Y. Eqbal, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 254; (2013) 9 SCC 516; AIR 2013 SC 3273; 2013 CRLJ 2299; JT 2013 (6) SC 529; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 851; 2013(6) SCALE 142; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attempt to murder, Reduction of sentence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 307, 323, 324, 435 and 447 - Attempt to murder - Grievous injury - Conviction - Reduction of sentence - Sustainability - Respondents/accused assaulted victim by blunt and sharp edged weapons - Charge sheet against accused was filed u/ss. 148, 149, 307, 323, 324, 435 and 447 IPC - Trial Court convicted accused - Aggrieved on appeal by accused persons against the order of conviction and revision was preferred by appellant for enhancement of sentence - HC dismissed the revision and partly allowed the appeal by reducing sentence to the period already undergone - Hence instant appeals - Whether the HC was justified in reducing the sentence awarded to accused persons to the period already undergone - Held, it was unfortunate that the HC failed to appreciate that the reduction of sentence merely on the ground of long pending trial was not justifiable - In SC's considered view, ground was irrelevant for the purpose of determining sentence to be awarded to accused - Courts could not let accused go scot-free on mere suspicion of eruption of enmity between the families - Reduction of sentence passed by the HC without appreciating the nature of offence, grievous injuries of witnesses/victims, was unsustainable - SC was satisfied from the statements of eye-witnesses coupled with the medical evidence, it was proved that accused caused injuries in the manner as propounded by prosecution - It was also proved that 'BS' inflicted injury with a blunt weapon on the left shoulder of victim - It was proved that the injury was caused by a sharp-edged weapon - HC failed to take note of a very relevant fact that with regard to the offence u/s. 307 IPC, 'RK' was charge sheeted individually for causing grievous injury on the head of 'MS' with an intention or knowledge and under such circumstances, if by that act, he had caused death of the said 'MS', he would have been guilty of murder - Impugned sentence imposed by the HC was set aside and restored the sentence imposed by the Trial Court - Appeals allowed.
