Dwarka Prasad Singh & Others v Harikant Prasad Singh & Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.K. Mukherjea

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 331; (1973) 1 SCC 179; AIR 1973 SC 655; 1973 LLJ 467; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 1064; 1973 UJ 467

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 1, r. 10, O. 22, r. 3 - Practice and Procedure - Necessary party - Suit for specific performance against vendor and subsequent purchaser - (A) Suit - Notice of a prior agreement of sale - Vendor, if necessary party Applicability of - (B) Plaintiff in the alternative claiming a decree for certain amount consisting of part consideration paid and certain other amount - Appeal against decree - Death of vendor during pendency of - LRs not brought on record - Held, vendor ia a necessary party - Held, r. 4 could not be invoked - Abatement of appeal against vendor was fatal to entire appeal as either inconsistent and contradictory decrees will have to be passed or proper relief could not be granted in his or his LR' s absence - Appeal dismissed.
