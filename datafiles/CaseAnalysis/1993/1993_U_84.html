B.K. Industries and Others Etc v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 April 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 684; (1993) Supp3 SCC 621; AIR 1993 SC 2123; 1993 (47) ECR 32; 1993 (65) E.L.T. 465; JT 1993 (2) SC 709; 1993(2) SCALE 541; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 51; 1993 (91) STC 548

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Vegetable Oils Cess Act, 1983, Cotton, Copra And Vegetable Oils Cess (Abolition) Act, 1987, Produce Cess Act, 1966, Vegetable Oil Cess Act, 1983, National Oilseeds and Vegetable Oils Development Board Act, 1983, Copra Cess Act, 1979

Summary: Indirect Tax - Vegetable Oils Cess Act, 1983, s.3 - National Oilseeds and Vegetable Oils Development Board Act, 1983 - Petitioners questioned the validity of the levy and collection of cess, u/s. 3 of the 1983 Cess Act - Hence, the present petition - Whether the levy of tax was impermissible under the 1983 Cess Act - Held, that as the Preamble to the 1983 Cess Act indicated, the levy of the said cess was not for the purpose of the Board but for the purpose of the development of the Oilseeds Industry and Vegetable Oils Industry, and the Board was merely a medium in that effort, so the cess was permissible - It should be noticed that the cess was to be credited to the Consolidated Fund of India, out of which requisite sums of money to be utilised for the purpose of the 1983 Board Act - There was nothing to show that the Board did not become operational except the bare assertion to that effect - Vague allegation could not merit any credence - Petitions dismissed.
