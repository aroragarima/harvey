Damoh Panna Sagar Rural Regional Bank and another v Munna Lal Jain

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1091; (2005) 10 SCC 84; (2005) SCC (L&S) 567; AIR 2005 SC 584; 2005 (1) AWC 561; 2005 (1) CLR 821; 2005 (3) ESC 338; 2005 (104) FLR 291; JT 2005 (1) SC 70; 2005 (1) LLJ 730; 2005 (1) LLN 662; 2004(10) SCALE 590; 2005 (1) SLR 599; 2005 (1) Supreme 427; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 530

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Whether judgment rendered by a DB of Madhya Pradesh HC directing Board of Directors of employer Bank to reconsider matter and pass any punishment other than dismissal, removal of termination of respondent is sustainable? - Held, there is no definite finding recorded that punishment is suffering from any infirmity - No basis has been indicated to direct re-consideration of quantum of punishment - Respondent had miserably failed to prove bonafides - If punishment imposed is shockingly disproportionate it would be appropriate to direct Disciplinary Authority or Appellate Authority to reconsider penalty imposed - Impugned judgment of HC cannot be maintained.
