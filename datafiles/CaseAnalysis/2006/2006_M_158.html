Union of India and Others v Messrs Asian Food Industries

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 914; (2006) 13 SCC 542; AIR 2007 SC 750; 2006 (204) E.L.T. 8; JT 2007 (10) SC 65; 2006(12) SCALE 105; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 485

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Exchange Control & FDI; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Bill Of Lading

Summary: Trade - Contract & Commercial - Customs - Foreign Trade (Development and Regulation) Act, 1992,ss. 3, 3(2), 3(3), 5 - Customs Act,1962,ss.11,39,50,51 - Constitution of India,1950,art. 14 -Export - Notification of ban - Relaxation - Shipment not permitted - Challenged - Respondents got export orders for supply of pulses and peas and exported material and got clearance for balance containers including bills of lading - Govt. of India banned export of pulses for 6 months by notification dt.27-6-2006 and communicated to all ports - Subsequently Govt. of India issued another notification dt.4-7-2006 permitting export of pulses against irrevocable letter of credit opened prior to 22-6-2006 - Respondents request of shipment was refused - Aggrieved respondents filed writ petitions separately and were allowed by concerned HC's - Hence instant appeals - As both appeals involved common question of law and facts were disposed of by common judgment - Whether Orders of HC were justified - Held, relevance should be actual permission of proper officer granting clearance and loading of goods for export - When permission was granted procedures laid down for export were held to have been complied with - SC in Talcher Municipality v. Talcher Regulated Market Committee and Another, 2004 Indlaw SC 506 and in K. Ramanathan v. State of Tamil Nadu and another, 1985 Indlaw SC 438 held that word regulation would be interpreted broadly and it might amount to prohibition - In terms of the 1992 Act and by policy and procedure terms were required to be applied in different situations - Prohibition promulgated by statutory order would have only prospective effect - Statute must be construed in light of subordinate legislations framed - When subordinate legislation had been framed by same authority which exercises power under policy intention of such policy maker must be found out from words used therein albeit having regard to rights of exporters which were sought to be protected - Judgment of Gujarat HC was upheld and Order of Delhi HC was set aside - Appeals disposed of.
