Sukh Ram v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Dwivedi, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 288; (1974) SCC (Cr) 186; (1974) 3 SCC 656; AIR 1974 SC 323; 1974 CRLJ 354; 1974 CrLR(SC) 43; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 518; 1974 UJ 98

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal -Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Murder with common intention -Charge mentioned that murder was committed by three named individuals - Evidence proved that murder was committed by appellant with two unknown person - Whether prejudice could be caused to appellant by acquittal of other accused? - Where charge specifically mentioned that murder was committed by three named individuals but evidence was led to show that appellant along with two unknown persons had committed crime - It was fully established that appellant was amongst three assailants of deceased and that pistol was fired at deceased in furtherance of common intention of all three assailants - Held, no prejudice could be said to have been caused to appellant by reason of his conviction u/s 302 r/w s. 34, IPC, 1860 even though two other accused specifically named in charge had been acquitted -Appeal dismissed.
