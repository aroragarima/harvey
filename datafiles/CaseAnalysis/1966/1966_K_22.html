State of Assam v Kripanath Sarma and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1967 SC 459; 1967 (2) LLJ 576; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Assam Elementary Education Act, 1962, Assam General Clauses Act (2 of 1915)

Summary: Service - Assam Basic Education Act, 1954, s. 14(3) (iii) - Assam General Clauses Act, 1915, s. 18 - Service - Termination - Justifiability - Assam basic education Act, 1954 was repealed by Assam elementary education Act, 1962 under which School Board's functioning was abolished and replaced with Deputy Inspectors of Schools as Assistant Secretaries of State Board - Respondents service was terminated by Assistant Secretary who got powers u/s. 14(3)(iii) of the 1962 Act - Aggrieved respondents filed writ before HC challenging orders of termination, which held that termination was not valid - Hence, instant appeal - Whether termination of service of respondents by Assistant Secretary was maintainable - Held, Assistant Secretary had no such authority u/s. 14(3) (iii) of the 1962 Act r/w s. 18 of the 1915 Act - Therefore, orders issued for terminating services of respondents were invalid, for they were not orders of State Board terminating services of respondents - Hence, they must be held to be orders of Assistant Secretary who had no power to terminate services of respondents - Appeal dismissed.
