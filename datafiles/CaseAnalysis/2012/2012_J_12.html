Bhau Ram v Janak Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

20 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 215; (2012) 8 SCC 701; AIR 2012 SC 3023; 2012 (94) ALR 207; 2012 (5) ALT 29; 2012 (5) AWC 5067; 2012 (3) CLT(SC) 130; 2012 (4) CTC 675; 2012 (6) MahLJ 758; 2012 (6) MLJ(SC) 755; 2012 (4) MPLJ 481; 2013 (169) PLR 152; 2012 (4) RCR(Civil) 571; 2012 (117) RD 371; 2012 (4) RLW 3371; 2012(6) SCALE 530; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1018

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 r. 11, O. 9 rr. 8 and 9 and O. 23 rr. 1 (3) and 4 (b) - Rejection of plaint - Challenged - Trial Court allowed the application of the defendant filed under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC on the ground that the plaint was barred under the provisions of O. 9 rr. 8 and 9 of CPC and O. 23 rr. 1 (3) and 4 (b) of CPC - The said order of the trial Court was set aside by the First Appellate Court on the ground that the trial Court had taken the pleas from the written statement of the defendant which was not permissible under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC and the HC in the second appeal confirmed the judgment of the First Appellate Court - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the application under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC filed by the defendant can be decided merely on the basis of the plaint - Held, only the averments in the plaint can be looked into while deciding the application under O. 7 r. 11 of CPC - This aspect has been rightly dealt with by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
