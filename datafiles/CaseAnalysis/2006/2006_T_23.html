T. Vijayalakshmi and Others v Town Planning Member and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 696; (2006) 8 SCC 502; AIR 2007 SC 25; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 604; JT 2006 (9) SC 297; 2006(10) SCALE 455; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 534

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act

Summary: [A.] Municipalities & Local Governments - Karnataka Town and Country Planning Act, ss. 2(7), 3A-E - Application of appellant for grant of sanction of plan - Whether revised comprehensive development plan proposed by Bangalore Development Authority would be accepted by State or not? - Held, an application for grant of permission for construction of a building is required to be decided in accordance with law applicable on day on which such permission is granted - A statutory authority must exercise its jurisdiction within a reasonable time - First Respondent is directed to consider application for grant of sanction or approval of building plans submitted before it at an early date - Appeals allowed.
