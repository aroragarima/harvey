Â T. Venkata Reddy and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 490; (1985) 3 SCC 198; (1985) SCC (L&S) 632; AIR 1985 SC 724; 1986 LabIC 357; 1985 (2) LLN 236; 1985(1) SCALE 613; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 509; 1985 (1) SLJ 561; 1985 (2) SLR 82; 1985 UJ 991

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 123 and 213 - Andhra Pradesh Abolition of Posts of Part-time Village Officers Ordinance, 1984, ss. 3 and 4 - Ordinance - Constitutional validity - Posts of part-time village officers abolished - Whether ordinance is void and ineffective owing to lack of application of mind by Governor to subject matter of ordinance? - Held, since Constitution says that ordinance making power is legislative power, neither motives of legislature in passing a statute is in jurisdiction of courts nor can courts examine whether legislature had applied its mind to provisions of a statute before passing it - It does not violate art. 21 of Constitution - Life and personal liberty - Petition dismissed.
