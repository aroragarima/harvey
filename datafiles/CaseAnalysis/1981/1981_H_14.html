Hasmat Rai and Another v Raghunath Prasad

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.S. Pathak, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 514; (1981) 3 SCC 103; AIR 1981 SC 1711; 1982 (1) RCJ 499; 1981(1) SCALE 714; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 605; 1981 UJ 477

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution, art.136 - M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 12(1)(h), (e), (f) and 18 - CPC, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Decree of eviction of building comprising of two premises - Ground, personal business requirement - Landlord acquiring posssession of one of the premises while pendency of second appeal - Tenant contended it is sufficient for landlord's need - (A) Appeal by special leave - Finding of fact, given by ignoring fact of acquisition - Binding nature of - (B) Whether tenant can rely upon development while pendency of second appeal - (C) No pleadings as per s. 12(1)(e) - Eviction of tenant in another premises - Whether can be granted on ground of need for residence - (D) Amendment of plaint in second appeal sought by tenant in smaller premises - That landlord acquired possession of larger premises, sufficient for his purpose - Permissibility - (E) Suit for eviction for starting shop of Chemist and Druggist - Getting possession of one of the premises having 18' frontage and 90' depth plus portion at the already in possession of landlord' - Sufficiency - (F) Suit for eviction - For reconstruction - Landlord coming in possession of larger premises - Smaller premises, direction u/s.18, whether to be issued in regard - Held, not binding - Tenant can rely on that development - Held, cannot be granted on ground of need for residence - Amendment, held, could not be refused on ground that it was moved 3 1/2 years after acquisition of premises by landlord - Held, in absence of pleading as to extent of landlord's requirement, HC should have held premises in landlord's possession as sufficient for his need - If landlord proves that he is interested in rebuilding the smaller premises also, necessary directions u/s. 18 must be given - Held, not binding - Tenant can rely on that development - Appeal Allowed.
