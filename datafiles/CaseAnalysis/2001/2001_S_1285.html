Suresh and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, B.N. Agrawal, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20691; (2001) SCC (Cr) 601; (2001) 3 SCC 673; AIR 2001 SC 1344; AIR 2001 SCW 1051; 2001 (1) CCR 302; 2001 (2) Crimes 108; 2001 CRLJ 1462; JT 2001 (3) SC 336; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 78; 2001(2) SCALE 363; 2001 (2) SCJ 481; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 263; 2001 (2) SLT 435B; 2001 (2) Supreme 266; 2001 (1) UC 678

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 32, 333, 34, 35, 36, 37, 38, 149 and 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Whether accused who is sought to be convicted with aid of that s. 34, should have done some act, even assuming that he shared common intention with other accused? - To attract s. 34, IPC two postulates are indispensable (1) Criminal act (consisting of a series of acts) should have been done, not by one person, but more than one person (2) Doing of every such individual act cumulatively resulting in commission of criminal offence should have been in furtherance of common intention of all such persons - Held, if no overt act is done by a person, even if he has common intention with others for accomplishment of crime cannot be convicted with aid of s. 34 - It is difficult to conclude that a person, merely because he was present at or near scene, without doing anything more, without even carrying a weapon and without even marching along with other assailants, could also be convicted with aid of s. 34, IPC for offence committed by other accused - Appeal dismissed.
