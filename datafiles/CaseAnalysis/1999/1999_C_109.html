Collector of Customs, Bombay v United Electrical Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 114; (2000) 10 SCC 31; AIR 1999 SC 3796; 1999 (108) E.L.T. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs Act, 1962, s.25 - General clauses Act, 1897, s. 13 - Notification No. 118/80-Cus, entry 58 - 'Machine' referred in notification granting exemption - Interpretation of - Respondent importing three machines in two separate consignments - Entry 58 which reads automatic lead welding, lacquering, testing colour coating, sorting and packing machine for resistors - Held, must be interpreted as 'machines' - An assessee can not get exemption unless all machines are imported as one composite machine is misinterpretation.
