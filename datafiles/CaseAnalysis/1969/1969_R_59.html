Â Rashbihari Panda v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Mohammad Hidayatullah, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 255; (1969) 1 SCC 414; AIR 1969 SC 1081; 1969 (35) CutLT 479; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 374

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Summary: Trade - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 and 9 - Orissa Kendu Leaves (Control of Trade) Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(f) and (g) and 14 - Whether adoption of new scheme of offering to enter into advance purchase contracts by private negotiations for selling Kendu leaves by Govt. is violative of arts. 14 and 19(1)(g)? - Held, right to make offers being open to a limited class of persons, it effectively shut out all other persons carrying on trade in Kendu leaves and also new entrants into that business - Thus, both schemes evolved by Govt. were violative of fundamental right of petitioners u/arts. 19(1)(g) and 14 because schemes gave rise to a monopoly in trade in Kendu leaves to certain traders, and singled out other traders for discriminatory treatment - Tenders for purchase of Kendu leaves be invited by Govt. in next season from all persons interested in trade V.P.S Scheme declared invalid - Order accordingly.
