HVPNL v Mahavir

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 4112; (2004) 10 SCC 86

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer dispute - Award passed - Afresh proceeding - Consumer dispute arose between parties - Respondent/complainant had filed complaint before District Forum, which allowed complaint and passed order in favour of respondent - On appeal, State Commission held that order of District Forum does not find any legal infirmity and dismissed appeal, which was also confirmed by National Commission on second appeal - Hence instant appeal.

Held, at admission stage, Court passed order on 21-7-2000, Court may point out that while dealing with first appeal and this is not way to dispose of matter. Appellate forum is bound to refer to pleadings of case, submissions of counsel, necessary points for consideration, discuss evidence and dispose of matter by giving valid reasons. It is very easy to dispose of any appeal in this fashion and higher Courts would not know that State Commission had applied its mind to case. Court hopes that such orders will not be passed by State Commission in future. Copy of this order may be communicated to Commission. Issue notice for remand of matter to State Commission, for disposal afresh in accordance with law. Status quo, as of today, shall be maintained by the parties. State Commission did not give any reason for dismissing first appeal same order was confirmed by National Commission. Inasmuch as there was no discussion by State Commission in first appeal and for reasons given by Court in order which have passed on 21-7-2000, orders of National Commission and State Commission are set aside and matter is remanded to State Commission to dispose of case in accordance with law and in light of order passed by Court on 21-7-2000 after giving notice to parties. Appeal allowed.
