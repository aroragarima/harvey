Girdhar Shankar Tawade v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 272; (2002) 5 SCC 177; (2002) SCC (Cr) 971; AIR 2002 SC 2078; 2002 (2) CCR 177; 2002 (2) Crimes 360; 2002 (5) CRJ 732; 2002 CRLJ 2814; JT 2002 (4) SC 357; 2002 (4) MahLJ 5; 2002 (4) MPLJ 5; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 589; 2002(4) SCALE 44; 2002 (3) SCJ 512; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 376; 2002 (3) SLT 447; 2002 (3) Supreme 583; 2002 (2) UC 411

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Though, acquittal of a charge under Section 306, IPC is not by itself a ground for acquittal under Section 498-A, IPC but to maintain a charge of 'cruelty' under Section 498-A, willful act or conduct ought to be the proximate cause and there must be available on record some material and cogent evidence.
