Visvesvaraya Technological University v Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 303; AIR 2016 SC 2014; 2016 (4) AWC 3551; [2016] 384 ITR 37; JT 2016 (4) SC 267; 2016 (4) MLJ 149; 2016(4) SCALE 246; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 362; [2016] 239 TAXMAN 395

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Hotel, Educational Purposes, Right To Information Act, 2005, Income-tax Rules, 1962, Income-tax Act, 1961, Vishwaraiah Techonological University Act, 1994

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 148, 10(23C)(iiiab) - A/ys 2004-2005 to 2009-2010 - Profit motive - Grants from Govt. - Notices u/s. 148 of the Act were issued to appellant-University for A/Ys 2004-2005 to 2009-2010 and later, appellant-University filed returns for said A/Ys declaring 'Nil' income and claimed exemption u/s. 10(23C)(iiiab) of the Act - However, AO refused claim of exemption and made assessments and the same was upheld by all authorities under the Act and also by HC by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant-University is entitled to claim exemption from payment of tax u/s.10(23C)(iiiab) of the Act.

Held, even in situation where direct Govt. grants were not forthcoming and allocation against permissible heads like salary, etc. had not been made, University thrived and prospered and even surplus accumulated over the years was ploughed back for educational purposes. Hence, condition that appellant-University exists 'solely for educational purposes and not for purposes of profit' is satisfied. However, appellant-University is neither directly nor even substantially financed by Govt. Hence, appellant-University had not satisfied second requirement of s.10(23C)(iiiab) of the Act and so, not entitled for exemption under said section. Hence, impugned judgment of HC and orders of authorities under the Act are upheld and appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Exemption u/s.10(23C)(iiiab) could be granted subject to conditions, firstly, the educational institution or the university has to be solely for the purpose of education and without any profit motive and secondly, it has to be wholly or substantially financed by Govt.
