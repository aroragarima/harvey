S. Noordeen v S. Thiru Venkita Reddiar And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 776; (1996) 3 SCC 289; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 301; AIR 1996 SC 1293; 1996 (1) CCC 277; 1996 (1) CLT 516; JT 1996 (2) SC 447; 1996 (1) KLT 761; 1996 (2) MLJ 7; 1996 (2) MLJ(SC) 7; 1996(2) SCALE 323; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 261; 1998 (Supp) RD 106; 1996 (2) Supreme 395

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attachment Of Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Registration Act, 1908, s.17(2) - CPC, 1908, O. 38, r. 8, O. 21, r. 58 - Whether compromise decree is compulsorily registrable - Adjudication of claim to properties which were attached before judgment was passed - Such proceedings are civil proceedings such properties become part of decree relating to subject matter of suit - Compromise decree with exception u/cl.(4) of s. 17(2) of the Act - Decree held, is not required to be compulsorily registered - Appeal dismissed.
