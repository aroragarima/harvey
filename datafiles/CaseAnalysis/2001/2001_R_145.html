Union of India and Others v Rakesh Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20210; (2001) 4 SCC 309; (2001) SCC (L&S) 707; AIR 2001 SC 1877; 2001 (2) CLR 253; 2001 (89) FLR 599; JT 2001 (4) SC 306; 2001 (34) LabIC 1406; 2001(3) SCALE 150; 2001 (3) SCJ 25; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 927; 2001 (2) SLR 261; 2001 (4) SLT 36; 2001 (3) Supreme 48; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1163

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Enrolled, civil services regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Border Security Force Act, 1968 - Civil Service Regulations - Border Security Force Rules, 1969, r. 19 - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972, r. 48 A - Whether members of BSF who have resigned from their posts after serving for ten or more years but less than 20 years are entitled to pension/pensionary benefits under relevant provisions of Border Security Force Act, 1968? - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules nowhere provide that a person who has resigned before completing 20 years of service as provided in r. 48 - A is entitled to pensionary benefits - R. 19 of BSF Rules also does not make any provision for grant of pensionary benefits - It only provides that if a member of Force who resigns and to whom permission in writing is granted to resign then authority granting such permission may reduce pensionary benefits if he is eligible to get pension - Held, respondents, who were permitted to resign from service u/r. 19 of BSF Rules before attainment of age of retirement are not entitled to get any pension under any of provisions under Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules and r. 49 only prescribes procedure for calculation and quantification of pension amount - GO D/- 27-12-1995 does not confer any additional right of pension on BSF employees - Appeals allowed.
