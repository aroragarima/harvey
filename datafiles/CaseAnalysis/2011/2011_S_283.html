State Of Rajasthan v Talevar & Anr

Supreme Court of India

17 June 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 388; (2011) 11 SCC 666; AIR 2011 SC 2271; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2340; 2011 CRLJ 3937; JT 2011 (7) SC 1; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 771; 2011 (3) RLW 2133; 2011(6) SCALE 575; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 1050

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 395, 396, 397, 120B and 412 IPC - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3/25 and 3/27 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Dacoity - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court convicted all the accused u/ss. 395, 396 and 397 of IPC and awarded them punishment to undergo life imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/- - u/ss. 395 and 396 of IPC and rigorous imprisonment for 7 years and a fine of Rs.500/- u/s. 397 of IPC - Trial court also convicted accused nos. 1, 2, and 3 u/ss. 3/25 and 3/27 of the Arms Act and awarded sentence to undergo 3 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 500/- - On appeal, HC acquitted the accused nos. 4 and 5 though maintained the conviction and sentence in respect of other accused - State filed appeal against said acquittal of accused no. 4 and 5 submitting that the recovery of some of the looted property had been made on the basis of the disclosure statements made by the accused nos. 4 and 5 - Whether adverse inference could be drawn against the accused nos. 4 and 5 merely on the basis of recoveries made on their disclosure statements? - Held, where only evidence against the accused was recovery of stolen properties, then although the circumstances may indicate that the theft and murder might have been committed at the same time, it was not safe to draw an inference that the person in possession of the stolen property had committed the murder - In the instant case, accused no. 4 was arrested on 24.12.1996 and looted property were alleged to have been recovered on his disclosure statement on 29.12.1996 - Similarly, accused no. 5 was arrested on 19.1.1997 and looted property were alleged to have been recovered on his disclosure statement on 26.01.1997 - Thus, it was evident that recovery on the disclosure statements of either of the accused persons was not in close proximity of time from the date of incident - More so, recovery was either of cash, small things or vehicles which can be passed from one person to another without any difficulty - Hence, no presumption can be drawn against the accused nos. 4 and 5 u/s. 114 of Evidence Act - No adverse inference can be drawn against accused no. 4 and 5 on the basis of recoveries made on their disclosure statements to connect them with the commission of the crime - Appeal dismissed.
