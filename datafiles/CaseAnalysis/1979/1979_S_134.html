Mundrika Prasad Sinha v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 186; (1979) 4 SCC 701; AIR 1979 SC 1871; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 759

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 and 226 - Govt. Pleader, authorised to represent Govt. in all civil cases, resisted to appointment of Assistant Govt. Pleaders by Govt. - Writ Petition dismissed by High Court - Question raised that such resistance from the part of Govt. is peril to public office of Govt. pleadership, with potential menace to administration of justice - Held, office is a public office is irresistible and ordering about a Govt. Pleader is obnoxious, but nothing savoring of such conduct is made out, although enter a caveat that Govt. under Constitution shall not play with Law Offices on political or other impertinent considerations as it may affect legality of action and subvert rule of law itself - Petition dismissed.
