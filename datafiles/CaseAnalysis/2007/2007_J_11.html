State Of Rajasthan And Others v Jagdish Chopra

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 866; (2007) 8 SCC 161; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 837; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 830; 2007 (115) FLR 195; JT 2007 (10) SC 534; 2007(10) SCALE 470; 2007 (6) SCJ 831; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 599; 2007 (5) SLR 809

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Rajasthan Education Subordinate Services Rules, 1971 - Service - Post of Teacher (Physical Education) - Writ petition by respondent - On the premise that the validity of the merit list had expired, Single Judge opined that he had no legal right to be appointed - Division Bench revered the order - Appeal against - Held, vacancies which arose in the subsequent years could be filled up from the select list prepared in the previous year and not in other manner - Even otherwise, in absence of any rule, ordinary period of validity of select list should be one year - Single Judge of the High Court was, therefore, correct in holding that the second respondent has no legal right to be appointed - Even selected candidates do not have legal right in this behalf - Appointment of the respondent may be directed to be continued as if he was appointed on and from the date he joined the service - Appeal partly allowed.
