New Jehangir Vakil Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North, Kutch and Saurashtra

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Sudhi Ranjan Das

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 170; AIR 1959 SC 1177; [1959] 37 ITR 11; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 249

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(1)(a), 66(1), 66(2), 66(4) - Non-resident Income - Question of Law - Supplementary statement of case - Remanding case - Appellant company manufacturing textile goods and treated as a non resident during calendar years 1942 and 1943 - Income-tax Officer computed British' Indian Income of appellant on a proportionate basis u/s. 4(1)(a) of the Act for the a/ys 1943-44 and 1944-45 - Appellant appealed before Tribunal which favored them and on appeal HC called for a supplement statement - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC had jurisdiction u/s. 66(4) of the Act to call for a supplemental statement of case - Held, question of law which assessee or Commissioner can require Tribunal to refer to HC was any question of law arising out of order of Tribunal so that if question of law which assessee or Commissioner requires Tribunal to so refer to HC did not arise out of its order Tribunal was not bound to refer same - HC had no jurisdiction u/s. 66(4) of the Act to direct Tribunal to submit a supplementary statement of case - Hence, matter remanded to HC to arrive at its decision on question of law referred to it in statement of case already submitted to it by Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
