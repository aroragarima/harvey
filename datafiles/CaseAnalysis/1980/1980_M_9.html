Kona Prabhakara Rao and Another v M. Seshagiri Rao and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 495; (1982) 1 SCC 442; AIR 1981 SC 658; 1980 UJ 715

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under

Summary: Election - Office of profit - Re-counting - Civil Appeal had been filed by appellant who was a candidate in elections and had been successfully elected to the Legislative Assembly of the state - Respondent who was the election petitioner and also one of the candidates who lost the election, filed an election petition before HC challenging the election of appellant - HC after taking the evidence and hearing the parties found that appellant indulged in corrupt practice and appellant at the time of filing his nomination papers was holding an office of profit under the Govt. and was disqualified from fighting the election - HC however did not go into the question of re-counting - Respondent accordingly filed appeal against that part of the judgment of HC by which his prayer for re-count and a declaration that he be declared duly elected was disallowed - Hence, the present appeal - Whether recounting would be allowed - Held, the counsel for both the parties had expressed their join consent that in view of the allegations made by appellant, the re-counting may be done in the Court instead of remitting the matter to HC which will involve lot of delay - Parties had further agreed that the report of the Registrar regarding the result of the re-count shall be binding on the parties without any further argument and the appeal should be decided in accordance with the order passed by this Court on the basis of the report of the Registrar - The Registrar [Judicial] would exercise all powers of the Returning Officer in re-counting - Appeal allowed.
