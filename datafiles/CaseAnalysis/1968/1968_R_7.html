Union Of India v Radha Kissen Agarwalla & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 162; (1969) 1 SCC 225; AIR 1969 SC 762; [1970] 40 Comp Cas 182; 1969 (19) FLR 67; 1969 LabIC 1146; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 28; 1969 SLR 439

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Provident Fund Sterling Accounts Rules, Provident Funds Act 1926

Summary: Provident Funds Act, 1925, s. 3 - Provident Fund Sterling Accounts Rules - Contract Act, 1872, s. 50 - CPC, 1908, s. 60(1)(k), O. 21, rr. 52 and 58 - Provident Funds - Subscriber to Railway Provident Fund electing to be governed by Provident Fund Sterling Accounts Rules - He requested payment in sterling through draft in a bank in England - Railway drawing cheques in favour of RBI with instructions to convert it into sterling and then to transmit amount to subscriber's banker in England - Attachment of cheques lying with RBI in execution of money decree - Validity of attachment challenged - Held attachment was contrary to terms of s. 3 of 1925 Act - Because obligation of railway administration could not be said to have been discharged till directions of subscriber were complied - RBI agent of Railway and not of subscriber so as long as money remained under control of Railway as PF, was exempt from attachment - Further held that Union of India, being trustee for subscriber had interest to maintain application for removal of attachment - Appeal Allowed.
