Ajit Kumar Palit v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 246; AIR 1963 SC 765; 1963 CRLJ 797; 1964 (1) SCJ 75; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 953

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure -West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1949, ss. 2 and 5 , Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 190, 191, 192, 193 and 194 - West Bengal Act, 1960 - Powers of Judge of Special court - Cognizance of offence - (A) Whether there is class or character of material that must be before a special judge before he can assume and exercise jurisdiction over a case? - Held, u/ss. 190, 191, 192 and 194 of CrPC there is no statutory requirement under CrPC to class or character of material that must be before a special judge before he can assume and exercise jurisdiction over a case - (B) Whether judge of a Special Court is u/s. 2 of Act of 1949 has to comply with requirements of CrPC u/s. 190? - Further held, judge of Special Court appointed u/s. 2 of Act of 1949 is not within class of Magistrates designated by s. 190(1) and hence there can be no question of such a judge having to comply with its requirements before he can 'take cognizance of an offence' - (C) Whether special judge can be deprived of jurisdiction by virtue of Amending act of 1949? - No express words in amending Act which made it retrospective or retroactive to operate from commencement of Act - Further held, special judge having validly acquired jurisdiction to proceed with trial of case allotted to him, there was nothing in amending Act to deprive him of that jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
