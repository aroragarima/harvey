State Of W.B. v Terra Firma Investment And Trading Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 557; (1995) 1 SCC 125; AIR 1995 SCW 1694; JT 1994 (7) SC 524; 1994(4) SCALE 1002; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 485

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Constitution - Calcutta Municipal Corporation Act 1980 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Urbanisation - Building plan - Applications for proposed construction of buildings in accordance to the new building rules were to be sanctioned - State Govt. had formed an Expert Committee to frame new rules to cope with the problems of urbanisation accentuating traffic congestion, lack of facilities for sewerage and sanitation, dearth of open space, etc. - Proceedings were initiated in the HC and exparte orders of injunction were granted restraining the State Govt. from publishing the new rules - State Govt. imposed one year ban on the submission of building plans for high-rise construction and new s. 398-A was introduced into the Calcutta Corporation (Amendment) Ordinance, 1989 - W.P. of respondent challenging validity of the new section was infructuous and was dismissed - On appeal by the respondents, directions were given by the HC to the Corporation to dispose of all application for sanction as per the existing building rules and the Amending Act was held to be unconstitutional - State aggrieved - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, the impugned order of the HC was held to be unreasonable and irrational to be violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, that no malice could be imputed to the legislature - Mere provision of imposing a ban on passing plans of buildings exceeding 13.5 metres in height for a period of one year could not be held violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - Respondent was not entitled to claim an absolute or vested right to get his plan passed by writ of a court - HC had erred by not examining as to what right the respondent had acquired by submission of the plan for construction of the high rise building before its application was rejected by a statutory provision - Appeals allowed.
