Ranchhoddas Chhaganlal v Devaji Supadu Dorik And Ors

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 144; (1977) 3 SCC 584; AIR 1977 SC 1517; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 621

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 39, 55 - Suit for specific performance - Part performance - Legality - Appellant (plaintiff) and respondent (defendants) entered into an agreement of sale in respect of sale of agricultural land of appellant - Respondents allegedly failed to perform their part of contract - Appellant filed a suit for specific performance of agreement of sale and for possession of land in question against respondents - Trial Court by its judgment decreed suit - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before HC - HC by impugned judgment reversed the judgment of Trial Court - Respondents contended that appellant was not competent to maintain suit by reason of provisions contained in ss. 39 and 55 of the Act and that appellant could not put an end to the contract if there was failure on part of respondent to perform the agreement - Whether respondent was ready and willing to perform the agreement entered into with appellant - Held, case of appellant was always that respondent refused to perform the agreement - Appellant all along asserted that agreement was that the property was agreed to be sold only for a sum of Rs.17000/- - Respondent refused to perform the agreement - Suit thus was competent and valid -Appellant was right in contention that there was never any performance in part by respondent of the contract between the parties - In instant case, respondent who was the transferee under the agreement did not perform his part of contract from 1952 till 1963 that was after the judgment passed by Trial Court - HC wrongly found that there was an extension of performance of contract by 1 year - It was well settled that there should be specific issues on-questions of fact - Parties did' not go to trial on that question and there the HC was in error in holding that there was an extension of time for performance of contract - It was thus erroneous to say as HC did that respondent could take advantage of period between 1953-54 - HC should not have reversed decree particularly when it was found that respondents failed first in regard to agreement alleged by respondents and second in allowing decree in favour of respondents on the plea of part performance of a contract which was never pleaded by respondents and was not a contract upon which there could be any performance in part - Impugned judgment passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
