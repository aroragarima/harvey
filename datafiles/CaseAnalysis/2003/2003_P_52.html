Narendra Nath Khaware v Parasnath Khaware and others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 372; (2003) 5 SCC 488; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1144; AIR 2003 SC 2325; 2003 (2) CCR 95; 2003 (2) Crimes 516; 2003 (5) CRJ 164; 2003 CRLJ 2340; 2003 (2) JCC 901; JT 2003 (4) SC 430; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 354; 2003(4) SCALE 128; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 683; 2003 (3) SLT 282; 2003 (3) Supreme 501

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: IPC - ss. 148, 302 r/w 149 - held, the High Court disposed of the appeal in a very casual and cavalier manner. The High Court being the Court of first appeal, was required to consider and re-appreciate the evidence on record. None of the three grounds mentioned by the High Court in its impugned judgment are really determinative of the fate of the appeal. - So long as the statute provides a right of appeal, the court will be failing in its duty if the appeal is disposed of in such a casual and cavalier manner as the High Court has done in the present case. - A fit case for remand so that the High Court can go into the evidence on record in detail and come to definite finding on the facts in issue.
