Balasubramaniam v State By Si Police, T. N. and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21005; (2002) 7 SCC 649; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1812; 2003 (2) CCR 213; 2002(8) SCALE 334; 2003 (4) SLT 148

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 195(1)(b)(ii) and 340(1) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 467, 471, 475 and 476 - Whether bar u/s. 195(1)(b)(ii) of CrPC would apply as against a private complaint for an offence u/ss. 467 and 471, IPC even in respect of a document which was forged before it was filed in court? - Main postulates for operation of bar First is, there must be allegation that an offence (it should be either an offence described in s. 463 or any other offence punishable u/ss. 471, 475 and 476, IPC has been committed; Second is that such offence should have been committed in respect of a document produced or given in evidence in a proceeding in any court - Held, offence should have been committed during time when document was in custodia legis - There must be allegation that an offence has been committed - If forgery has been committed while document was in custody of a court, then prosecution can be launched only with a complaint made by that court - Referred to larger bench - Order accordingly.
