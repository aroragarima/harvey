State Bank of India v Yasangi Venkateswara Rao

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 929; (1999) 2 SCC 375; AIR 1999 SC 896; 1999 (1) ARBLR 366; 2001 (1) BC 87; 1999 (1) CLT 105; [1999] 95 Comp Cas 805; JT 1999 (1) SC 145; 1999 (1) RAJ 383; 1999(1) SCALE 131; 1999 (1) SCJ 337; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 213; 1999 (1) SLT 284; 1999 (1) Supreme 196

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Banking Laws (Amendment) Act, 1984

Summary: Banking Regulation Act, 1949, s. 21 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245, sch. 7, list 1, entry 45 - Bank loan - Interest - Amount advanced against mortgage - Charging compound interest - Transaction not to be reopened by Court on account of excessive interest - S. 21 A enacting prohibition is within domain of Parliament - Mortgaging of a property is with a view to secure loan and has no relation whatsoever with quantum of interest to charged - Held, cannot be said to be excessive.
