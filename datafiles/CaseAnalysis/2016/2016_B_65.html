Madras Petrochem Limited and another v Bifr and others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1070; 2016 (2) CTC 780

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: abatement, amalgamation, appointed day, Arbitration, auction, audit, bond, borrower, commercial transaction, Constitution of India, contract, debentures, derogation, entertain and decide, Financial Institutions, Foreign Exchange, guarantor, ICICI, immovable property, in addition to, Industrial Reconstruction Bank of India, insolvency, Jurisdiction, Liquidator, loans and advances, margin, pledge, public sector banks, receiver, Reserve Bank of India, secured debt, Securities, shall include, shareholders, State Bank of India, State Financial Corporations, status quo, the Reserve Bank of India, tribunal, Unit Trust of India, uti, Without prejudice

Summary: (A) Banking & Finance â Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 â Recovery of Debts Due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act, 1993 â Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 â Secured creditors â Recovery of debt from Sick company â Appellant no.1-company filed reference u/s.15(1) of 1985 Act before BIFR and as such, after making inquiry under 1985 Act, it was declared sick and ICICI was appointed as Operating Agency â However, on failure of first and second rehabilitation scheme prepared by Operating Agency, on 30-4-2001, BIFR on basis of recommendation of Operating Agency, formed prima facie opinion for winding up of appellant no.1-company u/s.20(1) of 1985 Act and later, on 27-7-2001, BIFR confirmed said opinion after noting that appellant no.1-company had been enjoying protection under 1985 Act for last 12 years â As such, BIFR recommended to HC of Bombay for winding up of appellant no.1-company â On 4-2-2002, AAIFR dismissed appeal of appellant no.1-company challenging orders of BIFR â Later, appellants nos. 1 and 2 filed writ petition before HC of Delhi challenging orders of AAIFR and BIFR and vide impugned judgment, petition was dismissed â Based on winding up proceeding initiated by BHEL, an unsecured creditor of company and another winding up based on opinion of BIFR, HC of Bombay wound up appellant no.1-company â Hence, instant appeals â Whether 2002 Act prevails over 1985 Act.

Held, after the orders of BIFR and AAIFR were upheld by dismissal of WP by Delhi HC by impugned judgment, there could be no revival of reference proceedings before BIFR. Further, as 1985 Act overrides the provisions of 1993 Act, s.22 of 1985 Act would continue to apply in the case of unsecured creditors seeking to recover their debts from a sick industrial company. Further, where a secured creditor of a sick industrial company seeks to recover its debt as per s.13(2) of 2002 Act, then such secured creditor could realize the same u/s.13(4) of 2002 Act notwithstanding the provisions of s.22 of 1985 Act. Further, in case there are more than one secured creditor of sick industrial company or it has been jointly financed by secured creditors and at least 60% of such secured creditors in value of the amount outstanding as on a record date do not agree upon exercise of the right to realize their security under 2002 Act, then s.22 of 1985 Act would apply. However, if in case they agree to exercise their right then, 2002 Act would apply and s.22 of 1985 Act being inconsistent with the exercise of such right, would have no play. Further, reference u/s.15(1) of appellant no.1-company had abated as more than 3/4th of secured creditors involved have taken steps u/s.13(4) of 2002 Act. Appeals dismissed.

(B) Banking & Finance â Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 â Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 â Interpretation of âreference is pendingâ â Whether the expression âwhere a reference is pendingâ in s.15(1) proviso 3 of 1985 Act includes all proceedings before the BIFR or only proceedings at the initial reference stage.

Held, where secured creditors representing not less than 75% in value of the amount outstanding against financial assistance decide to enforce their security under 2002 Act, any reference pending under 1985 Act could not be proceeded with further - the proceedings under 1985 Act would abate. The expression âwhere a reference is pendingâ would necessarily include the inquiry stage before the Board u/s.16 of the Act. The reference could be said to be pending not only when an inquiry is instituted, but also after preparation and sanction of a scheme right till the stage the scheme has worked out successfully or till the BIFR gives its opinion to wind up the company. S.15(1) proviso 3 covers all references pending before the BIFR, no matter whether such reference is at the inquiry stage, scheme stage, or winding up stage. The Orissa HC is not correct in its conclusion on the interpretation of s.15(1) proviso 3 of 1985 Act. Appeals dismissed.
