Bramchari Sindheswar Shai and Others v State of W. B. and Others, Responednts

Supreme Court of India

02 July 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1526; (1995) 4 SCC 646; AIR 1995 SC 2089; JT 1995 (5) SC 205; 1995(4) SCALE 113; [1995] Supp1 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Tort

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 26, 26(a), 30(1) and 226 - Madras Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Act, 1951 - Ramakrishna Mission - (A) Whether entitled to protection u/art. 30(1) as a minority religion - (B) Religious denomination - Rights of - (1) Whether persons belonging to or owing their allegiance to mission are entitled to claim fundamental rights u/art. 26? - (2) Whether education institutions established and maintained by them are entitled to protection of art. 26(a)? - (C) Mandamus - Governing body of College established, managed by it and built on its own land - Conceding to request made by State Govt. - Held, (A) Not entitled as it should not distinct and separate from Hindu religion and not a minority religion - (B) Belong to religious denomination and entitled to claim fundamental rights u/art. 26 - Question left open to be decided in proper case - (C) College can be allowed to be administered by Ramakrishna Mission through its own Governing Body, as interests of Justice may suffer by directing State Government to constitute governing body on standard pattern of usual sponsored colleges - Appeal dismissed.
