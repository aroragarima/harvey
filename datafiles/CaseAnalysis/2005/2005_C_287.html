Sai Chalchitra v Commissioner, Meerut Mandal and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 779; (2005) 3 SCC 683; 2005 (5) Bom.C.R. 452; 2005 (2) MahLJ 900; 2005 (3) MPLJ 14

Case Digest

Subject: Media & Entertainment

Keywords: Legality, Cancellation of license

Summary: Media & Entertainment - U.P. Regulation of Cinema Act, 1955 - U.P. Cinema (Regulation of Exhibition by Means of Videos) Rules, 1988 - Cancellation of license - Legality - District Magistrate cancelled the licence given to respondent no 3 to run a video parlour - On appeal Commissioner set aside the order of District Magistrate - On appeal before HC, HC dismissed the petition - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC erred in dismissing the writ petition filed by the appellant on the ground of locus standi. The appellant being in the same trade as respondent no. 3 has a right to seek the cancellation of the licence granted to respondent no. 3 being in violation of the Act and the Rules. Case is remitted back to the HC for a fresh decision. Appeal allowed.
