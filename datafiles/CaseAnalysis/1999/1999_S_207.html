Arvind Alias Pappu v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 417; (1999) 4 SCC 486; (1999) SCC (Cr) 589; 1999 (3) CCR 75; 1999 (3) Crimes 74; 1999 (80) DLT 307; 1999 (1) JCC 283; JT 1999 (3) SC 554; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 810; 1999(4) SCALE 44; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 1217; 1999 (5) SLT 268; 1999 (5) Supreme 380

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Appellant accused of murder - Convicted - Appeal dismissed by High Court - Standard of proof - Circumstances from which conclusion of guilt is to be drawn have not only to be fully established but also that all circumstances so established should be of a conclusive nature and consistent only with hypothesis of guilt of accused and should not be capable of being explained by any other hypothesis, except guilt of accused and when all circumstances cumulatively taken together should lead to only irresistable conclusion that accused alone is the perpetrator of crime - Held, on facts and circumstances of case, presence of these witnesses at place of occurrence at relevant time was natural - They had no axe to grind against appellant - No reason why they should give false evidence against him - From evidence circumstances pointing to involvement of appellant in killing of deceased been established - Further appellant was untraced from day following incident for about one and a half years - Circumstances taken cumulatively point to only hypothesis of guilt of appellant - No material on record pointing towards his innocence - Appeal dismissed.
