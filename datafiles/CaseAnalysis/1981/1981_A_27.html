State of Punjab and Others v Ajudhia Nath and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 404; (1981) 3 SCC 251; AIR 1981 SC 1374; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 686; 1981 UJ 454

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Excise - Constitution, art. 226 - Punjab Excise Act, 1914, ss. 3(6-b), 31, 34 and 59(d) - Audi alterm partem - Licence to sell liquor - Failed to deposit the still head duty - Made applications claiming relief - Applications decided without affording to him an opportunity of being heard - Held, that the principle of natural justice does not come into play when the demand is merely for payment of a sum becoming due under the conditions subject to which the licence was granted - Appeals allowed.
