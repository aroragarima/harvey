State of Himachal Pradesh v Karanvir

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 238; (2006) 5 SCC 381; AIR 2006 SC 2211; 2006 CRLJ 2917; 2006 (2) JCC 946; JT 2006 (5) SC 479; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 379; 2006(5) SCALE 654; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 666

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 405, 409 - CrPC, 1973, s. 313 IPC, s 409 - Chief Judicial Magistrate sentenced respondent to undergo simple imprisonment for a period of six months and to pay a fine of Rs. 1, 000/ - Sessions Judge confirmed conviction and sentence - HC held that judgment of conviction and sentence was unsustainable - Whether having regard to facts and circumstances of this case, prosecution has been able to prove that respondent misappropriated the amount? - Held, actual manner of misappropriation is not required to be proved by prosecution - Once entrustment is proved, it was for accused to prove as to how property entrusted to him was dealt with in view of s 405 - Trial Judge and Sessions Judge arrived at concurrent findings of fact - Substantial punishment not awarded - Imposed a fine of Rs. 4, 000/- - Appeal allowed.
