Om Prakash Sharma v Ramesh Chand Prashar and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 362; AIR 2016 SC 2570; 2017 (1) CalLT(SC) 93; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 121; JT 2016 (5) SC 123; 2016(5) SCALE 566; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 35

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Essential conditions of bidding - Respondent no.1 filed writ petition before HC assailing issuance of Letter of Intent and consequent execution of sale deed in favour of appellant on ground that appellant had not submitted his annual turnover and net worth for last three years as stipulated in advertisement during bidding process - Single Judge of HC allowed petition observing inter alia that condition regarding submission of net worth was mandatory and that Committee could not have over-looked such condition and quashed Letter of Intent and Sale Deed and directed authorities to re-do entire process of selling Cafe in accordance with law - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal before DB of HC but DB of HC upheld view of Single Judge and dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether requirement to furnish Annual Turnover and Net Worth for last three years was a mandatory condition.

Held, the site in question was to be sold on outright sale basis. The advertisement or the stipulations therein did not contemplate creation or continuation of any relationship between the parties calling for continued existence of any particular level of financial parameters on part of bidder, except the ability to pay the price as per his bid. The condition was not an essential condition at all but was merely ancillary to achieve the main object that was to ensure that the bid amount was paid promptly. Further, appellant paid entire bid amount within prescribed period and Sale Deed was also executed in his favour. Poddar Steel Corporation v. Ganesh Engineering Works and Others, 1991 INDLAW SC 1002 relied on. Under such circumstances, authorities could validly deviate from and not insist upon strict literal compliance. Hence, discretion so exercised by authorities could not have been faulted and so, impugned Letter of Intent and Sale Deed are valid. Appeal is allowed and impugned orders of HC are set aside. Appeal disposed of.
