Food Corporation Of India & Anr v Seil Ltd. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 155; (2008) 3 SCC 440; AIR 2008 SC 1101; 2008 (5) AWC 4375; JT 2008 (2) SC 54; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 558; 2008(1) SCALE 437; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 663

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Socio-Economic

Summary: Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Alleged shortages in supply of sugar during the period 1983 to 1995 - Held, appellant could not have withheld payment on the basis of the purported shortages in supply of sugar under the contracts made by the respondents many many years back, save and except under the terms of binding contract - Having regard to the fact that respondents did not prefer any appeal increase in the rate of interest, as has been directed by the Division Bench, cannot be upheld - Appellant would pay the amount in question with interest as awarded by the learned Single Judge of the High Court - Appeals dismissed.
