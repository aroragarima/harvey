Milmet Oftho Industries and Others v Allergan Inc

Supreme Court of India

07 May 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 363; (2004) 12 SCC 624; AIR 2004 SC 3355; 2005 (1) CalLT(SC) 1; 5 CLC 884; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 486; 2004 (170) E.L.T. 260; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 175; JT 2004 (5) SC 477; 2004 (28) PTC 585; 2004(5) SCALE 772; [2004] Supp2 S.C.R. 586

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: [A.] Intellectual Property - Suit for an injunction based on an action for passing off in respect of mark 'OCUFLOX' used on a medicinal preparation manufactured and marketed by Respondents - Court has to keep in mind possibility that with passage of time, some conflict may occur between use of mark by Applicant in India and user by overseas company - If a mark in respect of a drug is associated with Respondents worldwide it would lead to an anomalous situation if an identical mark in respect of a similar drug is allowed to be sold in India - Ultimate test should be who is first in market - These are matters which would require examination on evidence - Trial Court is requested to dispose of suit.
