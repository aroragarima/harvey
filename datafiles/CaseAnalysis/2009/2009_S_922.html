Bhairon Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 688; (2009) 13 SCC 80; AIR 2009 SC 2603; 2009 CRLJ 3738; 2009 (3) JCC 2114; JT 2009 (7) SC 495; 2010 (5) RCR(Criminal) 48; 2009(8) SCALE 634; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 875; 2009 (2) UC 1124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bystanders

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 304B and 306 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 32(1) and 6 - Admissibility of evidence u/s. 32(1) Evidence Act - Cases in which statement of relevant fact by person who is dead or cannot be found, etc. is relevant - Subjecting cruelty to woman by husband or relatives of husband - In a case where accused has been acquitted of the offence punishable u/s. 304-B and 306 IPC, and the death of wife is neither homicidal nor suicidal but accidental, whether the oral evidence of witnesses about what the deceased had told them against the accused about the treatment meted out to her is admissible u/s. 32 (1) of the Evidence Act to sustain conviction under Section 498A, IPC? - Held, No - Provision of s. 32(1) Evidence Act, perused; position of law settled in 'Sharad Birdhichand Sarda vs. State of Maharashtra, (1984) 4 SCC 116' relied upon - 'Statements, written or verbal, of relevant facts made by a person who is dead,...... are themselves relevant facts in the following cases:- (1) ....when the statement is made by a person as to the cause of his death, or as to any of the circumstances of the transaction which resulted in his death, in cases in which the cause of that person's death comes into question.....', s. 32(1) - Death of deceased wife on 09-10-1990, marriage took place 10 years before death; dead body found in a well and cause of death was asphyxia due to drowning - Demand of dowry and subjecting wife to torture and harassment was coming out from the only testimonies of two brothers of deceased, PW4 and PW5 - Accused charged u/ss. 304B, 306, 498A of IPC and s. 3 of Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Trial Court convicted accused u/s. 498A IPC and u/s. 3 DPA but acquitted u/ss, 304B and 306 IPC - Conviction was based on testimonies of PW4 and PW5 - High Court maintained conviction u/s. 498A - Apart from the statement attributed to the deceased, none of the witnesses had spoken anything which they had seen directly insofar as torture and harassment to deceased was concerned - Evidence of PW4 and PW5 is inadmissible u/s. 32(1) Evidence Act and such evidence cannot be looked into for any purpose; Court observed that except s. 32(1) of the Evidence Act, there is no other provision under which the statement of a dead person can be looked into in evidence; what has been deposed by PW4 and PW5 has no connection with any circumstance of the transactions which resulted in her death, in a case in which the cause of death comes into question - Death of deceased was neither homicidal nor suicidal, it was accidental; since for an offence u/s. 498A simpliciter, the question of death is not and cannot be an issue for consideration, evidence of PW4 and PW5 is hardly an evidence in law to establish offence thereunder - S. 32(1) held not attracted - 'Inder Pal vs. State of MP, (2001) 10 SCC 736' - Conviction and sentence set aside - Appeal allowed.
