Uka Ram v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20178; (2001) 5 SCC 254; (2001) SCC (Cr) 847; AIR 2001 SC 1814; AIR 2001 SCW 1478; 2001 (2) Crimes 188; 2001 CRLJ 1821; JT 2001 (4) SC 472; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 416; 2001(3) SCALE 251; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 1052; 2001 (3) SLT 396; 2001 (3) Supreme 238

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 326 and 498A - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Appellant convicted for murder of his daughter and wife by burning them - Present appeal - Dying declaration made by deceased before police and magistrate - Alleged that deceased was suffering from mental illness - Prosecution is under a legal obligation to prove its case beyond all reasonable doubt and accused is only to probabilise his defence - Held, prosecution had not proved, beyond doubt, that dying declaration was true, voluntary and not influenced by any extraneous consideration - Despite knowing fact that deceased was a mental patient, investigating agency did not take any precaution to ensure whether incident was suicidal or homicidal - Probability of deceased committing suicide has not been eliminated - Doubt about mental condition of deceased at time she made dying declaration - It is a fit case in which appellant is entitled to benefit of doubt - As dying declaration, sole evidence upon which conviction is based, is not reliable beyond all reasonable doubt, conviction and sentence of appellant is not justified - Appellant acquitted - Appeal allowed.
