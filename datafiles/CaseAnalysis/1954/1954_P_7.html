Purushottam Jethanand v State of Kutch

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1954 SC 700; 1954 CRLJ 1751

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 162, 190(2) and 529 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 384 - Robbery - Conviction confirmed by Sessions Judge on appeal and upheld by Judicial Commr. on revision - Trial Magistrate had no power to take cognizance, whether proceedings void? - Held, no prejudice shown by appellant and defect cured by s. 529, CrPC - Effect of non-supply of statements of witnesses examined during investigation - Does not apply to trial of non-cognizable offence - A careful scrutiny of evidence, however, does not show that basic facts upon which two Courts relied are inconsistent - It would appear from evidence that appellant in course of his check of passports had suspicion that some of passport were not genuine - Distinction between a passport and emergency certificate sought to be made out, however, is not so clear on evidence - Thus, Court finds no reason to interfere with conviction or sentence - Appeal dismissed.
