V. S. Mallimath v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19892; (2001) 4 SCC 31; (2001) SCC (L&S) 629; AIR 2001 SC 1455; 2001 (89) FLR 834; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 267; JT 2001 (4) SC 1; 2001(2) SCALE 548; 2001 (2) SCJ 534; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 567; 2001 (2) SLR 423; 2001 (3) SLT 130; 2001 (2) Supreme 574

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Human Rights; Service

Keywords: Defence Services Regulations, High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954, All India Services, Indian Civil Service, Human Rights Commission Act, 1993, Defence Services Regulations., National Human Rights Commission Chairperson and Members (Salaries, Allowances and Other Conditions of Service) Rules, 1993

Summary: Human Rights - Advocates & Judges - Service - Protection of Human Rights Act, 1993 - High Court Judges (Conditions of Service) Act, 1954 - All India Services (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958, rr. 14, 15, 16, 17, 18 and 19 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Representation of People Act, 1951 - Human Rights Commission Act, 1993 - National Human Rights Commission Chairperson and Members (Salaries, Allowances and Other Conditions of Service) Rules, 1993 - (A) Whether pension as a Retired Chief Justice of High Court can be deducted from his salary as a Member of National Human Rights Commission under the proviso to r. 3(b)? - Court held, proviso to r. 3(b) would apply to the retired Chief Justice of India or the retired Chief Justice of HC and pension which they are in receipt of, apart from the disability or wound pension, has to be deducted from their salary, which they are entitled to under the Rules - (B) Whether Member of the Human Rights Commission is entitled to gratuity for the period he serves the Commission? - No, held, enabling provisions contained in rr. 16, 17 and 18 do not provide for payment of gratuity for a re-employed person - Petition dismissed.
