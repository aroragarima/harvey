Welset Engineers and Anr v Vikas Auto Industries and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 993; (2015) 10 SCC 609; 2006 (32) PTC 190

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Injunction

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 122, O 39, r. 2(a) - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 22 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 129 and 215 - Contempt of Courts (Bombay HC) Rules, 1994, Chap. LVIII - Whether High Court was justified in dismissing petition filed by appellant against respondents for contempt of an interim order passed by HC? - S. 22 of Contempt Act, 1971 provides right to proceed under Act is an additional right - HC overlooked s. 122, CPC - Rules u/Chap. LVIII of HC Rules lay down specific procedure for dealing with cases under Contempt of Courts Act, which are not overridden by provisions of O. 39, r. 2(a) CPC - Pritam Pal v HC of Madhya Pradesh, Jabalpur through Registrar, 1992 Indlaw SC 221 followed - Held, a party in breach of any order of Court whether interlocutory or final is subject to being proceeded against in contempt, orders are meant to be obeyed and a person, acting in breach of order does so at that person's peril - Thus, decision of HC is set aside and matter is remanded back to HC for disposal of contempt petition on merits - Appeal disposed of.
