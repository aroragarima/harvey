State Of Uttaranchal & Anr v Dinesh Kumar Sharma

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 898; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 594; (2007) 1 SCC 683; 2006 (7) AWC 7761; 2007 (1) ESC 132; 2006(13) SCALE 246; 2007 (2) SLR 832; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Promotion - Whether respondent has the right to claim promotion and seniority from 1995-96 when the vacancy arose or whether his seniority will be reckoned from the date of substantive appointment which is in the year 1999?; Whether High Court was justified in overlooking and ignoring the provisions of the U.P. Government Servants Seniority Rules, 1991 and grant a relief in favour of the respondents? - Held, a person appointed on promotion shall not get seniority of any earlier year but shall get the seniority of the year in which his/her appointment is made - Seniority also will be counted against the promotion/appointment in the cadre from the date of issuance of order of substantive appointment in the said cadre - High Court should not have interfered with this finding of the Appellants and directed them to "consider" the representation of the respondent - Appeal allowed.
