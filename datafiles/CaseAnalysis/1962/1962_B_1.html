Â Bhau Ram v B. Baijnath Singh

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 540; AIR 1962 SC 1476; 1962 JabLJ 676; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 724; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 724

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(f) - Rewa State Pre-emption Act, 1946, s. 10 - Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1913, s. 16 - Berar Land Revenue Code, 1928, ss. 174, 184 - (A) Whether a right of pre-emption by vicinage u/s. 10(2) of Rewa Act offends art. 19(1)(f)? - Held, second clause of s. 10 imposes an unreasonable restriction on right to acquire, hold or dispose of property guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution and must be struck down; so far as proviso is concerned it applies both to first and second clause and it will survive for purpose of first clause only - (B) Whether s. 16 of Punjab Act ultra vires art. 19(1)(f) of Constitution? Further held, sixth ground in s. 16 is unconstitutional, however right of pre-emption based on co-sharership, common staircase and common private passage from street provided in first, third and fourth grounds respectively in s. 16 are reasonable restriction on right to acquire, hold and dispose of property and is in interest of general public and are upheld - (C) Whether s. 16 of Punjab Act offends art. 14 of Constitution in view of exemptions provided to certain properties from application of Act in s. 5? - Further held, premises exempted u/s. 5 are practically of one class, namely, those to which public has to resort and it is this class which is distinct from rest of residential property meant for private residence of individuals which has been exempted; s. 16 cannot be said to violate art. 14 - (D) Whether right of pre-emption given by s. 174 of Berar Code is an unreasonable restriction on right to acquire, hold or dispose of property guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(f) ? - Further held, right of pre-emption under Chap. XIV is confined only to survey number which is one unit of assessment, occupants of which are co-sharers and are jointly and severally responsible for payment of land revenue; pre-emption as provided in r. 174 of Chap. XIV is really pre-emption in favour of co-sharers strictly so-called before there is any sub-division of a survey number and after such sub-division between persons who though not strictly co-sharers are still akin to co-sharers - Further held, provisions in Chap. 14 of Berar Land Revenue Code creating a right of pre-emption on sale of land are valid and fully within Constitution - C A 270 of 1955 allowed - C A 595 of 1960 and C A 430 of 1958 dismissed.
