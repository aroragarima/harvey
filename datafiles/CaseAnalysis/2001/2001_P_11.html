V. S. Achuthanandan v P. J. Francis and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19903; (2001) 3 SCC 81; AIR 2001 SC 837; JT 2001 (2) SC 337; 2001 (1) KLT 740; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 753; 2001(1) SCALE 513; 2001 (1) SCJ 675; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 713; 2001 (2) SLT 9; 2001 (1) Supreme 574

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7, r. 11(a) - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 116A - Conduct of Elections Rules, 1951 - Whether Designated Election Judge was justified in rejecting election petition at initial stage without affording appellant an opportunity to prove existence of circumstances prima facie justifying existence of grounds requiring re-count? - Averments made in petition and material brought on record by appellant did not make out a case for re-count - Appellant has indulged in a roving enquiry and has tried to fish out materials in hope that re-count, if allowed, may probably twist balance of votes in his favour which in facts and circumstances of case is nothing beyond a wishful thinking of appellant - Held, view taken by learned Designated Election Judge declining prayer for re-count and finding election petitioner not entitled to such relief is upheld - Appeal is devoid of any merit - Appeal dismissed.
