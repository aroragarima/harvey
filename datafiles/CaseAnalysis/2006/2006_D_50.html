Bhagwandas Tiwari & Ors. v Dewas Shajapur Kshetriya Gramin Bank & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1366; (2006) 12 SCC 574; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 125; AIR 2007 SC 994; 2007 (112) FLR 975; JT 2006 (10) SC 258; 2006(11) SCALE 593; 2007 (2) SLR 297; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 760

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Mode Of Selection, Regional Rural Banks Act, 1976, Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and others Employees) Rules, 1988, Indian Administrative Service/Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, Regional Rural Banks (Amendment) Act, 1987, Mysore State Civil Services General Recruitment Rules, 1957

Summary: Service - Regional Rural Banks (Appointment and Promotion of Officers and Other Employees) Rules, 1988, r. 5 - Promotion - Petitioners filed writ petition contending that respondent shifted from seniority-cum-merit to merit-cum-seniority by fixing criteria that only those employees who have secured 45 marks out of 60 shall be selected for promotion- Single Judge did not accept this contention and dismissed writ petition - On appeal, Division Bench upheld judgment of Single Judge - Whether Division Bench decision could be upheld? - Held, if minimum marks were 45 out of 60, means that an employee is to secure 75% of marks - Such a high percentage cannot be a measure of prescribing minimum marks to assess merit and would obviously be a case of shifting the focus to merit-cum-seniority - Division Bench's order set aside - Respondent No. 1 directed to issue fresh orders for promotion in with judgement - Appeal allowed.
