Birendra K. Singh v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2667; (2000) 8 SCC 498; (2001) SCC (Cr) 17; JT 2000 (8) SC 248

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302/34 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 227, 228 and 197 - Complaint - Murder - Appellant is a Police Sub-Inspector - Whether High Court erred in dismissing petition filed by Appellant questioning order of Magistrate taking cognizance of offence without sanction contemplated in s. 197 of CrPC? - Held, stage for raising such objection could be when accused are called upon to address arguments u/ss. 227 and 228 of CrPC - Case is committed to Court of Session - Appellant to raise contention relating to sanction u/s. 197 before Sessions Court at appropriate stage - Appeal disposed of.
