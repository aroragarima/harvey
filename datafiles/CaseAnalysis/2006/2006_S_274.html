Vithal v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 730; (2006) 13 SCC 54; (2008) 1 SCC (Cr) 91; 2007 CRLJ 317; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 289; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 689; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 763; 2006(10) SCALE 585; 2006 (3) ShimLC 329; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 234

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, a dying declaration if found to be acceptable, same need not be described to be in question and answer form - Dying declarations although are more than one, but being not contradictory to and inconsistent with each other, there is no reason as to why reliance should not be placed thereupon - Appeal dismissed.
