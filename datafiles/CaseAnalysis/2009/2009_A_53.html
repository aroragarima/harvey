Secretary, Forest Department and Others v Abdur Rasul Chowdhury

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 607; (2009) 7 SCC 305; AIR 2009 SC 2925; 2009 (2) CLR 519; 2009 (4) ESC 545; 2009 (122) FLR 462; 2009 (3) LLJ 354; 2009 (3) LLN 8; 2009(7) SCALE 139; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Recovery From Pension, West Bengal Service (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1971, Cooperative Federal Authority (Business) Regulations, 1976

Summary: Service - West Bengal Service (Death-cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1971, r. 10(1) - Retirement on account of superannuation during pendency of departmental enquiry proceedings - Respondent worked in wild life Division II - Gross financial irregularities - Memo served on 10-03-1987; explanation sought from respondent; explanation found to be unsatisfactory; order of suspension passed - Charge Memo served thereafter - Respondent retired on 31-03-1995 - No completion of departmental enquiry proceedings; retirement benefits not released - (A) Whether the employer could continue with the departmental enquiry proceedings initiated prior to the retirement of government servant by virtue of r. 10(1) of the Rules? - Held, Yes - Provision of r. 10(1) perused, interpretation given to the rule in 'State of West Bengal vs. Haresh C. Banerjee and Others, (2006) 7 SCC 651' noted - Employer can proceed with the departmental enquiry proceedings though the government servant has retired from service for imposing only punishment contemplated under the Rules - (B) Whether the delay in completing the domestic enquiry proceedings would be fatal to the proceedings? - Dependent on facts and circumstances of each case - If delay is satisfactorily explained than proceedings can continue - Decision in 'Deputy Registrar, Co-operative Societies vs. Sachindra Nath Pandy, (1995) 3 SCC 134' noted - Finding made by Administrative Tribunal after going through entire record, in instant case as to that respondent had left the head quarters without permission of the competent authority and for that reason proceedings could not be completed; finding upheld - Held, delay on account of respondent's non-cooperation - Direction issued as to completion of enquiry within 3 months - Impugned judgment upheld - Appeal allowed.
