Gujarat Industries and others v Commissioner of Central Excise-I, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 861; 2015 (326) E.L.T. 625; JT 2015 (12) SC 237

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Rules, 1944

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 2(f)(i) - Alleged Manufacture of Product - Payment of duty - Determination - Assessee is engaged in process of cold-rolling of hot- rolled stainless steel patti on job work basis - Assessee receives hot-rolled SS patti from other manufacturers and thereafter undertakes process of cold-rolling in cold-rolling mill - Further, patti are sent back to suppliers and assessee receives job charges from suppliers of materials - Assesse stated process of cold-rolling does not amount to manufacture under Act and accordingly did not take out central excise registration and did not discharge any central excise duty liability - Commissioner issued show cause notice to assessee on ground that process of cold- rolling undertaken by assessee amounts to manufacture and duty was demanded by authority - Further, order was passed confirming duty demand - Tribunal vide impugned order confirmed order passed by Commissioner - Further, Tribunal held that extended period of limitation under proviso to s. 11A of Act is invokable - Whether process of cold-rolling undertaken by assessee on hot-rolled stainless steel pattis amount to 'manufacture' within meaning of s. 2(f)(i) of Act.

Held, what emerges after cold rolling of hot rolled strips is altogether different product which has different physical properties. And, in explanatory Note to Chapter Heading 72.09 of HSN, it is further elaborated that because of their special properties. Hence, explanatory notes to HSN also lends support to fact that hot rolled products and cold- rolled products are two distinct products having their own identity, name, character and use. Cold-rolled strips are used for making cycle/auto-parts etc., whereas hot rolled strips cannot be used for this purpose. SC held that fact that degree of hardness can be achieved will not dilute applicability of Chapter Note 4 because every degree of hardening or type of tempering resulting from cold-rolling of flat-rolled products would amount to manufacturing within meaning of Chapter Note 4 of Chapter 72. Further, fact that annealing and pickling was done earlier by party sending goods for job work, will not make any difference because hardening or tempering of such flat-rolled products comes about only after cold-rolling. Further, with respect to extended period of limitation, SC held that assessee had not been paying any excise duty on process as according to it, this process did not amount to manufacture and no excise duty was paid. It is only on basis of intelligence report that assessee was evading central excise duty that matter came to notice of Revenue which led to exercise of issuing of notice. SC held that it is difficult to accept that assessee was under bona fide belief that excise duty was not payable and that it was not permissible for Department to avail larger period of limitation by invoking proviso to s. 11A of Act. Further, SC confirmed with finding of Commissioner that assessee, dealing with several similar manufacturing units who paid excise duty on identical processes, and doing job work on their behalf would have obviously known that excise registration was required for cold rolling mills in its factory for purpose of manufacturing cold-rolled pattis. Further, SC held that partner of assessee was fully aware that such activity was dutiable, in view of fact that four out of six units from which goods were received by company were paying duty on similar manufacturing activity. Hence, Commissioner is right in issuing show cause notice by invoking extended period of limitation and also holding that assessee had connived and deliberately acted in manner to defraud Revenue. Hence, SC rejected plea of assessee that impugned notice was time barred. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Assessee is liable to pay excise duty is process involved/work done by assessee amounts to manufacture of new product.
