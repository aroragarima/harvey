Pattipati Venkaiah v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 61; (1985) 4 SCC 80; (1985) SCC (Cr) 464; AIR 1985 SC 1715; 1985 (2) Crimes 746; 1985 CRLJ 2012; 1985 CRLR 454; 1985(2) SCALE 358; 1985 UJ 1048

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 379 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) filed instant appeal u/s. 379 of CrPC against impugned judgment of HC which reversed the order of acquittal passed by Trial Court and convicted the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to life imprisonment - Whether the defence had proved that FIR was really a false and fabricated document.

Held, on a sum total of the evidence and circumstances and considering the reasons given by the Trial Court for acquitting the accused, Trial Court has committed a serious error on a point of law while acquitting the accused without applying his mind to the effect of the evidence on record particularly of PW1 and 2 which has been perused by Court and Court see no reason to discard. Judgment of the Trial Court is extremely perverse. It is positively not a case in which another view is reasonably possible. Conviction and sentence awarded to the appellant by HC is upheld. Appeal dismissed.
