R. Narayanan v S. Semmalai and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 311; (1980) 2 SCC 537; AIR 1980 SC 206; 1980 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 571

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Order For

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss.81,84 - Election - Counting of votes - Rejection - Recount - Appellant contested elections for Assembly Constituency of Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly on behalf of AIADMK and Respondent for Congress party - Respondent alleged error in counting of votes in election contested where Appellant was elected - Respondent alleged that there was only minimal margin of 19 votes and sought recount of votes with Returning Officer and it was rejected - Respondent filed an Election Petition in HC u/ss.81, 84 of 1951 Act - Appellant in turn filed recrimination petition which was dismissed and recount was ordered - Aggrieved by order Appellant preferred appeal - Whether Appellant was entitled to relief against order passed by HC which dismissed recrimination petition filed by Appellant - Held, as per referred case evidence led by Respondent for order of recount of votes were unsatisfactory on consideration of Principles deduced from authorities - Recount and election of Respondent as ordered by HC was quashed - Appeal allowed.
