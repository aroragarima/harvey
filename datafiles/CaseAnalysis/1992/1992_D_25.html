Delhi Development Horticulture Employees’ Union v Delhi Administration, Delhi And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 957; (1992) 4 SCC 99; (1992) SCC (L&S) 805; AIR 1992 SC 789; 1992 (21) ATC 386; 1992 (64) FLR 1110; JT 1992 (1) SC 394; 1992 LabIC 847; 1992 (2) LLJ 452; 1992 (1) LLN 939; 1992(1) SCALE 294; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 565; 1992 (1) SLJ 201; 1992 (1) SLR 689; 1992 (1) UJ 676

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16, 21 and 41 - (A) Right to livelihood - Nature of - Whether Directive principle u/art. 41 ensuring right to work absolute? - (B) Object of Jawaharlal Nehru Rozgar Yojna - Persons employed under scheme - Whether can claim regularisation merely because they have put in more than Two hundred and forty days service? - Held, qualified by limits of economic capacity and development - Casual labour - Cannot claim regularisation - Petition dismissed.
