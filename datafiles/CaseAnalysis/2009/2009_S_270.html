Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited. And Another v Shri Dhanurdhar Champatiray

Supreme Court of India

11 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2033; (2010) 1 SCC 673; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 330; 2010 (1) AWC 917; JT 2009 (15) SC 310; 2010 (2) MPLJ 35; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 587; 2009(14) SCALE 545; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 398

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 2006

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 2006, s. 11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator - Respondent entered into contract with BSNL for construction of building as staff quarters - Dispute arose between parties and respondent requested Chief Engineer (Civil) for appointment of an arbitrator to adjudicate disputes between parties in terms of agreements - Appellants failed to respond to letters of respondent requiring them to appoint an arbitrator so respondent was constrained to file petitions u/s. 11(6) of Act for appointment of an Arbitrator - HC allowed application u/s. 11(6) of Act, and appointed Senior Advocate of Orissa High Court as sole arbitrator - Hence, present special leave petition - Held, HC did not appear to have focused on requirement to have due regard to qualifications required by agreement or other conditions necessary to secure appointment of an independent and impartial arbitrator - Impugned order of HC set aside - Matter remanded back to HC for reconsider - Appeals allowed.
