S.K. Mastan Bee v The General Manager, South Central Railway and another

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1444; (2003) SCC (L&S) 93; (2003) 1 SCC 184; AIR 2002 SCW 4856; 2003 (1) ESC 17; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 184; JT 2002 (10) SC 50; 2003 LabIC 76; 2003 (1) LLJ 561; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 79; 2002(9) SCALE 73; 2002 (7) SLR 1; 2002 (7) SLT 179; 2002 (8) Supreme 487; 2003 (1) UC 403; 2003 (1) UPLBEC 247

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Pension - Right to family pension on the death of the appellant's husband - Delay in approaching the Railways - What is the effect of such delay? - Held, it was an obligation of the Railways to have computed the family pension and offered the same to the widow of its employee as soon as it became due to her and also in view of the fact that her husband was only a Gangman in the Railways who might not have left behind sufficient resources for the appellant to agitate her rights and that the appellant is an illiterate - Very denial of her right to family pension as held by the learned Single Judge as well as the Division Bench is an erroneous decision on the part of the Railways and in fact amounting to a violation of the guarantee assured to the appellant under art.21 of the Constitution - HC in its Single Judge was justified in granting the relief to the appellant from the date from which it became due to her, that is from 21st November, 1969, the date of the death of her husband - Division Bench fell in error in restricting the payment of family pension from a date on which the appellant issued a legal notice to the Railways - Appeal allowed.
