M/S Dilawari Exporters v M/S Alitalia Cargo And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 287; (2010) 5 SCC 754; AIR 2010 SC 2233; 2010 (80) ALR 304; 2010 (4) AWC 3416; JT 2010 (4) SC 75; 2010 (2) OLR 86; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 833; 2010(3) SCALE 779; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 927; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 33

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Consumer Protection; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Prima Facie Evidence, Cargo

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Contract of carriage - Privity of contract - Complaint filed alleging deficiency in service on the part of the respondents, in particular by respondent no.1 (Airline) before the Commission - Commission observed that in the Air Waybill which is the prima facie evidence of conclusion of contract of the receipt of Cargo and the conditions of Carriage, the name of the Shipper was shown respondent no. 3 and not that of the appellant - Therefore, appellant has no locus-standi to file the complaint against respondent no.1 - Complaint was dismissed on the ground that there was no privity of contract between the appellant and respondent no.1 - Hence, present appeal - Whether there was any privity of contract between appellant and respondent no.1? - "Air waybills" according to both the contesting parties, are determinative of terms and conditions of contract between them - Held, as per s. 186 of the Contract Act agent having an authority to do an act has authority to do every lawful thing which is necessary in order to do such act - Onus to show that the act done by an agent was within the scope of his authority or ostensible authority held or exercised by him is on the person claiming against the principal - In present case, respondent no.3 had an express authority to receive the cargo for and on behalf of respondent no.1 - Though, in said Air Waybill the name of the Shipper was mentioned as that of respondent no.3 but the said Air Waybill was also signed by respondent no.3 as the agent of the carter-respondent no.1 - Respondent no.3 was, acting in dual capacity, one as a Shipper on behalf of the appellant and the other as an agent of respondent no.1 - That being so, respondent no.1 was bound by the acts of their agent, viz. respondent no.3, with all its results - Therefore, there was privity of contract between the appellant and respondent no.1 - Appeal allowed.

(B) Consumer Protection - Carriers & Transportation - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 23 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Deficiency in service - Delay in delivery of consignment - Appellant obtained an order from a concern based in New York, USA for export of goods - Appellant handed over the consignment of the said articles to respondent no.3 for onward dispatch to New York - Since, consignment did not reach New York by the stipulated date, the importer, cancelled the order and claimed damages from appellant - Alleging deficiency in service on the part of the respondents, in particular by respondent no.1 (Airline), the appellant filed a complaint before the Commission - Complaint was dismissed on the ground that there was no privity of contract between the appellant and respondent no.1 - Hence, present appeal - It was contended that respondent no.1 not being a party to the contract of carriage vis-a-vis the appellant, the respondent no. 1 cannot be held to be liable for any delay in delivery of the consignment in question - Held, respondent no.3 had an express authority to receive the cargo for and on behalf of respondent no.1 - Respondent no.3 was, acting in dual capacity, one as a Shipper on behalf of the appellant and the other as an agent of respondent no.1 - Respondent no.1 was bound by the acts of their agent, viz. respondent no.3 - Matter remitted to the Commission for fresh adjudication of the claim preferred by the appellant - Appeal allowed.
