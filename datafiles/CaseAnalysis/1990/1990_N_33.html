Uttar Pradesh Residents Employees Co-Operative House Buildin v New Okhla Industrial Development Authorityand Anr

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 207; (1990) Supp SCC 175; (2007) 15 SCC 515; AIR 1990 SC 1325; 1990 ALJ 315; JT 1990 (2) SC 445; 1990 SCALE 46; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 64; 1990 (2) UJ 12; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 957

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976, Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1912

Summary: Land & Property - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Area Development Act, 1976 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, 1912 - Uttar Pradesh Imposition of Ceiling on Land Holdings Act, 1960 - Notifications u/ss. 4 and 6 were issued stating that lands belonging to appellant society were needed for a public purpose - Society insisted on rehabilitation of members on original land on basis of policy of Govt. - Chief Executive Officer intimated society that authority had finally decided to offer lands in sectors 30, 31, 36 and 40 and that 20 per cent of amount had to be deposited, but society did not deposit amount by stipulated time - Society requested for extension of time, but NOIDA did not extend it and appellant society had not been allotted any land - Whether action of authority in not allotting land to appellant society was mala fide and also that action of authority in not extending time as prayed for arbitrary and discriminatory? - Held, impugned judgment of High Court is set aside - Directions issued to NOIDA that it would give to appellants such areas as this court might specify from sectors 40, 41 as also from sector 42 at price to be determined in accordance with judgment of this court - Supervision of this operation of course shall be by NOIDA but court hope and trust that society would co-operate with NOIDA in this regard - Appeal disposed of.
