Sneh Prabha Smt v Ravinder Kumar

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1742; (1995) Supp3 SCC 440; AIR 1995 SC 2170; 1996 (1) HLR 280

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 9 - Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 142 - Divorce - Whether it can be granted? - Held, decree for restitution of conjugal rights passed - Also, despite efforts for conciliation there was no chances of husband and wife living together - Marriage had irretrievably broken down, therefore, divorce be granted - Considering burden to pay towards education and other expenses and save for marriages of daughters, husband ordered to pay certain sum to daughters, till their marriage or they earn their livelihood - Also, daughters entitled for more financial assistance at time of their marriage - Appeal allowed.
