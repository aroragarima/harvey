(1) S. B. P. and Company; (2) B. T. Patil and v Patel Engineering Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1279; (2009) 10 SCC 293; 2010 (1) AWC 1053; 2010 (1) Bom.C.R. 382; 2009 (4) CompLJ 455; JT 2009 (13) SC 315; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 51; 2009(13) SCALE 335; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 340

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: De Facto, Different Intention, Termination Of Mandate, De Jure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration & Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 15(2), 11(6) - Appointment of arbitrator by HC - Appellant appointed 2nd respondent as an arbitrator - 1st respondent appointed another arbitrator, however, arbitrator declined to accept appointment - 1st respondent appointed a substitute arbitrator - Whether appointment of third arbitrator by HC u/s. 11(6) after the appointment of a substitute arbitrator by a party u/s.15 of the Act is legally correct - HC took a wrong view that a substitute arbitrator could be appointed u/s. 15(2) where the mandate of an already appointed arbitrator terminates - However, s. 15(2) does not per se apply to a case where an arbitrator appointed by a party to the agreement declines to accept the appointment or refuses to arbitrate in the matter - Agreements entered into between the appellant and 1st respondent do not contain a provision for appointment of a substitute arbitrator in case arbitrator appointed by either party was to decline to accept appointment or refuse to arbitrate in the matter - In the absence of a clear stipulation to the effect of appointment of substitute arbitrator in the agreements, 1st respondent cannot appoint a substitute arbitrator and HC erred in appointing the third arbitrator thereafter - Impugned HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
