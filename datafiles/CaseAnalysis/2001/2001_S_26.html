S. Pitchai Ganapathy v Commissioner, Hindu Religious and Charitable Endowments Department

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 15; (2001) 8 SCC 460; AIR 2001 SC 2875; 2002 AllCJ 636; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 205; JT 2001 (7) SC 544; 2001(6) SCALE 218; 2001 (3) SCJ 473; 2001 (6) SLT 505; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 1; 2001 (6) Supreme 849

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Private temples

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Private temples - Legality - Instant appeal was filed by appellant against judgment of HC. Held, reliance was placed on the statements made by petitioner no. 2 before Deputy Commissioner which is to the effect that plaintiffs appellants did not buy same from anyone and since his ancestors were enjoying, it had come to be enjoyed by them, he admitted that house taxes were paid in his capacity as trustee. The public could offer worship at temple only with their permission and they had never refused such permission at any time. The public also used to carry sandal pot from temple and each individual should pay 10 annas for the same. While devotees come for such pooja, they used to collect fees from them which would be taken away by them. The tax had not been levied on their original names but only as Manager of trustee or only in that capacity they had collected the rents. Exhibits A41 to A47 did not pertain to temple. The site mentioned in Exs.A49, 50, 51 and 52 did not pertain to suit site of temple but were lying in front of suit temple and aforesaid site and also site found in Ex.A53 were not that of temple but adjoining it. This evidence is fatal to case of plaintiffs appellants and, therefore, HC could firmly come to conclusion that presumption that temple is a public temple is not rebutted. when on questions of fact and on construction of documents, none of which can be treated to be documents of title, but only to indicate description of property or nature of possession and enjoyment of property thereof alone has been considered, there is hardly anything for SC to interfere with such orders. DB as well as Single Judge have each independently applied their mind to relevant documents and have arrived at identical conclusions. The anxiety shown by HC will clearly indicate that even if there was a wee-bit of material before Court the same would have been held in their favor. Appeal dismissed.
