M/S Jain Studios Limited Through Its President v Shin Satellite Public Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1501; (2006) 5 SCC 501; AIR 2006 SC 2686; 2006 (5) ALD(SC) 71; 2007 (1) AWC 171; JT 2006 (7) SC 40; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 601; 2006(7) SCALE 34; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 137 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Review of judgments or orders by SC - Instant review petition was filed for review the order passed in Arbitration Petition in Shin Satellite Public Co. Ltd. v. Jain Studios Ltd. 2006 INDLAW SC 24 and restore the Arbitration Petition to the file for reconsideration - Prayer was also made to nominate a arbitrator - (A) Maintainability of review petition - Respondent submitted that the decision of the Chief Justice or his nominee was 'final' and no review lie against such order - Held, an order passed by the Chief Justice of India or his nominee u/s. 11(6) of the Act was an 'order' within the meaning of art. 137 of the Constitution and was subject to review under the aforesaid provision - Hence, review petition was maintainable - (B) It was submitted that the applicant seeks to re agitate the same point which was advanced at the time of hearing - Held, when a prayer to appoint an arbitrator by the applicant had been made at the time when the Arbitration Petition was heard and was rejected, the same relief could not be sought by an indirect method by filing a review petition - Order accordingly.
