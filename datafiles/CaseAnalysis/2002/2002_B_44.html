Icds Limited v Beena Shabeer and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1604; (2002) 6 SCC 426; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1342; AIR 2002 SC 3014; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1555; 2002 (2) AllCriC 615; 2002 (3) AllCriR 2519; 2002 (4) AWC 2781; 2002 (3) BC 310; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 20; 2002 (3) CCR 139; 2002 (2) CGLJ 208; [2002] 111 Comp Cas 742; 2002 (4) Crimes 75; 2002 (6) CRJ 174; 2002 CRLJ 3935; 2002 (3) CTC 572; 2002 (3) JCC 1472; 2002 (2) JIC 819; JT 2002 (6) SC 119; 2002 (3) KLT 218; 2002 (4) MPLJ 1; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 74; 2002(5) SCALE 541; 2002 (4) SLT 701; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 488; 2002 (6) Supreme 25

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instrument Act, 1881, s.138 - Companies Act,1956 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.482 - Dishonour of Cheque - Guarantor - Challenged - Appellant registered Company incorporated under provisions of the 1956 Act - Husband of respondent No.1 entered into hire purchase agreement with appellant to buy Maruthi car - Respondent No.1's wife stood guarantor in respect of hire purchase facilities - Respondent No.1, on account of aforesaid transaction made part payment and issued cheque to appellant - Thus, said cheque was dishonoured and returned to appellant with remark 'insufficient funds' - Therefore, appellant issued statutory notice u/s.138 of the 1881 Act which did not get any reply - Therefore, appellant filed complaint u/s.138 of the 1881 Act before Chief Judicial Magistrate's Court - Thereafter respondent filed petition u/s.482 CrPC for quashing of Complaint which was given in favour of appellant, during pending of petition respondentNo.1 filed appeal before HC which was allowed and stated that cheque from guarantor it could not be said to had been issued for purpose of discharging any debt or liability and complaint u/s.138 could not be maintained - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was Justifiable - Held, that language of Statute depicts intent of law-makers to effect that wherever there was default on part of one in favour of another and in event cheque was issued in discharge of any debt or other liability there could not be any restriction or embargo in matter of application of provisions of s.138 of the Act - Therefore, HC fell into manifest error, quashed impugned judgment of HC and restored order of Additional Chief Judicial Magistrate's Court - Appeal allowed.
