Bahadur Singh v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 441; (2010) 4 SCC 445; 2010 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 97; JT 2010 (3) SC 566; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 157; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 586; 2010(3) SCALE 559; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 402

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 15 - Possession of contraband - Conviction - Challenged - Petitioner was convicted for an offence punishable u/s. 15 of the NDPS Act - Allegation against the petitioner that he had been found in possession of six bags each containing 32 kilograms of Poppy Husk without any permit or license, was found to have been proved by the Trial Court as well as the HC - Conviction challenged - (A) Non-compliance of the provisions of s. 42 of the NDPS Act - Held, in the instant case, as soon as the investigating officer reached the spot, he sent a wireless message to the Deputy Superintendent of Police, who was his immediate higher officer and subsequent to recovery of the contraband, a Ruqa containing all the facts and circumstances of the case was also sent to the Police Station from the spot from where the recovery was made on the basis whereof the First Information Report was registered and copies thereof were sent to the llaqa Magistrate and also to the higher police officers - Therefore, there was substantial compliance with the provisions of s. 42 of the NDPS Act and no prejudice was shown to have been caused to the petitioner on account of non-reduction of secret information into writing and non-sending of the same to the higher officer immediately thereafter - (B) Non-compliance of the provisions s. 57 of the NDPS Act - Held, provisions of s. 57 of NDPS Act was not mandatory - In the present case information of the arrest of the petitioner and seizure of the contraband had been duly reported to the local police station on the basis of which the First Information Report had been drawn up - (C) It was submitted that the petitioner had not been found to be in conscious possession of the seized Poppy Husk and the mere fact that the bags containing the Poppy Husk were recovered from his premises did not automatically establish "conscious possession" - Held, submissions with regard to conscious possession of the seized Poppy Husk cannot be accepted having particular regard to the fact that the six bags containing 32 kilograms of Poppy Husk in each of the bags were not only recovered from the premises of the petitioner but from a room which was opened by him with a key in his possession - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
