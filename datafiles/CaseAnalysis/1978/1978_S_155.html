Joginder Singh and Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 105; (1979) SCC (Cr) 295; (1979) 1 SCC 345; 1979 (16) ACC 43; AIR 1979 SC 339; 1979 CRLJ 333; 1979 CrLR(SC) 21; 1979 MLJ 470; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 306; 1979 UJ 124

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 193, 209 and 319 - Scope and ambit of s. 319 - Power to proceed against other persons appearing to be guilty of offence - Whether Sessions Court can add such a person as an accused in absence of any committal order against him - Construing meaning of expression 'any person not being accused' occurring in s. 319, meaning of? - Held, expression clearly covers any person who is not being tried already by Court and very purpose of enacting such a provision like - Appeal dismissed.
