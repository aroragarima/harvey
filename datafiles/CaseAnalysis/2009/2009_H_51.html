Union of India v Harpat Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 806; (2009) 14 SCC 375; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1422; 2009 (75) ALR 799; 2009 (4) AWC 3780; 2009(8) SCALE 201; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 705

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 18 and 23 - Acquisition of cluster of villages for development of Delhi - Lands involved were basically agricultural lands - Five villages were involved for which separate acquisition proceedings were undertaken - Two of the villages were 'Chilla Saroda Bangar' and 'Chilla Saroda Khader'; and instant appeal is with regard to lands comprising in such villages - Amount of compensation awarded in respect of other three villages were subject matters of decisions of the Court viz. 'Karan Singh v. UOI, (1997) 8 SCC 186' and 'DDA v. Bali Ram Sharma, (2004) 6 SCC 533' - In Karan Singh's case High Court had granted compensation @76,500/-per bigha which was upheld by the Court - When the nature of land sought to be acquired is different, the amount of compensation would be different keeping in view several factors, viz. date of notification, the class of land sought to be acquired whether fully irrigated, number of annual crops or as to whether it was a land where no cultivation or vegetation is done - As per decision in Karan Singh's and Bali Ram Sharma's case, Court proceeded on the basis that the minimum amount of compensation which must be paid should be determined at Rs. 76.55 per sq. yard - Court noticed that lands situated in both the villages were situated nearer Delhi as compared to the three villages; further undisputed fact noted that lands acquired were capable of being developed; fact noted that some construction activities had already started in the village in question by the DDA itself - Despite the fact that such villages are not yet fully developed some amenities like road, electricity, water, sewerage were there - Held, keeping in view of its proximity to Delhi interest of justice shall be subserved if the market value is raised to 10% from Rs. 76.55 per sq. yard - Court took under its consideration, while giving such holding, the fact that appeals were otherwise barred by limitation - Appeals disposed of.
