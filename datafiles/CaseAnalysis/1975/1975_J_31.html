Joint Secretary To The Government of India and v Khillu Ram and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, V.R. Krishna Iyer, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 188; (1976) 1 SCC 88; AIR 1975 SC 2275; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 78; 1975 UJ 868

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954 s. 33 - Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Rules, 1955 r.30 - Property allotment - Abrogation of rules with retrospective effect - Challenged - In 1950 respondent no.1 and 'X' with his son were allotted shop no.2 at Tripri township which was Govt. property - In 1951'X' and his son left Tripri to settle elsewhere and respondent no.2 had applied for allotment of their share - Managing Officer held respondent nos. 1 and 2 were in possession of shop as allottees with 2/3 and 1/3 share therein - Respondent no.1 aggrieved by said order preferred appeal before Settlement Officer, who set aside said order and remanded back for adjudication u/r.30 of 1955 Rules- After remand Asst. Settlement Officer allotted entire shop to respondent no.1 and revision against said order was dismissed - Late on r.30 of 1955 Rules was abrogated and respondent no.2 filed application u/s. 33 of the Act before Central govt. which set aside order of allotment and directed said property to be put for sale - Aggrieved respondent no.1 filed writ before HC, which held subsequent deletion of rule would not affect existing rights of respondent no1 and quashed order passed u/s.33 of the Act - Hence, instant appeal Whether deletion of r.30 of 1955 Rules, made any difference to rights of parties concerned.

Held, a displaced person has a right to get compensation in the form and manner prescribed by the Act and the Rules framed there under and r. 30 is in Chapter V of the Rules which deals with payment of compensation by transfer of acquired Evacuee Properties. Though disputed property is a government built property and not an acquired evacuee property, r. 43 in Chapter VI of the Rules which provides for payment of compensation by transfer of government built property says that the 'provisions of rr. 25 to 34 shall, so far as may be, apply to the transfer of any Govt. built property or Govt. plot under this Chapter'. Therefore, r.30 of rules prescribe that where the property is in the occupation of more than one person, it shall be offered to the person whose gross compensation is the highest and r. 30 of Rules deals not with the form of procedure but with a substantive right conferred by the Act on displaced persons. Further, r.30 is not an instrument or machinery for asserting the right conferred by the Act and it does not regulate the procedure for settlement of disputes concerning that right. Thus, deletion of the rule in 1963 cannot affect pending actions and rights of respondent nos. 1 and 2 must be governed by r. 30 which was in force in 1959 when the dispute arose and was decided by the Managing officer. Instant court followed HC judgement in Pt. Dev Raj v. Union of India & ors., 1972 Indlaw PNH 81, held that so far as rule 30 is concerned "the right which a displaced person claims under this rule cannot be adversely affected or taken away unless it is expressly stated in the amending provision, or the language of the Act. Hence, neither by express words nor by implication the amendment of the Rules in 1963 deleting r. 30 has been made retrospective in operation. Order accordingly.

Ratio - A displaced person has a right to the determination of his claim for compensation and its satisfaction in the prescribed manner and this is a substantive right.
