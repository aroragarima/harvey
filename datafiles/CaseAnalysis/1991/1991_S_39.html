Shiv Lal v Sat Parkash and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 429; (1993) Supp2 SCC 345; AIR 1993 SC 275

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Rent Control Act, ss. 13(2) and 15(5) - Eviction - Whether Respondents can be evicted from premises in question on the ground that they had ceased to occupy the building for a continuous period of more than four months without reasonable cause? - An examination of facts and circumstances of present case indicates that reconsideration of the evidence by HC was not justified - Further on being misled by its view that the cession of tenancy is a necessary element of s. 13(2)(v), HC proceeded to re-examine the evidence on the records, leading to impugned judgment - Therefore, impugned judgment is set aside and restore decrees passed by the trial court - Appeals allowed.
