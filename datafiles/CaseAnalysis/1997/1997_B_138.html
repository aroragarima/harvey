Tamil Nadu Electricity Board v Bridge Tunnel, Constructions and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2389; (1997) 4 SCC 121; AIR 1997 SC 1376; 1997 (2) ARBLR 1; JT 1997 (1) SC 467; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 96; 1997(2) SCALE 653; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 132; 1997 (3) Supreme 365

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 30 - Agreement - Dispute - Arbitration - Award - Challenged - (A) Whether arbitrator was not obliged to decide non-arbitrability of some of items claimed by respondents before/while giving a non-speaking award and whether a deemed decision could be given credence? - Held, arbitrator gets jurisdiction to decide dispute on merits only when he is specifically called upon to decide dispute in terms of contract - Arbitrator, while deciding admitted dispute, subject-matter of adjudication, may decide dispute in reference to agreement - That would be within his jurisdiction - In such jurisdictional issue, even if an error is committed that may not be an error apparent on face of record because arbitrator, chosen forum, may commit an error in exercising his jurisdiction - However, if he, by a speaking award, decides it on a wrong proposition of law, it will be an error apparent on face of record and liable to correction - (B) Grounds of challenge of an award? - Held, arbitration award can be challenged only when: (1) there is an error apparent on face of record and (2) that arbitrator has exceeded his jurisdiction - In latter case, Court can look into arbitration agreement, but, under former, it cannot do so unless agreement was incorporated or cited in award or evidence was made part of agreement - (C) When can court examine merit of award? - In case of non-speaking award, it is not open to court to go into merits - Only in a speaking award court can look into reasoning in award and correct wrong proposition of law or error of law - Court cannot probe into mental process of arbitrator and speculate, when no reasons have been given by arbitrator, as to what impelled arbitrator to arrive at his conclusion - But in later case Court, with reference to terms of contract/arbitration agreement, would consider whether or not arbitrator/umpire has exceeded his jurisdiction in awarding or refusing to award sum of money awarded or omitted a consolidated lump sum - (D) Procedure should be adopted when award is illegal - Held, once a finding is recorded that umpire/arbitrator has committed error of jurisdiction, two courses are open, viz., either to remit award to umpire for reconsideration or to set aside award in toto - Latter course would be appropriate in facts and circumstances in present case - Award cannot be upheld and set aside - Appeal allowed.
