Anil Kumar Singh v Shivnath Mishra And Gadasa Guru

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1133; (1995) 3 SCC 147; AIR 1995 SCW 1782; 1996 (27) ALR 203; 1995 (1) AnWR 8; 1994 (3) CCC 542; JT 1995 (1) SC 273; 1995 RD 90; 1994(4) SCALE 953; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 3, O. 1, r. 10(2), O. 6, r. 17, O. 22, r. 10 - Petitioner in a petition for specific performance of a contract of sale of land filed an application seeking leave to amend plaint by impleading respondent also as a party-defendant in suit - Whether person who has got his interest in property declared by an independent decree but not a party to agreement of sale, is a necessary and proper party to effectually and completely adjudicate upon and settle all questions involved in suit? - Held, a person may be added as a party-defendant to suit though no relief may be claimed against him/her provided his/her presence is necessary for a complete and final decision on question involved in suit, and such a person is only a proper party as distinguished from a necessary party - Appeal dismissed.
