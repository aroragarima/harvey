Harminder Kaur and Others v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 745; (2009) 13 SCC 90; AIR 2009 SC 2875; 2009 (3) ESC 535; JT 2009 (13) SC 550; 2009(7) SCALE 204; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 1050; 2009 (8) SLR 5

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: By Transfer On Deputation, Irregular Appointment, Chandigarh Education Service (School Cadre) (Group - C) Recruitment Rules, 1991

Summary: Service - Constitution - Chandigarh Education Service (School Cadre) (Group 'C') Recruitment Rules, 1991 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Regularisation - School teachers on contract basis - Claim of regularisation - Denied by CAT and High Court - Claim whether admissible or whether, having regard to the long tenure of service, appointment of the appellants should have been or could be directed to be regularized? - Held, No - Post of teachers in instant case 'General Central Service (Group C)'; a 'non-selection' post and governed by the Rules; appellant were called from Regional Employment Exchange; further undisputed position that they were appointed only for a specified period - Provision of Rules perused - Offers of appointment in no uncertain terms provided that the appointee would have no claim for regular appointment - Appellants sought to rely upon para 53 of 'Secretary, State of Karnataka vs. Uma Devi (2006) 4 SCC 1' with contention that principles stated in para 53 should be applied considering the fact that a large number of sanctioned posts have been lying vacant and appellants have the essential academic qualification - Long service by itself may not be a ground for directing regularisation; regularisation is not a mode of appointment; principles of equality u/arts. 14 and 16 are to be scrupulously followed when appointments in public office are required to be made, and when a departure is sought to be made for not following the conditions under respective statutory rules as also constitutional scheme, it is imperative that the same must be done within the four corners of the delegated power by the authority concerned - Regularisation was ordered in Uma Devi's case as one time measure, and there was made a specific observation in the words, '..but there should be no further bypassing of the constitutional requirement and regularisation or making permanent, those not duly appointed as per the constitutional scheme.' - A judgment of a Constitution Bench of this Court laying down the law within the meaning of art. 141 of the Constitution must be read in its entirely for the purpose of finding out the ratio laid down therein; Constitution Bench, in no uncertain terms, based its decision on the touchstone of the 'equality clause' contained in arts. 14 and 16 of the Constitution - Impugned judgement upheld - Observations made as to that 800 posts of teachers are lying vacant and appellants case be taken into consideration keeping in mind the desirability of relaxing the age limit - Appeals dismissed.
