Sita Ram Bhandar Society, New Delhi v Lt. Governor, Government Of Nct Delhi And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1167; (2009) 10 SCC 501; AIR 2010 SC 1143; 2010(2) ALL MR 473; 2010 (2) AWC 1264; JT 2009 (12) SC 324; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 138; 2009(12) SCALE 550; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 507; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 306

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Revenue Official

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Land acquisition - Possession - Method of take over - Collector or Revenue Official cannot enter into each bigha or biswas of large area of land to be acquired from large number of owners, hence a pragmatic approach to be adopted by Court - Recording of Panchanama can itself constitute evidence of taking over of possession.P /&gt;(B) Land & Property - Nature of land - Report of Khasra Girdwari - Proof of - A Khasra Girdwari is a mere crop inspection report entered twice a year, has no presumption of truth attached to it - Date of notification for acquisition is the relevant date for determining nature of land.P /&gt;(C) Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s.16 - Land acquisition - Acquisition of land of appellant society - Objection filed by appellant - Dismissal of objection - Award rendered by Land Acquisition Collector - Taking over of possession and validity of acquisition proceedings challenged - 2 writ petitions filed by appellant - Writ petitions dismissed by HC - Contention by appellant that symbolic possession of land not sufficient - Held, Panchanama recorded by Tehsildar and possession taken and handed over to Revenue Department - No question raised by appellant in earlier objection or earlier litigations with regard to existence of a wall in suit property, therefore, no omission in award with respect to existence of a wall - Stay of dispossession granted by HC in favor of appellant vacated automatically when the said writ petition dismissed thereafter - Further, re-entry by appellant on the acquired land on account of various interim orders of court would not obliterate the consequence of vesting of land with State u/s.16 of Act - Appeals dismissed.
