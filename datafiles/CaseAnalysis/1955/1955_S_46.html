Shri Virindar Kumar Satyawadi v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1956 SC 153; 1956 CRLJ 326; 1956 (58) PLR 583; 1956 SCJ 138; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Rule

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act,1951, s. 36 (2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 476 and 476 B - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 193 and 195 - Validity of nomination papers - Appeal against - (A)Whether offence u/s 193, IPC is appealable u/s 476 B of CrPC? - Held, that if complaint relates to offences mentioned in ss. 195(1) (b) and 195 (1) (c), an appeal would be competent, but not if it relates to offences mentioned in s. 195 (1) (a) - If offence is not committed in a judicial proceeding, then it will fall outside ambit of s. 195 (1) (b) of IPC - But if offence u/s 193 is committed in or in relation to a proceeding in court, then it will fall u/s 195 (1)(b), and order directing prosecution u/s 476 will be appelable u/s 476 B of CrPC - (B) Whether Returning Officer deciding on validity of a nomination paper u/s 36 (2) is appellable? - Returning Officer deciding on validity of a nomination paper u/s 36 (2) not a court for purpose of s. 195 (1) (b) of CrPC - Held, as regards to charge u/s 193, IPC, order of Magistrate (Returning Officer) is not appealable, as offence is not committed in or in relation to any proceeding in a court - (B) What distinguishes a court from a quasi-judicial tribunal? - Held, what distinguishes a court from a quasi-judicial tribunal is that it is charged with a duty to decide disputes in a judicial manner and declare rights of parties in a definitive judgment - It also imports an obligation on part of authority to decide matter on a consideration of evidence adduced and in accordance with law - Then a question, therefore, arises as to whether an authority created by an Act is a court as distinguished from a quasi-judicial tribunal, what has to be decided is whether having regard to provisions of Act it possesses all attributes of a Court - (C) Whether function of Returning Officer acting u/s 36 of Representation of People Act is judicial in character? - U/s 36 (2) Representation of People Act Returning Officer has to examine nomination paper and decide all objections which may be made thereto - This power is undoubtedly judicial in character - But in exercising this power, he is authorised to come to a decision 'after such summary enquiry, if any, as he thinks necessary' - Parties have no right to insist on producing evidence which they may desire to adduce in support of their case - Here there is no machinery provided for summoning of witnesses, or for compelling production of documents in an enquiry u/s 36 - Returning Officer is entitled to act suo motu in such a matter - Held, function of Returning Officer acting u/s 36 is judicial in character, but he is not to act judicially in discharging it as proceedings u/s 36 present a different picture - (D) Whether Presiding Officer acting as per s. 36 (2) of Act of 1951 can himself file complaint under ss. 181, 182 and 193, IPC? - S. 476 of CrPC does not preclude officer presiding over a Court from himself preferring a complaint, and that jurisdiction of Magistrate before whom complaints is laid to try it like any other complaint is not taken away by that section - Held, there is thus no legal impediment to a Returning Officer acting u/s 36 (2) of Act of 1951 filing a complaint under ss. 181 and 182 IPC as provided in s. 195(1)(a) and charging accused therein with also an offence u/s 193 of IPC -(E) Whether Supreme Court can interfere in special leave if Act does not provide for an appeal? - Whether action should be taken u/s 195, CrPC, is a matter primarily for court which hears application, and its discretion is not to be lightly interfered with in appeal, even when that is competent - Held, where legislature does not provide for an appeal, it is preposterous on part of the appellant to invite Supreme Court to interfere in special appeal - Appeal dismissed.
