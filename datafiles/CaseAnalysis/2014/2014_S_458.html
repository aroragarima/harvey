Atul Tripathi v State of Uttar Pradesh and another

Supreme Court of India

22 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 468; (2014) 9 SCC 177; AIR 2014 SC 3062; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3326; 2014 (3) CGLJ 582; 2014 CRLJ 3941; 2014 (3) JCC 2277; JT 2014 (8) SC 535; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 844; 2014(8) SCALE 663

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Opportunity to public prosecutor

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149 r/w ss. 302, 120B - Criminal Law (Amendment) Act, 2013, s. 7 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - Murder - Conviction - Opportunity to public prosecutor - Respondents accused were convicted by Session Judge u/ss. 147, 148, 149 r/w ss. 302, 120B IPC and u/s. 7 of the Act and out of 7 accused, 4 accused were granted bail - Whether Appellate Court, while considering release of convict on bail, should give opportunity to public prosecutor for showing cause in writing against such release where conviction was on offence punishable with death or imprisonment for life or for a term not less than ten years - Appellant contended that procedure contemplated u/s. 389 CrPC proviso had not been complied with while releasing accused on bail, hence, order passed by HC was liable to be set aside -

Held, Appellate Court, if inclined to consider release of convict sentenced to punishment for death or imprisonment for life or for period of ten years or more, should first give opportunity to public prosecutor to show cause in writing against such release - On such opportunity being given, State was required to file its objections, if any, in writing - In case public prosecutor did not file objections in writing, Appellate Court should, in its order, specify that no objection had been filed despite opportunity granted by Court - Court should judiciously consider all relevant factors whether specified in objections or not, like gravity of offence, nature of crime, age, criminal antecedents of convict, impact on public confidence in court, etc. before passing order for release - Admittedly, no such opportunity was granted to State as contemplated under first proviso of s. 389 Cr.PC in these appeals - Therefore, impugned orders to extent of release of private respondents on bail were set aside - HC should consider the matters afresh - Appeals allowed.
