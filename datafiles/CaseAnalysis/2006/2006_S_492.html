State of Maharashtra and Others v Mana Adim Jamat Mandal

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 600; (2006) 4 SCC 98; AIR 2006 SC 3446; 2006 (5) AWC 5249; 2006 (4) Bom.C.R. 299; JT 2006 (3) SC 338; 2006(3) SCALE 169; 2006 (3) SCJ 791; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1142

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Human Rights

Summary: Human Rights - Socio-Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 342 and 366 - Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes Orders (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Whether the Mana community in the State of Maharashtra is a Sub-Tribe of "Gond" and is a Scheduled Tribe or not? - Held, in Entry 18 of Second Schedule of Amendment Act of 1976 the word 'including' was deliberately omitted, which signifies that each one of the Tribe specifying in Entry 18 is deemed to be a separate Tribe by itself, therefore, 'Mana' is not a sub-Tribe of 'Gond' but a separate Tribe by itself and is a Scheduled Tribe - Appeals dismissed.
