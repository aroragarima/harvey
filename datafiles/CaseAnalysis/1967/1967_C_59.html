Commissioner of Income Tax, Gujarat v Tejaji Farasram Kharawalla Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 340; AIR 1968 SC 197; [1968] 67 ITR 95; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 37

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3)(vi) - Availablitiy of exemption u/s. 4(3)(vi) - Clause grants exemption in respect of expenses 'incurred' - Selling agent held an employment for profit under agreement with principals - Held, only expenses which are wholly and necessarily incurred in year of account in performance of its duties as selling agents are exemptable under referred section - Appeal allowed.
