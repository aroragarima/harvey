S. M. D. Kiran Pasha v Government of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 222; (1990) SCC (Cr) 110; (1990) 1 SCC 328; 1989 (3) Crimes 759; 1990 CRLR 55; 1990 CrLR(SC) 55; JT 1989 (4) SC 366; 1989(2) SCALE 1083; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Excise; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Excise - Andhra Pradesh Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Dacoits, Drug offenders, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Land Grabbers Act 1986 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Prevention of Black marketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980 - Popularity - Detention - Right to Liberty - Vague - Appellant states that he enjoys popularity with several important positions - Appellant contended that Respondent/State. through Excise and Police authorities foisting false cases upon him - Police having summoned appellant for taking his photograph as of criminals - Appellant filed W.P in HC was allowed - Excise authorities had registered some cases against appellant and he was granted bail - Appellant filed petition that actions initiated against him were part of political vendetta was allowed - Appellant was served detention order and detained in jail and released after four days - Appellant filed petition to refrain respondents from making order detaining him under provisions of Act was allowed - Detention order was served on appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in dismissing petition filed by appellant - Held, appellant's fundamental right to liberty was reflex of legal obligation of rest of society, including State, and it was appellant's legal power bestowed upon him to bring legal action enforcement of fulfillment of obligation existing towards him - Denial of legal action would, amount to denial of his right of enforcement of his right to liberty - Petition for writ of habeas corpus would not be substitute for this enforcement - No other alternative than to hold that detention order had not approved by State. within 12 days of its being made - It ceased to be in force for failure to refer appellant's case to Advisory Board within time prescribed by law - Appeal allowed.
