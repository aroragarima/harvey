Lt. Governor Of Himachal Pradesh v Avinash Sharma

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 69; (1970) 2 SCC 149; AIR 1970 SC 1576; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 ss. 4, 5(A), 6, 9(1), 17(1), 17(4) - General Clauses Act, 1897 s. 21 - Notification - Acquisition - Cancellation - Challenged - Dy. Commissioner acted on request of Air Force authorities took possession of land and record had not disclosed under which authority possession of land was taken and delivered - Later on correspondence made between State and Air Force authorities and notification u/s.4 of the Act was published - Composite notification u/ss. 6, 17(1), 17(4) of the Act by State declared land was required urgently u/s. 5(A) of the Act and possession would be taken over u/s.17(1) of the Act after expiry of 15 days from publication of notice u/s. 9(1) of the Act and concerned authority served notices u/s. 9 of the Act - On 5-10-1965 Govt. published order cancelling notification for acquisition of land - Respondent filed petition before Judicial Commissioner for quashing of said order which was granted - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, when a notification u/s. 17(1) of 1894 Act is issued, on the expiration of fifteen days from the publication of the notice mentioned u/s. 9 (1) of 1894 Act, the possession previously obtained will be deemed to be the possession of the Govt. u/s.17(1) of 1894 Act and the land will vest in the Govt. free from all encamp branches. Therefore, it is implicit that after possession has been taken pursuant to a notification u/s. 17 (1) of 1894 Act, the land is vested in the Govt. and the notification cannot be cancelled u/s. 21 of 1897 Act nor can the notification be withdrawn in exercise of the powers u/s. 48 of 1894 Act. Hence, possession of the land is taken u/s.17 (1) 1894 Act, the land vests in the Govt. and there is no provision by which land statutorily vested in the Govt. reverts to the original owner by mere cancellation of the notification. Appeal dismissed.
