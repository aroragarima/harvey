S.P. Anand, Indore v H.D. Deve Gowda and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3696; (1996) 6 SCC 734; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 459; AIR 1997 SC 272; AIR 1997 SCW 18; 1997 (1) AWC 2117; 1996 (4) CCC 136; 1996 (4) CLT 279; JT 1996 (10) SC 274; 1996(8) SCALE 191; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 486; 1996 (8) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 74, 75 and 226 - (A) Appointment of Prime Minister - Person who is not member of either House of Parliament can be appointed Prime Minister for six months - Whether such appointment militates against democratic principles embodied in Constitution? - (B) Whether of public interest litigation permitted at sweet will of petitioner - Unless court sees reason to permit withdrawal, guided by considerations of public interest, also ensuring that it does not result in abuse of process of law - (C) Filing of public interest litigation petition involving issues of constitutional laws - Whether petitioner should refrain from filing such petition having no expert knowledge in that field except for saying that they needed reconsideration he had no answer to correctness of decisions - Held, (A) such appointment does not militate against Constitution - (B) Not permitted at sweet will of petitioner - (C) Should refrain from filing such petition - Such a casual approach to considered decisions of Supreme Court even by a person well-versed in law would not be countenanced - Petition dismissed.
