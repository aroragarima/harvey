S. Nagarajan v District Collector, Salem and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2250; (1997) 2 SCC 571; (1997) SCC (L&S) 684; AIR 1997 SC 935; 1997 (1) CLT 388; JT 1997 (1) SC 692; 1997 (2) MLJ(SC) 33; 1997(1) SCALE 428; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 220; 1997 (1) SLR 525; 1997 (2) Supreme 116

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 341 and 342 - Limitation Act, 1908 - Proof of belonging to Scheduled Tribe - Caste of a candidate registered in school admission register as 'Hindu Reddi' - But subsequently interpolated as 'Konda Reddy' in different ink - Moreover his father despite of being in State Service was not availing of benefit of reservation - Whether candidate established his status as scheduled tribe? - Held, could not be said so - Appeal dismissed.
