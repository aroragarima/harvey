Chaugule v Bhagwat

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 586; (2012) 5 SCC 127; 2012 (4) ALD(SC) 84; 2012 (93) ALR 665; 2012 (4) Bom.C.R. 11; JT 2012 (5) SC 185; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 628; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 874; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 1031

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 110,116,78,81,101 - Nomination Forms - Rejected - Voters List - Substitution - Sustainability - Appellant was elected to Legislative Assembly, reserved for SC candidate - Election was challenged by original petitioner who had filed 3 nomination forms which were all rejected by returning officer on ground that proposer's name was not included in voters list - Returning officer found original petitioner ineligible to contest elections as candidate - Writ petition challenging rejection was filed before HC, which allowed petition quashing order of returning officer - Order of HC, was challenged by Election Commissioner before SC, notice was issued and stay on judgment was granted - Meanwhile election were conducted and appellant was declared elected - Original petitioner pending hearing of Election Petition filed application for withdrawal of Election Petition - HC, allowed application and disposed election petitioner as withdrawn - Respondent, filed application u/s. 110(3)(c) of Act, for substitution his name as election petitioner in election petition which was withdrawn, despite fact that he had neither filed nomination paper nor contested election, on ground that u/s. 116 of Act he was entitled to continue with election petition - HC, held that on a conjoint reading of ss. 78(b), 81(1) 110(3)(c) of Act, Applicant, was entitled to be substituted - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that since this case involved withdrawal of Election Petition by Election Petitioner and was not a case of abatement or substitution on death of Respondent, s. 110 stood attracted and not s. 116, hence respondent could not be permitted to substitute - Respondent contended that s. 110(3)(c) was very clear that expression 'a person used therein meant that any person who was eligible to be a Petitioner in an Election Petition, was entitled to be substituted in place of original Election Petitioner - Whether HC, was justified in permitting respondent to substitute original petitioner even after withdrawal of petition - Held, s. 110(3) (c) permits a person, who might himself had been a Petitioner, to apply for substitution as Petitioner in place of party withdrawing, however, as had been pointed out, said expression could not be held to apply across board in all cases, but had to fit in facts of each case - Election Petition filed by original petitioner was an action in personam and, was, therefore, confined to his own situation, had it been an action in rem, HC, would have been justified in substituting Respondent in place of original Election Petitioner - Complaint in Election Petition was that nomination paper of Election Petitioner had been wrongly rejected by Returning Officer, Respondent herein, who had been substituted in place of original petitioner, did not have same interest as original petitioner and, accordingly, HC, misconstrued provisions of s. 110(3)(c) of Act in applying conditions literally, without even satisfying itself that order fit in facts of case - Grievance of original Election Petitioner was not against elected candidate, but against action of Returning Officer in rejecting his nomination paper, once Election Petitioner decided not to pursue matter, Election Petition could not have been continued by a person, as contemplated in s. 110(3)(c) of Act - Appeal allowed.
