Kalarimadathil Unni v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

22 April 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 91; AIR 1966 SC 1874; 1966 CRLJ 1509; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, s. 300(3) r/w s. 34 - Appellants conspired together to burgle safe of Base Supply Office on eve of pay-day - They decoyed Lt Commander from his house on pretext that he was wanted at Naval Base - They then covered his mouth with adhesive plaster and tied a handkerchief over plaster and plugged his nostrils with cotton wool soaked in chloroform, tied his hands and legs with rope and deposited him in a shallow drain with his own shirt put under his head as a pillow - Whether offence was murder or culpable homicide? - Held, u/cl. 3 of s. 300, emphasis is on sufficiency of injury in ordinary course of nature to cause death - Acts of appellant are covered by this clause - All acts were done after deliberation by appellants - They were of a type which required more than one person to perpetrate - That there was a common intention admits of no doubt and as cl. 3 of s. 300 views consequence of act objectively, all those who shared common intention of causing bodily injury which was sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature must be held responsible for resulting offence - Offence was murder - Appellants were rightly adjudged guilty u/ss. 302/34, IPC - Appeals dismissed.
