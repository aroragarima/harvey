High Court of Punjab and Haryana and others v Jagdev Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

D.Y. Chandrachud, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 551; AIR 2016 SC 3523; 2016 (6) AWC 6510; 2016 (150) FLR 990; JT 2016 (7) SC 409; 2016 (2) OLR 581; 2016(7) SCALE 471; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 207

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Constitution of India, 1950, Haryana Civil Services (Judicial Branch) and Haryana Superior Judicial Service Revised Pay Rules, 2003, Haryana Civil Service (Judicial Branch) and Haryana Superior Judicial Service Revised Pay Rules, 2001

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Haryana Civil Service (Judicial Branch) and Haryana Superior Judicial Service Revised Pay Rules, 2001 - Haryana Civil Services (Judicial Branch) and Haryana Superior Judicial Service Revised Pay Rules, 2003 - Suspension - Revised pay scales - Challenged - Respondent was appointed as a Civil Judge and was promoted as Addl. Civil Judge - By a notification a pay scale of Rs. 10000-325-15200 (senior scale) was allowed under the 2001 Rules - Respondent was granted revised pay scale and while opting for the revised pay scale, the respondent undertook to refund any excess payment if it was so detected and demanded subsequently - Respondent was suspended and was compulsorily retired from service - A letter for recovery was served on respondent pursuant to direction of Registrar of HC - Respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order - HC found substance in the grievance of the Respondent that the excess payment made to him towards salary and allowance prior to his retirement could not be recovered at that stage, there being no fraud or misrepresentation on his part - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, while opting for benefit of revised pay scale, respondent was clearly on notice of fact that a future re-fixation or revision may warrant an adjustment of excess payment, if any, made. In instant case officer to whom payment was made in first instance was clearly placed on notice that any payment found to have been made in excess would be required to be refunded. The officer furnished an undertaking while opting for revised pay scale. He is bound by undertaking. The judgment of the HC which set aside the action for recovery is unsustainable. SC was of view that recovery should be made in reasonable installments. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Retired employee would be bound by the undertaking given by him to adjust any excess payment made to him.
