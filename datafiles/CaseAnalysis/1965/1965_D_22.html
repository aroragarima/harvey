Â Durgadas Shirali v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1966 SC 1078; 1966 CRLJ 812; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 573

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Defence Regulations

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Defence Of India Rules, 1962, r.30 - Consitution of India, 1950, arts.32 and 359 - Whether order of Dist. Magistrate placing petitioner under Arrest was mala fide and illegal? - District Magistrate has stated that he passed order of detention after satisfying himself on reports that petitioner was Secy. of Leftist wing of Communist Party of India, Bhilwara branch and that he was likely to act in a manner prejudicial to defence of India and civil defence, India's relations with foreign powers, public safety and maintenance of public order - Held, it is not possible to accept argument that District Magistrate did not apply his mind to facts of case - Petition dismissed.
