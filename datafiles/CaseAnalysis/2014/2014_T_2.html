T. N. Godavarman Thirumulpad v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Patnaik, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 158; (2014) 6 SCC 150; AIR 2014 SC 3614; JT 2014 (4) SC 181; 2014 (3) MLJ 110; 2014(3) SCALE 566

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Afforestation, Natural Resources, Doctrine Of Public Trust, Forest (Conservation) Ordinance, 1980, Tamil Nadu Hill Stations Preservation of Trees Act

Summary: Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution - Forest Act, 1927 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 - Tamil Nadu Hill Stations Preservation of Trees Act and the Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Environment Protection - Petitioner filed PIL u/art. 32 of Constitution for and on behalf of the people living in and around the Nilgiri Forest on the Western Ghats challenging the legality and the validity of the actions of the State of Tamil Nadu, the Collector, Nilgiris District and the District Forest Officer, Gudalur and the Timber Committee represented through the Collector, Nilgiris (Respondent Nos. 2 to 5 respectively), in destroying the tropical rain forest in the Gudalur and Nilgiri areas in violation of the 1927 Act, 1980 Act and 1986 Act - This, according to the petitioner, had resulted in serious ecological imbalances affecting lives and livelihood of the people living in the State of Tamil Nadu - Petitioner alleged that the respondents have in collusion with certain vested interests allowed trespassers to encroach and enter upon the forest land for the purpose of felling trees and conversion of forest land into plantations - Interlocutory Applications have been filed seeking either general or specific directions in relation to various issues concerning the protection and improvement of environment - Whether applications filed in PIL could be allowed -

Held, State Compensatory Afforestation Fund Management and Planning Authority (State CAMPA) had been constituted for each State/Union Territory - It had a three-tier structure - The Executive Committee functions under the Chairmanship of the Principal Chief Conservator of Forests was responsible for the Annual Plan of Operation (APO) for various works planned to be undertaken during each year - The Steering Committee under the Chairmanship of Chief Secretary was responsible for approving the APO for each year - The Chief Minister was the Chairman of the Governing Body which was responsible for overall guidance and policy issues - The Ad-hoc CAMPA releases the funds to each of the State CAMPAs as per the approved APO - At present, a total sum of Rs.1000 crore was permitted to be released to the State per year - The State- wise accounts of the principal amounts and cumulative interest be maintained by the Ad-hoc CAMPA - The funds were not permitted to be utilized for any purpose other than those authorized by the Court - The administrative expenses of CAMPA were incurred by the CEC - With the establishment of the Ad-hoc CAMPA, huge sums of money have accumulated which could be released to the State CAMPA for utilization, for protection and for the improvement of the national environment - Applications disposed of.
