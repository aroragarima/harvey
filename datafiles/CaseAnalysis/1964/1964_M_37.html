Â Gulraj Singh v Mota Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 125; AIR 1965 SC 608; 1964 (66) PLR 746; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 205

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Devolution Of Property, Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Family and Personal - Women and Children - Punjab Pre-emption Act , 1913, s. 15(2)(b) - Meaning of son or daughter - Legitimate or illegitimate children - Held, only legitimate son or legitimate daughter of the female vendor - Does not include illegitimate son or daughter - In the absence of an express provision to the contrary the normal rule of construction of the words 'child' 'son' or 'daughter' occurring in a statute is to include only legitimate children -Born in wedlock - Appeal dismissed.
