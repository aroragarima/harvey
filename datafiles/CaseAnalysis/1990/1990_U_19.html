Syed Farooq Mohammad v Union Of India And Anr

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 251; (1990) SCC (Cr) 500; (1990) 3 SCC 537; AIR 1990 SC 1597; 1990 (2) Crimes 619; 1990 CRLJ 1622; 1990 CrLR(SC) 432; JT 1990 (3) SC 102; 1990(1) SCALE 205; [1990] 3 S.C.R. 240; 1990 (2) UJ 395

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Illicit Traffic In Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1988, ss. 3(1), 7 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 22(5) and 166 - Drugs - Offence of transporting contraband goods - (A) Detention of owner of car - Challenged - Validity of order in light of ancillary issues - Statement of drivers u/s. 108 of Customs Act - (B) Delay - Proximate grounds - Impounding of cars having Brown Sugar - Next day, statements of drivers recorded - Next three months, reports of samples received - Detention order passed five months after cars impounded - Delay, if rendered detention illegal - (C) Supply of grounds - Bail application filed by detenu and order thereon not considered by detaining authority - Constitutionality - (D) Detenu appearing before customs authorities and applying for recording statements - Order of detention served on detenu after statement was recorded - Held, (A) Order of detention could not be challenged as illegal - Further held, (B) Not illegal and it could be said that order was made with promptitude - (C) Issue of prejudice does not arise for non-supply of that document to detenu - (D) On facts order was not illegal - It cannot be held that delay was not explained and link between grounds of detention and purpose of detention had been snapped - Could not be considered as there was also no allegation of mala fide etc against detaining authority - Petition dismissed.
