Commander, Headquarters, Calcutta And Others v Capt. Biplabendra Chanda

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1630; (1997) 1 SCC 208; (1997) SCC (L&S) 444; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 678; AIR 1997 SC 2607; 1997 (1) ESC 55; 1998 (78) FLR 548; JT 1997 (10) SC 371; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 242; 1997 LabIC 2646; 1997 (1) LLN 125; 1996(8) SCALE 66; 1997 (1) SLJ 143; 1997 (1) SLR 22; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 472; 1996 (8) Supreme 633

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Conditions of service - Change of - Power of the employer - Whether judgment of a Division of Bench of Calcutta High Court dismissing writ appeal preferred by appellants is correct? - It is open to State or to Corporation, as case may be, to change conditions of service ultimately - Terminal benefits as well as pensionary benefits constitute conditions of service - Employer has undoubted power to revise salaries and/or pay scales as also terminal benefits/pensionary benefits - Power to specify a date from which revision of pay scales or terminal benefits/pensionary benefits, as case may be, shall take effect is a concomitant of said power - So long as such date is specified in a reasonable manner, i.e., without bringing about a discrimination between similarly situated persons, no interference is called for by court in that behalf - Power of State to specify a date with effect from which Regulations framed, or amended, as case may be, shall come into force is unquestioned - A date can be specified both prospectively as well as retrospectively - Since prescription of date in this case is neither arbitrary nor unreasonable, complaint of discrimination must fail - Judgment of Division Bench of HC affirming decision of Learned Single Judge is set aside - Writ petition filed by respondent is dismissed - Appeal allowed.
