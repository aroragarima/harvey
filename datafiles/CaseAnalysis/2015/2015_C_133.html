Cargill India Private Limited v Commissioner of Customs and Central Excise, Visakhapatnam-II

Supreme Court of India

27 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 960; 2015 (325) E.L.T. 801; JT 2015 (11) SC 165; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 917

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Duty Drawback, Circular, Conversion, Shipping Bills, Benefit of Duty DrawBack, Non-Conversion

Summary: (A) Customs - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 - Customs Act, 1962 - Customs, Central Excise Duties and Service Tax Drawback Rules, 1995, r. 12(1)(a) - Shipping Bills - Duty Drawback - Conversion - Appellant had filed shipping bills for export of Soyabean meal, and while shipping bills, appellant did not claim any duty drawback - Appellant, instead, filed free shipping bills into drawback shipping bills - Appellant submitted application for conversion of said free shipping bills into drawback shipping bills, and also referred to circular which stated that competent authority could exercise his discretion as to giving duty drawback even without conversion - Application of appellant was rejected by competent authority - Whether appellant was entitled to claim conversion of free shipping bills into drawback shipping bills on basis of r. 12(1)(a) of the 1995 Rules.

Held, reading of r. 12(1)(a) of the 1995 Rules demonstrates that conversion is permissible only when exporter is able to satisfy Commissioner that 'for reasons beyond his control' drawback was not claimed. In instant case, no case was made out by appellant to suggest that claim for duty drawback was beyond control of appellant. Thus, appellant was not entitled to claim conversion of free shipping bills into drawback shipping bills. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Customs - Central Excise Act, 1944, s. 35 - Customs Act, 1962 - Customs, Central Excise Duties and Service Tax Drawback Rules, 1995, r. 12(1)(a) - Circular - Benefit of Duty DrawBack - Non-Conversion - Whether appellant was entitled to benefit of duty drawback, on strength of Circular, even without seeking conversion.

Held, said Circular provisions describes that it was permissible for competent authority to examine and consider individual requests on merits, and relaxation shall only apply in respect of drawback claims pertaining to All Industry Rates of drawback and it would not apply to brand rate of duty drawback, where rate is claimed in terms of rr. 6, 7 of the Rules. It is not in dispute that appellant wanted only 'All Industry Rates of duty drawback'. Thus, provisions of Circular would be applicable to instant case, and thereby, matter is remitted back to Competent authority for discretion. Appeals disposed of.
