Mahatma Phule Agricultural University and Others v Nasik Zilla Sheth Kamgar Union and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20462; (2001) 7 SCC 346; (2001) SCC (L&S) 1180; AIR 2001 SC 3228; 2001 (3) CLR 4; 2001 (90) FLR 761; JT 2001 (5) SC 623; 2001 (34) LabIC 3275; 2001 (2) LLJ 809; 2001(4) SCALE 482; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1089; 2001 (3) SLR 565; 2001 (5) SLT 401; 2001 (5) Supreme 256; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2286

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 12(5), 10(1)(d) and 33-C(2) - Maharashtra Agricultural Universities (Krishi Vidyapeeth) Act, 1983 - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Workers are daily wagers working in Agricultural Universities - Claimed permanent status - Denied - It was directed that amount of basic starting wages and dearness allowance, payable to a confirmed permanent workman of same or similar grade and category as his monthly wages was to paid to them - Whether challenge before High Court a challenge to computation of sum of Rs. 4, 16, 97, 937.98 and other to various orders passed in complaints under Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 is right? - If employees so covered may not be entitled to status of a permanent employees, cannot be deprived of privileges and benefits of permanent employee as envisaged by item 6 of sch. IV - Therefore entitled to wages and other benefits applicable to permanent employees - Held, these Universities are imparting education - For purpose of education it is necessary for them to maintain agricultural fields and to carry on experiments for that they required daily wagers - As daily wagers were required State Govt. cannot say that they would not pay daily wagers what is due to them - Respondent directed to clear all dues of workers who are eligible and covered by various complaints within a period of six weeks from today - Amount due and payable to workers shall carry an interest at rate of 6% from date of order of Industrial Court - Appeals disposed of.
