Lalith Mathur v L. Maheswara Rao

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2149; (2000) 10 SCC 285

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 215 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Winding up - Cease of employment - Alternative service - Respondent was employee of State Cooperative Rice Federation which was wound up and he ceased to be employee - Respondent filed writ petition seeking reliefs, inter alia, that his representation for absorption in alternative Govt. service may be directed to be considered by State Govt., which was allowed by HC - State Govt. considered representation and rejected claim of respondent for absorption in Govt. service - Aggrieved respondent challenged said order by filing contempt petition, which was allowed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, above will show that HC has directed State Govt. to absorb respondent against suitable post either in Govt. department or in any public sector undertaking. This order, in Courtâs opinion, is wholly without jurisdiction and could not have been made in proceedings under 1971 Act or u/art.215 of Constitution. HC in writ petition had issued direction for consideration of respondent's representation by State Govt. This direction was carried out by State Govt. which had considered and rejected representation on merits. Instead of challenging that order in fresh writ petition, respondent took recourse to contempt proceedings which did not lie as order had already been complied with by State Govt. which had considered representation and rejected it on merits. For reasons stated, judgment of HC is set aside and contempt petition filed by respondent is dismissed. Court makes it clear that it will be open to respondent to challenge order by which his representation was dismissed on merits, in such proceedings as he may be advised. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - If a party instead of challenging aggrieved order in fresh writ petition took recourse to contempt proceedings which did not lie order had already been complied with by opposite party/State, he is not liable to get any relief on such contempt petition.
