Avinash Nagra v Navodaya Vidyalaya Samiti Etc

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 336; (1997) 2 SCC 534; (1997) SCC (L&S) 565; 1997 (2) AWC 673; 2000 (3) CLR 22; 1997 (75) FLR 86; JT 1996 (10) SC 461; 1997 (2) LLJ 640; 1996(8) SCALE 319; 1997 (1) SLR 270; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 105

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination - Validity of - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 45, 51 and 51 A - Whether order terminating services of appellant in terms of his appointment letter is in violation of principles of natural justice? - Held, Director being highest authority, institution has power to take decisions - Decision of not conducting any enquiry exposing students and modesty of girl and to terminate services of appellant by giving one month's salary and allowances in lieu of notice was justified - Dispensing with regular enquiry under rules and denial of cross-examination are legal and not vitiated by violation of principles of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
