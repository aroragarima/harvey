Jeffrey J. Diermeier and another v State of West Bengal and another

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 410; (2010) 6 SCC 243; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2343; 2010 (3) JCC 2093; JT 2010 (5) SC 278; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 435; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 183; 2010(5) SCALE 695; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Institute of Chartered Financial Analysts of India University, Tripura Act, 2004

Summary: (A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Scope and ambit of inherent powers of the HC u/s. 482 of the CrPC - Held, s. 482 itself envisages three circumstances under which the inherent jurisdiction may be exercised, namely, (i) to give effect to an order under the Code; (ii) to prevent abuse of process of Court; and (iii) to otherwise secure the ends of justice - Power possessed by the HC under the said provision is very wide but is not unlimited - It has to be exercised sparingly, carefully and cautiously, ex debito justitiae to do real and substantial justice for which alone the court exists - Inherent jurisdiction does not confer an arbitrary power on the HC to act according to whim or caprice - Power exists to prevent abuse of authority and not to produce injustice - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 499 and 500 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Institute of Chartered Financial Analysts of India University, Tripura Act, 2004 - Defamation - Quashing of complaint - On being approached by the Institute of Chartered Financial Analysts of India (ICFAI), respondent no.2, Chartered Financial Analysts Institute (CFA Institute) entered into a licence agreement with them to conduct its CFA program in India - However, realising that respondent No.2 was not adhering to the required standards and quality in the said program, CFA Institute issued a notice of termination of licence with the respondent no. 2 - CFA Institute filed a suit for permanent injunction restraining respondent No.2 from using the trade marks, services, service marks or trade name CFA, Chartered Financial Analyst - Interim injunction was granted - However, respondent no.2, through its sponsored University issued an advertisement inviting applications for fresh enrolments for award of "CFA" certification - CFA Institute issued a public notice as "Word of Caution" stating continued unauthorized use of appellant's trademark through the sponsored Universities - Alleging that the said public notice was defamatory within the meaning of Section 499 of the IPC, respondent No.2 filed a private complaint against the appellant - It was alleged that CFA Institute issued the offending "Word of Caution" wherein they (1) deliberately and consciously did not publish the full text of the interim injunction granted by the HC against respondent no.2 and did not mention that order was with a rider that the said order will not come into effect till the end of the current academic session of CFA programme run by the society and (2) the defamatory advertisement portrays that the designation given by CFA Institute is the only valid designation and the CFA certificate given by the society is not valid - Trial court took cognizance of the complaint and issued summons to the appellants - Appellants filed petition seeking quashing of the complaint, which was dismissed - Hence, the present appeal - (A) Whether the allegations in the complaint constitute an offence of "defamation" as defined in s. 499 of the IPC and would attract the penal consequences envisaged in s. 500 of the IPC? - Held, to constitute "defamation" u/s. 499 of the IPC, there must be an imputation and such imputation must have been made with intention of harming or knowing or having reason to believe that it will harm the reputation of the person about whom it is made - In essence, the offence of defamation is the harm caused to the reputation of a person - In present case, it will neither be possible nor appropriate for SC to comment on the allegations levelled by respondent no.2 and record a final opinion whether these allegations do constitute defamation - (B) Whether Tenth Exception would be attracted in the instant case? - Held, to bring a case within the scope of the Tenth Exception, it must be proved that statement/publication was intended in "good faith" to convey a caution to one person against another; that such caution was intended for the good of the person to whom it was conveyed, or of such person in whom that person was interested, or for the "public good" - Where to the charge of defamation u/s. 500 IPC, the accused invokes the aid of Tenth Exception to Section 499 IPC, "good faith" and "public good" have both to be established by him - In the present case, in the absence of any evidence on record, it is difficult to return a finding whether or not the appellants satisfied the requirements of "good faith" and "public good" so as to fall within the ambit of the Tenth Exception to s. 499 of IPC - Reading the complaint as a whole, it is difficult to hold that a case for quashing of the complaint u/s. 482 of the CrPC was made out - HC was right in refusing to quash the complaint u/s. 500 of IPC - Appeal dismissed
