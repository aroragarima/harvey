Commissioner of Central Excise, Bangalore v M/s. Escorts Mahle Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 443; (2003) 4 SCC 758; AIR 2003 SC 2234; 2003 (108) ECR 631; 2003 (154) E.L.T. 321; JT 2003 (4) SC 547; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 746; 2003 (56) RLT 245; 2003(4) SCALE 544; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 1205; 2003 (3) SLT 536; 2003 (3) Supreme 725

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excise & Salt Act, 1944 - s. 35(G) - Central Excise Rules - r. 57A - Whether on the facts and in the circumstances of the case the appellate Tribunal is right in law in holding that the applicants are not eligible to MODVAT Credit in respect of Ramming Mass, Fibre glass and filter mesh used in or in relation to the manufacture of pistons on the ground that they are covered under Proviso to r. 57A of the Central Excise Rules. - Use of such chemicals is essential for the manufacturing process - held, the assesses are entitled to MODVAT Credit on Ramming Mass, Fibre glass and filter mesh.
