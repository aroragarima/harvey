R. Lakshmi Narayan v Santhi

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19862; (2001) 4 SCC 688; AIR 2001 SC 2110; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 40; 2001 (45) ALR 515; 2002 (4) AWC 2932; 2001 (1) JCC 337; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 213; 2001 (129) PLR 34; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 192; 2001(3) SCALE 579B; 2001 (4) SCJ 533; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 329; 2001 (3) SLT 749; 2002 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 631; 2001 (5) Supreme 520; 2001 (2) UC 145

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Hindu Marriage Act, 1995

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1995, ss. 5(ii) and 12(1) - Marriage - Divorce - Whether High Court justified in dismissing first appeal order and restoration of trial court judgment that marriage is not null and void u/ss. 12(1) and 5(ii)? - Held, it is not case of appellant that respondent was incapable of giving valid consent to marriage in consequence of unsoundness of mind at time of marriage - From facts found by appellate court it cannot be held that respondent has been suffering from a mental disorder of such a kind or to such an extent as to be unfit for marriage and procreation of children - HC cannot be faulted for having dismissed petition filed by appellant u/s. 12(1)(b) r/w s. 5(ii)(b) - Appeal dismissed.
