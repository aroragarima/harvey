Shamima Farooqui v Shahid Khan

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 240; (2015) 5 SCC 705; AIR 2015 SC 2025; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2046; 2015 CRLJ 2551; 2015 (3) CTC 80; 2015 (2) JCC 1285; JT 2015 (3) SC 576; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1615; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 237; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 628; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 526; 2015 (2) RLW 1769; 2015(4) SCALE 521; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 137; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 181

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Maintenance allowance - Appellant filed application u/s. 125 of CrPC for grant of maintenance at rate of Rs.4,000 p.m and after hearing both parties, Family Court directed respondent to pay appellant Rs.2,500 till date of judgment and thereafter Rs.4,000 per month from date of judgment till date of remarriage of appellant - However, on revision, HC modified order of Family Court by reducing maintenance allowance to Rs.2000 from 1-4-2012 till remarriage of appellant and hence, instant appeal by appellant - Whether impugned order is sustainable in law.

Held, order of Family Court is not manifestly perverse and there is nothing perceptible which would show that order is a sanctuary of errors. When substantial justice has been done, there was no reason to interfere. There could be shelter over her head in the parental house, but other real expenses could not be ignored. Solely because the husband had retired, there was no justification to reduce the maintenance by 50% and it only reflects the non-application of mind and hence, impugned order is not sustainable and so the same is set aside and orders of Family Court are restored. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - If the husband is healthy, able bodied and is in a position to support himself, then he is under legal obligation to support his wife, for wife's right to receive maintenance u/s. 125 of CrPC, unless disqualified, is an absolute right.
