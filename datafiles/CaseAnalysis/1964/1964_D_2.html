Â B. Basavalingappa v D. Munichinnappa

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 332; AIR 1965 SC 1269; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 316

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Socio - Economic - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 341 -The President's order of 1950 spells 'Bhovi' caste as 'Boyi' - Whether variation of spelling used will affect identity of caste and its categorization ? - Held, though same caste could be spelled differently at different places with due knowledge to Govt. it shall not affect categorization of established caste in a particular region - (B) Whether categorization of a caste can be established through admissibility of evidence ? - Further held, only in order to find out whether different spellings meant a particular established caste or not evidence like various census reports were considered in this case - Evidence was opened taking into facts of this case - Circumstances - It is not open for parties to provide evidence for challenging existence of a caste already included in an order already passed - Appeal dismissed.
