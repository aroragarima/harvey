Myla Venkateswarlu v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 588; (2012) 5 SCC 226; 2013 (1) CGLJ 426; 2012 CRLJ 2262; 2012 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 121; JT 2012 (4) SC 111; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 675; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 72; 2012 (2) RLW 1706; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 75

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 8(c),20(b)(i),41,42,50 - Recovery of Ganja - Violation of Procedure - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant-accused was convicted for offence u/ss. 8(c) r/w. s. 20(b)(i) of Act - Prosecution case was that on receiving reliable information about illegal sale of Ganja when police searched appellant and other accused they were found carrying Ganja packets - Trial Court convinced by evidence led by prosecution convicted appellant - Appeal preferred before HC, was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that there was violation of procedure contemplated u/s. 50 of Act - State contended that when appellant and other accused were stopped they removed Ganja packets from their pockets - Whether Trial Court and HC, were justified in convicting appellant - story that accused themselves took out Ganja packets from their pockets runs contrary to version of PW-1 and PW-2 and, therefore, it does not inspire confidence, if accused voluntarily took out Ganja packets, there was no question of conducting search in presence of a gazetted officer or a Magistrate, but, assuming that this right could be communicated to a suspect after seizure and assuming evidence of this witness to be true, it still does not indicate that requirement of s. 50(1) of Act was fulfilled - There was no clear communication to accused that they had a right to be searched in presence of a gazetted officer or a Magistrate, as it was noted, concept of substantial compliance could not be read into provisions of s. 50(1) of Act - Therefore, SC, had no hesitation in concluded that in this case, there was a breach of s. 50(1) of Act, since conviction of appellant was solely based on possession of Ganja recovered from him, set aside conviction - Appeal disposed of.
