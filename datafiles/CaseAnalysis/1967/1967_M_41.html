Messrs Killick Nixon v Commissioner of

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 232; AIR 1968 SC 9; [1967] 66 ITR 714; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 971

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Indian Income-tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - A/Y 1949-50 - Income on trading activity - Claim of exemption - Entitlement of - Assessee/firm was carrying diverse trading activities - By agreement dt.29-1-1948 assessee agreed to sell to 'K' goodwill of business of assessee freehold and leasehold hereditaments, plant and machinery, stock in trade and book debts, Govt. securities and shares and full benefit of all shipping and general agencies, distributorships etc. in consideration of 9,996 shares in the Vendee Company of the face value of Rs. 100/each and Rs. 400/ in cash - Assessee was dissolved and its business was discontinued w.e.f.1-2-1948 - In a proceeding for assessment to tax payable by assessee for A/Y 1949-50, Income-tax Officer assessed capital gains made by assessee, on the transfer of its capital assets to 2Companies - On appeal, Appellate Asst. Commissioner modified order and rejected claim of assessee to the benefit of s. 25 (3) & (4) of the Act - On appeal, Appellate Tribunal confirmed order passed by Appellate Asst. Commissioner - On reference, HC answered first question in the negative, and the second, third and fourth questions in the affirmative - Aggrieved by that instant appeals were filed - Whether income which was earned by trading activity was entitled to exemption u/s. 25 (3) of the Act - Held, there was no dispute in instant case about market value at the date of transfer of assets conveyed - First proviso therefore did not come into play - Dispute related to value to assessee on 1-1-1939 of three assets', the managing agencies, 240 shares of the Cement Agencies Ltd. and goodwill - Capital gain or loss had to be determined by deducting from market value of asset on 1-2-1948 the fair market value of those assets oil 1-1-1939, proved by assessee to the satisfaction of Income-tax Officer - Appellate Assistant Commissioner estimated value of the 3assets on 1-1-1939 at Rs. 51,40,802/- - It was true that onus lay upon assessee to prove fair market value of assets on 1-1-1939 to the satisfaction of Income-tax Officer and therefore of Tribunal - Tribunal did not consider evidence and disposed of claim of assessee after observing that value of assets could not exceed amount at which it was estimated by Appellate Asst. Commissioner - Under scheme of the Act, Tribunal was the final authority on questions of fact - Tribunal in deciding an appeal was bound to consider all the evidence, and argumerits raised before it by tile parties - Tribunal apparently did not consider the evidence - It merely recorded a bare conclusion without setting out any reasons in support thereof - It was therefore not possible to say whether Tribunal considered evidence and contentions raised by assessee, it could not be assumed merely because a conclusion was recorded that Tribunal considered evidence - Therefore, HC was right in recording an answer in affirmative on the fourth question - It would be the duty of Tribunal in disposing of the appeal u/s. 66 (5) of the Act to hear parties and to determine on a consideration of evidence value of 3assets on 1-1-1939 in the light of third proviso to s. 12B (2) - SC viewed that capital gains though they were income within meaning of s. 2(6C) as incorporated by Act 7 of 1939, and modified by Act 22 of 1947, were not income earned from trading activity carried on by an assessee, and therefore could not be admitted to exemption u/s. 25 (31) of the Act - It was only income which was earned by carrying on business which was entitled to exemption u/s. 25 (3) and capital gains not being income which arise from trading activity, they were not entitled to exemption - Appeals dismissed.
