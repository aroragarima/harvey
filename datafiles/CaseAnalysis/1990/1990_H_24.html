J. K. Aggarwal v Haryana Seeds Development Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 953; (1991) 2 SCC 283; (1991) SCC (L&S) 483; AIR 1991 SC 1221; 1991 (16) ATC 480; 1991 (1) CLR 988; JT 1993 (5) SC 191; 1991 LabIC 1008; 1991 (2) LLJ 412; 1991(1) SCALE 488; 1991 (3) SLJ 161; 1991 (5) SLR 78; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 13; 1991 (1) UJ 633

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Domestic Tribunal, Haryana Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules 1952

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 311 - Haryana Civil Services Appeal Rules, 1952, r. 7(5) - Whether denial of service of lawyer to delinquent employee amounts to denial of natural justice ? - As per r. 7(5) where charges are so serious as to entail dismissal from service, inquiry authority may permit services of a lawyer to delinquent employee - This rule vests a discretion - Decision has to be reached on a case to case basis on situational particularities and special requirements of justice of case - Where Presenting Officer was a person with legal attainments and employee had no legal background, refusal of service of a lawyer to delinquent resulted in denial of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
