Union of India and others v R. P. Singh

Supreme Court of India

22 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 380; (2014) 7 SCC 340; AIR 2014 SC 2541; 2014 (3) ALT(SC) 55; 2014 (5) AWC 4414; 2014 (143) FLR 31; 2014 (4) RLW 3620; 2014(7) SCALE 678; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 351; 2014 (6) SLR 401

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Departmental proceeding - Punishment - Challenged - Respondent was working as Assistant Engineer (Civil) with appellant department - Respondent was proceeded in departmental proceeding - Disciplinary authority called for representation from the respondent communicated inquiry report as well as opinion for disagreement requiring him to submit his explanation - Disciplinary authority imposed penalty of reduction of pay by two stages in time scale of pay of charged officer for period of two years without cumulative effect - Order was assailed before Tribunal - Tribunal negatived said stand on ground that no prejudice was caused to respondent - Respondent filed petition before HC - Petition was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, Courts/Tribunals would apply their judicial mind to the question and give their reasons for setting aside or not setting aside order of punishment - It was only if Court/Tribunal found that furnishing of report could have made difference to result in case then it should set aside order of punishment - Where after following said procedure Court/Tribunal sets aside order of punishment, proper relief that should be granted to direct reinstatement of employee with liberty to authority/ management to proceed with enquiry, by placing employee under suspension and continuing enquiry from that stage of furnishing with report - Question whether employee would be entitled to back wages and other benefits from date of dismissal to date of reinstatement, if ultimately ordered, should invariably left to be decided by authority concerned according to law, after culmination of proceedings and depending on final outcome - In instant case it was opined by Constitution Bench that non-supply of enquiry report was breach of principle of natural justice - Advice from UPSC, when utilized as material against delinquent officer, it should be supplied in advance - Said stage was in prevalence before decision of Constitution Bench - After said decision, authority should have clarified Rule regarding development in service jurisprudence - In view taken from Union Of India & Others v S.K. Kapoor 2011 Indlaw SCO 241, Government of India, Ministry of Personnel, PG & Pensions, Department of Personnel & Training vide Office Memorandum dt. 6-1- 2014 was issued with directions - Court opined that both Office Memoranda were not only in consonance with Union Of India & Others v S.K. Kapoor 2011 Indlaw SCO 241 but also in accordance with principles of natural justice which was stated in Managing Director ECIL Hyderabad Etc. v B. Karunakar Etc. 1993 Indlaw SC 430 - Hence, there was no reason to interfere with judgment and order of HC - Appeal dismissed.
