Air India v Nergesh Meerza and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A. Varadarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 165; (1981) 4 SCC 335; (1981) SCC (L&S) 599; AIR 1981 SC 1829; 1981 LabIC 1313; 1981 (2) LLJ 314; 1981 (2) LLN 572; 1981(3) SCALE 1275; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 438; 1981 (2) SLJ 349; 1982 (1) SLR 117

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Air Corporations Act, 1953 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Equal Remuneration Act, 1976 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Maternity Benefit Act, 1961 - Air India Employees Service Regulations - Questions of constitutionality of regns. 46(1) (c), 47 of the Regulations involved in the instant case - Regn. 46 provided for the retirement age of Air India employees and regn. 47 provided for extension of the employee beyond the age of retirement - It was alleged that the provisions of the disputed regns. were affecting the constitutional rights of the Air Hostesses - Whether, the disputed regns. were in fact affecting the constitutional rights of the Air Hostesses and what remedy they should be provided with - Held, after detailed discussion on provisions of the disputed regns. with regard to the constitutional provisions, instant court had revised the provisions of those regns. - Regn. 46 (i) (c) was amended so as to substitute for the words 'or a first pregnancy', the words 'or on a third pregnancy' - And it was ordered that so long as the Rule of Indian Airlines Corporation was not amended the General Manager would continue to extend the age of retirement of Indian Airlines Corporation Air Hostess upto 45 years subject to their being found medically fit - Petition partly allowed.
