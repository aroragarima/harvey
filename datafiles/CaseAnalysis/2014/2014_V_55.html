Vishal Goyal and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 298; (2014) 11 SCC 456; 2014 (5) AWC 4422; JT 2014 (7) SC 200; 2014(5) SCALE 706; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 606; 2014 (4) SLR 552

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Admission in Post graduate course

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Admission in Post graduate course - Maintainability - National Board of Examinations issued two Information Bulletins for Post Graduate Entrance Test, 2014 for admissions to State Quota seats in Government colleges and institutions and Government Quota seats in private colleges/institutions/deemed universities - One Bulletin contained all information for admission to MD/MS/Medical Post Graduate Diploma Courses (Medical) and other contained all information for admission to MDS/PG Diploma Courses (Dental) - Cl. 2 of these Information Bulletins laid down criteria for PGET-2014 and these Information Bulletins for PGET-2014 provided that no candidate should be admitted to professional educational institution unless candidate possesses qualifications or eligibility to appear for entrance test stipulated thereunder - Petitioner filed instant petitions related to admissions in Post Graduate Medical Dental courses in Government medical and dental colleges as well as in State Quota in private medical and dental colleges -

Held, scheme should not apply to private medical and dental colleges or institutions unless they were instrumentalities or agencies of State or opt to join scheme - Reason was that private medical and dental colleges or institutions not being State or its instrumentalities or its agencies were not subject to equality clauses in art. 14 of the Constitution, however moment some seats in private medical and dental colleges or institutions came to State quota, which have to be filled up by State or its instrumentality or its agency which were subject to equality clauses in art. 14 of the Constitution - It was declared in regards to two Information Bulletins for post graduate medical and dental courses for PGET-2014 as ultra-vires art. 14 of the Constitution and null and void - Respondent would now publish fresh Information Bulletins and do admissions to post graduate medical and dental courses in Government colleges as well as State quota of private colleges in accordance with law by the end of June, 2014 on basis of results of Entrance Test already held - Dr. Pradeep Jain Etc. v Union Of India And Ors. Etc. 1984 Indlaw SC 72, followed on - Petitions allowed.
