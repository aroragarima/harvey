National and Grindlays Bank Limited v Municipal Corporation of Greaterbombay

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 264; (1969) 1 SCC 541; AIR 1969 SC 1048; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Municipal Tax, Levied

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, ss. 146(2), 167, 217 - Municipal tax - Property tax - Levied - Legality - Appellant was the sole trustee of late Mr. FED and in that capacity was the owner of a plot of land - Said plot of land was leased by the former trustee of the estate to lessee - Lessee had constructed at his own cost a tiled house on the said plot of land - M area merged into Greater B - Upto the date of the merger the District Municipality was assessing and levying taxes on the land and the structure separately and recovering the same from the landlord and the tenant - After the merger, the Municipal Corporation issued a notice to the appellant u/s. 167 of the Act informing him that the assessment book had been amended by inserting, the name of appellant and that the rateable value of the house had been fixed at Rs. 430/- - Aggrieved appellant preferred an appeal u/s. 217 of the Act before the Small Causes Court, appeal was dismissed - On further appeal, HC dismissed the appeal - Later another appeal was preferred and the same was also dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the primary liability was imposed on appellant under the 1888 Act to pay property taxes to respondent in respect of land-owned by appellant and let on a monthly basis to a third party who has constructed a building thereon - Held, SC Should, however , assume in favour of appellant that the meaning of s. 146 (2) of the Act was obscure and that it was possible to interpret it as throwing the primary liability for payment of property tax upon the lessee who has constructed a, building on the land - Even upon that assumption the view of the law expressed by the HC in instant case ought not to be interfered with - In a case where the meaning of an enactment was obscure, the Court might resort to contemporary construction, that was the construction which the authorities have put upon it by their usage and conduct for a long period of time - In SC's opinion, the principle of contemporanea exposito applied to instant case - Act was passed in the year 1888 and there appeared to be a practice followed by the corporation for a very long time of treating the land and the building constructed upon it as single unit and charging the property tax upon the owner of the land in a case where the land was let for a period of less than one year to a tenant who has constructed a building thereon - Appeal dismissed.
