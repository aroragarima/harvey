South Konkan Distilleries and Another v Prabhakar Gajanan Naik and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1493; (2008) 14 SCC 632; AIR 2009 SC 1177; 2009 (1) ALD(SC) 1; 2008 (73) ALR 638; 2008 (4) AWC 4214; 2008 (6) Bom.C.R. 582; 2009 (1) CGLJ 350; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 33; JT 2008 (10) SC 405; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1137; 2008 (152) PLR 703; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 513; 2008 (2) RCR(Rent) 360; 2009 (106) RD 134; 2008(12) SCALE 481; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 295; 2009 (1) UC 11

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Plaint In a Suit

Summary: Suit for dissolution of partnership - Trial court rejected the application for amendment of written statement and the counter claim of the defendants/appellants - Appeal against - Held, courts generally, as a rule, decline to allow amendments, if a fresh suit on the amended claim would be barred by limitation on the date of filing of the application - But that would be a factor to be taken into account in the exercise of the discretion as to whether the amendment should be ordered, and does not affect the power of the Court to order it, if that is required in the interest of justice - By way of an amendment, the appellants are now completely making out a new case by alleging that the appellants were incurring damages on continuous basis, which is contrary to the pleadings made in the written statement - Appeal dismissed.
