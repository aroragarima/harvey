Â Madhya Pradesh Special Police Establishment v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

05 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh, H.K. Sema, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 998; (2004) 8 SCC 788; AIR 2005 SC 325; 2005 (1) ALD(SC) 57; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 98; 2004 (3) KLT 856; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 171; 2004 (4) RCR(Criminal) 963; 2004(9) SCALE 302; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 1020

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Prevention of Corruption Act, 1983, Jammu and Kashmir Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Act, 1988, Special Court Act, 1979, Madhya Pradesh Special Police Establishment Act, The Special Courts Act, 1979

Summary: Criminal - Corruption - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1983, ss. 13(1)(d) r/w 13(2) - IPC, 1860, s. 120B - Sanction for prosecuting two Ministers - Whether a Governor can act in his discretion and against aid and advice of Council of Ministers in a matter of grant of sanction for prosecution of Ministers for offences under Prevention of Corruption Act and/or under IPC? - Held, if Governor cannot act in his own discretion, there would be a complete breakdown of rule of law inasmuch as it would then be open for Govts. to refuse sanction in spite of overwhelming material showing that a prima-facie case is made out - If, in cases where prima-facie case is clearly made out, sanction to prosecute high functionaries is refused or withheld, democracy itself will be at stake - It would then lead to a situation where people in power may break law with impunity safe in knowledge that they will not be prosecuted as requisite sanction will not be granted - Order of Governor sanctioning prosecution should be given effect to and that of Council of Ministers refusing to do so may be set aside - Appeals allowed.
