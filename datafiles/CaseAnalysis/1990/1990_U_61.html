M. Ahamedkutty v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 681; (1990) 2 SCC 1; (1990) SCC (Cr) 258; 1990 (3) Crimes 273; 1990 CRLR 202; 1990 CrLR(SC) 202; 1990 (27) ECR 164; 1990 (47) E.L.T. 188; JT 1990 (1) SC 143; 1990(1) SCALE 108; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 209

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Import and Export Control Act, 1947 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.22(5) and 226 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 108 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 3(1)(i) and 9(1) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - (A) Delay between date of detention and date of passing detention order - Effect thereof - Held, where seemingly long time taken for passing detention order after prejudicial act is result of full and detailed investigation and consideration of facts of case, ground cannot be held to be remote and detention cannot be held to be bad on that ground - Courts should not merely on account of delay in making of an order of detention assume that such delay, if not satisfactorily explained, must necessarily give rise to an inference that there was no sufficient material for subjective satisfaction of detaining authority or that such subjective satisfaction was not genuinely reached - Held, order of detention was not rendered invalid thereby - (B) Delay in execution of detention order - Effect thereof - There must be a 'live and proximate link' between grounds of detention alleged by detaining authority and avowed purpose of detention, namely, prevention of smuggling activities; in appropriate cases it could be assumed that link was snapped if there was a long and unexplained delay between date of order of detention and arrest of detenu - In such a case order of detention could be struck down unless grounds indicated a fresh application of mind of detaining authority to new situation and changed circumstances, but where delay is not only adequately explained but also is found to be result of detenu's recalcitrant or refractory conduct in evading arrest, there is warrant to consider 'link' not snapped but strengthened - (C) Whether non-supply of bail application and bail order, violates art. 22(9) of Constitution? - Bail application and bail order were vital materials for consideration - Held, it amounted to denial of detenu's right to make an effective representation and it resulted in violation of art. 22(5) rendering continued detention of detenu illegal and entitling detenu to be set at liberty - Appeal allowed.
