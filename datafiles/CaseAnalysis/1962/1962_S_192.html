Â Swami Motor Transport Private Limited and Another v Sri Sankaraswamigal Mutt and Another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 489; AIR 1963 SC 864; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 146; 1964 (1) SCJ 530; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(f) and 31(1) - Madras Act XIX of 1955, ss. 3 and 9 - Act XIII of 1960 - Whether rights created by 1955 Act by extending provisions of ss. 3 and 9 of Principal Act i.e. 1921 Act to tenants had given them a right to property if so, whether 1960 Act infringes fundamental right of appellants u/art. 14? - Held, neither 1955 Act conferred any right as to superstructure u/s. 9 of Principal Act i.e. 1921 Act nor did 1960 Act take that right away - If this distinction between land and superstructure is borne in mind untenability of argument would become obvious - S. 9 of Principal Act, extended by 1955 Act, only confers a right in respect of land and not of superstructure - If that Act held field, appellants could have purchased land, but by reason of 1960 Act they could no longer do so - Neither 1955 Act conferred any right as to superstructure u/s 9 of Principal Act nor did 1960 Act take that right away - Thus, 1960 Act does not in any way affect appellants' fundamental right - Appeals dismissed.
