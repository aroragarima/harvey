The Municipal Corporation Of The City Of Ahmedabad v Ben Hiraben Manilal

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 94; (1983) 2 SCC 422; AIR 1983 SC 537; 1983 (2) GLR 1034; 1983(1) SCALE 327; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 676; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 676A; 1983 UJ 369

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949

Summary: Municipalities and Local Governments - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949 - Unauthorised construction - (A) Notice u/s.260(1)(a) of the Act - Notice served to person who was not its owner at the time of its erection - Validity - Held, it cannot be said that the notice issued by the Municipal Corporation was unauthorised or illegal, notice is still valid in view of availability of power u/s. 478 - (B) Question as to the scope and ambit of the power of the Municipal authorities u/s.260 r/w s. 478 of the Act - Even though the expressions in s.260 are not quite explicit, but if the provisions of s.260(1)(a) are read in conjunction with the latter part of the provisions of s.478 which stipulates specifically that if the person carrying out such work or doing such things is not the owner at the time of such notice, the owner at the time of giving such notice shall also be liable for carrying out the requisition of the Commissioner, makes it clear that the action for demolition or removal can be taken by the Corporation or Municipal authorities exercising power under provisions of the said Act against persons who had not themselves built the infringing portion - Appeal allowed.
