Â Maganlal Chaganlal Private Limited v Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Alagiriswami, P.N. Bhagwati, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Ray, V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 597; (1975) 1 SCC 339; AIR 1975 SC 648

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, 1888, s. 105-B - Suppression of material fact - Costs - Petition filed by appellant challenging order made u/s. 105-B of the Act was dismissed by HC inter alia on ground that provisions of Chapter V-A of the Act were not in contravention of art. 14 of Constitution - Hence, appellant filed instant appeal and Constitution Bench of instant Court which heard several appeals along with instant appeal held that Chapter V-A of the Act was not violative of art. 14 of Constitution and directed, DB to dispose of appeal - Whether costs awarded against petitioner by HC was too harsh.

Held, appellant had not suppressed deliberately any material fact from Court but costs awarded against appellant could not be reduced. Further, HC awarded costs not by way of penalty but that in the circumstances of the case the Court thought that 'the award for costs should be on the highest permissible scale. Appeal dismissed.
