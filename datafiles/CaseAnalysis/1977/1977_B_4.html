Manjusri Raha & Ors Etc v B.L. Gupta & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

09 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 200; (1977) 2 SCC 174; 1977 ACJ 134; AIR 1977 SC 1158; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 944; 1977 UJ 212

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Tort

Keywords: Cross Objection

Summary: Insurance - Carriers and Transportation - CPC, 1908, O 23, r.3 - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss.95(2)(d), 110 - Whether liability of Insurance companies with respect to compensation in case of a motor accident upto maximum of Rs. 2000 u/s. 95 (2)(d) of Motor Vehicles Act is justified? - Held, no - Value of an Indian life should be more than Rs. 2000 - It is just and fair that Legislature should make a suitable provision so as to pay adequate compensation by properly evaluating previous life of a citizen in its true perspective rather than devaluing human lives on basis of an artificial mathematical formula - Instead of limiting liability of Insurance Companies to a specified sum of money as representing value of human life, amount should be left to be determined by a Court in special circumstances of each case - Appeal partly allowed.
