Vam Organic Chemicals Limited and Another v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2288; (1997) 2 SCC 715; 1997 (2) AWC 984; JT 1997 (1) SC 625; 1997(1) SCALE 361; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 403; 1997 (1) Supreme 550

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Licence for denaturation of spirit, Levy of taxes

Summary: Excise - Administrative - Licence for denaturation of spirit - Levy of taxes - Legality - Appellants were manufacturing vinyl acetate monomer, a basic organic chemical for which industrial alcohol was main feed stock - Respondents issued impugned notification dt.18-5-1990 in exercise of powers conferred by s. 41 of the Act - S. 41 of the Act gives power to Excise Commissioner to make Rules, for regulating manufacture, supply, storage or sale of any intoxicant - Earlier Rule 2 was substituted by a new Rule 2 entitled 'Denaturation of Spirit' - Amended rule provides for a new licence for denaturation of spirit in a prescribed form to be issued by Collector to all distilleries situated within his district holding licence PD-1 or PD-2 and persons holding licences FL-16, FL-39, FL-40 and FL-41 to denature the spirit - Impugned notification prescribed that distilleries mentioned above and holding licence for denaturation of spirit should be liable to pay a denaturation fee at rate of 7 paise per litre in advance - Appellants hold licences in form of FL-39 to enable them to use industrial alcohol as main raw material for their product - Appellants were obliged to take out licence in form of DS-1 as prescribed in impugned notification and pay a licence fee at the rate of 7 paise per liter w.e.f 2-6-1990 - Appellants filed writ petition challenging the notification issued by respondents - Appellants alleged that State had no power to legislate in respect of industrial alcohol or to levy taxes in respect thereof and further that levy was not based on quid pro quo was otherwise bad - HC rejected contentions of appellants and dismissed the writ petition - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, impugned notification dt.18-5-1990 was issued to ensure that rectified spirit sought to be used for industrial purposes was not diverted for obtaining country liquor or other forms of potable liquor and that it was not concerned with equitable distribution and availability at a fair price of either rectified spirit or denatured spirit - HC opined that licence fee imposed for regulatory purposes might not carry with it any service rendered, but that such licence fee should be reasonable - It should be appropriate to look to expenditure which respondents incurred for administering regulation and if there was a broad co-relation between expenditure which State incurred and fees charged, fees would be sustained as reasonable - HC drawn a distinction between fees charged for licences, i.e., regulatory fees and fees for services rendered as compensatory fees - HC had taken view that in case of regulatory fees, like licence fees, existence of quid pro quo was not necessary although fee imposed should not be, in circumstances of case, excessive - Nature of work involved in supervising process of denaturation and consequent expenses incurred by respondents, fee of 7 paise per litre was reasonable and proper - HC was justified in passing the impugned order - Appeals dismissed.
