State Of Haryana & Anr. v Chanan Mal Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 116; (1977) 1 SCC 340; AIR 1976 SC 1654; 1980 ALJ 950; 1976 (32) FLR 353; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Punjab Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1964, Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition And Development) Amendment Act, 1957, Haryana Minerals (Vesting of Rights) Act 1973

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Mines & Minerals - Bihar Land Reforms Act, 1950 - Coal Bearing Areas (Acquisition and Development) Amendment Act, 1957 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 31 and 133 - Haryana Minerals (Vesting of Rights) Act, 1973, s. 3 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation of Development) Act, 1957, ss. 2, 3, 4-9 - Whether Haryana Act was beyond competence of State Legislature in as much as field in which this Act operated was necessarily occupied already by provisions of Central Act? - Held, Haryana Minerals (Vesting of Rights) Act, 1973, is valid, as it is not, in any way, repugnant to provisions of Mines and Minerals (Regulation of Development) Act 67 of 1957, made by Parliament, therefore ownership rights could be and have been validity acquired by Haryana Govt. under Haryana Act - Appeal allowed.
