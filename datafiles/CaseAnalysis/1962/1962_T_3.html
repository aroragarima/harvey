Â Automobile Transport(Rajasthan) Limited v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 203; AIR 1962 SC 1406; [1963] 1 S.C.R. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Pith And Substance, Companies Act, 1913, Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, Wear And Tear, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act, 1955, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954, Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Rajasthan Motor vehicles Taxation Act, 1951, ss. 4, 8 and 11 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 301 and 304(b) - Taxation laws - Interpretation of Statutes - Freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse - Imposition of tax on motor vehicles carrying passengers and goods within or through State - By State law namely Rajasthan Motor Vehicles Taxation Act - Constitutional validity of - If they come within reach of art. 301, regulatory measures or compensatory taxes, Whether restrictions on freedom of trade? - Held, interpretation of art. by considering its relation to other Parts of Constitution and valuable American and Australian decisions - Held that taxes imposed under Act, are compensatory in nature and do not hinder freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse assured by art. 301 and hence, Act does not violate provisions of that article - Appeal dismissed.
