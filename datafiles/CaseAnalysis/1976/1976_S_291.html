Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Suresh Chandra Jaipuria and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 652; (1976) 4 SCC 719; AIR 1976 SC 2621; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 10

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 39 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 41(h) - Plaintiff had purchased a house in Delhi free from all incumbrances, demands, or liabilities under sale deed, and vendor had undertaken to discharge the dues of house tax - Failure to discharge from the side of vendor - Suit for recovery of amount discharged by the vendee - Trial Court had found that demand which was being recovered from him was in respect of period subsequent to 31-3-1969, and was based on a reateable value of Rs. 37, 800/- per annum which had been provisionally adopted subject to results of proceedings in Courts of appropriate jurisdiction as to what correct basis of assessment was - Trial Judge had granted an interim injunction initially, but, after hearing parties, had vacated it on 18-10-1973, as he had found that no prima facie case was made out to grant it - On an appeal by plaintiff, Appellate Court, after considering all questions raised before it, dismissed appeal - HC, while agreeing with view of Appellate Court that balance of convenience was in favour of discharging interim injunction, held that, as there was a prima facie case that assessment had been erroneously made - Whether it is valid? - Held, appellant's contention is correct in submitting that Judge of HC had himself misapprehended law in holding that Courts below had failed to find a prima facie case because of a misconception of law - It also seems that attention of Judge was not directed towards s. 41(h) of Act which lays down that an injunction, which is a discretionary equitable relief, cannot be granted when an equally efficacious relief is obtainable in any other usual mode or proceeding except in cases of breach of trust - Appellant points out that there was ordinary machinery of appeal, u/s. 169 of Delhi Municipal Council Act, open to assessee respondent - It had not even been found that respondent was unable to deposit necessary amount before filing appeal - Appeal allowed.
