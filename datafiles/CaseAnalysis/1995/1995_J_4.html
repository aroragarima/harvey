J. Vasudevan v T.R. Dhananjaya

Supreme Court of India

08 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 998; (1995) 6 SCC 249; AIR 1996 SC 137; 1996 CRLJ 426; JT 1995 (7) SC 484; 1995(5) SCALE 245; 1996 (1) SLJ 185; 1995 (7) SLR 228; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 438

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act 1971, s.2 ; Constitution of India, art.311 - Influencing of judiciary was attempted by an I.A.S. officer who met Supreme Court Judge at his residence - He was making prayer of mercy in a matter relating to his colleague - It was proved that he had not met the Judge at instance of contemnor - Conduct of officer was most reprehensible and disapproved in strongest terms and later he realised his mistake - Supreme Court closing the matter by ordering that an entry would be made in his CCR about gross impropriety committed by him.
