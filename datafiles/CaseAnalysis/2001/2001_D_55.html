Adcon Electronics Private Limited v Daulat and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19988; (2001) 7 SCC 698; AIR 2001 SC 3712; 2002 AllCJ 192; 2001 (45) ALR 338; JT 2001 (7) SC 580; 2002 (130) PLR 678; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 806; 2001(6) SCALE 252; 2001 (3) SCJ 364; 2001 (6) SLT 534; 2003 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 286; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 30; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 29; 2001 (7) Supreme 134; 2001 (2) UC 571

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 22 - Poof Land - Whether a suit simpliciter for specific performance of contract for sale of immovable property is a 'suit for land' within cl. 12 of letters patent of High Court of judicature at Bombay? - Held, a 'suit for land' is a suit in which relief claimed relates to title to or delivery of possession of land or immovable property - In a suit for specific performance of contract for sale of immovable property containing a stipulation that on execution of sale deed possession of immovable property will be handed over to purchaser, it is implied that delivery of possession of immovable property is part of decree of specific performance of contract - No court can grant relief of possession of land or other immovable property, subject - matter of agreement for sale in regard to which specific performance is claimed, unless possession of immovable property is specifically prayed for - In instant case suit is for specific performance of agreement for sale of suit property wherein relief of delivery of suit property has not been specifically claimed, as such it cannot be treated as a 'suit for land' - Appeal dismissed.
