Hind Construction Contractors v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 285; (1979) 2 SCC 70; AIR 1979 SC 720; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 1147

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 80 - Rescission of contract - (A) Whether time was essence of contract? - Held, clauses in agreement pertaining to imposition of penalty and extension of time clearly implicate that time was never essence of contract - (B) Whether rescission of contract was wrongful and illegal? - Further held, yes - Respondent-defendant did not make time essence directing appellant-plaintiff to complete work within such period - Instead it rescinded contract by letter - Such rescission illegal and wrongful - Defendant committed breach of contract - As a result no forfeiture of security deposit - Breach of condition as to time for performance will entitle innocent party to consider breach as a repudiation of contract - Appeal allowed.
