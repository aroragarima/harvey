H. Siddiqui (Dead) By Lrs v A. Ramalingam

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 147; (2011) 4 SCC 240; AIR 2011 SC 1492; 2011 (3) ALD(SC) 147; 2011(2) ALL MR 938; 2011 (2) AWC 1704; 2011 (5) Bom.C.R. 673; 2011 (1) CLT(SC) 279; 2011 (4) CTC 343; 2011 (268) E.L.T. 436; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1429; JT 2011 (3) SC 522; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 887; 2011 (3) MPLJ 83; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 385; 2011(3) SCALE 290B; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41 r. 31 - Suit for specific performance - Plaintiff filed a suit for specific performance against defendant alleging that defendant had failed to execute sale deed after his brother, power of attorney holder, entered into an agreement for sale with plaintiff - Trial Court decreed suit in favour of plaintiff, however, HC set aside Trial Court decree in defendant's appeal - Hence instant appeal by plaintiff - Whether power of attorney had been executed by defendant in favour of his brother enabling him to alienate suit property and even if there was such power of attorney whether same had been proved in accordance with law - Held, Trial Court could not proceed in such an unwarranted manner for reason that defendant had merely admitted his signature on photocopy of power of attorney and did not admit contents thereof - Further, Trial Court should have borne in mind that admissibility of a document or contents thereof should not necessary lead to drawing any inference unless contents thereof had some probative value - HC, however, failed to realise that it was deciding First Appeal and that it had to be decided strictly in adherence with provisions contained in O. 41 r. 31 of CPC and once issue of alleged power of attorney was also raised as was evident from point (a) formulated by HC, Court should not have proceeded to point (b) without dealing with relevant issues involved in case, particularly, as to whether power of attorney had been executed by defendant in favour of his brother enabling him to alienate his share in property - Lower court orders, therefore, were set aside and matter remitted back to HC for fresh disposal - Appeal disposed of.
