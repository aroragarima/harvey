State of Punjab and Others v Brigadier Sukhjit Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 June 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1291; (1993) 3 SCC 459; AIR 1993 SCW 2560; JT 1993 (3) SC 748; 1995 (1) RCJ 183; 1994 (2) RCR(Rent) 66; 1993 (2) RentLR 183; 1993(3) SCALE 25; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 944; 1993 (2) UJ 455

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Determination of title - Heir apparent - Mandatory injunction - Double storied building situated in a complex known as Jallowkhana in Kapurthala was in possession of appellant - Respondent sought relief through mandatory injunction requiring State of Punjab to vacate premises - Appellant disputed that ownership belonged to State Govt. - Trial court partially decreed suit holding that except double storied building, in occupation of State of Punjab, remnants in Jallowkhana complex was owned and possessed by appellant - Two cross appeals were filed before District Judge - Appeal of State of Punjab was dismissed but cross appeal of appellant was allowed and mandatory injunction was issued to State to handover building - Two separate second appeals were filed by State of Punjab in HC but dismissed by means of common judgment under appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether mandatory injunction issued to State to handover building was maintainable - Held, property must remain with heir apparent - State of Punjab could not partially be heir apparent - State of Punjab had no title to disputed property and hence must obey the mandatory injunction issued by lower appellate court as confirmed by HC - Mandatory injunction granted by lower appellate court was weakly worded - Injunction should be carried out within a reasonable time - Reasonable time for the obedience of injunction was granted - Appeal dismissed.
