Â Willie (William) Slaney v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 80; AIR 1956 SC 116; 1956 CRLJ 291; 1956 (1) MLJ 100; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 100; 1956 SCJ 182; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1140

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Vivian Bose, J.

(A) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 300, 302 and 304 Part II - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly committed offences punishable u/ss. 34 and 302 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 34 and 302 IPC - Aggrieved, appellant preferred appeal before HC, which was affirmed the order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether instant case was u/s. 302 or u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Held, injury was inflicted with a hockey stick and head was fractured but deceased lived for 10 days - If case could not be placed as high as that and the act was only likely to cause death and there was no special knowledge, offence comes u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Doctor thought that it was only likely to cause death - All blows on head did not necessarily cause death, and as deceased lived for 10 days - It was unable to deduce from nature of injury and from mere fact of death that appellant had, or should have had, special knowledge that s. 300 IPC requires - Admittedly, there was no premeditation and there was a sudden fight, so unable to ascribe necessary knowledge to appellant, nor was injury sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - So offence falls u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Appellant was acquitted from charge of murder and alter the conviction to u/s. 304 Part II IPC and reduced the sentence to 5 years rigorous imprisonment - Order accordingly.

CHANDRASEKHARA AIYAR J.

(B) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 304 Part II - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether appellant was guilty of murder u/s. 302 IPC, or guilty only of culpable homicide, not amounting to murder, u/s. 304 Part II IPC - Held, HC did not addressed itself to nature of offence - It was obvious that appellant did not intend to kill deceased - Evidence of doctor was that injury was likely to result in fatal consequences - It was not enough to bring case within scope of s. 300 IPC - There was nothing to warrant to attribute to appellant knowledge that injury was liable to cause death or that it was so imminently dangerous that it should in all probability cause death - However, fact that deceased lived for 10 days afterwards shows that it was not sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - Elements specified in s. 300 IPC were thus wanting - Appellant was acquitted of charge of murder but was convicted u/s. 304 Part II, and sentenced to 5 years' rigorous imprisonment - Order accordingly.
