State Bank Of India v Indexport Registered And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Yogeshwar Dayal, Veeraswami Ramaswami, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1185; (1992) 3 SCC 159; AIR 1992 SC 1740; 1992 (2) CCC 290; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 1; JT 1992 (2) SC 223; 1992(1) SCALE 1109; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 1031

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Land and Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.68 - Contract Act, 1872, ss.128 ,140 - CPC, 1908, ss. 47, O. 21, r. 30 and O. 34, rr. 4and 5 - Execution Application against respondent (judgment-debtor-guarantor) - Composite money decree - Being both personal (against all defendants including guarantor) as well as mortgage decree without limitation on execution - Whether the decree-holder can be forced to first exhaust remedy by way of execution of mortgage decree alone and then to proceed against guarantor - When guarantor in the present suit never took any plea to the effect that his liability is only contingent if the remedies against the principal debtors fail to satisfy the dues of the decree-holde - Held, the decree-holder cannot be forced - Appeal allowed. .
