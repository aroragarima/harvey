Bihar School Examination Board v Suresh Prasad Sinha

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1093; (2009) 8 SCC 483; AIR 2010 SC 93; 2009 (77) ALR 473; 2009 (4) AWC 3593; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 516; 2010 (2) CGLJ 194; 2010 (1) CompLJ 1; 2009 (4) CPJ(Sc) 34; 2009 (4) ESC 714; 2009 (5) G.L.R. 4116; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 71; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 1306; 2010 (1) MPLJ 321; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 9; 2010 (1) RLW 682; 2009(12) SCALE 228; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 1239; 2010 (1) UC 140; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 2865

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Delay In Delivery Of Possession, Bihar School Examination Board Act, 1952

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s.2(1)(d) - Statutory Examination Board - Consumer - Definition of - Failure to publish result of respondent's son in Senior Secondary Examination - Respondent's son had to re-appear for examination and suffered loss of 1 year - Compensation sought - District Forum ordered compensation - Appellant Board's appeal was dismissed by State Commission - Appellant's appeal before National Commission also dismissed - Whether a statutory School Examination Board comes within purview of Consumer Protection Act? - Held, process of holding examinations, evaluating answer scripts, declaring results and issuing certificates are different stages of a single statutory non-commercial function - Functions of appellant cannot be divided into partly statutory and partly administrative - Appellant does not offer its 'service' to any candidate - Examination fee paid by student is not consideration for availment of any service - Deficiency in process of examination does not convert appellant into a service-provider for a consideration nor convert examinee a consumer, therefore respondent's complaint would not be maintainable - Appellant Board was not rendering any 'service', hence impugned orders of Consumer Fora set aside - Appeal allowed.
