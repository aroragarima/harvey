State of Gujarat and Another v Mahendrakumar Parshottambhai Desai (Dead)By L.Rs

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 124; (2006) 9 SCC 772; AIR 2006 SC 1864; 2006 (63) ALR 806; 2007 (5) AWC 5010; 2006 (3) G.L.R. 1821; JT 2006 (4) SC 600; 2006 (101) RD 143; 2006 (3) RLW 2097; 2006(4) SCALE 265; 2006 (5) SCJ 115; 2006 (2) UC 1258

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: (1) Code of Civil Procedure, 1908-Order XLI, Rule 27-Appeal-Additional evidence - Suit for declaration filed by appellant State dismissed-Appeal by State before High Court-Application for adducing additional evidence since evidence on record did not support case of appellant-Whether High Court justified in rejecting application of State for adducing additional evidence at appellate stage as intended only to fill up lacunae in case?-Held, "yes".

(2) Specific Relief Act, 1963-Sections 6, 8 and 11-Declaratory suit-Filed by appellant State for declaration of its ownership of lands in dispute-Dismissal of suit by trial court affirmed by High Court-Whether any interference called for with concurrent findings of fact recorded by two courts below on proper appreciation of evidence?-Held, "no"-However, in view of one notification acquiring certain land, on considerations of equity and justice, suit partly decreed in respect of land so acquired by State-And State of Gujarat declared owner of same.
