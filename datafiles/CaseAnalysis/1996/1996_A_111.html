Shri Dharamvir v Amar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 772; (1996) 3 SCC 158; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 245; AIR 1996 SC 2314; JT 1996 (2) SC 10; 1996(1) SCALE 697; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 156; 1996 (2) Supreme 72

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Representation of People (Amendment) Act, 1951

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 8A, 100(1)(b), 116A, 123 (2),(7) and (8) and 135(d) - (A) Disqualification from seeking election - Order of, cannot be passed by HC - (B) s. 116A - Appeal - Finding of fact - Based on appreciation of oral evidence - Opinion of trial Court as to where credibility lies - Whether can be interfered - (C) Applicability of ss.123 (2) and (7) - (D) Scope and applicability of ss. 100(1)(b) - (E) Applicability of ss. 123(8) and 135A(d) - Election petitioner alleging hooliganism and mutilation of votes at time of counting - s. 123 (8) dealing with booth capturing and s. 135A (d) inserted long after election and counting under challenge - Whether applicable - (F) s. 100(1)(d) - Election - Setting aside - Finding that during counting duplicate marks were put on votes cast in favour of petitioner - Thousands of votes cast in favour of petitioner were thus rendered invalid - All these fraudulent and nefarious acts were monitored and approved by returned candidate - (1) Whether result of election has been materially affected by improper rejection of votes - (2 )Whether returned candidate guilty of corrupt practice - (3) Whether his election liable to be set aside - (4)Whether scrutiny of double marked ballot paper conclusively determine as to which ballot papers had been initially marked in favour of which candidate - (5)Whether petitioner polled majority votes - (6)Whether declaration that petitioner is elected can be granted - Held, (A) Cannot be passed by HC - (B) Not to be lightly interfered - (C) Apply to pre-voting stage and not post-voting stage - (D) Does not refer to a corrupt or fraudulent act practised by a candidate or his henchmen at time of counting of votes and its impact on the results of the election - (E) Not applicable - (F) (1)Has been materially affected - (2)Guilty of corrupt practice - (3)Election set aside -(4) Cannot be conclusive determination - (5) Cannot be said that petitioner polled majority votes - (6)Declaration cannot be granted - Appeal disposed of.
