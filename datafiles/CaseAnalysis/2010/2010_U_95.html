Arun Raj v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 390; (2010) 6 SCC 457; JT 2010 (5) SC 1; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 713; 2010(5) SCALE 608; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 304 Part II - Army Act, 1950, ss. 69 and 164 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant, an army personnel, complained to the Higher Officer alleging abuse by deceased - The same night appellant stabbed the deceased to death - Court martial proceedings were initiated - General Court Martial found the appellant guilty of the offence and sentenced him to imprisonment for life and dismissal from service - Order of General Court was confirmed by the Confirming Authority - Appellant filed a petition before the Chief of Army Staff u/s. 164 of the Army Act, which was rejected - Appellant preferred writ petition before HC who dismissed said writ petition on the ground that charge u/s. 302 of IPC proved and the procedure under Army Act followed without any infringement of principles of natural justice - Appellant contended that stab injury caused to the deceased was a result of grave and sudden provocation and thus the incident took place on spur of moment and hence falls under Exception I of s. 300 of IPC - Appellant further contended that there was no intention on the part of the appellant to cause the death of the deceased and, hence, s. 304 Part II of the IPC which deals with culpable homicide not amounting to murder, will be attracted - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether there was sufficient provocation and the criterion under the provision are satisfied to bring the offence under the Exception I of s. 300 IPC? - Held, no - There was time lag of 40-45 minute after appellant had come from the office of Higher Officer after complaining and that he had got the knife which was sharp enough to have the knowledge that it might cause death of a human being when stabbed, that the knife was hidden and removed by appellant only when he was about to stab the deceased, that the appellant stabbed the deceased on the chest which is a fragile portion of the body and can cause death when stabbed by sharp weapon, rules out the possibility of the offence being committed due to 'grave and sudden' provocation - Hence, appellant cannot get benefit of the Exception I to s. 300 of IPC - (B) Conviction u/s. 304 Part II - Held, there was due deliberation on the part of the appellant and he assaulted the deceased a day after he misbehaved with him - Hence, contention that appellant had no intention to cause death of the deceased has no merit and, accordingly, it is rejected - Appellant rightly convicted u/s. 302 of IPC - Appeal dismissed.
