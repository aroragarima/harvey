Rai Sandeep and another v State of (NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 222; (2012) 8 SCC 21; AIR 2012 SC 3157; 2012 CRLJ 4119; 2012 (191) DLT 439; 2012 (4) MLJ(Crl) 253; 2012 (2) OLR 647; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 11; 2012 (4) RLW 3250; 2012(7) SCALE 188; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1153

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 (2)(g) - Gang rape - Conviction - Challenged - Appellants allegedly raped prosecutrix (PW-4) and while leaving the place of occurrence, took away a gold chain and a wrist watch - Trial court convicted appellants u/s. 376(2)(g) of IPC for the offence of gang rape - On appeal, HC upheld the order of the Trial court - Hence, the instant appeals - Appellants contended that the trial Court as well as the HC ignored the fact that the appellants were neither identified nor their presence was established at the place of occurrence - It was also contended that there were material contradictions in the evidence of PWs10 and 11, and that of the prosecutrix (PW4) and, therefore, the conviction and sentence imposed was liable to be set aside - Whether order of the trial court as affirmed by the HC could be upheld - Held, except the ipse-dixit of the prosecutrix that too in her chief examination, with various additions and total somersault in the cross examination with no support at all at the instance of PWs10 and 11 who according to her were present in the house at the time of occurrence, as well as, the FSL report, apart from various other discrepancies in the matter of recoveries, do not convincingly rope in the appellants to the alleged offence of 'gang rape' on the date and time alleged in the charge sheet - When on the face value the evidence was found to be defective, the attendant circumstances and other evidence have to be necessarily examined to see whether the allegation of gang rape was true - Unfortunately, the version of the so called eye witnesses to at least the initial part of the crime has not supported the story of the prosecution - The attendant circumstances also do not co-relate to the offence alleged against the appellants - Therefore, in the absence of proper corroboration of the prosecution version to the alleged offence, it would be unsafe to sustain the case of the prosecution - The prosecution has miserably failed to establish the guilt of gang rape falling u/s. 376 (2) (g) of IPC against the appellants - The conviction and sentence imposed on the appellants by the trial Court and confirmed by the impugned order of the HC cannot, therefore, be sustained - Appeals allowed.
