S. L. Goswami v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 79; (1972) 3 SCC 22; (1972) SCC (Cr) 258; AIR 1972 SC 716; 1972 CRLJ 511; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 948; 1972 UJ 476

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure -Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101,102,103 and 104 - Cheating - Burden of proof on Prosecution - Couldn't prove charges - Whether accused is liable to be convicted in instant case? - Held, that accused be acquitted as prosecution failed to prove guilt beyond doubt as a result accused was awarded benefit of doubt -(B)Whether onus at initial stage is on accused to prove facts for acquittal ? - Further held, It is only when this burden of proof on prosecution is discharged that it will be for accused to explain or controvert essential elements in prosecution case which would negative it - It is not however for accused even at initial stage to prove something which has to be eliminated by prosecution to establish ingredients of offence with which he is charged, and even if onus shifts upon accused and accused has to establish his plea, standard of proof is not same as that which rests upon prosecution-Where onus shifts to accused and evidence on his behalf probabilities plea he will be entitled to benefit of reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed.
