Krishna @ Krishnappa v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

14 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday U. Lalit, Dipak Misra, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 772; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 354; JT 2014 (12) SC 623; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 995; 2014(12) SCALE 723

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 511, 341 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was tried for having committed offences u/ss. 376, 341 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment acquitted appellants - On appeal HC by impugned judgment convicted appellant for offence u/ss. 376 r/w s. 511 and u/s. 341 of IPC and sentenced appellant to undergo RI for 2 years - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that conviction was not justified.

Held, as found by both the Courts below the offence u/s. 376 of IPC was not established at all. The reasons given by the Trial Court while acquitting the appellant, are quite sound and in any case, such view is definitely a possible view. The conclusions reached by the Trial Court cannot be said to be palpably wrong or based on erroneous view of the law, so as to call for interference by the HC. The HC was not justified in converting the case to that of attempt to commit rape and recording order of conviction. Impugned judgment and order of conviction passed by HC was set aside and judgment of Trial Court acquitting the appellant is restored. Appeal allowed.
