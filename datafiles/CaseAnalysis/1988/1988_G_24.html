Miss A. Sundarambal v Government Of Goa, Daman And Diu And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 529; (1988) 4 SCC 42; (1988) SCC (L&S) 842; AIR 1988 SC 1700; JT 1988 (3) SC 121; 1989 LabIC 1317; 1989 (1) LLJ 61; 1988 (2) LLN 608; 1988(2) SCALE 82; 1989 (1) SLJ 61; 1988 (3) SLR 517; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 604; 1988 (2) UJ 329

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Educational Institution, Air Force Act, 1950

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(j), 2(ss.) and 10, chap. 2, item 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 38 - Industrial Dispute - Teacher - Termination of service - School is 'industry' but teacher employed in school is not 'workman' - As class teachers were denied social justice - Govt. to provide adequate machinery - Whether dispute can be referred under Industrial Dispute Act - Held, dispute cannot be referred under Act - Compensation on suggestion of Supreme Court - Appeal dismissed.
