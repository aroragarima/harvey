Prabhu Chawla v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Shiva Kirti Singh, J. Chelameswar, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 636; AIR 2016 SC 4245; 2016 (4) CGLJ 396; 2017 CRLJ 1080; 2016 (5) CTC 779; 2016 (4) JCC 2385; JT 2016 (9) SC 61; 2016 (2) OLR 906; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 270; 2016 (4) RLW 3381; 2016(8) SCALE 545; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 281

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Interlocutory, Jurisdiction, Inherent Power, Abuse, Power Of The High Court, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397, 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 228A - Inherent jurisdiction - Barred - Alternative remedy - Appellants/accused filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of HC whereby Single Judge dismissed writ petitions filed by appellants u/s. 482 CrPC holding that petitions to be not maintainable in view of judgment of HC in the case of Sanjay Bhandari v. State of Rajasthan, 2009 Indlaw RAJ 34 holding that availability of remedy u/s. 397 CrPC. would make a petition u/s. 482 CrPC was not maintainable - DB found impugned order of HC to be against the law stated in Dhariwal Tobacco Products Ltd. and Ors. v. State of Maharashtra and another, 2008 Indlaw SC 2003, wherein DB held that availability of alternative remedy of criminal revision u/s. 397 CrPC by itself cannot be a good ground to dismiss an application u/s. 482 CrPC - However, DB noticed that a later judgment of SC in the case of Mohit alias Sonu and another v. State of Uttar Pradesh and another, 2013 Indlaw SC 384 apparently held to the contrary that when an order under assail is not interlocutory in nature and is amenable to the revisional jurisdiction of HC then there should be a bar in invoking the inherent jurisdiction of HC - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, any attempt to explain the law further as regards the issue relating to inherent power of HC u/s. 482 CrPC. is unwarranted. SC would simply reiterate that s. 482 CrPC begins with a non-obstante clause to state "Nothing in this Code shall be deemed to limit or affect the inherent powers of HC to make such orders as may be necessary to give effect to any order under this Code, or to prevent abuse of process of any Court or otherwise to secure the ends of justice." Since s. 397 CrPC. is attracted against all orders other than interlocutory, a contrary view would limit the availability of inherent powers u/s. 482 CrPC. only to petty interlocutory orders, a situation wholly unwarranted and undesirable. DB in the case of Mohit alias Sonu and another, 2013 Indlaw SC 384 in respect of inherent power of HC in s. 482 CrPC does not state the law correctly. SC records its respectful disagreement. Single Judge should have followed the law laid down by SC in the case of Dhariwal Tobacco Products Ltd., 2008 Indlaw SC 2003 and other earlier cases which were cited but wrongly ignored them in preference to a judgment of that Court in the case of Sanjay Bhandari, 2009 Indlaw RAJ 34 passed by another Single Judge. Impugned common order passed by HC was set aside and the matters are remitted back to HC for fresh hearing of petitions u/s. 482 CrPC in the light of law explained above and for disposal in accordance with law. Since the matters have remained pending for long, HC is requested to hear and decide the matters expeditiously, preferably within 6 months. Appeals allowed.
