Malla Prakasarao v Malla Janaki and others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Shivaraj V. Patil, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2397; (2004) 3 SCC 343; 2006 (1) ACC 300

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor accident claim - Compensation - Enhancement - Entitled to - Appellants (claimants) were dissatisfied with the compensation awarded by Tribunal filed appeals before HC, which were dismissed by impugned judgments of HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether impugned judgments passed by HC was justified - Held, in view of facts and circumstances of cases, multiplier of 9 applied by HC in determining the compensation to claimants was not appropriate - It was not disputed that deceased was a bank employee and was 46 years of age at the time of accident and that 14 years of service were still left to serve - multiplier of 12 ought to have been applied by HC - On application of multiplier of 12 the total amount of compensation comes to Rs.5,76,000/- - Liability of respondent (Insurance) was 40% of enhanced amount, respondent already deposited its share of compensation at the rate awarded by HC and should pay the balance amount with interest at the rate of 7% on the enhanced amount from the date of instant order - Appeals disposed of.
