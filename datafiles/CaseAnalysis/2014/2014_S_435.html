Narendra v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T. S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 570; (2014) 10 SCC 248; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3760; 2014 CRLJ 4396; JT 2014 (10) SC 96; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3475; 2014(10) SCALE 62

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 309, 300 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) was convicted by Trial Court and sentenced to under to imprisonment for life for alleged offence u/ss. 302, 309 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before DB - DB by impugned judgment confirmed conviction of appellant and the sentence imposed upon him - Hence, instant appeal - Whether act of appellant was in furtherance of suicide pact and whether homicide falls under Exception 5 of s. 300 of IPC and whether conviction of appellant wato be modified u/s. 304 Part I of IPC -

Held, accused has taken the defence plea of suicide pact even in the trial court while being questioned u/s. 313 of CrPC. The defence version is probabilized by the above facts and circumstances of the case. The death of deceased was not premeditated and the act of the accused causing death of deceased, it appears to be in furtherance of the understanding between them to commit suicide and the consent of the deceased and the act of the accused falls under Exception 5 of s. 300 of IPC. Since the accused intentionally caused the death; the appellant is found guilty u/s. 304 Part I of IPC. The appellant is stated to be in custody for more than 10 years. Conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of IPC is modified and the appellant is convicted u/s. 304 Part I of IPC and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for the period already undergone by him. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - To attract Exception 5 to s. 300 of IPC, there must have been free and voluntary consent of the deceased person. The onus of proving consent of the deceased person is on the accused. Exception 5 of s. 300 of IPC must receive a strict and not a liberal interpretation. The court must in each case consider the evidence and the surrounding circumstances while considering the question of consent.
