Â Raichand Amulakh Shah v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 194; AIR 1964 SC 1268; 1964 (66) BomLR 315; 1964 MPLJ 777; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 148

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Demurrage

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Indian Railways Act, 1890, s. 26 - Whether suits filed against Western Railway for refund of amounts collected from appellant firm as wharfage or demurrage would lie in a Civil Court? - (1) Whether wharfage and demurrage charges are 'terminals'? - Held, yes - (2) Whether any remedy is provided by Act for an aggrieved party to ask for a refund of charges collected? - Further held, Tribunal has no jurisdiction to decide whether rules empowering railway administration to levy a particular charge are ultra vires or whether railway administration collected amounts in excess of charges which it can legally levy under a rule - So no provision has been made under Act giving a remedy to an aggrieved party to ask for a refund of amounts - S. 26 cannot be a bar against maintainability of suits - Matter remitted back to Lower Court.
