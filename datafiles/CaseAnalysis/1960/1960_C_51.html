Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab, Himachal Pradesh, and Bilaspur v Thakar Das Bhargava

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 367; AIR 1960 SC 1219; [1960] 40 ITR 301; 1961 (1) SCJ 453; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 75

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, ss. 22(2), 34(1)(Vii) - Professional income - Trust for charity - Deduction - Exception - T / advocate /respondent assessed to income tax on a total assessable income , which also included the amount received for defending the accused persons in a case - Assessee claimed that said amount not a part of his professional income, because the amount was given to him in trust for charity - Claim not accepted by Income-tax Officer, nor by Appellate Assistant Commissioner - From order of Appellate Commissioner a further appeal filed to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal - HC answered the question in favour of the assessee - Hence instant appeal - Whether assessee was liable to pay tax for the sum , which arose out of his professional income - Held, yes - Once it is held that amount was received as his professional income, the assessee is clearly liable to pay tax thereon - View taken by Appellate Commissioner was correct view - Money when it was received by assessee was his professional income, though assessee expressed a desire earlier to create a charitable trust out of the money when received by him - Correct answer to question referred to HC is that the amount certain sum received by the assessee was professional income taxable in his hands - Appeal allowed.
