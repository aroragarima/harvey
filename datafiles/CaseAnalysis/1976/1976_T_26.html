Prem Ballab & Anr v The State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 191; (1977) SCC (Cr) 78; (1977) 1 SCC 173; AIR 1977 SC 56; 1977 CRLJ 12; 1976 CrLR(SC) 495; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 592

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Colouring Matter, Probation of Offenders Act 1958

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7(i) and 16(1)(a)(i) - Conviction of appellants u/s. 7(i) r/w. s. 16(1)(a)(i) of Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Challenged - What was sold by appellants was linseed oil which contained artificial dye - It may be made clear at out set that different clauses of s. 2(i) are not mutually exclusive - They overlap one another, red, it is quite possible that an article of food may be found adulterated under two or more clauses of s. 2(i) - Held, report of Public Analyst showed that linseed oil sold by appellants contained artificial dye and this was clearly prohibited under Rules - R. 23 provided that addition of a colouring matter to an article of food, except as specifically permitted by Rules, shall be prohibited - Only artificial dyes, which were permitted to be used in food, were those set out in r. 28, and r. 29 prohibited use of permitted coal tar dyes in or upon any food other than those enumerated in that Rule - Linseed oil was admittedly not one of articles of food enumerated in r. 29 and hence even permitted coal tar dyes could not be added to linseed oil - Linseed oil sold by appellants contained artificial dye which was prohibited under Rules - Where no colouring matter is permitted to be used in an article of food, what is prescribed in respect of article is that no colouring matter shall be used and if any colouring matter is present in article in breach of that prescription, it would clearly involve violation of cl. (j) of s. 2(i) - Adulteration is an economic offence prompted by profit motive and it is not likely to lend itself easily to therapeutic treatment by probationary measure - Conviction and sentence recorded against appellants confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
