Lakshmi Ammal v Madhavakrishnan (K. N.) And Ors

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 181; (1978) 4 SCC 15; AIR 1978 SC 1607; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 68; 1978 UJ 616

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Court Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1955

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Court Fees Act, 1870,s. 37(2), art.17(b) - Court fee - Implication - Whether on fact and circumstance of instant case, question of Court fee does have any implications on merits including validity or otherwise of Will.

Held, it is unfortunate that long years have been spent by Courts below on a combat between two parties on question of Court fee leaving real issues to be fought between them to come up leisurely. Two things have to be made clear. Courts should be anxious to grapple with real issues and not spend their energies on peripheral a ones. Court fee, if it seriously restricts rights of a person to seek his remedies in Courts of justice, should be strictly construed. After all access to justice is basis of legal system. In that view, where there is a doubt, reasonable of course, benefit must go to him who says that lesser Court fee alone be paid. In this particular case there is hardly any difficulty in holding that plaintiff has clearly alleged that she is in joint possession and is seeking partition and separate possession of her half share in suit properties as heir of âPâ/deceased. Obviously, Court fee that is payable is as she has claimed, namely u/s.37(2) of Act which corresponds to art.17(b) of Act which is predecessor legislation on subject. Court allow appeal and send case back to Trial Court and direct that Court to proceed with suit expeditiously. Decision on question of Court fee does not have any implications on merits including validity or otherwise of Will. Order accordingly.
