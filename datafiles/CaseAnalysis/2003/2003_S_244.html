Hira Lal and Others v State (Government of NCT) Delhi

Supreme Court of India

25 July 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 546; (2003) 8 SCC 80; (2003) SCC (Cr) 2016; AIR 2003 SC 2865; 2003 (3) CCR 41; 2003 (3) Crimes 186; 2003 (6) CRJ 508; 2003 CRLJ 3711; 2003 (105) DLT 705; 2003 (2) DMC 206; 2003 (2) JCC 1182; JT 2003 (6) SC 195; 2003 (3) RCR(Criminal) 830; 2003(5) SCALE 445; 2003 (1) SCW 3570; 2003 (4) SLT 594; [2003] Supp1 S.C.R. 734; 2003 (5) Supreme 112

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: [A.] Women & Children - Criminal - Dowry Prohibition (Amendment) Act, 1986 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s.2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306, 34, 498 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss.113B, 114(a) - Demand of dowry - Suicide - Held, basic requirement of cruelty or harassment soon before death to bring application of s 304-B is absent meaning of 'cruelty' or 'harassment' is same as prescribed in explanation to s 498-A under which 'cruelty' by itself amounts to an offence u/s. 304-B, it is 'dowry death' that is punishable and such death should have occurred within 7 years of marriage - No such period is mentioned in s 498-A A person charged and acquitted u/s. 304-B can be convicted u/s. 498-A without that charge being there, if such a case is made out - If case is established, there can be a conviction under both sections - Further held, on facts of case, even though it is difficult to sustain conviction u/s. 304 B, IPC, there are sufficient materials to convict accused-appellants in terms of s 306, IPC along with s 498-A, IPC - Appeal disposed of.
