Union of India and Others v R. K. Sharma

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20555; (2001) 9 SCC 592; (2002) SCC (Cr) 767; AIR 2001 SC 3053; 2001 (4) AWC 3222; 2001 (3) CLR 967; 2001 (91) FLR 1006; 2001 LabIC 4007; 2001(7) SCALE 70; 2002 (1) SLJ 323; 2001 (5) SLR 731; 2001 (7) SLT 214; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 664; 2001 (7) Supreme 497; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 111

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, ss. 71 and 72 - Army Rules, 1954, rr. 22, 23 and 24 - (A) Whether respondent is liable under four charges and subordinate court was right in holding them liable? - Both single judge as well as Division Bench have held that four charge set out have been proved and that respondent was guilty of those charges - Having so held it was not open to court to have interfered in sentence - Held awarding of sentence is within powers of court martial - No interference called for - (B) Whether both courts below have committed erred in coming to conclusion that sentence awarded was too harsh considering nature and degree of offence established? - Not only did respondent not obey command from headquarter but he falsified records in order to make out a case that he had already gone to two of forward posts - These are very serious offences - These are offences for which general court martial would have been justified in awarding imprisonment - Held, general court martial took a lenient view by merely dismissing him from service - There was just no justification for interference by High Court - (C) Whether during preliminary hearing when summary evidence was recorded, provisions of rr. 22, 23 and 24 of Army Rules, 1954, as well as Army Order 70/84 were not strictly followed? - Preliminary hearing u/rr. 22, 23 and 24 of Army Rules, 1954, this is a hearing which precedes court martial - In any event HC has held against respondent on this point and no appeal was filed by him - Further held, this point not having been raised in writ petition cannot now be urged before this court for first time - Petition dismissed.
