Anowar Hussain v Ajoy Kumar Mukherjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 506; AIR 1965 SC 1651; 1965 CRLJ 686

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 190 and 204 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 436 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133 (1)(c) - Judicial Officers Protection Act, 1850, s. 1 - Wrongful confinement - No formal complaint lodged against respondent charging him with an offence - Respondent alleged he had incurred displeasure of appellant and that latter had, with a view to insult and disgrace him, ordered his arrest, detention and imprisonment maliciously, recklessly and without any lawful excuse or justification - Whether in ordering arrest of respondent appellant acted in discharge of his judicial duties and therefore protected by Act? - Must be determined whether appellant had taken cognizance of offence u/s 190 of CrPC before issuing order to arrest - As no formal complaint lodged before appellant charging respondent with having committed an offence, power u/s 190 (1)(a) could not be exercised - Held, appellant not entitled to protection of Act - He was relying primarily upon his executive office - Appeal dismissed.
