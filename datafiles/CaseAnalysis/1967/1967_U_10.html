Â Anand Parkash Saksena v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 273; AIR 1968 SC 754; 1968 LabIC 865; 1969 (1) LLJ 373; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 611; 1968 SLR 582

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - All India Service (Discipline and Appeals) Rules, 1955 - I A S Recruitment Rules, 1954, rr. 4, 4(3), 8 and 9(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 32 - I A S Regulation of Seniority Rules, 1954, r. 3 - Regulation of Seniority Rules, 1954 - Special Recruitment Seniority Regulations, 1960, regns. 3 and 3(3)(b) - Indian Administrative Service (Special Recruitment) Regulations, 1956, regn. 3(3) - Petitioner an IAS - He was appointed to officiate in a senior post - In gradation list published his number is 70 - Challenged u/art. 32 of Constitution - (A) Whether 75 per cent of total number of senior posts is reserved exclusively for direct recruits? - Held, u/r. 9(1) of Recruitment Rules, number of persons recruited to senior posts u/r. 8 of Recruitment Rules by promotion or by selection cannot exceed 25 per cent of total number of senior posts - Remaining 75 per cent of senior posts may be filled by other recruits - Special recruits u/r. 4 of Recruitment Rules are appointed against 75 per cent quota - Their appointments are not counted against 25 per cent quota reserved for persons recruitment u/r. 8 - Posts held by officers in List III were excluded from cadre until they retired and were not counted against 25 per cent quota - (B) Whether seniority of respondents can be challenged? - Held, seniority of respondents was fixed in accordance with regn. 3(3) of Special Recruitment Seniority Regulations, regn. 3(3)(b) of Regulation of Seniority Rules and proviso thereto, and is not open to any challenge - (C) Whether r. 4(3) of Recruitment Rules is bad on ground of excessive delegation, of legislative power? - Held, r. 4(3) does not suffer from vice of excessive delegation - R. 4(3) authorities Central Govt. to make regulations for special recruitment - In making regulations, Central Govt. is to be guided by exigencies of service and advice of State Govts. and UPSC - These authorities are best judges of appropriate regulations to be made in matter - Special Recruitment Regulations 1960 framed u/r. 4(3) have adapted for purposes of special recruitment regulations for recruitment by competitive examination, promotion and selection with appropriate modifications - (D) Whether r. 3(3)(b) of Regulation of Seniority Rules makes unjust discrimination between a promotee and a direct recruit in matter of seniority by arbitrarily assigning a lower year of allotment to a promotee and is violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution? - Held, seniority of direct recruits inter se and promotees inter se is fixed by r. 4 object of r. 3(3)(b) is to fix seniority of promotees in relation to direct recruits - Promotees obtain promotion after long service in State Civil Services - R. 3(3)(b) attempts to strike a just balance between conflicting claims - It gives promotee year of allotment of junior-most direct recruit officiating continuously in a senior post earlier than date of commencement of such officiation by promotee - If no direct recruit was officiating continuously in a senior post on an earlier date - Seniority of promotee is determined ad hoc - Rule is not arbitrary or discriminatory and is not violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - (E) Validity of regn. 3(3) of Special Recruitment Seniority Regulations, 1960 on ground that it offends arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Held, Special recruits form a distinct class - They are neither direct recruits nor promotees - R. 9 of Recruitment Rules does not treat them as direct recruits - Regn. 3(3) of Special Recruits Seniority Regulations properly adopts formula applicable to promotees for fixing seniority of special recruits enlisted by promotion so that in matter of seniority all officers recruited from State Civil Service are placed on same footing - Regulation is not arbitrary nor violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Writ Petition dismissed.
