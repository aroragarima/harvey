Kanai Lal Garari and Others v Murari Ganguly and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 425; (1999) 6 SCC 35; 2000 (38) ALR 283; 2000 (1) CLT 223; JT 1999 (10) SC 45; 2000 (1) SLT 138; 2000 (Supp) RD 204; 1999 (10) Supreme 245

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Title and possession of the property - Whether the High Court was right in set aside the judgment of Lower Court and remand the matter for determine the matter afresh? - Held, u/s. 100 HC is required to frame a substantial question of law at beginning of hearing itself and/or if such substantial question(s) of law have already been raised in memorandum of appeal then HC is to hear parties on such question(s) - Judgment of HC does not disclose that any such substantial question(s) of law were raised at initial stage - Approach of HC is contrary to mandatory provisions of s. 100 - Judgment of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
