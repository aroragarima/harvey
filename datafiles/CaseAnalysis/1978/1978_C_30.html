Brij Bhushan Lal Parduman Kumar and another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Haryana, Himachal Pradesh and New Delhi-III

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 220; (1979) 3 SCC 14; (1979) SCC (Tax) 197; AIR 1979 SC 209; 1978 (7) CTR 134; [1978] 115 ITR 524; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 16; 1978 TAXLR 1346; 1978 UJ 818

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28 and 144 - Income Tax - (A) Certain materials supplied to assessee, a MES Contractor to be exclusively used in construction - At fixed rates by Department - Computation of income and profits of assessee - (B) Best judgment assessment - Principles - Held, cost of such materials cannot be included - Authority must make an honest and fair estimate of the income and though arbitrariness cannot be avoided , it must not be capricious but should have a reasonable nexus to the available material and the circumstances of the case - Appeals allowed.
