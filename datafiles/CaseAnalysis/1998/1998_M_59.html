M.S. Bindra v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 611; (1998) 7 SCC 310; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1812; AIR 1998 SC 3058; 1998 (2) CLR 852; 1998 (78) ECR 502; 1998 (80) FLR 452; JT 1998 (6) SC 34; 1998 LabIC 3491; 1999 (1) LLJ 923; 1998 (4) LLN 1; 1998(5) SCALE 45; 1999 (2) SLJ 96; 1998 (3) SLR 358; 1998 (7) SLT 239; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 232; 1998 (7) Supreme 90

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Validity of compulsory retirement - Held, if integrity of senior officers, who established unblemished reputation and earned encomiums from all concerned till then, is proclaimed as doubtful merely on strength of statements of persons prosecuted by such officers, what is safety of such officers more so when they have to embark on hazardous operations risking their lives against big business houses - There is utter dearth of evidence for Screening committee to conclude that appellant had doubtful integrity - Appeal allowed.
