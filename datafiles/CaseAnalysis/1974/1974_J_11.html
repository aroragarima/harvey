Jaikishan Dass Mull v Luchhiminarain Kanoria and Company

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 222; (1974) 2 SCC 521; AIR 1974 SC 1579

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 33; Bombay Cotton Contracts, Act, s. 5; Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952 If contract illegal with respect to Forward Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1952 - Whether arbitration agreement valid - Whether Contract legal - Requirement as to contract to be in prescribed form - Compliance - Term in prescribed form of contract - Whether dispensable on ground of inapplicability - When a contract is invalid, every part of it, including the clause as to arbitration contained therein must also be invalid - Contract not in the prescribed form as required by bye-law 1(b) would be contravention of bye-law 15 and hence illegal and viod under s.15(3)(A) r/w bye-law 17 - In order that a contract may be in the prescribed form, contract must contain all the terms and conditions set out in the prescribed form - Term in prescribed form of contract are not dispensable on ground of inapplicability - Prescribed from is imperative and a contract order to be valid must contain all the term and conditions set out in the prescribed form - No term or condition in the prescribed form can be dispensed with on the plea that it is inapplicable. Appeal Dismissed
