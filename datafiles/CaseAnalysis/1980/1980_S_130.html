Rajender Kumar Jain and Others v State Through Special Police Establishment and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 473; (1980) 3 SCC 435; (1980) SCC (Cr) 757; 1980 (17) ACC 201; AIR 1980 SC 1510; 1980 CRLJ 1084; 1980 CRLR 454; [1980] 3 S.C.R. 982

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CrPC, 1973, ss. 209 and 321 - Constitution, art. 136 - (A) Withdrawal from prosecution - (1) Whether magistrate can give consent to it - (2) Duties of Public Prosecutor and scope of jurisdiction of Court - (B) Constitution - Special leave petition to Supreme Court without going to High Court in first instance - Held, Committing Magistrate is competent to give such consent - Public Prosecutor to inform the Court and it shall be the duty of the Court to appraise itself of the reasons which prompt the Public Prosecutor to withdraw from the prosecution - Ordinarily such petitions are not entertained by the SC - Petition Dismissed.
