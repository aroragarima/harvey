Â Sohan Lal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

07 March 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Syed Jaffer Imam, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, P. Govinda Menon, J.L. Kapur, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 46; AIR 1957 SC 529; 1957 SCJ 489; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 738

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: misa, Public Premises (Eviction) Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Public Premises (Eviction) Act, 1950, s. 3 - Ordinance III of 1952, s. 25 - Allotment of houses to refugees from Pakistan - Eligibility criteria - House in question originally allotted to respondent on his prima facie satisfying criteria - Eviction of respondent and allotting same accommodation to appellant - Petition u/art. 226 filed in High Court by respondent against UOI which according to him had illegally evicted him and also against appellant at whose instance eviction was done, for restoration of property - HC allowed petition exercising its jurisdiction u/art. 226 - Whether in a proceeding u/art. 226 such declaration can be made and restoration of property to respondent be ordered? - Held, rights and title of parties and questions relating thereto should be decided in a properly constituted suit in a Civil Court rather than in proceedings u/art. 226 - Manner of eviction of respondent noted by Court - Eviction was found to be in contravention of express provision of s. 3 of Eviction Act, 1950 which made eviction illegal - Respondent was entitled to be evicted in due course of law - Writ of mandamus will lie against UOI to restore possession - Property in dispute was in possession of appellant, and there was no evidence and no finding by HC that appellant was in collusion with UOI or that he had knowledge that eviction of respondent was illegal - Normally a writ of mandamus does not issue to or an order in nature of mandamus is not made against a private individual - Such an order is made against a person directing him to do some particular thing, specified in order, which appertains to his office and is in nature of a public duty - No direct authority found on subject - Court referred and relied cases on election - Writ jurisdiction exercised by HC and direction issue by it, uncalled for - Appeal allowed.
