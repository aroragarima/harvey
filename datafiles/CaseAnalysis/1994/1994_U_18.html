Uday Pratap Singh and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1088; (1994) Supp3 SCC 451; (1995) SCC (L&S) 85; 1994 (28) ATC 453; 1994 (69) FLR 1148; JT 1994 (6) SC 344; 1994(4) SCALE 344; 1995 (1) SCJ 304; 1995 (1) SLJ 123; 1994 (5) SLR 608; [1994] Supp4 S.C.R. 72

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Bihar Finance Service Rules, 1953, rr. 3, 4 and 5 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Appellants and original writ petitioners who are respondents in these appeals belong to Bihar Finance Service which was originally consisting of two branches, namely, Senior Branch and Junior Branch - As per Constitution, seniority was to be reckoned from date of substantive appointment of incumbent concerned - Appellants herein were appointed to Junior Branch while original writ petitioners were appointed as direct recruits in Senior Branch - So far as original writ petitioners, respondents herein, are concerned, some of them were directly recruited and appointed in Senior Branch on and rest of original writ petitioners were appointed as direct recruits - State Govt. decided to merge different cadres existing in Civil Services, Finance Service and Education Service - Accordingly, by a Govt. resolution issued by Finance (Commercial Taxes) Department, State Govt. decided that both Senior and Junior Branches of Bihar Finance Service be merged - These two appeals by special leave arise out of judgment rendered by a Division Bench of High Court wherein HC has allowed writ petition of writ petitioners - Legality of executive order - Held, it is now well-settled that by an executive order statutory rules cannot be whittled down nor can any retrospective effect be given to such executive order so as to destroy any right which became crystallised - As rightly found by HC if appellants who entered merged cadre of Senior Branch only on particular date (2-11-1975) are to be treated as senior to respondents who had entered Senior Branch as direct recruits prior thereto, respondents would clearly get their constitutional rights guaranteed u/arts. 14 and 16 violated - No substance - Appeals dismissed.
