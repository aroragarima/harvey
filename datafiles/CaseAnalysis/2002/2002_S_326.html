Dharmendrasinh @ Mansing Ratansinh v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

17 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 246; (2002) 4 SCC 679; (2002) SCC (Cr) 859; AIR 2002 SC 1937; AIR 2002 SCW 1892; 2002 (2) CCR 144; 2002 (2) Crimes 294; 2002 (4) CRJ 268; 2002 CRLJ 2631; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 2702; 2002 (2) JCC 1049; JT 2002 (4) SC 239; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 691; 2002(3) SCALE 690; 2002 (3) SCJ 435; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 193; 2002 (3) SLT 306; 2002 (3) Supreme 501; 2002 (2) UC 146

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Though, the appellant was rightly convicted for the crime of murder based upon cogent evidence and independent circumstances, but, he cannot be held liable for awarding a death penalty, as, the crime was not committed for lust of power or with a view to grab any property nor in pursuance of any organized criminal or anti-social activity, and chances of repetition of such criminal acts at his hands making the society further vulnerable are also not apparent.
