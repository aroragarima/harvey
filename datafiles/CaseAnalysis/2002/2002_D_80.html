Director of Settlements, A. P. and Others v M. R. Apparao and Another

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Variava, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1956; (2002) 4 SCC 638; AIR 2002 SC 1598; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 367; JT 2002 (3) SC 304; 2002(3) SCALE 122; 2002 (2) SCJ 564; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 661; 2002 (2) SLT 778; 2002 (2) Supreme 584

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 141 and 142 - Andhra Pradesh Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1948, ss. 39, 44, 50 and 54 - Uttar Pradesh Minor Minerals (Concession) Rules, 1963, r. 72 - Present respondents along with several others filed writ petitions before Andhra Pradesh HC, seeking interim payments - High Court disposed of two writ petitions following earlier judgment in Venkatagiri's case, issued a writ of mandamus to make interim payments to respondents herein - This was not challenged by way of appeal to Supreme Court - Respondents then filed a writ petition to implement earlier order in their favour - Writ petition was dismissed on ground that judgment in Venkatagiri's case, having been set aside by Supreme Court, earlier decision in favour of respondents would not constitute an enforceable right and as such a writ of mandamus cannot be issued - (A) Whether decision of this Court D/- 06-02-1986 upholding constitutional validity of Amendment Act of 1971 reversing judgment of Andhra Pradesh HC in Venkatgiri case and further indicating that period during which interim payments are payable under Act ends with date of original determination made by Director u/s. 39(1) of Act, be held to be a law declared by Supreme Court u/art. 141 of Constitution, or it can be said to be per incurium? - Held, decision of this Court dated 06-1986 must be held to be a 'law declared' within ambit of art. 141 of Constitution and constitutional validity of Amendment Act 1971 is not open to be re-agitated and that judgment of Andhra Pradesh HC holding Amendment Act to be constitutionally invalid had been set aside by this court - (B) Whether judgment of HC in favour of respondents not being challenged has conferred an indefeasible right on respondents? - Further held, judgment of Andhra Pradesh HC in Venkatagiri's case, holding amendment Act to be constitutionally invalid, on being reversed by Supreme Court on a conclusion that said amendment is constitutionally valid, said dictum would be valid throughout country and for all persons, including respondents, even though judgment in their favour had not been assailed - (C) Whether HC would be justified in issuing a mandamus in changed circumstances? - Further held, so-called right of respondents, depending upon conclusion that amendment Act is constitutionally invalid and, therefore, right to get interim payment will continue till final decision of Board of Revenue cannot be sustained when SC itself has upheld constitutional validity of amendment Act - HC in exercise of power of issuance of mandamus could not have said anything contrary to that on ground that earlier judgment in favour of respondents became final, not being challenged - Appeal allowed.
