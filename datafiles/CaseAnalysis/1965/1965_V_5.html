V. S. S. V. Meenakshi Achi and Another v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 422; 1991 (94) CTR 195; [1966] 60 ITR 253

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Rubber Industry (Re-planting) Fund Ordinance, 1952, ss. 4 and 7 - Whether a 'receipt' is a capital or a revenue receipt? - Held, it is clear from facts that appellants were only partners and they were not exporters and therefore they did not pay any duty under Ordinance to Govt. - Payments to planters were made against expenditure incurred for maintaining rubber plantations - Having regard to facts, it must be held that amounts from fund earmarked for appellants on basis of rubber produced by them were paid against expenditure incurred by them for maintaining rubber plantation and producing rubber - If so, it follows that receipts by appellants during accounting year were revenue receipts and, therefore, liable to be included in their assessable income - Appeal dismissed.
