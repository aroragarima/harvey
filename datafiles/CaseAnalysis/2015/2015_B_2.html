Infrastructure Leasing and Financial Services Limited v B. P. L. Limited

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 13; (2015) 3 SCC 363; 2015(3) ALL MR 413; 2015 (1) CompLJ 501; 2015 (2) CTC 641; JT 2015 (1) SC 136; 2015(1) SCALE 186; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 273

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Satisfaction, Pledge, Unregistered

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 138, 139, 391 to 394 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 9 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 2 r. 2 - Scheme - Approval of - Respondent (BPL), entered into a joint venture with 'Sanyo Electric Co. Ltd.' and entered into a shareholder agreement - BPL was required to invest approximately Rs.46 crores in the joint venture company and to receive a net cash inflow of Rs.322 crores, for said purpose BPL proposed a scheme of arrangement which was modified - There were 36 creditors whose names featured in the scheme - After approval of the scheme the respondent filed an application u/s. 391 (1) of the Act r/w r. 9 the Rules seeking permission for holding a meeting for consideration for approval of compromise or arrangement proposed to be made between companies and the creditors - Appellant company had extended a short-term loan facility of Rs.150 million to the respondent company; that the respondent company had executed a deed of hypothecation in favour of the appellant - Respondent filed Forms 8 and 13 and the charge by way of hypothecation was duly registered with the Registrar of Companies - However, appellant had initiated an arbitration proceeding which resulted in the consent award - Respondent failed to pay the first instalment - Thereafter, respondent filed a petition u/ss. 391-394 of the Act for sanction of the scheme - Appellant filed objections to the scheme stating, inter alia, that it was an unsecured creditor - Company Judge held that the application preferred u/s. 391(1) of the Act was maintainable - On appeal, DB held that the appellant was a secured creditor after the hypothecation deed was executed; that once the charge had been created it continued to bind the parties till steps were regressed; and that the finding recorded by the company Judge was unexceptionable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant does not fall into the class of secured creditors, for it had initiated the arbitration proceeding and an award has been passed on consent.

Held, the consent award in an arbitral proceeding would not bar a suit for enforcement of the charge for the same reasons and it would not be hit by O. 2 r. 2 of CPC. In the instant case, the charge is by hypothecation and relates to movable property. The issue before the Company Court was quite different than that was before the Arbitral Tribunal. It has the status of a decree which is executable, as a decree having gone unchallenged, but the lis of framing a Scheme under the Act is of different character. It could not have been directly or substantially in issue before the Arbitrator. The status of the appellant as a secured creditor has not changed. Further, the formalities for creating the charge having duly followed, the DB has referred to the Form No. 8 and 13 and also adverted to the power of Registrar to make entries of satisfaction and release, as provided u/ss. 138 and 139 of the Act. The respondent was not at fault and, in any case, that would not change the status of the appellant as a secured creditor. The appellant cannot be treated as an unsecured creditor and it is not permissible for him to put forth a stand that it would not be bound by the Scheme that has been approved by the Company Judge. Appeal dismissed.
