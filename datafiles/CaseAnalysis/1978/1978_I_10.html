Tata Chemicals Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

23 March 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 164; (1978) 3 SCC 42; (1978) SCC (L&S) 418; AIR 1978 SC 828; 1978 (36) FLR 339; 1978 (19) GLR 575; 1978 LabIC 637; 1978 (2) LLJ 22; 1978 (1) LLN 547; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Majority Union, Cost Of Living, Consumer Price Index

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(k), 2(p), 10(1)(d), 10A(3A) and 18 - (A) Whether the settlement of 14-12-1973 covered the demand for Variable Dearness Allowance sponsored by the Sangh, and whether the Industrial Tribunal was incompetent to make the award in question during the currency of settlement arrived at by the Union recognised under the Code of Discipline? - Agreement of 14-12-1973 arrived at between the Employees' Union and the appellant Company related only to the special pay and did not cover the Sangh's demand for Variable Dearness Allowance - S. 2(k) of the Act does not restrict an 'industrial dispute' to a dispute between an employer and a recognised majority union but takes within it any dispute or difference between employer and workmen including a minority union - (B) Whether acceptance of benefits from the aforesaid settlement by the members of the Sangh operated as an implied agreement by acquiescence and debarred the Sangh from raising the demand? - An implied agreement by acquiescence, or by conduct such as acceptance of a benefit under an agreement to which the worker acquiescing or accepting the benefit was not a party, being outside the purview of the Act, is not binding on such a worker - (C) Whether the Tribunal could link the scheme of Dearness Allowance with Ahmedabad Dearness Allowance when the recommendation of the Wage Board had been accepted and implemented? - The material on the-record shows that appellant's financial position was so stable that it could not only give Variable Dearness Allowance but could pay even higher allowance as was being paid to its workmen in the Head Office at Bombay - (D) Whether in fixing Dearness Allowance, Tribunal was justified in going beyond the region and taking into consideration for the purpose of comparison the Dearness Allowance paid by other concerns the State? - Where there are no comparable concerns engaged in similar industry in the region, it is permissible for the Tribunal to look to such similar industries or in adjoining region in the State having similar economic conditions - Appeal dismissed.
