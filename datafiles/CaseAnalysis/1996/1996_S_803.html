Keshub Mahindra v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2410; (1996) 6 SCC 129; (1996) SCC (Cr) 1124; 1996 (2) ACC 292; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 657; 1996 (3) CCR 181; 1996 (3) Crimes 288; JT 1996 (8) SC 136; 1996(6) SCALE 522; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 285; 1996 (7) Supreme 77

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 299, 304, 304(II), 324, 326, 336, 337, 338, 34, 35, 39, 425, 429 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Harmful gas leakage - Death - Criminal offence - A highly dangerous and toxic gas escaped from the factory of accused which lead to grievous injuries and death of human beings and animals - Trial Court charged the accused under u/ss. 304, 326, 324, 429 read with s. 35 of the IPC - Appeal made to HC was dismissed and decision of Trial Court was upheld - Accused made appeal in special leave - Whether, charges levied on accused were liable to be set aside - Held, charge for the alleged negligence and rashness of their subordinates who actually operated the plant on that fateful night and for that purpose s. 35 of the IPC would prima facie get attracted against them - A mere look at s. 35 of the IPC showed that if the act alleged against those accused became criminal on account of their sharing common knowledge about the defective running of plant by the remaining accused who represented them on spot and who had to carry out their directions from them and who were otherwise required to supervise their activity then s. 35 of the IPC could at least prima facie be invoked against accused to be read with s. 304-A of the IPC - If the accused were found actually guilty of the charges framed against them or not would strictly depend upon the evidence that may be led at the stage of trial and the court would decide the culpability of the concerned accused, if any strictly confined to the evidence that would be decided at the stage of trial - Present observations should not be treated to be remotely suggested that the accused were guilty of the offences with which they were liable to be charged pursuant to present order - Charges framed against accused u/ss. 304, 324, 326 and 429 of the IPC with or without the aid of s. 35 of the IPC , as the case were quashed and set aside - Order accordingly.
