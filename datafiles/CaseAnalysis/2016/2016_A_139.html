Awadesh Kumar Jha @ Akhilesh Kumar Jha and another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 17; (2016) 3 SCC 8; AIR 2016 SC 373; 2016 CRLJ 1129; 2016 (1) JCC 528; JT 2016 (1) SC 186; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 816; 2016 (2) RLW 1281; 2016(1) SCALE 200

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, General Clauses Act, 1897, Further Investigation, Cognizable, Same Transaction, Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, Distinct

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 239 - Immoral Traffic (Prevention) Act, 1956, ss. 3,4,5,6,7 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 419, 420 - Discharge petition - Further investigation - Appellants were arrested in course of raid and booked for offences punishable u/ss. 3,4,5,6 and 7 of the Act and later, Magistrate took cognizance of said offences vide order dt.6-8-2008 - While so, appellants moved applications for bail but in said bail applications, it was alleged that appellants furnished wrong information regarding their names, father's names and address and so, second FIR was registered against appellants for offences punishable u/ss. 419 and 420 of IPC and Magistrate took cognizance of alleged offences vide order dt.11-9-2008 - Appellants filed revision petitions challenging first order of cognizance and Sessions Judge set aside said order of cognizance holding that no offence u/ss.3,4,5,6 and 7 of the Act was made out against appellants - Later, appellants filed application for discharge from offences alleged in second FIR but the same was dismissed vide order dt.4-12-2013 - Aggrieved, appellants field revision petition but the same was dismissed and so, approached HC but HC also dismissed petition and hence, instant appeal to allow application u/s. 239 of CrPC - Whether application filed by appellants u/s. 239 of CrPC could be allowed.

Held, offences alleged to have committed by appellants in second FIR are distinct offences committed by appellants and same have no connection with offences for which first FIR was registered against them and they could not, in any case, said to be in the form of the part of same transaction with alleged offences under the first FIR. Hence, no question of further investigation could be made by investigating agency on alleged offences arisen. Hence, impugned orders of HC and lower courts on application made u/s. 239 of CrPC by appellants could not be interfered with. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Term 'further investigation' occurred under s. 173(8) of CrPC connotes investigation of the case in continuation of the earlier investigation with respect to which the charge-sheet has already been filed.
