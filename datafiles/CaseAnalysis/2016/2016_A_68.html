Virender Khullar v American Consolidation Services Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 683; AIR 2016 SC 3798; 2016 (119) ALR 442; 2016 (5) AWC 5211; 2016 (6) Bom.C.R. 535; 2016 (3) CPJ(Sc) 22; JT 2016 (8) SC 170; 2016 (4) RCR(Civil) 120; 2016(8) SCALE 1; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, National Commission, Indian Contract Act, 1872

Summary: Consumer Protection - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 230 - Liability of intermediary consignee - Appellants entrusted consignments containing wearing apparels in December 1994 to respondent no.1 and cargo receipts were issued to them by respondent no.1 and as per cargo receipts so issued, consignments were to order of respondent no.2-bank - Respondent no.1 handed over consignments to respondent no.4 for delivery of consignments at the port of destination - However, when payment was not received till March 1995, appellants filed separate complaints before National Commission against respondent no.1 for different amounts - Upon hearing parties, National Commission vide separate orders accepted claims and directed respondent no.1 to pay amount with interest - However, respondent no.1 challenged said orders of National Commission before SC wherein SC set aside orders of National Commission and remanded matter to National Commission with liberty to appellants to implead consignee and carrier in their claim petitions - In pursuance of said orders of SC, respondent nos. 2,3,4 were impleaded and by impugned order National Commission held that it is only respondent no.3, the intermediary consignee of cartons who received the delivery of consignments without making payment to the bank or the complainants, and as such, liable to pay compensation to appellants and accordingly directed respondent no.3 to make payment to appellants - Hence, instant appeals by appellants as other respondents were held not liable to make the payment - Whether impugned order of National Commission could be upheld.

Held, since respondent no.1 was simply acting as an agent of Coronet Group Inc., as such, in view of s. 230 of 1872 Act, it could not be held personally liable to enforce the contract entered between its principal and the appellants and also, as respondent no.1, is not a consignee, but only an agent of intermediate consignee, could not be held to be liable in respect of claim made by appellants. Further, as respondent no.4 had carried the consignment and delivered the same as per Bill of Lading, there is no contract between appellants and respondent no.4. Further, respondent no.2-bank could not be held liable for the deficiency of service, as the amount was not collected from consignee, as such there was no question of remitting it to the appellants by the bank. Under such circumstances, respondent no.3 which is subsidiary to Coronet Group, the consignee named in cargo slips, is the only party which could be held liable for taking delivery without depositing the price of the goods with the Bank. Hence, there is no infirmity in impugned order passed by National Commission and so, appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
