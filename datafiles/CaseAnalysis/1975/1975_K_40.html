Kundur Rudrappa v Mysore Revenue Appellate Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 210; (1975) 2 SCC 411; (1975) SCC (Cr) 698; AIR 1975 SC 1807; 1975 CRLJ 1554; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 188; 1975 UJ 687

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Grant Of Permit

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Administrative - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 48, 64 - Mysore Motor Vehicles Rules, 1963, r. 119 - Mysore Motor Vehicles Rules, 1945, s. 151 - Grant of route permit - Jurisdiction of Tribunals - Sustainability - It was case of appellant that he was granted a stage carriage permit u/s. 48 of the Act for the route in question by Regional Transport Authority by its order dt.3-5-1963 - Aggrieved by said order, appeal were filed - Tribunal dismissed said appeal - On appeal, Revenue Appellate Tribunal also dismissed said appeal - On 25-4-1967, Secretary to Regional Transport Authority called upon appellant to produce relevant documents and certificate of registration for making necessary entry in permit - Appellant produced the same and permit was issued on the same day - Against order of issue of permit, respondents 4 to 13 filed appeals to Tribunal on the ground that Secretary to Regional Transport Authority had no jurisdiction to issue a permit u/r. 119 of the Rules after a lapse of such a long time from date of grant of permit - It was contended by appellant before appellate authorities that there was no period of limitation u/r. 151 of the 1945 Rules (old rules), which was applicable to his case, for the issue of a permit - Tribunal allowed appeals - On appeal, Revenue Appellate Tribunal dismissed appeals - Aggrieved by that order, writ petition was filed before HC - HC dismissed said petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether any appeal lay u/s. 64 of the Act to Tribunal against issue of a permit in pursuance of an earlier resolution of Regional Transport Authority granting permit - Held, there was no dispute that s. 64 of the Act was the only section creating rights of appeal against grant of permit and other matters - There was no appeal provided for u/s. 64 of the Act against an order issuing a permit in pursuance of order granting permit - Issuance of permit was only a ministerial act necessarily following grant of permit - Appeals before Tribunal and further appeal to Revenue Appellate Tribunal were, therefore, not competent u/s. 64 of the Act and both Tribunals had no jurisdiction to entertain appeals and to interfere with order of Regional Transport Authority granting permit which was already affirmed in appeal by Tribunal and further in second appeal by Revenue Appellate Tribunal - Therefore, there was clear error of jurisdiction on part of both Tribunals in interfering with grant of permit to appellant - Impugned order of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
