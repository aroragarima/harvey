M. Lachia Setty and Sons Limited Etc v Coffee Board, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 192; (1980) 4 SCC 636; AIR 1981 SC 162; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 884

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Socio-Economic; Agriculture & Agri. Products; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Coffee Act, 1942 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Loss on re-auction - Damages - Entitlement of - Various quantities of coffee lots were put up for sale by respondent/Coffee Board at its 'pool auction' - In that auction several registered dealers including the two appellants participated and lodged their bids - Bids of appellants were accepted by PW 3, though some of bids in respect of five lots were not highest, and they were declared to be successful bidders - On appellants' failure to pay for and take delivery of lots, another notice of re-sale was issued and lots were released on lower price - Respondent filed batch suits before Trial Court against defaulting bidders including appellants - Trial Court on appreciation of entire material accepted defenses raised by appellants and dismissed the suits - Respondent appealed before HC - HC allowed the appeals and decreed respondent's claims against appellants - Aggrieved by said order appellants filed instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, material on record clearly showed evidence of PW3/Chief Coffee Marketing Officer, had been that before commencement of 'pool auction' on that day he had issued oral warning to bidders that Govt. of India was concerned about increase in coffee prices and that they should not try to push up prices and corner stocks and DW1 had admitted that PW3 had given warning that higher bids would not be accepted - In spite of such warning being issued unnecessarily higher bids were given exceeding average prices prevailing in month of September 1952 - PW3 decided to accept lower bids in preference to higher ones - It was in these circumstances that at re-sale held on December 23, 1952 prices realized were lower than appellants' bids which had been accepted at 'pool auction' held on 7-10-1952 - It must be stated here that at re-sale admittedly only highest bids were accepted - It was impossible to subscribe to proposition that Board should had maintained high price level at cost of consumers merely with view to see that defaulting bidders did not suffer any loss on re-sale - Loss arising on re-sale, could not be regarded as 'unreal' loss - Attack of appellants against grant of damages to respondent on this ground was clearly unsustainable - Regard the alleged delay in holding re-sale it must be observed that both Trial Court as well as HC had taken view that same was held within reasonable time at next 'pool auction' conducted in normal course - Results of concerned 'pool auction' were declared on 8-10-1952 - Period of 17 days expired on 26-10-1952, but evidence on record showed that there was general request on behalf of successful bidders for extension of time for making payment and taking delivery and such extension had been granted by Board up to 10-11-1952 by issuing circular - There was no valid retraction of bids by appellants and to their knowledge their retraction had been rejected by Board on 8-10-1952 itself - Appellants were interested in extension granted by Board becomes evident from their telegram seeking confirmation of extension - Later some reasonable notice of re-sale would have to be issued, so defaulted coffee could not be put up for sale in pool auction that was held in month of November, 1952 - Next pool auction was to be held in December, 1952 and, after issuing notice of re-sale on 18-12-1952 the re-sale was held by conducting pool auction on 23-12-1952 - Both Courts were right in taking view that re-sale had been held within reasonable time - Appeals dismissed.
