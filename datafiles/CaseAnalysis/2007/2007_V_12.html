Lt. Governor Of Delhi And Others v V.K. Sodhi And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 812; (2007) 15 SCC 136; AIR 2007 SC 2885; 2007 (7) AWC 7459; JT 2007 (10) SC 137; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 902; 2007(10) SCALE 41; 2007 (6) SCJ 385; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 1027; 2007 (5) SLR 400; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 7

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: High Court held that State Council of Education, Research and Training is a State within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution Of India, 1950 and Lieutenant Governor of Delhi, SCERT and others are bound to implement policy decision of SCERT as reflected in reg. 67 framed by it as also the Advanced Career Promotion Scheme - Appeal against - Whether in the context of the functions entrusted to it, the rules and bye-laws that govern it and the financial position enjoyed by it, SCERT can be said to be financially, functionally and administratively dominated by or under the control of the Government? - Held, SCERT is subservient to the provisions of the Societies Registration Act, 1860 rather than to the State Government and that the intention was to keep SCERT as an independent body and the role of the State Government cannot be compared to that of the Central Government in the case of Council of Scientific and Industrial Research - High Court has not independently discussed the relevant rules governing the functioning and administration of SCERT - SCERT is not a State or other authority within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution and normally not amenable to the jurisdiction of the High Court - Appeals allowed.
