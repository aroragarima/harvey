Jatinder Singh and Another v Mehar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2069; (2009) 17 SCC 465; AIR 2009 SC 354; 2009(1) ALL MR 495; 2008 (73) ALR 295; 2008 (4) AWC 4151; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 424; 2008 (5) CTC 374; JT 2008 (11) SC 30; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 4376; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 45; 2008 (8) MLJ(SC) 1137; 2009 (153) PLR 435; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 253; 2009 (107) RD 327; 2008(12) SCALE 734

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.41 r. 27 - Suit for declaration - Additional evidence - Legality - Appellants/plaintiffs filed suit for declaration and challenged sale deed executed by respondent nos. 1 to 3 in favour of respondent nos. 9 and 10 as well as which suit was compromised with them - On appeal before HC appellants filed application under O.41 r.27 of CPC for acceptance of additional evidence which was not considered by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether application for acceptance of additional evidence under O.41 r. 27 of CPC would be allowable.

Held, when application for acceptance of additional evidence under O.41 r.27 of CPC was filed by appellants, it was duty of HC to deal with same on merits. That being admitted position, Court have no other alternative but to set aside judgment of HC and remit appeal back to it for decision afresh in second appeal along with application for acceptance of additional evidence in accordance with law. For reasons aforesaid, Judgment is set aside. Appeal is thus allowed to extent indicated above. Court does not feel inclined to entertain Interlocutory Application filed by petitioner during when matter was kept for Judgment. Accordingly, said application for interim direction is disposed of as infructuous. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Under O.47 r.27 of CPC provides that parties to appeal shall not be entitled to produce additional evidence, whether oral or documentary, in Appellate Court, but if Court from whose decree appeal is preferred has refused to admit evidence which ought to have been admitted.
