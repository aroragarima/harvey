Transmission Corporation of Andhra Pradesh Limited, Hyderabad v Sayeed Ahmed Siddiqui

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1595; (2007) 15 SCC 763

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Unauthorised absence, Termination

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - A.P. State Electricity Board Service Regulations, reg. 28(3) - Unauthorised absence - Termination - Reinstatement - Sustainability - Respondents were employees of appellants Electricity Board - Respondents remained unauthorisedly absent for long period - Respondents were deemed to have resigned from service by invoking reg. 28(3) of the Regulations by appellants - Respondents filed petition before HC - Petition was allowed - Appellant filed appeal before DB - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, respondents were reinstated by HC and ends of justice would be met if respondents were allowed to continue in service till superannuation without any back wages to be paid - Respondents submitted that appropriate orders as regards back wages could be made, however, benefit of continuity of service might be given to respondents for purpose of calculating retrial or terminal benefits - Appellants submitted that position of law as stated in decisions in regard to regn. on 28(3) was clear and appellants were not entitled to any back wages even if orders terminating services could be upheld - These were not cases where respondents' services were terminated on account of any misconduct but their services, it was ended by operation of second regn. 28(3) - Respondents had given their own explanation for unauthorisedly remaining absent - Hence, respondents should be allowed to superannuate and should be not be entitled to any back wages, however, they should be entitled for continuity of service only for purpose of calculating retiral or terminal benefits - Appeals disposed of.
