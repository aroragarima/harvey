Gail India Limited v Sales Tax Officer, Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1315; (2005) 10 SCC 192

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Gujarat Sales Tax Rules, 1970, Duty Entitlement Pass Book Scheme

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 21-E, 21-F - Inter-State sales - Liability to pay sales tax - Benefit of concessional rate - Jurisdiction - Appellant purchased gas from Oil and Natural Gas Commission (ONGC) which was supplied by pipelines from State of Gujarat to purchasers within Gujarat and in various other States those were only stock transfers in respect of which local sales tax had been paid not only in Gujarat but also in States in which sales took place - In respect of A/y, assessment orders were passed against appellant under 1956 Act, on ground that transfers effected by appellant were in fact inter-State sales within meaning of 1956 Act and, therefore, liable to Central sales tax and that in absence of any C form appellant was not entitled to benefit of concessional rate of sales tax duty - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether particular transactions entered between appellant were inter-State sales - Held, appellant had exported aluminum through Vishakhapatnam under Duty Entitlement Pass Book Scheme (DEPB) where appellant was entitled to get credit on import duty of goods which were allowed to be imported by appellant up to prescribed limit - However, right of appellant was recognized under EXIM Policy to be freely transferable - Show-cause notice was issued by Commercial Tax Officer claiming that transaction was local sale within meaning of 1957 Act - DEPB licenses were registered with Vishakhapatnam port through which exports were made and therefore, site of sales was Andhra Pradesh - Appellant submitted that sale was inter-State sale because licenses were granted by Director General of Foreign Trade under DEPB Scheme in Cuttack - Appellant carried on business in Bhubaneshwar where auction was held - Therefore, there was no question of sale being effected in Vishakhapatnam, because of extraterritorial element that purchasers of benefit under DEPB Scheme were from Bengal and Maharashtra, sales were inter-State sales - Thus, provisions of ss. 21-E, 21-F of 1957 Act, appellant could not have approached Tribunal or HC - Matters were transferred to Andhra Pradesh Sales Tax Appellate Tribunal for determining said issues and any other issues which were raised - Appeal disposed of.
