Shakuntala Modi (Ms.) v Om Prakash Bharuka

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 789; (1991) 2 SCC 706; AIR 1991 SC 1104; 1992 (19) ALR 168; 1991 (2) CCC 47; JT 1992 (1) SC 53; 1991 (2) UJ 92

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Custody Of Children, Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Transfer Of Proceedings, Requirement of

Summary: Women & Children - Practice & Procedure - Custody of children - Transfer of proceedings - Requirement of - Marriage between petitioner (wife) and respondent (husband) was dissolved by a decree of divorce - Petitioner and respondent were litigating for the custody of their children - Petitioner filed instant application for transfer of a case instituted by respondent before Guardian Judge, Delhi, to the Court of District Judge, Dibrugarh, where an earlier application filed by petitioner was pending.

Held, it is highly expedient that the cases are heard by the same Court. On an earlier occasion the respondent made an application for transfer of the Dibrugarh case of Delhi, which was rejected by the Court. The Dibrugarh case, therefore, has to be heard there. In the circumstances it is proper to transfer the Delhi case to Dibrugarh. Although it may cause the respondent some trouble of undertaking the journey to Dibrugarh but, for that reason in the facts of instant case it cannot be assumed that he will be prejudiced in prosecuting his case. If he is not in any financial difficulty, as it appears from the records and his own statement before Court, he can make an appropriate arrangement for his representation at Dibrugarh and may not remain at Dibrugarh continuously for looking after the cases. Petition allowed.
