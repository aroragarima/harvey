Ibrahim Haji Moidee and another v Food Inspector, Nileshwar and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 505; (1969) 3 SCC 901; (1970) SCC (Cr) 167; 1969 KLT 62

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Summary: Health & Drug - Socio-Economic - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7, 2(1)(a) and 16(1) - (A) Adulteration of food articles - Conviction of Accused no. 2 u/s. 16(1) of Adulteration Act - Challenged on ground of absence from place of alleged act to be done - Held, the judgment of the Sessions Judge on the face of it is manifestly illegal - For the purpose of a conviction under the charge on which A-2 was tried, it was immaterial whether he was an agent or a partner of A-1 - Once it is proved that it was he who sold the adulterated article, he was liable to be convicted u/s. 16(1), r/w. s. 7 of Act - (B) Interference by the High Court in the matter of sentence - Legality - Held, s. 16(1) of the Act prescribes a minimum sentence a minimum sentence of six months imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/ - It is true as provided in the proviso to that section, the court may for adequate and special reasons to be mentioned in the judgment impose a sentence of imprisonment for less than six months or a fine of less than Rs. 1,000/- - The Trial Court has imposed the minimum sentence prescribed u/s. 16(1) - The only reason given by the Sessions Judge for reducing that sentence is that the 1st accused was not present when the sale was effected - This is not a relevant consideration u/s. 16(1) - At any rate it cannot be considered as an 'adequate or special reason' - HC rightly convicted since no material found in evidence produced by accused no 2 and conviction confirmed - Appeal dismissed.
