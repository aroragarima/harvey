Â Hyderabad Chemical and Pharmaceuticalworks v State Of Andhra Pradesh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1964 SC 1870; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 376

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Medical and Toilet Preparation (Excise Duties) Act 16 of 1955, Hyderabad Medical Preparations and spirituous Rules 1345 F

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 277 - Excise - Trade - Medicinal & Toilet Preparations Excise Duties Act, 1955, s. 21, rr. 143 and 141 - Hyderabad Medicinal Preparations and Spirituous Rules 1345 F, r. 36 - Hyderabad Abkari Act, No. 1 of 1316 F - (A) Whether new rule in 1956 replaces old rules? - Held, proviso cannot change position in view of new Rules framed in 1956 with respect to medicinal preparations - As soon as new rule came into force old rules must fall and there is a specific provision in new rule (namely r. 143) which says that all rules made under any law corresponding to Act are hereby repealed - Supervisory staff which has to be paid for u/r. 36 is meant for supervision of manufacture of medicinal preparations and it is for that purpose only that expenses have to be borne by laboratory concerned - Purpose of rule therefore is clearly covered by Act and Rules framed thereunder and it cannot survive Act and Rules in view of s. 21 of Medicinal & Toilet Preparations Excise Duties Act, 1955 and r. 143 of 1956 Rules - (B) Whether r. 36 can be justified as a good rule under general law relating to alcohol and intoxicating drugs?- Further held, it cannot Act of 1955 or 1956 Rules make no provision for any such charge as is provided in r. 36 of 1345-F Rules - Levy u/r. 36 of 1345-F, cannot be justified on ground that under that rule supervisory staff has to see that general law relating to alcohol and intoxicating drugs is not violated - There is no doubt that field covered by r. 36 of 1345 F is completely covered by Rules framed under Act and therefore r. 36 can no longer be justified as good under general law relating to alcohol and intoxicating drugs - Intention being that duty under Act will cover all expenses for enforcing it - Fact that members of supervisory staff are servants of state makes no difference because they function under Act and rules framed there under and not under Hyderabad Act - Appeal allowed.
