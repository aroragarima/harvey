Sajeesh Babu K. v N.K. Santhosh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 631; (2012) 12 SCC 106

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Petroleum & Gas

Summary: Petroleum & Gas - Administrative - LPG distributorship - Grant for licence - Respondent no. 2 invited applications for grant of LPG distributorship, distributorship reserved for Scheduled Caste applicants - In total, 41 persons including appellant and respondent nos. 1 and 3 applied for grant of licence for same - Respondent no.2 after conducting interviews and evaluating merits and demerits of candidates as per procedure prescribed under guidelines for selection of 'Bharatgas' Distributors, selected appellant for grant of licence of LPG distributorship and issued him letter of intent - Respondent No.1 filed petition before HC Single Judge who allowed petition and quashed distributorship granted to appellant - DB of HC dismissed further appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether judgment passed by Single Bench and DB was liable to be set aside - Held, there was no allegation of mala fides against 3 experts in Selection Committee - Expert committee evaluated experience certificates produced by appellant, interviewed him by putting specific questions as to direct sale, home delivered products, hospitality/service industry etc. and awarded marks - In such circumstances, HC should not to have sat as Appellate Court on recommendations made by Expert committee - It was asserted by Corporation and informed to HC as well as to Court that in order to ascertain genuineness of contents of experience certificates, Corporation deputed responsible persons for verification and they met issuing authority and satisfied with correctness of their statement - In view of that aspect, satisfied that Single Judge as well as DB committed error in interfered with decision of Selection Committee - There was no allegation of mala fides against members of Selection Committee - Therefore, HC was not justified in upsetting decision of Selection Committee, particularly, in absence of any mala fides against them and there was no warrant for direction to re-assess marks of appellant afresh by excluded marks for certificates, particularly, in light of detailed explanation offered by Corporation about mode of selection - Hence, judgment of Single Judge and DB of HC was set aside and confirm decision of Selection Committee - Appeal allowed.
