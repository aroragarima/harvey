Union Of India & Ors. v E. Bashyan

Supreme Court of India

11 March 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 666; (1988) 2 SCC 196; (1988) SCC (L&S) 531; AIR 1988 SC 1000; 1988 (7) ATC 285; 1988 BLJR 477; 1988 (56) FLR 701; JT 1988 (1) SC 627; 1988 LabIC 1702; 1988 (2) LLJ 249; 1989 (1) LLN 201; 1988(1) SCALE 578; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 209; 1988 (1) UJ 621

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Enquiry Officer's report was not made available to delinquent before Disciplinary Authority passed final order recording finding of guilt against him - Whether failure to supply a copy of Report of Enquiry Officer to delinquent before Disciplinary Authority makes up his mind and records finding of guilt as against him would constitute violation of art. 311(2) of Constitution and violation of principles of natural justice? - Held, opportunity required to be given to a delinquent which must be reasonable opportunity compatible with principles of natural justice has not been dispensed with by virtue of amendment of art. 311 in 1977 - Non-supply of inquiry report would constitute violation of principles of natural justice and accordingly will be tantamount to denial of reasonable opportunity within meaning of art. 311(2) of Constitution - Matter referred to larger Bench.
