Godfrey Phillips India Limited v Ajay Kumar

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Cigarettes And Other Tobacco Products (Prohibition Of Advertisement And Regulation Of Trade And Commerce, Production, Supply And Distribution) Act, 2003

Summary: Cigarettes and other Tobacco Products (Prohibition of Advertisement and Regulation of Trade and Commerce, Production, Supply and Distribution) Act, 2003; Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Unfair trade practices - National Commission issued some directions against advertisement - Appeals against - Held, direction (i) was given without any material or evidence whatsoever and there was not even a suggestion/pleading that the advertisement was of Akshay Kumar or that he could perform certain stunts without duplicates - When such serious allegation which was required to be established was not even specifically pleaded and when nothing specific was indicated in the complaint, the Commission should not have given the direction on pure surmises - Corrective advertisement as directed by the National Commission could not have been given - Further, power for giving such direction was introduced under s. 14 of the Act w.e.f. 15.3.2003 - Award of compensation to the consumer for any loss or injury suffered due to negligence of the opposite party - Appeals allowed.
