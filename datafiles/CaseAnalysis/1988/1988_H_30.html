S.B. Mathur And Others v Hon’Ble The Chief Justice Of Delhi High Court,And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 August 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.H. Kania, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 591; (1989) Supp1 SCC 34; (1989) SCC (L&S) 183; AIR 1988 SC 2073; 1989 (9) ATC 286; JT 1988 (3) SC 507; 1989 LabIC 51; 1988(2) SCALE 615; 1988 (4) SLR 505; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 772; 1989 (1) UJ 82

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Administrative Instructions, Punjab Civil Service (Executive Branch) Rules, 1930, Delhi High Court Staff (Seniority) Rules 1971, Delhi High Court Establishment (Appointment and Conditions of service) Rules 1972

Summary: Service -Delhi High Court Act, 1966 - Delhi High Court Staff (Seniority) Rules 1971 - Delhi High Court Establishment (Appointment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 1972, r.7, sch.2, No.3 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Appointment of Assistant Registrar by selection - (A) Constitutionality of treating posts of Superintendents, Court Masters and Private Secretaries to Judges as equal status posts - (B) Whether consideration of 5 persons for each vacancy on the basis of seniority in combined seniority list is arbitrary when there were many eligible persons - (C) Use of combined seniority list of Superintendents, Court Masters and Private Secretaries - Sustainability - Held, does not violate rule of equality - Not arbitrary - Cannot be struck down on ground that prospects of promotion of Superintendents are reduced and validity of the decision to limit the zone of consideration cannot be successfully called in question on the ground that the manner in which the zone of consideration was limited was not uniform -Petition disposed of .
