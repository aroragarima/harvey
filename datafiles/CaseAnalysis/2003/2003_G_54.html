State of Andhra Pradesh And another v Goverdhanlal Pitti

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 262; (2003) 4 SCC 739; AIR 2003 SC 1941; 2003 (3) ALD(SC) 32; 2003 (51) ALR 200; 2003 (3) AWC 2417; 2003 (2) CGLJ 158; JT 2003 (5) SC 74; 2003 (2) KHCACJ 466; 2003 (2) MLJ(SC) 137; 2003 (2) RCR(Civil) 295; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 445; 2003 (1) RCR(Rent) 455; 2003(3) SCALE 107; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 908; 2003 (3) SLT 48; 2003 (Supp) RD 504; 2003 (2) Supreme 713; 2003 (2) UC 954; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1667

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land and Property - Land Acquisition Act - Acquisition of school building, which was hundred years old, dilapidated and in dangerous condition, by State - HC held the acquisition of the school building by the State as an action liable to be quashed on ground of its being 'malicious in law' - Whether HC's finding proper? - Held, resort to acquisition at a stage when there was no other alternative but to do so to serve a genuine public which was being fulfilled from 1954 signify more a reasonable and just exercise of statutory power - Such exercise of power cannot be condemned as one made in colourable or mala fide exercise of it - Public purpose does exist for the State to acquire the school premises - As a tenant the State had several inhibitions in law in effecting substantial repairs to the building or reconstructing it, but once the State acquires the school building, it had many options for making it suitable for continuing the school - Admittedly, the school building was hundred years old, dilapidated and in dangerous condition Shifting school building to the alternative site had, therefore, become necessary to avoid any possible catastrophe by collapse of the building - Appeal allowed.
