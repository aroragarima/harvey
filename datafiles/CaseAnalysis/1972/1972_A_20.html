Ajitprasad Ramkishan Singh v The State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 300; (1972) 2 SCC 180; (1972) SCC (Cr) 651; AIR 1972 SC 1631; 1972 CRLJ 1026; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 483; 1973 UJ 65

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Sample For Analysis

Summary: Criminal -Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954, ss. 7, 13(2) and 16(1)(a)(i) - Selling of milk having less fat - Milk had 2 7% of milk fat instead of 6% - Convicted accordingly - Contended that he was devoid of right to get his sample analysed by Director -Didn't file an application for re examination of sample - Whether detention of accused under Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954? -In absence of any application by accused u/s. 13(2) of Act of 1954 for getting sample analysed by Director accused could not complain that he was deprived of his right to have sample analysed by Director - Mere delay and laches on part of complainant in getting summons served was not, in absence of evidence to show that sample had deteriorated when summons was served, sufficient to hold that accused was prejudiced by reason of deprivation of right u/s. 13(2) - Held, accused was rightly convicted u/s. 16(1)(a)(i) r/w s.7(1) - Appeal Dismissed.
