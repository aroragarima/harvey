Rajeev Mankotia v The Secretary To The President Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1124; (1997) 10 SCC 441; AIR 1997 SC 2766; 1997 (3) CLT 70; JT 1997 (5) SC 663; 1997(4) SCALE 368; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 421; 1997 (6) Supreme 184

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1904

Summary: Ancient Monuments and Archeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958, s. 4 - Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1904 - Preservation of historical monument (Viceregal Lodge at Shimla, which was summer seat of Asian British Empire) - A witness to heralding new era of independence to India - The need for its preservatiion is also under consideration of Supreme Court - Held, notifying its entire area as historical heritage has become faite accompli by orders of SC, which also directed Govt. to maintain all national monuments under the respective Acts and ensure that all of them are properly maintained - Petition disposed of.
