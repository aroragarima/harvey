Â Bikas Chatterjee v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, C.K. Thakker, G.P. Mathur, R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1402; (2004) 7 SCC 634; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2018; 2004(10) SCALE 109

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 72, 161 and 226 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 376 and 380 - Convict in this case has been held guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 376, 380 and 302, IPC, 1860 and sentenced to death u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 - Judicial review against an order passed by President of India u/art. 72 of Constitution - Capital sentence - Held, petition for pardon filed before President of India remained pending receiving his consideration for about six weeks - No reason to assume that President of India has not applied his mind to all relevant facts and aspects of case - Nor court inclined to hold that there is any material which President considered relevant and was inclined to look into but was not before him or was not called for by him when he took decision to, reject petition for grant of pardon - No case has been made out for making a departure from presumption and assumption which attaches with order of President of India passed u/art. 72 of Constitution - Petition wholly devoid of any merit - Petition dismissed.
