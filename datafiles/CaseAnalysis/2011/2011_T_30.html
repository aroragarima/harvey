The Director General, Indian Council For Agricultural Research & Others v D. Sundara Raju

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 199; (2011) 6 SCC 605; 2011 (8) ADJ 36; 2011 (4) AWC 4223; 2011 (2) ESC 320; JT 2011 (4) SC 183; 2011(4) SCALE 83; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 95; 2011 (2) SLR 723; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1098

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Rajasthan Administrative Service Rules, 1954, Rajasthan Forest Service Rules, 1962, Combined Competitive Examination) Rules, 1962, Career Advancement Scheme, 2004

Summary: Service - Career Advancement Scheme (CAS) - Assessment for promotion - Allocation of 50% marks for personal interview - Justifiability - Respondent Scientist sought promotion to the post of Principal Scientist in terms of CAS formulated by appellant organization - Respondent appeared before Selection Committee for assessment and personal interview and Selection Committee awarded 49 marks in total to respondent - Since 60 marks was required to qualify for promotion, respondent's candidature was rejected as he secured only 49 marks - Respondent filed application before Tribunal challenging appellant's rejection order - Respondent contended that selection procedure adopted by the Selection Committee were against the norms of CAS - Tribunal set aside appellant's order holding that it had acted in arbitrary manner to allocate 50% marks for personal interview - Appellant challenged Tribunal order before HC - HC confirmed Tribunal order - Hence instant appeal - Whether allocation of 50% marks for personal interview in promotion to the post of Principal Scientist was justifiable - Held, promotion to post of Principal Scientist pertained to CAS and hence norms, rules and guidelines which were employed while granting benefit of CAS ought to be applied in respondent's case also - Appellant did not disclose to the respondent that interview would be held for evaluating personal or intellectual qualities attributable to a Scientist and that it should carry 50% of total marks - Had appellants disclosed method of evaluation respondent may have challenged same before participating in selection process - 50% marks allocated for interview were highly excessive for post of a Principal Scientist and contrary to settled legal position crystallized from a series of judgments of SC - Appellants were totally unjustified in allocating 50% marks for interview particularly when appellants did not even disclose to respondent that interview would also be held to evaluate suitability of candidate for said post - Procedure evolved by Selection Committee for evaluating respondent was totally arbitrary and contrary to settled legal position - Appellants themselves had found 50% marks for interview highly excessive subsequently changing the criterion from 50% to 10% - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
