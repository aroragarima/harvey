State of Himachal Pradesh v Keshav Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1697; (1996) 11 SCC 257; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 727; AIR 1997 SC 2181; JT 1996 (9) SC 208; 1996(7) SCALE 456; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 263; 1996 (7) Supreme 450

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Administrative - Revenue records - Respondents filed suit for declaration that they are owners in possession of suit land and for restraining appellant-State from interfering with possession of respondents - Trial Court, relying upon order passed by Assistant Settlement Officer, held that respondents are owners and decreed suit - On appeal, Lower Appellate Court confirmed decree of Trial Court and so, State preferred second appeal to HC - Single Judge of HC dismissed appeal solely relying on order of Assistant Settlement Officer - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondents established their title on land.

Held, alleged order made by Assistant Settlement Officer directing correction of record of right is not on record but respondents relied upon register where the correction appears to have been given effect to. In absence of said order of Assistant Settlement Officer directing necessary correction to be made in favour of respondents, it is not possible to envisage on what basis said direction was made. Nevertheless, entry in revenue papers could not form the basis for declaration of title in favour of respondents. Further, respondents could not point out any other document apart from the alleged correction made in register pursuant to order of Assistant Settlement Officer. Hence, lower courts committed serious error of law in declaring title of respondents on the basis of the order of correction and consequential entry in revenue papers. Hence, judgment and decree passed in all three forums are set aside. Appeal allowed.
