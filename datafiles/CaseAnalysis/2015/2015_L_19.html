Diwan Singh v Life Insurance Corporation of India and others

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 2; (2015) 2 SCC 341; 2015(2) ALL MR 481; 2015 (2) AWC 1181; 2015 (144) FLR 1009; JT 2015 (1) SC 1; 2015 (6) MahLJ 542; 2015 (4) MPLJ 491; 2015(1) SCALE 16; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2015 (2) SLR 1; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 20

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Life Insurance corporation of India (employees) Pension Rules, 1995

Summary: Service - Misconduct - Removal from service - Challenged - Appellant (Cashier in LIC) was removed from service on the ground of misconduct - Departmental appeal filed against said order was dismissed by the authority - HC allowed petition filed against said order - On appeal, DB partly allowed the appeal by substituting punishment of compulsory retirement in place of removal from service - Appellant challenged the order of the DB on the ground that the punishment of compulsory retirement was disproportionate, unreasonable and harsh.

Held, explanation put forth does not appear to be convincing, as the cashier would not have issued a receipt without counting the cash at the counter. The finding of the enquiry officer holding the appellant guilty, cannot be said to be against the evidence on record. As far as argument relating to quantum of punishment, SC do not find the punishment to be harsh or disproportionate to the guilt, in view of the nature of the charge of which the appellant is found guilty. No interference with impugned order. Appeal dismissed.
