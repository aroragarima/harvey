Sukanti Moharana v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 936; (2009) 9 SCC 163; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 349; JT 2009 (9) SC 697; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1252; 2009(10) SCALE 246; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 996

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Post Graduate

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 498A and 302 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Dying Declaration - Appellant, wife of brother in law of deceased - Dowry harassment and harassment on account of illicit affair between husband of deceased and appellant - Per prosecution case, appellant poured kerosene oil from behind the deceased when she was having her meal and alight her, and after that closed the door and went out - Trial Court convicted the appellant u/ss. 302, 304B and 498A of IPC, and u/s. 4 of DP Act; husband of the deceased was also convicted under same sections except s. 302, IPC - HC in appeal set aside conviction of appellant u/ss. 304B and 498A and u/s. 4 of DP Act but upheld conviction u/s. 302, IPC; and acquitted the husband - Judgment of HC whether proper and sustainable? - Held, yes - HC placed reliance on DD - DD was recorded by PW10 doctor in presence of other doctors; before such DD their was oral DD which was made by the deceased before her parents as well as other witnesses, and such witnesses duly testified before the Trial Court as to such declaration - DD perused, materials on record noted, contentions and submissions heard - Written DD duly corroborated by the oral DD - PW 10/doctor stated clearly in his evidence that the deceased was mentally clear and was able to make proper statement while making the DD - Further, contemporaneous evidence on record which made clear that the doctor recording the DD had recorded that the patient was oriented to time and place and mentally clear at the time of recording the DD - Written DD found to be reliable and cogent - Objections raised by defence found to be just technical - Findings and judgment of HC, upheld - Appeal dismissed.
