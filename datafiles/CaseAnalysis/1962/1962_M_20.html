Nibaran Chandra Bag Etc v Mahendra Nath Ghughu

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 443; AIR 1963 SC 1895; 1963 (1) KerLR 353; 1963 (2) SCJ 552; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 570; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1953 - Record-of-rights Of raiyat - (A) Whether courts below had erred in holding that appellant was not a raiyat? - Held, one of items of objection to record of rights raised by respondent related to an error in description of status of appellant as a raiyat - Further, appellant then raised no objection to examination by officer to correctness of that entry - (B) Whether respondent was a temporary lessee? - Further held, that respondent was in possession of this area from january 1954 was not disputed by appellant but his case was that respondent was his manager on a monthly salary - Respondent having admittedly been in actual possession of property, only controversy related character in which he was in possession - Even if description of respondent as lessee by third parties in several criminal proceedings be discarded as res inter alios acta, it was certainly within jurisdiction of a 'Settlement Officer' to appraise truth of story of appellant who claimed that respondent was his Manager on a salary - No just exception would be taken to action of Officer in drawing an inference adverse to appellant from non-production of his accounts to prove payment of salary to respondent - Officer could legally accept respondent's case that he was a lessee and not a Paid Manager and that his possession was attributable to that character - Taken in conjunction with antecedent history, it would not be an unreasonable inference to draw that character in which respondent was to remain in possession till he undertook to quit was as a lessee - Civil appeal 105/1960 filed by appellant dismissed - Civil Appeal filed by Respondent allowed.
