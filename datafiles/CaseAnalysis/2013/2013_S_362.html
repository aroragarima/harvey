Purushotham and others v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 783; (2014) 3 SCC 721; AIR 2014 SC 1430; 2014(1) ALL MR 945; 2013(14) SCALE 515

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Bangalore Development Authority Act, 1976, ss.2(bb), 2(bb)(vi), 38A(1),(2) - Use of Civic amenity area for purpose other than the one for which such area was reserved - Maintainability - Appellant impugned the judgment of a DB of HC whereby the High Court has declared the allotment of civic amenity site to respondent No. 3 for establishment of a petrol pump, null and void - By the impugned judgment, DB of HC on interpretation of s.38A of Act concluded that the allotment was in violation of s.38A(2) of Act - HC had concluded that the site at the time of its allotment to respondent No.3 was expressly earmarked for use as 'bank' - Therefore, in terms of s.38A of Act could not be leased, sold or otherwise transferred for a purpose other than the one for which such area was reserved - Since the site in question was earmarked/reserved for 'bank', it could not have been allotted for use as a petrol pump - HC also held that the allotment of the site was null and void as it was not in consonance of s.38A(2) of Act - HC further observed that even though both 'bank' and 'petrol pump' were civic amenities within the meaning of s. 2(bb) of Act, yet the mandate of s.38A of Act was clear and unambiguous - It was for the very civic amenity, for which the area was reserved, for which it had to be put to use - Appellant contended that HC had erred in holding that any area of particular civic amenity could not be subsequently changed to another user which also falls within the definition of a civic amenity - It was submitted by appellants that HC had failed to appreciate that the sites still remained allotted to a civic amenity - Merely, because the user was changed from public park to bank and to petrol pump would not violate the provisions contained in s.38A(1) and (2) of Act - It was contended that since the Notification was duly issued that petrol pump would be a civic amenity as provided u/s.2(bb)(vi) of Act, there was no violation of s.38A(2) of Act - Held, Act would have the authority to lease, sell or otherwise transfer any area reserved for the purpose for which such area was reserved, and no other - It clearly meant that the Govt. could pass on the responsibility to another concern, be it individual, company or corporation for the purposes of carrying on the activity for which the plot was reserved as a civic amenity - It did not give a licence to Act to convert the area reserved for civic amenities for activities which did not fall within the definition of civic amenities - S. 38(2) of Act was an embargo that even such sale or disposal otherwise of an area reserved for public parks, playground would not be permitted to private parties - Though such spaces, playgrounds and parks could be transferred to public authorities, but their user would be limited to the purposes for which they were reserved under the scheme - In case, a disposition was made for a purpose other than the one for which it was reserved, the Act had declared that, it should be null and void - R.3 of Act which the support was sought by the appellants could not be permitted to override the statutory provision contained in s.38A(1) and (2) of Act - Even otherwise, the rule only reiterated the statutory provision in s.38A(1) and (2) of Act - SC did not find any substance in the submission that the site was never allotted as a bank, and, therefore, it could be allotted as a petrol pump - SC did not find any merit in the submission that the term civic amenities would permit Act to change the reservation from one particular user to another without the necessary amendment in the development plan - Bangalore Medical Trust Vs. B.S. Muddappa & Ors. 1991 Indlaw SC 1038, relied on - Appeals dismissed.
