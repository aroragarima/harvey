Kanshi Ram and Another v Lachhman (Dead) Through LRs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20109; (2001) 5 SCC 546; AIR 2001 SC 2393; 2001 (44) ALR 505; JT 2001 (5) SC 324; 2001 (3) RCR(Civil) 766; 2001(4) SCALE 253; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 803; 2001 (5) SLT 154; 2001 (5) Supreme 9

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Final Decree For Foreclosure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Himachal Pradesh Restitution of Mortgaged Lands Act, 1976, s. 4 - Transfer of Property Act, 188, ss. 61 and 62 - Himachal Pradesh Debt Reduction Act, 1976, ss. 4, 5, 6, 7, 8 and 9 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 34, 16, 17, 2(ix), 2(xiv) and 2(xiii) - Whether High Court is right in dismissing suit filed by appellants as barred by limitation? - Held, First Appellate Court had given cogent reasons in support of its finding in favour of appellants - Suit was one for recovery of possession from mortgagee who was in unauthorised possession of mortgaged property after mortgage loan was satisfied - Cause of action for filing such a suit under Act arose when enactment was enforced in 1979 - Viewed from that angle suit was filed in time and Trial Court and First Appellate Court rightly recorded findings to that effect - HC erred in reversing concurrent finding of courts below on erroneous assumption that suit was one for redemption of mortgage simpliciter - Present suit is not one filed u/ss. 60 or 62 of Transfer of Property Act - It is a suit filed for relief on basis of Himachal Pradesh Debt Reduction Act, 1976 - Judgment of HC is set aside - Appeal allowed.
