Power Control Appliances and Others v Sumeet Machines Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mohan, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 19; (1994) 2 SCC 448; AIR 1994 SCW 2760; 1994 (1) ARBLR 308; JT 1994 (2) SC 994; 1994 (2) MLJ(SC) 74; 1995 (15) PTC 165; 1994(1) SCALE 446; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate; Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Companies Act, 1956 - Design Act, 1911 - Copyright Act, 1957, ss. 17, 19 and 54 - Whether, there is an infringement of the trade mark, the copyright and the design should not have dismissed the application for injunction solely on the ground of honest and concurrent user and on the plea of acquiescence? - Yes, Court held, plea of honest and concurrent user as stated in s. 12(3) of 1958 Act for securing the concurrent registration is not a valid defence for the infringement of copyright - Appeal allowed.
