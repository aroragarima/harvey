Â K. S. Srinivasan v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 153; AIR 1958 SC 419; 1958 SCJ 777; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 1295

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Indirect Tax; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, rr.6(ii), 4(b), 3 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 311(2) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - (A) Whether posts in abeyance comes within expression of word reduction U/r. 6 cl. (ii) of Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules? - Held, words and phrases necessarily take their meaning from context in which they are used - Word reduction in context of cls. (ii) is not necessarily confined to abolition, and keeping certain posts in abeyance comes within expression - (B) Whether conditions for application of Fundamental Rules, r. 9(31)(c), conditions for application of rules must be fulfilled?- Further held, two conditions must be fulfilled for application of Fundamental r. 9(31)(c) -(i) is that two time-scales must be identical and (ii) two posts must fall in same cadre or class in a cadre -(C) Whether an admission can operate as an estoppel under Evidence Act?- Further held, an admission is not conclusive proof of matter admitted, though it may in certain circumstances operate as an estoppel - (D) Whether a subordinate appointing authority making a declaration under rules can ignore or abrogate very rules under which he has to make declaration? - Further held, a subordinate appointing authority who has to make a declaration under rules cannot ignore or abrogate very rules under which he has to make declaration - An officer cannot claim benefit of r. 3 and ignore at same time condition laid down in r. 4(b) of Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules - To be bound by conditions of other part - (E) Whether there has been a violation of constitutional guarantee u/art. 311(2) of constitution in case of appellant?- Further held, there had been no violation of constitutional guarantee under art. 311 (2) in case of appellant - That being position, appellant had no quasi-permanent status in post of Assistant Station Direct and his service was liable to be terminated when there was a reduction in number of posts of Public Relations Officers with in meaning of cl (ii) of r. 6 nor was he entitled to benefit of proviso to cl. (ii) so far as post of Assistant Station Director was concerned - There had been no violation of constitutional guarantee under art. 311 (2) in case of appellant - No question of estoppel really arose - Appellant was not misled in any way as to his quasi-permanent status - Intention was not merely to move appellant from one post to other, but also to shift status and that could mean nothing less than that they intended him to have this status in new post - Appeal dismissed.
