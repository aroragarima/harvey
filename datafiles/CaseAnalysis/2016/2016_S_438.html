Lanco Anpara Power Limited v State of Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1033; 2016 (151) FLR 796; 2017 (1) ILR(Cut) 187; 2017 (1) MPLJ 454; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 731

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Welfare, Registration, Companies Act, 1956, Manufacturing Process, Industrial, Health, Minimum Wages Act, 1948, Activity, Factories Act, 1948, Social Security, Absence, Labour, Safety, Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, Mines Act, 1952, Building And Other Construction Workers (Regulation Of Employment And Conditions Of Service) Act, 1996, Building and Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Act, 1996, Building and Other Construction Workers' Welfare Cess Rules, 1998, Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, Uttar Pradesh Factories Rules, 1950, Uttar Pradesh Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Rules, 2009, Constitution of India, 1950, State Governments under the Building and Other Construction Workers' (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Ordinance, 1995

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948, ss.2(m),6 - Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 - Buildings and Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Act, 1996 Act - Application of Factories Act to construction workers - Interpretation of statutes - Appellants were issued show cause notices by authorities under the provisions of Building and Other Construction Workers (Regulation of Employment and Conditions of Service) Act, 1996 (BOCW Act) and Buildings and Other Construction Workers Welfare Cess Act, 1996 Act (Welfare Cess Act) and the same was challenged by appellants through writ petitions in various HCs on ground that provisions of said Acts were not applicable to them as were registered under 1948 Act - However, HCs held that appellants would not be covered by definition of factory as defined u/s.2(m) of 1948 Act in the absence of any operations/manufacturing process and, so, mere obtaining a license u/s.6 of 1948 Act would not suffice and rescue them from their liability to pay cess under Welfare Cess Act - Further, all HCs dismissed petitions filed before them - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the provisions of the 1948 Act apply to the construction of building/project of appellants.

Held, construction of building is not business activity of the appellants or manufacturing process. On other hand, buildings are being constructed for carrying out the particular manufacturing process, which, in most of the appeals, is generation, transmission and distribution of power. The provisions of the 1948 Act would apply only when the manufacturing process starts for which the building/project is being constructed and not to the activity of construction of the project. The workers who were engaged in construction of said buildings also do not fall within the definition of 'worker' under 1948 Act. Further, 'superior purpose' contained in BOCW Act and Welfare Cess Act has to be kept in mind when two enactments - 1948 Act on one hand and BOCW Act/Welfare Cess Act on other hand, are involved, both of which are welfare legislations. Furthermore, registration under 1948 Act becomes necessary in view of provisions contained in s.6 of said Act as the section requires taking of approval and registration of factories even at preparatory stage i.e., at the stage when the premises where factory is to operate has to ensure that construction will be done in such a manner that it takes care of safety measures etc. which are provided in 1948 Act. It means to ensure that construction is carried out in such a manner that provisions in 1948 Act to ensure health, safety and provisions relating to hazardous process as well as welfare measures are taken care of. Hence, when permissions for construction of factories is given, the purpose is altogether different. As such, construction workers are not covered by 1948 Act and hence, welfare measures specifically provided for such workers under BOCW Act and Welfare Cess Act could not be denied. Hence, all appeals along with writ petitions filed before SC and also transfer petition and transfer cases are dismissed with costs. Order accordingly.

Ratio - As per provisions of 1948 Act, 'factory' is that establishment where manufacturing process is carried on with or without the aid of power and carrying on manufacturing process or activity is prerequisite. Further, it covers only those workers who are engaged in the said manufacturing process.
