Â Harman Singh and Others v Regional Transport Authority, Culcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

Mehr Chand Mahajan, M. Patanjali Sastri, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 46; AIR 1954 SC 190; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 79; 1954 SCJ 46; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 42 - Bengal Motor Vehicles Rules, 1940, r. 179 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19(1)(g) - Charging tariff on taxis - Rates of tariff for different classes - By a notification Regional Transport Authority, Calcutta Region, invited applications from persons for the issue of permits for small motor taxi cabs - The said notification also invited representations against the issue of such permits - A number of associations and persons including the Calcutta Taxi Association and the Bengal Taxi Association, accordingly made representations - Objections rejected - (A) Whether a taxi owner has an option to charge a rate which is below the prescribed minimum? - Held, r. 179 of the Bengal Motor Vehicles Rules, 1940 prescribes maximum tariff for the different classes of taxis and it does not however prohibit the charge of a rate below the prescribed minimum if the taxi owner so wishes - (B) Whether introduction of small taxis at a cheaper rate is discriminatory within the ambit of art. 14 of the Constitution of India, 1950? - Held, introduction of small taxis at a cheaper rate is not discriminatory within the ambit of art. 14 as it is in the interest and for the benefit of a section of the public - There is however nothing unreasonable in this classification or discrimination which infringes the provisions of art. 14 of the Constitution, 1950 - (C) Whether art. 19(1)(g) gurantees a monopoly to a particular individual or association to carry on any occupation? - Held, art. 19(1)(g) does not gurantee any such monopoly to a particular individual or association to carry on any occupation - (C.1) Whether one persons running an identical business to another amount to violation fundamental rights guranteed by art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, 1950? - Held, the right to carry on the same profession is not a violation of fundamental right provided it is conducted in absence of any bad faith within the ambit of art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, 1950 - (C.2) Whether the discretion of the regional transport authority to issue permits at different rates of tariff to different classes of vehicles give rise to infringement of fundamental right u/art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, 1950? - Held, the power if exercised by the regional transport authority in a bona fide manner and for the benefit of the citizens of the town a case cannot be brought u/art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution, 1950 by the affected licence holders - Appeal dismissed.
