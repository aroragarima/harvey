Mohini Jain (Miss) v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 527; (1992) 3 SCC 666; AIR 1992 SC 1858; AIR 1992 SCW 2100; JT 1992 (4) SC 292; 1992(2) SCALE 90; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 658; 1992 (5) SLR 1; 1992 (2) UJ 331; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1198

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949

Summary: Constitution - Education - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Constitution, 1950, arts. 14, 21, 32, 38, 39 (a) and (f), 41 and 45 - Karnataka Educational Institutions (Prohibition of Capitation Fee) Act, 1984, s. 5(1) - Private College - Capitation fees - Notification of the Karnataka Govt. permitting the Private Medical Colleges in the State of Karnataka to charge exorbitant tuition fees from the students other than those admitted to the 'Govt. seats' - Whether there is 'right to education' guaranteed to the people of India under the Constitution and whether charging of capitation fee in consideration of admissions to educational institutions is arbitrary, unfair, unjust and as such violates the equality clause contained in art. 14 of the Constitution? - Court held according to various provisions of Constitution, every citizen has a 'right to education' under the Constitution, therefore charging of capitation fee in consideration of admissions to educational institutions is wholly arbitrary and violates the equality clause contained in art. 14 of Constitution - (B) - Whether the notification is violative of the provisions of the Act which in specific terms prohibit the charging of capitation fee by any educational institution in the State of Karnataka - Held, what is provided in para 1(d) and 1(c) of the notification D/- 05-06-1989 is capitation fee and not a tuition fee it has to be held that the notification is beyond the scope of the Act rather goes contrary to s. 3 of the Act and as such has to be set aside, therefore it is not permissible in law for any educational institution to charge capitation fee as a consideration for admission to the said institution - Petition allowed.
