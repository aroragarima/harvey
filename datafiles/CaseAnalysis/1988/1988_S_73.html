State Of Maharashtra & Another v Smt. Sushila Mafatlal Shah & Others

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 948; (1988) 4 SCC 490; (1989) SCC (Cr) 1; AIR 1988 SC 2090; 1988 (4) Bom.C.R. 441; JT 1988 (3) SC 646; 1988(2) SCALE 774; [1988] Supp2 S.C.R. 827

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, West Bengal (Prevention of Violent Activities) Act

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Customs - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 2, 3, 8 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Detention order - Challenged - Detenu was caught for the act of transporting ten gold biscuits of foreign origin - An order of detention u/s. 3(1) of the COFEPOSA Act was passed by 'X' in exercise of the powers specially conferred upon him by the Government of Maharashtra for the purpose of s. 3 of the COFEPOSA Act - In the grounds of detention the detenu was informed that he had a right to make a representation to the State Government as also to the Government of India against the order of detention - Subsequently, detenu preferred a representation addressed to 'X' which was forwarded by the Superintendent, Central Prison to the Government who rejected the same - Detenu's mother, respondent no. 1 filed a writ petition challenging the detention order on the ground that the order of detention having been passed u/s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA Act by 'X' in his capacity as a specially empowered person, that's why detenu had a right to make a representation, in the first instance, to 'X' himself as the detaining authority before availing of his right to make further representation and failure to notify him of this right in the grounds of detention violated art. 22(5) of the Constitution - Said contention found acceptance with the HC and the HC made the Rule absolute and quashed the order of detention - Appellant contended that neither art. 22(5) of the Constitution nor the provisions of the COFEPOSA Act afforded scope for holding that if an order of detention was passed by specially empowered officer of the State Government or the Central Government, a different kind of procedure had to be followed in the matter of affording opportunity to the detenu to make his representation against the order of detention - On the other hand, respondent contended that unlike other Preventive Detention Acts such as the National Security Act. etc., there is no provision in the COFEPOSA Act for confirmation by the Government of an order of detention passed by an officer specially empowered u/s. 3(1) of the COFEPOSA Act - Does an order passed by an officer of the State Government or the Central Government, specially empowered for the purposes of s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA Act by the respective Government, make him the Detaining Authority, and obligate him to inform the detenu that he has a threefold opportunity to make his representations i.e. the first to himself and the other two to the State Government and the Central Government? - Held, art. 22(5) of Constitution does not provide material for the detenu to contend that in addition to his right to make a representation to the State Government and the Central Government, he has a further right u/art. 22(5) of Constitution to make a representation to 'X' himself as he had made the order of detention - Even if an order of detention is made by a specially empowered officer of the Central Government or the State Government as the case may be, it is the concerned Government that would constitute the Detaining Authority under the Act and not the officer concerned who made the order of detention - Though by reason of s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA Act a specially empowered officer is entitled to pass an order of detention, his constitutional obligation is only to communicate expeditiously to the detenu the grounds of detention and also afford him opportunity to make representation to the appropriate Governments against his detention - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
