D. P. Mishra v Kamal Narayan Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 81; (1970) 2 SCC 369; AIR 1970 SC 1477; 1970 (2) SCJ 639; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 558; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 8

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses, Return Of Election Expenses

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 77, 99, 100(1)(b), II 6A, 123 (6) - Election petition - Amendment - Expenditure - Corrupt practice - Void - Appellant who stood as a candidate was declared elected - Respondent filed petition for setting aside the election of appellant on the grounds that latter had committed corrupt practices at the election in that he offered to bribe respondent as inducement for withdrawing from the contest and thereby committed a corrupt practice defined in s. 123 (1) of the 1951 Act - Tribunal negatived the allegations of corrupt practices and dismissed the petition - On appeal u/s. II 6A of the Act, against the order passed by Tribunal, HC set aside the order and declared election of appellant was void u/s. 100(1) (b) of the Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was right in allowing the amendment of petition, and whether the evidence established that corrupt practice within the meaning of s. 123 (6) r/w s. 77 of the Act was committed by appellant - Held, HC was right in granting the amendment - Respondent averred that appellant had incurred and authorised expenditure exceeding the permissible limit of Rs. 7,000/- fixed under the rules framed under the Act - Appellant in his statement of election expenses had disclosed that he had spent Rs, 6,324-14 - By adding to the particulars of expenditure incurred which obviously could not be within the knowledge of the election petitioner, no new plea of corrupt practice was set up by respondent - HC was of the view that on taking proper accounts of the expenditure incurred and allowing certain items which were included in the statement of account but which were not liable to be included, it was proved that appellant had spent an amount of Rs. 7,249-72 which exceeded the amount permissible u/s. 77 of the Act, and appellant having incurred or authorized expenditure in contravention of s. 77 of the Act he was guilty of a corrupt practice u/s. 123 (6) of the Act and the election of appellant was clearly void u/s. 100(1)(b) of the Act - SC agreed with the opinion of the HC - SC directed proceeding be remanded to HC and the HC do give notice to SS (authorised agent by appellant), u/s. 99 of the Act to appear and to show cause why he should not be named for committing corrupt practices - In pursuance of the show cause notice he would be entitled to an opportunity of cross examining witnesses who have already been examined by the Tribunal and had given evidence against him and he would be entitled to give evidence in his defence and of being heard - Order accordingly.
