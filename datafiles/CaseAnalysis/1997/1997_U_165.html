I.K. Sukhija and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2603; (1997) 6 SCC 406; (1997) SCC (L&S) 1512; AIR 1997 SC 2714; JT 1997 (6) SC 201; 1997 (4) LLN 43; 1997(4) SCALE 640; 1999 (1) SLJ 88; 1998 (1) SLR 294; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 627; 1997 (7) Supreme 335

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Posts and Telegraphs (Civil Engineering Electrical Gazetted Officers) Recruitment Rules, 1975, r. 5 - Service Rules - Seniority among direct recruit and promotees - Delay in finalising Draft Recruitment Rules of 1969 - Promotion as Asst. Engineers on ad hoc basis as per merit list against regular vacancies in promotion quota - Further appointment was not by way of stop-gap arrangement - If entitled to benefit of period of their ad hoc services in determination of seniority - Held, appellants are entitled to benefit of period of their ad hoc services for seniority purpose - Appeal allowed.
