Ramesh Rajagopal v Devi Polymers Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 271; (2016) 6 SCC 310; AIR 2016 SC 1920; 2016 CRLJ 2339; 2016 (4) CTC 769; 2016 (3) JCC 1585; JT 2016 (4) SC 210; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1863; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 506; 2016 (3) RLW 2525; 2016(4) SCALE 198; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 527

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; IT & E-Commerce; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: FIR, Computer, Quash, Forgery, Information Technology Act, 2000, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - IT & E-Commerce Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 409, 463, 468, 471 - Information Technology Act, 2000, ss. 65, 66 - Alleged forgery & Misappropriation of fund - Failure to quash Criminal proceeding - Justification - It is stated that appellant is Director of party company which is engaged in manufacturing goods - Further, with respect to consultancy service, separate entity was created - However, amount for consultancy service was paid from parent company on advise of appellant - Respondent alleged that there is forgery and misappropriation of funds as there is no existence of separate entity and payment is made from parent company - Hence, appellant was prosecuted u/ss. 120(b), 409, 468 and 471 of IPC read with ss. 65 and 66 of Act - HC vide impugned order failed to quash criminal proceeding against appellant - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, none of circumstances can lead to inference of commission of offence under IPC. Further, website of newly created company clearly stated that new entity is part of parent company and address of sister concern is shown to be same address as that of parent company. Hence, there is no attempt to project that sister concern is separate from parent company. Further, SC held that appellant has not received any amount nor has entered into any contract in his own name on basis of website. Further, with respect to offence committed under Act, SC held that nothing is brought on record to show that he did not have any authority to access computer system or computer network of company. Further, SC held that acts of appellant did not have any dishonest intention while considering allegations in respect of other offences. Hence, no case is made under Act. SC held that criminal proceedings initiated by respondent constitute abuse of process of Court; hence, criminal proceeding against appellant is quashed. Appeal allowed.
