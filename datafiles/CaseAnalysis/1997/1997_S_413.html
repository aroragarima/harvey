Kalika Tiwari and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

25 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1112; (1997) 4 SCC 445; (1997) SCC (Cr) 600; AIR 1997 SC 2186; 1997 (4) CCR 143; 1997 (2) Crimes 15; 1997 CRLJ 2531; JT 1997 (4) SC 405; 1997(3) SCALE 282; 1997 (2) SCJ 38; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 335; 1997 (3) Supreme 397

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 396 - Dacoity with murder - (A) Common intention - Need of, when murder by one or some of dacoits only - Proof of common intention or common object of all, if necessary - (B) Only source of light at place of occurrence in the village was earthen lamp - Proof as to identification under consideration - Held, every dacoit is liable to be punished under the section though all of them had no participation in murder - Identification of dacoits in such light not a problem for villagers as they were complainant's direct relations - Appeal partly allowed.
