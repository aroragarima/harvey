Maganlal and Others v Jaiswal Industries, Neemach and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 647; (1989) 4 SCC 344; AIR 1989 SC 2113; 1990 (1) BankCLR 211; 1990 (1) BC 1; 1989 (3) CompLJ 179; JT 1989 (3) SC 415; 1989(2) SCALE 208; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 696; 1990 (1) UJ 27; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 174

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Attachment Of Property, Final Decree For Sale, Conditional Sale, Application For Attachment, Mortgage By Conditional Sale, Anomalous

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - State Financial Corporations Act, 1951, ss. 31(1) and 32(8)- Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 34, r. 5 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 60, Proviso - (A) Sale of property mortgaged to financial corporation - In execution of order u/s. 32 of 1951 Act - Rights of debtor - Industry - Confirmation of sale - Appeal against -

(B) Fiction that order for sale is decree in suit and Financial Corporation was decree-holder and debtor a judgment - Debtor - Extent of - Held, (A) Debtor can invoke provisions of O. 34, r.5, redeem mortgage - (B) Is not decree for purpose of applying s. 34 of CPC, 1908 so cannot also be extended for treating application u/s. 31(1) as plaint for payment of Court fee - Appeal allowed.
