Union Of India v Glaxo India Ltd. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 201; (2011) 6 SCC 668; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 220; JT 2011 (4) SC 161; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 445; 2011(4) SCALE 157; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 50

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug

Keywords: Bulk Drug, Formulations, Drugs (Prices Control) Order, 1987, Pooled price, Sugar (Movement Control) Order, 1959, Drug (Price Control) Order, 1979, Drug (Price Control) Order, 1989, Drug (Price Control) Order, 1970

Summary: Health & Drug - Drug (Price Control) Order, 1979, paras 3 and 7(2)(a) - Drug (Price Control) Order, 1970 - Price fixation - Challenged - Respondent, engaged in manufacture and sale of 3 bulk drugs sold at the price declared by the respondent under DPCO, 1970 - Central Govt. by its order dt. 12.05.1981 fixed the maximum selling prices of said bulk drugs manufactured and sold by respondent - Respondent filed a review petition for review of the price fixation order passed by the Central Govt. - Central Govt. re-fixed the price of the 3 bulk drugs vide order dt. 20.11.1986 - Respondent filed writ petition against said order - HC directed the respondent to file another review petition before the Central Govt. for reconsideration of the price fixed by impugned price fixation order - Respondent again filed a review petition for review of the price fixation order dt. 20.11.1986 - Central Govt., vide its order dt. 02.01.1989, issued price fixation order fixing the price for impugned Drugs higher than the earlier price fixed vide order dt. 20.11.1986 - Subsequently, Central Govt. had issued demand of Rs. 71.21 Crores difference between the prices that the respondent was enjoying under DPCO, 1970 and the prices as notified by the Central Govt. vide order dt. 02.01.1989 - Respondent filed writ petition questioning the legality and validity of said demand raised by the Central Govt. - HC allowed said writ petition and quashed the demand made by the Central Govt. - Hence, the instant appeal - (A) Whether the Central Govt. was justified in issuing a demand based on Drug Prices fixed on 02.01.1989, instead of drug prices fixed on 20.11.1986? - Held, HC in its earlier order cleared that they were not setting aside the order dt. 20.11.1986 or staying further implementation of the said order or stay any proceedings for fixation of prices of various drug formulation of the respondent of which the Central Govt. may wish to initiate - HC had permitted the respondent to file review petition, if they so desire and further had directed the Central Govt. to pass an order as they deem fit - Hence, demand to be raised on the respondent was to be based on the prices fixed vide order dt. 20.11.1986 and not on the drug prices fixed on 02.01.1989 - (B) Whether the Central Govt. was justified in directing respondent to deposit an amount of Rs. 71.21 crores in the Drug Prices Equalization Account (DPEA)? - Held, para 7(2)(a) of DPCO, 1979 states that what can be directed by the Central Govt. to be deposited into DPEA by the manufacturer of bulk drugs and any formulations using those drugs or procured from outside - It also states that manufacturer must utilize in the formulation any bulk drug, it could either be from his own production or procured from any other sources - If the price of such bulk drugs was notified as lower than the price allowed to him in the price of his formulations, the Central Govt. may require the manufacturer of formulation the excess amount determined to be deposited into DPEA - Hence, Central Govt. was justified in directing respondent to deposit an amount of Rs. 71.21 crores in the DPEA - Appeal allowed.
