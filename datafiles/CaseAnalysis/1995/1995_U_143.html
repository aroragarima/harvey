Â Kamleshkumar Ishwardas Patel and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, A.M. Ahmadi, K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1014; (1995) 4 SCC 51; (1995) SCC (Cr) 643; 1995 (3) Bom.C.R. 69; 1995 (4) CCR 74; 1995 (3) Crimes 26; 1995 CRLJ 3105; 1995 (59) ECR 168; JT 1995 (3) SC 639; 1995(2) SCALE 681; 1995 (2) SCJ 683; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Illicit Traffic In Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1988 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Preventive detention - Detaining authority - Representation - Common question that arose for consideration in the present two appeals was in the context of orders for preventive detention passed by officers especially empowered by the Central Govt. under the 1974 Act and the 1988 Act - Person detained had a right to make a representation against the order of detention to the authority making the order and where representation made was not considered by the detaining authority the detention of the person was said to be illegal - First appeal related to the detention of appellant u/s .3 of the 1974 Act made by Joint Secretary to the Govt. of India, Ministry of Finance, Department of Revenue, as the officer specially empowered by the Central Govt. - Grounds of detention were served on the detenu and the detenu had made a representation to the officer who had made the order of detention as well as to the Advisory Board - Detenu was informed that the said representation was considered by the Central Govt. and the same had been rejected - Although the officer who made the order of detention did not consider the said representation, it was addressed to him and he forwarded the said representation with his recommendation that the representation may be rejected - Second appeal was made u/s. 3 of the 1974 Act by Joint Secretary to the Govt. of India, an officer who had been specially empowered u/s. 3(1) of the 1974 Act, for the detention of the husband of the appellant - Contentions urged on behalf of the appellant was that he had addressed a joint representation to the detaining authority, the Central Govt. and the Advisory Board and the same was submitted through the Superintendent and that the said representation was rejected by the Central Govt. and it was not considered and decided independently by the detaining authority himself - Whether the appellants' detention in both cases were illegal - Held, the failure on the part of the officer who made the order of detention to consider the representation of the detenu resulted in denial of the right of the detenu to make a representation recognised by art. 22(5) of the Constitution and the said denial rendered the detention of the detenu illegal and without the authority of law - Further detention of the detenue was illegal and liable to be set free - Appeal allowed.
