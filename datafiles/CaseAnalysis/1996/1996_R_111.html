Divya Dip Singh and Others v Ram Bachan Mishra and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1781; (1997) 1 SCC 504; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 37; AIR 1997 SC 1465; 1996 (4) CCC 190; JT 1996 (9) SC 576; 1996(7) SCALE 813; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 705; 1996 (8) Supreme 212

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Guardians and Wards Act, 1890

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Hindu Minority, Guardianship Act, 1956 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Bihar Consolidation of Holding and Prevention of Fragmentation Act, 1956, ss. 9-A and 12 - Whether minors can challenge sale which was effected by their natural guardian? - Held, appointment of guardian in title suit was one u/O. 32, r. 3 of CPC, which will not take away right of natural guardian forever - Once guardian appointed during pendency of suit was properly discharged, rights of natural guardian revive - Minors have not challenged sale within three years from their attaining majority, have no right to ignore sale as void - Further appellants themselves have accepted said sale by their father by writing a letter to Collector of Bhojpur at Arrah and requesting to collect loan arrears advanced against suit lands, from purchaser/first respondent - Still further, it is again common ground that appellants have not filed any objections to statements published u/s. 9-A and Scheme published u/s. 12 of Act within prescribed period - Appeal dismissed.
