State of Uttar Pradesh v Suresh Alias Chhavan and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, Baharul Islam, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 66; (1981) 3 SCC 635; (1981) SCC (Cr) 774; AIR 1982 SC 1076; 1982 ALJ 653; 1982 CRLJ 850; 1981 CRLR 409

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Murder, Conviction, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused were convicted by Trial Court u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 IPC for murder of husband of PW1 - On appeal, HC acquitted accused - Hence, instant appeal - Whether accused are falsely implicated in case.

Held, the Judges are not right in observing that all the three witnesses, PWs 1, 5 and 6 are interested. It is true that PW1 is the wife of deceased. But it is not possible to reject her evidence for that reason alone though her evidence would have to be scrutinised with care. The mere fact that the father of the accused had been prosecuted a case for the murder of PW5's brother is not a sufficient reason for holding that PW5 is interested in the prosecution of the accused in this case nearly 15 years after the murder of his elder brother. There is nothing on record to show that PW6 who belongs to another village is in any way related to the deceased or PW1. It is not reasonable to expect that PW1 and others should have waited for securing a faster moving vehicle for taking the injured to the hospital and thereby lose time in making medical attention available to him immediately. Deceased appears to have been seriously injured and he could not have been in a position to make a more coherent statement though it is seen from the evidence of the Doctor PW4 that he was conscious right through. PW3 had probably not understood the deceased correctly when he was narrating the dying declaration in relation to the third assailant. PWs 1 and 5 are natural witnesses. There is no reason to hold that PW6 could not have been present during the occurrence. Trial Court was perfectly justified in relying upon the oral evidence of PWs 1, 5 and 6 and the dying declaration and convicting the accused u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and that the Judges of the HC were not justified in rejecting the evidence and coming to the conclusion that the prosecution has not proved the time and place of the occurrence as well as the guilt of the accused beyond reasonable doubt. All the three accused guilty u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC of the murder of deceased. Appeal allowed.
