Maharashtra State Road Transport Corporation and v Casteribe Rajya P. Karmchari Sanghatana

Supreme Court of India

28 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1063; (2009) 8 SCC 556; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 605; 2009 (3) CLR 262; 2009 (123) FLR 136; 2009(12) SCALE 25; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 937; 2010 (3) SLR 588

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Piece Rate

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions & Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Unfair Labour Practice - Workmen of respondent-union was alleged to be employed as cleaners of buses by appellant-corporation - However, workmen were denied benefit of permanency - Unfair labour practice, alleged - Complaints of workmen were allowed by Industrial Tribunal - Tribunal's order confirmed by Single Judge - Appellant's Letters Patent appeal dismissed by DB - Legality - Held, posts of cleaners were existed in Corporation - Workmen recruited not in accordance with Standing Order, itself an unfair labour practice - Workmen were exploited by Corporation for years by engaging them on piece rate basis - Tribunal has power to direct employer to accord permanency to affected employees - Therefore, direction given by Tribunal to confer permanency to workmen was justifiable - No interference.P /&gt;(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions & Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Unfair Labour Practice - Unrecognized trade union - Complaints - Maintainability - Held, workmen deprived of benefits of status, wages and permanency applicable to post of cleaners - Similarly situated workmen filed complaint individually would get benefits of permanency applicable to post of cleaners - Although complaints filed by workmen on ground of unfair labour practice were non-maintainable, workmen would be entitled to get status, wages and other benefits of permanency to post of cleaners under special circumstances of present case - Appeals dismissed.
