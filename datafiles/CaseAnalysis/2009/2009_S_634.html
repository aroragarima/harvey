Devendra & Ors v State Of U.P. & Anr

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 786; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 461; (2009) 7 SCC 495; 2010 (1) MLJ(Crl) 1095; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 448; 2009(7) SCALE 613; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 872

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Keywords: Application For Impleadment, Subsequent Conduct, Civil Action

Summary: Criminal - Civil Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482 and 483 - Civil dispute as to partitioning of properties among co-shares - Whether a pure civil dispute can be a subject matter of a criminal proceeding u/ss. 420, 467, 468 and 469 of the Indian Penal Code is the question involved herein? - Criminal cases have to be proceeded with in accordance with the procedure as prescribed under CrPC and acts alleged to be an offence has to have ingredients of an offence as defined in IPC or other statute - In a given case a civil suit as also a criminal proceeding would be maintainable; they can run simultaneously; result in one proceeding would not be binding on the Court determining the issue before it in another proceeding - In instant case appellant nos. 1 and 2, and respondent no. 2 where co-sharers - Suit for partition was filed by one of the co-sharer in 1982 which went upto HC which passed its order on 07-11-1985 issuing notice etc. - Than in 1997 sale deed was executed by Appellant nos. 1 and 2 in favour of appellant nos. 3 and 4 - Respondent no. 2 in 2005, had filed suit for canceling such sale deed when he also had moved an application in police station wherein FIR was registered; application u/s. 482 of CrPC preferred by appellants for quashing such FIR, which was dismissed; after investigation, chargesheet was filed and cognizance was taken by Trial Court notably with no reason assigned for such taking of cognizance - After cognizance another application u/s. 482 was filed by appellants questioning legality of order of cognizance, which was again dismissed - Appellants thus filed present appeal - Whether appeal allowable? - Held, Yes - Admitted facts noted, contentions heard, precedents referred - Indisputably appellants are co-sharers, dispute between them is confined to the extent of their respective shares and it must be determined only in a civil suit; thus if appellants had executed a sale deed in favour of a third party by stating that they have one-third share over the entire properties, the same would not be binding on the complainant/respondent; if there is any threat as to dispossession from the property by co-sharers or third party, recourse to legal action could always be taken and even for that purpose a proceeding u/ss. 144/145 of CrPC would be maintainable; decision of a criminal court in the case as in hand would not be binding on the civil Court - Submission was made that by reason of such execution of sale deed claiming title over the property to which appellants were not entitled to, complainant/respondent had been cheated; submission to be rejected on ground that appellants had not made any representation; no contract and/or transaction had been entered into by and between the respondent/complainant and the appellants - No allegation that appellants had created any false documents and thus are guilty therefor - Court than deliberated upon power u/s. 482 CrPC - When the allegations made in the FIR or the evidences collected during investigation do not satisfy the ingredients of an offence, the superior Courts would not encourage harassment of a person in a criminal Court for nothing - On unreasoned order of cognizance Court noted that in a case as in hand where no case has been made out for taking cognizance of an offence u/s. 420 IPC, it was obligatory on the part of the CJM to apply his mind to the contents of chargesheet; such application of mind should have been reflected from the order, which is not so reflecting in the instant case, Court noted - Appeal allowed.
