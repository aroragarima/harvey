Mahabir Prasad v Jage Ram & Ors

Supreme Court of India

06 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 529; (1971) 1 SCC 265; AIR 1971 SC 742; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 301; 1971 UJ 151

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 22, r. 4 r/w O. 22, r. 11 - Delhi Land Reforms Act, 1954 - Property matter - Practice and Procedure - (A) Fact that person jointly interested in decree has been made a party respondent - And on his death his heirs have not been brought on record - Effect upon jurisdiction of Court - (B) When a party respondent in an appeal dies and one of his LRs is already on record in another capacity the appeal does not abate even though no application is made to bring them on record - Held, to does not per se divest the appellate court of its jurisdiction to pass decree in appeal under O. 41, r. 4 - Appeal Allowed.
