P. Sivanandi v Rajeev Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 63; (2017) 4 SCC 579; AIR 2017 SC 714; 2017 (1) CGLJ 519; 2017 (152) FLR 942; JT 2017 (2) SC 161; 2017 (1) OLR 734; 2017(2) SCALE 149

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Review, Selection, Promotion, Police, Year Of Allotment, Indian Administrative Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955, Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955

Summary: Service - Promotion - Applicability of ACR - S was promoted to Indian Police Service on basis of consideration of ACRs which were written beyond period prescribed by State Govt. - On application filed challenging promotion of S, Tribunal allowed original applications on finding that ACR for period 1-4-1993 to 15-7-1993 was invalid and could not have been considered by Review Select Committee since it was written beyond period prescribed by State Govt. and therefore S's selection was vitiated - on appeal HC did not accept S's contention and upheld view expressed by Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether, S is entitled to promotion. Held, fact that ACR of S was written and reviewed by his superior authorities after considerable delay cannot put him to any disadvantage. Writing and review of his ACR was beyond his control and no rational basis seen on which S could be disadvantaged merely because his superior officers were lax in discharge of their responsibilities. Hence, HC while upholding view expressed by Tribunal was in error in concluding that Review Select Committee could not consider ACR of S for period 1-4-1993 to 15-7-1993 and to this extent decision of HC is set aside. Further, there is nothing to suggest that Review Select Committee with UPSC did not consider case of S for promotion on merit or that view of Review Select Committee was perverse in any manner. It is not proper to interfere with decision arrived at by Review Select Committee with UPSC on basis of service record of S more so when it was submission of UPSC that what tilted scales in his favour was his ACR for period 1992-93 which was earlier missing and which was not taken into consideration on earlier occasion. Hence, order of HC is set aside. S will be entitled to all consequential benefits. Appeals allowed.
