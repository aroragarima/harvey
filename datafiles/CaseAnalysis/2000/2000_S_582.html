Chintamani Gajanan Velkar v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 562; (2000) 3 SCC 143; AIR 2000 SC 1073; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 221; JT 2000 (2) SC 62; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 359; 2000(1) SCALE 524; 2000 (2) SCJ 219; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 570; 2000 (2) SLT 297; 2000 (1) Supreme 550

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Private Forests (Acquisition) Act, 1975, ss. 2(f) and 6 - Indian Forest Act, 1927, ss. 35, 35(1), 35(2) and 35(3) - Appellant is landholder - He was in possession of various extents of lands - Appellant filed an application u/s. 6 of Maharashtra Private Forests (Acquisition) Act, 1975 which provides for settlement of disputes in relation to private forest land - Whether word 'issue' used in s. 2(f) of Maharashtra Act, 1975 means service of notice on landholder? - Procedure u/s. 35 is that a notice is to be issued - There is a difference between s. 2(f)(ii) and (iii) - So far as sub-cl. (ii) of s. 2(f) is concerned, it refers to various lands in respect of which notification has been issued u/s. 35(1) of Indian Forest Act, 1927 before appointed day 30-08-1975 - But sub-cl. (iii) of s. 2(f) does not refer to any notification that could be issued u/s. 35(1) of Indian Forest Act, 1927 - It only refers to notice that could be initially issued u/s. 35(3) - In a normal case, notice has to be issued u/s. 35(3) and should be served on landholders, objections thereto would have to be heard and an order could be passed treating them as forest land only after notification u/s. 35(1) was issued - In cases where a final notification has been issued u/s. 35(1) entire notified land would automatically vest in State on appointed date, namely, 30-08-1975 - Legislature has not made any discrimination in regard to matters where notification had been issued u/s. 35(1) on one hand and in cases where notification had not been issued and matter stood still at stage of notice u/s. 35(3) on other - In latter cases, legislature thought it fit to exclude 2 hectares of landholder from vesting - If that was done, a notice that was issued u/s. 35(3) would itself be sufficient and if such notice was issued before appointed day i.e., 30-08-1975 land would vest in State subject, of course that Collector has to specify particular extent of 2 hectares which can be retained by landholder - There is no need for any service of such notice before 30-08-1975, nor for an inquiry nor for a notification u/s. 35(1) - Appeal disposed of.
