Â Ghaio Mall and Sons v State of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 76; AIR 1959 SC 65; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1424

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab Excise Act, Government of Part C States Act, 1951, misa

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Excise - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Punjab Excise Act,1914, s. 59 - Delhi Liquor License Rules,1935, Chap 5, r. 1 - Govt. of Part C States Act, 1951, s. 38(3) -(A) Whether records produced by respondents complied with writ of certiorari u/art. 226 of Constitution or not? - Held, u/art. 226 of Constitution, whole object of a writ of certiorari is to bring up all records along with returns of inferior court or other quasi-judicial body for examination by superior court so that latter may be satisfied that inferior court or quasi-judicial body has not gone beyond its jurisdiction and has exercised its jurisdiction within limits fixed by law - Records before superior court disclose a state of affairs which does not reflect any credit on administration of ernstwhile State of Delhi - Non-production of records by respondents completely defeats purpose of writs which are issued - (B) Whether there was a valid grant of license or not? - Further held, there was no valid order granting license - Letter in question was an inter-departmental communication written with reference to an earlier communication made by Excise Commr. - Ex facie, it purported to be a reply to Excise Commissioner's letter - Delhi Liquor License Rules, 1935, Chap 5, r. 1 - Chief Commr. of Delhi is competent authority to grant license in Delhi - No license granted by him - Appeal allowed.
