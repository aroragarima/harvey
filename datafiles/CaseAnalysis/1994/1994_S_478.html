Supreme Court of India

27 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, M.N. Venkatachaliah, P.B. Sawant, S.C. Agrawal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1513; (1994) 4 SCC 192; AIR 1994 SC 2319; 1995 (1) Bom.C.R. 295; JT 1994 (3) SC 474; 1994 (1) RentLR 548; 1994(2) SCALE 705; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 746; 1994 (2) UJ 244

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Society

Summary: Land & Property - Bombay Land Requisition Act, 1948, ss. 1 and 6 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 300 A - (A) Act - If a rent control legislation - Deletion of provisions empowering State Govt. to require landlord to let out premises - (B) S. 1 - Requisitioning - Legislative intent - (C) Requisition order - Continuance for period of 30 years - Held, (A) It signifies that Act is not rent control legislation - (B) It was intended to be temporary arrangement - (C) Can be passed for permanent public purpose - Cannot continue indefinitely - Unreasonable - Petition dismissed.
