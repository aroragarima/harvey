Union Of India & Ors. v A.K. Pandey

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, B.N. Agrawal, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1529; (2009) 10 SCC 552; 2010 (3) AWC 2359; 2009 (5) ESC 748; JT 2009 (12) SC 364; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 81; 2009(12) SCALE 617; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 528; 2010 (3) SLR 704

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Court Martial, Corps, Active Service, Hyderabad District Municipalities Act, 1956, Arraignment

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Army Act, 1950, s. 164(2) - Army Rules, 1954, r. 34 - Warning to accused - Mandatory and Directory or Permissive Words - Respondent charge-sheeted - General Court Martial proceedings held - Respondent was awarded punishments; (i) to suffer rigorous imprisonment for three years and (ii) dismissal from service - Petition filed u/s. 164(2) of Act rejected - HC allowed writ petition filed thereagainst and set aside General Court Martial proceedings as well as order of punishment - Hence, present appeal - Whether provision in r. 34 of Rules regarding interval between information of charge to accused for which he is to be tried and his arraignment shall not be less than ninety-six hours mandatory? - Held, prohibitive or negative words ordinarily indicative of mandatory nature of provision; although not conclusive - When word 'shall' is followed by prohibitive or negative words, legislative intention of making provision absolute, peremptory and imperative becomes loud and clear and ordinarily has to be inferred as such - Provision contained in r. 34 regarding interval of ninety-six hours from service of charge/charges for which an accused is to be tried and his arraignment is mandatory - In present case, interval between respondent having been informed of charges for which he was to be tried and his arraignment was less than ninety-six hours - Mandatory provision contained in r. 34 having been breached, DB cannot be said to have erred in affirming order of Single Judge setting aside proceedings of General Court Martial - Appeal dismissed.
