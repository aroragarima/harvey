Bakhshish Singh Brar v Smt. Gurmej Kaur And Anr

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28505; (1987) 4 SCC 663; (1988) SCC (Cr) 29; AIR 1988 SC 257; 1987 (3) Crimes 612; 1988 CRLJ 419; 1987 CrLR(SC) 649; JT 1987 (4) SC 390; 1987(2) SCALE 828; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 450; 1988 (1) UJ 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, s. 197 - Sanction to prosecute - Cognizance of offences u/ss. 149, 302, 323 etc. taken on complaint against police officers for causing death - Trial Court holding that it could decide necessity of sanction after gathering materials and evidence - High Court refusing to interfere - Validity - Order of Trial Court, is legal - Held, HC order is also proper - Petition dismissed.
