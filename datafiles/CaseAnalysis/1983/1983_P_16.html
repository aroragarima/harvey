M.G.Brothers Lorry Service v Prasad Textiles

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, D.P. Madon

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 274; (1983) 3 SCC 61; 1983 ACJ 507; AIR 1984 SC 15; 1983(1) SCALE 481; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 1027; 1983 UJ 488

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Carriage Of Goods By Sea

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Carriers Act, 1865, ss.10, 6, 8 and Pre. - Contract Act, 1972, s.23 - Consignment was accepted for transport by the appellant, at Guntur subject to special conditions printed on the reverse of t he Way Bill - Condition limiting carrier's liability - Validity of - Held, any contract or bargain which seeks to defeat the liability of the carriers as enacted by law, would, in our opinion, defeat the provisions of the Act (provisions of s. 10) - And in the light of s. 23 of Contract Act, 1972 it is void - Appeals dismissed.
