M/S. Vijay Industries v M/S. Natl Technologies Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2174; (2009) 3 SCC 527; AIR 2009 SC 1695; [2009] 147 Comp Cas 490; 2009 (2) CompLJ 175; 2009 (2) DRTC 1; JT 2009 (2) SC 41; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 943; 2009(1) SCALE 466; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 972

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Appellant a small scale unit registered with District Industries Centre supplied Castor Oil to respondent valued at Rs. 89,13,589 and sum of 49,99,000 was paid by respondent which was adjusted fist towards interest and balance against principal amount - Demand of Rs. 64,58,457 was made against appellant - Notice was served on Respondent - Respondent agreed to pay sum of Rs. 2,00,000 per week to restore supply of Castor Oil - Respondent paid Rs. 8 lakhs and appellant refused to make further supplies - Board meeting was held and legal notice u/s. 434 of 1956 Act was served on respondent - Appellant filed winding up petition u/s. 433(e) and 433(f) r/w. 439 of 1956 Act before HC - Respondent in counter affidavit denied liability to pay interest - Appellant contended that credit bills was to be paid by respondent - Petition was allowed by HC - Aggrieved by said order respondent filed appeal DB which was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether interest payable on sum due would be a debt so as to attract the provisions of Ss. 433 and 434 of the 1956 Act was maintainable - Held, SC relied upon Pfizer Ltd. v. Usan Laboratories P. Ltd. 1984 Indlaw MUM 178 holding that only because there is a dispute in regard to the rate of interest, the winding up petition cannot be thrown out on that ground alone - Further, interest of justice would be subserved if we in exercise of our jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution direct that the respondent to pay simple interest on the admitted sum at the rate of 12% per annum on the balance amount instead of 24% per annum within eight weeks from the date of amount became due till it is paid failing which the consequences provided in law shall ensue - Appeal allowed.
