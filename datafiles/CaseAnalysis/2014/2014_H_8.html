HUDA and another v Kedar Nath

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Kurian Joseph, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 742; (2015) 1 SCC 186; AIR 2015 SC 2609; 2015 (110) ALR 461; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 370; JT 2014 (12) SC 393; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1199; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 86; 2014 (2) RCR(Rent) 501; 2015 (128) RD 313; 2014(12) SCALE 473

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Compounded interest

Summary: Civil Procedure - Compounded interest - Sustainability - Respondent participated in auction conducted by appellants for disposal of certain booths - Respondent was allotted the booth - Respondent committed several irregularities in making payment - Respondent did not pay remaining installments, he was called to pay with interest at 18% p.a. compounded quarterly - Respondent filed suit challenging validity of charging 18% compound interest and resumption of the booth - Suit was decreed - Appeal was filed by appellant which was dismissed - Appeal before HC - Appeal was dismissed by HC and observed that appellant was entitled to charge only 10% interest and not 18% interest compounded quarterly - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by HC was sustainable.

Held, it is an admitted fact that the respondent did not make payment of the unpaid installments and also that appellant has right to resume possession of the booth in the event of non-payment of the auction price of the booth..Even if is to conclude that HC was right in not permitting appellant to recover compound interest at 18% per annum, it is admitted fact that the respondent did not pay interest even at 10% per annum, to which he could not have objected. In spite of fact that the amount was to be paid before 1993, even today in 2014, neither respondent has paid the unpaid amount along with interest, nor has he shown willingness to make the payment when ample opportunities were given to him by Court for making payment along with interest at 10% per annum on the amount due and payable. It clearly shows respondent is not having bona fide intention and is merely trying to remain in possession without making payment of the bid amount, which he had agreed to pay. It is not in dispute that appellant authority is entitled to take possession in the event of non- payment of entire price of the booth which respondent as auction purchaser had agreed to pay. Thus, respondent has been committing default continuously. Respondent has also put up illegal construction on the booth/land allotted to him without taking any permission from concerned authority. Thus, appellant is entitled to resume possession of the booth on account of non-payment of the price, but still the respondent is in possession of the same. Courts below had become more lenient than necessary towards respondent by permitting him to retain possession and make payment along with 10% interest on the amount due and payable by him. It would not be proper to grant any further accommodation to respondent, who has admittedly not paid the amount due - Hence, orders passed by Court below is quashed and set aside and it would be open to appellant to take possession of booth in question in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.
