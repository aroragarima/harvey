Registrar, Co-Operative Societies Haryana v Israil Khan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1478; (2010) 1 SCC 440; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 318; 2010 (3) AWC 2231; JT 2009 (15) SC 45; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 832; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 929; 2009(13) SCALE 484; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1162; 2010 (3) SLR 359

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Haryana Co-Operative Societies Act, 1984, Primary Co-operative Credit & Service Society Staff Service Rules, 1992

Summary: Service - Primary Co-operative Credit & Service Society Staff Service Rules, 1992 - Recovery of unauthorised emoluments paid to employees - Respondents and other employees submitted representations for regular pay scales, instead of consolidated pay - Managing Committee of society passed resolution extending benefit of regular pay scale to respondents with retrospective effect - Deputy Registrar of Co-operative Societies rescinded resolution and directed Society to recover back excess payment made to employees - On appeal, HC held that resolution of Managing Committee was illegal, but refused to direct refund thereof - Whether HC, having held that employees were not entitled to said benefit, committed error in refusing to direct refund thereof? - Held, Rules specifically provided that employees should be paid a consolidated salary - Therefore without amendment to Rules, Managing Committees could not have passed a resolution for giving benefit of regular pay scales that too with retrospective effect to employees - Impugned orders of HC holding that illegal payments to respondents need not be refunded to them set aside - Appeals allowed.
