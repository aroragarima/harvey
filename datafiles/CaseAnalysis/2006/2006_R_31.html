State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Raj Kishore Yadav and Anr

Supreme Court of India

20 June 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 277; (2006) 5 SCC 673; 2006 (5) ADJ 109; 2006 (7) AWC 7148; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 250; JT 2006 (7) SC 200; 2006(6) SCALE 492; 2006 (5) SLR 376; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 63

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Appeal against HC order - Modifying the punishment by way of stoppage of two increments with cumulative effect, quashing the order of dismissal from service awarded to the respondent herein and ordered reinstatement with all pecuniary and consequential service benefit - Charges are very serious in nature and were proved beyond doubt - Held, order passed by the High Court quashing the order of dismissal erroneous - High Court has limited scope of interference in the administrative action of the State in exercise of extraordinary jurisdiction u/art. 226 - Findings recorded by the Enquiry Officer and the order of punishment of dismissal from service should not have been disturbed - Order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
