Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Bidhu Bhusan Sarkar (Deceased, Through His Legal Representative)

Supreme Court of India

03 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1967 SC 916; [1967] 63 ITR 278; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 685

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - Validity of notice u/s. 34 by Principal Income Tax Officer (P.I.T.O.) - When the Addl. Income Tax Officer (A.I.T.O.) on the basis of its proceedings initiated after notice u/s. 34 had given its order and if valid, was the assessment in pursuance thereof barred by time? - The proceedings before the A.I.T.O. terminated by passing the order and did not any longer continue to remain pending - An order of transfer can be validly made even if there be no proceedings pending for assessment of tax and the purpose of the transfer may be that all future proceedings are to take place before the officer to whom the case of the assessee is transferred - After transfer, the P.I.T.O. became seized of the jurisdiction to take any proceedings against the assessee which the law permitted - Notice was, competently issued by him and was also valid, because it was issued before the expiry of eight years from the end of the relevant assessment year - Answered in favour of Revenue.
