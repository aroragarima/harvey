Union of India and Others v Dwarka Prasad Tiwari

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1016; (2006) 10 SCC 388; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1903; 2006 (6) AWC 6075; JT 2006 (9) SC 18; 2007 (2) MLJ 278; 2007 (2) MLJ(SC) 278; 2006(10) SCALE 233; 2006 (8) SCJ 30; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 217; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 218

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Broadcasting Act, 1981

Summary: Service - Central Reserve Police Force Rules, 1955, r. 27 - Respondent was posted as a constable with Central Reserve Police Force - He fired one bullet without orders and without any sufficient reason - Departmental inquiry in terms of r. 27 was ordered alleging misconduct and negligence/remissness in discharge of his duty in his capacity as a member of Force - Dismissed from services u/r. 27(a)(i) - High Court set aside order of punishment - Present appeal - Held, Court should not interfere with administrator's decision unless it was illogical or suffers from procedural impropriety or was shocking to conscience of Court, in sense that it was in defiance of logic or moral standards - Scope of judicial review is limited to deficiency in decision-making process and not decision - In a normal course if punishment imposed is shockingly disproportionate it would be appropriate to direct Disciplinary Authority or Appellate Authority to reconsider penalty imposed - HC has not indicated any reason for coming to conclusion that punishment was shockingly disproportionate - HC's order therefore reflects non application of mind - Impugned order of HC is set aside - Matter is remitted to HC - Appeal filed by UOI allowed - Appeal filed by respondent dismissed.
