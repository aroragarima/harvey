Sanjay Verma v Manik Roy and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1280; (2006) 13 SCC 608; AIR 2007 SC 1332; 2007 (66) ALR 304; 2007 (1) AWC 462; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 145; 2007 (3) MLJ(SC) 377; 2007 (147) PLR 229; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 408; 2007 (102) RD 253; 2006(14) SCALE 80; [2006] Supp10 S.C.R. 469

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1, r. 10 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 52 - Suit for specific performance of contract - Lis Pendens - Application for impleadment - Allowed the respondent to implead by High Court - Whether such Order of allowing impleadment is valid? - Held, respondents being transferee's pendente lite without leave of court cannot as of right seek impleadment in suit - As order of court had not been obtained for alienation of properties - Alienation obviously would be hit by doctrine of lis pendens by operation of s. 52 - Mere pendency of a suit does not prevent one of parties from dealing with property constituting subject matter of suit - S. 52 only postulates a condition that alienation will in no manner affect rights of other party under any decree which may be passed in suit unless property was alienated with permission of Court - Order of High Court set aside - Appeal allowed.
