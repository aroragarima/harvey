Mannan Lal v Mst. Chhotaka Bibi

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 101; (1970) 1 SCC 769; AIR 1971 SC 1374; 1971 ALJ 569; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 253

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Deficiency Of Court Fees

Summary: U.P. High Court (Abolition of Letters Patent Appeals) Act, 1962, s. 3 - Court Fees Act, 1870, s. 4 - CPC, 1908, s.149, O. 41, r. 1 - Bar against filing Letters Patent Appeals - Determination - Insufficiently stamped memorandum of appeal - Actually filed before the commencement of 1962 Act - Directions issued by High Court to accept it on payment of deficit fees - If so acceprted even after the commencement of the Act, it can be treated as an appeal pending on the date immediately, or preceding the date of enforcement of 1962 Act - Held, in such case the bar of s. 3 will not operate - Appeal Allowed.
