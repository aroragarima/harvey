Santosh Kumar and Others v G.R. Chawla and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 727; (2003) 10 SCC 513; AIR 2003 SC 3304; 2003 (5) AWC 4464; 2003 (99) FLR 348; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 54; 2003 LabIC 3479; 2003 (3) LLN 1181; 2003(7) SCALE 339; 2003 (1) SCW 4692; 2003 (6) SLR 460; 2003 (5) SLT 325; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 561; 2003 (6) Supreme 502

Case Digest

Subject: Health & Drug; Service

Summary: U.P. Regularisation of Ad hoc Appointments (for Posts Within the Purview of the PSC) Rules, 1979 - U.P. Inspectors of Drugs (Services) Rules, 1980 - Appointment - Eligibility - Whether ad hoc services can be counted for the purpose of seniority on the basis of continuous officiation - Held, the contention has to be negatived in view of the specific finding by the High Court that direct recruits were appointed either on 16/17.09.1982 and the services of the appellants were regularised only on 22.09.1982. The rule of the seniority clearly provides that seniority in any category or cadre post shall be determined from the date of the order of substantive appointment.
