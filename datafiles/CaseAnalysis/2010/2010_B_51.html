Sushila Chemicals Private Limited and another v Bharat Coking Coal Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 863; (2010) 10 SCC 388; JT 2010 (11) SC 266; 2010(9) SCALE 593; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 518

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Mines & Minerals - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Termination of agreement - Legality - Pursuant to public advertisements issued by Coal India Limited (respondent No.2) calling upon entrepreneurs to establish coal based industries, appellants purchased the technology and established plants for manufacturing special smokeless fuel - Thereafter, on recommendation of Bharat Coking Coal Limited (BCCL; respondent No.1), Coal India Limited granted coal linkage to the appellants - Later, Government of India adopted a new coal distribution policy under which coal was to be supplied to different consumers through a Fuel Supply Agreement (FSA) - In accordance with this new policy, BCCL entered into FSA with the appellants for supply of coal - Clause 4.4 of FSA provided that the total quantity of coal supplied to the appellants under the agreement is meant for use in the plants of the appellants and the appellants shall not sell or divert or transfer the coal for any purpose whatsoever and in the event they engage or plan to engage into any such re-sale or trade, the BCCL shall terminate the FSA forthwith without any liabilities or damages whatsoever payable to the appellants - Thereafter, FIR was registered by Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) against appellants alleging mis-utilization of the allotted coal and sale of the allotted coal by the appellants in the open market - Hence, BCCL suspended supply of coal to appellants and a show cause notice was issued to appellants - Appellants filed writ petitions challenging the suspension of supply of coal by BCCL to the appellants - Single Judge held that BCCL could not suspend the supplies of coal to the appellants on the mere institution of a criminal case by the CBI - On appeal, DB of HC reversed the order passed by Single Judge - Hence, present appeal - Whether suspension of supplies of coal by BCCL without terminating the agreement (FSA) was unreasonable and arbitrary and violative of art. 14 of the Constitution? - Held, Coal India Limited and BCCL were Government Companies of the Government of India and were bound by the policy decision of the Government of India and under the new Coal Distribution Policy mis-utilization of allotted coal and black-marketing of such coal by the appellants was to be checked - Moreover, it is clear from Clause 4.4 of the FSA and the new Coal Distribution Policy decision, the very object of FSA as well as policy decision of the Government was to allot coal to the appellants for utilization in their plants and not for any other purpose and hence, BCCL will have the right to suspend supplies of coal to the appellants where it has doubts that the appellants may mis-utilize the allotted coal and divert or sell the same in open market - Therefore, if the FIR lodged by the CBI, created serious doubts that the allotted coal may be diverted or sold in the open market instead of being utilized in the plants of the appellants, the BCCL would be within its rights to suspend the supplies of coal to the appellants till the doubts are cleared in appropriate proceedings - Hence, Coal India Limited and BCCL did not act arbitrarily or unreasonably to suspend the supplies of coal under FSA to the appellants - DB of the HC was, therefore, right in setting aside the judgment and order of the Single Judge quashing the order of the BCCL suspending supplies of coal to the appellants - Appeals dismissed.
