Dr Subodh Nautiyal v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, R.M. Sahai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 906; (1993) Supp1 SCC 593; AIR 1991 SC 1131; 1992 (7) SLR 740

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Education - Admission to Medical Colleges - Seeking direction - Held, course throughout India is intended to commence on a particular date and, therefore, admissions must be over prior to date of such commencement - Intention of this Court has been to appropriately regulate teaching in Medical Colleges both at undergraduate as also in post-graduate level - Happenings which have been noticed by this Court and which are being placed before court now and then clearly indicate that intention of this Court is not being kept in view by authorities charged with implementation - Therefore, scheme is not getting enforced in proper way - This is a technical course and to admit a student four months after commencement would not at all be correct - Application dismissed.
