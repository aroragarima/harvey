Shakuntala Bai and others v Mahaveer Prasad

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 445; (2015) 10 SCC 550; AIR 2015 SC 2769; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 39; 2015(5) ALL MR 414; 2015 (112) ALR 667; 2015 (6) ALT(SC) 8; 2015 (5) Bom.C.R. 80; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 359; JT 2015 (6) SC 331; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2509; 2016 (3) MahLJ 639; 2015 (5) MLJ 448; 2016 (2) MPLJ 533; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 708; 2015 (129) RD 608; 2015(7) SCALE 391; [2015] 10 S.C.R. 266; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Bequest, Succession Certificate

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 372 - Genuineness of Will - Respondent no. 1 filed application before Trial Court u/s. 372 of the Act for issuance of succession certificate on ground that testator executed Will dt.15-11-1978 in supersession of earlier Wills dt.1-11-1962 and 23-12-1974 and bequeathed property mentioned therein to him - However, on perusal of evidence, Trial Court held that Will dt.15-11-1978 was not valid one and returned finding that Will dt.23-12-2974 was last the valid instrument of bequest - Aggrieved, respondent no. 1 filed appeal before HC and Single Judge of HC allowed appeal and directed issuance of succession certificate - Aggrieved, appellants filed appeal before DB of HC but the same was dismissed and hence, instant appeals - Whether Will dt.15-11-1978 is genuine and valid.

Held, AW3 and AW4 have been able to satisfactorily prove the execution of the Will dt.15-11-1978 and the attestation thereof by two witnesses, as required in law. The signature of testator on said document has been endorsed by both the handwriting experts. The report of Forensic Science Lab also corroborates this finding. The recitals of the Will also provide sufficient justification for the bequest in favour of respondent no. 1. The fact that wife and daughter of the testator had, at all relevant time supported respondent no. 1 in his initiatives to obtain the succession certificate is also a formidable factor in his favour as well as in endorsement of genuineness of Will dt.15-11-1978. Further, even though Will dt.23-12-1974 had been registered, yet, no steps had been taken by the non-applicants to obtain the probate thereof. Hence, in view of factual background, the dispensation made by testator in favour of respondent no.1 could not be repudiated to be in defiance of logic or unfair vis-a-vis the other members of the family and there are no vitiating or suspicious circumstances invalidating the bequest. Hence, conclusions recorded by HC are plausible being based on the materials on record and thus do not warrant any interference in the appeals. On an overall consideration of the pleadings and the evidence adduced, the findings of Trial Court have been rightly reversed. Hence, appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
