Surendra Chauhan v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 223; (2000) 4 SCC 110; (2000) SCC (Cr) 772; AIR 2000 SC 1436; AIR 2000 SCW 1005; 2000 (1) CCR 343; 2000 (2) Crimes 117; 2000 CRLJ 1789; 2000 (2) JCC 621; JT 2000 (3) SC 507; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 421; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 372; 2000(2) SCALE 513; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 515; 2000 (3) SLT 209; 2000 (3) Supreme 189

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Abortion

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 314 - Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971, s. 4 - Common Intention - Accused having illicit relations with deceased - Pregnancy of deceased - Taking to incompetent doctor with intent to cause her miscarriage - Clinic not certified within meaning of s. 4 of Medical Termination of Pregnancy Act, 1971 - Held, common intention proved but no overt act is attributed to individual accused s. 34 attracts as essentially involving vicarious liability - Act was done by doctor in furtherance of common intention of accused - Conviction proper.
