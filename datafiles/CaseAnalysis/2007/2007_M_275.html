Messrs Arvind Constructions Company Private Limited v Messrs Kalinga Mining Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 565; (2007) 6 SCC 798; AIR 2007 SC 2144; 2007 (3) AWC 2910; JT 2007 (7) SC 32; 2007 (148) PLR 766; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 773; 2007(7) SCALE 567; 2007 (5) SCJ 188; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 180

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Respondent Firm entered into an agency agreement with the appellant, a private limited company for a term of 10 years - Application under s. 9 dismissed by High Court - Appeal against - Plea that since the agreement relied upon by the appellant in the light of the irrevocable Power of Attorney was co-terminus with the mining lease granted to the respondent firm by the State Government, the same could not be terminated and would not come to an end by efflux of time - Prima facie, it is not possible to say that the High Court was wrong in thinking that it may be a case where an injunction could not be granted in view of the provisions of the Specific Relief Act - Aspects to be considered by the Arbitral Tribunal - Appeal dismissed.
