Union of India v Shankar Lal Soni and another

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2113; (2010) 12 SCC 563; 2010 (6) AWC 5545; JT 2010 (4) SC 70; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 550; 2010(3) SCALE 774; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 593

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Socio-Economic

Keywords: M.P. Excise Act

Summary: Socio-Economic - Constitution - Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950 - Public Interest Litigation (PIL) - Airline and railway concessional ticket to senior citizen - Imposition of condition - Legality - Senior Advocate wrote a letter to HC complaining that though senior citizens were entitled to Airlines tickets on concession, conditions such as, applying for a concessional ticket 7 days in advance of the journey and stay a minimum of 2 days at the outgoing destination in order to be eligible for the return ticket, had been, in fact, nullified the concessions - HC took up the matter as PIL and found that conditions were unreasonable - HC issued directions to Indian Airlines and Indian Railways - Appellant/Union of India contended that policy matters were matters of administrative law and best left to the administration and could not be a cause for interference by the court unless they could be said to be totally arbitrary or violative of some Statute or Law - Held, a concession granted by a carrier be it the Railways or the Airlines or the Road Transport Corporation is a concession only and no person is entitled to insist that the concession should be with conditions determined by that person - No reasons were given by the HC as to why the Court found that some of the conditions imposed were not justified - HC proceeded only on the basis of its subjective satisfaction to arrive at the conclusion that the conditions were not to the benefit of senior citizens ignoring the basic nature of a concession given on the basis of administrative policy and ignoring the effect that they could have on the concessionaires - A decision to grant a certain concession or a certain benefit and the conditions for their grant are a matter for the administrators alone and the court should not interfere in the matter on the premise that it was of the opinion that some of the conditions imposed were not justified - A concession based on an administrative decision de hors a statute as in the present case stands on a yet weaker footing - Therefore, HC order is completely unsustainable and hence set aside - Appeals allowed.
