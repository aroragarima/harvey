Chinnaswamy v State Of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1962 SC 1788; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 111; 1963 CRLJ 8; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 412

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 417, 423(4), 439 and 439(1)(4) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 17, 25, 26, 27 and 87 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 380, 411 and 457 - (A) Whether it is open to a High Court in revision to set aside an order of acquittal even at instance of private parties, though State may not have thought fit to appeal? - Held, HC can only in exceptional cases, when there is some glaring defect in procedure or there is a manifest error on a point of law and consequently there has been a flagrant miscarriage of justice - (B) Whether recovery statements made by accused under s. 27 are wholly admissible or only that part of it is admissible, where he stated that he would point out place but not that part where he stated that he had hidden? - Extent of information admissible must depend on exact nature of discovered to which such information is required to relate - It is only that part which distinctly relates to discovery which is admissible; but if any part of statement distinctly relates to discovery it will be admissible wholly and court cannot say that it will excise one part of statement because it is of a confessional nature - (c) Whether appeal court should be ordered to re-hear appeal after admitting statement it had ruled out or there should be a retrial? - It depends upon facts of each case whether HC would order appeal court to re-hear appeal or would order a retrial by Trial Court - Appeal Court when it re-hears appeal should not be influenced by any observations of HC on appreciation of evidence and should bring to bear its own mind on evidence after taking into consideration that part of evidence which was, considered inadmissible previously by it - Held, Appeal allowed.
