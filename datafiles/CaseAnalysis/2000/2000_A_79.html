Agricultural Produce Market Committee v Ashok Harikuni and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2824; (2000) 8 SCC 61; AIR 2000 SC 3116; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 1; 2000 (2) LLJ 1382; 2000(6) SCALE 461; 2001 (7) SLR 286; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 379

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 9 - Karnataka Agricultural Produce Marketing (Regulation), ss. 3, 4, 7, 20, 58, 59, 62, 63, 65, 66, 67, 69, 72, 73, 83, 143, 144, 2(a), 2(j) and 9(3) - Central Provinces and Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947, s. 2(14) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 309 , 310 and 311 - Whether the appellant, an Agricultural Produce Market Committee is an "industry" as contemplated under the Industrial Disputes Act, If yes, will not an employee under the State Act be governed by the Central Act ? - Exclusion of the Central Act is limited to the sphere as specified under this sub-section, namely, payment of compensation to the officers or servants of the transferred employees, thus by necessary implication, other field under the Central Act is made applicable. Hence this submission has no merit. - In view of the settled legal principle the width of "industry" being of widest amplitude and in view of the Preamble, Objects and Reasons and the scheme of the Act, the predominant object clearly being regulation and control of trading of agricultural produce, thus the appellant committee including its functionaries cannot be said to be performing functions which are sovereign in character - The appellant would fall within the definition of "industry" under s. 2(j) of the Central Act - Held, the respondent employees are "workman" under the Central Act as held by the Labour Court and confirmed by the High Court - The Labour Court has dealt with each individual cases and came to the conclusion in favour of respondent employees which has also been confirmed by Learned Single Judge and Division Bench of the High Court, which does not call for any interference- Appeals have no merit and are dismissed.
