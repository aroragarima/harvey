State of Tamil Nadu v Rajendran

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M. Srinivasan, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 740; (1999) 8 SCC 679; (2000) SCC (Cr) 40; AIR 1999 SC 3535; 1999 CRLJ 4552; JT 1999 (7) SC 348; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 89

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 436 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 366 and 386 - (A) Powers of Court - Death sentence - Confirmation of - Reference is made to High Court u/s. 366 of CrPC by Sessions Judge on passing a sentence of death - Whether HC can reappreciate and reassess entire facts and law so as to arrive at its independent conclusion? - Without appreciating evidence and other materials on record, come to conclusion that accused is entitled to benefit of doubt - (B) Murder - Circumstantial evidence - Wife and two children of accused burnt in hut - Accused was inside house on date of incident - Was seen coming out of hut from roof by neighbours - Standing outside as silent spectator - Death of wife on account of asphyxia due to strangulation - Accused offering no explanation - Whether accused could be convicted u/ss. 302 and 436 of IPC? - Held, (A) it is duty of HC in death reference to consider evidence afresh - Merely on surmises and conjectures, said conclusion cannot be sustained - (B) Liable to be convicted u/ss. 302 and 436 - Penalty of death sentence commuted to imprisonment for life as case does not represent one of rarest of rare cases - Appeal allowed.
