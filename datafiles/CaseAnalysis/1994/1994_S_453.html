State of Madhya Pradesh and Others v Krishnadas Tikaram

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1059; (1995) Supp1 SCC 587; 1994 (56) DLT 533; JT 1994 (6) SC 576; 1995 (1) RentLR 105; 1994(4) SCALE 523; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 747

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Land & Property - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 - Grant of lease - Whether cancellation of lease granted to respondent valid ? - It is settled law that grant or renewal is a fresh grant and must be made consistent with law - In case State Government decides to grant fresh lease or renewal of lease it is mandatory that it should obtain prior approval of Central Govt. - Held, no prior approval of Central Govt. had been obtained u/s. 2 of Act - State Govt. realised mistake in directing renewal only when Forest Department had objected to renewal of lease in favour of respondent - Therefore, cancellation of order before it came into effect by registering had been properly made by appellant - Appeal allowed.
