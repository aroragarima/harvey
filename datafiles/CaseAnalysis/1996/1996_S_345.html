State of Taiml Nadu v Sivarasan Alias Raghu Alias Sivarasa and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1833; (1997) SCC (Cr) 362; (1997) 1 SCC 682; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 345; 1997 (1) CCR 1; 1997 (1) Crimes 170; JT 1996 (10) SC 141; 1996(7) SCALE 920; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 243; 1997 (2) Supreme 379

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities ( Prevention) Act, 1987 - Accused, were Sri Lankan nationals and members of LTTE, and were engaged in obtaining explosive substances, manufacturing bombs and sending them to LTTE - Accused were convicted, u/s. 120-B of the IPC, r/w. s. 3(3) of the 1987 Act, but were acquitted - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the accused were properly acquitted - Held, that the attempt to commit suicide was established beyond reasonable doubt - However, the acquittal of all the accused for the offence punishable, u/s. 120-B of the IPC, r/w. s. 3(3) of the 1987 Act, was confirmed, and accused was convicted, u/s. 353 of the IPC - Prosecution had failed to establish that any of the accused had the intention to endanger life or cause serious injury to property - It was rightly held that the charge, u/s. 120-B of the IPC, r/w. s. 3(3) of the 1987 Act, had not been proved by the prosecution - Appeal partly allowed.
