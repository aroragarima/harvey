Gujarat Steel Tube Company Limited v Virchandbhai B. Shah and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1381; (1999) 8 SCC 11; AIR 1999 SC 3839; 2000 (1) Bom.C.R. 906; [1999] 98 Comp Cas 716; 2000 (1) G.L.R. 916; JT 1999 (8) SC 298; 1999(6) SCALE 537; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 624

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, Sick Industrial Company (Special Provisions) Act, 1985

Summary: Corporate - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s.11(4) and 12(2) - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 15(1) and 22(1) - Transfer of Property Act , s. 106 - Whether the High Court was right in concluding that the provisions of s. 22 of the SICA, 1985 did not in any way prevent the filing of an eviction petition on the ground of non-payment of rent and that the order u/s. 11(4) of the Bombay Rent Act could be passed? - The petitioner was entitled, at the time when the application u/s. 11(4) was filed, to the protection of the Rent Control Act and no more - The filing of an eviction petition on the ground of non-payment of rent cannot be regarded as filing of a suit for recovery of money - If a tenant does not pay the rent, then the protection which is given by the Rent Control Act against his eviction is taken away and with the non-payment of rent an order of eviction may be passed - When an application u/s. 11(4) is filed, the Trial Court in effect gives an opportunity to the tenant to pay the rent failing which the consequences provided for in the sub-s. would follow - An application u/s. 11(4) cannot be regarded as being akin to a suit for recovery of money - S. 22(1) was not applicable to the eviction proceedings instituted by the landlord against the sick company - Held, petition is dismissed
