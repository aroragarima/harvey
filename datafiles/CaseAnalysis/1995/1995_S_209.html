Shri Suryakant Venkatrao Mahadik v Smt. Saroj Sandesh Naik (Bh

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, N.P. Singh, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1860; (1996) 1 SCC 384; JT 1995 (8) SC 686; 1995(7) SCALE 92; 1996 (1) SCJ 508; [1995] Supp6 S.C.R. 530; 1996 (1) Supreme 213

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 100(1)(b) and 123(3) - Whether High Court rightly declared election of Petitioner void on ground u/s. 100(1)(b) of Act for committing corrupt practice? - Held, what is material, is kind of use made of term 'Hindutva' and manner in which it was meant to be understood by audience to which speech was addressed - This particular speech by appellant was an appeal by a Hindu to a congregation of Hindu devotees in a Hindu temple during a Hindu religious festival with emphasis on Hindu religion for giving votes to a Hindu candidate espousing cause of Hindu religion - Word 'Hindutva' used in speech of appellant at that time, place and occasion has to be understood only as an appeal on ground of Hindu religion amounting to a corrupt practice u/s. 123(3) - Appeal dismissed.
