State Of U . P ., Etc . v Rafiquddin & Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 1181; (1987) Supp SCC 401; (1988) SCC (L&S) 183; AIR 1988 SC 162; 1987 (5) ATC 257; JT 1987 (4) SC 251; 1988 LabIC 344; 1987(2) SCALE 947; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 794; 1988 (1) SLR 491

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Service Regulations, U.P. Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, Selection - Interview, Procedure - Examination, Conditions Of Service., Seniority - Cadre, Determination Of Seniority - Date Of Appointment

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 309 and 234 - Uttar Pradesh Civil Service (Judicial Branch) Rules, 1951, rr. 19 and 22 - Determination of seniority - Held, distressed to find that Public Service Commission has been changing norms for considering suitability of candidates at behest of State Govt. after declaration of results - It has to act in an independent manner in making selection on prescribed norms and may consult State Govt. and High Court in prescribing norms for judging suitability of candidates if no norms are prescribed in Rules - Once norms and selection on conclusion of competitive examination has been done it should not reopen selection by lowering down norms at instance of Govt. - No system followed in making appointments as some candidates selected in subsequent examination were appointed earlier to those selected under earlier examination, with result those appointed to service later in time are made senior to those appointed in service earlier in time in accordance with r. 22 - It is necessary that effort should be made to appoint successful candidates of a particular examination before candidate of subsequent examination is appointed - If this is not possible State Govt. and HC should consider amending r. 22 to ensure that length of service rendered by an offer is respected - Appeal allowed.
