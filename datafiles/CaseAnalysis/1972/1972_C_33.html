M/S. Produce Exchange Corpn. Ltd. v Commissioner Of Excise, Assam & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 143; (1972) 3 SCC 713; AIR 1972 SC 2281; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 201; 1973 UJ 23

Case Digest

Subject: Socio-Economic

Summary: Eastern Bengal And Assam Excise Act, 1910, ss. 19 and 36 - Grant of exclusive privilege of supplying country spirit to retail vendors - (A) Govt. can, on good grounds, prefer one seller to another - Validity - (B) Rules framed u/s. 36 of 1910 Act, r. 93 - Whether Govt. can negotiate - Held, there was justification in preferring a manufacturer to others and evidently the idea was that there should be reasonable guarantee in the matter of supply of country liquor - Govt. can negotiate either with the tenderers or with others - Appeal Dismissed.
