M/S Suraj Mal Ram Niwas Oil Mills (P.) Ltd. v United India Insurance Co. Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 859; (2010) 10 SCC 567; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 23B; 2011(1) ALL MR 453; 2010 (6) AWC 6396; JT 2010 (11) SC 404; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 164; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 845B; 2010(10) SCALE 640

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Appellant had obtained an open transit insurance policy from the respondent covering 'all types of edible oils in tin' transported by rail/road (which had to be declared) - On 14.08.1992, appellant dispatched 1194 tins of oil valued at ' 5,84,790/- by rail and also by road to one 'X' - Railway wagon carrying the impugned goods met with an accident, resulting in extensive damage to the consignment - Appellant did not informed respondent about the said accident and forwarded the consignment in damaged condition by road - Subsequently, appellant claimed for the damages - Said claim was rejected by the respondent on the ground that the as per the terms and conditions of the policy, appellant were supposed to declare each and every dispatch which appellant failed to do - Respondent also rejected claim of the appellant on the ground that appellant have violated the terms and conditions of the policy by removing goods from the rail accident site without survey of the loss having been done by the respondent's Surveyor immediately after the accident - Appellant filed a complaint before the State Commission, preferring a claim of 5,50,798/- along with interest at the rate of 24% against the respondent - State Commission allowed the complaint of the appellant and directed the respondent to pay 4,39,178/- with interest at the rate of 12% per annum - On appeal, National Commission held that the respondent's repudiation of the claim of the appellant was in order as appellant failed to report the loss caused by Rail accident and removal of consignment without giving Surveyor a chance to assess the loss at first hand and also appellant's not reporting each and every dispatch as per terms of the policy - Hence, present appeal - Whether National Commission rightly held that the respondent rightly repudiate the claim of the appellant? - Held, yes - Claim of the appellant must fail on the short ground that there was a breach of the afore-extracted special condition i.e. each and every consignment must be declared before dispatch of goods, incorporated in the cover note - Therefore, there was a breach of the special condition in the cover note for the insurance policy on the part of the appellant and, therefore, the repudiation of the claim of the appellant by the respondent was justified - Appeal dismissed.
