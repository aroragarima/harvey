Poulose and Mathen v Collector of Central Excise, and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Paripoornan, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3065; (1997) 3 SCC 50; AIR 1997 SC 965; 1997 (69) ECR 5; 1997 (90) E.L.T. 264; JT 1997 (2) SC 213; 1997 (18) RLT 663; 1997(1) SCALE 661; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 888; 1997 (3) Supreme 448

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 3 - Levy of excise duty - Trade Notice - (A) Validity of non serving of proper notice to company before saddling liability for a period beyond period mentioned in notice - (B) Tribunal casually referred to later tariff advice No. 6/85, without fully and effectively appreciating its contents, scope and impact of earlier tariff advice - Conclusion based on surmises also ignored trade notice without proper reasons - Validity of Order - (C) Different views as to trade advice - Effect - Held, it is unfair and vitiates proceedings - Order of Tribunal, held, liable to be set aside - Assessee should be given benefit of doubt and opinion which is in its favour should be given effect to - Appeal allowed.
