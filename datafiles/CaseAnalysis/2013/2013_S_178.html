State Of J&K & Ors. v Sat Pal

Supreme Court of India

05 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 69; (2013) 11 SCC 737; AIR 2013 SC 1258; 2013(3) ALL MR 401; JT 2013 (2) SC 530; 2013(2) SCALE 256; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 648; 2013 (2) SLR 601

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136,142 - Recruitment - Merit List - Waiting List - Eligibility - Public Works Department conducted a process of selection, for recruitment against posts of Junior Engineer (Civil) Grade-II. respondent participated in process of selection and his named figured in final merit/select list of SC candidates - Respondent upon knowing that selected SC candidate did not join even after appointment letter was offered made representation before authorities pointed out, that since he has not joined he may be given opportunity as in merit/select list, his name figured immediately after name of selected candidate - Respondents representation remained undecided as such respondent filed writ petition contending that waiting list was valid for one year as such he was eligible for consideration - HC, issued notice to appellant for filing objections, but no objections were filed as such HC, passed orders directing appellant to examine claim of respondent for appointment - Appellant rejected claim of respondent, against which appellant filed Contempt petition and HC, passed order directing appellant to appoint respondent against post - Appellant filed LPA against orders of Single Judge in contempt petition contending that orders passed by Single Judge were not permissible in exercise of contempt jurisdiction, DB, held appeal as not maintainable - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC, was justified in passing orders directing appellant to appoint respondent for post and whether representation made by respondent was time barred - Held, reason indicated by appellants in declining claim of respondent for appointment out of waiting list was clearly unjustified, waiting list would start to operate only after posts for which recruitment was conducted, had been completed - Waiting list would commence to operate, when offers of appointment had been issued to those emerging on top of merit list, existence of a waiting list, allows room to appointing authority to fill up vacancies which arise during subsistence of waiting list which commences to operate, after vacancies for which recruitment process had been conducted had been filled up - Offer was made, consequent upon his selection in process of recruitment, validity of waiting list, in facts of this case, had to be determined with reference to 22-4-2008, because vacancy was offered to candidate on 22-4-2008, it was vacancy, for which respondent had approached HC, it was acknowledged position recorded by appellants in impugned order, that waiting list was valid till May, 2008 - If was found eligible for appointment against vacancy in question out of same waiting list, respondent herein would be equally eligible for appointment against vacancy - Date of filing of representation by parties concerned and/or date on which competent authority chooses to fill up vacancy in question, was of no consequence whatsoever, only relevant date was date of arising of vacancy, it would be a different legal proposition, if appointing authority decides not to fill up an available vacancy, despite availability of candidates on waiting list - Offer made to candidate on 22-4-2008 by itself, leads to inference that vacancy under reference arose within period of one year, i.e., during period of validity of waiting list postulated by rules, offer of vacancy to candidate, negates proposition posed above, i.e., desire of employer not to fill up vacancy, appellants wished to fill up vacancy under reference, moreover, this was not a case where respondent was seeking appointment against a vacancy, over and above posts for which process of selection/ recruitment was conducted - Based on inference, SC, had no hesitation in concluding that appellants ought to have appointed respondent, against vacancy which was offered - Appeal disposed of.
