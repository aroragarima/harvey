Mr. "X" v Hospital "Z"

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, P. Venkatarama Reddi, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1478; (2003) 1 SCC 500; AIR 2003 SC 664; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 24; 2003 (1) AllCJ 702; 2003 (51) ALR 11; 2003 (1) AWC 571; 2003 (1) CPJ(Sc) 14; 2003 (3) CRJ 535; JT 2002 (10) SC 214; 2003 (1) MLJ(SC) 89B; 2002(9) SCALE 224; 2002 (7) SLT 218; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 661; 2003 (1) Supreme 66; 2003 (1) UC 286

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family and Personal - Marriage - Whether a person suffering from HIV(+) contracting marriage with a willing partner after disclosing the factum of disease to that partner will be committing an offence within the meaning of s.269 and 270 IPC? - SC had by an order on 21/9/1988 dismissed the said petition while giving several findings, particularly those relating to 'suspended right to marry'- Held, the SC had rested its decision on the facts of the case that it was open to the hospital or the Doctor concerned to reveal such information to persons related to the girl whom he intended to marry and she had a right to know about the HIV positive status of the appellant - If that was so, there was no need for the Court to go further and declare in general as to what rights and obligations arise in such context as to right to privacy or confidentiality or whether such persons are entitled to be married or not or in the event such persons marry they would commit an offence under law or whether such right is suspended during the period of illness - Observations made by the Court, except to the extent of holding that the appellant's right was not affected in any manner in revealing his HIV positive status to the relatives of his fiancÃ©e, were uncalled for - Appeals Disposed Of.
