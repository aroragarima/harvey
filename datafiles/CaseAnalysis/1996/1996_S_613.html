Ashwani Kumar and Others v State of Bihar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 372; (1997) 2 SCC 1; (1997) SCC (L&S) 267; AIR 1997 SC 1628; 1997 (75) FLR 25; JT 1997 (1) SC 243; 1997 LabIC 578; 1997 (2) LLJ 856; 1996(9) SCALE 731; 1997 (1) SLJ 178; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 120; 1998 (2) UPLBEC 1550

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 16(1), 21, 226 and 309 - Appointments - Legality - Whether High Court was right in upholding action of Govt. of cancelling all appointments made by Dr. Mallick? - Held, Dr. Mallick was Director, Tuberculosis Centre at Patna - Recruitments made by Dr. Mallick were arbitrary, capricious and were null and void as he violated established norms and procedures for recruiting such employees - Class III posts recruitments according to merit list prepared on basis of marks obtained by candidates at school or college examinations and appointment to vacant posts to be made according to instructions enclosed with Resolution concerned - Class IV servants, Committee appointed for purpose to publish advertisement through employment exchange as per direction contained in Appointment Department - Committee to select candidates on merits following prescribed procedure in appropriate circulars and rules and follow rules of reservation as in vogue and prepare merit list and should submit it to Govt. - Appeals dismissed.
