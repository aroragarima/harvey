Larsen And Toubro Ltd. & Anr. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 310; (2011) 5 SCC 430; AIR 2011 SC 2194; 2011 (3) AWC 3138; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 251; JT 2011 (6) SC 1; 2011 (5) RCR(Civil) 568; 2011(5) SCALE 335; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1127

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Rejection of bid - Validity - Respondent No.1 sent a Request For Proposal (RFP) to the several persons including Petitioner No.1 for supply of 20 Fast Patrol Vessels (FPV) for the Indian Coast Guard - In response to the said RFP, the Petitioner No.1 submitted its bid containing a technical proposal and a commercial proposal in two parts - Although, the offer of the Petitioner No.1 was found to be the lowest (L-1), its bid was held to be non-responsive, because, despite the tender condition that the price was to be firm and fixed for the entire duration of the contract and would not be subject to escalation, the Petitioner No.1 had claimed the benefit of Foreign Exchange Rate Variation - Contract Negotiation Committee awarded the contract to Respondent No. 4 - Writ petition was filed thereagainst - HC held that since the terms and conditions of the price to be firm and fixed was one of the more important ingredients of the tender, the submission of a bid which violated the said condition rendered the bid non-responsive - HC also rejected the Petitioner's contention that as per the bid documents the Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) method was required to be used to arrive at the actual and final cost which would be payable by the Respondent Nos.1 to 3, for the contract in question - Writ petition was dismissed - Hence, instant Special leave petition - Held, HC did not commit any error in dismissing the Writ Petition filed by the Petitioners, since in the absence of compliance with the terms and conditions relating to firm and fixed price offer, the Petitioners stood excluded from consideration - Offer in this regard made by the Respondent No.4 satisfied the requirements of a firm and fixed offer, since once the commercial bids were opened, there was no further scope of the rates being altered, which was not so in the case of the Petitioners, which tried to make its bid responsive by withdrawing the initial offer and substituting the same with another - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
