Shahzad Hasan Khan v Ishtiaq Hasan Khan And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, M.P. Thakkar

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28259; (1987) SCC (Cr) 415; (1987) 2 SCC 684; AIR 1987 SC 1613; 1987 (2) Crimes 438; 1987 CRLJ 1872; 1987 CrLR(SC) 430; JT 1987 (2) SC 323; 1987(1) SCALE 1249; [1987] 3 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss.437, 439 - Bail - (A) As a part of practice prevailing in the High Court, the subsequent application was placed before the judge who had earlier disposed of the application (3 times) - Another Judge also directing placing of subsequent application before, however, subsequently disposing himself - Validity - (B) Grant of bail - Sustainability of order granting bail to an accused who committed murder in broad day light as witnessed by no. of persons - And also there was allegation of tampering of evidence - Held, not proper - Held, order of grant of bail is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
