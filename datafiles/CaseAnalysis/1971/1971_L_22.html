Lt. Col. S. K. Kashyap and Another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 817; (1971) 2 SCC 126; (1971) SCC (Cr) 452; AIR 1971 SC 1120; 1971 CRLJ 832; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 881

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Court Martial

Summary: Army Act,1950 - Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1952 - Criminal Law Amendment (Amending) Act, 1966 - (A) Army Act, 1950, ss. 122, 126 - Prosecution of certain army officers - Prosecution along with certain civilians before Special Judge on charges of conspiracy and corruption - Commanding Officer suggested institution of Court Martial proceedings after expiry of period of limitation as prescribed u/s. 122 - (B) Criminal Courts and Court Martial (Adjustment of Jurisdiction) Rules, 1952 - (1) C.O. informed Special Judge that Court Martial proceeding would not be instituted and military authorities never asked Criminal Court for delivery of army officers to them - Inference - (2) Fact that C.O. exercised discretion u/r.5 by communicating Criminal Court that accused army officers should be tried by Court Martial - Cancellation - Permissibility - (C) Criminal Law Amendment (Amending) Act, 1966 - (1) Word 'pending' u/s.5, meaning of - (2) In order that case be pending on 30-06-1966 within s. 5 (1) (a) charges against accused need not have been actually framed - Held, special Judge was not unjustified in asking C.O. to make reference to Central Govt. for determination as to Court before which proceedings would be started - The only conclusion could be that the C.O. formed opinion that accused army officers should be tried by Criminal Court, hence there was no conflict of jurisdiction of Criminal Court and Court Martial to try the case - Does not debar him from cancelling the said communication if after consideration of facts and circumstances he finds that Court Martial proceedings should not be instituted - Word 'pending' means that matter is not concluded and Court having cognizance of it can make order on matter in issue - Appeal Dismissed.
