State of West Bengal v Haresh C. Banerjee and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 462; (2006) 7 SCC 651; 2006 (5) ESC 255A; JT 2006 (8) SC 501; 2006(8) SCALE 693; 2006 (3) ShimLC 304; 2006 (5) SLR 621; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 620; 2006 (6) Supreme 727

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 31(1),19(1)(f), 300A, 309 - West Bengal Services (Death-cum-Retirement Benefit) Rules, 1971, r. 10(1) - Validity of r.10(1) - r.10(1) provides for withholding of pension - HC held that r.10(1) to be ultra vires provisions of arts.19(1)(f), 31(1) and pension was a property and its payment does not depend upon discretion of Govt. - Appeal against - Held, r.10(1) is authority of law under which pension could be withheld on compliance of stipulations of rule - Such a rule could not be held ultra vires even at a point of time when pension was a property to which art.19(1)(f) was applicable - Appeal allowed.
