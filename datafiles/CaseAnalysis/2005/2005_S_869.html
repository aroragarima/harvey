Surinder Singh @ Shingara Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 627; (2005) 7 SCC 387; (2005) SCC (Cr) 1674; 2005 CRLJ 4119; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 139; JT 2005 (8) SC 157; 2005(7) SCALE 131B; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1172

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 21 - CrPC, 1973 - Right to speedy Trial - Conviction of appellant for offences u/s. 302 r/w 34 IPC - Application filed by appellant for bail pending appeal against conviction - Appellant's case that he having undergone more than three years of actual sentence, deserved to be released on bail in view of law laid down in Dharampal's case (2000 (1) C.L.R.74) - Bail application - Dismissed by HC on ground that appellant had remained in actual custody after conviction only for two years and five months - Appealed herein - Right to speedy trial is a fundamental right implicit in content of art. 21 - Directions contained in Dharampal's case are only in nature of guidelines which have to be kept by in mind by Courts while dealing with application for grant of bail - Neither any hard and fast rule of universal application nor any invariable rule was laid down in said judgment which is to be observed with mathematical precision - Bail granted to appellant.
