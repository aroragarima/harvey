Mysore Rolling Mills (P) Limited v Collector Of Central Excise, Belgaum

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28214; (1987) 1 SCC 695; AIR 1987 SC 1488; 1987 (1) CCC 921; 1987 (11) ECC 421; 1987 (11) ECR 216; 1987 (28) E.L.T. 50; JT 1987 (1) SC 476; 1987(1) SCALE 382; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 318; 1987 (1) UJ 630

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4(i)(a) proviso, 4(1)(b), 11A and 35L - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 6(b)(i), 9 and 10(i)(c) - Excise Duty - (A) Whether handling charges could be added (are part of value) for computation of excise duty - (B) Determination of tenability of the contention that the period of limitation under the old provision having expired, the five year rule which has been applied was not available to be applied - And notice was barred by limitation - Tribunal found that the period of five years was applicable as it is a case of suppression - Supreme Court held that the handling charges represented pre-manufacturing cost and become a part of the value for computation of duty - Rule is retrospective in operation; once the rule comes into existence and jurisdiction under the rule is invoked it has got to cover a period upto five years preceding the date of issue of notice - Appeal dismissed.
