Tilak Ram and Others v Nathu and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 400; AIR 1967 SC 935

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, s. 19 - Acknowledgement - Determination of - Whether documents produced by appellants constitutes acknowledgment hence, giving them a fresh period of limitation? - Statement on which a plea of acknowledgment is based must relate to a subsisting liability - Words used in acknowledgment must indicate jural relationship between parties and it must appear that such a statement is made with intention of admitting that jural relationship - Held, neither document can be said to be one made with intention of admitting jural relationship nor with any consciousness or intention to admit jural relationship between them or to admit fact of said mortgages being subsisting at time when plaint was filed - Statement, therefore, cannot be, availed of as an acknowledgment of a subsisting jural relationship or of a subsisting right and a corresponding liability of being redeemed - None of these statements can be regarded as acknowledgment within meaning of s. 19 - Appeal dismissed.
