Avishek Goenka v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 221; (2012) 8 SCC 441; 2012 (3) ACC 358; AIR 2012 SC 3230; 2012 (6) ALD(SC) 80; 2012 (94) ALR 710; 2012 (4) CTC 772; 2012 (2) OLR 547; 2012(7) SCALE 97; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1126

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Practice & Procedure - Motor Vehicles Rules, 1989, r. 100 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 141 - Tinted Windows - Visual Light Transmission (VLT) - Application for clarification and/or modification - Applicants filed an application seeking clarification and/or modification of the SC judgement Avishek Goenka v. Union of India and another 2012 Indlaw SC 177 - Applicants submitted that they were not parties to the writ petition and were not aware of the proceedings before SC - Thus, their submissions could not be considered by the SC, hence, the SC judgement Avishek Goenka v. Union of India and another 2012 Indlaw SC 177 requires modification - Applicants also contended that r. 100(2) of 1989 Rules uses the expression 'maintained' which implies that safety glasses, including the wind screen, can be maintained with requisite VLT percentage even by use of black films - It was also contended that para 27 of the SC judgement Avishek Goenka v. Union of India and another 2012 Indlaw SC 177 needs modification by substituting the words 'use of black films of any VLT percentage' by the words 'use of black films of impermissible VLT percentage' - Whether application filed by the applicants could be allowed - Held, there was no challenge to r. 100 of the 1989 Rules - SC in Avishek Goenka v. Union of India and another 2012 Indlaw SC 177 has interpreted the r. 100 of the 1989 Rules de hors the other factors - Once SC interprets a provision of law, the law so declared would be the law of the land in terms of art. 141 of the Constitution - The law so declared was binding on all and must be enforced in terms thereof - Having interpreted the Rule to mean that it was the safety glasses alone with requisite VLT that can be fixed in a vehicle, it was not for SC to change the language of the said Rule - It would, primarily, be a legislative function and no role was to be performed by SC - Grounds raised by the applicants were primarily the grounds of inconvenience - Enforcement of law, if causes any inconvenience, was no ground for rendering a provision on the statute book to be unenforceable - Moreover, applicants fairly conceded that they were not raising any challenge to r. 100 of the 1989 Rules - Once that position was accepted, there was no reason to alter the interpretation given by us to the said Rule in SC earlier judgment - Hence, in terms of r. 100 of 1989 Rules, no material including films of any VLT can be pasted on the safety glasses of the car and this law was required to be enforced without demur and delay - Order accordingly.
