State of Orissa v Kanduri Sahoo

Supreme Court of India

04 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1128; (2004) 1 SCC 337; (2004) SCC (Cr) 368; AIR 2004 SC 833; 2003 (4) CCR 498; 2004 (1) Crimes 21; 2004 CRLJ 842; 2004 (91) ECC 161; JT 2003 (10) SC 273; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 196; 2003(10) SCALE 533; 2003 (1) SCW 7054; 2004 (1) ShimLC 258; 2003 (7) SLT 637; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 487; 2003 (8) Supreme 640

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Summary: Narcotic Drugs & Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - s. 20(b)(i) - Ganja - Accused acquitted of charges by the High Court although was found guilty by the Trial Court - No explanation about the custody of the articles for a period of 4 days in the Excise Malkhana - Held, though there was no specific order of the SDJM for custody of the articles when the accused was produced in the Court, it was clearly stated in the forwarding report that the seized articles and the sample thereof were produced in the Court along with the accused - Further held, conviction by the Trial Court restored.
