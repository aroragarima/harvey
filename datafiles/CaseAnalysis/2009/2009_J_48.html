Rekha Rana v Jaipal Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 847; (2009) 17 SCC 115; AIR 2009 SC 2946; 2009 (76) ALR 484; JT 2009 (9) SC 285; 2009 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1876; 2009 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2522; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 93; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 800; 2009(9) SCALE 462; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 20

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Ballot Papers, Order For, Electoral Rolls, Casting Of Votes, Marked Electoral Rolls, Purity

Summary: [A] Election - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, s. 94 - 'Secrecy of ballot' - Purity of ballot - Held, out of two competing principles, 'purity of election' principle must have its way and that 'rule of secrecy', as contemplated in Section 94 of Act, cannot be pressed into service to suppress a wrong coming to light and to protect a fraud on election process - Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;[B] Election - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, s. 94 - 'Secrecy of ballot' - Held, 'secrecy of ballot' principle pre-supposes a validly cast vote, sanctity and sacrosanct of which must in all events be preserved - Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;[C] Election - Representation of Peoples Act, 1951, s. 62(4) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 94-A - Opening marked electoral rolls - High Court directed opening of sealed marked electoral rolls - Whether impugned direction infringes principle of 'secrecy of ballot' and therefore, it is bad in law? - Election petitioner challenged election of appellant mainly on ground that appellant was beneficiary of a large number of void votes cast in her favour by impersonation - Factum of casting of votes by a particular elector could be proved only on basis of marked electoral rolls - Held, marked electoral roll is maintained primarily for purpose of identifying elector and as such, it cannot be said that its production would impair 'secrecy of ballot' principle - Exercise of extrication of void votes u/s. 62(4) of Act would not in any manner impinge on 'secrecy of ballot', especially when void votes are those which have to be treated as no votes at all - - On pleadings of parties, a case for inspection of marked electoral roll had been made out - No infirmity in impugned direction warranting interference - Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;[D] Election - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, r. 93(1), 93(1)(d) - Scope of Rule 93 - Inspection of Electoral Roll - Contention that twin conditions as contemplated in Rule 93 were not satisfied - Inspection under said Rule can be allowed only when two conditions are satisfied (i) material facts on basis of which inspection of documents is sought, must be clearly and specifically pleaded; and (ii) Court must be satisfied on evidence, even if in form of affidavit, that it is necessary to allow inspection in interest of justice - Held, in instant case, election petitioner specifically mentioned names of persons who had been impersonated or had double voted - Election petitioner had placed on record sufficient material to substantiate allegations made in election petition - No substance in contention that twin conditions as contemplated in Rule 93 were not satisfied - Appeal dismissed.
