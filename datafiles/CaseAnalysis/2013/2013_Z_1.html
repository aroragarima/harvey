State Of U.P. & Anr. v Zila Parishad Ghaziabad & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 60; 2013 (4) ADJ 468; AIR 2013 SC 1276; 2013 (2) AWC 1287; JT 2013 (2) SC 419; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 146; 2013 (118) RD 623; 2013(2) SCALE 158; 2013 (1) WLN(SC) 105

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: Constitution (Seventy-Third Amendment) Act, 1992, U.P. Panchayat Raj Act, 1947, Public Distribution System Control Order, 2001, Uttar Pradesh scheduled Commodities Distribution Order, 1990, U.P. Scheduled Commodities Distribution Order, 2004, U.P. Kshetriya Panchayats and Zila Panchayats Act, 1961, Public Distribution (Control) (Amendment) Order, 2004

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - U.P. Panchayat Rajya Act, 1947 - U.P. Kshetriya Panchayats and Zila Panchayats Act, 1961, ss. 32,33 - Constitution of India, 1950, 243G,243N,254 - Public Distribution System Control Order, 2001 - Essential Commodities - Distribution - Notification - Validity - Sustainability - Public Distribution System (PDS) was formulated for urban/rural consumers with objective of monitoring supplies of food grains and other essential commodities, of securing their equitable distribution and availability at fair price, and of stabilizing prices of essential commodities in open market, various schemes were prepared to help beneficiaries - GOI, issued notification dt. 9-6-1978 delegating power to State Govt. to passed orders specifying condition pertaining to PDS - State issued a GO designating officers of District level, to ensure proper supply and distribution of such commodities - Govt. issued G.O. providing for implementation of reservation policy in favour of persons belonging to SC/ST/Other BC and further for horizontal reservation for women, handicapped persons, etc., and for allotment of fair price shops in rural areas to them under PDS - Respondent No.1, filed writ petition challenging order of withdrawal of distribution from Gram Panchayats, on ground that, in view of the provisions of Ss. 32, 33 of Act 1961, PDS could only be assigned to Kshetriya and Zila Panchayats, hence, direction was sought to quash existing public distribution system of essential commodities in rural areas under impugned GO - Petition was opposed by Govt., but same was allowed - Hence instant appeal -

Appellants contended that art. 243-G was merely an enabling provision and not a source of power, therefore, it was open to Govt. to appoint either Kshetriya Panchayat or District Collector or any other authority for said purpose, further contended that respondent No. 1 did not have locus standi to file writ petition - Respondent No.1 has submitted that if submissions advanced on behalf of appellants were accepted, amendment in Constitution introducing arts. 243-G, 243-N, become meaningless - (A) Whether GO issued by Govt. providing for implementation of reservation policy in favour of persons belonging to SC/ST/Other BC was sustainable - Held, art. 243-G was an enabling provision as it enables Panchayats to function as institutions of self-government, further, Court noted that such law may contain provisions for devolution of powers and responsibilities upon Panchayats, subject to such conditions as may be specified therein, with respect to implementation of schemes for economic development and social justice as may be entrusted to them, including those in relations to matters listed in Eleventh Schedule - Enabling provisions were further subject to conditions as may be specified, therefore, it was for State Legislature to consider conditions and to make laws accordingly - It was also open to State to eliminate or modify same - Therefore, it was apparent that art. 243-G read with Eleventh Schedule was not a source of legislative power, and it was only an enabling provision that empowers a State to endow functions and devolve powers and responsibilities to local bodies by enacting relevant laws, local bodies could only implement schemes entrusted to them by State - Appeal allowed.
