Chhaganlal Keshavlal Mehta v Patel Narandas Haribhai

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 323; (1982) 1 SCC 223; AIR 1982 SC 121; 1982 (8) ALR 108; 1982 (1) GLR 325; 1981(3) SCALE 1861; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 166; 1982 UJ 120

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 60 - Suit for redemption - Maintainability of - Doctrine of estoppel - Applicability of - Held, the mortgage cannot be split up and must be treated as one and indivisible security - The suit was barred by estoppel - A co-mortgagor cannot be permitted to redeem his own share of mortgaged property only on payment of proportionate part of amount remaining due, i.e., integrity of mortgage cannot be broken - Even if all mortgagees are not before court in a suit filed by mortgagor for redemption of property, but mortgagor is prepared to pay entire amount due at foot of mortgage to such mortgagees as are before court and gives up his right under mortgage as against those mortgagees who are not before court, court can pass a decree for redemption directing that entire mortgage amount should be paid to mortgagees who are actually before court - The endorsements have to be read not in isolation but with reference to notices sent - Estoppel deals with questions of facts and not of rights, a man is not estopped from asserting a right which he had said that he will not assert and that there can be no estoppel against a statute - On facts and circumstances, not possible to hold that ingredients of s. 115 of Evidence Act have been fulfilled and view taken by DB of High Court is fully warranted by law - Appeal dismissed.
