Balram Singh Yadav @ Balram Yadav v Abhay Kumar Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 369; (2014) 6 SCC 699; AIR 2014 SC 2297; 2014(4) ALL MR 969; 2014 (105) ALR 696; 2014 (4) AWC 4134; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 14; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3062; 2014 (2) OLR 242; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 119; 2014(7) SCALE 429; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 532

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Representation of the People (2nd amendment) Act, 1956

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of the People (2nd amendment) Act, 1956 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 33(5) - Election - Invalidation order - Legality - Respondent filed nomination paper along with fifty others to enter into contest as member of Bihar Legislative Council - Returning Officer, while accepting nomination papers of all candidates, rejected nomination paper of respondent on ground that he had not filed relevant electoral roll which was required to be done, for he belonged to another constituency - After election respondent instituted election petition before HC seeking declaration that rejection of his nomination paper was incorrect and inappropriate - HC passed impugned order - Whether HC by order impugned was justified in invalidating election of appellant who was elected as member of Bihar Legislative Assembly in election held on 10-7-2003 -

Held, on perusal of evidence of PW1, respondent and Returning Officer, it was perceptible that said respondent had not filed electoral roll of 1998 which was latest electoral roll as on 1-1-2002 - On the date of scrutiny, respondent was absent - HC, as noticeable, had referred to order of rejection of nomination paper by Returning Officer and opined that none had filed electoral roll of 1-1-2002 and, therefore, nomination paper could not have been rejected - Aforesaid view was resultant of erroneous perception of fact - Ground that was indicated by Returning Officer was that valid electoral roll as on 1-1-2002 had not been filed - It had come in evidence that no electoral roll was prepared on that date and latest electoral roll was that of 1998 - Respondent had not filed same, in fact he had filed electoral roll of 1995 - It was also clear from evidence that at time of scrutiny, he was not present - SC had no scintilla of doubt that HC had fallen into serious error by setting aside election of appellant - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside and treat election of appellant as valid and further direct that appellant should get entire remuneration for period for which he was elected as member of legislative Council - Appeal allowed.
