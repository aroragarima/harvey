Raymond Synthetics Limited And Ors. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 955; (1992) 2 SCC 255; AIR 1992 SC 847; [1992] 73 Comp Cas 762; 1992 (1) CompLJ 209; JT 1992 (1) SC 463; 1992(1) SCALE 264; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 481

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Articles Of Association, Capital Issue

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act ,1956, ss. 73(2A), 73(3) and 73(4) - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956,s.22 - Constitution , art. 226 - Securities Contracts (Regulation) Act, 1956 - Excess subscription for shares - Company is liable to pay interest due to default in repayment of excess money received - The queation arise - When does a company become liable to pay interest u/s. 73(2A) of 1956 Act - Either on the date of the allotment, or on the expiry of 10 weeks from the date of closing of the subscription lists, referred to in s. 73(1-A), or, as contended by company, on the expiry of the period mentioned in the prospectus - Held, the liability of a company to repay the excess money u/s. 73(2A) arises on the expiry of 10 weeks from the date of the closing of the subscription lists, and the interest begins to accrue thereon at the end of 8 days therefrom - Neither the date of allotment nor the date specified in the prospectus is relevant - Order accordingly.
