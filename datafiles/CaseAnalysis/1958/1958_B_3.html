B. N. Srikantiah and Others v State of Mysore

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 43; AIR 1958 SC 672; 1958 CRLJ 1251; 1958 SCJ 918; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 496

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal- Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 300, 34, 31 and 149 Criminal Procedure Code,1973, ss. 232(1) and 535 - Accused charged with being member of an unlawful assembly - Common object to murder of deceased - (A)Whether on basis of injuries caused on body, intention of accused can be concluded? - Held, where there were 24 injuries on person of deceased and out of them 21 were incised and they were either on his head or neck or shoulders and on forearms - That all parts of body on which injuries were caused, except perhaps last, were vital parts of body and accused who caused those injuries with deadly weapons must be fixed with intention of causing such bodily injury or injuries as would fall within s. 300 of IPC - (B) Whether intention to cause death or inflict injury is a question of fact or law u/s. 34 of IPC?- Further held, that intention is a question of fact which is to be gathered from acts of parties u/s. 34 of IPC - (C) Whether on omission of mentioning s. 34 of IPC in charge, effect case under CrPC,1898?- Further held, that object of a charge is to warn accused person of case he is to answer - Omission of mention s. 34 of in charge will give no effect to case unless prejudice is shown to have resulted in consequence thereof u/ss 232(1) and 535 of CrPC - (D) Whether s. 34 of IPC is creates a substantive offence?- Further held, that s. 34 is only a rule of evidence and does not create a substantive offence - It means, that if two or more persons intentionally do a thing jointly it is just same as if each of them had done individually (E) Whether appellants have been rightly convicted by High Court? -In case of imperfection of a charge if prejudice is shown a conviction of an accused would be insupportable - In present case common intention of appellants is indicated by their conduct, ferocity of attack, weapon used, sites of injuries and their nature and there was preconcert as shown by evidence of PWs 10 and 11- Further held, appellants have been rightly convicted of murder as sharers in offence - Appeal dismissed.
