Samee Khan v Bindu Khan

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 862; (1998) 7 SCC 59; AIR 1998 SC 2765; 1999 (1) AWC 18; 1998 (3) CLT 187; JT 1998 (6) SC 163; 1998 (2) KLT 48; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 37; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 110; 1998 (89) RD 761; 1998(5) SCALE 40; 1998 (7) SLT 219; 1998 (Supp) RD 519; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 244; 1998 (7) Supreme 85

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Breach Of Injunction, Attachment Of Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Procedure, 1908, O. 39, r. 2 A - Breach of injunction - Court is empowered either order detention of disobeying party or attach his property - Whether both steps can be resorted to or one of them alone need be chosen? - Held, both steps can be resorted to or one of them alone need be chosen subject to facts in each case - Petition dismissed.
