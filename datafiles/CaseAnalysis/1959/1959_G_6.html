Om Prabha Jain v Gian Chand and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 245; AIR 1959 SC 837; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 516

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dismissed

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 90(3), 98, 116 A and 117 - Meaning of Trial as per Act of 1951 - Remission of amount with petition in whose favour the respondent, Gian Chand, filed an election petition for a declaration that appellant's election was void - When case was ready for argument, appellant made an application to Tribunal for an order dismissing petition u/s. 90(3) of Representation of People Act, 1951, which is later set out, on ground that s. 117 of that Act had not been compiled with - (A) Whether word 'trial' as per Representation of People Act,1951 means that stage of trial where evidence is tendered and arguments are addressed? - s. 90(3) of Act of 1951 brings to an end proceedings arising out of a petition, after it is made, nothing more remains for Election Tribunal to try or do in respect of that petition - Held, word 'trial' as used in Act of 1951 clearly means entire proceeding before a Tribunal from reference to it by Election Commission to conclusion - There is therefore no reason to give it a restricted meaning in s. 98 - (B) Whether s. 117 of Representation of People Act,1951 complied with in instant case? - Further held, that as form of receipt contains no other heading for indicating person in whose favour money was paid and as it was paid in favour of somebody, that made it perfectly clear that words 'on whose behalf meant in whose favour' - Money was thus properly deposited 'in favour of' Secy. and was thus in full compliance with s. 117 - Appeal dismissed.
