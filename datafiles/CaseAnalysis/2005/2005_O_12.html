Harbans v Om Prakash and others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 730; (2006) 1 SCC 129; AIR 2006 SC 686; 2006 (1) AWC 312; JT 2005 (9) SC 625; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 54; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 841; 2005 (4) RLW 2923; 2005(9) SCALE 288; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 141

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Clog

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 60 - Mortgage - Suit by appellant/plaintiff seeking declaration to effect that he had become owner in possession to extent of 1/2 share suit property, on ground of foreclosure since limitation for redemption of land had expired - Held, for 'once a mortgage always a mortgage' and therefore always redeemable - Factual position as noticed by Courts below, and legal principles indicated inevitable result of this appeal is dismissal - Appeal dismissed.
