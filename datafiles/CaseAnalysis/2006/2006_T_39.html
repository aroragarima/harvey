Tarai Food Limited v Commissioner of Central Excise, Meerut-II

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 665; (2007) 12 SCC 721; 2006 (198) E.L.T. 323; 2006 (8) S.T.R. 442

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Intellectual Property; Trade

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Standard Weights and Measures (Packets Commodities) Act, 1977 - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Whether Trench Fries' should be classified under sub-headings 2001.10 or 2001.90 of Chap. 20 of Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985? - There is a value attached to brand name, a value which has been recognized in tariff entry by providing for levy of excise duty on goods bearing a brand name - It may be that appellant had deliberately omitted brand name in selling 'French Fries' to avail of nil rate of tariff - This cannot detract from consequences which would follow in law - Held, if assesse opts not to take advantage of brand name in its trade, it could at least have benefit of rate of duty applicable to unbranded product - Appeals allowed.
