Shri Sudarshanacharaya v Shri Purushottamacharya & Anr

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 277; (2012) 9 SCC 241; AIR 2012 SC 3854; 2012 CRLJ 4559; JT 2012 (8) SC 632; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2013 (1) RLW 679; 2012(8) SCALE 450

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 244 and 245 (1) - Misappropriation - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Denied - Challenged - Complainant filed a complaint against the appellant alleging misappropriation of the trust's silver and gold articles, jewellery and other articles - Trial Court discharged the appellant u/s. 245 (1) of the CrPC on the ground that the complainant was absent on the date fixed for production of evidence - Complainant filed appeal against said order before Addl. Sessions Judge - Appellant filed two application before Addl. Sessions Judge seeking opportunity to produce evidence u/s. 244 of the CrPC and also requested to take further action only after recording evidence - Addl. Sessions Judge set aside the discharge order and remanded the matter to the trial court with a direction to provide adequate opportunity to the complainant to produce evidence - HC dismissed appeal filed against said order - Appellant again preferred a discharge application stating that 'X' who had executed power of attorney in favour of the complainant had died and therefore, all actions taken by the complainant have become null and void - Trial court dismissed said application on the ground that the appellant was a clever person and he was willfully delaying the case - Appellant filed appeal before HC for quashing of the complaint - HC refused to quash the complaint on the ground that there was no ground for doing so - HC expedited the proceedings and directed that the case will be heard on day-to-day basis - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, pertinently, the trial court's order in which the conduct of the appellant has been severely criticized has been confirmed by the HC - Prima facie whenever the case was about to be heard, the appellant tried to put a spoke by filing an application for discharge - The Trial Court and the HC had directed that the case should proceed expeditiously or on day-to-day basis - Even by the impugned order, the HC has directed that the case shall commence on day-to-day basis - Appellant has challenged the said order - Had the appellant not raised repeated challenges, the case would have proceeded and perhaps got over by now - Prima facie conduct of the appellant appears to be bad - Hence, allowing the proceedings to continue would be an abuse of the process of the court - Trial court was directed to take-up the case for final disposal and dispose it of within a period of 4 months from that date - Appeal dismissed.
