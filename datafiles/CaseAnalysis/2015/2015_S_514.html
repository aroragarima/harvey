Bivash Chandra Debnath @ Bivash D. and others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 272; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2020; 2015 (2) CalLT(SC) 1; 2015 (2) JCC 1166; JT 2015 (4) SC 522; 2015 (2) MLJ(Crl) 289; 2015(5) SCALE 307; [2015] 3 S.C.R. 906

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sudden Fight

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 149, 302, 300, 304 - Culpable homicide - On land belonging to PW1 and his brother-in-law, appellants along with others started digging for constructing irrigation channel and when the same was objected by brother-in-law of PW1, appellants along with 27 others, assaulted them causing injuries, of which one succumbed to death - Charges were framed against 30 accused including appellants and Trial Court convicted appellants for murder of deceased and acquitted others and appeal against the same was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal by three appellants - Whether conviction of appellants u/s. 302 read with s. 149 of IPC could be upheld.

Held, there were four eye witnesses who have deposed about role of appellants in commission of crime and eye witnesses, PWs 2,3,4 corroborated oral testimony of PW1 and even though eye witnesses are related to PW1, yet, merely for reason that they were related, their testimony could not be disbelieved and more so, when their presence with appellants at the spot appears to be natural. Further, said eye witnesses have no personal enmity with appellants. Furthermore, only for reason that 27 others got acquitted as charge could not be proved against them beyond reasonable doubt, it could not be said that persons who committed the crime were only three in number or that the assembly was of three persons only. There is ample evidence on record to suggest that the appellants whose role is specifically proved on the record, were accompanies with others. Even otherwise, since there is sufficient evidence proving the role of appellants that they caused injuries, to deceased which resulted in his death, even without aid of s. 149 of IPC, appellants are liable to be held guilty for causing death of deceased with common intention with aid of s. 34 of IPC. However, as there was sudden fight between appellants and the deceased who was accompanied by PWs 1,2,3,4 and further that there was no premeditation on part of appellants to commit the murder, and have not acted in 'unusual manner', their acts are covered under Exception 4 to s. 300 of IPC and offence committed by appellants is not punishable u/s.302 of IPC but u/s. 304 Part I of IPC. Hence, conviction and sentence recorded u/s. 302 read with s. 149 of IPC is set aside and all three appellants were convicted u/s. 304 Part I read with s. 34 of IPC. Appeal partly allowed.
