Commissioner of Income Tax, Ahmedabad v A. Abdul Rahim and Company, Baroda

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 233; AIR 1965 SC 1703; [1965] 55 ITR 651; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6B), 26A and 59 - Indian Partnership Act, s. 4 - Whether the ITO can refuse to register a genuine partnership entered into between more than two persons on the ground that one of them is only a benamidar for another? - The finding given by the tribunal that the instrument of partnership was genuine - The individual shares of the partners as shown in the instrument of partnership have been specified in the application - Held, ITO can only reject the the registration u/s. 26A if he comes to the conclusion that the partnership is not genuine or the instrument of partnership does not specify correctly the individual shares of the partners - Appeal dismissed.
