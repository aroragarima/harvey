Antonetto John D'souza @ Johnny D'souza v Aldila Braganza

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 417; AIR 2014 SC 3559; 2014 (106) ALR 213; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 519; 2014(8) SCALE 288; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 88; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 170

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Mamlatdars' Courts Act, 1906

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Easemetry right - Denied - Sustainability - Appeal was directed against the judgement passed by HC in Writ Petition No.622 of 2013 - By the impugned judgment, HC quashed the order passed by the Mamlatdar, Bardez and order passed by the Additional Collector-II, North Goad and remitted the matter to Mamlatdar, Bardez to decide whether he had jurisdiction/powers to re-open the proceedings in question and to pass appropriate orders - Appellant/respondent contended that in terms of the order dt. 27-4-2009 of the Panchayat, access was provided through property for the benefit of appellant and other residents of the locality after the NOC of the owner of the property was taken, the only issue that was being considered by HC was that of alternative access - Grievance of appellant was that at some points the minimum access of 1.5 metres was not available was also assured to be made available by the Panchayat by removing the trees - Therefore, the direction of the HC to reopen the entire issue was uncalled for -

Held, HC considering the fact that disputed question of fact was involved in the case by order dt. 10-5-2011 observed that it was not for HC to reconsider the matter in the writ petition which had already been disposed of - However, in case the basis on which the petition was disposed of was not found at loco as sought by appellant such grievance would have to be raised by the appellant before the Mamlatdar in accordance with law - Therefore, without going into the correctness of the contentions of the parties in respect of the alternative access, the respondent was given liberty to approach the Mamlatdar, with regard to her claim of access to her property, who was asked to decide the same after hearing the concerned parties in accordance with law - No direction was issued by HC to reopen the matter - HC had also not directed the Mamlatdar, Bardez to consider the question as to whether he had jurisdiction/powers to reopen the proceedings - Such being the position, it was not open for HC in a subsequent writ petition to pass any order enlarging the order and direction issued by HC in the earlier Writ Petition No.422/2008 - At best, HC could have asked the Village Panchayat, Calagute to issue completion certificate, if the same had not been issued pursuant to the direction of the HC dt. 4-5-2009 in Writ Petition No.422/2008 - It was only after issuance of the completion certificate, the respondent could have decided whether she was satisfied with such completion certificate or not - Appellant having accepted and given undertaking to withdraw the application before the Mamlatdar Court there was no question of remitting the matter to the Mamlatdar - For the reason SC set aside the judgment and order dt. 18-11-2013 with liberty to respondent to move before the Court of competent jurisdiction, if completion certificate was not issued by the Village Panchayat in terms of the order passed in Writ Petition No.422/2008 or if the respondent did not satisfy with such completion certificate - Appeal allowed.
