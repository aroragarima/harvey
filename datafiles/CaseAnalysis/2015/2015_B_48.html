Jitendra Vora v Bhavana Y. Shah and another

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 642; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 4081; 2015 (6) Bom.C.R. 481; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 35; [2015] 193 Comp Cas 86; 2016 (2) CompLJ 519; 2015 CRLJ 4764; 2015 (1) JCC (NI) 297; JT 2015 (8) SC 310; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3466; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 550; 2015(9) SCALE 767; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 219

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques, Attorney, Cheque, Liability, Legality

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 378(4) - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141 - Dishonour of cheques - Liability - Legality - Appellant (complainant) filed a complaint for alleged offence u/s. 138 of NI Act against respondents (accused), who were proprietor and Attorney holder of the Firm 'E' and 'A' - The drawer of the cheque was 'E' - Trial Court acquitted respondent on the ground that appellant did not institute the case against the partnership Firm 'E' of which respondents were proprietor and Attorney holder of the Firm - Aggrieved appellant filed an application for leave to appeal u/s. 378(4) of CrPC before HC, which was dismissed by impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in dismissing the application for leave to appeal against Trial Court judgment.

Held, the liabilities of 'A' were never taken over by 'E', thus reasoning given by HC, is absolutely flawless - Anil Hada v. Indian Acrylic Ltd., 1999 Indlaw SC 853, relied on - Appeal dismissed.
