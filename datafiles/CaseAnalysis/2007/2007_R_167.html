P.T. Munichikkanna Reddy and Others v Revamma and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 411; (2007) 6 SCC 59; AIR 2007 SC 1753; 2007 (4) ALD(SC) 47; 2007 (3) AWC 2411; 2007 (3) CHN 116; JT 2007 (6) SC 86; 2007 (4) KarLJ 274; 2007 (6) MahLJ 336; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 912; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 847; 2007(6) SCALE 95; 2007 (6) SCJ 583; [2007] 5 S.C.R. 491

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Commons Registration Act 1965, Land Registration Act, 2002

Summary: Father of the Respondent No. 1 purchased a portion measuring 1 acre 21 guntas from the owner of land possessing 5 acre and 23 guntas on 11.09.1933 and by two different sale deeds, dated 11.04.1934 and 5.07.1936, the appellants herein purchased 2 acre 15 guntas and 3 acre 8 guntas of land respectively, out of the said plot - Possession by appellants alleged to be disturbed by respondents - Appellants claimed title on the basis of adverse possession - Appeal against the judgement of High Court setting aside the judgement of Trial Court wherein the Trial Court held that appellants have acquired title by adverse possession since they have been in possession of the lands for a period of more than 50 years -Held, adverse possession is a right which comes into play not just because someone loses his right to reclaim the property out of continuous and willful neglect but also on account of possessor's positive intent to dispossess, it is important to take into account before stripping somebody of his lawful title, whether there is an adverse possessor worthy and exhibiting more urgent and genuine desire to dispossess and step into the shoes of the paper-owner of the property - Appeal dismissed.
