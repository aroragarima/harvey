P. Purushottam Reddy And Anr. v Pratap Steels Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 55; (2002) 2 SCC 686; AIR 2002 SC 771; AIR 2002 SCW 417; 2002 (48) ALR 319; 2002 (1) ARC 376; 2002 (3) AWC 2364; 2002 (2) CRC 236; JT 2002 (5) SC 5; 2002 (2) LCD 831; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 99; 2002(1) SCALE 447; 2002 (1) SCJ 458; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 586; 2002 (1) SLT 552; 2002 (1) Supreme 357; 2002 (1) UC 517B

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: An appellate court should be circumspect in ordering a remand when the case is not covered either by Rule 23 or Rule 23A or Rule 25 of the Code of Civil Procedure as an unwarranted order of remand gives the litigation an undeserved lease of life and, therefore, must be avoided.
