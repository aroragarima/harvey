Workmen of Balmer Lawrie and Company, Limited v Lawrie and Company Limited, and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 65; AIR 1964 SC 728; 1964 (8) FLR 112; 1964 (1) LLJ 380; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 344

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Revision of pay - Validity of - Held, considerations which are relevant and material in determining revision of wage scales -The wage-scales are devised and wage-structures constructed as matters of long-term policy, and so, industrial adjudication would naturally be reluctant to interfere with wage-structures without justification or in a light-hearted manner - When a wage-structure is framed, all relevant factors are taken into account and normally it should remain in operation for a fairly long period; but it would be unreasonable to introduce considerations of res judicata as such, because for various reasons which constitute special characteristics of industrial adjudication said technical considerations would be inadmissible - If paying capacity of employer increases or cost of living shows an upward trend, or there are other anomalies, mistakes or errors in award fixing wage-structure, or there has been a rise in wage-structure in comparable industries in region, industrial employees would be justified in making a claim for re-examination of wage-structure and if such a claim is referred for industrial adjudication, adjudicator would not normally be justified in rejecting it solely on ground that enough time has not passed after making of award, or that material change in relevant circumstances had not been proved - What is total capital invested by concern, what is extent of its business what is order of profits made by concern, what are dividends paid, how many employees are employed by concern and what is its standing in industry to which it belongs, these and other matters have to be examined by industrial adjudication in determining question as to whether one concern is comparable with another in matter of fixing wages - Further held, tribunal was in error in refusing to consider merits of appellants' claim in regard to modification and increase in wage-scales - Award set aside - Matter remitted back to tribunal.
