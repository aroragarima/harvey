State of Mysore v C. R. Seshadri and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 23; (1974) SCC (L&S) 264; (1974) 4 SCC 308; AIR 1974 SC 460; 1974 LabIC 362; 1974 (1) LLJ 301; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 87; 1974 SLJ 209; 1974 (1) SLR 407; 1974 UJ 146

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Notional Promotion, Entitlement of, Fixation in gradation list

Summary: Service - Notional promotion - Fixation in gradation list - Entitlement of - Respondent filed a petition before HC to quash the order denying him credit for service while he was Private Secretary to 3 Ministers and sought a direction for payment of such amounts as he would have got had his due inter se seniority and promotion been accorded to him - HC by impugned judgment allowed the petition - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in the absence of positive proof of the relevant service rules, it is hazardous to assume that by efflux of time the respondent would have spiraled up to Dy. Secretary-ship. How could Court speculate in retrospect what the rule was and whether the respondent would have been selected on merit and on the strength of such dubious hypothesis direct retroactive, promotion and back pay?. The frontiers of judicial power cannot be stretched thus for. The proper direction can only be that government will re-consider the case of the respondent afresh for purposes of notional promotion, If the service rule entitles him to promotion on the ground of seniority alone, Government will except for the strongest reason grant the benefit of promotion with effect from the date 'V' became Dy. Secretary. Nothing has been suggested against the respondent in his carrier to disentitle him to promotion and Court have no doubt Govt. will give him his meed. However, if the criterion for promotion is one of seniority-cum-merit, comparative merit may have to be assessed if length of service is equal or an outstanding junior is available for promotion. There is no reason to regard the respondent eligibility on merit for Dy. Secretary-ship to be denied or delayed when 'V' was promoted. Appeal dismissed.
