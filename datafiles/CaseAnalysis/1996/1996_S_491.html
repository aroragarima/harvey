Pawan Kumar v State Of Haryana And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 446; (1996) 4 SCC 17; (1996) SCC (Cr) 583; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 1; AIR 1996 SC 3300; 1996 (2) CLR 2; JT 1996 (5) SC 155; 1996 (2) LLJ 703; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 392; 1996 (4) Supreme 764; 1996 (4) UPLBEC 2761

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Criminal - Constitution, art. 311 - IPC, 1860, s. 294 - Termination of service - Validity - Termination ordered on ground of conviction of servant for offence u/s. 294, of Code, on presumption that conviction u/s. 294 of Code per se indicated moral turpitude - Accused-servant, not confronted with summary of allegations - List of offences prepared after accepting recommendations of Government that those convicted for offences involving moral turpitude should not be taken -Not including offence u/s. 294 of Code - Held, termination set aside - Appeal allowed.
