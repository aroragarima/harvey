Doordarshan Cameramen's Welfare Association (Regd. v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 881; (1990) Supp SCC 262; AIR 1990 SC 1387; 1991 (15) ATC 882; 1990 (60) FLR 797; JT 1990 (2) SC 118; 1990 LabIC 1218; 1990 (1) LLN 803; 1990(1) SCALE 751; 1991 (3) SLR 18; 1990 (1) UJ 679; 1990 (2) UPLBEC 793

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Parity in pay scale - Petition u/art. 32 has been filed by the Union representing the members of the staff working in the camera section of the Doordarshan Kendra - There are three petitioners and they all are claiming for parity in pay scales on the ground that they were performing same or similar duties as their counterparts in the Films Division - Held, all the petitioners are equivalent to their respective counterparts - It would be unreasonable and unjust to discriminate against the said artistes of the Doordarshan in the matter of pay scale - Further held, all the petitioners will also be entitled to the substituted scales of pay and consequential benefits - Respondents are directed to disburse to the petitioners the arrears amounts being the difference in the pay scales - Petition allowed.
