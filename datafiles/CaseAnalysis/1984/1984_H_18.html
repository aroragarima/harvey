Workmen Of Hindustan Steel Ltd And Anr v Hindustan Steel Ltd. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 249; (1984) Supp SCC 554; (1985) SCC (L&S) 260; AIR 1985 SC 251; 1985 (50) FLR 147; 1985 LabIC 534; 1985 (1) LLJ 267; 1985 (1) LLN 789; 1984(2) SCALE 927; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 428; 1985 (1) SLJ 109; 1984 (3) SLR 188; 1985 UJ 281

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Reduction In Rank

Summary: Service - Constitution, arts. 14, 311(2), Proviso, cl. (b)(3) - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 3 - Standing Orders of Hindustan Steel Ltd., Standing Order No. 32 - Public servant - Dismissal - Dispensation of enquiry - Wife of an employee filed report with police against delinquent employee - Whether it is a ground germane to dispensation of enquiry - Grounds held, must be germane - Standing Order confers arbitrary power in the matter of dispensation of enquiry - Need to bring it in tune with philosophy of art. 311(2), Proviso emphasised - Appeal disposed of.
