Supreme Court of India

07 November 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 173; AIR 1968 SC 647; 1970 (1) LLJ 662; 1968 (2) SCJ 236; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 154; 1968 SLR 344

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Orissa Superior Judicial Service (Senior Branch)

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 235 and 236 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Transfer and posting of Judicial officers in Administrative posts - Powers of HC - Conflict between HC and Govt. - Held, while sparing the service of any judicial officer to the govt. it is open to the HC to fix the period during which he may hold any executive post. At the end of that period, the govt. is bound to allow him to go back to his parent dept. unless the HC agrees to spare his services for some more time. The period during which a judicial officer should serve in an executive post must be settled by agreement between the HC and the govt.. If there is no such agreement it is open to the govt. to send him back to his parent dept. at any time it pleases. It is equally open to the HC to recall him whenever 'it thinks fit. Each of the organs of the State has a special role of its own. The Constitution expects all of them to work in harmony in a spirit of service. Hence, the HC would be within its powers in posting Officers as district and sessions judges. However, it would be beyond the powers of the HC to post the Officers as the law secretary, superintendent and legal remembrancer and deputy law secretary. Appeals partly allowed.
