Monotosh Kumar Mitra by Lrs. v Amarendranath Shaw (Dead) and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 155; (2000) 2 SCC 672; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 58; 2000 (40) ALR 754; JT 2000 (2) SC 275; 2000 (91) RD 612; 2000(1) SCALE 637; 2000(1) SCALE 1441; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1023; 2000 (4) SLT 535; 2000 (2) Supreme 66

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Final Decree For Sale

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Bengal Money Lenders Act, 1940, s. 34 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 34, r. 2 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 137 - In a mortgage suit, a preliminary decree was passed in favour of appellant on 18-11-1968 - Decree directed payment in four equal installments - First installment was payable on or before 31-3-1969 - Decree further stipulated that in case of default of payment of any one of installments, plaintiff may, subject to provisions of Bengal Money Lenders Act, 1940, apply to Court for a final decree for sale of mortgaged property and on such application being granted - On failure of defendants to pay any installments, appellant served on them notice under aforesaid provision and an application will be made in HC within 30 days for final decree for sale of property - Defendants having still failed to make any payment, an application for passing final decree was filed by appellant in HC on or about 15-2-1973 - Said application was dismissed by Single Judge as time-barred and appeal having been dismissed by DB - Whether such dismissal is legal? - Held, it is evident from s. 34 of Act that in default of payment of any installment, date of such default shall be deemed to be date fixed for payment of whole amount - Whole amount found or declared due under or by a preliminary decree becomes payable and plaintiff becomes entitled to apply for a final decree - A right accrued to appellant to apply for final decree on default having been committed by defendants in payment of amount of first installment - It is a mortgage decree where under preliminary decree, an opportunity is granted to defendants to pay mortgage amount in default whereof plaintiff becomes entitled to apply for a final decree for sale of mortgaged property - That right accrued to plaintiff in this case on 31-3-1969 - Plaintiff cannot extend period of limitation by delaying service of notice on defendant u/s. 34 of Act, which is a precondition for making of an application by plaintiff for passing of final decree - Period of limitation would start running from time right to apply for final decree accrues - It may also be noted that notice u/s. 34 sent by appellant was based on all four defaults committed by defendants and it was not stated that any default was waived or condoned - Relied upon in Nalini Kanto Bhattacharjee v. Mohan Chand Biswas (AIR 1960 Cal 477) decision, application of appellant for passing of final decree was dismissed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
