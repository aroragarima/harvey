Techi Tagi Tara v Rajendra Singh Bhandari and others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 711; 2017 (5) AWC 5232

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environment, Pollution Control Boards, Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, Water (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1974, National Green Tribunal Act, 2010, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Administrative - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Appointment of chairperson - Reconsideration - Validity - Appellants filed instant appeals challenging impugned order of Tribunal whereby Tribunal held that persons appointed to State Pollution Control Boards (SPCBs) did not have necessary expertise or qualifications to be members or chairpersons of such high powered and specialized statutory bodies and did not deserve their appointment or nomination and directed State Govts. to reconsider appointments and laying down guidelines for appointment to SPCBs - Whether exercise of jurisdiction by Tribunal in directing State Govts. to reconsider the appointment of Chairperson and members of SPCBs was valid.

Held, SC appreciate the anxiety of Tribunal to preserve and protect the environment as a part of its statutory functions, however, SC cannot extend those concepts to the extent of enabling Tribunal to consider who should be appointed as a Chairperson or a member of any SPCB or who should not be so appointed. It would have been more appropriate for Tribunal to have required the claimant to approach a constitutional Court for the relief prayed for in original application. To such extent direction given by Tribunal should be set aside as being without jurisdiction. However, some States have implemented the order of Tribunal and removed some members while others have approached SC and obtained an interim stay order. Those officials who were removed pursuant to the order of Tribunal (including appellant) have an independent cause of action and SC leave it open to them to challenge their removal in appropriate and independent proceedings. That was an issue between the removed official and State Govt. the removal was not a public interest issue and SC cannot reverse the situation. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Administrative - Recruitment - Guidelines framed - Applicability - Whether laying down guidelines for appointment of Chairperson and members of SPCBs was valid.

Held, each State Govt. to consider and decide what was the right thing to do under the circumstances should an unqualified or inexperienced person be appointed or should SPCB be a representative but expert body. Therefore, in matters relating to the protection and preservation of environment (through appointment of officials to SPCBs) Central Govt. as well as State Govts. have to walk the extra mile. There should be considerable deliberation before an appointment was made and only the best should be appointed to SPCB. It was necessary in that regard for Executive to consider and frame appropriate rules for appointment of such persons who would add lustre and value to SPCB. Additionally, various committees have given sufficient guidelines for appointment of Chairperson and members of SPCBs. SC direct Executive in all States to frame appropriate guidelines or recruitment rules within 6 months, considering the institutional requirements of SPCBs and the law laid down by statute, SC and as per the reports of various committees and authorities and ensure that suitable professionals and experts were appointed to SPCBs. Any damage to environment could be permanent and irreversible or at least long-lasting. Appeals disposed of.
