Eliamma and Another v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 127; (2009) 11 SCC 42; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 1227; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 202; JT 2009 (2) SC 358; 2009 (3) KarLJ 443; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 47; 2009(2) SCALE 484; [2009] 3 S.C.R. 135

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specially Empowered, Extra Judicial Confession

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 304 Part II, 201 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, evidence of PW 1 has been analysed by both the trial court and the High Court and have been found to be cogent and credible - Effect, relevance and applicability of s. 360 Cr.P.C. have not been considered by the courts below - Conviction upheld - Remitted the matter to the trial court for limited purpose for deciding whether the benefit under s. 360 Cr.P.C. can be extended to the appellants - Appeal partly allowed.
