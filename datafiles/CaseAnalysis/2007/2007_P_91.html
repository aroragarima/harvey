Tejshree Ghag v Prakash Parashuram Patil and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 563; (2007) 6 SCC 220; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 451; AIR 2007 SC 2141; 2007(5) ALL MR 900; 2007 (68) ALR 654; 2007 (5) Bom.C.R. 199; 2007(8) SCALE 165; 2007 (4) SCJ 802; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 214; 2007 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion, Reduction In Rank, Loss Of Seniority

Summary: Service - Orders for transfer of respondents on the basis of provisional seniority - High Court held that transfer of persons holding substantive posts carrying lower time scale of pay would be violative of art. 14, 16 and 311 of the Constitution of India - Appeal against - Held, where executive order results in civil consequences, principles of natural justice are required to be complied with prior thereto - It is not a case where an order of transfer was passed by way of change of place of employment within an organization simpliciter - If orders of transfer substantially affect the status of an employee, the same would be violative of the conditions of service and, thus, illegal - Transfers must be made to an equivalent post - Orders of transfer impugned before the Tribunal in any event could not have been passed without complying with the principles of natural justice - Appeal dismissed.
