Harbans Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Amarendra Nath Sen, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 68; (1982) 2 SCC 101; (1982) SCC (Cr) 361; AIR 1982 SC 849; 1982 CRLJ 795; 1982 CrLR(SC) 71; 1982 MLJ 409; 1982(1) SCALE 95; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 235; 1982 UJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 53 and 302 - Petition for commuting death sentence - Appellant involved in a multiple murder case along with two other - One was executed while other was entitled to commutation - Petitioner was also supposed to be executed on same day but he filed a petition u/art. 32 and obtained stay of execution - Mercy petition once rejected by president - Held, since they were convicted were under a commom judgement, it would be unjust to confirm death sentence imposed upon petitioner, hence, it would be better to refer it back to president - Order accordingly.
