Gurbachan Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 181; AIR 1957 SC 623; 1957 CRLJ 1009

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 161, 162, 154, 173, 207-A, 251 (A), 286 and 374 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Arms Act,s. 19 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Whether statements of witnesses u/s. 161 CrPC not supplied to accused for defence in trial under Arms Act affects rights and trial? - Scope of s.161 CrPC discussed - According to amendment it is open to prosecution with permission of Court to use such a statement in order to contradict a witness in manner provided by s.145 of Evidence Act though before amendment, prosecution could not make use of any such statement to contradict a witness - After amendment there is a provision to effect that copies should be given earlier - Held, no provision in CrPC that copies of statements recorded u/s. 161 in a connected case should be made available to defence though there is nothing prohibiting it - In chap. XXIII CrPC procedure is laid down for trial to close of cases for prosecution and defence - No where in this Chap. any direction, or rule to effect that in a Session trial defence is to be supplied with copies of statements taken u/s. 161 CrPC - Reason, is that such statements should have been given u/s. 207-A CrPC in initial stage of inquiry before Committing Court - No non-observance of a mandatory rule guiding conduct of trial in Sessions Court - No prejudice has been caused to accused by his not having been supplied with statements of witnesses recorded by police during investigation of Arms Act case, when Sessions trial was going on - Appeal dismissed.
