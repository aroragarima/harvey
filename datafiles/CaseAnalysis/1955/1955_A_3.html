A.V. D'COSTA v B. C. Patel and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, B. Jagannadhadas

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 15; AIR 1955 SC 412; 1955 (1) LLJ 363; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 213; 1955 SCJ 363; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 1353

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 39(d) - Payment of Wages Act, 1936, ss. (2) 15, 3 - Casual labourer - Daily wages - Sustainability - Respondent no. 2 was employee of Central Railway represented by appellant who had been nominated by Railway Administration as responsible for payment of wages u/s. 3 of the Act - Respondent no. 2 as carpenter on received daily wages and had been treated as daily rated casual labourer - Respondent No. 2 continued receiving wages without any demur, and granting receipts for wages received - Application was made by official of Registered Trade Union u/s. 15(2) of the Act against G.I.P. Ry. administration through its Divisional Engineer - Respondent no. 2 alleged that his wages due in respect of six months had not been paid or had been subjected to illegal deductions - Claim for compensation was made - Appellant resisted claim - Authority decided that position of respondent no. 2 was not that of casual labourer but that of 'temporary employee' and that therefore he was entitled to be on scale of Rs. 55150 plus allowances admissible - Authority allowed respondent no. 2 application - Against said orders of Authority appellant moved HC by application u/art. 226 of Constitution for quashing Authority's orders - HC dismissed application - Appellant preferred appeal - DB by its order dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether having regard to relevant provisions of the Act respondent no. 1 was competent to pass orders it did which orders had been upheld by HC - Held, order of Tribunal proceeded on view that applicant was presently entitled to be treated as monthly-rated temporary employee and not as daily-rated casual labourer by virtue of directions of Railway Board for implementation of scheme of classification and that therefore he was entitled to appropriate higher wage - Court had not been shown any material to indicate that this higher classification of applicant depended not on mere application, of classification scheme and rules there under, to him but upon any determination by departmental higher authority - If decision of Tribunal in this behalf was wrong, appropriate remedy for Railway Authority was by way of appeal u/s. 17 of the Act - Since finding of Tribunal in this case involved case of as many as six persons and net additional amount ordered was sum its finding was appealable u/s. 17 of the Act - Whether or not employee was entitled to wages of higher category than what he was till then drawing would depend entirely on scope of rules with reference to which he was entitled to become one in higher category and it could not be assumed priori that such claim was claim to 'prospective wages' - Hence, there was no sufficient reason to reverse judgment of HC - Appeal allowed.
