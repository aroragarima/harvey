Messrs Hajee S.V.M. Mohamed Jamaludeen Bros. and v The Government Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

24 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1039; (1997) 3 SCC 466; AIR 1997 SC 1368; 1997 (1) ARBLR 493; 1997 (3) AWC 1636; 1997 (2) CLT 133; JT 1997 (3) SC 21; 1997(2) SCALE 304; 1997 (3) SCJ 14; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 413; 1997 (2) Supreme 710

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Government Grants Act, 1895, ss. 2 and 3 - Easements Act, 1882 - (A) Whether lease of land granted by Government covered by ss. 2 and 3 - (B) Whether licence coupled with interest created by instrument within ambit of grant - Held, (A)it is covered by protection envisaged in ss. 2 and 3 - (B) without a grant in the general sense no licence can be created - Word grant used in the Act of 1895 can, therefore envelop within it everything granted by the Government to any person - Licence to collect chank shells from some coastalsites which the licencee obtained from Government by virtue of agreement would undoubtedly fall within the ambit of grant envisaged in the Act of 1895 -Appeal dismissed.
