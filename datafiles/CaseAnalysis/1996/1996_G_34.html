J.S. Parihar v Ganpat Duggar and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2533; (1996) 6 SCC 291; (1996) SCC (L&S) 1422; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 710; AIR 1997 SC 113; JT 1996 (9) SC 608; 1997 (1) LLN 66; 1996(7) SCALE 351; 1997 (1) SLJ 236; 1996 (6) SLR 723; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 110; 1996 (6) Supreme 133

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocate & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 12, 19 and 39 - High Court quashed the seniority list prepared by the Govt. as unconstitutional and was quashed and directed to prepare list afresh - Non-compliance of the order of Single Judge - Contempt initiated u/s. 12 - Single Judge found that respondents had not willfully disobeyed the orders and had given directions - State filed appeal u/s. 19 against the directions - Division Bench held that appeal u/s. 19 is not maintainable - Whether it is legally valid? - Held, appeal would lay u/s. 19 when an order in exercise of jurisdiction of HC punishing contemnor has been passed - In the instant case, there is no order punishing respondent for violation of orders of HC - Appeal u/s. 19 would not lie - Appeal dismissed.
