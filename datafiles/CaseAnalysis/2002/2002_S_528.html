Ravinder Singh @ Bittu v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 291; (2002) 9 SCC 55; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1062; AIR 2002 SC 2241; 2002 (2) AllCriC 56; 2002 (2) AllCriR 1488; 2002 (2) CCR 164; 2002 (2) Crimes 380; 2002 (5) CRJ 644; 2002 CRLJ 2957; 2002 (2) JCC 1059; JT 2002 (4) SC 470; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 610; 2002(4) SCALE 240; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 622; 2002 (3) SLT 411; 2002 (3) Supreme 606; 2002 (2) UC 385B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Robbery and bomb blast

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s.25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 306(4)(a) - Explosives Substances Act, 1984, s. 3 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24, 25, 26, 28, 30 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120, 302, 307, 313, 34, 392 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 15, 19, 3, 4 - Robbery and bomb blast - Conviction - Confessional statement - Appellant and co-accused were convicted for offence u/ss. 3 and 4 of 1987 Act r/w ss. 120, 302, 392, 307 with s. 34 of IPC and s. 3 of 1984 Act and s. 25 of 1959 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that as there are inherent contradictions in confessional statement of appellant when compared with confessional statement made by co-accused, Designated Court committed serious illegality in convicting appellant by relying upon his confessional statement.

Held, the confession made by the appellant is voluntary and truthful and was recorded, by due observance of all the safeguards provided u/s. 15 of 1987 Act and the appellant could be convicted solely on the basis of his confession. The confessional statement made by the appellant is not required to be examined with reference to the confessional statement made by co-accused, and, therefore, there is no question of contradictions between the two confessions. PW3 has given a detailed version of the manner in which the appellant was inserting his hand in the cloth bag which had in it the bomb with a view to fix the wire. PW3 also identified the appellant in the test identification parade being the person who was sitting in the train opposite her. The version given by the appellant in his confessional statement finds sufficient corroboration from the testimony of PW3. The conviction of the appellant does not deserve to be disturbed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Judicial confessional made are usually retracted but retracted confessions are good confessions if held to have been made voluntarily and in accordance with the provisions of law.
