State of Haryana v Mukesh Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 686; (2011) 10 SCC 404; AIR 2012 SC 559; 2012 (2) ALD(SC) 101; 2012 (90) ALR 216; 2012 (1) ALT 1; 2012 (1) AWC 997; 2011 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3240; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 768; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 17; 2012 (115) RD 349; 2012 (2) RLW 1280; 2011(11) SCALE 266; [2011] 14 S.C.R. 211

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Limitation Act, 1980

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Ownership - Adverse possession - Validity - Appellant/State had filed a Civil Suit through Superintendent of Police, seeking a relief of declaration to effect that it had acquired rights of ownership by way of adverse possession over land situated in revenue estate - Trial Court decided Issues in favour of defendants holding that appellanthaving no right or title in suit property had neither locus standi nor cause of action to file instant suit - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant petition - Whether suit filed by state seeking right of ownership by adverse possession was sustainable - Held, State filed a totally frivolous petition and unnecessarily wasted time of SC and demonstrating its evil design of grabbing properties of lawful owners in a clandestine manner - Petition dismissed.
