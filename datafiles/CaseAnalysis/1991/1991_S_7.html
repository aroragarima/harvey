S. Sai Reddy v S. Narayana Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 465; (1991) 3 SCC 647; AIR 1991 SCW 488

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Unmarried

Summary: Family & Personal - Women & Children - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 29 A - Appellant filed suit for partition of ancestral joint family property claiming his share in it - Subordinate Judge accepted appellant's claim and passed preliminary decree - Defendants preferred appeal High Court confirmed findings of trial court with direction of maintenance and marriage of respondents 2 to 5 shall be borne equally by appellant - During pendency of proceedings in trial court before final decree was passed 1956 act was amended and respondents 2 to 5 became entitled in joint family property - Application of respondents for share in property was rejected by trial court - On appeal HC declared that in view of newly added s. 29-A of 1956 Act, daughters were entitled to share in joint family property - Whether HC committed an error in directing trial court to allot shares to unmarried daughters? - Preliminary decree which merely declares shares which are themselves liable to change does not bring about any irreversible situation - Held, unless a partition of property is effected by metes and bounds, daughters cannot be deprived of benefits conferred by act - HC has rightly held that since final decree had not been passed and property had not been divided by metes and bounds, cl (iv) to s. 29-A was not attracted in present case - Appeal dismissed.
