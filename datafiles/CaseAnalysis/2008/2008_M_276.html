Mangoo @ Mangal Singh v State Of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2542; (2008) 8 SCC 283; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 487; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1102; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 636; 2008(10) SCALE 493; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 300, 302, 304 - Murder - Arrest - Maintainability - Appellant and co- accused were alleged to have committed offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w s.149 IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant and all others - Appellant and others preferred appeal before HC which was dismissed - Except appellant all other preferred SLP which were allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether stand taken by HC was maintainable - Held, Court relied upon Harbans Singh v. State of UP. & Ors. 1982 Indlaw SC 68 and said that it would be a sheer travesty of justice, if for very same offence one had to pay extreme penalty of death whereas other accused was to get life-imprisonment - Further, by exercising suo motu power Court had granted benefit of acquittal, conviction for lesser offence or reduction of sentence - Thus, conviction of appellant was altered to s. 304 IPC - Appeal allowed.
