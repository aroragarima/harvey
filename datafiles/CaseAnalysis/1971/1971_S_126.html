State Of Madhya Pradesh And Another v Dadabhoy's New Chirimiri Ponri Hill Colliery

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.M. Sikri, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 295; (1972) 1 SCC 298; AIR 1972 SC 614; [1972] 2 S.C.R. 609; 1972 UJ 410

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Amendment Act, 1958

Summary: Mines and Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948, s. 30A - (A) Notification u/s. 30A applying s. 9(1) subject to modification that lessees under pre-1949 leases were to pay royalty - Construction of : Royalty at the rate provided under their leases or at 21/2% whichever was higher - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - Where two instructions of a provision are possible - Rules of construction - Held, what the notification meant was that instead of the rate flowing from the application of s. 9 (1) and sch.2, a modified rate should be applied, that is, 'in lieu of the rate of royalty' specified in the sch. 2, royalty at the agreed rate should be charged if it was lower than 5%, or at 21/2 % minimum, whichever was higher - Held, one which sustains its validity must be preferred and a Court construing a provision of law must presume that the intention of the authority making it was not to exceed its power and to enact it validly - Appeal dismissed.
