Messrs Allen Berry and Company Private Limited v Union of India, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 485; (1971) 1 SCC 295; AIR 1971 SC 696; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 282

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 30 - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 77 - Agreement - Validity - Award was in respect of certain disputes between appellant company and UOI in matter of disposals of United States surplus war materials left by Govt. of U.S.A. at end of last World War - These surplus materials were sold to appellant company by Director-General, Disposals through correspondence and sale-notes - These contracts of sale were subject to General Conditions of Contract - Disputes having arisen parties were referred to two arbitrators nominated by parties, and to Umpire - At end of arbitration, umpire disallowed all claims made by appellant company except one for which he was awarded Rs. 6,94,000/- and held, in respect of counter-claims filed by Govt. of India, that appellant company was liable to pay to Govt. Rs. 34,70,226.50 - Award was challenged and was declined to be set aside however, District Court held that award suffered from error apparent on face in respect of appellant's claim and further held that counter-claims made by Govt. were not covered by reference, and consequently umpire had no jurisdiction -Declining to set aside award District Court remitted it for reconsideration and readjustment of amount of costs - Dissatisfied appellant company filed appeal before HC and UOI filed cross-objections - HC dismissed both appeal and cross-objections and upheld judgment of District Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, error in present award could not be said to be error apparent on face of award entitling court to consider various documents placed in evidence before Umpire but not incorporated in award so as to form part of it and then to make search if they had been misconstrued - Award ,made it clear that he took into account entire evidence and then came to conclude that it did not assist company in contention as to scope of sales - Court further held that it was not correct to say that umpire could award only that amount which Govt. had actually paid and that umpire should have taken account from Govt. - It was never case of appellant company that Govt. had claimed ground rent higher than compensation it was liable to pay - Hence, Court was not prepared to say that discretion exercised by Umpire in matter of costs was exercised in breach of any legal provision or unreasonably which could justify Court's intervention - Appeal dismissed.
