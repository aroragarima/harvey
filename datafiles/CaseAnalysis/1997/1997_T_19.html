Godhra Electricity Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

03 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3263; (1997) 4 SCC 530; AIR 1997 SC 2350; 1997 (139) CTR 564; [1997] 224 ITR 746; JT 1997 (4) SC 493; 1997(3) SCALE 409; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 539; 1997 (3) Supreme 642; [1997] 91 TAXMAN 351; 1997 (2) TLR 642

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 5 and 145 - Question regarding accrual of income of assessee - Whether Tribunal was right in law in holding that amount of which had accrued to assessee during previous year could not be included in computation of total income of assessee? - Held, it cannot be held that there was real accrual of income to assessee in respect of enhanced charges for supply of electricity which were added by ITO while passing assessment orders in respect of assessment years under consideration - Tribunal rightly held that claim at increased rates as made by assessee on basis of which necessary entries were made represented only hypothetical income and impugned amounts as brought to tax by ITO did not represent income which had really accrued to assessee during relevant previous years - Appeal allowed.
