U. P. S. R. T. C. v Mamta and others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 133; (2016) 4 SCC 172; 2016 (1) ACC 707; 2016 ACJ 699; AIR 2016 SC 948; 2016 (2) ALD(SC) 136; 2016 (116) ALR 562; JT 2016 (2) SC 364; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1566; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 98; 2016 (3) RLW 2541; 2016(2) SCALE 457; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 71; 2016 (2) WLN(SC) 178

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Contributory Negligence, Claim Petition, Contributory, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 20, r. 4(2), O. 41, r. 31 - Failure to consider facts and issues raised - Compensation awarded - Justification - Respondent claimant filed petition against appellant Corporation before Tribunal claiming compensation on grounds that on day on incident, deceased died to negligence of appellant's bus as it was coming in wrong direction in high speed - Tribunal, vide award dt. 18-2-2014, partly allowed claim petition and awarded total sum of Rs.24,73,252/- along with interest @ 6% p.a. from date of filing till its realization to respondents - Further, HC vide impugned order dismissed appeal filed by appellant and upheld order passed by Tribunal - Appellant contended that HC adverting to all factual details and grounds raised in appeal, disposed of appeal in cryptic manner - Whether impugned order passed by HC was in accordance with law.

Held, HC had neither set out facts of case of parties, nor dealt with any of submissions urged, nor took note of grounds raised by appellant and nor made any attempt to appreciate evidence in light of settled legal principles applicable to issues arising in case to find out as to whether award of Tribunal is legally sustainable or not. SC held that as first appellate Court, it was duty of HC to have decided appeal keeping in view powers conferred on it by statute. Further, impugned judgment does not satisfy requirements of O. 20, r. 4(2) of CPC read with O.41, r. 31 of CPC which requires that judgment shall contain concise statement of case, points for determination, decisions thereon and reasons. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Further, HC would decide appeal strictly in accordance with law on merits after affording opportunity of hearing to both parties. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Judgment of appellate court must reflect that it has taken into consideration facts, issues raised by parties, findings supported by reasons.
