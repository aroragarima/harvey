Â Maharana Shri Jayavantsinhji, v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 128; AIR 1959 SC 547; 1959 (61) BomLR 945; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 911; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 911

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879, Gujarat Taluqdars Act, 1888, Bombay Taluqdari Tenure Abolition Act, 1949

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Land & Property - Revenue - Taluqdars' Act, 1888, ss. 2 and 22 - Whether taluqdars would be liable to pay land revenue in accordance with provisions of Land Revenue Code or not?- Held, considering ss. 2 and 22 of Taluqdars Act, s. 3 of Abolition Act, ss. 117-R, 117-D and 117-C of Bombay Land Revenue Code, 1879 - Entire scheme of Abolition Act was that after passing of that Act, taluqdars became occupants with result that they would be liable to pay land revenue in accordance with provisions of Land Revenue Code notwithstanding declaration made or agreement entered into by Govt. with them in regard to jama payable by them - Sub-s. (2) was only enacted to preserve to them concession till period fixed had expired - Therefore, declaration made by Governor in Council in 1925-26 expired in 1955-56 and appellants became liable to pay entire land revenue according to settlement registers from year 1955-56 - Appeal dismissed.
