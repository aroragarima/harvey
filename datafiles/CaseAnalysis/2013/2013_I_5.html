FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Ibrahim Musa Chauhan @ Baba Chauhan v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

21 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 168; JT 2013 (5) SC 68; 2013(4) SCALE 207

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Explosives Rules, 1983, Bombay serial blasts, Conscious possession of contraband and weapons, Commission terrorists acts, Possession of contraband, Possession of arms, Recovery of contraband, Specific knowledge, Aiding to terrorists, Limited possession of contraband, Disposal of contraband, Hawala business, Rendering of financial assistance, Purchase of weapons

Summary: (A)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201, 212 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5, 6, 15, 2(1)(a)(iii) - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25(1-A), (1-B)(a) - Explosive Substance Act, 1908, s. 4(b) - Explosives Act, 1884, ss. 9-B(1)(b) - Prevention of Damage to Public Property Act, 1984, s. 4 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - Bombay serial blasts - Conscious possession of contraband and weapons - Commission terrorists acts - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused nos. 41, 67, 95, 123, 47, 91, 101, 107, 96, 50, 97 and 122) and several other co-accused persons allegedly committed offences u/ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201, 212 of IPC, u/ss. 3(3)(4), 5, 6, 2(1)(a)(iii) of TADA, u/ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25(1-A), (1-B)(a) of 1959 Act, u/ss. 3, 4(a)(b), 5, 6 of 1908 Act, u/ss. 9B(1)(a)(b)(c) of 1884 Act and u/s. 4 of 1984 Act - Designated Trial Court, under 1987 Act, convicted appellant u/ss. 3(3), 5, 6 of TADA, u/ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25(1-A), (1-B)(a) of 1959 Act, u/s. 4(b) of 1908 Act and u/ss. 9-B(1)(b) of 1884 Act and sentenced him to undergo RI for 10 years - However, appellant was acquitted for offences under IPC - Aggrieved appellant and other co-accused persons filed instant appeals - Appellant contended that confessional statement of appellant and other co-accused were recorded without meeting requirements of s. 15 of TADA and Rules framed thereunder - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-41) was justified - Held, it could not be accepted that a recovery made from an open space or a public place which was accessible to everyone, should not be taken into consideration for any reason - Reasoning behind it, was that, it would be accused alone who would be having knowledge of place, where a thing was hidden - Other persons who had access to place would not be aware of the fact that an accused, after commission of an offence, had concealed contraband material beneath the earth, or in garbage - Only requirements under statutory provisions were that a person should be in possession of some contraband material; person should have knowledge of his possession i.e. conscious possession; it should be in notified area - Once possession was established, burden was on the accused to show that he was not in conscious possession - In instant case, Designated Trial Court concluded that appellant was aware that arms and ammunition which were handled by him were to be used during riots against Hindus - Father of appellant collected a bag of contraband kept with one accused through another, and handed over the same to police - Designated Trial Court held that confessions of co-accused established the role played by appellant in supplying weapons to co-accused (A-117) - Thus, it was clear that fourth person referred to in confession of A-117 was appellant - Trial Court concluded that appellant was in unauthorised possession of AK-56 rifles, magazines, ammunition and hand granades, and that he had distributed a part of the material to A-117, kept hand grenades with himself, and had handed over an AK-56 rifle to another co-accused (dead) - Appellant was in unauthorised possession of weapons in a notified area, and having failed to rebut the presumption i.e. that same were being for the purpose of commission of a terrorist act, appellant was liable to be convicted u/s. 5 of TADA - Thus, appellant was introduced to co-accused (A-117) at the residence of A-117 and that appellant witnessed the handing over of contraband to A-117 and that appellant was in conscious possession of certain contraband items and that recovery of contraband material which was effected upon the making of a disclosure statement by appellant, taken place at a dumping ground for waste - Impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court was based on evidence and justified - Appeals disposed of.

(B)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 302, 307, 326, 324, 427, 435, 436, 201, 212 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5, 6 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25(1-A), (1-B)(a) - Explosives Act, 1884 - Bombay serial blasts - Possession of contraband - Commission terrorists acts - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (Accused no. 67) was justified - Held, appellant (A-67) was in possession of contraband material in an unauthorised manner within notified area and said material being capable of attracting provisions of s. 5 of TADA - Though appellant by committing acts had contravened provisions of 1959 Act and 1884 Act, still his intent behind committing acts being not for helping any terrorist, he could not be held guilty for commission of any offence u/s. 6 of TADA - Recovery of 2 suit cases containing arms and ammunition stood fully proved by conjoint reading of depositions of PWs 506, 282 and 37 - Impugned conviction was justified - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(3) - Possession of arms - Commission terrorists acts - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-95) was justified - Held, from evidence on record appellant had gone to Dubai at the behest of A-65 and, thereafter, to Pakistan for attending training camps and acquired training in handling arms and ammunition and thereafter, returned to India - There was no evidence to show that appellant had committed any offence and participated in any other act on fateful day - Appellant had obtained training for commission of terrorist acts, he could not be acquitted of charges u/s. 3(3) of TADA - Impugned conviction was justified - Appeals disposed of.

(D)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5 - Possession of contraband - Commission terrorists acts - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A- 123) was justified - Held, appellant (A-123) did not make any attempt to rebut the knowledge about nature of arms and ammunition - Thus, conclusion drawn that appellant was in possession of contraband material unauthorisedly within notified area of Bombay and committed offence u/s. 5 of TADA and committed perpetratory act for commission of terrorist acts and was guilty u/s. 3(3) of TADA - Involvement of appellant in offences for which charges were found proved against him by Trial Court, stood fully established - Court could not pass lesser punishment than what was prescribed under 1987 Act - Impugned conviction was justified -Appeals disposed of.

(E)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(3) - Recovery of contraband - Specific knowledge - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-47) was justified - Held, merely because contraband was recovered from a public place, i.e. a place accessible to public at large, same did not mean that recovery was to be discarded - Articles were hidden by digging up earth, covering the same up with garbage or other material, which remained in specific knowledge of appellant (A-47) i.e. where and manner in which articles were hidden - Recovery could not be discarded for want of signature of accused on recovery memo - Impugned conviction was justified -Appeals disposed of.

(F)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5, 6 - Possession of contraband - Aiding to terrorists - Conviction Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-91) was justified - Held, considering the manner in which and reason because of which appellant (A-91) had agreed to keep contraband at his house, and fact that same were not for the purpose of aiding a terrorist, appellant was not held guilty for offence u/s. 6 of TADA - Act committed by appellant at behest of A-137, and that same could not be said to have been done for the purpose of furthering the object of a criminal conspiracy - There was no evidence available to establish nexus of appellant with a conspiracy - Impugned conviction was justified - Appeals disposed of.

(G)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 3(3) - Commission of terrorists acts - Conviction Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-96) was justified - Held, appellant (A-96) was simply present in the next room when meeting was held and she was asked to serve tea - Appellant was held to be entitled for benefit of doubt - Appellant was acquitted of her charge u/s. 3(3) of TADA - Impugned conviction was set aside - Appeals disposed of.

(H)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5 - Limited possession of contraband - Disposal of contraband - Conviction Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-50) was justified - Held, being in possession of material for a limited time period, renders him guilty for commission of offence u/s. 5 of TADA - Appellant was also guilty u/s. 201 of IPC, as even though he might not have directly involved in disposal of contraband, the same was disposed of upon his instructions, and for that, he had paid a huge amount - Impugned conviction was justified -Appeals disposed of.

(I)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 2(1)(a)(iii), 3(3), 5, 21(2) - Foreign Exchange and Regulation Act, 1973 - Hawala business - Rendering of financial assistance - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-97) was justified - Held, general principle was that a person involved rendering financial assistance to accused of terrorist/disruptive activities, or could be reasonably suspected in indulging in such activities - Hawala business was done only on the basis of commission by exchanging money among persons and receiving commission - Appellant was working as a carrier or agent, between persons indulging in money transactions in India or abroad, without having any knowledge whatsoever, that prime accused or his associates or any other co-accused were indulging in terrorist activities - There was nothing on record to show that appellant indulged in such activities though he might be involved in other illegal activities - provision of s. 21(2) of TADA could be resorted to, only in case it was proved by prosecution that accused rendered any financial assistance to a person who has already been facing charge of terrorist or disruptive activities or he had reasons to suspect that person to whom financial help was rendered was indulging in such activities - There was a burden on prosecution first to prove said condition - There was nothing on record to show that during the time appellant facilitated financial transaction of prime accused in the fake account named 'HATHI' and that he had reason to suspect that prime accused or his associates were indulging in disruptive activities, or had been accused in such activities - Appellant might be guilty of running and indulging in fraudulent banking activities, or may be violating of provisions of other statutes but could not be held guilty of offences u/s. 3(3) of TADA - Provisions of TADA were not attracted far appellant - Impugned conviction was set aside - Appeals disposed of.

(J)Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3(3), 5 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 3, 7 r/w ss. 25(1-A), (1-B)(a) - Purchase of weapons - Commission of terrorists acts - Conviction - Sustainability - Whether impugned conviction recorded by Trial Court against appellant (A-122) was justified - Held, it was evident that a pistol was sold by A-39 to appellant (A-122), and that it was A-39, who had taught appellant how to use cartridges - Trial Court after appreciation of evidence, held that though appellant was in possession of arms and ammunition in an unauthorized manner, same did not in any way, show complicity of appellant in conspiracy relating to serial blast - Impugned conviction was justified - Appeals disposed of.
