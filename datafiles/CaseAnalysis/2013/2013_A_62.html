Arathi Bandi and another v Bandi Jagadrakshaka Rao and another

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 443; (2013) 15 SCC 790; AIR 2014 SC 918; 2014 (1) ALD(SC) 88; 2013 (4) JCC 2368; JT 2013 (11) SC 48; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 968; 2013(9) SCALE 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 13(c) and (d) - Custody of minor son, an US citizen - Custody order from a US court - Marriage between the parties was solemnized according to Hindu rights on 9-11-2003 in Atlanta, USA - After marriage, they had settled down in Seattle, USA - X, a child was born in USA and, therefore, was a USA citizen by birth - Respondent No.1 (husband) filed a petition for dissolution of marriage in Superior Court of Washington - Petitioner (Wife) submitted a complaint of domestic violence - X was to remain in the custody of petitioner with limited visitation rights were granted to the respondent No. 1 - Petitioner filed motion for continuance of trial, permanent relocation to India with X and requesting the court to order the respondent No. 1 to undergo domestic violence assessment - Superior Court of Washington denied petitioner's motion - In violation of the aforesaid orders, the petitioner travelled to India with X on 17-7-2008 - Superior Court of Washington modified the parenting plan and respondent No. 1 was made custodial parent and the wife was granted visitation rights - Respondent No. 1 filed a Habeas Corpus petition in the HC for production of the X before the Court and permit him to take custody of the X in compliance of the orders passed by the Superior Court of Washington - HC directed that petitioner shall obtain necessary travel tickets for the respondent No. 1 and the child for their visit to the place where US Court was situated - Petitioner shall deposit a sum of $5000 in the name of the respondent No. 1 for enabling him to engage an advocate in US and to submit to the jurisdiction of the US Court - Further directed that petitioner shall make necessary arrangements for the stay of the respondent No. 1 and the child for a period of 15 on their landing in USA - On petitioner providing travel tickets, depositing the amount as ordered above, and intimating the date of departure, if respondent no. 1 fails to submit to the jurisdiction of the US Court along with the X, in obedience to the orders passed in writ of Habeas Corpus by the US Court, he shall handover the custody of the child to the petitioner, who in turn shall produce the child before the US Court and custody of the child will abide by the decision of the US Court since the child was a citizen of USA - Petitioner aggrieved by the aforesaid directions, filed the special leave petitions giving rise to the present appeals - Whether order of the HC could be upheld - Held, no relief could be granted to the petitioner in the present proceedings given her conduct in removing X from US in defiance of the orders of the Court of competent jurisdiction - Petitioner had not pursued any legal proceeding for seeking custody of X - Petitioner had also not sought a declaration that the orders passed by the American Courts were null and void and were without jurisdiction - Therefore, HC cannot be said to have acted erroneously - Directions issued by the HC in the impugned order were upheld with the additions and modifications - SC directed that petitioner shall make necessary arrangements for the stay of the respondent No.1 and the child in suitable accommodation in a locality according to her status prior to the dissolution of marriage for a period of 3 months on their landing in US - Prior to making any travel arrangements for the respondent No. 1 and X, the petitioner shall move the Court of Competent Jurisdiction in US for withdrawal of the bailable warrants issued against the respondent No.1 to enable him to attend the custody proceedings in the US Courts - Upon the bailable warrants having been withdrawn, the petitioner shall personally escort respondent No.1 and X from India to the US - Appeals dismissed.
