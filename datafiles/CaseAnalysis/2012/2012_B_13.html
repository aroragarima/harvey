Bavo @ Manubhai Ambalal Thakore v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

03 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 23; (2012) 2 SCC 684; AIR 2012 SC 979; 2012 CRLJ 1573; JT 2012 (2) SC 29; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 718; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 820; 2012 (2) RLW 1873; 2012(2) SCALE 321; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 822; 2012 (2) WLN(SC) 63

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376 and 506(2) - Rape - Conviction - Reduction of sentence - Entitlement - Appellant allegedly raped complainant's daughter - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 376 and 506(2) of IPC and sentenced him to life imprisonment with fine of Rs. 20,000/- - Appeal was filed before HC which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that his age was 18-19 years at the time of incident and he hailed from poor family, so award of life imprisonment and fine of Rs.20,000/- was excessive - Whether sentence imposed by Trial Court should be reduced - Held, considering the fact that victim was aged about 7 years on the date of incident and appellant was 18/19 years old and also of the fact that incident occurred nearly 10 years ago, award of life imprisonment which was maximum prescribed was not warranted and also in view of the mandate of s. 376(2)(f) IPC, ends of justice would be met by imposing rigorous imprisonment for 10 years - Further, appellant hailed from poor family and was not in position to pay such huge amount as fine which was not disputed by State - Fine was reduced from Rs. 20,000/- to Rs. 1,000/ - Appeal disposed of.
