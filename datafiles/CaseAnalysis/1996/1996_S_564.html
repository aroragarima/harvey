Prasanna Kumar Roy Karmakar v State Of West Bengal And Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 687; (1996) 3 SCC 403; 1996 (3) AD(SC) 604; AIR 1996 SC 1517; 1996 (2) CCC 79; 1996 (2) CLT 71; JT 1996 (3) SC 647; 1997 (1) RCJ 223; 1996 (2) RCR(Rent) 129; 1996 (1) RentLR 414; 1996(3) SCALE 239; [1996] 3 S.C.R. 912; 1996 (3) Supreme 421; 1996 (2) UJ 468

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Constitution - Constitution, art. 133 - Appeal - Private dispute between landlord and tenant - Order of writ - Court directing police authorities to allow landlord to have ingress and egress to and from disputed premises - Tenant evicted with help of Police on basis of said order - Appellate Court though set aside order of writ Court but not granting any relief to tenant - Held, Case remanded to appellate Court for restoring tenant back into possession of disputed premises - Petition allowed.
