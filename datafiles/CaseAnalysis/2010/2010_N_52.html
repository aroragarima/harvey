Birla Technologies Limited v Neutral Glass And Allied Industries Limited

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1079; (2011) 1 SCC 525; 2011 (84) ALR 459; 2011 (3) AWC 2647; 2011 (1) CompLJ 649; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 201; JT 2010 (13) SC 524; 2010(13) SCALE 360; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 111; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 8

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 2(1)(d)(ii) - 'Consumer' - Appellant sent a detailed proposal for developing certain computer software for the respondent at a cost of Rs.36 lacs - Said proposal was accepted by the respondent - Appellant developed the software for the respondent for which respondent expressed its satisfaction over the working of the Modules - Later respondent started complaining about the working of that software and filed complained before State Commission alleging deficiency in services rendered by the appellant - Appellant raised a preliminary objection that the respondent was not a 'Consumer' within the meaning of the 1986 Act on the ground that the services availed by the respondent from appellant were exclusively for a commercial purpose - The State Commission accepted the appellant's preliminary objection and dismissed the complaint - On appeal, National Commission held that the 'goods' purchased by the respondent from the appellant were being used by the respondent for a commercial purpose, and, therefore, the respondent was not a 'consumer' within the meaning of s. 2(1)(d)(i) of the 1986 Act - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the National Commission could be upheld? - Held, goods and services hired for commercial purposes cannot be the subject matter of a consumer complaint under the Consumer Protection Act - In the present case, impugned complaint was not maintainable, on the count that the goods have been purchased for commercial purposes - Impugned decision of the National Commission set aside - Appeal allowed.
