Â Anant Prasad Lakshminivas Generiwal v State of Andhra Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 221; AIR 1963 SC 853; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1964 (1) SCJ 615; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 844

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Charitable and Religious Trusts Act, 1920, Part B States (Laws) Act, 1951, Charitable Endowments Act, 1890

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Constitution - Charitable and Religious Trusts Act, 1920, s. 3 - Hyderabad Endowments Regulations 1940, ss. 2, 4(b)and 3 to 18 - Madhya Pradesh Public Trusts Act 1951, ss. 2(4), 3 and 7(1) CPC, 1908, 92 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 19 - Whether the Regulations give power for removal of trustee under any circumstances and if so whether removal in this case has taken place as provided under Regulations and Rules framed thereunder? - Held, there is no provision for removal of a trustee, merely because he has not appeared in answer to a notice u/s. 6 of Regulations for registration of endowment - All that Director of Endowments was entitled to do on basis of notice was to proceed to register endowment, even if appellant failed to appear, after making such inquiries and take further action as justified by other provisions of Regulations - On basis of that notice it was not open to him to pass orders for removal of appellant from trusteeship and taking over of management of trust by Govt. - These orders are set aside as ultra vires - Further held, assuming Regulations and Rules providing for removal of trustee, are constitutional - Appeal dismissed but Writ Petition allowed.
