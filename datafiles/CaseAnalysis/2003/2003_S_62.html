Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission v Sanjay Kumar Singh

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 602; (2003) 7 SCC 657; (2003) SCC (L&S) 1081; AIR 2003 SC 3626; 2003 (52) ALR 701; 2004 (1) AWC 146; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 371; 2003 (4) ESC 411; JT 2003 (8) SC 79; 2003 LabIC 3134; 2003(6) SCALE 271B; 2003 (7) SLR 6; 2003 (5) SLT 199; 2003 (5) Supreme 433; 2004 (1) UC 122; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 217

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1952 - art. 342 - U.P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and other Backward Classes) Act, 1994 - Scheduled Tribes - Whether the respondent can claim the benefit of reservation in public service in the State of U.P. as a member of Scheduled Tribe though 'Naga' is not specified to be a Scheduled Tribe in that State - Held, the appellant cannot be treated as a Scheduled Tribe candidate so as to qualify himself to claim reservation against the vacancy reserved for Scheduled Tribe in public services in the State of U.P. - Further held, however, in the peculiar circumstances of the case, the ends of justice would be met if the appellants are directed to consider the case of the respondent in general category, and if in comparison with the general category candidates selected, the respondent had secured higher marks/grading, he should be offered appointment to an appropriate post against one of the existing vacancies.
