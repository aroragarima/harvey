Bata Shoe Company Private Limited and Others v Collector of Central Excise and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Balakrishna Eradi, D.A. Desai, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 420; (1985) 3 SCC 97; AIR 1985 SC 1070; 1985 (2) CompLJ 218; 1985 CRLR 422; 1985 CrLR(SC) 422; 1985 ECR 913; 1985 (21) E.L.T. 9; 1985(1) SCALE 923; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 960; 1985 TAXLR 2534

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Trade Discount

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 4, sch. 1, item 36 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8(1) - Companies Act, 1956 - Whether the mode of determination of 'value' prescribed by s.4 is not attracted while computing the ' value' of the articles of footwear for the purposes of testing the availability of the exemption granted under the Notification D/- July 26, 1967 - Interpretation of this Notification - Held, for all purposes connected with the determination of chargeability and levy of duty the provisions of the section are to be applied for computation of the value' of the article - Section not only lays down formula for determination of value 'for purpose of duty' but also for exemption from duty - Appeal No. 1469/72 dismissed and Appeal No. 353/1971 and 1470/2 allowed.
