Madhukar Sadbha Shivarkar (D) by Lrs. and others v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 108; (2015) 6 SCC 557; 2015(4) ALL MR 923; 2016 (1) MahLJ 140; 2015(2) SCALE 342

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Leases

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holdings) Act, 1961, s. 14 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 162 - Appointment of Enquiry Officer - Executive power of State - Company had taken 5000 acres of land on lease from various land owners and created 125 sub-leases in favour of its shareholders and allotted land - While so, when Act came into force, it was alleged that said shareholders of Company in collusion with revenue authorities manipulated revenue records of land involved in proceedings with mala fide intention to show that besides the holdings of land by Company, there were 384 sub-leases of land altogether and also destroyed records and false revenue records were created to substantiate said 384 sub leases - Later, in 1964, original landowners filed complaint with ACB against Company and its shareholders challenging manipulation of revenue records - In 1974, ACB obtained permission to investigate alleged fraud and criminal cases were tried against shareholders and were convicted - While so, in land ceiling proceedings of land holdings of shareholders of Company, it was held by Tahsildar that everybody held land within ceiling limit and order attained finality in 1977 as appeals were dismissed - Appeals against said orders of conviction were dismissed and SLP's before SC were also dismissed in year 1985 - In view of said conviction, State govt. in exercise of power u/s. 14(4) of the Act, appointed Enquiry Officer to make enquiry pertaining to list of bogus landholders - Hence, writ petitions were filed before HC challenging appointment of Enquiry Officer - HC vide common judgment and order, upheld appointment of Enquiry Officer - Hence instant appeals.

Held, some of the appellants were convicted and sentenced for offences punishable under IPC, in relation to offences committed by them in respect of land holdings and in view of judgment passed in those cases having attained finality before SC, State Govt., after examining representations made by land owners with reference to relevant land records of land holders of the villages, has rightly exercised its statutory power by appointing Sub-divisional Officer as Enquiry Officer at first instance and later on appointing Deputy Commissioner to enquire into the matter which is in the larger public interest. Further, orders of State Govt. could be traceable to executive power of State Govt. u/art. 162 of Constitution having regard to magnitude of alleged fraud in relation to vast extent of land holding obtained by declarants by giving false declarations with a view to come out from clutches of land ceiling provisions of the Act, which is the prima facie view taken by State Govt. and the same could not be found fault with. Hence, orders impugned in writ petitions which are affirmed by HC, are perfectly legal and valid. Appeals disposed of.
