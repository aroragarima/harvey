The State Of A.P. & Ors. v M/S. Star Bone Mill & Fertiliser Co.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 107; (2013) 9 SCC 319; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 921; 2013 (5) ALD(SC) 34; 2013 (97) ALR 744; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 315; 2013 (2) CTC 347; JT 2013 (3) SC 401; 2013 (120) RD 643; 2013(2) SCALE 728; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 394; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Land Encroachment Act

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 90, 110 - Continued possession - Claim of title - Challenged - Respondent (plaintiff) filed a suit for declaration of title and for injunction against appellant (State) and others - Trial Court decreed suit holding that appellant was not owner of suit land, and that respondent had a better title over it - Aggrieved, appellants filed an appeal before HC, challenging judgment and decree passed by Trial Court, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that lease deed executed by appellant (State) in favour of vendor of respondent should be considered in light of provisions of s. 90 of the Act and that questioned sale deed in favour of respondent - Whether impugned judgment and decree passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC were justified - Held, a person must establish that he has continued possession of suit property, while other side claiming title, should make out a case of trespass/encroachment etc., - Where the apparent title was with plaintiffs, it was incumbent upon defendant, that in order to displace that claim of apparent title and to establish beneficial title in himself, he should establish by way of satisfactory evidence, circumstances that favour his version - Even, a revenue record was not a document of title - It merely raise a presumption in regard to possession - Presumption of possession and/or continuity thereof, both forward and backward, could also be raised u/s. 110 of the Act - Courts below have failed to appreciate that mere acceptance of municipal tax or agricultural tax by a person, could not stop the State from challenging ownership of land, as there might not be estoppel against statute - Nor could such a presumption arise in case of grant of loan by a bank upon it hypothecating the property - Courts below erred in holding that revenue records confer title, for the reason that they merely show possession of a person - Sale deed upon which respondent relied on was invalid and inoperative, as documents on record established that vendor of respondent was merely a lessee of appellant (State) - Impugned judgment and decree passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
