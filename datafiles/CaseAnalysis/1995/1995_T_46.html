T. R. Dhananjaya v J. Vasudevan

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 212; (1995) 5 SCC 619; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1265; AIR 1996 SC 302; 1995 (31) ATC 177; 1995 (3) AWC 1709; 1995 (2) CLR 98; JT 1995 (6) SC 234; [1995] Supp3 S.C.R. 64; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1632

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2 and 12 - Wilful disobedience of Courts Order - Petitioner's claim for promotion accepted by High Court - Decision affirmed by Supreme Court - Right of petitioner to promotion with all consequential benefits reiterated by Supreme Court in its clarificatory order - Fact that petitioner was not eligible under relevant Rules for promotion never brought to notice of Court - Whether refusing promotion to petitioner on such pretext amounted to contempt - Held, Amounted to contempt - Govt. cannot go behind court orders and truncate their effect by hovering over rules - Sentence of one month single imprisonment imposed on contemnor - Petition allowed.
