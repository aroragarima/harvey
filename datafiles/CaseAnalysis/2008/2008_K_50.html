Umesh Challiyil v K.P.Rajendran

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 639; (2008) 11 SCC 740; AIR 2008 SC 1577; 2008(3) ALL MR 488; 2008 (2) AWC 1552; JT 2008 (3) SC 232; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 375; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 247; 2008(3) SCALE 252; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Statement Of Fact, Allegations Of Corrupt Practice, Contents Of Petition, Corrupt Practice, Dismissal Of Election Petition, Source Of Information

Summary: Representation of the People Act, 1951; Conduct of Election Rules, 1961 - Single Judge dismissed election petition as it was not properly affirmed as under ss. 83 & 85 of 1951 Act - Appeal against - Held, defects which have been pointed out in this election petition was purely cosmetic and it does not go to the root of the matter and secondly even if the Court found them of serious nature then at least the court should have given an opportunity to the petitioner to rectify such defects - Appeal allowed.
