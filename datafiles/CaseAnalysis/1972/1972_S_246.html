R. M. Malkani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 200; (1973) SCC (Cr) 399; (1973) 1 SCC 471; AIR 1973 SC 157; 1973 CRLJ 228; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Telecom

Summary: Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 25 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 7 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 20(3)and 21 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 162 - (A) Damaging or tampering with telegraphs - Where a person talking on telephone allows a police officer to record it on tape or to hear it - And whether he is damaging, removing, tampering, touching machinery, battery line or post for intercepting or acquainting himself with contents of any message within this section? - (B) Evidence - Tape recorded conversation - Admissibility - (C) Appreciation of evidence - Admissibility of evidence which is illegally obtained - Held, it cannot be said that police officer is doing so within this section - Held, tape recorded conversation was admissible in evidence - Even if evidence is illegally obtained it is admissible - Appeal dismissed.
