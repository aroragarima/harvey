Â Election Commission of India v N.G. Ranga and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia, A.D. Koshal, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 191; (1978) 4 SCC 181; AIR 1978 SC 1609; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 210; 1978 UJ 639

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Election

Keywords: Issuance Of Notice, Opinion of Election Commission

Summary: Constitution - Administrative - Election - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 84, 101, 102, 103, 103(2), 104 - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Disqualification - Opinion of Election Commission - Issuance of notice - Jurisdiction - Respondent no. 1 was a declared elected candidate to the Lok Sabha in a bye-election - Appellant, Election Commission of Indian (EC) issued show cause notice to respondent no. 1 and upon explanation submitted by him, not further action was took thereafter - Respondent no. 2, voter from constituency of respondent no.1, filed a petition to the President of India, u/arts. 84, 101 to 104 of Constitution - President passed an order u/art. 103(2) of Constitution and sought opinion of appellant - Thereafter, appellant issued notice to respondent no. 1 to submit reply to allegations contained in petition filed before President - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 filed petition before HC to forbid appellant from taking further steps pursuant to notice and that appellant had no jurisdiction to inquire into petition submitted to President, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal - Whether appellant had jurisdiction to issue notice to respondent no. 1 and appellant committed any error of law or acted beyond its jurisdiction in seeking explanation of respondent no. 1 - Held, by cl. (2) of art. 103 of Constitution, President was bound to obtain opinion of appellant before giving his decision on question - Not only that, but President was further bound to act according to opinion given by appellant - President acted both in exercise of constitutional authority and in discharge of his constitutional obligation in referring the question raised by respondent no. 2's petition for opinion of appellant - The Act conferred extensive powers on EC in regard to inquiries pertaining to questions referred by President for its opinion u/art. 103 of Constitution - By 42nd Amendment to the Constitution, art. 103(2) of Constitution required the President to consult EC - Amended Article expressly conferred power on EC to make, for that purpose, 'such inquiry as it thinks fit' - Commission was charged with obligation to tender its opinion to President, it had the power to make such inquiry as it thought fit in order to enable it to express its opinion, which under the law as it stood then, was binding on President - 42nd Amendment to Constitution expressed clearly what was necessarily implicit in old provision - If the Constitution envisaged that EC should have the power to make such inquiry as it thinks fit even when its opinion was not binding on President who was merely required to 'consult' the EC it could not be that the EC could tender its binding opinion without the right and, nay the duty, of making the necessary inquiry - HC misdirected itself in reaching the conclusion that appellant acted beyond its jurisdiction in issuing notice to respondent no. 1 calling upon him to submit his explanation in regard to the allegations made by respondent no. 2 in his petition to President - Appeal allowed.
