Â Pioneer Traders and Others, Pondicherry v Chief Controller of Imports and Exports, Pondicherry and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 9; AIR 1963 SC 734; 1984 ECR 162; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1376; 1964 (1) SCJ 595; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 349

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution - Customs - Trade - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 32, 19(1)(f) and 19(1)(g) - Sea Customs Act, 1878, s.167(8) - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3(2) - (A) Whether Customs authority functioning u/s. 167(8) is acting judicially or quasi judicially ? - Held, quasi judicial - Customs authority has duty to act judicially in deciding question of confiscation and penalty - Where an authority whether judicial or quasi-judicial, has in law no jurisdiction to make an Order omission by a party to raise before authority relevant facts for deciding that question cannot clothe it with jurisdiction - (B) Whether a writ can be issue if finding is erroneous u/art. 226? - Further held, no, writ cannot be issued - Petition dismissed.
