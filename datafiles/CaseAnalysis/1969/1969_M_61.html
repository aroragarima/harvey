Commissioner of Sales Tax, Madhya Pradesh v Minerva Minerals

Supreme Court of India

05 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 68; 1971 MPLJ 538; 1970 (25) STC 64

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Sales Tax

Keywords: Companies Act, 1913, Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947, Central Provinces And Berar Sales Tax Rules, 1947

Summary: Sales Tax - Companies Act, 1913 - Central Provinces and Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947 ss. 2(c), 2(f), 4(1), 8 and 17 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 119 and 120 - Central Provinces And Berar Sales Tax Rules, 1947 - Whether a registered dealer, who had his head office at Nagpur, under Act, 1947, continued to be a registered dealer in Madhya Pradesh, formed on 01-11-1956, after reorganisation of States if his business was partly carried on in area which went to Maharashtra State and partly in area which formed part of new Madhya Pradesh? - U/s. 17 of Act a registered dealer is required to give within prescribed period information to prescribed authority in case he discontinues his business or if there is any change in his place of business or if he opens a new place of business - If a registered dealer carries on business in places not disclosed in registration certificate he will have to be treated as an unregistered dealer vis-a-vis those places - Held, respondent cannot be treated as a registered dealer in new State of Madhya Pradesh on strength of registration certificate issued to respondent before coming into force of States Reorganisation Act as no place of business in that area was specified in registration certificate - Appeal dismissed.
