Parsram and Another v Shivchand and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 335; (1969) 1 SCC 20; AIR 1969 SC 597; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 997

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951, s. 33(2) - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order 1950, para 2 r/w Part X, Item 9 of Part X - Reorganisation of Punjab Act, 1966 - Constitution (Scheduled Castes) (Union Territories) Order, 1951 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 341(1) - Reserved constituency - For Scheduled Castes (SC) - Whether 'mochis' are classed together with chamars in new State of Punjab? - As per amended Order, 1950 position is that, Item No. 9 remained unaltered so far as new State of Punjab is concerned - Item no. 9 of Part X as stood originally, had not included mochis in list - Held, so far as chamars and mochis are concerned, a reference to Constitution (Scheduled Castes) Order, 1950 shows that President was not of opinion that they were to be considered to belong to same caste in all different States - In several States chamars and mochis were put on same footing but not so in State of Punjab - Even after Reorganisation of Punjab Act, 1966 when question of specification of Scheduled Castes in territories created came up for his consideration President did not take view that mochis should be classed with chamars in so far as States of Haryana, Punjab concerned - Appeal dismissed.
