Â Major U. R. Bhatt v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.C. Das Gupta, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 14; AIR 1962 SC 1344; 1963 (65) BomLR 267; 1962 (1) LLJ 656; 1963 MahLJ 457; 1963 MPLJ 449; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 549

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Whether Governor-General was at fault to order discharge of appellant without fresh enquiry? - It is not within competence of civil court to sit in judgment over decision of authority who is competent by law to dismiss a public servant provided he has been afforded an opportunity to defend himself consistently with substance of constitutional guarantee - Held, enquiry officer afforded to appellant an opportunity to remain present and to make his defence - Appellant declined to take part in proceedings before enquiry officer thus enquiry made cannot be challenged either on ground of unfairness or incompleteness - No need to hold fresh enquiry - Order of discharge from service not liable to be questioned on ground that materials may not have justified passing of that order - Appeal dismissed.
