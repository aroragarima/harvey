Apparel Export Promotion Council v A. K. Chopra

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1320; (1999) SCC (L&S) 405; (1999) 1 SCC 759; AIR 1999 SC 625; 1999 (2) AWC 1115; JT 1999 (1) SC 61; 1999 (1) LLJ 962; 1999 (1) LLN 1067; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 117; 2000 (1) SLJ 65; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 551

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Removal from service - Validity - Whether observations made by High Court to effect that since respondent did not 'actually molest' Miss X but only 'tried to molest' her and, therefore, his removal from service was not warranted? - Held, Statement of victim must be appreciated in background of entire case - Where evidence of victim inspires confidence, courts are obliged to rely on it - Sympathy in such cases in favour of superior officer is wholly misplaced and mercy has no relevance - HC overlooked ground realities and ignored fact that conduct of respondent against his junior female employee, Miss X, was wholly against moral sanctions, decency and was offensive to her modesty - No justification for HC to interfere with punishment imposed by departmental authorities - Order of HC is set aside and punishment imposed by Disciplinary Authority and Departmental Appellate Authority on respondent is upheld and restored - Appeal allowed.
