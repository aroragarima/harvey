Gangaram v N. Shankar Reddy

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 225; (1988) 4 SCC 648; AIR 1989 SC 302; JT 1988 (4) SC 100; 1988 (2) RCJ 653; 1988 (2) RCR(Rent) 477; 1988(2) SCALE 1017; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 433; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 433

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Requirement of additional space

Summary: Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 10(3), 10(3)(c), 19(3)(a)(iii) - Requirement of additional space - Eviction - Justifiability - Respondent (landlord) filed an eviction petition u/s. 10(3) of the Act for eviction of appellant (tenant) on the ground of requirement of additional space - Rent Controller dismissed petition holding that respondent was not entitled to evict appellant either u/s. 19(3)(a)(iii) or u/s. 10(3) of the Act - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before Appellate Authority, which was allowed holding that even though leased premises had a separate municipal door number it could be treated as forming part of building in respondent's occupation because both buildings were owned by respondent and besides two buildings were separated only by a single wall - Aggrieved appellant filed a revision before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that u/s. 10(3)(c) of the Act respondent would be entitle to seek eviction of appellant for purposes of additional accommodation for himself only if portion occupied by appellant forms part of same building occupied by respondent and that s. 10(3)(c) of the Act would not apply to a case where landlord and tenant re occupying different buildings even though the 2 buildings might be owned by same landlord - Whether appellant was liable to be evicted u/s. 10(3)(c) of the Act - Held, what s. 10(3)(c) of the Act envisaged was the oneness of building and not the oneness of ownership of 2 different buildings, one occupied by landlord and other by tenant - Significant words used in s. 10(3)(c) of the Act were 'landlord who was occupying only a part of a building' and 'any tenant occupying the whole or any portion of remaining part of the building' - No one could say that 2 adjoining buildings bearing different door numbers, one occupied by landlord and other by tenant would make them one and the same building if they were owned by one person and separate buildings if they were owned by 2 different persons - It was not for Court to read s. 10(3)(c) of the Act beyond its terms oblivious to the limitations contained therein and hold that a separate tenanted building adjoining the building in owner's occupation would also form part of latter building - Impugned order of Appellate Authority and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
