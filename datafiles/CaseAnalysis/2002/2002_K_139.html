Jinia Keotin & Ors. v Kumar Sitaram Manjhi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1526; (2003) 1 SCC 730; 2003 (2) ALD(SC) 87; 2003 (3) AWC 2288; 2003 (4) Bom.C.R. 320; 2003 (1) CGLJ 162; 2003 (1) DMC 1; 2003 (1) JCC 182; JT 2002 (10) SC 571; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 469; 2002(9) SCALE 690; 2003 (1) SLT 40; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 689; 2003 (1) Supreme 441

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage & Divorce

Summary: Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - S. 16 - interpretation of - held, there is no room for according upon such children who but for Section 16 would have been branded as illegitimate any further rights than envisaged therein by resorting to any presumptive or inferential process of reasoning, having recourse to the mere object or purpose of enacting Section 16 of the Act. Any attempt to do so would amount to doing not only violence to the provision specifically engrafted in Sub-section (3) of Section 16 of the Act but also would attempt to court relegislating on the subject under the guise of interpretation, against even the will expressed in the enactment itself. - The view taken by the courts below cannot be considered to suffer from any serious infirmity to call for interference.
