Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kanpur v Kamla Town Trust

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 644; (1996) 7 SCC 349; AIR 1996 SC 620; 1996 (130) CTR 112; [1996] 217 ITR 699; JT 1995 (8) SC 364; 1995(6) SCALE 517; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 300; [1996] 84 TAXMAN 248; 1996 (2) TLR 145

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Exemption From Payment

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Indian Trusts Act, 1882 ,s. 6 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 26 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 4(3), Expln. - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(15) and 11 - Evidence Act, 1872, s.11 - Income Tax assessment of charitable trust -Trust created by Settlor Company to construct colony for their workmen - Rectification of instrument of trust - Rectificatified deed made workmen in the town and surrounding areas etc. as beneficiaries - (A) Whether Civil Court is competent to consider non-fulfilment of condition precedent of mutual mistake to document - (B) Determination of relevency of Order by Civil Court in assessment proceedings before ITO - (C) Nature of Trust after rectifiction of deed - Deed not projecting an object of providing relief to poor workmen only - Nor advancing any other object of general public utility - If Expln. to s. 4 (3) of the Act is attracted - Held, would not divest Court of its jurisdiction to entertain rectification proceedings - Is almost a tripartite transaction - Though not judgments in rem they are relevant - Rectified trust deed could be said to have created public charitable trust - Appeals pertaining to AYs 1956-57 to 1965-66 dismissed ; Appeals pertaining to AYs 1949-50 to 1955-56 allowed.
