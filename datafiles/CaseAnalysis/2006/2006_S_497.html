Sandeep Subhash Parate v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 441; (2006) 7 SCC 501; AIR 2006 SC 3102; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 556; JT 2006 (7) SC 604; 2007 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 2006(8) SCALE 503; 2006 (5) SLR 515; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 282; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2902

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Family & Personal

Summary: [A.] Education - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Appellant claims himself to be a member of 'Halba' community - Obtained admission in courses of Bachelor of Engineering (Instrumentation Engineering) - Later realized that he did not belong to 'Halba' community - Prayer to allow to obtain degrees - Held, he had obtained his admission in a professional institution not purely on basis of his merits but on basis that he belonged to a reserved category - He might have been under a bona fide belief that Koshti-Halbas were members of a Scheduled Tribe - Not found any lack of bona fide on part of appellant - He be allowed to obtain degree subject to payment of Rs. 1 lakh in favour of State of Maharashtra so as to recompense State to some extent amount spent on him for imparting education as a reserved category candidate - Appeal partly allowed.
