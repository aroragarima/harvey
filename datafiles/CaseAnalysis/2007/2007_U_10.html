Sanjay Singh and Another v U.P. Public Service Commission, Allahabad and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Y.K. Sabharwal, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 652; (2007) 3 SCC 720; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 870; AIR 2007 SC 950; 2007 (2) ALJ 247; 2007 (5) AWC 4641; 2007 (1) ESC 107; JT 2007 (2) SC 534; 2007(2) SCALE 1; 2007 (4) SCJ 218; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 235; 2007 (2) SLR 594; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1068

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conduct Of Business, U.P. State Public Service Commission (Regulation of Procedure and Conduct of Business) Act, 1974

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Uttar Pradesh Public Service Commission (Regulation of Procedure and Conduct of Business)Act, 1974 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 137, 234, 309 - Recruitment - Examination - Valuation - Results - Justifiability - Petitioners appeared for written examination - Results were declared on basis of 'scaled marks' and marks awarded in interview - Appointments were made for post of JC on recommendations of commission - Hence, instant petitions - Petitioner contended that statistical scaling system adopted by Commission was illegal as it was contrary to Uttar Pradesh Judicial Service Rules, 2001 - Further, conversion of their raw marks into scaled marks was illegal as it was done by applying an arbitrary, irrational and inappropriate scaling formula - Whether 'scaling system' adopted by Commission was arbitrary and irrational, and decision in S. C. Dixit 2003 Indlaw SC 948 (supra) approving 'scaling system' requires reconsideration - Held, judicial service rules entrust function of conducting examinations to commission, which - did not prescribe manner and procedure for holding examination and valuation of answer-scripts and award of final marks and declaration of results - When a large number of candidates appear for an examination, it was necessary to had uniformity and consistency in valuation of answer- scripts - Commission constituted a committee to carry out an in-depth study into matter and suggest appropriate means to ensure that evaluation was on more equitable basis - In instant case, there was no proper or adequate study before introduction of scaling system which was primarily intended for preparing common merit list in regard to candidates who take examination in different optional subjects had been inappropriately and mechanically applied to situation where need was to eliminate examiner variability on account of strict/liberal valuation - Thus, scaling system adopted by commission leads to irrational results - Therefore, approval of scaling system in Uttar Pradesh Public Services Commission v Subhash Chandra Dixit and Others 2003 Indlaw SC 948 was no longer valid and direct moderation, which would be prospective in its application and would not affect selection and appointments already made in pursuance of 2003 examination - Petitions partly allowed.
