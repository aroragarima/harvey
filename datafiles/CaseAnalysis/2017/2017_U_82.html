Union of India and others v S. Ravichandran and others

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Gupta, Madan B. Lokur, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 610; (2017) 8 SCC 535; 2017 (6) MLJ 748

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Border Security Force (Assistant Commandant) Recruitment Rules, 1985, BSF (General Duty Officers) Recruitment Rules, 2001

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Intervention of Courts in policy matter of promotions - Private respondents filed WP before HC praying for implementation of decision taken on 28-8-2000/31-8-2000, for creation of 26 posts of Assistant Commandant and 8 posts of Deputy Commandant in ministerial cadre in BSF - Upon hearing parties, HC allowed writ petition on ground that once the right of ministerial cadre to be promoted against 10% of promotion quota to post of Assistant Commandant had been taken away, the department was under an obligation to give effect to decision taken by Ministry of Finance on 28-8-2000 and conveyed on 31-8-2000 - Hence, instant appeal by UOI contending that decision taken on 28-8-2000/31-8-2000 is superseded by the cadre review took place on 28-11-2003 - Whether subject matter of communications dt.28-8-2000/31-8-2000 are different.

Held, though subject matter of two communications dt.31-8-2000 and 28-11-2003 are slightly different, yet, the subject matter of the letter dt.28-11-2003 encompasses the entire supervisory and support infrastructure of BSF which includes the ministerial cadre and it was issued in suppression of all orders on said subject matter. Further, communication dt.28-11-2003 deals with restructuring of all posts from Constable to DIG including the posts meant for ministerial cadre. As such, there is no legal infirmity in communication dt.28-11-2003 which supersedes the decision dt.28-8-2000/31-8-2000. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.
