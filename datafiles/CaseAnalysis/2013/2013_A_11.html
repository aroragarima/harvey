Abdul Nasar Adam Ismail Through Abdul Basheer Adam Ismail v The State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 195; (2013) 4 SCC 435; AIR 2013 SC 1376; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1922; JT 2013 (6) SC 622; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 682; 2013(5) SCALE 264; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 86

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Possession of smuggled goods - Detention - Delay in representation - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly concealed two packets of gold chains in his undergarments and under knee caps - Detaining authority seized gold from appellant valued at Rs.95,35,932/- and ordered detention of appellant u/s. 3(1) of the Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition challenging order of detention before HC - DB dismissed writ petition confirming order of detaining authority - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment and order of HC was sustainable - Held, if it was assumed that there was some delay in considering representation, same would not vitiate original order of detention - By reason of delay, only further detention of detenu would become illegal - Delay in considering representation did not vitiate order of detention itself - Detention order passed at satisfaction of detaining authority on basis of material available in no manner gets vitiated for reason of non-consideration of representation made by detenu to Central Govt. - Further, initial order of detention was not rendered void ab initio - In K. M. Abdulla Kunhi and B. L. Abdul Khader v Union of India and Others State of Karnataka and Others 1991 Indlaw SC 473, SC held that any unexplained delay in disposal of representation of detenu would be a breach of Constitutional imperative and it would render continued detention impermissible and illegal and set aside continued detention of detenu - Hence, order of detention was valid however, on account of delay in disposal of representation of detenu by State Govt., continued detention of detenu was illegal - Therefore, detaining authority were directed that detenu be released from detention - Appeal disposed of.
