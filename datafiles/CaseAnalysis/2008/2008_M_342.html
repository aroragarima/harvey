Commissioner of Central Excise and Customs v Mds Switchgear Limited

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1842; (2008) 17 SCC 71; 2008 (229) E.L.T. 485

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Practice & Procedure - Practice of raising value - Semi-finished goods - Excess Modvat credit - Assessee after carrying out certain operation cleared goods at lower value than landing cost of semi-finished received from their unit - Revenue after verification issued show cause notice as to why Modvat credit should not be disallowed - Commissioner confirmed demand of Rs. 13,08,701/- and imposed penalty and recovery - Aggrieved assessee preferred appeal before Tribunal and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Tribunal has come to the conclusion that in fact there was no loss of revenue. It accepted appeal by recording reasons. Counsel appearing for Revenue could not assail any of findings recorded by Tribunal. SC agrees with view taken by Tribunal and find no merit in these appeals which are dismissed leaving parties to bear their own costs. Appeals dismissed.
