Jai Prakash Gupta (D) Through LRs v Riyaz Ahamad and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Tarun Chatterjee, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1474; (2009) 10 SCC 197; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 294; 2009 (77) ALR 622; 2010 (1) AWC 324; JT 2009 (14) SC 29; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 149; 2010 (158) PLR 673; 2009 (2) RCR(Rent) 474; 2009(13) SCALE 466; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 525

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Application For Release, Release Proceeding

Summary: Rent Control - U.P. Act No. 13 of 1972, s. 21(1)(a) - Eviction - Subsequent developments - Effect thereof - Father of present landlord filed application for release of Shop on ground that shop in dispute is required for his bonafide need - Prescribed Authority rejected application - Appeal thereagainst allowed by Additional District Judge - Tenant filed writ petition - HC set aside judgment and order of Additional District Judge and remanded same to it to consider effect of subsequent developments on question of bonafide requirement of landlord and also on comparative hardship of parties - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC justified in remanding case back for disposal to Appellate Court on sole reasoning of subsequent developments, without looking at findings of Appellate Court? - Held, recorded findings are insufficient to decide matter in light of subsequent developments - HC fully justified in setting aside order of Appellate Court in view of fact that all facts stated need to be gone into - However, considering that litigation is pending for 15 years, HC not justified in sending matter to Appellate Court for fresh decision - It would have been appropriate and proper and in the interest of justice for HC to have kept Writ Petition pending before it while sending back issue on effect of subsequent developments to Appellate Court for taking evidence and findings arrived - Matter needs to be examined on further evidence after restoring Writ Petition before HC - Judgment of HC set aside and Writ Petition restored - Appeal allowed.
