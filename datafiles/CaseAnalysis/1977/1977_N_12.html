Narayana Prabhu Venkateswara Prabhu v Narayana Prabhu Krishna Prabhu (Dead) By L. Rs

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 377; (1977) 2 SCC 181; AIR 1977 SC 1268; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 636

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Whether there is bar of res judicata does not depend on existence of right of appeal of same nature against each of two decisions but on question whether same issue has been heard and finally decided? - Held, in partition suit each party claiming that property is joint, asserts a right and litigates under a title which is common to others who make identical claims and if very issue is litigated in another suit and decided, others making same claim cannot be held to be claiming a right 'in common for themselves and others' therefore each of them can be deemed, by reason of Explanation VI to s. 11, to represent all those nature of whose claims and interest are common or identical - Appeal dismissed.
