State of Rajasthan and Another v Talib Khan and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

24 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1382; (1996) 11 SCC 393; (1997) SCC (Cr) 29; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 340; AIR 1997 SC 1559; 1997 (34) AllCriC 166; 1996 (4) CCR 284; 1996 (4) Crimes 189; 1997 CRLJ 408; JT 1996 (10) SC 471; 1996(8) SCALE 169; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 742; 1996 (8) Supreme 258

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - National Security Act, 1980, Ss. 8(1), 3(1) 3(2) and 3(3) - Order of Detention - Right of Representation - Breach - Non-supply of Grounds of Detention - Respondents/Detenue were detained u/s. 3(1) of Act, 1980 by proceedings of District Magistrate - Petitioner/State Govt. confirmed order of detention - Respondent challenged orders of detention, on ground that exceptional circumstances and reasons recorded by District Magistrate were not communicated to them and order of detention was in violation of Art. 22(5) of Constitution - HC, by impugned order enlarged respondents from detention - Hence instant appeal - Whether non-supply of exceptional circumstances and reasons recorded for grounds of detention within outer limit, vitiates order of detention - Held, Respondents had valuable right of representation against order of detention to appropriate Govt. or to Advisor Board - Unless exceptional circumstances and reasons for non-supply of grounds of detention and documents in support thereof or communicated to them - Ground of non-communication or their non-supply by itself was not sufficient to hold that order of detention was in violation of Art. 22(5) of Constitution - Division Bench was not right in concluding that respondents were deprived of making representation at earliest opportunity - Court hold that District Magistrate was prevented, due to exceptional circumstances of grounds of detention and documents in support thereof could not be supplied to Respondents within five days - HC not justified in law to hold that non- communication of ground of exceptional circumstances as reasons recorded by District Magistrate, vitiated order of detention - Appeals allowed.
