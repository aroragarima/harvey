Bijendra Nath Srivastava (Dead) Through LRs v Mayank Srivastava and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2199; (1994) 6 SCC 117; AIR 1994 SC 2562; 1994 (3) ALT 27; 1994 (2) ARBLR 277; 1994 (3) CCC 183; 1994 (2) HLR 346; JT 1994 (5) SC 195; 1994(3) SCALE 739; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Reasoned Award, Award Costs, Misconducted The Proceedings, Judicial Misconduct, 47. The appeal is, therefore, allowed. The judgment of the High Court is set aside and the judgment of the Additional District & Sessions Judge for making the award the rule of the court is restored. The parties are left to bear their own costs.

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14, 17, 30, 33 and 35 - Power of High Court to set aside arbitral award - Held, power of court to set aside an award is restricted to grounds set out in s. 30 of Act, namely, (a) where arbitrator has misconducted himself or proceedings; (b) where award has been made after issue of an order by court superseding arbitration or after arbitration proceedings have become invalid u/s. 35, and; (c) where award has been improperly procured or is otherwise invalid - In absence of any reasons for making award, it is not open to court to interfere with award - Court cannot probe mental process of arbitrator and speculate, where no reasons are given by arbitrator, as to what impelled arbitrator to arrive at this conclusion - An award is not invalid merely because by a process of inference and argument it may be demonstrated that arbitrator has committed grave mistake in arriving at his conclusion - Reasonableness of reasons given by arbitrator cannot, however, be challenged - Arbitrator is sole judge of quality as well as quantity of evidence and it will not be for court to take upon itself task of being a judge of evidence before arbitrator - Court should approach an award with a desire to support it, if that is reasonably possible, rather than to destroy it by calling it illegal - Appeal allowed.
