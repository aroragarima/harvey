Rajeswari Amma And Another v Joseph And Another

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1748; (1995) 2 SCC 159; AIR 1995 SC 719; AIR 1995 SCW 509; 1995 (1) RentLR 367; 1995(1) SCALE 149; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 133 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 11 and 115 - Revision - (A) Decree-holders divided property among themselves pursunt to order of possession passed by Execution Court - Subsequently, revision by judgment-debtor impleading all but one decree holder - (B) Admissibility of new plea raising question of law - New plea raised in civil appeal that common and inseparable order passed in favour of decree holders cannot be revised in absence of one of decree holders - Held, revisional court cannot set aside order of execution court which was common, inseparable and has become final against decree-holder who was not joined in revision - Can be raised as new plea in appeal - Appeal allowed.
