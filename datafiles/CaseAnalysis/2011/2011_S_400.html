Shagun Mahila Udyogik Sahakari Sanstha Maryadit v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 661; (2011) 9 SCC 340; 2012 (1) Bom.C.R. 697; JT 2011 (9) SC 305; 2012 (1) MahLJ 32; 2012 (1) MPLJ 34; 2011(9) SCALE 271; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 789

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Trust & Association - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Bombay Public Trusts Act, 1950 - Scheme - Contract - Entitlement - Appellant/society was registered under the Act, 1860, having years of experience in supplying hot cooked meal for children and other beneficiaries of angan wadu centres in respondent-state - Respondent no. 1, passed a resolution not only prescribing procedure for implementing revised norms but also revised rates in all categories of beneficiaries, as per Integrated Child Development Services Scheme (ICDS) - Expression of Interest(EOI) was taken out by respondent no. 2, and in pursuance applications were received by respondent no.1 - EOI was challenged before DB - DB dismissed petitions , holding petitioners were not entitled as per tender document - Appellant made representations requesting respondents to consider them for supply of food under scheme - Respondents awarded contract to respondent no. 4 to 6 - Aggrieved by action of respondent no.1 and 2, appellant filed petition, which affirmed decision of awarding contract to respondent No. 4 to 6 - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that conditions no.6,7,8 and 9 in EOI were arbitrary - On expiry of 1 year contract, it would be appropriate to invite fresh tenders - Extension of contract for 3 years was contrary and liable to declared illegal - Whether DB was justified in upholding decision of awarding contract to respondents no. 4 to 6 - Held, DB had correctly observed validity of eligibility criteria contained in Cl. 6 of tender and confirmed that only respondent no. 4 to 6 were suitable for grant of contract - Food which was to be supplied to recipients as part of supplementary nutrition programme had to be prepared in manner prescriber by Govt. for safety and nutrient composition of food - It could not be left to uncertainties of machinery available with individual manufacturers - Successful supplier had to be duty bound to necessarily comply with all specification laid down by Govt. in its norms - Various documents had clearly demonstrate that appellant was not eligible at all to be even considered in tender process - Instant appeal was wholly devoid of any merit and hence dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
