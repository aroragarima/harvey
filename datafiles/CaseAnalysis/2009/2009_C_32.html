Pinaki Chatterjee and Others v Central Administrative Tribunal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1931; (2009) 5 SCC 193; AIR 2009 SC 2623; 2009 (3) AWC 2282; 2009 (121) FLR 723; 2009 (3) LLJ 347; 2009(5) SCALE 168; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 368; 2010 (2) SLR 701

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization - Appellants were direct causal appointees in Group C posts in a railway project - Appellants sought regularization - Respondent-administration denied the same - Appellants filed application before Tribunal - Tribunal directed respondents to consider the appellant for Group D posts - Appellants filed writ petition before HC - HC dismissed the writ petition - Whether the services of the appellants should be regularized in Group C posts as they were appointed directly thereto - Decision of SC in Union of India v. Moti Lal, 1996 INDLAW SC 3242 applied - Held, engagement of any casual labour under the railway project, did not amount to any regular recruitment - Although, Railway Administration had committed serious illegalities in making recruitment directly to the Group C posts, the same by itself would not confer any right upon the appellants for being regularized in the said post - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
