K.R. Steel Union Limited v Commissioner of Customs, Kandla

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 445; (2001) 4 SCC 736; AIR 2001 SC 1899; 2001 (96) ECR 209; 2001 (129) E.L.T. 273; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 485; JT 2001 (4) SC 385; 2001(3) SCALE 170; 2001 (3) SLT 887; 2001 (3) Supreme 131

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Excise - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 111(d) and 112(a) - Notification No. 77/90-Cus, D/-17-4-1980 - Whether import of Thyristor Converter and ceramic wool by appellant as parts of Temper Mill and Bright Annealing Furnace is in contravention of import permit issued to appellant? - Held, Notification cannot be read in a narrow manner so as to defeat object of notification - Permission does not indicate that each and every part of above machinery should necessarily be second-hand - Finding of tribunal reversed to extent it was challenged - Appeal partly allowed.
