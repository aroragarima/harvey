Sushil Kumar And Anr. v Ram Prakash And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 311; (1988) 2 SCC 77; AIR 1988 SC 576; 1988 (1) CCC 409; 1988 (1) HLR 573; JT 1988 (1) SC 387; 1988(1) SCALE 80; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 623; 1988 (1) ShimLC 262; 1988 (1) UJ 529

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Recovery suit - Decreed - Legality - Plaintiff filed suit before Trial Court and the same was dismissed - On appeal before Appellate Court - Appellate Court reversed decree of Trial Court and dismissed the suit - Plaintiff filed appeal before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, a decree for perpetual injunction is made u/s. 37 (2) of the Act. Such an injunction can be granted upon merits of suit. Injunction would be to restrain defendant perpetually from commission of an act, which would be contrary to rights of plaintiff s. 38 of the Act governs grant of perpetual injunction and s. 37 (3) of the Act - Provisions of s. 38 of the Act have to be read along with s. 41 of the Act. S. 41 of the Act provides that an injunction cannot be granted in cases falling u/cls. (a) to (j). Cl. (h) thereunder provides that an injunction cannot be granted when a party could obtain an efficacious relief by any other usual mode of proceeding. The coparcener has adequate remedy to impeach alienation made by karta, he cannot, therefore, move Court for an injunction restraining the karta from alienating coparcenary property. Appeal dismissed.
