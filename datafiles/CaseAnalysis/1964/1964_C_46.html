Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Ajax Products Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 261; AIR 1965 SC 1358; [1965] 55 ITR 741; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 700

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922,s.10(2)(Vii) - Voluntary liquidation - Cessation - Surplus income - Assessment - Respondent/assessee was a public limited company incorporated to carry on business in manufacture and sale of steel and abrasives products - Company at an extraordinary general body meeting made a resolution to go into voluntary liquidation and Liquidator was appointed by resolution - Liquidator executed a sale deed to G and transferred plant, machinery and buildings for a sum of Rs. 10,00,000 - Amount was made up of Rs. 1, 00,000 being value of land and Rs. 1, 31,732 being value of buildings and Rs. 7, 68,268 being value of plant and machinery - Income Tax Officer assumed sale was result of collusion between vendor and vendee and came to conclusion that assessee had realized full original cost of buildings and machinery and treated sum of Rs. 5,36,034 which was allowed as depreciation in respect of buildings and machinery in previous years as profits - On appeal Appellate Assistant Commissioner found valuation fixed in sale deed executed by assessee in favor of G was genuine and determined profits liable to tax at a sum of Rs. 3, 23,461 and rejected contention of assesses that second proviso to S. 10(2) (vii) of Act was not applicable - Tribunal estimated value of buildings at a sum of Rs. 2,32,963 which gave a profit on sale of Rs. 1,25,000 instead of Rs. 23,41,1 showed by assesse and rejected contention of assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether surplus income deemed to be profits of assessee - Held, tax shall be payable by an assessee under head Profits and gains of, business, profession or vocation in respect of profit or gains of any business profession or vocation carried on by him - Whenever a sale takes place after cessation of business surplus must be deemed to be profits of year previous to year in which sale took place and for purpose of proviso, business must also be deemed to have been conducted by assessee during previous year - Appeal dismissed.
