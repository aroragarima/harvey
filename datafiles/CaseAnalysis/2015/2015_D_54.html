Dhannulal and others v Ganeshram and another

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 241; 2015 (3) ADJ 697; AIR 2015 SC 2382; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 120; 2015 (110) ALR 215; 2015 (4) AWC 3539; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 74; 2015 (3) CTC 222; JT 2015 (3) SC 559; 2015 (3) MLJ 635; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 701; 2015 (4) RLW 3310; 2015(4) SCALE 613; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 199

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Continuous cohabitation - Presumption of marriage - Validity of Will - Plaintiff filed suit for declaration, possession and damages in relation to two suit houses pleading inter alia that sale deed executed by Late Phoolbasa Bai in favour of defendant no. 5 be declared illegal, void and not binding on him - Trial Court dismissed suit holding that Will executed by Late Phoolbasa Bai in favour of defendant nos. 1 to 4 is legal and sale effected during her pendency of suit in favour of defendant no. 5 was also legal and valid and also that plaintiff failed to prove that Late Phoolbasa Bai was concubine of Chhatrapati - Aggrieved, plaintiff filed appeal before HC which partly dismissed appeal upholding sale deed executed in favour of defendant no. 5 but had set aside finding of Trial Court on issue of Will alleged to have been executed by Late Phoolbasa Bai - Hence, instant cross appeals by both sides, plaintiff against finding of marriage between Late Phoolbasa Bai and Chhatrapati and defendants against findings on Will - Whether appeals could be allowed.

Held, relationship of Chhatrapati and Late Phoolbasa Bai was not denied and also it was not denied that they had been living together as husband and wife in joint family. Hence, there is strong presumption in favour of validity of the marriage and legitimacy of their child for the reason that their relationship is recognized by all persons concerned. Further, instead of adducing unimpeachable evidence, it was pleaded by plaintiff that defendants failed to prove fact that Late Phoolbasa Bai was legally married wife of Chhatrapati. Hence, HC rightly concluded that Phoolbasa Bai was legally married wife of Chhatrapati. Hence, appeal filed by plaintiff is liable to be dismissed. Further, the suspicious circumstance in execution of Will was that when the Will was being executed, the thumb impression over the alleged Will was also taken by beneficiaries and the document-writer was shown to be scribe of the document, whereas the document was not scribed by him. Further, Late Phoolbasa Bai although filed written statement before her death, she had not whisper anything about the Will in the written statement when said written statement was filed in 1987 and alleged Will was executed in 1977. Hence, findings of HC on Will could not be interfered with and appeal of defendants also liable to be dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - It is presumed in law in favour of marriage and against concubinage, when a man and woman have cohabited continuously for a long time.
