Dr Ramesh Chandra Tyagi v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

11 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1650; (1994) 2 SCC 416; (1994) SCC (L&S) 562; 1994 (27) ATC 112; 1994 (1) CLR 659; 1994 (68) FLR 688; JT 1994 (1) SC 530; 1994 (2) LLJ 192; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 915; 1994 (1) SLR 838; 1994 (2) UPLBEC 935

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Tort

Keywords: Research, Learning

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Tort - Whether transfer of appellant from an important post to a post of low profile was justified? - Held, authorities too did not adopt any reasonable or rational attitude - They were out to squeeze appellant and were not willing to budge and consider even when Director of Pune Institute requested them not to post him there as sending such a person was waste for a man of such high calibre - Terms and conditions of appointment provide that he could be transferred anywhere in country - Appeals allowed.
