Central Coalfields Limited v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 726; (1995) 2 SCC 11; JT 1995 (1) SC 561; 1995(1) SCALE 13; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 554

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Coal Bearing Areas Act, 1957 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973 - Mines and Minerals Act, 1957 - Construction - Ultra-vires - overlap - Appellant involved in construction of office building staff quarters - Respondent No.2 /Chairman of Special Areas Development Authority passed order directing appellant u/s.26 of 1973 Act to pull down constructions as it was contrary to provisions of Act- Appellant filed W.P against order was dismissed by HC , that Act was intra-vires to powers of legislature - Hence instant appeal - Whether provisions of 1973 Act overlaps field occupied by provisions of 1957 Act - Held, no specific plea that these two villages were within mining areas and that zonal declaration over laps mining area - Under Coal Act and 1957 Act, mining area stands vested in appellant and it was entitled to carry on mining operations under Act and Rules - Operation of 1973 Act in relation to mining operation was void - HC had rightly declared that operation of 1973 Act to extent does not trench upon field of operation under provisions of Central Acts - Provisions being construed in that backdrop and operational efficacy, was not overlapping of operation of 1973 Act and Central Acts - Both could harmoniously coexist and operate in respective areas without colliding with each other - It would be open to appellant either to bring it to notice of Director and had it corrected but appellant was not controverted - Appeal dismissed.
