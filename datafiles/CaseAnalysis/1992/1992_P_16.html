State Of Tamil Nadu v P.K. Shamsudeen

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 46; (1992) 3 SCC 523; (1992) SCC (Cr) 698; AIR 1992 SC 1937; AIR 1992 SCW 2210; 1992 (2) CCR 229; 1992 (2) Crimes 1148; 1992 CRLJ 3141; 1992 CRLR 574; 1992 CrLR(SC) 574; JT 1992 (4) SC 179; 1992(2) SCALE 52; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 587; 1992 (2) UJ 674

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3 - Appeal against judgment and order of High Court issuing a writ of mandamus against present appellant directing it to forbear from implementing order of detention issued by it against respondents, under provisions of 'COFEPOSA' - Order of injunction based on ground of delay in execution of detention order - Held, order passed was not justified and plea of delay was also not taken before High Court in earlier writ petition impugning detention order, therefore Order of injunction is set aside - Appeal allowed.
