Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board v C. Kenchappa and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 627; (2006) 6 SCC 371; AIR 2006 SC 2038; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 84; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 319; JT 2006 (5) SC 556; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 130; 2006(6) SCALE 1; 2006 (5) SCJ 171; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 226 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board (KIADB) Act, 1966, ss. 3(1), 28 and 31 - Concept of 'Sustainable development' - Respondent agriculturists, affected by acquisition of lands of different villages, filed a writ petition u/art. 226 - Division bench in impugned judgment held that for maintaining ecological equilibrium and pollution free atmosphere of villages, appellant, KIADB be directed to leave a land of one kilo metre (for short one km ) as a buffer zone from outer periphery of village in order to maintain a 'green area' towards preservation of land for grazing of cattle, agricultural operation and for development of social forestry and to develop area into a green belt - Whether directions given in impugned judgment are contrary to express statutory provisions, particularly ss. 3(1) and 47 of Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board (KIADB) Act, 1966, if so said impugned judgment can be set aside? - Held, directions given in impugned judgment are in lines of directions given by this court M C Mehta v. UOI [supra] and are based on NEERI's recommendations were capable of proper implementation - If directions given in impugned judgment are properly implemented then appellant cannot acquire any land for development - This may not have been underlying idea behind judgment but it seems to be obvious consequence of a direction given by division bench in this case - In this view of matter, said directions given in impugned judgment are set aside - 'Development that meets needs of present without compromising ability of future generations to meet their own needs' is sustainable development - Appeal allowed.
