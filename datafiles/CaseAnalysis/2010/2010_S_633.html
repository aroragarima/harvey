Maharshi Dayanand University v Surjeet Kaur

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 508; (2010) 11 SCC 159; 2010 (82) ALR 218; 2011 (1) AWC 109; 2010 (2) CCC 8; JT 2010 (7) SC 179; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 345; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 89; 2010(7) SCALE 194; [2010] 8 S.C.R. 475; 2010 (3) UC 1942; 2010 (4) UPLBEC 3247

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Education

Keywords: Bihar School Examination Board Act, 1952

Summary: Consumer Protection - Education - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, s. 21 - Respondent took admission as a regular student to pursue an M.A. course - In the same academic session respondent also applied for admission in the B.Ed. (correspondence course) without disclosing the fact that she was already pursuing the regular course of M.A. - In view of the General Rules of Examination, she was informed that she could pursue only one course - Respondent opted for pursuing her M.A. course - Subsequently, the University through Notification provided a chance to ex. students who had not been able to complete their post-graduation/B.Ed. courses within the span of prescribed period - Respondent applied under the said Notification for appearing in B.Ed. examination and succeeded in appearing in the examinations and also passed the same - However, appellant-University refused to confer the degree of B.Ed. on the respondent - Aggrieved, the respondent approached the District Forum - District Forum passed an order in favour of the respondent and directed the appellant to issue the B.Ed. degree and compensation to the respondent - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed by State Commission and it was held that District Forum should not have entertained the complaint - Revision filed before the National Commission was allowed - National Commission held that respondent was entitled for the relief claimed and accordingly the appellant was directed to issue the B.Ed. degree - Hence, present appeal - (A) Violation of statutory provision - Held, as per General Rules of Examination a candidate who is pursuing a regular course for an examination in full subjects of the University cannot be simultaneously permitted to appear in another regular course of the same University or of another University or Board - Appellant was, therefore, absolutely right in withholding this privilege from the respondent - Contention of appellant therefore, has to be accepted that the Rule being prohibitory in nature, the District Forum or the National Commission could not have issued a direction which violates the aforesaid statutory provision - Further, Court has no competence to issue a direction contrary to law nor the Court can direct an authority to act in contravention of statutory provisions - (B) Competence of the District Forum and the hierarchy of the Tribunals constituted under the Act to entertain such a complaint - Held, respondent as a student is neither a consumer nor is the appellant rendering any service - Further, appellant is an autonomous body and the decision of the appellant and the statutory provisions have to be implemented through its officers - Hence, District Forum had no jurisdiction to entertain any such complaint - Entire exercise of entertaining the complaint by the District Forum and the award of relief approved by the National Commission do not conform to law - Judgment and order of the District Forum and the National Commission set aside - Appeal allowed.
