Â Vishnu Agencies Private Limited and Another v Commercial Tax Officer and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.S. Kailasam, M. Hameedullah Beg, N.L. Untwalia, P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 409; (1978) SCC (Tax) 31; (1978) 1 SCC 520; AIR 1978 SC 449; 1978 (7) CTR 89; [1978] 2 S.C.R. 433; 1978 (42) STC 31

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 - West Bengal Cement Control Act, 26 of 1948 - Statutory sales - Appellant was licensed stockist of cement and was permitted to stock cement in godown, to be supplied to persons in whose favour allotment orders were issued by authorities concerned - Appellant supplied cement to various allottees from time to time in pursuance of allotment orders issued - Appellant was assessed to sales tax by CTO in respect of transactions - Appellant contended that transactions were not eligible to sales tax - Appellate authority rejected contention and upheld assessments - Single judge restrained respondents from imposing sales tax on transactions - Division bench set aside judgment of single judge - Another appeal was made before court where in similar question under Andhra Pradesh Paddy Procurement (Levy) Orders was raised - Paddy growers in State were under obligation to sell paddy to licensed agents appointed by State Govt. at prices fixed by it - HC held that transactions amounted to sales and were taxable - Whether transactions made by appellant should be subject to sales tax - Held, transactions between appellant and allottees were sales within meaning of s. 2(g) of the 1941 Act - Transactions between growers and procuring agents as also those between rice-millers on one hand and wholesalers or retailers on other were sales within meaning of s. 2(n) of Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act - Turnover was accordingly eligible to sales tax - SC decision in Indian Steel and Wire Products, 1967 Indlaw SC 222, followed - SC decision in Andhra Sugars, 1967 Indlaw SC 418, followed - SC decision in Oil and Natural Gas Commission, 1976 Indlaw SC 71, followed - Appeals dismissed.
