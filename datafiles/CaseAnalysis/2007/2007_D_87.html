Dinesh Dalmia v C.B.I.

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 935; (2007) 8 SCC 770; AIR 2008 SC 78; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3290; 2008 CRLJ 337; JT 2007 (11) SC 164; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 282; 2007(11) SCALE 228; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 1124; 2008 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 128

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delayed Investigation, List Of Witnesses, Judicial Remand, TADA Act, Detention In Custody

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 167(2), 309(2) - Interpretation of - Appellant proceeded against for commission of offences under ss. 409, 420, 120B of IPC - Held, power of a court to direct remand of an accused either in terms of s. 167(2) or s. 309(2) thereof will depend on the stages of the trial - S. 167(2) would be attracted in a case where cognizance has not been taken, s. 309(2) would be attracted only after cognizance has been taken - High Court is correct in its finding that, in the fact situation obtaining, appellant had no statutory right to be released on bail - Appeal dismissed.
