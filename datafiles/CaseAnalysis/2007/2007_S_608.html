Appasaheb and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 664; (2007) 3 SCC (Cr) 468; (2007) 9 SCC 721; AIR 2007 SC 763; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 859; 2007 (2) CGLJ 55; 2007 (1) JCC 147; 2007 (4) KLT 463; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 747; 2007(1) SCALE 50; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 164

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Dowry Death - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304 B - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 2 - Deceased was married to appellant No. 1 - Whether appellant can be convicted u/s. 304 B? - Held, no medical examination showed no sign of external or internal injury on deceased body and cause of death was by poisoning - No evidence on record to prove alleged demand of dowry - No evidence to show that deceased committed suicide due to harassment or any demand for 'dowry' was made - Essential ingredient of s. 304 B of IPC not established - Conviction of appellants not sustainable - Appeals allowed.
