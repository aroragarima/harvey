Syndicate Bank v General Secretary, Syndicate Bank Staff Association and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 295; (2000) 5 SCC 65; (2000) SCC (L&S) 601; AIR 2000 SC 2198; 2000 (2) CLR 472; JT 2000 (5) SC 243; 2000 (33) LabIC 2326; 2000 (1) LLJ 1630; 2000 (2) LLN 942; 2000(4) SCALE 59; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 285; 2000 (3) SLR 129; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1618

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Banking Companies Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings Act 1969, s. 19 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Termination of service due to unauthorised absence from duty - Served notices calling upon him to report for duty - Failure to respond to notice - Bank, under Bipartite settlement, treated him to have voluntarily retired from service - Tribunal and High Court Terminate services on ground of absence - Held, setting aside of order of termination of services on ground of absence of notice to delinquent and direction for reinstatement by Tribunal and HC - Illegal.
