Rohtas Industries Limited v S. D. Agarwal and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 1424; (1969) 1 SCC 325; AIR 1969 SC 707; [1969] 39 Comp Cas 781; 1969 (1) CompLJ 350; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 108

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Agricultural Marketing Act, 1958, Finance Act, 1940, Banking Companies (Legal Practitioners Accounts) Act, 1949

Summary: Companies Act, 1956, ss. 235, 236 and 237 - Review petition - Investigation intocompany's affairs - Power of Central Govt. u/ss. 235 , 237 (b) of the Companies Act, is discretionary - But subject to checks like u/s.237 (a) and u/s.235 of appointing inspector and fulfilling conditions precedents - (A) matter to be considered - (B) Scope and interpretation of - ' If in the opinion of the Central Government there are circumstances suggesting.....' u/s.237(b) of the Act - Existence of circumstances is condition precedent to formation of opinion by Government - The existence of circumstances but not the opinion formed thereon is under / open to judicial review -

Held, investigation being a serious matter should be ordered on good or satisfactory grounds by reasonable authority otherwise liable to be struck down - Held on facts that opinion was not in accordance with law and the order for investigation must be set aside - Order Accordingly.
