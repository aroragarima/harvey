Randhir Singh v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 108; (1982) SCC (L&S) 119; (1982) 1 SCC 618; AIR 1982 SC 879; 1982 (44) FLR 299; 1982 LabIC 806; 1982 (1) LLJ 344; 1982 (1) LLN 327; 1982(1) SCALE 110; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 298; 1982 (1) SLJ 490; 1982 (1) SLR 756

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 19, 32 and 39 (d) - 'Equal pay for equal work' - Grievance of petitioner is that while considering question of scales of pay of police personnel, Pay Commission failed to consider drivers as a separate category and ignored special considerations which prevailed in case of drivers in other departments and which should have, therefore, prevailed in case of driver-constables - Construing arts. 14 and 16 in light of Preamble and art. 39(d) court is of view that principle 'Equal pay for Equal work' is deducible from those Article and may be properly applied to cases of unequal scales of pay based on no classification or irrational classification though these drawing different scales of pay do idential work under same employer - There cannot be slightest doubt that drivers in Delhi Police Force perform same functions and duties as other drivers in service of Delhi Administration and Central Govt. - If anything, by reason of their investiture with 'powers, functions and privileges of a police officer', their duties and responsibilities are more arduous - Direction issued to respondents to fix scale of pay of petitioner and drivers-constables of Delhi Police Force atleast on a par with that of drivers of Railway Protection Force - Petition allowed.
