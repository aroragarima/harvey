Goa Foundation and another v State of Goa and another

Supreme Court of India

29 March 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 245; (2016) 6 SCC 602; AIR 2016 SC 1653; 2016 (116) ALR 493; 2016 (3) Bom.C.R. 747; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 87; JT 2016 (3) SC 428; 2016 (4) MLJ 137; 2016 (132) RD 149; 2016(3) SCALE 460; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Companies Act, 1956, Retrospective Effect, State Legislature, Repugnancy, Goa, Daman & Diu Town and Country Planning Act, 1974, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Land Acquisition (Goa Amendment) Ordinance, 2009, Land Acquisition (Goa Amendment) Act, 2009, Goa State Reorganization Act, 1987

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 40(1)(aa), 41 - Land Acquisition (Goa Amendment) Act, 2009 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Repugnancy between State Act and Central Act - Respondent no.3 addressed letter to Govt. to initiate acquisition proceedings under 1894 Act so as to acquire land covered by survey nos. 803, 804 and accordingly, notification u/s. 4 of 1894 Act was issued declaring that said land was needed for public purpose of tourism development - Later, parties entered into agreement as required u/s. 41 of 1894 Act and declaration u/s. 6 was made and then, respondent no.3 was put in possession of said land - After sometime, application was made on behalf of respondent no.3 to Panjim Planning and Development Authority (PPDA) for grant of permission for extension of existing hotel building on survey nos. 787,788 and 789 and the same was accepted and later, renewal/extension of permission granted was sought with deviation to include survey no. 803 which was acquired land - Permission was granted by PPDA to carry out development on land covered, amongst others, by survey no. 803 - Later, construction was raised by respondent no.3 inter alia on land covered by survey no. 803 but the same was challenged inter alia by petitioner and by order dt. 25-4-2000, HC upheld challenge and construction made by respondent no.3 was ordered to be demolished and land resumed - Aggrieved, respondent no.3 challenged said order of HC before SC but the same was dismissed with directions - Later, Amendment Act was passed by Legislative Assembly of Goa and original agreement was amended by supplementary agreement - Hence, instant petition by petitioner, which is NGO, challenging validity of Amendment Act - Whether State Legislature is competent to enact the State Amendment Act in view of the earlier decision of SC dt.20-9-2009.

Held, ss. 41(6) to 41(9) introduced in 1894 Act by Goa State Amendment renders ineffective cl. 4(viii) of the Agreement executed by the parties u/s. 41 of 1894 Act. With cl.4(viii) being deleted the embargo on constructions on the acquired land is removed. It was said cl.4(viii) and its legal effect, in view of s.42, that was the basis of the Court's decision dt.20-1-2009 holding the construction raised by respondent no.3 on the acquired land to be illegal and contrary to 1894 Act. Once cl.4(viii) is removed the basis of the earlier judgment stands extinguished. It was hence, within domain of the legislature to being about the Amendment Act with retrospective effect, the Legislative field also being in the Concurrent List, namely, Entry No.42 of List III of Seventh Schedule to Constitution. Further, s. 41 of 1894 Act and the terms of the agreement executed there under are silent with regard to modification, variation or deletion, subtraction of the terms of the agreement. The State Amendment, by no means, sets the law in a collision course with the Central/Principal enactment. Further, if the provisions of the State Amendment are to be tested on the anvil of the finding of SC that the acquisition in the instant case is u/s. 40(1)(aa) of 1894 Act, the deletion of the relevant clause of the agreement as made by said amendment could appear to be really in furtherance of the purpose of the acquisition u/1894 Act. Hence, there is no repugnancy between 1894 Act and State Amendment as urged by petitioner. Petition dismissed.
