Chairman & Managing Director, India Airlines v Binod Kumar Sinha & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21388; (2001) 8 SCC 722; (2002) SCC (L&S) 71; AIR 2001 SC 3988; 2001 (91) FLR 754; JT 2001 (8) SC 364; 2001(6) SCALE 633; 2001 (5) SLR 662; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 561; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2455

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Indian Airlines Employees (Aircraft Engineering Department) Service Regulations, 1959

Summary: Service - Constitution - Indian Airlines Employees (Aircraft Engineering Department) Service Regulations, 1959, regn. 13(b) - Air Corporations Act, 1953 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 16, 21 and 23 - Petition was filed before HC challenging the validity of regn. 13(b), substituted by notification providing that no employee of a national carriers can resign from employment except after a notice of six months - Original Regn. 13(b) provided that a Pilot could resign from his service after giving 30 days notice or offering one month basic pay to the employer in lieu of the said 30 days notice - HC quashed the regulation - Hence, present appeal - Whether the circular issued offends any of the provisions of the Constitution or such embargo has any nexus with the safety of the air craft operation and has any relevance to the air transport services? - Held, if a person is in employment he is certainly subject to certain terms and conditions and he can quit his employment under those terms and conditions only which cannot be stated to be violative of arts. 14, 16, 21 and 23, much less art. 19(1)(g) of the Constitution unless on examination such conditions are held invalid - In the present case, if in violation of conditions imposed by circular, employees of the national carriers leave their employment to join employment in any other air service it would affect the operation of the air carriers and, therefore, the circular was issued to give effect to that objective underlying regn. 13(b) and cannot term it to be invalid - If the provision of regn. 13(b) is valid in law and in violation of which an employee seeks to join employment with an air taxi operator, the restriction in the circular cannot be an infringement of his rights arising u/arts. 14, 16, 19(1)(g), 21 and 23 of the Constitution - Further, what should be the duration of a reasonable notice in such circumstances is a matter to be decided in each case depending upon the exigencies, needs or necessities and the essentiality of the service concerned - In the present case, no such exercise was done by the HC to find out whether regn. 13(b) is valid or not - Order made by the HC needs to be set aside - Appeals allowed.
