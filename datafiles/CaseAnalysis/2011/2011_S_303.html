State Of U.P. & Ors. v Rakesh Kumar Keshari & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

04 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 307; (2011) 5 SCC 341; 2011 (8) ADJ 71; AIR 2011 SC 1705; 2011 (2) AllCJ 1604; 2011 (3) AWC 3153; 2011 (3) ESC 413; 2011 (130) FLR 1; JT 2011 (5) SC 343; 2011 (2) LCD 1252; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 791; 2011(5) SCALE 303; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 1060; 2011 (3) SLR 690; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1259

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Advocates & Judges; Service

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Administrative - Service - Uttar Pradesh Legal Remembrancer's Manual, rr. 7.06, 7.07 - Assistant District Government Counsel (Criminal) (ADGC) - Extension of tenure - Entitlement - District Judge, after being satisfied with work and conduct of respondents, recommended to District Magistrate to extend respondents' terms in posts of ADGC - District Magistrate sent recommendation to State Government to extend terms - Post of ADGC on which respondents were working were advertised by District Magistrate - In pursuance of said advertisement, respondents also applied for vacancy - District Judge submitted two lists of candidates to District Magistrate - District Magistrate submitted panel to Special Secretary and Upper Legal Remembrance (appellants) for making appointment - Appellants returned panel list without assigning any reason and directed District Magistrate to advertise posts of ADGC afresh for appointment - District Magistrate issued fresh advertisement - Respondents challenged appellants' direction to District Magistrate for re-advertising posts - HC set aside appellants' order holding that unless panel submitted by District Magistrate in consultation with District Judge was rejected by appellants on some disclosed grounds, it was not open to appellants to ask District Magistrate to constitute revised panel - Whether appellant's direction to District Magistrate to re-advertise post in question was legal - Held, percentage of success in cases handled by 1st respondent was nil whereas in case of 2nd respondent percentage was only 17 and therefore when matter of renewal of their tenure was considered by appellant, appellant had decided not to extend terms of those Government Counsel whose success rate was very low - On so many occasions respondents had approached Government for extension of their terms and many recommendations were forwarded to Government but since performance of respondents was not found to be satisfactory, a decision was taken not to renew their terms and to issue advertisement for selection of better candidates - In view of rr. 7.06 and 7.07 of L.R. Manual as well as in view of poor performance of respondents as ADGC, right of State Government to engage, disengage and renew terms of its Counsel and Law Officers in keeping with need to best safeguard public interest and monetary considerations, suitability of incumbent and interest of Government as client, would have to be upheld - Decision of State Government not to accept recommendation made by District Magistrate should not be said to be arbitrary - ADGC were not only officers of Court but also representatives of State - ADGC represented interest of general public before a Court of law - However, in matter of engagement of ADGC a concept of public office did not come into play and choice was that of Government and none could claim a right to be appointed - Thus it was not open to respondents to file writ petition u/art. 226 of Constitution for compelling appellants to utilize their services as Advocates irrespective of choice of State - It was for State to select its own Counsel - In view of poor performance of respondents in handling/conducting criminal cases, HC committed a grave error in giving direction to District Magistrate to forward better particulars of 10 candidates whose names were included in two panels prepared by District Judge and in setting aside order calling upon District Magistrate to send another panel/list for appointment to posts of ADGC - Impugned HC order, therefore, was set aside - Appeal allowed.
