Devdutta And Ors. Etc. v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 425; (1991) Supp2 SCC 553; 1992 (19) ATC 154; 1991 (1) LLJ 492; 1991 (1) LLN 1094; 1990(2) SCALE 692; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 154; 1991 (1) SLJ 168; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (General Conditions of Service) Rules 1961 - Madhya Pradesh Sales Tax Subordinate Class II Executive Service Recruitment rules 1966 - Determination of seniority - Held, a rule of seniority namely that 'the courts will only insist that rules so formulated should be reasonable, just and equitable' with result that if they meet this requirement court will not interfere, apply even to a case of executive action of determining seniority if above test is satisfied - It is also relevant to point out that no statutory rule has been brought to our notice which may run counter to decision mentioned above taken by Govt. - Two rules are (i) Madhya Pradesh Civil Services (General Conditions of Service) Rules, 1961, and (ii) Madhya Pradesh Sales Tax Subordinate Class III Executive Service Recruitment Rules, 1966 In so far as 1966 rules are concerned, they do not contain any provision about seniority - 1961 rules, no doubt, deal with seniority in r. 12 but having gone through said rule case of absorbed sales tax inspectors does not fall under any of categories enumerated therein - Decision of State Govt. did not satisfy above test and was either arbitrary or discriminatory - It was not a fit case for interference by High Court in exercise to its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution with decision of State Govt. - Appeal allowed.
