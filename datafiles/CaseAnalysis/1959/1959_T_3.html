SHRI JAGDISH MILLS Limited v COMMISSIONER of Income Tax, BOMBAY NORTH, KUTCH

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 193; AIR 1959 SC 1160; [1959] 37 ITR 114; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 4(1)(a) - Income within Jurisdiction - Profit and gains - Course of Business - Cheque Payment - Cheques received by appellant in Baroda and these cheques were cashed and proceeds thereof were received by appellant in either Bombay or Ahmedabad and accordingly income, profits and gains were received by appellant within the taxable territories and Income Tax authorities charged Tax which was affirmed by Tribunal and HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Income accrued within Jurisdiction and liable to be taxed - Held, yes - Stipulation in contract between appellant and Govt. was that payment would be made by cheques -According to course of business parties must have intended that cheques should be sent by post which was usual and normal agency for transmission of such articles - Hence, Income accrued within Jurisdiction and liable to be taxed - Appeal dismissed.
