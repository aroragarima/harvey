Damodar Valley Corporation v K. K. Kar

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 209; (1974) 1 SCC 141; AIR 1974 SC 158; 1974 (3) CTR 101; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 240

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contract & Commercial -Arbitration Act,1940, ss.9(6)and 73 - Contract Act,1872, s.62- Contract with arbitration clause - Plea of full and final settlement between parties - Jurisdiction of court to decide question as to whether there was full or final settlement or not - Distinction between full and final settlement of claim under contract and termination of contract - (A)Whether court on an application u/ss 9 (b) and 33 of Arbitration Act was entitled to enquire into truth and validity of averment as to whether there was or was not final settlement on ground that if that was proved, it would bar a reference to arbitration in as much as arbitration clause itself would perish? - Held, that question whether there had been a full and final settlement of a claim under contract was itself a dispute arising 'upon' or 'in relation to' or in connection with contract A claim for damages was a dispute or difference which arose between respondent and appellant, and was 'upon' or in relation to' or 'in connection with' contract, and reference to arbitrator by respondent was not barred - (B) Whether a dispute can be referred to arbitration where subsistence of contract is disputed? - As contract subsists for determination of rights and obligations of parties, arbitration clause also survives. This is not a case where plea is that contract is void, illegal or fraudulent etc, in which case, entire contract along with arbitration clause is non existent or voidable - Further held, where dispute between parties is that contract itself does not subsist either as a result of its being substituted by a new contract or by rescission or alteration, that dispute cannot be referred to arbitration as arbitration clause itself would perish if averment is found to be valid - Appeal partly allowed.
