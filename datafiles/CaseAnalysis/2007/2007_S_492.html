Brij Lal (Dead) By Lrs. and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1368; (2007) 14 SCC 332; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 208; JT 2008 (1) SC 55; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 271; 2007(14) SCALE 495; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 574

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Keywords: Review Application, Punjab Security of Land Tenures Rules, 1956, Allotment Orders, Punjab Security of Land Tenures (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1962

Summary: Punjab Security of Land Tenures Act, 1953, s. 10 A(b); Punjab Security of Land Tenures (Amendment and Validation) Act, 1962 - Held, High Court has not recorded any finding as to whether after a long lapse of time, the action taken by the non official respondents in challenging the order in favour of the appellants disentitleS them from any relief - Though the expression used in s. 18(6) of the Haryana Act is 'at any time', obviously it has to be a reasonable time and if action is taken to impugn the order after long passage of time, the Court has to examine whether it would be proper to grant a relief prayer for the same - Remitted matter to the High Court - Appeals partly allowed.
