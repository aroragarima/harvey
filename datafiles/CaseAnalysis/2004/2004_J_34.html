Jamshed Hormusji Wadia v Board of Trustees, Port of Mumbai and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 5; (2004) 3 SCC 214; AIR 2004 SC 1815; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 125; 2004 (176) E.L.T. 24; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 41; JT 2004 (1) SC 232; 2004(1) SCALE 341; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 483; 2004 (2) SLT 904; 2004 (1) Supreme 975

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor, Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879, Bombay Port Trust Act, 1873

Summary: [A.] Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 - Bombay Port Trust Act, 1873 - Bombay Port Trust Act, 1879 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 1 r. 8, O. 41 r. 22, s. 381 - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Lease - Rent - Evaluation - Termination of tenancy - Notice - Status of BPT as a landlord - Held, matter between parties has to be decided by treating Compromise Proposals dated 13 th August 1991 as base Any going behind would unsettle settled issues - Expressly or by necessary implication - Directions issued - Maintainability of petition - Further held, issue which is sought to be re-agitated stands concluded by earlier order of remand passed by Court respondent cannot now, in second round of appeal to this Court, be permitted to urge such pleas as it could have urged in earlier round or which it urged and was not accepted by this Court.
