Salim Zia v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 464; (1979) SCC (Cr) 568; (1979) 2 SCC 648; AIR 1979 SC 391; 1979 CRLJ 323; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 394

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Right to Private Defence - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 25 and 27 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 717 - Conviction of appellant u/s. 302, IPC, 1860, and s. 27 of Arms Act, 1959 - On appeal, High Court set aside acquittal of appellant - Present appeal - (A) Whether appellant was protected by right of private defence of person or property? - It is true that burden on an accused person to establish plea of self defence is not as onerous as one which lies on prosecution - Accused need not establish plea to hilt and may discharge his onus by establishing a mere preponderance of probabilities either by laying basis for that plea in cross-examination of prosecution witnesses or by adducing defence evidence - Held, appellant has not discharged that burden - (B) Power of HC to review and reverse acquittal order - HC in an appeal against an order of acquittal u/s. 417 of CrPC, 1973, has full power to review at large evidence on which order of acquittal was founded and to reach conclusion that upon evidence, order of acquittal should be reversed - Held, HC was fully justified in reversing Trial Court order of acquittal of appellant which was made on basis of surmises and conjectures and convicting him specially when prosecution story that appellant fired at deceased without any justifiable provocation was established to hilt by evidence of a number of prosecution witnesses who even according to Sessions Judge gave a true account of occurrence - Appeals dismissed.
