Kendriya Karamchari Sehkari Greh Nirman Samiti Limited and others v State Of Uttar Pradesh And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2586; (2009) 1 SCC 754; AIR 2009 SC 1677; 2009 (1) AWC 615; 2009 (4) MLJ(SC) 492; 2009 (2) MPLJ 532; 2008(15) SCALE 293; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 810

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 18 and 28A - Re-determination of amount of compensation - Land acquisition proceedings was initiated - Compensation was awarded to the land-owners - Land-owners being not satisfied by amount of award sought reference u/s. 18 of the Act - Compensation was enhanced by the Reference Court - Appellant could not make reference along with other land-owners, therefore, filed an application u/s. 28A of the Act praying benefit allowable to him on the basis of the order passed by the Reference Court - Meanwhile, order of Reference Court was challenged by HC - HC stayed the operation of the order of Reference Court - Appellant being aggrieved by the non-disposal of his application u/s. 28A of the Act, because of Government Orders, challenged the validity of both the Government Orders before HC and also direction to decide his application u/s. 28A of the Act - HC dismissed the writ petition - Whether Collector was right in not deciding the application filed by appellant u/s. 28A of the Act? - Held, once Reference Court decides the matter and enhances the compensation, a person who is otherwise eligible to similar relief and who has not sought reference, may apply u/s. 28A of the Act - However, if Reference Court decides the matter and the State or acquiring body challenges such enhanced amount of compensation and the matter is pending either before the HC or before SC, the Collector would be within his power to keep the application u/s. 28A of the Act pending till the matter is finally decided - It is, in the light of the said circumstance that the State of UP issued two government orders - In the present case, Collector was right in not deciding the application in view of the fact that the order passed by the Reference Court was challenged before the HC - Validity of Government Orders upheld - Appeals partly allowed.
