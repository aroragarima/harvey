Malik Brothers v Narendra Dadhich and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1391; (1999) 6 SCC 552; AIR 1999 SC 3211; 1999 (37) ALR 313; 1999 (4) CompLJ 119; JT 1999 (6) SC 266; 2000 (1) MLJ 67; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 732

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Reference of dispute to arbitration - Decision as to - Appellant highest bidder could not deposit balance amount - Due to non-deposit of balance amount, earlier amount deposited stood forfeited which was challenged by appellant - Refer all disputes pertaining to land, which was subject matter of auction for arbitration - Said decision of Improvement Trust challanged in High Court by writ petition - Held, not to be characterised as not bona fide without any material - Setting aside of reference and award by HC by entertaining public interest litigation at instance of person not having any interest - Not proper.
