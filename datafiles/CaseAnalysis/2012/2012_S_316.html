Modern Dental College and Research Centre and others v State of Madhya Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, Balbir Singh Chauhan, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 585; (2012) 4 SCC 707; 2012 (4) AWC 3482; 2012 (2) JabLJ 181; JT 2012 (4) SC 69; 2012 (4) MLJ(SC) 640; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 685; 2012 (3) SLR 854; 2012 (2) UPLBEC 873

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Niji Vyavsayik Shikshan Sanstha (Pravesh Ka Viniyaman Avam Shulk Ka Nirdharan) Adhiniyam, 2007, Madhya Pradesh Private Medical and Dental Under Graduate Course Entrance Examination Rules, 2011, M.P. Admission Rules, 2008

Summary: Education - Practice & Procedure - Administrative - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Private Medical and Dental Under Graduate Course Entrance Examination Rules, 2011 - M.P. Admission Rules, 2008 - NRI Seats - Admission - Mode of admission - Unfilled Seats - Common Entrance - Ultra Vires - Writ Petitions were filed challenging Constitutional validity Act and Rules framed by State Govt. - HC, through its judgment repelled challenge to Act and Rules but declared that provisions of Rule 10(2)(iii) of 2009 as ultra vires, also held that Judgment would not affect Common Entrance Test already conducted by VYAPAM for year 2009-10 - Aggrieved Civil appeal was filed before DB, which had prima facie found that provisions of Act, handing over entire selection process to State Govt. or agencies appointed by State Govt. for undergraduate, graduate and postgraduate medical/dental colleges and fee fixation was contrary to and inconsistent with principles laid down - Govt. framed entrance examination rules incorporating r. 5 with regard to unfilled NRI seats as per orders passed by SC, wherein it was held that unfilled NRI seats had to be transferred to general pool to be filled up on basis of merit of candidates in State-level common entrance test - Applicants noticing judgments would seriously affects rights of unaided educational institutions in matter of filling up of unfilled NRI seats, for appropriate modification/clarification filed interlocutory applications - Hence instant applications - Whether unfilled NRI seats were to be transferred to general pool and be shared equally to be filled up on basis of Common Entrance Test conducted by the State level Committee VYAPAM or by Common Entrance Test conducted by Association of Private Dental and Medical Colleges APDMC - Held, principles laid down by a larger Benches of this Court, in matter of filling up of NRI seats were not correctly understood or applied by this Court while interpreting r. 8 of 2008, Rules, finding recorded in previous case that unfilled seats in NRI quota in unaided professional colleges should be treated as a part of general pool and be shared equally by State and unaided professional colleges goes contrary to principles laid down by eleven-Judges Bench as well as Judgments rendered by three Judges Bench - Wrong interpretation given by was seen incorporated in r. 5 of 2011, as well, which could not be legally sustained - Hence, overruling direction given by 2 Judges of this Court in previous judgments, SC, held that it was open to unaided professional educational institutions to fill up unfilled NRI seats for year 2012-13 and for succeeding years through entrance test conducted by them till disposal of appeal subject to conditions laid down strictly on basis of merits - Applications allowed.
