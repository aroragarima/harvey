Commissioner of Customs (Port), Chennai v Toyota Kirloskar Motor Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1115; (2007) 5 SCC 371; 2007 (213) E.L.T. 4; JT 2007 (8) SC 194; 2007(8) SCALE 151; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 94

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Importation, Valuation Of Goods, Customs Valuation (Determination Of Price Of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, Buying Commission, Place Of Importation, Bill Of Export, Delivery At The Time And Place Of Importation, Rate Of Exchange, Time And Place Of Importation Or Exportation, Importation Into

Summary: Customs - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(22) and 2(23) - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Customs Valuation (Determination of Price of Imported Goods) Rules, 1988, rr. 4 and 9(1)(c) - What should be valuation of capital goods and parts imported by respondent from Toyota Motor Corporation for manufacture of automobile in India? - R. 9 clearly states that charges or costs envisaged there under were not to be included in value of imported goods subject to satisfying requirement of proviso that charges were distinguishable from price actually paid or payable for imported goods - Held, CESTAT cannot be said to have committed any error in arriving at its decision in impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
