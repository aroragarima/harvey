Mohinder Kumar Etc v State of Haryana and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 262; (1985) 4 SCC 221; AIR 1986 SC 244; 1986 (1) CCC 389; 1986 (1) RCR(Rent) 74; 1985 (2) RentLR 644; 1985(2) SCALE 795; [1985] Supp2 S.C.R. 859; 1985 UJ 978

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Rent Control - Haryana Urban (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1973, s. 1(3) - Notification D/- 31-01-1973 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Provision of s. 1(3) and notification whether constitutionally valid on touchstone of art. 14? - (i) Whether the provision which seeks to distinctively between buildings which were constructed before commencement of Act and buildings where were constructed thereafter, have reasonable nexus with the object of the provision? - Held, yes - Giving concessions, exemptions and protection to newly constructed buildings has nexus with the object and purpose of the enactment - Constitutional validity of s. 1(3) and notification, upheld - Appeal dismissed.
