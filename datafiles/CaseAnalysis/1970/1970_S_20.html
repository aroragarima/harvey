Gajanan And Ors. v Seth Brindaban

Supreme Court of India

20 July 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

I.D. Dua, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 322; (1970) 2 SCC 360; AIR 1970 SC 2007; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Pradesh Moneylender's Act, 1934 s. 11-F - Instant appeal was directed against the money decree passed in favor of the respondent against the appellant in the suit filed against them by the respondent for recovery of mortgage money - Whether, on the fact that the instant transaction was an isolated transaction of lending money, HC had rightly passed the money decree to the respondent - Held, HC had rightly passed the money decree to the respondent since, s. 11-F of the Act on its plain reading only prohibits the carrying on of the business of moneylending in any district without holding a valid registration certificate in respect of that district and it did not prohibit and, therefore, did not invalidate an isolated transaction of lending money - Appeal dismissed.
