Shivgiri Associates and others v Metso Mineral (India) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 535; (2014) 12 SCC 366; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 3735; 2015 (2) BC 38; 2014 (6) Bom.C.R. 30; 2014 (4) CompLJ 186; 2014 (4) JCC (NI) 220; JT 2014 (9) SC 292; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3323; 2015 (177) PLR 2; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 264; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 121; 2014(9) SCALE 477; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 279

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Territorial jurisdiction - Determination of - Instant appeal filed challenging the order of the Single Judge of the Punjab & Haryana HC holding that since the notice as contemplated in s. 138 of the Act, had been dispatched from Gurgaon, Haryana and additionally, a response thereto was dispatched to and received at Gurgaon, Courts at Gurgaon possessed jurisdiction to entertain and decide the complaint - Whether Courts at Gurgaon possessed jurisdiction to entertain and decide the complaint -

Held, issuance of the notice had relevance to the question of criminal territorial jurisdiction u/s. 138 of the Act - Judgment of SC in Dashrath Rupsingh Rathod v. State of Maharashtra, 2014 Indlaw SC 494, applied - - In the instant case, dishonoured cheques were drawn on the appellant's bank at Bangalore - Subsequently, on presentation of the cheques for encashment by the respondent through its bankers at Bangalore, they were dishonoured - Hence, Courts at Gurgaon did not possess territorial jurisdiction to entertain the present proceedings u/s. 138 of the Act because, on the instructions of the respondent, a legal notice of demand emanated from that city - Complaint be returned to the complainant/respondent for refilling in the appropriate Court at Bangalore, Karnataka - If the complaint was re-filed in the appropriate Court in Bangalore within 30 days, it would be deemed to have been filed within limitation - Appeal allowed.
