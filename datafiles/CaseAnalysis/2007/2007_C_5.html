The Commissioner, Karnataka Housing Board v C. Muddaiah

Supreme Court of India

07 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 882; (2007) 7 SCC 689; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 748; AIR 2007 SC 3100; 2007(6) ALL MR 403; 2007 (4) AWC 4028; JT 2007 (10) SC 609; 2008 (1) KarLJ 249; 2007(10) SCALE 625; 2007 (6) SCJ 938; [2007] 9 S.C.R. 784; 2007 (6) SLR 157

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Karnataka State Civil Services (Regulation of Promotion, Pay and Pension) Act, 1973

Summary: Karnataka State Civil Services (Regulation of Promotion, Pay and Pension) Act, 1973 - Direction to the Board to reassign seniority of the writ-petitioner by placing him above respondent Nos. 2 to 34 and to grant 'other consequential benefits' - Grievance that petitioner was not granted consequential benefits - Contempt petitions dismissed - Writ petition against also dismissed by Single Bench - Division Bench allowed appeal - Appeal against - Held, in spite of clear direction issued by a competent Court, no payment was made and an express order was passed to the effect that the writ- petitioner would not be entitled to pay as he had not worked - Division Bench was justified in granting the prayer - No ground to interfere with the order passed by the Division Bench of the High Court - Appeal dismissed.
