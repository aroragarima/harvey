State of U. P. and others v Dr. Dinesh Singh Chauhan

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, T.S. Thakur, D.Y. Chandrachud

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 621; (2016) 9 SCC 749; AIR 2016 SC 3841; 2016 (118) ALR 527; 2016 (5) AWC 5376; JT 2016 (8) SC 201; 2016 (6) MLJ 582; 2016(8) SCALE 16

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, Medical Council Of India, Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, Service Quota Act, 2008, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: First set of appeals (arising from SLP (C) Nos: 13832, 13872, 14427 and 15154-56 of 2016),

(A) Education - Service - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 20, 33 - Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regns. 9, 9(2) - PG courses admissions - In-service candidates -Reservation - Petitioners/appellants who were members of Provincial Medical Health Services in State filed writ petitions before HC claiming that they were entitled to be considered for admission in PG Courses against 30% quota for in-service candidates - HC quashed G.O. dt. 28-2-2014 and directed that admissions to PG "Degree" Courses be proceeded strictly on merits amongst candidates who have obtained requisite minimum marks in the common entrance examination in question - HC further held that as per regn. 9 of the Regulations, the in-service candidates who have worked in remote and difficult areas in State would be eligible for weightage of marks as incentive at the rate of 10% of marks obtained for each year of service in such areas upto the maximum of 30% marks obtained in National Eligibility-cum-Entrance Test - Hence, instant appeals - Whether HC exceeded its jurisdiction in setting aside G.O. dt. 28-02-2014 providing for reservation to in-service candidates, when writ petition filed by in-service candidates was limited to equate them with in-service candidates who had the experience of working in remote or difficult areas.

Held, regn. 9 of the Regulations even if read liberally does not provide for reservation for in-service candidates, but only of giving a weightage in the form of incentive marks as specified to the class of in- service candidates (who have served in notified remote and difficult areas in the State). Further, weightage of marks so allotted is required to be reckoned while preparing the merit list of candidates. The admission process in the present case is governed by the Regulations which have come into force from Academic Year 2013-14. There is nothing in this regn. to even remotely indicate that a separate channel for admission to in-service candidates must be provided, at least in respect of PG "Degree" Courses. If the Regulations intended a similar separate channel for in-service candidates even in respect of PG "Degree" Courses, that position would have been made clear in regn. 9 of the Regulations itself. In absence thereof, it must be presumed that a separate channel for in-service candidates is not permissible for admission to PG "Degree" Courses. Thus, State Govt. in law, had no authority to issue a G.O. dt. 28-02-2014, to provide to the contrary. However, in instant case, Medical Council of India itself has framed a regn. predicating one merit list by adding the weightage of marks assigned to in-service candidates for determining their merit in the NEET. Therefore, regn. as brought into force, after successive amendments is an attempt to undo the mischief. HC was fully justified in setting aside the said G.O. dt. 28-02-2014, being contrary to the mandate of regn. 9 of the Regulations as applicable from Academic Year 2013-14. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Education - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15, 16, 32 - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 20, 33 - Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regns. 9, 9(2) - Incentive marks - Excessive - Eligibility - Whether the norm specified in regn. 9 of the Regulations regarding incentive marks can be termed as excessive and unreasonable.

Held, regn. 9 of the Regulations is a complete Code. It prescribes basis for determining eligibilities of candidates including the method to be adopted for determining the inter se merit, on the basis of one merit list of candidates appearing in the same NEET including by giving commensurate weightage of marks to the in-service candidates. Such provision has been brought into force in larger public interest and not merely to provide institutional preference or for that matter to create separate channel for the in-service candidate, muchless reservation. It is unfathomable as to how such a provision can be said to be unreasonable or irrational. In instant case, there is no separate channel for two categories in respect of PG "Degree" Courses, as was the dispensation in that case. On the other hand, only one merit list is prepared and all available seats in terms of regn. 9 of the Regulations are thrown open to both categories of candidates. The Authorities are therefore, obliged to continue with the admission process strictly in conformity with regn. 9 of the Regulations. The fact that most of the direct candidates who have secured higher marks in the NEET than the in-service candidates, may not be in a position to get a subject or college of their choice, and are likely to secure a subject or college not acceptable to them, cannot be the basis to question the validity of proviso to Cl. IV of regn. 9 of the Regulations. The purpose behind proviso is to encourage graduates to join as medical officers and serve in notified remote and difficult areas of State. Mere hypothesis that State Govt. may take an improper decision whilst notifying the area as remote and difficult cannot be the basis to hold that regn. 9 of the Regulations and in particular proviso to Cl. IV is unreasonable. Thus, procedure evolved in regn. 9 of the Regulations in general and the proviso to Cl. (IV) in particular is just, proper and reasonable and also fulfill the test of art.14 of Constitution being in larger public interest. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Education - Service - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 20, 33 - Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regns. 9, 9(2) - Admissions - Academic year - Prospective effect - Whether the arrangement directed in terms of order dt. 12-05-2016 by SC should have prospective effect or also apply to admissions for academic year 2015-16.

Held, HC was justified in quashing the stated G.O. providing for reservation to in- service candidates, being violative of regn. 9 of the Regulations as in force. However, SC modify the operative direction given by HC and direct that admission process for Academic Year 2016-17 onwards to PG Degree Course in the State should proceed as per regn. 9 of the Regulations including by giving incentive marks to eligible in-service candidates in terms of proviso to Cl. IV of regn. 9 of the Regulations (equivalent to third proviso to regn. 9(2) of the Old Regulations reproduced in the interim order dt. 12-05-2016). The operative order of HC to bring it in conformity with the direction contained in the interim order dt. 12-05-2016 but to be made applicable to academic year 2016-17 onwards on the basis of regn. 9 of the Regulations as in force. SC is conscious of the fact that such arrangement is likely to affect some of the direct candidates, if not a large number of candidates whose applications were already processed by competent Authority for concerned PG Degree Course for academic year 2016-17. However, their admissions cannot be validated in breach of or disregarding the mandate of regn. 9 of the Regulations as in force. Appeals disposed of.

Second set of appeal (arising from SLP (Civil) No.15529/2016)

(D) Education - Service - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 20, 33 - Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regns. 9, 9(2) - PG courses admissions - Eligibility - Medical Officers of State Medical Colleges filed writ petitions seeking admission to PG Degree Courses as they were also eligible candidates in terms of regn. 9 of the Regulations and should have been considered at the time of preparing a fresh merit list - DB dismissed petitions holding that it was not feasible for Dept. to consider the claim of eligible in-service candidates who had not submitted applications/documents before the notified date - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, SC have no hesitation in upholding the view taken byHC that the direction to prepare fresh merit list vide interim order dt. 12-05-2016 was in respect of only such eligible in-service candidates as had submitted applications for admission to PG Degree Courses for the relevant academic year within stipulated time. The direction in the interim order was not to consider all similarly placed persons (eligible in-service candidates) irrespective of whether they had made applications for admission to Post Graduate Degree Courses or otherwise. Appeals disposed of.

Third set of proceedings being Writ Petition (Civil) No. 372/2016

(E) Education - Service - Indian Medical Council Act, 1956, ss. 20, 33 - Service Quota Act, 2008, s. 5(4) - Post Graduate Medical Education Regulations, 2000, regns. 9, 9(2) - PG courses - Inter-se merit - Determination - Petitioners filed writ petitions to take admissions to various PG "Degree" Courses in State and who claim to have been affected by the dispensation specified in the interim order passed by SC dt.12-05-2016 - Petitioners contended that they have been dislodged from the respective PG Degree Courses in which they were already admitted in the concerned medical colleges and even started pursuing their courses.

Held, SC held regn. 9 of the Regulations to be a complete Code and a provision for determining inter-se merit of the candidates including by giving weightage of marks as incentive to eligible in-service candidates who have worked in notified remote or difficult areas in the State, which is just, reasonable and necessary in larger public interest. SC make it clear that SC have not examined the correctness of the fresh merit list prepared by concerned Authority in terms of interim order dt. 12-05-2016. If any candidate is aggrieved on account of wrong placement in the fresh merit list or being in violation of instant decision, will be free to question the same by way of appropriate proceedings. That challenge can be considered on its own merit. Appeals disposed of.
