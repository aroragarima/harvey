Â Shitla Sahai Srivastava v North-Eastern Railway, Gorakhpur (By General Manager)

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Satyanarayana Raju, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1966 SC 1197; 1966 (13) FLR 407; 1966 (2) LLJ 755; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 61

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Selection - Provisional - Deletion of name from list - Challenge - After examination and interview, Board prepared panel for promotions - Appellant was given twelfth rank in panel - Final list of persons brought on panel was published in Railway Gazette and appellant was shown at serial no. 13 in that panel - But note was appended to notification stating that selection of appellant and five others was provisional - Chief Commercial Superintendent under orders of General Manager deleted name of appellant and five others - Appellant filed petition, impugning validity of order by which his name had been removed from panel - Single Judge dismissed petition filed by appellant and held that deletion of appellant's name from panel did not amounted to reduction in rank u/art. 311 of the Constitution - Appellant preferred appeal which was summarily dismissed by Division Bench - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether deletion of appellant's name from panel amounted to reduction in rank within meaning of art. 311 of the Constitution - Held, in panel prepared by Selection Board word 'provisional' was specifically noted against name of appellant which clearly showed that he did not acquired right to post - Thus, deletion of his name from panel did not attract provisions of art. 311 of the Constitution - Further, striking out of appellants name from panel affected his chances of future promotion, it was point of substance and should be upheld - As authorities should before striking out name of appellant from panel was to give him opportunity of showing cause as to why his name should not be struck off from panel and order could only be made after giving opportunity of being heard - However, it was true that respondent made incorrect assessment of anticipated number of vacancies, but fact remained that his inclusion in panel was expressly stated to be provisional - Therefore, appellant could not complain of any infraction of guarantee given by the Constitution to Government servants - Appeal dismissed.
