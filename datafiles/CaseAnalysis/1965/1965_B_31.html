Bhikari v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 108; AIR 1966 SC 1; 1966 CRLJ 63; 1966 (2) SCJ 281; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Insanity

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 84, 302, 307 and 324 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 87, 88 and 342 - Appellant armed with a sickle attacks. - Appellant rips open child's chest with sickle as a result of which she dies almost immediately - Mens rea being an essential ingredient of all offences with which appellant was charged his conviction with respect to any of them cannot be sustained for simple reason that no intention to cause death or to cause any injury whether resulting in death or not could possibly be attributed to a person who, when he committed acts, was insane- Whether Burden of proof is on prosecution to prove or to disprove exceptions invoked by accused? - Where intention has to be established, burden of proving offence lies on prosecution - Where plea of insanity is invoked by accused burden of proof shifts on him to establish it - Held, s. 105 of Evidence Act, 1872 places upon accused person burden of proving exception relied upon by him - Appeal dismissed.
