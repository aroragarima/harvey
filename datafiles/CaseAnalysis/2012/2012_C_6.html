Dipak Shubhashchandra Meh v C.B.I. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 38; (2012) 4 SCC 134; AIR 2012 SC 949; [2012] 170 Comp Cas 244; 2012 CRLJ 1664; 2012 (1) JCC 757; JT 2012 (2) SC 439; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 99; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 870; 2012(2) SCALE 401; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 278

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Economic Offence - Defaulter - Medical grounds - Supplementary Charge Sheet - Due to non-payment of advances from various banks, complaints were filed against company as well as promoters and directors - Appellant Joint Managing Director of Company bail was rejected by HC - Hence instant appeal - Petitioner contended that Company was a Government of India recognized and had been accredited with many awards and had raised huge loans from several banks and due to non-payment of dues entire properties of appellant and their companies were attached by court orders/tribunals - There was no progress in the trial as much as supplementary charge sheet had been filed - Further appellant was diagnosed for hypertension and acidity by jail dispensary - Whether appellant is liable to be enlarged on bail considering exceptional case of high magnitude - Held, SC should not ordinarily, in exceptional cases, interfere with orders granting/refusing bail by HC - Court granting bail should exercise its discretion in a judicious manner and not as a matter of course - Though at stage of granting bail, a detailed examination of evidence and elaborate documentation of merits of case need not be undertaken, there is a need to indicate in such orders reasons for prima facie concluding why bail was being granted - SC had repeatedly held that when undertrial prisoners are detained in jail custody to an indefinite period, art 21 of the Constitution is violated - Appellant along with others were charged with economic offences of huge magnitude - Investigating Agency has completed investigation and submitted charge sheet including additional charge sheet, fact remains that necessary charges have not been framed, therefore, presence of appellant in custody may not be necessary for further investigation - In view of same, considering health condition as supported by documents including certificate of Medical Officer, Central Jail Dispensary, appellant is entitled to an order of bail pending trial on stringent conditions in order to safe guard interest of CBI - Appeal disposed of.
