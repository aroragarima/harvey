Prem Ram v Managing Director, Uttarakhand Pey Jal & Nirman Nigam, Dehradun and others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 995; 2015 (146) FLR 398; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 192; 2015 (5) SLR 435

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Establishment, Junior, Labour

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Reinstatement in Service - Denial of Regularisation Challenged - Appellant/workmen was initially appointed on daily wage basis by respondent/corporation and was terminated from service - Labour Court reinstated workmen into service with 50 percent back wages and continuity of service and HC affirmed award of Labour Court - Single Judge of HC dismissed writ petition of workmen against denial of regularisation of service by corporation and DB of HC dismissed appeal of workmen - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, what is important is that the appellant had been appointed as early as in the year 1988 and had by the time the decision of SC in Umadevi's (3) case pronounced, already completed more than 10 years service. Govt. has formulated rules for regularization of such daily-wagers no matter the same are the subject matter of a challenge before the HC. What is noteworthy is that neither the State Government nor the Jal Nigam has resented the idea of regularization of those who have served for over a decade. The rules providing for regularization are a sufficient enough indication of that fact. Therefore, there is no impediment in directing regularization of the service of the appellant on the analogy of his juniors with effect from the date his juniors were regularized and for the release of all retiral benefits in his favour on that basis by treating him to be in continuous service till the date of his superannuation. Hence, orders passed by HC are set aside and writ petition of employee is disposed of in terms of directions issued. Appeal allowed.
