Mathew P. Thomas v Kerala State Civil Supply Corpn. Ltd. and others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 162; (2003) SCC (L&S) 262; (2003) 3 SCC 263; AIR 2003 SC 1789; 2003 (2) AWC 1193; 2003 (1) CLR 790; 2003 (2) ESC 177; 2003 (96) FLR 1166; JT 2003 (2) SC 162; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 557; 2003 (1) KLT 874; 2003 LabIC 1165; 2003 (2) LLJ 272; 2003(2) SCALE 254; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 220; 2003 (2) SLJ 327; 2003 (3) SLR 150; 2003 (2) SLT 323; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1233

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - termination of - alleged misconduct of the appellant - when an order of termination can be treated as simplicitor and when it can be treated as punitive and when a stigma is said to be attached to an employee discharged during period of probation? - held, whether an order of terminating is simplicitor or punitive has ultimately to be decided having due regard to the facts and circumstances of each case. When, the facade of the termination order may be simplicitor but the real face behind it is to get rid of services of a probationer on the basis of misconduct, in such cases it becomes necessary to travel beyond the order of termination simplicitor to find out what in reality is the background and what weighed with the employer to terminate the services of a probationer. In the present case, circumstances indicate that his order of termination was simplicitor.
