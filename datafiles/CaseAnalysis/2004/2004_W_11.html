Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Bench

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Defence of India Rules, 1962, Mysore Sales Tax Act, Madras General Sales Tax Act, Sugar-Cane Act, 1934, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Tamil Nadu Public Men (Criminal Misconduct) Act, 1973, Uttar Pradesh Sugar Factories Control Act, 1938, Bihar Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1981, India (Central Government and Legislature) Act, 1946, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958, Madhya Pradesh Sugar (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1958, Madras Essential Articles Control & Requisitioning (Temporary Powers) Act, 1949, Andhra Pradesh (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1961, Punjab Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Sugar Industry (Protection) Act, 1932, Uttar Pradesh (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Rules, 1954, Cinemas Regulations Act, 1952, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of SUttar Pradeshply and Purchase) Order, 1954

Summary: U.P. Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953; Essential Commodities Act, s.3; U.P. (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Rules, 1954 - Competence of State Government to fix State Advised Price for purchase of sugarcane by an occupier of a sugar factory over and above minimum price fixed by Central Government; validity of procedure adopted for ensuring payment of aforesaid price to a sugarcane grower - Regulatory power possessed by State Government shall also include power to fix price of sugarcane - Central Government only fixes minimum price and it is always open to State Government to fix a higher price - Under enactments made by State Legislatures areas are reserved for sugar factories and canegrowers therein are compelled to supply sugarcane to them and therefore State Government has incidental power to fix price of sugarcane which will also be statutory price.
