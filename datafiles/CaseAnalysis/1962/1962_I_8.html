Indore Malwa United Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Bombay

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 334; AIR 1966 SC 1466; [1962] 45 ITR 210; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Place of assessment - Taxable territories - Appellant, a limited liability company, was the assessee company - Respondent was the Commissioner of Income-tax, Bombay - Assessee company carried on a business of manufacture and sale of textile goods - Manufacture was made at its mills in Indore which was an Indian State before integration and had its own law as to income-tax known as the Indore Industrial Tax Rules, 1927 - Sales of textile goods were made at various places, some inside and some outside the taxable territories of British India - For and up to the A/Y 1949-50, assessee company was treated as a non resident within the meaning of s. 4A of the 1922 Act - For the A/Ys 1950-51, and 1951-52, the accounting years were the calendar years 1946 and 1950 respectively - Indore became a part of the taxable territories within the meaning of the 1922 Act - Part of its profits up to the A/Y 1949-50, which was received in British India was subjected to tax together with its other income which accrued in British India, namely, interest on securities and interest on bank accounts - The course of the assessment proceedings for 1950-51, the assessee company claimed that it was entitled to a set off of the entire losses of the A/Y 1948-49 which was common ground before the Tribunal, and came to Rs. 5,19,590 and not merely the proportionate loss - Assessee company also claimed that the depreciation allowances of the two years 1948-49 and 1949-50 to which effect could not be given in those years and which had, therefore, to be carried forward should be added to the depreciation allowance of 1950-51 and be set off against the profits and gains of the assessee company liable to assessment in the A/Ys in question - It was alleged that the assessment of the assessee company for the A/Ys 1948-49 and 1949-50 was made both under the 1922 Act and under the Indore Industrial Tax Rules, 1927 - Two claims were made by the assessee company in the course of the assessment proceedings for 1950-51, one was with regard to the loss of Rs. 5,19,590/ and the assessee company's contention was that it was entitled to set off this loss against the profits made in its business in that year and it also contended that it was entitled to carry forward the unabsorbed depreciation into that year - Whether loss of profits and gains of assessee could be set off u/s.24 of the 1922 Act - Held, s. 24 of the 1922 Act was applicable only to such loss of profits and gains which if they had been profits and gains would have been assessable in British India or the taxable territories but in case of non residents income accruing or arising without British India or without taxable territories not being liable to be assessed and loss of such profits and gains not contemplated to be set off u/s. 24 of the 1922 Act - In the instant case, for determining the nature of the losses under consideration the relevant year was 1948-49, the year in which the losses occurred and the view that for the application of ss. (2) of s. 24, the losses must be such losses as could have been set off under sub-s.(1) of s. 24 of the 1922 Act - Loss amounting to Rs. 5,19,590/ was not such a loss as could have been set off either under ss.(1) or ss.(2) of s. 24 of the 1922 Act - Appeals dismissed.
