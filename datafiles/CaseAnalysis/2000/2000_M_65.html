Enforcement Directorate v M. Samba Siva Rao

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, Doraiswamy Raju, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 798; (2000) 5 SCC 431; (2000) SCC (Cr) 974; AIR 2000 SC 2128; 2000 (2) CCR 185; 2000 (2) Crimes 308; 2000 CRLJ 3170; 2000 (90) ECR 649; 2001 (134) E.L.T. 605; 2000 (2) JCC 535; JT 2000 (6) SC 514; 2000 (2) KLT 805; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 852; 2000(4) SCALE 505; 2000 (4) SLT 578; [2000] Supp1 S.C.R. 51; 2000 (4) Supreme 10

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Fema

Keywords: Non-compliance of summons

Summary: FEMA - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 5 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 40, 56 - Non-compliance of summons - Legality - HC held that provisions of s. 56 of the Act will not get attracted for violations of directions u/s. 40 of the Act - Complaints filed for such violation and cognizance taken in complaint cases were quashed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether refusal on part of person, who is summoned u/s. 40 of the Act to comply with directions under summons, would attract provisions of s. 56 of the Act.

Held, the summons issued u/s. 40 of the Act, if not obeyed, must be held to be a contravention of the provisions of the Act and at any rate, a contravention of a direction issued under the Act, and therefore, such contravention would squarely come within the ambit of s. 56 of the Act. HC in the impugned Judgment is of the view that s. 56 of the Act would bring within its sweep only such violation or contravention which under different provisions of the Act have been deemed to be a contravention under the Act like ss. 43(4), 8(1) r/w s.45(1), s. 49 of the Act and so on. Such interpretation given, would make the power to summons u/s. 40 of the Act meaningless and the provisions of sub-section(3) of s. 40 of the Act making it bounden duty for the persons summoned to attend purposeless. Violation or contravention of the directions given under the summons u/s. 40 of the Act would come within the purview of s. 56 of the Act and, therefore would be punishable there under, and the impugned judgment of the HC must be held to have been wrongly decided. Impugned judgments of the Single Judge of HC set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - The Act, being special law, containing provisions for investigation, enquiry, search, seizure, trial and imposition of punishment for offences under the Act, s.5 of CrPC is not applicable in respect of offences under the Act.
