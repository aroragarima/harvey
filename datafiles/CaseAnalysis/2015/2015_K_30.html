Kalyan Chemicals v Government of Andhra Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

12 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 555; 2015 (5) ALD(SC) 183; JT 2015 (7) SC 250; 2015(8) SCALE 677; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Denatured Spirit and Denatured Spirituous Preparations Rules, 1971

Summary: Excise - Andhra Pradesh Excise Act, 1968, s. 72(3) - Andhra Pradesh Denatured Spirit and Denatured Spirituous Preparations Rules, 1971 - Imposition of Administrative Fees - Amendment to have retrospective effect - Justification - Appellant is manufacturer of product for which basic raw material is industrial alcohol - Further, Govt. introduced collection of administrative Fee of 50 paise per bulk litre in lieu of withdrawal of collection of privilege fees and this rule was given retrospective effect from 25-10-1989 - Appellants had paid amount towards fees it was to get refund of Rs. after adjustment of equal amount towards administrative fees - Appellant contended that amendment cannot be given retrospective effect, and that fees should be levied at rate of 7 paise per litre, since amount was found to be 'reasonable and proper' in earlier case - HC upheld legality of levy of Administrative Fee at rate of 50 paise per bulk litre or any other rate as may be fixed by Govt. from time to time on industrial alcohol obtained from distillery - Hence, present appeal.

Held, with respect to rule be made with retrospective effect, SC held that s. 72(3) of Act specifically allows that any rules under this Act may be made with retrospective effect and when such rule is made reason for making rule shall be specified in statement to be laid before both Houses of State Legislature. Further, with respect to rate at which fees is levied, SC held that rate was deemed to be reasonable on facts of case which does not in any way indicate that larger amount would be excessive especially with passage of time. SC concurred with finding of HC that Regulatory Fees intended to prevent conversion of alcoholic liquor for industrial use to that for human consumption is legal, and need not be strictly quid pro quo as long as it is not excessive. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Rules passed by authority cannot be challenged when same is in accordance with principles laid down in statute.
