Rikhabdas v Ballabhdas and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 November 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 510; AIR 1962 SC 551; 1962 ALJ 714; 1962 (2) ALT(SC) 407; 1962 (2) MLJ 98; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 98; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 475; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Accidental Slip

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 16(1)(c), 14(1), 13(d) and 15 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 151 - (A) Whether an award remitted for rewriting it on a stamped paper would be considered as if is remitted to arbitrators for reconsideration? - Held, no - Reconsideration by arbitrators necessarily imports fresh consideration of matters already considered by them - Can only be as to merits of award - They reconsider nothing when they rewrite award on a stamped paper - An order of court remitting an award to arbitrator for resubmission after duly stamping it and registering it cannot, therefore, be based on s. 16(1)(c) - Such an order cannot be supported by ss. 14(1) and 13(d) or 15 of Arbitration Act or by s. 151, CPC - Award had been already made and arbitrator had become functus officio - S. 151 of Code cannot give court power to direct arbitrator to make a fresh award, that would be against well-established principles of law of arbitration - Appeal allowed.
