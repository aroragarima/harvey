Commissioner of Income Tax and Excess Profits v South India Pictures Limited, Karaikudi

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 79; AIR 1956 SC 492; [1956] 29 ITR 910; 1956 (2) MLJ(SC) 21; 1956 SCJ 479; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 223

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1955 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 SS.10, 10(5-A), 66(1) - Capital Expenditure - Capital Receipt - Capital Asset - Excess Profits Tax - In 1945 respondent company received payment of sum of Rs. 26, 000 from JP company pursuant to agreement between assessee and JP company - Income-Tax Officer took the view that sum was in the nature of a revenue receipt and liable to be brought to account for purposes of calculating the tax - Appellate Assistant Commissioner upheld this decision - On further appeal , Appellate Tribunal held sum received was capital receipt - Tribunal reversed the decision of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - HC agreed with Appellate Tribunal and answered the question in the negative - Hence instant appeal - Whether sum received by assessee was revenue receipt assessable under Indian Income-Tax Act - Held, that payment of Rs. 26, 000 received by assessee from producers was in consideration of surrender by assessee of capital assets which it had acquired from producers under three agreements and constituted a capital receipt not liable to tax for concerned assessment year - Both the Income-tax Appellate Tribunal and the High Court relied upon the decision of the Privy Council in Commissioner of Income-tax, Bengal v. Shaw Wallace & Co and were of the opinion that the present case was covered by that decision - Appeal allowed.
