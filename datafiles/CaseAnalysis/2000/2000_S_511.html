T. K. Gopal Alias Gopi v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 328; (2000) 6 SCC 168; (2000) SCC (Cr) 1037; AIR 2000 SC 1669; 2000 (3) CCR 57; 2000 (2) Crimes 245; 2000 CRLJ 2286; 2000 (2) JCC 497; JT 2000 (6) SC 177; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 832; 2000(4) SCALE 373; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 1040; 2000 (5) SLT 1; 2000 (3) Supreme 706

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376 - Punishment provided for rape was in dispute - Accused committing rape on 11/2 years girl - Incident 9 years old - Accused remaining in custody since date of incident - Accused showing signs of reformation and his daughters have now come of age and are to be married - Considering these extenuating circumstances, sentence of 10 years R.I. was given - Held, punishment awarded was proper and notice to enhance it to life imprisonment was recalled.
