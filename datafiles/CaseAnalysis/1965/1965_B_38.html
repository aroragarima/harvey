Bombay Labour Union Representing The Workmen Of Messrs v Messrs International Franchises Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 229; AIR 1966 SC 942; 1966 (12) FLR 161; 1966 (1) LLJ 417; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 493

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Pharmaceutical, Woman

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Unmarried woman is compelled to resign immediately she marries without regard to her continued efficiency - Whether such a rule against women, valid? - Held, that there is no good and convincing reason why such a rule should continue in one department of Pharmaceutical Industry - Fact that such a rule exists in other such concerns is no justification, if rule cannot be justified on its own merits - Drastic rule not justified - Such a rule should be abrogated in interest of social justice - Appeal allowed.
