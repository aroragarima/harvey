A.N. Pathak and Others v Secretary To The Government, Ministry Ofdefence And Another

Supreme Court of India

12 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Khalid, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28861; (1987) Supp SCC 763; (1988) SCC (L&S) 370; AIR 1987 SC 716; 1988 (6) ATC 6626; 1987 (1) CCC 603; 1987 (54) FLR 556; JT 1987 (1) SC 414; 1987 LabIC 638; 1987 (2) LLJ 140; 1987 (1) LLN 780; 1987(1) SCALE 307; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 281; 1987 (3) SLJ 39; 1987 (1) SLR 788; 1987 (1) UJ 653

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16, 32, 226, 227 and 311 - Defence Production (Directorate of Production and Inspection, Naval) Group A and Group B Technical Post Recruitment Rules, 1976, sch. column 10 and sch. column 11 - Service matter - Delay in making direct recruitment - (A) Practice and Procedure - Maintainability of joint petition by several petitioners as to different questions - Having separate cause of action - (B) Direct recruit appointed, later given seniority over promotee to maintain quota rule - Held, joint petition was maintainable even if dates of appointments, promotion, etc. were different - Not proper and promotees should not suffer adverse consequences - Petition allowed.
