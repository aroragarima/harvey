Â Mahant Shri Srinivasa Ramanuj Das v Surajnarayan Dass and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1967 SC 256; [1966] Supp S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Orissa Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1939, ss. 6 (12), Explanation 6(7) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101, 102, 103, 104 and 114, Illustration (g) - Whether Math, a private Math and property is that of Math or of Mahant? - Held, body of disciples is a very unascertainable body - Math is not a private Math - Oral and documentary evidence showed properties to be that of Math - Where spiritual preceptor, his disciples and disciples in succession are persons interested in such a Math and gift of properties are made to Math though in name of Mahant, such a Math is a public one - When premises of a Math are used both for secular and religious purposes, under explanation to s. 6(12) - It is clear that it is not only deemed to be a religious endowment but is deemed to be a Hindu public religious endowment to which Act applies - Whether adverse inference for non-production of documents can be made against plaintiff - Held, no - Adverse inference against plaintiff for such non-production of documents cannot be drawn - But court can take into consideration in weighing evidence or any direct inferences from established facts that documents might have favoured defendant's case - Appeal allowed.
