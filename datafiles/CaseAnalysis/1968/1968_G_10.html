Gujarat Electricity Board v Girdharlal Motilal and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 100; AIR 1969 SC 267; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 589

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Administrative - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 as amended by Act 32 of 1959, s.6(1) - Issue of notice - Validity - Power was conferred on appellant/Electricity Board to purchase property of respondents/licensees but that right could be exercised only in manner provided in the Act and not in any other way - Appellant served upon respondent notice on 23-6-1961 - As instant notice was issued after the Act was amended, question was whether that notice complied with requirements of s.6(1)(a) of the Act - HC concluded that it was not valid notice - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of HC was justified - Held, issuing of notice strictly in accordance with provisions of s.6(1) of the Act was condition precedent to exercise of power conferred on State Electricity Board to purchase undertaking - In instant case, SC was not satisfied that requirements of law had at least been substantially complied with - On reading that notice licensees could not have been definite whether State Electricity Board purported to exercise power under law as it was on date of notice or as it was under un-amended Act - Rights and liabilities of Electricity Board and licenses before Act 32 of 1959 came into force were substantially different from their rights and liabilities under the Act - Further, on reading impugned notice it could not have been clear to licensee that he had been called upon to sell undertaking in accordance with law as it then stood - SC agreed with HC that impugned notice was invalid and by virtue of that notice appellant could not compel respondents to sell undertaking in question - Appeal dismissed.
