Ramashish Yadav and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

09 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 693; (1999) 8 SCC 555; (2000) SCC (Cr) 9; AIR 1999 SC 3830; 1999 (3) CCR 251; 2000 CRLJ 12; 1999 (2) JCC 471; JT 1999 (6) SC 560; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 171; 1999(5) SCALE 457; 1999 (7) SLT 628; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 285; 1999 (8) Supreme 6

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 141, 149 and 300 - (A) Unlawful assembly - Accused persons ploughing lands - Subsequently on being protested they attacked party of deceased persons - Not established by prosecution that they had one of five objects enumerated in s. 141 - Whether they can be said to have constituted an unlawful assembly within meaning of s. 141 when they had gone to field and were ploughing - (B) Murder - Accused fired gunshot which caused death - Consistent evidence of witnesses to that effect, corroborated by medical evidence - Whether conviction u/s. 300 proper? - (C) Common intention - Fact that two accused persons came and caught hold of deceased - And other two accused persons gave blows to deceased and caused his death - Whether it can be said that accused persons who caught hold of deceased shared common intention with accused inflicting blows? - Held, (A) their conviction by taking recourse to s. 149 - Not permissible - (B) Accused convicted u/s. 302 and sentenced to imprisonment for life - (C) Common intention requires pre-arranged plan and it presupposes prior concert - Cannot be said to be present - Accused 'RP' and 'RY' cannot be held guilty u/s. 302/34, IPC but accused ' Y' and 'SL' did commit offence u/s. 302/34, having assaulted deceased on his head by means of gandasa (chopper) on account of which he died - Appeals disposed of.
