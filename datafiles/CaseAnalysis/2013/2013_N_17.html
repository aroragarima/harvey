Garre Mallikharjuna Rao (D) By Lrs. & Ors. v Nalabothu Punniah

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 134; (2013) 4 SCC 546; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 778; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 128; 2013 (99) ALR 891; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 1; JT 2013 (9) SC 341; 2013 (5) MahLJ 181; 2013 (3) MPLJ 581; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 529; 2013 (120) RD 155; 2013(3) SCALE 344; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Execution of sale deed

Summary: Civil Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Suit for specific performance - Execution of sale deed - Legality - Respondent/plaintiff filed suits for specific performance against appellant/defendant - Trial Court dismissed the suit - On appeal filed by respondent, HC allowed the appeal and appellant was directed to execute sale deed - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in passing the impugned judgment and decree -

Held, judgment and order of the Trial Court was based on proper appreciation of evidence - HC erred in relying upon untrustworthy, shaky and vague evidence to grant the discretionary relief of specific performance in contravention of the mandate of s. 20 of the Act - Impugned judgment was set aside - Judgment and decree of the Trial Court was restored i.e. the suit filed by respondent was dismissed - Appeal allowed.
