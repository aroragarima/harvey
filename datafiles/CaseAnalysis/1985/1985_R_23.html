State of Uttar Pradesh v Ram Sagar Yadav and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 31; (1985) SCC (Cr) 127; (1985) 1 SCC 552; 1985 ACC 19; AIR 1985 SC 416; 1985 AllCriC 79; 1985 BBCJ 66; 1986 CRLJ 836; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 621

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 300, cl 2 - Murder - In Police Station - (A) Need of corroboration of evidentiary value of dying declaration - (B) Evidence and proof - Accused mercilessly beated in police station after brought into on false charge of dacoity - Death by Constables resorted to - SHO present there and creating false record showing him as being away - Real reason for bringing was a complaint against one Constable demanding of bribe - (C) Accused beated person with intention to cause bodily injury with knowledge that it would cause death of victim - Determination of offence - Held, dying declaration true and can be acted upon without corroboration - SHO, Constable, against whom complaint was made and Constables who brought accused to police station are guilty - Offence is murder - Appeal allowed.
