Rachakonda Narayana v Ponthala Parvathamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21138; (2001) 8 SCC 173; AIR 2001 SC 3353; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 126; 2002 AllCJ 487; 2001 (45) ALR 137; 2001 (4) AWC 2932; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 144; JT 2001 (7) SC 63; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 610; 2001(5) SCALE 413; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 77; [2001] Supp2 S.C.R. 71; 2001 (6) Supreme 321; 2001 (2) UC 428

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 12(3) and 16(c) - Specific Relief Act, 1877, ss. 13, 14, 15 and 17 - Appellant brought a suit for specific performance, and since title of respondent was found to be defective, hence plaintiff prayed for specific performance of agreement to sell off two acres of patta land only after proportionate deduction of price for land or in alternative, substitute patta land for price agreed upon between parties - Whether relief u/s. 12(3) of Act can be claimed at appellate stage by amendment in plaint? - Held, that it is open to plaintiff to relinquish part of performance of contract after paying entire consideration of contracted amount at appellate stage of proceedings if defaulting party is not capable to perform whole of contract - Appeal allowed.
