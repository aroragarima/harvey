Chandran v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 188; (1978) 4 SCC 90; (1978) SCC (Cr) 528; AIR 1978 SC 1574; 1978 CRLJ 1693; 1978 CrLR(SC) 455; [1979] 1 S.C.R. 176; 1978 UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Certify, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 302 and 379 - Appellant and other co-accused murdered the deceased, an old widow - Appellant happened to be one of ex-employees of the deceased - Neck and hand of the deceased were severed with a sharp weapon - The motive behind the murder was theft as the jewels of the deceased were removed after she was killed - Conviction under ss. 34, 302 and 379 - Whether conviction of appellant was justified for murder and theft - Held, no - Magistrate recording the confession of the appellant, failed to satisfy him as to the voluntariness of the confession - Held, conviction of appellant for offence of murder liable to be set aside while conviction for offence theft upheld - Appeal partly allowed.
