Lohit Kau S H A L v State Of Har Y A N A

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1255; (2009) 17 SCC 106; 2009(13) SCALE 15

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364-A, 342, 120-B - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 25, 26, 27 - Kidnap - Conviction - Absence of evidence - Sustainability - It was alleged that appellant had received severe setback in his business, he planned to kidnap child of complainant for ransom - Accused were charged for offences u/ss.364-A, 342 r/w s.120-B of IPC - Sessions Court discharged some of accused persons - Trial Court convicted remaining accused persons including appellant for alleged offences - As against four appeals filed by appellant were dismissed by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether conviction and sentence of appellant was liable to be set aside - Held, Courts below relied upon disclosure statements of co-accused recorded by police while they were in police custody - However, so called disclosure statements made by co-accused were hit by ss. 25, 26 of the Act, as they had status of being statements made to police and thus, not admissible in evidence - Further, such statement could only be used to limited extent provided u/s.27 of the Act and that too only against person making statement - As far as recovery of Maruti car allegedly used in kidnapping was concerned, it was clear that Maruti Car recovered from appellant did not figure at initial stage in kidnapping of child - There was absolutely no evidence to suggest that Maruti Car was used in kidnapping at any later state - Arrest memo pertaining to appellant and recovery of Maruti Car was not statement u/s.27 of the Act as vehicle was seized from appellant at time of his arrest - Therefore, no evidence was found to indicate that conspiracy for kidnapping was hatched by appellant - Appellant being close relative had advised kidnapped child's family not to inform police as victim would then be gravely endangered and that best option was to pay up and recover victim - Thus, that could not by itself be circumstance to be taken against appellant - Hence, conviction and sentence of appellant was liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
