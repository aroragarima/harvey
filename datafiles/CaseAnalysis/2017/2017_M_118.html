Manik Chand Jain v Md. Ahiya

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 439; AIR 2017 SC 1623; 2017 (3) MLJ 453; 2017 (1) RCR(Rent) 308B; 2017(3) SCALE 389

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Defaulter, Assam Urban Areas Rent Control Act, 1972, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Arrears of rent - Plaintiff filed suit for decree of ejectment in respect of suit premises on ground of bona fide need of setting up his son in business and also prayed decree for recovery of Rs. 86,400 as arrears of rent along with interest - Trial Court held that inter alia plaintiff is entitled for recovery of possession and that defendant is defaulter - Aggrieved, defendant filed appeal and plaintiff also filed cross appeal - Appellate Court vide its judgment dt.21-6-2012 returned finding that monthly rent of suit premises was Rs.2,400 from date of possession - Further, considering amount of Rs. 1,82,785.95 paid by defendant and after adjusting the same in the rent to which plaintiff was entitled, it was held that still the defendant is defaulter to tune of Rs.7,614 - As such, appeal filed by defendant was dismissed and cross appeal of plaintiff was allowed by holding that plaintiff is entitled to get decree of ejectment and recovery of arrears of rent amounting to Rs.7,614 - Aggrieved, defendant filed revision before HC but the same was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether findings of Appellate Court are sustainable.

Held, considering cl.6 of the Agreement dt.1-8-1988 and findings recorded by Appellate Court and HC, monthly rent from April, 1990 was Rs.2,400. Considering cl.7 of the Agreement which provided for adjustment of 50% of the rent towards advance and payment of rent at the rate of Rs.1,200, defendant was liable to pay rent at the rate of Rs.1,200. The other part of Rs.1,200 was to be adjusted towards the advance made and till entire advance is not adjusted the rent was also not to be increased as per condition of the Agreement. As such, lawful payment which was entitled to be received by plaintiff was Rs.1,200 and admittedly defendant paid only Rs.700 even after taking possession of shop in April, 1990 and deposited the same amount of Rs.700 in the court from April, 1994. Hence, the deposit made by defendant in the court was not the deposit of the lawful rent. Further, even after adjustment of entire advance amount, there is still default of Rs.7,614 and as such, no error was committed by the lower courts in holding the defendant as defaulter. The Appellate Court thus, proceeded to adjust the entire advance made by defendant and still default having been found decree of ejectment had been passed and the same could not be interfered with. There is no error in judgment and order of Appellate Court directing eviction and arrears of rent and as such, HC rightly dismissed revision. Appeal dismissed.
