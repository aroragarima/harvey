Loonkaran Sethiya v State Bank of Jaipur and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 442; AIR 1969 SC 73; [1968] 38 Comp Cas 760; 1968 (2) CompLJ 265; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 122

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 146 and 47 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 202 - (A) Whether power of attorney in question is a power coupled with interest and is revocable? - Held, transaction entered into under document amounts to an equitable assignment of decree in favour of Bank - It is clear both from tenor of document as well as from its terms - It is settled law that where agency is created for valuable consideration and authority is given to effectuate a security or to secure interest of agent, authority cannot be revoked - Document itself says that power given to Bank is irrevocable - (B) Whether from terms of power of attorney it can conclude that appellant had transferred or assigned his rights in decree or any portion thereof in favour of Bank? - Held, document specifically says that Bank should execute decree on behalf of appellant - As per terms of document appellant continues to be owner of amount due under decree - Bank was merely authorised to act as his agent, and it is not possible to hold that in law Bank was an assignee of decree - Interest of appellant under decree cannot be said to have, been transferred to Bank either in writing or by operation - (C) Whether power given to Bank amounts in equity to an assignment of decree or any portion thereof to Bank? - Held, Power of attorney is an engagement to pay out of particular fund debt due to Bank and hence same constitutes an equitable assignment of amount due - In equity, an order given by a debtor to his creditor upon a third person, having assets of debtor to pay creditor out of such fund is a binding equitable assignment of so much of fund - Appeal dismissed.
