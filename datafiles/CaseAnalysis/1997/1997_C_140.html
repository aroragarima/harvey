Reliance Cellulose Products Limited, Hyderabad v Collector of Central Excise, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

08 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2607; (1997) 6 SCC 464; AIR 1997 SC 3414; 1997 (71) ECR 513; 1997 (93) E.L.T. 646; JT 1997 (5) SC 742; 1997 (21) RLT 117; 1997(4) SCALE 434; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 485; 1997 (6) Supreme 237

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1986, Item 15 A and 68 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944 - Sodium Carboxymethyl Cellulose specifically taxable u/Entry 15 A(1) - Product manufactured by assessee (Sodium Carboxymethyl Cellulose) tested and it was found to be Cellulose Ether - Whether classifiable u/Item 15-A? - Whether excisable commodities to be understood in technical sense only or in sense in which market understands them? - Held, (A) product classifiable u/Item 15-A and not under Item 69 - (B) when a technical term used, it must be presumed that legislature has used term in their technical sense - Appeal dismissed.
