Gem Granites v Commissioner of Income Tax (And Others Appeals)

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1052; (2005) 1 SCC 289; AIR 2005 SC 1455; [2004] 271 ITR 322; JT 2004 (10) SC 34; 2004(9) SCALE 647; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 332; [2004] 141 TAXMAN 328

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 80 HHC - Appellant exporter of granite claims deduction u/s. 80 HH of 1961 Act in respect of profits from its export business - Appellant claimed that although granite is mineral, there was distinction between granite in its raw form and granite in its finished form or granite which has been subjected to process of cutting and polishing - Whether appellant is entitled to deduction u/s. 80 HH of 1961 act? - Held, word 'minerals' in sub-s. (2)(b) of s. 80 HHC must be read in context of 'mineral oil' and 'ores' with which it is associated - Granite being covered by words minerals in exclusionary cl. (b) of sub-s. (2) of s. 80 HHC, that for unamended s. 80 HHC (2)(b) cut and polished granite would be a mineral - Amendment of 1991 to s. 80 HHC conclusively demonstrated that word minerals and ores ought to be construed widely and in an unrestricted manner - Although Customs Tariff Act and Central Excise Tariff Act both draw distinction between minerals and processed minerals, however, classification relevant for purpose of determination of rate of duty could not be imported into Income Tax Act which made no such distinction - Benefit of s. 80 HHC could not be granted to appellant - Appeal dismissed.
