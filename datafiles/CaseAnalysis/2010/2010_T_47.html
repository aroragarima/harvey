Khazia Mohammed Muzammil v The State Of Karnataka & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 496; (2010) 8 SCC 155; 2010(5) ALL MR 426; JT 2010 (7) SC 149; 2011 (1) KarLJ 529; 2010(6) SCALE 710; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 1061

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, Punjab Educational Service (Provincialised Cadre) Class III Rules, 1961, Madhya Pradesh Government Servants General Conditions of Service Rules, 1961, Karnataka Civil Services (Classifications, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1957, Gujarat Judicial Service Recruitment Rules, 1961, Karnataka Civil Services (Probation) Rules, 1977

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Karnataka Judicial Services (Recruitment) Rules, 1983 - Kerala Civil Service (Probation) Rules, 1977, rr. 5(1), 6(1) - Deemed confirmation - Entitlement - Appellant/advocate appointed as District Judge on probation for two years - HC Sub-Committee recommended to Full Court for discharge of the appellant from service - HC Registrar General addressed a communication to the Chief Secretary of the State seeking discharge of appellant in terms of r. 6(1) of 1977 Rules on the ground that appellant was not 'suitable for the post' - Government issued a notification discharging the appellant from service - Appellant challenged the discharge notification before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Appellant contended that he had completed the probation for two years and there was no specific communication issued to him by the authority extending his probation period, hence, he would be deemed to be confirmed as per 1977 Rules - Held, appellant was appointed on probation for a period of two years, however, no letter of extension of probation or order stating that the appellant has completed the period of probation successfully in terms of r. 5(1) of 1977 Rules was ever issued - Till the issuance of such an order and certificate of satisfactorily completion of probation period, the appellant cannot claim to be a confirmed employee by virtue of principle of automatic or deemed confirmation - Employee's period of probation remains in force till written document of successful completion of probation is issued by the Competent Authority - Further, even prior to appellant's selection as a member of the Higher Judicial Services, his name was placed for surveillance at a Police Station - Original service record of the appellant also does not reflect that he was an officer of outstanding calibre or had done extraordinary judicial work - Therefore, no interference with the impugned judgment of the HC as the same does not suffer from any factual or legal infirmity - Appeal dismissed.
