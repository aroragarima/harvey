P.R. Murlidharan and Others v Swami Dharmananda Theertha Padar and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 85; (2006) 4 SCC 501; 2006 (63) ALR 502; 2006 (2) AWC 1694; JT 2006 (3) SC 556; 2006 (101) RD 41; 2006(3) SCALE 361; 2006(3) SCALE 1634; 2006 (3) SCJ 776; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1163

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 226 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 9, r. 9 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Office of Madathipathi and Sthiradhyakshan - Suit by respondent for a declaration that he was entitled to continue in said capacity, dismissed - Writ praying for police protection allowed by HC holding that he was entitled to hold said office - Appeal against - Held, Construction of trust and rights and obligations thereunder were in question - A person could not approach HC for purpose of determining such disputed questions of fact which was beyond scope and purport of jurisdiction of HC while exercising writ jurisdiction as it also involved determination of disputed questions of fact - A writ proceeding cannot be a substitute for a civil suit - Appeal allowed.
