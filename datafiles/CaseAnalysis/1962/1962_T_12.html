East India Commerclal Company, Limited Calcutta and Another v Collector of Customs, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 165; AIR 1962 SC 1893; 1984 ECR 138; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1342; [1963] 3 S.C.R. 338

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Importation

Summary: Customs - Trade - Indirect Tax - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 19, 167 and 182 - Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, ss. 3, 4 and 5 - Licence conditions - Appellant company was granted a licence to import a large quantity of electrical instruments subject to the condition that the goods would be utilised only for consumption as raw material or accessories in the licence holder's factory and that no portion thereof would be sold to any party - After the goods arrived in India, appellant took delivery of them on payment of customs duty - On information that the impugned goods were being sold in the market in breach of the conditions of the licence, authorities confiscate entire consignment from the godown of the appellant - On appeal, magistrate held that the s. 5 of 1947 Act penalised only a contravention of an order made or deemed to have been made under the said Act, but did not penalise the contravention of the conditions of licence issued under the Act or issued under a statutory order made under the Act - Said order was confirmed by the HC - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether the goods were liable to confiscation because they had been disposed of in breach of the condition of the licence under which they had been imported ? - Held, u/s. 167(8) of the Sea Customs Act, 1878, r/w. s.3(2) of the Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, only the goods imported in contravention of an order under the latter Act were liable to be confiscated, but the section did not expressly or by necessarily implication empower the authority concerned to confiscate the goods imported under a valid licence on the ground that a condition of the licence not imposed by the order was infringed or violated - The infringement of a condition in the licence was not an infringement of the order and did not, therefore, attract s. 167(8) of the Sea Customs Act - (B) Whether collector has power to confiscate on ground that a condition of licence under which goods had been imported had been disobeyed? - Held, there is no lack of jurisdiction in Collector, he has jurisdiction to decide what is a breach of an order and, therefore, whether breach of a condition of a licence is breach of an order or not - In the present case, as the goods were imported under a valid licence they could not be considered as goods either prohibited or restricted within the meaning of s. 167 (8) of the Sea Customs Act and, therefore, the Collector of Customs had no jurisdiction to proceed with the enquiry under that section - Appeal allowed.
