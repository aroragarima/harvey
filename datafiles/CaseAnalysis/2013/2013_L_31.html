Lucknow K. Gramin Bank (Now Allahabad, U.P. Gramin Bank) and another v Rajendra Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 470; (2013) 12 SCC 372; AIR 2013 SC 3540; 2013 (139) FLR 290; 2013 (6) MLJ 204; 2013(9) SCALE 653; 2013 (5) SLR 638

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Parity

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950 - Direction to Disciplinary authority - Jurisdiction of Court - Appellant-Bank issued separate charge-sheets to six employees leveling identical charges - Appellant-Bank initially chose to proceed and conduct the enquiry only against the respondents - Disciplinary Authority imposed the punishment of dismissal from service in respect of all the three respondents - Departmental appeals filed thereagainst were dismissed - Writ petitions were filed before HC - HC disposed of the writ petitions by setting aside the order of the punishment passed by the Appellate Authority with the directions that appeals of the respondents be reconsidered - HC also specifically ordered that the Appellate Authority to take a decision and award 'minor punishment' as had been done in the case of other three employees - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the HC was justified in giving such a mandamus or it should have referred the matter back to the Bank with the direction to take a fresh decision in the matter - Held, when charge(s) of misconduct was proved in an enquiry the quantum of punishment to be imposed in a particular case was essentially the domain of departmental authorities; Courts could not assume the function of disciplinary/departmental authorities and to decide the quantum of punishment; Limited judicial review was available to interfere with the punishment imposed by the disciplinary authority, only in cases where such penalty was found to be shocking to the conscience of the Court; Even in such a case Court by itself could not mandate as to what should be the penalty in such a case; only exception to the said principle, would be in those cases where the co-delinquent was awarded lesser punishment by the disciplinary authority even when the charges of misconduct was identical or the co-delinquent was foisted with more serious charges - In the instant case, charges in respect of two sets of employees were identical - Though the other set of employee accepted the charges on the first day of enquiry, a factor which was to be kept in mind, that even those employees had denied the charges in the first instance and accepted these charges only in the departmental enquiry, that too after realizing that similar charges had been proved against the respondents in the departmental enquiry - Therefore, it was not a case where those employees had expressed the unconditional apology in the first instance - HC did not look into all these aspects and mandated the appellate authority to pass orders imposing a specific penalty only - Direction of the HC was, accordingly, set aside and the matter was remitted back to the appellate authority to take a decision imposing appropriate penalty on the respondents - Appeals allowed.
