Somnath Sarkar v Utpal Basu Mallick and another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 671; (2013) 16 SCC 465; AIR 2014 SC 771; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 1096; 2013 (4) BC 434; 2014 (1) Bom.C.R. 25; 2014 CRLJ 179; 2013 (4) JCC (NI) 209; 2013 (4) KLT 350; 2014 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 524; 2013 (4) RCR(Civil) 743; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 648; 2014 (2) RLW 1738; 2013(12) SCALE 484; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 935

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Banking & Finance - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 357 -Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Imposition of additional fine - Legality - Appellant issued a cheque in favour of the respondent No.1-complainant for a sum of Rs.69,500/, which cheque on presentation was dishonourned with the endorsement 'insufficient funds' - After due compliance with the statutory provisions contained in the Act, prosecution was commenced - Metropolitan Magistrate convicted the appellant for the offence u/s. 138 of the Act and sentenced him to six months simple imprisonment and to pay compensation of Rs.80,000/ u/s. 357(3) of CrPC - On appeal, both the conviction and sentence were upheld by the Additional District & Sessions Judge - In a revision petition, HC upheld the conviction, but imposed an additional fine of Rs.69,500/- (cheque amount) in lieu of six months simple imprisonment awarded by the Metropolitan Magistrate - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in directing payment of an additional fine of Rs.69,500/, having regard to the fact that the appellant had already paid the sum of Rs.80,000/ to the complainant towards compensation - Held, total compensation payable u/s. 138 of the Act r/w s. 357(3) of CrPC was Rs.80,000/ i.e., the cheque amount of Rs.69,500/ together with Rs.10,500/ (interest amount) - Sum had been duly paid to the respondent No. 1-complainant - Payment of further sum of Rs.69,500/, would be over and above the said sum of Rs.80,000/ and this would violate s. 138 of the Act inasmuch as it would exceed the double of the cheque amount - Appeal allowed.
