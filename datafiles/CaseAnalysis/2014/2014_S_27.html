Sandhya v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 422; (2014) 13 SCC 260; 2014 (4) AWC 3852; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 509; 2014 (142) FLR 618; 2014 (5) MLJ 765; 2014(8) SCALE 210; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 255

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization - Denied - Challenged - Services of the appellant (Unpaid worker) was terminated by the respondents on the ground appellant was not working on the date when Government Resolution dt. 10-3-2005 came into force - Appellant challenged said order of termination - Tribunal set aside the order of termination with direction to the respondents to take action for regularisation of service of appellant - On appeal, HC held that the appellant was not entitled for regularization of her service as per Government Resolution dt. 10-3-2005 - Whether order of the HC could be upheld -

Held, appellant rightly contended that the HC had misguided itself by holding that the appellant was not in service since July, 2002 and was not working on the date of Government Resolution dt. 10-3-2005 - Order of termination dt. 20-4-1998 was set aside by the Tribunal by its order dt. 24-11- 2011 - Tribunal directed the respondents to consider the case of appellant for regularization in terms of Government Resolution dt. 10-3-2005 - Order of termination being set aside, in the eye of law the appellant shall be deemed to be continued in service even on 10-3-2005 i.e. the date when the Government Resolution was issued - Such being the position of law, the appellant was entitled for regularization - But the HC was not correct in holding that the appellant was not in service on 10-3-2005 and wrongly rejected her claim for regularization - Impugned judgment passed by the HC could not be upheld - Appeal allowed.
