Indian Overseas Bank v Industrial Chain Concern

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 175; (1990) 1 SCC 484; 1990 (2) BankCLR 200; 1990 (2) BC 103; 1990 (1) CCC 277; [1990] 67 Comp Cas 255; JT 1989 (4) SC 334; 1990 (1) MLJ 40; 1990 (1) MLJ(SC) 40; 1989(2) SCALE 1014; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 27; 1990 (1) UPLBEC 636

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance

Keywords: Banker And Customer, Collecting Banker, Holder For Value

Summary: Banking & Finance - Negotiable Instruments Act, 188, s. 131 - (A) Whether in facts of case Bank could be held to have been negligent while opening account? - Held, standard of care to be taken by a bank in opening an account - Test for deciding negligence is to see whether rules or instructions of banks were followed or not - Except when circumstances of a case so justifies, in making inquiries banker's attitude may be solicitous and not detective - Bank was not negligent in opening account - (B) Whether bank was negligent in collecting cheques? - Bank normally has an obligation to collect customer's cheques paid into his account - In collecting a cheque on account of a customer banker is protected by s. 131 - Test of negligence for purpose of s. 131 is whether transaction of paying in any given cheque coupled with circumstances antecedent and present is so out of ordinary course that it ought to arouse doubts in banker's mind and cause him to make inquiries - No evidence in this case to hold that there were notices and circumstances which ought to arouse suspicion of bank - Also, while dealing with a customer for collecting a cheque, there is no contractual relation between collecting banker and true owner - Further held, Bank was not negligent in collecting cheques - Appeal allowed.
