Nagashetty v United India Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19794; (2001) 8 SCC 56; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1408; 2001 (2) ACC 303; AIR 2001 SC 3356; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 60; 2001 (44) ALR 816; 2001 (4) AWC 2512; [2001] 106 Comp Cas 732; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 150; JT 2001 (6) SC 482; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 597; 2001(5) SCALE 322; 2001 (4) SCJ 380; 2001 (5) SLT 848; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 160; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 656; 2001 (6) Supreme 171; 2001 (2) UC 393

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Central Motor Vehicle Rules, 1989 - Whether merely because a trailer was attached to tractor and tractor was used for carrying goods, licence to drive a tractor becomes ineffective? - Held, merely because a trailer is added either to a tractor or to a motor vehicle that by itself does not make that tractor or motor vehicle a transport vehicle - Tractor or motor vehicle remains a tractor or motor vehicle - If a person has a valid driving licence to drive a tractor or a motor vehicle he continues to have a valid licence to drive that tractor or a motor vehicle even if a trailer is attached to it and some goods are carried in it - Insurance policy has been issued for a tractor - In this insurance policy, an additional premium of Rs. 12 has been taken for a trailer - Therefore, insurance policy covers not just tractor but also a trailer attached to tractor - Appeal disposed of.
