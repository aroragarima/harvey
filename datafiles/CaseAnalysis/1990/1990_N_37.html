Nirmal Chandra Bhattacharjee and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, M.H. Kania

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 460; (1991) Supp2 SCC 363; 1992 (19) ATC 302; 1991 (62) FLR 171; JT 1991 (5) SC 35; 1991 (1) LLN 316; 1990(2) SCALE 548; 1991 (1) SLR 761

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Consequential benefits

Summary: Service - Promotion - Consequential benefits - Sustainability - Respondents and appellants were working in class 'D' of Railways - Next class above was 'C' - 33 1/2 per cent of posts in 'C' class were to be filled by promotion from class 'D' - Post of Ticket Collector was vacant in class 'C' - Respondents were selected candidates - Appellants challenged selection of respondent - Appellants claimed incumbents of class 'D' to the extent of 65 % were placed in higher scale of respondents, became members of class 'C', therefore appellants could not avail of benefit of 33 1/2 per cent promotional quota reserved for class 'D' - Tribunal dismissed appellants claim - Hence instant appeal - Whether the orders of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, vacancies against which appellants were promoted had occurred prior to restructuring of these posts - It was further not disputed that various other posts to which class 'IV' employees could be promoted were filled prior to 1-8-1983 - Selection process in of Ticket Collectors had started prior to 1-8-1983 - If department would have proceeded with selection well within time and would have completed it before 1-8-1983 then appellants would have become Ticket Collectors without any difficulty - Mistake or delay on part of department should not be permitted to recoil on appellants - Appellants promotion to the post of Ticket Collector should be deemed to have been validly made and should be restored to their position from which they had been reverted in consequence of order passed by Tribunal and should be entitled to consequential benefits - Appeal disposed of.
