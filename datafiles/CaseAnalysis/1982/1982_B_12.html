Â Sanjeev Coke Manufacturing Company v Bharat Coking Coal Ltd. And Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Amarendra Nath Sen, P.N. Bhagwati, E.S. Venkataramiah, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 134; (1983) 1 SCC 147; AIR 1983 SC 239; 1982(2) SCALE 1193; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 1000; 1983 UJ 81

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals

Keywords: Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974, Constitution (Thirty-Ninth Amendment) Act, 1975, Coal Mines Nationalisation

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Sick Textile Undertakings (Nationalisation) Act, 1974 - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act 1973 - Coking Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1972 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Coking Coal Mines (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971 - Nationalization of coal mines - Legality - All coal mines known to exist in country were nationalized - Along with them coke oven plants in or belonging to mines also stand nationalized - All other coke oven plants were left out of scheme of nationalization - Design revealed by Acts was that mining of coal was reserved entirely for public sector, all existing coal mines and management of mines which might be discovered in future was automatically taken over by Central Govt. until nationalization by appropriate legislation, and, manufacture of hard coke from coal was reserved for joint sector and so all coke oven plants belonging to or in coal mines and twelve specified coke oven plants were nationalized while all other coke oven plants were left for private exploitation, there was no ban against any new coke oven plants being set up - Other coke oven plants standing in exactly same position as coke oven plants of petitioners were left out and had not been nationalized there was rank discrimination - Petitioner/Sanjeev Coke Manufacturing Company, who were owners of coke oven plant described in Item 9 of the Second Schedule and Bhowra Coke Company, who were owners of Coke oven plant described in Item 2 of Second Schedule filed writ petitions before HC challenging inclusion of these coke oven plants in Second Schedule and same was withdrawn to SC for disposal - Hence instant petition - Whether 1972 Act was entitled to protection of art 31C of Constitution, which was introduced by 1971 Act as it stood before Forty-second Amendment - Held, validity of inclusion of coke oven plants belonging to petitioners in second schedule had been challenged mainly on ground that other coke oven plants standing in exactly same position as coke oven plants of petitioners were left out and had not been nationalized - Principal answer of Central Govt. to charge of discrimination was that provisions of Act were immune from challenge based on ground of discrimination in view of protection afforded by art. 31C of Constitution - There was logical basis for nationalization of coke oven plants of petitioners, leaving out few and SC was not satisfied that there has been any rank or arbitrary discrimination in violation of art. 14 - Even if on basis of doctrinaire and formalistic attitude, it could be said that art. 14 had been infringed, art. 31C of Constitution and appropriate declaration, in peculiar facts and circumstances of this case, would provide necessary remedy for such violation, if there be any - Applicability of art. 31C and validity of declaration would, to my mind, depend on particular facts and circumstances of case - In present case as State had enacted law in directing its policy towards securing principles formulated in art. 39 (b) of Constitution, art. 31C was properly attracted and declaration was valid - However, when citizen was deprived of his property by State action and feels aggrieved by act of State and approaches Court and if it could not be said that his grievance was absolutely frivolous, citizen in such case should not be saddled with costs simply because Court finds that his grievance had no valid legal basis - It could not be said that petitions filed by petitioners were vexatious - Petition dismissed.
