Lachman Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

28 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 351; (2006) 10 SCC 524; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 123; AIR 2006 SC 2763; 2006 CRLJ 4041; 2006 (3) JCC 1318; JT 2006 (7) SC 60; 2006 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2691; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 904; 2007 (1) RLW 196; 2006(7) SCALE 423; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 307, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Appeal against conviction - Held, evidence of witnesses, more particularly, injured witnesses have been carefully analysed by Trial Court and HC - No discrepancy of any vital nature which will affect credibility of witnesses - Cogent evidence of injured witnesses about role played by two of accused persons, accused no 1 and 2 - Evidence is inadequate so far accused no 3 is concerned - Conviction altered - Appeal partly allowed.
