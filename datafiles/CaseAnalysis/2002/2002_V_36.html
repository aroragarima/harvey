State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Vijay Kumar Jain

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 168; (2002) SCC (L&S) 455; (2002) 3 SCC 641; AIR 2002 SC 1345; 2002 (2) AWC 1230; 2002 (2) CLR 1; 2002 CRLJ 1097; 2002 (93) FLR 328; JT 2002 (3) SC 76; 2002 LabIC 1097; 2002 (2) LLJ 533; 2002(2) SCALE 632; 2002 (2) SCJ 441; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 439; 2002 (2) SLJ 302; 2002 (3) SLR 363; 2002 (2) SLT 528; 2002 (2) Supreme 409; 2002 (1) UC 661; 2002 (2) UPLBEC 1167

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Fundamental Rules

Summary: The government's right to compulsorily retire an employee is a method to ensure efficiency in public service and while doing so the government is entitled under Fundamental Rule 56 of the U.P. Fundamental Rules to take into account the entire service record, character roll or confidential report with emphasis on the later entries in the character roll of an employee.
