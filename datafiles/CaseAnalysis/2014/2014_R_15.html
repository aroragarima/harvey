George Bhaktan v Rabindra Lele and others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 668; 2014 (4) CGLJ 446; JT 2014 (12) SC 464; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4377; 2014 (2) OLR 1055; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 501; 2014(11) SCALE 613; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 520

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonor of cheque

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 425, 468, 471 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonor of cheque - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Respondents accused issued impugned cheque, which was dishonored - Appellant initiated proceedings against respondent u/ss. 425, 468, 471 of IPC and u/s. 138 of the Act - Trial Court took cognizance in respect of offences - Respondents filed petition before Single Judge - Single Judge quashed order of cognizance passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC is justified in quashing order passed by Trial Court.

Held, allegation in complaint is that respondents had forged signature of complainant and submitted to Corporation seeking extension of period of supply. Thereafter, seeking certain relief a suit was filed and in suit the document was filed. There is no allegation that this document was forged when matter was subjudice before Trial Court. Thus, dicta of Constitution Bench is squarely applicable. HC has clearly erred which makes impugned order wholly indefensible. SC had already taken note of submission of respondent counsel that HC has not adverted to factual score whether a case has been made out on basis of material brought on record. In absence of any findings in that regard by HC, SC do not intend to take up burden. That makes it obligatory on our part to set aside order passed by HC and remand matter to it for fresh consideration whether in obtaining factual matrix order of cognizance deserves to be lancinated. SC request HC to dispose of petition within time framed. Order passed by HC is set aside and matter is remanded to HC. Appeal allowed.
