Nund and Samont Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 240; (1970) 3 SCC 859; AIR 1970 SC 1377; [1970] 78 ITR 268; 1970 UJ 621

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(4A) and 66A(2) - Practice and Procedure - (A) Certificate that matter involves substantial question of law - It must state what in view of the High Court, the substantial question of law is - Validity of certificate - (B) Claim for remuneration paid to directors in terms of Articles of Association - ITO has jurisdiction to determine reasonableness of claim - To show that claim is reasonable - On whom burden of proof lies - Held, merely saying that it fulfils requirements u/s. 66A (2) and is a fit case for appeal to Supreme Court, is a defective certificate - Burden to show that claim is reasonable is on assessee - Appeal Dismissed.
