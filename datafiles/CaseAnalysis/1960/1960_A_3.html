Â Abdul Hakim Quraishi and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 397; AIR 1961 SC 448; 1961 CRLJ 573; 1962 (2) SCJ 523; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 610

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Prevention of Cow Slaughter Act, 1955, Bihar Preservation And Improvement Of Animals Act, 1956, Madhya Pradesh Agricultural Cattle Preservation Act, 1959, Uttar Pradesh Prevention of Cow Slaughter (Amendment) Act, 1958, Madhya Pradesh Agricultural Cattle Preservation Rules, 1959

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts.14,19(1), 19(1)(f), 48 - Madhya Pradesh Agricultural Cattle Preservation Act, 1959 - Uttar Pradesh Prevention of Cow Slaughter (Amendment) Act, 1958 - Uttar Pradesh Prevention of Cow Slaughter Act, 1955 - Trade and profession - Permitted - Infringed fundamental rights - Constitutional validity - Petitioners challenged various provisions of Acts in 1958 were engaged in butcher's trade and its subsidiary undertakings - Challenged Constitutional validity of Acts on ground that they infringed their fundamental rights u/arts.14, 19(1)(f) and (g) of Constitution - Bihar Act, U. P. Act, C. P. and Berar Act placed a total ban on slaughter of animals was permitted only under certificate granted by proper authorities - Three Acts were enacted in pursuance of directive principle of State policy contained in art. 48 of Constitution - Court directed respondent States not to enforce their respective Acts so far as they were declared void by it - Petitioners challenged validity of number of provisions of enactments and rules - Hence, instant petitions - Whether legislation was constitutionally valid- Held, amending or new legislation had put in other restrictions so arbitrary and unreasonable in nature that in effect amount to prohibition or destruction of petitioner's right to carry on their trade and profession - Age limit from 15 to 20 years was arbitrary, unreasonable and against general public interests - Repugnant to and infringes fundamental rights of petitioners u/art.19 (1)(f) and (g) of Constitution - Impugned provision permits its slaughter and therefore legislation challenged conforms decision and does not violate any fundamental right - Respondent Stated that bulls or bullocks do not become unfit at age of 12 or 14 years as alleged by petitioners - Committee feels that words 'fifteen years' will not be sufficient for preservation of animals - It would be better if those words are substituted by the words 'twenty-five years' - Total ban on their slaughter had been detained to be valid - Petition disposed of.
