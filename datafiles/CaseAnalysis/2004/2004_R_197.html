Commissioner of Central Excise, Trichy v Rukmani Pakkwell Traders

Supreme Court of India

17 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 301; (2004) 11 SCC 801; AIR 2004 SC 4906; 2004 (165) E.L.T. 481; 2004 (29) PTC 527; 2004 (61) RLT 378; 2005(5) SCALE 315

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Benefit under notification - Claim of exemption - Eligibility - Sustainability of order - Respondents/ scented supari traders, claimed benefit of Notification, which grants exemption, amongst others, to scented supari - However, Assistant Collector confirmed demand on ground that respondent were not eligible to get exemption under Notification - As against appeal filed by respondents was dismissed by Commissioner (Appeals) whereas, Tribunal had by impugned Judgment allowed appeal - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether impugned judgment of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, Circular clarified that if there were more than one registered owners in respect of same trade mark then merely because other person had same registered mark in some other goods would not preclude owner of trade mark from getting benefits of circular - Thus, admittedly respondents were not owners of trademark 'ARR' and they did not claim to have any rights in photograph of founder of group, therefore, reliance by Tribunal on was is entirely erroneous - Tribunal proceeds on basis that exemption could be denied only if trade mark or brand name was used in respect of same goods for which trade mark was registered - Clause 4 of Notification and the explanation make it clear that exemption would not apply if specified goods/scented supari bears brand or trade name of another person - Hence, impugned judgment of Tribunal was clearly erroneous and unsustainable - Appeals allowed.
