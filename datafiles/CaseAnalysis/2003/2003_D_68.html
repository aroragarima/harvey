Messrs Ashok Paper Kamgar Union and Others v Dharam Godha and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 741; (2003) 11 SCC 1; AIR 2004 SC 105; 2003 (5) AWC 4351; 2004 CRLJ 1239; JT 2003 (7) SC 581; 2003(7) SCALE 230; 2003 (1) SCW 5674; 2003 (5) SLT 703; 2003 (6) Supreme 970

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950 - art. 129 - Contempt - Violation of orders passed by the court - Held, the facts mentioned show that none of the respondents to the petition can be held to be directly responsible if the Scheme which had been formulated by Government of India on 28.6.1996 and had been approved by this Court by the order dated 8.7.1996 could not be implemented in letter and spirit as many factors have contributed to the same - Further held, therefore, it is not possible to hold that the charge of having committed contempt of Court on account of alleged non-compliance of the orders passed by this Court has been established against any one of the respondents.
