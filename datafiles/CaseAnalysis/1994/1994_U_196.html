P.Rathinam v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 454; (1994) 3 SCC 394; (1994) SCC (Cr) 740; AIR 1994 SC 1844; AIR 1994 SCW 1764; 1994 (2) CCR 351; 1994 (2) Crimes 228; 1994 CRLJ 1605; JT 1994 (3) SC 392; 1994(2) SCALE 674; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 673

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Tort

Keywords: Betting, Right To Die, Euthanasia, Felony

Summary: Criminal - Bombay Prohibition Act, 1949 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 21 - Suicide Act, 1961 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 309, 292 and 306 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 368 - Two petitions at hand have assailed validity of s. 309 IPC, 1860 by contending that same is violative of arts. 14 and 21 and prayer is to declare s. void - There is no doubt that suicide is intentional taking of one's life - In so far as treating of different attempts to commit suicide by same measure is concerned, same also cannot be regarded as violative of art. 14, in as much as nature, gravity and extent of attempt may be taken care of by tailoring sentence appropriately - Whether s. 309 is violative of art. 21? - Held, art. 21 has enough of positive content in it - 'Life' in art. 21 means right to live with human dignity and same does not merely connote continued drudgery - If a person has a right to live, question is whether he has right not to live - A person cannot be forced to enjoy right to life to his detriment, disadvantage or disliking - Right to live of which art. 21 speaks of can be said to bring in its trail right not to live a forced life - Humanitarianism is throbbing principle of legislation - Law cannot be cruel, which it would be if persons attempting suicide are treated as criminals and are prosecuted to get them punished, whereas what they need is psychiatric treatment, because suicide basically is a 'call for help' - Sentencing has been regarded as a subtle art of healing, and legal and political people uninstructed in humanist strategy of reformation, fail even on first principles - It would be wrong to think that a person attempting to commit suicide does not get punished - Agony undergone by him and ignominy to be undergone is definitely a punishment, though not a corporal punishment; but then, s. 309 has provided for a sentence - Various social forces like economy, religion and socioeconomic status are responsible for suicides - Suicide knows no barriers of race, religion, caste, age or sex - There can be no justification to prosecute such sacrificers of their lives - Suicide, as has already been noted, is a psychiatric problem and not a manifestation of criminal instinct - So what is needed to take care of suicide-prone persons is wise counselling (of a psychiatrist) and not harsh treatment meted out by a heartless prosecutor - It is sanctity of human life which is said to be defaced when one commits suicide and question of morality, therefore, arises - Morality has no defined contours and it would be too hazardous to make a bold and bald statement that commission of suicide is per se an immoral act - Commission of suicide is not against public policy - S. 309, IPC, 1860 deserves to be effaced from statute book to humanise our penal laws - Further, suicide or attempt to commit it causes no harm to others, because of which State's interference with personal liberty of persons concerned is not called for - Ss. 309 violates art. 21, so, it is unconstitutional, void - Writ petitions allowed.
