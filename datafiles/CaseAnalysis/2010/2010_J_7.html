Jabar Singh v Dinesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 164; (2010) 3 SCC 757; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 781; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 1277; 2010 CRLJ 2043; JT 2010 (2) SC 603; 2010 (3) MLJ(Crl) 362; 2010 (2) RCR(Criminal) 309; 2010(2) SCALE 856; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss. 7A, 49 and 49(1) - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules 2007, r. 12 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Claim of juvenility - Presumption and determination of age - Criminal case was registered against respondent no. 1 for offence punishable u/s. 302, IPC - Before framing of charges, respondent no. 1 filed application claiming juvenility - Trial Court rejected the application - On revision, HC held that respondent No.1 was a juvenile on the date of the commission of the offence and set aside the order of the Trial Court - Hence, present appeal - (A) Applicability of s. 49 of the Act - Held, s. 49 of the Act is attracted when a person is brought before the competent authority and not otherwise - In the present case, the respondent No. 1 was not brought before the competent authority, namely, the Juvenile Justice Board - Hence, s. 49 of the Act was not attracted and the competent authority had no jurisdiction to make an enquiry as to the age of respondent No. 1 as provided u/s. 49(1) of the Act - (B) Age determination enquiry - Held, respondent No.1 was before the Trial Court when he filed an application claiming juvenility and it was, therefore, for the Trial Court to make an enquiry and take such evidence as may be necessary to determine the age of respondent No.1 and decide upon his claim of juvenility - Further, when Trial Court rejected claim of respondent No.1, s. 7A of the Act laying down the procedure to be followed when claim of juvenility is raised before any court and r. 12 of the Rules laying down the procedure to be followed in determination of age of a juvenile in conflict with law had not come into force - Therefore, Trial Court, was not required to follow the procedure laid down in s. 7A of the Act or r. 12 of the Rules - (C) Whether HC right in reversing the findings of the Trial Court in exercise of its revisional jurisdiction? - Held, no - Age of respondent No.1 was a question of fact, which was to be decided on the evidence brought on record before the court and it was for the Trial Court to appreciate the evidence and determine the age of respondent No.1 at the time of commission of the alleged offence - Trial Court, has given good reasons for discarding the evidence adduced by the Respondent No.1 in support of his claim that he was a juvenile at the time of commission of the alleged offence - Such determination on a question of fact made by the Trial Court on the basis of the evidence or material before it and other relevant factors could not be disturbed by the HC in exercise of its revisional powers - Impugned order of the HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
