Commissioner Of Central Excise, New Delhi v M/S Connaught Plaza Restaurant (P) Ltd., New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 489; (2012) 13 SCC 639; 2012 (6) CTC 807; 2012 (286) E.L.T. 321; JT 2012 (11) SC 502; 2012(11) SCALE 339; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 365

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Ice Cream, Indian Tariff Act, 1934, C.P. & Berar Sales Tax Act, 1947

Summary: Exercise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Act, 1944, ss. 11A, 11A(1), 11C, 35L - Indian Tariff Act, 1985, s. 2 - Indian Tariff Act, 1934 - Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1958 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1955 - Assessment - Classified Products - Exemptions - Sustainability - Revenue-Appellant had issued 3 show cause notices to assessee for the periods April 1997 to March 2000 alleged that soft serve ice-cream was classifiable under chapter 21 relating to Miscellaneous Edible Preparations of Act, attracting 16% duty under heading 21.05, sub-heading 2105.00 -Ice-cream and other edible ice, whether or not containing cocoa, invoking proviso of s. 11A (1) of Act, additional duty was also demanded - First show cause notice was adjudicated by authority as soft server and was classified under heading 04.04, describing goods as other dairy produce, edible products of animal origin, not elsewhere specified or included, it also held that process undertaken by assessee amounted to manufacture and extended period of limitation was not applicable - Second and Third show cause notice, adjudicating authority concluded that, soft serve was classifiable under heading 21.05, process undertaken by assessee for conversion of soft serve mix to 'soft serve' amounted to manufacture and that assessee was not entitled to small scale exemption because of use of brand name - Aggrieved cross appeals were filed before Customs, Excise and Gold (Control) Appellate Tribunal, which held that process undertaken by assessee, namely, conversion of soft serve mix to 'soft serve' amounted to manufacture and that 'soft serve' was classifiable under sub-heading 2108.91, describing goods as Edible preparations, not elsewhere specified or included - not bearing a brand name, attracting nil rate of duty - Hence this appeal u/s. 35L of Act - Assessee contended that 'soft serve' was an ice-cream, under notification No.16/2003-CE (NT), granting exemption to softy ice-cream dispensed through a vending machine, issued u/s. 11C of Act, and assessee would not be liable to pay any Excise duty in respect of softy ice-cream during relevant period - Whether Tribunal was justified in classifying soft serve under sub-heading 2108.91 and not under heading 21.05 - Held, 3 show cause notices had been issued to assessee alleging that 'soft serve' was classifiable under heading 21.05 and attracted duty @ 16%, show cause notices issued by revenue also indicated that assessee was liable to pay additional duty u/s. 11A of Act - This clearly showed that excise duty was payable by assessee and was being levied by revenue, therefore, assessee's case does not fall within ambit of notification and was not eligible for exemption granted to softy ice-cream, dispensed through a vending machine for relevant period - Appeals allowed.
