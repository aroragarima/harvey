Phatu Rochiram Mulchandani v Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 161; (2015) 5 SCC 244; AIR 2014 SC 2700; 2014 (2) CLT(SC) 45; [2014] 186 Comp Cas 111; 2015 (1) CompLJ 262; JT 2014 (4) SC 243; 2014 (3) KarLJ 203; 2014(3) SCALE 676; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 710

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Liquidation, Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, Onerous, Allotment of plot under lease, Liquidation of company, Cancellation without permission, Cancellation of lease, Pending liquidation proceedings, Prior permission of Company Court, Resumption of land

Summary: (A) Corporate - Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391, 394, 537 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 34B - Allotment of plot under lease - Liquidation of company - Cancellation without permission - Legality - Respondent no. 2 (company) was ordered to be wound up by the Company Judge and liquidation proceedings were pending before Company Judge - Respondent no. 1 (Board) allotted an industrial plots to respondent no. 2 (company) under the lease agreement had terminated the lease - Company Judged on application filed by the Board, had directed the liquidator to release the said land to the Board - Aggrieved appellant who claimed to be the promoter/shareholder of the Company filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed by impugned judgment holding that appellant had no locus standi to question the orders - Aggrieved appellant questioning the veracity of the orders on the ground that it was the property of Company which could not have been released in favour of the Board - Whether Company had acquired the ownership of plots in question and, thus, Board was precluded from terminating the lease and resuming the plots -

Held, It was not in doubt that while construing an agreement, it was not the nomenclature but the substance thereof needs to be looked into - Thus, mainly because the agreement in question is termed as 'Lease Agreement' that by itself would not be the sole determinative factor - However, various clauses of the agreement also clearly manifest that it was an agreement vide which lease for 11 years period was created in favour of the Company - In instant case it was found as a matter of record that Company failed to complete the construction and start factory on the demised land - In fact, no factory could be set up at all - It was clear that right to purchase the land did not fructify in favour of the Company - On the contrary, while the relationship between the Company and the Board was still that of lessee and lessor, lease came to be determined by the Board because of the breach of the covenants of lease agreement - Thus, Company had not become the owner of the plots in question - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Corporate - Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391, 394, 537 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 34B - Allotment of plot under lease - Cancellation of lease - Legality - Whether in the event it was decided that property was on lease with Company, whether the notice terminating the Lease Agreement was legal and justified -

Held, termination notice mentioned that Board was formally terminating the lease by notice dt.19-1-202 - Termination was done u/s. 34B of 1966 Act - Company had committed clear breach in not completing the project and setting up the factory within the time given on the Lease Agreement or the time as extended by the Board - In such circumstances, Lease agreement gave a definite right to the Board to terminate the lease - Board was very well within its right to terminate the lease as provided in the Lease Agreement - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Corporate - Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391, 394, 537 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 34B - Allotment of plot under lease - Pending liquidation proceedings - Cancellation of lease - Prior permission of Company Court - Legality - Whether prior permission of the Company Court was required to terminate the Lease Agreement by the Board since the Company was under liquidation -

Held, prior permission of Court was required in respect of any attachment, distress or execution put in force or for sale of the properties or effects of the Company - Serving of cancellation notice simplicitor would not come within the mischief of s. 537 of 1956 Act as that by itself did not amount to attachment, distress or execution etc - No doubt, after the commencement of winding up, possession of the land could not be taken without the leave of the Court - Precisely for that reason the Board had filed the application seeking permission - But according to Court no such prior permission was required before cancelling the lease - In fact, it was only after the cancellation of the leases that Board would become entitled to file such an application u/s. 537 of 1956 Act - Court hold that no prior permission was required by the Board for cancelling the lease - Appeal dismissed.

(D)Corporate - Land & Property - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 391, 394, 537 - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Act, 1966, s. 34B - Allotment of plot under lease -Cancellation of lease - Resumption of land - Legality - Whether circumstances warranted the Company Court to allow the application of Board to resume the land and take possession thereof -

Held, when it was found that the Company was not able to establish the factory for which the land was allotted, under the statute itself powers were given to the Board to cancel the allotment and resume such land - Company was in liquidation and till date there was no validly propounded scheme of rehabilitation u/ss. 391 to 394 of the 1956 Act - Some obscure proposals, without concrete Scheme as required under 1956 Act, could not be made a sheet anchor to come in the way of the rights of the Board which still remains the owners of those plots - It could not even be said that the land in question was required by the Company - Liquidator could claim rights over that land only if it had become the property of the Company and ownership was vested in it - Appeal dismissed.
