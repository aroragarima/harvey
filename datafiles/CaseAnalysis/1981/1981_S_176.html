Krishna Pillai Sree Kumar and Another v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.D. Koshal, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 110; (1981) Supp SCC 31; (1981) SCC (Cr) 669; AIR 1981 SC 1237; 1981 CRLJ 743; 1981 CRLR 252; 1981 KLT 317; 1981 MLJ 539

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Criminal trial - (A) Need of motive in criminal trial - (B) Appreciation of evidence - Discrepancies - A shortcoming from which no criminal case is free - Held, it is not a sine qua non for the success of the prosecution that the motive must be proved - So long as the other evidence remains convincing and is not open to reasonable doubt, a conviction may well be based on it - If inconsistencies, etc. go to the root of the matter, defence may be justified in seeking advantage of the incongruities obtaining in the evidence, but if it pertain to insignificant aspects, no such benefit may be available - Appeal Dismissed.
