Yunus (Baboobhai) A. Hamid Padvekar v State of Maharashtra, Through Its Secretary and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 74; (2009) 3 SCC 281; 2009 (6) Bom.C.R. 476; JT 2009 (3) SC 487; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 208; 2009(2) SCALE 125; [2009] 1 S.C.R. 689; 2009 (3) SLR 579

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mango Trees

Summary: Land & Property - Maharashtra Industrial Development Act, 1961 - Petition for restoration of the acquired land to the original owners dismissed on the ground that it was highly belated - Appeal against - held, Delay or laches is one of the factors which is to be borne in mind by the High Courts when they exercise their discretionary powers under art. 226 - High Court may refuse to invoke its extraordinary powers if there is such negligence or omission on the part of the applicant to assert his right as taken in conjunction with the lapse of time and other circumstances, causes prejudice to the opposite party - Appeal dismissed.
