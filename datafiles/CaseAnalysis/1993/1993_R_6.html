Employees' State Insurance Corporation v R. K. Swamy and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 741; (1994) 1 SCC 445; (1994) SCC (L&S) 586; AIR 1994 SC 1154; 1994 (1) CompLJ 255; 1993 (67) FLR 1145; JT 1993 (6) SC 176; 1994 (1) LLJ 636; 1993 (2) LLN 639; 1993(4) SCALE 284

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Service

Keywords: Working Journalists (Conditions of Service) and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1955

Summary: Corporate - Indirect Tax - Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Service - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - Issue - Whether advertising agencies are shops for the purposes of the application of the Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 - The word 'shop' means 'place of business', therefore the appellant's premises were held to be a shop carrying on a systematic economic or commercial activity - Held, all the appeals (except the appeal arising from SLP (C) No. 12696 of 1987) are allowed and the judgments and orders under appeal are set aside.
