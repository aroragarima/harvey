State Of West Bengal v Atul Krishna Shaw And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 405; (1991) Supp1 SCC 414; AIR 1990 SC 2205; 1990(2) SCALE 406; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 91; [1990] Supp1 S.C.R. 91; 1990 (2) UJ 681

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Intermediary, West Bengal Estate Acquisition Act, 1953, Titles

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - West Bengal Estates Acquisition Act, 1954, ss. 6(1)(e) and 44 - (A) Quasi-judicial proceeding - Findings of fact - Interference - (B) Speaking order - Necessity - (C) Tank fishery - Settlement Officer holding that no fishery existed on disputed plots - Based on sufficient and reliable evidence - Appellate authority can set aside, if it assigns its own reasons - Its order conjectures and surmises - Held, (A) High Court or Supreme Court would not interfere - (B) It is one of essentials of justice - (C) Liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
