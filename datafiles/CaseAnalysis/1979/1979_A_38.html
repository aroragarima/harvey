State of Uttar Pradesh v Ashok Kumar and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 599; (1979) 3 SCC 1; (1979) SCC (Cr) 606; 1979 (16) ACC 173; AIR 1979 SC 874; 1979 CRLJ 905; 1979 MLJ 544; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art.136 - Order of acquittal by High Court - Prosecution not proved its case - Held, glaring defects and discrepancies in evidence - Supreme Court refused to interfere with order of acquittal passed by HC - It is well-settled that Supreme Court would not normally interfere with an order of acquittal in special leave unless there are cogent reasons for doing so, or unless there is a gross violation of any procedure of law which results in serious miscarriage of justice - Appeal dismissed.
