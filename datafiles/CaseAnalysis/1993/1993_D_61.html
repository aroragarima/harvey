Chancellor v Dr Bijay Ananda Kar

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 257; (1994) 1 SCC 169; (1994) SCC (L&S) 296; AIR 1994 SC 579; 1994 (26) ATC 570; 1994 (1) CLR 172; 1994 (68) FLR 69; JT 1993 (6) SC 473; 1994 LabIC 231; 1994 (1) LLJ 364; 1994 (1) LLN 25; 1993(4) SCALE 349; 1994 (1) SLR 17; 1994 (1) UJ 131

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133 and 226 - Writ petition challenge as to appointment of Professor in University - (A) Suppression of facts that some letters were written by Selection Committee to Chancellor stating that appointed candidate did not possess requisite qualification - Sustainability of High Court Order on basis of said letters - (B) Writ petition found liable to be dismissed on ground that petitioner did not approach HC with clean hands - Costs - Held, liable to be set aside - Considering fact that petitioner was part of teaching fraternity, costs were not awarded.
