Bharatkhand Textile Manufacturing Company Limited and Others v Textile Labour Association, Ahmedabad

Supreme Court of India

17 March 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 149; AIR 1960 SC 833; 1960 (1) FLR 89; 1960 (2) LLJ 21; 1962 (1) SCJ 290; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Delhi Laws Act, 1915, Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Bombay Industrial Relation Act, 1946, s. 42(2) - Delhi Laws Act, s. 7 - Provident Funds Act, 1925 - Scheme for gratuity - Preliminary Objection - Retrenchment - Claim for gratuity - Appellants ought to state at outset material facts leading to dispute in impugned scheme for gratuity had framed - Objection was upheld by Board and it made no provision for gratuity - Respondent alleged that there was sufficient justification for modifying previous award and for introducing scheme of gratuity as claimed by it - Preliminary objections against competence of proceedings and disputed respondent's claim for gratuity on merits - Industrial Court had overruled all preliminary objections - Appeal against award passed by Industrial Court by which a scheme for gratuity had been framed in favour of workmen represented by respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether industrial court was in error in dealing with claim for gratuity on industry wise basis - Held, industrial court had rightly pointed out that imposition of higher duty was consequence of excessive increase in prices of mill cloth and in fact it was levied to mop off those extra profits - Validity of scheme was based on assumption that in working out preliminary figures before framing scheme industrial court had committed an error - Industrial court observed that largest number of persons retired voluntarily on payment of gratuity - Agreement between Association and respondent whereby respondent agreed to rationalization which involved retrenchment of staff on condition that surplus staff retrenched would be given gratuity - Scheme framed by industrial court does not suffer from any infirmities as alleged by appellant - Appeal dismissed.
