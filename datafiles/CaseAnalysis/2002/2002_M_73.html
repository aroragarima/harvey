State of Maharashtra v Mahalaxmi Stores

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1432; (2003) 1 SCC 70; 2003 (109) ECR 529; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 30; JT 2002 (9) SC 633; 2002(8) SCALE 625; 2002 (7) SLT 29; 2003 (129) STC 79; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 292; 2002 (8) Supreme 417

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948

Summary: Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Ss. 61(1) and 2(17) - was the Tribunal justified in holding that crushing of boulders resulting in metal of different sizes ordinarily known as 'Gitti' does not amount to manufacture? - held, every type of variation of the goods or finishing of goods would not amount to manufacture unless it results in emergence of new commercial commodity - The Supreme Court in Commissioner of Sales Tax, Uttar Pradesh vs. Lal Kunwa Stone Crusher (P) Ltd. (2000 (3) S.C.C. 525), on an identical question, expressed the view that when stone boulders were crushed into stone chips, gitti and stone ballast, the process could not be termed as 'manufacture' - no illegality in the impugned judgment of the High Court.
