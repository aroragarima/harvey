Bipinchandra Gamanlal Chokshi and another v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, R.F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 897; AIR 2016 SC 267; 2016 CRLJ 650; JT 2016 (1) SC 211; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 418; 2016(1) SCALE 50; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1077

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: FEMA, Conservation Of Foreign Exchange And Prevention Of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, Smugglers and Foreign Exchange Manipulators (Forfeiture of Property) Act, 1976

Summary: Customs - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, ss. 12A, , 12A(3), 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 352(1) - Alleged Detention - Challenge to order of detention disallowed - Justification - Proclamation of emergency u/art. 352(1) of Constitution was declared on 25-6-1975 - Further, State issued declaration u/s. 12A of Act, that detention of appellant was necessary for dealing effectively with emergency contemplated u/s. 12(a)(2) of Act - State on 11-6-1976 ordered detention of appellant u/s. 3(1) of Act - Further, detention order passed against appellant was reviewed on 26-6-1976 - Competent Authority held that detention of appellant should continue - Further, first review u/s. 12A(3) of Act took place on 4-10-1976, however, order of detention of appellant was affirmed - Further, second review u/s. 12A(3) of Act was held on 9-2-1977 and it was held by Competent Authority that detention of appellant should be continued - Further, emergency declared was revoked by President on 21-3-1977 and on same day, State revoked order of detention passed against appellant - Further, appellant challenged order of his detention dt. 11-6-1976, however, challenge was made by appellant, after order of his detention dt. 11-6-1976, had been revoked by order dt. 21-3-1977 - Lower court passed order against appellant - Further, order passed by lower Court was affirmed by DB - Whether right of appellant to assail order of his detention dated 11-6-1976 stood foreclosed.

Held, order of detention u/s. 3 of Act was passed on 11-6-1976. Further, after passing of detention order, on same day, State Govt. issued declaration u/s. 12A of Act, with reference to detention of appellant. Again, on lifting of emergency on 21-3-1977, declaration u/s. 12A of Act ceased to be operative, with reference to detention of appellant. SC held that at beginning of order of detention, and at time of revocation thereof, whilst detention order subsisted only within limited scope of s. 3 of Act read with s. 12A of Act thereof, there was really no occasion for appellant to assail same thereafter, on any of grounds as may have been available to him. SC held that appellant had no occasion whatsoever to challenge to order of his detention, on grounds available to him, while detention order subsisted under limited scope of s. 3 of Act read with s. 12A thereof after 21-3-1977, as order u/s. 3 of Act could not have been subject matter of challenge as detenu was released on same day. SC held that appellant had no opportunity whatsoever to assail order of his detention, after his release. As soon as declaration u/s. 12A of Act was revoked, appellant was ordered to be released. Further, three of appellant's brothers who raised challenge, to order of their preventive detention, were successful in having same set aside and appellant is possibly similarly situated as his three brothers, hence, he should have same right as was availed of by his three brothers. Hence, impugned order passed by HC in not allowing appellant to raise challenge to order of his detention dt. 11-6-1976, was wholly unjustified. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Court is not justified in disallowing detenu to challenge his order of detention when detenu was not given any opportunity to assail order of his detention.
