Daroga Singh & Ors. v B.K. Pandey

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 252; (2004) 5 SCC 26; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1521; AIR 2004 SC 2579; 2004 CRLJ 2084; JT 2004 (4) SC 608; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 717; 2004(4) SCALE 554; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 113

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Contempt of Courts Act, 1926

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Contempt Of Courts Act, 1971, s. 15 - Commissions of Inquiry Act, 1952 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 175, 178, 179, 180, 201, 228, 302, 34, 480 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1926, s. 2(3) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 227, 235 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, s. 349 - Criminal contempt of court - Police officers attacking district judge in a pre-planned manner - Whether constitutes offence u/s. 228 of IPC and if contempt proceedings properly initiated - Held, intentional insult to a Judge or interruption of court proceedings is punishable u/s. 228 but not as a contempt of Court Also held, definition of criminal contempt is wide enough to include any act by a person which would either scandalize court or which would tend to interfere with administration of justice - Also includes any act which lowers authority of Court or prejudices or interferes with due course of any judicial proceedings - Not limited to offering of intentional insult to Judge or interruption of judicial proceedings - Appeals dismissed and directions issued.
