S. K. Giri v Home Secretary, Ministry of Home Affairs and

Supreme Court of India

21 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1167; (1995) Supp3 SCC 519; (1995) SCC (L&S) 1299; AIR 1996 SC 350; 1995 (31) ATC 206; 1995 (3) AWC 1807; 1995 (2) CLR 597; JT 1995 (6) SC 154; 1996 (1) LLJ 814; 1995 (2) LLN 1110; 1995(5) SCALE 22; 1995 (5) SLR 220; 1995 (2) UJ 580; 1995 (3) UPLBEC 1611

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s.11A - Misconduct - Dismissal - Proportionality - Appellant Head Security Guard in Central Industrial Security Force - Removal of coal by intruders from area which was his duty point - Alleged to have been absent during duty hours - Dismissal - Held, on particular point of time he had gone to report his higher authorities - Cannot be regarded as act of absence from duty - Dismissal is harsh and disproportionate.
