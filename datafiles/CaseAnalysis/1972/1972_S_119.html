Sohrab S/O Belinayata and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 103; (1972) 3 SCC 751; (1972) SCC (Cr) 819; AIR 1972 SC 2020; 1972 CRLJ 1302; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 472; 1973 UJ 43

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Long standing enmity between appellant and deceased - Appellants conviction u/s. 302/34 - Challenged - Held, broad features of evidence of prosecution case cannot be doubted - Appellants and deceased were in field where dead body was found and deceased was chased by appellant and shot dead - Eye witnesses may not have noticed minute details, but salient features of prosecution story were established by their evidence - Merely because there have been discrepancies and contradictions in evidence of witnesses does not mean that entire evidence of prosecution has to be discarded - Conviction justified - Appeal dismissed.
