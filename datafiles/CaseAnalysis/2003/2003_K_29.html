M. Chinnasamy v K.C. Palanisamy and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1064; (2004) 6 SCC 341; AIR 2004 SC 541; JT 2003 (9) SC 161; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 303; 2003(10) SCALE 103B; 2003 (1) SCW 6434; 2003 (7) SLT 705; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 17; 2003 (8) Supreme 386

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Agents

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - Jurisdiction of election tribunal to direct inspection of ballot papers and recounting of votes polled while determining an election petition - Held, there cannot be any doubt or dispute that only because a recounting has been directed, it would be held to be sacrosanct to the effect that although in a given case the court may find such evidence to be at variance with the pleadings, the same must be taken into consideration It is now well-settled principles of law that evidence adduced beyond the pleadings would not be admissible nor any evidence can be permitted to be adduced which is at variance with the pleadings The court at a later stage of the trial as also the appellate court having regard to the rule of pleadings would be entitled to reject the evidence wherefor there does not exist any pleading - Further held, a prima facie case must be made out for scrutiny and recounting of ballot papers, where it is of the opinion that the errors are of such magnitude as to materially affect the election - Appeal allowed.
