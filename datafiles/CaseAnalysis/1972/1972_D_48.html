Dr. Jiwan Lal and Others v Brij Mohan Mehra and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Dwivedi, D.G. Palekar, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 486; (1972) 2 SCC 757; AIR 1973 SC 559; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Contract Act, 1872, ss. 7 and 10 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - (A) Agreement - Vendor agreed to sell certain premises and purchasers agreed to purchase and a clause of agreement made vendor liable to execute sale deed within a certain time - (B) Evidence - Waiver - Stipulation in a contract is for exclusive benefit of one contracting party and does not create liabilities against him - (C) Specific Relief - Where plaintiffs have been pursuing their claim under an agreement and specific performance of agreement on date of institution of suit was not likely to cause any prejudice to other party - Dismissal due to delay - Held, there was a concluded contract - He can waive it unilaterally - Suit could not be dismissed on account of delay - Appeal allowed.
