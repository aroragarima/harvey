Lakhmi Chand v Reliance General Insurance

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 18; (2016) 3 SCC 100; 2016 ACJ 551; AIR 2016 SC 315; 2016 (3) ALD(SC) 53; 2016 (114) ALR 901; 2016 (3) CalLT 40; 2016 (2) CPJ(Sc) 3; JT 2016 (1) SC 178; 2016 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 220; 2016 (5) MahLJ 527; 2016 (4) MPLJ 95; 2016 (2) RLW 1262; 2016(1) SCALE 213; 2016 (1) WLN(SC) 103

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Insurance

Keywords: Consumer, District Forum, State Commission, National Commission, Surveyor, Accident

Summary: Consumer Protection - Insurance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Deficiency in service - Aggrieved by letter of rejection of claim towards repair of his vehicle by respondent-Company, appellant filed complaint before District Forum alleging that rejection of claim amounts to deficiency in service on part of respondent-Company - District Forum directed respondent-Company to settle claim of appellant on non-standard basis upto 75% of amount spent for effecting repairs to damaged vehicle and also directed to pay Rs.2,000 inter alia for rendering deficient service - On appeal, State Commission set aside order of District Forum and dismissed complaint of appellant - On revision before National Commission by appellant, order of State Commission was upheld and revision was dismissed and review petition against said order on revision petition was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeals challenging orders of National Commission dismissing revision and review petitions - Whether impugned orders of National Commission are sustainable.

Held, respondent-Company has not produced any evidence on record to prove that the accident occurred on account of the overloading of passengers in the goods carrying vehicle. On contrary, accident was in fact caused on account of rash and negligent driving of offending vehicle by its driver, against whom criminal case was registered. However, said facts were not considered by either State Commission or National Commission while exercising their jurisdiction and setting aside order of District Forum. Hence, appeals are allowed with costs and orders of National Commission are set aside and orders of District Forum are restored. Appeals disposed of.
