Bijoy Kumar Dugar v Bidyadhar Dutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 68; (2006) 3 SCC 242; 2006 (2) ACC 36; AIR 2006 SC 1255; 2006 (2) AWC 1456; 2006 (5) Bom.C.R. 282; 2006 (3) CTC 122; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 82; JT 2006 (3) SC 94; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 213; 2006 (143) PLR 329; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 590; 2006(2) SCALE 744; 2006 (3) SCJ 210; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 803; 2006 (1) UC 385

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: [A.] Insurance - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 136, 226, 227 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 170 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - Motor Accidents claim - Claimed a sum of Rs. 25, 00, 000/- - Tribunal held that accident was as a result of contributory negligence and insurer respondent No 3 was liable to pay half of amount of compensation, i e Rs. 1, 76, 800/- And amount of compensation as awarded be paid with interest at rate of 10% per annum from date of filing of claim petition - HC disallowed enhancement of compensation and held that claimants entitled to interest on award amount only for a period of two years - Appeal against by claimants - Held, there is no evidence brought on record by claimants to show future prospects of deceased - Amount of compensation awarded by MACT to claimants is just and equitable and warrants no further enhancement - WP filed by Insurance Company was not maintainable against order of MACT awarding interest at rate of 10% per annum on amount of compensation from date of institution of claim petition till date of payment - Award of MACT granting compensation to claimants along with interest is fully justified - Appeals disposed of.
