Adil Jamshed Frenchman (D) By Lrs v Sardar Dastur Schools Trust and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 108; (2005) 2 SCC 476; AIR 2005 SC 996; 2005 (58) ALR 838; 2005 (2) AWC 1128; 2005 (3) Bom.C.R. 710; JT 2005 (2) SC 332; 2005 (2) MahLJ 5; 2005 (141) PLR 227; 2005 (1) RCR(Rent) 284; 2005 (99) RD 724; 2005(2) SCALE 145

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O. 41, r. 27 - Production of additional evidence - Application therefor - Whether HC was justified in interfering with discretion exercised by first appellate court permitting additional evidence? - Held, correspondence entered into by landlord with a third party could not have been within knowledge of tenant - Tenants' statement that documents could not have been produced before trial court, in spite of the exercise of due diligence, was highly probable - HC was not justified in interfering with discretion exercised by first appellate court permitting additional evidence - Appeal allowed.
