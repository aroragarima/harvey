Workmen of Western India Match Company v Western India Match Company

Supreme Court of India

11 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 91; AIR 1966 SC 976; 1962 (5) FLR 129; 1962 (1) LLJ 661; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 27

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 19(2) - Dearness allowance - Entitled to - Whether respondent is paying to factory employees works out to something very much below what corresponding employees at sales office get? - Order comparable concerns give better terms to their employees than respondent; Present rates are inadequate in view of rise in cost of living and; respondent is making large profits and can easily afford an upward revision in dearness allowance and scales of pay - A settlement arrived at between employer and employees shall binding for such period as is agreed upon by them and if no such period is agreed upon for a period of six months from date of settlement and shall continue to be binding on them after expiry of that period until expiry of two months from date on which a notice in writing of his intention to terminate settlement is given by one of parties to other party - Employer has large discretion in so far as most important condition of service is concerned, that is, pay, so long as it is not below minimum wages prescribed - It is a well known fact that biggest employer, State, does not offer uniform condition of service to all employees doing work which, broadly speaking, may be called similar - An award of industrial tribunal, cannot be reopened unless it is established that there has been a change in circumstances on which award is based - Object of awarding dearness allowance is to neutralize, at least partially, rise in cost of living - Held, Company's offer is a reasonable one and tribunal's finding in this regard is one of fact and cannot be permitted to be challenged in an appeal u/art. 136 - Appeal partly allowed.
