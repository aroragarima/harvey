The Iron And Steel Co. Ltd v M/S. Tiwari Road Lines

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1211; (2007) 5 SCC 703; AIR 2007 SC 2064; 2007 (68) ALR 642; 2007 (7) AWC 6616; 2007 (5) CompLJ 1; JT 2007 (6) SC 526; 2007 (5) MLJ(SC) 599; 2007 (3) RCR(Civil) 831; 2007(6) SCALE 682; 2007 (4) SCJ 689; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 156

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.11 cl. 1 to 7 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20(4) - Contract - Reference of Dispute to Arbitration - Jurisdiction - Appellant enchased bank guarantee furnished in respect of contract awarded to respondent - Respondent filed application u/s. 11 of the 1996 Act, before Trial Court for appointment of arbitrator - Trial Court appointed Retired judicial officer as Arbitrator - Appellant filed writ petition before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Trial Court erred in entertaining application moved by respondent as no part of cause of action had accrued - Further, furnishing of bank guarantee was only a requirement of contract, which could be furnished from anywhere in country - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, in matter of settlement of dispute by arbitration, agreement executed by parties had to be given great importance and agreed procedure for appointing arbitrators had been placed on high pedestal and has to be given preference to any other mode for securing appointment of an arbitrator- In instant case, parties agreed for settlement of disputes by arbitration in accordance with rules of arbitration of Indian council of Arbitration, cl. 13 of agreement - Thus application moved by respondent to Trial Court was wholly illegal and without jurisdiction - Hence, judgment of Trial Court and HC were set aside - Appeal allowed.
