Union of India and Others v Ishwar Singh Khatri and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 August 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 570; (1992) Supp3 SCC 84; (1992) SCC (L&S) 999; 1992 (21) ATC 851; 1993(2) SCALE 730

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 16(1) and 226 - Whether petitioner was right in inviting fresh applications when respondents were already waiting for their appointment? - Held, selection board prepared the panels containing 1492 candidates as against the then available vacancies - It goes without saying that the selected candidates have a right to get appointment - It cannot be said that anticipated vacancies arising up to preparation of panels were not taken into consideration by selection board while preparing panels for recruitment tribunal after examining entire matter has concluded that selection board headed by Director of Education or Addl. Director on a few occasion was aware of number of vacancies then available for finalisation of panels of selected candidates - If number of actual and anticipated vacancies was disproportionately less than size of panels, section board could also not have recorded in minutes as follows - 'The panel of selected candidates will be valid till all candidates are offered appointments' - Appeal disposed of.
