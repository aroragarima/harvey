V. Venugopala Varma Rajah v Commissioner Of Income-Tax, Kerala

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 371; (1970) 1 SCC 43; AIR 1970 SC 2051; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 547

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 - Sale of trees - Trees regenerate - Capital realization - Assessee cut down trees and sold - Trees were not removed with roots - Stumps of trees remained in land so that trees might regenerate - Whether amount received for cutting and removing of trees might considered as income or realization of capital - Held, trunks cut so that stumps remain intact and capable of regeneration, receipts from sale of trunks would be in nature of income - By selling a part of trunk, assessee did not realise part of his capital - Appeal dismissed.
