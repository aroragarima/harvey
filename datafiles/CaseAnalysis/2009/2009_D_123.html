State of Uttar Pradesh v Dinesh

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 220; (2009) 11 SCC 566; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 272; JT 2009 (3) SC 106; 2009 (3) MLJ(Crl) 319; 2009(3) SCALE 345; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1175

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts, Hearing Of Appeal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 302 - Appeal against acquittal - Held, an appellate court has full power to review, reappreciate and reconsider the evidence upon which the order of acquittal is founded - If two reasonable conclusions are possible on the basis of the evidence on record, the appellate court should not disturb the finding of acquittal recorded by the trial court - Though ocular testimony of witnesses had greater evidentiary value vis-'-vis medical evidence, but when medical evidence totally improbablises the ocular testimony, that becomes a relevant factor in the process of evaluation of evidence - Appeal dismissed.
