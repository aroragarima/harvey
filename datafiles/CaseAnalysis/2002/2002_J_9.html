Bijay Kumar Mahanty v Jadu @ Ram Chandra Sahoo

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1493; (2003) SCC (Cr) 407; (2003) 1 SCC 644; AIR 2003 SC 657; 2003 (1) ALD(SC) 114; 2003 (1) CCR 31; 2003 (1) Crimes 257; 2003 (3) CRJ 525; 2003 CRLJ 841; JT 2002 (10) SC 349; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 60; 2003 (1) RCR(Criminal) 229; 2002(9) SCALE 311; 2002 (7) SLT 367; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 89; 2003 (1) Supreme 46; 2003 (1) UC 438

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - S. 19 - whether the respondent had produced, before the appellant police officer, the certified copy of the order of bail at the time of his arrest. - The High Court held that copy of the bail order was produced before the appellant who arrested the respondent despite it. - Held, no fault can be found with the finding of the High Court that the act was a result of revenge which prompted the appellant to act against his belief that the respondent had been granted bail. The High Court has rightly held the appellant guilty of contempt of court. - Further, the respondent was deprived of his personal liberty despite grant of bail by the Sessions Judge. It is of paramount public interest that the people, after obtaining an order of the Court, should not feel helpless or without any remedy when such order is flouted.
