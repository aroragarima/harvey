Smt. Sudha Shrivastava v Comptroller and Auditor General of India

Supreme Court of India

08 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1087; (1996) 1 SCC 63; (1996) SCC (L&S) 259; AIR 1996 SC 571; 1996 (32) ATC 205; 1996 (1) CalLT(SC) 66; JT 1995 (9) SC 358; 1995(6) SCALE 449; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 797

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industrial-Disputes Act, 1947 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Promotion - Denial - Pending Criminal proceedings - Death of the delinquent - Deceased delinquent found to be acquitted - Claim of legal heirs after acquittal - Benefit accrued thereof - Whether heir of a civil servant who was prosecuted in a court of law but was ultimately acquitted, though by that time he had died, can be permitted to continue proceedings before court and claim grant of retrospective promotion to deceased and consequential monetary benefits? - Held, death of workman during pendency of proceedings cannot deprive heirs or legal representations of their right to continue proceedings and claim benefits as successors to deceased workman - When an employee is completely exonerated and is not visited with penalty, then he has to be given benefit of salary of higher post along with other benefit on date on which he would normally have been promoted but for disciplinary/criminal proceedings - Appeal allowed.
