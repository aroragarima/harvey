Union of India and another v Ashok Kumar Aggarwal

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 792; (2013) 16 SCC 147; 2014 (140) FLR 737; 2013(14) SCALE 323; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 629; 2014 (1) SLR 217

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Executive Instructions, Central Vigilance Commission Regulations

Summary: Service - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10 - Suspension order - Validity - Respondent Indian Revenue Service officer was suspended from service in view of pendency of two criminal cases - Tribunal quashed suspension order - Later, HC affirmed Tribunal order - Hence, instant appeal by department - Held, though Tribunal took note of the fact that charges against respondent were grave, it held that continuance of respondent's suspension was not tenable - Hence, Tribunal quashed suspension orders, however, gave liberty to appellants to continue suspension if facts and circumstances warranted at the time of commencement of criminal trial - Instead of ensuring compliance of Tribunal order, competent authority passed an order that suspension order would continue till further review - Thus, order renewal suspension was a nullity being in contravention of final order of Tribunal which attained finality - It was not permissible for appellants to consider renewal of suspension order or to pass a fresh order without challenging Tribunal order - Appellants' attitude tantamounts to contempt of court and arbitrariness as it was not permissible for executive to scrutinize order of court - Further, HC granted interim staying operation of Tribunal order but that would not absolve appellants from passing an illegal, unwarranted and uncalled for order of renewal of suspension - Tribunal and HC were right that appellants had not followed directions of Tribunal issued earlier and mandate of O.M. dt. 7-1-2004 - Appellants acted in contravention Tribunal order and therefore, there was no occasion for appellants for passing order renewal of suspension - Orders passed by appellants were in contravention of not only of order of the court but also to office memorandum and statutory rules - Appeal dismissed.
