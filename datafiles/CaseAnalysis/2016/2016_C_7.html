C.I.T. and another v Yokogawa India Limited

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 898; (2017) 2 SCC 1; AIR 2017 SC 463; [2017] 391 ITR 274; JT 2016 (12) SC 253; 2016(12) SCALE 992; [2016] 9 S.C.R. 163; [2017] 244 TAXMAN 273

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Retrospective Effect, Industrial Undertaking, Set Off, Total Income, Depreciation, Finance Act, 2003, Finance Act, 2000, Profits And Gains, Unabsorbed Depreciation, Manufacture Or Produce, Income-tax Act, 1961

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 10A, 10B, 2(45), 70, 72, 74, 80HHC, 80HHE - Deduction of Profit & Gains - Computation of Total Income Challenged - Respondent/assessee manufacturer of computer software and earned profits and gains from export of it - Amendment was made to s. 10A of the Act , which had changed its colour from being an exemption section to a provision providing for deduction of profits and gains derived by eligible unit from total income of assessee - Three circulars were issued to provide exemption and deduction - Revenue contended that deduction were required and allowed at stage of computation of total income under chapter VI of the Act - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, the difference between the two expressions 'exemption' and 'deduction', though broadly may appear to be the same i.e. immunity from taxation, the practical effect of it in the light of the specific provisions contained in different parts of the Act would be wholly different. As per s. 10A of the Act the deductions contemplated therein is qua the eligible undertaking of an assessee standing on its own and without reference to the other eligible or non-eligible units or undertakings of the assessee. The benefit of deduction is given by the Act the individual undertaking and resultantly flows to the assessee. This is also more than clear from the contemporaneous Circular No. 794 dt. 9-8-2000. It is only logical and natural that the stage of deduction of the profits and gains of the business of an eligible undertaking has to be made independently and, therefore, immediately after the stage of determination of its profits and gains. At that stage the aggregate of the incomes under other heads and the provisions for set off and carry forward contained in ss. 70, 72 and 74 of the Act would be premature for application. The deductions u/s. 10A of the Act therefore would be prior to the commencement of the exercise to be undertaken under Chapter VI of the Act for arriving at the total income of the assessee from the gross total income. Hence, answer the appeals by holding that s. 10A of the Act is a provision for deduction, the stage of deduction would be while computing the gross total income of the eligible undertaking under Chapter IV of the Act and not at the stage of computation of the total income under Chapter VI. Appeals disposed of.
