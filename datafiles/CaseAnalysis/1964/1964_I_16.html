Hochtief Gammon v Industrial Tribunal, Bhubaneshwar, Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1964 SC 1746; 1964 (9) FLR 101; 1964 (2) LLJ 460; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 596

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1), 11(3) and 18(3)(b) - (A) Whether u/ss. 18(B), 18(3)(b), 11(3) and 10(1) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, tribunal can summon any person who is not a party to suit? - Held, power to add a party to proceedings pending before a tribunal which may be exercised under Code of Civil Procedure under O. 1, r. 10 is not included in s. 11 (3), and there is no other section which confers such a power on tribunal - If s. 18(b) contemplates that persons other than parties to industrial dispute can be summoned, there is no specific provision conferring power on tribunal to summon them, and that inevitably suggests that power must be read as being implicit in s. 18(b) itself - (B) Whether order given by tribunal against a person who is not a party to suit is binding? - Further held, result of relevant provisions ss. 18(b), 11(3) and 10(1) is that if Industrial Tribunal, while dealing with an industrial dispute, came to conclusion that persons other than those mentioned as parties to industrial dispute were necessary for a valid determination of said dispute, it had power to summon them and if such persons were summoned to appear in proceedings award that Industrial Tribunal may ultimately pronounce would be binding on them - (C) Whether tribunal can exceed terms of reference of its dispute? - Further held, it is not open to Tribunal to travel materially beyond terms of reference - Where certain points of dispute have been referred to Industrial Tribunal for adjudication it may, while dealing with said points deal with matters incidental thereto, and that means that if, while dealing with such incidental matters, tribunal feels that some persons who are not joined to reference should be brought before it, it may be able to make an order in that behalf u/s. 18(3)(b) as it now stands - Material words in s. 18(3)(b) are same as they were originally included in s. 18(b), and so, implied power which could be exercised by Industrial Tribunal u/s. 18(b) can now be exercised by it u/s. 18(3)(b) - If tribunal thinks that parties who were summoned to appear before it were so summoned without proper cause, it may record its opinion to that effect and then award which it pronounces would not be binding on them - (D) Who can be summoned u/s. 18(3)(b) of IDA? - Further held, test always must be, is addition of party necessary to make adjudication itself effective and enforceable - Would non-joinder of party make arbitration proceedings ineffective and unenforceable? - It is in light of this test that implied power of tribunal to add parties must be held to be limited - Appeal dismissed.
