J. R. Kapoor v Micronix India

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1101; (1994) Supp3 SCC 215; 1994 (2) ARBLR 274; JT 1994 (5) SC 37; 1994 (14) PTC 260; 1994(3) SCALE 732; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 567; [1994] Supp2 S.C.R. 567; 1994 (2) UJ 717

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Summary: Intellectual Property - Miscellaneous - Injunction against use of the trade name 'MICROTEL', the logo 'M' and packaging carton - Single Judge granted injunction and DB dismissed appeal - Whether HC was right in exercising its jurisdiction in granting interim injunction restraining the appellant from using his trade name, logo and the carton? - No, Court held,manner in which the said words are written by both the parties on their respective cartons are not to misguide or confuse the buyers and also respondent-plaintiff's carton is in black and white the appellant's carton is fully in colour - Appeal allowed.
