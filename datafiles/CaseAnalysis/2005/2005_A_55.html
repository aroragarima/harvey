Aanaimuthu Thevar (Dead) By Lrs. v Alagammal And Others

Supreme Court of India

12 July 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1251; (2005) 6 SCC 202; AIR 2005 SC 4004; 2005 (3) AWC 2677; 2005 (3) JhrCR(SC) 203; JT 2005 (6) SC 333; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 1; 2005(5) SCALE 513; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 549; 2005 (3) UC 1922

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Doctrine Of Constructive

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Res judicata - 'M' as the mortgagor under mortgage deed and 'C' as the mortgagee jointly filed civil suit against respondent no. 1 (wife of 'M') for seeking permanent injunction to restrain respondent no. 1 from interfering with the possession of the suit house on the ground that it was owned and possessed by them - Suit was resisted by the respondent no. 1 on the basis of settlement and relinquishment of the suit house in her favour by the husband in the village panchayat - Trial Court held that relief of permanent injunction to restrain the respondent no. 1 from enjoying the suit property could not be granted - Judgment of the Trial Court was not appealed against and attained finality - Thereafter, 'M' sold the suit house by registered sale-deed in favour of appellant - On the basis of his purchase, the appellant instituted civil suit seeking declaration of his title and possession from respondent no. 1 and her children - Respondents filed cross suit seeking permanent injunction against the appellant claiming ownership of the suit house under the settlement in panchayat and the consequent judgment and decree passed inter se between her and her husband 'M' in civil suit - Trial Court and the first appellate court by common judgments respectively rejected the plea of res judicata raised by the respondent no. 1 and her children on the ground that in the former suit the question of title to the suit house was neither directly nor substantially involved and decided - Trial Court and the first appellate court, therefore, decreed the suit of the appellant and dismissed the cross suit - HC in the second appeals, however, took a contrary view and reversed the judgments of the courts below - HC concluded that the bar of res judicata squarely applied to the subsequent suit filed by the appellant - It was observed that in the former suit, the right and title of husband 'M' to the suit house was substantially involved which was negated and the right of the respondents was upheld - Present appeal - Whether the judgment in the former suit directly or constructively operates as res judicata in the subsequent suits which have given rise to these appeals? - Held, former suit was jointly filed by 'M' as owner and mortgagor with the mortgagee - Subsequent suit was by the appellant who was purchaser from 'M' - Appellant was, therefore, litigating under the same title which 'M' had in the suit house - Former suit in which decree of permanent injunction was sought was clearly founded on the claim of 'M' as the owner of the suit house to execute a mortgage - Issue of title or ownership of the suit house was thus directly or substantially involved in the former suit - Plea of ownership to the suit house was substantially involved for seeking relief of permanent injunction - Such plea of ownership could and ought to have been raised in the former suit - Therefore, this subsequent suit filed by the present appellant as purchaser from 'M' barred by constructive res judicata and the HC was right in holding accordingly - Judgment of HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
