P.H. Paul Manoj Pandian v Mr. P. Veldurai

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 255; (2011) 5 SCC 214; AIR 2011 SC 1660; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 80; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 805; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 723; 2011(4) SCALE 596; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 254

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Order For

Summary: Election - Parliament & Legislature - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 9A, 80, 81, 82, 83, 84, 100(1)(a) and 116-A - Election petition - Subsisting Government Contracts - Both appellant and respondent filed their nomination papers for the 13 Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly - During the scrutiny of the nomination papers, appellant raised an objection that since the respondent had subsisting contracts with the Government, his nomination papers should not be accepted - Respondent contended that the contracts entered into by him with the Government were terminated before filing of the nomination papers and, therefore, his nomination papers were not liable to be rejected - The Returning Officer overruled the objections filed by the appellant - Election took place and respondent was declared elected - Subsequently, appellant filed election petition contending that the respondent had not terminated his subsisting contracts in terms of G.O. dt. 16.11.1951 and mere removal of the name of the respondent from the list of approved contractors should not be construed as termination of the contracts - Single Judge dismissed said election petition filed by the appellant - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether election of the respondent could be set aside on the ground that the respondent had subsisting contracts with the Government at the time of filing of nominations papers? - Held, in terms of G.O. dt. 16.11.1951, power to terminate the contract was only with the Chief Engineer - Neither the Divisional Engineer was competent to terminate the contracts awarded to the respondent nor the Superintending Engineer was competent to ratify an order passed by the Divisional Engineer cancelling the contracts awarded to the respondent - Therefore, the assertion made by the respondent that his contracts were terminated by the Divisional Engineer by passing an order, which was subsequently ratified by the Superintending Engineer was of no avail - The contracts entered into between the respondent and the Superintending Engineer were never terminated by the Chief Engineer - Hence, on the date of submission of nomination papers by the respondent as well as on the date of scrutiny of the nomination papers, the contracts entered into by the respondent with the Government were subsisting - Respondent had incurred disqualification u/s. 9A of the Act and his election from the Constituency in question was declared to be illegal, null and void - Appeal allowed.
