State of Punjab v Gian Chand and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 557; (2012) 13 SCC 505; AIR 2013 SC 599; 2013 (136) FLR 328; JT 2013 (1) SC 357; 2013 (1) LLJ 225; 2013 (1) MLJ 391; 2012(12) SCALE 499; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 1100; 2013 (1) SLR 765

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Commutation Of Pension

Summary: Service - Superannuation - Calculation of commutation of pension - Entitlement - Challenged - Respondent Nos. 1 to 26, were in service on different posts and were superannuated on different dates from 31-7-2003 to 31-10-2006 - Respondents grievance was in relation to the table of calculation of commutation of pension, which had been replaced to the disadvantage of the persons who had retired within the above-referred period - common and hence all of them filed a common petition challenging the circular dt. 29-7-2003 issued by the Govt. - HC allowed the petition and held that no justification or clarification had been provided by the State, while making a feeble attempt to defend its stand and there was no rational basis for providing the cut off dates between the period from 31-7-2003 to 30-10-2006 - Hence instant appeal - Whether calculation of commutation of pension by state was justified - Held, affidavit dt. 7-1-2011 was filed on behalf of the State taking the ground that the State had faced an acute financial crisis in the year 2003 and, in fact, was in a virtual debt trap - Since the commutation of pension was essentially loan/advance against the future payments of the monthly pension, the State Govt. could ill-afford to raise further debt at higher rate of interest to make such payments to employees at concessional effective rate of interest which was as low as 4.75 per cent per annum - Another affidavit was filed with the leave of the Court bringing on record the policy of the Govt., formula adopted for commutation factor and giving facts and figures as to how the circular dt. 29-7-2003 came to be issued - There was no doubt that the circular dated 29-7-2003 did not contain any reason, whatsoever, for passing a directive, which enmass adversely affected the people who have retired in the period between 31-7-2003 to 31-10-2006 - Impugned judgment did not even discuss the plea of arbitrariness and discrimination in its proper perspective - Court also had not deliberated upon as to whether the law stated by SC in V. Kasturi v. Managing Director, State Bank of India, 1998 Indlaw SC 348 to the facts of instant case or not - For lack of proper reasoning in the judgment of the HC, in view of the additional pleas raised before instant Court which had significant ramifications in law and with regard to the liability of the State, SC was left with no option but to set aside the judgment of the HC under appeal and remit the matter to the HC for fresh decision in accordance with law - Appeals partly allowed.
