Sole Trustee, Loka Shikshana Trust v Commissioner of Income Tax, Mysore

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, M. Hameedullah Beg, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 332; (1976) SCC (Tax) 14; (1976) 1 SCC 254; AIR 1976 SC 10; 1975 CTR 281; [1975] 101 ITR 234; [1976] 1 S.C.R. 461; 1976 TAXLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Exemption From Tax, Ejusdem Generis, Change In Law, Question Of Fact And Law, Charitable or Religious entities

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Mortgage - Exemption - Appellant was a sole trustee of the trust holding properties mentioned in a schedule attached to a deed of trust - Main object of the Trust was education and this may be achieved by conducting and helping educational institutions having similar objects - Income tax officer sent a communication to the trust to the effect that since the only activity of the trust was printing, publication and sale of newspaper, weekly and monthly journal, the trust carried on an activity for profit and was not entitled to exemption - Whether income of the trust was entitled to exemption u/s. 11 of the 1961 Act r/w s. 2(15) of the Act for the A/Y 1962-63 - Held, it appeared that from under the provisions of the earlier trust, the trustee had carried on a lucrative business of printing - Printing business must have been lucrative because investments of profits from it together with some possible donations expanded the assets of the redeclared trust of 1962 so much that the schedule attached to the trust deed of 1962 showed their value to be Rs. 2,97,658/- Appellant trustee himself to the Income-tax Department for the A/Y 1962-63 with which that the trust had made quite considerable profits from various activities carried on as a part of its ordinary and regular business - Items of expense consisted of money spent on repairs of buildings, payments of taxes purchases of newsprint and other kinds of paper ink, photographic materials, blocks, binding, stitching and packing materials, payments of salaries, wages and allowances to the staff - Original trustee could invest trust moneys and profits in any investment authorised by law for the investment of Trust funds or in shares or securities or debentures of Limited Companies in India or outside - He had the power to mortgage sell transfer and give on lease or to otherwise deal with the Trust property or any portion thereof for the purpose of the Trust and to borrow moneys or raise loans for the purpose of the Trust whenever he may deem it necessary to do so - If profit making resulted from them and these profits could be utilised for non-charitable purposes the trust which made this possible would not be exempt from paying income tax - Trustees were given the power of deciding what purpose was allowed to or like an object covered by the trust and how it was to be served by a diversion of trust properties and funds - If the trustee was given the power to determine the proportion of such diversion, as he was given here, the trust could not be said to be wholly charitable - Trust deed itself showed that the sole trustee had managed to make the satisfaction of the needs mentioned above a highly profitable business - Deed put no condition upon the conduct of the newspaper and publishing business from which it could be infered that it was to be on no profit and no loss basis - That character was determined by the absence of terms which could eliminate or prevent profit making from becoming the real or dominant purpose of the trust - Appeals dismissed.
