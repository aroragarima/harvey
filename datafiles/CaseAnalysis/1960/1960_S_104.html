Swadeshi Industries Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 42; AIR 1960 SC 1258; 1960 (1) FLR 576; 1960 (2) LLJ 78

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Trade - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 22, 23 and 24 - (A) Whether justification of a strike is a question of fact? - Held, justification of a strike depends upon all facts and circumstances - It is not vitiated merely because it placed undue weight on one or more circumstances - Whether strike as a part of collective bargaining is justified? - Further held, collective bargaining for securing improvement on matters like basic pay, dearness allowance, bonus, etc. is primary object of a trade union - A strike in pursuance of such demands is justified - (B) Whether an order of reinstatement in event of wrongful dismissal is time barred? - Further held, reinstatement cannot ordinarily be refused merely because a long time has elapsed - Appeal dismissed.
