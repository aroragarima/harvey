Supreme Court of India

08 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, V.N. Khare, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 258; (2004) 4 SCC 311; AIR 2004 SC 2371; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 50; 2004 (2) BC 397; 2004 (4) Bom.C.R. 530; [2004] 120 Comp Cas 373; 2004 (2) CompLJ 209; 2004 (110) DLT 665; 2004 DRTC 107; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1150; JT 2004 (4) SC 308; 2004 (2) KLT 273; 2004 (42) MLJ 1090; 2004 (138) PLR 271; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 665; 2004(4) SCALE 338; 2004 (2) SLT 991; 2004 (3) Supreme 243; 182 TAXATION 1; [2004] 136 TAXMAN 360; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 2191

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate

Keywords: Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, Bombay Tenancy Act, 1939, Onerous, Madras Agriculturists Debt Relief Act, 1938

Summary: [A.] Banking & Finance - Securitisation And Reconstruction Of Financial Assets And Enforcement Of Security Interest Act, 2002, ss. 13, 13(4), 15, 17, 31, 34, 35 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986, s. 22 - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951, s. 29 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 226 - Recovery Of Debts Due To Banks And Financial Institutions Act, 1993 - Security Interest (Enforcement) Rules, 2002, r. 9 - Constitutional validity of Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002 - Held, it cannot be said that a step taken towards securitisation of debts and to evolve means for faster recovery of NPAs was not called for or that it was superimposition of undesired law since one legislation was already operating in field namely Recovery of Debts due to Banks and Financial Institutions Act - Legislation is a matter of policy and for larger public good - Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, s.13 - Further held, necessary to communicate reasons for not accepting objections raised by borrower in reply to notice u/s.13(2) of Act - More particularly as creditor is empowered to enforce security himself without intervention of Court - On measures having been taken under sub-section (4) of Section 13 and before date of sale/auction of property - It would be open for borrower to file an appeal (petition) u/s. 17 of Act before DRT - DRT shall have jurisdiction to pass any stay/interim order subject to condition at it may deem fit and proper to impose - Securitization and Reconstruction of Financial Assets and Enforcement of Security Interest Act, 2002, s.17(2) - Amount of deposit of 75% of demand, at initial proceeding not a reasonable condition - Oppressive and arbitrary - Condition should not be left operative in expectation of reasonable exercise of discretion by DRT - Held violative of art.14 of Constitution - Struck down.
