Commissioner of Income Tax (Central), Calcutta v Daulatram Rawatmull

Supreme Court of India

26 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 307; [1964] 53 ITR 574

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.66 - Fixed deposit - Income - Notice - Profits - Assessment - Respondent was registered firm under 1922 Act - Income Tax Officer issued a notice u/s.34 of Act calling upon firm to file a revised return of income for corresponding accounting year - Firm showed an income of Rs.2,75, 168 according to its original assessment as reduced in appeal - Income Tax Officer inspected books of account of assessee and found amount of Rs. 5,00,000 was tendered in cash to Bank - In view of Income Tax Officer amounts of fixed deposit receipts credited in Bank names of three sons of partners of assessee were secreted profits belonging to assessee and added sum of Rs.15,00,000 to its total assessable income and completed reassessment - Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax assumed assessee was able to explain source of Rs. 50,000 only out of deposit of Rs.5,00,000 - Appellate Assistant Commissioner modified order of Income Tax Officer and added an amount of Rs.14,50,000 out of amount of Rs.15,60,000 representing three fixed deposit receipts in names of three sons of three partners of assessee as secreted profits - Hence instant appeal - Whether sum of Rs.5,00,000 deposited by assessee was a secreted profits - Held, amount was not utilized by assessee for an overdraft and neither amount found its way into assessee's account books - Evidence showed that firm was interested in money and concealment was of the firm - Tribunal had rightly pointed out similarity of transactions in respect of three deposit receipts and proximity of time were matters which may raise suspicion and deposit was apparently obtained in name of benami for real owner - Circumstances relied upon fail to establish one link which must be established by evidence that assessee was concerned with transaction of deposit made - Appeal dismissed.
