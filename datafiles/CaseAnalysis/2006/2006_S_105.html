Star India (Private) Limited v Sea TV Network Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1573; (2006) 4 SCC 130(B)

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Telecom

Summary: Telecom - Media & Entertainment - Particulars of subscribers â Appropriate advertisement - CAS permitted subscriber to exercise choice to have only those pay channels which subscriber chooses to watch - Appellant alleged that Cable operators and MSOs did not give complete and accurate list of ultimate subscribers and their area of operation - Respondent alleged that it would be in interest of all concerned to have details of subscribers of cable operators correctly identified, provided that such identification was done uniformly and in nondiscriminatory manner - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, it would be necessary to give adequate opportunity to all stakeholders concerned to file their submissions. For this purpose, appropriate advertisement shall be published by TRAI in two leading newspapers as well as on its own website informing Associations of Cable Operators, MSOs and subscribers. Relying on orders dt. 24-8-2005 and 17-1-2006 of TDSAT, various petitions are being filed and are pending, seeking similar orders. It would be appropriate for TDSAT not to pass any final orders in these matters pending further orders of SC. TDSAT is also hearing question as to directions which can be given to Associations of Cable Operators and MSOs for disclosing particulars of their subscribers. Pendency of these matters before instant Court shall not stand in way of TDSAT hearing those cases and in fact it would be of assistance to have views of TDSAT before SC when matters are next taken up. Further, pendency of these matters shall not stand in way of Central Govt. Order accordingly.
