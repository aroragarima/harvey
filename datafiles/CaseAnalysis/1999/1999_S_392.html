State of Karnataka v K. Yarappa Reddy

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 529; (1999) 8 SCC 715; (2000) SCC (Cr) 61; AIR 2000 SC 185; 2000 CRLJ 400; JT 1999 (8) SC 10; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 359; 1999 (8) Supreme 496

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 153 and 159 - Murder - Testimony of eye - witness - Murder took place in house of star witness and only witness to murder - She did not witness appellant attacking deceased but saw him standing near dead body of deceased with a blood stained chopper in his hand - Prosecution witness has supported prosecution story beyond any doubt - Appellant himself surrendered himself to police and knife - Whether appellants conviction on basis of this evidence is justified? - As general rule of evidence is one of prohibiting evidence on collateral issues and since it is only by way of exception that such evidence can be permitted, court must guard that defence evidence falls strictly within exception - Basic requirement for adducing such contradictory evidence is that witness whose impartiality is sought to be contradicted with help of such evidence, should have been asked about it and he should have denied it - Without adopting such a preliminary recourse it would be meaningless if not unfair, to bring in a new witness to speak something fresh about a witness already examined - Held, yes - High Court rejected her evidence on trivial ground that she failed to cry/shout on witnessing murder is erroneous - Courts should not expect set reaction from any eye witness on seeing murder - Different persons may give different reactions to an incident like murder - Restore conviction and sentence passed by trial court on respondent - Appeal allowed.
