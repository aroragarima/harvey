Apm Terminals B.V. v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 347; (2011) 6 SCC 756; 2011 (2) BC 681; 2011 (4) Bom.C.R. 10; JT 2011 (6) SC 289; 2011 (6) MLJ(SC) 176; 2011(6) SCALE 271; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 600

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tender process - Jawaharlal Nehru Port Trust (JNPT) Bulk Terminal was designed to handle goods imported in bulk - Since 1996, it was the policy of the Central Government to permit participation/investment by the private sector in utilizing the assets of the Port - Appellant was a Company incorporated under the laws of the Netherlands - Together with the Container Corporation of India Limited it formed a Joint Venture Company - Said Joint Venture Company (GTI) was the successful bidder in the Tender floated by JNPT for development of its existing Bulk Terminal into a Container Terminal - GTI was also permitted to bid in the Tender for the development of the Second Container Terminal at JNPT - On completion of the bidding process, the work of development of the Second Container Terminal was awarded to GTI for a term of 30 years from the date of the Licence Agreement - Meanwhile, a decision was taken by the Union of India to alter the 1996 policy indicating that the JNPT should proceed to invite global competitive bidding for an independent 'Stand Alone' Container Terminal to expand the Container towards North of JNPT which was designated as Third Container Terminal - As a result, neither the appellant nor its affiliates and/or subsidiaries/allied organizations were permitted to participate in the bid for the Stand Alone Container Terminal - Thereafter, JNPT floated yet another Tender for development of the Third Container Terminal at JNPT, inviting Requests for Qualification for selection of a developer for the development of the said terminal - GTI's plea to allow it to participate in the bid was rejected - Appellant was subsequently barred from participating in the Tender process for the development of the Third Container Terminal at JNPT - Later, appellant was invited to participate in the process for grant of licence for the Fourth Container Terminal - However, appellant was informed that GTI and/or its associates/allied organizations had been disqualified from bidding for the Fourth Container Terminal in view of Clause 8.31 of the Licence Agreement which provide that appellant shall forego the right to bid for either directly or indirectly, including being a Management Contractor through any associate company - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal challenging the validity and propriety of the decision taken by the Board of Trustees of the JNPT, to exclude the appellant from participating in the tender process for the Fourth Container Terminal under the JNPT, through public-private partnership, and praying for quashing of the said decision with leave to the appellant to participate in the tender process - Appellant Company claimed that on account of subsequent resolutions adopted by the Board of Trustees of JNPT, which had the effect of altering the policy with regard to entrustment of operational facilities at the port to provide competition and to prevent monopolies, the provisions of Clause 8.31 would have to be reconsidered in the light of the changed circumstances - Held, Government had the discretion to adopt a different policy, alter or change its policy to make it more effective - Such change in policy must be free from arbitrariness, irrationality, bias and malice and must be in conformity with the principle of Wednesbury reasonableness - It was not as if the right of a licensee to bid for a further container terminal berth was excluded for the entire period of the Licence Agreement but in order to ensure proper competition and participation by all intending tenderers, the said policy was also been altered to enable such licensees to bid for the next but one tender as and when invited - However, as far as the appellant was concerned, it was because of certain fortuitous circumstances that it came to be excluded from the tender process for the Fourth Container Terminal - Appellant having been excluded from one bid on the basis of an existing policy, could not be debarred from participating in the next bid, by taking recourse to a different yardstick - Such a course of action would be contrary to public policy - Authorities of the JNPT shall allow the appellant to continue to participate in the tender process for the Fourth Container Terminal and the decision to the contrary conveyed to the appellant was quashed - Order accordingly.
