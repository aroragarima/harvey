Dr. M. V. Nair v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, A.S. Anand, L.M. Sharma

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 859; (1993) 2 SCC 429; (1993) SCC (L&S) 512; 1993 (24) ATC 236; 1993 (1) CLR 449; 1993 (66) FLR 476; JT 1993 (1) SC 255; 1993 LabIC 1111; 1993 (2) LLJ 347; 1993 (1) LLN 982; 1993(1) SCALE 180; 1993 (1) SLJ 111; 1993 (1) SLR 708; 1993 (1) UJ 385; 1993 (2) UPLBEC 833

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research

Summary: Service - Matter pertains to appointment to post of Director in National Research Laboratory for Conservation of Cultural Property on transfer on deputation basis - Six names were received including appellant, and respondent 3 - Appellant selected - Selection questioned by respondent 3 - Contended that relaxation granted in favour of appellant - Respondent, UOI contended that no relaxation was granted - Direction given to respondents by Tribunal to hold a fresh selection for said post - Present appeal - Held, it was necessary for Tribunal to have recorded finding on correctness of submission of respondents - Without doing so, Tribunal could not have set aside appointment of appellant to said post - Tribunal, also not justified in stating, that inasmuch as respondent 3 has become eligible his suitability should be considered along with other eligible candidates - Suitability and eligibility have to be considered with reference to last date for receiving applications - Judgment of Tribunal set aside - Matter remitted back to Tribunal.
