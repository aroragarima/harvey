Bakhtawar Singh and Another v Sada Kaur and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2368; (1996) 11 SCC 167; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 700; AIR 1996 SC 3488; 1996 (3) CLT 317; 1997 (1) HLR 718; JT 1996 (8) SC 407; 1999 (90) RD 623; 1997 (1) RentLR 470; 1996(6) SCALE 222; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 235; 1996 (6) Supreme 353

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 14 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 23, r. 1 - Limitation Act - Exclusion of time spent in prosecuting cause in court without jurisdiction - Suit for possession - Earlier suit on same cause alleged to be withdrawn with liberty to file fresh suit - But no evidence adduced and suit was bound to fail for some formal defect made out - Held, time spent in prosecuting earlier suit cannot be fresh excluded while computing limitation for suit - Appeal dismissed.
