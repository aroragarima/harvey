Union Of India, Etc. v Rake Sh Kumar And Ors.,Etc

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, P. Sathasivam, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 16; (2010) 4 SCC 50; AIR 2010 SC 3244; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 106; JT 2010 (1) SC 396; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 145; 2010(1) SCALE 281; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 483

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Gram Sabha, Affirmative Action, Fifth Schedule, Provisions of the Panchayats (Extension to the Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996, Jharkhand Panchayati Raj Act, 2001, Madhya Pradesh Panchayati Raj Act, Jharkhand Panchayat Reservation Act, 2001, Scheduled Area (Part A States) Order, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Municipalities & Local Governments - Panchayats (Extension to the Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996 (PESA), s. 4(g) - Jharkhand Panchayat Raj Act, 2001 (JPRA), ss. 21(B), 40(B), 55(B), 17(B)(2), 36(B)(2) and 51(B)(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 243-M(4)(b) - Reservation of Panchayat seats to Scheduled Tribes - Validity of the second proviso to s. 4(g) of PESA - Respondents challenged the second proviso to s. 4(g) of PESA whereby all the seats of Chairpersons of Panchayats at all three tiers in Scheduled Areas were to be reserved in favour of Scheduled Tribes - HC held that the impugned proviso was unconstitutional - (A) Whether HC is justified in striking down ss. 21(B), 40(B) and 55(B) of the JPRA which give effect to the second proviso to s. 4 of the PESA - Held, art. 243-M(4)(b) of the Constitution expressly empowers Parliament to provide for 'exceptions and modifications' in the application of Part IX of the Constitution to Scheduled Areas, therefore, exclusive representation of Scheduled Tribes in the Chairperson positions in Panchayats located in Scheduled Areas, is constitutionally permissible - Proviso to s. 4(g) of the PESA contemplate certain exceptions to the norm of 'proportionate representation' and the same exceptional treatment was incorporated in the impugned provisions of the JPRA - Therefore, HC erred in striking down ss. 21(B), 40(B) and 55(B) of the JPRA, hence the same is constitutional - (B) Whether HC is justified in striking down ss. 17(B)(2), 36(B)(2) and 51(B)(2) of the JPRA as unconstitutional by virtue of reasoning that reservations to the extent of 80% of the seats in Panchayats were excessive, arbitrary and disproportionate, thereby violating art. 14 of the Constitution - Held, in addition to the 50% reservations in favour of Scheduled Tribes, the State is also under an obligation to account for the interests of Scheduled Castes and Other Backward Classes - Such protection of interest of other backward classes contemplated in ss. 17(B)(2), 36(B)(2) and 51(B)(2) of the JPRA which incorporate the standard of 'proportionate representation' for Scheduled Castes and Backward Classes in such a manner that the total reservations do not exceed 80% - However, depending on the demographic profile of a particular constituency, it is possible that the total reservations could well fall short of the 80% upper ceiling in some scheduled areas - In Scheduled Areas where the extent of the population belonging to the Scheduled Castes and Backward Classes exceeds 30% of the total population, the upper ceiling of 80% would become operative - Therefore, the total reservations exceeding 50% of the seats in Panchayats located in Scheduled Areas are permissible on account of the exceptional treatment mandated u/art. 243-M(4)(b) and hence, ss. 17(B)(2), 36(B)(2) and 51(B)(2) of the JPRA are constitutionally valid - Order of the HC is set aside - Appeals allowed.
