Delhi State Indus. Dev. Corpn. Limited v Ashok Kumar Madan

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 39; (2015) 4 SCC 245; AIR 2015 SC 993; 2015 (2) AWC 1808; JT 2015 (1) SC 411; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 471; 2015(1) SCALE 570

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Cancellation of allocation of plot

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Delayed payment - Cancellation of allocation of plot - Justifiability - Respondent was running a commercial/industrial establishment, made an application for allotment of a plot under the 'Relocation Scheme', which was accepted and was allotted a plot by appellant (corporation) - However, allotment in favour of respondent was cancelled and amount was refunded with interest - Aggrieved by cancellation order, respondent filed a petition before Single Judge, which was disposed of and quashed cancellation letter and appellant was directed to make available an alternate plot to respondent in case original plot allotted to respondent was not available - Appeal filed by appellant before DB was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that respondent made a default in payment of initial 50% of the cost of plot despite the extension of time given by SC in M.C.Mehta v Union Of India And Ors, 1991 Indlaw SC 571.

Held, respondent had paid interest on the delayed payment to the account of the appellant, which was accepted by it and it did not take any action either against its officer or for the return of money to the respondent between the period 2004-2008, certainly created equity in favour of the respondent, as observed by Single Judge and judgment and order was rightly confirmed by DB. Appellant has failed to satisfy the Court with cogent and reasonable explanation as to why the money paid by the respondent for the allotment of the plot was not returned to him by the appellant which has led him to believe that his delayed payment towards the cost of the allotted plot had been accepted by the appellant. Respondent has been wrongfully denied the benefit of allotment of the plot. Therefore, the quashing of the cancellation of the allotted plot by the HC is legal and valid, the same does not warrant interference by Court. Appellant is directed to re-allot the plot originally allotted to the respondent, and if the same is not available, an alternative plot, be allotted to him within a period of 4 weeks. Appeal dismissed.
