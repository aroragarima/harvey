State of Maharashtra v Marwanjee F. Desai and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20364; (2002) 2 SCC 318; AIR 2002 SC 456; 2002 (2) Bom.C.R. 33; JT 2001 (10) SC 379; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 442; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 16; 2001(8) SCALE 509; 2001 (8) SLT 421; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 647; 2001 (8) Supreme 698

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Discovery and production of documents

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Bombay Government Premises (Eviction) Act, 1955, ss. 4, 7, 9 and 10 - Competent authority - Decision of competent authority to evacuate and State Govt. notification of rejecting such decision in dispute - Whether High Court in its decision that State Govt. has no right of appeal against the decision of competent authority is valid and in accordance with law? - Held, appointment of competent authority is administrative and neither judicial nor quasi judicial u/s. 3 of Act and HC erred in its decision and directed to be heard by District Judge - Appeal allowed without any costs.
