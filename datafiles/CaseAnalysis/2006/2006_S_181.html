Mohd. Yousuf v Smt. Afaq Jahan and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1360; (2006) 1 SCC (Cr) 460; (2006) 1 SCC 627; 2006 (2) ADJ 214; AIR 2006 SC 705; 2006 CRLJ 788; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1742; 2006 (1) JCC 189; JT 2006 (1) SC 10; 2006 (1) RCR(Criminal) 450; 2006 (2) RLW 962; 2006(1) SCALE 1; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1; 2006 (1) UC 95

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 156(3) and 202 - Appellant shown to be guarantor for loan taken by respondent no.1 - Appellant contended that respondent had forged some documents and he had never signed document and his signature was forged - Appellant filed application before CJM alleging commission of offences by respondent - CJM directed police to register and investigate the case - Respondent challenged order of CJM - High Court quashed charge sheet on grounds that magistrate had no power to order registration of case - Whether HC right in quashing charge sheet? - Magistrate orders investigation under Chap. XII he does so before he takes cognizance of offence - Investigation is only for helping Magistrate to decide whether or not there is sufficient ground for him to proceed further - Petition addresses to magistrate containing allegation is complaint - Held, before taking cognizance of offence, Magistrate can order investigation u/s. 156(3) of CrPC, 1973 - Order passed by HC unsustainable and quashed - Appeal allowed.
