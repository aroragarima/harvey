A.C. Muthiah v Board Of Control For Cricket In India And Another

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 322; (2011) 6 SCC 617; JT 2011 (5) SC 286; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 484; 2011(5) SCALE 68; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 445

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: ICC Regulations, Board of Control for Cricket in India Code, 2008, BCCI Regulations, 1994

Summary: J. M. Panchal, J.

(A) Trusts & Associations - Administrative - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Board of Control for Cricket in India (BCCI) Regulations - Administrator - Meaning - 1st respondent (BCCI) sent an invitation to a company, represented by its Managing Director (2nd respondent) to participate in auction conducted by Indian Premier League (IPL) - 2nd respondent was Honorary Treasurer of BCCI and President of State Cricket Association at relevant time - 2nd respondent participated in auction and was awarded franchised IPL rights for ownership of a team - Appellant, being Ex-President of BCCI, addressed a letter to President of BCCI and made complaint about violation of cl. 6.2.4 of BCCI Regulations by 2nd respondent alleging that he was liable to be penalized since he was Managing Director of a company, which was one of the franchisee of IPL and IPL being an event of BCCI, he had thereby acquired commercial interest and violated terms of cl. 6.2.4 of BCCI Regulations - Since no action was taken by President of BCCI on complaints submitted by appellant, appellant filed a civil suit in HC seeking a permanent injunction to restrain BCCI from permitting 2nd respondent to participate in General Body Meeting or on any other subsequent date, which would be convened in relation to election of new office bearers - Appellant also sought permanent mandatory injunction directing BCCI to initiate inquiry u/cl. 32(ii) of Regulations, by appointing a Commissioner to make a preliminary inquiry against 2nd respondent - Another relief claimed by appellant in said suit was for mandatory injunction directing BCCI to exercise his powers as per cl. 8(6) of Regulations by suspending 2nd respondent as Treasurer of BCCI pending inquiry - HC did not grant ex-parte temporary injunctions as prayed for by appellant - 2nd respondent subsequently participated in Annual General Meeting wherein he was elected as Secretary of BCCI - On the same day an amendment in cl. 6.2.4 of BCCI Regulations was made to effect that participation of administrators in IPL or Champions League Twenty 20 would be allowable - Aggrieved by amendment, appellant filed another suit seeking for declaration of amended cl. 6.4.2 as illegal and mandatory injunction against respondents - Single Judge and DB subsequently dismissed appellant's suits - Whether appellant should be regarded as administrator within meaning of Rules and Regulations of BCCI - Held, appellant was not a member, as defined in cl. 1(b) of Regulations and he was not a Full Member or an Association member nor an Affiliate Member of BCCI within meaning of cl. 3 of Regulations - Though term 'Administrator' included a past president, same would be applicable to a past president only in so far as he was connected with administration of BCCI in representing as a nominated member of sub-committee of BCCI - Appellant would have to be considered as an outsider and hence, he was not entitled to maintain two suits against BCCI claiming that he was an administrator - Further, appellant had no where claimed in plaints of two suits that he sought to maintain suits as an administrator - Thus finding recorded by Single Judge which was confirmed by DB that appellant did not claim or seek to maintain suits as an administrator, was upheld - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 34 - Declaratory suit - Relief u/s. 34 - Entitlement - Whether appellant was entitled to declaratory decree u/s. 34 of Act - Held, averments made in plaints of two suits did not indicate that appellant was claiming that he was entitled to declaration relating to a legal character or he was claiming any legal character - Appellant was not claiming any legal character in BCCI nor was he claiming any right to any of properties of BCCI - Further, records did not indicate that any personal right of appellant was infringed - Appellant, therefore, had not instituted two suits u/s. 34 of Act - Appeal dismissed.

(C) Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 91, O. 1 r. 8 - Suits filed in personal capacity - Nature of - Whether appellant had filed suit in a representative character spoken of, and referred to in O. 1 r. 8 of CPC or whether he had filed public interest suits u/s. 91 of CPC - Held, appellant filed suits in his individual capacity - Paragraph which dealt with cause of action in plaint inter alia mentioned that appellant had filed suits in capacity of an Administrator - Further, instant suits had not been filed by appellant invoking provisions of s. 91 of CPC - Appellant could have filed a Public Interest Litigation in form of writ petition - Appellant's claim that he was an administrator of BCCI and had filed two suits in that capacity was rightly not accepted by HC - Appeal dismissed.

(D) Trusts & Association - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Amendment of bye-laws of Society - Interference - Scope - Whether appellant's challenge of amendment made in bye-laws of BCCI was sustainable - Held, memorandum, rules and bye-laws framed by BCCI were found to be in conformity with object of Act and, therefore, it was registered as a Society under Act - It was an admitted case between parties that no amendment had been made to registered memorandum, rules and regulations or bye-laws - Appellant failed to establish that amendment made in subsidiary regulation 6.2.4 was opposed to policy laid down in Act or memorandum and rules and regulations and/or bye-laws of BCCI approved under said Act - Second suit at instance of appellant, therefore, was not maintainable at all - In a private Society what was in interest of Society had to be primarily decided by Society alone and such a question was not left for determination of an outside agency - Interest of Society was paramount and that interest would prevail so long as there was nothing in Act or rules governing Society prohibiting promotion of such interest - Hence, appellant, who was not even a member of Society, could not challenge validity of bye-laws of Society, which had been validly passed by General Body of Society - As was evident from record of case amendments in subsidiary regulation were made by General Body unanimously and, therefore, second suit would have to be regarded as not maintainable - Further, BCCI should not be regarded as an instrumentality of State and hence two suits filed by appellant were not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.

(E) Trusts & Association - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Participation of IPL auction - Violation of Players Regulations - Whether 2nd respondent, being a participant in IPL auction, had violated unamended clause 6.2.4 of Rules and Regulations of BCCI - Held, Players Regulations promulgated on year 2000 had not contemplated nor covered events like IPL, Champions League T20, etc., because they were unknown and never existed - Expression matches or events in unamended cl. 6.2.4 of Rules and Regulations should not be construed to include events like T20 cricket as those events were introduced after year 2000 - Contention of appellant that 2nd respondent had violated unamended Players Regulations and injunctions as prayed for should have been granted, could not be accepted and was rejected - Appeal dismissed.

(F) Trusts & Association - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975, s. 12 - Amendment in Players Regulations - Validity - Whether Players Regulations were properly amended or amendments made were illegal - Held, record showed that not a single member objected to proposed amendment - On contrary record unerringly showed that resolution relating to impugned amendment was passed unanimously by members - Rules and Regulations, which were organic and constitutional documents of association, were framed under Act - S. 12 provided that it was only if a bye-law or objects of association mentioned in Memorandum was intended to be amended that such amendment was required to be registered, however, that provision had no application to amendments of Players' Regulations, which had been framed by BCCI's working committee - Even if a member had limited rights against body/institution of which he was a member, a member should show that impugned act was ultra vires constitution or Memorandum of Association or bye-laws of Society and that act complained of constituted a fraud on member or that impugned action was illegal - Appellant was admittedly not even a member of BCCI and was, therefore, not entitled prima facie to challenge process of amendment, hence, amendments carried out in cl. 6.2.4 were perfectly legal and valid - Appeal dismissed.

(G) Trusts & Association - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6 r. 4 - Amendment of BCCI Regulation - Conflict of interest - Mala fide - Whether there was any conflict of interest as far as participation of 2nd respondent in IPL auction or any mala fides in amendment of cl. 6.2.4 of Regulations - Held, contention relating to conflict of interest was thoroughly misconceived and proceeded on certain presumptions which had no factual basis - As far as BCCI is concerned, all decisions relating to management and administration were taken by its Managing Committee - Managing Committee consisted of nine members of whom 2nd respondent was one of members - Bye-laws of BCCI unerringly indicated that all decisions, which may be taken by Managing Committee of BCCI, had got to be approved by General Body - Though appellant had claimed that there was factual and palpable conflict of interest, appellant could not explain to Court as to what was factual conflict of interest and how BCCI was put to financial loss because of participation by 2nd respondent in bidding process for IPL team - Appellant having claimed that there was factual and palpable conflict of interest between BCCI and company represented by 2nd respondent, should have attempted to make same good by necessary and specific averments - However, pleadings did not even remotely satisfy requirements of O. 6 r. 4 CPC - Plaint did not disclose that there were any particulars or specific allegations of mala fides against sub-committee, Managing Committee or General Body of BCCI - Amendment in cl. 6.2.4 was introduced after it had passed scrutiny of three different committees - In absence of necessary pleadings it would be difficult for appellant to get any relief in two suits - Plea based on mala fides and conflict of interest in amending cl. 6.2.4 could not be accepted - Appeal dismissed.

(H) Trusts & Association - Practice & Procedure - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Injunction - Grant of - Whether HC was right in refusing to grant mandatory temporary injunction/temporary injunctions claimed by appellant - Held, appellant failed to establish strong prima facie case in his favour for grant of mandatory temporary injunctions - Appellant's suits were not maintainable, therefore, he was not entitled to any interim relief - Amendment impugned was not found to be contrary to provisions of Act - Appellant failed to establish that because of so called conflict of interest, 2nd respondent had caused financial loss to BCCI - Further, irreparable injury was likely to be suffered by 2nd respondent if interim reliefs as claimed by appellant were granted - 2nd respondent explained in his pleadings that, in terms of Memorandum and Regulations of BCCI, he would not be able to contest election for post of President for next twelve years if he was restrained from contesting for post of President in the current year - Hence, prejudice would be caused to 2nd respondent if injunctions as prayed for by appellant were granted - Further, balance of convenience was also in favour of 2nd respondent because even if suits were decreed, no personal relief would accrue for benefit of appellant - HC, therefore, was justified in not granting temporary injunction claimed by appellant - Appeal dismissed.

Gyan Sudha Misra, J.

(I) Trusts & Associations - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975 - Past President of BCCI - Locus standi - Whether appellant had locus standi to file a civil suit in HC so as to challenge amendment introduced by BCCI u/cl. 6.2.4 of its Regulations - Held, suits were filed by appellant in capacity of past president of BCCI since he was an Administrator u/cl. 1(n) of Regulation - As such, appellant was competent to institute a suit in his individual capacity since cl. 1(n) of Regulation should not be allowed to result into a provision rendering it nugatory by overlooking express provision of definition of Administrator which unambiguously includes past President - Cl. 1(n) clearly indicated that past presidents also had been unequivocally included within meaning of 'Administrator' and while an Administrator could also be included as a Member of Sub-Committee for Disciplinary Committee, it should not be interpreted so as to infer that former president stood excluded from definition of Administrator until and unless he was a member of Sub-Committee for disciplinary proceedings - Hence, definition of term 'Administrator' did not exclude past president from meaning of Administrator so as to hold that action taken by Administrator by filing a civil suit and questioning amendment introduced by BCCI in cl. 6.2.4 was not fit to be entertained on ground that appellant had no locus standi to challenge amendment on ground of his competence or locus standi - Appeal dismissed.

(J) Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 12, 136, 226, 227 - Suits against BCCI - Maintainability - Whether appellant's plea of violation of fundamental rights of BCCI since BCCI was not a State within meaning of art. 12 of Constitution - Held, instant appeal by special leave was not a petition u/art. 32 of Constitution but was an appeal u/art. 136 of Constitution arising out of an order passed in a civil suit refusing to grant injunction which was filed in two regular civil suits - Once, it was held that appellant was also an Administrator of BCCI in view of definition of Administrator, his competence to challenge amendment introduced in regulation of BCCI should not be held as not maintainable on ground that BCCI was not a 'State' within meaning of art.12 of Constitution as civil suits could surely be filed and could be held maintainable if plaintiff was able to make out a case that cause of action had arisen for filing a suit and if he was able to sustain cause of action and he also was able to establish that he was proper party to suit, same would have to be tried by Court and should not be dismissed on ground of its maintainability - In fact, when a civil suit was filed for seeking civil remedy, question whether contesting party satisfied condition that it was an instrumentality of State was of no relevance as civil courts did not have to discharge constitutional function so as to enter into this question - Appeal allowed.

(K) Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Injunction - Grant of - Whether HC was justified in rejecting appellant's application for injunction - Held, BCCI itself incorporated cl. 6.2.4 wherein it laid down that administrator should not have directly or indirectly any commercial interest in any of events of BCCI - BCCI subsequently without any deliberation and discussion introduced an amendment into clause by making Twenty -20 IPL or Champions League Matches an exception to that rule for which BCCI could not come out with any plausible explanation - Appellant clearly came out with a strong prima facie case that amendment was introduced with an oblique motive to benefit 2nd respondent so that he could not be held disqualified from participating in auction and own a team in IPL while continuing as Treasurer and thereafter as Secretary of BCCI and hence an Administrator and thus appellant succeeded in establishing his plea that amendment introduced by BCCI in cl. 6.2.4 was an abuse of amending power exercised by BCCI in so far as power of amendment was introduced not to promote game of cricket but to promote interest of 2nd respondent as it was more than clear that without amendment, 2nd respondent would not have been entitled to participate in bid as he was a Treasurer of BCCI and hence without amendment he was not eligible even to participate in bid and enjoy dual status of that of an office bearer of BCCI as Treasurer and also own a team in IPL - Appellant fully succeeded in making out a prima facie case that impugned amendment smacked of arbitrariness and bias in favour of 2nd respondent and hence it was a fit case for grant of injunction keeping impugned amendment introduced in cl. 6.2.4 of BCCI's Regulation under suspension or abeyance - Courts below while considering application for injunction was fully competent to mould relief in a given circumstance or situation which it had miserably failed to do - Further, rule of equity and fairness provided that no one who stood in a position of trust towards another could in matters affected by that position, advanced his own interests for example, by trading and making a profit at that other's expense as rule of legal prudence mandated that once a fiduciary was shown to be in breach of his duty of loyalty, he should disgorge any benefit gained even though he might have acted honestly and in his principal's best interest - In instant matter, when BCCI held auction for owning IPL team and an Administrator i.e., 2nd respondent participated in bid, variety of real and/or perceived conflict of interest should not be ruled out including access to insider information, possible undue influence on decision makers who held auction and like - Hence, injunction granted by directing suspension of operation of impugned amendment - Appeal allowed.
