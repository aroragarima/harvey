M.S. Madhusoodhanan and Another v Kerala Kaumudi Private Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 612; (2004) 9 SCC 204; AIR 2004 SC 909; 2003 (6) ALD(SC) 1; [2003] 117 Comp Cas 19; JT 2003 (6) SC 560; 2003(6) SCALE 191; 2003 (1) SCW 7165; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 107

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Indian Companies Act, 1913 - Companies Act, 1956 - ss.31(1), 53, 108, 155, 193, 194, 195, 284, 446 - Indian Sale of Goods Act, 1930, ss. 2(10), 4, 8(2), 9, 9(2) - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 29 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114, 16, 4 - Controlling interest in a company - Held, transfers by Mani and his children were effected validly to Madhusoodhanan their prayer for rectification of share register is therefore rejected - Since further resolution to delete art.74 formed no part of notice of Extraordinary General Meeting, which in all fairness it should have, special resolution on basis of such defective notice is insupportable in law and cannot be given effect to - This finding is sufficient to hold that deletion of art.74 of Articles of company was invalid and that therefore Madhusoodhanan continued to be managing director of Kerala Kaumudi as claimed by him in CS 3/89 - Once it is held that Madhusoodhanan and his group all of whom held shares in Kerala Kaumudi, were not given notice to apply for allotment of additional shares, it must be held that subsequent allotment of shares to Ravi and Srinivasan at meeting held on 8-8-86 and affirmation of such allotment at meeting allegedly held on 16-6-86 were vitiated thereby and invalid - Further held, Articles of Association of KIPL themselves require compliance with s 108 before any transfer can be effected - When minutes recorded that share transfer deeds had been placed before Board, when transfers were approved by Board in presence of only witness for petitioners, and when none of documents which were duly maintained by company recording transfers of shares had been disproved, there cannot be a finding that share transfer deeds must have been improperly stamped or executed in violation of provisions of s 108, Companies Act.
