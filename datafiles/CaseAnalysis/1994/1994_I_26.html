Asstt. Excise Commissioner v Issac Peter

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Yogeshwar Dayal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2076; (1994) 4 SCC 104; AIR 1994 SCW 2616; JT 1994 (2) SC 140; 1994 (1) KarLJ 645; 1994 (1) KLT 698; 1994(1) SCALE 715; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 67

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Kerala Abkari Act, 1902, ss. 15 and 18-A - Kerala Abkari Shops (Disposal in Auction) Rules, 1974, rr. 6(26) and 85 - (A) Whether there was a failure on part of State in supplying additional quantities of arrack undertaken by it to supply? - Use of words 'may' and 'permit' in r. 8(1) clearly indicate a discretion in authority and discretion vested in Excise Commr. has to be exercised in a fair and reasonable manner - Held, said sub-r. can in no event be read as creating an obligation upon State to supply quantities demanded by licensee even if no such supplies are available - (B) Whether licensees entitled to any rebate/remission in amounts payable by them under contracts, on account of such failure, if any? - R. 6(26) which says that no remission or abatement in licence fee shall be 'claimable' by licensee 'on any account whatsoever' should be read along with r. 8(1), which sets out only situation in which duty and commission payable will be adjusted e.g. where Govt. fails to perform its statutory obligation i.e., if it fails to supply monthly quota referred to in r. 8(1) it may not be open to Govt. to invoke r. 6(26) - Further held, not only conditions are statutory in this case but they are formally drawn in shape of statutory rules and in absence of a statutory right in licensees to get additional supplies demanded by him, there is no basis in law for claim of remission or rebate - (C) Whether rule of legitimate expectation which licensees entertained in view of practice during previous years applicable in present case? - Further held, rule cannot be invoked to modify or vary express terms of contract, more so when they are statutory in nature - (D) Whether doctrine of fairness and reasonableness can be invoked in present case? - Doctrine of fairness or duty to act fairly and reasonably is a doctrine developed in administrative law filed to ensure rule of law and to prevent failure of justice where action is administrative in nature, but it can certainly not be invoked to amend, alter or vary express terms of contract between parties, even if contract is governed by statutory provisions, i.e., where it is a statutory contract - Further held, in case of contracts freely entered into with State, like present ones, there is no room for invoking doctrine of fairness and reasonableness against one party to contract (State), for purpose of altering or adding to terms and conditions of the contract, merely because it happens to be State - Order accordingly.
