Daya Kishan v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 401; (2010) 5 SCC 81; AIR 2010 SC 2147; 2010 CRLJ 2886; 2010 (3) JCC 1727; JT 2010 (4) SC 325; 2010(4) SCALE 263; [2010] 4 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Lalkara

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302 and 307 - Unlawful assembly - Murder - Attempt to murder - Conviction - Challenged - Sessions Judge framed charge against the appellant and other accused for commission of offences punishable u/ss. 148, 149, 323, 307 and 302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 IPC, s. 307 r/w s. 149 of IPC, s. 323 r/w s. 149 of IPC and s. 148 of IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - It was submitted that appellant could not have been fastened with the liability u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC for the death of deceased, which was caused by the other accused 'P' - Whether Sessions Court and the HC committed error in convicting the appellant u/s. 302 with the aid of s. 149 of IPC for causing death of deceased? - Held, s. 149 of IPC creates a constructive or vicarious liability on the members of the unlawful assembly for the unlawful acts committed pursuant to the common object by any other member of that assembly - Basis of the constructive guilt u/s. 149 of IPC is mere membership of the unlawful assembly, with the requisite common object or knowledge - There are two essential ingredients of s. 149, viz., (1) commission of an offence by any member of an unlawful assembly and (2) such offence must have been committed in prosecution of the common object of that assembly or must be such as the members of that assembly knew to be likely to be committed - In the present case, no evidence could be adduced by the prosecution to establish that common object of the unlawful assembly was to murder deceased or cause any injury to him - Prosecution has not led the evidence to establish nexus between the common object and the offence committed - Mere fact that the appellant was armed with a lathi by itself would not prove that he shared common object with which the main accused 'P' was inspired - On the peculiar facts and in the circumstances of the case it can be concluded that the appellant did not share common object of one of the members of the unlawful assembly to cause death of deceased - Conviction of the appellant recorded u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC set aside - Conviction of the appellant u/s. 307 r/w s. 149 of IPC for attempting to commit murder of injured witness, u/s. 323 r/w s. 149 of IPC and u/s. 148 IPC confirmed - Appeal disposed of.
