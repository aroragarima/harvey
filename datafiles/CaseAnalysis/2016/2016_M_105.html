Muddasani Venkata Narsaiah (D) Through Lrs. v Muddasani Sarojana

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 338; AIR 2016 SC 2250; 2016 (4) ALD(SC) 59; 2016(3) ALL MR 901; 2016 (117) ALR 19; 2016 (3) AWC 2949; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 207; 2016 (4) CTC 888; JT 2016 (12) SC 471; 2016 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2806; 2016 (5) MLJ 73; 2016 (2) OLR 324; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 236; 2016(4) SCALE 635; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Specific Relief Act, 1963

Summary: Land & Property - Alleged Execution of Sale deed - Title to property - Determination - Plaintiff filed suit before Trial Court for possession of disputed property and mesne profits based upon title - It is stated that after death of husband, property was succeeded by his wife - Further, after death of widow wife, property was succeeded by deceased widow's sister-in-law which was sold to plaintiff vide sale deed - However, defendant no.3 stated that she is adopted child of widowed wife, hence, defendant no.3 succeeded to property by inheritance - Trial Court and HC held that plaintiff failed to prove execution of sale deed in his favor, hence, suit for possession and mesne profits was not maintainable - Hence, present appeal.

Held, HC has ignored pleadings of parties and evidence on question of execution of sale deed which establishes that sale deed had been executed by sister-in-law of widow in favour of plaintiff. Further, in written statement filed on behalf of defendants, sale deed was denied for want of knowledge. Further, SC held that defendant no. 3 has not been able to establish her case that she is adopted daughter of deceased widow; hence, being third party, she could not have questioned execution of sale deed. Hence, HC held that HC erred in law in dismissing suit. SC held that property on death of deceased widow had been passed on to her sister-in law being class IInd heir and she had right to sell property to plaintiff. Even if deceased's sister in law had not placed plaintiff in possession of property on strength of his title conferred by way of sale deed in question he had right to recover possession. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and first Appellate Court is restored. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Title to property by way of execution of sale deed has to be proved on evidence on record.
