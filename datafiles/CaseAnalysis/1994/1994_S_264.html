Bajirao T.Kote (Dead ) By Lrs. & Anr. v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 685; (1995) 2 SCC 442; AIR 1995 SCW 218; 1995 (1) AnWR 13; JT 1995 (1) SC 85; 1994(5) SCALE 138; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 413

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Whether public purpose 'acquisition for Saibaba Sansthan' specified in notification u/s. 4(1) is vague and liable to be quashed? - When State Govt. exercised power u/s. 4(1) for public purpose, exercise of power cannot be invalidated on grounds of mala fides or colourable exercise of power so long as public purpose is shown and land is needed or is likely to be needed and purpose subsists at time of exercise of power - It is primarily for State Govt. to decide whether there exists public purpose or not, and it is not for Courts to evaluate evidence and come to its own conclusion whether or not there is public purpose unless it comes to conclusion that exercise of power serves no public purpose or serves a private purpose - Failure to purchase land by negotiation and exercise of power u/s. 4(1) cannot be considered to be a mala fide or colourable exercise of power - Held, it cannot be againsaid that providing access to temples is not a public purpose and exercise of power u/s. 4(1), therefore is neither colourable nor mala fide - Appeal dismissed.
