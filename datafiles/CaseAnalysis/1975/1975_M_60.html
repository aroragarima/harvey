Messrs Titagarh Paper Mills Limited v Orissa State Electricity Board and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 369; (1975) 2 SCC 436

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Arbitration & ADR - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49 and 59, sch. VI - Coal surcharge - Levy thereof - Against appellant - (A) Whether justified? - (B) Whether Board had power to levy such surcharge? - Before constitution of Board, agreement entered between State of Orissa and appellant - Terms and conditions - Appellant was to get electricity from 'hydro power station' - Neither s. 49 nor s. 59 confers any authority on Board to enhance rates for supply of electricity where they are fixed under a stipulation made in an agreement - Referred to Indian Aluminium Company v. Kerala State Electricity Board (1975 Indlaw SC 399) - (C) Whether the dispute referable to arbitration proceedings? - Press note in question - Relevant clause of arbitration agreement perused - Held, it only applies to a dispute or difference as to supply of electrical energy hereunder of pressure thereof or as to interpretation of this agreement, of right of supplier of consumer respectively to determine same or any other question, matter or thing arising thereunder - Instant dispute not referable to arbitration - Appeal dismissed.
