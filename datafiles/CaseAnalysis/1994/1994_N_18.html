U. P. Financial Corporation and Others v Naini Oxygen and Acetylene Gas Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1203; (1995) 2 SCC 754; AIR 1995 SCW 254; [1995] 82 Comp Cas 671; JT 1994 (7) SC 551; 1994(4) SCALE 1076; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 654

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Indirect Tax; Service

Keywords: Irbi

Summary: Banking & Finance - State Financial Corporation Act, 1951 - Uttar Pradesh Public Moneys (Recovery of Dues) Act - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Whether Corporation is obliged to invest further or should it realise its loan from sale of assets of Company? - Corporation is an independent autonomous statutory body - Unless malafide, even wrong decision taken by it is not open to challenge - Not for courts or third party to substitute its decision - So, conduct of Corporation cannot make it liable - Held, Corporation free to proceed as per law - Appeal allowed.
