M. S. Shivananda v Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 66; (1980) SCC (L&S) 131; (1980) 1 SCC 149; AIR 1980 SC 77; 1979 (39) FLR 452; 1980 (1) LLJ 77; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 684; 1979 (2) SLR 774; 1979 UJ 893

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Karnataka Contract Carriages (Acquisition) Act, 1976

Summary: Karnataka Contract Carriages (Acquisition) Act, 1976, ss. 19(3) and 31(2) - Karnataka Contract Carriages (Acquisition) Ordinance, 1976, cl. 20(3) - Whether employees of erstwhile contract carriage operators in State of Karnataka acquired a vested right of absorption in service with Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation under sub-cl. (3) to cl. 20 of 1976 Ordinance? - Held, not permissible, they do not acquire any vested rights under repealed Ordinance - Ratio of absorption is altered retrospectively by Act and assuming that employees had vested right of absorption under Ordinance it is expressly taken away by Act - Appeal dismissed.
