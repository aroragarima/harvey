Gangadharan v Janardhana Mallan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, A.M. Ahmadi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3787; (1996) 9 SCC 53; 1996 (5) AD(SC) 109; AIR 1996 SC 2127; 1996 (2) CCC 337; JT 1996 (5) SC 82; 1996(4) SCALE 537; 1996 (2) SCJ 506; 1998 (Supp) RD 19; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 713; 1996 (4) Supreme 356

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Law - Alienation - Respondents challenged alienation of joint Hindu family property on ground of lack of legal necessity - Trial Court and District Court dismissed the suit as major part of consideration was utilized to pay off family debts - High Court reversed findings of Trial Court and District Court - Whether the decision of the HC was sustainable? - Held, no - Alienee is required to establish is legal necessity for the transaction - Not necessary to prove that whole consideration was applied for meeting family necessity - Substantial portion of consideration used to pay off family debt - No strait jacket formula as to what proportion of the consideration should be shown to have been antecedent debt in order to sustain an alienation - Order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
