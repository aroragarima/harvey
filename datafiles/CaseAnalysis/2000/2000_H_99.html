V. H. Patel and Company and Others v Hirubhai Himabhai Patel and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 275; (2000) 4 SCC 368; 2000 (2) ARBLR 291; 2000 (4) Bom.C.R. 91; JT 2000 (5) SC 51; 2000 (20) PTC 250; 2000 (2) RAJ 215; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 735; 2000(3) SCALE 369; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 113; 2000 (4) Supreme 671

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Intellectual Property - Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958 - Whether arbitrator was competent to entertain the counter-claim filed by Respondent 1 for dissolution of the firm and falls within the scope of the terms of reference made by court earlier? - Court held, if under the terms of the reference all disputes and difference arising between the parties have been referred to arbitration, the arbitrator will, in general, be able to deal with all matters, including dissolution, no principle of law or any provision which bars an arbitrator to examine such a question - Petition dismissed.
