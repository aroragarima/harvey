Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation v B. S. Hullikatti

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20334; (2001) SCC (L&S) 469; (2001) 2 SCC 574; AIR 2001 SC 930; 2001 (1) CLR 699; JT 2001 (2) SC 72; 2001 (34) LabIC 740; 2001 (1) LLJ 725; 2001 (1) LLN 893; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 7; 2001(1) SCALE 373; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 487; 2001 (1) UC 396; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 829

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Misconduct - Reinstatement - Entitled to - Held, respondent has been in service for 22 years and thus is expected to be aware of correct fare - It is misplaced sympathy by labour courts in such cases when on checking it is found that bus conductors have either not issued tickets to a large number of passengers, though they should have, or have issued tickets of a lower denomination knowing fully well correct fare to be charged - It is responsibility of bus conductors to collect correct fare from passengers and deposit same with Co. - They act in a fiduciary capacity and it would be a case of gross misconduct if knowingly they do not collect any fare or correct amount of fare - Dismissal is justifiable - Appeal disposed of.
