Ashok Kumar Todi v Kishwar Jahan & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 142; (2011) 3 SCC 758; AIR 2011 SC 1254; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 1627; 2011 (3) CalLT 9; 2011 CRLJ 2317; JT 2011 (3) SC 50; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 570; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 145; 2011(3) SCALE 94; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 597

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173 - Special Marriage Act, 1954, s. 11 - Unnatural death of deceased who was married to petitioner's daughter under the Special Marriage Act - It was alleged that the deceased was killed by the hired men at the instance of the petitioner - Investigation started into deceased's death - The deceased's mother and brother not satisfied with the investigation by the State CID as well as due to mounting pressure by higher officials of the State Police Department, filed writ application praying for criminal investigation to be conducted by CBI and not by CID - Interim orders passed by Single Judge directing CBI to investigate into the cause of unnatural death of the deceased - Report was submitted by CBI - Thereafter, Single Judge allowed the writ application directing enquiry by CBI - Pursuant to that order, CBI after investigation filed a charge sheet u/s. 120-B r/w ss. 306 and 506 of IPC - On appeal, DB set aside the judgment and order passed by the Single Judge and directed the CBI to start investigation afresh in accordance with law by treating the complaint filed by the brother of the deceased as FIR and to register a case of murder - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether the order of the Single Judge was sustainable? - Held, it was established from the materials on record that there was genuine apprehension in the mind of the family members of deceased that there might not be fair investigation at the instance of the CID in respect of the unnatural death of deceased because of the alleged involvement of the high police officials in the post marital dispute between accused persons and the deceased - Single Judge duly considered the materials presented and on finding that the investigation by the State CID was not proper, therefore, the CBI was directed to investigate the cause of death of deceased - Single Judge was fully justified in passing interim order appointing the CBI to investigate into the unnatural death of deceased and submit a report - Also, Single Judge's final order accepting the report and granting opportunity to the CBI to proceed in accordance with law for filing charge sheet before the Competent Court u/s. 173(2) of the CrPC was justified - (B) Whether holding of DB that the Single Judge had not restrained the State CID from proceeding with the investigation in accordance with the CrPC was correct? - Held, when the Single Judge on satisfying himself based on the materials, particularly, the conduct of the State Police and the apprehension of the mother and brother of the deceased about getting fair justice at the hands of the State CID directed investigation by the CBI, there could not be any parallel investigation by the State CID - In the interim order, the Single Judge decided the question whether investigation by the CID was just, fair and proper or whether such investigation should be conducted by the CBI - Hence, merely because no injunction was passed against the CID from continuing with the investigation in the matter or no order was passed directing the CID to handover all the papers relating to investigation conducted by them to the CBI, did not mean that CID was free to continue with their investigation - Thus, the finding of the DB that the Single Judge appointed CBI as its 'Special Officer' was patently against all canons of justice, equity and fair play in action - (C) Whether the decision of the DB, setting aside the order of the Single Judge, directing the CBI to start investigation afresh? - Held, once an FIR had been registered lawfully and investigation had been conducted leading to filing of charge sheet before the competent court of law for the trial of accused persons, there was no justifiable reason for the DB to direct re-registration of the same by lodging another FIR after three years and proceed with the investigation which had already been concluded by the CBI - Further, direction of the Single Judge was in accordance with law and the CBI investigated the case in terms of the said order and submitted report based on which it was permitted to file a report before an appropriate Court and also adduced liberty to reinvestigate the issue if not arise, the DB erred in directing the CBI to start investigation afresh - There was no need to register another FIR when in respect of the same offence an FIR had already been registered - Appeals disposed of.
