Sahebrao Mohan Berad v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 178; (2011) 4 SCC 249; 2011 CRLJ 2157; 2011 (2) G.L.R. 1778; JT 2011 (3) SC 477; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 185; 2011 (2) RCR(Criminal) 608; 2011(3) SCALE 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 201 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - A report was filed before police stating that the deceased (wife of appellant) met with an accidental death due to drowning - During the investigation it was found that the deceased met with the homicidal death and accordingly police submitted charge-sheet u/ss. 302 r/w ss. 34 and 201 of the IPC against the appellant and his parents - Trial Court acquitted all of them - On appeal, as the Judges constituting the Bench differed in their opinion regarding the guilt of the appellant and the cause of death, the appeal was referred for decision to third Judge - It was held that the circumstances proved guilt of appellant beyond all reasonable doubt - Order of the Trial Court acquitting the appellant was set aside and he was held guilty for offence punishable u/s. 302 and 201 of the IPC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether deceased met with an accidental death due to drowning and, therefore, the conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 and 201 of the IPC was not correct? - Held, as per medical evidence the death was due to strangulation - Doctor specifically denied the suggestion that the deceased met with an accidental death due to drowning - Hence, HC was right in holding that the circumstances proved pointed out towards the guilt of the appellant and further deceased met with a homicidal death - Appeal dismissed.
