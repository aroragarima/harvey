Afsar Sheikh and Another v Soleman Bibi and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 379; (1976) 2 SCC 142; AIR 1976 SC 163; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 327

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 -ss.100,101,103- Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Will - Misrepresentation - Evidence - Undue influence - Plaintiff's alleged S fraudulently got executed and registered will, in favour of his wife as he was illiterate and aged of 90 years - Plaintiff stated appellant/A took him for execution of deed cancelling Will and got executed and registered Hiba in his favour purporting to be transfer of lands by him - A sold some of land to defendant - Plaintiff contended that sale-deed executed by A was bogus and without consideration and did not confer title on transferee - A and defendant denied allegations of fraud and misrepresentation - Defendant stated plaintiff transferred land voluntarily and executed Hiba as donor - Defendant averred that he was in possession of lands since execution of Hiba - Trial Court found that there was no fraud or misrepresentation on part of A - Plaintiff appealed to District Court was dismissed - Plaintiff appealed to HC was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether burden of proof shifted to defendant in execution of hiba was justified by HC - Held, there was no evidence to show that mental capacity of donor was temporally or permanently affected by old age that he could not understand nature of deed and its execution - None of circumstances mentioned u/s. 16(2), had proved that donee was in position to dominate will of donor - Plaintiff failed to prove that defendant was in position to dominate his will - Hence, plaintiff had failed to substantiate first element essential to proof of undue influence - HC was wrong in holding burden had shifted defendant to show that Hiba was not induced by undue influence - Appeal allowed.
