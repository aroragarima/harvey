Tata Teleservices Limited v Bharat Sanchar Nigam Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. Sudershan Reddy

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2483; (2008) 10 SCC 556; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1041; 2008 (2) CompLJ 405; JT 2008 (5) SC 657; 2008(6) SCALE 523; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 308

Case Digest

Subject: Telecom

Keywords: Access Providers, TRAI (Amendment) Act, 2000, Satellite, Wll, Wireline, Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges Regulation 2003

Summary: Telecom - Administrative - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 4 - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997, ss. 11(1)(b)(i), 11(b)(ii), 13, 36 - TRAI (Amendment) Act, 2000 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges (IUC) Regulation 2003 - Telecommunication Interconnection Usage Charges Regulation 2003 - Access Providers - Tariff Cellular - Service Provider - ADC was levy imposed by Regulator on operators (service providers) to support roll out of telephones in rural areas - Since BSNL owns 99% of rural phones, ADC constitutes levy for appellant and subsidy for BSNL - March, 1997, Telecom Regulatory Authority of India ('TRAI') Act was enacted - Govt. introduced New Telecommunication Policy ('NTP') in 1999 and proceeded to implement said policy - Amendment segregated Regulatory and Dispute Settlement norms of original TRAI - TDSAT was conferred with original and appellate jurisdictions as per new regime - In order to separate policy making and service provision roles of DoT, Govt. created Department of Telecom Services ('DTS'), which was later turned into corporate entity - Under NTP, 1999, all new cellular mobile service providers had to pay fixed fees upon entry, and then pay portion of their revenues to Govt. - However, after August, 1999 revenue-sharing arrangement came into effect - Given ambitious target to achieve tele-density of 7%, NTP 1999 sought to bring private players into basic service which was minimum facility and in which mobility as feature of telecom service was not part of basic service - Permissibility to provide service was determined by terms and conditions of licence granted by DoT whereas obligation to pay interconnection usage charges/ADC was determined by TRAI through its regulations framed u/s. 36 of the 1997 Act in conformity with licence conditions - By policy decision of Govt. of India in 2001, basic service operators having licence for providing fixed service were allowed to provide Wireless Local Loop Mobile [WLL(M))] service within purview of their basic service licence - During 1997 April, 2003, there was no liability to pay ADC - On 1-11-2003, DoT introduced Unified Access Service (UAS) licence which allowed its holder to provide wire-line as well as wireless services in a service area - However, wireless services included full mobile, limited mobile and fixed wireless services under UAS licence - Existing service providers were given option to stay on their original licence or change to UAS licence to facilitate communications convergence by allowing value-added services on same licence - However, all telecom operators had to pay IUC including ADC in accordance with IUC Regulations framed by TRAI WLL Technology - Hence, instant appeals - Whether appellant was liable to pay Access Deficit Charges (ADC) to BSNL for period commencing from 14-11-2004 to 26-8-2005 in respect of its service provided under its brand name 'WALKY' - Held, context in which Consultation Paper emanated had been explained by us in our Judgment in Civil Appeal of Reliance Infocomm Ltd - Court held that it had looked into technology aspect and policy framework for levy of ADC hence there was no unilateralism as alleged by appellant - It was alleged by appellants and also by Reliance Infocomm Ltd. in conjoint appeal which was separately dealt with in subsequent judgment that BSNL had also not disclosed their numbering levels for their fixed wireless service and for their LL(M) services which they had been providing during relevant period in name of 'Tarang', which according to appellants, would now constitutes WLL(M) service - According to appellants, BSNL had also been providing fixed wireless phone services which had limited mobility - This was matter of quantification - That stage had not yet arrived - BSNL, had fairly stated that BSNL would abide by parameters laid down in judgment and whatever adjustments required to be made in that regard in context of claims and counter claims, same shall be worked out in near future - Court further expressed no opinion on point of quantification which question did not arise even before TDSAT in present case - Services of appellants vide instrument Walky falls in category of WLL(M) service and, accordingly, appellants would be liable to pay ADC in that regard during relevant period 14-11-2004 to 26-8-2005 - Appeals dismissed.
