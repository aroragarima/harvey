Ram Chandra Bhagat v State Of Jharkhand

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.R. Dave, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 464; (2013) 1 SCC 562; JT 2012 (11) SC 277; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 178; 2012(11) SCALE 115; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 1050

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 439 - Deceitful cohabitation - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offence u/s. 493 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 493 of IPC and sentenced him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 3 months and to pay fine - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was dismissed - Thereafter, appellant filed a revision petition before HC, which was dismissed by impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of conviction of appellant was sustainable - Held, appellant used to visit complainant (victim) from time to time and had promised complainant to marry her - Perusal of evidence, appellant had got a form, with regard to marriage registration, signed by complainant - Form was signed by appellant and induced the complainant to sign the form so as to get married, which fact made the complainant to believe that appellant had married her and, thus, she had started residing with him as his wife - In fact, appellant did not marry the complainant, though rituals necessary for Hindu marriage were never performed - It is an admitted fact that no marriage was took place between complainant and appellant but only on the basis of documents signed by complainant at instance of appellant, complainant was made to believe that she was a lawfully married wife of appellant - As a result of cohabitation, complainant had given birth to 2 children and several ceremonies in relation to birth of children were performed by appellant, which ultimately resulted into a belief in the mind of complainant that she was a lawfully married wife of appellant though she was not - Appellant had practiced deception and as a result thereof the complainant believed that she was a lawfully married wife of appellant and thereafter there was cohabitation and sexual intercourse as a result of deception - Impugned conviction order was justified - Appeal dismissed.

R. M. Lodha, J.

Held, if a woman was induced to change her status from that of an unmarried to that of a married woman with all duties and obligations pertaining to changed relationship and that result was accomplished by deceit, such woman within the law could be said to have deceived and offence u/s. 493 of IPC was brought home - Essence of an offence u/s. 493 IPC was, practice of deception by a man on a woman as a consequence of which the woman was led to believe that she was lawfully married to him although she was not and then make her cohabit with him - In instant case, an agreement (Ex.2) was executed for marriage certificate on 4-6-1982 wherein appellant admitted that he was living a normal family life as a married couple with complainant - Appellant had practiced deception on complainant causing a false belief of existence of lawful marriage and making her cohabit with him in that belief - Appeal dismissed.
