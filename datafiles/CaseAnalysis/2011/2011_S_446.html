Rajesh Kumar v State through (Government of NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

28 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 678; (2011) 13 SCC 706; 2011 (183) DLT 78; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 85; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 459; 2011(11) SCALE 182; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 92

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Mitigating Circumstance

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure (Amendment) Act, 1955 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 268, 309, 367, 367(5) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 235, 235(2), 313, 354(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.302 - American Constitution - Murder of Two Children - Conviction - Death Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted u/s. 302 IPC and awarded death sentence for committing murder of two children - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and upheld conviction by holding that case had fallen in category of rarest of rare cases - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that Trial Court and HC had improperly balanced consideration of aggravating and mitigating circumstances and death sentence awarded should not be sustained - Whether mitigating circumstances were properly weighed in accordance with well-known judicial principles to award of death sentence to appellant for murdering two children - Held, u/s. 235(2) of the 1973 Code, question of sentence was in altogether different domain in which facts and factors which operate on entirely different order than those which come into play on question of conviction - Person might be deprived of his life or personal liberty in accordance with fair, just and reasonable procedure established by valid law - English Bill of Rights drafted century ago postulates that excessive bail ought not to be required, nor excessive fines imposed, nor cruel and unusual punishments inflicted - HC while discussing mitigating circumstances as against aggravating circumstances had not properly followed principles discussed in Bachan Singh's case 1980 Indlaw SC 586 - Categories of mitigating and aggravating circumstances were never close and no Court could give exhaustive list of such circumstances - State had failed to show that appellant was continuing threat to society or that appellant was beyond reform and rehabilitation - HC had taken very narrow and myopic view of mitigating circumstances about appellant - It was no doubt that murder was committed in instant case in very brutal and inhuman fashion, but that alone should not justify infliction of death penalty - Every murder was brutal, and difference between one from other might be on account of mitigating or aggravating features surrounding murder - Mitigating circumstances in favour of appellant were not properly considered - Except in 'rarest of rare cases' and for 'special reasons' death sentence should not be imposed as alternative option to imposition of life sentence - Hence, death sentence imposed by HC Should not be sustained and death sentence imposed upon appellant was substituted by sentence of imprisonment for life - Appeal allowed.
