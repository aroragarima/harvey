Union of India and others v Sohan Lal Sayal and others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 709; 2015 (145) FLR 242; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 948

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Telephone, Junior

Summary: Service - Criteria for determination of seniority - Appellant-BSNL took steps by which seniority which was determined in year 1993 and covered by 17 lists were reversed and different set of officers were favoured with seniority and promotions - Aggrieved by such reversal, employees who were part of Lists drawn in year 1993, approached Tribunal and by its order dt.29-5-2009, Tribunal interfered with subsequent action of appellant-BSNL and assailing said order, appellant-BSNL filed writ petition before HC wherein impugned order was passed upholding orders of Tribunal - Hence, instant appeal by appellant-BSNL - Whether criteria applicable for purpose of determining seniority inter-se is order of passing departmental qualifying examination or relevant recruitment order/ order of entry.

Held, after specific directions contained in paragraphs 17 and 19 of Union of India v. Madras Telephones Scheduled Castes & Scheduled Tribes Social Welfare Association, 2000 INDLAW SC 2861, when rights of private respondents got crystallized based on specific stand of the appellant taken in its undertaking dt.27-2-1992 and subsequent 17 seniority lists drawn by it, appellant was wholly unjustified in having taken a 'U'-turn in year 2000 and reverse seniority of all those who were covered by those 17 Lists. Further, when in said judgment, SC made categorical and clear pronouncement as to how the latter principle laid down in judgment of Union of India v. Madras Telephones Scheduled Castes & Scheduled Tribes Social Welfare Association, 1997 INDLAW SC 3052 should prevail without affecting the rights of those whose cases were already determined and reached a finality based on orders of the Courts, appellant ought not to have meddled with their seniority and subsequent promotions and the benefits granted on that basis in respect of those officers covered by 17 Lists drawn in the year 1993. Hence, there is no scope to interfere with impugned judgment and so, same are upheld. Further, in the interest of appellant-BSNL and large number of employees whose grievances are to be sufficiently examined, considered and safeguarded with minimum disturbance in the matter of fixing their seniority as well as promotions already granted in their favour or to be restored, detailed consideration of respective stand need to be made and hence, Expert Committee is constituted. Further, Expert Committee is to suggest the way out for balancing rights of two groups of employees based on principles laid down in instant judgment. Hence, interim applications are allowed and leave granted for all petitions for special leave and appeals disposed of with directions to list matters for orders after receipt of Report from Expert Committee. Order accordingly.
