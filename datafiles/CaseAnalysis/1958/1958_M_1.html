S. Pl. Narayanan Chettiar v M. Ar. Annamalai Chettiar

Supreme Court of India

31 October 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 171; AIR 1959 SC 275; 1959 (2) AnWR 55; 1959 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 237; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 237

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Agriculture & Agri Products - Madras Agriculturists' Relief (Amendment) Act, s. 19(2) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Appellant made an application to Trial Court for scaling down decretal debt u/s. 19(2) of Act which was added by amending Act - Trial Court held that decree could be scaled down u/s. 19(2) of Act, but it had no jurisdiction to grant that relief as decree sought to be scaled down had been passed by High Court - Appellant took an appeal to HC and also made a separate application in HC for scaling down decretal debt under Act - HC dismissed application - Whether such dismissal is valid in law? - Held, ground on which HC refused relief u/s. 19(2) of Act was that 'the retrospective operation of s. 19(2) was controlled by s. 16 of Act XXIII of 1948' and that cl. (ii) of s. 16 applied and as appellant whose appeal was pending at commencement of amending Act did not apply for scaling down before decree was passed although he had opportunity to do so, his application subsequent to decree was barred by principle of res judicata - But it is said that principle of res judicata has no application to present case as s. 19(1) which is incorporated by reference in s. 19(2) says that a petitioner would be entitled to relief given to him under that section notwithstanding anything stated in CPC, 1908 - Object of Act was to relieve an agriculturist of heavy burden of indebtedness and with that object in view provisions were made in Act to scale down certain debts - Legislature again stepped in and passed amending Act, object of which was to give further relief in case of debts incurred before commencement of Act but where decree was passed after such commencement and, secondly, where both debt incurred and decree passed were of a date after Act - By amending Act several amendments were made in Act which were made applicable to suits and proceedings set out in s. 16 of amending Act - One such amendment was addition of s. 19(2) and amendment of s. 19 of Act which was renumbered as s. 19(1) - This section which provides for scaling down of decrees - Three clauses of s. 16 are independent of each other and cl. (ii) is in two parts and talks of two different situations, one is when no decree or order has been passed and other is when decree or order passed has not become final - There is, however, a common element, and common element is that cl. (ii) refers to suits and proceedings instituted before 25-01-1949 first situation envisaged by cl. (ii) arose in present case and appellant-debtor was entitled to avail himself of all or any of amendments made by amending Act, including amendment made in s. 19 (2) thereof - In alternative, if word 'decree' or 'order' means any decree or any order, even then cl. (ii) applies, because decree of dismissal passed in suit had not become final on 25-01-1949, for an appeal was then pending - Position before Supreme Court is a suit which was instituted before commencement of amending Act, namely, 25-01-1949 - This suit was pending on that date and no decree or order for repayment of debt had been made by that date - Decree for repayment of debt was made some two years after, on 09-03-1951- Therefore, two conditions laid down for first part of cl. (ii) are present and it clearly applies to present case - Relief u/s. 19(2), in terms, is a relief which can be claimed after a decree for repayment of debt has been passed - It is not disputed that debt in present case was payable at such commencement and court has passed a decree for repayment of that debt after such commencement - S. 19(2) in terms applied, and by reason of provisions of sub-section (1) of s. 19 which were also attracted to present case, appellant-debtor was entitled to relief asked for notwithstanding anything contained in CPC, 1908 - Appeal allowed.
