Smt. Kamla v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

18 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 100; (1993) 1 SCC 1; AIR 1993 SC 374; 1992 (3) CCR 430; 1992 (3) Crimes 1088; 1993 CRLJ 68; 1993 CRLR 1; JT 1992 (6) SC 707; 1992(3) SCALE 205; 1993 (1) UJ 89

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Appellant is mother-in-law of deceased Smt. Kamal, convicted by Trial Court u/s. 302, IPC, 1860 - Reliability on dying declaration was in question - Deceased while dying gave four contradictory statements - Whether appellant is liable to be convicted on basis of dying declaration ? - Inconsistency between Ex. PB/2 and Ex. PD is enough to manifest that deceased was wavering - Held, under instant circumstances it is highly unsafe to convict appellant on sole basis of dying declaration Ex. PB/2 recorded by P.W. 2- Conviction and sentence passed against appellant are set aside - Appeal allowed.
