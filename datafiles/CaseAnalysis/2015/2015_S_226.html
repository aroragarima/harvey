Jasmer Singh v State of Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 28; (2015) 4 SCC 458; 2015 (3) ALD(SC) 44; 2015 (144) FLR 837; 2015 (5) MahLJ 580; 2015 (4) MPLJ 5; 2015(1) SCALE 360

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Retrenchment Compensation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(c), 25F, 25F(a), 25F(b), 25G and 25H - Termination - Reinstatement with back wages - Appellant's (Workman) services were terminated without complying with the mandatory provisions of ss. 25-F, 25-G and 25-H of the Act - Respondent (Management) neither issued notice nor notice pay nor retrenchment compensation was given to him - Appellant raised an industrial dispute - Labour Court set aside the order of termination passed against the appellant and awarded reinstatement in his job with continuity of service and full back wages to him - Single Judge allowed the appeal filed against said order - DB affirmed the order of the Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, the workman has worked for more than 240 days in a calendar year and termination order is void ab initio in law for non-compliance of ss. 25-F (cls. (a) and (b)), 25-G and 25-H of the Act, therefore, the Labour Court has rightly set aside the order of termination of services of the workman and awarded the order of reinstatement with continuity of service and full back wages. The judgment and order passed by the Single Judge which is affirmed by the DB in its judgment and order are set aside and the Award of the Labour Court is restored. Appeal allowed.
