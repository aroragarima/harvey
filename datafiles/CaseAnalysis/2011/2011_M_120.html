Mohd. Imran Khan v State (Govt. of NCT of Delhi)

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 646; (2011) 10 SCC 192; 2012 CRLJ 693; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 75; 2011(11) SCALE 376; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1030

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 376 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant accused allegedly kidnapped and raped the prosecutrix - Trial Court convicted and sentenced the appellant u/ss. 363, 376 - Appellant filed appeal - HC set aside the conviction u/s. 363, however, maintained the conviction u/s. 376 and reduced the sentence from 7 years RI to 5 years RI - Whether the conviction and sentence recorded by the Trial Court and its confirmation by the HC was sustainable - Held, all the prosecution witnesses had faced grilling cross- examination but nothing could be elicited to discredit any part of their evidence - Prosecution evidence was accepted by both the courts and no cogent reason was found to interfere with the same - On the basis of production of the original Birth Certificate, it had been proved that the prosecutrix was less than 16 years of age on the date of incident - Trial Court came to the conclusion that there was no reason to disbelieve the prosecutrix, as no self-respecting girl would level a false charge of rape against anyone by staking her own honour - Evidence of rape stood fully corroborated by the medical evidence - MLC of the prosecutrix was duly supported by PW2 doctor - Instant case was not a fit case to reduce the sentence in a proved case of rape of minor - No merit in appeal - Appeal dismissed.
