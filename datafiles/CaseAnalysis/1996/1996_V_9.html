Secretary To Govt. Of Karnatakaand Anr. v V. Harishbabu

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2984; (1996) 5 SCC 400; AIR 1996 SC 3421; 1996 (2) ARBLR 276; 1996 ARBLR 495; 1996 (3) CCC 215; 1996 (3) CLT 118; JT 1996 (6) SC 489; 1996(5) SCALE 345; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 215; 1996 (6) Supreme 27

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Indian limitation act,1908 ; Arbitration Act,1940 - Award - Filing in Court - Limitation to set it aside - Commences from date of notice by Court under s. 14(2) of Arbitration Act - The notice under s.14(1) of arbitration Act is to be treated as sufficient to inform the party concerned - Held, that a notice issued by the arbitrator under s. 14 (1)is not a substitute for a notice which is mandatorily required to be issued by the Court and served upon the parties regarding the filing of the award under s. 14 (2) of the act.
