Malkiat Singh And Ors. v State Of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, A.M. Ahmadi, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1040; (1991) 4 SCC 341; (1991) SCC (Cr) 976; 1991 (2) Crimes 191; 1991 CRLR 480; 1991 CrLR(SC) 480; JT 1991 (2) SC 190; 1991(1) SCALE 722; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 161, 162, 172, 174, 175, 313 and 366 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 6, 11 and 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 302 and 307 - Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act 1984, ss. 14 and 15 - Murder - Whether identity of appellants as assailants proved beyond reasonable doubt? - Held, evidence of PW3 and PW4 rightly accepted - Omissions and discrepancies in General Diary Entry book cannot be used as evidence by accused in view of s. 145 of Evidence Act - Sentence of death of A-1 commuted to life imprisonment and as A-2 did not share common intention, he is entitled to benefit of doubt and hence acquitted of all charges and conviction of A-3 maintained.
