Assistant Collector Of Central Excise And Customs v J.C. Shah

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 570; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 317

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises & Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excises Rules, 1944 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Whether respondent, who carry on business in manufacture of art silk fabrics, are entitled to claim exemption from excise duty under Item 12 A(v) of Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, are two partnerships of which respondent is a partner same person as respondent? - Context seems to require that words 'the same person' cannot receive liberal construction for which appellants contend - Held, for purpose of Item 12 A(v), three persons cannot be said to be same person - Appeals dismissed.
