Girish Vyas and others v The State Of Maharastra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 736; (2012) 3 SCC 619; AIR 2012 SC 2043; 2012(5) ALL MR 392; [2011] 12 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bangalore Development Act, 1976, Bombay Primary Education Act, 1947, Bombay Town Planning Act, Maharashtra Government Rules, Bombay Town Planning Act 1954, Bombay Town Planning Act 1957, 1954 Act, D.C. Regulations, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 6, 9 and 11, - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1954, s.10 - Maharashtra Regional Town Planning Act, 1966, ss.126, 162 (1) and 165 - Land Acquisition - Public Purpose - Reverse of Public Amenity - Sustainability - One of appellant was owner of property bearing Final Plot. No. 110 (F.P.No.) which was acquired by Govt. under the 1894 Act - Said land was reserved for garden under 1966 Development Plan (DP) which was sanctioned by State Govt. u/s.10 of the 1954, Act and said Act was repealed and replaced by the 1966 Actw.e.f 11-1-1967 - Subsequently said F.P. No.110 was changed to use for Primary school - Municipal Corporation (MC) took all necessary steps to acquire said plot for same - Landowner/appellant appointed another appellant who was into constructions for development of private residence on instruction from Urban Development Department (UDD) which was under one of appellants - Inspite of reservation for primary school said plot was permitted to be developed for private residence floating all norms mandatory and legal provisions - Aggrieved, respondents filed PIL before HC which was allowed and directed to initiate criminal investigation against appellants - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether State Govt. instructed Municipal Corporation to shift reservation for public amenity such as primary school on F.P. No. 110 and instruct to grant development permission for residential purpose thereon without modifying DP was sustainable - Held, direction given by State Govt. for deletion of reservation on F.P.No.110, and consequent Commencement and Occupation certificates issued by MC in favour of developer were in complete subversion of statutory requirements of the 1966 Act and development permission was wholly illegal and unjustified - (B) Whether landowner and developer to defend decision of Govt. in their favour on basis of provision in erstwhile Town Planning Scheme as against purpose for which land was reserved under presently prevalent DP and such favour justify another section of statute which was not invoked by Govt. was sustainable - Held, appellant's right of development for residential purposes on concerned plot under erstwhile Town Planning scheme subsisted in spite of coming into force of Development Plan reserving plot for primary school, was liable to be rejected - Acquisition of F.P.No.110 land was complete with declaration u/s. 126 of the 1966 Act, r/w s.6 of the 1894 Act, and same was valid and legal - Developer should inform MC whether he was giving up claim over construction of ten storied building apart from tenants' building in favour of MC, failing which either developer or MC should take steps for demolition of disputed building as per time frame laid down by Court - Former occupants of F.P No. 110 would continue to reside in building constructed for tenants on terms stated and Corporation would not be required to pay any amount to developer for tenants' building constructed by him, nor for ten storied building in event he gave up his claim over it in favour of MC - (C) Whether judgment/ order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, direction of HC in impugned judgment dt.15-.3-.1999 in petitions for demolition of concerned building was fully legal and justified and order passed by HC directing Municipal Corporation to move for revival of appeal was therefore necessary in light of law and strictures passed by HC against the then CM (appellant) and the then Minister (appellant) of State were maintained and prayer to expunge remarks was rejected - Remarks against Municipal Commissioner (appellant ) were however deleted - In Common Cause A Registered Society Vs. Union of India 1999 Indlaw SC 503 relied on - Order directing criminal investigation was warranted in law and deleted - However, CM and Minister (appellants) of State were directed to pay cost of Rs.15,000/- to each of 2 petitioners towards 10 appeals and cost of Rs.10,000/- in writ petitions payable by each of appellants to writ petitioners - State Govt. and Planning authorities under the 1966 Act should hereafter scrupulously follow directions and suggested safeguards with respect to spaces meant for public amenities - Appeals disposed of.
