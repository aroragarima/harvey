State of Madhya Pradesh and another v Anshuman Shukla

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, T.S. Thakur, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 511; (2014) 10 SCC 814; AIR 2014 SC 3403; 2015 (1) CGLJ 405; 2014 (3) JabLJ 173; JT 2014 (9) SC 158; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3500; 2014(4) M.P.H.T. 119; 2015 (2) MPLJ 1; 2014(9) SCALE 273; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Kerala Buildings (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1965, Madhya Pradesh Madhyastham Adhikaran Act, 1983

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Madhya Pradesh Madhyastham Adhikaran Adhiniyam, 1983, s. 7 - Limitation Act, 1963, s. 5 - Limitation law - Applicability on 1983, Act - Respondent filed a petition u/s. 7 of the 1983, Act raising certain claims about the works contract executed between the parties - Petition was partly allowed by the Madhya Pradesh Arbitration Tribunal - Being aggrieved, the appellants filed a revision before HC u/s. 19 of the 1983, Act, along with an application u/s. 5 of the 1963, Act to condone the delay in filing the revision - Revision which was barred by time of 80 days was dismissed by the HC - On appeal, DB of SC held that the case of Nagar Palika Parishad, Morena, 2004 MLJ 374 had been incorrectly dismissed at the threshold and that the same required consideration by a larger Bench - Hence, instant case - Whether the provisions of 1963, Act were applicable to the provisions of 1983, Act -

Held, s. 19 of the 1983, Act, did not contain any express rider on the power of the HC to entertain an application for revision after the expiry of the prescribed period of three months - On the contrary, the HC was conferred with suo moto power, to call for the record of an award at any time - No express exclusion had been incorporated therein, and there is neither any evidence to suggest that the legislative intent was to bar the application of s. 5 of the 1963, Act on s. 19 of the 1983, Act - It could not, therefore, be said that the legislative intent was to exclude the applicability of s. 5 of the 1963, Act to s. 19 of the 1983, Act - Case of Nagar Palika Parishad, Morena was decided erroneously - S. 5 of the 1963, Act was applicable to s. 19 of the 1983, Act - Impugned judgments and orders were set aside - Delay in filing revision petitions was condoned and the cases remanded to HC to examine the same on merits - Appeals allowed.
