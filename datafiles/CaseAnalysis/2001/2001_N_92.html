Surendra Kumar v Nathulal and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20142; (2001) 5 SCC 46; AIR 2001 SC 2040; 2001 (4) ALD(SC) 26; 2001 (44) ALR 656; JT 2001 (Supp1) SC 520; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 753; 2001(3) SCALE 647; 2001 (4) SLT 470; 2001 (3) Supreme 622; 2001 (2) UC 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 123 - Registration Act, 1908 - Smt Parsadi and Hanumandas mortgaged suit property with possession to Nathulal, respondent 1 herein - Smt Chand Bai, original owner of suit property executed a gift deed in favour of Surendra Kumar, appellant herein - Appellant filed a civil suit for redemption of mortgage - Appellate court held that deed of gift was not duly proved and plaintiff was not entitled to get a decree in suit - Whether gift deed was not duly proved since one of attestors has not been examined as witness? - Held, on a plain reading of proviso to s. 68 of Indian Evidence Act, it is manifest that a registered deed of gift can be received in evidence without examining one of attestors if person who has executed deed of gift has not specifically denied its execution - Donor has specifically admitted execution in this case - Appeals allowed.
