Owners and Parties Interested In The Vessel M. V. v State Trading Corporation of India Limited and

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19783; (2001) 7 SCC 473; AIR 2001 SC 3381; 2001 (45) ALR 178; 2001 (3) ARBLR 96; JT 2001 (6) SC 610; 2001 (3) RAJ 1; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 260; 2001(5) SCALE 356; 2001 (6) SLT 6; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 699; 2001 (6) Supreme 282; 2001 (2) UC 535

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Arbitration & ADR -- Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 45 - Agreements - Arbitration clause - Interpretation - Validity - Appellants were owners and parties interested in ship and respondent no. 2 was charterer of ship under agreement dt. 8-5-1997 - Five bills of lading dt. 26-5-1997 were issued by appellants and respondent no.2 agreed to carry on board 11,433.510 MT of peas from Canada to Calcutta in good order and condition - Respondent no.1 was owner of consignment - Respondent no.1 filed suit in HC against appellants and respondent no. 2 claiming damages for loss suffered due to damage caused to his goods of 4,910 MT in shipment - Appellants/defendant no.1 and respondent no.2/defendant no.2 application for arbitration was rejected by Single Judge holding that arbitration clause in agreement was not applicable - Appeal filed by appellant /defendant no.1 and respondent no.2/defendant no.2 was dismissed by DB confirming Single Judge Order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in rejecting arbitration application - Held, intention of parties should be considered as expressed by several Indian and England Courts - Both agreements to be read together to ascertain intention of parties and attempts should be made to give liberal meaning to incorporation clause to make it valid but not to invalidate - If no inconsistency if arbitration clause was incorporated to bill of lading then arbitration clause could be applied to bill of lading - Parties to bill of lading were aware of arbitration clause in charter party agreement and hence deemed to have incorporated same clause in bill of lading and intention of parties to abide by arbitration clause was clear - It was to be decided by arbitrator whether particular dispute comes within arbitration clause or not - Parties took care not to couch interpretation clause in Bill of Lading in general terms but had made their intention clear that disputes arising there under should be resolved by arbitration according to clause in Charter Party Agreement - Intention of parties to incorporate arbitration clause in Charter Party Agreement to Bill of Lading was clear and should be given effect - Directions issued to Trial Court - HC Order was not sustainable and hence set aside - Appeal allowed.
