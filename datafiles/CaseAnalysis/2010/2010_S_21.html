Skyline Education Institute (India) Private Ltd. v S.L. Vaswani And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Tarun Chatterjee, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 10; (2010) 2 SCC 142; AIR 2010 SC 3221; 2010(2) ALL MR 427; 2010 (1) AWC 684; 2010 (1) CLT(SC) 21; 2011 (3) ESC 458; JT 2010 (3) SC 136; 2010 (42) PTC 217; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 436; 2010(1) SCALE 83; 2010 (1) UC 503

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property

Keywords: Generic Word

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trademarks Act, 1999 - Appellant/Skyline Education Institute alleged that the respondent/Charitable Society were using the word 'Skyline' in the name of their newly established educational institution - Appellant filed a suit for permanent injunction against respondent - Single Judge partly allowed the suit temporarily restraining the respondents from starting any new courses which were similar to the courses run by the appellant - Respondents challenged the order of Single Judge before DB - DB vacated the interim injunction of Single Judge vide impugned order holding that the balance of convenience and equity was in favour of respondents - Whether impugned order is vitiated by any error of law apparent in the record - Held, prima facie case, balance of convenience and equity are in favour of respondents - Word 'Skyline' was a generic word because it was being used by a large number of people in India and abroad - Further, the reason that the appellant got registration of trademarks 'Skyline MEDALLIST' and 'Skyline Institute' during the pendency of the present suit, is not sufficient for entertaining the appellant's prayer for temporary injunction - Findings recorded by the Single Judge and DB are based on a correct and balanced consideration of various facets of the case, however, Single Judge's direction to the respondents to append a note in the advertisement that they are not related to appellant, is set aside - Appeals dismissed.
