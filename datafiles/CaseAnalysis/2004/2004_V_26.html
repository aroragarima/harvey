State Of U.P. v Veer Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 326; (2004) 10 SCC 117; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1672; AIR 2004 SC 4614; 2004 ALJ 2809; 2004 CRLJ 3835; 2004 (3) JhrCR(SC) 50; JT 2004 (Supp2) SC 90; 2004 (2) RCR(Criminal) 952; 2004(5) SCALE 159; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 790; 2004 (2) UC 917

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302, 307, 452 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 155, 157, 32 - When maker of purported dying declaration survives same is not statement u/s. 32 of Indian Evidence Act, but is a statement in terms of Section 164 of Code - It can be used u/s. 157 of Evidence Act for purpose of corroboration and u/s. 155 for purpose of contradiction - HC disposed of appeal only on basis of erroneous conclusion and not considered other evidence on record - Remit matter back to HC.
