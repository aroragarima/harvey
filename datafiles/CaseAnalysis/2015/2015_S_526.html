Huawei Technologies Company Limited v Sterlite Technologies Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 598; (2016) 1 SCC 721; 2015 (6) AWC 5446; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 98; 2015 (4) CompLJ 342; JT 2015 (8) SC 94; 2016 (4) MahLJ 101; 2016 (3) MPLJ 15; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 128; 2015(9) SCALE 537

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitrator & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 11(6), 15(2) - Dispute - Application for appointment of arbitrator - Justification - Respondent and petitioner entered into contract for supply of material for project - However, petitioner stated that though it had complied with all terms and conditions of said supply contract and had shipped/delivered all equipment's on time, respondent had failed to make full payment of amounts due - Further, notice was issued by petitioner for invoking arbitration clause, however, as no reply was received by respondent, petitioner appointed sole arbitrator - Further, respondent raised dispute to appointment of arbitrator and rejected appointment of arbitrator as sole arbitrator - Further, sole arbitrator vide order recused himself from proceedings - Hence, present application is filed u/s. 11(6) of Act seeking appointment of Sole Arbitrator in terms of clause of contract between parties.

Held, u/s. 15(2) of Act in situation where mandate of arbitrator terminates, substitute arbitrator is required to be appointed according to rules that were applicable to appointment of arbitrator who is replaced. SC held that in present case, there are no institutional rules under which disputes between parties are to be referred to arbitration, hence, expression 'rules' appearing in s. 15(2) of Act will have to be understood with reference to provisions for appointment contained in Supply Contract. Supply Contract contemplates appointment of sole arbitrator by parties by mutual consent. In situation where original arbitrator had recused himself substitute or new arbitrator is required to be appointed according to rules that were applicable to appointment of original arbitrator as this is mandate of s. 15(2) of Act. It was, therefore, incumbent on petitioner to give notice and explore possibility of naming arbitrator by mutual consent and only on failure thereof present application u/s. 11(6) of Act could/should have been filed. However, same had not been followed. Hence, SC held that present application/arbitration petition to be premature. Petition disposed of.

Ratio - Application for appointment of arbitrator cannot be entertained when same is premature and is not filed following procedure laid down in statute.
