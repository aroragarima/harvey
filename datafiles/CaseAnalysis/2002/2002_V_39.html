Bharatiya Gramin Punarrachana Sanstha v Vijay Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, S.N. Variava

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1937; (2002) 6 SCC 707; (2002) SCC (L&S) 964; AIR 2002 SC 3092; 2003 (3) Bom.C.R. 191; 2002 (95) FLR 8; 2002 (3) JhrCR(SC) 121; JT 2002 (6) SC 201; 2002 LabIC 3278; 2002 (3) LLJ 1121; 2002(6) SCALE 75; 2002 (5) SLR 624; 2002 (5) SLT 17; 2002 (5) Supreme 481

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Maharashtra Employees of Private Schools Registration Act, 1977, ss. 5(2), 5(4) and 5(5) - Termination - Appellant is a public trust and a society under Bombay Public Trusts Act and Societies Registration Act - First respondent applied for post of Lab-Attendant in response to an advertisement - He was selected and appointed by appellant for a period of two years - First respondent's services were terminated - Whether valid? - Held, first respondent was not put on probation - Order of termination was passed before expiry of said period of two years - Under said order of appointment first respondent is entitled to remain in service till end of academic year 1997-98 - Appeal allowed
