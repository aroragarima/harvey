Bhupinder Singh v Daljit Kaur

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, P.N. Shingal, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 367; (1979) SCC (Cr) 302; (1979) 1 SCC 352; AIR 1979 SC 442; 1979 ALJ 209; 1979 CRLJ 198; 1979 CRLR 377; 1979 MLJ 446; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 292

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Whether co-habitation or compromise - Order u/s. 125 of granting of maintenance - Whether subsequent co-habitation or compromise is a valid defence to execution of such order - Held, until order is modified or cancelled by a higher Court its validity survives - It is enforceable and no plea that there has been cohabitation in interregnum or that there has been a compromise between parties can hold good as a valid defence - Order accordingly.
