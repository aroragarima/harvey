Nirmal Singh Kahlon and another v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

22 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2643; (2009) 1 SCC 441; AIR 2009 SC 984; 2009 CRLJ 958; JT 2008 (12) SC 331; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 4468; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 134; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 3; 2008(14) SCALE 639; [2008] 14 S.C.R. 1049; 2009 (2) SLJ 371

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Punjab Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1947

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468 and 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1) (d)(e) and 13(2) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Selection of Panchayat Secretaries - A writ petition was filed challenging selection of 908 candidates to the post of Panchayat Secretaries made at the time when appellant was the Rural Development and Panchayat Minister in the State - HC formed a prima facie opinion that irregularities in the selection process had been committed and directed inquiry in the matter - An enquiry was made by 'X', the then Financial Commissioner, Rural Development and Panchayat Department and opined that the entire selection process was required to be set aside - 'X' recommended that an investigation into the whole affair be made by the Vigilance Department - Pursuant thereto a FIR was lodged by the Vigilance Department against several persons including appellant for alleged commission of offences u/ss. 420, 467, 468 and 120(B) of the IPC and ss. 13(1) (d)(e) and 13(2) of the 1988 Act - A charge sheet was filed against appellant and appellant No. 1 (Ex. Deputy Director of Rural Development and Panchayats) - Subsequently, Secretary of the Department recommended investigation of the case by the Crime Branch of the State - HC by an order dt. 31.10.2002 observed that the State Government had the option either to make further investigation by removing the persons name in the report from their respective offices or to direct CBI probe - State opted to handover the investigation to CBI - Pursuant to HC direction, State issued notification notifying handling over of the investigation to CBI - Appellants filed applications seeking recalling of order of HC and for quashing the said notification and the consequential FIR filed by the CBI- Said applications were dismissed by HC - Appellants filed appeal against said order contending the HC had no jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution to direct fresh investigation by CBI, after filing of the chargesheet in the matter - Whether appellants submissions could be accepted and order of the HC could be set aside? - Held, the state in terms of special statute, viz., the Police Act can always request the CBI to make an investigation/further investigation - Said power of the State is wholly unrestricted by s. 36 of CrPC or otherwise - As a logical corollary if while making preliminary inquiry pursuant to the notification issued by the State, the CBI comes to know of commission of other and further offence involving a larger conspiracy which required prosecution against a large number of persons who had not been proceeded against at all by the local police officers, even lodging of second FIR would not be a bar - If lodging of the second FIR is legally permissible, only because the same has been done at the instance of the HC could not lead SC to arrive at a conclusion that its direction in that behalf was wholly without jurisdiction - It will bear repetition to state that law as it stands permits the HC and SC to direct investigation made by the CBI - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeals dismissed.
