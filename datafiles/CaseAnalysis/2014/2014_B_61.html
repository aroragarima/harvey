Bhavnagar Municipal Corporation and another v Jadeja Govubha Chhanubha and another

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 818; AIR 2015 SC 609; 2015 (144) FLR 177; JT 2014 (13) SC 407; 2015 (1) LLJ 1; 2014(13) SCALE 434; 2015 (2) SLR 504

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Length Of Service, Retrenchment Compensation

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Dispute Act, 1947 - Reinstatement - Legality - Respondent was employed as a Conductor in the Transport Department of the appellant- Corporation on daily-wage basis in October, 1987 - Respondent served in that capacity till 31-3-1989 when his services were terminated - Industrial Dispute was raised - Labour Court placed reliance upon a Xerox copy of a certificate allegedly issued by an officer of the appellant-Corporation certifying that the respondent had worked as a Conductor for said period - Labour Court held the termination of the respondent from service to be illegal and directed reinstatement with 65% back wages - Single Judge partly allowed the petition filed against the award and set aside the award to the extent it directed payment of 65% back wages - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, strict rules of evidence, are not applicable to the proceedings before the Labour Court. That being so the admission of the Xerox copy of the certificate, without any objection from the appellant-Corporation, cannot be faulted at this belated stage. Further, Labour Court committed no mistake nor was there any room for the HC to interfere with the finding especially when the findings could not be described as perverse or without any evidence. The HC was also justified in directing deletion of the back wages from the award made by the Labour Court against which deletion, the respondent did not agitate either before the DB by filing an appeal or before SC. However, in view of fact that the respondent today may be past fifty, instant case is case where reinstatement must give way to award of compensation as reinstatement of the respondent in service does not appear to be an acceptable option. Award of a sum of Rs.2,50,000/ should meet the ends of justice. Appeals partly allowed.
