Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras v Messrs Khoday Eswarsa and Sons

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 254; (1971) 3 SCC 555; AIR 1972 SC 132; 1972 (1) CTR 295; [1972] 83 ITR 369; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 846

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 28(1)(c) and 66(2) - A/y 1955-56 - Penalty u/s. 28(1) - Respondent, a manufacturer of silk, carbon papers, type-writer ribbons, liquor, spirituous drugs and chemicals etc., filed return showing Rs. 51,214 as a taxable income - Income Tax Officer (ITO) made several additions to the amount shown in the return and ultimately fixed the total assessable income in the sum of Rs. 3,30,474/- - ITO also imposed penalty on the assessee - On appeal, appellate authority reduced the taxable amount and the taxable income was fixed in the sum of Rs. 2,09,575 - Tribunal set aside the order of the ITO as confirmed by the Appellate Authority levying penalty on the respondent - Commissioner filed an application u/s. 66(1) of the IT Act, requiring the Tribunal to refer the question i.e. whether Tribunal was right in cancelling the penalty of Rs. 35,000 levied u/s. 28 (1) (c) of the IT Act to be referred to HC - Tribunal rejected the said application on the ground that the reasons given by it for setting aside the penalty proceedings were all based on findings of fact and that no question of law arose out of those findings - HC also dismissed appeal filed against said tribunal - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, penalty proceedings being penal in character, the Department must establish that the receipt of the amount in dispute constitutes income of the assessee - Apart from the falsity of the explanation given by the assessee, the Department must have before it before levying penalty cogent material or evidence from which it could be inferred that the assessee has consciously concealed the particulars of his income or had deliberately furnished inaccurate particulars in respect of the same and that the disputed amount is a revenue receipt - In the present case, Department had no material or evidence from which it could be reasonably inferred that the assessee had consciously concealed the particulars of his income or had deliberately' furnished inaccurate particulars - HC order upheld - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
