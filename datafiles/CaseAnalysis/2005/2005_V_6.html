N. R. Govindarajan v V. K. Rajagopalan and others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1641; (2005) 12 SCC 362

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure,1908, O.14 r.2 - Determination of preliminary issue - Appellant-defendant had claimed that Court fee paid by plaintiff was deficit and plea deserved to be heard and decided as preliminary issue - HC dismissed appellants claim - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, counsel for appellant submitted that the issue as to Court fee can be heard and decided as preliminary legal issue, inasmuch as appellant does not propose to adduce any evidence in support of plea taken in written statement in this regard. That being stand taken, in terms of O.14 r.2 of CPC, issue could have been heard and decided as preliminary issue. Therefore, impugned order of HC is set aside and Trial Court is directed to frame an issue on question of Court fee in terms of the plea raised in written statement and hear and decide the same as a preliminary issue before proceeding ahead. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - When claim of party for determination of issue as preliminary issue is justified, then rejection of same is not justified.
