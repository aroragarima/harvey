B. N. Mutto & Anr v T. K. Nandi

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 112; (1979) 1 SCC 361; AIR 1979 SC 460; 1979 (1) RCJ 316; 1979 (1) RCR(Rent) 144; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(7), 14A(1), 14(1)(6) and 25C(2) - Eviction - Denied - Sustainability - Appeal was by special leave by the landlord against the judgment of HC whereby it allowed revision of the respondent/tenant and set aside the order of eviction passed by the Rent Controller rejecting the application of the respondent seeking permission to contest the proceedings for eviction filed by tile appellants u/s. 14(A)(1) of Act - Whether dismissal of order of eviction was sustainable - Held, s. 14(7) of Act laid down that where an order for the recovery of possession of any premises is made on the ground specified in cl. (e) of the proviso to sub-s. (1), landlord should not be entitled to obtain possession thereof before the expiration of period of six months from the date of the order - S. 25C of Act made exception to the requirement of s. 14(6) of Act to the effect that where landlord was in occupation of any residential premises allotted to him by the Central Govt. or any local authority and who fulfilled the requirements of s.14A(1) of Act the requirement u/s. 14(6) of Act that he would not be entitled to possession unless a period of five years had elapsed from the date of his acquisition of the premises was not applicable - In other words, appellant could straightway obtain possession without the impediment imposed u/s. 14(6) of Act - Great stress was laid by respondent on s.25C(2) of Act which provided that in the case of a landlord who, being a person of the category specified in 25C(2)(1), had obtained, on the ground specified in cl. (e) of the proviso to s. 14(1) of Act or u/s. 14A of Act, an order for the eviction of a tenant from any premises, the provisions of s. 14(7) of Act should have effect as if for the words "six months", occurring therein, the words "two months" were substituted - Contention was that if s. 14A(1) of Act stood by itself and if appellant applying u/s. 14A(1) of Act would straightway get the possession after the tenant could not contest the suit on the grounds specified in s.25B(5) of Act there was no need for mentioning the provisions of s. 14(1)(6) of Act and s. 14(1)(7) of Act and prescribing lesser period for a prescribed period u/s. 14(7) of Act - Appeal allowed.
