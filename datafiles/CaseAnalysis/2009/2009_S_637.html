Mukesh Kumar Agrawal v State Of U.P. & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 947; (2009) 13 SCC 693; 2010 (1) AWC 537; JT 2009 (13) SC 643; 2009(10) SCALE 534; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: U.P. High Speed Diesel Oil and Light Diesel Oil (Maintenance of Supplies and Distribution) Order, 1981

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Writ petition - Maintainability - Doctrine of alternate remedy - Appellant a dealer in HSDO and LDO(High Speed Diesel Oil and Light Diesel Oil) - Conduct of raids allegedly at the behest of one political leader - Cancellation of licence - Spate of writ petitions - Writ petition in question was dismissed on ground of availability of alternate remedy - Dismissal, whether proper? - Held, Yes - Appellant has raised pure questions of fact for determination in the writ proceedings in respect whereof the Licensing Authority itself was required to go into the materials brought on record by both the parties; it is neither denied nor disputed that the order passed by the Licensing Authority canceling the licence of a dealer is an appellabe one; Appellate Authority is entitled to go into question of both law and fact - Allegation of malice made in petition was found to be vague in character by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
