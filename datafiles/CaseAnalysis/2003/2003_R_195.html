Ramabai Padmakar Patil (Dead) through LRs and v Rukminibai Vishnu Vekhande and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 614; (2003) 8 SCC 537; AIR 2003 SC 3109; 2003 (6) ALD(SC) 123; 2003 (53) ALR 125; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 565; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 205; JT 2005 (11) SC 567; 2003 (4) RCR(Civil) 92; 2003 (2) RD 640; 2003(6) SCALE 452; 2003 (1) SCW 4018; 2003 (4) SLT 916; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 583; 2003 (6) Supreme 190

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Hindu Succession Act, 1956 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Authenticity of Will - Decree of declaration and injunction - Will executed in favour of plaintiff, who was a widow, by her mother, Smt Yamunabai, excluding other daughters - Held, fact that all other daughters were excluded could not be a ground to cast any doubt regarding authenticity of Will It is a case of making provision for a widowed daughter who had been left a destitute on account of death of her husband at a very early age - Besides, no evidence has been adduced by defendants to show that at time of execution of Will, Smt Yamunabai had been suffering from any such ailment which had impaired her mental faculties to such an extent that she was unable to understand real nature of document which she was executing - Further held, since Madhav died after enforcement of Hindu Succession Act, his widow will have only 1/8 th share in estate left by Madhav, which alone would go to plaintiff on basis of Will executed in her favour plaintiff-appellant, in addition to her own share, will also be entitled to 1/8 th share of her mother Smt Yamunabai on basis of Will executed in her favour.
