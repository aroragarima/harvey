Andhra Pradesh Grain & Seed Merchants’ Association Etc. Etc v Union Of India & Anr

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 90; (1970) 2 SCC 71; (1970) SCC (Cr) 307; AIR 1971 SC 2346; 1970 (1) ALT(SC) 281; 1971 CRLJ 1556; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 69; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 166

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Misbranding, Sells

Summary: Health & Drug - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(3) - Constitutional validity of the Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - (A) Whether Constitution art. 20(3) is violated by the Prevention Of Food Adulteration Act, 1954? - By enacting that a plea by the vendor in a prosecution for an offence pertaining to sale of adulterated or misbranded article of food, that he was ignorant of the nature, substance or quality will not be a defence, the guarantee u/art. 20(3) is not infringed - Held, The Act does not infringe the guarantee of art. 20(3) of the Constitution - (B) Whether restrictions imposed upon the conduct of business by traders in foodstuffs are unreasonable? - The Courts will not strike down an Act as imposing unreasonable restrictions merely because it creates an absolute liability for infringement of the law which involves grave danger to public health - Held,the restrictions imposed upon the conduct of business by traders in foodstuffs cannot be deemed unreasonable - Petition dismissed.
