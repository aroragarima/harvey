Krishna Mochi and Others v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1672; (2002) 6 SCC 81; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1220; AIR 2002 SC 1965; 2002 (3) CalLT(SC) 9; 2002 (2) CCR 104; 2002 (2) Crimes 236; 2002 (4) CRJ 731; 2002 CRLJ 2645; JT 2002 (4) SC 186; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 567; 2002(3) SCALE 602; 2002 (3) SCJ 163; [2002] 3 S.C.R. 1; 2002 (3) SLT 250; 2002 (3) Supreme 369

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bride Burning, communal harmony, communal violence, communalism

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code,1860, ss. 302, 149 - Terrorist and Disruptive activities Prevention Act, 1987 (TADA) - Heinous Crime - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants allegedly caused gruesome carnage, 35 persons lost their lives, some houses/huts were burnt, number of persons were injured - Respondent/ State approached Designated Court of Sessions Judge which convicted some appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 149 and acquitted A1, A10, A11 and A12, convicted A5, A8, A9, A13 sentenced to death and convicted A2, A4, A6, A7, life imprisonment, A3 RI for 10 years - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by lower Court was sustainable - Held, relied on Dilavar Hussain v. State of Gujarat 1990 Indlaw SC 241, credibility of witnesses had to be measured with same yardstick, whether, it was ordinary crime or crime emanating due to communal frenzy - Law did not make any distinction either in leading of evidence or in its assessment - Rule was one and only one namely, whether depositions were honest and true - Therefore, in instant case it could be said without any doubt that almost all witnesses had exaggerated to large extent by naming number of persons as accused but they could identify only one or two accused - It would clearly reveal that for one or other reason, witnesses were naming number of persons as accused who were not known to them or whom they had not seen at time of incident - In that set of circumstances, their evidence to large extent becomes doubtful and/or tutored - Nowhere witnesses assign any specific role to accused, except their presence in mob at time of offence and nowhere stated that accused were having any weapons - Investigating officers had not recovered any weapon of offence or any incriminating article from possession of any of accused - Therefore, it was not fit case to impose death penalty - Further, appeal filed by A2 and A9 was allowed and was acquitted of charges for which he was facing trial and was ordered to be released forthwith, if not required in any other case - Appeal filed by A5, A8, A13 was partly allowed and their conviction was upheld, however, imposition of death penalty was altered to life imprisonment - Hence, death referred by State was dismissed - Appeal disposed of
