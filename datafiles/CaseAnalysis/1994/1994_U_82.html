Sri Ranga Match Industries v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

25 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1922; (1994) Supp2 SCC 726; JT 1994 (1) SC 621; 1994(1) SCALE 427

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Finance Act, 1982, Life Insurance Corporation (Modification of Settlements) Act, 1976, Life Insurance Corporation (Amendment) Act, 1981

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Finance Act, 1982, s. 52 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 (1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Whether s. 52 is constitutionally valid, insofar as it gave retrospective effect to Notification No. 22 of 1982? - Mandamus issued by High Court could not prevent Central Govt. from withdrawing said notification - If appellants cannot avail of benefit of Notification No. 22, they cannot blame Govt. for that - Legislature is open to remove basis of, or rectify defect/lacuna in law on which a judgment is based and thereby rendering judgment inoperative - So long as manner in which basis of judgment has been removed is within legislative competence, Court cannot interfere - Held, method adopted in this particular case cannot be said to be impermissible - Superseding of said Notification with prospective effect is not questioned by appellants - If it can be done prospectively, it can equally be done retrospectively as has been done by a parliamentary enactment - Appeals dismissed.
