Mohan Lal v Management of Messrs Bharat Electronics Limited

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 410; (1981) 3 SCC 225; (1981) SCC (L&S) 478; AIR 1981 SC 1253; 1981 (42) FLR 389; 1981 LabIC 806; 1981 (2) LLJ 70; 1981 (2) LLN 23; 1981(1) SCALE 872; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 518; 1981 (2) SLR 11; 1981 UJ 943

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1964 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 25 F - Termination - Validity of - Whether termination of services of Shri Mohan Lal is illegal and/or unjustified and if so, to what relief is he entitled and what directions are necessary in this respect? - Held, appellant has satisfied both eligibility qualifications prescribed in s. 25 F for claiming retrenchment compensation - Appellant has satisfactorily established that his case is not covered by any of excepted or excluded categories and he has rendered continuous service for one year - Therefore, termination of his service would constitute retrenchment - As pre-condition for a valid retrenchment has not been satisfied termination of service is ab initio void, invalid and inoperative - Appellant must, therefore, be deemed to be in continuous service - Appeal allowed.
