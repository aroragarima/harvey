Subhransu Sekhar Padhi v Gunamani Swain and others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 543; (2014) 12 SCC 368; AIR 2015 SC 542; 2015 (2) ALD(SC) 12; 2014 (107) ALR 386; 2014 (2) DRTC 761; 2015 (1) DRTC 345; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3433; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 854; 2015 (126) RD 262; 2014(9) SCALE 494; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Banking & Finance - Contract & Commercial - State Financial Corporations, Act, 1951, ss. 3, 29, 31 - Seizure and sale of mortgage property - Legality - Challenging the seizure and sale of the mortgage property, petition came to be filed by the wife and children of the mortgagor - Appellant and OSFC contested the writ petition - By the impugned judgment writ petition was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the Corporation set up u/s. 3 of the Act was entitled to take recourse to the remedy available to it u/s. 29 of the Act even after having obtained an order or a decree after invoking the provisions of s. 31 of the Act but without executing that decree/order -

Held, appellant was the purchaser of the property sold u/s. 29 of the Act, who parted with the money in order to purchase the property - Appellant was a victim of an illegal procedure adopted by Corporation - Law regarding the authority of the Corporations to invoke the provisions of s. 29 with respect to properties other than those belonging to defaulter industrial concern was clearly declared by in Corporation by its judgment whereas the sale in question before us was, almost a year later - Authorities of respondent No.9 Corporation sold the properties to appellant in flagrant violation of the settled position of law - SC, therefore, directed respondent No. 9 to refund the amount of Rs.10,09,000/- to appellant with interest calculated at the rate 12% p.a. - However, it was open to the Corporation to recover the amounts either from the defaulter - industrial concern or from such other third party against whom the Corporation has a legal right to proceed - Appeal dismissed.
