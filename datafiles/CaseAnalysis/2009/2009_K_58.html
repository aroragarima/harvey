Nelapatla Ramaiah And Others. v Kamatam Bikshamaiah And Others.

Supreme Court of India

15 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2037; (2010) 2 SCC 139; JT 2009 (15) SC 306; 2009(14) SCALE 620; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 473

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Hyderabad Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950

Summary: Land & Property - Hyderabad Tenancy and Agricultural Lands Act, 1950 - Adverse possession - Applicability of s. 47 - Plaintiff executed the agreement for sale and made over possession of the lands in question to the intending purchaser - Thereafter, there were a series of transactions involving the suit lands and possession changed hands several times - Plaintiff filed suit for injunction and relief for declaration of title and delivery of possession of the plaint schedule property, which was dismissed - On appeal, First Appellate Court decreed the suit on the finding that all the various transactions were hit by ss. 47 and 50B of the Act - Second appeal was filed before HC - HC held that possession of the beneficiaries of the various transactions involving the land was adverse to the interest of the plaintiffs and the said transfers were not effected by the provisions of ss. 47 and 50B of the Act - Hence, present Special Leave Petition - Held, plaintiffs had not at any point of time objected to the agreements for sale entered into after original agreement despite the same being adverse to their interest - HC correctly held that the possession of the defendants was adverse to the interests of the plaintiff - No reason to interfere with the judgment and decree of the HC - Special Leave Petition dismissed.
