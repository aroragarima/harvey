T. Barai v Henry Ah Hoe and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 35; (1983) SCC (Cr) 143; (1983) 1 SCC 177; AIR 1983 SC 150; 1983 CRLJ 164; [1983] 1 S.C.R. 905

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20(1) - Bengal General Clauses Act, 1899, s. 8 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 6 - Whether pending proceedings to be governed by change of procedure brought about by s. 16-A of Act as introduced by Central Amendment Act and further whether continued operation of repealed West Bengal Amendment Act is preserved with regard to punishment to be imposed? - Held, just as a person accused of commission of an offence has no right to trial by a particular court or to a particular procedure, prosecutor equally has no right to insist upon that accused be subjected to an enhanced punishment under repealed Act - In case of a simple repeal, there is scarcely any room for expression of a contrary opinion - But when repeal is followed by fresh legislation on same subject, Court would undoubtedly have to look to provisions of new Act, but only for purpose of determining whether they indicate a different intention - Rule of beneficial construction requires that even ex post facto law of such a type should be applied to mitigate rigour of law - Provisions of s. 6 of General Clauses Act, 1897 will apply to a case of repeal even if there is simultaneous enactment unless a contrary intention can be gathered from new enactment - Appeal dismissed.
