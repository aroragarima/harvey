R. L. Kapur v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 165; (1972) SCC (Cr) 380; (1972) 1 SCC 651; AIR 1972 SC 858; 1972 CRLJ 643; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 55; [1972] 3 S.C.R. 417

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Court Of Record

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Court - General Clauses Act, s. 25 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 63 to 70 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 215 - Appellant was punished for contempt by award of imprisonment and fine - Appellant had also deposited sum of Rs. 500 as security for appearance - Appellant served imprisonment but did not pay fine - Two applications were filed, one by appellant for refund of amount deposited and other by State for adjustment of said amount towards fine unpaid - High Court allowed State's application for satisfaction of said sum towards fine unpaid - Whether power of HC to punish contempt of itself arises under Contempt of Courts Act, 1952, and s. 25 of General Clauses Act, ss. 63 to 70 of IPC, 1860 and relevant provisions of CrPC would apply? - Held, yes - Art. 215 provides that every HC shall have all powers to punish for contempt of itself - S. 3 of Contempt of Courts Act, 1952 provides every HC shall have jurisdiction, powers and authority for contempt of courts subordinate to it as well as contempt of itself - Appeal dismissed.
