Kumar Sudhendu Narain Deb v Renuka Biswas (Mrs) and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 359; (1992) 1 SCC 206; AIR 1992 SC 385; JT 1991 (4) SC 320; 1992 RD 33; 1991(2) SCALE 990; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 233; 1992 (1) UJ 183

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Decree-Holder, Final Decree For Sale, Suit For Recovery, Enforcement

Summary: Civil Procedure - Suit for recovery of money - Sale of mortgaged property - Enforcement -Respondents/mortgagees filed a mortgage suit for recovery of mortgage money against appellant/mortgagor and also sought enforcement and sale of mortgaged property - Trial Court passed a preliminary decree and allowed mortgagors to pay decretal amount in 15 equal annual installments - Respondent no.8 filed an application against preliminary decree in HC praying for stay of execution of decree, which was pending -Meantime, Trial Court passed final decree though appeal against preliminary decree, was pending in HC - Respondents filed an execution petition for realisation of their own share of the decreetal amount - Executing Court ordered that valuations be incorporated in sale proclamation - However, sale did not took place - Appellant made an objection u/s. 47 CPC and an application under O. 21 r. 90 CPC for setting aside sale - HC dismissed application and objection - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, a Court sale held in execution of a final decree passed in a suit for recovery of mortgage money could be upset under provisions of s. 47 CPC on displacement of preliminary decree on which such final decree was based - Held, fact that decree was consensual in nature, having been passed between parties to suit was of no consequence - However, property, was not put to sale in execution of preliminary decree - Thus, auction purchasers could not claim themselves to be parties to suit at the time of or at any time prior to passing of preliminary decree - Thus, interpretation that auction purchaser at a sale and execution of final decree should be deemed to be party to suit at and prior to stage when preliminary decree was passed unless sustaining would be contrary to spirit and scheme of O. 34 CPC - In terms of preliminary appellate decree and fulfillment of obligations of defendants of payment of sum as struck, there remained no occasion for entertaining, maintaining or sustaining application of plaintiff mortgagees for sale of property mortgaged and on that basis auction sale in favour of auction purchasers and confirmation of that sale automatically becomes non-est - Hence, HC went wrong in rejecting objection of appellant - Appellant was directed to burden himself in paying to auction purchasers, interest on their blocked sum - Appeal allowed.
