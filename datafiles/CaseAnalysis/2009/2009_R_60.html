Raj Kumar v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 896; (2009) 15 SCC 292; JT 2009 (9) SC 413; 2009(9) SCALE 495; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 49

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Self-Inflicted

Summary: [A] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 299 and 300 - Murder or culpable homicide - Relevant factors - Question to be considered would be first stage whether accused has done an act by doing which he has caused death of another, second stage whether that act of accused amounts to culpable homicide as defined in S.299 and third stage whether facts proved by prosecution bring case within ambit of any of four clauses of definition of murder contained in S.300 IPC - If answer to this question is in negative, offence would be culpable homicide not amounting to murder and if this question found in positive, but case comes within any of exceptions enumerated in S.300 IPC, offence would still be culpable homicide not amounting to murder punishable under First Part S.304 IPC - Appeal dismissed.P /&gt;[B] Criminal - IPC, 1860, ss. 302 and 498A - Murder - Dying declarations - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant was married to deceased -P /&gt;(A) Medical evidence - Held, Medical Officer stated that all injuries found on body of deceased were ante mortem and were sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - Finding recorded by Sessions Court and High Court that deceased had died a homicidal death eminently just and hereby confirmed -P /&gt;(B) Dying declaration - Whether reliable - Two dying declarations of deceased were recorded, one by Executive Magistrate and another by Head Constable - Held, consistent version of incident given in both dying declarations -P /&gt;(C) Testimony of child witness - Whether reliable? - Defence could not even prima facie establish that child witness had given tutored version of incident before Court - No major contradictions and/or improvements with reference to his earlier police statement could be brought to light at all - Held, no reason to discredit evidence of child witness - P /&gt;(D) IPC, 1860, s. 300, Exception 1 - Grave and sudden provocation - As deceased refused to withdraw maintenance proceedings and had inflicted only one blow which ultimately resulted into her death - Held, when lady, entitled to initiate maintenance proceedings against her husband, refuses to accede to unreasonable demand made by her husband to withdraw maintenance proceedings, it can hardly be said that her denial to accede to such unreasonable demand would amount to grave and sudden provocation within meaning of 'Exception 1' of s. 300 IPC - Facts of case clearly indicate that so called provocation was sought by appellant himself as an excuse for killing his wife and, therefore, appellant not entitled to benefit of provisions of 'Exception 1' to s. 300 IPC - P /&gt;(E) IPC, 1860, s. 304 Part 1 - Contention that only single blow was inflicted - Whether to be accepted? - Held, no universal rule that whenever a single blow inflicted resulting into death of victim, case would fall either under Part I or Part II of s. 304 IPC - Each case of single blow to be decided on facts and circumstances obtaining in case - Finding recorded by Sessions Court and High Court that appellant was author of fatal injury inflicted on head of deceased, well founded -P /&gt;(F) IPC, 1860, ss. 299 and 300 - Murder or culpable homicide - Held, it is proved beyond pale of doubt by prosecution that appellant had done act of giving blow on head of deceased and by doing this act, had caused death of deceased - Medical evidence also established that injuries sustained by deceased were sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause her death, which would bring instant case within purview of Clause 'Thirdly' of S. 300 IPC, which defines and explains as to when culpable homicide is murder -P /&gt;(G) Conviction - Whether u/s. 302 or 304 IPC - Held, it was intention of appellant to cause that very injury which ultimately proved fatal - Offence committed by appellant would be punishable as murder u/s. 302 IPC and his case would not fall under first part or second part of S.304 IPC - Appeal dismissed.
