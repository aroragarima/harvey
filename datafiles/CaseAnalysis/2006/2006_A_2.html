Joseph Antony Lazarus (Dead) By LRs v A. J. Francis

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 249; (2006) 9 SCC 515; AIR 2006 SC 1895; 2006 (63) ALR 781; 2006 (2) AWC 2004; 2006 (2) CTC 756; JT 2006 (4) SC 321; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 428; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 570; 2006 (101) RD 61; 2006(4) SCALE 17; 2006 (3) SCJ 806; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 709; 2006 (2) UC 1188

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Will - Genuineness - Proof-Difficult to understand as to why testatrix omitted to mention two of her sons in Will although she took pains to mention that appellant and her other son had looked after her-And paid all instalments of house property in question-Testatrix aged 83 years-Two signatures of testatrix on each page of Will-But two signatures entirely different-Will allegedly executed on 5.7.1979 but registered on 7.7.1980 after more than one year-No other document produced to establish that testatrix signed her name differently-Advocate drafting Will not examined -Sub-Registrar before whom Will said to have been presented for registration also not examined-Both could have conclusively proved preparation, execution and registration of Will-Hence, genuine doubt on genuineness of Will-No reason to differ from view taken by Division Bench of High Court-No merit in appeal.
