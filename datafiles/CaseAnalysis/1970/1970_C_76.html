Commissioner of Income Tax, U.P., and Another v J. P. Kanodia and Company

Supreme Court of India

28 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 225; (1970) 2 SCC 7; (1970) SCC (Cr) 30; AIR 1970 SC 1588; [1970] 77 ITR 515; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 418

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 23(5), 23(6), 24(1), First proviso - (A) Registered firm - Assessment - Powers of ITO to inquire whether a partner represented some other person - (B) Set off of loss against profit - Set off speculative losses against profits from other business activities of the same year - Permissibility - Held, once ITO has granted registration of a firm, he must allocate the profits in accordance with the deed of partnership registered by him and to the persons admitted to the benefits thereof according to their respective shares and he cannot proceed to inquire - Assessee is not entitled to set off speculative losses against profits from other business activities of the same year - Appeal Partly Allowed.
