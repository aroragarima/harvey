Indofil Industries Limited and others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Khanwilkar, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 858; (2017) 8 SCC 656; 2017 (3) MLJ(Crl) 647; 2017(7) SCALE 312

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Show Cause Notice, Criminal Prosecution, Insecticides Act, 1968, Opportunity, Reliance, Abuse, Insecticide, Expiry, Analyst, Insecticides Rules, 1971, Insecticide Analyst, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Insecticides Act, 1968, ss. 16, 17, 18, 19, 21, 22(6)(ii), 24(4), 29, 3(c), 3(k)(i), 30, 33, 33(1) - Manufacture of insecticide - Misbranded - Validity - Dealer displayed insecticide manufactured by appellants for sale - Insecticide Inspector took sample and opined that tested sample was misbranded and issued show cause notice - Dealer filed an application before Trial Court seeking for analysis of sample which was allowed - Central Insecticide Laboratory(CIL) opined that sample does not conform to relevant specification in active ingredients and was misbranded - Appellants filed petition before HC for quashing of prosecution instituted against them by State for an offence punishable u/ss. 3(k) (i), 17, 18, 29, 30 and 33 of the Act which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, if the report of CIL was obtained before filing of complaint or pursuant to the direction given by concerned Magistrate and made part of record of criminal prosecution, as in instant case, accused named in complaint cannot ask for analysis of sample already used at the instance of the person from whom it was taken and was named as co-accused. The provisions of the Act predicate that Insecticide Inspector has ample power to send sample for testing or analysis to CIL on his own. Rather, it was his duty to do so if such an express request was made by the person from whom the sample was taken. Further, SC held that Insecticide Inspector should take prompt steps to send the sample to CIL immediately after the protest was notified about the report given by State Insecticide Analyst. Insecticide Inspector has the option either to send the third sample on the request made by the person from whom it was collected to CIL for testing or analysis or to launch a criminal prosecution and submit the third sample in the concerned Court well before the expiry of shelf life of Insecticide to enable accused named in the complaint to ask for testing or analysis thereof in CIL. HC was right in dismissing the petition to quash criminal proceedings pending against appellants in respect of misbranded insecticide. Appeal dismissed.
