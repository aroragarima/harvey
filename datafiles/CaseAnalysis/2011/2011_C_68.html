Commissioner Of Customs v Sayed Ali & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 115; (2011) 3 SCC 537; 2011 (265) E.L.T. 17; JT 2011 (2) SC 360; 2011 (2) RCR(Civil) 780; 2011(2) SCALE 515; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1045

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: CEGAT, Collector Of Customs, Proper Officer

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Customs Act, 1962, s. 2(34), 28 and 111(d) - Jurisdiction of Collector - Respondents were charged with misusing the Export Pass Book Scheme by selling goods cleared duty free in the open market or selling the pass book on premium in violation of the ITC restriction imposed on such sale - Collector of Customs (Preventive) issued show cause notice asking the respondents to show cause as to why the goods under seizure valued at Rs. 1,04,118.52/- should not be confiscated, and customs duty amounting to Rs. 5,07,274/- be not levied in terms of s. 28(1) of the Act - In reply respondents questioned the jurisdiction of the Collector of Customs (Preventive) to issue show cause notice u/s. 28 of the Act on the ground that the jurisdiction of a Commissioner by virtue of Notification No. 251/83 being more specific and limited in nature - The Collector of Customs (Preventive) rejected the objection of the respondents and confirmed the demand of duty of Rs. 5,07,274/- u/s. 28(1) of the Act - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed against said order - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether Collector of Customs (Preventive) have a jurisdiction to issue show cause notice in terms of s. 28 of the Act? - Held, Collector of Customs (Preventive), not being a 'proper officer' within the meaning of s. 2(34) of the Act, was not competent to issue show cause notice u/s. 28 of the Act - Moreover, nothing has been brought on record to show that the Collector of Customs (Preventive), who had issued the show cause notice was assigned the functions u/s. 28 of the Act as 'proper officer' either by the Board or the Collector/Commissioner of Customs - Notifications No. 251-Cus. issued by the Central Government, appointing Collector of Customs (Preventive) etc. to be the Collector of Customs for Bombay, Thane and Kolaba Districts in the State of Maharashtra did not ipso facto confer jurisdiction on him to exercise power entrusted to the 'proper officers' for the purpose of s. 28 of the Act - Order of the tribunal - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
