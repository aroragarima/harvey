Union of India and Others v Shatabadi Trading and Investment P. Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 278; (2001) 6 SCC 748; AIR 2001 SC 3211; 2001 (169) CTR 408; [2001] 251 ITR 93; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 269; JT 2001 (6) SC 488; 2001(5) SCALE 149; 2001 (5) SLT 730; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 416; 2001 (5) Supreme 735

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Land & Property - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136 and 226 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 269 UD - Agreement was entered into to sell - Intended seller and purchaser filed Form No. 37-I for issuance of a 'no objection certificate' - Purchase order was passed by Appropriate Authority and, on same day, owner of land was directed to surrender possession of subject property - Appropriate Authority passed an order for acquisition - High Court granted interim order of stay - Against said interim order a special leave petition was preferred before Supreme Court - Supreme Court directed that property be auctioned subject to bid confirmation by Supreme Court - Auction held - Sale deed executed by Appropriate Authority in favour of highest bidders - Challenged - Held, approach of HC that in cases where property is a tenanted property and is being sold department cannot adopt stand of invoking provisions of Chap. XX-C may not be correct - Having given due deductions towards tenancy arising in respect of property and on comparison with similar properties situated elsewhere, though not tenanted, but after discounting for tenancy if a mode of valuation is adopted by Department, HC could not have termed same as illegal, irrational or arbitrary - Basis of valuation had been disclosed to party concerned in show-cause notice in sufficient detail - Non-supply of valuation report itself may not be vital to case - When transferor without demur allowed property to be sold pursuant to orders of Supreme Court and that sale having taken place and Supreme Court having affirmed same and proceedings by way of special leave petition filed u/art. 136 of Constitution, 1950 coming to an end as having become infructuous, HC could not have brushed aside that sale in manner it has been done - Appeals allowed.
