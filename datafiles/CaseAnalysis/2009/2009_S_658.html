Mahtab Singh & Anr v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 444; (2009) 13 SCC 670; AIR 2009 SC 2298; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 332; 2009(5) SCALE 500; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 848

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Court's Jurisdiction

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 r/w 34 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 4 and 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 378 and 386 - Acquittal by Trial Court on ground that prosecution failed to bring home the charge beyond reasonable doubt - Acquittal reversed by High Court - Whether prosecution has proved the case against accused beyond reasonable doubt or whether HC was justified in reversing the acquittal? - Evidence of eye witnesses, police authorities, and expert witnesses, perused - Deceased and his brother had demanded money owing to them by appellant and his father - Appellant started rebuking deceased and his brother - Appellant raised a cry and called his son to kill the deceased - Time of occurrence was 9:00 pm - From autopsy report and testimony of Doctor witness, Court held that death was homicidal - Deceased and his brother were returning from the betel shop of one Rajveer when the incident took place, and the place of incidence was hardly 2-3 steps away from such betel shop - Court took surprise on the fact that Rajveer's statement was neither recorded u/s. 161 of CrPC nor he was tendered in examination before the Court - Further surprise was that lantern which was claimed by prosecution that it was burning at the time of occurrence due to which PW1 and PW2 could see the culprits, was never seized by the Investigation Officer - Such omissions struck at the very genesis of the crime and rendered it doubtful and full of suspicion - Evidence of PW1 who was brother of deceased also found to be highly doubtful due to the reason of his having inimical terms with the appellant - Factum of FIR being registered after 45 minutes of incidence when police station was just a furlong away from the place of occurrence, makes prosecution's case doubtful - PW2 was the another eye witness - Court on facts of case, observed that his presence at the site was highly unnatural on the reasoning that he did not belonged to the village where incidence had happened as he was from the other village, and he claimed to be there for the purpose of grinding his food grain, and doubt lied from the fact that at 9:00 pm it would be unnatural that someone from other village could do so - Thus testimonies of two crucial witnesses not confirming with collateral circumstances as well as probabilities - Testimony thus shrouded with grave suspicion and serious doubts - Court further deliberated upon scope of exercise of power of appellate Court against judgment of acquittal u/ss. 378 and 386 of CrPC - Precedents on the subject referred - Principles quoted - HC found to have seriously erred in disturbing the judgement of acquittal and finding of guilt - Impugned judgment of HC thus set aside - Appeal Allowed.
