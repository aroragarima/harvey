Mehraj Singh v State Of U.P

Supreme Court of India

21 April 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 450; (1994) 5 SCC 188; (1994) SCC (Cr) 1391; AIR 1994 SCW 2210; 1994 ALJ 1032; 1995 (19) AllCriR 176; 1994 (2) Crimes 154; 1995 CRLJ 457; JT 1994 (3) SC 440; [1994] 3 S.C.R. 592

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellate Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 154, 157 and 174 - (A) Whether FIR was ante-timed and had not been recorded till the inquest proceedings were over? - There is no evidence to show as to when did the copy of the FIR, special report, which was required to be despatched under the statutory provisions of s. 154 r/w s. 157, CrPC, 1973 promptly, to the Magistrate was actually despatched - Statement of prosecution witness that the about occurrence appears to have been made because she wanted to back up the prosecution story by stating that the FIR had been lodged promptly at 12.45 pm - No explanation has been furnished for the omission of the vital matters, such as crime No., heading of the case, from the inquest report - Inquest report is not signed by any of the eyewitnesses - Held, that the FIR in the case was not recorded at the time as alleged by the prosecution - (B) Whether ocular testimony was contradicted by medical evidence? - Absence of blood from the place where the dead body was found, as admitted by PW also suggests that the occurrence did not take place in the manner suggested by the prosecution - Possibility of three types of sharp-edged weapons were used cannot be ruled out on account of the incised wound - Further held, that the ocular testimony does not fit in with the medical evidence and instead it contradicts it - Case against both the appellants has not been proved beyond a reasonable doubt and that they are entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
