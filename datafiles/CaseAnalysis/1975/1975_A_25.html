Anil Kumar Roy Chowdhury and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 339; (1976) 4 SCC 776; AIR 1976 SC 772; 1976 CTR 98; [1976] 102 ITR 12; 1976 UJ 28

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Family Property, Question Of Fact And Law, Agricultural Income-Tax

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 101 and 104 - (A) Income Tax - Reference u/s. 66 - Nature and scope of jurisdiction of High Court - Duty of HC to accept findings of fact made by Appellate Tribunal - (B) Evidence Act - Assessment in respect of income from Agricultural activities - Claim of assessee that the income did not belong to the HUF, but to its members in their individual capacity - Burden of proof - Held, HC has no jurisdiction go behind the order, to investigate what case the assessee had initially made when the agreed statement of case sets out the assessee's claim as finally made and considered - Burden rests on the Department to prove that the income belonged to the HUF - Appeals Allowed.
