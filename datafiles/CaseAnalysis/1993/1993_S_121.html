Subodh Kumar Gupta v Shrikant Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1738; (1993) 4 SCC 1; AIR 1994 SCW 533; 1993 (22) ALR 426; JT 1993 (4) SC 601; 1994 (1) MLJ(SC) 8; 1993(3) SCALE 482; [1993] Supp1 S.C.R. 660

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 15, 16 and 20 - Territorial jurisdiction of the Civil Court - Whether, court competent to have jurisdiction if part of cause action has not arisen within its territorial limits? - Held, cls. (a) and (b) of s. 20, are not attracted in the facts of the present case - None of defendants at the time of the commencement of the suit actually and voluntarily resided or carried on business or personally worked for gain within the territorial jurisdiction of the Chandigarh Court - Cl. (b) can apply only if at least one of the defendants actually and voluntarily resided or carried on business or personally worked for gain while the others did not - But that is not the case here - The case must, therefore, be governed by cl. (c) which requires that the whole or part of the cause of action must be shown to have arisen within the territorial limits of the Chandigarh Court - Now as is evident from the averments in the plaint no part of the cause of action arose within the territorial jurisdiction of the Chandigarh Court - Therefore, Single Judge correctly held that the Chandigarh Court has no jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
