Toran Singh v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1913; (2002) 6 SCC 494; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1377; AIR 2002 SC 2807; 2002 (3) CCR 85; 2002 (3) Crimes 82; 2002 (6) CRJ 70; 2002 CRLJ 3737; 2002 (2) JCC 1304; JT 2002 (5) SC 498; 2002(5) SCALE 407; 2002 (4) SLT 569; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 390; 2002 (5) Supreme 219; 2002 (2) UC 550

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Appellant charged for murder of deceased - Witness was son of deceased - Motive for alleged act said to be that deceased had eloped with wife of accused - Whether conviction of appellant can be based upon testimony of prosecution witness? - Held, no - Conduct of witness is shrouded in mystery - He did not raise any hue and cry when his father was being attacked - Appellant could not inflict injuries since he had only one hand and could have easily been overpowered - Serious infirmities and improbabilities of prosecution case giving rise to grave doubts as to involvement of appellant in commission of offence - Appellant entitled to benefit of doubt - Appeal allowed.
