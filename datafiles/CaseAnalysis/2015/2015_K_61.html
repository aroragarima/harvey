Mehmood Ul Rehman and another v Khazir Mohammad Tunda and others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 413; AIR 2015 SC 2195; 2015 CRLJ 2856; 2015 (2) JCC 1337; JT 2015 (5) SC 195; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 517; 2015(4) SCALE 381; [2015] 4 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Precedent, Speaking Order, Judicial Notice, Pollution

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 190, 200, 204 - Opinion of Magistrate - Cognizance of offence - On complaint filed by respondent no. 1 against appellants, Magistrate passed order initiating proceedings - Aggrieved, appellants filed petition before HC to quash said proceedings, but HC rejected petition and hence, instant appeals - Whether Magistrate is justified in initiation of proceedings against appellants.

Held, even though no formal or speaking or reasoned orders are required at the stage of s. 190/204 of CrPC, yet, there should be sufficient indication on the application of mind by the Magistrate to the facts constituting commission of an offence and the statements recorded u/s. 200 of CrPC so as to proceed against the offender. However, there is no indication on the application of mind by Magistrate in taking cognizance and issuing process to the appellants. Hence, appeals are allowed and orders of Magistrate and HC are set aside and matter is remitted to Magistrate for fresh consideration. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - If the complaint prima facie does not disclose commission of any offence, then Magistrate should not take cognizance u/s. 190(1)(a) of CrPC and should reject the complaint.
