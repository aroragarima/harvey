Seth R. Dalmia v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 218; (1977) 4 SCC 329; AIR 1977 SC 2394; [1978] 48 Comp Cas 1; 1977 CTR 378; [1977] 110 ITR 644; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 537; 1977 UJ 642

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922 , ss. 10, 10(2)(xv) and 12(2) - Income Tax - Assessee acquired shares from borrowed money - When interest paid on such loan is deductible - Damages paid by assessee due to his own default whether capital expenditure in the light of s.10(2)(xv) and s.10 of the Act - Whether he is entitled to claim deduction of interest and exemption of dividend - Held, for deduction certain conditions to be fulfilled -Capital expenditure rather than a revenue one - Deduction allowed , but exemption could not be claimed - Appeal Partly Allowed.
