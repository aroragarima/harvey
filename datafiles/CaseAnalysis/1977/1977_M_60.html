Chhaganlal v Municipal Corporation

Supreme Court of India

28 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 367; (1977) 2 SCC 409; AIR 1977 SC 1555; 1977 (6) CTR 271; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 871; 1977 UJ 139

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Madhya Pradesh Municipal Corporation Act, 1956, s. 147(1) - Property tax - Assessment - Challenged - Appellant (Factory owner) whose house-tax was fixed by respondent (Municipal Corporation) to be paid w.e.f 1-4-1954 - Against said assessment appellant preferred an appeal before Municipal Appeal Committee which was allowed and remanded case to respondent - After remand respondent reduced tax but again assessment was objected by appellant u/s. 147(1) of the Act, before Municipal Commissioner which was dismissed - Aggrieved appellant appealed before First Additional District Judge who allowed appeal and remanded matter for fresh disposal and after remand respondent reduced tax further to be paid w.e.f 1-4-1954 and appellant filed objections which were rejected by Commissioner - Against Commissioners order appellant filed an appeal to Second Additional District Judge who partly allowed appeal and held that property tax had to be paid only from 1-4-1965 and not from 1-4-1954 - Aggrieved respondent filed revision before HC which was allowed and held that house-tax had to be paid from 1-4-1954 - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, appellants counsel pleaded that question involved total lack of powers of HC and this Court should hold that order of HC was without jurisdiction - This Court had laid down principles governing interference u/s. 115 of CPC was Baldevdas Shivlal & Anr. v. Filmistan Distributors (India) (P) Ltd. & Ors., 1969 Indlaw SC 184, M/s. D.L.F. Housing and Construction Co. (P) Ltd. v. Sarup singh and Ors., 1969 Indlaw SC 391, and The Managing Director (MIG) Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd. Balanagar Hyderabad and Anr. v. Ajit Prasad Tarway, Manager (Purchase and Stores) Hindustan Aeronautics Ltd. Balangar Hyderabad1971 Indlaw SC 27 - These cases had been referred to in recent decision of SC in The Municipal Corporation of Delhi v. Suresh Chandra Jaipuria and Anr. 1976 Indlaw SC 652, and attention of Judges of HC was drawn to the law declared by this Court - This Court considered it unnecessary to discuss law on subject over again as that appeal was liable to be dismissed on ground that point was not taken before HC and discretion of this Court to interfere or not was beyond question - Thus, in result SC found that there was no substance in instant appeal and dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
