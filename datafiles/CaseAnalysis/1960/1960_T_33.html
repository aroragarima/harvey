Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay North v Chandulal Keshavlal and Company

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 507; AIR 1960 SC 738; [1960] 30 Comp Cas 261; [1960] 38 ITR 601; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 101; 1960 SCJ 1258; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 38

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 (2) (xv) - Managing agent - Commission - Commercial expediency - Subsidiary company - Appellant and respondent was a partnership firm which by an agreement was appointed Managing Agent of X Mills Ltd for sake of convenience respondent firm - By cl. 4 of agreement Managing Agent was to get a commission of 4% on sale proceeds of cloth, yarn or other goods manufactured and sold by company and 15% on amount of bills for charges of ginning and pressing and dyeing or bleaching and on amount of labour bills and other work done in running of factory - Amount of commission was to be credited in account of Managing Agent every six months and it was entitled to interest at the rate of 6%. per annum on amount credited - Total commission for accounting year 1950 was Rs. 3,09,114 at oral request of Board of Directors Managing Agent agreed to accept a sum of Rs. 1,00,000 only as its commission which was credited to account of Managing Agent in books of company at end of year - Income tax Officer and Appellate Assistant Commissioner detained amount which accrued as commission to Managing Agent was Rs. 3,09,114 and amount was taxable - Appeal was taken to Income-tax Appellate Tribunal by Managing Agent - HC decided in favor of appellant - Hence this instant appeal - Whether sum was an allowable deduction from assessee's income - Held, if payment or expenditure was incurred for purpose of trade of assessee it does not matter that payment may injure to benefit of a third party - Managing Agent amount was expended for reasons of commercial expediency, it was not given as a bounty but to strengthen Managed Company and if financial position of Managed Company became strong Managing Agent would benefit thereby - Deduction of sum must be given up for reasons of commercial expediency and it may be voluntary but so long as it was incurred for assessee's benefit deduction would be claimable - Appeal dismissed.
