State of Bihar v Gopi Kishore Prasad

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 49; AIR 1960 SC 689; 1960 (1) LLJ 577

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Whether discharge amounted to punishment so that provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution are applicable to a probationer in Bihar subordinate civil service, who has been discharged as unsuitable on grounds of notoriety for corruption and unsatisfactory work in discharge of his public duties ? - Held, discharge on these grounds amounted to punishment - Petitioner had been wrongly deprived of that protection - His removal from service was not in accordance with requirements of Constitution and, therefore, could not be sustained - Discharge being clearly by way of punishment he was entitled to protection of art. 311(2) - Appeal dismissed.
