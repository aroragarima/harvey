Saroj @ Suraj Panchal and another v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

03 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 225; (2014) 4 SCC 802; 2014 CRLJ 3134; 2014 (2) JCC 1238; JT 2014 (4) SC 608; 2014 (2) RCR(Criminal) 625; 2014(4) SCALE 358; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 646

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - A case was registered against accused persons/appellants u/ss. 300, 304, 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Aggrieved appellants appealed to HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was liable to set aside -

Held, nature of injuries sustained by deceased and circumstances as enumerated it could be concluded that death was caused by acts of appellants done with intention of causing such bodily injury as was likely to cause death and therefore offence would squarely come within first part of s. 304 of IPC and appellants would be liable to be convicted for said offence - Conviction of appellants u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC was liable to be set aside - Imposition of seven years rigorous imprisonment on each of appellants for conviction u/s. 304 Part I of IPC would meet ends of justice - Conviction of appellants for offence u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentence of life imprisonment each imposed on them were set aside and instead they were convicted for offence u/s. 304 Part I r/w s. 34 of IPC and sentenced to undergo seven years rigorous imprisonment - Appeal partly allowed.
