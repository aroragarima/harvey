A. Tripathi and Another v J.P. Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1340; (1993) SCC (L&S) 213; (1993) 1 SCC 426; 1993 (23) ATC 650; 1993 (1) CLR 313; 1993 (66) FLR 494; JT 1993 (1) SC 115; 1994 (1) LLJ 390; 1993(1) SCALE 21; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 20; 1993 (3) SLJ 118; 1993 (1) SLR 555; 1993 (1) UPLBEC 703

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Respondent was selected for the post of Addl. Chief Engineer, Level 11 by the Selection Committee in 1985 and his name was brought on the merit list - He was not offered the appointment and in 1989 fresh selections were held by Selection Committee - Appellants were selected by the Selection Committee in the year 1989 whereas respondent was not found fit by the Selection Committee on the basis of his service record - Appellants were promoted to post of Addl. Chief Engineer - Respondent challenged the selection before HC, which was allowed - HC set aside selection made in year 1989 and directed that respondent be given appointment on the basis of his selection made in the year 1985 - Sustainability of judgment passed by HC? - Held, in the facts of the case, it will be proper to cancel the selection of appellants, since the selection made by the committee constituted in 1989 is legal and valid and the panel prepared by Selection Committee in 1985 came to an end - Further held, since selection made by the committee of 1985 was not challenged and it is no body's case that such selection is otherwise illegal and invalid - Consequently, it will be unjust and unfair to deny promotion to respondent to the post of Addl. Chief Engineer against the available vacancy existing in 1989 - Directions issued regarding promotion - Appeals disposed of.
