Navaneethammal v Arjuna Chetty

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2161; (1996) 6 SCC 166; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 721; AIR 1996 SC 3521; AIR 1996 SCW 3635; 1996 (4) CCC 5; 1996 (4) CLT 31; JT 1996 (7) SC 698; 1997 (6) KarLJ 140; 1996 (2) RentLR 336; 1996(6) SCALE 407; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 582; 1996 (7) Supreme 136

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 139 - Second appeal - Concurrent findings - Interference by High Court must be avoided generally - Appreciation of evidence by HC (A) Just to replace findings to Lower Courts - Validity - (B) View taken by first Appellate Court based on material - Another view possible on reappreciation of evidence - (C) Suit for recovery of possession - Filed within five years after tenant first openly set up hostile title - Limitation - Not proper - Held, HC should not reappreciate evidence to reach another view - It is within limitation - Appeal allowed.
