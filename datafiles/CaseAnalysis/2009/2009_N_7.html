P. K. Palanisamy v N. Arumugham and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1090; (2009) 9 SCC 173; 2009(5) ALL MR 957; 2010 (1) AWC 498; 2009 (4) CTC 187; JT 2009 (10) SC 79; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 123; 2010 (1) MPLJ 513; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 129; 2009 (108) RD 452; 2009(10) SCALE 79; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Deficiency Of Court Fees, Registration Of Plaint, Rejection Of Plaint, Application For Attachment

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s.149, O.7 r.11(c) - Money suit - Rejection of plaint - Appellant filed suit against respondent for recovery of loan advance to him - Appellant failed to deposit deficit court fee within time - Trial Court accepted court fee after grant of several extension and thereby condoned delay in refilling plaint - Respondent filed written statement, however, remained absent - Trial Court passed ex parte decree in favor of appellant - Respondent filed application to set aside ex parte decree - Trial Court allowed respondent's application with cost of Rs. 1000 - Respondent filed Revision before HC - Single Judge allowed Revision with condition of pre deposit of Rs. 3, 00, 000 - Respondent deposited money and got revived suit - Examination of appellant and his witnesses were over - Respondent sought rejection of plaint on ground of limitation and delay condonation in refilling of plaint by Trial Court without notice to him - Trial Court dismissed respondent's application for rejection of plaint - Respondent filed Revision before HC - Revision allowed - Legality - Held, once application for extension of time u/s.149 allowed, application for rejection of plaint under O.7 r.11(c) would have no application - Application for rejection of suit subsequent to its registration appeared to be mala fide - If Trial Court did not entertain plea of respondent, HC should not have interfered therewith - Respondents in their written statement did not raise any objection with regard to extension of time - Trial Court satisfied bona fide of appellant and thereby plaint accepted on payment of deficit court fee - Trial Court started trial and witnesses were examined by both parties - Respondents or his counsel did not have occasion to inspect records earlier, was unbelievable - Reason of wrong mentioning of s.149 instead of s.148 in application for extension of time would not render said application not maintainable or order passed thereon would be nullity - Application for rejecting plaint would not be entertainable at the stage of evidence - Therefore, respondents' application for rejection of plaint could not have been maintained, hence impugned judgment set aside - Appeal allowed.
