Shri Balaganesan Metals v Shri M.N. Shanmugham Chetty And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28252; (1987) 2 SCC 707; AIR 1987 SC 1668; 1987 (1) CCC 1054; JT 1987 (2) SC 247; 1989 (1) MLJ(SC) 4; 1987 (2) RCJ 75; 1987 (1) RCR(Rent) 586; 1987 (2) RentLR 391; 1987(1) SCALE 1110; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 1173

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Civil Procedure - Tamil Nadu Buildings, (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, ss.2(2), 10(3)(a)(i) and 10(3)(c) - Tenancy - Appeal against order of eviction u/s. 10(3)(c) - Contentions raised that: (A) Since the ground floor constitutes a building by itself within the meaning of s.2(2) of the Act, the respondents can seek eviction only u/s.10(3) (a)(i) of the Act and not u/s. 10(3)(c) - Held, contention untenable - (B) Need for additional accommodation for residence -Even if the respondents are entitled to invoke Section 10(3)(c) they can seek eviction only if they require the ground floor for non-residential purposes and not for residential purposes - Contention held, tenable - And other contention as to comparative hardship and interference with finding of appellate authority also failed - (C) Suit for eviction by predecessor-in-title dismissed - Subsequent eviction proceedings by legatees - Malafide, if - Held, mala fides cannot be attributed because of dismissal of earlier proceedings - Appeal dismissed.
