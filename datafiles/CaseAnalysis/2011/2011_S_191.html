Krishan Kumar Malik v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Deepak Verma, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 401; (2011) 7 SCC 130; AIR 2011 SC 2877; 2011 CRLJ 4274; JT 2011 (7) SC 94; 2011 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2302; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 931; 2011 (2) OLR 307; 2011(6) SCALE 759; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 774

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 366 and 376 (2) - Abduction and rape - Conviction - Challenged - Trial court convicted A1 to A8 guilty for commission of offence punishable u/s. 366 of IPC and also A1 to A4, guilty for commission of offences u/s. 376(2)(g) of IPC - Trial Court awarded 10 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 2,000/- each u/s. 376 of IPC and 5 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/- each u/s. 366 of IPC to A1 to A4 - Trial court also awarded 3 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/- each to A5 and A6 - Trial court also awarded 5 years rigorous imprisonment and a fine of Rs. 1,000/- to A7 and A8 - On appeal, HC acquitted A3 and A4 to all the charges levelled against them - Thus out of the initial 8, only the remaining 6 accused were found to have committed offences u/s. 366 of IPC and, in addition, A1 and A2 were also found to have committed offences u/s. 376 (2) (g) of the IPC, by the HC - However, only A1 filed appeal against said order - Whether A1 had committed the offence of abduction and rape on the prosecutrix or whether he has been falsely implicated? - Held, prosecutrix had not mentioned the name of A1 in the FIR lodged by her promptly, even though she was aware of his name - No explanation had been offered by her in this regard - Prosecutrix admitted in her cross examination that she had come to know the names of all the accused during the course of occurrence, as they were talking each other's name - Despite that she did not name the A1 in the FIR - These omissions speak volumes against her and her credibility stands shaken - Admittedly prosecutrix was already shown A1 and other accused at the Police Station, after they were arrested - Thus, her dock identification in Court had become meaningless - To hold an accused guilty for commission of an offence of rape, the solitary evidence of prosecutrix was sufficient provided the same inspires confidence and appears to be absolutely trustworthy, unblemished and should be of sterling quality - But, in the instant case, the evidence of the prosecutrix, showing several lacunae, would go to show that her evidence does not fall in that category and cannot be relied upon to hold A1 guilty of the said offences - Hence, judgment and order of conviction as recorded by the trial court and confirmed by the HC to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
