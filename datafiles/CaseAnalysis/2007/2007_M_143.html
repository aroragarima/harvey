Messrs Sriram Industrial Enterprises Limited v Mahak Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 230; (2007) 4 SCC 94; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 961; AIR 2007 SC 1370; 2007 (3) ALJ 253; 2007 (5) AWC 5332; 2007 (2) CLR 744; 2007 (2) ESC 328; JT 2007 (4) SC 278; 2007 (2) LLN 144; 2007(4) SCALE 237; 2007 (7) SCJ 176; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 783; 2007 (2) UPLBEC 1560

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6 N - Retrenchment of respondents - Drawing an adverse inference against petitioner herein for non-production of the documents in its possession and holding that the petitioner had failed to discharge the onus and disprove the workmens' claim, the High Court held that Tribunal should have drawn an adverse presumption - Petition against - Held, having correctly interpreted the provisions of s. 6 N High Court rightly drew an adverse presumption for non-production of the Attendance Registers and the Muster Rolls for the years 1991 onwards - The best evidence having been withheld, the High Court was entitled to draw such adverse inference - Petitions dismissed.
