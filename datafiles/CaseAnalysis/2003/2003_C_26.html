Bokajan Cement Corporation Employees' Union v Cement Corporation of India Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1422; (2004) SCC (L&S) 23; (2004) 1 SCC 142; AIR 2004 SC 245; 2004 (1) CLR 272; 2004 (4) ESC 488; 2003 (99) FLR 1044; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 41; JT 2003 (8) SC 482; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 708; 2004 (1) LLJ 197; 2004 (1) LLN 20; 2003(9) SCALE 477; 2003 (1) SCW 5953; 2004 (1) SLR 99; 2003 (7) SLT 45; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 400; 2003 (7) Supreme 770; 2003 (7) Supreme 970; 2004 (1) UPLBEC 462

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Trade Unions Act, 1926, ss. 15, 22, 4, 5, 6, 6(e) - Trade Union - Cessation of employment -Validity - HC had held that right to continue as member of trade union continued so long as employee was actually employed - For its conclusion reliance had been placed by HC on s. 6 of the Act and cl. 5 of the Constitution of Bokajan Cement Corporation Employees Union??? - HC had held that membership of trade union was not benefit that accrues to employee so as to claim its continuance even after he ceases to be in employment; his right continues so long as he remains employed and on cessation of employment, membership of trade union ceases - Hence, instant appeal - Whether employee as result of cessation of employment would lose his right to continue as member of trade union - Held, there was no provision in the Act on Constitution of trade union providing for automatic cessation of membership on cessation of employment - In view of provisions in Constitution of trade union and in absence of any provision providing for cessation of membership as result of cessation of employment, it could not be held that employee would cease to be member of trade union on termination of his employment -Hence, impugned judgment was set aside judgment of learned Single judge of HC was restored - Appeal allowed.
