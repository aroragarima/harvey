Essco Fabs Private Limited and another v State of Haryana And Another

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2494; (2009) 2 SCC 377; AIR 2009 SC 1552; 2009 (1) AWC 594; JT 2008 (12) SC 315; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1150; 2008(14) SCALE 495; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 779

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition (Amendment and Validation) Ordinance, 1967, Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 1923, Kanpur Urban Area Development Act, 1945, HUDA Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 5, 6, 17 - Land acquisition - Right of hearing of land owners - Dispensation of - Respondent issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act for acquiring the disputed land and thereafter, issued final notification u/s. 6 of the Act - Since the land acquisition proceedings could not be completed within the stipulated time, said notification lapsed and the disputed land stood released - Appellant/Company bought the disputed land as a part of its unit expansion - Appellant sought permission for change of user - Respondents refused the permission on the ground that there was a proposal for acquisition of disputed land - Respondents issued notification u/s. 4(1) and final notification u/s. 6 passed on the very next day by invoking urgent clause u/s. 17 of the Act - Appellant contended that they were denied the valuable right of raising objections u/s. 5A - Held, after the issuance of preliminary notification u/s. 4 and before the issuance of final notification u/s. 6 of the Act, the appropriate Government is enjoined to hear persons interested in the property before they are deprived of their ownership rights - Respondents cannot dispense with the mandatory procedure u/s. 5A unless the issuance of notification u/s. 17(4) - No urgency clause could have been invoked by the respondents and inquiry and hearing of objections provided by s. 5A of the Act could not have been dispensed with - Therefore, actions of issuance of urgency clause u/s. 17(4), dispensing with inquiry u/s. 5A and issuance of final notification u/s. 6(1) are quashed and set aside - Followed the decision of SC in Union of India & Ors. v. Mukesh Hans, 2004 INDLAW SC 1413 - Appeals allowed.
