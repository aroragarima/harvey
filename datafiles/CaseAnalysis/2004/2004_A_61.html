State Of Gujarat & Ors. v Akhil Gujarat Pravasi V.S. Mahamandal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 263; (2004) 5 SCC 155; AIR 2004 SC 3894; 2004 (3) G.L.R. 1904; JT 2004 (4) SC 470; 2004(4) SCALE 487

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Indirect Tax - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(g), 21, r. 300A - Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax Act, 1958, ss. 3A(1), 3A(2) - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Bombay Motor Vehicles Rules, 1959, r. 5 - Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax (Gujarat Amendment) Rules, 2001 - Bombay Motor Vehicles Tax Act amended by Gujarat Act No 9 of 2002 in order to validate imposition and collection of tax on designated omnibuses - Courts lean more readily in favour of upholding constitutionality of taxing law in view of complexities involved in social and economic life of community - Unless fiscal law in question is manifestly discriminatory, Court should refrain from striking it down on ground of discrimination - Tax imposed upon designated omnibuses not discriminatory.
