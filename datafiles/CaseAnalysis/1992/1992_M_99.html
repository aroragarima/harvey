Madhya Pradesh Housing Board Etc. v Mohd. Shafi And Ors. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.H. Kania, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 881; (1992) 2 SCC 168; 1992 (20) ALR 771; 1992 (1) CCC 507; JT 1992 (3) SC 523; 1992(1) SCALE 322; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 657; 1992 (Supp) RD 76; 1992 (2) UJ 73

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1)and 17(1) - Whether notification u/s. 4(1) was vague and invalid for non-compliance with mandatory requirements of Act and recourse to urgency provisions u/s. 17(1) could not be had since land was not 'waste or arable', a sine qua non for exercising powers u/s. 17(1) ? - Notification u/s. 4 is required to give sufficient clarity of not only 'public purpose' for which acquisition proceedings are being commenced but also 'locality' where land is situate to enable 'interested' persons to know as to which land is being acquired and for what purpose and to take further steps under Act by filing objections etc. - If notification u/s. 4(1) is defective, it not only vitiates notification, but also renders all subsequent proceedings connected with acquisition, bad - Held, not only non-disclosure of 'locality' with precision invalidates notification but even 'public purpose' mentioned in schedule to notification as 'residential' is hopelessly vague and conveys no idea about purpose of acquisition rendering notifications as invalid in law - Petition dismissed.
