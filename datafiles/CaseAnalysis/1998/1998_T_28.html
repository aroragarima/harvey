Vimal Kumari v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1953; (1998) 4 SCC 114; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1018; 1998 (1) CLR 882; 1998 (79) FLR 147; JT 1998 (2) SC 111; 1998 (1) LLJ 1071; 1998 (3) LLN 55; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 9; 1998(2) SCALE 31; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 658; 1998 (2) SLJ 61; 1998 (2) SLR 230; 1998 (3) SLT 397; 1998 (2) Supreme 246

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution - Haryana Social Welfare and Relief Organisation Service Group 'C' Rules, 1983 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 - Reversion - Validity - Whether any promotion or reversion under Draft Rules is valid? - Draft Rules cannot be treated to be Rules made u/art. 309 of Constitution and cannot legally exclude operation of any existing executive or administrative instruction on subjects covered by Draft Rules nor can such Draft Rules exclude jurisdiction of Govt., or for that matter, any other authority, including appointing authority, from issuing executive instructions for regulating conditions of service of employees working under them - Held, promotions were made on basis of 'seniority' which, in absence of any Rule made u/art. 309, could be legally adopted as reasonable basis for promotion - Seniority have been adopted as criteria for making promotion on post of Superintendent could not have been displaced by Draft Rules and High Court could not have invoked any provision of those Draft Rules which have been lying frozen at their embryonic stage for more than ten years - Appeal allowed.
