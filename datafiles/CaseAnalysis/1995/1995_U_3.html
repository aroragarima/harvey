State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v U. P. Madhyamik Shiksha Parishad Shramik Sangh and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 771; (1996) 7 SCC 34; (1996) SCC (L&S) 371; AIR 1996 SC 708; 1996 (32) ATC 517; JT 1995 (9) SC 132; 1995(6) SCALE 434; 1996 (1) SLR 303; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law -Minimum Wages Act - Equal pay - Regularisation - Entitled to - Whether High Court decision to pay equal pay for equal work and also regularisation of appellant's services by creating appropriate posts is correct? - Held, unless posts are created, appellant are not entitled to be fitted into any regular post - Performance of manual duty may be like duty of regular Class IV employees - They are not entitled for payment of equal wages so long as there are no posts created in that behalf - No posts are created or existing, court cannot uphold direction issued by HC to pay equal wages or to regularise their services - Appeal allowed.
