Smt. Sarabati Devi and Another v Smt. Usha Devi

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 252; (1984) SCC (Tax) 59; (1984) 1 SCC 424; 1984 ACJ 138; AIR 1984 SC 346; 1984 ALJ 194; 1984 (10) ALR 268; 1984 BLJR 21; [1984] 55 Comp Cas 214; 1983(2) SCALE 869; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 992; 1984 UJ 866

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Insurance

Keywords: Death Of The Assured, Policy Holder, Policy Of Life Insurance, Nomination, Policy Money, Married Women's Property Act, 1874

Summary: Insurance - Family & Personal - Insurance Act, 1938, s. 39 - Life insurance policy - Law of succession - Right of Nominee - Entitlement of amount - Whether a nominee u/s. 39 of the Act gets an absolute right to the amount due under a life insurance policy on the death of the assured.

Held, mere nomination made u/s. 39 of the Act does not have the effect of conferring on the nominee any beneficial interest in the amount payable under the life insurance policy on the death of the assured. The nomination only indicates the hand which is authorised to receive the amount, on the payment of which the insurer gets a valid discharge of its liability under the policy, The amount however, can be claimed by the heirs of the assured in accordance with the law of succession governing them. Appeal allowed.
