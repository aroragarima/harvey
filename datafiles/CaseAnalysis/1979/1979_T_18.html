Â Bareilly Holdings Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, N.L. Untwalia, O. Chinnappa Reddy, A.P. Sen, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 10; (1979) 3 SCC 257; (1979) SCC (L&S) 262; AIR 1979 SC 1211; 1979 (38) FLR 275; 1979 LabIC 600; 1979 (1) LLJ 352; 1979 (1) LLN 516; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 236; 1979 UJ 356

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 4(k) - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 74, 49, 72, 97(1) and 46 - Employees' State Insurance (General) Regulations, 1950 - Whether action of employers in deducting half wage corresponding to sickness benefit to which workmen are entitled under E. S. I. Act in event of workmen not availing services of E. S. I. is legal?- Held, providing for periodical payments to an insured worker in case of sickness, Legislature did not intend to substitute any of those benefits for workmen's right to get leave on full pay on grounds of sickness - A workman becomes entitled to sickness benefit only if he is qualified for it and he gets a cash benefit only if he avails himself of sickness benefit - Thus, it is only when a workman, in fact obtains or receives a cash benefit then employer can exercise his right to make a deduction from wages due to him by way of leave salary - Appeal dismissed.
