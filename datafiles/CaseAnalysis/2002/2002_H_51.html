Hemalatha Gargya v Commissioner of Income Tax and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1934; (2003) 9 SCC 510; 2003 (182) CTR 107; 2003(5) SCALE 15; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 382; [2003] 128 TAXMAN 190

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1997 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Voluntary disclosure of income - Income chargeable to tax - Benefit of scheme - Refund of amount - Voluntary disclosure of income scheme was introduced and contained in 1997 Act came into force with effect from 1-7-1997 and remained in operation till 31-3-1998 - S.64 of 1961 Act provided that any person make declaration after date of commencement of scheme in accordance with provisions of s.65, such person entitled to payment of tax on undisclosed income - Whether assessees entitled to benefit of scheme - Held, tax payable under scheme in respect of voluntarily disclosed income should paid by declarant and declaration accompanied by proof of payment of such tax - Where assessees sought to claim benefit under statutory scheme they are bound to comply strictly with conditions under which benefit was granted - Assessees not entitled to benefit of scheme since payments made by them were not in terms of scheme - Revenue Authorities directed to refund or adjust amounts already deposited by assessees in purported compliance with provisions of scheme to concerned assessees in accordance with law - Appeal disposed of.
