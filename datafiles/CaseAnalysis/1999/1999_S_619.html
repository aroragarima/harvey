Padam Singh v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

30 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, M.B. Shah

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 857; (2000) SCC (Cr) 285; (2000) 1 SCC 621; AIR 2000 SC 361; 2000 ALJ 123; 1999 (4) CCR 297; 2000 (1) Crimes 41; 2000 CRLJ 489; 2000 (1) JCC 138; JT 1999 (9) SC 351; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 138; 1999(7) SCALE 268; 2000 (1) SCJ 143; 1999 (10) SLT 157; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 59; 1999 (9) Supreme 545

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 149, 302, 307, 324 and 342 - (A) Whether in facts and circumstance, case was proved beyond reasonable doubt? - When prosecution does not explain injury sustained by accused at about time of occurrence or in course of occurrence, Court can draw inference that prosecution has suppressed genesis and origin of occurrence and has thus, not presented true version - Held, there had been firing at place of occurrence that has not been established by prosecution witnesses - Though a dead body was found and Panchayatnama of dead body was prepared, but no Panch had been examined - Pellet marks which were found on wall, were not shown in site plan - Accused is entitled to benefit of being acquitted - (B) Whether omissions and contradictions between statement made u/s. 161 and statement made in Court, as brought out in cross-examination, makes witnesses unreliable? - Credibility of a witness has to be adjudged by Appellate Court in drawing inference from proved and admitted facts - Further held, witnesses do not stand test of stricter scrutiny, they being admittedly inimical towards accused persons - In this view of matter, no reliance could have been placed on their testimony and as such conviction of appellant cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
