Addagada Raghavamma and Another v Addagada Chenchamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 337; AIR 1964 SC 136; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 933

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Severance

Summary: Land & Property - In or about year 1895, there was a partition of joint family properties between Veeranna and his four sons - Veeranna's second son Pitchayya had predeceased him leaving his widow Raghavamma - Sometime before his death, Pitchayya took Venkayya, son of his brother Chimpirayya in adoption - Venkayya died leaving behind a son Subbarao - Chimpirayya died having executed a will whereunder he gave his properties in equal shares to Subbarao and his grand-daughter - Thereunder he also directed Raghavamma to take possession of entire property belonging to him, to manage same - Raghavamma allowed Chenchamma, Chimpirayya's daughter-in-law to manage entire property and she accordingly came into possession of entire property after death of Chimpirayya - (A) Whether adoption of Venkayya was true and valid? - Held, circumstances obtaining at that time were as follows : Chimpirayya was about 40 years old; he had only one son, Venkayya, who was aged about 2 years Pitchayya was about 25 years old and, therefore, ordinarily he had every prospect of having children of his own; it is, therefore highly improbable, unless there are special circumstances, that an only son of an elder brother was taken in adoption by his younger brother - No documents of adoption were executed - (B) Whether Pitchayya and Chimpirayya were divided as alleged? - Further held, partition is alleged to have taken place in or about year 1895; but no partition deed was executed to evidence same -This evidence does not contain any admission that there was a partition inter se between four brothers; indeed it only supports case that there was a partition between children of Veeranna by his two wives - (C) Whether there was a division in status between Chimpirayya and other member of joint Hindu family i.e. Subbarao, before Chimpirayya executed will, or at any rate on date when he executed it? - Further held, conflicting descriptions in various documents introduce ambiguity rather than clarity in matter of any such declaration of intention - Be it as it may, Court cannot therefore hold that there is any such clear and unambiguous declaration of intention made by Chimpirayya to divide himself from Venkayya - (D) Whether a member of a joint Hindu family becomes separated from other members of family by a mere declaration of his unequivocal intention to divide from family without bringing same to knowledge of other member of family? - Further held, knowledge of members of family of manifested intention of one of them to separate from them is a necessary condition for bringing about that member's severance from family - It will have to be held that on death of Chimpirayya his interest devolved on Subbarao and, therefore, his will, even if it could be relied upon for ascertaining his intention to separate from family, could not convey his interest in family property, as it has not been established that Subbarao or his guardian had knowledge of contents of said will before Chimpirayya died - Appeal dismissed.
