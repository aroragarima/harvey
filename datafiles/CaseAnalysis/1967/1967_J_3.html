Â Jagdish Pandey v Chancellor University of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 249; AIR 1968 SC 353; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 231; 1968 SLR 252

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Bihar State Universities (University of Bihar, Bhagalpur and Ranchi (Amendment) Act 13 of 1962)

Summary: Education - Constitution - Bihar State Universities (University of Bihar, Bhagalpur and Ranchi) (Amendment),1962, ss. 4 and 48-A - Magadh University Act, 1961, s. 48 A - Appointment, termination of teachers - Affliated colleges - Administrative Law - (A)Whether order passed by Chancellor giving appellant time to appear at an examination to enable him to obtain a second class Master's degree, otherwise his services might be terminated as valid? - Held, order D/- 18-02-1863 passed by Chancellor requiring governing body of College to give appellant time to appear at an examination to enable him to obtain a second class Master's degree, otherwise his services might be terminated, is not valid- (B) Whether appellant must be deemed to have minimum qualification ? - Further held, appellant must be deemed to have minimum qualification of a second class Master's degree by virtue of sub-r. (6) of Statutes and as such he was qualified for appointment as Principal of that College - Appeal allowed.
