Upadhyaya Hargovind Devshanker v Dhirendrasinh Virbhadrasinhji Solanki And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 373; (1988) 2 SCC 1; AIR 1988 SC 915; JT 1988 (1) SC 350; 1988(1) SCALE 342; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 1043; 1988 (1) UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Dispute, Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election - Constitution, 1950, arts. 329(b), 327 and 136 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 80 A, 86, 98, 99 and 116 A - Letters Patent of Gujarat High Court, cl. 15 - Maintainability of appeal - Election petition - Interlocutory order passed by learned single judge - Appeal under Letters Patent of Gujarat HC - (A) Whether division bench of HC has jurisdiction to hear an appeal against interlocutory order passed by single judge who was trying election petition ? - Held, that division bench of HC has no jurisdiction to hear appeal against interlocutory order passed by learned single judge who was trying election petition as jurisdiction exercisable by single judge under Act is a special jurisdiction conferred on HC by virtue of art. 329(b) of Constitution -Conferment of power to try an election petition filed under Act of 1951 does not amount to enlargement of existing jurisdiction of HC - (B) Whether Supreme Court has any jurisdiction to deal with any matter arising out of an election petition filed under Act of, 1971 - Further held, Supreme Court exercising appellate powers u/s. 116 A of Act in respect of orders passed u/ss. 98 or 99 of Act or u/art. 136 of Constitution in respect of other orders can have any jurisdiction to deal with any matter arising out of an election petition filed under Act, 1971 - Appeal allowed.
