Â Rameshwar Prasad and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Agrawal, Ashok Bhan, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 16; (2006) 2 SCC 1; AIR 2006 SC 980; JT 2006 (1) SC 457; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 67; 2006(1) SCALE 385; 2006 (1) SCJ 477; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 562

Case Digest

Subject: Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Broadcasting Act, 1981, House Of The People, Constitution (Fifty-Second Amendment) Act, 1985, Government of India Act, 1858, Ajmer-Merwara (Extension of Laws) Act, 1947, Defence (General) Regulations, 1939, Constitution (Ninety-First Amendment) Act, 2003

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 103(2), 143, 153, 154, 155, 159, 163, 164, 172, 174(1), 174(2)(b), 188, 192(2), 200, 256, 257, 275, 276, 277, 277A, 278, 32, 352, 355, 356, 356(1), 356(3), 361(1), 361(2), 74(2), 75 - Representation Of People Act, 1951, s. 73 (the Act) - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 93 - Constitution (Fifty-Second Amendment) Act, 1985 - Constitution (Ninety-First Amendment) Act, 2003 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 106, s. 123 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988 - Dissolution of Legislative Assembly u/ art.356(1) - Holding of first meeting of assembly as Contemplated by art.172 is not necessary to exercise power of dissolution - Assembly is duly constituted on issuance of notification u/s.73 of the Act - Udai Narain Sinha v. State of U.P. and Ors. (AIR 1987 Allahabad 293) overruled - Object of recommending dissolution should not be promotion of political interest of one or other party - Satisfaction of President - Words 'or otherwise' in art. 356 are of vide amplitude - Appointment of Governor - Criteria suggested by Sarkaria Commission observed almost in total breach - Recommendation with the object to prevent a political party from staking claim to form Govt. is mala fide - Plea that dissolution was to prevent defection has no relevance - Dissolution cannot be resorted to for good governance or cleansing of politics - Immorality is also not a ground recognised by Constitution for formation of satisfaction to dissolve Assembly - Presumption of allurement or horse trading cannot be raised - Scope of Judicial review - Questions whether Governor's report is based on relevant material, whether report is made bona fide and whether facts have been duly verified can be examined by Court - Plea of absence of judicially manageable standards is untenable - Proclamation as to dissolution of Legislative Assembly is not a Legislative Act - It is a constitutional function - Governor although enjoys complete immunity, his actions can be examined on the ground of mala fide - Held, proclamation D/- 23-5-2005 dissolving Bihar Assembly held invalid - Status quo ante refused to be restored since scheduled elections had reached an advanced stage - And it was not proper to put State in another spell of uncertainty having regards to the number of members belonging to different political parties - Petitions disposed of.
