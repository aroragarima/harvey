Pravasi Bhalai Sangathan v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Sikri, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 159; (2014) 11 SCC 477; AIR 2014 SC 1591; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 158; 2014 (104) ALR 200; 2014 (3) AWC 2769; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 809; JT 2014 (4) SC 170; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 363; 2014(3) SCALE 552; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 446

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Protection of Civil Rights Act, 1955, Cable Television Networks Rules, 1994, Election Symbols (Reservation and Allotment) Order, 1968, Information Technology (Intermediaries guidelines) Rules, 2011, Religious Institutions (Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1980

Summary: (1) Constitution of India-Articles 19 (1) (a) and 32-"Hate speeches" delivered by elected representatives, political and religious leaders-Statutory provisions and particularly penal law provide sufficient remedy to curb menace of "hate speeches"-Person aggrieved must resort to remedy provided under particular law-Legislature already provided sufficient and effective remedy for prosecution of author of "hate speeches"-Effective regulation of "hate speeches" at all levels required as author of such speeches can be booked under existing penal and all law enforcing agencies must ensure existing law is not rendered dead letter-Supreme Court not to entertain petition for issuing certain guidelines/directions to curb menace of "hate speeches"-National Human Rights Commission well within its power to initiate suo motu proceedings against alleged authors of hate speech-Law Commission of India requested to examine issues raised, define expression "hate speech" and make recommendations to Parliament to strengthen Election Commission to curb menace of "hate speeches" irrespective of whenever made.

(2) Constitution of India-Article 142-Directions/guidelines by Supreme Court-Direction can be issued by Supreme Court only when there is total vacuum in law-In case of vacuum of legal regime to deal with particular situation court may issue guidelines to provide absolution till such time as Legislature acts to perform its role by enacting proper legislation to cover the field-Supreme Court should not grant relief or pass order/direction which is not capable of implementation.
