Kunwar Arun Kumar v U.P. Hill Electronics Corporation Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1801; (1997) 2 SCC 191; (1997) SCC (L&S) 558; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 546; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 17; 1997 (3) LLJ 791; 1997 (1) LLN 570; 1996(8) SCALE 426; 1997 (1) SLJ 234; 1997 (1) SLR 136; [1996] Supp8 S.C.R. 19; 1997 (1) Supreme 187

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Whether conclusion of High Court that respondents had totally lost confidence in appellant and that he was totally unsuitable for job for and, therefore, he was found not entitled to any enquiry is justified? - Appointing authority has to look into performance of work and duties during period of probation and if they record a finding that during that probation period, work and performance of duties were unsatisfactory, they are entitled to terminate service without conducting any enquiry - That does not amount to any stigma - Respondents have recorded finding that petitioner was regularly absent on one ground or other - Held, thus, no illegality in action taken by respondents - Appeal dismissed.
