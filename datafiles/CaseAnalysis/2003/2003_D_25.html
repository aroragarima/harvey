State of Punjab v Darshan Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 October 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 891; (2004) 1 SCC 328; AIR 2003 SC 4179; 2004 (2) CHN 102; 2003 (99) FLR 1171; 2004 (1) JhrCR(SC) 38; JT 2005 (11) SC 285; 2004 (1) MLJ(SC) 61; 2004 (2) MPLJ 302; 2003(9) SCALE 35; 2003 (1) SCW 5488; 2004 (1) SLR 39; 2003 (6) SLT 582; [2003] Supp4 S.C.R. 1042; 2003 (7) Supreme 474

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - s. 152 - Scope of exercising power under s. 152 - Held, the settled position of law is that after the passing of the judgment, decree or, order, the same becomes final subject to any further avenues of remedies provided in respect of the same and the very Court or the tribunal cannot, on mere change of view, is not entitled to vary the terms of the judgments, decrees and orders earlier passed except by means of review, if statutorily provided specifically therefor and subject to the conditions or limitations provided therein. The provision cannot be invoked to modified alter or add to the terms of the original order or decree so as to, in effect, pass an effective judicial order after the judgment in the case.
