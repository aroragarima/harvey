Mysore State Road Transport Corporation and another v Mirja Khasim Ali Beg and another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 338; (1977) SCC (L&S) 282; (1977) 2 SCC 457; AIR 1977 SC 747; 1977 LabIC 272; 1977 (1) LLJ 262; 1977 (1) LLN 201; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 282; 1977 (1) SLR 237

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, ss. 116(2) and 115(7) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(1) - Civil Procedure Code, O. 11 r. 2 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240(2) - Whether impugned orders of dismissal from service are passed by competent authority? - Held, power can be conferred on an officer other than appointing authority to dismiss a Govt. servant provided he is not subordinate in rank to appointing officer or authority - Word 'subordinate' in art. 311(1) must be properly construed to mean subordination in rank and not subordination of function; otherwise, protection referred to in art. 311 would be illusory - Word 'subordinate' in art. 311(1) of Constitution of India, 1950 means subordinate in rank and not with reference to functions exercised, consequently, when no officer of equal rank to the appointing officer is available then the order of dismissal or removal will have to be passed by an officer of superior rank - Discretion has been wrongly exercised in favour of first respondent and orders of dismissal from service passed is overruled - Appeal dismissed.
