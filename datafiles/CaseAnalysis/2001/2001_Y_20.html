Linder Frank Wolfgang v Yogesh D. Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19832; (2001) 8 SCC 22; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1406; AIR 2001 SC 3895; 2001 (4) CCR 33; 2001 (3) Crimes 385; 2001 CRLJ 4646; 2002 (79) ECC 225; 2001 (133) E.L.T. 10; 2001 (133) E.L.T. 16; 2002 (1) G.L.R. 683; 2001 (2) JCC 158; JT 2001 (7) SC 459; 2002 (1) RCR(Criminal) 294; 2001(6) SCALE 232; 2001 (6) SLT 548; 2001 (6) Supreme 892

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Gold (Control) Act, 1968

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962, s. 135 - Gold (Control) Act, 1968, ss. 85 and 86 - Appellant convicted under Customs Act - Appellant does not challenge conviction but only seeks remission of 7 months as he needs to visit his old mother in Poland who was quite aged and ill - Held, keeping in view that only short period of 4 months are remaining in completion of sentence - Order of sentence is modified to extent of period of imprisonment already undergone by him - Appeal disposed of.
