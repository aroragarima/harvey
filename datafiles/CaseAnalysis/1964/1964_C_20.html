Â H. R. S. Murthy v Collector of Chittoor and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 141; AIR 1965 SC 177; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 125; 1964 (2) SCJ 557; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 666

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Indirect Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Madras District Boards Act 1920 - Mines & Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1948 - (A) Whether meaning of expression 'royalty' in s. 79(1) of Act include royalty payable under a mining lease on ore won by lessee? - Held, where land is held on lease 'royalty' which follows expression 'lease amount' is something other than return to lessor or licenser for, use of land surface and represents as it normally connotes payment made for materials or minerals won from land - (B) Whether provision imposing land cess under mining leases by Act, 1948 or in any event, by Act, 1957 are overlapping ? - Further held, ss. 78 and 79 have nothing to do, are not concerned with development of miness, minerals or their regulation thus there is no connection between regulation, development of mines, minerals dealt with in Central Acts, levy, collection of land-cess for which provision is made by ss. 78 and 79 of Act - Hence, there is nothing in common between Act and Central Acts of 1948 and 1957 - No over-lapping - (C) Whether land cess which, was demanded by impugned notices can be recovered as an arrear of land revenue under law? - Further held, cess u/s. 78 would be 'a cess lawfully imposed upon land' and would therefore be covered by Madra Revenue Recovery Act - Appeals and Petitions dismissed.
