M.C. Mehta v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

02 April 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 312; (2003) 5 SCC 376; AIR 2003 SC 3469; 2004 (1) CalLT(SC) 22; 2003 CRLJ 2045; JT 2003 (3) SC 505; 2003(3) SCALE 575; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 329; 2003 (1) SCW 1975

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Human Rights

Summary: Environment, Wild Life and Animal - Air (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Act, 1981, s.21 - Expert Committee of CPCB categorised Hot Mix Plants as hazardous industry ('Ha' category) - As per Master Plan 2001, all hazardous/noxious industries ordered to be shifted out of the U T of Delhi - Despite order of closing down of polluting unit and allotment of alternative site, respondent continued operating his unit - Unit sealed - Order challenged - Held, it is apparent that the contemner was taking the Court for a ride by raiding one Court or the other deliberately with oblique motive to circumvent the Court's orders thereby salvaging himself by feigning ignorance of the SC's order which was in the knowledge of the contemner - Further, no apology, muchless unconditional apology, was tendered by the respondent In fact, the respondent defended his action and also found fault with the orders passed by this Court - Therefore, the apology so tendered by the contemner is not a product of remorse or contrition - Apology rejected - In air pollution related matter or in any matter relating to environmental hazard, if the orders of the highest court are disobeyed as sought to be done in this case, the health hazard of the entire society is at great risk - Contemner sentenced to imprisonment in addition to costs - Petitions Disposed of.
