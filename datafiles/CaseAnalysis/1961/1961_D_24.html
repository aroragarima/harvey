Collector of Customs, Baroda v Digvijaysinhji Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited, Jamnagar

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 7; AIR 1961 SC 1549; 1962 BLJR 185; 1962 (64) BomLR 154; 1961 CRLJ 720; 1983 ECR 2363; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1337; 1963 (1) SCJ 274; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 896

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Person Concerned, Importation, Adjudged

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 190 and 193 - Whether Chief Customs Authority is 'an officer of Customs' who has adjudged a penalty or rate, as case may be within meaning of Act? - Held, Chief Customs Authority has no power to impose a penalty for first time u/s. 188 of Act - But has power u/s. 190 of Act to commute order of confiscation to a penalty not exceeding value of goods confiscated - Therefore, Chief Customs Authority has jurisdiction to pass order - Order subject to condition that it has obtained consent of party whose goods have been confiscated - Appeal dismissed.
