Shalini v New English High School Association and others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 829; (2013) 16 SCC 526; 2014(1) ALL MR 904; 2014 (3) Bom.C.R. 113; JT 2014 (1) SC 296; 2014 (2) MahLJ 913; 2014 (1) MLJ 731; 2013(15) SCALE 273; [2013] 12 S.C.R. 807; 2014 (4) SLR 1; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 627

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Constitution (Scheduled Tribes) Order, 1950, Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, De-notified Tribes, (Vimukta Jatis), Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Category Regulation of Issuance and Verification of Caste Certificate Act, 2000, Benefit of caste

Summary: Service - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes, De-notified Tribes, (Vimukta Jatis) Nomadic Tribes, Other Backward Classes and Special Backward Category (Regulation of Issuance and Verification of) Caste Certificate Act, 2000, s. 10 - Removal from service - Reinstatement - Benefit of caste - Entitled to - Appellant (employee) was employed as Asst. Teacher against a vacancy earmarked for ST candidate, she filed a caste certificate issued by competent Authority testifying her to belong to 'Halba Schedule Tribe Category' - Caste Scrutiny Committee found appellant was not entitled to claim the benefit ensured to 'Halbas' and by an order held to be invalid - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which granted protection in service on the basis of Govt. Resolution dt.15-6-1995 - However, protracted litigation ensued eventually resulting in filing another petition before HC - Single Judge by its order set aside reinstatement order passed by School Tribunal, which was affirmed by DB in impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant's employment was justifiably terminated because a Caste Scrutiny Committee after a passage of several decades, found her disentitled to claim the benefits ensured to 'Halbas' - Held, there was no falsity in the claim of appellant and thus that she could not be viewed as having filed a 'false' Caste Certificate, the rigours of s. 10 of the 2000 Act would not apply to appellant's case - A perusal of the Order of Scheduled Tribe Caste Certificate Committee, shows that the Committee was satisfied that her claim to the caste of 'Gadwal Koshti' was correct but that she did not belong to 'Halba' Scheduled Tribe - Said Govt. Resolution specifically declared that 'Gadwal Koshti' was basically backward in social, economic and educational viewpoint and was thus 'special backward class' vide Govt. Resolution dt.7-12-1994 - A person who had honestly, in contradistinction with falsely, claimed consanguinity with a certain group which was later on found not to belong to an envisaged ST but to a special backward class be visited with termination of her employment; that was not the intent of law, and certainly was not what Three-Judge Bench was confronted with in Union of India v. Dattatray, 2008 Indlaw SC 628 - Thus, appellant should have been debarred from any further advantage that would ensure to persons belonging to the 'Halba' Tribe - Appellant was reinstated in service but without any back wages - No further promotion as ST candidate unless appellant was otherwise entitled as a special backward class candidate - Appeal disposed of.
