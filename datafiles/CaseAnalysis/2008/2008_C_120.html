Kerala State Electricity Board v Chinamma Antony

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1066; (2008) 11 SCC 476; 2008 (73) ALR 159; 2008 (6) AWC 6422; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 135; 2008(6) SCALE 170; [2008] 10 S.C.R. 1008

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: ELECTRICITY&ENERGY, PRACTICE&PROCEDURE

Summary: Constitution, art. 136 - Maintainability of decision based of decision which set aside by superior Court - Appeal by special leave - Against dismissal of revision petition of State Electricity Board by High Court - Petition against order of enhanced compensation for alleged loss for drawl of electricity line over claimant's property - Relying on a full Bench decision of HC in Kumba Amma v. K.S.E.B. [2002 (1) KLT 542], the HC dismissed the Petition - Board submitted that the decision relied upon was set aside by Supreme Court in another case - No appearance on behalf of the respondent despite notice - SC held, set aside the impugned order of the High Court and remit the matter to it for fresh consideration keeping in view the principles set out in the decisions - Appeal Allowed.
