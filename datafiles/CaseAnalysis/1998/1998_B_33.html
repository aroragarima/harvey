Vasant D. Bhavsar v Bar Council of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1348; (1999) 1 SCC 45; 1999 (37) ALR 115; 1999 (4) AWC 2822; JT 1999 (5) SC 420; 1998(6) SCALE 282; 1999 (5) SLT 303; 1998 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 6; 2000 (Supp) RD 509

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 35 and 36-B - Evidence to support complaint against advocates - Whether appellant is to be charged of contempt without a speaking order by disciplinary Committees of Bar Councils, based on statements of complainant? - Orders passed by Disciplinary Committees of Bar Councils in disciplinary matters should be speaking orders - They must set out reasons for which they are passed - Where orders are based upon evidence, as is usually case with complaints against advocates, there must be some analysis of evidence and conclusion must be based on such analysis - Held, reading of evidence leads to grave doubt about veracity of complainant and benefit of doubt must go to appellant, it is not enough to state conclusions without indicating material on record upon which such conclusions are based - Appeal disposed of.
