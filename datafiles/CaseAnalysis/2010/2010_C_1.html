C. M. C. Limited v Madhya Pradesh Stock Exchange and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 171

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s.34 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.9 rr.4 and 9, s.151 - Dispute - Arbitration - Sustainability - Arbitral award dt. 20-10-2003 was made in favour of the appellant in a claim against respondent No.1 - Respondent No.1 filed an application u/s.34 of Act for setting aside the said award - Said application was dismissed for non-prosecution on 27-6-2006 - Respondent No.1 filed an application under O.9, rr.4 and 9 r/w. s.151 of CPC for setting aside the dismissal - Appellant raised a preliminary objection that the petition was not maintainable - Additional District Judge before whom the said application was made by order dt. 5-8-2008, held that the application for setting aside the dismissal, was maintainable - Appellant challenged the said order by filing a writ petition before HC - HC, by the impugned order dt. 12-12-2008 dismissed the writ petition filed by the appellant holding that the appropriate remedy was by way of revision u/s.115 of CPC - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC rightly dismissed the writ petition filed by the appellant u/s.34 of Act holding that the appropriate remedy was by way of revision u/s.115 of CPC -

Held, SC in one case had held that having regard to the object of the Act, which was to provide expeditious alternative binding dispute resolution process with the minimal court intervention, proceedings u/s.34 of the Act, were intended to be summary proceedings and not full fledged trials in the manner of civil suits under CPC - Fiza Developers & Inter-Trade (P) Ltd. V. AMCI (I) Pvt. Ltd. & Anr. 2009 Indlaw SC 1512, relied on - In view of it, respondent No.1 did not dispute the position that the proceedings u/s.34 of Act would have to be disposed of expeditiously and assured that respondent No.1 would cooperate with the civil court for early disposal of the proceedings u/s.34 of the Act, as summary proceedings - Thus, there was no need to examine the question of law raised in this appeal (relating to maintainability of writ petition) on merits - By consent and in exercise of our power to do complete justice, SC set aside the order dt. 27-6-2006 dismissing the respondent No.1's application u/s.34 of the Act - Appeal disposed of.
