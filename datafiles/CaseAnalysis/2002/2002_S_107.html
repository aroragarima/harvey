Pratibha Singh and another v Shanti Devi Prasad and another

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1458; (2003) 2 SCC 330; AIR 2003 SC 643; 2003 (1) CGLJ 239; JT 2002 (10) SC 302; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 316; 2002(9) SCALE 133; 2002 (7) SLT 125; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 406; 2002 (8) Supreme 553; 2003 (1) UC 299

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC - O. 7 r. 3, O. 20 r. 3, O. 41 r. 6, ss 152 and 47 - Failure on the part of plaintiffs to give correct, specific and exact description of the immovable property forming subject matter of suit, added by omission on the part of the Trial Court to insist on compliance by the draftsman of the plaint with the rules of pleadings - What is the effect of such mistake and decree been awarded by Trial Court - Court exercised power u/art. 142 of Constitution - Held, sub-plots 595/I and 595/II were not capable of being identified merely by boundaries nor by numbers as sub-plot numbers do not appear in records of settlement or survey - Plaintiffs ought to have filed map of the suit property annexed with the plaint - The Trial Court should have, while scrutinizing the plaint, pointed out the omission on the part of the plaintiffs and should have insisted on a map of the immovable property forming subject-matter of the suit being filed - Further held, when the suit as to immovable property has been decreed and the property is not definitely identified, the defect in the court record caused by overlooking of provisions contained in Order 7 Rule 3 and Order 20 Rule 3 of the CPC is capable of being cured - Resort can be had to s 152 or s 47 of the CPC, depending on the facts and circumstances of each case, which of the two provisions would be more appropriate, just and convenient to invoke - sale consideration - held, in the absence of any further and final order having been made, the amount of security demanded by the HC through its interim order should have remained as security liable to be released in favour of the person who had deposited the amount of security - Appeals Disposed Of.
