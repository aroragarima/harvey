Prakash and others v Phulavati and others

Supreme Court of India

16 October 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 894; (2016) 2 SCC 36; AIR 2016 SC 769; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 180; 2016 (114) ALR 240; 2015 (6) ALT(SC) 34; 2016 (1) AWC 331; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 419; 2016 (1) CalLT(SC) 79; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 250; 2015 (6) CTC 576; 2015 (2) ILR(Cut) 1048; JT 2015 (11) SC 173; 2015 (6) KarLJ 177; 2015 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3961; 2016 (1) MahLJ 1; 2015 (8) MLJ 115; 2016 (1) MPLJ 108; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 952; 2016 (130) RD 718; 2016 (2) RLW 1337; 2015(11) SCALE 643; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 579; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 146

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Keywords: Indian Succession Act, 1925, Joint Family, Women, West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, Muslim Women (Protection of Rights on Divorce) Act, 1986, Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005, Punjab Pre-Emption (Repeal) Act, 1973, Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, 1956, Punjab Pre-emption Act, 1930, A.P. Amendment Act, 1986

Summary: Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Succession (Amendment) Act, 2005 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 6 - Retrospective effect of Amendment Act - Determination - Respondent filed suit before Trial Court for partition and separate possession for property in question - Trial Court partly decreed suit in favor of respondent - Respondent filed appeal before HC with grievance that she became coparcener under 2005 Amendment Act, hence, she was entitled to inherit coparcenary property equal to her brothers - HC vide impugned order upheld plea of restrospectivity in favour of respondents by which appellants are aggrieved - Appellant contended that respondent could not claim any share in self acquired property of members of joint family and that claim of respondent had to be dealt with only u/s. 6 of 1956 Act as it stood prior to 2005 Amendment - Further, appellant contended that 2005 Amendment Act was not applicable to claim of daughter i.e. respondent when her father who was coparcener in joint hindu family died prior to 9-9-2005 - Whether 2005 Amendment Act will have retrospective effect.

Held, text of amendment itself clearly provides that right conferred on 'daughter of coparcener' is 'on and from commencement of 2005 Act. SC held that there is neither any express provision for giving retrospective effect to amended provision nor necessary intendment to that effect. Requirement of partition being registered can have no application to statutory notional partition on opening of succession as per unamended provision, having regard to nature of such partition which is by operation of law. SC held that legislature has expressly made Amendment applicable on and from its commencement and only if death of coparcener in question is after Amendment. Hence, no other interpretation is possible in view of express language of statute. SC held that proviso to s. 6(1) and s. 6(5) clearly intend to exclude transactions referred to therein which may have taken place prior to 20-12-2004 on which date Bill was introduced. Explanation cannot permit reopening of partitions which were valid when effected. Object of giving finality to transactions prior to 20-12-2004 is not to make main provision retrospective in any manner. Hence, SC held that rights under amendment are applicable to living daughters of living coparceners as on 9-9-2005 irrespective of when such daughters are born. Disposition or alienation including partitions which may have taken place before 20-12-2004 as per law applicable prior to said date will remain unaffected. Any transaction of partition effected thereafter will be governed by Explanation. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - 2005 Amendment Act will not have retrospective effect as legislature doesnot intend for giving retrospective effect.
