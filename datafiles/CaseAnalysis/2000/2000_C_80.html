Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Cheruvakkara Nafeessu and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2708; (2001) 2 SCC 491; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1289; 2001 (1) ACC 335; AIR 2000 SCW 4535; 2001 (42) ALR 525; 2001 (1) AWC 574; [2001] 104 Comp Cas 89; JT 2001 (1) SC 341; 2001 (1) KLT 498; 2001 (127) PLR 422; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 475; 2000(8) SCALE 412; 2000 (8) SLT 801; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 670; 2000 (8) Supreme 467; 2001 (1) UC 273

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Corporate

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 95(1)(b) and 96 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - (A) What is extent of liability of an insurance company towards third party as per s. 95(1)(b) of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939? - Held, a conjoint reading of all terms of policy of insurance executed in this case indicate that total extent of liability of insurance policy is Rs. 50,000 but Company is liable to indemnify insured against all sums including claimant's costs and expenses which insured becomes liable to pay and nothing in policy affects right of any person indemnified by policy or any other person to recover an amount under or by virtue of provisions of s. 96 of Act - However, insured is liable to repay to Company all sums paid by Company which Company would not have been liable to pay but for condition of liability relating to third party - (B) What are its rights in case of payment of an amount in excess of limits of liability under insurance policy vis-a-vis insured? - Further held, payment of higher premium does not mean that limit of liability with regard to third-party risk became unlimited or higher than statutory liability fixed u/s. 95(2) of Act - In facts and circumstances of this case, Claims Tribunal and High Court were not justified in rejecting right of appellant Company to recover from insured excess amount paid in execution and discharge of award of Tribunal - Appeal allowed.
