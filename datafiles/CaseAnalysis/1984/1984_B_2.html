Karnataka State Road Transport Corporation v B. A. Jayaram And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 426; (1984) Supp SCC 244; AIR 1984 SC 790; 1984(1) SCALE 142; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 768; 1984 UJ 364

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, ss. 57(8) and 68FF - Motor Vehicles - (A) Whether s.57(8) creates a legal fiction by reason of which the grant of an application for variation in the conditions of permit in respect of matter set out in that sub-section results in the grant of a new permit ? - (2) Whether an increase in the number of trips or the number of vehicle above the maximum specified in an existing inter-State stage carriage permit would be inconsistent with the provisions of the said scheme ?-Held, des not result in grant of new permit - Not inconsistent with provisions of scheme - Appeal dismissed.
