Commercial Motors Limited v Commissioner of Trade Tax Lucknow, Uttar Pradesh and others

Supreme Court of India

11 September 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 620; AIR 2015 SC 3763; 2015(9) SCALE 668; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 1036; [2015] 86 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Bengal Sales Tax Rules, 1941, Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) (Third Amendment) Act, 1974

Summary: Indirect Tax - Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, s. 21(1) - Re-assessment - Limitation period - Determination - Appellant is registered dealer under Act and authorised to deal with scooters manufactured by company and during assessment year 1990-1991, he sols scooters and submitted certificates for claiming exemption - Further, AO granted exemption to assessee - However, later revenue held that original assessment is defective, hence, demanded tax on differential amount - Assessee contended that proceedings u/s. 21(2) of Act could not be initiated against it as same was barred by limitation being initiated after lapse of six years from date of end of assessment year i.e. 31-3-1997 - HC held that notice issued by department to assessee was within time and passed order against assessee - Hence, present appeal.

Held, A/Y in question is 1990-1991 or year ending 31-3-1991. Original assessment order is dt. 25-2-1995 and notice for reassessment is dt. 13-3-2002. For purpose of limitation u/s. 21(1) of Act and first proviso, period of limitation is to be counted from end of relevant A/Y. i.e. 31.3.1991. Hence notice dt. 13-3-2002 was beyond six years or even eight years of end of A/Y i.e. 1990-1991. Further, SC held that period of six years would have full effect in respect of fresh assessment or reassessment, where notice is issued or after date proviso came into force. Further, SC held that while reducing period from eight years to six years, time has been specified to complete assessment or reassessment by 31-3-2002. SC held that legislative intent was not to do away and erase limitation period, but date 31-3-2002 was incorporated only to protect cases which could be earlier governed by limitation period of eight years. Hence, 2001 amendment is not fully retrospective, but it is partly retrospective. It reduces limitation period from eight years to six years and simultaneously protects and safeguards interest of revenue in respect of cases within eight years and six years provided reassessments are completed by 31-3-2002. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Initiation of re-assessment proceeding is set aside being barred by limitation. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Re-assessment proceedings cannot be initiated by authority beyond limitation period.
