State Of Bihar And Others v Nirmal Kumar Gupta

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 332; (2013) 2 SCC 565; JT 2013 (1) SC 485; [2013] 1 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Bihar Excise (Settlement of Licences for retail sale of country/spiced country liquor) Rules, 2004

Summary: Excise - Administrative - Bihar Excise (Settlement of Licenses for retail sale of country/spiced country liquor) Rules, 2004, r. 24 - Group excise shops - Issue of license - Settlement - Validity - Collector issued sale notification dt. 17-5-2006 for settlement of various excise shops in groups in district - Further, group 'ka' excise shops were settled in favour of respondent at a monthly license fee of Rs. 8,29,600/- Commissioner approved grant of license in respect of group 'ka' shops - However, there was breach of conditions of license, a demand notice was issued to respondent - Respondent challenged demand notice before Commissioner, who rejected application - Aggrieved, respondent filed writ petition before HC challenging impugned order which was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether DB had correctly interpreted effect and impact of the Rules and sale notification published by Collector for various excise shops in groups in said district for year 2006-07 and terms of license - Held, respondent had availed benefit of license being fully aware of the Rules, notification and terms incorporated in license - Further, the Rules provide that respondent had to pay from date of settlement - Thus, there could not be any trace of doubt that respondent had to be made liable to pay license fee from date of settlement - There could not have been condonation of default - Such a concept was alien to present nature of trade and a licensee could not claim any benefit under same as whole thing was governed by command of the Rules - However, auction-purchaser was liable to pay from date of issuance of license but not from date of settlement as that runs counter to plain language of r. 24 of the Rules - Hence, liability accrued from date of settlement - Respondent accepted license knowing fully well terms and conditions of license and that he had to pay license fee from date of settlement - Therefore, order passed by Commissioner was just and proper and there was no warrant on part of HC to interfere with same and same was set aside - Appeal allowed.
