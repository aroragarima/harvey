Ram Vishal (Dead) By Lrs. and others v Jagannath and another

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 2398; (2004) 9 SCC 302

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rewa State Land Revenue and Tenancy Code, 1935, Suit for declaration, Gift deed, Mitakshara school of Hindu law

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Practice & Procedure - Hindu Women's Right to Property Act, 1937, ss. 3(1), (2), 4 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 14 - Mitakshara school of Hindu law - Suit for declaration - Gift deed - Validity - Appellants (plaintiffs) filed suit for declaration that gift deed in respect of alleged ancestral properties of appellants, executed by donor of the respondents (defendants) was null and void and not binding upon appellants - Trial Court decreed the suit - Aggrieved respondents filed an appeal before Appellate Court, which was allowed holding that donor of respondents had acquired a right in suit properties under the 1937 Act - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the donor of respondent had acquired any right over the property which would fructify into a full ownership by virtue of s. 14 of 1956 Act - Held, parties were governed by the Mitakshara school of Hindu law - S. 4 of 1937 Act clearly laid down that provision of that Act would not apply to the property of any Hindu dying before the commencement of that Act - The donor (widow of 'T') of respondents could only claim a right in the property of her father-in-law, 'S' (who died in 1952), provided that Act gave her any rights - Under Mitakshara school of Hindu law, if a Hindu dies intestate leaving joint family property then s. 3(2) of 1937 Act would apply - U/s. 3(2) of 1937 Act, it was only the widow of that person who gets the same interest as that person would have had - Donor of respondent was not the widow of 'S' and could make no claim u/s. 3(2) of 1937 Act - As the property was joint family property she could make no claim u/s. 3(1) of 1937 Act also - First Appellate Court was wrong in concluding that donor of respondent acquired rights under 1937 Act - HC dismissed appeal on the footing that even if 1937 Act did not apply the donor of respondent got a right of maintenance under the Rewa Code - Both sides fairly point out that Rewa Code did not confer any right of maintenance on a female in joint family property - HC was not right in concluding that the donor of respondent got a right of maintenance under the Rewa Code - A mere right of maintenance without actual acquisition in any manner was not sufficient to attract s. 14 of 1956 Act - The donor of respondent had no right at all which could fructify into full ownership under 1956 Act - HC and First Appellate Court were wrong in reversing the judgment of Trial Court - Judgment of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
