Deepak Khinchi v State Of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 133; (2012) 5 SCC 284; 2012 (3) JCC 1821; JT 2012 (4) SC 453; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 878; 2012(5) SCALE 4; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 568

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 285, 286, 323, 324, 304 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Sanction to prosecute - Competent Authority - Delay - Sustainability - Chargesheet against appellant was filed u/ss. 285, 286, 323, 324 and 304 of IPC and u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908 Act without obtaining sanction from competent authority as required u/s. 7 of 1908 Act -Prosecution filed application before Sessions Judge along with sanction letter issued by District Magistrate for sanction to prosecute appellant u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908 Act - Sessions Judge rejected application on ground that sanction to prosecute appellant u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 had been granted by District Magistrate, however, it was not u/s. 7 of 1908 Act - Prosecution again submitted application u/s. 311 of CrPC before Sessions Judge, Sessions Judge allowed application - Appellant challenged order before HC, HC upheld order of Sessions Judge - Aggrieved instant appeal - Appellant contended that since prosecution had deliberately delayed obtaining sanction, it should not be now allowed to fill in lacuna, such a course would result in abuse of process of court - Whether HC was justified in upholding order of Sessions Judge granting permission to prosecute appellant u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908,- Act - Held, at no stage, sanction was refused by competent authority, it was not case of appellant that sanction was granted by authority, which was not competent - It was true that proceedings were sought to be initiated under 1908 Act against appellant after three years, but, in facts of instant case, where 14 innocent persons lost their lives and several persons were severely injured due to blast which took place in appellant's shop, three years period could not be termed as delay - It was also duty of court to see that perpetrators of crime were tried and convicted if offences were proved against them - Lapse of three years had not caused prejudice to accused - It was true that Sessions Judge had, discharged appellant of charges u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908, Act because there was no sanction, but, prosecution had now obtained sanction, Sessions Judge had accepted sanction and had directed that trial should be started against appellant for offences u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908, Act, as well - Order of Sessions Judge was affirmed by HC - In view of legal position and facts of case, no reason was found to interfere in matter and direction issued to Trial Court to frame additional charges against appellant u/ss. 3, 4, 5 and 6 of 1908 Act and to proceed with trial - Appeal disposed of.
