Bakhshish Singh v State of Punjab and another

Supreme Court of India

06 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 493; (2013) 12 SCC 187; AIR 2013 SC 3403; 2013(10) SCALE 54

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 160, 161, 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellant along with other accused person allegedly murdered deceased - A case was registered against appellant u/ss. 302, 120B of IPC - Trial Court found petitioner guilty of offence and passed conviction order - Appellant filed appeal before HC - HC affirmed conviction of appellant as held Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, SC perused record of proceedings of election - Election proceedings show appellant had objected to said proceedings on ground that it was illegal - SC was not inclined to go into correctness of his objections - Suffice it to say that appellant was opposed to deceased - To get Chairmanship of Trust or in any case to remove deceased was sufficiently strong motive to have opponent eliminated particularly when seen in surrounding circumstances - It appeared that relationship between deceased and PW 1 on one hand and appellant and his wife on other hand was completely hostile - PW 1 had stated that appellant and his wife used to give threats to her and deceased in meetings for Chairmanship of said Trust - Appellant and his wife had given threats to rape her daughter, who was studying in same institute and that they could achieve it as their relatives were hardened criminals - As regards, her statement that she had proposed her daughter to migrate to some other institute and when she was confronted with absence of such statement in her examination-in-chief, she clarified that it was implied that after taking her from said institute she was to be admitted to some other institute - SC found that cross examination was not sufficient to dislodge testimony of PW 1 that appellant had an animosity of high degree against her husband and herself which became motive for murder in question - This inference was fortified by fact that other accused persons did not appear to have any personal motive or grudge for murdering deceased - SC found no reason to interfere with findings of HC that motive of appellant was established to eliminate deceased from declarations that he would rape daughter of deceased, who was studying in institute and that he would gain control of Institute in which huge finances were involved - It was not possible to determine exact cause of motive, or exact factor which impelled motive - When high degree of animosity was established existence of motive might be taken to be established - On reading evidence of PW1 and other prosecution witnesses SC found same to be convincing, reliable and trustworthy - SC found no reason to disbelieve aforesaid statements - Appellant was directed to surrender before Trial Court to undergo remaining period of sentence - If appellant does not surrender, Trial Court was directed to take appropriate action - Order accordingly.
