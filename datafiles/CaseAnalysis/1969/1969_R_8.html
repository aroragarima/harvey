Union of India and Others v R. S. Dhaba

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 301; (1969) 3 SCC 603; AIR 1969 SC 21; [1971] 79 ITR 143; 1969 SLR 442; 1969 UJ 376

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Promotion - Reversion - Maintainability - Respondent was confirmed by income-tax dept. as upper division clerk on 1-12-1949 - Respondent was promoted as inspector of income tax in an officiating capacity by order of Commissioner of income-tax on 25-10-1951 - Respondent was reverted from his officiating position - Respondent moved HC for grant of writ to quash order of reversion which was allowed - Appellant preferred appeal on said order before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Held, SC relied on I. N. Saksena v. State of Madhya Pradesh 1976 Indlaw SC 572 it was stated that HC was in error in holding that reversion of respondent from position of officiating Income-tax Officer, class II, to a lower position as inspector of income-tax was tantamount to a reduction in rank and that respondent was entitled to safeguards provided in art. 311 of Constitution - Appeal allowed.
