Vidur Impex and Traders Private Limited and another v Tosh Apartments Pvt. Ltd. And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 235; (2012) 8 SCC 384; AIR 2012 SC 2925; 2012 (94) ALR 718; 2012 (4) CLT(SC) 228; 2012 (4) CompLJ 433; 2013 (1) MLJ 267; 2012 (117) RD 568; 2013 (4) RLW 3005; 2012(7) SCALE 448; [2012] 10 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Purchase of suit property - In violation of order of injunction - Sustainability - Appellants along with five other companies purchased suit property in violation of order of injunction - After the filing of suit against such purchase, DB approved the rejection of the appellants' prayer for impleadment as parties by observing that after executing the agreement for sale in favour of X Developers they did not have any subsisting interest in the property - DB also agreed with Single Judge that the application filed by the appellants lacked bona fides because they purchased the suit property from respondent No. 2 despite the order of injunction passed by HC and there was no tangible explanation for filing the application after long time gap of about 8 years - (A) Whether Single Judge and the DB of the HC committed error by dismissing the appellants' application for impleadment as parties to suit - Held, respondent No. 1 had filed suit for specific performance of agreement executed by respondent No. 1 - Appellants and X Developers were total strangers to that agreement - They came into the picture only when respondent No. 2 entered into clandestine transaction with the appellants for sale of the suit property and executed the agreements for sale, which were followed by registered sale deeds and the appellants executed agreement for sale in favour of X Developers - These transactions were in clear violation of the order of injunction passed by HC which had restrained respondent No. 2 from alienating the suit property or creating third party interest - To put it differently, the agreements for sale and the sale deeds executed by respondent No. 2 in favour of the appellants did not have any legal sanctity - Status of the agreement for sale executed by the appellants in favour of X Developers was no different - These transactions did not confer any right upon the appellants or X Developers - Therefore, their presence was not at all necessary for adjudication of the question whether respondent Nos. 1 and 2 had entered into binding agreement and whether respondent No. 1 was entitled to decree of specific performance of the said agreement - Although, the appellants had pleaded that at the time of execution of the agreements for sale by respondent No. 2 in their favour, they did not know about the suit filed by respondent No. 1, it was difficult, if not impossible, to accept their statement because the smallness of time gap between the agreements for sale and the sale deeds executed by respondent No. 2 in favour of the appellants and the execution of agreement for sale by the appellants in favour of X Developers would make any person of ordinary prudence to believe that respondent No. 2, appellants and X Developers had entered into these transactions with the sole object of frustrating agreement for sale executed in favour of respondent No. 1 and the suit pending before HC - In any case, the appellants would be deemed to have become aware of the same on receipt of summons for the suit filed by respondent No. 2 for annulment of the agreements for sale and the sale deeds in which respondent No. 2 had clearly made mention of Suit filed by respondent No. 1 for specific performance of agreement for sale and injunction or at least when Single Judge of HC entertained appeal filed by respondent No. 1 and restrained respondent Nos. 2 and 4 from transferring possession of the suit property to the appellants - However, in the application for impleadment filed by them, the appellants did not offer any tangible explanation as to why the application for impleadment was filed only after 7 years of the passing of injunction order and, that constituted valid ground for declining their prayer for impleadment as parties to Suit - Therefore, their presence was neither required to decide the controversy involved in the suit filed by respondent No. 1 nor required to pass effective decree - (B) Whether the Delhi High Court was justified in appointing the receiver and directing him to take possession of the property - Held, though counsel appearing for X Developers had sought to invoke the doctrine of comity of jurisdictions of the Courts for continuance of the receiver appointed by another HC, HC did not find any merit in his submission - It was not in dispute that respondent No. 1 had filed the suit for specific performance and Single Judge had passed the order of injunction - Arbitral award for specific performance of the agreement for sale of the same property entered into between the appellants and X Developers was obtained on 7-1-1999 - Execution proceedings were instituted in another HC in 2000 and the order for appointment of receiver was passed on 12-8-2000 - It is thus clear that when X Developers approached another HC, HC was already seized with the suit involving the subject matter of the award - Contention of the appellants and X Developers that they were unaware of the proceedings before HC could not be accepted because in Suit filed by respondent No. 2 for declaring that the agreements for sale and the sale deeds relied upon by the appellants were false and fabricated, specific reference was made to the suit filed by respondent No. 1 - That apart, in its order dt. 15-2-2001 passed in the application filed by respondent No. 4, Single Judge of another HC categorically observed that the said Court had not been apprised of the facts relating to the suit pending before HC and the injunction orders passed therein including order dt. 8-2-2001 restraining the receiver of another HC from taking possession of the property and that if these facts had been disclosed, the Court would have been slow in passing the order that it had passed earlier and hence the order passed by it, if it was in conflict with the order passed by HC, would be subject to that order and X Developers who was party to the proceedings before HC could approach the said Court for obtaining appropriate orders - That showed that on being apprised of the correct facts, Single Judge of another HC had shown due respect to the orders passed by HC and directed that the same should operate till they were modified or vacated at the instance of the appellants or X Developers - Course of action adopted by another HC was in consonance with the notion of judicial propriety - Therefore, X Developers could not invoke the doctrine of comity of jurisdictions of the Courts for seeking continuance of the receiver appointed by another HC - Single Judge and the DB of HC had assigned detailed and cogent reasons for appointing receiver to take care of the suit property - Clandestine nature of the transactions entered into between respondent No. 2 and the appellants on the one hand and the appellants and X Developers on the other would give rise to strong presumption that if receiver was not appointed, further attempts would be made to alienate the property in similar fashion - Therefore, HC did not find any valid ground much less justification to interfere with the impugned order or the one passed by Single Judge of HC - Appeal dismissed.
