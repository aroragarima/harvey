State of Bihar and another v Arvind Kumar and another

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 376; (2012) 12 SCC 395; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 8; 2012 CRLJ 3756; 2012 (3) JCC 2007; 2012 (4) RCR(Criminal) 209; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Public Distribution System (Control) Order, 2001

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss.451, 457 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955,ss. 10,3,6A,6C,7 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 421,424 - Illegal Storage of Food Grains - Public Distribution Scheme - Release of Confiscated Goods - Sustainability - Appellants seized huge quantity of wheat from the premises of respondents under Act for illegal storage of subsidized food grains of Public Distribution Scheme, bags had seal of FCI and case u/ss. 7,10 of Act and ss. 421,424 of IPC was registered - None from company where raid were conducted claimed seized material, but respondent filed writ petition for quashing confiscated proceedings and for release of goods - HC, allowed petition of respondents, who filed application before CJM for releasing where in pursuance of order of HC - CJM, dismissed application on ground that respondents could not produce any document to show their ownership to seized material - Aggrieved respondents filed appeal before HC, who allowed appeal - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether CJM was justified in dismissing application for release of goods seized-confiscated - Held, HC, had only taken note of fact that respondents were prepared to furnish adequate/sufficient security to satisfaction of court for release of wheat, CJM came to conclusion after appreciating evidence on record that respondents-applicants were not in a position to show any document which may show their ownership to wheat, there was no justification for the HC to issue directions for release of such material merely because applicant could furnish security - Any stranger or third party may give sufficient security and get seized goods release in his favour, such a course was not permissible even while deciding application u/ss. 451,457 of CrPC - SC, set aside judgments and orders and remanded case back to HC to consider afresh after examining all factual and legal issues involved in case - Appeal disposed of.
