Mahipal Singh v Central Bureau of Investigation, New Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 680; (2014) 11 SCC 282; AIR 2014 SC 2660; 2014 CRLJ 3818; JT 2014 (9) SC 444; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 244; [2014] 4 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Organised crime, Ingredients constituting the offence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B r/w ss. 420, 467, 471, 511 - Maharashtra Control of Organised Crime Act, 1999, ss. 2(1)(e), 3 - Organised crime - Ingredients constituting the offence - Legality - Respondent (CBI) charge sheeted appellant (accused) for allegedly offence u/s. 120B r/w ss. 420, 467, 471, 511 of IPC and u/s. 3 of the Act, related to rigging of results of various entrance examinations for admission to postgraduate courses in medical colleges conducted by All India Institute of Medical Sciences (AIIMS) - CBI secured appellant's remand under the act from Designated Court, which was challenged in writ petitions - However, HC upheld order of DIG, CBI invoking s. 3 of the Act and dismissed both writ petitions filed against the orders of remand for offence under the Act - Hence, instant SLPs - Appellant contended that case should not be governed by the Act and that competent court taking cognizance of offence was not satisfied.

Held, to bring an accused within the mischief of penal provision, ingredients of the offence have to be satisfied on the date the offence was committed. Art. 20(1) of Constitution permits conviction of a person for an offence for violation of law in force at the time of commission of the act charged as an offence. In instant case, examinations alleged to have been rigged had taken place in January, 2010, June, 2010, November, 2010 and January, 2011 and the date on which the first information reports were registered, more than one charge-sheets were not filed against the accused for the offence of specified nature within the preceding period of ten years and further, the Court had not taken cognizance in such number of cases. On the date of commission of the offence, all the ingredients to bring the act within s. 3 of the Act have not been satisfied. There may be a case in which the investigating agency does not know exactly the date on which the crime was committed;, in such a case the date on which the offence comes to the notice of the investigating agency, the ingredients constituting the offence have to be satisfied. An act which is not an offence on the date of its commission or the date on which it came to be known, cannot be treated as an offence because of certain events taking place later on. Submission of charge-sheets in more than one case and taking cognizance in such number of cases are ingredients of the offence and have to be satisfied on the date the crime was committed or came to be known. On the date the offence was committed or came to be known, one of the ingredients of the offence, i.e. submission of charge-sheet and cognizance of offence of specified nature in more than one case within the preceding period of ten years, has not been satisfied. Therefore, the appellant cannot be prosecuted for the offence u/s. 3 of the Act. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Procedural requirement for prosecution of a person for an offence can later on be satisfied but ingredients constituting the offence must exist on the date the crime is committed or detected.
