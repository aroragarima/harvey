Kushro S. Gandhi & Ors. v N.A. Gajdar & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

27 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 350; (1969) 1 SCC 358; AIR 1970 SC 1468; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 959

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 24 and 115 - Four plaintiffs filed a suit for damages against the six defendants - Allegations that defendants conspired among themselves to injure and harass the plaintiffs - Revision to High Court against interlocutory order of Trial Court - (A) Whether HC in revision try other issues arising in case, if the parties conceded? - Held, No - Action of HC cannot be justified u/s. 24 - HC has not purported to withdraw a suit and try same - Even if parties concede, HC cannot in revision try other issues arising in case in a revision against an interlocutory order - (B) Tort - liability of tortfeasor's - Where in a suit for damages for defamation against several defendants, one of defendants tenders an apology which is accepted by plaintiff and embodied in a decree, it cannot be treated to be a full satisfaction for tort alleged to have been committed by defendants - Held, plaintiff must have received full satisfaction or which law must consider as such from a tortfeasor before other joint tortfeasor's can rely on accord and satisfaction - This rule would recognize that liability of tortfeasor's is joint and several - What is full satisfaction will depend on facts and circumstances of case - Appeal allowed.
