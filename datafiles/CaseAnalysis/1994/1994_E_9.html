Sahni Silk Mills Private Limited and Another v Employees' State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

14 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, Kuldip Singh, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 852; (1994) 5 SCC 346; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1096; AIR 1994 SCW 3832; 1994 (2) CLR 632; 1994 (55) DLT 556; 1994 (69) FLR 685; JT 1994 (5) SC 11; 1994 (2) LLJ 1105; 1994 (2) LLN 1060; 1994(3) SCALE 324; 1995 (1) SCJ 36; 1995 (2) SLR 81; [1994] Supp1 S.C.R. 626; 1994 (2) UJ 393; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1974

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Summary: Administrative - Insurance - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 85-B, 94-A - Issue - Whether the power to recover the damages u/s. 85-B could have been validly delegated u/s. 94-A aforesaid to the Regional Directors? - s. 94-A does not specifically provide that any officer or authority subordinate to the Corporation to whom the power has been delegated by the Corporation, may in his turn authorise any other officer to exercise or perform that power or function - Parliament while introducing s. 94-A, only conceived direct delegation by the Corporation to different officers or authorities, subordinate to the Corporation, and there is no scope for such delegate to sub-delegate that power, by authorising any other officer to exercise or perform the power so delegated - Appeals allowed conditionlly.
