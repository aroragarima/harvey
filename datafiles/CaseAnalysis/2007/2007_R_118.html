Food Corporation Of India And Others v Ramesh Kumar

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 810; (2007) 8 SCC 141; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 801; AIR 2007 SC 2864; 2007 (4) AWC 3725; JT 2007 (10) SC 185; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2795; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2881; 2007 (3) LLJ 833; 2007 (4) LLN 558; 2007(10) SCALE 4; 2007 (6) SCJ 231; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 940; 2007 (5) SLR 565; 2008 (1) UPLBEC 147

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Voluntary retirement - Respondent an employee of the Food Corporation of India - Respondent has withdrawn his application - Despite withdrawal, his offer for voluntary retirement was accepted - High Court quashed the order - When the applicant has made the application for withdrawal before it could be accepted, can the Food Corporation of India still enforce the offer of voluntary retirement upon the incumbent? - Held, if the incumbent revokes his offer before the Corporation accepts it then in that case, the revocation of the offer is complete and the Corporation cannot act upon that offer - Management has to take a decision within three months - Once the revocation is made by the incumbent before three months then in that case the Corporation cannot act upon the offer of voluntary retirement unless it is accepted prior to its withdrawal - Appeals dismissed.
