Rajeshwari v Puran Indoria

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 496; (2005) 7 SCC 60; 2005 (61) ALR 145; 2005 (4) AWC 3869C; JT 2005 (7) SC 630; 2005 (4) MLJ(SC) 157; 2005 (4) RCR(Civil) 36; 2005 (99) RD 621; 2005(6) SCALE 762; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 1016

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 41, r. 31, ss. 96,100, 115, - Specific Relief Act, ss. 10, 16, 20 - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - Specific performance of an agreement for sale - Suit decreed - Appeal dismissed by High Court holding that no substantial question of law involved - Appealed herein - Held, HC not justified in dismissing the second appeal by a non- speaking order - It is necessary for HC to consider whether a substantial question of law is involved or not and to give its reasons for coming to its conclusion either way, and if it finds that a substantial question of law or substantial questions of law is or are involved, to frame that question or those questions and to answer it or them in accordance with law - Remanded second appeal for a proper decision by HC - Appeal allowed.
