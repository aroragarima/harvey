Indian Hume Pipe Company Limited v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 237; AIR 1968 SC 1002; 1968 (17) FLR 145; 1968 LabIC 1229; 1969 (1) LLJ 242; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 130

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - (A) Whether closure of factory at Barakar is bona fide and in circumstances justified? To what relief, if any, are workmen entitled? - It was not open to tribunal to go into question as to motive of appellant in closing down its factory at Barakar and to enquire whether it was bona fide, or malafide with some oblique purpose, namely to punish workmen for union activities in fighting appellant - Relied on Pipraich Sugar Mills Ltd v P S M Mazdoor Union (1956 Indlaw SC 101 at 239) - Held, once Tribunal finds that an employer has closed its factory as a matter of fact it is not concerned to go into question as to motive which guided him and to come to a conclusion that because of previous history of dispute between employer and employees closure was not justified Such a closure cannot give rise to an industrial dispute - (B) Whether retrenchment of specified workmen is justified ? To what relief, if any, are they entitled ? - It is not disputed that no such plea was taken in written statement of union and with all respect to Tribunal, it was not competent to go into that question at all - Held, Tribunal could not examine question of retrenchment of 12 workmen and give them relief it sought to do - Appeal allowed.
