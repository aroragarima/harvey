Mamleshwar Prasad and Another v Kanhaiya Lal (Dead) Through L.Rs.

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 415; (1975) 2 SCC 232; AIR 1975 SC 907; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 834; 1975 UJ 293

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Precedents - Applicability of Principle of judgment per incuriam - A prior decision of the Court on identical facts and law binds the Court on the same points in a later case - Held, rest of the appeals must fail in limine and no principle of judgment per incuriam could salvage the case - Appeals dismissed.
