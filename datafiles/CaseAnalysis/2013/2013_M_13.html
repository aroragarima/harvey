M/S Bagai Construction Thr. Its Proprietor Mr. Lalit Bagai v M/S Gupta Building Material Store

Supreme Court of India

22 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, J. S. KHEHAR

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 113; (2013) 14 SCC 1; AIR 2013 SC 1849; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 3; 2013 (97) ALR 728; 2013 (3) AWC 2358; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 240; JT 2013 (3) SC 192; 2013 (3) MLJ 185; 2013 (170) PLR 259; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 304; 2013(2) SCALE 769; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 116; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 27

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil procedure, 1908, O. 8 r. 17, O. 7 r. 14, s. 151 - Suit for recovery - Evidence not produced - Sustainability - Respondent /plaintiff filed a suit for recovery against appellant - Meantime, respondent filed two applications, one for placing on record certain documents and other for seeking permission to recall PW1 for proving certain documents by leading his additional evidence - Trial Court dismissed both applications - Aggrieved, respondent filed revision petition before HC - Single Judge allowed revision and set aside order of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether respondent had made out a case for allowing applications one filed under O. 8 r. 17 r/w s. 151 CPC and another application under O. 7 r. 14 r/w s. 151 CPC - Held, plaintiff filed both applications after more than sufficient opportunity had been granted to it to prove its case - During entire trial, those documents have remained in exclusive possession of plaintiff, still plaintiff had not placed those bills on record - Further, final arguments were heard on number of times and judgment was reserved and only thereafter, in order to improve its case, plaintiff came forward with such an application to avoid final judgment against it - Such course was not permissible even with aid of s. 151 CPC - Hence, plaintiff had filed those two applications before Trial Court in order to overcome lacunae in plaint, pleadings and evidence - Thus, impugned order of HC was set aside and order of Trial Court was restored - Appeal allowed.
