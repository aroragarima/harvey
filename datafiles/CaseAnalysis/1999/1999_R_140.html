State Bank of India and Another v Rattan Singh

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1628; (2000) 10 SCC 396; 2000 (87) FLR 581; 2000 (2) LLJ 1472

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Respondent suspended on account of certain acts of gross misconduct on his part - Petition filed by respondent against order of suspension as no departmental enquiry had been initiated and he had been kept under suspension - Petition was dismissed by Learned Single Judge - Whether Division Bench of High Court was justified in setting aside order of suspension and allowing petition of respondent? - When a bank employee is being prosecuted, bank has power to suspend employee under Bipartite Settlement cls. 19. 3(a) and 1912(b) - Cl. 19.3(a) is similar to paragraph 521(2)(a) - Held, Division Bench of HC was not right in setting aside order of suspension - Judgment and order of Division Bench of HC set aside and petition dismissed - Appeal allowed.
