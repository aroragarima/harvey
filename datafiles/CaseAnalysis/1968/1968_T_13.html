Â Dhulabhai and Others v State of Madhya Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 137; AIR 1969 SC 78; 1969 JabLJ 1; 1969 MahLJ 1; 1969 MPLJ 1; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 662; 1968 (22) STC 416

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Collected, Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act, 1950, ss. 5 and 17 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 301 - Appellants, dealers of tobacco, get their tobacco locally or import it from extra-state places - The State Government u/s. 5 of the Act issued notifications dt. 30.04.1950, 22.05.1950, 24.10.1953 and 21.01.1954 imposing sales tax on importers of tobacco and collected the same from the appellants - Appellants filed suits for refund of the said tax on the ground that SC in State of Madhya Pradesh vs. Bhailal Bhai and Others 1964 INDLAW SC 96, held that notifications dt. 30.04.1950, 22.05.1950, 24.10.1953 and 21.01.1954 was ultra vires of art. 301 of Constitution as an equal tax was not levied on similar goods produced within State - Following said decision, the trial court decreed the suits filed by the appellants - State filed appeal against said order contending that suits filed by the appellants were not maintainable in view of s. 17 of the Act which provides that no assessment made under the Act shall be called in question in any court - HC held that the suits filed by the appellants were not maintainable - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether suits filed by the appellants were not maintainable in view of s. 17 of the Act? - Held, an exclusion of the jurisdiction of civil court was not readily to be inferred unless the following conditions apply (i) where the statute gives a finality to the orders of the special tribunals the Civil Court's jurisdiction must be held to be excluded if there was adequate remedy to do what the Civil Courts would normally do in a suit (ii) where there was an express bar of the jurisdiction of the court, an examination of the scheme of the particular Act to find the adequacy or the sufficiency of the remedies provided may be relevant but was not decisive to sustain the jurisdiction of the civil court (iii) challenge to the provisions of the particular Act as ultra vires cannot be brought before Tribunals constituted under that Act - Even the HC cannot go into that question on a revision or reference from the decision of the Tribunals (iv) when a provision was already declared unconstitutional or the constitutionality of any provision was to be challenged, a suit was open (v) where the particular Act contains no machinery for refund of tax collected in excess of constitutional limits or illegally collected a suit lies (vi) questions of the correctness of the assessment apart from its constitutionality were for the decision of the authorities and a civil suit does not lie if the orders of the authorities were declared to be final or there was an express prohibition in the particular Act - In the instant case, though the Madhya Bharat Sales Tax Act contains provisions for appeal, revision, rectification and reference to the HC, the notifications having been rightly declared void in the earlier decision of the SC, the appellants could take advantage of the fact that tax was levied without a complete charging section - Appeal allowed.
