K. Narendra v Riviera Apartments Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1540; (1999) 5 SCC 77; AIR 1999 SC 2309; AIR 1999 SCW 2378; 1999 (5) ALD(SC) 14; 1999 (80) DLT 435; JT 1999 (4) SC 428; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 777

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 20 and 18 - Decree for specific performance - Variation - No time limit for performance of agreement - Showing intention of parties to perform contract within period of about 21/2 years to 31/2 years - Failure of purchasers to perform their obligations within reasonable time - Astronomical rise in value of land meanwhile - Part of property rendered inalienable subsequently having been acquired by State and some part found to be inalienable under Urban Land Ceiling Regulation Act - Held, discretion ought not to be exercised when part of property was rendered inalienable having been acquired by State and part found to be in excess of ceiling limit - Plea that they agree to execute sale deed of remaining land without corresponding reduction of price and be permitted accordingly - Cannot be allowed - Case is not covered by any of situations contemplated by s. 18.
