A. P. SRTC v P. Thirupal Reddy

Supreme Court of India

14 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1499; (2005) 12 SCC 189

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Enhancement of compensation - Scooterist and pillion rider/respondent met with accident where vehicle belonged to appellant/State transport Corporation was involved -Tribunal estimated disability caused by injury to pillion rider at 15 per cent and awarded sum of Rs 34,552 as compensation - On appeal, HC placing reliance on disability certificate enhanced quantum of compensation and awarded sum of Rs. 1, 53,867 - Hence instant appeal.

Held, out of seven injuries caused in accident to pillion rider, only one injury was the grievous which would not cause any permanent disability if properly treated. There was no justification for HC to rely on disability certificate and to enhance compensation by treating injury as permanent disability. HC committed gross error in overlooking fact that medical certificate was rejected by Tribunal for non-examination of that doctor. Tribunal had determined physical disability at 15 per cent which was just and fair compensation. HC erred in disturbing same and enhancing compensation. Set aside impugned order and restored award of Tribunal. Appeal allowed.
