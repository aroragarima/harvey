Laxman Prasad v Prodigy Electronics Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1270; (2008) 1 SCC 618; AIR 2008 SC 685; 2008 (70) ALR 339; 2008 (1) AWC 644; 2008 (1) CalLT(SC) 58; JT 2007 (13) SC 439; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1055; 2008 (37) PTC 209; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 574; 2007(14) SCALE 65; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rendition Of Account, Foreign Country, Place Of Suing, Return Of Plaint, Compensation For Wrongs To Persons Or Movables

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 7 rr. 10, 11 - Suit for permanent and mandatory injunction against the defendant as also for damages by ordering rendition of accounts and an application under O. 39 rr. 1, 2 r/w s. 151 for interim injunction restraining the defendant from using the name 'Prodigy', 'Prodigy Circuit' or any other identical or deceptively similar name or from passing off any such identical or deceptively similar trade mark or trade name - Application praying for rejection/return of plaint for presentation to proper Court dismissed - Appeal against - 'Held, 'cause of action' and 'applicability of law' are two distinct, different and independent things and one cannot be confused with the other - Clause 18 provides for applicability of law and it specifically declares that the terms and conditions of the agreement shall be interpreted in accordance with 'the laws of Hong Kong Special Administrative Region' - That, does not mean that a suit can be instituted only in Hong Kong and not in any other country - Since it is alleged that appellant- defendant had committed breach of agreement by using trade mark/trade name in Trade Fair, 2005 in Delhi, a part of cause of action has arisen in Delhi - Appeal dismissed.
