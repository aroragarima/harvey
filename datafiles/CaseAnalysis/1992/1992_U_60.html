Nelson Motis v Union of India and Anr.

Supreme Court of India

02 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.M. Punchhi, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1256; (1992) 4 SCC 711; (1993) SCC (L&S) 13; AIR 1992 SC 1981; 1993 (23) ATC 382; 1992 (65) FLR 853; JT 1992 (5) SC 511; 1992 LabIC 2037; 1992 (2) LLJ 744; 1992 (2) LLN 1059; 1992(2) SCALE 476; 1992 (3) SLJ 65; 1992 (5) SLR 394; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 325; 1993 (1) UJ 45; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1189

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 10(4)(3) - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - (A) Disciplinary proceedings - Nature and scope - Acquittal in criminal case, if conclude disciplinary proceedings - (B) Suspension - Automatic suspension in case order of removal, dismissal etc. is set aside and Disciplinary Authority decides to hold further enquiry - Whether sub-rule (4) to r. 10 can be read down as limited only to employees who were earlier under suspension - Validity - Held, (A) Different from that of a criminal case, acquittal in criminal case cannot therefore conclude disciplinary proceedings - Cannot be read down as - (B) It is also not discriminatory - Appeal dismissed.
