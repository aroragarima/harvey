Commissioner of Income Tax, Jaipur v Sirehmal Nawalakha

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 280; (2001) 6 SCC 641; AIR 2001 SC 3648; 2001 (45) ALR 149; 2001 (169) CTR 493; [2001] 251 ITR 108; JT 2001 (7) SC 69; 2001(5) SCALE 394; 2001 (6) SLT 22; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 81; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 615; 2001 (6) Supreme 237; 2001 (2) UC 366

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Gift Tax Act, 1958, ss. 4 and 17 - Registration Act, 1908, s. 17 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 122, 123 and 2(xii) - Whether on facts and circumstances of case, tribunal was justified in law in holding that no valid gift of out-houses of building named as 'Deep Shikha' was made by assessee to his wife in terms of Gift Tax Act, 1958? - Held, certain transactions may not be regarded as a gift for purposes of Transfer of Property Act but would fall within ambit of expression 'gift' by virtue of s. 4 of Gift Tax Act, but in each one of cases referred to in s. 4 there has to be a transfer of immovable property which in certain circumstances is to be regarded as a gift - What is, however, important is that there has to be a transfer of property and a transfer by reason of s. 17 of Registration Act, 1908 can only be by way of a registered document - High Court did not even refer to provisions of Registration Act and, therefore, fell in error in coming to conclusion that case fell within provisions of s. 4 of Gift Tax Act, 1958 and, therefore, as it was a deemed gift it was not necessary that document had to be registered - General law did not stand abrogated and requirement of complying with provisions of Transfer of Property Act and Registration Act had to be fulfilled - HC, therefore, erred in answering question of law in negative and against revenue - Appeal allowed.
