Firm of Bhagat Ram Mohanlal v Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax, Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 12; AIR 1956 SC 374; [1956] 29 ITR 521; 1956 (1) MLJ 160; 1956 (1) MLJ(SC) 160; 1956 SCJ 312; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940, s. 7 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Partnership - Excess Profits Tax - Sustainability - Laws Appellant was firm registered u/s.26A of the Act - During accounting years ending 1943 and 1944, it made profits on which it was assessed to excess profits tax respectively of Rs. 10,02350 and Rs. 13,00550 - During year 1944 1945 it sustained loss of Rs. 15,771, and adding it to sum of Rs. 37,800 which was standard profits for business, Excess Profits Tax Officer determined deficiency of profits for year at Rs. 53,571 - u/s. 7 of the Act Excess Profits Tax Officer passed directed refund of Rs. 23,028100 which had been paid by appellant as excess profits tax for those years - There was reconstitution of firm - Commissioner of Excess Profits Tax, issued notice and appellant appeared - By order Commissioner held that there was change in persons carrying on business, and that award of relief u/s. 7 ?? by Excess Profits Tax Officer was mistake - It was further directed that Rs. 11,51450 which had been refunded to it should be collected - Appellant moved to HC u/art. 226 seeking quashing order of Commissioner - HC dismissed petitions - Hence, instant appeal - Whether by reason of partition of joint family and reconstitution of firm under deed there was change in persons carrying on business within s. 8(1) of the Act and whether order of Commissioner was bad on ground that there was no mistake apparent from record, as required by s. 20 of the Act - Held, joint family of which they were members not being partner in new firm it having ceased to exist by reason of partition there was having regard to definition in s. 2(17) of the Act, change in persons who carried on business - View was taken in Shanmugavel Nadar and Sons V. Commissioner of Income Tax 1948 Indlaw MAD 1 and Court agreed with same - It was true that order of Excess Profits Tax Officer had not mentioned facts, but they appeared from record of income tax proceedings which included registration certificates of firm u/s. 26A of the Act and returns made by firm disclosing names of partners and their respective shares - It was argued for appellant that records were inadmissible for purpose of proceedings u/s. 20 of the Act, because record referred to and contemplated by that section must be record of excess profits tax proceedings, and that records of income tax proceedings could not be used under that section - Court rejected this contention - Appeal dismissed.
