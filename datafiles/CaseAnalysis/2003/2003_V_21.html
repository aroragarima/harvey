Smt. Shakila Abdul Gafar Khan v Vasant Raghunath Dhoble and Another

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 728; (2003) 7 SCC 749; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1918; AIR 2003 SC 4567; 2003 (4) CCR 108; 2004 (1) CGLJ 47; 2003 (4) Crimes 106; 2003 CRLJ 4548; 2003 (3) JCC 1586; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 282; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 459; 2003(7) SCALE 213; 2003 (5) SLT 668; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 426; 2003 (6) Supreme 748

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Custodial Death

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Criminal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 20(3), 21, 22 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 330, 331 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 174, 208, 54 - Universal Declaration of Human Rights, 1948 - Custodial torture - Insufficient evidence against erring police officials - Compensation granted - Held, amount of compensation shall be as a palliative measure and does not preclude affected person(s) from bringing a suit to recover appropriate damages from State Govt. and its erring officials if such a remedy is available in law suit it goes without saying, if filed, shall be decided in accordance with law, uninfluenced by any finding, observation or conclusion herein - Further held, an enquiry be conducted by Head of Police force of State under direct control of Chief Secretary of State, to find out as to who were persons responsible for injuries on body of deceased If on further enquiry and on basis of materials collected it appears that accused who is being acquitted had a role to play, it shall be open to authorities to initiate proceedings for action and same shall be taken notwithstanding order of acquittal passed by HC - This is so, because on materials now placed on record acquittal was justified - Action will also be taken against officials who did not register FIR and authorities who were requested to conduct crime branch enquiry but yet do not appear to have done anything in matter - Awarding compensation also shall not be considered as a factor to decide either way as to whether any particular official was responsible for custodial torture.
