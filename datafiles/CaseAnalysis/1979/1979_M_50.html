Income Tax Officer and Others v Messrs Madnani Engineering Works Limited, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 300; (1979) 2 SCC 455; AIR 1979 SC 1450; 1979 (12) CTR 144; [1979] 118 ITR 1; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 905; 1979 TAXLR 1087; [1979] 1 TAXMAN 345; 1979 UJ 406

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147(a) - Income Tax assessment - Disclosure of material facts, fully and fairly - If failure to do so - When assessee had produced all hundis relating to loans obtained from creditors and book entries showing payment of interest before ITO - ITO asserted that advanced loans were bogus - Held, mere assertion of belief by ITO without any material in support thereof, that persons alleged to have advanced loans were bogus, would not warrant recourse to s. 147(a) as assessee could not be said to have failed to disclose - Appeal dismissed.
