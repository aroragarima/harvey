Deewan Singh & Ors. v Rajendra Pd. Ardevi & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 3; (2007) 10 SCC 528; AIR 2007 SC 767; JT 2007 (1) SC 223; 2007(1) SCALE 32; [2007] 1 S.C.R. 30

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Rajasthan Public Trust Act, 1959; Rajasthan Public Trusts Rules, 1962 - Management of a temple - Whether State Government is obligated to constitute a committee of management of a public trust to which Chapter X of the Act applies?; Whether the constitution of such a committee of management falls within the discretionary jurisdiction of the State Government? - State Government does not have any discretionary jurisdiction to exercise in the matter of appointment of a committee of management - It is imperative in nature - State not only in the earlier round of litigation but also before the High Court had taken a categorical stand that it had all along been ready and willing to act in terms of the provisions of Chapter X of the Act and appoint a Committee; it cannot take a different stand now - Appeals disposed of.
