Byram Pestonji Gariwala v Union Bank Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 836; (1992) 1 SCC 31; AIR 1991 SC 2234; AIR 1991 SCW 2567; 1992 (1) AnWR 16; 1991 (2) BC 558; 1992 (1) BLJR 1; 1991 (3) CCC 381; JT 1991 (4) SC 15; 1992 (1) KLT 368; 1992 (2) MLJ 1; 1992 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 1991 (2) RCR(Rent) 659; 1992 RD 22; 1991 (2) RentLR 400; 1991(2) SCALE 625; [1991] Supp1 S.C.R. 187

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Recognised Agent

Summary: Advocates and Judges - CPC, 1908, Pre. , O. 23 r. 3, O. 3 r. 1 and s. 11 - CPC (Amendment) Act, 1976 - Counsel can sign in his name for the party - Vakalatnama is more than a contract of agency - Compromise decree signed by Counsel and not by parties in person - Is binding, executable and operates as res judicata even if it extends beyond subject matter of suit - The expression 'in writing and signed by the parties' in O. 23, r. 1 and implied authority given by amendment of 1976 to CPC, 1908 - The issue is - Whether it was with in the power of the Counsel to enter into compromise - Held, the Counsel held to be included in the term 'party' and found to be legally competent to sign adecree of compromise for the partries - Appeal dismissed.
