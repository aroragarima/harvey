Â Rao Nihalkaran v Ramgopal

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 379; AIR 1966 SC 1485; 1966 MPLJ 712; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 427

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Possession Of Land

Summary: Land & Property - Madhya Bharat Muafi and Inam Tenants and Sub-tenants Protection Act, 1954 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, ss. 185 and 193 - What is meaning of expression 'tenant' used in s. 185(1) cl. (ii)(a) of Code ? - Cl.(ii)(b) of s. 185(1) provides that in Madhya Bharat region every person who at commencement of Code holds any land as ryotwari sub-lessee as defined in Madhya Bharat Ryotwari Sub-Lessee Protection Act, 1955 shall be called an occupancy tenant; unless a ryotwari sub-lessee as defined in Act 29 of 1955 included a sub-lessee whose tenure was terminated before commencement of Code, that clause would not apply to any concrete case; ryotwari sub-lessees who had ceased by determination of sub-leases to have right in lands were still protected from eviction during pendency of Act 29 of 1955, and by s. 185(1)(ii)(b) of Code upon ryotwari sub-lessees rights of occupancy tenants were conferred; if ryotwari sub-lessees of disabled persons mentioned in sub-s (2) of s. 74 of Act 66 of 1950 cannot claim rights of occupancy tenants by virtue of s. 185(3) and other ryotwari sub-lessees cannot qualify for those rights because of determination of their interest as sub-lessees by virtue of ss. 75 and 76 of Act 66 of 1950, s. 185(1)(ii)(b) will not apply to any class of ryotwari sub-lessees which is a strong ground in support of view taken by HC that expression 'ryotwari sub-lessee' in s. 185(1)(ii)(b) includes persons whose contractual relation has been determined either under terms of contract of sub-lease or statutorily under Act 66 of 1950 and if that be true meaning of expression 'ryotwari sub-lessee' there would be no reason to think that Legislature sought to make a distinction between tenants, sub-tenants and ordinary tenants of Inam land in s. 185(1)(ii)(a) and ryotwari sub-lessees of other lands in s. 185(1)(ii)(b) - Held, a member belonging to those classes would therefore be included in protection provided at some time prior to date on which Code was brought into force, if he was in possession of land as a tenant, sub-tenant or ordinary tenant and he continued to hold land till date of commencement of Code and there is nothing in s. 262(2) which seeks to nullify statutory conferment of occupancy rights upon persons in position of tenants, sub-tenants or ordinary tenants against whom proceedings were taken at date when Code was brought into force - Appeal dismissed.
