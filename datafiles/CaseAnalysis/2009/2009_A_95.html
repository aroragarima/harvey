Mohd. Sohrab Khan v Aligarh Muslim University and others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2164; (2009) 4 SCC 555; 2009 (3) ADJ 477; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1648; 2009 (4) ALJ 523; 2009 (2) AllCJ 1112; 2009 (4) AWC 3843; 2009 (2) ESC 177; JT 2009 (2) SC 666; 2009 (5) MLJ 301; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 301; 2009(3) SCALE 638; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 907; 2010 (1) SLJ 156; 2009 (1) UPLBEC 769

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Aligarh Muslim University Act, 1920

Summary: Service - Selection process - Legality - Aligarh Muslim University issued an advertisement for filling up post of Chemistry Lecturers in the University - Essential qualification for the said post was First Class Masters' Degree in the appropriate branch of teaching post in Humanities and Sciences - Both Petitioner and 'A' submitted their applications to be considered as against the post which was advertised - Petitioner had a First Class Masters' Degree in Chemistry (Pure) whereas 'A' was holding a First Class Masters Degree in Industrial Chemistry - Selection Committee changed the rule in the midstream and selected 'A' on the ground that a Masters Degree Holder in Industrial Chemistry would be better suited for manning the said post without there being any specific advertisement in that regard - Writ petition was filed thereagainst wherein it was held that the appointment of 'A' to the said post was not legal as he did not possess the minimum qualification - HC set aside the order of appointment with a direction to the University to initiate fresh selection process - Present appeals - Whether HC was justified in rejecting the candidature of 'A' as against the said post which was advertised for pure Chemistry stream? - Held, whenever and wherever the University desired to fill up a post at variance with the main subject, it is specifically notified and indicated in the said advertisement - Further, University can always have a person as a Lecturer in a particular discipline that it desires to have, but the same must be specifically stated in the advertisement itself - Also, in the matter of selection of candidates, opinion of the Selection Committee should be final, but at the same time, the Selection Committee cannot act arbitrarily and cannot change the criteria/qualification in the selection process during its midstream - In the present case, post advertised was meant for a person belonging to the pure Chemistry Department - Hence, advertisement which was issued for filling up the post of Lecturer in Chemistry could not have been filled up by a person belonging to the subject of Industrial Chemistry when the same having been specifically not mentioned in the advertisement that a Masters Degree holder in the said subject would also be suitable for being considered - Further, Selection Committee changed the essential qualification laid down in the advertisement midstream which was not permissible - Hence, as 'A' did not possess a degree in pure Chemistry and therefore, it was rightly held by HC that he did not possess the minimum qualification required for filling up the post of Chemistry Lecturer Chemistry as pure Chemistry and Industrial Chemistry were two different subjects - HC was justified in rejecting the candidature of 'A' as against the said post which was advertised for pure Chemistry stream - However, with the appointment of 'A' to the said post, the list recommended by the Selection Committee and approved by the other competent authority has lapsed - Order passed by the HC giving liberty to the University to lay down the qualification necessary for filling up the aforesaid post upheld - Appeals disposed of.
