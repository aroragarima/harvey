Costao Fernandes v State At The Instance of D. S. P., Cbi, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 165; (1996) SCC (Cr) 489; (1996) 7 SCC 516; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 90; AIR 1996 SC 1383; 1996 (1) CCR 193; 1996 (1) Crimes 125; 1996 CRLJ 1723; 1996 (67) ECR 358; 1996 (82) E.L.T. 433; JT 1996 (2) SC 519; 1996 (4) KarLJ 618; 1996(2) SCALE 298; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 868; 1996 (2) Supreme 247

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs Act, 1962, s. 155 - Whether protection u/s. 155 of Customs Act is available in case smuggler gets killed while appellant was doing his duty u/s. 106 of Act ? - If while engaged in his official duty, appellant was assaulted, and 22 times at that with an instrument like knife, he could not have allowed himself to be killed, but had to defend himself by retaliation - Killing was thus not divorced from performance of duty enjoined by s. 106 of Act - Held, protection u/s. 155 available - Prosecution against appellant quashed - Appeal dismissed.
