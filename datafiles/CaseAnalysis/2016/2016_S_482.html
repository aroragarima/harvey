State of Uttar Pradesh and others v Subhash Chandra Jaiswal and others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 925; (2017) 5 SCC 163; 2017 (1) AWC 261; JT 2017 (2) SC 608; 2016(12) SCALE 627

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Excise

Keywords: Lis, U.P. Excise Settlement of Licenses for Retail Sale of Foreign Liquor (Excluding Beer and Wine) Rules, 2001, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, U.P. Excise (Settlement of Licenses for Retail License for Model Shop of Foreign Liquor) Rules, 2003, U.P. Excise (Settlement of Licenses Retail Sale of Country Liquor) Rules, 2002

Summary: Administrative - Excise - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 419, 420, 467, 468, 471 - Grant of License - Directions Challenged - Collector granted license to run liquor shop to respondent nos. 2-3 and respondent no.1/complainant filed complaint against licensee for commission of fraud and forgery - Police failed to take proceedings against respondent nos. 2-3 - HC disposed of writ petition of complainant and issued certain directions - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, Constitution specifically demarcated the ambit of power and the boundaries of the three organs of the society by laying down the principles of separation of powers, which is being adhered to for carrying out democratic functioning of the country. So far as the legislation is concerned, the exclusive domain is with the legislature. Subordinate legislations are framed by the executive by exercising the delegated power conferred by the statute, which is the rule making power. The judiciary has been vested with the power to interpret the aforesaid legislations and to give effect to them since the parameters of the jurisdiction of both the organs are earmarked. Therefore, it is always appropriate for each of the organs to function within its domain. A court cannot take steps for framing a policy. Directions are not to be issued solely out of concern. They have to be founded on certain legally justifiable principles that have roots in the laws of the country. In instant case, the directions issued by the HC and the queries made by it related to various spheres are constrained to think, the HC should not have gone into. High Court has crossed the boundaries of the controversy that was before it. Hence, impugned order issued by HC is set aside and HC is requested to dispose of writ petition in accordance with law. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Court cannot direct a legislature to enact a particular law.
