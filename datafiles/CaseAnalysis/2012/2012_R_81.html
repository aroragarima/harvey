Rushikesh Tanaji Bhoite v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 2; (2012) 2 SCC 72; AIR 2012 SC 890; 2012 CRLJ 1334; JT 2012 (1) SC 1; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 197; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 862; 2012(1) SCALE 53; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 640

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers Drug-offenders Dangerous persons and Video pirates Act, 1981

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers, Drug Offenders and Dangerous Persons Act, s. 3 (1) - Detention - Maintainability - Appellant was detained in Central prison under provisions of Maharashtra Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Slumlords, Bootleggers, Drug Offenders and Dangerous Persons Act - Legality of detention order was challenged in HC, was dismissed - Hence, instant petition - Appellant contended that detenu was released on bail and conditions imposed were relaxed and was enjoying freedom before order of detention was issued - Detention order or grounds supplied to detenu did not show that detaining authority was aware of bail granted - Whether detaining authority was justified in issuing detention orders against appellants - Held, that order of bail was neither placed before detaining authority at time of passing order of detention nor detaining authority was aware of order of bail, as such detention order is rendered invalid and other offences referred suffer remoteness and want of proximity - None of criminal cases, except offence registered which is referred in grounds for detention can be proximity to order of detention - Appeal allowed.
