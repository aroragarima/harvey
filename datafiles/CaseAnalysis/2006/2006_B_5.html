Standard Chartered Bank Limited v B. N. Raman

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1313; (2006) 5 SCC 727; AIR 2006 SC 2810; 2006 (64) ALR 594; 2007 (1) ALT 8; 2006 (4) AWC 3308; 2006 (6) Bom.C.R. 775; 2007 (1) CCC 340; 2006 (3) CPJ(Sc) 1; 2006 (2) DRTC 381; JT 2006 (6) SC 368; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1306; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 625; 2006(7) SCALE 108; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 558

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Keywords: Safe Custody, Safe Deposit, Deposit Receipt

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 2(1)(d) and 2(1)(o) - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, s. 5(b) - Question regarding rate of interest to be given - Court has to select a date which puts plaintiff in same position in which he would have been, had defendant discharged his obligation when he ought to have done, bearing in mind that rate of exchange is a fluctuating factor - Encashment of dollar denominated deposits have certain economic implications - In cases of this type, burden is on complainant to show rate of exchange prevalent on afore stated dates in order to assist court to arrive at indicative prices -In present case, none of these factors have been considered by State Commission - Held, neither State Commission nor National Commission has examined this question regarding selection of appropriate date, appropriate rate of exchange on that particular date as also rate of interest which appellant was required to pay - Matter remitted to State Commission - Appeal partly allowed.
