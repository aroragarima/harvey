Oriental Insurance Company Limited v Zaharulnisha and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 729; (2008) 12 SCC 385; 2008 (4) ACC 781; 2008 (5) ADJ 155; AIR 2008 SC 2218; 2008(4) ALL MR 413; 2008 (71) ALR 835; 2008 (3) AWC 2170; 2008 (151) PLR 252; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 913; 2008(7) SCALE 310; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 58

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Tort

Keywords: Arrears Of Land Revenue, Social Welfare Legislation, Damage To Property, Use Of Motor Vehicle, Person In Charge, Use Of Vehicle, Welfare Legislation, Third Party Risks, Insurance Of Vehicle, Licensed Driver, Policy Conditions, Negligent, Branch Manager, Application Form, Statutory Deposit, Motor Vehicle Rules, 1989

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Appeal to challenge order dismissing appeal filed to challenge award passed by Tribunal wherein Tribunal held that the accident was due to rash and negligent driving of the scooter by driver-Ram Surat and granted Rs. 3, 01, 500/- as compensation with interest at 9% per annum in favour of the claimants and against the second respondent-owner of the scooter and appellant-insurance company - Whether the appellant insurance company could be held liable to pay the amount of compensation for the default of the scooterist who was not holding licence for driving two wheeler scooter but had driving licence of different class of vehicle in terms of s. 10 of the MV Act? - Held, where the insurers, relying upon the provisions of violation of law by the assured, take an exception to pay the assured or a third party, they must prove a wilful violation of the law by the assured - Provisions of sub-ss. (4) and (5) of s. 149 of the MV Act may be considered as to the liability of the insurer to satisfy the decree at the first instance - Liability of the insurer is a statutory one; liability of the insurer to satisfy the decree passed in favour of a third party is also statutory - Appellant insurance company cannot be held liable to pay the amount of compensation to the claimants for the cause of death of Shukurullah in road accident which had occurred due to rash and negligent driving of scooter by Ram Surat who admittedly had no valid and effective licence to drive the vehicle on the day of accident - Scooterist was possessing driving licence of driving HMV and he was driving totally different class of vehicle which act of his is in violation of s. 10(2) of the MV Act - Appeal allowed.
