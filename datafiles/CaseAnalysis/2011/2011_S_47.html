D. Ethiraj v Secretary To Govt. And Others

Supreme Court of India

11 October 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 722; (2011) 10 SCC 398; 2012 CRLJ 1440; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 351; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 720; [2011] 15 S.C.R. 1048

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.366, 109, 119, 121 to 130 - Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1961, ss. 2, 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 161 - Notification - Prisoners - Remission - Entitlement - Appellant was found guilty of offences u/s. 366 r/w 109 , 119 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellant to undergo 3 years rigorous imprisonment for an offence u/ss. 366 109, and one year rigorous imprisonment for an offence u/s. 119, IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction and sentence of trial court - Appellant filed for remission before DB , which was declined - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to remission, as per G.O - Held, a prisoner could be on bail on a particular day that was just a fortuitous circumstance - What Court had to consider was actual period of sentence undergone by prisoner and whether by reason of period actually undergone, prisoner qualifies for remission - In instant case, appellant had undergone more than 6 months of imprisonment prior to date of judgment rendered by DB - Therefore, judgment of HC was set aside - Appellant was entitled for remission - Appeal allowed.
