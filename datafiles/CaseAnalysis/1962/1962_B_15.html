Â Mysore State Electricity Board v Bangalore Woollen, Cotton and Silk Mills Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 389; AIR 1963 SC 1128; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 127; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 127

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 76 - (A) What is test for deciding that a decision operates as res judicata ? - Held, in order to decide whether a decision in an earlier litigation operates as res judicata, court must look at nature of litigation, what were issues raised therein and what was actually needed in it - (B) When does s. 60 of impugned Act apply? - Further held, a pre-requisite condition for application of s. 60 is that contract made by State Govt. or thing done by it must be for any of purpose of 1948 Act - (C) Whether s. 49 of impugned Act applied to Board after it was constituted? - Further held, it had no application in 1956, and it cannot be said, that any dispute as to revision of rates made by State Govt. in 1956 was a question which arose under 1948 Act - Mere fact that Board is successor-interest of State Govt. and supply of electricity is one of purposes of 1948 act does not mean that revision of rates in 1956 and a dispute raised as to such revision, became a question under 1948 Act by reason of demand made by Board of arrears due in respect of revised rate - (D) Whether expression 'subject to provisions of this Act' occurring in s. 49 attracted s. 76? - Further held, it does not - S. 49 does not contemplate that consumers may raise a dispute with regard to terms and conditions and on such a dispute being raised, it shall be determined by arbitration as required by s. 76(1) of 1948 Act - Expression of this Act merely means that if there are any provisions regulating Board in matter of supplying electricity to any person not being a licensee, then supply by Board will be subject to those provisions - There is no provision which regulates Board in matter of charges which it may fix for supply of electricity - (E) Whether provisions of 1910 Act or 1948 Act make present dispute a matter directed or required to be referred to arbitration either under s. 52 of 1910 Act or s. 76 (2) of 1948 Act ?- Expression 'having regard to' in s. 49 does not contemplate that a consumer of electricity can raise a dispute as against Board on footing that Board did not pay due regard to nature and geographical position of supply and purposes for which it was required - Respondents can call for an arbitration under s. 76(1) of Act, if they can establish that dispute in present case is a question which arises under 1948 Act - Appeals allowed.
