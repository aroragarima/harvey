Nar Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

11 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 767; (2015) 1 SCC 496; AIR 2015 SC 310; 2015 CRLJ 576; JT 2014 (12) SC 574; 2015 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 11; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 596; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 929; 2015 (2) RLW 1387; 2014(12) SCALE 622

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Examination Of Accused, Material Evidence

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 313 - Non-compliance of s. 313 of CrPC - Effect thereof - Instant appeal filed against the judgment passed by the HC, dismissing the appeal of accused-appellant thereby confirming the conviction of the appellant u/s. 302 of and conviction u/s. 25(1B) of the Act - Accused contended that since the material evidence and Ballistic Expert opinion was not put to him in his statement u/s. 313 of CrPC, it should be completely excluded from consideration and barring the same, there is no other evidence to sustain the conviction - Whether non-compliance of the mandatory provisions of s. 313 of CrPC vitiates the trial and conviction of the appellant.

Held, question whether a trial is vitiated or not depends upon the degree of the error and the accused must show that non-compliance of s. 313 of CrPC has materially prejudiced him or is likely to cause prejudice to him. Such prejudice should also demonstrate that it has occasioned failure of justice to the accused. The burden is upon the accused to prove that prejudice has been caused to him or in the facts and circumstances of the case, such prejudice may be implicit and the Court may draw an inference of such prejudice. In the instant case, objection as to the defective s. 313 of CrPC statement has been raised only before SC for the first time. Accused is not entitled for acquittal on the ground of non-compliance of mandatory provisions of s. 313 of CrPC. However, accused is prejudiced on account of omission to put the question as to the opinion of Ballistic Expert which was relied upon by the Trial court as well as by the HC. The matter is remitted back to the Trial Court for proceeding with the matter afresh from the stage of recording statement of the accused u/s. 313 CrPC. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Merely because of defective questioning u/s. 313 of Code of Criminal Procedure, it cannot be inferred that any prejudice had been caused to the accused, even assuming that some incriminating circumstances in the prosecution case had been left out.
