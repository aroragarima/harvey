Ritesh Chakarvarti v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1419; (2006) 12 SCC 321; 2006 (3) JCC (Narcotics) 150; JT 2006 (12) SC 416; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 480; 2006(9) SCALE 644; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 772

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 42, 50, 57, 8 - Possession of narcotic substances - Conviction - Sustainability - Inspector in Central Bureau of Narcotics (Bureau) received an information, appellant carrying contraband and Inspector apprehended him and search was conducted and opium weighing 1.300 kgs. was recovered - Trial Court convicted appellant u/s. 8 of the Act, 1985 - Aggrieved by order, appellant filed appeal before HC, which affirmed conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal - Whether judgment of conviction and sentence of Trial Court and HC was justified - Held, Jagdish v. State of M.P., 2001 Indlaw SC 21047, relied on - SC set aside judgment of conviction where independent witnesses denied that search and seizure for recovery of opium took place in their presence and adverse inference was further drawn as to sub-Inspector therein brought down only one passenger from bus if he did not have any prior information or entertain any suspicion regarding involvement of Appellant therein being in possession or smuggling of opium - Hence, Court should also take care of fact that person might not be convicted on misguided suspicion, although effect of illegal search might not have any direct impact on prosecution case but same would have bearing on appreciation of evidence of official witnesses and other materials depending on facts and circumstances of each case - Therefore, appellant was entitled to benefit of doubt and set aside order of HC - Appeal allowed.
