State of Haryana v Ram Mehar and others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 623; (2016) 8 SCC 762; AIR 2016 SC 3942; 2016 CRLJ 4666; 2016 (4) JCC 2336; JT 2016 (8) SC 369; 2017 (1) MLJ(Crl) 437; 2016 (4) RCR(Criminal) 154; [2016] 5 S.C.R. 172

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fair Trial, Jurisdiction, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Opportunity, Proof, Criminal Court, Criminal Trial, Prayer, Power Of The Court, Denial, Criminal Justice, Speedy Trial, Change, Defence Counsel, Recalling, Lacuna, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 231(2), 309, 311, 313, 406, 482 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 138 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 114, 120B, 147, 148, 149, 201, 302, 307, 323, 325, 332, 34, 353, 381, 382, 427, 436, 452 - Conviction - Recall of witnesses - Sustainability - Accused/respondents were armed with door beams and shockers went upstairs inside the company smashed the glass walls of conference room and threw chairs and table tops towards management officials and set on fire the entire office - Deceased/General Manager, Human Resources of Company was burnt alive - FIR was lodged against accused and during trial accused were charged for offences punishable u/ss. 147, 148, 149, 452, 302, 307, 436, 323, 332, 353, 427, 114, 201, 120B, 34, 325, 381 & 382 IPC - Respondents filed an application before Trial court seeking recall of witnesses u/s. 311 r/w s. 231(2) CrPC which was rejected - Aggrieved, respondents filed writ petitions before HC - HC allowed petitions holding that accused were charged with heinous offences including one u/s. 302 IPC and recalling is not for the purpose of setting up a new case or make the witnesses turn hostile but only to have a proper defence - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the reasons ascribed by HC were germane for exercise of power u/s. 311 CrPC.

Held, decisions which have used the words that Court should be magnanimous, needless to give special emphasis, did not mean to convey individual generosity or magnanimity which is founded on any kind of fanciful notion. The approach may be liberal but that does not necessarily mean "the liberal approach" shall be the rule and all other parameters shall become exceptions. Recall of some witnesses by prosecution at one point of time, can never be ground to entertain a petition by defence though no acceptable ground is made out. In instant case prosecution had examined all the witnesses. The statements of all accused persons had been recorded u/s. 313 CrPC. The defence had examined 15 witnesses. The singular ground which prominently comes to surface is that earlier counsel who was engaged by defence had not put some questions and failed to put some questions and give certain suggestions. In such a situation recalling of witnesses indubitably cannot form the foundation. If it is accepted as a ground, there would be possibility of a retrial. There may be an occasion when such a ground may weigh with Court but definitely instant case does not arouse judicial conscience within the established norms of s. 311 CrPC for exercise of such jurisdiction. HC has failed to appreciate that witnesses have been sought to be recalled for further cross-examination to elicit certain facts for establishing certain discrepancies and also to be given certain suggestions. Further, exercise of power u/s. 311 CrPC can be sought to be invoked either by prosecution or by accused persons or by Court itself. HC has been moved by the ground that accused were in the custody and the concept of speedy trial is not nullified and no prejudice is caused, and, therefore, principle of magnanimity should apply. Suffice it to say, a criminal trial does not singularly centres around accused. The cry of collective may not be uttered in decibels which are physically audible in Court premises, but Court has to remain sensitive to such silent cries and agonies, for the society seeks justice. Therefore, a balance has to be struck. HC has fallen into absolute error in axing the order passed by Trial Judge. Thus, when the concept of fair trial is limitlessly stretched, having no boundaries, orders like instant one may fall in the arena of sanctuary of errors. Hence, SC reiterate the necessity of doctrine of balance. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and that of Trial Court was restored. Appeals allowed.
