J.K. Iron and Steel Company Limited, Kanpur v Iron and Steel Mazdoor Union, Kanpur

Supreme Court of India

23 December 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, N. Chandrasekhara Aiyar

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 42; AIR 1956 SC 231; 1956 (1) LLJ 227A; 1956 SCJ 270; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 1315

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal, misa

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 3,4,8 - Settlement - Retrenchment - Sustainability - Ministry of Commerce in Govt. of India ordered Company to shift its Jute Baling Hoops factory from Kanpur to Calcutta - As no land was available in Calcutta no effect could be given to order - Iron and Steel Controller ordered Company to stop rolling of jute baling hoops at once - Accordingly production of these hoops was stopped - At same time there was scarcity of scrap iron and Company's case was that forced it to reduce working of its furnace from three shifts day to one - Company stated that because of these two causes it was obliged to retrench its staff - Notice was issued and Twenty five of 128 accepted their wages and other dues in full settlement but remaining 103 refused - This was forwarded to State Govt. and Governor of State referred issue to Regional Conciliation Officer u/ss. 3, 4 and 8 of the Act for adjudication - It was held that Company was not entitled to resort to retrenchment except as last resort and that in circumstances of instant case these workmen should (1) have been offered the option of employment in the new set up at Calcutta; and (2) those that did not want it should have been laid off in rotation instead of being retrenched and accordingly ordered that should be done and drew up graduated scale of compensation - Hence, instant appeal - Whether retrenchment order passed was justified - Held, agreement had been reached between parties placed before Court towards end of arguments but SC had not looked at it because counsel for Mazdoor Union said it did not cover case of these retrenched workers - Company insisted that it did - Award and decision of Labour Appellate Tribunal were set aside and case was remitted to Labour Appellate Tribunal for re-bearing of appeals filed before it and for fresh decision in light of foregoing observations - As there was no agreement between parties on subject, Labour Appellate Tribunal or Adjudicator, as the case may be, were ordered to make suitable orders - Appeals disposed of.
