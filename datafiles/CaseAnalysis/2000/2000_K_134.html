C. T. O. (Int) No. I and Others v Ki-Hi-Tech Secure Print Limited

Supreme Court of India

29 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 187; (2000) 5 SCC 55; JT 2000 (7) SC 432; 2001 (121) STC 611; 2000 (6) Supreme 468

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 20 - Interpretation of notification which exempts from tax payable under Act on sales of all books and periodicals w.e.f. 01-04-1964 - By notification D/- 29-01-1964 exemption from tax payable was granted under Act on sales of 'all books and periodicals' w.e.f. 01-04-1964 - Subsequently by GOMs No 625 Rev (CT - II) D/- 31-07- 1996 said exemption was confined only to periodicals and printed books for reading - Pursuant to which notices were issued to dealers in terms of s. 20 to reopen assessments - Validity of those notices was challenged before High Court - HC relying upon decision in Govindaswamy Binding Works v State of A P 1971 Indlaw AP 9 allowed writ petitions - A division bench of HC in that decision took view that expression 'all books' are not technical words, that they are plain and simple words understood in common parlance as taking within their range every possible kind of books and word 'all' will make it abundantly manifest and plain that notification was issued to cover all cases of sales of books and is not restricted in its application to books of literary material or other kinds of reading material - Held, it would be unreasonable to upset meaning given to expression used in enactment which was in force for nearly three decades - In fact, Govt. subsequently has taken note of this decision and has restricted exemption only to periodicals and books for reading - No justification to interfere with order under appeal - Appeal dismissed.
