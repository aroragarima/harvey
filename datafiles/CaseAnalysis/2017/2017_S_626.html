State through Central Bureau of Investigation v Kalyan Singh (Former CM of UP) and others

Supreme Court of India

19 April 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 280; (2017) 7 SCC 444; AIR 2017 SC 2020; 2017 CRLJ 2867; 2017 (2) MLJ(Crl) 369; 2017(5) SCALE 36

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conspiracy, FIR, CBI, Jurisdiction, Defect, Right Of Appeal, Subordinate, Criminal Court, Special Judge, Proviso, Same Transaction, Joint Trial, Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1952, Government of India Act, 1935, Consultation, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Plea

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Joint trial of accused - Instant matter with regard to demolition of Babri Masjid wherein initially two FIRs were lodged - One against lakhs of kar sewaks alleging offences of dacoity, robbery, causing of hurt, injuring/defiling places of public worship, promoting enmity between two groups on grounds of religion, etc. and another FIR lodged against eight persons - L.K.Advani, Ashok singhal, Vinay Katiar, Uma Bharati, Sadhvi Ritambara, Murli Manohar Joshi, Giriraj Kishore and Vishnu Hari Dalmia for offences u/ss.153-A, 153-B and s.505 of IPC - Later, 47 FIRs were lodged and all the cases including case out of FIR against kar sewaks were subsequently committed to Court of Sessions, Lucknow but case out of second FIR was not transferred to Court of Session, Lucknow and Special Court at Lucknow continued to try the case - While so, CBI filed consolidated charge-sheet against 48 persons and later filed supplementary charge-sheet against 8 persons - While so, Special Judge, Lucknow passed order dt.9-9-1997 to the effect that there was a prima facie case against all accused persons for framing charges of criminal conspiracy u/s.120-B read with various other sections of IPC - Further, Court held that since all the offences were committed in course of same transaction, the same warranted a joint trial and that the case was exclusively triable by Court of Special Judge, Lucknow - Aggrieved by said orders, Criminal Revision Petitions were filed before HC and vide judgment dt.12-2-2001, HC inter alia held that notification dt.8-10-1993 amending notification dt.9-9-1993 was invalid as there was no consultation with HC before issuing said notification and so, Special Court has no jurisdiction to inquire into and commit to Court of Sessions against eight accused - Later, CBI requested Govt. to rectify defect in notification but State Govt. rejected the said request - While so, order dt.12-2-2001 became final - Later, CBI filed supplementary charge sheet against 8 accused persons for offences u/ss.153A, 153B, 505 read with ss.147 and 149 of IPC before Judicial Magistrate, Rae Bareilly - Special Court vide order dt.4-5-2001, dropped proceedings against 21 persons including 8 persons - L.K.Advani, Ashok singhal, Vinay Katiar, Uma Bharati, Sadhvi Ritambara, Murli Manohar Joshi, Giriraj Kishore and Vishnu Hari Dalmia - Revision was filed against said order and by impugned judgment dt.22-5-2010, HC upheld judgment dt.4-5-2001 and held that there were two classes of accused, namely, leaders who were on the dais exhorting Kar Sewaks at 200 meters from Masjid and Kar Sewaks themselves and that their nature of accusations and involvement was for different criminal offence - Further, it was held that from the very beginning two separate FIRs were filed because of two different places of occurrence and different nature of accusation, CBI's preparing a joint charge-sheet for all 49 FIRS was impugn and ultimately upheld order dt.4-5-2001 and dismissed revision - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned orders of HC are sustainable.

Held, judgment dt.12-2-2001 clearly and unequivocally held that a joint charge sheet had been filed by CBI on ground that all the offences were committed in the course of the same transaction to accomplish the conspiracy alleged. The evidence for all the offences is almost the same and the offences could not be separated from each other, irrespective of fact that 49 different FIRs were lodged. As such, holding to the contrary, the impugned judgment which upheld the judgment dt.4-5-2001, is clearly erroneous. Further, offence of criminal conspiracy is already there in the joint charge sheet filed by CBI against all the named accused, that includes the 21 accused who have been discharged. Hence, it is clear that the said accused could not possibly have been discharged, as they were already arrayed as accused insofar as the charge of criminal conspiracy was concerned, which would be gone into by Special Judge, Lucknow, while dealing with the offences made out in FIR filed against kar sewaks. Further, impugned judgment also artificially divided offences and offenders into two groups which had not followed from judgment dt.12-2-2001. Further, filing of supplementary charge sheet against 8 accused persons which is going on separately at Rae Bareilly and the dropping altogether of charges against the 13 accused persons, after the Judgment dt.12-2-2001 has completely derailed the joint trial envisaged and has resulted in a fractured prosecution going on in two places simultaneously based on a joint charge sheet filed by CBI itself. Further, since the charge of criminal conspiracy against all 21 accused is already in the joint charge sheet filed by CBI at Lucknow, said charge could be added to the charges already framed against the survivors of the group of 8 accused. As against the survivors of the group of 13, IPC offences mentioned in joint charge sheet also need to be added. Hence, impugned judgment is set aside and directions are issued to the effect that proceedings in Court of Special Judicial Magistrate at Rae Bareilly are transferred to Court of Additional Sessions at Lucknow; Court of Sessions to frame an additional charge u/s.120-B aginst L.K.Advani, Vinay Katiar, Uma Bharati, Sadhvi Ritambara, Murli Manohar Joshi and Vishnu Hari Dalmia; and also frame additional charges u/s.120-B and other provisions of IPC mentioned in joint charge sheet filed by CBI against Champat Rai Bansal, Satish Pradhan, Dharam Das, Mahant Nritya Gopal Das, Mahamadleshwar Jagdish Muni, Ram Bilas Vadanti, Vaikunth Lal Sharma and Satish Chandra Nagar; further, as Kalyan Singh, being Governor of Rajasthan, is entitled to immunity u/art.361 of the Constitution, Court of Sessions to frame charges and move against him after he ceases to be Governor; further, there would be no de novo trial and no transfer of the Judge conducting trial until entire trial concludes; furthermore, CBI to ensure that on every date fixed for evidence, some PWs are present in Court and Sessions Court to complete trial and deliver judgment within period of 2 years from date of receipt of instant judgment. Appeal disposed of.
