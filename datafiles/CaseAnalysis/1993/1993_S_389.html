Â T. M. A. Pai Foundation and Others (I) v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Agrawal, S. Mohan, S.P. Bharucha, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 513; (1993) 4 SCC 276; AIR 1994 SC 13; JT 1993 (5) SC 704; 1993(3) SCALE 989; 1993 (5) SLR 776; 1994 (1) UJ 211

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Medical Council

Summary: Education - Reservation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 30 and 226 - Admission to foreign students in Private Medical Colleges with permission of Govt. - (A) Permission of intake up to 50% of capacity was not enforceable from academic year as per Supreme Court judgment (1991 INDLAW SC 991) - Grant of permission was in view of assurance held out by GOI to Govt. of Malaysia - (B) If minority institution can adopt its own method of selection for admission to minority community student - Held, considering fact that 1993-94 being year of adjustment and transition, Supreme Court allowed private colleges to admit NRIs and foreign students up to maximum 15% - No, State/affiliating University can direct that minority students must also be admitted only on basis of inter se merit determined on basis of common entrance test - Matter referred to larger Bench.
