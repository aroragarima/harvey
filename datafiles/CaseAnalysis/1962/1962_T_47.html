Â Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v State of Bihar (And connected appeals)

Supreme Court of India

24 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 265; AIR 1963 SC 577; 1963 BLJR 412; [1963] 48 ITR 123; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 199

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Bengal Cess Act, 1880, ss. 5 & 6 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Excess Profits Tax Act, 1940 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Assessment - Mining concessions - Validity - Imposition of cess - Appellants owned certain mines in Bihar - Appellant had taken on lease certain iron ore mines - Appellant holds mining concessions for iron and manganese ore and ore extracted by it is utilised for manufacture of iron & steel and steel products at company's factories - Appellant had taken on lease certain mines and ore mined by it was manufactured into copper and copper products at its factory - Company was not assessed to cess on ore mined - Company sold quantity of iron ore extracted by it - Assessment to cess under Act was made against it in respect of that year -Taxation was varied was raised to Rs. 1/4/- per toil by reason of an agreement between company and State Govt. - Validity of imposition of a cess u/ ss. 5 & 6 of 1880 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether annual profit from mine where ore produced from mine was sold but utilised as raw material for manufacture of - Held, there was no scope for application of rule of construction - Construction of ss.5,6 and 72 that where activities other than mere winning were carried on by an assessee - There was transaction of sale of ultimate product and profit derived from working of mine - Assessee from mining operation could be disintegrated and ascertained and tax levee thereon - Annual profit from mine for purpose of being brought to tax u/ss.6,72 - Appeal disposed of.
