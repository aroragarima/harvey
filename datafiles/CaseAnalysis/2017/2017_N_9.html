J. Vasanthi and others v N. Ramani Kanthammal (Dead) represented by Lrs. and others

Supreme Court of India

10 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, A.M. Khanwilkar, Mohan M. Shantanagoudar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 611; 2017 (5) AWC 4892; JT 2017 (8) SC 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Jurisdiction, Partition, Consequential, Declaratory Decree, Court Fees Act, 1870, Tamil Nadu Court-Fees and Suits Valuation Act, 1955, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Tamil Nadu Court-Fees And Suits Valuation Act, 1955, s. 40 - Sale deeds undervalued - Challenged - Plaintiffs filed suit seeking declaration that sale deeds executed in favour of appellants/defendants be null and void - Subsequently, appellants filed interlocutory application directing plaintiff to pay Court fees u/s. 40 of the Act - Trial Court dismissed application relying on SC case in Siddha Construction Private Limited v. M. Shanmugam and Others, 2006 Indlaw MAD 1454 - On appeal, HC affirmed judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC took note of the fact that when respondent no. 1 was not a party to the document, the relief sought for in suit would not come u/s. 40 of the Act. HC was not justified in holding that court fee is required to be paid on the sale consideration mentioned in sale deeds. SC might reiterate that proper valuation of suit property stands on a different footing than applicability of a particular provision of an Act under which court fee was payable and in such a situation, it was not correct to say that it has to be determined on the basis of evidence and it is a matter for the benefit of the revenue and State and not to arm a contesting party with a weapon of defence to obstruct the trial of an action. It was because the Act empowers defendant to raise the plea of jurisdiction on a different yardstick. Impugned orders passed by Trial Court and HC being unsustainable were set aside. Trial Court was directed to grant 3 months time to plaintiff to pay the requisite Court fee. Appeal allowed.
