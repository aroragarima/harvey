Oriental Investment Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 216; AIR 1969 SC 460; [1969] 72 ITR 408; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 46

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66(1) - Issue (A) Whether assessee company be treated as a dealer in investments and properties or as investor and whether profits and losses arising from sale of shares, securities and immovable properties of assessee company can be taxed as business profits? - Circumstances that a company had within its objects dealings in investments, shares and properties does not give it characteristics of a dealer in shares, it is relevant and not conclusive - Contention of appellant that questions applied for reference u/s. 66(1) were not appropriately framed was considered well founded - Question involved in this case was not a question of pure fact but was a mixed question of fact and law - Held, in interest of justice, question for reference was reframed and ITAT directed to make a fresh statement of case - Appeal allowed.
