Kathuria Public Schools and others v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Shiva Kirti Singh, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 148; (2016) 4 SCC 732; (2016) 4 SCC 735; AIR 2016 SC 1664; JT 2016 (2) SC 307; 2016(2) SCALE 466

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Administrator, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Land acquisition - Extension of time - Appellant school land was acquired by respondent - Appellant did not vacate acquired land - On respondents Contempt petition, appellant undertook to deliver vacant possession - Later appellant filed writ petition restraining respondent from taking over physical possession of acquired land on the ground that acquisition had been elapsed - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence, appellant filed appeal before SC - Appeal was disposed of by SC holding that since possession of land had already been taken over and contempt matter disposed of, the HC should decide writ petition of appellants afresh - Thus instant applications for extension of time for running school in spite of possession having been deemed to have been taken over by respondent.

Held, possession of the entire land stands handed over to respondent as on 30-4-2013. Nothing stands excluded. The school was taken over by the Administrator. Only further direction required is that the respondent may take over the school from the Administrator in accordance with law on which the Administrator appointed by the Court will stand discharged. Applications disposed of.
