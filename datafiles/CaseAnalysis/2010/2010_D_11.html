DLF Universal Limited and others v Director, T. and C. Planning Haryana and another

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1019; AIR 2011 SC 1463; JT 2010 (12) SC 496; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 792; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 235; 2010(12) SCALE 246

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Sewers, Haryana Development and Regulation of Urban Area Rules, 1976

Summary: Administrative - Practice & Procedure - Land & Property - Haryana Development and Regulation of Urban Areas Act, 1975 - Haryana Development and Regulation of Urban Area Rules, 1976 - Functions and duties of the Director Town and Country Planning and the power conferred upon him under the provisions of the Act and Rules - Functions and duties of the Director Town and Country Planning - Appellants were granted licence under the provisions of the Act and the Rules framed thereunder, for setting up residential colonies - Appellants entered into required agreements with the Governor of Haryana acting through Director Town and Country Planning - Appellants acting under the licence so granted and the agreements, commenced setting up colonies by dividing the land into plots and the plots were sold to various buyers - Mutual rights and obligations between the appellants and the plot/flat buyers was structured by the agreements voluntarily entered into by them and all terms and conditions, covenants were mutually agreed by and between the parties - However, Director without any notice whatsoever to any of the appellants issued the impugned directions - HC upheld the validity of the impugned memo and accordingly dismissed the Writ Petitions - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether statute confers any authority or jurisdiction upon the Director to meddle with the terms of agreement entered into by and between the owners and the purchasers of plots/flats? - Held, Director's functions and duties as well as power is completely structured by the statute and the Rules - Director is empowered to issue appropriate directions in order to ensure strict compliance of the terms and conditions of licence subject to which the colony is to be set up by the owner or colonizer - However, Director is not authorized to interfere with agreements voluntarily entered into by and between the owner/colonizer and the purchasers of plots/flats - Agreed terms and conditions by and between the parties do not require the approval or ratification by the Director nor is the Director authorized to issue any direction to amend, modify or alter any of the clauses in the agreement entered into by and between the parties - Further, there is no provision in the Act, Rules or in the licence that empowers the Director to fix the sale price of the plots or the cost of flats - Director is not authorized or empowered to review or evaluate the terms of contract and resolve the disputes, if any, between the owners/colonizers and the purchasers of plots/flats - Director is not empowered to meddle with the transactions and put any restriction on the rights of the owner/colonizer in the matter of sale and purchase of plots/flats - (B) Whether s. 5 of the Act and r. 11B r/w r. 26(2) of the Rules in any manner prohibit collection of additional charges characterized as 'extension fee' by the owner/colonizer? - Held, nothing in these provisions empowering the Director to issue the impugned directions prohibiting the owners to collect the extension fee for the delayed construction of buildings by the purchasers of the plots - (C) Transfer Fee - Whether owner/colonizer in law after obtaining full payments from the allottees was prohibited from transferring the plots to the nominees of the allottees and allottees' right to nominate another person as purchaser of the property can be denied by the colonizer? - Held, conveyance deed executed by the owner is the one which is executed either in favour of the allottee or his nominee as the case may be on which a proper stamp duty and registration fee is required to be paid - However, Director has no power under the Act or the Rules to issue any such direction altogether prohibiting such nomination of another person thereby substituting the allottee - (D) Maintenance Fee - Whether Director empowered to issue any directions, directing the appellants to stop charging maintenance fee from the plot/flat holders? - Held, Director has no authority or power under the Act to issue any directions directing the owners/colonizers to incur maintenance expenses, by deeming the same to be part of the internal development works covered by s. 2(i) - Impugned directions issued by the Director were beyond the limits provided by the empowering Act - Appeals allowed.
