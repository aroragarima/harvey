Deoki Panjhiyara v Shashi Bhushan Narayan Azad and another

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 540; (2013) 2 SCC 137; AIR 2013 SC 346; 2013 ALL MR (Cri) 1099; 2013 CRLJ 684; 2013 (2) CTC 232; 2013 (1) JCC 508; JT 2012 (12) SC 575; 2013 (1) MLJ(Crl) 137; 2013 (1) OLR 891; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 400; 2013 (1) RCR(Criminal) 338; 2012(12) SCALE 282; [2012] 11 S.C.R. 825

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Family & Personal - Protection of Women from Domestic Violence Act, 2005, s. 12 - Special Marriage Act, 1954, s. 13 - Maintenance - Void Marriage - Marital Status - Eligibility - Appellant-wife, was married to respondent and had filed a petition u/s. 12 of Act seeking certain reliefs including damages and maintenance - Appellant field application for interim maintenance which was granted by Trial Court and was affirmed by Sessions Judge - Aggrieved respondent had filed writ petition before HC, during pendency respondent sought recall of order passed by Trial Court on ground that his marriage with appellant was void on ground that at time of his marriage with appellant, appellant was already married to another - Trial Court rejected application, against which respondent filed revision before HC, which disposed of application by common order holding that marriage certificate was conclusive proof of First Marriage of appellant, which had effect of rendering marriage between appellant and respondent null and void and held that appellant was not legally wedded wife of respondent and she was not entitled for maintenance - Hence present appeals - Appellant contended that she denies her earlier marriage and even assuming marriage between appellant and respondent to be void still she was eligible for maintenance under Act, as they lived together and relationship in nature of marriage existed - Whether appellant was eligible for maintenance, even after it was proved that marriage was null and void - Held, if according to respondent, marriage between him and appellant was void on account of previous marriage of appellant, respondent ought to have obtained necessary declaration from competent court in view of highly contentious questions raised by appellant on aforesaid score - It was only upon a declaration of nullity or annulment of marriage between parties by a competent court that any consideration of question whether parties had lived in a 'relationship in nature of marriage' would be justified, in absence of any valid decree of nullity or necessary declaration court would have to proceed on footing that relationship between parties was one of marriage and not in nature of marriage - Mere production of a marriage certificate issued u/s. 13 of 1954, Act, in support of claimed first marriage of appellant was not sufficient for any of courts, including HC, to render a complete and effective decision with regard to marital status of parties and that too in a collateral proceeding for maintenance - Hence, interference made by HC with grant of maintenance in favour of appellant was not at all justified - Appeals allowed.
