Mithu Singh v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20216; (2001) 4 SCC 193; (2001) SCC (Cr) 668; AIR 2001 SC 1929; AIR 2001 SCW 1377; 2001 CRLJ 1820; 2001 (1) JCC 235; JT 2001 (4) SC 216; 2001 (2) RCR(Criminal) 396; 2001(3) SCALE 27; 2001 (3) SLT 166B; 2001 (3) Supreme 77; 2001 (1) UC 570

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 27 - Convicted u/s. 302/34 - Challenged - Relations strained between parties - Whether appellant shared common intention with other accused and whether conviction of appellant proper? - Held, no overt act shown regarding appellant and he is only shown to have come to house of deceased with other accused - There is a difference between common intention and similar intention - Nothing on record to show that appellant shared common intention - Conviction u/s. 302/34 set aside - As pistol is not shown to be licensed, and was also used, his conviction u/s. 27 cannot be found fault with - Conviction for s. 27 Arms act maintained - Appeal partly allowed.
