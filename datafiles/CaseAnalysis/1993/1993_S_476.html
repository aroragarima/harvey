State of Punjab and Others v Joginder Singh Dhatt

Supreme Court of India

12 July 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1782; AIR 1993 SC 2486; JT 1993 (Supp) SC 485

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Challenge to transfer of respondent from Hoshiarpur to Sangrur purely on administrative grounds - Mid-term transfer - Contrary to Govt. instructions - Within two years of the superannuation - Pendency of the inquiry - Whether High Court justified in quashing transfer order? Held, no - Courts have no jurisdiction to interfere with the order of transfer - HC grossly erred in quashing the order of transfer of the respondent - HC not justified in extending its jurisdiction under art. 226 in a matter where, on the face of it, no injustice was caused - Appeal allowed.
