Commissioner of Income Tax v Alom Extrusions Limited

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1832; (2010) 1 SCC 489; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 154; [2009] 319 ITR 306; JT 2009 (14) SC 441; 2009(14) SCALE 163; [2009] 15 S.C.R. 1154

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Superannuation Fund, Accrual System

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 43B - Deduction u/s. 43B - Prior to Finance Act, 2003, according to second proviso of s. 43B, payment made by employer towards contribution to provident fund or any other welfare fund was allowable as deduction, if paid before date for filing Return of income and necessary evidence of such payment was enclosed with Return of income - Government introduced Finance Act, 2003, by which second proviso stood deleted w.e.f. 1-04-2004 - According to Department omission of second proviso giving relief to assessee operated only w.e.f. 1-04-2004, whereas, according to assessee said Finance Act, 2003, operated w.e.f. 1-04-1988 retrospectively - Whether omission of second proviso to s. 43-B of Act by Finance Act, 2003, operated w.e.f 1-04-2004 or whether it operated retrospectively w.e.f. 1-04-1988? - Held, second proviso of s. 43B resulted in implementation problems, and which resulted in enactment of Finance Act, 2003, deleting second proviso and bringing about uniformity in first proviso by equating tax, duty, cess and fee with contributions to welfare funds - Once this uniformity is brought about in first proviso, then, Finance Act, 2003, which is made applicable by Parliament only w.e.f. 1-04-2004 would become curative in nature, hence, it would apply retrospectively w.e.f. 1-04-1988 - No merit in Department's appeal - Revenue's appeal dismissed.
