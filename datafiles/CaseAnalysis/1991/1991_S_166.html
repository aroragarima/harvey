Sevaka Perumal, Etc. v State Of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 683; (1991) 3 SCC 471; (1991) SCC (Cr) 724; AIR 1991 SC 1463; AIR 1991 SCW 1368; 1991 (2) Crimes 515; 1991 CRLJ 1845; 1991 CrLR(SC) 521; JT 1991 (2) SC 546; 1991(1) SCALE 914; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 711; 1991 (2) UJ 513

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300 and 302 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Appellants were accused of murdering young boys for gain as a means to living - Committed four murder in five years - Convicted by lower court but decision reversed by High Court -Proof of murder was in question as evidence was by an approver and petitioner appealed for benefit of doubt - Plea for commuting sentence - Held, offence must be proved by direct or circumstantial evidence although dead body may not be traced -The benefit of doubt does not enter in area of consideration of imposing sentence and commuted death sentence into life imprisonment on ground that accused were young men and bread winners of family consisting young wife, minor children and aged parents - Reasonable doubt - Not relevant for interference with sentence - Only in proof of offence - Appeal dismissed.
