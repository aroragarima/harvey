Brij Sunder Kapoor and Others v I Additional District Judge and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 259; (1989) 1 SCC 561; AIR 1989 SC 572; 1989 ALJ 354; 1989 (15) ALR 117; 1989 (1) AWC 45; JT 1988 (4) SC 529; 1989 (1) RCR(Rent) 43; 1988(2) SCALE 1418; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 558; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 558

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Authorisation, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, Cantonments (Extension Of Rent Control Laws) Act, 1957, Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent and Eviction Act, 1947, Uttar Pradesh Cantonments (Control of Rent and Eviction) (Repeal) Act, 1971, Cantonments (House-Accommodation) Act, 1923, Cantonments (Extension of Rent Control Laws) Amendment Act,1972

Summary: Rent Control - Cantonments (Extension of Rent Control Laws) Act, 1957, ss. 3, 3(1) and 3(4) - Uttar Pradesh (Temporary) Control of Rent, Eviction Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Building (Regulation of Letting, Rent, Eviction) Act, 1972, ss. 3(e) and 21 - General Clauses Act, 1897, ss. 14 and 21 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 245 - Notifications D/- 03-04-1972 and 01-09-1973 and 17-02-1982 - Cantonment Areas - (A) Notification under 1957 Act extending 1947 Act to cantonments in U.P. - Applicability of 1947 Act, considering existence of 1952 Act - (B) Power to extend Laws to Cantonment Areas - (1) If gets exhausted by single invocation - (2) Housing accommodation laws prevalent in State - Delegation of power to extend them - If excessive delegation - (C) Application u/s. 21 of 1972 Act - Disposal, by prescribed authority appointed u/s. 3(e) of Amended Act - Validity - Held, (A) Competent, as existence of Act held to be immaterial - (B) Does not get exhausted and ss. 14 and 21 of General Clauses Act apply - Does not suffer from vice of excessive delegation - (C) Valid - Appeal dismissed.
