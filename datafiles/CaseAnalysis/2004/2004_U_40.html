Tamil Nadu Kalyana Mandapam Assn. v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

15 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 291; (2004) 5 SCC 632; AIR 2004 SC 3757; 2004 (4) CompLJ 108; 2004 (188) CTR 297; 114 ECR 129; 2004 (167) E.L.T. 3; [2004] 267 ITR 9; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 66; JT 2004 (4) SC 568; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 118; 2004 (62) RLT 115; 2004(4) SCALE 647; 2004 (135) STC 480; [2004] Supp1 S.C.R. 169; 181 TAXATION 15; [2004] 136 TAXMAN 596

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Finance Act, 1998

Summary: Finance Act, 1994, ss.66, 67 (o); Service Tax Rules, 1994, r.2(1)(d)(ix) - Whether provisions in Finance Act, 1994 imposing service tax on services rendered by Mandap-Keepers are intra vires of Constitution - Whether 'Aspect Theory' laid in case of Federation of Hotel and Restaurant vs. Union of India & Ors. can be applied - Making available a premises for a period of few hours for specific purpose of being utilized as a Mandap whether with or without other services would itself be a service and cannot be classified as any other kind of legal concept - A tax cannot be struck down on ground of lack of legislative competence by enquiring whether definition accords what layman's view of service - Aspect theory upheld - Appeal dismissed
