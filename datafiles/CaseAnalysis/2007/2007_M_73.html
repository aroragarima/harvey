Regional Provident Fund Commissioner v M/S Raj’S Continental Exports (P) Ltd

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 191; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 37; (2007) 4 SCC 239; 2007 (6) AWC 6530; 2007 (2) CLR 755; 2007 (114) FLR 987; 2007 (6) KarLJ 547; 2007 (2) LLJ 553; 2007 (3) LLN 67; 2007(4) SCALE 129; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 636; 2007 (3) SLR 888

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Employees' Provident Funds & Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, s. 7A - Respondent claimed in-fancy protection - High Court held that respondent company and M/s Continental Exporters were not one and the same and merely because the proprietor of the one concern was the Managing Director of the other that by itself is not sufficient to establish that one was branch of the other - Appeal against - Respondent was separately registered under the Factories Act, 1948 - It was separately registered under the Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 and the Employees State Insurance Act - There was total independence of the two units - Single Judge and the Division Bench were right in their conclusion that the respondent is not a branch of M/s. Continental Exporters - Appeal dismissed.
