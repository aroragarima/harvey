Uddar Gagan Properies Limited v Sant Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Adarsh Kumar Goel

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 373; AIR 2016 SC 2526; 2016 (117) ALR 216; 2016 (4) AWC 3976; 2016 (2) CLT(SC) 267; JT 2016 (5) SC 389; 2016 (5) MLJ 82; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 131; 2016 (132) RD 393; 2016(5) SCALE 546; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 214

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Acquisition Of Land, Public Purpose, Haryana Development and Regulation of Urban Areas Act, 1975, Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1977, Constitution of India, 1950, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 48 - Haryana Development and Regulation of Urban Areas Act, 1975 - Acquisition of land - Doctrine of Public Trust - Haryana Urban Development Authority issued final notification u/s.6 of 1894 Act for acquisition of land and later award was passed and while so, appellant entered into collaboration agreements with land owners whose land was under acquisition on for development of colony in accordance with 1975 Act - Later, licenses were granted to appellant to develop colony and land was released from acquisition - Land owners filed petitions before HC wherein impugned judgment was passed by HC - Whether power of State to acquire land for public purpose was used to facilitate transfer of title of land of original owners to a private builder to advance the business interest of the said builder.

Held, there is no objection to acquisition of land for a compelling public purpose or to regulated development of colonies, but entertaining an application for releasing of land in favor of builder who comes into picture after acquisition notification and release of land to such builder tantamounts to acquisition for a private purpose. It amounts to transfer of resources of poor for the benefit of rich and amounts to permitting profiteering at the cost of livelihood and existence of a farmer and the same is against philosophy of Constitution and in violation of guaranteed fundamental rights of equality and right to property and to life. Further, when the land sought to be acquired for a public purpose is allowed to be transferred to private persons, any administrative action or private transaction could be held to be vitiated by fraud and there is no legal sanction for such action. Further, while notification u/s.4 of 1894 Act could not prevent creation of an encumbrance on the land, such encumbrance does not bind the Govt. Hence, there are no grounds to interfere with findings recorded by HC that there was an abuse of power in releasing land in favour of builder. However, order of HC is modified to extent that notification and Award are upheld but release orders in favour of builder in respect of land covered by Award in exercise of powers u/s. 48 are quashed and consequently all licenses granted in respect of land covered by acquisition stand transferred to HUDA. Further, sale deeds and other agreements in favor of builder in respect of said land are quashed and builder is not entitled to recover consideration paid to owners but will be entitled to reimbursement . Order accordingly.

Ratio - Under scheme of 1894 Act, land losers get compensation as on date of s.4 notification. Any transfer of title thereafter for release of land to a person who is not owner on date of notification u/s.4 of 1894 Act could be viewed as abuse of power u/s. 48 of 1894 Act.
