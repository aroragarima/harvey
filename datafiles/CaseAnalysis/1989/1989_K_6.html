Modern Hotel, Gudur Represented Bym.N. Narayanan v K. Radhakrishnaiah And Ors

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.N. Venkatachaliah

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 821; (1989) 2 SCC 686; AIR 1989 SC 1510; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 143; 1989 (1) RCJ 594; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 306; 1991 (1) RentLR 34; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 725; 1989 (2) UJ 153

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Buildings Control Act 1960

Summary: Rent Control - Andhra Pradesh Buildings (Lease, Rent and Eviction) Control Act, 1960, ss. 7 and 10 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 111(g) - Eviction - (A) Landlord holding big amount on tenants account for years against clear statutory bar - Failure of tenant to pay rent for some months - Whether liable to be evicted for being defaulter? - (B) Lease for 30 years - Absence of forfeiture clause - Contractual tenancy subsisting under provisions of Act - Eviction from such tenancy during subsistence of lease - Permissibility - Held, tenant could not be said to be defaulter, so not liable to be evicted - Not permissible - Appeal allowed.
