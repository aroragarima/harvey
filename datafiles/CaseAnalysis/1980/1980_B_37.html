State of Tamil Nadu v Binny Limited, Madras

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 90; (1980) Supp SCC 686; AIR 1980 SC 2038; 1981 (1) MLJ(SC) 2; 1982 (49) STC 17; 1980 UJ 895

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Taxability, Sales incidental to carrying on business

Summary: Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 2(b)(ii) - Sales incidental to carrying on business - Taxability - Respondent (assessee) was manufacture of textiles - Assessee claimed that there was no connection between the activity of manufacture of textiles carried on by assessee and sales of provisions in the store maintained by it nor was it possible to say that the sales effected by assessee were incidental to the manufacture of textiles - However, HC by impugned judgment held that sales of provisions effected by assessee in a workmen's store maintained by it are not assessable to tax under the Act - Hence, instant appea - Whether sales of provisions effected by assessee in a workmen's store maintained by it are assessable to tax under the Act.

Held, Court fail to see how a store run by the owner of a textile undertaking for sale of provisions to the workmen employees in the factory can be said to be anything other than incidental to the business of manufacture of textiles. The activity of selling provisions to workmen in the stores was incidental to the business of manufacture of textiles and the sales were, therefore, transactions falling within the definition of "business" in cl. (ii) of s. 2(d) of the Act. The assessee carried on business of selling provisions in the store and the sales attracted the liability to tax under the Act as it existed during the year of assessment. Impugned judgment of HC and order made by Tribunal are set aside - Order of sales tax Authorities holding that the sales in question were assessable to tax under the Act is restored. Appeal allowed.
