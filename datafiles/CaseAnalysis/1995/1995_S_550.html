State of Orissa and Others v Adwait Charan Mohanty and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1495; (1995) Supp1 SCC 470; (1995) SCC (L&S) 522; 1995 (29) ATC 365; 1995 (70) FLR 776; JT 1995 (2) SC 6; 1996 (3) LLJ 49; 1995(1) SCALE 410; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 614; 1995 (2) SLJ 199; 1995 (1) SLR 723; 1995 (1) UJ 626

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Orissa Service Code, r. 71(a) - Superannuation - Whether a Class III Govt. servant, on attaining superannuation age of 58 years, is required to retire or whether he is entitled to remain in service until he attains superannuation age of 60 years as a workman within meaning of Code? - Held, r. 71(a) of Code and second proviso and note appended to it must be read together harmoniously to give effect to every part of it - Object of Code appears to be to bring artisan-workman governed by statutory rules on a par with Class IV employee and he alone is required to retire on completion of 60 years under second proviso to r. 71(a) but not gazetted or non-gazetted Class III Govt. servants or even in Class II or I - Tribunal committed grievous and manifest error of law in not considering these cases in this perspective - It has solely and wholly concentrated on definition of 'workman' and 'industrial establishment' to give benefit of extended superannuation to respondents - Appeal allowed.
