State of Uttar Pradesh v Dharmendra Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.S.M. Quadri

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 731; (1999) 8 SCC 325; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1425; AIR 1999 SC 3789; AIR 1999 SCW 3854; 1999 ALJ 2402; 1999 (4) CCR 25; 2000 CRLJ 5; 1999 (2) JCC 567; JT 1999 (7) SC 207; 1999 (4) RCR(Criminal) 250; 1999(6) SCALE 113; 2000 (1) SCJ 55; 1999 (8) SLT 3; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 52; 1999 (8) Supreme 183

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 377(3),386 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 149, 302 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Respondents/accused along with 4 other accused murdered 5 persons including woman and children by causing multiple stab injuries - Trial Court convicted all accused by awarding life sentence to 4 accused and death sentence to respondents - All convicted accused filed appeal in HC challenging capital punishment and life imprisonment - HC upheld conviction of life sentence to all accused and death sentence of respondents was reduced to life imprisonment - Hence, instant appeals by State Govt. - Whether HC judgment was sustainable - Held, appeal filed by respondents in SC was dismissed - SC adopts analogous principles and follows 'fair procedure' depending on facts and circumstances of each case - Lower Courts below had considered entire material on record and have concurrently found respondents guilty of offence charged - Respondents with help of other 4 accused killed helpless woman and children without any enimity with them by inflicting 53 stab injuries on vital parts and denuding of lower part of body of woman amounts to barbaric act and showed perversity of respondents who totally lacked human sensitivity and Trial Court was justified in awarding death sentence to respondents - HC erred in not confirming death sentence and converting it into life imprisonment by wrongly concluding that they were in death cell which was totally incorrect - Sentence was matter of discretion following judicial lines and appellate courts should not interfere unless there were strong and cogent reasons - HC refusal to confirm death sentence awarded by trial court was factually incorrect and opposed to accepted legal principles - Sentence awarded by Trial Court was just and proper - Judgment of HC was set aside and confirmed death sentence awarded by Trial Court - Appeals allowed.
