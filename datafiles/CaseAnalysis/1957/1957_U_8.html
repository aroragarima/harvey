Â Puranlal Lakhanpal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

24 May 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Syed Jaffer Imam, A.K. Sarkar, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 40; AIR 1958 SC 163; 1958 CRLJ 283; 1958 SCJ 510; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 460

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, ss. 3 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21, 22(4) and 22(7) - (A)Whether u/art. 22(4) Advisory Board has power to determine length of detention? Held, no - Advisory Board only has to determine whether detention is justified or not - Decision as to period of detention must be of detaining authority - (B) Whether s. 11(1) of Preventive Detention Act, 1950 is violative of Constitution, in view of art. 22(4)(a)? - Held, expression 'such detention' u/art. 22(4)(a) refers to preventive detention and not to how long person is to be detained - Failure of Advisory Board to state that there is sufficient cause for detention of person concerned for more than three months is no violation of constitutional mandate - (C) Whether a particular order of detention made by an executive authority can be challenged on ground that it is vitiated by malafide intent ? - Held, yes - Malafide is established if order was made for ulterior purpose or purposes other than those mentioned in order - Appeal allowed.
