Pyare Lal Sharma and another v Managing Director and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 July 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 652; (1989) 3 SCC 448; (1989) SCC (L&S) 484; AIR 1989 SC 1854; [1990] 67 Comp Cas 195; 1989 (3) CompLJ 70; 1989 (25) ECR 6; 1989 (59) FLR 220; JT 1989 (3) SC 133; 1989 LabIC 1941; 1990 (1) LLJ 32; 1989 (2) LLN 292; [1989] 3 S.C.R. 428; 1989 (3) SLJ 1; 1989 (4) SLR 1; 1989 (2) UJ 526

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Industries Employees Service Rule and regulations

Summary: Service - Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Jammu and Kashmir Industries Employees Service Rule and Regulations, regns. 16 and 14 - (A) Whether regns. 16 and 14 is arbitrary and as such ultra vires art. 14 of Constitution? - Whether three months' notice or pay in lieu of notice period was required to be given? - Under this regulation services of an employee may be terminated (a) if his post is abolished or (b) if he is declared on medical grounds to be unfit for further service or (c) he remains on unauthorised absence or (d) if he takes part in active politics - It is clear that three months notice or pay in lieu, is only required when termination is under ground (a) or (b) - Regarding (c) and (d), regulation provides for a 15 days' notice to explain conduct satisfactorily - (B) Whether Managing Authority possessed sufficient authority to dismiss delinquent ? - Powers of Board of Directors to appoint officers of terminated employees category were delegated to Managing Director on September 12, 1974 and as such from that date Managing Director became appointing authority - There is no provision in Articles of Association or regulations of company giving same protection to employees of company as is given to civil servants u/art. 311(1) of Constitution - (C) Whether impugned order is violative of rules of natural justice so much so that ground of taking part in active politics was not mentioned in show cause notice? - Held, grounds (c) and (d) in regns. 16 and 14, exclusively and individually, are sufficient to terminate services of an employee - Finding in termination order regarding taking part in active politics cannot be sustained because no notice in this respect was given to delinquent employee but order of termination can be supported on ground of remaining on unauthorised absence from duty - (D) Whether period of absence, which was prior to date of coming into force of amended regns. 16 and 14, could be taken into consideration for invoking ground (c) of regulation ? - Further held, it is basic principle of natural justice that no one can be penalised on ground of a conduct which was not penal on day it was committed - Date of show-cause notice being April 21, 1983 unauthorised absence from duty which has been taken into consideration is from December 20, 1982 to April 20, 1983 - Whole of this period being prior to date of amendment of regns. 16 and 14 same could not be made as a ground for proceeding under ground (c) of regns. 16 and 14 - Civil Appeal No 3154 of 1985 partly allowed - Civil Appeal No 3155 of 1985 dismissed.
