Subhas Chandra Das Mushib v Ganga Prosad Das Mushib and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 56; AIR 1967 SC 878; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Civil Procedure - Contract & Commercial - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r.4 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 16 - Whether gift deed in question was executed under 'undue influence'? - To establish undue influence firstly relation between parties must be such that one is in a position to dominate will of other - Secondly, whether transaction has been induced by undue influence - Thirdly, onus probandi - If transaction appears to be unconscionable, then burden of proving exercise of undue influence falls upon person who was in position to dominate will - Unconscionableness of bargain is not first thing to be considered - Rather relation of parties and if it was such as to put one to dominate will of other - Merely because parties were nearly related undue influence cannot be presumed - Court must also, in first place, scrutinize pleadings to ensure that case of undue influence has been made out clearly with full particulars - A vague or general plea cannot suffice - Held, no ingredient of undue influence could me made out - Appeal allowed.
