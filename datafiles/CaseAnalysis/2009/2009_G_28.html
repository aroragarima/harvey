Gajara Vishnu Gosavi v Prakash Nanasahed Kamble And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1171; (2009) 10 SCC 654; 2010 (2) AWC 1277; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 388; 2009 (5) CTC 380; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 7; JT 2009 (13) SC 432; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 690; 2009 (4) RCR(Civil) 674; 2010 (109) RD 570; 2009(13) SCALE 33; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 569

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for declaration - Allegation of illegal occupation of suit property by respondents - Suit filed by appellant purchaser for obstruction free possession - Contention by respondent that suit property was not a partitioned property, therefore, predecessor-in-interest of appellant had no valid title - Suit dismissed by Trial Court - Trial Court's order affirmed by First Appellate Court - Orders of lower courts affirmed by HC also in Second Appeal - Legality - Undivided share of a co-parcener can be a subject matter of sale/transfer but possession cannot be handed over to vendee unless property partitioned by metes and bounds either by decree of court or by settlement among co-sharers - Held, no partition had been effected on suit property - Such finding of facts by all 3 courts below not required to be interfered as it was not perverse or based on no evidence or contrary to evidence on record - No interference - Appeal dismissed.
