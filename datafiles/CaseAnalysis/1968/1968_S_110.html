Â Minor P. Rajendran v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 341; AIR 1968 SC 1012; 1968 (2) MLJ(SC) 121; 1968 (2) SCJ 801; [1968] 2 S.C.R. 786; 1968 (2) SLJ 801

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 15(1), 15(4) and 21 - Madras Educational Rules, rr. 2, 3, 4, 5, 7, 8, 9 and 10(d) - State of Madras promulgated rules for selection of candidates for admission to First Year integrated M.B.B.S. Course - (A) Whether r. 5, which provides for reservation for socially and educationally backward classes is bad, as it violates art. 15 of the Constitution on the ground that it is based entirely on consideration of caste? - Art. 15 forbids discrimination against any citizen on grounds only of religion, race, caste, sex, place of birth or any of them - At same time art. 15(4) inter alia permits State to make any special provision for advancement of any socially and educationally backward classes of citizens - Case of State of Madras is that castes included in list are only a compendious indication of class of people in those castes and these classes of people had been put in list for purpose of art. 15(4) because they had been found to be socially and educationally backward - List shows certain castes, members of those castes are really classes of educationally and socially backward citizens - Held, though list is prepared caste-wise, castes included therein are as a whole educationally and socially backward and therefore list is not violative of art. 15 - (B) Whether district wise allocation u/r. 8 on ground that it violates art. 15(1)? - What exactly 'nativity' means is not clear from rule - Dictionary meaning of word 'nativity' is birth and when Rules provide for nativity certificate they really mean place of birth - However, it appears 'that place of birth of candidate is nowhere mentioned in Rules - Held, there is not a clear violation of art. 15(1) for district which candidate may claim does not depend upon place of his birth - R. 8 cannot be struck down on ground that it discriminates on basis of place of birth of candidate concerned - (B) Whether district wise allocation u/r. 8 on ground that it violates art. 14? - Art. 14 does not forbid classification, but classification has to be justified on basis of nexus between classification and object to be achieved, even assuming that territorial classification may be a reasonable classification - Held, question whether district wise allocation is violative of art. 14 will depend on what is object to be achieved in matter of admission to medical colleges - As object to be achieved is to get best talent for admission to professional colleges, allocation of seats district wise has no reasonable relation with object to be achieved - Further held, allocation of seats on district wise basis is violative of art. 14 - R. 8 providing for district wise allocation is bad, as it violates art. 14 and is struck down - Petition partly allowed.
