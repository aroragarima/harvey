Ram Lal v Sub Divisional Magistrate-cum-Executive Magistrate, Hoshiarpur and others

Supreme Court of India

03 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1830; 2004(1) SCALE 36

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Remuneration, Appointment of Court Receiver

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 145, 146, 482 - Appointment of Court Receiver - Remuneration - Executive magistrate appointed Tahsildar as receiver and fixed remuneration at 2% from total receipts from company - On application, HC held that no error in appointment of receiver whereas directed Magistrate to fix remuneration afresh - Magistrate fixed monthly remuneration of Rs. 3000/- from date of appointment - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal which was confirmed by HC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, Court appointed Receiver and it is his sole responsibility to manage and be answerable to the actual running of the concern during the relevant point of time. Keeping into consideration volume of work, the erroneous responsibilities involved in running the concern, the ordeals to be undertaken and the actual results and targets which seem to have been achieved also, the originally fixed remuneration of 2% commission on the receipts, cannot be said to be on the higher or arbitrary side and that the orders of the Sub-Divisional Magisrate-cum- Executive Magistrate and that the Single Judge of the HC wherein it has been reduced to a paltry sum of Rs.3,000/- per month cannot be approved and sustained as being either reasonable or commensurate with the actual services rendered by the Receiver. orders of the Executive Magistrate and that of the HC is set aside to the extent it fixed a flat rate of Rs.3,000/- per month and the original rate of 2% on the receipts shall stand restored. Appeal disposed of.
