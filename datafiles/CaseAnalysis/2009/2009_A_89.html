Secretary, M/O Defence and Others v Ajit Singh

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1851; (2009) 7 SCC 328; (2009) 2 SCC (L&S) 336; 2009(14) SCALE 413; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 934; 2011 (4) SLR 278

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Disability Pension

Summary: Service - Defence & Security Forces - Army Pension Regulations, rr. 173, 423 - Disability pension - Respondent filed a suit for declaration to effect that he is entitled to disability pension as during course of his service, he has sustained 20% disability on account of electric shock suffered by him while he was on casual leave - Trial Court held that Respondent was entitled to disability pension - On appeal, HC confirmed order of Respondent - Appellant contended that as respondent had not completed 10 years of service and had been discharged after four years eleven months and two days of service, therefore, there is no question of granting any disability pension - Whether HC's order could be upheld? - Held, as per SC judgement in Union of India & Others Vs. Keshar Singh 2007 INDLAW SC 394 and Union of India & Others Vs. Surinder Singh Rathore 2008 INDLAW SC 398, Respondent not entitled to disability pension as disability is not attributable to or aggravate by military service - Moreover, he had not completed period of requisite service and therefore not entitled to disability pension - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
