Â R. C. Jall v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 546; AIR 1962 SC 1281; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 436; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 436

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Emergency Provisions

Summary: Civil Procedure - Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950 - General Clauses Act, 1897 - Limitation Act, 1963, art.149 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 - Consignments - Recovery of cess - Barred by limitation - Maintainability - Central Govt. was authorized to levy and collect as a cess on all coal and coke dispatched from collieries in British India a duty of excise at such rate, not exceeding Rs.140 per ton - Appellant amalgamated Coalfields dispatched by rail three consignments of coal to appellant No.1 - Appellant No.1 took delivery of coal after paying freight, but by mistake cess payable as surcharge was not recovered from him at time of delivery of goods - Union of India representing Central and Western Railways filed a suit for recovery of mid cess - Appellants pleaded that levy was illegal and suit was barred by limitation - HC detained that it was within time and appellants were liable to pay cess - Hence, instant appeal - Whether suit was barred by limitation and appellants were liable to pay cess - Held, art.149 of 1963 Act r/w art.120 of Act applied and suit was within time - Ordinance collected and persons liable to pay duty - Central Govt. was authorized to apply Coal Production Fund to such purposes connected with coal industry - Consignee/Appellant was liable to pay it and that HC was wrong against him - Statutory liability of consignor of consignee must be understood to provide only for recovery of undercharges from persons statutorily liable to pay in accordance with principles governing railway freight charges - Appeals disposed of.
