Â Waman Rao & Ors. Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 300; (1980) 3 SCC 587; (1981) 2 SCC 362; AIR 1981 SC 271; 1980 KLT 578; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Constitutionality, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Defence Of India Act, 1971, Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, House of the People (Extension of Duration) Act, 1976, House of the People ( Extension of Duration) Amendment Act, 1976, Constitution (Fifth Amendment) Act,1955, House of the People (Extension of Duration) Act,1976

Summary: Constitution - Constitution (First Amendment)Act, 1951 - Constitution(Fourth Amendment)Act, 1955 - Maharashtra Agricultural Lands (Ceiling on Holding s) Act, 1961 - Constitutionality - Amendment - Ceiling on agricultural holdings - Validity of Articles - Ceiling on agricultural holdings was imposed in Maharashtra, which was brought into operation - Validity of Acts were challenged in Bombay HC in a large group of over 2660 petitions - HC repelled that challenge by judgment - Appeals were filed against decision of Bombay HC which were also dismissed - Fresh petitions were filed after proclamation of emergency was lifted and Court accepted for review of judgment - Whether Petitioners were entitled to relief against order passed by Bombay HC - Held, as per referred case amendment introduced by S.4 of 1951 Act did not damage or destroy basic structure of Constitution - Application of rule of stare decisions was held unnecessary - Amendments of Articles were held valid to extent to which its constitutionality were upheld - Petitions were dismissed with costs and stay orders granted were vacated - Order accordingly.
