Sheela Barse v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, R.S. Pathak, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 71; (1983) 2 SCC 96; (1983) SCC (Cr) 353; AIR 1983 SC 378; 1983 (1) Crimes 602; 1983 CRLJ 642; 1983 CrLR(SC) 207; 1983(1) SCALE 140; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 337

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Summary: Women & Children - Human Rights - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 21 and 39 - PIL - Custodial violence to women prisoners - In police lock up - Held, legal assistance to a poor or indigent accused who is arrested and put in jeopardy of his life or personal liberty is a constitutional imperative mandated not only by art. 39 but also by arts. 14 and 21 of Constitution of India, 1950 - It is a necessary sine qua non of justice and where it is not provided, injustice is likely to result and undeniably every act of injustice corrodes foundations of democracy and rule of law - Lawyers must realise that law is not a pleasant retreat, only concerned merely with mechanical interpretation of rules made by legislature but it is a teeming open ended avenue through which most of traffic of human existence passes - It is for lawyers to minimise numbers of casualties who go without legal assistance - Directions given by Courts regarding rights of prisoners - If directions given are carried out both in letter and spirit, they will afford considerable protection to prisoners in police lock ups and save them from possible torture or ill-treatment - Petition disposed of.
