Maharao Bhim Singh of Kota, through Maharao Brij Raj Singh, Kota v Commissioner of Income-tax, Jaipur

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 874; (2017) 1 SCC 554; AIR 2017 SC 142; [2017] 390 ITR 532; JT 2016 (11) SC 443; 2016(12) SCALE 500; [2017] 244 TAXMAN 139

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Bona Fide, Total Income, Let Out, Annual Value, Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, Rental Income, Wealth-Tax Act, 1957, Requisitioning and Acquisition of Immovable Property Act, 1952, Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971, Merged States (Taxation Concessions) Order, 1949, Income-tax Act, 1961, Part B States (Taxation Concessions) Order, 1950

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(19A), 22, 23, 256(1), 256(2) - Part B States (Taxation Concessions) Order, 1950 - Property requisitioned - Tax levied - Applicability - Central Govt. issued a notification dt. 14-05-1954 declaring appellant's two palaces as his official residences - Assessment proceedings were initiated against appellant regarding taxability of income derived by appellant from the part of property requisitioned by Defence Ministry - On appeal, Commissioner held that appellant was entitled to claim full benefit of exemption for his official residence u/s. 10(19A) of the Act - On further appeal, Tribunal affirmed Order of Commissioner - Aggrieved, respondents filed writ petition before HC - HC held that appellant was required to pay income-tax on the income derived by him from the portion let out under the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the exemption would cease to apply to let out portion thereby subjecting the income derived from let out portion to payment of income-tax in the hands of Ruler.

Held, view taken by HC in case of Commissioner of Income Tax v. Bharatchandra Banjdeo, 1985 Indlaw MP 55 and in Commissioner of Income Tax v. H. H. Maharao Bhim Singh, 2001 Indlaw RAJ 402 by rightly placing reliance on Bharatchandra Banjdeo's case, 1985 Indlaw MP 55, was correct view and SC found no good ground to take any other view. S. 23(2) and (3) of the Act uses the expression "house or part of a house". Such expression does not find place in s. 10(19A) of the Act. Equally, SC do not find any such expression in s. 23 of the Act specifically dealing with the cases relating to "palace". That significant departure of the words in s. 10(19A) of the Act and s. 23 of the Act also suggest that the Legislature did not intend to tax portion of the "palace" by splitting it in parts. Once assessee was able to fulfill the conditions specified in section for claiming exemption under the Act then provisions dealing with grant of exemption should be construed liberally because the exemptions were for the benefit of assessee. Therefore, there was no reason much less justifiable reason for Revenue to have pursued the same issue any more in higher courts. Though principle of res judicata does not apply to income-tax proceedings and each a/y was an independent year in itself, yet, in SC view, in the absence of any valid and convincing reason, there was no justification on the part of Revenue to have pursued the same issue again to higher Courts. There should be a finality attached to the issue once it stands decided by higher Courts on merits. Such principle applies to instant case on all force against Revenue. Appeal allowed.
