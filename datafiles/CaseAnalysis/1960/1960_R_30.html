Â Rameshwar Dayal v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 187; AIR 1961 SC 816; 1961 (2) SCJ 285; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 874

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Election

Keywords: Right To Practise, Stamp Act, 1899, Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, Legal Practitioners Act, 1879, Bar Council Act 1926 (38 of 1926), High Court (Punjab) Order 1947

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Election - Bar Council Act, 1926 - Indian Bar Councils Act, 1926, s. 8(2)(a) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Councils Act - Stamp Act, 1899 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 124, 217 and 233 - Legal Practitioners Act, 1879 - Representation of the People Act, 1951 - High Court (Punjab) Order, 1947 - Whether respondents 2 to 6 fulfilled the requirements of art. 233 of the Constitution, 1950 when they were appointed as District Judges? - Held, unless there is clear proof of a breach of a statutory rule in making any of the appointments under consideration, the point does not merit any discussion - Learned counsel was unable to point out any such statutory rule and except making a general grievance, that too many persons have been recruited from the Bar, he was unable even to substantiate that the one-third reservation made in favour of the service members has been violated - Appeal dismissed.
