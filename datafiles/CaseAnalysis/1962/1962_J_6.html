Jagannath Prasad v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 404; AIR 1963 SC 416; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 194; 1963 CRLJ 330; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 850; 1963 (14) STC 536

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 3, 3A and 14(d) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 471 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 195 (2) - Assessee firm not including transactions of purchase of vegetable ghee in his turnover on ground that purchases were from outside Uttar Pradesh - (A) Whether taking advantage of ineffective notification amounts to an offence u/s. 3A of Uttar Pradesh Sale Tax Act, 1948? - Held, it amounts to an offence - U/s. 3A, prior to its amendment in 1952, Govt. could declare tax to be payable at a single point but there were two requirements; there had to be a notification in official gazette declaring point at which tax was payable and in series of sales by successive dealers it had to be 'as may be prescribed' i.e., as may be prescribed by rules - Notification No. 1(3) d/- 8-6-1948 issued under s. 3A declaring that proceeds of sales of vegetable ghee imported from outside Uttar Pradesh shall not be included in turnover of dealer other than importer himself was ineffective in absence of any rules made under s. 3A - In trying to get benefit under ineffective notification issued under s. 3A appellants evaded payment of tax under s. 3, which they were in any case liable to pay, it could not be said therefore that no offence was committed under s. 14(d) of Act - (B) Whether to prove one case by best evidence, means to produce forged documents before court? - Further held, when a fact has to be proved before a court or a tribunal, and Court or tribunal calls upon person who is relying up on a fact to prove it by best evidence it cannot be a defence as to offence of forgery if that best evidence which in this case, was invoices turn out to be forged documents - (C) Whether a complaint by Sale Tax Officer u/s. 195 is necessary for taking cognizance of an offence under s. 471 IPC ? - Further held, committed Sales Tax Officer is not a Court within meaning of s. 195(2) CrPC and a complaint by him under s. 195 is not necessary for taking cognizance of an offence under s. 471 IPC - Appeal dismissed.
