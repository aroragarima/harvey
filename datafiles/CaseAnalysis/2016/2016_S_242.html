Dharam Pal v State of Haryana and others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 111; (2016) 4 SCC 160; AIR 2016 SC 618; 2016 (3) CalLT(SC) 77; 2016 (1) JCC 425; JT 2016 (3) SC 131; 2016(1) SCALE 635; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 194

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: CBI, Restriction, Further Investigation, Credibility, Right To Information Act, 2005, Permissibility, Transfer of investigation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 365, 376(2)G, 506, 201 and 120-B - Scheduled Castes and the Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989, s. 3 - Transfer of investigation - Permissibility - Accused were charged for offences u/ss. 363, 365, 376(2)G, 506, 201 and 120-B IPC and u/s. 3 of the Act - Trial Court acquitted accused - Appellants filed appeal before HC which is still pending - Assertions made by appellant have been negative by HC - Hence instant appeal - Whether a situation calls for issuance of direction for transfer of the investigation to the CBI.

Held, there was a request by Additional Chief Secretary for handing over the investigation to the CBI, that departmental action was taken against the investigating authorities for negligent investigation, that the concerned ASI has been reverted to the post of Head Constable, and that apart, certain material witnesses have not been examined by the investigating agency without any rhyme or reason. The reasoning of HC is as the trial has commenced, there cannot be a transfer of the case to another investigating agency. The purpose is, there has to be a fair investigation and a fair trial. The fair trial may be quite difficult unless there is a fair investigation. SC is absolutely conscious that direction for further investigation by another agency has to be very sparingly issued but the facts depicted in instant case compels to exercise the said power. It is the bounden duty of a Court of law to uphold the truth and truth means absence of deceit, absence of fraud and in a criminal investigation a real and fair investigation, not an investigation that reveals itself as a sham one. It is not acceptable. It has to be kept uppermost in mind that impartial and truthful investigation is imperative. If there is indentation or concavity in the investigation, can the 'faith' in investigation be regarded as the gospel truth? Will it have the sanctity or the purity of a genuine investigation? If a grave suspicion arises with regard to the investigation, should a Constitutional Court close its hands and accept the proposition that as the trial has commenced, the matter is beyond it? That is the 'tour de force' of the prosecution and if we allow ourselves to say so it has become "'id'ee fixe" but in our view the imperium of the Constitutional Courts cannot be stifled or smothered by bon mot or polemic. Of course, the suspicion must have some sort of base and foundation and not a figment of one's wild imagination. One may think an impartial investigation would be a nostrum but not doing so would be like playing possum. As has been stated earlier facts are self-evident and the grieved protagonist, a person belonging to the lower strata. He should not harbor the feeling that he is an "orphan under law". Hence, order of HC is set aside and it is directed that the CBI shall conduct the investigation and file the report before the Trial Court. Till the report by the CBI is filed, Trial Court shall not proceed with the trial. Appeal allowed.
