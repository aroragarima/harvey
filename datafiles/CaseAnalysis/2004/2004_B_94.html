Commissioner of Central Excise, Chandigarh-II v Bhalla Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 996; (2005) 8 SCC 308; AIR 2005 SC 2891; 2004 (173) E.L.T. 225; 2004 (65) RLT 444

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Intellectual Property

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 5(A) - Trade Marks Act, 1999 - Whether brand name or trade name should have been used by such other person in respect of specified goods of same kind or class as goods manufactured by assessees claiming exemption? - Notification clearly indicates that assessee will be debarred only if it uses on goods in respect of which exemption is sought, same/similar brand name with intention of indicating a connection with assessees' goods and such other person or uses name in such a manner that it would indicate such connection - Held, if assessee is able to satisfy assessing authorities that there was no such intention or that user of brand name was entirely fortuitous and could not on a fair appraisal of marks indicate any such connection, it would be entitled to benefit of exemption - Appeals allowed.
