Â Messrs Nand Lal Raj Kishan v Commissioner of Sales Tax, Delhi and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 122; 1962 (1) SCJ 154; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 283; 1961 (12) STC 324

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Sales Tax

Keywords: Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941, Tax Levied

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act, 1941,ss. 5(2)(a)(ii) and 8A - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 32 - Whether s. 8A of Bengal Finance (Sales Tax) Act is ultra vires Constitution? - Power of Commr. u/s. 8A is subject to condition that it must appear to him to be necessary so to do for proper realisation of tax levied under Act - Power to levy a tax includes power to impose reasonable safeguards in collecting it, and demanding security for proper payment of tax payable under Act, it is neither an arbitrary nor an unreasonable restriction - Amount that can be demanded as security must have relation to payment of tax for which person concerned may be or become liable under Act - Held, s. 8A is not ultra vires. - Appeal dismissed.
