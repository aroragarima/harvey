Additional General Manager/Human Resource Bharat Heavy Electricals Limited v Suresh Ramkrishna Burde

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1396; (2007) 2 SCC 152; (2007) 5 SCC 336; AIR 2007 SC 2048; 2007 (6) AWC 5919; 2007 (5) Bom.C.R. 57; 2007 (114) FLR 212; JT 2007 (6) SC 630; 2007 (4) MahLJ 1; 2007 (4) MLJ(SC) 515; 2007 (3) MPLJ 1; 2007(7) SCALE 112; 2007 (6) SCJ 812; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 388; 2007 (4) SLR 126

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Termination - Reinstatement - Complaint against respondent and others that they had produced false caste certificates and had thereby got appointment on vacancies which were reserved for members of Scheduled Tribes - Scrutiny Committee vide its report found that respondent belonged to 'Koshti' caste and he did not belong to 'Halba' Scheduled Tribe - Order was challenged by respondent by filing writ petition before High Court and same was finally disposed of - Whether judgment passed by HC was correct or not? - Principle, which seems to have been followed by court is, that, where a person secures an appointment on basis of a false caste certificate, he cannot be allowed to retain benefit of wrong committed by him and his services are liable to be terminated - However, where a person has got admission in a professional course like engineering or MBBS and has successfully completed course after studying for prescribed period and has passed examination, his case may, on special facts, be considered on a different footing - Skill acquired by him can be gainfully utilized by society - In such cases professional degree obtained by student may be protected though he may have got admission by producing a false caste certificate - No hard and fast rule can be laid down - Further court observed that if falsehood of caste certificate submitted by student is detected within a short period of his getting admission in professional course, his admission would be liable to be cancelled - However, where he has completed course and has passed all examinations and acquired degree, his case may be treated on a different footing - In such cases only a limited relief of protection of his professional degree may be granted - In this case respondent got appointment on a post, which was reserved for a member of Scheduled Tribe - On receiving complaints employer referred matter to District Collector and also to Scrutiny Committee - Subsequent period has been spent in making enquiry and in litigation as respondent filed three writ petitions - Held, respondent's services were rightly terminated by appellant and HC was in error in directing his reinstatement - Therefore, order passed by HC, has to be set aside - Petition dismissed.
