Style (Dress Land) and Others v Union Territory, Chandigarh and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 646; (1999) 7 SCC 89; AIR 1999 SC 3678; JT 1999 (6) SC 67; 1999(5) SCALE 74; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 591

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Capital of Punjab (Development and Regulation) Act, 1952 , ss. 3, 3(1), 22 - Tenancy - Rent increase - Challenged - Appellants were tenants of shops in shopping complex given of lease by respondents and lease deeds were executed - Respondents as per rental agreement enhanced rent from Rs.2671 to 14,000/- per month which was disputed and challenged by appellants by filing writ petitions in HC and one such writ petition was dismissed by DB holding that enhanced rent was in no way unreasonable or arbitrary - Appellants writ petitions were dismissed by HC directing respondents to consider appellants lease extension if agreed by appellants within 3 months and to pay total arrears with interest @18% within 6 months of judgment - Aggrieved appellants filed review petition and was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeals - Whether judgment of HC was justified - Held, as per ss. 3 and 22 of the Act respondent had obligation to make rules for regulating terms and conditions - Respondents action in absence of rules was not fair and could be termed arbitrary and discriminatory - SC in Ramana Dayaram Shetty vs. The International Airport Authority of India & Ors,1979 Indlaw SC 16 and in Punnan Thomas v. State of Kerala,1968 Indlaw KER 128 and in Erusian Equipment and Chemicals Ltd Vs. State of West Bengal,1974 Indlaw SC 171 held that Govt. should not act like individual in selecting recipients for its largess and should be subject to restraints in democratic society - HC had examined matter in depth and rightly concluded that procedure adopted for enhancing rent could not be termed as arbitrary or discriminatory or unreasonable - Enhancement of rents should be uniformly applied to all shopkeepers - Mere inaction against certain tenants could not be made basis for setting aside action initiated against the appellants - HC rightly issued appropriate directions to respondents for initiating similar actions against those tenants who were left out and was assured by respondents - SC in Chanigarh Administration & Anr. Vs. Jagjit Singh & Anr, 1995 Indlaw SC 1752 held that merely because respondent/authority had passed one illegal/unwarranted order it would not entitle HC to compel authority to repeat that illegality over again and again - Settled principle of law was that as and when party applies and obtains stay from Court it was always at risk and responsibility of that party applying - Mere passing of stay order could not be presumed to be conferment of any additional right upon litigating party - SC in Shree Chamundi Mopeds Ltd. Vs. Church of South India Trust Assn,1992 Indlaw SC 1284 held that order would not mean that order stayed had been wiped out from existence - SC in Kanoria Chemicals and Industries Ltd. & Ors. Vs. U.P. State Electricity Board & Ors,1997 Indlaw SC 2560 held that grant of stay could not relieve litigants from their obligation to pay late payment with interest when their writ petition was dismissed - SC in Kashyap Zip Industries vs. Union of India,1992 Indlaw SC 717 granted interest to Revenue for duration of stay under court's order since petitioners were benefitted of keeping back payment of duty under orders of Court - HC was right in directing payment of interest for arrears of rent during stay order but awarding 18% was not justified and hence reduced it to 15% - Respondents were directed to frame rules as per ss. 3 and 22 and then only could enhance rentals and till then respondents were not authorized to enhance rentals - Appeals disposed of.
