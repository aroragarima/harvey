K. Srinivas v K. Sunita

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 777; 2014 (10) ADJ 408; 2015(2) ALL MR 435; 2015 (108) ALR 742; 2015 (1) AWC 80; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 398; JT 2014 (13) SC 8; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4536; 2015 (1) OLR 267; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 38; 2014(13) SCALE 89B; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 298; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 162

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Marriage Laws (Amendment) Bill, 2013

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 148A, 307, 384, 324 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, ss. 4 and 6 - Decree of divorce - Filing of false criminal cases - Matrimonial cruelty - Appellant filed an original petition praying for divorce on the ground of cruelty as well as of the irretrievable breakdown of the marriage - Respondent-Wife retorted by filing a criminal complaint against the appellant and seven members of his family for offences u/s. 307 r/w ss. 34, 148A, 384, 324 of the IPC, and ss. 4 and 6 of the 1961 Act - Respondent-Wife also filed a petition u/s. 9 of the 1955 Act for restitution of conjugal rights - Family Court granted a divorce to the appellant on the ground of cruelty as also irretrievable breakdown of marriage; it rejected the respondent's petition u/s. 9 of the 1955 Act - Appeal filed thereagainst was allowed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the filing of a false criminal complaint sufficiently proves matrimonial cruelty as would entitle the injured party to claim dissolution of marriage.

Held, criminal complaint was filed by the wife after filing of the husband's divorce petition, and being subsequent events could have been looked into by the Court. In any event, both the parties were fully aware of this facet of cruelty which was allegedly suffered by the husband. When evidence was lead, as also when arguments were addressed, objection had not been raised on behalf of the Respondent-Wife that this aspect of cruelty was beyond the pleadings. The Respondent-Wife had filed a false criminal complaint, and even one such complaint is sufficient to constitute matrimonial cruelty. The marriage of the parties dissolved u/s. 13(1)(ia) of the 1955 Act. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - False criminal complaint is sufficient to constitute matrimonial cruelty and divorce can granted on this ground.
