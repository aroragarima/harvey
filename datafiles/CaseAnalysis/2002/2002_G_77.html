Darshan Singh and Others v Gujjar Singh (Dead) by Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 5; (2002) 2 SCC 62; AIR 2002 SC 606; 2002 (46) ALR 451; 2002 (1) CalLT(SC) 27; JT 2002 (1) SC 11; 2002 (1) MLJ(SC) 142; 2002 (131) PLR 233; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 260; 2002(1) SCALE 70; 2002 (1) SCJ 208; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 91; 2002 (1) SLT 119

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Keywords: Revenue Record

Summary: Possession of a property belonging to several co-sharers by one co-sharer shall be deemed to be adverse if, there has been a clear ouster of other co-sharers by denying their title and revenue record mutated in the name of one co-sharer after rejecting the claims of other collaterals.
