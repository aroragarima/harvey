A.P. Electricity Regulatory Commission v R.V.K. Energy Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 953; (2008) 17 SCC 769; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1196; JT 2008 (7) SC 138; 2008(9) SCALE 529; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 579

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Indian Electricity Act, 2003, Supply Of Energy, Commencement Of Supply, Tie-Line, Non-Licensee, Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1998

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Electricity Reforms Act, 1998; Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Whether Commission had the jurisdiction to issue directions to MPPs to supply power to APTRANSCO apart from its power to grant licence or grant exemption in terms of s. 14 of the APER Act, 1988 but also in exercise of its power to regulate supply and all that is contained in s. 11 thereof? - Held, if the State had accorded sanction for sale of electrical energy generated by the MPPs, the Commission save and except for cogent and compelling reasons could not have directed the sale of entire production of electricity energy to APTRANSCO - It is one thing to say that the privileges conferred by G.O.Ms. issued by the State Government were prior to the coming into force of the APER Act, 1988 and appointment of the Commission, but then the Commission was bound to give due weight to the policy decision taken by the State even prior to its establishment and coming into force of the APER Act, 1988, particularly when the Act was enacted in furtherance thereof - It was necessary for the MPPs to apply for licence under Section 14 of the Act - While considering the application for grant of exemption, the Commission did not have any jurisdiction to issue a direction that all MPPs must supply electricity to APTRANSCO only - Power and extent of jurisdiction of the Commission to regulate supply is a wide one but the same, does not extend to prohibition or positive direction that the supply of total energy produced must be made to APTRNASCO while exercising the said jurisdiction - Appeals disposed of.
