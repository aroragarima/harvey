Â Parshotam Lal Dhingra v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1958 SC 36; 1958 ALJ 372; 1958 (1) LLJ 544; 1958 SCJ 217; [1958] 1 S.C.R. 828

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Reduction In Rank, Quasi-Permanent, Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, Railway Fundamental Rules, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, 1949, Central Civil Services (Temporary Service) Rules, Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850, Indian Civil Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 311, 311(1), 311(2), 313, - Reversion - Compliance - Sustainability - Appellant joined service as Signaller (Telegraphist) and was selected for post of Assistant Superintendent Railway Telegraphs - Applicant relieved SR and GS made certain adverse remarks against appellant in his confidential report - Confidential report came before C.S.T.E. who confirmed views expressed by GS and added his own opinion which was also adverse to appellant - Remarks were placed before General Manager - Appellant made representation against remarks - Appellant appealed to General Manager for reconsideration and thereafter appealed to Railway Board and made representation also to President of India - Petitioner filed petition u/art. 226 of Constitution and order was held invalid for non-compliance with provisions of art. 311(2) of Constitution - On Appeal filed by UOI DB reversed order of HC and dismissed petitioner's application - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by General Manager amounted to reduction in rank within meaning of art. 311(2) of Constitution - Held, in instant case order of reversion was non-committal - Order merely said that BNC was appointed to officiate in appellant's place and that on relief appellant would revert to lower rank - Order does not stand alone and though various administrative notings were irrelevant, General Manager's remarks which formed real foundation of order, could not be ignored because sting lied there and two must be read together - Constitutional guarantees of art. 311 of Constitution could not be evaded by passing non-committal order that was innocuous and at same time making another order in secret that would have attracted art. 311 of the Constitution had it been made openly - Consequences of art. 311 could not be evaded by splitting up order into two parts - Hence, art. 311 of Constitution applied whenever any substantial evil follows over and above purely 'contractual one' - Appeal allowed.
