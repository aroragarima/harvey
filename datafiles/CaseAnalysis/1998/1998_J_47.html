S M F Sultan Abdul Kader v Joint Secretary, To Government of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 743; (1998) 8 SCC 343; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1534; 1999 (38) AllCriC 88; 1998 (3) AWC 1797; 1998 (3) CCR 4; 1998 (2) Crimes 339; 1998 (77) ECR 680; 1999 (1) JCC 55; JT 1998 (4) SC 457; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 376; 1998(4) SCALE 3; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 508; 1998 (5) SLT 296; 1998 (5) Supreme 107

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Whether order of detention passed against appellant u/s. 3(1) of COFEPOSA Act, 1974, was valid or not? - Unreasonable delay in executing order creates a serious doubt regarding genuineness of detaining authority as regards immediate necessity of detaining petitioner in order to prevent from carrying on prejudicial activity referred to in grounds of detention - Held, order of detention was passed by detaining authority was not in lawful exercise of power vested in him - Petition allowed.
