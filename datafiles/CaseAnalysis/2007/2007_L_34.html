National Insurance Co. Ltd v Laxmi Narain Dhut

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 179; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 142; (2007) 3 SCC 700; 2007 (2) ACC 28; AIR 2007 SC 1563; 2007 (3) ALD(SC) 112; 2007(3) ALL MR 834; 2007 (5) AWC 5394; [2007] 136 Comp Cas 678; 2007 (3) CPJ(Sc) 13; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2565; JT 2007 (4) SC 169; 2007 (2) KLT 470; 2007 (4) MahLJ 388; 2007 (3) MPLJ 25; 2007 (146) PLR 510; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 345; 2007(4) SCALE 36; 2007 (3) SCJ 684; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 579; 2007 (2) UC 1233

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Third Party Rights Against Insurer's Act, 1930, Consumer Protection Act, 1985

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 149 - High Court held that principles laid down by this Court in National Insurance Co. Ltd. v. Swaran Singh (2004 (3) SCC 297) is applicable even to claims other than third party claims - Appeal against - Held, decision in Swaran Singh's case has no application to cases other than third party risks - Where originally the license was a fake one, renewal cannot cure the inherent fatality - In case of third party risks the insurer has to indemnify the amount and if so advised to recover the same from the insured - Appeals allowed. 4. The concept of purposive interpretation has no application to cases relatable to Section 149 of the Act
