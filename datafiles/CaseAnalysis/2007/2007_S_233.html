Spic Pharmaceuticals Division v Authority Under Sec. 48(1) of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1394; (2007) 2 SCC 616; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 746; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 712; 2007 (5) ALD(SC) 18; 2007 (113) FLR 353; 2007 (2) LLJ 14; 2007(3) SCALE 703; 2007 (6) SCJ 510; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 428; 2007 (3) SLR 902

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service; Women & Children

Keywords: Appeals Disposed Of, Medical Representative, Industrial Disputes, Maternity Benefit, Maternity Benefit Act, 1961, Sales Promotion Employees (Conditions of Service) Act, 1976, Andhra Pradesh Shops and Establishment Act, 1988

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Andhra Pradesh Shops and Establishment Act, 1988 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965 - Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Sales Promotion Employees(Conditions of Service) Act, 1976 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Whether authority under Shops Act had no jurisdiction to entertain appeals preferred by employees as service conditions of employer were governed and regulated by provisions of Act which is a special enactment? - Held, Forums created under ID Act, 1947, on facts of case can more effectively deal with issues raised - It is not to be understood that appellate authorities under Act do not have jurisdiction - It specifically provided that forum under ID Act, 1947 can be approached - Concerned State Govts i.e. Karnataka, Tamil Nadu and Maharashtra shall make reference to appropriate forum under ID Act, 1947 within a month from today - Appeal allowed.
