New Mangalore Port Listed Workers Managing Committee Now Represented by New Mangalore Port Trust v Regional Director, ESI Corporation, Bangalore, Karanataka

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 734; (2015) 1 SCC 446; 2014 (143) FLR 934; 2015 (4) KarLJ 42; 2015 (1) LLJ 105; 2014(12) SCALE 358; [2014] 11 S.C.R. 428

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative

Keywords: ESI, Unloading, Esi Corporation, Major Port Trusts Act, 1963

Summary: Administrative - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 75(g) - Major Port Trust Act, 1963 - Notification - Appropriate Govt. - Notification was issued by the State Govt. to extend the provisions of the 1948 Act - Appellant objected to the said notification - Appellant approached ESI Court u/s. 75(g) of the 1948 Act claiming that the provisions of the 1948 Act were not applicable to them as they were governed by the provisions of 1963 Act and that the Central Govt. is the appropriate government with respect to the appellant-Committee - Petition filed by appellant, ESI Court by order, held that the notification of the State Govt. was not applicable to the Committee as the 'appropriate government' to extend the provisions of the Act to the said Committee is the Central Govt. - On appeal HC set aside the findings of the ESI Court and held that the Managing Committee is covered within the purview of the notification issued by the State Govt. - Hence instant appeal - Whether the rendering of services of loading and unloading by appellant-Management Committee to NMPT was an integral part of NMPT and whether the State Govt. is the 'appropriate government' to issue the notification -

Held, by a perusal of the judgment of the HC, it appears that the HC has not examined the testimony of AW1 and 2, and their evidence that prior to 15-3-1990 the workers were under the administrative control of the NMPT. The questions viz. (i) whether the workers of the Managing Committee were registered as Stevedores engaged in loading and unloading work of NMPT and whether they were under the administrative control of NMPT, (ii) whether the services rendered by the workers of the Managing Committee was an integral part of NMPT and if that be so, whether the 'appropriate government' is the Central Government and (iii) whether the workers of the Managing Committee were extended medical facilities and other benefits on par with other employees of the NMPT and other relevant questions remain unanswered. In SC's view, the HC has not considered the above questions in the light of the evidence of AWs 1 and 2. That apart, HC did not have the benefit of considering the notification issued by the GoI dt. 20-7-2009, extending the provisions of the Act to NMPT and the exemption granted by the GoI by its notification dt. 3-9-2010. Impugned judgment passed by HC is set aside and the matter is remitted back to the HC for consideration of the matter afresh. Appeal allowed.
