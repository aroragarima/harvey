Rameshwar Prasad and Others v Messrs Shyam Beharilaljagannathand Others

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 325; AIR 1963 SC 1901; 1964 ALJ 109; 1963 (2) ILR(All) 933; 1965 (1) SCJ 114; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 549

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ejectment Suit

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Suit for ejectment and recovery of rent filed against defendant - One of appellant died and appeal stand abated as far as that appellant is concerned - Whether High Court was right to have heard appeal of surviving appellants against joint decree? - Legal representatives of dead appellant cannot eject defendant from premises in suit - O. 41 r. 4 was not applicable as second appeal was filed by all plaintiffs against whole decree and it cannot be said that surviving appellants filed appeal as representative of dead appellant - Held, HC could not have heard appeal of surviving appellants when appeal of dead appellant had abated - Common right and interest is available in getting a decree of ejectment and such decree could have been on common to all of them - Appeal dismissed.
