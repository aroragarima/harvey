Padubidri Damodar Shenoy v Indian Airlines Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1127; (2009) 10 SCC 514; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1921; 2010 (3) AWC 2347; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 324; 2009 (3) CLR 666; 2009 (5) ESC 760; 2009 (123) FLR 679; JT 2009 (12) SC 108; 2009 (4) LLJ 399; 2009(12) SCALE 456; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 356; 2009 (6) SLR 19; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 315

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Service Regulations, regn.12 and 12(b) - Voluntary retirement - Interpretation of regn.12 of Service Regulations - Respondent no. 1 issued Voluntary Retirement scheme for its employees - Appellant applied for voluntary retirement under said scheme - Thereafter, appellant gave a notice u/regn.12(b) of Service Regulations - Appellant request for voluntary retirement from service was not acceded to by competent authority - Appellant's writ petition thereagainst was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Appellant contended that employer can stop voluntary retirement by not granting approval within period of notice and communicating same to concerned employee that employer was not giving his approval - Regn.12 of Service Regulations, inter alia, enables an employee to seek voluntary retirement on attaining age of 55 years or on completion of 20 years of continuous service by giving three months notice - Proviso appended to regn.12 provides that voluntary retirement u/cl.(b) of regn.12 'shall be subject to approval of competent authority' - Use of word 'shall' in proviso, prima facie leads to an inference that provision is imperative - It is followed by words, 'subject to approval' - Effect of use of words 'subject to' is to introduce a condition - Expression, 'shall be subject to approval' is indicative of its intendment that voluntary retirement applied by employees covered by cl.(b) of regn.12 is effective only upon approval by competent authority - Also notice of three months for voluntary retirement given by an employee covered by cl.(b) of regn.12 remains valid even if no communication is received within notice period but it becomes effective only on its approval by competent authority - Held, since notice for voluntary retirement by an employee who has not attained 55 years but has completed 20 years of continuous service, under proviso appended to regn.12(b), is subject to approval by competent authority and that approval was not granted, voluntary retirement of respondent never came into effect - Appeal dismissed.
