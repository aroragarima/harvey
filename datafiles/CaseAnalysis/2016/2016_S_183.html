FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Sheikh Sintha Madhar @ Jaffer @ Sintha etc. and another v State Represented by Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

13 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Amitava Roy

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 261; AIR 2016 SC 1844; 2017 (1) CalLT(SC) 9; 2016 CRLJ 2356; 2016 (3) JCC 1504; JT 2016 (4) SC 84; 2016 (2) OLR 131; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 818; 2016 (4) RLW 2725; 2016(4) SCALE 133; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 936

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conspiracy, Identification, Identity, Distance, Indian Penal Code, 1860

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 302, 201, 120B - Alleged Conspiracy - Murder - Conviction - It is alleged that accused 13 in numbers hatched conspiracy to kill deceased person who was involved in propagation of Hindu religion - Further, it is stated that accused persons formed unlawful assembly and assaulted deceased person with weapon which resulted in his death - Trial Court vide order convicted appellant accused 7 in nos. u/ss. 148,302,201,120B of IPC, however, other accused person were acquitted of all charges - HC confirmed order passed by Trial Court - Whether impugned order passed by HC against accused person is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to statements of PW1 daughter of deceased and PW65 neighbour of deceased who witnessed incident, SC held that statement are not contradictory to each other, and are complementary to each other. Further, SC agreed with view of HC that PW1 could have seen assailants at night because area was illuminated by electric lights all around. Further, PW1 identified all seven accused appellants in Court and in TIP. Further, SC held that conspiracy before incident is proved by PW36 who heard discussion between accused persons to kill deceased person which is collaborated by statements of PW23 and PW24 who stated their presence at place around that time. Further, PW 36 and PW 37 saw injury on accused no. 3 on day of incident which was supported by PW17 who treated accused person. Further, with respect to conviction of accused person no.7 for offences u/s. 109 read with s. 302 IPC, SC held that when accused nos. 8 to 13 had been acquitted of his charges, hence, accused no.7 is also acquitted as charges against him are not proved beyond reasonable. Hence, SC held that conviction of accused person by lower Courts is justified and in accordance with law, however, conviction of accused no.7 is set aside. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Conviction of accused person based on evidence on record and testimony of witness is justified and in accordance with law.
