Uptron India Limited v Shammi Bhan and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1692; (1998) 6 SCC 538; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1601; AIR 1998 SC 1681; 1998 (1) CLR 1043; 1998 (79) FLR 233; JT 1998 (3) SC 47; 1998 LabIC 1545; 1998 (1) LLJ 1165; 1998 (2) LLN 959; 1998 (3) MLJ(SC) 76; 1998(2) SCALE 586; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 719; 1998 (3) SLJ 223; 1998 (2) SLR 544; 1998 (4) SLT 152; 1998 (5) Supreme 203; 1999 (1) UPLBEC 778

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Employment (Standing Order ) Act, 1946

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art.311 - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 35 - Industrial Employment (Standing Orders) Act, 1946, s. 5 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(oo), 2(bb) and 25(F) - Whether termination of Govt. employee from service either by giving him a month's or three months' notice, or pay in lieu thereof or even without notice, is permissible - Whether wrong concession on question of law made by counsel, is a binding on his client - Whether certified standing orders, having clause providing for automatic termination of service of permanent employee, having no provision for opportunity of hearing to delinquent employee, is valid - Whether termination of employee of statutory corporation from services by mere issuing letter as per certified standing orders and non-compliance with other legal requirements, amounts to retrenchment - Held, it is not permissible - It is not binding on his client - Clause is invalid - It amounts to 'retrenchment' within meaning of s. 2(oo).
