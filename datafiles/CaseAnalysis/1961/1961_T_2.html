Additional Income Tax Officer, Salem v E. Alfred

Supreme Court of India

20 October 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1962 SC 663; [1962] 44 ITR 442; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 54; 1962 (2) SCJ 156; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 143; [1962] Supp1 S.C.R. 143

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 46(1), 24B(2) and 29 - (A) Does "LR" fall within ' words other person' in s. 29 of Income Tax Act, 1922? - Held, no - S. 29 makes no difference in liability of LR to penalty u/s. 46(1) - If LR is an 'assessee' by fiction, then fiction has to be worked out to its logical conclusion - Words 'other person' cannot apply to a LR - (B) Whether penalty can be imposed on LR if he makes a default in payment of tax u/s. 24b(2) of Income Tax Act, 1922? - Further held, yes, it can be imposed - Fiction making LR an assessee doesn't come to an end after assessment - Fiction can't be limited by provisions of law for a totally different position - Penalty can be imposed on LR - Appeal allowed.
