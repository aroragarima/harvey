K. L. Verma v State and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1762; (1998) 9 SCC 348; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1031; JT 1998 (8) SC 521

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 197 and 438 - Order of High Court granting anticipatory bail - HC directed notice to issue on question - Whether sanction u/s. 197? - Refused to grant stay of further proceedings but merely issued notice on stay application - Challenged - Held, anticipatory bail may be granted for a duration which may extend to date on which bail application is disposed of or even a few days thereafter to enable accused persons to move higher court, if they so desire - Further held, proper course for HC was to decide on question of requirement of sanction and if HC could not do so, to have stayed further proceedings till that vital question was answered - Directed that till HC decides question of sanction u/s. 197 CrPC, further proceedings in trial court shall stand stayed - Petition disposed of.
