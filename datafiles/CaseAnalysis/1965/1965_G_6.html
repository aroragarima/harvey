Dr. Jagjit Singh v Giani Kartar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 499; AIR 1966 SC 773; 1967 (1) SCJ 762

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, ss. 116(A), 83(1)(a), 92, 123(1)(A)(a), (b) and 100(1)(d)(iv) - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 123(4), O. 8, r. 5 - (A) Whether appellant was right in contending that findings recorded by Tribunal in favour of respondent No. 1 on two issues were justified? - Both Tribunal and High Court have elaborately considered oral evidence led by parties and have examined probabilities in case and conduct of parties respectively - It appears from these findings that neither HC, nor Tribunal was satisfied that it would be safe to accept evidence adduced by appellant and hold that respondent No. 1 was guilty of charge of excessive expenditure or of offering a bribe to P W 31 - (B) Whether strict rule of pleadings prescribed by O. 8, r.5 applies to election proceedings with all it rigour? - Held, it is true that s. 90 of the Act provides that subject to provisions of this Act and of any rules made there under, every election petition shall be tried by Tribunal, as nearly as may be, in accordance with procedure applicable under CPC, 1908, to trial of suits - What section provides is that proceedings should be tried 'as nearly as may be' according to the CPC, 1908 - Having regard to nature of corrupt practice which is prescribed by s. 123(4), strict rule of pleadings prescribed by O. 8, r. 5 of Code can be blindly invoked in election proceedings of this type - Appeal dismissed.
