Sahebrao and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 189; (2006) 9 SCC 794; AIR 2006 SC 2002; 2006 CRLJ 2881; 2006 (2) JCC 871; JT 2006 (11) SC 260; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 855; 2006(5) SCALE 172; 2006 (5) SCJ 686; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Women & Children - IPC, 1860, ss. 304-B, 306, 498-A - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 113-A - Trial Court found appellants guilty - Sentenced them to undergo RI for three years and fine of Rs. 500/- - HC confirmed sentence passed by trial Court - Appeal against - Held, facts clearly establish that husband and his elder brother subjected deceased to cruelty - Prosecution sufficiently proved by cogent evidence that accused-appellants by series of acts and conduct created such a difficult and hostile environment for deceased that she was compelled to commit suicide - HC has rightly upheld conviction of accused-appellants u/ss. 306, 498 - Appeals dismissed.
