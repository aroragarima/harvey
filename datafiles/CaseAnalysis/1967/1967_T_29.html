Jamatraj Kewalji Govani v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 122; AIR 1968 SC 178; 1968 CRLJ 231; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 415

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Customs Act, 1962 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Seized - Summons - Custom Authorities - Shop of the appellant was searched by the Enforcement branch of Reserve Bank of India - Nothing incriminating was found in the shop but some items were recovered - Officers of the Enforcement Branch appear to have informed the customs authorities - It was stated in the warrant that there were reasons to believe that prohibited and dutiable goods liable to confiscation and documents and things useful for and relevant to the proceedings were secreted in the shop - Officers were accordingly charged with the duty to search and seize such prohibited and dutiable goods, documents and things in the shop u/s. 110 of the 1962 Act - Search was carried out and goods were seized - Watches were seized among other articles - Summons u/s. 108 was issued to appellant - Whether the court had exceeded its jurisdiction in acting under the second part of s. 540 of the CrPC - Held, this was not a case in which the prosecution was trying to fill a gap in the prosecution case - Court was right in thinking that a just decision of the case required that the nature of the belief underlying the seizure should be before it on oath of the person making the seizure so that appellant might be required, as the policy of the Customs Act, 1962 requires, to prove his innocent possession - Appellant had really no defense in view of the Control Order of 1955 and the gap of time between the promulgation of the order and the date of the seizures - So it could not be said that the court had exceeded its jurisdiction in acting under the second part of s. 540 of the CrPC - Appeal dismissed.
