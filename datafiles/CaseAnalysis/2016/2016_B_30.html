Balveer Singh and another v State of Rajasthan and another

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 351; (2016) 6 SCC 680; AIR 2016 SC 2266; 2016 (3) JCC 2085; JT 2016 (5) SC 1; 2016 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2307; 2016 (3) MLJ(Crl) 235; 2016 (3) RLW 2340; 2016(5) SCALE 59; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Suicide, FIR, Jurisdiction, Final Report, Summons, Cognizance, Police Report, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306, 498A - Application for cognizance of offence rejected - Second Cognizance by Trial Court - Justification - It is alleged that deceased was murdered by accused husband and appellants i.e. his parents for not satisfying their dowry demands - FIR was lodged u/ss. 304-B and 498-A of IPC - Charge-sheet was filed against appellant for committing offence u/s. 306 of IPC, i.e. abetting suicide committed by deceased - Magistrate dismissed application of respondent for taking cognizance for offence punishable u/ss. 304-B and 498A of IPC, however, Trial Court allowed application of respondent , hence, he directed issuance of bailable warrant against appellants - HC dismissed revision filed by appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, situation in present case is not where investigation report/charge sheet filed u/s. 173(8) of CrPC implicated appellants and appellants contended that they are wrongly implicated. Further, police itself had mentioned in its final report that case against appellants had not been made out. Further, SC held that order of Magistrate was passed with due application of mind whereby he refused to take cognizance of alleged offence against appellants and confined it only to son of appellants and order of Magistrate was not challenged. Further, SC held that proper opportunity was given to appellants who had filed reply to application of complainant and Trial Court had also heard their arguments. Hence, SC held that order of Magistrate refusing to take cognizance against appellants is revisable. Impugned order passed by Trail Court is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - When offence is exclusively triable by Trial Court, Magistrate must commit case to Trial Court and cannot refuse to take cognizance of offence and acquit accused on basis of material produced before it.
