Pankaj Bhargava and Another v Mohinder Nath and Another

Supreme Court of India

11 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, N.D. Ojha, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 551; (1991) 1 SCC 556; AIR 1991 SC 1233; JT 1990 (4) SC 628; 1991 (2) RCJ 112; 1993 (1) RCR(Rent) 96; 1991 (1) RentLR 87; 1990(2) SCALE 1251; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 508

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 21 and 39 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Property - Limited tenancy - Appeal - Substantial question of law - (A) Construction of disputed document which directly and substantially affecting rights of parties - (B) Permission challenge on ground of fraud - Held, can be considered as substantial one between parties - Collateral attack is not permissible - Appeal dismissed.
