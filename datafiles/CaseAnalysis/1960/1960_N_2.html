Â Sant Ram(In Re) v Na

Supreme Court of India

07 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 196; AIR 1960 SC 932; 1961 (1) AnWR 1; 1961 (1) KerLR 80; 1961 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; 1961 (1) SCJ 98; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 499

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Supreme Court Rules 1950

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 145 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 409 - Supreme Court Rules, 1950, O. IV-A, rr. 24 and 24(2) - Professional misconduct - Expression 'Tout' - Rule making power - (A) Whether Supreme Court has jurisdiction to frame rules u/art. 145 ? - Held, court is competent to frame rules regulating conduct in and out of court bearing on professional activities of an advocate of Supreme Court and has inherent jurisdiction to regulate its proceedings relating to conduct of persons appearing before it, in and out of court - Apart from this, Constitution itself has authorised court to make rules for regulating generally practice and procedure of court - (B) Whether r. 24 of Supreme Court rules is ultra vires rule-making power of court? - When apex court, as in r. 23, provides that an advocate shall be guilty of professional misconduct if he accepts an engagement in any legal business through a person included in list of touts, such a rule cannot be said to be beyond rule-making powers of apex court - With a view to enforcing that rule, a 'tout' was to be defined, which is done by explnation to r. 23 - Further held, r. 24, which lays down procedure for publishing lists of touts, for holding an enquiry to determine whether or not a particular person should be included in such a list must be equally within purview of rulemaking power of this court - Held, therefore, it is futile to contend that r. 24 in question is ultra vires rule- (C) Whether r. 24 infringes arts. 14, 19 and 21 of constitution? - All persons who are included in list of touts u/r. 24 will be liable to be dealt with in same way irrespective of any other considerations - Hence there is no room for any discrimination so far as precincts of this court are concerned - Again rule, made with a view to ensure purity and soundness of profession of law so far as advocates of apex court are concerned - If appellant has been rightly declared to be a tout, he cannot justly complain that he is being deprived of right to carry out his occupation, an occupation which is regarded as having a corrupting influence - A tout as such cannot claim any rights in relation to business of court and it is incumbent on every court where legal practitioners are allowed to appear and plead to see that toutism is completely eliminated - Appeal dismissed.
