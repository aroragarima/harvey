Sunder Lal v Paramsukhdas

Supreme Court of India

25 August 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 426; AIR 1968 SC 366; 1968 (70) BomLR 353; 1969 MahLJ 610; 1969 MPLJ 598; 1968 (1) SCJ 685; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 362

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18, 20, 21 and 53 - Berar Regulation of Agricultural Leases Act, 1951 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115 - (A) Whether Paramsukhdas is not a person interested in compensation and is not entitled to be impleaded as a party to references u/s. 18 of Act? - U/s. 18 any person interested can claim a reference, a person claiming an interest in compensation would also be entitled to claim a reference and is entitled to be heard u/ss. 20 and 21 - Held, Paramsukhdas was clearly a person interested in objections pending before Court in references made to it and that he was also a person whose interest would be affected by objections within s. 21 and was accordingly entitled to be made a party - (B) Jurisdiction of High Court u/s. 115 CPC to interfere with orders of Civil Judge? - Further held, HC is quite right in holding that orders of Civil Judge were not awards within s. 54 of Act and awards had still to be made - If no appeal lies, then revisions were competent and HC was right in entertaining revisions because Civil Judge had either refused to exercise jurisdiction vesting in him or had acted with material irregularity in exercise of his jurisdiction - Appeal dismissed.
