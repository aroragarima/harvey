Â Corporation of Calcutta v Calcutta Tramways Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 130; AIR 1964 SC 1279; 1964 CRLJ 354; [1964] 5 S.C.R. 25

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 13, 19(1)(g), 19(6), and 245 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, s. 437(1) (b)-Reasonable restriction on trade - Validity of Calcutta Municipal Act (33 of 1951) S. 437(1)(b) - Whether s. 437(1)(b) of Calcutta Municipal Act amounts to an unreasonable restriction on right to carry on trade etc., enshrined in art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution? - Conferment of such a power on a municipal body which has effect of imposing restrictions on carrying on trade etc. cannot be said to be a reasonable restriction within meaning of art. 19(6) - Such a provision puts carrying on trade by those residing within limits of Municipal Corporation entirely at its mercy, if it chooses to exercise that power capriciously, arbitrarily or unreasonable, though not mala fide - Held, s. 437(1)(b) of Calcutta Municipal Act consisting of words, 'which opinion shall be conclusive and shall not be challenged in any court' which makes opinion of corporation conclusive and non-justiciable in any court amount to an unreasonable restriction on right to carry on trade etc., enshrined in art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution - Matter remitted to Magistrate.
