T. Narayanappa (D) by Lrs. and others v Teekya Naika and others

Supreme Court of India

27 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 2219; (2000) 10 SCC 363

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prohibition of Transfer of Certain Lands) Act, 1978 - Purchaser of property - Petitioner referred few judgments of SC and raised contentions regarding validity of relevant provisions of 1978 Act in respect of right of purchaser of property from allottee of land under Act - Petitioner also claimed that there was contrary opinion was expressed by various Benchwes of this Court in subject matter - Hence instant petition.

Held, first judgment was 1984 Indlaw SC 139 where a three-Judge Bench of Court upheld validity of Act. Subsequently, in 1994 Indlaw SC 1412 three-Judge Bench of this Court stated that purchaser from SC/ST grantee could acquire title by adverse possession for 12 years in case grantee-vendor was vested under grant with absolute ownership. However, if land was not granted to vendor absolutely but Government retained title, then adverse possession for 30 years would be necessary. While things stood thus, two-Judge Bench of Court in Papaiah case observed that any number of years of adverse possession, be it 12 years or 30 years, would not help purchaser from SC/ST grantee inasmuch as there was principle of public policy which precluded acquisition of title by adverse possession. But in R. Chandevarappa case, two-Judge Bench of Court observed that, in facts of case, there was no plea of adverse possession for 30 years and no relief could be granted to purchaser. Again, in D.N. Venkatarayappa case, case in which notice on review petition has since been ordered, two-Judge Bench of Court observed that adverse possession was not pleaded nor proved and hence no relief could be granted. These two cases R. Chandevarappa and D.N. Venkatarayappa proceed on basis that there could be adverse possession for 30 years and appear prima facie to go against what was laid down in Papaiah case. But, at same time, they do not appear to be inconsistent with judgment in K.T. Huchegowda case rendered by three Judges. Inasmuch as D.N. Venkatarayappa case is already under review, Court direct that this matter may be tagged on with said Review Petition No. 1861 of 1997 in CA No. 4616 of 1997 leaving to that Bench to consider if matters have to go before three-Judge Bench to consider if Papaiah case was correctly decided. Order accordingly.
