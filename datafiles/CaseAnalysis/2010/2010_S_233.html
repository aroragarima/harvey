Sawaran Lata Etc. v State Of Haryana & Ors

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 272; (2010) 4 SCC 532; AIR 2010 SC 1664; 2010 (80) ALR 315; 2010 (3) AWC 2443; JT 2010 (3) SC 602; 2010 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1532; 2010 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 1590; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 121; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 695; 2010 (110) RD 407; 2010(3) SCALE 583; 2010 (2) UPLBEC 1540

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 11 - Challenge to acquisition proceedings - Time-barred - Petitions filed challenging the land acquisition proceedings was dismissed on the ground of delay - Review Petitions filed with delay of 48 days' were also dismissed - Hence, present petitions, filed with 172 days' delay - Whether the acquisition proceedings can be challenged at a belated stage? - Held, in a number of decisions of SC its been held that when a person challenges s. 4 Notification on any ground, it should be challenged within a reasonable period, and if the acquisition is challenged at a belated stage, the petition deserves to be dismissed only on this count - In the present case, ground taken by petitioners that they were not aware of acquisition proceedings cannot be accepted - Petitions dismissed on the ground of delay - Petitions dismissed.
