Assistant Collector of Central Excise v Jainson Hosiery Industries

Supreme Court of India

27 July 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, D.A. Desai, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 384; (1979) 4 SCC 22; (1979) SCC (Cr) 896; AIR 1979 SC 1889; 1979 (4) E.L.T. 511; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 134

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Act, 1944 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Seizure of goods - Relief (A) Determination of legality of interference by High Court under writ jurisdiction - (1) When an alternative statutory remedy was available - (2) When criminal investigations were pending - Held, unless normal statutory remedy is likely to be too dilatory or difficult to give reasonably quick relief, HC should be loath to act u/art. 226 - HC's will bear in mind need for extreme reluctance when, during investigation, any relief, interim or final, which has a tendency to slow down or otherwise hamper investigation, is sought - (B) Investigation - Considerations while releasing goods - Further held, courts must be very careful to see that every condition or need that investigator points out as essential for discharging his investigative functions, should be readily conceded by Court unless plainly unreasonable - Petition dismissed.
