State Of Assam v Mafizuddin Ahmed

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 60; (1983) 2 SCC 14; (1983) SCC (Cr) 325; AIR 1983 SC 274; 1983 (1) Crimes 380; 1983 CRLJ 426; 1983 CrLR(SC) 163; 1983(1) SCALE 16; [1983] 2 S.C.R. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s.302 - Death by burning alive - Credibility of evidence of dying declaration child witness - High Court did not find it safe to convict the respondent on the basis of dying declaration and the statement of child , son of deceased - Validity of High Court order setting aside conviction on its basis - Held, the cumulative effect of all the circumstances is that they cast doubt about the truthfulness of the dying declaration - It is not outside the realm of probability that her statement may have been inspired by her uncle and, therefore, it will not be safe to base the conviction of respondent on such a dying declaration and the fact that child was tutored is fully borne out by his own statement - HC held was right in setting aside conviction - Appeal dismissed.
