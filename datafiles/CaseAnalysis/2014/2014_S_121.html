Vikram Bakshi and others v Sonia Khosla (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 337; [2014] 184 Comp Cas 392; 2014 (3) CompLJ 264; JT 2014 (8) SC 123; 2014(6) SCALE 514; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 762

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Uniform Model Mediation Act

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 340, 195(i)(b)(ii) - CLB - Impugned order - Legality - Company Law Board (CLB) passed orders dt. 31-1-2008 directing the maintenance of status quo with regard to the shareholding and the Directors of the Company as it existed on the date of the filing of the petition i.e. 13-8-2007 - Observations were made in said order that respondent had tried to overreach the CLB by changing it composition and to increase the share capital of the Company - On appeal filed by respondent before HC, HC dismissed the appeal - Review petitions were also dismissed by HC - Respondent filed an application u/s. 340 CrPC before the CLB alleging that forged documents were filed before the CLB - Respondent sought prosecution of petitioners u/s. 195(i)(b)(ii) r/w s. 340 CrPC alleging that the minutes of the AGM of the Company allegedly held on 30-9-2006 were forged - HC passed orders dt. 15-2-2010 and that order was the subject matter of challenge in instant proceedings - Hence instant petitions - Petitioners contended that application u/s. 340 CrPC was not maintainable -

Held, it was not necessary to either enforce orders dt. 31-1-2008 passed by the CLB or orders dt. 11-4-2008 passed by HC - Fact remained that there was a complete deadlock, as far as affairs of the Company were concerned - Project was not taken off - It was almost dead - Unless the parties re-concile, there was no chance for a joint venture i.e. to develop the resort, as per the MOU - It was only after the decision of CLB, whereby the respective rights of the parties were crystallised, it would be possible to know about the future of this project - Even the Company in question was also defunct as it had no other business activity or venture - SC was of the opinion that more appropriate orders would be to direct the parties to maintain status quo in the meantime, during the pendency of the said company petition before the CLB - Petitions disposed of.
