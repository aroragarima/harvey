Smt. Dayawati and Another v Inderjit and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, K. Subba Rao, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 391; AIR 1966 SC 1423; 1966 CurLJ 477; 1966 (68) PLR 218; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Exchange Control & FDI

Summary: Banking & Finance - Punjab Relief of Indebtedness Act, ss. 5 and 6 - Usurious Loans Act, s. 3 - Interest - Whether, High Court was right in reducing interest by applying ss. 5 and 6 Punjab Relief Act? - Ordinarily appellate court cannot take into account new law - But if new law, expressly or by clear intendment, takes in even pending matters, appellate court may give effect to such law even after judgment of trial court - Held, s. 6 is retrospective - Further, 'suit pending' u/s 6 means a live suit and includes an appeal - HC right in applying s. 3 of Usurious Loans Act (as amended) - Appeal dismissed.
