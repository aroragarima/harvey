Union Territory, Chandigarh and Others v Mohinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 438; (1997) 3 SCC 68; (1997) SCC (L&S) 633; AIR 1997 SC 1201; 1997 (2) AWC 716; 1997 (76) FLR 289; JT 1997 (2) SC 504; 1997 LabIC 1493; 1997 (1) LLJ 826; 1997 (2) LLN 70; 1997(2) SCALE 85; 1997 (2) SCJ 134; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 71; 1997 (1) SLR 707; 1997 (2) Supreme 217; 1997 (1) UJ 590

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311(2) and (3) - Whether Tribunal correctly concluded that that ground upon which Senior Superintendent has dispensed with enquiry is not sustainable in law and respondent should be reinstated? - When any question arises whether it is reasonably practicable to hold an inquiry as contemplated by cl. (2), decision of authority empowered to dismiss such person shall be final - Tribunal has not referred to cl. (3) - It is reported that respondent 'is a terror in area' who intimidated complainant therefore Senior Superintendent of Police cannot be said to be not justified in holding that it is not reasonably practicable to hold an inquiry against respondent - Judgment of Tribunal is set aside - Appeal allowed.
