Messrs Surajmull Nagarmull v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

17 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 172; AIR 1963 SC 393; 1964 (1) SCJ 271; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 163

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Land & Property - Defence of India Act, 1939 - Government of India Act, 1935 - Appellants were tenants of three warehouses and vacant land appurtenant thereto, belonging to Sri Hanuman Seva Trust - By an order dated August 17, 1943 and issued u/r. 75 A of Defence of India Rules, 1939, warehouses were requisitioned and possession thereof was taken on September 21, 1943 - As amount of compensation payable to owner of warehouses could not be fixed by agreement- An Arbitrator was appointed u/s. 19(1)(b) of Defence of India Act, 1939 - Before Arbitrator, Sri Hanuman Seva Trust claimed compensation as owners of warehouses - Appellant claimed compensation of Rs. 1,00,000 for loss of earnings, etc. - Arbitrator observed that appellants had failed to prove any actual loss of business in consequence of requisition, and rejected claim of appellants - On appeal, High Court also dismissed appeal - Whether High Court was right in not entertaining appeal? - Held, an appeal is a creature of statute - Arbitrator not being a court subordinate to HC, an appeal would lie only if it is expressly so provided - Legislature has provided that, where amount of compensation awarded does not exceed Rs. 5,000, no appeal shall lie against award - If Arbitrator rejects claim and refuses to award anything case would, in our judgment, fall within 2nd proviso to r. 19 as being one where amount of compensation awarded does not exceed Rs. 5,000 - HC was right in its decision - Appeal dismissed.
