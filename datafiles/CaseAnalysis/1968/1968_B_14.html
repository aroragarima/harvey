Balchand v Income Tax Officer, Sagar

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 327; AIR 1969 SC 496; [1969] 72 ITR 197; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 704

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Notice Of Reassessment

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34 - ITO issued notices u/s. 148 calling for return of income on the ground that the appellant had escaped assessment - Appellant moved for a writ of mandamus or prohibition restraining the ITO from moving under the notices - (A) Appellant contended that he was first asked by notice to file a return for the 1946-47, and that initiation of proceedings on that notice for reassessment of income in 1945-46 was incompetent - Held, appellant was clearly informed that he had to file a return for the year ending March 31, 1946 - (B) Appellant contended that a notice u/s. 34 was incompetent so long as the return filed for 1946-47 was not considered and disposed of - Held, such a voluntary return does not operate as a bar to the Income-tax Officer issuing a notice of reassessment - Where the income of the assessee has been assessed to tax, it is not open to the assessee on coming to learn or apprehending that proceedings u/s. 34 of the Act will be taken against him to file a voluntary return and avoid the issue of a notice u/s. 34 against him - Appeal dismissed with costs.
