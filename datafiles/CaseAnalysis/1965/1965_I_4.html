Hari Prasad Jayantilal & Co. Ltd. v Income-Tax Officer, Special Investigation Circle-B.

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 207; AIR 1966 SC 1481; 1966 (1) CompLJ 230; 1966 (1) ITJ 453; [1966] 59 ITR 794; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 732

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Articles Of Association, Finance Bill, Finance Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2(6 A)(c), 35(10), 487 and 35(10) - Companies Act, 1913, Sch.1 ,art. 95 - Income Tax - Company - Member's winding up u/s. 487 - Liquidator appointed - Distribution of accumulated reserves by liquidator with income-tax refund certificate - (A) Whether power to withdraw rebate u/s. 35(10) is exercisable within four years from end of financial year in which amount of rebate was availed of for declaring dividends - (B) Effect of winding up on the status of company and such distribution - (C) Whether such distribution amounts to distibution of dividend or assets - (D) Whether compliance with provisions of art. 95 of Sch.1 of Companies Act, 1913 is necessary - Held, power is not to be restricted to four years before April, 1956 - Co. still retains its corporate existence and powers and liquidator, merely acts as agent or administrator -Distribution is of dividends and not of assets of Co. by virtue of s.2(6 A)(c) ; It is also distribution of dividend in hands of Co. within meaning of s.35(10) - Not necessary - Appeal dismissed.
