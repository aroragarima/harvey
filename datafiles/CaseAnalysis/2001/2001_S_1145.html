Â B. R. Kapur v State of Tamil Nadu and Another

Supreme Court of India

21 September 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, G.B. Pattanaik, Y.K. Sabharwal, Ruma Pal, Brijesh Kumar

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19827; (2001) 7 SCC 231; AIR 2001 SC 3435; 2002 (46) ALR 156; 2001 (2) CGLJ 446; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 180; JT 2001 (8) SC 40; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 165; 2001(6) SCALE 309; 2001 (4) SCJ 82; 2001 (5) SLR 749; 2001 (6) SLT 659; [2001] Supp3 S.C.R. 191; 2001 (7) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Prohibition Act, Religious Institutions (Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1988, Commission Of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987

Summary: Parliament & Legislature - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 409, 153-A, 171-E, 171-F, 376(1), 376(2), 376 A-D, 498A, 505(2) and 505(3) - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(c), 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 389 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 8(3), 8-A, 9, 9-A, 10 and 10A - Customs Act, 1962 - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, 1967 - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, ss. 3 and 4 - Religious Institutions (Prevention of Misuse) Act, 1988 ss. 3, 4, 5, 6 and 7 - Places of Worship (Special Provisions) Act, 1991, s. 61 - Prevention of Insults to National Honour Act, 1971, ss. 3 and 2 - Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961 - Commission of Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Drugs and Cosmetics Act, 1940 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Govt. of India Act, 1935 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 163(1), 164, 173, 177, 191 and 368 - (A) Whether a person who has been convicted of a criminal offence and whose conviction has not been suspended pending appeal can be sworn in and can continue to function as CM of a state? - Implicit in art. 164 (4) r/w sub-arts. (1) and (2) is requirement that a minister who is not a member of legislature must seek election to legislature and, in event of his failing to secure a seat in legislature within six months, he must cease to be a minister - A minister who is a member of legislature is required to meet constitutional standards of qualification and disqualification but that a minister who is not a member of legislature need not - Held, non-legislator can be made a CM or Minister u/art. 164 only if he has qualifications for membership of Legislature prescribed by art. 173 and is not disqualified from membership thereof by reason of disqualifications set out in art. 191 - (B) Whether second respondent was qualified for membership of legislature and not disqualified when she was appointed CM? - In those cases where sentence is imposed on a day later than date of conviction disqualification would be attracted on date on which sentence was imposed because only then would a person be both convicted of offence and sentenced to imprisonment for not less than two years which is cumulatively requisite to attract disqualification u/s. 8(3) Representation of People Act - On date on which second respondent was sworn in as CM she was disqualified, by reason of her convictions under Prevention of Corruption Act and sentences of imprisonment of not less than two years, for becoming a member of legislature u/s. 8(3) of Representation of People Act - If Governor invited leader of party which had a majority in legislature to form a Govt, it would, if leader was a non-legislator, thereafter not be open to court in quo warranto proceedings to decide that Chief Minister was disqualified - Further held, in appointment of second respondent as CM there has been a clear infringement of a constitutional provision and that a writ of quo warranto must issue - Appointment of a person to office of Chief Minister who is not qualified to hold it should be struck down at earliest - A person who is convicted for a criminal offence and sentenced to imprisonment for a period of not less than two years cannot be appointed Chief Minister of a state u/arts. 164(1) r/w (4) and cannot continue to function as such - Order accordingly.
