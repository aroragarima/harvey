State By Inspector of Police, Nib, Madurai, Tamil v Rajangam

Supreme Court of India

30 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1816; (2010) 15 SCC 369

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Contraband articles - Case registered - Investigation - Acquittal - accused was found in possession of a bag which contained the narcotic substance - PW6 registered a case and took up investigation - Accused was convicted by Trial Court - Aggrieved, on appeal HC concluded that PW6 who registered the case would not have investigated the case and acquitted appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether PW6 who registered the crime could have investigated the case or an independent officer ought to have investigated the case - Held, Megna Singh v. State of Haryana, 1995 Indlaw SC 1730, followed - HC was justified in acquitting accused - There was no infirmity in the view which was taken by the HC in the impugned judgment - No interference was called for - Appeal dismissed.
