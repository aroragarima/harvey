Delhi Gymkhana Club Limited v Employees State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

28 October 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 731; (2015) 1 SCC 142; 2015 (1) ALD(SC) 19; 2014 (143) FLR 927; JT 2014 (12) SC 235; 2014 (4) KLT 936; 2014 (4) LLJ 707; 2015 (3) MahLJ 1; 2015(3) M.P.H.T. 205; 2015 (2) MPLJ 276; 2015 (1) OLR 156; 2014(12) SCALE 363; 2015 (1) SLR 360

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Employeesâ insurance

Summary: Insurance - Trusts & Associations - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948, s. 2(14AA) - Non-profit organization - Manufacturing process - Employees' insurance - Respondent (ESI Corporation) sought to apply the provisions of the Act on the appellant-club, on the ground that preparation of food items amounts to 'manufacturing process' and that appellant was a factory/establishment covered under the provisions of the Act - Appellant filed a petition in ESI Court, which was allowed holding that preparation of eatables did not fall under 'manufacturing process' and the Act was not applicable - Aggrieved respondent filed an appeal before HC, which was allowed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether kitchen of appellant-club and catering section thereon come within the meaning of 'factory' and 'manufacturing process' as defined in the Act.

Held, HC has rightly held that the preparation of food items in the kitchen of the appellant-club amounts to "manufacturing process" and that the employees are covered under the purview of the Act. Considering the activities of the appellant-club and that the kitchen catering forms an integral part of the appellant-club, the High Court rightly held that the appellant- club falls within the purview of the Act and we do not find any infirmity in the order passed by the HC. Further, ESI contribution ought to have been paid when the demand was made in 1986. It is very unfortunate that the appellant-club has not paid the ESI contribution of its employees for more than three decades and is not justified in seeking for prospective operation of the order. The impugned order of the HC does not suffer from any infirmity warranting interference. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Even though the term 'kitchen', 'catering' of a club may not be called a factory in common parlance, having regard to the definition of 'manufacturing process' and that the Act is a beneficial legislation, a liberal interpretation has to be adopted.
