Allahabad Jal Sansthan v Daya Shankar Rai and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 349; (2005) 5 SCC 124; (2005) SCC (L&S) 631; AIR 2005 SC 2371; 2005 (5) AWC 4648; 2005 (2) CLR 453; 2005 (2) ESC 288; 2005 (105) FLR 943; JT 2005 (5) SC 112; 2005 (2) LLN 967; 2005(4) SCALE 580; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 1077; 2005 (4) Supreme 31; 2005 (2) UPLBEC 1405

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6-N - Service - Whether Respondent was entitled to be granted full back wages in facts and circumstances of this case? - Held, a pragmatic approach requires that the respondent had been reinstated in service and keeping in view of fact that he had not raised any plea or adduced any evidence to effect that he was remained unemployed throughout from 24/1/1987 to 27/2/2001 - Respondent is directed to be paid 50% of back wages - Appeal partly allowed.
