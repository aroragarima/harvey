Union Of India & Ors. v Jujhar Singh

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 446; (2011) 7 SCC 735; AIR 2011 SC 2598; 2011(4) ALL MR 918; 2011 (6) AWC 6027; 2011 (130) FLR 803; JT 2011 (7) SC 418; 2011 (3) KLT 520; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 168; 2011(7) SCALE 618; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 258; 2011 (4) SLR 640; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3405

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Armed Forces, Pension Regulations for the Army, 1961, Entitlement Rules, 1982

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - Pension Regulations for the Army, 1961, regn. 179 - Injury attributed other than military service - Disability pension - Entitlement - Respondent Army man met with an accident while he was on annual leave and was placed in medical category BEE (Permanent) after treatment and percentage of disability was ascertained as 20% - After respondent joined duty, he was kept under observation by the Medical Board and his disability was assessed as 60% for two years - Respondent was superannuated from service and he was granted normal service pension - Respondent made a representation before the authorities claiming disability pension on the ground that he was having disability on the date of retirement - Appellant authorities rejected respondent's representation - Respondent preferred writ petition - Single Judge allowed the writ petition by holding that the respondent was entitled for disability pension u/regn. 179 - Appellant's appeal thereafter was dismissed by DB - Whether the disability in an accident suffered by the respondent during his annual leave while doing his personal work would amount to the disability attributable to or aggravated by military service - Held, respondent was not on military duty at the time of the accident in terms of Para 12 (d) of Entitlement Rules, 1982 as clarified vide Government of India, Ministry referred letter No.1(1)/81(PEN)C/Vol.II dated 27-10-1998 - Hence, the injuries sustained could not be held to be attributable to the military service - Single Judge failed to appreciate that u/regn. 179 a personnel would be granted disability pension only if he was found suffering from disability which was attributable to or aggravated by military service and recorded by Service Medical Authorities - In the instant case, medical authorities had recorded a specific finding to the effect that disability was neither attributable to nor aggravated by the military service, that had not been appreciated either by the Single Judge or by the DB - HC also failed to appreciate that the Medical Board was a Specialized Authority composed of expert medical doctors and it was the final authority to give information regarding attributability and aggravation of the disability to the military service and the condition of service resulting in the disablement of the individual - HC failed to appreciate that even though the respondent sustained injuries while he was on annual leave, he was kept in service till superannuation and he was superannuated from service - Further, respondent was also granted full normal pension as admissible under the Regulations - Member of the Armed Forces who was claiming disability pension should be able to show a normal nexus between the act, omission or commission resulting in an injury to the person and the normal expected standard of duties and way of life expected from member of such forces - Inasmuch as the respondent sustained disability when he was on annual leave that too at his home town in a road accident, the conclusion of the Single Judge that he was entitled to disability pension u/regn. 179 was not based on any material whatsoever - However, DB, without assigning any reason, by way of a cryptic order, confirmed the Single Judge order - Impugned judgements of Single Judge and DB were set aside - Appeal allowed.
