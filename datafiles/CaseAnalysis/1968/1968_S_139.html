Ram Charan and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

01 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 461; AIR 1968 SC 1270; 1968 CRLJ 1473; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 354

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 -- Code of Criminal Procedure, Code, 1973, s. 164 - Murder - Conviction - Statement of witness - Victim carried leather bag in which he kept money which had received as sale proceeds by him - When witness and victim reached near drain they met accused - Victim started walking with accused, while witness followed little behind - Accused attack on victim which saw by witness and First Information Report (FIR) was lodged of offence u/ss. 302 r/w. 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted accused u/ss. 302 r/w 34 of IPC - Appeal filed before HC in which HC confirmed convictions - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether statement of witness recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC would be discard if he stick to his statement - Held, witnesses was able to sign but he put thumb impression which could not effectively deny later - Certificate appeared statements of witnesses in which endorsement was not proper but it was difficult to follow from endorsement that any threat was given to witnesses or evidence given by witnesses suspect or less believable - None of facts casted doubt on prosecution story - If statement of witness was previously recorded u/s, 164 of CrPC it lead to inference that there was time when police thought witness may change but if witness sticks to statement made by him throughout, mere fact that his statement was previously recorded u/s. 164 of CrPC would not be sufficient to discard it - Court should receive it with caution and if there was other circumstances on record which lend support to truth of evidence of such witness it was acted upon - Appeal dismissed.
