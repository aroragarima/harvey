Grasim Industries Limited v Collector of Customs, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, N. Santosh Hegde

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1053; (2002) 4 SCC 297; AIR 2002 SC 1706; 2002 (81) ECC 22; 2002 (141) E.L.T. 593; JT 2002 (3) SC 551; 2002 (49) RLT 627; 2002(3) SCALE 349; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 945; 2002 (3) SLT 60; 2002 (128) STC 349; 2002 (3) Supreme 155

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Ejusdem Generis, Attorney-General

Summary: Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 130-E - United Towns Electrical Company Act, 1902 - Whether Karbate Tubes made of artificial graphite impregnated with Phenolic resin which are parts of Heat exchangers are classifiable under Tariff Item, sub-heading 6815.10 or sub-heading 8419.50 of Customs Tariff Act? - Note 1(a) of Chapter 84, as noted above, is clear and unambiguous - It does not speak of a class, category or genus followed by general words - Rule of ejusdem generis has no application - Held, said product would be classifiable under sub-heading 6815.10 - Appeal dismissed.
