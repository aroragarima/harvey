Major S.S. Khanna v Brig F.J. Dhillon

Supreme Court of India

14 August 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.K. Sarkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 321; AIR 1964 SC 497; 1963 ALJ 1068; 1964 (66) PLR 115; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 409

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 115 - Suit filed for dissolution of partnership and rendition of accounts - Parties arrived at compromise with scheme of winding up - Trial Court held that suits were not maintainable - Whether decision can be said to be 'cases' 'decided' by subordinate judge whether suits answer description 'in which no appeal lies'? - Decision of Subordinate Court is amenable to revisional jurisdiction of High Court unless that jurisdiction is clearly barred by a special law or an appeal lies therefrom - HC also pointed out that Subordinate Judge did not dismiss suits and did not draw up decrees for that purpose, is itself an exercise of jurisdiction with material irregularity if not also illegality - Power is exercisable in respect of all orders involving jurisdiction in which no appeal lies to HC - Held, decision that suits were not maintainable and yet keeping them pending was itself an exercise of jurisdiction with a material irregularity - If Trial Court dismissed suits and passed decree there would lie appeal and no revision would have lain - Order passed by Trial Court not a decree so not appealable - Appeal dismissed.
