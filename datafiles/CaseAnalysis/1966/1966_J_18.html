Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, Calcutta v Juggilal Kamalapat

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1967 SC 401; [1967] 63 ITR 292; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 784

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 26 A, 66 (A) (2) and 66 (4) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Registration Act, 1908 - Partners of firm transferred business assets by a non-registered relinquishment deed - Whether non-registration of relinquishment deed invalidate transfer of business assets to new partnership ? - Held, deed of relinquishment could, at best, be held to be invalid in so far as it affected immovable properties included in assets of firm; but to extent that it purported to transfer movable assets of firm, document would remain valid - Partnership under a deed of partnership, which was properly executed, and in these circumstances, partnership that came into existence was clearly valid in law - Appeal dismissed.
