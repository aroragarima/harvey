FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

Durga Burman (Roy) v State of Sikkim

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 492; (2014) 13 SCC 35; AIR 2014 SC 2993; 2014 CRLJ 4059; JT 2014 (8) SC 484; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 190; 2014(9) SCALE 49; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 311

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Murder

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 380 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused no. 2) and co-accused allegedly committed murder and committed theft of wrist watch - Appellant and co-accused were charged u/ss. 302, 380 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court by its judgment convicted the accused u/ss. 302, 380 r/w s. 34 if IPC - On appeal HC by impugned judgment acquitted A1 holding that there was no evidence against him except that in the morning on date of occurrence he was present in the house of deceased and remain absconded till he was arrested on 8-7-2001 and that an act of absconding was no doubt a relevant piece of evidence but the said act did not by itself lead to a conclusion that he was guilty and that there was no other incriminating material against him to connect with offence and that suspicion however strong be could not take the place of proof and that prosecution was not able to prove its case against A1 beyond reasonable doubt - HC found the appellant clearly guilty of offences punishable u/ss. 302 and 380 of IPC and conviction recorded by Trial Court u/ss. 302, 380 r/w s. 34 of IPC was hereby converted to one u/ss. 302 and 380 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed instant appeal -

Held, none of the circumstances by itself would lead to the irresistible conclusion that appellant was the author of the crimes u/ss. 302 and 380 of IPC - It was in evidence of PW 3 and 4, the key witnesses that apart from the appellant, A1 was also seen in the house of the deceased and, according to prosecution also, as noted in their report, it was A1 'who quietly put a strip of cloth round the neck of the deceased and strangulated her' - It was in evidence that both the accused belonged to 'J' - It was the case of prosecution itself that A1 had received a message on the evening of 5-7-2001 that his mother was seriously ill and she was at home - PW13 did does not support the case of prosecution that she had seen the appellant in the afternoon of 4-7-2001 as proceeding to 'S' - PW13 was specific and categoric of that date because it was the first death anniversary of her father-in-law - Appellant was in fact not absconding - They had gone to their native place 'J' and they were arrested from their respective homes only - The only other ground was that of recovery u/s. 27 of 1872 Act, recovery of wrist watch which was alleged to have been stolen by appellant - From the evidence available on record, Court find it extremely difficult to place reliance on that recovery for many reasons - Wrist watch belongs to PW1, husband of deceased - PW 2 and 3 were the sons of deceased and were staying with PW1 and the deceased - PW 1, 2 and 3 did not have a case that the wrist watch belonging to PW1 had been stolen by the appellant - They did not also have a case about the money that has been allegedly taken by the accused after committing murder - There was not even a whisper in the evidence of PW1, 2 and 3 regarding the theft of either the wrist watch or the cash except for the identification of wrist watch by PW1 as belonging to him - There was not even a reference to the alleged missing of the wrist watch since 6-7-2001 or the loss of cash - It was only in the evidence of PW16 (IO) that the accused had a motive of committing theft after murdering the deceased and that an amount of Rs.2,300/- and wrist watch belonging to PW1 had been taken by accused - Another significant aspect in instant case was that all ornaments worn by deceased were on the body and nothing had been removed - If the accused had a motive to commit theft, it was only normal that they would lay their hands on the jewellery as well - On the basis of evidence, Court find it extremely difficult to hold that the prosecution had laid a foundation for an effective prosecution and has proved beyond doubt that it was the appellant who committed the murder of deceased - Instant case was set up only on circumstantial evidence - All the circumstances should lead to, without breaking the chain, the involvement of accused and accused only - On the only ground that the accused was seen with the deceased in the morning of the date of incident and that they were not seen in that place for another two days, could not, by themselves, lead to the conclusion that it was appellant who authored the crime - Evidence available on record would on the contrary give an indication that theft was a story of IO only - Neither PW1 whose wrist watch was said to be stolen nor the sons of deceased (PW 2 and 3) have any case of alleged theft of wrist watch or cash - Recovery was also doubtful - There was no consistent version of the recovery - Person from whom the recovery had effected, viz., mother of appellant, was not examined - Despite the availability of appellant, recovery was through his mother - There was no explanation as to how she got to watch - Recovery of wrist watch from the house of appellant was the only ground on which HC maintained the conviction of appellant - Prosecution miserably failed in proving the case against appellant and appellant was entitled to succeed - Appeal allowed.
