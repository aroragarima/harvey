Haryana State Elctronics Development Corporation Limited v Mamni

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 190; (2006) 9 SCC 434; AIR 2006 SC 2427; 2006 (6) AWC 5437; JT 2006 (11) SC 232; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1434; 2006 (3) RLW 2545; 2006(5) SCALE 164; 2006 (5) SCJ 526; 2007 (1) SLR 482; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 638

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10(1)(c), 11-A, 2(oo)(bb), 25(F) - Appointment on ad hoc basis - Absence from duty - Termination - Labour Court directed reinstatement of respondent with back wages on premise that she had completed 240 days of work during a period of twelve months immediately preceding date of termination of her services and in view of fact that conditions laid down u/s. 25(F) had not been complied with by Appellant - Appeal against - Held, respondent was appointed on an ad hoc basis - When advertisement was issued for filling up said post, the Respondent did not even apply therefor - Modify impugned order by directing that respondent shall be compensated by payment of a sum of Rs. 25, 000/- in stead of order for reinstatement with back wages - Appeal partly allowed.
