State Of Uttar Pradesh v Batuk Deo Patil Tripathi & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 February 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 141; (1978) 2 SCC 102; (1978) SCC (L&S) 147; 1978 ALJ 477; 1978 LabIC 839; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 131; 1979 SLJ 245; 1978 (2) SLR 1; 1978 UJ 237

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 225, 233, 235 - Civil Service Regulations, art.465A (1) - Rules-of Court, 1952, Chap. 3, r. 1 - Scope of art. 235 - Respondent 1 joined Judicial Service as Munsif in 1943 and after intervening promotions, he was appointed as District Judge on 13-4-1969 - U/art. 465-A note 1 of Regulations, as adopted for application in State, State Govt. might at any time, without assigning any reason, require any officer to retire on 3months' notice or pay in lieu of whole or part thereof after he had attained age of 50 years - Such decision was required to be taken by Govt. in its Administrative Dept. and only if it appeared to it to be in public interest to do so - Some time in 1969 State Govt. requested respondent 2/HC to instant appeal, to screen cases of judicial officers in order to determine which of them should be retired compulsorily under aforesaid provision - In one of the meetings of Administrative Committee of HC which was held on 9-1-1974, it was resolved by Committee that respondent no. 1 should be retired compulsorily from service - Registrar of HC communicated said decision of Committee to appellant/State Govt. and thereafter, he circulated to all Judges of HC, for their intimation, decision taken by Committee - Governor of State accepted recommendation of Committee and retired respondent no. 1 compulsorily by order dt.27-2-1975 - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition against appellant/State and respondent no.2/HC challenging validity of aforesaid order - DB rejected contention of appellant and directed that papers of the case be placed before Justice for constituting a Full Bench to consider question 'whether in view of art. 233 of Constitution, consultation with entire HC was necessary before making an order of compulsory retirement against District Judge - Full Bench held that District Judge could be compulsorily retired from service on the opinion recorded by Administrative Committee - Hence instant appeal - Whether District Judge could be compulsorily retired from service on the basis of opinion recorded by Administrative Committee constituted u/r. 1 of Chap. 3 of 1952 Rules - Held, compulsory retirement of Judges of District Court and subordinate courts was a matter which falls squarely within power of control vested in HCs by art. 235 of Constitution - In instant case, respondent was compulsorily retired from service as District Judge, on the recommendation of Administrative Committee of HC; and it was clear from facts and sequence of events that other Judges of HC had no effective opportunity to consider propriety or correctness of decision of Administrative Committee recommending to State Govt. that respondent be retired compulsorily - According to view of majority, act of Court in allowing Administrative Committee to decide that question u/r. 1 of Chap. 3 of 1952 Rules was an act of 'self- abnegations' and therefore void - This approach betrays, with respect, a misunderstanding of object of art. 235 of Constitution - Ideal which inspired provision that control over District Courts and courts subordinate thereto should vest in HC was that those wings of judiciary should be independent of executive - Nature of power which that article conferred on HCs, SC was of the opinion that it was wrong to characterise as 'delegation' the process whereby entire HC authorises a judge or some of judges of Court to act on behalf of the whole court - Administrative functions were only a part, though an important part, of HCs Constitutional functions - Judicial functions ought to occupy and do in fact consume the best part of a Judge's time - For balancing these two-fold functions it was inevitable that administrative duties should be left to be discharged, by some on behalf of all judges - Amplitude of power conferred by art. 235 of Constitution, imperative need that HCs should be enabled to transact their administrative business more conveniently and on awareness of realities of situation, particularly of practical difficulties involved in a consideration by whole court, even by circulation, of every day-to-day matter pertaining to control over District and subordinate Courts, lead to the conclusion that by rules framed u/art. 235 of Constitution HC ought to be conceded the power to authorise on Administrative Judge or an Administrative Committee of Judges to act on behalf of Court - Accordingly, SC uphold minority judgment of Full Bench that r. 1 of Chap.3 of 1952 Rules framed by Allahabad HC was within framework of art. 235 of Constitution - Recommendation made by Administrative Committee that respondent should be compulsorily retired could not therefore be said to suffer from any legal or Constitutional infirmity - Appeal allowed.
