All India State Bank Officers Federation through its President and Others v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 786; (1990) Supp SCC 336; (1991) SCC (L&S) 429; 1991 (16) ATC 454; 1990 (61) FLR 447; JT 1990 (2) SC 243; 1990 SCALE 40; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 493; 1990 (2) SLR 764

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - Writ petition - Preliminary objection - As to that writ petition filed by appellant/federation ought to have been dismissed on ground of suppression of material facts from High Court and resultant abuse of process of Court - Whether preliminary objection tenable? - Appellant/federation filed writ petition against new promotion policy adopted by State Bank of India and its implemention - Fact of filing of writ petition before another HC wherein stay was sought and rejected, noted by Court - Conduct of appellant/federation noted - Finding, there was suppression of material fact and abuse of process of Court - (i) Whether dismissal of petition called for? - Held, no - Court sympathised with officers who may suffer as a consequence of new promotion policy, which they wish to challenge but for misstatements or wrong steps taken by officers of federation perhaps, in their over anxiety to get quick interim relief - Further held, preliminary objection overruled - Direction issued as to proceeding further with petition.
