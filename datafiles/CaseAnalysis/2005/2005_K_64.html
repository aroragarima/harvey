Bhagwan Dass and Another v Kamal Abrol and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

P.P. Naolekar, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 373; (2005) 11 SCC 66; AIR 2005 SC 2583; 2005 (5) AWC 4616; JT 2005 (5) SC 339; 2005(5) SCALE 179; [2005] Supp1 S.C.R. 342

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Petroleum & Gas; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Trade - Allotment of dealership/distributorship of LPG - Whether eligibility criterion of being resident of Kangra district has to be construed to be a permanent or de facto residence or temporary or de jure residence? - Held, when agency requires full time working dealer it would be only possible if person actually resides in Kangra district and not working through agent or servants engaged for said purpose - Dealer is required to be a de facto resident of place from where dealership license is to be issued and it is not permissible to have casual connection or temporary residence at that place - Appeal disposed of.
