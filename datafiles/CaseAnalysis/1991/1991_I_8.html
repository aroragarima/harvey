Â Indian Ex-Services League and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Jagannatha Shetty, L.M. Sharma, M.H. Kania, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 487; (1991) 2 SCC 104; (1991) SCC (L&S) 536; AIR 1991 SC 1182; 1991 (16) ATC 488; 1991 (62) FLR 287; 1992 (1) LLJ 765; 1991 (1) LLN 387; 1991(1) SCALE 81; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 158; 1991 (1) SLR 745; 1991 (2) UJ 94; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 403

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Computation Of Pension, Armed Forces, Ex-servicemen, Liberalised Pension Scheme, High Court Judges (Conditions Of Service) Amendment Act, 1976

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Armed Forces personnel - 'One rank, one pension - Whether GOs in the present case were not issued in implementation of the decision in D.S. Nakara v. Union of India (1983) 1 SCC 3051983? - Court held, no - Claim of the petitioners is untenable and it proceeds on a misreading of the Nakara (1983) 1 SCC 3051983 - Conclusion of the Constitution Bench in Nakara (1983) 1 SCC 3051983 (1) was that the benefits of liberalisation and the extent thereof given in accordance with the liberalised pension scheme have to be given equally to all retirees irrespective of their date of retirement and those benefits cannot be confined only to the persons who retired on or after the specified date because for the purpose of grant of the benefits of liberalisation in pension, all retirees constitute one class irrespective of their date of retirement - In order to give effect to this conclusion the only relief granted was to strike down that portion of the memoranda by which the benefit of the liberalised pension scheme was confined only to persons retiring on or after the specified date with the result that the benefit was extended to all retirees, irrespective of their date of retirement - Once this position emerging from the decision in Nakara (1983) 1 SCC 3051983 (1), is borne in mind, the fallacy in the petitioners' contention in these Writ Petitions becomes obvious and their claim based only on Nakara (1983) 1 SCC 3051983 (1) is untenable - Petitions dismissed.
