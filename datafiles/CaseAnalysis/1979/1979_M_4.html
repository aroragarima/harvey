M. Mammutti v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

15 February 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 471; (1979) 4 SCC 723; (1980) SCC (Cr) 170; AIR 1979 SC 1705; 1979 CRLJ 1383; 1979 UJ 271

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Committing Court

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 489C and 489B - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 342 - Proof of possession of easily detectible counterfeit currency notes - Presumption that of knowlege cannot be drawn - Examination of accused u/s. 342 of CrPC, 1973 - No specific questions asked to find out whether he knew that notes in his possession were counterfeit - Conviction u/ss 489B and 489C of IPC, 1860 - Legality - Held, conviction not proper - Appeal allowed.
