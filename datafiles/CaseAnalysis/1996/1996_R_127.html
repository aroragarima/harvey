Union of India and Others v Ram Narain Bishwanath and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1648; (1998) 9 SCC 285; 1997 (96) E.L.T. 224

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Customs - Jurisdiction - Goods imported by respondents were cleared at Paradip port in State of Orissa - Goods were then seized by Customs authorities in West Bengal on ground that they had been imported on strength of fictitious licences - Whether tribunal was justified in holding that Customs authorities in West Bengal had no jurisdiction to pass such order? - Held, customs authorities at Paradip should have initiated proceedings against respondents and not Customs authorities in West Bengal - Appeal allowed.
