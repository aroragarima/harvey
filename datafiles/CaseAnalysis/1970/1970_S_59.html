Sirpur Paper Mill Ltd v The Commissioner Of Wealth Tax, Hyderarad

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 276; (1970) 1 SCC 795; AIR 1970 SC 1520; [1970] 77 ITR 6; 1971 (1) MLJ 14; 1971 (1) MLJ(SC) 14; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 304

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, ss. 7(2), 13 and 25 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Income Tax Act, 1961 - Claim of depreciation allowance on plant, building and machinery - Denied - Sustainability - In proceedings for determination of wealth tax for the A/Y 1957-58 and 1958-59 appellant/Company claimed depreciation allowance on plant, building and machinery at the rates prescribed under 1961 Act and the Rules framed there under - Wealth Tax Officer adopted the method prescribed by s. 7 (2) of 1957 Act and' admitted the value of the assets as shown in the certified balance sheets on the respective valuation dates - In appeal, appellate Assistant Commissioner of Wealth Tax confirmed the order passed by the Wealth Tax Officer - Appellant then moved revision applications before the Commissioner of Wealth Tax/respondent u/s. 25 of 1957 Act - Against the order passed by the Commissioner of Wealth Tax rejecting the applications appellant had filed these appeals u/art. 136 of the Constitution - Whether respondent rightly rejected the applications of appellant - Held, power conferred by s. 25 of 1957 Act was not administrative it was quasijudicial - Expression "may make such inquiry and pass such order thereon" did not confer any absolute discretion on respondent - In exercise of the power the respondent should bring to bear unbiased mind, consider impartially the objections raised by the aggrieved party, and decide the dispute according to procedure consistent with the principles of natural justice, he could not permit his judgment to be influenced by matters not disclosed to the assesses, nor by dictation of another authority - S. 13 of 1957 Act provided that all officers and other persons employed in the execution of the Act should observe and follow the orders, instructions and directions of the Board - These instructions might control the exercise of the power of the officers of the Dept. in matters administrative but not quasi-judicial - Proviso to s. 13 of 1957 Act was somewhat obscure in its import - It enacted that no orders, instructions or directions should be given by the Board so as to interfere with the discretion of the Appellate Assistant Commissioner of Wealth Tax in the exercise of his appellate functions - It did not, however, imply that the Board might give any directions or instructions to the Wealth Tax Officer or to the Commissioner in exercise of his quasi-judicial function - Such an interpretation would be plainly contrary to the scheme of 1957 Act and the nature of the power conferred upon the authorities 'invested with quasi-judicial power - Respondent appeared to have wholly misapprehended the true character of the jurisdiction with which he is by the Act entrusted and has surrendered his judgment to the directions of the Board of Revenue - Order sheet of respondent was bearing, eloquent testimony to the manner in which respondent had merely carried out the directions of the Board of Revenue, instead of deciding the case according to his own judgment - SC set aside the order passed by respondent and directed that the revision applications should be heard and disposed of according to law and uninfluenced by any instructions or directions given by the Board of Revenue - Order accordingly.
