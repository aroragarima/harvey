Rohilkhand Medical College and Hospital, Bareilly v Medical Council of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 571; (2013) 15 SCC 516; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 394; 2013 (4) CLT(SC) 133; JT 2013 (12) SC 199; 2013(11) SCALE 183; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 692; 2013 (6) SLR 585; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 611

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Prohibition of Unfair Practices in Technical Educational Institutions, Medical Educational Institutions and University Bill, 2010, Enhancement of Annual Intake Capacity in Under-graduate Courses in Medical College for the Academic Session 2013-14 Only Regulation, 2013, Minimum Standard Requirements for the Medical College for 100 Admissions Annually Regulations, 1999, Establishment of Medical College Regulations (Amendment), 2010, University Grants Commission Regulations

Summary: Education - Regulations for Establishment of Medical College, 2010, cl. 8(3)(1)(d) - Admission to Post Graduate Medical Course - Sanction by Medical Council of India (MCI) - Revocation of - Petitioner College sought extension of renewal of permission for admission of 3rd batch of 100 seats of M.B.B.S. for the academic year 2008-09 to MCI - After several inspection and compliance of various deficiencies pointed out by inspection team, Central Govt. accorded sanction - Later, MCI accorded sanction for renewal of permission for admission for second batch of MBBS students against increased intake i.e. from100 to 150 seats to the College for the academic year 2013-14 - MCI, in the meantime, received a confidential letter from CBI informing that CBI had registered a corruption case against Chairman of petitioner College and officers of concerned Ministry - MCI revoked permission accorded to second batch for increased intake - Hence, instant writ petition - Held, decision taken by MCI was in accordance with the Regulation - Petitioners were governed by the Regulations, especially cl. 8(3)(1)(d), in the event of which, when MIC found that college had employed fake/forged documents for renewal of permission/recognition for processing applications etc., that institute would not be able to be considered for renewal of permission/recognition for award of MBBS Degree or processing application for post-graduate courses for two academic years - In the instant case, CBI letter was received on 11-7-2013 by MCI and it was placed before Board of Governors on 12-7-2013 and revocation order was passed on 13-7-2013 revoking renewal of permission - Once medical council found that sanction had been obtained on the basis of fake and forge documents, cl. 8(3)(1)(d) kicked in and fraud unraveled everything - Hence, no good reason to invoke art. 32 of the Constitution of India and none of fundamental rights guaranteed to petitioners stood violated - Petition dismissed.
