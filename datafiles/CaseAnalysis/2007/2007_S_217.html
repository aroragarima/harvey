Soma Chakravarty v State Through Cbi

Supreme Court of India

10 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 872; (2007) 5 SCC 403; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 514; AIR 2007 SC 2149; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1707; JT 2007 (7) SC 181; 2007(7) SCALE 25; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 324

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Probable Consequence, Aiding And Abetting

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 420 r/w 120-B, 429, 468, 471; Prevention of Corruption Act - Investigation framed the impugned charges against the appellant - Against the framing of charges a criminal revision was filed in the High Court which dismissed - Appeal against - Held, if on the basis of material on record the Court could form an opinion that the accused might have committed offence it can frame the charge, though for conviction the conclusion is required to be proved beyond reasonable doubt that the accused has committed the offence - There are serious allegations of misappropriation of a huge amount of money belonging to the government, and it cannot be said at this stage that there is no material at all for framing the charge against her - No infirmity in the impugned judgment - Appeal dismissed.
