Â Ziyauddin Burhanuddin Bukhari v Brijmohan Ramdass Mehra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

25 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, P.K. Goswami, A. Alagiriswami, N.L. Untwalia, Hans Raj Khanna, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 179; (1976) 2 SCC 17; AIR 1975 SC 1788; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 281

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Representation of People Act, 1951,ss. 80,99, 116 A, 123 - Whether HC had rightly held that statements and speeches delivered by appellant amounted to a violation of s. 123(3A) of Act? - HC was right in construing speech as highly inflammatory - It certainly amounted to assertion that Muslim religion (or, what Bukhari thought it was) was in danger - It is a fair construction on speech to hold that it amounted to at least a violation of s. 123(3) of Act - Language employed, in context of its purposes, fall within purview of Act - Held, no reason to differ from view adopted by HC that statements amounted to electoral offences struck by s. 123(3A) - HC was right in construing speech as highly inflammatory - It certainly amounted to assertion that Muslim religion was in danger - Appeal dismissed.
