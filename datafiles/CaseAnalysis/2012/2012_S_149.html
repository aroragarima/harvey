Sunil Kumar v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

27 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 106; (2012) 5 SCC 398; AIR 2012 SC 1754; 2012 CRLJ 2093; 2012 (2) JCC 1392; JT 2012 (4) SC 32; 2012 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1489; 2012 (2) OLR 107; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 464; 2012 (3) RLW 2535; 2012 (4) RLW 3055; 2012(4) SCALE 21; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 184

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 360, 362 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 7 - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Food Adulteration - Conviction - Modification - Validity - Trial Court convicted petitioner u/s. 7 of 1955, Act, concluding that he was in possession of 1370 liters of blue kerosene and indulging in unauthorised sale thereof in violation of the provisions of Act, 1955 - Petitioner filed appeal before HC, HC dismissed appeal - Petitioner filed application for modifying judgment and order giving him benefit u/s. 360 of Cr.P.C and s. 4 of 1958, Act, it was dismissed - Petitioner filed SLP which was dismissed - Petitioner once again approached HC u/s. 362 of Cr.P.C for review - HC in impugned judgment concluded that court could not entertain the petition having become functus officio - Hence instant petition - Whether petitioner can be granted relief u/s. 360 of Cr.P.C - Held, SC, had held that adulteration of food was a menace to public health and statute had been enacted with aim of eradicating that anti-social evils and for ensuring purity in the articles of food - Legislature had thought it fit to prescribe minimum sentence of imprisonment, therefore, court should not lightly resort to provisions of 1958, Act in case of an accused found guilty of offences, hence petition was misconceived and untenable - Petition dismissed.
