State of Uttar Pradesh v Ram Swarup Saroj

Supreme Court of India

07 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, A.S. Anand, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2891; (2000) 3 SCC 699; AIR 2000 SC 1097; 2000 ALJ 709; 2000 (2) AWC 1356; 2000 (85) FLR 119; JT 2000 (2) SC 632; 2000 LabIC 1019; 2000 (2) LLN 82; 2000 (2) MLJ(SC) 170; 2000(2) SCALE 223; 2000 (2) SLR 18; 2000 (2) SLT 696; 2000 (2) Supreme 162; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 956

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Appointment of Munsifs - Respondent, who belongs to a Schedule Caste also appeared in written examination and was declared having passed same - Name of respondent did not figure in list of successful candidates - Validity of procedure challenged - High Court allowed writ petition - Resulted in altering order of merit - Revised list finalised in 1996 - Successful candidates belonging to scheduled caste category all excepting one 'N' joined service - Respondent prayed that direction to State of Uttar Pradesh to offer vacancy created by non-joining of 'N' to respondent - Whether respondent would be entitled to be appointed in one vacancy available in scheduled caste category? - List of selected candidates for appointment to State services remains valid for a period of one year - Select list was finalised in month of November, 1996 and writ petition was filed by respondent in month of October, 1997, i.e. before expiry of one year from date of list - Merely because a period of one year has elapsed during pendency of litigation, to grant of relief to which respondent has been found entitled to by HC, cannot be declined - Held, implementation of order of HC under appeal not stayed - Appellant to comply with direction issued by HC - Appeal dismissed.
