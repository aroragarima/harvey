Jitendra Kumar Singh And Another. v State Of U.P. And Others.

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 33; (2010) 3 SCC 119; AIR 2010 SC 1851; 2010 (4) AWC 4125; JT 2010 (1) SC 177; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 307; 2010(1) SCALE 358; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 325; 2010 (1) SLR 526

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Ex-servicemen, Sports Quota, U.P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1994, UP Public Service (Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1993, UP Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes, Scheduled Tribes and Other Backward Classes) Ordinance, 1994

Summary: (A) Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16. 16(4) - U.P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1994 - Relaxation in age and fee - Reservation of posts for reserved category, Women Candidates and Sportspersons - Extent of selection of a reserve category candidate against unreserved seats - Advertisement was issued for direct recruitment on post of Sub Inspectors in Civil Police (SICP) and Platoon Commanders (PC) - 2% posts were reserved for outstanding Sportspersons and recruitments to these posts were to be made by a separate advertisement - 10% of posts were reserved for women - Selection was challenged in a number of writ petitions by unsuccessful candidates - Single judge directed respondents to recalculate number of posts of general category candidates by applying 2% reservation for sports men horizontally and adding 2% posts of sports men also while calculating total number of vacancies of general category candidates - In appeal, DB held that if a reserved category candidate has secured marks more than last General Category candidate, he is entitled to be selected against unreserved seat without being adjusted against reserved seat - DB also held that reservation in favour of sportspersons quota (2%) has to operate horizontally, therefore, 29 vacancies which remained unfilled could not have been carried forward - Hence, present appeals by unsuccessful candidates as well as State of UP - Whether relaxation in fee and age given to reserve category would fall within definition of 'reservation' to exclude candidates from open competition on seats meant for General Category Candidates? - Held, with age relaxation and fee concession, reserved candidates are merely brought within zone of consideration, so that they can participate in open competition on merit - It is only thereafter merit of candidates is to be determined without any further concessions in favour of reserved category candidates - In present case, concessions availed of by reserved category candidates in age relaxation and fee concession had no relevance to determination of inter se merit on basis of final written test and interview - Therefore, such relaxations cannot deprive a reserved category candidate of right to be considered as a general category candidate on basis of merit in competitive examination - Once candidate participates in written examination, it is immaterial as to which category, candidate belongs - Appeals filed by General Category candidates dismissed - Appeals disposed of.

(B) Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16. 16(4) - U.P. Public Services (Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes) Act, 1994 - Reservation of posts for Sportspersons - Carry forward of posts - It was submitted that against 67 posts of general category reserved for women only 15 qualified candidates were available and they were duly selected - Remaining 52 unfilled posts were filled up from male candidates - Therefore, there remained no unfilled vacancy in general category - It was further submitted that Single Judge erred by directing appellants to fill up vacancy which were excluded from 2% sports quota from aforesaid selection - Challenged - Held, any posts reserved for women which remain unfilled have to be filled up from amongst suitable male candidates - There is a specific prohibition that posts shall not be carried forward for future - Therefore, view expressed by Single Judge cannot be sustained - Furthermore, State has not carried forward any of general category posts reserved for women and outstanding sportspersons - All posts remaining unfilled, in category reserved for women have been filled up by suitable male candidates, therefore, clearly no post has been carried forward - DB erred in issuing directions to appellants to fill in unfilled vacancies reserved for women candidates from suitable male candidates - Appeals filed by State and Director General of Police allowed - Deduction of 34 posts for separate selection would not in any manner affect the overall ratio of reservation as provided by law - Furthermore, there is no carry forward of any post - Separate selection is clearly part and parcel of the main selection - Conclusions recorded by the Single Judge and the Division Bench with regard to the 34 posts reserved for the outstanding sportsmen category i.e. (29 SICP) + (5 PC) also cannot be sustained - Appeals disposed of.

(C) Constitution - Precedent - Held, merely quoting isolated observations in a judgment cannot be treated as a precedent de hors facts and circumstances in which said observation was made - Appeals disposed of.
