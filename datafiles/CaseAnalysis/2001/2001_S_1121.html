Ganesh Lal v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

31 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 142; (2002) SCC (Cr) 247; (2002) 1 SCC 731; 2001 (4) CCR 261; 2002 (1) Crimes 172; 2002 CRLJ 967; JT 2001 (9) SC 383; 2001(7) SCALE 551; 2001 (7) SLT 585; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 619; 2001 (8) Supreme 317; 2002 (1) UC 78

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Under Section 313 of Code of Criminal Procedure, if, several pieces of incriminating circumstantial evidence are available against the accused then, their having remained totally unexplained by the accused, forges a complete chain of incriminating circumstantial evidence so as to fasten guilt upon the accused beyond any reasonable doubt.
