P. A. Narayanan v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1461; (1998) 3 SCC 67; AIR 1998 SC 1659; 1998 (4) AWC 833; 1998 (3) Bom.C.R. 625; 1998 (1) CLT 153; JT 1998 (1) SC 749; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 121B; 1998(1) SCALE 657; [1998] 1 S.C.R. 899; 1998 (2) SLR 247; 1998 (3) SLT 16; 1998 (2) Supreme 71

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Tort - Carriers & Transportation - Railways Act, 1989, ss. 123(c) and 124 - Railways - Entitlement to claim for compensation for death due to untoward incident - Deceased robbed and assaulted while travelling in local train - Failure of guard and motorman to stop train in spite of alarm proved - Casual approach, dereliction of duty resulting in loss of life - Held, liability arises as fault of Railway Official - Compensation awarded - Appeal allowed.
