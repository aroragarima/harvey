Sashi Jena and Others v Khadal Swain and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 136; (2004) 4 SCC 236; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1077; AIR 2004 SC 1492; 2004 (1) CGLJ 417; 2004 (2) Crimes 153; 2004 CRLJ 1394; 2004 (1) JCC 420; JT 2004 (2) SC 339; 2004(2) SCALE 348; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 260; 2004 (2) SLT 650; 2004 (2) Supreme 140; 2004 (1) UC 698

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 157, 33 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, ss. 164, 202 - Criminal law - Practice and procedure - Proceedings u/s. 202 of CrPC 1973 - During course of inquiry accused has no right much less opportunity to cross examine a prosecution witness, statement of such a witness recorded during course of inquiry is not admissible in evidence u/s. 33 of Evidence Act - Cannot form basis of conviction of an accused - Statement of PW 1 recorded during course of enquiry u/s. 202 of Code cannot be used against accused for any purpose - Chandra Deo Singh vs Prokash Chandra Bose @ Chabi Bose and Anr, 1963 Indlaw SC 398 followed - Held on facts, prosecution has failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - Convictions set aside.
