Jagdish Dutt and Another v Dharam Pal and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 456; (1999) 3 SCC 644; AIR 1999 SC 1694; 1999 (3) CLT 26; JT 1999 (3) SC 49; 1999 (2) MLJ(SC) 85; 1999 (1) RCR(Rent) 466; 1999 (3) SCJ 148; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 576; 1999 (3) SLT 501; 1999 (4) Supreme 19

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 47, O. XXI, r. 15 - Decree is passed in favour of a joint family - Question relating to effect of purchase of undivided interest of some of coparceners in family of decree holder in respect of property which is subject matter of execution? - A joint decree for actual possession of immovable property is passed and one of coparceners assigns or transfers his interest in subject matter of decree in favour of judgment debtor, decree gets extinguished to extent of interest so assigned and execution could lie only to extent of remaining part of decree - Held, a joint decree can be executed as a whole since it is not divisible and it can be executed in part only where shares of decree holders are defined - Purchaser of undivided interest of a coparcener in an immovable property cannot claim to be in joint possession of that property with all other coparceners - O. XXI, r. 15 enables a joint decree - holder to execute a decree in its entirety but if whole of decree cannot be executed, this provision cannot be of any avail - Petition dismissed.
