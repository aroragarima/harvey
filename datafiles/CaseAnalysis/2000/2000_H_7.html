Roochira Ceramics v H. U. D. A. and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3524; (2002) 9 SCC 599; AIR 2002 SC 2380; 2001 (128) PLR 218; 2001 (2) RCR(Civil) 617B

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Haryana Urban Development Authority Act (13 of 1977), Default in payments, Land allotment, Charge of interest, Enhanced rate

Summary: Land & Property - Municipalities & Local Governments - Practice & Procedure - Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1977, s. 17(4) - Land allotment - Default in payments - Charge of interest - Enhanced rate - Justifiability - Appellant was allotted with an industrial plot by respondent (Authority) and allotment order indicated that in case of default of payment of installment, interest at the rate of 10% would be charged - Appellant committed default in payment of installments - However, in an appeal filed before SC by respondent, SC under a clarification order of its judgment; directed that if appellant pays the entire arrears as demanded by respondent within 3 months, the forfeiture effected by respondent should not take effect - Estate Officer made a demand from the appellant at an enhanced rate of interest at the rate of 18% - Aggrieved appellant filed a petition before HC, which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified - Held, respondents were entitled to charge interest at the rate of 10% only and not 18% - Since appellant had deposited interest at the rate of 18%, respondent Authority was required to refund the excess of interest realised from appellant - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
