Express Newspapers Private Limited and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 379; (1986) SCC (Cr) 67; (1986) 1 SCC 133; (1986) 1 SCC 259; AIR 1986 SC 872; 1986 (29) DLT 131; 1985(2) SCALE 973; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 382; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 382

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature; Rent Control

Keywords: Freedom Of Speech And Expression, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Allocation of Business Rules, 1961, Delhi Municipal Corporation (Buildings) Bye-laws, 1959, Delhi (Control of Building Operations) Act, 1955

Summary: Constitution - Media & Entertainment - Allocation of business Rules, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19(1)(a), 19(1)(g), 32, 53 and 239(1) - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Delhi Municipal Corporation (Buildings) Bye-laws, 1959 - Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957, ss. 343 and 344 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971, ss. 2(1) and 82(e) - Petitions u/art. 32 of Constitution, 1950 are by Petitioner No. 1, Express Newspapers Pvt. Ltd. engaged in business of printing and publishing national newspaper Indian Express - Respondent No. 1 is UOI - Petitioners challenge constitutional validity of a notice of re-entry upon forfeiture of lease issued purporting to be on behalf of lessor i.e., Govt. of India - Said notice required Petitioner No. 1 to show cause why UOI should not re-enter upon and take possession of demised premises - Also challenge validity of earlier notice issued to show cause why aforesaid buildings being unauthorized should not be demolished u/ss. 343 and 344 of Delhi Municipal Corporation Act, 1957 - Alleged that impugned notices constitute an act of personal vendetta against Express Group of Newspapers and are violative of arts. 14, 19(1)(a) and 19(1)(g) of Constitution, 1950 - (A) Whether this petition is maintainable? - Held, that impugned notices of re-entry upon forfeiture of lease and of threatened demolition of Express Buildings are intended and meant to silence voice of Indian Express - It constitute a direct and immediate threat to freedom of press and are thus violative of art. 19(1)(a) r/w art. 14 of Constitution, 1950 - Therefore, maintainable u/art. 32 - (B) Whether express building be considered as public premise u/s. 82(e) of Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorized Occupants) Act, 1971? - Held, this building was constructed with sanction of UOI - Demised on perpetual lease by registered lease-deed D/- 17-03-1958 - By no process of reasoning, be regarded as public premises - There is no question of lessor applying for eviction u/s. 2(1) - Further held that Estate Officer has no authority to direct their eviction thereof by summary process - (C) Whether Lt. Governor of Delhi could usurp functions of UOI, and direct an investigation into affairs of UOI? - Held, art. 53 vest executive power in President which he can exercise by delegating powers to his subordinate - There was not any order issued by President u/art. 239(1) of Constitution, 1950 investing Chief Commr. of Delhi to deal with property of Union - (D) When can power be said to be exercised malafidely and fraudulently? - Held, that a power is exercised fraudulently if its repository intends to achieve an object other than that for which he believes power to have been conferred - A power is exercised maliciously if its repository is motivated by personal animosity towards those who are directly affected by its exercise (E) Whether impugned Executive action was mala fide and politically motivated? - Held, that provisions was used for an 'alien' purpose and in bad faith i.e., for demolition of express Buildings with a mark of retribution or political vendetta for role of Indian Express during period of Emergency and thereby to bring about closure of Indian Express - (F) Application of doctrine of promissory estoppel - No Unauthorized deviations from sanctioned plan - Permission granted by Minister for Works and Housing for construction of new Express Building with an increased FAR of 360 with a double basement for installation of printing press was not in violation of Master Plan for Delhi or Zonal Development Plan for D-II area or Delhi Municipal Corporation (Buildings) Bye-laws, 1959 inasmuch as ex facie bye law 26 r/w 25(2)(IV-B) was not applicable to press area on Mathura Road - Held, lessor is clearly precluded from contending that order of Minister was illegal, improper or invalid by application of doctrine of promissory estoppel - Petition allowed.
