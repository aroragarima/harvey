N. Harihara Krishnan v J. Thomas

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

J. Chelameswar, S. Abdul Nazeer

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 756

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Investigating Agency, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Indian Contract Act, 1872, Sufficient Cause, Cheque, Cognizance, Proviso, Expiry, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173, 319 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 138(b), 141(1)(b), 142, 142(1)(a), 142(b) - Dishonor of cheque -Conviction - Sustainability - Respondent/complainant filed complaint u/s. 138 of the Act against appellant for issuance of cheques which were dishonored due to insufficient fund - Trial Court allowed application - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and affirmed impugned judgment of Trial Court - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, SC was of opinion that it was difficult to understand the conclusions recorded by both Courts below which were wholly illogical. The question whether respondent had sufficient cause for not filing the complaint against appellant's company within the period prescribed under the Act was not examined by either of Courts below. As rightly pointed out, the application which was the subject matter of appeal purportedly filed invoking s. 319 CrPC was only a device by which respondent seeks to initiate prosecution against appellant's company beyond the period of limitation stipulated under the Act. SC found no reason to condone the delay. The justification advanced by respondent that it was during the course of trial, respondent realized that cheque in question was drawn on account of appellant's company was a manifestly false statement. On the face of the cheque, it was clear that it was drawn on account of appellant's company. Admittedly respondent issued a notice contemplated u/s. 138(b) of the Act to appellant's company which fact was recorded by HC. Impugned judgment of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
