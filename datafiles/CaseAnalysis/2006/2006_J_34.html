Chairman, U.P.Jal Nigam & Anr. v Jaswant Singh & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 751; (2006) 11 SCC 464; AIR 2007 SC 924; 2006 (7) AWC 7435; 2007 (1) ESC 40; JT 2006 (10) SC 500; 2007 (2) LLJ 17; 2006(12) SCALE 347; 2007 (1) SLR 560; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 916

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 226, 32 - SC disposed of case of Harwindra Kumar along with other appeals and held that employees of Nigam are entitled to continue up to 60 years - Respondents are employees of Uttar Pradesh Jal Nigam - Whether employees who did not wake up to challenge their retirement and accepted same, collected their post retirement benefits, can such persons be given relief in light of subsequent decision delivered by this Court ? - Held, respondents are guilty since respondents has acquiesced in accepting retirement and did not challenge same in time - Only those persons who have filed WPs when they were in service or who have obtained interim order for their retirement, those persons should be allowed to stand to benefit and not others - Appeals disposed of.
