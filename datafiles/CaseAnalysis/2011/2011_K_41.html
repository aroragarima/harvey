The Secretary, All India Pre-Medical/Pre-Dental Examination, C.B.S.E. And Others v Khushboo Shrivastava And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 501; (2014) 14 SCC 523; 2011 (9) ADJ 12; 2012(1) ALL MR 436; 2011 (6) AWC 5678; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 352; 2011 (4) ESC 637; JT 2011 (11) SC 648; 2011(9) SCALE 63; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 286; 2011 (5) SLR 466

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Education - Practice & Procedure - Evaluation of answer sheet by HC - Legality - Respondent appeared for All India Pre-Medical/Pre-Dental Entrance Examination, 2007, conducted by the appellant Board - Respondent submitted representation to appellant for re-examination and re-totalling of her marks in Physics, Chemistry and Biology - Appellant informed that there was no provision for re-checking/re-evaluation of answer sheets of the candidates - Respondent filed writ petition - Single Judge compared the answers of the 1st respondent with the model answers and held that respondent was not given marks for her answers to the two questions - However, Single Judge held that the seats for the Pre-Medical Course on the basis of the All India Pre-Medical/Pre-Dental Entrance Examination, 2007 were already allotted to the successful candidates and the successful candidates had completed one year study and therefore no relief could be granted to the 1st respondent - Respondent filed writ appeal - DB moulded the relief granted by Single Judge and directed that 1st respondent be admitted in the MBBS Course in the next academic session 2009-2010 - Whether the HC was justified in awarding two additional marks after scrutinizing the answer sheets of respondent - Held, bye-laws of the All India Pre- Medical/Pre-Dental Entrance Examination, 2007 conducted by the appellant did not provide for re-examination or re- evaluation of answers sheets - Hence, the appellants could not have allowed such re-examination or re-evaluation on the representation of the 1st respondent and accordingly rejected the representation of the 1st respondent for re-examination/re-evaluation of her answers sheets - Neither the Single Judge nor the DB could have substituted his/its own views for that of the examiners and awarded two additional marks to the 1st respondent for the two answers in exercise of powers of judicial review u/art. 226 of the Constitution as these were purely academic matters - Impugned judgement of the HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
