Rambir Singh and others v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2228; (2009) 17 SCC 574; 2011 (5) RCR(Criminal) 737; 2009(13) SCALE 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000, ss.7-A, 7A(1), 7-A(2), 15 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Rules, 2007, rr.12,98 - Conviction of juvenile - Sustainability - Petitioners/accused persons-appellants were convicted by Court below, filed instant appeal - On application, appellant no.4 claimed that he was a juvenile on date of commission of alleged offence and question of juvenility of appellant no.4 was not been taken either before Trial Court or before HC - Whether question of juvenility can be raised at any stage before any Court and even after conviction.

Held, having regard to provisions of Act, and amendments effected thereto in 2006, by introduction of s.7-A of Act, and framing of Rules, having particular regard to r.12 of Rules, it is now settled that such a question of juvenility can be raised at any time before any Court and even after conviction. Having regard to, case of appellant no.4 will have to be treated separately from other appellants. In school certificate of Higher School Examination conducted in year 1989, it appears that date of birth of appellant is shown as 15-12-1972, which would make age of appellant less than 18 years on date of alleged incident. Since in this case appellant no.4 has already been convicted and sentenced to life imprisonment, his case will be covered by provisions of s.7-A (2) of Act. Having regard to, let case of appellant no.4 be transmitted to Juvenile Justice Board, for passing appropriate orders in terms of s.15 of Act, r/w r.98 of Rules, within two months from date of receipt of a copy of this order. Appeal as far as appellant no.4 is concerned, is accordingly, allowed to said extent. Let appeal as far as other appellants are concerned be listed for hearing in usual course. Order accordingly.
