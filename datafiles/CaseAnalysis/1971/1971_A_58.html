Azam Jha Bahadur (Dead) By His Legal Representatives v Expenditure Tax Officer, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 412; (1971) 3 SCC 621; AIR 1972 SC 2319; [1972] 83 ITR 92; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 470

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Family Property, Evasion Of Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Expenditure Tax Act, 1957, amended by Finance Act, 1959, ss. 16(a), 16(b), 16(i)(a)(b) - A/ys 1959-1960, 1960-1961 and 1961-1962 - Supplementary returns - Assessment re-opened - Challenged - Assessee filed returns for purpose of assessment of Expenditure Tax for A/y 1959-1960, 1960-1961 and 1961-1962 and were completed - Thereafter, Expenditure Tax Officer issued notices u/s. 16 of the Act and called assessee to file supplementary returns for three years in question on ground assessee's expenditure had escaped assessment or had been under- assessed - Assessee filed supplementary returns declaring the same expenditure as shown in original returns - Further, assessee or assessee's representative was informed by Expenditure Tax Officer that assessments had been reopened for including expenditure incurred by assessee's wife - Aggrieved assessee filed petitions in HC challenging reopening of assessments which were dismissed - Appeals were filed by assessee under Letters Patent which were decided by full bench of HC, who upheld judgment of learned single judge and dismissed appeals and wrote separate judgment and expressed somewhat different view on some of points - Hence, instant appeals by both parties - Whether order passed by full bench of HC was sustainable - Held, it was no doubt true that impression created by notices which were issued and correspondence which followed between assessee and Expenditure Tax Officer was that notices had been issued u/s. 16 (a) of the Act but in petitions and returns which were filed, both sides were quite clear that matter was not confined only to s. 16 (i)(a) and (b)?? figured prominently - This Court were unable to see that notices which had been issued were confined only to terms of s. 16(a)??, but it was not disputed by assessee that if matter was covered by s. 16(b), they would be perfectly valid - Therefore, pleadings in petitions covered both clauses of s. 16?? and in any case, Expenditure Tax Officer had made positive averment that information with regard to expenditure incurred by assessee's wife became available to him only on dt. 5-5-1962 - Thus notices which were issued on that date relating to A/ys 1959-1960, 1960-1961 and 1961-1962, were within period of four years which was limit prescribed with regard to action u/cl. (b) limit being more in respect of cl. (a) - In SC judgment, that concludes matter because it was nowhere controverted in HC that 'requisite information come into possession of Expenditure Tax Officer only on dt. 5-5-1962 - In result Civil Appeal Nos. 1794-1796 of 1967 were dismissed but other set of Civil Appeal Nos. 2389-2391 of 1968, of Commissioner of Expenditure Tax were allowed - Therefore, answer given by HC in that case to question referred by Tribunal shall stand discharged and instead answer to question would be in affirmative and in favour of Revenue - Appeals disposed of.
