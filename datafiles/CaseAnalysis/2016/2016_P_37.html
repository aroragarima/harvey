Pepsico India Holding Private Limited and another v Grocery Market and Shops Board and others

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 155; (2016) 4 SCC 493; AIR 2016 SC 841; 2016(3) ALL MR 412; 2016 (3) AWC 2277; 2016 (150) FLR 129; JT 2016 (2) SC 217; 2016 (2) LLJ 185; 2016(2) SCALE 320; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 305; 2016 (3) SLR 35

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Welfare, Contract Labour, provident fund, Registration, Chemical, Health, Loading, Cleaning, Contract Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970, Labour, Safety, Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and Other Manual Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969, Grocery Markets or Shops Unprotected Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Scheme, 1970, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 254 - Contract Labour (Regulation and Abolition) Act, 1970. s. 30 (1) - Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and Other Manual Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969, ss. 3, 4(1), 4(1)(b), 5 - Grocery Markets or Shops Unprotected Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Scheme, 1970 - Manufacture of chemical product - Application of Act - Determination - It was stated that u/s. 5 of 1969 Act, if any question arises whether any scheme applies to any class of unprotected workers, matter shall be referred to State Govt. and decision of State Govt. shall be taken after consulting Advisory Committee constituted u/s. 14 of 1969 Act shall be final - Appellant no.1 company was engaged in manufacturing Polystyrene - With respect to application of 1969 Act and 1970 Scheme, State Govt. held that they are applicable to appellant no.1 company, hence, application of appellant u/s. 5 of 1969 Act was rejected by Govt. - Further, HC vide impugned order dismissed petition and upheld order passed by State Govt. on grounds that appellant no.1 was manufacturing Polystyrene which was not a petrochemical product but a chemical product and chemical product was covered under Act and Scheme - Whether 1969 Act and 1970 Scheme is applicable to appellant no.1 company.

Held, contention of appellant that 1970 Scheme, insofar as it provides for employment in factory manufacturing chemical products, is ultra vires Schedule to 1969 Act, has to be rejected on ground that cl. 5 of Schedule to Act is residuary clause which would rope in employment in factories in connection with loading, unloading, etc. carried on by workers not covered by any other entries in Schedule. Further, SC held that manufacture of petro chemicals in factories is not covered by any other entry including entry 4 to Schedule. Hence, provisions of 1970 Scheme dealing with manufacture of petro chemicals in factories would be within coverage of residuary entry i.e. Item 5 of Schedule to 1969 Act. Hence, no part of 1970 Scheme is ultra vires 1969 Act. Further, submission that appellant allegedly manufactures petro chemical products and not chemical products has been correctly repelled by DB by stating that 'petro chemical products' would be species of genus 'chemical products'. Further, appellant has admitted that it manufactures polystyrene (granules). Polystyrene in turn has been described as inexpensive and hard plastic which is vinyl polymer. Further, s. 4(1) of 1969 Act also makes it clear that State Govt. may make one or more Schemes for any scheduled employment or group of scheduled employments. Hence, present scheme specifically takes within its ken factories manufacturing chemical products covered by entry 5 in schedule to 1969 Act, and would therefore be scheme which provides for registration of unprotected workers in different scheduled employments and/or group of scheduled employments. Further, with respect contention 1969 Act being inconsistent with 1970 Act would be repugnant to said Act, hence, invalid u/art. 254 of Constitution, SC held that s. 30(1) of 1970 Act provides that despite provisions of 1970 Act being allegedly inconsistent with 1969 State Act, yet if contract labour employed in establishment are entitled to benefits which are more favourable to them than those to which they would be entitled under 1970 Act, contract labour shall continue to be entitled to more favourable benefits, notwithstanding that they also receive benefits in respect of other matters under Central Parliamentary Act. Appeal dismissed.

(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Administrative - Maharashtra Mathadi, Hamal and Other Manual Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Act, 1969 - Grocery Markets or Shops Unprotected Workers (Regulation of Employment and Welfare) Scheme, 1970 - Manufacture of Soft Drinks - Application of Act - Determination - Appellant in instant case was company which was manufacturing soft drinks being aerated water and bottled water - Further, State Govt. vide order dt. 18-8-2008 made u/s. 5 of 1969 Act held that 1970 Scheme, was applicable to Company - Further, HC vide impugned order upheld order passed by State Govt. on ground that all items used by appellant in manufacturing process were grocery items, hence, 1970 Act was rightly applicable to appellant company - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, expression 'grocery' in 2005, when Act was sought to be applied to appellant company, would include soft drinks manufactured by appellant company and bottled water as daily household goods among middle class and rich sections of society. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed

Ratio - In case of social legislation, Court needs to apply doctrine of construing legislation in expansive manner to further object of welfare Legislation.
