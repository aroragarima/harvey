Akmal Ahmad v State of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1032; (1999) 3 SCC 337; (1999) SCC (Cr) 425; AIR 1999 SC 1315; 1999 (38) AllCriC 623; 1999 (36) ALR 202; 1999 (2) CCR 54; 1999 (2) Crimes 12; 1999 CRLJ 2041; JT 1999 (2) SC 388; 1999 (2) RCR(Criminal) 265; 1999(2) SCALE 234; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 160; 1999 (3) SLT 192; 1999 (3) Supreme 151

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Foreigners

Summary: Indian Passport Act, 1967, ss. 3 and 12 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987, s. 5 - Unlawful possession of fire-arms-Proof - Accused arrested and found in possession of fire-arm without licence in a notified area - Held, search and seizure made by police not vitiated merely because it was not supported by independent witness - Presumption that such possession was with intention to use them for a terrorist or disruptive act can be raised - No rebuttal of said presumption - Conviction of accused under s. 5, proper.
