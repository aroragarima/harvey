Vasudeo Vishwanath Saraf v New Education Institute and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

B.C. Ray, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 140; (1986) 4 SCC 31; (1986) 4 SCC 33; (1986) SCC (L&S) 711; AIR 1986 SC 2105; 1986 ATC 334; [1986] 60 Comp Cas 943; 1986 (57) CTR 100; [1986] 161 ITR 835; JT 1986 SC 80; 1986 (2) LLN 757; 1986(2) SCALE 258; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 458; 1986 (3) SLR 96; 1986 (63) STC 363; 1986 (2) UJ 682

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Without giving proper reasons - Sustainability - Petitioner was appointed as Assistant Teacher by registered society/respondent No. 1 - Petitioner was promoted as supervisor and thereafter worked as Principal till reversion by resolution of the managing committee of the Institute - Petitioner filed suit against resolution that petitioner was entitled to have all benefits and emoluments as principal of the said institution which was dismissed by Trial Court - Appellate court allowed appeal holding that petitioner was entitled to have all the benefits and emoluments as principal - Respondent No.1 filed appeal before HC - During pendency of appeal, respondent No. 1 commenced departmental enquiry against petitioner - Enquiry Committee terminated petitioner's services - Petitioner filed appeal to Deputy Director of Education/respondent No. 4 and directed petitioner's service should not be terminated till Civil Court would decide suit - Petitioner made representation to Govt. to decide about appeal - Govt. forwarded to School Tribunal which was dismissed without giving proper reasons - Petitioner filed petition before HC which was rejected - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC and Tribunal rightly rejected appeal of petitioner without giving proper reasons - Held, it was cardinal principle of rule of law which governed policy that Court including Writ Court was required to record reasons while disposing of writ petition in order to enable litigants particularly by aggrieved party to know the reasons which weighed with the mind of Court in determining questions of facts and law raised in writ petition or in action brought - It was imperative for fair and equitable administration of justice when there was statutory provision for appeal to higher Court in hierarchy of Courts in order to enable superior Court or Appellate Court to know or to be apprised of the reasons which impelled Court to pass order in question - Recording of reasons in deciding cases or applications affecting rights of parties was mandatory requirement to be fulfilled in consonance with principles of natural justice - It was no answer to instant legal position that for purpose of expeditious disposal of cases laconic order like dismissed or rejected would be made without passing reasoned order or speaking order - In view taken from Madhya Pradesh Industries Ltd. v. Union of India Ors., 1965 Indlaw SC 171 and Mahabir Jute Mills v. Shibbon Lal, 1975 Indlaw SC 36 stated that where it had been observed that practice of executive authority dismissing statutory appeals against order which seriously prejudice rights of aggrieved party without giving reasons was negation of rule of law - Appeal allowed.
