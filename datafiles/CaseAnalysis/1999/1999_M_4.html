Collector of Central Excise, Baroda v Cotspun Limited

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, S.S.M. Quadri, V.N. Khare, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 82; (1999) 7 SCC 633; AIR 2000 SC 197; 1999 (84) ECR 817; 1999 (113) E.L.T. 353; JT 1999 (7) SC 477; 1999 (34) RLT 709; 1999(6) SCALE 223; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 184

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise reassessment - Differential duty - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10, 173-B - Classification list filed by Respondent for NES yarn manufactured by it was approved - Differential duty demanded from retrospective effect - Whether levy of duty on revised assessment to take place from retrospective effect? - In 1994 Indlaw SC 775, it was held by Supreme Court that: 'Once Dept accepted price list, acted upon it and goods were cleared with knowledge of Department,then in absence of any amendment in law or judicial pronouncement, reclassification should be effective from date Department issued show cause notice; reason for it is clearance with knowledge of Department and no intention to evade payment of duty' - Levy of excise duty on basis of an approved classification list is correct levy at least until such time as to correctness of approval is questioned by issuance to assessee of a show cause notice - Levy of excise duty on basis of an approved classification list is not a short levy - Differential duty cannot be recovered on ground that it is a short levy - No short levy and respondent not liable to pay differential duty with retrospective effect - Held, aforesaid decision of Supreme Court correctly lays down law - Appeal dismissed.
