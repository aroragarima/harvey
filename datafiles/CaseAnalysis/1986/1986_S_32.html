Commissioner Of Income Tax, Kanpur v Saran Engineering Co. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

31 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 181; (1986) 3 SCC 662; AIR 1986 SC 1943; 1986 (58) CTR 183; [1986] 161 ITR 741; JT 1986 SC 7; 1986(2) SCALE 112; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 398; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 550; 1986 (2) TLR 1298; 1986 (2) UJ 485

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Super Profit Tax Act, 1963, ss. 2(9), sch. 2, r. 1 - Companies (Profits) Sur tax Act, 1964, s. 2(8), sch. 2, r. 1 - Computation of capital of the company - (A) Super Profit Tax Act - (1) Capital reserve - Amount, not earmarked for any existing liability - (2) Stock and Stores Reserve - Amount not representing any existing provision for existing liability to meet any specific contingency for safeguarding against diminution of the value of the stocks and stores - (3) Bad and Doubtful Debts Reserve - Amount more than that reasonably necessary to be provided in respect of such debts - (4) Loan and Insurance Reserve - Fund free from any burden and not utilised for any purpose - Transferred to General Reserve Account - (5) Investment reserve - Unanticipated future losses - Reserve created for adjusting losses normally arising in course of business - Reserve created out of capital profit - Treatment of - (B) Companies (Profits) Sur-tax Act - Provision and Reserve - (1) Whether Forfeited Moneys Reserve can be treated as reserve - (2) Provision and Reserve - Fund created for payment of liability, not already arisen - (A) Held, it represented reserve - It must be treated as reserve - Held, it constituted reserve, it could not be said that by very nomenclature this was not a reserve and true nature of transaction had to be examined - Held, it must be treated as reserve - It would be treated as other reserve - (B) Held, it could not be treated as reserve - It is 'other reserve' - Appeal partly allowed.
