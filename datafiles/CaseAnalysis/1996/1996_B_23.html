Baddula Lakshmaiah and Others v Sri Anjaneya Swami Temple and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 February 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 579; (1996) 3 SCC 52; 1996 (2) AD(SC) 377; 1996 (1) CCC 297; 1996 (1) CLT 523; JT 1996 (3) SC 198; 1996 (4) KarLJ 737; 1996(2) SCALE 409; [1996] 2 S.C.R. 906; 1996 (2) Supreme 461

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Interpretation of deeds and documents - Whether Letters Patent Bench of the High Court erred fresh reconciliation of the two documents, arriving at different results than those arrived at earlier by the two Courts? - Such is not an appeal against an order of a Subordinate Court and in such appellate jurisdiction the HC exercises the powers of a Court of error - Held, construction of the two documents involved, in the very nature of their import, a mixed question of law and fact, well within the powers of the Letters Patent Bench to decide - Letters Patent Bench committed no error in re-doing the exercise to reconcile those two questioned documents so as to get to the result in favour of the temple-respondent - Appeal dismissed.
