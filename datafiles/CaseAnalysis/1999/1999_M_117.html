Olympus Superstructures Private Limited v Meena Vijay Khetan and Others

Supreme Court of India

11 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1985; (1999) 5 SCC 651; AIR 1999 SC 2102; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 355; 2001 (4) CompLJ 370; JT 1999 (3) SC 514; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 490

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Arbitral Award, Admissibility Of Evidence, Accord And Satisfaction, The English Arbitration Act, 1996, English Arbitration Act, 1950, 38. For the aforesaid reasons, the appeals fail and are dismissed but in the circumstances without costs.

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 5, 16, 30, 33, 34 and 34(2)(b)(i) - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Agreements under which appellant agreed to sell Flats - Arbitration clause - Disputes - Arbitrator passed award - Appellant challenged awards u/s. 34 - (A) Whether disputes and differences arising under Interior Design Agreement are integrally connected with disputes and differences arising from main contract? - Held, they are - Coverage of two agreements makes it clear that execution of Interior Design Agreement is connected with execution of main agreement - (B) Whether an arbitrator is not entitled to pass an award directing specific performance of an agreement of sale and subject-matter of dispute is not capable of arbitration u/s. 34(2)(b)(i) of Act? - Held, right to specific performance of an agreement of sale deals with contractual rights and it is certainly open to parties to agree with a view to shorten litigation in regular courts to refer issues relating to specific performance to arbitration - There is no prohibition in Specific Relief Act, 1963 that issues relating to specific performance of contract relating to immovable property cannot be referred to arbitration - Nor is there such a prohibition contained in Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Merely because sections of Specific Relief Act confer discretion on courts to grant specific performance of a contract does not mean that parties cannot agree that discretion will be exercised by a forum of their choice - Disputes relating to specific performance of a contract can be referred to arbitration and s. 34(2)(b)(i) is not attracted - (C) Whether appellant could question factual findings relating to default, time being of essence, readiness and willingness etc. before arbitrator u/s. 34 of the Act? - U/cl. (b) of s. 34(2), interference is permissible by court only if (i) subject-matter of dispute is not capable of settlement by arbitration under law for time being in force, or (ii) arbitral amount is in conflict with public policy in India - Factual points raised in present case do not fall within s. 34(2)(b)(ii) - Subject-matter of dispute is not incapable of settlement by arbitration under law for time being in force - Nor is any point raised that arbitral award is in conflict with public policy of India - Merits of award on facts of case do not fall u/s. 34(2)(b) of Act - Appeals dismissed.
