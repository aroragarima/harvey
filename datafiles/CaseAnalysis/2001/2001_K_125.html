U. P. Pollution Control Board and Others v Kanoria Industrial Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20329; (2001) 2 SCC 549; AIR 2001 SC 787; 2001 ALJ 425; 2001 (1) AWC 629; 2003 (180) CTR 402; 2001 (128) E.L.T. 3; [2003] 259 ITR 321; JT 2001 (2) SC 103; 2001 (2) MLJ(SC) 36; 2001(1) SCALE 381; 2001 (2) SCJ 108; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 559; 2001 (1) SLT 731; 2002 (128) STC 26; 2001 (1) Supreme 507

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977

Summary: Indirect Tax - Water - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Water (Prevention and Control of Pollution) Cess Act, 1977 - Whether petition u/art. 226 maintainable for refund of amount paid under protest by respondents as water cess under provisions of Act? - Petition is merely for refund of money - Held, collection of money as cess was itself without authority of law there is no good reason to deny a relief of refund to citizens in such cases on principles of public interest and equity - Further held, distinction is made between claims made for refund where collection of tax was illegal and not authorised and cases where rule had been struck down on ground that it had been framed inarticulately and was not clear enough - Further, held, claim for refund cannot be denied merely on ground that petitioners passed on money to state Govt. and in turn money was sent to Central Govt. - Petitions dismissed.
