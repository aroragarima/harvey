Messrs Construction India v Secretary, Works Department, Government of Orissa and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

Sujata V. Manohar, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1194; (1998) 2 SCC 89; AIR 1998 SC 717; 1998 (1) ARBLR 127; 1998 (1) CLT 63; JT 1997 (10) SC 220; 1998 (2) MLJ(SC) 85; 1998 (1) RAJ 133; 1997(7) SCALE 693; 1998 (2) SLT 85; [1997] Supp6 S.C.R. 371

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 8 - Agreements between appellants and respondents - Arbitration clauses - Application made by appellant u/s. 8 of Arbitration Act, 1940 - Court appointed arbitrator by consent of parties from panel of names given by both parties - Arbitration proceedings - Respondents filed their counter-claim - Awards passed - Respondents challenged these awards - Jurisdiction of arbitrator under challenge - Held, jurisdiction which is conferred on an arbitrator is on account of consent of parties to arbitration agreement - Withdrawing objection as to jurisdiction of arbitrator amounts to acceptance of jurisdiction - Therefore, precluded from objecting to such appointment as invalidating in subsequent proceedings - Further held, mistake in recording minutes of one date cannot be ground for setting aside arbitration award - Award can be set aside only when there is non application of mind by arbitrator - Appeal allowed.
