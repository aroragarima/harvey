Nathu v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 1956 SC 56; 1956 CRLJ 152

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Constitution Of India, 1950 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Appellant convicted on confession statement of other two accused persons before magistrate - Whether conviction of appellant proper? - Held, confession statement can be relied only if there is other evidence also leading to conviction - Further held, no substantive and independent evidence on which a conviction could be based so confessions cannot be used for sustaining conviction - Conviction set-aside - Appeal allowed.
