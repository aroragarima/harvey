Rudul Sah v State of Bihar and Another

Supreme Court of India

01 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, Amarendra Nath Sen, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 231; (1983) 4 SCC 141; (1983) SCC (Cr) 798; AIR 1983 SC 1086; 1983 CRLJ 1644; 1983 CrLR(SC) 638; 1983(2) SCALE 103; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 508; 1983 UJ 759

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Inter-State Migrant Workmen (Regulation Of Employment And Conditions Of Service), Illegal detention

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 32 - Illegal detention of petitioner after acquittal in trial and compensation for that matter - Petitioner was acquitted on 3-06-1968 but released on 16-10-1982 i.e. released after more than 14 years - Held, state should pay petitioner a further sum of Rs. 30,000/- as an interim measure and it did not preclude petitioner from bringing a suit to recover appropriate damages from State and its erring officials - Appeal allowed.
