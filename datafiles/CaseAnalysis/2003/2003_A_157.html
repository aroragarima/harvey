K. Shamrao and others v Assistant Charity Commissioner

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 222; (2003) 3 SCC 563; AIR 2003 SC 1828; 2003 (2) CCR 7; 2003 (4) CRJ 304; 2003 CRLJ 1575; 2003 (1) JCC 487; JT 2003 (2) SC 378; 2003(2) SCALE 585; [2003] 2 S.C.R. 523; 2003 (2) SLT 500; 2003 (2) Supreme 445; 2003 (2) UPLBEC 1245

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950, Contempt Of Courts Act, 1952, Bihar and Orissa Co-operative Societies Act 1935, Public Servants Inquiries Act, 1850

Summary: Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Ss. 2 and 3 - Bombay Public Trust Act, 1950 - S. 5 - whether Assistant Charity Commissioner appointed under Section 5 of the Bombay Public Trust Act as applicable to Karnataka is a 'court' for the purposes of the Contempt of Courts Act - Held, the Assistant Charity Commissioner has almost all the powers which an ordinary Civil Court has including power of summoning witnesses, compelling production of documents, examining witnesses on oath and coming to a definite conclusion on the evidence induced and arguments submitted. - Therefore, the Assistant Charity Commissioner is a Court for the purposes of Sections 2 and 3 of the Contempt of Courts Act, 1971.
