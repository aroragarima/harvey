Bhoop Alleged Son of Sheo v Matadin Bhardwaj (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

04 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, K. Jagannatha Shetty

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 233; (1991) 2 SCC 128; AIR 1991 SC 373; 1991 (1) BLJR 603; 1991 (1) CCC 403; JT 1990 (4) SC 594; 1991 RD 34; 1990(2) SCALE 1204; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 410; 1991 (1) UJ 247

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 146, O. 20, rr. 14 and 16 - (A) Pre-emption decree - Decree-holder directed by Court to deposit past consideration within specified time - Delay of one day as presiding officer of court was transferred - Whether party could be made to suffer for no fault of his own? - (B) Nature of Right of Pre-emption - Pre-emptor decree-holder depositing purchase money within time - Title in pre-emptional land accrues to him - Assignment of this right in land by decree-holder - If amount to transfer of mere decree - Held, No delay - Does not amount to, and amount must be taken to have been deposited within time allowed - Assignee is entitled to execute decree and obtain possession in view of s. 146 and 0. 20, rr. 14 and 16 of CPC, 1908 -

Appeal dismissed.
