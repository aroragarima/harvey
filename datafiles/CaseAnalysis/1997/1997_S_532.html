Jaspal Singh Arora v State of Madhya Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2092; (1998) 9 SCC 594

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961

Summary: Municipalities & Local Governments - Election - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 243-ZG - Challenge to elections - Whether election could be called in question in Writ proceedings? - Held, election could not be called in question except by an election petition as provided under that Act - Apart from bar u/art. 243-ZG, on settled principles interference u/art. 226 of Constitution for purpose of setting aside election to a municipality was not called for because of statutory provision for election petition and also fact that an earlier writ petition for same purpose by a defeated candidate had been dismissed by High Court - Appeals allowed.
