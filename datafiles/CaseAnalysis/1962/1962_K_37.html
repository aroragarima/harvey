Â Gujarat University, Ahmedabad v Krishna Ranganath Mudholkar and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 September 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 491; AIR 1963 SC 703; 1963 (4) G.L.R. 450; 1964 (1) SCJ 504; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 112

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Education - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Prohibition Act - Gujarat University Act, 1949, ss.4 (27) 18(1) (XIV) and 38 A - Admission - Sanction of University - Medium of instruction - Respondent No. 2 joined First Year Arts Class of St. Xavier's College, affiliated to Gujarat University where instruction was imparted through medium of English and after successfully completing that course sought admission to classes preparing for Intermediate Arts Examination of University through medium of English - Principal of college informed that provisions of 1949 Act and statutes 207, 208 and 209 framed by Senate of University, as amended in 1961, he could not be admitted without sanction of University - Respondent No.1 moved Vice-Chancellor but sanction was refused - Respondents moved HC u/art.226 of Constitution for writs requiring university and Principal of College not to enforce provisions of ss. 4 (27)and 38-A of 1949 Act - Court holding in favour of respondents - Impugned provisions were valid - Whether University had power to prescribe an exclusive medium of instruction u/s.38A of 1949 Act - Held, constitution depended upon State Legislatures and universities for imparting education at university level - Particular university accelerated pace of introduction of a regional language as medium of instruction at university level, but other universities were following a more cautious policy - University had power to prescribe an exclusive medium of instruction u/s.38A of 1949 Act which was a consequential provision would be valid - University was well within its rights in prescribing, by statutes, two languages as media of instruction to replace English by stages - Appeals disposed of.
