Seth Badri Prasad and Others v Seth Nagarmal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 126; AIR 1959 SC 559; [1959] 29 Comp Cas 229; 1959 CompLJ 205; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 769; [1959] Supp1 S.C.R. 769

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Stamp Act, Indian Contract Act

Summary: Corporate - Practice & Procedure - Rewa State Companies Act, 1935, s. 4 - Indian Partnership Act, 1932, s. 69(3)(a) - Accounts - Partnership Firm - Unregistered - Challenged - Association consisted of 25 members who made contributions to the initial capital of association - Respondent no. 1 was President of said Association from January 1946 to 26-6- 1946 - Before that one of plaintiffs/appellants was President and Association worked till February 1948, then cloth was decontrolled and work of Association came to end - On 25-6- 1949, 13 members of Association out of 25 brought suit, and in plaint they alleged that respondent no. 1, who was President of Association had given account of income and expenditure for the months of January, February and March, 1946, but had given no accounts for months of April, May and June, 1946 before Trial Court which passes preliminary decree in favour of appellants - Aggrieved respondent filed appeal before Judicial Commissioner who allowed appeal - Hence, instant appeal - Whether preliminary objection was to be considered - Held, that analogy of s. 69(3)(a) of the 1932 Act was enough to point out that under the 1932 Act unregistered firm was not illegal, there was no direct compulsion that partnership firm must be registered, though disabilities consequent on non-registration might be extremely inconvenient - Moreover, suit before Court was not one for accounts of dissolved firm, but for accounts of illegal association which was in existence at relevant period for which accounts were asked - Hence, preliminary objection succeeds - Appeal dismissed.
