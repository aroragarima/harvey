U.P. Junior Doctors' Action Committee and Others v Dr. B. Sheetal Nandwani and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 882; (1992) Supp1 SCC 680; AIR 1992 SC 671; AIR 1991 SCW 333; 1992 ALJ 224; JT 1992 (1) SC 571; 1991(2) SCALE 1103; 1992 (2) SLR 94; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 384; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 1331

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Medical Council

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 226 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 94(e) - Admission to post-graduate courses in Medical Colleges - Whether should be based on selection test or on basis of MBBS results alone ? - Provisional admission - Interlocutory stage - Whether can normally be granted ? - Held, to have compliance of art. 14 a broad-based arrangement should be made - No admission should be permitted on basis of MBBS result - Should not be granted unless special reasons exist - Petition dismissed.
