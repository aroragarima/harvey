G. Sarana v University of Lucknow and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 383; (1976) SCC (L&S) 474; (1976) 3 SCC 585; AIR 1976 SC 2428; 1976 (2) ALR 621; 1976 LabIC 1546; 1977 (1) LLJ 68; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 64; 1976 SLJ 562; 1976 (2) SLR 509; 1976 UJ 701

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Natural justice - Bias - Writ challenging decision of selection board on ground of bias of member - Whether a member of an administrative board while exercising quasi-judicial powers or discharging quasi-judicial functions was biased, for it is difficult to prove mind of a person? - What has to be seen is whether there is a reasonable ground for believing that he was likely to have been biased - In deciding question of bias, human probabilities and ordinary course of human conduct have to be taken into consideration - In a group de liberation and decision like that of a selection board, members do not function as computers - Each member of group or board is bound to influence others, more so if member concerned is a person with special knowledge - His bias is likely to operate in a subtle manner - Held, where a candidate for selection knowing fully well relevant facts about the members of selection board voluntarily appeared for interview without raising any kind of objection against constitution of selection board and took a chance of favourable recommendation in his favour, it was not open to him to turn round and question constitution of board when decision was unfavourable to him - Further held, writ petition or for that matter present appeal not maintainable when recommendation of selection committee has still to be scrutinized and other remedies by way of representation are still open to appellant and have not been exhausted - Appeal dismissed.
