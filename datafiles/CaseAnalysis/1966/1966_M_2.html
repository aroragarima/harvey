K. P. Raghavan and Another v M. H. Abbas and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 397; AIR 1967 SC 740; 1967 CRLJ 653

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 207-A and 209 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 330 - Voluntarily causing hurt to extort confession - Appellants had allegedly beaten up complainants to extort confession - Complaint purported to bring a charge u/s. 330, IPC offence exclusively triable by Court of Session - Magistrate refused to commit case to sessions for trial - On appeal to Sessions Court by complainants, Sessions Judge ordered commitment to Sessions Court and order upheld by High Court - Whether case against appellants can be committed to Sessions Court for facing trial? - Held, there was direct evidence regarding offence committed by appellants and Magistrate was wrong in not committing case to Sessions Court - Sessions Court and HC order upheld - 'Bipat Gope v. State of Bihar' 1962 Indlaw SC 210 followed - Appeal dismissed.
