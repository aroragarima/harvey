Tulsidas Kalichand v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I.

Supreme Court of India

03 January 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 58; AIR 1961 SC 1023; [1961] 42 ITR 1; 1962 (2) MLJ(SC) 123; 1962 (2) SCJ 500; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 351

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Trusts Act

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 16(1) (c) - Indian Trusts Act, 1882, ss. 5, 6 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Trusts Act - Assessment year - Settlement or disposition - Formal transfer - Adequate consideration - Assessment year 1952-1953 under consideration 'A' one of four appellants made declaration of trust in favour of his wife - Assessee contended that income after being grossed up was not liable to be included in his total income in view of third proviso to s. 16(1)(c) of 1922 Act - Income Tax Officer ordered that words of proviso income arising to any person by virtue of settlement or disposition did not apply to this income - Appellate Assistant Commissioner also dismissed the appeal - On appeal Tribunal concluded that case was covered either by s.16(3) (a)(iii) or by s.16(3)(b) and income from shares was liable to be included in income of 'A' - HC concluded that s. 16(1)(c) was not satisfied and s. 16(3)(b) was not applicable - Whether there was transfer of assets to wife or to 'any person' for benefit of wife and whether there was adequate consideration for transfer - Held, transfer by 'A' to himself as trustee though there was no formal transfer - Deed regarded as involving transfer by husband to trustee and even though husband was same individual in his capacity as trustee he must be regarded as person distinct from transferor and s.16(3)(b) covers the case - Words adequate consideration denote consideration other than mere love and affection in case of wife may be presumed - When law insists that there should be adequate consideration and not good consideration it excludes mere love and affection - Case falls within special rules concerning wife and minor child laid down in s. 16(3) (b) and not within third proviso to s. 16(1) (c) - There was transfer of assets to husband/trustee for benefit of wife - Order passed by HC justified - Appeal dismissed.
