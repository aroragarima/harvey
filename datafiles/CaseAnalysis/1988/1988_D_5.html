State of Orissa v Dandasi Sahu

Supreme Court of India

22 July 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 920; (1988) 4 SCC 12; AIR 1988 SC 1791; 1988 (2) ARBLR 384; JT 1988 (3) SC 364; 1988 (2) KerLJ 323; 1988(2) SCALE 285; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 562; 1988 (2) UJ 463

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution of India,1950 - Whether appeal under special appeal can be postponed because a larger bench is hearing same? - Whether pendency of appeal challenging validity of non reasoned award before larger bench of Supreme Court would not warrant postponement of appeal u/art. 136 against non-reasoned award which is under challenge? - Whether award without reason is bad per se? - No apparent mistake on face of award - Award amount quite high - Interest pendente lite - Held, appeal is not required to be postponed despite being non-reasoned - Award without reasons is not bad per se - The award will neither be remitted nor set aside - Higher award amount does not vitiate it - Interest pendente lite can not be granted.
