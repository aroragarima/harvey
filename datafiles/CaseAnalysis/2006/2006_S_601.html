P. Mani v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 307; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 36; (2006) 3 SCC 161; AIR 2006 SC 1319; 2006 CRLJ 1629; 2006 (3) CTC 193; 2006 (1) JCC 447; JT 2006 (2) SC 607; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 159; 2006(2) SCALE 482; 2006 (4) SCJ 783; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 486

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 106, 113A - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 307 - Conviction for Murder - Ambiguity in Evidences - Sentence - Sustainability - Appellant was convicted on charge of commission of offence u/s. 302 IPC and sentenced to undergo rigorous imprisonment for life - HC held that burden of proof lied upon appellant in terms of s. 106 of the Act, in view of fact that appellant did not suffer any burn injury - Appellant contended that witnesses did not support prosecution case, Trial Court and HC acted illegally in passing judgment and order of conviction and sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether convicting appellant for murder, relying only on dying declaration, was sustainable - Held, it was for prosecution to prove involvement of accused beyond all reasonable doubt - S. 106 of the Act, to which reference was made by HC, should not be said to have any application whatsoever - No evidence had been brought by prosecution to show as to since when appellant made himself unavailable for arrest and/or absconding - Materials brought on records did not support prosecution case, however, support defence - Indisputably conviction could be recorded on basis of dying declaration alone, however, dying declaration should be wholly reliable - Circumstances which had been brought on records clearly pointed out that what might had been stated in dying declaration might not be correct - In instant case, where appellant had been charged u/s. 302 IPC, presumption in terms of s. 113A of the Act was not available - Evidences supplied were not enough to determine guilt of accused - Hence, appellant was entitled to benefit of doubt and appellant should be released to with if not required in any other case - Appeal allowed.
