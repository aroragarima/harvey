Maqbool Alam Khan v Mst. Khodaija and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 370; AIR 1966 SC 1194; 1966 BLJR 566; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 479

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 123 - (A) Whether res judicata applies to restitution under s. 144, CPC and whether such an application is an application for execution? - Held, an application for restitution under s. 144 of CPC is an application for execution of a decree - Principle of res judicata applies to execution proceedings - If name removed from prior suit then right to sue comes back - (B) Whether a property can be validly gifted under Muslim law if property to be gifted is in possession of lessee, mortgagee or trespasser? - Held, three pillars of a valid gift under Mahomedan law are (a) manifestation of wish to give on part of donor (b) acceptance of donee either impliedly or expressly and (c) taking of possession of subject-matter of gift by donee, either actually or constructively - Donor may lawfully make a gift of a property in possession of a trespasser, provided donor either obtains and gives possession of property to donee or does all that he can to put it within power of donee to obtain possession - Mere declaration of donor and acceptance by donee not enough - To validate gift, there must also be either delivery of possession, or failing such delivery, some overt act by donor to put it within power of donee to obtain possession otherwise gift is invalid - Appeal dismissed.
