In Re: Shri ’M’, An Advocate Ofthe Supreme Court Of India v Na

Supreme Court of India

17 October 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

B. Jagannadhadas, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 33; AIR 1957 SC 149; 1956 ALJ 153; 1957 CRLJ 300; 1957 SCJ 105; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 811

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Supreme Court Rules,1950 (as amended), O. 4, r. 30 - Criminal Procedure Code 1898, s. 251 A - Bar Councils Act, 1926, s. 10 - Legal Practitioners Act, 1879, s. 13 - Legal Practitioners Act,1879, s. 13 - Shri 'M', an Agent of Supreme Court, became an 'Advocate on record' under new rules of this Court - He acted for appellant in a criminal appeal - A sum of Rs. 750 was supplied to Shri 'M' for printing charges which was deposited in due course in Punjab High Court from whose judgment appeal arose - There remained an unspent balance of Rs. 242-1-9 out of this Shri 'M' withdrew money from High Court without authority and knowledge of complainant - Complainant demanded refund of same - Shri 'M' first denied receipt of money and thereafter, refused to refund it -(A )Whether O. 4, r. 30 precluded chamber of judges to reframe or add charges specified in original summon for professional misconduct against advocate? - Special Bench treated enquiry in chambers as preliminary enquiry regarding original summons with respect to complaint of professional misconduct against advocate and heard arguments on both sides with reference to matter of that enquiry, then coming to conclusion that it was not a case for discharge at that stage they reframed charge framed by chamber Judge and added a fresh charge - Held, terms of O. 4, r. 30 does not preclude them from adopting course they had including reframing or adding to charges specified in original summons, where material at preliminary enquiry justified same -(B) Whether there are any specified rules governing professional misconduct of honorable profession and if no are there any rigid standards? - Held, that Court in dealing with cases of professional misconduct is not concerned with ordinary legal rights, but with special and rigid rules of professional conduct expected of and applied to a specially privileged class of persons but this is not any reason for accepting a less rigid standard - (C) Whether agent or advocate in his high standard profession can retain or appropriate money, other than earmarked fees, given by clients?- Held, that high standards of profession demand that when moneys of client come into possession of an Agent or an Advocate, otherwise than as earmarked fees, he has to treat himself as in position of a trustee for client in respect of said moneys - Even if he has a lien on such moneys, it would be improper for him to retain, i.e. to appropriate same towards his fees without consent, express or implied, of his client or without an order of Court - It may be that in certain circumstances he is entitled to exercise a lien, but he has to give reasonable intimation both of fact of moneys having come into his hands and of exercise of his lien over them until his account is settled - Petition dismissed.
