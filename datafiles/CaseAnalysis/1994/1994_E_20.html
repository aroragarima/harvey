Mahesh Narsayya Chintal v Executive Engineer, Jayakwadi Project, Canal

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 933; (1994) Supp3 SCC 255; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1400; 1994 (28) ATC 355; 1994 (2) CLR 830; 1994 (69) FLR 691; JT 1994 (5) SC 328; 1995 (1) LLJ 445; 1995 (1) LLN 3; 1994(3) SCALE 671; 1994 (4) SLR 626; 1995 (1) UJ 715; 1994 (3) UPLBEC 1967

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33-C(2) - Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practises Act, 1971, s. 28 - Whether appellant was a Govt. servant even though he was transferred to a different scheme? - Labour Court categorically recorded a finding that appellant continued to be an employee under Executive Engineer (Respondent 1) and service conditions of appellant remained unchanged although he worked with Corporation on being transferred to Scheme - As order of Labour Court was not challenged before High Court or in any other forum, quasi-judicial order has attained finality and is binding between appellant and respondent-State - Appeal allowed.
