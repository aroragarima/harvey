S. Naqui Imam and another v Masood Manzar and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1530; (2008) 17 SCC 633

Case Digest

Subject: Education; Election

Keywords: Contempt proceedings, Election of Managing Committee

Summary: Education - Election - Election of Managing Committee - Delay - Contempt proceedings - Respondent Director of Higher Education in terms of order of SC was directed to conduct election of Managing Committee and Chairman of Council either by himself or to nominate person not below rank of District Education Officer or Inspector of Colleges or equivalent post in State as Special Officer for proper conduct of election - Election process was directed to be completed by 28-2-2002 - Certain other directions were also issued including direction to then management to furnish detailed statement of accounts to Director of Higher Education or his nominee after the same was audited by recognised Chartered Accountant - Director was given liberty to take assistance from any party or parties as he might deem fit and proper - Elections it was admitted were not completed within stipulated time fixed in order dated 22-11-2001 - It seemed that elections were held only on 23-6-2002 - Hence instant petition filed for contempt proceedings against respondents - Whether it was appropriate to initiate contempt proceedings - Held, it was evident that respondent was utterly negligent - He knew about time-frame fixed by SC - Respondent had also been given liberty to appoint nominee for conduct of election, which nominee was appointed after expiry of stipulated period and same was position about filing application seeking extension - Despite this attitude SC was of view, it was possible as submitted that respondent on account of personal difficulties might not be in proper state of mind and thus SC refrained from initiating contempt proceedings against him on account of non-holding of election within stipulated period - If respondents no. 1, to no.3 were not cooperative, respondent was not without remedy but for same reason and it was not to be fit case for initiating contempt proceedings - Respondents no.1 to no. 3 sought to explain that they had appointed on 7-12-2001, Chartered Accountant to audit accounts of college - Chartered Accountant had intimated them by letter dtd. 24-12-2001 that university audit was going on and account books and other records were with university and, therefore, no auditing could be done till account books and relevant documents were returned by university to auditing team - Statement of accounts audited by university was received by management on 14-6-2002 and same was sent to Director on 19-6-2002 and thereafter statement of accounts dtd. 21-6-2002 audited by Chartered Accountant was received by management on 25-6-2002 and was sent to authority concerned on 3-7-2002 - Hence, it was not appropriate to initiate contempt proceedings - Petition dismissed.
