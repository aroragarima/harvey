Krishnaswamy S.Pd. and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 55; (2006) 3 SCC 286; 2006 (201) CTR 183; [2006] 281 ITR 305; JT 2006 (2) SC 536; 2006(2) SCALE 441; 2006 (3) SCJ 388; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 390; [2006] 151 TAXMAN 286

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: [A.] Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 269-UD, 269-UE, 269D, 269UD(1) - Income Tax Rules, 1962, r. 48(L) - Order for compulsory purchase - Challenge on ground that no reasons were stated, as to on what basis valuation of property was arrived at and since order was non-reasoned without giving opportunities to appellants same was liable to be quashed - HC held that because of clarificatory order of this Court in C B Gautam v Union of India and Ors [1992 Indlaw SC 1163] appellants were not entitled to any relief in instant case - Appeal against - Held, there can be no interference as property which is subject matter of compulsory purchase u/s. 269 UD had already been sold by public auction before decision in C B Gautam's case, and as there was no challenge by owner of property - Appeal dismissed.
