Union of India and Another v Ladu Lal Jain

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 151; AIR 1963 SC 1681; 1964 (1) MLJ(SC) 38; 1964 (1) SCJ 101; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 624

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 20, 80 and 115 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 298 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2 (j) - Suit was instituted by plaintiff against Union Of India and Northern Frontier Railway for recovery of money on account of non delivery of goods - Whether running of railways, ceases to be a business when they are run by Govt. and can they be sued? - Held, 'profit element' is not a necessary ingredient of carrying on business, it is to be presumed that Railways are run on a profit basis, therefore, Union Of India carries on business of running railways and can be sued in court within whose territorial jurisdiction headquarters of one of railways run by Union is situated - Appeal dismissed.
