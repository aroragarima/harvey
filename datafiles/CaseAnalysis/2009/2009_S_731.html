Koppisetti Subbharao @ Subramaniam v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

29 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 625; (2009) 12 SCC 331; AIR 2009 SC 2684; 2009 CRLJ 3480; 2009 (3) G.L.R. 2701; 2009 (4) JCC 3061; 2009 (2) KLT 531; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 860; 2009 (4) RLW 3614; 2009(6) SCALE 528; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 157

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Bigamy

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A and 494 - Inherent power of High Court - Registration of case u/ss. 498A r/w 34 IPC against three accused persons including appellant - Delayed trial of accused 1 due to his absence - Trial of accused 2 and 3 proceeded with and were awarded acquittal - Proceedings against A1 - Application u/s. 482 moved for quashing such proceedings on ground that complainant was not legally wedded wife of appellant as he was already married, thus s. 498A had no application to the facts of case; and further ground was acquittal of A2 and A3 - High Court dismissed application on the ground that disputed questions of fact are involved - Whether rejection of application u/s. 482 justified? - Held, Yes - Huge deliberations entered into on aspect of 'sanctity of first Hindu marriage', 'dowry', offence of 'bigamy' vis-a-vis '..subjecting of the woman to cruelty' u/s. 498A, 'dowry death' u/s. 304B, presumption of marriage - Question, who would be covered by the expression 'husband' for attracting s. 498A is in issue in instant case, Court observed - Disputed questions of fact thus are involved - Impugned order of HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
