Commissioner of Income Tax v Arvind Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 306; (1992) Supp2 SCC 190; AIR 1992 SC 718; 1992 (101) CTR 91; [1992] 193 ITR 255; JT 1991 (5) SC 341; 1991(2) SCALE 1320; [1991] Supp3 S.C.R. 303; [1992] 60 TAXMAN 192; 1992 (2) TLR 320

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Corporate - Income Tax and Direct Taxes- Companies Act, 1956 - Income Tax Act,1961, ss. 43A and 145 - Devaluation of Indian currency on a particular date - Consequential enhancement in the liability - Of person (s) who had imported assets and for which payment was not fully made on the date of devaluation - Question arise as to how this increase in liability is to be : (A) Accounted for in the books of account , and - (B) taken into account for purposes of certain allowances like depreciation allowance and development rebate available u/ss. 145 ,32 and 43A of 1961 Act - Held, the effect of devaluation on Accounts can be either; mentioned by way of a note tn the accounts for the earlier year in case the balance sheet in respect thereof has not yet been finalised or actually to give effect to the necessary adjustment in the subsequent years instead of reopening the closed accounts of the earlier year - For depreciation allowance irrespective of position in earlier years, the written down value in the year in which the increased liability has arisen should be taken on the basis of the increased cost minus depreciation earlier allowed on the basis of the old cost - And for the purpose of granting developent rebate - Variation due to fluctuation in exchange rate - Not to be considered - Appeal Allowed.
