B. S. Gaur v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3044; (2001) 9 SCC 706; 2000 (4) AWC 3027; 2000 (3) CLR 414; JT 2000 (10) SC 37; 2000 (5) SLR 276; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 32

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Promotion - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - (A) C.A. No. 4681 of 1996 - Whether appellant was entitled to claim promotion being a Scheduled Caste? - During pendency of matter, Scheduled Caste certificate issued to appellant, was revoked on ground that he was not a Scheduled Caste - Once certificate has been revoked, status of appellant of being a Scheduled Caste has come under cloud - Merely because appellant has obtained a stay order does not conclude dispute - C.A. No. 4682/1996 - (B) Whether denial of promotion as Senior Technical Asst. (Publication) to appellant is valid? - There is no dispute that there are only three posts and one of three posts is occupied by general candidate and second by a member of Scheduled Castes and, therefore, third post could not be allowed to go to reserved candidate - Appeals dismissed.
