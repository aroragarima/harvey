Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab, Jammu and v Raghbir Singh

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 184; AIR 1966 SC 18; [1965] 57 ITR 408; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 684

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Trust Deed, Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 16(1)(c) and 63 - Estate of joint Hindu family of which respondent was a member was partitioned - Respondent was allotted properties and 400 shares of Simbhaoli Sugar Mills Pvt. Ltd, and was made liable to pay a business debt amounting - Respondent executed a deed of trust in respect of 300 out of shares of Simbhaoli Sugar Mills which fell of to his share - (A)Whether u/s 16(1) of Income tax Act, 1922 actual retransfer income or assets or exercise of power to reassume is necessary ? - If there is a provision in a settlement or disposition for retransfer of income or assets or which confers a right to reassume power over income or assets, settlement or disposition is deemed to be statutorily revocable - Provision may even be for retransfer indirectly or for conferring power to reassume indirectly over income or assets - Held, no actual retransfer or exercise of power to reassume is necessary if there be a provision of nature contemplated, proviso operates - (B)Whether benefit received by assessee is within abit of proviso 1 of s.16(1)(c) of Income tax Act, 1922 ? - Where income from shares belonging to assessee since execution of deed of settlement by him arose to trustees and it was liable to be applied for purposes mentioned in deed along with a direction that income had to be applied for satisfaction of debts which settlor was under an obligation to discharge - Further held, that even though settlor assessee got a benefit from trust consequent upon satisfaction of his liability yet on that account first proviso did not apply - Reason was that cases in which there was a settlement, but there was no provision in settlement for retransfer or right to reassume power did not fall within proviso, even if as a result of settlement, settlor obtained a benefit - Appeal dismissed.
