Mohd. Yunus v Mohd. Mustaqim and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 84; (1983) 4 SCC 566; AIR 1984 SC 38; 1983(2) SCALE 1013; [1984] 1 S.C.R. 211; 1984 UJ 132

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Civil Procedure - Constitution - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 115, O. 21 r. 92, O. 21 r. 2 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 227 - Application for setting aside sale - Jurisdiction - Maintainability - Petitioner/stranger to estate of deceased/surety made an application stating that his name be substituted as he was grandnephew of surety and also his heir and successor-in-interest under an alleged Will executed by surety - Trial Court dismissed application holding that there was no question of allowing substitution of name of petitioner by Court under its inherent powers - Petitioner filed another application u/s. 151 CPC for setting aside sale on ground that there was material irregularity in publishing and conducting same - Trial Court dismissed application holding that application made by petitioner was an application under O. 21 r. 89 CPC, was barred by limitation - Aggrieved, petitioner filed writ petition before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant petition - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and decree of Courts below - Held, there could be no certification of an adjustment between decree-holder and judgment-debtor under O. 21 r. 2 CPC after an auction- sale was held in a case where a third party's interest intervenes - In such a case, Court had no alternative but to confirm sale under O. 21 r. 92 CPC - In instant case, there was no error of law much less an error apparent on face of record - There was no failure on part of Trial Court to exercise jurisdiction nor did it act in disregard of principles of natural justice - Nor was procedure adopted by him not in consonance with procedure established by law - However, if no appeal lay against impugned orders of trial Court, petitioner had remedy of filing a revision before HC u/s. 115 CPC - HC had no jurisdiction to interfere with impugned orders passed by Trial Court u/art. 227 of Constitution - A mere wrong decision without anything more was not enough to attract jurisdiction of HC u/art. 227 of Constitution - Petition dismissed.
