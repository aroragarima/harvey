East India Transformer & Switch Gear (P) Ltd. v Collector of Central Excise, Meerut, U.P.

Supreme Court of India

08 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2148; (1997) 6 SCC 64; 1997 (92) E.L.T. 449; JT 1997 (10) SC 705; 1997 (20) RLT 927

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 8 - Exemption Notification D/-30-04-1975, issued under provisions of r. 8 - Whether Tribunal erred in holding that appellant was liable to pay duty on value of spare parts manufactured and used in old transformers without considering relevant notification? - Said notification applies to use of goods falling under Tariff Item 68 which are manufactured in a factory and are intended to be used in that factory - Parts that assessee manufactured in its factory and used in its factory in repair of old transformers were, therefore, entitled to benefit of said notification - Insofar as parts which were purchased from market and upon which excise duty had already been paid are concerned, there was no obligation to pay excise duty over again - Held, judgment and order of Tribunal, insofar as it deals with parts used in repair of old transformers, set aside - Appeal allowed.
