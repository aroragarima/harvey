Subhash & Another v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Shingal, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 220; (1976) SCC (Cr) 483; (1976) 3 SCC 629; AIR 1976 SC 1924; 1979 CRLJ 1521; 1976 CrLR(SC) 251; 1977 MLJ 129; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 587

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 366, 375 and 376 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Session Court awarded death sentence and life imprisonment to accused - High Court violated provision of ss. 375 and 376 CrPC - Obliged to examine entire evidence for itself, apart from and independently of Sessions Court's appraisal and assessment of that evidence - Whether HC is justified in deciding that appellants have committed murder? - Where HC is under an obligation to proceed in accordance with provisions of ss. 375 and 376 of CrPC, the HC must not only see whether order passed by Sessions Court is correct but it is under an obligation to examine entire evidence for itself, apart from and independently of Sessions Court's appraisal and assessment of that evidence - Held, several significant discrepancies in evidence which either escaped attention of HC or were not given due and rightful importance - Conviction of accused set aside - Appeal allowed.
