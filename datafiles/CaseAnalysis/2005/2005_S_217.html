Chairman, Oil and Natural Gas Corporation Limited and Another v Shyamal Chandra Bhowmik

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1036; (2006) SCC (L&S) 113; (2006) 1 SCC 337; AIR 2006 SC 392; 2006 (5) AWC 4354; 2006 (1) JhrCR(SC) 309; JT 2005 (10) SC 72; 2006 (2) RLW 1247; 2005(9) SCALE 473; 2006 (1) SLR 43; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 406; 2006 (1) UPLBEC 3

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Cooperative, Lay Off, Mechanic

Summary: - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 10 and 25-F. - Whether the High Courts conclusion that the appellant /ONGC had not established that the respondent had not worked for more than 240 days in twelve months preceding retrenchment and that it failed to establish that the respondent was a member of any union is justified? - Approach of the High Court is not correct- Relevant issues were not considered in its proper perspective. The respective stand of the respondent was to be examined in the light of law - The question of shifting of onus assumes relevance only when evidence is led - High Courts should not entertain writ petitions directly when claim of service of more than 240 days in a year is raised - Whether a person has worked for more than 240 days or not is a disputed question of fact which is not to be examined by the High Court - Proper remedy for the person making such a claim is to raise an industrial dispute under the Act so that the evidence can be analysed and conclusion can be arrived at - The legal position has not been analysed in the proper perspective, it would be appropriate if the matter is decided by the forum - Held, in case a dispute is raised before the appropriate Govt., it shall refer the matter to the concerned tribunal for adjudication - Held, appeal is accordingly allowed.
