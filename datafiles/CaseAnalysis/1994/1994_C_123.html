Bhagatram Rajiv Kumar v Commissioner Of Sales Tax Madhya Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 700; (1995) Supp1 SCC 673; JT 1994 (7) SC 573; 1994(4) SCALE 1103; 1995 (96) STC 654; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 91

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Madhya Pradesh Sthaniya Kshetra Me Mal Ke Pravesh Par Kar Adhiniyam, 1976(Entry Tax Act), s. 3(1)(a) - (A) Whether entry tax on goods such as sugar on which no sales tax is leviable, could be subjected to levy u/s. 3(1) (a)? - (1) What is nature of levy under Act? - S. 3(1)(a) has two limbs one, levying tax and other fixing person from whom it shall be realised - Later part of it is more a part of machinery provision and it cannot control main or substantive part of section - Taxable event is entry of goods in local area of State by a dealer - (2) Expression used 'liable to tax', whether includes only those goods on which sales tax under Sales Tax Act is payable? - No - Expression 'liable to tax' has been used to identify person who shall pay entry tax - Tax is leviable on all goods specified in Schedule II brought for consumption, use or sale; but it shall be realised only from those persons who are dealers registered under Sales Tax Act and are liable to pay tax - Held, Entry tax is payable on goods such as sugar on which no sales tax is leviable as it is one of items enlisted in Sch II - Appeal dismissed.
