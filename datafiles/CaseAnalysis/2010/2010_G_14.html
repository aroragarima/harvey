State Of Karnataka And Others v Gadilingappa And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 60; (2010) 2 SCC 728; AIR 2010 SC 2548; 2010 (6) AWC 6044; 2010 (2) ESC 186; 2010 (125) FLR 266; JT 2010 (1) SC 521; 2010 (2) KarLJ 626; 2010(1) SCALE 655; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 815; 2010 (1) SLR 798

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization - Respondents claimed regularization of their services submitting that they had rendered long continuous service as honorary teachers - Appellant no.1 rejected said claim of the respondents on the ground that any consideration for regularization or absorption can be made only in regard to those candidates who possessed the minimum prescribed qualification for the post of the teachers and as the respondents did not possesses the minimum prescribed qualifications of T.C.H., they could not be considered for regularization or absorption - Applications filed against said order was rejected by tribunal - DB set aside the decision of the tribunal and allowed the claim of the respondents for regularization of their services - Hence, present appeal - Whether respondents are eligible for regularization of their services? - Held, none of the respondents had undergone the T.C.H. course, which was the minimum prescribed qualification at the relevant time for being appointed to the post of a teacher - Since the respondents did not possess the minimum prescribed qualification and because of which their appointment was in contravention of the Cadre and recruitment Rules, their appointments were illegal appointments - Following SC judgements of Secretary, State of Karnataka and Others vs. Umadevi (3) and Others 2006 INDLAW SC 125 and Official Liquidator vs. Dayanand 2008 INDLAW SC 1715, the claim of the respondents for regularization cannot be sustained - Appeals allowed.
