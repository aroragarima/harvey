State of Rajasthan and others v Gotan Lime Stone Khanji Udyog Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

20 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 57; (2016) 4 SCC 469; AIR 2016 SC 510; 2016(4) ALL MR 450; 2016 (2) AWC 2140; [2016] 195 Comp Cas 263; JT 2016 (1) SC 512; 2016 (2) RLW 1439; 2016(1) SCALE 392; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Partnership, Rajasthan Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1986, Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2015

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Contract & Commercial - Mines and Minerals (Development and Regulation) Amendment Act, 2015 - Rajasthan Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1986 - Lifting of corporate veil - Transfer of mining lease - GLKU held mining lease for mining and later applied for transfer of lease in favour of respondent no.1 pleading that respondent no, 1 was only private limited company which was mere change of form of its own business by converting itself from partnership firm into private limited company and that partners of firm and Directors of company were same on transfer - Transfer was allowed on 25-4-2012 but after seeking said permission, newly formed company sold its entire shareholding to another company allegedly for Rs.160 Crore alleged to be sale price of mining lease - Hence, show cause notice was issued to respondent no.1 and later, order dt.25-4-2012 permitting transfer was rescinded and declared void vide order dt,16-12-2014 - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before HC challenging show cause notice, order dt.16-12-2014 and Single Judge of HC allowed the same - Aggrieved, appellant and impleaded party filed appeals before DB of HC but the same were dismissed by impugned order affirming orders of Single Judge - Hence, instant appeal - Whether transfer of lease could be declared void.

Held, lessee could either operate the mine or surrender or transfer only with permission of the authority as legally required. However, in instant case, lessee has achieved indirectly what could not be achieved directly by concealing the real nature of the transaction. The corporate entity has been used to conceal the real transaction of transfer of mining lease to a third party for consideration without statutory consent by terming it as two separate transactions - the first of transforming a partnership into a company and the second of sale of entire shareholding to another company. The real transaction is sale of mining lease which is not legally permitted. Thus, doctrine of lifting the veil has to be applied to give effect to law which is sought to be circumvented. However, Single Judge and DB of HC have gone by only one aspect of law, i.e., general principle that sale of shares by itself is not sale of assets. However, said principle is subject to the doctrine of piercing of corporate veil wherever necessary to give effect to the policy of law and said principle clearly applies to instant case as shares were transferred to cover up the real transaction which is sale of mining lease for consideration without the previous consent of competent authority, as statutorily required. Hence, view of HC is not sustainable and so, judgment of HC is set aside and State is directed to frame and notify its policy in the matter. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Doctrine of lifting the veil could be invoked if the public interest so requires or if there is allegation of violation of law by using the device of a corporate entity. Further, Mining rights belong to the State and Rules prohibit transfer of mining lease for consideration without the previous consent of competent authority in writing.
