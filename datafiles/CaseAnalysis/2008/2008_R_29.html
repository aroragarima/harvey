Rabindra Singh v Financial Commissioner, Coopration, Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 869; (2008) 7 SCC 663; 2008 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3089; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 1149; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 929; 2008(8) SCALE 242; [2008] 8 S.C.R. 813

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Process Server, Review Application, Foreign Country, Mode Of Making Proclamation

Summary: Suit for partition - Ex-parte decree - Jurisdiction of a Land Revenue Court to set aside an ex-parte decree - Held, what matters for exercise of jurisdiction is the source of power and not the failure to mention the correct provisions of law - Even in the absence of any express provision having regard to the principles of natural justice in such a proceeding, the courts will have ample jurisdiction to set aside an ex parte decree, subject of course to the statutory interdict - Knowledge on the part of the constituted attorney would not be such which would come on the way of the appellant in maintaining an application for setting aside an ex parte decree - Such a contention cannot be raised in a proceeding for setting aside an ex-parte decree - Courts below ought to have held that the appellant had been able to establish sufficient cause for an order setting aside the ex-parte decree - Appeal allowed.
