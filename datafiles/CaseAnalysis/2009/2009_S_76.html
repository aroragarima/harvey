State of West Bengal and Others v Samar Kumar Sarkar

Supreme Court of India

14 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 849; (2009) 15 SCC 444; 2010(1) ALL MR 436; 2009 (4) AWC 3215; 2009 (4) ESC 697; 2009 (122) FLR 659; 2009 (4) LLJ 328; 2009 (6) MLJ(SC) 80; 2009(9) SCALE 523; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 896; 2011 (7) SLR 449

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 227, 228 - Transfer of case - Power of HC - Application before CAT transferred by HC for its own consideration HC exercised its power u/art. 227 of Constitution - Legality - HC can withdraw a case from subordinate Court and decide the same by itself or decide the question of law and return the same to the Court from which it is drawn u/art. 228 of Constitution but not u/art. 227 - HC would have directed the Tribunal to dispose of the matter expeditiously u/art. 227 of the Constitution instead of transferring matter to itself - Therefore, impugned order set aside and directed the Tribunal to dispose the matter expeditiously - Appeal allowed.
