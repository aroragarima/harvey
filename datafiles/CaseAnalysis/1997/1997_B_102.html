State Of Rajasthan v Bhup Ram

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3041; (1997) 10 SCC 675; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1032; 1997 (1) CCR 132; 1997 (1) Crimes 62; JT 1997 (1) SC 479; 1997(1) SCALE 274; 1997 (1) SCJ 80; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 190; 1997 (1) Supreme 405

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 293 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Respondent murdered his wife - In dying declaration deceased implicated respondent - Bullet recovered from body matched with pistol recovered from respondent - Maker of FIR, father of deceased, turned hostile besides other witnesses - Trial Court still convicted on basis of bullet and pistol matching and dying declaration - HC acquitted respondent - Held, HC view unjustified in acquiting respondent - Dying declaration clear and unambiguous - Trial Court order restored - Appeal allowed.
