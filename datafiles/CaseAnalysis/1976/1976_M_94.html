Musa Khan and Others v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, P.N. Bhagwati, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 462; (1977) SCC (Cr) 164; (1977) 1 SCC 733; AIR 1976 SC 2566; 1976 CRLJ 1987; 1976 CRLR 466

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 ss. 4 and 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323, 392, 395, 149, 425 and 457 - Dacoity - Appellants convicted by Trial Court and confirmed by High Court - Whether conviction of appellants proper? - Held, mere presence of some people at scene of occurrence does not make them members of unlawful assembly - Some of accused persons acquitted by courts below and some of them given benefit of Probation of Offenders Act, 1958 - No reasonable evidence to prove participation of appellants in offence - Convictions set aside - Appeal allowed.
