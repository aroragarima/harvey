Uday Chakraborty and others v State of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

08 July 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 492; (2010) 7 SCC 518; AIR 2010 SC 3506; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 2616; 2010 (4) CalLT 63; 2010 CRLJ 3862; JT 2010 (6) SC 608; 2010 (3) RCR(Criminal) 611; 2010(6) SCALE 743; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 1048; 2010 (2) UC 1283

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A/304B - Cruelty against married woman - Conviction - Challenged - Couple has not even completed a period of 2 years of their marriage when, on 18-04-1996, it was alleged that because of dowry, the appellant and other family members tortured deceased physically and mentally and forcibly burnt her - Trial court convicted all the five accused persons for an offence punishable u/ss. 498A/304B of the IPC and sentenced them for 7 years rigorous imprisonment - On appeal, HC acquitted two persons and convicted three persons for the offence - Appellants filed present appeal against HC order for setting aside the order of conviction and sentence and for an order of acquittal submitting that Trial Court as well as the HC failed to examine the ingredients of the offence u/ss. 304B and 498A of IPC - Appellants also submitted that the complaint lodged by the father of the deceased did not contain any allegation of demand of dowry, therefore, there was no basis whatsoever to prosecute the appellants - Whether HC order could be upheld? - Held, yes - Prosecution has examined as many as 31 witnesses including the Investigating Officer, doctors and relatives of the deceased - The cumulative effect of the documentary and oral evidence on record clearly shows that the appellants have been rightly found guilty of the offence by the HC - Appellant has not been able to bring to notice any evidence or piece of material thereof which has not been considered by the Courts below in its correct perspective - Offence u/ss. 304B r/w. 498A of IPC is made out in this case and has been proved by the prosecution beyond any reasonable doubt - Appeal dismissed.
