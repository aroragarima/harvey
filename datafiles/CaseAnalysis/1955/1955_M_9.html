Â Maharaj Umeg Slng and Others v State of Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 18; AIR 1955 SC 540; 1955 (57) BomLR 709; 1955 SCJ 472; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 164

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950 arts. 3, 4, 5, 31-A(2)(a), 32, 245, 246, 294, 303 and 363 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Merged Territories and Areas (Jagirs Abolition) Act, 1953, - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Abolition of Jagirs - Petitions u/art. 32 are directed against 1953 Act, passed by Legislature of State of Bombay to abolish jagirs in merged territories and merged areas in State of Bombay - Whether provisions of impugned Act, were discriminatory against States and their subjects within meaning of cl.5 of letters of guarantee? - Held, even if it could be demonstrated that provisions of impugned Act were confiscatory as well as discriminatory in manner suggested, jagirs of petitioners (except in case of petitioner in petition no. 364 of 1954) were all estates within meaning of the term as defined in art. 31-A(2)(a) and even if impugned Act provided for acquisition of estates, or of any rights therein, or for extinguishment or modification of any such rights, impugned Act could not be challenged as void on ground that it was inconsistent with or took away or abridged any of fundamental rights conferred by Part 11 I - Held, any challenge therefore on ground of impugned Act violating fundamental rights of Petitioners u/art. 14 or 19(1)(f) or 31(2) was not available to petitioners - Petitions dismissed.
