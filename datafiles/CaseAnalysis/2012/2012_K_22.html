Kathi Bharat Vajsur & Anr. v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, A.R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 322; (2012) 5 SCC 724; AIR 2012 SC 2163; 2012 CRLJ 2717; JT 2012 (5) SC 557; 2012 (5) RCR(Criminal) 782; 2012(5) SCALE 207; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 850

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 161 - Murder - Conviction - Challenged - Accused persons allegedly shot fired at deceased - Due to the injuries caused, deceased died on the spot - Trial Court, after considering the entire evidence on record, acquitted the accused persons, on the ground that the prosecution failed to prove its case beyond reasonable doubt - On appeal, HC has reversed the order of acquittal passed by the trial court and convicted the accused persons for the offence punishable u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 of the IPC - Hence, the instant appeal - Accused persons contended that there were material contradictions in the statements of PW5 and PW6 and the evidence that was tendered in the Court during the trial - Whether HC rightly convicted accused persons u/s. 302 r/w. s. 34 of the IPC - Held, SC in Sunil Kumar Sambhudayal Gupta (Dr.) v. State of Maharashtra 2010 INDLAW SC 963, held that minor contradictions, inconsistencies, embellishments or improvements on trivial matters without effecting the core of the prosecution case should not be made a ground to reject the evidence in its entirety - In the instant case, it was not in dispute that in the impugned incident, one person was killed and the other person was seriously injured - In the trial, the injured has fully supported the case of the prosecution - His evidence finds support from the evidence of PW6 and the evidence of Doctor, PW 16 - Trial Court had erred in holding that the prosecution had not been able to prove the case beyond reasonable doubt - Perhaps the Trial Court took a hyper-technical view by primarily concentrating on minor contradictions to hold that the prosecution has failed to prove the guilt of the accused persons beyond reasonable doubt - No infirmity in the judgment of the HC - Appeal dismissed.
