Kakumanu Peda Subbayya and Another v Kakumanu Akkamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1958 SC 1042; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 60; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1249

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Family & Personal

Summary: Family & Personal - Constitution - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100, O. 22, r. 1 - (A) Whether right of partition implicit in a coparcener can be claimed by a minor coparcener also? - Held, yes - There is no distinction between a major and minor coparcener's right to partition in a Hindu Undivided Family - Minor can sue for partition and obtain a decree if his next friend can show that it is for minor's benefit - (B) Whether suit for partition by minor coparcener abates on his death? - Further held, suit for partition is a suit for property and not a suit for damages for a personal wrong - Suit can be continued by his Legal Representative - (C) What is effective date of severance in status in a partition suit? - Further held, division in status takes place when there is an unambiguous declaration by a co-parcener of his intention to separate by instituting a suit for partition - (D)Whether HC can interfere in findings of fact recorded by lower courts in a second appeal? - Further held, HC can interfere only if Courts below have misunderstood real point for determination in arriving at findings of fact or have based findings on inadmissible evidence - Appeal dismissed.
