Â Saravanabhavan and Govindaswamy v State of Madras

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 504; AIR 1966 SC 1273; 1966 CRLJ 949; 1967 (1) MLJ(SC) 129

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Fingerprints

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136-Code of Criminal Procedure,1898, s. 337 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 114, Illus.(b), 133 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 302/34, 449 - Murder - Approvers Evidence Sustainability - Appellants were two condemned prisoners under sentence of death passed on them by Trial Court and confirmed by HC - They had been convicted u/s. 302 r/w ss.34 and 449 IPC on being found guilty of murders of PG, S and MA - Appeal by special leave against judgment of HC was preferred - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, on careful consideration of evidence and circumstances of instant case Court opined that approvers' evidence was incredible and his taking part in crime had not been corroborated by any independent evidence besides his own statement - There was also in our opinion hardly any corroboration of approver's incredible evidence in material particulars connecting other accused with crime - Hence, conviction of appellants was set aside and ordered their acquittal - Appeal disposed of.
