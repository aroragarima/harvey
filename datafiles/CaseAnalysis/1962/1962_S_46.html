State of Kerala v Shri M. Appukutty

Supreme Court of India

11 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 286; AIR 1963 SC 796; 1963 (2) MLJ(SC) 131; 1963 (14) STC 242; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 563

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Power To Assess

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, ss. 9, 11, 12, 12-A, 12-B, 17 and 19 - (A)- Whether High Court had erred in holding that notice issued by Deputy Commissioner is without jurisdiction? - Held, sub-r. 3A of r. 17 on its plain construction confers jurisdiction on revising authority to issue notice which it did issue - HC had erred in holding notice to be without jurisdiction - (B) Whether r. 17 under which notice was issued was ultra vires substantive provisions of Act? - S. 19(1) of Act provides that 'the State Govt. may make rules to carry out purposes of this Act and long title of Act is an Act to, provide for levy of general tax on sale of goods in State of Madras - Further held, r. 17 and various clauses thereof made u/s. 19 are not beyond rule making power of State Govt. as contained in s. 19 - Appeal allowed.
