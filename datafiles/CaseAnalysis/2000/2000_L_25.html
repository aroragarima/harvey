Land Acquisition Officer v B. Vijender Reddy and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3761; (2001) 10 SCC 669; 2002 (2) AllCJ 1030; 2002 (1) CRC 458

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - (A) Whether rate for determining compensation of land acquired could be enhanced from rate recorded in sale deeds by Rs. 10,000 per acre per year for 3 years? - In fixation of rate of compensation under Act, it must spring from totality of evidence, pattern of rate, pattern of escalation and escalation of price in years preceding and succeeding s. 4 notification etc. - Held, sale deeds indicate that during these 3 years there has not been any escalation of prices of land in area in question, hence no justification to enhance rate - (B) Whether any reduction for development charge from rate fixed could be done? - Further held, map on record shows it is a fully developed area and in present case appellant has neither pleaded nor led any evidence in this regard - HC had held acquired land to be better situated than lands covered in relied upon sale deeds, hence not a fit case to further reduce rate from what is fixed in sale deeds - Appeals partly allowed.
