Jainendra Singh v State Of U.P. Tr.Prinl.Sec. Home & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 248; (2012) 8 SCC 748; 2012 (6) AWC 6332; 2012 (134) FLR 1054; 2012 (7) MLJ(SC) 68; 2012(7) SCALE 22; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 1047; 2012 (5) SLR 545; 2012 (4) UPLBEC 2675

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Service

Summary: Service - Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 323, 336 - Post of Constable - Selection - Training - Concealment of facts - Termination - Sustainability - Appellant selected for post of constable and was sent to training - Appellant cleared physical, written tests and interview - Appointing authority passed termination orders, on 27-10-2007, against appellant after it came to light that criminal case was pending against him, contrary to his declaration dt. 10-11-2006 that he was not involved in any criminal case, and case ended in acquittal - Termination orders were challenged before HC - HC declined to interfere while holding that appellant deliberately concealed vital information in order to secure employment and subsequent acquittal would not ensure to his benefit - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that in various decisions of SC the court interfered with orders of termination - Whether HC was justified in declining grant of relief to appellant - Held, decisions in which principle laid down in Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan V. Ram Ratan Yadav, 2003 Indlaw SC 247, had been either followed or similar view had been taken, common thread was found in all those decisions in having laid down law that suppression of material information which candidate was called upon to furnish and which he failed to do, such concealment would result in serious consequences - State would be well within its powers to resort to cancellation of such appointment when the appointee was undergoing probation in order to ensure cleanliness in service - Decisions in cases such as Commissioner of Police, Delhi & Anr. V. Dhaval Singh, 1998 Indlaw SC 853, Kamal Nayan Mishra V. State of Madhya Pradesh & Ors, 2009 Indlaw SC 2073 took different view than what was expressed in Kendriya Vidyalaya Sangathan V. Ratan Yadav - After considering principles laid down in majority of decisions, the question that loomed large was in respect of consideration of such claim by candidates who deliberately suppressed information at the time of recruitment, could there be different yardsticks applied in the matter of grant of relief - It would be appropriate to refer the issues to larger Bench of SC for an authoritative pronouncement so that there would be no conflict of views and which would enable Courts to apply law uniformly while dealing with such issues - Registry was directed to place all the relevant documents before Chief Justice for constitution of larger Bench - Order accordingly.
