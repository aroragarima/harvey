Saraswati Devi (D) By Lr. v Delhi Devt. Authority & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

29 January 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 52; (2013) 3 SCC 571; AIR 2013 SC 1717; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 68; 2013 (97) ALR 480; 2013 (2) AWC 2118; 2013 (1) CLT(SC) 201; 2013 (1) CTC 812; 2013 (197) DLT 81; JT 2013 (2) SC 335; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 431; 2013(2) SCALE 61; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 922

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Displaced Person, 1955 Rules

Summary: Administrative - Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4,5A,6,17(1)(iv) - Displaced Person, Displaced Persons (Compensation and Rehabilitation) Act, 1954, 1955 Rules, r. 90 - Evacuee Property - Auction - Sale Certificate - Land Acquisition - Sustainability - Appellants husband was declared highest bidder in public auction for sale of dispute property in year 1960 and Assistant Settlement Commissioner (Rural), Ministry of Rehabilitation issued letter requiring appellants husband to deposit balance price within 15 days - Appellant deposited balance price within time - On 7-3-1962, Administration issued notification u/s. 4 r/w s. 17(1)(iv) of Act proposing to acquire land which included appellants land, due to urgency s. 5A was dispensed with - Declaration u/s.6 was made and award was passed on 30-6-1962 - In 1981 Sale Certificate was registered, appellant after learning about acquisition made representation before authorities but without any result, hence appellant challenged acquisition before HC by filing writ petition - Appellant had contended that in 1962 land belonged to Central Govt. being an Evacuee land acquired u/s. 12 of Act and as such land could not have been acquired under Act - SJ, accepted pleading of appellant and quashed notification u/s. 4 of Act - Respondent aggrieved preferred LPA with delay application, DB allowed appeal holding that right held by auction purchaser could be legally acquired by way of Notification u/s. 4 of Act notwithstanding transfer of title in respect of land in question in his favour - Hence instant appeal - Whether DB, was justified in reversing order of SJ, and holding notification valid - Held, though approval to highest bid given by appellant's husband in respect of subject property was given on 31-10-1960, payment of full price by appellant was made pursuant to communication dt. 16-06-1980, but by that time subject land already stood acquired by Administration and, therefore, despite payment of full price by appellant in 1980 and issuance of sale certificate, no title came to be vested in appellant - Legal position that Court had summarised with regard to transfer of title in respect of property forming part of compensation pool put to public auction u/r. 90 of 1955 Rules did not help appellant at all because of completion of acquisition proceedings in 1962 much before payment of full purchase price by appellant - In absence of any title in favour of appellant or her husband on date of acquisition, challenge to such acquisition could not have been allowed by SJ, DB had rightly set aside erroneous order - Appeal dismissed.
