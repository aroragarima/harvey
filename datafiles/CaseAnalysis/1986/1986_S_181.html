D.K. Trivedi and Sons and Others Etc v State of Gujarat and Others Etc

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 654; (1986) Supp SCC 20; AIR 1986 SC 1323; 1986 (2) CCC 481; 1986 (2) GLR 1250; 1986(1) SCALE 1133; 1986 (1) SCJ 475; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 479; 1986 (2) UJ 301

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Gujarat Minor Mineral Rules, 1966 - Lease - Parties were persons to whom the State of Gujarat had granted quarry leases and mining leases in respect of minor minerals. Govt. of Gujarat made under the 1966 Rules - Leases were given in the form prescribed by the 1966 Rules - In view of several representations made to it the Govt. of Gujarat decided to refund the amount of royalty, if any, collected -Appellant filed a W.P. in the Gujarat HC, challenging the enhancement - The W.P. was rejected - Whether the rule making power of the State Govt.s under s. 15(1) of the 1957 Act included a power to charge dead rent and royalty - Held, the 1976 Notification substituted Schedule II to the Gujarat Rules so as to enhance the rates of dead rent - Rates of dead rent could not be enhanced by the State Govt. more than once during any period of four years - During that particular period of four years the rates of dead rent had already been enhanced - Second enhancement made during the same period by the 1976 Notification was not permissible in law and the 1976 Notification must, be held to be invalid -Course adopted by the HC was not correct - If the HC thought that the point raised by the Appellants was the same as was pending in this Court, it ought to have stayed the hearing of the W.P.s until this Court disposed of the other matters - Appeals disposed of.
