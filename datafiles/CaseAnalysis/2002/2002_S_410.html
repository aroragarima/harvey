Jawahar Lal Sazawal and others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 117; (2002) 3 SCC 219; (2002) SCC (L&S) 381; AIR 2002 SC 1187; AIR 2002 SCW 961; 2002 (93) FLR 278; JT 2002 (2) SC 340; 2002 LabIC 994; 2002 (2) LLJ 836; 2002 (2) LLN 20; 2002(2) SCALE 339; 2002 (2) SCJ 188; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 66; 2002 (2) SLJ 284; 2002 (2) SLR 412; 2002 (2) SLT 234; 2002 (2) Supreme 178; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 905

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Constitution, Companies Act, 1977, Jammu and Kashmir Civil Service Regulations, 1956

Summary: When, there were no statute or statutory rules by which the permanent posts held by the appellants were abolished, then, there was no question of the appellants exercising any option or surrendering their status under Article 207 of the Jammu and Kashmir Civil Service Regulations, 1956, and therefore, the appellants were and continue to be servants of the State Government and as permanent residents of the State of J & K are entitled under Section 10 of the State Constitution to be treated on par with other Government servants in keeping with Article 14 and 16 of the Constitution of India.
