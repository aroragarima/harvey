Prem Prakash v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, A. Varadarajan, Amarendra Nath Sen

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 188; (1984) Supp SCC 687; (1985) SCC (L&S) 349; AIR 1984 SC 1831; 1986 (52) FLR 345; 1984 LabIC 1448; 1985 (2) LLJ 341; 1984 (2) LLN 737; 1984(2) SCALE 205; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 564; 1984 (2) SLJ 376; 1985 (2) SLR 757; 1984 UJ 1128

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Denied, Appointment in judicial services, Claim of reservation

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Delhi Judicial Services Rules, 1970, rr. 18, 28 - Appointment in judicial services - Claim of reservation - Denied - Legality - Registrar of Delhi HC issued advertisement that Delhi HC would will hold an examination for recruitment of officers to Delhi Judicial Service - Petitioners who belonged to SC/ST category, appeared for examination and passed it - HC prepared a merit list of 11 candidates as against 16 vacancies which were advertised - Petitioner no. 1 was 11th in merit list another petitioner was 10th - Thereafter, petitioners did not figure in final list of candidates selected by Administrator - Aggrieved petitioners for their exclusion from list and non-appointment filed instant petition u/art. 32 of Constitution - Hence, instant petitions - Respondent (Asst. Registrar of HC) contended that r. 28 provided for special representation for members of SC/ST, was applicable to recruitment made through a competitive examination only and, thus, no reservations were made either in favour of Sc/ST at the stage of initial recruitment to Service, which was not by competitive examination - Whether petitioners were entitled to be appointed as claimed - Held, in counter-affidavit of HC contained a candid admission that when, in 1980 and 1981, HC examined question of reservation of vacancies for SC/ST right from the stage of initial constitution of Service in 1971, it found that 'there was apparently a violation of statutory Rules and vacancies had been wrongly calculated by Registry of HC ' - HC rectified its error by recommending the appointment of 2 Sc/ST candidates for 1980 vacancies from amongst the candidates who had qualified in 1979 competitive examination but who, on account of a mistaken calculation, were not appointed to reserved seats - HC again fell into an error while deciding whether, after accommodating two person in 1980 selection, reserved vacancies were still available in which petitioners could be appointed - Counter-affidavit states expressly that availability of vacancies for candidates of reserved category was determined on basis that only 7 candidates had qualified for general seats - That was neither justified by the Rules and administrative instructions nor indeed does such a method of fixation of reserved vacancies disclose any acceptable basis - 16 Vacancies were advertised in first instance out of which, 11 were for general candidates and 5 for reserved categories - It might be assumed that Administration was not bound to fill all the vacancies which were advertised and indeed, if number of candidates who qualify in competitive examination was less than the number of vacancies which were advertised, it was obvious that the vacancies which could be filled would be less than the vacancies which were advertised - But the availability of vacancies for reserved categories could not be made to depend upon the accidental circumstance of how many candidates have qualified for general seats - That would be contrary to the instructions contained in para 4.2 and 9.2 of the Brochure on 'Reservation for Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes in Services', 1978, issued by GOI - In the process of remedying injustice which was done to two SC/ST candidates of 1979, no injustice could be caused to petitioners who had qualified for reserved seats in examination held in 1980 - Quota of seats available for reserved candidates could not be made to depend on fortuitous circumstance as to how many candidates have qualified for general seats - Reserved quota should be fixed on the basis of total number of vacancies which were to be filled at a given point of time - HC and Delhi Administration was directed to appointment of petitioners - Petitions allowed.
