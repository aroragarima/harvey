Â Bihar Legal Support Society, v The Chief Justice Of India And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, G.L. Oza, M.M. Dutt, Ranganath Misra, V. Khalid

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 32; (1986) SCC (Cr) 537; (1986) 4 SCC 767; AIR 1987 SC 38; 1987 BBCJ 23; 1987 BLJ 89; 1987 CRLJ 313; JT 1986 SC 884; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Criminal - Constitution, art.136 - Special Leave Petitions - Bail - Whether special leave petitions against refusal of bail or anticipatory bail should be listed immediately or not - In the same manner in which the SLP of two 'big industrialists' was taken up by a Bench which sat late at night - It is a question within the administrative jurisdiction of the Chief Justice - Held, preference to weaker section of society - Supreme Court should not ordinarily, save in exceptional cases, interfere with orders granting or refusing bail or anticipatory bail, because these are matters in which the High Court should normally be the final arbiter - Petition disposed of.
