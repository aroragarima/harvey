Umesh Kumar Sinha v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 559; (2010) 6 SCC 718; 2010 (125) FLR 752; JT 2010 (6) SC 247; 2010(4) SCALE 193; 2010 (5) SLR 708

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Bihar Pension Rules, r. 43(b) - Forfeiture of gratuity - Appellant retired on 31.3.1998 while serving as the Managing Director of Bihar State Pharmaceutical and Chemical Development Corporation Ltd. - He was suspended pending inquiry and disciplinary proceedings were initiated against him, when he was in service - The disciplinary proceedings were continued after his retirement and Enquiry Officer submitted a report holding that five out of the nine charges were proved - Thereafter, the disciplinary authority imposed the punishment of forfeiture of entire gratuity u/r. 43(b) of Rules - Appellant filed writ petition praying that the said order to be quashed - Said writ petition was dismissed by HC - Review application filed by the appellant was also dismissed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, the principal charge which was held to be proved against appellant was in regard to purchase of machinery, part payment for which was made by him even before its delivery - Appellant had defended himself by stating that the purchase order for the said machinery had been placed by his predecessor and having regard to the terms of the contract he was bound to make payment and accordingly he made the payment of Rs.72,387/- - Having regard to the nature of misconduct for which the punishment was imposed, the forfeiture of the entire gratuity appears to be excessive and disproportionate - Forfeiture of half of the gratuity would serve the ends of justice - The balance of the gratuity should therefore be released to the appellant - Appeal partly allowed.
