Daulat Ram Chauhan v Anand Sharma

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 96; (1984) 2 SCC 64; AIR 1984 SC 621; 1984(1) SCALE 81; [1984] 2 S.C.R. 419; 1984 UJ 902

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act , 1951, ss. 123, 82(b) and 86 - Election petition - (A) Alleged corrupt practice in election - Essential ingredients of pleadings - Held, allegation must be clear and specific and not to be left for Court to infer by reasoning - (B) Whether or not the respondent had alleged that two other candidates (who had withdrawn) indulged in corrupt practice as defined in s.123 of the Act - They shouted the slogans with the consent of the appellant - Held, corrupt practice -however election petition was not liable to be dismissed in limine u/s.86 of the Act - Appeal posted for hearing on merits in respect of other issues - Preliminary objection over-ruled.
