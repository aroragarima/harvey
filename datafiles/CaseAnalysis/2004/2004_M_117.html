Shipping Corporation Of India Ltd v Machado Brothers & Ors

Supreme Court of India

25 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 215; (2004) 11 SCC 168; AIR 2004 SC 2093; 2004 (3) ALD(SC) 126; 2004 (110) DLT 649; JT 2004 (4) SC 25; JT 2004 (Supp1) SC 528; 2004 (138) PLR 301; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 557; 2004(4) SCALE 39; 2004 (2) SLT 958

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 12 r. 6, O. 15 r. 1, O. 15 r. 3, O. 23 r. 1, O. 7 r. 11, s. 151 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 14, 34 - If there is no specific provision which prohibits grant of relief sought in an application filed u/s. 151 of Code, courts have all necessary powers u/s. 151 CPC to make a suitable order to prevent abuse of process of court - Has to first consider whether exercise of such power is expressly prohibited by any other provisions of Code and if there is no such prohibition then Court will consider whether such power should be exercised or not on basis of facts mentioned in application Appellant contends that during pendency of first suit, certain subsequent events have taken place which has made first suit infructuous and in law said suit cannot be kept pending and continued solely for purpose of continuing an interim order made in said suit - If original proceeding has become infructuous, ex debito justitiae, it will be duty of court to take such action as is necessary in interest of justice which includes disposing of infructuous litigation - Parties can invoke s.151 for this purpose - Appeal allowed.
