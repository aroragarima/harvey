Inspector of Police, Tamil Nadu v Balaprasanna

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1127; (2008) 11 SCC 645; (2008) 3 SCC (Cr) 959; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 648; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 3257; 2008 CRLJ 4332; JT 2008 (8) SC 242; 2008(10) SCALE 466; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 31

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Foreigners; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Appeal is dismissed, Bloodstains

Summary: IPC, 1860 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 27 - (A) Conviction u/ss. 302 and 392 r/w 392 of the Code - Appeal by special leave seeking interference - Against order acquittal for murder of a Sri Lankan student - Accused is also a Sri Lankan staying in same premises - Circumstantial evidence - Confessional statements - Alleged motive was money - Death is homicidal - Accused was in need of money - Present at the time of occurrence - Statement of the accused leading to recovery of incriminating materials such as knife, rope and valuable articles - High Court found circumstances insufficient and directed acquittal - (B) Requirements of the s. 27 of 1872 Act - What - Supreme Court analyzing the HC judgment in the background of what has been stated by SC as regards circumstantial evidence, held that judgment does not suffer from any infirmity to warrant interference - Requirement of s. 27 highlighted - Appeal Dismissed.
