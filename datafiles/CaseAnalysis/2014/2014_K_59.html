Amar Nath v Kewla Devi and another

Supreme Court of India

22 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 281; (2014) 11 SCC 273; 2014 (3) AWC 3109; JT 2014 (5) SC 570; 2014 (2) OLR 88; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 151; 2014 (124) RD 61; 2014 (2) RLW 1629; 2014(5) SCALE 472; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 21

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: U. P. Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950-Section 331-U. P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1953-Section 49-Specific Relief Act, 1963-Sections 34 and 41-Suit for possession of suit schedule property based on title and for quashing order passed by Consolidation Officer-Trial court and Consolidation Officer passed judgments that are bad in law-Impugned judgment of High Court passed in second appellate jurisdiction set aside-Well-reasoned judgment of first appellate court-Upheld-Appellant entitled to 1/2 share owner of land in question and has right to ancestral property-Competent authority directed to record name of appellant in revenue record as 1/2 share owner of land in dispute.
