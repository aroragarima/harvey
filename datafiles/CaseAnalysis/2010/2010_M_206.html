Monica Bedi and others v State Of A.P.

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 950; (2011) 1 SCC 284; 2011 CRLJ 427; JT 2010 (12) SC 239; 2011 (2) MLJ(Crl) 287; 2010(11) SCALE 629

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Second Prosecution, Foreign Exchange Regulations Act, 1947, Rajasthan Sati (Prevention) Act, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 419, 420 and 468 - Passports Act, 1967, s. 12 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d) and 13(2) - Possession of fake passport - Forgery for purpose of cheating - CBI laid charge sheet against 10 accused persons - It was alleged that appellants entered into a conspiracy in order to secure a false passport, for the benefit of Monica Bedi (A-3) so as to enable her to utilize the same to leave the country and travel abroad - A-3 travelled abroad on the strength of the passport secured by her in the assumed name - She entered Portugal with the aid of passport for which she faced prosecution and conviction and sentence in Portugal - Trial Judge found A-3 guilty of the offences punishable u/ss. 120-B, 419 and 420 of IPC but acquitted of the charge u/s. 12 of the Passports Act, 1967; A-5 guilty of the offences u/ss. 120-B, 419 r/w 109, 420 r/w 109, 468 of IPC and ss. 13(1)(d) r/w 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act; A-7 guilty of the offence u/s. 468 of IPC and A-8 guilty of the offences u/ss. 120B, 420, 419 r/w 109 of IPC, s. 420 r/w s. 109 of IPC and u/ss. 13(1)(d) r/w 13(2) of the Prevention of Corruption Act - On appeal, HC confirmed the conviction of the appellants - Present appeals - (A) Double Jeopardy - Whether appellant's guaranteed fundamental right u/art. 20(2) was infringed? - It was submitted appellant was tried and convicted by a competent court of jurisdiction at Lisbon for being in possession of fake passport and, therefore, her trial and conviction for possessing the same passport before the C.B.I. Court at Hyderabad amounts to double jeopardy - Held, what is prohibited u/art. 20(2) is, the second prosecution and conviction must be for the same offence - If the offences are distinct, there is no question of the rule as to double jeopardy being applicable - Test to ascertain is whether two offences are the same, and not the identity of the allegations but the identity of the ingredients of the offences - Further, same set of facts can constitute offences under two different laws - An act or an omission can amount to and constitute an offence under IPC and at the same time constitute an offence under any other law - The bar to the punishment to the offender twice over for the same offence would arise only where the ingredients of both the offences are the same - In the present case, findings and conclusions reached by the Constitutional Court at Lisbon do not support the plea of the appellant - Appellant's plea of double jeopardy wholly untenable and unsustainable - (B) Conviction of A-3 - Whether the courts below committed any error in convicting and sentencing the appellant for the charged offences? - Held, she was involved in the conspiracy as proved at both stages i.e. pre-passport application stage and post-passport application stage - Conspiracy itself was hatched only with a view to secure a passport for her benefit enabling her to travel abroad in the in the assumed name - Sequence of events clearly prove the charges levelled against A-3 - Conviction u/ss. 120B, 419 and 420 of IPC upheld - Sentence awarded under each count directed to run concurrently reduced to that of the period already undergone by her while maintaining the sentence of fine awarded by the courts below - (C) Conviction of A-5, A-7 and A-8 - Held, HC was not justified in convicting A-7 at all for it had found no case against the appellant made out u/s. 120B of IPC and further found that there was no evidence to assume that he was involved with the other accused for the purpose of cheating - There was no evidence that A-7 prepared false document with intent to cause damage or injury, to the public or to any person, or to support any claim or title, or to cause any person to part with property, or to enter into any express or implied contract, or with intent to commit fraud - Conviction u/s. 465 of IPC unsustainable - However, conviction of A-5 and A-8 for the charged offences upheld - Appeals partly allowed.
