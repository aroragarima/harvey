Union of India and another v Pushpavathi and others

Supreme Court of India

06 February 2018

Case Analysis

Bench

Abhay Manohar Sapre, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2018 Indlaw SC 44

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: amount awarded, applicant, apportionment, award by Collector, Collector's award, Constitution, crops, diminution, expenses, fifteen per cent, inquiry, jurisdiction, land acquired, Land Acquisition Officer, Limitation, market value, mining compensation, Oil Mills, person interested, public purpose, Reference to Court, standing crops, standing crops or trees, the date of the Collector's, trees

Summary: Land & Property â Payment of interest â Statutory remedy â Respondents filed writ petitions before HC challenging legality and correctness of the order dt.14-12-1998 passed by Collector declining to award interest on compensation determined by him u/s.28-A proceedings â Appellants raised preliminary objection to the maintainability of writ petitions by contending that remedy for respondents lies in applying for making reference to Civil Court but not in filing writ petitions u/art.226 of Constitution â However, Single Judge of HC overruled preliminary objection and allowed writ petitions on merits and awarded interest as claimed by respondents â Aggrieved, UOI filed appeals but same were dismissed by DB of HC â Hence, instant appeals â Whether preliminary objection raised by appellants before HC regarding maintainability of writ petitions are sustainable.

Held, dispute relating to non-award of interest payable to the landowners u/ss.28 or/and 34 of the Act is not specified u/s.18 and hence it is not capable of being referred by the Collector to the Civil Court u/s.18 of the Act. Further, since payment of interest is statutory in character and being statutory, it is mandatory for payment once conditions specified u/ss. 28 or/and 34 are fulfilled. Further, such issue is required to be decided keeping in view the wording of s.18 of the Act, which specifies the issues on which the reference could be made to the Court and non-award of interest is not the issue specified in s.18 of the Act. Hence, no reference could be made to the Civil Court to decide the issue of non-award of interest u/s.18 of the Act. Further, same reasoning is applicable to the cases falling u/ss.28A(2) and (3) of the Act because any dispute, whether arising u/s.11 or 28A(2), is referable to the Civil Court in reference by the Collector u/s.18 of the Act. Hence, dispute in relation to non-award of interest could be raised by an aggrieved person only by taking recourse to art.226 of the Constitution in writ petition and reference u/s.18 or s.28A(3) could not be considered to be an alternative statutory remedy available to the landowner for getting the question of non-award of interest payable u/ss.28 or/and 34 of the Act decided by the Civil Court. Hence, HC was right in entertaining writ petitions filed by respondents. Appeals dismissed.
