Lal Mandi v State of W. B

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 996; (1995) SCC (Cr) 560; (1995) 3 SCC 603; AIR 1995 SC 2265; AIR 1995 SCW 1848; 1995 (2) CalLT(SC) 28; 1995 (2) CCR 93; 1995 (2) Crimes 185; 1995 CRLJ 2659; JT 1997 (10) SC 586; 1995(2) SCALE 571; 1995 (3) SCJ 39; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 134; 1995 (2) UJ 237

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302 and 325 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 386 - Appeal challenging conviction - Held, Appellate Court failed to appreciate evidence on record and arrived at independent finding on such appraisal amounts to failure in discharge of one of its essential duties - Presence of eye witnesses at place of occurrence also doubtful - No role regarding causing death or destroying of evidence - Accused liable to be convicted u/ss. 325/34 and not u/ss. 302/34 - Appeal partly allowed.
