State of Madhya Pradesh v Awadh Kishore Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1058; (2004) SCC (Cr) 353; (2004) 1 SCC 691; AIR 2004 SC 517; 2003 (4) CCR 455; 2004 (2) Crimes 310; 2004 (2) CRJ 161; 2004 CRLJ 598; JT 2003 (9) SC 284; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 233; 2003(9) SCALE 704; 2003 (1) SCW 6501; 2003 (7) SLT 323; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 672; 2004 (2) Supreme 501; 2004 (1) UC 561

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service; Socio-Economic

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - s. 482 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - s. 13(1)(e) - Legality of judgment to quash the investigation and proceedings under Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988 - Disproportionate assets - Public servant - Incomplete investigation - Held, while exercising jurisdiction under s. 482, CrPC, 1973, it is not permissible for the Court to act as if it was a trial Judge. Even when charge is framed in that stage, the Court has to only prima facie be satisfied about existence of sufficient ground for proceeding against the accused. For that limited purpose, the Court can evaluate material and documents on records, but it cannot appreciate evidence. The Court to not required to appreciate evidence to conclude whether the materials produced are sufficient or not for convicting the accused - Further held, the High Court was not justified in quashing the investigation and proceedings in the connected case.
