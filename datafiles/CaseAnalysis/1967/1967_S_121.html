Â Devi Dass Gopal Krishnan and Others v State of Punjab and Others. (And Others Cases)

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Vishishtha Bhargava, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 90; AIR 1967 SC 1895; 1969 (1) ITJ 224; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 557; 1967 (20) STC 430

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Declared Goods, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1959, Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1958, Pondicherry General Sales Tax Act, 1965, East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, East Punjab General Sales Tax (Second Amendment) Act, 1952, Finance (No. 2) Act, 1957, Finance (No.2 ) Act, 1957

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 2(ff) and 5 - Central Sales Tax (Amendment) Act, 1958 - (A) Whether as s. 5 of East Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1948 was held to be still-born and that, as said section was charging section, entire Act was void, with result Act 19 of 1952, which amended s. 5 with retrospective effect could not breathe a new life into said Act? - Held, Legislature is to lay down in Act conferring such a power of fixation of rates, clear legislative policy or guidelines in that regard - As Act did not prescribe any such policy, it must be held that s. 5 of said Act, as it stood before amendment, was void - In present case charging section was intact and what was struck out was only section providing for rates - It cannot, therefore, be said that when s. 5 was struck out s. 4 or other sections fell with it - (B) Whether s. 2(ff) introduced by Act 7 of 1958 defining 'purchase', subject to conditions mentioned therein as a taxing event was ultra vires State Legislature? - Further held, Sales Tax Act applies only to 'sale' as defined in Act - Under cl. (ff) of s. 2 of Act it is defined as a transfer of property - Whether it is sale or purchase transaction is same - Expression 'acquisition' in s. 2(ff) of Act means only 'transfer' - Expression 'valuable consideration' takes colour from preceding expression 'cash or deferred payment' - If so, it can only mean some other monetary payment in nature of cash or deferred payment - S. 2(ff) is not void for legislative incompetence - (C) Whether amendment was also bad in that it made an unreasonable discrimination in matter of taxation between same classes of goods based on character of purchaser? - Further held, a close scrutiny of s. 2(ff) discloses that there is a reasonable classification in said definition - There is certainly a reasonable relation between object of statute and differences between two categories of transactions - (D) Whether amended provision, s. 2(ff), was also void because it contravened ss. 14 and 15 of Central Sales Tax Act, 1956? - Further held, tax has no nexus with manufacture of goods - Purpose for which goods are purchased is only relevant for fixing taxable event, but tax is on purchase of goods - Taxable event is fixed before goods are actually manufactured - In order to be an excise duty (a) levy must be upon 'goods' and (b) taxable event must be manufacture or production of goods - Tax under Act is a purchase tax and not an excise duty - Also, this argument misses point that goods purchased and goods sold are not identical ones - Manufacture changes identity - Therefore, same goods are not taxed at two stages - Appeals dismissed.
