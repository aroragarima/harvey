Union of India v Amrit Lal Manchanda and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1096; (2004) 3 SCC 75; (2004) SCC (Cr) 662; AIR 2004 SC 1625; 2004 CRLJ 1426; JT 2004 (2) SC 378; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 996; 2004 (61) RLT 121; 2004(2) SCALE 480; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 422

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Detention Order - Quashing of Proceedings - Justifiability - Detention order was passed by appellant against respondent u/s. 3(1) of the Act - Respondent filed petition before HC, for stay operation of order - Appellant filed review petition before HC - HC relied on Sunil Fulchand Shah v. Union of India and Ors. 2000 Indlaw SC 685 and held that detention order was unsustainable - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC was erred in relying on Sunil Fulchand case (supra) which had nothing to do with a challenge to order of detention before its execution - Whether order of HC was justifiable - Held, preventive detention was an anticipatory measure and did not relate to an offence, while criminal proceedings was to punish a person for an offence committed by him - They were not parallel proceedings - The object of law of preventive detention was not punitive but only preventive , which would be resorted to when executive was convinced that such detention was necessary in order to prevent person detained from acting in a manner prejudicial to certain objects which were specified by concerned law - In instant case, HC did not appear to had consider case in background whether any relief was available to respondent even before order of detention was executed - Merely because HC had granted stay on detention order, respondent could not take advantage of order passed by HC to contend that there was passage of time - Respondent could not be allowed to had unfair advantage and double benefit of his own action, which delayed execution of detention order - Hence, order of HC was set aside- Appeals allowed.
