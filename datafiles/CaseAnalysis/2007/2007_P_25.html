D.K. Ganesh Babu v P.T. Manokaran & Ors

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 146; (2007) 2 SCC (Cr) 345; (2007) 4 SCC 434; AIR 2007 SC 1450; 2007 CRLJ 1827; 2007 (1) JCC 872; JT 2007 (3) SC 572; 2007(3) SCALE 445; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 438; Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 304; Dowry Prohibition Act, 1961, s. 4 - Appeal against grant of bail - Held, an application under Section 438 of the Code can be moved only by a person who has not already been arrested - Held, in the very nature of the direction which the Court can issue under s. 438 of the Code, it is clear that the direction is to be issued only at the pre-arrest stage - Direction becomes operative only after arrest - While dealing with an application under Section 438 of the Code the Court cannot restrain arrest - Modified directions - Appeal partly allowed.
