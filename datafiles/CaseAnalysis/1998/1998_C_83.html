O. R. G. Systems, Baroda v Collector of Central Excise, Vadodara

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1051; (1998) 6 SCC 56; AIR 1998 SC 2662; 1998 (77) ECR 619; 1998 (102) E.L.T. 3; JT 1998 (5) SC 92; 1998 (27) RLT 743; 1998(4) SCALE 208; [1998] 3 S.C.R. 820; 1998 (6) SLT 227; 1998 (6) Supreme 16

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4, chap. I, item 33 DD - Determination of assessable value for imposing of duty on computers manufactured by appellant - Computers were complete - They were cleared after complying with all requirements and payment of duties - Peripheral devices and other system software merely meant to increase memory or storage capacity of computers were also imported by appellant after paying countervailing duty - Held, value of peripheral devices and/or computer systems sold as well as value of service charges recovered could not be included in assessable value of computers.
