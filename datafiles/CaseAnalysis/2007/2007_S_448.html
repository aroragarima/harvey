Byvarapu Raju v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

28 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 596; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 30; (2007) 11 SCC 218; AIR 2007 SC 1904; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 2026; 2007 CRLJ 3204; JT 2007 (8) SC 177; 2007 (3) RCR(Criminal) 219; 2007(8) SCALE 95; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 815; 2007 (4) UC 461

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Subsequent Conduct, Bloodstained Earth

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 201 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, for bringing in operation of Exception 4 to s. 300 it has to be established that the act was committed without premeditation, in a sudden fight in the heat of passion upon a sudden quarrel without the offender having taken undue advantage and not having acted in a cruel or unusual manner - On the background facts considered it is clear that to the present case the Exception 4 to s. 300 applies - Altered the conviction from s. 302 to s. 304 Part I - Conviction in terms of s. 201 is well founded and does not warrant interference - Custodial sentence of 10 years imposed - Appeal partly allowed.
