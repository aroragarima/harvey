State of Himachal Pradesh v Raja Mahendra Pal and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1047; (1999) 4 SCC 43; AIR 1999 SC 1786; JT 1999 (2) SC 604; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Companies Act, 1956 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 21 and 226 - Indian Forest Act, 1878, ss. 2(2), 28, 29(a) and 31 - Himachal Pradesh (Acquisition of Management) Act, 1992 - Himachal Pradesh Forest Produce (Regulation of Trade) Act, 1982, s. 6 - Forest Act, 1927, s. 2(2) - Whether respondent could claim equal rights with respect to revenue earned from Forests by appellants having erstwhile rights over same? - Held, acceptance of his position as a Forest Superintendent clearly established that respondent 1 had accepted State Govt. to be dominant owner of property and that he was merely an officer appointed by Govt. - But for his position as a Forest Officer, he had no jurisdiction to deal with forest or even enter into it - Rights conferred on his forefathers stood extinguished - Respondent 1 entitlement in present case was restricted only to extent of sharing of royalty and not for anything more - Appeal allowed.
