Regional Manager and Disciplinary Authority, State Bank of India, Hyderabad and Another v S. Mohammed Gaffar

Supreme Court of India

16 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1765; (2002) 7 SCC 168; (2002) SCC (L&S) 1030; AIR 2002 SC 3037; 2002 (95) FLR 3; JT 2002 (6) SC 157; 2002 LabIC 2976; 2002 (3) LLJ 529; 2002(5) SCALE 619; 2003 (1) SLJ 181; 2002 (5) SLR 605; 2002 (4) SLT 755; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 573; 2002 (5) Supreme 323

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - State Bank of India - Conduct Rules under Sastry Award and Desai Award - Misconduct - Respondent was a bank clerk and presently promoted to post of head clerk - (A) Whether misconduct on part of respondent was 'gross or 'minor'? - Respondent while working in establishment section of bank and preparing establishment register got included unauthorisedly three increments for himself pertaining to three consecutive years, to which he was not legitimately entitled, without any approval or sanction of competent authority - Held, in context in which words 'gross misconduct' have to be construed for this case, charge held proved would definitely constitute 'gross misconduct' - (B) Whether punishment awarded by disciplinary authority whether to be interfered ordinarily by HC u/art. 226 jurisdiction? - In departmental proceedings, insofar as imposition of penalty or punishment is concerned, unless punishment or penalty imposed by disciplinary or Appellate Authority is either impermissible or such that it shocks conscience of HC, it should not normally interfere with same or substitute its own opinion and either impose some other punishment or penalty or direct authority to impose a particular nature or category of punishment of its choice - Further held, imposition or penalty not to be ordinarily interfered - Appeal allowed.
