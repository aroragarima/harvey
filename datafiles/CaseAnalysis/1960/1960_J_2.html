J. K. Cotton Spinning and Weaving Mills Company v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 445; AIR 1961 SC 1170; 1961 (2) FLR 529; 1961 (1) LLJ 540; 1962 (1) SCJ 417; [1961] 3 S.C.R. 185

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Labour Appellate Tribunal

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - U.P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 3 and 8 - Govt. order, 10-03-1948, cls. 5(a) and 23 - Maintainability of an application made by the Employers' Association of Northern India, Kanpur on behalf of the J. K. Cotton and Weaving Mills Co. Ltd., a member of the Association in connection with the proposed termination of service of certain members of its Watch and Ward Staff - Whether an application u/cl. 5(a) is maintainable? - In the instant case an inquiry was pending before a Conciliation Officer, cl. 23 of the Order, applied in respect of any discharge or dismissal of a workman and employer could not take advantage of cl. 5(a) of the Govt. Order and such an application could not in law be entertained by the Board - Held, application u/cl. 5(a) filed on 13-06-1950 was not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
