K. Seetharam v B. U. Papamma and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 April 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 19883; (2001) 4 SCC 322; AIR 2001 SC 2182; 2001 (45) ALR 747; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 145; JT 2001 (Supp2) SC 403; 2002 (93) RD 44; 2001(3) SCALE 468; 2001 (3) SLT 723; 2001 (3) Supreme 443; 2001 (1) UC 643

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Suit for a decree of permanent injunction - Whether defendant had admitted case of plaintiff in written statement, as such suit should have been decreed on basis of admission of defendant in pleading? - Held, question for determination is whether defendant, while constructing building, had encroached upon a portion of suit land, if question is answered in negative, suit will fail for want of cause of action - Commr.'s report was accepted by trial court - Considering report along with other evidence on record, Court held that plaintiff failed to prove that defendant had encroached on suit land - Averments in para 6 of written statement, do not give any indication that defendant admitted case stated in plaint - On other hand, averments clearly indicate that defendant denied that plaintiff had any cause of action for filing suit - High Court was clearly in error in setting aside judgment of trial court on mistaken notion that case of plaintiff was admitted by defendant in his pleading - Judgment of HC is clearly unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
