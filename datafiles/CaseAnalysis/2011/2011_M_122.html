Fida Hussain and others v Moradabad Development Authority & Anr

Supreme Court of India

19 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 510; (2011) 12 SCC 615; 2011 (5) AWC 5003; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 121; JT 2011 (8) SC 333; 2011(7) SCALE 757; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 290; 2011 (4) UPLBEC 3370

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition Act, 1898

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 11 - Acquisition of land - Compensation - Principal of res judicata - Justifiability of order - There were several appeals in which appeals stated that State Govt. had taken physical possession of land acquired by appellants - In accordance with s. 11 of the Act, Land Acquisition Officer assessed market value of acquired as compensation, however, appellants were dissatisfied with said award and Reference Court had enhanced compensation towards land of appellants - As against appeal preferred by State, Reference Court and HC had allowed same in light of judgment of Court in first appeal - Whether principal of res judicata would apply in case of appellants and order of HC was justified - Held, it could not be said that HC had adopted erroneous approach or employed wrong principles in regard to claim for enhancement of compensation - In appeals, challenge was for compensation assessed for lands notified and acquired under same notification pertaining to same villages - Therefore, it would not be proper to take different view, on ground that what was considered by SC was on different fact situation - Thus, principles of res judicata would apply only when lis was inter-parties and had attained finality of issues involved - Said principles would, however, had no application interalia in case where judgment and/or order had been passed by Court having no jurisdiction thereof and/or involving pure question of law - Hence principle of res judicata would, therefore, had no application in facts of appellants case - Appeal dismissed.

(B) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Substitution of application - Opportunity of hearing - Whether application for substitution was rejected, and by that order, appeal had abated - Held, there were no ground found in appeal paper books, regarding substitution of application - Even order by which application for substitution was supposedly rejected by HC had not been annexed - On perusal of appeal paper books it was find that in some of appeals, presence of counsel was recorded, though some of appellants could not have been heard by HC due to non-listing of matter or disposal in absence of advocate, it was clear from impugned orders enclosed in some of appeal paper books that counsel for some of appellants had been heard - It was settled position that Court speaks through its order and whatever stated therein had to be read as correct and, therefore, it was better to go by what was recorded in impugned judgment, rather than what counsel had stated at time of hearing of appeals - Hence, SC had not inclined to accept that counsel were not heard in all matters against which appeals were filed - Appeal dismissed.
