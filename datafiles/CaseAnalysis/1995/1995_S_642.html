Sneh Prabha Smt. and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 825; (1996) 7 SCC 426; AIR 1996 SC 540; 1995 (4) CCC 254; 1996 (1) CLT 119; JT 1995 (8) SC 267; 1996 (1) RentLR 44; 1995(6) SCALE 393; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 264

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Land acquisition - Land Policy - Benefit of - Entitled to - Whether appellant is not entitled to benefits of Land Policy? - Held, though appellant had applied within time to avail of benefits of Land Policy and she was asked to deposit compensation received for land acquired, Trust was not in know of fact that appellant had purchased land after publication of s. 4(1) notification - When appellant sought for execution of lease deed she was called upon to produce her title deed which, when produced, disclosed that she had purchased land after s. 4(1) notification was published - In other words, she fell into main part of general land policy and GO No. 342 D/- 8-12-1971 - Thereby, there is no order passed in her favour to extend benefits of Land Policy nor was any agreement to lease said land in her favour was entered into and registered although she purchased land after notification u/s. 4(1) was published - It would thus be clear that appellant had not fulfilled all conditions mentioned in Para 2 of GO No. 1802 D/- 2-6-1972 - Appellant is not entitled to benefits of Land Policy - Appeal allowed.
