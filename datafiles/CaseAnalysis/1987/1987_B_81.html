State of Uttar Pradesh v Brahma Datt Sharma and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Singh, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28625; (1987) 2 SCC 179; AIR 1987 SC 943; 1987 (3) ATC 319; 1987 (1) CCC 832; 1987 (54) FLR 524; JT 1987 (1) SC 571; 1987 LabIC 689; 1987 (1) LLN 768; 1987(1) SCALE 457; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 444; 1987 (3) SLR 51; 1987 (2) UJ 55

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Age Of Superannuation, Uttar Pradesh Civil Service Regulations

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 and 226 - Uttar Pradesh Civil Service Regulations, arts. 348 A and 470(b) - Pensionary benefits - Forfeiture - Legality of - Whether High Court of Allahabad is correct in quashing State Govt's notice dated 29-01-1986 issued under art. 470(b) of Civil Service Regulations calling upon respondent to show cause as to why his pension and gratuity be not forfeited? - Held, purpose of issuing show cause notice is to afford opportunity of hearing to Govt. servant and once cause is shown it is open to Govt. to consider matter in light of facts and submissions placed by Govt. servant and only thereafter a final decision in matter could be taken - Interference by court before that stage would be premature - Respondents' writ petition challenging order of dismissal had been finally disposed of on 10-08-1984, thereafter nothing remained pending before HC - No miscellaneous application could be filed in writ petition to revive proceedings in respect of subsequent event after two years - If respondent was aggrieved by notice d/- 29-01-1986 he could have filed a separate petition u/art. 226 of Constitution challenging validity of notice as it provided as separate cause of action to him - Respondent was not entitled to assail validity of notice before HC by means of a miscellaneous application in writ petition which had already been decided - HC had no jurisdiction to entertain application as no proceedings were pending before it - HC ought not have interfered with show cause notice - Further held, HC committed error in entertaining respondent's application which was founded on a separate cause of action - Appeal allowed.
