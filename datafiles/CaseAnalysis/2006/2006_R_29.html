Depot Manager, A.P.S.R.T.C. v Raghuda Siva Sankar Prasad

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 744; (2007) 1 SCC 222; AIR 2007 SC 152; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 4; 2007 (5) AWC 4588; JT 2006 (9) SC 576; 2006(11) SCALE 316; 2007 (1) SLR 486; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 625; 2007 (1) UPLBEC 75

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Service - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11-A - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 226 - APSRTC Employees Conduct Regulations, 1963 - Charge of theft - Removal from service - HC held that punishment of removal was not in consonance with gravity of charges proved against respondent and directed reinstatement of respondent with continuity of service but without back wages - Appeal against - Held, HC failed to appreciate statement given by delinquent employee admitting guilt and however ordered reinstatement, continuity of service but without back wages - Once Labour Court in its award held removal from service by taking into consideration entire facts and circumstances of case, it does not deserve interference - Respondent has no legal right to continue in Corporation - Appeal allowed.
