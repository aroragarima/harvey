Abdul Karim and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2795; (2000) 8 SCC 710; (2001) SCC (Cr) 59; AIR 2001 SC 116; 2000 (4) CCR 244; 2000 (4) Crimes 203; 2001 CRLJ 148; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 363; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 688; 2000(7) SCALE 313; 2000 (8) SLT 241; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 382; 2000 (7) Supreme 436

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Service; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Criminal Procedure Code, 1963, Government Order, TADA Act, Border Area, Terrorist, Tamil Nadu Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Preventive Act, 1987

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 321 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Indian Explosives Act, 1884 - Arms Act, 1959 - Tamil Nadu Terrorist and Disruptive Activities Preventive Act, 1987 - National Security Act, 1980 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946 - Several cases pending against one veerappan, a poacher and ivory smuggler - Verrappan abducted one famous kannad film actor and made some demands against his release - Public prosecutor made application thereby withdrawing prosecution under charges of TADA - Present Special Leave Petition by father of victim - Discretion exercisable u/s. 321 is fettered only by a consent from court on a consideration of material before it - Application u/s. 321 must aver that Public Prosecutor is, in good faith, satisfied, on consideration of all relevant material, that his withdrawal from prosecution is in public interest and it will not stifle or thwart process of law or cause injustice - Held, application was made in panic and to secure release of abducted person - But orders granting consent to withdrawal from prosecution bad - Appeal allowed.
