National Institute of Medical Science University Rajasthan and another v State of Rajasthan and others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, Deepak Gupta

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 860

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Land Revenue Act, 1956, Rajasthan Tenancy Act, 1955, Jaipur Development Authority Act, 1982, Constitution of India, 1950, Rajasthan Tenancy Act, 1956, Rajasthan Land Revenue Allotment of Unoccupied Government Agricultural Lands for the Construction of Schools, Colleges, Dispensaries, Dharamshalas and Other Buildings of Public Utility Rules, 1963, Rajasthan Improvement Trust Disposal of Urban Land Rules, 1974

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Allotment of land - Illegal encroachment - Entitled to - Petitioner/University filed an application for allotment of land in question - However, no favourable action was taken on the representations made - Petitioners filed writ petition before HC seeking that directions to be issued in its favour for allotment of land which was dismissed - On appeals, DB dismissed appeals - Hence, instant special leave petitions.

Held, it has also come on record as a matter of fact that petitioners had encroached land in question. Due to illegal and unlawful construction having been made in the area by several parties including petitioners Lake was absolutely dry and residents of city were suffering from water shortage because of that since water from the region was being supplied to city before the area dried up. There was no law that supports brazenness of petitioners in wantonly encroaching land in question and making huge constructions thereon. Despite orders passed by HC and in suo motu petition and views expressed by Monitoring Committee appointed by HC as well as Expert Committee set up by State Govt. and plan of action having been prepared by State Govt. nothing substantive appears to have been achieved on the ground over the years. Petitioners were powerful and influential entity and it was able to successfully frustrate its eviction and demolition of construction for at least one decade. SC direct demolition of unauthorized construction by or on behalf of petitioners on land in question. The demolition should be carried out by State Development Authority with the assistance of State Govt. and Collector on or before 30-11-2017. SC impose costs of Rs.10 lakhs per petition for brazen encroachment. The amount should be deposited by petitioners in the Registry of SC within 6 weeks. Petitions dismissed.
