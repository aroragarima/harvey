Swaran Lata v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 January 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 9; (1979) 3 SCC 165; (1979) SCC (L&S) 237; 1980 (17) DLT 140; 1979 (1) LLJ 273; 1979 (2) LLN 301; [1979] 2 S.C.R. 953; 1979 SLJ 170; 1979 (1) SLR 710

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - (A) Whether court can provide relief if the equitable right of the person is violated - Held, courts can only act where there is any infringement of a right but not merely an equitable considerations - Govt. has authority to decide the matter - (B) Whether the failure of the Union Public Service Commission to re-advertise the post lead to violation of art. 16(1)? - Held, no relaxation in qualifications can be made when an advertisement has duly been issued inviting applications and persons possessing the qualifications advertised, as prescribed by the rules are available and have submitted their applications - The advertisement itself contained a relaxation clause and, therefore, nothing prevented respondent No. 7 from making an application, if she felt that she was better - Held that contention appears to be an afterthought and must be rejected - (C) Whether the decision of the Commission to relax the qualification is just? - Held, appellant cannot approbate and reprobate - She had willingly, of her own accord, applied for the post, in response to the advertisement issued by the Union Public Service Commission for direct recruitment - She cannot be heard to say that the selection of respondent No. 6 by direct recruitment through the Commission was invalid, as being contrary to the directions issued by the Central Govt. u/s. 84 of the Act - She fully knew that under the terms of the advertisement, the Commission had reserved to itself the power to relax any of the essential qualifications - (D) Whether the Selection Commission decision to appoint respondent No. 6 was a biased decision? - Held, no - There was nothing wrong in the Union Public Service Commission taking such expert advice - Selection Committee was wholly uninfluenced by any other consideration except merit - The allegations which are of a personal nature are not borne out at all - Director has recorded appreciation of her as Principal of the institute. This clearly shows that he had no particular animus against her - There is nothing to suggest that the reasons given by the Home Secretary were not his own reasons based upon his own information - Appeal dismissed.
