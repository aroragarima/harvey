Smt. Khatoon Begum and Others v Union Of India And Ors. Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

09 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 262; (1981) 2 SCC 480; (1981) SCC (Cr) 493; AIR 1981 SC 1077; [1983] 53 Comp Cas 244; 1981 CRLJ 606; 1981 CrLR(SC) 268; 1981 MLJ 466; 1981(1) SCALE 507; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 137

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Summary: Defence & Security - National Security Act, 1980, s. 8 - Constitution Of India, art. 22(5) - Appeal against preventive detention - Delay in considering representation of detenu - Whether representation by detenu can be delayed under National Security Act, 1980? - It is essential that any law providing for preventive detention and any authority obliged to make orders for preventive detention should adopt procedures calculated towards expeditious consideration of representations made by detenus - Held, a person preventively detained under provisions of National Security Act is entitled to be released if there is delay in consideration of representation made by him to detaining authority - A certain amount of delay being inevitable cannot be urged - Delay in consideration of representation should not be allowed to prejudice detention - Petition allowed.
