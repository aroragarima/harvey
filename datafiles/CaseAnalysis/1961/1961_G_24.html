Government of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Raja Mohammad Amir Ahmad Khan

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 392; AIR 1961 SC 787; 1962 (1) ALT(SC) 416; 1962 (1) MLJ 170; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 170; 1962 (1) SCJ 436; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 97

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Stamp Act, 1899, ss. 31, 32, 33 and 48 - Whether or not imposition of stamp duty and penalty wasvvalid when instrument was merely brought before Collector for seeking his advice as to what proper duty would be? - Scheme of Act shows that where a person is simply seeking opinion of Collector as to proper duty in regard to an instrument, he approaches him u/s. 31 if it is properly stamped and person executing document wants to proceed with effectuating document or using it for purposes of evidence, he is to make up duty and u/s. 32, Collector will then make an endorsement and instrument will be treated as if it was duly stamped from very beginning, but if he does not want to proceed any further than seeking determination of duty payable then no consequence will follow and an executed document is in same position as an instrument which is unexecuted and unstamped and after determination of duty Collector becomes functus officio and provisions of s. 33 have no application and provisions of that section are a subsequent stage when something more than mere asking of opinion of Collector is to be done - Held, as soon as Collector determined duty he became functus officio and could not impound instrument u/s. 33 - Appeal dismissed.
