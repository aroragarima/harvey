State of Punjab v Jagir Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A. Alagiriswami

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 164; (1973) SCC (Cr) 886; (1974) 3 SCC 277; AIR 1973 SC 2407; 1973 CRLJ 1589; 1973 CRLR 570; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, s. 367 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - (A) Conclusion about guilt of accused - Duty of Court - (B) Constitution - Appeal against acquittal of accused from offence u/s 302 of IPC, 1860 - Sentence of death, when not called for - Held, court should not reject trustworthy evidence on fanciful grounds resulting in miscarriage of justice, duty of court pointed - When a period of two years has expired since acquittal of accused by High Court - Appeal allowed.
