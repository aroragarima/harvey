Punjab Co-Operative Bank Limited v R. S. Bhatia (Dead) Through L. Rs

Supreme Court of India

13 August 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A. Alagiriswami, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 40; (1975) SCC (L&S) 394; (1975) 4 SCC 696; AIR 1975 SC 1898; 1975 (31) FLR 326; 1975 LabIC 1439; 1975 (2) LLJ 373; 1975 UJ 685

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: CPC, 1908, s. 11 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33A, 2(s) and 33C(2) - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Question whether respondent was a workman or not within the meaning of Central Act - Decision in proceeding u/s. 33-A given by competent Labour Court - Trial of same issue in proceeding u/s. 33-C(2) between the same parties - (B) Accountant - Mere fact that he used to sign salary bills of staff - If 'employed mainly in a managerial or administrative capacity' - (C) Claim of workman u/s. 33C(2) - Plea of employer that it was barred by time or was not fit to be entertained on ground of delay - (D) Claim u/s. 33-C (2) - For benefits arising out of award - Argument that claim ought to have been entertained by the Govt. u/s. 33-C(1) - Trial of same issue in held barred on the principles of res judicata - Fact held, did not make him 'employed mainly in a managerial or administrative capacity' - Held rightly rejected by Labour - Not tenable - Appeal Dismissed.
