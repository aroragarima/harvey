Punjab State Industrial Development Corporation v P.N.F.C.Karamchari Sangh and Another

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Kumar, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 111; (2006) 4 SCC 367; 2006 (2) BC 559; JT 2006 (4) SC 337; 2006 (143) PLR 308; 2006(4) SCALE 39; 2006 (4) SCJ 763; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 446(2)(d), 529A - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1985 - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 9 - PNFC was promoted by PSIDC - Winding up order was passed in PNFC - Stopped paying wages to its workers - Chief Minister ordered disbursement of 6 months salary - Company Judge passed an order directing PSIDC to release funds in terms of order of Chief Minister - Review dismissed - Appeal against - Whether liability with respect to money due from Company in liquidation towards its workers could be fastened on an independent corporation? - Held, note of Chief Minister cannot be said to be a directive of State Govt. nor it is in relation to broad policy of Company - PSIDC could not be made liable for dues owed by PNFC to its workers - Appeal allowed.
