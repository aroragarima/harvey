A. K. Mehaboob v Intelligence Officer, Narcotics Control Bureau With P. K. Naushad

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3747; (2001) 10 SCC 203; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 49; JT 2001 (1) SC 614

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Appeals disposed of.

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21 and 29 - (A) Whether statement made to officer u/s. 67 could be wholly relied upon? - (B) Whether there was violation of ss. 42 and 52? - Held, statement retracted by accused himself and that it was coaxed out of him and it had no corroboration - Both contentions cannot be countenanced by court for two reasons - One is that they were not seriously pressed into service before High Court - Second is that there is no factual basis for such contentions - S. 30 not included in charge nor in discussion therefore not be fair to ferret out a new provision from Act to fasten appellant with a penal liability which may visit him with very serious consequences - Appeal disposed of.
