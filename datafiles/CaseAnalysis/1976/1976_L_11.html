Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Madras v Late R. Sridharan (By Legal Representatives) and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 307; (1976) 4 SCC 489; 1978 HLR 600; [1976] 104 ITR 436; 1977 (1) MLJ 5; 1977 (1) MLJ(SC) 5; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 478; 1976 UJ 668

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Special Marriage Act, 1954 - Hindu family property - Succession - An individual, claimed to be assessed in status of a member of HUF, contended that property held by him was ancestral and his son was a Hindu - Income Tax officer (ITO), Wealth Tax officer (WTO) and Expenditure Tax officer (ETO) refused to accede to contention and assessed in status of an individual - Assessee appealed to Appellate Assistant Commissioner but remained unsuccessful - Orders passed by Respective officers and Appellate Assistant Commissioner were affirmed in appeals by Appellate Tribunal - Appellate Tribunal referred question of law for opinion of HC - HC answered question in affirmative but granted certificate of fitness for appeal to SC - After death of assessee, his wife filed a wealth tax return claiming that assessment should be made in status of HUF - WTO rejected claim holding that she was not a Hindu and in any case since her marriage with assessee was under Special Marriage Act, 1954, her son had no right by birth in properties obtained by assessee on partition - Appeal was made to Appellate Assistant Commissioner who affirmed order - In appeal HC answered question in affirmative against the Revenue - Hence, instant appeals - Whether assessee and his son constituted a HUF for purposes of assessment - Held, any child legitimate or illegitimate, one of whose parents was a Hindu by religion and who was brought up as a Hindu was a Hindu - Assessee and his son were followers of Hinduism as recognized by society - Son of assessee was Hindu and he could validly be a member of HUF headed by his father and be governed by Hindu law - Discretion vested in a Hindu assessee to treat his properties as joint family properties by taking into his fold his Hindu sons so as to constitute joint family properties - Hence, answer given by HC for both appeals was right - Appeals dismissed.
