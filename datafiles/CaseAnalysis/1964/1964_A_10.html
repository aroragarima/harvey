State of Andhra Pradesh v Abdul Bakhi and Bros

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 164; AIR 1965 SC 531; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 29; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 664; 1964 (15) STC 644

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950, Sales Tax Rules 1950

Summary: Sales Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, s. 22(1) - Hyderabad General Sales Tax Act, 1950, ss. 2(e), 2(m) and 4 - Sales Tax Rules 1950, rr. 5 and 40 - Whether High Court had erred in modifying order of tax authorities and excluding from computation of taxable turnover price paid by respondents for tanning bark used in tannery? - Held, a person to be a dealer must be engaged in business of buying or selling or supplying goods - It cannot be said in present case that tanning bark was bought by respondent for any purpose unconnected with business carried on by them, viz, manufacture and sale of dressed hides and skins - High Court had erred - Appeal allowed.
