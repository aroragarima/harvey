Nandlal Misra v K. L. Misra

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 129; AIR 1960 SC 882; 1960 CRLJ 1246; 1961 (1) SCJ 65; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 431

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 200-203, 480 and 488 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Preliminary enquiry before notice invalidating inquiry - (A) Whether s. 488 of CrPC, 1973 contemplates preliminary inquiry before issuing notice?- Held, does not contemplate - However, it lays down that all evidence under that chapter should be taken in presence of respondent or his pleader, indicating thereby that one enquiry only should be held after notice - (B) Whether Supreme Court would interfere u/art. 136 in a case of invalidating inquiry? - Held,ordinarily Supreme Court would not interfere in a case of invalidating inquiry Constitution u/art. 136 but considering special circumstance of case Supreme Court interfered and set aside orders of magistrate on ground of illegal procedure followed by him - Appeal allowed.
