Ravinder Singh Gorkhi v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 643; (2006) 5 SCC 584; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 632; AIR 2006 SC 2157; 2006 CRLJ 2791; 2006 (130) DLT 602; 2006 (2) JCC 856; JT 2006 (5) SC 468; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 45; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 156; 2006(5) SCALE 682; 2006 (6) SCJ 195; [2006] Supp2 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951, Reformatory Schools Act, 1897

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 35 - Uttar Pradesh Children Act, 1951 ss. 2(4) and 27 - Juvenile Justice Act, 2000 - Appellant convicted and sentence for offence of murder - Appellant claimed benefit of s. 2(4) of 1951 act on ground that he was minor at time of commission of offence - Appellant minor as per school leaving certificate - Whether school leaving certificate purported to have been issued by authorities of primary school would attract provision of s. 35 of Indian Evidence Act? - School leaving certificate not issued in ordinary course of business of school - No record to show that date of birth was recorded in register maintained by school as per requirement of law u/s. 35 - Age of appellant not verified at time of making entries in school leaving certificate - School leaving certificate was not original one - Document is only admissible u/s. 35 if it is in nature of entry in any public or official register if it states fact in issue or relevant fact if entry is made by public servant in discharge of official duty or by any person in performance of duty specially enjoined by law of country and if all persons concerned indisputably must have an access thereto - School leaving certificate not issued by concerned person who was in school at time of admission of appellant cannot be relied upon - Held, prosecution has proved its case - Court estimated his age to be 18 years - Appeal dismissed.
