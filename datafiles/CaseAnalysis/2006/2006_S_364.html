Zahira Habibullah Sheikh and Another v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 639; (2006) 2 SCC (Cr) 8; (2006) 3 SCC 374; AIR 2006 SC 1367; 2006 CRLJ 1694; 2006 (2) G.L.R. 1493; 2006 (1) JCC 427; JT 2006 (3) SC 399; 2006 (2) RCR(Criminal) 448; 2006 (5) SCJ 536; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1081

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Summary: Criminal - Constitution - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 391, 173(8) and 311 - Constitution of India, 1950, Entry 77 List 1, arts. 129 and 142(2) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 60, 64 and 91 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1987 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 15 - Absence of an atmosphere conducive to fair trial - Zahira who was projected as star witness made a grievance that she was intimidated, threatened and coerced to depart from truth - Trial Court on basis of statements made by witnesses directed acquittal of accused persons - Before High Court an application u/s. 391 of CrPC, highlighting necessity for accepting additional evidence was filed - Foundation was statement made by Zahira - Prayer not accepted - Appeal - (A) Detailed examination is necessary as to which version of Zahira is a truthful version as she had made departure from her statements/stands at different points of time - Held, Registrar General of this Court is directed to conduct inquiry and submit a report to this Court within three months - Direct Registrar to file an affidavit indicating details of her accounts and properties to show inducement - Inquiry Officer has categorically recorded that Zahira had changed her stands at different stages and has departed from statements made before this Court - Found that Zahira has not been able to explain assets in her possession in spite of several opportunities having been granted - Money has exchanged hands and that was main inducement - Element of threat cannot be altogether ruled out - (B) Whether procedure adopted during enquiry was fair? - On a bare perusal of proceedings of enquiry, it is clear that procedure adopted was quite transparent - Further Further held, that allegation that Inquiry Officer acted with some pre-conceived ideas and/or report was based on presumptions is not correct - (C) What is fate of ongoing trial in HC in which she was a witness - Acceptance of report in present proceedings cannot have any determinative role in trial - Further held, that Zahira's role in whole case is an eye-opener for all concerned with administration of criminal justice - Criminal justice system is likely to be affected if persons like Zahira are to be left unpunished - Zahira could not explain her assets and explanations given by her in respect of sources of bank deposits etc have been found to be unacceptable - Further held, that Zahira has committed contempt of this Court - (D) What is restriction or limitation on extent of punishment, which this Court may award while exercising its contempt jurisdiction? - Since, no such law has been enacted by Parliament, nature of punishment prescribed under Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 may act as a guide - But extent of punishment as prescribed under that Act can apply only to HCs, because 1971 Act ipso facto does not deal with contempt jurisdiction of Supreme Court - Except that s. 15 of Act prescribes procedural mode for taking cognizance of criminal contempt by Supreme Court also - S. 15, however is not a substantive provision conferring contempt jurisdiction - Held that it may be decided in a proper case, when so raised - (E) Fair Trial - U/s. 311, CrPC it is plain duty of a Court to examine such of those witnesses as it considers, absolutely necessary for doing justice between State and subject - Duty cast upon Court to arrive at truth by all lawful means and one of such means is examination of witnesses of its own accord - Second part of it imposes upon Magistrate an obligation that Court shall summon and examine all persons whose evidence appears to be essential to just decision of case - Fate of proceedings cannot always be left entirely in hands of parties, as concept of fair trial entails familiar triangulation of interests of accused, victim and society and it is community that acts through State and prosecuting agencies - Fair trial means a trial in which bias or prejudice for or against accused, witnesses, or cause which is being tried is eliminated - If witnesses get threatened or are forced to give false evidence that also would not result in a fair trial - State has a definite role to play in protecting witnesses - It has to ensure that during a trial in Court witness could safely depose truth without any fear - Applications disposed of.
