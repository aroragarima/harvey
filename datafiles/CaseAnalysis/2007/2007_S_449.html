Sanapareddy Maheedhar and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1295; (2007) 13 SCC 165; AIR 2008 SC 787; 2008 CRLJ 1375; 2008 (1) JCC 495; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 293; 2007(14) SCALE 321; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 478

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Matrimonial Offence

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 498A, 406; Dowry Prohibition Act 1961, ss. 4, 6 - Petition for quashing the proceedings dismissed - Appeal against - Held, while deciding a petition filed for quashing the FIR or complaint or restraining the competent authority from investigating the allegations contained in the FIR or complaint or for stalling the trial of the case, the High Court should be extremely careful and circumspect - If the High Court is satisfied that the complaint does not disclose commission of any offence or prosecution is barred by limitation or that the proceedings of criminal case would result in failure of justice, then it may exercise inherent power under s. 482 of Cr.P.C - Continuation of proceedings will amount to abuse of the process of the Court - Appeal allowed.
