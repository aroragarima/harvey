Dakshin Haryana Bijli Vitran Nigam Ltd v M/S. Paramount Polymers Pvt. Ltd

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1411; (2006) 13 SCC 101; AIR 2007 SC 2; 2007 (1) AWC 45; 2007 (1) Bom.C.R. 906; JT 2006 (9) SC 349; 2006(10) SCALE 488; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 635

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Haryana Government Electrical Undertakings (Dues Recovery) Act, 1970

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss. 49, 24 - State Financial Corporations Act, 1951, s. 29 - Consumption charges - Default in paying electrical charges - Disconnection - Terms & Condition of supply of electrical energy - Succeed - Respondent no. 1 applied for connection on dt. 1-1-2002 - Appellant- Company took stand that unless respondent no. 1 paid amount outstanding against 'X' prior consumer, no fresh connection could be given to respondent no.1 - Respondent no. 1 filed suit, before Trial Court, seeking to restrain appellant-Company from interfering with use of generators by respondent no.1 for generating electricity for its use and for mandatory injunction directing appellant- Even while suit was pending, respondent no. 1 filed writ petition before Single Judge, praying for issue of writ of certiorari to quash circular dt. 27-11-2001 and directing appellant to provide electric connection temporarily to enable respondent no.1 to run its factory during pendency of Writ Petition and for other incidental reliefs - Single Judge held, there was no charge on premises for electricity charges run up by 'X' and purchase by respondent no.1 was on 22-4-1999, - Amendment promulgated on 27-11-2001 could not be applied in case of respondent no.1 and that appellant- Company was bound to provide electric connection to respondent no. 1 without insisting on Terms and Conditions introduced with effect from 27-11-2001 - Thus, it was directed that appellant release power connection to respondent no.1 expeditiously - Aggrieved by this appellant filed appeal before DB, which was dismissed and held, relied on in Isha Marbles and Others v Bihar State Electricity Board and Others 1995 Indlaw SC 383 - Amendment of Terms and Conditions of Supply could not be made applicable since purchase by respondent no. 1 was prior to introduction of condition 21A and as legislation which affected substantive rights were presumed prospective - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in allowing petition filed by respondent no. 1 in terms of decision in Isha Marbles and Others v Bihar State Electricity Board and Others 1995 Indlaw SC 383 - Held, decision in Isha Marbles 1995 Indlaw SC 383 was by itself not an answer to validity of cl. 21A of the terms and conditions inserted by notification - U/s. 49 of the 1948 Act, licensee or rather, Electricity Board, was entitled to set down terms and conditions for supply of electrical energy - Hence, decision in Isha Marbles 1995 Indlaw SC 383 could not be applied to strike down condition imposed and respondent no.1 had to make out case independent on ratio of Isha Marbles 1995 Indlaw SC 383, though it could rely on its ratio if it was helpful, for attacking insertion of such condition for supply of electrical energy and SC was essentially dealing with construction of s. 24 of the 1948 Act in arriving at its conclusion decision in Isha Marbles 1995 Indlaw SC 383 does not arise in present case and set aside orders of Single Judge and DB of HC and held, respondent no. 1 should lose benefit of fresh connection, but considering that respondent no. 1 was industrial undertaking and taking note of plea that investments have been made by it to make unit workable and it would be appropriate to direct respondent no.1 to deposit portion of amount in arrears as condition for continuance of supply to it by appellant on payment of regular monthly bills as per the terms - Appeal allowed.
