Himanchal Pradesh State Electricity Board and another v Ranjeet Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

05 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2760; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 1115

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33C(2) - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, s. 10 - Reinstatement - Back wages - Respondents were employed on daily wages basis - Reference was made to Labour Court which held that respective applicants/respondents were entitled to be paid minimum statutory bonus within stipulated time - Petitions were filed against impugned order before HC which were allowed - Hence, instant Appeals - Whether HC was right in presuming that award of Labour Court for grant of back wages was implied in relief of reinstatement - Held, whenever workman was entitled to receive from his employer any money or any benefit which was capable of being computed in terms of money and which he was entitled to receive from his employer and was denied of such benefit could approach Labour Court u/s. 33-C(2) of 1947 Act - However, it could not be said that such right or benefit had accrued to workman as specific question of relief granted, was confined only to reinstatement without stating anything more as to back wages - Thus, appropriate forum wherein such question of back wages could be decided was only in proceeding to whom reference u/s.10 of 1965 Act was made - It would be incorrect to state that merely upon reinstatement, workman would be entitled, under terms of award, to all his arrears of pay and allowances - Hence, HC ought not to have presumed that award of Labour Court for grant of back wages was implied in relief of reinstatement or that award of reinstatement itself conferred right for claim of back wages - Appeals allowed.

(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947,s. 33C(2) - Dispute relating to Bonus - Jurisdiction - Whether Labour Court had jurisdiction to decide issue relating to 'Bonus' - Held, when claim was made before Labour Court u/s. 33(C)(2) of the Act, it should clearly understand limitations under which it was to function - It could not arrogate to itself functions of Industrial Tribunal which alone was entitled to make adjudications in nature of determinations like respondent's right to relief - Labour Court under the Act could decide only matters specified in Second Schedule - However, 'Bonus' was not covered by Second Schedule - Further if there was disagreement regarding pre existing rights then that had to be decided by competent authority - Hence, question of entitlement to bonus could not be decided by Labour Court - Appeals allowed.
