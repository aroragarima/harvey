Union Of India Etc. Etc. v K.V. Jankiraman Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, M.H. Kania, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1034; (1991) 4 SCC 109; (1991) SCC (L&S) 387; AIR 1991 SC 2010; 1993 (23) ATC 322; 1991 (63) FLR 766; JT 1991 (3) SC 527; 1991 (2) LLJ 570; 1992 (1) LLN 24; 1991(2) SCALE 423; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 790; 1991 (5) SLR 602; 1992 (1) UPLBEC 445

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Right To Promotion

Summary: Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14, 39, 311 and 309 - Officer Memo no. 2011/1/79 Estta D/- 30-01-1982 - Para 3(d)(iii) - Fundamental Rules r. 17(1) - Government of India (Department of Personnel and Training) - Promotion - (A) When resorting to sealed cover procedure is permissible - In light of pendency of preliminary investigation prior to that stage - (B) Denial of benefits of promotional post on exoneration of employee in criminal /disciplinary proceedings - (C) Constitutionality of treating case of employee who were found guilty of misconduct differently by not placing on par with other employees - Held, permissible only after charge memo/charge sheet is issued to employee and pendency not sufficient to enable authorities to adopt said procedure - Not permissible, as principle of 'no work no pay' is not applicable in such case and r. 17(1) of Fundamental Rules would also be inapplicable - Not discriminatory - Appeal allowed.
