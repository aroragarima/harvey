Kewal Pati Smt v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1088; (1995) SCC (Cr) 556; (1995) 3 SCC 600; 1995 ACJ 859; AIR 1995 SCW 2236; 1995 (3) CCR 1; 1995 (2) Crimes 304; 1995 CRLJ 2920; 1995(2) SCALE 729; 1995 (2) SCJ 540; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 207; 1995 (2) UJ 291

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 303 - Uttar Pradesh Jail Manual - Compensation - Petition entertained on a letter sent by wife of deceased who was killed by a co-accused - Affidavit filed on behalf of Govt. as well stating that there was no provisions in Uttar Pradesh Jail Manual for grant of compensation to family of deceased convict - Held, prisoner does not cease to have his constitutional right except to extent he has been deprived of it in accordance with law - Untimely death has deprived petitioner and her children of his company and affection - They are entitled to be compensated - Writ Petition allowed.
