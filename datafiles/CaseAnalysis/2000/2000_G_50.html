Gobind Ram v Gian Chand

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Phukan, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 477; (2000) 7 SCC 548; AIR 2000 SC 3106; 2000 (41) ALR 601; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 101; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 674; 2001 (92) RD 14; 2000(6) SCALE 522; 2000 (7) SLT 152; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 443; 2000 (6) Supreme 438

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Land & Property; Service; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 20 - Decreeing Specific Performance - Is discretion of court - Agreement for sale of property - Respondent paid earnest money - Failure of appellant to execute sale deed led to filing of suit for specific performance of contract - Appellant offered to pay money to respondent to cancel contract but respondent deposited balance consideration - Held, respondent not taking any unfair advantage over appellant - Compensation is no relief in this case - However, to mitigate hardship respondent directed to pay further amount to appellant on his giving possession of suit property.
