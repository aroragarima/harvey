Sharp Business Machines Private Limited, Bangalore v Collector of Customs, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 399; (1991) SCC (Cr) 114; (1991) 1 SCC 154; 1990 CRLR 615; 1990 (30) ECC 27; 1990 (31) ECR 177; 1990 (49) E.L.T. 640; JT 1991 (4) SC 74; 1990(2) SCALE 435; [1990] 1 S.C.R. 28; [1990] supp1 S.C.R. 28

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Importation, Misdeclaration, Sea Customs Act 1962, Imports (Control) Order 1955

Summary: Customs - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Sea Customs Act 1962 - Customs Act, 1962 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975 - Misdeclaration of value - Confiscation of goods - Legality - Appellant/Sharp Business Machines (Pvt.) Ltd company was small scale manufacturing unit - Appellant started manufacture of plain paper copiers and obtained license in this regard from licensing authority - Appellant imported components and consumables in SKD/CKD for plain paper copiers - Appellant sought clearance of imported goods under bills of entry - Appellant declared total value of consignment as Rs. 99,612 - Proceedings were held before Appraiser of Customs for verification of goods and their valuation - Collector of Customs held that there was misdeclaration of value of goods and passed order for confiscation of entire goods with an option to company to redeem them on payment of fine - Aggrieved appellant filed appeals before Tribunal and same was dismissed - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned order passed by Collector of Customs was justified - Held, import of fully assembled copiers was prohibited - Appellant was only entitled to import 62% of components - As already mentioned above, device adopted by appellant in present case was complete fraud on Import Policy and appellant was doing indirectly what he was not permitted to do directly - SC viewed that facts in present case were more akin and similar to facts of case Girdharilal Bansidhar v Union of India 1964 Indlaw SC 415 which was distinguished in case of Union of India v. Tara Chand Gupta & Bros 1971 Indlaw SC 126 - Appellant also assailed finding recorded by Collector and upheld by Tribunal and argued that components imported by appellant tallied with parts which were permitted under license - SC did not find any force in this submission - Collector had given detailed reasons for holding that imported goods were not covered by valid license and Tribunal having upheld such finding, same could not be challenged by appellant before SC - This was a case where appellant had not only violated terms and conditions of license but also committed fraud on Import Policy itself - There was no ground or justification to reduce penalty or fine - Appeal dismissed.
