Luxmi Tea Company Limited v Pradip Kumar Sarkar

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 174; (1989) Supp2 SCC 656; 1990 (2) CalLT(SC) 17; [1990] 67 Comp Cas 518; 1989 (3) CompLJ 285; JT 1989 (4) SC 350; 1989(2) SCALE 1035; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 82

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 108, 155 and 111 (2) - Transfer of shares - Refusal - Can a company refuse to register transfer of shares on basis of inherent power under Article of Association? - Unless a legal impediment in transfer of a share, a shareholder has right to transfer his share - Neither a specific no residuary power of refusal provided - Undeclared inherent power not exercisable to refuse registration in interest of company - Evidence not proved that prices paid higher than mentioned in transfer deeds - Appeal dismissed.
