Â Puranlal Lakhanpal v President of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 481; AIR 1961 SC 1519; 1962 (1) SCJ 670; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 688

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 81 and 370(1)(d) - Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order (1954) Para 5(c) - Scope of - Power of 'Modification', u/art. 370(1)(d) - Power to amend provisions of Constitution included - Effect - Whether President had power to make modification vested u/art. 81 - Introduction of indirect election in place of direct election, to seats in House of People from State of Jammu and Kashmir not unconstitutional - Para 5(c) of Constitution (Application to Jammu and Kashmir) Order, 1954, made by President u/art. 370(1), in matter of modification of art. 81, is not unconstitutional - Element of election still remains in matter of filling those seats, though it has been made indirect - In these circumstances it cannot be said that there has been a radical alteration in art. 81 by modification effected by Order - In context of Constitution court must give widest effect to meaning of word 'modification' used in art. 370(1) and in that sense it includes an amendment - There is no reasons to limit word 'modifications' as used in art. 370(1) only to such modifications as do not make any radical transformation - Therefore, President had power to make modification which he did in art. 81 - Petition dismissed.
