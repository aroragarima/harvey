Senior Electric Inspector and Others v Laxmi Narayan Chopra and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 443; AIR 1962 SC 159; 1962 (1) SCJ 593; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 146

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Telecom

Keywords: Apparatus

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Telecom - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, ss. 3 and 34 - Telegraph line - Interruption to electricity supply - Notice - Jurisdiction - Respondent had a business wherein electric moors were operated for working drills - At a distance of 100 feet from respondent's factory there was a Post and Telegraph Wireless Station which handled public messages in large volume - Severe electrical interference was observed in said Station due to local induction from respondent's factory - Senior Electric Inspector-appellant made an order u/s. 34(2)(b) of 1885 Act which required the respondent to remedy the injuries that affected the lines used for wireless telegraphic communications at the Wireless Receiving Centre - Respondent filed a writ u/art. 226 of the Constitution and prayed to direct the appellants to withdraw and cancel the said order - HC allowed the petition and held that there was no 'telegraph line' in the Post and Telegraph Wireless Station within the meaning of s. 34(2)(b) of 1885 Act, and therefore, the notice issued by the appellant was without jurisdiction - Appellant filed the instant appeal and challenged the decision of HC - Whether, the decision of HC was liable to be set aside - Held, 'telegraph line' was sufficiently comprehensive to take in the wires used for the purpose of the apparatus which received communications by means of electricity of the Post and Telegraph Wireless Station - Telegraph line meant a wire or wires used for the purpose of an appliance or apparatus which received telegraphic or other communications by means of electricity - Wires of the aerial as well as of the apparatus were used for the purpose of the apparatus which received communications and the chit was received by apparatus employed telegraph within the meaning of s. 3(4) of 1885 Act - Any works for the generation transmission supply, or used of electrical energy injuriously affected such a telegraph line the said Govt. was authorized to take appropriate action u/s. 34 of the 1885 Act hence, the decision of HC was liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
