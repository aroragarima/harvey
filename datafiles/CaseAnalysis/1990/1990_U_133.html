Raj Kumar Karwal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 990; (1990) SCC (Cr) 330; (1990) SCC (Cr) 331; (1990) 2 SCC 409; AIR 1991 SC 45; 1990 (3) Bom.C.R. 517; 1990 (2) Crimes 435; 1991 CRLJ 97; 1990 CRLR 358; 1990 (41) DLT 49; 1990 (48) E.L.T. 496; JT 1990 (1) SC 503; 1990(1) SCALE 509; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 63; 1990 (2) UJ 373

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Opium Act, 1878

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 173 and 439 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 12 - Indian Evidence Act, 1873, s. 25 - Customs Act, 1962, s. 135A - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 53, 21, 23, 29, 30, 41, 42 and 43 - Whether officers of Department of Revenue Intelligence (DRI) who have been invested with powers of an officer-in-charge of a police station u/s. 53 of Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 are police officers within meaning of s. 25 of Evidence Act? - Held, no - Test for determining whether a person is a police officer for purpose of s. 25 of Evidence Act would be whether powers of a police officer which are conferred on him or which are exercisable by him because he is deemed to be an officer in charge of a police station establish a direct or substantial relationship with prohibition enacted by s. 25, that is, recording of a confession - Provisions found in Chap. V of Act show that there is nothing in Act to indicate that all powers under Chap. XII of Code, including power to file a report u/s. 173 of Code have been expressly conferred on officers who are invested with powers of an officer-in-charge of a police station u/s. 53 - Special procedure has been prescribed for disposal of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances having regard to factors set out in s. 52 A - Role of officers effecting arrest or seizure, except in case of a police officer, ends with disposal of person arrested and article seized in manner provided by ss. 52 and 52A of Act - Appeal dismissed.
