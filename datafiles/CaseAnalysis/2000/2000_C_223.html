Chelmsford Club v Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi

Supreme Court of India

02 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 127; (2000) 3 SCC 214; AIR 2000 SC 1092; 2000 (159) CTR 235; [2000] 243 ITR 89; JT 2000 (2) SC 580; 2000(2) SCALE 201; [2000] 109 TAXMAN 215

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 2(24)(vii) and 22 - Appellant runs a club on 'no-profit-no-loss' basis exclusively for its members and their guests - Members pay for all their expenses, not entitled to any share in profits - Surplus, if any, used for maintenance and development of club - (A) Annual letting value of club building - Assessability under the head 'income from property' - Determination of - Held, annual value of clubhouse, as contemplated in s. 22 of Act, outside purview of levy of income tax - (B) Incidence of tax u/s. 22 - Determination of - Held u/s. 22 of act, tax levied is on income from property and not on property itself - (C) Principle of mutuality - Applicability of - Held, business of appellant governed by principle of mutuality - S. 2(24) of Act recognises principle of mutuality and excludes all businesses involving such principle from purview of Act, except mentioned in cl. (vii) of section - Appeals allowed.
