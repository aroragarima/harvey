Â State Of Gujarat v Shri Shantilal Mangaldas & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover, Mohammad Hidayatullah, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 254; (1969) 1 SCC 509; AIR 1969 SC 634; 1969 (10) G.L.R. 879; 1969 (2) MLJ 59; 1969 (2) MLJ(SC) 59; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 341

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pith And Substance, Competence Of The Legislature

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31(2) - Bombay Town Planning Act, 1955, ss. 53 and 67 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961 - Bombay Town Planning Act 1955 - In writ petition field by respondent No.1 HC had declared ss. 53 and 67 of Act ultra vires, insofar as they authorised the local authority/respondent No.2 in this appeal, to acquire lands under town-planning scheme, and as corollary to that view was declared invalid the City Wall Improvement Town Planning Scheme No. 5 framed in exercise of the powers conferred under Act - Whether the scheme of the Act which provided for adjustment of the market value of land at the date of the declaration of intention of making scheme against market value of the land which went to form the reconstituted plot, if any, specified a principle for determination of compensation to be given within the meaning of Art. 31(2) of Constitution - Held, once the draft town planning scheme was sanctioned, land became subject to the provisions of the Act, and the final town- planning scheme being sanctioned, by statutory operation the title of the various owners was readjusted and the lands needed for public purpose vested in respondent No.2 - Land required for any of the purposes of town-planning scheme could not be acquired, otherwise than under the Act, for it was settled rule of interpretation of statutes that when power was given under statute to do a certain thing in' certain way the thing should be done in that way or not at all - Again it could not be said that because it was possible for the State, if so minded, to acquire lands for a public purpose of a local authority, the statutory effect given to a town-planning scheme results in discrimination between persons similarly circumstanced - SC in one case had struck down the acquisition on the ground that when the lands were acquired by the State Govt. for a housing scheme, the claimant gets much smaller compensation than the compensation claimant would get if the land or similar lands were acquired for the same public purpose under 1894 Act - It was held that the discrimination between persons whose lands were acquired for housing schemes and those whose lands were acquired for other public purposes could not be sustained on any principle of reasonable classification - SC denied acquisition because the State Govt. could resort to one of the two methods of acquisition , 1894 Act and 1961 Act and no guidance was given by the Legislature about the statute which should be resorted to in given case of acquisition for housing scheme - Power to choose could, therefore, be exercised arbitrarily - Under 1955 Act, there was no acquisition by the State Govt. of land needed for town-planning scheme - When the town Planning, Scheme came into operation the land needed by local authority vested by vitrue of s. 53(a) of 1955 Act and' that-vesting for purposes of the guarantee u/art. 31(2) of Constitution was deemed compulsory acquisition for a public purpose - To lands which were subject to the scheme, provisions of ss. 53 and 67 of 1955 Act would apply, and the compensation would be determined only in the, manner prescribed by the Act - There were therefore two separate provisions, one for acquisition by the State Govt., and the other in which the statutory vesting of land operates as acquisition for the purpose of town- planning by the local authority - State Govt. could acquire the land under 1894 Act, and the local authority only under 1955 Act - There was no option to the local authority to resort to one or the other of the alternative methods which result in acquisition - Contention that the provisions of ss. 53 and 67 of 1955 Act were invalid on the ground that they denied the equal protection of the laws or 'equality before the laws should therefore stood rejected - HC had apparently not considered the other arguments which were advanced at the Bar, and had observed that it was not necessary to consider those other contentions raised in the petition - As the petition was not heard by HC in respect of the other contentions which respondent No.1 might choose to raise, SC set aside the order passed by HC declaring s. 53 r/w. s. 67 of 1955 Act insofar as it authorised acquisition of land by the local authority under a town-planning scheme, as violative of Art. 31(2) of the Constitution, and the acquisition of respondent No.1's land under the City Wall Improvement Town Planning Scheme No. 5 as invalid - Appeal allowed.
