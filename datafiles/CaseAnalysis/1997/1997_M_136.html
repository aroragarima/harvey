Mangej Singh and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3176; (1998) 9 SCC 471; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1225; 1999 (1) CLR 249; 1999 (81) FLR 100; JT 1998 (8) SC 176; 1999 (3) SLR 14

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Selection - Denial - Justifiability - Whether order of Tribunal setting aside selection of appellants and directing fresh elections to be made, justified? - Held, Tribunal set aside selection test on two grounds: (1) Vacancies arising from 1979 to 1982 were clubbed together which caused prejudice to some candidates - No factual data on record to show how many vacancies arose in 1979 to 1982 and how many of selected candidates had completed three years of service in 1979 thus, respondents contentions not admissible - (2) Aim of examiners be to assess general suitability of Class IV employees offering themselves for promotion - A subsequent letter of Railway Board stated that it is not necessary to test working knowledge of English for promotions for employees in Hindi speaking areas - Respondents failed in examination and were not qualified for selection - Appeal allowed.
