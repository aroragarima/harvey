State Bank of India and Another v Raj Kumar

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 172; (2010) 11 SCC 661; 2010 (125) FLR 572; 2010 (2) LLJ 669; 2010 (158) PLR 703; 2010(3) SCALE 635

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1959, Compassionate Appointment Scheme, SBI Scheme

Summary: Service - Appointment on compassionate grounds - Applicability of old scheme for compassionate appointment vis-'-vis new scheme for ex-gratia payment - Respondent's father died while in service - Respondent's mother made applications for appointment on compassionate grounds - During pendency of application, compassionate appointment scheme was substituted by the "SBI Scheme for payment of ex-gratia Lumpsum Amount" - Appellant-bank advised the family of the deceased to make an application under the new scheme for ex-gratia payment - Feeling aggrieved, the respondent filed a writ petition - Single Judge of the HC directed the appellant-bank to reconsider the case of the respondent for appointment on compassionate grounds, holding that the old scheme applied and the new scheme was only prospective in operation - Said order was affirmed by the DB of HC - Hence, present appeal - Held, as compassionate appointment is a concession and not a right, the employer may wind up the scheme or modify the scheme at any time depending upon its policies, financial capacity and availability of posts claim for - Compassionate appointment is traceable only to the scheme framed by the employer for such employment and there is no right whatsoever outside such scheme - Further where the earlier scheme is abolished and the new scheme which replaces it specifically provides that all pending applications will be considered only in terms of the new scheme, then the new scheme alone will apply - In the present case, new scheme specifically provided that all pending applications will be considered under the new scheme - Therefore, new scheme alone will apply even in respect of pending applications - Respondent was not entitled to be considered for compassionate appointment - Respondent and/or his family may file a fresh application under the new scheme - Appeal partly allowed.
