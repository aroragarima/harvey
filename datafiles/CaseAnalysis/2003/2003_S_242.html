Supreme Court of India

26 March 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 285; (2003) 5 SCC 291; (2003) SCC (Cr) 1001; AIR 2003 SC 1311; 2003 (2) CCR 38; 2003 (2) Crimes 439; 2003 (4) CRJ 239; 2003 CRLJ 2021; 2003 (104) DLT 100; 2003 (1) JCC 585; 2003 (3) JhrCR(SC) 33; JT 2003 (3) SC 249; 2003 (2) RCR(Criminal) 789; 2003(3) SCALE 398; [2003] 3 S.C.R. 25; 2003 (2) SLT 862; 2003 (3) Supreme 397

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Miscellaneous; Petroleum & Gas; Socio-Economic

Summary: Explosive Substances Act - Ss. 3 and 4 - Terrorist and Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act - Ss. 3 and 5 - conviction under - appeal - Held, the prosecution has established its case against the appellant beyond any shadow of doubt and there is absolutely no ground which may warrant interference with the impugned judgment and order of the Designated Court.
