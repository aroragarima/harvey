Kalyan Chandra Sarkar v Rajesh Ranjan @ Pappu Yadav and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 24; (2005) 2 SCC 42; (2005) SCC (Cr) 489; AIR 2005 SC 921; 2005 CRLJ 944; 2005 (2) G.L.R. 921; JT 2005 (1) SC 482; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 703; 2005(1) SCALE 385

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Defence and Security Forces - Practice and Procedure - CrPC - HC granted bail after eight attempts - Appeal against order by which accused was granted bail - Existence of prima facie case against accused - Evidentiary value of retracted confession, whether considered by HC as well as by SC in previous proceedings or not? - Held, question of prima facie case and admissibility as well as evidentiary value of retracted confession having already been considered by HC and SC in previous proceedings same could not have been made as basis by HC in impugned order to grant bail without there being fresh material - HC was totally in error in allowing bail application - Appeal allowed.
