Nadir Khan v State (Delhi Administration)

Supreme Court of India

03 June 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 148; (1975) 2 SCC 406; (1975) SCC (Cr) 622; AIR 1976 SC 2205; 1976 CRLJ 1721; 1975 CrLR(SC) 434; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 489; 1975 UJ 549

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Excise - Punjab Excise Act, s. 61(a) - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 377, 386(c)(iii) and 401 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Illegal possession of ganja - Prosecuted under Punjab Excise Act - No right to appeal under act - Revision application not granted - Appealed u/s. 482 of CrPC, r/w art. 227 of Constitution - High Court taking suo moto action u/s. 377, CrPC, 1973 and enhanced sentence to six months - Above action of HC was challenged - Whether s. 377, CrPC, 1973 excludes rivisional jurisdiction of HC for enhancement of sentence in appropriate case? -Held, right to State u/s. 377, CrPC, 1973 to appeal against inadequacy of sentence does not exclude revisional jurisdiction of HC to act suo motu for enhancement of sentence in appropriate case has to be left to discretion of HC - Provisions u/s. 401 r/w s. 386 (c) (iii) CrPC, 1973 are clearly supplemental to those u/s. 377 whereby appeals are provided for and against inadequacy of sentence at instance of State Govt. or Central Govt. as case may be - Appeal dismissed.
