Dhayanand v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, M.K. Mukherjee

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1488; (1996) 7 SCC 47; (1996) SCC (L&S) 450; AIR 1996 SC 2877; 1996 (32) ATC 771; 1996 LabIC 917; 1995(5) SCALE 286B; 1996 (1) SLJ 136; 1995 (6) SLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Eligibility - Seniority

Summary: Service - Punjab Govt. National Emergency (Concession) Rules, 1965 - Union Territory of Chandigarh Employees Rules, 1966 - Whether the benefit of earlier military service could be given to the concerned employees appointed after 01-11-1966 in the services of the Union Territory of Chandigarh? - Held, the Punjab Recruitment of Ex-servicemen Rules, 1982 repealed the Punjab Govt. National Emergency (Concession) Rules, 1965 but the saving clause therein, preserved the rights which had accrued to any person under the repealed rules - All the employees, in these matters were appointed after 01-11-1966 but before the application of 1982 Rules - There is no controversy that if the 1965 Rules continued to be in force in the Union Territory after 01-11-1966 unless repudiated or repealed, the concerned employees in these matters, would be entitled to its benefit - Rules were framed by the President, called the Conditions of Service of Union Territory of Chandigarh Employees Rules, 1966 but these rules were not applicable to matters relating to probation, confirmation, seniority and promotion - Therefore, the Punjab Govt. National Emergency (Concession) Rules, 1965 continued to apply in the Union Territory of Chandigarh even after 01-11/1966 - Appeal dismissed.
