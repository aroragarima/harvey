State of Uttar Pradesh v Bahadur Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 214; (1983) 3 SCC 73; AIR 1983 SC 845; 1983 (9) ALR 447; [1983] 54 Comp Cas 275; 1983 (2) CompLJ 192; 1983 (35) CTR 398; 1983 ECR 1556; [1983] 142 ITR 745; [1983] 15 TAXMAN 12; 1983 UJ 424

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226, 227 - Condonation of delay - Whether HC was justified in dismissing writ petition u/arts. 226 and 227 of the Constitution filed by State on sole ground that petition was filed after long delay - Held, HC observed that usual period of limitation was 90 days for filing writ petition and held that petition was delayed by 42 days - No such period of limitation prescribed by any statute nor any such provision was brought to notice - HC was not justified in declining to examine case on merits - Order of HC set aside and writ petition remitted to HC - Appeal allowed.
