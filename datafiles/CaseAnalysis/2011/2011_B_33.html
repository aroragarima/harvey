State Of Orissa & Ors v Bhagyadhar Dash

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 416; (2011) 7 SCC 406; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 135; 2011 (88) ALR 250; 2011 (4) AWC 3932; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 6; 2012 (1) CTC 778; JT 2011 (7) SC 283; 2012 (1) MLJ(SC) 347; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 160; 2011(7) SCALE 71; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 967

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 11 - Arbitration Agreement - Dispute - Settlement - Validity - State aggrieved by orders of HC allowing applications filed u/s. 11 of the 1996 Act filed by respondents/contractors and appointing arbitrators to decide disputes raised by them against State - HC held that last sentence of proviso to cl 10 of conditions of contract (forming part of the agreements between State and contractors) was an Arbitration agreement - Appellants challenged said orders on ground that there was no Arbitration agreement and therefore applications u/s. 11 of the Act filed by contractors ought to have been dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether said clause was Arbitration agreement and essentials of an Arbitration agreement - Held, it did not refer to arbitration as mode of settlement of disputes - It did not provide for reference of disputes between parties to arbitration - It did not provide or refer to any procedure which would show that Superintending Engineer was to act judicially after considering submissions of both parties - It did not make decision of Superintending Engineer final on any dispute, other than claim for increase in rates for non-tendered item and it operates in a limited sphere, that was, where in regard to non-tendered additional work executed by contractor, if contractor was not satisfied with unilateral determination of rate therefore by Engineer-in-Charge rate for such work will be finally determined by Superintending Engineer - Provision was made with intention to avoid future disputes regarding rates for non-tendered item and not for reference of future disputes or settlement of future disputes - Hence, clause could by no stretch of imagination be considered to be Arbitration agreement - Further, said clause was deleted by State from Standard Conditions of Contract by official Memorandum - Contracts entered by State thereafter did not have said arbitration clause, though other Conditions of Contract remained same - Conditions of Contract forming part of these contracts did not contain arbitration clause - Proviso to cl. 10, which provides that decision of Superintending Engineer was 'final', merely discloses an intention to exclude rates for extra items decided by Superintending Engineer from scope of arbitration, as an excepted matter, when there was an arbitration agreement (cl. 23) in contract - Hence, when arbitration agreement was deleted, provision dealing with non-tendered items cannot be described as arbitration agreement - Appeals allowed.
