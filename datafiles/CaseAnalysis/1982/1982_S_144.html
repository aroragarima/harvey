Sukhnandan Saran Dinesh Kumar & Another Etc. Etc. v Union Of India & Another Etc. Etc

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1982

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1982 Indlaw SC 111; (1982) 2 SCC 150; AIR 1982 SC 902; 1982(1) SCALE 165; [1982] 3 S.C.R. 371; 1982 UJ 503

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Keywords: Khandsari Sugar, Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953, Sugarcane (Control) Order 1966

Summary: Agriculture & Agri. Products - Administrative - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(6) - Uttar Pradesh Sugarcane (Regulation of Supply and Purchase) Act, 1953 - Sugarcane (Control) Order 1966 - Power to fix minimum price of sugar - Rebate sought - Legality - Petitioners were producers of sugar by open pan process - Subsequently, Central Govt./respondents issued notification GSR 197 (E)/Ess. Com./Sugarcane dt. 20-3-1978, directing that where sugarcane was brought in bundles and was weighed as such a rebate in regard to binding material at 0.625 kg. per quintal be allowed - Hence, instant writ petitions - Whether restriction was reasonable and imposed in interest of general public - Held, impugned restriction was entirely reasonable - If price or minimum price of sugarcane was fixed producers of sugar would try to circumvent price by unrealistic and impermissible deductions - Further, rebate for weight of binding material seems to be a source for indulging in such nefarious, if not wholly fraudulent conduct - State could impose reasonable restrictions u/art. 19(6) of Constitution to prevent fraud or where advantage of a fraudulent conduct was sought to be taken - However, restriction was imposed in interest of cane growers and State while ensuring that sugar, a commodity of daily consumption by almost everyone in Country was available to everyone at a fair price - Further, grower of sugarcane another weaker section of society was not left to vagaries of trade or powerful sugar industry - To strike balance between conflicting interests not only State acquired power to fix minimum price of sugar and that such wholesome effort might not work to disadvantage of sugarcane growers section of society, power to prescribe rate of rebate was acquired - Thus, power to fix price or minimum price comprehends power to so regulate supply as to ensure price so fixed and to ensure that in name of unauthorised and unwarranted deduction price fixed or negotiated was not rendered illusory - Hence, restriction was both reasonable and was imposed in interest of general public and had a rational relation to object sought to be achieved by the Order - Petitions dismissed.
