Absar Alam @ Afsar Alam v State Of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 46; (2012) 2 SCC 728; AIR 2012 SC 968; 2012 CRLJ 1624; JT 2012 (2) SC 77; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 486; 2012 (2) RLW 1865; 2012(2) SCALE 350; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 890

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code 1860, ss. 302 and 201 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly killed his mother by cutting her neck and severing her head and thereafter fled from house with head of his mother leaving behind her body - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 201 of IPC - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal - HC upheld and confirmed death sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that offence was committed in fit of passion and not after pre-meditation - Whether HC was justified in confirming death sentence of appellant imposed by Trial Court - Held, mental condition of accused, which led to assault, could not be lost sight of and while such mental condition of accused would not be relevant to judge culpability, it was certainly a factor while considering question of sentence - Factual scenario gave impressions of impulsive act of accused and not of planned assaults and in this peculiar background, death sentence would not be proper - It was not one of rarest of rare cases where death sentence could be awarded - Death sentence of appellant u/s. 302 of IPC was converted into life imprisonment - Appeal partly allowed.
