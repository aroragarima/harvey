B. Shah v Presiding Officer Labour Court, Coimbatore and others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

Jaswant Singh, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 29; (1977) 4 SCC 384; (1977) SCC (L&S) 560; AIR 1978 SC 12; 1977 (35) FLR 414; 1977 LabIC 1847; 1978 (1) LLJ 29; 1977 (2) LLN 606; [1978] 1 S.C.R. 701; 1978 SLJ 183; 1977 UJ 699

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Women & Children

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Maternity Benefits Act, 1961, ss. 5(1), 2(1), 5(3), 2(2), 3(n), 4, 6, 6(4) and 6(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 226 and 42 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 33C(2) - Maternity Protection Convention (Revised), 1952, arts. 4 and 3 - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 - Maternity benefit - Computation of period - Whether respondent's claim of maternity benefit for entire period of twelve weeks including Sundays is valid? - Held, Maternity Protection Convention (Revised), 1952 adopted by general conference of International Labour Organisation - Term week has to be taken to signify a cycle of seven days including Sundays - Language in which sub-ss. (1) and (3) of s. 5 are couched also shows that Legislature intended that computation of maternity benefit is to be made for entire period of woman worker's actual absence - Computation of maternity benefit has to be made for all days including Sundays and rest days which maybe wage less holidays comprised in actual period of absence of woman intending up to six weeks preceding and excluding day of delivery as also for all days falling within six weeks immediately following day of delivery - Appeal dismissed.
