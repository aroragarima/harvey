Saju v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2774; (2001) SCC (Cr) 160; (2001) 1 SCC 378; AIR 2001 SC 175; 2000 (4) CCR 214; 2000 (4) Crimes 247; 2001 CRLJ 102; 2001 (1) JCC 10; JT 2000 (Supp2) SC 609; 2000 (4) RCR(Criminal) 747; 2000(7) SCALE 424; 2000 (3) SCJ 252; 2000 (8) SLT 177; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 621; 2000 (7) Supreme 529; 2001 (1) UC 257

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 10 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 302 and 120-B - Criminal conspiracy - Murder - Common Intention - (A) Whether accused and his accomplice had a common intention to kill deceased? - Held, to prove charge of criminal conspiracy prosecution required to establish that two or more persons had agreed to do or caused to be done, an illegal act or an act which is not legal, by illegal means - Immaterial whether illegal act is ultimate object of such crime or is merely incidental to that object - To attract s. 120-B it has to be proved that all accused had intention and they had agreed to commit crime - Very agreement, is ingredient of offence - Not necessary that all conspirators must know each and every detail of conspiracy as long as they are co-conspirators in main object of conspiracy - (B) Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 10 - Held act or action of one of accused cannot be used as evidence against another - Exception has been carved out u/s. 10 in case of conspiracy - To attract applicability of s. 10 court must have reasonable ground to believe that two or more persons had conspired together for committing an offence - It is only then that evidence of action or statement made by one of accused could be used as evidence against other - In absence of existence of circumstances suggesting hatching of criminal conspiracy, appellant could not have been convicted and sentenced with aid of s. 120-B or s. 109, IPC - (C) Abetment - Held, to prove charge of abetment, prosecution is required to prove that abettor had instigated for doing of a particular thing or engaged with one or more other person or persons in any conspiracy for doing of that thing or intentionally aided by an act of illegal omission, doing of that thing - Prosecution failed to prove existence of any of ingredients of s. 107, IPC - Prosecution did not prove charge of conspiracy against appellant beyond all reasonable doubt - Appellant is entitled to benefit of reasonable doubt - Appeal allowed
