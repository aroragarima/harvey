G. A. Galiakotwala and Company Private Limited v The State Of Madras

Supreme Court of India

22 March 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 118; (1976) 3 SCC 14; AIR 1976 SC 2084; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 717; 1976 (37) STC 536; 1976 TAXLR 1627; 1976 UJ 365

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 3(a), 3(b) and 8(5) - T.N. General Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 4 - (A) Inter-State sale - Appropriation of goods to contract - Determination of Place - (B) Exemption of declared goods - Applicability of G.O. No. 3602 - Held, no unconditional appropriation of goods at Bombay towards the contract entered and property passed only when the mills took delivery of the railway receipts - Exemption applies only in cases where assessee pays a tax u/s. 4 of 1959 Act in respect of local sales preceding inter-State transaction - Appeal Dismissed.
