Roger Shashoua and others v Mukesh Sharma and others

Supreme Court of India

04 July 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 487; AIR 2017 SC 3166; 2017(7) SCALE 260

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration Agreement, Arbitral Tribunal, Curial Law, Jurisdiction, Arbitration And Conciliation Act, 1996, Arbitration Clause, Conciliation, Absence, English Arbitration Act, 1950, ICC Rules, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 , ss. 2(2), 34,9 - Territorial Jurisdiction - Petition u/s.34 of the Act was filed before District Judge, UP but by order dt.6-7-2011, District Judge declined to entertain application on ground of lack of territorial jurisdiction and returned it to be filed before appropriate Court and on appeal, HC of Allahabad also dismissed it on ground of maintainability - Aggrieved, writ petition was filed challenging said orders of District Judge and while so, petition u/s.34 was also filed before HC of Delhi - Meanwhile, ITE filed SLP before SC wherein orders were passed to transfer writ petition filed before HC of Allahabad to HC of Delhi and to be heard by same Judge who is hearing petition u/s.34 of the Act - In pursuance of said orders of SC, Single Judge of HC of Delhi heard the matters and by impugned order held that application filed u/s.34 of the Act is maintainable and that HC of Delhi has territorial jurisdiction to deal with the same - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Part I of the Act is applicable to the instant matter.

Held, since what was stated in Shashoua case as regards the determination of seat/place on one hand and venue on the other was accepted by SC, the conclusion in said case could not be avoided by the parties. Further, agreement in question having been interpreted in a particular manner by English courts and the said interpretation having gained acceptation by SC, the courts in India have no jurisdiction. Hence, appeals are allowed and impugned judgment of HC of Delhi is set aside. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Stipulations in the agreement are required to be studiedly analysed and appropriately appreciated for the purpose of arriving at whether there is express or implied exclusion and further meaning of the term 'seat of arbitration'.
