Messrs Sitalpur Sugar Works Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

10 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 184; [1963] 49 ITR 160; [1964] 3 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(2)(vi) and 10(2)(xv) - (A) Whether expenses incurred on shifting of factory was revenue in nature or a capital expenditure? - Expenses were not incurred for purpose of carrying on concern but it was incurred in setting up concern with a greater advantage for trade - It was incurred towards effecting a permanent improvement in profit making machinery, that is, in capital assets - Held, expenditure in question was capital expenditure - (B) Whether depreciation on expenses so incurred is allowed? - No tangible asset had been acquired by expenditure - Expenses were incurred for acquiring an advantage for trade and could not be treated as an improvement in capital assets - Further held, depreciation on such expenses not allowable - Appeal dismissed.
