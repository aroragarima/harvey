State of Sikkim and others v Adup Tshering Bhutia and others

Supreme Court of India

18 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 95; (2014) 12 SCC 507; 2014 (141) FLR 976; JT 2014 (13) SC 357; 2014(2) SCALE 373; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 1003; 2015 (2) SLR 484

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces

Summary: Defence & Armed Forces - Sikkim Police Force (Recruitment, Promotion and Seniority) Rules, 2000 - Promotion - Maintainability - Respondent joined Sikkim Police as Constable and was absorbed in Sikkim Vigilance Police - Respondent was promoted as sub-inspector and was further promoted as Inspector - On account of retrospective promotion granted to members of Sikkim Police Force based on date of appointment/promotion as sub-inspector in case of other two services, respondent became junior to them, affecting his chances of promotion to post of Deputy Superintendent of Police - Respondent filed petition before HC - Petition was allowed by HC Direction by HC was to grant promotion with effect from date the first promotion was granted to any other private respondent with all consequential including monitory benefits - Hence instant appeal - Whether amended Rule on fixation of seniority satisfied the test of reasonableness -

Held, HC erred in holding that acquired or accrued rights of respondents were affected by the fixation of seniority at level of sub-inspector of Police - It was to be noted that, but for merger, neither respondents nor members of two other police forces, thats Sikkim Vigilance Police and Sikkim Armed Force, could have got any promotion at all to post of Deputy Superintendent of Police - Purpose of integration was to remove inequality and provided them with opportunity for promotion to post of Deputy Superintendent of Police - If length of continuous service in highest cadre of some similar services was taken as basis of fixing seniority and for further promotion to higher posts that would certainly result in deeper injustice to members of other services - It was hence State, after due deliberations and based also on report of expert Committee consisting of top level offices in State, took equitable decision to make post of sub-inspector of Police, where there was direct level entry in one of services, as determining factor for fixation of seniority - Respondents did not suffer any demotion in process but continued in post of inspector - Only thing that his compeers in Sikkim Police Force who could not get accelerated promotion to post of inspector, but who were admittedly senior to him if date of appointment to post of sub- inspector was taken, were given deemed date of promotion to post of inspector based on seniority at level of sub- inspector - Amended rule certainly had thus nexus to injustice sought to be removed so as to balance equity - It was neither irrational nor arbitrary - When Sikkim Vigilance Police was formed, respondent opted for that and he was absorbed in that Police wherein he got accelerated promotions to various posts of head constable, assistant sub-inspector, sub-inspector and Inspector - However, it appeared that such ground with regard to his original date of entry as police constable in 1974 was not taken anywhere - It was already provided therein that relative seniority of members recruited directly would be fixed based on date of induction to cadre - In other words, date of induction to cadre where there was direct recruitment was basis of fixation of seniority in instant case at the level of sub-inspector - Thus, amendment was merely clarificatory in nature and, therefore, it was deemed to exist from original date of commencement of the 2000 Rule - HC already protected promotions granted to private respondents but HC had struck down the Rule and quashed seniority list - HC missed crucial consideration with regard to principles set by State with regard to fixation of seniority, purpose sought to be achieved in the process, relevant considerations which lead to decision and materials included report of expert committee which were relied on by State in process of making and taking of decision - Retrospectivity given to private respondents by giving deemed date of promotion was neither arbitrary nor unreasonable - On contrary, it was perfectly just, fair and equitable in given circumstances without which integration of services would have resulted in graver inequality and injustice to members of major service. Hence, impugned judgment was set aside and held that benefits granted by HC in impugned Judgment to respondent should not be disturbed - Appeal allowed.
