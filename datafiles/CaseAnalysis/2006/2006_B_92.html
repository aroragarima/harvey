Sankar Deb Acharya and others v Biswanath Chakraborty and others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1474; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 192; (2007) 1 SCC 309; 2007 (1) CHN 54; JT 2006 (9) SC 370; 2006(10) SCALE 528; 2007 (1) SLR 75; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Government of West Bengal framed rules called West Bengal Services (Revision of Pay & Allowances) Rules, 1981, West Bengal Services (Appointment, Probation & Confirmation) Rules, 1979, West Bengal Services (Revision of Pay and Allowances) Rules, 1970, West Bengal Services (Training & Examination) Rules, 1953, West Bengal Service (Determination of Seniority) Rule, 1981

Summary: Service - West Bengal Services (Appointment, Probation & Confirmation) Rules, 1979, r. 5 - West Bengal Services (Revision of Pay & Allowances) Rules, 1981 - Direct recruits - Inter se Seniority - Determination - Appellants and respondents were direct recruits at entry scale 17 and respondents joined duty earlier than appellants' date of joining - Appellants, however, passed the departmental examination and were confirmed in service in terms of rules prior to the respondents - State Government subsequently issued its promotional policy under 1981 Rules on the basis of merit cum seniority from within the respective service and departmental cadres - State Government, by vide memo dated 12-10-1983, imposed an additional condition prescribing a minimum period of six years' service in scale 17 for promotion to scale 18 and a minimum combined period of 13 years in scale 17 and 18 before promotion to scale 19 - Respondents challenged the memo dated 12-10-1983 before the HC - Single Judge quashed the memo - State Government issued fresh promotion order dated 11-9-1991 and directed the compliance with the relevant service rules before giving effect to the promotion order - Appellants challenged the fresh promotion order dated 11-9-1991 before the HC - HC directed the State Government to determine the seniority of the respective employee from the date of their initial appointment - State Government, consequently, passed an order dated 28-3-2001 - Respondents challenged the order dated 28-3-2001 before the Tribunal - Tribunal remanded the matter back to the Government - Appellants challenged Tribunal's remand order before the HC - HC, by impugned order, set aside the order dated 28-3-2001 and directed the State Government to act in terms of order dated 11-9-1991 - Whether the respondents could be made seniors to the appellants solely on the ground that they had joined the service earlier to the appellants' date of joining - Held, respondents were appointed to the service after the examination conducted by the Public Service Commission and recommended by the Commission, however, the appointments were purely temporary and terminable at the discretion of the Government with one month's notice on either side or on payment of one month's pay in lieu thereof - Since respondents were appointed on temporary basis, the services of the respondents would fall within the mischief of r. 5 of the 1979 Rules where the requirement of passing of the departmental examination was essential before confirmation by a declaration by the appointing authority in that behalf - Further, promotion policy announced by the Government had clearly disclosed that the consideration was merit-cum-seniority - Service Rules also contemplated passing of departmental examination as a condition precedent for completion of probation and confirmation - In the scheme of Rules and policy of promotion, the consideration being merit cum seniority, the sole basis of judging merit was the passing of the departmental examination - Appellants, therefore, were entitled to be placed senior to the respondents since they had passed the departmental test in terms of the service rules prior to the respondents - Impugned order of HC, therefore, was not sustainable and hence set aside and order passed by the Government dated 28-3-2001 was restored - Appeals allowed.
