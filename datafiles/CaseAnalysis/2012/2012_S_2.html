MSR Leathers v S. Palaniappan And another

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 624; (2013) 1 SCC 177; 2013 (5) CompLJ 42; 2013 CRLJ 1112; 2012 (6) CTC 101; 2012 (193) DLT 90; 2012 (4) JCC (NI) 289; 2013 (3) MahLJ 597; 2013(1) M.P.H.T. 432; 2013 (2) MPLJ 542; [2012] 9 S.C.R. 165

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 142 - Dishonour of cheque - Multiple presentation of cheque during its validity period - Four cheques were issued by the respondent-company in favour of the appellant which were presented to the bank for collection - Cheques were dishonoured in terms of memo for insufficiency of funds - Notice was issued - Despite receipt of the notice by the respondent the payment was not arranged - Cheques were presented for the second time to the bank and were dishonoured for a second time in terms of a memo once again on the ground of insufficiency of funds - Despite receipt of the notice, no payment was arranged - Complaint Case was filed for the offence punishable u/s. 138 r/w s. 142 of the Act - Magistrate dismissed the applications for discharge - HC, by the order impugned, allowed the revision and quashed the orders passed by the Magistrate relying upon the decision of SC in Sadanandan Bhadran's case 1998 Indlaw SC 1179 according to which a complaint based on a second or successive dishonour of the cheque was not maintainable if no complaint based on an earlier dishonour, followed by the statutory notice issued on the basis thereof, had been filed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether an offence punishable u/s. 138 of the Act could be committed only once as held by SC in Sadanandan Bhadran's case 1998 Indlaw SC 1179 -

Held, neither s. 138 nor s. 142 of the Act or any other provision contained in the Act forbid the holder or payee of the cheque from presenting the cheque for encashment on any number of occasions within a period of six months of its issue or within the period of its validity, whichever was earlier - Also, simply because the prosecution for an offence u/s. 138 of the Act should on the language of s. 142 of the Act be instituted within one month from the date of the failure of the drawer to make the payment did not militate against the accrual of multiple causes of action to the holder of the cheque upon failure of the drawer to make the payment of the cheque amount - So long as the cheque remain unpaid it was the continuing obligation of the drawer to make good the same by either arranging the funds in the account on which the cheque was drawn or liquidating the liability otherwise - Omission or the failure of the holder to institute prosecution did not, therefore, give any immunity to the drawer so long as the cheque is dishonoured within its validity period and the conditions precedent for prosecution in terms of the proviso to s. 138 of the Act were satisfied - Hence, prosecution based on a second or successive default in payment of the cheque amount should not be impermissible simply because no prosecution based on the first default which was followed by a statutory notice and a failure to pay had not been launched - There was no real or qualitative difference between a case where default was committed and prosecution immediately launched and another where the prosecution was deferred till the cheque presented again get dishonoured for the second or successive time - Prosecution based upon second or successive dishonour of the cheque was also permissible so long as the same satisfied the requirements stipulated in the proviso to s. 138 of the Act - Decision in Sadanandan Bhadran's case 1998 Indlaw SC 1179 overruled - Reference answered.
