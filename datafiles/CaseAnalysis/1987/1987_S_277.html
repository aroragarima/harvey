Balbir Singh v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Natarajan, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28120; (1987) SCC (Cr) 193; (1987) 1 SCC 533; AIR 1987 SC 1053; 1987 (2) Crimes 874; 1987 CRLJ 853; 1987 CrLR(SC) 202; JT 1987 (1) SC 210; 1987(1) SCALE 127; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 1095; 1988 (1) ShimLC 126; 1987 (1) UJ 360

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Terrorist And Disruptive Activities (Prevention) Act, 1985, s. 4 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 156 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 3 - Appellant challenging his conviction under above Act for making inflammatory speech before demonstrators and inciting them to violence - Held, prosecution evidence lacked in credibility but also suffered from numerous infirmities - Investigation was not done before accused was charge-sheeted - There was evidence to show that without making any effective investigation police authorities have lightly launched a prosecution against appellant solely on basis of report given by constable and head constable - Prosecution case not proved beyond reasonable doubt and, therefore, conviction of accused set aside - Appeal allowed.
