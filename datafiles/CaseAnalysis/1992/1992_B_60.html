Commissioner Of Sales Tax, Bombay Etc.Etc. v Bharat Petroleum Corporation Ltd. Etc.Etc.

Supreme Court of India

18 February 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S.C. Agrawal, Veeraswami Ramaswami

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1227; (1992) 2 SCC 579; AIR 1992 SC 959; 1992 (41) ECR 1; 1995 (77) E.L.T. 790; JT 1992 (2) SC 603; 1992(1) SCALE 398; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 807; 1992 (85) STC 220

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 42 - Bombay Sales Tax Rules, 1959, r. 41 - Set-off, refund of tax paid by manufacturer on purchase of raw material - (A) Restriction that goods purchased should be used by dealer for manufacture of taxable goods for sale by him - (B) Subsidiary products turned out regularly in course of manufacture of main product - Effect - (C) Manufacturing process yielding simultaneously both taxable and non-taxable goods - Apportionment of relief of set-off, refund on basis of turnovers of taxable product and non-taxable product - Permissibility - Held, (A) Restriction cannot be read into r. 41 - (B) Manufacturer of main product can also be said to manufacture subsidiary product - (C) Not permissible as rule does not require that purchased raw material must be used only for manufacture of taxable goods for sale - Appeal dismissed.
