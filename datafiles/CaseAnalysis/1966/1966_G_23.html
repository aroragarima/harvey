Â Govind Dattatray Kelkar and Others v Chief Controller of Imports and Exports and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri, R.S. Bachawat, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 267; AIR 1967 SC 839; 1968 (16) FLR 88; 1967 (1) LLJ 691; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 29

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution

Keywords: Bombay Sales Act, 1953

Summary: Administrative - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, u/arts. 16, 309 - Direct recruitment - Appointment - Constitutional validity - Petitioners filed writ before SC - Whether appointment of respondent nos. 4 to 74 by direct recruitment as Assistant Controllers of Imports and Exports was constitutionally valid - Held, no rules u/art. 309 of Constitution were made in time and as appointments were made on ad hoc basis without consulting Union Public Service Commission - Therefore, lot of confusion was introduced in administrative set up with result that persons who acted as Assistant Controllers for a number of years had to be superseded by direct recruits - Hence, another formula could have been more equitably evolved was not for us to say, but fact remains that petitioners could not be blamed for coming to court for getting their rights settled one way or other - Petition dismissed.
