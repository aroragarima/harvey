Coal India Limited and others v Coal Consumers Association and others

Supreme Court of India

15 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 759; (2010) 10 SCC 395; JT 2010 (10) SC 120; 2010(9) SCALE 599; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 533

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Supply of coal - Suspension - Legality - 2nd petitioner/Company entered into Fuel Supply Agreement with respondent/Coal Consumer industries wherein respondents agreed not to resell the supplies in open market - CBI registered a case against 10 similarly situated industries for resale of coal supplies - 2nd petitioner suspended the supply to respondent/industries against whom no FIR had been lodged by the CBI - Respondent challenged 2nd petitioner's suspension order before the HC - HC stayed the suspension order - Hence the present petition - Whether 2nd petitioner ought to have vacated its order suspending the supply to respondents - Held, no FIR was lodged by the CBI alleging that the supplies of coal made to the respondent/industrial consumers had not been utilized in their respective industrial units - Respondents filed sufficient materials before the HC to show that the agencies of the State Government, namely, the District Industries Centres, had evaluated the genuine consumption and monitored the use of coal by the industrial consumers in their respective industries - Further, 2nd petitioner issued notices to respondent/industrial consumers to furnish documents in proof of the end-use of coal allotted to them for their respective industries for the financial year 2009-2010 and the respondents had furnished some materials pursuant to the said notices, however, instead of examining those materials relating to the end-use of coal in the industrial units of the industrial consumers furnished by the respondents, 2nd petitioner had suspended the supplies of coal to respondents soon after the CBI filed charge sheets against some other industrial consumers - Materials placed before the HC prima facie showed that the 2nd petitioner order suspending the supplies of coal to the respondent/industrial consumers was arbitrary and unfair and the HC was justified in staying the order as an interim measure - Petitions dismissed.
