Surjit Lal Chhabda v Commissioner Of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 192; (1976) 3 SCC 142; AIR 1976 SC 109; 1976 CTR 140; 1978 HLR 144; [1975] 101 ITR 776; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 164; 1976 TAXLR 108

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Practice and Procedure - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 2 (9), 3 and 55 - Whether income from the property which was earlier assessed as income of the assessee after being thrown into the common stock of joint family and abandoning of all rights and claims in the property by the assessee, be assessed as the income of HUF of which assessee was Karta? - Assessee appellant cannot constitute a coparcenary with his wife and unmarried daughter but under the Income tax Act a HUF, not a coparcenary, is a taxable unit - The appellant's wife became his sapinda on her marriage with him - The daughter too, on her birth, became a sapinda and until she leaves the family by marriage, the tie of sapindaship will bind her to the family of her birth - The appellant thus constituted a HUF with his wife and unmarried daughter - The appellant has no son - His wife and unmarried daughter, not being coparceners of the appellant, neither have a right by birth in the property nor the right to demand its partition nor indeed the right to restrain the appellant from alienating the property for any purpose whatsoever - Wife and unmarried daughter were entitled to be maintained by the appellant assessee - Held, since the personal law of the appellant regards him as the owner of property and the income there from as his income even after the property was thrown into the family hotchpot, the income would be chargeable to income tax as his individual income and not that of the family - Appeal dismissed.
