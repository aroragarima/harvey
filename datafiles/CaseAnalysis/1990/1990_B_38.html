Workmen and another v Bharat Fritz Werner Private Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, M.M. Punchhi, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 709; (1990) SCC (L&S) 523; (1990) 3 SCC 565; AIR 1990 SC 1054; 1990 (1) CLR 875; 1990 (60) FLR 482; JT 1990 (1) SC 305; 1990 LabIC 844; 1990 (2) LLJ 226; 1990(1) SCALE 268; 1990 (2) SLR 20; 1990 (1) UJ 344; 1990 (3) UPLBEC 1695

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11A, ss. ch 2, item 6 - Dismissal - (A) Compensation - For loss of future employment - Awarded around Rs. 1 lakh, considering wages and benefits like, bonus ex gratia payment for loss of employment for 52 months, gratuity etc - Adequacy - (B) High Court finding, acts of misconduct were not such as to warrant dismissal and denial of half of back wages for six years was adequate punishment - Jurisdiction - (C) Ordering of reinstatement - Propriety - Misconduct involving acts of threatening of highest executive with dire consequences, wrongfully confining him in his room and compelling him to withdraw certain notice - Held, (A) adequate - (B) HC could do so in view of powers conferred on Tribunal u/s. 11A - (C) Reinstatement not warranted as acts are subversive of discipline - Order accordingly.
