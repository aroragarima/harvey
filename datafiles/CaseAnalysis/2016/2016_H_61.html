Nashik Workers Union v Hindustan Aeronautics Limited

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 311; (2016) 6 SCC 224; AIR 2016 SC 1108; JT 2016 (3) SC 291; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 973; 2016 (3) SLR 772

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Establishment, Industrial, Agent, Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, Industrial Dispute, Labour Court, Appropriate Government, Engineering, Contract Labour (Regulation And Abolition) Act, 1970, Controlled, Labour, Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951, Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947- Maharashtra Recognition of Trade Unions and Prevention of Unfair Labour Practices Act, 1971 - Compliant filed by workers - Reference to State Govt. - Justification - Appellant workers Union filed complaint under 1971 Act for reinstatement of trainees with continuity of services and back wages - Order was passed in favor of appellant Union - Respondent contended that 'appropriate Govt.' in respect of dispute was Central Govt. and not State Govt., hence, 1971 Act would not apply - Lower Court held that State Govt. is appropriate Govt. for purpose of 1971 Act - It was contended by the appellant before the DB that the Central Govt. is the appropriate Govt. in relation to the employer company and not the State Govt. and to bolster the said submission reliance was placed on Steel Authority of India and others v. National Union Waterfront Workers and others 2001 Indlaw SC 20484 and also on Hindustan Aeronautics Limited & another v. Hindustan Aeronautical Canteen Kamgar Sangh & others (2007) 15 SCC 51, (HAL 2) 2002 Indlaw SC 2093 - DB relying on said decisions opined that the appropriate Govt. for the purpose of the 1947 Act is the Central Govt. which is the appropriate Govt. in relation to the company and, accordingly, the complaints filed by the Union against the company were not maintainable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the decision in HAL 2 2002 Indlaw SC 2093 can be regarded as a binding precedent.

Held, HAL 2 2002 Indlaw SC 2093 has not taken note of earlier decision in HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164. It has been clearly held in HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164 that regard being had to the dictionary clause of 1947 Act for the purpose of Hindustan Aeronautics Limited, it is the State Govt. which has to make the reference. In HAL 2 2002 Indlaw SC 2093 (supra) the Court has referred to decision in SAIL's case and opined that it is undisputed that Hindustan Aeronautics Limited is an undertaking of the Central Govt. and it is the Central Govt. which exercises full control over the same and, therefore, the appropriate Govt. is the Central Govt.. This analysis runs counter to HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164 (supra) and as well the ratio of the decision in SAIL's case. Thus, two aspects, first, the HAL 2 (supra) does not take note of HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164 and second, it proceeds on the basis of undisputed facts which are not stated. It is to be noted that there is nothing in the order in HAL 2 (supra) to suggest that Hindustan Aeronautics Limited is an agent of the Central Govt. Hence, as HAL 2 has not noticed HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164 which has been approved in SAIL's case, it cannot be considered as a binding precedent. Therefore, HAL 1 1975 Indlaw SC 164 still holds good and lays down the correct law and SC is bound by it as its foundation flows from Heavy Engineering Mazdoor Union 1969 Indlaw SC 343 which has been approved in SAIL 2001 Indlaw SC 20484 (supra) with some divergence as has been stated in Tata Memorial Hospital Workers Union 2010 Indlaw SC 697. HAL 2 cannot be regarded as a binding precedent. DB has not applied the ratio in SAIL's case correctly and, therefore, the entire anlaysis has to be held to be fallacious. The impugned order is set aside. Matter remitted to lower Court. Appeals allowed.
