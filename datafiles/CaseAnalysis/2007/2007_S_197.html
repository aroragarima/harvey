Chaturbhuj v Sita Bai

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1390; (2008) 2 SCC 316; AIR 2008 SC 530; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 860; 2008 (3) CGLJ 473; 2008 CRLJ 727; 2008 (2) G.L.R. 1159; JT 2008 (1) SC 78; 2008 (1) KLT 41; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 481; 2008 (149) PLR 263; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 136; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 163; 2007(13) SCALE 402; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 577

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Interim Maintenance, Indian Majority Act, Indian Majority Act, 1875

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482, 125 - Revisional court analysed the evidence and held that the appellant's monthly income was more than Rs.10, 000/- and the amount received as rent by the respondent-claimant was not sufficient to maintain herself - High Court noticed that conclusions have been arrived at on appreciation of evidence and, therefore, there is no scope for any interference - Appeal against - Held, whether deserted wife was unable to maintain herself, has to be decided on the basis of the material placed on record - Revisional Court and the High Court have analysed the evidence and held that the respondent wife was unable to maintain herself - Conclusions are essentially factual and they are not perverse - Appeal dismissed.
