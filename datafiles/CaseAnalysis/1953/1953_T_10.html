Â Dr. Ram Krishan Bhardwaj v State of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1953

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Patanjali Sastri, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati

Where Reported

1953 Indlaw SC 11; AIR 1953 SC 318; 1953 CRLJ 1241; 1953 SCJ 444; [1953] 4 S.C.R. 708

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) and 21 - Preventive Detention Act, 1950, s. 7 - Grounds of Preventive detention - Rrequisites of procedure established by law -(A)Whether s. 7 of Preventive Detention Act,1950 is violative of personal liberty of an individual ? - Held, s. 7 preventive detention is a serious invasion of personal liberty and such meagre safeguards as Constitution has provided against improper exercise of power must be jealously watched and enforced by court - (B) What are grounds for a detention to be held to be in accordance with procedure established by law within meaning of art. 21? - A petitioner has right, u/art. 22(5) of Constitution, to be furnished with particulars of grounds of his detention sufficient to enable him to make a representation which on being considered may give relief to him - This constitutional requirement must be satisfied with respect to each of grounds communicated to person detained, subject of course to a claim of privilege u/cl.(6) of art. 22 - Further held, Where detention has not been done in regard to one of grounds mentioned in statement of grounds, petitioner's detention cannot be held to be in accordance with Procedure established by law within meaning of art. 21 and he is, therefore,entitled to be released - Petition allowed.
