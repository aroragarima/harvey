M/S. Peekay Re-Rolling Mills (P) Ltd v The Assistant Commissioner & Anr

Supreme Court of India

20 March 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 708; (2007) 4 SCC 30; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 307; 2007 (219) E.L.T. 3; JT 2007 (4) SC 589; 2007(5) SCALE 96; 2007 (5) SCJ 754; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 185; 2009 (13) S.T.R. 305; [2007] 6 VST 541

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Liability To Tax, Declared Goods, Purchase Turnover, Point Of Levy, Sale In The State, Subsequent Sale, Bihar Finance Act, 1981, Jammu and Kashmir General Sales Tax Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963 - Section 14/15, Central Act - Steel re-rolling mills - Levy of tax on sale or purchase of goods - Goods made liable to tax under s. 5 of the State Act and exempted by a notification under s. 10 sought to be taxed under s. 5A in the hands of the purchaser - Whether the tax sought to be levied under s. 5A would amount to tax at a second stage and therefore violate s. 15, Central Act - Held, by virtue of s. 15, Central Act, declared goods once made liable to tax can not be made to suffer an additional tax liability - Exemption does not negate a levy of tax altogether - Despite an exemption, the liability to tax remains unaffected, only the subsequent requirement of payment of tax to fulfil the liability is done away with - Collection and levy are distinct and that collection is not an essential facet of levy - Liability to tax or taxability under s. 5 of the State Act remains unaffected by an exemption under s. 10 of the State Act - Respondent can not validly shift the burden of tax to the purchaser under s. 5A of the State Act for the same would violate the condition of single-stage tax under s. 15 of the Central Act.
