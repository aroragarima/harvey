Dr. Monica Kumar & Anr v State Of U. P. & Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Lokeshwar Singh Panta, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2637; (2008) 8 SCC 781; AIR 2008 SC 2781; 2008 (3) JCC 1662; JT 2008 (7) SC 194; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 785; 2008 (3) RCR(Criminal) 548; 2008(9) SCALE 166; [2008] 9 S.C.R. 943

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vindictive

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 347, 502, 506, 342, 352, 354 and 427 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 482, 156 - FIR - Quashing of - 3rd respondent/Warden of Girls Hostel in collusion with 2nd respondent/Chairman of the College Trust allegedly filed a frivolous criminal complaint against the appellant/students - Police registered the case against appellants u/ss. 452, 323, 504 and 506 IPC - Appellants filed a petition u/s. 482 of the CrPC before the HC to quash the FIR registered against them - HC dismissed appellants' petitions - Hence the present appeal - Whether the registration of criminal case against the appellants were the acts of victimization of appellants with mala fide intention - Held, a series of civil writ petitions and criminal proceedings besides contempt proceedings were initiated by the appellants in which allegations of mala fides, acts of victimization and physical and mental harassment were alleged against the 2nd respondent in his personal capacity and also as a Chairman of the College Trust - Appellants filed application u/s. 156(3) CrPC before the Chief Judicial Magistrate on the basis of which a case u/ss. 347, 502, 506, 342, 352, 354 and 427 IPC had been registered against the 2nd respondent - 2nd respondent challenged the order of the Magistrate but he could not succeed - Entire details of the facts of the present case indicated that the appellants during their study of MBBS Course had some problems with the 2nd respondent - Record would reveal that both the appellants being NRI candidates had undergone physical and mental agony and torture during their students' career in pursuing the MBBS course in 2nd respondent's College - Appellants had spent most of their precious time in litigation in the courts fighting for their genuine and legitimate claims - Entire backdrop of the peculiar facts of the case indicated that the present case is a fit case for exercising jurisdiction u/art. 142 of the Constitution of India - Therefore, impugned criminal charges framed by the Trial Court against the appellants are quashed and impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.
