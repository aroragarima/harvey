Laxman Naik v State Of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

22 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1365; (1994) 3 SCC 381; (1994) SCC (Cr) 656; AIR 1995 SC 1387; AIR 1995 SCW 2057; 1994 (2) CCR 299; 1994 (1) Crimes 806; 1995 CRLJ 2692; 1994 JCC 376; JT 1994 (2) SC 39; 1994(1) SCALE 692; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 94; 1994 (1) UJ 645

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 376 - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 354(3) - Appeal against conviction and sentence imposed - Conviction of accused based on circumstantial evidence - Death sentence imposed - Whether justified? - Circumstances relied upon in support of conviction must be fully established and chain of evidence furnished by those circumstances must be so complete as not to leave any reasonable ground for a conclusion consistent with innocence of accused - Accused committed rape of his brother's daughter aged 7, and then killed her - It was a cold-blooded and brutal crime - Case of accused fall under category of rarest of rare case - Capital punishment awarded - Appeal dismissed.
