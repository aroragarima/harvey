B. Venkatamuni v C.J. Ayodhya Ram Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 October 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 712; (2006) 13 SCC 449; AIR 2007 SC 311; 2007 (2) ALD(SC) 35; 2006 (65) ALR 902; 2007 (1) AWC 596; 2007 (1) CHN 29; JT 2006 (9) SC 415; 2007 (1) RCR(Civil) 277; 2007 (102) RD 102; 2006(11) SCALE 148; [2006] Supp7 S.C.R. 787

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Summary: [A.] Land & Property - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 295, 63 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 67, 68 - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Will - Execution - Held, DB was entirely wrong in proceeding on premise that compliance of legal formalities as regards proof of Will would sub-serve purpose and suspicious circumstances surrounding execution thereof is not of much significance - Appeal allowed.
