Textile Association (India) Bombay Unit v Balmohan Gopal Kurup and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, M.M. Punchhi, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 346; (1990) 4 SCC 700; AIR 1990 SC 2053; 1991 (17) ALR 541; 1991 (3) Bom.C.R. 639; JT 1991 (2) SC 434; 1991 (2) RCJ 718; 1991 (1) RCR(Rent) 49

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 13 - Deseased tenant - Widow and two sons - Suit for eviction on ground of bona fide requirement and default in payment of rent - One of sons (respondent) not impleaded - Ex parte decree in favour of landlord - (A) Whether decree obtained against mother and brother is binding against respondent? - (B) Whether decree can be kept alive against two other tenants? - (C) Whether possession could be given to respondent exclusively - (D) If decree to be set aside? - Held, (A) Is not binding against respondent - (B) Decree cannot be so kept alive - (C) Possession could not be given to respondent exclusively - (D) Decree set aside with direction to implead respondent as party to suit - Appeal partly allowed.
