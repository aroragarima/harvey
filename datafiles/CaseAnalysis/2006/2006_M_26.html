M.C. Mehta v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

27 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 859; (2007) 1 SCC 110; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 264; AIR 2007 SC 1087; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 266; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 683

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468, 471 - Prevention Of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 13(i)(d) - Water (Prevention And Control Of Pollution) Act, 1974 - Central Vigilance Commission Act, 2003, s. 8(1)(a) - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 142, 32 - Delhi Special Police Establishment Act, 1946, ss. 3, 4(1) - Issue of Taj Heritage Corridor Project - CBI inquiry was ordered - Whether Director, CBI, who has not given his own independent opinion, was right in referring matter for opinion to Attorney General of India, particularly when entire investigation and law officers' team was ad idem in its opinion on filing of charge-sheet and only on dissenting opinion of Director of Prosecution, whose opinion is also based on interpretation of legal evidence, which stage has not even arrived? - Held, there was no difference of opinion in matter of investigation between concerned officers of CBI and, therefore, there was no question of Director, CBI referring matter to Attorney General of India - Order accordingly.
