Delhi Development Authority v Bhola Nath Sharma (Dead) By L.Rs. And Others

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1063; (2011) 2 SCC 54; 2011 (1) ACR 1029; 2011 (1) ADJ 644; 2011 (1) AllCriC 605; 2011(2) ALL MR 482; 2011 (2) AWC 2126; 2010 (175) DLT 282; 2011 (1) JIC 596; JT 2010 (13) SC 324; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 255; 2011 (1) RCR(Civil) 820; 2011(2) SCALE 54; 2010(13) SCALE 214

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Delhi Development Act, 1957, s. 22 (1) - Land acquisition - Determination of compensation - Failure to give notice to 'interested persons' - State Government issued notification u/s. 4(1) of 1894 Act for acquiring respondents' lands for the planned development of the State - State Government, subsequently, issued declaration u/s. 6 of 1894 Act - Land Acquisition Collector (LAC) passed award - Respondents raised objections with regard to the quantum of compensation - Matter referred to the Reference Court - Reference Court enhanced compensation - Respondents filed appeal before the HC for further enhancement - HC allowed respondents' appeal and enhanced compensation - State Government, subsequently, asked the appellant/Authority to release compensation to respondents - Hence the present appeal - Appellant/Authority contended that the impugned judgment was liable to be set aside because the LAC and the Reference Court did not give opportunity to the appellant/Authority to participate in the proceedings held before them for determining the amount of compensation despite the fact that the land was acquired at its instance and was transferred to it u/s. 22(1) of the 1957 Act - Whether the appellant/Authority, at whose instance land of the respondents was acquired and who was asked to release compensation can be treated as 'person interested' within the meaning of s. 3(b) of the 1894 Act and it was entitled to an opportunity to participate in the proceedings held before the LAC and the Reference Court for determining compensation - Held, appellant/Authority is a 'local authority' u/s. 3 of the 1957 Act and hence 'person interested' u/s. 3(b) of the 1894 Act - S. 50(2) of 1894 Act represents statutory embodiment of one of the facets of the rules of natural justice - Object underlying s. 50(2) is to afford an opportunity to the local authority or company to participate in the proceedings held before the Collector or the Court for determining the amount of compensation and to show that claim made by the land owner for payment of compensation is legally untenable or unjustified - Such course is possible only if the Collector or the concerned Court gives notice to the local authority or the concerned company - If notice is not given, the local authority or the company cannot avail the opportunity envisaged in s. 50(2) to adduce evidence for the purpose of determining the amount of compensation - Therefore, even though the plain language of s. 50(2) of the 1894 Act does not, in terms, cast a duty on the Collector or the Court to issue notice to the local authority or the company to appear and adduce evidence, the said requirement has to be read as implicit in the provision, else the same will become illusory - Hence, appellant/Authority was entitled to participate in the proceedings held before the LAC and consequently, the award passed by the LAC and the Reference Court are nullity - HC also committed serious error by further enhancing the amount of compensation payable to the contesting respondents without requiring them to implead the appellant/Authority as party respondent so as to enable it to contest their prayer for grant of higher compensation - Impugned judgments of the HC and the Reference Court are set aside and matter remitted back to the Reference Court for deciding the references afresh after giving opportunity of hearing to the parties, which shall necessarily include opportunity to adduce evidence for the purpose of determining the amount of compensation - Appeal disposed of.
