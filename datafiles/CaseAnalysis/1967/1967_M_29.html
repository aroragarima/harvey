Maneklal Jinabhai Kot v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 January 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam, Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 135; AIR 1967 SC 1226; 1967 CRLJ 1092; 1967 (15) FLR 63; 1967 (1) LLJ 724; 1968 (1) MLJ(SC) 127; [1967] 2 S.C.R. 507

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: (A) Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948 - Guilt - Mens Rea - Factory inspector - Various Departments - Appellant was the manager of a mill - Inspector of Factories found on a visit to the factory concerned that certain workers actually working in the stamping department, at that time - Period of work for Group 11, Relay 11, was from 4 p.m. to 8 p.m. and from 8.30 p.m. to 1.00. a.m. - According to the Inspector, the workers concerned were doing work at 3 a.m., otherwise than in accordance with the notice of periods of work displayed in the factory and entries made in the register of adult workers and, therefore, there has been a contravention of the provisions of s. 63 of the Act, punishable u/s. 92 thereof - Inspector had filed a group of 4 complaints against the appellant - On receiving summons, the appellant, who was the Manager of the Mill concerned filed a complaint before the Magistrate, u/s. 92, read with s. 101 of the Act - Respondents 2 and 3 were impleaded as accused - Appellant manager had given instructions to the departmental heads to comply with the provisions of the Act - Appellant alleged that respondent nos. 2 and 3 were actual offenders and were responsible for allowing the concerned workmen to work at 3 a.m. in the stamping dept. of mills - Appellant prayed for an inquiry into his allegations and to hold respondents 2 and 3 guilty of the offence of violation of the provisions of s. 63 of the Act - Whether the appellant has established that he has used due diligence to enforce the execution of the Act - Held, the appellant had already stated that each department in the mill had department heads appointed by the Management and that they were required to comply with the provisions of the Act - Evidence of the appellant has not been, in any manner controverted by the prosecution - Factory Inspector has admitted, as P.W. 1, that there were several departments in this Mill and that heads were appointed to be in charge of each department - Respondent no. 2 who was a salesman was in charge of the Stamping Dept. which was part of Cloth Dept. - In the evidence it was not controverted, that the appellant had issued various circulars from time to time to the various heads of departments, insisting upon the strict compliance with the provisions of the Act - Factory Inspector pretended ignorance in the start that the appellant had issued the circulars but then accepted the same - Hence, the appellant has used due diligence to enforce the execution of the Act, in which case, cl. (a) of s. 101 was satisfied - Appeal allowed.

(B) Labour & Industrial Law - Factories Act, 1948 - Guilt - Mens Rea - Factory inspector - Various Departments - Whether the appellant had established that respondents 2 and 3 are the persons who committed the offence, in question, without his knowledge, consent or connivance - Held, both respondent nos. 2 and 3 had admitted their guilt and they have stated that the appellant was not informed by either of them about the proposed working, of the third shift on the morning - They had also stated that the working of the third shift was without the knowledge, consent or connivance of the appellant - Both of them have stated that the appellant had specifically warned them against double employment - Hence, the appellant had established that respondents 2 and 3 are the persons who committed the offence, in question, without his knowledge, consent or connivance - Appeal allowed.
