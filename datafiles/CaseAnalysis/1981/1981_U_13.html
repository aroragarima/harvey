Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta, V.D. Tulzapurkar, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Justiciability, Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951Constitution (Second Amendment) Act, 1952, Prevention of Blackmarketing and Maintenance of Supplies of Essential Commodities Act, 1980

Summary: Constitution, 1950 - Pre., arts. 13,13(2), 14, 19, 21, 22, 32, 123, 123 (2), 356, 357, 367, 367(2), 368(1), 368(2) - Constitution of India (as amended by forty fourth amendment) Act, 1978, ss. 1(2) and 3 - National Security Act, 1980, Pre. , ss. 3(1), 3(2), 3(3), 5, 8, 9, 10, 11, 11(2) 13, 16 - (A) Constitution - (1) Power to issue Ordinances - Character - (2) Word 'law' in art.21 , if includes an Ordinance - (3) President's satisfaction under Arts.123 and 356 - Justiciability - (4) Constitutional adjudication - Question of academic importance - (5) Power to issue Ordinance - Scope - (6) Power to issue Ordinance - Effect - (7) Preventive detention - Permisssibility under Constitution - (8) Whether internal contradiction between the provisions of art. 368(2) and those of s. 1(2) of the 44th Amendment Act - (9) Vires of s. 1(2) as to power of amendment conferred upon the Parliament by art. 368 (1) - (10) Amendment receiving assent of President - Notification, however, brining into force s. 3 not issued by Central Government - Mandamus, if can be issued - (11) Whether violative of art. 14, as it confers uncontrolled power on the Executive - (12) Central Government's failure to bring s. 3 into force - Malafide, if any -

(B) National Security Act - (1) Validity challenged - Grounds - (I) Vaugeness - (II) Constitutionality - (III) Conferring of unreasonable powers upon District Magistrate or Commissioner of Police - (2) Rule to be followed - (I) Intention in 'such place' - (II) Communication of grounds of detention - Period for -Normal rule - (IV) unwarranted protection to officers - (3) Maximum period of detention - Determination - (4) Provisions as to constitution of Advisory Board in conformity of unmended art. 22 (4) of Constitution - Validity - (5) Procedure to be followed by Advisory Board - Legality - Rights available to detenue - Extent of - (6) Detention of person after submission of its report by Advisory Board - Question as to - (7) Proceedings before Advisory Board - If required to be open to public - (8) Treatment to detenus - Held, (A) Legislative and not executive in character - Ordinance is 'law' within meaning of art. 21 - Observations in 1977 INDLAW SC 272, held no longer good law in view of the 44th Constitution Amendment - Need not to decided - Not confined to virgin land or fresh field only - And arts.14, 19, and 21 not reduced to dead letters - Not basically impermissible under the Constitution - No antithesis between art. 368(2) and s. 1(2) of Ammending Act - Held, s. 1(2) is not ultra vires - Remedy, is not the writ of mandamus - Not violative of art.14 on the ground - Not mala fide - (B) Provisions cannot be struck down on ground of vagueness and Act is not unconstitutional on the ground that by its nature it is generally violative of arts. 14, 19, 21 and 22 of Constitution moreover it is not invalid on ground of conferring of unreasonable powers also held not invalid on ground of unwarranted protection to officers - Normal rule to be followed , and the ground of detention must be communicated to the detenu without avoidable delay - Discretionary - Not bad - Advisory Board, is free to regulate its own procedure within the constraints of the Constitution and the statute - Cannot be decided by Board - Need not be open to public - Reasonable facilities should be provided and detenus under the Act must be kept separately - Order Accordingly.
