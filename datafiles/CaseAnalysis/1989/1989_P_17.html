Punjab National Bank v P.K. Mittal

Supreme Court of India

13 February 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 455; (1989) Supp2 SCC 175; (1990) SCC (L&S) 143; AIR 1989 SC 1083; 1990 (12) ATC 683; 1989 (2) BankCLR 29; 1989 (2) CompLJ 184; 1989 (58) FLR 449; JT 1989 (1) SC 264; 1989 LabIC 1248; 1989 (1) LLJ 368; 1989 (1) LLN 533; 1989(1) SCALE 353; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 612; 1989 (2) SLJ 1; 1989 (1) SLR 596; 1989 (1) UJ 495

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Trade

Keywords: Punjab National Bank (Officers) Service Regulations 1979

Summary: Banking & Finance - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, s. 19 - Regulations under (Punjab National Bank), regn. 20 - Permanent officer of bank - Sending letter to bank purporting to resign from future date - Resignation would become effective on either from that date or on expiry of three months from date of resignation - (A) Acceptance of resignation by bank from earlier date - Legality - (B) Withdrawal of resignation before date on which resignation would have become effective - Whether legal? - Held, (A) Illegal - (B) Legal despite absence of provision in regulations permitting such withdrawal - Appeal dismissed.
