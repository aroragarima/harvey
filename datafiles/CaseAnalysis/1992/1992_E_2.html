Cochin Shipping Co. and Others v E.S.I. Corporation

Supreme Court of India

19 August 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, S. Mohan, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 282; (1992) SCC (L&S) 85; (1992) 4 SCC 245; (1992) SCC (L&S) 986; AIR 1993 SC 252; 1992 (65) FLR 676; JT 1992 (4) SC 602; 1992 LabIC 2577; 1993 (1) LLJ 482; 1992 (2) LLN 737; 1992(2) SCALE 221; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 909; 1992 (5) SLR 536; 1992 (2) UJ 660

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Summary: Insurance - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948 - The appellant was served with notice enclosing certain notification, whereby the Act was extended to certain classes of establishments specified in the Schedule wherein 20 or more persons were employees or were employed, during that period - The appellant replied that it did not fall under the purview of the said notification and was therefore, not liable to comply with any of the provisions of the Act - The stand of the appellant was that the company did not come within the notification as the appellant was not a shop and was carrying on business of clearing and forwarding - Whether, appellant was coming within the purview of notification - Held, the appellant was carrying on stevedoring, clearing and forwarding operations - Clearing the documents, even it be in the custom house, was the carrier's job, so it could not be gainsaid that the appellant was rendering service to cater the needs of exporters and importers and others who want to carry the goods further, hence, it was a shop carrying on a systematic economic or a commercial activity and that would be enough to bring the appellant in the purview of notification without specifically enumerating the specific activities carried on by the appellant - The contention of the appellant was not sustainable - Appeals dismissed.
