Ashok Kumar and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

Case Analysis

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Amount Awarded, Amount Claimed, Highway, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Land Acquisition Act, 1894

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4 - Acquisition of land - Enhancement of compensation - Jurisdiction of Court - Land in question was acquired by respondent State by issuance of notification u/s. 4 of Act - Purpose of acquisition was residential and commercial - Further, it was stated that SC in many cases has based fixation of land value at Rs. 250/- per square yard - Appellant contended that adjoining villages for properties acquired for same purpose, SC having fixed land value at Rs. 250/- per square yard and above, hence, appellants may also be granted same value - Respondent contended that even according to appellants, their claim was only Rs.125/- per square yard - Further, land of appellants did not have same advantage when compared to other properties for which SC had fixed land value at Rs.250/- per square yard and above - Whether it is justified for Court to award compensation taking into facts and circumstances of each case.

Held, pre-amended provision put cap on maximum; compensation by court should not be beyond amount claimed. Further, amendment in 1984, on contrary, put cap on minimum; compensation cannot be less that what was awarded by Land Acquisition Collector. Hence, amount of compensation that court can award is no longer restricted to amount claimed by applicant and it is duty of Court to award just and fair compensation taking into consideration true market value and other relevant factors, irrespective of claim made by owner. SC held that with respect to appellant's property, properties do not touch national highway. Further, it is situated about 375 yards away from national highway and it appears that there is only narrow Road connecting properties of appellants to national highway. Hence, it will not be just and proper to award land value of Rs.250/- per square yard, which is granted to property in adjoining village. Hence, just and fair compensation in case of appellants would be Rs. 200/- per square yard. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - Court is justified to fix just and fair compensation for acquisition of land taking into consideration marker value, location of land and other relevant factors.
