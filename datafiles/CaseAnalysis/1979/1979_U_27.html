Organo Chemical Industries and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 July 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 24; (1979) 4 SCC 573; (1980) SCC (L&S) 92; AIR 1979 SC 1803; 1979 (39) FLR 309; 1979 LabIC 1261; 1979 (2) LLJ 416; 1979 (2) LLN 358; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 61

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Employees' Provident Funds and Miscellaneous Provisions Act, 1952, ss. 14 B, 5 and 1(3) - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 39, 41, 14, 20(2) and 32 - Whether s. 14 B of E.P.F. and M.P. Act is unconstitutional and if not what is semantic-juristic sweep of expression 'damages' used in Act? - Held, when power has to be exercised by one of highest officers, fact that no appeal has been provided for 'is a matter of no moment' - There is always a presumption that public officials would discharge their duties honestly and in accordance with rules of law - Power being vested not in any minor official but in top-ranking authority - In circumstances, absence of a provision for appeal or revision can be of no consequence - Expression 'damages' occurring in s. 14 B is, in substance, a penalty imposed on employer for breach of statutory obligation and also to provide reparation for amount of loss suffered by employees - Word 'damages' in s. 14 B is related to word 'default' - Words used in s. 14 B are default in payment of contribution - Word 'default' in s. 14 B must mean 'failure in performance' or 'failure to act' - Petition dismissed.
