Union of India and Others v M. Bhaskaran

Supreme Court of India

30 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 2143; (1995) Supp4 SCC 100; (1996) SCC (L&S) 162; AIR 1996 SC 686; 1996 (32) ATC 94; 1996 LabIC 581; 1996 (1) LLJ 781; 1995(6) SCALE 214; 1995 (5) SLR 796; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 526; 1996 (1) UJ 600

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Railways Act, 1890, s.71E; Railway Services (Conduct) Rules, 1966, r.3(1); Evidence Act, 1872, s.115; Constitution of India, art.311 - Service - Removal of - Misconduct - Bogus and forged casual labourer service cards - Employment snatched by workman - Order passed after departmental proceedings and hearing workmen - Held, liable to be recalled and was voidable at option of employer - The employee continued in service for number of years on basis of such appointment - Cannot create equity in their favour nor estoppel against employer.
