Rita Markandey v Surjit Singh Arora

Supreme Court of India

27 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2512; (1996) 6 SCC 14; 1996 (7) AD(SC) 868; AIR 1997 SC 2174; 1997 CRLJ 2503; JT 1996 (8) SC 590; 1996 (2) RCR(Rent) 472; 1996(7) SCALE 275; [1996] Supp7 S.C.R. 56; 1996 (7) Supreme 233; 1996 (2) UJ 787

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, ss. 2(b), 2(C) and 12 - Tenancy - Eviction - Vacation of premises - False affidavits by contemner in Supreme Court - (A) Civil Contempt - Supreme Court ordered extension of time - On agreement rather than representation by tenant - Breach of order, if makes tenant liable for contempt - (B) Criminal contempt - False affidavits given before Supreme Court from time to time that he had already vacated premises though he had not done so - (C) Punishment - When on non-bailable warrant being issued, he was in custody for some days and possession handed over by him to landlord - Held, tenant cannot be held liable for contempt - Guilty of criminal contempt of Court as by filing affidavits he had not only deliberately attempted to impede administration of justice but also succeeded in his attempt in delaying delivery of possession - Ordered to pay fine of Rs. 2000 though not imprisoned so as to deter others from filing false affidavits - Rule is made absolute.
