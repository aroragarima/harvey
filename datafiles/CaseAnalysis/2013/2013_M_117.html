Esha Ekta Apartments Co-Operative Housing Society Limited And Others v Municipal Corporation Of Mumbai And Others

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Sudhansu Jyoti Mukhopadhaya

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 119; (2013) 5 SCC 357; AIR 2013 SC 1861; 2013(2) ALL MR 901; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 49; JT 2013 (3) SC 336; 2013 (5) MahLJ 30; 2013(3) SCALE 63; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 478

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulations of the Promotion of Construction, etc.) Rules, 1964, Development Control Regulations, 1991

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulation of Construction, Management and Transfer) Act, 1963, ss. 3(1), 4(1), 7 and 13 - Registration Act, 1908 - Maharashtra Ownership Flats (Regulations of the Promotion of Construction, etc.) Rules, 1964, r. 3 - Illegal construction - Regularization - Sustainability - Appellant's right was to be heard in petition filed by the builder who had raised the building in violation of the sanctioned plan - Whether the orders passed by Deputy Chief Engineer, Building Proposals (City) of Municipal Corporation and the Appellate Authority refusing to regularize the illegal constructions made on Plot by appellant were legally sustainable - Held, s. 3 of 1963 Act specified general liabilities of the promoter - S. 3(1) of 1963 Act thereof contained non-obstante cl. and declared that notwithstanding anything in any other law, promoter who intended to construct or constructs a block or building of flats, all or some of which were to be taken or taken on ownership basis, should in all transactions with persons intending to take or taking one or more of such flats, would be liable to give or produce, or cause to be given or produced, the information and the documents mentioned in the section - S. 4(1) of 1963 Act also contained non-obstante clause and laid down that promoter who intended to construct or constructed block or building of flats should before accepting any money as advance payment or deposit, which should not be more than 20 percent of the sale price, entered into written agreement for sale with the buyer - S. 4(1A) of 1963 Act specified the particulars to be included in such agreement and the documents which should form part of it - S. 4(2) of 1963 Act casted duty on the promoter to get the agreement registered in accordance with the provisions of 1908 Act - S. 7 of 1963 Act contained prohibition against alterations or additions in the plans and specification without the consent of the persons who had agreed to take the flats - Promoter was also required to rectify the defects noticed within three years - S. 7(2) of 1963 Act casted duty on the promoter to construct and complete the building in accordance with the plans and specifications - S. 13 of 1963 Act postulated punishment to any promoter who was found guilty of violating the provisions - R. 3 of Rules laid down the manner of making disclosure by the promoter to the flat buyers - R. 5 of Rules specified the particulars to be incorporated in the agreement required to be entered into between the promoter and the flat purchaser - Form V appended to Rules contained the model form of agreement to be entered into between promoter and flat purchaser - Jayantilal Investments v. Madhuvihar Cooperative Housing Society, 2007 Indlaw SC 20, relied on - Appeals dismissed.
