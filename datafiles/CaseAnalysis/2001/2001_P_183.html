Agra District Co-Operative Bank Limited v Prescribed Authority, Labour Court, U. P. and

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Shivaraj V. Patil

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20495; AIR 2001 SC 2396; 2001 (2) AWC 1433; 2001 (2) CLR 5; 2001 (89) FLR 609; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 421; JT 2001 (4) SC 381; 2001 LabIC 2374; 2001 (1) LLJ 1404; 2001 (8) SLR 261; 2001 (5) SLT 133; 2001 (4) Supreme 388; 2001 (2) UPLBEC 1333

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Co-operative Societies Act, s. 70 - U. P. Co-operative Societies Employees Service Regulations, 1975 - Selection - Issuance of appointment Letter - Cancellation - Challenge - Respondent Nos. 2, 3 were selected by Selection Committee constituted in terms of Regulations as clerks/cashiers - Appointment letters were issued to them and they also reported to duty - Thereafter, by resolution made said recruitment was cancelled - Respondent Nos. 2, 3 filed petition before HC challenging action of Managing Committee/appellant - HC declined to interfere with action and asked parties to work out their respective alternative remedies -Thus, dispute was raised before Labour Court and Labour Court declined to interfere on ground that HC had already decided matter - Again respondent Nos. 2, 3 approached HC for appropriate reliefs and HC set aside Award made by Labour Court and remitted matter to Labour Court for fresh consideration on merits of matter - Labour Court concluded that termination of services of respondent Nos. 2, 3 was invalid and directed their reinstatement - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether termination of services of respondent Nos. 2, 3 was invalid - Held, when question of employment arises, certainly it could not be said that doors of Labour Court were shut - However, it was possible that in certain cases s. 70 of Act could be attracted - If parties avail of remedy in one of jurisdictions, that proceeding should be pursued to its logical end and should not be given up in middle and start another proceeding under another enactment - Further, appointments had been made early though services were terminated parties concerned invoked jurisdiction of Labour Court and Labour Court directed their reinstatement and they were reinstated from date of termination itself - Thus, they should be deemed to be in service for more than 20 years now and to disturb their services at that stage would be harsh and inhuman - Appeal dismissed.
