Dr. Rajesh Talwar and another v Central Bureau of Investigation and another

Supreme Court of India

08 October 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 666; (2014) 1 SCC 628; 2013 (4) JCC 2734; 2013 (4) MLJ(Crl) 362; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 687; 2013(12) SCALE 464; [2013] 10 S.C.R. 504

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 91, 233, 311 and 482 - Rejection of application - Validity - Petitioners were being tried for charges of committing the murder of their daughter and their domestic helper in their house - At the initial stage, the investigation was conducted by the UP Police, however, it was later transferred to the Central Bureau of Investigation (CBI) - Closure report was submitted before the Magistrate who disagreed with it and issued the process to the petitioners for the charge of committing the double murder - Evidence of the prosecution was closed and the statements of the accused were being recorded - At this stage, application u/s. 311 of CrPC for examining 7 other left over witnesses was filed alongwith another application u/s. 233 of CrPC r/w s. 91 of CrPC, in respect of the reports of certain tests conducted on 3 persons who at one time were suspected accused and had been in police custody - Applications were disposed of by the Trial Court allowing them partly - Thereafter, petition was filed u/s. 482 of CrPC, which was rejected by HC on the ground that the application was vexatious and intended to only delay the proceedings as was also found by the Trial Court - Hence, instant petition - Whether HC rightly rejected the application filed by the petitioners - Held, initial applications were made at a stage where the prosecution evidence had been concluded and the defence had entered and almost concluded its evidence - Petitioners had, without raising any objection that the reports and documents allegedly proved by the witnesses had not been supplied to them or made part of the Court record, participated in the examination and cross-examination of two witnesses - Hence, it was evident that petitioners had been adopting dilatory tactics - Instant petition was filed after two months of passing of impugned order - No interference required in the impugned order - Petition dismissed.
