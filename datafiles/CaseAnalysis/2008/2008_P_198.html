Vishwanath Dadoba Karale v Prisa Shantappa Upadhye (D) Through LRs.

Supreme Court of India

13 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2414; (2008) 11 SCC 504; AIR 2008 SC 2510; 2008 (71) ALR 477; 2008 (3) AWC 2221; 2008 (3) Bom.C.R. 279; JT 2008 (4) SC 506; 2008 (4) MLJ(SC) 461; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 998; 2008 (150) PLR 532; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 47; 2008 (105) RD 68; 2008(4) SCALE 652; [2008] 4 S.C.R. 993; 2008 (2) UC 881

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Mortgage By Conditional Sale

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(c) - Mortgage by conditional sale - Predecessor in interest of the respondent executed a conditional sale deed of impugned property for five years with a stipulation to re-purchase the same by paying back same amount at any time within the said period of five years - Subsequently, when respondent offered said amount to the appellant, he did not accept it stating that he had acquired an absolute title thereto - Respondent filed a suit for redemption of mortgage which was dismissed by trial court as well as by first appellate court - On appeal, HC decreed suit holding that the transaction constituted a conditional mortgage - Hence, present appeal - Whether the transaction in question contemplated conditional sale with an option to purchase or it was a conditional mortgage? - Held, when an absolute transfer of property is made, it cannot be limited to a period - Impugned transaction shows that the appellant was to have title in the property for a period of five years - Such a transaction, cannot be construed to be a transaction of sale - It was a mortgage as has rightly been held by the HC - Appeal dismissed.
