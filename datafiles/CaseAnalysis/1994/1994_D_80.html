Dolat Ram v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1812; (1995) SCC (Cr) 237; (1995) 1 SCC 349; 1995 (32) ACC 149; AIR 1995 SCW 3129; 1994 (4) CCR 851; 1994 (3) Crimes 1013; 1995 CRLJ 3648; JT 1995 (1) SC 127; 1994(4) SCALE 1119; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Dowry death - Sessions judge granted anticipatory bail to the in-laws but on appeal by State the bail order was canceled by High Court - Whether HC correct in cancelling the bail? - Bail once granted should not be canceled in a mechanical manner without considering whether any supervening circumstances have rendered it no longer conducive to a fair trial to allow the accused to retain his freedom by enjoying the concession of bail during the trial - These principles, it appears were lost sight of by the High Court when it decided to cancel the bail, already granted - Held, HC erred in cancelling the bail order - Bail can be cancelled only if there is possibility of accused absconding or abuse of liberty - HC order set aside - Appeal allowed.
