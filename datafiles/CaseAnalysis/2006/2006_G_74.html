Gurpreet Singh Bhullar & Anr v Union Of India & Ors

Supreme Court of India

08 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, AR. Lakshmanan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1513; (2006) 3 SCC 758; AIR 2006 SC 1484; 2006 (5) AWC 5171; JT 2006 (3) SC 444; 2006 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1043; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 23; 2006(3) SCALE 134; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 1110; 2006 (2) SLR 808; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1220

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations, 1955

Summary: Service - Indian Police Service (Appointment by Promotion) Regulations 1955, regns. 5(5) and 7(3) - Criminal case was registered against the respondent Nos. 5, Superintendent of Police and chargesheet was filed - Meanwhile, a Notification was issued by the Government of India in which the name of respondent Nos. 5 was included in the select list of 1999 and 2000 with a condition that the name of respondent Nos. 5 was included in the list provisionally, subject to his clearance in criminal case pending against him - Notification was challenged before Central Administrative Tribunal - Central Administrative Tribunal directed that respondent Nos. 5 be given benefit of being placed in the select panel of the year 1999-2000 without taking consideration of the pendency of the criminal case - It was further directed that the promotion of respondent Nos. 5 to IPS should remain subject to the outcome of the result of criminal case pending against him - Writ Petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - HC held that criminal proceedings could only be held to be pending against the Officer if the charge was framed by the trial court, but in the instant case, the charge was not framed - Hence, the instant special leave petition - Whether order passed by HC was correct? - Held, Explanation 1 to Regulation 5(5) and proviso to regn. 7(3), stated about chargesheet being filed against an officer in a court of law - Explanation 1 make it clear that the proceedings should be treated as pending only if a chargesheet was actually been issued to the officer or filed in a Court - The Explanation nowhere stated about charges having been framed by the Trial Court - There was no concept of charges being framed under the Regulation - HC committed the fundamental error by misinterpreting the explanation 1 to regns. 5(5) and 7(3) of the Regulations - Impugned order of the HC not legally sustainable and accordingly, quashed - Appeal allowed.
