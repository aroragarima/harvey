Mayawati v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

06 July 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Dipak Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 180; (2012) 8 SCC 106; AIR 2012 SC 3765; 2012 CRLJ 4282; 2012 (4) CTC 322; 2012 (3) JCC 1955; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 622; 2012(6) SCALE 239; [2012] 7 S.C.R. 33

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Intervene

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, s. 13 (2) r/w. s. 13 (1)(e) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 - Quashing of FIR - SC in writ petition No. 13381 of 1984 directed the CBI to conduct an inquiry on the basis of an I.A. filed in the said writ petition alleging various irregularities committed by the officers/persons in the Taj Heritage Corridor Project and to submit a Preliminary Report - SC issued certain directions to the CBI to interrogate and verify the assets of the persons concerned with regard to outflow of Rs. 17 crores which was alleged to have been released without proper sanction for the said Project - Pursuant to said orders of SC, an FIR was lodged on 5-10-2003 u/s. 120-B r/w. ss. 420, 467, 468 and 471 of IPC and u/s. 13(2) r/w. s. 13(1)(d) of the Act against several persons including the petitioner (Than the Chief Minister of State) - Petitioner challenged said FIR submitting that SC directed the CBI to conduct an inquiry pertaining to the assets of the officers/individuals concerned in the said Project in order to ascertain whether any mis-appropriation of funds have been done with regard to outflow of Rs. 17 crores released for the construction of said Project - Petitioner further contended that contrary to the order of SC, the CBI registered another FIR on the same date i.e. 5-10-2003 only against the petitioner alleging that in pursuance of the SC order, they conducted an inquiry with regard to the acquiring of disproportionate movable and immovable assets by the petitioner and her close relatives, however, there was no direction or observation by SC to inquire into the assets of the petitioner not related to the said project case - Whether FIR dt. 5-10-2003 lodged u/s. 13(2) r/w. s. 13 (1) (e) of the Act against the petitioner to investigate into the matter of alleged disproportionate assets was beyond the scope of the directions passed by SC in the order dt. 18-9-2003 in W.P. (C) No. 13381 of 1984 - Held, direction of SC in writ petition No. 13381 of 1984 was confined to find out as to who cleared the project of Taj Corridor and for what purpose it was cleared and whether there was any illegality or irregularity committed by officers and other persons concerned in the State - There was no such finding or satisfaction recorded by SC in the matter of disproportionate assets of the petitioner and, in fact, the petitioner was not a party before SC in the case in question - From the perusal of said order, SC was also satisfied that there could not have been any material before SC about the disproportionate assets case of the petitioner beyond the Taj Corridor Project case and there was no such question or issue about disproportionate assets of the petitioner - In view of the same, giving any direction to lodge FIR relating to disproportionate assets case did not arise - Since the order dt. 18-9-2003 does not contain any specific direction regarding lodging of FIR in the matter of disproportionate assets case against the petitioner, CBI was not justified in proceeding with the FIR dt. 5-10-2003 - CBI had exceeded its jurisdiction in lodging FIR dt. 5-10-2003 in the absence of any direction from SC in the order dt. 18-9-2003 or in any subsequent orders - The impugned FIR was without jurisdiction and any investigation pursuant thereto was illegal and liable to be quashed, accordingly quashed - Petition allowed.
