Union Of India And Others v Ex-Constable Amrik Singh

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, S. Ratnavel Pandian

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 484; (1991) SCC (L&S) 579; (1991) 1 SCC 654; AIR 1991 SC 564; 1991 (16) ATC 497; 1991 (1) CLR 993; 1991 CRLJ 664; 1991 CrLR(SC) 394; 1993 (66) FLR 961; JT 1991 (1) SC 282; 1991(1) SCALE 91; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 182; 1992 (3) SLJ 10; 1991 (2) SLR 103; 1991 (1) UJ 684; 1991 (1) UPLBEC 325

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces

Keywords: Border Security Force Act, 1968.

Summary: Criminal - Defence & Security Forces - Constitution - Border Security Force Act, 1968, s. 117(2) -Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Petition under, against sentence by convict - Principles of natural justice - Giving of personal hearing to convict, while disposing of a petition u/s. 117(2) of 1968 Act by member of Border Security Force against sentence - Whether necessary? - Held, not necessary - Disposal of such a petition does not attract principles of natural justice - Appeal allowed.
