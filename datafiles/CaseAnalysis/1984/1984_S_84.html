J. Mohapatra And Co And Another v State Of Orissa And Another

Supreme Court of India

10 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Madon, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 217; (1984) 4 SCC 103; AIR 1984 SC 1572; 1985 (1) CCC 141; 1984(2) SCALE 191; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 322; 1984 UJ 1136

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Education - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 54, 136 and 226 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Selection of books for Libraries by State govt., on recommendation of Committees - Selection of some books submitted by publisher - Objection as to constitution of Committee - (A) Who can apply, u/art. 226 - In the light of ancillary issues that (1) Whether publisher waived the objection, by submitting books - (2) Person not submitting any book for selection, if aggrieved - (B) Bias, if - Author, a member of the Committee submitting books is interested in matter - (A) Held, publisher had not waived the objection merely by submitting books for selection - Was a person aggrieved - (B) Held, such person should not be member of committees - Appeal allowed.
