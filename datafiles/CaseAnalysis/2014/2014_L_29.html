Liyakat and another v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, A.M. Sapre

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 663; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1256; JT 2014 (10) SC 584; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3860; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 365; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 676; 2014(11) SCALE 541

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302, 304B, 34, 498A - Dowry death - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused persons/appellants committed offence punishable u/ss. 201, 302, 304B, 498A r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC partly allowed the appeal and remanded matter to Trial Court for further trial - Hence instant appeal.

Held, HC fell in error in coming to above conclusion. It is an admitted fact that accused persons immediately after alleged suicide did not give any report to police about her unnatural death. There is no denial to this fact and accused are fully aware about fact that they have not reported matter to police. From bare perusal of the statement recorded it is evident that Court elaborately put questions to accused and same have been answered in detail. The entire incident has been fully apprised to accused including that accused was confronted with Exb. 14,15,16 and 17 to effect that accused, who was absconding, was finally arrested. In answer, accused said 'not aware'. Same answer was given by another accused 'K'. Court apprised accused persons in very elaborate manner about incident that took place, sequence of events and material on evidence brought on record. Accused persons were fully aware about all these evidences. Appellants did not raise question before Trial Court that any prejudice has been caused to them in examination. The burden is on accused to establish that by not appraising all incriminating evidences and inculpatory material that had come in prosecution evidence against them, prejudice has been caused resulting in miscarriage of justice. In the instant case, no prejudice or miscarriage of justice has been done to appellants. HC has erred in law in setting aside Trial Court judgment and remanding matter back for retrial and afresh decision. It is fit case where HC should decide appeal on merit. Order passed by HC was set aside and matter is remanded back to HC to decide appeal on merit. Appeal disposed of.
