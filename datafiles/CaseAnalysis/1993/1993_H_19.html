Employees' State Insurance Corporation v Harrison Malayalam Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

P.B. Sawant, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 598; (1993) 4 SCC 361; (1994) SCC (L&S) 1; AIR 1993 SC 2655; 1994 (1) CLR 15; JT 1993 (5) SC 96; 1994 (1) LLJ 12

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Employees State Insurance (Central) Rules, 1950

Summary: Contract and Commercial - Specific Relief Act, 1963 - Contract of Repurchase - Sale of land - Two sale deeds - Vendees executing two Agreements of repurchase - Within stipulated time - Vendors executing Agreements of sale in favour of , appellant, an outsider - And handed over Agreements of repurchase to him - One of previous vendees died - His legal representatives (LRs), including other previous vendees , refused to execute the re-conveyance deed - Appellant filed two suits for specific performance of agreement of re-conveyance, delivery of possession and mesne profits - Against vendors and LRs - Depositing some amount in court - Willing to perform - Ex parte decrees - Final - Application by LRs - Decrees set aside - LRs sue - Trial court decreed against - Appealed in HC- Allowed, and both suits against them dismissed - Appeal against - Facts admitted - No controversy as regards the execution of document - Per HC, rights of reconveyance was not assigned - . Stipulate time for repurchase differ among two agreements of sale - Important - To see if Plaintiff can stand in the shoes of vendors to enforce the agreement entered into between them and previous vendees - Curtailment of time it was reasonable - No privity was intended - Plaintiff not 'representative-in-interest' u/s. 15(b) of the Act - Cannot sue for specific Performance - On deeds of re-conveyance - No question of assignment of any right, can arise - Held, on construing in instant case held that right to repurchase was not personal and was assignable - Assignee was entitled to enforce the contract by filing suit for specific performance - Assignee fall within meaning of representative-in-interest as contemplated u/cI. (b) of s. 15 - Appeal allowed.
