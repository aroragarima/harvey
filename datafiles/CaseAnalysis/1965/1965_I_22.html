Syndicate Bank, Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 13; AIR 1966 SC 1283; 1966 (1) FLR 159; 1966 (1) LLJ 440; 1966 (2) MLJ(SC) 57

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, ss. 10(1)(d) - K Veeranna was employed as a clerk by syndicate Bank, Ltd in Vijayawada branch - Order of transfer to Banganpalli branch - Refused to join and applied for medical leave - Veeranna claimed that he was entitled to exemption from transfer under Sastri award on being treasurer of respondent's union - Whether Industrial Tribunal was justified in holding transfer of K Veeranna as malafide? - Banks are entitled to decide on a consideration of necessities of banking business whether transfer of an employee should be made to a particular branch - Management of bank is in best position to judge how to distribute its employees between different branches - Held, the finding of tribunal that transfer of Veeranna is mala fide is not supported by any evidence and it is perverse and defective in law - Appeal allowed.
