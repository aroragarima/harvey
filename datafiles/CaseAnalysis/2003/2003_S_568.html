Jitendra and Another v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 748; (2004) 10 SCC 562; (2004) SCC (Cr) 2028; AIR 2003 SC 4236; 2003 (4) CCR 78; 2003 (4) Crimes 225; 2004 (1) CRJ 256; 2003 CRLJ 4985; 2003 (89) ECC 705; 2003 (3) JCC 1502; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 331; 2003 (4) KHCACJ 505; 2003 (4) RCR(Criminal) 360; 2003 (58) RLT 725; 2003(7) SCALE 619; 2003 (5) SLT 554; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 918; 2003 (7) Supreme 68

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Cross-Examination, Charge - Sheet, Code of Criminal Procedure, Criminal Procedure

Summary: Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985 - ss. 8, 18, 20, 20(b) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - s. 465 - Seizure of charas and ganja - Conviction - No independent witness as to the recovery of the drugs from the possession of accused - Non-production of siezed articles before the court - Panchas have turned hostile - Investigating officer was also not examined - Held, mere oral evidence as to their features and production of panchnama does not discharges the heavy burden which lies on the prosecution, particularly where the offence is punishable with a stringent sentence as under the NDPS, Act - Further held, taking the cumulative effect of all the circumstances, the material placed on record by the prosecution does not bring home the charge beyond reasonable doubt. It would be unsafe to convict the appellants on the material placed on record. They are certainly entitled to the benefit of doubt.
