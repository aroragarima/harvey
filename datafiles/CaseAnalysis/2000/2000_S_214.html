Smt. Dilboo (Dead) By Lrs. & Ors v Smt. Dhanraji (Dead) And Ors

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, V.N. Khare

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 538; (2000) 7 SCC 702; AIR 2000 SC 3146; 2000 ALJ 2481; 2000 (4) AWC 3210; JT 2000 (10) SC 385; 2000 (1) MLJ(SC) 141; 2000 (4) RCR(Civil) 734; 2001 (92) RD 197; 2000(6) SCALE 332; 2000 (6) SLT 680; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 214; 2000 (7) Supreme 471

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC,1908, s. 100 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, arts. 134 and 148 - Suit for redemption of mortgage - Question of fact - Whether the predecessors-in-title were heirs of mortgagor - Concurrent findings - Relation not poved, statements of interested parties not considered as proof - Other although competent choose to stay out of witness box - Respondent acquired no title and had no right to claim redemption - (A) Reappreciation of evidence by HC in appeal and arriving at contrary conclusion - Correctness - (B) Suit filed after 58 years, if barred - Held, in the circumstances, it would not only be error in law but also on facts - Suit held was barred by limitation and otherwise also - Appeal allowed. .
