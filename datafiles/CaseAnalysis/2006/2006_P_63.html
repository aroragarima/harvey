A. Sudhakar v Post Master General, Hyderabad and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 97; (2006) 4 SCC 348; 2006 (5) AWC 5256; JT 2006 (4) SC 68; 2006(3) SCALE 524; 2006 (4) SCJ 534; [2006] 3 S.C.R. 373; 2006 (2) SLR 714; 2006 (2) UPLBEC 1297

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964, KVP rules 1988

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 311(2) - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - KVP rules 1988, rr. 7, r. 8 - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, rr. 11, 14(11), 14(15) - CCS (Conduct) Rules, 1964, rr. 17, 3(1)(i), 3(1)(ii) - Order of compulsory retirement - Plea that disciplinary authority who passed order of compulsory retirement had no jurisdiction and order passed was improper without following procedure and documents on which reliance was placed contained certain anomalies as regard names of signatory - Held, appellant case is in no manner prejudiced by holding that the Director of Postal Services was the appointing authority - No grievance made by appellant that he was prejudiced for non-supply of copy of a document - Temporary defalcation of any amount itself was sufficient for disciplinary authority to impose punishment of compulsory retirement upon Appellant - Appeal dismissed.
