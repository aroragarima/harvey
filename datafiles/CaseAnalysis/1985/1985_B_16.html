Commissioner of Income Tax, Delhi and Others v Bansi Dhar and Sons and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 December 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 16; (1986) 1 SCC 523; AIR 1986 SC 421; 1986 (50) CTR 250; 1986 (24) E.L.T. 193; [1986] 157 ITR 665; [1985] supp3 S.C.R. 850; 1986 TAXLR 317; [1986] 24 TAXMAN 11

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 66 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 256 - Whether High Court has jurisdiction to grant stay or pass interim orders in pending references u/s. 66 of 1922 Act and s.256 of 1961 Act? - S. 66 of 1922 Act or s. 256 of 1961 Act is a special jurisdiction of a limited nature conferred not by Civil Procedure Code or by Charters or by Special Acts constituting such HCs but by special provisions of Income-tax Act, 1922 or 1961 for limited purpose of obtaining HC's opinion on questions of law - In giving that opinion properly if any question of incidental or ancillary power arises such as giving an opportunity or restoring a reference dismissed without hearing or giving some additional time to file paper book, such powers inhered to jurisdiction conferred upon it - But such incidental powers cannot be so construed as to confer power of stay of recovery of taxes pending a reference which lie in domain of an appellate authority - Therefore, concept of granting stay in a reference ex debito justitiae does not arise - Held, it cannot be said that HC has inherent power or incidental power in matter of a reference pending before it to grant stay of realisation or to grant injunction - Power must remain within jurisdiction of appellate authority and pendency of a reference does not detract from that jurisdiction of appellate authority - HC was in error in exercising its jurisdiction by passing an order for stay of realisation u/s. 151 of CPC in a pending reference - Appeals allowed.
