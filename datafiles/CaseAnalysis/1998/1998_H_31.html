Indu Kakkar v Haryana State Industrial Development Corporation and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2041; (1999) 2 SCC 37; AIR 1999 SC 296; 1999 (1) CLT 57; JT 1998 (8) SC 294; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 132; 1999 (90) RD 78; 1998(6) SCALE 387; 1999 (1) SCJ 256; 1998 (10) SLT 17; [1998] Supp3 S.C.R. 277; 1998 (9) Supreme 179

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Summary: Haryana Urban Development Authority Act, 1977, s. 54 - Haryana Urban Development (Disposal of Land and Buildings) Regulations, 1978, Regn. 20 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 11, 31, 32 and 130 - Allotment of plot by Industrial Development Corporation - Purpose was establishment of industrial unit - Failed to its purpose - Resumption of plot by allottor, corporation - Suit against - Transfer of plot by allottee during pendency - Held, transferee of allottee cannot claim any other right which even allottee did not have - Condition that industrial unit should be established within specified time failing which interest shall cease - Is valid condition - Agreement between parties is valid and binding on parties.
