Aparna A. Shah v Sheth Developers Private Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Jagdish Singh Khehar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 370; (2013) 8 SCC 71; AIR 2013 SC 3210; 2013 (3) BC 491; 2013 (4) Bom.C.R. 879; 2013 (3) CompLJ 449; 2013 CRLJ 3743; 2013 (3) JCC (NI) 169; JT 2013 (9) SC 258; 2013 (3) KLT 190; 2014 (1) MahLJ 1; 2014(1) M.P.H.T. 1; 2014 (1) MPLJ 42; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 680; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 686; 2013(8) SCALE 140; [2013] 7 S.C.R. 69; 2013 (3) WLN(SC) 105

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Permissibility, Cheque bounce

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138, 141 - Cheque bounce - Permissibility - Respondent company was engaged in business of land development and constructions - Appellant and her husband, were Land aggregators and Developers - Respondent agreed for development of land jointly with appellant and her husband - Therefore, on understanding between respondent and appellant cheque of Rs. 25 crores was issued by husband of appellant from their joint account - Respondent deposited cheque and said cheque was dishonoured due to 'insufficient funds' - Complaint was filed against appellants before Trial Court - Trial Court issued process against appellants - Appellants filed application, which was dismissed - Appellant filed petition before HC - HC partly allowed petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether order recorded by HC was justifiable -

Held, u/s. 138 of the Act, it was only drawer of cheque who could be prosecuted - In instant case, admittedly, appellant was not drawer of cheque and appellant had not signed the same and fact remained that her husband alone put his signature - U/s. 138 of the Act, in case of issuance of cheque from joint accounts, joint account holder could not be prosecuted unless cheque was signed by each and every person who was joint account holder - Said principle was exception to s. 141 of the Act which would have no application to instant case - Proceedings filed u/s. 138 of the Act could not be used as arm twisting tactics to recover amount allegedly due from appellant - It could not be said that complainant had no remedy against appellant but certainly not u/s. 138 of the Act - Culpability attached to dishonour of cheque could in no case 'except in case of s. 141 of the Act' be extended to those on whose behalf cheque was issued - Appeal allowed.
