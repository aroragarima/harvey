Godrej and Boyce Manufacturing Company Limited v Deputy Commissioner of Income Tax and another

Supreme Court of India

08 May 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 368; (2017) 7 SCC 421; AIR 2017 SC 2675; [2017] 394 ITR 449; JT 2017 (5) SC 549; 2017 (5) MLJ 310; 2017(6) SCALE 209; [2017] 247 TAXMAN 361

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Asset, Fair Market Value, Companies Act, 1956, Market Value, Assessing Officer, Total Income, Accumulated Profits, Additional Income, Dividends, Expenditure Incurred, Finance Act, 2001, Finance Act, 2003, Finance Act, 2002, Expenditure, Finance Act, 1997, Mutual Fund, Finance Act, 2006, Income-tax Rules, 1962, Income-tax Act, 1961, Finance Bill 2006

Summary: (A) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(33), 115O, 115O(1), 115O(4), 115O(5), 14, 14A, 14A(1), 14A(2), 14A(3), 194, 195, 196C, 199, 52(2), 56, 57 - A/Ys 1999-2000 and 2001-2002 - Dividend income - Tax levied - Validity - Appellant/Company was engaged in the business of manufacture of steel furniture - AO for A/Ys 1999-2000 and 2001-2002 disallowed notionally computed interest expenditure as being relatable to earning of dividend income - On appeal, Commissioner allowed appeal and reversed the order of AO - On further appeal, Tribunal held that sub-sections (2) and (3) of s. 14A of the Act were retrospectively applicable to A/Y 2002-2003 and matter should be remanded to AO - Aggrieved, appellant filed writ petition before HC - HC upheld order of Tribunal holding that tax paid u/s. 115O of the Act was an additional tax and that same was not a tax on dividend income of assesse - Hence, instant appeal - Whether s. 14A of the Act includes dividend income on shares in respect of which tax was payable u/s. 115O of the Act and income on units of mutual funds on which tax was payable u/s. 115R of the Act.

Held, so long as the dividend income was taxable in the hands of dividend paying company, same is not includible in the total income of assessee. At such point of time when the said position was reversed it was assessee who was liable to pay tax on such dividend income. In such a situation assessee was entitled u/s. 57 of the Act to claim the benefit of exemption of expenditure incurred to earn such income. Once ss. 10(33) and 115O of the Act was reintroduced the position was reversed. Therefore, s. 14A of the Act would operate to disallow deduction of all expenditure incurred in earning the dividend income u/s. 115O of the Act which was not includible in the total income of assessee. The other species of dividend income which attracts levy of income tax at the hands of assessee was treated differently and made liable to tax under the aforesaid provisions of the Act. In fact, if the argument was that tax paid by dividend paying company u/s. 115O was to be understood to be on behalf of recipient assessee, the provisions of s. 57 of the Act should enable assessee to claim deduction of expenditure incurred to earn income on which such tax was paid. Such a position in law would be wholly incongruous in view of s. 10(33) of the Act. Thus, s. 14A of the Act would apply to dividend income on which tax was payable u/s. 115O of the Act. Appeal allowed.

(B) Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 10(33), 115O, 115O(1), 115O(4), 115O(5), 14, 14A, 14A(1), 14A(2), 14A(3), 194, 195, 196C, 199, 52(2), 56, 57 - A/Ys 1999-2000 and 2001-2002 - Income-tax Rules, 1962, r. 8D - Assessment proceedings - Challenged - Whether there could at all be any question of the provisions of s. 14A of the Act in appellant's case.

Held, sub-sections (2) and (3) of s.14A of the Act r/w r. 8D of the Rules merely prescribe a formula for determination of expenditure incurred in relation to income which does not form part of the total income under the Act in a situation where AO was not satisfied with the claim of assessee. It was only thereafter that the provisions of s. 14A(2) and (3) of the Act r/w r. 8D of the Rules or a best judgment determination, as earlier prevailing, would become applicable. In instant case, SC do not find any mention of the reasons which had prevailed upon AO, while dealing with A/Y 2002-2003, to hold that claims of assessee that no expenditure was incurred to earn the dividend income cannot be accepted and why the orders of Tribunal for earlier A/Ys were not acceptable to AO, particularly, in the absence of any new fact or change of circumstances. Neither any basis was disclosed establishing a reasonable nexus between expenditure disallowed and dividend income received. It was true that the principle of res judicata would not apply to assessment proceedings under the Act, the need for consistency and certainty and existence of strong and compelling reasons for a departure from a settled position has to be spelt out which conspicuously is absent in instant case. Thus, for A/Y in question i.e. 2002-2003, assessee was entitled to the full benefit of claim of dividend income without any deductions. Impugned order of HC was set aside. Appeal allowed.
