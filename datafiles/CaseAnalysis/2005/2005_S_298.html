State Bank of Patiala v Phoolpati

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 150; (2005) 3 SCC 88; (2005) SCC (L&S) 340; AIR 2005 SC 1918; 2005 (2) AWC 1243; 2005 (2) ESC 213; 2005 (105) FLR 59; 2005 (2) JhrCR(SC) 106; 2005 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 577; 2005 (2) LLJ 473; 2005 (2) LLN 659; 2005(2) SCALE 378; [2005] 2 S.C.R. 345; 2005 (4) SLR 133; 2005 (2) Supreme 226

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Service

Summary: Banking and Finance - Service - Appellant-Bank relieved Hari Ram (HR), deceased husband of respondent - Whether HC right in holding that appellant-Bank was not justified in relieving Hari Ram? - HR had tendered his resignation which was sought to be withdrawn - Withdrawal was not accepted and subsequently there was reiteration of the prayer for voluntary resignation - Held, during his life time HR never questioned legality of Bank's action in relieving him from duty w.e.f. 5/3/2002 by accepting his resignation - Admissible service benefits were accepted by respondent - HC was not justified in interfering with order of appellant-Bank relieving HR w.e.f. 5/3/2002 consequent upon accepting his prayer for resignation - Appeal allowed.
