Bishambhar Dayal Chandra Mohan And Others. Etc. Etc. v State Of Uttar Pradesh & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, Baharul Islam

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 304; (1982) 1 SCC 39; (1982) SCC (Cr) 53; AIR 1982 SC 33; 1982 CRLR 99; 1981(3) SCALE 1685; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1137; 1982 UJ 802

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Trade

Keywords: Constitution (Fourth Amendment) Act, 1955, Constitution (Third Amendment) Act, 1954

Summary: Constitution - Trade - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 28A to 28C - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 32, 162, 301, 303 and 304 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955, s. 6 - Whether setting up check-posts on borders of State and directing seizure of wheat in transit through State, on strength of impugned teleprinter message, conflicted with guarantees of inter-State trade and commerce dealt with by art. 301 of Constitution, 1950? - Held, instructions conveyed by State Govt. by teleprinter message imposing requirement for making of an endorsement by Deputy Marketing Officer or Senior Marketing Officer or physical verification of stocks of wheat during course of transit, are not a 'restriction' on fundamental right to carry on trade or business guaranteed u/art. 19(1)(g) or on freedom of trade, commerce and intercourse u/art. 301 - Petition disposed of.
