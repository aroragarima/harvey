Chandra Mohan v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 August 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 17; AIR 1966 SC 1987; 1967 (14) FLR 386; 1966 (2) ILR(All) 855; 1967 (1) LLJ 412; 1967 (2) SCJ 717; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 77

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Legislations referred: Art. 226 of the Constitution of India, 1950 - Arts. 237, 133(1)(a) and 132(1) - Held, for the recruitment of District Judges are constitutionally void and, therefore, the appointments made thereunder were illegal. We set aside the order of High Court and issue a writ of mandamus to respondent 1, not to make any appointment by direct recruitment to the Uttar Pradesh Higher Judicial Service in pursuance of the selections made under the said rules - Scope of the field of recruitment to the cadre of District Judges - Whether can the Governor, after the Constitution, directly appoint persons from services other than the Judicial Service as District Judges in consultation with the High Court?
