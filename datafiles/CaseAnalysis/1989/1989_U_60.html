Messrs Siddeshwari Cotton Mills Private Limited v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

17 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 811; (1989) 2 SCC 458; AIR 1989 SC 1019; 1989 CRLR 221; 1989 CrLR(SC) 221; 1989 (37) DLT 383; 1989 (20) ECC 1; 1989 (21) ECR 7; 1989 (39) E.L.T. 498; JT 1989 (1) SC 150; 1989(1) SCALE 101; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 214; 1989 (75) STC 75; 1989 (1) UJ 369

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Summary: Excise - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35L - Appellant concern was a manufacturer of cotton fabric on power looms which is exempt from excise duties by way of notifications - Whether appellant loses benefit of exemption by process of calendering on plant situated in premises? - Held, no - Process of calendaring is a process undertaken by which cloth is run between rollers to improve its appearance - Case remitted back to Appellate Tribunal for disposal on merits - Appeal allowed.
