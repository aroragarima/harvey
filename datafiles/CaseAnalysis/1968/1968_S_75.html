Akhtar Alam v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 456; (1969) 1 SCC 142; AIR 1969 SC 142; 1970 ALJ 846; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 682

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Law Amendment Act, 1958

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, s. 5(2) - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 21 and 21(12) - Meaning of 'public servant' u/s. 21 - Whether an employee of State Electricity Board is a 'public servant'? - Test laid down to determine nature of employment; (1) whether he is in service or pay of corporation, and (2) whether he is himself either armed with some authority or representative character by corporation or whether his duties are immediately auxiliary to those of someone who is armed with such authority or representative character - Held, an officer in service or pay of corporation as defined in s. 21(12) and therefore a 'public servant' within meaning of section and also of s. 2 of Act - Appeal dismissed.
