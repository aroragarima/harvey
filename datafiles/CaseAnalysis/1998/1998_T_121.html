State of Tripura v Tripura Bar Association and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1642; (1998) 5 SCC 637; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1426; AIR 1999 SC 1494; AIR 1998 SCW 3840; 1998 (80) FLR 699; JT 1998 (6) SC 601

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950 - Courts - Precedents - Latter division bench cannot take view different from that taken by earlier one - If wanted to do so, they have to refer matter to a larger bench - Division bench of Gauhati High Court taking different view - Reasons given for not referring matter to larger bench - Held not justified - Judgment of latter division bench liable to be set aside - Appeals disposed of.
