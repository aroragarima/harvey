National Human Rights Commission v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 589; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 44; (2009) 6 SCC 767; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 318; 2009 (2) G.L.R. 1672; 2009 (2) MLJ(Crl) 1037; 2009(6) SCALE 509; [2009] 7 S.C.R. 236

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Public Right, Commission Report, TADA Act, Travelling Allowance, Public Trial, Criminal Justice and Public Order Act, 1994, Crime Control Act, 1970, Witness Security Reform Act, 1984, Comprehensive Crime Control Act, 1984, Witness Protection Act, 1996, Australian Witness Protection Act, 1994, South Africa Witness Protection Act, 1998, Director of Public Prosecutions Act, 1983, Prosecution of Offences Act, 1974, Crime Victims Rights Act, 2004

Summary: Criminal - Cr.P.C, 1973, s. 173 (8) - Issue of ensuring a fair trial - Protection of a witness - Direction to undertake inquiry/investigation including further investigation in nine cases - Held, supplementary charge sheets shall be filed in each of these cases as the SIT has found further material and/or has identified other accused against whom charges are now to be brought - Stay on the conduct of the trials are vacated in order to enable the trials to continue - SIT would continue to function and carry out any investigations that are yet to be completed, or any further investigation that may arise in the course of the trials - Directions issued.
