Tarsem Singh v State of Punjab and others

Supreme Court of India

25 January 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1553; (2006) 13 SCC 581; 2006(11) SCALE 104

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 311 - Dismissal from service - Legality - Appellant was charge-sheeted for commission of misconduct - On the basis of the allegations alone and without any further material, PPS, Commandant, concluded that the appellant could win over aggrieved people as well as witnesses from giving evidence by threatening and other means, a formal departmental proceeding need not be initiated - Appellant was dismissed from service - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed by Appellate Authority - Writ petition was filed thereagainst, wherein HC directed, authority to pass an appropriate order - Pursuant to direction, the Inspector General of Police, upon hearing the appellant, passed an order dismissing the said representation - Writ petition filed by the appellant against the said order, was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of dismissal passed against appellant was justified - Held, in awarding the punishment of dismissal from service upon the appellant no formal enquiry was held purportedly on the ground that the same enquiry could be dispensed with, under proviso (b) appended to art. 311(2) of the Constitution - However, purported reason for dispensing with the departmental proceedings was not supported by any document - Hence, in view of the fact that no material had been placed by the respondents to satisfy the Court that it was necessary to dispense with a formal enquiry in terms of proviso (b) appended to art. 311(2) of the Constitution, impugned orders could not be sustained - Appellant was directed to be reinstated in service - Appeal allowed.
