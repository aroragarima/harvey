Bombay Telephone Canteen Employees’ Association, Prabhadevi v Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

09 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2466; (1997) 6 SCC 723; (1998) SCC (L&S) 386; AIR 1997 SC 2817; 1997 (2) CLR 218; JT 1997 (6) SC 57; 1997 (2) LLJ 647; [1997] Supp2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Corporate Body, Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Constitution, arts. 14, 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Bombay Industrial Disputes Act, 1938 - Administrative Tribunals Act, 1985, s. 19 - Termination - Employees working in canteen of Telephone Nigam - Reference - (A) Where protection against arbitrary action of employer or security of tenure, is avilable in law - (B) Tenability of reference when employer admitting them as her employees - Held, remedy either u/art. 226 or of reference u/s.10 of Industrial Disputes Act is available - Reference, not tenable - Not tenable as employees have remedy u/art. 226 of Constitution and s.19 of Administrative Tribunals Act - Petition dismissed.
