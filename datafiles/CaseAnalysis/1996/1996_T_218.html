L.M. Mahurkar v The Bar Council Of Maharashtra And Another

Supreme Court of India

08 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Sen, S.B. Majmudar, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2929; (1996) 9 SCC 192; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 411; AIR 1996 SC 1602; 1996 (2) CCC 126; JT 1996 (4) SC 224; 1996(3) SCALE 399; 1996 (101) STC 541; [1996] Supp1 S.C.R. 85; 1996 (3) Supreme 643; 1996 (2) UJ 201

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961, s. 24(3)(aa) - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 71 - Bombay Sales Tax Rules, 1959, r. 66 - Sales Tax practitioner under Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Not possessing requisite educational qualifications under Advocates Act, 1961 - Whether entitled to be enrolled as an Advocate? - Held, he is entitled - And there is nothing in provisions of Bombay Sales Tax Act or Rules framed thereunder to suggest that right of appearance before a sales tax authority amounts to entitlement to practise profession of law as contemplated by s. 24(3)(aa) of Advocates Act - Appeal dismissed.
