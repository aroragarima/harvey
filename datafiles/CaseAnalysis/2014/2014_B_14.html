State of Madhya Pradesh v Bablu

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 659; (2014) 9 SCC 281; AIR 2015 SC 102; 2014 CRLJ 4565; 2014 (3) JabLJ 429; 2014 (4) JCC 2683; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 761; 2015 (2) RLW 1150; 2014(9) SCALE 678; [2014] 9 S.C.R. 467

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Modesty

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 360 - Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes (Prevention of Atrocities) Act, 1989 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 323 and 354 - Outrage modesty of woman - Prosecution alleged that respondent tried to outrage modesty of the complainant and when she screamed for help he fled away - Special Judge convicted the respondent for the offences punishable u/ss. 323 and 354 of IPC sentencing him to undergo rigorous imprisonment for 6 months with fine of Rs.500/- - Respondent preferred an appeal before HC where he did not challenge his conviction but prayed for reduction of awarded sentence - HC reduced respondent sentence to the period already undergone - Hence, instant appeal - Whether the period of sentence for the offence punishable u/s. 354 of IPC can be reduced to the period undergone for only 21 days just on the ground that the respondent was first time offender and was facing trial since 2003.

Held, liberal attitude by imposing meagre sentences or taking sympathetic view merely on account of lapse of time in respect of such offences will be counter-productive in the long run and against societal interest which needs to be cared for and strengthened by string of deterrence in built in the sentencing system. HC in a very casual manner reduced the sentence of the respondent to the period already undergone merely on the ground that the respondent is first offender. If such a view is taken, the respondent, who commit such offence, will be emboldened and repeat such crime, which is totally detrimental to the society. Impugned order was set aside. Appeal allowed.
