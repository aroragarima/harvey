Late Nawab Sir Mir Osman Ali Khan v Commissioner Of Wealth Tax, Hyderabad

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 794; (1986) Supp SCC 700; (1987) SCC (Tax) 88; AIR 1987 SC 522; 1986 (57) CTR 89; [1986] 162 ITR 888; JT 1986 SC 684B; 1986(2) SCALE 626; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 1072; [1986] 28 TAXMAN 641; 1986 (2) TLR 1502

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s.8 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s.2(31) - Whether upon terms and conditions it was intended by the settlers that the beneficiaries should receive the properties in their individual capacity or in a representative capacity as Kartas of the respective Hindu undivided families - Appeals involve the construction of two trust- deeds couched in identical terms - Conferring interest on grandsons, executed respectively by a male member and female member of Hindu family - Deeds providing for distribution of properties of settlers in equal share on the basis of certain event - Held, on terms of deeds that intention was that properties were intended to devolve on beneficiaries in their individual capacity - Appeal allowed.
