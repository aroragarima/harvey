Bihar State Electricity Board, Patna And Ors v Green Rubber Industries And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

24 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, M. Fathima Beevi

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 327; (1990) 1 SCC 731; AIR 1990 SC 699; 1990 BBCJ 51; JT 1989 (4) SC 421; 1989(2) SCALE 1196; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 275

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Electricity & Energy; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Electricity & Energy - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - Electricity Act, 1910 - Minimum Guaranteed Charges - (A) Validity and reasonableness - Respondents got connection in month of April, applied for reduction of required KVA which was granted, in subsequent month, again in next month itself i.e., in June it applied for disconnection - Bills were issued by Board as Minimum Guaranteed Charges from June to September - Failure to pay bill - Disconnection towards end of month of September - Validity of Bills and certificate proceedings challenged before High Court - HC quashed bills and certificate proceedings - Present appeal - (B) Whether despite fact that supply line was disconnected on 28-09-1981, firm was still liable to pay minimum guaranteed charges under agreement? - Held, minimum guaranteed charges would be payable by consumer irrespective of whether energy to that extent has been consumed or not - If amount of a bill submitted according to law remains unpaid after due date specified in bill, Board may discontinue supply after giving consumer not less than 7 clear day's notice - Consumer cannot determine agreement before expiry of two years and there is nothing to show in this case that he did so after expiry thereof with previous notice - In case of disconnection of supply by Board in exercise of its powers under agreement it would be open for consumer to apply for reconnection in accordance with law within remainder period of above given compulsorily availing of supply or that of notice whichever is longer, and on his failure to do so he will be deemed to have given a notice on date of disconnection in terms of aforesaid cl. 9(a) for determination of agreement and on expiry of remainder period of compulsorily availing of supply or notice, whichever is longer, agreement shall cease and determine - Disconnection on default of consumer have been effected on 28-09-1981 and consumer having not applied for reconnection, it would be deemed to have given a notice on date of disconnection in terms of cl. 9 (a) for determination of agreement and agreement must be taken to have ceased and determined either at end of notice or at end of period of compulsorily availing of supply, that is, two years of agreement whichever is longer - Relevant agreement having been executed on 2-05-1981 it would expire on 1-05-1983 - Disconnection having been effected on 28-09-1981 period of deemed notice of seven days expired before period of compulsorily availing of supply under agreement expired and hence agreement must be deemed to have determined only on 1-05-1983 - During this period consumer's liability to pay minimum guaranteed charges must be held to have continued - Charging 'minimum guaranteed charges', practice thereof in different nations, precedents settled thereupon, looked upon and debated - Stipulation as to minimum guaranteed charges valid and reasonable - Agreement was reasonable and valid and it was not determined with disconnection of supply to respondent firm by Board on 28-09-1981 but only according to stipulations in cl. 9 (a) - Impugned judgement set aside - Appeal allowed.
