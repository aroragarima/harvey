Â Balmadies Plantations Limited and another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, S.M. Sikri, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 751; (1972) 2 SCC 133; AIR 1972 SC 2240; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 1; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 258

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Land reforms - Gudalur Janmam Estates (Abolition and Conversion into Ryotwari) Act, 1969, ss. 3 and 11 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 31 A(2)(a)(i) - 'Janmam Estates' - 'Ryotwari Estates' - Constitutional validity of Act on touchstone of arts. 14, 19 and 31 - Vesting of estates in Govt. - Contention raised by petitioners that their lands in Gudalur Taluk were previously 'janmam' estates but subsequently became ryotwari estates, especially after resettlement of 1926 and, as such provisions of Act were not applicable to those lands - Certain lands covered by forests also sought to be taken over by Govt. - (A) Whether Act covered by protection u/art. 31A? - Held, provisions of s. 3 of Act in so far as they relate to transfer of forests in 'janmam' estates in question are violative of Constitution - Such extent of provision thus struck down - On ground of severability remaining provision held to be valid and constitutional - Vires upheld in part - (B) Whether 'janmam' rights in lands in question have been converted into 'ryotwari' estate? - Held, exclusive right to, and hereditary possession of soil in Malabar is denoted by term 'jenmam' which means birthright and holder thereof is known as jenmi, jenmakaran or mutalalan - A janmam right is freehold interest in a property situated in Kerala - 'Ryotwari' interest; holding of lands on lease from Govt., basic idea of ryotwari settlement is that every bit of land is assessed to a certain revenue and assigned a survey number for a period of years, which is usually thirty, and each occupant of such land holds it subject to his paying land revenue fixed on that land, but it is open to occupant to relinquish his land or to take new land which has been relinquished by some other occupant or become otherwise available on payment of assessment; 'Land System of British India by Baden Powell' - Fact that in addition to janmam right in State of Kerala, janmam right in State of Madras was also mentioned in cl. (2)(a)(i) of art. 31-A as a result of amendment, shows that janmam rights in lands in question were assumed by legislature to be in existence - (C) Whether acquisition of lands in question is for agrarian reform? - Held, object and general scheme of Act is to abolish intermediaries between State and cultivator and to help actual cultivator by giving him status of direct relationship between himself and State - Act, as such, in its broad outlines should be held to be a measure of agrarian reform and would consequently be protected by art. 31-A of Constitution - Act thus is immune from attack on ground of being violative of arts. 14, 19 and 31 - (D) Whether acquisition of forests is in furtherance of objective of agrarian reform? - Held, nothing in Act to indicate as to what could be purpose for which said forests would be used after transfer of 'janmam' land containing forests to Govt. - No material on record to indicate that transfer of forests from 'janmi' to Govt. is linked in any way with a scheme of agrarian reform or betterment of village economy - Held, acquisition of forests on 'janmam' land is not protected by art. 31-A - Petition disposed of.
