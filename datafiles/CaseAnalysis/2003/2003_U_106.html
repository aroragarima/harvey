State of Uttar Pradesh and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

04 February 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 460; (2003) 3 SCC 239; AIR 2003 SC 1147; 2004 (190) CTR 569; 2004 (170) E.L.T. 385; JT 2003 (1) SC 574; 2003(1) SCALE 615; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 785; 2003 (1) SLT 754; 2003 (130) STC 1; 2006 (3) S.T.R. 98; 2003 (1) Supreme 819

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Transfer Of Property, Indian Telegraph Rules, 1951, Deferred Payment, Finance Act, 1997, Indian Sale Of Goods Act, 1930, Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948, Madhya Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1959, India Telegraph Act, 1885

Summary: Uttar Pradesh Trade Tax Act, 1948 - ss.2(c)(iv), 2(d), 3-F, 7(3) - Finance Act - Constitution of India -art.285(1) - India Telegraph Act, 1885 - The Indian Telegraph Rules, 1951 - Non-filing of return by DoT - Can rentals collected by DoT from the subscribers of telephone in the State, be assessed to tax under the U.P. Act? - Held, (1) DoT is a 'dealer' as defined in s.2(c), U.P. Act; (2) it collects rentals for the supply of transfer of use of telephone connection, which is compendiously called 'service'; (3) the supply of telephone satisfies the requirements of a transfer of the right to use the goods within the meaning of 'sale' in s.2(h); (4) also receives consideration - The requirements of charging s.3 read with s.3(f) are satisfied - Dot to file returns -Cases have to go back to the assessing officer for raising fresh demand
