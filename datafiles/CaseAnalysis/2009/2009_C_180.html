Commissioner of Income Tax, Faridabad v Ghanshyam (HUF)

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 890; (2009) 8 SCC 412; [2009] 315 ITR 1; JT 2009 (9) SC 445; 2009(9) SCALE 567; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 1025

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Additional Consideration, Additional Demand, Dealing In Land, Revenue's Appeal Allowed

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 45 (5), 54H - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 23(1), 23(1A) and 23(2) - Assessment Year 1999-2000 - Capital gain -Enhanced compensation with interest - Assessee received enhanced compensation on its lands being acquired by HUDA as also interest thereon during previous year relevant to assessment year 1999-2000 - Assessee filed its return for assessment year 1999-2000 in which he did not offer amount of enhanced compensation and interest received on plea that amount of enhanced compensation received had not accrued to assessee during year of receipt as entire amount was in dispute in appeal before HC which appeal stood filed by State against order of Reference Court granting enhanced compensation - Amount was received by assessee in terms of interim order of HC against assessee's furnishing security to satisfaction of executing court - Assessing Officer alleged that amount by which compensation or consideration stood enhanced or further enhanced by Court, is deemed income chargeable under head 'Capital Gains' of previous year - Commissioner allowed appeal filed against said order which was also affirmed by Tribunal - HC also dismissed appeal filed by dept. -P /&gt;(A) Whether ITAT was right in ordering deletion of enhanced compensation and interest thereon from total income of assessee on ground that said two items, awarded by Reference Court, was under dispute in First Appeal before HC? - Enhanced compensation with interest is taxable in year of receipt, even if receipt is on security - Held, in case of compulsory acquisition of an asset, capital gains in compensation, as originally awarded, is charged to tax in year in which transfer by way of compulsory acquisition takes place, but additional compensation is brought to tax only in year in which it is received - Interest is also a part of enhanced compensation, hence it is also charged in year of receipt -P /&gt;(B) Whether judgment of SC in Hindustan Housing 1986 INDLAW SC 180 would apply to present case which arises under Income-tax Act, 1961? - Held, judgment of SC in Hindustan Housing was delivered on 29.7.86, prior to 1.4.88 when S. 45(5) stood incorporated by Finance Act 1987 w.e.f. 1.4.88 - Not applicable to present case - Issue was settled but since matters are decade old and court not aware of what has happened in Land Acquisition Act proceedings in pending appeals, matter directed to recomputation - Revenue's appeal allowed.
