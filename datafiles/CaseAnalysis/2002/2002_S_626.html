Mani Kumar Thapa v State of Sikkim

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1919; (2002) 7 SCC 157; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1637; AIR 2002 SC 2920; 2002 (3) CCR 199; 2002 (2) CGLJ 374; 2002 (3) Crimes 138; 2002 (6) CRJ 415; 2002 CRLJ 4069; 2002 (3) JCC 1515; JT 2002 (6) SC 349; 2002 (3) RCR(Criminal) 795; 2002(6) SCALE 1; 2002 (4) SLT 790; 2002 (5) Supreme 357

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 364, 302, 201 r/w s. 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant was Sub-Inspector of Police along with accused no. 1 who was then Sub-Divisional Police Officer was charged for offence u/ss. 364, 302, 201 r/w s. 34 IPC for committing abduction and murder of deceased and causing disappearance of evidence - Accused no. 1 died during trial of case - Appellant being found guilty by Special Judge Human Rights was convicted and sentenced to undergo imprisonment for life - For offence u/s. 364 IPC, appellant was sentenced to undergo RI for 6 months and for the offence u/s. 201 he was further sentenced to undergo RI for 3 years and to pay a fine of Rs.5,000/- - Appeal against conviction and sentence to HC was filed and it failed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, evidence of PW-5 that appellant had told him that after body of deceased was taken from place where it had fallen in first instance, appellant had taken away certain possible identification materials like Panchayat seal and some personal papers with view to create false evidence as to whereabouts of deceased - This also indicates involvement of appellant in crime - These circumstances and inferences drawn from such proved circumstances establish beyond all reasonable doubt that appellant did share common intention of A-1 in taking deceased away in jeep driven by A-1 and causing murder, therefore, hypothesis of innocence pleaded on behalf of appellant was not in consonance with innocence of appellant - On contrary, from chain of circumstantial evidence prosecution had been able to establish beyond all reasonable doubt that appellant did share common intention of A-1 in abducting deceased, causing his death as also causing disappearance of evidence of offence u/s. 201 IPC - Appeal dismissed.
