Sanjay Dhar v J. and K. Public Service Commission and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3558; (2000) 8 SCC 182; AIR 2000 SC 3238; JT 2000 (Supp1) SC 469; 2000(6) SCALE 689; 2000 (8) SLR 3

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution; Service

Summary: Service - Advocates & Judges - J & K Civil Service (Judicial) Recruitment Rules, 1967, r. 9 - Appointment - Issuance for certificate - Participation for selection - Relief for appointment - Entitlement for appointment - Appellant was practicing in HC made application to Registrar and placed certificate issued by Registrar, HC before District and Sessions Judge, which was submitted to Public Service Commission (PSC) for post of Munsif - However, PSC did not feel satisfied with certificate informed to appellant that his application was rejected for non-production of a valid actual practice certificate as required by r. 9 of the Act - As against civil writ petition filed by appellant, HC passed interim order directing PSC to permit appellant to appear in examination as well as in interview - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether certificate of practice furnished by appellant satisfied requirement of r. 9 of the Rules and appellant was wrongfully denied appointment for selections - Held, certificate filed by appellant before PSC satisfied requirement of r. 9 of the Rules and PSC was not justified in rejecting application of appellant holding him to be ineligible - As appellant participated in process of selection protected by interim orders of HC and was successful having secured third position in select list, he could not have been denied appointment - Appellant was, therefore, fully entitled to relief of his appointment being calculated w.e.f. same date with which candidates found their place in order of appointments issued pursuant to select list prepared by PSC were appointed and deserved to be assigned notionally place in seniority consistently with order of merit assigned by PSC - HC and Govt. (Law Department) were not justified in by passing judicial order of HC and made appointments exhausted all available vacancies - Hence, right of appellant, if otherwise sustainable, could not be allowed to be lost merely because of appointment was made wittingly or unwittingly in defiance of judicial order of HC - Appeal allowed.
