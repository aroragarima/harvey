Om Prakash Srivastava v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1032; (2006) 3 SCC (Cr) 24; (2006) 6 SCC 207; AIR 2007 SC 1834; 2006 (131) DLT 557; JT 2006 (7) SC 35; 2006 (3) RCR(Civil) 720; 2006 (3) RCR(Criminal) 855; 2006(7) SCALE 318; [2006] Supp3 S.C.R. 803

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Extradition Act, 1962, S. 21 - Jurisdiction - Appellant was facing trial in eight cases - Writ petition alleging violation of s. 21 and he was being kept in solitary confinement without proper medical aid in the Central Jail - Delhi High Court held that the Allahabad High Court would have also jurisdiction to deal with grievances of the writ petitioner and can deal with conditions of prisoners in that State more effectively, though the Delhi High Court may have jurisdiction - Appeal against - Held, High Court has not dealt with the question as to whether it had jurisdiction to deal with the writ petition - It says that jurisdiction may be there, but the Allahabad High Court can deal with the matter more effectively - That is not certainly a correct way to deal with the writ petition - Remitted the matter to it for fresh hearing on merits - Appeal disposed of.
