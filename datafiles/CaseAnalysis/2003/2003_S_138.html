Sheikh Noor And Anr. v Sheikh G.S. Ibrahim (Dead) By Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 579; (2003) 7 SCC 321; AIR 2003 SC 4163; 2003 (5) ALD(SC) 98; 2003 (52) ALR 686; 2004 (1) Bom.C.R. 670; 2004 (1) G.L.R. 284; JT 2003 (6) SC 455; 2003 (3) KHCACJ 678; 2003 (2) RCJ 31; 2003 (2) RCR(Rent) 218; 2003(6) SCALE 72; 2003 (4) SLT 956; [2003] Supp2 S.C.R. 204; 2003 (5) Supreme 305

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - s. 109 - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Property auctioned due to non-payment of municipal taxes - Disputed property bought by the person who was previously collecting rent from the auctioned property on instruction from the original owner - Ownership rights transferred to another person - Tenants intimated of transfer of rights and directed to pay rent to the new landlord - Tenants refused to pay rent on the ground that a valid title was not passed from the auction - Held, the correct position of law is that a transferee is not entitled to recover the arrears as rent for the property on transfer unless the right to recover the arrears is also transferred. If right to recover the arrears is assigned, then the transferee/landlord can recover those arrears as rent, and if not paid, maintain a petition for eviction under the rent laws for those arrears as well.
