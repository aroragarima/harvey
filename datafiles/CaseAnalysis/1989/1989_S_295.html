Pal Singh v Sunder Singh (Dead) By Lrs. And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 January 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 696; (1989) 1 SCC 444; AIR 1989 SC 758; JT 1989 (1) SC 67; 1989 (1) RCJ 202; 1989 (2) RCR(Rent) 331; 1989 (1) RentLR 345; 1989(1) SCALE 36; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 67; 1989 (1) UJ 316

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, ss. 14(1)(e) and 39 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 116 - Eviction suit - (A) By only one of co-owners - Others not objecting to claim - Tenanted premises allotted to share of landlord co-owner, who filed suit - Maintainability - (B) New point raised for first time in second appeal and not in first appeal or in pleading of petition - (C) Interference by High Court - Finding of fact as to bona fide nature of need of landlord - Finding by Appellate Court - Commissioner appointed by HC to confirm it - HC after considering report not interfering with findings of Appellate Court - Validity - Held, (A) Maintainable as tenant paying rent to him and s. 116 of Evidence Act attracted - (B) Cannot be raised in second appeal - (C) Justified - Appeal dismissed.
