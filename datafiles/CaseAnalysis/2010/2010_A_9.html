Sri Jeyaram Educational Trust And Others v A.G.Syed Mohideen And Others

Supreme Court of India

22 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 51; (2010) 2 SCC 513; AIR 2010 SC 671; 2010 (78) ALR 904; 2010 (2) AWC 1240; 2010 (1) CTC 602; 2010 (2) JhrCR(SC) 20; JT 2010 (1) SC 391; 2010 (1) KLT 425; 2010 (2) MLJ(SC) 801; 2010 (1) OLR 838; 2010 (1) RCR(Civil) 875; 2010 (110) RD 169; 2010(1) SCALE 561; 2010 (1) UC 514

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873

Summary: Constitution - Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Tamil Nadu Civil Courts Act, 1873, s. 12 - Jurisdiction - District Court - Respondents instituted a suit against appellants u/s. 92 of CPC, seeking a direction to the second appellant to repay all the amounts spent by him contrary to the terms of the supplementary deed of Trust - Appellants filed memo stating that District Court did not have jurisdiction to entertain any suit u/s. 92 of CPC - Said memo was dismissed by District Judge on the ground that he had jurisdiction to entertain the suit, as the value of the suit was Rs.10 lakh - Revision filed against said order was also dismissed by HC - Hence present appeal - Whether District Court in the State of Tamil Nadu, does not have jurisdiction to try a suit u/s. 92 of CPC? - Held, s. 92 is a self contained provision, and conferment of jurisdiction in regard to suits under that section does not depend upon the value of the subject matter of the suit - Moreover, pecuniary limits mentioned in s. 12 of the Civil Courts Act, do not apply to suits u/s. 92 of CPC - Therefore, insofar as the suits u/s. 92 are concerned, the District Courts and Sub- ordinate Courts will have concurrent jurisdiction without reference to any pecuniary limits - Appeal dismissed

Constitution - Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 92 - Interpretation of s. 92 - Whether the word 'or' was used in s. 92 of CPC in a substitutive sense? - Held, it is clear from s. 92 of CPC that the legislature did not want to go by the general rule contained in s. 15 of CPC - Intention of the law makers was that such suits should be tried by the District Court - At the same time, the law makers contemplated that if there was heavy work load on the District Court, the State Government should be enabled to empower any other court, also to entertain such suits - Therefore, the word 'or' is used in the ordinary and normal sense, that is to denote an alternative, giving a choice - Provisions of s. 92 do not give room for interpreting the word 'or' as a substitutive, so as to lead to an interpretation that when the Government notified any other court, such notified court alone will have jurisdiction and not the District Court - Appeal dismissed
