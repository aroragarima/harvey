Â In The Supreme Court of India v Civil Advisory Jurisdiction

Supreme Court of India

28 October 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, M.K. Mukherjee, S.B. Majmudar, Sujata V. Manohar, G.T. Nanavati, S. Saghir Ahmad, K. Venkataswami, G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1548

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Constitution - Constitution (Fifteenth Amendment) Act, 1963 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Government Of India Act, 1935 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Judicial Review - Appointment of Judges - Power of Transfer - Constitutional Functionaries - Justifiability -Petitioner/President of India, referred questions to SC(Chief Justice) of India for consideration and to report its opinion, with regard to appointments of Judges to SC and HC, to transfers of HC Judges - In exercise of this power, President of India had made present reference - Hence instant petition - Whether Chief Justice of India was conferred upon powers with regard to appointments of Judges to SC and HC, to transfers of HC Judges - Held, no one could be appointed to SC, unless his appointment was in conformity with opinion of Chief Justice of India - Judicial review was limited with area of justifiability to constitutional requirement of recommendation of Chief Justice of India for exercise of power u/art. 222 of Constitution, by President of India - Power of transfer were to be exercised by highest constitutional functionaries in country in manner indicated, which provided several inbuilt checks against likelihood of arbitrariness or bias - Chief Justice of India should obtain views of Chief Justice of HC from which proposed transfer was to be effected - Order accordingly.
