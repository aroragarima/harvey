R. Mahalingam v Chairman, Tamil Nadu Public Service Commission and another

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 430; (2013) 14 SCC 379; JT 2013 (3) SC 425; 2013 (2) SLR 760

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Tamil Nadu Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules

Summary: Service - Tamil Nadu Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, r. 17(b) - Imposition of departmental enquiry - Guilty of charges - Dismissal from service - Legality - Appellant joined service as Junior Asst. in respondent commission - Respondent directed appellant to work as Invigilator at B Women Arts College - In examination held on 17-2-1990, 6 candidates sitting were given question papers of afternoon examination in morning examination - Respondent imposed departmental inquiry against appellant u/r. 17(b) of the Rules - Thereafter, Enquiry Officer submitted report holding appellant guilty of all charges - Appellant filed appeal before Chairman, which was dismissed - Chairman reiterated the findings recorded by Enquiry Officer and Secretary that appellant had unauthorisedly done work of Invigilator and prevented Chief Invigilator from reporting the matter to Commission - Appellant filed writ petition before Single Judge, which was dismissed - Aggrieved, appellant filed appeal before DB, which was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Single Judge and DB of HC was justified in passing the impugned orders - Held, main allegations leveled against appellant were that he had unauthorisedly worked as Invigilator and prevented Chief Invigilator from sending report about incident involving mixing of question papers of 2 sessions - Neither of them relied upon same for recording a finding of guilty against appellant - Appellant had produced order by which he had been nominated to work as Invigilator and he had no role in bringing question papers from office of Commission or distribution thereof - Single Judge and DB of HC failed to take cognizance of admission made by Chief Invigilator that on a request made by him - HC also did not pay due weightage to fact that appellant had not handled question papers at any stage and he had no role in distribution of wrong question papers to 6 candidates - Appellant had no role to play in matter of mixing of question papers of afternoon examination with morning examination - Before Enquiry Officer, no evidence was produced by Commission to prove that appellant had custody of question papers - Therefore, appellant could not be blamed for distribution of wrong question papers to candidates or so-called leakage of question papers - Order of punishment passed by Secretary of Commission and appellate order passed by Chairman of Commission were quashed - Appellant should be entitled to all consequential benefits including arrears of salary for period during which he was kept out of employment - Appeal allowed.
