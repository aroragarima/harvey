Hukumchand Mills Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Central Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Vishishtha Bhargava, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1967 SC 455; 2001 (167) CTR 280; [1967] 63 ITR 232; [1967] 1 S.C.R. 463

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Taxes & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922 - Assessment years - Depreciation - Computation of taxable income - Jurisdiction - Assessee was assessed in determination in assessments for years was proper written down value of buildings, machinery etc. of assessee for calculating depreciation allowance u/s. 10(2)(vi) of 1922 Act - Assessee contended that original cost of machinery, buildings etc. should be taken for assessment purpose - No depreciation allowed under Act, assessee contended that original cost should be taken as basis of allowing depreciation without taking into consideration number of years during which machinery working or depreciation suffered or written down value entered in books - Income Tax Officer and Appellate Assistant Commissioner rejected contention of assessee - Tribunal ordered that only part of depreciation entered into computation of taxable income of assessee under 192 Act could be treated as depreciation 'actually allowed' and not total depreciation went into computation of total income - Whether any depreciation was allowed under Industrial Tax Rules and whether such depreciation should be taken into account for purpose of computing written down value - Held, proper written down value of buildings, machinery etc. of assessee for calculating depreciation allowance u/s. 10(2)(vi) of 1922 Act - RR. 12 and 28 are not strictly applicable and Tribunal got sufficient power u/s. 33(4) of 1922 Act with regard to application of Taxation Laws Order and remand case to Income Tax Officer - RR. 12 and 28 are not exhaustive of powers of Appellate Tribunal - Powers of Tribunal expressed in s. 33(3) and (4) of 1922 Act, states appeal to Appellate Tribunal shall be in prescribed form and shall be verified in prescribed manner - Rules are merely procedural in character and not in any way circumscribe or control power of Tribunal u/s. 33(4) of 1922 Act - Tribunal had jurisdiction to entertain and to direct Income Tax Officer to find any depreciation actually allowed under Industrial Tax Rules and taken into consideration for purpose of computing written down value - Appeal dismissed.
