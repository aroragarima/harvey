Guru Granth Saheb Sthan Meerghat Vanaras v Ved Prakash and others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 278; (2013) 7 SCC 622; AIR 2013 SC 2024; 2013 (99) ALR 32; 2013 (3) Bom.C.R. 887; 2013 (3) CGLJ 142; 2013 (3) CTC 667; 2013 (3) JabLJ 1; JT 2013 (8) SC 252; 2013 (2) KLT 692; 2013 (2) OLR 562; 2013 (171) PLR 827; 2013 (2) RCR(Civil) 924; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 947; 2013 (120) RD 767; 2013(6) SCALE 576; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 1042

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 10, 151 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 420, 467, 468 - Proceedings in civil suit - Stayed - Decision of criminal case - Maintainability - Appellant lodged an FIR against respondent nos. 1 to 4 for commission of offences u/ss. 420, 467, 468 and 120B IPC alleging that they had executed a false, forged and fabricated Will in name of X with intention to grab his property and also obtained a mutation order from Tehsildar - Appellant filed suit against respondent nos. 1 to 4 praying for declaration of title, perpetual injunction and possession in respect of disputed lands and for annulling sale deed and mutation order and Will forged by respondent nos. 1 to 4 -Respondent nos. 1 to 4 filed an application u/s. 10 r/w s. 151 CPC for staying proceedings in civil suit during pendency of criminal case - Trial Court dismissed application - Respondent nos. 1 to 4 filed writ petition challenging impugned order of Trial Court in HC - DB by impugned order had set aside order of Trial Court and had stayed proceedings in Civil Suit till decision of criminal case - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in staying proceedings in civil suit till decision in criminal case - Held, HC was not justified in staying proceedings in civil suit till decision of criminal case - Firstly, because even if there was possibility of conflicting decisions in civil and criminal Courts, such an eventuality could not be taken as a relevant consideration - Secondly, in instant case there was no likelihood of any embarrassment to respondent nos. 1 to 4 as they had already filed written statement in civil suit and based on pleadings of parties issues were framed - Hence, findings that might be arrived at by civil Court would not at all prejudice defence(s) of respondent nos. 1 to 4 in criminal proceedings - Impugned order passed by DB was set aside - Appeal allowed.
