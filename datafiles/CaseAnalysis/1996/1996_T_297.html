U. P. State Road Transport Corporation and others v Trilok Chandra and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.K. Mukherjee, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2879; (1996) 4 SCC 362; 1996 (1) ACC 592; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 587; 1996 (3) AWC 1489; 1996 (2) CCC 344; JT 1996 (5) SC 356; 1996(4) SCALE 522; 1996 (2) SCJ 447; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 443; 1996 (4) Supreme 479; 1996 (2) UJ 292

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Future Pecuniary Benefit

Summary: Civil Procedure - Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Fatal accident - Compensation - Life expectancy - Multiplier - Future pecuniary benefit - Victim, aged 26 years, had met with a fatal accident - Victim was knocked down by an omnibus which belonged to U.P. State Road Transport Corporation - Victim's legal representatives preferred a claim for compensation and taking his earning capacity at Rs.300/- per month, it was estimated that the victim spent Rs.200/- per month on his family members - Victims life expectancy was fixed to be 60 years, thereby the Tribunal deducted 36 years and held that the family was deprived of his earning for 24 years - Thus, the compensation was worked out at Rs.57,600/- (200x12x24) - This amount was raised to Rs.81,600/- as it was realised that the Tribunal had wrongly taken the age of the victim at 36 instead of 26 years and therefore, committed an error in employing the multiplier of 24 years purchase factor instead of 34 years purchase factor - Thus, the compensation was 81,600/- - HC had fixed the compensation at Rs.57,600/- - Hence the instant appeal by the State against the method and multiplier employed by Tribunal for calculating the compensation and for reduction in the amount of compensation - Whether the Tribunal was right in employing the multiplier of 24 or the HC was right in employing the multiplier of 34 and whether the quantum of compensation was to be reduced - Held, the most commonly practiced method of assessing the losses suffered was to calculate the loss for a year and then to capitalize the amount by a suitable multiplier - Further loss suffered on account loss of expectation of life was added - Also, by the amendment of 1994 to the Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 compensation was to be determined u/ss. 163A and 163B of the 1994 amendment Act - The 1994 amendment provided for payment of compensation to the legal representatives of the victim or injured by fixing the mode of calculating compensation for third party accident and injury claims arising out of fatal accidents - Amount of compensation was calculated on the basis of the age group of the victims of accident and the multiplier fixed by the 1994 amendment for each age group - Quantum of compensation in thousand payable to the heirs of the deceased victim was also indicated on the basis of the multiplier - Hence, as per ss. 163A and 163B of the 1994 Amendment, the Tribunal/Court fell into an error in the choice of the multiplier, but the multiplier used was excessive and the multiplicand used was very low for calculating the loss of dependency - If corrections were made to the multiplicand and to the use of the correct multiplier, the compensation would amount to the same figure - Hence, compensation awarded by the Tribunal was not changed - Appeal partly allowed to the extent that the Tribunal had erred in employing the correct multiplier - Appeal partly allowed.
