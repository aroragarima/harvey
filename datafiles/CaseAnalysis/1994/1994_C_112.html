Tata Iron and Steel Company Limited v Collector of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1848; (1995) 1 SCC 323; 1995 (56) ECR 643; 1995 (75) E.L.T. 3; JT 1995 (1) SC 172; 1994(5) SCALE 301; [1994] Supp6 S.C.R. 648

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Indirect Tax

Summary: Customs - Indirect Tax - Central Excise and Salt Act, 1944 - Whether scrap obtained by appellant in course of manufacture of iron and steel and steel products was dutiable under Item 26 or 26AA of Tariff Schedule? - Under Entry 26AA what is exigible to duty is semi-finished steel including blooms, billets, slabs, sheet bars etc., semi finished cannot be described as scrap - In commercial sense, scrap and semi-finished products cannot be understood in same sense - Held, attempt of Department to levy duty on scrap under Item 26AA was not correct - Scrap cleared by appellant in each year having been melted and re-used as iron ingot was remelting scrap dutiable under Item 26 of the Tariff Schedule - Appeal allowed.
