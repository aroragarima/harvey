The Rajasthan State Industrial Development And Investment Corporation & Anr. v Diamond And Gem Development Corporation Ltd. & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 84; (2013) 5 SCC 427; (2013) 5 SCC 470; AIR 2013 SC 1241; JT 2013 (3) SC 409; 2013 (2) MLJ 756; 2013 (120) RD 144; 2013(2) SCALE 452; [2013] 4 S.C.R. 331

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Rajasthan Land Revenue (Industrial Area Allotment) Rules, 1959, Rajasthan State Industrial & Investment Corporation Limited (Disposal of Land) Rules, 1979

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, s. 4(1) - Rajasthan Land Revenue (Industrial area Allotment) Rules, 1959, r. 11-A - Rajasthan State Industrial & Investment Corporation Limited (Disposal of Land) Rules, 1979 - Allotment of land - Execution of lease deed - Cancellation of lease deed - Challenged - Land admeasuring 607 Bighas and 5 Biswas situated in revenue estate of villages stood notified u/s. 4(1) of the Act, for public purpose i.e. industrial development, to be executed by appellant Rajasthan State Industrial Development and Investment Corporation (RIICO) - Possession of land was taken over by Govt. and was subsequently handed over to appellant-RIICO - However, RIICO made allotment of land admeasuring 105 acres to respondent no.1 company, to facilitate establishment of a Gem Industrial Estate for the manufacturing of Gem stones - Lease deed was executed between appellant and respondent-company and respondent should complete said project within a period of 5 years - Appellant issued a show cause notice, to determine lease in light of lease agreement, in lieu of fact that respondent-company had not made any progress regarding completion of project, and even after expiry of period of 5 years, only 10% of total construction stood completed - Lease deed was cancelled by order dt. 1-10-1996 and possession of land in dispute was taken back by appellant on 3-10-1996 - Respondent-company filed writ petition, challenging cancellation order dt. 1-10-1996 and taking over of possession by appellant on 3-10-1996 - HC vide impugned judgment quashed order of cancellation, and directed restoration of possession of aforesaid land to respondent, and further, also directed appellant to provide to respondent, approach/access road demanded by it - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in quashing order of cancellation of deed, and directed restoration of possession of aforesaid land to respondent - Held, State exercised its power in transferring land to RIICO under the 1959 Rules - However, further allotment by RIICO to respondent was under the 1979 Rules - Therefore, HC committed an error treating that whole case was governed only under the 1959 Rules, and that the 1979 Rules had no application at all - R. 11-A of the 1959 Rules, as amended created a legal fiction by which respondent had become a lessee and State, lessor and the order passed by appellant-RIICO, was wholly without jurisdiction - RIICO had no authority to cancel allotment of land made in favour of respondent, since it was only State that had authority to cancel said allotment - HC also erred in holding that provision of providing access road was an obligation on part of appellant-RIICO, deciding this on equitable grounds - Terms of lease deed clearly stipulated that in case appellant-RIICO provided the access road, it would be vested with right to collect charges incurred by it from respondent - However, only 10% of total construction work stood completed by respondent-company - No proper application was ever filed for seeking extension of time by respondent, as per the Rules - However, cl. 2(d) of lease deed entire project was to be completed within a period of 5 years - But it was evident that construction was just made on fraction of entire land - Impugned order of HC was set aside and order of cancellation of allotment in favour of respondent company by appellant was restored - Appeals allowed.
