Sahodra Bai Rai v Ram Singh Aharwar

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, R.S. Bachawat, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 247; AIR 1968 SC 1079; 1968 ALJ 782; 1968 ALJ 1079; 1968 BLJR 810; 1968 JabLJ 877; 1968 MahLJ 697; 1968 MPLJ 713; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 13

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Election - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81 and 83 - Whether election petition is liable to be dismissed for contravention of s. 81(3) of Representation of People Act, 1951? - As copy of Annexure A to petition was not given along with petition for being served on respondents - Since election petition itself reproduced whole of pamphlet in a translation in English, it could be said that averments with regard to pamphlet were themselves a part of petition and therefore, pamphlet was served upon respondents although in a translation and not in original documents which are merely evidence in case but which for reasons of clarity, and to lend force to petition are not kept back but produced or filed with election petitions - They are, in no sense, an integral part of averments of petition but are only evidence of those averments and in proof thereof - Pamphlet therefore must be treated as a document and not as a part of election petition in so far as averments are concerned - It would be stretching words of sub-s. (2) of s. 83 too far to think that every document produced as evidence in election petition becomes a part of election petition proper - Held, whether or not s. 86(1) is mandatory or directory there was no breach of provisions of Representation of People Act in regard to filing of election or service of copies thereof and order under appeal was therefore erroneous - Appeal allowed.
