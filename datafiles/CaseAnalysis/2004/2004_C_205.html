Commissioner of Endowments and Others v Vittal Rao and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1548; (2005) 4 SCC 120; AIR 2005 SC 454; 2005 (2) ALD(SC) 112; 2005 (2) AWC 1984; JT 2004 (10) SC 113; 2004(9) SCALE 660; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 374; 2005 (1) UC 379

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Andra Pradesh Writ Proceedings Rules, 1977

Summary: Land & Property - Trusts & Associations - Civil Procedure - Andhra Pradesh Charitable and Hindu Religious Institutions and Endowments Act, 1987, ss. 14, 16, 42, 80(1), 87 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 34(3) - Indian Succession Act, 1925, s. 373 - Andhra Pradesh (Telangana Areas) Wakf Rules, r. 156 - Trusteeship - Dispute - Compromise - Implementation - Sustainability - Father of respondent no. 1 filed suit for declaration that father of respondent no. 1 was hereditary Mutawalli of temple and for perpetual injunction against authorities and individuals, restraining them from interfering with his Towliatship and from constituting or reconstituting any committee for temple - Trial Court dismissed suit - Appeal against judgment of Trial Court was allowed Appellate Court - Temple Committee filed second appeal in HC, which was dismissed - SLPs filed by State and Temple Committee were also dismissed - Thereafter, on 25-6-1990, Commissioner of Endowments appointed Executive Officer for management of temple - Respondent no. 1 filed writ petition in HC to quash order of Commissioner of Endowments - Meanwhile, Officers of Endowment Department filed writ petition seeking direction to appoint Executive Officer to manage affairs of temple - Single Judge disposed of writ petition by directing implementation of terms of compromise contained in Govt. Memorandum dt. 27-10-1989 within four weeks from date of order - Private respondents nos. 5 to 8, who were impleaded subsequently in writ petition, contending to be purchasers of land, filed writ appeal, which was not allowed - Subsequently, State filed review petition contending that despite permission granted to Commissioner to enter into compromise by virtue of Government Memorandum dt. 27-10-1989, no compromise was in fact entered into and hence, it was unenforceable and that proposal for compromise was wrongly interpreted in earlier judgments - DB dismissed review petition - Commissioner of Endowments filed SLP against judgment in review petition, which was dismissed - Subsequently, appellants filed appeal before HC, which was also dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether compromise agreed by appellants and respondent no.1 in handing over trusteeship to respondent no. 1 was sustainable - Held, consent order passed in writ petition was not contrary to provisions of the 1987 Act - O. 23 r. 3 CPC should not be strictly applied to proceedings u/art. 226 of Constitution - Decisions and findings recorded in first round of litigation did operate as res judicata against appellants - Gift deed, which was interpreted by Appellate Court and same interpretation was accepted by HC holding that it conveyed absolute gift of land in favour of ancestors of respondent no. 1, temple was private property and that land was not endowed property - Single Judge disposed of writ petition by consent of parties accepting compromise memo dt. 27-10-1989, however, no appeal against judgment was filed by appellants - Memorandum was issued by State at instance of Endowment Commissioner and same was accepted by respondent no. 1, though not initially, however, during pendency of writ petition in HC - It would not be correct to say that terms of O. 23 r. 3 should be mandatorily complied with while HC exercising jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution - DB was right in not disturbing order of Single Judge accepting compromise as represented by Counsel for parties - Hence, there was no valid reason to interfere with the impugned judgment of DB - Appeal dismissed.
