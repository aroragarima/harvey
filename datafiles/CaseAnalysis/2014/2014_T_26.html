Secretary to Government, School Education Department, Chennai and others v Thiru R. Govindaswamy and others

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 110; (2014) 4 SCC 769; 2014(2) ALL MR 935; 2014 (4) AWC 3936; 2014 (141) FLR 538; JT 2014 (4) SC 451; 2014 (2) LLJ 1; 2014(3) SCALE 34; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 84; 2014 (5) SLR 353; 2014 (1) UPLBEC 608; 2014 (2) WLN(SC) 66

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Regularization - Entitlement of - Respondents were appointed as part-time sweepers by appellant from 1987 till 1993 as their initial appointments were issued to the respondents and others on 1-12-1987, 2-5-1991, 1-4-1993, 10-4-1993, 27-5-1999 and 19-1-2001 - As the respondents and others were working for more than 10 years, they filed Writ Petition before HC for seeking regularisation of their services - Said Writ Petitions were allowed by the common judgment and order with the direction to regularise the services of the respondents on full time basis based on the individual representation after verifying their service particulars from the date of completion of 10 years of service with time scale of pay - hence instant appeals - Appellant had submitted that a direction to regularise the part-time employees itself was contrary to law and the said direction could not have been issued - It further submitted that as the impugned judgments and orders had been complied with and the appellant was not going to disturb any of the respondents and others, the law should be clarified on the issue so that in future the HC might not use the impugned judgment as a precedent -

Held, SC in one case had considered the scope of regularisation of irregular or part-time appointments in all possible eventualities and laid down well-settled principles relating to regularisation and parity in pay relevant in the context of the issues involved therein - Appeals were squarely covered by cls. (ii), (iv) and (v) of judgment - Therefore, the appeals were allowed - However, in light of the facts and circumstances of the case as appellant had submitted that the appellant had already implemented the impugned judgments and did not want to disturb the services of the respondents, the services of the respondents which stood regularised should not be affected - State of Rajasthan & Ors. v. Daya Lal & Ors., 2011 Indlaw SC 37, relied on - Appeals disposed of.
