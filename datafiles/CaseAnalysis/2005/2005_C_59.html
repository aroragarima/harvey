State Of Haryana And Others v Charanjit Singh And Others., Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1245; (2006) 9 SCC 321; AIR 2006 SC 161; JT 2005 (12) SC 475; 2005(8) SCALE 482; 2005 (6) SLR 693; [2005] Supp4 S.C.R. 57

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Principle of 'Equal pay for equal work' - Applicability of - Respondents, daily wagers, claimed the minimum wages payable under the pay-scale of regular Class IV employees from the date of their appointments - Question whether or not respondents were entitled to the minimum pay-scale of a regular Class IV employee was referred to a Full Bench for consideration - Following the Full Bench decision, respondents were directed to be given the minimum wages in the scale payable to a regular Class IV employee from the date of the filing of the respective petition - Appeal - Respondents relied on SC decision in Surinder Singh & Anr. vs. Engineer-in-Chief, CPWD & Ors. 1986 INDLAW SC 780 wherein the daily wagers were given salary and allowances which were payable to the regular employees from the date on which they were employed but in series of other decisions rendered by SC in State of Haryana & Ors. vs. Jasmer Singh & Ors. 1996 INDLAW SC 1679 it was held that the daily rated workmen who were to be paid minimum wages admissible to such workmen as prescribed and not in the pay scale applicable to similar employees working on regular service - In view of conflict of decision, matter referred to three judge bench - Held, doctrine of 'equal pay for equal work' is not an abstract doctrine and is capable of being enforced in a Court of law, but equal pay must be for equal work of equal value - Principle of 'equal pay for equal work' has no mechanical application in every case - Art. 14 permits reasonable classification based on qualities or characteristics of persons recruited and grouped together, as against those who were left out - Application of the principle of 'equal pay for equal work' requires consideration of various dimensions of a given job - Classification based on difference in educational qualifications justifies a difference in pay scales - Party who claims equal pay for equal work has to make necessary averments and prove that all things are equal - In each case the Court must satisfy itself that the burden of proving that the work and conditions are equal is discharged by the aggrieved employee - Thus, before any direction can be issued by a Court, the Court must first see that there are necessary averments and there is a proof - However, in all these cases, HC blindly proceeded on the basis that the doctrine of equal pay for equal work applies without examining any relevant factors - All the impugned Judgments set aside - Authorities in the cases of Jasmer Singh, State of Haryana vs. Tilak Raj 2003 INDLAW SC 512, Orissa University of Agriculture & Technology vs. Manoj K. Mohanty 2003 INDLAW SC 348 and Government of West Bengal vs. Tarun K. Roy 2003 INDLAW SC 1039 lay down the correct law - Matters remitted back to the HC - Appeals disposed of.
