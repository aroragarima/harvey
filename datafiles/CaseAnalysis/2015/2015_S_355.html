Ravindra v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 154; (2015) 4 SCC 491; AIR 2015 SC 1369; 2015 (2) CGLJ 142; 2015 CRLJ 1943; 2016 (1) JabLJ 123; 2015 (2) JCC 1152; JT 2015 (2) SC 521; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1569; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 731; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 124; 2015(2) SCALE 693; [2015] 2 S.C.R. 860

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Conviction And Sentence, Improbable, Adequate and special reasons, Lesser sentence

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376(1) - Conviction and sentence - Lesser sentence - Adequate and special reasons - Appellant accused allegedly committed rape on complainant prosecutrix - Accused was charged for offence punishable u/s. 376 IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused - Appeal before HC - HC upheld the conviction and sentence of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Whether the case falls under proviso to s. 376 IPC to award lesser sentence for 'adequate and special reason'.

Held, medical examination was done on the same day on which the alleged offence was committed, and going by medical examination report and statement of PW3, it is improbable that rape was committed. Chemical Examiner report found that sample of semen was not sufficient to link the same to accused, notwithstanding that absence of spermatozoa on vaginal smear could not be allowed to tell against the version of the prosecutrix as held in Narayanamma v State Of Karnataka 1994 Indlaw SC 480. Instant case requires corroboration as it has minor contradictions and is not corroborated by other prosecution witnesses. The two maternal uncles PW4 and PW5 of the prosecutrix did not support her and were declared hostile. Incident took place 20 years ago and now with passage of time both victim and accused are married (not to each other) and they have entered into compromise. Appellant contended that this is an adequate and special reason for awarding lesser sentence. Thus, case of the appellant is a fit case for invoking the proviso to s. 376(2)(g) IPC for awarding lesser sentence, as the incident is 20 years old and the fact that parties are married and have entered into compromise, are the adequate and special reasons. Therefore, although conviction of appellant is upheld, however the sentence is reduced to the period already undergone by appellant. Appeal disposed of.
