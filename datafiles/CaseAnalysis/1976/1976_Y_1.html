Y.B. Patil and Others v Y.L. Patil

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, N.L. Untwalia, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 177; (1976) 4 SCC 66; AIR 1977 SC 392; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 320

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908,s.11 - Res judicata - Applicability in subsequent stage of same proceeding - HC held, finding of fact that appellants were strangers to watan was not to be reopened by HC - Held, it is well settled that principle of res judicata can be invoked not only in separate subsequent proceedings, but also in subsequent stage of same proceedings - Once an order made in course of a proceeding becomes final, it would be binding at subsequent stage of that proceeding - Further held, HC at time of decision of earlier writ petition recorded a finding and gave directions to Tribunal not to reopen questions of fact in revision; which Tribunal complied with - Appellants are bound by HC judgment and it is not open to them to go behind that judgment in this appeal - No appeal wag filed against that judgment and it has become final - Appeal dismissed.
