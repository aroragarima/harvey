K. L. Bakolia v State Through Director, C.B.I.

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 377; (2015) 8 SCC 395; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 2471; 2015 (3) CGLJ 228; JT 2015 (5) SC 241; 2015 (3) MLJ(Crl) 126; 2015(6) SCALE 547; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 201

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 7 - Demand of Bribe - Conviction - Justification - Complainant PW4 under contract provided security service on annual basis - Further, for renewal of contract, PW 4 contacted appellant who demanded bribe - Complainant informed same to CBI and raiding party was constituted and pre-trap proceedings were conducted - Further, appellant was got red handed by CBI officer - Charge sheet was filed against appellant u/s. 7 and s. 13 (2) read with s. 13 (1) (d) of Act - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 7 , 13(2) read with s. 13(1)(d) of Act which was affirmed by HC - Whether conviction of appellant by lower Court is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, courts below recorded concurrent findings that there was evidence on record to substantiate fact that there was demand and complainant paid bribe amount to appellant who has accepted same. Courts below also recorded concurrent findings that there is no reason to discredit testimony of complainant PW4 and Inspector of Police PW 7. SC held that incident had taken place in year 1996 about nineteen years ago and for all these years appellant has undergone agony of criminal proceedings. Keeping in view passage of time and that appellant is now aged seventy four years, while upholding conviction of appellant, interest of justice would be met by reducing sentence of rigorous imprisonment of four years to one year rigorous imprisonment. Hence, order passed by lower Court is modified. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Order of sentence passed against accused can be reduced by Courts taking into consideration age of accused, time when offence was committed and nature of offence.
