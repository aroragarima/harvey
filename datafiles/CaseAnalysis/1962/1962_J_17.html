Jitmal Bhuramal v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bihar and Orissa

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, S.K. Das, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 332; [1962] 44 ITR 887

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(2)(Xv) - Assessee was the HUF - HUF was doing business, under two names, Jitmal Bhuramal and Bhuramal Hiralal and through its karta (Hiralal) was also a partner in another firm called Hiralal Gulzarilal - Tribunal in the instant case did not allowed the deduction of the amounts paid to Gulzarilal and Madanlal was not allowed - Reference made in that favor to the HC was decided against the assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether, in the facts and circumstances of the case, the assessee, namely, the HUF, who was a partner in the partnership business of Hiralal Gulzarilal, was entitled to a deduction from the assessment of income-tax for the relevant year u/s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - Held, HUF was allowed to deduct salaries paid to members of the family, if the payment was made as a matter of commercial or business expediency, but the service should be to the family - In the instant case it was decided that the junior members of the coparcenary were serving the assessee which means they were serving an entity, which was separate and distinct from the HUF and since, the coparcenary had no place in the partnership, any service to the partnership could not be described as service to the HUF, it was sufficient to attract the application of s. 10(2)(xv) of the Act - And hence, no deduction was allowed - Appeal dismissed.
