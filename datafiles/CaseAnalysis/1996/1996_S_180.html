Meerut Development Authority and Others v Satbir Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Faizan Uddin, G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2440; (1996) 11 SCC 462; 1996 (8) AD(SC) 164; AIR 1997 SC 1467; JT 1996 (9) SC 382; 1996(7) SCALE 470; [1996] Supp6 S.C.R. 529; 1996 (7) Supreme 669; 1997 (1) UJ 29

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition Officer, Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973, Land Acquisition (U.P. Amendment And Validation) Act, 1991, Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 2002

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition (Amendment) Act, 2002 - Land Acquisition (U.P. Amendment And Validation) Act, 1991 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, Ss. 3, 4 [1], 6, 17 [4] - Prevention of Food Adulteration Act, 1954 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Planning and Development Act, 1973 - Notification - Validity - Exercise of Power - Declarations - Notification u/s. 4 [1] of the Act, 1894 was published u/s. 17 [4] of Act - Respondents' petitions challenging validity of notification u/s. 4 [1] and declaration u/s. 6 of the Act were not allowed - HC concluded same as not sustainable, but declaration u/s. 6 of the Act was quashed on ground that publication of declaration along notification was invalid in law after Amendment Act 68 of 1984 - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant/State after having Amendment received assent of President, amendment by proviso to S. 17(4) was invalid - Held, proviso apply not only to notifications and declarations published after Amendment Act 68 of 1984 came into force, but also to future declarations - Proviso would operate prospectively and retrospectively applying to previous notifications and declarations - After appellant/State Amendment was reserved and received assent of President, amendment by proviso to S. 17(4) of the Act was not invalid - S. 3 of Amendment Act 5 of 1991 sought to validate illegal declarations made with publication of notification - Amendment was not valid in law and exercise of power was arbitrary - Appeals of claimants/respondents dismissed - Appeal disposed of.
