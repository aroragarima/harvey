Supreme Court of India

01 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 442; (2012) 6 SCC 228; AIR 2012 SC 1890; JT 2012 (4) SC 510; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 380; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 818; [2012] 5 S.C.R. 599

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, Armed Forces (Jammu and Kashmir) Special Powers Act, 1990, Income Tax Act, Sales Tax Act

Summary: Criminal - Ranbir Penal Code, 1989, s. 307 - Arms Act, 1959, ss. 7 and 25 - Code of Criminal Procedure, s. 561A - Armed Forces J & K (Special Powers) Act, 1990, s. 7 - Attempt to murder - Charge sheet - Prayer for not entertaining charge sheet - Sustainability - Appellant committed offence of attempt to murder and was in illegal possession of arms which were used in violation of arms license - Appellant was apprehended by army - Appellant was charged u/ss. 307 of RPC along with ss. 7 and 25 of 1959 Act - CBI (Central Bureau of Investigation) filed charge sheet before Trial Court - On the other hand appellant field before Trial Court that act of army was unlawful - However, plea of appellant was turned down not only in Trial Court but also before HC - Thus, appeal was preferred against the impugned judgment and order passed by HC u/s. 561A of CrPC rejecting the appellant's application for not entertaining the charge sheet filed by CBI - Whether Trial whether trial would be by court-martial or criminal court - Held, conjoint reading of relevant statutory provisions and rules made it clear that term institution contained in s. 7 of 1990 Act meant taking cognizance of offence and not mere presentation of charge sheet by the investigating agency - Competent Army Authority had to exercise discretion to opt as to whether trial would be by court-martial or criminal court after filing of charge sheet and not after cognizance of offence was taken by Court - Facts of instant case required sanction of Central Govt. to proceed with criminal prosecution - Case option was made to try accused by court-martial and sanction of Central Govt. was not required - Competent authority was directed to take decision whether trial would be by criminal court or by court-martial and communicate to Trial Court concerned immediately thereafter and should conclude strictly in accordance with law - Appeals disposed of.
