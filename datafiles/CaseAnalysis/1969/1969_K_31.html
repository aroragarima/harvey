Raghunath and Others v Kedar Nath

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 412; (1969) 1 SCC 497; AIR 1969 SC 1316; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 4 and 54 - Registration Act, 1908 s. 49 - (A) Ex. 4 dated 27-07-1922 construed along with Ex. A 26 which forms part of same transaction - Whether such construction, make out the impugned transaction a usufructary mortgage or an outright sale? - Held, Ex. A 26 D/- 08-10-1922 is not a registered document, and is hence not admissible in evidence to prove nature of transaction covered by registered mortgage deed Ex. 4 D/- 27-07-1922 - If Ex. 4 is taken by itself, there is no doubt that transaction is one of mortgage - (B) Whether in order to avoid payment of Haqe-chaharum, original deed D/- 27-07-1922 was drafted and executed in form of a mortgage but it was actually meant to be an outright sale? - Held, Ex. A 26 was required to be registered u/s. 54 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - In absence of such registration this document cannot be received in evidence of any transaction affecting property in view of s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 - (C) Whether effect of s. 4 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 was not to make s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 applicable to documents which are compulsorily registrable by provisions of s. 54, para 2 of Transfer of Property Act, 1882? - Held, inserting in s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 words 'or by any provision of Transfer of Property Act, 1882 has made it clear that documents in supplemental list i.e. documents of which registration is necessary under Transfer of Property Act, 1882 but not under Registration Act, 1908 fall within scope of s. 49 of Registration Act, 1908 and if not registered are not admissible as evidence of any transaction affecting any immovable property comprised therein, and do not affect any such immovable property - Ex. A-26 being unregistered is not admissible in evidence - Appeals dismissed.
