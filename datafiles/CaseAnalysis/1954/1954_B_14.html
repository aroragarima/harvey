Â Bholanath J. Thaker v State of Saurashtra

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, Vivian Bose, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 198; AIR 1954 SC 680; 1955 (1) LLJ 355

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 363 - Dhara (Act) No. 29 of St. 2004 - Suit for compensation against Saurashtra State for premature retirement from service - Appellant was appointed officiating Sarnyayadhis in Wadhwan State on a monthly salary - Ruler of Wadhwan State Dhara (Act) No. 29 of St. 2004 which fixed superannuation age for State Civil servant at 60 and appellant thus became entitled to remain in service till he completed his age of 60 years - Dhara No. 29 of St. 2004 gave appellant certain statutory rights - When Wadhwan State merged with Saurashtra State and again when it acceded to Dominion of India all existing laws continued until repealed - Terms of Covenant showed that existing laws were to continue and whatever rights of appellant were under existing laws were available for enforcement to appellant and there was no bar to Municipal Courts entertaining a suit to enforce such rights - In this view of matter art. 363 of Constitution of India, 1950 could not be invoked - Held, if Saurashtra State chose to compulsorily retire appellant it could only do so on payment of reasonable compensation and in arriving at figure of reasonable compensation Saurashtra State would have to take into consideration tenure of Appellant's service with Wadhwan State which entitled him to continue in such service until he attained his age of 60 years - Appeal allowed.
