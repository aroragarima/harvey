Commissioner of Income Tax, Punjab, Himachal Pradesh and Jammu and Kashmir v Chander Bhan Harbhajan Lal

Supreme Court of India

04 January 1966

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1966 Indlaw SC 239; AIR 1966 SC 1490; [1966] 60 ITR 188; [1966] 3 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 ss. 26A, 66(1) - Partnership Firm - Individual Capacity - Assessment - Challenged - Respondent/assessee firm Rupar constituted under instrument of partnership dt. 5-12-1952 and was partner in another firm, name of Ferozepore firm consisting of 8 partners and constituted under deed dt. 14-6-1952, capital of assessee firm was supplied by other Ferozepore firm and applied to Income-tax Officer for registration of firm u/s. 26-A of the Act which rejected application u/s. 26-A of the Act - Respondent filed application before Appellate Assistant Commissioner which was set aside order of Income Tax Officer and held that one of partner was partner of assessee firm in his individual capacity and not as representative on behalf of all partners of Ferozepore firm - Appellant filed appeal before Income-tax Appellate Tribunal which upheld findings of Appellate Assistant Commissioner - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether any substantial question of law arises out of order of Tribunal - Held, quite plainly, relevant clause in deed dt. 14-6-1952 was not part of agreement of partnership dt. 5-12-1952 constituting assessee firm, and did not affect Tight of partners of assessee firm, to claim registration of assessee firm u/s. 26-A of the Act - It was not possible to say that there were no materials on record to support finding that Gosain Chander Bhan was partner of assessee firm in his individual capacity and not as representing Ferozepore firm - Therefore, question whether there was sub-partnership between members of Ferozepore firm in respect of share of Gosain Chander Bhan was not material because assuming that there was no sub-partnership members of Ferozepore firm did not become partners in the assessee firm by virtue of the relevant clause in deed dt. 14-6-1952 or otherwise - Court satisfied that no substantial question of law arises out of order of Appellate Tribunal -Thus, question of law was not substantial and answer to question was self-evident HC was not bound to require Tribunal to refer question - Hence, Court opined HC in exercise of its discretion u/s. 66(2) of the Act rightly rejected appellant's application - Appeals dismissed.
