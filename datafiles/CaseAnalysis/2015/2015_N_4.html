Union of India and others v N. S. Rathnam and Sons

Supreme Court of India

29 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 504; (2015) 10 SCC 681; AIR 2016 SC 1273; 2015 (322) E.L.T. 353; JT 2015 (6) SC 535; 2015 (6) MLJ 204; 2015(8) SCALE 301; [2015] 8 S.C.R. 751

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Duty Of Excise, Central Excise Rules, 1944, Exemption Notification, Andhra Pradesh State Entertainment Tax Act

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3 - Exemption to pay duty - Creation of different class - Violation of fundamental right - Respondent is engaged in business of ship breaking activities and it had imported foreign vessel for purpose of breaking it and selling it as scrap - Further, iron and steel scrap which was obtained by breaking ship was cleared by respondent on payment of central excise duty at rate of Rs.365/- per tonne as per notification dt. 1-3-1986 - Further, notification was issued on 27-3-1987 which pertain to same goods, however, vide first notification, if customs duty leviable on import of ship for purpose of breaking is paid at rate of Rs.1,035/- per LDT along with additional duty leviable thereon u/s. 3 of Act, excise duty payable is at rate of Rs.365/- per tonne, exempting remainder as specified in Schedule and as per other notification, if customs duty has been paid at rate of Rs.1400/- per LDT, scrap obtained from breaking of such ships is exempted from entire excise duty - Respondent had cleared goods without payment of any excise duty on assumption that there was exemption of payment of entire excise duty, appellant issued show cause notice for demand of excise duty - Respondent contended that notification which provided total exemption is arbitrary as it resulted in distinction between two categories of persons who have paid customs duty, viz. one set of persons who have paid customs duty at rate of Rs.1,400/- per LDT and second set of persons who have paid customs duty of lesser amount though as per s. 3 of Act - Lower Court dismissed petition, however, DB reversed order passed by Lower court and held that second category of persons shall also be entitled to benefit of notification in question - Whether impugned notification contravenes art. 14 of Constitution.

Held, two notifications both dt. 27-3-1987 pertain to same goods those falling under heading under Act, duty is leviable on these goods u/s. 3 of Act. Further, said duty can be paid under any of two methods. When two methods are permissible under statutory scheme itself, option is that of assessee to choose in all those methods to pay custom duty. Duty, thus, paid is to be naturally treated as validly paid. Merely because with adoption of one particular method duty that becomes payable is lesser would not mean that two such persons belong to different categories. SC held that important factors for purposes of parity are same in instant case, viz. goods are same; they fall under same Heading and custom duty is leviable as per Act which has been paid. Hence, impugned notification giving exemption only to those persons who paid particular amount of duty, namely Rs.1,400/- per LDT, would not mean that such persons belong to different category and would be entitled to exemption and not other persons like respondent herein who paid duty on same goods under same Act but on formula which he opted and which is permissible, which rate of duty comes to Rs.1,035/- per LDT. Further, no rational policy is mentioned for creating two different classes and no reply to this was given by appellants even in counter affidavit filed to petition. Hence, SC upheld view taken by HC, however modified same only to extent that respondent shall also be entitled to benefit of exemption Notification subject to condition that duty already paid by respondent on LDT, would be taken into account and only balance out of it would be subject to excise duty. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Creation of two different classes for providing benefit without any rational policy is violation of fundamental rights of citizens.
