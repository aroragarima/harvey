Anumati v Punjab National Bank

Supreme Court of India

25 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ruma Pal, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 912; (2004) 8 SCC 498; AIR 2005 SC 29; 2005 (58) ALR 323; 2005 (1) AWC 515; 2005 (1) BC 345; 2005 (2) Bom.C.R. 729; 2005 (11) CCC 329; [2005] 126 Comp Cas 322; 2005 (2) CompLJ 1; 2004 (4) CPJ(Sc) 21; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 58; JT 2004 (9) SC 250; 2005 (1) KLT 211; 2005 (139) PLR 385; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 742; 2004(9) SCALE 10; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 610; 2005 (1) UPLBEC 740

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Legal Notice, Banking Law

Summary: Consumer Protection - Banking and Finance - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Deficiency of service - Whether a fixed deposit jointly owned with an 'either or survivor' clause be pledged by one of account holders with Bank and whether Bank can adjust amount of fixed deposit against such pledge, without authority, knowledge or concurrence of other account holder? - Held, since receipt was in joint name of appellant and her husband, respondent Bank should not have accepted any pledge of account without informing appellant and getting her consent - Since it had not done so, service rendered by Bank to appellant was deficient - Bank had no right to refuse payment of amount deposited to appellant - Appeal allowed.
