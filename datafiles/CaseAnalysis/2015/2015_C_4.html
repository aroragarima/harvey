Central Bank of India and another v C. L. Vimla

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 307; (2015) 7 SCC 337; AIR 2015 SC 2280; 2015 (4) ALD(SC) 92; 2015(4) ALL MR 414; 2015 (111) ALR 228; 2016 (2) CalLT(SC) 9; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 227; 2016 (1) CTC 466; 2016 (1) DRTC 169; 2016 (1) MahLJ 134; 2015 (4) MLJ 489; 2016 (1) MPLJ 101; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 90; 2015 (128) RD 631; 2015 (4) RLW 3503; 2015(5) SCALE 615; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 200

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure

Keywords: Income Tax (Certificate Proceedings) Rules, 1962

Summary: Banking & Finance - Civil Procedure - Legal Services Authority Act, 1987,ss.21,25 -Indian Contract Act, 1872,s.128 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O.9 r.13, O.21 r.64 - Mortgage of Property - Non pay-ment of loan - Auction of property - Respondent No.1 a senior citizen was guarantor - Suit Property was mortgaged in favour of appellant/Central Bank of India for raising a loan for family business - Business suffered loss and respondents were unable to repay mortgage amount - Bank filed application before Debt Recovery Tribunal for settlement before Lok Adalat - Settlement was not within knowledge of guarantor - Writ petition filed by respondent No.1 before HC for setting aside award of Lok Adalat was dismissed on ground of latches - Respondent No.1 approached Lok Adalat by filing application under O. 9 r. 13 of CPC r/w ss. 21 and 25 of 1987 Act - During pendency of petition Recovery Officer conducted auction - HC concluded that auction was violative of O. 21 r. 64 of CPC - Hence instant appeal.

Held, liability of guarantor u/s. 128 of 1872 Act. Legislature has succinctly stated that liability of guarantor is co-extensive with that of principal debtor unless it is otherwise provided by contract. Only exception to nature of liability of guarantor is provided in Section itself, which is only if it stated explicitly to be otherwise in Contract. , equity and good conscience also has to play a role in matter in question on given facts and after considering conduct of respondents in matter. Auction purchaser had nothing to do in holding auction. Auction purchaser rather deposited money after bonafidely participating in auction and, in fact, suffered for long time to pay a price by participating in auction proceedings. Principal debtors were not prepared to pay back amount to Bank and did not choose to defend themselves properly. Conduct of principal debtors also cannot be overlooked by Court. Court set aside order passed by HC and hold that since auction purchaser has already paid full amount of sale consideration and is in possession of property in question for more than about 8 years, for equity and good con-science, and not to intend to interfere with his possession and set aside order passed by HC. Appeal allowed.
