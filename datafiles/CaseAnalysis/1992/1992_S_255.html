S. A. Khan v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 801; (1993) 2 SCC 327; (1993) SCC (L&S) 481; AIR 1993 SC 1152; 1993 (24) ATC 138; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 458; 1993 LabIC 450; 1993 (2) LLJ 273; 1993 (2) LLN 26; 1993 (1) SLR 399; [1992] Supp3 S.C.R. 749

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 32 - Indian Penal Code, 1856, ss. 34, 161, 165, 302 and 307 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Order of suspension - Allegation of CM misused his power an authority - Held, petitioner contended that suspension order has been passed in mala fide exercise of discretionary power for improper purpose, for simple reason that petitioner has been in charge of investigation of case of corruption registered against the CM and is patently unconstitutional and illegal besides being vitiated by malice both in law and facts and that it is in gross violation of art. 14 of Constitution as it has been passed by misuse of power in a very arbitrary and capricious manner - No force in argument that there is a gross violation of art. 14 of Constitution giving rise to filing of WP u/art. 32 of Constitution - Statutory remedy is available to petitioner against the order of suspension - Appeal dismissed.
