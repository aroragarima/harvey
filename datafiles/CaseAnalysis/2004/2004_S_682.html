Bankat and Another v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1016; (2005) SCC (Cr) 316; (2005) 1 SCC 343; AIR 2005 SC 368; 2005 CRLJ 646; JT 2004 (10) SC 471; 2005 (2) MahLJ 707; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 306; 2004(9) SCALE 679; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 325, 326, 34 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 320(9), 326 - Conviction and sentence - Appeal against conviction - Whether conviction sustainable?; whether offences are compoundable? - Held, Courts below concurrently found that accused persons were responsible for injuries on victims, therefore, in view of evidence of eye-witnesses, more particularly, injured witnesses there is no scope for interference with conclusions arrived at - Only offences which are covered by Table 1 or Table 2 can be compounded and rest of offences punishable under IPC could not be compounded - Appeal disposed of.
