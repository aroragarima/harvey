Killick Nixon Limited v Deputy Commissioner of Income Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Mrs. Justice Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1709; (2003) 1 SCC 145; AIR 2003 SC 312; 2002 (178) CTR 387; [2002] 258 ITR 627; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 47; 2002(8) SCALE 586; 2002 (7) SLT 18; 2004 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 866; [2002] Supp4 S.C.R. 348; 2002 (8) Supreme 334

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Capital Gains, Bad Debts, Foreign Travel, Business Income or Loss

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 142(1) and 143(3) - Whether High Court rightly dismissed writ petition u/art. 226 of Constitution of India, 1950 by which appellant challenged notice issued u/s. 142(1) of Income Tax Act, 1961? - Held, no demand notice/refund order could have been issued if assessment was not complete - If Assessing Officer had not completely assessed income after taking note of four issues remitted to him, there was no question of determining revised total income, much less was there any scope for issuing a demand notice/refund order at that stage - Order to be made by designated authority u/s. 90 is a considered order which is intended to be conducive in respect of tax arrears and sums payable after such determination towards full and final settlement of tax arrears - Once declarant makes payment of amount so determined u/s. 90, immunity u/s. 91 springs into effect - Upon such declaration being made, tax arrears being determined, paid and certificate issued under Kar Vivad Samadhan Scheme, there is no jurisdiction for Assessing Officer to reopen assessment by a notice u/s. 143 of Act except where case falls under proviso (2) of sub-section (1) of s. 90 as it is found that any material particular furnished in declaration is found to be false - In present case, it is not case of revenue that any material particular furnished by appellant-assessee in declaration was found to be false - Consequently, Assessing Officer could not have reopened assessment by a notice u/s. 143 of Act - High Court erred in both counts in dismissing writ petition - Appeal allowed.
