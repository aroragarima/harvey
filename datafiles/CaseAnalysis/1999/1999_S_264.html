Paparambaka Rosamma and Others v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

13 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1517; (1999) 7 SCC 695; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1361; AIR 1999 SC 3455; AIR 1999 SCW 3440; 1999 (37) ALR 353; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 44; 1999 CRLJ 4321; JT 1999 (6) SC 585; [1999] Supp2 S.C.R. 328

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 114, 302, 307 and 498 A - It is a case of circumstantial evidence, only circumstances relied upon by prosecution is dying declaration - Whether Magistrate could have come to a definite conclusion that injured was in a fit state of mind to make a declaration in absence of a certificate by doctor certifying state of mind that existed before recording dying declaration? - In medical science two stages namely conscious and a fit state of mind are distinct and are not synonymous - One may be conscious but not necessarily in a fit state of mind - This distinction was overlooked by courts below - Held, statements in dying declaration raise a reasonable doubt as to whether she was in a fit disposing state of mind at time when dying declaration was recorded - Statements in dying declaration are not sufficient to substantiate prosecution case that deceased was meted out with ill - treatment, an offence punishable u/s. 498 - Conviction and sentence quashed and set aside - Appeal allowed.
