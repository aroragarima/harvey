Sri Dokka Samuel v Dr. Jacob Lazarus Chelly

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.T. Nanavati, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3012; (1997) 4 SCC 478; 1997 (2) CLT 167; JT 1997 (4) SC 306; 1997(3) SCALE 335; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1137; 1997 (4) Supreme 643

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. XLVII - Respondents filed a suit for declaration that he had purchased two plots and for recovery of possession - Trial court dismissed suit - On appeal, it was decreed - In review application, Single Judge reversed decree of Appellate Court and confirmed that of Trial Court - Present Appeal - Whether High Court justified in reviewing earlier order and reversing finding recorded by Appellate Court? - Omission to cite an authority of law is not a ground for reviewing prior judgment saying that there is an error apparent on face of record - Held, respondent himself in an earlier suit had pleaded that it was agreement of sale, in view of such an admission, HC wrongly reversed decree of Appellate Court holding transaction to be a real sale - Further held, since respondent had claimed that it is not sale deed but was executed for collateral purpose, it was for respondent to establish that sale was for real consideration and he had valid sale deed duly executed by appellant - HC wrongly places burden on appellant and reviewed order and heard matter on merits - Entire approach of learned single judge is not correct in law - Impugned order of HC set aside - Appeal allowed.
