Dharangadhara Chemical Works Limited v State of Saurashtra

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, S.K. Das, P. Govinda Menon

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 96; AIR 1957 SC 264; 1957 (1) LLJ 477; 1957 SCJ 208; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(s) and 15 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133, 226 and 227 - Agaries working in salt works at Kuda in Rann of Cutch are workmen within meaning of term as defined in Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Whether u/s. 2(s) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 a person can be a workman even though he is paid not, per day but by job? - Essential condition of a person being a workman within terms of definition in s. 2(s), is that he should be employed to do work in industry - Prima facie test for determination of relationship between master and servant is existence of right in master to supervise and control work done by servant not only in matter of directing what work servant is to do but also manner in which he shall do his work, having regard to nature of work there was due control and supervision by employer - Held, a person can be a workman even though he is paid not, per day but by job - (B) Whether workman ceases to be one u/s. 2(s) of Industrial Disputes Act by fact that he gets other persons to work along with him and that those persons are controlled and paid by him? - Broad distinction between a workman and independent contractor lies in this that while former agrees himself to work, latter agrees to get other persons to work - Held, a person who agrees himself to work and does so work and is therefore, a workman does not cease to be such by reason merely of fact that he gets other persons to work along with him and that those persons are controlled and paid by him - (C) Whether High Court u/arts. 226 and 227 can interfere with fact finding of Industrial Tribunal? - Held, High Court, exercising its jurisdiction u/arts. 226 and 227 of Constitution, was not competent to set aside finding of fact recorded by Industrial Tribunal and Supreme Court entertaining an appeal from decision of High Court, should also not interfere with that finding of fact - Appeal dismissed.
