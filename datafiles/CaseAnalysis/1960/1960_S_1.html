Satyadhyan Ghosal and others v Deorajin Debi and another

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 413; AIR 1960 SC 941; 1962 (1) SCJ 268; [1960] 3 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale, Non Appealable Order

Summary: Land & Property - Calcutta Thika Tenancy (Amendment) Act, 1953, s. 105 (2) - Calcutta Thika Tenancy Act, 1949, s. 28 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Landlords who having obtained a decree for ejectment against tenants, respondents herein, have not yet been able to get possession in execution thereof - Whether a matter which is decided should always be treated as final in later stages of proceeding in a higher court which had not considered it at all merely on ground that no appeal lay or no appeal was preferred? - Held, s. 105(2) precludes an appellant from taking on an appeal from final decree, any objection that might have been urged by way of appeal from an order of remand - It is clear therefore that an interlocutory order which had not been appealed from either because no appeal lay or even though an appeal lay an appeal was not taken could be challenged in an appeal from final decree or order - If however an appeal did not lie from order of remand correctness thereof could be challenged by an appeal from final decision as in cases of other interlocutory orders - Second sub-section did not apply to Privy Council and can have no application to appeals to Supreme Court - There appears to be no reason therefore why appellant should be precluded from raising before this court question about applicability of s. 28 merely because he had not appealed from HC's order of remand - Relied on Mahadeolal Kanodia's Case ([1960] 3 S C R 5781960 (3) SCR 5781960 Indlaw SC 175) - S. 28 after its omission by Amending Act is not available in respect of proceedings pending on date of commencement of Thika Tenancy Ordinance of 1952 - Appeal allowed.
