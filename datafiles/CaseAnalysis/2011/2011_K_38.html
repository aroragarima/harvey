Orissa Power Transmission Corporation Ltd. v Khageswar Sundaray & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 489; (2011) 8 SCC 269; 2011 (131) FLR 66; JT 2011 (8) SC 618; 2011 (2) OLR 809; 2011(8) SCALE 629; [2011] 10 S.C.R. 196

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Advance increments - Entitlement - Appellant Electricity Board, by an office order, granted two advance increments to Lower Division Clerks (LDCs) on their becoming graduates while in service - However, Anomaly Committee recommended that such benefit of two advance increment should be confined to only those employees who had become graduates or pass Accounts Examinations on or before 30-6-1971 - Appellant Board accepted the recommendation of Anomaly Committee in a meeting held on 12-5-1973 and consequently passed the circular dated 16-7-1973 - Respondents who passed the graduate examination in the years 1974, 1975 and 1976 challenged the circular dated 16-7-1973 which confined the benefit of advance increments to employees who graduated or had passed the Accounts Examinations on or before 30-6-1971 - HC allowed respondents' writ petition and accordingly quashed the circular dated 16-7-1973 - Whether the respondents were entitled to the two advance increments - Held, if respondents desired to challenge the decision of the appellant Board as arbitrary and discriminatory, they should have placed sufficient materials before the court to demonstrate that the cut-off date of 30-6-1971 adopted by the appellant Board was arbitrary and discriminatory and that the decision of the appellant was in violation of art. 14 of the Constitution - In the impugned order, the HC had not referred to any such materials and had instead held that the proceedings of the meeting of the appellant Board did not disclose any reason, far less any justifiable reason, to confine the benefit of two advance increments to employees who graduated or passed the Accounts Examinations on or before 30-6-1971 - View taken by the HC that in the absence of any reason given by the decision of the appellant Board in its meeting held on 12-5-1973 to fix the cut-off date of 30-6-1971 for becoming a graduate or passing the Accounts Examinations for an employee to be entitled to the two advance increments, its decision was arbitrary and discriminatory was not sustainable in law - Appellant Board as the employer was fully with its powers to decide the cut-off date for the employees to become a graduate or passing the Accounts Examinations to be eligible to the two advance increments in the revised scales of pay and the decision of the appellant Board could not be held to be arbitrary only because the reason for decision was not stated in the proceedings of the meeting of the appellant in which the decision was taken - Impugned order of the HC, therefore, was set aside - Petition dismissed.
