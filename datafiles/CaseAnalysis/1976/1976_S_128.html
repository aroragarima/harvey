Parmeshwari Devi v State and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, P.N. Bhagwati, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 277; (1977) SCC (Cr) 74; (1977) 1 SCC 169; AIR 1977 SC 403; 1977 CRLJ 245; 1976 CrLR(SC) 553; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Rent Control

Summary: Criminal - Revision - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 94(1) - Complainant filed an application for a direction to appellant to file original deed of dissolution - Appellant not party to trial and no reason whatsoever was given in the application why the document was likely to be in her possession - Appellant took ground that documents were not in their possession - Appellant was directed to appear before court and produce documents - Appellant claimed that she was a pardanashin lady and may not be summoned but Magistrate dismissed her application - Whether order of Magistrate was unjustified? - No provision to provide that the person who appears in the court, in pursuance of its summons u/s. 94(1), thereby becomes a witness and can be examined and cross-examined by the court although he has not been cited as a witness in the proceedings - Held, yes - Magistrate could have issued search warrant if he had reason to believe that appellant was in possession of document - No provision to provide for recoding of appellant's statement on oath for her inability to produce document - Appeal allowed.
