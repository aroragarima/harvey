Gulabchand Bapalal Modi v Municipal Corporation of Ahmedabad City

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 695; (1971) 1 SCC 823; AIR 1971 SC 2100; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 942

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution, art. 14 - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, sch.A, chap. 8, rr. 10 and 19 - (A) Constitution - Absence of provision prescribing maximum rate in Act - Delegates power to the Corporation to levy property tax - Whether render such delegation arbitrary or excessive - (B) Municipalities - Bombay Provincial Municipal Corporation Act, 1949, sch.A, chap. 8, rr,19, 10 - (1) The r. 19 , gives discretion to Commissioner to maintain either one assessment book for whole city or ward assessment- books separately for each ward - (2) The r. 19 providing that entry made u/r. 9 (e) in respect of amount of tax shall be conclusive evidence - Applicability - Scope - Held, mere absence of provision prescribing maximum rate does not per se render such delegation in any manner arbitrary or excessive - Rule gives discretion to Commissioner to maintain either one assessment book for whole city or ward assessment books separately for each ward - Applies not only to ward assessment books but also to assessment book prepared for whole city - Appeal Dismissed.
