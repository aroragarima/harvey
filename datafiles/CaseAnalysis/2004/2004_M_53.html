Milkfood Ltd. v M/S Gmc Ice Cream (P) Ltd

Supreme Court of India

05 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 432; (2004) 7 SCC 288; AIR 2004 SC 3145; 2004 (1) ARBLR 613; 5 CLC 685; [2004] 121 Comp Cas 581; 2004 (92) CompLJ 16; 2004 (110) DLT 778; JT 2004 (4) SC 393; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 87; 2004 (2) RCR(Civil) 642; 2004(4) SCALE 291

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Domestic Award, Curial Law, Appeals Disposed Of, Foreign Awards, Fifteen Clear Days, Different Intention, Repeal And Saving, New York Convention Awards

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940; Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Whether 1940 Act or 1996 Act applicable - Appeals were pending before High Court under provisions of old Act and, therefore, appeals are required to be decided on basis of statutory provisions under said Act - Arbitrator was appointed prior to 21.8.1996, old Act was held to be applicable - Nowhere lays down that in a pending arbitral proceeding, which was being conducted as per procedure prescribed under old Act, parties have option of changing procedure - 1940 Act applicable
