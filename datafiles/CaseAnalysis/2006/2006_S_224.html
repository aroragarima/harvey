State Bank of India and Others v Ramesh Dinkar Punde

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

H.K. Sema, A.K. Mathur

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 415; (2006) 7 SCC 212; 2006 (6) AWC 6399; 2007 (2) Bom.C.R. 225; JT 2006 (7) SC 383; 2006(8) SCALE 11; 2006 (6) SCJ 225; 2007 (2) SLR 303; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 511; 2006 (3) UPLBEC 2472

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 309 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - State Bank of India (Supervising Staff) Service Rules, r. 32(4) - Penalty of removal - HC set aside removal with a direction to appellant to reinstate respondent with all consequential benefits including that of back wages, to be paid within a period of three months - Appeal against - Held, it is impermissible for HC to re-appreciate evidence which had been considered by Inquiry Officer, a Disciplinary Authority and Appellate Authority - Appeal allowed.
