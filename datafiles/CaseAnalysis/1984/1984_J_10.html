State v Jaspal Singh Gill

Supreme Court of India

25 June 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 163; (1984) SCC (Cr) 444; (1984) 3 SCC 555; AIR 1984 SC 1503; 1984 CRLJ 1211; 1984 CrLR(SC) 285; 1984(1) SCALE 990; [1984] 3 S.C.R. 993; 1984 (8) ShimLC 366; 1984 UJ 969

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 437 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B - Official Secrets Act, 1923, s. 3 - Bail - Grant of - The respondent passed classified information on defence matters to U.S. Intelligence Operator for monetary consideration - Held, the copy of Defence telephone directory was a restricted document, disclosure of contents of which to unauthorised persons is prejudicial to interest and security of country - Court before granting bail in cases involving non-bailable offences particularly where trial has not yet commenced should take into consideration various matters such as nature and seriousness of offence, character of evidence, circumstances which are peculiar to accused, a reasonable possibility of presence of accused not being secured at trial, reasonable apprehension of witnesses being tampered with, larger interests of public or State and similar other considerations - HC should not have enlarged respondent on bail in larger interests of state and directed him to remain in judicial custody until further orders - Order of the HC set aside - Appeal disposed of.
