P.G.F. Limited and others v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 888; 2013 (2) CompLJ 1; JT 2013 (4) SC 146; [2013] 6 S.C.R. 32

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Arrangement, Investment, Chit Funds Act, 1982, Securities And Exchange Board Of India (Mutual Funds) Regulations, 1996, Experts, Madhya Pradesh Nikshepakon Ke lliton Ka Sanrakshan Adhiniyam, 2000

Summary: Securities and Exchange Board of India - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act,1992, ss.2(ba),11AA - Business activity - Collective investment - Validity of provision - Respondent no.2 issued communication based on order of HC wherein appellant was directed to comply with said directions - Respondent no.2 by order of HC directed appellant to submit its reply to show cause notice and furnish all requisite information to respondent no.2 who directed to provide opportunity of personal hearing to appellant and also directed to be kept in abeyance, till final order was passed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellant business activities such as sale of agricultural land and sale and development of agricultural land, would fall within category of collective investment schemes as specified u/s.2(ba) r/w s.11AA of the Act and order can be sustained.

Held, appellant is not a financial institution at all and though it is company, was not receiving deposits under any scheme of arrangement. Since on very many occasions a challenge to a provision of law, as to its Constitutionality is raised with a view to thwart the applicability and rigour of those provisions and as an escape route from the applicability of those provisions of law and thereby create an impediment for the concerned authorities and the institutions who are to monitor those persons who seek such challenges by abusing the process of the Court. Paramount object of s.11AA of the Act was with a view to protect the gullible investors. When examine the nature of the activity of appellant by way of sale and development of agricultural land, it will be necessary to mention for the second respondent and find out as to how far s.11AA of the Act, would apply and thereby the validity of s.11AA of the Act has to be upheld and the various grounds raised on behalf of the PGF Limited do not in any way impinge upon the said Section. Further, the activity of sale and development of agricultural land propounded by the PGF Limited based on the terms contained in the application and the agreement signed by the customers is nothing but a scheme. Therefore, s.11AA of the Act is constitutionally valid. Thus activity of appellant, namely, the sale and development of agricultural land squarely falls within the definition of collective investment scheme u/s.2(ba) read along with s.11AA(ii) of the Act and consequently the order of the respondent no.2 is perfectly justified and there is no scope to interfere with the same. Appeal dismissed.
