State of Punjab v Khemi Ram

Supreme Court of India

06 October 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Shelat, Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam, I.D. Dua

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 384; (1969) 3 SCC 28; AIR 1970 SC 214; 1970 (21) FLR 138; 1970 LabIC 271; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 657; 1969 SLJ 833; 1969 SLR 833; 1969 UJ 721

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Service

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Society

Summary: Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, r. 3.26(d) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Punjab Civil Services (Punishment and Appeal) Rules, 1952, r. 3.26(d) - Service matter - (A) Meaning of word 'communicate' - Order of suspension passed against Govt. servant - Effective from, when - (B) Govt. servant on verge of retirement - Disciplinary action against him - Procedure - Held, takes effect from date of communication and not from date of actual receipt - It must be done before he retires as provided by r. 3.26 (d) and if a disciplinary enquiry cannot be concluded before the date of such retirement, the course open to the Govt. is to pass an order of suspension and refuse to permit the concerned public servant to retire and retain him in service till such enquiry is completed and a final order is passed therein - Appeal Allowed.
