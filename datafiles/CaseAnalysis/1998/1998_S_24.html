U. P. Jal Nigam v S. C. Atri and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 April 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, K. Venkataswami, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 142; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1733; (1999) 1 SCC 241; AIR 1999 SC 3362; 2000 (84) FLR 86; JT 1998 (9) SC 362; 1999 LabIC 1976; 2000 (1) LLJ 409; 1999 (5) SLT 87

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Jal Nigam Service of Engineers (Public Health Branch) Regulations, 1978, regn. 23(6) - Seniority - Computation of period from it reckons - Held, regn. 23(6) provides that inter se seniority of persons promoted to post of Superintending Engineer shall be same as in substantive post held by them at time of promotion - Only exception is contained in proviso to this regulation which says that if any person senior to others was not promoted on account of unsuitability and is promoted subsequently, he would be treated as junior to persons already promoted - On date on which respondent was considered along with other Executive Engineers for promotion to post of Superintending Engineer, there was an adverse entry for year relevant in his character roll on account of which he was not promoted although a representation against adverse entry was pending - This adverse entry was subsequently expunged as his representation was allowed - Effect of order allowing expunction of adverse entry would be that on date on which he was considered for promotion to post of Superintending Engineer, there existed no adverse entry in his character roll - Subsequently, it cannot be said that respondent was not promoted on account of his unsuitability - That being so, respondent on being promoted, though subsequently, shall be entitled to reckon his seniority with effect from date on which a person junior to him was promoted to post of Superintending Engineer - Appeal dismissed.
