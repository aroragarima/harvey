Delhi Cloth and General Mills v Additional Industrial Tribunal (Sri Rameshwar Dayal) and Another

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 28; AIR 1961 SC 689; 1961 (2) FLR 30; 1960 (2) LLJ 712; 1961 (1) SCJ 502; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 590

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33 and 33 A - Whether interim relief of reinstatement is proper by application u/s. 33 A of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 based on dismissal? - Held, in an inquiry u/s. 33 -A, employee would not succeed in obtaining an order of reinstatement merely by proving contravention of s. 33 by employer - Not open to tribunal to order reinstatement or full wages in case employer did not take back workman in his service, as an interim relief - Appeal allowed - Tribunal to reconsider applications.
