Ragu Thilak D. Jhon v S. Rayappan and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20331; (2001) 2 SCC 472; AIR 2001 SC 699; AIR 2001 SCW 342; 2001 (2) ALD(SC) 31; 2001 (1) AllCJ 507; 2001 (42) ALR 582; 2001 (1) AWC 622; 2001 (1) CGLJ 312; JT 2001 (2) SC 11; 2001 (1) RCR(Civil) 726; 2001(1) SCALE 354; 2001 (1) SLT 668; 2001 (1) Supreme 328; 2001 (1) UC 354

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 6, r. 17 - Application u/O. 6, r. 17 CPC, 1908 for amendment of plaint rejected by trail court and High Court that it would result in introducing a new case and cause of action - Correctness of order of HC challenged - Held, power to allow amendment is wide and can be exercised at any stage of proceedings in interests of justice therefore order of HC is not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
