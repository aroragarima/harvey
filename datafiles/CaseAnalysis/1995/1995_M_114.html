Â Union of India v Meera Mohideen

Supreme Court of India

14 April 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, S.C. Agrawal, S.P. Bharucha, K.S. Paripoornan, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 415

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Preventive Detention Act, 1950

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22 - Preventive detention - Representation by detenue - Jurisdiction - Detenues such as respondent indulged in illicit smuggling of narcotic drugs and psychotropic substances on large scale and were involved in other anti-national activities which were very harmful to national economy - Hence, were detained - Appellant urged that nature of activities of had not justified interference with orders of detention made against them - Appellant urged that representation envisaged by art. 22(5) of Constitution had to be made to Advisory Board referred to in art. 22(4) of Constitution since right that had been conferred on person detained was to have matter of his detention considered by Advisory Board - Hence instant appeal - Whether when order for preventive detention was passed by officer especially empowered to do so by Central Govt. or State Govt., was said officer required to consider representation submitted by respondent - Held, there was divergence in decisions of SC on question in issue - Question posed had to be considered in light of provisions that related to preventive detention contained in art. 22 of Constitution as well as provisions contained in relevant statutes - Art. 22 of Constitution prescribed protection for persons detained - Provision had same force and sanctity as any other provision that related to fundamental rights - Art. 22(5) of Constitution had not indicated authority to whom representation was to be made - Court held that representation was to be made to authority which had power to rescind or revoke decision, if need arose - Right to make representation carried with it corresponding obligation on authority that made order of detention to inform person detained of his right to make representation against order of detention to authorities who was required to consider such representation - Right of persons detained to make representation against order of detention comprehended right to make such representation to authority which could grant such relief - Enactments had not shown that role of officer came to end after he made detention order and that thereafter he ceased to be detaining authority and concerned Govt. assumed role of detaining authority - It was therefore held that officer specially empowered for purpose continued to be detaining authority and was not displaced by concerned Govt. after he made order of detention - Hence, by virtue of his being detaining authority he was required to consider representation of person detained against order of detention - Court considered consequences of detenues activities while it discharged its constitutional obligation to enforce fundamental rights especially right to personal liberty- As preventive detention involved serious encroachment on right to personal liberty, art. 22 contained safeguards for protection of persons sought to be preventively detained - Accordingly, respondent's contention was rejected - Appeal disposed of.
