Bhagat Ram and Another v Suresh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1393; (2003) 12 SCC 35; AIR 2004 SC 436; 2004 (54) ALR 594; 2004 (2) AWC 1179; 2004 (1) CHN 144; 2004 (98) CutLT 114; 2004 (2) JhrCR(SC) 150; 2004 (1) RCR(Civil) 285; 2004 (2) RD 47; 2003(10) SCALE 131; 2003 (1) SCW 6518; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Labour & Industrial Law; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 68 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882 - Mast Ram executed a will in name of Muni Devi - Codicil, which was attested by one witness and signed by Registrar, provided that his property was to remain in name of Muni Devi during her life time and after her death, it shall go to share of Bhagat Ram and Chajju Ram, appellants herein - Muni Devi, during her lifetime, sold property to respondents - (A) Whether formalities attaching with execution of Will need to be carried out in relation to a codicil also, and if so, whether a codicil is also required to be proved in same manner as a Will? - Held, codicil, though it may have been executed separately and at a place or time different from Will, forms part of related Will - That being nature and character of codicil, flowing from definition itself, it would be anomalous to accept contention that though a Will is required to be executed and proved as per rules contained in Succession Act and Evidence Act but a document explaining, altering or adding to Will not forming part of Will is not required to be executed or proved in same manner - (B) Whether a Registrar of Deeds can also be an attesting witness? - Further held, Registrar of Deeds who has registered a document in discharge of his statutory duty, does not become an attesting witness to deed solely on account of his having discharged statutory duties relating to registration of a document - To be an attesting witness registrar should have attested signature of testator in manner contemplated by s. 53(c) of Succession Act - A Registrar of Deeds, before he be termed an attesting witness shall have to be called in the witness box - Court must feel satisfied by his testimony that what he did satisfies requirement of being an attesting witness - (C) Whether registration of a Will or codicil dispenses with need of proving execution and attestation of Will in manner required by s. 68 of Evidence Act? - Further held, on account of registration of a document, including a will or codicil, a presumption as to correctness or regularity of attestation cannot be drawn - Appeal dismissed.
