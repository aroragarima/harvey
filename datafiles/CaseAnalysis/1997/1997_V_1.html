Uttam Namdeo Mahale v Uttam Namdeo Mahale

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1618; (1997) 6 SCC 73; AIR 1997 SC 2695; 1998 (1) Bom.C.R. 786; 1997 (2) CLT 489; JT 1997 (5) SC 632; 1997(4) SCALE 337; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 257; 1997 (5) Supreme 578

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Limitation Act, 1963 - Mamalatdar's Court Act, 1906, s. 21 - Eviction Order - Execution of - Limitation - Held, s. 21 of Act does not prescribe any limitation within which order is to be executed - In absence of any specific limitation rule general law of limitation provided in Limitation Act stand secluded - No limitation has been prescribed and it can be executed at any time, especially when law of limitation for purpose of this appeal is not there - Where there is statutory rule operating in field, implied power of exercise of right within reasonable limitation does not arise - Appeal dismissed.
