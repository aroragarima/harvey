Kishori v State (Nct) of Delhi

Supreme Court of India

17 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Mohapatra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 900; (2000) 2 SCC 83; (2000) SCC (Cr) 324; AIR 2000 SC 562; 2000 (1) CCR 65; 2000 (1) Crimes 100; 2000 CRLJ 756; 2000 (1) JCC 131; JT 1999 (10) SC 275; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 402; 2000(1) SCALE 1; 2000 (1) SLT 108; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 494; 2000 (1) Supreme 51

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Notions

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Death sentence - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 161, 319 and 366 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 397, 148 and 149 - Murder - Rioting against sikhs - Death sentence awarded by Trial Court and confirmed by High Court - Hence, present appeal - Held, ample evidence to show that appellant was member of unlawful assembly and responsible for murder of deceased - Appellant stabbed deceased during rioting - No reason to interfere with judgment of courts below - But case does not fall under category of rarest of rare case - Bachan Singh v. State of Punjab 1980 Indlaw SC 586 followed - Kishori v. State of Delhi 1998 Indlaw SC 876 relied on - Death sentence commuted to life imprisonment - Appeal dismissed.
