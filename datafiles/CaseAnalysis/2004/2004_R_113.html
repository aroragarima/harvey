M/S. Nicks (India) Tools v Ram Surat & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 658; (2004) 8 SCC 222; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1081; AIR 2004 SC 4348; 2004 (4) AWC 2965; 2004 (3) CLR 557; JT 2004 (7) SC 320; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 163; 2004(7) SCALE 241; 2004 (6) SLR 8; 2004 (3) UPLBEC 3131

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - s. 10 - Bank wages - Entitlement thereto - Conclusion of Labour Court while reducing back wages, whether proper? - LC reduced the back wages based on one judgment of the High Court of Punjab & Haryana which, as noticed by present Court, was overruled by a subsequent judgment of a Division Bench - Gainful employment - Precedent is settled that it is not an inevitable conclusion that every time a reinstatement is ordered, full back wages was the only consequence - In facts of case the trial court apart from generally observing that in Ludhiana, there must have been job opportunities available, on facts it did not rely upon any particular material to hold either such job was in fact available to the respondent and he refused to accept the same or he was otherwise gainfully employed during the period he was kept out of work - HC's judgment awarding full back wages, upheld - Appeals dismissed.
