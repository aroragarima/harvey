Nivrutti Pandurang Kokate & Ors v State Of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 223; (2008) 12 SCC 565; AIR 2008 SC 1460; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1183; JT 2008 (2) SC 556; 2008 (2) RCR(Criminal) 74; 2008(2) SCALE 663; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 44

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Old Age, Blood Stained Clothes

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302, 201 r/w 34 - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Held, though it is an established principle that child witnesses are dangerous witnesses as they are pliable and liable to be influenced easily, shaken and moulded, but it is also an accepted norm that if after careful scrutiny of their evidence the court comes to the conclusion that there is an impress of truth in it, there is no obstacle in the way of accepting the evidence of a child witness - Appeal dismissed.
