Lakhan v State Of M.P.

Supreme Court of India

09 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 628; (2010) 8 SCC 514; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3308; JT 2010 (8) SC 363; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 90; 2011(1) M.P.H.T. 1; 2010 (4) RCR(Criminal) 31; 2010(8) SCALE 56; [2010] 9 S.C.R. 705

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Murder - Dying declaration - Conviction - Challenged - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to imprisonment for life - Conviction and sentence confirmed by HC - Hence, present appeal - Two dying declaration were recorded first by Executive Magistrate and second by Police Officer - Which dying declaration can be relied upon in the facts and circumstances of this case? - Held, in case, there are multiple dying declarations and there are inconsistencies between them, generally, the dying declaration recorded by the higher officer like a Magistrate can be relied upon, provided that there is no circumstance giving rise to any suspicion about its truthfulness - In case, there are circumstances wherein the declaration had been made, not voluntarily and even otherwise, it is not supported by the other evidence, the Court has to scrutinize the facts of an individual case very carefully and take a decision as to which of the declarations is worth reliance - In the present case, first Dying Declaration was recorded by the Executive Magistrate after getting a Certificate from the Doctor, in which deceased did not make allegation against any of her family members, rather, she said that it was an accident - However, such a statement was not supported by the medical evidence for the reason that the injuries on her body were found on the upper part of her body and it was not possible to have such burn injuries in case of the kind of accident as she had disclosed in the first declaration - Second Dying Declaration was recorded by a Police Officer after getting a Certificate of fitness from the Doctor, which was corroborated by the medical evidence and free from any suspicious circumstances and it was also corroborated by the oral declaration made by the deceased to her parents - Concurrent findings of fact recorded by the Courts below do not warrant any interference - Conviction upheld - Appeal dismissed.
