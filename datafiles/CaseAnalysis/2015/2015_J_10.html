Jage Ram and others v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 55; 2015 ALL MR (Cri) 1223; 2015 (2) JCC 988; JT 2015 (1) SC 434; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1052; 2015 (1) MLJ(Crl) 623; 2015 (1) RCR(Criminal) 802; 2015(1) SCALE 741; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 1004

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 307, 325, 323 - Attempt to murder - Grievous hurt - Conviction - Appellants allegedly attempted to murder PW5 and PW6 - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellants u/ss. 307, 325 IPC - On appeal, HC confirmed conviction with some modification - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, evidence of PWs. 5 and 6 is amply corroborated by medical evidence. PWs. 5 and 6 being injured witnesses, their evidence is entitled to great weight. Cogent and convincing grounds are required to discard the evidence of injured witnesses. In the light of the fact that PWs. 5 and 6 were injured witnesses, courts below tested their evidence for its credibility and recorded concurrent findings that PWs. 5 and 6 are trustworthy witnesses. Hence, no reason is found to take a different view. Further, with regard to the weapon used for causing the head injuries to injured, nature of injures, situs of the injury and the severity of the blows, courts below recorded concurrent findings convicting the appellant No. 2 u/s. 307 IPC, which is unassailable. Therefore, conviction of appellant No. 1 u/s. 325 IPC, appellant nos. 2 and 3 u/s. 323 IPC is confirmed and the sentence is reduced to the period already undergone by each of them. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - The burden is on the prosecution that accused had attempted to commit the murder of the prosecution witness. Whether the accused person intended to commit murder of another person would depend upon the facts and circumstances of each case. To justify a conviction u/s. 307 IPC, it is not essential that fatal injury capable of causing death should have been caused.
