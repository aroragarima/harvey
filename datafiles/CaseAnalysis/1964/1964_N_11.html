Â Narottam Kishore Dev Varma and Others v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.M. Sikri, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 156; AIR 1964 SC 1590; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 55

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 87 B - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14,19, 291 and 311 - (A) What is objective behind ss. 86, 87, 87 A and 87 B? - Held, legislative background of s. 87 B cannot be divorced from historical background which is to be found for instance, in art. 362 - Privileges conferred on rulers of former Indian states has its origin in these agreement and covenants - One of privileges is that of extra territory and exemption from civil jurisdiction except with sanction of Central Govt. - It was thought that privilege which was claimed by foreign Rulers of Indian States prior to independence of country should be continued even after independence was attained and State had become part of India, and that is how in 1951, CPC was amended and present ss. 86, 87, 87 A and 87 B came to be enacted in present form - (B) Whether special treatment given to Indian ruler by impugned Act violates arts. 14 and 19 of constitution?- Further held, Rulers of Indian states form a class by themselves and special treatment given to them by impugned provisions cannot be said to be based on unconstitutional discrimination - Cannot be said to be unreasonable - (C) Whether sanction should be granted to a person who intends to sue a ruler of a former Indian state ? - Further held, it does not appear very satisfactory that an intended action against ruler of a former Indian State should be stifled by refusing to grant litigant sanction under s. 87 B - If power to grant sanction is exercised in a sensible way and is not used for stifling claims which are not far-fetched or frivolous, that may prevent growth of discontent in mind of litigants against artificial provisions prescribed by s. 87 B - Appeal dismissed.
