Supreme Court of India

01 February 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 160; AIR 1957 SC 540; 1957 SCJ 439; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 488

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950, Payment Order, Federal Court (Enlargement of Jurisdiction) Act, 1947, Andhra State Act, 1953, Abolition of Privy Council Jurisdiction Act 1949

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 136, 135 and 133 - Andhra State Act, 1953, ss. 28, 39(2) and 38 - Government of India Act, 1935, ss. 200 and 205 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 109 and 110 - North-western Provinces Tenancy Act, 1901, s. 177 - Letters Patent, 1865, cls. 15 and 39 - Indian Income Tax Act, s. 66 - East Punjab Evacuees' (Administration of Property) Act, 1947, s. 5(A) and (B) - (A) Whether a right of appeal in respect of a pending action can be treated as a substantive right vesting in litigant on commencement of action? - Held, s. 177 of old Act provided as follows an appeal shall lie to district judge from decree of an asst. collector of first class or of a collector in any of suits included in group A of Fourth Schedule - Principle must involve that an admixture of different systems is not to be applied to a single case - It is quite true that suitor cannot enter Court C without going through Court B, but neither can he enter Court B till Court A has given its decision - Right must be a right to take matter to Court C in due course of existing law - (B) Whether it is any necessary part of intendment of Letters Patent that they should operate upon appeals? - Held, in spite of wide language of amended cl. 15 of Letters Patent, namely, that an appeal shall lie from judgment of Single judge only where judge who passed judgment declared that case was a fit one for appeal - Special Bench found nothing in that language to indicate that it applied to a decree passed after amendment came into force or that it took away vested right - Appeal allowed.
