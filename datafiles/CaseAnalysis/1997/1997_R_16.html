R. Pandian and Anothera. Deivendran Son of M. Ammavasithevar v State of Tamil Naduthrough The Secretary, Department of Home

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, G.N. Ray

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 664

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Curable, Ballistics

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 460, 306 and 337 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120 B, 326 and 449 - Conviction u/ss. 120 B, 148, 449, 302/34 and 326/34 - (A) Whether approver's evidence can at all be relied upon to bring home the charge against the accused persons? - Extent of corroboration that is required before the acceptance of the evidence of approver would depend upon the facts and circumstances of the case - Evidence of approver implicating several accused persons in commission of the offence could not only be corroborated generally but also qua each accused - But that does not mean that there should be independent corroboration of every particular circumstances from and independent source - There must be some additional evidence rendering it probable that the story of the accomplice is true - (B) Whether Chief Judicial Magistrate could have at all granted pardon to the accused even after committal of the proceedings to the Court of Sessions? - Once a commitment is made the committing Magistrate no longer retains jurisdiction over the proceedings - After a criminal proceeding is committed to a Court of Sessions it is only the Court of Sessions which has the jurisdiction to tender pardon to an accused and the Chief Judicial Magistrate does not posses any such jurisdiction - In such a case if the said Chief Judicial Magistrate tenders pardon then that would not be a curable irregularity within the ambit of cl. (g) of s. 460 of the CrPC, 1973 - (C) When an accused is granted pardon after the case is committed to Court of Sessions, would it be necessary to comply with sub-s. (4)(a) of s. 306, CrPC, 1973 - It is clear from the scheme of s. 337 that what is required is that a person who accepts a tender of pardon must be examined as a witness at the different stages of the proceedings - (D) Excluding evidence of the approver from consideration whether prosecution case can be held to be proved beyond reasonable doubt? - Evidence by no stretch of imagination beings the case in hand to be one of the rarest of rare cases where the extreme penalty of death can be awarded - Uphold the conviction of accused u/s. 302 IPC, 1860 but set aside the sentence of death - Conviction of the appellants u/s. 120B IPC, 1860 cannot be sustained as evidence of the approver being out of consideration the said charge cannot be said to have been established beyond reasonable doubt - All the appellants are acquitted from the said charge - Appeals disposed of.
