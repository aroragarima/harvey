Westarly Dkhar and others v Sehekaya Lyngdoh

Supreme Court of India

28 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.F. Nariman, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 57; (2015) 4 SCC 292; AIR 2015 SC 1092; 2015 (109) ALR 727; 2015 (1) CTC 566; JT 2015 (1) SC 613; 2015 (2) MLJ 121; 2015 (127) RD 712; 2015(1) SCALE 734; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 794

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Passports Act, 1967, United Khasi-Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Administration of Justice) Rules, 1953

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - United Khasi-Jaintia Hills Autonomous District (Administration of Justice) Rules, 1953 - Appeal to District Council Court - Suit was filed by the respondent and an ad-interim ex-parte injunction was granted by the Subordinate District Council Court - Appeal against said order was admitted by District Council Court and order was stayed - By a further order the ad-interim ex-parte injunction was set aside as the District Council Court allowed the appeal - Revision Petition was filed against the said order, and by the impugned order, the revision was allowed stating that since an appeal had been filed within 30 days of the ad-interim ex-parte order, it would not be maintainable under the CPC and, therefore, the appellate order was set aside - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, as per the Rules, an appeal is provided as a matter of right from all 'decisions' of a Subordinate District Council Court to the District Council Court. An appeal is provided as a matter of right u/r. 28 of the Rules and only the spirit of the CPC applies. An interim order is a 'decision' for the purpose of the Rules. Hence, on the facts of instant case, the appeal becomes maintainable because r. 28 of the Rules provides for such appeal without any requirement that ordinarily it should be filed only after 30 days. Appeal allowed.
