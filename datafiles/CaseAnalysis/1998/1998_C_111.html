Messrs Hasimara Industries Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax West Bengal-Ix and Another

Supreme Court of India

13 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, Ms. Justice Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 730; (1998) 5 SCC 33; AIR 1998 SC 2349; [1998] 231 ITR 842; JT 1998 (4) SC 53; 1998(3) SCALE 598; 1998 (5) SLT 34; 1998 (5) Supreme 93

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961 - Business Expenditure - Capital Loss - Exemption - Assessee's business was manufacture and sale of tea and was not engaged in cotton manufacturing business at all - Business of running a cotton mill was not its own but was only operating said mill under leave and licence agreement - Amount of advance in a sum of Rupees of twenty lakhs was given as capital to lessor who in turn had to modernize mill - Assessee claimed exemption which was not allowed by respondent and HC affirmed decision of respondent - Hence, instant appeal - Whether deductibility of a sum of Rupees twenty lakhs out of assessee's profits as sum was given by way of advance for modernisation of plants - Amount of advance in a sum of Rupees of twenty lakhs was given not for its own purpose by way of business expenditure for modernishing mill but as capital to lessor who in turn had to modernise the mill -Transaction entered not in nature of a loan transaction or a money lending transaction and loss suffered by assess was a capital loss - Hence, amount could not be deducted from assessee as income from business loss - Appeal dismissed.
