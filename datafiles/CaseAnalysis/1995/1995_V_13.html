Indian Medical Association v V.P. Shantha & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

13 November 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

S.C. Agrawal, B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 132; (1995) 6 SCC 651; AIR 1996 SC 550; AIR 1995 SCW 4463; [1996] 86 Comp Cas 806; 1996 (1) CompLJ 15; 1995 CPJ(Sc) 1; 1995 (3) CTJ 969; JT 1995 (8) SC 119; 1995(6) SCALE 273; [1995] Supp5 S.C.R. 110; 1996 (1) UJ 664; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 227

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Corporate; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss.2(1)(o), 2(1)(d), 2(1)(g), and 14(1)(d); Constitution of India, arts.14 and 19(1)(g) - 'Service' and 'Deficiency' - Medical practitioners - Held, are within purview of 'service' when charging fees - If free or on salary from hospital administration or charge on consolidated fund - Then excluded from 'service' - Charges borne by insurance company under medi-care policy or by employer of patient as part of conditions of service - Is not service rendered free of charge - comes under s.2 (1)(o) - Deficiency under such 'service' constitutes liability against medical practitioners.
