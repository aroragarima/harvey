Savitri Pandey v Prem Chandra Pandey

Supreme Court of India

08 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 10; (2002) 2 SCC 73; AIR 2002 SC 591; 2002 ALJ 355; 2002 (46) ALR 465; 2002 (1) AWC 472; 2002 (6) Bom.C.R. 511; 2002 (1) CalLT(SC) 32; 2002 (2) G.L.R. 1369; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 377; JT 2002 (1) SC 25; 2002 (1) KarLJ 193; 2002 (2) MLJ(SC) 1; 2002 (1) RCR(Civil) 719; 2002(1) SCALE 33; 2002 (1) SCJ 6; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 50; 2002 (1) SLT 103; 2002 (1) Supreme 90; 2002 (1) UC 299

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Summary: No party to the marriage can allege desertion unless the parties had recognised and discharged the common obligation of married life which essentially requires cohabitation between the parties for the purpose of consummating the marriage. Further, under Section 28(4) of the Hindu Marriage Act, a period of 30 days is insufficient and inadequate and a minimum period of 90 days should be prescribed for filing an appeal against any judgment and decree under the Act.
