Swadeshi Polytex Limited v Collector Of Central Excise

Supreme Court of India

23 November 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 323; (1990) SCC (Tax) 292; (1990) 2 SCC 358; AIR 1990 SC 301; 1990 CRLR 116; 1990 CrLR(SC) 116; 1990 (40) DLT 61; 1990 (25) ECC 152; 1990 (28) ECR 289; 1989 (44) E.L.T. 794; JT 1989 (Supp) SC 347; 1989(2) SCALE 1208; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 262

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Customs; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Excise Rules 1944 (contd)

Summary: Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35-L - Customs Tariff Act, 1975, s. 3 - Central Excise Rules, r. 56-A - Manufacture of polyester fiber falling under Tariff Item 18 of erstwhile Central Excise Tariff - Ethylene glycol and DMT (Dimethyl Tetraphthalate) being used - Ethylene glycol fell under Tariff Item 68 of erstwhile Central Excise Tariff - A notification exempted all excisable goods on which duty of excise was leviable in manufacture of which any goods falling under Tariff Item 68 had been used - Set off of duty on ethylene glycol claimed - Whether set off of duty paid on inputs is admissible only if finished excisable goods manufactured therefrom, is not exempted from duty - Held, credit of duty could not be reduced to extent of ethylene glycol contained in methanol as ineligible - Full effect to exemption notification should not be given by any circuitous process of interpretation.
