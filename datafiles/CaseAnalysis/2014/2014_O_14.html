Dr. Suhas H. Pophale v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and its Estate Officer

Supreme Court of India

11 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 83; (2014) 4 SCC 657; AIR 2014 SC 1509; 2014 (3) ALD(SC) 40; 2014(3) ALL MR 894; 2014 (103) ALR 692; JT 2014 (3) SC 15; 2014 (1) RCR(Rent) 163; 2014(2) SCALE 223; [2014] 2 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Insurance Amendment Act, 1950, Bombay Rent Act, 1947, Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1958, General Insurance (Emergency Provisions) Act, 1971, Jammu and Kashmir Shri Mata Vaishno Devi Shrine Act, 1988, Punjab Public Premises and Land (Eviction and Rent Recovery), Act, 1959, Recovery of damages

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent Act, 1947, s. 15A - Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971 - Suit for eviction - Recovery of damages - Legality - Respondent no. 1/Land lord filed eviction suit before Court of Small Causes/respondent no. 2 - Respondent no. 2 passed an order directing eviction of tenant and appellant, and also for recovery of damages - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before Trial Court - Trial Court set aside order of damages, and remanded matter to respondent no. 2 - Respondent no. 2 upheld order of eviction - Appellant filed petition before HC and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that right from stage of proceedings before respondent no. 2, and even before HC, was that his occupation of concerned premises was protected under newly added s. 15A of 1947 Act with effect from 1-2-1973, prior to respondent no. 1 acquiring title over property from 1-1-1974 - Whether rights of an occupant/licensee/tenant protected under 1947 Act and its successor 1999 Act, could be adversely affected by application of 1971 Act -

Held, s. 15 of 1947 Act created bar of jurisdiction to entertain suits or proceedings in respect of eviction of any person in an unauthorized occupation - Relationship between respondent no. 1 and such other Govt. Companies or Corporations, on one hand and their occupants/licencees/tenants on other hand was concerned, such persons who were in occupation prior to premises belonging to or taken on lease by such entities, would continue to be governed by 1947 Act for all purposes - 1971 Act would apply only to those who come in such occupation after such date - Thus, there was no occasion to have dual procedure - Occupants of these properties were earlier tenants of erstwhile Insurance Companies which were private landlords - They have not chosen to be tenants of Govt. Companies - Their status as occupants of Public Insurance Companies has been thrust upon them by 1971 Act - As far as eviction of unauthorized occupants from public premises was concerned, undoubtedly it was covered under 1971 Act but it was so covered from 16-9-1958, or from later date when concerned premises become public premises by virtue of concerned premises vesting into Govt. company or corporation like LIC or Nationalised Banks or General Insurance Companies like respondent no. 1 - Thus there were two categories of occupants of these public corporations who get excluded from coverage of Act itself - Impugned order passed by HC was set aside - Order passed by Trial Court as well as respondent no. 2 was set aside - Appeal allowed.
