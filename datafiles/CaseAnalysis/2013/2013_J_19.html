Debabrata Dash And Anr. v Jatindra Prasad Das & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Madan B. Lokur, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 137; (2013) 3 SCC 658; AIR 2013 SC 1700; 2013 (137) FLR 576; JT 2013 (8) SC 384; 2013 (3) MLJ 347; 2013(3) M.P.H.T. 1; 2013 (2) OLR 73; 2013(3) SCALE 378; [2013] 2 S.C.R. 331; 2013 (4) SLR 456

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Orissa Superior Judicial Service and Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 2007, Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 1994, Orissa Judicial Service (Special Schemes) Rules, 2001, Orissa Judicial Service Rules, 1964, Fast Track Courts Scheme

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Orissa Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1963 - Orissa Judicial Service (Special Schemes) Rules, 2001 - Promotion - On the basis of additional responsibilities handled - Sustainability - Orissa Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1963 - Inter se seniority between the appellants and respondent no. 1 in the Senior Branch cadre of Orissa Superior Judicial Service was the subject matter of this appeal - Whether service rendered by petitioner in the Fast Track Court as Additional District Judge was to be taken into account while fixing his seniority after regularization of his service in the Senior Branch cadre under the Orissa Superior Judicial Service Rules, 1963 - Held, SC observed that the Fast Track Court Judges were appointed under separate set of rules than the rules governing the regular appointment to the State Higher Judicial Service - SC noted that while appointing Fast Track Court Judges, it was clearly stipulated that such appointments would be ad hoc and temporary and that the appointees should not derive any benefit from such appointments - SC had indicated that on 5-1-2002 or 26-4-2002, there was no vacancy in the cadre of Superior Judicial Service (Senior Branch) for being filled up by promotion - Such vacancy in the Senior Branch cadre of the service occurred on 15.12.2003 and from that date the writ petitioner was given benefit of his service rendered in the Fast Track Court - Administrative decision by SC was in accord with the 1963 Rules, 2001 Rules - Vew of DB in the impugned judgment was legally unsustainable - Impugned judgment was liable to be set aside and was set aside - Brij Mohan Lal v. Union of India and Others, 2012 Indlaw SC 319, relied on - Appeal allowed.
