Centre for Public Interest Litigation and another v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 29; (2012) 3 SCC 1; AIR 2012 SC 1002; 2012 (1) CLT 385; 2012 (1) CLT(SC) 385; 2012 (1) CompLJ 497; JT 2012 (2) SC 154; 2012 (2) MLJ(SC) 111; 2012(2) SCALE 180; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 147

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Telecom

Summary: (A) Telecom - Administrative - Constitution - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - 2G Spectrum Scam - Spectrum allocation - Duty of State - Petitioners filed instant writ petition challenging grant of Unified Access Service Licenses (UAS Licenses) to private respondents, contending that procedure adopted by DoT (Department of Telecom) was arbitrary, illegal and in violation of art. 14 of Constitution - Petitioner further contended that once HC had held that cut-off date, i.e., 25-9-2007 fixed for consideration of applications was arbitrary and unconstitutional, entire procedure adopted by DoT for grant of UAS Licenses with approval of Minister of Communications and Information Technology (Minister of C&IT) was liable to be declared illegal and quashed - Respondents contended that Telecom Regulatory Authority of India (TRAI) had already initiated action for levy of penalty/liquidated damages for non-compliance of roll out obligations and violation of conditions of license and that notices issued by TRAI alleging the same was already challenged before Appellate Tribunal (TDSAT) and interim orders were passed, that remedy, if any, available to petitioners was to approach TDSAT - Respondents further contended that UAS Licenses were given strictly in accordance with modified first-come-first-served policy and private respondents were able to fulfill Letter of Intent (LoI) conditions because newspapers had already published stories about possible grant of licenses in month of January, 2008 - Whether Government had right to alienate, transfer or distribute natural resources/national assets otherwise than by following fair and transparent method consistent with fundamentals of equality clause enshrined in the Constitution - Held, spectrum was internationally accepted as a scarce, finite and renewable natural resource which was susceptible to degradation in case of inefficient utilization and it had high economic value in the light of demand for it on account of tremendous growth in telecom sector - Though Spectrum did not belong to particular State, right of use was granted to States as per international norms - Further, natural resources were vested with Government as a matter of trust, thus it was solemn duty of State to protect national interest and natural resources should always be used in interests of country and not for private interests - Thus, State was legal owner of natural resources as trustee of people and although it was empowered to distribute the same, process of distribution should be guided by Constitutional principles including doctrine of equality and larger public good - Petitions allowed.

(B) Telecom - Administrative - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Spectrum allocation - Recommendation of TRAI - Justifiability - Whether recommendations made by TRAI on 28-8-2007 for grant of UAS License with 2G spectrum in 800, 900 and 1800 MHz at price fixed in 2001, which were approved by DoT were contrary to decision taken by Council of Ministers on 31-10-2003 - Held, mechanism evolved by TRAI for allocation of spectrum and methodology adopted by Minister of C&IT and DoT officers for grant of UAS Licenses might have caused huge loss to nation - Further, recommendations made by TRAI were flawed in many respects and implementation thereof by DoT resulted in gross violation of objective of National Telecom Policy, 1999 and decision taken by Council of Ministers on 31-10-2003 - Thus, being an expert body, it was incumbent upon TRAI to make suitable recommendations even for 2G bands especially in light of deficiencies of present system which it had itself pointed out - Hence, there was no merit in reasoning of TRAI that consideration of maintaining a level playing field prevented a realistic reassessment of entry fee - Petitions allowed.

(C) Telecom - Administrative - Constitution - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Spectrum allocation - First-come-first-served policy - Legality - Whether policy of first-come-first-served followed by DoT for grant of licenses was ultra vires provisions of art. 14 of Constitution and was arbitrarily changed by Minister of C&IT, without consulting TRAI, with a view to favour some of applicants - Held, duly publicized auction conducted fairly and impartially was best method for discharging allocation and methods like first-come-first-served when used for alienation of natural resources/public property were likely to be misused by unscrupulous people who were only interested in garnering maximum financial benefit and had no respect for Constitutional ethos and values - Further, while transferring or alienating natural resources, State was duty bound to adopt method of auction by giving wide publicity so that all eligible persons could participate in process - Thus, exercise undertaken by DoT officers between September, 2007 and March 2008, under leadership of Minister of C&IT was wholly arbitrary, capricious and contrary to public interest apart from being violation of doctrine of equality - Minister of C&IT brushed aside suggestion made by Minister of Law and Justice for placing matter before Empowered Group of Ministers - Further, Minister of C&IT rejected Prime Minister's recommendation of auction by saying that it would be unfair, discriminatory, arbitrary and capricious to auction spectrum to new applicants because it would not give them level playing field - Further, cut-off date, i.e. 25-9-2007 decided by Minister of C&IT on 2-11-2007 was not made public till 10-1-2008 and first-come-first- served policy, which was being followed since 2003 was changed by him on 7-1-2008 and was incorporated in press release dated 10-1-2008, which enabled some of applicants, who had access either to Minister or DoT officers to get demand drafts, bank guarantee, etc. prepared in advance for compliance of conditions of LoI, which was basis for determination of seniority for grant of licenses and allocation of spectrum - However, manner in which exercise for grant of LoIs to applicants was conducted on 10-1-2008 left no room for doubt that everything was stage managed to favour those who were able to know in advance change in implementation of first-come-first served policy, and as a result of that, some companies which had submitted applications in 2004 or 2006 were pushed down in priority and those who had applied between August and September 2007 succeeded in getting higher seniority entitling them to allocation of spectrum on priority basis - Therefore, when it was clearly demonstrated that policy framed by State or its agency/instrumentality and/or its implementation was contrary to public interest or was in violation of Constitutional principles, it was duty of Court to exercise its jurisdiction in larger public interest and reject stock plea of State that scope of judicial review should not be exceeded beyond the recognized parameters - Petitions allowed.

(D) Telecom - Administrative - Telecom Regulatory Authority of India Act, 1997 - Illegal allocation of 2G band - Consequence - Whether licenses granted to ineligible applicants and those who failed to fulfill terms and conditions of license were liable to be quashed - Held, licenses granted to private respondents on or after 10-1-2008 pursuant to two press releases issued on 10-1-2008 and subsequent allocation of spectrum to licensees were declared illegal and were quashed - Directions issued that TRAI should make fresh recommendations for grant of license and allocation of spectrum in 2G band in 22 Service Areas by auction, as was done for allocation of spectrum in 3G band and respondent nos. 4, 6, 7 and 10 should pay cost of Rs. 50 lakhs each because they too were benefited by wholly arbitrary and unconstitutional exercise undertaken by DoT for grant of UAS Licenses and allocation of spectrum in 2G band - Petitions allowed.
