Thakur Ram v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, R.S. Bachawat, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 411; AIR 1966 SC 911; 1976 (2) ALT(SC) 122; 1966 CRLJ 700; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 207 and 437 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 384 and 109 - Committal Proceedings - During proceedings before Magistrate, prosecution made applications for framing charge, stay of proceedings and committing case to cCourt of Sessions all of which were rejected - Revision petition filed before Session Judge which observed case is of serious nature and to be tried by Court of Sessions and allowed revision - Whether Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to make an order directing Magistrate to commit case for trial by a Court of Sessions? - Held, Yes - S. 437 makes it discretionary upon Sessions Judge to order commitment when offence triable by Sessions Court - Sessions Judge had jurisdiction to make an order directing Magistrate to commit case for trial by a Court of Sessions - Law gives discretion to be exercised judicially - Since case is at defence stage, committal to Sessions Court would cause undue hardship an prejudice to accused - There is nothing in language of s. 437 from which it could be said that this power is not exercisable during pendency of a trial before a Magistrate or that this power can be exercised only where Magistrate has made an express order of discharge - Trial to proceed before Magistrate - Appeal allowed.
