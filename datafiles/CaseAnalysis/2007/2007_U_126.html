Sayed Abdul Ala v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1472; (2007) 15 SCC 208; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3583; 2007 (4) JCC (Narcotics) 162; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 853; 2007(12) SCALE 345; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 631

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Illicit Traffic in Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1988 - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, s. 37 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - Preventive Detention - Order of detention - Validity thereof - Whether detaining authority applied its mind by considering relevant facts while passing order of detention? - Held, appellant was accused under NDPS Act, 1985 and was in judicial custody at relevant point of time - Appellant/accused had moved a bail application - Limitation of Court to grant bail in view of s. 37 of NDPS Act, 1985 - Proposition of law laid down, for checking validity of order of detention which was passed when detenu was in custody, firstly, if authority passing order is aware of fact that he is actually in custody, secondly, if he had a reason to believe on basis of reliable material placed before him (a) that there is a real possibility of his being released on bail, and (b) that on being released, he would in all probability indulge in prejudicial activities, and thirdly, it is felt essential to detain him to prevent him from so doing - Satisfaction recorded not sufficient and thus order of detention is unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
