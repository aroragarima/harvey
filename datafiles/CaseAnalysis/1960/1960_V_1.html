Vadilal Panchal v Dattatraya Dulaji Ghadigaonker and Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 192; AIR 1960 SC 1113; 1960 CRLJ 1499; 1961 (2) MLJ(SC) 16; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Indian Evidence Act, Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 192, 200, 202, 203 and 499 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 105 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 76, 79 and 96 - Respondent made complaint before Presidency Magistrate for CID inquiry into death of one person - Complaint was dismissed by Magistrate - On appeal, High Court directed Magistrate to issue process and deal with matter - Present appeal - Held, s. 200 says inter alia what a Magistrate taking cognisance of an offence on complaint shall do on receipt of such a complaint - Scope of an inquiry under section is limited to finding out truth or falsehood of complaint in order to determine question of issue of process - It is manifestly clear from provisions of s. 203 that judgment which Magistrate has to form must be based on statements of complainant and his witnesses and result of investigation or inquiry - This does not mean that Magistrate is bound to accept result of inquiry or investigation or that he must accept any plea that is set up on behalf of person complained against - If Magistrate has not misdirected himself as to scope of an enquiry u/s. 202 and has applied his mind judicially to materials before him, it would be erroneous in law to hold that a plea based on an exception can never be accepted by him in arriving at his judgment - Further held, in present case it cannot be said that Magistrate was wrong in his judgment that there was no sufficient ground for proceeding further on complaint - HC set aside order of Magistrate on an erroneous view of scope of s. 203 - Appeal allowed.
