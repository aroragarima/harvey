M.D., Balasaheb Desai Sahakari S. K. Limited v Kashinath Ganapati Kambale

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1961; (2009) 2 SCC 288; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 372; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1087; 2009 (1) AWC 862; 2009 (2) Bom.C.R. 102; 2008 (5) ESC 767; JT 2009 (1) SC 521; 2009 (2) LLJ 210; 2009(1) SCALE 112; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 446; 2008 (8) SLR 20

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Cooperative

Summary: Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 - Misconduct - Termination - Issue on the quantum of back wages - Held, burden of proof to show that the workman was not gainfully employed is not on the employer - Respondent not only was found guilty of remaining unauthorizedly absent but also guilty of misbehaviour with his superiors, leaving place of work early without permission and without leave, signing the muster for showing presence although he was absent - Forfeiture of 50% back wages was not an adequate punishment - No back-wages should have been awarded in favour of the respondent - Appeal partly allowed.
