Â A. V. Thomas and Company, Limited, Alleppey v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, Raghubar Dayal

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 381; [1963] 48 ITR 67; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 776

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income-Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(3)(xv) - Assessee appellant claimed deduction in assessment year as bad debt which was written-off in its books of account - High Court disallowed claim - Whether HC right in disallowing claim of appellant? - It was held that amount claimed by assessee Co. as deduction was not admissible either u/s. 10(2)(xi) or 10(2)(xv) - ITO held that debt was written off at time when it was neither bad not doubtful and claim to write off was premature - Debt to be debt proper had to be one which if good would have swelled taxable profits - Advance paid by assessee Co. to another to purchase shares cannot be said to be incidental to trading activities of assessee Co. - Assessee Co. itself was altering entries to convert advance into debt so as to be able to write it off and claim benefit of s. 10(2)(xi) - Held, s. 10(2)(xi) wasz inapplicable to factsz of thisz case - Appeal dismissed.
