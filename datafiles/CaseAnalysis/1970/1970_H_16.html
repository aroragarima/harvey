Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal, and Another v Hemchandra Kar and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 206; (1970) 1 SCC 780; AIR 1971 SC 2331; [1970] 77 ITR 1; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(a) - HUF - And Indivisdual members - Reopening of income tax assessment - On first re-opening - Primary facts necessary for re-assessment of the family were in possession of ITO - Who proceeded to re-assess members by including the amounts in their individual accounts ( amount in question) - After some days he change issued another notice u/s. 34 to family seeking to include the amount in question in the income of the family -Legality - Held, he could not a few days later merely change his opinion and issue another notice u/s. 34 - Appeal Dismissed.
