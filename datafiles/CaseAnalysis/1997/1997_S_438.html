Nishar Ahmed Fajmohmed Kaji v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1402; (1998) 9 SCC 23; (1998) SCC (Cr) 967; 1998 (1) CCR 17; JT 1997 (9) SC 67; 1997(6) SCALE 686; 1998 (1) SLT 48; 1997 (9) Supreme 323

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302, 34 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25-A - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant (accused) allegedly committed offences u/ss. 302 and 34 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant u/ss. 302 and 34 of IPC and u/s. 25-A of the Act and sentenced him to undergo imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned conviction judgment passed by Trial Court and affirmed by HC was justified - Held, evidence of PW16 (head constable) was quite natural and in circumstances of case there could not have been any record for the absence of appellant for about an hour and a half from the premises of Court - Trial Court and HC discussed evidence of PW16 at length and considered in proper perspective - There was no reason whatever to differ from Courts below and disbelieve PW16 - There was nothing on record to show how 4 eye-witnesses were interested to speak against appellant - Nothing was placed on record to indicate any enmity or motive on their part to speak against appellant - Mere fact that 3 of them were Advocates and 4th was driver of deceased was not sufficient to dub them as interested persons - There was no doubt whatever that witnesses were in same car by which deceased went to Court and they were all present at the scene of occurrence - All eye witnesses had known appellant for more than 3 years prior to occurrence - Their presence at scene of occurrence was quite natural and established - They had seen the appellant running away from stops of Court room - Courts below were not in error in accepting their testimony - There was no discrepancy between medical evidence and ocular evidence - Impugned conviction of appellant was justified - Appeal dismissed.
