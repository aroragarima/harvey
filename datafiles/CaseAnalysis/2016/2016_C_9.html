Tamil Nadu Generation and Distribution Corporation Limited (TANGEDCO), represented by Its Chairman and Managing Director and another v CSEPDI - Trishe Consortium, represented by Its Managing Director and another

Supreme Court of India

18 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 1018; (2017) 4 SCC 318; AIR 2016 SC 4879; 2016 (6) CTC 295; [2016] 7 S.C.R. 495

Case Digest

Subject: Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Contract, Attorney, Tamil Nadu Transparency In Tenders Act, 1998, Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Rules, 2000

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Act, 1998 - Tamil Nadu Transparency in Tenders Rules, 2000 - Financial consultant report - Judicial review - Scope - Corporation floated tender for setting up two units of Thermal Power Project and four bidders including respondents participated - While so, respondent no.1 filed writ petition assailing letter dt.27-9-2014 by which contract was awarded to respondent no.2 and also letter dt.10-1-2014 and sought for annulments of letters and further for issue of directions to Corporation to determine award of tender strictly in terms of tender documents taking into account bid of respondent no.1 and that of respondent no.2 - However, Single Judge of HC dismissed writ petition and opined that the conduct of process of evaluation of the tenders had not appeared to be arbitrary, capricious or unfair; and that price bids of the bidders had been evaluated as per the parameters indicated in the tender notification by an independent consultant who was selected as per Board Resolution that was within the knowledge of both bidders - Aggrieved, respondent no.1 filed appeal and DB of HC observed that approach of Corporation was unfair in tendering process and allowed appeals and directed Corporation to evaluate the price bid of the respondents in the light of its findings and considering all relevant parameters including the representations, documents submitted by respondent no.1 and to record detailed reasons for the decision and communicate the same to the respondent no.1 so as to comply with the requirement of the provisions of the Act and the Rules and various decisions of SC - Hence, instant appeals by Corporation and successful bidder- respondent no.2 - Whether Evaluation Report dt. 30-5-2014 by Consultant, is prima facie erroneous, requiring interference within the parameters of judicial review.

Held, Consultant analyzed offers regard being had to the tender conditions and the analysis and determination made by the financial consultant has been carried out before receipt of any additional documents from either side. The documents were called for by the owner from both the qualifying bidders in a transparent manner and the same have been considered at the time of evaluation by the Consultant. Further, in a complex fiscal evaluation the Court has to apply the doctrine of restraint. Several aspects, clauses, contingencies, etc. have to be factored and the calculations are best left to experts and those who have knowledge and skills in the field. The financial computation involved, the capacity and efficiency of the bidder and the perception of feasibility of completion of the project have to be left to the wisdom of the financial experts and consultants. The courts could not enter into the said realm in exercise of power of judicial review. Further, Court could not sit in appeal over the financial consultant's assessment and the report is neither ex facie erroneous nor could perceive as flawed for being perverse or absurd. The DB through delineation adopted approach of an appellate forum or authority and extended the principle of judicial review to certain areas to which it could not have and so, the judgment and order of DB followed the path of error in continuum. Hence, impugned order of DB of HC is set aside and appeals are allowed. Appeals disposed of.
