Piarey Lal v Hori Lal

Supreme Court of India

07 February 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Shingal, P.K. Goswami

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 195; (1977) 2 SCC 221; AIR 1977 SC 1226; 1977 (3) ALR 177; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 915; 1977 UJ 201

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: U.P. Consolidation of Holdings Act, 1954, s. 30 - Constitution, arts. 133, 226 and 227 - Contract Act, 1872, s. 56 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 54 and 55(1)(d) - Interpretation - Consolidation - Order of allotting new plots for earlier plots in respect of which agreement for sale had been executed - Agreement whether rendered void u/s.56, Contract Act - Held, when property lost as a result of the consolidation dcheme and rights, title, and interests ceased in that property by virtue of s. 30(a) of the Act, agreement for sale became void within the meaning of s. 56 of the Contract Act, and it is futile to urge that they were saved by cls.(a) or (b) of s. 30 of the Act - Appeal Allowed.
