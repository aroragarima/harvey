Jugal Kishore Rameshwardas v Mrs. Goolbai Hormusji

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1955

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1955 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1955 SC 812; 1956 (58) BomLR 486; 1956 SCJ 64; [1955] 2 S.C.R. 857

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Municipalities & Local Governments - Bombay Securities Contracts Control Act, 1925, ss. 3, 5 and 6 - Contract - Repudiation of contract on ground no authorisation so forth - Notice to respondent for appointment of arbitrator from her side - Respondent declines to appoint on reason of contract notes becoming void as such no arbitration - Award passed - Challenged - Dispute was one which arose out of contract of employment of appellant by respondent as broker and not out of any contract of sale or purchase of securities - The relationship between respondent and appellant was one of principal and agent and not that of seller and purchaser - Contract of employment is not itself a contract of sale or purchase, as such it does not become ipso facto void - To constitute an arbitration agreement in writing it is not necessary that it should be signed by parties, it is sufficient if the terms are reduced to writing and agreement of parties thereto is established - Held, that arbitration proceedings are not incompetent and that award made therein is not void on ground that the contracts containing agreement are void - Appeal allowed.
