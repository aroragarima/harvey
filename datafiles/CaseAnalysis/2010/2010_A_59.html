Amar Singh and another v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

15 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 960; (2011) 1 SCC 210; 2011 (1) AWC 78; JT 2010 (12) SC 451; 2010(12) SCALE 69

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Parliament & Legislature

Summary: Constitution - Parliament & Legislature - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 and Tenth Schedule para 2(1) - Petitioners, former Members of Samajwadi Party(a political party) were expelled from Samajwadi Party for their alleged anti-party activities on 2.02.2010 and have been declared unattached parliamentarians - Petitioners filed writ petition u/art. 32 of Constitution submitting that they have imminent threat to their continuance as Members of Parliament, both Rajya Sabha and Lok Sabha, in view of the decision of SC in G. Viswanathan vs. Hon'ble Speaker Tamil Nadu Legislative Assembly, Madras & Anr. 1996 INDLAW SC 1136 wherein SC held that the even if a member is expelled from his party, for the purposes of the Tenth Schedule, he/she would not cease to be a member of the political party that had set him up as a candidate for the election and he would continue to remain attached to that political party even if he is treated as 'unattached' - Petitioners further submitted that on account of the decision in G. Viswanathan's case, even an expelled Member stood exposed to the party whip in the House, if he was to be deemed to be, for all practical purposes, a member of the said party which had expelled him, in the House - Whether writ petition filed by the petitioners could be allowed? - Held, decision in G. Viswanathan's case merits another look as far as the Members of the House who are expelled from their parties on whose banner they had been elected to the House, are concerned, as they would be left completely vulnerable to the whims and fancies of the leaders of their parties - SC issued Rule in the said writ petitions i.e. no action should be taken against the petitioners under the anti-defection law and referred the matter to Larger Bench - Referred to Larger Bench.
