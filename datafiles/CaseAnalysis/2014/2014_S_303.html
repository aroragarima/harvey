M. R. Purushotham v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

C. Nagappan, Madan B. Lokur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 667; (2015) 3 SCC 247; 2015 CRLJ 72; 2014 (6) KarLJ 32; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 444; 2014(11) SCALE 467; 2015 (1) WLN(SC) 168

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Illegal Gratification

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 7 - Illegal gratification - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2), 7 of the Act - Trial Court passed acquittal order - State filed appeal before HC - HC set aside order of acquittal and convicted appellant - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC is sustainable -

Held, complainant PW1 did not support prosecution case insofar as demand by appellant is concerned. No other evidence was adduced by prosecution to prove demand made by appellant with the complainant. When PW1 himself had disowned what he has stated in his initial complaint before PW4 Inspector and there is no other evidence to prove that appellant had made any demand, evidence of PW3 and contents of complaint cannot be relied upon to conclude that said material furnishes proof of demand allegedly made by appellant. HC was not correct in holding demand alleged to be made by appellant as proved. Mere possession and recovery of currency notes from appellant without proof of demand will not bring home the offence u/s. 13(1)(d) of the Act and conviction and sentence imposed on appellant are liable to be set aside. Conviction imposed on appellant is set aside and he is acquitted. Appeal allowed.
