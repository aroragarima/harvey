Securities And Exchange Board Of India v M/S. Informetics Valuation And Rating Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 103; (2013) 9 SCC 532; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 863; 2013 (2) CompLJ 38; JT 2013 (3) SC 577; 2013(2) SCALE 715; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 426

Case Digest

Subject: Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Securities and Exchange Board of India (Credit Rating Agencies) Regulations, 1999

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Securities And Exchange Board Of India Act, 1992, ss. 11, 15Z and 30 - CRA Regulations, 1999, regns. 5, 6 and 7 - Order of Tribunal - Challenged - Sustainability - Appeal u/s. 15Z of Act was directed against the impugned judgment and final order passed by Tribunal by which the appeal filed by respondent was allowed, and the order passed by the Whole Time Member of SEBI and communication of SEBI was set aside - By the impugned order, Tribunal had remanded the matter back to the appellant to consider the application of the respondent seeking registration as Credit Rating Agency (CRA) without requiring the respondent to produce Audited Annual Accounts of the respondent's promoters for the two years ending December, 2010 - Whether order of Tribunal was sustainable - Held, SC considered the entire material and the submissions made by parties - Controversy raised revolved around the interpretation of the provisions contained in regn. 4(e), Form A r/w. regn. 7 of Regulations - Regulations were made in exercise of the powers conferred on the Board by s. 30 r/w. s. 11 of Act - S. 30 of Act empowered the Board by notification to make regulations consistent with the Act and to carry out the purposes of Act - S. 30 (2)(d) empowered the Board to make regulations with regard to the conditions subject to which certificate of registration was to be issued, the amount of fee to be paid for the certificate of registration and the manner of suspension or cancellation of certificate of registration u/s. 12 of Act - S. 11 of Act empowered the SEBI to take measures to protect the interest of investors and to regulate the security market by regulating and registering the working of stock progress and other intermediaries such as credit rating agencies, who might be associated with the securities market in any manner - Regn. 2(h) of Regulation defined CRA as body corporate, which was engaged in or proposes to be engaged in the business of rating of securities offered by way of public or rights issue - Regn. 2(b) of Regulation defined associate in relation to credit rating agency to include person (i) who, directly or indirectly, by himself, or in combination with relatives, owns or controls shared carrying not less than ten percent of the voting rights of the credit rating agency, or - (ii) in respect of whom the credit rating agency, directly or indirectly, by itself, or in combination with other persons, owns or controls shares carrying not less than ten percent of the voting rights, or - (iii) majority of the directors of which, own or control shares carrying not less than ten percent of the voting rights of the credit rating agency, or - (iv) whose director, officer or employee was also director, officer or employee of the credit rating agency - Regn. 3(1) of Regulation provided that any person proposing to commence any activity as credit rating agency should make application to the Board for the grant of certificate of registration for the purpose - Regn. 3(3) of Regulation provided that such application should be made to the Board in Form A of the Schedule of the Regulations - Reading of Regns. 4, 5 and 6 of Regulations together left no manner of doubt that the SEBI had no discretion not to reject the application if it did not satisfy the conditions laid down in Regns. 4 and 5 of Regulation - In fact, Regn. 4 of Regulation mandated that the Board should not consider application for registration under Regn. 3 of Regulation unless the applicant was promoted by person belonging to any of the categories - Similarly, Regn. 5 of Regulation mandated that the Board should not consider an application for the grant of certificate under regn. 3 of Regulation unless the applicant satisfied all the conditions which were set out u/cl. 5 of Regulation - Regn. 6 of Regulation again was mandatory in nature, which provided that application which was not complete in all respects or did not conform to the requirement of Regn. 5 of Regulation or instructions specified in Form A should be rejected by the Board - It appeared, therefore, that the intention of the legislature, as expressed through the regulations, was to put closure to the consideration of the application on the basis of the information submitted on the date of application - Board had the minimal discretion to extend the period for removal of objections upon hearing the applicant firstly for 30 days and thereafter for another 30 days - In other words, Regn. 7 of Regulation enabled the Board to ask for further information within the extended time stipulated in Regn. 6 of Regulation - For the purpose of processing of the application, information/material for removal of objections had to be provided within the time stipulated by Board - But the maximum period provided was sixty days - There was no scope under the regulations for the time to be extended any further - Information sought should be in relation to the five years preceding the date of the application - In view of the matter, HC was of the opinion that the directions issued by Tribunal that the Board could not have directed the respondent to produce the Audited Accounts for the two years beyond the date of the application, were in consonance with the provisions of Regulations - Under Regn. 7 of Regulation, Board would have the power to seek further information or clarification for the purpose of processing of the application - That further information would relate only to the basic information with regard to the Audited Accounts for the five years preceding the date of the application - Therefore, the observations made by SAT were perfectly justified - Order accordingly.
