Abhayanand Mishra v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 471; AIR 1961 SC 1698; 1961 CRLJ 822; 1962 (1) SCJ 183; [1962] 2 S.C.R. 241

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Admission Card

Summary: Criminal - Attempt to commit offence - Cheating - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 420 and 511 - Appellant submitted forged certificates to University to appear as private candidate for post-graduate examination - Appellant was convicted for attempting to cheat - Conviction of appellant u/s. 420 along with s. 511 was challenged - Whether conviction of appellant for attempt to cheat was justified? - Held, yes - Essential conditions required to constitute said offence of cheating (a) Intention to commit particular offence (b) Acting towards commission of offence after having made preparations - In present case, preparation was complete when appellant prepared application for submission to University - Dispatching of application amounted to attempting to commit offence cheating - Appellants conviction is justified - Appeal dismissed.
