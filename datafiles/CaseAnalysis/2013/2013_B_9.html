Union of India and others v B. V. Gopinath and others

Supreme Court of India

05 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 574; (2014) 1 SCC 352; AIR 2014 SC 88; 2013(6) ALL MR 462; 2013 (139) FLR 831; JT 2013 (12) SC 392; 2013(11) SCALE 162; 2013 (6) SLR 124

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Decision To Initiate

Summary: Service - Constitution - Central Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1965, r. 14 - Constitution of India,1950, art.311 - Chargesheet - Approval - Jurisdiction - Respondent no.1 joined Indian Revenue Service as Assistant Commissioner of Income Tax - Respondent no.1 was served with charge sheet u/r.14 of the Rules on allegation that he was alleged to have approached one Chartered Accountant for securing his transfer by offering bribe to P.A. to then Minister of State (Revenue) - Appeal was filed claiming that charge sheet was without jurisdiction as same was not approved by Finance Minister - CAT quashed impugned charge sheet - Petition was filed against impugned order before HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether impugned charge sheet/charge memo was non est in eye of law as it was not approved by disciplinary authority - Held, disciplinary proceedings against respondent were initiated in terms of r.14 of the Rules - Once disciplinary authority approved initiation of disciplinary proceedings, charge sheet could not be drawn up by authority other than disciplinary authority - That would destroy underlying protection guaranteed u/art. 311(1) of Constitution - Such procedure would do violence to protective provisions contained u/art.311(2) of Constitution - Such charge sheet could be issued upon approval by appointing authority i.e. Finance Minister - File was put up to Finance Minister by Director General of Income Tax (Vigilance) seeking approval of Finance Minister for sanctioning prosecution against one officer and for initiation of major penalty proceeding against officers mentioned in note including respondent - Thus, it appeared that charge memo was not put up for approval by Finance Minister - Therefore, approval granted by Finance Minister for initiation of departmental proceedings would not amount to approval of charge memo - Hence, charge sheet/charge memo against respondent no.1 was non est in eye of law as it was not approved by disciplinary authority - Appeals dismissed.
