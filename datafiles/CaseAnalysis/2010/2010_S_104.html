M/S. Kranti Associates Pvt. Ltd. & Anr. v Sh. Masood Ahmed Khan & Others

Supreme Court of India

08 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 731; (2010) 9 SCC 496; 2011 (2) ALD(SC) 54; 2010(6) ALL MR 992; 2010 (82) ALR 913; 2011 (2) AWC 1577; 2011 (273) E.L.T. 345; JT 2010 (9) SC 362; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 1033; 2010 (4) RCR(Civil) 600; 2010(9) SCALE 199; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 1070

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Railway Servants (Discipline and Appeal) Rules, 1968, Mineral and Concessions Rules

Summary: Consumer Protection - Practice & Procedure - Consumer Protection Act, 1986 - Appeal before the National Consumer Commission (NCC) - Cryptic order - Legality - Appellant and respondents challenged the State Commission order before the NCC by filing respective revision petitions - NCC disposed the revision of the parties by just confirming the State Commission order - Whether the NCC can pass an order without giving any reason by just affirming the State Commission order - Held, a quasi-judicial authority must record reasons in support of its conclusions - Reasons in support of decisions must be cogent, clear and succinct - A pretence of reasons or 'rubber-stamp reasons' is not to be equated with a valid decision making process - Therefore, NCC cannot, considering the way it is structured, dismiss the revision petition by refusing to give any reasons and by just affirming the order of the State Commission - Hence, impugned order of the NCC is set aside matter remanded back to NCC for deciding the matter by passing a reasoned order - Respondent's appeal was not properly considered by the State Commission - State Commission dismissed the respondent's appeal for the reasons given in its order in connection with the appeal of the appellant - Since the respondent has filed a separate appeal, it has a right to be heard independently in support of its appeal - State Commission denied respondent's right of appeal - Respondent's matter, therefore, remanded back to the State Commission - Appeal allowed.
