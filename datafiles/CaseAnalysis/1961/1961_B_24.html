Bhagwan Dayal v Mst. Reoti Devi

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 437; AIR 1962 SC 287; 1962 (1) SCJ 348; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 440

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Land & Property - Family & Personal - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 11 - Agra Tenancy Act, s. 271 - Hindu Law -(A) Whether decree in revenue court operates as res judicata in a subsequent suit in a Civil Court? - Held, subsequent suit not in nature of suits specified in sch. 4 of Agra Tenancy Act - Revenue Court had no exclusive jurisdiction to entertain a suit of nature of subsequent suit u/s. 230 of Agra Tenancy Act - Decision on said issue in Revenue Court could not operate as res-judicata u/s. 11 of CPC, 1908 - For necessary condition of competency of that court to try subsequent suit was lacking - (B) What is nature of evidence required to establish reunion of joint family partition? - Further held, agreement of reunion need not be express - But implied from conduct - Conduct to be of incontrovertible character - Appeal dismissed.
