Pukhraj v D. R. Kohli

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, J.L. Kapur, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 150; AIR 1962 SC 1559; 1963 (65) BomLR 281; 1984 ECR 121; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1360; 1964 (1) SCJ 281; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 866; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Customs; Indirect Tax; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Customs - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 19, 167(8) and 178 A - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947, ss. 8(1) and 23-A - (A) Whether s. 178 A of Sea Customs Act is ultra vires Constitution being violative of art. 19(1)? - Judgment of constitutional Bench has upheld validity of s. 178 A in Civil Appeals Nos. 408 to 410 of 1960 and other companion appeals - (B) Whether confiscation of gold was justified u/s. 167(8) because it has been found by High Court that appellant is not a person concerned in offence of importation of said gold? - Held, language in item (8) of s. 167 is clear and it permits imposition of a penalty in excess of Rs. 1000/- Appeal dismissed.
