Workmen of Williamson Magor and Company Limited v Williamson Magor and Company Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Baharul Islam, V.D. Tulzapurkar, A. Varadarajan

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 384; (1982) SCC (L&S) 42; (1982) 1 SCC 117; AIR 1982 SC 78; 1982 (44) FLR 71; 1982 LabIC 33; 1982 (3) LLJ 33; 1981(3) SCALE 1825; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 42; 1982 (1) SLJ 363; 1982 UJ 79

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Promotion - Whether promotion of workmen mentioned in annexure 'A' from General Grade to Special Grade or Supervisory Grade in preference to workmen mentioned in annexure 'B' is justified? - To what relief, if any, are workmen mentioned in annexure 'B' entitled? - Held, before Tribunal, parties led evidence and proved Exts. K-l and K-2, settlements arrived at between management of union earlier - Although in spite of allegations of mala fide, union has not been able to prove factual mala fide, in this case malice in law and effectual victimization are obvious due to fact that unjustified promotions of some junior persons were made superseding, without any reason or necessity, cases of a large number of senior persons - Interpretation of word 'victimization' in normal meaning is of being victim of unfair and arbitrary action, and hold that there was victimization of superseded workmen - Promotions/upgradations of three employees from General Grade to Special Grade clerks in preference to twelve workmen mentioned in annexure 'A', which is subject matter of first reference, namely, No. 3278IR/IR/11 L-13271 D/- 2-4-1974, and promotions/upgradations of persons mentioned in annexure 'A' from General Grade to Special Grade or Supervisor Grade in preference to workmen mentioned in annexure 'B', which is subject matter of second reference, namely, Reference No. 7537-132/71 D/- 10-12-1974, are cancelled - Appeal allowed.
