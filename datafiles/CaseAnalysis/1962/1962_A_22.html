Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay City I, Bombay v Amarchand N. Shroff, By His Heirs and Legal Representatives

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 478; AIR 1963 SC 1448; [1963] 48 ITR 59; 1963 (1) SCJ 411; [1963] Supp1 S.C.R. 699

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s.24B - Income of Deceased - Legal Representative - Income in Previous Year - Appellant/Revenue charged Income Tax on A and after death of A legal representative of A was charged with income tax u/s. 24B of the Act - HC ordered against revenue/appellant that Income Tax cannot be charged in hands of legal representative of A - Hence, instant appeal - Whether income received subsequent to previous year in which A died is liable to be assessed to income-tax u/s. 24B of the Act as his income in hands of his heirs and legal representatives - Held, amounts sought to be taxed and which have not to be liable to tax were those which were not received in previous year and were therefore not liable to tax in several years of assessment - Income which was not deemed by fiction to have been received by dead person and therefore they were not liable to be taxed as income of the deceased and not liable to be taxed in hands of the heirs and legal representatives who cannot be deemed to be assessees for the purpose of assessment in regard to those years - Appeals dismissed.
