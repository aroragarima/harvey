Harshendra Choubisa and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 July 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Venkatarama Reddi, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1986; (2002) 6 SCC 393; (2002) SCC (L&S) 913; AIR 2002 SC 2897; 2002 (4) AWC 3286; 2002 (95) FLR 285; JT 2002 (6) SC 553; 2002 LabIC 2964; 2002 (4) LLN 17; 2002(5) SCALE 441; 2003 (2) SLR 479; 2002 (4) SLT 621; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 309; 2002 (5) Supreme 224

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Service

Summary: Service - Municipalities & Local Governments - Eligibility - Bonus marks - Validity - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 - Rajasthan Panchayati Raj Act, 1994, s. 102 - Rajasthan Panchayati Raj Rules, 1996 - Appeals were filed against judgment of HC - Writ petition was filed by respondent who was applicant for post of Gram Sewak cum Paden Sachive - Result of written examination was declared and respondent was not selected - Respondent filed writ petition questioning circular dated 22-02-1999 as it provided for bonus marks to applicants belonging to districts and rural area of concerned district - Full Bench of HC held that giving of weightage on ground of residence in district and rural areas by adding bonus marks was unconstitutional - Appeals against judgment were disposed of - Judgment of HC had been applied to selection of Gram Sewaks by impugned judgment - HC directed preparation of fresh merit list of candidates without adding bonus marks on account of residence of any candidate - With this direction writ petition was disposed of - Judgments were referred to State Govt. (Panchayati Raj Department) by order issued instructions to Chief Executive Officers of Zila Parishads directed them to recast merit list of Gram Sewaks appointed after date of judgment of HC by excluding bonus marks and regulate appointments accordingly - Whether order passed by Sate govt. in view of judgments previously passed was justified - Held, there was no factual nor rational basis to treat each District as separate unit for purpose of offering public employment - It was wrong to assume that candidates belonging to rural areas would be better suited to serve those areas than candidates living in nearby towns - Criterion of merit could not be allowed to be diluted by taking resort to such artificial differentiation and irrelevant assumptions - Court further held that addition of bonus marks to applicants belonging to same district and rural areas of that district would amount to discrimination which falls foul of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Claims of respondents were ordered to be considered afresh in light of judgment vis a vis candidates appointed on or after 27-07-2000 or those in select list who were yet to be appointed - On such consideration, if those writ petitioners were found to have superior merit in case bonus marks of 10% and/or 5% were excluded, they should be offered appointments, if necessary, by displacing candidates appointed on or after 27-07-2000 - Appeals disposed of.
