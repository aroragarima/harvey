Dandu Lakshmi Reddy v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1291; (1999) 7 SCC 69; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1176; AIR 1999 SC 3255; AIR 1999 SCW 3235; 1999 CRLJ 4287; JT 1999 (6) SC 166; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 535

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 34 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 162 and 161 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 32 - Murder - Dying declaration - Deceased, was given in marriage to appellant about 8 years before her death - Deceased set on fire by appellant - Sub-Inspector of Police recorded her statement - In both dying declarations she attributed to appellant and his mother cause of her devastating burns - Whether two dying declarations contain nothing but truth? - First among such tests is to scrutinise whether there are inherent improbabilities in that version - Next test is whether there is any inherent contradiction therein - When sphere of scrutiny of dying declaration is a restricted area, court cannot afford to sideline material divergence relating to this very occasion of crime - Either context spoken to in one was wrong or that in other was wrong - Both could be reconciled with each other only with much strain as it relates to opportunity for culprit to commit offence - Adopting such a strain to detriment of accused in a criminal case is not a feasible course - Neighbours who gave evidence said that two persons were brainwashing deceased at hospital - Moreover parents said that deceased caught fire while cooking milk and was also mentally unsound - If court has even a slight doubt about mental soundness of author of dying declaration it would be unsafe to base a conviction on such a statement - Held, material contradiction as between two dying declarations regarding context in which deceased caught fire - As dying declaration tested on touchstones does not stand scrutiny it will be unsafe to convict any person on strength of such a fragile and rickety dying declaration - Sentence and conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
