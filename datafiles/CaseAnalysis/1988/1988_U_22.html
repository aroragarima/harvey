Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Central Excises and Salt and Additional Duties of Excises (Amendment) Act, 1980

Summary: Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, ss. 4, 2(f), sch 1, Tariff Item 19, 22 - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 173 B - Additional Duties of Excise (Goods of Special Importance) Act, 1957, s. 3 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(g), 246, sch. 7, List I, Entry 84 and Entry 97 - Manufacture of Grey fabric - (A) Processes of bleaching, dyeing, printing, sizing, shrink-proofing etc. - If manufacture - (B) Amendment in Act - Enlarging scope of word 'manufacture' constitutionally - (C) Amendment only in 1944, but no corresponding amendment in 1957 Act - Levy of additional duty - Legality - (D) Retrospective amending provisions - (E) Processed Grey-fabric - Assessable value - Computation of - Held, (A) Amount to manufacture - (B) Not ultra vires Entry 84, List I as amendment even though not covered by Entry 84, could be supported by Entry 97 - (C) Not illegal since what applies to main levy applies to additional duties as well - (D) Not violates art. 19(1)(g) - (E) Assessable value should not comprise only processing charges - Order accordingly.
