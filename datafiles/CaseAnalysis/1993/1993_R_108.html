Prestolite (India) Limited v Regional Director and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 October 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 519; (1994) Supp3 SCC 690; (1995) SCC (L&S) 202; AIR 1994 SC 521; 1995 (2) LLJ 622; 1995 (2) LLN 667

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Delay in payment - Penalty - Regional Director imposed damages on appellant-company on account of delay in payment of dues under the Act and so, appellant-company filed application u/s. 75 of the Act before Judge, ESI Court, but the same was dismissed - Hence, appeal before HC was preferred but the same was dismissed in terms of decision made in another appeal concerning similar matters - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order of Regional Director is sustainable.

Held, impugned order has been passed without indicating any reason whatsoever as to why grounds for delayed payment were not to be accepted. There is no indication as to why the imposition of damages at the rate specified in the order was required to be made. Simply because the appellant-company had not appeared in person and produced materials to support the objection, the same could not be discarded in limine and on contrary, objection should have been considered on merits. Hence, impugned orders are set aside and Regional Director is directed to rehear the matter. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Even if regulations prescribe general guidelines and upper limits at which imposition of damages could be made, yet, adjudicating authority should take into consideration mitigating circumstances in finally deciding the matter and should not act mechanically in applying uppermost limit.
