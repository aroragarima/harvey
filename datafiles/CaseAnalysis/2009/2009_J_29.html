Chairman, Disciplinary Authority, Rani Lakshmi Bai Kshetriya Gramin Bank v Jagdish Sharan Varshney and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 387; (2009) 4 SCC 240; AIR 2009 SC 3276; 2009 (2) CLR 828; JT 2009 (4) SC 519; 2009 (3) LLJ 624; 2009(4) SCALE 169; [2009] 5 S.C.R. 326; 2009 (5) SLR 512

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Rani Laxmibai Kshetriya Gramin Bank Officers and Employees Service Rules, 2000

Summary: Service - Rani Laxmibai Kshetriya Gramin Bank Officers and Employees Service Rules, 2000 - Punishment of fixing his pay scale at Rs.7780/- by reducing the increment of time scale by 15 under reg. 38(1) - Whether order of affirmation needs reasons? - Held, whether there was an application of mind or not can only be disclosed by some reasons, at least in brief, mentioned in the order of the appellate authority - It is an essential requirement of the rule of law that some reasons, at least in brief, must be disclosed in a judicial or quasi-judicial order, even if it is an order of affirmation - Matter is remanded to the appellate authority to decide the appeal - Appeal partly allowed.
