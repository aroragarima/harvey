Ganpatbhai Mahijibhai Solanki v State Of Gujarat & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 401; (2008) 12 SCC 353; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 986; 2008 (2) AWC 1252; JT 2008 (3) SC 452; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 982; 2008 (151) PLR 458; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 382; 2008(3) SCALE 556; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 938

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte, Human Rights Act 1998, Urban Land (Ceiling & Regulation) Repeal Act, 1999

Summary: Urban Land Ceiling Act, 1976 - Whether suppression of a material fact would entail allowing of an application for condonation of 2205 days delay in filing a review application? - Held, fraud vitiates all solemn acts - If an order is obtained by reason of commission of fraud, even the principles of natural justice are not required to be complied with for setting aside the same - Held, allottees have acquired a statutory right - Only because the State was not aware of the factual position and/or the legal implication of the 1999 Act which led to withdrawal of the writ petition from the High Court, the same by itself may not be sufficient to deprive the allottees from their legal right to hold the said land - Appeal dismissed.
