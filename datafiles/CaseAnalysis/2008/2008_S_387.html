Somesh Tiwari v Union Of India And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2546; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 411; (2009) 2 SCC 592; AIR 2009 SC 1399; 2009 (1) ESC 124; JT 2009 (1) SC 96; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 727; 2009(1) SCALE 63; [2008] 17 S.C.R. 711; 2009 (3) SLR 506

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Transfer - Appellant was transferred despite allegations made against him were found to be untrue in enquiry - On appeal, tribunal upheld transfer order - Appellant filed writ petition against said order - HC quashed an order of transfer passed against the appellant on the ground that order of transfer could not be passed on the basis of an anonymous complaint alleging caste-bias against appellant, which on enquiry were found to be incorrect - HC further held that appellant would not be entitled to salary for the period commencing 15 days after the modified order of transfer to Ahmedabad till the date he again joined his duties at the original place - Hence, present appeal - Whether HC while quashing an order of transfer passed against the appellant was correct in directing that he would not be entitled to salary for the period commencing 15 days after the modified order of transfer to Ahmedabad was passed till the date he again joined his duties at the original place? - Held, HC while exercising its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of the Constitution of India must consider the fact of each case - Mechanical application of the normal rule 'no work no pay' may in a case of this nature, be found to be wholly unjust - No absolute proposition of law in this behalf can be laid down - Interest of justice would be subserved if during the period from 28-12-2005 till his joining his post at Bhopal, the appellant is treated to be on leave, respondents are directed to pass an appropriate order invoking the leave rules applicable in this behalf - HC's order modified to the aforesaid extent - Appeal partly allowed.
