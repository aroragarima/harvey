Special Land Acquisition Officer & Anr v M.K. Rafiq Saheb

Supreme Court of India

05 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 418; (2011) 7 SCC 714; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 105; 2011 (88) ALR 493; 2011 (5) AWC 5110; 2011 (3) CLT(SC) 1; JT 2011 (7) SC 277; 2011 (6) KarLJ 224; 2011 (4) MPLJ 242; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 700; 2011 (114) RD 425; 2011(7) SCALE 152; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 1088; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2089; 2011 (4) WLN(SC) 64

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894,ss. 4(1) and 18 - Land acquisition - Enhancement of compensation - A notification was published u/s. 4(1) of the Act for the acquisition of the land of the respondents - The Special Land Acquisition Officer (SLAO) passed an award granting compensation at Rs.1,30,000/- per acre along with statutory benefits to the respondents - SLAO also held that the impugned lands were agricultural land and no sale transactions relating to the same were available - Respondents filed a reference u/s. 18 of the Act for enhancement of compensation - The Reference Court enhanced compensation to Rs.4,00,000/- per acre and also awarded statutory benefits - The Reference Court also held that it could not be said that the impugned land was agricultural land for all practical purposes since it was situated by the side of a residential locality and was in the midst of a highly developed industrial locality - The respondent, still dissatisfied with the compensation awarded, filed an appeal before the HC who enhanced the compensation to Rs. 35,17,470/- per acre and also awarded all other statutory benefits - Hence, the instant appeal - Whether the quantum of compensation awarded by the HC in a land acquisition dispute was excessive or not? - Held, the judgment of the HC was well reasoned and well considered - The only issue was that Ex. P-5, which was relied upon by the HC, relates to a small piece of land, whereas the acquisition was of a larger piece of land - It was not an absolute rule that when the acquired land was a large tract of land, sale instances relating to smaller pieces of land cannot be considered as there were certain circumstances when sale deeds of small pieces of land can be used to determine the value of acquired land which is comparatively large in area - However, HC made a 50% deduction since the sale instance Ex. P-5 related to a smaller piece of land - The said deduction should be increased to 60%, which was fair, just and reasonable in the circumstances - Impugned order of the HC could be modified to the extent - Appeal dismissed.
