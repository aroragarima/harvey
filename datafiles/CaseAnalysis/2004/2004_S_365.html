Deoraj v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 245; (2004) 4 SCC 697; AIR 2004 SC 1975; 2004 (5) AWC 4246; JT 2004 (4) SC 440; 2004 (3) MahLJ 343; 2004 (3) MLJ(SC) 155; 2004(4) SCALE 274; 2004 (3) Supreme 126

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 136 - Maharashtra Co-operative Society Act, 1960, ss. 144Y, 73-B, 73G - Appellant sought for his being declared duly elected Chairman of Sangh -SC in its exercise of jurisdiction under art.136 of Constitution does not interfere with orders of interim nature passed by HC or Tribunals -however, if a foolproof case for grant of interim relief was made out in favour of petitioner in HC on basis of material available before Court - Prayer for grant of interim relief as made by WPer/appellant ought to be allowed - Respondents directed to announce result of election in accordance with election programme dated 11/12/2003 post haste and act accordingly - Appeal allowed.
