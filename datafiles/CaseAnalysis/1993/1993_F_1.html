State of West Bengal v Falguni Dutta and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Ahmadi, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1725; (1993) 3 SCC 288; (1993) SCC (Cr) 815; 1993 (22) ALR 120; 1993 (2) Crimes 321; 1993 CRLR 437; JT 1993 (3) SC 288; 1993(2) SCALE 743; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 570

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Essential Commodities Act, 1955 - Police party raided the business premises and godown of the respondents on 16-3-1984, and in the presence of the respondent, seized certain essential commodities, stored in contravention of certain orders, u/s. 3, r/w. s.5, of the 1955 Act - Accused was arrested on the same day but the charge sheet was submitted after an expiry of a period of 6 months, from the date of arrest - Accused was discharged by the HC - Hence, the present appeal - Whether the accused should be discharged - Held, that though the proposition that in the case of the offence punishable u/s. 7(1)(a)(ii) of the 1955 Act which was tried by a Special Court constituted u/s. 12-A, of the 1955 Act, the provision of sub-s. (5) of S. 167 of the CrPC, got attracted if the investigation had not been completed within the period allowed by that sub-section, was accepted, but it was difficult to sustain that part of the order of HC by which the order of Special Court taking cognizance of the offence on the police report, i.e., charge-sheet submitted u/s. 173(2) of the CrPC, came to be quashed - Special Court was competent to entertain the police report restricted to six months investigation and take cognizance on the basis thereof, so it be directed that the Special Court would proceed with the trial from that stage onwards and complete the same as early as possible in accordance with law - Appeal partly allowed.
