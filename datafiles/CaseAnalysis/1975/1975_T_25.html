Union Of India & Ors. v Tata Iron & Steel Co. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

31 January 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, A.N. Ray, Hans Raj Khanna

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 96; (1975) 1 SCC 789; AIR 1975 SC 769; 1975 CTR 40; 1978 (2) E.L.T. 439; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 418; 1975 TAXLR 1446; 1975 UJ 205

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, sch. 1, item 26AA(iii) - Whether a product is 'skelp' or 'strip' - Taxing authorities not at all certain about a uniform definition of 'skelp' - Distinguishing it from 'strip' - No standard or test - Interference by High Court - Legality - Held, it is not for the HC nor for the Supreme Court to come to a conclusion on facts as to whether the product can truly come under the description of skelp - Appeal Dismissed.
