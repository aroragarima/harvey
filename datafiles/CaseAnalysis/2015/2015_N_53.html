Rajasthan Housing Board v New Pink City Nirman Sahkari Samiti Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Arun Mishra, A.K. Sikri, H.L. Dattu

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 300; (2015) 7 SCC 601; AIR 2015 SC 2126; 2015 (4) CTC 205; JT 2015 (4) SC 435; 2015(5) SCALE 716; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 365

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, Orissa Scheduled Areas Transfer of Immovable Property (By Scheduled Tribes) Regulation, 1956, Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, Constitution Scheduled Castes Order, 1950

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 - Notice of award - Limitation - State Govt. issued notification u/s. 4 of the Act and acquired land for purpose of housing scheme and later possession was handed over u/s. 9 of the Act to Housing Board - Objections filed by Society were rejected and later, Award was passed by Land Acquisition Officer (LAO) and notice u/s. 12(2) of the Act was issued to Society in respect of Awards passed - Society applied for reference u/s. 18 of Act and one of the Khatedars also sought reference and Civil Court answered reference determining compensation at Rs.260 per sq.yd. and objection raised by Housing Board with regard to entitlement of Society u/s. 42 of Rajasthan Tenancy Act was not sustained - On appeal, HC vide impugned judgment reduced compensation to Rs.100 per sq yd. and later DB of HC affirmed said award and also directed to consider allotment of 25% of developed land in view of circular dt.27-10-2005 - Hence, instant appeals - Whether reference made in respect of four cases in which award was passed on 30-11-1982 was within period of limitation.

Held, Society had preferred objections and was aware of the land acquisition process and determination of compensation and has filed objections which stood rejected on 4-9-1982. Thus, the constructive knowledge of the award is fairly attributable to it when it was so passed. Further, Society had actively participated in the other pending cases with respect to determination of compensation in which award had been passed on 2-1-1989. Thus, the reference sought on the strength of the notice u/s. 12(2) of Act issued and received on 31-12-1988 would not provide limitation to the Society for seeking reference with respect to the four cases in which the award was passed on 30-11-1982 as notice to it was wholly unnecessary in view of rejection of its objection on the ground that it was not having right, title or interest in the land. Thus, it could not be said to be 'person interested' in view of the order dt.4-9-1982. Hence, in view of conjoint reading of ss. 12(2) and 18(2) of the Act, it was not open to the LAO to refer the case to the Civil Court on the basis of the time barred application. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Land & Property - Sale in favour of person not belonging to SC - Whether in view of s. 42 of the Act, the transaction entered into by Society with original Khatedars are void and whether on that basis it had right to maintain the reference and to claim compensation.

Held, as per affidavits of Khatedars filed by Society, caste of Khatedars been mentioned as 'Bairwa' caste which is scheduled caste. There is not even an evasive denial that Khatedars do not belong to SC. Even in additional affidavit filed on behalf of the Society in the wake of the rejoinder filed by the petitioner in reply to the counter affidavit of respondent no. 2, the case of the original Khatedars has not been disputed. Hence, original Khatedars are 'Bairwaâ by caste which is Scheduled Caste and are entitled to the protection of the provisions contained in s. 42 of the Act. Hence, as s. 42 of the Act makes sale in favour of a person not belonging to Schedule Caste void, agreements which were purportedly entered into by Society with the Khatedars are void. Further, the right to claim compensation could not be enforced by Society on basis of such transaction as that would defeat the very object of the Act and the Constitutional provisions. The right to claim compensation accrues from right, title or interest in the land and that could not be transferred by virtue of mandate of s. 42 to juristic person like Society. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Land & Property - Developed land - Whether direction of HC to consider allotment of land and quantum of compensation awarded is sustainable.

Held, prevalent instructions which have been modified had not conferred any right on Society or the Khatedars to claim the developed land. It was not a case of surrender of land; thus there was no question of the provisions of the circular dt.27-10-2005 being applied as the said circular was in the form of guidelines for future acquisitions where Khatedars surrendered their lands and award has not been passed. Hence, DB of HC gravely erred in law while issuing directions which were wholly unwarranted and uncalled for. However, quantum of compensation awarded is appropriate. Hence, only Khatedars, in case some of them have died, their legal representatives would be entitled to receive the compensation which was determined. Further, direction of HC to grant 25% of developed land is set aside and appeals of Rajasthan Housing Board and Khatedars are allowed and other appeals are dismissed. Appeals disposed of.
