Ramesh Hirachand Kundanmal v Municipal Corporation of Greater Bombay and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Fathima Beevi, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 964; (1992) 2 SCC 524; AIR 1992 SCW 846; 1992 (2) AWC 1134; 1992 (3) Bom.C.R. 110; 1992 (1) CCC 594; JT 1992 (2) SC 116; 1992 (2) MLJ(SC) 55; 1992 (1) RCR(Rent) 644; 1992 RD 227; 1992(1) SCALE 530; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 1; 1992 (2) UJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution

Summary: Civil Procedure - Necessary party - Bombay Municipal Corporation Act, s. 351 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 1, rr. 3 and 10 - Appellant is in possession of service station created on land held by respondent 2 (Hindustan Petroleum Corporation Limited) as lessee - Dispute between appellant and respondent 1(Municipal Corporation) is demolition of unauthorised construction in pursuance to notice u/s. 351 of Act - Appellant instituted suit before City Civil Court, challenging validity of notice and for injunction restraining Municipal Corporation from demolishing structures - No notice has been issued to respondent 2 by Municipal Corporation - Respondent 2 applied for being impleaded as additional defendant in suit - Court directed appellant to add respondent 2 as defendant - Appellant filed Writ Petition challenging correctness of order - High Court dismissed Writ Petition - Whether respondent 2 is a necessary or proper party? - Held, proper and necessary parties, as they have interest in subject matter of litigation and presence will be necessary and proper to effectively adjudicate upon and determine cause of action in suit - Notice does not relate to that structure but is in relation to two chattels started to have been created by present appellant unauthorisedly - Respondent 2 had no interest in these chattels and demolition of same in pursuance to notice is not a mater which affects legal rights of respondent - No direct interest in subject matter of litigation and addition of respondent would result a new cause of action and widen issue - Appeal allowed.
