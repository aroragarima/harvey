B. G.Somanna & Sons Etc. v State Of Andhra Pradesh & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 July 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, I.D. Dua, D.G. Palekar

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 304; (1974) 3 SCC 235; AIR 1972 SC 2227; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 708; 1972 (30) STC 281; 1972 TAXLR 2499

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Indirect Tax - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957 s.5 - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 s. 15(a) - Purchases - Turnover - Liable to pay tax - Challenged - Appellants were millers and filed writ before HC against proceeding for assessment of Sales Tax and challenged validity of Item 6 of Sch. III of 1957 Act relating to levy of tax contravening provision u/s. 15(a) of 1956 Act, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals.

Held, turnover relating to the purchases became charged with the liability to pay tax as soon as those purchases were made by the assessee-millers. Therefore, whenever a miller purchases groundnut, the turnover relating to that purchase becomes exgible to tax subject to such exemptions as may be given under the Act. Thus, as soon as a first miller purchases groundnut, the turnover relating to that purchase, becomes liable to tax and the act of purchase by a miller attracts the liability to pay tax u/s. 5 r/w Schedule III item 6 of 1957 Act. Therefore, his subsequent dealings in those goods become irrelevant and each of the appellants becomes liable to the payment of tax as a purchasing miller just as a last dealer would be liable on the purchases made by him. Hence, the last dealer and the miller, who purchases presumably to convert the groundnuts into other products, are placed on an equal footing and there is no possibility of double taxation or of taxation of the same product at more than one point of purchase. Appeals dismissed.
