Â Madhya Pradesh Industries Limited v Income Tax Officer, Nagpur

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, G.K. Mitter, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 207; (1970) 2 SCC 32; AIR 1970 SC 1011; [1970] 77 ITR 268; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 266

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Constitution, art. 226 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 34(1)(a) - (A) Writ petition u/art. 226 - Challenging jurisdiction of ITO - To issue notice u/s. 34(1)(a) 1922 Act - Ground, officer had no reasons to believe - Assertion not controverted by filing affidavit or production of relevant material by ITO - No proceeding before issuing notice to enable him to form the necessary belief - Proceedings - Jurisdiction - Held, proceedings quashed as being without jurisdiction - No jurisdiction - Appeals Allowed.
