Som Lal v Vijay Laxmi & Ors

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Mathur, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 436; (2008) 11 SCC 413; AIR 2008 SC 2088; 2008 (2) ESC 263; JT 2008 (4) SC 403; 2008 (151) PLR 435; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 760; 2008(4) SCALE 554; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 49; 2008 (1) UC 622

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Punjab State Election Commission Act, 1994, Punjab Panchayati Raj Act, 1994

Summary: Punjab Panchayati Raj Act, 1994 - Election - High Court held that appellant was holding the whole-time salaried office of a statutory body as he was on the rolls of the Market Committee, Sirsa as Fireman on 29.6.2003 and he was disqualified from contesting the election as Sarpanch, Gram Panchayat - Appeal against - Held, a salaried employee of any local authority, statutory corporation or Board or a Co- operative Society can not be held to have held the office of profit under s. 11 - Appeal allowed.
