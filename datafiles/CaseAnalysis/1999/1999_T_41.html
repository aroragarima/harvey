State Bank of India and Others v T. J. Paul

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1057; (1999) 4 SCC 759; (1999) SCC (L&S) 922; AIR 1999 SC 1994; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 802; 1999 (3) ESC 1708; 1999 (82) FLR 497; JT 1999 (3) SC 385; 1999 LabIC 2091; 1999 (2) LLJ 51; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 1060; 1999 (5) Supreme 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Bank of Cochin Service Code, Paras. 22(iv) and 22(v) - Gross misconduct - Bank employee - Likelihood of serious loss due to negligence - Proof of - Held, is not necessary - Substitution of punishment of dismissal by punishment of removal by appellate authority - Improper, as Para. 22 (v) not provide 'removal' as punishment for serious misconduct.
