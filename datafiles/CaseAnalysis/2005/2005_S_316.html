State Of Punjab & Ors. v Amar Nath Goyal & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

11 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 477; (2005) 6 SCC 754; (2005) SCC (L&S) 910; AIR 2006 SC 171; 2005 (4) ESC 489; 2005 (106) FLR 1062; JT 2005 (7) SC 301; 2005 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 424; 2005(6) SCALE 409; 2006 (1) SLR 145; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 549; 2005 (6) Supreme 469; 2005 (3) UPLBEC 2976

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Service - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 14 - Punjab Civil Services Rules - Central Civil Services (Pension) Rules, 1972 - Whether decision of Central and State Govts to restrict revision of quantum of gratuity as well as increased ceiling of gratuity consequent upon merger of a portion of dearness allowance into dearness pay reckonable for purpose of calculating gratuity, was irrational or arbitrary? - Held, cut-off date has been fixed as 01-04-1995 on a very valid ground, namely, that of financial constraints - Fixing of cut-off date was not arbitrary, irrational and had rational basis or that it does not offend art. 14 - Appeals disposed of.
