Â P. Vajravelu Mudaliar v Special Deputy Collector, Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, S.M. Sikri

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 380; AIR 1965 SC 1017; 1964 (2) MLJ(SC) 173; 1964 (2) SCJ 703; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 614

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19, 31 and 32 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4(1), 5(a) and 17(4) - Land Acquisition (Madras Amendment) Act, 1961 - Madras State Housing Board Act, 1961 - Madras Town Planning Act, 1920 - Notification - Acquisition - Public purpose - Subsequently enacted amending act providing for acquisition of lands for housing schemes and laying down principles for fixing compensation different from those prescribed in Principal Act - 'compensation' in art. 31(2) - Estate - (A) Whether Amendment Act, 1961 is infringing provisions of Constitution? - (B) What is effect of ouster of jurisdiction of court to question law on ground that 'compensation' provided by law is not adequate? - Held, (A) object is to acquire lands for housing schemes at low price, for achieving that object, any land falling in any of said categories can be acquired under Amending Act, for public purpose any such land can be acquired under Principal Act - Amending Act clearly infringes art. 14 of Constitution and is void - (B) Art. 31A would apply only to a law made for acquisition by State of any 'estate' or any rights therein or for extinguishment or modification of such rights if such acquisition, extinguishment or modification is connected with agrarian reform - Said law cannot be questioned on ground that compensation provided by that law is not adequate - Order accordingly.
