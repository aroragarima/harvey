Satish Kumar Sharma v Bar Council of H. P

Supreme Court of India

03 January 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

Shivaraj V. Patil, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20363; (2001) 2 SCC 365; (2001) SCC (L&S) 422; AIR 2001 SC 509; 2001 (3) G.L.R. 1867; JT 2001 (1) SC 236; 2001 (1) MLJ(SC) 166; 2001(1) SCALE 23; 2001 (1) SCJ 169; [2001] 1 S.C.R. 34; 2001 (2) SLJ 355; 2001 (1) SLT 318; 2001 (1) Supreme 56; 2001 (1) UC 405; 2001 (1) UPLBEC 601

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges

Keywords: Right To Practise, Enrolment As An Advocate

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Bar Council of India Rules, r. 49 - Advocates Act, 1961, ss. 24(1)(e), 28(2)(d) and 35(3)(d) - Advocates (Right To Take Up Law Teaching) Rules, 1979, r. 3 - Appellant has assailed resolution of respondent withdrawing his enrollment as well as order of HC upholding it - Appellant was appointed as Assistant (Legal) - Held, no rules were framed by respondent entitling a law officer appointed as a full-time salaried employee coming within meaning of para 3 of r. 49 to enroll as an advocate - Such an enrollment has to come from rules made u/s. 28(2)(d) r/w s. 24(1)(e) of Act - Hence it necessarily follows that if there is no rule in this regard, there is no entitlement - At any rate from orders of appointment it cannot be said that he was/is required to act or plead in courts on behalf of employer mainly or exclusively so as to come within meaning of 'Law Officer' for purpose of r. 49 - Mere occasional appearances in some courts on behalf of board even if they be, could not bring appellant within meaning of 'Law Officer' in terms of para 3 of r. 49 - Appeal dismissed.
