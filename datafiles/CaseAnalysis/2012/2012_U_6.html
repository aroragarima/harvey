State Of Orissa & Ors. v Ujjal Kumar Burdhan

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

D.K. Jain, Anil R. Dave

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 91; (2012) 4 SCC 547; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 802; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1773; 2012 CRLJ 2015; 2012 (2) JCC 1218; JT 2012 (3) SC 333; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 782; 2012 (1) OLR 897; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 467; 2012(3) SCALE 508; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 512

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Rice and Paddy Procurement (Levy) and Restriction on Sale and Movement Order, 1982

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Irregularities - Departmental Inquiry - Quashed - Sustainability - On receipt of complaint, civil supply department initiated inquiry against relating to processing of paddy for and on behalf of FCI - Preliminary inquiry conducted by Food and Supply department revealed certain irregularities in procurement and milling of paddy by respondent - Subsequent departmental inquiry recommended initiation of a proper administrative action against respondent - State Government directed Vigilance Cell of Police department to conduct a preliminary inquiry regarding alleged criminal acts - Writ petition was filed for issue of enforcement certificate and interim order - Another Writ petition was filed before HC for quashing inquiry proceedings initiated by State Vigilance department, HC by its impugned orders quashed investigation - Aggrieved instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in quashing investigation initiated by Vigilance Department - Held, HC had committed a grave error of fact in observing that respondent had been exonerated in two inquiries held previously as both inquiry reports had in fact concluded that respondent had committed serious irregularities and proper action needs to be initiated against him - Those inquiries were departmental inquiries and what had been quashed by impugned judgment was initiation of police investigation - Impugned order noted that in view of arbitration agreement between agent and Government, all alleged violations fell within purview of, Act and therefore, respondent could not be held liable for any criminal offence - HC, interference with investigation was totally unwarranted and therefore, impugned order cannot be sustained - Appeal allowed.
