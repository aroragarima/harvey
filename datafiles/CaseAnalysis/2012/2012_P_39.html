Promode Dey v State Of West Bengal

Supreme Court of India

22 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Patnaik, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 96; (2012) 4 SCC 559; AIR 2012 SC 1598; 2012 CRLJ 2086; JT 2012 (3) SC 399; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 285; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 380; 2012(3) SCALE 577; [2012] 3 S.C.R. 887

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Murder - Substantial evidence - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant allegedly committed an offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced him to suffer for rigorous imprisonment for life on the basis of sole testimony of a child witness (PW2) - On appeal, HC confirmed order of Trial Court - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that prosecution was not able to prove that he had committed murder of the deceased beyond reasonable doubt - Whether prosecution proved guilty of appellant beyond reasonable doubt - Held, offence was proved against appellant by substantive evidence of PW 2 and the evidence of PW 1, PW 8, PW 11 and by the fact of recovery of a weapon used for crime at the instance of appellant - Impugned order sustained - Appeal dismissed.
