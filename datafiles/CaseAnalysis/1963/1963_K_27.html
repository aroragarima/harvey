Â Prativa Bose v Kumar Rupendra Deb Raikat and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 May 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, S.K. Das, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 188; AIR 1965 SC 540; [1964] 4 S.C.R. 69

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Family & Personal

Keywords: Inheritance, Bengal, Agra And Assam Civil Courts Act, 1887, Bengal Wills and Intestacy Regulation (5 of 1799)

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure Code, 1908- Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Bengal Wills and Intestacy Regulation, 1799 - Whether s. III and not s. IV of Regulation applies to facts of case? - Held, ss. III and IV cover entire possibilities about heirs of deceased - Former deals when there be only one heir and latter when there be more heirs than one - It is only when such a dispute between various claimants is brought before court that it gets seized of matter and, on a regular suit being preferred, first step it had to take suo motu was to take good and sufficient security from party in possession who had obviously taken possession in defiance of provisions of first part of s. IV - On other hand, in case of deceased leaving a single heir, s. III permits heir to take possession of estate peacefully and he takes possession lawfully - Any rival claimant, challenging his title to property has therefore to establish his case in court of law according to procedure laid down -S. III covers case of persons who would like to have their shares continue as a joint undivided estate and also of those who would like to have their shares separate - Section IV then provides that in case of those who would like to hold property as a joint undivided estate, a manager for their joint estate was to be appointed under rules contained in ss. XXIII to XXVI of Regulation VIII of 1793 - It is not correct as observed by HC, that s. III and first part of s. IV of Regulation do not cover cases where each of several persons claims to be single heir and where out of several persons some claim to be heirs while some others also claim to be heirs - Second part of s. IV does not apply to case where deceased dies intestate leaving only one heir entitled to succeed to entire estate, a case which is covered by s. III of this Regulation - Appeal allowed.
