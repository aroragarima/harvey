Babubhai Udesinh Parmar v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

24 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1294; (2006) 12 SCC 268; (2007) 1 SCC (Cr) 702; AIR 2007 SC 420; 2007 CRLJ 786; 2007 (1) G.L.R. 742; 2006 (3) JCC 2128; 2007 (1) RCR(Criminal) 862; 2006(12) SCALE 385; [2006] Supp9 S.C.R. 401

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Self Defence, Police Protection

Summary: Criminal - Confession by accused - Indian Evidence Act, 1872,s. 24 - Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 164 - Confession of appellant was recorded - He was produced before magistrate after he was in judicial custody for 16 days - Magistrate failed to test voluntariness of confession - Whether appellant can be convicted on basis of this confession? - Held, no - Mandatory safeguards provided u/s. 164 of CrPC, 1973 not complied with - Serious inconsistencies were also found in evidence of witnesses - Conviction set aside - Appeal allowed.
