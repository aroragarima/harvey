Â R. Chitralekha and Another v State of Mysore and Others

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Raghubar Dayal, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1964 SC 1823; [1964] 6 S.C.R. 368

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Education; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Pith And Substance

Summary: Constitution - Education - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 15 (4) - Mysore University Act, 1956 - Reservation - Validity, of orders made by Govt. of Mysore in respect of admissions to Engineering and Medical Colleges in State of Mysore - Govt. by an order defined backward classes and directed that 30 percent of seats in professional and technical colleges and institutions shall be reserved for them and 18 percent to Scheduled Castes and Scheduled Tribes - Interpretation of classes as castes? - Held, laying down of criteria for ascertainment of social and educational backwardness of a class is a complex problem depending upon many circumstances which may vary from State to State and even from place to place in a State - But under no circumstances a class can be equated to a 'caste', though caste of an individual or a group of individual may be considered along with other relevant factors in putting him in a particular class - Court further held, if in a given situation caste is excluded in ascertaining a class within meaning of art. 15 (4), it does not vitiate classification if it satisfied other tests - Appeals dismissed.
