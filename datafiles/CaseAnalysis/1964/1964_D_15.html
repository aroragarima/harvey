Â K. L. Johar and Company v Deputy Commercial Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 373; AIR 1965 SC 1082; 1965 (1) MLJ(SC) 95; 1965 (1) SCJ 541; [1965] 2 S.C.R. 112; 1965 (16) STC 213

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Sale Price, Sales Tax Authorities, Second Sale

Summary: Sales Tax - Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 2(h) - Constitution - (A) Whether hire purchase transactions carried out by appellant can be made subject to sales tax under Act on basis of Explanation I of s. 2(h)? - State legislature when it proceeds to legislate Entry 54 of List II of Sch.7 to Constitution, can only tax sale within meaning of that word as defined in Sale of Goods Act, 1939 - Sales tax authorities may split up hire into two parts, namely, amount paid as consideration for use of vehicle so long as it was property of owner, and payment for option on a future date to purchase vehicle at a nominal price - It is therefore for sales tax authorities to find out price of vehicle on which tax has to be paid - Held, Explanation I as it stands is beyond legislative competence of state legislature - Direction issued to sales tax authorities to determine price - Appeal partly allowed.
