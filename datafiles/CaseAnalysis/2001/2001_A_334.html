N. Kamalam (Dead) and Another v Ayyasamy and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, A.P. Misra

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20055; (2001) 7 SCC 503; AIR 2001 SC 2802; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 69; 2002 (1) AllCJ 266; 2001 (44) ALR 737; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 272; JT 2001 (6) SC 219; 2001 (3) MLJ(SC) 150; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 193; 2001(5) SCALE 65; 2001 (3) SCJ 75; 2001 (5) SLT 659; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 272; 2001 (5) Supreme 689; 2001 (2) UC 613

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Wills Act, 1837

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, ss. 3 and 59 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 45, 47, 67 and 68 - Code of Civil Propcedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 27 - Wills Act, 1837 - Indian Succession Act, 1925, ss. 59 and 63 - Administration of Justice Act, 1982, s. 9 - Wills Act, 1837, s. 9 - One-half portion of eastern side has been purchased by plaintiff-appellants from two persons viz Amasaveni and Attiammal, being two sisters who inherited property by virtue of a will executed by their father, one Masaney Gowder in their favour on 29-01-1969 - Respondents contended that suit property was purchased from funds of joint family in name of Byrammal during 1922 and suit property has always been treated as joint family property and as such Masaney Gowder has had no right to execute a will over half portion of suit property on eastern side, neither was he eligible to sell same - Learned subordinate judge records a finding that it has not been proved as per law with sufficient evidence that Masaney Gowder had executed will - Admittedly, two attesting witnesses to will being exhibit A-I have not been examined in proof of due execution thereof - (A) Whether rejection of additional evidence by appellate court was erroneous? - Held, a plain reading of O. 41, r. 27 would depict that rejection of claim for production of additional evidence after a period of 10 years from date of filing of appeal, as noticed above, cannot be termed to be erroneous or an illegal exercise of discretion - (B) Is will valid? - Further held, s. 63 of Indian Succession Act r/w s. 68 of Evidence Act and s. 3 of Transfer of Property Act makes a mandatory obligation to have document attested and evidence of such attestation be made available before court at time of trial - Animus to attest, in this case, is not available, so far as scribe is concerned, he is not a witness to will but a mere writer of will - Statutory requirement as noticed above cannot thus be transposed in favour of writer, rather goes against propounder since both witnesses are named therein with detailed address and no attempt has been made to bring them or to produce them before court so as to satisfy judicial conscience - Appeal dismissed.
