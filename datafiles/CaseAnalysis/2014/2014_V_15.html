Precious Jewels and another v Varun Gems

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 498; 2014 (5) AWC 4353; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3284; 2014 (60) PTC 465; 2014(9) SCALE 146; 2014 (3) WLN(SC) 138

Case Digest

Subject: Intellectual Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Intellectual Property - Practice & Procedure - Trade Marks Act, 1999, s.35 - Trade mark infringement - Sustainability - Plaintiff/defendant claiming trade mark of their surname 'RAKYAN' filed a suit praying, inter alia, that the defendants/appellants should be restrained from doing their business in the name and style of 'NEENA AND RAVI RAKYAN' - In the said suit, an application seeking interim relief was filed whereby it was prayed that the defendants should be restrained from doing the business in the name and style of 'NEENA AND RAVI RAKYAN' - By virtue of the impugned order, the defendants were restrained from doing their business in the concerned name and therefore, the appellants had approached HC - Whether appellants were rightly held guilty of infringing trademark of another business man -

Held, as the suit was pending for its final disposal and HC was merely concerned with an interlocutory order, without expressing any opinion, SC was of the view that the interlocutory order passed by the Court below was not just and proper in view of the provisions of s.35 of the Act - S.35 of the Act permitted anyone to do his business in his own name in a bona fide manner - In the instant case, it was not in dispute that the defendants were doing their business in their own name and their bona fides was not disputed - It was also not in dispute that the plaintiff and appellants were related to each other and practically all the family members are in the business of jewellery - SC had perused the hoardings of the shops where they were doing the business and upon perusal of the hoardings SC did not find any similarity between them - Looking at the provisions of s.35 of the Act, there was no prima facie case in favour of the plaintiff and therefore, the appellant could not have been restrained from doing their business - SC, therefore, quashed and set aside the impugned order granting interim relief in favour of the defendant - Appeal allowed.
