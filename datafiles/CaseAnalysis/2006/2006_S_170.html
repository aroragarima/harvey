Shri Shekhar Ghosh v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 735; (2007) 1 SCC 331; 2007 (66) ALR 180; 2006 (7) AWC 7672; 2007 (2) JhrCR(SC) 267; JT 2007 (1) SC 63; 2006(11) SCALE 363; 2007 (1) SLR 756; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 274

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: [A.] Service - Promotion - Allegation of forged documents - Appellant was directed to be repatriated - Held, hearing was sought to be given was a post-decisional one, which is bad in law - Copy of complaint was not supplied to Appellant at furtherance if not proposed that a mistake was sought to be rectified - No charges were framed - No witness was examined an no Inquiry Officer arrived at any finding that Appellant was guilty of charges levelled against him - Tribunal or HC did not consider these aspects of matter - Appeal allowed.
