Bachan Singh v Kartar Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

V.N. Khare, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 21276; 2002 (46) ALR 841; 2002 (1) AWC 199; JT 2001 (10) SC 64; 2002 (131) PLR 512; 2002 (3) RCR(Civil) 495

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 44 - Indian Registration Act - Whether the consent decree passed in the present case was void, having obtained by fraud and, therefore, it could be avoided under s. 44 of the Evidence Act, 1872? - Held, Court was not correct - A consent decree passed by the court is not required to be registered under the provisions of the Indian Registration Act and, therefore, the view taken by the First Appellate Court was not legally correct - Appeal dismissed.
