Union of India v Bright Power Projects (India) Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Anil R. Dave, Vikramajit Sen, Pinaki Chandra Ghose

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 446; (2015) 9 SCC 695; AIR 2015 SC 2749; 2015(5) ALL MR 471; 2015 (4) AWC 3862; 2015 (3) CGLJ 289; 2015 (4) CompLJ 305; JT 2015 (6) SC 176; 2016 (3) MahLJ 1; 2015 (5) MLJ 491; 2016 (2) MPLJ 346; 2016 (3) RCR(Civil) 696; 2015(7) SCALE 638; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 488; 2015 (4) WLN(SC) 35

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Justifiability

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 31(7), 31(7)(a) - Contract - Interest - Justifiability - Appellant and the respondent-contractor had entered into a contract whereby the respondent had to construct certain structures - Provision in the contract stated that no interest should be paid on the amount payable to the respondent - In the course of execution of the contract, a dispute had arisen between the parties and tribunal had awarded interest to the respondent on the amount awarded, from the date of the reference till the date of the award - Whether tribunal was justified in awarding interest for the period commencing from the date of reference till the date of the award.

Held, it had been specifically understood between the parties that no interest was to be paid on the earnest money, security deposit and the amount payable to the contractor under the contract. When parties to the contract had agreed to the fact that interest would not be awarded on the amount payable to the contractor under the contract, they were bound by their understanding. Having once agreed that the contractor would not claim any interest on the amount to be paid under the contract, appellant could not have claimed interest either before a civil court or before an Arbitral Tribunal. In view of Saraswat's case and cl. 13(3) of the contract, tribunal was not justified in awarding interest from the date of entering upon the reference to the Arbitral Tribunal till the date of the award. Appeal Allowed.
