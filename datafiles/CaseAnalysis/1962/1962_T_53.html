Ramji Missir and Another v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

06 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar, K. Subba Rao, Syed Jaffer Imam

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 416; AIR 1963 SC 1088; 1963 CRLJ 173; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 745; [1963] Supp2 S.C.R. 745

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Women & Children - Socio-Economic - Criminal - Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 6(1) and 11 - (A) What is object of Probation of Offenders Act, 1958? - Held, objective is to prevent turning of youthful offenders into criminals by their association with hardened criminal of mature age within walls of a prison - Method adopted is to attempt their possible reformation instead of inflicting on them normal punishment for their crime - (B) Whether s. 6 of impugned act applies to person above 21 yrs of age? - Further held, question of age of person is relevant not for purpose of determining his guilt but only for purpose of punishment - If offender is not a person under age of 21 on date when court found him guilty, s. 6(1) has no application to him (b) Probation of Offenders Act, 1958, ss. 11(1) and 6 - Courts exercising appellate or revisional jurisdiction - Can exercise jurisdiction under s. 6 - Beneficial statutes - To be given wider interpretation - (C) Whether courts mentioned in s. 11 is empowered to exercise jurisdiction u/s. 6? - Further held, words in s. 11(1) pass on order under Act is to be understood to mean to exercise powers or jurisdiction conferred by Act - This section which is to apply 'notwithstanding anything in Code or any other law' to all courts empowered to sentence offenders to imprisonment - Courts mentioned in s. 11, be trial courts or exercising appellate or revisional jurisdiction, are thereby empowered to exercise jurisdiction conferred on Courts not only under ss. 3 and 4 and consequential provisions but also u/s. 6 - Appeal partly allowed.
