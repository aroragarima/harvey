Poddar Steel Corporation v Ganesh Engineering Works And Others.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1002; (1991) 3 SCC 273; AIR 1991 SC 1579; 1991 ALJ 706; 1991 (2) ARBLR 94; 1991 (2) BankCLR 457; 1991 (2) CompLJ 275; JT 1991 (2) SC 577; 1991(1) SCALE 928; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 696; 1991 (2) UJ 181

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Land & Property; Trade

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 299 - Auction sale by Govt. - Conditions in auction notice that earnest money be deposited by cash or by demand draft drawn on State Bank - Highest tenderer deposited earnest money by certified cheque of Union Bank - Whether literal compliance of terms can be waived and tender can be accepted ? - Held, not that every term must be literally complied with - Payment of earnest money by certified cheque of Union Bank of India drawn on its own branch could be treated as sufficient compliance of term - Literal compliance can be waived and tender can be accepted - Appeal allowed.
