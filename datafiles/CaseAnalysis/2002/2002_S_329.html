Harisingh M. Vasava v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

27 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 114; (2002) 3 SCC 475; (2002) SCC (Cr) 654; AIR 2002 SC 1212; 2002 (1) CCR 207; 2002 (2) Crimes 73; 2002 (3) CRJ 163; 2002 CRLJ 1771; 2002 (3) G.L.R. 1851; 2002 (1) JCC 521; JT 2002 (2) SC 333; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 36; 2002(2) SCALE 324; 2002 (2) SCJ 181; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 57; 2002 (2) SLT 211; 2002 (2) Supreme 111; 2002 (1) UC 353B

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: In a case where the trial court has taken a view based upon conjectures and hypothesis and not on the legal evidence, a duty is cast upon the High Court to re-appreciate the evidence in acquittal appeal for the purposes of ascertaining as to whether the accused has committed any offence or not.
