Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Motor accident claim - Enhancement of compensation - Entitlement - Appellants husband died in road accident - Appellant and other legal representatives filed petition u/s. 166 of the Act, for award of compensation as accident was caused due to rash and negligent driving - Tribunal awarded total compensation Rs. 1,32,000/- with interest at rate of 12% - HC awarded total compensation of Rs.1,77,500/- with interest at rate of 7% - Appellant not satisfied filed instant appeal - Whether compensation awarded to appellant was just and reasonable or was he entitled to enhanced compensation - Held, it was not possible to approve view taken by Tribunal which was reiterated by HC without assigning reasons that deceased would have spent 1/3rd of deceased total earning, towards personal expenses - Tribunal's observation was that two sons of appellant should not be treated dependant on their father because they were not minor was neither here nor there - No question was put to appellant about source of sustenance of appellants two sons - Therefore, there was no reason for Tribunal to assume that thesons who had become major can no longer be regarded dependant on deceased - Impugned judgment of award of Tribunal were set aside and claimants was entitled to compensation of Rs. 3,19,840/- Respondent No.1(Insurance Company) was directed to pay appellant total amount of compensation and should be free to deduct amount already paid to appellant - Appeal allowed.
