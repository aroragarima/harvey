Ruli Ram and Another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1927; (2002) 7 SCC 691; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1834; AIR 2002 SC 3360; 2002 (4) CCR 48; 2002 (4) Crimes 344; 2002 CRLJ 4337; JT 2002 (7) SC 267; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 187; 2002(6) SCALE 506; 2002 (4) SCJ 332; 2002 (5) SLT 349; [2002] Supp2 S.C.R. 426; 2002 (6) Supreme 550

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Trial Court held appellants guilty u/s. 304 Part II IPC - In appeal by State DB held that appellants were to be convicted u/s. 302 IPC - Trial Court awarded sentence of 10 years RI but HC substituted it by imprisonment of life - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, Trial Court and HC had analysedevidences in detail and had held it to be plausible and acceptable, and suffered from no infirmity - According to rule laid down in Virsa Singh's case 1958 Indlaw SC 82 even if intention of accused was limited to infliction of bodily injury sufficient to cause death in ordinary course of nature, and did not extend to intention of causing death offence would be murder - Court further held that no injuries were caused to deceased before they were thrown in pond, and there was no attempt to even strangulate them - Appellants could be attributed knowledge that natural and proper consequences of their acts were likely to cause death - HC had not indicated any basis to hold that case was covered by s. 302 IPC - There was only casual observation that murders were committed intentionally because relatives of deceased had not agreed to vote in favour of appellant's candidate - Proper provision to be applied was s.304 Part II IPC - Hence, sentence of 10 years RI awarded by Trial Court was held appropriate - - Appeal allowed.
