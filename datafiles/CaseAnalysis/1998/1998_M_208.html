Municipal Corporation of Delhi v Nirmal Sachdeva Smt. and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 December 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 2254; (2001) 10 SCC 364; 2002 (3) LLJ 261

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 s. 33C(2) - Summary dismissal - Validity - Respondent no. 1 was appointed by petitioner on 21-12-1965 as Auxiliary Nurse-Midwife with usual allowances admissible under the Rules - By notification dt. 5-12-1962, issued by Municipal Corporation it was declared as essentially intermittent - On 5-11-1979, respondent no. 1 filed application u/s. 33-C(2) of the Act before Labour Court for claim which was claimed on account of overtime allowance for period between 21-12-1966 and 29-9-1976 and sum on account of difference in salary together with claim for travelling allowance - Application was allowed by the Labour Court - Petitioner filed Writ Petitioner before HC, which was rejected in limine on ground of unclean hands - Hence instant appeal - Whether dismissal of writ petition on ground of coming to Court with unclean hands was justified - Held, equitable maxim that he who comes to equity should come with clean hands ought not to be read with very wide meaning attributed to it, but be introduced with care and caution in proper perspective - It was not material fact which gone to root of matter that there had been tendency to suppress or mislead but it was fact that statement was said to have been made in Court which did not find place in the order or in proceeding-sheet and merely because averments to factum of non-recording thereof have been taken recourse to, could petition be dismissed in limine on ground of unclean hands, answer again could not but be in negative - Thus, it was not abuse of process of the Court neither could it be said to be so misleading so as to warrant the Court to dismiss application - Question of dismissal of writ petition on ground of unclean hands could not subserve ends of justice and matter ought to have been dealt with on its merits with proper appreciation of facts - Therefore, SC set aside order of HC of Delhi and remitted matter to Delhi HC for being dealt with on merits upon affording opportunity of hearing to parties - Appeal allowed.
