Voltas Limited v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, Arun Mishra

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 254; (2015) 7 SCC 527; 2015(4) SCALE 456; [2015] 5 S.C.R. 320; [2015] 80 VST 12

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969

Summary: Indirect Tax - Sales Tax - Gujarat Sales Tax Act, 1969, s. 55A - Installation of Water Chilling Plant - Rate of Tax Composition - Appellant was company engaged in business of execution of jobs design, supply and installation of air-conditioning plants construed to be indivisible works contracts - Order was placed by one industry with appellant for water chilling plant at its factory - Appellant contended that works contract would attract the rate of composition prescribed against Entry No.5 of the schedule i.e. fabrication and installation of plant and machinery - Revenue authority held that works contract of appellant was covered by Entry No.2 of the schedule as the appellant had to air-condition the plant to be erected by it - HC confirmed decision of revenue authority - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, fabrication in terms of the work order is a distinctly independent yet integral segment of the works contract contributing to the final physical form of the water chilling plant with the characteristics intended, it cannot be construed to be, synonymous to the installation thereof. HC by overlooking the component of fabrication in the works contract opined that the same was within the purview of Entry No.2 and not Entry No.5. Description of the works contract, being of determinative bearing for ascertaining the composition rate of tax, it is to be opined that in the face of the design parameters insisted upon in the work order and consequential process of fabrication involved to cater thereto, that the works contract involved squarely falls within the ambit of Entry No.5 of the Notification. Exclusion of work of fabrication from the works contract as per the work order would render it (works contract) truncated to a form not intended by the customer. Exclusion of work of fabrication would strike as well at the root of the mandate of correlation of a works contract and the corresponding composition rate of tax as envisaged by s. 55A of the 1969 Act and the notification issued thereunder. Appellant's works contract for fabrication and installation of water chilling plant would fall under Entry 5 of the Schedule to the notification issued u/s. 55A of the 1969 Act and would be taxable at the rate of 5% as prescribed thereby. Appeal allowed.
