Union Of India & Ors. v Manab Kumar Guha

Supreme Court of India

28 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Chandramauli Kumar Prasad, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 173; (2011) 11 SCC 535; AIR 2012 SC (Supp) 357; JT 2011 (4) SC 19; 2011(3) SCALE 145; [2011] 3 S.C.R. 272; 2011 (3) SLR 685

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Compulsory retirement - Victim filed a complaint against petitioner, constable in the Railway Protection Force, that he alongwith another constable assaulted him and robbed money from his possession - The enquiry officer held him guilty of the charge and the disciplinary authority agreeing with the same inflicted the punishment of removal from service - On appeal, Appellate Authority set aside the order of removal and directed for de novo enquiry - Thereafter a de novo enquiry was held and enquiry officer held the petitioner guilty of the charge - On appeal, Appellate Authority substituted the punishment of removal from service to that of compulsory retirement - Writ petition filed thereagainst was dismissed - On further appeal, DB set aside the order of the Single Judge and quashed the order of compulsory retirement - Instant appeal - Appellant submitted that the order of compulsory retirement ought not to have been set aside on the ground of non-examination of victim - Held, finding recorded by the enquiry officer was based on the materials on record and on proper appreciation of evidence which could not be said to be perverse calling for interference by the HC in exercise of its power of judicial review - DB erred in setting aside the order of Single Judge and quashing the order of compulsory retirement - - Appeal allowed.
