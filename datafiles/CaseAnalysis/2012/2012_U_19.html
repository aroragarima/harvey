Union Of India And Others v S. Srinivasan and another

Supreme Court of India

21 May 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 140; (2012) 7 SCC 683; AIR 2012 SC 3791; 2012 (281) E.L.T. 3; 2012 (134) FLR 186; JT 2012 (5) SC 618; 2012(5) SCALE 702; [2012] 6 S.C.R. 34

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Constitution

Keywords: Appellate Tribunal for Foreign Exchange (Recruitment, Salary and Allowances and Other Conditions of Service of Chairperson and Members) Rules, 2000, Central Electricity Regulatory Commission (Procedure, Terms and Conditions for Grant of Trading Licence and other Related Matters) Regulations, 2004

Summary: Constitution - Advocates & Judges - Appellate Tribunal for Foreign Exchange (Recruitment, Salary and Allowances and Other Conditions of Service of Chairperson and Members) Rules, 2000, r. 5 - Foreign Exchange Management Act, 1999, ss. 20, 21 - Constitutionality of provision of Rules - Petition was filed for a declaration that r. 5 of the Rules, 2000 was ultra vires the Act; for quashment of certain notifications issued by the Govt. of India appointing part time Members of the Appellate Tribunal; and further to quash the appointment of respondent No. 3 to act as the Chairperson as he was a part time Member and also was not eligible to hold the post - HC declared the first and second proviso to r. 5 of the Rules as ultra vires of the Act and quashed the appointments of respondent Nos. 3 and 4 who were appointed as part time Members and further quashed the appointment of respondent No. 3 as the acting Chairperson of the Appellate Tribunal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether first and second proviso to r. 5 of the Rules as ultra vires s. 21(1)(b) of the Act - Held, if the officer belonging to Indian Legal Services was qualified to be a district judge, he could compete and be selected for the post of Member and that qualification was to be in accord with the pronouncements of law of HC - S. 20, the enabling provision, empowered the Central Govt. to fix such number of persons as the Govt. may deem fit - R. 5 provide that a tribunal shall have one Chairperson and Members not exceeding four - First proviso stipulated that the number of either full time Members or part time Members not to exceed two - This proviso introduced the concept of part time Member - Hence, it traveled beyond the enabling provision and was totally inconsistent with it - Rule did not conform to the main enactment - Therefore, HC was justified in declaring the said provision as ultra vires - Further, second proviso, which provides for qualification of a part time Member, was an innovative one and was incorporated to bring in only part time Members and once the introduction of part time Members was treated to be ultra vires the Act, the rest part of the Rule was absolutely redundant - Appeal dismissed.
