R.C.Sharma v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 221; (1976) SCC (L&S) 463; (1976) 3 SCC 574; AIR 1976 SC 2037; 1976 LabIC 1333; 1976 SLJ 516; 1976 (2) SLR 265; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 580; 1976 UJ 576

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Reduction in rank - Sustainability - Appeal by special leave against the judgment and order of DB of HC dismissing a plaintiff's/appellant's first appeal arising out of an original suit for a declaration that the order passed by the Commissioner of Income-tax reducing the appellant in rank from the post of an Income-tax Officer to that of an Income-tax Inspector, was void and inoperative - It appeared that the appellant was in service when he was prematurely retired - Appellant also claimed Rs. 20,904/-as arrears of salary, but he reduced this claim to Rs. 16,561.29 - Whether refusal of fitness certificate for filing appeal before SC was sustainable - Held, HC refusing the certificate of fitness of the case for appeal to SC observed that questions had been attempted to be raised before it in asking for certification which had not been argued at the time when the first appeal was heard by the HC - SC found that Judges who dismissed the application for certificate of fitness of the case had also heard the arguments in the first appeal - There was no affidavit before SC that any particular points argued before the DB of HC had not been referred to or dealt with by the Bench - Moreover, the DB of HC had probably not dealt with all arguments on questions of fact because it did not consider it necessary to do so - After all, it was not hearing appeal against the findings of the departmental authorities - Furthermore, after hearing the arguments of appellant, SC was unable to see any point which could be raised on behalf of the appellant capable of vitiating the departmental proceedings - Unless such a point could be raised, there could be no declaration that the departmental proceedings were null and void - Appeal dismissed.
