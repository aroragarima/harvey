West Bengal State Electricity Board and Others v Desh Bandhu Ghosh and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 February 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 439; (1985) 3 SCC 116; (1985) SCC (L&S) 607; AIR 1985 SC 722; 1985 CRLR 420; 1985 (50) FLR 456; 1985 LabIC 885; 1985 (1) LLJ 373; 1985 (2) LLN 37; 1985(1) SCALE 297; [1985] 2 S.C.R. 1014; 1985 (1) SLJ 318; 1985 (2) SLR 304; 1985 UJ 671

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution, art. 14 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 79 - W.B. Electricity Board's Regulations, Regn. 34 - Whether Regulation 34 which enabled Board to terminate services of permanent employee by giving three months' notice or salary in lieu thereof is constitutionally valid? - Held, no - The regulation is totally arbitrary and confers on Board a power which is capable of vicious discrimination - Appeal dismissed.
