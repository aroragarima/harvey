Â Lalji Haridas v R. H. Bhatt and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 August 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 252; [1965] 55 ITR 415

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Saurashtra Income-tax Ordinance Act, 1949, s. 46(1)(a) - Issuance of notice - Assessment - Challenge - Maintainability - Order of assessment was passed against appellant, assessing him to pay income-tax - Before making order of assessment, notice had been issued against appellant u/s. 46(1)(a) of the 1949 as large amount of income received by appellant had escaped assessment - Appellant made return showing income of some amount for year in question but Respondent No. 1 was not satisfied with explanation given by appellant, and so he passed order of assessment - Appellant made appeal to Appellate Assistant Commissioner in which appellate authority passed order and remanded proceedings to respondent No. 1 - After matter was remanded to respondent No. 1, appellant was required, to appear before respondent No. 1 but instead of complying with requisition contained in said letters, appellant moved HC through petition which resulted into rejection - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether appellants appeal was maintainable and genuine - Held, it was illustrated in case that how assessee could prolonged assessment proceedings by adopting judicial proceedings available to him under law and thereby postpone indefinitely final disposal of said proceedings by income-tax authorities - Thus, it was clear that main object which appellant had in mind in moving instant court u/art. 136 was to gain time and that is why at final hearing, not even attempt was made to raise any question about jurisdiction of respondent No. 1 or validity of law under which he was proceeding against - Therefore, there was no substance in appeal and it should be dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
