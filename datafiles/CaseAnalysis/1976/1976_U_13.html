Caetano Piedade Fernandes and Another v Union Territory of Goa, Daman and Diu Panaji, Goa

Supreme Court of India

01 September 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, N.L. Untwalia, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 483; (1977) SCC (Cr) 154; (1977) 1 SCC 707; AIR 1977 SC 135; 1977 CRLJ 167; 1976 CRLR 393

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34 and 302 - Supreme Court (Enlargement of Criminal Appellant Jurisdiction) Act, 1970, s. 2(1)(a) - Whether appellants rightly convicted on basis of oral evidence of 'X' and 'A' and certain circumstantial evidence? - X was a child witness and there are serious infirmities affecting his evidence: (i) he is supposed to have given name of appellant 2 as assailant even though he had never seen him before date of incident; (ii) according to him, only one blow was delivered by each of appellants, but medical evidence shows that deceased had received as many as 9 injuries - 'A's' evidence also suffers from inherent improbabilities - Evidence as to test identification parade also has no meaning, since 'X' in his evidence did not depose that he had identified appellants at test identification parade; also, if evidence of 'X' suffers from serious infirmities and cannot be accepted, evidence in regard to identification by him at test identification parade cannot improve situation - Further, these circumstances cannot be regarded as incriminating circumstances: (i) appellant 1 refusing to open door when police went to arrest him since it was about midnight, there was nothing unnatural in it' (ii) seizure of black cloth, cord and white thread from house of first appellant as they had no such identifying marks as would establish their connection with first appellant - Held, case's circumstances, no doubt, raise suspicion against appellants, specially appellant 1, but suspicion cannot take place of proof; view taken by learned Sessions Judge was a highly possible view and learned Addl. Judicial Commr. was in error in reversing that view and convicting appellants - Appeal allowed.
