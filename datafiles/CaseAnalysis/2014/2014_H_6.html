Surendra Mohan Arora v HDFC Bank Limited and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Pinaki Chandra Ghose, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 306; AIR 2014 SC 2871; 2014 (5) ALD(SC) 136; 2014(4) ALL MR 953; 2014 (4) ALT(SC) 60; 2014 (4) AWC 4124; 2014 (4) Bom.C.R. 94; 2014 (3) CompLJ 201; 2014 (2) CPJ(Sc) 1; JT 2014 (5) SC 416; 2014 (6) MLJ 110; 2014 (2) RCR(Civil) 887; 2014(5) SCALE 631; [2014] 5 S.C.R. 835

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Keywords: Professional Services, levy a penalty, Pre-payment of loan

Summary: Consumer Protection - Consumer Protection Act, 1986, ss. 22, 30A - Consumer Protection Regulations, 2005, reg. 15 - Unfair trade practice - Professional services - Levy a penalty - Pre-payment of loan - Legality - Appellant filed complaint before District Forum against respondent no. 1/HDFC Bank Ltd for indulging in unfair trade practice on ground of failure to provide professional services to appellant resulting in pre-payment of loan to respondent no. 1 seeking to levy a penalty for pre-payment - District Forum passed order in favor of appellant - Respondent no. 1 preferred appeal before State Commission and the same was dismissed - Revision petition was filed before National Consumer Disputes Redressal Commission (National Commission) - National Commission set aside orders of District Forum and State Commission - Aggrieved appellant filed review application before National Commission and the same was dismissed - Appellant filed writ petition before HC questioning vires of regn. 15 of the Regulations framed under the Act and the same was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, SC did not find any dispute that Regulations had been framed in accordance with power conferred u/s. 30A of the Act on Commission, thereby effecting its right to frame Regulations - Regulations was framed in accordance with law - Court minutely gone through regn. 15(2) of the Regulations and found that power to deal with review applications lies with Commission - Procedure was to be adopted by National Commission, whether review petition would be decided after hearing parties orally or could be disposed of by way of circulation - SC did not find that any mischief was done by framing said Regulations - Said Regulations u/s. 22 of the Act, could not be said to be ultra vires the said Act - There was no reason to believe that National Commission by enacting regn. 15 of Regulations exceeded its jurisdiction or power vested in it u/s. 30A of the Act, as had been tried to be contended by appellant - It appeared to SC for filing appeal by appellant, was only curtail rights of National Commission to adopt procedure whether review petitions would be decided after granting an opportunity of being heard to petitioner - From order of HC, SC found that no such request was made in application before National Commission for such hearing - HC correctly held that writ petition was misconceived and devoid of merit without even laying basic foundation for having sought an oral hearing of review application - No reason to interfere with order passed by HC - Appeal dismissed.
