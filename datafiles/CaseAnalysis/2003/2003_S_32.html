K. Pandurangan v S.S.R. Velusamy and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 760; (2003) 8 SCC 625; AIR 2003 SC 3318; 2003 (4) CCR 76; 2003 (4) Crimes 96; 2004 (1) CRJ 237; 2003 CRLJ 4964; 2003 (3) JCC 1653; JT 2003 (Supp1) SC 583; 2005 (3) RCR(Criminal) 11; 2003(7) SCALE 624; 2003 (1) SCW 4712; 2003 (5) SLT 551; 2003 (6) Supreme 719; 2004 (1) UC 361

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 109, 409, 420, 468, 477(a) - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss. 432, 432(5) - HC set aside judgment of lower appellate court which granted appellants benefit of remission - Maintainability of revision at instance of complainant - Right of lower appellate court to grant various remissions under various notifications issued by State - Held, under provisions of CrPC, 1973, court has suo moto power of revision, if that be so, question of same being invoked at instance of an outsider would not make any difference because ultimately it is power of revision which is already vested with HC statutorily that is being exercised by HC - Therefore, whether same is done by itself or at instance of a third party will not affect such power of HC - Further held, right to grant remission is governed by provisions of s 432, CrPC, 1973, which vests said power with appropriate Govt. and not in any court - Even that power is subject to conditions enumerated in that section and one such condition is that an accused person who is being granted remission of sentence will have to be in custody, when decision to grant remission is made by Govt. concerned - This was not factual position in this case apart from fact Court has no jurisdiction of remission of sentence u/s. 432 of Code - Therefore, first appellate court was not justified in granting remission -Matter remanded back to trial court.
