Harish Tara Refrageractories (P) Ltd. v Certificate Officer

Supreme Court of India

11 July 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1665; (1994) 5 SCC 324; 1994(3) SCALE 269

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Constitution - Banking & Finance - Bihar And Orissa Public Demands Recovery Act, 1914 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Bihar Act amended thereby incorporating 'any money payable to State Bank of India' within list of Public Demands - Whether Bihar Legislature had legislative competence to enact law providing for recovery of bank dues as arrears of land revenue? - Legislation comes squarely within Entries 11-A and 13 of the Concurrent List - Amending Act passed by the Bihar Legislature had effect of merely enlarging the jurisdiction of the Certificate Officer - This legislation was within the competence of the Bihar Legislature and will not be bad even if it incidentally trenches upon the field reserved for Parliament under List I of the Seventh Schedule - Recoveries of monies due to State-owned banks was primarily and purely a matter of procedure and inevitably these matters could, therefore, be left to State Govts. - Court of Certificate officer is a court created by State Govt. - State Legislature under Entry 11-A or Entry 13 would not be barred from either legislating about recoveries through Certificate Officer - Held, monies due to State-owned banks would come well within admit of public demands and equally their recovery both within and outside State, by virtue of Entry 43 of Concurrent List III - Appeal dismissed.
