N. R. Dongre and Others v Whirlpool Corporation and Anr

Supreme Court of India

30 August 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 200; (1996) 5 SCC 714; 1996 (6) AD(SC) 710; AIR 1996 SCW 3514; 1996 (2) ARBLR 488; 1996 (67) ECR 232; JT 1996 (7) SC 555; 1996 (16) PTC 583; 1996(6) SCALE 276; [1996] 5 S.C.R. 369; [1996] Supp5 S.C.R. 369

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Intellectual Property - Trade and Merchandise Marks Act, 1958, s. 27 (2) - Trademark and Passing off action - Whether use of term Whirlpool by USHA LEXUS (Appellant - Defendants) would entitle respondent - plaintiffs (Whirlpool Corporation and TVS - Whirlpool) to a interlocutory injunction in a suit for passing off? - Where a prior user and a cross border reputation was established by plaintiffs and refusal of injunction would cause irreparable injury to plaintiffs reputation and goodwill, plaintiffs would be entitled to a grant of interlocutory injunction? - Where defendants had failed to show any plausible reason for adopting name, its use even on an insignificant strip would prima facie establish a case for passing off action and entitle plaintiffs to an interlocutory injunction - Held, trial judge had exercised his discretion in granting interlocutory injunction in a manner tenable in law and hence appellate court would not interfere with it - Appeal dismissed.
