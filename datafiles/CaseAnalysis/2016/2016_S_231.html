Harijan Bhala Teja v State of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

27 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Prafulla C. Pant, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 319; AIR 2016 SC 2065; 2016 CRLJ 2565; 2016 (3) JCC 1755; JT 2016 (4) SC 409; 2016(4) SCALE 397; [2016] 2 S.C.R. 203

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Suicide, Bone, Indian Evidence Act, 1872, Dead Body, Inquest Report, Two Views, Hanging, Autopsy, Strangulation, Fracture, Sarpanch, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302 - Alleged Murder - Conviction - Justification - It is alleged that deceased wife was murdered by appellant husband by strangulating her - Charge Sheet was filed against filed against appellant u/ss. 302, 201 of IPC - Trial Court acquitted accused from all charges on ground that prosecution has failed to prove charge - HC vide impugned order reversed order passed by Trial Court acquitting accused persons and convicted accused persons u/s. 302 of IPC - Hence, present appeal.

Held, appellant and deceased were living together in house when death of appellant's wife occurred. Further, no post mortem examination was done, nor any information was given to police or deceased's family regarding death of deceased by appellant. SC held that facts coupled with circumstances that they were not leading congenial marital life, unnatural conduct of accused subsequent to incident, spot map showing rafter of roof to be at such height as was unapproachable for committing suicide -cumulatively lead only to conclusion that accused alone was author of crime and had taken false defence that he had seen deceased to have committed suicide by hanging herself. Hence, SC held that impugned order passed by HC convicting accused is justified and in accordance with law. Appeals dismissed.

Ratio - Conviction based on evidence on record and when prosecution has proved guilt of accused beyond reasonable doubt is justified and in accordance with law.
