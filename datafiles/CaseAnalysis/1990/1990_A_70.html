Hamda Ammal v Avadiappa Pathar and 3 Others

Supreme Court of India

07 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

N.M. Kasliwal, K.N. Saikia, T.K. Thommen

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 303; (1991) 1 SCC 715; 1991 (1) AWC 157; 1991 (1) BLJR 615; JT 1990 (4) SC 391; 1991 (1) MLJ(SC) 52; 1990(2) SCALE 970; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 594; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 594

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Code of Civil Procedure 1908, Transfer of Property (Amendment) Supplementary Act, 1929, Indian Regulation Act 1908, Bihar Land Reforms (Fixation Of Ceiling Area And Acquisition Of Surplus Land)

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Specific Relief Act, 1877 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 10 - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, ss. 64, O. 38, rr. 5 and 10 - Indian Registration Act 1908, ss. 47 and 49 - Whether appellant is entitled to property sold in her favour by virtue of sale deed dated September 9, 1970 but registered subsequently on October 26, 1970, or respondent has a better claim to property on account of an attachment before judgment made on September 17, 1970 in suit filed by him on September 13, 1970 i e prior to date of registration of sale deed in favour of appellant? - A transaction of sale having already taken place even prior to institution of a suit cannot be said to have been made with intention to obstruct or delay execution of any decree - Attachment before judgment shall not affect rights, existing prior to attachment, of persons not parties to suit - After registration it will relate back to date of execution of sale deed - Held, property in question admittedly belonged to defendant-judgment debtors (vendors) and once it is held that a sale deed had already been executed by them in favour of appellant on September 9, 1970 and only its registration remained, then neither attachment before judgment nor a subsequent attachment or court sale can confer any title by preventing relation back - Fact that document of sale had not been registered until after attachment makes no difference - Appeal allowed.
