L. Chandrakishore Singh v State of Manipur and others

Supreme Court of India

01 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 216; (1999) 8 SCC 287; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1460; AIR 1999 SC 3616; AIR 1999 SCW 3631; 2000 (1) CLR 6; 1999 (83) FLR 770; JT 1999 (7) SC 576; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution of India, 1950, art. 309 and 16 - Manipur Police Service Rules, 1965, rr. 24, 14, 15 and 5 - Seniority - Computation - Benefit of officiating service - Availability - Officiating appointment given to appellant who was a r. 24 selectee - Appointment not a local arrangement as per r. 25 - Held, no difference in substance between selectee under r. 24 and selectee under rr. 14 and 16 - Officiating appointment of appellant has to be treated as date of regular appointment - To be computed by giving benefit of officiating service - Service rendered on officiating appointment or on probation cannot be ignored while reckoning length of continuous officiation.
