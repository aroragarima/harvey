Ganga Ram Moolchandani and Others v State of Rajasthan and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20084; (2001) 6 SCC 89; (2001) SCC (L&S) 928; AIR 2001 SC 2616; 2001 (3) AWC 2290; 2001 (3) CLR 582; 2001 (90) FLR 812; JT 2001 (5) SC 470; 2001 (34) LabIC 2795; 2001(4) SCALE 371; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 992; 2002 (1) SLJ 197; 2001 (3) SLR 616; 2001 (5) SLT 276; 2001 (5) Supreme 169

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Gujarat Co-operative Societies' Act, 1961, Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1969, Andhra State Judicial Service Rules, Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules

Summary: Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 233 - Rajasthan Higher Judicial Service Rules, 1969, rr. 8(ii) and 15(ii) - Whether rules making only advocates practising in Rajasthan High Court or courts subordinate thereto as eligible for consideration to post of Rajasthan higher judicial service is violative of fundamental rights or not? - Held, if for appointment in subordinate judicial service, neither is there any requirement of knowledge of local laws nor regional language, it is fail to understand how same is required for higher judicial service in very same state - Purpose of framing such a rule is knowledge of local law and regional language in order to stand test of art. 14 of Constitution, is fallacious - There is no intelligible differentia which distinguishes advocates practising within State of Rajasthan and those practising outside Rajasthan but within territory of India - Civil Appeal No. 722 of 1999 allowed - Civil Appeal No. 2411 of 1999 dismissed.
