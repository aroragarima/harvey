Thakur Sukhpal Singh v Thakur Kalyan Singh

Supreme Court of India

02 May 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, J.L. Kapur, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 509; AIR 1963 SC 146; 1962 ALJ 1075; 1963 (1) ALT(SC) 181; 1962 BLJR 977; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 733

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, O. 41, rr. 16(1), 17, 30, 31 and 32 - (A) Whether an Appellate Court can dismiss an appeal for default when appellant appears at hearing but does not address Court - Held, an Appellate Court can dismiss an appeal for default when appellant appears at hearing but does not address court and is not bound decide case on merits based upon material record - (1)What are duties of an appellant when a judgment is under an appeal - Further held, appellant must show reasons for which an earlier judgment is erroneous so that court can call upon respondent for a reply - When appellants do not submit anything, its deemed that case has not been argued -Appeal dismissed.
