Chaudhari Ramjibhai Narasangbhai v State of Gujarat and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 965; (2004) 1 SCC 184; (2004) SCC (Cr) 269; AIR 2004 SC 313; 2003 (4) CCR 376; 2003 (4) Crimes 471; 2004 CRLJ 280; 2004 (2) G.L.R. 1232; 2003 (3) JCC 1964; JT 2003 (9) SC 96; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1; 2003(9) SCALE 437; 2003 (1) SCW 6180; 2003 (7) SLT 163; [2003] Supp5 S.C.R. 390; 2003 (8) Supreme 67

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860 - ss. 304 Part II, 325, 447 - High Court convicted the accused setting aside the judgment of acquittal passed by the Trial Court - Contradictory statements - Held, the High Court has applied the correct principles in law while directing conviction of appellant by reversing judgment of acquittal passed by the trial Court. The conclusions arrived at do not suffer from any patent error of law or perversity of approach and total lack of evidence to warrant interference.
