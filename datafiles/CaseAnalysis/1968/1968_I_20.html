Churakulam Tea Estate Private, Limited v Its Workmen and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 39; AIR 1969 SC 998; 1969 (19) FLR 203; 1969 LabIC 1396; 1969 (2) LLJ 407; 1969 (2) SCJ 282; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 931

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 2(c), 23(a), 24(1)(i), 25 E(iii) - Legality of award of bonus as an implied condition of service - Held, a term, for paying bonus, may be implied, even though payment may not have been at a uniform rate throughout; and, under such circumstances, it is open to tribunal itself to consider what should be quantum of payment in a particular year - When question of customary and traditional bonus arises for adjudication, considerations may be somewhat different - An additional circumstance has also been insisted upon, that payment must have been at a uniform rate throughout to justify an inference that payment at such and such a rate had become customary and traditional in particular concern - It is impossible to infer an implied condition of service where payment has not been uniform in past, unless such payment can be connected with some festival - Tribunal was wrong in holding that an inference could be drawn for payment of bonus as an implied condition of service in circumstances of present case when payment was not uniform in past even though it was not connected with any festival - Appeal allowed.
