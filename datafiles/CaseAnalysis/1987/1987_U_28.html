K.I. Shephard And Ors. Etc. Etc. v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

18 September 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28776; (1987) 4 SCC 431; (1987) SCC (L&S) 438; AIR 1988 SC 686; 1988 (1) BankCLR 517; [1988] 63 Comp Cas 244; 1989 (1) CompLJ 167; JT 1987 (3) SC 600; 1988 LabIC 1497; 1988 (1) LLJ 162; 1987 (2) LLN 864; 1987(2) SCALE 599; [1988] 1 S.C.R. 188; 1988 (1) SLJ 105

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution

Keywords: Banking Laws (Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1963

Summary: Constitution - Banking and Finance - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 7A and 45 - Constitution, arts. 16, 21, 31A and 32 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966 O. 41, r. 1 - Amalgamation Scheme under provisions of 1949 Act - Some employees have to be excluded - Names of such employees have to be included in scheme at draft stage - Scheme making process is not legislative but executive - Principles of natural justice apply - Employees thrown out of employment raising plea of not been heard - Amalgamation as such not challenged - Constitutional issues - Questions arise as to - (A) Vires of part of s. 45 the 1949 act with the arts. 16, 21 and 31A of the constitution - (B) Whether the post-decisional hearing can be justified - (C) Whether s. 7A is applicable - (D) Whether the employees who were excluded but who did not come to Court will also get benefit of decision - (E) Whether the petition u/art. 32 of the excluded employees be allowed on ground that they were not given hearing - in the light of s. 35 of CPC, 1908 and O. 41, r. 1 of Supreme Court Rules - Relief of continuity of service granted - Held that the vires need not be decided as cases could be disposed of without reference to question of vires - post-decisional hearing cannot be justified - And s. 7A is not applicable - Such employees will also get benefit of decision and they cannot be penalised - Appeal allowed.
