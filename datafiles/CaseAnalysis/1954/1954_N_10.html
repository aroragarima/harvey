Â Navinchandra Mafatlal, Bombay v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

01 November 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 70; AIR 1955 SC 58; [1954] 26 ITR 758; 1955 (1) MLJ(SC) 87; 1955 SCJ 158; [1955] 1 S.C.R. 829

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax and Excess Profits Tax (Amendment) Act,1947, s. 1 - Appeal against assesment of representing capital gains assessed in hands of appellant u/s. 12-B of Act- Said amount of capital gains was earned by appellant from property where assessee had a half share in certain immovable properties situate in Bombay which were sold by assessee and his co-owners during relevant accounting year - (A)Whether Act of 1947 which amended Income Tax Act by enlarging definition of term income in s. 2(6-C) and introducing a new head of income in s. 6 and inserting new s. 12B is valid ? - It will be wrong to interpret word 'income' in entry 54 in light of any supposed English legislative practice - Word should be given its widest connotation in view of fact that it occurs in a legislative head conferring legislative power - Held, Act of 1947 which amended Income Tax Act by enlarging definition of term income in s. 2(6-C) and introducing a new head of income in s. 6 and inserting new s. 12 B is therefore intra vires powers of Central Legislature acting under entry 54 in List I of seventh sch. of Govt. of India Act,1935 - (B) What is fundamental rule in interpretation of statutes? - Held that cardinal rule at interpretation, however, is that words would be read in their ordinary, natural and grammatical meaning subject to this rider that in construing words in a constitutional enactment conferring legislative power most liberal construction should be put upon words so that same may have effect in their widest amplitude - Appeal dismissed.
