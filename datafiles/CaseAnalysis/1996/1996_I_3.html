Srikrishna Private Limited and Others v I. T. O., Calcutta and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2212; (1996) 9 SCC 534; AIR 1996 SC 3409; 1996 (135) CTR 75; [1996] 221 ITR 538; JT 1996 (6) SC 440; 1996(5) SCALE 353; [1996] Supp3 S.C.R. 627; 1996 (5) Supreme 484; [1996] 87 TAXMAN 315; 1996 (2) TLR 696

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 147 and 148 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Assessment year 1959-60 and 1960-61 - Persons alleged to have advanced hundi loans to assessee, established to be bogus or mere lenders in assessment proceedings of subsequent year - Reopening of assessment by ITO on ground of failure to disclose fully and truly all material facts - Held, creating and recording bogus entries of loans, assessee cannot claim that he had truly and fully disclosed all material facts necessary for his assessment for that year - ITO investigated said assertion in subsequent years - But this fact does not relieve assessee of his duty to disclose facts truly and fully - Appeal dismissed.
