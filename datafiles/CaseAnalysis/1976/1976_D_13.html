Damadilal and Others v Parashram and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

A.C. Gupta, Y.V. Chandrachud, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 386; (1976) 4 SCC 855; AIR 1976 SC 2229; 1976 RCJ 717; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 645

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: M.P. Accommodation Control Act, 1961, ss. 2(i) and 12(i)(a) - CPC,1908, s. 100, O. 41, r. 33 - (A) Tenancy - (1) Statutory tenant, if has heritable interest in premises - (2) Payment of rent - Whether cheque sent by tenant to landlord is a lawful tender - (B) Second appeal - Question of bona fide requirement of landlord - Lower appellate court overlooking a very material part of evidence bearing on the question - Finding of fact arrived at ignoring important and relevant evidence - High Court setting aside finding - Held, statutory tenant u/s. 2(i) has a heritable interest in the premises - In absence of an agreement to the contrary a such Cheque is a valid tender of rent - Finding held, is bad in law and HC is justified in setting aside such a finding - Appeal dismissed.
