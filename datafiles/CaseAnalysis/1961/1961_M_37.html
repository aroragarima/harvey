Messrs Bharat Sugar Mills Limited v Shri Jai Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 430; 1961 (3) FLR 371; 1961 (2) LLJ 644; 1962 (1) SCJ 340; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 684

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Charge- Sheet

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - National bank - Industrial Dispute Act, s. 33 - Dismissal of workmen for misconduct - Whether management was acting bona fide and victimised members of union which employer had refused to recognise? - Tribunal's order in refusing permission to dismiss these workmen was entirely wrong and unjust and cannot be allowed to stand - As however even though no stay of Tribunal's order was granted when special leave was allowed and still workmen concerned have not been allowed to work or paid their wages, permission should not be granted to dismiss them - In cases of this nature enquiry should be held as early as possible, specially when management takes step of putting workmen under suspension - Held, application was rightly refused as evidence adduced before Industrial Tribunal does not establish charge of misconduct against them - Appeal partly allowed.
