State Of Punjab v Hari Singh & Anr

Supreme Court of India

21 March 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 66; (1974) 4 SCC 552; (1974) SCC (Cr) 588; AIR 1974 SC 1168; 1974 CRLJ 822; 1974 CRLR 389; [1974] 3 S.C.R. 725

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Kirpan

Summary: Constitution, art. 13 - CrPC, 1898, s. 367 - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Appreciation of evidence - (A) Appeal against acquittal - Appreciation of evidence - Court - (B) Fact that a witnesses is related to victim - Testimony rejected - (C) Murder case - Appreciation of evidence - Deceased and the other victim were attacked almost simultaneously - View of the High Court that other victim would have got up and run away or shouted earlier had he been really awake - (D) Criminal trial - Applicability of maxim 'falsus in uno falsus in omnibus' - Held, Interference by Supreme Court as HC had missed the core of truth in the case and had unjustifiably rejected the prosecution case - Mere fact that a witnesses is related to victim is not ground for rejecting testimony - HC made an error of throwing away the prosecution case, without attempting to separate chaff from grain on wrong assumption that the two were inseparable - In appraising evidence the Courts in India do not act on the maxim - Appeal Allowed.
