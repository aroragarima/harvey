Rajeswar Tiwari and others v Nanda Kishore Roy

Supreme Court of India

19 August 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 658; (2010) 8 SCC 442; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 3271; 2011 (1) CalLT 1; [2011] 333 ITR 534; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 136; JT 2010 (8) SC 624; 2011 (1) MLJ(Crl) 407; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 909; 2010(8) SCALE 283; [2010] 10 S.C.R. 444; [2011] 200 TAXMAN 139

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B and 406 - Quashing of complaint - Respondent (employee) filed a complaint u/ss. 461/468/406 r/w s. 120B of IPC against the appellants (employer) alleging discrimination by deducting an amount per month from monthly salary as Income Tax - Judicial Magistrate directed issuance of summons against the appellants for an offence u/ss. 406/120B of the IPC - Appellants filed application u/s. 482 of the CrPC for quashing of the complaint which was dismissed - Hence, present appeal - Held, adequate materials available to show that the proceedings are of a civil nature and same if adjudicated by the criminal court would be an abuse of process of court - In the present case, in view of the statutory provisions of the Income Tax Act and assertion of the appellants the proper remedy for the respondent was to approach the authority/officer concerned and not by filing the complaint - Moreover, complaint does not disclose any case to proceed against the appellant - Hence, HC committed an error, firstly, in not assigning any reason and passing a cryptic order and secondly, failed to exercise its jurisdiction u/s. 482 when the complaint does not disclose any offence of criminal nature - Criminal proceedings quashed - Appeal allowed.
