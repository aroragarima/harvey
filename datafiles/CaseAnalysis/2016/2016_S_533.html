Susanta Das and another v State of Orissa

Supreme Court of India

06 January 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 51; (2016) 4 SCC 371; AIR 2016 SC 589; 2016 (2) JCC 943; JT 2016 (1) SC 477; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 252B; 2016(1) SCALE 367; [2016] 1 S.C.R. 137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Common Object, Assembly, Medical Evidence, Unlawful Assembly

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 302, 307, 326, 341 - Evidence - Conviction - Challenged - Accused were charged with offences u/ss. 147, 148, 341,326,307, 302 r/w s. 149 of IPC and after analyzing evidence, Trial Court held that there was no clinching evidence to support individual role played by each of accused except accused-A and accordingly, acquitted other four accused persons and convicted accused-A for offences u/ss.302 and 326 of IPC - Aggrieved by conviction, accused-A filed appeal and State also filed appeal against acquittal of other four accused persons - HC vide impugned judgment, allowed appeal of State and convicted four accused persons and dismissed appeal by accused-A - Hence, instant appeals by all accused persons - Whether conviction of all accused persons could be upheld.

Held, cumulative consideration of evidence of PWs 1,7,8, 11 amply disclose that there were five persons who were involved in occurrence apart from specific role played by each one of them. Hence, involvement and extent of participation by all accused has been sufficiently established by prosecution with required evidence. Further, on appreciation of evidence of eye witnesses account, supporting version of other witnesses read along with expert medical opinion, again supported by Post Mortem report and injury report, there is no reason to take different view than what has been taken by DB in impugned judgment. Furthermore, in view of principles laid down by SC, implication of all five accused was perfectly justified and was supported by legal evidence as was spoken to by relevant witnesses which was duly corroborated by medical evidence. Hence, mere non mentioning of two of the names in FIR could not be fatal to case of prosecution. Hence, appeals are dismissed. Appeals dismissed.
