P. Narayanappa and Another v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, K.G. Balakrishnan, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 830; (2006) 7 SCC 578; AIR 2006 SC 3001; JT 2006 (8) SC 185; 2006 (5) KarLJ 563; 2006(8) SCALE 199; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 716

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board Act, 1966 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Karnataka Land Reforms Act, 1961 - Notifications were issued u/ss. 28(1) and 28(4) of Karnataka Industrial Areas Development Board Act, 1966 for acquisition of land for establishment and development of industries - Land has been acquired in order to benefit a company, namely, Vikas Telecom (Private) Limited for purpose of setting up a technology park, research and development centers, etc - Whether notification u/s. 28(1) of Act is liable to be struck down as purpose of acquisition, namely, purpose for which company, Vikas Telecom (Private) Limited, wanted land was not mentioned and, therefore, appellant landowners did not get an adequate opportunity to make an effective objection against proposed acquisition? - Held, s. 28 (1) clearly shows that land can be acquired for (i) development by Board or (ii) for any other purpose in furtherance of objects of Act - Scheme of Act does not show that at time of acquisition of land and issuing a preliminary notification u/s. 28(1) of Act, complete details of nature of industry or infrastructural facility proposed to be set up should also be mentioned - Appeals dismissed.
