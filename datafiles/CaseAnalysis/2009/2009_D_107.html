Kishan Lal v Dharmendra Bafna & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 954; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 611; (2009) 7 SCC 685; AIR 2009 SC 2932; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 3071; 2009 CRLJ 3721; 2009 (3) JCC 2273; JT 2009 (9) SC 669; 2009 (4) MLJ(Crl) 397; 2009 (3) RCR(Criminal) 828; 2009(9) SCALE 768; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 234

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - CrPC, 1973, ss. 173 (8), 482, 319 - Re-investigation - Jurisdiction of Magistrate - Appellant filed a criminal complaint against accused 1 to10 for cheating a sum of money - IO filed charge sheet against 1st and 2nd accused and dropped charge against other accused persons - Magistrate took cognizance of offence against 1st and 2nd accused - Appellant filed application u/s. 482 for setting aside the order of Magistrate and for further investigation to implicate accused 3 to 10 - HC allowed application - Magistrate ordered for further investigation - Accused 3 to 10 filed Revision before HC - HC allowed Revision - Legality - Held, matter investigated by two specialized agencies - IO filed affidavit to the effect that he had conducted thorough investigation - IO has given the reasons for why he had dropped accused 3 to 10 - IO considered the dealings by and between the parties inter se and the litigations filed by them against each other - Magistrate's order for reinvestigation is vague in nature - Order has not been pointed out in what respect investigation has not been carried out and what to be unearthed - IO not biased towards appellant - Appellant can invoke the remedy u/s. 319 for further investigation at the stage of trial if the appropriate case made out - Directed the Magistrate to consider the matter in its own merit without any prejudice to the observations of appellate courts - No merit - Appeal dismissed.
