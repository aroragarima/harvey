Indu Shekhar Singh and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 620; (2006) 8 SCC 129; AIR 2006 SC 2432; 2006 (4) ALD(SC) 66; 2006 (3) AWC 2187; JT 2006 (5) SC 260; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 135; 2006(5) SCALE 107; 2006 (4) SCJ 562; 2007 (2) SLR 694; [2006] Supp1 S.C.R. 497

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Benefit of past service

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Development Authorities Rules, 1985, r. 7 - Benefit of past service - Notification - Validity - Respondent no. 2, 3, 4 were appointed respectively - Accordingly a notification was issued and respondents were asked to communicate if they would like to be absorbed in authorized centralized service subject to conditions - Respondents resigned from their services and accepted offer of State - Appellants were placed above respondent no. 2, 3, 4 and 6 in seniority list - Respondents filed Writ petition before HC and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeals.

Held, ad hoc or temporary promotion granted to appellants on 3-5-1986 and 13-1-1987 respectively on nonexistent post of Assistant Executive Engineer would not, therefore, confer any right of seniority on them. Thus, for all intent and purport for purpose of determination of seniority, appellants were not promoted at all. Once they have been absorbed with respondent no. 1 and other employees similarly situated, their inter se seniority would be governed by statutory rules operating field. Case of appellants vis-, a-vis respondent no. 2 although may be governed by special rules, in terms of r. 7 of the Rules, same has to be determined on criterion of continuous length of service including service rendered in a Development Authority, or Improvement Trust on similar posts. Appellants, it will bear repetition to state, although were promoted at one point of time on purely ad hoc basis to post of Assistant Executive Engineer as said posts even in their parent authority were not of similar type, same would not be relevant for purpose of determining inter se seniority. If rule of continuous service in same and similar posts is to be resorted to, date of initial appointment would be relevant criterion therefor. As the post of Assistant Executive Engineer was not a cadre post, appellants cannot be said to have been working on a higher post for purpose of r. 7 of the Rules. Impugned judgment cannot be sustained, which is set aside. Appeals allowed.
