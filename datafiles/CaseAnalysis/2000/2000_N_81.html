State of Haryana v Nauratta Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 March 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, D.P. Mohapatra

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 490; (2000) 3 SCC 514; (2000) SCC (Cr) 711; AIR 2000 SC 1179; 2000 (1) CCR 330; 2000 (2) Crimes 6; 2000 CRLJ 1710; 2000 (2) JCC 429; JT 2000 (3) SC 85; 2000(2) M.P.H.T. 377; 2000 (2) RCR(Criminal) 294; 2000(2) SCALE 321; 2000 (3) SCJ 174; [2000] 2 S.C.R. 246; 2000 (3) SLT 34; 2000 (2) Supreme 249

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Convict undergoing sentence - Claim, remission for period he was on bail - Power of Govt. u/ss. 432 and 389 of Code - Whenever sentence is suspended by Court, convict is entitled to be released on bail - As per s. 432(1) of Code, remission is ' remit punishment to which he has been sentenced' - Claim if can be granted - Held, remission cannot be granted in respect of period during which accused was released on bail - Appeal allowed.
