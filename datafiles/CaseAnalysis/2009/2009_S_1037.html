State of Rajasthan and Others v Dev Ganga Enterprises

Supreme Court of India

08 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1839; (2010) 1 SCC 505; AIR 2010 SC 378; 2010 (1) JhrCR(SC) 150; JT 2009 (15) SC 82; 2010 (2) RLW 1062; 2009(14) SCALE 420; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 269

Case Digest

Subject: Mines & Minerals; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Concession Rules, Mineral Concession, Minor Mineral Concession

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Mines & Minerals - Rajasthan Minor Mineral Concession Rules, 1986, rr. 3(xiii-a), 32, 35(g)(iii), 37 and 61 - Excess Royalty Collection Contract - Respondents entered into an 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract' with State of Rajasthan which required contractor to pay annual fixed amount in instalments - State raised demand for interest on ground that there was delay on part of Respondents in paying instalments - Respondent paid said amount under protest and filed a suit for setting aside said demand - Trial Court dismissed said suit - HC allowed appeal filed against said order and held that respondent was entitled to a grace period of 15 days u/r. 61 of Rules, after tenth day of 'month' when amount fell due - Hence, present appeal - Whether contractor is entitled to a grace period of 15 days (from date when amount becomes due) for paying dues without interest u/r. 61 of said Rules? - Held, after amendment in 1994 introducing concept of 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract', rr 32, 34, 35 and 37 were amended by inserting 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract' wherever words 'Royalty Collection Contract' occurred - But significantly such an insertion was not made in r. 61 - In absence of any reference of 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract Amount' in r. 61, 15 days interest holiday is not available in regard to 'Excess Royalty Collection Contracts', even though it may be available in regard to four categories of dues referred to therein - Impugned order of HC set aside - Trial Court's order upheld - Appeal allowed.P /&gt;Contract & Commercial - Royalty Collection Contracts and Excess Royalty Collection Contract - Difference - Held, Royalty Collection Contracts refer to contracts given to contractors to collect royalty on behalf of Government from quarry licensees and short term licence holders who excavate minor minerals - On other hand, Excess Royalty Collection Contracts refer to contracts given to contractors to collect royalty in excess of annual dead rent on behalf of Government from holders of mining leases - R. 61 specifically refers to dues in regard to 'dead rent, royalty, quarry licence fee and royalty collection contract amounts' - 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract Amount' is neither a dead rent, nor royalty nor quarry licence fee nor a royalty collection contract amount - 'Royalty Collection Contract' was a concept that was conceived and contemplated in Rules as originally framed - On other hand, 'Excess Royalty Collection Contract' was a new concept introduced by amendment dt. 12-8-1994 by inserting a new definition under clause (xiii-a) of r. 3 - Therefore an Excess Royalty Collection Contract cannot be equated with a Royalty Collection Contract - Appeal allowed.
