Koteswar Vittal Kamath v K. Rangapa Baliga & Co.

Supreme Court of India

09 December 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, J.M. Shelat, C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 167; (1969) 1 SCC 255; AIR 1969 SC 504; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 40

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Municipalities & Local Governments; Parliament & Legislature

Keywords: Rules Of Procedure, Rules of Procedure and Conduct of Business in Lok Sabha, Travancore Cochin Public Safety Measures Act 5 of 1950

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Travancore Cochin Vegetable Oils and Oilcakes (Forward Contract Prohibition) Order, 1950, ss. 3, 8 and 73 - Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act, 1946 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 300, 301, 303 and 304 - Travancore Cochin Public Safety Measures Act, 1950 - Whether contracts were valid under Prohibition Order of 1950 ? - Held, No - Prohibition Order of 1950 validly continued in force by Act 5 of 1950 and to have continued to remain in force thereafter u/s. 17(4) of Essential Supplies (Temporary Powers) Act 24 of 1946 therefore, transactions entered into between appellant and respondent were prohibited and, having been entered into against provisions of law - Appeal allowed.
