Supreme Court of India

11 April 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 285; (1985) 3 SCC 189; (1985) SCC (L&S) 623; AIR 1985 SC 915; 1985 (51) FLR 71; 1985 LabIC 1001; 1985 (2) LLJ 187; 1985 (2) LLN 280; 1985(1) SCALE 713; [1985] 3 S.C.R. 686; 1985 (2) SLJ 108; 1985 (2) SLR 57; 1985 UJ 939

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes (Amendment) Act, 1971

Summary: Labour and Industrial Laws - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10(1) - Constitution, arts. 32 and 226 - Reference - Refusal to make - Mandamus - Govt. acting on extraneous and irrelevant grounds and on round not germane to determination - If writ of Mandamus can be issued - Writ of mandamus calling upon the Govt. to reconsider its decision can be issued - Petition allowed.
