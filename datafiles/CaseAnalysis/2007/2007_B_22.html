District Red Cross Society v Babita Arora And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 August 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

G.P. Mathur, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 809; (2007) 7 SCC 366; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 631; AIR 2007 SC 2879; 2007 (4) AWC 3720; 2007 (114) FLR 1040; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 76; JT 2007 (10) SC 161; 2007 (4) KLT 279; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 375; 2008 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 524; 2007 (3) LLJ 777; 2007 (4) LLN 199; 2007 (4) MPLJ 426; 2007(10) SCALE 22; 2007 (6) SCJ 264; [2007] 8 S.C.R. 1018; 2007 (5) SLR 554; 2008 (1) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Charitable, Closure

Summary: Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Respondent was appointed as staff nurse in the appellant District Red Cross Society - Termination - Whether the respondent is entitled to protection of ss. 25F and 25G of if the establishment in which she was working itself has been closed down though certain other wings or units of the appellant District Red Cross Society, Karnal, have not been closed down and are still functioning - Held, if the entire establishment of the employer is not closed down but only a unit or undertaking is closed down which has no functional integrity with other units or undertaking, the provisions of s. 25FFF will get attracted and the workmen are only entitled to compensation as provided in s. 25FFF which has to be calculated in accordance with s. 25F - Tribunal and High Court erred in holding that termination of services of the respondent would amount to retrenchment - Respondent would be entitled to compensation only in accordance with s. 25FFF - Appeal allowed.
