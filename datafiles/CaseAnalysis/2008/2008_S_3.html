(1) Karnataka Bank Limited; (2) Messrs Ch v (1) State of Andhra Pradesh and Others; (2)

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

B. SUDARSHAN REDDY, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 75; (2008) 2 SCC 254; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1377; 2008 (3) MLJ(SC) 517; 2008(1) SCALE 660; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 986; [2008] 12 VST 459

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Andhra Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1965, Andhra Pradesh Gram Panchayats Act, 1964, Andhra Pradesh Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act, 1987, Constitution (Sixtieth Amendment) Act, 1988, Gujarat Agricultural Land Ceiling Act, 1961

Summary: Andhra Pradesh Tax on Professions, Trades, Callings and Employments Act, 1987 - Constitutional validity of the explanation to the definition of the term 'person' defined in s. 2(j) - Whether the Explanation to the definition of the term 'person' defined under s. 2(j) and Explanation No.I to the First Schedule of the Act is violative of the Article 267 (2) of the Constitution? - Held, definition of the word 'person' in the impugned Explanation and also Explanation No. I to the First Schedule of the Act is not intended to tax a person at a rate higher than Rs.2500/- per annum, per person, but to treat even a branch of a firm, company, corporation or other corporate body, any society, club or association as a separate person, and therefore, a separate assessee within the meaning of s. 2 (b) and Andhra Pradesh State Legislature has undoubtedly competency to adopt such a devise of taxation - Andhra Pradesh State Legislature did not violate the mandate of Article 276(2) of the Constitution - Appeals allowed.
