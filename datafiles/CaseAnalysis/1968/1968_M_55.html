Harcharan Singh v Mohinder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vishishtha Bhargava

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 89; AIR 1968 SC 1500; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 198

Case Digest

Subject: Election

Keywords: Election Petition, General Elections, Election Dispute, Electoral Roll, Delimitation Of Constituencies

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 100(1)(d)(1) - Nomination - Electoral Registration - Validity - At general elections held in February 1967 appellant polled largest number of votes and was declared elected - Respondent no. 1 who was candidate at election, applied to HC for setting aside election of appellant on ground that nomination of appellant who was not voter in Constituency was improperly accepted by Returning Officer, for appellant had failed to file before scrutiny copy of electoral roll or relevant part thereof or certified copy of relevant entries of poll pertaining to constituency to which he belonged, and that result of election to Constituency of appellant was materially affected by improper acceptance of his nomination - HC upheld contention and set aside election of appellant and declared election of appellant void u/s. 100(1)(d)(1) of the Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, purpose of diverse provisions of election law which may appear to be technical was to safeguard purity of election process, and Courts will not ordinarily minimize their operation - If there was any reason to think that appellant was negligent, or that on account of defects which were found in copy produced by appellant purity of election process was likely to be affected Court would have been loath to disagree with HC- In instant case appellant moved Electoral Registration Officer for copy certifying correctness of entries in list which had been supplied to him, and Electoral Registration Officer supplied to him copy which though defective, did include sufficient particulars for identifying appellant - No objection was raised before returning officer and that officer after holding inquiry was apparently of view that there was no defect which could be regarded as of substantial character - Appeal allowed.
