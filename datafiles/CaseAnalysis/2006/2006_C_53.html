Tulsi and Others v Chandrika Prasad and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 787; (2006) 8 SCC 322; AIR 2006 SC 3359; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 20; 2006 (65) ALR 311; 2006 (4) AWC 3644; 2007 (2) CGLJ 205; 2006 (4) CTC 766; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 87; JT 2006 (8) SC 158; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 209; 2006 (2) RCR(Rent) 336; 2007 (1) RLW 614; 2006(8) SCALE 515; 2006 (Supp) RD 415; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 255

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 58(c) - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - On 30-12-1968, one Jawala Prasad Sah, defendant No. 3 in suit transferred northern part of house property to one Balmukund Chaudhary by way of mortgage for a consideration of Rs. 4,300 repayable by 30-01-1971 - He sold entire property to plaintiffs for a valuable consideration of Rs. 14,000 - It included right to redeem mortgage - Respondents filed a suit for a decree for redemption of said mortgage as also a decree for mesne profit - In alternative, a prayer for a decree of specific performance was made - Whether deed D/- 30-12-1968 constitutes a sale with condition of purchase or mortgage by way of conditional sale? - Held, referring to s. 58(c) of Transfer of Property Act, it was also held that transaction in question was not partial but an absolute one - In terms of s. 58(c), a transaction may be held to be a mortgage with conditional sale if it is evidenced by one document - Transaction in this case has been evidenced by one document - S. 58(c) will, therefore, apply - It was clearly stipulated in deed that in event, executant repayed entire consideration by 30-12-1971, purchaser would reconvey property and furthermore deliver possession thereof - Sale was to become absolute only when transferee failed to pay said amount within stipulated period - Courts below have also taken into consideration contemporaneous conduct of parties in treating transaction to be one of mortgage and not of sale - Parties intended to enter into a transaction of mortgage and not sale - Appeal dismissed.
