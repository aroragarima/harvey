Unikat Sankunni Menon v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 124; AIR 1968 SC 81; 1968 (2) LLJ 129; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 430; 1967 SLR 499

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Rajasthan Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1961 - Rajasthan Secretariat Service Rules, 1954 - Rajasthan Civil Services (Rationalisation of Pay Scales) Rules and Schedules, 1956 -Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 16 and 226 - Appellant was in service of Rajasthan Govt. - Rajasthan Secretariat Service Rules, 1954 were framed by Rajpramukh u/art. 309 of Constitution of India - Under these rules, appellant became a member of Rajasthan Secretariat Service (RSS) - Subsequently, grades for posts of Dy. Secretaries and Asst. Secretaries were again revised by promulgating Rajasthan Civil Services (Revised Pay) Rules, 1961 - Rules that were applicable to members of Rajasthan Administrative Service (RAS ) when holding posts of Deputy Secretaries - In case of members of RAS appointed to posts of Deputy Secretaries, a special pay remained admissible, while principle of granting special pay to members of RSS on appointment as Deputy Secretaries was abolished - Appellant filed a petition u/art. 226 claiming that words 'without special pay' in Rules and Schedules, 1956 may be declared as invalid and violative of arts. 14 and 16 of Constitution - High Court dismissed petition - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether Rules are invalid? - Held, in promulgating these various rules, revising pay scales applicable to Deputy Secretaries, ensured that no revised rule operated to prejudice of a member of RSS , as compared with earlier rules under which rights had vested in him - Rules thus applicable to member of RSS on appointment to post of Deputy Secretary, against which appellant made his grievance cannot be held to be vitiated in any manner, if considered by themselves in light of rights which members of RSS possessed from time to time - Rules as framed, are thus, based on well recognised principles for granting salary to members of different services, even when they are appointed to same post - In these circumstances, no question arises of any discrimination u/art. 14 of Constitution or of any denial of equality of opportunity u/art. 16 of Constitution - (B) Whether on appointment as Deputy Secretary, he must be held entitled to receive special pay on ground of being placed on parity with members of RAS? - Held, appellant comes to post of a Deputy Secretary from RSS , which is a service distinct and separate from RAS methods of recruitment, qualifications, etc., of two services are not identical - In their ordinary time scale, two services do not carry same grades - Even posts, for which recruitment in two services is made, are, to a major extent, different - Members of RSS are meant to be employed in Secretariat only, while members of RAS are mostly meant for posts which are outside Secretariat though some posts in Secretariat can be filled by members of RAS - In such a case, where appointment is made to posts of Deputy Secretaries of govt. servants belonging to two different and separate Services, there can arise no question of a claim that all of them, when working as Deputy Secretaries, must receive identical salaries, or must necessarily both be given special pay - No such equality is required either by art. 14 or art. 16 of Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
