Thakur Kishan Singh v Arvind Kumar

Supreme Court of India

07 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, R.M. Sahai

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 818; (1994) 6 SCC 591; AIR 1995 SC 73; AIR 1994 SCW 408; JT 1997 (10) SC 611; 1994 (2) RentLR 368; 1994(4) SCALE 176; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 199; 1995 (1) UJ 401

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Land & Property - Constitution - Registration Act, 1908, s. 47 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 107 - Limitation Act, 1963, arts. 64 and 65 - (A) Registration Act, s. 47 - Lease-deed registered four months after its execution? When it takes effect - (B) Execution of agricultural lease - Specific exclusion of agricultural lease from operation of Act - Tenancy Act of State not containing any provision like s. 107 of 1882 Act - Whether principles of s. 107 of 1882 Act can be extended to said lease - (C) Adverse possession - Person initially was holding permissive possession of land - Heavy burden lies on him to establish that it became adverse - Whether possession for long time converts permissive possession into adverse possession - (D) Appeal - Reappreciation of evidence - If permissible - Held, (A) From date of its execution - (B) Cannot be extended - (C) Mere possession does not convert permissive possession into adverse possession - (D) Not permissible - Appeal dismissed.
