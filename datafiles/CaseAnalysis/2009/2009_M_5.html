(1) Reshma Kumari and Others; (2) Puneet Kaur and v (1) Madan Mohan and Another; (2) Delhi Transport

Supreme Court of India

23 July 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1089; (2009) 13 SCC 422; 2012 (1) ACC 323; 2009(5) ALL MR 438; 2010 (2) AWC 1793; JT 2009 (10) SC 90; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 908; 2009(10) SCALE 90; [2009] 11 S.C.R. 305

Case Digest

Subject: Insurance

Keywords: Computation Of Compensation, Loss Of Expectation Of Life, Span Of Life, Award Of Damages, Matter referred to Larger Bench, Life Expectancy, Conventional Figure, Permanent Total Disablement, Permanent Partial Disablement, Actuaries Act, 2006

Summary: Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Motor vehicle accident - Compensation - Determination - Structured Formula in Second Schedule - Application of - Held, amount specified in Second Schedule only required to be paid irrespective of amount arrived at in multiplier system - Act does not speak of multiplier system - Multiplier applied only in non fatal accident - Age and income required to be considered while awarding compensation of death in fatal accidental cases wherein multiplier has no application - However, amount of compensation payable in case of permanent total disability or permanent partial disability, would require to be arrived at by multiplying annual loss of income by considering age of victim at the time of accident - Further, in partial permanent disablement such amount specified in permanent total disablement under item (a) of Second Schedule required to be paid - Compensation payable in 'fault liability' and 'no fault liability' appeared to have wide variation on same class of cases - Logic implies that why an injured claimant or his heirs would get a lesser amount than one specified in Second Schedule on death especially proof of negligence on part of driver of offending vehicle established - Divergence of opinion - No clarification from Parliament - Matter referred to Larger Bench.
