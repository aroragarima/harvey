State Of West Bengal Etc v State Of West Bengal Etc

Supreme Court of India

03 May 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 54; (1977) 3 SCC 440; (1977) SCC (Cr) 520; AIR 1977 SC 1772; 1977 CRLJ 1164; 1977 CrLR(SC) 342; 1977 MLJ 592; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 758

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Criminal Law (Amendment) Act 1952, Criminal Law Amendment Act 1952, West Bengal Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949

Summary: Criminal - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2), 6 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120 B, 21 - W B Criminal Law Amendment (Special Courts) Act, 1949, ss. 4(1), 4(2), 10 - Criminal conspiracy - Offence committed by ex-public servant during service - State reffered case to special court - (a) Whether the High Court was justifies in holding that order of allotment of case to Special Court illegal, as accused was no more a public servant? - Held, that HC was wrong in doing so, as date for determining offence is date of commission of offence when person arraigned must be a public servant and retirement, resignation, dismissal or removal of a public servant would not wipe out offence which he had committed while in service -(B) Whether special court has jurisdiction to try case u/s. 10 of Act of 1949? - There is no merit in submission that because of s. 10 of Act of 1949 Special Court cannot be said to have jurisdiction to try offence as respondent is not a public servant he is outside provisions of Bengal Act, as well as Prevention of Corruption Act - Further held, even under Prevention of Corruption Act, an outsider can be prosecuted u/s. 5(3) of Act when a person habitually commits an offence punishable u/s. 165 A, IPC, 1860 - (C) Whether this appeal can be referred to larger bench? - Further held, There is no substance in contention that this appeal should be referred to a larger Bench u/s. 4(1) of Bengal Act, scheduled offences which include an offence u/s. 5 (2) of Act of 1947 as also conspiracy to commit that offence shall be triable by Special Courts only -No other court can, therefore, try these offences - (D) What would be crucial date for taking cognizance of offence in instant case? - U/s 6(1) of Act of 1947,as in case of s. 190(1), Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, court takes cognizance of an offence and not an offender - Further held, crucial date for taking cognizance in this case is date when case was received by Special Court on being allotted by State Govt. u/s. 4 (2) of Bengal Act, 1967 - Appeal allowed.
