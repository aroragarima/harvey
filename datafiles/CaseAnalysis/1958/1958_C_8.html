Â Mahadayal Premchandra v Commercial Tax Officer, Calcutta and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 April 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, S.K. Das, J.L. Kapur, Vivian Bose

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 189; AIR 1958 SC 667; 1958 (2) MLJ(SC) 93; 1958 SCJ 728; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 551; 1958 (9) STC 428

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Sales Tax - Bengal Finance (Sales-tax) Act, 1941, s. 2(g) explanations 2, 2(c), 3 and 5(1) - Sale transaction vis-a-vis agency transaction - 'Dealer' and 'turnover' - 'Dealer' as per extended meaning given to it under explanation 3 to s. 2(c) - Appellants carrying on business of selling goods, partly in wholesale and partly in retail of woolen and cotton fabrics and other products and also they were commission agents for such goods - Respondents sought to assess tax on transaction undertaken as commission agent by appellant/assessee - Respondents sought to apply extended definition of dealer as per explanation 3 of s. 2(c), to appellant/assessee and extended definition of sales under explanation 2 to s. 2(g) in respect of disputed transaction - Whether action of respondents, justified? - Appellant/assessee were agent for Mills which was located in Kanpur (UP) - It could not be said of them that they carried on business of selling goods in West Bengal - Mills had neither any office in West Bengal nor had they established any business through appellants or otherwise of selling goods in question in West Bengal - Only thing which was done in this connection was that appellants canvassed orders as commission agents of Mills in West Bengal and forwarded these orders to Mills, which accepted them and executed same - Privity of contract was established between customers on one hand and Mills on other - Business was, if at all, one of selling goods in Kanpur and despatching them to West Bengal for purpose of consumption therein - Held, transactions in dispute were not covered by explanation 3 to s. 2(c) of Act and appellants could not in respect of such business be deemed to be a 'dealer' within meaning of that explanation - Appeal allowed.
