Chandra Prakash Shahi v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 297; (2000) 5 SCC 152; (2000) SCC (L&S) 613; AIR 2000 SC 1706; 2000 ALJ 1445; 2000 (3) AWC 1848; 2000 (1) CLR 347; JT 2000 (5) SC 181; 2000 (3) LLN 21; 2000(4) SCALE 209; [2000] 3 S.C.R. 529; 2000 (3) SLJ 312; 2000 (2) SLR 772; 2000 (2) UPLBEC 1661

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Bombay Prohibition Act 1949, Uttar Pradesh Temporary Government Servants (Termination Of Service) Rules, 1975

Summary: Service - Uttar Pradesh Temporary Govt. Servants (Termination of Service) Rules, 1975, r. 3 - Uttar Pradesh Police regulations, para 541 - Whether order terminating services of appellant without service of notice was valid? - Held, termination was founded on report of preliminary inquiry as employer had not held preliminary inquiry to find out whether appellant was suitable for further retention in service as he had already completed period of probation quite a few years ago but was held to find out his involvement - In this situation, particularly when it is admitted by respondent that performance of appellant throughout was unblemished, order was definitely punitive in character as it was founded on allegations of misconduct - There has been total non-compliance with provisions of para 541 of Uttar Pradesh Police Regulations and services of appellant were terminated without ever issuing him any notice intimating grounds on which his services were proposed to be terminated nor was his explanation ever obtained - Appeal allowed.
