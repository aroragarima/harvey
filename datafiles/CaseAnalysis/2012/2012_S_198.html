Yumman Ongbi Lembi Leima v State Of Manipur & Ors

Supreme Court of India

04 January 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Surinder Singh Nijjar, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 1; (2012) 2 SCC 176; AIR 2012 SC 321; 2012 (4) CGLJ 358; 2012 (1) JCC 531; JT 2012 (1) SC 11; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 356; 2012 (1) OLR 550; 2012 (1) RCR(Criminal) 514; 2012(1) SCALE 2; [2012] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Unlawful Activities (Prevention) Act, National Security Act, 1980 - Detention - National Security - Public Order - Sustainability - Appellants husband was involved in several criminal cases and several FIR's were registered against him and he was released on bail - FIR u/s. 302 of IPC was registered against husband of appellant and was arrested and was produced before Magistrate - He was served with detention order issued by District Magistrate under National Security Act, 1980 which was approved by Governor dt 07-02-1022 fixing detention period for 12 months - Appellant challenged in HC but was rejected - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that detainee had been arrested in connection with several cases but was released on bail in all cases which were punishable under IPC and same cannot be equated with national security - Further contended that since detainee was already in custody detaining authority ought not to have passed detention order - Respondent contended that detainee was potential danger to society and his activities were prejudicial to maintenance of public order and after his release he may continue such activities - Whether the detention orders passed by the detaining authority against detainee is justified - Held, that extra-ordinary powers of detaining an individual in contravention of provisions of Article 22(2) of Constitution was not warranted in instant case, where grounds of detention do not disclose any material which was before detaining authority - When Courts thought it fit to release appellants husband on bail in connection with cases against which he has been arrested, mere apprehension that he was likely to be released on bail as ground of detention is not justified - Appeal allowed.
