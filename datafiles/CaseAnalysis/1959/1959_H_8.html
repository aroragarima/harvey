Hotel Imperial, New Delhi, and Others v Hotel Workers' Union

Supreme Court of India

21 May 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 30; AIR 1959 SC 1342; 1959 (2) LLJ 544; 1960 SCJ 317; [1960] 1 S.C.R. 476

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract & Commercial - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33, 10(4), 2(b), 15, 17 and 17 A - (A)Whether a master has implied power to suspend employee under a contract of service? - Held, no - Such power can only be created by a statute governing contract or by an express term in contract itself - If employee is suspended without any authority in that regard, master will have to pay wages during period of suspension - (B)Whether there is an implied power of suspension available to employer in view of s. 33 of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, yes - Employer can after holding proper enquiry suspend his employee pending proceedings u/s. 33 - (C)Whether an order granting interim relief is an award within meaning of s. 2(b) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 and must therefore be published u/s. 17? - Further held, Supreme Court has power to grant interim relief in same manner as Industrial Tribunal and its order need not be sent to Govt. for publication, for ss. 15, 17 and 17-A do not apply to order of Supreme Court - Tribunal to determine other issues - Appeal partly allowed.
