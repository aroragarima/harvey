Ujagar Singh v Mst. Jeo

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sarkar, Syed Jaffer Imam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 73; AIR 1959 SC 1041; 1960 SCJ 16; [1959] 2 S.C.R. 781

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Succession & Inheritance

Summary: Land & Property - Evidence Act - Punjab Laws Act, 1872 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Hindu Law of Inheritance (Amendment) Act, 1929 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - After Sahib Singh's death, ownership of his lands passed to his mother, Kishen Kaur - On Kishen Kaur's death disputes arose between Sahib Singh's sister, respondent in this appeal and his agnatic relation, appellant, as to ownership of lands - Whether according to customs in Punjab among agriculturist tribes was that sisters were excluded by collaterals in matter of succession to both ancestral and non-ancestral properties? - Held, there is a formidable array of authorities in support of either view - Not prepared to say that a custom giving preference to collaterals over sisters in matter of inheritance to non-ancestral properties has been so widely or uniformly recognised by courts as would justify taking judicial notice of it - High Court right in its view that it could not be held on, authority of para 24 in Rattigan's digest that a general custom excluding sisters from inheritance as against collaterals, existed - Appellant failed to establish custom alleged by him - Respondent proved custom which she set up - HC discussed evidence led by respondent, and found it acceptable - No reason to take a contrary view - Appeal dismissed.
