Jatin C. Jhaveri and another v Union of India and another

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Uday Umesh Lalit, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 2016 SC 2573; 2016(4) ALL MR 957; 2016 (117) ALR 494; 2016 (336) E.L.T. 3; 2016 (3) RCR(Criminal) 161; 2016(5) SCALE 583; 2016 (3) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Fema

Keywords: CEGAT, Currency, Customs, Customs Act, 1962, INDIRECT TAX, Any Goods, Confiscation, Seized, Foreign Currency, Any Person, Baggage, Time, India, Or Otherwise, Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973

Summary: FEMA - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1973, ss. 8(1), (64(2), 63 - Confiscation of currency - Permission of RBI - X was caught by Custom Officers with suitcase containing US $ 289,250 and briefcase with US $ 114,300 and on questioning, he disclosed that his brother was working with petitioner-J and that his trip was finalized and arranged by him - Notice was issued u/s.8(1) read with s.64(2) of the Act and later proceedings were initiated under the Act and Special Director passed order imposing penalty on petitioner-J, X and D and ordered confiscation of currency u/s. 63 of the Act - All three of them preferred appeals before Appellate Tribunal and Appellate Tribunal disposed of appeals holding that D could not be held guilty of charge of abetment in acquiring and transferring of Foreign Exchange unlawfully and in appeal preferred by X, confiscation of currency was affirmed but penalty was reduced to Rs. 1 Lakh and in appeal preferred by petitioner-J, order of confiscation of US $ 289,250 was quashed on ground that acquisition of currency was duly explained - Aggrieved, UOI challenged order of Appellate Tribunal before HC and petitioner-J preferred writ petition before HC contending that he was entitled to release of US $ 289,250 along with interest - HC passed common judgment and affirmed view taken by Appellate Tribunal and dismissed appeal and allowed writ petition holding that petitioner-J was entitled to currency amount but without any interest thereon - Aggrieved, petitioner-J preferred SLP against rejection of grant of interest and UOI preferred SLP challenging dismissal of appeal - Whether petitioner-J is entitled for currency amount.

Held, in his statement dt.12-10-1993, petitioner-J clearly dissociated himself and disowned the currency in question and the said statement was made more than two months after the seizure. Hence, subsequent reliance on currency declaration forms was, rightly found suspicious by Special Director in his order dt.4-10-1999. Further, no special or general permission as contemplated u/s.8(1) of FERA was produced or relied upon by petitioner-J and on contrary, petitioner-J had not even applied for and got such permission. Hence, orders of Appellate Tribunal and HC are set aside and appeal of UOI is allowed and order of Special Director is restored. Further, appeal of petitioner-J is dismissed. Appeals disposed of.

Ratio - In proceedings under FERA, emphasis is, on such acquisition of foreign exchange without the prior general or special permission of Reserve Bank. Any failure in that behalf would lead to incidents including confiscation u/s. 63 of FERA.
