Prem Nath Bali v Registrar, High Court of Delhi and another

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 869; AIR 2016 SC 101; 2016(1) ALL MR 963; 2016 (2) AWC 1373; 2016 (148) FLR 736; JT 2015 (12) SC 267; 2016 (1) RLW 845; 2016 (1) SLR 507

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Compulsory Retirement

Summary: Service - Alleged Misconduct - Suspension - Justification - Appellant joined office of court was promoted - Appellant filed compliant against other officer in charge alleging that she was discharging her duty effectively - Further, complainant was filed against appellant by other employee alleging that quarreled with her and used unwanted words in office - Preliminary enquiry was made and departmental enquiry was also held against appellant - Appellant was placed under suspension - Trial court vide order revoked order of suspension with immediate effect - However, Trial Court imposed major penalty of compulsory retirement on appellant and that appellant will not be entitled to any amount more than allowances already paid during period of suspension - HC vide impugned dismissed appeal filed by appellant - Hence, present appeal.

Held, inquiry officer fully observed principle of natural justice while conducting departmental proceedings. It is not in dispute that appellant was served with detailed charge sheet along with documents referred to therein. Parties were then given full opportunity to adduce evidence and which they availed of by examining witnesses in their support and by cross-examining each of them. SC held that HC examined this issue in detail and rightly recorded finding that inquiry officer observed principle of natural justice in departmental proceedings and found no fault in proceedings so as to entitle court to interfere in writ jurisdiction. Further, with respect to punishment of compulsory retirement inflicted on appellant, SC held that it is settled principle of law that once charges leveled against delinquent employee are proved then it is for appointing authority to decide as to what punishment should be imposed on delinquent employee as per Rules. SC held that in present case, appointing authority, keeping in view nature and gravity of charges, findings of inquiry officer, entire service record of delinquent employee and all relevant factors relating to delinquent, exercised its discretion and then imposed punishment as provided in Rules. Once such discretion is exercised by appointing authority in inflicting punishment (whether minor or major) then Courts would not interfere in quantum of punishment and only in rare and appropriate case substitutes punishment. SC held that appellant is unable to prove that punishment was arbitrary inflicted upon him. Further, with respect to non-entitlement of allowance during suspension period, SC held that no justification was forthcoming from respondents' side to explain undue delay in completion of departmental inquiry. Further, respondents while calculating qualifying service of appellant for determining his pension did not take into account period of suspension from 6-2-1990 to 1-3.1999. SC held that taking into consideration totality of facts and circumstances, period of suspension should have been taken into account by respondents for determining appellant's pension. SC directed respondents to re-determine appellant's pension by taking into account period of suspension and then pay to appellant arrears of difference amount from date he became eligible to claim pension and then to continue to pay appellant re-determined pension regularly in future as per Rules. Appeal partly allowed.

Ratio - Suspension of employee from service is justified when departmental enquiry and other procedures are carried in accordance with law.
