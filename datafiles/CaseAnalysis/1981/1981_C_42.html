Annapurna Biscuit Manufacturing Company, Kanpur v Commissioner of Sales Tax, U.P., Lucknow

Supreme Court of India

28 July 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, R.S. Pathak, V. Balakrishna Eradi

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 241; (1981) 3 SCC 542; AIR 1981 SC 1656; 1981 ALJ 906; 1981(3) SCALE 1101; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 149; 1981 (48) STC 254; 1981 TAXLR 3055

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Sales Tax - Uttar Pradesh Sales Tax Act, 1948 - Appellant engaged in the business of manufacture and sale of biscuits intended for human consumption - Whether expression 'cooked food' used in certain notifications issued under the Act can be construed as including within its meaning 'biscuits' also? - It is a well settled rule of construction that the words used in a law imposing a tax should be construed in the same way in which they are understood in ordinary parlance in the area in which the law is in force - Ordinarily biscuit is not understood as cooked food - Held, in the context and background of the notification biscuit cannot be treated as cooked food - Appeal dismissed.
