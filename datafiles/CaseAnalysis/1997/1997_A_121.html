Ansuyaben Kantilal Bhatt v Rashiklal Manilal Shah and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

D.P. Wadhwa, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 3033; (1997) 5 SCC 457; AIR 1997 SC 2510; 1997 (4) Bom.C.R. 354; JT 1997 (4) SC 747; 1997 (1) RCR(Rent) 670; 1997 (1) RentLR 551; 1997(3) SCALE 670; 1997 (2) SCJ 294; [1997] 3 S.C.R. 784; 1997 (4) Supreme 284; 1997 (1) UJ 816

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Rent Control - Eviction - Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947, s. 11 - Landlord filed a suit in 1966 for eviction of tenant for personal occupation - Application was filed on two grounds, namely, one she needs premises for personal occupation and on default in payment of rent for more than six months - Held, it need not be now decided due to long lapse of time of 31 years - No immediate need anymore - His son who 24 years of age is now going to retire - Under these circumstances, as and when he retires from service and if he intends to set up business, it is always open to make necessary application - Further held, since standard rent has already been fixed by court and deposited by tenant, default does not subsist - Concept of willful default does not apply to action under Act - Therefore, if there is any default in payment of rent and if amount so paid gets adjusted after determination of standard rent, necessarily, default originally existing ceases to operate - Appeal disposed of.
