Bharat Hydro Power Corp. Ltd. & Ors. v State Of Assam & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 40; (2004) 2 SCC 553; AIR 2004 SC 3173; JT 2004 (1) SC 63; 2004(1) SCALE 211; [2004] 1 S.C.R. 284; 2004 (1) SLT 973; 2004 (1) Supreme 285

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: State Legislature, Repugnancy, Bharat Hydro Power Corporation Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1996, Indian Electricity (Amendment) Act, 1959, Assam Act, 1997

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 245, 246, 254, 254(2) - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Assam Bharat Hydro Power Corporation Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1996 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 37 - Indian Electricity (Amendment) Act, 1959 - Repugnancy between state and central legislation - Assam Bharat Hydro Power Corporation Limited (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertaking) Act, 1996 and between Indian Electricity Act, 1910, Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948 - State and central Acts operate in two different fields - No encroachment - State Act to acquire undertaking and pay compensation as provided in Act - Both Central Acts make general provisions with regard to supply and use of electrical energy - No semblance of conflict - Acts can operate simultaneously as they do not occupy same field - No repugnancy.
