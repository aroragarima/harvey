Â State Of Madhya Pradesh v Veereshwar Rao Agnihotry

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1957

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Govinda Menon, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1957 Indlaw SC 30; AIR 1957 SC 592; 1957 CRLJ 892; 1957 SCJ 519; [1957] 1 S.C.R. 868

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898, Prevention of Corruption Act, misa

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 403 - Prevention of Corruption Act,1947, ss. 7,5(4) and 5(2) - Indian Penal Code,1860, s. 409 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 26 - Appeal against judgment and order of acquittal passed - detention for offences u/ss. 468, 477-A and 409 IPC,1860 - (A)Whether an offence u/s. 5(2) of Prevention of Corruption Act,1947 and s. 409 of IPC,1860 identical? - Held, offence of criminal misconduct punishable u/s. 5(2) of Prevention of Corruption Act is not identical in essence, import and content with an offence u/s. 409 of IPC - Offence of criminal misconduct is a new offence created by that enactment - It does not repeal s. 409 of IPC,1860 - (A1) Whether s. 403 has any application where there is one trial for several offences? - Further held, s. 403(1) has no application to case where there is only one trial for several offences - (A2) Whether art. 20 of Constitution would apply in cases where accused has not been tried twice for an offence? - Further held, principle 'Nemo debet bis vexari si constat curice quod sit pro una et eadem causa' meaning 'No man shall be twice punished if it appears to court that it is for one and same cause' as embodied in art. 20 of Constitution cannot apply as accused was not prosecuted after he has already been tried and acquitted for same offence in an earlier trial - (B)Whether non compliance with s. 5(4) would take away jurisdiction of special magistrate u/s. 7 of Prevention of Corruption Act,1947 to try case? - Held, even though provisions of s. 5(4) of Prevention of Corruption Act,1947 has not been complied with, Special Magistrate shall not be barred from trying case u/s 7 of Act of 1947 - Appeal allowed.
