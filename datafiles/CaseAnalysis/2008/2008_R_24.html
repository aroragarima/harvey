Delhi Development Authority v R.S. Sharma and Company, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

26 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1345; (2008) 13 SCC 80; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 717; 2009 (1) AWC 246; 2010 (5) CompLJ 50; 2008 (152) DLT 678; JT 2008 (9) SC 362; 2009 (1) MLJ(SC) 327; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 165; 2008(11) SCALE 663; [2008] 12 S.C.R. 785

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Solicitor

Summary: Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Agreement for carrying out the work for development of the land entered between appellant and respondent - Held, clause 3.16 of the Agreement makes it clear that the contractor is wholly responsible for all the extra leads - Award of the Arbitrator as well as the judgment of the Division Bench clearly shows that they did not advert to the clause 3.16 - Extra cartage has been awarded by the Arbitrator without adverting to clause 3.16 of the Agreement, hence, the single Judge was wholly justified in partially setting aside the Award in respect of the claims with respect to the extra cartage - Appeal allowed.
