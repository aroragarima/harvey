Â Shivdeo Singh and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 517; AIR 1963 SC 1909

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Administration of Evacuee Property (Central) Rules (1950), rr.14 and 49 - Certain agricultural lands were allotted to a number of displaced persons on a quasi-permanent basis - Director of Rehabilitation declared village to be a 'fauji 'village and stated that land therein will have to be reallotted though, according to statement of facts in High Court, Director of Rehabilitation had actually passed an order canceling allotment already made in favour of 'non- fauji' families - Appellants fall under category of 'non-faujis' - Appellant preferred a Writ Petition before HC for quashing order - Petition was allowed - Eventually respondents 3 to 14 who are members of 'fauji' families, in whose favour either allotment had been made or was intended to be made, preferred a petition before HC under art. 226 of Constitution - Appellants preferred an appeal before HC under Letters Patent, which have been dismissed - Whether the HC's exercise of power of review in art. 226 petition, is right? - Held, it is sufficient to say that there is nothing in art. 226 of Constitution to preclude a HC from exercising power of review which inheres in every court of plenary jurisdiction to prevent miscarriage of justice or to correct grave and palpable errors committed by it - Here previous order of Khosla, J affected interests of persons who were not made parties to proceeding before him - It was at their instance and for giving them a hearing that Khosla, J entertained second petition - In doing so, he merely did what principles of natural justice required him to do - It is precisely because they were not made parties to previous proceedings, though their interests were sought to be affected by decision of HC, that second application was entertained by Khosla, J - Appeal dismissed.
