Chief Settlement Commissioner v Om Parkash and Ors

Supreme Court of India

05 April 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 138; AIR 1969 SC 33; 1968 (70) PLR 1082; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 655

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Keywords: East Punjab Evacuees' (Administration of Property) Act, 1947, East Punjab Refugees (Registration of Land Claims) Act, 1948

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution - Punjab Tenancy Act, 1887 - East Punjab Evacuees' (Administration of Property) Act, 1947 - East Punjab Refugees (Registration of Land Claims) Act, 1948, ss. 2(E) and 2(D)- Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 13 - Definition of 'displaced person' in para 2(e) of Notification issued u/r made pursuant to s. 22(2)(f) and (ff) and definition of 'Refugee' u/s. 2(d) of Refugees Act, 1948, person with property in Pakistan coming to India prior to partition dying in June 1947 - Shown as owner of properties in revenue records received from West Punjab (A) Whether such person is displaced person? - Definition of a 'displaced person' in para 2(e) of Notification of 8-07-1949, issued by Custodian in accordance with provisions of East Punjab Evacuees Act 1947, and Rules made thereunder, or of a 'Refugee' in s. 2(d) of East Punjab Refugees Act, 1948, show that these expressions have been used in relevant enactments with reference to a person who has migrated to India as a result of disturbances or fear of disturbances or partition of country - (1) Whether allotment of land in India must be made in his name or in name of his heirs? - It is applicable only to such persons who are land-holders at time of their becoming displaced persons or refugees and who died afterwards before allotment could be made in their favour - Held, if a person had died before disturbances took place or he had never migrated to India as a result of disturbances and he died before such migration, he could not come within meaning of expression 'displaced person' or word 'refugee' under relevant statutory enactments - Appeal dismissed.
