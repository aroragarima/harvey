Nil Ratan Kundu and Another v Abhijit Kundu

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1619; (2008) 9 SCC 413; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 732; 2008 (6) ALD(SC) 105; 2009 (1) AWC 377; 2008 (4) CTC 425; 2009 (1) JhrCR(SC) 6; JT 2008 (8) SC 592; 2008 (7) MLJ(SC) 248; 2008 (3) RCR(Civil) 936; 2008(11) SCALE 437; [2008] 11 S.C.R. 1111

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Family & Personal; Women & Children

Keywords: Custody Of Children, Power Of The Court, Grand Father, Grand Mother, Offence Punishable, Step-Mother, Undivided Interest, Father's Sister, Welfare Of Minor, Powers Of Court

Summary: Family & Personal - Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956, ss. 6 and 13 - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955 - Guardians and Wards Act, 1890, ss. 7, 12, 25 and 17 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 498-A - Constitution of India, 1950 - Father claiming custody of minor child - From maternal grand father and grand mother - Application under Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Held, principles in relation to custody of a minor child are well settled - In determining question as to who should be given custody of a minor child, paramount consideration is 'welfare of child' and not rights of parents under a statute for time being in force - In instant case, on overall considerations it is convinced that Courts below were not right or justified in granting custody of minor to respondent herein without applying relevant and well-settled principle of welfare of child as paramount consideration - Trial Court ought to have ascertained wishes of minor as to with whom he wanted to stay - Appeal allowed.
