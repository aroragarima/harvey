State of Bihar v Banshi Ram Modi and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1985

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1985 Indlaw SC 160; (1985) 3 SCC 643; AIR 1985 SC 814; 1985(1) SCALE 1201; [1985] Supp1 S.C.R. 345; 1985 UJ 928

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980, s. 2 (ii) - Whether mining operations which were carried on in five acres of land for purpose of winning felspar and quartz, were illegal by reason of absence of previous approval of Central Govt. granted under Act? - If State Govt. permits lessee by amendment of lease deed to win and remove felspar and quartz also in addition to mica it cannot be said that State Govt. has violated s. 2 of Act because thereby no permission for fresh breaking up of forest land is being given - Result of taking contrary view will be that while digging for purposes of winning mica can go on, lessee would be deprived of collecting felspar or quartz which he may come across while he is carrying on mining operations for winning mica - That would lead to an unreasonable result which would not in any way subserve object of Act - While before granting permission to start mining operations on a virgin area s. 2 of Act has to be complied with, it is not necessary to seek prior approval of Central Govt. for purposes of carrying out mining operations in a forest area which is broken up or cleared before commencement of Act - Respondent given an undertaking that he would confine his mining operations only to extent of five acres of land on which mining operations have already been carried out and will not fell or remove any standing trees - Held, respondent is entitled to carry on mining operations in said five acres of land for purposes of removing falser and quartz - Appeal disposed of.
