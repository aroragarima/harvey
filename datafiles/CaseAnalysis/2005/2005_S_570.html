Bhaurao Dagdu Paralkar v State of Maharashtra and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 471; (2005) 7 SCC 605; AIR 2005 SC 3330; 2005 (4) AWC 3460; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 770; 2005 (4) ESC 542; JT 2005 (7) SC 530; 2005(6) SCALE 625; 2005 (5) SLR 595; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 774; 2005 (5) Supreme 836

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: [A.] Socio-Economic - Pension - Freedom Fighters' Pension Scheme - Bogus claimants - High Court took five sample cases and came to hold that documents produced were sufficient to substantiate claims - Appealed herein - Held, sampling cannot be method for determining truth or otherwise of allegations or claims made - Each case was required to be individually examined - Appointed a retired Judge to examine 354 cases - Appeals allowed.
