Parivartan Kendra v Union of India and others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

M.Y. Eqbal, C. Nagappan

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 848; (2016) 3 SCC 571; 2016 (1) AWC 577; 2016 (1) Bom.C.R. 785; 2016 (1) CGLJ 244; 2015 (4) CLT(SC) 308; JT 2015 (12) SC 14; 2015 (8) MLJ 504; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 336; 2015(13) SCALE 325; [2015] 12 S.C.R. 607

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Socio-Economic

Keywords: The Union Territories

Summary: Criminal - Acid attack victim - Payment of compensation and rehabilitation - On day of incident, two dalit girls i.e. victims were attacked by four assailants who threw acid on face and bodies of girls while they were sleeping on their rooftops - Further, it is stated that victim was paid compensation of Rs. 2,42,000/- for State Govt. for treatment of both victims - Petitioner contended that more that Rs. 5 lakhs had already been spent on their treatment and still victims require more treatment - Further, petitioner contended that proper and adequate treatment was not given to victim and compensation was not adequately paid to victim -Petitioner has sought justice, compensation and restoration of dignity of survivors of acid attack - Hence, present appeal.

Held, due to acid attack, victim had undergone several surgeries, and has to undergo many more corrective and curative surgeries for her treatment. Further, medical treatment, which she is required to undergo, there are many other consequences, which acid attack brings out in life of victim. SC held that considering plight of victim and victim will not be able to lead normal life and cannot dream of marriage prospects since her skin is fragile due to acid attack she would have to take care of it for rest of her life. Hence, after care and rehabilitation cost that has to be incurred will have huge financial implications on her and her family. Hence, SC thought it fit to enhance compensation and mere amount of Rs. 3 lakhs will not be of any help to such victim. Further, SC held that enhancement of compensation will act in two ways i.e.it will help victim in rehabilitation and it will also make State to implement guidelines properly as State will try to comply with it in its true sprit so that crime of acid attack can be prevented in future. Further, Laxmi v Union of India and others, 2013 Indlaw SCO 1380 ,relied on, SC held that it doesn't put bar on Govt. to award compensation limited to Rs.3 Lakhs. State has discretion to provide more compensation to victim in case of acid attack as guidelines. SC held that taking into consideration severity of victim's injury, expenditure with regard to grafting and reconstruction surgery, physical and mental pain, etc., victim should be compensated at Rs. 10 Lakhs. Further, SC directed State Govt. to compensate main victim's sister, to tune of Rs. 3 Lakhs. Further, State shall upon itself take full responsibility for treatment and rehabilitation of victims of acid attack as per guidelines provided in earlier judgement. Further, SC directed States and Union Territories to consider plight of such victims and take appropriate steps with regard to inclusion of their names under disability list. Petition allowed.

Ratio - Court is justified to award compensation to victim of acid attack and issue further directions taking into consideration their rehabilitation and treatment given to them.
