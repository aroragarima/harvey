Gobardhan Das v Lachhmi Ram and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 200; AIR 1954 SC 689

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 133(1) - Partnership business - Application for setting aside award although same had not been filed in Court - Whether arbitrators acted within scope of powers conferred upon them by agreement to refer for arbitration? - Arbitrators negatived the appellant's claim against respondents on extraneous ground that they had contributed labour and were men in poor circumstances as against appellant who was alleged to be a rich man - Award thus arrived at was anything but a settlement of dispute which had arisen between parties and which had necessitated reference to arbitration - Held, arbitrators exceeded scope of their authority and their award cannot be allowed to stand - Agreement for reference to arbitration should be liberally construed by court, but it is no ground for sustaining award where arbitrators have clearly misdirected themselves and have exceeded scope of their authority - Award set aside with costs - Appeal allowed.
