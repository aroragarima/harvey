Bisra Stone Lime Company Limited and Another v Orissa State Electricity Board & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 364; (1976) 2 SCC 167; AIR 1976 SC 127; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Electricity & Energy

Summary: Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, s. 49 - Constitution, arts. 14 and 226 - (A) Permissibility to enhancerates of tariff by way of surcharge - (B) Other remedy open - Arbitration clause in agreement between a textile manufacturing company and State Govt.in 1960 - State Electricity Board as constituted in 1962 assuming all obligations of State Govt. and as such bound by agreement - Whether Board has power to levy surcharge in view of certain clause and that as to interpretation of the clause - (C) Plea of 'undue favour' u/s. 49 of the Act, if at par with that of discrimination u/art. 14 of Constitution - (A) Held, merely because there was special agreement between the parties it could not be said that the stipulated rates could not be increased by adding the surcharge in question - (B) Question held, to be covered by arbitration clause - Company should pursue remedy of arbitration instead of extraordinary jurisdiction of HC u/art. 226 - (C) Plea of discrimination which is available when art.14 of Constitution is in free play is not at par with the interdict of 'undue favour' u/s.49 of the Act - Appeal Dismissed.
