An Advocate v Bar Council of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 242; (1989) Supp2 SCC 25; (1989) SCC (Cr) 625; AIR 1989 SC 245; 1988 (2) CCC 920; JT 1988 (4) SC 376; 1989 (1) KLT 116; 1988(2) SCALE 1362; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 361; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 361

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Right To Practise, Attorney-General

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Advocates Act, 1961 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 4, r. 30 - Professional misconduct - Appellant suspended from practicing for 3 years on account of professional misconduct - Suspension on account of withdrawal of suit without permission of client - Appeal preferred on ground of violation of natural justice - No charges framed specifying nature and content of professional misconduct attributed to appellant - Whether appellant denied of natural justice of being heard? - Held, evidence proved that appellant not fully informed about charges against him and not given opportunity to defend - Disciplinary Committee has not recorded any finding on facts and doctrine of benefit of doubt not applied - Impugned order untenable and so have to set aside - Matter remitted back to Bar Council of India - Appeal allowed.
