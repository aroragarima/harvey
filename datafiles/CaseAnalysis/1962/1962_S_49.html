Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 431; AIR 1962 SC 1314; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 549

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 110 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, ss. 73 and 74 - Suit for damages for breach of contract - High Court dismissed suit as there being no substantial question of law - Whether document referred raises substantial question of law? - Proper test for determining question of law is to see whether it is of general importance or it directly affect rights of parties and of so whether it is either an open question not finally decided by court? - High Court was in error to say that there was no substantial question of law involved so appeal maintainable - HC was right in awarding compensation at rate of Rs. 6000 per month for unexpired period - Held, under general law right to claim damages excluded by proving compensation in express terms - Decree of HC affirmed - Appeal dismissed.
