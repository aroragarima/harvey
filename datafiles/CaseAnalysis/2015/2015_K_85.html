Kulwinder Singh and another v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 437; (2015) 6 SCC 674; AIR 2015 SC 2488; 2015 CRLJ 3160; JT 2015 (5) SC 545; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2097; 2015 (2) RCR(Criminal) 918; 2015 (4) RLW 3400; 2015(6) SCALE 1; [2015] 6 S.C.R. 175

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 15, 50 - Alleged possession of drugs - Conviction - Justification - Appellant accused were travelling in bus and on search police narcotics drugs in excess quantity - Trail Court convicted appellants accused u/s. 15 of Act - Order passed by Trail Court is confirmed by HC - Appellant contended that identity of appellants were not established during trial inasmuch as no identification parade was conducted by investigating officer - Further, prosecution had not proved that accused-appellants were in conscious possession of narcotic drug - Whether impugned order passed by lower Courts convicting appellant accused is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, with respect to test identification parade. SC held that test identification parade has not been held. PW's 2, 3 have identified accused-appellants in court and as per their evidence they had seen accused-appellants in torch light and they had also seen them running away. It has also come in evidence that they chased them but they could not be apprehended. Trial Court and HC held that it was 4:00 a.m. in month of April, hence, it was not all that dark and with help of torch light, they could have identified accused persons. Further, suggestion given to these witnesses is absolutely vague. Nothing really has been elicited in cross-examination to discard testimony of these witnesses. SC held that as witnesses have identified accused-appellants in Court and except giving bald suggestion that they have not seen accused persons, there is nothing in cross-examination, hence, SC is disposed to accept identification in Court. Further, with respect to appellant accused being in conscious possession of drugs, SC held that when truck was stopped by police, had accused-appellants no knowledge about contents of bags, they would not have run away from spot. Further, they absconded for few days from their village. They have not taken plea that they were taking any lift in truck and their presence in truck has been proven by prosecution. Further, it is not small bag lying in corner of truck that accused-appellants can advance plea that they were not aware of it. SC held that there were 110 bags of drugs being carried in truck. Their presence which has been proven, establishes their control over bags. Hence, circumstances clearly establish that they were aware of drugs inside bags, hence, it is difficult to accept that they were not in conscious possession of said articles. Further, with respect to non-compliance of s. 50 of Act, SC held that bags containing drugs were seized from truck and it is not case of personal search of person, hence, s. 50 of Act would not be applicable. Further, SC held that case of prosecution cannot be rejected solely on ground that independent witnesses have not been examined when, on perusal of evidence on record Court finds that case put forth by prosecution is trustworthy. Hence, order passed by lower Courts convicting appellants accused is justified and in accordance with law. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Once possession is found, accused is presumed to be in conscious possession and when evidence of official witnesses are trustworthy and credible, there is no reason not to rest conviction on basis of their evidence.
