Shiv Dutt Jadiya v Ganga Devi

Supreme Court of India

20 February 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 121; (2002) 3 SCC 189; AIR 2002 SC 1163; JT 2002 (2) SC 311; 2002 (1) RCR(Rent) 335; 2002(2) SCALE 297; 2002 (2) SCJ 150; [2002] 1 S.C.R. 1167; 2002 (2) SLT 165; 2002 (2) Supreme 102

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Rent Control

Summary: When, the tenant was continuing to deposit the rent during the pendency of appeal preferred by the landlord against the decree in the first suit, then, no cause of action arose to the landlord for filing a second suit for eviction and holding the tenant a defaulter for the same period, since, the tenant is not required to pay the same rent twice over personally to the landlord or to tender the same in one of the modes prescribed by Section 19-A of the Rajasthan Premises (Control of Rent and Eviction) Act, 1950.
