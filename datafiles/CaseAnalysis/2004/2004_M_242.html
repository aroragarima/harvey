Dharma Prathishthanam v Messrs Madhok Construction Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, G.P. Mathur, P.P. Naolekar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 923; (2005) 9 SCC 686; AIR 2005 SC 214; 2004 (114) DLT 681; JT 2004 (9) SC 335; 2005 (1) MLJ(SC) 70; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 769; 2004(9) SCALE 205; [2004] Supp5 S.C.R. 866

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration and ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 14 and 17 - Award given by arbitrator ex-parte - Whether, in light of a general provision as in cl. 35, respondent could have unilaterally appointed an arbitrator without consent of appellant and could have made a reference to such arbitrator again without reference of disputes having been consented to by appellant? - There is ample judicial opinion available for proposition that reference to a sole arbitrator as contemplated by para 1 of First Schedule has to be a consensual reference and not a unilateral reference by one party alone to which other party does not consent - A unilateral appointment and a unilateral reference both will be illegal - Appeal allowed.
