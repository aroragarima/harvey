Shankaria v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

26 April 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, N.L. Untwalia, P.S. Kailasam

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 175; (1978) 3 SCC 435; (1978) SCC (Cr) 439; AIR 1978 SC 1248; 1979 CRLJ 1251; 1978 CrLR(SC) 245; [1978] 3 S.C.R. 736

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal - Code Of Criminal Procedure, 1973 s. 164 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 s. 24 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 302 - Confession - Appellants convicted u/s. 302 IPC - Conviction based on confessional statement - Whether confession made was appellant was vlountary? - No ocular evidence of eye-witnesses - No statutory provision in s. 164 CrPC that there should be interval of 24 hours or more between preliminary questioning of accused and recording of his confession - Appellant had no less than 38 hours at his disposal whilst he was in judicial custody free from fear or influence of Police, to think and decide whether or not to make confession - Magistrate having reasons to believe that confession was made voluntarily - Nothing probable or unbelievable in statement of confession - Confession statement appears to be spontaneous account, studded with such vivid details about manner of commission of crimes in question which only perpetrator of crimes could know - Circumstantial facts found at spot tell tale which is consistent with one told by appellant - Medical evidence corroborates confessional statement - Held, established cogently that confession was voluntarily made and it is true - Appeal dismissed.
