Delhi Development Authority v Pushpendra Kumar Jain

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.C. Sen

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1072; (1994) Supp3 SCC 494; AIR 1995 SC 1; 1994 (24) ALR 535; JT 1994 (6) SC 292; 1994(4) SCALE 297; [1994] Supp3 S.C.R. 770; 1995 (1) UJ 59

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Land and Property - Land and Property - Appellant, Delhi Development Authority (DDA) prepared and published a scheme called 'Registration Scheme of New Pattern, 1979 of intending purchasers/needy citizens of flats to be constructed by DDA - Appellant has been adopting the method of draw of lots among the registered applicants to select the allottees - Substantial enhancement of land rates in the region of about fifty to seventy per cent - Validity of revision of Land rates? - Held, since the right to flat arises only on communication of letter of allotment, price or rates prevailing on the date of such communication is applicable unless otherwise provided in the Scheme -If in case the respondent is not willing to take or accept the allotment at such rate, it is always open to him to decline the allotment - Thus procedure is not unfair - Further held, validity or justification of revision of land rates by circular dt/-06/12/1990 was not questioned in petition nor has it been pronounced upon by HC nor has it been urged before Apex Court - Therefore, Court proceeds on the assumption that revision of land rates is valid - Appeal allowed. -
