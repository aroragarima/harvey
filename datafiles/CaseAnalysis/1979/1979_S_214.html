Shivnarayan Laxminarayan Joshi and Others v State of Maharashtra and another

Supreme Court of India

10 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, A.P. Sen

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 359; (1980) 2 SCC 465; (1980) SCC (Cr) 493; AIR 1980 SC 439; 1980 CRLJ 388; 1980 CRLR 1

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120-B, 477-A, 409 - Misappropriation - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants/accused allegedly committed offence u/ss. 120-B, 477-A, 409 of IPC - Trial Court convicted and sentenced appellants u/ss. 120-B, 477-A, 409 of IPC - Appellants preferred an appeal before HC which were upheld or modified to some extent - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, SC was unable to find any grave error of law so as to vitiate findings of HC and called for this Court interference - For all those reasons, therefore, all appeals were dismissed with modification in case of appellant no. 1, as indicated above and bail of appellant nos. 2, 3, 4 and 24, were cancelled and they would now surrender and serve out remaining portion of sentence - Therefore, as regards appeal against acquittal filed by State with regard to other accused persons who were acquitted by HC this Court did not find any substance in this appeal because HC had clearly fond that they had no clear knowledge of conspiracy and even approver had made number of inconsistent statements regarding participation of acquitted respondents in conspiracy - Thus, for those reasons, this Court fully agreed with order of HC in acquitting respondents and appeal filed by state was also therefore dismissed - Appeals disposed of.
