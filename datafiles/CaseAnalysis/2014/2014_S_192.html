Ranjan Kumar Etc. v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 312; 2014(6) SCALE 579

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Appointments, Quashing of appointments

Summary: Service - Appointments - Quashing of appointments - Justifiability - Advertisement issued by respondent concerned department of State Government, 182 persons were appointed on post of Medical Laboratory Technician MLT - Appellants who participated in interview could not be selected as they obtained lesser marks than successful candidates - Appellants filed petitions before Single Judge - Single Judge quashed the appointment of a number of appointees in respect of the post, namely, Medical Laboratory Technician (MLT) on the ground that the procedure adopted for selection was vitiated as the candidates were selected only by interview without holding any written test though the past practice was to conduct an examination and thereafter hold interview for selection; that the interview was held in a hurried manner; and that the posts being technical in nature, holding of an examination was warranted - Appeals before DB - DB upheld the order of Single Judge - Hence instant appeals - Whether the order passed by HC was justified -

Held, at time of selection and appointment there was no rule or regulation, procedure used to be adopted by administrative instructions, that apart, it was not large body of appointees but only 182 appointees - Quite apart from that persons who were impleaded, were not treated to be in representative capacity - It was further observed that number of selected candidates was not many and there was no difficulty for appellant to implead them as parties in proceeding - Ultimately, Court held that when all selected candidates were not impleaded as parties to petition, no relief could be granted to appellant therein - When respondents appeared in interview knowing fully well process, they could not have resiled later on or taken somersault saying that procedure as adopted by department was vitiated - Hence, judgment and order passed by DB and Single Judge was set aside - Appeals allowed.
