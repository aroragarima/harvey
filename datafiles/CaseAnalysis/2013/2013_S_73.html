Nirma Industries Ltd. And Anr. v Securities And Exchange Board Of India

Supreme Court of India

09 May 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 319; (2013) 8 SCC 20; AIR 2013 SC 2360; 2013 (3) CompLJ 275; 2014 (2) DRTC 457; JT 2013 (7) SC 185; 2013(7) SCALE 261; [2013] 3 S.C.R. 662

Case Digest

Subject: Capital Market; Securities Exchange Board of India

Keywords: Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) (Second Amendment) Regulations, 2002, SEBI Regulations, 2003

Summary: Securities Exchange Board of India - Capital Market - Securities and Exchange Board of India Act, 1992, s. 11 - Securities and Exchange Board of India (Substantial Acquisition of Shares and Takeovers) Regulations, 1997, regn. 27(1)(d) - Withdrawal of public offer -Interests of investors - Validity - Appellants filed an application for withdrawal of an offer to acquire equity shares of 'X' under the Regulations which was rejected by an order by Securities Exchange Board of India (SEBI) - On appeal, Tribunal dismissed appeal and upheld order of SEBI - Hence, instant appeal - Whether impugned order of SEBI as upheld by Tribunal was justified - Held, all material was placed by appellants before SEBI and same material was also placed before Merchant Bankers - Further, necessary clarifications as required by Merchant Bankers was also given in subsequent correspondences - Therefore, it could not be said that substantial justice was not done in case of appellants - Firstly, neither appellants nor their Merchant Bankers requested for an opportunity for a personal hearing - Secondly, in instant case, SEBI had not issued any instructions or directions u/s. 11 of the Act which requires that rules of natural justice be complied with - Thirdly, it could not be said that appellants were condemned unheard as entire material on which appellants were relying was placed before SEBI. It is upon consideration of the entire matter that the offer of the appellants was rejected - Therefore, it could not be said that appellants were in any manner prejudiced by non-grant of opportunity of personal hearing - However, in instant case, SC was not satisfied that circumstances were such which would make it impossible for acquirer to perform public offer - Possibility that acquirer would end-up making loses instead of generating a huge profit would not bring situation within realm of impossibility - Hence, SEBI had all powers to protect interests of investors in securities and also to ensure orderly, regulated, and transparent functioning of stock markets - Encouraging such a practice of an offer being withdrawn which had become uneconomical would have a destabilizing effect in securities market - Appellants could not be permitted to take advantage of its own laxity to justify seeking withdrawal of public offer under the Regulation - Permitting them to do so would deprive ordinary shareholders of their valuable right to have an exit option under the Regulations - Thus, appellants were prevaricating and did not agree with interpretation placed on regn. 27(1)(d) of the Regulations by Merchant Banker - Therefore, contention of appellants that there was delay on part of SEBI in approving draft letter of offer was dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
