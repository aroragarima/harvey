State Of U.P. v Shri Kishan

Supreme Court of India

30 November 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1766; (2005) 10 SCC 420; 2005 CRLJ 333; JT 2004 (10) SC 289; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 385; 2004(10) SCALE 150; [2004] Supp6 S.C.R. 530

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 302 and 304 - Imposition of sentence - Accused faced trial for offence punishable u/s. 302 IPC for having caused homicidal death of deceased - Though the accused was charged for commission of offence punishable u/s. 302 of IPC, the Trial Judge held that appropriate conviction would be u/s. 304 Part II of IPC and rigorous imprisonment for 7 years was awarded - On appeal, HC reduced the custodial sentence to the period already undergone - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC justified in reducing the period of sentence awarded to the accused? - Held, it was the duty of every court to award proper sentence having regard to the nature of the offence and the manner in which it was executed or committed - After giving due consideration to the facts and circumstances of each case, for deciding just and appropriate sentence to be awarded for an offence, the aggravating and mitigating factors and circumstances in which a crime was committed were to be delicately balanced on the basis of really relevant circumstances in a dispassionate manner by the court - It was expected that the courts would operate the sentencing system so as to impose such sentence which reflects the conscience of the society and the sentencing process had to be stern where it should be - Court would be failing in its duty if appropriate punishment was not awarded for a crime which had been committed not only against the individual victim but also against the society to which the criminal and victim belong - Since all these aspects had not been noted by the HC and by a practically unreasoned order the matter was disposed of in a most unsatisfactory manner, it would be appropriate for the HC to rehear the appeal on the question of sentence - Appeal disposed of.
