Commissioner Of Wealth Tax. Kanpur Etc. Etc v Chander Sen Etc.

Supreme Court of India

16 July 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 172; (1986) 3 SCC 567; (1986) SCC (Tax) 641; AIR 1986 SC 1753; 1986 (58) CTR 119; [1986] 161 ITR 370; 1986(2) SCALE 75; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 254; [1986] 27 TAXMAN 330; 1986 (2) TLR 1328

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Women & Children

Keywords: Coparcener, Female Heirs, Succession & Inheritance, After-Born Son

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 3 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, Pre. and s. 8 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 37 - Taxation - Partition of joint family business - Among father, A and his only son, B - Business continued as partnership firm - B forming joint family with his own sons - Death of A - Amount standing to credit of A in the account of firm devolves on son as his individual income - (A) Whether such income can be included in computing net wealth of son's joint family - (B) Interest credited to A's account in firm - If it is deductible in computing income of B's family - Held, s. 8 of Hindu Succession Act, 1956 has modified the old Hindu Law hence such income cannot be included - It is deductible - Appeals dismissed.
