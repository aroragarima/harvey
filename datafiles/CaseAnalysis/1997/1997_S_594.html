State of Orissa v B.N. Agarwalla Etc

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, A.M. Ahmadi, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 814; (1997) 2 SCC 469; AIR 1997 SC 925; 1997 (1) ARBLR 612; 1997 (2) AWC 824; 1997 (1) CLT 375; JT 1997 (2) SC 51; 1997(1) SCALE 599; 1997 (1) SCJ 251; [1997] 1 S.C.R. 704; 1997 (2) Supreme 43

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Interest Act, 1978 - Jurisdiction of arbitrator to award interest - Pre-reference period -Whether the arbitrator had jurisdiction to award pendente lite interest and pre-reference period.

Held, the arbitrator has the jurisdiction to aware pre-reference interest in cases which arose after the Act has become applicable and with regard to those cases pertaining to period prior to the applicability of the Act, in the absence of any substantive law, contract or usage, the arbitrator has no jurisdiction to award interest. The power of the arbitrator to award interest for the post award period also exist. Order accordingly.
