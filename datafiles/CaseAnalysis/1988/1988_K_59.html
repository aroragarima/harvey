Collector of Central Excise, Kanpur v Krishna Carbon Paper Company

Supreme Court of India

16 September 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 917; (1989) SCC (Tax) 42; (1989) 1 SCC 150; AIR 1988 SC 2223; 1989 (19) ECC 32; 1989 (20) ECR 273; 1988 (37) E.L.T. 480; JT 1988 (4) SC 762; 1988(2) SCALE 880; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 12; 1989 (72) STC 280; [1988] Supp3 S.C.R. 12

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Tariff Item, Paper Boards

Summary: Excise - Classification - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 35L(b) - Whether carbon papers could be included in 'all kinds of paper including paper which have been subjected to coating', would come within sub-item (2) of item 17 or under residuary item 68 of Central Excise Tariff? - Tribunal held that on basis of definition of paper as it was in year 1976 carbon paper would come within item 17(2)of Tariff Items - A word which is not defined in an enactment has to be understood in its popular and commercial sense with reference to context in which it occurs - Word has to be understood according to well established canon of construction in sense in which persons dealing with and using article understand it - Held, it is sufficient to hold that it was covered by item 17(2) of PG No. 28 C.E.T and would not fall into residuary entry - Appeal must be allowed - Further held, case remanded back to Tribunal to decide having regard to facts - Appeal disposed of.
