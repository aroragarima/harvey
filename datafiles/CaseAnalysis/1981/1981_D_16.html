Sardar Govindrao Mahadik and Another v Devi Sahai and Others

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai, R.B. Misra

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 382; (1982) 1 SCC 237; AIR 1982 SC 989; 1982(1) SCALE 191; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 186

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conditional Sale

Summary: Constitution, arts. 133 - CPC, 1908, O. 22 r. 10, s. 146, O.21 rr. 92, 94, s. 60, O.38, r. 5 - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 53-A - (A) New plea - Admissibility - Contention of facts based on evidence advanced for the first time - Arising out of record and proceedings - (B) Transfer of property - Conditions precedent for appplicability of doctrine of part-performance - Acts anterior to contract or merely incidental to contract - (C) CPC, 1908 - (1) Property sold to decree-holder auction purchaser - Confirmed and sale certificate granted - Maintainability of his application u/r. 10 of O. 22 in another redemption suit - (2) Attachment before judgment - Object of - (3) Auction-purchaser, decree-holder himself - Setting aside of decree in appeal - Auction-purchaser, if entitled to any protection - (A) SC examined them on merits - (B) Held, no evidence of part performance - (C) Auction-purchaser not acquiring equity of redemption but relief open to him - To give an assurance to the plaintiff that his decree if made would be satisfied - Ordinarily entitled, but if the auction-purchaser is the decree-holder himself, he should not be entitled to any protection - Appeals allowed.
