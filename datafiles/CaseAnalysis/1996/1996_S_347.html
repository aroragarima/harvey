Delhi Development Authority v Skipper Construction Company Private Limited And Another

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3923; (1996) 4 SCC 622; AIR 1996 SC 2005; AIR 1996 SCW 2401; 1996 (2) CCC 241; [1997] 89 Comp Cas 362; 1996 (4) CompLJ 233; JT 1996 (4) SC 679; 1996(4) SCALE 202; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 295

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Corporate; Municipalities & Local Governments

Summary: Constitution of India, art. 142, 129, 300 A and 19(1)(g) - Contract Act, 1872, s. 182 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 39, r. 1 - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971, s. 12 - Lifting corporate veil - Equitable relief - Vicarious liability - Corporate bodies created by an individual and his family members - Property acquired by indulging in corrupt, illegal acts and by defrauding people - Absence of fiduciary relationship - Held, court can treat them as one entity - Can pass necessary orders to restore position prior to fraud - Officials of public institution acting beyond authority and with mala fide intent - May not be just and fair to bind institution with their acts and deeds.
