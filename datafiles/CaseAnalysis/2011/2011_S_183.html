Daya Nand v State Of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Aftab Alam, R.M. Lodha

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 19; (2011) 2 SCC 224; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 614; 2011 CRLJ 1157; 2011 (1) JCC 325; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 245; JT 2011 (1) SC 106; 2011 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 541; 2011 (1) OLR 811; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 420; 2011(1) SCALE 143; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 173

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Juvenile Justice Act, 1986 - Juvenile Justice (Care and Protection of Children) Act, 2000 - Plea of juvenility - Appellant was charged u/s. 376 r/w s. 511 of IPC - Appellant raised plea of juvenility - Juvenile Justice Court found that the appellant was a juvenile - On appeal, Sessions Judge relying on s. 2(h) of 1986 Act, which defined a 'juvenile' to mean a boy who has not attained the age of 16 years or a girl who has not attained the age of 18 years held that on the date of occurrence, the appellant was over 16 years of age, he did not come within the definition of 'juvenile' under the 1986 Act - Appellant was not tried before a Juvenile Court, but before the Additional Sessions Judge and was convicted u/s. 376 r/w s. 511 of IPC - Appeal filed thereagainst was dismissed and plea of juvenility was also rejected by HC - Present appeal - Whether appellant entitled to benefit of 2000 Act? - Held, 1986 Act was replaced by the 2000 Act that came into force on April 1, 2001 - 2000 Act defined 'juvenile or child' in s. 2(k) to mean a person who has not completed eighteenth years of age - Hence, in view of the Juvenile Justice Act as it stands after the amendments introduced into it and following the decision in Hari Ram v. State of Rajasthan and Another 2009 INDLAW SC 606 wherein it was held that juvenile who had not completed eighteen years on the date of commission of the offence was also entitled to the benefits of the 2000 Act, the appellant cannot be kept in prison to undergo the sentence imposed by the Additional Sessions Judge and affirmed by the HC - The sentence imposed against the appellant set aside and he directed to be released from prison - Appeal disposed of.
