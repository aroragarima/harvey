Shahzada Nand and Sons v Commissioner of Income Tax, Patiala

Supreme Court of India

12 April 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, N.L. Untwalia

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 210; (1977) 3 SCC 432; AIR 1977 SC 1182; 1977 CTR 246; [1977] 108 ITR 358; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 529

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, s. 36 (1) (ii) - Commission to the employees - Assessee-firm was sole selling agents of OCM in respect of yarn, cloth and blankets manufactured by OCM and for the sales affected by the assessee-firm as such sole selling agents, commission was paid to the assessee-firm by OCM - Since, assessee-firm showed very satisfactory turnover from year to year, OCM started giving to the assessee-firm in addition to the usual commission - Hence, assesee-firm paid the commission to the employees for their performance - Assessee-firm claimed that commission paid as a deductible expenditure - Income Tax Officer disallowed the claim and even Tribunal also took the same view and held that since there was no proof to show that any services for which payment of commission in addition to salary and bonus could be justified, commission could not be said to have been paid for services rendered so as to attract the applicability of s. 36 (1) (ii) of the Act - HC answered question in favour of the Revenue - Whether, certain commission paid by the assessee to two of its employees was an allowable expenditure in computing the profits of the assessee-firm from business u/s. 36 (1) (ii) of the Act - Held, commission paid to an employee should be for services rendered by him and there should be some services rendered by an employee and where commission was paid for the services so rendered, s. 36 (1) (ii) of the Act would apply and the commission to the extent to which it was found reasonable would be an allowable expenditure under that provision - It was not necessary that the commission should be paid under a contractual obligation, it could be purely voluntary - Commission aid to an employee could not be branded as unreasonable merely because the employee had done in the relevant accounting year the same work which he was doing in the earlier years - Hence, sum paid by the assessee-firm by way of commission during the relevant accounting year was reasonable having regard to all the circumstances of the case and it ought to had been allowed as a deductible expenditure u/s. 36 (1) (ii) of the Act - Appeal allowed.
