Satyavir Singh Rathi v State Thr. C.B.I.

Supreme Court of India

02 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.S. Bedi, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 309; (2011) 6 SCC 1; AIR 2011 SC 1748; 2011 CRLJ 2908; JT 2011 (5) SC 533; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 900; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 805; 2011(5) SCALE 339; [2011] 6 S.C.R. 138

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 120-B, 300, 302 and 307 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Encounter case - Conviction - Challenged - Appellant 'S', Assistant Commissioner of Police and the In-Charge of the Inter-State Cell, received information that a criminal would be present at a particular place - Deceased was mistaken for said criminal - Car occupied by deceased was surrounded by the police officials who fired from almost all sides killing deceased persons and causing grievous injuries to one person - On inspection of the car, Inspector recovered a 7.65 mm pistol loaded with 7 live cartridges in the magazine, a misfired cartridge in the breech and two spent cartridge cases of 7.65 mm bore from inside the car - FIR u/ss. 186/353/307 of the IPC and s. 25 of the Arms Act was registered against the occupants of the Car - Subsequently, another FIR was registered against the police personnel (appellants) involved in the shootout for an offence punishable u/ss. 302 r/w s. 34 of the IPC - In the complaint, it was alleged that the police officials had surrounded the car and had fired indiscriminately and without cause, at the occupants killing the two and causing grievous injuries to the third - CBI, on investigation, came to the conclusion that the police party headed by Appellant 'S' and Inspector 'A' had fired on the car without provocation and that FIR, registered on the complaint of Inspector Appellant 'S', was intended to act as a cover-up for the incident and to justify the police action - CBI accordingly found that no shot had been fired from inside the car by deceased, as alleged, and that the claim in this FIR that two police officials, who were a part of the police party, had sustained gun shot injuries as a result of firing from the Car, was false - Charge sheet was filed before the Chief Metropolitan Magistrate - Magistrate took cognizance for the offences punishable u/s. 302/307/201/120-B/34 against 10 members of the police party - Appellants were convicted - On appeal, HC held that the conviction of the appellants u/s. 302/120B of the IPC could not be sustained and they were entitled to acquittal of that charge, but their conviction and sentence u/ss. 302 and 307 of the IPC was liable to be maintained with the aid of s. 34 of the IPC instead of s. 120B of the IPC - Conviction and sentence of Appellant 'S' and Inspector 'A' u/ss. 193, 201/34 and 203/34 of the IPC was maintained - Hence, instant appeal - (A) Defence story - Held, evidence on record revealed that the defence story projected was a false one and the police officials involved having realized almost immediately after the incident that they had made a horrific mistake, immediately set about creating a false defence - Trial Court and the HC have correctly opined on the basis of the overall assessment that the defence version was a concoction and that the prosecution story that it was the unprovoked firing by the appellants which had led to the death of deceased persons, had been proved on record - (B) Whether appellants were entitled to claim the benefit of Exception 3 to s. 300 of the IPC - Held, this Exception pre-supposes that a public servant who causes death, must do so in good faith and in due discharge of his duty as a public servant and without ill-will towards the person whose death was caused - In the instant case, appellants had fired without provocation at the Car killing two innocent persons and injuring one - (C) Common intention - Whether there was common intention on the part of the appellants to commit murder? - It was submitted that the finding of the HC that all the appellants were guilty under Section 302/34 etc. was wrong - Held, HC was, justified in holding that in the view of the facts of the case, it was not necessary to assign a specific role to each individual appellant as the firing at the Car was undoubtedly with a clear intent to annihilate those in it and was resorted to in furtherance of the common intention of all the appellants - Appellants were liable to conviction u/ss. 302/34 etc. of the IPC - (D) Appellants referred to s. 140 of the Delhi Police Act, 1978 to contend that as cognizance in the matter had been taken more than three months from the date of the incident, the prosecution itself was barred - Held, in the instant case, it could not, be claimed that a case of murder would fall within the expression 'colour of duty' - There was no connection between the act of the appellants and the allegations against them - S. 140 of the Delhi Police Act would, therefore, have absolutely no relevance in instant case - No fault with the judgments of the Trial Court as well as the HC - Appeals dismissed.
