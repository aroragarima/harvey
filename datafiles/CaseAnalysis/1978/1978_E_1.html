Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal v Edward Keventer Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

24 August 1978

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1978 Indlaw SC 402; (1979) 1 SCC 19; AIR 1978 SC 1586; 1978 (7) CTR 164; [1978] 115 ITR 149; 1978 UJ 682

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10(4 A) - Whether Tribunal was right in holding that disallowance of amounts in question out of remuneration and commission paid to four directors of company for assessment years by resort to s. 10(4 A) of Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, was unjustified? - Four directors were attending to business of assessee and, in fact, this was clearly borne out from material produced on behalf of assessee in proceedings before tax authorities - Held, no fault can be found with reasoning in taking view against revenue on question of disallowance of aforesaid amounts out of remuneration and commission paid to four directors u/s. 10(4 A) - Further held, despite huge annual turnover and large number of centres to look after and supervise, assessee incurred a total expenditure of not more than Rs. 5,000 per month at head office and this expenditure of about Rs. 5,000 per month was inclusive of remuneration and commission paid to four directors - It is impossible to say in circumstances that remuneration and commission paid to four directors was excessive or unreasonable - High Court was right in answering question in favour of assessee - Appeal dismissed.
