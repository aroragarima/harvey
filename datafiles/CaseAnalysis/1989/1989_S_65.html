Kalyan Singh, London Trained Cutter, Johri Bazar, Jaipur v Smt. Chhoti and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 December 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, A.M. Ahmadi, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 141; (1990) 1 SCC 266; AIR 1990 SC 396; 1990 ALR 155; JT 1989 (4) SC 439; 1990 RD 198; 1989(2) SCALE 1238; [1989] Supp2 S.C.R. 356

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Criminal

Keywords: Mesne Profits, Declaratory Suit

Summary: Civil - Will - Sale deed - Serious doubts were cast on the genuineness of the will as the same was not produced when required - Whether it can be concluded that the will was genuine - Held, no - Circumstances surrounding the will are unnatural - Will not produced even when it was required by various authorities - Trustworthy and unimpeachable evidence should be produced before the court to establish genuineness and authenticity of the will - Lack of satisfactory circumstances to prove authenticity of the will - Appeal allowed.
