Shiv Kumar and Others v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 May 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1469; (1994) 4 SCC 445; (1994) SCC (L&S) 904; 1994 (2) CLR 408; 1994 (69) FLR 250; JT 1994 (4) SC 162; 1995 (1) LLJ 1162; 1994 (2) LLN 425; 1994(2) SCALE 839; 1994 (2) SLJ 151; 1994 (2) SLR 704; 1994 (2) UJ 431

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25-N - Whether there is material on record to show that workmen concerned had been served with copies of application as required by s. 25-N r/w r. 76-A of Industrial Rules, 1957? - R. 76-A(2) requires that application shall be made in triplication and copies of same shall be served by employer on workmen concerned and 'proof to that effect shall also be submitted by employer along with application' - Application here has not mentioned anything about 'proof' of service to workmen concerned - Statement in counter-affidavit that proof of service had been submitted to specified authority is not satisfactory - Held, permission granted to retrench 21 appellant-workmen of respondent-management cannot be said to be in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
