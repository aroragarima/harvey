Banarasi Devi v Income Tax Officer

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Subba Rao, S.M. Sikri, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1964 SC 1742; [1964] 53 ITR 100; 1964 (2) SCJ 258; [1964] 7 S.C.R. 539

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Municipalities -Local Govts - Income tax (Amendment) Act, 1959, ss. 4 and 34 - General Clauses Act, s. 27 - Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, ss. 553, 554 and 555 - (A) Whether s. 4 applies to cases where notice is issued in time but served out of time?- Held, s. 4 of Act saves notice, re-assessment made in pursuance there of even in those case where although notice had been served beyond period of 8 years prescribed by s. 34 of Income Tax Act, 1922, it had been issued within that period - If a statute professes to impose a charge intention to impose a charge on subject must be shown by clear and unambiguous language - This rule is applied to s. 4 too - If words of section are clear and excludes class of cases where notices were sent before 8 years from date of assessment but served thereafter Court has to give them that meaning - (B) What does word issued means in s. 4 of Act? - Further held, if word 'issued' means 'sent', then u/s. 34(1)(a) of Act, a notice could be served only within 8 years from relevant assessment year - Though Act does not provide any period for sending of notice - So, 'sent' cannot be used - Before amendment of s. 34(1)(a) equated meaning served by court - Expressions 'issued' and 'served' are used as inter-changeable terms both in dictionaries and in other statutes - Dictionary meaning of word 'issue' is Act of sending out, put into circulation, delivery with authority or delivery - S. 27 of General Clauses Act that parliament has used words 'serve', 'give' and 'send' as inter-changeable words - Ss. 553, 554 and 555 of Calcutta Municipal Act, 1951, two expressions 'issued to' or 'served upon' are used as equivalent expressions - Expression 'issued' in s. 4 of Amending Act must be given that meaning which carries out intention of legislature, which is to save validity of a notice issued beyond prescribed time, in preference to that which defeats it - Appeal dismissed.
