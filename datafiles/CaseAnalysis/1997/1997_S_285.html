Tanviben Pankajkumar Divetia v State Of Gujarat

Supreme Court of India

06 May 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

G.N. Ray, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1449; (1997) 7 SCC 156; (1997) SCC (Cr) 1004; AIR 1997 SC 2193; 1997 (2) CCR 57; 1997 (2) Crimes 109; 1997 CRLJ 2535; 1997 (1) GLH 851; 1997 (2) G.L.R. 1346; JT 1997 (5) SC 233; 1997(4) SCALE 33; 1997 (2) SCJ 444; [1997] Supp1 S.C.R. 96; 1997 (4) Supreme 517; 1997 (2) UJ 205

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Ransacked, Third Judge, Surmise

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 ss. 34, 120B and 302 - Murder - Whether High Court was right in convicting the appellant u/s. 302? - Circumstantial evidence not sufficient for conviction of appellant for offence u/ss. 34 and 302 - Decision of court in convicting appellant has been result of suspicious circumstances entering adjudicating through process of Court - Held, conviction and consequential sentence passed against appellant set aside and the appellant acquitted - More the suspicious circumstances, more care and caution are required to be taken otherwise the suspicious circumstances may unwittingly enter the adjudicating thought process of the Court even though the suspicious circumstances had not been clearly established by clinching and reliable evidences - Appeal allowed.
