Anand Swarup Mahesh Kumar v The Commissioner Of Sales Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 September 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, P.N. Bhagwati

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 288; (1980) 4 SCC 451; (1981) SCC (Tax) 1; AIR 1981 SC 440; [1981] 1 S.C.R. 707; 1980 (46) STC 477; 1980 TAXLR 1789; 1980 UJ 921

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Keywords: Trader, Commission Agent, Collected, Market Fee, Madras Prohibition Act, 1937

Summary: U.P. Sales Tax Act, 1948, ss. 3D, 3D(1), 3D(4) and 3F - Madras Prohibition Act, 1937 - Sales Tax - (A) Goods notified u/s. 3-D(1) of 1948 Act - Levy of additional tax on purchase turnover - Provision of s. 3-F overrides s. 3-D(4) - Valdity of demand of additional tax - (B) Whether market fees payable to Commission Agent can be included in turnover of purchases - (C) Commission payable by purchaser to Commission Agent dealer - It includeable in turnover - Held, is valid - Cannot be included in turnover of purchases - Can be included in turnover of purchases - Appeal Partly Allowed.
