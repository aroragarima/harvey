Vinay Tyagi v Irshad Ali @ Deepak and others

Supreme Court of India

13 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 547; (2013) 5 SCC 762; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 5200; 2013 CRLJ 754; JT 2013 (1) SC 97; 2013 (1) OLR 953; 2013 (2) RCR(Criminal) 197; 2012(12) SCALE 343; [2012] 13 S.C.R. 1005

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Power Of The Court

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Explosive Substances Act, 1908 ss. 4, 5 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 120B, 121, 121A, 122, 123 - Arms Act, 1959, s. 25 - Complaint - Investigation - Cognizance - Sustainability -Respondent no.1 was working as an informer of Special Cell of Delhi Police in the year 2000 and was also working in a similar capacity for Intelligence Bureau - On 11-12- 2005, respondent refused to join militant camp in Jammu & Kashmir in order to give information with respect to their activities to Intelligence Bureau - However, on 9-2- 2006, a report was published in newspaper, through SHO, Police Station, with photograph of respondent no.2 seeking help of general public in tracing him - Subsequently, Special Cell of Delhi Police falsely implicated both respondents in a case, FIR No. 10/2006, u/ss. 4 and 5 of the 1908 Act and u/ss. 120B, 121 and 122 IPC r/w s. 25 of the 1959 Act - Aggrieved, brother of accused filed a petition in HC stating that both accused were working as 'informers', and that they had been falsely implicated in case and, inter alia, praying that investigation in relation to FIR No.10/2006 be transferred to CBI - HC called upon CBI to undertake an inquiry into matter and submit a report to HC within four weeks - CBI filed closure report stating that accused persons were working as 'informers' of Special Cell of Delhi Police and Intelligence Bureau Officials and that it was a false case - Respondent no.2 filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC praying that proceedings pending before Trial Court pertaining to FIR No.10/2006, be quashed - HC held that once report was filed by CBI, that agency had to be treated as investigating agency in case and closure report ought to have been considered by Trial Court and remanded case to Trial Court - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether in exercise of its powers u/s. 173 CrPC Trial Court had jurisdiction to ignore any one of reports, where there were two reports by same or different investigating agencies in furtherance of orders of a Court, If so, to what effect - Held, Court of competent jurisdiction was duty bound to consider all reports, entire records and documents submitted therewith by Investigating Agency as its report in terms of s. 173(2) CrPC but it was subjected to exceptions - Once a Report u/s. 173(2) CrPC had been filed, it could only be cancelled, proceeded further or case closed by Court of competent jurisdiction and in accordance with law - Neither Police nor specialised investigating agency had any right to cancel said Report - Further, in instant case, HC had passed no order or direction staying further investigation by Delhi Police or proceedings before Court of competent jurisdiction - Thus, Trial Court had to consider entire record, including both Delhi Police Report filed u/s. 173(2) CrPC as well as Closure Report filed by CBI and documents filed along with these reports - (B) Whether CBI was empowered to conduct 'fresh'/'re-investigation' when cognizance had already been taken by Court of competent jurisdiction on basis of a police report u/s. 173 CrPC - Held, no investigating agency was empowered to conduct a 'fresh', 'de novo' or 're-investigation' in relation to offence for which it had already filed a report in terms of s. 173(2) CrPC - It was only upon orders of higher Courts empowered to pass such orders that aforesaid investigation could be conducted, in which event higher courts would have to pass a specific order with regard to fate of investigation already conducted and report so filed before Trial Court - Thus, CBI itself understood order of Court and conducted only 'further investigation' as was evident from status report filed by CBI before HC - Hence, impugned order of HC was set aside - Order accordingly.
