Â M. S. Sheriff v State of Madras and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1954

Case Analysis

Bench

Vivian Bose, Mehr Chand Mahajan, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Ghulam Hasan, Bijan Kumar Mukherjea

Where Reported

1954 Indlaw SC 29; AIR 1954 SC 397; 1954 CRLJ 1019; 1954 (1) MLJ(SC) 699; 1954 SCJ 458; [1954] 1 S.C.R. 1144

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal- Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1898, ss. 195(3), 476 B, 476, 439 and 561 A - (A) Is it necessary to consider whether there is a class of decrees or sentences in court which are at all open to appeal, before court can apply definition of word Court in s. 195(3) - Held, if there are not, matter ends and there is no right of appeal under s. 475-B - If there are, then court has to see to which court, those appeals will 'ordinarily' lie - (B) Whether 'it is expedient in interests of justice' that an enquiry should be made and a complaint filed under ss. 476 and 476 B ?- Further held, where lower court has scrutinised evidence minutely and disclosed ample material on which a judicial mind could reasonably reach conclusion that there is a matter which requires investigation in a criminal court and that it is expedient in interest of justice to have it enquire into, there is no reason for interfering with lower court's discretion - Court in passing an order under s. 476 should not express any opinion on guilt or innocence of accused - (C) Whether civil proceedings can be stayed running simultaneously with criminal proceedings on same subject matter? - Further held, as between civil and criminal proceedings, criminal matters should be given precedence - Civil suits should be stayed till criminal proceedings have finished - Appeal dismissed.
