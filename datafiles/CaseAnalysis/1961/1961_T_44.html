Poohari Fakir Sadavarthy of Bondilipuram v Commissioner, Hindu Religious and Charitableendowments

Supreme Court of India

22 December 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Raghubar Dayal, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 236; AIR 1963 SC 510; 1962 (2) ALT(SC) 274; [1962] Supp2 S.C.R. 276

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Charity, Religious Endowments Act, 1863, Religious Endowments Act

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Hindu Religious Endowments Act, 1927, s. 9(12) - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 35 - (A) When can a religious institution be considered as a temple? - Held, a religious institution will be a temple if two conditions are satisfied - One is that it is a place of public religious worship and other is that it is dedicated to or is for benefit of, or is used as of right by, Hindu community, or any section thereof, as a place of religious worship - (B) Whether grants given to an institution in personal capacity of head can be consider as grant given to a temple - Further held, it cannot be - Where original grants in favor of an institution are in name of head of institution for purpose of sadavarthy (feeding of travelers and pilgrims) and for his own maintenance and prayer - Though head of institution had build a temple for his private worship, there was no mention of it in various endowments for period, which were for head who ran sadavarthy institution and were always in his name - There was no evidence that Hindu public went to temple for worship as a matter of right - Appeal allowed.
