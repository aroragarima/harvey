State (Delhi Administration) v I. K. Nangia And Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 October 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Sen, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 80; (1980) SCC (Cr) 220; (1980) 1 SCC 258; AIR 1979 SC 1977; 1980 CRLJ 834; 1980 CrLR(SC) 58; 1980 (17) DLT 164; 1980 MLJ 343; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 1016

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection; Criminal; Health & Drug; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Food Adulteration

Summary: . Constitution - Constitution,art.21 - CrPC,1973 - Under-trial prisoners in detention for period longer than the maximum term of sentence if convicted -(A) Whether detention of undertrial prisoners for periods longer than the maximum term for which they could have been sentenced violative of art. 21 of the Constitution of India? - Where certain Undertrial prisoners remained in jail without trial for periods longer than the maximum term for which they could have been sentenced it convicted, and such persons had been in detention for period longer than the maximum without their trial having been commenced, Held,the continued detention was clearly illegal and in violation of their fundamental right u/art. 21 of the Constitution of India - (B) What is the duty of the magistrate and government towards under trial prisoner ? -Held, Magistrate must, before making an order of further remand to judicial custody, point out to the undertrial prisoner of his entitlement to be released on bail - State Government to provide at its own cost a lawyer to the under-trial prisoner with a view to unable him to apply for bail in exercise of his right under proviso (a) to Sub-s (2) of s.167(2) proviso (a) ,CrPC ,1973 - Directions accordingly - Order accordingly
