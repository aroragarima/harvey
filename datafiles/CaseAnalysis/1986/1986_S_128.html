Suraj Pal Sahu v State Of Maharashtra Andors.

Supreme Court of India

25 September 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 785; (1986) 4 SCC 378; (1986) SCC (Cr) 452; AIR 1986 SC 2177; 1987 (2) Bom.C.R. 383; 1987 (1) Crimes 8; 1986 CRLJ 2047; 1986 CrLR(SC) 521; JT 1986 SC 538; 1986(2) SCALE 484; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 837

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Foreign Power

Summary: Criminal - National Security Act, 1980, ss. 3(1) and 3(2) -Constitution Of India, art. 22(5) - Detention order challenged - Accused involved in theft of railway material - (A) Whether when a man has been discharged in a criminal case, grounds in that case can be grounds for preventive detention? - Held, It cannot be said that simply because a man has been discharged in a criminal case, grounds in that case cannot be grounds for preventive detention - But there must be live link between grounds of criminal activities alleged by detaining authority and purpose of detention - This credible chain would be snapped if there was too long and unexplained an interval between offending acts and order of detention - (B) What are factors to be kept in mind in deciding whether person proposed to be detained comes within mischief of Act of 1980 - Held, it has to be borne in mind that having regard to purpose of Act, detaining authority must take into consideration rational, proximate, reasonable past and present and that should be basis for horoscope for future so as to determine whether person proposed to be detained comes within mischief of Act - If person is in detention or is under trial and his conviction is unlikely but his conduct comes within mischief of Act then authority is entitled to take a rational view of matter - Petition dismissed.
