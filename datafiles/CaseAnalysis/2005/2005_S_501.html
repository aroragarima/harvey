Sree Balaji Rice Mill, Bellary v State of Karnataka

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, S.N. Variava, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 254; (2005) 4 SCC 21; AIR 2005 SC 2197; JT 2005 (5) SC 539; 2005(3) SCALE 547; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 12; 2005 (140) STC 267

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Sales Tax

Keywords: Excess Collection Of Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Karnataka Sales Tax Act, 1957, s.22A - Scope, effect of s. 22 A - Assessment order - Penalty - Levy of penalty by means of an order in revision proceedings - Reasonableness - Whether a penalty order u/s.18 A of Act forms a part of an assessment order? - HC held that the Appellate Authority competent to levy a penalty for the first time - Held, non-levy of penalty is itself an illegality caused by a failure to exercise jurisdiction by Assessing Authority and therefore, prejudicial to interests of Revenue - Levy of penalty by means of an order in revision proceedings is reasonable, just and proper and therefore, not liable to be faulted - Appeal dismissed.
