Mahendra Singh Dhantwal v Hindustan Motors Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 May 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Goswami, Hans Raj Khanna, V.R. Krishna Iyer

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 229; (1976) 4 SCC 606; (1977) SCC (L&S) 20; AIR 1976 SC 2062; 1976 (33) FLR 67; 1976 LabIC 1337; 1976 (2) LLJ 259; 1977 (1) SCJ 299; 1976 SLJ 521; [1976] Supp S.C.R. 635

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 133(1)(c) and 226 - Industrial Disputes (Amendment and Miscellaneous Provisions) Act, 1956 - Industrial Disputes (Appellate Tribunal) Act, 1950 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 33, 33A and 33C - Termination simpliciter - Procedure of - Whether if a particular order of termination of service is not on account of misconduct and is merely a termination simpliciter, employer is still required to make an application u/s. 33 of Act? - Held, it is, however, unexceptionable that if an employer passes an order of termination of service in exercise of his right under a contract or in accordance with provision of standing orders and Tribunal finds that order is not on account of any misconduct, question of violation of s. 33 would not arise - Termination simpliciter or automatic termination under standing orders is outside scope of s. 33 of Act - This does not mean that employer has last word - It is also not correct that in case of a complaint u/s. 33 - A Tribunal would be debarred from going into question whether order is one of dismissal for misconduct and not termination simpliciter - Appeal allowed.
