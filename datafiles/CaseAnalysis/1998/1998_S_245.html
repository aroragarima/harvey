Texmaco Limited and Another v State of Andhra Pradesh and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, G.T. Nanavati, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1147; (2000) 1 SCC 763; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 31; 2000 (118) STC 290

Case Digest

Subject: Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956 - Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, 1957, ss. 30-B and 30-C - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 and 142 - Whether order of Supreme Court in Indian Cement v State of A P 1988 Indlaw SC 687 was prospective in nature? - In said case concessions were given to manufacturers of cement within State of Andhra Pradesh - Rate of sales tax was reduced - Notifications were quashed on basis that they impeded free flow of trade between States - Held, attention of learned Judges who delivered judgment and passed said order was not drawn to fact that assessees would have to pay by way of sales tax amounts which they had not, and could not, under provisions of Andhra Pradesh General Sales Tax Act, have collected from their customers - Quashing of notifications should have effect of putting local cement industry and cement industry outside State on a par, it could not place former in a disadvantageous position qua latter - Therefore, it is not just or equitable to permit State to collect concessional amounts - order stand reviewed so that following sentence is added to it - Order accordingly.
