National Insurance Company Ltd v Balakrishnan & Another

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 467; (2013) 1 SCC 731; 2012 (4) ACC 700; 2013 ACJ 199; AIR 2013 SC 473; 2013 (1) ALD(SC) 106; 2013 (96) ALR 204; 2013 (1) AWC 810; JT 2012 (11) SC 260; 2013 (170) PLR 1; 2013 (1) RCR(Civil) 762; 2012(11) SCALE 122; [2012] 12 S.C.R. 565

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance

Keywords: Motor Insurance, Instructions

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Companies Act, 1956 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 140,147,166 - Accident - Injuries - Claim Compensation - Comprehensive Policy - Owner of Car - Liability - Respondent No. 1 met with an accident travelling in car belonging to respondent No. 2 and filed compensation claim petition for an amount of Rs. 20,00,0,,,/- u/ss. 140,147,166 of Act, jointly and severally from appellant as well as company as it was insured with company - Insurer- appellant resisted on ground that claimant suppressed fact that he was managing director of company and even if petition was entertained insurance company could not be held liable to indemnify respondent as appellant was himself owner being Managing Director as such he could not be treated third party - Tribunal holding issue as rash and negligent driving of driver and injuries sustained by injured and liability of insurance company awarded an amount of Rs. 8,63,200 as compensation with 7.5 % interest per annum - Aggrieved appeal was filed before HC, which held that HC treated company to be owner of vehicle and repelled stand that Managing Director was owner, and further held that as he was only an occupant of car insurance company was liable to indemnify owner for claim put forth by victim - Further opined that if no premium was paid to cover owner, insurer was not liable to make good loss but if another person traveled with owner and suffered injuries insurer was liable to pay compensation, and dismissed appeal by insurer - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that assuming he was owner being a signatory in RC book, liability of company was limited upto Rs.2,00,000/- and under no circumstances a non-fare paying passenger would be covered under policy - Whether appellant-insurer was liable to compensate claim of company for having sustained injuries - Held, there was no scintilla of doubt that a 'comprehensive/package policy' would cover liability of insurer for payment of compensation for occupant in a car, there was no cavil that an 'Act Policy' stood on a different footing from a 'Comprehensive/Package Policy' - As circulars had made position very clear and IRDA, which was presently statutory authority, has commanded insurance companies stating that a 'Comprehensive/Package Policy' covered liability, there could not be any dispute in that regard - SC, clarified that earlier pronouncements were rendered in respect of 'Act Policy' which admittedly could not cover a third party risk of an occupant in a car, but, if policy was a 'Comprehensive/Package Policy', liability would be covered - In view of analysis, SC, found it to apposite set aside finding of HC and tribunal as regards liability of insurer and remit matter to tribunal to scrutinize policy in a proper perspective and, if necessary, by taking additional evidence and if conclusion was arrived at that policy in question was a 'Comprehensive/Package Policy', liability would be fastened on insurer, as far as other findings recorded by tribunal and affirmed by HC were concerned, they remained undisturbed - Appeal allowed.
