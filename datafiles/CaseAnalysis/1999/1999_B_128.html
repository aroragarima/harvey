Jyotsana Arvindkumar Shah and Others v Bombay Hospital Trust

Supreme Court of India

22 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Venkataswami, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 933; (1999) 4 SCC 325; 1999 (37) ALR 119; 1999 (3) AWC 2505; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 435; 1999 (3) CPJ 1C; JT 1999 (5) SC 228; 1999 (7) SLT 146; 1999 (1) Supreme 303

Case Digest

Subject: Consumer Protection

Summary: Consumer Protection Act, 1986- Sections 15 and 17-State Commission-Power to set aside ex parte reasoned order passed on merits-Deficiency in service- Medical carelessness and negligence on part of respondent hospital-Absence of respondent-State Commission proceeding ex parte and passing reasoned order on merits awarding compensation with interest-Respondent filing writ petition-High Court dismissing writ petition observing that respondent could make application for setting aside ex parte order if permissible under law-State Commission setting aside ex parte order-Revisions not entertained by National Commission-Validity-State Commission has no jurisdiction under Act to set aside ex parte reasoned order-State Commission influenced by concluding portion of judgment of High Court-But failing to appreciate Hih Court's observation âif permissible under the lawâ-And law not so permitting-Hence order of State Commission not sustainable-Order of State Commission as confirmed by National Commission set aside.
