Radhasoami Satsang v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 November 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 948; (1992) 1 SCC 659; AIR 1992 SC 377; 1991 (100) CTR 267; [1992] 193 ITR 321; JT 1991 (4) SC 313; 1991(2) SCALE 1199; [1991] Supp2 S.C.R. 312; [1992] 60 TAXMAN 248; 1992 (2) TLR 179

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income-tax Act, 1961, ss. 11, 12 and 143 - Is tribunal justified in holding that income derived from a religious institution should be exempted u/ss. 11 and 12 of Act? - Applicability of principle of res judicata - Held, property was subject to legal liability of being used for religious or charitable purpose - Income derived by satsang is entitled to exemption u/ss. 11 and 12 - Principles of res judicata do not apply to income-tax proceedings - Appeals allowed.
