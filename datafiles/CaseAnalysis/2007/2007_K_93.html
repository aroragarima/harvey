Lucknow Development Authority v Krishna Gopal Lahoti and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1071; (2008) 1 SCC 554; AIR 2008 SC 399; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 18; 2008(1) ALL MR 475; 2007 (69) ALR 933; 2007 (6) ALT 53; JT 2007 (12) SC 489; 2007 (4) KLT 1046; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1038; 2007 (8) SCJ 472; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 921

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Madhya Pradesh Town Improvement Trust Act, 1960

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908; Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 54 - Tribunal determined the market value of the land at Rs.6/- per sq. ft. and accordingly compensation was awarded by the impugned Award - Appeal against - Held, where there is no other material it may in appropriate cases be open to the adjudicating Court to make comparison of the prices paid for small plots of land - Necessary deductions/adjustments have to be made while determining the prices - First criterion to be taken into consideration is the market value of the land on the date of the publication of the notification - Sustained the market value fixed but instead of 25% development charges one-third has to be deducted - Appeal partly allowed.
