Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Sugauli Sugar Works Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Srinivasan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1056; (1999) 2 SCC 355; AIR 1999 SC 1144; 1999 (152) CTR 46; [1999] 236 ITR 518; JT 1999 (1) SC 329; 1999(1) SCALE 323; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 400; 1999 (1) SLT 520; [1999] 102 TAXMAN 713; 1999 (2) TLR 358

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 41 - Whether transfer of an entry into the capital reserve is a unilateral act of assessee, who is a debtor to its employees? - Held, no - Relied on CIT v. Rashmi Trading (1975 Indlaw GUJ 52) - Expiry of the period of limitation prescribed under the Limitation Act could not extinguish the debt but it would only prevent the creditor from enforcing the debt - Mere entry in the books of accounts of the debtor made unilaterally without any act on the part of the creditor will not enable the debtor to say that the liability has come to an end - Apart from that, that will not by itself confer any benefit on the debtor as contemplated by the s. 41 - Appeal dismissed.
