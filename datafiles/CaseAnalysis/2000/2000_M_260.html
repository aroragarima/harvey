U. P. State Road Transport Corporation v Mohan Lal Gupta and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 April 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Majmudar, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 3037; (2000) 9 SCC 521; 2000 (4) AWC 3058; 2000 (3) CLR 407; JT 2000 (10) SC 228; 2001 (34) LabIC 2122; 2000 (5) SLR 323

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Termination of services of workman - Whether proper and valid? - Question of award of any minor punishment does not and cannot arise and neither Labour Court could alter punishment of termination of service having regard to its assessment of facts and contentions as regards validity of Inquiry proceedings - Employee has been found guilty of misappropriation and in such an event if appellant -corporation loses its confidence in employee, it will be neither proper nor fair on part of Court to substitute finding and confidence of employer with that of its own in allowing reinstatement - Held, misconduct stands proved - Labour Court cannot exercise its discretion and alter punishment - High Court in error in rejecting writ petition - Termination order as passed by appellant-corporation stands restored - Appeal allowed.
