T. H. Musthaffa v M. P. Varghese and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 745; (1999) 8 SCC 692; AIR 2000 SC 153; JT 1999 (7) SC 427; 1999(6) SCALE 190; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 162

Case Digest

Subject: Election; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Election - Practice & Procedure - Representation of People Act, 1951, ss. 81(3), 83(1), 83(2) and 86(1) - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 39 (2)(b)and 56 (2)(b) - Whether High Court order of refusing recounting was correct? - Held, it is not made clear as to how many votes are liable to be rejected for using wrong instrument by voters for expressing their preference - There is no further indication as to how many of such votes had been polled in favour of respondent 1 so as to materially affect result of election - If pleadings did not contain necessary foundation for raising an appropriate issue, same cannot go to trial - Any amount of evidence in that regard, however excellent same may be, will be futile - Present is not a case where a voter had made use of an instrument which was not supplied to him for purpose of marking his preference, but one where preference was exercised with instrument supplied to him for purpose and, therefore, there is no violation of rules in marking ballot papers - Pleadings do not indicate errors made either with reference to number of ballot papers or table or round in which such mistakes occurred - Except making vague statements, appellant has neither pleaded nor given any testimony through witnesses - Appeal dismissed.
