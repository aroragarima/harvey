Govind Yadav v The New India Insurance Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Surinder Singh Nijjar

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 777; (2011) 10 SCC 683; 2011 (4) ACC 668; 2012 (2) ACC 519; 2012 ACJ 28; 2012 (1) AWC 204; 2011 (4) CPJ(Sc) 111; 2012 (1) CTC 437; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 719; 2012 (165) PLR 705; 2011 (4) RCR(Civil) 817; 2012 (1) RLW 9

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Insurance

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Insurance - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Enhancement of compensation - Appellant suffered grievous injures in an accident due to rash and negligent driving by driver - Due to which his left leg was amputated and suffered 70% disability - Appellant filed claim petition which was contested by respondent (Insurance company) - Tribunal awarded total compensation of Rs.2,56,800/- with interest at the rate of 6% - Appellant not satisfied with the compensation, filed appeal before HC for enhancement of compensation - HC modified the award given by Tribunal and granted the enhancement of Rs.50,000/- - Appellant still not satisfied filed instant appeal - (A) Whether compensation awarded to appellant was just and reasonable or was he entitled to enhanced compensation - Held, an injured person not only be compensated for physical injury and treatment , but also for loss earning and his inability to lead normal life and enjoy amenities - In the instant case, Tribunal and HC overlooked appellants minimum wages paid - Hence, Tribunal and HC gravely erred in not awarding just and reasonable compensation for future treatment - (B) Whether it was permissible to award compensation in instalments or recurring compensation to meet the future medical expenses of the victim - Held , it's difficult to hold for future medical expenses which were required to be incurred by a victim, fresh award should be passed - Court should arrive at a reasonable estimate on the basis of the evidence brought on record - Considering that appellant had to change artificial leg periodically, additional compensation was awarded - It would be just and reasonable to award for loss of amenities and enjoyment of life - Appellant was entitled to total compensation with interest within a period - Impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
