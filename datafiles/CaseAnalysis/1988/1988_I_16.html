Ram Dass v Ishwar Chander and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, R.S. Pathak, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 921; (1988) 3 SCC 131; AIR 1988 SC 1422; JT 1988 (2) SC 426; 1988 (2) PLR 478; 1988 (2) RCJ 133; 1988 (1) RCR(Rent) 625; 1988 (2) RentLR 135; 1988(1) SCALE 1136; [1988] Supp1 S.C.R. 239; 1988 (2) UJ 271

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Rent Control - CPC, 1908 - East Punjab Urban Rent Restriction Act, 1949, ss. 13 and 15(5) - Tenancy - (A) Revision - (1) Powers of High Court as to appraisal - (2) HC taking into consideration subsequent event - (C) Eviction - Claim for additional accommodation - Ground, bona fide requirement - Subsequently acquisition of building in name of wife of one of the landlords - Whether it will tilt the balance in favour of tenant as the order of eviction was supportable on basis of need of other co-owners - Held, in appropriate case HC can reappraise evidence if findings of appellate Court are found to be infirm in law - No interference, as Court can take a cautious cognizance of subsequent events to mould relief - Held, it would not - Appeal dismissed.
