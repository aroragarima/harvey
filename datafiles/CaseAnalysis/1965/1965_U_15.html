Â Sahib Singh Dugal v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, S.M. Sikri, Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 162; AIR 1966 SC 340; 1966 CRLJ 305; 1966 (1) SCJ 221; [1966] 1 S.C.R. 313

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Past Activities

Summary: Criminal - Official Secrets Act, s. 3 - Defence of India Rules, 1962, rr. 30(1)(b) and 30(4) - Petitioners in jail for three months in a pending criminal case - Detention on account of past conduct - (A) Whether a person already in jail for a criminal case can be or ordered to be detained in order to prevent him from acting in a manner prejudicial to public safety ? - Held, when a person was already in jail for an indefinite length of time in connection with a criminal case pending against him it would be impossible for authority to come to conclusion that such a person's detention is necessary in order to prevent him from acting in a manner prejudicial to public safety etc. - (B) Whether order of detention in instant case is malafide? - Further held, fact that it was originally intended to prosecute appellants u/s 3 of Official Secrets Act and when authorities were unable to get sufficient evidence to obtain a conviction they decided to drop criminal proceedings and to order their detention, by itself was not sufficient to lead to inference that action of detaining authority was mala fide - Petition dismissed.
