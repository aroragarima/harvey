Citizens For Democracy v State Of Assam And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N. Venkatachala

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 902; (1995) SCC (Cr) 600; (1995) 3 SCC 743; AIR 1996 SC 2193; 1995 (3) CCR 7; 1996 CRLJ 3247; 1995 JCC 378; JT 1995 (4) SC 475; 1995 (2) MLJ(SC) 66; 1995(3) SCALE 98; 1995 (2) SCJ 308; [1995] 3 S.C.R. 943; 1995 (2) UJ 431

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Tort

Keywords: Freedom Of Movement

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14, 19 and 21 - Handcuffing of prisoners - As a rule it shall be rule that handcuffs or other fetters shall not be forced on a prisoner - Matter came for consideration before Supreme Court - Held, peculiar and special characterstics of each individual prisoner be considered - No handcuffing except under special reasons - Directions in this regard issued by Supreme Court - Petition allowed.
