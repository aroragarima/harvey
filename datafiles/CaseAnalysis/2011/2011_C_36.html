Mrs. Asha Sharma v Chandigarh Administration & Ors

Supreme Court of India

30 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 834; (2011) 10 SCC 86; 2011 (8) MLJ(SC) 705; 2012 (1) RCR(Civil) 25; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 881; 2011 (6) SLR 485

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Government Accommodation, Government Residences (Chandigarh Administration General Pool) Allotment Rules, 1996, the Allotment Rules

Summary: (A) Service - Government Residences (Chandigarh Administration General Pool) Allotment Rules, 1996, r.7 - Allotment of house - Imposition of conditions - Applicability of - Appellant was appointed as State Information Commissioner and she was entitled to Govt. accommodation and salary/ allowances - Appellant had applied to authorities as proceedings for eviction began against her before Estate Officer - Estate Officer by order declared appellant as unauthorized occupant and passed order of eviction - Appellant made appeal before Additional District Judge which was dismissed - Appeal made before HC and Single Judge allowed appeal modified order - Aggrieved by directions issued by Single Judge, administration preferred appeal before Division Bench where directions of Single Judge was stayed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether imposition of condition with respect to allotment of house was in conformity with Rule - Held, r. 7 of the Rules, which dealt with creation of pools of residences, provides for earmarking of houses for specified officers from different branches of State Administration and that houses which was not so earmarked for any particular class of Govt. employees would be allotted to general pool of Administration - Rule and its sub-rules read together did not suffer from vice of arbitrariness, as earmarking of houses was known concept in relation to allotment of houses - Directions attempted to ensure that there should not be more than one earmarked house for same post as per need - Clarification or explanatory direction would also ensure timely vacation of earmarked houses by officers concerned, upon their transfer, promotion or posting to post where they were not entitled to earmarked accommodation - Thus, there was no reason to interfere with imposition of such condition which was in conformity with spirit of Rule - Hence, no new house for any category/post should be earmarked unless house already earmarked for such category/post was vacated and placed in general pool of Administration for allotment in accordance with the Rules - Appeal disposed of.

(B) Service - Government Residences (Chandigarh Administration General Pool) Allotment Rules, 1996, rr.8, 11(1)(e),(f) - Out-of-Turn allotment - Allotment of houses - Discretionary powers - Applicability - Whether Out-of-Turn allotment and allotment of houses was in exercise of discretionary powers of administrator - Held, administrator was given power to add or withdraw houses from any pool for purposes of allotment to any class or category of eligible government employees and could also change classification of houses on recommendation of House Allotment Committee - R. 11 of the Rules deals with Out-of-Turn Allotments, i.e. House Allotment Committee could allot house on Out-of-Turn basis to cases specified - R. 11 of the Rules was very comprehensive rule which deals with specific situations where Out-of-Turn Allotment was permissible - Allotment Rules were intended to control exercise of discretion by authorities concerned in granting out-of-turn allotments - There was some vagueness in r. 11(1)(e) of the Rules, Out-of-Turn Allotments to Govt. employee due to 'functional requirements' of post and that expression was neither explained nor any guidelines was issued in that regard - Criteria provided in guideline for allotments made in public interest u/r. 11(1)(f) of the Rules was quite similar to criteria for determining functional requirements - Thus, category of 'functional requirement' allotment was nothing but category created to allow more and more allotments - In light of rules, absolute restriction on Out-of-Turn Allotments imposed by Single Judge could not be just and fair and would be opposed to statutory provisions of Rules - Therefore, it was unable to sustain such restriction - It was primarily to control exercise of discretionary power as well as to ensure that persons entitled to residential accommodation in general pool were not made to wait unduly for indefinite period - Hence, r. 8 of the Rules was not to allow discretionary allotment but was to provide overall powers of coordination and control to Administrator - Appeal disposed of.
