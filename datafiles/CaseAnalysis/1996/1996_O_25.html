Agricultural and Processed Food Products v Oswal Agro Furane

Supreme Court of India

30 April 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, B.N. Kirpal

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 2864; (1996) 4 SCC 297; 1996 (4) AD(SC) 516; AIR 1996 SC 1947; 1996 (63) DLT 636; 1996 (65) ECR 5; 1996 (85) E.L.T. 3; JT 1996 (5) SC 48; 1996(4) SCALE 109; [1996] Supp2 S.C.R. 73; 1996 (4) Supreme 386

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Warehouse, Registration and Licensing of Industrial Undertakings Rules, 1952, Bombay General Act, 1904

Summary: Imports and Exports (Control) Act, 1947, s. 3 - Export (Control) Order (1988), cls. 3, 4 and 15(j) - Non-basmati rice - Export at price less than fixed by authority - Allowed under conditional interim order of court - Exporter bound under order to make a good difference between actual sell price and price fixed by authority if it is found that exporter was not entitled to export non-basmati rice without authorisation - Exporter taking benefit of court order - Held, plea that he is not liable to pay difference in prices not available to exporter - Fixing minimum price to export by authority - Not illegal or without authority.
