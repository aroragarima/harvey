Sarguja Transport Service v State Transport Appellate Tribunal, M.P., Gwalior

Supreme Court of India

12 November 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

E.S. Venkataramiah, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 238; (1987) 1 SCC 5; (1987) SCC (Cr) 19; AIR 1987 SC 88; 1987 CRLR 245; 1987 CrLR(SC) 245; JT 1986 SC 808; 1986(2) SCALE 757; [1987] 1 S.C.R. 200; 1987 (1) UJ 28

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ground Of Res Judicata

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, O.23 r.1 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Maintainability of fresh petition u/art. 226 in respect of same cause of action - When previous petition u/art. 226 withdrawn without taking permission to institute a fresh petition - Held, not maintainable and as rule of public policy u/r.1 of O.23 of CPC, 1908 applies to such case except for writ of habeas corpus - Petition dismissed.
