Abhyudya Sanstha v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 343; (2011) 6 SCC 145; AIR 2011 SC 2353; 2011(3) ALL MR 969; 2011 (3) ESC 488; JT 2011 (6) SC 195; 2011(6) SCALE 251; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 611; 2011 (3) SLR 714; 2011 (3) UPLBEC 2392

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - National Council for Teacher Education Act, 1993 - National Council for Teacher Education (Recognition, Norms and Procedure) Regulations, 2007 - Appellant institutions submitted applications in 2006 and 2007 for grant of recognition for starting D.Ed course - Appellants were informed that their cases would not be processed in view of the recommendations made by the State Government that there was no requirement of trained teachers in the State - Meanwhile, respondent Nos.6 and 7 filed writ petition questioning the exercise undertaken by Western Regional Committee for grant of recognition to over 290 institutions - They alleged that recognition was granted in total disregard of the provisions of the Act and the Regulations and that the views of the State Government were completely ignored - HC quashed the recognitions granted by Western Regional Committee - Although, the appellants were not at all affected by the order of the HC, they filed special leave petitions and prayed for setting aside orders passed by the HC - SC entertained the special leave petitions along with large number of other similar cases filed by those who had been granted recognition by Western Regional Committee, issued notices and passed order of status quo - Interim orders were also passed directing the State Government to allot students to the appellants for D. Ed course - (A) Whether appellants were entitled to question the order passed by the HC whereby recognition granted to over 290 institutions was cancelled? - Held, appellants deserve to be non suited because they have not approached the Court with clean hands - Very fact that each of the appellants had submitted application in terms of regn. 7 and made itself available for inspection by the team constituted by Western Regional Committee showed that they were fully aware of the fact that they could get recognition only after fulfilling the conditions specified in the Act and the Regulations and that Western Regional Committee had not granted recognition to them - Notwithstanding this, they made bold statement that they had been granted recognition by the competent authority and thereby succeeded in persuading SC to entertain the special leave petitions and pass interim orders - Appellants were not entitled to relief u/art. 136 of the Constitution - (B) Whether the Court should direct regularisation of the admission of the students, who were allotted to the appellants by the State Government etc. pursuant to the directions given by SC - Held, although, in the absence of cogent material, it was not possible to record a finding that the students were party to the patently wrong and misleading statement made by the appellants, the Court could not overlook the fact that none of the appellants was granted recognition by Western Regional Committee and in view of the prohibition contained in s. 17A of the Act r/w regn. 8(12), the appellants could not have admitted any student - There was no valid ground to confer legitimacy upon the admission made by the appellants in a clandestine manner - It was not proper to issue direction for regularising the admissions made by the appellants on the strength of the interim orders passed by SC - Appeals dismissed.
