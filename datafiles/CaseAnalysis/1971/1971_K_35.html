Commissioner of Income Tax, A. P v Kotrika Venkataswamy and Sons

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 325; (1972) 4 SCC 84; [1971] 79 ITR 499; 1972 (1) MLJ 24; 1972 (1) MLJ(SC) 24; 1971 UJ 163

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss.28(1)(c), 66(1), 66(2) - Inflation - Additional income - Penalty - Failure of concealment - Correctness of decision - Assessee declared in his return for A/Y 1943-44 an income of Rs.34,560 - On examination of accounts Income-tax Officer found that there was inflation in purchase, spurious cash credits, diversion of sales, and bogus speculation losses were claimed - Income-tax Officer made additions under four heads of different items of income and brought to tax an income of Rs.1,43,433 - Order passed by Income-tax Officer was substantially confirmed in appeal by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and by Tribunal - Income-tax Officer also commenced a proceeding u/s. 28(1)(c) of Act and imposed a penalty of Rs. 24,500 upon assessee - On appeals Appellate Assistant Commissioner confirmed order passed by Income-tax Officer - Whether Appellate Tribunal justified in concluding that failure of concealment of income in respect of additions by department - Held, there was no suppression of sales on facts disclosed, no case could be referred to HC u/s. 66(1) seeking to upset that conclusion - Question of law arose which would justify the Tribunal in making a reference u/s.66 (1)of Act to the HC for calling for a statement of case from the Tribunal - No such question was included in the application for reference u/s. 66(1) - Appellant could not ask HC to call for a statement of case on a question on which the Tribunal was not asked to submit a statement - U/s. 66(2) of Act, HC may call for a statement of case if the HC was not satisfied about correctness of decision of Tribunal refusing to state a case to the HC - HC cannot obviously be satisfied that t decision of Tribunal in not submitting a statement on a question was incorrect when Tribunal was never asked to submit a statement of case on that question - Appeal dismissed.
