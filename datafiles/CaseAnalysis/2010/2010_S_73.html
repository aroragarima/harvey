Maharashtra University of Health Sciences and Others v Satchikitsa Prasarak Mandal and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 February 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 150; (2010) 3 SCC 786; AIR 2010 SC 1325; 2010 (2) Bom.C.R. 783; 2010 (124) FLR 1082; JT 2010 (2) SC 397; 2010 (4) MLJ(SC) 148; 2010(2) SCALE 672; [2010] 3 S.C.R. 91; 2010 (2) SLR 482

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Constitution; Service

Summary: (A) Constitution - Administrative - Service - Maharashtra University of Health Sciences Act, 1998, ss. 2(53) and 53 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 468, 471, 354, 509, 506 r/w. s. 34 - Jurisdiction - Termination - Respondents no. 5 and 6 were appointed lecturers in the college run by respondent no. 1 but their appointments were not approved however they continued to work as lecturers in said college - Services of the respondents no. 5 and 6 were terminated by the said college - Respondent no. 6 filed complaint against respondents no. 1, 2 and 4 before Grievance Committee alleging ill-treatment and sexual harassment which she and other lady lecturers and employees of the college including the respondent no. 5 were subjected to by the authorities of the said college - Grievance Committee directed respondents no. 1 and 2 to take steps against respondents no. 3 and 4 with a direction to suspend them and it was also directed that the respondent no. 5 may be reinstated - Grievance Committee also directed that approval granted in respect of the service of respondents no. 3 and 4 (permanent teachers) be frozen - Respondent college refused to comply with the directions issued by University contending that appellant-University does not have the power to freeze the approval of appointment of permanent teachers and asked appellant to withdraw its communication - On appeal, HC partly allowed the petition on the ground that Grievance Committee constituted u/s. 53 of the said Act, has no jurisdiction to take cognizance of any complaint filed by the respondents no. 5 and 6, as they are not approved teachers of the respondent college- Hence, present appeal - Whether HC was in error in deciding that the Grievance Committee constituted u/s. 53 of the said Act, has no jurisdiction to take cognizance of any complaint filed by the respondents no. 5 and 6, as they are not approved teachers of the respondent college? - Held, purpose of setting up the Grievance Committee u/s. 53 of the Act is to provide an effective grievance redressal forum to teachers and other employees - Any interpretation of 'teachers' u/s. 2(35) of the Act which denies the persons covered u/s. 2(35) of the Act an access to the said forum completely nullifies the dominant purpose of creating such a forum - By creating such a forum the University virtually exercised its authority and jurisdiction as a loco-parentis over teachers-both approved and unapproved and who are working in various colleges affiliated with it - Idea is to give such teachers and employees a protection against any kind of harassment which they might receive in their work place - Hence, HC erred by holding that the Grievance Committee has no jurisdiction to entertain the complaints made by respondents no. 5 and 6 since they are not approved teachers - HC's order set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Constitution - Administrative - Service - Maharashtra University of Health Sciences Act, 1998, ss. 2(53) and 53 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 509 - Principle of 'ejusdem generis'- Where there is statutory indication to the contrary the definition of teacher u/s. 2(35) cannot be read on the basis of ejusdem generis nor can the definition be confined to only approved teachers - That is against the very essence of the doctrine of ejusdem generis - Purpose of this doctrine is to reconcile any incompatibility between specific and general words so that all words in a Statute can be given effect and no word becomes superfluous - Appeal allowed
