Â Lachhman Das On Behalf of Firm Tilak Ram Ram Bux v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 April 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 394; AIR 1963 SC 222; [1963] 2 S.C.R. 353

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Family & Personal

Keywords: Cash Credit, Rajasthan Public Demands Recovery Act, 1952, Co-operative Societies Act, Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Taxation on Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Taxation of Income (Investigation Commission) Act, 1947, Patiala Recovery of State Dues Act, State Reorganisation Act

Summary: Civil Procedure - International - Covenant d/- 5-5-1948 for establishment of Pepsu Union, arts. VI and X - Constitution Of India, 1950 arts. 363, 362, 19(1)(f), 19(1)(g), 32, 226 and 14 - Patiala Recovery of State Dues Act, 2002 Bk - (A) Whether when new State came into existence Rulers have any authority to enter into any Supplementary Covenant ? - Held, no -It is prohibited - under covenant there was a complete divestiture of all sovereign rights of Rulers - Raj Pramukh had power under that article 'to make and promulgate Ordinances for peace and good Govt. of Union or any part thereof - Under art. VI, all powers of Rulers are to vest in Union, and even if whole of legislative power is not exercisable by Raj Pramukh by reason of art. X, it is in Union that residue of power must be held to be lodged and not with Rulers - (B) Whether dispute arising out of any provision of Covenant can be taken up by Civil courts? - Further held, Civil court cannot u/art. 363 of Constitution - But provisions excluding jurisdiction of Civil courts in such cases do not affect jurisdiction of either High Court u/art. 226 or of Supreme Court u/art. 32 or art. 136 to interfere when grounds, therefore, are established - (C) Whether in setting up separate authorities for determination of disputes and in prescribing a special procedure to be followed by them or recovery of dues by summary process, Patiala Recovery of State Dues Act infringe art. 14 of Constitution? - Further held, it does not - While art. 14 prohibits discriminatory legislation directed against one individual or class of individuals, it does not forbid reasonable classification, and for this purpose even one person or group of persons can be a class - A law which provides for State funds being advanced to customers through State Bank can also provide for its being recovered in same manner as revenue - (D) Whether Patiala Recovery of State Dues Act, 2002 Bk and rules made thereunder can be attacked as contravening art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution? - Further held, no because impugned enactments do not trench either directly or indirectly on right of a person to carry on trade or business - A law with respect of recovery of debts is not one with respect to carrying on of trade or business, though debtor might be a trader - Reasonable restrictions has to obey - But when operation of a law is attracted by reason of a contract, which a person is free to enter into at hisown will and choice, it may be said that inhibition u/art. 19 has no application, parties being left to their rights and remedies under contract - They are reasonable and do not violate any rules of natural justice, so, not violative of art. 19(1)(f) - It is a general law applicable to realization of all revenue due to State, dues to Bank being expressly included in definition of 'State dues' in s. 3(1) of Act, and it is of pattern usually adopted in Revenue Laws - Appeals and petitions dismissed.
