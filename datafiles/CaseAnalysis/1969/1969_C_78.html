V.D. M. R. M. M. R. M. Muthiah Chettiar v Commissioner of Income Tax, Madras

Supreme Court of India

14 February 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, V. Ramaswami

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 239; (1969) 1 SCC 675; AIR 1970 SC 10; [1969] 74 ITR 183; 1970 (1) MLJ(SC) 16; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 715

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act,1922, s 34 - Firm - Taxable - Re-assessment - Inclusion of share - 'M' for the sake of brevity constituted a Hindu undivided family owned a 3/5th share - Firm was assessed under 1922 Act in status of a firm resident within taxable territories 'M' separated from family taking his 1/5th share in Firm - Three sons of 'M' took in equal shares remaining 2/5th share - Assessee's share of profit and remittances from Income-tax officer assessee had a remittance of Rs.6,188-12-0 from Firm - 'M' was assessed in respect of his share in income of firm and from other sources - Income-tax Officer issued notices of reassessment to 'M' u/s. 34(1) (a) of 1922 Act for three years - Income-tax Officer assumed that income of sons of 'M' should included u/s.16(3)(a)(ii) of Act had escaped assessment - Tribunal contended by 'M' that he had fully and truly disclosed all particulars required to disclose in returns of his income for three years - Whether re-assessment made on assessee u/s.34 of 1922 Act was valid for three years - Held, Company and local authority and of every firm and other association of persons or partners of firm or members of association individually was charged to tax for that year and subject to provisions of 1922 Act - Rules imposed no obligation upon assessee to disclose to Income-tax Officer relating to income of any other person by law taxable in his hands assessee was bound to disclose - Order of re-assessment was made well within four years from date of last day of year of assessment - Order passed by HC in so far as it relates to years was set aside and answer in negative was recorded - Answer recorded by HC was confirmed - Order accordingly.
