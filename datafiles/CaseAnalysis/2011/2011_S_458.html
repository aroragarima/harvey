Sudhir Kumar Consul v Allahabad Bank

Supreme Court of India

21 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, D.K. Jain

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 119; (2011) 3 SCC 486; 2011 (129) FLR 141; JT 2011 (2) SC 418; 2011 (2) LLJ 199; 2011(2) SCALE 661; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 1119; 2011 (2) SLR 312; 2011 (2) UPLBEC 1091

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Allahabad Bank Officers Service Regulations, 1979, Allahabad Bank Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme, 2000, Allahabad Bank Employees (Pension) Regulations, 1995, Allahabad Bank Employees (Pension) Regulation, 1993, Allahabad Bank Employees Pension Scheme, 1890

Summary: Service - Allahabad Bank Employees Pension Scheme, 1890 - Allahabad Bank Officers Service Regulations, 1979, regn. 46 - Allahabad Bank Employees Voluntary Retirement Scheme, 2000 - Pension in lieu of gratuity - Entitlement - Appellant was appointed as a Clerk in the respondent bank on 21-02-1976 - Appellant was promoted to the post of JMG-Scale-I Officer Grade on 02-05-1983 and services of the appellant, after promotion, were governed by the 1979 Regulations - Pursuant to a Tripartite Settlement, respondent formulated a draft/proposed Allahabad Bank Employees (Pension) Regulation, 1993 - Draft/proposed 1993 Regulations provided the option to the employees, who were on the rolls of the Bank as on 31-10-1993, to opt for pension as per the 1890 Pension Scheme plus Contributory Provident Fund (CPF) - Appellant claimed pension under the 1890 Pension Scheme in terms of the draft/proposed 1993 Regulations on 30-11-1994 - Subsequently, on 29-09-1995, the respondent formally adopted the Allahabad Bank Employees (Pension) Regulation, 1995, pursuant to another Tripartite Settlement - 1995 Regulations superseded the draft/proposed 1993 Regulations by further extending the benefit under the draft/proposed 1993 Regulations to the employees who were on the rolls of Bank as on 29-09-1995 to opt for pension as per the 1890 Pension Scheme plus CPF - Further, the 1995 Regulations, in express terms, had validated the earlier options exercised by the employees in accordance with the draft/proposed 1993 Regulations - Appellant applied for the voluntary retirement pursuant to the 2000 Voluntary Retirement Scheme, which was accepted and the appellant stood relieved from the services of the respondent Bank - After retirement, the appellant was offered gratuity under the Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 by the respondent, which the appellant declined to accept - Appellant made a request to the competent authority for sanction of pension in lieu of gratuity, but his request was rejected by the respondent as not maintainable on the ground that an officer employed or appointed after 01-07-1079 was ineligible for pension under the 1890 Pension Scheme in view of regn. 46 of the 1979 Regulations - Being aggrieved, the appellant approached the HC by filing a writ petition and the same was partly allowed directing the respondent to pay gratuity to the appellant as per regn. 46(2) of the 1979 Regulations - Appellant, aggrieved by the Judgment and Order of the HC in Writ Petition, filed a Review Application, which was rejected subsequently - Hence the instant appeal - (A) Whether the appellant was eligible and entitled for the pensionary benefits under the 1890 Pension Scheme in terms of the 1979 Regulations - Held, appellant was not entitled to claim pensionary benefit in view of regn. 46 (1) of the 1979 Regulations - Regn. 46 (1) provided pensionary benefit under existing supplementary pension Scheme in lieu of gratuity only to those officers who were officers on the appointed date i.e. the officers who were appointed on or before 01-07-1979 - Further, Provision 3 of the 1890 Pension Scheme stipulated that the officers who were recruited or promoted after 01-07-1979, i.e. the date of implementation of the 1979 Regulations, were not entitled for pension as per the said Regulations - Appellant was working with the respondent as a Clerk on 01-07-1979 and was promoted as an officer only in 1983 - Therefore, the appellant was not eligible to claim any benefit under the 1890 Pension Scheme - (B) Whether the stipulation in regn. 46(1) of the 1979 Regulations was constitutionally valid - Held, regn. 46 (1) of 1979 Regulations laid down a reasonable criteria for differentiation between the officers appointed prior to or on 01-07-1979 and after the said date, hence the said regn. 46 (1) was in consonance with the art. 14 of the Constitution - Further, fixing of the cut-off date for granting retirement benefits such as gratuity or pension under the different schemes incorporated in the subordinate legislation, thereby, creating two distinct and separate classes of employees was well within the ambit of art. 14 of the Constitution - Differential treatment of two sets of officers appointed prior to the notified date would not offend art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal dismissed.
