Smt. Bhagwan Kaur v Shri Maharaj Krishan Sharma and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 October 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

Hans Raj Khanna, Y.V. Chandrachud

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 459; (1973) 4 SCC 46; (1973) SCC (Cr) 687; AIR 1973 SC 1346; 1973 CRLJ 1143; 1974 (2) ILR(Del) 10; [1973] 2 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - (A) Appeal u/art. 136 - Reappraisal of evidence - (B) Evidence Act, 1872, s. 45 - Expert evidence - Evidence of handwriting expert - Frail character and its fallibilities have been quite often noticed - Duty of Court - Held, Supreme Court in an appeal u/art. 136 does not normally reappraise evidence unless it finds some glaring infirmity in judgment of High Court as might have resulted in miscarriage of justice - Court should be wary to give too much weight to evidence of handwriting expert and conclusions based upon mere comparison of handwriting must at best be indecisive and yield to positive evidence in case - Appeal dismissed.
