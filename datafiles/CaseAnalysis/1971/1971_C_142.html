Commissioner of Wealth Tax, Bihar and Orissa v Kripashankar Dayashanker Worah

Supreme Court of India

29 July 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 379; (1971) 2 SCC 570; AIR 1971 SC 2463; [1971] 81 ITR 763; [1971] S.C.R. 968; [1971] Supp S.C.R. 968

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Wealth Tax Act, 1957, s. 21(1) - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 41(1) - (A) Wealth Tax - (1) Provision of s. 21(1) of 1957 Act - Assets held by a trustee - Assessablity to wealthtax - (2) Provision of s. 21(4) of 1957 Act - Shares of beneficiaries under a trust whether indeterminate - Settlor creating trust and his wife are alive, they have a right to be maintained out of the income of the trust properties and a right of residence in the house situate in that property - (B) Interpretation of statutes - Taxing Statute - A taxing provision must receive a strict construction at the hands of Courts and if there is any ambiguity, the benefit of that ambiguity must go to the assessee - Held, a trustee holding assets for benefits of beneficiaries under a trust-deed is assessable to wealth tax under the provision - Settlor's minor sons have a right to be maintained and educated, the shares of the beneficiaries are indeterminate - But if the intention of the legislature is clear and beyond doubt then fact that provision could have been more artistically drafted cannot be a ground to treat any part of a provision as otiose -

Appeals Allowed.
