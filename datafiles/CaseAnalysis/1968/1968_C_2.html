C. R. Nagappa v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 268; AIR 1969 SC 888; [1969] 73 ITR 626; [1969] 1 S.C.R. 979

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Association Of Persons, Guardian, Assessment Year, Charge Of Income Tax

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 64(v) and 161(2) - Whether income under deeds of trust be included in total income of assessee where the assessments on the minor beneficiaries has been made for the same assessment year and whether application of s. 161(2) bars application of s. 64(v) while computing total income of the assessee - Assets settled upon trustees for the benefit of minor children, the income from the assets intended for their benefit is, by clause (v) of s. 64, liable to be included in the income of the assessee - Held, the assessment of minor beneficiaries will not affect the validity of the inclusion of the trust income in the assessment made u/s. 64(v) - No doubt that all the assessments made against the minor beneficiaries will be annulled and tax, if any, recovered will be refunded - Appeal dismissed.
