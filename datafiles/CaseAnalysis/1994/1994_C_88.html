Bhanabhai Khalpabhai v Collector Of Customs

Supreme Court of India

08 March 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

N.P. Singh, K. Jayachandra Reddy

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1609; (1994) Supp2 SCC 143; (1994) SCC (Cr) 882; 1994 (2) CCR 417; 1994 (1) Crimes 940; 1996 (62) ECR 500; 1994 (71) E.L.T. 3; JT 1994 (2) SC 591; 1994 (1) RLT 284; 1994(2) SCALE 238; [1994] 2 S.C.R. 348

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Specified Goods

Summary: Criminal - Customs - Constitution - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 108, 124, 135(1)(a) and 138 A - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 20 - Notification No. C S R 37, D/- 03-01-1969 - Notification No. C S R 38, D/- 03-01-1969 - (A) Whether statement of accused recorded by Customs Officer u/s. 108 of Act before any complaint or first information had been lodged against him, can be used against such accused when he was charged for having committed an offence u/s. 135(1)(a) of Act? - When Customs Officer recorded statement of accused he was not formally accused of any offence and as such statement recorded by office of was not hit by art. 20(3) of Constitution - (B) Whether on basis of recovery, appellant can be held to be guilty for an offence u/s. 135(1)(a) of Act? - Held, in view of s. 138-A, once a presumption is raised about a culpable mental state on part of accused, accused has to prove as a defence that no such mental state with respect to act charged, did exist - Prosecution has established that appellant was concerned in an attempt at evasion of duty - Appeal dismissed.
