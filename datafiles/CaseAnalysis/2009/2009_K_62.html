Divisional Manager, Rajasthan S.R.T.C. v Kamruddin

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 767; (2009) 7 SCC 552; AIR 2009 SC 2528; 2009 (2) CLR 659; 2009 (3) ESC 468; 2009 (121) FLR 979; 2009 (3) LLJ 591; 2009(8) SCALE 182; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1180; 2009 (4) SLR 177

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Re Employment

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 11A - Misconduct - Termination - Respondent a bus conductor - Misconduct as to non-issuance of tickets to passenger; misconduct committed for five times and warning was served on one occasion - Disciplinary enquiry, suspension and finally termination - Labour Court held punishment to be disproportionate and ordered reinstatement - High Court in turn dismissed appeal filed thereagainst - What is the extent of interference with the quantum of punishment imposed by an employer on a delinquent employee, by the LC in exercise of its power u/s. 11A of ID Act? - Held, LC has power to interfere but it has to be exercised judiciously - (A) Whether such power had been exercised judiciously? - Held, No - Misconduct proved; conduct of domestic enquiry undisputedly was fair and without violation of principles of natural justice; termination was ordered while period of probation was not over; performance during the period was unsatisfactory - Impugned judgement unsustainable, set aside - Appeal allowed.
