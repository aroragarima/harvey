Sampat Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

07 January 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

G.K. Mitter, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 266; (1969) 1 SCC 367; AIR 1969 SC 956; 1969 CRLJ 1480; [1969] 3 S.C.R. 228

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 148, 149, 302, 304, 323 and 324 - Private Defence - Long standing disputes existing between families of appellant and deceased over money matters - On day of incident, quarrel took place between deceased and appellant's brother - Appellant found deceased sitting on his brothers chest and his brother struggling beneath deceased to set himself free - Appellant in an attempt to save his brother inflicted injury to deceased with a sharp edged weapon - Whether appellant's conviction is not justified in view of fact that he exercised right of private defence to save his brother? - Held, no - Appellant exceeded his right of private defence - Injury inflicted sufficient in ordinary course of nature to cause death - Sufficient other evidence to warrant conclusion that the right of private defence had been exceeded - Appeal dismissed.
