Durga Prasanna Tripathy v Arundhati Tripathy

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Ruma Pal

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 486; (2005) 7 SCC 353; AIR 2005 SC 3297; 2005 (61) ALR 173; 2005 (4) AWC 3649; 2006 (1) Bom.C.R. 386; 2006 (1) G.L.R. 24; 2005 (4) JhrCR(SC) 42; JT 2005 (7) SC 596; 2005 (3) RCR(Civil) 819; 2005 (4) RLW 2558; 2005(6) SCALE 657; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 833

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Family & Personal - Hindu Marriage Act, 1955, s. 13(1) - Divorce on ground of cruelty and desertion - Family Court granting decree for divorce - High Court reversed order - Appealed herein - Held, Family Court, on examination of evidence on record, and having observed demeanor of witnesses concluded that appellant had proved that respondent is not only cruel but also deserted him since more than 7 years - There has been an irretrievable breakdown of marriage - Appeal allowed.
