Delta International Limited v Shyam Sundar Ganeriwala and Another

Supreme Court of India

09 April 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, G.B. Pattanaik

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1559; (1999) 4 SCC 545; AIR 1999 SC 2607; JT 1999 (3) SC 1; 1999 (3) MLJ(SC) 70; [1999] 2 S.C.R. 541

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Fixtures

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of Property Act, 1882, s. 105 - West Bengal Premises Tenancy Act, 1956, s. 14 - Terms of deed state that agreement was license and should not be construed as lease - (A) Parties conscious that sub-lease is illegal - Tenant could not create a sub-tenancy without prior written consent of landlord - No contention raised by defendants that licence deed is a camouflage to circumvent provisions of rent law - (B) Whether document is lease or licence? - Held, (A) Real test is to find out 'the intention of parties' - Intention of parties is meaning of words they have used - (B) In cases of exclusive possession, line between a lease and a licence is very thin - Agreement was deed of 'leave and licence' and not 'lease' - Appeal allowed.
