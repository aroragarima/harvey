Kashmir Singh v Harnam Singh and Another

Supreme Court of India

03 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 316; (2008) 12 SCC 796; AIR 2008 SC 1749; 2008 (3) AWC 2323; JT 2008 (4) SC 24; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 896; 2008 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1170; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 688; 2009 (107) RD 168B; 2008(4) SCALE 300; [2008] 3 S.C.R. 763; 2008 (3) WLN(SC) 35

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 100 - Plea that no substantial question of law was formulated and Second appeal would not have been allowed without formulating any such question - Held, a second appeal can be filed only if a substantial question of law is involved in the case - Memorandum of appeal must precisely state the substantial question of law involved and the High Court is obliged to satisfy itself regarding the existence of such a question - Mere appreciation of facts, the documentary evidence or the meaning of entries and the contents of the documents cannot be held to be raising a substantial question of law - Remitted the matter to High Court.
