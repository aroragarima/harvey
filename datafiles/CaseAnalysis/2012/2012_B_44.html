Union of India and another v Brigadier P. S. Gill and another

Supreme Court of India

23 March 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 104; (2012) 4 SCC 463; AIR 2012 SC 1280; 2012 ALL MR (Cri) 1754; 2012 (92) ALR 294; 2012 (4) CalLT 43; 2012 (279) E.L.T. 321; 2012 (133) FLR 403; JT 2012 (3) SC 519; 2012 (3) MLJ(Crl) 542; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 550; 2012(4) SCALE 1; [2012] 2 S.C.R. 571; 2014 (7) SLR 766

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Terrorist Affected Areas (Special Courts) Act, 1984, Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Practice & Procedure - Armed Forces Tribunal Act, 2007, ss. 30, 31 - Order by Armed Forces Tribunal (Tribunal) - Appeal before SC - Maintainability - Appellant UOI filed instant appeal challenging order passed by Tribunal contending that appeal against Tribunal order was not maintainable before SC - (A) Whether an aggrieved party could file an appeal against any such final decision or order of Tribunal u/s. 30 of the Act before SC - Held, there was no vested right of appeal against a final order or decision of Tribunal to SC other than those falling u/s. 30(2) of the Act - Only mode to bring up matter to SC in appeal was either by way of certificate obtained from Tribunal that decided matter or by obtaining leave of SC u/s. 31 for filing an appeal depending upon whether SC considered point involved in the case to be one that ought to be considered by SC - (B) Whether an application for permission to file an appeal u/s. 31 could be moved directly before SC without first approaching Tribunal for a certificate in terms of first part of s. 31(1) of the Act - Held, aggrieved party could not approach SC directly for grant of leave to file an appeal u/s. 31(1) r/w s. 31(2) of the Act - Scheme of s. 31 being that an application for grant of a certificate should first be moved before Tribunal, before aggrieved party should approach SC for grant of leave to file an appeal - Purpose underlying s. 31 appeared to be that if Tribunal itself granted a certificate of fitness for filing an appeal, it would be unnecessary for aggrieved party to approach SC for a leave to file such an appeal - An appeal by certificate would then be maintainable as a matter of right in view of s. 30 which used expression 'an appeal shall lie to the Supreme Court' - (C) Whether aggrieved party was prevented from seeking orders from SC for leave to file an appeal in the event of any urgency - Held, an application for leave to appeal was deemed to have been disposed of at expiration of time within which it would have been made but was not made within that time - Further, an application for grant of certificate before Tribunal could be made even orally and in case Tribunal was not inclined to grant certificate prayed for, request could be rejected straightaway in which event the aggrieved party could approach SC for grant of leave to file an appeal under second part of s. 31(1) - Once such an application was filed, appeal was treated as pending till such time the same was disposed of - Appeals dismissed.
