Medical Council of India v Kalinga Institute of Medical Sciences (KIMS) and others

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Madan B. Lokur, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 342; AIR 2016 SC 2294; 2016 (3) AWC 2954; 2016 (1) ILR(Cut) 1050; JT 2016 (5) SC 21; 2016 (5) MLJ 66; 2016(4) SCALE 649; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 403

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Keywords: Medical College, Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 1999, Constitution of India, 1950

Summary: Education - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Deficiency in institution - Increase in capacity of student allowed by HC - Jurisdiction of HC - Establishment of Medical College Regulations, 1999 - Respondent Medical Institution was granted permission to admit additional 50 students over and above 100 students which it was entitled for academic Year 2014-1015, however, on inspection which was conducted twice, certain deficiencies were found and permission to increase capacity of student was declined - HC vide impugned order set aside inspection report and passed order in favor of respondent Institution - Hence, present appeal.

Held, HC went into details on issues which were not within domain of HC in exercise of its jurisdiction u/art. 226 of Constitution. Further, HC did not appreciate that inspection was carried out by eminent Professors from reputed medical institutions who were experts in field and best persons to give unbiased report on facilities in respondent institution. HC u/art. 226 of Constitution was not tasked to minutely examine contents of inspection report and weigh them against objections of respondent. Hence, SC held that HC has exceeded its jurisdiction. SC held that appellant and Central Govt. each having twice considered inspection report submitted by neutral medical Professors, with Central Govt. having given personal hearing to respondent, HC should not have exceeded its jurisdiction. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside. However, admission of students from academic year 2014-2015 is not disturbed taking into consideration suffering of students if admission is cancelled. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - When expert body certifies that facilities in medical college are inadequate, Courts should not take different view in matter except when mala fide intention of Inspection team is alleged.
