Suman Verma v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 744; (2004) 12 SCC 58; AIR 2004 SC 4800; 2004 (4) AWC 3381; 2005 (1) JhrCR(SC) 63; JT 2004 (8) SC 19; 2004(8) SCALE 153; 2004 (6) SLR 138; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 647

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Service

Summary: Service - Employment - Through Employment Exchange (EE) - Appellant registered therewith - Getting appointment in a Post Office as Extra Departmental Branch Post Master - Criteria of possessing agricultural land and having residence in the area where employment is to be given satisfied - Appellant served for 8 years - Challenged made by respondent no. 6 to the appointment of appellant on ground of her being more qualified in terms of marks obtained in Matriculation examination among other grounds - Employments authorities denied post to respondent no. 6 on ground that she was not in possession of any agricultural land in her name and thus is ineligible - Respondent no. 6 contended that she had become owner of agricultural land on basis of one gift deed on Oct. 1996 and got her name mutated in Revenue records on Nov. 22, 1996 and date for consideration of application was Nov. 12, 1996 - What is the effect or whether respondent acquired ownership before last date? - Held, owning of agricultural property and getting name entered in Revenue Record are two different and distinct things - Mutation entry does not confer right or title to the property - Before last date, she possessed property - Appeal dismissed.
