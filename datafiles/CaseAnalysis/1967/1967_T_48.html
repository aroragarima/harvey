Capital Multi-Purpose Co-Operative Societybhopal And Others v The State Of M.P. & Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 120; AIR 1967 SC 1815; [1967] 3 S.C.R. 329

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Carriers & Transportation; Constitution

Keywords: Nationalisation, Delhi Road Transport Authority Act, 1950

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution - Administrative - Road Transport Corporation Act, 1950 - Constitution (First Amendment) Act, 1951 - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 68 A, 68 C, 68 D, and Chap. 4 A - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 166(3) - (A)Should schemes for taking over of certain routes by MPSRTC be held bad for non-compliance with s. 68 C and rules framed thereunder for absence of necessary particulars which would enable appellants to formulate their objections? - So long as a scheme meets requirements of section and rules, it is enough for validly originating proceeding - Thereafter objectors are open to file objections in light of four purposes as indicated and proceedings being quasi judicial, State Govt. or authority concerned can consider objections and approve or modify or reject scheme - Held, schemes have given sufficient details to enable appellants to file their objections - (B) Whether Special Secretary who heard objections on behalf of State Govt. authorised to do so being appointed under Rules of Business u/art. 166 (3)? - Only limitation is that person appointed cannot be below rank of a Secretary - As regards procedural part, appointment may be by notification as provided under new provision or by an order under Rules of Business - Further held, objection that authority was not appointed under new provision fails - (C) Whether order approving schemes was invalid for not saying that schemes fulfilled purposes mentioned in s.68 C r/w. s. 68 D i.e., scheme provides efficient, adequate, economical and properly coordinated road transport service? - There is no express requirement of authority hearing objections to come to some finding of fact as a condition precedent to its final order - Whole object of hearing objections is to consider whether scheme provides an efficient, adequate, economical and properly coordinated road transport service - Authority cannot summon witnesses or order discovery and inspection of documents, as Act has not provided for any such thing - In absence of any power to ensure obedience all that authority can do is to issue letters requesting persons to appear - If authority decides not to issue such letters, absence of effective hearing can't be alleged - With resources of Govt. behind authorities, it would be futile to say that Central Govt., State Govt. or authorities backed by it could not have equipment and finances to carry out schemes - Very fact that a scheme is proposed suggests that authorities would carry it out - (D) Whether hearing given by authority adequate and real? - Comparative merits of Corporation as against individual operators need not be judged under Chapter IV A in public interest - Further held, hearing provided was both adequate and real - Appeal dismissed.
