State Of U.P. & Anr v Radhey Shyam Rai

Supreme Court of India

06 March 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 296; (2009) 5 SCC 577; 2009 (2) AWC 1771; 2009 (2) CTC 372; 2009 (121) FLR 736; JT 2009 (3) SC 393; 2009 (2) LLN 84; 2009 (3) MLJ(SC) 1350; 2009(3) SCALE 754; [2009] 4 S.C.R. 143; 2011 (7) SLR 235

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conduct Of Business, Uttar Pradesh Sugar Cane (Purchase Tax) Act, 1961

Summary: Constitution - Whether the Uttar Pradesh Ganna Kishan Sansthan a society registered under the Societies Registration Act is a 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution of India? - Held, for the purpose of determining the question as to whether a society registered under the Societies Registration Act would be a 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution of India or not, the history of its constitution plays an important role - Sansthan is required to obey all the directions issued by the State Government from time to time - Full Bench of the High Court has rightly held the Sansthan a 'State' within the meaning of art. 12 of the Constitution of India - Appeal dismissed.
