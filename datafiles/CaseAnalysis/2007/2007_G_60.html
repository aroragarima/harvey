Government of Karnataka and Others v Gowramma and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1328; (2007) 13 SCC 482; AIR 2008 SC 863; 2008 (70) ALR 335; 2008 (1) AWC 883; 2008 (2) KarLJ 177; 2007(14) SCALE 613; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 939

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Karnataka Preservation of Trees Act, 1976

Summary: Karnataka Preservation of Trees Act, 1976 - Respondents filed a suit for recovery of a sum of Rs.1, 47, 965.20 on the ground that being owners of Trees which were transported to the Government go down on the basis of the permission granted by the present appellants, the value of the Trees has to be paid by the government - Held, there was no challenge to the conditions stipulated and it was accepted that the trees were reserved trees - What is the effect of this admission, was not examined by the High Court - Appeal allowed.
