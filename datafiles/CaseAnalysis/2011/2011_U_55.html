K.J.S. Buttar v Union Of India And Anr

Supreme Court of India

31 March 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Gyan Sudha Misra

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 211; (2011) 11 SCC 429; 2011 (8) ADJ 88; 2011 (2) AllCJ 1125; 2011 (5) AWC 4776; 2011 (2) ESC 340; JT 2011 (3) SC 625; 2011 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1592; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 600; 2011(4) SCALE 138; [2011] 4 S.C.R. 136; 2011 (2) SLR 758

Case Digest

Subject: Defence & Security Forces; Service

Keywords: Disability Pension, Armed Forces

Summary: Defence & Security Forces - Service - War Injury Pension - Revision - Entitlement - Appellant Captain was released from service due to serious injuries of permanent nature attributable in military service - Release Medical Board assessed appellant's disability at 50% - Ministry of Defence (MoD), by letter dated 31-1-2001, revised rates of War Injury Pension pursuant to Fifth Pay Commission recommendations - Appellant sought revised benefits in terms of Notification dated 31-1-2001 with regard to War Injury Pension, assessment of 75% disability instead of 50% for the purpose of disability pension and grant of service element for full 10 years instead of 2 years - Respondent authority rejected appellant's request on ground that appellant had retired before 1-1-1996, and hence in terms of Notification dated 31-1-2001 he could not get benefits as they were granted to officers who retired on or after 1-1-1996 - Appellant petitioned to HC for issuance of writ - HC dismissed writ petition - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant was entitled to benefit in terms of Notification dated 31-1-2001 - Held, restriction of benefit to only officers who were invalided out of service after 1-1-1996 was violative of art. 14 of the Constitution and hence illegal - Appellant was entitled to benefit of para 7.2 of Notification dated 31-1-2001 according to which where disability was assessed between 50% and 75% then same should be treated as 75%, and it made no difference whether he was invalided from service before or after 1-1-1996 - Hence appellant was entitled to revised disability pension with arrears from 1-1-1996, and interest at 8% per annum on same - MoD was granting War Injury Pension to pre 1996 retirees also in terms of para 10.1 of Ministry's letter No.1(5)/87/D(Pen-Ser) dated 30-10-1987 and mode of calculation however was changed by Notification dated 31-1-2001 which was restricted to post 1996 retirees - Appellant, therefore, was entitled to War Injury Pension even prior to 1-1-1996 and especially in view of instructions dated 31-1-2001 issued by Government of India - Instruction dated 31-1-2001 was initially for persons retiring after 1-1-1996 but later on by virtue of subsequent Notification dated 16-5-2001 it was extended to pre 1996 retirees also on rationalization of scheme - Further, appellant was invalided out and released in a low medical category with permanent disability assessed at 50% by Release Medical Board - As per Defence Service Regulation/Pension regulation for Army 1961 where any officer was found suffering from disability attributable to or aggravated by Military Service he should be deemed to have been invalided out of service, hence, appellant was entitled to benefit of said Regulation - Appellant, therefore, was entitled to grant of War Injury Pension with effect from 1-1-1996, computation of disability at 75% for the purpose of disability pension with arrears and grant of 10 years' commission service in view of SC decision in Union of India & Anr. v. C.S. Sidhu, 2010 INDLAW SC 248 - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeal allowed.
