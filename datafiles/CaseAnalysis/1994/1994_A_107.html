Santosh Dev v Archna Guha

Supreme Court of India

03 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, B.L. Hansaria

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1384; (1994) 2 SCC 420; (1994) SCC (Cr) 546; 1994 (31) ACC 250; AIR 1995 SCW 1725; 1994 (2) CCR 326; 1994 (1) Crimes 548; 1995 CRLJ 2640; JT 1994 (1) SC 413; 1994(1) SCALE 423; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 549; 1994 (1) UJ 512

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Defence & Security Forces; Tort

Keywords: Defence Regulations

Summary: Criminal - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 21 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971,s. 3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 325, 330, 331 and 509 - Criminal Penal Code, 1973, ss. 244 and 245 - Respondent filed private complaints against appellants for torturing her, and her family members in torture cell - Application of appellant u/s. 245 CrPC dismissed by magistrate and High Court - Whether appellants can avail benefit of s. 245 CrPC? - Held, Trial Court and HC rightly dismissed applications of appellants in interest of justice and evidence of complainant already recorded within meaning of 'evidence' though she has not been cross-examined yet - Delay caused by appellants themselves and cannot take benefit of it - Appeal dismissed.
