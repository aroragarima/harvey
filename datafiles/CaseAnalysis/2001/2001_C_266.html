Mohan Wahi v Commissioner of Income Tax and Others

Supreme Court of India

30 March 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, Doraiswamy Raju

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 232; (2001) 4 SCC 362; AIR 2001 SC 3906; 2001 (167) CTR 86; [2001] 248 ITR 799; JT 2001 (4) SC 343; 2001(3) SCALE 138; [2001] 2 S.C.R. 912; 2001 (3) SLT 274; 2001 (4) Supreme 544; [2001] 116 TAXMAN 63

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Land & Property - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Taxation Laws (Continuation And Validation Of Recovery Proceedings) Act, 1964, s. 3 - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 29 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 156, 220, 222, 224, 225 and 264 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, s. 146 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 - House property devolved upon four sons of deceased P, S, R and K-P and S, entered into a partnership known as UPCC - Business collapsed - Income Tax assessments of firm UPCC were finalised for asst. yrs. 1967-68 to 1969-70 - House property attached - Public auction - Respondent No. 3 purchased property - Suit filed submitting that undivided property of four brothers and in any case share of brothers, who were not partners in firm, could not have been attached and advertised for sale for recovery of dues against firm - Ad-interim injunction, not to confirm sale - Issues arise - (A) Whether TRO could have confirmed sale on 25-03-1998, when demands on account of tax for recovery of which tax recovery certificates were issued had admittedly ceased to exist? - Held, sale was held on 11-01-1980 - No application was filed for setting aside sale either by assessee or by auction-purchaser or by anyone interested in property - On expiry of 30 days from date of sale TRO could have passed an order confirming sale - However, TRO was injuncted by Civil Court from confirming sale - Interim order issued by Civil Court ceased to operate on 12-01-1998, whereafter an order of confirmation was passed on 25-03-1998, by TRO ignoring, or unmindful of, important event which had taken place in between - Before 25-03-1998, demand against assessee admittedly stood reduced to nil - This fact was in notice of ITO as well as CIT - Attention of ITO as also TRO was also invited by firm M/s UPCC - In view of facts within knowledge of department and communications so made, TRO could not have confirmed sale on 25-03-1998 - (B) What is effect of a notice of demand u/s. 156 of IT Act, 1961 having not been served on assessee on sale held for recovery of arrears of Income Tax? - Held, notice of demand was not served on assessee - Very foundation for initiating recovery proceedings, therefore, was non-existent and assessee could neither have deemed to be in default nor any proceedings for recovery of tax could have been initiated against him - Plea as to non-service of demand notice having been raised before High Court, HC should not have adopted too technical a approach by refusing to deal with plea because it was not raised in manner in which HC thought it should have been raised - Sale of suit property in favour of respondent No. 3 is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
