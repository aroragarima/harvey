Harjit Singh and Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 144; (2007) 9 SCC 582; (2007) 2 SCC (L&S) 997; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1017; 2007(6) ALL MR 982; 2007 (6) AWC 5717; 2007 (113) FLR 318; JT 2007 (8) SC 549; 2007 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1052; 2007 (4) LLN 166; 2007(3) SCALE 553; 2007 (5) SCJ 937; [2007] 3 S.C.R. 130; 2007 (4) SLR 645

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Unfitness

Summary: Punjab Police Rules - Service - Misconduct - Order of dismissal from service - Negligence of being absent from duty - Held, in case of habitual absence, a punishment of dismissal of service would be just - In the facts and circumstances of this case and in particular having regard to the passage of time, punishment of compulsory Retirement will meet the ends of justice - If otherwise eligible, the delinquents would be entitled to retiral benefits - Appeal partly allowed.
