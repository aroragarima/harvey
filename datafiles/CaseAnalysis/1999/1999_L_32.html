Sub-Inspector Rooplal and Another v Lt. Governor Through Chief Secretary, Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.P. Bharucha, R.C. Lahoti

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 895; (2000) SCC (L&S) 213; (2000) 1 SCC 644; AIR 2000 SC 594; JT 1999 (9) SC 597; 2000 LabIC 370; 2000 (1) LLN 347; 2000 (2) SLJ 395; 1999 (6) SLR 623; 1999 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 125; [1999] Supp5 S.C.R. 310

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Deputationist, Delhi Police (Appointment and Recruitment) Rules, 1980

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 16 - Delhi Police (Appointment and Recruitment) Rules, 1980, rr. 5(h) and 17 - Whether a Sub-Inspector of BSF when transferred on deputation to Delhi Police in cadre of Sub-Inspector (Executive) on being permanently absorbed in transferred post, is entitled to count his service in BSF for purpose seniority in cadre of Sub-Inspector (Executive) in Delhi Police or not? - Held, it will be against all rules of service jurisprudence, if a Govt. servant holding a particular post is transferred to same or an equivalent post in another Govt. department, period of his service in post before his transfer is not taken into consideration in computing his seniority in transferred post - Any rule, regulation or executive instruction which has effect of taking away service rendered by a deputationist in an equivalent cadre in parent department while counting his seniority in deputed post would be violative of art. 14 and 16 of Constitution - Hence, liable to be struck down - Right of appellant-petitioners to count their service from date of their regular appointment in post of Sub-Inspector in BSF, while computing their seniority in cadre of Sub-Inspector (Executive) in Delhi Police, is restored - Appeal partly allowed.
