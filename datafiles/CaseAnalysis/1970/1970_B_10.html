Baidyanath Ayurved Bhawan Private Limited v Excise Commissioner, U.P

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 14; (1971) 1 SCC 4; AIR 1971 SC 378; 1999 (110) E.L.T. 363; [1971] 2 S.C.R. 590; 1971 UJ 48

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, sch. I, item 1, ss. 3(1) and 4 - Excise duty on medicinal preparation - Appellant was a manufacturer of certain medicines with the aid of substances like tincture, spirit, etc. which contain alcohol - The Superintendent of Excise called upon the appellant to pay duty under the Act on the medicinal preparation on the ground that they contain alcohol - Appellant resisted the demand on the ground that the medicines in question were not prepared by adding pure alcohol; the fact that the tincture which is a component of that preparation contains alcohol does not make it a preparation containing alcohol - Said contention was rejected by the Superintendent of Excise as well as by the HC - Hence, present appeal - Whether the preparation in question do not attract duty because alcohol was not directly added to the solution? - Held, in order to attract duty all that is required is that a medicinal preparation should contain alcohol - Alcohol may be a part of the preparation either because it is directly added to the solution or it came to be included in that medicinal preparation because of one of the components of that preparation contained alcohol - According to the plain language of the s. 4 of 1955 Act, all that is required is that the preparation should contain alcohol - Hence, excise duty to be leviable on impugned medicinal preparation - Impugned order of the HC could be upheld - Appeal dismissed.
