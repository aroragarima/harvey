Income Tax Officer v M.K. Mohammed Kunhi

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 218; AIR 1969 SC 430; [1969] 71 ITR 815; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 65

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Practice & Procedure - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 254 and 255 - A/ys 1954-55, 1960-61 and 1961-62 - Jurisdiction - Appellate Tribunal - Penalties u/s. 271(1)(c) of 1961 Act were imposed on assessee for concealment of particular income and furnishing inaccurate particulars - Assessee preferred appeals to the Appellate Tribunal and made an interim prayer for stay of collection of the penalties imposed - Tribunal declined to order any stay holding that it had no power to grant such a prayer - On appeal, HC held that the Appellate Tribunal had the power to stay the proceedings as also the collection of the penalties pending the appeal since that power was incidental and ancillary to its appellate jurisdiction - Hence, present appeal - Whether the Appellate Tribunal has the power to stay the recovery of penalty imposed by the departmental authorities on an assessee during the pendency of an appeal before it? - Held, Appellate Tribunal must be held to have the power to grant stay as incidental or ancillary to its appellate jurisdiction - This is particularly so when s. 220(6) of 1961 Act deals expressly with a situation when an appeal is pending before the Appellate Assistant Commissioner, but the Act is silent in that behalf when an appeal is pending before the Appellate Tribunal - Hence, it could well be said that when s. 254 of 1961 Act confers appellate jurisdiction, it impliedly grants the power of doing all such acts, or employing such means, as are essentially necessary to its execution and that the statutory power carries with it the duty in proper cases to make such orders for staying proceedings as will prevent the appeal if successful from being rendered nugatory - Moreover, power of stay by the Tribunal is not likely to be exercised in a routine way in view of the special nature of taxation and revenue laws except when a strong prima facie case is made out that the Tribunal will consider whether to stay the recovery proceedings and on what conditions and where the Tribunal is satisfied that the entire purpose of the appeal will be frustrated by allowing the recovery proceedings to continue during the pendency of the appeal - Impugned order of the HC upheld - Appeal dismissed.
