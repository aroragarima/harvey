Sushil K. Chakravarty (D) Thr. Lrs. v M/S. Tej Properties Pvt. Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Jagdish Singh Khehar, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 160; (2013) 9 SCC 642; AIR 2013 SC 1732; 2013 (4) ALD(SC) 89; 2013(2) ALL MR 928; 2013 (98) ALR 251; 2013 (2) CLT(SC) 23; 2013 (2) CTC 656; JT 2013 (4) SC 580; 2014 (1) MahLJ 643; 2013 (3) MLJ 817; 2014 (1) MPLJ 275; 2013 (3) RCR(Civil) 161; 2013 (119) RD 802; 2013(4) SCALE 535; [2013] 5 S.C.R. 243; 2014 (4) WLN(SC) 68

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Pronounced, Sole Defendant, Impleadment of legal heirs

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 22 r. 4(4) - Suit for specific performance - Impleadment of legal heirs - Legality - Plaintiff(respondent) filed a suit for specific performance of an agreement to sell, executed by plaintiff with original defendant - Thereafter, original defendant died - Single Judge decreed suit without impleading LRs/representatives of original defendant - Aggrieved appellants filed an appeal before DB, which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether Single Judge was justified to proceed with suit without insisting on impleadment of LRs of deceased defendant -

Held, Court had taken a conscious decision under O. 22 r. 4(4) of CPC - Trial Court could proceed with a suit under aforementioned provision, without impleading legal representatives of a defendant, who having filed a written statement had failed to appear and contest suit, if Court considers it fit to do so - Thus, all ingredients of O. 22 r. 4(4) of CPC stood fully satisfied in facts and circumstances of instant case - When suit was allowed to proceed further, without insisting on impleadment of legal representatives of deceased it was done on Court's satisfaction, that it was a fit case to exempt plaintiff from necessity of impleading legal representatives of original defendant - Hence, that could only had been done, on satisfaction that parameters postulated under O. 22 r. 4(4) of CPC, stood complied - Thus, Single Judge committed no error in proceeding with matter as ex-parte, as against original defendant without impleading his LRs in his place - Single Judge was justified in proceeding with the matter ex-parte, as against sole defendant, without impleading his LRs in his place - Impugned judgment in determination, with reference to O. 22 r. 4(4) of CPC was upheld - Appeals dismissed.
