Nova Ads v Metropolitan Transport Corporation and others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 851; AIR 2015 SC 1468; 2015(1) ALL MR 912; 2015 (1) BC 250; 2015 (1) CTC 308; JT 2014 (14) SC 233; 2014(13) SCALE 768

Case Digest

Subject: Municipalities & Local Governments; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919

Summary: (A) Municipalities & Local Governments - Contract & Commercial - Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919, ss. 285A, 285B - Erection of bus shelter - Authority of transport corporation - Respondent MTCL issued advertisements for erection and maintenance of certain bus shelters - Respondent MTCL entered into sponsorship agreement with sponsors for erection of bus shelters - Several tenderers challenged authority of respondent MTCL to erect bus shelters on its own or to give permission to sponsors - HC issued various directions holding that respondent MTCL had no jurisdiction - Sponsors challenged HC order by filing SLPs before SC - In the meantime, parties entered into a settlement - SC disposed of SLPs on the basis of settlement - Hence, instant writ petition and appeals seeking to recall earlier order of SC - Whether the respondent MTCL has authority to erect and maintain bus shelters.

Held, a conjoint reading of s. 285A and 285B make it absolutely plain that the Municipal Corporation has the power under the Act to control the cart-stand which includes a stand for carriages including motor-vehicles and levy fees and also provide for licence, even for private cart-stand. The legislature has conferred power on the Corporation to take necessary action for public convenience and make provisions for the cart-stand which includes the motor vehicles. The exception carved out by a proviso to s. 285A of the Act does not remotely suggest that the legislature has even conceived of any other body like respondent MTCL, which is a State undertaking, to even construct the bus shelters. S. 285A of the Act has to be read in juxtaposition with s. 285 of the Act and by no stretch of suggestion, it can be read to include bus shelters. The Corporation has the authority to deal with the 'stands' and have the obligation to control and manage the bus shelters for public convenience. It is within the authority of the Corporation to think of appropriate management. The State Govt. could have issued a notification specifying certain places as stands for motor vehicles of the Transport Department which may include State transport undertakings i.e. MTCL, but the State Government has no statutory authority to issue a notification allowing the State transport undertakings to provide shelters for passengers. Hence, the opinion expressed by the HC that the Corporation has the power or authority to deal with the streets, subject to restrictions under the Act and the respondent MTCL has no power or authority to deal with the same on the basis of the government order is absolutely justified in law. Order accordingly.

(B) Municipalities & Local Governments - Contract & Commercial - Chennai City Municipal Corporation Act, 1919 - Erection of bus shelter - Settlement by transport corporation - Legality - Whether the agreement entered into between appellant and respondent MTCL is sustainable in law.

Held, when a power had not been conferred on MTCL to enter into settlement with appellants and it exercises that power under the cloak of a power conferred, it really paved the path of deviance. The appellants could not have legitimately entered into a settlement with the MTCL. It could not have entered into an agreement with the State undertaking. This was a clear deceit on the part of the appellants in collusion with the MTCL to frustrate the legal rights of the Corporation. It is another matter that the Corporation did not wake up to save its own interest. The writ petitioner, for his own individual interest, made a prayer to recall of the order and thereafter, the Corporation has woken from slumber. It was a loss to the Corporation and the Corporation is a public body and it is expected to protect and handle its finances for the benefit of the persons who are covered under the Act. The conduct of the appellants, from any angle, is absolutely depreciable. A public authority like the Corporation is not supposed to enter into this kind of private negotiations without calling for a tender, especially while entering into a contract for the purpose of providing bus shelters. The contracts entered into by the appellants with the MTCL cannot be sustained and they are accordingly annulled. It is directed that the Corporation shall take over the management of the bus shelters forthwith and shall proceed to deal with them for all purposes by taking recourse to procedure of tender or auction which should be fair and transparent. Impugned HC order is affirmed to the extent it holds that the Corporation has the authority. Order accordingly.

Ratio - Wherever a contract is to be awarded or a licence is sought to be given, it is obligatory on the part of the public authority to adopt a transparent and fair method.
