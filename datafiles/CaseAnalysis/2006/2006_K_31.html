I. Nelson And Another v Kallayam Pastorate And Others

Supreme Court of India

14 September 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 1443; (2006) 11 SCC 624; AIR 2007 SC 1337; 2007 (1) ALD(SC) 9; 2007 (1) AWC 162; JT 2006 (12) SC 432; 2006(9) SCALE 245; [2006] Supp6 S.C.R. 219

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Summary: Trusts & Associations - Societies Registration Act, 1860 - Tamil Nadu Societies Registration Act, 1975, s. 53 - Suit was filed in the HC against the Indian Evangelical Lutheran Church (Church) registered under the Societies Registration Act, 1860 for a declaration that the elections conducted by the third defendant Church without the assistance and effective participation of the plaintiff and the fourth defendant was non est in law and therefore null and void and seeking permanent injunction restraining the third defendant from functioning as election commissioner of Church - Advocate Commissioner was appointed to hold elections and elections were held under the supervision of the said Advocate Commissioner - Advocate Commissioner, however, submitted a report in which he contended that the society has become defunct - HC proceeded to set aside all the elections in their entirety on the premise that the society has become a defunct one - Appellant and other members filed an application for impleading them as parties, inter alia, on the premise that they were validly elected - By reason of the impugned order accordingly, DB dismissed the said petitions - Hence, present appeal - Held, society, presumably keeping in view the nature of its activities, was required to be compulsorily registered and that it was so registered under the Central Act of 1860 - Hence, once it is held that the society was registered under the 1860 Act; in terms of s. 53 of the 1975 Act it shall be deemed to be registered thereunder - Having regard to the provisions contained in s. 53 thereof, once the society became a society registered under the 1975 Act, all the consequences arising thereunder shall ensue - It was, therefore, for the statutory authorities to take recourse to such actions as are provided for in the 1975 Act or the Rules framed thereunder - In the event, the society became defunct or other statutory requirements were not complied with by the members of the society, penal measures could have been taken but in no situation the election of the office bearers could have been set aside - Once a valid election was held, the HC could not have directed setting aside of an election only on the purported ground that it became defunct - No reason as to why the elected members should not be allowed to carry on the activities of the society wherefor they were duly elected - Directed that Chartered Accountant appointed by the Administrators shall continue to function and shall submit a report before the Church Council with a copy to the Administrators - Appeal allowed.
