M/s. Easland Combines, Coimbatore v The Collector of Central Excise, Coimbatore

Supreme Court of India

13 January 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 9; (2003) 3 SCC 410; AIR 2003 SC 843; 2003 (85) ECC 496; 2003 (106) ECR 594; 2003 (152) E.L.T. 39; JT 2003 (1) SC 106; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 459; 2003 (1) RCR(Civil) 569; 2003 (54) RLT 373; 2003(1) SCALE 123; [2003] 1 S.C.R. 98; 2003 (1) SLT 361; 2003 (1) Supreme 374

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Recovery Of Duties, Finance Act,2000

Summary: [A.] Excise - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, s.11A - Central Excise Rules, 1944, rr. 10, 173, 173B, 173C, 173Q, 9(2) - Central Excise Act, 1994, s.11 A - Effect of amendments in s 11 A of Act - By this amendment, basis for arriving at conclusion that Rule 10 (now Section 11 A) does not deal with classification list or relate to re-opening of classification list is altered by specifically providing that in such cases also, show-cause notice could be issued - Held, legislature can always render a judicial decision ineffective by enacting a valid law on topic within its legislative field by fundamentally altering or changing its character retrospectively - Courts are not concerned with legislative policy or with result, whether injurious or otherwise, by giving effect to language used nor is it function of Court where meaning is clear not to give effect to it merely because it would lead to some hardship - Whether there was any ground for invoking first proviso to sub-section (1) of Section 11 A of Act? - Further held, mere failure to pay duty and/or take out a licence which is not due to any fraud, collusion or willful misstatement or suppression of fact or contravention of any provision is not sufficient to attract extended period of limitation.
