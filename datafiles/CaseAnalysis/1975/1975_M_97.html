Northern India Iron and Steel Company and Others v State Of Haryana & Anr

Supreme Court of India

10 November 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, A.N. Ray, Kuttyil Kurien Mathew

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 264; (1976) 2 SCC 877; AIR 1976 SC 1100; [1976] 2 S.C.R. 677

Case Digest

Subject: Electricity & Energy; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition

Summary: Electricity (Supply) Act , 1948, s. 49 - Indian Electricity Act, 1910 - Punjab Electricity (Duty) Act, 1958, s. 3 - Punjab Electricity (Duty) Rules, 1958, r. 3(1) - (A) Inability of Board to supply energy due to power cut - Permissibility as to proportionate reduction of monthly demand charge from consumer - (B) Supply governed by two-part tariff - Mode of levy of duty - Held, monthly demand charge for a particular month will have to be assessed in accordance with cl.4(b) of tariff framed u/s. 49 and therefrom a proportionate reduction as per sub-cl. (f) is to be made - Held, duty under 1958 Act is chargeable not only on the energy charge but also on the demand charge when the supply is governed by two-part tariff and it is chargeable on the actual amount of demand charge realisable from the consumer - Appeal Partly Allowed.
