D.G., Revenue Intelligence and Investment v Amrit Lal Mehta

Supreme Court of India

26 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, AR. Lakshmanan, S.H. Kapadia

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 1066; (2007) 15 SCC 510; 2007 (220) E.L.T. 9

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Levy Of Duty, Adjudication Proceedings

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Rewards Committee concluded that Rs. 9.5 lakhs payable to appellant, which sum has been paid - To this extent, appellant cannot have any grievance - High Court to decide question of contempt - This Order does not indicate that this Court has accepted that sum of Rs. 9.5 lakhs was compensation payable nor that this Court is accepting that a higher amount is payable - Further, in Reward Scheme, there is no vested right in person to claim a reward - Payment at highest can be an ex gratia payment - No justification to grant interest on ex gratia payment - Held, portion of HC order where interest is directed to be paid as well as portion awarding costs is set aside - Appeal dismissed.
