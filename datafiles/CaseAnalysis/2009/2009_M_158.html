Shiromani Gurudwara Prabandhak Committee v Mahant Prem Dass

Supreme Court of India

24 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 217; (2009) 15 SCC 381; JT 2009 (3) SC 57; 2009 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 956; 2009 (2) RCR(Civil) 297; 2009(3) SCALE 47; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 1099

Case Digest

Subject: Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Sikh Gurdwaras Act, 1925

Summary: Trusts & Associations - High Court declared an institution allegedly known as "Gurdwara Sahib Gurdwara Bhagat Bhagwan" not to be a Sikh Gurdwara - Appeal against - Held, institution was only known as a Dera or Dera Sadhan or Mausooma Dharamshala Sadhan and it gave the impression that it was a charitable Institution for poor faqirs and travellers, as also offered shelters to them and the Mahants, who governed the Institution, were noble persons - There is no evidence that this was a Sikh Institution from its inception till today or the Sikhs had any say in the matter of appointment of Mahants - There is no evidence of there being a regular Granthi in the Institution there existed even a Nishan Sahib - There was no further evidence that the Sikh religious ceremonies were ever held or there is Katha or Parvachan in a congregation - Appeal dismissed.
