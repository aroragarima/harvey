Messrs Impression Prints v Commissioner of Central Excise, Delhi-1

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 491; (2005) 7 SCC 497; 2005 (187) E.L.T. 170; JT 2005 (7) SC 585; 2005(6) SCALE 697; [2005] Supp2 S.C.R. 904

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Excise; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: [A.] Excise - Central Excises and Salt Act, 1944, s. 2(f) - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, ss. 11AC, 7 - Bed sheets, bed covers and pillow cases - Whether Appellants are entitled to benefit of Notification No 65/87-CE? - Held, without printing and colouring it is impossible to manufacture printed bedsheets, bed covers and pillow cases - In such cases it is irrelevant that at an intermediate stage some other excisable commodity comes into existence - Cotton fabrics are manufactured in process of manufacture of printed bedsheets, bed covers and pillow cases - Benefit of Notification is not available - Appeal dismissed.
