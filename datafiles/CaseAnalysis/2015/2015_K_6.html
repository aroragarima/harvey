K. Devakimma and others v Tirumala Tirupati Devasthanams and another

Supreme Court of India

23 April 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.M. Sapre, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 290; AIR 2015 SC 3375; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 7; 2015(4) ALL MR 967; 2015 (5) AWC 5364; 2015 (2) CLT(SC) 101; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 1001; 2015 (128) RD 706; 2015(5) SCALE 454

Case Digest

Subject: Administrative; Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquired

Summary: Land & Property - Administrative - Acquisition of land - Enhanced rate of compensation reduced by HC - Justification - Land in question including land of petitioner was acquired was State after complying due procedure under law - compensation was granted vide order by lower authority which was enhanced by Reference Court - However, in appeal filed before HC compensation granted at enhanced rate was reduced - Hence, present appeal.

Held, Reference Court took into consideration rates of lands prevailing in years 1957, 1962 and 1976without taking into account rates of lands prevalent in years 1986 and 1987 though they related to lands sold in near proximity with acquisition of lands in question on ground that appeals were pending in relation to these lands against orders/awards and then taking into account appreciation in value of land in last 25 years at Rs.3/- per square feet p.a. fixed fair market value of land in question between Rs.80 to Rs.100/- per square feet. SC held that reasoning of Reference Court is in conformity with principle of law laid down by SC wherein this Court has in no uncertain held that recourse can be taken in appropriate cases to mode of determining market value of acquired land by providing appropriate escalation over proved market value of nearby lands in previous years where there is no evidence of any contemporaneous sale transactions or acquisition of comparable lands in neighborhood. Hence, SC held that Reference Court has rightly relied on rates determined by it in relation to adjacent lands and applied principle of giving escalation to rates determined yearly and worked out rates between 80/- to 100/- per square feet. Further, appellants landowners were justified in filing copies of orders/awards passed in relation to adjacent lands for proving market rate of land in question because as mentioned above, these lands were situated in same area nearer to lands in question and were also acquired for same public purpose. Hence, rate fixed by Reference Court between Rs.80/- to Rs.100/- per square feet for lands in question was just and proper and HC erred in reducing same to Rs.30/- per square feet. However, SC held that Reference Court having held that appellants were entitled to compensation at rate varying between Rs.80/- to Rs.100/- per square feet, should have fixed one uniform rate for entire land rather than to fix different rates such as Rs.80/-, Rs.86/-, Rs.90/- and Rs.100/- per square feet for different landowners. Since, land of all appellants was more or less similar in nature and no evidence was adduced by appellants to prove any significant improvement/addition or/dissimilarity in land or its quality, Reference Court should have fixed one uniform rate, hence, SC fixed rate and granted compensation at rate of Rs.90 per square feet. Hence, order passed by HC is set aside and resorted order of Reference Court which is modified. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Escalation in price of land depends upon nature of land and its surrounding, its benefit should have given for determining price of land in question by taking into account rate of land fixed in relation to land acquired in past years.
