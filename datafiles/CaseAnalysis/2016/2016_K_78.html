Kundan Lal and another v Kamruddin and another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

R. Banumathi, R.K. Agrawal

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 932; (2017) 1 SCC 403; 2017 (3) ALD(SC) 98; 2017 (120) ALR 712; 2017 (3) MahLJ 48; 2017 (2) MPLJ 538; 2017 (1) RCR(Rent) 475; 2017 (135) RD 58; 2017(1) SCALE 121; [2016] 11 S.C.R. 99; 2017 (1) WLN(SC) 210

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Land & Property

Summary: Rent Control - Suit property - Possession - Validity - Appellants filed instant appeal challenging impugned order of HC wherein HC confirmed judgment of Courts below wherein appellants were directed to hand-over possession of suit property in question.

Held, respondent no. 1/plaintiff was allotted area measuring 126 square yards 270 w.e.f. 01-04-1990. Trial Court as well as Appellate Court noted that site plan on the back of said allotment order shows that suit property was a part of allotted area measuring 126 square yards. Respondent no. 1 has produced receipts regarding payment of rent to appellant no. 3, regarding Khasra No. 267 which were PW2/2 to PW10 and PW5/1 to PW5/3 and PW6/1 to PW6/3. The contesting defendants have also placed on record receipts DW3/1, and DW5/1 to DW5/4. Courts below held that Mark A clearly shows that the same related to property bearing Khasra No. 267 and in that way the same cannot be connected to suit land. Therefore, Courts below have recorded concurrent findings of fact that appellant was in possession and allotted different survey number in Khasra No. 267 and he has no right to claim suit property. Appellants were granted 6 months' time to vacate suit premises subject to filing usual undertaking in the Registry of SC within 4 weeks. Appeal dismissed.
