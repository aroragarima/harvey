Ranen Roy v Prakash Mitra

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 1706; (1998) 9 SCC 689; JT 1998 (9) SC 33; 1999 (1) RCR(Rent) 114

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 , O. 23, r. 1 (4) - Delhi Rent Control Act, 1958, s. 21 - Respondent made an application u/s. 21 of Act before Rent Controller seeking permission to let out first floor of demised premises to appellant for a period of 2 years - Statements of parties were recorded ARC and granted permission to respondent to let out first floor of suit premises to appellant for residential purposes for a period of 24 months with effect from date of order - On 26-5-1978, respondent made an application before ARC for issuance of warrant of possession of first floor of premises let out to appellant since appellant had not vacated those premises by that date, even though period of two years had expired - Application seeking warrants of possession was resisted and objections were filed by appellant to that application - Before application of respondent could be considered on merits, it appears, appellant made a statement withdrawing his objections - Prayer for withdrawal of objections was unqualified and no liberty was sought by appellant to file fresh objections - On 5-1-1979, objections were permitted to be withdrawn and ARC granted further one year's time to appellant to vacate and hand over vacant possession of premises to respondent - After expiry of one year appellant filed yet another application before Rent Controller raising fresh objections to application seeking warrants of possession with prayer that objections be decided on merits - That application was allowed respondent made an application before ARC stating that since earlier objections had been withdrawn without reservation, no fresh objections could be filed by appellant - Landlord's application was dismissed on 29-5-1981 - ARC held that second set of objections filed by appellant were barred by principle of res judicata - Tenant's appeal before Rent Control Tribunal against order - High Court dismissed second appeal - Whether principle of res judicata applicable in the second set objections? - Held, it seen from statement that it was unqualified and no reservation had been made - O. 23, sub-r. (4) of r. 1 of CPC provides that where plaintiff either abandons any suit or part of a claim under sub-r. (1) or withdraws from a suit or part of a claim without seeking any liberty to institute a fresh suit in respect of subject-matter of such suit or part of claim, he shall be precluded from instituting any fresh suit in respect of that subject-matter or that part of claim - This provision is clearly attracted to facts of present case - Appellant's second set of objections, therefore, not maintainable - Appeal dismissed.
