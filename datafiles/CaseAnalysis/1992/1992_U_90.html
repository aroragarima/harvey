N. K. Bapna v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 May 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, Veeraswami Ramaswami, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 471; (1992) 3 SCC 512; (1992) SCC (Cr) 683; [1992] 75 Comp Cas 745; 1992 CRLR 503; 1992 CrLR(SC) 503; 1992 (42) ECR 630; 1992 (60) E.L.T. 13; JT 1992 (4) SC 49; 1992(1) SCALE 1135; [1992] 3 S.C.R. 267; 1992 (2) UJ 417

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1) - Customs Act, 1962, ss. 2(39), 49, 72 and 111 - Abetment of smuggling - (A) Whether an order of detention can be challenged without detention order and grounds of detention being served on petitioner? - Held, it is well settled that even in a case of preventive detention, it is not necessary for proposed detenu to wait till a detention order is served upon him before challenging detention order conflicting claims of State and fundamental right of a citizen have been reconciled and limitations, if any, have been precisely enunciated in case of Smt Alka Subhash Gadia [1990 Indlaw SC 636] - (B) Whether once goods are assessed to customs duty and cleared from customs area and concept of 'smuggling' can have no meaning in respect of such goods thereafter? - Held, if there is prima facie, smuggling or abetment of smuggling, it is open to competent authorities to issue a detention order which may be challenged later on merits even general concept of smuggling contains two elements: one, bringing into India of goods import of which is prohibited and two, bringing, into country's trade stream, of goods import of which is permitted without paying customs duties with which they are chargeable second eventuality can occur not only where there is a clandestine import evading assessment of duty but also where there is a clandestine removal without payment of assessed duty; where goods are warehoused u/s. 49 and they are clandestinely removed, there would be 'smuggling' as duties payable thereon have been evaded altogether, but even in a case where goods are assessed to duty and allowed to be warehoused u/s. 59, clandestine removal can result in loss of duty provision in s. 72 for collection of duty and forfeiture of bond furnished to secure due payment of duty may not always be an adequate over to Revenue if goods are spirited away without permission mere fact that goods have been ostensibly cleared, after assessment of duty, to a warehouse does not preclude applicability of concept of smuggling even in such a case - (C) Detention - Whether valid? - Held, on conspectus of facts, activity of company would amount to smuggling and that of petitioner to abetment of smuggling, if they had removed, or caused or abetted removal of goods from bonded warehouse without permission of concerned authorities order of detention proposed cannot be said to proceed on a basis totally extraneous to provisions of Act nor can it be said that grounds of detention are vague, irrelevant or extraneous to purpose or provisions of Act - HC's order dismissing Writ Petition is upheld though not reasoning of Division Bench - Petition dismissed.
