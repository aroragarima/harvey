Renusagar Power Company Limited v General Electric Company and Another

Supreme Court of India

16 August 1984

Case Analysis

Bench

V.D. Tulzapurkar, R.S. Pathak

Where Reported

1984 Indlaw SC 288; (1984) 4 SCC 679; AIR 1985 SC 1156; 1984 ARBLR 240; 1984 (3) CompLJ 110; 1984(2) SCALE 321; [1985] 1 S.C.R. 432

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration (Protocol And Convention) Act, 1937, Arbitration Act 1899, English Arbitration Act (ENG), English Arbitration Act, 1950 (ENG)

Summary: Arbitration - Enforcement of Foreign Award - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 3 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 34 - Contract entered into between the parties - Disputes arose and matter was referred for arbitration - Appellant contended that claims raised by respondent stood beyond scope of arbitration agreement - Whether in view of arbitration clause, the questions of existence, validity or effect of the agreement of the petitioner should be stayed so as to enable the arbitrators to proceed with the reference and pass award? - Held, no - Question to be considered in regard to foreign awards made under the Foreign Awards and shall be governed by the provisions of the Act - Determination of the arbitrators is subject to the decision of the Court which can be had either before the arbitration proceedings commence or during their pendency - Contention that the arbitrators will have no jurisdiction to decide questions regarding the existence, validity or effect (scope) of the arbitration agreement is not sustainable - Delaying tactics of raising a plea of lack of arbitrator's jurisdiction should be avoided - Appeal dismissed.
