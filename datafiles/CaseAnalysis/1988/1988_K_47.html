Fertilizers And Chemicals Travancore Limited v Kerala State Electricity Board And Another

Supreme Court of India

05 May 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

M.N. Venkatachaliah, S. Natarajan

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 878; (1988) 3 SCC 382; AIR 1988 SC 1989; JT 1988 (2) SC 540; 1988 (2) KLT 117; 1988(1) SCALE 1023; [1988] 3 S.C.R. 925; 1988 (2) UJ 184

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Electricity & Energy

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Banking and Finance - Electricity and Energy - All India Services (Death-Cum-Retirement Benefits) Rules, 1958 - Kerala State Electricity Board (General Tariffs) Regulations 1965 - Electricity (Supply) Act, 1948, ss.49, 49(3) - Electricity supply - Writ petition challenging the unilateral enhancement of the electricity tariff from Rs. 110 per K. W. p.a. to Rs. 200 per K. W. p.a. by Board - (A) Construction of (Interpretation) agreement - Precarious in regard to period of operation and terminable by Board - (B) Tariff increased except where consumer had protection of agreement u/s.49(3) which prevented a unilateral increase - Differential treatment - Constitutionality - Held, it cannot be construed as one which was intended to give statutory protection for tariff by means of special agreement by exercise of statutory power of Board u/s.49(3) - No discrimination, subsistence of agreement u/s.49(3) can form valid ground for differential treatment - Appeal dismissed.
