A.C. Ananthaswamy & Others v Boraiah (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

20 August 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 639; (2004) 8 SCC 588; 2004 (57) ALR 255; 2004 (4) AWC 3016; JT 2004 (8) SC 581; 2004 (138) PLR 557; 2004 (4) RCR(Civil) 243; 2004(7) SCALE 80; [2004] Supp3 S.C.R. 649

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Ex-Parte

Summary: Civil Procedure - CPC, 1908, s. 9 and O. 9 r. 13 - Non service of summons - Whether non service of summons constitute fraud or misrepresentation? - Held, fraud is to be pleaded and proved - Level of proof required in such cases is extremely higher - No evidence of fraud - Where only fraud alleged is a bare non service of summons then such a suit to set aside decree on alleged ground of fraud was not maintainable - No substantial question of law arises - Appeal dismissed.
