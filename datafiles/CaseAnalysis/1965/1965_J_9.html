Â State of Bombay v Jagmohandas and Another

Supreme Court of India

19 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 68; AIR 1966 SC 1412; [1966] 60 ITR 206; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 279; 1966 (17) STC 529

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indirect Tax - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946, ss. 13 and 20 - Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1952, ss. 19 and 29 - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 67 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 286(1)(a) - Refund - Advance sales tax - Amount was paid as advance tax in respect of sale of goods effected outside State of Bombay - These sales were taxable under Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946 - That Act became void by virtue of art. 286(1)(a) and this amount was paid under a mistake of law, after realising that they had committed a mistake filed suits for refund - Mistake was discovered when Governor of Bombay promulgated Bombay Ordinance No. 2 of 1952 - Whether suits could be barred by limitation? - S. 13 of Bombay Sales Tax Act does not create an implied bar - Held, suits were not barred either expressly by provisions of s. 20 or impliedly by s. 13 of Bombay Sales Tax Act, 1946 - Suits were also not barred by law of limitation, since suits were filed within period prescribed by art. 96 of Sch.1 of Limitation Act, 1908 - Appeal dismissed.
