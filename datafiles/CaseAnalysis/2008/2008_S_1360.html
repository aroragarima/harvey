Gaurav Nagpal v Sumedha Nagpal

Supreme Court of India

19 November 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1872; (2009) 1 SCC 42; AIR 2009 SC 557; 2009(1) ALL MR 925; 2009 (74) ALR 474; 2009 (1) AWC 281; 2009 (1) Bom.C.R. 58; 2009 (6) CTC 497; 2009 (2) JhrCR(SC) 162; JT 2008 (12) SC 115; 2008 (6) Law Herald (P&H) 4484; 2009 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 91; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 1036; 2009 (2) MPLJ 504; 2009 (153) PLR 113; 2008 (4) RCR(Civil) 928; 2008(14) SCALE 228; [2008] 16 S.C.R. 396

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure; Women & Children

Keywords: Custody Of Children, Undivided Interest, Welfare Of Minor, Children s, Powers Of Court, Step- Mother, Hindu Marriage Act, 1950

Summary: Hindu Minority and Guardianship Act, 1956; Guardians and Wards Act, 1890 - Held, in the appointment or declaration of any person as guardian of a Hindu minor by a court, the welfare of the minor shall be the paramount consideration - In determining the question as to who should be given custody of a minor child, the paramount consideration is the 'welfare of the child' and not rights of the parents under a statute for the time being in force - Due regard has of course to be given to the right of the father as natural guardian but if the custody of the father cannot promote the welfare of the children, he may be refused such guardianship - Appeal dismissed.
