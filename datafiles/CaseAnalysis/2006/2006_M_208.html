Rashtriya Ispat Nigam Limited and Another v Messrs Verma Transport Company

Supreme Court of India

08 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Dalveer Bhandari

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 392; (2006) 7 SCC 275; AIR 2006 SC 2800; 2006 (4) AWC 4011; 2007 (6) CompLJ 240; JT 2006 (7) SC 404; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1310; 2006 (4) RCR(Civil) 478; 2006(7) SCALE 565; [2006] Supp4 S.C.R. 332

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Foreign Award, Geneva Convention

Summary: [A.] Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, (the Act), s. 8 - CPC, 1908, o. 39, rr. 1, 2, ss. 151, 94 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 34 - English Arbitration Act, 1899, s. 4 - Arbitration (Protocol and Convention) Act, 1937, s. 3 - Foreign Awards (Recognition and Enforcement) Act, 1961, s. 3 - Interpretation and application of s. 8 of the Act - Service of notice under arbitration agreement is not mandatory - Respondent firm already filed suit for grant of permanent injunction restraining Appellants from in any manner blacklisting Respondent-firm or terminating the contract - Filing of reply to injunction application, could not be a ground to refuse to entertain plea taken by appellants that suit should be referred to arbitral tribunal; more so when in its application filed u/s. 8, Appellants questioned jurisdiction of Court in proceeding with matter - Expression 'first statement on the substance of the dispute' u/s. 8(1),must be distinguished from the expression 'written statement' - It implies submission of party to jurisdiction of judicial authority - Both causes of action, illegal termination of contract and blacklisting of the firm, arose out of terms of contract thus s. 8 validly attracted - Finding by High Court that Respondent - firm had been blacklisted without any notice, not proper as only notice to show cause in that regard had been issued, final decision in regard to blacklisting was yet to be taken - Appeals allowed.
