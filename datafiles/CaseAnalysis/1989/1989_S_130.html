Aphali Pharmaceuticals Ltd. v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, G.L. Oza

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 230; (1989) 4 SCC 378; AIR 1989 SC 2227; 1989 (3) CompLJ 227; 1989 (44) E.L.T. 613; JT 1989 (3) SC 720; 1989(2) SCALE 617; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 129; 1991 (81) STC 113; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 129

Case Digest

Subject: Excise

Keywords: Enactment, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, Design Act, 1911, Finance (No. 2) Act, 1962, Medicinal And Toilet Prepratiuons (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Rules 1956, Drugs[and Cosmetics] Act, 1940, Drugs (Amendment) Act, 1955, Drugs and Cosmetics (Amendment) Act, 1964

Summary: Excise - Medicinal and Toilet Preparations (Excise Duties) Act, 1955, sch., items 1 and 3, expln. - Interpretation of Statutes - Internal aid - Proviso. - Ayurvedic medicinal preparation known as 'Ashvagandhaarist' - Containing self-generated alcohol - Not consumable as ordinary alcoholic beverage - Covered under item 1 and not item 3 of 1955 Act - No duty is payable on it - (A) Whether the scope of the explanation can be enlarged by circular, issued by Director of Prohibition and Central Excise - (B) Whether an explanation to Schedule amounts to an explanation in the Act itself - Scope of explanation in the Act held, cannot be enlarged by issuing circular - In case of conflict between body of Act and Schedule, the former prevails - Appeal allowed.
