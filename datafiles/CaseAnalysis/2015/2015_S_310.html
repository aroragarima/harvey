State of Kerala and others v S. Unnikrishnan Nair and others

Supreme Court of India

13 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 567; (2015) 9 SCC 639; AIR 2015 SC 3351; 2015 CRLJ 4495; 2016 (1) JCC 25; JT 2015 (7) SC 357; 2015 (4) MLJ(Crl) 122; 2015(9) SCALE 7; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 56

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Instigation

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 306 - Abatement to suicide - Deceased was CIO in lock death case of Sampath and while investigation was in progress, deceased committed suicide leaving behind suicide note to effect that respondent nos. 1 and 2, who were team of officers assisting him, were responsible for his situation and also advocate had some role and that CJM had put pressure on him - On basis of said suicide note, criminal law was set in motion arraying respondent nos. 1 and 2 as accused and aggrieved, respondents approached HC u/s. 482 of CrPC and HC quashed FIR against respondents by its impugned order - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC is justified in quashing FIR lodged against respondents for offence punishable u/s. 306 of IPC.

Held, suicide note really does not state about any continuous conduct of harassment. Hence, HC is justified in quashing proceedings. V.P.Shrivastava v. Indian Explosives Limited and Others, 2010 INDLAW SC 780, followed. Appeal dismissed.

Ratio - Where no prima facie case is made out against the accused, then HC is obliged in law to exercise the jurisdiction u/s. 482 of CrPC and quash the proceedings.
