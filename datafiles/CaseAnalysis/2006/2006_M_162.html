Jaswant Singh Gill v Messrs Bharat Coking Coal Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 841; (2007) 1 SCC (L&S) 584; (2007) 1 SCC 663; 2007 (5) AWC 5227; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 129; JT 2007 (1) SC 6; 2007 (2) LLJ 206; 2006(11) SCALE 624; 2007 (2) SLR 319; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 1064

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Orissa State Financial Corporation Employees' Provident Fund Regulations, 1959, Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978

Summary: Payment of Gratuity Act, 1972 - Appellant joined as a Chief General Manager in respondent company - Charge sheet against him - Appellant was allowed to retire during pendency of the departmental proceeding - Gratuity - Whether provisions of the Act shall prevail over the rules framed by Coal India Limited, holding company of Respondent No. 1, known as Coal India Executives' Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978? - Held, Rules framed by the Coal India Limited are not statutory rules - They have been made by the holding company of Respondent No. 1. - Provisions of the Act, therefore, must prevail over the Rules - Disciplinary authority has not quantified the loss or damage - It was not found that the damages or loss caused to Respondent No. 1 was more than the amount of gratuity payable to the appellant - Appeal allowed.
