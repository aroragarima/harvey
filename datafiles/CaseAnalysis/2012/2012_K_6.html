K. Venkateshwarlu v State of Andhra Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2012 Indlaw SC 372; (2012) 8 SCC 73; AIR 2012 SC 2955; 2012 CRLJ 4388; 2012 (4) JCC 2758; 2012 (3) RCR(Criminal) 990; 2013 (1) RLW 176

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.376 - Rape - Reversal of acquittal - Challenged - Appellant/accused allegedly committed rape on PW no.2/victim - Trial Court acquitted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC on the ground that victim and her mother did not speak anything about rape and child witnesses stated that they were kept by police in police station prior to giving evidence and therefore, their evidence could not be relied upon - On appeal, HC convicted appellant for offence punishable u/s. 376 of IPC and held that prosecution proved its case beyond reasonable doubt - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended that HC had to bear in mind that presumption of innocence of an accused was strengthened by his acquittal and unless there were strong and compelling circumstances which rebut that presumption and conclusively establish guilt of accused, the order of acquittal could not be set aside - Held, evidence of child witness had to be subjected to closest scrutiny and could be accepted only if court comes to conclusion that child understands the question put to him and he is capable of giving rational answers - In instant case, evidence of child witnesses PW nos. 4, 5, 6 and 7 had made prosecution case suspect and these statements could not be relied upon because there was intrinsic evidence to show that all these witnesses were under pressure of police - Further, Trial Court had rightly discarded evidence as unworthy of reliance and HC erred in taking it into consideration - It was a case where neither evidence of parents of victim PW no. 2 nor evidence of PW no. 2 nor evidence of child witnesses, who claim to have witnessed incident, nor medical evidence supported prosecution case - Besides, all pancha witnesses had turned hostile - A needle of suspicion did point out to the appellant because he was a police constable and in a small village where the incident took place, witnesses might be scared to depose against him because of his clout - Impugned order of conviction was set aside - Appeal disposed of.
