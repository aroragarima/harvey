Ahmedabad Manufacturing and Calico Printing Company Limited v Workmen and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.B. Misra, A.D. Koshal

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 418; (1981) 2 SCC 663; (1982) SCC (L&S) 36; AIR 1981 SC 960; 1981 (42) FLR 309; 1981 (1) LLJ 489; 1981 (1) LLN 563; 1981(1) SCALE 515; [1981] 3 S.C.R. 213; 1981 UJ 350

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 136 and 226 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Unconditional withdrawal of special leave petition u/art. 136 - Subsequent writ petition u/art. 226, dismissed - If discretionary - Permission to withdraw a leave petition cannot be equated with an order of its dismissal - And High Court has not exercised a proper and sound discretion in dismissing writ petition in limine on sole ground that application for special leave on same facts and grounds had been withdrawn unconditionally - Held, it is wrong exercise of discretion - Dismissal is illegal as it does not amount to res judicata - Appeal allowed.
