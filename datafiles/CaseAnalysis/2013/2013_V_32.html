Vinod Raghuvanshi v Ajay Arora and others

Supreme Court of India

23 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 891; (2013) 10 SCC 581; 2013 (4) JCC 2782; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 256

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860,ss.420,467,468,471,120-B - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973,s.216 - Respondent no. 1 filed criminal complaint u/ss.420, 467, 468, 471 and 120-B IPC against nine partners of reconstituted firm alleging that said partners had replaced Deed of Partnership in bank, and forged partnership deed was implanted in excise office in its place to deprive him of profits of firm - CJM recorded statement of complainant and after considering pre-charge evidence, registered case against appellant and two others u/ss.420 and 120- B IPC - Appellant filed application for quashing of complaint - HC dismissed said application - Hence, instant Appeal.

Held, in complaint filed by respondent no.1 in the court of CJM, serious allegations of cheating by replacing partnership deed by forged partnership deed with connivance of appellant and other officers in his office were made. Particulars had been furnished to establish that partnership deed was forged document. Record of Excise Commissioner, makes it evident that excise auction for year 2003-04 had been on basis of partnership deed and said deed was on record. As regards what had happened in office of appellant and who had done it and whether appellant can be held responsible for same, would depend upon evidence adduced in Court. When prosecution at initial stage is to be quashed, test to be applied by Court is whether uncontroverted allegations as made, prima facie establish offence. At this stage neither court can embark upon inquiry, whether allegations in complaint are likely to be established by evidence or nor court should judge the probability, reliability or genuineness of allegations made therein. Charge sheet filed or charges framed at the initial stage can be altered/amended or charge can be added at subsequent stage, after the evidence is adduced in view of provisions of s.216 of CrPC. Thus, there is no cogent reason to interfere with impugned complaint or orders impugned. Appeal dismissed.
