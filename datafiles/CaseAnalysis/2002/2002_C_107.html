Flash Laboratories Ltd. v Collector of Central Excise, New Delhi

Supreme Court of India

20 December 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1570; (2003) 2 SCC 86; AIR 2003 SC 1894; [2003] 116 Comp Cas 577; 2003 (85) ECC 510; 2003 (106) ECR 631; 2003 (151) E.L.T. 241; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 366; 2003 (54) RLT 1; 2002(9) SCALE 686; 2003 (1) SCW 1368; 2003 (1) SLT 118; [2002] Supp5 S.C.R. 698; 2003 (1) Supreme 478

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Keywords: Excisability of goods

Summary: Excise - Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985 - Allegation that Messrs Parle Products Limited and Messrs Parle Biscuits Limited were 'related persons' and were purchasing goods at lower prices and selling the same at higher prices and that the appellant had not filed the price list in Part IV for the sale to a related person, rather they filed the price list in Part I - Meaning of the definition of 'related person' - Held, a related person is a person who is so associated with the assessee that they have interest, direct or indirect, in the business of each other and includes a holding company or a subsidiary company and is being used in sufficient restricted sense for employing a legal fiction - There is mutuality of interest between the three companies as sixty per cent of the products of appellant are sold to Messrs Parle Products Limited and the remaining forty per cent of the total products of tooth paste is being sold to Messrs Parle Biscuits Limited - No reason to disagree with the views expressed by the Tribunal - Appeals Dismissed.
