Ramesh Kumar v State of Chhattisgarh

Supreme Court of India

17 October 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

R.C. Lahoti, K.G. Balakrishnan, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 115; (2001) 9 SCC 618; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1088; AIR 2001 SC 3837; 2001 (4) CCR 178; 2001 (4) Crimes 360; 2001 CRLJ 4724; 2002 (1) JCC 87; JT 2001 (8) SC 599; 2001 (4) RCR(Criminal) 537; 2001(7) SCALE 298; 2001 (5) SCJ 94; 2001 (7) SLT 356; [2001] Supp4 S.C.R. 247; 2001 (7) Supreme 737

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction, Abetment of suicide, Sustainability

Summary: Criminal - Indian penal Code, 1860, ss. 306, 498A - Abetment of suicide - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants accused (husband and in-laws) harassed and mal treated deceased for demand of dowry - Accused compelled deceased to commit suicide - Accused were charged for offence punishable u/ss. 306, 498A IPC _ Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused husband and acquitted accused in laws - Appeal before HC, which was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by Trial Court was sustainable.

Held, there is no evidence and material available on record wherefrom an inference of the accused having abetted the commission of suicide by deceased may necessarily be drawn. The totality of the circumstances, especially the dying-declaration and the suicide notes left by deceased herself, which fall for consideration within the expression 'all the other circumstances of the case' employed, do not permit the presumption there under being raised against the accused. The accused , therefore deserves to be acquitted of the charge u/s. 306 IPC. Hence, conviction of accused u/s. 306 IPC and sentence passed thereon are set aside. His conviction u/s. 498A IPC and sentence passed thereon are maintained. Appeal partly allowed.
