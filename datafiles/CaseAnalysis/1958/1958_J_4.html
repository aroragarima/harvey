Union of India v Jeewan Ram

Supreme Court of India

13 March 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

S.K. Das, Sudhi Ranjan Das, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar, A.K. Sarkar

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 211; AIR 1958 SC 905

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Service - Railway Establishment Code, r. 1702 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 240 - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, s. 80 - Respondent, who was plaintiff in court of first instance, was appointed an assistant booking clerk at Ajmer - Respondent received a memorandum from Station Master, Ajmer, to effect that he had been placed under suspension from that date under orders of Traffic Superintendent, Ajmer - A charge sheet was drawn up against respondent with regard to allegations referred to above and he was directed to show cause why he should not be punished with dismissal from service u/r. 1702 of Indian Railway Establishment Code, Vol. 1 - This charge sheet was served and he was asked to submit his written explanation within seven days - Respondent submitted his explanation - An order was passed against respondent stating that he would be given one month's pay in lieu of notice of removal from service - Whether such order is penal order? - Held, order itself stated in cl. (c) that respondent was deprived of half of his pay during his period of suspension, this makes it abundantly clear that order was a penal order because if order were a termination of service in exercise of contracted right only, only question before the court is if order D/- 16-3-1949, imposes penal consequences - It clearly does, because it withholds part of pay of respondent during period of suspension - In cl. (d) impugned order stated that respondent had a right of appeal u/r. 1717, that also shows that order was clearly intended to be a penal order, that is, an order only by way of punishment and principles laid down in two decisions in cases of Parshotam Lal Dhingra v. UOI, (1957 Indlaw SC 103) and Khem Chand v. UOI, (1957 Indlaw SC 12) should apply in present case - Impugned order clearly contravened provisions of s. 240(3) of Government of India Act, 1935, it was illegal and ineffective - Appeal dismissed.
