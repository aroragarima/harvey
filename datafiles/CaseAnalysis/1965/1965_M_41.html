Messrs British Paints (India) Limited v Its Workmen

Supreme Court of India

04 November 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, P. Satyanarayana Raju

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 223; AIR 1966 SC 732; 1966 (12) FLR 145; 1966 (1) LLJ 407; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 523

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Two matters in dispute between management and workmen were referred to tribunal relating to (i) age of retirement of workmen at head office and factory of company and (ii) introduction of gratuity scheme for workmen employed at head office and factory - Tribunal fixed age of retirement for clerical and subordinate staff at 58 years and for workmen in factory a 55 years - Tribunal also introduced a gratuity scheme of five years - (A) Whether age of retirement in case of factory workmen in present company should be fixed at age of 60 years? - Considering general improvement in standard of health in this country and also considering that longevity has increased, fixation of age of retirement at 60 years appears to be quite reasonable in present circumstances - Held, age should be fixed at 60 - Therefore even in case of future factory-workmen no reason why age of retirement should be fixed at a lower level - (B) Whether there should be difference in age of retirement of factory workers and clerical and subordinate staffs? - Held, there is no reason for making a difference in age of retirement as between clerical and subordinate staff on one hand and factory workmen on other, unless such differences can be justified on cogent and valid grounds - Present company is a paints manufacturing company, no arduous work for workmen as compared to work of clerical and subordinate staff - (C) Whether tribunal was correct in prescribing five years as minimum period of qualifying service for gratuity? - Reason for providing a longer minimum period for earning gratuity in case of voluntary retirement or resignation is to see that workmen do not leave one concern after another after putting short minimum service qualifying for gratuity - Further held, gratuity scheme is modified in this regard and order that in case of voluntary retirement or resignation by an employee before reaching age of superannuation, minimum period of qualifying service for gratuity should be ten years, and not five years as prescribed by tribunal - Appeals partly allowed.
