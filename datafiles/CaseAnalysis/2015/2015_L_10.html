Lalaram and others v Jaipur Development Authority and another

Supreme Court of India

01 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Amitava Roy, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 809; JT 2015 (11) SC 421; 2015 (8) MLJ 807; 2015(13) SCALE 78

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Land Acquisition, Preventive Detention Act, 1950, Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, Airports Authority Of India Act, 1994, Jaipur Development Authority Act, 1982, Goa (Prohibition of Further Payment and Recovery of Rebate Benefits) Act, 2002, Rajasthan Improvement Trust (Disposal of Urban Land) Rules, 1974, Airports Authority of India (Management of Airports) Regulations, 2003, Rajasthan Rules of Business

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950 - Compensation for acquisition of land - Notification for acquisition of land of appellants for purpose of Army was issued on 8-5-1981 and award was passed by LAO on 26-3-1983 and possession was taken over on 26-3-1983 - Later, compensation awarded was challenged and Reference Court fixed the same at Rs.15000 per bigha - However, amount of compensation deposited in Court was not at enhanced rate fixed by Reference Court and affirmed by HC but at Rs.1500 per bigha as awarded by LAO - Meanwhile, State Govt. issued circulars whereby it was decided that, in cases where compensation amount awarded had not been paid, though award had been passed, opportunity to khatedars (land owners) to opt for developed land ought to be afforded and on basis of merit of such claims, 15%developed land be allotted to them and option was made valid till 31-3-2001 and later through another circular, khatedars were left at liberty to exercise their option till 28-2-2002 to be allotted 15% developed land in the scheme area by allotment committee of concerned organization, after the approval from the State - In pursuance of the same, appellants exercised their options and submitted applications within time allotted and recorded their request for 15% developed land in Vidyadhar Nagar Scheme - While so, JDA (Jaipur Development Authority) issued auction notice for sale of plots in Vidyadhar Nagar Scheme and appellants challenged the same before Tribunal alleging discrimination on ground that persons similarly situated like appellants, had been allotted developed lands in Vidyadhar Nagar Scheme, while they were sought to be deprived by assailed initiative to auction the land within the said scheme - Tribunal by its order dt.18-8-2003, annulled auction notice and held that JDA could not sell or auction plots till appellants were allotted 15% developed land in Vidyadhar nagar Scheme - Aggrieved, JDA filed appeal before HC but the same was dismissed - However, on 1-7-2005, letter was addressed to JDA by State Govt., offering allotment of land to khatedars at villages Lalchandpura and Anandtpura - Aggrieved, appellants again approached Tribunal afresh and Tribunal held that appellants were entitled to 15% developed land in Vidyadhar Nagar Scheme - However, JDA had not taken any action and so, appellants approached HC through writ petition complaining of non-compliance of operative directions contained in judgment and order dt.18-10-2005 of Tribunal - Single Judge of HC passed directions to JDA to comply with directions and aggrieved, JDA filed special appeal but the same was dismissed - Aggrieved, JDA filed SLP before SC and during said period, JDA filed appeal against order of Tribunal dt.18-10-2005 before HC by filing writ petition but the same was dismissed - However, on appeal, DB of HC held that decision impugned before Tribunal was beyond its jurisdiction and it was not open for Tribunal to direct respondents for allotment of land at Vidyadhar nagar - Further, appellants were left at liberty to receive amount of compensation awarded at Rs.15000 per bigha which was deposited - Aggrieved by orders of DB of HC, instant appeals were preferred - Whether impugned judgment of DB of HC could be upheld.

Held, in view of overwhelming and unambiguous verified averments made on behalf of the State as well as the sequence of orders/circulars on the issue involved, at relevant point of time, i.e., 13-12-2001, the departmental minister was in exclusive charge and was competent to take a final decision the issue of acquisition of land, release thereof from acquisition and allotment of land in lieu of compensation and thus, said circular does represent an enforceable state policy. Further, annulment of circular dt.13-12-2001 only on the ground of its non conformance with the mandate of arts.166(1) and 166(2) of Constitution, without any reference to Rules of Business u/art. 166(3), could not be accepted. Furthermore, no plea was either raised or examined, based on its repugnance with the Rules of Business u/art. 166(3). On a concatenation of the stream of events, traced from the acquisition of land involved, circular dt.13-12-2001 is indeed a policy decision of the State Govt., regarding the allotment of developed land in lieu of compensation to the persons and is enforceable against it. Further, process leading to allotment of land at Lalchandpura and Anantpur villages, originated from circular dt.13-12-2001, and received approval of Chief Minister at an appropriate stage. Hence, all concerned State functionaries were not only aware of relevance and obligatory bearing of said circular, but also had participated in exercise, contemplated by it for allotment of developed land in lieu of compensation. The respondents, in the totality of the existent facts and circumstances are stopped from questioning the statutes and efficacy of said circular in vesting right in appellants to claim their due in law there under. Hence, appellants are entitled to be allotted their quota of 15% developed land in terms of policy/circular dt.13-12-2001 in one or more available plots at Vidyadhar Nagar, Gokul Nagar, Truck Terminal and Vaishali Nagar as enumerated by them in their affidavit dt.17-8-2015 and respondents are directed to accommodate appellants accordingly. Hence, mpugned judgment and order is set aside. Appeals allowed.
