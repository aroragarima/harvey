Pyare Mohan Lal v State of Jharkhand and others

Supreme Court of India

10 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, J.M. Panchal, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 738; (2010) 10 SCC 693; AIR 2010 SC 3753; 2010(6) ALL MR 910; 2010 (4) ESC 590; 2010 (127) FLR 402; 2010 (4) JhrCR(SC) 93; JT 2010 (10) SC 502; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 143; 2011(1) M.P.H.T. 213; 2011 (1) RLW 321; 2010(9) SCALE 528; [2010] 11 S.C.R. 216; 2010 (6) SLR 4

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Continuance In Service, Jharkhand Civil Services Code

Summary: Service - Compulsory retirement - Validity - Petitioner/Munsif in Bihar Judicial Service was promoted to the post of Subordinate Judge - Consequent to the bifurcation of the State of Bihar and formation of the State of Jharkhand, the services of the petitioner were allocated to the Jharkhand State by the order of the Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pension - Petitioner was appointed as a Sub-Judge in Jharkhand, and, subsequently, the petitioner was placed at the disposal of the State of Jharkhand as Under Secretary-cum-Deputy Legal Remembrancer and Law Officer in the Law Department - Jharkhand HC recommended the name of the petitioner along with others for promotion to the post of Additional District Judge on ad hoc basis - Jharkhand HC on administrative side recommended compulsory retirement of six judicial officers including the petitioner, and in pursuance thereof, the 2nd respondent issued a consequential order of compulsory retirement of the petitioner, in public interest, invoking the provisions of r. 74(b)(ii) of the Jharkhand Civil Services Code along with five other judicial officers - Hence the present writ petition - Whether order of compulsory retirement of petitioner was valid - Held, service record of the petitioner indicated that petitioner remained an average officer throughout his service career and could never improve - Petitioner was given adverse entries regarding his integrity/reputation as not good in the relevant years and remarks to that effect by the Inspecting Judges - Petitioner did not challenge the said adverse entries, rather he considered it appropriate to challenge only the order of compulsory retirement which had been a consequential effect of such adverse entries - A single adverse entry regarding the integrity of an officer even in remote past is sufficient to award compulsory retirement - Petitioner's case was considered by a Committee of Judges of the HC duly constituted by the Chief Justice and then the report of the Committee was placed before the Full Court - A decision was taken by the Full Court after due deliberation on the matter, therefore, there was hardly any chance to allege non-application of mind or mala fide - Promotion of petitioner as Additional District Judge on ad hoc basis was not definitely a promotion on merit - HC objectively decided to recommend his compulsory retirement and the State Authorities acted accordingly - No fault can be found with the decision making process or with the decision with respect to compulsory retirement - No reason to interfere with the impugned order - Petition dismissed.
