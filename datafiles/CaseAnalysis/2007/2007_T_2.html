Abdul Raheem v Karnataka Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 November 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1149; (2007) 14 SCC 138; AIR 2008 SC 956; 2008 (70) ALR 158; 2007 (7) AWC 7180; JT 2007 (13) SC 63; 2008 (2) KarLJ 445; 2008 (2) MLJ(SC) 922; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 105; 2007(13) SCALE 336; 2007 (8) SCJ 597; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 389

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Suit for specific performance of contract - An agreement for sale was entered into by and between the defendant - appellant and the plaintiff - Respondent No. 1 - Suit dismissed - High Court held that it was for the defendant to bear the conversion expenses. Admittedly when the amount of Rs. 94, 000/- and odd was paid to him, he was bound to perform his part of contract by executing a deed of sale in favour of the plaintiff respondent No. 1 - Appeal against - Held, reversal of a finding of fact arrived at by the First Appellate Court ignoring vital documents may lead to a substantial question of law - There cannot be any doubt whatsoever that consideration of irrelevant fact and non-consideration of relevant fact would give rise to a substantial question of law - Remitted matter back to the High Court for consideration of the matter afresh upon formulation of a substantial question of law - Appeal allowed.
