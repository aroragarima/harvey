Rotork Controls India Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Chennai

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 638; (2009) 13 SCC 283; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1697; [2009] 314 ITR 62; 2009(7) SCALE 456; [2009] 8 S.C.R. 1152

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1975, Approved Gratuity Fund, Annuity Policy, Annuity, Income From Assessment, Payments Not Deductible

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 28, 37, 40A(7), 145 - Assessment years 1991-92, 1992-93, 1993-94 and 1994- 95 - Deduction u/s. 37 of Act - Warranty claims - Appellant-company sells Valve Actuators - At time of sale appellant (assessee) provides a Standard Warranty whereby in event of any Beacon Rotork Actuator or part thereof becoming defective within 12 months from date of commissioning or 18 months from date of despatch whichever is earlier, company undertakes to rectify or replace defective part free of charge - For assessment year 1991-92, assessee made a provision for warranty at Rs.10, 18, 800/- at rate of 1.5% of turnover - Said provision was made by assessee on account of warranty claims likely to arise on sales effected by appellant and to cover up that expenditure - As provision made was for Rs.10, 18, 800/- which exceeded actual expenditure, appellant reversed Rs.5, 00, 246 as Reversal of Excess Provision - Consequently, assessee claimed deduction in respect of net provision of Rs.5, 18, 554/- which was disallowed by Assessing Officer on ground that liability was merely a contingent liability not allowable as a deduction u/s. 37 of Act, 1961 - Same was upheld by CIT (A) - On appeal, by applying Rule of Consistency, Tribunal held that assessee was entitled to deduction u/s. 37 of 1961, Act in respect of provision for warranty amounting to Rs.5, 18, 554 - Dept. filed appeal against said order in Madras HC - HC held that assessee was not entitled to deduction in respect of provision made for warranty claims on ground that no obligation was ever cast on date of sale and consequently there was no accrued liability - Whether HC decision could be upheld and assessee was not entitled to deduction u/s. 37 of Act in respect of warranty claims? - If historical trend indicates that large number of sophisticated goods were being manufactured in past and in past if facts established show that defects existed in some of items manufactured and sold then provision made for warranty in respect of army of such sophisticated goods would be entitled to deduction from gross receipts u/s. 37 of 1961 Act - Held, a provision is a liability which can be measured only by using a substantial degree of estimation - A provision is recognized when: (a) an enterprise has a present obligation as a result of a past event; (b) it is probable that an outflow of resources will be required to settle obligation; and (c) a reliable estimate can be made of amount of obligation, if these conditions are not met, no provision can be recognized - Warranty provision needs to be recognized because appellant is an enterprise having a present obligation as a result of past events resulting in an outflow of resources - Lastly, a reliable estimate can be made of amount of obligation - Hence, all three conditions for recognition of a provision are satisfied in this case - Moreover, HC failed to notice 'reversal' which constituted part of data systematically maintained by assessee over last decade - Hence, impugned HC judgement set aside - Appellants are entitled to deduction of warranty claims u/s. 37 of Act - Appeals allowed.
