K. Guruprasad Rao v State of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

01 July 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, Ranjana Prakash Desai

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 628; (2013) 8 SCC 418; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 696; 2013(8) SCALE 629; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 581

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Rehabilitation, Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1904, Rajasthan Forest Act, 1953, Mineral Conservation and Development Rules, 1988, Provisions of the Panchayats (Extension to the Scheduled Areas) Act, 1996, Mysore Ancient Monuments Preservation Act, 1925, Ancient Monuments Protection Act, 1882, Federal Antiquities Act, Karnataka Ancient and Historical Monuments and Archeological Sites and Remains Act, 1961, Rajasthan Wild Animals and Birds Protection Act, 1951, Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Bill, Karnataka Ancient and Historical Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Rules, 1966, Karnataka Historical and Archaeological Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Rules, 1968

Summary: (A) Environment, Wildlife & Animal -- Mines & Minerals - Karnataka Ancient and Historical Monuments and Archeological Sites and Remains Act, 1961 s. 4 - Forest (Conservation) Act, 1980 s.2 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 s. 133 - Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Rules, 1959 r.10 - Ancient Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Act, 1958 s.38 - Karnataka Ancient and Historical Monuments and Archaeological Sites and Remains Rules, 1966 rr. 11 to 15 - Protected monument - 'Protected Area' - 'Safe Zone' - Mining activity - Caused damage to structure of temple - License - Two temples for whose protection appellant had made efforts for last many years and temple was built with massive granite blocks in typical trabeate system - Temple was declared as protected monument by Govt. of Karnataka u/s.4 of 1961 Act - By notification, area of 9 acres 12 cents in all four sides of temple was declared as 'Protected Area' - By another notification, State Govt. declared area within radius of 200 meters from periphery and precincts of one temple as 'Safe Zone', where no mining activity could be conducted - During currency of mining lease, lessee died and his wife was permitted to carry on mining operations in name of respondent no.4 - Director of ancient monuments inspected and found that mining activity was causing damage to structure of temple - Assistant Commissioner sent letter to respondent no.4 and issued notice to respondent no.4 that if needful was not done, action would be taken u/s.133 of CrPC - Appellant filed petition - HC accepted report of committee and dismissed petition - Hence, instant Appeal -Whether respondents could obtain license for permission to undertake mining operations in protected /or regulated area - Held, both Central Govt. and State Govt. framed rules for grant of permission/license in prescribed form to undertake any mining operations in protected and/or regulated area - R.10 of 1959 Rules, which was framed u/s. 38 of 1958 Act and rr.11 to 15 of 1966 Rules provided that, no person should undertake any mining operations in regulated area other than on strength of license granted by competent authority - However, material placed on record did not show that private respondents had obtained such license u/ 1966 rr. for permission to undertake mining operations within prohibited and/or regulated area - Therefore, could not be allowed to operate mines in protected and/or regulated area - Further, argument of private respondents did not found any merit because committee had thoroughly scrutinized reports sent by two expert bodies, i.e., CIMFR, and NIT and then decided that area surrounding temple should be divided into two zones, i.e., core zone and buffer zone and there should be total ban on mining within core zone while mining be permitted in buffer zone under supervision of expert body - Appeal allowed.

(B) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Mines & Minerals - Ban on mining operation - Cause financial loss - Sustainable development - Whether ban on mining operations/activities in core zone would impact on iron ore supply and would cause financial loss - Held, argument of State and private respondents that ban on mining operations/activities in core zone would adversely impact on iron ore supply and would cause financial loss to leaseholders as well as State appears - However, larger public interest and interest of future generations, did not think that this would be very heavy price to be paid by some individuals and State - Thus, Court had often used principle of sustainable development to balance requirement of development and environmental protection and issued several directions for protection of natural resources including air, water, forest, flora and fauna as also wildlife - Court had recognized that right to development includes whole spectrum of civil, cultural, economic, political and social process, for improvement of peoples well being and realization of their full potential - Appeal allowed.

(D) Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Mines & Minerals - Creation of core zone and buffer zone - Whether recommendations and suggestions made by Committee for creation of core zone and buffer zone could create balance with benefits which might accrued from mining and other development related activities - Held, protection of ancient monuments had necessarily to be kept in mind while carrying out development activities - Protection and preservation of ancient monuments for benefit of future generations had to be balanced with benefits which might accrued from mining and other development related activities - However, recommendations and suggestions made by committee for creation of core zone and buffer zone create that balance - Thus, mining activity was to create financial wealth for leaseholders and also State, immense cultural and historic wealth, not to mention wealth of information which temple provides could not be ignored and every effort had to be made to protect temple - Appeal allowed.

(E) Environment, Wildlife & Animal, Mines & Minerals - Use of ripper dozer and rock dozer -Whether use of Ripper Dozer and Rock Breaker would produce vibration which might cause harm to temple - Held, submission of respondent did not merit acceptance because in paragraph 6 of suggestions, committee appointed by Court had already indicated that mining in Buffer Zone might be permitted with controlled blasting or without blasting by using Ripper Dozer/Rock Breaker or any other machinery and taking adequate measures towards generation, propagation, suppression and deposition of airborne dust to be closely monitored by experts from IBM - Appeal allowed.
