R.K. Parvatharaj Gupta v K.C. Jayadeva Reddy

Supreme Court of India

02 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 39; (2006) 2 SCC 428; 2006 (63) ALR 143; 2006 (2) AWC 1336; 2006 (1) CTC 828; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 240; JT 2006 (2) SC 156; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 110; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 779; 2006(2) SCALE 156; 2006 (2) SCJ 239; [2006] 1 S.C.R. 1059; 2006 (Supp) RD 379

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Contract & Commercial - Limitation - Limitation Act, 1963, art. 54 - Suit for specific performance - Suit dismissed as time barred by lower court - Second Appeal by respondent allowed decreeing suit for specific performance - Appeal against - Held, even though time for performance was not fixed in agreement for sale, on receipt of notice, respondent had notice that performance was being refused, if he failed to fulfil his obligation under contract within 15 days of receipt of notice - Suit should have been filed within a period of three years from date of expiry of fifteen days from date of receipt of said notice - Suit time barred - Appeal allowed.
