Carona Limited v Messrs Parvathy Swaminathan and Sons

Supreme Court of India

05 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 994; (2007) 8 SCC 559; AIR 2008 SC 187; 2007 (6) Bom.C.R. 801; 2008 (1) DRTC 428; JT 2007 (11) SC 484; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 51; 2007 (2) RCR(Rent) 489; 2007(11) SCALE 630; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 656

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Bombay Rents, Hotel and Lodging House Rates Control (Amendment) Act, 1943

Summary: Maharashtra Rent Control Act, 1999 - Decree of eviction - Appeal against - Constitutional validity of s. 3(1) (b) - Held, s. 3(1)(b) clearly provides that "the said Act shall not apply to any premises let or sub-let to banks, or any Public Sector Undertaking or any Corporation established by or under any Central or State Act, or foreign missions, international agencies, multinational companies, and private limited companies and public limited companies having a paid up share capital of rupees one crore or more - Courts below were right in holding that the date on which tenancy was determined, the right in favour of the landlord got accrued - Such right could not have been set at naught by the tenant by unilateral act by passing a resolution to reduce 'paid up share capital' of the Company - Appeal dismissed.
