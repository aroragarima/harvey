S. K. L. Company v Chief Commercial Officer and others

Supreme Court of India

29 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Vikramajit Sen, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 911; AIR 2016 SC 193; 2016 (1) AWC 911; 2016 (1) BC 454; JT 2016 (1) SC 20; 2016 (2) KarLJ 545; 2016 (1) MLJ 473; 2016(1) SCALE 16

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Auction, Railways Act, 1989, Capacity, Indian Railways Act, 1890

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Railways Act, 1989 - Leasing compartment to private party - Increase in Tariff Rate - Justification - Respondent issued impugned tender notice on 19-6-2000, inviting sealed tenders from traders and other interested parties for leasing of luggage compartment on payment of lump sum rate for loading of parcels by certain trains for period of two years - However, respondents had noticed that in some trains, most of time, luggage capacity available in luggage was not being fully utilized resulting in loss of revenue - Hence, it was decided to lease luggage space in compartment to traders and other interested persons after inviting tenders from them - Hence, luggage compartment of train was leased for period of two years to company - Appellant filed petition before HC challenging impugned tender notice on ground that appellant was denied facility of transporting goods at rates specified in Coaching Tariff - Lower court allowed petitions and quashed impugned notification, holding that awarding of contract of lease of luggage compartment is bad in law - However, SC reversed order passed by lower court and passed order in favor of respondent - Whether impugned notice is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, railway being State monopoly undertaking, it had to be statutorily controlled from abusing its monopolistic character by prohibiting it from giving any undue or unreasonable preference or advantage or acting in any manner which would evidence undue or unreasonable prejudice or disadvantage in any respect. However, Central Govt. to meet needs of country arising in any eventuality can give directions for giving special facilities for or preference to transport of goods or any class of goods. Further, with respect to rate charged by Railway Administration, SC held that onus to prove alleged unreasonableness of freight rests on company. It is for company to establish that rate charged by Railway Administration for carriage is unreasonable. Further, even if Railways are earning some surplus income after deducting operation cost that by itself is no ground to hold that freight charged is per se unreasonable. Hence, cost of operation cannot by itself be basis for judging reasonableness of rate charged. However, SC directed respondents to fix outer or upper limit of rates chargeable by contractors for different trains as respondent are bound to follow and implement provisions of Act. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Policies issued by authority should not only be profit oriented without taking into consideration fundamental rights of individual under Constitution.
