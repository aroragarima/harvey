Inder Mohan Goswami and Another v State of Uttaranchal and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Dalveer Bhandari, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1024; (2007) 12 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC 251; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 3302; 2007 (5) CTC 614; 2007 (144) DLT 257; 2007 (4) JCC 2843; JT 2007 (11) SC 499; 2007 (4) RCR(Criminal) 548; 2007(12) SCALE 15; 2007(12) SCALE 32; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 847

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Non-Bailable Warrants

Summary: Indian Penal Code, ss. 420, 467; Code of Criminal Procedure, s.482 - Breach of contractual obligations - Criminal Proceedings - Special Judicial Magistrate, Rishikesh issued a non-bailable warrant against the appellants on the basis of First Information Report under ss. 420, 467 IPC filed by the respondents - High Court dismissed petition under s. 482 Cr.P.C. filed by the appellants on the ground that the records show that the allegations in the FIR constitute an offence as alleged by the complainant - Appeal against - Held, basic ingredients of offence under s. 467 are altogether missing even in the allegations of the FIR against the appellants - Non-bailable warrant should be issued to bring a person to court when summons of bailable warrants would be unlikely to have the desired result - Warrants either bailable or non-bailable should never be issued without proper scrutiny of facts and complete application of mind, due to the extremely serious consequences and ramifications which ensue on issuance of warrants - Impugned judgment of the High Court in declining to exercise its inherent power has led to grave miscarriage of justice - Appeal disposed of.
