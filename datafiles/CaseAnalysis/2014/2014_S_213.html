Pooja Abhishek Goyal v State of Gujarat and others

Supreme Court of India

25 April 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Gyan Sudha Misra, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 309; (2014) 6 SCC 404; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4492; 2014 (3) JCC 1790; 2014(5) SCALE 721; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 855

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Summary: Criminal - Family & Personal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 498A, 406, 34, 114 - Dowry Prohibition Act, ss. 3, 4 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 173(8) - Application for recovery of stridhan - Directions - Sustainability - Petitioner had filed complaint against respondent Nos. 2 to 6 u/ss. 498-A, 406, 34 and 114 and u/ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - Petitioner filed application for an appropriate order and to direct further investigation u/s. 173 (8) of CrPC with a special direction that the same be conducted under the direct supervision of an officer not below the rank of Asstt. Commissioner of Police of zone - Trial Court allowed the application - Respondents dissatisfied with the order filed revision application - Revision Court allowed the petition and set aside the order passed by Trial Court - Petitioner filed an application before HC against the said order - Single Judge dismissed the application - Hence instant special leave application - Whether Single Judge was right in dismissing the application filed by petitioner -

Held, SC agreed with the view of Single Judge that all steps pertaining to the investigation of the stridhan property of petitioner was allowed in favour of petitioner and even suo moto investigation was conducted by the police which subsequently was confirmed by the order of the Magistrate - As per the averment of petitioner the Revisional Court interfered and disturbed the course of investigation, but HC appeared to have correctly noted that the Revisional Court has also permitted further investigation by the concerned officer in charge of the Police Station in regard to the complaint of petitioner alleging non-recovery of her stridhan property - Petitioner has not moved SC bonafide but perhaps to teach a lesson to respondent/husband rather than recovery of her stridhan property - In any view, if the investigation conducted by the authorities did not suffer from the lacunae or serious infirmity, SC did not see any reason to issue any further direction to the court below to take steps in the matter - Petition dismissed.
