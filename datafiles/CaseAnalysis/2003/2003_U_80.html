N.D. Jayal and Another v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 September 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

D.M. Dharmadhikari, S. Rajendra Babu, G.P. Mathur

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 720; (2004) 9 SCC 362; AIR 2004 SC 867; JT 2003 (Supp2) SC 1; 2003(7) SCALE 54; 2003 (1) SCW 7105; [2003] Supp3 S.C.R. 152; 2003 (6) Supreme 572

Case Digest

Subject: Environment, Wildlife & Animal

Keywords: Environmental Appraisal Committee, Sustainable Development, Water Logging, Dams, Displacement, Irrigation, Natural Resources, Tehri Dam, Environment (Protection) (Second Amendment) Rules, 1999

Summary: [A.] Constitution - Environment, Wildlife & Animal - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 21, 32 - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, s. 4(1) - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986, ss. 19(b), 3, 3(2) - Environment (Protection) (Second Amendment) Rules, 1999, rr. 5, 5(3) - Safety and environmental aspects of Tehri Dam - Rehabilitation - Held, at this penultimate stage of proceedings, it is too late in day to think as to why decision was taken to construct project or decisions have been taken to continue project though at one stage it was thought it would not be appropriate to continue same - Further held, when Govt. or concerned authorities after due consideration of all viewpoints and full application of mind took a decision, then it is not appropriate for Court to interfere - Such matters must be left to mature wisdom of Govt. or implementing agency - It is their forte In such cases, if situation demands, Courts should take only a detached decision based on pattern of well-settled principles of administrative law - If any such decision is based on irrelevant consideration or non-consideration of material or is thoroughly arbitrary, then Court will get in way - Further, to ensure that all conditions for environmental clearance are fulfilled and for proper monitoring, all cases are transferred to HC of Uttaranchal to be dealt with by a DB - HC shall deal with these matters as if filed before it and shall be at liberty to pass any orders - However, it is made clear that it shall not be open to re-examine matters such as safety of dam and other aspects relating to implementation of conditions contained in Govt. orders which have been decided in this case - Further held, present status of dam project, keeping in view pari passu condition on which 'environmental clearance' has been granted by MoEF, calls for issuing following directions to respondents who represent various Ministries and; departments of Central and State Govt. as also Corporation to which project has been entrusted for implementation.
