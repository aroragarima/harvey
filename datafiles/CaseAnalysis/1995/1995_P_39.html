Rabindra Nath Mukherjee and Another v Panchanan Banerjee (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 May 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1215; (1995) 4 SCC 459; AIR 1995 SC 1684; 1995 (2) CCC 638; JT 1995 (7) SC 177; 1995(3) SCALE 455; 1995 (2) UJ 177

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Civil Procedure - Succession Act, 1925, s. 63 - Validity of Will challenged - Will debarring natural heirs - Will was registered and Sub-registrar certified that will had been read over to executor who, on doing so, admitted contents - Fact that witnesses to document were interested is insignificant - Active part played by a close relation of one of executors in execution of will - Under these circumstances it can be said that whole idea behind execution of will is to interfere with normal line of succession and there is suspicion about voluntary character of document - Appeal allowed.
