People‘S Union For Civilliberties (Pucl) v The Union Of India And Another

Supreme Court of India

18 December 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1508; (1997) 1 SCC 301; AIR 1997 SC 568; AIR 1997 SCW 113; 1997 (3) Bom.C.R. 38; 1996 (4) CCC 277; 1997 (66) DLT 197; 1996(9) SCALE 318; [1996] Supp10 S.C.R. 321; 1997 (1) UJ 187

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Telecom

Keywords: Sovereignty And Integrity Of India

Summary: Constitution - Telecom - Indian Telegraph Act, 1885, s. 5(2) - Constitution, arts. 19(1)(a) and 21 - Conctitutional validity of s. 5(2) of Telegraph Act? - (A) Whether telephone tapping violates arts. 19(1)(a) and 21 - (1) Whether right to privacy would include telephone conversation in the privacy of home or office - (2) Whether person is exercising his right to freedom of speech and expression when talking on telephone - (B) Whether occurrence of public emergency or existence of public safety interest are sine qua non for application of provisions of s. 5 (2) - Held, (A) telephone tapping would affects arts. 19(1)(a) and 21 - (1) Right to privacy is part of right to life and it includes telephone conversation - (2) Person is exercising his right to freedom of speech and expression when he is talking on telephone - (B) They are sine qua non for application of s. 5 (2) - Petition allowed.
