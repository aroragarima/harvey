Sundaram Finance Limited v Nepc India Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 January 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, Sujata V. Manohar

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 1959; (1999) 2 SCC 479; AIR 1999 SC 565; AIR 1999 SCW 225; 1999 (1) ARBLR 305; 1999 (3) CompLJ 205; JT 1999 (1) SC 49; 1999 (1) RAJ 365; 1999(1) SCALE 40; 1999 (1) SCJ 289; [1999] 1 S.C.R. 89; 1999 (1) SLT 179; 1999 (1) Supreme 126

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Corporate

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 41(b),(c) - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 2(d), 9, 17, 21 and 36 - Whether under s. 9 of the Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 the court has jurisdiction to pass interim orders even before arbitral proceedings commence and before an Arbitrator is appointed?- Arbitration proceedings can commence and continue notwithstanding a party to the arbitration agreement having approached the court for an order for interim protection - Party to an arbitration agreement can approach the court for interim relief not only during the arbitral proceedings, but even before the arbitral proceedings - The power to grant an interim injunction under s. 44 of the Act extends to the granting of a Mareva injunction - It may also include granting an interim mandatory injunction, although the Court will be slow to grant an injunction which provides a remedy of essentially the same kind as is ultimately being sought from the Arbitral Tribunal - Held, even before the commencement of arbitral proceedings, the Court can grant interim relief - Held, High Court erred in coming to the conclusion that the Trial Court had no jurisdiction in entertaining the application under s. 9 because arbitration proceedings had not been initiated by the appellant - Held, HC has not considered the merits of the case, it is directed that the petition filed by the respondent, challenging the order of the Trial Court, be decided on merits - Appeals disposed of.
