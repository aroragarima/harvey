Messrs Saharanpur Electric Supply Company Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax

Supreme Court of India

15 January 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ranganathan, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 825; (1992) 2 SCC 736; 1992 (101) CTR 452; [1992] 194 ITR 294; JT 1992 (1) SC 287; 1992(1) SCALE 16; [1992] 1 S.C.R. 117

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 10 - Income Tax Amendment Act, 1953 - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 34(3) and 43 - Dispute as to computation of depreciation on service lines installed by assessees, a part of expenditure incurred in connection with installation of which is recovered by assessees from consumers of electricity - Computation of depreciation for assets acquired in previous years relevant to assessment year 1961-62 or earlier assessment years - (A) Whether figure of actual cost ascertained in respect of any asset in any of earlier previous years cannot be altered in a subsequent year and whether, both 1922 Act and 1961 Act envisage continuance of figure of actual cost once arrived at in respect of any asset throughout lifetime of such asset? - S. 43(6) specifically deals with two categories of assets: (i) those acquired during relevant previous year, and (ii) those acquired earlier to that; even in respect of latter class of assets, Act envisages computation of actual cost of asset and deduction therefrom of all depreciation allowed in earlier years in respect of that asset; whatever its validity over period of continuous operation of same Act (1922 or 1961), it can have no application for assessment year 1962-63 - Held, there is no provision in 1961 Act which permits or compels adoption or continuance of figure of actual cost and written down value determined under earlier statute which has been repealed by 1961 Act - (B) Whether actual cost cannot be altered merely because a subsequent legislation provides for a different formula for ascertainment of actual cost? - Held, where subsequent information, factual or legal reveals that actual cost determined originally was wrong, there can be no doubt that original figure of actual cost has to be altered, if need be, and if possible, by reopening earlier assessment and, if that be not possible, at least for future - (C) Whether legislation can be given retrospective effect so as to affect existing rights unless it says so expressly or by necessary implication? - Present is not case where actual cost as determined in assessment year 1962-63 is sought to be applied to revise computations for earlier years but that though in respect of these particular assets, assessee might have obtained depreciation for earlier assessment years on basis of a higher figure, that will no longer be available in future and that figure of actual cost should be taken not as was originally calculated but only at a lower figure for assessment years 1962-63 and onwards - Held, it is no doubt true that in past years asset had become eligible to amounts of depreciation aggregate of which exceeds actual cost as presently determined and, if that depreciation is deducted from actual cost subsequently arrived at, a negative figure may result, however, there was no negative written down value in earlier years and, equally, there will be none in year of revision as effect of proviso is not to produce a negative written down value but only to preclude further grant of depreciation on asset in future; object of provision dealing with grant of depreciation is to enable him to get capital expenditure incurred by him in acquiring asset written off to his profits over years - (D) Whether there is an indication in language of s. 43(6) itself to show that it is available to be invoked only in respect of assets which had been acquired in earlier years? - Held, it is incontrovertible that, u/s. 43(1) r/w s. 43(6) officer has to determine actual cost for all assets, new and old, and definition in s. 43(1) only requires that, at time of doing so, he has to examine whether actual cost has been fully laid out by assessee or has been met by someone else in whole or in part; words 'has been met' squarely fit into this reading of section and suggestion that use of words 'has been met' lends support to an interpretation restricting definition in s. 43(1) to assets acquired in previous year cannot be accepted - (E) Whether revenue's interpretation will result in absurdities and anomalies, viz (i) it may lead to computation of a negative written down value and consequent difficulties in applying various other statutory provisions, (ii) that interpretation is incompatible with terms of explanations 2, 4 and 6 to s. 43(6), (iii) that it might lead to difficulties in calculation of assessable profits u/s. 41(2) or allowance u/s. 32(1)(iii)? - Held, (i) proviso to cl. (c) really places a limitation on depreciation deductible at any point of time and, hence, there can never be a negative written down value, (ii) explanations 2 and 4 fall in line with suggested interpretation, once it is understood that reference to 'depreciation actually allowed' should be read subject to limitation of cl. (c) of proviso to s. 10(2)(vi) [now s. 34(3)] and explanation 6 offers no difficulty as relationship as 'parent' and 'subsidiary' between companies involved in transfer for purposes of this clause has to be determined as at time of transfer of asset and will not be a wobbling or fluctuating one, (iii) this is only a seeming anomaly; assessee having paid nothing for asset, its actual cost should be taken at nil, a stand in which there is no absurdity - Appeals dismissed.
