Smt. Pushpa v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

31 May 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 390; (1979) SCC (Cr) 1015; (1980) Supp SCC 391; AIR 1979 SC 1953; 1979 CRLJ 1314; 1979 CRLR 472; 1980 MLJ 320

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Specially Empowered

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950 - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974 - Validity of detention - Representations - Rejection - Sustainability - Hence, instant petition filed u/art. 32 of Constitution for writ of habeas corpus by wife of detenu, has been detained by order made by respondent/chief Secretary on dt. 1-27-1979 u/s. 3(1) of the Act and validity of detention of detenu has been questioned - Two representations dt. 2-23-1979 and 3-27-1979 made by detenu have been dealt with and rejected by Chief Secretary - Detaining authority supplied copies on 3-7-1979 and on 3-17-1979 detenu made application complaining that copies were illegible and copies of some documents were still not supplied though long time had elapsed whereupon further set of copies was supplied to detenu on 3- 22-1979 - Hence, Petitioner contended that representations have been rejected by respondent who made detention order though he was not competent to do so because representation has to be considered by appropriate Govt. and not by officer who was especially authorized to make detention order u/s. 3 of the Act - Whether order of detention by detaining authority was justified - Held, mind of detaining authority was influenced by fact that e detenu was foreigner but there was nothing to show that question of status of detenu whether he was Indian citizen or foreigner has at all influenced mind of detaining authority in passing order - This aspect had not entered assessment and evaluation of facts leading to passing of detention order and it has been averred that, by enquiries made it transpired that detenu was not foreign national and therefore, order directing him to report to Foreigners' Registration Office was withdrawn - However, there was nothing to show that apart from relevant considerations, allegation that petitioner was foreign national according to belief of custom authorities or detaining authority has at all influenced mind of detaining authority or that this aspect has stealthily crept into decision of detaining authority directing detention of detenu - Petition dismissed.
