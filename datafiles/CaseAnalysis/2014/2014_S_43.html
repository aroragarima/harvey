Sardar Tajender Singh Ghambhir and another v Sardar Gurpreet Singh and others

Supreme Court of India

12 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.F. Nariman, Kurian Joseph

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 633; (2014) 10 SCC 702; AIR 2015 SC 242; 2014(6) ALL MR 461; 2015 (108) ALR 235; 2015 (1) AWC 264; 2014 (4) CLT(SC) 16; 2014 (6) CTC 116; JT 2014 (13) SC 66; 2014 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 4385; 2014 (4) RCR(Civil) 521; 2015 (126) RD 352; 2015 (2) RLW 1341; 2014(11) SCALE 110; [2014] 10 S.C.R. 527; 2015 (2) WLN(SC) 40

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure

Keywords: Deficit court-fee

Summary: Civil Procedure - Court Fees Act, 1870, ss. 12(ii), 6(2), 6(3) - Deficit court-fee - Legality - Appellants plaintiffs filed suit before Trial Court for declaration and injunction - There was deficiency in payment of court-fee - Respondent no. 1 and 2 filed appeal before Appellate Court and the same was dismissed - Aggrieved respondent no. 1 and 2 filed petition before HC and the same was allowed - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC is justified.

Held, HC was clearly in error in invoking above provision without appreciating fact that there was no order by Trial Court directing plaintiffs to make good deficit court-fee within particular time. HC was in error in holding that deficiency in court-fee in respect of plaint cannot be made good during appellate stage. HC overlooked well known legal position that appeal is continuation of suit and power of Appellate Court is co-extensive with that of Trial Court. It failed to bear in mind that what could be done by Trial Court in proceeding of suit, can always be done by Appellate Court in interest of justice. HC failed to consider s. 12, cl. (ii) of 1870 Act. Provision clearly empowers Appellate Court to direct party to make up deficit court-fee in plaint at appellate stage. Power exercised by Appellate Court can be traced to s. 12, cl. (ii) of 1870 Act as well. Order of Appellate Court being eminently just and proper, there was no justification for HC to invoke its power and interfere with an order which effectively advanced cause of justice. Impugned order is unsustainable in eye of law and deserves to set-aside. Appeal allowed.
