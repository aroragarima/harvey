Suseel Finance and Leasing Company v M. Lata and others

Supreme Court of India

19 March 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

S.N. Variava, H.K. Sema

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 2063; (2004) 13 SCC 675

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Consumer Protection

Summary: Civil Procedure - Withdrawal of petition - Filing of review - Special leave petitions were against order dt.12-12-2003 passed in review petitions - Petitioners sought permission to withdraw special leave petitions to seek review of order of National Commission - Hence instant petition.

Held, it is clear from the order that the matter was argued for some time and it is clear that the Court was against the petitioners. Counsel applied for withdrawal on the ground that a review will be applied for. Review applications were filed before HC which has now been dismissed. By these special leave petitions order dismissing review petitions has been challenged. Order rejecting application for review, a special leave petition is not maintainable. Special leave petition is maintainable only in the context of it not being barred on principles of res judicata. In both cases referred by petitioner the question whether a special leave petition is against an order disposing of a review petition was not considered at all and these cases therefore have no relevance at all. Special leave petitions are not maintainable. Petitions dismissed.

Ratio - Special leave petition is not maintainable before SC when order rejecting an application for review of dismissal of SLP itself is dismissed.
