Satyanarayana Sinha v Messrs S. Lal and Company Private Limited

Supreme Court of India

10 September 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, S.N. Dwivedi

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 195; (1973) 2 SCC 696; (1973) SCC (Cr) 1002; AIR 1973 SC 2720; [1974] 1 S.C.R. 615

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Mines & Minerals; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant of lease, Challenge by non-party to suit, Locus standi

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Mineral Concession Rules, rr. 5, 58 and 59 - Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957, s. 31 - Constitution of India, 1950 - Grant of lease - Challenge by non-party to suit - Locus standi - Effect - Appellant was granted a mining lease by respondent No. 3 with the prior approval of respondent No. 2 for mining a mineral - Respondent No. 1 filed writ petition challenging the lease on the ground that he had earlier applied for a mining lease over an area of 280.62 acres which was included in the lease granted to appellant - Petition of respondent No. 1 was rejected - On revision under r. 54 of the Rules before respondent No. 2 set aside the orders of State Govt. and directed to give further consideration and pass appropriate orders - Aggrieved appellant contended that he was not served with the notice and challenged the ex-parte order - HC came to the conclusion that the conditions required for relaxation of the Rules in special cases u/s. 31 of the 1957 Act r/w rr. 58 and 59 of the Rules, were not complied with while according its approval for the grant of the mining lease to appellant - HC allowed the petition and quashed the lease in favour of appellant - Hence instant appeal - Appellant contended appellant did not have an opportunity of urging before the Court that the petition filed by respondent No. 1 was not maintainable, because he was not a person aggrieved as the area for which respondent No. 1 had made an application for the grant of mining lease was not included in the area granted to appellant - Appellant further contended that no infringement of the provisions of s. 31 of the Act r/w rr. 58 and 59 of the Rules - Whether petition filed before HC by respondent No. 1 was maintainable - Held, petition filed by a person who was not the person aggrieved, was not maintainable - It was admitted by respondents No. 2 and 3 that the application made by respondent No. 1 was not in respect of the area which was granted to, appellant and consequently respondent No. 1 had no-interest in the subject-matter of the lease - In respect of persons who were strangers and who seek to invoke the jurisdiction of the HC or of SC, difficulty sometimes arose because of nature and extent of the right or interest which was said to have been infringed, and whether the infringement in some way affects such persons - There was no clear enunciation of principles on which the Court would exercise its jurisdiction - In instant case, however, respondent No. 1 had not challenged the grant of the lease on the ground of ex debito justitiae but had done so on the ground of direct infringement of his right to be granted a mining lease over 280.62 acres for which appellant was given a lease along with other area - No such right of respondent No. 1 was affected, he had no locus standi - Respondent No. 1 was neither a party nor a person aggrieved or affected, his writ petition in HC was not maintainable - Petition filed by respondent No. 1 in HC dismissed - Appeal allowed.
