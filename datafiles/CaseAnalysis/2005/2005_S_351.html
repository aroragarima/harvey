Holiram Bordoloi v State of Assam

Supreme Court of India

08 April 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 263; (2005) 3 SCC 793; AIR 2005 SC 2059; 2005 CRLJ 2174; JT 2005 (4) SC 156; 2005 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 486; 2005 (2) RCR(Criminal) 419; 2005(3) SCALE 804; [2005] 3 S.C.R. 406

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Eye-Witness, Gunshot injury, Code of Criminal Procedure, Criminal Procedure

Summary: [A.] Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure 1973, ss.37 , 43 , 129 , 313 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss.147, 148, 149, 436, 326, 302 - Accused found guilty of offences - Capital punishment awarded by Sessions Judge and confirmed by HC - Appeal against conviction and sentence - Involvement of appellant in this incident not conclusive - Whether present case falls in category of rarest of rare cases where death penalty is to be imposed on appellant? - Held, evidence adduced by prosecution proves beyond reasonable doubt actual involvement of appellant - Neither perusal of evidence on record nor statement u/s.313 CrPC, provided for any mitigating circumstance in favour of appellant - Appeal dismissed.
