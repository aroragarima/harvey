Sheikh Abdul Rashid and others v State of Jammu and Kashmir and others

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, J.M. Panchal

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1588; (2008) 1 SCC 722; AIR 2007 SC (Supp) 1613; JT 2008 (1) SC 127; 2007(13) SCALE 755; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 940; 2007 (8) SLR 661

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1956, Jammu and Kashmir Police (Gazetted) Service Recruitment Rules, 1984, Jammu and Kashmir Police Manual

Summary: Service - Jammu and Kashmir Civil Services (Classification, Control and Appeal) Rules, 1956, r. 24 - Jammu and Kashmir Police Manual - Inter se seniority between appellants and respondents - Appellants were appointed as direct recruits in post of Sub-Inspectors - Respondents were working as Assistant Sub-Inspectors at relevant time - Names of respondents were entered in List E and they were promoted on officiating basis - Government of Jammu and Kashmir issued a notification directing their promotion with retrospective effect ignoring seniority of appellants - Said order was cancelled - On same day, however, promotions were directed to be granted with retrospective effect from date on which respondents were brought on promotion in List E - Writ petition filed thereagainst by appellants was allowed - DB accepted said findings of Single Judge - It, however, opined that as both appellants as also respondents were already promoted to posts of Deputy Superintendent of Police in J & K Police (Gazetted) Service, governed by another set of rules, writ petition became infructuous and direction of Single Judge to re-draw seniority list placing respondents above appellants in seniority list of Sub-Inspectors was not necessary to be implemented - Present appeal - Appellants submitted that DB committed a serious error insofar as it failed to take into consideration that unless and until appellants are held to be entitled to promotion in terms of the rules, their cases may not be considered for promotion to post of Superintendent of Police - Held, matter relating to seniority and promotion is governed by Rules, 1956 and Jammu and Kashmir Police Manual - R. 24 of 1956 Rules provides that seniority should be determined on basis of initial appointment to post - Regn. 382 of Police Manual provides for power to make officiating promotions which in case of promotion to Sub-Inspectors and Assistant Sub-Inspectors was within jurisdiction of Inspector General of Police - Clause (2) of Regn. 390 provides that entry in List E would be entry point for purpose of promotion to post of Sub-Inspector - Rules being statutory in nature and having been framed under Jammu and Kashmir Civil Servants Removal of Doubts and Declaration of Rights Ordinance, 1956 have statutory force, executive order in question was required to be issued in consonance and not in derogation thereof - In the present case, no retrospective promotion could be granted as has been held by both Single Judge as also DB and also entry in List E, having regard to Regn. 390 of Jammu and Kashmir Police Manual per se did not confer any right upon respondents to be promoted from that date - Further, although statute provides for grant of officiating promotion, but same is not conducive in sense that even after getting officiating promotion, one may be reverted to original post held by him - Impugned judgment set aside - Appeals allowed.
