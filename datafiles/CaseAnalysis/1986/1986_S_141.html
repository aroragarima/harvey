Sumer Chand Sharma And Anr. v State Of U.P. And Anr. Etc. Etc.

Supreme Court of India

24 April 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, E.S. Venkataramiah

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 303; (1986) 3 SCC 263; AIR 1986 SC 1112; 1986 ALJ 597; 1986 (12) ALR 591; 1986(1) SCALE 1280; [1986] 2 S.C.R. 766

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Carriers and Transportation - Contract and Commercial - UP Motor Vehicles Special Provisions Act, 1976, ss. 1(3) and.5 - UP Road Transport Services (Development) Act,1955, s. 10(1)(c) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 135 - Motor Vehicles - Whether private operators plying stage carriages on routes are eligibile for authorisation u/s. 5 of 1976 Act when they the route partly overlapped nationalised routes - Held, plying of stage carriages by the private operators before the commencement of 1976 Act pursuant to the alleged practice which has grown up in Uttar Pradesh or under interim orders of a court must be considered to be unauthorised so as to disentitle the private operator from seeking the benefit of s. 5 - Appeal dismissed.
