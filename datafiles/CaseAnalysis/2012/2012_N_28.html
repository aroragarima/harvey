Param Pal Singh through Father v National Insurance Company and another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2012

Case Analysis

Bench

Fakkir Mohamed Ibrahim Kalifulla, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Compensation - Appellant claimed himself to be the adopted son of the deceased, who was working as Truck Driver - Appellant filed application before the Commissioner of Workmen's Compensation contending that the death of the deceased was in the course of his employment with the trade and business of the respondent no. 2 and that his death was due to stress and strain while driving the said truck continuously over a period of time and claimed compensation - Age of the deceased was stated to be 45 years at the time of his death - - Commissioner repelled both the contentions of the respondents, namely, about the locus of the appellant as well as the causal connection of the death of the deceased with that of his employment and awarded the compensation - On appeal, HC passed the impugned order setting aside the order passed by the Commissioner - Hence, instant appeal - Whether death of the deceased was in an accident arising out of and in the course of his employment with the respondent no. 2 -

Held, as per evidence on record, deceased was simply travelling in the vehicle and that there was no obligation for him to undertake the work of driving - Also, evidence as stood established proved the fact that the deceased was actually driving the truck and that in the course of such driving activity as he felt uncomfortable he safely parked the vehicle on the side of the road near a hotel soon where after he breathed his last - In such circumstances, conclusion of the Commissioner of Workmen's Compensation that the death of the deceased was in an accident arising out of and in the course of his employment with the respondent no. 2 was perfectly justified and the conclusion to the contrary reached by the HC in the order impugned set aside - Order of the Commissioner for Workmen's Compensation restored - Appeal allowed.
