Post Graduate Institute of Medical Education and Research, Chandigarh v Raj Kumar and Others

Supreme Court of India

02 November 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 71; (2001) 2 SCC 54; (2001) SCC (L&S) 365; AIR 2001 SC 479; AIR 2001 SCW 77; 2001 (1) AWC 571; 2001 (1) CLR 1055; 2001 (88) FLR 688; JT 2001 (1) SC 336; 2001 LabIC 476; 2001 (1) LLJ 546; 2001 (1) LLN 804; 2000(8) SCALE 469; 2001 (1) SLT 178; [2000] Supp4 S.C.R. 350; 2000 (8) Supreme 603

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Daily Rate, There shall, however, be no order as to costs

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 25 B and 25 F - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Back wages - Entitlement - Whether Labour Court was justified in awarding 60% of back wages while ordering reinstatement with continuity of service? - Payment of back wages having a discretionary element involved in it has to be dealt with, in facts and circumstances of each case and no strait-jacket formula can be evolved, though, however, there is statutory sanction to direct payment of back wages in its entirety - Issue of back wages has been dealt with by Labour Court having regard to facts and circumstances of matter in issue upon exercise of its discretion in a manner which cannot but be judicious in nature - In event Court's interference is sought for there exists an obligation on part of High Court to record in judgment, reasoning before denouncing a judgment of an inferior Tribunal, in absence of which, judgment cannot stand scrutiny of otherwise being reasonable - HC did not record any reason far less any appreciable reason before denouncing judgment - Orders under appeal set aside - Appeal allowed.
