Tin Plate Dealers Association Private Limited and others v Satish Chandra Sanwalka and others

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 715; (2016) 10 SCC 1; AIR 2016 SC 4705; 2017 (1) CompLJ 216; JT 2016 (12) SC 614; 2016(9) SCALE 758; [2016] 8 S.C.R. 145

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate

Keywords: Articles Of Association, Board Of Directors, Balance Sheet, Company Law Board, Winding Up, Forfeited, Meeting, Companies Act, 1956, Allotment, Share Capital, Conversion, Clb, Dividend, Reserve, Shareholders, Unpaid, Vote, Preference, Preference Share, Oppression, Poll, Code of Civil Procedure, 1908

Summary: (A) Corporate - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100(4), 10F - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 - Authorized capital increase - Without notice - Challenged -Respondents/Sanwalka Group filed company petition u/ss. 397/398 of the Act against appellants/Gupta Group contending that Gupta Group without notice to them had increased the authorized capital of the company to Rs. 5 crores in an Extra Ordinary General Meeting of the Company held on 5-7-1994 and no notice of the said meeting was given to Sanwalka Goup - Company Law Board (CLB) by an order held that u/art. 18 of the Articles of Association of Company, it is Gupta Group who were liable to pay the dues, if any, on the said forfeited shares and issue of bonus shares against revaluation reserve was contrary to the provisions of art. 96 of Table A of the Act - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal - Hence, instant appeals - Whether the company petition filed by Sanwalka Group before CLB was maintainable.

Held, it was found by CLB as well as HC that even issue of notice of the call in terms of s. 53 of the Act had not been proved by Gupta Group. Further, call notice dt. 5-1-1991 and forfeiture of the shares held by Sanwalka Group upon alleged failure to comply with the said notice, does not appear to be inconformity with cls. 14 to 18 of Articles of Association of Company. There is no co-relation between the amounts mentioned in the call notice dt. 5-1-1991 and the Balance Sheet dt. 31-3-1992, the members of Sanwalka Group were removed from the Board of Directors on 1-7-1991 i.e. before finalisation of Balance Sheet dt. 31-3-1992. In any case, the procedure for forfeiture of shares as a consequence of failure to respond to a call notice are unambiguously set out in details in the Articles of Association of Company. A balance sheet does not and cannot operate as an alternative to a call notice. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Corporate - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100(4), 10F - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 - Equity shares - Bonafides - Legality - Whether the issue of 25,000 new ordinary shares was legal.

Held, there can be no manner of doubt that the decision of Board to issue the said shares has to be tested in the light of the wide powers of Board to act in such matters as has been laid down by SC in Needle Industries (India) Ltd. & Ors. v. Needle Industries Newey (India) Holding Ltd. & Ors., 1981 Indlaw SC 403. The power of Board of Directors of Company to issue fresh shares must always be viewed as an adjunct of its extensive powers under the Act and the bona fides of such an exercise cannot be called into question by construing the power to issue fresh shares to be limited by any particular purpose or purposes. This was the view of CLB. However, the same would not detract from the fundamental principle of fair play that is to be expected from the Board of Directors in making a fair and proportionate distribution/allotment of such fresh shares. The direction of CLB upheld by HC that allotment from the aforesaid 25,000 newly issued ordinary equity shares should be proportionate to the shareholding of the two groups taking the members of the Sanwalka Group as having continued to be members of the company, will, therefore, not require any interference. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Corporate - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100(4), 10F - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 - Preference shares - Fair value - Validity - Whether the issue of 3065 ordinary equity shares as against the preference shares was legal.

Held, resolution of Board dt. 5-7-1994 relating to the conversion of preference shares into equity shares proceeds on the basis that dividends in respect of the 3065 shares have not been paid and in lieu thereof the shareholders had agreed to receive an equivalent number of equity shares. The above statement of fact is difficult to accept. Neither is the period during which dividends had not been paid is specified, nor is the amount due indicated. No material has been laid to show that the 3065 equity shares represent a fair value of the dividends claimed to be unpaid. What cannot also be lost sight of is that the preference shares in question were held by the Gupta Group who was in control of the company at that point of time. A number of self-serving decisions by Gupta Group and its conduct of the business of company in a manner detrimental to the interest of company, would make it extremely perilous to rely on the version available in the resolution of Board for allotment of 3065 equity shares in place of preference shares in question. Thus, it would be just and proper to strike down the conversion of 3065 preference shares into equity shares and revert the preference shares to its earlier status to be dealt with in the future in accordance with law. Appeals disposed of.

(D) Corporate - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 100(4), 10F - Companies Act, 1956, ss. 397, 398 - Industrial plot - Lease agreement execution - Legality - Whether the issue of lease agreement executed by company in respect of the industrial plot was legal.

Held, HC in the order under challenge has taken into account that apart from the industrial plot in question the Company has no subsisting business and that the terms of the lease entered into by Gupta Group in respect of the said property are wholly adverse to the Company's interest. Appeals disposed of.
