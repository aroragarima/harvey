Arjun Khiamal Makhijani v Jamnadas C. Tuliani and Others

Supreme Court of India

05 October 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

N.D. Ojha, M.N. Venkatachaliah, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 154; (1989) 4 SCC 612; 1990 (1) Bom.C.R. 334; JT 1989 (4) SC 74; 1990 (1) RCJ 218; 1989 (2) RentLR 569; 1989(2) SCALE 780; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 380; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 380

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Arrears of rent

Summary: Rent Control - Bombay Rent, Hotel and lodging House Rates Control Act, 1947 - Eviction - Arrears of rent - Legality - Respondent no. 1/landlord filed suit for ejectment against appellants/tenants on ground that they were in arrears of rent for a period of more than 6 months which was not paid in spite of a notice of demand - Trial Court decreed suit - On appeals, Appellate Court dismissed appeals - Aggrieved, appellants filed two writ petitions in HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeals - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in impugned judgment and decree of HC - Held, finding of HC that appellant No. 6 was not in possession was based on circumstance that on that date garage was in possession of a receiver appointed by Court and not in possession of appellant No. 6 - Further, HC had emphasised in its judgment under appeal that no positive plea of sub-tenancy, whether lawful or unlawful, was raised by appellant No. 6 in Trial Court - That apart, appellant No. 6 in unequivocal terms admitted in his deposition also before Trial Court that he came in possession by virtue of agreement with son of appellant No. 1 - Thus, finding of HC that garage had not illegally been sub-let to appellant No. 6 and that said appellant was a trespasser was unassailable - Hence, appellant No. 6 could not derive any benefit out of amended s. 15(2) of the Act - Therefore, HC had not committed any error in holding that appellant No. 6 was a trespasser - Appeals dismissed.
