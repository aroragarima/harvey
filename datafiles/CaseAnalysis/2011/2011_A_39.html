Ajitsingh Harnamsingh Gujral v State of Maharashtra

Supreme Court of India

13 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Markandey Katju, Chandramauli Kumar Prasad

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 687; (2011) 14 SCC 401; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 841; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 169; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 1000

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Code Criminal Procedure, 1898

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellant/accused had a quarrel with his wife police made an inquiry from servants and then a report of murder was lodged accused pleaded not guilty to charges - Trial Court convicted and imposed penalty of death upon accused - Appellant/Accused filed an appeal before HC - HC dismissed appeal and upheld death sentence - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant/accused guilty of murder if he was, should be given death sentence - Held, it was evident that when children of accused grew up they often resisted and protested against dictatorial behaviour of appellant, and this led to a lot of friction in family - Hence, appellant did not had happy married life with his wife, rather it was just reverse - - However, instant case was of circumstantial evidence and in cases of circumstantial evidence settled law was that prosecution should establish entire chain of circumstances which connects accused to crime -Thus accused deserves death penalty where murder was grotesque, diabolical, revolting or of a dastardly manner so as to arouse intense and extreme indignation of community, and when collective conscience of community was petrified, or outraged -- Thus all requisites for death penalty were satisfied in instant case for reasons given above - It was only legislature which can abolish death penalty and not courts - As long as death penalty exists in statute book it had to be imposed in some cases, otherwise it will tantamount to repeal of death penalty by t judiciary - It was not for judiciary to repeal or amend law, as that was in domain of legislature - accordingly dismissed - Appeal dismissed.
