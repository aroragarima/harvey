Kirloskar Brothers Limited v Employees' State Insurance Corporation

Supreme Court of India

24 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3147; (1996) SCC (L&S) 533; (1996) 2 SCC 682; AIR 1996 SC 3261; 1996 (33) ATC 231; 1996 (1) CLR 668; 1996 (1) CLT 474; 1996 (72) FLR 697; JT 1996 (2) SC 159; 1996 LabIC 1718; 1996 (1) LLJ 156; 1996 (2) LLN 22; 1996(2) SCALE 1; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 884; 1996 (5) Supreme 241; 1996 (2) UPLBEC 1031

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Medical Care, Universal Declaration of Human Rights

Summary: Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 2(17), 2(9), 1(5) and 39 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 21 - 'Principal Employer' - Employees working in regional offices of factory - Applicability of Act - Connection between main office/factory where goods are manufactured and finished goods which are sold or distributed in regional offices - Held, person keeping control or is responsible for supervision of establishment at respective regional offices, is 'Principal employer' - Person appointed for sale or distribution of products is 'employee' - Act applies to employees of regional offices - Quantum of business transaction is not relevant consideration - Medical facilities, is fundamental and human right of employees.
