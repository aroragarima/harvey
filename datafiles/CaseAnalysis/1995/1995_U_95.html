O. S. Singh and Another v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

12 October 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, S.C. Agrawal

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1393; (1996) 7 SCC 37; (1996) SCC (L&S) 373; 1996 (32) ATC 520; JT 1995 (7) SC 664; 1995(6) SCALE 8; 1995 (5) SLR 626; [1995] Supp4 S.C.R. 261; 1996 (1) UPLBEC 193

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Keywords: Master And Servant Relationship

Summary: Service - IPS (Regulation of Seniority) Rules, 1954, r. 3(3)(b) - Allotment year - Determination of - Whether year of allotment of an officer of State Police Service has to be determined in accordance with r. 3(3)(b) of Seniority Rules? - Governing factor for assignment of year of allotment u/r. 3(3)(b) is, continuous officiation in a senior post by a directly recruited officer as well as promotee officer - There is a casus omissus in r. 3(3)(b) in matter of assignment of year of allotment for an officer appointed by promotion who has not officiated prior to his substantive appointment to service and said omission cannot be filled by Court by judicial interpretation - This would only mean that Seniority Rules are silent in matter of assignment of year of allotment of such a promotee officer - In that event competent authority can assign year of allotment to such an officer in exercise of its administrative discretion - Held, case of promotee had been ignored from consideration, and on reconsideration promotee has been appointed retrospectively w.e.f. 31-03-1976 - Tribunal committed gross error in deciding year of allotment of petitioner only by taking into consideration his date of continuous officiation in senior post w.e.f. 30-03-1978 and ignoring order of Central Govt. on reconsideration appointing him to Indian Police Service w.e.f. 30-03-1976 - Appeals allowed.
