Kishore Kumar Khaitan and Another v Praveen Kumar Singh

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

P.K. Balasubramanyan, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 43; (2006) 3 SCC 312; AIR 2006 SC 1474; 2006 (2) ALD(SC) 65; 2006 (62) ALR 776; 2006 (2) AWC 1142; 2006 (3) CTC 185; 2006 (3) JhrCR(SC) 162; JT 2006 (2) SC 351; 2006 (2) MLJ(SC) 206; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 735; 2006 (100) RD 456; 2006 (2) RLW 938; 2006(2) SCALE 304; 2006 (2) SCJ 585; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 176

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Forcible Dispossession

Summary: [A.] Practice & Procedure - Rent Control - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 39, rr. 1, o. 43, r. 1, s. 151 - Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, s. 107 Suit by respondent for a declaration of his status as a tenant of suit property and for a perpetual injunction restraining appellants, owners of building - Interim order of status quo - Allegation that respondent dispossessed while status quo pending - District court upheld claim of plaintiff that he was dispossessed after interim order of status quo was passed and interim mandatory injunction granted - Appeal against - Held, tenancy claimed by plaintiff remains to be proved in suit - Disturbance of status quo by defendants has not been established - Plaintiff has not laid foundation for grant of an interim order of mandatory injunction in his favour - Orders of HC and that of Additional District Court are set aside and trial court is requested to dispose of suit - Appeal allowed.
