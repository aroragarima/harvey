Regional Provident Fundcommissioner, Bombay v Shree Krishna Metal Manufacturing co.

Supreme Court of India

14 March 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 368; AIR 1962 SC 1536; 1963 (65) BomLR 274; 1962 (4) FLR 473; 1962 (1) LLJ 427; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 815; [1962] Supp3 S.C.R. 815

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Employee's Provident Funds Act, 1952, s. 1(3)(a) - Whether s. 1(3)(a) excludes composite factories from its scope? - Held, when Legislature has described factories as factories engaged in any industry, it did not intend that said factories should be exclusively engaged in industry specified in Sch. I - Word,factory used in s. 1(3)(a) has a comprehensive meaning and it includes premises in which any manufacturing process is being carried on as described in definition - Besides, s. 1(3)(a), is it has been amended in 1956, now refers to every establishment which is a factory engaged in any industry specified in Schedule I and introduction of word establishment clearly shows that it may consist of different factories dealing with different industries and yet considered as one establishment, it may fall u/s. 1(3)(a), provided other requirements of said section are satisfied - Therefore, concept of 'establishment' being, of such a comprehensive character, insertion of word 'establishment' in s. 1(3)(a) by Amending Act of 1956 helps to negative argument that factory therein contemplated cannot be a composite factory - (B) Whether even if a composite factory is included in s. 1(3)(a), before provisions of Act can be applied to it, it must be shown that 50 or more persons are employed in that unit of factory which is engaged in industry specified in sch. I? - Further held, ordinary rule of grammar on which this construction is based cannot be treated as an invariable rule which must always and in every ease be accepted without regard to context - In order that factories should fall within scope of provision, they must satisfy two tests they must be engaged in any industry specified in sch. I; they must have employed 50 or more persons - First adjectival clause is in nature of a parenthetical clause and so clause beginning with words 'in which' must necessarily qualified word 'factories' and not word 'Industry' - (C) Whether High Court was right in holding that company and Mills are outside purview of s. 1(3)(a)? - Further held, this company carries on four different kinds of activities and it is impossible to hold that activity in relation to industry which falls in sch. I is either minor, subsidiary or incidental to other activities - This activity is as much work of company as other activities are and so, company must be hold to be a factory u/s. 1(3)(a) so far as first test is concerned - For purpose of present writ proceedings, test of numerical strength can be said to have been satisfied - View taken by HC that company is outside s. 1(3)(a) is erroneous in law and must be reversed - Appeal No. 361 of 1959 filed by regional Commr. is allowed - Writ petition filed by company is dismissed with costs - Appeal 387 of 1959 dismissed - Appeal 361 of 1959 allowed.
