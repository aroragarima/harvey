Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore and Others v B. C. Srinivasa Setty and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, P.N. Bhagwati, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 422; (1981) 2 SCC 460; AIR 1981 SC 972; 1981 (21) CTR 138; [1981] 128 ITR 294; 1981(1) SCALE 384; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 938; 1981 TAXLR 641; [1981] 5 TAXMAN 1; 1981 UJ 391

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s, 45 - Capital Gains - Assessee, a registered firm was dissolved by a deed dt. 01.12.1965 - At the time of dissolution, the goodwill of the firm was valued at Rs. 1, 50, 000/- - A new partnership by the same name was constituted under an instrument dt. 2.12.1965 and it took overall the assets, including the goodwill, and liabilities of the dissolved firm - The Income-Tax Officer made an assessment on the dissolved firm for the a/y 1966-67 but did not include any amount on account of the gain arising on transfer of the goodwill - On appeal, Commissioner being of the view that the assessment order was prejudicial to the Revenue, decided to invoke his revisional jurisdiction and setting aside the assessment order directed the Income-Tax Officer to make a fresh assessment after taking into account the capital gain arising on the sale of the goodwill - Tribunal allowed the appeal filed against said order and held that the sale did not attract tax on capital gains u/s. 45 of IT Act, 1961 - On appeal, HC held that value of the consideration received by the assessee for the transfer of its goodwill was not liable to capital gains tax u/s. 45 of the IT Act - Hence, present appeal - Whether the transfer of the goodwill of a newly commenced business can give rise to a capital gain taxable u/s. 45 of IT Act, 1961? - Held, if the goodwill generated in a new business is regarded as acquired at a cost and subsequently posses to an assessee in any of the modes specified in sub-section (1) of s. 49 of IT Act, it will become necessary to determine the cost of acquisition to the previous owner - Having regard to the nature of the asset, it will be impossible to determine such cost of acquisition - Nor call sub-section (3) of s. 55 of IT Act be invoked, because the date of acquisition by the previous owner will remain unknown - Hence, goodwill generated in a newly commenced business cannot be described as an 'asset' within the terms of s. 45 of IT Act and therefore its transfer is not subject to income-tax under the head 'Capital gain' - Impugned order of HC upheld - Revenue's appeals dismissed.
