FOR EDUCATIONAL USE ONLY

State of Kerala and others v Fr. William Fernandez Etc.

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Ashok Bhushan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 870

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Services, Customs Act, 1962, Central Excises And Salt Act, 1944, Sales Tax, Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, State Legislature, Legislative Policy, Legislative Power, Local Authorities, Government of India Act, 1935, Madras General Sales Tax Act, 1939, Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994, Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, Travancore-Cochin General Sales Tax Act, Legislative Lists, Kerala Building Tax Act, 1975, Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas For Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, Constitution Act, Maharashtra Municipalities (Octroi) Rules, 1968, Uttar Pradesh Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 2007, Orissa Entry Tax (Amendment) Act, 2005, Central Provinces and Berar Electricity Duty Act, 1949, Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, Constitution of India, 1950, Constitution of the United States, Central Province Municipalities Act, 1922, Bihar Act 16 of 1993, Bihar Act, 9 of 2003, Jharkhand Act 2 of 2002, Bihar Amendment Act, 2006, Bihar Act 19 of 2006, Uttarakhand Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Area Act, 2009, West Bengal Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Area Act, 2012, Orissa Entry Tax Rules, 2000

Summary: (A) Indirect Tax - Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas for Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, ss. 2(c), 3(1), 4 - Bihar Amendment Act, 2006 - Respondents/assesses imported motor vehicles from abroad after obtaining custom clearance in appellants/State - However, appellants levied entry tax on goods imported by respondents - Respondents filed writ petition challenging the same which was dismissed - On appeals, DB allowed appeals and declared that vehicles bought from outside the country were not liable to pay entry tax - Hence, instant appeals - Entry tax legislation - Clarificatory - Whether entry tax Legislations contemplated levy of Entry Tax on imported goods.

Held, charging event arises on entry of scheduled goods into a local area. Any goods which are entering into a local area of a State whether coming from another local area of State, any other State or outside the country, the charging event is same for all goods entering into local area. SC was of view that charging Section was clear, unambiguous and provisions cannot be read to mean that imported goods coming from outside the country are excluded from charge of entry tax. No such indication was discernible from any provision of the Act. Charging event was complete as and when goods enter into local area for use, sale or consumption irrespective of its origin. Therefore, s. 2(d) r/w s. 3 of the 1999 Act does not exclude the charging of entry tax on goods entering into local area for consumption, use or sale from outside country. The 2006 Act intent and purpose of amendment was clear that it was clarificatory and explanatory. It did not introduce a concept which was not already there. Thus, all the three legislations clearly did not exclude goods coming from outside the territory of country and the definition of entry of goods read with charging section clearly included all goods entering into a local area. Appeals disposed of.

(B) Indirect Tax - Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas for Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, ss. 2(c), 3(1), 4 - Export duty - Overlap - Whether Entry Tax Legislations was trenching the field of "import and export", "duties of custom" reserved to Parliament.

Held, in construction of a taxing entry, an interpretation which may lead to overlapping should be eschewed. If the taxing power was within a particular legislative field, it would follow that other fields in legislative lists should be construed to exclude such field. It was Parliament which has exclusive legislative competence to make a law under Entry 41 and under Entry 83 on duties of customs including export duties. There was no clash/overlap between entry levied by State under Entry 52 List II and custom duty levied by UOI under Entry 83 List I. Therefore, entry tax legislations do not intrude in the legislative field reserved for Parliament under Entry 41 and under Entry 83 of List I. State Legislature was fully competent to impose tax on the entry of goods into a local area for consumption, sale and use. Appeals disposed of.

(C) Indirect Tax - Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas for Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, ss. 2(c), 3(1), 4 - Import of goods - Tax imposed - Validity - Whether importation of goods outside country continues till the goods reach in the premises/factory of importer, during which period State has legislative competence to impose any tax.

Held, event for levy of customs duty, which was in the domain of Parliament, was entirely different from that of event of entry tax. The liability to pay State entry tax arises only when goods enter into a local area for consumption, use and sale which event was entirely different and separate from levy of customs duty which was on import. There was no inhibition on Parliament in exercising its legislative power under Entry 41 List I to define customs frontiers and further legislate with regard to duties of customs. Further, import country from where goods are imported and continue when goods enter into customs frontiers of imported country and ends when goods are released for home consumption. Till the event of import was over Parliamentary Legislation, control of UOI continues for ensuring the realisation of customs duties. Taxing event with regard to levy of customs duty by Parliament and levy of entry tax by States under Entry 52 List II are entirely different and separate. The taxing event pertaining to levy of entry tax occurs only after taxing event of levy of customs duty was over. Thus, State Legislation imposing entry tax in no manner encroaches upon Parliamentary Legislation under Entry 41 and Entry 83 there was no invalidity in levy of entry tax by States. Appeals disposed of.

(D) Indirect Tax - Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas for Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, ss. 2(c), 3(1), 4 - Unbroken package doctrine - Levying tax - Applicability - Whether doctrine of unbroken package as evolved by American Court were to apply with regard to imported goods of petitioners prohibiting State from levying any tax till the goods were first sold/dealt by importer.

Held, US SC itself has abandoned the Original Package theory and it was held that imported goods are not immuned from non-discriminatory ad valorem taxes imposed by State. Goods imported after having been released from customs barriers are not immuned from any kind of State taxation, which fall equally on other similar goods and contention of counsel for petitioner that immunity from State taxation should continue till it reaches in the premises where it was to be taken for consumption, sale and use cannot be accepted. Appeals disposed of.

(E) Indirect Tax - Kerala Tax on Entry of Goods into Local Areas Act, 1994 - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Bihar Tax on Entry of Goods Into Local Areas for Consumption, Use Or Sale Therein Act, 1993, ss. 2(c), 3(1), 4 - Custom duty - Non-inclusion - Validity - Whether in the definition of purchase value non-inclusion of custom duty was indicator of fact that legislature never intended to levy entry tax on imported goods.

Held, from the definition of purchase value given in s. 2(j) of the 1999 Act three aspects are noticeable. Firstly, purchase value means value of scheduled goods ascertained from original invoice or bill. Secondly, it includes insurance charges excise duty and other charges mentioned therein. And thirdly, other charges incidental to the purchase of such goods. The original invoice or bill of scheduled goods, generally include the entire value including import duty or custom duty and in any event inclusion of 'all other charges incidental to the purchase of such goods' has to necessarily mean all charges including custom duty which was incidental to the purchase. Thus, non-inclusion of custom duty specifically in definition of purchase value in s. 2(j) of the 1999 Act was inconsequential and cannot lead to mean that legislature never intended to include the imported goods under the entry tax legislation. Appeals disposed of.

(F) Indirect Tax - Entry tax - Legislative policy - Applicability - Whether entry tax legislations are not covered by Entry 52 List II. Held, taxes which are to be used by local authorities could be collected by local authorities as well as by State Govt. It was the matter of legislative policy as to how the tax was collected and distributed. Under List II Entry 5, State has legislative power to lay down powers of Municipal Corporation by legislation. It was again legislative policy that as what machinery was to be provided by State legislature regarding collection of taxes on the entry of goods into a local area for consumption, use or sale. No capital could be made on the submission that since tax was not being collected by local authorities it was beyond the power of State under Entry 52 List II. Appeals disposed of.

(G) Indirect Tax - Orissa Entry Tax Act, 1999, ss. 2(d), 2(j), 3, 9C - Plant imported - Knocked out - Validity - Whether plant imported in knocked out condition was covered by the 1999 Act.

Held, plant in a knocked out condition was nothing but a collection of machineries. The plant being a wide term including machinery SC fail to see how a knocked out plant should not be covered by Item No. 9 of Part II of the Schedule. Machinery and equipments are wide words which should also cover plant in a knocked out condition. Thus, plant imported in knocked out condition was fully covered with the definition of machinery and equipment under the 1999 Act. Appeals disposed of.
