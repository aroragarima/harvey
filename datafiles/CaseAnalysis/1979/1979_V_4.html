23 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

N.L. Untwalia, Y.V. Chandrachud, P.N. Shingal, P.S. Kailasam, E.S. Venkataramiah, O. Chinnappa Reddy, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 373; (1979) 4 SCC 214; AIR 1979 SC 1745; 1979 (5) ALR 573; 1979 MahLJ 773; 1979 (2) RCJ 358; 1979 (2) RCR(Rent) 352; 1979 (2) RentLR 235; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 334

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Summary: Rent Control - Tamil Nadu Building (Lease and Rent Control) Act, 1960, s. 10(3)(a)(iii) - Transfer of Property Act, ss. 106 and 111(h) - Eviction -Whether in order to get a decree or order for eviction against a tenant under any State Rent Control Act, it is necessary to give a notice u/s. 106 of Transfer of Property Act? - Held, conclusion that determination of a lease in accordance with Transfer of Property Act is unnecessary and mere surplusage because landlord cannot get eviction of tenant even after determined by efflux of time - If termination of contractual tenancy by notice does not, because of Rent Act provisions, entitle landlord to recover possession and he becomes entitled only if he makes out a case under special provision of State Rent Act then, termination of contractual relationship by a notice is not necessary - Purpose of s. 106 of Act is merely to terminate contract which overriding Rent Acts do not permit to be terminated - Special procedure provided by Act displaces requirements of procedure for eviction under Transfer of Property Act and by an ordinary civil suit - A tenancy is essentially based on and governed by an agreement or contract even when a statute intervenes to limit area within which an agreement of contract operates, or, subject contractual rights to statutory rights and obligations - Tenant becomes liable to be evicted and forfeiture comes into play only if he has incurred liability to be evicted under State Rent Act, not otherwise - Appeal dismissed.
