Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation and Another v Bal Mukund Bairwa

Supreme Court of India

12 February 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, S.B. Sinha, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 2092; (2009) 4 SCC 299; (2009) 1 SCC (L&S) 812; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 1857; 2009 (3) ALD(SC) 104; 2009 (4) AWC 3503; JT 2009 (2) SC 423; [2009] 2 S.C.R. 161

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Service

Keywords: Air Force Act, 1950, Navy Act, 1957, Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation Employees Service Regulations, 1965, Rajasthan State Road Transport Workers and Workshop Employees Standing Orders, 1965

Summary: Constitution - Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Road Transport Corporations Act, 1950 - Rajasthan State Road Transport Corporation Employees Service Regulations, 1965 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - Rajasthan State Road Transport Workers and Workshop Employees Standing Orders, 1965 - Jurisdiction of a civil court - Present reference made by a DB for resolution of a purported conflict in two three-Judge' Bench judgments of HC in the cases of Rajasthan State Road Transport corporation & Anr. vs. Krishna Kant & Ors. 1995 INDLAW SC 936 and Rajasthan SRTC & Ors. vs. Khadarmal [2006 (1) SCC 59] - Conflict relates to the jurisdiction of the civil court to entertain suits questioning orders of termination passed by the appellant-Corporation against the respondents - Suits were filed by the respondents, inter alia, on the premise that termination of their services was in violation of the principles of natural justice - (A) Whether in a case of this nature where violation is alleged as regards compliance of principles of natural justice either on common law principles or in terms of the statutory Regulations framed by the appellant-Corporation, which is a fundamental right in terms of art. 14 of the Constitution of India, a civil suit will be maintainable? - Held, in terms of s. 9 of the CPC, civil courts can try all suits, unless barred by statute, either expressly or by necessary implication - Question in regard to the jurisdiction of the civil court must, therefore, be addressed having regard to the fact as to which rights or obligations are sought to be enforced for the purpose of invoking or excluding the jurisdiction of a civil court - In a case where no enquiry has been conducted, there would be violation of the statutory Regulation as also the right of equality as contained in art. 14 of the Constitution - In such situation, a civil suit will be maintainable for the purpose of declaration that the termination of service was illegal and the consequences flowing therefrom - However, if a suit is filed alleging violation of a right by a workman and a corresponding obligation on the part of the employer under the Industrial Disputes Act or the Certified Standing Orders, a civil suit may not lie - Further, in the event it is found that the action on the part of State is violative of the constitutional provisions or the mandatory requirements of a statute or statutory rules, the civil court would have the jurisdiction to direct reinstatement with full back wages - (B) Principles of 'Harmonious Construction' - Held, where the conditions of service are governed by two statutes, the effect thereof on an order passed against an employee/workman in violation of a rule which would attract both the statutes - An attempt shall be made in a case of that nature to apply the principles of 'Harmonious Construction' - (C) Applicability of art. 254(1) of the Constitution - Held, rights and obligations of the employer having arisen under two Parliamentary Acts, the question of invoking the provisions of art. 254(1) of the Constitution would also not arise - Provisions of both the statutes must be given effect to - (D) Doctrine of prospective overruling - Held, application of doctrine of prospective overruling in Krishna Kant may not be correct because either a court has the requisite jurisdiction or it does not have - Further, doctrine of prospective overruling has a limited application - It ordinarily applies where a statute is declared ultra vires and not in a case where the decree or order is passed by a court/tribunal in respect whereof it had no jurisdiction - Question of law answered - Matters be placed before the DB for consideration of the facts of each case - Order accordingly.
