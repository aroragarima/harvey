Mumbai International Airport Private Limited and another v M/S Golden Chariot Airport & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

22 September 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 776; (2010) 10 SCC 422; 2011 (86) ALR 47; 2011 (2) AWC 1278; 2011 (1) Bom.C.R. 527; JT 2010 (10) SC 381; 2010(10) SCALE 69; [2010] 12 S.C.R. 326

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Airports Authority Of India (Amendment) Act, 2003, Indian Easements Act, 1882, Airports Authority Of India Act, 1994, Airports Authority of India Act, 1991, Airports Authority of India Contract Regulations, 2003

Summary: (A) Contract & Commercial - Land & Property - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971, ss. 5, 9 - Extension of license - Oral consent - Reliability - Appellant/Authority entered into a license agreement with 1st respondent/Company for running a restaurant in front of a airport terminal for a period of three years -Appellant allegedly extended the license orally and thereby 1st respondent invested considerably in the building of restaurant - Appellant sought eviction of 1st respondent from the demised premises before the expiry of extension period - 1st respondent filed a suit for permanent injunction against the appellant wherein 1st respondent pleaded that the license in question was irrevocable - Trial Court returned the plaint on the ground that the Trial Court had no pecuniary jurisdiction over the matter - 1st respondent filed appeal before the HC - 1st respondent pleaded that they would ready to drop the prayer that the license in question was irrevocable - HC remanded the matter back to Trial Court for fresh disposal - Trial Court dismissed the suit - Appellant, subsequently, initiated the eviction proceedings before the Estate Officer - Estate Officer held that the 1st respondent was in unauthorized occupation of the licensed premises, which was a public premises and it was liable to be evicted from the said premises u/s. 5 of the Act - 1st respondent filed an appeal before the District Court u/s. 9 of the Act - District Court dismissed the appeal - 1st respondent challenged the District Court order before the HC - HC, by impugned order, allowed the writ petition and set aside the District Court order - Hence the present appeal - Whether the 1st respondent was entitled to continue the occupation in demised premises - Held, alleged oral extension of license by some officers of appellant was of no legal consequence - No such assurance had been proved by 1st respondent, even if it was proved, such assurance did not and could not bind the appellant - Being a statutory corporation, appellant is totally bound by the Act and the Regulations framed under the Act with regard to the extension of license - Clauses of the licence clearly revealed that by its terms the licence was revocable - When the Trial Court returned the plaint filed by the 1st respondent it came up in appeal against the said Order before the HC wherein it expressly gave up its claim of irrevocable licence in order to revive the suit - On such stand being taken, the HC remanded the suit back to Trial Court - Therefore, 1st respondent took a stand before the Court of Law and also got the benefit as a result of taking such stand in as much as it got the suit revived and tried and got the benefit of an interim order in the said proceedings - Since the suit went for three years before the Trial Court, 1st respondent could run the restaurant in the demised premises during that period - Further, reasoning given by the Estate Officer, for not calling the officers of appellant to prove the case of oral extension of licence of the 1st respondent, is sound and does not call for any interference - No error on Estate Officer order and hence the impugned HC order is set aside - Appeal allowed.

(B) Contract & Commercial - Constitution - Public Premises (Eviction of Unauthorised Occupants) Act, 1971- Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - License to run the business in airport terminal - Extension - Denial - Plea of discrimination - Sustainability - Appellant/licensor initiated eviction proceedings against 1st respondent/licensee before the Estate Officer - 1st respondent contended that appellant had extended the license period to other similar situated licensees but not to him and hence there was discrimination - Estate Officer rejected 1st respondent's plea of discrimination - Whether rejection of 1st respondent's plea of discrimination was justifiable - Held, Estate Officer held that it had to decide whether the contesting respondent was in unauthorized occupation of the public premises within the meaning of the 1971 Act and his enquiry cannot be widened by including a plea of discrimination u/art. 14 of the Constitution raised by the 1st respondent - Findings of the Estate Officer with regard to the rejection of plea of discrimination was correct since the sole purpose of enquiry was to decide whether the contesting respondent was in unauthorized occupation under the provisions of the Act - Further, 1st respondent's plea of discrimination was not factually correct in as much as the licence of the 1st respondent was also extended twice - In any event, a plea of discrimination can only be raised in aid of a right, however, 1st respondent had no right in law, to get its licence extended - Therefore, 1st respondent cannot have a plea of negative equality u/art. 14 of the Constitution - Appeal allowed.
