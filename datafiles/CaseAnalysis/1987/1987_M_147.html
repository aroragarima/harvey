Collector Land Acquisition, Anantnag And Anr. v Mst. Katiji And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1987

Case Analysis

Bench

M.P. Thakkar, B.C. Ray

Where Reported

1987 Indlaw SC 28811; (1987) 2 SCC 107; (1989) SCC (Tax) 172; AIR 1987 SC 1353; 1987 (13) ALR 306; [1987] 62 Comp Cas 370; 1987 (62) CTR 23; 1988 (19) ECR 565; 1987 (28) E.L.T. 185; 1987 (55) FLR 609; [1987] 167 ITR 471; JT 1987 (3) SC 537; 1987 (1) LLJ 500; 1987(3) SCALE 413; [1987] 2 S.C.R. 387; 1987 (66) STC 228; [1987] 35 TAXMAN 17; 1987 (2) UJ 29

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure; Sales Tax

Summary: Practice and Procedure - Limitation Act, 1963, s.5 - Application for condonation of delay by State Govt. - (A) Whether to condone, or not to condone the delay is in question - Sufficient cause exists for the delay - Held, delay is condoned and order dismissing the appeal as time-barred is set aside - (B) Whether or not to apply the same standard in applying the 'sufficient cause' test to all the litigants regardless of their personality - A step-motherly treatment when the 'State' is the applicant praying for condonation of delay - Held, such treatment is not warranted - Appeal allowed.
