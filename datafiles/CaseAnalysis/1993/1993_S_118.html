Rajasthan Housing Board and Others v Shri Kishan and Others

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, Kuldip Singh

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 862; (1993) 2 SCC 84; AIR 1993 SCW 1163; 1993 (3) AWC 1540; 1993 (1) CCC 673; JT 1993 (1) SC 298; 1993 (1) RentLR 281; 1993(1) SCALE 183; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 269; 1993 (1) UJ 340

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953, ss. 4(1), 5-A, 6, 17(1) and 17(4) - Writ Petition against notification dismissed - Later Division Bench referred matter to Full Bench of High Court which quashed notification issued by Govt. of Rajasthan u/s. 4(1) of Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953 proposing to acquire a large extent of land for benefit of Rajasthan Housing Board - Notification was issued u/s. 17(4) dispensing with provisions of s. 5-A - Whether it is permissible to treat entire land as arable if some fraction of an arable land is used for residential and other purposes, and issue notification u/s. 17(4) r/w s. 17(1) of Rajasthan Land Acquisition Act, 1953? - Held, s. 17(4) does not require that notification itself should recite fact that land concerned is waste or arable, Govt. was not acquiring any property which was substantially covered by buildings - It acquired land in outskirts of city which was described as arable land by Collector - Where a large extent of land is being acquired for planned development of urban area it would not be proper to leave small portions over which some super-structures have been constructed out of development scheme - In such a situation where there is real urgency it would be difficult to apply s. 5-A of Act - Land is waste or arable, has to be judged by looking at general nature and condition of land - Appeal allowed.
