Basheer @ N. P. Basheer v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

09 February 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Srikrishna, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1092; (2004) 3 SCC 609; (2004) SCC (Cr) 1107; AIR 2004 SC 2757; 2004 CRLJ 1418; 2004 (175) E.L.T. 12; JT 2004 (2) SC 299; 2004 (2) KLT 39; 2004 (1) RCR(Criminal) 1008; 2004 (60) RLT 843; 2004(2) SCALE 415; [2004] 2 S.C.R. 224; 2004 (1) UC 670

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Health & Drug

Keywords: Material Change, Criminal Statute, Personal Consumption

Summary: Criminal - Health & Drug - Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances Act, 1985, ss. 21, 22 and 27 - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Constitutional validity of proviso to sub-s. (1) of s. 41 of Narcotic Drugs and Psychotropic Substances (Amendment) Act, 2001 (Act 9 of 2001) - Held, if an amending Act reduces punishment for an offence, there is no reason why accused should not have benefit of such reduced punishment - Despite retrospectively, they ought to be applied to cases pending before court or even to cases pending investigation on date on which Amending Act came into force - Such application would not be hit by art. 20(1) of Constitution - Merely because classification has not been carried out with mathematical precision, or that there are some categories distributed across dividing line, is hardly a ground for holding that legislation falls foul of art. 14, as long as there is broad discernible classification based on intelligible differentia, which advance object of legislation, even if it be class legislation and as long as extent of over-inclusiveness or under-inclusiveness of classification is marginal, Constitutional vice of infringement of art. 14 would not infect legislation - In cases of pending trials, and cases pending investigation, trial is yet to conclude, hence, retrospective mollification of rigour of punishment has been made applicable - Appeal disposed of.
