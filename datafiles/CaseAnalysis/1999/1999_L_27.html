State of Himachal Pradesh v Lekh Raj and Another

Supreme Court of India

02 November 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

R.P. Sethi, S. Saghir Ahmad

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 488; (2000) SCC (Cr) 147; (2000) 1 SCC 247; AIR 1999 SC 3916; AIR 1999 SCW 4008; 1999 (4) CCR 244; 2000 CRLJ 44; 2000 (1) JCC 57; JT 1999 (9) SC 43; 2000 (1) RCR(Criminal) 10; 1999(7) SCALE 86; 2000 (1) SCJ 363; 1999 (9) SLT 222; [1999] Supp4 S.C.R. 286; 1999 (9) Supreme 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 376(2)(g) - Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 3 and 9 - (A) Whether absence of identification parade is fatal to rape prosecution? - Held, yes - Identity of accused cannot be said to be established - Not to be held guilty - (B) Whether absence of dead or mobile spermatozoa in vaginal smear and slides will defeat allegation of rape? - Further held, no - Possibility of prosecutrix being subjected to forcible sexual intercourse cannot be ruled out - Medico - legal certificate described that injuries on person of prosecutrix reflected signs of struggle - Injuries also synchronized with time of alleged incident - Prosecutrix cannot be said to have any ulterior motive - Accused to be convicted - (C) Difference between description of area of crime in evidence provided and FIR, whether defeats prosecution? - Discrepancy in mentioning road as path in FIR - Proscecutrix was at that time nervous, hesitant and scared - Discrepancy is trivial not amounting to contradictions and does not render prosecution doubtful - Appeal partly allowed.
