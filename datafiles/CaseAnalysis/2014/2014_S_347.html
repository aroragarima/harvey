Munna v State of Madhya Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

16 September 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 624; (2014) 10 SCC 254; 2014 (4) CGLJ 323; 2014 (3) JabLJ 213; 2014 (2) OLR 995; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 377; 2014(10) SCALE 613; [2014] 7 S.C.R. 822

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 376, 450 - Rape - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused/appellant committed offence punishable u/ss. 376, 450 of IPC - Trail Court passed Conviction order - Hence instant appeal -

Held, while absence of injuries or absence of raising alarm or delay in FIR may not by itself be enough to disbelieve version of prosecutrix in view of statutory presumption u/s. 114A of the Act but if such statement has inherent infirmities, creating doubt about its veracity, same may not be acted upon. SC are conscious of sensitivity with which heinous offence u/s. 376 of IPC has to be treated but in present case circumstances taken as a whole create doubt about correctness of prosecution version. A case is made out for giving benefit of doubt to accused. Conviction order was set aside. Appeal allowed.
