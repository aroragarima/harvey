Â The Amalgamated Coalfields Ltd. And Others v The Janapada Sabha, Chhindwara

Supreme Court of India

10 February 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, S.K. Das, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 201; AIR 1961 SC 964; 1964 CRLJ 443; 1962 (1) SCJ 445; [1962] 1 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Defence & Security Forces; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Municipalities & Local Governments

Keywords: Attorney-General

Summary: Mines & Minerals - Constitution - Central Provinces Local Self Govt. Act, 1920, s. 51 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 227 and 372 - Government of India Act, 1935, s. 143 - Whether levy of coal tax by local authority which was authorized by local legislature was violative of petitioner's right u/art. 19(1)(f) and (g) - Held, validity of any Act of any local legislature shall not be open to question in any legal proceedings on ground that Act affects a central subject which is of wider import and designed to remove all questions of legislative competence of type now put forward from purview of Courts - Objection that 'coal tax' or some of components of it, could have been imposed only by Central Govt. or Union Govt. is no ground for impugning continued validity and eligibility

of tax - It is needless to add that if tax fell within Provincial or State List, levy would be valid u/s. 292 of Act, 1935 and art. 372 of Constitution even without aid of special provision in s. 143 or art. 277 - Petition dismissed.
