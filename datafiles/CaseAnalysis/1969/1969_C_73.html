Commissioner of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh v Lady Kanchanbai

Supreme Court of India

16 December 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 335; (1970) 1 SCC 140; AIR 1970 SC 691; [1970] 77 ITR 123; [1970] 3 S.C.R. 323

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Vocation

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s.2(11)(i)(a) - Taxable territory - Assessee/Respondent, Hindu Undivided Family, derives income from property, business, etc with branches at several places - Prior to 1950-51, assessee assessed under 1922 Act in status of nonresident Hindu Undivided Family and income from former Indian States was not subject to tax - After Constitution came force, taxable territories was incorporated in areas in which assessee carrying businesses - For 1950-51, assessee liable to be taxed in its income for previous year - Income accrued from its businesses in 1950-51 year ends on diwali day, but assessee chose financial year ending on March 31, 1950 for purpose of accounts as previous year - Income Tax Officer and Appellate Assistant Commissioner rejected claim of assessee - Income Tax Appellate Tribunal reversed that finding and agreed with stand taken by assessee - Reference made to HC u/s. 66(1) of 1922 Act - HC agreed with view of tribunal and answered in favour of assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether income outside taxable territories before its commencement was taxable - Held, income derived by assessee from businesses carried on by it in territories outside taxable territories were not brought to tax under 1922 Act - View of HC was agreed as s.2 (1)(i)(a) means income from particular source, brought to tax under 1922 Act and not taken into consideration for computing total world income of assessee and assessee refers to person whose income, profits or gains, in particular source had been once assessed to tax - Appeal dismissed.
