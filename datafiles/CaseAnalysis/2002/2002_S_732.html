Ram Govind Upadhyay v Sudarshan Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

U.C. Banerjee, Y.K. Sabharwal

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 179; (2002) 3 SCC 598; (2002) SCC (Cr) 688; AIR 2002 SC 1475; 2002 (2) CCR 16; 2002 (2) Crimes 15; 2002 (3) CRJ 639; 2002 CRLJ 1849; 2003 (1) G.L.R. 829; 2002 (1) JCC 622; JT 2002 (3) SC 185; 2002 (2) RCR(Criminal) 250; 2002(3) SCALE 12; 2002 (2) SCJ 499; [2002] 2 S.C.R. 526; 2002 (2) SLT 587; 2002 (2) Supreme 457; 2002 (1) UC 609

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: The High Court committed a manifest error in the matter of grant of bail when public tranquility has been stated to be disturbed on the Election Day and when there is an obstruction for the exercise of a right guaranteed under the Constitution and when there is an existence of crime against the society at large.
