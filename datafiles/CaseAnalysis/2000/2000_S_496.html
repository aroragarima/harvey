Mallikarjuna Mudhagal Nagappa and Others v State of Karnataka and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

Doraiswamy Raju, M. Jagannadha Rao

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 266; (2000) 7 SCC 238; AIR 2000 SC 2976; 2000 (6) ALD(SC) 75; 2000 (41) ALR 74; 2000 (4) AWC 3052; 2001 (1) ESC 60; JT 2000 (10) SC 176; 2000(6) SCALE 311; 2000 (5) SLR 286; 2000 (5) SLR 288; 2000 (6) SLT 635; [2000] Supp3 S.C.R. 102; 2000 (6) Supreme 209; 2000 (3) UPLBEC 2440

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - Limit for intake of students - Institution has two sections - Maximum number that can be admitted was 60 - Maximum intake of the institution was 30 students per section - Petitioners were the extra 15 students who were admitted, the total being 75 students - Students and institution had filed a joint writ petition, which were dismissed - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, admission was illegal. If the admission was not valid and was beyond the permitted quota of 60, no relief can be given to the petitioners. Petitions dismissed.
