Murugan and Another v State, Represented By Public Prosecutor, Madras and Another

Supreme Court of India

30 September 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 1573; (2008) 16 SCC 40; AIR 2009 SC 72; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 2955; JT 2008 (10) SC 631; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 33; 2008 (4) RCR(Criminal) 906; 2009 (1) RLW 544; 2008(13) SCALE 117; [2008] 13 S.C.R. 1160

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Demeanour Of Witness, Power Of The Court, Finding Of Facts, Hearing Of Appeal

Summary: IPC, 1860, ss. 307, 307 r/w 109 - Trial Court acquitted appellant - High Court held, that the prosecution established the accusations against the appellants - Appeal against conviction - Held, merely because P.W.2 was conscious at that time, it cannot be said that the statement should not have been recorded from P.W.1 and the same is doubtful - No law prohibits the police officer from recording complaint relating to the occurrence, that too, from an eye witness - Ground of acquittal as recorded by trial Court is not at all a proper ground - High Court's judgment is correct - Appeal dismissed.
