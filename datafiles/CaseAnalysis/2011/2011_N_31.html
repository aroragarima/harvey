Morvi Sarvajanik Kelavni Mandal Sanchalit MSKM B.Ed. College and another v National Council For Teachers’Education And Others

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 756; (2012) 2 SCC 16; 2012 (91) ALR 530; 2012 (3) AWC 2671; 2012 (2) Bom.C.R. 621; 2012 (3) MahLJ 510; 2012 (3) MLJ(SC) 134; 2012 (2) MPLJ 490; 2011(13) SCALE 598; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 555; 2012 (1) SLR 791; 2012 (1) UPLBEC 312

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - National Council of Teachers' Education Act, 1993 - National Council of Teachers' Education (Recognition Norms & Procedure) Regulations, 2007 - Recognition to B.Ed. college - Withdrawal - Legality - 1st respondent Council, by order dated 20-7-2011, withdrew recognition granted to appellant educational institution for conducting B.Ed. course - Appellant challenged 1st respondent's order before HC - HC issued an interim order allowing appellant to admit students for academic year 2011-2012 and directed 1st respondent to conduct inspection afresh - 1st respondent, in the meantime, issued a modified order dated 24-8-2011 withdrawing recognition relying upon latest inspection team report - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition and disallowed it to continue academic session 2011-2012 - (A) Whether 1st respondent was justified in withdrawing recognition granted to appellant institution - Held, appellant institution was inspected more than once and several deficiencies that seriously affected its capacity to impart quality education and training to future teachers specifically pointed out - Inadequacy of space and staff, apart from other requirements stipulated under the provisions of the Act and the Regulations, was something which disqualified any institution from seeking recognition - Such deficiencies was not disputed nor could the same be disputed in the light of inspecting teams reports from time to time, including latest inspection report that was conducted pursuant to HC direction - It was difficult to appreciate how appellant institution could have reported compliance with the requirements of the Regulations and complete removal of deficiencies after HC order when appellant had neither land standing in its name nor building constructed in which it could conduct training programme - Appellant institution was being run in a building which was shared by two other colleges was itself sufficient to justify withdrawal of recognition granted in its favour - It was also noted by inspecting team that four lecturers employed by appellant did not have requisite M.Ed. qualification - Appellant institution was lacking in essential infrastructural facilities that clearly justified withdrawal of recognition earlier granted to it - (B) Whether HC was justified in not allowing students admitted for academic session 2011-2012 to continue in appellant institution - Held, recognition of appellant stood withdrawn on 20-7- 2011 which meant that while it had no effect qua admissions for academic session 2010-2011 it was certainly operative qua admissions made for academic session 2011-12 which commenced from 1-8-2011 onwards - Modification of order of withdrawal on 24-8- 2011 did not obliterate earlier order dated 20-7-2011 - Modifying order would relate back and be effective from 20-7- 2011 when the recognition was first withdrawn - Hence, admissions made for academic session 2011-2012 were not protected under statute - Further, students were not allowed to continue in unrecognised institutions only on sympathetic considerations - Appeal dismissed.
