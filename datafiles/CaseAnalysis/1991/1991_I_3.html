Attar Singh Gurmukh Singh v Income Tax Officer, Ludhiana Etc.

Supreme Court of India

07 August 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jagannatha Shetty, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 927; (1991) 4 SCC 385; AIR 1991 SC 2109; 1991 (3) CompLJ 58; 1991 (97) CTR 251; [1991] 191 ITR 667; JT 1991 (3) SC 352; 1991(2) SCALE 254; [1991] 3 S.C.R. 405; [1991] 59 TAXMAN 11; 1992 (2) TLR 222

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 40 A - Tax Rules, 1962, r. 6 DD - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(f) - Business expenditure - Disallowance of deduction on payment exceeding Rs. 100,000 made otherwise than by crossed cheque - Whether amounts to curtailing freedom of trade or business - Held, payments made for purchases would also be covered by word 'expenditure' and such payments can be disallowed if they are made in cash in sums exceeding amount specified u/s. 40 A(3) - It does not amount to curtailing freedom of trade or business - Appeals dismissed.
