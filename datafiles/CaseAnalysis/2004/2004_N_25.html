Bharat Petroleum Corporation Limited and another v N.R. Vairamani and another

Supreme Court of India

01 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, C.K. Thakker

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 831; (2004) 8 SCC 579; AIR 2004 SC 4778; 2004 (58) ALR 139; 2004 (58) ALR 145; JT 2004 (8) SC 171; 2004 (4) MLJ(SC) 147; 2004 (2) RCR(Rent) 486; 2004(8) SCALE 376; [2004] Supp4 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Presidency Small Causes Courts Act, 1882, Tamil Nadu City Tenants Protection Act, 1921, Madras City Tenant's Protection (Amendment) Act, 1973

Summary: Rent Control - Tamil Nadu City Tenant's Protection Act, 1921, ss. 3 and 9 - Eviction - Whether eviction can be granted? - Held, in a writ petition some benefits available to tenant under Tenants Act could not have been diluted - Right conferred by s 9 is a statutory right to purchase land and it does not create any interest or right to property - Tenant's right to secure only such portion of holding as may be necessary for his convenient enjoyment is equitable in nature - Landlord to move the appropriate Court and initiate proceedings as prescribed under Tenants Act - Appeal allowed.
