Krishna Janardhan Bhat v Dattatraya G. Hegde

Supreme Court of India

11 January 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 49; (2008) 4 SCC 54; (2008) 2 SCC (Cr) 166; AIR 2008 SC 1325; 2008 ALL MR (Cri) 1164; 2008 (2) BC 44; 2008 (5) Bom.C.R. 470; [2008] 141 Comp Cas 665; 2012 (5) CompLJ 237; 2008 CRLJ 1172; 2008 (1) CTC 433; 2008 (1) JCC (NI) 50; JT 2008 (1) SC 485; 2009 (6) KarLJ 538; 2008 (1) KLT 425; 2008 (4) MahLJ 354; 2008 (1) RCR(Civil) 498; 2008 (1) RCR(Criminal) 695; 2008(1) SCALE 421; [2008] 1 S.C.R. 605; 2008 (2) WLN(SC) 1

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Legally Recoverable, Cheques, Negative Evidence

Summary: Negotiable Instruments Act, s. 139 - Appeal against conviction - Held, courts below, committed a serious error in proceeding on the basis that for proving the defence the accused is required to step into the witness box and unless he does so he would not be discharging his burden - Appeal allowed.
