State Of Tamil Nadu v Pyare Lal Malhotra Etc.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, R.S. Sarkaria, P.N. Shingal

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 88; (1976) SCC (Tax) 102; (1976) 1 SCC 834; AIR 1976 SC 800; 1976 (5) CTR 278; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1582; [1976] 3 S.C.R. 168; 1976 (37) STC 319; 1976 TAXLR 1519

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Keywords: Central Act

Summary: Sales Tax - Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, s. 14 - Tamil Nadu Sales Tax Act, 1959, s. 15 - Declared goods - Tax levied - Validity - Instant appeals filed assailing the judgment of DB dismissing writ petitions filed against notices issued by a Commercial Tax officer showing institution of Sales tax assessment proceedings in respect of certain iron and steel goods for the A/y 1965-66 and 1966-67 - It was claimed, on behalf of the dealers, sought to be assessed in each case, that, by reason of the restrictions imposed by s. 15 of the Act, the levy of tax was not permissible - Whether tax could be levied on goods(iron and steel) in question - Held, it was true that question whether goods to be taxed have been subjected to a manufacturing process so as to produce a new market able commodity, was the decisive test in determining whether an excise duty was leviable or not on certain goods - No doubt, in the law dealing with the sales tax, the taxable event was the sale and not the manufacture of goods - Nevertheless, if the question was whether a new commercial commodity had come into existence or not, so that its sale was a new taxable event, in the Sales Tax law, it might also become necessary to consider whether a manufacturing process, which has altered the identity of the commercial commodity, had taken place - Law of sale tax was also concerned with 'goods' of various descriptions - It, therefore, becomes necessary to determine when they cease to be goods of one taxable description and become those of a commercially different category and description - It appeared that the position had been simplified by the amendment of the law, as indicated above, so that each of the categories falling under 'Iron and Steel' constitutes a new species of commercial commodity more clearly new - It follows that when one commercial commodity was transformed into another, it becomes a separate commodity for purposes of sales tax - It had not been shown that any provision of the 1959 Act violates s. 15 of 1956 Act - Impugned orders of HC were set aside and restored the orders of the assessing authorities - Appeals allowed.
