D. M. Nagaraja v Government of Karnataka and others

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 662; (2011) 10 SCC 215; AIR 2012 SC 295; 2012 (1) KarLJ 1; 2012 (1) MLJ(Crl) 345; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 264; 2011(10) SCALE 592; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 458

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Continuous

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Constitution - Karnataka Prevention of Dangerous Activities of Bootleggers, Drug-Offenders, Gamblers, Goondas, Immoral Traffic Offenders and Slum-Grabbers Act, 1985, ss. 2(a),3 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143,147,148,149,302 - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19, 22(5) - Detention - Representation - Delay - Justifiability - Appellant/detenue was detained for period of 12 months, in spite of making representation to withdraw detention order to respondents - Appellant/detenue challenged detention order before DB - DB upheld detention order and dismissed petition - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant/detenue contended that action could be taken against detenue under ordinary laws and there was no need to detain him under the Act - Whether order of detention passed by respondents and its confirmation by HC was justifiable - Held, appellant-detenue started his career in criminal field when he was 30 years old and now he was about 60 years , who had systematically committed various criminal activities - Appellant /detenue had no regard for human life - Various punishments imposed had not deterred him to put a stop to his criminal activities - All these aspects had been meticulously considered by respondents and after finding that in order to maintain public order, since his activities were prejudicial to public, causing harm and danger, respondents detained him for a period of 12 months, which was approved by advisory board and State Govt. - There was no constitutional mandate u/art. 22 (5) much less any statutory requirement to consider representation before confirming order of detention - Appeal dismissed.
