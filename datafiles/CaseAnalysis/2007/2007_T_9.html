T. Ashok Pai v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bangalore

Supreme Court of India

18 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 582; (2007) 7 SCC 162; [2007] 292 ITR 11; JT 2007 (8) SC 525; 2007(8) SCALE 354; [2007] 7 S.C.R. 556; [2007] 161 TAXMAN 340

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Finance Act, 1975

Summary: Income Tax Act - Plea that Tribunal having arrived at a finding of fact that the appellant was not guilty of deliberate concealment of his income and thus, having no mens rea in this behalf, the impugned judgment cannot be sustained - Held, if an explanation given by the assessee with regard to the mistake committed by him has been treated to be bona fide and it has been found as of fact that he had acted on the basis of wrong legal advice, the question of his failure to discharge his burden in terms of explanation appended to s. 271(1)(C) would not arise - It is not a case where penalty has been imposed for breach of contravention of a commercial statute where lack of or intention to contravene or existence of bona fie may not be of much importance - It is also not a case where penalty is mandatorily impossible - It was, therefore, not a case where the enabling provision should have been invoked - Appeal allowed.
