Bhuwan Mohan Singh v Meena and others

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, V. Gopala Gowda

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 452; (2015) 6 SCC 353; AIR 2014 SC 2875; 2014 (106) ALR 217; 2014 CRLJ 3979; 2014 (5) CTC 528; 2014 (3) JCC 2048; JT 2014 (8) SC 359; 2014 (3) KLT 314; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 108; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 749; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 723; 2014 (125) RD 431; 2014 (4) RLW 2959; 2014(8) SCALE 573; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 858

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Family & Personal - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 125 - Maintenance - Sustainability - Respondent wife and appellant husband was married as per hindu rites and ritual - Respondent under certain circumstances had to leave marital home and thereafter filed application u/s. 125 CrPC in Family Court and claimed Rs.6000/- per month towards maintenance - Family Court awarded monthly maintenance of Rs.2500/- to respondent and Rs.1500/- to respondent-son and directed that maintenance to be paid from date of order - Being dissatisfied with d order respondent filed petition before HC - Respondent contended that noted that maintenance should have been granted from date of application, and that she received nothing during proceedings and suffered immensely - Single Judge directed that maintenance should be granted from date of filing of application - Hence instant appeal- Whether order recorded by HC was sustainable -

Held, there was enormous delay in disposal of proceeding u/s. 125 CrPC and most of time husband had taken adjournments and sometimes, Court dealt with matter showing total laxity - Wife sustained herself as far as she could in that state for period of 9 years - Circumstances, required grant of maintenance from date of application and by so granting HC had not committed any legal infirmity - Hence, SC concur with order of HC - However, it was directed that appellant might be allowed to pay arrears along with maintenance awarded - Hence, SC directed that while paying maintenance as fixed by Family Court per month by 5th of each succeeding month, arrears should be paid in proportionate manner within period of 3 years from today - Appeal dismissed.
