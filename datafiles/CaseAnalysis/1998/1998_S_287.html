Kuldeep K. Mahato v State of Bihar

Supreme Court of India

06 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Kurdukar, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 1008; (1998) 6 SCC 420; (1998) SCC (Cr) 1460; AIR 1998 SC 2694; 1998 (3) CCR 177; 1998 (3) Crimes 151; 1998 CRLJ 4033; JT 1998 (5) SC 395; 1998 (3) RCR(Criminal) 799; 1998(4) SCALE 476; 1998 (6) SLT 579; 1998 (6) Supreme 290

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 363, 366, 368 and 376 - Kidnapping and rape - Appellant convicted by Trial Court and High Court - Conviction challenged - Held, age of prosecutrix found to be below 18 yrs so charge of kidnapping stands proved - Conviction u/s. 366 cannot be sustained as from evidence of prosecutrix it was not conclusive that kidnapping was effected to marry her - But charge of rape cannot be sustained as conduct of prosecutrix is doubtful as she had enough opportunity to run away but did not and hence indicating consent - Her entire conduct clearly shows that she was a consenting party and if this be so, conviction of appellant u/s. 376 cannot be sustained - Appeal partly allowed.
