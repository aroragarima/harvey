Jetha Bhaya Odedara v Ganga Maldebhai Odedara And Anr.

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

T.S. Thakur, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 773; (2012) 2 SCC 150; AIR 2012 SC 775; 2012 (1) CGLJ 368; 2012 CRLJ 998; 2012 (2) RCR(Criminal) 920; [2011] 16 S.C.R. 370

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 - Arms Act 1959, s. 25(1) - Bombay Police Act 1951, s.135 - Enlargement on Bail - Legality - Pending cases - Petitioner/complainant alleged that respondent had abused, assaulted and fired multiple rounds from pistol in air and exhorted his companions to kill him and others - Crime was registered against respondent u/ss. 302, 307, 323, 324 of IPC r/w s. 25(1) of 1959 Act and s. 135 of 1951 Act - Respondent filed application for bail before Trial Court - Trial Court dismissed application holding that respondent was involved in several criminal cases apart from instant case - Respondent appealed to HC - HC granted bail holding that no injury was caused with the help of firearm - Aggrieved by HC order, petitioner/complainant filed instant petition - Petitioner alleged that respondent and other accused persons were a desperate gang active and there were several criminal cases pending against them - Whether instant petition was instigated in pursuance to gang war between respondent and petitioner - Held, incident appeared to be result of gang war of which respondent, petitioner and witnesses were active members of gangs - Respondent was enlarged on bail nearly two years back - Respondent did not try to tamper with evidence or committed any other act, which effected trial - No gunshot injury was inflicted to either petitioner or deceased or any other person involved in incident - However, nothing held in the instant order should prejudice either prosecution or defence and prosecution was free to apply for cancellation of bail order - Petition dismissed.
