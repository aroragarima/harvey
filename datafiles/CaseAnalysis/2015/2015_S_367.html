Godrej and Boyce Manufacturing Company Limited v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Gopala Gowda, R. Banumathi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 35; 2015(2) ALL MR 921; 2015 (2) Bom.C.R. 354; JT 2015 (1) SC 404; 2015 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1430; 2015 (2) MLJ 243; 2015 (3) RCR(Civil) 668; 2015(1) SCALE 578; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 477

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Maharashtra Regional and Town Planning Act, 1966, ss. 127, 37 and 37(1) - Land Acquisition Act, 1894 - Modification of reserved land - Challenged - Petitioner filed writ petition challenging the correctness of the notification dt. 5-8-2008 proposing the modification of reservation of the land in question from the Railway line to Development Plan Road - Petitioner by placing strong reliance upon s. 127 of the 1966 Act, contended that the proposed modification by the Urban Development Department was impermissible in law as the State Govt. had no power to do so - Further contended that the land which was reserved earlier in the Development Plan for railway line, the period of 10 years and 6 months after issuing notice was lapsed, now proposed to be reserved for Development Plan Road - HC dismissed the writ petition by holding that the action of the State Govt. was only proposed modification and therefore, the writ petition cannot be entertained at this stage - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, HC has not examined the impugned notification from the view point of s. 127 of the 1966 Act and interpretation of the said provision in the case of Girnar Traders v. State of Maharashtra And Others 2007 Indlaw SC 858, therefore, giving liberty to the petitioner by the HC to file objections to the proposed notification is futile exercise on the part of the petitioner for the reason that the State Govt., once the purpose the land was reserved has not been utilized for that purpose and a valid statutory right is acquired by the land owner/interested person after expiry of 10 years from the date of reservation made in the Development Plan and 6 months notice period is also expired, the State Govt. has not commenced the proceedings to acquire the land by following the procedure as provided u/ss. 4 and 6 of the repealed 1894 Act. Therefore, the land which was reserved for the above purpose is lapsed and it ensures to the benefit of the petitioner. Therefore, it is not open for the State Govt. to issue the impugned notification proposing to modify the Development Plan from deleting for the purpose of Railways and adding to the Development Plan for the formation of Development Plan Road after lapse of 10 years and expiry of 6 months notice served upon the State Govt. The order passed by the HC as well as the impugned notification issued by the State Govt. are vitiated in law and liable to be set aside and quashed and SC order accordingly. Appeal allowed.
