Rahul Agarwal v Rakesh Jain and Another

Supreme Court of India

18 January 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, B.N. Srikrishna

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 23; (2005) 2 SCC 377; (2005) SCC (Cr) 506; AIR 2005 SC 910; 2005 (2) CGLJ 97; 2005 CRLJ 963; 2005 (1) RCR(Criminal) 700; 2005(1) SCALE 381; [2005] 1 S.C.R. 521

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice and Procedure - CrPC, s. 321 - IPC, ss. 341, 294 and 506(2) r/w s. 34 - Withdrawal of the prosecution u/s. 321- Withdrawal of a case pending - Whether HC right in allowing withdrawal of a case pending against first respondent before Judicial First Class Magistrate? - Held, reason given by Single Judge in impugned order is not correct, and when case was about to be over same should not have been allowed to be withdrawn by holding that trial had been pending for over seven years - Directed Judicial First Class Magistrate to restore case to file - Appeal allowed.
