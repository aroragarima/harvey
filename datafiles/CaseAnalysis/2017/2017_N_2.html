Nitya Dharmananda @ K. Lenin and another v Gopal Sheelum Reddy also Known as Nithya Bhaktananda and another

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Adarsh Kumar Goel, Uday Umesh Lalit

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SCO 330; 2017(14) SCALE 319

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: age, appreciation, charge sheet, complainant, criminal justice, criminal justice system, dealing, fir, harm, innocence, inquiry, interest of justice, prayer, production, prosecutor, summons, version

Summary: Criminal â Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.376 â Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s.91 â Summoning of documents â Discretion by Court â Respondent accused was charge sheeted for offences u/s.376 of IPC whereupon accused approached HC praying that entire material available with investigator, which was not made part of charge-sheet ought to be summoned u/s.91 of CrPC and HC allowed application â Hence, instant appeal.

Held, ordinarily the Court has to proceed on the basis of material produced with the charge sheet for dealing with the issue of charge but if the court is satisfied that there is material of sterling quality which has been withheld by investigator or prosecutor, the court is not debarred from summoning or relying upon the same even if such document is not a part of the charge sheet. However, it could not be said that defence has a right to invoke s.91 de hors the satisfaction of the court, at the stage of charge. Hence, impugned judgment of HC is not sustainable and so, the same is set aside. Appeals disposed of.
