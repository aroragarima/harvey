General Manager (Osd), Bengal Nagpur Cotton Mills Rajnandgaon v Bharat Lal And Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, A.K. Patnaik

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1071; (2011) 1 SCC 635; 2011 (128) FLR 560; JT 2010 (13) SC 729; 2011 (1) LLJ 321; 2010(13) SCALE 308; 2011 (1) SLR 140

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Contract Labour (Regulation & Abolition) Act, 1970 - Madhya Pradesh Industrial Relations Act, 1960, s. 31(3) - Discharge from service - Validity - Appellant/Mill entered into a security service agreement with 2nd respondent - 2nd respondent appointed 1st respondent as security guard in appellant's premises - 2nd respondent, subsequently, discharged 1st respondent from service - Appellant terminated the security service agreement with 2nd respondent at later date - 1st respondent raised an industrial dispute u/s. 31(3) of the 1960 Act - Labour Court directed the appellant to reinstate 1st respondent in his previous post and to pay him all arrears - Appellant filed appeal before the Industrial Court - Industrial Court confirmed the Labour Court order - Appellant challenged the Industrial Court order before the HC - HC dismissed appellant's writ petition - Hence the present appeal - Whether 1st respondent was the direct employee of the appellant - Held, two of the well-recognized tests to find out whether the contract labour are the direct employees of the principal employer are: (i) whether the principal employer pays the salary instead of the contractor; and (ii) whether the principal employer controls and supervises the work of the employee - In the instant case, 1st respondent failed to prove that he was paid salary directly by the principal employer (appellant) and not the contractor (2nd respondent) - Further, 1st respondent did not establish that he was working under the direct control and supervision of the principal employer - Industrial Court misconstrued the meaning of the terms 'control and supervision' and held that as the officers of appellant were giving some instructions to the 1st respondent working as a guard, he was deemed to be working under the control and supervision of the appellant - Therefore, Industrial Court committed a serious error in arriving at the impugned findings and it ought to have held that 1st respondent was not a direct employee of the appellant - Further, 1st respondent was employed in another organization during the pendency of the dispute, therefore, he is not entitled to the back wages - Impugned orders of the Labour Court, Industrial Court and the HC are set aside - Appeal allowed.
