Jammu And Kashmir State Forest Corporation v Abdul Karim Wani And Others

Supreme Court of India

31 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

L.M. Sharma, R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 558; (1989) 2 SCC 701; AIR 1989 SC 1498; 1989 (2) ARBLR 145; 1989 (2) CCC 228; JT 1989 (3) SC 99; 1989(1) SCALE 933; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 380; 1989 (2) UJ 262

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Civil Procedure; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Jammu and Kashmir Arbitration Act, 2002 , ss. 18, 20, 41 and 41(b), sch. 2, Paras. 1, 3 and 4 - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 20 - Arbitration - Reference - Dispute - Contractor claim for additional work - Under contract for extraction of specified volume of timber from forest area - Claim that, permissible if good performance - Claim based on practice, custom and assurance given by Forest Department - (A) Whether court can express opinion on merits of dispute while considering question of referability of dispute - (B) - Whether dispute arises out of contract between parties? - (C) Whether dispute is covered under arbitration? - (D) Power of court to issue interim orders at such time - Scope - Held, (A) Court should not express any opinion on merits of dispute - (B ) Cannot be said so - (C) Not covered by arbitration agreement, so cannot be referred - (D) Scope explained - Order accordingly.
