Balraj v State Of U.P.

Supreme Court of India

12 December 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Jayachandra Reddy, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1683; (1994) 4 SCC 29; (1994) SCC (Cr) 823; AIR 1995 SC 1935; 1994 (2) CCR 390; 1994 (2) Crimes 87; 1995 CRLJ 3217; JT 1994 (3) SC 649; 1994(2) SCALE 518; 1994 (1) UJ 601

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 302 - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s. 357(3) - Appeal challenging conviction and death sentence - Accused attacked all members of family and acted in highly disturbed mental condition - Therefore, murders cannot be said to be cold-blooded - Nor committed for personal gain - No direct evidence to connect accused with murders - Award of death sentence cannot be awarded in such circumstances - Wife of deceased awarded Rs. 10,000 by way of compensation - Appeal allowed.
