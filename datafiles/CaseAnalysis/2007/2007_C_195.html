Messrs Kerala State Electricity Board v Commissioner of Central Excise, Thiruvananthapuram

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1304; (2008) 1 SCC 780; AIR 2008 SC 798; 2008 (2) RCR(Civil) 1; 2007(14) SCALE 311; [2007] 13 S.C.R. 420; 2008 (9) S.T.R. 3; [2007] 12 VST 1

Case Digest

Subject: Service Tax

Keywords: Punjab Passengers and Goods Taxation Act, 1952, Finance Act,2000

Summary: Finance Act, 1994 - Whether the appellant - Kerala State Electricity Board, the service recipient, within the meaning of provisions of Finance Act, 1994, levying service tax, is liable to pay any interest on the amount of tax due to the respondent? - Held, it was the appellant who had provided space and accommodation to the personnel of M/s SNC Lavalin in their office premises and borne expenditure related thereto - Service provider did not have any independent office - Clause 16.1 of the contract obligated the foreign company responsible only for filing of returns, estimates, accounts, information and details complete and accurate in all respects as may be required by any law or regulation - Terms of the agreement show that the responsibility of meeting the service tax liability was on the service recipient and despite the amendment of r. 6 (1) w.e.f. 16.8.2002 agreement still held good as the service recipient being the appellant had taken up the responsibility of meeting the liability of the foreign company - Appeal dismissed.
