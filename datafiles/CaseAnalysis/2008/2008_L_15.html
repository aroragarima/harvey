Mohammedia Co-Oprative Building Society v Lakshmi S. Co-Oprative Building Society Limited

Supreme Court of India

06 May 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2106; (2008) 7 SCC 310; AIR 2009 SC (Supp) 590; 2008 (4) ALD(SC) 86; JT 2008 (6) SC 250; 2009 (2) MLJ(SC) 934; 2008 (4) MPLJ 401; 2009 (106) RD 116; 2008(7) SCALE 172; [2008] 7 S.C.R. 762; 2008 (3) UC 1457

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954

Summary: Civil Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Land & Property - Muslim Wakfs Act, 1954, ss. 3F, 43(2) - Specific Relief Act, 1963, ss. 15, 17 - Urban Land (Ceiling and Regulation) Act, 1976 - Suit for specific performance - Validity - Respondent Nos. 2 to 9 allegedly entered into agreement for sale with respondent no. 1 agreeing to transfer wakf property - Allegedly, sum in cash was paid as advance - Notification was issued inviting objections within one month from date of said notification - Allegedly, upon publication of said advertisement, several co-operative societies made their respective offers - Offer made by respondent no. 1 was said to have been accepted - Agreement for sale was executed which was, however, contended by appellant as also respondent Nos. 2 to 9 that said agreement was a forged document - Some correspondences had allegedly passed between respondent no. 1 and Wakf Board whereby respondent no. 1 was said to have raised its offer from Rs.70, 000/- to Rs.1, 26, 000/- by letter - Appellant herein also said to have initially made an offer of Rs.1, 26, 000/- - A bid was conducted wherein appellant offered highest bid of Rs. 1, 28, 000/- - Respondent no. 1 contended that pursuant to said purported agreement for sale it had been put in possession but it was threatened to be dispossessed - Respondent no. 1 filed suit for permanent injunction - Allegedly, it was dispossessed filed and suit for specific performance of contract - Both suits were consolidated - Trial Judge by judgment and order dated decreed said relief of Specific Performance on its depositing balance sale consideration - Three appeals were preferred before HC - HC dismissed all appeals - Hence instant appeals - Whether order & judgment Trial Court and HC sustainable - Held, Trial Judge and HC committed a serious error in holding that as within period of 30 days, objections had not been received notification issued became final - Further SC, stated that issue required serious considerations at hands of Courts below keeping in view fact that suit filed by plaintiffs was one for specific performance of contract - In any event, as said purported letter was neither published in Gazette nor was issued upon receipt of objections and offers from other societies, same could not have been treated to be final order - Hence, impugned judgment could not be sustained - Further held that, State's jurisdiction in matter was supervisory in nature - Wakf Board was statutory body - It was duty of the State to oversee its functions - Property belonging to wakf could not be permitted to be withered away at instance of office bearers of the Board or those in charge of wakf - Govt. should had taken purport of its orders and memos issued by it to their logical conclusion - Therefore, Govt. was well advised to cause enquiry to be made into entire affairs of Wakf Board and others concerned vis-a-vis transactions carried out in matter, albeit after giving opportunity of hearing to parties - Govt. would initiate appropriate proceedings and take such action or actions - Appeals allowed.
