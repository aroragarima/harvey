Indian Oil Corporation Limited v Amritsar Gas Service and Others

Supreme Court of India

19 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, M. Fathima Beevi, S. Ranganathan

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 1008; (1991) 1 SCC 533; 1991 (1) ARBLR 97; JT 1990 (4) SC 601; 1990(2) SCALE 1056; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 196; 1991 (1) UJ 96

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Specific Relief Act, 1963, s. 14 (1) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - (A) Whether validity of award has to be tested on principles of private law and law of contracts and not on touchstone of constitutional limitations? - Held, Questions of public law based on art. 14 of Constitution do not arise for decision in present case and matter must be decided strictly in realm of private law rights governed by general law relating to contracts with reference to provisions of Specific Relief Act providing for non-enforceability of certain types of contracts - (B) When is occasion to make a counter-claim in written statement, Counter-claim could arise only after order of reference? - Whether relief granted in award that is price of cylinders and regulators taken away by appellant-Corporation from plaintiff-respondent? - Further held, no - These articles did not belong to plaintiff-respondent no. 1 and were property of appellant-Corporation and also direction to pay its price to plaintiff-respondent no. 1 also discloses an error of law apparent on face of award - Award modified as indicated - Appeal disposed of.
