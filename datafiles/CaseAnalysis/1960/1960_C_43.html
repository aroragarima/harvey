Raghuvanshi Mills, Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

07 December 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Mohammad Hidayatullah, J.L. Kapur, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 202; AIR 1961 SC 743; [1961] 41 ITR 613; 1961 (2) SCJ 327; [1961] 2 S.C.R. 978

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Corporate - Income Tax Act, 1922 (before amendment by Finance Act, 1955, s. 23A, proviso, 3 Explanation - Applicability of s. 23A, proviso, 3, Explanation of Income Tax Act, 1922 - Tests for deciding company in which public are substantially interested - Held, essence of Explanation in s. 23A lies in words 'unconditionally' and 'beneficially'- No person who holds a share or shares not for his own benefit but for benefit of another and who does not exercise his voting power freely, can be said to come under that body, which is designated 'public' - If union of persons possesses more than 75% of voting power, then company is not one in which public is substantially interested - Appeal allowed.
