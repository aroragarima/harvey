Rajratha Naranbhai Mills. Co. Ltd. v Sales Tax Officer, Petlad

Supreme Court of India

19 February 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

M.M. Punchhi, K.N. Saikia

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 1079; (1991) 3 SCC 283; [1991] 71 Comp Cas 149; 1991 (1) CompLJ 229; 1991 (95) CTR 132; 1991 (52) E.L.T. 335; [1991] 189 ITR 90A; JT 1991 (2) SC 117; 1991(1) SCALE 244; [1991] 1 S.C.R. 527; 1991 (81) STC 233

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Corporate - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Companies Act, 1956, s. 530(1)(a) - Companies (Court) Rules, 1959, r. 164 - Companies Act, 1913, s.230(1)(a) - What is ambit of State's claim to priority in relation to revenues, taxes, cesses and rates, due from a company in liquidation? - Words 'having become due and payable within 12 months next before relevant date' need be understood to mean putting a restriction or cordoning off amount for which priority is claimable - Priority is in respect only of debts those of which become due and payable because liability to those is rooted, founded and belonging to that period of 12 months prior to relevant date - Held, direct liquidator to re-examine claim for priority in accordance with interpretation of provision as to whether liability to sales-tax belongs to and is founded within period of 12 months next before 26th June, 1967 - Appeal allowed.
