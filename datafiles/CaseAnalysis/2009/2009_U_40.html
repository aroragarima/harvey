Tahazhathe Purayil Sarabi & Ors v Union Of India & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

14 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

Altamas Kabir, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1581; (2009) 7 SCC 372; (2009) 3 SCC (Cr) 408; 2009 (4) ACC 921; AIR 2009 SC 3098; 2010 (3) ALD(SC) 25; 2010(4) ALL MR 472; 2009 (4) AWC 3171; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 338; 2009 (4) KLT 370; 2009(8) SCALE 545; [2009] 10 S.C.R. 70

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Arbitration Act, 1940, s. 9, - Banking Companies (Acquisition and Transfer of Undertakings) Act, 1970, - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O.2 r. 2 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 392 - Interest Act, 1978, s. 3 - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, s. 166 - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881 - Railway Claims Tribunal Act, 1987, s. 16 - Railways Act, 1989, ss. 123(c), 123(c)(2), 124, 124A, 127 - Death - Compensation - Rate of Interest - Date of incident - Validity - Appellants filed for compensation before Railway Claims Tribunal - Tribunal directed State and its authorities to pay to appellants compensation of Rs.4, 00, 000/-, out of which sum of Rs.2, 00, 000/- was given to appellant no. 1, sum Rs. 1,50,000/- was allotted to appellant no.2 and Rs.50,000/- was allotted to appellant no.3, moreover, it was directed that appellants would be entitled to 6.5% interest p. a on award amount from date of default - Award of Tribunal was challenged before HC by appellants herein on ground that claim for compensation would arise from date of incident - Appeal was dismissed by HC - Hence, instant appeal - Whether both Tribunal and HC were justified in not granting interest on amount of award from date of incident or from date of filing of Claim Petition till actual payment of awarded sum - Held, neither the 1987 Act, nor the 1989 Act made provision for payment of interest on any awarded amount - There was no denying fact that right to claim compensation accrued on date of incident - Where statute did not make any specific provision for payment of interest on any awarded sum, power of Courts to grant interest could also be referred to from provisions of the 1978 Act and CPC - S. 3 of the 1978 Act, which conferred power on Court to allow interest - Apart from provisions of the 1978 Act, s. 34 CPC also empowers Court to order interest on decree for payment of money - More consistent view, applied by Courts had been that interest was payable from date when claim for principal sum was made viz. date of institution of proceedings till recovery of amount - Hence, Order of HC affirming order of Tribunal had been modified and it was directed that awarded sum would carry interest at rate of 6% simple interest p. a from date of application till date of Award and, thereafter, at rate of 9% p. a till date of actual payment of same - Appeal allowed.
