R. Gandhi v Union of India and Another

Supreme Court of India

23 August 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 661; (1999) 8 SCC 106; (1999) SCC (L&S) 1436; 1999 (2) CLR 838; 1999 (3) ESC 1753; 1999 (83) FLR 207; JT 1999 (6) SC 207; 1999(5) SCALE 151; [1999] Supp1 S.C.R. 661; 1999 (7) Supreme 290; 2000 (1) UPLBEC 269

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Whether order of High Court dismissing petition of appellant claiming that memorandum does not apply to Judges of HC and Supreme Court of India, valid in law? - Held, it is a well-settled principle that the words in the judgment of the Court cannot be interpreted as the words in a statute - Supreme Court issued direction that payment of reduced pension on account of commutation of a part of pension of a Govt. employee to be only for a period of 15 years 'from date of retirement' - UOI issued impugned memorandum changing it to 'from date of commutation of pension'- Action of Govt. arbitrary in paying reduced pension as a result of commutation of 1/3 rd pension for rest of life of pensioners - Court never intended to confer any unfair or undue advantage on pensioners - It only ensured fairness in treatment of pensioners at hands of Govt. - Impugned memorandum does not incorporate any condition contrary to judgment of this Court in Common Cause - HC was right in dismissing writ petition - Appeal dismissed.
