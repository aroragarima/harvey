Indo-Aden Salt Manufacturing and Trading Company Private Limited v Commissioner of Income Tax, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Mukharji, K.N. Singh

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 331; (1986) Supp SCC 279; AIR 1986 SC 1857; 1986 (2) CCC 33; 1986 (58) CTR 9; [1986] 159 ITR 624; JT 1986 SC 642; 1986(1) SCALE 555; [1986] 1 S.C.R. 627; 1986 TAXLR 876; [1986] 25 TAXMAN 356; 1986 (2) TLR 876; 1986 (2) UJ 480

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, s. 147(a) - Reassessment proceedings - Validity - If there is omission to disclose material facts, then, subject to the other conditions, jurisdiction to re-open is attracted - Non-disclosure of primary facts which has caused escapement of income in the assessment was basically a question of fact - Held, assessee fails to disclose fully and truly all material facts thus ITO had jurisdiction to reopen the assessment - Appeal dismissed.
