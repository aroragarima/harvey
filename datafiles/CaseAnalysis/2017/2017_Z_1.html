Z v State of Bihar and others

Supreme Court of India

17 August 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Amitava Roy, A.M. Khanwilkar

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 627; 2017 (5) AWC 4719; JT 2017 (8) SC 282

Case Digest

Subject: Human Rights; Women & Children

Keywords: Guardian, Abortion, Rape, Health, Medical Termination Of Pregnancy Act, 1971, Supervision, Persons With Disabilities (Equal Opportunities, Protection Of Rights And Full Participation) Act, 1995, Pregnancy, Woman, Termination Of Pregnancy, National Trust For Welfare Of Persons With Autism, Cerebral Palsy, Mental Retardation And Multiple Disabilities Act, 1999, Indian Penal Code, 1860, Constitution of India, 1950, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Human Immunodeficiency Virus and Acquired Immune Deficiency Syndrome (Prevention and Control) Act, 2017, Mental Healthcare Act, 2017, Medical Termination of Pregnancy Regulations, 2003

Summary: Human Rights - Women & Children - Medical termination of pregnancy Act, 1971 - Delay in medical termination of pregnancy - Compensation under the domain of public law remedy - Victim-appellant who was living on footpath was brought to Shelter Home and tests conducted on her showed that she was pregnant - Appellant revealed that she was raped and sought to terminate pregnancy - Appellant was taken to PMCH on 14-3-2017 for termination but the same was not done - Later, on 3-4-2017 was taken to PMCH but termination was not carried out and by that time, her pregnancy was 20 weeks old and was also found to be HIV+ve - Since termination was not carried out, appellant approached HC with prayer to ascertain physical condition including stage of pregnancy and to direct for termination of pregnancy as she had been sexually assaulted and also as she was HIV+ve - However, Single Judge of HC declined to permit medical termination of pregnancy of the appellant though issued various directions for the benefit of appellant - Hence, appellant filed instant appeal and SC directed Medical Board of AIIMS to examine appellant - Later, basing on opinion of said Board, termination of pregnancy was denied and so, it was contended by appellant that she is entitled to get compensation for delay caused due to which she was compelled to undergo existing miserable situation - Whether appellant is entitled for compensation within the domain of public law remedy.

Held, Single Judge should have been more alive to provisions of the Act and the necessity of consent only of the appellant in the facts of the case. Further, there was no reason to implead husband and father of appellant. Further, appellant was a destitute, a victim of rape and was staying in a shelter home and calling for a medical report was justified but to delay it further was not at all warranted. Appellant had gone from a women rehabilitation center, had given consent for termination of pregnancy and alleged about rape committed on her, but termination was not carried out. Hence, there was negligence in carrying out the statutory duty, as a result of which, appellant has been constrained to suffer grave mental injury and under such circumstances, State is bound to compensate appellant under public law remedy. Further, termination of pregnancy could have been risky to the life of appellant as per report of Medical Board at AIIMS which constituted as per direction of SC on 3-5-2017 but such situation could have been avoided had the decision been taken at appropriate time by government hospital at Patna with regard to termination of pregnancy and for the negligence and carelessness of hospital, appellant had been constrained to suffer. Hence, in view of mental injury that victim has to suffer, appellant is granted compensation of Rs. 10,00,000 from State and also child to be born, is to be given proper treatment and nutrition by State. Appeal disposed of.
