Appropriate Authority and Another v Kailash Suneja and Another

Supreme Court of India

07 August 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Rajendra Babu, K.G. Balakrishnan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 276; (2001) 6 SCC 563; AIR 2001 SC 2821; 2001 (169) CTR 401; [2001] 251 ITR 1; 2001 (3) JhrCR(SC) 237; JT 2001 (6) SC 252; 2001 (4) RCR(Civil) 1; 2001(5) SCALE 99; 2001 (5) SLT 718; [2001] Supp1 S.C.R. 343; 2001 (6) Supreme 428

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Appropriate authority - Fair Market value - Justifiability - Property comprised of plot was agreed to be sold - Agreement provided for symbolic delivery of possession - Application in Form 37(I) was filed - Appropriate Authority worked out fair market value of property and apparent consideration fell short by 24 per cent of fair market value - Appropriate authority compared property in question with three different properties as sale instances to arrive at correct market value of value of the property in question - HC disapproved this process of arriving at figures and that apparent consideration fell short of fair market value by more than 15 per cent and, therefore, HC held that action was incorrect - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in interfering with authority on arriving fair market value - Held that, if there were loopholes or lacunae in process of reasoning adopted by that authority in reaching conclusion that tenanted property would be vacated soon or that property was close to vicinity of situation of subject property if compared, adopting different methods of valuation, then parties who appear before authorities will definitely have reason to have a heart burn - If one method of valuation was adopted and benefit was given to one party and why that method was not adopted in other case to reach conclusion other way was not clear and it was unjustifiable - If in this background HC examined matter and arrived at conclusion one way or other - Hence, SC did not think it was necessary to interfere with that finding in proceeding - Appeal dismissed.
