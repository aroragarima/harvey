Jayaswal Neco Limited v Commnr. of Central Excise, Raipur

Supreme Court of India

13 May 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, R. F. Nariman

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 379; 2015(4) SCALE 446

Case Digest

Subject: Excise; Indirect Tax

Keywords: Central Sales Tax Act, 1956, Customs Tariff Act, 1975, Central Excise Tariff Act, 1985, Modvat Credit

Summary: Excise - Indirect Tax - Central Excise Rules, 1944, r. 57Q - Manufacture of goods - Denial to grant credit - Justification - Appellant is engaged in manufacture of goods falling under Schedule to Act and is availing MODVAT credit on various capital goods and parts thereof u/r. 57Q of Rules - However, respondent authority issued two show cause notices thereby proposing to deny appellant MODVAT credit availed by them u/r. 57Q of Rules - Commissioner held that goods in question were not covered within definition of 'capital goods' u/r. 57Q of Rules and credit on goods in question was denied by Commissioner - Order passed by Commissioner is affirmed by higher authorities - Whether appellant is entitled to avail credit on goods in question .

Held, use of railway tracks is related to actual production of goods and without use of said railway track commercial production would be inexpedient. Further, railway tracks are installed not only within plant, but main objective and purpose for which capital expenditure on laying these railway tracks is incurred by appellant is for transporting hot metal in ladle placed on ladle car from blast furnace to pig casting machine through ladle car where hot metal is poured into pig casting machine for manufacture of pig iron, hence, use of railway tracks inside plant not only form process of manufacturing, but it is inseparable and integral part of said process inasmuch as without aforesaid activity for which railway tracks are used, there cannot be manufacturing of pig iron. SC held that Commissioner in spite of taking note of aforesaid use of railway tracks and accepting same as correct, Commissioner denied relief to appellant on extraneous ground, i.e., railway tracks were used for other purposes. SC held that it could not be ground to deny relief inasmuch as by incidental use of railway tracks for some other innocuous purpose, it does not lose character of being integral part of manufacturing process. Further, with respect to order passed by higher authority, SC held that it has affirmed order of Commissioner by simply observing that Commissioner has arrived at conclusion that these goods are not being used directly or indirectly for producing or processing goods or for bringing about any change for manufacture of final product. Hence, order passed by lower authorities against appellant is set aside and appellant is entitled to avail credit with respect to railway track material used for handling raw materials and process goods. Appeal allowed.

Ratio - Credit cannot be denied to applicant when goods in question are capital goods and are used in manufacturing process.
