Ahamed Nassar v State of Tamil Nadu and Others

Supreme Court of India

14 October 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

A.P. Misra, K.T. Thomas

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 778; (1999) 8 SCC 473; (1999) SCC (Cr) 1469; AIR 1999 SC 3897; 1999 (4) Crimes 358; 2000 CRLJ 33; JT 1999 (8) SC 252; 1999(6) SCALE 539; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 657

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Human Rights

Summary: Constitution - Criminal - Human Rights - Conservation of Foreign Exchange and Prevention of Smuggling Activities Act, 1974, s. 3(1)(i) - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 22(5) - (A) Whether in matters of personal liberty mode of communication should be through air or through special messenger? - Held, proposition is too far fetched to be accepted - Delay owing to postal authorities cannot be construed as callous, slack or casual disposition of representation - (B) Whether non placement of material documents before formal order of detention was passed in spite of receipt of same vitiated order? - Further held, yes, it does vitiate order as it was a relevant material document - (C) whether subjective satisfaction of authorities in exercising rejection of bail application was on valid grounds? - Further held, yes - Order of rejection and also contents of bail application were existing before detaining authority - Conclusion of detaining authority on facts of present case that 'there is likelihood of his being released on bail' cannot be said to be based on no relevant material and detention order was not vitiated - Merely because no bail application was then pending is no premise to hold that there was no likelihood of his being released on bail - Petition allowed.
