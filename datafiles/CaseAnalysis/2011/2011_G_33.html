State of Maharashtra and another v Goraksha Ambaji Adsul and another

Supreme Court of India

07 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 517; (2011) 7 SCC 437; AIR 2011 SC 2689; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2695; 2011 CRLJ 4286; 2011 (3) MLJ(Crl) 987; 2011 (3) RCR(Criminal) 558; [2011] 9 S.C.R. 41

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Approver

Summary: Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 164, 201, 302, 313, 354(3), 354(5), 366 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 201, 302 - Murder - Conviction - Death Penalty - Sustainability - Accused were alleged to have committed murder - Trial Court convicted and sentenced accused to death penalty for committing murder u/ss. 302, 201 IPC- Aggrieved by extreme punishment accused filed appeal before HC - HC declined to confirm death penalty and held accused guilty of u/ss. 302 ,201 IPC and sentenced accused to undergo life imprisonment - State claiming that said conversion by HC was not appropriate in facts and circumstances of case and HC in its judgment had fallen in error of law as well as failed in appreciation of evidence -- Hence, instant appeals - (A)Whether order of conviction by HC was sustainable - Held, prosecution had been able to prove complete chain of events which points only towards guilt of accused - Even in a case of circumstantial evidence, if prosecution was able to establish chain of events to satisfy ingredients of commission of an offence, accused would be liable to suffer consequences of his proven guilt - In instant case, right from evidence of entire family having last dinner together and administering of pedas with sedatives or poisonous substances to recovery of bodies of deceased at different railway stations chain of events stands proved beyond reasonable doubt - Thus, unable to find any error in concurrent findings recorded by Courts holding accused guilty of an offence u/ss.302 and CrPC - (B) Whether SC should exercise its judicial discretion to enhance punishment from life imprisonment to death sentence, as contemplated on behalf of State in its appeal - Held, awarding of death sentence amounts to taking away life of individual, which was most valuable right available, whether viewed from Constitutional point of view or from human rights point of view - Condition of providing special reasons for awarding death penalty was not to be construed linguistically but it was to satisfy basic features of reasoning supporting and making award of death penalty unquestionable - Circumstances and manner of committing crime should be such that it pricks judicial conscience of Court to extent that only and inevitable conclusion should be awarding of death penalty - Accused belonged to Armed forces, his father had married for second time and had children from second wife - There were continuous quarrels with regard to division of property and during these quarrels accused was stated to have even hit his father - It was a pressure which had increased with passage of time and probably this frustration attained limit of commission of such heinous crime by accused - - Further, intensity of bitterness between the members of the family had exacerbated the thoughts of revenge and retaliation in him and constant nagging would have to be taken as a mitigating circumstance in commission of crimeThus, did not find that instant case falls in category of 'rarest of rare cases' - Appeals dismissed.
