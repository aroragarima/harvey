Supreme Court of India

18 April 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, J.L. Kapur

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 414; AIR 1960 SC 1223; 1961 (63) BomLR 127; 1960 (1) FLR 392; 1960 (2) LLJ 592; 1961 (2) SCJ 360; [1961] 1 S.C.R. 227

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Industrial Disputes, Failure Report, Conciliator

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 12(4), 12(5) - Demand Bonus - Claim of reference - Rejected - Validity - Respondents raised four demands to company as well as to Assistant Commissioner of Labour /conciliation officer, and requested to commence conciliation proceedings at a nearly date - Soon thereafter company declared bonus equivalent to 1/4 of basic earnings for year 1952-1953, and respondents had decided to accept bonus offered without prejudice to demand already submitted by them in that behalf - Thereafter, conciliation officer examined demands made by respondents and admitted into conciliation only two of them and remaining demands were not admitted in conciliation - Conciliator, however, submitted his failure report u/s. 12(4) of the Act to appellant/Govt. who concluded that dispute in question should not be referred to an Tribunal for its adjudication - Aggrieved respondents filed petition in HC , praying for issue of writ of mandamus against appellant which was accepted - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was justified - Held, claim for bonus was based on consideration that by their contribution to profits of employer, employees were entitled to claim share in said profits, and so any punitive action taken by Govt. by refusing refer for adjudication an industrial dispute for bonus would, in SC opinion, be wholly inconsistent with object of the Act - If Govt. had given some relevant reasons which were based on, or were consequence of, misconduct to which reference was made it might had been another matter - Under those circumstances SC was unable to bold that HC was in error in coming to conclusion that impugned decision of Govt. was wholly punitive in character and might in circumstances be treated as based on consideration which was not germane and was extraneous - It was clear that the Act had passed in order to make provision for investigation and settlement of industrial disputes, and if it appears that in cases falling u/s. 12(5) of the Act investigation and settlement of any industrial dispute was prevented by appropriate Govt. by refusing to made reference on grounds which were wholly irrelevant and extraneous case for issue of writ of mandamus was clearly established - In result SC confirmed HC order - Appeals dismissed.
