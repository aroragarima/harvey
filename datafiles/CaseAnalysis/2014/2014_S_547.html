Y. Sleebachen Etc. v Superintending Engineer WRO/PWD and another

Supreme Court of India

04 August 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Sikri, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 497; (2015) 5 SCC 747; 2014 (6) ALD(SC) 157; 2015 (108) ALR 240; 2014 (5) AWC 4737; 2014 (3) CLT(SC) 242; 2015 (2) CTC 452; JT 2014 (9) SC 251; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3264; 2015 (1) RCR(Civil) 89; 2015 (126) RD 653; 2014(9) SCALE 166

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Dispute on contracts

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, ss. 34, 37, 37(1)(b) - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 23 r. 3, O. 3 r. 1 - Dispute on contracts - Award - Legality - Appellant/Engineering Contractor was awarded three contracts by respondents - Disputes arose between parties relating to contracts - Department appointed Arbitrator accordingly awards were passed - Respondent no. 1 filed petitions before Trial Court challenging awards passed by Arbitrator - Trial Court passed consent decree - Aggrieved respondents filed appeals before HC - HC allowed the appeal and set aside order passed by Trial Court - Hence instant appeals - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC was justified -

Held, respondents counsel was candid in accepting that not only no action was taken, said counsel continued to be on panel of Govt. and was entrusted in with further briefs of Court cases - This showed that respondents have tried to wriggle out of valid compromise by taking such spacious plea which could not be countenanced - Arbitral awards were given in favor of appellant way back, appellant was yet to reap benefits - Respondent no. 1 challenged these awards by filing applications - When proceedings were pending, respondents came out with proposal to negotiate and try to amicably settle matters, keeping in view otherwise laudable decision taken by PWD to settle such disputes as was clear from letter - Negotiations took place - Though appellant had agreed to forgo substantial part of award in terms of interest etc., talks failed at that time as respondents wanted 10% reduction in principal amount as well, whereas appellant was conceding to give up only 5% of principal amount - Appellant agreed to give further concessions in Court when matter came vide his 3 memos - These memos showed that appellant had given said offer due to acute financial crisis he was suffering from as he wanted to satisfy his creditors including his bankers to whom he owed substantial amounts - Even after settlement was fructified, resulting into passing of agreed orders, it has resulted into legal tangle, and appellant has not been able to get even said agreed amount - HC was not justified in setting aside consent decree passed by Trial Court - Such consent decree operates as an estoppel and was binding on parties from which respondents could not wriggle out by taking an afterthought plea that its lawyer was not authorized to enter into such settlement - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside and consent decrees passed by Trial Court were restored - Appeals allowed.
