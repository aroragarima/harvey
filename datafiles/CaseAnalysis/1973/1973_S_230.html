State of Uttar Pradesh and Others v Sughar Singh

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, M. Hameedullah Beg

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 65; (1974) SCC (L&S) 124; (1974) 1 SCC 218; AIR 1974 SC 423; 1974 LabIC 353; 1974 (1) LLJ 260; [1974] 2 S.C.R. 335; 1974 SLJ 474; 1974 (1) SLR 435

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Constitution, arts. 14, 16, 311 , 311(2) - Service Matter - (A) Reduction in rank - Reversion of one out of several officers from officiating post to substantive post - Adverse entry in character roll as basis for reversion - (B) Order of reversion when amounts to reduction in rank - (C) Reversion from officiating post to substantive post - Motive - Relevance - Order amounts to reduction in rank and is also discriminatory - An order of reversion is in its immediate effect bound always to be a reduction in rank - Motive behind reversion not relevant - Appeal Dismissed.
