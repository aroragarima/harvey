Ajeet Seeds Limited v K. Gopala Krishnaiah

Supreme Court of India

16 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 476; (2014) 12 SCC 685; AIR 2014 SC 3057; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 2738; 2014 (3) BC 572; 2015 (3) CGLJ 514; 2014 CRLJ 3937; 2014 (4) JCC (NI) 222; 2014 (3) RCR(Civil) 931; 2014 (3) RCR(Criminal) 840; 2014(8) SCALE 716; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 880

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonor of cheque

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - General Clauses Act, 1897, s. 27 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, s. 114 - Dishonor of cheque - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Respondent/accused issued impugned cheque to appellant/complainant which was dishonored for insufficiency of funds - Appellant initiated proceedings against respondent for offence punishable u/s. 138 of the Act - Trial Court issued process - Respondent filed revision before Appellate Court and the same was dismissed - Respondent filed petition before HC - HC allowed the petition and quashed the complaint - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned order passed by HC was justified -

Held, it was clear that s. 114 of 1872 Act enables Court to presume that in common course of natural events, communication would have been delivered at address of addressee - S. 27 of the Act gives rise to presumption that service of notice has been effected when it was sent to correct address by registered post - It was not necessary to aver in complaint that in spite of return of notice unserved, it was deemed to have been served or that addressee was deemed to have knowledge of notice - Unless and until contrary was proved by addressee, service of notice was deemed to have been effected at time at which letter would have been delivered in ordinary course of business - It must be held that HC clearly erred in quashing complaint on ground that there was no recital in complaint that notice u/s. 138 of 1881 Act was served upon accused - HC erred in quashing complaint on ground that there was no proof either that notice was served or it was returned unserved/unclaimed - Impugned judgment was set aside and instant complaint was restored - Appeal allowed.
