Deputy Commercial Tax Officer and Others v Coromandel Pharmaceuticals and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 March 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2239; (1997) 10 SCC 649; AIR 1997 SC 2027; 1997 (3) AWC 1421; 1997 (2) CLT 160; [1997] 89 Comp Cas 1; 1997 (2) CompLJ 164; 1999 (82) FLR 186; JT 1997 (3) SC 660; 1997(2) SCALE 640; [1997] 2 S.C.R. 1026; 1997 (105) STC 327; 1997 (4) Supreme 259

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Unfair Trade Practices

Summary: Corporate - Companies Act, 1956 - Sick Industrial Companies (Special Provisions) Act, 1986 - Board of Industrial and Financial Reconstruction Regulations, 1987 - Sales Tax Act, ss. 22(1) and 22(5) - Sales Tax - (A) Whether scheme was sanctioned on 19-11-1990 for which proceedings were initiated by the revenue for the assessment years 1992-93 and 1993-94 and the assessment orders for these years were passed on 03-01-1994 and in 1995, long after the sanctioned scheme was brought into force? - What is contemplated by s. 22(1) of the Act? - Held, s. 22(1) should be read down or understood as contended by the revenue - The decision to the contrary by the High Court is unreasonable and unsustainable - Appeal allowed.
