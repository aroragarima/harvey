Â Raghunatharao Ganpatrao v Union Of India

Supreme Court of India

04 February 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Ratnavel Pandian, L.M. Sharma, S. Mohan, B.P. Jeevan Reddy, S.P. Bharucha

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 600; (1994) Supp1 SCC 191; AIR 1993 SC 1267; JT 1993 (1) SC 374; 1993(1) SCALE 363; [1993] 1 S.C.R. 480

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Morality, Indian Independence Act, 1947, Constitution (Seventeenth Amendment) Act, 1964, Constitution (Twenty-Fourth Amendment) Act, 1971, Constitution (Twenty-Sixth Amendment) Act, 1971, Titles

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, arts. 291 and 362 (omitted) - Scope - Privy purse - Whether sacrosanct? - Held, no obligation flows from covenants - Covenants have no existence at all after Amendment - Basic structure - Not changed or altered - Act is valid in its entirety.
