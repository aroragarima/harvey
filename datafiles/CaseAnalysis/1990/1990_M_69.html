Commissioner of Income Tax, Calcutta v Messrs British Paints India Limited

Supreme Court of India

13 December 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

T.K. Thommen, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 622; (1992) Supp1 SCC 55; AIR 1991 SC 1338; 1992 (3) CompLJ 187; 1991 (91) CTR 108; [1991] 188 ITR 44; JT 1990 (4) SC 694; 1990(2) SCALE 1261; [1990] Supp3 S.C.R. 525; [1991] 54 TAXMAN 499; 1991 (2) TLR 322

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 143, 145 and 256 - Evidence Act 1872, s. 115 - Valuation by Assessing Officer - Methods to be followed - System of accounting - Any system of accounting which excludes all costs other than cost of raw material of goods in process and finished products is not proper - Assessing Officer can determine correct taxable income u/s. 145 - Assessing Officer is not bound by method followed in earlier years - Findings of fact by Assessing officer based on cogent evidence and correct principles and also affirmed by Tribunal cannot be interfered by High Court - Appeal allowed.
