Sitabai & Anr v Ram Chandra

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1969

Case Analysis

Bench

V. Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1969 Indlaw SC 113; (1969) 2 SCC 544; AIR 1970 SC 343; 1969 MahLJ 926; 1969 MPLJ 868; [1970] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Keywords: Coparcenary Property, Illegitimate, Charge For Maintenance, Coparcener, Conditions For a Valid Adoption, Coparcenary Interest

Summary: Hindu Adoptions and Maintenance Act, 1956, ss.12, 11(vi) and 14(4) - Madhya Bharat Land Revenue and Tenancy Act, 1950, ss.86, 87, 89 - Constitution, art.136 - (A) Hindu law - (1) HUF - Effect of temporary reduction of co-parcenary unit to single individual - (2) Adoption - Effect - Ties of adopted child with his family of birth are severed and replaced by those of adoptive family - HUF consisting of two brothers - On death of one brother, his widow begetting illegitimate son from surviving brother - Adoption of male child by her, thereafter - Adopted son - Entitlement - (B) Tenancy - Rights of ordinary tenant - Nature - (C) Appeal by special leave u/art. 136 - Plea that jurisdiction of Civil Court was barred by provisions of 1950 Act, decided against respondent by first two courts - Decision not challenged before HC in second appeal - Plea cannot be raised in appeal to SC - Character of joint family property does not change - Adopted son becomes coparcener and he is entitled to joint property in preference to illegitimate child - Rights are heritable - Plea cannot be raised in appeal to SC - Appeal Allowed.
