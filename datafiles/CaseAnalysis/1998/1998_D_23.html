Commissioner of Police, Delhi and Another v Dhaval Singh

Supreme Court of India

01 May 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 853; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1740; (1999) 1 SCC 246; AIR 1999 SC 2326; JT 1998 (9) SC 429; 1999 LabIC 2619; 1999 (3) LLN 870

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Recruitment - Procedure - Cancellation of candidature - Respondent alleged to have concealed from mentioning in application form against relevant columns about pendency of criminal case against him - Respondent, however, voluntarily conveyed that inadvertant mistake had been committed - Whether orders of cancellation of candidature and rejection of representation are correct? - It is not as if information was given by respondent regarding inadvertent mistake committed by him after he had been acquitted by trial court - It was much before that it is also obvious that information was conveyed voluntarily - Held, cancellation of candidature under circumstances was without any proper application of mind and without taking into consideration all relevant material - Appeal dismissed.
