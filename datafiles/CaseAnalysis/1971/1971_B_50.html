Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal I v Birla Cotton Spinning and Weaving Mills Limited

Supreme Court of India

17 August 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 393; (1971) 3 SCC 344; AIR 1972 SC 19; [1971] 82 ITR 166; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 283

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Indian Income Tax Act, 1922, ss. 10(1), 10(2)(Xv) - A/y 1952-1953, 1953-1954 and 1954-1955 - Claim for deduction - Entitled to - Assessee (Public limited company) during A/y 1952-1953, 1953-1954 and 1954-1955, spend sum of money for representing its case before Investigation Commission relating to past A/y 1941-1942 to 1947-1948 and claimed deduction u/s. 10 (2) (xv) or in alternative u/s. 10 (1) of the Act but Income tax Officer disallowed assessee's claim and his order was upheld by Appellate Assistant Commissioner and Appellate Tribunal - Thereupon assessee moved to Tribunal to state case and refer question of law to HC and Tribunal referred question of law to HC who allowed assessee's claim - Hence, instant appeals - Whether order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, HC in judgment under appeal, after discussion of relevant case law, approached matter in that way - proceeding before Investigation Commission was not civil proceeding but it was statutory proceeding with view to collecting materials for more taxation - Therefore, if proceeding touched business of assessee expenditure incurred by assessee in safeguarding its interest before Commission would be an allowable deduction - It was pointed out and that was based on material on record that Commission was holding an investigation on suspected escapement of income to tune of about Rs. 4 cores - Taxes levied on that income and penalties imposed would naturally had been very heavy for business of assessee and might had either crippled or annihilated it and to preserve business from an investigation which, according to assessee, was unlawful assessee was justified in taking proper steps and spending money - Therefore, such an expenditure 'was not for earning profits but was aimed at preservation of business from inroads of piece of legislation which, it was maintained, was unconstitutional and was so held by this Court later in certain decisions that had already been mentioned - However, expenditure which was incurred by assessee in opposing coercive governmental action with object of saving taxation and safeguarding business was justified by commercial expediency and was, therefore, allowable u/s. 10 (2) (xv) of the Act - Hence, SC had no doubt that above approached of HC and its ultimate decision were fully justified on principle and authority - Thus, in result all those appeals fail and were dismissed - Appeals dismissed.
