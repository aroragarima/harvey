Bishundeo Narain Rai (Dead) By LRs and Others v Anmol Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 August 1998

Case Analysis

Bench

S.S.M. Quadri, K. Venkataswami

Where Reported

1998 Indlaw SC 833; (1998) 7 SCC 498; AIR 1998 SC 3006; 1998 (3) CLT 169; JT 1998 (5) SC 570; 1999 (1) MLJ(SC) 54; 1998(4) SCALE 624; 1998 (7) SLT 58; [1998] Supp1 S.C.R. 66; 1998 (6) Supreme 509

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Transfer of property Act, ss. 8 and 54(4)(b) - Plaintiffs-appellants purchased land from Defendant No. 1 as Karta of Hindus Undivided Family and its members (vendors) under a registered sale deed D/- 30-04-1963 (Ex C) - On the alleged ground of the first purchasers not paying the entire consideration amount, the vendors by a registered document D/- 19-06-1963, Ex H, purported to cancel Ex C, and on 08-07-1963 executed a second sale deed, Ex C/1, in favour of respondents 1 to 5 (hereinafter referred to as the second purchasers) - First purchasers filed Title Suit claiming that under Ex C, they purchased the suit property for valuable consideration, and acquired absolute title thereunder and that out of sale consideration, only a sum of Rs 235/- Remained to be paid, which they deposited in the court to the credit of the vendors along with the plaint - Whether Exs H and C/1 were illegal, ineffective and not binding on the first purchasers? - Held, a plain reading of Ex C indicates conveying of title to the property absolutely for consideration as cutlined; by virtue of the sale the purchasers were put in possession of the property conveyed, became entitled to the custody of the sale deed and other documents and enjoyment of the property by them - These factors satisfy all the requirements of absolute sale - No recital in Ex C is brought to our notice to indicate any contra-intention - It was not open to the vendors to unilaterally cancel Exhibit-C by executing Ex H - Trial court on interpretation of Ex C came to the conclusion that the title to the suit property passed on to the first purchasers on execution and registration of Ex C - Appeal allowed.
