Executive Officer, Antiyur Town Panchayat v G. Arumugam (Dead) by Lrs.

Supreme Court of India

19 January 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Kurian Joseph, M.Y. Eqbal

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 41; (2015) 3 SCC 569; 2015 (110) ALR 62; 2015 (1) CLT(SC) 190; 2015 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2138; 2015 (6) MahLJ 518; 2015 (4) MPLJ 502; 2015 (2) RCR(Civil) 337; 2015 (128) RD 80; 2015(1) SCALE 474; [2015] 1 S.C.R. 152

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Filing of second appeal - Condoning delay - Respondent filed suit for declaration and possession - Trial Court dismissed suit, however, First Appellate Court decreed suit on first appeal - Appellant filed second appeal with a delay condonation application - HC dismissed delay condonation application - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, delay occasioned only on account of the deliberate lapses on the part of appellant Executive Officer of the Panchayat at the relevant time. Who else are involved in the process, is not quite clear. If the court is convinced that there had been an attempt on the part of the government officials or public servants to defeat justice by causing delay, the court, in view of the larger public interest, should take a lenient view in such situations, condone the delay, howsoever huge may be the delay, and have the matter decided on merits. Hence, impugned order was set aside and condoned delay in filing second appeal. Appeal allowed.
