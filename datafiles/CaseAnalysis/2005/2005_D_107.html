Harshad Chimanlal Modi v Dlf Universal Limited and Another

Supreme Court of India

14 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Arijit Pasayat

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 802; (2006) 1 SCC 364; AIR 2006 SC 646; 2006 (62) ALR 335; 2006 (1) AWC 411; 2006 (1) CTC 64; 2005 (125) DLT 790; 2006 (2) JhrCR(SC) 3; JT 2005 (10) SC 567; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 188; 2006 (100) RD 394; 2005(10) SCALE 144; 2006 (1) SCJ 204; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 740

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Constitution; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Civil Procedure - Code Of Civil Procedure, 1908, o. 18, r. 15, o. 6, r. 17, o. 7, r. 10, ss. 151, 16, 20, 24, 25 - Constitution Of India, 1950, art. 142 - Suit for declaration, for specific performance of agreement, for possession of property and for permanent injunction - Property was situated at Gurgaon - Order stating that Delhi court has no jurisdiction - Confirmed by HC and SC - Application seeking direction to Gurgaon Court to take up suit from stage at which it stands transferred and to decide it expeditiously - Held, this is not a case of 'transfer' of a suit but of lack of jurisdiction of Court - o. 18, r. 15 also cannot be pressed in service which covers those cases where a successor judge deals with evidence recorded by his predecessor and proceeds with suit from stage at which it was left by his predecessor - Application dismissed.
