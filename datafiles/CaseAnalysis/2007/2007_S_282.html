State Of Bihar And Others v Bihar State +2 Lecturers Associations And Others

Supreme Court of India

15 May 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 517; (2008) 7 SCC 231; (2008) 2 SCC (L&S) 415; AIR 2007 SC 1948; 2007(4) ALL MR 739; 2007 (6) AWC 5732; 2007 (3) ESC 447; 2007 (4) JhrCR(SC) 135; JT 2007 (7) SC 273; 2007(7) SCALE 697; 2007 (5) SCJ 812; [2007] 6 S.C.R. 631; 2007 (4) SLR 754; 2007 (3) UPLBEC 2400

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: West Bengal Special Courts Act, 1950, Elementary School Teachers Promotion Rules, 1993

Summary: Service - Appointment of +2 Lecturers in Secondary Schools - Difference of pay scales between lecturers who are trained and lecturers who are untrained - Single Judge held that in making distinction between trained lecturers and untrained lecturers and in fixing different pay scales, State Government had not violated any provision of the Constitution - Division Bench directed the State Authorities to grant uniform pay scales to trained and untrained lecturers - Appeal against - Held, training was one of the most important factors for determining pay scales - Trained lecturers and untrained lecturers, therefore, can neither be said to be similarly circumstanced nor they form one and the same class - Classification is reasonable and is based on intelligible differentia which distinguishes one class (trained) included therein from the other class (untrained) which is left out - Not intend to interfere with the final direction issued by the Division Bench - Appeal disposed of.
