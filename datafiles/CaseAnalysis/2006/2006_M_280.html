Union of India v Munshi Ram (Dead) By Lrs. and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 March 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Singh, Altamas Kabir

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 222; (2006) 4 SCC 538; AIR 2006 SC 1716; 2006 (2) AWC 1697; 2006 (2) CTC 534; 2006 (3) Law Herald (P&H) 2167; 2006 (3) MLJ(SC) 292; 2006 (2) RCR(Civil) 763; 2006(3) SCALE 225; 2006 (3) SCJ 534; [2006] 2 S.C.R. 816; 2006 (Supp) RD 648; 2006 (2) UC 862

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 18 and 28-A - Whether redetermination of compensation u/s. 28-A payable to claimants who had not preferred reference u/s. 18 claiming compensation at same rates as were awarded to other claimants by Reference Court must be on basis of decree as originally passed by Reference Court or on basis of decree as modified by Higher Courts? - Decree of Reference Court means decree as modified in appeal by Higher Courts or an incongruous position may emerge that a person who did not challenge award of Collector and did not claim a reference u/s. 18 would get a higher compensation than one who challenged award and claimed a reference, but in whose case a higher compensation determined by Reference Court was subsequently reduced by superior Court - Held, u/s. 28-A, compensation payable to applicants is same which is finally payable to those claimants who sought reference u/s. 18 and in case of reduction of compensation by superior Courts, applicants u/s. 28-A may be directed to refund excess amount received by them in light of reduced compensation finally awarded - Appeals allowed.
