All India Anna Dravida Munnetra Kazhagam v L.K. Tripathi And Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 466; (2009) 5 SCC 417; (2009) 2 SCC (Cr) 673; AIR 2009 SC 1314; 2009 (5) MLJ(SC) 782; 2009 (2) RCR(Criminal) 658; 2009(4) SCALE 547; [2009] 6 S.C.R. 583

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Fundamental Duties, Court Of Record, Contumacious Conduct

Summary: Constitution - Practice & Procedure - Contempt of Courts Act, 1971 - Constitution of India - Call by the political parties for hartal - (A) Whether respondent Nos.1 to 5 have willfully disobeyed order of S.C? - Held, Court did not direct respondent nos. 1 to 3 to act in any particular manner or take certain specific steps to meet the challenge likely to be posed by implementation of the call given by Democratic Progressive Alliance - Respondent nos.1 to 3 cannot be held guilty of disobeying or violating the Court's order - Bald statement contained in the contempt petitions that violence and coercion was resorted to by members of the party headed by respondent no.4 has remained unsubstantiated - (B) whether respondent No.6 is guilty of criminal contempt within the meaning of s. 2(c)? - Held, charge cannot be framed against him with reference to s. 2(c) because the petitioners have not produced any legally admissible evidence to prove the contents of the speech allegedly made by the said respondent - Petitions dismissed.
