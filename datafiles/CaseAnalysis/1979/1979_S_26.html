Profulla Chorone Requitte and Others v Satya Chorone Requitte

Supreme Court of India

02 March 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, V.D. Tulzapurkar

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 446; (1979) 3 SCC 409; AIR 1979 SC 1682; 1979 (27) BLJR 257; [1979] 3 S.C.R. 431; 1979 UJ 854

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Keywords: Shebaits

Summary: CPC, 1908, O. 1, r. 9 - Hindu Law - (A) Religious Endowment - Shebaitship - Being property devolves like any other species of heritable property - Non-disposition shebati rights - Devolves on heirs of founder under Hindu Law in absence of any usage or custom of different nature - (B) Construction of Will - Illustration - Words 'to hold, retain and use the premises an endowed or debutter property for the service and worship of my family Thakur' - Intentional omission of words 'management', 'manager', 'custodian of idol' or 'ministrant of the idol' - And that house be also used by heirs for residence - Trustees not expressely constituted as shebaits - (C) Practice and Procedure - Non-joinder of parties - Suit by Trustees of endowed property of family deity for possession of certain rooms against one of the Shebaits - Non -pleading of of all the Shebaits (necessary parties) - Concept of shebaitship and distinction between public and private debutter explained - Held that shebaitship remained undisposed of, and, as such the heirs of testator became coshebaits of the deity with right to use the house as their dwelling house - Held, suit by Trustees was not properly constituted and must be dismissed - Petition Dismissed.
