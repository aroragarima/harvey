State of Karnataka v Hemareddy Alias Vemareddy and Another

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

A. Varadarajan, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 425; (1981) 2 SCC 185; (1981) SCC (Cr) 395; AIR 1981 SC 1417; 1981 CRLJ 1019; 1981 CrLR(SC) 278; 1981(1) SCALE 206; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 695

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 -

Indian Penal Code, 1860 - Forged sale deed - Conviction - Acquittal - Guilty - Charge sheet was filed on appellants/H and P, that they had conspired to cheat N and to deprive him of lands and put forward P as N's wife and got sale deed executed by her - Appellants were charged for offences u/ss. 120-B, 193, 465, 467, 468 and 420 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants u/s. 467 of IPC and sentenced to rigorous imprisonment with fine - On appeal, HC modified sentence awarded to appellants - Hence instant appeal - Whether appellant's sentence modified by HC was justified - Held, no offence u/s. 193 of IPC within scope of s. 195(1)(b) of CrPC could be stated to had committed by H as forged sale deed was not put in evidence at any stage in redemption suit filed by complainant - Trial Court could not had taken cognizance of offence u/s. 193 of IPC - Offence of abetment of forgery was complete when forged sale deed was fabricated and registered - HC was not right in law, that complaint was totally not maintainable against 'H', u/s. 195(1) (b) of 1973 Act , that he had committed offence punishable u/s. 193 of IPC - Modification of sentence awarded to 'P' was confirmed - 'H' found to be guilty u/s. 467 of IPC and convicted for sentence to undergo rigorous imprisonment with fine - Appeal dismissed.
