Jaswant Singh v Virender Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, Faizan Uddin

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 2156; (1995) Supp1 SCC 384; AIR 1995 SC 520; 1994(4) SCALE 872; 1995 (2) SCJ 54; [1994] Supp5 S.C.R. 336

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Summary: Election - Representation of the People Act, 1951, s. 116-A - Irregularity in counting - Allegation of - The elections to Haryana legislative assembly were held along with elections to parliament in respect of seats falling within State of Haryana - Appellant and respondents 1 to 16 were candidates from 71 Narnaund Assembly constituency - Respondent 1, a candidate sponsored by Janata Dal was declared elected by polling 20,011 votes - The appellant, a nominee of Indian National Congress secured 19,973 votes i.e., 38 votes less than returned candidate - (A) Whether result of election in respect of Respondent 1 has been materially affected on grounds stated by appellant? - Held, the re-count ordered by Single Judge confirmed correctness of result of election as well as generally correctness of votes polled by appellant and returned candidate - Allegations of appellant regarding illegalities and irregularities committed during counting and for non-compliance with provisions of Act, rules and orders framed there under or in guidelines contained in Handbook during counting has not been substantiated or established - Result of election insofar as returned candidate is concerned has not been materially affected - (B) Irregularity in sealing the election materials - Whether violation of instructions contained in Handbook and rules and orders, in matter of sealing of election material, after declaration of election result can be ground for avoiding result of election? - Held, the election of a returned candidate can only be avoided on any of grounds contained in s. 100 of Act of 1951 - Further held, alleged tampering with seals of some of packets containing ballot papers, after declaration of result, is not a ground for avoiding an election through an election petition - Appeal dismissed.
