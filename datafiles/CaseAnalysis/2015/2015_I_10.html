Vaish Aggarwal Panchayat v Inder Kumar and others

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Prafulla C. Pant

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 582; AIR 2015 SC 3357; 2015 (6) ALD(SC) 1; 2015 (5) AWC 5141; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 326; 2015 (6) CTC 555; JT 2015 (8) SC 248; 2015 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3492; 2015 (4) RCR(Civil) 167; 2016 (1) RLW 608; 2015(9) SCALE 270; [2015] 9 S.C.R. 637

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Res Judicata, Maintainability

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Suit for specific performance - Maintainability - Respondent Nos. 1 and 2 filed suit against respondent no.3 and respondent no.5 for a decree of specific performance of agreement to sell of suit land with the consequential relief of permanent injunction - Suit was decreed by Trial Court - Petitioner Society filed objection and claimed to be the owner of the land, which was rejected - Petitioner filed application for stay of execution , which was dismissed - Petitioner filed appeal before District Judge who allowed the appeal and the suit was ordered to be restored and tried - Respondents no.1 and no.2 filed revision before HC - Revision was allowed - Hence instant appeal.

Held, allegations in the plaint are absolutely different, there is an asseveration of fraud and collusion. There is an assertion that in the earlier suit a decree came to be passed because of fraud and collusion. In such a fact situation, HC has fallen into error by expressing the view that the plea of resjudicata was obvious from the plaint. In fact, a finding has been recorded by the HC accepting the plea taken in the written statement. Thus, in the obtaining factual matrix there should have been a trial with regard to all the issues framed. Hence, order passed by the HC is set aside and that of the Appellate Court is restored. Appeal dismissed.
