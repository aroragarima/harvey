Board of Trustees of The Port of Bombay v Sriyanesh Knitters

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Kirpal, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 102; (1999) 7 SCC 359; AIR 1999 SC 2947; 1999 (4) Bom.C.R. 830; 1999 (112) E.L.T. 373; JT 1999 (5) SC 172; [1999] 3 S.C.R. 1238

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Practice & Procedure; Contract & Commercial

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Contract & Commercial - Practice & Procedure - Major Port Trusts Act, 1963 ss. 42, 61, 131 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 s. 171 - Past imports due - General lien - Validity - Respondents/importers had imported various consignments of woollen rags at Bombay Port - After considerable period of time imported goods were confiscated by custom authorities but importers secured orders to get goods released on payment of fine - During this period imported goods remained at docks till order of confiscation was passed - In respect of period during which goods remained at docks appellants/Board issued notices to respondents demanding demurrage charges - Subsequently, Acrylic fibre was imported by respondents, when consignment arrived in Bombay Port - Appellant did not release goods but, demanded payment in respect of wharfage and demurrage which was due from these respondents in respect of earlier consignments of woollen rags - Refusal of appellants to allow removal of goods was based on circular dt. 2-10-1979 which was issued by appellants - Respondents filed writ petitions in HC seeking declaration that aforesaid circular was ultra virus of the 1963 Act - Single judge by judgment dt. 24-11-1982 allowed writ petition - Appellants were directed to withdraw or cancel the circular dt. 2-10-1979 and it was, inter alia, held that appellants herein could not in law claim general lien u/s. 171 of the 1872 Act - Hence instant appeal - Whether impugned judgment of HC was justified - Held, if payment was not made by consignee to wharfinger, in case where s. 171 of the 1872 Act applies, wharfinger could only retain goods bailed as security and would have to take recourse to other proceedings in accordance with law for securing an order which would then enable goods to be sold for realisation of amounts due to it - Therefore, it might in this connection, be necessary for wharfinger to file suit for recovery of amount due to it and s. 131 of the 1963 Act clearly provided that such remedy of filing suit was available to Board - Added advantage of sale given by s. 61 of the 1963 Act in respect of current dues could not be regarded as whittling down right of general lien contained in s. 171 of the 1872 Act in respect of old dues - S. 171 of the Contract Act enables these 5 categories to retain as security goods bailed to them in respect of general balance of account - General balance of account had to be of amount legally due to bankers, factors, wharfingers, attorneys and policy brokers - Appellants come in category of wharfingers, namely, owners of wharf - Duties which they were required to perform were provided in statute itself, namely, s. 42 of the 1963 Act - In other words services which were undertaken u/s. 42 of the 1963 Act have to be paid for and any amount due in respect thereof would be regarded as general balance of account - There was no reason to give restricted meaning to expression general balance of account to mean only wharfage charges which, according to respondents, would imply charges for loading or unloading of goods, and would not include demurrage - Thus, once goods were taken charge of by appellants as wharfingers then in respect of services rendered, as contemplated by s. 42 of the 1963 Act , if there was any amount which was due and payable to it same would be regarded as general balance of account in respect of which it had general lien over goods bailed to it - Therefore, circular dt. 2-10-1979 issued by appellants was valid and appellants could retain goods which were in their possession as bailees as security for realisation of amount of wharfage, demurrage and other charges which were due to them - SC allowed these appeals and set aside judgment of HC with result that writ petitions filed by respondents in HC stand dismissed - Appeals allowed.
