Bharatha Matha And Another v R. Vijaya Renganathan And Others

Supreme Court of India

17 May 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Swatanter Kumar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 430; (2010) 11 SCC 483; AIR 2010 SC 2685; 2010 (4) ALD(SC) 152; 2010 (81) ALR 230; 2011 (2) AWC 1376; 2010 (3) CTC 654; JT 2010 (5) SC 534; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 953; 2010 (3) RCR(Civil) 252; 2010 (111) RD 421; 2010(6) SCALE 53; [2010] 7 S.C.R. 154

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 112 - Predecessor-in-interest of appellants, instituted a suit, against the respondents and their predecessor-in- interest claiming the share of her brother 'X' on the ground that he died unmarried and intestate and that 'Y', the defendant No. 1 in the suit was a legally wedded wife of one 'Z' who was still alive, therefore, her claim that she had live-in-relationship with plaintiff's brother 'X' and had two children from him, had to be ignored - Defendants/respondents contested the suit denying the marriage between defendant No. 1 and the said 'Z' - Trial Court decreed the suit and held that defendant No.1 in the suit was wife of 'Z' who was alive at the time of filing the suit - There had been no legal separation between them - Therefore, the question of live-in-relationship of defendant no. 1 with 'X' could not arise - Appellate Court dismissed appeal filed against said order - HC allowed appeal filed by defendant no. 1 - Hence, present appeal - Whether order of HC could be upheld and defendant no. 1 entitled to claim inheritance in property of 'X'? - Held, Courts below had appreciated the entire evidence and came to the conclusion that defendant no.1 was legally wedded wife of 'Z' and thus did not presume her marriage with 'X' - HC without making any reference to the evidence of the plaintiff's witnesses, reversed the finding of fact and reached the conclusion that merely live-in-relationship between the said two parties would lead the presumption of marriage between them. - HC erred in not appreciating that the judgments of the Courts below could be based on another presumption provided u/s. 112 of the Evidence Act, 1872 - Moreover, a child born of void or voidable marriage is not entitled to claim inheritance in ancestral coparcenery property but is entitled only to claim share in self acquired properties, if any - In present case, defendants had not pleaded at any stage that the Suit land was a self acquired property of 'X' - It is evident from the record that 'X' did not partition his joint family properties and died issueless and intestate in 1974 - Therefore, the question of inheritance of coparcenery property by the illegitimate children, who were born out of the live-in-relationship, could not arise - Judgment of HC is liable to be set aside - Appeal allowed.
