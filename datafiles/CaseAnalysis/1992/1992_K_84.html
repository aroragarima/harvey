Kumar Padma Prasad v Union Of India And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuldip Singh, N.M. Kasliwal, P.B. Sawant

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 1056; (1992) 2 SCC 428; (1992) SCC (L&S) 561; AIR 1992 SC 1213; 1992 (20) ATC 239; JT 1992 (2) SC 247; 1993 (2) LLJ 972; 1992 (1) LLN 951; 1992(1) SCALE 581; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 109; 1992 (2) SLJ 115; 1992 (2) SLR 210; 1992 (1) UJ 639; 1992 (2) UPLBEC 981

Case Digest

Subject: Advocates & Judges; Service

Keywords: Mizoram Judicial Service Rules, 1986

Summary: Advocates & Judges - Service - Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 217(2)(a), 226 and 236(b) - (A) Petition challenging selection of one Srivastava for appointment as a Judge of Gauhati High Court on ground that he was not qualified - Whether qualified? - (B) Expression 'judicial office' and 'judicial service' - Meanings of - Appointment as HC Judge u/art. 217(2)(a) - Eligibility - (C) Person holding office of Legal Remembrancer-cum-Secretary, Law and Judicial, Mizoram - Is it judicial office - (D) Administrative action - Judges of HC registering public interest litigation - Suo motu assuming judicial jurisdiction to probe whether Secretary acted honestly or dishonestly in matter of purchase of books etc to probe into matter of purely administrative nature relating to HC - Whether justifiable - Held, not qualified - Eligibility u/art. 217(2)(a), a person must hold a 'judicial office' which must be part of 'judicial service' of State - It is non judicial office under control of executive - Not justifiable - Judges should not have assumed jurisdiction to probe into matter of purely administrative nature - Petition allowed.
