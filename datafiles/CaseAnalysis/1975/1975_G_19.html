Superintendent and Remembrancer of Legal Affairs v Girish Kumar Navalakha

Supreme Court of India

03 March 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

Kuttyil Kurien Mathew, A.N. Ray, V.R. Krishna Iyer, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 118; (1975) SCC (Cr) 718; (1975) 4 SCC 754; AIR 1975 SC 1030; [1975] 45 Comp Cas 400; 1975 CRLJ 874; 1975 CrLR(SC) 245; [1975] 3 S.C.R. 802

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Constitution; Corporate; Criminal

Keywords: Legislation, Notification, Reserve Bank

Summary: Constitution, art. 14 - FERA, 1947, ss. 23(1), 23A and 23(1A) - (A) Constitutionality of s. 23(1A) of FERA , 1947 - (B) Statute creating economic offences - Construction of provisions - Matters to be consideed while determination of legislative intent - Held, not violative of art. 14 - Persons contravening provisions mentioned in s.23(1A) cannot be said to be similarly situated with persons contravening other provisions of the Act - Proper approach indicated - Appeal Allowed.
