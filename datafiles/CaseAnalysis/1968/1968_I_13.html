Indian Oxygen Limited v Udaynath Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

25 July 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

C.A. Vaidyialingam

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 24; 1970 (21) FLR 350; 1970 (2) LLJ 413

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 9 and 33 - Request, made by workmen of appellant company, that carbide drums should be sold to them at concessional rates - Appellant Company indicated price of various types of drum - Complaint filed u/s. 33-A, by some of workmen alleging that sale of drums, by management, at a concessional rate, to workmen, had become a part of their conditions of service, and that company has committed a breach of condition, by refusing to sell carbide drums at concessional rates - Whether appellant can be said to have committed breach of conditions of service of workmen? - Management was only considering a request, made by workmen, for sale of drums, as and when available, at concessional rates, and at reasonable intervals - No obligation was, as such, imposed on management, or of any right being vested in workmen to compel management to sell drums to them - Appellant was to supply carbide drums at concessional rates as per availability hence it cannot be said that there was agreement between appellant company and respondent - Held, it cannot be said that appellant has committed breach of conditions of service - Appeals allowed.
