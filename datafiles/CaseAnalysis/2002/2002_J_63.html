Justice K.P. Mohapatra v Sri Ram Chandra Nayak and others

Supreme Court of India

09 October 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.M. Dharmadhikari

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1147; (2002) 8 SCC 1; AIR 2002 SC 3578; 2003 (1) JhrCR(SC) 8; JT 2002 (8) SC 101; 2002(7) SCALE 377; 2002 (3) SCJ 648; 2002 (5) SLT 631; [2002] Supp3 S.C.R. 166; 2002 (7) Supreme 138

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Orissa Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 1995

Summary: Orissa Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 1995 -- S 3(1) - what is the requirement and what meaning could be assigned to 'Consultation' as contemplated under Section 3(1) of the Orissa Lokpal and Lokayuktas Act, 1995? Held, consultation with the Chief Justice is mandatory and his opinion would have primacy. The nature of the consultation with the Leader of the Opposition is to apprise him about the proposal of selecting a person to the post and also to take his views on the said proposal.
