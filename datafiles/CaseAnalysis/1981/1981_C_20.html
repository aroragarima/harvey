Gangabai W/O Rambilas Gilda v Chhabubai W/O Pukharajji Gandhi

Supreme Court of India

06 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 386; (1982) 1 SCC 4; AIR 1982 SC 20; 1982 (2) Bom.C.R. 137; 1982 MahLJ 1; 1982 (1) RCR(Rent) 384; 1981(3) SCALE 1753; [1982] 1 S.C.R. 1176; 1982 UJ 1

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Declaratory Suit

Summary: Rent Control - Practice & Procedure - Civil Procedure Code, 1908, s. 11 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 91 and 92 - Provincial Small Cause Courts Act, 1887, s. 23 - Agreement with nominal document of sale and a rent note - Suit for declaration by respondent to be owner of house property - Trial Court decreed suit, sale deed was never intended to be acted upon - Appellant appealed, District Court did not accept case - In Second Appeal High Court affirmed findings of Trial Court - (A) Whether Small Causes Court decrees, in view of general principles of res judicata, precluded trial of question whether sale transaction was a genuine transaction? - Held, a question of title in a Small Cause suit can be regarded as incidental only to substantial issue in suit and cannot operate as res judicata in a subsequent suit in which question of title is directly raised therefore finding rendered by Court of Small Causes in two suits filed by appellant that document executed by respondent is a sale deed cannot operate as res judicata - (B) Whether sub-s. (1) of s. 92 of Evidence Act, 1872, bars respondent from contending that there was no sale? - Held, sub-section is not attracted when case of a party is that transaction recorded in document was never intended to be acted upon at all between parties and that document is a sham - Appeal dismissed.
