Govind Prakash Verma v Life Insurance Corporation of India and others

Supreme Court of India

23 January 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Brijesh Kumar, Arun Kumar

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1743; (2005) 10 SCC 289; 2004 (4) JhrCR(SC) 93

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Sustainability, Appointment on compassionate grounds

Summary: Service - Appointment on compassionate grounds - Terminal benefits - Denial - Sustainability - Appellants filed petition before Single Judge for relief of compassionate appointment to the appellant on the death of his father during the course of employment - Petition was dismissed - Appeal before DB - Appeal was dismissed - Hence instant appeal - Whether the order recorded by HC was maintainable - Held, it was wholly irrelevant for departmental authorities and Single Judge to take into consideration amount which was being paid as family pension to widow of deceased and other amounts paid on account of terminal benefits under the Rules - Scheme of compassionate appointment was over and above whatever was admissible to legal representatives of deceased employee as benefits of service which one gets on death of employee - Therefore, compassionate appointment should not be refused on ground that any member of family received amounts admissible under the Rules - Other aspects, on which Officer was required to make enquiries, was conveniently omitted and not whisper was found in report submitted by Officer - Therefore, orders passed by HC was not sustainable - Respondents have wrongly refused compassionate appointment to appellant - Inference of gainful employment of elder brother could not be acted upon - Terminal benefits received by widow and family pension could not be taken into account - Hence, orders passed by HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
