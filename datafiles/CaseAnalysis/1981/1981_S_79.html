Western Coalfields Limited v Special Area Development Authority, Korba andAnother

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1981

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.V. Chandrachud, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1981 Indlaw SC 385; (1982) 1 SCC 125; AIR 1982 SC 697; 1982 (2) CompLJ 793; 1981(3) SCALE 1775; [1982] 2 S.C.R. 1

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax

Keywords: Mines and Minerals (Regulation and Development) Amendment Act, 1958

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indirect Tax - Coal Mines (Nationalisation) Act, 1973 - Industries (Development And Regulation) Act, 1951 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam (23 of 1973), s. 65 ,64, 71, 55 and 68 - Madhya Pradesh Municipalities Act, 1961, s. 127, 135, 161, 132, and 164(3) - Whether tax was leviable on the property of the appellant since the company was owned wholly by the Govt. of India and that respondent 1 was estopped from levying the property tax by reason of the agreement of 1976? - Subsequent amendments made to the Municipal Corporation Act and the Municipalities Act, 1961, will also apply to the power of taxation - The power to impose tax on lands and buildings is derived by the State Legislature from entry 49 of List II: "Taxes on lands and buildings" - The power of the municipalities to levy tax on lands and buildings has been conferred by the State Legislature on the Special Area Development Authorities - Those authorities have the power to levy that tax in order effectively to discharge the municipal functions which are passed on to them - Entry 54 of List I does not contemplate the taking over of municipal functions - In the meeting of 29-1-1976, respondent 1 had decided to give up its right to impose the octroi tax only - The Chairman of respondent 1 acted beyond the scope of his authority in entering into the agreement with the appellant companies, under which respondent 1 bound itself not to impose any tax whatsoever - Appeal fails, dismissed
