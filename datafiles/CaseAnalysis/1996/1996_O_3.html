O. K. Bhardwaj v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 October 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

B.P. Jeevan Reddy, K.S. Paripoornan

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 4103; (2001) 9 SCC 180

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Departmental Enquiry - Case of minor penalty - Opportunity of hearing - (A) Whether punishment imposing stoppage of three increments with cumulative effect is not a major penalty? - Held, having regard to rule position which expressly says that 'withholding increments of pay with or without cumulative effect' is a minor penalty - (B)Whether in case of minor penalties, it is not necessary to give opportunity to employee to give explanation and it is also not necessary to hear him before awarding penalty? - Further held, even in case of a minor penalty an opportunity has to be given to delinquent employee to have his say or to file his explanation with respect to charges against him - Moreover, if charges are factual and if they are denied by delinquent employee, an enquiry should also be called for - This is minimum requirement of principle of natural justice and said requirement cannot be dispensed with - Since High Court has not considered matter from above angle that is on merits proper course is to remit matter to HC to consider whether an opportunity was given to appellant to put forward his case and whether in light of facts and circumstances of case, an enquiry was called for and if called for, was it held according to law and principles of natural justice, and to dispose of matter according to law - Appeal allowed.
