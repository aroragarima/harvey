Savithri and Others v Karthyayani Amma and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1425; (2007) 11 SCC 621; AIR 2008 SC 300; 2008 (1) AWC 126; JT 2007 (12) SC 248; 2007 (4) KLT 811; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 1337; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 749; 2007(12) SCALE 403; 2007 (8) SCJ 125; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 404

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Family & Personal

Keywords: Mental Capacity, Grandmother

Summary: Family & Personal - Indian Succession Act, 1925 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 63 and 68 - Marumakkattayam School of Law - Relationship between S and wife was strained- S living with sister and her children - Suspicious circumstances surrounding Execution of Will - Testator unwell - (A) Whether he possessed mental capacity to understand contents of Will and whether same was free and/ or voluntary? - (B)Whether Will executed by one S is valid? - Held, a Will like any other document is to be proved in terms of provisions of Indian Succession Act and Indian Evidence Act - Onus of proving Will is on propounder - Testamentary capacity of propounder must also be established - Execution of Will by testator has to be proved - At least one attesting witness is required to be examined for purpose of proving execution of Will - It is required to be shown that Will has been signed by testator with his free Will and that at relevant time he was in sound disposing state of mind and understood nature and effect of disposition - It is also required to be established that he has signed Will in presence of two witnesses who attested his signature in his presence or in presence of each other - Only when there exist suspicious circumstances, onus would be on propounder to explain them to satisfaction of court before it can be accepted as genuine - According to appellants themselves, signature of testator on Will was obtained under undue influence or coercion - Onus to prove same was on them - They have failed to do so if propounder proves that Will was signed by testator and he at relevant time was in sound disposing state of mind and understood nature and effect of disposition, onus stands discharged - In fact situation obtaining herein that any such suspicious circumstance was existing - It is not unmindful of fact that court must satisfy its conscience before its genuineness is accepted - But what is necessary therefore, is a rational approach - Deprivation of a due share by natural heirs itself is not a factor which would lead to conclusion that there exist suspicious circumstances - Background facts should also be taken into consideration - Son was not meeting his father - He had not been attending to him - He was not even meeting expenses for his treatment from 1959, when he lost his job till his death in 1978 - Testator was living with his sister and her children - If in that situation, if testator executed a Will in their favour, no exception thereto can be taken - Even then, something was left for appellant - No legal infirmity in judgment of HC - Appeal dismissed.
