Kalyaneshwari v Union Of India & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

21 January 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Swatanter Kumar, S.H. Kapadia, K.S. Panicker Radhakrishnan

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 69; (2011) 3 SCC 287; 2011(3) ALL MR 462; JT 2011 (2) SC 38; 2011 (3) MLJ(SC) 739; 2012 (2) RCR(Civil) 626; 2011(1) SCALE 651; [2011] 1 S.C.R. 894

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Controlled, Hazardous, Ilo, White Asbestos (Ban on Use and Import) Bill, 2009

Summary: Constitution - Labour & Industrial Law - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 32 - Environment (Protection) Act, 1986 - Factories Act, 1948 - Ban on the manufacture and use of asbestos - Present petition u/art. 32 of the Constitution filed by the petitioner (non-governmental organization) seeking direction to the Union of India and other respondent-States to immediately ban all uses of asbestos in any manner whatsoever - Whether any statutory, fundamental or other right of any person is being violated and an activity which is prohibited under law is being carried out i.e. production and manufacture of asbestos and allied products? - Held, there is no law enacted so far which requires banning of any activity in regard to asbestos at the stage of mining, manufacture or production despite its adverse effects on human health - It is not for SC to legislate and ban an activity under relevant laws - Further, every factory using or manufacturing asbestos, obtains a licence under the Factories Act as well as permission from the competent authorities including permission under the Environmental Laws - Further, reply affidavits filed by different States as well as Union of India clearly bring out that such activity, wherever is being carried out, is in accordance with specified parameters and under due supervision - Hence, once all the laws in force have been complied with and directions of SC as contained in the case of Consumer Education and Research Centre v. Union of India 1995 INDLAW SC 1501 are carried out in their true spirit, there was no reason as to why SC, in exercise of its extraordinary jurisdiction u/art. 32 of the Constitution, should ban such an activity when admittedly large number of families are dependent upon such processes - Direction issued - Writ petition disposed of.
