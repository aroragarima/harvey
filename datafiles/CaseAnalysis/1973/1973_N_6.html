Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal Ii v Naga Hills Tea Company Limited

Supreme Court of India

08 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Hans Raj Khanna, P. Jaganmohan Reddy

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 250; (1973) 4 SCC 200; AIR 1973 SC 2524; 1973 (2) CTR 329; [1973] 89 ITR 236; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 510; 1973 TAXLR 1565

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Keywords: Finance Act, 1959

Summary: Income Tax and Direct Taxes - Finance Act, 1959 - (A) Provision in sch.1, Para D, Proviso 2, cl.(1)(a) - Unabsorbed reduction in rebate for the year 1957-58 - Set off against rebate - (B) Interpretation of Statutes - When provision of a taxing statute can be reasonably interpreted in two ways - Held, cannot be set off against rebate available for assessment year 1959-60 - interpretation which is favourable to the assessees, has got to be accepted - Appeal dismissed.
