Commissioner Of Income Tax, Madhya Pradesh,Nagpur v Hukamchand Mohanlal

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 249; (1972) 4 SCC 368; AIR 1971 SC 2591; 1972 (1) CTR 273; [1971] 82 ITR 624; [1972] 1 S.C.R. 786

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922 ss. 24(B), 41(1) - Assessed - Income - Deceased - Liable to be Taxed - Assessee carried business and succeeded to business on death of her husband - Assessee's firm had recovered certain amount towards sales-tax from Assessee's husband - On appeal filed by firm, Asst. Commissioner had remitted Rs. 24,341, which was returned by firm to assessee' husband by way of draft - ITO sought to tax this amount u/s.41(1) of the Act, assessee contended that said income was income of her husband and not her income, which was dismissed - Assessee filed appeal before Tribunal which allowed contention of assessee - CIT filed appeal before HC, which answered question in favour of assessee - Hence, instant appeal - Whether amount of Rs.24,341 was liable to tax u/s.41(1) of the Act.

Held, any income received in the year subsequent to the previous year or the accounting year could not be called income received by the deceased person. Thus the provisions u/ s. 24(B) did not extend to tax liability of the estate of a deceased person beyond the previous or the accounting year - That section, cannot possibly apply to the instant case because the assessee who is now sought to be taxed is not the assessee contemplated by that section and assessee u/s. 41 (1) of the Act, having died the Revenue could not take any advantage of its provisions. Hence, HC rightly observed that the question whether the amount of Rs. 24,341/- was liable to tax as the personal income of the assessee did not arise in the present case in which the sole point to be decided was whether that amount was assessable in the assessee's hands u/s. 41 (1) of the Act. Appeal dismissed.
