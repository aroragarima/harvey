Harkesh Chand v Krishan Gopal Mehta and others

Supreme Court of India

13 February 2017

Case Analysis

Bench

Sharad Arvind Bobde, Ashok Bhushan

Where Reported

2017 Indlaw SC 114; (2017) 4 SCC 537; AIR 2017 SC 969; 2017 (121) ALR 877; JT 2017 (2) SC 419; 2017 (3) MLJ 44; 2017 (1) RCR(Rent) 218; 2017 (135) RD 710; 2017(2) SCALE 756

Case Digest

Subject: Rent Control

Keywords: Transfer Of Property Act, 1882, Suit For Eviction, Mines Act, 1923, Bombay General Clauses Act, 1904, Mines Act, 1952, Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972, Arms Act, 1878, Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904, United Provinces (Temporary) Accommodation Requisition Act, 1947

Summary: Rent Control - United Provinces (Temporary) Accommodation Requisition Act, 1947 - Uttar Pradesh Urban Buildings (Regulation of Letting, Rent and Eviction) Act, 1972 - Uttar Pradesh General Clauses Act, 1904 - Eviction of tenant - Applicability of notification that was issued under repealed Act - Landlord filed suit for eviction on 1-10-1972 against tenant in respect of small shop located in Doiwala area that was given on rent but said suit was dismissed with costs on ground that though 1947 Act was repealed by the time suit was filed and 1972 Act came into force, still, only the 1947 Act was applicable to suit property - Aggrieved, landlord filed revision petition before Revision Court but the same was dismissed holding that notification by which provisions of 1947 Act were applicable to Doiwala area in year 1949 continued and remained in force on date when notice of termination of tenancy was issued and accordingly, tenancy was protected by 1947 Act - Aggrieved, landlord filed writ petition before HC wherein HC upheld landlord's plea that notice which terminated tenancy of the shop under the provisions of 1882 Act was valid and further, HC held that there was no protection to tenants in Doiwala area under any law during the period between 15-7-1972 and 23-1-1973 and thus, notice terminating the tenancy was valid and accordingly, allowed writ petition and set aside orders of Trial Court and Revision Court - Hence, instant appeal by tenant - Whether there existed a protection of tenants in Doiwala area under 1947 Act by virtue of notification dt.31-3-1949.

Held, there was nothing inconsistent between the protection accorded to tenants under 1947 Act as applied to Doiwala town by notification dt.31-3-1949, and the protection accorded to tenants in the re-enacted provision of 1972 Act, both of which regulated the eviction of tenants in the whole of Uttar Pradesh. S.21 of the 1972 Act provided the same restrictions on the eviction of tenants on specified grounds that s.3(a) of 1947 Act did. Thus, there is no inconsistency whatsoever found between the two provisions. Further, there was nothing in 1972 Act that shows that such a protection was intended to be removed from any area or the Doiwala area and the contrary is clear from the fact that notification expressly applying the re-enacted provisions to Doiwala area was issued on 21-3-1973. Hence, there could be no inconsistency between the notification applying the Act to the Doiwala area, and the re-enacted provisions of the Act unless the 1972 Act clearly expresses an intention to remove the protection accorded to tenants from an area. Further, none of the conditions which derogate from the continuation of the notification exist in instant case. There is no express provision to the contrary, there is no suppression by any statutory instrument under the re-enacted provisions and there is nothing inconsistent in the continuance of the notification with any of the re-enacted provisions. Further, the notification under 1947 Act continued in spite of its repeal and the enactment of the 1972 Act. It could not be said that in the hiatus between the repeal of 1947 Act and the issuance of a notification applying the 1972 Act to the Doiwala area, the Legislature intended that the tenants had no protection from eviction and there was an unrestricted right to evict them. Hence, 1947 Act is applicable to Doiwala area by virtue of notification dt.31-3-1949, when the suit for eviction of tenant was filed. The suit is untenable for the want of permission under the provision of 1972 Act and is liable to be dismissed. However, in view of circumstances of the case and in the interest of justice, tenant is directed to hand over possession of the premises to landlord after a period of three years from date of instant order and till the time tenant hands over possession, tenant to pay rent of Rs.4000. Appeal disposed of.

Ratio - Any statutory instrument issued under the repealed enactment continues in force as if it were issued under the re-enacted provisions to the extent that it is not inconsistent with the re-enacted provisions. Such continuance exists till the statutory instrument is superseded by a statutory instrument issued under the re-enacted provisions.
