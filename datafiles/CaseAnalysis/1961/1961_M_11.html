Abdul Kadir Shamsuddin Bubere v Madhav Prabhakar Oak

Supreme Court of India

20 September 1961

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1961 Indlaw SC 338; AIR 1962 SC 406; 1962 (1) AnWR 276; 1962 (64) BomLR 267; 1962 (1) MLJ(SC) 276; 1962 (1) SCJ 620; [1962] 3 S.C.R. 702

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - S. 20 - Whether Ambikabai admittedly had a share in the forest and as she was not a party to the application there could be no reference to arbitration as the whole dispute as to the forest would not be before the arbitrators? - If Anant may not be able to represent the interest of Ambikabai in the arbitration proceedings that will follow in this case, that is no reason for not giving effect to the arbitration clause in the agreement of 27-02-1953 as between the parties to that agreement, hence the contention of the appellant fails - Whether the respondents only desired in their application that the disputes arising out of the agreements be referred to arbitration but did not include the other agreement and therefore no reference should be made as it would be a piecemeal reference resulting in splitting up the cause of action? - The agreement of May 1952 would have to be considered by any arbitrator who is going into the dispute arising out of the agreement of October 1948 - It cannot be said that the reference desired in this case is piecemeal and split up the case of action as such the contention of the appellant fails - Whether the dispute sought to be referred was not covered by the arbitration clause? - Arbitration clause provides for reference to arbitration of all disputes arising out of agreements - In the language of the arbitration clause it cannot be possible said that the dispute which has been raised in the present case is outside the terms of the arbitration clause hence, the contention of the appellant in this behalf fails - Whether the respondents had made allegations of fraud against the appellant in their application and that was also a ground for not referring the dispute to arbitration? - That merely because some allegations have been made that accounts are not correct or that certain items are exaggerated and so on that is not enough to induce the court to refuse to make a reference to arbitration as such this contention of the appellant must also therefore fail - Held - Appeal dismissed with costs.
