Prabhakar V. Sinari v Shanker Anant Verlekar

Supreme Court of India

29 November 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 179; AIR 1969 SC 686; 1969 CRLJ 1057; 1969 LLJ 1057; 1969 (2) MLJ 1; [1969] 2 S.C.R. 1013

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure 1898

Summary: Criminal - Criminal Procedure Code, 1898, s. 197 - Whether previous sanction under s. 197 of CrPC, 1898 was required to prosecute Appellant (Deputy Superintendent of Police)? - Held, it was not every offence committed by a public servant which required sanction u/s. 197 of CrPC, 1898 nor every act done by him while he was actually engaged in performance of his official duties but if act complained of was directly concerned with his official duties so that, if questioned, it could be claimed to have been done by virtue of office, then sanction would be necessary and that would be so, irrespective of whether it was in fact, a proper discharge of his duties or not - In what capacity appellant came to spot not clear - On basis of statement of complainant it was not established that he came in capacity of a police officer - If a person is in possession of some property and other persons are threatening to dispossess him it is no part of duty of a police officer to take sides and decide dispute - Thus, appellant could not be regarded to have committed alleged act while acting or purporting to act in discharge of his official duties - Appeal dismissed.
