Controller of Estate-Duty, Madras v C. R. Ramachandra Gounder

Supreme Court of India

27 February 1973

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Jaganmohan Reddy, Hans Raj Khanna, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1973 Indlaw SC 254; (1973) 4 SCC 102; (1973) SCC (Tax) 388; AIR 1973 SC 1170; 1973 (2) CTR 193; 1974 (1) ITJ 5; [1973] 88 ITR 448; 1974 (1) MLJ(SC) 36; 1974 (1) SCJ 104; [1973] 3 S.C.R. 554; 1973 TAXLR 841; 1973 UJ 608

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax

Summary: Land & Property - Estate Duty Act, 1953 - Applicability of 10 -(A) Gift by father in favour of sons, of house property occupied by firm as tenant at will - Father, a partner of firm dies - (B) Transfer of certain sum from father's account in a firm in favour of his sons - Held, such a property could not be included in deceased's property u/s 10, because benefit, father had as a partner of firm was not in any way referable to gift - Such a gift of Rs.1 Lakh could not be included in property of deceased within s.10 - Appeal dismissed.
