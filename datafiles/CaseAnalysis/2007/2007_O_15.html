Smt. Thokchom Ongbi Sangeeta @ Sangi Devi and Another v Oriental Insurance Company Limited and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

Arijit Pasayat, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1061; (2007) 11 SCC 750; 2007 (4) ACC 377; AIR 2008 SC 245; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 81; 2007 (69) ALR 903; 2007 (4) AWC 3955; 2007 (1) CCC 459; JT 2007 (12) SC 279; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 151; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 764; 2007(12) SCALE 448; 2007 (8) SCJ 150; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 609

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Insurance; Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Contractual Liability, Legislative Intent, Appeals Disposed Of, Public Service Vehicle, Third Party Risks, Carried For Hire Or Reward

Summary: Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Tribunal by common judgment and award awarded compensation of Rs.2, 99, 464/- in one case and also an award of Rs.1, 62, 000/- in another case - High Court by the impugned judgment, accepted the plea and held that the insurer was not liable to pay the compensation - Appeal against - Provisions of the Act do not enjoin any statutory liability on the owner of a vehicle to get his vehicle insured for any passenger traveling in a goods carriage and the insurer would have no liability therefore - Remitted matter to High Court for the limited purpose of fixing the responsibility of the person who is to satisfy the Award made by the Tribunal - Appeals disposed of.
