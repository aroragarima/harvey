Â Moon Mills, Limited v Industrial Court, Bombay (M. R. Meher), and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 February 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, J.C. Shah, S.M. Sikri, C.A. Vaidyialingam, K. Subba Rao

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 22; AIR 1967 SC 1450; 1967 (14) FLR 375; 1967 (2) LLJ 34

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Practice & Procedure - Bombay Industrial Relations Act, 1946 ss.66, 72(1), 114(2) - Agreement - Bonus - Award - Maintainability - Appellant carried on business as cotton textile mill - Respondent No.2 was representative union of cotton textile industry gave notice of change in connection with method to be adopted for grant of bonus for years 1953 to 1957 and entered into agreement with mill owners - Therefore, dispute arise out of notice, was referred to arbitration of Industrial Court by submission in writing dt. 28-2-1956 u/s.66 of the Act and award was passed - Appellant failed to carry out directions contained in award - Aggrieved respondent No.2 made application before Labour Court contending that appellant had committed illegal change under the Act which allowed application and gave directions to appellant - Aggrieved appellant filed appeal before Industrial court which rejected appeal and affirmed Labour Court's order - Aggrieved appellant filed application before Single Judge which was dismissed - Appellant again filed letter patents appeal before DB which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC order was just and proper - Held, relied on New Maneck Chowk Spinning and Weaving Company, Limited v Textile Labour Association, Ahmedabad 1960 Indlaw SC 204 it could not be enforced by industrial adjudication against will of any of parties - Therefore, impugned notification should be bad inasmuch as it goes beyond powers conferred on by State Govt. u/s. 114(2) of the Act and should be struck down - In instant case Court opined that there was no such negligence or laches or acquiescence on part of appellant as might disentitle to grant of writ and set aside judgment of DB and appellant was granted writ of certiorari for quashing order of Industrial Court, and Labour Court - Hence, Court however, made clear that decision would not prejudice trial of any references with respect to bonus which might be pending or which might hereafter be made between appellant and its employees in respect of years 1955 and 1956 - If such references were pending or should hereafter be made, they would be proceeded with and decided in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
