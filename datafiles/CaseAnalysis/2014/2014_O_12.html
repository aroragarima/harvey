Oriental Bank of Commerce and others v S. S. Sheokand and another

Supreme Court of India

26 February 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, J. Chelameswar

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 125; (2014) 5 SCC 172; 2014 (3) AWC 2587; 2014 (141) FLR 598; JT 2014 (3) SC 476; 2014 (3) LLJ 241; 2014(2) SCALE 682; [2014] 3 S.C.R. 341; 2014 (3) SLR 96

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: (1) Employment-Imposition of major penalty of reduction of two stages in pay scale-Respondent, senior manager in appellant-bank-Respondent purchased third party cheques/drafts of huge amounts beyond discretionary powers of lending-This was done without completing pre-sanction formalities-Decision to impose major punishment had to be interfered with because of manner in which decision was taken-That being so, order of High Court quashing punishment will remain-However, direction of High Court to appellant-bank to consider respondent for promotion-Not sustainable-Inasmuch as record of respondent was not satisfactory there was no occasion for High Court to give any such direction on footing that respondent was denied consideration for promotion only because he had suffered punishment-Whereas impugned judgment and order of High Court setting aside punishment will remain, direction to consider respondent for promotion and give him benefits on that footing liable to be set aside and accordingly directed-Respondent, however, to get monetary benefits on footing that said punishment is quashed.

(2) Employment-Promotion-Promotion as such, and in any case, to higher post-Cannot be insisted as matter of right.
