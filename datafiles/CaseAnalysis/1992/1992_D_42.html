Union Territory Of Chandigarh v Dilbagh Singh And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

03 November 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Venkatachala, Yogeshwar Dayal, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 425; (1993) SCC (L&S) 144; (1993) 1 SCC 154; AIR 1993 SC 796; 1993 (23) ATC 431; 1993 (1) CLR 517; JT 1992 (Supp) SC 712; 1992 LabIC 2548; 1993 (2) LLJ 1043; 1993 (1) LLN 248; 1992(3) SCALE 80; 1993 (1) SLR 451

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 and 311 - Cancellation of selection list prepared in an unfair and injudicious manner - Selectees, neither have right of appointment unless so provided by Rules nor right of hearing where cancellation is for bona fide and valid reasons - Issue arise as to: (A) Hearing to members of selection committee - (B) Appeal against cancellation of list when enquiry revealing of favouritism and corruption - On ground that charges levelled are not established by direct evidence - Held, it is not a requirement of principles of natural justice - Not open to be challenged - Appeal allowed.
