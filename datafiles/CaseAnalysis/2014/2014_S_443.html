Paulmeli and another v State of Tamil Nadu Through Inspector of Police

Supreme Court of India

23 May 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, A.K. Sikri

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 386; (2014) 13 SCC 90; 2014 CRLJ 3240; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 335; 2014(7) SCALE 508; [2014] 6 S.C.R. 420

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Murder, Conviction

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 147, 148, 324, 326, 307 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants (accused) allegedly encircled the deceased and caused indiscriminate cuts over his body using an 'Aruval' which caused instantaneous death and sons of deceased were also got injured and appellants thereby committed offence murder and were charged with offences u/ss. 147, 148, 324, 326, 307 and 302 of IPC - Trial Court convicted appellants and convicted them u/s. 302 of IPC and sentenced them to under imprisonment for life - Aggrieved appellants filed appeal, before HC which was dismissed by impugned judgment - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that on the basis of same evidence, 15 accused persons were acquitted and that appellants could not have been convicted - Whether impugned conviction of appellants was justified -

Held, Court did not find any force in the submissions appellants that the injuries attributed to appellants could not be caused by 'Aruval' as the findings recorded by Trial Court in that regard was that all injuries might be caused by the attack of Aruval - It had further been held by Trial Court that appellants came with 'Aruval' and attacked the deceased indiscriminately causing injuries on the neck, chest and other parts of body, though, inadvertently, the Trial Court had mentioned that the injuries found on all over the body, had caused the death - Deposition of PW3, it was submitted that he might not be a trustworthy witness as he was not an independent witness - His evidence revealed that he reached the place after the incident was over and he saw the accused people leaving the place of occurrence - Had he not been a truthful witness he could have definitely improved his version and could depose describing the overt acts of appellants - Thus, submission so advanced had no force - PW2 (son of deceased) had supported the case of prosecution so far as appellants were concerned - PW2 was declared hostile when he did not name the other accused, who stood acquitted by Courts below and there could be no difficultly to accept his deposition to that extent - Law could be summarised to the effect that evidence of a hostile witness could not be discarded as a whole, and relevant parts thereof which were admissible in law, could be used by prosecution or the defence - In instant case, there might be some exaggeration in depositions of prosecution witnesses - Courts below had not accepted the evidence to that extent and have given benefit of doubt - Thus, it had to be appraised in each case as to what extent the evidence was worthy of credence, and merely because in some respects the Court consider the same to be insufficient or unworthy of reliance, it did not necessarily follow as a matter of law that it should be disregarded in all respects as well - Appeal was devoid of any merit - Appeal dismissed.
