Harkirat Singh v Amrinder Singh

Supreme Court of India

16 December 2005

Case Analysis

Bench

C.K. Thakker, Y.K. Sabharwal, P.K. Balasubramanyan

Where Reported

2005 Indlaw SC 809; (2005) 13 SCC 511; AIR 2006 SC 713; 2006 (5) AWC 4946; JT 2005 (10) SC 513; 2006 (1) Law Herald (P&H) 1; 2006 (1) RCR(Civil) 132; 2005(10) SCALE 200; 2006 (1) SCJ 761; [2005] Supp5 S.C.R. 817

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Election; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Election Expenses, Election Dispute, District Election Officer, Grounds For Declaring Election To Be Void

Summary: [A.] Election - Representation Of People Act, 1951, ss. 100, 123, 123(7)(a), 77, 80, 81, 82, 83, 86, 86(5) - CPC, 1908, o. 25, r. 4, o. 6, rr. 15, o. 7, r. 11 - Conduct of Election Rules, 1961, rr. 90, 94-A - Challenge to election of respondent to Punjab Legislative Assembly on corrupt practice of procuring assistance for his election from persons who are in service of Govt. - Distinction between 'material facts' and 'particulars' - Held, HC was wrong in dismissing election petition on ground that material facts had not been set out in election petition and election petition did not disclose a cause of action - Appeal allowed.
