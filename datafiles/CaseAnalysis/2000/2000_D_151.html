U. P. State Sugar Corporation Limited v Dy. Director of Consolidation and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

S. Saghir Ahmad, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 706; (2000) 2 SCC 572; AIR 2000 SC 878; 2000 ALJ 504; 2000 (38) ALR 798; 2000 (2) AWC 933; JT 2000 (1) SC 555; 2000 (91) RD 165; 2000(1) SCALE 435; 2000 (2) SCJ 295; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 673; 2000 (2) SLT 122; 2000 (1) Supreme 435

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Municipalities & Local Governments; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Land & Property - Board of Revenue Act, 1913 - Indian Limitation Act, 1908 - Uttar Pradesh Zamindari Abolition and Land Reforms Act, 1950 - On commencement of proceedings under Uttar Pradesh Consolidation of Holdings Act, respondent, filed objections claiming 'sirdari' rights over plots referred to above on basis of a lease executed in his favour by Chairman, Land Management Committee - Appellant, also filed objections claiming sirdari rights over those plots over which there existed their storage tank for molasses and a tank for sullage water - Whether respondent had sirdari rights over plots? - Held, plots have to be vested in Gaon Sabha, by a notification u/s. 117 of U. P. Z. A. and L. R. Act for Land Management Committee to admit any person on plot - Before a property is vested in Gaon Sabha, it should have first vested in State Govt. u/s. 6 of Act - In view of provisions of ss. 7(aa) and 9 of Act, land in dispute, which was held by Consolidation Officer and Settlement Officer (Consolidation) to be land appurtenant to staff quarters of Sugar Mill, had not vested in State u/s. 6 of Act as a consequence of notification issued u/s. 4 of Act - Once these plots did not vest in State, they would not vest in Gaon Sabha and Gaon Sabha had, therefore no jurisdiction to grant lease of those plots to respondent - Such a lease was a void document from inception and, consequently, jurisdiction of Consolidation Authorities was not affected - Appeal allowed.
