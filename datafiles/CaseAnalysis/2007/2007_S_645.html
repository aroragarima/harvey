Devinder Singh and Others v State of Punjab and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 October 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1389; (2008) 1 SCC 728; AIR 2008 SC 261; 2008 (1) ALD(SC) 84; 2008 (1) AWC 168; 2008 (1) CalLT(SC) 31; JT 2007 (12) SC 256; 2007 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3168; 2007 (5) Law Herald (P&H) 3459; 2008 (1) MLJ(SC) 887; 2007 (4) RCR(Civil) 799; 2007(12) SCALE 496; [2007] 11 S.C.R. 475

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Public Office, Negotiation

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 6, 9, 41, 44(B), 55 and 5(A) - Land Acquisition (Companies) Rules, 1963, r. 4 - Whether acquisition has been made for a public purpose? - Whether Land Acquisition Act is an expropriatory legislation? - Held, State ordinarily can acquire a property in exercise of its power of eminent domain subject to existence of public purpose and on payment of reasonable compensation in terms of provisions of Act - State has been empowered to acquire land also for companies and for purposes other than public purpose - S. 6(3) says is that declaration shall be conclusive evidence that land is needed for a public purpose or for a company In this case declaration was that land was needed for a company and that according to s. 6(3) is conclusive evidence that land is so needed - (B) Whether r. 4 of Companies Rules is mandatory or directory in nature? - Held, r. 4 of Rules employs word shall not once place but twice - Ordinarily, it is imperative in character - Before issuance of s. 4 notification, there is no requirement as such of compliance with procedure contemplated by r. 4 of Rules - Therefore unable to subscribe to view that enquiry by r. 4 must precede issuance of notification u/s. 4(1) of Act - Appeal allowed.
