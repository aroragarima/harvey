Â Indian Steel & Wire Products Ltd. v State Of Madras

Supreme Court of India

11 September 1967

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, G.K. Mitter

Where Reported

1967 Indlaw SC 222; AIR 1968 SC 478; [1968] 1 S.C.R. 479; 1968 (21) STC 138

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Sales Tax

Summary: T.N. General Sales Tax Act, 1939, s. 2(h) -Indian Income Tax Act, 1922 - Iron and Steel (Control of Production and Distribution) Order, 1941, cls. 2, 10(b), 4 and 5 - Constitution, sch. 7, list 2, entry 54 - Sale of Goods Act, 1930, s. 4 - Sales Tax - Sale - Meaning u/s. 2(h) of 1939 Act - Essential conditions of a valid sale - Sale of iron and steel products to various buyers in pursuance of orders of Controller exercising powers unders Order of 1941 - Whether the transaction amounts sale and is liable to Sales Tax - Whether levy is constitutional - Held, transactions constitute sale - Liability to sales tax arises - Levy not outside powers of State legislature under entry 54 list II, sch. VII of the Constitution -

Appeal Dismissed.
