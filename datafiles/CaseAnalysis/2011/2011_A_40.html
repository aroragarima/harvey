Akhil Bhartiya Upbhokta Congress v State Of Madhya Pradesh And Others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

G.S. Singhvi, A.K. Ganguly

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 232; (2011) 5 SCC 29; AIR 2011 SC 1834; 2011 (4) AWC 3760; JT 2011 (4) SC 311; 2011 (4) MLJ(SC) 972; 2011(4) SCALE 355; [2011] 5 S.C.R. 77

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Permanent Lease, Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, Madhya Pradesh Zila Yojana Samiti Adhiniyam, 1995, Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Viksit Bhoomiyo, Griho, Bhavano Tatha Anya Sanrachanao Ka Vyayan Niyam, 1975, Madhya Pradesh Government Rules

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Trusts & Associations - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973, s. 23A - Allotment of land to trust - Violations of procedure - Challenged - 'X' by describing himself as a Convenor of the Memorial Trust, made a request to the Principal Secretary for reservation of 30 acres land in favour of Memorial Trust to enable it to establish an All India Training Institute - Principal Secretary opined that impugned land cannot be reserved for the Memorial Trust on the ground that as per Bhopal Development Plan, impugned land was reserved for public and semi public (health) purposes - Subsequently, 'X', by describing himself as Managing Trustee of respondent no.5 (Memorial Trust), submitted fresh proposal for reservation of impugned land in favour of respondent no. 5 - Respondent no. 3 (Commissioner-cum-Director, Town and Country Planning) informed the Principal Secretary that impugned land was earmarked for public and semi-public (health) purpose and if land was to be allotted to the Memorial Trust, then the earlier land use would be required to be cancelled - However, without effecting change of land, the State Government reserved impugned land in favour of the respondent no. 5 - Thereafter, respondent no. 5 informed respondent no. 4 (Collector) that the respondent no. 5 would require only 20 acres land and made a request that the premium may be waived because the Trust was being established in public interest and it will have no source of income - The political set up of the State Government readily obliged respondent no. 5 and it was decided that the impugned land be given to the respondent no. 5 by charging annual lease rent of Rs.1 only - Thereafter, State Government issued notification dt. 6.6.2008 u/s. 23-A(1)(a) of the Act proposing change of land use in respect of impugned land and invited objections/suggestions - 5 persons filed objections against the proposed change of land use but the same were rejected as untenable - Thereafter, final notification dt. 5.9.2008 was issued u/s. 23-A(2) of the Act - Appellant, engaged in public welfare activities, challenged said allotment of land to respondent no.5 on the grounds of violation of art. 14 of the Constitution and the provisions of the Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973 - DB dismissed said writ petition - Hence, the instant appeal - (A) Whether the decision of the State Government to allot 20 acres land to respondent no. 5 without any advertisement and without inviting other similarly situated organisations/institutions to participate in the process of allotment was contrary to art. 14 of the Constitution and the provisions of the Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973? - Held, State and/or its agencies/instrumentalities cannot give largesse to any person according to the whims of the political entities and/or officers of the State - Every action/decision of the State and/or its gencies/instrumentalities to give largesse or confer benefit must be founded on a sound, transparent, discernible and well defined policy - Any allotment of land or grant of other form of largesse by the State or its agencies/instrumentalities by treating the exercise as a private venture was liable to be treated as arbitrary, discriminatory and an act of favoritism and nepotism - In the instant case, the application for reservation of land was made by 'X', in his capacity as convener of Memorial Trust - Respondents have not placed on record any document to show that on the date of application, the Memorial Trust was registered as a public trust - The exercise for reservation of 30 acres land and allotment of 20 acres was not preceded by any advertisement in the newspaper or by any other recognized mode of publicity inviting applications from organizations/institutions like the Memorial Trust or respondent no.5 for allotment of land and everything was done by the political and non-political functionaries of the State as if they were under a legal obligation to allot land to the Memorial Trust and/or respondent No.5 - Moreover, all its trustees were members of a particular party and the entire exercise for the reservation and allotment of land and waiver of major portion of the premium was undertaken because political functionaries of the State wanted to favour respondent no. 5 - Therefore, allotment of impugned land in favour of respondent no. 5 was contrary to art. 14 of the Constitution and the provisions of the Madhya Pradesh Nagar Tatha Gram Nivesh Adhiniyam, 1973 - (B) Whether notifications dt. 6.6.2008 and 5.9.2008 by which the Bhopal Development Plan was modified were ultra vires the provisions of s. 23-A of the Act? - Held, in the Bhopal Development plan, the use of land which was reserved and allotted to respondent no.5 was shown as public and semi public (health) purpose - The State Government modified the plan by invoking s. 23-A(1)(a) of the Act for the purpose of facilitating establishment of an institute by respondent no. 5 and not for any proposed project of the Government of India or the State Government and its enterprises - The exercise undertaken for the change of land use, which resulted in modification of the development plan was an empty formality because land had been allotted to respondent no.5 almost two years prior to the issue of notification u/s. 23-A (1)(a) of the Act - Hence, modification of the development plan was ultra vires the provisions of s. 23-A(1)(a) of the Act - Impugned order of the DB set aside - The allotment of 20 acres land to respondent No.5 declared illegal and quashed - Notifications dt. 6.6.2008 and 5.9.2008 issued by the State Government u/s. 23-A(1)(a) and (2) quashed - Appeal allowed.
