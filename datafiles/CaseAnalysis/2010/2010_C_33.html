Chaitanya Prakash And Anr. v H. Omkarappa

Supreme Court of India

12 January 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

Mukundakam Sharma, V.S. Sirpurkar

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 15; (2010) 2 SCC 623; AIR 2010 SC (Supp) 444; 2010(2) ALL MR 1006; 2010 (4) AWC 4195; 2010 (124) FLR 503; JT 2010 (1) SC 217; 2010 (2) LLJ 146; 2010 (3) MLJ(SC) 753; 2010(1) SCALE 386; [2010] 1 S.C.R. 467; 2010 (1) SLR 517; 2010 (1) UPLBEC 328

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Termination - Respondent's services was terminated by appellants for unsatisfactory work - Respondent filed writ praying for setting aside and quashing order issued by appellants contending that appellant's order was stigmatic - Respondent also contended that subsequent to order of termination, respondent applied for post of Managing Director of Spices Trading Corporation Ltd. but he was not called for interview held during selection process in view of letter sent by appellants bringing to their notice misconduct of respondent - HC allowed said writ petition holding that order of termination passed by appellants against respondent was stigmatic, and therefore, said order could not have been given effect to without giving an opportunity to respondent - Hence, present appeal - Whether impugned order passed by appellants against respondent terminating his service during period of probation was an order of termination simpliciter due to unsatisfactory service or 'stigmatic' due to misconduct? - Held, appellant had time and again specifically brought to notice of respondent his short comings and no misconduct as such is alleged against respondent by appellant and therefore present case is a case of termination simpliciter due to unsuitability of respondent and not a case of punishment for misconduct - Moreover, appellants informed Spices Trading Corporation Ltd. company on being specifically asked by said company about performance of respondent and consequently it was informed that his service was terminated due to unsuitability, which is a fact - If, they would have not intimated same to company despite their specific query then they would have been suppressing material fact - Hence, impugned order is not stigmatic - HC's order set aside and appellant's order restored - Appeal allowed.
