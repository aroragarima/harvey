Mannan S. K. and others v State of West Bengal and another

Supreme Court of India

03 July 2014

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjana Prakash Desai, N.V. Ramana

Where Reported

2014 Indlaw SC 428; (2014) 13 SCC 59; AIR 2014 SC 2950; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 4463; 2014 (4) CalLT(SC) 13; 2014 CRLJ 4072; JT 2014 (10) SC 156; 2014 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 2904; 2014 (4) MLJ(Crl) 82; 2014 (4) RCR(Criminal) 617; 2014(8) SCALE 187; [2014] 8 S.C.R. 155

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Criminal - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 34, 304, 326 and 447 - Recalling of witness - Permissibility - FIR was registered u/ss. 447, 326, 304 r/w s. 34 of the IPC and ss. 3 and 4 of the Act - PW15 (Investigating Officer) was examined and he stated in his evidence that he had recorded deceased statement at the scene of offence - In the cross- examination he stated that he had recorded one statement of deceased and this statement was not brought on record - After one month, prosecution filed application for recalling PW15 because the prosecution wanted to bring on record statement of deceased which it had inadvertently omitted to do - Trial Court rejected the application - On appeal, HC reversed the Trial Court's order - HC held that the non-exhibiting of the statement of deceased was mistake of the prosecution and no advantage could flow from the said mistake to the accused - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was justified -

Held, PW15 stated in the court that he had recorded the statement of deceased - Thus, this fact was known to the defence and he was cross-examined by the defence - Said statement was not brought on record through PW15 died after the said statement was recorded - Said statement, therefore, became vital to the prosecution - Prosecution wanted to treat it as a dying declaration - Therefore, it was an essential material to the just decision of the case - Though, the fact of the recording of the statement was deposed to by PW15, since due to oversight it was not brought on record, application was made u/s. 311 of the CrPC praying for recall of PW15 - This could not be termed as an inherent weakness or a latent wedge in the matrix of the prosecution case - Since the accused knew that such a statement was recorded by PW15, no prejudice could be said to have been caused to the accused - Impugned order merits no interference - Appeal dismissed.
