Institute Of Chartered Accountants v L.K. Ratna And Others

Supreme Court of India

21 October 1986

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, S. Mukharji

Where Reported

1986 Indlaw SC 473; (1986) 4 SCC 537; AIR 1987 SC 71; 1986 ATC 714; [1987] 61 Comp Cas 266; 1986 (3) CompLJ 352; 1986 (57) CTR 121; [1987] 164 ITR 1; JT 1986 SC 671; 1987 (1) LLN 4; 1986(2) SCALE 634; [1986] 3 S.C.R. 1049; [1986] 28 TAXMAN 654

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Criminal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Corporate - Chartered Accountants Act, 1949 - Minimum Wages Act, 1948 - Allegation of misconduct made by member - Report of Disciplinary Committee submitted - Consideration by Council - Whether a member of Institute of Chartered Accountants of India is entitled to hearing by Council of Institute on submission of report by Disciplinary Committee to Council of its enquiry into allegations of misconduct against member - Whether proceeding is vitiated by presence of members of Disciplinary Committee when Council proceeds to consider report of Disciplinary Committee? - Whether Council is obliged to give reasons for its finding that a member is guilty of misconduct? - Held, member accused of misconduct is entitled to a hearing by Council when he comes to know that he is or is not guilty as per report of Disciplinary Committee - Power to find and record whether a member is guilty of misconduct is to entire Council itself and not to a few of its members who constitute Disciplinary Committee - Order must contain reasons - In fairness and justice, member is entitled to know why he has been found guilty - Order accordingly.
