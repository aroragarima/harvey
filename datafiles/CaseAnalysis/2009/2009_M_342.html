Chairman Cum Managing Director, Coal India v Mukul Kumar Choudhuri & Ors

Supreme Court of India

24 August 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1049; (2009) 15 SCC 620; AIR 2010 SC 75; 2009 (3) CLR 645; 2010 (2) ESC 248; 2009 (123) FLR 601; 2009 (8) MLJ(SC) 460; 2009(11) SCALE 608; [2009] 13 S.C.R. 487; 2009 (7) SLR 251; 2009 (7) SLR 546

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Absence From Duty, Coal India Executives Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978

Summary: Service - Coal India Executives Conduct Discipline and Appeal Rules, 1978, r.29 -Back wages - Award of - Termination of 1st respondent-employee on charge of unauthorized absence for 6 months - Termination set aside by Single Judge, however, back wages denied - Back wages allowed by DB in 1st respondent's appeal - Legality - Contention of appellant that charge has been fully admitted by 1st respondent - Held, no scope left to differ with conclusion arrived at by Inquiry Officer about proof of charges when delinquent fully admitted charges - No procedural illegality or irregularity in departmental enquiry - Misconduct of 1st respondent was clearly established - Single Judge and DB fell into grave error - However, punishment awarded was disproportionate to delinquency - Therefore, denial of back wages to be regarded as punishment instead of termination - Appeal partly allowed.
