Punjab Sikh Regular Motor Service, Moudhapara v Regional Transport Authority, Raipur and Another

Supreme Court of India

15 October 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Mohammad Hidayatullah

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 243; AIR 1966 SC 1318; 1966 JabLJ 684; 1966 MPLJ 836; 1966 (2) SCJ 354; [1966] 2 S.C.R. 221

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Grant Of Permit

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act 1939, ss. 5 and 45 - Central Provinces and Berar Motor Vehicles Rules, rr.62 and 63 - (A) Whether an application for renewal of stage carriage permit by grant of counter-signature must be made within period prescribed by s. 58 (2) of 1939 Act? - Held, a proper construction of rules made by State Govt. in regard to grant of permits and countersignatures of inter-regional permits RTA, Raipur was not competent to renew countersignature on permit for tile interregional route granted by RTA, Bilaspur in present case - (B) Whether RTA, Raipur was competent to countersign Renewal, if so, whether order of RTA, Raipur granting countersignature of permit was illegal and ultra vires? - Further held, if RTA, Bilaspur had power to renew countersignature on permit u/r. 63, it must be held that RTA, Raipur had no such power u/r. 62 because latter rule is expressly made subject to provisions of r. 63, and power granted to RTA u/s 62 is taken away by provisions of r. 63 - It follows, therefore, that RTA, Raipur was not competent to renew countersignature on permit in present case and RTA, Bilaspur was alone competent to renew countersignature of permit - Futher Court held acordingly, that order of RTA, Raipur granting countersignature of permit was illegal and ultra vires and was rightly quashed by High Court - Appeal dismissed.
