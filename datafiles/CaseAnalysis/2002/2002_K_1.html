K. M. Mathew v K. A. Abraham and Others

Supreme Court of India

23 August 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1649; (2002) 6 SCC 670; (2002) SCC (Cr) 1480; AIR 2002 SC 2989; 2002 (3) CCR 237; 2002 (4) Crimes 17; 2002 (6) CRJ 385; 2002 CRLJ 4111; 2002 (3) JCC 1523; JT 2002 (6) SC 248; 2002 (3) KLT 282; 2002 (4) RCR(Criminal) 288; 2002(6) SCALE 82; 2002 (5) SLT 30; [2002] Supp1 S.C.R. 662; 2002 (5) Supreme 511

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Media & Entertainment - Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 319 - Press & Registration Act, 1867, s. 7 - Indian penal Code,1860, s. 500 - Libelous Matter - Defamation - Challenged - Appellants/Managing Editor, Chief Editor or Resident Editor were alleged that they had knowledge of libelous matter which was published in their newspaper publications, and were responsible for such publication and thus committed offence of defamation besides other allied offences - Respondents approached Trial Court which took cognizance of offences and issued summons to appellants - Aggrieved, appellants challenged said prosecution before HC contended that s. 7 of the Act said that appellants were not liable to be prosecuted only appellant/Editor of News paper was liable be prosecuted for any of offence for such libelous publication - According to appellants in instant case Chief Editor of 'Malayalam Manorma' and that there was also Editor for publication who alone could be charged for said offence u/s. 500 IPC in view of statutory presumption u/s. 7 of the Act - Therefore, HC rejected contentions raised by appellants and passed order against appellants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether HC's impugned order was justifiable - Held, in State of Bihar vs. Rajendra Agrawalla 1996 Indlaw SC 4034, Santosh De & Anr. vs. Archna Gupta & Ors. 1994 Indlaw SC 1384, respondent/complainant in each case had alleged that appellants who were either Managing Editor, Chief Editor or Resident Editor had knowledge and were responsible for publishing defamatory matter in their respective newspaper publications - Moreover, in none of cases, appellanthad come forward and pleaded guilty to effect that he was person responsible for selecting alleged defamatory matter published - Further, it was matter of evidence in each case - If complaint was allowed to proceed only against appellant whose name was printed in newspaper against whom there was statutory presumption u/s. 7 of the Act and in case such 'Editor' succeeds in proving that he was not 'Editor' having control over selection of alleged libelous matter published in newspaper, respondent/complainant would be left without any remedy to redress his grievance against real culprit - Court opined that u/s.319 CrPC had powers, but such powers were circumscribed by limitations - Thus, Court did not find any reasons to quash proceedings initiated against appellants and appeals were devoid of merits - Hence, proceedings initiated against appellants be continued in accordance with law - Appeals dismissed.
