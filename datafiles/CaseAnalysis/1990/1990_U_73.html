A.K. Bhatnagar and Others v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

09 November 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, K. Ramaswamy, M.M. Punchhi

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 961; (1991) 1 SCC 544; (1991) SCC (L&S) 601; 1991 (16) ATC 501; 1991 (1) CLR 70; 1991 (62) FLR 231; JT 1990 (4) SC 610; 1991 (1) LLN 1; 1990(2) SCALE 949; 1991 (1) SLR 191; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 638; 1991 (1) UJ 50

Case Digest

Subject: Indirect Tax; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Service - Central Information Service Rules, 1959, rr. 4 and 6 - Inter se seniority - Determination - Whether regularised employees to be placed below regular recruits up to 1970 on basis of year's examination? - Seniority is an incidence of service and where service rules prescribe method of its computation, it is squarely governed by such rules - In absence of a provision, ordinarily length of service is taken into account - Held, Union Govt. was right in regularising services under notification to direct that regularised officers would be placed below regular recruits through 1970 examination - Since rules are clear and Govt. action was within purview of rules, judicial interference was not called for - Further held, rules framed in exercise of powers conferred under proviso to art. 309 of Constitution are solemn rules having binding effect - Acting in a manner contrary to rules does created problem and dislocation - Appeal disposed of.
