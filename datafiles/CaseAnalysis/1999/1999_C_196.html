United Commercial Bank, Calcutta v Commissioner of Income Tax, West Bengal-III, Calcutta

Supreme Court of India

29 September 1999

Case Analysis

Bench

M.B. Shah, D.P. Wadhwa

Where Reported

1999 Indlaw SC 757; (1999) 8 SCC 338; AIR 2000 SC 94; 2000 (1) CalLT(SC) 57; 1999 (4) CompLJ 393; 1999 (156) CTR 380; [1999] 240 ITR 355; JT 1999 (7) SC 544; 1999 (10) SLT 104; [1999] Supp3 S.C.R. 254; [1999] 106 TAXMAN 601

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Banking & Finance - Corporate - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 145(1), 256(1), 263 - Banking Regulation Act, 1949, ss. 29 and 53 - Whether the national loss in the investment trading (India) by working out a difference between the book value of shares shown in the final account and their market price as on the last due date of the accounts, is admissible to be deducted from the book profits of the assessee Bank and whether the case of State Bank of Travancore v. CIT [1986 Indlaw SC 796] is applicable to the facts of the present case? - The Central Govt., in the exercise of the powers conferred by s. 53 of the Banking Regulation Act, 1949 and on the recommendation of RBI, permitted the assessee not to disclose the market value of its investment in the balance-sheet required to be maintained as per the statutory form - The assessee was maintaining its accounts on mercantile system, he was entitled to show his real income by taking into account market value of such investments in arriving at real taxable income, hence, on that basis, for valuing the closing stock, it is open to the assessee to value it at the cost or market value, whichever is lower, in the balance-sheet, if the securities and shares are valued at cost but from that no firm conclusion can be drawn, a taxpayer is free to employ for the purpose of his trade, his own method of keeping accounts, and for that purpose, to value stock-in-trade either at cost or market price - U/s. 145, IT Act, 1949 in a case where accounts are correct and complete but the method employed is such that in the opinion of the ITO, the income cannot be properly deduced therefrom, the computation shall be made in such manner and on such basis as the Income Tax Officer may determine - The case of State Bank of Travancore does not lay down any rule that whatever is not mentioned in the prescribed statutory balance-sheet is not to be taken into account for deciding real taxable income - Held, the questions referred by the Tribunal are answered in favour of the assessee and against the Revenue - Appeal allowed.
