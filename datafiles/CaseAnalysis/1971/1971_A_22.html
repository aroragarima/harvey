Alloy Steel Project v Workmen

Supreme Court of India

02 February 1971

Case Analysis

Bench

Vishishtha Bhargava, C.A. Vaidyialingam, J.M. Shelat

Where Reported

1971 Indlaw SC 128; (1971) 1 SCC 536; 1971 (22) FLR 181; 1971 (1) LLJ 217; [1971] 3 S.C.R. 629

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Separate establishment, Payment of bonus

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Payment of Bonus Act, 1965, ss. 16(1) and 16(2) - Separate establishment - Payment of bonus - Entitled to - Appellant project was an undertaking owned, controlled and managed by Govt. Company M - Appellant project was started in year 1961 and it went into production in year 1964-65 - However, no profit was earned up to year '1967-68 - Workmen claimed bonus at minimum rate prescribed under the Act - Tribunal held that appellant project could not be treated as a separate establishment under the Act, a Company was itself an establishment, so that all units of a Company like M would constitute one establishment - Since project had not earning any profits Tribunal directed payment of bonus at minimum rate of 4 per cent of wages prescribed by the Act - Aggrieved, appellant filed instant appeal - Whether Tribunal was justified in passing the impugned order - Held, if appellant project was treated as a new undertaking set up by existing establishments of M, exemption u/s. 16(1) of the Act should be available to it - However, provision to s. 16 (2) of the Act also did not stand in way of instant claim, because there was no evidence at all that in any year, after appellant project was set up bonus was paid to employees of all units on basis of consolidated profits of all such units - Only exception had in case, of workmen of Head Office where no separate profit and loss was worked out and bonus was paid on basis of consolidated Profits of all units belonging to M - Calculation of bonus on basis of consolidated accounts was justified, but that did not affect principle to be applied to separate units for which separate accounts, separate balance-sheets and separate profit and loss statements were maintained - Provision to s. 16(2) of the Act only comes in way it bonus was paid in any year to employees of all units on basis of consolidated accounts - Appellant Project should treated as a separate establishment newly set up in year 1961 - It went into production in 1964-65 and did not, earn any profits at all till 1967-68 - Therefore, no bonus was payable, to, workmen of this undertaking for year 1965-66 in view of provisions of s. 16(1) of the Act - Order of Tribunal was set aside - Appeal allowed.
