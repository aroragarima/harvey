Malayalam Plantations Ltd. v State Of Kerala & Anr.

Supreme Court of India

09 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

P. Sathasivam, Balbir Singh Chauhan

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 957; (2010) 13 SCC 487; 2011 (1) CTC 122; JT 2010 (12) SC 128; 2010 (4) KLT 647; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 635; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 609; 2010(11) SCALE 614

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 41, r. 27 - Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971 - Additional evidence - The Kerala Private Forests (Vesting and Assignment) Act, 1971 came into force with 10.05.1971 under which an area of 5131 hectares of land which was in the possession of the appellant was deemed to be vested under the said Act - Appellant filed applications before Forest Tribunal challenging the vesting of 2588 hectares out of the 5131 hectares which were either planted with eucalyptus by the appellant prior to the vesting or were utilized for the purpose of fuel requirement for its factory and for the use of its employees - Forest Tribunal exempted and excluded 1400 hectares of land from the vesting provisions of the Act as areas required for firewood purposes - Appellant and State both filed appeal against said order with application for reception of additional evidence - HC dismissed said application and exempted the 730.58 hectares of eucalyptus lands from vesting but held that no forest areas could be exempted for the purpose of firewood - Whether order of the HC could be upheld? - Held, if any petition is filed u/o. 41 r. 27 in an appeal, it is incumbent on the part of the appellate Court to consider at the time of hearing the appeal on merits so as to find out whether the documents or evidence sought to be adduced have any relevance/bearing in the issues involved - In the present case, though the State Government has filed separate application u/O. 41 r. 27, the HC failed to consider the same - Impugned judgement of HC set aside - Matter remanded to the HC for fresh disposal - Appeals allowed.
