Messrs Alopi Parshad and Sons, Limited v Union of India

Supreme Court of India

20 January 1960

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1960 Indlaw SC 428; AIR 1960 SC 588; 1960 (2) MLJ 46; 1960 (2) MLJ(SC) 46; 1960 SCJ 763; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 793

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution; Criminal; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Indian Contract Act, Schedules, misa

Summary: Contract & Commercial - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 56 - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Whether view of High Court was right in deciding that there was apparent errors on face of award and same was liable to be set aside? - Compensation quantum merit is awarded for work done or services rendered, when price thereof is not fixed by a contract - For work done or services rendered pursuant to terms of a contract, compensation quantum meruit cannot be awarded where contract provides for consideration payable in that behalf - Held, there was no 'legal basis for awarding any compensation' to agents for any loss which they might have sustained - Further held, award of arbitrators was liable to be set aside because of an error apparent on face of award - Appeal dismissed.
