Shailaja Shivajirao Patil v President Hon. Khasdar Ugs Sanstha and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 January 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1194; (2002) 10 SCC 394; (2003) SCC (L&S) 763; 2002 (1) AWC 681; 2002 (1) CLR 949; 2002 (92) FLR 1041; JT 2002 (1) SC 431; 2002 (2) LLJ 813; 2002 (2) LLN 880; 2002 (2) SLR 370; 2002 (2) Supreme 292; 2002 (1) UPLBEC 652

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Termination - Probation period - Whether order of termination of probationer stigmatic? - Order of appointment itself unequivocally indicated tenure of appointment, and that appointment could be terminated at any time without notice - Held, no - Order of termination passed in terms of contract of appointment and order of termination does not cast any stigma, and as such cannot be held to be penal in nature - Employer is entitled to satisfy himself as to competence of probationer to be confirmed in service and for this purpose satisfy itself fairly to truth of allegations made about concerned employee - Appeal dismissed.
