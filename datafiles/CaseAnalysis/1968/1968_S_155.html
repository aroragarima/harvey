State of Mysore and Another v Syed Mahmood and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 March 1968

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, G.K. Mitter, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1968 Indlaw SC 227; AIR 1968 SC 1113; 1968 LabIC 1291; 1970 (1) LLJ 370; 1968 (2) SCJ 713; [1968] 3 S.C.R. 363; 1968 SLR 738

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Seniority list - Promotion - Maintainability - On re-organisation of States services of respondents were allotted to State of Mysore and they were employed there as junior statistical assistants - Head of dept. of Statistics under directions of State Govt. prepared tentative seniority list of non gazetted staff as holding equivalent posts of junior statistical assistants in State - Before revising this tentative seniority list State Govt. directed that all statistical assistants and statistical inspectors of State and head compiler should be treated and promoted as senior statistical assistants - As result of this direction officers ranking below respondents in seniority list were promoted to higher posts - In making their promotions, State Govt. did not consider fitness of respondents for promotion at all - At much later date, they were promoted as senior statistical assistants - State Govt. published revised seniority list placing inspectors and head compilers in category of senior statistical assistants - Respondents filed separate petitions in HC asking for quashing seniority list published and directing State Govt. to consider their case for promotion as senior statistical assistants with retrospective effect - As objections to seniority list were still under consideration by State Govt. HC refused to quash this seniority list but it directed State Govt. to promote respondents as from respective dates on which respondents junior to them were promoted as senior statistical assistants - Hence, instant appeal - Whether order passed by HC was maintainable - Held, where promotion was based on seniority cum-merit officer could not claim promotion as matter of right by virtue of seniority alone - If he was found unfit to discharge duties of higher post, he may be passed over and officer junior to him may be promoted - Court opined that State Govt. should be directed to consider fitness of respondents for promotion in 1959 - If on such examination State Govt. arbitrarily refused to promote them, different considerations would arise - State Govt. would upon such consideration be under duty to promote them if they were then fit to discharge duties of higher post and if it fails to perform its duty, Court may direct it to promote them as from 1959 - Appeal allowed.
