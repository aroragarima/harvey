Amrit Banaspati Company Limited And Another v State of Punjab and Another

Supreme Court of India

05 March 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Sahai, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 445; (1992) 2 SCC 411; AIR 1992 SC 1075; AIR 1992 SCW 953; 1997 (68) ECR 551; 1992 (59) E.L.T. 13; JT 1992 (2) SC 217; 1992(1) SCALE 540; [1992] 2 S.C.R. 13; 1992 (85) STC 493; 1992 (1) UJ 599

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1978

Summary: Constitution - Contract & Commercial - Sales Tax - Constitution of India, 1950 - Indian Contract Act, 1872 - Punjab General Sales Tax Act, 1978 - Industry - Concession by Govt. - Promissory estoppel - (A) Whether finding of the Division Bench, was not justified in holding that the Govt. officials had extended promise, unauthorizedly and beyond scope of their authority? - Held, if an industry is exempt from tax the ultimate beneficiary is the consumer - The industry is allowed to overcome its teething period by selling its products at comparatively cheaper rate as compared to others, therefore, both the manufacture, and consumer gain, one by concession of non-levy and other by non-payment and such provisions in an Act or Notification or orders issued by Govt. are neither illegal not against public policy - (B)Whether refund of tax is made in consequence of excess payment was legally correct? - Held, No provision or agreement to refund tax due or realized in accordance with law cannot be comprehended and no law can be made to refund tax to a manufacturer realised under a statute,hence, invalid and ultra vires - Appeal dismissed.
