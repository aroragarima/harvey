P. K. Pradhan v State of Sikkim Represented By The Central Bureau of Investigation

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

B.N. Agrawal, G.B. Pattanaik, S.N. Phukan

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20078; (2001) 6 SCC 704; (2001) SCC (Cr) 1234; AIR 2001 SC 2547; AIR 2001 SCW 2648; 2001 (3) CalLT(SC) 68; 2001 (3) CCR 95; 2001 (3) Crimes 323; 2002 (1) CRJ 715; 2001 CRLJ 3505; JT 2001 (5) SC 610; 2001 (3) RCR(Criminal) 835; 2001(4) SCALE 508; 2001 (4) SCJ 362; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1119; 2001 (5) SLT 347; 2001 (5) Supreme 289; 2001 (2) UC 30

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Municipalities & Local Governments; Socio-Economic

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1974, Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 197 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 120B - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, ss. 5(2), 5(1)(d) and 6 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(2), 13(1)(d) and 19 - Whether sanction u/s. 197 is required for prosecution of appellant for offences punishable under IPC, 1860 and Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947? - For claiming protection u/s. 197, it has to be shown by accused that there is reasonable connection between act complained of and discharge of official duty that same cannot be separated from discharge of official duty - Official act can be performed in discharge of official duty as well as in dereliction of it - No sanction would be required if there was no reasonable connection between them - In order to come to conclusion whether claim of accused that act that he did was in course of performance of his duty was a reasonable one and neither pretended nor fanciful, can be examined during course of trial by giving opportunity to defence to establish it - Held, facts in present case are required to be established which can be done at trial - Therefore, it is not possible to grant any relief to appellant at this stage - Appeal dismissed.
