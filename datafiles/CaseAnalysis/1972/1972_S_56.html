State of Haryana v Shamsher Jang Bahadur

Supreme Court of India

19 April 1972

Case Analysis

Bench

K.S. Hegde, G.K. Mitter, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1972 Indlaw SC 37; (1972) 2 SCC 188; AIR 1972 SC 1546; 1972 LabIC 824; 1972 (2) LLJ 186; [1973] 1 S.C.R. 249; 1972 SLR 441; 1972 UJ 895

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 162 and 309 - States Reorganisation Act, 1956, s. 115(7) - Service Rules - Rules framed u/art. 309, prescribing conditions of service - Extent of power of Govt. to issue administrative instructions relating to rules - Held,Govt. is not competent to alter service conditions under Rules by means of administrative instructions - Further if approval of Central Govt. is not obtained for issuing those instructions relating to conditions of service, instructions would be invalid as violative of s. 115 (7) of 1956 Act - Appeal dismissed.
