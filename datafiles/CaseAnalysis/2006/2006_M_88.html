Regional Manager, Sbi. v Mahatma Mishra

Supreme Court of India

01 November 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 736; (2006) 13 SCC 727; 2006 (6) AWC 5413; JT 2006 (10) SC 179; 2006(11) SCALE 258; 2006 (6) SLR 648; [2006] Supp8 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act

Summary: [A.] Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 11-A, 25-F, 25-H - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 6-N - Employment Exchanges (Compulsory Notification of Vacancies) Act, 1959 - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 12, 14, 142, 16 - Retrenchment - Held, as was rightly observed by HC, respondent was not entitled to a permanent status - If he was not entitled to conferment of any permanent status having worked only for 88 days and that too in year 1982, he was entitled to be reinstated in service and that too with full backwages - No finding of fact has been arrived at that termination of service of respondent was illegal - Question of directing an award reinstating him in service did not and could not arise - Appeal allowed.
