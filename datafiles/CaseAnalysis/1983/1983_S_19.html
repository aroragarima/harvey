Transport Commissioner, Andhra Pradesh v Sardar Ali, Bus Owner

Supreme Court of India

26 August 1983

Case Analysis

Bench

O. Chinnappa Reddy, D.A. Desai

Where Reported

1983 Indlaw SC 130; (1983) 4 SCC 245; (1983) SCC (Cr) 827; 1984 (2) ACC 153; AIR 1983 SC 1225; 1983 CRLJ 1506; 1983 CrLR(SC) 643; 1983(2) SCALE 206; [1983] 3 S.C.R. 729; 1983 UJ 835

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation; Constitution; Criminal

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 19(1)(g) - Motor Vehicles Act, 1939, s. 129-A - Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, ss. 4(2) and 452 - Whether s. 129-A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 is ultra-vires Constitution of India, 1950 on ground that it infringes art. 19(1)(g)? - Held, no - High Court appeared to think that s. 129-A was an unreasonable restriction on fundamental right guaranteed by art. 19(1)(g) because Act did not provide for confiscation of vehicle; there was no provision in Act indicating what should be done after seizing and detaining vehicle; seizure by police officer or authorised person was based on his subjective satisfaction etc. - Whenever an offence under a law other than IPC, 1860 is committed and that law does not itself regulate procedure to be followed, there is no option but to look to provisions of CrPC, 1973 - HC has totally ignored provisions of CrPC, 1973 - Context of s. 129-A in scheme emerges from interlacing of provisions of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 and CrPC, 1973 - There is no infringement of fundamental right guaranteed by art. 19(1)(g) of Constitution and s. 129-A of Motor Vehicles Act, 1939 is intra-vires Constitution - Appeal allowed.
