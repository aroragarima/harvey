Â Sri. C. I. Emden v State of Uttar Pradesh

Supreme Court of India

15 December 1959

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, K.C. Das Gupta, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1959 Indlaw SC 204; AIR 1960 SC 548; 1960 CRLJ 729; 1960 SCJ 368; [1960] 2 S.C.R. 592

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Prohibition Act

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 161 - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1947, s. 5(2) - Bribery - Conviction - Challenged - Validity - Appellant/accused was caught red handed while accepting bribe from complainant and charges were framed against him u/s.161 of 1860 Act and s. 5(2) of the 1947 Act - Appellant denied charge of bribe and pleaded that he only borrowed money - Trial judge convicted appellant with one year RI and to fine of Rs. 500 u/s. 161 of IPC and 2 years RI u/s.5 of the 1947 Act holding that presumption u/s. 4 of the 1947 Act was not raised and rebutted by accused - Appellant filed appeal and HC confirmed conviction u/s. 161 IPC but reduced sentence to one year u/s. 5(2) of the 1947 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appeal was maintainable - Held, the 1947 Act was enacted to prevent bribery and corruption and s. 4(1) of the 1947 Act provides that it should be presumed that accused accepted bribe unless contrary was proved by accused - Legislature made this provision as it was not easy to establish charge of bribery beyond reasonable doubt and it was not violative of art. 14 of Constitution - HC was justified in raising presumption as admitted by appellant/accused that he received amount from complainant and that amount was other than legal remuneration - HC considered several aspects and concluded that explanation of accused was improbable and unreasonable - Order of HC was upheld - Appeal dismissed.
