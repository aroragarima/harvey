Rangarao v Kamlakant

Supreme Court of India

17 February 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, S. Mohan

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1343; (1995) Supp1 SCC 271; 1996 (2) RCJ 433; 1995 (2) RCR(Rent) 703

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - The suit for possession filed by the landlord-appellant ended in a compromise decree - Thereafter, what had happened is a notification exempting certain categories of buildings from the purview of Central Provinces and Berar Letting of Houses and Rent Control Order, 1949 issued u/cl. 30 of the said order came to be struck down on the ground that it was violative of art. 14 of the Constitution - When the decree was sought to be executed, the tenant albeit the compromise raised an objection that the decree became unexecutable since the Civil Court had lost jurisdiction to pass an order of eviction in view of the decision - The High Court sustained the objection of the judgment-debtor - Whether the HC thoroughly misunderstood the scope of the judgment declaring the notification u/cl. 30 in relation to exemption of certain categories of buildings as bad? - Held, it is undeniable that on the date when the compromise memo fruitioned into a decree on 03-01-1985, the Civil Court had every jurisdiction to pass such a decree - The said notification issued u/cl. 30 came to be struck down on 19-06-1985 - No judgment of any Court can have any retrospective operation because that is the plenary power of Parliament - If that be so, the HC had clearly gone wrong in holding that the decree on the date of execution is a nullity - Appeal allowed.
