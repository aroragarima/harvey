Â Sales Tax Officer, Banaras and Others v Kanhaiya Lal Mukundlal Saraf

Supreme Court of India

23 September 1958

Case Analysis

Bench

Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, K. Subba Rao, Kailas Nath Wanchoo

Where Reported

1958 Indlaw SC 178; AIR 1959 SC 135; 1959 (1) MLJ(SC) 35; [1959] 1 S.C.R. 1350; 1958 (9) STC 747

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Family & Personal; Sales Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Indian Partnership Act, 1932 - Indian Contract Act, 1872, s. 72 - Constitution of India, 1950 art. 133(1)(b) - Whether High Court erred in concluding that s. 72 of Indian Contract Act applied to present case and State Govt. must refund money unlawfully received by it from respondent on account of Sales Tax? - On a true interpretation of s. 72 of Indian Contract Act only two circumstances there indicated as entitling party to recover money back are that monies must have been paid by mistake or under coercion - Held, s. 72 of Contract Act is applicable - Appeal dismissed.
