Dhananjoy Chatterjee Alias Dhana v State of W. B

Supreme Court of India

11 January 1994

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, N.P. Singh

Where Reported

1994 Indlaw SC 1743; (1994) 2 SCC 220; (1994) SCC (Cr) 358; AIR 1995 SCW 510; 1994 (2) AWC 1010; 1994 (1) CalLT(SC) 28; 1994 (1) CCR 89; JT 1994 (1) SC 33; 1994(1) SCALE 48; [1994] 1 S.C.R. 37; 1994 (2) UJ 106

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Sacred

Summary: Criminal - Murder - Rape - Circumstantial Evidence - IPC, 1860,ss. 302 and 376 - Offence of rape and murder of deceased was conclusively established by medical evidence - Whether appellant was assailant who had raped and murdered deceased? - Held, yes - Circumstantial evidence point to guilt of appellant beyond reasonable doubt - Appellant had been teasing deceased for past few days and had every motive to commit crime - Appellant had gone to house of deceased when she was alone and committed offence - Appellant's articles found from house of deceased - Visit of appellant to house of deceased also proved - Appellant committed heinous crime on a young girl who was defenseless and unprotected - Keeping in view medical evidence and state in which body of deceased was found, it is obvious that a most heinous type of barbaric rape and murder was committed on a helpless and defenceless school-going girl of 18 years - Death sentence awarded - Appeal dismissed.
