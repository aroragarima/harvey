State of Kerala v K. T. Shaduli Grocery Dealer Etc.

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, S. Murtaza Fazal Ali, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 413; (1977) 2 SCC 777; AIR 1977 SC 1627; 1977 (6) CTR 260; [1977] 3 S.C.R. 233; 1977 (39) STC 478; 1977 UJ 318

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Kerala General Sales Tax Act, 1963, s. 17(3) - Kerala General Sales Tax Rules, 1963, r. 15 - Constitution, art. 226 - Ondition precedent for applicability of s. 17(3) - Best judgment assessment - STO refused to summon wholesale dealers for cross-examination - Assessment orders, if vitiated - in the light of principles of natural justice - Held, act of the STO clearly constituted infraction of the right conferred on the assessee by the second part of the proviso and that vitiated the orders of assessment made against the assessee - Appeal Dismissed.
