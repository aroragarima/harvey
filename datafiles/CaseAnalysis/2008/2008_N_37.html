Union of India and Others v Naman Singh Sekhawat

Supreme Court of India

14 March 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 524; (2008) 4 SCC 1; AIR 2008 SC (Supp) 1892; 2008(6) ALL MR 404; 2008 (225) E.L.T. 161; 2008 (3) ESC 391; 2008 (118) FLR 1121; JT 2008 (4) SC 150; 2008 (2) LLN 783; 2008(4) SCALE 360; [2008] 5 S.C.R. 137; 2008 (2) UPLBEC 1890

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: CCS (CC&A) Rules, 1965

Summary: Service - Criminal proceeding initiated against respondent for the alleged commission of offences under ss. 409, 120-B of the Indian Penal Code r/w s. 13(2) of Foreigners Act and s. 27 of the Arms Act as also under s. 110 of the Customs Act, 1962 dropped - Disciplinary Authority passed an order of dismissal - Central Administrative Tribunal directed reinstatement of respondent - Appeal against - Held, criminal court has given a positive finding that the prosecution has not been able to prove that the accused had misappropriated the goods - His visit to the border for discharging his duties did not tantamount to misuse of the post or the authority - No evidence has been presented that he did not have the authority to go to the border side on official duties and even the department had not forbidden him from going to that place - It was held that as misappropriation of the property has not been proved, the question of any criminal conspiracy did not arise - Appeal dismissed.
