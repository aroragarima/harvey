C. Padma and Others v Dy. Secretary To The Government of T.N. and Others

Supreme Court of India

22 November 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 1954; (1997) 2 SCC 627; 1997 (1) AD(SC) 246; 1997 (1) CLT 241; JT 1996 (Supp) SC 263; 1996(9) SCALE 278; [1996] Supp9 S.C.R. 158; 1997 (1) Supreme 208

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Land Acquisition Act, 1894, ss. 4, 40 and 44-B - Whether appellant right in contending that since when original purpose for which land was acquired had ceased to be in operation, appellants entitled to restitution of possession taken from them? - Acquisition proceeding had become final, compensation was paid and thereafter lands stood vested in state - Question stood finally settled 32 years ago - Public purpose for which acquisition was made was substituted for another public purpose - Writ petition cannot be entertained after three decades on ground that either original purpose was not public purpose or land cannot be used for any other purpose - Appeal dismissed.
