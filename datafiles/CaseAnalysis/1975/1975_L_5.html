Lala Balmukund (Dead) Through L.Rs v Lajwanti and Others

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1975

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Sarkaria, Y.V. Chandrachud, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1975 Indlaw SC 131; (1975) 1 SCC 725; AIR 1975 SC 1089; 1975 (1) ALR 295; [1975] Supp S.C.R. 44; 1975 UJ 357

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Delay In Filing Appeal, Computation of period

Summary: Civil Procedure - Practice & Procedure - Indian Limitation Act, 1908, ss. 5, 12(2) - Delay in filing appeal - Computation of period - Legality - Respondents (plaintiff) filed a suit for dissolution of partnership, rendition of accounts and recovery against appellants (defendants) - Trial Court decreed suit and application for a copy of final decree was filed and was delivered to counsel for appellant on 1-2-1957 - Information about supply of copy was received by appellant on 3-2-1957 - Appellant filed an appeal on 12-2-1957 before Lower Appellate Court along with an application u/s. 5 of the Limitation Act, for condonation of delay in respect of period from 6-2- 1957 to 12-2-1957 - By impugned judgment Lower Appellate Court dismissed appeal as time barred - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that entire period between the date of judgment and signing of decree, in circumstances of case, was the 'time requisite for obtaining a copy of decree should have been excluded, as such u/s. 12(2) of 1908 Act - Whether impugned judgment passed by HC in interpreting s. 12(2) of 1908 Act was justified - Held, action on the part of appellant in applying for a copy of decree was not always decisive factor in considering whether any time should be so excluded - In a case where various steps might have to be taken by parties before a decree could be ready and signed, Court would have to consider whether any of the time taken for preparation of decree could be attributed to the fault or negligence of appellant - If any of the time could be so attributed, then that time could not be excluded u/s. 12(12) of 1908 - Limitation Act deprived or restricted the right of an aggrieved person to have recourse to legal remedy, and where its language was ambiguous, that construction should be preferred which preserves such remedy to one which bars or defeats it - Court ought to avoid an interpretation upon a statute of Limitation by implication or inference as may have a penalising effect unless it was driven to do so by the irresistible force of language employed by legislature - Expression 'time requisite' in the phrase in question, means all the time counted from the date of pronouncement of judgment (the same being under O. 20 r. 7 of CPC, the date of decree) which would be properly required for getting a copy of decree, including the time which should ex-necessitates elapse in the circumstances of particular case, before a decree was drawn up and signed - If any period of delay in preparing the decree was attributable to default or negligence of appellant, the latter should not be entitled to exclusion of such period u/s. 12(2) of 1908 Act - Under the judgment or any rules of Court, appellant was not required to take any step towards preparation of decree - No period of delay in drawing up the decree was attributable to the fault of appellant - Delay was mainly due to the delayed deposit of the court-fee by respondent (plaintiffs) and partly due to laxity of the office of Court - Appellant was entitled to exclusion of entire time between date of pronouncement of judgment and date of signing of decree, as the 'time requisite for obtaining a copy of decree' - After such exclusion-avoiding double counting-his appeal filed in Lower Appellate Court on 12-2-1957, was fully within time - Impugned judgment of HC was set aside - Appeal allowed.
