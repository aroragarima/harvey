Maya Devi and another v State of Haryana

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

R.K. Agrawal, Vikramajit Sen

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 847; AIR 2016 SC 125; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 416; 2016 CRLJ 629; 2016 (1) JCC 737; JT 2015 (12) SC 24; 2016 (1) MLJ(Crl) 114; 2016 (1) RCR(Criminal) 407; 2015(13) SCALE 336; [2015] 11 S.C.R. 903

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Onus, Environment, Continuous, Presume

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 304B, 306, 34, 498A - Dowry Death - Conviction Challenged - Appellant no.2/husband was married to victim/wife - However, wife was subjected to cruelty for demand for dowry by appellant no.1/mother in law as well as husband, which resulted in dowry death of victim - Trial Court convicted appellant nos. 1-2 u/s. 498A and 304B of IPC and sentenced to suffer RI for life and RI for two years with fine of Rs. 500 each respectively -DB of HC in appeal modified sentence of life imprisonment to that of RI for years for offence u/s. 304 B of IPC, maintained sentence in respect of other offence and also dismissed revision filed by complainant - Hence, instant appeal.

Held, in order to convict an accused for the offence punishable u/s. 304-B of IPC, the following essentials must be satisfied:(i) the death of a woman must have been caused by burns or bodily injury or otherwise than under normal circumstances; (ii) such death must have occurred within seven years of her marriage;(iii) soon before her death, the woman must have been subjected to cruelty or harassment by her husband or any relatives of her husband;(iv) such cruelty or harassment must be for, or in connection with, demand for dowry. Further, the provisions of s. 113B of the Indian Evidence Act, 1872, provided for presumption that the accused is responsible for dowry death. There must be existence of a proximate and live link between the effect of cruelty based on dowry demand and the death concerned. If the alleged incident of cruelty is remote in time and has become stale enough not to disturb the mental equilibrium of the woman concerned, it would be of no consequence. In instant case, wife died an unnatural death by committing suicide as she was subjected to cruelty/harassment by her husband and in-laws in connection with the demand for dowry. The legal requirement for an offence falling under above mentioned provisions is clearly established. Hence, impugned conviction and sentence imposed does not warrant any interference. Appeal dismissed.
