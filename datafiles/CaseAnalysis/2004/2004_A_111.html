Anil Mahajan v Bhor Industries Limited and another

Supreme Court of India

06 October 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

Y.K. Sabharwal, D.M. Dharmadhikari, Tarun Chatterjee

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 1771; (2005) 10 SCC 228; 2006 (4) RCR(Criminal) 834

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 415, 418, 420 - Delivery of goods - Failure to make payment - Issuance of process - Presence/absence of cheating - Complainant delivered reels of steel grip tapes to accused and accused made only part-payment of those goods - It was alleged by complainant that accused did not make further payment despite repeated and MOU was signed with mala tide and criminal intention of grabbing money and goods from complainant's - Hence, complainant filed complain u/ss. 415, 418 and 420 of IPC - Magistrate issued process against accused, however, order of Magistrate was challenged where Additional Sessions Judge set aside order of Magistrate and order of Additional Sessions Judge had been set aside by HC - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether mere use of the expression 'cheating' in complaint was of consequence in appellant's case and Additional Sessions Judge had rightly set aside order of Magistrate - Held, except mention of words 'deceive' and 'cheat' in complaint filed before Magistrate and 'cheating' in complaint filed before police, there was no averment about deceit, cheating or fraudulent intention of accused at time of entering into MOU wherefrom it could be inferred that accused had intention to deceive complainant to pay - According to complainant, accused made part payment out of total amount - Additional reason for not going into these aspects was that civil suit was pending inter se parties for amounts in question - After examining of complaint and it was clear from its substance that present was simple case of civil disputes between parties - Requisite averments so as to make out case of cheating were absolutely absent - Hence, Additional Sessions Judge had rightly set aside order of Magistrate issuing process to appellant - Appeal dismissed.
