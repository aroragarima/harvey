Â Vallabhdas Liladhar and Others v Assistant Collector of Customs

Supreme Court of India

27 January 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Kailas Nath Wanchoo, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, A.K. Sarkar, K.C. Das Gupta, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, S.K. Das

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 99; AIR 1965 SC 481; 1965 CRLJ 490; 1984 ECR 461; 1983 (13) E.L.T. 1408; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 854

Case Digest

Subject: Customs

Keywords: Sea Customs Act 1878

Summary: Customs - Foreign Exchange Regulation Act, 1947 - Indian Evidence Act, 1872, ss. 24 and 25 - Sea Customs Act, 1878, ss. 167(81), 178-A and 186 - (A) Whether statement made to customs authorities were inadmissible in evidence? - As statements bore signature of appellants which are admitted, they must be held to be proved by this admission? - (B) Whether statement made before Collector of Customs was admissible in evidence u/s. 24 and 25 of Indian Evidence Act? - Customs officers are not police officers and statements made to them were not inadmissible u/s. 25-(C) Whether there could be trial in criminal court after gold was confiscated and penalty imposed, in view of s. 186 of Sea Customs Act? - Held, intention of legislature by provision in s. 186 was clearly to allow a prosecution under any other law even though there might be award of confiscation, penalty or increased rate of duty under Act - No interference - Appeals dismissed.
