Standard Chartered Bank v State of Maharashtra and others

Supreme Court of India

06 April 2016

Case Analysis

Bench

Dipak Misra, Shiva Kirti Singh

Where Reported

2016 Indlaw SC 265; (2016) 6 SCC 62; AIR 2016 SC 1750; 2016 ALL MR (Cri) 2238; 2016 (4) Bom.C.R. 11; [2016] 198 Comp Cas 177; 2016 (2) CompLJ 257; 2016 CRLJ 2362; 2016 (3) JCC (NI) 113; JT 2016 (4) SC 197; 2016 (2) Law Herald (P&H) 1764; 2016 (5) MahLJ 44; 2016 (2) MLJ(Crl) 703; 2016 (4) MPLJ 36; 2016 (2) OLR 109; 2016 (2) RCR(Civil) 818; 2016 (2) RCR(Criminal) 778B; 2016(4) SCALE 1; [2016] 4 S.C.R. 288

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Vicarious Liability, Companies Act, 1956, Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, Managing Director, Single Judge, Summons, Secretary, Abuse, Quash, Cheque, Cognizance, Registered, Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, Complainant

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheques - Summons quashed against Directors - Liability of Directors - Respondent company availed loan from appellant bank for which cheques were issued, however, cheques were dishonoured on presentation - Further, appellant filed three complaints u/s. 138 of Act - Summons were issued by Magistrate against company, its directors, managing director, chairman, however, HC vide impugned quashed summon issued against respondents i.e. Whole Time Directors on ground that there are no allegations against respondents connecting them with affairs of company, however, summon against other accused persons is maintained - Whether impugned order quashing summons against Directors is justified and in accordance with law.

Held, Gunmala Sales Private Limited v (1) Anu Mehta and others; (2) Navkar Infra Projects Private Limited; (3) Navkar Build home Private Limited, 2014 Indlaw SC 722, relied on, where it was held that in order to quash criminal proceeding, Director should produce evidence to prove that he is not connected in issuance of cheques. SC held that in present case there are seven accused persons and considering totality of assertions made in complaint and also taking note of averments put forth relating to respondent Nos. 2 and 3 that they are whole-time Director and Executive Director and they were in charge of day to day affairs of Company, SC held that HC fell into grave error by coming to conclusion that there are no specific averments in complaint for issuance of summons against accused persons. Hence, impugned order passed by HC is set aside and Magistrate is directed to proceed with complaint cases in accordance with law. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - If person who commits offence u/s. 138 of Act is company, company and other person in charge of or responsible to company for conduct of business of company at time of commission of offence is deemed to be guilty of offence. Hence, it creates constructive liability on persons responsible for conduct of business of company.
