Hindustan Times Limited, New Delhi v Their Workmen

Supreme Court of India

14 December 1962

Case Analysis

Bench

K.C. Das Gupta, Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1962 Indlaw SC 436; AIR 1963 SC 1332; 1963 (6) FLR 313A; 1963 (1) LLJ 108A; [1964] 1 S.C.R. 234

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Fair Wage, Age Of Superannuation, Casual Leave, Cost Of Living

Summary: Labour & Industrial Law - Delhi Shops and Establishments Act, 1954 - Employees State Insurance Act, 1948, ss. 46(1)(a), 47, 48, 49 and 56 - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947 - (A) Whether wage scale fixed by tribunal is too heavy for Co. to bear? - Held, Co. has been prospering - It has its own aeroplanes and possesses immovable properties of considerable value - It has built up good reserves and inspite of that it has been making good profits and has financial stability - (B) Whether wage scale fixed by tribunal operates unfavorably on this Co.? - Held, it has also to be borne in mind that Times of India, Delhi and Statesman, Delhi, are much smaller units - These Companies are mere adjuncts to Times of India, Bombay and Statesman, Calcutta, respectively - Therefore, even if for some categories wage scale under award is higher than that in Times of India, Delhi and Statesman, Delhi, that would be no ground for modifying award in favour of Co. - (C) Whether increase given by award over previous wage scale falls far short of justice? - Held, wage scale of Co. has remained practically stationary for last 12 years and that it is indisputably well below fair wage and further fact that even smaller concerns in this region, like Times of India, Delhi and Statesman, Delhi, have been paying more to some categories of its workmen, wage scale as fixed by tribunal should be raised at least for some of categories - (D) Whether Co.'s appeal in connection with interim agreement is valid? - Held, not desirable that workmen should be encouraged to treat their undertakings as of no value - (E) Question of Leave Rules? - Held, Legislature did not intend to substitute any of these benefits for workmen's right to get leave on full pay on ground of sickness - Employees' State Insurance Act, 1948 does not provide for any leave to workmen on ground of sickness - (F) Whether tribunal's direction as regards sickness leave offend provisions of Delhi Shops and Establishments Act, 1954? - Held, the Co.'s financial resources are strong and stable and that not only has Co. been prospering in recent years but that its future prospects are also bright - Scheme of gratuity is unduly not favorable to workmen or that it places any undue strain on Co.'s financial resources - Appeal allowed partly.
