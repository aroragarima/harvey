Kikar Singh v State of Rajasthan

Supreme Court of India

12 May 1993

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, A.S. Anand

Where Reported

1993 Indlaw SC 1406; (1993) 4 SCC 238; (1993) SCC (Cr) 1156; AIR 1993 SC 2426; 1993 (2) CCR 164; 1993 (2) Crimes 487; 1993 CRLJ 3255; 1993 CrLR(SC) 406; JT 1993 (3) SC 508; 1993(2) SCALE 917; [1993] 3 S.C.R. 696; 1993 (2) UJ 137

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - IPC, 1860, s. 300 - Murder - Deceased was unarmed and accused was heavily armed - the fight was sudden and occured in a heat of passion, the accused kept on inflicting fatal blows on deceased fallen on ground - Whether exception 4 under s.300 of I.P.C attracted ? - Held, for application of exception (4) to s. 300 of IPC all the conditions enumerated therein must be satisfied - The act must be committed without premeditation in a sudden fight in the heat of passion (2) upon a sudden quarrel; (3) without the offender's having taken undue advantage (4) and the accused had not acted in a cruel or unusual manner - None of the above provisions attracted - The accused was rightly convicted under s. 302 I.P.C - Appeal dismissed.
