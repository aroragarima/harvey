J.G.Engineers Private Limited v Union Of India And Another.

Supreme Court of India

28 April 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.V. Raveendran, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 296; (2011) 5 SCC 758; AIR 2011 SC 2477; 2011 (5) AWC 4492; 2011 (2) CLT(SC) 220; 2011 (4) CTC 81; JT 2011 (5) SC 380; 2011 (5) MLJ(SC) 693; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 683; 2011(5) SCALE 46; [2011] 8 S.C.R. 486

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Contract & Commercial

Summary: (A) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 34 - Delay in completion - Claims relating to liquidated damages - Nature of - Respondent awarded construction contract to appellant contractor - Appellant failed to complete work within stipulated time - Respondent terminated contract - Disputes that arose between parties were referred to arbitration - Arbitrator awarded a sum with interest in favour of appellant - Respondent filed application for setting aside arbitral award u/s. 34 before District Court - District Court dismissed application - Respondent filed appeal before HC - HC, by impugned order, set aside several claims raised by appellant and directed to adjust those claims with due amount payable by respondent - Whether HC was justified in setting aside award in respect of claims relating to levy of liquidated damages on appellant for delay in completion of work on ground that they related to 'excepted matters' - Held, question whether appellant was responsible or respondents were responsible for delay in execution of work, was arbitrable - Arbitrator had examined said issue and had recorded a categorical finding that respondents were responsible for delay in execution of work and contractor was not responsible - Arbitrator also found that respondents were in breach and termination of contract was illegal - Respondents, therefore, were not entitled to levy liquidated damages nor entitled to claim from contractor extra cost (including any escalation in regard to such extra cost) in getting work completed through an alternative agency - Even though decision as to rate of liquidated damages and decision as to what was actual excess cost in getting work completed through an alternative agency, were excepted matters, they were not relevant for deciding claims 1, 3 and 11, as right to levy liquidated damages or claim excess costs would arise only if contractor was responsible for delay and was in breach - In view of finding of arbitrator that it was not the appellants but the respondents responsible for delay, question of respondents levying liquidated damages or claiming excess cost in getting work completed as damages, did not arise - Once it was held that contractor was not responsible for delay and delay occurred only on account of omissions and commissions on part of respondents, it followed that provisions which make decision of Superintending Engineer or Engineer-in-Charge final and conclusive, would be irrelevant - Arbitrator would have jurisdiction to try and decide all claims of contractor as also claims of respondents - Award of Arbitrator on items 1, 3 and 11 had to be upheld and conclusion of HC that award in respect of those claims had to be set aside as they related to excepted matters, could not be sustained - Appeal allowed.

(B) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Setting aside of sustainable claims - Legality - Whether HC was justified in setting aside award in regard to claim nos. 2, 4, 6, 7, 8 and 9 - Held, if an award dealt with and decided several claims separately and distinctly, even if court found that award in regard to some items was bad, court can segregate award on items which did not suffer from any infirmity and uphold award to that extent - As awards on items 2, 4, 6, 7, 8 and 9 were upheld by civil court and as HC in appeal did not find any infirmity in regard to award on those claims, judgment of HC setting aside award in regard to claims 2,4,6,7,8 and 9 of appellant therefore could not be sustained - Award in regard to claims 2, 4, 6, 7, 8 and 9, therefore, were upheld - Appeal allowed.

(C) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Escalation cost - Impermissible clause in contract - Effect - Whether HC was justified in holding that claim no. 5 for escalation was barred by cl. 10(cc) of contract - Held, claim no.(5) was for payment of escalation u/cl. 10(cc) of contract for work done beyond stipulated date till date of termination - Stipulated date for completion was 9-1-1995 - Respondents granted first extension up to 31-7-1995 without levy of liquidated damages - In fact respondent had paid escalation in prices u/cl. 10(cc) up to June 1995 - Contractor was however permitted to continue work without levy of any liquidated damages, until termination on 14-3-1996 - It was only on 30-9-1999 after contractor had submitted its statement of claim on 17-4-1997, respondents chose to levy liquidated damages for period 1-10-1995 to 14-3-1996 - In view of finding of Arbitrator that contractor was not responsible for delay, contractor was entitled to second extension from 1-8-1995 also without levy of penalty - In fact, having extended time till 31-7-1995 without any levy of liquidated damages, respondents could not have retrospectively levied liquidated damages on 30-9-1999 from 10-1-1995 - HC therefore committed an error in setting aside award in regard to claim No.5 on ground that it violated cl. 10(cc) of contract - Appeal allowed.

(D) Arbitration & ADR - Contract & Commercial - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996 - Rejection of counter-claims - Legality - Whether HC was justified in setting aside award rejecting counter-claims 1 to 4 - Held, once Arbitrator recorded finding on consideration of evidence/material, that contractor was not responsible for delay and that termination was wrongful and that respondents were liable for consequences arising out of wrongful termination of contract, question of respondents claims with regard to: (i) extra expenditure incurred for work done through another contractor; (ii) levy of liquidated damages; (iii) escalation cost; and (iv) cost of arbitration, did not arise - HC proceeded on erroneous assumption that when cls. (2) and (3) of agreement made decisions of Superintending Engineer/Engineer-in-Charge final as to quantum of liquidated damages and quantum of extra cost in getting balance work completed - Once it was held that issues relating to who committed breach and who was responsible for delay were arbitrable, findings of arbitrator that contractor was not responsible for delay and that termination of contract was illegal were not open to challenge - Rejection of counter claims of respondents, therefore, was unexceptionable and HC's finding that arbitrator ought not to have rejected them becomes unsustainable - Award of Arbitrator rejecting counter claims was, therefore, upheld and impugned HC judgement was set aside - Appeal allowed.
