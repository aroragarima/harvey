D.D.A. And Ors.. v Joginder S. Monga And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2003

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Ashok Bhan

Where Reported

2003 Indlaw SC 1519; (2004) 2 SCC 297; AIR 2004 SC 3291; 2003(10) SCALE 707; [2003] Supp6 S.C.R. 786; 2004 (2) Supreme 559

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Delhi Development Authority (Disposal of Developed Nazul Land) Rules, 1961

Summary: Civil Procedure - Land & Property - Delhi Development Act, 1957 - Delhi Development Authority (Disposal of Developed Nazul Land) Rules, 1981 - Lands in question being Nazul lands were governed by the provisions of the Act and the Rules - Pursuant to the provisions of the Act and the Rules, the appellant granted lease in favour of a Cooperative Society, which was formed by the Government servants - They in terms of the provisions of the Rules were required to execute deeds of sub-lease in favour of the lessee as also the President of India - A statutory sub-lease was executed in favour of member of cooperative society - In terms of proviso appended to sub Clause (b) of Clause 6 of the dead of sub lease, the lessor was entitled to recover a portion of the unearned increase in the value - Determination of the lessor in respect of the market value therefor was to be final and binding - By reason of Clause X(a), of the said deed the President delegated his power to the Chief Commissioner of Delhi - Purported to be in exercise of such delegated power, the Delhi Administration fixed the market price of the nazul lands situated in different localities for the purpose of recovery of increase in the cost of the land upon sale in terms whereof the price of the land was determined at Rs. 10500 per sq. Metre - The said circular letter was communicated to the concerned officers - Respondent No. 1 thereafter filed an application before the competent authority of the appellant for sale of the leasehold property indicating the cost of construction and price of the plot as Rs. 5,00,00,000 - Government of India, however, without enforcing any increase in the sale price of the land extended the validity of the land rates in force by a circular letter - Appellant despite the same proceeded on the basis that having regard to the fact that the purchaser had agreed to pay the consideration of Rs. 5,00,00,000 and further agreed to bear the cost of difference in unearned increase, the market value of the land would be Rs. 7,50,00,000 and on that basis demanded a sum of Rs. 3,62,44,420 as a condition of grant of permission - Respondents filed writ petitions before the HC questioning the said demand - During the pendency of the proceedings, however, a purported resolution was passed by the DDA to the effect that unearned increase should be worked out on the basis of sale consideration shown in the agreement of sale or income tax clearance certificate, as the case may be, if it was higher than the floor level rate of DDA - HC held that that the DDA, had no power to issue the demands - DDA was directed to issue fresh demand to the respondents and it was also directed that in case any amount had been paid on the basis of the impugned demand, the DDA shall be entitled to appropriate only that portion of the amount calculated in accordance with fixation of market rate of land as issued by the Delhi Administration and accepted by the DDA and should pay back the balance with interest at 18% p.a. from the date of payment by the respondents - Instant appeals - (A) Whether as the statutory sub lease refers to the market value of the residential plot, the circular letters could not override the same and, thus, were illegal? - Held, authority of the Lt. Governor was to be exercised by the Delhi Administration but such an authority being delegated one, the Union of India could not be said to have denuded of its power to issue statutory directions as and when necessary or to issue policy decision in terms of the Act or the Rules - The power to fix market value was that of the lessor; whereas the Chief Commissioner was delegated with the power of the lessor - When a market value was fixed in case of a locality by the lessor or his delegated authority, the same would be binding on them - Circular letters were valid - Determination of market value by reason of such circular letters, thus, became a part of the terms of the lease having regard to the finality clause attached thereto - (B) Rate of interest - Held, grant of interest pendente lite and for future was a discretionary remedy - The court of appeal could, therefore, exercise the same power while finally disposing the lis as that of the HC keeping in view the principle engrafted in s. 34 of the Code of Civil Procedure - The rate of interest may have to be fixed having regard to the principle of restitution - In the facts and circumstances of instant case, grant of 9% interest would meet the ends of justice - Appeal dismissed.
