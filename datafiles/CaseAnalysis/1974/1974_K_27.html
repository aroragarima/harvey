Khudiram Das v State of West Bengal and Others

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1974

Case Analysis

Bench

P.N. Bhagwati, P. Jaganmohan Reddy, P.K. Goswami, R.S. Sarkaria

Where Reported

1974 Indlaw SC 467; (1975) 2 SCC 81; (1975) SCC (Cr) 435; AIR 1975 SC 550; [1975] 2 S.C.R. 832

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Foreign Power

Summary: Criminal - Detention - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 19(a) to 19(d), 19(g), 32 and 22 - Maintenance of Internal Security Act, 1971, ss. 3, 4, 5, 6, 7, 8, 11, 12 and 19 - Habeas Corpus - Petitioner a habitual criminal and history sheeter detained by orders of Magistrate - Held, required grounds were validly communicated to petitioner - Only requirement of communication is in regard to the basic facts and materials which constitute grounds of detention and if there are other particulars, besides the grounds of detention which are communicated to the State Govt., they need not be disclosed to the petitioner - Non-disclosure of it to petitioner did not have effect of invalidating approval of State Govt. to order of detention - Further held, s. 3 of the Act in so far as it empowers the detaining authority to exercise the power of detention on basis of its subjective satisfaction, however, does not impose unreasonable restrictions on the fundamental rights of the petitioner petitioner u/cls. (a) to (d) and (g) of art. 19, and is not ultra vires and void - No infirmity in detention order passed and no prejudice caused to petitioner - Detention order valid - Rule discharged.
