Â Mithilesh Kumar Sinha and Another v Returning Officer For Presidential Election and Others

Supreme Court of India

17 September 1992

Case Analysis

Bench

J.S. Verma, A.S. Anand, K. Jayachandra Reddy, S.C. Agrawal, Yogeshwar Dayal

Where Reported

1992 Indlaw SC 617; (1993) Supp4 SCC 386; AIR 1993 SC 20; JT 1992 (5) SC 479; 1992(2) SCALE 566; [1992] Supp1 S.C.R. 651

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Election

Keywords: Office Of Profit Under, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, President (Discharge of Functions) Act, 1969, Salaries and Allowances of Ministers Act, 1952, Salary, Allowances And Pension Of Members Of Parliament Act, 1954, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Rules, 1974, Constitution (Eleventh Amendment) Act,1961, Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections (Amendment) Act, 1977

Summary: Constitution - Election - Constitution of India,1950, arts. 14, 38, 52, 53, 54, 58(2), 60, 71, 79, 102, 145 - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Act, 1952, ss. 5(C), 5B(6), 13(a), 14(A),18(1), 19, 21(3) - Representation of the People Act, 1950, s. 16 - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections (Amendment) Act, 1977 - Indian Penal Code,1860 - Supreme Court Rules, 1966, O. 23 r. 6, O. 39 r. 7 - Presidential and Vice-Presidential Elections Rules, 1974,r. 4 - Election - Challenged - Validity - Petitioners filed separate election petitions challenging election of ninth President of India - Preliminary objections raised by respondents regarding maintainability of petition - Whether election petitions were maintainable - Held, Courts trying election petitions were empowered to exercise original jurisdiction relating to rejection of a plaint where it does not disclose a cause of action - In nomination forms elector could subscribe as proposer or seconder to only one nomination paper and if he subscribed for more than one nomination paper then firstly submitted nomination to returning officer was valid and subsequently submitted nomination would be inoperative and ineffective - RO had accepted first nomination and rightly rejected subsequent nomination of petitioner as same proposer had signed both nomination papers as it fell short of required number of proposers as required - Petitioner had no locus standi to challenge election of returned candidate as failed to present election petition in accordance with s.14-A of the 1952 Act r/w O. 39 r. 7 of the 1966 Rules - Petitioner in election petition no. 2 was duly nominated candidate and filed petition in accordance to law but petition did not disclose any cause of action for trial and only casual ground referred by petitioner was undue influence - SC in Charan Lal Sahu v. Neelam Sanjeeva Reddy, 1978 Indlaw SC 328 and in Charan Lal Sahu v. Giani Zail Singh,1983 Indlaw SC 250 and Charan Lal Sahu v. Shri Fakruddin Ali Ahmed 1974 Indlaw SC 339 held that election petitions suffering from locus standi without any cause of action should be dismissed on preliminary objection/issue - Petitions were dismissed as filed casually mentioning several provisions without indicating any basis for challenge - Petitions dismissed.
