Ajoy Acharya v State Bureau of Investment against Economic Offences

Supreme Court of India

17 September 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

J. S. KHEHAR, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 608; (2013) 16 SCC 728; 2013 CRLJ 4763; 2013 (4) RCR(Criminal) 443; 2013(11) SCALE 496; [2013] 9 S.C.R. 457

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2) and 19 - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 409, 406, 467, 468 and 120B - Investigation into the affairs of the Madhya Pradesh State Industrial Development Corporation (MPSIDC) was ordered by the State Government - Thereupon, a FIR was registered u/ss. 409, 406, 467, 468 and 120B IPC and ss. 13(1)(d) r/w s. 13(2) of Act - Allegations levelled in the FIR were, that the functionaries of the MPSIDC had permitted investment by way of inter corporate deposits (ICD's) through a resolution of the Board of Directors (of the MPSIDC) - By the instant resolution, the Board (MPSIDC) authorized its Managing Director, to extend short term loans (including ICD's) out of the surplus funds with the MPSIDC, on suitable terms and conditions - Gravamen of the accusation was, that the Board of Directors' resolution was passed in disregard of an earlier decision taken in the Cabinet Review Meeting, wherein a decision was taken that the MPSIDC would not extend financial assistance to industries - FIR was filed, and the Special Judge, took cognizance thereof - Petitioner filed a petition seeking discharge on the ground, that prosecution had been initiated against him without seeking sanction of the competent authority - Said petition was dismissed by Special Judge - Petitioner preferred Criminal Revision Petition in the HC - Said Criminal Revision Petition was dismissed by a DB - Hence, instant appeals - (A) Whether sanction at the hands of the Governor of the State of Madhya Pradesh, (who had the power to remove any Director appointed or nominated by him under clause 89 of the Memorandum and Articles of Association of the MPSIDC), was a prerequisite before taking cognizance in the matter - Held, no - Petitioner remained a nominee Director of the MPSIDC from 1993 to 1998 - First charge sheet in the matter was filed on 24-9-2007 - Well before the filing of the first charge sheet, the petitioner had relinquished charge of the office which he was alleged to have abused/misused (i.e. the office of nominee Director of the MPSIDC) - Since the petitioner was not holding the public office which he was alleged to have abused, when the first charge sheet was filed, there was no need to obtain any sanction before proceeding to prosecute the appellant, for the offences alleged against him - (B) Whether sanction before prosecution was required from each of the competent authorities entitled to remove petitioner from the offices held by him, in situations wherein the petitioner holds a plurality of offices - Held, if an petitioner holds a plurality of offices, each one of which makes him a public servant, sanction was essential only at the hands of the competent authority (entitled to remove him from service) of the office which he had allegedly misused - This leads to the clear inference, that other public offices held by the petitioner wherein an petitioner holds a plurality of offices, were irrelevant for purposes of obtaining sanction prior to prosecution - Appeals dismissed.
