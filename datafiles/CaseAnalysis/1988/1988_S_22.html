Supreme Court of India

09 February 1988

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranganath Misra, G.L. Oza, M.M. Dutt

Where Reported

1988 Indlaw SC 599; (1988) SCC (Cr) 234; (1988) 1 SCC 692; AIR 1988 SC 709; 1988 (2) Bom.C.R. 232; 1988 (1) Crimes 780; 1988 CRLJ 853; 1988 CRLR 129; 1988 CrLR(SC) 129; JT 1988 (1) SC 279; 1988(1) SCALE 261; [1988] 2 S.C.R. 930

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Trusts & Associations

Keywords: Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973 (as amended by Act No.25 of 2005 & Act No.2 of 2006)

Summary: Criminal Procedure Code, 1974, s. 482 - Trusts Act, 1882, s. 53 - Breach of trust - Trust created with one of trustees member of settlor's family - Complaint against trustee and office bearers for offences u/ss. 406 and 467 etc. - Criminal proceedings were quashed by High Court against two persons - Held, alleged breach of trust constituted only civil wrong and therefore, proceedings were liable to be quashed.
