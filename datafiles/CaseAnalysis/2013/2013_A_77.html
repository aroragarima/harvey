Ashfaq Ahmed Quereshi and another v Namrata Chopra and others

Supreme Court of India

17 December 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, Sharad Arvind Bobde

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 842; 2014 ALL MR (Cri) 390; 2014 (2) JCC 1358; JT 2014 (5) SC 142; 2014 (1) RCR(Criminal) 528; 2013(15) SCALE 479

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860, s. 420 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 482 - Criminal proceedings - Quashing of - Sustainability - Appellants/complainants filed complaint u/s. 200 of CrPC against respondents No. 1 and 2 - Trial Court took cognizance for the offence u/s. 420 IPC - Respondents filed petition u/s. 482 CrPC for quash - HC quashed the criminal proceedings - Hence instant appeal - Whether HC was justified in quashing the criminal proceedings - Held, there was sufficient evidence to show that the property belonged not only to respondent Nos.1 and 2, but they were the owners alongwith respondent Nos. 3 and 4 - There were disputes regarding partition and demarcation of shares between the respective parties - Sale deeds were also on record that their shares were sold not only by respondent Nos. 3 and 4 but also by respondent Nos. 1 and 2 subsequently and there was no land available - SC was of the considered opinion that there was no occasion for the High Court to allow the petition u/s. 482 CrPC and quash the criminal proceedings qua the said respondents - Impugned judgment and order was set aside and Trial Court was directed to proceed against respondents in accordance with law - Appeal allowed.
