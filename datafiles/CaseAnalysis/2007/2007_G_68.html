Commissioner of Income Tax v Gujarat Maritime Board

Supreme Court of India

05 December 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.H. Kapadia, B. SUDARSHAN REDDY

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1417; (2007) 14 SCC 704; [2007] 295 ITR 561; JT 2007 (13) SC 375; 2007(14) SCALE 21; [2007] 12 S.C.R. 962; [2008] 116 TAXMAN 58

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Carriers & Transportation; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Water

Keywords: Voluntary Contributions, Gujarat Maritime Board Act, 1981

Summary: Income Tax Act, 1961- Gujarat Maritime Board constituted u/s. 3(2) of Gujarat Maritime Board Act, 1981 - Whether Maritime Board is entitled to status of charitable institution u/s. 11 of the 1961 Act? - Interpretation of "any other object of general public utility" u/s. 2(15) of 1961 Act - Held, expression would prima facie include all objects which promote welfare of general public; if primary purpose and predominant object are to promote welfare of general public purpose would be charitable purpose - When an object is to promote or protect the interest of a particular trade or industry that object becomes an object of public utility, but not so, if it seeks to promote the interest of those who conduct the said trade or industry - Case is covered by judgment of CIT v. Andhra Pradesh State Road Transport Corporation in which it has been held that since Corporation was established for purpose of providing efficient transport system, having no profit motive, though it earns income in process, it is not liable to income tax - Maritime Board is established for development of minor ports with no profit motive - Maritime Board is under legal obligation to apply income which arises directly and substantially from business held under trust for development of minor ports - Appeal dismissed.
