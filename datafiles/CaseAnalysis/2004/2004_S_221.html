The Regional Manager Rajasthan State Road v Sohan Lal etc.

Supreme Court of India

27 September 2004

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, S.B. Sinha

Where Reported

2004 Indlaw SC 828; (2004) 8 SCC 218; (2004) SCC (L&S) 1078; AIR 2004 SC 4828; 2004 (4) AWC 3387; 2004 (3) CLR 778; 2004 (103) FLR 425; JT 2004 (8) SC 113; 2004(8) SCALE 325; 2004 (6) SLR 96

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law; Service

Summary: Labour and Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 25F and 33(2A) - Termination - Employer contending that appointment of respondent/workman was purely temporary on daily wages basis and since his services were not required, the same was terminated which was permissible as per the terms of the letter of appointment and further it was said that the respondent/workman had not completed 240 days of continuous service in any year in the Corporation and therefore there was no need to comply with s. 25F of ID Act - Respondent/workman on the other hand contending that termination was a colourable exercise of power because during his service in the Corporation an inspection was carried out by the checking staff, when he was on duty in a bus, during the course of investigation, a false case of non-issuance of ticket to six passengers was made against him and since the Management was not in a position to prove the said charge it took recourse to his discharge from service without holding a proper enquiry which amounts to a colourable exercise of power - Finding by Industrial Tribunal after examination of evidence, that the Corporation has proved the misconduct as well as the unruly behaviour of the Conductor during the inspection, therefore, it came to the conclusion that even on the ground of misconduct termination of the respondent was justified - Petition thereagainst filed by respondent/workman - In view of finding as to misconduct which was upheld by HC in its single judge Bench and further by Division Bench, can offer of respondent/workman as to foregoing the back wages in lieu of his being re-instated can be considered? - Held, No - Unless and until the finding of the tribunal on misconduct was set aside - Appeals allowed.
