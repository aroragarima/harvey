Ram Charan Das v Girjanandini Devi and Others

Supreme Court of India

20 April 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

J.R. Madholkar, A.K. Sarkar, R.S. Bachawat

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 140; AIR 1966 SC 323; 1966 (1) SCJ 61; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 841

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Women & Children

Keywords: Debt Laws

Summary: Land & Property - Indian Evidence Act, 1872 - Uttar Pradesh Court of Wards Act, 1912 - (A) What is legal effect of a deed, Exhibit Y 13, D/- 31-03-1933 described in paper-book as a deed of partition? - Held, document read as a whole leaves no doubt that it was intended to be a final settlement of disputes amongst parties - If it were intended to be otherwise it would have been natural to find an express statement somewhere in document to show that it was intended to be a temporary settlement only - Consideration for such a settlement, if one may put it that way, is expectation that such a settlement will result in establishing or ensuring amity and goodwill amongst persons bearing relationship with one another - That consideration having been passed by each of disputants settlement consisting of recognition of right asserted by each other cannot be permitted to be impeached thereafter - (B) Whether validity of transaction evidenced by deed is affected by reason of fact that property comprised therein was at time of its execution, under management of Court of Wards? - Further held, in first place once it is held that transaction being a family settlement is not an alienation, it cannot amount to creation of an interest - Every party taking benefit under a family settlement must necessarily be shown to have, under law, a claim to a share in property - Since it was not a bona fide surrender it was regarded as one creating only an interest in property which was under superintendence of Court of Wards - Appeal dismissed.
