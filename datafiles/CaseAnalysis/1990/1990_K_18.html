Pollisetti Pullamma And Ors. v Kalluri Rameswaramma And Ors.

Supreme Court of India

26 October 1990

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, K. Ramaswamy

Where Reported

1990 Indlaw SC 1034; AIR 1991 SC 604; AIR 1991 SCW 81; JT 1990 (4) SC 293; [1990] 2 S.C.R. 393; [1990] Supp2 S.C.R. 393

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Agriculture & Agri. Products

Summary: Land & Property - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estates Land Act, 1908, s. 3(10)(b)(i) - Andhra Pradesh (Andhra Area) Estates (Abolition and Conversion Into Ryotwari) Act, 1948, s. 15 - Estates Abolition Act, s. 17(1) - Estates Land Act, s. 3(15)(c) - Madras Estates Land (Reduction of Rent) Act, 1947 - Appellant, are tenant, respondent, are landholders - After Abolition Act, respondents landholders claimed that lands in question were either under their personal cultivation or they intended to resume those for private cultivation and as such those were their private lands and they were entitled to ryotwari pattas - Tenants, on contrary, claimed that lands were in their possession and they were entitled to ryotwari pattas - Whether appellants or respondents are entitled to ryotwari pattas under Abolition of Estates Act? - Held, a person who has occupied ryoti land for a continuous period of 12 years shall be deemed to be a ryot for all purposes of Estates Land Act - Conferment of occupancy right on ryot in ryoti land was an object of Estates Land Act - Where land proved or admitted to be once ryoti land is claimed to have been converted into private land, claim is untenable unless landholder proves direct cultivation for a period of 12 years before 01-07-1908 - It was common ground that nature of lands at inception, whether ryoti or private, was not known and that burden of establishing that lands were private lands was on landholders - Only mode of proof attempted by landholders was grant of short-term leases and change of tenants and rent - Lands were not established to be private lands and no attempt was made to prove personal cultivation or any intention to resume personal cultivation - Appeals allowed.
