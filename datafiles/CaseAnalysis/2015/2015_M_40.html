State of Madhya Pradesh v Maharani Ushadevi

Supreme Court of India

15 July 2015

Case Analysis

Bench

N.V. Ramana, Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2015 Indlaw SC 486; (2015) 8 SCC 672; AIR 2015 SC 2699; 2015 (112) ALR 655; 2015 (3) CLT(SC) 165; 2015 (3) JabLJ 1; JT 2015 (6) SC 297; 2015 (5) MLJ 884; 2015 (129) RD 597; 2015(8) SCALE 15; [2015] 7 S.C.R. 737

Case Digest

Subject: Civil Procedure; Land & Property

Keywords: Grass, Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, Madhya Bharat Land Revenue and Tenancy Act, 1950, Indore Land Revenue and Tenancy Act, 1931

Summary: (A) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 363 - Claim of private property - Bar under constitution - Maintainability of Suit - Plaintiff daughter and sole heir of Ruler of Holkar State stated that property in question is private property and during lifetime of Maharaja he was depositing revenue charges with treasury of Holkar State - Further, birs were transferred to Army Department of Holkar State, however, on expiry of term it was again transferred to Maharaja and from that date, birs are in continuous possession and enjoyment of plaintiff's family - Further, in year 1948, Holkar State along with other princely States was merged with Dominion of India as per Covenant which was later on re-organised as part of present State of Madhya Pradesh and as per Covenant entered between Maharaja and Govt. of India, by communication land in question being managed by Household Department became exclusive and individual property of father of plaintiff - Plaintiff stated that State Govt. passed orders and issued notice requiring plaintiff to handover possession of land in question on ground that State Govt. has declared suit schedule property as property of State - Trial Court dismissed suit of plaintiff and held that transfer of Suit schedule lands to Household Department is without any authority and therefore bad - Further, plaintiff was not in possession of suit properties either in form of ordinary tenant, Govt. lessee or land owner and in view of bar contained in art. 363 of Constitution, suit is not maintainable - HC reversed order passed by Trial Court and passed order in favor of plaintiff - Whether suit falls in ambit of art. 363 of Constitution and is maintainable.

Held, Covenant was act of State and any dispute arising out of its terms cannot form subject matter in any Court including SC and there cannot be any implied recognition of property as private property at any later stages when opportunity had already been granted to raise issue in terms of art. 12 before defined period - Further, properties do not find place in Covenant. Draupadi Devi 2004, Indlaw SC 717, relied on SC held that plaintiff is trying to interpret Covenant that all properties which are in custody of Household Department are personal properties of Ruler which is impermissible. SC held that Court below erred in entertaining suit without properly taking into consideration judgments and proposition of law. SC is of view that relief in suit falls within ambit of art. 363 of Constitution and is not maintainable. Further, in spite of Maharaja and plaintiff were in continuous possession of property and paid revenue charges for properties, however long plaintiff's possession may be and paying of taxes will not give her any right seeking declaration of ownership when these properties are part of Covenant and calls for interpretation of Covenant. Appeals allowed.

(B) Land & Property - Civil Procedure - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 363 - Madhya Pradesh Land Revenue Code, 1959, s. 158(2) - Title to property - Claim to be Bhumiswami - Determination - Whether Court was right in extending benefit of bhumiswami u/s. 158(2) of Code to plaintiff.

Held, plaintiff cannot seek status of Bhumiswami independent of Covenant because rights u/s. 158(2) of Code arise out of Covenant itself. Source to hold land arises by virtue of Covenant. SC held that when right so claimed by way of Covenant is disputed and relief of settling these disputes is barred u/art. 363 of Constitution, one cannot claim to be 'Bhumiswami' u/s. 158(2) of Code, independent of Covenant. Hence, SC held that suit filed by plaintiff for declaration and injunction is barred u/art. 363 of Constitution and plaintiff is not entitled for any relief u/s. 158(2) of Code claiming rights of Bhumiswami. Impugned order passed by HC is set aside. Appeals allowed.

Ratio - Title of property in favor of private party is subject to covenant entered by State.
