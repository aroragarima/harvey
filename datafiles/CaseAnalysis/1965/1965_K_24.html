Kesharichand Jaisukhal v Shillong Banking Corporation

Supreme Court of India

16 February 1965

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Bachawat, Vaidynathier Ramaswami, Raghubar Dayal, J.R. Madholkar

Where Reported

1965 Indlaw SC 103; AIR 1965 SC 1711; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 514; 1965 (1) CompLJ 355; [1965] 3 S.C.R. 110

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Banking & Finance - Limitation Act, 1908, art. 85 - Banking Companies Act, 1949, ss. 45D and 45O - Banking - Banker and customer relationship - Combined overdraft and deposit account - Dishonour of cheque deposited by customer - Credit given in account - Effect on position of bank - Application u/s. 45D of 1949 Act - Limitation - Duty of Bank while collection of cheque - Held, entry not barred u/art. 85, Limitation Act, 1908 - Bank is duty bound to act according to customer's directions or usages prevailing in its absence and to use reasonable skill and diligence to secure customer's interests - Appeal dismissed.
