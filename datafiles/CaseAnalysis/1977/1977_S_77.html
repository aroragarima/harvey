State Of Madhya Pradesh v State Of Maharashtra & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

05 January 1977

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Ray, M. Hameedullah Beg, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1977 Indlaw SC 192; (1977) SCC (L&S) 232; (1977) 2 SCC 288; AIR 1977 SC 1466; 1977 (34) FLR 371; 1977 LabIC 697; 1977 (2) LLJ 369; 1978 (1) LLN 29; [1977] 2 S.C.R. 555; 1977 (1) SLR 433; 1977 UJ 122

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Keywords: Actionable Wrong

Summary: Service - Fundamental Rules, rr. 52, 53 - Suspension - Reinstatement - Subsistence allowance - Validity - Plaintiff/respondent no. 2 was appointed as Assistant Surgeon - Plaintiff requested Civil Surgeon for relief as he was not keeping good health in anticipation of sanction of leave by Govt. - Show cause notice was issued wherein charges were framed against plaintiff - Later, by an order plaintiff was suspended - Plaintiff filed suit against suspension order alleging that both Maharashtra and Madhya Pradesh States were liable - Trial Court passed a decree in favour of plaintiff declaring that plaintiff should be reinstated and both States were liable for payment of arrears of salary - On appeal, HC dismissed appeal and confirmed judgment and decree of Trial Court however modified decree and held Madhya Pradesh liable - Hence, instant appeal - Whether there was any perversity or illegality in judgment and decree of Courts below- Held, since plaintiff was under suspension his suit was within a period of 3 years from date of his reinstatement - During period of suspension he was not entitled to salary u/r. 53 of the Fundamental Rules - Therefore, plaintiff's cause of action for salary for period of suspension did not accrue until he was reinstated - However, plaintiff's salary accrued only when he was reinstated as a result of decree setting aside orders of suspension - Order of suspension did not put an end to plaintiff's service and during suspension there was suspension allowance - In Khem Chand v. Union of India, 1962 Indlaw SC 78 SC held that real effect of order of suspension was that though he continues to be a member of service he wsa not permitted to work and was paid only subsistence allowance which was less than his salary - U/r. 52 of the Fundamental Rules pay and allowance of a Govt. servant who was dismissed or removed from service cease from date of his dismissal or removal - Therefore, there would be no question of salary accruing or accruing due so long as orders of suspension and dismissal stand to plaintiff - HC was correct in conclusion that plaintiff's claim for salary accrued due only on order of dismissal was set aside - Appeal dismissed.
