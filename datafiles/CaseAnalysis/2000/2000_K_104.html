K. R. Lakshman and Others v Karnataka Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 December 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

G.B. Pattanaik, B.N. Agrawal

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2901; (2001) SCC (L&S) 245; (2001) 1 SCC 442; AIR 2001 SC 595; 2001 (1) CLR 601; JT 2001 (1) SC 196; 2001 (34) LabIC 526; 2001 (1) LLN 871; 2000(8) SCALE 230; 2001 (1) SCJ 393; 2001 (2) SLJ 334; 2000 (6) SLR 16; 2001 (1) SLT 153; [2000] Supp5 S.C.R. 535; 2000 (8) Supreme 385

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Promotion - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 14 - Appellants are technically qualified direct recruits to post of Operator/Overseer/Meter Reader/Asst. Store Keeper - Karnataka Electricity Board Recruitment and Promotion Regulations, 1969 were amended on 3-2-1982, providing a ratio of 1:1 for promotion to post of Junior Engineer (Electrical) between technically qualified (Direct Recruit) and technically not qualified (Promotees) - Whether providing ratio for promotion is discriminatory? - Division Bench relying upon decisions of Supreme Court in P. Murugesan v. State of Tamil Nadu, and S. N. Deshpande v. Maharashtra I.D. Corporation, 1992 Indlaw SC 690 held its not discriminatory - Held, 60% of posts in cadre of Junior Engineers were meant to be filled up by different ratio from amongst persons in so-called combined cadre and 35% quota, which was available for promotion for such common cadre people was further bifurcated between qualified direct recruits, who are appellants and technically unqualified promotees, who are private respondents in ratio 1:1 - Such a classification cannot be held to be discriminatory, if object sought to be achieved is looked into - In view of law laid down in Triloki Nath Khosa's case, 1973 Indlaw SC 304 and in view of judgment in P. Murugesan and in Shamkant Narayan Deshpande, Division Bench of High Court was fully justified in coming to conclusion that by providing a ratio of 1:1 in matter of promotion there has been no violation of art. 14 of Constitution - Appeals dismissed.
