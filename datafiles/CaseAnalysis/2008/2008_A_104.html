S.L.Construction & Anr v Alapati Srinivasa Rao & Anr

Supreme Court of India

23 October 2008

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Cyriac Joseph

Where Reported

2008 Indlaw SC 2119; (2009) 1 SCC 500; (2009) 1 SCC (Cr) 558; AIR 2009 SC 1538; 2009 ALL MR (Cri) 1758; 2009 (5) Bom.C.R. 902; 2008 (6) CTC 314; 2009 (1) JCC (NI) 12; JT 2008 (13) SC 472; 2009 (2) KLT 572; 2009 (1) MLJ(Crl) 767; 2009 (2) MPLJ 36; 2009 (1) RCR(Civil) 25B; 2009 (1) RCR(Criminal) 218; 2008(15) SCALE 308; [2008] 15 S.C.R. 51

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Dishonour of cheques, Legal Notice, Bouncing Of Cheques, Public Financial Institutions, Negotiable Instruments Laws (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Criminal - Banking & Finance - Practice & Procedure - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 138 - Dishonour of cheque - Issuance of notices - Compliance of legal requirement - Maintainability of petition - Appellants had entered into some business transactions in regard to supply of certain materials with Respondent No. 1 - Appellant No. 2 as proprietor of appellant No. 1 issued cheque in favour of complainant-respondent and cheque was issued by way of security - Said cheque was presented in bank and returned unpaid by banker of appellants on ground of insufficient funds - Even in second time and said cheque was again presented before bank, and was again dishonoured - Respondents served another notice upon appellant No. 2 describing him as Proprietor of S.L. Structures and Engineers and calling upon him to pay said amount within 15 days from date of receipt thereof - As no payment was received from appellant pursuant to said notice, complaint petition was filed - Upon receipt of summons, appellants moved HC which was dismissed - Hence, instant Appeal - Whether legal requirements were complied with by respondent so as to enable him to maintain complaint petition - Held, cheque could be presented within six months and cheque was presented for third time i.e., within prescribed period - What prohibited was presentation of cheque within aforementioned period and not number of times it was presented - It could be immaterial that whether for one reason or other complainant had to present cheque for third time or not - Successive issuance of notices having been made u/s. 138 of the Act, respondent merely made all attempts to comply with legal requirements - First notice had not served and second notice having been withdrawn in terms of reply issued by advocate for appellants themselves, complainant could not be said to have committed any illegality in presenting cheque for third time and issuing third notice upon defaulter - As issuance of cheque, non-payment thereof on presentation, issuance of valid notice calling upon drawer of cheque to pay amount and appellants' failure to pay to complainant amount within period of 15 days from date of receipt of copy of said notice upon them, cause of action arose for filing complaint petition - Thus, as cause of action for filing complaint arose only once and not more than once, HC could not be said to be committed any error in passing judgment - Appeal dismissed.
