Bhagat Ram (Dead) By LRs v Teja Singh (Dead) By LRs

Supreme Court of India

06 November 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.G. Balakrishnan, U.C. Banerjee

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20389; (2002) 1 SCC 210; AIR 2002 SC 1; 2001 (6) ALD(SC) 140; 2002 (46) ALR 672; 2002 (1) AWC 435; [2001] 252 ITR 324; 2002 (1) JhrCR(SC) 400; JT 2001 (10) SC 270; 2002 (1) KarLJ 141; 2002 (93) RD 94; 2001(8) SCALE 93; 2001 (5) SCJ 309; 2001 (8) SLT 96; 2002 (Supp) Bom.C.R. 753; 2001 (Supp) MLJ(SC) 130; [2001] Supp5 S.C.R. 143; 2001 (8) Supreme 130; 2002 (1) UC 203

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Income Tax & Direct Taxes

Summary: Family & Personal - Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, ss. 14 and 15 - Appellant filed suit for property devolved to him after death of his sister u/s. 15 - Respondent died but no step taken to implead legal heirs - Appellant legal heirs brought in record - After appeal disposed of original appellant died and legal heirs filed an application to implead in this appeal - Whether appellant can claim property of his sister? - Even if the female Hindu who is having a limited ownership becomes full owner by virtue of s. 14(1) of the Act, the rules of succession given under sub-section (2) of s. 15 can be applied - Held, it is source from which property was inherited by female, which is more important for purpose of devolution of her property - If female Hindu who is having a limited ownership becomes full owner by virtue of s. 14(1) of Act, rules of succession given under sub-section (2) of s. 15 can be applied - Cl. (a) of sub-section (2) of s. 15 is appropriate rule to be applied for succession of property left by sister of original appellant - Appeal allowed.
