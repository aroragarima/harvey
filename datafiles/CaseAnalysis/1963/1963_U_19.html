University of Delhi and Another v Ram Nath

Supreme Court of India

01 April 1963

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, Kailas Nath Wanchoo, K.C. Das Gupta

Where Reported

1963 Indlaw SC 98; AIR 1963 SC 1873; 1963 (7) FLR 177; 1963 (2) LLJ 335; [1964] 2 S.C.R. 703

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Keywords: Research, Educational Institution, Central Provinces And Berar Industrial Disputes Settlement Act, 1947

Summary: Education - Labour & Industrial - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 2(j), 2(g) and 2(s) - Does work of imparting education by educational institutions come under 'Industry' u/s. 2(j) of Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - Held, It is unreasonable to regard work of imparting education as industry only because its minor, subsidiary and incidental work may seem to partake of character of service which may fall under s. 2(j) - Work of teaching cannot be assimilated as 'Industrial Process' - Appeal allowed.
