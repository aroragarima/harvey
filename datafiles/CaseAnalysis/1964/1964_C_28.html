Padmavati R. Saraiya and Others v Commissioner of Income Tax Bombay City-1

Supreme Court of India

22 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

S.M. Sikri, K. Subba Rao, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 186; AIR 1965 SC 1263; [1965] 35 Comp Cas 251; [1965] 55 ITR 699; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 307

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Income Tax Act, 1922, s. 2(15) - Income Tax - Double Taxation - Agreement for Avoidance of Double Taxation in India and Pakistan, 1947, arts. IV and VI and Schedule - Dividends received in India on profits earned in Pakistan - Dividend declared - Resolution making dividend payable after remittance from Pakistan becomes free - (A) Total Income - Whether dividend form part of assesses total income within s. 2 (15) of Act - (B) Taxation method - Does Agreement restrict power of assessment u/s. 49 AA of Act - (C) When dividend deemed to be paid to shareholder - Dividend held, forms part of assessee's total income - Agreement does not restrict power, allows only abatement on production of certificate - Dividend held not paid to assessee considering s. 16(2) - Appeal dismissed.
