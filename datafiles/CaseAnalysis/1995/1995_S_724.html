State of Punjab v Surja Ram

Supreme Court of India

09 August 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

M.K. Mukherjee, G.T. Nanavati

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 518; (1995) Supp3 SCC 419; (1995) SCC (Cr) 937; AIR 1995 SC 2413; 1995 (4) CCR 26; 1995 (3) Crimes 503; 1995 CRLJ 4161; 1995 CRLR 4161; 1995 (124) CTR 26; JT 1995 (6) SC 383; 1995(4) SCALE 689; [1995] Supp2 S.C.R. 590; 1995 (2) UJ 757

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Indian Penal Code, 1860, s.300; Criminal Procedure Code, 1973, s.154 - Murder - FIR - Lodged promptly by eye-witness the daughter of deceased father and son - Apprised her maternal uncle first instead of going to Police Station with Sarpanch of village - Other eye-witness' name not in FIR - Evidence of both eye-witnesses corroborated by medical evidence - Held, daughter's conduct under situation was normal and natural - No adverse conclusions against her conduct - Evidence of both eye-witness trustworthy - Conviction proper.
