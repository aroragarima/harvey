Everest Advertising Private Limited v State, Government of Nct of Delhi and Others

Supreme Court of India

10 April 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 334; (2007) 5 SCC 54; AIR 2007 SC 1650; 2007 ALL MR (Cri) 1741; 2007 (3) ALT 81; 2007 (3) CompLJ 410; 2007 CRLJ 2442; 2007 (3) G.L.R. 2062; 2007 (2) JCC (NI) 141; JT 2007 (5) SC 529; 2007 (3) KLT 821; 2007 (4) MPLJ 337; 2007 (146) PLR 833; 2007 (2) RCR(Civil) 738; 2007 (2) RCR(Criminal) 575; 2007(5) SCALE 479; 2007 (6) SCJ 775; [2007] 4 S.C.R. 1055; 2007 (3) Supreme 821

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Code of Criminal Procedure, ss. 200, 204; Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, s. 141 - Respondent Nos. 2 and 3 are Chairman and Managing Director of a Company - Processes were directed to be issued - Recalled the order and held that allegations in the complaint are far from sufficient to summon respondents 2 and 3 for the offence of the company - Appeal against - Held, not only cheques were issued having regard to the huge amount payable by Accused No. 6 to the Company but also as a result of fall out of non-payment thereof, negotiations were held between the parties wherein Respondent Nos. 2 and 3 took part - There cannot be any doubt that the ingredients of the provisions of s. 141 stand satisfied - Appeal allowed.
