Vijay Kumar v State Of U.P. And Another

Supreme Court of India

03 August 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

J.M. Panchal, H.L. Gokhale

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 476; (2011) 8 SCC 136; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 2893; 2012 CRLJ 305; 2011 (6) CTC 445; 2012 (2) MLJ(Crl) 284; 2011 (2) OLR 658; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 208; 2011(8) SCALE 221; [2011] 11 S.C.R. 893

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Prevention of Corruption Act, 1988, ss. 13(1)(d), 13(2) - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, s. 311 - Power to summon the witness - Exercise of power - Appellant's daughter filed suit for prohibitory injunction against respondent Municipality - Appellant lodged a criminal complaint against 2nd respondent Executive Officer of Municipality alleging that 2nd respondent demanded bribe for settling the matter - In the meantime, appellant's daughter went abroad and appellant looked after the civil suit of his daughter - 2nd respondent was charge-sheeted u/s. 13(2) r/w s. 13(1)(d) of the Act - Trial Court completed the evidence of prosecution and the case posted for recording the statements of accused u/s. 313 of CrPC - 2nd respondent accused filed application seeking to summon appellant's daughter as court witness - Trial Court dismissed respondents' application - Respondents' filed revision before the HC - HC, by impugned order, allowed the revision and directed the Trial Court to summon appellant's daughter -Whether the HC was justified in exercising the power u/s. 311 CrPC and directing the Trial Court to summon appellant's daughter as court witness - Held, record nowhere showed that any complaint was filed by appellant's daughter against any of the accused making grievance that they had demanded any bribe amount from her - Case of the prosecution was simple that in order to settle the matter relating to construction of boundaries on the disputed property, which was being supervised by the appellant, the 2nd respondent and another accused had demanded a sum as bribe amount from the appellant as a result of which the appellant had filed complaint pursuant to which a trap was laid and accused were arrested while receiving the amount - After framing of charge and commencement of trial several witnesses were examined by the prosecution, who had been cross- examined by the accused - Appellant's daughter had nothing to do with the bribe case either as a complainant or as a witness to the trap arranged by the police - Appellant's daughter name did not figure as one of the witnesses to be examined by the prosecution when charge-sheet was submitted in Trial Court - However, HC without specifying as to how appellant's daughter was a material witness or how her evidence was essential for just decision of the case, had directed the Trial Court to summon appellant's daughter as a court witness u/s. 311 of the CrPC and to examine her - Though s. 311 conferred vast discretion upon the court and was expressed in the widest possible terms, the discretionary power under the said Section could be invoked only for the ends of justice - Discretionary power should be exercised consistently with the provisions of the Code and the principles of criminal law - Discretionary power conferred u/s. 311 had to be exercised judicially for reasons stated by the Court and not arbitrarily or capriciously - Before directing the Trial Court to examine appellant's daughter as a court witness, the HC did not examine the reasons assigned by the Trial Court as to why it was not necessary to examine her as a court witness and had given the impugned direction without assigning any reason - HC failed to consider the case of the prosecution that the application was submitted by the 2nd respondent only to delay the trial and no case was made out by the 2nd respondent as to why direction should be given to examine appellant's daughter as a court witness - In a bribe case what was required to be proved by the prosecution was that there was a demand of bribe by the accused from the complainant and that pursuant to the said demand, bribe amount was accepted by the accused - To prove that case it was not necessary for the court to examine appellant's daughter as a court witness - Neither the 2nd respondent in his application nor the court in the impugned judgment had specified the reason as to why and how examination of appellant's daughter as a court witness was necessary - Hence, power u/s. 311 of the CrPC was exercised arbitrarily by HC and, therefore, the impugned judgment was set aside - Appeal allowed.
