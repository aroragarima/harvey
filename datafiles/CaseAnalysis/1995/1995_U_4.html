U.P.S.R.T.C. v U. P. Parivahan Nigam Shishukhs Berozgar Sangh and Others

Supreme Court of India

12 January 1995

Case Analysis

Bench

B.L. Hansaria, Kuldip Singh, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1995 Indlaw SC 1775; (1995) 2 SCC 1; (1995) SCC (L&S) 386; AIR 1995 SC 1115; AIR 1995 SCW 1145; 1995 ALJ 767; 1995 (3) ALT 13; 1995 (29) ATC 171; 1995 (70) FLR 890; JT 1995 (2) SC 26; 1995 LabIC 1361; 1995 (2) LLJ 854; 1995 (1) LLN 788; 1995(1) SCALE 127; [1995] 1 S.C.R. 204; 1995 (1) SLR 609

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Service - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Apprentices Act, 1961, s. 22 - Workmen's Compensation Act, 1923 - Evidence Act, 1872, s. 115 - Employment of apprentice trainees at Service Regulations of the Corporation - Circular by Corporation and Memo of Directorate of Training saying that full efforts should be made to provide trainees with employment - However State Govt. desires for reservation of 50% vacancies for apprentice trainees - (A) Applicability of promissory estopped - (B) Factors that should be kept in mind while considering their claim for appointment - Held, circular and memo falls short of any promise - Factors that should be kept in mind while considering their claim for appointment, stated - Appeals/special leave petitions are disposed of.
