State Of Maharashtra v M/S. Hindustan Construction Company Ltd.

Supreme Court of India

01 April 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 238; (2010) 4 SCC 518; AIR 2010 SC 1299; 2010 (80) ALR 237; 2010 (3) AWC 2451; 2010 (3) Bom.C.R. 11; 2010 (2) CompLJ 289; 2010 (3) CTC 452; JT 2010 (3) SC 420; 2010 (7) MLJ(SC) 124; 2010 (2) RCR(Civil) 614; 2010 (110) RD 773; 2010(3) SCALE 466; 2010 (2) WLN(SC) 81

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Arbitration and Conciliation Bill, 1995

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration and Conciliation Act, 1996, s. 37 - Appeal u/s. 37 - Amendment thereof - Appellant/State awarded a construction contract to the respondent/Company - Dispute arose between the parties in respect of the work carried out by the respondent - Dispute referred to the Arbitral Tribunal - Arbitral Tribunal awarded compensation to respondent - Appellant filed application before the District Court for setting aside the award on various grounds - District Court rejected appellant's application - Appellant filed arbitration appeal before the HC - Appellant, subsequently, filed an application before the HC seeking amendment to the memorandum of arbitration appeal by adding some new additional grounds - Single Judge dismissed the application - Whether in an appeal u/s. 37 of the Act from an order refusing to set aside the award, an amendment in the memorandum of appeal to raise additional/new grounds can be permitted - Held, grounds sought to be added in the memorandum of arbitration appeal by way of amendment were absolutely new grounds for which there was no foundation in the application for setting aside the award - Such new grounds containing new material/facts could not have been introduced for the first time in an appeal when admittedly these grounds were not originally raised in the arbitration petition for setting aside the award - Further, no prayer was made by the appellant for amendment in the petition u/s. 34 before the concerned court or at the appellate stage - In such circumstances, an amendment in the memorandum of appeal u/s. 37 to raise additional/new grounds cannot be permitted - Therefore, discretion exercised by the Single Judge in refusing to grant leave to appellant to amend the memorandum of arbitration appeal does not suffer from any illegality - Appeal dismissed.
