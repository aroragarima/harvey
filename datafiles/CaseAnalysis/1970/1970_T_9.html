Lakshmi Amma and Another v Talengalanarayana Bhatta and Another

Supreme Court of India

10 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

A.N. Grover, J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 542; (1970) 3 SCC 159; AIR 1970 SC 1367

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal

Summary: Contract Act, 1872, s. 16 - Land and Property - Deed of settlement - Executant settled entire property in favour of one of grandsons - Excluded his own issues and other grand children - Negligible provison made for third wife, other two being died before - Absence of provision even regarding her right to reside in house till her death - Executant himself debarred from dealing with property as an owner during lifetime - Finding that executant was of advanced age and in state of senility and suffering from ailments like diabetes - Facts and circumstances leading to grave suspicion as to genuineness of execution - Grandson (settlee) failing to discharge the burden of establishing that deed was executed by executant voluntarily and without any external pressure or influence while he was not of infirm mind and was fully aware of dispositions - Held, settlement deed was invalid - Appeal Allowed.
