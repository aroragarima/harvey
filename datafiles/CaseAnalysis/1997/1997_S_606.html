State of Punjab and Others v Devinder Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

21 July 1997

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Jagannadha Rao, S.B. Majmudar

Where Reported

1997 Indlaw SC 2091; (1998) 9 SCC 595; (1998) SCC (L&S) 1261

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Whether High Court was justified in directing appellant-State to pay to respondents salary and allowances as are being paid to regular employees holding similar posts? - Held, principle of 'Equal Pay for Equal Work' can ensure to respondents to limited extent that when they were found to have been giving similar work as Ledger Clerks/Ledger Keepers they could have been paid minimum of pay scale of a Ledger Keeper which was available to regularly appointed Ledger Keepers/Ledger Clerks - Further held, respondents would be entitled to minimum of pay scale of Ledger Keepers which may be available to regularly appointed Ledger Keepers and they cannot be straightaway paid running time scale as they were not regularly appointed as Ledger Keepers/Ledger Clerks - Order accordingly.
