State Of Tamil Nadu v Seshachalam

Supreme Court of India

18 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 917; (2007) 10 SCC 137; AIR 2008 SC 647; 2008 (5) AWC 4647; 2007 (4) ESC 583; JT 2007 (11) SC 358; 2007 (6) MLJ(SC) 738; 2007(11) SCALE 239; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 53; 2007 (6) SLR 102

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Practice & Procedure; Service

Keywords: Upgradation Of Post, Special Courts, Revision Of Pension, Liberalised Pension Scheme

Summary: Service - Classification - High Court held that respondents who had not been in service on or before 28.1.1994 came within the scope and ambit of the GOMs and Government Order applied not only to the existing staff but also to the retired employees and as such a beneficial interpretation to the said Government Order should be given as the said provisions have to be read in consonance with art. 14 - Appeal against - Held, promotions to the employees of the Finance Department were given systematically over a long period of time but no such grievance was made nor any application was filed before the appropriate forum - Only because a cut off date has been fixed, the same per se cannot be said to be arbitrary as some date is required to be fixed for that purpose - Impugned order of the High Court set aside - Appeals allowed.
