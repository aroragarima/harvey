Johri Singh v Sukh Pal Singh and Others

Supreme Court of India

04 September 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

K.N. Saikia, N.D. Ojha

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 143; (1989) 4 SCC 403; AIR 1989 SC 2073; 1989 (15) ALR 703; 1991 (1) BC 84; 1989 (3) CCC 48; JT 1989 (3) SC 582; 1989 (2) PLR 617; 1989(2) SCALE 518; [1989] 1 S.C.R. 17; [1989] supp1 S.C.R. 17

Case Digest

Subject: Banking & Finance; Civil Procedure; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Civil Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, ss. 115 and 148, O. 20, r.14 - Court decreed a claim of pre-emption in favour of appellant and against respondents subject to a deposit - Appellant deposited 4/5 of amount and filed an execution petition - Court wrongly proceeded on notion that deposit fell short by Rs. 100 - High Court set aside decree of pre-emption granted in favour of appellant - Whether Court can extend time owing to inadvertent arithmetical mistake u/s. 148, CPC and whether HC erred in interfering with that order? - Held, yes - Courts have power u/s. 148 of CPC, 1908 to grant time - Purpose is to serve ends of justice - Courts have discretion to extend time when mistake or delay is bona fide - Order of HC not sustainable - Appeal allowed.
