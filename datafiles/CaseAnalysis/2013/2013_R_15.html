Raghbir Chand and others v State of Punjab

Supreme Court of India

05 August 2013

Case Analysis

Bench

Ranjan Gogoi

Where Reported

2013 Indlaw SC 486; (2013) 12 SCC 294; AIR 2013 SC (Supp) 1005; 2013 CRLJ 4456; 2013 (4) JCC 2564; JT 2013 (11) SC 4; 2013 (4) Law Herald (P&H) 3173; 2013 (3) RCR(Criminal) 976; [2013] 11 S.C.R. 744

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 300, 302, 304, 323, 324, 34 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Accused persons/appellants allegedly murdered deceased - A case was registered against appellants u/ss. 300, 302, 304, 323, 324 r/w s. 34 of IPC - Trial Court passed conviction order - Appellants filed appeal before HC - HC affirmed order passed by Trial Court - Aggrieved by affirmation of conviction appellants filed instant appeal - Whether impugned conviction order passed by HC was sustainable - Held, after arrival of deceased, appellant No. 4 gave 4-5 blows in abdomen of deceased as result of which he fell down - Prosecution evidence also demonstrates that after deceased had fallen down on ground none of other appellants had assaulted him - In view of SC, could not constitute safe and sufficient basis for us to come to conclusion that an inference of common intention of all four appellants to cause death of deceased could be safely made so as to hold appellant no. 1, 2 and 3 vicariously liable for death of deceased - After carefully weighed facts and circumstances of case and options and conclusions that said facts would reasonably admit, SC opinioned that correct conclusion in present case would be that appellant no. 4 had requisite intention if not of causing death at least of causing such bodily injury which was likely to cause death - Acts attributable to appellant no. 4 do not also attract any of exceptions enumerated u/s. 300 of IPC - SC therefore affirmed conviction and sentence of appellant no. 4 u/s. 302 of IPC - Insofar as conviction of said appellant for offences u/s. 324 and 323 r/w s. 34 of IPC was concerned, SC had no hesitation in affirming same - Conviction of appellant no. 1, 2 and 3 u/s. 302 r/w s. 34 of IPC was set aside while their conviction u/s. 324 with aid of s. 34 and s. 323 of IPC sentences imposed upon them were maintained - Conviction of appellant no. 4 u/ss. 302, 324, 323 r/w s. 34 of IPC as well as sentences imposed were maintained - Appeal partly allowed.
