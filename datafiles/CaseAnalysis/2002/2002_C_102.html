Shalimar Rubber Industries and others v Collector of Central Excise, Cochin

Supreme Court of India

22 November 2002

Case Analysis

Bench

N. Santosh Hegde, B.P. Singh

Where Reported

2002 Indlaw SC 1406; (2003) 1 SCC 156; AIR 2003 SC 237; 2003 (2) CalLT(SC) 18; 2002 (84) ECC 718; 2002 (146) E.L.T. 248; JT 2002 (9) SC 435; 2003 (1) KHCACJ 82; 2002 (53) RLT 503; 2002(8) SCALE 544; 2002 (6) SLT 547; 2002 (8) Supreme 262

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax; Trade

Summary: Central Excise Tariff Act - S. 6 - Central Excise Rules - Rules 173B, 173C, 173F, 173G and 174 - violation of - show cause notice issued for not maintaining proper accounts and for evading payment of duty - collector after enquiry imposed penalty - directed confiscation of property - held, if the Collector can accept a statement allegedly made by a partner of the Universal Agencies which is not confirmed by his oral evidence in the inquiry and not subjected to cross-examination, we fail to understand how he could reject the letter signed by the very same person wherein he has given a diametrically opposed statement - collector used a different yardstick in assessing the evidence - no clandestine purchase of carbon black from M/s. Universal Agencies - if, this vital material as to the clandestine purchase of carbon black from M/s. Universal Agencies by the appellant is excluded, then, the foundation of the department's case fails.
