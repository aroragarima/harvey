Madnani Construction Corporation Private Limited v Union of India and Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

A.K. Ganguly, Markandey Katju

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1828; (2010) 1 SCC 549; AIR 2010 SC 383; 2010 (2) ALD(SC) 34; 2010(1) ALL MR 460; 2010 (1) AWC 862; 2010 (3) CTC 327; 2010 (1) ESC 31; JT 2009 (14) SC 612; 2010 (1) MLJ(SC) 797; 2010 (2) MPLJ 10; 2009(14) SCALE 399; [2009] 16 S.C.R. 216

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR

Keywords: Absence Of Arbitrator

Summary: Arbitration & ADR - Arbitration Act, 1940 - Interest Act, 1978 - Arbitral award - Challenged - Agreement entered between appellant and respondent (North Eastern Railway) - Dispute arose - Application filed for appointment of an arbitrator - Civil Judge appointed Arbitrator - Award passed by Arbitrator - Appellant filed application u/s.17 of Arbitration Act for passing a decree according to the award - Respondent filed application u/s. 30 r/w s. 33 of Act for setting aside award and also filed objections against application filed u/s.17 of Act - Civil Judge made award a Rule of Court - Appeal filed thereagainst was partly allowed by HC on the ground that certain matters allowed by arbitrator were excepted matters and non-arbitrable and the Arbitrator committed an illegality in allowing them while the remaining were arbitrable - Further, the High Court relied on cl. 30 and cl. 52 to hold that payment of interest was barred - Hence, present appeal - (A) Whether HC was justified in holding that certain items were excepted matters and were non-arbitrable and Arbitrator committed an illegality in allowing them? - Held, when arbitrator on basis of record and materials placed before him by respondents came to a specific findings which have not been stigmatized as perverse by HC, HC in reaching its conclusions cannot ignore those findings - However, in present case, HC completely ignored factual finding by arbitrator - Further, there is clear finding of arbitrator that order to deny claims of contractor as covered under excepted matters, procedure prescribed for bringing those claims under excepted matters has not been followed and HC has not expressed any dis-agreement on that - Therefore, finding of HC that those items are non-arbitrable cannot be sustained - (B) Arbitrator's jurisdiction and authority to grant interest - Whether HC justified in concluding that contract prohibited payment of interest and award for interest and therefore it could not be awarded? - Just a stipulation in contract purporting to indicate non-payment of interest cannot denude arbitrator from awarding interest - Held, in the instant case relevant clauses do not contain any prohibition on arbitrator to grant interest - HC was not right in interfering with arbitrator's award on matter of interest on basis of those clauses - In view of SC judgment Secretary, Irrigation Department, Government of Orissa and Others vs. G.C. Roy, 1991 INDLAW SC 421 no interference is called for with award passed by arbitrator - Judgment of HC set aside and award is upheld - Appeal allowed.
