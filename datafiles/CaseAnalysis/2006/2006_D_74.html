Director General, Directorate General of Doordarshan and Others v Anand Patwardhan and Another

Supreme Court of India

25 August 2006

Case Analysis

Bench

AR. Lakshmanan, Lokeshwar Singh Panta

Where Reported

2006 Indlaw SC 445; (2006) 8 SCC 433; AIR 2006 SC 3346; 2007 (1) JhrCR(SC) 321; JT 2006 (8) SC 255; 2006 (4) MLJ(SC) 1599; 2006(8) SCALE 575; [2006] Supp5 S.C.R. 403

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution; Criminal; Media & Entertainment; Practice & Procedure

Summary: [A.] Media & Entertainment - Constitution - Constitution Of India, 1950, arts. 19(1)(a), 19(2) - Cinematograph Act, 1952 - IPC,1860, s. 292 - Decision of appellant Doordarshan not to telecast documentary film made by respondent titled 'Father, son and Holy War' - Bombay HC directed appellant to telecast documentary film - Appeal against - Whether any film producer has a right to insist that his film must be shown on Doordarshan and whether HC was justified in directing screening of film certified as U/A notwithstanding the fact that as a matter of policy, Doordarshan does not telecast adult film? - Whether policy of Doordarshan of not telecasting adult movies can be said to be violative of art.19(1)(a) as has been held by HC? - Held, film in its entirety has a serious message to convey and is relevant in present context Doordarshan being a State controlled agency funded by public funds could not have denied access to screen respondent's documentary except on specified valid grounds - Appeal dismissed.
