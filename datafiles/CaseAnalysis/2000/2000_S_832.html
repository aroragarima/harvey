State of Orissa v Sudhakar Das (Dead) By Lrs

Supreme Court of India

23 February 2000

Case Analysis

Bench

A.S. Anand, R.C. Lahoti, S. Rajendra Babu

Where Reported

2000 Indlaw SC 2559; (2000) 3 SCC 27; AIR 2000 SC 1294; 2000 (4) ALD(SC) 56; 2000 ALJ 869; 2000 (39) ALR 326; 2000 (1) ARBLR 444; 2000 (3) Bom.C.R. 499; 2000 (2) CLT 29; JT 2000 (2) SC 465; 2000 (125) PLR 763; 2000 (2) RAJ 30; 2000 (2) RCR(Civil) 509A; 2000(2) SCALE 124; 2000 (2) SCJ 111; [2000] 1 S.C.R. 1136; 2000 (2) SLT 410; 2000 (2) Supreme 119

Case Digest

Subject: Arbitration & ADR; Constitution

Summary: Arbitration Act, 1940, ss. 20 and 29 - Arbitrator - Powers - Agreement not containing escalation clause - Arbitrator cannot infer any jurisdiction to award any amount towards escalation but can grant pendente lite interest if dispute is referred covering claim for interest - Held, award decree relating to pre-reference interest can be executed only on furnishing a bank guarantee to extent of that amount - Subject to decision of Constitution Bench to which issue has been referred.
