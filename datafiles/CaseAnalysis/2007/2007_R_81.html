Director, Krishi Utpadan Mandi Samiti and another v Ram Kishan Daya Ram and Company

Supreme Court of India

19 September 2007

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, H.S. Bedi

Where Reported

2007 Indlaw SC 1620; (2007) 10 SCC 56; 2008 (1) AWC 1083; JT 2008 (1) SC 84; 2007 (103) RD 631; 2007(12) SCALE 172; [2007] 10 S.C.R. 587; 2007 (3) UC 1961

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure; Trade

Summary: Trade - Practice & Procedure - Uttar Pradesh Krishi Utpadan Mandi Adhiniyam, 1964, ss. 2, 17 - Market fee - Levy and demand - Legality - State Market Committee levied market fee for the purchase of tendu leaves by appellant/Corporation - Appellant, in turn, demanded the market fee from respondent/individual purchasers - Respondent challenged the demand and levy before the HC - HC directed the appellant to refund the collected levy back to respondents - Whether cl. (3) of sub-s. (iii) of s. 17 was attracted in the instant case - Held, definition of term 'trader' u/s. 2(f) of the Act did not envisage that all traders must be licensed traders for the purpose of realisation of the market fee - Proviso appended to cl. (3) of sub-s. (iii) of s. 17 of the Act, in no uncertain terms, provided that the trader would be bound to pay the market fee and should not be absolved from such liability on the ground that he had not realised it from the purchaser - Appellant could not have escaped its liability from payment of the market fee, only because the appellant as also the State lost the writ petition - Appellant, therefore, did not entitle to fall back upon the respondent for the purpose of realisation of market fee - No infirmity in the impugned judgement - Appeals dismissed.
