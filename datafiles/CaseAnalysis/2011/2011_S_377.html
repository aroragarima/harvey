Ramachandran and others Etc. v State of Kerala

Supreme Court of India

02 September 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

Balbir Singh Chauhan, P. Sathasivam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 612; (2011) 9 SCC 257; 2011 CRLJ 4845; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 1056; 2011 (4) RCR(Criminal) 480; 2011(9) SCALE 676; [2011] 13 S.C.R. 923

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Criminal - Practice & Procedure - Indian Penal Code, 1860, ss. 143,147,148, 149,307,332, 449,427 and 302 - Murder - Conviction - Sustainability - Appellants /accused allegedly murdered deceased - Trial Court found appellant/accused guilty and convicted u/ss. 143,147,148,307,323,324,449,427,302 and 149 IPC - Appellant / accused filed appeal before HC - HC modified order and conviction of A.7, 10 and 11 u/s. 302 was set aside - However confirmed conviction and sentence for other offences - Hence, instant appeal - Appellant contended that set of appellants who had been convicted u/ss. 302, 149 IPC could have been convicted u/s. 304-part I, IPC - Whether confirmation of conviction by HC was sustainable - Held, once it was established that unlawful assembly had common object, it was not necessary that all persons forming unlawful assembly should be shown to had committed some overt act - For purpose of incurring vicarious liability under provision, liability of other members of unlawful assembly for offence committed during continuance of occurrence, rests upon fact whether other members knew before hand that offence actually committed was likely to be committed in prosecution of common object - In instant case 17 accused gathered and waited for appropriate time knowing it well that PW.2 would return from temple - Accused were carrying one sword stick, knife, two choppers and twelve iron rods, which were used for committing offence and causing injuries to victims and deceased received as many as 34 injuries - In view of above circumstances, it could not be held that appellants had not participated to prosecute a 'common object' - Thus, both Trial Court and HC had proceeded in correct perspective and applied provision of s. 149 IPC correctly - Hence, conviction of appellants was upheld - Appeal disposed of.
