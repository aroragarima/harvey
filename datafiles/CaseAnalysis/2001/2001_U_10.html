Sapan Kumar Pandit v U. P. State Electricity Board and Others

Supreme Court of India

24 July 2001

Case Analysis

Bench

K.T. Thomas, R.P. Sethi

Where Reported

2001 Indlaw SC 20464; (2001) 6 SCC 222; (2001) SCC (L&S) 946; AIR 2001 SC 2562; 2001 (5) ALD(SC) 31; 2001 (3) AWC 2342; 2001 (3) CLR 16; 2001 (90) FLR 754; JT 2001 (5) SC 592; 2001 LabIC 2814; 2001 (2) LLJ 788; 2001 (3) LLN 862; 2001(4) SCALE 467; 2001 (4) SCJ 332; [2001] 3 S.C.R. 1081; 2001 (3) SLR 608; 2001 (5) SLT 380; 2001 (5) Supreme 235; 2001 (3) UPLBEC 2099

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Corporate - Labour & Industrial Law - Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, s. 10 - Uttar Pradesh Industrial Disputes Act, 1947, ss. 4-K and 33-A - Whether a state Govt. can revive a dispute which had submerged in stupor by long lapse of time and re-kindled by making a reference of it to adjudication as no time limit is fixed for making the reference for a dispute for adjudication in s. 4-K of the U. P. Industrial Disputes Act, 1947? - The period envisaged by the expression 'at any time' terminates with the eclipse of the industrial dispute, i.e. if the dispute existed on the day reference was made by Govt., it is idle to ascertain the number of years which elapsed since the commencement of the dispute to determine whether the delay would have extinguished the power of the Govt. to make the reference - The only authority which can form such an opinion is the Govt. and if the Govt. decides to make the reference there is a presumption that in the opinion of the Govt. there existed such a dispute - Held, on the date of reference in this case the conciliation proceedings were not concluded and if so, it cannot be said that the dispute did not exist on that day - When the dispute remained alive though not galvanized by the workmen or the Union on account of other justified reasons it does not cause the dispute to wane into total eclipse - When the Govt. have chosen to refer the dispute for adjudication u/s. 4-K, the reference could not be quashed merely on the ground of delay - Appeal allowed.
