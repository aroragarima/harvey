Deputy Custodian, Evacuee Property, New Delhiand v Official Receiver of The Estate of Daulat

Supreme Court of India

03 September 1964

Case Analysis

Bench

Pralhad Balacharya Gajendragadkar, N. Rajagopala Ayyangar, J.C. Shah

Where Reported

1964 Indlaw SC 123; AIR 1965 SC 951; [1965] 1 S.C.R. 220

Case Digest

Subject: Corporate; Land & Property; Contract & Commercial

Keywords: Evacuee Property & Displaced Persons

Summary: Land & Property - Administration of Evacuee Property Act, 1950 - Provincial Insolvency Act, 1920 - Whether declaration u/s. 7(1) can be made in respect of properties which have been transferred by intending evacuees before migration? - Held, inclusive part of definition prescribed by s. 2(f) brings within scope of definition, properties which are transferred by an Indian citizen before he migrated to Pakistan - If intending evacuees transferred their properties before migration, their properties would be deemed to be evacuee properties for purpose of s. 7(l) and transferees would have to submit to vesting of said properties in Custodian u/s. 8 (1) of Act - Ss. 7(1 A) and 7(2) deal with other cases of transfer and make them ineffective for purpose of s. 7 (1) - Legislature has taken precaution of prescribing a blanket ban on transfers of all properties in respect of which proceedings have commenced u/s. 7(1) - This ban operates even in respect of properties which may ultimately be found to be not evacuee properties, and that means intention of legislature clearly was to leave all properties as they were when proceedings have been commenced in respect of them u/s. 7(1) - Position appears to be that transfers made by intending evacuees before they migrated from India come within definition of evacuee property, and declaration can be made in respect of properties so transferred u/s. 7(1) - Appeal allowed.
