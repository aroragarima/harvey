Vasudev Dhanjibhai Modi v Rajabhai Abdul Rehman and Others

Supreme Court of India

18 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, K.S. Hegde, A.N. Grover

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 86; (1970) 1 SCC 670; AIR 1970 SC 1475; 1970 (2) MLJ(SC) 85; 1970 RCJ 716; 1970 RCR(Rent) 427; [1971] 1 S.C.R. 66

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property; Practice & Procedure

Keywords: Bombay Reorganisation Act, 1960, Arbitration Act 1899, Jurisdiction of executing Court

Summary: Land & Property - Practice & Procedure - Suits Valuation Act, 1887, s. 11 - Execution of decree - Jurisdiction of executing Court - Legality - Appellant (landlord) filed a suit for ejectment and for payment of rent in arrears against respondent (tenant) - Trial Court dismissed suit - Aggrieved appellant filed an appeal before Lower Appellate Court, which was allowed and decree of ejectment was passed in favour of appellant, which was confirmed in revision before HC - Thereafter, appellant field execution petition, wherein respondent contended that Trial Court had no jurisdiction to entertain suit - However, in a petition before HC filed by respondent, HC reversed order of Trial Court and dismissed execution petition - Hence, instant appeal - Whether Trial Court had jurisdiction to execute the decree passed by it - Held, suit for ejectment against respondent was instituted by appellant in Court of Small Causes - No objection was raised that Court had no jurisdiction to entertain the suit - Objection was not raised even in appeal, nor before HC - Trial Court dismissed the suit on merits; the decree was reversed by Lower Appellate Court and that decree was confirmed by HC - Objection was raised for the first time when the decree was sought to be executed - Court executing a decree could not go behind the decree : between the parties or their representatives it should take the decree according to its tenor, and could not entertain any objection that decree was incorrect in law or on facts - Until it was set aside by an appropriate proceeding in appeal or revision, a decree even if it be erroneous was still binding between parties - Question of jurisdiction could not be permitted to be raised in an execution proceeding so as to displace jurisdiction of Court which passed decree - Appeal dismissed.
