National Insurance Company Ltd v Smt. Saroj & Ors.

Supreme Court of India

12 May 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

S.B. Sinha, Mukundakam Sharma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 766; (2009) 13 SCC 508; 2009 (3) ACC 1; 2009 (76) ALR 155; 2009 (4) AWC 3337; 2009 (4) JhrCR(SC) 41; 2009 (156) PLR 768; 2009 (3) RCR(Civil) 431; 2009(8) SCALE 188; [2009] 9 S.C.R. 81

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Keywords: Loss Of Future Earning

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988 - Vehicular accident - Claim of compensation - Sum claimed Rs. 20, 00, 000/- - Awarded by MACT, Rs. 22, 12, 200/- - Deceased who was aged 41 yrs, 10 mns and 9 days, was occupying the post of Technical in a Weld Shop Work in Maruti Udyog Limited; net salary Rs. 16, 110 pm - MACT and High Court on evidences brought on record found salary at Rs. 17, 244.95 pm; multiplier adopted was 16 - Whether quantum of compensation awarded by MACT and upheld by HC, proper? - Held, Yes - It was not disputed that multiplier method can be applied for the purpose of determination of the amount of compensation in a motor accident in terms of provisions of MV Act - Court noted the dichotomy existent in the paras 5 and 6 of the Note appended to sch. II of Act in terms whereof the multiplier was to be adopted only in case of permanent total or partial disability, and posits question as to 'why the injured claimant and/or heirs and legal representatives of the victim in a case of death on proof of negligence on the part of the driver of a motor vehicle would get a lesser amount than the one specified in the Second Schedule although both are similarly situated? - Two sets of decisions - Bar though had accepted that the multiplier specified in the sch. II should be taken to be the guidelines - Being without merit - Appeal dismissed.
