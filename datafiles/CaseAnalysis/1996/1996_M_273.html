Commissioner of Income Tax, Central-I, Bombay v Messrs. Empire Estate, Bombay

Supreme Court of India

29 January 1996

Case Analysis

Bench

S.P. Bharucha, Sujata V. Manohar, J.S. Verma

Where Reported

1996 Indlaw SC 3435; (1996) 2 SCC 345; 1996 (1) AD(SC) 963; 1996 (132) CTR 221; [1996] 218 ITR 355; JT 1996 (1) SC 675; 1996(1) SCALE 572; [1996] 1 S.C.R. 1004; 1996 (1) Supreme 640; [1996] 85 TAXMAN 153; 1996 (2) TLR 277

Case Digest

Subject: Income Tax & Direct Taxes; Indirect Tax; Practice & Procedure

Summary: Income Tax & Direct Taxes - Trusts & Associations - Income Tax Act, 1961, ss. 187(2), 170, 143, 144, 257 and 188 - Partnership Act, s. 42 - Whether Tribunal justified in holding that there should be two assessments as the assessee's case did not fall within the provisions of s. 187(2) of the Income Tax Act, 1961? - S. 188 states that where a firm carrying on a business is succeeded by another firm and the case is not covered by s. 187, separate assessments have to be made on predecessor firm and the successor firm - Partnership deeds provided that the death of a partner would not dissolve the partnerships - Death of a partner, therefore, did not dissolve the partnerships and the businesses were continued by reconstituted partnerships - It is a case of reconstitution of partnership and clubbing periods, made one assessment - Answered in favour of respondent - Order accordingly.
