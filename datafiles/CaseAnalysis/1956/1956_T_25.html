Â Banarsi Das and Others v State of Uttar Pradesh and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1956

Case Analysis

Bench

Bhuvaneshwar Prasad Sinha, Natwarlal Harilal Bhagwati, Sudhi Ranjan Das, Syed Jaffer Imam, T.L. Venkatarama Aiyyar

Where Reported

1956 Indlaw SC 73; AIR 1956 SC 520; 1956 ALJ 517; 1956 SCJ 529; [1956] 1 S.C.R. 357

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Government Servants Conduct Rules, Zamindari Abolition Act

Summary: Constitution - Constitution of India, 1950, arts. 14 and 16 - Revised Land Records Manual of State of UP, 1953 - 'Patwaris' of State of UP - Part time employees - Formed themselves into an association by name 'The U P Patwari's Association', with a view to improving their prospects and emoluments - 'Patwaris' were much in demand due to Zamindari Abolition Act - Association held meetings and passed resolutions demanding increase in pay etc. - Representations made to State authorities - Went into pen down strike - Association de-recognised by State - Revised Land Records Manual passed, embodying new amended rules regarding recruitment, conditions of service and duties of 'patwaris' - Agitation thereagainst - Mass resignation - Accepted by State - Petitioners relieved of their duties - Govt. announced creation of a new service of 'Lekhpals' and proceeded to organise that service by recruiting new personnel which included most of old patwaris - New cadre also included all those patwaris whose record of service was free from blemishes and who had withdrawn their resignations - Govt. thus had given locus poenitentiae to those of ex-patwaris who have realised their mistake in joining agitation - Those who were left out, challenged whole process on ground of arts. 14 and 16 - Held, Govt. is within its rights to lay down certain qualifications for new recruits - It is entitled to exclude those persons who have betrayed a lack of proper sense of discipline - It cannot therefore be said that Govt. has denied an equal opportunity to those who are equal in all respects - If persons already under Govt. employment on part-time basis had shown themselves not to be amenable to proper discipline in Govt. offices, it was open to Govt. not to appoint such persons to permanent cadre of Lekhpals because such persons could not be said to be as efficient as those who had excellent records of service and had shown greater sense of responsibility to their employers - Denial of equality of opportunity u/art. 16, not made out - Petitions dismissed.
