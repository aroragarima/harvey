Harshendra Kumar D. v Rebatilata Koley Etc.

Supreme Court of India

08 February 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

R.M. Lodha, Aftab Alam

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 87; (2011) 3 SCC 351; AIR 2011 SC 1090; 2011 ALL MR (Cri) 955; 2011 (1) BC 685; [2011] 162 Comp Cas 247; 2011 (1) CompLJ 546; 2011 CRLJ 1626; 2011 (1) JCC (NI) 42; JT 2011 (2) SC 586; 2011 (1) KLT 732; 2011 (4) MLJ(Crl) 171; 2011 (1) RCR(Criminal) 887; 2011(2) SCALE 278; [2011] 2 S.C.R. 670; 2011 (1) WLN(SC) 42

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Keywords: Public Financial Institutions and Negotiable Instruments Laws (Amendment) Act, 1988

Summary: Criminal - Negotiable Instruments Act, 1881, ss. 138 and 141 - Code of Criminal Procedure, 1973, ss. 397 and 401 - Quashing of criminal proceedings - Dishonour of Cheque - Liability of Director of Company - Complainants were interested in business relationship with the Company for the sale of bio-ceramic products - Complainants, for the orders they had placed, issued demand drafts in favour of Company - It was alleged that the Company had not delivered the products ordered by the complainants and accordingly they asked the Company for return of their money - Company issued cheques, which were dishonoured by the Bank/s on presentation - Complainants filed complaints u/s. 138 r/w s. 141 of the NI Act - Metropolitan Magistrate issued summons to all the accused persons including the appellant (director of the company) - Appellant challenged the proceedings initiated by the complainants against him - It was submitted that the cheques which were issued on behalf of the Company to the complainants were issued after appellant's resignation - HC held that resignation by the appellant as Director of the Company was a defence of the accused and the defence was a matter for consideration at the trial on the basis of evidence which could not be decided by the Court in revisional jurisdiction - Hence, instant appeal - Held, words 'every person who, at the time the offence was committed', occurring in s.141(1) of the NI Act indicate that criminal liability of a director must be determined on the date the offence was alleged to have been committed - A director whose resignation had been accepted by the company and that had been duly notified to the Registrar of Companies could not be made accountable and fastened with liability for anything done by the company after the acceptance of his resignation - In the instant case, on the date the offence was committed by the Company, the appellant was not the Director; he had nothing to do with the affairs of the Company - Judgment of the HC and the summons issued by the Metropolitan Magistrate to the appellant were set aside - Complaints as against the appellant quashed - Appeals allowed.
