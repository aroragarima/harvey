Bhagwan Budha Prathmik Technical Training College Nirmali v State of Bihar and another

Supreme Court of India

23 November 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Gokhale, R.V. Raveendran

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 992; (2010) 13 SCC 572; 2011(1) ALL MR 434; 2011 (84) ALR 258; 2011 (4) AWC 4096; 2010 (5) ESC 704; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 17; JT 2010 (13) SC 201; 2011 (1) MLJ(SC) 1025; 2010(12) SCALE 364; 2011 (1) UPLBEC 72

Case Digest

Subject: Education

Summary: Education - National Council for Teachers Education Act, 1993 - National Council for Teachers Education (NCTE) - Recognition - Grant of - Appellant/institution admitted students for teachers' training course pending approval/recognition from respondent/State - Appellant filed a writ petition seeking direction to respondent/State for affording recognition - HC directed the respondent to grant recognition to appellant - In the meantime, students completed their course and as a result, respondent/State allowed the students to appear for the examination on the basis of another HC order - Respondent/State withheld the result of students which were subjected to the grant of recognition - Subsequently, 1993 Act came into force, however, appellant did not take any steps to obtain recognition from NCTE - Students waiting for the results filed writ petition - Single Judge dismissed writ petition, however, DB disposed of the writ appeal in view of the statement by the counsel on behalf of the respondent/State that the recognition would be processed very soon - Appellant filed a representation to the Education Officer - Education Officer, consequently, granted recognition - Since the results were not declared, appellant filed another writ petition - Single Judge dismissed the writ petition holding that appellant has to seek recognition from NCTE and not from State Government - Appellant filed appeal before the DB which was dismissed later - Respondent/State, in the meantime, rescinded the recognition granted to the appellant - (A) Whether the students of the appellant are allowed to write the examination of State Education Board - Held, HC allowed the students for examination only if the State Education Board was satisfied as to the bona fides and genuineness of the students and the results were not to be published until the question of recognition was decided finally by the respondent - Since the appellant/institution was closed down, Board could not ascertain the bona fides and genuineness of the claims of the students after so many years to permit them to appear for the examination - What facilities were available in appellant/institution at the relevant time can also not be now ascertained by NCTE - Thus the students of the appellant cannot be permitted to take the Board examination since the appellant does not have the recognition either from the State Government or from NCTE - (B) Whether the State Government had the authority to issue the order granting recognition for the period 1987-95 when the 1993 Act had already come in force way back in July 1995 - Held, every institution offering a course of training in teacher education has to obtain a recognition from the Regional Committee of NCTE after the appointed date u/s. 14 of the 1993 Act - State Government cannot exercise power of granting recognition after the appointed date of 1993 Act nor can the State Education Board to hold examination of the students of a teacher training institute after the appointed day unless the institute is recognized by Regional Committee of the NCTE as laid down in s. 16 of the Act - State Governments' order of recognition was outside its powers and therefore the State Government was quite right in rescinding the same by a subsequent order - An institution claiming to teach such students should conduct itself with all the more responsibility and ought to be more careful and diligent - When the NCTE Act came into force in 1995, the institution should have applied to the concerned authorities diligently within the time stipulated - Therefore, impugned orders of HC do not call for any interference - Appeal disposed of.
