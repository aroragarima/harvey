Sundari and Others v Laxmi and Others

Supreme Court of India

28 August 1979

Case Analysis

Bench

P.S. Kailasam, A.C. Gupta

Where Reported

1979 Indlaw SC 316; (1980) 1 SCC 19; AIR 1980 SC 198; 1980 (1) KarLJ 1; [1980] 1 S.C.R. 404

Case Digest

Subject: Family & Personal; Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Family & Personal - Madras Aliyasanthana Act, 1949 - Hindu Succession Act, 1956, s. 7(2) - Suit for partition - Intestate succession - Legality - Petitioners and respondents were governed by Aliyasanthana Law prevalent in their district and were members of a 'Kutumba' descended from a common ancestress - On appeal before HC, HC held that the award decree in earlier suit in between parties did not amount to partition and that suit for partition was maintainable - However, def.nos. 22 to 24 were male members and prior to passing of final decree def. nos. 23 and 24 were died - LRs of def.no. 23 filed a petition claiming that out of the share allotted to 'kavaru' of def. nos. 22 to 24, one-third representing the share or interest of def.nos. 24 and 23 be allotted to them - Petition was opposed on the ground that each one of def.nos. 22, 23 and 24 was a separate 'nissanthathi kavaru' and on the death of each of def. nos. 24 and 23 his share or interest devolved upon the 'santhathi kavarus' nearest to him to which def.nos. 11, 12 and 16 belonged - Trial Court dismissed petitions - On appeal before HC, HC held that property descended according to the rules of intestate succession contained in 1956 Act - Hence, instant appeal - Appellants contended that in law there was no undivided interest in the property which def.nos. 24 and 23 owned at the time of their death as required u/s. 7(2) of 1956 Act - How far Aliyasanthana Act regarding partition and succession was affected by the 1956 Act - Held, provisions of Aliyasanthana law whether customary or statutory would cease to apply, in so far as they were inconsistent with the provisions of 1956 Act - Effect of provisions of 1956 Act was that after the coming into force of 1956 Act an undivided interest of a Hindu would devolve as provided for u/s. 7(2) of 1956 Act while in the case of separate property it would devolve on his heirs as provided for in the 1956 Act - Even though a 'nissanthathi kavaru' might have a limited interest as the devolution prescribed for in Aliyasanthana Act was no more applicable the devolution would be under 1956 Act - In instant case, property was found to be undivided as between def. nos. 22, 23 and 24 and thus the position was that on the death of each one of defendants his undivided interest would devolve on his heirs - Separate property was not enlarged into an absolute estate u/s. 7(2) of 1956 Act but on death it devolves on the heirs as provided under 1956 Act - Thus, it would not revert back to the 'kutumba' but only to the heirs as provided for under 1956 Act - Appeal dismissed.
