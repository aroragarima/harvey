Neelu Chopra & Anr. v Bharti

Supreme Court of India

07 October 2009

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, Deepak Verma

Where Reported

2009 Indlaw SC 1496; (2009) 10 SCC 184; 2010 ALL MR (Cri) 658; 2010 CRLJ 448; 2009 (4) JCC 3021; JT 2009 (13) SC 334; 2010 (4) MLJ(Crl) 80; 2010 (1) RCR(Criminal) 115; 2009(13) SCALE 313; [2009] 14 S.C.R. 1074

Case Digest

Subject: Criminal

Summary: Criminal - Indian Penal Code, 1860 s. 114 - Criminal Procedure Code, ss. 406, 482 498A - Dowry - Complaint filed by wife against husband and his parents (appellants) - Husband died - High Court, held that the complaint did show material sufficient to proceed against appellants however, did not quash the complaint - Appeal filed - Held, on perusal of the complaint it is inordinately vague with no particulars given - In order to lodge a proper compliant, mere mention of the relevant sections and the language of those sections is not adequate - What is required to be brought to the notice of the court is the particulars of the offence committed by each and every accused and the role played by each and every accused in committing of that offence - Therefore, it would be an abuse of process of law to allow the prosecution to continue against the aged parents of the husband - Moreover, despite service of notice, the complainant neither appeared before this court nor engaged any counsel to represent her - Judgment of the High Court is set aside - Complaint is quashed - Appeal is allowed accordingly.
