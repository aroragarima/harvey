Brij Bihari Lal Agarwal v High Court of Madhya

Supreme Court of India

26 November 1980

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, O. Chinnappa Reddy

Where Reported

1980 Indlaw SC 232; (1981) SCC (L&S) 238; (1981) 1 SCC 490; AIR 1981 SC 594; 1981 (42) FLR 102; 1981 LabIC 137; 1982 (1) LLJ 1; 1981 (1) LLN 228; [1981] 2 S.C.R. 297; 1981 (1) SLJ 412; 1980 (3) SLR 583; 1981 UJ 15

Case Digest

Subject: Labour & Industrial Law

Summary: Advocates & Judges - M.P. Shashkiya Sewak (Adhiwarshika Ayu) Sanshodhan Adhiniyam. 1976, r. 56(3) - Compulsory retirement - Challenged - Appellant was member of judicial service and was promoted to District and Sessions Judge - On 6-10-1977, order was made appointing him to Selection Grade post and he was thereafter appointed temporarily as Presiding Judge, of State Transport Appellate Tribunal - On 28-9-1979, State made an order u/r.56(3) of 1976, purporting to retire appellant in public interest - Aggrieved appellant filed writ before HC against retirement order which was dismissed - Hence, instant appeal - Whether appellant should be compulsorily retired.

Held, when considering question of compulsory retirement, it is no doubt desirable to make an overall assessment of Govt. servant's record, more than ordinary value should be attached to the confidential reports pertaining to the years immediately preceding such consideration. It is possible that a Govt. servant may possess a somewhat erratic record in the early years of service, but with the passage of time he may have so greatly improved that it would be of advantage to continue him in service up to the statutory age of superannuation. Therefore, recommendation by the HC recording its satisfaction that the appellant should be compulsorily retired, and the consequent order of the State acting on that recommendation, must be regarded as invalid. Thus, HC should consider the case again and take a fresh decision on the question whether it should recommend the compulsory retirement of the appellant, and for the State Govt. to act on that recommendation if it is made. Hence, appeal is allowed and the order dt. 28-9-1979 made by the State compulsorily retiring the appellant from service, and the recommendation of the HC on which the order is based, are quashed. Appeal allowed.
