Om Parkash v State of Haryana and Others

Supreme Court of India

16 March 1970

Case Analysis

Bench

J.C. Shah, A.N. Grover, K.S. Hegde

Where Reported

1970 Indlaw SC 534; (1971) 3 SCC 792; 1970 UJ 481

Case Digest

Subject: Land & Property

Summary: Land & Property - Constitution of India, 1950, art. 226 - Mines & Minerals (Regulation and Development) Act, 1957 - Appellant filed a petition in High Court for a writ restraining State from taking any proceedings for holding auctions for right to win Saltpetre to prejudice of appellant - HC summarily rejected petition - Appeal - Held, no rule that HC will not try issues of fact in a writ petition - In each case court has to consider whether party seeking relief has an alternative remedy which is equally efficacious by a suit, whether refusal to grant relief in a writ petition may amount to denying relief, whether claim is based substantially upon consideration of evidence oral and documentary of a complicated nature and whether case is otherwise fit for trial in exercise of jurisdiction to issue high prerogative writs - Further held, HC did not call upon State of file an affidavit and did not consider whether facts raised were so complicated or that for other reasons it would be inappropriate to try dispute in writ petition - HC was in error in summarily rejecting petition filed by appellant - Case is remanded to HC to be dealt with according to law - Appeal allowed.
