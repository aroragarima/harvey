Saroj And Others v Het Lal And Others

Supreme Court of India

07 December 2010

Case Analysis

Bench

V.S. Sirpurkar, T.S. Thakur

Where Reported

2010 Indlaw SC 1058; (2011) 1 SCC 388; 2011 ACJ 552; AIR 2011 SC 671; 2011(2) ALL MR 973; 2011 (84) ALR 442; 2011 (1) AWC 1; 2011 (1) JhrCR(SC) 160; JT 2010 (13) SC 739; 2011 (2) MLJ(SC) 619; 2011 (164) PLR 131; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 203; 2010(13) SCALE 185

Case Digest

Subject: Carriers & Transportation

Summary: Carriers & Transportation - Motor Vehicles Act, 1988, ss. 161, 163 and 166 - Accidental claim - Compensation - Appellants, legal representatives of deceased who died in a motor vehicular accident, filed a claim petition - It was alleged that motorcycle met with an accident as it was hit by a vehicle, which was being driven in a rash and negligent manner by respondent No.1 (driver) - Claim was opposed by the respondent No.1 and he pleaded that no such accident ever took place though he admitted that he was the driver of vehicle - Respondent No.2 (Owner of the vehicle) admitted the claim made in the claim petition to the effect that the vehicle was engaged in an accident with the vehicle of the deceased and that it was being driven by respondent No.1 who was his driver - However, Tribunal held that this was a case of hit and run accident and dismissed the claim petition - On appeal, HC confirmed the order and findings given by the Tribunal including a finding that the offending vehicle owned by respondent No.2 and allegedly driven by respondent No.1 was not involved in the accident - Hence, present appeal - Whether it was a hit and run case and that the concerned vehicle belonging to respondent No.2 was not involved in the accident? - Held, it was an admitted position in the pleadings of respondent No.2 that firstly, vehicle was involved in an accident with the motorcycle and secondly, the said vehicle was being driven by respondent No.1 - This admission in the pleadings was itself sufficient to hold that the concerned vehicle belonging to respondent No.2 was involved in the accident - This admission was never traversed by respondent No.2 and, thus, there was no occasion to hold that the said vehicle was not involved and that it was a hit and run case - Both the Courts below have failed to note this fact - Both the Courts below erred in giving the finding that it was a hit and run case and that the concerned vehicle belonging to respondent No.2 was not involved in the accident - Matter remitted back to the Tribunal to decide the liability of the respondents on account of the accident - Order accordingly.
