Ayurveda Pharmacy and Another v State of Tamil Nadu

Supreme Court of India

15 March 1989

Case Analysis

Bench

R.S. Pathak, Ranganath Misra

Where Reported

1989 Indlaw SC 21; (1989) 2 SCC 285; (1989) SCC (Cr) 612; AIR 1989 SC 1230; 1990 CRLJ 62; 1989 (25) ECR 139; 1989 (40) E.L.T. 273; JT 1989 (1) SC 539; 1989(1) SCALE 624; [1989] 2 S.C.R. 37; 1989 (2) UJ 116

Case Digest

Subject: Customs; Excise; Indirect Tax

Summary: Sales Tax - Tamil Nadu General Sales Tax Act, 1959 - Item Nos. 95 and 135 - Whether two medicines (Arishtams and Asavas) attract different considerations from those applied to other medicinal preparations? - No reason why these two medicines should be treated differently from general class of Ayurvedic medicines - Where commodities belong to same class, there must be a rational basis for discriminating between one commodity and another for purpose of imposing tax - Held, two preparations are medical preparations and even though they contain a high alcohol content, so long as they continue to be identified as medical preparations they must be treated, for purpose of sales tax law, in like manner as medical preparations generally, including those containing lower percentage of alcohol - Appeals allowed.
