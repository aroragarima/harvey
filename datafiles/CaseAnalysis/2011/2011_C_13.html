Mathai M. Paikeday v C.K. Antony

Supreme Court of India

11 July 2011

Case Analysis

Bench

H.L. Dattu, G.S. Singhvi

Where Reported

2011 Indlaw SC 475; (2011) 13 SCC 174; 2011 (6) ALD(SC) 100; 2011(5) ALL MR 485; 2012 (90) ALR 694; 2011 (5) AWC 4900; 2011 (4) CLT(SC) 290; 2011 (6) CTC 347; JT 2011 (12) SC 51; 2011 (7) MLJ(SC) 1052; 2011 (2) OLR 502; 2011 (3) RCR(Civil) 869; 2012 (115) RD 435; 2011 (3) RLW 2513; 2011(7) SCALE 718; [2011] 7 S.C.R. 230

Case Digest

Subject: Practice & Procedure

Summary: Practice & Procedure - Code of Civil Procedure, 1908, O. 33 r. 1, O. 44 r. 1 - Indigent prosecution - Entitlement - Appellant filed two suits for recovery of money against respondent - Trial Court decreed the suit in favour of appellant - Respondent filed appeal before the HC along with petitions to prosecute the said appeals as an indigent person under O. 44 r. 1 CPC - HC, by impugned order, permitted the respondent to prosecute the appeal as an indigent person - Whether the respondent was an indigent person as not possessed of sufficient means to pay the court fees and, consequently, entitled to avail the benefits under O. 44 of CPC - Held, respondent was a retired Deputy Conservator of Forest in the State and drawing a pension of rupees 10,500/- - Petitioner also stated in his deposition before the HC that his son was employed abroad and did not regularly send him money and in response to a suggestion, whether his bank account disclosed the amount of money sent by his son, he did not deny the suggestion - However, it was noteworthy to mention that respondent had never denied that his son sent him money - Further, the respondent had failed to establish that the amount of money received from his son was not substantial or insufficient to pay court fee by not producing passbook of his bank account - Non-production of bank account transaction details, amounted to suppression of the facts and in view of that, an adverse inference could be drawn against the respondent that he was receiving a substantial or sufficient amount of money from his son - Therefore, the amount of money received by the respondent from his son and by way of pension amounts to a sufficient means to pay court fee which disentitled him to be an indigent person under O. 33 r. 1 and O. 44 r. 1 CPC - Respondent should not be declared as an indigent person in order to prosecute appeals before the HC - Impugned HC order was set aside - Appeals allowed.
