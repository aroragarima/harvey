Union Of India & Anr. v K.S. Subramanian

Supreme Court of India

30 July 1976

Case Analysis

Bench

M. Hameedullah Beg, A.N. Ray, Jaswant Singh

Where Reported

1976 Indlaw SC 235; (1976) SCC (L&S) 492; (1976) 3 SCC 677; AIR 1976 SC 2433; 1976 LabIC 1551; 1977 (1) LLJ 5; 1977 (1) LLN 213; [1977] 1 S.C.R. 87; 1976 SLJ 539; 1976 (2) SLR 519; 1976 UJ 717

Case Digest

Subject: Constitution

Keywords: Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951

Summary: Constitution - Limitation Act, 1908 - Indian Railway Establishment Code, 1951 - Duty of High Court while appplication of precedents - Held, HC should try to find out and follow the opinions expressed by larger benches of the Supreme Court in preference to those expressed by smaller benches of the Court - If precedents are not applicable to facts of the case they should say so giving reasons supporting its point of view - Appeal allowed.
