Municipal Corporation, Raipur v Ashok Kumar Misra

Supreme Court of India

16 April 1991

Case Analysis

Bench

K. Ramaswamy, N.M. Kasliwal

Where Reported

1991 Indlaw SC 136; (1991) 3 SCC 325; (1991) SCC (L&S) 1046; AIR 1991 SC 1402; 1991 (16) ATC 927; JT 1991 (2) SC 599; 1991 LabIC 1266; 1991 (2) LLJ 343; 1991 (2) LLN 481; 1991(1) SCALE 753; [1991] 2 S.C.R. 320; 1991 (2) SLR 615; 1991 (2) UJ 170

Case Digest

Subject: Service

Summary: Service - Madhya Pradesh Civil Service Classification Control & Appeal (Rules), 1966 - Municipal Officers and Servants Recruitment Rules - Madhya Pradesh Government Servants' General Conditions of Service Rules, 1961 - Probation - Termination - Condition precedent - Error in law - Respondent, a clerk, was employed by the appellant and placed on probation for two years - After the expiry of the two year probation period, appellant terminated respondent's services with one months' notice in writing - Respondent filed a suit that the termination was violative of r. 9A of the 1966 Rules - On appeal, the HC allowed the appeal and decreed the respondent's suit - Appellant contended that the HC committed manifest error of law in finding that the respondent was deemed to have been confirmed u/r. 14 of the Municipal Officers and Servants Recruitment Rules which were no longer were in force - And that r. 8 of the 1961 expressly provided confirmation of probation as a condition precedent - Whether the HC committed an error in law in decreeing the respondent's suit - Held, reliance by the HC on r. 14 of the Municipal Officers and Servants Recruitment Rules was wrong as the Municipal Officers and Servants Recruitment Rules were superseded and were no longer in force based on s. 25(1) of the 1922 Act - R. 8 of the 1961 Rules, expressly postulated that the period of probation was subject to extension by order in writing for another period of one year - Passing the prescribed examinations and successful completion of probation and to make an order of confirmation were conditions precedent - Mere expiry of the initial period of probation did not automatically have the effect of deemed confirmation and the status of a deemed confirmation of the probation - An express order in that regard only conferred the status of an approved probationer - Thus note to r. 8(2) r/w r. 8(6) of the 1961 Rules manifested the legislative intent that confirmation of the probation of the respondent was to be made only on successful completion of the probation and the passing of the prescribed examinations - As it was not the respondent's case that he passed all the examinations - He was deemed to be continued on probation - Before confirmation the appointing authority was empowered to terminate the service of the probationer by issuing one month's notice in writing and on expiry thereof the service stood terminated without any further notice - Within three months from the date of expiry of original two years period of probation and within one year's period, the order of termination was made - Hence, the question of conducting an inquiry under the 1966 Rules did not arise - HC, therefore, committed manifest error of law in decreeing the respondent's suit - Appeal allowed.
